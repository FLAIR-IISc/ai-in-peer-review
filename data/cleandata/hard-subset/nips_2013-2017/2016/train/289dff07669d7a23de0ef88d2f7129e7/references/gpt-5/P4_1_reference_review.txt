Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES, a variant of CMA-ES that maintains and updates a triangular Cholesky factor A of the covariance matrix C = A A^T directly, instead of updating C and recomputing its square root via eigendecomposition. The key change is to replace the covariance update (rank-1 and rank-µ) with a sequence of rank-one updates applied to A (Algorithm 2), yielding an O(µ d^2) update per iteration. For step-size adaptation (CSA), which in standard CMA-ES uses C^{-1/2}, the authors approximate it by using A^{-1} and ignoring the orthogonal factor that relates C^{1/2} and A. They justify this approximation by showing that the sequence of orthogonal factors converges (Lemma 1) under the assumption that the normalized covariance shape converges, implying the rotation error diminishes in norm due to exponential forgetting.

This builds on prior attempts to reduce CMA-ES computational cost: delayed eigendecomposition [Hansen and Ostermeier], restricted models (e.g., diagonal/separable variants [Ros and Hansen; Loshchilov]), and direct updates of factorized forms (non-triangular Cholesky [Suttorp et al., 2009] and the triangular factor for special CMA variants [Krause and Igel, 2015]). The novelty here is (i) extending triangular Cholesky updates to standard CMA-ES with rank-µ, and (ii) a theoretical argument for why replacing C^{-1/2} by A^{-1} in CSA should not degrade performance asymptotically.

Experiments compare Cholesky-CMA-ES against a reference CMA-ES that recomputes the matrix square root each iteration (CMA-ES-Ref), a delayed-decomposition variant (CMA-ES/d), and an adaptation of the non-triangular Cholesky approach (Suttorp-CMA-ES), on standard noiseless test functions (Sphere, Cigar, Discus, Ellipsoid, Rosenbrock, Diffpowers) with random rotations and dimensions up to 256. Cholesky-CMA-ES matches CMA-ES-Ref in function evaluations and achieves substantial wall-clock speedups (e.g., ~20× at d=64), with growing advantage as d increases.

Quality:
- Technical soundness: The factor-update formulation is consistent with rank-one updates to a Cholesky factor for positive semidefinite additions. The complexity claim O(µ d^2) for full-covariance adaptation is reasonable and close to optimal given Θ(d^2) data movement per update. The CSA approximation is only partially justified: Lemma 1 relies on the convergence of the normalized covariance shape, which is not established for standard CMA-ES (only for simplified models in expectation). The argument that the rotation error vanishes due to exponential forgetting is plausible but lacks a bound on the induced bias or variance in step-size adaptation during transients.
- A notable technical error appears in the claims: “the diagonal elements of a triangular Cholesky factor are the square roots of the eigenvalues of the factorized matrix.” This is incorrect in general; the Cholesky diagonal entries are not related to eigenvalues except in special cases (e.g., diagonal matrices). This undermines the stated “eigenvalues for free” advantage. The paper should correct this and, if eigenvalues are needed (e.g., for conditioning monitoring), clarify whether approximate proxies or occasional eigendecompositions are used.
- Numerical stability of Algorithm 2 is not discussed. Standard cholupdate algorithms and their stability properties (e.g., using Givens/Householder transformations) should be cited and/or compared. Given only positive rank-one updates are used (β > 0), stability is likely acceptable, but empirical evidence or references would help. Also, using triangular solves rather than explicitly forming A^{-1} is preferable for numerical stability and performance.
- Experimental methodology is generally sound (100 trials, random rotations). However, excluding non-converged Rosenbrock trials may bias comparisons; a more standard treatment is to report success rate and expected running time (ERT). Hardware and BLAS details are not specified; since wall-clock time is central, a reproducibility note (CPU model, BLAS, compiler flags) is important. It would also be informative to report sampling cost (λ d^2) vs. update cost and how often p_sigma uses triangular solves.

Clarity:
The paper is mostly clear and well-organized. The rationale for the CSA approximation is explained concisely. The algorithms are helpful; still, a short derivation or reference for Algorithm 2 and comments on when it is numerically safe would improve clarity. There are typesetting/formatting issues in the references (placeholders like “?\Q2015\E”) that should be fixed. The “eigenvalues from the diagonal” statement should be corrected.

Originality:
The paper provides a nontrivial extension of triangular Cholesky updates from µ=1 and specific CMA variants to the standard CMA-ES with rank-µ, along with a new CSA approximation and justification. Relative to Suttorp et al. (non-triangular factor) and Krause & Igel (special case), this is a meaningful advance.

Significance:
Reducing the dominant per-iteration cost of full-covariance CMA-ES without sacrificing evaluation efficiency is practically important. The reported speedups (while maintaining evaluation counts) suggest substantial impact for medium-to-high dimensional black-box optimization where full covariance is still feasible. The approach preserves the modeling capacity of full CMA-ES, unlike diagonal/limited-memory variants.

Pros:
- Clear, simple algorithmic modification with a strong engineering payoff.
- Complexity improvement to O(µ d^2) with arguments for optimality in the full-covariance regime.
- Plausible theoretical justification for the CSA approximation.
- Extensive empirical comparison on standard benchmarks with rotation invariance tested; evaluation efficiency preserved, large wall-clock gains.

Cons:
- Incorrect claim about Cholesky diagonal equaling eigenvalues; needs correction.
- CSA approximation lacks rigorous bounds; convergence assumption not established for standard CMA-ES.
- Excluding failed runs on Rosenbrock; missing success-rate/ERT reporting.
- Limited scope of experiments: noiseless synthetic functions only; no reinforcement learning or noisy/hyperparameter-tuning tasks where CMA-ES is commonly used.
- Reproducibility details (hardware, BLAS, code release) and stability analysis of Algorithm 2 are insufficiently documented.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Correct the eigenvalue claim; clarify how conditioning is monitored without eigendecompositions.
- Provide numerical stability discussion and, if possible, comparisons to standard cholupdate routines; recommend triangular solves instead of forming A^{-1}.
- Report success rates/ERT and include the previously excluded Rosenbrock trials in aggregate statistics.
- Add experiments on noisy objectives and a real application (e.g., a small RL policy search) to test robustness.
- Include full runtime environment details and release code to support reproducibility.
- If available, summarize the supplementary analysis of rotation change (E_t) and its empirical decay.

Overall assessment:
A solid, practically impactful contribution that extends prior work to standard CMA-ES with convincing empirical speedups and modest theoretical support. With corrections and strengthened evaluation, it would be a strong addition to the literature. I recommend acceptance after addressing the noted issues, especially the incorrect eigenvalue claim and experimental reporting.
