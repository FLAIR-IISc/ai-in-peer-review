- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing costly matrix square root with Cholesky factor to achieve optimal asymptotic time and storage complexity.
- Claims O(μd²) time complexity (μ = O(log d)) vs standard O(d³), while preserving optimization performance empirically.
- Builds on prior Cholesky-based CMA-ES work (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015) but extends to standard CMA-ES with theoretical justification.
- Main theoretical contribution: Lemma 1 showing the approximation error from using the Cholesky factor vanishes asymptotically.
- Empirical results show equivalent function-evaluation performance to standard CMA-ES and substantial runtime speedups (e.g., 20× at d=64).
- Experiments use multiple benchmark functions and dimensions (4–256) with attention to rotation invariance.
- Addresses a real computational bottleneck, making full-covariance CMA-ES more practical for larger problems.
- Presentation and algorithms (Algorithm 1 & 2) are clear and reproducible.
- Overstates novelty: insufficiently distinguishes from earlier Cholesky-based CMA-ES variants that achieved similar complexity.
- Lacks numerical stability analysis and empirical evidence (e.g., condition numbers, behavior on ill-conditioned problems).
- Insufficient discussion of practical limits (cache effects, scaling to very large dimensions) and possible adaptations.
- Missing comparisons with more recent complexity-reduction CMA-ES variants (e.g., LM-CMA), weakening the empirical baseline.
- Overall assessment: strong technical contribution with sound theory and experiments, meeting conference standards but needing clearer positioning vs prior work.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify novelty, add numerical stability discussion/plots, and include comparisons with recent variants.
