- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace costly matrix square root with Cholesky factorization to reduce runtime from O(d³) toward O(µd²).
- Builds on prior CMA-ES work and earlier Cholesky attempts but adds theoretical justification (Lemma 1) for the approximation.
- Lemma 1: error from replacing C^{-1/2}_t with A^{-1}_t vanishes as the algorithm converges.
- Empirical results: matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations while substantially reducing wall-clock time across benchmarks and dimensions (d=4..256).
- Practical impact: achieves lower time complexity and same storage O(d²), and yields eigenvalue estimates via Cholesky diagonals.
- Paper is well organized with a clear Algorithm 1 and helpful background.
- Theoretical limitation: Lemma 1 assumes Ct converges, but CMA-ES randomness means Ct may not converge and non-convergent error bounds are not analyzed.
- Insufficient implementation details for reproducibility (Shark integration, numerical stability choices, parameter settings).
- Lacks comparative analysis with low-rank/approximate methods for very large dimensions.
- Cache effects observed (e.g., d=256 exceeding L2 cache) are not addressed in the complexity/scalability discussion.
- Complexity claim needs clarification: O(µd²) is only O(d²) if µ=O(1); with typical µ=O(log d) actual cost is O(d² log d).
- Recommendation: accept — significant, well-supported contribution with strong theory (Lemma 1) and convincing empirical runtime gains.
- Pro-accept: theoretically justified complexity reduction, empirical runtime benefits without performance loss, addresses key CMA-ES bottleneck, practical advantages (eigenvalues).
- Con-accept: relies on convergence assumption, lacks full reproducibility details, and omits trade-off analysis with low-rank approximations.
