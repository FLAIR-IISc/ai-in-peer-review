- Main contribution: use triangular Cholesky factorization to eliminate the square root in standard CMA-ES, reducing computation for matrix inverse.
- Provides theoretical justification that the Cholesky-based approach does not degrade algorithmic performance.
- Method reduces runtime complexity while keeping the number of objective function evaluations essentially unchanged.
- Paper is a valuable implementation of Cholesky factorization for CMA-ES.
- Reviewer asks for a clearer theoretical explanation of why the proposed computation is "numerically more stable."
- Notes that Cholesky-CMA-ES retains the same evaluation count as standard CMA-ES but lowers wall-clock time, with savings growing as dimensionality increases.
- Suggests exploring combination with low-dimensional approximation (Loshchilov, 2015) to better handle high-dimensional problems like neural network training.
