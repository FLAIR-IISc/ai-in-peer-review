- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that approximates CMA-ES with complexity O(μ d^2) vs O(d^3).
- Key idea: maintain and update a lower-triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance/square-root.
- Main difference from original CMA-ES: omission of estimating a random orthogonal matrix E_t each iteration.
- Theoretical claim: under restrictive convergence assumptions, E_t → I, so Cholesky and full CMA-ES become asymptotically equivalent.
- Empirical results: runtime and convergence plots on standard benchmarks show substantial speed-up with no loss of optimization performance on synthetic tests.
- Baselines: compared to two state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants that delay full covariance updates.
- Strength: solid contribution to speeding up CMA-ES, useful when evaluations are cheap and dimensionality is high.
- Practical limitation: in most real problems objective evaluation time dominates and typical dimensions are modest, so speed-up may have limited practical impact.
- Concern: theoretical assumptions are restrictive and formal guarantees are limited.
- Suggested experiment: track evolution of E_t (e.g., in various norms) by running full CMA-ES and the approximation simultaneously.
- Suggested: report actual wall-clock runtimes and performance of full CMA-ES without delay strategies for completeness.
- Suggested: run quadratics with continuously varied spectra (cf. Stich & Müller) to study solution quality and E_t error versus spectral distribution.
- Suggested: include a real-world experiment where speed matters (e.g., real-time robotics policy gradients).
