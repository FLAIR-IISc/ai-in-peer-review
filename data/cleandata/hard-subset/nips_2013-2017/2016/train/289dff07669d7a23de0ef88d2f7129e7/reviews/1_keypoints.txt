- Proposes a CMA-ES variant that updates the covariance via triangular Cholesky factors, cutting update complexity and memory.
- Approach effectively adapts the underlying metric and yields covariance eigenvalues for free.
- Empirical results show no worsening in function-evaluation counts compared to standard CMA-ES.
- Demonstrates substantial wall-clock speedups, particularly in large-dimensional problems.
- Likely a significant contribution and candidate default for medium/large-scale CMA-ES due to clear practical advantages.
- Paper is well written and thoroughly evaluated; relevant to ML, information geometry, reinforcement and supervised learning.
