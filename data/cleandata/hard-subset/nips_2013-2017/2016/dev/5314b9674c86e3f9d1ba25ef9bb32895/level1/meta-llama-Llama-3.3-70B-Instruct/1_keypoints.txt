- Proposes a novel supervised-learning method using matrix product states (MPS) / tensor networks to parameterize non-linear kernel models.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on MNIST with test error <1%.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation of the tensor network framework and the sweeping optimization algorithm.  
- Provides a thorough literature review and clearly distinguishes the approach from prior tensor-method work.  
- Key innovation: using tensor networks to regularize and optimize the weight tensor.  
- Strength: detailed presentation of the sweeping optimization training procedure.  
- Weakness: assumes substantial background in tensor methods and ML, limiting accessibility for non-experts.  
- Weakness: lacks clear empirical comparison to other state-of-the-art supervised learning methods.  
- Weakness: no detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the sweeping optimization, raising scalability concerns.  
- Overall: solid, novel contribution with strong results but would benefit from clearer comparisons and complexity analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong real-world results, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: high background assumption, missing benchmarks, and insufficient complexity analysis.
