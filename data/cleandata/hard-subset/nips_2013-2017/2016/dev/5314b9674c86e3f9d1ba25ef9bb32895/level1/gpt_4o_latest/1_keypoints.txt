- Paper applies tensor networks (MPS) to supervised learning, parameterizing non-linear kernel models with MPS.  
- Authors directly optimize the weight vector in MPS form, bypassing the kernel trick.  
- Method achieves strong MNIST performance (<1% test error).  
- Tensor networks avoid the curse of dimensionality via compressed high-order tensor representation.  
- Adaptive optimization of internal tensor dimensions offers model flexibility and potential regularization.  
- Approach is computationally efficient, scaling linearly with training set size.  
- Algorithm leverages physics methods (DMRG) and allows potential for parallel computation.  
- Paper provides interpretation of learned structure, aiding feature selection and model insight.  
- Work builds on prior tensor-network and tensor-decomposition research in physics and ML.  
- Manuscript is technically rigorous with clear derivations and a well-motivated sweeping optimization algorithm.  
- Presentation is clear, reproducible, includes tensor diagrams and public code.  
- The application of MPS to parameterize kernel models and interpret learned structure is original.  
- Results are significant given competitive performance with relatively low computational complexity.  
- Experimental scope is limited (only MNIST), leaving generalizability unproven.  
- Difficulty integrating stochastic gradient descent with the sweeping algorithm limits scalability.  
- Feature map choice is ad hoc and not systematically analyzed for its impact on performance.  
- Lacks direct empirical comparisons to state-of-the-art kernel methods and neural networks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, efficient approach with strong results and interpretability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited evaluation and optimization refinements needed for larger datasets.  
- Recommendation: accept, as the paper makes a significant and promising contribution despite areas for improvement.
