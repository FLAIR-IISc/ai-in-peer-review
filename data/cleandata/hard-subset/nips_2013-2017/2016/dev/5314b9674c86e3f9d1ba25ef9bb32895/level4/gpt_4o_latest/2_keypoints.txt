- Paper idea: Uses tensor-product local feature maps and matrix-product-state (MPS) parameterization to build classifiers, demonstrated on MNIST.

- Clarity/accessibility: Paper is hard to follow for ML readers; needs a dedicated background section on tensor networks, MPS algorithms, and relevant physics references.

- Notation problems: Notation is cumbersome and ambiguous (e.g., Eq.2 x vs xj and N; Eq.3 indexing of φ; undefined A in Eq.5; typos and inconsistent symbols).

- Experimental weaknesses: Evaluation is insufficient—only MNIST used and crucial details (e.g., train/test split, protocol) are missing; more datasets and thorough experimental reporting required.

- Feature-map selection: Choice of local feature map and hyperparameters (d, m) is not justified or generalised; guidance on selecting these in practice is lacking.

- MPS uniqueness: Paper does not discuss whether the MPS decomposition is unique or how non-uniqueness would affect training and final models.

- Novelty vs prior work: Prior work (e.g., [4]) already applies MPS for similar tasks; manuscript fails to clearly state its novel contributions relative to that work.

- Relation to kernels: Should explore connections between the proposed tensor-network function space and ANOVA/product kernels / RKHS constructions.
