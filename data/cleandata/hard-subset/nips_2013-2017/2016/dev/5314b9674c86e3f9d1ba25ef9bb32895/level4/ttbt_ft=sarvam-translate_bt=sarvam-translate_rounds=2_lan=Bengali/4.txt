The authors have addressed the problem of image classification using tensor networks and optimization algorithms for them. A brief theoretical evaluation of their method and its results on the MNIST dataset are provided. I have some issues with this paper. I would have liked to see a better presentation of this paper in the literature. For example, classification is one of the main applications of GLM (in this case, it is called logistic regression), and tensor methods have been used to optimize the mixture of GLMs [1]. The paper lacks depth. The authors have mainly proposed a new algorithm, where a strong argument has not been given to make the reader interested in the algorithm: classification or regression algorithms are usually compared in terms of their expressability (for example, dense in L2 - the authors have given an informal argument here as to why their model should be class dense) and the convergence rate; the minimax optimal rate for most regression/classification methods is known so far, and I expect some discussion on the statistical properties of the regressors. One might motivate the algorithm by beneficial computational runtime/complexity compared to other methods. But that has not been done here either. Finally, a strong empirical performance can be used as an inspiration, and the authors have provided some results on the MNIST dataset. But the empirical evaluation is completely trivial (a small section in the middle of page 6). So the paper seems to be just another heuristic for a well-known machine learning problem, and one wonders why anyone would bother.
