This paper proposes the use of the Matrix Product State Decomposition (MPS) of a tensor network for a general multiclass supervised classification problem. The paper is well-written, although it assumes that the reader is somewhat familiar with the concepts of MPS and tensor networks, techniques commonly used in the field of quantum physics. The proposed methodology is based on previous work (citations [3] and [4]), and the paper's contribution is incremental and similar in nature to the aforementioned work. The paper's main contribution can be summarized into two main parts: a) It defines a tensor-inspired encoding (kernel) that can be applied to any classification problem where the data is represented in vector form of a fixed length. b) It optimizes the MPS representation for the classification task using gradient descent, [4] in contrast to where the MPS representation is first chosen in an unsupervised manner and then later used in a different way for the classification task by applying a classification algorithm to the resulting transformed data. 1) The paper assumes some familiarity with tensor networks and MPS. This may be common in the field of quantum physics but not in the NIPS community (in my opinion). Therefore, the paper could be greatly improved by better familiarizing the reader with the concepts and building from the bottom up. This is not an easy task due to the amount of space available in the NIPS paper, but improving the paper's readability is still necessary. 2) Please add dimensions to the vectors and matrices. This will also improve readability. 3) Line 153 mentions that a zig-zag ordering is used, but figure 8 does not depict a zig-zag pattern. Is this a typo? Did you use the wrong figure? Please clarify. 4) Please explain in detail the difference between the work presented here and [3] and [4]. This information may be useful for a reader with little or no experience with tensor networks to follow the paper and understand the contribution of the work presented here. 5) The connection to neural networks is also very informative for this audience, although it is briefly mentioned at the end of the paper. Please elaborate on this topic. 6) Can you explain how initialization is done? How sensitive is the problem to local minima? How did you deal with this problem? 7) It would be helpful to mention which tools were used to calculate the SVD.
