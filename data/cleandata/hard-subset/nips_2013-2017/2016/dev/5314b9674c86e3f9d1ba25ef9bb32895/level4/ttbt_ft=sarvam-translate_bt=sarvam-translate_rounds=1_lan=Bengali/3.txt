The use of matrix product states in image classification. A very interesting and powerful aspect of this paper is that it beautifully connects various fields. It proposes the use of tensor networks with matrix product states (also known as tensor trains, depending on the specific research area) for image classification. On the other hand, the presentation could be improved and clarified in some areas: - Section 5 "Estimating weight tensors as MPS": It is not clear in what sense or how a specific tensor is being estimated. Did you mean that if we take all the singular values in equation (11), it will be consistent with the estimated tensor? - In section 7, the authors mention some unresolved issues about regularization. However, I think the answer already exists ( implicitly) in equations (11) and (14): Equation (14) is closely related to the work of Signoretto M., Tran D.Q., De Laathouwer L., Suens J.A.K., Learning with Tensors: A Framework Based on Convex Optimization and Spectral Regularization, Machine Learning, Vol. 94, No. 3, March 2014, pp. 303-351, where regularization is applied to learn a tensor model, which is related to multilinear SVM, nuclear norm regularization, and singular value thresholding operations. Since only m largest singular values are kept in equation (11), it is also affecting the rank of the tensor. In other words, the authors did not add regularization to equation (6), but rather controlled the rank through the SVM transceiver operation in (11). - [15] also explains how to solve a linear system using Cichocki MPS. In fact, equations (6)(7) are also linear within the unknown tensor. So it is not clear why the method proposed in [15] cannot be directly applied here. What is different from [15] or what is creating additional problems in this paper? - Please explain in more detail how \tilde{\Phi}_n is obtained from the given data. - Equations (7)-(8): Explain what you mean by "leading-order update". - I would suggest that an algorithm should also be added to the paper to step-by-step clarify what to do from the given training data to finally get the solution B. - Equation (3): Are these not Fourier features? (Note: Random Fourier features are currently used even in large-scale problems, which are not limited to images only)
