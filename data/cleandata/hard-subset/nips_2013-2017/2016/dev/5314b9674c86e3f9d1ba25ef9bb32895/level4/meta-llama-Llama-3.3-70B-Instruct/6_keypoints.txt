- Proposes a novel image classification algorithm using tensor networks (matrix product states) to optimize supervised learning.
- Maps input vectors X to a high-dimensional feature space via Φ(x) and classifies with f(x) = W · Φ(x).
- Addresses the exponential/infinite size of Φ(x) and W by approximating W with a tensor network instead of using the kernel trick (Eq. 5).
- Introduces a "sweeping" optimization algorithm (Eq. 7) that leverages W’s structure to minimize a quadratic classification cost.
- Effectively adapts tensor-network optimization techniques to supervised learning tasks.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.
- Experimental validation is limited to MNIST with under 1% test error.
- Major limitation: lack of evaluations on additional benchmark datasets and comparisons with state-of-the-art learning and feature-selection methods.
- Reviewer recommendation: weak accept, requests clarifications during rebuttal.
