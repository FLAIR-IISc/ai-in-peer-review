This paper develops an efficient algorithm for classifying images. The authors demonstrate a method for optimizing a tensor network optimization algorithm for supervised learning tasks using matrix product states. The authors first mapped a very large vector X into a feature map Φ(x) in a high-dimensional space, and then used a decision function f(x) = W × Φ(x) to classify these vectors. Because the feature vector Φ(x) and weight vector W can rapidly become large or even infinite, the authors propose a new approach that differs from kernel tricks (reference 9). This approach uses a tensor network to estimate the optimal weight vector W (equation 5) by extracting hidden information within the trained model and leveraging the structure of W, developing a "sweeping" optimization algorithm (equation 7) that minimizes the quadratic cost function defining the classification task. This approach uses a tensor network to estimate the optimal weight vector W (equation 5) by extracting hidden information within the trained model and leveraging the structure of W, developing a "sweeping" optimization algorithm (equation 7) that minimizes the quadratic cost function defining the classification task. Overall, I think this paper is well-organized and clearly written; the proposed approach is novel and makes a significant contribution to the field of machine learning. It is good to establish the relationship between tensor networks and feature selection. However, a major problem with this paper is the extent of experimentation. To demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, the authors use a single dataset, i.e., MINST, and achieve a test set classification error of less than 1%. It would be better if the authors could apply the proposed approach to other datasets widely used in this field and show the results. More importantly, there is a lack of comparison with state-of-the-art learning algorithms and feature selection methods, making it difficult to assess the performance of the proposed approach. I hope the authors can clarify these questions in the rebuttal process. Overall, my opinion of this paper is weak.
