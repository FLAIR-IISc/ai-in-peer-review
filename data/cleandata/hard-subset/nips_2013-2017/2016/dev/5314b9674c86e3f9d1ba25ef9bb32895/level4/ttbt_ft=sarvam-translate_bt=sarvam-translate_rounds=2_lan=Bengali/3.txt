The use of the matrix product state in image classification. A very interesting and powerful aspect of this paper is that it beautifully connects various fields. It proposes the use of tensor networks with the matrix product state for image classification (also known as tensor trains, depending on the specific research area). On the other hand, the presentation could be improved and clarified in some areas: - Section 5 "Estimating weight tensors as MPS": It is not clear in what sense or how a specific tensor is being estimated. Did you mean that if we take all the unit values of equation (11), it will be consistent with the tensor being estimated? - In section 7, the authors mention some unresolved issues about regularization. However, I think the answer already exists (indirectly) in equations (11) and (14): Equation (14) is closely related to the work of Signoretto M, Tran D.Q., De Laathouwer L, Suens J.A.K., Learning with Tensors: Convex Optimization and Spectral Regularization, Machine Learning, Vol. 94, No. 3, March 2014, pp. 303-351, where regularization is applied to learn a tensor model, which is related to multilinear SVM, nuclear norm regularization, and unit value thresholding operations. Since only m largest unit values are kept in equation (11), it is also affecting the rank of the tensor. In other words, the authors did not add regularization to equation (6), but rather controlled the rank through the SVM transceiver operation in (11). - [15] Also explains how to solve a linear system using a neural network. In fact, equations (6)(7) are linear even in the unknown tensor. So it is not clear why the method proposed in [15] cannot be directly applied here. What is different from [15] or creating additional problems in this paper? - Explain in more detail how to obtain \tilde{\Phi}_n from the given data. - Equations (7)-(8): Explain what you mean by "leading-order update". - I would suggest that an algorithm should also be added to the paper to clarify step-by-step what to do from the given training data so that the final solution B can be obtained. - Equation (3): Are these not Fourier features? (Note: Random Fourier features are currently used even in large-scale problems, which are not limited to images only)
