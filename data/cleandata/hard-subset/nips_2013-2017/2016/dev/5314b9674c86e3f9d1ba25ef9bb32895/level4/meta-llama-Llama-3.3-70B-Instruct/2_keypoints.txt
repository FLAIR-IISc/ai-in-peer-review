- Manuscript applies tensor networks/MPS to supervised learning by building tensor-product feature maps, representing parameters as an MPS, providing an optimization algorithm, and validating on MNIST.  
- The paper is intriguing but not ready for publication due to several critical issues.  
- Hard to follow for ML readers; needs a dedicated background section explaining tensor networks, MPS algorithms, and relevant physics context.  
- Notation is dense and confusing (unclear relations of x vs xj, meaning of N, odd indexing of φ by sj, undefined A, and typographical errors).  
- Experimental validation is weak—only MNIST used and essential details (train/test split, etc.) are missing.  
- Criteria for choosing the local feature map and hyperparameters (d, m) are unspecified and unclear for non-image data.  
- Unclear whether the MPS decomposition is unique and how non-uniqueness would affect learning.  
- Novelty over prior work (e.g., [4]) is not clearly articulated; differences must be explicitly stated.  
- Suggest investigating connections between the tensor-network representation and ANOVA/product-kernel function spaces.
