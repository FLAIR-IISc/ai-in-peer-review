- Presents an efficient image classification algorithm adapting tensor network optimization (matrix product states) to supervised learning.  
- Maps inputs X to a much higher-dimensional feature space Φ(x) and uses decision function f(x)=W×Φ(x).  
- Notes Φ(x) and W can grow exponentially or be infinite, so authors avoid the kernel trick.  
- Proposes approximating the optimal weight vector W with a tensor network (Eq. 5).  
- Develops a "sweeping" optimization algorithm (Eq. 7) to minimize a quadratic cost by exploiting W’s structure.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and offers an innovative connection between tensor networks and feature selection.  
- Major limitation: experiments use only MNIST, despite reporting <1% test error.  
- Lacks comparison to state-of-the-art learning algorithms and feature selection methods, making relative performance unclear.  
- Reviewer recommendation: weak accept; encourages authors to address dataset breadth and comparison concerns in rebuttal.
