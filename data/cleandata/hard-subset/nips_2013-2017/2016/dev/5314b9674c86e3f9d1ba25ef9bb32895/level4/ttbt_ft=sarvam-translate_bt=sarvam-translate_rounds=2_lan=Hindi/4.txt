The authors address the problem of image classification using a tensor network and optimization algorithms for these problems. Their methods and results on the MNIST dataset are provided with minimal theoretical evaluation. I have some issues with this paper. I would prefer to see a better embedding of this paper in the literature. For example, classification is one of the main applications of GLMs (in this case called logistic regression), and tensor methods have been used to optimize the mixture of GLMs [1]. The paper lacks depth. The authors essentially propose a new algorithm without giving strong arguments for the reader to be interested in the algorithm: classification or regression algorithms are usually compared in terms of their expressive power (e.g., density in L2 - the authors give an informal argument here as to why their model should be dense in class) and convergence rates; minimax optimal rates for most regression/classification methods are known by now, and I expect some discussion on the statistical properties of an estimator. The algorithm may also be motivated by beneficial computational runtime/complexity compared to other approaches. But this is not done here either. Finally, a strong empirical performance can be used as motivation, and the authors provide some results on the MNIST dataset. But the empirical evaluation is very low overall (a small section in the middle of page 6). Therefore, the paper leaves a well-understood machine learning problem with another approximate method, and one wonders why one should bother.
