This paper examines tensor networks derived from quantum physics to provide a supervised learning algorithm. A major drawback of this work is that it is not motivated, in the sense that tensor networks are used for the definition of feature maps. It is clear that the use of tensor networks gives rise to many difficulties in the optimization process. We advise the authors to study the potential use of kernel tricks, which can reduce computational complexity, as it has already been extensively tested in kernel machines, such as support vector machines. Within the proposed classification model given in Section 3, it is difficult to understand the issue of labels, namely how the label index is arbitrarily placed on a position of the tensor, and how/why it can be moved to another tensor. This can be seen as affecting the label on a single pixel of the image, not the entire image. This is due to the model under consideration (4)-(5), which does not seem suitable for multiclass classification. The authors used a quadratic loss in (6), which was motivated by obtaining the best test results. They should also mention other loss functions used in comparison. It is noteworthy that the quadratic loss provides a relatively easy calculation, unlike some other loss functions, due to its derivation. There are several issues regarding the experiments: -Many details are missing, such as the number of data used in the experiments, i.e., N, and the number of classes, i.e., L. -To reduce training time, 28x28 images were sub-sampled to 14x14. However, nothing has been said about the computational complexity of the algorithm. This is a major issue to be addressed in the paper. -It is not clear how training and test errors are estimated. -The proposed classification algorithm has not been compared to any state-of-the-art classification methods. It should be compared in terms of estimated errors as well as computational complexity.
