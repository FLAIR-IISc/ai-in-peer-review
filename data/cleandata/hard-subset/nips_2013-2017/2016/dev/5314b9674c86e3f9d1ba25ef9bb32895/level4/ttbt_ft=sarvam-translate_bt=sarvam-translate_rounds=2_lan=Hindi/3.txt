The use of matrix product states in image classification. A very interesting and strong aspect of this paper is that it connects different fields well. It proposes the use of tensor networks with matrix product states (also called tensor trains based on specific research area) for image classification. On the other hand, the presentation could be improved and clarified at a few points: - Section 5 "To estimate the weight tensor as MPS": It is not clear in what sense or how a certain tensor is estimated. Does it correspond to the tensor to be estimated if we assume all singular values in (11)? - In Section 7, the authors mention some open problems about regularization. However, I think the answer is already (indirectly) present in equations (11) and (14): Equation (14) is closely related to the work of Signeto M., Tran Dinh Q., De Laathuyer L., Suenken J.A.K., Learning with Tensor: A Framework Based on Convex Optimization and Spectral Regularization, Machine Learning, Vol. 94, No. 3, March 2014, pp. 303-351, where regularization is applied to the learning of a tensor model, which is related to multivariate SVD, nuclear norm regularization, and singular value thresholding operations. Because only m largest singular values are kept in equation (11), it is also affecting the rank of the tensor. In other words, the authors do not add regularization to equation (6), but instead control the rank by SVD truncation operations in (11). - [15] It is also explained in Cichocki how to solve a linear system using MPS. In fact, equations (6)(7) are linear even in the unknown tensor. Therefore, it is not clear why the method proposed in [15] cannot be directly applied here. What is different from [15] or what is making the problem more complex in this paper? - Please explain in more detail how \tilde{\Phi}_n is obtained from the given data. - Equations (7)-(8): Please explain what you mean by "leading-order update". - I would also suggest adding an algorithm to the paper so that it becomes clear what needs to be done to start from the given training data and finally obtain the solution B. - Equation (3): Are these not Fourier features? (Note: Random Fourier features are currently used even in large-scale problems, which are not limited to images)
