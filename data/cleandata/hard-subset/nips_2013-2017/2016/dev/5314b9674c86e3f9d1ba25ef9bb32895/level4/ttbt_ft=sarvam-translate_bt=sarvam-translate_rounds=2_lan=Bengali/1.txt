This paper proposes the use of Matrix Product States Decomposition (MPS) of Tensor Networks for classification problems under a general supervised multi-class setting. The paper is well-written, but assumes that the reader has some understanding of MPS and Tensor Networks, which are widely used techniques in the field of quantum physics. The proposed method is based on previous work (citations [3] and [4]) and the contribution of this paper is incremental and similar to previous work. The main contribution of this paper can be summarized in two parts: a) It defines a Tensor Inspired Encoding (kernel) that can be used for any classification problem where the data is represented in the form of vectors of a specific length. b) It optimizes the MPS representation for hand classification tasks using gradient descent [4]. In contrast to MPS representation, where the MPS representation is first selected in an unsupervised manner and then applied to the transformed data using a classification algorithm in a discrete manner. 1) This paper assumes that the reader has some understanding of Tensor Networks and MPS. This may be normal in the field of quantum physics, but not so much in the NIPS community (in my opinion). Therefore, this paper can be further improved by introducing the reader to the concepts more thoroughly and building from the bottom up. This is not an easy task due to the amount of space available in the NIPS paper, but the readability of the paper still needs to be improved. 2) Add dimensions to the vectors and matrices. This will help improve readability. 3) Line 153 mentions that a zig-zag ordering is used, but Figure 8 does not show a zig-zag pattern. Is this a typo? Did you use the wrong figure? Please clarify. 4) Explain in detail the difference between the work presented here and [3] and [4], as this information may be helpful for the reader with little or no experience with Tensor Networks to follow the paper and understand the contribution of the work presented here. 5) Also, the connection with neural networks is very informative for these readers, but it is briefly mentioned at the end of the paper. Please discuss this in detail. 6) Can you explain how the initialization is done? How sensitive is the problem to local minima? How did you solve this problem? 7) It would be helpful to mention which tool was used to calculate the SVD.
