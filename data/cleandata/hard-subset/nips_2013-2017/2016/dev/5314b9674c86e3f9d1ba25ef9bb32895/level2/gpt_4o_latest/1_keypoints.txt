- Paper applies matrix product states (MPS) tensor networks to supervised learning, parameterizing non-linear kernel models.  
- Achieves strong empirical performance on MNIST (test error 0.97% with bond dimension 120).  
- Claims computational efficiency: algorithm scales linearly with training set size.  
- Highlights adaptability: internal MPS dimensions can focus on relevant data correlations.  
- Discusses interpretability and implicit feature selection enabled by tensor network structure.  
- Code is publicly available, supporting reproducibility.  
- Experiments are limited to MNIST, restricting evidence of general applicability.  
- Optimization is challenging; blending SGD with MPS sweeping was unsuccessful.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of regularization effects and broader generalization behavior.  
- Empirical comparison with related and state-of-the-art methods (e.g., Novikov et al.) is insufficient.  
- Suggests extending experiments to more diverse datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, ImageNet).  
- Recommends investigating advanced optimization techniques (adaptive learning rates, momentum).  
- Advises providing more detailed comparisons with tensor-based and kernel methods.  
- Proposes exploring different feature maps and tensor network structures (PEPS, MERA).  
- Overall recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing limitations and expanding experimental evaluation.
