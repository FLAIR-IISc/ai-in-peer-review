- Proposes a novel supervised learning method using tensor networks (matrix product states) to parameterize non-linear kernel models.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on MNIST, reporting test error <1%.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of the tensor network framework and its ML application.  
- Main claim: tensor networks can efficiently optimize the weight vector and yield strong performance on some tasks.  
- Experimental MNIST results are used to support the main claim.  
- Method is practically useful for high-dimensional data, offering potentially more efficient and scalable learning.  
- Authors show good knowledge of relevant literature and motivation for using tensor networks.  
- Novelty lies in applying tensor networks specifically to supervised learning tasks.  
- Paper is complete and provides sufficient experimental and implementation details for reproducibility.  
- Limitation: lacks a thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art methods on MNIST (e.g., CNNs, SVMs).  
- Limitation: does not clearly quantify limitations such as computational cost of optimizing the tensor network.  
- Suggestion: additional experiments on other datasets needed to demonstrate generality.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong MNIST results, clear exposition, and potential efficiency/scalability benefits.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient SOTA comparisons, unclear limitations/costs, and limited cross-dataset validation.
