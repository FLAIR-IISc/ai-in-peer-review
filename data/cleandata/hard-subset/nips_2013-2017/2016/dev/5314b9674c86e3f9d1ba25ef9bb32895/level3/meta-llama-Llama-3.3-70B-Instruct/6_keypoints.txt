- Proposes Multimodal Residual Networks (MRN) extending deep residual learning to multimodal VQA.  
- MRN learns joint vision-language representations using element-wise multiplication and residual mappings.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the Visual QA dataset for Open-Ended and Multiple-Choice tasks.  
- Introduces a novel visualization method for attention effects of joint residual mappings.  
- Uses element-wise multiplication as an implicit attention mechanism, distinct from traditional attentional models.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Experiments are limited to the Visual QA dataset.  
- Lacks comparisons with other state-of-the-art learning algorithms and feature selection methods.  
- Results analysis is insufficient, lacking breakdowns by question/image types.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical performance, and insightful visualizations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments, missing baselines/comparisons, and shallow analysis.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10; Clarity 9/10; Originality 9/10; Significance 8.5/10.  
- Recommendation: address experimental limitations and provide more detailed comparative and per-question/image analyses.
