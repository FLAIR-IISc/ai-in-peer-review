- Proposes Multimodal Residual Networks (MRN) applying deep residual learning to multimodal VQA.  
- Introduces a novel visualization method using back-propagation to show attention effects of joint residual mappings.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the Visual QA dataset for Open-Ended and Multiple-Choice tasks.  
- Implicitly models attention without introducing explicit attention parameters.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured, making the approach easy to follow.  
- Strength: innovative multimodal learning approach and a new visualization technique.  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation on Visual QA (reported SOTA performance).  
- Weakness: lacks rigorous theoretical analysis and deeper embedding in existing literature (e.g., tensor networks, optimization).  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation reportedly limited to MNIST in places and needs broader, more comprehensive comparisons.  
- Weakness: some terms and notation are unclear or undefined (e.g., “exponentially large,” “delta^ellLn”).  
- Needs clearer definitions, more precise notation, and extensive comparison with other methods.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to add theoretical analysis, expand empirical evaluation, and clarify notation.
