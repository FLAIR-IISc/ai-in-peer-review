- Proposes Multimodal Residual Networks (MRN), extending deep residual learning to visual question answering.  
- Claims MRN effectively learns joint vision-language representations and achieves state-of-the-art on Visual QA.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with a good introduction and thorough related work.  
- Significant technical contributions: detailed architecture, joint residual function, and visualization method.  
- Experimental results reported as impressive for both Open-Ended and Multiple-Choice tasks.  
- Lacks clear motivation for using tensor networks to define the feature map.  
- Tensor-network formulation creates optimization difficulties; authors should consider the kernel trick to reduce complexity.  
- Proposed classification model has label indexing/placement issues, making it unsuitable for multiclass classification.  
- Experiments miss important details (data size, number of classes) and lack comparisons with state-of-the-art classification methods.  
- Choice of quadratic loss is justified by ease, but other loss functions used in the field should be compared.  
- Overall technically sound, original, and significant; advances the state of the art in multimodal learning.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address tensor-network motivation/optimization, classification labeling, and experimental detail/comparisons.
