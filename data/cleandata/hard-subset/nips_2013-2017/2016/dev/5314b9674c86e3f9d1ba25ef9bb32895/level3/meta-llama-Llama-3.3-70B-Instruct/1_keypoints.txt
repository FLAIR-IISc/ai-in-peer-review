- Proposes Multimodal Residual Networks (MRN), extending deep residual learning to multimodal VQA tasks.  
- Introduces a three-block layered MRN that achieves state-of-the-art results on Visual QA for both Open-Ended and Multiple-Choice tasks.  
- Presents a novel visualization method using back-propagation on collapsed visual features to interpret spatial attention effects.  
- Strength: effectively learns joint representations from vision and language.  
- Strength: achieves strong empirical performance on a challenging dataset.  
- Strength: visualization method is a significant contribution to model interpretability.  
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with residual learning and attention models; needs a better introductory explanation.  
- Weakness: paper omits dimensions for vectors and matrices, reducing readability.  
- Weakness: contains a potential typo or incorrect figure reference regarding zig-zag ordering.  
- Quality: overall good and complete, with well-supported claims.  
- Clarity: fair — some sections are clear while others require more effort to understand.  
- Originality: high, combining familiar techniques in a novel way.  
- Significance: high, with important results and potential impact on multimodal learning.  
- Recommendation: accept, provided authors address clarity and formatting issues.
