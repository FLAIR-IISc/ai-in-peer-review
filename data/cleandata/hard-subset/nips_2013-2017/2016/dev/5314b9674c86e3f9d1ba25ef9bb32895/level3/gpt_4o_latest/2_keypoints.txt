- Paper applies matrix-product state (MPS) tensor networks to supervised learning, demonstrating <1% test error on MNIST (bond dim 120) and discussing representation power, feature selection, and interpretability.

Strengths:
- Novel cross-disciplinary approach: brings tensor network techniques from physics into ML.
- Efficient optimization: training scales linearly with dataset size, improving over classical kernel methods.
- Interpretability: offers insights into implicit feature selection and model structure.
- Reproducibility: authors provide publicly available code.

Weaknesses:
- Assumes prior knowledge of tensor networks/MPS, making it inaccessible to many ML readers.
- Dense and ambiguous notation with unclear equations and sweeping algorithm description.
- Limited experiments: only MNIST, unclear data splitting, and no diverse benchmarks to test generality.
- Poorly justified hyperparameters (local feature maps, bond dimensions) with limited guidance for other modalities.
- Unclear novelty and insufficient comparison to prior work (e.g., [4]) and related kernels.
- No discussion of the impact of MPS non-uniqueness on learning and generalization.

Recommendations:
- Add an accessible introduction to tensor networks for ML audiences.
- Clarify notation, equations, and the sweeping optimization with diagrams and stepwise explanation.
- Expand experimental validation to diverse datasets and detail data-splitting procedures.
- Justify hyperparameter choices and discuss applicability across data modalities.
- Address MPS non-uniqueness and more clearly differentiate contributions from prior work.

Pro/Con summary:
- Pros: novel approach, computational efficiency, interpretability, reproducible code.
- Cons: limited accessibility, weak experimental validation, unclear novelty, insufficient hyperparameter explanation.

Final decision:
- Weak Reject.
