- Proposes Multimodal Residual Networks (MRN) that extend deep residual learning to multimodal inputs for VQA.  
- Reports state-of-the-art results on the Visual QA dataset for both Open-Ended and Multiple-Choice tasks.  
- Introduces a novel back-propagation-based visualization method to show attention effects of joint residual mappings.  
- Novel idea of applying tensor networks to machine learning is interesting and promising.  
- Readability is poor due to insufficient explanation of tensor networks and MPS decomposition for an ML audience.  
- Heavy, confusing notation with unclear relationships between variables and many undefined terms.  
- Experiments are weak: evaluation on a single dataset and missing important experimental-setting details.  
- Choice of local feature map is critical but not clearly justified or guided, including parameter selection.  
- Unclear how uniqueness of MPS decomposition affects learning; theoretical implications are not addressed.  
- Paper does not sufficiently distinguish itself from existing work or compare against other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Overall significance limited by weak experiments, lack of comparative analysis, and unclear exposition.  
- Recommended improvements: explain tensor networks/MPS clearly, simplify notation, provide full experimental details, run more extensive comparisons and analyses.  
- Suggest exploring connections between ANOVA kernels and tensor network representations as a potential research direction.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel tensor-network idea, SOTA empirical results, and new visualization technique.  
- Rejection arguments: poor clarity/readability, limited and weak experiments, insufficient evidence, and weak differentiation from prior work.
