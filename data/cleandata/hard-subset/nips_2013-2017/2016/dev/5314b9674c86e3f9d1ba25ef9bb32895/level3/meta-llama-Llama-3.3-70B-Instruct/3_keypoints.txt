- Proposes Multimodal Residual Networks (MRN) extending deep residual learning to multimodal (vision+language) inputs.  
- Learns joint representations via element-wise multiplication, addressing attention-network bottlenecks.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized with thorough background and related work.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on Visual QA for both Open-Ended and Multiple-Choice tasks.  
- Introduces a novel high-resolution visualization method for the attention effect of joint residual mappings.  
- Uses element-wise multiplication as an implicit and innovative attention mechanism.  
- Sections 5 and 7 need clearer presentation, particularly on tensor approximation and open regularization problems.  
- Authors control tensor rank via SVD truncation, but must explain how this implicitly provides regularization.  
- Reviewer questions why method in [15] cannot be directly applied to solve the linear system and asks what differentiates this problem.  
- More detail needed on how \tilde{\Phi}_n is obtained from the data.  
- Require explanation of the "leading-order update" in equations 7–8.  
- Suggest adding an explicit algorithm to outline the step-by-step process for obtaining solution B from training data.  
- Note that features in equation 3 resemble Fourier features used in large-scale problems; recommend exploring this connection.  
- Overall: strong, novel approach with potential impact, but requires clarification and additional technical details before acceptance.  
- Pros: novelty, SOTA results, novel visualization, potential impact on multimodal learning.  
- Cons: unclear tensor/regularization treatment, insufficient differentiation from prior work [15], and several presentation gaps.
