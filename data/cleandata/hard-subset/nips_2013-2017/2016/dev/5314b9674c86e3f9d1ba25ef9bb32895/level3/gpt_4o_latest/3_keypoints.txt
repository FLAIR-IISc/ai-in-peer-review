- Summary: Introduces using matrix product states (MPS) from tensor networks for image classification, achieving <1% MNIST error with small bond dimensions and offering computational efficiency and interpretability.  
- Strength: Interdisciplinary contribution combining tensor networks and machine learning, opening new research directions.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance—sub-1% test error on MNIST with small MPS bond dimensions.  
- Strength: Computational efficiency—training scales linearly with dataset size, better than kernel methods.  
- Strength: Improved interpretability via MPS structure, aiding feature selection and model understanding.  
- Strength: Reproducibility enhanced by publicly available code.  
- Weakness: Presentation unclear on approximating the weight tensor as an MPS and on deriving \tilde{\Phi}_n; "leading-order update" in equations (7)-(8) needs clarification.  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of relationship to prior work, specifically the approach in [15] (Cichocki).  
- Weakness: Superficial discussion of regularization benefits; lacks connection to spectral regularization and SVD truncation literature.  
- Weakness: No step-by-step algorithm provided, reducing usability for practitioners.  
- Weakness: Equation (3) suggests Fourier features but the link to random Fourier features and large-scale kernel methods is not explored.  
- Suggestion: Improve clarity of technical sections (MPS approximation and gradient updates) with diagrams or pseudocode.  
- Suggestion: Provide a detailed comparison with [15] and related works to situate contributions.  
- Suggestion: Expand discussion on regularization and explicitly link to prior spectral methods research.  
- Suggestion: Include a step-by-step algorithm to aid reproducibility and practitioner use.  
- Suggestion: Discuss connections between the proposed feature map and random Fourier features to relate to kernel literature.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and strengthen connections to prior work.
