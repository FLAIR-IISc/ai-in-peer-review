- Novelty: Innovative application of tensor networks (MPS) from quantum physics to supervised learning by approximating weight tensors.
- Efficiency: Algorithm reportedly scales linearly with training set size, offering potential advantages over traditional kernel methods.
- Interpretability: Tensor network structure provides insights into representational power and implicit feature selection.
- Empirical performance: Achieves <1% test error on MNIST (with downsampled 14x14 images) using relatively small MPS bond dimensions.
- Feature map motivation lacking: Choice of quantum-inspired feature map is ad hoc and not well-justified or compared to alternatives.
- Optimization concerns: Sweeping optimization is described but potential convergence issues and hyperparameter sensitivities are not addressed.
- Computational complexity unclear: Downsampling noted but overall computational and scalability analysis is insufficient.
- Label placement questionable: The placement of the label index in the MPS is not well-justified and may be inappropriate for multiclass tasks.
- Limited benchmarking: No comparison to state-of-the-art classification methods in error rates or computational efficiency.
- Missing experimental details: Key dataset details (N, L) and procedures for estimating training/test errors are absent.
- Loss function choice: Use of quadratic loss is chosen for simplicity without comparison to standard classification losses like cross-entropy.
- Acceptance arguments: Novel approach, competitive MNIST results, efficient scaling, and interpretability potential.
- Rejection arguments: Insufficient justification for key design choices, missing comparisons, unclear experimental and complexity reporting.
- Recommendation: Major revision required to address motivation, label placement, experimental details, comparisons, and complexity analysis.
