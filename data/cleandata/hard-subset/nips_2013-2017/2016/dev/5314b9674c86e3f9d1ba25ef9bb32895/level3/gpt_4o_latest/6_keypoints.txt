- Proposes using matrix product states (MPS) to parameterize non-linear kernel learning models, avoiding the traditional kernel trick.  
- Introduces a "sweeping" optimization algorithm inspired by DMRG to minimize a quadratic cost function for the MPS parameters.  
- Validated on MNIST, achieving under 1% test error with bond dimension 120.  
- Provides analysis of representational power and implicit feature selection enabled by the tensor network structure.  
- Technically sound and establishes a novel connection between tensor networks and machine learning.  
- Sweeping algorithm is well-motivated and scales linearly with training set size, addressing kernel method scalability.  
- Methodology is clearly explained with helpful tensor diagrams, enhancing clarity and interpretability.  
- Experimental results show competitive accuracy with relatively small MPS bond dimensions.  
- Evaluation is limited to MNIST, so generalizability to more diverse datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, ImageNet) is unproven.  
- Lacks direct comparisons with state-of-the-art algorithms (deep nets, other feature selection methods).  
- Experiments omit standard optimization techniques (mini-batches, adaptive learning rates), which may reduce practical applicability.  
- Recommendation: weak accept—original and rigorous but needs broader experiments and comparisons to strengthen impact.
