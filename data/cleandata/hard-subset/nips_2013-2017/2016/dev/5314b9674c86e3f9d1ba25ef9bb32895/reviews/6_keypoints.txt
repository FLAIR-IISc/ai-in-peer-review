- Proposes an efficient image classification algorithm by adapting tensor-network (matrix product state) optimization to supervised learning.  
- Maps input vectors x into a high-dimensional feature space Φ(x) and uses decision function f(x)=W·Φ(x).  
- Notes Φ(x) and W can be exponentially large or infinite, so authors propose an alternative to the kernel trick to handle this.  
- Uses a tensor-network approximation of the weight vector W (Eq.5) to capture structure and hidden information.  
- Develops a "sweeping" optimization algorithm (Eq.7) to minimize a quadratic classification cost.  
- Paper is well organized, clearly written, novel, and connects tensor networks with feature selection.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: only tested on a single dataset (MNIST) despite achieving <1% test error.  
- Lacks comparisons with state-of-the-art learning algorithms and feature selection methods, hindering performance assessment.  
- Reviewer requests clarification in the rebuttal and gives an overall recommendation of weak accept.
