- Paper proposes using tensor networks (MPS) for supervised learning, presenting an optimization algorithm, MNIST experiments, and a discussion of the function class.  
- Idea is interesting but the manuscript has several major issues that prevent publication in its current form.  
- Readability: provide more background on tensor networks, MPS algorithms, and relevant physics literature for an ML audience (suggest a dedicated section).  
- Notation: notation is heavy and unclear (e.g., relation between x and x_j, definition of N, φ indexing, undefined A in Eq.5) and contains typos/phrasing errors.  
- Experiments: evaluation is weak—only MNIST used; need more datasets and full experimental details (e.g., train/test splits).  
- Feature-map and hyperparameters: choice of local feature map and parameters (d, m) is unclear and appears tailored to images; need guidance on how to choose them more generally.  
- MPS uniqueness: unclear whether MPS decomposition is unique and how non-uniqueness would affect learning.  
- Novelty: prior work (ref. [4]) used MPS for feature extraction/classification; authors must clearly state novel contributions and differences (current discussion too brief).  
- Relation to kernels: recommend studying the connection between ANOVA/product kernels and the proposed tensor-network function spaces.
