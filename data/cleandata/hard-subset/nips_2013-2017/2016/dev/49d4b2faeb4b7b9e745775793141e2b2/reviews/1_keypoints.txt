- Main contribution: hierarchical VAE architecture (MatNets) with deterministic connections between latents and observation to enable incremental generation.  
- Architecture consists of bottom-up, top-down, and merge modules directing information flow.  
- MatNets resemble DRAW and Probabilistic Ladder Networks (PLNs); the claimed novelty is deterministic connections in the generative hierarchical model.  
- Experimental results: competitive or state-of-the-art density modeling on MNIST, Omniglot, and CIFAR.  
- Key weakness: no experiments or ablations demonstrating the importance of deterministic connections in the inference or generative model.  
- Paper therefore shows strong performance but lacks direct evidence supporting its central claim.  
- Presentation issues: probabilistic/high-level model should be presented before procedural computation details for clarity.  
- Related work/citation gaps: fails to cite PLNs and DRAW, and omits prior use of convolutional GRUs in generative models (e.g., [24]).  
- Unclear whether the inference regularization from Section 2.3 was used in any experiments.  
- Minor errors/typos: Equations 11 and 12 should include log p(x|z).
