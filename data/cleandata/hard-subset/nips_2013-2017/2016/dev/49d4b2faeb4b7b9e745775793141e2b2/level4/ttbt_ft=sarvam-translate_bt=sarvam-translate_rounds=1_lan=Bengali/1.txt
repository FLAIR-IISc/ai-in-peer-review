The success of models like DRAW stems from their incremental approach to data generation and their connection to the deterministic connections between each latent variable and observation. For this reason, the authors apply the same design principles to hierarchical latent variable models. The resulting models are called Matryoshka Networks (MatNets). These networks consist of a bottom-up (BU) network, a top-down network (TD), and a merge module that directs the flow of information between the two. The MatNet architecture is reminiscent of the Probabilistic Ladder Network (PLN) [28]. Here, the inference model from observations includes a deterministic path in the latent layers and a top-down factorization of the variational posterior. The main difference is the deterministic connection between the latent variable and the observation in the Matryoshka network, which is absent in the PLN. MatNets have been evaluated through density modeling in MNIST, Omniglot, and CIFAR, and have yielded highly competitive or state-of-the-art results. This is an interesting and well-conducted paper. The main contribution of this paper is a hierarchical VAE architecture with deterministic connections in inference and generative models, which implements the incremental generation of observations. A similar approach has been followed in DRAW, but the latent variable layers in models like DRAW do not create any hierarchy (and are all essentially at the same level). The Prob Ladder Net has all the proposed features except the deterministic connection of the generative model. Therefore, the main new contribution here is the introduction of deterministic connections in a generative hierarchical model. Surprisingly, there are no experiments in the experimental section demonstrating the importance of deterministic connections in inference models and/or generative models. Instead, the experiments show that the proposed architecture performs very well on several datasets, which, while interesting, does not directly prove the main claim of the paper. The paper is well-written, but the presentation of the proposed architecture is not clear enough to be easily understood. It would have been much easier to read if a high-level (possible) description of the model had been given before specifying the correct calculations involved (such as the procedural description in section 2.1). In some places, the lack of citations may make some statements seem like novelty claims. For example, the discussion of using convolutional GRU modules failed to mention its prior use in generative models [24]. Due to the many similarities between DRAW and PLN, it is surprising that neither is mentioned in the relevant work section. Although the relationship between PLN and DRAW is well explained in the introduction, there is no mention of PLN in the paper, which the authors should correct.
