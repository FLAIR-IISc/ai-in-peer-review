The success of models like DRAW stems from its evolving data generation methods and their connection to the decisive link between each latent variable and observation. For this reason, the authors apply the same design principles to hierarchical latent variable models. The resulting models are called Matryoshka Networks (MatNets). These networks consist of a bottom-up (BU) network, a top-down network (TD), and a merge module that indicates the flow of information between the two. The MatNet architecture is reminiscent of the Probabilistic Ladder Network (PLN) [28]. Here, the model's inference from observations includes a decisive path in the latent layers and a top-down factorization of the variational posterior. The main difference is the decisive link between the latent variable and the observation in the Matryoshka network, which is absent in the PLN. MatNets have been evaluated through density modeling in MNIST, Omniglot, and CIFAR, and have yielded highly competitive or state-of-the-art results. It is an interesting and well-conducted paper. The main contribution of this paper is a hierarchical VAE architecture with decisive links in inference and generative models, which implements an increasing generation of observations. A similar approach has been followed in DRAW, but in models like DRAW, the latent variable layers do not form any hierarchy (and all are essentially in the same layer). Prob Ladder Net has all the proposed features except the decisive link in the generative model. Therefore, the main new contribution here is the introduction of decisive links in a generative hierarchical model. Surprisingly, there are no experiments in the experimental section demonstrating the importance of decisive links in inference models and/or generative models. Instead, the experiments show that the proposed architecture performs very well on several datasets, which, while interesting, does not directly prove the main claim of the paper. The paper is well-written, but the presentation of the proposed architecture is not clear enough to be easily understood. It would have been much easier to read if a high-level (possible) description had been given before specifying the exact calculations of the model (such as the procedural description in section 2.1). In some places, some statements may seem like novel claims due to the lack of citations. For example, the discussion of using a convolutional GRU module failed to mention its prior use in generative models [24]. Given the many similarities between DRAW and PLN, it is surprising that neither is mentioned in the relevant work section. Although the relationship between PLN and DRAW is well explained in the introduction, there is no mention of PLN in the paper, which the authors should correct.
