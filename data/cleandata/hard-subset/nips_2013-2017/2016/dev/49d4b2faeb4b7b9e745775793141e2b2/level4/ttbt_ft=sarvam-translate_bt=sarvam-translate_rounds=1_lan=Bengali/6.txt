This paper proposes a new class of architecture called Matryoshka Networks (MatNets), which combines the advantages of models like DRAW and Ladder Networks to help learn deep generative models in a hierarchical manner and jointly train inference/generation. The paper demonstrates the quantitative performance of MatNets on MNIST, Omniglot, and CIFAR10. This paper proposes an interesting framework for learning deep generative models. The authors have shown excellent results in terms of injecting missing regions of the image. However, it would be more convincing if classification results (supervised/semi-supervised/ unsupervised) were included on benchmarks like MNIST and CIFAR10. The paper is well-written. The paper's claims, explanations, and justifications are clear and easy to follow.
