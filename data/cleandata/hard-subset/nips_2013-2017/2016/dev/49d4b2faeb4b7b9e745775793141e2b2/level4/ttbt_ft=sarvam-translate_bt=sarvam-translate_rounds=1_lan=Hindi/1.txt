Arguing that the success of models like DRAW is due to their hierarchical approach to generating data and the deterministic connections existing between each latent variable and observation, the authors apply similar design principles to hierarchical latent variable models. The resulting models, called Matryoshka Networks (MatNets), include a bottom-up (BU) network, a top-down network (TD), and a merge module that directs the flow of information between the two. The MatNet architecture is reminiscent of probabilistic ladder networks (PLNs) [28], featuring a deterministic path from observation to the latent layers in the prediction model and a top-down factorization of the variable posterior. The primary difference in Matryoshka Networks is the deterministic connections between the latent variable and observation, which are not present in PLNs. MatNets are evaluated on density modeling on MNIST, Omniglot, and CIFAR, yielding either highly competitive or state-of-the-art results. This is an interesting and quite well-executed paper. The paper's main contribution is a hierarchical VAE architecture with deterministic connections in prediction and generative models, which implements the sequential production of observations. DRAW also adopts a similar approach, but the latent variable layers in DRAW-like models do not form a hierarchy (and are all essentially at the same level). Prob Ladder Nets have all the proposed features except for deterministic connections in the generative model. Thus, the main innovative contribution here is the introduction of deterministic connections in generative hierarchical models. Surprisingly, there are no experiments in the experimental section demonstrating the importance of deterministic connections in prediction models and/or generative models. Instead, the experiments show that the proposed architecture performs very well on several datasets, which is interesting but does not provide direct evidence for the importance of the paper's central claim. The paper is quite well-written, but the presentation of the proposed architecture is not clear enough to be easily understood. It would be much easier to read if the high-level ( probabilistic) description of the model came before the specification of the exact calculations (i.e., the procedural description in section 2.1). Due to the lack of citations in some places, some statements may seem like claims of novelty. For example, the discussion of using convolutional GRU modules [24] fails to mention their prior use in the generative model. Given the many similarities between DRAW and PLNs, it is surprising that neither is mentioned in the relevant work section. While the relationship between DRAW and PLNs is well explained in the introduction, PLNs are not mentioned at all in the paper, which the authors should correct.
