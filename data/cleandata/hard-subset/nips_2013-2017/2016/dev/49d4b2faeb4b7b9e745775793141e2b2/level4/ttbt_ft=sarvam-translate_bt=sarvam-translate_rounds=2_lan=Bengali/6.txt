This paper proposes a new class of architecture called MatNets, which combines the advantages of models like DRAW and Ladder Networks to help learn deep generative models in a hierarchical manner and provide collaborative inference/generation training. The paper demonstrates the quantitative performance of MatNets on MNIST, Omniglot, and CIFAR10. This paper proposes an interesting framework for learning deep generative models. The authors have shown excellent results in terms of injecting missing regions of images. However, it would be more reliable if classification results (supervised/semi-supervised/unsupervised) were included in benchmarks like MNIST and CIFAR10. The paper is well-written. The paper's claims, explanations, and justifications are clear and easy to follow.
