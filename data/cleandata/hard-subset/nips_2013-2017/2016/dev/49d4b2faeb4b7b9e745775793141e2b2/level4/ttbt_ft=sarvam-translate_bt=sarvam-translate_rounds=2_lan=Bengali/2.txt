The authors have presented a new hierarchical generative model called MATNET. It combines recent advances in this field from draw-type models, ladder networks, residual networks, etc. In some datasets (Munist, Omniglot, Cipher10), it achieves state-of-the-art results in NLL, and in other datasets ( সেলবএ, LSUUN), it shows some good quality inpainting results.

Technical Quality: I felt that the experimental section was very clear; a simple and reproducible experimental protocol was followed and compared with state-of-the-art methods, even some very recent methods (such as Pixel RNN). I wanted to see a comparison with other methods for quality results, which I did not see in the supplementary material.

Novelty: The authors have used recent successes in this field and moved forward by using GMM as a precursor instead of a single Gaussian prior.

Clarity: Figure 1 could have been better quality and clearer. Also, it was not clear what the difference was between the supplementary material and the main paper submission (except for the additional figure section in the supplementary material).
