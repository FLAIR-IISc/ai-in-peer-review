The author describes a method for training deep, generative models that allows for greater information flow and more effective training. They present a Matryoshka network similar to Lapgan and ResNet, which includes a top-down network, a bottom-up network, and a set of merge modules. The merge modules compare top-down states with bottom-up module states, choosing a perturbation to minimize reconstruction error. While the details of specific sections are generally sufficient (such as the good use of equations to clarify calculations), there is room for improvement in clarity and flow within various sections and subsections. Sections 2.2 and the end of Section 2.1 need to more clearly link sampling/estimation/distribution to the network components. For example, x (in Section 2.2) is never defined as anything other than a random variable (until line 132, where it is vaguely defined as unknown or known data). How is this variable x related to the network? How is the secret variable z initialized? What is the effect of this initialization? Is there a typo at line 71? It states that conv (h, w) is a convolution of the input x with the kernel w, but says nothing about h. And, x is not an input to the conv function. The qualitative results are quite impressive. The quantitative results show that the proposed model performs better than previous methods, although it is much more complex. Before delving into the details of the network's individual components, it would be helpful to clearly define where the network's inputs and outputs (and how they are used) are defined. It would be even more helpful if the inputs and outputs of each component of the network (top-down, bottom-up, merge module) were defined before explaining the mechanism. I would like to see more analysis on the qualitative results, in addition to explanations about the quantitative results. Can you provide more measurements besides the NLL? What about reconstruction error? It would be great to see some qualitative comparisons with previous methods.
