- Paper proposes Matryoshka network for training deep generative models with improved information flow, composed of top-down, bottom-up networks and merge modules.  
- Merge modules compare top-down and bottom-up states and choose perturbations to minimize reconstruction error.  
- Technical sections and equations are generally clear, but overall clarity and section flow need improvement.  
- Section 2.2 and end of Section 2.1 fail to clearly connect sampling/inference/distributions to network components, leaving integration unclear.  
- Variable x is poorly defined (only later described vaguely as known/unknown data); its role in the network needs explicit explanation.  
- Unclear how latent variables z are initialized and what effect initialization has on performance.  
- Possible typo/ambiguity on line 71: conv(h, w) description omits role of h and does not explicitly show x as an input.  
- Qualitative results are visually impressive.  
- Quantitative results show the model outperforms prior methods but at substantially higher complexity.  
- Recommend adding a concise overview of network inputs/outputs and explicitly defining inputs/outputs for top-down, bottom-up, and merge modules before technical details.  
- Suggest deeper analysis of qualitative results to complement quantitative findings.  
- Ask for additional evaluation metrics beyond NLL (e.g., reconstruction error) for a more comprehensive assessment.  
- Recommend qualitative comparisons with previous methods to contextualize performance.
