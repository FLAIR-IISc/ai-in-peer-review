The authors have proposed an architecture for deep generative models that enables learning of deeper models without sacrificing training performance. It is believed that including lateral, shortcut, and residual connections within the network will improve training performance. The proposed architecture provides state-of-the-art performance on several benchmark datasets. The authors provide reasonable justification for the proposed architecture, which includes key insights from the success of recent work on deep recurrent networks and semi-supervised ladder networks. The published results are indeed impressive. Although the basis of this paper is focused on the training performance of the proposed networks, their training performance is only evidenced in the form of performance metrics on various benchmarks. If the training performance of these networks is a primary contribution to this work, further analysis, or specifically experiments highlighting training performance, is desirable.
