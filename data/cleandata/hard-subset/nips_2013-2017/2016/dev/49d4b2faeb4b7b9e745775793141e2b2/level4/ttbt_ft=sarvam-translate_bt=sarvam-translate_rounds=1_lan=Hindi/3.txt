The authors propose an architecture for deep generative models that enables deep models to learn without losing their training capacity. It is believed that the improvement in training capacity arises from incorporating lateral, shortcut, and residual connections within the network. The proposed architecture delivers state-of-the-art performance on several benchmark datasets. The authors provide a reasonable justification for the proposed architecture, drawing key insights from recent successes in deep recurrent networks and semi-supervised ladder networks. The reported results are indeed impressive. While the paper's focus is on the training capacity of the proposed network, their evidence of training capacity comes only in the form of performance metrics on various benchmarks. If the training capacity of these networks is the primary contribution of this work, further analysis, or specifically experiments highlighting training capacity, would be desirable.
