The authors have presented a new hierarchical generative model called MatNet. It combines recent advances in this field from DRAW-type models, ladder networks, residual networks, etc. In some datasets (MNIST, Omniglot, CIFAR 10), it achieves state-of-the-art results in NLL, and in other datasets (CelebA, LSUN), it shows some good quality inpainting results.

Technical Quality: I felt that the experimental section was very clear; a simple and reproducible experimental protocol was followed and compared to state-of-the-art methods, even some very recent methods (such as Pixel RNN). I would have liked to see a comparison with other methods for the quality results, which I did not see in the supplementary materials.

Novelty: The authors have used recent successes in this field and moved forward by using GMM as a precursor instead of a single Gaussian prior.

Clarity: Figure 1 could have been of better quality and clearer. Also, it was not clear what the difference was between the supplementary materials and the original paper submission (except for the additional image section in the supplementary materials).
