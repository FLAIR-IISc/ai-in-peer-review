The authors present a new hierarchical generative model, MatNet. It incorporates recent advances in the field using models such as DRAW-type models, ladder networks, residual nets, etc. It achieves state-of-the-art results in the context of NLL for some datasets (MNIST, Omniglot, CIFAR 10), and displays some good qualitative input results on other datasets (CelebA, LSUN). Technical Quality: I felt that the experimental section was very clear; a straightforward and reproducible experimental protocol was followed, and comparisons were made with state-of-the-art methods, even some methods that were very recent (e.g., pixel RNN). I would have preferred to see a side-by-side comparison with other methods for the qualitative results, which I didn't see even in the supplementary material. Novelty: The authors used recent successes in the field and built upon them, for example, using a GMM on z_0 instead of a single Gaussian prior. Clarity: Figure 1 could have been of much better quality and clearer. Also, it was not clear what the difference was between the supplementary material and the actual paper submission (except for the section on additional images in the supplementary material).
