The authors have proposed an architecture for deep generative models that enables learning of deeper models without sacrificing training performance. It is believed that incorporating lateral, shortcut, and residual connections within the network will improve training performance. The proposed architecture provides state-of-the-art performance on several benchmark datasets. The authors provide reasonable justification for the proposed architecture, including key insights from recent work on deep recurrent networks and semi-supervised ladder networks. The published results are indeed impressive. Although the basis of this paper focuses on the training performance of the proposed networks, their training performance is only proven in the form of performance metrics on various benchmarks. If the training performance of these networks is a primary contribution to this work, further analysis, or specifically experiments highlighting the training performance, is desirable.
