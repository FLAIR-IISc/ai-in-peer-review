- Proposes an architecture for deep generative models that enables training of deeper networks without losing trainability.  
- Enhanced trainability attributed to combining lateral, shortcut, and residual connections.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on multiple benchmark datasets.  
- Provides a well-reasoned design justification drawing on advances in deep recurrent networks and semi-supervised ladder networks.  
- Reported empirical results are impressive.  
- Critique: claim that improved trainability is a central contribution is supported only by benchmark performance.  
- Recommendation: include additional analyses or experiments explicitly demonstrating trainability to strengthen the paper.
