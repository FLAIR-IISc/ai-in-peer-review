- Proposes predicting distributions over coefficients in an overcomplete depth representation for monocular depth estimation.  
- Network outputs per-coefficient distributions to express confidence and ambiguity.  
- Uses a globalization procedure to combine local distributions into a full scene depth map.  
- Paper is well-written with clear claims, explanations, and derivations.  
- Provides a thorough review of related work.  
- Evaluated on the NYU v2 dataset and achieves state-of-the-art performance on most metrics.  
- Includes detailed analysis of contributions from different components of the overcomplete representation.  
- Strengths: novel approach, thorough evaluation, clear organization, and discussion of potential applications.  
- Weakness: potential reproducibility challenges due to complex architecture and large dataset, though code and pretrained model are provided.  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance; technically sound.  
- Arguments for acceptance: originality, strong empirical results, clear writing, and detailed analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reproducibility concerns and limited evaluation across datasets/settings.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
