- Proposes a novel monocular depth method that predicts distributions over coefficients in an overcomplete depth representation.
- Network outputs per-coefficient distributions to express confidence and ambiguity in predictions.
- Uses a globalization procedure to combine local coefficient distributions into a full scene depth map.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the network architecture and globalization method.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the NYU v2 depth benchmark.
- Provides thorough analysis of the contributions of different components and method accuracy.
- Strength: novel departure from direct depth regression and CRF-based smoothing.
- Strength: overcomplete representation plus distributional outputs effectively capture ambiguity and uncertainty.
- Weakness: experimental section needs more side-by-side qualitative comparisons.
- Weakness: unclear distinction between supplementary material and the main paper.
- Weakness: poor quality of Figure 1 makes the architecture harder to understand.
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance; technically sound with supported claims.
- Overall recommendation: accept — strengths outweigh weaknesses.
