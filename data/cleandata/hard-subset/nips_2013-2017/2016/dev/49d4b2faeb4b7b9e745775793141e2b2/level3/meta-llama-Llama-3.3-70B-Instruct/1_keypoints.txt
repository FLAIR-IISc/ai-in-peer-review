- Proposes training a network to predict distributions over coefficients in an overcomplete depth representation rather than single depth values.
- Network outputs per-coefficient distributions, allowing expression of prediction confidence and ambiguity.
- Introduces a globalization procedure to fuse local coefficient distributions into a single consistent depth map.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the network architecture and globalization method.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the NYU v2 depth benchmark.
- Ablation study provides insights into how different components of the overcomplete representation affect accuracy.
- Strength: novel approach enabling uncertainty modeling in monocular depth estimation.
- Strength: overcomplete representation captures a wide range of depth cues.
- Strength: efficient globalization yields fast and accurate depth maps.
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of performance across different scene types and object classes.
- Weakness: limited comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods on additional benchmarks.
- Weakness: limited discussion of interpretability of predicted distributions and the globalization step.
- Technical quality: sound, claims supported by experiments, and carefully evaluated.
- Originality: novel contributions in representation and globalization.
- Significance: high potential impact for robotics, autonomous driving, and AR applications.
- Recommendation: accept.
