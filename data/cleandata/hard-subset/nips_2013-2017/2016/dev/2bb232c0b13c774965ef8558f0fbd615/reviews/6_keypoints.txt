- Proposes joint segmentation and transcription for handwritten paragraph recognition using multidimensional LSTM-RNNs.  
- Builds on prior work (ref [6]) by replacing standard collapse with a weighted collapsing step.  
- Weighted collapse forms a weighted sum of feature vectors, inducing an attentional mechanism in recognition.  
- Replaces per-character softmax decoder with a bidirectional LSTM (BLSTM) decoder that outputs entire text lines.  
- BLSTM decoder yields a reported 20×–30× speed-up (likely in iterations) compared to prior methods.  
- Method achieves improved recognition accuracy over past approaches; results are clearly strong.  
- Contributions (weighted collapse/attention and BLSTM line decoder) are reasonable extensions but not highly surprising.  
- The claimed speed-up is not well quantified in the manuscript.  
- Manuscript contains a large number of typos that need correction.
