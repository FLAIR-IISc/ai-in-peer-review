- Addresses end-to-end paragraph-level handwriting recognition, replacing the conventional two-step line segmentation + recognition pipeline.  
- Proposes an attention-based model that implicitly performs line segmentation within the recognition model.  
- Natural and sensible formulation that fits current deep-learning trends; paper is well written and easy to follow.  
- Experimental results show the approach outperforms conventional two-step methods even when those use ground-truth line segmentation.  
- Competitive with state-of-the-art line-level methods on RIMES and IAM despite not using the sophisticated pre-processing others used.  
- Claims to be the first work to apply an attention-based, line-segmentation-free method to paragraph-level handwriting recognition.  
- Highlights that end-to-end line-segmentation-free recognition has been desired but challenging; deep learning enables this progress.  
- Reviewer asks for explanation why the attention-based model outperforms line-based models using ground-truth segmentation.  
- Reviewer requests reporting computational time as additional valuable information.
