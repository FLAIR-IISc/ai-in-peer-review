- Introduces an attention mechanism for offline handwriting recognition on unsegmented paragraphs, using MD-LSTMs for features, attention, and sometimes decoding.  
- Learned attention unexpectedly outperforms ground-truth segmentation, a finding that needs explanation.  
- Character error rates on standard datasets are impressive.  
- Word error rates are competitive but do not break state-of-the-art.  
- System achieves competitive results despite ignoring ground-truth segmentation and using a simplistic language model.  
- Extending single-line recognition techniques to multi-line is an elegant and natural advance; this work appears mature relative to similar efforts.  
- Technical exposition is clear and sufficient; the colorized attention figure is particularly helpful.  
- Benchmark comparisons are apples-to-oranges (baselines use stronger language models and ground-truth segmentation) and the discussion is underdeveloped.  
- The paper lacks analysis on why learned attention outperforms ground truth—possible causes like annotation noise or cross-line context should be explored.  
- Writing quality needs improvement: many typos, grammatical errors, tense shifts, and an occasionally overly competitive tone that weakens credibility.
