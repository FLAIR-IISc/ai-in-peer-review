- Proposes joint segmentation and transcription of handwritten paragraphs.
- Uses MDLSTMs for input encoding and for an attention network to segment lines.
- Employs a Bidirectional-LSTM decoder to transcribe concatenated encoded sequences.
- Trains end-to-end by minimizing a CTC loss over the entire paragraph.
- Advances prior work by removing the need for pre-segmented lines and has real-world applicability.
- Missing baseline using simpler convolutional or affine layers, which would be easier to train than MDLSTMs.
- Model complexity could grow if adding another MDLSTM+CTC level to detect paragraph boundaries.
- Shows improvements over the authors’ baseline and on the RIMES benchmark, but not on the IAM benchmark.
