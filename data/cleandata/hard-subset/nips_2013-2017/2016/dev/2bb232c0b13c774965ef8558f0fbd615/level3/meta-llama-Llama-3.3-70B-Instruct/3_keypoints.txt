- Proposes a novel attention-based end-to-end model for offline handwriting recognition that removes explicit line segmentation.  
- Modifies MDLSTM-RNN by replacing the collapse layer with a recurrent attention network to iteratively focus on individual text lines.  
- Outperforms conventional two-step methods (even with ground-truth line segmentation) and is competitive with state-of-the-art line-level methods on Rimes and IAM.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Provides a thorough related-work discussion highlighting limitations of existing approaches.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, including baselines, SOTA comparisons, and analyses of components (decoder, line segmentation).  
- Strength: novel model with potential to improve recognition accuracy and efficiency.  
- Strength: thorough experiments that illuminate strengths and weaknesses.  
- Strength: clear and well-organized presentation.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational time, resource usage, and efficiency comparisons with SOTA.  
- Weakness: insufficient insights into interpretability of the attention mechanism and implicit line segmentation.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of potential applications, limitations, and future research directions.  
- Overall: significant contribution with promising results; minor additional analyses would strengthen the paper.  
- Pro-accept: innovation, comprehensive evaluation, and clarity support acceptance.  
- Con-accept: needs more efficiency analysis, interpretability study, and broader discussion to be stronger.
