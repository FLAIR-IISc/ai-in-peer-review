- Novelty: Introduces an attention-weighted collapse replacing standard collapse, enabling implicit line segmentation and end-to-end paragraph transcription without line-level annotations.  
- Contribution: Integrates attention into MDLSTMs and BLSTM decoder, extending prior work and addressing segmentation errors in traditional pipelines.  
- Results (Rimes): Achieves state-of-the-art character error rates on the Rimes dataset, demonstrating efficacy of the approach.  
- Efficiency claim: Authors claim the model is 20–30× faster than prior methods, suggesting significant computational benefits.  
- Visualization: Includes attention visualizations that illustrate implicit line segmentation and model behavior.  
- Results (IAM): Underperforms on the IAM dataset, indicating limited generalization or data-sensitivity.  
- Data limitation: Poor IAM performance likely due to limited training data and punctuation handling issues.  
- Punctuation: Attention mechanism struggles to recognize punctuation, impacting transcription accuracy.  
- Lack of self-containment: Paper relies heavily on prior MDLSTM literature (e.g., Graves et al.), reducing accessibility for readers unfamiliar with that work.  
- Implementation details: Insufficient description of how prior methods were implemented, obscuring the exact contributions of modifications.  
- Runtime validation missing: No concrete runtime benchmarks or comparisons provided to substantiate speedup claims.  
- Suggested fixes: Provide deeper explanation of why the method outperforms line-labeled approaches, especially BLSTM linguistic dependencies.  
- Suggested fixes: Add runtime benchmarks and concrete efficiency comparisons.  
- Suggested fixes: Explore cross-dataset training, domain adaptation, or data augmentation to improve IAM performance.  
- Suggested fixes: Investigate methods (e.g., multi-scale attention, extra objectives) to improve punctuation recognition.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions pending improved clarity and runtime validation.
