- Proposes TGGF: a two-stage algorithm (orthogonality-promoting initialization + truncated generalized gradient refinement) for quadratic systems.
- Claims exact recovery from a near-optimal number of noise-free measurements with linear computational complexity.
- Paper is well-written with consistent notation and adequate references.
- Numerical results show TGGF outperforms Truncated Wirtinger Flow (TWF) and Wirtinger Flow (WF).
- Strength: novel algorithm achieving exact recovery with near-optimal sample complexity.
- Strength: simple, effective truncation rule that boosts performance.
- Strength: empirical superiority over existing state-of-the-art solvers.
- Weakness: lacks detailed computational complexity analysis and explicit comparison to other solvers.
- Weakness: insufficient discussion/insight into parameter choices (step size, truncation threshold) and their effects.
- Weakness: missing additional numerical results, notably running-time comparisons with competitors.
- Meets conference criteria: technically sound, clear, original, and significant.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong theoretical and empirical support, clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: needs deeper complexity analysis, parameter studies, and runtime experiments.
- Overall recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing the highlighted weaknesses.
