- Proposes end-to-end handwritten paragraph recognition using MDLSTM with attention and a weighted collapse layer for implicit line segmentation; uses BLSTM decoder and CTC loss; evaluated on Rimes and IAM.  
- Strength: Integrates line segmentation into transcription model, reducing preprocessing and moving toward fully end-to-end recognition.  
- Strength: Model is technically well-motivated, building on MDLSTMs and attention, with a robust experimental setup and comparisons.  
- Strength: Eliminates explicit line segmentation, improving applicability where line-level annotations are unavailable or noisy.  
- Strength: Achieves state-of-the-art character error rate (CER) on the Rimes dataset.  
- Weakness: Lacks simpler baselines (e.g., convolutional or affine models) to isolate the contributions of attention and MDLSTM.  
- Weakness: Increased model complexity due to MDLSTM layers may hinder scalability to larger datasets or full-page recognition.  
- Weakness: Underperforms on IAM, especially in word error rate (WER), with issues handling punctuation and small characters.  
- Weakness: Method is limited to paragraph-level recognition and requires prior layout analysis; full-page extension remains open.  
- Pro (accept): Innovative implicit line segmentation and competitive Rimes performance demonstrate meaningful progress.  
- Con (reject/concern): Limited IAM gains, missing simple baselines, and scalability limits weaken the contribution.  
- Recommendation: Accept the paper for its novel end-to-end approach and Rimes results, but request inclusion of simpler baselines and discussion/plans for extending to full-page recognition.
