- Replaces global average pooling in MDLSTM collapse layer with weighted average pooling; weights learned by a recurrent MDLSTM attention layer.
- Uses CTC loss to enable end-to-end paragraph recognition without character- or line-level alignment.
- Interprets the attention mechanism as an iterative “soft” line segmentation that focuses on one text line per time step.
- Decodes whole lines at each time step, yielding significant speed and efficiency gains over character-level decoding.
- Demonstrates competitive or superior performance on Rimes and IAM datasets, outperforming prior methods including [6].
- Reviewer questions the level of technical novelty relative to [6]; the approach may be evolutionary rather than fundamentally new—clarification requested.
- Recommends visualizing attention maps for cases where input lines exceed model time steps to better understand model behavior.
- Notes scalability limitation: method is confined to paragraph-level transcription and cannot handle arbitrary reading orders or complex document layouts without extra preprocessing.
- Strengths summarized: end-to-end processing, strong performance, computational efficiency, and interpretability via attention.
- Recommendation: strong contribution and suitable for acceptance provided authors clarify novelty, address scalability, and add attention visualizations.
