- End-to-end paragraph recognizer using MDLSTM-RNN + CTC with a weighted collapse and BLSTM decoder.  
- Weighted collapse replaces traditional collapse to provide an attention-like implicit line segmentation.  
- BLSTM decoder replaces softmax to process full text lines, claimed 20x–30x speed-up.  
- Competitive or superior accuracy on RIMES and IAM datasets, sometimes outperforming SOTA.  
- Eliminates explicit line segmentation, addressing a major error-prone pipeline step.  
- Weighted collapse is a meaningful contribution enabling iterative focus on lines.  
- BLSTM decoder models inter-line dependencies and improves transcription accuracy.  
- Reported performance gains and speed-up are practically noteworthy.  
- Experiments are comprehensive with comparisons to baselines and different segmentation scenarios.  
- Contributions are incremental rather than transformative.  
- The 20x–30x speed-up claim is insufficiently quantified and lacks detailed evidence.  
- Manuscript has numerous typos and requires clearer organization and descriptions.  
- Method is limited to paragraph-level transcription and cannot handle complex document layouts or arbitrary reading orders.  
- Implementation details (hyperparameters, training dynamics) are insufficient for full reproducibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a relevant problem, well-motivated modifications, end-to-end simplification with tangible improvements.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental advance, manuscript needs substantial revision, and speed-up claim lacks rigorous validation.  
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance contingent on fixing clarity/typos, providing stronger speed-up evidence, and improving reproducibility details.
