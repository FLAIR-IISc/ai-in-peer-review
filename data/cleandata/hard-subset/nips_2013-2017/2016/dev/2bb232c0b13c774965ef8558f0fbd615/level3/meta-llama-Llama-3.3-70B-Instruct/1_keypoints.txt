- Proposes a novel end-to-end offline handwriting recognition method by modifying MDLSTM-RNN to process full handwritten paragraphs.
- Introduces an attention mechanism that iteratively focuses on single text lines, enabling implicit line segmentation.
- Achieves competitive results on RIMES and IAM datasets, showing the approach's potential.
- Strength: removes need for explicit line segmentation, a key practical advantage.
- Strength: attention-based implicit line segmentation is a notable contribution and appears effective.
- Strength: paper is well-organized and clearly written.
- Weakness: insufficient in-paper explanation of the MDLSTM architecture, forcing readers to consult external sources.
- Weakness: unclear implementation details and unexplained claims (notably the 20–30× speed improvement versus Graves et al and other peculiar results).
- Weakness: model struggles with punctuation recognition, a significant limitation acknowledged by authors.
- Suggested improvement: add more experiments (e.g., training on combined datasets, fine-tuning on IAM) to boost performance and robustness.
- Suggested improvement: provide deeper analysis of the attention mechanism’s role in implicit line segmentation.
- Suggested improvement: explore methods to improve punctuation handling.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with potential; recommended accept with minor revisions to address the above weaknesses.
