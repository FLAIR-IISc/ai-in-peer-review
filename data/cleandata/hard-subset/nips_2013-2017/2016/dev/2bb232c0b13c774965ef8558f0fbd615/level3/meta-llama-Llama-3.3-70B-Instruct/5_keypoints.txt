- Proposes Truncated Generalized Gradient Flow (TGGF), a two-stage algorithm (orthogonality-promoting initialization + truncated generalized gradient refinement) for quadratic systems.
- Claims exact recovery with high probability and runtime linear in data-reading time.
- Paper is well-structured and easy to follow with clear problem setup and detailed algorithm description.
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis and performance guarantees.
- Numerical results show TGGF outperforming state-of-the-art solvers (TWF, WF).
- Strength: novel algorithm achieving state-of-the-art empirical performance.
- Strength: simple, effective truncation rule that improves performance.
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to established benchmarks; current comparisons raise questions.
- Weakness: inadequate discussion of why learned attention outperforms ground truth and its implications.
- Weakness: writing quality suffers from typos, grammatical errors, and inconsistent tenses.
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, and originality; technically sound and significant contribution.
- Recommendation: accept, but authors should address benchmarking, attention analysis, and writing issues.
