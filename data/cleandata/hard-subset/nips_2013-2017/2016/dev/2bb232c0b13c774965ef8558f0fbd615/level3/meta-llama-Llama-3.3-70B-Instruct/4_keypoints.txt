- Proposes modifying MD-LSTM collapse layer by replacing global average pooling with learned weighted average pooling via a recurrent attention layer, trained with CTC.
- Attention acts as a "soft" line segmentation; model achieves competitive performance on Rimes and IAM datasets.
- Strength: enables end-to-end handwriting recognition without explicit line segmentation.
- Strength: recurrent attention to learn pooling weights is a novel architectural contribution.
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation with detailed model description and experiments.
- Weakness: technical novelty is questionable — main change is decoding a whole line per time step rather than per character.
- Weakness: lacks visualization of attentions when input has more lines than configured time steps to assess failure modes.
- Quality: well-written manuscript with claims supported by experimental results.
- Originality: moderate — builds on existing MD-LSTM and attention ideas.
- Significance: high — tackles a hard problem in handwriting recognition and offers a practical solution.
- Arguments for acceptance: removes need for explicit line segmentation, shows competitive results, and is clearly presented.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty and missing crucial attention-visualization experiments.
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing novelty concerns and adding attention visualizations for multi-line cases.
