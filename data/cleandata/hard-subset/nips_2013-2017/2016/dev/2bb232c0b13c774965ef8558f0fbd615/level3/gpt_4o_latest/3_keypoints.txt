- Proposes an end-to-end paragraph-level handwriting recognition model that integrates line segmentation and recognition using an attention-based iterative weighted collapse in place of the traditional collapse layer.
- Replaces MDLSTM-RNN’s collapse layer with an attention-driven iterative weighted collapse, enabling implicit line segmentation without explicit pre-processing.
- Demonstrates competitive or superior performance on Rimes and IAM datasets, outperforming some line-level methods even when they use ground-truth line segmentation.
- Attention mechanism effectively focuses on specific text lines iteratively, enhancing interpretability and drawing on successful attention paradigms from other domains.
- Paper is well-written with clear methodology, experimental setup, and attention visualizations that aid understanding.
- Work has high potential impact by encouraging end-to-end, segmentation-free approaches in handwriting recognition research.
- Lacks sufficient theoretical or empirical analysis explaining why the attention-based model outperforms line-based methods (e.g., role of BLSTM decoder and inter-line dependencies).
- Does not report computational time, memory usage, or resource requirements for training and inference, limiting assessment of practicality.
- Scalability is limited: method targets paragraph-level recognition with a fixed reading order and lacks experiments or solutions for full-page or complex layouts.
- Results on IAM rely heavily on a language model, which may mask the model’s raw handwriting recognition capabilities.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, impactful approach with strong empirical results and clear presentation likely to advance the field.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing computational efficiency metrics and deeper analysis of superiority; unaddressed scalability limits broader applicability.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address computational analysis, provide deeper explanation of model advantages, and discuss scalability.
