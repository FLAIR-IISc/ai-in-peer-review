- Proposes a novel joint paragraph segmentation and transcription method using MDLSTMs with a BiLSTM decoder, attention network, and CTC loss.  
- Extends prior work by removing the requirement for pre-segmented text lines, enabling implicit line segmentation.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.  
- Introduction gives thorough background on offline handwriting recognition and current model limitations.  
- Related work section is comprehensive, covering traditional segmentation and recent attention-based models.  
- Model is technically sound with a clear explanation of architecture and training process.  
- Attention mechanism for implicit line segmentation is a key technical contribution.  
- Experiments conducted on Rimes and IAM public databases.  
- Results: outperforms baseline and is competitive with state-of-the-art on Rimes.  
- Results: weaker performance on IAM; language model influence is significant for this dataset.  
- Strength: clear presentation and well-designed experiments with thorough result analysis.  
- Weakness: lacks baseline comparisons to simpler models (e.g., convolutional or affine layers).  
- Weakness: model complexity may grow with additional MDLSTM/CTC layers, complicating training and evaluation.  
- Overall: significant contribution with real-world applicability; promising results but some evaluation and complexity concerns remain.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, technical soundness, strong Rimes performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weaker IAM results, potential training/evaluation complexity, missing simple-model baselines.
