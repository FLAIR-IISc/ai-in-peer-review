- Proposes a novel end-to-end offline handwriting recognition model that replaces the MDLSTM collapse layer with a recurrent attention mechanism to process whole paragraphs.
- Attention mechanism enables iterative focusing on single text lines, performing implicit line segmentation without explicit line detection.
- Model achieves competitive results with state-of-the-art systems on IAM and Rimes datasets while removing the need for explicit line segmentation.
- Reported inference is orders of magnitude faster than previous approaches, improving practicality for real-world use.
- Paper is well-written with a clear overview of related work and a thorough explanation of the proposed model.
- Experiments are well-designed and effectively evaluate the proposed approach.
- Main limitation: model is restricted to paragraph-level images and requires prior document layout analysis to detect paragraphs.
- May not generalize well to complex documents with multiple text blocks or arbitrary reading orders.
- Language model used in experiments is likely suboptimal; better language models could improve results.
- Needs more detailed analysis and discussion of results, including error analysis and comparisons to other top systems.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective approach with competitive results, solid experimental design, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited applicability beyond paragraph-level input, additional engineering required for real-world deployment, and insufficient result analysis.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with potential for improved performance and efficiency, but would benefit from revisions addressing limitations and deeper evaluation.
