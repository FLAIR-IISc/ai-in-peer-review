The authors examine the introduction of a focus mechanism in an offline handwriting recognition system, which points to paragraphs not connected to the handwritten text. MD-LSTM is used for feature extraction, attention calculation, and (in some cases) decoding. Interestingly, the attention learned in the authors' system performs better than ground truth segmentation. Character error rates on standard datasets appear quite impressive. While the system's word error rates do not break the state of the art, they are quite competitive, despite ignoring both ground truth segmentation and the relatively simple language model. Positives: -The idea of implementing the technique is both an elegant idea and a natural next step to generalize single-line handwriting recognition to multi-line. It is not surprising that it is one of at least two tasks (mentioned by the authors) that are trying similar techniques (this is not relevant, but I consider it one of the more mature/developed tasks). -It is impressive (as is often the case these days) that such a system works perfectly, works so well alone. -The technical details provided by the authors are sufficient, and generally easy to follow. The colored attention figure is particularly satisfactory. Areas for improvement: -The comparison to established benchmarks raises more questions than answers. As the authors noted, this comparison is from apples to oranges. Reference models use more complex language models and ground truth segmentation. The latter is unavoidable due to the fundamental nature of the problem, but differences in language models are absolutely unnecessary. The discussion of this comparison also seems underdeveloped and unpublished, and I am sure the authors could do better. -The authors do not adequately discuss the importance/origin of the learned attention performing better than ground truth. Is this due to noise in the annotations? Taking advantage of the information contained in neighboring lines? This is particularly important, as all the models compared in Section 5.4 use this ground truth. -Finally, while the paper is well-organized and easy to follow, the quality of writing leaves some desirable things missing. There are too many typos, grammatical errors, and changing tenses. Furthermore, the sometimes competitive tone, especially compared to other works, undermines the authors' credibility. Their results stand up quite well even in neutral light.
