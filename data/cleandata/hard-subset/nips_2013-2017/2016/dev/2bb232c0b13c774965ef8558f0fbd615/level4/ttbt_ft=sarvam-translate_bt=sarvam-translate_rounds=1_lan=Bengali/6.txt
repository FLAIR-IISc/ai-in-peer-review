This paper proposes a joint segmentation and transcription approach for handwritten text recognition. It builds upon previous work using multi-dimensional LSTM-RRN for this purpose. Such a model network uses Connectivity Temporal Classification (CTC) for training. This paper builds upon reference [6] using a 'collapsing' step, where a weighted sum of feature vector sequences is created before the final step. This weighted collapse introduces an attentional mechanism in the recognition step and improves performance. The final step uses a bidirectional LSTM decoder instead of a softmax. By doing this, the decoder outputs the entire text line instead of individual characters. This potentially increases speed by 20X to 30X in terms of the number of iterations. The proposed method improves recognition accuracy compared to previous methods. The contribution of this paper is to suggest the use of a weighted collapse layer (which provides an attentional mechanism) and a BLSTM decoder (for outputting sentences) for handwriting recognition. These are reasonable next steps that further enhance previous work but lack a surprising element. The improvement in accuracy in the results is very clear and quite good. However, the other claim, which concerns speed improvement, was not well measured. A major problem with this manuscript is the large number of typos. Hopefully, the authors will be able to correct these if the manuscript is accepted for publication.
