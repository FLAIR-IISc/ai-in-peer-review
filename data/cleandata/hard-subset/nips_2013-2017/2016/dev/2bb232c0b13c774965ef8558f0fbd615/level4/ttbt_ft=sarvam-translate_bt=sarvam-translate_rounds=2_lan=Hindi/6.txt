This paper proposes a combined partitioning and transcription approach for identifying handwritten paragraphs. It builds upon previous work using multi-dimensional LSTM-RRN for this purpose. Such a model uses Connectionist Temporal Classification (CTC) to train the network. The paper is based on the reference [6] using a weighted 'collapsing' stage, where a weighted sum of feature vector sequences is created before the final stage. This weighted collapse inspires a attention mechanism in the recognition stage and improves performance. The final stage uses a bidirectional LSTM decoder instead of SoftMax. In doing so, the decoder outputs entire text lines instead of individual characters. This provides a speed of 20X to 30X in terms of the number of iterations. The proposed method has improved identification accuracy compared to previous approaches. The contribution of this paper is to suggest using a weighted collapse layer (providing an attention mechanism) and a BLSTM decoder for identifying handwriting (to output sentences). These are reasonable next steps based on previous work but seem to lack an element of surprise. The improvement in accuracy is very evident in the results, and they are quite good. However, the second claim, which is about speed-up, is not well quantified. A major problem with this manuscript is the large number of typing errors. Hopefully, the authors can correct these if the manuscript is accepted for publication.
