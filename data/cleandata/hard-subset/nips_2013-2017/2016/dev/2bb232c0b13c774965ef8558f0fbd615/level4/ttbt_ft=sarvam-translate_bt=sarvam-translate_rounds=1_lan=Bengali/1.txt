The authors have developed an end-to-end system for parsing handwritten text from image (multiple horizontal lines). They achieved this by adding an attention mechanism to the single-line parser of Graves et al. [18] and then decoding the text using bidirectional-LSTM (instead of SoftMax, which was mainly used by Graves et al.). They evaluated the recognition performance in the RIMS and IAM datasets. The performance is comparable to the state-of-the-art method in RIMS, but lags behind in IAM. This is a very nice application of guided attention on an image to identify lines of handwritten text (as the authors correctly point out, it is based on the method of Ju et al. [38] and others, who used region focus on an image). In terms of presentation, the paper could have been written more self-dependently. It is difficult to understand the model presented in the paper without referring to Graves et al. [18]. If the MD-LSTM model is explained in more detail, it would help improve section 3 (as in [18]). Here are some strange results and statements that should be explained by the authors: - Why does the proposed method perform better than methods that use ground truth line labeling (in Table 2 and section 5.3)? Is this because the line breaks of ground truth are used to restart the decoder RNN, but this restart does not occur in the character sequence of the attention mechanism? To answer this question, more detailed information about the authors' implementation should be provided. - Line 142, the authors say their method is 20-30 times faster than the character decoder method of Bleuche et al. [6], but does not provide actual time for comparison. - In terms of results, performance for CER% in the RIMS dataset is better than the state-of-the-art method (for one setting), but is significantly worse in the IAM dataset, possibly due to a lack of training data. An easy test to check this would be to first train the model on test+training data from another dataset and then fine-tune it on IAM training data. I suggest the authors add this test. - Line 213, why does the attention mechanism miss punctuation?
