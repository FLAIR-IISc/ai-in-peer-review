This paper addresses the problem of end-to-end handwriting recognition at the paragraph level. The traditional approach involves two steps: line segmentation and line recognition. This work integrates both steps using a attention-based model. Experimental results show that the proposed approach performs better than traditional two-step approaches, even when traditional approaches use ground-truth line segmentation. It also demonstrates that the proposed approach provides good results compared to state-of-the-art line-level methods reported on the Rhemes and IAM datasets, even though the proposed approach does not use the sophisticated pre-processing methods used in previous methods. State-of-the-art handwriting recognition has been adopting a two-step approach for decades, and researchers are focusing on improving individual components. End-to-end line-segmentation-free algorithms are desired, but this has been a challenging problem. However, recent advances in deep learning have opened the door to building such systems. This work comes in this context. This work proposes integrating both steps using a attention-based model. The model uses an attention mechanism to implicitly perform line segmentation. The formulation is very natural and understandable. Experimental results show the effectiveness of the approach. Given current trends in related fields, implementing a attention-based model for end-to-end paragraph-level handwriting recognition is something that is expected to be done. As far as I know, this is the first work that actually does this. The paper is well-written and easy to understand. Comments: Some clarification is needed as to why a attention-based model is better than a line-based model with ground-truth line segmentation. Reporting of computational time would be valuable.
