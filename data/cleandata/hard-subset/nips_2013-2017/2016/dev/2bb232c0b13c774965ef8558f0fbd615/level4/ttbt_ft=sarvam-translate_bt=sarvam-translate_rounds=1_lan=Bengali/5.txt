Authors have explored the introduction of a process to focus on an offline handwriting recognition system, pointing to continuous paragraphs of handwritten text. MD-LSTM is used for feature extraction, attention calculation, and (in some cases) decoding. Interestingly, the authors' system has surpassed learned attention in ground truth segmentation. The character error rate seems quite impressive in the standard dataset. Although the system's word error rate is not state-of-the-art, it is quite competitive despite using ground truth segmentation and a relatively simple language model. Positive aspects: -The idea of applying a technique to generalize single-line handwriting recognition to multi-line handwriting is a refined concept and a natural next step. It is not surprising that it is at least one of two tasks (mentioned by the authors) that are trying similar techniques (although it is not relevant, but I consider it a more mature/advanced task of the two). -It is impressive (as is often seen these days) that such a system works, even as well as it does. -The technical explanations given by the authors are sufficient and generally easy to follow. The figure of colored attention is particularly satisfactory. Areas for improvement: -Comparisons with established standards raise more questions than answers. As the authors noted, this comparison is like apples and oranges. Reference models use more complex language models and use ground truth segmentation. The latter is unavoidable because the fundamental nature of the problem has been considered, but the gap within the language model is unnecessary. The discussion of this comparison also seems underdeveloped and uninformative, and I am confident that the authors could have done better. -The authors do not adequately discuss the significance/origin of learned attention surpassing ground truth. Is this due to confusion in the annotations? Using information in the adjacent lines? This is particularly important because the models compared in section 5.4 all use this ground truth. -Finally, although the paper is well-organized and easy to follow, the writing quality is somewhat disappointing. There are too many typos, grammatical errors, and tense errors. Moreover, especially when compared to other works, the occasional competitive tone undermines the authors' credibility. Their results are quite good even in a neutral light.
