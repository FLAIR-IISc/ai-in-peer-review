- Proposes an end-to-end attention-based model for paragraph-level handwriting recognition, replacing traditional two-stage (line segmentation + recognition) pipelines.  
- Integrates segmentation and recognition into a single model, with attention providing implicit line segmentation.  
- Demonstrates that the proposed method outperforms conventional two-step approaches, even when those use ground-truth line segmentation.  
- Achieves performance comparable to state-of-the-art line-level methods on Rimes and IAM without complex pre-processing.  
- Frames the approach as a natural, intuitive application of recent deep-learning/attention advances to a long-standing challenge.  
- Claims novelty as the first successful implementation of an attention-based end-to-end paragraph recognizer (to the reviewer’s knowledge).  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Reviewer requests more explanation for why the attention model outperforms line-based models with ground-truth segmentation.  
- Reviewer suggests reporting computational time to provide additional practical insights.
