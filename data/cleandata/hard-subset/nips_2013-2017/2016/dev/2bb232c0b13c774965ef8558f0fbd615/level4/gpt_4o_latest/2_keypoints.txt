- Proposes a method to jointly segment and transcribe handwritten paragraphs into text.  
- Uses MDLSTMs for both input encoding and as an attention mechanism to segment individual text lines.  
- Employs a Bidirectional-LSTM decoder to generate text from concatenated encoded sequences.  
- Optimizes the entire paragraph with a CTC loss.  
- Advances over prior methods that require explicit line segmentation and shows practical applicability.  
- Lacks a comparison baseline using only Convolutional or Affine layers, which are simpler to train than MDLSTMs.  
- Model complexity could increase notably if an additional MDLSTM+CTC layer were added to detect paragraph boundaries.  
- Shows improvements over the authors’ baseline and on the Rimes benchmark, but does not beat prior state-of-the-art on the IAM benchmark.
