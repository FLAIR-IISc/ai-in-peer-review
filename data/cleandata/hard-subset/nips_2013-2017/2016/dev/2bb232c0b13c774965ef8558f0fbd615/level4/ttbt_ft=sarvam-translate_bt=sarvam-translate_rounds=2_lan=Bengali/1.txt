The authors have developed an end-to-end system for parsing handwritten text from images (multiple horizontal lines). They achieved this by incorporating a focus process into Graves et al.'s single-line parser [18] and then decoding the text using bidirectional-LTSM (instead of SoftMax, which was primarily used by Graves et al.). They evaluated the recognition performance in the RIMS and IAM datasets. The performance is comparable to state-of-the-art methods in RIMS, but lags behind in IAM. This is a very nice application of focused attention on an image to identify lines of handwritten text (as the authors correctly note, it is based on the method of Ju et al. [38] and others, who used region focus on an image). In terms of presentation, the paper could have been written more self-dependently. Without mentioning Graves et al., it is difficult to understand the model presented in the paper [18]. If the MD-LTSM model is explained in more detail, it would help improve section 3 (e.g., [18]). Here are some odd results and statements that should be explained by the authors: - Why does the proposed method perform better than methods that use ground truth line labeling (in Table 2 and section 5.3)? Is this because the line breaks of ground truth are used to restart the decoder RNN, but this restart does not occur in the character sequence of the focus process? To answer this question, the authors should provide more detailed information about their implementation. - Line 142, the authors say their method is 20-30 times faster than Bleuche et al.'s character decoder method [6], but does not provide actual time for comparison. - In terms of results, performance is better than state-of-the-art methods for CER% in the RIMS dataset (for one setting), but significantly worse in the IAM dataset, possibly due to a lack of training data. An easy test to check this would be to first train the model on test+training data from another dataset and then fine-tune it on IAM training data. I suggest the authors add this test. - Line 213, why does the focus process miss punctuation?
