The authors have developed an end-to-end system for parsing handwritten text from paragraph (multiple horizontal lines) images. They achieve this by adding an attention mechanism to Graves et al.'s [18] single-line parser and then decoding the text using bidirectional-LSTM (instead of Softmax, as originally used by Graves et al.). They evaluate the recognition performance on the Rhymes and IAM datasets. The performance is comparable to state-of-the-art methods on Rhymes, but lags behind on IAM. This is a very good application of directed attention on an image to identify the sequence of handwritten text lines (as the authors correctly pointed out, it is based on the approach of Xu et al. [38] and others, who used field attention on an image). On display, the paper can be made more self-sufficient. It is difficult to understand the model presented in the paper without mentioning Graves et al. [18]. If the MD-LSTM model is explained in more detail, it would help improve Section 3 (as in [18]). There are some strange results and statements that the authors should explain: - Why does the proposed approach perform better than methods using ground truth line labeling (in Table 2 and Section 5.3)? Is this because line breaks in ground truth are used to restart the decoder RNN, but this does not occur in the character sequence of the reinitialization attention mechanism? The authors should provide more details on Graves et al.'s implementation to answer this question. - Line 142, the authors say that their method is 20-30 times faster than Bleuche et al.'s [6] character decoder method, but do not provide real-time for comparison. - In terms of results, the performance is better than state-of-the-art (for one setting) for CER% on the Rhymes dataset, but significantly worse on the IAM dataset, perhaps due to a lack of training data. A simple experiment to test this would be to first train the model on the test+training data of the second dataset and then fine-tune it on the IAM training data. I suggest that the authors add this experiment. - Line 213, why does the attention mechanism skip punctuation?
