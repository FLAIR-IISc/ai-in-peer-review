This paper discusses the problem of edge-to-edge handwriting recognition at the paragraph level. The conventional method involves two steps: line segmentation and line recognition. This work combines these two steps using a focus-based model. Experimental results show that the proposed method surpasses the conventional two-step method, even if conventional methods use ground-truth line segmentation. It also shows that the proposed method provides better results than the state-of-the-art line-level methods reported in the RIMS and IAM datasets, even though the proposed method does not use the advanced pre-processing methods used in previous methods. Improved handwriting recognition has been following the two-step method for decades, and researchers are focusing on improving individual components. Edge-to-edge line-segmentation-free algorithms were desirable, but it was a difficult problem. However, recent advances in deep learning have opened the door to creating such systems. This work comes in that context. This work proposes combining the two steps using a focus-based model. The focus process is used to perform line segmentation within the model. The formula is very natural and understandable. Experimental results show the effectiveness of the method. Considering the current trends in related fields, it is expected that a focus-based model will be applied for edge-to-edge paragraph-level handwriting recognition. As far as I know, this is the first work that actually does this. The paper is well-written and easy to understand. Comments: Some explanation is needed as to why a focus-based model is better than a line-based model with ground-truth line segmentation. It would be valuable to provide the computational time.
