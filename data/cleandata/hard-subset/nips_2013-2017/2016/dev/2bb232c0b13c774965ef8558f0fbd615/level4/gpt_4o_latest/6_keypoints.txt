- Introduces a joint segmentation-and-transcription approach for handwritten paragraph recognition using multidimensional LSTM-RNNs.  
- Network is trained with Connectionist Temporal Classification (CTC).  
- Proposes a weighted "collapse" layer that computes a weighted sum of feature sequences, adding an attentional mechanism.  
- Replaces softmax with a bidirectional LSTM (BLSTM) decoder that outputs full text lines instead of individual characters.  
- Reported result: substantial speed-up (claimed 20x–30x, likely in iterations) from using the BLSTM decoder.  
- Demonstrates improved recognition accuracy over prior methods.  
- Key contributions: the weighted collapse (attention) layer and BLSTM sentence-level decoder.  
- Contributions are logical extensions of prior work rather than surprising or groundbreaking.  
- Weaknesses: the speed-up claim is not adequately quantified, and the manuscript contains numerous typos needing correction.
