- Integrates an attention mechanism into offline handwriting recognition for unsegmented paragraphs using MD-LSTMs for feature extraction, attention, and sometimes decoding.  
- Learned attention outperforms ground-truth segmentation within the proposed system.  
- Achieves impressive character error rates; word error rates are competitive but not state-of-the-art given no ground-truth segmentation and a simple language model.  
- Strength: Extending single-line recognition techniques to multi-line is an elegant, logical progression and this work is more mature than related efforts.  
- Strength: The system functions robustly and performs well despite the task complexity.  
- Strength: Technical presentation is detailed and clear; colorized attention visualizations are particularly illustrative.  
- Improvement: Benchmark comparisons are unfair due to disparities in language-model complexity and segmentation; the discussion of these issues is underdeveloped.  
- Improvement: The paper lacks analysis explaining why learned attention outperforms ground-truth segmentation (e.g., annotation noise or contextual cues).  
- Improvement: Writing quality needs work—numerous typos, grammatical errors, tense inconsistencies—and the occasionally competitive tone weakens credibility.
