- End-to-end system: adds attention to Graves et al.'s single-line parser and replaces the softmax decoder with BiLSTMs; evaluated on Rimes and IAM, matching state-of-the-art on Rimes but underperforming on IAM.  
- Positive: commendable application of guided/region-based attention building on prior works (e.g., Xu et al.).  
- Exposition weak: paper is not self-contained and needs a more detailed description of the MD-LSTM model (section 3) so readers need not know Graves et al. [18].  
- Unexpected result: method outperforms approaches using ground-truth line labels—requires clarification and more implementation details of Graves et al.'s method; consider the effect of decoder RNN re-initialization.  
- Unsupported speedup claim: statement of 20–30× faster than Bluche et al. [6] lacks runtime benchmarks—provide actual runtime comparisons.  
- IAM performance concern: poorer results likely due to limited training data; suggest pretraining on combined other-dataset data then fine-tuning on IAM to test this hypothesis.  
- Attention failure on punctuation: authors should explain why the attention mechanism misses punctuation marks.  
- Overall: addressing the above issues will improve the paper’s clarity, rigor, and impact.
