- Proposes an integrated segmentation-and-transcription method for handwritten paragraph recognition built on multidimensional LSTM-RNNs.  
- Uses CTC for training and extends prior work by adding a weighted collapsing step that computes a weighted sum of feature vectors.  
- The weighted collapse acts as an attention mechanism that improves recognition.  
- Replaces per-character softmax with a bidirectional LSTM decoder to output whole text lines.  
- Reports a 20–30× speed-up in iteration count and improved recognition accuracy versus previous approaches.  
- Main contributions are the weighted collapse layer (enabling attention) and the BLSTM sentence-level decoder.  
- Novelty is limited—approach is a logical extension of existing work rather than a major leap.  
- Speed-up claim is not thoroughly quantified.  
- Manuscript contains numerous typos that must be corrected.
