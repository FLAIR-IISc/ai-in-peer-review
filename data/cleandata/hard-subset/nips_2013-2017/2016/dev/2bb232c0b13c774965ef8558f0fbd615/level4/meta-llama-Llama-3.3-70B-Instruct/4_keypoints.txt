- Proposes adapting the collapse layer of MD-LSTMs for handwriting recognition, building on prior work [18].  
- Similar to [6] in using character-level decoding per time step.  
- Replaces global average pooling with weighted average pooling (Eq. 2), where weights are learned.  
- Weights are produced by a recurrent attention layer (MD-LSTM) that uses previous weights and the encoded feature vector.  
- Uses CTC loss to compute transcription loss directly without character/line-level alignment.  
- Interprets the attention mechanism as a form of “soft” line segmentation.  
- Provides comparative experiments showing superior or comparable performance to other methods, including [6].  
- Main difference from [6]: decodes an entire line at each time step instead of a single character, yielding faster inference and better performance.  
- Reviewer uncertain about the novelty/technical contribution and requests clarification in the rebuttal.  
- Suggests visualizing attention when inputs have more lines than time steps to validate robustness and provide insights.
