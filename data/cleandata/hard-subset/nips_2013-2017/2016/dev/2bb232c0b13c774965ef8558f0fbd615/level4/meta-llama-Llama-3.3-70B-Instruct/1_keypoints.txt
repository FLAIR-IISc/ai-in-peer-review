- Proposes a comprehensive system for parsing multi-line handwritten text images, extending Graves et al.'s single-line parser with an attention mechanism and using Bidirectional LSTMs for decoding.  
- Evaluated on Rimes and IAM datasets: comparable to state-of-the-art on Rimes but underperforms on IAM.  
- Notable application of guided attention to locate sequences of handwritten lines, extending prior region-attention work (e.g., Xu et al.).  
- Paper needs a more detailed, self-contained explanation of the MD-LSTM model (similar to Graves et al.), especially in section 3.  
- The reported superiority over methods using ground-truth line labels (Table 2, Sec 5.3) may stem from differences in decoder RNN re-initialization; authors should fully describe their implementation of Graves et al.'s method.  
- The claimed 20–30× speedup over Bluche et al.'s character decoder lacks concrete timing comparisons to substantiate it.  
- The Rimes vs. IAM performance gap (possibly due to limited training data) should be probed via experiments such as training on combined data from the other dataset then fine-tuning on IAM.  
- The attention mechanism's failure to recognize punctuation needs a detailed analysis of causes and possible remedies.
