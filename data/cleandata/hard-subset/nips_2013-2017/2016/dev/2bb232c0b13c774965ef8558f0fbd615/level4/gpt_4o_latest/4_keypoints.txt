- Proposes replacing global average pooling in the MD-LSTM collapse layer with weighted average pooling (Eq. 2).  
- Weights are learned via a recurrent attention MD-LSTM that uses previous weights and the encoded feature vector.  
- Uses CTC loss to compute transcription loss directly, removing need for character- or line-level alignment.  
- Attention mechanism can be interpreted as a “soft” line segmentation.  
- Demonstrates better or comparable performance versus prior methods, including [6].  
- Method decodes an entire line at each time step (vs. single-character decoding in [6]), yielding improved speed and performance.  
- Reviewer questions whether the technical novelty over [6] is sufficient and asks for clarification in the rebuttal.  
- Suggests visualizing attention in cases where the number of image lines exceeds configured time steps to support claims that the method still performs well.  
- Situates work as a modification of the collapse layer originally introduced in [18] and closely aligned with [6].
