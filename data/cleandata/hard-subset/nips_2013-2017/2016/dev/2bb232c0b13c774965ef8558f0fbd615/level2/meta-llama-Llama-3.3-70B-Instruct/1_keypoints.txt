- Proposes a novel modification of MDLSTM-RNNs for end-to-end offline handwriting recognition of full paragraphs without explicit line segmentation.
- Main claim: model transcribes complete paragraphs competitively with state-of-the-art systems that use ground-truth line segmentation.
- Introduces an attention mechanism that iteratively focuses on single text lines, enabling implicit line segmentation.
- Supported by theoretical analysis and experiments on two public datasets (Rimes and IAM).
- Experimental results show large improvements over the baseline and better performance than explicit/automatic line-segmentation methods.
- Paper is well-written, ideas are clearly presented, and related work is thoroughly reviewed.
- Strength: novel and significant approach that advances paragraph-level handwriting recognition.
- Strength: thorough experimental validation demonstrating effectiveness.
- Weakness: model is limited to paragraphs and may not extend easily to full multi-page documents.
- Weakness: attention mechanism may fail with arbitrary or nonstandard reading orders.
- Weakness: requires a separate document layout analysis step to detect paragraphs before application.
- Recommendation: accept — strengths outweigh weaknesses, with suggested revisions to address limitations.
