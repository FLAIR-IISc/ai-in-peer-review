- Proposes a novel diffusion convolutional network (DCNN) for node-, edge-, and graph-level classification on graph-structured data.  
- Introduces a diffusion-convolution operation that maps node features to diffusion-process-derived representations.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on node classification tasks.  
- Only achieves comparable (not superior) performance on graph classification tasks.  
- Paper is well-written with clear formal definition, prediction/learning procedures, and experiments.  
- Notation and figures need improvement; a small running example would aid clarity.  
- Strength: novel combination of convolutional neural networks with graph diffusion.  
- Strength: thorough analysis of limitations, including scalability and locality concerns.  
- Weakness: no experimental results reported for edge classification tasks.  
- Weakness: limited performance on graph classification compared to node tasks.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of relationships to other graph neural networks, probabilistic relational models, and kernel methods.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, significant contribution; recommend acceptance if authors address noted limitations.
