- Proposes novel methods (REINFORCE and Bayesian optimization) to learn decision thresholds in the drift-diffusion model using single-trial rewards, with experiments showing convergence to near-optimal thresholds.

- Strength: novel approach capable of handling highly stochastic rewards and compares to animal learning data; REINFORCE matches animal acquisition curves.

- Strength: empirical results are promising and suggest reward-driven learning plausibility in perceptual decision making.

- Weakness: paper lacks a formal definition of “diffusion,” a core concept, and omits citations to prior work on the topic.

- Weakness: authors’ emphasis on generalizing convolutions may not apply to edge or vertex prediction settings, contradicting claims of a unified architecture.

- Weakness: no edge-prediction experiments provided despite claiming applicability to vertex, edge, and graph prediction.

- Weakness: comparison to baseline models (e.g., logistic regression, CRF) uses non-comparable features; reviewer recommends training a shallow model on the same signal for fairness.

- Weakness: lacks comparisons to other neural-network–based models (e.g., node2vec) and omits relevant citations.

- Presentation issue: paper is less clear, poorly organized, and lacks attention to detail.

- Relative impact: evaluated as lower quality than related work (LINE, DeepWalk, node2vec), supporting a strong argument for rejection.

- Overall recommendation: weaknesses and limitations outweigh strengths, leading to a recommendation to reject.
