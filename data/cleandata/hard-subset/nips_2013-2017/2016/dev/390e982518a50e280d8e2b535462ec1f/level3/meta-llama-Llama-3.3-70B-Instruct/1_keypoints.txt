- Proposes diffusion-convolutional neural networks (DCNNs) that combine multiple graph kernels with a diffusion-convolutional net to learn kernel weights from graph labels.
- Addresses an important open problem—learning kernels from graph labels—that prior work has not comprehensively tackled.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; introduction is motivating and related work is thorough.
- Technical contributions are significant; the DCNN model, diffusion-convolution operation, and learning procedure are clearly explained.
- Experiments are informative and comprehensive, evaluating both node and graph classification tasks.
- DCNNs perform strongly on node classification but show weaker performance on some graph classification experiments.
- Definitions of certain terms (e.g., power spectrum P* and hop) lack clarity and may confuse readers.
- Analysis of experimental results, particularly for graph classification, is limited and could be more detailed.
- Paper meets conference criteria for quality, originality, and significance; claims are supported by theory and experiments.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve term definitions and deepen result analysis.
