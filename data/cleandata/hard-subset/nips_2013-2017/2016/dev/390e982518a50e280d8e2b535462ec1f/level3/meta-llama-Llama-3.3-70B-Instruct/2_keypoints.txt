- Proposes diffusion-convolutional neural networks (DCNNs) for node, edge, and graph classification.
- Introduces a diffusion-convolution operation to learn representations that capture graph diffusion and local vicinity.
- Strong empirical performance on node classification, outperforming probabilistic relational models and kernel methods.
- Graph classification results are weak or not significantly better than baseline methods.
- Defines edge-classification models but provides no empirical results to support them.
- Comparison with baseline methods is questionable since baselines may not have had access to the same information.
- Fails to empirically compare against existing neural-network models for graphs despite discussing them in related work.
- Authors acknowledge scalability issues and potential inability to encode long-range spatial dependencies.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and technically sound with reproducible details.
- Experiments are thorough and results are well-presented; related work discussion is comprehensive.
- Strengths: novel model, clear presentation, thorough experiments, promising node-classification results.
- Weaknesses: limited/uneven baseline comparisons, lack of edge results, scalability and long-range dependency concerns.
- Pro-acceptance: novel approach, good node results, clarity, and experimental thoroughness.
- Con-acceptance: questionable comparisons, unimpressive graph-classification performance, missing edge experiments, scalability limitations.
