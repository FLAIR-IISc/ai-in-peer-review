- Proposes a novel approach to learning decision thresholds in the drift-diffusion model using single-trial rewards.  
- Introduces two optimization methods: REINFORCE and Bayesian optimization.  
- Uses Wald's trial-averaged cost function to derive single-trial rewards.  
- Thorough analysis of each method’s strengths and weaknesses (convergence rates, variability, computational costs).  
- REINFORCE shown to be a plausible model of animal learning and consistent with rodent and bat data.  
- Bayesian optimization achieves performance but is computationally expensive.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and logically organized.  
- Results are presented clearly and supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Includes a detailed comparison with experimental animal learning studies, increasing relevance.  
- Bridges theoretical decision-making models and empirical animal data effectively.  
- Focus is primarily on the drift-diffusion model, leaving generalization to other models unclear.  
- Bayesian optimization may be unsuitable for large-scale applications due to cost.  
- Paper could benefit from more discussion of limitations and possible extensions.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing limitations and future extensions.
