- Introduces Diffusion-Convolutional Neural Networks (DCNNs) for node, edge, and graph classification on graphs.  
- Highlights DCNN advantages: isomorphism invariance, computational efficiency, and flexibility for various graph tasks.  
- Strong empirical performance on node classification, outperforming several baselines.  
- Underwhelming performance on graph-level classification tasks.  
- Provides detailed theoretical foundations and analysis of computational efficiency.  
- Scalability to medium-sized graphs and ability to encode structural and feature information.  
- Node classification comparisons may be biased because baselines were not given equivalent information.  
- Edge classification model defined but no empirical results reported.  
- Lacks comparison to other graph neural networks (e.g., GCNs), limiting evaluation of originality and significance.  
- Contribution unclear in light of prior work (e.g., diffusion kernels), suggesting limited novelty.  
- Paper is generally well-written, but Figure 1 is too small and related-work discussion should be improved.  
- Pros summarized: strong node results, well-motivated/theoretically sound model, efficient GPU implementation.  
- Cons summarized: poor graph classification performance, potential bias in comparisons, missing edge experiments, insufficient GNN baselines, unclear contribution.  
- Recommendation: weak reject; authors should provide fairer comparisons, include edge classification results, and benchmark against other GNNs for resubmission.
