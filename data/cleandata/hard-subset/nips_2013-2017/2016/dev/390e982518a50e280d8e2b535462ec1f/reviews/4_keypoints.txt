- Proposes a new diffusion convolutional network (DCNN) for node-, edge-, and graph-level classification on graph-structured data.
- Core operation maps node features to the results of a diffusion process starting at those nodes.
- Parameters are tied by diffusion/search depth rather than by position as in standard CNNs.
- Theoretical result: DCNN activations are invariant to node indexing (isomorphic graphs yield identical representations).
- Empirical results: state-of-the-art performance on node classification tasks.
- Empirical results: comparable (but not superior) performance on graph classification tasks.
- No experimental results reported for edge classification despite claiming edge-level capability.
- Presentation is generally good but notation and Figures 1(a–c) could be clearer; a small running-graph example would help.
