- Proposes a novel diffusion-based neural network (DCNN) for classification on generic graphs that leverages multi-hop architecture, node attributes, and graph structure.  
- Empirically outperforms several simple baselines on average but appears to have relatively poor scalability.  
- Incorporating graph diffusion into neural networks is an interesting and novel idea, and the paper motivates the problem well.  
- Scalability concern: three separate models (node, graph, edge) are proposed but no empirical results for edge classification are reported—possibly due to huge parameter size (eq. 6).  
- Scalability concern: authors should report runtimes for current experiments to assess practical scalability.  
- Experimental comparisons may be unfair: DCNN uses node features plus structure while most baselines use only structure, so gains may stem from extra input information.  
- Recommend reporting performance of DCNN using only structural inputs (purely structural variants 101–104) to make comparisons meaningful.  
- Missing baselines: recent neural graph architectures that extend CNNs to graphs were not included, making it hard to judge whether DCNN is state-of-the-art.  
- Relationship to CNN is unclear: paper claims DCNN extends CNN, but it’s not shown how DCNN subsumes standard CNN as a special case (cites Bruna/Henaff).
