- Proposes a novel neural network architecture for graph prediction, framed as a generalization of convolution beyond grids.  
- Shows improved performance over several kernel methods, especially on vertex classification, but not on graph classification.  
- Experimental results are promising but the presentation and experiments have significant concerns.  
- Core concept "diffusion" is not formally defined or cited, leaving a critical omission in the presentation.  
- Claim of generalizing convolutions is overstated—spatial invariance is unclear for edge/vertex prediction settings.  
- No edge-prediction experiments despite claims the architecture unifies vertex, edge, and graph prediction; no comment on structure learning.  
- Baselines are inadequately controlled: logistic regression and CRF lack comparable neighbor/diffusion-based features, so comparisons are unfair.  
- Authors must train shallow models on the same diffusion-based features to demonstrate the neural model’s advantage in accuracy or speed.  
- Missing comparisons to recent neural-network graph embedding methods (e.g., node2vec, DeepWalk, LINE) and omission of citations is unacceptable.  
- Reviewer downgraded after authors’ response; believes prior work has stronger experiments and that this paper’s quality is lower than those baselines.  
- Strengths noted: architecture is elegant and paper is well-written aside from the diffusion omission.  
- Overall assessment: experiments not convincing; paper is borderline-to-reject unless authors address methodological and bibliographic shortcomings.
