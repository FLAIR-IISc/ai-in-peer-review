The research paper proposes a new neural network model for classification tasks on generic graphs, which is an extension of CNN. The model explicitly considers graph expansion through a multi-hop architecture and is capable of utilizing both node attributes and graph structure during training. Although the model exceeds several common baselines on average, it appears to suffer from relatively poor scalability. The idea of incorporating graph expansion into the neural network seems both interesting and novel. The authors have also done a good job in explaining the problem. However, overall, I think a few aspects of the work could be improved: Scalability: 1. The authors have proposed three separate models for classification of nodes, graphs, and edges. However, no empirical results have been reported for edge classification. I wondered if this was due to a scalability problem, as the parameter size for edge classification can be extremely large even for medium-sized graphs (according to eq (6)). 2. It would be helpful to include the current test run times. Regarding the test settings: 1. The test comparisons seem unfair. While the CNN uses both node attributes and graph structure, at least 4 of the 5 baselines (l1-logistic, l2-logistic, KED, KLED) are entirely dependent on the graph structure as input, making it difficult to determine whether the CNN's performance increase is due to a good model design or simply due to additional side information. To make the comparison more meaningful, the performance of at least the fully structured CNN (101-104) should be reported. 2. Section 5 mentions several recent neural architectures for graph-based learning, which are similar in concept to CNN's extension in irregular graph domains. However, none of those models were included as baselines for the empirical comparison. Without a strong baseline, it is difficult to judge whether the proposed CNN is actually achieving state-of-the-art performance (or close to it). Relationship with CNN: Unlike existing works that explicitly generalize the convolution operator on 2D-grids [1,2], it is unclear whether the proposed CNN in this work can be seen as an extension of CNN (as the authors claim), as it is not clear how the CNN will incorporate CNN as a special case. [1] Bruna, Joan, et al. "Spectral Networks and Locally Connected Networks on Graphs." arXiv preprint arXiv:1312.6203 (2013). [2] Henaff, Michael, Joan Bruna, and Ian Lequn. "Deep Convolutional Networks on Graph-Structured Data." arXiv preprint arXiv:1506.05163 (2015).
