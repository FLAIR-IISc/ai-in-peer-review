- Introduces a novel graph neural architecture claimed as a generalization of convolution, showing strong vertex-classification results but weaker graph-classification performance.
- The concept of diffusion, central to the method, is not formally defined or cited, leaving a critical methodological gap.
- Claiming a convolution generalization is overstated since spatial invariance of convolution is not evident for edge/vertex prediction and the benefit appears limited to vertex classification.
- No experiments or discussion provided for edge prediction despite claims of unified support, and applicability to structure learning is not addressed.
- Baseline comparisons are flawed: logistic regression only used vertex features (not neighbor/diffusion features), and CRF featureization is unspecified, making results unfair.
- Given the use of up to 5-hop diffusion, baselines (LR/CRF) should be provided equivalent multi-hop features for a meaningful comparison.
- The paper omits comparisons and citations to other neural graph methods (e.g., node2vec, LINE, DeepWalk), which undermines the experimental comprehensiveness.
- After author response, reviewer found the omission of related work and weaker experimental quality unacceptable, leading to a downgraded evaluation.
- Overall: elegant and well-written but experimental and citation deficiencies make the evaluation unconvincing; reviewer recommends rejection.
