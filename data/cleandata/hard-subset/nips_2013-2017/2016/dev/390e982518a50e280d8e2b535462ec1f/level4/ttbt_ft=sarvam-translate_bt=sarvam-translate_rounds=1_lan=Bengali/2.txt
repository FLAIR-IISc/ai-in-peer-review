This paper proposes a neural network model for node, edge, and graph classification problems based on integrating local environment information from each node in the graph. They achieved good results in node classification but not in graph classification. This paper presents a reasonable model for node classification that uses local information to improve performance. However, it was difficult for me to evaluate the quality because I am not an expert in this field: 1. The authors achieved good results in node classification but not in graph classification. Even in the case of node classification results, it seemed that most were not exposed to the same information as the baseline method, so it is difficult to say whether the difference in performance is due to that or the model itself. 2. The authors mention in related work that there are other works on using neural network models for graphs, but they do not compare them anywhere. Why? 3. The authors defined the model for edge classification but there are no empirical results. Why? In conclusion, the authors presented a reasonable model for using neural networks for graphs, but as an expert, it was difficult to understand how much contribution it made compared to existing literature.
