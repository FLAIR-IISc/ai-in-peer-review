This paper proposes a neural network model for node, edge, and graph classification problems based on integrating information from the local environment of each node within the graph. They achieve good results for node classification but not for graph classification. This paper presents a reasonable model for node classification that uses local information to improve performance. However, due to a lack of expertise in this area, it was difficult for me to assess the quality for several reasons: 1. The authors achieve good results for node classification but not for graph classification. However, even for node classification results, it seems that most baselines are not exposed to the same information as the method, so it is difficult to tell whether the difference in performance is due to that or the model. 2. The authors mention in related works that there are other tasks for using neural network models for graphs, but they do not compare them anywhere, why? 3. The authors have defined the model for edge classification but there are no empirical results. Why? In conclusion, the authors present a reasonable model for using neural networks for graphs, but due to a lack of expertise, it was difficult to understand the extent of contribution compared to existing literature.
