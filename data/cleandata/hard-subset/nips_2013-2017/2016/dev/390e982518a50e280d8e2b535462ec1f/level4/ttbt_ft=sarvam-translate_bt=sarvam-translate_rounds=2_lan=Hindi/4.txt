The authors propose a new convolutional neural network (CNN)-based model called Diffusion Convolutional Network (DCCN) for classifying graph-structured data at the level of nodes, edges, and the entire graph. The fundamental operation in DCCN is a mapping from the nodes and their properties to the results of a diffusion process starting from those nodes. Unlike standard CNNs, DCCN parameters are bound to the search depth rather than their position in the grid. The authors theoretically establish that DCCN activations of two isomorphic input graphs are identical, meaning the DCCN representation is invariant with respect to node indices. The authors compare the DCCN model on node and graph classification tasks with other models, reporting state-of-the-art performance on the former and comparable performance on the latter. The authors propose a novel model called Diffusion Convolutional Network (DCCN) for classifying graph-structured data at the level of nodes, edges, and the entire graph. The model is impressive in that it achieves state-of-the-art performance on node classification tasks. However, it does not perform as well on graph classification tasks. The authors do not present any results for applying the DCCN model to edge classification tasks. The authors have done a good job of explaining their model, but they could have been more explicit by using a running example with notation and a small graph in Figure 1 (a-c).
