- Proposed a novel convolutional neural network, the diffusion convolutional network (DCNN), for node-, edge-, and graph-level classification on graphs.  
- DCNN core operation maps nodes and their features to outcomes of a diffusion process initiated at those nodes.  
- Parameters are tied by search depth rather than by spatial position, unlike traditional CNNs.  
- The authors prove that DCNN activations are identical for isomorphic input graphs, giving invariance to node indexing.  
- Empirical evaluation: achieves state-of-the-art on node classification and comparable performance on graph classification.  
- Missing evaluation results for edge classification despite claiming support for it.  
- Presentation critique: notation and Figure 1(a–c) lack clarity; a small running graph example would help.
