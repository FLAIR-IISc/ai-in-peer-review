The authors have proposed a new convolutional neural network (CNN) based model called Diffusion Convolutional Network (DCNN) for classifying graph-structured data at the node, edge, and complete graph levels. The fundamental mechanism of a DCNN is the mapping of the results of a diffusion process starting from the nodes and their attributes to those nodes. Unlike standard CNNs, DCNN parameters are bound by search depth instead of their location in the grid. The authors theoretically proved that the DCNN activation of two isomorphic input graphs is the same, i.e., the DCNN representation is invariant with respect to node indices. The authors compared the DCNN model with other models in node and graph classification tasks and reported state-of-the-art performance in the former and comparable performance in the latter. The authors have proposed a new model called Diffusion Convolutional Network (DCNN) for classifying graph-structured data at the node, edge, and complete graph levels. The model is impressive in that it achieves state-of-the-art performance in node classification tasks. However, it does not perform as well in graph classification tasks. The authors did not present any results of applying the DCNN model in edge classification tasks. The authors have done a good job of explaining their model, but they could have been clearer with the notation and Figure 1 (a-c) by using a smaller graph as a running example.
