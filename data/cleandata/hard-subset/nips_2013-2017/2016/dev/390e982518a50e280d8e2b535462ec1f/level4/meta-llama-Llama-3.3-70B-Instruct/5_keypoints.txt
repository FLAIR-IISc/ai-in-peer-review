- Proposes a novel neural network architecture for graph classification that incorporates graph diffusion via a multi-hop design.  
- Effectively leverages both node attributes and graph structure during training.  
- Demonstrates superior average performance compared to several simple baselines.  
- Scalability concern: edge-classification results are not reported, possibly due to large parameter sizes (eq. 6) making medium-scale graphs infeasible.  
- Scalability concern: runtimes for experiments are not reported, leaving practical efficiency unclear.  
- Experimental comparison may be unfair: baselines use only graph structure while the proposed model uses both features and structure—need results for a purely structural version (eqs.101–104).  
- Missing strong baselines: recent graph-CNN/neural architectures (e.g., Bruna et al., Henaff et al.) are not included, so state-of-the-art claims are unsupported.  
- Conceptual clarity issue: unclear how the model genuinely extends CNNs or generalizes the convolution operator on grids, and it’s not shown to subsume CNN as a special case.  
- Motivation and idea of integrating graph diffusion into neural networks are interesting and well-motivated, but several methodological and experimental gaps remain.
