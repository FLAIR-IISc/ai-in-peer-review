The research paper proposes a new neural network model for classification tasks on generic graphs, which is an extension of CNN. The model explicitly considers graph expansion through a multi-hop architecture and is capable of using both node attributes and graph structures during training. Although the model exceeds several common baselines on average, it seems to suffer from relatively poor scalability. The idea of incorporating graph expansion into neural networks seems both interesting and novel. The authors have also done a good job of explaining the problem. However, overall, I think a few aspects of the work could be improved: Scalability: 1. The authors have proposed three separate models for classification of nodes, graphs, and edges. However, no empirical results have been reported for edge classification. I wondered if this was due to a scalability problem, as the parameter size for edge classification can be extremely large even for moderately sized graphs (according to eq (6)). 2. It would be helpful to include the current test run times. Regarding the test settings: 1. The test comparisons seem unfair. Although CNN uses both node attributes and graph structures, at least 4 of the 5 baselines (l1-logistic, l2-logistic, KED, KLED) are entirely dependent on the graph structure as input, which makes it difficult to determine whether the CNN's performance increase is due to good model design or simply due to additional side information. To make the comparison more meaningful, the performance of at least the fully structured CNNs (101-104) should be reported. 2. Section 5 mentions several recent neural architectures for graph-based learning, which are similar to the concept of CNN extensions in irregular graph domains. However, none of those models were included as baselines for the empirical comparison. Without a strong baseline, it is difficult to judge whether the proposed CNN is actually achieving (or approaching) state-of-the-art performance. Relationship with CNN: Unlike existing works that explicitly generalize the convolution operator to 2D-grids [1,2], it is unclear whether the CNN proposed in this work can be seen as an extension of CNN (as the authors claim), as it is unclear how CNN will incorporate CNN as a special case. [1] Bruna, Joan, et al. "Spectral Networks and Locally Connected Networks on Graphs." arXiv preprint arXiv:1312.6203 (2013). [2] Henaff, Michael, Joan Bruna, and Ian Lequn. "Deep Convolution Networks on Graph-Structured Data." arXiv preprint arXiv:1506.05163 (2015).
