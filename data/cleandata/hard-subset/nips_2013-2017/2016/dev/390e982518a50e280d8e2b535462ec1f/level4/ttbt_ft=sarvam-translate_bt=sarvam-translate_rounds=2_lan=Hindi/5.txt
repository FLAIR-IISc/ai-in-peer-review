This paper proposes a new neural network model as an extension of CNNs for classification tasks on regular graphs. This model explicitly considers graph propagation through a multi-hop architecture and is capable of leveraging both node attributes and graph structures during training. While the model performs better than several simple baselines on average, it seems to suffer from relatively poor scalability. The idea of incorporating graph propagation into neural networks seems both interesting and novel. The authors have also done a good job of motivating the problem. However, overall, I feel that many aspects of the work could be improved further: Scalability: 1. The authors proposed three different models for node, graph, and edge classification. However, no empirical performance of edge classification was reported. I was wondering if this is due to scalability issues because the parameter size for edge classification can be too large even for medium-sized graphs (according to eq (6)). 2. It would be helpful to include the run times of the current experiments. Regarding the experiment settings: 1. The experiment comparison seems inappropriate. While the CNN uses both node features and graph structures, at least 4 out of 5 baselines (l1-Logistic, l2-Logistic, KED, KLED) are entirely dependent on graph structures as their input, it is uncertain whether the CNN's performance advantage is due to a good model design or simply due to additional side information. To make the comparison more meaningful, the performance of at least purely structural CNNs (101-104) should be reported. 2. Section 5 mentions several recent neural architectures for graph-based learning that are based on the same idea of extending CNNs to irregular graph domains. However, none of these models were included as baselines for empirical comparison. Without strong baselines, it is difficult to assess whether the proposed CNN is actually achieving state-of-the-art performance (or close to it). Relationship with CNNs: Unlike existing works that explicitly generalize the convolution operator to a 2D-grid [1,2], it is not clear whether the proposed CNN in this work can be seen as an extension of CNNs (as claimed by the authors), because it is not clear how CNNs will incorporate CNNs as a special case.
