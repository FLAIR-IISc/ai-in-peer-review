- Proposes a new neural network (DCNN) for classification on generic graphs, claimed as an extension of CNNs.  
- Explicitly models graph diffusion via a multi-hop architecture and leverages both node attributes and graph structure.  
- Provides strong motivation and an innovative integration of graph diffusion into neural networks.  
- Reports superior average performance compared to several simple baselines.  
- Scalability concerns: three models (node, graph, edge) are introduced but no empirical results for edge classification, suggesting parameter-size/scalability issues (see Eq.6).  
- Experiments lack runtime metrics; reporting them would clarify scalability.  
- Experimental fairness issue: DCNN uses node features while most baselines use only graph structure—need a purely structural DCNN baseline to isolate gains from side information.  
- Missing empirical comparisons to recent graph-CNN neural architectures; without strong baselines it’s hard to assess competitiveness or state-of-the-art performance.  
- Unclear theoretical relationship to CNNs: the paper does not show how DCNN subsumes or explicitly generalizes convolution on 2D grids.
