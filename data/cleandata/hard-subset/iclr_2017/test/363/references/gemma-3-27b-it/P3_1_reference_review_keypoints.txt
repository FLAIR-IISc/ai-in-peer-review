- Evaluates the compare-aggregate framework across MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI, finding element-wise SUB and MULT comparison functions perform best; code released.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions—solid empirical study with incremental but valuable insights.  
- Strong empirical results: competitive or state-of-the-art performance on all four datasets; SUB and MULT consistently strong.  
- Systematic evaluation: rigorous comparison of six comparison functions, including intuitive element-wise operations.  
- Well-motivated: addresses limits of single-vector representations and the overlooked role of the comparison function.  
- Relevance: findings are valuable for the broad NLP sequence-matching community.  
- Clear presentation: well-written paper with informative figures and tables.  
- Ablation study could be deeper—investigate interactions between preprocessing layer and attention, including per-dataset removals.

- Report hyperparameter sensitivity, especially hidden dimensionality (l) and optimizer choices.
