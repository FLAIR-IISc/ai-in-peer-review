- Paper evaluates a general "compare-aggregate" framework for sequence matching across QA and entailment tasks.  
- Systematically compares different comparison functions and highlights element-wise subtraction and multiplication as effective choices.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI.  
- Authors release code, improving reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Approach is well-motivated, filling a gap in systematic evaluation of compare-aggregate methods.  
- Strong empirical results show the simple SUB and MULT functions often outperform more complex baselines.  
- Experimental setup is comprehensive with details on datasets, hyperparameters, and baselines.  
- Ablation studies are included but brief and could be expanded.  
- Paper lacks deeper analysis explaining why SUB and MULT work well (e.g., visualizations or representation analysis).  
- Limitations aren’t explicitly discussed (e.g., performance on very long sequences or complex reasoning tasks).  
- Preprocessing layer’s role and learned gate behavior need clearer explanation.  
- Statistical significance testing of results is missing and should be added.  
- Open questions for authors: theoretical motivation for SUB/MULT, details/patterns of preprocessing gate values, scaling to longer sequences, and rationale for using CNNs versus alternatives.
