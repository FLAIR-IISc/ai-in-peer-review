- Investigates the compare-aggregate framework for sequence matching across MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI.  
- Systematically evaluates six comparison functions and finds element-wise subtraction (SUB) and multiplication (MULT) perform best.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance, often beating state-of-the-art, and provides ablation studies.  
- Authors release code to support reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Clear motivation and well-defined problem framing for sequence matching.  
- Thorough evaluation across four diverse datasets showing generalizability.  
- Simple element-wise operations outperform more complex methods like neural tensor networks.  
- Ablation studies offer insight into component importance.  
- Code release is a valuable contribution for reproducibility.  
- Suggest deeper analysis of why SUB and MULT work (e.g., visualizing representations, dimension importance).  
- Request more comprehensive hyperparameter sensitivity analysis across datasets.  
- Recommend reporting statistical significance for reported improvements.  
- Ask for discussion on why SNLI results are competitive but not state-of-the-art and possible dataset-specific fixes.  
- Clarify why LSTM preprocessing was used only for SNLI and whether it was detrimental elsewhere.  
- Suggest adding theoretical justification relating element-wise ops to semantic similarity or distance metrics.  
- Question: ask for intuition/hypotheses on SUB and MULT success.  
- Question: did authors try other regularization (L1/L2) beyond dropout?  
- Question: were SNLI-specific modifications (e.g., bi-directional attention) explored?  
- Question: request details on computational cost and memory usage of different comparison functions.  
- Question: ask about applicability to other sequence matching tasks beyond those studied.  
- Overall appraisal: well-written, insightful, and a valuable empirical contribution; addressing suggestions would strengthen impact.
