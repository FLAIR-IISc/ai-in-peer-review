- Paper claims the compare-aggregate framework is broadly applicable and that simple element-wise SUB and MULT comparison functions outperform more complex methods across tasks.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions due to solid empirical study and clear results.
- Clear problem definition and motivation: highlights lack of cross-dataset evaluation and attention to the comparison function.
- Sound methodology: well-defined experimental setup across four diverse datasets with appropriate baselines and some ablation studies.
- Convincing results: SUB and MULT consistently outperform other comparison functions, with consistent (though not extensively tested) significance; CNN attention visualizations provide intuition.
- Reproducibility: code is provided, enhancing reproducibility.
- Request to report statistical significance: include p-values or confidence intervals for reported performance differences.
- Ablation study expansion: need more detailed ablations isolating preprocessing (LSTM) and attention impacts.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: analyze sensitivity beyond dropout (e.g., other hyperparameters and their effects).
- Discuss negative results: explain why some comparison functions (e.g., NTN, cosine) perform worse and theoretical limitations.
- Related work expansion: compare and contrast with recent transformer-based sequence matching methods (e.g., BERT).
- Questions for authors: provide statistical tests, intuitions for why SUB/MULT capture semantics better than learned networks, scalability comparisons with complex methods, and details on hyperparameter tuning challenges and sensitivities.
