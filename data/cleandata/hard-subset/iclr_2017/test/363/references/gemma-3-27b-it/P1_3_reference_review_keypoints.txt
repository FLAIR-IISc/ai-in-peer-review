- Paper evaluates the compare-aggregate framework across multiple NLP tasks and finds element-wise SUB and MULT outperform neural and cosine baselines; attention visualizations support the model.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions for a solid empirical contribution and practical insight.  
- Clear problem definition and strong motivation for focusing on the comparison function.  
- Strong empirical results: SUB and MULT consistently outperform alternatives across MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI.  
- Rigorous experimentation: systematic evaluation of six comparison functions while controlling other model aspects.  
- Reproducibility: authors released code to facilitate verification and follow-up work.  
- Insightful analysis: attention visualizations, though limited, help explain model behavior.  
- Request for theoretical justification: explain why SUB and MULT work so well (e.g., info-theoretic or geometric reasoning).  
- Ablation study needs more detail on exact configurations and their impacts.  
- Clarify dataset-specific model choices and whether dataset properties favor certain comparison functions or architectures.  
- Add hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, particularly for hidden dimensionality (l).  
- Include qualitative error analysis to reveal model limitations and guide future work.  
- Question: Why are element-wise operations so effective—simplicity, efficiency, or embedding structure?  
- Question: Did the authors try order-sensitive preprocessing (e.g., RNNs) on all datasets and how did it affect results and cost?  
- Question: Why is SNLI performance competitive but not state-of-the-art, and can tuning or architecture changes help?  
- Question: Provide computational cost comparisons (training/inference) among comparison functions, especially SUB/MULT vs NTN.
