- Paper proposes a compare-aggregate pipeline: gating preprocessing, one-way attention, per-word comparison f(aj,hj), and CNN aggregation.
- Core contribution: systematic evaluation of six comparison functions (SUB, MULT, SUBMULT+NN, NN, NTN, EUCCOS).
- Empirical scope: evaluated on SNLI, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, MovieQA; strong/SOTA on three tasks and competitive on SNLI.
- Main finding: simple element-wise comparisons (SUB, MULT) and SUBMULT+NN are robust, parameter-light, and effective.
- Decision: Weak accept.
- Strength: Well-motivated isolation of the comparison function with broad cross-task empirical validation.
- Practical value: Demonstrates strong baselines that are simple and efficient compared to heavier parameterized comparators.
- Novelty concern: Conceptual advance is incremental — architecture closely resembles prior compare-aggregate/decomposable-attention work.
- Missing methodological details: CNN hyperparameters, attention softmax axis, and exact SNLI LSTM/preprocessing description are under-specified.
- Rigor concerns: No reporting of variance or statistical significance, limited hyperparameter tuning discussion, and unclear fairness of literature baselines.
- Efficiency/scale: No parameter counts or runtime comparisons to support claims about SUB/MULT being a “good compromise.”
- Design-choice justification: Use of unidirectional attention, CNN aggregation, and order-insensitive gating is not sufficiently justified or ablated.
- Ablation gaps: Need comparisons to bi-directional attention, RNN/Transformer aggregation, and pooling baselines to isolate sources of gains.
- Analysis gaps: Current visual/qualitative analyses lack systematic error stratification (lexical overlap, negation, length, OOV).
- Suggested reporting: Provide means/std over multiple seeds, statistical tests, parameter/runtime counts, and matched in-house baselines.
- Suggested experiments: Test bi-attention, different aggregators, contextual encoders (BiLSTM/CNN/Transformer) before attention, and ablate gating.
- Clarification requests: Are embeddings frozen or fine-tuned across tasks; how sensitive are results to embedding tuning; did you try bi-attention?
- Specific questions: Which axis is softmax applied over in G; CNN filter widths/number of filters; do rankings of f change with different aggregators?
- Fairness note: Ensure baselines are matched for embedding tuning/features/training regimes or provide controlled re-implementations.
