- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for matching text sequences and evaluates it across four NLP tasks.  
- Systematically evaluates six word-level comparison functions, introducing SUB and MULT and finding SUBMULT+NN performs best.  
- Recommends accepting the paper.  
- Fills a literature gap by demonstrating the framework's generalizability across diverse sequence-matching problems.  
- Shows SUB and MULT (element-wise) are novel, interpretable, and often outperform NN/NTN and distance metrics; MULT is best on WikiQA.  
- Experimental setup is robust with appropriate baselines, ablation studies, and visualizations; achieves state-of-the-art or competitive results.
