- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for sequence matching and evaluates it across four tasks (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI).  
- Systematically compares six comparison functions and introduces element-wise SUB and MULT, with SUBMULT+NN performing best overall.  
- Recommendation: weak acceptance (borderline); useful empirical contribution but limited novelty and experimental rigor.  
- Strength: Empirical validation across four diverse tasks extends prior work that focused on one or two tasks.  
- Strength: Systematic evaluation of six comparison functions offers practical insights for future research.  
- Strength: Simple element-wise operations (SUB, MULT) perform surprisingly well compared to more complex neural methods.  
- Results: Outperforms baselines on three datasets and is competitive on the fourth.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty—SUB and MULT were used previously (Mou et al., 2016) and extension beyond that is unclear.  
- Weakness: Unfair function comparisons—different parameter counts (NN/NTN vs. parameter-free SUB/MULT) are not normalized.  
- Weakness: Insufficient implementation details hinder reproducibility (hyperparameters, exact CNN/preprocessing specs).  
- Weakness: Claim of generality is questionable because task-specific implementation variations (e.g., MovieQA) exist.  
- Feedback: Authors should explicitly state how their work extends prior use of SUB and MULT and what new insights were gained.  
- Feedback: Compare functions while accounting for parameter counts (e.g., performance vs. parameter-count table).  
- Feedback: Strengthen ablation analysis by explaining why preprocessing/attention help and when they matter (e.g., sequence length differences).  
- Feedback: Provide full implementation details per dataset (CNN window sizes, filters, dropout, preprocessing implementations).  
- Feedback: Explain how contextual/word-order information is captured if preprocessing is order-insensitive.  
- Question: How does this work specifically extend Mou et al. (2016) given their use of SUB and MULT?  
- Question: How do authors justify comparing functions without accounting for model complexity differences?  
- Question: How can the model be made truly general without task-specific adaptations?  
- Question: Is the MovieQA performance gap between EUCCOS and SUBMULT+NN due to vector dimensionality or a deeper representational difference?  
- Conclusion: Empirically valuable contribution that needs clearer novelty claims and stronger experimental rigor; weak accept recommended pending revisions.
