- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for matching text sequences, evaluated across four datasets.  
- Two main contributions: demonstrates framework effectiveness on MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI; systematically evaluates six word-level comparison functions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Strength: Robust experimental design across diverse sequence matching tasks.  
- Strength: Systematic comparison with clear rationale for six comparison functions.  
- Key empirical finding: Simple element-wise operations (SUB and MULT) outperform more complex neural comparison functions.  
- Practical insight: Importance of preprocessing and attention layers varies by task; attention is more important when sequence length differs greatly.  
- Missing: No statistical significance testing reported for performance improvements.  
- Missing: No discussion of computational efficiency across comparison functions.  
- Revision request: Clarify novelty relative to prior use of SUB and MULT (e.g., Mou et al. 2016) within this framework.  
- Revision request: Add statistical significance testing (e.g., t-tests) for reported gains.  
- Revision request: Include computational efficiency analysis to aid practical adoption.  
- Revision request: Expand ablation study to explain why certain comparison functions excel on specific tasks.  
- Revision request: Discuss relevance/integration with more recent transformer/BERT-based models.  
- Question: Explain how word-by-word comparison compensates for an order-insensitive preprocessing layer.  
- Question: Discuss trade-offs between model complexity and performance given SUB/MULT's parameter-free success on WikiQA.  
- Question: Provide mathematical intuition for why SUB preserves dimensionality information and helps matching.  
- Question: Supply concrete visual examples supporting the claim that attention matters more for tasks with big length differences.  
- Overall: Strong, empirically valuable paper whose minor revisions would improve clarity and practical utility.
