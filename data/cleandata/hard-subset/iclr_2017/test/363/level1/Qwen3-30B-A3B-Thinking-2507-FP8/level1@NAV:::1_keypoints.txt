- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for sequence matching and evaluates it across multiple NLP tasks.  
- Systematically compares six word-level comparison functions on four datasets (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI).  
- Introduces element-wise comparison functions (SUB and MULT) that outperform more complex neural-network-based methods.  
- Strong recommendation to accept based on novelty and empirical results.  
- Fills a literature gap by showing the framework’s general applicability across diverse sequence-matching tasks.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and convincing, demonstrating simple element-wise ops can beat standard NN approaches.  
- Ablation studies show the relative importance of preprocessing vs. attention layers varies by task.  
- Suggests further analysis on why SUB and MULT work so well (relation to Euclidean distance, information preservation).  
- Recommends adding computational-complexity comparisons of the different comparison functions for practical deployment.  
- Asks authors to clarify what aspects of information SUB/MULT preserve that make them superior to NN/NTN methods.
