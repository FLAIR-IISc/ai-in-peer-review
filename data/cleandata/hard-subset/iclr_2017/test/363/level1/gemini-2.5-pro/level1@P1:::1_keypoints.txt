- Proposes a "compare-aggregate" neural framework that does word-level comparisons between sequences and aggregates them with a CNN for final predictions.  
- Applies the single architecture to SNLI, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and MovieQA, achieving state-of-the-art or competitive results.  
- Systematically evaluates six comparison functions and finds simple element-wise functions (SUB, MULT) outperform complex parameterized models (NN, NTN).  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Strong empirical validation and generality shown by consistent performance across four distinct NLP tasks.  
- Core novelty: insightful, systematic analysis of the comparison function showing the effectiveness of preserving per-dimension difference/similarity signals.  
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly written, with claims strongly supported by experiments.  
- Unique contribution relative to concurrent work is the focused study of the comparison component within a general architecture.  
- Table 3: SUBMULT+NN attains new SOTA on three datasets and SUB/MULT beat NN/NTN; Table 4 ablation shows attention and preprocessing are beneficial.  
- Suggest discussing limitations of the order-insensitive preprocessing and detailing how LSTM was used for SNLI (and comparing versions).  
- Recommend justifying the one-directional attention choice versus symmetric attention.  
- Advise clarifying Figure 2 visualizations—specify what values represent and what high activations indicate.  
- Questions: explain LSTM integration in preprocessing for SNLI and its impact; analyze relative contributions of SUB vs MULT in SUBMULT+NN across tasks; hypothesize why EUCCOS is strong on MovieQA despite its low-dimensional signal.
