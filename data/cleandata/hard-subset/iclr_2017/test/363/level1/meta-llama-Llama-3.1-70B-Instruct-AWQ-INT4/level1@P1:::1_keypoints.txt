- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for text sequence matching with preprocessing, attention, comparison, and CNN aggregation layers.  
- Focuses on the comparison layer and introduces six comparison functions (element-wise ops, NNs, neural tensor networks).  
- Evaluated on four datasets (including QA and textual entailment), achieving state-of-the-art on three and competitive on the fourth.  
- Finds simple element-wise comparison functions can outperform more complex neural-network-based functions.
