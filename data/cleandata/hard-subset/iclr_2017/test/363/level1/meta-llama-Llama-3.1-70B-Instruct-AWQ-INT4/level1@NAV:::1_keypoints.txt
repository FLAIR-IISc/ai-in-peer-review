- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for text sequence matching with four layers: preprocessing, attention, comparison, and aggregation.  
- Emphasizes the comparison function and introduces six options, including uncommon element-wise operations.  
- Evaluated on four datasets spanning tasks (question answering, textual entailment) and achieves state-of-the-art on three and competitive results on the fourth.  
- Reviewer decision: Accept.  
- Paper addresses a specific, well-defined NLP problem (text sequence matching).  
- Approach is well-motivated and grounded in related work.  
- Provides thorough multi-dataset evaluation demonstrating effectiveness and generalizability.  
- Clear, concise introduction and explanation of contributions.  
- Includes detailed result analysis and an ablation study clarifying component contributions.  
- Lacks a detailed discussion of limitations and future work.  
- Could improve interpretability (e.g., visualizations of attention weights and comparison outputs).  
- Needs more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art models.  
- Questions for authors: provide interpretability visualizations; plans for handling OOV and long-range dependencies; and details on computational resources and training/evaluation cost relative to SOTA.
