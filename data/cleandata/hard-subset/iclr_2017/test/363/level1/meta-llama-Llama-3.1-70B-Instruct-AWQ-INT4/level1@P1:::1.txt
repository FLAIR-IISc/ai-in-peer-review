**Summary**

The paper proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for matching text sequences, which is evaluated on four different datasets representing different tasks. The framework consists of a preprocessing layer, an attention mechanism, a comparison layer, and an aggregation layer using a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN). The authors focus on the comparison layer and propose six different comparison functions, including element-wise operations, neural networks, and neural tensor networks. The experimental results show that the proposed framework achieves state-of-the-art performance on three datasets and competitive performance on the fourth dataset. The authors also find that some simple comparison functions based on element-wise operations can outperform more complex neural network-based comparison functions.
