**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for matching text sequences, which is evaluated on four different datasets representing different tasks. The framework consists of four layers: preprocessing, attention, comparison, and aggregation. The authors focus on the comparison function and propose six different options, including some uncommon ones based on element-wise operations. The paper claims that the proposed framework is effective for a wide range of sequence matching problems and that some simple comparison functions can outperform more complex neural network-based comparison functions.
