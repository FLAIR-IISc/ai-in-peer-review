**Summary of the paper**

The paper proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for matching text sequences, which is applicable to various natural language processing tasks such as machine comprehension, answer selection, and text entailment. The framework consists of four layers: preprocessing, attention, comparison, and aggregation. The authors focus on the comparison layer and propose six different comparison functions, including two novel element-wise comparison functions, SUBTRACTION (SUB) and MULTIPLICATION (MULT). The model is evaluated on four different datasets, and the results show that the proposed framework achieves state-of-the-art performance on three datasets and competitive performance on the fourth dataset. The authors also conduct an ablation study to analyze the importance of each component in the framework.
