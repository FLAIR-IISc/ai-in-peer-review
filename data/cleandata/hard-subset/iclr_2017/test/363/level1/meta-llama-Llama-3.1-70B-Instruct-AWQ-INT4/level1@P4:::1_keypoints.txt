- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for sequence matching with four layers: preprocessing, attention, comparison, aggregation.  
- Paper focuses on the comparison layer and evaluates six comparison functions, including uncommon element-wise operations.  
- Claims state-of-the-art performance on MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Reason 1: technically sound framework with clear explanations and multi-dataset evaluation.  
- Reason 2: novel contribution by systematically evaluating different comparison functions and showing cross-task effectiveness.  
- Supporting argument: detailed layer descriptions improve understandability and reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: provide more details on the preprocessing layer and its impact, benefits, and limitations.  
- Suggestion: add deeper analysis of the attention mechanism, especially its role when sequence lengths differ greatly.  
- Suggestion: discuss the model’s limitations and potential application scenarios.  
- Question: request more details on preprocessing and its effect on performance.  
- Question: ask how the model could be extended to handle multiple or much longer sequences.  
- Question: request additional analysis of the attention mechanism’s contribution to performance.
