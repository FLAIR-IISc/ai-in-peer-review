- Proposal: compare-aggregate framework evaluating different word-level comparison functions across four datasets, finding element-wise SUB and MULT outperform NN and NTN.
- Decision: Reject — contributions seen as incremental with insufficient novelty for ICLR.
- Performance: Improvements observed but not large enough to justify a major contribution.
- Theory gap: No compelling theoretical explanation for why element-wise operations work well; claims remain hypotheses.
- Ablation issues: Ablation study lacks detail and does not isolate comparison function effects or report statistical significance.
- Experimental concerns: Evaluation relies mainly on accuracy/MAP/MRR without deeper error analysis or qualitative insights.
- Robustness: Heavy reliance on pre-trained GloVe and minimal hyperparameter tuning (only dropout discussed) raises robustness questions.
- Preprocessing claim: Claim that preprocessing layer is order-insensitive is surprising and unexplained given non-linear activations.
- CNN details missing: Description of the aggregation CNN (filters, kernel sizes, pooling) is vague or absent.
- Statistical rigor: No statistical significance testing (p-values) reported for results.
- Suggested improvements: Provide theoretical justification linking element-wise ops to embedding properties or semantic similarity.
- Suggested improvements: Expand and detail ablation studies, including internal comparison-function variants (e.g., remove MULT from SUBMULT).
- Suggested improvements: Conduct detailed error analysis to show where methods succeed or fail and compare error patterns across functions.
- Suggested improvements: Perform thorough hyperparameter searches and report ranges/selection procedures.
- Suggested improvements: Compare against stronger, more recent baselines on each dataset.
- Questions for authors: Request theoretical explanation for SUB/MULT effectiveness and properties that suit sequence matching.
- Questions for authors: Ask what hyperparameter ranges/search methods were used and how final values were chosen.
- Questions for authors: Request full CNN architecture details (num filters, kernel sizes, pooling).
- Questions for authors: Provide example cases where SUB/MULT succeed but other methods fail.
- Questions for authors: Clarify why preprocessing with tanh/sigma is considered order-insensitive.
