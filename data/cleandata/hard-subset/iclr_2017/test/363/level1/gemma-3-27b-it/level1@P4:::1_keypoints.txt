- Decision: Reject — primary reasons are insufficient novelty and shallow empirical evaluation.
- Main claim: “Compare-aggregate” framework is validated, but contribution is largely empirical and incremental.
- Key empirical finding: Element-wise comparison functions (SUB, MULT) outperform standard NN/Tensor functions on tested datasets.
- Novelty concern: Prior work (e.g., He & Lin 2016, Wang & Jiang 2016b, Parikh et al. 2016) already covers similar similarity measures and frameworks.
- Marginal gains: Reported improvements are small and not shown to be substantial over existing methods.
- No statistical analysis: Results lack p-values/confidence intervals, so significance of differences is unclear.
- Insufficient ablation: Ablation study is limited and does not clarify each component’s contribution.
- Superficial analysis: Paper notes trends but fails to explain why element-wise ops work or under what conditions.
- Weak visualization: Figure 2 is limited and does not convincingly support claims about model focus.
- Baselines inadequate: Comparisons do not include the strongest or most recent state-of-the-art models for each task.
- Suggested improvement — statistics: Perform thorough significance testing for reported results.
- Suggested improvement — ablations/error analysis: Expand ablation study and include detailed error analysis to identify failure modes.
- Suggested improvement — theory: Provide theoretical justification or connections explaining why element-wise operations are effective.
- Suggested improvement — robustness: Report hyperparameter sensitivity and computational cost comparisons among functions.
- Questions for authors: How do SUB/MULT compare directly to cosine/Euclidean/dot (He & Lin 2016)? Were any statistical tests run?
- Questions for authors: Can you provide more visualization examples and explain CNN focus relating to correct answers?
- Questions for authors: What is the computational overhead of each comparison function, and how does the order-insensitive preprocessing layer still aid performance?
