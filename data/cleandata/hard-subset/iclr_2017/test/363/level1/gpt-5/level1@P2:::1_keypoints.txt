- Proposed a compare-aggregate architecture: word-level attention, word-by-word comparison, CNN aggregator for sequence matching.
- Main contribution is a systematic study of six word-level comparison functions (NN, NTN, EUCCOS, SUB, MULT, SUBMULT+NN), finding element-wise SUB/MULT and SUBMULT+NN outperform NN/NTN.
- Evaluated on MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI; competitive or SOTA on three datasets with ablations showing attention and preprocessing gate matter; code released.
- Decision: Accept — strong, consistent empirical evidence and actionable guidance that element-wise comparisons are effective and sample-efficient.
- Paper is well-motivated, fills gaps in cross-task evaluation and lack of systematic comparator study, and relates SUB/MULT to Euclidean/cosine intuitively.
- Results support claims via ablations and cross-dataset comparisons; EUCCOS is sometimes competitive; implementation details provided.
- Caveat: Baselines use reported numbers only; no unified re-implementation, and no reporting of statistical significance or variance.
- Caveat: The “general” framework is slightly task-adapted (e.g., added LSTM for SNLI), which weakens the claim of strict uniformity.
- Caveat: Training specifics for large candidate pools (InsuranceQA) and negative sampling vs full softmax are under-specified, affecting fairness/complexity.
- Suggestion: Report mean/std across multiple seeds and include significance tests for comparison-function claims.
- Suggestion: Clarify candidate sampling/training details for InsuranceQA and WikiQA and how multi-label WikiQA is handled with a softmax.
- Suggestion: Provide CNN aggregator hyperparameters (filter widths, counts, pooling) and a sensitivity analysis, since aggregator design may bias results.
- Suggestion: Explore bidirectional attention, test embedding fine-tuning, and report whether element-wise advantages persist.
- Suggestion: Include computational (speed/memory) comparisons between NN/NTN and SUB/MULT and targeted diagnostics (length mismatch, lexical overlap, adversarial cases).
- Suggestion: Consider calibration or pairwise/listwise objectives for multi-answer datasets or justify the chosen loss.
- Questions for authors: detail K-candidate construction during training, precise WikiQA training objective, CNN hyperparameters, seed variance, LSTM/gating setup for SNLI, bidirectional attention/normalization tests, truncation/padding policies, embedding fine-tuning results, and error analysis separating shallow vs deep reasoning failures.
