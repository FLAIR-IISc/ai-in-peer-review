- Proposes a compare-aggregate framework for sequence matching: attention-based word alignment, comparison via a family of functions, and CNN aggregation for prediction.  
- Main contribution: systematic study of six comparison functions (NN, NTN, EUCCOS, SUB, MULT, SUBMULT+NN).  
- Key finding: simple element-wise operators (SUB, MULT) and their combination outperform more complex neural comparators across tasks.  
- Evaluated on four datasets across QA and NLI (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI) with state-of-the-art or highly competitive results.  
- Includes ablation studies highlighting roles of attention and preprocessing gates.  
- Paper is clearly written, broadly applicable, and accompanied by released code.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Primary reason 1: Strong, consistent empirical evidence across benchmarks favoring simple comparators.  
- Primary reason 2: Clean, generalizable compare-aggregate instantiation with careful, task-spanning analysis useful to practitioners.  
- Problem/motivation: Focuses on the under-explored choice of comparison function within common sequence-matching paradigms.  
- Correctness/rigor: Standard, sound architecture; experiments cover multiple datasets with task-specific adaptations and relevant ablations.  
- Cross-dataset consistency of SUB/MULT provides credible support for the main claim.  
- Scope/novelty: Incremental architecture-wise but valuable methodological contribution and compelling negative result for complex comparators.  
- Suggest reporting statistical significance and variance across runs, as some gains are modest and may be noisy.  
- Request more implementation details (CNN filter widths/pooling, parameter counts) and computational cost comparisons for fairness.  
- Recommend stronger error analysis and a larger qualitative study beyond two visualizations.  
- Ask to clarify fairness of baseline comparisons given differing embedding and training choices.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to embedding updates, fine-tuning, and different pre-trained embeddings?  
- Questions for authors: exact CNN aggregation hyperparameters and their impact?  
- Questions for authors: was parameter count controlled when comparing NN/NTN vs SUB/MULT?  
- Questions for authors: are improvements statistically significant across multiple seeds?  
- Questions for authors: for SNLI, how was the LSTM integrated into preprocessing, and does order-sensitive preprocessing help other tasks?
