- Tackles sequence matching with a general “compare-aggregate” framework: attention-align tokens, apply per-token comparison functions, aggregate with a CNN.  
- Main contribution is a systematic study of six comparison functions (NN, NTN, EUCCOS, SUB, MULT, SUBMULT+NN).  
- Key finding: simple element-wise operators (SUB, MULT, SUBMULT+NN) consistently outperform NN/NTN and rival distance-based comparators.  
- Evaluated on four datasets (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI); achieves SOTA on three and competitive results on SNLI.  
- Ablations show attention matters more when sequence lengths differ, preprocessing/gating matters more when lengths are similar.  
- Emphasizes simplicity, generality, and efficiency (avoids expensive cross-sentence RNNs for most tasks) and releases code.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Primary reason for accept: strong, consistent empirical evidence across diverse tasks isolating the role of the comparison function.  
- Framework is simple, broadly applicable, and delivers practically useful insight about comparator choice.  
- Problem/motivation are well-positioned relative to prior attention-based and compare-aggregate models.  
- Method is clearly specified: gating layer, unidirectional attention, per-token comparator choices, CNN aggregator.  
- Empirical support includes SOTA results, ablations, and visualizations showing intuitive CNN aggregation of alignments.  
- Scientific rigor: comparators tested within the same pipeline, training details provided, code released for reproducibility.  
- Novelty is incremental: main novelty is comparative analysis and a simple CNN aggregator.  
- Some implementation details are underspecified (CNN filter widths, number of filters, pooling).  
- No statistical significance or run variance reported.  
- Baseline fairness concern: baselines taken from reported numbers rather than re-implemented under controlled conditions.  
- The order-insensitive gating may be insufficiently compared to order-sensitive encoders for some tasks.  
- Suggested improvements: report variance/significance, provide full CNN hyperparameters and sensitivity analysis.  
- Suggested improvements: include parameter counts per comparator and learning curves to support overfitting/regularization claims.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify WikiQA training objective (pointwise/pairwise/listwise) and handling of multiple positives/K.  
- Suggested improvements: try bi-directional attention (Q→A and A→Q) and report its effect.  
- Suggested improvements: consider fine-tuning embeddings or using subword embeddings and report sensitivity to embedding dimension.  
- Suggested improvements: deepen gating analysis (visualize by POS/IDF, compare to static IDF weighting).  
- Suggested improvements: report efficiency metrics (training/inference time and memory) across comparators.  
- Questions for authors include: exact WikiQA loss setup for multiple correct answers; CNN filter widths, pooling, and padding/truncation strategy; rationale for bilinear vs dot-product attention and its effect; whether embeddings were fine-tuned or subword OOV handling tried; variance/std across runs; candidate pool size for InsuranceQA and MovieQA softmax details; evidence that gates correlate with task-relevant words; and experiments with BiLSTM preprocessing or bi-attention.
