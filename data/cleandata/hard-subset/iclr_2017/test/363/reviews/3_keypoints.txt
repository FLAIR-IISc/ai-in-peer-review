- Proposes a general modeling strategy for two-text NLU: soft attention to align tokens, a comparison function on attention pairs, and CNN aggregation to produce outputs.  
- Provides a detailed empirical analysis of the comparison component.  
- Timely and relevant work that is straightforward, yields good results, and addresses an important NLP problem.  
- Borderline incremental relative to prior work (including authors' earlier work and Parikh et al.), but nevertheless makes sufficient contributions — reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- For longer sequences (all tasks except SNLI) the model is insensitive to word order, which empirically limits its upper-bound performance and should be noted in intro/discussion.  
- The attention mechanism appears closer to Luong et al. (2015)'s bilinear/general scoring than to Bahdanau et al.; cite Luong or a more relevant reference.  
- NTN risks overfitting due to many parameters; try an llm tensor variant (input dim l, smaller output dim m) to reduce capacity.  
- SubMultNN resembles the sentence-level matching strategy from Lili Mou et al.; note this similarity.  
- Question why the same preprocessing parameters are used for question and answer—these may require different weighting.
