- Proposes a compare-aggregate model for tasks that require semantic comparison of text sequences (e.g., QA, textual entailment).  
- Framework: apply a convolutional neural network (aggregation) after element-wise comparison operations over attentive LSTM outputs.  
- Main contribution: systematic comparison of several matching methods, showing element-wise subtraction and multiplication generally perform best across four datasets.  
- Weakness: work is incremental and offers limited novel innovation.  
- Suggestion: include qualitative analysis showing how subtraction, multiplication, and other comparison functions behave on varied sentence types.
