- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for text sequence matching.
- Tests the framework on four datasets: MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI.
- Main contributions: demonstrates framework effectiveness across tasks and systematically evaluates multiple comparison functions.
- Includes uncommon element-wise comparison functions as part of the evaluation.
- Paper is well-organized with clear explanation of framework, comparison functions, and experimental setup.
- Results are clearly presented with thorough analysis.
- Provides a comprehensive related work review situating the contribution within sequence matching research.
- Recommendation: accept, citing well-motivated, well-executed research and significant contribution to NLP.
- Supporting points: framework importance for NLP tasks and valuable insights from systematic comparison-function evaluation.
- Limitation: lacks detailed analysis of preprocessing and attention mechanisms.
- Limitation: analysis of strengths/weaknesses of different comparison functions could be more thorough.
- Limitation: discussion of practical implications and real-world application of results is insufficient.
- Questions for authors: provide more details on preprocessing and attention, deeper analysis of comparison functions, and discussion of real-world applicability.
