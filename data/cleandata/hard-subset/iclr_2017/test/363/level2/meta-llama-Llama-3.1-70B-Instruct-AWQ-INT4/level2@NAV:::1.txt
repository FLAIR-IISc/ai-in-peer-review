**Summary of the Paper**

The paper presents a general "compare-aggregate" framework for matching text sequences, which is a crucial task in many natural language processing problems. The framework consists of four layers: preprocessing, attention, comparison, and aggregation. The authors focus on the comparison function, which is used to match two word embeddings, and evaluate six different comparison functions, including neural network, neural tensor network, Euclidean distance, cosine similarity, subtraction, and multiplication. The authors also experiment with four different datasets, namely, MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI, and show that their model achieves state-of-the-art performance on most of the datasets.
