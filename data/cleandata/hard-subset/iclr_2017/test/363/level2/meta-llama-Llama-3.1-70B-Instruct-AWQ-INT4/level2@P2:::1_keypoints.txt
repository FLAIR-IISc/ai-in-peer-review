- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for text sequence matching with four layers: preprocessing, attention, comparison, aggregation.
- Focuses on the comparison function and evaluates six options, including element-wise operations.
- Evaluated on four datasets across different tasks.
- Achieves state-of-the-art on three datasets and competitive results on the fourth.
- Finds that simple element-wise comparison functions can outperform more complex neural comparison functions.
- Well-motivated, well-placed in literature, and clearly written.
- Thorough evaluation and clear presentation of results.
- Provides useful analysis and insights into why the framework is effective.
- Does not include a detailed computational complexity analysis despite noting potential expense for long sequences.
- Lacks a detailed experimental comparison with other state-of-the-art models.
- Asks authors for a detailed computational complexity analysis.
- Requests a more detailed experimental comparison with SOTA models.
- Inquires about extending the framework to other tasks and domains.
