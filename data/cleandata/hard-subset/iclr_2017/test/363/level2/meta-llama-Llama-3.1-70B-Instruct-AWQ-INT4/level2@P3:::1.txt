**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for matching text sequences, which is evaluated on four different datasets representing different tasks. The framework consists of four layers: preprocessing, attention, comparison, and aggregation. The authors focus on the comparison function and propose six different options, including some uncommon ones based on element-wise operations. The experimental results show that the proposed framework achieves state-of-the-art performance on three datasets and competitive performance on the fourth dataset. The authors also find that some simple comparison functions can outperform more complex neural network-based comparison functions.
