This paper proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for matching text sequences, which is a crucial component in many natural language processing tasks. The authors argue that this framework can be effective for a wide range of sequence matching problems and test it on four different datasets, namely MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI. The main contributions of this work are twofold: (1) demonstrating the effectiveness of the "compare-aggregate" framework on multiple datasets, and (2) conducting a systematic evaluation of different comparison functions, including some uncommon ones based on element-wise operations.
