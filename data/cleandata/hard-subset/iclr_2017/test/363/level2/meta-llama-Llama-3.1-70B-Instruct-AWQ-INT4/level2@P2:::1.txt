**Paper Summary**

This paper proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for matching text sequences, which is evaluated on four different datasets representing different tasks. The framework consists of four layers: preprocessing, attention, comparison, and aggregation. The authors focus on the comparison function and evaluate six different options, including some uncommon ones based on element-wise operations. The results show that the proposed framework achieves state-of-the-art performance on three datasets and competitive performance on the fourth dataset. The authors also find that some simple comparison functions can outperform more complex neural network-based comparison functions.

**Decision**

I decide to **Accept** this paper. The paper tackles an important problem in natural language processing, and the proposed framework is well-motivated and well-placed in the literature. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their framework on four different datasets, and the results are convincing. The paper also provides a good analysis of the results and discusses the implications of the findings.
