- Presents a "compare-aggregate" framework for matching text sequences and systematically evaluates word-level comparison functions.  
- Proposes a general model applicable to multiple sequence matching tasks (QA, textual entailment).  
- Key finding: simple element-wise comparison functions (SUB, MULT) outperform more complex neural approaches across datasets.  
- Demonstrates generalizability by testing on four datasets: MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI.  
- Systematic comparison of six comparison functions provides practical insights for future model design.  
- Ablation study shows both preprocessing and attention layers are important to performance.  
- Recommends acceptance to ICLR 2017 based on strong empirical evidence and contributions.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and model description is sufficiently detailed for reproducibility.  
- Authors released code online, enhancing reproducibility.  
- Improvement: clarify and provide more intuition for the preprocessing layer and how gate values operate and relate to attention.  
- Improvement: explain the claimed relationship between SUB and Euclidean distance.  
- Improvement: report computational complexity and parameter counts for each comparison function (e.g., SUB/MULT vs NN/NTN).  
- Improvement: clarify the claim that the preprocessing layer is order-insensitive and discuss its effect on contextual modeling.  
- Improvement: analyze why EUCCOS performs particularly well on MovieQA and which dataset characteristics favor it.  
- Question: how are Wi, Wu, bi, and bu initialized in preprocessing, and does initialization significantly affect performance?  
- Question: do SUB and MULT truly have no learnable parameters in implementation, and how does that affect model capacity?  
- Question: is the InsuranceQA improvement consistent across all comparison functions or driven by specific ones?  
- Question: how was the attention layer's importance measured for tasks with large length differences, and does this pattern hold across comparison functions?
