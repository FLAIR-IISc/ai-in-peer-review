- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for sequence matching with two claims: general applicability and superiority of simple element-wise comparison functions (SUB, MULT).
- Recommends acceptance based on strong empirical support across four diverse datasets (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI).
- Model achieves state-of-the-art or competitive results on three datasets.
- Element-wise comparison functions consistently outperform standard neural network and neural tensor network approaches.
- Experimental design is rigorous, with thorough ablation studies showing the importance of preprocessing and attention layers.
- Provides sufficient implementation details for reproducibility.
- Addresses a gap in prior work by systematically evaluating comparison functions across multiple tasks.
- Finding that simple element-wise operations can beat more complex models is surprising and practically valuable.
- Suggests adding a more detailed theoretical explanation for why element-wise operations work better despite lower expressiveness.
- Requests clarification on whether gains come from the comparison functions themselves or the overall model architecture.
- Recommends comparing model complexity and training time across comparison functions to aid practitioners.
- Wants a more detailed analysis of visualization/attention patterns to explain why certain functions work better for specific tasks.
