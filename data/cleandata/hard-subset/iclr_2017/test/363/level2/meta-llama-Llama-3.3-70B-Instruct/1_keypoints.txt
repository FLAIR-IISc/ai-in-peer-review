- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for sequence matching with a model evaluated on MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI.
- Model architecture comprises four layers: preprocessing, attention, comparison, and aggregation.
- Paper focuses on the comparison function between paired word vectors and evaluates six variants (NN, NTN, Euclidean, cosine, subtraction, multiplication).
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, using multiple datasets, metrics, and baselines.
- Results show state-of-the-art performance on several datasets and competitive results on others.
- Provides detailed analysis of the comparison functions and their effects on performance.
- Overall assessment: well-motivated, well-executed study recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
- Suggested revision: provide more details on the preprocessing layer and its impact.
- Suggested revision: include deeper analysis of the attention mechanism and its role.
- Suggested revision: add more related work on sequence matching and the compare-aggregate framework.
- Questions for authors: plans for multi-task learning extensions, potential model improvements and limitations, and implications/applicability to other sequence matching tasks.
