- Proposes a compare-aggregate framework: attention-based word alignment, element-wise comparison functions, and CNN aggregation.
- Core claims: framework is effective across diverse sequence-matching tasks and simple element-wise comparators (SUB, MULT, SUBMULT+NN) outperform complex NN/NTN comparators.
- Evaluated on four benchmarks (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI) with state-of-the-art or competitive results and released code.
- Decision: Accept — clear, well-motivated empirical study showing practical, parameter-efficient design choices.
- Targets the specific problem of comparing aligned word vectors in attention-based QA and NLI pipelines.
- Well grounded in prior work (match-LSTM, decomposable attention, CNN/RNN encoders); fills a gap by focusing on the comparison function.
- Empirical evidence: consistent trends across datasets; SUBMULT+NN best overall; SUB/MULT/EUCCOS often competitive.
- Ablation studies demonstrate the importance of attention and preprocessing gates across different input lengths.
- Architecture is simple, modular, broadly applicable, and practical for practitioners.
- Finding that element-wise operations are robust and parameter-efficient is actionable guidance for model design.
- Model and training details are mostly sufficient; framework description is clear.
- Recommend reporting statistical significance, confidence intervals, and variance over multiple runs.
- Need to ensure parameter-count parity when comparing NN/NTN vs. element-wise methods and discuss regularization/overfitting risks for NTN.
- Expand implementation details: CNN filter widths, number of filters, pooling strategy, and report training/inference speed and memory.
- Suggest error analysis per dataset and qualitative examples explaining why SUB/MULT succeed.
- Discuss effects of bidirectional attention, order-sensitive preprocessing, and the impact of training embeddings vs. fixed embeddings.
- Clarify baseline comparability (data splits, preprocessing) and, if possible, re-run key baselines under a shared setting.
- Questions for authors: Did you enforce parameter-budget fairness across comparison functions and how sensitive are results to capacity/dropout?
- Questions for authors: Are improvements statistically significant across multiple seeds?
- Questions for authors: What are the exact CNN hyperparameters and their sensitivity?
- Questions for authors: How does updating embeddings vs. fixed GloVe affect comparator rankings?
- Questions for authors: How is very long context handled in MovieQA (truncation/stride) and does performance vary with plot length?
- Questions for authors: Would bidirectional attention or multi-head scoring change the relative advantage of element-wise comparisons?
