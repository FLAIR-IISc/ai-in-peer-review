- Decision: Accept — well-motivated paper with convincing empirical evidence.  
- Core contribution: Systematic investigation of comparison functions within a "compare-aggregate" sequence-matching framework.  
- Key empirical finding: Simple element-wise operations (SUB and MULT) often outperform more complex comparison functions (NN, NTN).  
- Clear problem definition and motivation: Importance of sequence matching and the overlooked role of comparison-function design are well articulated.  
- Sound methodology: Logical extension of the compare-aggregate framework and reasonable selection of comparison functions.  
- Dataset diversity: Evaluation on four datasets (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI) strengthens generalizability.  
- Strong empirical results: SUB and MULT consistently competitive or superior; visualizations support model behavior.  
- Ablation studies: Performed but brief; show preprocessing and attention layers matter.  
- Reproducibility: Authors plan to release code, facilitating follow-up research.  
- Related work gap: Related work section could better cover recent advances (e.g., self-attention, transformers) and other sequence-matching architectures.  
- Ablation study expansion needed: Recommend exploring embedding sizes, CNN filter sizes, and component interactions in more detail.  
- Statistical significance missing: No significance tests reported; adding them would strengthen claims.  
- Hyperparameter tuning details lacking: More transparency needed on tuning procedures (learning rate, batch size, ranges, search strategies) across datasets.  
- Error analysis missing: Include qualitative examples of failures and discuss causes to guide future work.  
- SNLI performance: Competitive but not state-of-the-art; authors should discuss reasons for this gap.  
- Questions for authors: Explain intuition for SUB/MULT effectiveness and their semantic properties; detail hyperparameter tuning procedures; report experiments with order-sensitive preprocessing (LSTM/GRU); provide concrete error examples and limitations.
