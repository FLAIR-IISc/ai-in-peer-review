- Presents a compare-aggregate framework for sequence matching and evaluates different comparison functions.  
- Experiments conducted on four datasets: MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI.  
- Key finding: simple element-wise comparison functions (SUB and MULT) perform surprisingly well, often outperforming more complex neural approaches.  
- Well-motivated work that builds on existing compare-aggregate literature.  
- Recommendation: Accept — valuable empirical investigation with useful, counter-intuitive insight for practitioners.  
- Systematic evaluation and diverse datasets lend robustness to the conclusions.  
- Clear model description, experimental setup, and ablation studies showing the importance of attention and preprocessing layers.  
- Code availability noted, aiding reproducibility.  
- Comparisons to relevant baselines included for context.  
- Weakness: lacks deeper analysis explaining why element-wise operations work so well.  
- Weakness: preprocessing layer description is brief and is order-insensitive; potential downsides not discussed.  
- Suggestion: explore order-sensitive preprocessing alternatives (e.g., RNNs) and visualize/analyze learned representations or attention.  
- Question: sensitivity of results to hyperparameters (embedding size, CNN filter sizes) and whether tuning was dataset-specific.
