- Proposes a compare-aggregate model using attentive LSTM comparisons followed by a CNN aggregator for sequence matching tasks (QA, entailment).  
- Systematically evaluates several element-wise comparison functions (including subtraction and multiplication) and aggregation methods.  
- Finds simple element-wise subtraction and multiplication generally perform best across four datasets.  
- Main contribution is an empirical evaluation that informs design choices for sequence matching models.  
- Decision: accept.  
- Key reason 1: approach is well-motivated and grounded in literature.  
- Key reason 2: provides a systematic, thorough evaluation of comparison functions.  
- Strengths: clear writing, well-explained model and experimental setup, and concise result presentation with discussion of implications.  
- Suggested improvements: add qualitative analysis of comparison behaviors on varied sentence types.  
- Suggested improvements: novelty is incremental; consider exploring new comparison functions or more challenging datasets.  
- Suggested improvements: include a more detailed discussion of model and evaluation limitations.  
- Questions for authors: provide details on preprocessing (gate values, outer product), CNN hyperparameter selection and its impact, and examples showing how comparison functions behave on varied sentence types.
