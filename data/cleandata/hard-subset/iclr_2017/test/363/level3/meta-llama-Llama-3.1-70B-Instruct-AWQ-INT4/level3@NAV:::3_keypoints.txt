- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for sequence matching with four layers: preprocessing, attention, comparison, aggregation.  
- Focuses on the comparison function and evaluates six options, including novel element-wise operations.  
- Evaluated on MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI, achieving state-of-the-art on most datasets.  
- Recommendation: accept the paper.  
- Main reasons to accept: tackles an important problem and presents a general, widely applicable framework.  
- Main reasons to accept: thorough evaluation across four diverse datasets demonstrating effectiveness and robustness.  
- Strengths: well-motivated, clear related work overview, organized writing, and thorough result analysis.  
- Innovation: use of element-wise operations in the comparison function is novel and insightful.  
- Contribution: detailed analysis of different comparison functions helps understand their trade-offs.  
- Request for more details on the preprocessing layer (choice of gate values and its performance impact).  
- Request for more analysis and visualizations of the attention mechanism and its effect on performance.  
- Request for more details on the CNN layer (filter sizes and impact) and inclusion of additional baselines in evaluation.  
- Questions to authors: provide preprocessing gate choices and impact, hyperparameter selection process, attention visualizations/analysis, and plans/challenges for extending to multi-task learning.
