This paper proposes a compare-aggregate model for tasks that require semantic comparison of text sequences, such as question answering and textual entailment. The model applies a convolutional neural network (CNN) after element-wise comparison operations over attentive LSTM outputs. The authors systematically compare several matching methods and find that element-wise subtraction and multiplication generally perform best across four datasets.

The specific question tackled by this paper is how to effectively compare two sequences of text to judge their semantic similarity or relatedness. The authors argue that the general "compare-aggregate" framework is effective for a wide range of sequence matching problems and present a model that follows this framework. They also conduct a systematic evaluation of different comparison functions and show that a comparison function based on element-wise operations works best across different datasets.
