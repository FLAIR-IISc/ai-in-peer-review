**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a compare-aggregate model for tasks that require semantic comparison of text sequences, such as question answering (QA) and textual entailment. The framework consists of applying a convolutional neural network (CNN) for aggregation after element-wise comparison operations over attentive LSTM outputs. The authors systematically compare several matching methods, including element-wise subtraction and multiplication, and show that these simple comparison functions generally perform best across four datasets. The main contribution of the paper is the evaluation of different comparison functions, which provides insights into the design of sequence matching models.
