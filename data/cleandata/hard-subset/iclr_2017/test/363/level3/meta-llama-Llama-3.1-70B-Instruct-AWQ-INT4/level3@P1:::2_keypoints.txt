- Proposes a "compare-aggregate" framework: word-level matching between two sequences followed by CNN aggregation for text matching tasks.  
- Systematically evaluates six comparison functions, including neural-net, neural-tensor, and element-wise operations.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI.  
- Main contribution: shows framework effectiveness across multiple datasets and tasks.  
- Second contribution: shows simple element-wise comparison functions can outperform more complex neural approaches.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation of preprocessing, attention, comparison, and aggregation components.  
- Thorough related-work review situates the approach within NLP literature.  
- Reasons for acceptance: novel/effective framework and comprehensive evaluation of comparison functions.  
- Supporting evidence: solid motivation, strong experimental results, and comprehensive comparisons.  
- Request for more analysis of preprocessing and attention benefits and ablation results without them.  
- Suggest improving Figure 2 readability and/or providing a more detailed explanation.  
- Ask for deeper analysis on why element-wise functions outperform complex models and dataset/task characteristics that favor them.
