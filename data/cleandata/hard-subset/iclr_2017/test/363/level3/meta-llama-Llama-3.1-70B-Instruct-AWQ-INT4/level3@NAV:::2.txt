**Paper Review**

**Summary**

This paper proposes a compare-aggregate framework for matching text sequences, which performs word-level matching followed by aggregation using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs). The authors evaluate six different comparison functions and test their model on four different datasets, achieving state-of-the-art performance on three of them.

**Decision**

I recommend accepting this paper, with some minor revisions.

**Reasons for Decision**

The paper tackles a specific problem in natural language processing, namely sequence matching, and proposes a well-motivated approach to solve it. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their model on four different datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness and robustness. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach and results.

**Supporting Arguments**

1. The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the limitations of previous approaches to sequence matching.
2. The proposed compare-aggregate framework is a novel and effective approach to sequence matching, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of its performance on four different datasets.
3. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the results, including an ablation study that demonstrates the importance of the preprocessing and attention layers.

**Additional Feedback**

1. The paper would benefit from a clearer explanation of the preprocessing and attention layers, including the motivation behind their design.
2. The authors should provide more details about the hyperparameter tuning process, including the range of values tested for each hyperparameter.
3. The paper could benefit from additional visualizations, such as plots of the attention weights or the convolutional layer outputs.
4. The authors should consider providing more analysis of the results, including a discussion of the strengths and weaknesses of each comparison function.

**Questions for Authors**

1. Can you provide more details about the preprocessing and attention layers, including the motivation behind their design?
2. How did you tune the hyperparameters for each dataset, and what was the range of values tested for each hyperparameter?
3. Can you provide more visualizations of the model's outputs, such as plots of the attention weights or the convolutional layer outputs?
4. Can you provide more analysis of the results, including a discussion of the strengths and weaknesses of each comparison function?

**Typos and Minor Issues**

1. Page 4, line 5: "including a some" should be "including some".
2. Figure 2 is hard to read, especially in print. Consider improving its quality or providing a higher-resolution version.
