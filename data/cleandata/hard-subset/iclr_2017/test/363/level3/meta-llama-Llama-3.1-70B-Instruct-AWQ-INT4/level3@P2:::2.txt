**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for matching text sequences, which performs word-level matching followed by aggregation using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs). The framework is evaluated on four different datasets, including MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI, and is compared to several published baselines. The authors also investigate the effectiveness of six different comparison functions, including neural networks, neural tensor networks, Euclidean distance, cosine similarity, subtraction, and multiplication. The results show that the proposed framework achieves state-of-the-art performance on several datasets, and that some simple comparison functions can outperform more complex neural network-based approaches.
