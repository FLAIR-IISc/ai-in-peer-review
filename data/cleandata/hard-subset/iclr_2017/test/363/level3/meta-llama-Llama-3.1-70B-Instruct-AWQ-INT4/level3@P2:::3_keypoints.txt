- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for sequence matching with preprocessing, attention, comparison, and CNN aggregation components.
- Evaluated on MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI, achieving state-of-the-art on three of four datasets.
- Recommendation: accept — tackles an important problem and provides a strong empirical study.
- Key contribution: systematic empirical analysis of six comparison functions, showing simple element-wise operations can outperform complex neural functions.
- Includes ablation studies assessing the importance of the preprocessing layer and attention mechanism.
- Provides detailed result analysis with attention and convolutional-layer visualizations.
- Limitation: model is insensitive to word order for longer sequences (all tasks except SNLI); needs further discussion and potential remedies.
- Attention mechanism resembles Luong et al. (2015) bilinear/general scoring and should be properly cited.
- Neural tensor network (NTN) comparison risks overfitting due to high parameter count; suggest lower-capacity lllm tensor variant.
- SubMultNN comparison is similar to sentence-level matching from Lili Mou et al.; this similarity should be noted and discussed.
- Unclear why the same preprocessing parameters are used for question and answer — they may require different weighting.
- Questions for authors: expand on addressing word-order insensitivity in future work.
- Questions for authors: provide more details on the preprocessing layer and rationale for shared parameters between question and answer.
- Questions for authors: elaborate on the relationship between SubMultNN and Lili Mou et al.’s sentence-level matching and possible combined/extended approaches.
