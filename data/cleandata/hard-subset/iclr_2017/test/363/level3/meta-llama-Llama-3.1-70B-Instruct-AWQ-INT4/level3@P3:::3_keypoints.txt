- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for text sequence matching with soft attention, pairwise comparison, and CNN aggregation.  
- Provides detailed empirical analysis of six comparison functions across four datasets.  
- Finds simple element-wise comparison functions often outperform standard neural networks and neural tensor networks.  
- Framework applies to multiple tasks (question answering, textual entailment) and achieves competitive state-of-the-art results.  
- Paper is well-written, experiments are well-designed and executed; recommendation to accept.  
- Main contribution is the systematic evaluation of different comparison functions.  
- Limitation: model is insensitive to word order for longer sequences, potentially limiting upper-bound performance.  
- Suggestion: cite Luong et al. (2015) for attention, which may be closer to the approach than Bahdanau et al.  
- Suggestion: NTN may overfit due to many parameters—consider lower-dimensional variants or stronger regularization.  
- Suggestion: Acknowledge similarity of SubMultNN to Lili Mou et al.'s sentence-level matching and discuss differences.  
- Suggestion: Consider using different preprocessing/weighting parameters for question and answer inputs.  
- Question: Provide details on hyperparameter selection—tuning on a dev set or chosen from prior knowledge?  
- Question: How will the word-order insensitivity limitation be addressed in future work?  
- Question: Provide details on computational resources used for training (single GPU vs. cluster).
