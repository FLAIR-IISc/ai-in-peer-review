- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for sequence matching with four layers: preprocessing, attention, comparison, aggregation.  
- Systematically evaluates six comparison functions (element-wise, neural nets, neural tensor nets) across four datasets.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art on three datasets and competitive performance on the fourth.  
- Finds element-wise comparison (especially subtraction and multiplication) generally outperforms neural and tensor networks.  
- Decision: accept with minor revisions.  
- Main contribution: thorough, well-motivated framework and systematic comparison of comparison functions.  
- Concern: novelty is incremental—builds on existing work without exploring substantially new architectures.  
- Suggestion: add qualitative analyses (attention visualizations, error analysis, example successes/failures) to better explain behaviors.  
- Suggestion: explore more novel comparison functions or architectures (e.g., graph-based models, external knowledge).  
- Question: provide more details on how the preprocessing layer affects performance.  
- Question: how to extend the framework to handle more complex tasks (multiple sequences, external knowledge), and request examples illustrating element-wise methods' success/failure and associated attention weights.
