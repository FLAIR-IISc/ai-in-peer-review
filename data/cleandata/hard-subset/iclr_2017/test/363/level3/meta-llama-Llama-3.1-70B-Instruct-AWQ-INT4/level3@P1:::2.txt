This paper proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for matching text sequences, which is a crucial component in various natural language processing tasks such as machine comprehension, answer selection, and text entailment. The framework first performs word-level matching between two sequences and then aggregates the matching results using a convolutional neural network (CNN). The authors systematically evaluate six different comparison functions for word-level matching, including neural network-based, neural tensor network-based, and element-wise operations-based functions.

The paper makes two main contributions. First, it demonstrates the effectiveness of the "compare-aggregate" framework on four different datasets, including MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI, achieving state-of-the-art performance on most of them. Second, it provides a comprehensive evaluation of different comparison functions, showing that simple element-wise operations-based functions can outperform more complex neural network-based functions.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed framework, the comparison functions, and the experimental results. The paper also includes a thorough review of related work, which helps to situate the proposed framework within the broader context of natural language processing research.

I decide to accept this paper, with the following two key reasons:
