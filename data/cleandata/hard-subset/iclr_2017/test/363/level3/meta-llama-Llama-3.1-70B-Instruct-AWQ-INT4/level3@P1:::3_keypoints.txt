- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for text sequence matching using soft attention, a comparison function, and a CNN aggregator.  
- Evaluates six comparison functions across four datasets and shows state-of-the-art performance on several tasks.  
- Finds simple comparison functions (element-wise subtraction, multiplication) can outperform more complex neural-network comparisons.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, noting the contribution is timely and relevant despite being borderline incremental.  
- Framework is effective for matching text sequences as evidenced by empirical results.  
- Provides a detailed empirical analysis that helps understand strengths and weaknesses of different comparison functions.  
- Limitation: model is largely insensitive to word order for longer sequences, which limits its upper-bound performance and should be noted.  
- Attention mechanism resembles Luong et al. (2015) bilinear/general scoring more than Bahdanau et al.; Luong should be cited.  
- Neural tensor network (NTN) variant risks overfitting due to high parameter count; consider lower-capacity tensor variants.  
- SubMultNN comparison is similar to sentence-level matching strategies from Lili Mou et al.; this similarity should be acknowledged.  
- Preprocessing uses the same parameters for questions and answers, which may be suboptimal—consider separate preprocessing/weighting.  
- Request for more details on how CNN aggregation hyperparameters were chosen.  
- Ask how and why the specific comparison functions were selected for evaluation.  
- Request insights into why simple comparison functions outperformed complex ones, and whether the framework has been/applicable to other tasks (e.g., MT, summarization) and multi-task learning extensions.
