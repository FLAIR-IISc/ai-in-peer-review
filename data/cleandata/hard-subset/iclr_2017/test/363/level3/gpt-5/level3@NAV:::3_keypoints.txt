- Proposed a compare-aggregate strategy: soft attention for alignment, pairwise comparison functions, and CNN aggregation for prediction.
- Main contribution: systematic empirical analysis of comparison functions across MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI.
- Finding: simple element-wise operations (SUB, MULT, SUB+MULT) consistently match or outperform NN/NTN and distance baselines.
- Approach is timely, easy to implement, and achieves strong results on important NLP tasks.
- Decision: Accept — well-executed, empirically compelling, and provides actionable cross-task insights despite some incremental aspects.
- Clear problem motivation and literature placement; focused ablation on comparison functions is novel and useful.
- Results are strong, consistent, reproducible (code released), and competitive across diverse tasks.
- Caveat: preprocessing is order-insensitive for longer sequences (all tasks except SNLI), likely limiting the model’s upper bound.
- Citation fix: attention scoring is closer to Luong et al. (2015) bilinear/general attention than Bahdanau — update citations.
- NTN likely overfits due to parameter count; consider low-rank/tensor variants (l×l×m with m << l) or CP/Tucker factorization.
- Acknowledge similarity: SUBMULTNN resembles sentence-pair heuristic matching in Lili Mou et al. (2016).
- Question using identical preprocessing for Q and A; asymmetry may benefit from separate gates or role conditioning.
- Recommend testing order-sensitivity: add positional encodings, lightweight contextualization, and a permutation-sensitivity test.
- Request reporting of parameter counts, training variance across seeds, and runtime/memory metrics.
- Suggest comparing CNN aggregation to a Decomposable-style sum/FF aggregator on the same comparison vectors.
- Question: Did you try separate preprocessing parameters for Q vs A or role-conditioned gates?
- Question: How sensitive are the conclusions to updating embeddings and to random seeds?
- Question: Can you provide parameter counts and learning curves for NN vs NTN vs SUB/MULT?
- Question: Does a low-rank NTN (l×l×m) close the gap without overfitting?
- Question: How does CNN aggregation compare to simple pooling/FF on the same comparison vectors?
- Question: Can you quantify performance loss when shuffling word order in long sequences?
