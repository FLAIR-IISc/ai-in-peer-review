- Paper studies a compare-aggregate framework (align→compare→aggregate with CNN) and systematically evaluates six comparison functions (NN, NTN, EUCCOS, SUB, MULT, SUBMULT+NN).  
- Central finding: simple element-wise functions (SUB, MULT, and their combination) often outperform more complex neural comparison functions.  
- Experiments on MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI show competitive or state-of-the-art results on three QA datasets and strong SNLI performance.  
- Decision: weak accept — motivated by a thorough multi-dataset empirical study and actionable finding about SUB/MULT.  
- Strength: clear writing, simple broadly applicable model, and useful empirical insights for the community.  
- Empirical rigor: evaluations against published baselines with ablation indicating preprocessing (gating) and attention help, varying by task.  
- Scientific clarity: convincingly supports SUB/MULT effectiveness; EUCCOS is competitive in some settings.  
- Caveat: dimensionality mismatch (EUCCOS 2-D vs. others l-D) may bias aggregation capacity and should be controlled.  
- Caveat: architectural novelty is incremental; main contribution is comparative analysis and cross-task validation.  
- Caveat: need reporting of variance across random seeds and statistical significance to increase confidence.  
- Ablation request: report per-dataset results removing preprocessing, removing attention, and removing both, with confidence intervals over multiple runs.  
- Fairness control request: equalize output dimensionality (e.g., project EUCCOS to l dims or others to 2-D) before CNN and report impact.  
- Efficiency request: include parameter counts and training/inference speed comparisons, especially NTN vs. SUB/MULT.  
- Sensitivity request: report sensitivity to comparison dimensionality l, CNN filter widths/counts, and whether gains persist when embeddings are fine-tuned.  
- Stability request: provide mean±std over multiple random seeds (≥3–5 runs) and significance tests against baselines.  
- Architectural clarification: specify CNN details (filter sizes, counts, pooling) and any task-specific tuning.  
- Attention question: justify one-directional attention choice and report whether bi-directional attention (A→Q and Q→A) helps.  
- Preprocessing question: did a light contextualizer (e.g., shallow BiLSTM) or fine-tuning embeddings affect results and cost?  
- MovieQA detail request: describe handling of long plots (truncation, sliding windows) and memory/alignment effects.  
- Presentation fixes: improve Figure 2 readability, fix typo (“including a some”), and add a consolidated table of comparison-function results across tasks.  
- Training detail requests: report CNN pooling operation, regularization beyond dropout (weight decay, layer norm), and their effects on different comparison functions.  
- Generality question: comment on transferability of element-wise findings to other pairwise tasks (paraphrase ID, dialogue response selection).  
- Quantification request: for imbalanced-length datasets, quantify how much attention vs. preprocessing contributes per dataset.
