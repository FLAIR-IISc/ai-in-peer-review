- Paper summary: presents a general compare-aggregate framework for sequence matching with attention, a pluggable comparison module, and CNN aggregation; empirically studies six comparison functions across tasks.  
- Main empirical finding: simple element-wise operators (SUB, MULT), especially SUBMULT+NN, perform as well or better than more complex neural alternatives.  
- Results: achieves state-of-the-art or best-reported results on three datasets and competitive results on SNLI.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Strong cross-task empirical evidence that simple, principled comparison functions match or outperform complex models.  
- Key reason 2: Clear, general framework with careful ablations and public code increases reproducibility and community value.  
- Motivation: Systematically fills a gap by dissecting comparison functions in compare-aggregate models for QA and entailment.  
- Relation to prior work: Similar to decomposable attention and match-LSTM but differs in CNN aggregation, one-directional attention, and focus on word-level comparison functions.  
- Connection to heuristics: SUB and MULT mirror sentence-level heuristic matching (difference and product) and extend their evaluation to word-level comparisons.  
- Evidence and rigor: Evaluations use four benchmarks with appropriate metrics, strong baselines, ablations, and cross-dataset consistency for SUB/MULT/SUBMULT+NN.  
- Proposed explanation: Element-wise operators act as a middle ground between rigid distance metrics and over-parameterized neural layers; data supports this.  
- Limitation: Preprocessing for long sequences is order-insensitive (bag-of-words with gates), which may cap performance in some settings.  
- Novelty: Incremental relative to prior compare-aggregate models, but systematic multi-dataset analysis is a meaningful contribution.  
- Clarify attention: Attention scoring resembles Luong et al.’s bilinear “general” form more than Bahdanau’s; cite Luong (2015) and emphasize asymmetry (A attends to Q).  
- Discuss order sensitivity: Emphasize order-insensitive preprocessing early and consider positional encodings or local RNN/CNN to improve robustness on long contexts.  
- Parameterization/overfitting: NTN likely overfits due to cubic parameters; report parameter counts and consider low-rank or factorized tensor variants to equalize capacity.  
- Shared preprocessing params: Using shared gating for Q and A may be suboptimal; consider separate or conditional gates since salience differs by role.  
- Experimental clarity—variance/significance: Report variance over runs and statistical significance where margins are small.  
- Experimental clarity—CNN details: Provide CNN aggregation hyperparameters (filter widths, counts, pooling) and justify CNN vs RNN/mean pooling empirically.  
- Experimental clarity—compute costs: Analyze training time and memory, especially attention matrix costs for long sequences.  
- Experimental clarity—embeddings/OOV: Explore embedding fine-tuning and better OOV handling rather than freezing GloVe with zero init.  
- Analysis suggestions: Provide error typology, per-length and lexical-overlap breakdowns, and compare one-way vs two-way attention behavior.  
- Question 1: Did you try fine-tuning word or subword embeddings and how did that affect performance?  
- Question 2: Can you report parameter counts and training time per comparison function and include significance tests over seeds?  
- Question 3: How sensitive are results to CNN hyperparameters and does an RNN/biLSTM aggregator change comparison-function rankings?  
- Question 4: For long contexts (MovieQA), did you try bidirectional/co-attention or multi-hop attention and would that change comparison-function merits?  
- Question 5: Did separate or conditional preprocessing/gating for Q and A help?  
- Question 6: For NTN, did low-rank/factorized or reduced-output variants reduce overfitting and how did they compare to SUBMULT+NN?  
- Question 7: What is the impact of order-sensitive preprocessing (shallow CNN/GRU) on WikiQA/InsuranceQA—do SUB/MULT remain best?
