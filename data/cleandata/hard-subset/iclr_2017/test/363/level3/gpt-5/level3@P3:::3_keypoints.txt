- Proposes a modular compare-aggregate architecture for two-text NLU: attention aligns tokens, a comparison function compares aligned pairs, CNN aggregates to outputs.
- Main contribution: systematic empirical analysis of six comparison functions (NN, NTN, EUCCOS, SUB, MULT, SUBMULT+NN), finding simple element-wise ops (SUB, MULT) and their combo with a small NN perform best.
- Evaluated on MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI with strong/state-of-the-art results and a reproducible pipeline.
- Decision: Accept.
- Paper is practically useful: provides a general, reusable modeling recipe and novel analysis of comparison functions.
- Despite similarity to prior compare-aggregate work, the empirical breadth, ablations, and insights justify contribution.
- Problem: Efficiently and effectively matching two text sequences across tasks, focusing on which comparison function works best.
- Literature placement: Builds on decomposable/compare-aggregate and attentive matching, positioned relative to Parikh et al., Wang & Jiang, He & Lin.
- Results generally support claims; ablations isolate attention, preprocessing, and comparison impacts.
- Limitation: Order-insensitive preprocessing for long sequences (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA) likely limits the performance ceiling.
- Attention formulation: Eq.2 resembles Luong et al.’s bilinear “general” attention more than Bahdanau’s additive attention and should be cited/clarified.
- NTN likely overfits due to high parameter count; recommend low-rank/factorized tensor variants and reporting parameter/regularization effects.
- SUBMULT+NN is similar to Mou et al.’s heuristic matching (|u−v| and u⊙v); acknowledge this relation and note word-level application.
- Preprocessing parameter sharing (Wi, Wu, bi, bu) across Q/A needs justification; untying may help QA/MC.
- Directionality: One-directional attention used; compare to bidirectional/co-attention (Parikh et al.) to show when one-directional suffices.
- Embeddings: GloVe were frozen; experiment with fine-tuning or task-tuned embeddings to check robustness of findings.
- Efficiency: Report throughput and parameter counts per comparison function (NN vs SUB/MULT vs NTN) to clarify trade-offs.
- Question — Attention variant: Is Eq.2 equivalent to Luong “general” attention and why prefer it over additive attention?
- Question — Directionality: Were symmetric/bidirectional attention or co-attention tried, and what were their effects?
- Question — Order modeling: Have lightweight order-aware preprocessors (BiLSTM, temporal CNN) been tried and how do they affect results and comparison-function ranking?
- Question — NTN capacity: What are parameter counts and validation curves per comparison function; would low-rank/factorized NTN mitigate overfitting?
- Question — Preprocessing sharing: Did untying Wi/Wu/biases for Q vs A improve performance for QA tasks?
- Question — Mou et al. relation: Please comment on the relationship between SUB/MULT features and Mou et al., and why element-wise ops work well at token alignment.
- Question — Embedding tuning: Did fine-tuning embeddings change relative performance of comparison functions or close gaps (e.g., on SNLI)?
- Question — Aggregation: Why choose CNN aggregation over pooling or RNNs, and how sensitive are results to CNN window sizes and depths?
