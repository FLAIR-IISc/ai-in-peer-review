- Proposes a compare-aggregate model using attention over LSTM outputs and CNNs to aggregate word-level comparisons.  
- Main contribution: systematic evaluation of different comparison functions, finding element-wise subtraction and multiplication generally perform best.  
- Evaluated on four datasets: MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI.  
- Builds on existing compare-aggregate framework; offers empirical insight into the role of comparison functions.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection due to limited novelty and incremental contribution.  
- Improvements from SUB and MULT are interesting but seen as empirical observations, not a theoretical advance.  
- Experiments are reasonably thorough and results support claims, but gains are not substantial enough for acceptance.  
- Suggests adding qualitative analysis and visualizations of comparison vectors to show where methods succeed/fail.  
- Recommends discussing why SUB and MULT work well, providing theoretical or speculative justification.  
- Asks for clarification on preprocessing/order-insensitivity, exploration of more complex combinations of SUB and MULT, and detailed ablation/hyperparameter sensitivity.
