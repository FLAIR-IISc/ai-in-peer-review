- Paper proposes a compare-aggregate framework for sequence matching and systematically studies different comparison functions.  
- Finds simple element-wise comparison functions (SUB and MULT) often outperform more complex neural and tensor-based comparators.  
- Evaluated on four datasets (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI) with competitive or state-of-the-art results, notably on InsuranceQA.  
- Paper is well-written, builds on prior work (match-LSTM, decomposable attention), and includes released code for reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Strong motivation for studying comparison functions and clear positioning within existing literature.  
- Empirical evaluation is robust with comprehensive baselines and ablation studies showing the importance of preprocessing and attention layers.  
- Figure 2 visualization is hard to interpret; needs higher contrast, clearer axis labels, or an alternative format (e.g., heatmap).  
- Reviewer requests clearer explanation and results showing the individual contributions of preprocessing and attention (include experiments without them).  
- Ask for a brief analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (embedding dims, CNN filter sizes) to assess robustness.  
- Minor typo: change "including a some" to "including some" (page 4, line 5).  
- Specific questions for authors: provide results without preprocessing/attention; theoretical insight why SUB/MULT work well; discuss potential of order-sensitive preprocessing; and detail hyperparameter tuning ranges and selection procedure.
