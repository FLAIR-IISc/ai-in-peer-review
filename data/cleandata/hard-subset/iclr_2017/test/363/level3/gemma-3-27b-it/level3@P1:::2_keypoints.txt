- Proposes a compare-aggregate framework that compares word embeddings with various word-level comparison functions and aggregates with CNNs.  
- Systematic evaluation finds simple element-wise operations (SUB, MULT) often outperform complex neural comparison functions.  
- Demonstrates competitive or state-of-the-art performance on MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI.  
- Strong experimental setup with comparisons to solid baselines and supportive ablation studies.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explains comparison functions and architecture, and includes code for reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Request to quantitatively show the contribution of preprocessing and attention by reporting results without them.  
- Suggest improving Figure 2 visual clarity (larger fonts, clearer highlights).  
- Recommend discussing why NN and NTN comparison functions underperformed (e.g., overfitting, optimization issues).  
- Ask for discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity across different comparison functions.  
- Question: Provide results for experiments without the preprocessing and attention layers.  
- Question: Why was a CNN chosen for aggregation instead of attention mechanisms or recurrent networks?  
- Question: Did you try order-sensitive preprocessing (LSTM/GRU) and how did it compare?  
- Question: Any hypotheses or training indications explaining the negative results for NN and NTN (overfitting, gradient issues, etc.)?
