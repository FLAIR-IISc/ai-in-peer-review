- Decision: Accept — well-motivated paper with thorough experiments and competitive/state-of-the-art results.
- Contribution: Systematic evaluation of different comparison functions within the compare-aggregate sequence-matching framework.
- Key finding: Simple element-wise comparison operations (SUB, MULT) can outperform more complex methods (NN, NTN).
- Datasets: Evaluated on four diverse benchmarks (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI).
- Results: Strong performance overall, notably exceeding prior work on InsuranceQA and WikiQA.
- Experimental rigor: Comprehensive setup with relevant baselines, cross-task evaluation, and inclusion of code for reproducibility.
- Ablation: Ablation study exists but is brief and lacks detailed quantification of preprocessing and attention layer contributions.
- Presentation issues: Minor typo (“including a some”) and Figure 2 is hard to interpret in black-and-white; improve font, colors, and caption.
- Missing analyses: Need results without preprocessing/attention to quantify their impact across datasets.
- Efficiency concern: Paper does not quantify computational cost; trade-offs between accuracy and complexity of comparison functions are unclear.
- Embedding choice: Authors used GloVe but did not report comparisons with other pretrained embeddings (Word2Vec, fastText).
- Request for details: Clarify CNN aggregation hyperparameters (filter sizes, number of filters) and rationale for those choices.
