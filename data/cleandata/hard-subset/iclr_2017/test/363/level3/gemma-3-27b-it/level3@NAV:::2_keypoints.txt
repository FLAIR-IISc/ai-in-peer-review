- Proposes a compare-aggregate framework using various word-level comparison functions followed by CNN-based aggregation.  
- Evaluated on four datasets (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI) with competitive and often superior performance.  
- Well-motivated focus on the under-explored word-level comparison function in sequence matching.  
- Extensive experiments demonstrate generalizability and provide solid empirical evidence—decision: Accept.  
- Finds simple element-wise comparison functions perform surprisingly well, challenging reliance on more complex neural methods.  
- Paper is clearly written and contributions are well articulated; minor typo: “including a some” → “including some.”  
- Preprocessing and attention layers are not fully justified; authors should explain their intended benefits and report results without them.  
- Figure 2 is low-resolution and hard to interpret; improve resolution and clarity.  
- Ablation study is helpful but should include separate analysis of SUB and MULT in the SUBMULT+NN comparison to assess their individual contributions.  
- Builds on prior compare-aggregate work and presents statistically significant improvements over baselines.
