- Decision: Reject — judged incremental because it primarily offers systematic evaluation rather than a novel methodological or theoretical contribution.

- Supporting argument: Several tested comparison functions (e.g., SUB, MULT) were already used in prior work, reducing novelty.

- Supporting argument: Strong empirical evidence but the paper does not substantially advance understanding beyond a thorough experimental study.

- Question 1: Can you provide theoretical or empirical justification for why element-wise subtraction and multiplication capture semantic relations better than neural-network-based approaches?
