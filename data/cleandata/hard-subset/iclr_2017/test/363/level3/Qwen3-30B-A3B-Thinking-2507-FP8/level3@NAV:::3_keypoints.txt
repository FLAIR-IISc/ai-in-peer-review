- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" sequence-matching framework using soft attention, various comparison functions (including element-wise SUB and MULT), and CNN aggregation.  
- Evaluates across MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI, finding simple element-wise comparison functions often outperform complex neural comparators.  
- Empirical analysis is thorough and shows consistent improvements over baselines on three of the four datasets.  
- Recommendation: accept, since the systematic evaluation and surprising effectiveness of simple operations provide sufficient novelty despite being incremental.  
- Note limitation: model is insensitive to word order for longer sequences (all tasks except SNLI), which empirically limits performance.  
- Citation fix: cite Luong et al. (2015) for the attention mechanism, as it better matches their bilinear scoring than Bahdanau et al.  
- Methodological fixes: address NTN overfitting by suggesting a reduced-capacity tensor variant; acknowledge similarity between SubMultNN and Mou et al.'s sentence-level matching; clarify why identical preprocessing parameters are used for both sequences (question vs. answer weighting).
