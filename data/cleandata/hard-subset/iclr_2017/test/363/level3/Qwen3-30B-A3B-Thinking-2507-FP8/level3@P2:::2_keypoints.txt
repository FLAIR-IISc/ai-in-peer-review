- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework that does word-level matching followed by CNN-based aggregation for sequence matching.
- Systematically evaluates six comparison functions, introducing element-wise SUB and MULT operations.
- Demonstrates SUB and MULT outperform more complex neural-network comparison methods across four diverse NLP tasks (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI).
- Shows strong, often state-of-the-art or competitive results with thorough experiments and appropriate metrics (accuracy, MAP, MRR).
- Ablation studies support the importance of key components in the framework.
- Paper is well-written, well-positioned in related work, and provides valuable design insights for sequence matching.
- Addresses prior limitations by testing across multiple tasks and systematically comparing comparison functions.
- Decision: recommend accept due to clear contribution and empirical evidence.
- Request clarification on the contribution of the preprocessing layer and suggestion to report results without it to isolate its effect.
- Note that Figure 2 is hard to read in print and should be improved for publication.
- Minor typo to fix: change "including a some" to "including some."
- Ask authors to clarify how their one-directional attention differs from prior two-directional attention and why it suffices.
- Request more insight into why simple element-wise operations outperform expressive neural networks and whether this generalizes beyond NLP.
