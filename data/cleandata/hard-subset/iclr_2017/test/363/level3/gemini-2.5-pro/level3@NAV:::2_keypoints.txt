- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework that matches text by word-level comparisons followed by CNN aggregation.  
- Systematically evaluates six comparison functions ranging from neural networks to element-wise operations.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness and generalizability across four datasets (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI) with state-of-the-art or competitive results.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Main strength: convincing cross-task validation showing robustness and wide applicability of the architecture.  
- Main strength: empirical finding that simple element-wise functions (SUB, MULT) can outperform more complex parameterized alternatives.  
- Evidence: rigorous multi-task experiments strengthen the claim of general utility.
