- Presents a general "compare-aggregate" framework: soft attention to align tokens, comparison functions on aligned pairs, CNN aggregation for final output.  
- Key contribution is a systematic empirical analysis of six comparison functions showing simple element-wise ops can beat complex, parameter-heavy methods like Neural Tensor Networks.  
- Recommendation: accept — timely, straightforward yet powerful model with strong results on four benchmark datasets.  
- Paper is somewhat incremental but valuable for its generalization of the approach and detailed practical investigation of the comparison component.  
- Primary strength: thorough empirical validation across four distinct tasks, convincingly demonstrating general applicability.  
- Empirical finding: non-parametric SUB and MULT operations are particularly effective.  
- Main limitation: model is insensitive to word order for longer sequences (except SNLI), which constrains its performance ceiling and should be discussed more prominently.  
- Suggestion: clarify that the attention mechanism resembles Luong et al. (2015)'s "general" scoring rather than Bahdanau et al. (2014).  
- Suggestion: consider a low-rank NTN variant to reduce NTN's large parameter count and overfitting risk.  
- Suggestion: explicitly note similarity between SubMultNN and Mou et al. (2016)'s sentence-level matching strategy.  
- Question for authors: why use identical preprocessing parameters for question and answer sequences, and would separate parameters be beneficial given structural and importance differences?
