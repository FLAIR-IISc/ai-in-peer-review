- Proposal of a general "compare-aggregate" framework for sequence matching (QA, entailment) using soft attention, various comparison functions, and CNN aggregation.  
- Systematic evaluation of six comparison functions, including novel element-wise ops (SUB, MULT, SUBMULT+NN).  
- Empirical validation across four datasets (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI) showing state-of-the-art or competitive performance.  
- Code released publicly, enhancing reproducibility and practical impact.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Timely and relevant contribution addressing sequence matching in NLP with representation learning.  
- Strong scientific rigor with extensive experiments and comparisons to baselines and prior work.  
- Incremental but meaningful advance by systematically analyzing comparison functions and generalizing across tasks.  
- Practical impact due to code availability and detailed analysis for future research.  
- Limitation: model insensitive to word order in preprocessing, harming performance on longer sequences.  
- Related-work citation issues: attention aligns more with Luong et al. (2015) and SubMultNN resembles Lili Mou’s work—should be cited.  
- Overfitting risk: Neural Tensor Network (NTN) has many parameters and may overfit; consider reducing dimensions or regularization.  
- Preprocessing shares parameters for questions and answers, which may be suboptimal; task-specific preprocessing should be considered.  
- Questions for authors: performance on longer sequences and potential integration of RNNs/transformers; rationale for choosing CNN for aggregation; evidence of NTN overfitting and mitigation; rationale and experiments for shared vs. task-specific preprocessing.
