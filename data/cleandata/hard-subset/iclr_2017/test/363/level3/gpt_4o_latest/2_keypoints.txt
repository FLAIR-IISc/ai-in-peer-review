- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework using word-level matching and CNN-based aggregation, evaluated across four datasets.

- Systematically compares six comparison functions (including new element-wise SUB, MULT, SUBMULT+NN).

- SUBMULT+NN generally yields the best performance.

- Achieves state-of-the-art or competitive results on MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI.

- Emphasizes the importance of preprocessing and attention layers and releases code publicly.

- Decision: Accept.

- Strength: Novelty and generalizability demonstrated across diverse tasks.

- Strength: Experimental rigor with extensive results and ablation studies.

- Strength: Clear writing and accessible presentation; reproducible code.

- Suggestion: Report results without preprocessing and attention to quantify their contributions.
