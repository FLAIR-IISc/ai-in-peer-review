- Paper proposes a general model for pairwise NLU tasks using soft attention to align two texts, a comparison function on attention pairs, and CNN aggregation to produce outputs.
- Provides detailed empirical analysis of the comparison component.
- Recommends acceptance due to timeliness and substantial contributions to NLU.
- Approach is straightforward, reasonable, and yields competitive empirical results.
- Main limitation: model is largely insensitive to word order for longer sequences, which caps potential performance.
- Suggest mentioning the word-order insensitivity explicitly in the introduction or discussion.
- Attention mechanism resembles Luong et al. (2015) bilinear/general strategy rather than Bahdanau et al.; cite accordingly.
- NTN may overfit due to many parameters—suggest trying an llm-style tensor with smaller output dimension.
- Note that SubMultNN resembles the sentence-level matching strategy from Lili Mou et al.
- Question use of identical preprocessing parameters for question and answer—these may need different weighting.
- Requests from authors: clarify preprocessing details and parameter sharing, explain plans to handle word-order insensitivity, and consider other comparison functions or architectures.
