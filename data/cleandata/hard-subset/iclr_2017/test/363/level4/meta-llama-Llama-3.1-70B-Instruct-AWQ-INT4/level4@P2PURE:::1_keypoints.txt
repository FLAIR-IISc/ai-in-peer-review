- Proposes a compare-aggregate model that uses attentive LSTM outputs, element-wise comparison, then CNN aggregation for semantic sequence comparison (e.g., QA, textual entailment).

- Main contribution: evaluates multiple comparison methods and shows element-wise subtraction and multiplication generally outperform others across four datasets.

- Experimental result: subtraction and multiplication yield consistent performance gains in the evaluated tasks.

- Limitation: work is incremental and lacks strong novelty.

- Suggested improvement: needs deeper qualitative analysis of how different comparison functions behave on varied sentence types.
