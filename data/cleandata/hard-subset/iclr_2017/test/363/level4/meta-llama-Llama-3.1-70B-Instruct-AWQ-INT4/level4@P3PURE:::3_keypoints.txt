- Proposes a general alignment-based NLU model using soft attention to align token pairs and a CNN-aggregated comparison component, with detailed empirical analysis of that component.
- Timely, straightforward representation-learning approach that yields strong empirical results and merits acceptance despite being somewhat incremental.
- Major limitation: for longer sequences the model is insensitive to word order (except SNLI), which caps potential performance and should be emphasized earlier.
- Attention formulation resembles Luong et al. (2015)’s bilinear/general strategy more than Bahdanau’s and should cite the more relevant reference.
- Neural Tensor Network (NTN) risks overfitting due to large parameter count; try a reduced-output tensor (l x l x m) to mitigate this.
- The SubMultNN closely resembles the sentence-matching strategy used in Lili Mou et al.; this similarity should be noted.
- The same preprocessing parameters are used for question and answer; allowing different parameterizations could improve performance.
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution with strengths that outweigh its limitations.
