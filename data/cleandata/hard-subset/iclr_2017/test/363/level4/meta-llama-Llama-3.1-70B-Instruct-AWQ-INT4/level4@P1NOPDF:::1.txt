This paper proposes a compare-aggregate model for NLP tasks that require semantically comparing text sequences, such as question answering and textual entailment.

The basic framework of this model involves applying a convolutional neural network (CNN) for aggregation after an element-wise operation for comparison over the attentive outputs of long short-term memory (LSTM) networks. 

The key contribution of this paper lies in the comparison component, where several different methods for matching text sequences are evaluated. The results show that element-wise subtraction and multiplication operations generally achieve better performance on four different datasets.

While the paper demonstrates the effectiveness of its proposed approach, a notable weakness is that this work is incremental and somewhat lacking in innovation. A more in-depth, qualitative evaluation of how different comparison functions (including subtraction, multiplication, and others) perform on varied types of sentences would be more insightful and strengthen the paper's contributions.
