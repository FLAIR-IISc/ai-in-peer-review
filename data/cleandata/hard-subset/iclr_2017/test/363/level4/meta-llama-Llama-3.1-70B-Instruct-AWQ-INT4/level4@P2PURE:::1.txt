This paper proposes a compare-aggregate model for NLP tasks that involve semantically comparing text sequences, such as question answering and textual entailment. The model's basic framework consists of applying a convolutional neural network (CNN) for aggregation after an element-wise operation for comparison over the attentive outputs of Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks.

A key contribution of this paper is the comparison component, where several different methods for matching text sequences are evaluated, and element-wise subtraction and multiplication operations are shown to generally achieve better performance across four distinct datasets. 

However, a limitation of this work is that it is incremental and somewhat lacking in innovation. A more in-depth qualitative evaluation of how different comparison functions, including subtraction and multiplication, perform on varied sentence types would be a more compelling contribution.
