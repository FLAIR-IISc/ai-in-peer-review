**Summary of the Paper**

The paper presents a general "compare-aggregate" framework for matching text sequences, which is a crucial component in various natural language processing (NLP) tasks, such as machine comprehension, answer selection, and text entailment. The proposed framework consists of four layers: preprocessing, attention, comparison, and aggregation. The authors evaluate their model on four different datasets, including MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI, and demonstrate its effectiveness in various sequence matching tasks.

**Decision**

Based on the review guidelines, I decide to **accept** this paper. The main reasons for this decision are:

1. The paper presents a well-motivated and timely approach to modeling natural language understanding problems with two distinct textual inputs.
2. The authors provide a thorough empirical analysis of the comparison component of the model, which is a crucial aspect of the framework.

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The framework is straightforward and reasonable, and the empirical analysis is thorough and well-executed. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed framework in various sequence matching tasks.

The paper also contributes to the existing literature by providing a detailed analysis of the comparison component of the model, which is a crucial aspect of the framework. The authors evaluate six different comparison functions and demonstrate that some simple comparison functions can achieve better performance than more complex neural network-based comparison functions.
