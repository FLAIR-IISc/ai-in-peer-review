This paper proposes a compare-aggregate framework that performs word-level matching followed by aggregation with convolutional neural networks. It compares six different comparison functions and evaluates them on four datasets, providing extensive experimental results that are compared against various published baselines.

The paper is well-written overall. However, there are a few areas that require attention. On page 4, line 5, the text should be corrected to "including some" instead of "including a some".

A key aspect of the proposed framework is the inclusion of a preprocessing and attention step. However, the benefits of this step are not clearly articulated. To further demonstrate its effectiveness, it would be beneficial to provide results without this step, enabling a more direct comparison.

Additionally, Figure 2 is of poor quality, particularly when printed in hard copy. Enhancing the figure's resolution or clarity would improve the overall readability of the paper.
