1. Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for sequence matching with four layers: preprocessing, attention, comparison, and aggregation.  
2. Introduces six comparison functions: neural network, neural tensor network, Euclidean, cosine, subtraction, and multiplication.  
3. Evaluated on four datasets and achieves competitive performance; some simple comparison functions beat more complex ones.  
4. Recommendation: accept — approach is timely, straightforward, and a substantial contribution.  
5. Well-motivated and general framework applicable to various two-text-input NLU tasks.  
6. Thorough empirical analysis of the comparison component is a key contribution.  
7. Limitation: model is not sensitive to word order, imposing a strong upper bound on performance.  
8. Attention strategy resembles Luong et al. (2015) bilinear/general attention and should be cited.  
9. Suggest reducing neural tensor network output dimension to lower overfitting risk.  
10. Note that SubMultNN is similar to the sentence-matching strategy in Lili Mou’s paper and should be acknowledged.  
11. Recommend using different preprocessing parameters for question and answer in equation (1).  
12. Questions for authors: how to address word-order sensitivity, why shared preprocessing parameters were used, and how to extend the framework to other NLU tasks.
