- Proposes a general model for two-text NLU tasks using soft attention to align tokens, a comparison function on attention pairs, and a CNN aggregator to produce outputs.  
- Provides a detailed empirical analysis of the comparison component and demonstrates competitive performance.  
- Timely and straightforward approach addressing important NLU problems; valuable contribution despite some incremental aspects.  
- Model is largely insensitive to word order for longer sequences, which limits its upper-bound performance and should be noted in intro/discussion.  
- Attention mechanism resembles Luong et al. (2015) (general/bilinear); paper should cite that work for proper attribution.  
- Neural Tensor Network (NTN) risks overfitting due to many parameters; try smaller output-dimension variants (e.g., l x l x m) to mitigate.  
- SubMultNN comparison function is similar to Lili Mou’s sentence-level matching; acknowledge this similarity to clarify novelty.  
- Equation (1) uses shared preprocessing parameters for question and answer; consider separate parameters as they may need different weighting.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, potentially impactful work with minor revisions to address the above points.
