- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework for matching text sequences with four layers: preprocessing, attention, comparison, aggregation.
- Evaluates the model on MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI, showing effectiveness across sequence matching tasks.
- Decision: accept.
- Main reason for acceptance: well-motivated, timely approach for modeling two-input natural language understanding problems.
- Strong empirical analysis of the comparison component, a crucial part of the framework.
- Paper is well-written, straightforward, and the framework is reasonable.
- Experiments show some simple comparison functions outperform more complex neural-network-based functions.
- Suggest adding discussion about the model's insensitivity to word order and its potential performance upper bound.
- Recommend citing Luong et al. (2015) as a more relevant attention reference.
- Suggest using an NTN variant with input dim l and smaller output dim m to reduce overfitting risk.
- Note SubMultNN is similar to the sentence-level matching strategy in Lili Mou's work.
- Propose using different preprocessing parameters for questions and answers, and request more details on preprocessing, multi-task extensions, and convolutional max-value visualizations.
