- Framework: models two-text inputs via soft attention alignments, compares attention query/result pairs, and aggregates representations with a CNN to produce outputs.
- Empirical contribution: provides a detailed analysis of the comparison component, is straightforward, timely, yields strong results, and merits acceptance despite being incremental.
- Limitation: for longer sequences the model is insensitive to word order, imposing a significant upper bound on performance and should be noted earlier.
- Citation: the attention mechanism aligns more closely with Luong et al. (2015) bilinear/general strategy than with Bahdanau; cite the more relevant work.
- NTN concern: the Neural Tensor Network has many parameters and risks overfitting; consider a reduced-rank llm tensor (input dim l, smaller output dim m).
- Similarity: SubMultNN strongly resembles the sentence-matching approach in Lili Mou’s work and this resemblance should be acknowledged.
- Parameter sharing: using identical preprocessing parameters for question and answer (eq.1) may be suboptimal; separate weights might improve performance.
