- Proposes a general approach for two-input NLU tasks using soft attention to align tokens, a comparison function to produce paired representations, and a CNN to aggregate into a final vector.  
- Provides detailed empirical analysis focused on the model’s comparison component.  
- Timely and addresses a major open issue in NLP; approach is straightforward and yields strong empirical results.  
- Considered incremental relative to the authors’ earlier work and Parikh et al., but contributes enough to merit strong acceptance.  
- Major limitation: for longer sequences the model is largely insensitive to word order, imposing a performance upper bound and should be highlighted earlier.  
- Attention mechanism resembles Luong et al.’s general/bilinear strategy more than Bahdanau’s and should cite the more relevant work.  
- NTN variant may overfit due to many parameters; explore an l x l x m tensor (smaller output dim) as a mitigation.  
- SubMultNN closely resembles the sentence-level matching in Lili Mou’s paper and this similarity should be acknowledged.  
- Question about model design: why share the same preprocessing parameters for question and answer inputs when they might benefit from different weights?
