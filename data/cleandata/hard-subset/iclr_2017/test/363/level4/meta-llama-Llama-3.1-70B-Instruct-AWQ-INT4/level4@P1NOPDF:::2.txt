This paper proposes a compare-aggregate framework that leverages word-level matching followed by aggregation using convolutional neural networks. The authors conduct a thorough evaluation of six different comparison functions across four datasets, providing extensive experimental results that are compared against various published baselines.

The paper is well-written overall, effectively conveying the authors' ideas and methodology.

A few detailed comments are provided below to further enhance the manuscript:

* On page 4, line 5, a minor correction is needed: "including a some" should be revised to "including some".
* The authors may want to consider discussing the benefits of incorporating the preprocessing and attention steps in their framework. To further substantiate their claim, it would be helpful to provide results without these steps to demonstrate their effectiveness.
* Figure 2 is somewhat difficult to read, particularly when printed in hard copy. The authors may want to enhance the figure's quality to improve its readability.
