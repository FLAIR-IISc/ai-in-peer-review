- Proposes a "compare-aggregate" model for semantically comparing text sequences (e.g., QA, textual entailment).  
- Claims a general framework effective across sequence-matching problems.  
- Reports state-of-the-art performance on MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI.  
- Systematically evaluates different comparison functions and finds simple element-wise operations can outperform complex neural functions.  
- Reviewer accepts the paper, citing thorough multi-dataset evaluation and systematic comparison as key reasons.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear model explanation, detailed experiments, and good related-work positioning.  
- Main weakness: limited novelty—the framework is not new; contribution is largely empirical evaluation.  
- Suggests adding qualitative analysis of how comparison functions behave on different sentence types to deepen insight.  
- Requests clarification on how comparison functions were selected and whether others were considered.  
- Asks how results would change with a different aggregation method (e.g., RNN vs CNN).  
- Requests more analysis of SNLI results, specifically why the model did not reach state-of-the-art there.
