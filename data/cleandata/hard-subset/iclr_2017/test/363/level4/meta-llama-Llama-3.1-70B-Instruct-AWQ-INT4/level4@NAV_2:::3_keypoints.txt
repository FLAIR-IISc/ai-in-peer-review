- Proposed model: soft attention aligns tokens of two texts, a comparison function derives aligned representations, CNN aggregates them to produce output.
- Paper frames this as a general modeling strategy for paired-text NLU problems and analyzes the comparison component in detail.
- Recommendation: accept — contribution is timely, relevant, and addresses a major open NLP issue.
- Paper is well-motivated, well-situated in prior work, and shows clear domain understanding.
- Empirical support: thorough evaluation on four datasets across different tasks with competitive performance.
- Results indicate the comparison component effectively captures semantic similarity between inputs.
- Contribution: general, task-agnostic approach and detailed empirical analysis of the comparison function.
- Limitation: model’s insensitivity to word order should be discussed more prominently.
- Methodological note: attention is based on Luong et al. ’15 (general/bilinear) and should be cited explicitly.
- Suggestion: explore a smaller-output-dimension NTN to reduce overfitting risk.
- Observation: SubMultNN comparison resembles the approach in Lili Mou’s paper and should be acknowledged.
- Suggestion: consider using different preprocessing parameters for question and answer in equation (1).
- Clarification requested: why the same preprocessing parameters were used for question and answer.
- Clarification requested: how authors plan to address word-order insensitivity in future work.
- Clarification requested: details on computational resources and scalability to larger datasets.
