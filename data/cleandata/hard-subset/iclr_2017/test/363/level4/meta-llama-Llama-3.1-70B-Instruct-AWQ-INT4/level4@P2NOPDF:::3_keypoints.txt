- Proposes a general NLU framework for aligned text pairs: soft attention → comparison function → CNN aggregation → output.  
- Provides a detailed empirical analysis of the comparison component.  
- Timely and important problem; method is simple, reasonable, and yields strong results.  
- Builds on prior work (including Parikh et al. and authors’ own) but makes a substantial contribution.  
- Model appears insensitive to word order for longer sequences (except SNLI), which may limit performance and should be noted in intro/discussion.  
- Attention mechanism resembles Luong et al. (2015)’s general/bilinear attention more than Bahdanau’s and should cite Luong et al.  
- Neural Tensor Network has many parameters and may overfit; explore or discuss a smaller l×l×m output-dimension tensor.  
- SubMultNN is similar to the sentence-level matching in Lili Mou’s work and should be acknowledged.  
- Using the same preprocessing parameters for question and answer needs justification, since different weights might help.  
- Overall a valuable paper that, with revisions addressing these points, could have significant impact.
