- Proposes a compare–aggregate framework for sequence comparison tasks using attentive LSTMs followed by element-wise comparison and CNN aggregation.
- Implements and evaluates several comparison methods within this framework.
- Finds element-wise subtraction and multiplication generally outperform other comparison functions across four datasets.
- Suggests simple element-wise operations can effectively capture semantic relationships between text sequences.
- Novelty is limited—approach is largely incremental over existing techniques.
- Recommends deeper qualitative analysis of comparison functions on diverse sentence types to clarify strengths and weaknesses.
