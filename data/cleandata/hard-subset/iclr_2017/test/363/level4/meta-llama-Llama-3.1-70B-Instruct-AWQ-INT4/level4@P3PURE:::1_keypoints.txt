- Proposes a compare-aggregate model that applies element-wise comparison on attentive LSTM outputs followed by CNN-based aggregation for sequence comparison tasks (QA, entailment).  
- Main contribution is a systematic evaluation of multiple element-wise matching methods, finding subtraction and multiplication generally perform best across four datasets.  
- Drawback: work feels incremental and offers limited novelty.  
- Needs more comprehensive qualitative analysis of how subtraction, multiplication, and other comparison functions behave on varied sentence types to increase impact.
