- Proposal: Introduces a "compare-aggregate" framework doing word-level comparisons followed by CNN aggregation; evaluates six comparison functions on four datasets with competitive performance.

- Decision: Accept — motivated approach and solid placement in literature.

- Main contributions: Shows framework is effective across sequence-matching tasks and finds simple element-wise comparison functions can outperform NN/NTN methods.

- Experiments: Thorough, extensive evaluation with clear analysis and strong writing; good related-work coverage.

- Suggested improvements: Explain benefits of preprocessing and attention more clearly; include results without those steps (ablation); improve readability of Figure 2.

- Questions for authors: Request details on preprocessing/attention contribution, plans for multi-task learning extension, and potential applications beyond the four datasets.
