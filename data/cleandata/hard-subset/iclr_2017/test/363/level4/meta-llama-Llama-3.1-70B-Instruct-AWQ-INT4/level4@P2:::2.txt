**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

This paper presents a general "compare-aggregate" framework for matching text sequences, which performs word-level matching followed by aggregation using convolutional neural networks. The authors evaluate six different comparison functions and test their model on four datasets, achieving competitive performance against various published baselines.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

Based on the paper's content and the experimental results, I decide to **Accept** this paper. The key reasons for this decision are:

1. The paper presents a well-motivated and well-placed approach in the literature, addressing the limitations of previous studies on sequence matching problems.
2. The authors conduct a thorough evaluation of their model on four different datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness and competitiveness against various baselines.

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper's contributions are twofold. First, the authors show that their model, following the "compare-aggregate" framework, is effective for a wide range of sequence matching problems. Second, they conduct a systematic evaluation of different comparison functions, finding that some simple comparison functions based on element-wise operations can work better than standard neural network and neural tensor network comparison functions.

The experimental results are extensive and well-presented, with the authors providing a clear analysis of the results and discussing the implications of their findings. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough review of related work in the field.
