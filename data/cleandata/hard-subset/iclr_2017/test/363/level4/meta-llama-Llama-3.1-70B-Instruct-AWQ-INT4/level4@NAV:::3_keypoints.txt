- Proposes a general framework for two-text NLU: soft attention for token alignment, a comparison function, CNN aggregation to a single output vector, with in-depth empirical analysis of the comparison component.

- Timely and relevant work; straightforward, reasonable approach with promising results and sufficient novelty over prior work to merit strong consideration.

- Model is largely insensitive to word order, which may impose a performance upper bound on longer-sequence tasks; recommend highlighting this limitation in the introduction/discussion.

- The attention mechanism is closer to Luong et al.'s general/bilinear formulation than Bahdanau's; cite Luong et al. or a more relevant reference.

- Neural Tensor Network (NTN) may overfit due to many parameters; consider using a smaller output-dimension tensor (e.g., l x l x m).

- SubMultNN resembles the sentence-level matching method in Lili Mou et al.; acknowledge this similarity.

- Using identical preprocessing parameters for questions and answers (eq. 1) may be suboptimal; consider separate weights for each.
