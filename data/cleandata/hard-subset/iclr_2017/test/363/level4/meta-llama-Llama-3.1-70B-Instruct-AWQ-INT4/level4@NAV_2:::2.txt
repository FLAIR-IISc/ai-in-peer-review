This paper presents a compare-aggregate framework that performs word-level matching followed by aggregation with convolutional neural networks. It compares six different comparison functions and evaluates them on four datasets. Extensive experimental results have been reported and compared against various published baselines.

I recommend accepting this paper, with two key reasons for this choice. Firstly, the paper tackles a specific and well-defined problem in natural language processing, namely, the comparison between sequences. Secondly, the approach is well-motivated and grounded in the literature, and the paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed model on multiple datasets.

The paper is well-written overall, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their proposed framework, including the preprocessing, attention, comparison, and aggregation steps. The experimental results are also well-presented, and the authors provide a thorough comparison with various published baselines.

However, I do have a few minor suggestions for improvement. On page 4, line 5, the text "including a some" should be changed to "including some". Additionally, it would be helpful to provide more insight into the benefits of the preprocessing and attention steps. For example, the authors could provide results without these steps to demonstrate their effectiveness. Finally, Figure 2 is difficult to read, especially when printed in hard copy. The authors may want to consider enhancing the quality of this figure.

Some additional feedback to help improve the paper:

* It would be helpful to provide more discussion on the limitations of the proposed framework and potential avenues for future work.
* The authors may want to consider providing more details on the hyperparameter tuning process and the sensitivity of the results to different hyperparameter settings.
* It would be interesting to see how the proposed framework performs on other sequence matching tasks beyond the four datasets evaluated in the paper.

Questions for the authors:

* Can you provide more insight into why the preprocessing and attention steps are necessary, and how they contribute to the overall performance of the framework?
* How do you plan to extend the proposed framework to other sequence matching tasks, and what challenges do you anticipate in doing so?
* Are there any plans to release the code for the proposed framework, and if so, how will it be made available to the research community?
