This paper proposes a compare-aggregate model for NLP tasks that involve semantically comparing text sequences, such as question answering and textual entailment. The model's basic framework applies a convolutional neural network (CNN) for aggregation after an element-wise operation for comparison over the attentive outputs of Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks.

The key contribution of this paper lies in the comparison component, where it evaluates several different methods for matching text sequences. Notably, the authors demonstrate that element-wise subtraction and multiplication operations generally achieve better performance on four distinct datasets. This finding suggests that these simple comparison operations can effectively capture semantic relationships between text sequences.

However, a limitation of this work is that it builds incrementally upon existing ideas, and its novelty is somewhat limited. To further strengthen the paper, a more in-depth qualitative evaluation would be valuable, examining how different comparison functions (including subtraction, multiplication, and others) perform on diverse types of sentences. Such an analysis would provide deeper insights into the strengths and weaknesses of each comparison method, enhancing the paper's overall impact and contribution to the field.
