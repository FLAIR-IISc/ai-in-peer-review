- Proposes a compare-aggregate model for tasks needing semantic comparison of text sequences (e.g., QA, entailment).  
- Model framework: attentive LSTMs followed by element-wise comparison and CNN-based aggregation.  
- Core contribution: systematic evaluation of multiple comparison methods for sequence matching.  
- Key empirical finding: element-wise subtraction and multiplication generally outperform other matching operations.  
- Evaluation conducted on four different datasets, showing consistent results.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with thorough experimental evaluation.  
- Primary limitation: builds on existing ideas and may be viewed as incremental with limited novelty.  
- Suggested improvement: include qualitative analyses showing how different comparison functions behave on varied sentence types.  
- Overall impact limited by lack of significant innovation and absence of deeper qualitative insights.
