This paper proposes a compare-aggregate framework that performs word-level matching followed by aggregation using convolutional neural networks, and evaluates six different comparison functions across four datasets. The authors provide extensive experimental results, comparing their approach to various published baselines.

The paper is generally well-written. However, there are a few areas that require attention:

* On page 4, line 5, a minor correction is needed: "including a some" should be revised to "including some".
* The benefits of the preprocessing and attention steps are not entirely clear. To better understand their impact, it would be helpful to include results obtained without these steps.
* Figure 2 is difficult to read, particularly when printed in hard copy. The authors may want to consider enhancing the figure's quality to improve its clarity.
