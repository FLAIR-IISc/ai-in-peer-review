- Proposes a compare-aggregate model: apply element-wise comparison over attentive LSTM outputs followed by CNN-based aggregation for sequence matching tasks.
- Primary focus is on the comparison step, evaluating several matching methods.
- Evaluates element-wise subtraction, multiplication, neural networks, and neural tensor networks as comparison functions.
- Empirical result: simple element-wise subtraction and multiplication generally outperform more complex neural-network-based approaches.
- Demonstrates competitive performance on four different datasets, showing effectiveness and generalizability.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on comprehensive evaluation of comparison functions and strong empirical results.
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation of model and experiments.
