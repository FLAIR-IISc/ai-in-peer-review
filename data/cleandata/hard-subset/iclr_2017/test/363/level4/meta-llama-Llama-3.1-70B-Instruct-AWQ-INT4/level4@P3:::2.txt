This paper proposes a compare-aggregate framework that performs word-level matching followed by aggregation with convolutional neural networks. It compares six different comparison functions and evaluates them on four datasets. Extensive experimental results have been reported and compared against various published baselines.

**Decision:** Accept

**Reasons for the decision:** The paper presents a well-motivated and well-executed study on the compare-aggregate framework for sequence matching problems. The authors provide a thorough analysis of different comparison functions and evaluate their approach on multiple datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness and generalizability.

**Supporting arguments:**

* The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it a pleasure to read.
* The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem of sequence matching and the motivations behind their approach.
* The proposed framework is well-motivated and supported by a thorough analysis of different comparison functions.
* The experimental results are extensive and well-presented, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach on multiple datasets.

**Additional feedback:**

* To improve the paper, the authors could provide more insight into the benefits of the preprocessing and attention steps. Specifically, they could provide results without these steps to demonstrate their importance.
* Figure 2 is difficult to read, especially when printed. The authors could enhance the quality of the figure to make it easier to understand.
* The authors could also consider providing more analysis on the results, such as discussing the strengths and weaknesses of each comparison function and how they relate to the specific tasks and datasets.

**Questions for the authors:**

* Can you provide more insight into the benefits of the preprocessing and attention steps? Specifically, can you provide results without these steps to demonstrate their importance?
* How do you think the proposed framework could be extended or modified to handle more complex sequence matching tasks?
* Are there any plans to release the code or data used in the experiments to facilitate reproducibility and further research?
