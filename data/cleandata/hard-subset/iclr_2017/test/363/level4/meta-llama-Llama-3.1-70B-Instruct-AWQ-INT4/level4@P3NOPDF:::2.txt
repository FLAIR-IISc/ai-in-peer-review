This paper proposes a compare-aggregate framework that leverages word-level matching followed by aggregation using convolutional neural networks. The authors conduct a comprehensive evaluation of six different comparison functions on four datasets, providing extensive experimental results that are compared against various published baselines.

Overall, the paper is well-written, clearly conveying the proposed methodology and experimental results. The authors demonstrate a thorough understanding of the research area and provide a well-structured presentation of their contributions.

A few detailed comments are provided below to further enhance the quality of the manuscript:

1. On page 4, line 5, a minor typo is present: "including a some" should be corrected to "including some".

2. The authors incorporate a preprocessing and attention step in their framework. It would be beneficial to provide a clear explanation of the benefits of this step and its impact on the overall performance of the proposed model. To further substantiate this, the authors could provide additional experimental results demonstrating the performance of the model without this step.

3. Figure 2 is somewhat difficult to read, particularly when printed in hard copy. The authors are encouraged to enhance the quality of this figure to improve its readability. This could involve adjusting the font sizes, colors, or layout to ensure that the figure remains clear and concise in various formats.

By addressing these minor issues, the authors can further strengthen their manuscript and provide a more polished presentation of their research contributions.
