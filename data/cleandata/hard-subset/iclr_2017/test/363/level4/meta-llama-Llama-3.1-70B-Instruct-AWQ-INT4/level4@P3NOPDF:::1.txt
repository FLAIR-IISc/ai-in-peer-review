This paper proposes a compare-aggregate model for NLP tasks that require semantically comparing text sequences, such as question answering and textual entailment. The basic framework of this model involves applying a convolutional neural network (CNN) for aggregation after an element-wise operation for comparison over the attentive outputs of Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks.

The core contribution of this paper lies in its comparison component, where several different methods for matching text sequences are evaluated. Notably, the authors demonstrate that element-wise subtraction and multiplication operations generally achieve better performance on four different datasets. This finding provides valuable insights into the design of comparison mechanisms for text sequence matching tasks.

While the paper presents a thorough evaluation of various comparison methods, its primary limitation is that it builds upon existing ideas and may be perceived as incremental work with limited innovation. To further strengthen the paper, it would be beneficial to include a qualitative evaluation of how different comparison functions, including subtraction, multiplication, and others, perform on varied types of sentences. Such an analysis would provide a deeper understanding of the strengths and weaknesses of each comparison method and shed light on the potential applications and limitations of the proposed model.

Overall, the paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' line of reasoning. The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the results are presented in a clear and concise manner. However, the lack of significant innovation and the limited qualitative analysis are drawbacks that prevent the paper from having a more substantial impact.
