- Proposes a compare-aggregate model for semantic sequence comparison tasks (e.g., QA and textual entailment).
- Architecture uses LSTMs with attention, element-wise comparison operations, then CNNs for aggregation.
- Main contribution is systematic evaluation of multiple element-wise comparison methods.
- Empirical result: element-wise subtraction and multiplication generally perform best across four datasets.
- Weakness: incremental contribution and limited innovation; needs deeper qualitative analysis of how comparison functions behave on different sentence types.
