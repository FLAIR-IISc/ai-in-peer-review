This paper proposes a compare-aggregate model for NLP tasks that require semantically comparing text sequences, such as question answering and textual entailment. The basic framework of this model applies a convolutional neural network (aggregation) after an element-wise operation (comparison) over the attentive outputs of the LSTMs.

The key contribution of this paper lies in the comparison component, where several different methods for matching text sequences are evaluated, and element-wise subtraction and multiplication operations are demonstrated to achieve generally better performance on four different datasets.

A potential drawback of this work is that it is incremental and somewhat lacking in innovation. A more comprehensive qualitative evaluation of how subtraction, multiplication, and other comparison functions perform on varied kinds of sentences would be more insightful and increase the paper's impact.
