This paper proposes a compare-aggregate model for NLP tasks that require semantically comparing text sequences, such as question answering and textual entailment. The model's framework applies a convolutional neural network (aggregation) after an element-wise operation (comparison) over the attentive outputs of the LSTMs. The comparison component is the highlighted part of the model, where several different methods for matching text sequences are compared, and element-wise subtraction/multiplication operations are demonstrated to achieve generally better performance on four different datasets.

I recommend accepting this paper, with two key reasons for this choice. Firstly, the paper provides a thorough evaluation of different comparison functions for matching text sequences, which is a valuable contribution to the field of NLP. Secondly, the paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed model on four different datasets, showing its generalizability and robustness.

The supporting arguments for the reasons for the decision are as follows. The paper's evaluation of different comparison functions is comprehensive and well-motivated, and the results provide useful insights into the strengths and weaknesses of each approach. The use of four different datasets also helps to establish the model's generalizability and robustness. Furthermore, the paper's discussion of the results is clear and well-organized, making it easy to follow and understand.

Additional feedback is provided to help improve the paper. While the paper is well-written and easy to follow, there are a few areas that could be improved. Firstly, the introduction could be stronger, providing more context and motivation for the research. Secondly, the paper could benefit from more qualitative analysis of the results, such as a discussion of how the different comparison functions perform on different types of sentences. This would help to provide a more nuanced understanding of the strengths and weaknesses of each approach.
