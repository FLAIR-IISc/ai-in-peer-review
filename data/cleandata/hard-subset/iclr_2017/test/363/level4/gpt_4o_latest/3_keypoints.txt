- Proposes a general soft-attention alignment framework for two-text NLU tasks: attention -> compare -> CNN aggregation -> output, with empirical analysis of the comparison component.  
- Timely, relevant, well-motivated method that achieves strong results; somewhat incremental relative to prior work (authors/Parikh et al.) but deserving of acceptance.  
- Limitation: for longer sequences (all except SNLI) the model ignores word order, imposing a significant upper bound on performance; suggest highlighting this in intro/discussion.  
- Attention used appears closer to Luong et al.'s general/bilinear strategy than Bahdanau et al.; recommend citing Luong et al. or similar.  
- NTN may overfit due to many parameters; ask whether a reduced-output-dimension variant (l x l x m tensor) was tried.  
- SubMultNN resembles the sentence-level matching strategy from Lili Mou et al.; note this similarity.  
- Question why the same preprocessing parameters are used for question and answer—different parameterizations might be beneficial given their different roles.
