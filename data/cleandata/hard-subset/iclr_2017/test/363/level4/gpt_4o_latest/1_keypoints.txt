- Introduces a compare-aggregate model for semantic comparison tasks (QA, textual entailment).  
- Model structure: attentive LSTMs followed by element-wise comparison and a CNN-based aggregation.  
- Main contribution: systematic evaluation of various comparison methods for matching text sequences.  
- Finding: element-wise subtraction and multiplication consistently outperform other comparisons across four datasets.  
- Limitation: work is incremental with limited novelty and lacks qualitative analysis of how different comparison functions behave on varied sentence types.
