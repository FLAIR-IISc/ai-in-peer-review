This paper proposes a comparison-based ensemble model for NLP tasks that require semantic comparison of text sequences, such as question answering and textual entailment.

The core of this model is to apply a convolutional neural network (ensemble) after component-based operations (comparison) on the attentive outputs of LSTMs.

The discussed part is comparison, where this paper compares various methods for matching text sequences, and component-based subtract/multiply operations are demonstrated to achieve better performance in general across four different datasets.

The weak points are that it is a growing field and lacks innovation. A qualitative assessment of how subtract, multiply, and other comparison functions perform on different types of sentences would be more interesting.
