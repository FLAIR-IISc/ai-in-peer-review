This research paper proposes a comparison-based model for NLP tasks that requires meaningful comparison of text sequences, such as question-answer and text-related implications.

The core framework of this model involves applying a convolutional neural network (overall) after element-wise operations (comparison) on the attentive output of an LSTM.

The highlighted part is comparison, where the paper compares several different methods for combining text sequences, and element-wise subtraction/multiplication operations are demonstrated to achieve generally better performance on four different datasets.

While the weak point is that it is a sequential task and lacks innovation, a qualitative evaluation of how subtraction, multiplication, and other comparison functions perform on different types of sentences would be more interesting.
