This paper proposes a compare-aggregate model for NLP tasks that require semantic comparison of text sequences, such as question answering and textual entailment. The model's framework first computes attentive representations of the input sequences using LSTMs, then performs an element-wise comparison between these representations, and finally aggregates the results using a convolutional neural network.

The paper's main contribution lies in its exploration of the comparison step. The authors systematically evaluate several methods for matching text sequences and demonstrate that element-wise subtraction and multiplication operations generally achieve the best performance across four different datasets.

However, a key weakness of the paper is that the work is incremental and demonstrates limited novelty. The contribution would be more compelling with a qualitative evaluation. For instance, an analysis of how subtraction, multiplication, and other comparison functions perform on different types of sentence pairs would provide valuable insight into their respective strengths and weaknesses.
