This paper proposes a compare-aggregate framework for modeling sentence pairs. The method first performs word-level matching between two sentences and then uses a convolutional neural network to aggregate the matching results. The authors conduct a systematic evaluation, comparing six different comparison functions across four datasets. The paper includes extensive experimental results and compares the proposed approach against a wide range of published baselines.

Overall, the paper is well-written and easy to follow.

I have a few detailed comments and suggestions:

*   On page 4, line 5, there is a minor typo: "including a some" should be "including some."
*   The benefit of the preprocessing and attention steps is not explicitly justified. Could the authors provide an ablation study showing the model's performance without these components? This would help clarify their contribution to the overall results.
*   Figure 2 is difficult to read due to low resolution, especially when printed. Please provide a higher-quality version of this figure to improve legibility.
