- Proposes a "compare-aggregate" framework using attentive LSTMs to generate sequence representations, element-wise comparison, and CNN-based aggregation for final prediction.  
- Systematically explores several element-wise matching functions (concatenation, subtraction, multiplication) in the comparison step.  
- Key empirical finding: simple operations—element-wise subtraction and multiplication—are computationally efficient and consistently strong across four benchmark datasets.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and results are clearly presented.  
- Main weakness: work is incremental, composing well-established components (LSTM+attention, element-wise ops, CNN) without substantial technical novelty.  
- Paper lacks qualitative analysis explaining why subtraction and multiplication perform well and in which linguistic scenarios they excel.  
- Recommendation: include concrete examples and analysis (e.g., contradiction, paraphrase, entailment) to provide deeper insight and increase the paper's impact.
