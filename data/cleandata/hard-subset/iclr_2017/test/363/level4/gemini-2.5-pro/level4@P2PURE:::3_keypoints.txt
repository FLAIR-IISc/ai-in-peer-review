- Proposes a general approach for aligning two texts using soft attention, a comparison function over attention pairs, and CNN aggregation; shows strong empirical results and detailed analysis.  
- Timely and sensible contribution to alignment-based NLU tasks, somewhat incremental relative to prior work (including authors' and Parikh et al.) but substantial enough for acceptance.  
- Model is insensitive to word order for longer sequences (all tasks except SNLI), creating a hard ceiling on potential performance; this limitation should be highlighted in the introduction/discussion.  
- The attention mechanism resembles the bilinear/general strategy of Luong et al. (2015) more than Bahdanau et al. (2015); cite Luong or a more directly relevant reference.  
- The Neural Tensor Network (NTN) has many parameters and risks overfitting; try projecting inputs to a smaller dimension (e.g., tensor of size l x l x m with m < l).  
- The SubMultNN component strongly resembles the sentence-level matching strategy in Mou et al.; the similarity should be acknowledged.  
- Question the use of shared preprocessing parameters for question and answer in Eq.(1); separate parameterizations might better capture differing feature needs.
