- Proposes a compare-aggregate model for semantic comparison tasks (e.g., QA, textual entailment).
- Uses attentive LSTM representations, element-wise comparison operations, then a CNN-based aggregation.
- Main contribution is an empirical comparison of several text-matching methods.
- Experiments show simple element-wise subtraction and multiplication perform strongly across four datasets.
- Main weakness is incremental contribution and limited novelty.
- Recommends adding qualitative analysis of how different comparison functions behave on varied sentence pair types.
