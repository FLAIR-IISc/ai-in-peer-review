- Paper proposes a "compare-aggregate" framework: word-level comparisons produce a matrix which a CNN aggregates to predict sentence-pair relations.  
- Evaluated six comparison functions across four datasets; results are competitive and sometimes state-of-the-art.  
- Strength: simple yet effective architecture that separates local word comparisons from global aggregation.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation across multiple datasets and systematic comparison of six comparison functions.  
- Strength: paper is clearly written and motivations/design choices are well explained.  
- Main weakness: no ablation studies to quantify the contributions of model components.  
- Request: isolate impact of preprocessing and attention layers (e.g., ablate attention or use only pretrained embeddings).  
- Minor: Figure 2 is hard to read—regenerate at higher resolution with larger fonts.  
- Minor: typo on page 4, line 5: "including a some" → "including some".
