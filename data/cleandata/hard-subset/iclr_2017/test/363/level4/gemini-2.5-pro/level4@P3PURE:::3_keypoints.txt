- Presents a general soft-attention + comparison + CNN aggregation model for two-alignable-text NLU tasks; shows strong empirical results and recommends acceptance.  
- Timely work addressing multi-text language understanding; model is general, straightforward, and contribution is substantial despite some overlap with prior art.  
- Major limitation: model (for longer sequences) is insensitive to word order, imposing a strong upper bound on performance and should be highlighted more prominently.  
- Attention mechanism is closer to Luong et al.'s bilinear alignment than Bahdanau et al.; Luong (2015) should be cited.  
- Neural Tensor Network (NTN) has many parameters and risks overfitting; suggest trying a smaller output dimension (l x l x m) variant.  
- SubMultNN comparison closely resembles Mou et al.'s sentence-level matching; this similarity should be explicitly acknowledged.  
- Equation (1) uses the same preprocessing parameters for both inputs; authors should justify why shared rather than distinct preprocessing is used or consider separate transforms.
