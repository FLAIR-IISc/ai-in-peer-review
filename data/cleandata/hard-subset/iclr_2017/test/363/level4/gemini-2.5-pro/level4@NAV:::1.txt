This work introduces a compare-aggregate architecture for natural language processing tasks that hinge on the semantic comparison of text sequences, such as question answering and recognizing textual entailment. The model's fundamental framework involves applying an element-wise comparison operation to the attentive outputs of LSTMs, followed by an aggregation step using a convolutional neural network.

The central contribution is a systematic investigation of the comparison stage, where the authors evaluate several methods for matching text sequences. Across four different datasets, the results demonstrate that element-wise subtraction and multiplication operations generally achieve the most robust performance.

However, the primary weakness of this paper is its incremental nature and a relative lack of significant innovation. The work would be more compelling with the inclusion of a qualitative evaluation that examines how the various comparison functions, including subtraction and multiplication, perform on different types of sentence structures and semantic relationships.
