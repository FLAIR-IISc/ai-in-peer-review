- Proposes a compare–aggregate model for semantic comparison tasks (e.g., QA, textual entailment).  
- Model pipeline: attentive LSTM representations → element-wise comparison → CNN aggregation.  
- Main contribution: systematic exploration of the comparison step across multiple matching methods.  
- Empirical finding: element-wise subtraction and multiplication generally perform best on four datasets.  
- Key weakness: work is incremental with limited novelty.  
- Suggested improvement: include qualitative analysis of how different comparison functions behave on various sentence-pair types.
