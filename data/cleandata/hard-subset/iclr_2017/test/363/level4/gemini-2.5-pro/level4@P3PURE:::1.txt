This paper proposes a compare-aggregate model for NLP tasks that require semantic comparison of text sequences, such as question answering and textual entailment. The model's framework consists of applying a convolutional neural network (aggregation) to the output of an element-wise comparison operation performed on attentive LSTM representations of the input sequences.

The primary contribution is a comparison of several methods for matching text sequences. The experiments demonstrate that simple element-wise subtraction and multiplication operations achieve strong performance across four different datasets.

However, the work's main weakness is its incremental nature and limited novelty. The paper would be significantly strengthened by a qualitative evaluation. An analysis of how the different comparison functions (e.g., subtraction, multiplication) perform on diverse types of sentence pairs would offer valuable insights into their respective strengths and weaknesses.
