- Introduces a general framework for two-alignable-text tasks using soft attention, a comparison function, and a CNN aggregator.  
- Provides strong empirical performance and an in-depth empirical investigation of the comparison component.  
- Research is timely and relevant; approach is intuitive and sound with enough novelty to merit acceptance.  
- Model implementation for longer sequences is insensitive to word order, imposing a significant ceiling on capabilities and should be highlighted.  
- The attention variant resembles Luong et al. (2015) more than Bahdanau et al.; Luong (or a more appropriate citation) should be added.  
- Neural Tensor Network (NTN) is prone to overfitting; consider projecting inputs to a smaller output dimension (l x l x m tensor) as an experiment.  
- SubMultNN component strongly resembles the sentence-level matching technique from Lili Mou et al.  
- Request clarification on using shared preprocessing parameters for question and answer in Eq.(1), since separate parameterizations might be beneficial.
