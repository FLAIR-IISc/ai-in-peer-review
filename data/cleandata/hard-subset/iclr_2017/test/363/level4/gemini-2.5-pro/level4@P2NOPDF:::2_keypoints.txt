- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework: word-level comparison functions on contextualized representations, aggregated by a CNN for NLI and sentence similarity.
- Systematic study of six comparison functions with extensive empirical evaluation on four benchmark datasets against many published baselines.
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and easy to follow.
- Major strength: comprehensive experiments and strong, competitive results.
- Strength: systematic comparison yields useful insights about effective word-level interactions.
- Weakness: unclear contribution of the initial preprocessing/attention step — needs an ablation study (model without this step).
- Weakness: lacks deeper analysis or intuition explaining why some comparison functions outperform others; add qualitative analysis.
- Minor: Figure 2 resolution is poor; improve clarity for print.
- Minor: Typo on Page 4, Line 5 — change "including a some" to "including some".
