This work introduces a compare-aggregate architecture that operates by performing word-level matching and subsequently using convolutional neural networks for aggregation. The authors investigate six different comparison functions and assess their performance across four distinct datasets. A comprehensive empirical evaluation is presented, with performance benchmarked against multiple established baselines.

The manuscript is clear and well-organized.

A few suggestions for improvement:
*   On page 4, line 5, there appears to be a minor typo: "including a some" should be "including some".
*   The contribution of the preprocessing and attention steps is not entirely clear. Could the authors provide an ablation study detailing the model's performance without these components to clarify their impact?
*   Figure 2 is difficult to interpret due to its low resolution, especially in the printed version of the paper. Please consider improving its quality for better readability.
