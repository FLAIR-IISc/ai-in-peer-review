- Introduces a compare-aggregate architecture for NLP tasks that require semantic comparison (e.g., QA, textual entailment).
- Model performs element-wise comparison on attentive LSTM outputs followed by aggregation via a CNN.
- Central contribution is a systematic evaluation of various comparison/matching methods.
- Experiments on four datasets show element-wise subtraction and multiplication yield the most robust performance.
- Primary weakness: incremental work with limited novelty and no qualitative analysis of how comparison functions handle different sentence structures or semantic relations.
