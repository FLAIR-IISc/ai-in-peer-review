- Proposes a "compare-aggregate" framework for sequence semantic comparison tasks (e.g., QA, entailment).
- Model pipeline: encode inputs with LSTMs + attention → element-wise comparison → CNN to aggregate comparisons into prediction.
- Main contribution: systematic exploration and evaluation of different element-wise comparison functions.
- Empirical result: simple element-wise operators (subtraction, multiplication) consistently perform strongly across tasks.
- Experiments on four datasets show these simple operations often outperform more complex matching methods.
- Strength: solid empirical study highlighting an effective, efficient comparison approach.
- Weakness: largely incremental—combines established components; novelty is mostly limited to the comparison-function study.
- Recommendation: provide deeper qualitative/analytical insight into why subtraction and multiplication work, including tests on phenomena like negation, paraphrase, and logical inference.
