This paper proposes a compare-aggregate model for NLP tasks that require semantic comparison of text sequences, such as question answering and textual entailment. The model's framework involves applying an element-wise comparison operation to the attentive outputs of LSTMs, followed by an aggregation step using a convolutional neural network.

The paper's primary contribution is a systematic exploration of different methods for matching text sequences. The experiments demonstrate that simple element-wise subtraction and multiplication operations generally achieve superior performance across four different datasets.

However, the main weakness of the paper is its incremental nature and limited novelty. The work could be significantly strengthened by a qualitative evaluation that provides insight into how different comparison functions (e.g., subtraction, multiplication) perform on various types of sentence pairs.
