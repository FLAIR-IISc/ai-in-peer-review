- Introduces a three-stage framework for reasoning over two texts: soft attention alignment, comparison function on aligned pairs, and CNN aggregation into a fixed-size vector for prediction.
- Presents a general, intuitive, and effective representation learning approach with strong empirical performance across tasks.
- Systematic empirical analysis of different comparison functions is a major strength and useful for future work.
- Strong recommendation for acceptance based on contributions and results.
- Limitation: processing comparison vectors as an unordered set makes the model insensitive to word order, potentially capping performance on complex reasoning tasks — should be noted up front.
- Attention mechanism resembles Luong et al. (2015) dot-product/bilinear attention and should cite that work rather than only Bahdanau et al. (2014).
- The SubMultNN comparison function closely resembles Mou et al. (2015)’s sentence-level matching and should be explicitly acknowledged.
- NTN is highly parameterized and prone to overfitting; authors should report if they tried parameter-reduction (e.g., fewer tensor slices) for better generalization.
- Equation (1) appears to tie the same MLP for preprocessing both inputs; authors should justify parameter sharing or report experiments with untied MLPs to allow specialized representations.
