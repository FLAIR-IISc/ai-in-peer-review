- Proposes a general framework for two-text NLU: soft attention to align tokens, a comparison function on attention pairs to produce comparison vectors, then a CNN aggregates them into a fixed-size vector for final prediction.  
- Provides a detailed empirical analysis of the comparison component and demonstrates strong empirical performance across tasks.  
- Timely, intuitive, and effective approach; borderline incremental relative to authors' prior work and Parikh et al. (2016) but contains substantial contributions—reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.  
- Limitation: for longer sequences the model’s aggregated representations do not explicitly encode word order, likely imposing an upper bound on performance and should be noted earlier in the paper.  
- The attention formulation appears closer to Luong et al. (2015)’s bilinear/general attention than Bahdanau et al. (2014); the former should be cited.  
- The Neural Tensor Network (NTN) adds many parameters and may overfit; suggest experimenting with a constrained NTN (e.g., reduce output dim m < l) to lower capacity.  
- The SubMultNN comparison function strongly resembles Mou et al. (2016)’s sentence-level matching and this similarity should be explicitly acknowledged.  
- Question: why use a shared feed-forward preprocessing network for both inputs—separate, non-shared projections might better capture the distinct roles of question vs. source text.
