- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework: attention for soft alignments → comparison functions → CNN aggregation, evaluated on matching two text sequences.  
- Main contributions: demonstrates framework effectiveness across four datasets and provides a systematic empirical analysis of comparison functions.  
- Decision: Accept — reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Rationale: timely and important problem; simple, well-motivated, general framework that achieves excellent empirical results.  
- Rationale: broad evaluation and systematic investigation of comparison functions yields valuable community insights.  
- Key empirical finding: simple element-wise functions (SUB, MULT) outperform more complex functions (NN, NTN).  
- Relation to prior work: framework is similar to Parikh et al. (2016) but differentiated by empirical focus on the comparison component.  
- Model limitation: preprocessing layer is order-insensitive, which may bound performance on syntax-sensitive tasks and should be noted.  
- Citation issue: attention formulation resembles Luong et al. (2015) more than Bahdanau et al. (2014); cite accordingly.  
- Prior-connection: SUBMULT+NN is conceptually similar to Mou et al. (2016).  
- Parameter-tying question: ask whether preprocessing parameters for question and answer should be untied to learn different schemes.  
- NTN concern: NTN may overfit due to many parameters; suggest experimenting with rank-reduced tensor variants to reduce parameters.
