This paper introduces a compare-aggregate framework that first performs word-level matching and then aggregates the results using a convolutional neural network. The authors evaluate six different comparison functions within this framework and demonstrate its performance on four datasets. The paper includes extensive experimental results and comparisons against numerous published baselines.

Overall, the paper is well-written and easy to follow.

A few detailed comments:
*   On page 4, line 5, "including a some" should be corrected to "including some."
*   The benefits of the preprocessing and attention steps are not clearly justified. To demonstrate their contribution, could the authors provide results from an ablation study showing performance without these components?
*   Figure 2 is difficult to read due to its low resolution, especially in a printed copy. Please enhance its quality.
