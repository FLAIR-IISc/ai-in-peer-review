- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework that does fine-grained word-level comparisons then aggregates with a CNN for final prediction.  
- Demonstrates broad applicability and strong performance across four datasets (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI).  
- Conducts a systematic analysis of six word-level comparison functions.  
- Finds simple element-wise functions (SUB, MULT) often outperform complex, parameter-heavy models (NN, NTN).  
- SUBMULT+NN variant yields the best overall performance, highlighting the value of simple, well-motivated operations.  
- Decision: Accept.
