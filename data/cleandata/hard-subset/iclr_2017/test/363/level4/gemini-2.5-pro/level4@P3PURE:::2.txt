This paper proposes a compare-aggregate framework that performs word-level matching followed by aggregation with a convolutional neural network. The authors systematically compare six different comparison functions and evaluate the framework on four datasets. The paper is well-written, and the experimental results are extensive, including comparisons against various published baselines.

A few detailed comments:

*   The contribution of the preprocessing and attention steps is not clearly justified. To better understand the benefit of these components, it would be valuable for the authors to provide an ablation study that reports the model's performance without them. This would help clarify the individual impact of each part of the proposed architecture.
*   Figure 2 is difficult to read, particularly in a printed hard copy. Please enhance the image quality to improve its legibility.
*   On page 4, line 5, "including a some" should be corrected to "including some."
