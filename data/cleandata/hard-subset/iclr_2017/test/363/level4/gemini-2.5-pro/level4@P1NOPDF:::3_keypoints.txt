- Proposes a general modular framework for aligning two texts: soft attention → comparison of attention pairs → CNN aggregation for final prediction.
- Two main contributions: the modular alignment-comparison-aggregation architecture and a detailed empirical analysis of comparison function choices.
- Method is timely and yields strong empirical results on natural language understanding alignment tasks.
- Work is somewhat incremental relative to prior research (including authors' and Parikh et al.) but still makes substantial contributions; strong recommendation for acceptance.
- Model is largely insensitive to word order within each input (except SNLI), which limits performance on tasks where order is important; this limitation should be noted in intro/conclusion.
- The attention mechanism resembles Luong et al. (2015) (general/bilinear) more than Bahdanau et al. (2015); cite Luong or a more relevant reference.
- NTN has many parameters and risks overfitting; authors should try or discuss rank-reduced versions (e.g., l x m x l tensor).
- SubMultNN comparison closely resembles the sentence-level matching method from Mou et al.; note the similarity.
- Equation (1) uses shared preprocessing parameters for question and answer—justify or consider separate transformations since inputs play different roles.
