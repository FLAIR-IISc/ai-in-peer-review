- Proposes a general "compare-aggregate" framework: attention to align, word-level comparison, CNN aggregation for sequence matching.  
- Achieves strong or state-of-the-art results on four datasets (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI).  
- Systematic empirical comparison of six comparison functions.  
- Finds simple element-wise subtraction and multiplication outperform parameterized NN/NTN methods.  
- Demonstrates framework generality across diverse tasks (machine comprehension, answer selection/ranking, textual entailment).  
- Shows that preserving dimensional information with simple similarity-like ops is an effective inductive bias.  
- Builds on prior work (e.g., Parikh et al.) but provides substantial, distinct contributions via breadth and analysis.  
- Potential incremental appearance is mitigated by rigorous cross-task validation and focused ablation studies.

- Suggestion: explicitly relate SUBMULT+NN to Mou et al. (2016)’s sentence-level matching.
