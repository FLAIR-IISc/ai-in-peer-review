This paper presents a compare-aggregate model for NLP tasks that require semantic comparison of text sequences, such as question answering and textual entailment. The model first applies attention over LSTM outputs, performs element-wise comparison operations, and then aggregates the results with a convolutional neural network. The key contribution lies in the comparison stage: the paper evaluates several methods for matching text sequences and shows that element-wise subtraction and multiplication generally yield better performance across four datasets. The main weakness is that the contribution is incremental and somewhat lacking in novelty. A qualitative analysis of how subtraction, multiplication, and other comparison functions behave across different types of sentences would make the work more compelling.
