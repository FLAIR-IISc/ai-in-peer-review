- Proposes a general alignment–comparison–aggregation framework for pairwise NLU (attention to align tokens, comparison module for local matches, CNN aggregator for prediction).
- Careful empirical study of the comparison component; method is straightforward, sensible, and effective.
- Contribution is somewhat incremental relative to prior work but provides enough empirical depth and clarity to warrant acceptance.
- Word-order sensitivity: instantiated model is order-insensitive for longer sequences (except SNLI); this limitation should be explicitly noted and its scope/rationale discussed.
- Attention citation: the attention used is closer to Luong et al. (2015) bilinear/general form than Bahdanau et al.; cite the appropriate reference.
- NTN capacity control: suggest evaluating NTN variants with fewer tensor slices (m < l), low-rank factorization, or weight tying to address overfitting and parameter count.
- Relation to prior matching functions: SubMultNN resembles Lili Mou’s sentence-level matching; explicitly acknowledge this and clarify what is novel.
- Parameter sharing in preprocessing: question tying preprocessing weights across the two inputs; recommend relaxing sharing or reporting comparisons.
- Overall assessment: clear, well-executed paper with solid empirical analysis; recommended for acceptance.
