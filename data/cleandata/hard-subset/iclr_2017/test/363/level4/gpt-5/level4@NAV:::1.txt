This paper introduces a compare–aggregate architecture for NLP tasks that require semantic comparison of text sequences, such as question answering and textual entailment.
The core framework applies an element-wise comparison over the attention-weighted outputs of LSTMs, followed by a convolutional neural network for aggregation.
The focus is on the comparison step: the paper evaluates several sequence-matching methods and shows that element-wise subtraction and multiplication generally achieve better performance across four datasets.
However, the contribution is somewhat incremental and lacks strong novelty; a qualitative analysis of how subtraction, multiplication, and other comparison functions behave on different types of sentences would be more compelling.
