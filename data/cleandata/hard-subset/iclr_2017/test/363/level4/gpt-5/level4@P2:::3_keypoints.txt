- Proposes a compare-aggregate framework: attention-based word alignment, per-word comparison functions, and CNN aggregation for prediction.
- Systematically studies six comparison functions (NN, NTN, EUCCOS, SUB, MULT, SUBMULT+NN) and finds simple element-wise ops often outperform more expressive models.
- Evaluated on four datasets (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI) with state-of-the-art or highly competitive results.
- Decision: Accept.
- Main reasons for acceptance: strong empirical performance across diverse benchmarks and a clear, well-executed ablation/comparison study that gives actionable insights.
- Frames sequence matching as alignment + local comparison + global aggregation and situates work relative to existing compare-aggregate methods.
- Novel contribution is the systematic evaluation showing element-wise comparisons are a robust middle ground between overly flexible and overly compressed measures.
- Empirical ablations show preprocessing gates and attention matter, and analysis links their roles to sequence-length asymmetries.
- SUBMULT+NN consistently performs well, supporting the paper’s central claim.
- Methodology is straightforward and reproducible; code availability is noted.
- Limitation: preprocessing layer is order-insensitive for longer-sequence tasks, which may cap performance; recommend emphasizing this and testing lightweight order-aware encoders.
- Clarify attention formulation and cite Luong et al. if the bilinear/general form is used rather than Bahdanau et al.
- NTN may overfit due to parameter count; try reduced-rank/factorized NTN or smaller output dimensions to strengthen comparisons.
- Clarify relation to prior heuristic matching (e.g., Mou et al.) and articulate novelty at the word-level with attention-weighted alignment.
- Clarify whether preprocessing parameters are shared between Q and A and motivate the choice; compare shared vs. separate parameters.
- Consider experiments with bidirectional or iterative attention to compare with the current one-directional attention.
- Discuss embedding treatment: GloVe frozen and OOVs zero-initialized; evaluate fine-tuning or subword models to address OOVs.
- Provide more hyperparameter and efficiency details (CNN filter sizes/counts, window widths, runtime/memory) and report variance/significance over multiple runs.
- Include a brief qualitative/error analysis to show when order-insensitive preprocessing suffices and where it fails.
