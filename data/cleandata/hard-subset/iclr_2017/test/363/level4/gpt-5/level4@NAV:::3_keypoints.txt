- Proposes a general framework: soft attention aligns two texts, a comparison module forms representations from aligned pairs, and a CNN aggregates them to compute outputs.  
- Presents both a broadly applicable modeling recipe and a detailed empirical study of the comparison component.  
- Timely, sensible approach that delivers strong results; contribution is somewhat incremental but merits acceptance.  
- For longer-sequence tasks (except SNLI) the model is insensitive to word order, which likely imposes an upper bound and should be highlighted in the introduction/discussion.  
- The attention formulation resembles Luong et al. (2015)’s general/bilinear approach more than Bahdanau et al.; cite Luong (or a directly relevant reference).  
- The NTN is prone to overfitting due to large parameter count; try a reduced-dimension tensor (l × l × m) to mitigate this.  
- SubMultNN closely resembles the sentence-level matching strategy in the Lili Mou paper cited.  
- Question why preprocessing parameters are shared for question and answer—these inputs may benefit from different feature emphasis.
