- Proposes a compare–aggregate architecture for semantic text comparison (e.g., QA, entailment): attention over LSTM encodings → element-wise comparison → CNN aggregation.
- Main contribution is a systematic examination of the comparison step, evaluating multiple matching functions.
- Empirical finding: simple element-wise subtraction and multiplication generally outperform other matching functions across four datasets.
- Primary weakness: limited novelty; approach feels incremental.
- Suggested improvement: include qualitative analysis showing how subtraction, multiplication, and other functions behave on different sentence-pair types to explain when and why each is effective.
