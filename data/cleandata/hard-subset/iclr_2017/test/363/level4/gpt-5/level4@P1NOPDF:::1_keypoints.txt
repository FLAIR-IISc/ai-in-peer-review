- Proposes a compare–aggregate model: attention over LSTM encodings, element-wise comparison of attentive representations, then CNN aggregation.  
- Main contribution is systematic evaluation of comparison functions, showing element-wise subtraction and multiplication outperform alternatives across four datasets.  
- Empirical findings are clearly presented and well-supported by results.  
- Paper feels incremental with limited novelty, largely composing known components.  
- Lacks deeper analysis to explain why certain comparison operators work better.  
- Recommends qualitative evaluation of comparison functions on varied sentence-pair types (paraphrases, contradictions, lexical vs. syntactic variation) to clarify conditions and behavior.
