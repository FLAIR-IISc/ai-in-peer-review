- Proposes a general NLU framework for two aligned texts: soft attention to align tokens, token-pair comparisons mapped to vectors, CNN aggregates comparisons for final prediction, with empirical analysis of the comparison module.  
- Reviewer finds the work timely, clear, reasonable, and empirically strong; recommends acceptance despite being somewhat incremental.  
- Limitation: model is largely insensitive to word order for longer inputs (all except SNLI), likely imposing an upper bound on performance; should be noted in intro/discussion.  
- Attention appears closer to Luong et al.’s multiplicative/bilinear attention than Bahdanau’s additive attention—cite Luong et al. (2015).  
- Concern that NTN may overfit due to parameter count; suggest trying a smaller-output-dimension tensor (l×l×m) to reduce parameters while retaining expressivity.  
- SubMultNN closely resembles the sentence-level matching strategy in Lili Mou et al.; acknowledge this conceptual overlap.  
- Question the rationale for sharing preprocessing parameters for question and answer in Eq. (1); recommend justification or an ablation comparing shared vs. separate parameters.
