This paper proposes a compare–aggregate framework that performs word-level matching followed by aggregation using convolutional neural networks. It compares six different comparison functions and evaluates them on four datasets. The experimental section is extensive and includes comparisons against a range of published baselines. Overall, the paper is well written.

A few detailed comments:
- Page 4, line 5: “including a some” should be “including some.”
- What is the benefit of the preprocessing and attention steps? Please provide results without these components to quantify their contribution.
- Figure 2 is hard to read, especially in printed form. Please improve its resolution and increase font sizes to enhance legibility.
