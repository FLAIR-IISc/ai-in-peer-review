This paper proposes a compare-aggregate model for NLP tasks that require semantic comparison of text sequences, such as question answering and textual entailment. The framework encodes sequences with LSTMs, uses attention to produce aligned representations, applies an element-wise comparison operation to the attentive outputs, and then aggregates the resulting signals with a convolutional neural network.

The main focus is the comparison module: the paper systematically evaluates several methods for matching text sequences and shows that element-wise subtraction and multiplication generally yield better performance across four different datasets.

The primary weakness is that the contribution is incremental and offers limited novelty. In addition, a qualitative evaluation examining how subtraction, multiplication, and other comparison functions behave on different types of sentences would make the findings more informative and compelling.
