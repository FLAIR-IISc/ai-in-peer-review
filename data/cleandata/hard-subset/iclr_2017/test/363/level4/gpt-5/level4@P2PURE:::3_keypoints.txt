- Proposes a general NLU model for two aligned texts: soft token-to-token attention, a comparison function over attention pairs, and CNN aggregation to a final vector.  
- Provides a detailed empirical analysis of the model’s comparison component.  
- Work is timely, general, straightforward, empirically competitive; incremental relative to prior work but adds enough to merit acceptance.  
- For longer sequences (all datasets except SNLI) the model is largely insensitive to word order, likely limiting peak performance — should be noted earlier.  
- The attention resembles Luong et al. (2015)’s general/bilinear formulation rather than Bahdanau’s; cite that work.  
- NTN has many parameters and overfits; ask whether a low-rank variant (l × l × m tensor) was tried.  
- SubMultNN is very similar to the sentence-level matching in Lili Mou et al.; this resemblance should be pointed out.  
- Question sharing preprocessing parameters for question and answer — they may need different feature weighting and shouldn’t necessarily share parameters.
