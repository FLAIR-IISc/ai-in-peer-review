- Paper proposes a compare-aggregate model for semantic comparison tasks (QA, textual entailment).  
- Architecture: attention over LSTM outputs → element-wise comparison operations → CNN aggregation.  
- Main contribution: systematic evaluation of comparison methods in the comparison stage.  
- Empirical finding: element-wise subtraction and multiplication generally outperform other comparison functions across four datasets.  
- Weakness: contribution is incremental and has limited novelty.  
- Suggested improvement: add qualitative analysis showing how subtraction, multiplication, and other functions behave on different sentence types.
