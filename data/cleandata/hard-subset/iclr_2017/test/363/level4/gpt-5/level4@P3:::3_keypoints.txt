- Proposes a general compare–aggregate framework for sequence matching with gating preprocessing, one-directional attention, word-level comparison, and CNN aggregation.  
- Systematically evaluates six word-level comparison functions (NN, NTN, Euclidean+cosine, SUB, MULT, SUBMULT+NN).  
- Finds simple element-wise operations, especially SUBMULT+NN, outperform NN/NTN comparators across tasks.  
- Achieves competitive or near-SOTA results on MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI.  
- Ablation studies show gating-based preprocessing and the attention layer are important for performance.  
- Strong cross-task empirical performance with a simple, modular architecture motivates acceptance.  
- Paper fills a gap by focusing on comparison functions within the compare–aggregate family (vs. default NN comparators).  
- Experimental setup includes implementation details and released code, aiding reproducibility.  
- Concerns about order-insensitivity: model is largely order-insensitive in three tasks and this limitation should be highlighted and quantified.  
- Attention formulation is closer to Luong et al. (2015); recommend citing and clarifying the scoring function.  
- NTN comparator may overfit due to many parameters; suggest experiments with reduced dimensionality, low-rank factorization, or stronger regularization.  
- Clarify novelty relative to prior element-wise matching work and whether the contribution is evaluation or new combination insights.  
- Question parameter sharing in preprocessing (tied gates for Q and A); request results for untied or partially shared parameters.  
- Provide aggregation details (CNN filter widths, number of filters, pooling) and comparisons to other aggregators (BiLSTM/GRU/self-attention).  
- Discuss fairness of baseline comparisons, preprocessing/embedding differences, candidate pool setups, and report variance across seeds/statistical significance.  
- Report whether embedding fine-tuning (full or partial) was tried and its effect.  
- Add discussion of efficiency and scalability, especially O(Q×A) attention and NTN vs. element-wise comparator overhead.  
- For SNLI, clarify the added LSTM preprocessing variant and its impact versus the order-insensitive model and decomposable attention.  
- Report results for symmetric/bidirectional attention versus the chosen one-directional attention.  
- For MovieQA tri-partite setup, state whether joint/conditional A–P matching on Q or joint attention among three sequences was tried.  
- Improve visualizations by explaining activation normalization/selection (e.g., max-over-time across filters).
