This paper proposes a compare–aggregate framework that first performs word-level matching and then aggregates the results using convolutional neural networks. It examines six comparison functions and evaluates them on four datasets. The authors report extensive experiments and compare against various published baselines. Overall, the paper is clearly written.

A few detailed comments:
- Page 4, line 5: “including a some” should be “including some.”
- What is the benefit of the preprocessing and attention steps? Could you provide results without them (i.e., an ablation) to quantify their contribution?
- Figure 2 is hard to read, especially in printed form. Please improve its resolution and readability.
