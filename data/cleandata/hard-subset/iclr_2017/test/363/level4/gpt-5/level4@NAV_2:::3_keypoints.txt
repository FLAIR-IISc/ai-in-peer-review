- Proposes a general compare-aggregate framework: soft-attend tokens, compare with various functions, aggregate via CNN for prediction.
- Systematically studies six comparison functions (NN, NTN, Euclid+cosine, SUB, MULT, SUBMULT+NN) across four datasets (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI).
- Finds simple element-wise comparisons often outperform more complex NN/NTN approaches, yielding competitive or state-of-the-art results.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: Strong cross-task empirical validation of a simple, general sequence-matching strategy.
- Key reason: Clear analysis showing lightweight element-wise functions can beat NN/NTN baselines.
- Timeliness/relevance: Provides a clear, reusable blueprint for core NLU sequence-matching tasks.
- Empirical strength: Best or highly competitive results on multiple QA datasets and solid SNLI performance using simple components.
- Insightful analysis: Head-to-head comparison of comparison functions offers practical guidance (SUB/MULT/SUBMULT+NN dominate).
- Simplicity/scope: Straightforward, adaptable architecture that delivers robust gains despite incremental novelty.
- Word-order sensitivity: Preprocessing is order-insensitive for longer-sequence datasets, which may limit performance ceiling and should be noted.
- Attention clarification: Attention appears closer to Luong’s general/bilinear scoring; cite and clarify exact form used.
- NTN capacity/overfitting: NTN has many parameters and may overfit; report reduced-output or low-rank variants as ablations.
- Prior work relation: SUBMULT+NN resembles Mou et al. (2016) heuristics; explicitly position and contrast your word-level formulation.
- Parameter tying: Eq. (1) seems to share preprocessing parameters for Q and A; consider discussing untied parameters and their impact.
- Question—attention: Which exact attention scoring function was used; include explicit form and citation?
- Question—NTN ablations: Were lower-dimensional or reduced-rank NTNs tried, and how did they perform?
- Question—preprocessing tying: Did you try untied preprocessing parameters for Q and A; what was the effect or rationale for tying?
- Question—order sensitivity experiments: Did you test order-sensitive preprocessing (LSTM/GRU) on non-SNLI datasets and how did it trade off with compute?
- Question—relation to Mou et al.: How does SUBMULT+NN at the word level differ from Mou et al.’s sentence-level matching, and what is the novel contribution?
