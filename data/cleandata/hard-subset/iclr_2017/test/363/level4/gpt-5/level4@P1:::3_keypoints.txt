- Proposes a general compare-aggregate architecture: attention-based token alignment, per-token comparison, CNN aggregation for final prediction.
- Systematically evaluates six comparison functions (NN, NTN, EUCCOS, SUB, MULT, SUBMULT+NN).
- Finds simple element-wise comparators (SUB, MULT, SUBMULT+NN) often outperform NN and NTN.
- Evaluated on four datasets (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI) with state-of-the-art or competitive results.
- Decision: Accept — clear general modeling strategy with strong cross-task empirical results.
- Motivation: Argues for word-level comparison with aggregation rather than compressing sequences to single vectors.
- Placement: Builds on match-LSTM, decomposable attention, and pairwise interaction models while consolidating a general framework.
- Contribution: Thorough head-to-head analysis of comparison functions, highlighting underused element-wise operations.
- Empirical ablations: Both preprocessing and attention matter; attention is more critical for disparate-length sequences, preprocessing more for similar-length sequences.
- Consistency: Findings about comparator effectiveness hold across diverse tasks, supporting claimed generality.
- Limitation: Preprocessing layer is order-insensitive for longer sequences, which likely caps achievable accuracy.
- Novelty: Incremental relative to related work, but cross-task validation and comparator analysis provide useful new insights.
- Suggestion: Discuss order-sensitivity limitation earlier and clarify whether order-aware preprocessing (LSTM/GRU) was tried for QA tasks.
- Suggestion: Cite Luong et al. (2015) for the bilinear/general attention formulation rather than Bahdanau et al.
- Question: Did you try reducing NTN output dimension m to mitigate overfitting, and how did it compare to SUB/MULT?
- Question: Clarify relation between SubMultNN and prior element-wise matching (Mou et al., 2016) at the word level.
- Question: Why share preprocessing parameters between question and answer in Eq. (1), and did separate parameters help?
- Question: Did you experiment with order-aware preprocessing on long-sequence tasks and what were the performance/efficiency tradeoffs?
- Question: Confirm the precise attention scoring function and whether it follows Luong’s general/bilinear form.
- Question: Provide parameter counts and training times across the six comparison functions to contextualize performance vs. efficiency.
- Assessment summary: Problem and motivation are clear; placement is appropriate; empirical results and ablations support claims despite noted limitations.
