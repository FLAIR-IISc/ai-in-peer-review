- Presents a general model for two-text NLU: soft attention derives token alignments, a comparison function builds pairwise representations, and a CNN aggregates them for prediction.
- Represents alignments as pairs of attention queries and outputs and provides detailed empirical analysis of the comparison component.
- Approach is timely, simple, general, and achieves strong empirical results.
- Contribution is somewhat incremental relative to the authors’ prior work and Parikh et al., but still advances the area; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
- For longer sequences (all but SNLI) the current implementation is insensitive to word order, which likely limits performance and should be noted in the introduction/discussion.
- Attention formulation appears closer to Luong et al. (2015) than Bahdanau et al.; please cite Luong or another directly relevant reference.
- NTN has many parameters and risks overfitting—try an NTN with input dim l and smaller output dim m (l×l×m tensor) to reduce capacity.
- Point out that SubMultNN closely resembles the sentence-level matching strategy in the Lili Mou paper cited.
- Question the use of shared preprocessing parameters for question and answer inputs—different weighting schemes may be beneficial.
