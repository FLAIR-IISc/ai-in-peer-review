- Proposes a compare-aggregate framework doing word-level comparison with six comparison functions and CNN aggregation for sentence pair matching.  
- Clear, modular architecture separating word-level comparison from aggregation, making it understandable and reusable.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation across four datasets and multiple baselines, systematically comparing six comparison functions.  
- Writing and presentation are generally strong and the paper is well organized.  
- Ablation studies are missing: quantify the contributions of preprocessing and attention (report results without preprocessing, without attention, and with components added independently).  
- Provide interpretation of the six comparison functions: explain when and why specific functions work better, with qualitative insights or examples.  
- Figure 2 is hard to read in print; increase resolution, font size, line thickness, contrast, and ensure grayscale distinguishability.  
- Minor typo: change “including a some” to “including some” (page 4, line 5).  
- Suggest adding an ablation table showing preprocessing and attention effects, at least for a representative dataset per task.  
- Suggest a short discussion interpreting performance differences among comparison functions (robustness, sensitivity to domain/sequence length).  
- Recommend a small error analysis of typical failure cases to clarify limitations and guide future work.  
- Advise noting computational trade-offs (runtime, parameter counts) of attention and complex comparison functions, possibly in the appendix.  
- Overall: solid, well-written paper with comprehensive experiments; addressing ablations, figure readability, and interpretability would strengthen contributions.
