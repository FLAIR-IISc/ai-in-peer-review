- Proposes a general framework for paired-text NLU: soft attention → token-level comparisons → CNN aggregation for prediction.
- Positions the method as broadly applicable and provides focused empirical analysis of the comparison component.

Strengths:
- Timely and relevant work on a central NLP problem (paired-text understanding).
- Clear, modular, and easily adaptable architecture.
- Competitive empirical results across multiple benchmarks despite simple components.
- Valuable, actionable analysis of the comparison function.

Weaknesses/concerns:
- Model is largely order-insensitive for long sequences (except SNLI), which limits performance and interpretability and should be highlighted earlier.
- Contributions are somewhat incremental relative to the authors’ prior work and Parikh et al.; novelty should be articulated more sharply.

Specific suggestions:
- Attention citation: the attention formulation is closer to Luong et al. (2015) than Bahdanau et al.; cite and clarify the scoring function.
- NTN overfitting risk: evaluate reduced-capacity or low-rank/factorized NTN variants to show trade-offs between expressiveness and generalization.
- Relation to prior matching methods: explicitly acknowledge similarity between SubMultNN and Lili Mou’s sentence-level matching and clarify distinctions.
- Parameter-sharing choice: justify tied encoders for both inputs and report results for untied encoders or lightweight adapters to allow asymmetry.
- Order-sensitivity experiments: include ablations or discussion on order-aware aggregation alternatives (order-aware CNNs, RNNs, self-attention) even if left for future work.

Clarity and presentation:
- Well written with a clear modular decomposition that aids reproducibility.
- Recommend adding a concise summary table/figure of comparison-module variants, capacities, and observed behaviors.

Overall assessment:
- Practical, well-motivated framework with strong empirical analysis; some incremental elements and important order-insensitivity limitations remain.
- With minor revisions (better positioning, attention citation, and explicit mention of order-sensitivity), the paper is suitable for acceptance.

Recommendation: Accept.
