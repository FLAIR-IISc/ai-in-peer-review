Review- This manuscript introduces a compare-aggregate framework that first conducts word-level matching and then aggregates using convolutional neural networks. It examines six distinct comparison functions and evaluates them on four datasets. The authors present extensive experimental results and benchmark against multiple published baselines.
The paper is well written overall.
A few detailed comments:
* page 4, line 5: including a some -> including some
* What is the benefit of the preprocessing and attention components? Please also report results without them.
* Figure 2 is difficult to read, especially in printed form. Please enhance its quality/resolution.
