This paper presents a general approach for modeling natural language understanding problems with two different text inputs (e.g., a question and a source text) that can be aligned in some way. In this approach, light attention is first used to create an alignment between the tokens of the two texts, then a comparison function creates a representation using the resulting alignments (presented as attention query and attention result) that are aggregated into a single vector by CNN, from which an output can be computed. This paper presents it as an overall modeling technique that can work quite well and also provides a detailed empirical analysis of the comparison element of the model.

This work is timely. Such language understanding problems are a major unsolved problem in NLP, and are on the verge of being solved through representation learning methods. This work presents a simple and reasonable approach and shows that it can give good results. This work is close (compared to their previous work or Parikh et al.), but it contributes in a significant way that I would strongly recommend adopting.

Details:
- The model, as implemented for at least long sequence problems (everything except SNLI), is not sensitive to word order. It is empirically competitive, but this insensitivity places a strong upper limit on its performance. The paper clarifies this, but it seems important enough to mention briefly in the introduction or discussion section.
- If I understand correctly, your attention strategy is like the '15 general/quadratic strategy' of Lang et al., rather than the previous Bahdanu work. You should probably cite your previous one (or any other reference that is more directly relevant to that strategy).
- Since there is a risk of overfitting due to the large number of parameters in NNTN, have you tried using a version with input dimension l and output dimension m (thus an llm tensor)?
- You should probably mention that SubMLTNN looks like the sentence-level matching strategy in Lilly Ma's paper.
- (1) Is there any reason to use the same parameters for question and answer pre-processing? Different things may need to be given more importance for these.
