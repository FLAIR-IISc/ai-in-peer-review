- Proposes a unified framework for two-text inputs using soft attention to align tokens, a comparison function on attention query/result pairs, and CNN aggregation to produce outputs.  
- Provides in-depth empirical analysis of the comparison component and shows competitive results.  
- Timely and relevant work for representation-learning approaches to language understanding; straightforward and reasonable method.  
- Considered incremental relative to prior work (including authors’ and Parikh et al.) but makes a substantial contribution worthy of acceptance.  
- Limitation: model’s insensitivity to word order (except SNLI) may cap performance on longer sequences and should be highlighted.  
- Attention mechanism resembles Luong et al.’s general/bilinear approach more than Bahdanau’s and should cite the more relevant reference.  
- Risk of NTN overfitting due to large parameter count—suggest exploring a reduced-output tensor (llm) variant.  
- Note the similarity between SubMultNN and the sentence-level matching strategy in Lili Mou et al.; acknowledge this overlap.  
- Using identical preprocessing parameters for question and answer (eq.1) may be suboptimal—consider allowing different weighting schemes.
