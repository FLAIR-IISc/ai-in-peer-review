- Proposes a compare-aggregate framework performing word-level matching followed by CNN aggregation.
- Compares six different comparison functions, including element-wise SUB and MULT.
- Evaluates the model on four datasets (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI) with extensive experiments.
- Paper is overall well-written and presents a thorough investigation.
- Decision: Accept — approach is well-motivated and empirically validated, achieving competitive or state-of-the-art results.
- Clear problem definition and motivation for focusing on the comparison function in sequence matching.
- Strong empirical validation: comprehensive baselines, cross-dataset generalizability, and supporting ablation study.
- Novel contribution in systematically evaluating comparison functions; simple element-wise operations outperform more complex methods.
- Minor typo: "including a some" → "including some".
- Recommend discussing computational complexity and efficiency trade-offs of comparison functions.
- Figure 2 visualization is hard to interpret; improve font size or visual encoding.
- Request more detailed analysis of individual contributions of preprocessing and attention layers.
- Question: clarify choice and effect of embedding dimensionality d.
- Question: justify why order-insensitive preprocessing suffices for MovieQA and InsuranceQA and whether positional information was considered.
- Question: analyze learned weights of the final NN in SUBMULT+NN to understand SUB vs MULT contributions.
- Question: why element-wise comparison functions are not widely used despite strong performance — any hypotheses?
