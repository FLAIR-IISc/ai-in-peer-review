- Proposes a compare-aggregate model for NLP tasks requiring semantic comparison (e.g., QA, textual entailment).  
- Core architecture: element-wise comparison of attentive LSTM outputs followed by convolutional neural network aggregation.  
- Main contribution: systematic exploration of comparison methods, showing element-wise subtraction and multiplication generally outperform others across four datasets.  
- Limitation: work appears incremental rather than a substantial innovation.  
- Suggestion: lacks qualitative analysis explaining why subtraction/multiplication differ in performance across sentence types.
