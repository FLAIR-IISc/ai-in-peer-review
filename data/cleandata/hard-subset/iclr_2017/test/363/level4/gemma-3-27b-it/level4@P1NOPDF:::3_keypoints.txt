- Proposes a general approach for two-text NLU: soft attention to align tokens, a comparison function on attention pairs, CNN aggregation into a single vector, and final output prediction.  
- Demonstrates the approach is viable with detailed empirical analysis of the comparison component and strong results.  
- Timely and relevant work addressing major open challenges in NLP with representation learning.  
- Somewhat incremental relative to prior work (authors’ and Parikh et al.) but makes sufficient contributions to merit strong acceptance.  
- Implementation for longer sequences is largely insensitive to word order, which limits potential performance and should be highlighted in the introduction/conclusion.  
- Attention mechanism appears closer to Luong et al. (2015) than Bahdanau et al.; Luong (or similar) should be cited.  
- NTN has many parameters and risks overfitting—did the authors try a smaller-output-dimension variant (l x l x m tensor) to reduce parameters?  
- SubMultNN closely resembles the sentence-level matching strategy in Lili Mou’s paper and this similarity should be noted.  
- Questioning the use of identical preprocessing parameters for question and answer—different weighting schemes might be beneficial.
