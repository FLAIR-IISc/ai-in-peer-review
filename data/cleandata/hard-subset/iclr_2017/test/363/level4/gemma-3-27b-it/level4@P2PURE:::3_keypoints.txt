- Introduces a general modeling approach for NLU tasks with two aligned texts (e.g., QA, reading comprehension).
- Uses soft attention to align tokens between the two texts.
- Employs a comparison function on attention-derived query-result pairs to generate representations.
- Aggregates comparison representations via a CNN into a single vector for output.
- Provides thorough empirical analysis of the comparison component and demonstrates strong performance.
- Conceptually simple, well-justified, and relevant given current representation-learning advances.
- Builds on prior work (including authors’ and Parikh et al.) but makes substantive contributions warranting acceptance.
- Limitation: model for longer sequences appears insensitive to word order; this should be discussed in intro/conclusion.
- Attention formulation is more similar to Luong et al. (2015) than Bahdanau et al.; that citation should be included.
- Question about overfitting in NTN: did authors try reduced output-dimension variants (e.g., ll → llm tensor)?
- Suggest acknowledging similarity between SubMultNN and the sentence-level matching in Lili Mou et al.
- Ask rationale for using identical preprocessing parameters for question and answer; different weightings may be optimal.
