- Proposes a compare-aggregate model using CNN aggregation after element-wise comparison of attentive LSTM outputs for semantic text comparison tasks.  
- Core contribution is a systematic empirical comparison of matching functions, finding element-wise subtraction and multiplication generally outperform others.  
- Motivation for exploring comparison functions is clearly articulated, noting limits of concatenation/averaging.  
- Framework and experimental setup are well-defined and comprehensive, using established datasets for robust evaluation.  
- Results convincingly show subtraction and multiplication yield consistent, statistically significant improvements over alternatives.  
- Ablation studies effectively isolate the impact of the comparison function.  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes appropriate baselines for fair comparison.  
- Contribution is incremental rather than a major conceptual breakthrough; related ideas have appeared previously.  
- Paper lacks deeper discussion of why subtraction and multiplication are particularly effective (mechanisms like feature interactions or suppression).  
- Recommends qualitative analysis (case studies, examples) to show where different functions excel and to visualize comparison outputs.  
- Suggests analyzing error cases to reveal limitations and future directions.  
- Minor suggestions: provide more hyperparameter tuning details (especially CNN), state computational costs for comparison functions, and clarify applicability to other NLP tasks.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed empirical study with consistent gains, but would benefit from stronger analysis and related-work contextualization.
