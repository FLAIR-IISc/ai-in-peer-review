- Proposal: a compare-aggregate model using element-wise comparison on attentive LSTM outputs followed by CNN aggregation for sequence matching tasks.
- Key contribution: comparative analysis showing element-wise subtraction and multiplication often outperform more complex matching methods.
- Empirical evaluation: comprehensive experiments on four datasets (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI) demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach.
- Result presentation: results are clearly reported and the authors provide code.
- Verdict: Reject — work is sound but contributions are incremental and lack substantial novelty.
- Motivation: builds sensibly on prior attention and pairwise interaction work (Wang & Jiang; He & Lin; Parikh et al.).
- Strength: systematic evaluation reveals the surprising strength of simple element-wise operations.
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis of why SUB and MULT perform well and whether dataset properties favor them.
- Suggestion: study sensitivity to embedding sizes and different CNN architectures.
- Suggestion: perform more granular ablation of preprocessing and attention layers.
- Suggestion: include qualitative error analysis to expose model limitations and failure modes.
- Questions for authors: explain intuition behind element-wise ops and their semantic capture (differences/commonalities).
- Questions for authors: clarify whether order-sensitive preprocessing (LSTMs/GRUs) was tried and report results.
- Questions for authors: for datasets where attention is crucial, show attention-weight examples to illustrate focus.
- Questions for authors: report how performance varies with different word embedding sizes.
