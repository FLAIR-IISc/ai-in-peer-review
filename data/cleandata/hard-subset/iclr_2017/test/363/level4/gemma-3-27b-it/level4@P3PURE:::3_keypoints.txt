- Proposes a general modeling approach for two-text NLU tasks using soft attention to align tokens, a comparison function on attention pairs, and a CNN to aggregate representations, with detailed empirical analysis of the comparison component.
- Timely and practical work that yields strong results; contribution is somewhat incremental relative to prior work but substantial enough to merit acceptance.
- Model (for longer sequences) is largely insensitive to word order, which limits potential performance and should be noted in the introduction/discussion.
- The attention mechanism resembles Luong et al. (2015)’s general/bilinear attention more than Bahdanau’s and should be cited accordingly.
- Large NTN parameterization risks overfitting; authors should consider variants with input dim l and smaller output dim m (an l×l×m tensor).
- The SubMultNN component closely resembles the sentence-level matching strategy from Lili Mou et al. and this similarity should be acknowledged.
- Questioning the choice to share preprocessing parameters for question and answer inputs—different weighting schemes might be more appropriate.
