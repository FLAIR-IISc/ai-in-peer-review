- Presents a compare-aggregate framework using soft attention to align two texts, comparison functions to derive representations, and a CNN to aggregate into a prediction vector.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness and provides detailed empirical analysis of the comparison component.  
- Recommended for acceptance as timely, straightforward, and yielding strong results across diverse NLP tasks.  
- Shows generality by evaluating on four datasets: MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI.  
- Systematic comparison of six comparison functions finds element-wise SUB and MULT often outperform neural and neural tensor approaches.  
- Notes potential incremental contribution but deems it substantial enough for acceptance.  
- Suggests discussing the model’s word-order insensitivity for longer sequences and its potential impact on performance.  
- Recommends citing Luong et al. (2015) (general/bilinear attention) as more directly relevant than Bahdanau et al. (2014).  
- Asks whether a reduced output-dimension NTN (smaller m) was explored to improve parameter efficiency and reduce overfitting.  
- Points out similarity between SubMultNN and Mou et al. (2016) sentence-level matching and suggests acknowledging it.  
- Questions the rationale for using identical preprocessing parameters for both question and answer inputs.  
- Requests clarification on how the hidden-layer dimensionality l was chosen (experimentally or by convention).  
- Asks for sensitivity analysis of attention and preprocessing hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate, dropout).  
- Requests statistical significance measures (confidence intervals or p-values) for claimed state-of-the-art improvements.
