This paper introduces a compare-aggregate framework for sentence similarity, leveraging word-level matching followed by aggregation using convolutional neural networks. The authors systematically investigate six different comparison functions and rigorously evaluate their performance across four datasets. Extensive experimental results are presented, demonstrating competitive performance against a range of established baselines.

Overall, the paper is clearly written and well-structured. The motivation is sound, and the experimental setup appears thorough.

However, a few minor points could be addressed to further strengthen the contribution:

* On page 4, line 5, “including a some” should be corrected to “including some.”

* The role of the preprocessing and attention mechanism is not fully elucidated. It would be valuable to understand the specific benefits these components provide to the overall performance. Presenting results without this preprocessing and attention step would allow for a clearer assessment of their impact. Specifically, quantifying the improvement gained from each component would be informative.

* Figure 2 is difficult to interpret, particularly when viewed in printed form. Enhancing the visual clarity of the figure – perhaps through increased resolution, clearer labeling, or a different visual representation – would significantly improve its readability and impact.
