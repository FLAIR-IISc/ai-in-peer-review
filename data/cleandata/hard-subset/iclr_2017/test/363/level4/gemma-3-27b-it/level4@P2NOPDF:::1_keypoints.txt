- Introduces a compare-aggregate model that performs element-wise comparison on attentive LSTM outputs and aggregates results with a CNN.  
- Main contribution is a comparative analysis of different sequence-matching methods across multiple NLP tasks.  
- Empirical finding: element-wise subtraction and multiplication consistently outperform other comparison functions on four datasets.  
- Suggests subtraction and multiplication effectively capture semantic relationships for QA and textual entailment.  
- Results are solid but represent an incremental improvement rather than a fundamentally novel approach.  
- Paper lacks deeper qualitative analysis explaining how and why subtraction/multiplication succeed or fail on different sentence structures.  
- Recommends analyzing specific failure/success cases (e.g., negation, coreference) to provide nuanced insights.  
- Overall: a practical, empirically supported improvement whose impact would increase with more qualitative investigation.
