The paper presents a general approach to modeling for natural language understanding problems with two distinct textual inputs that require alignment, such as a question and a source text. The approach utilizes soft attention to derive alignments between the tokens of the two texts, followed by a comparison function that leverages these alignments to create representations aggregated by a CNN into a single vector for output computation. The paper demonstrates that this approach is effective and provides a detailed empirical analysis of the comparison function component.

I recommend acceptance of this paper. The work is timely, addressing a major open issue in NLP – language understanding problems involving paired text – and presents a straightforward, reasonable, and effective solution. The empirical results demonstrate the model’s strong performance and contribute substantially to the field.

The model, excluding SNLI, does not consider word order, potentially limiting its performance. While the paper acknowledges this, briefly mentioning it in the introduction or discussion sections would be beneficial. The attention mechanism employed appears more closely aligned with the Luong et al. (2015) approach, and citing this work (or a more relevant reference) would be appropriate. Given the potential for overfitting with the Neural Tensor Network (NTN) due to its parameter count, did you explore a version with a reduced output dimension (e.g., mapping to a smaller 'm' dimension from the initial 'l' dimension)? The SubMultNN comparison function shares similarities with the sentence-level matching strategy proposed by Mou et al. (2016), and acknowledging this connection would be worthwhile. Finally, is there a specific reason for using identical parameters for preprocessing the question and answer in Equation (1)? Allowing for different weighting schemes could potentially improve performance.
