- Proposes a general framework for NLU with two related texts: soft attention to align tokens, compare aligned pairs, then aggregate comparisons via a CNN for output.
- Strong empirical study of the comparison function; approach is simple, effective, and yields strong performance; novel enough despite prior related work—recommend accept.
- Major limitation: model shows lack of sensitivity to word order on longer sequences (except SNLI); paper should explicitly discuss potential performance impact.
- Attention implementation appears closer to Luong et al. (2015)’s general/bilinear attention than Bahdanau’s; Luong (or similar) should be cited.
- NTN component risks overfitting due to high parameter count; authors should report whether lower-dimensional output configurations (smaller m) were tried.
- SubMultNN is very similar to the sentence-matching method in Lili Mou’s paper and this resemblance should be acknowledged.
- Question why identical preprocessing parameters are used for question and answer inputs in step (1); different weighting schemes might be preferable for each input.
