- Decision: Accept.  
- Core contribution: Proposes a compare-aggregate framework for sequence matching using word-level comparisons aggregated by CNNs.  
- Novelty: Systematic comparison of six comparison functions, highlighting often-overlooked element-wise operations.  
- Datasets: Evaluated on MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI.  
- Empirical results: Strong performance, often surpassing state-of-the-art across multiple datasets.  
- Ablation studies: Table 4 ablations support the importance of model components.  
- Code release: Authors provide code, enhancing reproducibility.  
- Positioning: Well-motivated extension of prior work (Wang & Jiang, He & Lin, Parikh et al.).  
- Preprocessing & attention: Need more detailed justification and analysis of why these layers improve performance.  
- Figure 2: Visualization is hard to interpret; recommend clearer fonts, colors, or alternative visualizations.  
- Minor typo: Page 4, line 5 — "including a some" should be "including some."  
- Comparison function analysis: Suggest deeper analysis of dataset characteristics that favor particular functions (e.g., SUB/MULT differences between WikiQA and SNLI).  
- Questions for authors: Explain preprocessing/gating choices and order-insensitivity; report experiments with alternative attention mechanisms; provide higher-resolution/alternative Figure 2; clarify limitations on datasets where the model isn't SOTA.
