This paper proposes a compare-aggregate model for natural language processing tasks requiring semantic comparison of text sequences, such as question answering and textual entailment.
The core framework applies a convolutional neural network (aggregation) following an element-wise operation (comparison) performed on the attentive outputs of LSTMs. 
The key contribution lies in the comparison stage, where the authors explore several methods for matching text sequences, demonstrating that element-wise subtraction and multiplication operations generally outperform other approaches across four datasets.
However, a potential weakness is that this work represents an incremental improvement rather than a substantial innovation. A qualitative evaluation exploring *why* subtraction, multiplication, and other comparison functions perform differently on various sentence types would be particularly insightful.
