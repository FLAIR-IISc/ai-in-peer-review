- Proposes a general model for paired-text NLU: soft attention aligns token pairs, a learned comparison function processes alignments, and a CNN aggregates results to predict outputs.  
- Timely and well-motivated work addressing important paired-text representation learning problems.  
- Strength: general, modular design with a focus on a learnable comparison function enabling task flexibility.  
- Strength: thorough empirical analysis and competitive performance across benchmarks.  
- Weakness: model appears insensitive to word order on longer sequences; paper should discuss impact and explore ways to incorporate order.  
- Methodological note: the attention used aligns more with Luong et al. (2015) than Bahdanau et al. (2015); cite Luong (or other relevant work).  
- NTN concern: NTN’s large parameter count risks overfitting; consider lower-dimensional output mappings to improve parameter efficiency.  
- SubMultNN resembles the sentence-level matching from Lili Mou et al.; acknowledge this relation in related work.  
- Question preprocessing uses shared parameters for both texts—reconsider or justify sharing vs. task-specific preprocessing/weighting.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contributions and experiments; recommend acceptance to ICLR 2017.
