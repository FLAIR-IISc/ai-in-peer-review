- Proposes a general approach for two-text NLU: soft attention aligns tokens into query-result pairs, a comparison function creates contextualized representations, and a CNN aggregates them for output.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance and provides thorough analysis focused on the comparison function component.  
- Method is conceptually simple, broadly applicable, and offers novel improvements over prior work (including Parikh et al.).  
- Concern: model appears insensitive to word order for longer sequences (SNLI is an exception); this limitation and its performance impact should be discussed.  
- Citation issue: the attention mechanism used aligns more with Luong et al. (2015)’s general/bilinear attention than Bahdanau et al. (2015); update citations accordingly.  
- NTN may overfit due to large parameter count; consider reducing output dimensionality (e.g., map l → m and use an llm tensor) to improve generalization.  
- The SubMultNN comparison function closely resembles Lili Mou’s sentence-level matching strategy; highlight this connection for readers.  
- Question on preprocessing: the same parameters are used for question and answer preprocessing—justify shared parameterization or explore independent preprocessing to better weight input-specific features.
