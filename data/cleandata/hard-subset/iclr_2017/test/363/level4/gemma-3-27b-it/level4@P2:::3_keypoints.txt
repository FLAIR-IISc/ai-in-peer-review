- Proposes a "compare-aggregate" framework: attention-based alignment, comparison functions, and CNN aggregation for sequence matching.
- Decision: Accept.
- Main contribution: systematic evaluation of comparison functions, showing element-wise operations can outperform more complex models.
- Strong empirical results across MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, and SNLI.
- Finding: simple element-wise SUB and MULT are computationally efficient, parameter-light, and often superior to NN/NTN.
- Ablation studies and visualizations corroborate the reported behaviors and results.
- Code release improves reproducibility.
- Limitation: preprocessing layer is order-insensitive, so model may ignore word order in longer sequences.
- Suggestion: emphasize the word-order limitation and explore methods to incorporate order (e.g., positional embeddings).
- Citation issue: attention implementation aligns more with Luong et al. (2015) than Bahdanau et al. (2014).
- NTN concern: potential overfitting—consider reduced-dimensionality tensor outputs to cut parameters.
- Prior work connection: SubMultNN resembles Mou et al. (2016); recommend explicit acknowledgment.
- Suggestion: consider task-specific parameterization of the preprocessing layer to weight question vs. answer words differently.
- Request: report computational cost and parameter counts for each comparison function.
- Request: provide intuition for why SUB and MULT are so effective—what semantic relationships they capture?
- Request: show attention weight examples to illustrate attention's role for tasks with large sequence-length differences.
- Request: report any experiments incorporating positional embeddings or other order information into preprocessing and their impact.
