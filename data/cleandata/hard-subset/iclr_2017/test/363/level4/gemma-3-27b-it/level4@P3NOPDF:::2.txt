## Review for ICLR 2017 Submission

This paper presents a compelling compare-aggregate framework for sentence similarity, building upon word-level matching and subsequent aggregation via convolutional neural networks. The authors systematically investigate six distinct comparison functions to quantify word-level similarity and comprehensively evaluate their approach across four diverse datasets. The experimental results are extensive and demonstrate competitive performance against a range of established baselines.

Overall, the paper is well-written and clearly articulates the proposed methodology. The motivation for exploring various comparison functions within a convolutional architecture is sound, and the thorough experimental evaluation lends credibility to the findings.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The systematic comparison of different word-level comparison functions within a convolutional aggregation framework is a valuable contribution. While individual components (word-level comparison, CNNs for sentence encoding) are known, their combination and rigorous evaluation are novel.
* **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The paper’s strength lies in its thorough empirical analysis. Evaluating on four datasets—STS benchmark, SICK-Relatedness, SICK-Entailment, and TREC QA—demonstrates the generalizability of the proposed approach.  The inclusion of numerous baselines provides a strong context for assessing the performance gains.
* **Clear Presentation:** The methodology is explained in a clear and concise manner. The organization of the paper is logical, and the diagrams (with the noted exception below) aid in understanding the proposed architecture.
* **Strong Performance:** The reported results show that the proposed framework achieves competitive, and in some cases, state-of-the-art performance on several of the tested datasets.

**Weaknesses & Questions:**

* **Impact of Preprocessing & Attention:** The paper mentions a preprocessing and attention step prior to the compare-aggregate framework. However, the benefit of these components is not sufficiently justified.  A key question remains: how significantly do these steps contribute to the overall performance? Providing ablation study results *without* the preprocessing and attention mechanisms would clarify their importance and justify their inclusion in the final architecture. This is crucial for understanding the true contribution of the core compare-aggregate framework.
* **Figure Clarity:** Figure 2 is difficult to interpret, particularly in printed form. The details within the figure appear cramped, hindering a clear understanding of the architecture depicted.  Enhancing the visual clarity of this figure – potentially through increased font sizes, improved spacing, or a redesign – is essential.
* **Comparison Function Analysis:** While the paper compares six comparison functions, a deeper analysis of *why* certain functions perform better than others would be beneficial. Is there a correlation between the properties of the comparison function (e.g., sensitivity to magnitude differences, ability to capture semantic similarity) and performance on specific datasets? Such insights could provide more actionable guidance for future research.
* **Computational Cost:** The paper focuses primarily on accuracy. A brief discussion of the computational cost of the different comparison functions and the overall framework, relative to the baselines, would be valuable.

**Minor Comments:**

* Page 4, line 5: “including a some” should be “including some”.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid contribution with a well-defined research question, a clear methodology, and a thorough empirical evaluation. The systematic comparison of word-level matching functions within a CNN framework is a valuable addition to the literature on sentence similarity. Addressing the concerns regarding the impact of preprocessing/attention, the clarity of Figure 2, and providing more nuanced analysis of the comparison functions would significantly strengthen the paper.  I believe that with these revisions, this paper would be a worthy addition to ICLR 2017.



**Confidence:** 7/10 (Strongly Positive, but contingent on addressing the identified weaknesses).
