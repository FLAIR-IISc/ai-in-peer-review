This paper introduces a compare-aggregate model for natural language processing tasks involving semantic text comparison, such as question answering and textual entailment.
The model’s core framework applies a convolutional neural network (aggregation) following an element-wise operation (comparison) performed on the attentive outputs of LSTMs. 
A key contribution lies in the comparison stage, where the authors evaluate several methods for matching text sequences, demonstrating that element-wise subtraction and multiplication operations generally outperform other approaches across four datasets.
However, the work is somewhat incremental and lacks substantial novelty. A qualitative analysis of how subtraction, multiplication, and other comparison functions perform on diverse sentence types would strengthen the findings and be of greater interest.
