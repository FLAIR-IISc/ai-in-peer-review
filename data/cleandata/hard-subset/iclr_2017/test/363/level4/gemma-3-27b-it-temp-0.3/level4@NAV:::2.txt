This paper introduces a compare-aggregate framework utilizing word-level matching and convolutional neural networks for aggregation. The authors systematically compare six distinct comparison functions, assessing their performance across four datasets. The work includes a comprehensive experimental evaluation, with results benchmarked against a range of existing published baselines.

The manuscript is generally well-written and clear. 

Specific comments for consideration:
* Page 4, line 5: Please correct “including a some” to “including some.”
* The motivation for the preprocessing and attention mechanism would be strengthened by presenting results demonstrating their impact – could you provide performance data without these components?
* Figure 2 is difficult to interpret, particularly in printed form. Improving the figure’s resolution and clarity would be beneficial.
