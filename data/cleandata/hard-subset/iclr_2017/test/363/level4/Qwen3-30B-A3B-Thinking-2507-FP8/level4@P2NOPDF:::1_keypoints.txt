- Proposes a compare-aggregate architecture for semantic text comparison (QA, entailment) using attention + LSTM encoding, element-wise comparisons, and CNN aggregation.
- Core contribution is an empirical evaluation of multiple comparison operations, finding subtraction and multiplication consistently outperform alternatives across four benchmarks.
- Provides solid empirical evidence that subtraction and multiplication effectively capture semantic relationships.
- Experimental validation is thorough and results are reproducible.
- The approach is incremental, building on established components (attention, LSTMs, CNNs) rather than offering a novel mechanism.
- Lacks theoretical or mechanistic explanation for why subtraction and multiplication succeed.
- Missing qualitative analysis of operation behavior on nuanced linguistic phenomena (negation, complex syntax, paraphrases).
- Well-written and technically sound, but the incremental nature may not meet ICLR’s novelty standards.
- Suggested improvements: add qualitative analyses and explicitly situate findings within existing comparison methods; currently functions mainly as a reliable empirical benchmark.
