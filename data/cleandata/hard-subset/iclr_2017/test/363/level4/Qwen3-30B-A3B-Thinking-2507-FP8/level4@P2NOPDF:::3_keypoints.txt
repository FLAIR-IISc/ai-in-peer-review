- Proposes a general framework for NLU on paired texts using soft attention to align tokens, a comparison function to build context-aware representations, and CNNs to aggregate for prediction.  
- Framework is conceptually simple, versatile, and achieves strong empirical performance across benchmarks with detailed analysis of the comparison component.  
- Addresses a timely problem of modeling semantic relationships between text pairs and builds meaningfully on modern representation learning.  
- Builds on prior work (e.g., Parikh et al., 2016) but claims substantial contribution via general architecture and comprehensive validation; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.  
- Limitation: model appears insensitive to word order for longer sequences; this performance ceiling should be discussed explicitly.  
- Attention mechanism is bilinear and aligns more closely with Luong et al. (2015) than Bahdanau et al. (2014); Luong et al. should be cited.  
- NTN has high parameter count; authors should consider and report dimensionality-reduction strategies to reduce overfitting on small datasets.  
- SubMultNN resembles the sentence-matching strategy of Mou et al. (2015); the similarity should be acknowledged and distinctions clarified.  
- Using identical preprocessing parameters for question and passage is questionable; authors should justify or explore asymmetric preprocessing.  
- Overall: compelling, analytically grounded contribution with solid results; addressing the above points would strengthen clarity and novelty.
