- Introduces a compare-aggregate model: element-wise operations on attention-weighted LSTM outputs followed by a CNN aggregator.  
- Evaluates multiple comparison mechanisms across four benchmarks, finding subtraction and multiplication consistently best and achieving SOTA on two tasks.  
- Strong contribution: systematic comparison showing simple element-wise operations are highly effective for semantic matching.  
- Thorough evaluation across diverse datasets increases reliability and provides a useful reference for practitioners.  
- Limited novelty: architecture is incremental over existing frameworks (e.g., Match-Simple, Decomposable Attention) with no new structural or theoretical advances.  
- Lacks analysis of why certain operations work better; missing investigation across linguistic phenomena (negation, syntax, discourse), attention visualizations, and error cases.  
- Needs clearer contrasting with prior work and explicit discussion of trade-offs versus more complex alternatives (e.g., neural matching networks).  
- Overall: robust empirical study but conceptual/theoretical weaknesses and insufficient qualitative analysis make it an incremental rather than transformative contribution for ICLR.
