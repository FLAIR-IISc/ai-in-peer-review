- Proposes a compare-aggregate model for semantic comparison tasks (e.g., question answering, textual entailment).
- Architecture: element-wise comparison on attentional LSTM outputs followed by a convolutional neural network for aggregation.
- Main contribution: systematic evaluation of multiple matching strategies; element-wise subtraction and multiplication consistently perform best across four datasets.
- The work is an incremental advance with limited novelty.
- Recommendation: add qualitative analysis showing how subtraction, multiplication, and other comparison functions behave across varied sentence structures.
