- Introduces a compare-and-aggregate framework for word-level matching using CNNs for aggregation.
- Systematically evaluates six distinct comparison functions across four benchmark datasets.
- Provides a comprehensive experimental comparison against multiple established baselines.
- Formalizes a flexible framework and empirically validates its components, showing consistent improvements.
- Choice of comparison functions and datasets is appropriate and results are convincing.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly articulated, and technically accessible.
- Minor typo: change "including a some" to "including some" (Page 4, line 5).
- Missing ablation: authors should justify and quantify the necessity of preprocessing and attention (provide performance with/without these components).
- Figure 2 is hard to read in print—improve contrast, resolution, marker/line styles, or simplify the layout.
- Overall quality is good and the work is suitable for acceptance at ICLR 2017.
