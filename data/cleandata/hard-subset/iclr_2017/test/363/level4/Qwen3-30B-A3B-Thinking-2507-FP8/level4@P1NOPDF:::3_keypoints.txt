- Proposes a general framework for two-input NLU: soft attention to get token alignments, a comparison function on attention pairs, and CNN aggregation for prediction.  
- Framework is straightforward, well-motivated, broadly applicable, and achieves competitive empirical results.  
- Paper builds on prior work (e.g., Parikh et al., 2016) but contributions are substantial enough for acceptance.  
- Model’s insensitivity to word order limits performance on longer sequences and this limitation should be explicitly highlighted.  
- The attention formulation is closer to Luong et al. (2015)’s bilinear approach and should be cited/acknowledged.  
- NTN has high parameter count and overfitting risk; evaluate a reduced-dimension tensor variant as a mitigation.  
- SubMultNN closely resembles Mou et al. (2015)’s sentence-matching strategy and should be explicitly referenced.  
- Using identical preprocessing parameters for both inputs may be suboptimal—authors should justify or explore distinct parameterizations.
