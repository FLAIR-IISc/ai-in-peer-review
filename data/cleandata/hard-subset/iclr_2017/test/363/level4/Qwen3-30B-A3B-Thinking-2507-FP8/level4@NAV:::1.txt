

This paper introduces a compare-aggregate model for NLP tasks requiring semantic comparison of text sequences, such as question answering and textual entailment. The framework employs a convolutional neural network (aggregation) following an element-wise comparison operation applied to the attentive LSTM outputs. The key contribution centers on the comparison module, where multiple matching strategies are evaluated; element-wise subtraction and multiplication consistently demonstrate superior performance across four distinct datasets. However, the work constitutes an incremental contribution with limited novelty. A qualitative analysis of how subtraction, multiplication, and alternative comparison functions behave across diverse sentence structures would significantly strengthen the study.
