- Proposes a general framework for pairwise NLU: soft attention for token alignments, a comparison function on aligned pairs, and CNN aggregation to a single output vector.  
- Shows strong empirical performance and provides detailed analysis of the comparison component.  
- Timely and relevant to ongoing advances in representation learning for language understanding.  
- Approach is straightforward and reasonable, though somewhat incremental relative to prior work (including authors’ earlier papers and Parikh et al.).  
- Model implementation for longer sequences is order-insensitive (except SNLI), which gives competitive results but creates a hard performance ceiling and should be noted in the introduction/discussion.  
- The attention mechanism is closer to Luong et al. (2015)’s bilinear formulation than to Bahdanau et al.; Luong should be cited.  
- NTN risks overfitting due to many parameters; recommend testing a reduced-rank variant (l × m tensor with m < l).  
- SubMultNN is very similar to the sentence-matching method in Lili Mou et al.; acknowledge this resemblance.  
- Using identical preprocessing parameters for question and answer inputs may be suboptimal since they likely need different weighting.
