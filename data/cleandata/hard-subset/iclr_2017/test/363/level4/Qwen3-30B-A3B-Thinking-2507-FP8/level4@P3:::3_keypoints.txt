- Presents a general "compare-aggregate" framework for sequence matching, comparing word-level representations with various functions then aggregating via CNN.  
- Systematically evaluates six comparison functions (including novel element-wise SUB and MULT) across four datasets (MovieQA, InsuranceQA, WikiQA, SNLI).  
- Finds simple element-wise operations (SUB, MULT, and their combination) often outperform more complex neural comparison methods.  
- Demonstrates the framework’s generalizability across multiple sequence matching tasks, not limited to one domain.  
- Decision: Accept — empirical results are strong and findings have clear implications for future work.  
- Key reason: Generalizability shown across three QA datasets and one textual entailment dataset.  
- Key reason: Thorough, systematic evaluation revealing surprising effectiveness of simple element-wise comparisons.  
- Supporting argument: Establishes broader utility of the compare-aggregate approach beyond specific tasks.  
- Supporting argument: Ablation studies show importance of components like attention and preprocessing across tasks with varying sequence lengths.  
- Improvement: Discuss the limitation of order insensitivity in the preprocessing layer more prominently (intro/related work).  
- Improvement: Clarify rationale for using identical preprocessing parameters for both sequences and consider different weighting schemes.  
- Improvement: Explain what is meant by the attention mechanism being "one-directional" (e.g., directionality details).  
- Improvement: Provide more intuition for why SUBMULT+NN performs best beyond stating it combines SUB and MULT.  
- Question: How does order-insensitive preprocessing affect handling tasks where word order is critical, and what trade-offs exist?  
- Question: Why does the model achieve competitive but not state-of-the-art results on SNLI, and how might limitations be addressed?  
- Question: Can the authors provide semantic intuition for why element-wise operations (SUB, MULT) work well for sequence matching?  
- Question: Is there theoretical or empirical justification for using the same preprocessing parameters for both sequences, or would different parameters help?
