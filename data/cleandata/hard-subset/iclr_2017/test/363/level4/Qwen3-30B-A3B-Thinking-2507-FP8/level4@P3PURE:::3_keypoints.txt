- Paper proposes a general NLU approach for two aligned texts using soft attention to get token alignments, pairwise comparison of attention queries/values, and CNN aggregation to produce outputs.  
- Method is broadly applicable and empirically strong, with a detailed analysis of the comparison component.  
- Work is timely and straightforward; contribution is somewhat incremental relative to prior work but still merits strong acceptance.  
- Model (for longer sequences) is insensitive to word order, which limits performance and should be noted in the introduction/discussion.  
- The attention used is closer to Luong et al.’s general/bilinear strategy than to Bahdanau et al.; cite Luong et al. or a more direct reference.  
- NTN risks overfitting due to many parameters; evaluate a variant with input dim l and reduced output dim m (l×l×m tensor).  
- SubMultNN is similar to the sentence-level matching in Lili Mou et al.; acknowledge this similarity.  
- Using identical preprocessing parameters for question and answer (Eq.1) is questionable; consider distinct weighting for each component.
