- Proposes a general approach for aligned two-text NLU: soft attention for token-alignments, compare aligned pairs, aggregate via CNN for output.
- Empirically effective and broadly applicable, with detailed analysis of the comparison component.
- Timely and reasonable contribution; mildly incremental over the authors' prior work and Parikh et al., but substantive enough for acceptance.
- For longer-sequence tasks the implemented model is insensitive to word order, which limits an upper bound on performance and should be noted in the introduction/discussion.
- The attention mechanism is closer to Luong et al. (2015)’s general/bilinear formulation than Bahdanau et al.; Luong (or a more direct citation) should be cited.
- NTN has high parameter count and overfitting risk; test a variant with input dim l and reduced output dim m (l×l×m tensor) to mitigate this.
- SubMultNN is similar to sentence-level matching in Lili Mou et al.; this similarity should be acknowledged.
- Using identical preprocessing parameters for questions and answers (Eq.1) seems unjustified; different weighting schemes may be needed.
