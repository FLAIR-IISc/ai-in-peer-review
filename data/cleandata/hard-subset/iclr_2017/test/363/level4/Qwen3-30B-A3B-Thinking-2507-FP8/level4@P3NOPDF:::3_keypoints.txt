- Proposes a general architecture for two aligned text inputs using soft attention → comparison (e.g., NTN, SubMult) → CNN aggregation, with strong empirical results (including SNLI).  
- Strength: clear, flexible modeling strategy that effectively handles diverse NLU tasks via token-level alignments and principled fusion.  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation of different comparison functions, yielding useful insights into their relative strengths/limitations.  
- Limitation: model is insensitive to word order—CNN aggregation discards sequential information, limiting performance on tasks requiring temporal/structural reasoning; this should be stated explicitly.  
- Limitation: the attention formulation more closely follows Luong et al. (2015) bilinear attention than Bahdanau et al. (2015) additive attention and should cite Luong.  
- Limitation: NTN comparison may overfit due to large parameter count; consider dimensionality reduction (e.g., m < l for the tensor) or mention mitigation strategies.  
- Minor: SubMultNN closely resembles Mou et al. (2015)’s sentence-matching strategy and should be acknowledged.  
- Minor: sharing preprocessing parameters for both inputs (Eq. 1) is questionable—separate encoders or justification for shared weights should be discussed.  
- Overall recommendation: substantial, well-evaluated contribution; strongly recommend acceptance with the above minor revisions.
