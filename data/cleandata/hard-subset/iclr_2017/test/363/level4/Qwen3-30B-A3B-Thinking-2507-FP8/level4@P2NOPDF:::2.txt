

This paper introduces a compare-and-aggregate framework for word-level matching, utilizing convolutional neural networks for subsequent aggregation. The authors systematically evaluate six distinct comparison functions across four benchmark datasets, presenting a comprehensive experimental evaluation against multiple established baselines. The work is well-organized and clearly articulated, making the technical contributions accessible.

The paper makes a solid contribution to the field of text matching by formalizing a flexible framework and empirically validating its components. The choice of comparison functions and datasets is appropriate, and the results demonstrate consistent improvements over prior methods. The writing is generally strong, with logical flow and sufficient detail in methodology.

Minor points for improvement:
*   Page 4, line 5: Correct "including a some" to "including some".
*   Preprocessing and attention steps: The paper states these are included but does not explicitly justify their necessity or quantify their individual contribution. To strengthen the analysis, the authors should provide ablation results demonstrating the impact of removing these components (e.g., performance with and without preprocessing/attention). This would clarify whether the observed gains are primarily due to the core compare-aggregate mechanism or these auxiliary steps.
*   Figure 2: The visualization is difficult to interpret in printed form due to low contrast and dense overlapping elements. The authors should enhance the figure's resolution and clarity, potentially by using distinct line styles, increasing marker sizes, or simplifying the layout for better readability in hard copy.

The overall quality of the work is good, and the results are convincing. The framework is well-motivated, the experiments are thorough, and the presentation is largely clear. Addressing the minor points above would further solidify the paper's contribution and readability. The work is suitable for acceptance at ICLR 2017.
