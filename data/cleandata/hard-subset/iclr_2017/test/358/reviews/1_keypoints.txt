- Proposal: hierarchical generative model with a lower level for points within datasets and a higher level modeling unordered sets of datasets.  
- Core idea: use a "double" variational bound where a higher-level latent describes datasets and a lower-level latent describes individual examples.  
- Importance: hierarchical modeling is an important, high-impact, and under-explored problem in deep learning.  
- Pro: few-shot learning results look good (reviewer not an expert in that area).  
- Pro: the "double" variational bound idea is well presented and appears widely applicable.  
- Question: are minibatches used when training the statistic network, and if so, do they give an unbiased estimator of the full gradient?  
- Concern: training the statistic network on minibatches may prevent learning dataset-wide features (e.g., “any example has a feature”), limiting model expressiveness.  
- Suggestion: hierarchical forecasting (e.g., electricity or sales) could be an interesting practical application.
