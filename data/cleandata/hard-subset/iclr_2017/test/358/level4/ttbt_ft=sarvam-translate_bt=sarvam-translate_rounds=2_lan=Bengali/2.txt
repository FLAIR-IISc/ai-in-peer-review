This paper presents a method for learning to infer things from a set of data points. This method is a hierarchical version of VAE, where the top layer contains an abstract context unit that summarizes a dataset. Experiments show that this method is capable of learning distributions from a small number of examples, enabling "learning while learning."

Overall, this paper is a great addition to the literature on learning from one or a few shots. The method is conceptually simple and elegant, and it seems to work well. Compared to other recent papers on one-shot learning, the proposed method is simple and is based on unsupervised representation learning. The paper is clearly written and a pleasure to read.

The paper's name is overly grand compared to what was done; it doesn't seem to have much in common with the proposed method, unless one wants to mean "statistics innovator."

The experiments were well-chosen, and considering the simplicity of the method, the results of learning from a few shots seem quite good.

The spatial MNIST dataset is interesting and could be a good toy benchmark. The inputs in Figure 4 seem quite dense; shouldn't the method be able to identify distributions with fewer samples? (Small point: the red dots in Figure 4 don't seem to correspond to the meaningful dots claimed in the text.)
