This paper proposes a hierarchical generative model where the lower-level datasets contain points, and the higher-level models consist of unordered sets of datasets. Its core concept is to use a "double" variational bound, where a higher-level latent variable describes the dataset, and a lower-level latent variable describes the individual examples.

Hierarchical modeling is an important and high-impact problem, and I believe it has been under-discussed in the deep learning literature.

Advantages:
- Learning outcomes appear good with a small number of shots, although I am not an expert in this field.
- The concept of using a "double" variational bound in hierarchical generative models is well-presented and seems to be widely applicable.

Questions:
- When training a statistical network, are minibatches (i.e., subsets of examples) used?
- If not, would using minibatches actually give you an unbiased estimator of the complete gradient (if you use all the examples)? For example, if the statistical network wants to extract a specific feature from any example of the dataset and consider it as a feature, what would happen?
