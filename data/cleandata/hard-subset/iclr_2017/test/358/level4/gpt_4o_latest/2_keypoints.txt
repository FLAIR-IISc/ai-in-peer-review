- Introduces a hierarchical VAE with a top-layer context unit that summarizes a dataset for predicting outcomes from sets.
- Method effectively "learns to learn," inferring distributions from few examples.
- Valuable contribution to one-/few-shot learning; conceptually straightforward, elegant, and strong-performing.
- Simpler than recent one-shot approaches and leverages unsupervised representation learning.
- Paper is well-written and enjoyable to read.
- Title feels overly ambitious and the term "statistician" is a poor fit unless interpreted loosely.
- Experiments are thoughtfully designed and few-shot results appear robust given the simplicity.
- Spatial MNIST is an intriguing and potentially useful toy benchmark.
- Concern: Figure 4 inputs look overly dense—should be able to infer distributions with fewer samples.
- Minor issue: red points in Figure 4 don't match the described meaning.
- Question for authors: will the code be made publicly available?
