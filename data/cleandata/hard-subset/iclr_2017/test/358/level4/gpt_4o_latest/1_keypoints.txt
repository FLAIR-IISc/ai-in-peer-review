- Introduces a hierarchical generative model with two levels (point-level and dataset-level) using a "double" variational bound with dataset and example latent variables.  
- Notes hierarchical modeling is important and relatively under-explored in deep learning.  
- Pros: Few-shot learning results are promising.  
- Pros: The double variational bound idea is clearly explained and broadly applicable.  
- Question: Are minibatches (subsets of examples) used to train the statistic network?  
- Question/concern: If minibatches are used, do they give an unbiased estimator of the full gradient, and can the statistic network learn dataset-level features that require seeing all examples (e.g., presence of a feature)?  
- Concern: Training on minibatches instead of whole datasets may limit the model’s expressive capacity.
