- Proposes the "neural statistician," a hierarchical VAE that learns dataset-level representations via a top-level context latent variable.  
- Uses a permutation-invariant "statistic network" to produce summary statistics for entire datasets.  
- Enables unsupervised learning of meaningful dataset summaries that support generative modeling of datasets.  
- Demonstrates utility on clustering, few-shot generation, and few-shot classification tasks.  
- Shows generalization to new classes and distributions from a small number of examples (few-shot).  
- Empirical results are solid and the approach is conceptually simple and elegant.  
- Offers a principled unsupervised alternative to supervised meta-learning methods for few-shot tasks.  
- 1-D synthetic and Spatial MNIST experiments effectively illustrate learning of dataset structure.  
- Few-shot classification results (Omniglot) are compelling given the unsupervised training.  
- Minor concern: the title "neural statistician" may overstate the link to human statistical reasoning.  
- Question about Spatial MNIST: can the model perform well with much sparser input points to substantiate data-efficiency claims?  
- Clarification requested on why selected red points in Figure 4 are considered a "sensible" representative subset.  
- Asks whether the authors will release code and the Spatial MNIST benchmark for the community.
