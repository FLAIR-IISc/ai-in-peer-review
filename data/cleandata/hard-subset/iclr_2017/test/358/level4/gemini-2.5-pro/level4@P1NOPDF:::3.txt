The authors introduce a variant of the variational autoencoder (VAE) that incorporates dataset-level latent variables. The core idea is clearly motivated and well-described. In my view, the paper's most significant contribution is moving beyond the relatively simple graphical model of the standard VAE, thereby introducing more expressive probabilistic structures to the deep learning community.

**Comments:**

*   I am not convinced by the choice of the term "statistician." Learning an approximate posterior over summary statistics is just one of many possible ways to summarize a dataset with a neural network; for instance, one could also use a maximum likelihood approach. The paper's clarity would be improved by avoiding newly-coined terms like "statistic network" and instead using the standard and more accurate terminology of "approximate posterior" for the inference network.

*   The experiments are well-executed, and I appreciate the authors' response to my previous question regarding "one-shot generation." However, I believe the language surrounding this concept, particularly at the end of page 6, requires further clarification. My interpretation of the procedure shown in Figure 5 is as follows: given an input set, the model computes the approximate posterior over the context vector *c*, and then generates new samples from the generative model using context vectors sampled from this posterior. On this point, I would like to request clarification:
    
    a) For the generation process shown, are the data-point-specific latent variables *z* sampled from the prior *p(z|c)* or from their own approximate posterior *q(z|x,c)*?
    
    b) While I agree that the generated samples appear to be of high quality, this is a qualitative assessment. A key advantage of VAEs over GANs is the ability to perform quantitative evaluation using log-probabilities. To that end, a more rigorous evaluation of "one-shot generation" performance would involve reporting the log-likelihood of held-out data. Specifically, the authors could report log *p(x|c)* (with *c* sampled from the approximate posterior of a context set) or the marginal log-likelihood log *p(x)* on held-out datasets. I suspect the log-likelihood performance of this model, when compared to a standard VAE lacking the context variable, would be quite impressive and would significantly strengthen the paper's claims. Given that this is a standard evaluation metric for VAEs, its inclusion seems both valuable and straightforward.
