This paper proposes a hierarchical generative model that operates on two levels: a lower level modeling individual data points within a dataset, and a higher level modeling the distribution over unordered sets of datasets. The core technical idea is to use a "double" variational bound, where a high-level latent variable represents properties of a dataset and a low-level latent variable represents properties of an individual example.

Hierarchical modeling is an important and impactful problem that remains relatively under-explored in the deep learning literature, and this work is a welcome contribution to the area.

**Pros:**
- The idea of using a "double" variational bound in a hierarchical generative model is well-presented and seems widely applicable.
- The results on few-shot learning are promising. While I am not an expert in this particular area, the empirical performance appears strong.

**Questions:**
I have a key question regarding the training of the statistic network. Are minibatches of examples used when computing the dataset-level representation, or is the full dataset always used?

If minibatches are used, it is not clear whether this provides an unbiased estimator of the gradient that would be computed using the full dataset. For instance, consider a scenario where the statistic network needs to capture a property based on the *presence* of a specific feature in *any* example within the dataset. This type of global characterization seems consistent with the graphical model shown in Figure 1 (right). However, if the statistic network is trained on minibatches, it will not be able to reliably learn such a characterization, as any given minibatch may be missing the example with the crucial feature. Using minibatches to train the statistic network, as opposed to the full set of examples, would therefore seem to limit the expressive power of the model. I would appreciate clarification on this training procedure and the authors' thoughts on this potential limitation.

**Suggestions:**
As a suggestion for future work, hierarchical forecasting (e.g., for electricity demand or product sales) could be an interesting and practical application domain for this type of model.
