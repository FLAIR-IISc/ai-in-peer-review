- Proposes a VAE extension that models dataset-level latent variables; compelling motivation and clear methodology, offering a more expressive graphical model.

- Major contribution: departs from conventional VAE graphical model to introduce a richer, dataset-level latent structure.

- Terminology concern: calling the module a "statistician"/"statistic network" is questionable—recommend using established terms like "approximate posterior" to avoid misleading specificity.

- Experiments are well-executed, but the "one-shot generation" description needs clarification (author's interpretation: infer context posterior from an input set, sample context, then generate outputs).

- Clarification requested (a): during generation, are instance-specific latents z sampled from the prior or inferred via the approximate posterior?

- Major missing evaluation (b): need quantitative likelihood-based metrics (e.g., log p(x|c) or marginal log p(x) on held-out data) and comparisons to a baseline VAE without dataset-level context to substantiate one-shot generation claims.

- Suggest improving wording near the end of page 6 and adding the recommended quantitative analyses to strengthen the paper.
