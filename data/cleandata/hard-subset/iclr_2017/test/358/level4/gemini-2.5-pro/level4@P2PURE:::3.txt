This paper proposes a variant of the Variational Autoencoder (VAE) that incorporates dataset-level latent variables. The motivation for this approach is clear and the model is well-described. A key contribution of this work is its exploration of graphical models that are more complex than those found in standard VAEs, thereby introducing more expressive model structures to the deep learning community.

Comments:
- The choice of the term "statistician" is not entirely clear. While the model learns an approximate posterior over summary statistics, this is not the only method for summarizing a dataset with a neural network; for instance, a maximum likelihood approach could also be considered. The paper's clarity could be improved by using standard terminology. Specifically, referring to the network as learning an "approximate posterior" would be more precise and less ambiguous than coining new terms like "statistic network."

- The experiments are well-executed, and I thank the authors for their response to my previous question about "one-shot generation." However, the description of this concept, particularly at the end of page 6, requires further clarification. As I understand it, the process shown in Figure 5 involves first computing the approximate posterior over the context vector `c` given an input set, and then generating new samples from the forward model conditioned on draws from this posterior. Clarification is needed on the following points:
    (a) When generating new data, are the instance-specific latent vectors `z` drawn from the prior (the forward model) or inferred using the approximate posterior?
    (b) While the generated samples appear to be of high quality, this is a qualitative assessment. A major advantage of VAEs over GANs is their ability to provide a quantitative measure of performance through log-probabilities. A more rigorous evaluation of the "one-shot generation" capability would involve reporting quantitative metrics, such as the log-likelihood of held-out data. This could be `log p(x | c)`, where `c` is inferred from a support set, or the marginal log-likelihood `log p(x)` for new, unseen datasets. I suspect that the proposed model would demonstrate a significant improvement in log-likelihood over a vanilla VAE baseline that lacks the context variable. Given the potential to strongly bolster the paper's claims, it is unclear why this quantitative analysis has been omitted.
