- Introduces the Neural Statistician: a hierarchical generative model (VAE extension) that learns dataset-level representations via a dataset-level context c and per-example latent z.
- Proposes a learned, unsupervised "statistic network" q(c|D) to infer posterior over the context and compute dataset summary statistics.
- Demonstrates capabilities on clustering, few-shot generation, and few-shot classification, with strong Omniglot results.
- Decision: Accept.
- Addresses the under-explored problem of learning representations for sets of data points; hierarchical modeling is well-motivated and impactful.
- Presents an elegant, principled extension of VAEs using a variational objective to learn dataset statistics; broadly applicable.
- Formal hierarchical approach separates within-dataset vs between-dataset variability and enables unsupervised discovery of dataset-level features.
- Experimental validation is thorough across simple (1-D) to complex (faces, characters) datasets.
- Main concern: unclear minibatching strategy—does q(c|D) process entire datasets or subsampled examples during each gradient update?
- Subsampling within datasets could prevent learning statistics that require full-set information (e.g., global max) and may limit the statistic network’s expressive power.
- Needs clarification whether intra-dataset minibatching yields an unbiased estimator of the gradient of the full-dataset variational lower bound LD and the theoretical implications.
- Suggestion: consider exploring practical applications such as hierarchical forecasting (e.g., related time series across products/regions).
