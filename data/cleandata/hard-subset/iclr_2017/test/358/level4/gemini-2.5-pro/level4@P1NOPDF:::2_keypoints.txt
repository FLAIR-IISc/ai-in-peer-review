- Reviewer submitted late due to technical issues with OpenReview.  
- Paper proposes a hierarchical VAE for learning from sets, with a dataset-level context unit enabling "learning to learn."  
- Method framed as useful for inferring distributions from few examples (one- and few-shot learning).  
- Contribution is valuable: conceptually simple, elegant, and empirically strong.  
- Key advantage: relative simplicity and basis in unsupervised representation learning compared to recent one-shot methods.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to read.  
- Title is overly ambitious and the analogy to a human statistician is misleading.  
- Experiments are well-chosen and few-shot results are strong given model simplicity.  
- Spatial MNIST dataset is an interesting and potentially useful toy benchmark.  
- Concern: Figure 4’s input point sets look overly dense; model might be expected to infer distributions from fewer samples.  
- Minor nit: red points in Figure 4 do not match the meaningful locations described in the text.  
- Asks whether the authors will release their code.
