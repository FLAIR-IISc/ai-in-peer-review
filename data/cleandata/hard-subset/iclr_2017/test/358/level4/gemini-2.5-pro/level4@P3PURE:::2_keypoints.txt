- Reviewer apologized for late submission due to OpenReview technical issues.
- Paper proposes learning from sets via a hierarchical VAE with a top-layer context unit summarizing datasets.
- Model demonstrates "learning to learn" by inferring distributions from few examples.
- Contribution is valuable for one- and few-shot learning.
- Method is conceptually simple, elegant, and performs well; notable for simplicity and unsupervised representation learning foundation.
- Paper is clearly written and enjoyable to review.
- Title is overly grandiose; model lacks strong resemblance to a human statistician unless the term is used loosely.
- Experiments are well-chosen; few-shot results are solid and spatial MNIST is a useful toy benchmark.
- Question about Figure 4: input samples appear dense and may require fewer samples to recognize the simple underlying distribution; request for authors' comment.
- Minor Figure 4 issue: red points do not seem to correspond to the claimed "meaningful points."
- Inquiry whether the authors will release their code.
