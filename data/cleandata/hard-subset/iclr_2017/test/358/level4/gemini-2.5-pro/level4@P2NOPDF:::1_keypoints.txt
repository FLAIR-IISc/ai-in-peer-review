- Introduces a hierarchical generative model with two levels: per-example latent variables and a dataset-level latent variable using a nested ("double") variational bound.  
- Tackles the under-explored problem of hierarchical modeling within deep learning.  
- Framework is clearly presented, elegant, and broadly applicable to nested/grouped data problems.  
- Empirical results on few-shot learning are strong and competitive.  
- Major concern: unclear how the statistic network is trained—does it process entire datasets or minibatches?  
- If trained with minibatches, gradient estimates may be biased and the statistic network may be unable to capture global dataset-level properties (e.g., existential features present anywhere in the set).  
- This limitation could restrict the model to learning decomposable statistics (means, variances) rather than holistic set properties.  
- Authors should clarify the implementation/training details and theoretical implications of using minibatches for the statistic network.  
- Suggest exploring practical applications such as hierarchical time-series forecasting (electricity consumption, sales) to demonstrate broader utility.
