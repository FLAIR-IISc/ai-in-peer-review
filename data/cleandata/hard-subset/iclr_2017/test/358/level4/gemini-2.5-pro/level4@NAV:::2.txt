This paper introduces a method for learning from sets of data points, framed as a hierarchical Variational Autoencoder. In this model, the highest layer features an abstract context unit that generates a summary of a given dataset. The experiments effectively demonstrate that the method is capable of meta-learning, acquiring the ability to infer distributions from a limited number of examples.

Overall, this work is a valuable contribution to the field of one-shot and few-shot learning. The approach is conceptually simple and elegant while delivering strong performance. A key advantage over other recent methods is its relative simplicity and its foundation in unsupervised representation learning. The paper is well-written and a pleasure to follow.

The title of the paper may be overly ambitious relative to the described contribution; the proposed method's connection to the functions of a statistician is not immediately apparent.

The experiments are thoughtfully designed, and the few-shot learning results are quite compelling, especially considering the model's simplicity. The proposed spatial MNIST dataset is an interesting contribution and has the potential to become a useful toy benchmark. A question regarding Figure 4: the input samples appear rather dense. Is the model not capable of recognizing the distribution from fewer samples? (A minor point: the correspondence of the red points in Figure 4 to the "meaningful points" mentioned in the text was not clear.)
