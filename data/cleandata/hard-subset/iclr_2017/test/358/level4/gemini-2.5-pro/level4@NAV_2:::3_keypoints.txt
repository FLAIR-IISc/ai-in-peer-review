- Introduces the "neural statistician": a hierarchical VAE that learns dataset-level summary statistics (a shared context variable) in an unsupervised way.  
- Extends deep generative models to operate on sets, a significant and timely contribution.  
- Demonstrated across tasks: dataset clustering, few-shot generation, and classification.  
- Qualitative few-shot generation results (Omniglot, YouTube Faces) are impressive.  
- Recommendation: accept, based on novelty and strong few-shot learning performance.  
- Main strength: elegant hierarchical approach that treats datasets as fundamental units and infers a shared context to capture dataset-level properties.  
- Main weakness: lacks quantitative, likelihood-based evaluations to rigorously support claims of high-quality generation.  
- Missed opportunity: no log-probability scores on held-out data or comparisons to baselines (e.g., standard VAE).  
- Suggestion: use more standard VAE terminology—call the "statistic network" the inference network q(c|D) for clarity.  
- Suggestion: add quantitative metrics such as log p(x|c) or marginal log p(x) on held-out test sets and compare to baselines.  
- Question: In few-shot sampling, are data-point latents z drawn from the prior p(z|c) or inferred via q(z|x,c) from the conditioning data?  
- Question: Why use KL divergence between context posteriors for classification, and were alternatives like test-image likelihood under conditioned models considered?
