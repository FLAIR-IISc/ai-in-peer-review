- Introduces the Neural Statistician: a hierarchical VAE that learns dataset-level representations rather than per-point ones.
- Uses a top-level context latent variable inferred from a dataset to condition the generative process for all points in the set.
- Learns summary statistics in an unsupervised way, capturing each set’s underlying data-generating distribution.
- Enables effective few-shot learning and rapid generation from new data distributions with few examples.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: synthetic data, a new Spatial MNIST benchmark, OMNIGLOT, YouTube Faces, and classification tasks.
- Results on few-shot tasks are compelling and competitive with more complex supervised methods.
- Paper is conceptually elegant, a simple yet powerful VAE extension, and is very well written.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Minor critique: the name “Neural Statistician” may overstate the connection to human statisticians.
- Minor critique: Figure 4’s selected representative points (red) are not always clearly meaningful; need clarification of the greedy selection intuition.
- Question for authors: how does performance degrade when the number of samples per dataset is reduced at inference (Spatial MNIST)?
- Question for authors: will the code be released publicly to facilitate reproducibility?
