The authors introduce a variant of the variational autoencoder (VAE) that models dataset-level latent variables. The core idea is clearly motivated and well-described. The paper's primary contribution is its move beyond the simple graphical model structures common in traditional VAEs, introducing more complex and interesting structures to the deep learning community.

**Comments:**

*   The choice of the term "statistician" is not well-justified. While the proposed model learns an approximate posterior over summary statistics, this is not the only way to summarize a dataset with a neural network; one could also consider a maximum likelihood approach, for example. The paper's clarity would be improved by avoiding newly coined terms like "statistic network" and instead using the more standard and accurate "approximate posterior."

*   The experiments are well-executed, but the evaluation of "one-shot generation" could be strengthened. I appreciate the authors' response on this topic, but the language at the end of page 6 still requires clarification.
    *   My understanding of the process for Figure 5 is as follows: an input set is used to compute the approximate posterior over the context vector `c`, and then new data is generated from the forward model conditioned on samples of `c`. Please clarify: are the data-point-dependent latent vectors `z` also sampled from their approximate posterior, or are they sampled from the prior (i.e., the forward model)?
    *   While the generated samples appear to be of high quality, this is a qualitative claim that needs to be substantiated with quantitative evidence. A major advantage of VAEs over GANs is the ability to compute log-probabilities, which provides a natural metric for evaluation. To properly assess the "one-shot generation" performance, the authors should report the log-likelihood of held-out data, such as log p(x|c) (with `c` sampled from the approximate posterior for a given dataset) or the marginal log-likelihood log p(x). I suspect that the performance of this model, when compared to a vanilla VAE lacking the context variable, would be quite impressive on this metric. Including this analysis would substantially strengthen the paper's claims.
