- Introduces a hierarchical generative framework with a double variational bound: higher-level latents for datasets and lower-level latents for individual examples.  
- Hierarchical modeling is important and under-explored in deep learning.  
- Few-shot learning results are promising.  
- The double variational bound concept is clearly explained and broadly applicable.  
- Question: Is the statistic network trained with minibatches of examples from a dataset?  
- Concern: Minibatch training may not provide an unbiased gradient estimate versus using full datasets and could limit the model’s ability to detect dataset-level features present only in rare examples.  
- Suggestion: Explore hierarchical forecasting (e.g., electricity or sales) as a practical application.
