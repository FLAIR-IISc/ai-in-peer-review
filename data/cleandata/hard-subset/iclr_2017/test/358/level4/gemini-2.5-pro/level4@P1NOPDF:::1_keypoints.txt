- Proposes a hierarchical generative model with two levels: per-example and per-dataset latent variables using a "double" variational bound.  
- Core technical idea: a high-level latent encodes dataset properties while a low-level latent encodes individual example properties.  
- Notes that hierarchical modeling is important, under-explored, and this work is a welcome contribution.  
- Pro: the double variational bound idea is well-presented and appears broadly applicable.  
- Pro: empirical results on few-shot learning are promising and appear strong.  
- Key question: unclear whether the statistic network computes dataset-level representations from full datasets or from minibatches during training.  
- Concern: if minibatches are used, gradient estimates may be biased and the model may fail to capture dataset-level properties that depend on rare examples, limiting expressivity.  
- Suggestion: apply the model to hierarchical forecasting tasks (e.g., electricity demand or product sales) as future work.
