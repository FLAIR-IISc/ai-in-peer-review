- Paper proposes a hierarchical generative model with a dataset-level latent and example-level latents.  
- Uses a "double" variational bound to jointly model distributions over datasets and over individual data points.  
- Hierarchical modeling is an important, under-explored area; the work is a valuable contribution.  
- Pros: promising results on few-shot learning.  
- Pros: the double variational bound idea is well-presented, intuitive, and broadly applicable.  
- Main concern: unclear whether the statistic network is trained on full datasets or minibatches.  
- Concern: training on minibatches may produce a biased gradient and prevent learning dataset-level features that appear only in some examples.  
- Request for clarification on training details and implications for the model’s expressive power.  
- Suggestion: evaluate or apply the model to hierarchical forecasting tasks (e.g., electricity or sales data).
