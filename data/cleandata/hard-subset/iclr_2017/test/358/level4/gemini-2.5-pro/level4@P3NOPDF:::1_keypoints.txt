- Proposes a hierarchical generative model that models individual data points (lower level) and unordered sets of datasets (higher level).  
- Core technical contribution: hierarchical variational inference with a nested ELBO using dataset-level and example-level latent variables.  
- Addresses an important, under-explored problem of hierarchical modeling in deep learning and provides a principled framework for dataset-level representations.  
- Strength: elegant, well-presented nested variational bound that is general and applicable to grouped/hierarchical data.  
- Strength: strong empirical results on few-shot learning benchmarks, showing the model’s practical utility.  
- Clarification requested: whether the statistic network is trained on full datasets or on minibatches of examples during training.  
- Concern: if minibatches are used, gradient estimates may be biased and the statistic network could fail to learn global dataset properties (e.g., existential statistics requiring seeing the whole set).  
- Asks authors to comment on how minibatching affects the model’s ability to learn dataset-level/global properties given the graphical model dependency of c on the full set {x_n}.  
- Suggestion for future work: apply the model to hierarchical time-series forecasting (e.g., national→regional→local electricity demand or retail sales).
