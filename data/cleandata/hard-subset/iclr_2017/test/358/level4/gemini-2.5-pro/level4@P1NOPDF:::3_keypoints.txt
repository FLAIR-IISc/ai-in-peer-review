- Introduces a VAE variant with dataset-level latent variables; core idea is well-motivated and moves beyond the standard VAE graphical model to more expressive probabilistic structures.

- The coined term "statistician"/"statistic network" is misleading; authors should use standard terminology like "approximate posterior" or "inference network."

- Experiments are well-executed and the authors’ prior clarification on one-shot generation is appreciated.

- Clarification requested: in the one-shot generation procedure (Fig. 5), are datapoint-specific latent variables z sampled from the prior p(z|c) or from the approximate posterior q(z|x,c)?

- Recommend adding quantitative evaluation using log-probabilities—e.g., report log p(x|c) (with c sampled from the posterior over a context set) and/or marginal log p(x) on held-out data.

- Expectation that log-likelihoods will favor this model over a standard VAE; including these metrics would significantly strengthen the paper.
