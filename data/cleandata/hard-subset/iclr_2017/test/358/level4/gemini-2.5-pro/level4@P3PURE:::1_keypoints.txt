- Proposes a hierarchical generative model with two stochastic levels: a dataset-level latent for unordered sets and an example-level latent for individual points.  
- Core technical contribution is a "double" variational bound introducing higher- and lower-level latent variables for datasets and examples.  
- Addresses an important, under-explored problem in hierarchical modeling with a novel, principled approach.  
- Strength: shows promising results on few-shot learning tasks.  
- Strength: the double variational bound is well-presented and appears generalizable to other hierarchical generative models.  
- Main concern: unclear how the statistic network is trained — whether minibatches are used during training of dataset statistics.  
- Key issue: using minibatches may yield biased gradient estimates w.r.t. the full dataset and prevent learning global dataset-level features that appear only in some examples.  
- Requests clarification from authors on whether minibatch training limits the statistic network’s ability to capture dataset-wide statistics.  
- Suggestion: consider hierarchical forecasting (e.g., electricity or sales data) as an additional practical application.
