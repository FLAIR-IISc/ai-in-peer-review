Apologies for the late review; I encountered technical issues with OpenReview that prevented me from submitting it sooner.

This paper introduces a method for learning from sets of data points, framed as a hierarchical Variational Autoencoder (VAE). At the top of the hierarchy, an abstract context unit summarizes an entire dataset, enabling the model to "learn to learn" and infer distributions from a small number of examples.

Overall, this is a valuable contribution to the literature on one- and few-shot learning. The method is conceptually simple and elegant, and appears to perform well. A key advantage over other recent approaches to one-shot learning is its relative simplicity and its foundation in unsupervised representation learning. The paper is also exceptionally well-written and a pleasure to read.

The paper's title feels overly ambitious relative to the scope of the work presented. The proposed method has little in common with the work of a human statistician, unless one uses the most literal definition of the term: "one who devises statistics."

The experiments are well-chosen, and the few-shot learning results are strong, especially considering the model's simplicity. The introduced spatial MNIST dataset is interesting and could serve as a useful toy benchmark. One observation regarding the experiments: the input point sets in Figure 4 appear quite dense. I would have expected the model to be capable of recognizing the underlying distribution from fewer samples. On a minor note (a nitpick), the red points in Figure 4 do not appear to correspond to the meaningful locations described in the text.

Finally, will the authors be releasing their code?
