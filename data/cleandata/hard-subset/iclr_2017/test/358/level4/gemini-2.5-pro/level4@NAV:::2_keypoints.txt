- Reviewer had delayed submission due to technical issues with OpenReview.  
- Paper proposes a hierarchical VAE for learning from sets with a top-layer abstract context unit summarizing a dataset.  
- Experiments show the method can perform meta-learning and infer distributions from few examples.  
- Work is a valuable contribution to one-shot and few-shot learning.  
- Approach is conceptually simple, elegant, and delivers strong performance.  
- Key advantage: relative simplicity and grounding in unsupervised representation learning versus recent methods.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Title may be overly ambitious; connection to a statistician’s functions is not clear.  
- Experiments are thoughtfully designed and few-shot results are compelling given model simplicity.  
- Spatial MNIST is an interesting contribution and could be a useful toy benchmark.  
- Figure 4 concerns: input samples appear dense—can the model infer the distribution from fewer samples; unclear correspondence of red points to “meaningful points.”  
- Asks whether the authors will release their code.
