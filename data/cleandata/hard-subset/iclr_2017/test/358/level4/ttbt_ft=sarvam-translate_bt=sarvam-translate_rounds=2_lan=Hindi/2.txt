This paper presents a method of learning to infer things from a set of data points. This method is a hierarchical version of VAE, in which the top layer contains a summary reference unit that summarizes a dataset. Experiments show that this method is capable of "learning to learn" by acquiring the ability to learn a distribution from a few examples.

Overall, this paper is a good contribution to the literature on one-shot or few-shot learning. The method is conceptually simple and elegant, and seems to perform well. Compared to other recent papers on one-shot learning, the proposed method is simple and based on unsupervised representation learning. The paper is clearly written and enjoyable to read.

The name of the paper is quite grand for what it does; the proposed method has little in common with a statistician, unless one means "someone who thinks statistics."

The experiments are well-chosen, and the results of the few-shot learning seem quite solid, given the simplicity of the method.

The Spatial MNIST dataset is interesting and could make a good toy benchmark. However, the inputs in Figure 4 seem quite dense; shouldn't the method be able to recognize a distribution with fewer samples? (Note: The red dots in Figure 4 do not seem to correspond to the meaningful dots claimed in the text.)
