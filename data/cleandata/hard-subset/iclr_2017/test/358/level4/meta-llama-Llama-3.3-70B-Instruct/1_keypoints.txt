- Proposes a novel hierarchical generative model with a lower-level per-example latent and a higher-level latent for unordered sets of datasets.
- Core idea is a "double" variational bound where dataset-level and example-level latents are jointly modeled.
- Hierarchical modeling in deep learning is important and underexplored.
- Strength: Few-shot learning results look promising (needs domain expertise for full assessment).
- Strength: The double variational bound is clearly presented and appears broadly applicable.
- Question: Does training the statistic network use minibatches of examples from each dataset?
- Concern: Training on minibatches might not give an unbiased estimate of the full gradient and could prevent learning dataset-level features that appear only across entire datasets.
- Suggestion: Applying the model to hierarchical forecasting tasks (e.g., electricity demand, sales) could be a useful extension.
