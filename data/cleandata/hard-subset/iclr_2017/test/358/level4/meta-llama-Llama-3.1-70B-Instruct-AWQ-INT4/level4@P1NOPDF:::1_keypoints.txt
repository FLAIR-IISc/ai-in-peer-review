- Paper proposes a hierarchical generative model with dataset-level and example-level latents using a "double" variational bound.  
- Hierarchical modeling is a crucial, high-impact problem that is underexplored in deep learning.  
- Pros: Few-shot learning results look promising (reviewer not an expert in few-shot).  
- Pros: The "double" variational bound idea is clearly presented and broadly applicable.  
- Question: Does the statistic network get trained using minibatches of examples?  
- Question: If minibatches are used, do they provide an unbiased estimator of the full gradient—could minibatches prevent learning dataset-level features that require seeing all examples (e.g., “any example has feature X”)?  
- Suggestion: Apply the model to hierarchical forecasting tasks (e.g., electricity or sales) as a practical use case.
