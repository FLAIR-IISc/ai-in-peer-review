- Proposes a hierarchical generative model ("Neural Statistician") with dataset-level and example-level latent variables and a double variational bound.  
- Addresses the under-explored, high-impact problem of hierarchical modeling in deep learning.  
- Claims a flexible, unsupervised method for learning representations of entire datasets.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on tasks: clustering, transferring generative models to new datasets, selecting representative samples, and classifying unseen classes.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing a well-motivated problem and a well-supported approach grounded in literature.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation of the model, components, and training procedure.  
- Strength: comprehensive related work review situating the approach in context.  
- Strength: strong experimental results, including few-shot learning and competitive comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Question/concern: training the statistic network with minibatches—potential bias and loss of expressive power if dataset-level features require seeing all examples.  
- Suggestion: explore additional practical applications such as hierarchical forecasting (e.g., electricity/sales).  
- Overall judgment: significant contribution demonstrating the potential of dataset-level representation learning.
