This paper proposes a hierarchical generative model that captures the structure of datasets at two levels: the lower level represents individual points within a dataset, while the higher level models unordered sets of datasets. The core idea is to employ a "double" variational bound, where a higher-level latent variable describes the dataset as a whole, and a lower-level latent variable describes each individual example.

Hierarchical modeling is a crucial and high-impact problem that has received relatively little attention in the deep learning literature, making this work a valuable contribution.

The paper has several strengths:

* The few-shot learning results are promising, although a more in-depth evaluation by an expert in this area would be beneficial.
* The idea of using a "double" variational bound in a hierarchical generative model is well-presented and appears to be widely applicable.

However, some questions and concerns arise:

* When training the statistic network, are minibatches (i.e., subsets of examples) used? If not, using minibatches would likely result in a biased estimator of the full gradient, as it would not account for all examples in the dataset. For instance, if the statistic network aims to identify whether any example in the dataset possesses a certain feature, training on minibatches might limit its ability to learn this characterization, as some examples would be missing from the minibatch. Some potential suggestions for future work:

* Hierarchical forecasting, such as predicting electricity consumption or sales, could be an interesting and practical application of this type of model, allowing for the incorporation of multiple levels of data (e.g., individual households, regional trends, and national patterns).
