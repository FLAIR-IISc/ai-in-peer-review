- Paper proposes a hierarchical generative model with lower-level points and a higher-level model of unordered sets of datasets using a "double" variational bound.  
- The method uses a higher-level latent variable for datasets and a lower-level latent for individual examples.  
- Hierarchical modeling is an important, high-impact, and relatively under-explored area in deep learning.  
- Strength: Few-shot learning results appear promising.  
- Strength: The "double" variational bound concept is clearly presented and broadly applicable.  
- Major question: Is the statistic network trained using full datasets or minibatches (subsets of examples)?  
- Concern: If trained on minibatches, the statistic network may not provide an unbiased estimator of the full-gradient or capture dataset-level features that require observing all examples (e.g., presence of a rare feature).  
- Risk: Using minibatches for the statistic network could limit the model’s expressive power for dataset-level characterizations.  
- Suggested future work: Apply the model to hierarchical forecasting tasks (e.g., electricity or sales).
