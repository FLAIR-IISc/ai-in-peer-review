- Proposes a hierarchical generative model with two levels: per-example latents and dataset-level latents using a "double" variational bound.  
- Higher-level latent models unordered sets of datasets; lower-level latent models individual points within a dataset.  
- Hierarchical modeling is an important, high-impact problem that has been underexplored in deep learning, making the work valuable.  
- Strength: promising few-shot learning results, though deeper evaluation by a domain expert is recommended.  
- Strength: the "double" variational bound idea is clearly presented and potentially widely applicable.  
- Concern: training the statistic network with minibatches may produce a biased gradient estimator that fails to capture dataset-wide properties.  
- Example concern: minibatch training could prevent the statistic network from detecting features present only in examples absent from a minibatch (related to Fig. 1).  
- Suggestion for future work: apply the model to hierarchical forecasting problems (e.g., household, regional, national electricity or sales forecasting).
