- Proposes a hierarchical VAE with an abstract context unit that summarizes a dataset for predicting from sets of points.  
- Enables "learning to learn" by acquiring the ability to infer distributions from small numbers of examples (few-shot).  
- Method is conceptually simple, elegant, and based on unsupervised representation learning.  
- Empirically performs well on few-shot tasks and compares favorably to recent one-shot approaches due to its simplicity.  
- Extension of the VAE to include a dataset-level context unit is a natural and intuitive modeling choice.  
- Experiments demonstrate the ability to learn distributions from few examples, supporting the few-shot claim.  
- Introduces the spatial MNIST toy benchmark as an interesting dataset for evaluation.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Critique: Paper title/name is overly grandiose; "statistician" analogy is misleading.  
- Concern: Figure 4 inputs look dense—should the method recognize the distribution with fewer samples?  
- Issue: Red points in Figure 4 do not appear to correspond to the meaningful points claimed in the text.  
- Query: Will the authors release the code?  
- Request: Provide more details on how the abstract context unit is learned and how it summarizes a dataset.  
- Request: Report performance on more complex datasets with multiple modes or non-linear relationships.  
- Request: Explain why unsupervised representation learning yields strong few-shot performance in this method.
