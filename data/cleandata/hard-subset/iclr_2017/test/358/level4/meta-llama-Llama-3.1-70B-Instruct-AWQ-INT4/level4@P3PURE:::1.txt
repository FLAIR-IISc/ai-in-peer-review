This paper proposes a hierarchical generative model, where the lower level consists of points within datasets and the higher level models unordered sets of datasets. The basic idea is to utilize a "double" variational bound, where a higher-level latent variable describes datasets and a lower-level latent variable describes individual examples.

Hierarchical modeling is an important and high-impact problem that has been relatively under-explored in the deep learning literature.

The paper has several strengths:

* The few-shot learning results appear promising, although I am not an expert in this area.
* The concept of employing a "double" variational bound in a hierarchical generative model is well-presented and seems widely applicable.

However, I have some questions regarding the training process:

* When training the statistic network, are minibatches (i.e., subsets of examples) used?
* If not, does using minibatches provide an unbiased estimator of the full gradient (if all examples were used)? For instance, what if the statistic network aims to identify whether any example in the dataset has a certain feature and treats that as the characterization? If the statistic network is trained on minibatches, it may not be able to learn this characterization, as a given minibatch may be missing some examples from the dataset. Using minibatches (instead of all examples in the dataset) to train the statistic network may limit the model's expressive power.

Some potential suggestions for future work:

* Hierarchical forecasting (e.g., electricity or sales) could be an interesting and practical use case for this type of model.
