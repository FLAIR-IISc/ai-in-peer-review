This paper introduces a variant of the variational autoencoder (VAE) that models dataset-level latent variables, presenting a clear and well-motivated idea. The greatest contribution of this work lies in its extension beyond the relatively simple graphical model structure of traditional VAEs, introducing more complex and interesting structures to the deep learning community.

The authors propose a novel approach to modeling datasets, which they term a "statistician." However, it is unclear why this term is necessary, as learning an approximate posterior over summary statistics is not the only conceivable way to summarize a dataset using a neural network. Alternative approaches, such as maximum likelihood estimation, could also be employed. To improve clarity, the paper could avoid coining new terms like "statistic network" and instead use more accurate and established terminology, such as "approximate posterior."

The experimental results are well-presented and appreciated, particularly the response to the question regarding "one shot generation." Nevertheless, some language requires clarification, specifically at the end of page 6. Based on Figure 5, it appears that the process involves taking an input set, computing the approximate posterior over the context vector, and then generating samples from the forward model using samples from the approximate posterior. To ensure understanding, clarification on the following points would be beneficial:

(a) Are the data point-dependent vectors z generated from the forward model or taken from the approximate posterior?

(b) While the samples are of high quality, this statement is subjective and lacks quantification. A key advantage of VAEs over GANs is the availability of natural methods for computing log-probabilities. To provide a more comprehensive evaluation, the authors could report log p(x | c) (where c is sampled from the approximate posterior) or log p(x) for held-out datasets. It is likely that the log probability performance of these networks, relative to a vanilla VAE without the context latent variable, would be impressive. Including such an analysis would strengthen the paper.

Overall, the paper presents a promising extension of VAEs, and addressing these points would enhance its clarity and impact.
