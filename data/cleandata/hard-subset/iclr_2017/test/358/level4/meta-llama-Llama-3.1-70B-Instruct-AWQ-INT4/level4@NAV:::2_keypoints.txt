- Apology for delayed review due to OpenReview technical issues.
- Proposes a hierarchical VAE with a top-layer context unit that summarizes a dataset for learning predictive models from sets.
- Demonstrates "learning to learn" ability to infer distributions from limited examples (few-shot).
- Valuable contribution to one-/few-shot learning; method is conceptually straightforward, elegant, and shows promising performance.
- Simpler than recent one-shot approaches and uses unsupervised representation learning; paper is well-written.
- Title may be misleading—method has limited connection to statistical reasoning beyond general statistical analysis.
- Experimental design is appropriate and few-shot results are convincing.
- Spatial MNIST is a useful dataset contribution and potential benchmark.
- Concern that input density in Figure 4 is high, raising doubt about performance with fewer samples.
- Concern that the red points in Figure 4 do not appear to be meaningful as claimed.
- Request for authors to indicate whether they will release the code.
