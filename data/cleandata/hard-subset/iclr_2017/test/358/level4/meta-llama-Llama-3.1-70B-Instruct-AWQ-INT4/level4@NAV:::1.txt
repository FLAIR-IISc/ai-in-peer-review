This paper presents a hierarchical generative model that leverages a dual-level architecture, where the lower level focuses on individual data points and the higher level models unordered sets of datasets. The core concept revolves around employing a "double" variational bound, utilizing a higher-level latent variable to describe datasets and a lower-level latent variable to describe individual examples.

Hierarchical modeling is a significant and high-impact problem that remains under-explored in the Deep Learning literature, making this work a valuable contribution.

The paper has several strengths:

* The few-shot learning results appear promising, although a more in-depth evaluation by a specialist in this area would be beneficial.
* The introduction of a "double" variational bound in a hierarchical generative model is well-articulated and demonstrates broad applicability.

However, some questions arise:

* During the training of the statistic network, are minibatches (i.e., subsets of examples) utilized?
* If not, would employing minibatches provide an unbiased estimator of the full gradient if all examples were used? Training the statistic network on minibatches might limit the model's expressive power, as a given minibatch may not include all examples from the dataset.

* Exploring hierarchical forecasting applications, such as predicting electricity consumption or sales, which could be a practical and interesting use case for this type of model.
