This paper proposes a hierarchical generative model that incorporates a lower level consisting of points within datasets and a higher level modeling unordered sets of datasets. The core idea is to utilize a "double" variational bound, where a higher-level latent variable describes datasets and a lower-level latent variable describes individual examples.

Hierarchical modeling is a crucial and high-impact problem that, in my opinion, has not received sufficient attention in the deep learning literature.

Pros:

* The few-shot learning results appear promising, although I am not an expert in this area.
* The concept of employing a "double" variational bound in a hierarchical generative model is well-presented and seems to have broad applicability.

Questions:

* When training the statistic network, are minibatches (i.e., subsets of the examples) used?
* If not, does using minibatches provide an unbiased estimator of the full gradient (as if all examples were used)? For instance, what if the statistic network aims to identify whether any example from the dataset has a certain feature and treats that as the characterization? However, if the statistic network is trained on minibatches, it may not be able to learn this characterization, as a given minibatch will likely be missing some examples from the dataset. Using minibatches (as opposed to using all examples in the dataset) to train the statistic network seems to limit the expressive power of the model.

Suggestions:

* Hierarchical forecasting (e.g., electricity or sales) could be an interesting and practical use case for this type of model, and it may be worth exploring in future work.
