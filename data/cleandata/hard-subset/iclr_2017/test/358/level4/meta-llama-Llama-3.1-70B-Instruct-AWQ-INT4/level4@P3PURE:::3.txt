The authors introduce a variant of the variational autoencoder (VAE) that models dataset-level latent variables, a concept that is clearly motivated and well described. In my opinion, the greatest contribution of this paper lies in its extension of the traditional VAE's relatively simple graphical model structure, thereby introducing more complex and interesting structures to the deep learning community.

I have several comments regarding the paper. Firstly, the term "statistician" is unclear to me. Learning an approximate posterior over summary statistics is not the only conceivable way to summarize a dataset using a neural network; alternative approaches, such as maximum likelihood estimation, could also be considered. To improve clarity, the paper could avoid coining new terms like "statistic network" and instead use the more accurate term "approximate posterior".

The experiments presented in the paper are well conducted, and I appreciate the authors' response to my inquiry regarding "one-shot generation". However, I believe the language used in this context requires further clarification, particularly at the end of page 6. My understanding of Figure 5 is as follows: given an input set, the approximate posterior over the context vector is computed, and then samples are generated from the forward model using samples from the approximate posterior. I would like clarification on two points: (a) Are the data point-dependent vectors z generated from the forward model or taken from the approximate posterior? (b) While the samples appear to be of high quality, this statement is not quantified. One of the advantages of VAEs over GANs is the availability of natural methods for computing log-probabilities. Therefore, a more rigorous way to evaluate the "one-shot generation" performance would be to report log p(x | c) (where c is sampled from the approximate posterior) or log p(x) for held-out datasets. I suspect that the log probability performance of these networks relative to a vanilla VAE without the context latent variable will be impressive, and I do not see a reason for omitting this information.
