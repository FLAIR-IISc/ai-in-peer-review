- Proposes a VAE extension that models dataset-level latent variables, termed the "neural statistician."
- Learns dataset representations via a "statistic network" to enable clustering, generative transfer, representative selection, and few-shot classification.
- Demonstrated effectiveness on synthetic data, MNIST, OMNIGLOT, and YouTube Faces.
- Recommendation: accept, citing novelty, strong motivation, clear presentation, and convincing experiments.
- Main technical contribution: extending VAE to model dataset-level latents and learn an approximate posterior over summary statistics.
- Experiments indicate the model learns meaningful dataset-level representations and performs various tasks well.
- Suggest renaming "statistician"/"statistic network" to a clearer term (e.g., "approximate posterior") to avoid misleading implications.
- Request clearer description of experimental procedures, especially how "one-shot generation" and data point–dependent z vectors were obtained.
- Recommend adding quantitative evaluations (e.g., log-probability metrics) for a more comprehensive assessment.
- Ask about practical use cases and performance when the number of available datasets is limited.
