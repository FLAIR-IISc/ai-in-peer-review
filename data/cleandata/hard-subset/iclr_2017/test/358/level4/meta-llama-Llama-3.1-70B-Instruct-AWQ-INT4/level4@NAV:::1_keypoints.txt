- Presents a hierarchical generative model with a dual-level architecture: lower-level latents for individual datapoints and higher-level latents for unordered sets of datasets, using a "double" variational bound.  
- Hierarchical modeling is an important, under-explored problem; the work is a valuable contribution.  
- Strength: Few-shot learning results look promising, though they need deeper evaluation by a specialist.  
- Strength: The "double" variational bound is clearly explained and shows broad applicability.  
- Question: Does training of the statistic network use minibatches (subsets of examples)?  
- Question/concern: If minibatches are used, would they provide an unbiased estimator of the full gradient, and might minibatch training limit the statistic network’s ability to capture features present only across the full dataset (per the Fig.1 graphical model)?  
- Future work suggestion: Explore hierarchical forecasting applications (e.g., electricity consumption or sales) as practical use cases.
