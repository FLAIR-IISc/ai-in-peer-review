- Proposes a VAE variant that incorporates dataset-level latent variables; idea is well-motivated and clearly described.  
- Main contribution: extends the traditional VAE graphical model to include more complex dataset-level architectures.  
- The term "statistician"/"statistic network" is unclear and misleading; prefer calling it an "approximate posterior" over summary statistics.  
- Learning an approximate posterior over summary statistics is not the only option; alternative summarization methods (e.g., maximum likelihood) should be considered or discussed.  
- Experiments are well-executed and the authors’ response on "one-shot generation" is appreciated.  
- Language and explanation at the end of page 6 require clarification to avoid confusion.  
- Figure 5 should be interpreted as: compute the approximate posterior over the context vector from an input set, then generate samples from the forward model using samples from that approximate posterior.  
- Clarify whether data point–dependent vectors z are sampled from the forward model or taken from the approximate posterior.  
- Quantify "one-shot generation" performance by reporting log p(x | c) or log p(x) on held-out data and compare to a vanilla VAE to strengthen the evaluation.
