The authors propose a variant of the variational autoencoder (VAE) that incorporates dataset-level latent variables. The idea is well-motivated and clearly described. The primary contribution of this paper lies in its extension of the traditional VAE's relatively simple graphical model structure, introducing more complex and interesting architectures to the deep learning community.

The paper's key concept, the "statistician," is somewhat unclear. Learning an approximate posterior over summary statistics is not the only conceivable approach to summarizing a dataset using a neural network. Alternative methods, such as a maximum likelihood approach, could also be considered. To improve clarity, the authors may want to avoid introducing new terms like "statistic network" and instead use the more accurate "approximate posterior."

The experiments presented in the paper are well-executed, and the authors' response to the question regarding "one-shot generation" is appreciated. However, the language used in the explanation, particularly at the end of page 6, requires clarification. The interpretation of Figure 5 is as follows: given an input set, the approximate posterior over the context vector is computed, and then samples are generated from the forward model using samples from the approximate posterior. To further clarify this process, two questions arise: 

(a) Are the data point-dependent vectors z generated from the forward model or taken from the approximate posterior? 
(b) While the samples generated are of high quality, this statement is subjective and lacks quantification. A key advantage of VAEs over GANs is the ability to compute log-probabilities naturally. Therefore, a more rigorous evaluation of "one-shot generation" performance would be to report log p(x | c) (where c is sampled from the approximate posterior) or log p(x) for held-out datasets. It is expected that the log probability performance of these networks, relative to a vanilla VAE without the context latent variable, would be impressive. Including this information would strengthen the paper.
