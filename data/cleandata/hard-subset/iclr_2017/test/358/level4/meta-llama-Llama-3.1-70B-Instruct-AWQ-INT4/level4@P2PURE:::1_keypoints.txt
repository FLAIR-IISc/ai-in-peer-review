- Proposes a two-level hierarchical generative model using a "double" variational bound: lower-level latents for examples, higher-level latents for unordered sets of datasets.
- Lower level focuses on individual points; higher level captures dataset-level characteristics.
- Hierarchical modeling is an important, under-explored problem, so the work is a valuable contribution.
- Strength: few-shot learning results appear promising.
- Strength: the "double" variational bound concept is well-presented and likely broadly applicable.
- Question: unclear whether the statistic network is trained using minibatches of examples.
- Concern: training the statistic network on minibatches may not provide an unbiased estimator of the full gradient and could prevent learning dataset-level features (e.g., detecting if any example has a specific property).
- Suggestion for future work: apply the model to hierarchical forecasting tasks (e.g., electricity or sales).
