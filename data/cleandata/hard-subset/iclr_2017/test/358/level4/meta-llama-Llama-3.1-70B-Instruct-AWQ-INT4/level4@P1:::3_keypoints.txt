- Main contribution: introduces a VAE variant that models dataset-level latent variables, moving beyond simple VAE graphical models.
- Overall assessment: idea is well motivated and well described, but paper is rejected.
- Primary reason for rejection 1: terminology and definitions are unclear and sometimes misleading.
- Primary reason for rejection 2: experimental results need stronger quantitative evaluation.
- Terminology critique: calling components a "statistic network" is misleading; prefer framing as an "approximate posterior" over summary statistics.
- Request for language clarity: wording around one-shot generation (end of page 6) and interpretation of Figure 5 needs clarification.
- Figure 5 clarification needed: confirm process—compute approximate posterior over context c from input set, then generate using forward model with samples from that posterior.
- Specific question (a): are data-point-dependent latent vectors z generated by the forward model or taken from the approximate posterior?
- Specific question (b): request to report proper log-probability metrics (e.g., log p(x|c) or log p(x) on held-out datasets) to quantify one-shot generation and compare to a vanilla VAE.
- Experimental suggestions: include additional quantitative metrics such as log-probability scores and classification accuracy.
- Comparative discussion request: add a detailed comparison to other dataset-level learning approaches, e.g., set-to-sequence models.
- Limitation discussion: paper should address weaknesses such as requiring many datasets for training.
- Reproducibility request: provide more details on experimental setup, hyperparameters, and architecture choices for each experiment.
