The authors introduce a variant of the variational autoencoder (VAE) that models dataset-level latent variables. The idea is clearly motivated and well described. In my mind the greatest contribution of this paper is the movement beyond the relatively simple graphical model structure of the traditional VAEs and the introduction of more interesting structures to the deep learning community.

I recommend accepting this paper, with two key reasons for this choice. Firstly, the authors propose a novel extension of the VAE that can learn to compute summary statistics of datasets in an unsupervised fashion. Secondly, the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on various tasks, including clustering datasets, transferring generative models to new datasets, selecting representative samples of datasets, and classifying previously unseen classes.

The paper provides a thorough explanation of the proposed approach, including the neural statistician model, the variational autoencoder, and the statistic network. The authors also provide a clear problem statement, discussing the limitations of traditional VAEs and the need for a more sophisticated approach to modeling datasets. The related work section is comprehensive, covering various topics such as topic models, transfer learning, one-shot learning, and multiple-instance learning.

The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating the ability of the neural statistician model to learn useful representations of datasets. The authors provide visualizations of the learned representations, which help to illustrate the effectiveness of the approach. The comparison to other methods, such as matching networks, is also useful in evaluating the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed approach.

However, I do have some minor suggestions for improvement. Firstly, the authors could provide more details on the implementation of the statistic network, including the choice of architecture and hyperparameters. Secondly, the authors could consider providing more quantitative evaluations of the proposed approach, such as log-probability performance on held-out datasets. Finally, the authors could clarify the language used in the paper, avoiding the use of terms such as "statistic network" and instead using more accurate descriptions such as "approximate posterior".
