This paper proposes a novel approach for learning predictive models from sets of data points. The method is a hierarchical extension of the Variational Autoencoder (VAE), featuring a top-layer abstract context unit that summarizes a dataset. Experimental results demonstrate the method's ability to "learn to learn" by acquiring the capacity to learn distributions from limited examples.

Overall, this paper is a valuable contribution to the literature on one- or few-shot learning. The proposed method is conceptually straightforward and elegant, yielding promising performance. Notably, the method is simpler than other recent approaches to one-shot learning and relies on unsupervised representation learning. The paper is well-written and engaging to read.

However, the paper's title may be somewhat misleading, as the proposed method does not appear to have a strong connection to statistical reasoning beyond the general concept of statistical analysis.

The experimental design is well-chosen, and the few-shot learning results are convincing, given the method's simplicity. The spatial MNIST dataset is an interesting contribution and may serve as a useful benchmark for future research. Nevertheless, the input density in Figure 4 seems relatively high; it is unclear whether the method can recognize distributions with fewer samples. Additionally, the red points in Figure 4 do not appear to correspond to meaningful points, contrary to the text's claim.

Finally, I would appreciate it if the authors could provide information on whether they plan to release the code for their method.
