This paper proposes a method for learning to predict from sets of data points, extending the Variational Autoencoder (VAE) framework in a hierarchical manner. The top layer of this hierarchy consists of an abstract context unit that summarizes a dataset. The experiments demonstrate the method's ability to "learn to learn" by acquiring the capacity to learn distributions from a small number of examples.

Overall, this paper is a valuable contribution to the literature on one- or few-shot learning. The method is conceptually simple, elegant, and achieves good performance. Compared to recent papers on one-shot learning, the proposed approach is simpler and relies on unsupervised representation learning. The paper is well-written and enjoyable to read.

However, the paper's title seems overly ambitious, as the proposed method does not share significant connections with the concept of a statistician, unless interpreted in a very broad sense.

The experiments are well-designed, and the few-shot learning results appear solid, considering the method's simplicity. The spatial MNIST dataset is an interesting contribution and could serve as a useful toy benchmark. Nevertheless, the inputs in Figure 4 seem relatively dense, which raises the question of whether the method could recognize the distribution with fewer samples. Additionally, the red points in Figure 4 do not appear to correspond to meaningful points, as claimed in the text.

I would appreciate it if the authors could clarify their plans regarding code release.
