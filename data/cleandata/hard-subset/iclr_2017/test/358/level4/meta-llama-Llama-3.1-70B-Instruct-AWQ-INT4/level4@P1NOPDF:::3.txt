The authors introduce a variant of the variational autoencoder (VAE) that models dataset-level latent variables. The idea is clearly motivated and well described. In my opinion, the greatest contribution of this paper is its extension beyond the relatively simple graphical model structure of traditional VAEs, introducing more complex and interesting structures to the deep learning community.

Comments:
- I am unclear about the rationale behind referring to the proposed model as a "statistician". Learning an approximate posterior over summary statistics is not the only conceivable way to summarize a dataset using a neural network; alternative approaches, such as maximum likelihood estimation, could also be employed. In general, I believe the paper would benefit from avoiding the introduction of new terminology, such as "statistic network", and instead adhering to the more accurate description of an "approximate posterior".
- The experiments presented are well conducted, and I appreciate the authors' response to my inquiry regarding "one-shot generation". Nevertheless, I still think that the language used in this context requires clarification, particularly at the end of page 6. Based on my understanding of Figure 5, the process involves the following steps: taking an input set, computing the approximate posterior over the context vector, and then generating samples from the forward model using samples from the approximate posterior. I would like clarification on the following points:
(a) Are the data point-dependent vectors z generated from the forward model or sampled from the approximate posterior?
(b) While the samples appear to be of high quality, this assessment is subjective and not quantified. A key advantage of VAEs over GANs is the ability to compute log-probabilities in a principled manner. To evaluate the performance of "one-shot generation" in a more rigorous manner, it would be beneficial to report log p(x | c) (where c is sampled from the approximate posterior) or log p(x) for held-out datasets. I suspect that the log-probability performance of these networks, relative to a vanilla VAE without the context latent variable, would be impressive. I do not see a compelling reason for omitting this analysis.
