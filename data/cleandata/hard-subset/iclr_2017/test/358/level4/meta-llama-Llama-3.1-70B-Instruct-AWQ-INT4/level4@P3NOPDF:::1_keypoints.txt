- Proposes a hierarchical generative model with two levels: per-example latent variables and per-dataset latent variables.  
- Introduces a "double" variational bound where a higher-level latent describes datasets and a lower-level latent describes examples.  
- Tackles the underexplored, high-impact problem of hierarchical modeling in deep learning.  
- Promising few-shot learning results, though more domain-expert evaluation is recommended.  
- The double variational bound idea is well-presented and potentially widely applicable across domains.  
- Unclear whether the statistic network is trained with minibatches; this affects gradient unbiasedness and practicality.  
- Using minibatches may limit the statistic network’s ability to detect dataset-wide features absent from individual minibatches.  
- The concern about minibatching aligns with the graphical model in Figure 1 and needs clarification.  
- Suggests exploring additional applications (e.g., hierarchical forecasting for electricity or sales) to demonstrate practicality and limits.  
- Overall, a novel and potentially impactful contribution, but questions about training details and broader evaluation remain.
