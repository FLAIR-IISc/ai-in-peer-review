- Proposes a hierarchical generative model with lower-level example latents and higher-level dataset latents using a "double" variational bound.  
- Focuses on learning representations of datasets (unordered sets) — a well-motivated and under-explored hierarchical modeling problem.  
- Novel approach that is clearly presented and shows promise for few-shot learning.  
- Accepted primarily for novelty and potential impact; the double variational bound is broadly applicable.  
- Experimental validation on synthetic 1-D, spatial MNIST, OMNIGLOT, and YouTube Faces supports the model's ability to learn dataset representations and perform few-shot learning.  
- Raises concern about training the statistic network with minibatches: minibatch training may not capture dataset-level features present only in some examples and could limit model expressivity.  
- Queries whether minibatch-based training gives an unbiased estimator of the full gradient and how that affects learning dataset-level characterizations.  
- Suggests the paper should discuss limitations, particularly the model’s dataset-hungry nature and need for large datasets to learn useful representations.  
- Recommends exploring practical applications such as hierarchical forecasting (electricity/sales) in future work.  
- Overall judgment: significant potential contribution to hierarchical modeling and few-shot learning.
