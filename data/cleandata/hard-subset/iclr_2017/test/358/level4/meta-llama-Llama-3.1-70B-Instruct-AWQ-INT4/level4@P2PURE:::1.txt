This paper proposes a hierarchical generative model that employs a two-level structure, where the lower level focuses on individual points within datasets, and the higher level models unordered sets of datasets. The core idea revolves around utilizing a "double" variational bound, where a higher-level latent variable captures dataset-level characteristics, and a lower-level latent variable describes individual examples.

Hierarchical modeling is a crucial and high-impact problem that remains under-explored in the deep learning literature, making this work a valuable contribution.

The paper has several strengths:

- The few-shot learning results appear promising, although I am not an expert in this area.
- The concept of using a "double" variational bound in a hierarchical generative model is well-presented and seems to be widely applicable.

However, I have some questions regarding the training process:

- When training the statistic network, are minibatches (i.e., subsets of examples) used?
- If not, does using minibatches provide an unbiased estimator of the full gradient (as if all examples were used)? For instance, consider a scenario where the statistic network aims to identify whether any example in the dataset has a specific feature and treats this as the characterization. If the statistic network is trained on minibatches, it may struggle to learn this characterization, as a given minibatch may not contain all examples from the dataset. Using minibatches (instead of all dataset examples) to train the statistic network may limit the model's expressive power.

Some potential suggestions for future work:

- Hierarchical forecasting (e.g., electricity or sales) could be an interesting and practical use case for this type of model.
