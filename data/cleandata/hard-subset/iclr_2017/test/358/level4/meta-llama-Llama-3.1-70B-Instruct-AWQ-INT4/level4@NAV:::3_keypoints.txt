- Proposes a VAE variant with dataset-level latent variables; well-motivated and notable for introducing more complex graphical model structures to deep learning.  
- Main contribution is moving beyond the traditional simple VAE graphical model to richer, dataset-level latent structure.  
- The term "statistician" is unclear and misleading; learning an approximate posterior over summary statistics is not the only summarization method.  
- Recommend avoiding the new term "statistic network" and instead use the clearer term "approximate posterior."  
- Experiments are well-executed and the authors’ response on "one-shot generation" was appreciated, but language at the end of page 6 needs clarification.  
- Figure 5 suggests computing the approximate posterior over the context vector from an input set and then generating samples from the forward model using samples from that posterior—this process should be explicitly clarified.  
- Two technical clarifications requested: (a) Are data point–dependent vectors z generated from the forward model or taken from the approximate posterior? (b) Provide quantitative evaluation for one-shot generation (e.g., report log p(x|c) or log p(x) on held-out data) and compare to a vanilla VAE without context latent variable.
