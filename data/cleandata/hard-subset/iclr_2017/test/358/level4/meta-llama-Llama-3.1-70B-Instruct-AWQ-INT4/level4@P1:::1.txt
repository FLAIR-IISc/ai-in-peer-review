This paper proposes a hierarchical generative model where the lower level consists of points within datasets and the higher level models unordered sets of datasets. The basic idea is to use a "double" variational bound where a higher level latent variable describes datasets and a lower level latent variable describes individual examples.

Hierarchical modeling is an important and high impact problem, and I think that it's under-explored in the Deep Learning literature.

The paper claims to contribute to the development of a highly flexible model that can learn representations of datasets in an unsupervised fashion. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach, called the Neural Statistician, on various tasks such as clustering, transferring generative models to new datasets, selecting representative samples of datasets, and classifying previously unseen classes.

I decide to accept this paper with two key reasons for this choice. Firstly, the paper tackles a specific and well-motivated problem in the field of machine learning, which is learning representations of datasets. Secondly, the authors provide a well-supported approach that is grounded in the literature and demonstrate its effectiveness through various experiments.

One of the strengths of the paper is its clear and well-structured presentation of the proposed approach. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the Neural Statistician model, including its components and the training procedure. The paper also includes a comprehensive review of related work, which helps to situate the proposed approach within the broader context of machine learning research.

The experimental results presented in the paper are also a strong point. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the Neural Statistician on various tasks, including few-shot learning, and provide a comparison with other state-of-the-art approaches. The results show that the proposed approach can achieve competitive performance on these tasks.

However, I do have some questions and suggestions for the authors to consider. When training the statistic network, are minibatches (i.e., subsets of the examples) used? If not, does using minibatches actually give you an unbiased estimator of the full gradient (if you had used all examples)? For example, what if the statistic network wants to pull out if any example from the dataset has a certain feature and treat that as the characterization. If your statistic network is trained on minibatches, it won't be able to learn this characterization, because a given minibatch will be missing some of the examples from the dataset. Using minibatches (as opposed to using all examples in the dataset) to train the statistic network seems like it would limit the expressive power of the model.

Additionally, I think it would be interesting to explore other potential applications of the Neural Statistician model, such as hierarchical forecasting (electricity/sales). This could be an interesting and practical use case for this type of model.

Overall, I believe that this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of machine learning and demonstrates the potential of the Neural Statistician model for learning representations of datasets.
