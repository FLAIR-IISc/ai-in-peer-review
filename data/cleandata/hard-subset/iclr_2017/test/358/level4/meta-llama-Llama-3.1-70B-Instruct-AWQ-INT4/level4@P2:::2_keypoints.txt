- Proposes extending VAEs to model whole datasets via a hierarchical generative process with a dataset-level context unit.  
- Demonstrates capability to "learn to learn" and perform few-shot distribution learning from small sample sets.  
- Method is conceptually simple, elegant, and based on unsupervised representation learning.  
- Paper is clearly written and a pleasure to read.  
- Performance and chosen experiments are solid and appropriate for one-/few-shot learning.  
- Title "Neural Statistician" is overblown and the connection to an actual statistician is unclear.  
- Concerns about the spatial MNIST experiment: input density seems high and model should perhaps recognize distributions with fewer samples.  
- Questionable claim about red points in Figure 4 not appearing to correspond to meaningful points.  
- Recommend releasing code to aid reproducibility and further research.  
- Request more experimental details (hyperparameters, computational resources).  
- Suggest additional comparisons to other one-shot learning methods, especially supervised representation-based approaches.  
- Decision: Accept, based on novelty of unsupervised one-shot method and solid experiments.
