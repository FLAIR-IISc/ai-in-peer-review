- Proposes a hierarchical VAE for set-input prediction with a top-layer context unit that summarizes a dataset.  
- Shows the model can "learn to learn" and infer distributions from few examples (few-shot learning).  
- Conceptually simple, elegant method that performs well and adds to one-/few-shot learning literature.  
- Simpler than recent one-shot approaches and based on unsupervised representation learning.  
- Paper is clearly written and enjoyable to read.  
- Paper title/name is overly grandiose and not really aligned with the notion of a "statistician."  
- Experiments are well chosen and few-shot results are convincing given the method's simplicity.  
- Spatial MNIST is an interesting toy benchmark; concern that Figure 4 inputs look dense and the model might need fewer samples; red points in Figure 4 may not match the text's claim.  
- Asks whether the authors will release the code.
