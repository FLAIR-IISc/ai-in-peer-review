- Proposes a hierarchical generative model ("neural statistician") with dataset-level and example-level latents using a double variational bound.  
- Highlights that hierarchical modeling is important and under-explored in deep learning.  
- Tackles unsupervised learning of dataset representations; approach is well-motivated and clearly explained.  
- Shows concrete applications: clustering, generative model transfer, representative sample selection, and classifying unseen classes.  
- Empirically validated on synthetic 1-D, spatial MNIST, OMNIGLOT, and YouTube Faces, demonstrating effective dataset representations and few-shot learning.  
- Recommends acceptance based on originality, impact, and thorough evaluation.  
- Requests clarification on training the statistic network with minibatches and whether minibatching yields an unbiased gradient or limits expressive power.  
- Suggests exploring practical applications like hierarchical forecasting (electricity/sales) in future work.  
- Concludes the paper is strong and impactful, needing only minor revisions to address questions above.
