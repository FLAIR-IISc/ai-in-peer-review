- Proposes the "neural statistician": an extension of VAEs that learns unsupervised dataset-level statistics to enable efficient learning from new datasets for supervised and unsupervised tasks.

- Demonstrates effectiveness on clustering datasets, transferring generative models to new datasets, selecting representative samples, and classifying previously unseen classes.

- Reviewer recommends acceptance because the approach is well-motivated, well-situated in the literature, and empirically strong.

- Contribution is significant: offers a new perspective on modeling datasets, is conceptually simple, easy to implement, and valuable for one- or few-shot learning.

- Paper is clearly written with well-chosen experiments and solid results.

- Minor criticism: the name "neural statistician" may be overly grandiose.

- Few-shot learning results are solid; spatial MNIST is an interesting toy benchmark.

- Concern: Figure 4 inputs look dense; want to see performance with fewer samples and clarification on red points that may not be meaningful.

- Suggestion: release code to facilitate reproducibility and further experimentation.

- Suggestion: evaluate on more complex datasets and compare against state-of-the-art methods.

- Requests for more details: design and implementation of the statistic network.

- Requests for more details: how stochastic layer count and latent dimensionalities are chosen, required computational resources and scaling, applicability to transfer/meta-learning, and behavior on imbalanced or varying-size datasets.
