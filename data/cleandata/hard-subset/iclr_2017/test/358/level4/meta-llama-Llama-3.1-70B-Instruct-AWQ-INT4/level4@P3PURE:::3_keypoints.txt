- Introduces a VAE variant that models dataset-level latent variables with clear motivation and description.  
- Main contribution is extending the traditional VAE graphical model to richer dataset-level structure.  
- The term "statistician" is unclear and unnecessary; prefer using the standard term "approximate posterior" instead of coining "statistic network."  
- Learning an approximate posterior over summary statistics is not the only summarization approach (e.g., MLE); paper should acknowledge alternatives.  
- Experiments are well conducted, but the wording around "one-shot generation" (especially end of page 6) needs clarification.  
- Interpretation of Figure 5: compute the approximate posterior over the context vector from the input set, then generate samples from the forward model using samples from that posterior.  
- Clarify whether data point–dependent latent vectors z are drawn from the forward model or taken from the approximate posterior.  
- The claim of high-quality one-shot samples is unquantified; the paper should report log p(x|c) or log p(x) on held-out data to evaluate generation performance.  
- Since VAEs permit log-probability evaluation, include quantitative comparisons (e.g., vs. a vanilla VAE without the context latent) to demonstrate empirical gains.
