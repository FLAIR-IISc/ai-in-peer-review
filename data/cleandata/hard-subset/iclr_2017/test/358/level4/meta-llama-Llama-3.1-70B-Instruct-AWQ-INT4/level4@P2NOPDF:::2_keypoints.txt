- Proposes a hierarchical extension of VAEs for predicting from sets, with a top-layer abstract context unit summarizing a dataset.  
- Experiments demonstrate "learning to learn" ability: acquiring distributions from few examples.  
- Valuable contribution to one- or few-shot learning literature.  
- Method is conceptually simple, elegant, and achieves good performance.  
- Simpler than many recent one-shot methods and relies on unsupervised representation learning.  
- Paper is well-written and enjoyable to read.  
- Title seems overly ambitious; weak connection to the notion of a "statistician" unless very broadly interpreted.  
- Experiments are well-designed and few-shot results are solid given the method's simplicity.  
- Spatial MNIST dataset is an interesting and potentially useful toy benchmark.  
- Inputs in Figure 4 look relatively dense, raising whether the method could recognize distributions with fewer samples.  
- Red points in Figure 4 do not appear to correspond to meaningful points as claimed.  
- Please clarify plans for releasing the code.
