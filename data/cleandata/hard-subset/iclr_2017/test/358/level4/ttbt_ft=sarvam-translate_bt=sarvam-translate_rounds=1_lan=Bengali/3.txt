The authors have introduced a different type of variational autoencoder (VAE) that models dataset-level latent variables. The concept is clearly inspired and well-described. In my opinion, the biggest contribution of this paper is moving away from the relatively simple graphical model structure of traditional VAEs and introducing a more appealing structure in the deep learning community.

Comments:

- It is not clear to me why it should be called "statisticsian." A heuristic backward learning with a neural network on summary statistics is not the only conceivable way to summarize a dataset. One could consider a maximum likelihood method. Generally, it seemed that the paper could have been clearer if it had avoided creating new terms like "statistics network" and emphasized more accurate "heuristic backward learning."
- I appreciate the tests are beautiful, and the response to my question regarding "one-shot generation." I still think clarity of language is needed, especially at the end of page 6. My understanding of Figure 5 is as follows: take an input set, calculate the heuristic backward on the context vector, then generate the sample from the forward model given the heuristic backward. I want clarity on the following:
(a) Is the data point dependent vector z generated from the forward model or taken from the heuristic backward?
(b) I agree that the samples are high-quality, but this is not a quantitative statement. The advantage of VAE over GAN is that we have a natural way to calculate log-probability. To that end, a "correct" way to calculate "one-shot generation" performance is log p(x | c) (where c is the sample taken from the heuristic backward) or log p(x) reported for the hold-out dataset. I doubt that the log-probability performance of these networks will be as impressive as that of a vanilla VAE without the context latent variable. I still see no reason not to include it.
