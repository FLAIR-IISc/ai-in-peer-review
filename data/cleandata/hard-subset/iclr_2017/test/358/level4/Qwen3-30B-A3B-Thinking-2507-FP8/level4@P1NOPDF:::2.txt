This paper introduces a hierarchical variational autoencoder (VAE) for learning to predict properties from unordered sets of data points. The key innovation is a top-level abstract context layer that summarizes a dataset, enabling the model to "learn to learn" by rapidly adapting to new distributions from few examples. The method offers a conceptually elegant and effective approach to few-shot learning, leveraging unsupervised representation learning without complex meta-learning components. Compared to recent work, it stands out for its simplicity and strong empirical performance. The paper is well-written and a pleasure to read.  

That said, the title’s claim of "statistical learning" feels exaggerated relative to the contribution. The method bears little substantive resemblance to statistical learning theory, unless interpreted as "someone who devises statistics," which seems misleading.  

Experiments are well-chosen and demonstrate robust few-shot performance. The spatial MNIST benchmark is promising as a toy testbed, though Figure 4’s input density appears unnecessarily high—given the model’s capability, it should generalize from fewer samples. Additionally, the red points in Figure 4 do not align with the distributions described in the text, raising a minor inconsistency.  

The authors should clarify whether code will be released, as this would greatly facilitate reproducibility and community adoption.
