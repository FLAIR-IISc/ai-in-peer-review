

The authors introduce a novel extension of variational autoencoders that learns to compute summary statistics of datasets in an unsupervised manner. This "neural statistician" model enables efficient learning from new datasets for both unsupervised and supervised tasks, including clustering datasets, transferring generative models, selecting representative samples, and classifying previously unseen classes. The approach is well-motivated by the need to model datasets as the key objects rather than individual datapoints, and it makes a significant contribution to the literature by extending the traditional VAE framework to handle dataset-level latent variables.

Accept. The paper makes a clear theoretical contribution by extending VAEs to model dataset-level statistics, and the experimental results across multiple challenging domains (simple 1D distributions, spatial MNIST, OMNIGLOT, and YouTube Faces) demonstrate the model's effectiveness and versatility.

The key strength of this work is its principled approach to dataset representation that enables data efficiency and parameter efficiency while supporting few-shot learning capabilities. The model successfully learns to cluster datasets by distribution family, transfer generative models to new datasets, select representative samples, and classify unseen classes with high-quality results. The experimental design is comprehensive and demonstrates the model's robustness across different data modalities and task types. The paper effectively shows how the neural statistician can handle datasets of varying sizes and structures, providing a flexible framework for dataset-level representation learning.

The paper would benefit from more precise terminology. The use of "statistician" and "statistic network" is potentially confusing, as these terms are not standard in statistics or machine learning literature. The authors should consider using more conventional terms such as "approximate posterior network" or "dataset representation network" to avoid confusion with statistical concepts. Additionally, the description of the one-shot generation process in Figure 5 would be clearer if the paper explicitly stated whether the latent vectors z are generated from the forward model or sampled from the approximate posterior during generation.

I would like clarification on two specific points regarding the one-shot generation process:
(a) When generating samples conditioned on an input set, are the latent vectors z generated from the forward model (p(z|c)) or are they sampled from the approximate posterior (q(z|x,c))?
(b) The paper states the samples are "of high quality," but does not provide quantitative evidence. It would strengthen the paper to report log p(x|c) or log p(x) for held-out datasets to quantify the generation quality relative to a vanilla VAE without the context latent variable, as this is a natural metric for generative models.
