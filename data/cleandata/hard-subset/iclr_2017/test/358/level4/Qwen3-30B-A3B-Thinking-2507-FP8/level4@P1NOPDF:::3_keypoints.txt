- Introduces a VAE variant with dataset-level latent variables; core idea is clearly motivated and well-explained.  
- Notable contribution: departs from simple VAE graphical structure, introducing more complex dataset-level modeling.  
- The term "statistician" is misleading; learning an approximate posterior over summary statistics is not the only approach.  
- Recommend avoiding novel terms like "statistic network" and using standard terminology such as "approximate posterior" for clarity.  
- Experiments are well-designed and the authors addressed the reviewer’s prior "one-shot generation" query.  
- Page 6 description needs clarification: suggested process is (1) compute approximate posterior over context from input set, (2) sample from this posterior, (3) generate data via the forward model.  
- Clarify whether data-point-dependent vectors z are generated by the forward model or sampled directly from the approximate posterior.  
- One-shot generation claims need quantitative evaluation: report log p(x|c) (with c sampled from the approximate posterior) or log p(x) on held-out datasets.  
- The paper should compare these log-probability metrics to a vanilla VAE to substantiate claimed improvements; current lack of quantitative comparison is a notable omission.
