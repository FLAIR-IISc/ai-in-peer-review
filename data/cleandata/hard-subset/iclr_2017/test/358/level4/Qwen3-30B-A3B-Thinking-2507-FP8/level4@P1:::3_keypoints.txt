- Paper introduces "Neural Statistician": a VAE extension that learns dataset-level summary statistics (context) for unsupervised dataset modeling and transfer.  
- Model treats datasets as primary objects and learns summary statistics enabling tasks like clustering, generative transfer, representative sampling, and unseen-class classification.  
- Experiments shown on synthetic 1D, spatial MNIST, Omniglot, and YouTube Faces.  
- Recommendation: reject.  
- Major concern 1: misleading terminology ("neural statistician", "statistic network") that obscures the model as a hierarchical VAE / approximate posterior over a context variable.  
- Major concern 2: lack of quantitative evidence for one-shot learning claims; presented samples are qualitative only.  
- Reviewer requests proper quantitative metrics (e.g., log p(x|c) or log p(x)) on held-out datasets to support one-shot claims.  
- Suggestion: replace nonstandard terms with standard ones like "context encoder" or "dataset summarizer."  
- Suggestion: report log-likelihoods for one-shot generation and compare against a baseline VAE without the context variable.  
- Clarification needed for Figure 5: specify whether z vectors are drawn from the generative prior p(z|c) or from the approximate posterior q(z|x,c).  
- Questions to authors: (a) Are z vectors for Figure 5 sampled from p(z|c) or taken from q(z|x,c)? (b) Can authors provide log p(x) or log p(x|c) scores for one-shot generation and baseline comparisons?
