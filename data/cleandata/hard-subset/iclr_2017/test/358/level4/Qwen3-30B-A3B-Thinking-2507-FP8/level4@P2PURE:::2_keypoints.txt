- Paper proposes a hierarchical VAE with a top-layer abstract context unit that summarizes a dataset.  
- Experiments show the model can "learn to learn" and infer distributions from minimal examples.  
- Valuable contribution to one- and few-shot learning literature.  
- Method is conceptually simple, elegant, empirically effective, and leverages unsupervised representation learning.  
- Simpler than many recent one-shot learning methods.  
- Paper is clearly written and a pleasure to read.  
- Title is overly grandiose and has little substantive connection to statistics unless interpreted loosely.  
- Experiments are well-designed and few-shot results appear robust given the method’s simplicity.  
- Spatial MNIST is an interesting toy benchmark, but Figure 4 inputs look overly dense—should require fewer samples to recognize the distribution.  
- Nitpick: the red points in Figure 4 do not align with the claimed meaningful points in the text.  
- Reviewer asks whether the authors will release the code.
