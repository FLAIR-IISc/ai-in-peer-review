

The authors introduce a variant of the variational autoencoder (VAE) that models dataset-level latent variables. The core idea is clearly motivated and well-explained. A key contribution of this work is its departure from the relatively simple graphical structure of traditional VAEs, introducing more complex modeling approaches to the deep learning community.

Comments:
- The term "statistician" is misleading. Learning an approximate posterior over summary statistics is not the only viable method for dataset summarization via neural networks (e.g., maximum likelihood approaches exist). The paper would benefit from avoiding novel terminology like "statistic network" and instead using standard terms such as "approximate posterior" for clarity.
- The experiments are well-designed, and I appreciate the authors' response to my prior query about "one-shot generation." However, the description on page 6 requires clarification. Based on Figure 5, the process appears to be: (1) compute an approximate posterior over the context vector from an input set, (2) sample from this posterior, and (3) generate data using the forward model. I request explicit clarification on:
  (a) Whether the data-point-dependent vectors *z* are generated by the forward model or sampled directly from the approximate posterior.
  (b) While the generated samples appear high-quality, this remains qualitative. A key advantage of VAEs over GANs is the ability to compute log-probabilities. To rigorously evaluate "one-shot generation," the authors should report log *p*(*x* | *c*) (where *c* is sampled from the approximate posterior) or log *p*(*x*) for held-out datasets. I expect these metrics to demonstrate significant improvements over a vanilla VAE without dataset-level latent variables, and including them would substantially strengthen the evaluation. The current lack of quantitative comparison on this established VAE metric is a notable omission.
