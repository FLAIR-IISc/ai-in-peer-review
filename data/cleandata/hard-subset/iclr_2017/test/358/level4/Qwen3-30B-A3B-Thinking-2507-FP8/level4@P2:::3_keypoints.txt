- Introduces a VAE extension that models entire datasets as fundamental units, learning dataset-level summary representations.
- Proposes the "neural statistician" framework to compute dataset representations for clustering, model transfer, representative sampling, and few-shot classification.
- Well-motivated conceptual shift from data points to datasets with theoretical grounding.
- Empirically validated on synthetic 1D distributions, spatial MNIST, Omniglot, and YouTube Faces in both generative and discriminative settings.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: Significant contribution by enabling dataset-focused representation learning that supports efficient transfer and few-shot learning.
- Core technical idea: extend VAEs with dataset-level latent variables to learn summary statistics of entire datasets.
- Experiments are comprehensive and demonstrate versatility; few-shot Omniglot results are particularly strong.
- Good comparison to related work (e.g., matching networks) highlighting advantages for unlabeled datasets.
- Feedback: The term "statistician" is misleading; recommend using "context encoder" or "dataset encoder."
- Feedback: Clarify Figure 5’s generation process—whether z is sampled from p(z|c) or from q(z|x,c).
- Feedback: Provide quantitative few-shot generation metrics (e.g., log p(x|c) or log p(x)) rather than only qualitative samples.
- Feedback: Clarify distinction between learned representations and traditional statistical summaries (means, variances).
- Question: In Fig.5, are generated z drawn from p(z|c) or from q(z|x,c)?
- Question: Can you report log p(x|c) or log p(x) for few-shot generation tasks?
- Question: How does the model handle varying dataset sizes beyond the mentioned sample dropout?
- Question: Please elaborate on the clustering methodology and evaluation metrics used for dataset clustering.
- Overall: Valuable contribution; with terminology adjustments and additional quantitative evaluation, it’s well-suited for ICLR.
