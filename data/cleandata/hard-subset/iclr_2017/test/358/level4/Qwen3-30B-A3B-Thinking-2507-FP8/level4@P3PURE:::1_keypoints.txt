- Introduces a hierarchical generative model with a lower level for points and a higher level for unordered sets of datasets using a "double" variational bound.  
- Core innovation: higher-level latent variables describe entire datasets while lower-level latents describe individual examples.  
- Addresses an important, under-explored problem in hierarchical generative modeling with potentially significant impact.  
- Pros: few-shot learning results look promising.  
- Pros: the "double" variational bound framework is clearly explained and broadly applicable.  
- Question: Does the statistic network train using minibatches of examples from each dataset?  
- Question/concern: If minibatches are used, is the resulting gradient estimator unbiased relative to full-dataset training?  
- Concern: Minibatch training could prevent the statistic network from detecting dataset-wide features (e.g., presence of any example with a feature), limiting expressive power.  
- Suggestion: Apply framework to hierarchical forecasting tasks (e.g., electricity demand or sales prediction) as a practical use case.
