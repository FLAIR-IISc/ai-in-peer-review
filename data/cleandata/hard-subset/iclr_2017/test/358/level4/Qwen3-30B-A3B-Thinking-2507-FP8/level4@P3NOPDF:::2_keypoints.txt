- Introduces a hierarchical VAE for meta-learning from set-structured data, using an abstract context unit to summarize datasets for fast adaptation.  
- Core contribution: an elegant unsupervised approach to few-shot learning that generalizes distributional knowledge from minimal examples.  
- Method is conceptually simple yet effective, providing a compelling alternative to more complex recent works.  
- Presentation is clear and well-structured, making technical contributions accessible.  
- Experimental design is robust, showing strong performance on Omniglot and Mini-ImageNet given the method’s simplicity.  
- Spatial MNIST is a useful toy benchmark for visualizing distributional learning, but Figure 4 raises concerns about claimed sample efficiency.  
- The red points in Figure 4 appear to require dense input coverage and may not match the claimed distributions (e.g., center at (0.5,0.5)); caption/figure need revision.  
- The title "Learning to Learn Like a Statistician" is overly ambitious/misleading and should be replaced with a more precise title (e.g., "Hierarchical VAEs for Meta-Learning from Sets").  
- Methodologically valuable: unsupervised foundation distinguishes it from supervised meta-learning (e.g., MAML) and ablations show the hierarchical context unit is necessary.  
- Paper should more explicitly contrast its unsupervised advantage against supervised baselines in the discussion.  
- Authors should confirm whether code will be publicly released to ensure reproducibility.  
- Overall: strong submission that merits publication pending minor revisions to title, figure details, and code commitment.
