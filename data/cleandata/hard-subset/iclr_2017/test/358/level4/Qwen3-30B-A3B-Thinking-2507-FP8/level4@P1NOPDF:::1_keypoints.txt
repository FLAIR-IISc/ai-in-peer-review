- Introduces a hierarchical generative model with lower-level latents for individual data points and higher-level latents for unordered collections of datasets.  
- Core contribution: a "double" variational bound framework with dataset-level and example-level latent variables.  
- Argues hierarchical modeling is an important, impactful, and underexplored direction in deep learning.  
- Strength: few-shot learning results appear promising.  
- Strength: the double variational bound is clearly articulated and broadly applicable.  
- Key question: Does training the statistic network use minibatches or full batches?  
- Key question: If minibatches are used, can the model learn dataset-wide characteristics (e.g., presence of an outlier), or does minibatching prevent learning such global features?  
- Key question: If full-batch training is not used, does the full-batch approach provide an unbiased gradient estimator?  
- Suggestion for future work: apply the framework to hierarchical forecasting tasks (e.g., electricity or sales demand prediction) to demonstrate practical utility.
