- Introduces a hierarchical VAE variant for predicting from sets with an abstract context unit at the top to summarize datasets.  
- Demonstrates the model’s ability to "learn to learn" by acquiring distributional knowledge from minimal examples.  
- Offers a valuable, conceptually straightforward contribution to one- and few-shot learning, relying on unsupervised representation learning.  
- Simple and effective approach that distinguishes itself from recent alternatives.  
- Paper is well-written and engaging.  
- Title is overly grandiose and not substantively aligned with the role of a statistician.  
- Experiments are appropriately designed and few-shot results are robust given the method’s simplicity.  
- Spatial MNIST is a compelling toy benchmark; Figure 4 raises concerns about high input density and misaligned red points versus textual claims.  
- Requests clarification on whether the authors will release the code.
