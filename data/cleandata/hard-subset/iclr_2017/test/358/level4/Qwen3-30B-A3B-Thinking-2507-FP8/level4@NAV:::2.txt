This paper introduces a hierarchical variant of the VAE for learning to predict from sets of data points, featuring an abstract context unit at the top layer to summarize datasets. Experiments demonstrate the method’s capacity to "learn to learn" by acquiring distributional knowledge from minimal examples.  
Overall, the work offers a valuable contribution to one- and few-shot learning literature. The approach is conceptually straightforward and effective, with simplicity and reliance on unsupervised representation learning distinguishing it from recent alternatives. The paper is well-written and engaging.  
However, the title is excessively grandiose relative to the work’s scope; the method bears little substantive resemblance to a statistician’s role, beyond a playful interpretation of "someone who thinks up statistics."  
Experiments are appropriately designed, with few-shot results robust given the method’s simplicity. The spatial MNIST dataset presents a compelling toy benchmark, though Figure 4’s input density appears unnecessarily high—could the method identify the distribution with fewer samples? (Minor note: The red points in Figure 4 do not align with the textual claim of meaningful representation.)  
Will the authors release the code?
