- Introduces a novel VAE variant with dataset-level latent variables; core idea is well-motivated and significant.  
- The term "statistician" is misleading; replace "statistic network" with standard terms like "approximate posterior network" and avoid new jargon.  
- Clarify "one-shot generation": ambiguous whether context vectors z are sampled from the approximate posterior or directly used as inputs.  
- Lacks quantitative validation: report log-likelihoods log p(x | c) on held-out data (with c sampled from the posterior) and compare to a standard VAE.  
- Experiments and ablation studies are solid; Table 2 shows dataset-level latent variable is necessary.  
- Minor wording: prefer "dataset-level latent representations" to avoid confusion with per-dataset parameters.  
- Maintain consistent distinction between context vector c and latent z; Figure 5 currently conflates them.  
- Temper the claim of generalization to unseen datasets—current results only cover training datasets.  
- Overall: promising and publishable with revisions—clarify terminology, refine generation procedure exposition (Figure 5), and add log-likelihood metrics.
