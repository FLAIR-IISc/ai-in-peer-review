

The authors present a variant of the variational autoencoder (VAE) that models dataset-level latent variables. The motivation and description are clear, and the primary contribution lies in advancing beyond the conventional graphical model structure of standard VAEs by introducing more complex architectures to the deep learning community.  

Comments:  
- The term "statistician" is misleading; learning an approximate posterior over summary statistics is not the sole approach for dataset summarization via neural networks (e.g., maximum likelihood methods are viable). The paper would benefit from clearer terminology, avoiding novel terms like "statistic network" in favor of the precise "approximate posterior."  
- The experiments are well-executed, and I appreciate the authors' response regarding "one-shot generation." However, the description at the end of page 6 requires clarification. Specifically, for Figure 5:  
  (a) Are the data-dependent vectors \(z\) generated from the forward model or sampled from the approximate posterior?  
  (b) While sample quality is high, this claim lacks quantitative validation. VAEs offer a key advantage over GANs via computable log-probabilities. To properly evaluate "one-shot generation," the paper should report \(\log p(x \mid c)\) (where \(c\) is sampled from the approximate posterior) or \(\log p(x)\) for held-out datasets. Comparing these metrics against a vanilla VAE (without context latent variables) would demonstrate the method’s statistical advantage, and such analysis should be included.
