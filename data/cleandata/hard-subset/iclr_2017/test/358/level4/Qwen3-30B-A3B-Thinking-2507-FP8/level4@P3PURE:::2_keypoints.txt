Introduces a hierarchical VAE with an abstract context unit that summarizes entire datasets for set-based property prediction.  
Enables "learn to learn" few-shot learning by acquiring distributional knowledge from minimal examples.  
Valuable contribution due to conceptual simplicity, strong empirical performance, and an unsupervised representation learning foundation.  
Streamlined architecture compared to recent approaches; paper is clearly written and highly readable.  
Title's claim of "statistical learning" is overstated and misleading relative to formal statistical learning.  
Experiments are well-designed and few-shot results are robust given the method's simplicity.  
Spatial MNIST is a promising toy benchmark used in the evaluations.  
Figure 4 concerns: input representations appear overly dense, suggesting similar distribution recognition might need fewer samples.  
Figure 4 concern: red points lack clear correspondence to the distributions described in the text.  
Recommend releasing the code to facilitate reproducibility.
