- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Main contribution: introduces the "neural statistician" that extends VAEs to learn unsupervised dataset-level summary statistics via a hierarchical generative model with a dataset-level context variable.  
- Methodological novelty: uses a double variational bound to provide principled unsupervised learning of dataset representations.  
- Empirical validation: shows versatility across 1D distributions, spatial MNIST, Omniglot, and YouTube Faces for clustering, generative model transfer, representative selection, and few-shot classification.  
- Strengths: well-motivated, theoretically sound, empirically validated, data- and parameter-efficient, and applicable to both unsupervised and supervised tasks.  
- Limitation/requested elaboration: clarify how the statistic network handles datasets of varying sizes, a potential limitation for real-world inconsistent dataset sizes.  
- Suggested extension: demonstrate practical applications beyond current experiments (e.g., hierarchical forecasting for electricity or sales) to highlight real-world utility.  
- Clarification questions about training: are minibatches of examples within a dataset used during optimization, and if so, does that provide an unbiased estimator of the full gradient given the risk of missing dataset-level features in subsets?
