

The paper introduces a novel approach called the "neural statistician" that extends variational autoencoders to learn unsupervised summary statistics of entire datasets. The model computes representations of datasets as a whole rather than individual data points, enabling efficient learning from new datasets for both unsupervised and supervised tasks. The authors demonstrate the method's ability to cluster datasets, transfer generative models to new datasets, select representative samples, and classify previously unseen classes.

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.

The paper's key strengths lie in its conceptual simplicity and elegant approach to dataset representation learning. The method provides a principled unsupervised way to learn dataset statistics, which is a significant contribution to few-shot learning literature. The experimental results across multiple domains (simple 1D distributions, spatial MNIST, OMNIGLOT, and YouTube Faces) demonstrate strong performance with minimal supervision requirements, making it a valuable addition to the field.

The paper is clearly written and well-organized, with thorough experimental validation. The approach is well-motivated within the literature on transfer learning and few-shot learning, and the method's parameter efficiency and data efficiency advantages are well-explained. The experiments are well-designed to demonstrate the method's capabilities across different tasks and dataset structures.

For improvement, consider revising the title and method name to better reflect the technical contribution. The current "neural statistician" terminology may be misleading as it doesn't strongly connect to statistical theory or practice. A more descriptive name would help readers better understand the method's actual contribution. Additionally, the explanation of the spatial MNIST representative sampling in Figure 4 could be strengthened to clarify why the selected samples appear as they do, particularly regarding the red points mentioned in the paper.

I would appreciate clarification on the following points:
1. Could the authors provide a more precise justification for the "neural statistician" naming convention, particularly regarding its relationship to statistical theory?
2. For the spatial MNIST experiment, could you elaborate on why the method requires relatively dense sampling to effectively capture dataset structure, and whether it would perform well with substantially fewer samples?
3. Will the authors be releasing the code to facilitate reproducibility and further research?
