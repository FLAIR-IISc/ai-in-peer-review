This paper introduces a method for predicting properties from sets of data points using a hierarchical variant of the VAE, where the top layer employs an abstract context unit to summarize a dataset. Experiments demonstrate the method’s ability to "learn to learn" by acquiring the capacity to infer distributions from minimal examples.  
Overall, this work provides a valuable contribution to one- or few-shot learning literature. The approach is conceptually simple, elegant, and empirically effective. Compared to recent one-shot learning methods, the proposed technique is notably simpler and leverages unsupervised representation learning. The paper is clearly written and a pleasure to read.  
However, the title is overly grandiose relative to the scope of the work; the method bears little substantive connection to statistics unless interpreted as "someone who devises statistics."  
Experiments are well-designed, and the few-shot learning results appear robust given the method’s simplicity. The spatial MNIST dataset serves as an interesting toy benchmark, though the inputs in Figure 4 appear overly dense—shouldn’t the method recognize the distribution with fewer samples? (Nitpick: the red points in Figure 4 do not align with the claimed meaningful points as described in the text.)
