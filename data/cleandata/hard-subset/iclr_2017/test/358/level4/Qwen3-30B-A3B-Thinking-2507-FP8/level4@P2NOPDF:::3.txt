

The authors propose a novel variant of the variational autoencoder (VAE) that incorporates dataset-level latent variables, extending beyond the standard VAE's per-sample latent structure. This conceptual shift represents a significant contribution, as it introduces more complex graphical model architectures to the deep learning community and addresses a meaningful gap in modeling dataset-wide dependencies. The core idea is well-motivated and clearly articulated.

**Major Concerns:**  
1. **Terminology:** The paper introduces the term "statistic network" to describe the component learning the approximate posterior over dataset summaries. This is misleading and unnecessary. Learning an approximate posterior over summary statistics is not the only viable approach (e.g., maximum likelihood or other summary representations could be used). The authors should replace "statistic network" with "approximate posterior network" or similar, adhering to standard probabilistic terminology. The current phrasing risks confusion and undermines precision.

2. **Experimental Evaluation:** While the experiments demonstrate visually plausible samples, the evaluation lacks quantitative rigor. The paper claims "one-shot generation" success but fails to provide a metric that aligns with VAEs' key strength: tractable log-probability estimation. To properly assess performance, the authors should report **log p(x | c)** (where *c* is sampled from the approximate posterior) or **log p(x)** for held-out datasets. This metric would directly quantify how well the model captures dataset-level structure compared to a standard VAE (which lacks the context latent variable). Without it, the claimed advantage remains qualitative and unverified. The authors should include these scores in the revision.

**Specific Clarifications Needed:**  
- **Figure 5 (Page 6):** The caption and description require clarification. Based on the figure, the process appears to be: (1) Compute approximate posterior *q(c | X)* over the context vector *c* for a dataset *X*; (2) Generate new samples *x* using the forward model *p(x | c)* with *c* sampled from *q(c | X)*. However, the following points are ambiguous:  
  (a) Are the generated data points *z* (context vectors) drawn from *q(c | X)* or directly from the prior *p(c)*? The text suggests the former, but this must be explicitly stated.  
  (b) The phrase "one-shot generation" is not quantified. The paper should clarify whether "one-shot" refers to generating *all* samples from a single *c* (as implied) and provide the log-probability metric mentioned above to substantiate quality claims.

**Overall Assessment:**  
The paper presents a compelling conceptual advancement with clear potential. However, the current version lacks terminological precision and fails to leverage the VAE framework’s statistical strengths for rigorous evaluation. Addressing the terminology issue and adding the requested log-probability metrics would substantially strengthen the contribution. The experimental section must be revised to include these quantitative comparisons, as they are essential for validating the paper’s core claim about dataset-level modeling. With these revisions, the work would make a valuable contribution to the field.
