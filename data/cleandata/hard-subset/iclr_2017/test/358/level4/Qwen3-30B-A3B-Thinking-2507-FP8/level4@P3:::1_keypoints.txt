- Introduces the "neural statistician," an unsupervised method to learn dataset-level summary statistics by extending VAEs with a statistic network that outputs a dataset summary vector.  
- Reframes modeling to treat datasets as primary objects, enabling transfer, clustering, representative selection, and classifying unseen classes.  
- Demonstrated across synthetic 1D tasks, spatial MNIST, Omniglot, and YouTube Faces with promising few-shot learning results.  
- Decision: Accept — significant contribution to hierarchical modeling and dataset representation with strong experiments.  
- Addresses an important gap: principled methods for learning representations of datasets rather than individual points.  
- Proposes an elegant VAE extension with a double variational bound handling dataset-level context and point-level latent variables.  
- Statistic network can learn meaningful unsupervised representations; experiments are comprehensive and Omniglot few-shot results are competitive despite weaker supervision.  
- Clearly relates the approach to transfer learning, one-shot learning, and other related work; theoretical foundation justified via the variational lower bound.  
- Suggest clarifying the training procedure for the statistic network, specifically whether minibatches subsample within datasets or process whole datasets.  
- Recommend more detailed analysis of handling varying dataset sizes and clearer rationale/impact for "sample dropout."  
- Ask for a more thorough comparison with multiple-instance learning literature.  
- Question: Do you train the statistic network on minibatches within datasets or entire datasets, and how does that affect capturing dataset-wide characteristics?  
- Question: Provide theoretical or empirical support for the claim that the sample mean gives the statistic network substantial representational power, especially for approximating arbitrary moments.  
- Question: In YouTube Faces, how does using shared variance parameters across datapoints affect modeling within-dataset versus across-dataset variability?  
- Overall: Paper is well-written, technically sound, and valuable; clarifying the statistic network training procedure would strengthen it further.
