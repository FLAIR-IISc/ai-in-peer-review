- Introduces a hierarchical generative model with lower-level latent variables for examples and higher-level latent variables for unordered sets of datasets, trained with a “double” variational bound.  
- Hierarchical modeling is an important, under-explored area in deep learning with significant potential impact.  
- The double variational-bound formulation and the idea of a dataset-level latent (statistic) network is clearly articulated and appears broadly applicable.  
- Few-shot learning results look promising (reviewer not an expert in that domain).  
- Question: Does training of the statistic network use minibatches or the full dataset?  
- Concern: Training the statistic network on minibatches may fail to capture dataset-level properties that depend on any single example’s presence (e.g., “exists” features), reducing expressiveness.  
- Suggestion: Evaluate the model on hierarchical forecasting tasks (e.g., electricity or sales prediction) to demonstrate practical utility.
