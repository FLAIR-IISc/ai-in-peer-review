- Proposes a hierarchical generative framework with lower-level latents for individual data points and higher-level latents for unordered collections of datasets.  
- Uses a "double" variational bound where dataset-level and example-level latent variables are jointly modeled.  
- Notes hierarchical modeling is an important, underexplored direction in deep learning.  
- Pros: few-shot learning results look promising (reviewer not expert in few-shot).  
- Pros: the double variational bound idea is well-motivated and likely broadly applicable.  
- Question: Does the statistic network train with minibatches, and if not, how is it handled?  
- Concern: Minibatch training may not provide an unbiased estimator for dataset-level statistics—e.g., detecting if any example in a dataset has a feature could be impossible from partial minibatches, limiting expressivity.  
- Suggestion: Demonstrate the framework on hierarchical forecasting tasks (electricity demand, sales) for practical impact.
