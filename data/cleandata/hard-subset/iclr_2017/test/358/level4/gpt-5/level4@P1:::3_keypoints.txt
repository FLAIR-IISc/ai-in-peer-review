- Paper extends VAEs with a dataset-level latent "context" c and a statistic network q(c|D) mapping unordered sets to a posterior over c, enabling unsupervised dataset summaries.
- Approach respects exchangeability via pooling and uses hierarchical latents with skip connections and amortized inference over z and c.
- Key contribution: showing how dataset-level latent variables can be integrated into VAE-style deep generative models and trained end-to-end.
- Decision: Accept.
- Main reasons for acceptance: novel, principled extension of VAEs to dataset-level modeling and strong, diverse empirical demonstrations.
- Empirical support: qualitative and quantitative results on synthetic 1D, spatial MNIST, Omniglot, and YouTube Faces, with competitive few-shot classification especially in 5-way settings.
- Problem addressed: learning to summarize i.i.d. datasets via a context variable for fast adaptation to new datasets/classes.
- Literature placement: well situated relative to topic models, structured VAEs, transfer and few-shot learning; uses continuous latents and neural parameterizations rather than discrete topic models.
- Main shortcoming: lack of quantitative likelihood-based evaluation (e.g., log p(x|c), log p(x), IWAE) to substantiate one-shot generation quality and compare to a VAE without c.
- Terminology suggestion: avoid "neural statistician/statistic network" in favor of clearer terms like "context inference network" or "approximate posterior over c."
- Clarify generation pipeline and "one-shot generation" wording (provide an explicit algorithmic description mirroring Algorithms 1–2).
- Evaluation suggestions: report held-out log-likelihoods—(i) log p(x|c) with c from q(c|D), (ii) overall log p(x) on new datasets, and (iii) a baseline VAE without c.
- Questions for authors: clarify whether samples in Fig.5 use z sampled from p(z|c) or from q(z|x,c), and provide quantitative one-shot generation metrics versus a vanilla VAE.
- Further questions: consider ablations/alternatives for set summarization (e.g., mean vs max pooling, sample-dropout) and whether conditioning on the full support set improves few-shot classification compared to independent q(c|Di) comparisons.
