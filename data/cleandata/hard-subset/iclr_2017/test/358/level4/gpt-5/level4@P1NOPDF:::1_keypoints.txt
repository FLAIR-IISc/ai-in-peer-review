- Proposes a hierarchical generative model with two latent levels: a dataset-level variable (for unordered sets) and an example-level variable (for points).

- Central idea is a “double” variational bound with a higher-level latent for datasets and a lower-level latent for examples.

- Notes hierarchical modeling is important, high-impact, and under-explored in deep learning.

- Pros: few-shot learning results appear strong.

- Pros: the double variational bound is clearly presented and seems broadly applicable.

- Question: Is the statistic network (which aggregates examples to produce the dataset-level representation) trained using minibatches (subsets of examples per dataset)?

- Question: If minibatches are used, does this give an unbiased estimator of the gradient compared to using all examples in the dataset?

- Concern: Training the statistic network on minibatches may fail to learn “any-of” dataset-level features because informative examples can be omitted from many minibatches.

- General concern: Using subsets rather than full datasets for the statistic network may limit the model’s effective expressive power.

- Suggestion: Apply the model to hierarchical forecasting tasks (e.g., electricity demand or retail sales) as a practical application.
