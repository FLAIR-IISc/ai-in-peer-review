Proposes a hierarchical VAE with a dataset-level latent "context" c inferred by an exchangeable statistic network q(c|D) that conditions per-instance latents.  
Trained unsupervised to produce dataset-level statistics enabling transfer and few-shot generative modeling; demonstrated on 1D distribution clustering, Omniglot, spatial MNIST subset selection, and YouTube Faces.  
Decision: Accept — a clean, well-motivated, simple unsupervised generative approach with solid empirical results.  
Motivation: Explicitly addresses learning representations of datasets (sets of i.i.d. samples) using a hierarchical, exchangeable inference framework.  
Literature placement: Connects to parameter-transfer ideas, topic models, and one-shot methods; offers an unsupervised alternative to supervised matching-network style approaches.  
Evidence and rigor: Experiments support claims—learned dataset statistics are meaningful, transfer to new classes works, competitive few-shot classification (notably 5-way), spatial MNIST is a useful toy, and YouTube Faces shows identity-consistent generation proof-of-concept.  
Naming critique: “Neural statistician” may overclaim; a more descriptive title is recommended.  
Spatial MNIST critique: Inputs look dense—clarify sample efficiency and report how performance degrades with fewer samples.  
Figure 4 critique: Red points do not appear to match the text’s description; reconcile or adjust the figure and annotations.  
Reproducibility: Request release of training and evaluation code to encourage adoption and fair comparison.  
Questions for authors: Will you release code? How does spatial MNIST performance change with fewer points per set? How were the red points in Figure 4 selected and what precisely do they denote?  
Overall: Strong, clear, and useful contribution to learning from sets and unsupervised few-shot learning.
