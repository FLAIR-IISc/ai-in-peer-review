Apologies for the late review—I had technical issues with OpenReview that prevented me from posting earlier.

This paper proposes a method for predicting from sets of data points. The model is a hierarchical VAE in which a top-level context variable summarizes a dataset. Experiments show that the model “learns to learn,” acquiring the ability to infer distributions from small numbers of examples.

Overall, this is a welcome addition to the one-/few-shot learning literature. The approach is conceptually simple and elegant, and it appears to perform well. Compared with other recent one-shot approaches, the proposed method is simpler and grounded in unsupervised representation learning. The paper is clearly written and a pleasure to read.

However, the title feels overly grandiose relative to the actual contribution; the method has little to do with a statistician, unless by that one means “someone who invents statistics.”

The experiments are well chosen, and the few-shot results seem solid given the simplicity of the method.

The spatial MNIST dataset is interesting and could serve as a useful toy benchmark. That said, the inputs in Figure 4 look quite dense—shouldn’t the method be able to recognize the distribution with fewer samples? (Minor nitpick: the red points in Figure 4 do not appear to correspond to meaningful points as claimed in the text.)

Will the authors release the code?
