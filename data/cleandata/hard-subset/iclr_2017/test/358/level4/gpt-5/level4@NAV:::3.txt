The authors present an extension of the variational autoencoder (VAE) that incorporates dataset-level latent variables. The motivation is clear and the exposition is strong. In my view, the primary contribution is pushing beyond the standard, relatively simple graphical structure of conventional VAEs and introducing richer probabilistic structures to the deep learning community.

Comments:
- I am not convinced by the term “statistician.” Inferring an approximate posterior over summary statistics is not the only conceivable way to summarize a dataset with a neural network; for example, one could pursue a maximum likelihood approach, among others. Overall, the paper would be clearer if it avoided coining new terms like “statistic network” and instead used the more precise “approximate posterior.”
- The experiments are solid, and I appreciate the response to my question about “one-shot generation.” However, the phrasing still needs clarification, particularly at the end of page 6. My reading of Figure 5 is: take an input set, infer the approximate posterior over the context vector, then generate from the forward model using samples from that posterior. I would like clarification on:
(a) Are the data-point-specific vectors z generated by the forward model, or taken from the approximate posterior?
(b) I agree the samples look high quality, but that is qualitative. A key advantage of VAEs over GANs is the ability to compute log-probabilities. Thus, a principled way to assess “one-shot generation” would be to report log p(x | c) (with c sampled from the approximate posterior) or log p(x) on held-out datasets. I suspect these models would perform well relative to a vanilla VAE without the context latent variable, so I see no reason not to include such results.
