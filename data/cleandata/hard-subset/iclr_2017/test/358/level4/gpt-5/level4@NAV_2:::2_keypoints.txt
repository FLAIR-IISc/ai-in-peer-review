- Proposes a hierarchical VAE with a dataset-level latent “context” that learns unsupervised set-level representations to enable few-shot inference.
- Decision: Accept — method is conceptually clean, unsupervised, well-executed, with strong few-shot empirical results and clear writing.
- Simpler and more generally grounded in unsupervised representation learning than many recent one-/few-shot methods while remaining competitive.
- Experiments are thoughtfully chosen and convincingly demonstrate few-shot generalization.
- Introduces spatial MNIST as an interesting and potentially useful toy benchmark for set-level inference.
- Title/terminology (“neural statistician”) is arguably overstated; the model learns summary statistics rather than broad statistical analysis.
- Spatial MNIST inputs appear dense; the model might identify underlying distributions with substantially fewer points than shown.
- The red-highlighted points in Fig. 4 do not clearly match the claimed “representative” samples.
- Requests clarification on how performance on spatial MNIST depends on number of samples per set and asking for results across decreasing sample counts.
- Asks authors to clarify how highlighted points were selected under the divergence-based criterion and whether code/pretrained models will be released for reproducibility.
