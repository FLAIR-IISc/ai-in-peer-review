- Introduces a hierarchical VAE with a top-layer context unit that summarizes an entire dataset for set-based prediction.  
- Demonstrates the model effectively "learns to learn," inferring distributions from small numbers of examples (few-shot).  
- Solid contribution to one-/few-shot learning; conceptually simple, elegant, and appears to perform well.  
- Simpler and more grounded in unsupervised representation learning than many recent one-shot methods.  
- Paper is clearly written and enjoyable to read.  
- Title is overly grand and not closely aligned with how a statistician actually works.  
- Experiments are well chosen and few-shot results are strong given the simplicity of the approach.  
- Spatial MNIST is an interesting, potentially useful toy benchmark.  
- Concern: inputs in Figure 4 appear dense — model should recognize underlying distribution with fewer samples; red points in Figure 4 do not match the claimed meaningful points.  
- Asks whether the authors will release the code.
