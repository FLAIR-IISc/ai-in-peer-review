This paper proposes a variational autoencoder (VAE) variant that introduces dataset-level latent variables (“context” variables) to capture shared structure across examples. The idea is well motivated and clearly presented. In my view, the main contribution is pushing beyond the relatively simple graphical structures of standard VAEs and exposing the deep learning community to richer latent-variable models.

Comments:
- Terminology. I am not convinced by the term “statistician,” nor by “statistic network.” Learning an approximate posterior over summary statistics is only one way to summarize a dataset with a neural network; alternatives (e.g., maximum-likelihood pooling, other deterministic or learned aggregation schemes) are possible. The paper would read more clearly if it avoided coining new terms and instead used established language such as “approximate posterior over the dataset-level latent variable” or simply “inference network.”

- Experiments and “one-shot generation.” I appreciate the response to my earlier question, but the language around “one-shot generation” (especially at the end of page 6) still needs clarification. My current understanding of Figure 5 is:
  - Given an input set, compute the approximate posterior over the context variable c.
  - Sample c from this posterior and then generate observations from the forward model.

  Please clarify:
  (a) Are the per-datapoint latent variables z sampled from the generative model (e.g., p(z | c) and then p(x | z, c)), or are they taken from the approximate posterior q(z | x, c)?  
  (b) While the samples are visually high quality, this is not quantified. A key advantage of VAEs over GANs is the ability to compute likelihood-based metrics. A principled way to assess “one-shot generation” would be to report log p(x | c) where c is sampled from q(c | D), or log p(x) (or ELBO estimates) for held-out datasets. A direct comparison of these metrics to a vanilla VAE without a context latent variable would likely showcase the benefits of your model; I see no reason not to include such results.

Overall, this is a strong and timely contribution that encourages more expressive latent-variable structures in deep generative modeling. With clearer terminology and quantitative evaluations of the “one-shot” setting, the paper would be significantly strengthened.
