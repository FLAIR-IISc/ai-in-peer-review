This paper introduces a hierarchical generative model in which the lower level models individual datapoints within datasets, and the higher level models unordered sets of datasets. The core idea is to optimize a “double” variational bound, with a dataset-level latent variable describing datasets and an example-level latent variable describing individual examples. Hierarchical modeling is an important, high-impact problem that I believe is under-explored in the deep learning literature.

Pros:
- The few-shot learning results look good, though I am not an expert in this area.
- The use of a “double” variational bound in a hierarchical generative model is clearly presented and appears broadly applicable.

Questions:
- When training the statistic network, do you use minibatches (i.e., subsets of the examples)?
- If minibatches are used, do they provide an unbiased estimator of the full gradient you would obtain if you used all examples? For instance, suppose the statistic network needs to detect whether any example in a dataset exhibits a particular feature, which seems consistent with the graphical model on the right side of Figure 1. If the statistic network is trained on minibatches, it may fail to learn this characterization because a given minibatch could miss the relevant example. Using minibatches rather than all examples to train the statistic network may therefore limit the model’s expressive power.

Suggestions:
- Hierarchical forecasting (e.g., electricity or sales) could be an interesting and practical application for this type of model.
