- Proposes a VAE variant with dataset-level latent variables; well-motivated and advances richer graphical structures beyond traditional VAEs.
- Main contribution: bringing richer, dataset-level latent structure to the deep learning community.
- Terminology concern: avoid coining “statistician”/“statistic network”; use the precise term “approximate posterior” instead.
- Experiments are solid, but some language needs clarification (notably end of page 6).
- Clarify the one-shot generation pipeline in Figure 5: computing the approximate posterior over the context vector then generating from the forward model.
- Question (a): are data-point–specific z sampled from the generative model or taken from the approximate posterior?
- Recommendation (b): report quantitative log-probability metrics (e.g., log p(x|c) with c~q(c|D) and/or log p(x) on held-out data) to evaluate one-shot generation.
- Expectation: the model should show stronger log-probability performance than a vanilla VAE; there’s no reason not to include these results.
