- Introduces a hierarchical generative framework with a dataset-level context latent and per-example latents, yielding a nested (“double”) variational bound.  
- Uses an exchangeable statistic network to map sets to a posterior over dataset context, enabling unsupervised dataset-level summaries.  
- Presents the approach as a general extension of VAEs that captures within-dataset and across-dataset structure.  
- Evaluated on dataset clustering, few-shot transfer/generation, representative subset selection, and few-shot classification with strong qualitative and quantitative results.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Main reasons for acceptance: clear motivation for the hierarchical double-variational formulation and strong experimental support on few-shot and related tasks.  
- Problem significance: addresses relatively under-explored hierarchical modeling across datasets and links well to transfer learning via shared priors/parameters.  
- Technical soundness: nested variational bound and exchangeable statistic network are well-presented and consistent with VAE methodology.  
- Empirical support: experiments on synthetic 1-D, spatial MNIST, OMNIGLOT, and YouTube Faces align with target use cases and show competitive performance.  
- Clarify statistic network training protocol: specify whether minibatches subsample datasets, points within datasets, or both and how pooling is applied.  
- Discuss estimator properties: analyze whether within-dataset subsampling yields unbiased gradient estimators relative to using full sets and under which pooling operators.  
- Consider expressivity of pooling: discuss alternatives to mean pooling (max, log-sum-exp, learned attention) and implications for representing rare “existence” features.  
- Recommend ablations/sensitivity analyses: vary per-dataset sample size, pooling operator, and sample dropout to strengthen empirical claims.  
- Suggest potential practical application: hierarchical forecasting (e.g., electricity demand or retail sales) as a domain where dataset-level contexts could improve efficiency.  
- Questions for authors: detail exact batching scheme (tensor shapes, per-dataset subsampling, variability across experiments).  
- Questions for authors: if subsampling is used, is the gradient estimator unbiased and how do you mitigate missing rare examples with mean pooling (e.g., pooling choice, larger per-set samples, architectural fixes)?
