- Proposes a hierarchical VAE with a top-level context variable that summarizes a dataset for set-based prediction.  
- Demonstrates "learn-to-learn" capabilities: inferring distributions from only a few examples.  
- Conceptually simple, elegant method that appears to perform well in experiments.  
- Simpler and more grounded in unsupervised representation learning than many recent one-/few-shot approaches.  
- Paper is clearly written and pleasant to read.  
- Title is overly grandiose and the "statistician" analogy is misleading.  
- Experiments are well chosen and few-shot results are solid.  
- Spatial MNIST dataset is an interesting and potentially useful toy benchmark.  
- Concern: Figure 4 inputs look dense — model should perhaps recognize the distribution with fewer samples.  
- Minor nit: red points in Figure 4 do not appear to correspond to the claimed meaningful points.  
- Question: Will the authors release the code?
