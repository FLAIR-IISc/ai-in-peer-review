Apologies for the delayed review — I’ve been dealing with OpenReview technical issues that prevented me from posting.

This paper introduces a method for predicting from sets of data points. The approach is a hierarchical VAE in which the top level is an abstract context variable that summarizes a dataset. The experiments indicate that the model can “learn to learn,” acquiring the ability to infer distributions from only a few examples.

Overall, this work is a welcome addition to the one-/few-shot learning literature. The method is conceptually simple and elegant, and it appears to perform well. Compared to other recent one-shot approaches, it is simpler and grounded in unsupervised representation learning. The paper is clearly written and a pleasure to read.

The title is overly grandiose relative to the actual contributions; the method doesn’t seem closely related to a statistician, unless by that one means “someone who invents statistics.”

The experiments are well chosen, and the few-shot results seem quite solid given the simplicity of the approach.

The spatial MNIST dataset is interesting and could serve as a useful toy benchmark. The inputs in Figure 4 look rather dense; shouldn’t the method recognize the distribution with fewer samples? (Minor nit: the red points in Figure 4 don’t appear to correspond to meaningful points as claimed in the text.)

Will the authors release the code?
