Apologies for the late review—I encountered technical issues with OpenReview that prevented earlier submission.

This paper proposes a method for learning to make predictions from sets of data points. The approach is a hierarchical VAE in which the top layer is an abstract context unit that summarizes a dataset. The experiments indicate that the model can “learn to learn,” acquiring the ability to infer distributions from small numbers of examples.

Overall, this is a solid contribution to the one-/few-shot learning literature. The method is conceptually simple and elegant, and it appears to perform well. Relative to other recent one-shot methods, the approach is simpler and grounded in unsupervised representation learning. The paper is clearly written and a pleasure to read.

That said, the title feels overly grandiose relative to the scope of the work; the method does not seem to have much to do with a “statistician,” except perhaps in the loose sense of “someone who devises statistics.”

The experiments are well chosen, and the few-shot results look quite strong given the method’s simplicity. The Spatial MNIST dataset is interesting and could serve as a useful toy benchmark. However, the inputs in Figure 4 appear quite dense; shouldn’t the method recognize the distribution with fewer samples? As a minor nitpick, the red points in Figure 4 do not seem to correspond to meaningful points as claimed in the text.

Will the authors release the code?
