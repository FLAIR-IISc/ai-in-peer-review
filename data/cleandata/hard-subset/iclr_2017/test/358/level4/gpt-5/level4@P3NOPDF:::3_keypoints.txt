- Proposes a VAE variant with a dataset-level (context) latent and amortized inference over sets to capture global structure.
- Main contribution: demonstrating the benefit of structured, hierarchical latent-variable models over flat VAEs.
- Strength: clear, well-motivated idea highlighting dataset-level structure in deep generative models.
- Strength: intuitive description of modeling/inference over sets and visually improved sample quality and coherence.
- Strength: likely to stimulate further research on structured VAEs and set-level amortized inference.
- Concern: “statistician” / “statistic network” terminology is unnecessary and potentially misleading; use standard terms like “approximate posterior over the dataset-level latent” or “context inference network.”
- Request: explicitly specify the “one-shot generation” pipeline (compute q(c|S), sample c, then generate x) and provide pseudocode.
- Clarify whether per-datapoint latents z are sampled from the prior p(z|c) (pure generation) or from the inference network q(z|x,c) (not pure generation).
- State how many examples are used to form q(c|S), sensitivity to that number, and whether any observed x are reused during generation.
- Request quantitative evaluation of “one-shot” performance via conditional held-out log-probabilities/ELBOs and unconditional held-out log-probabilities, compared to a capacity-matched VAE and hierarchical baselines.
- If exact likelihoods are intractable, use IWAE or tighter bounds; report whether c is marginalized or approximated and how many samples are used at evaluation.
- Clarity suggestions: precisely define “one-shot generation” (k=1 vs k>1 vs unconditional), provide a clear graphical model, and describe training vs test-time inference (which networks, reparameterization, sampled vs deterministic steps).
- Recommend consistently using “context” for the dataset-level latent and situating the approach relative to other dataset summarization strategies (MLE point estimates, deep set encoders).
- Minor comments: report size of context-defining set S at test time, include ablations over |S|, show variability/standard errors across runs, and consider posterior calibration or posterior predictive checks.
- Overall: well-motivated and timely with compelling qualitative results, but needs standardized terminology, explicit one-shot procedure, and quantitative likelihood-based metrics to substantiate claims.
