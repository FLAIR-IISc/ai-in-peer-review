- Reviewer apologized for delayed submission due to OpenReview technical issues.
- Paper proposes a hierarchical VAE with a dataset-level latent “context” variable to summarize sets and condition per-element inference/generation.
- Experiments indicate meta-learning behavior: the model can infer underlying distributions from few examples (one- and few-shot learning).
- Contribution is solid: conceptually simple, elegant, performs well, and is simpler than many recent one-shot methods by relying on unsupervised representation learning.
- Paper is clearly written and enjoyable to read.
- Title is overly ambitious; the “statistician” framing is weak unless it just means learning useful summary statistics.
- Experiments are well chosen; spatial MNIST is an interesting, potentially useful toy benchmark.
- Figure 4 inputs look dense—model might be able to identify distributions with fewer samples; red points in Figure 4 may not match the claimed “meaningful” points.
- Asks whether the authors will release the code.
