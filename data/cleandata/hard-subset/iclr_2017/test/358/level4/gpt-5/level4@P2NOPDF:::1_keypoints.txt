- Introduces a hierarchical generative model with dataset-level and example-level latents and a “double” variational bound for unordered collections of datasets.  
- Hierarchical modeling is important, high-impact, and under-explored; this work is a valuable step forward.  
- Positive: few-shot learning results are encouraging (though reviewer not a specialist in that subfield).  
- Positive: double variational bound is clearly presented and appears broadly applicable.  
- Question: Do you train the statistic network using minibatches (subsets of examples from each dataset)?  
- Question: If minibatches are used, is the resulting gradient an unbiased estimator of the full-dataset gradient?  
- Concern: Minibatch training may prevent the statistic network from learning “any-instance” features if relevant examples are omitted from batches.  
- Concern: More generally, does minibatch training limit the statistic network’s expressive power versus using full datasets, and how do you mitigate this (e.g., aggregation schemes, importance weighting, batch construction)?  
- Suggestion: Apply the model to hierarchical forecasting tasks (e.g., electricity demand or retail sales across aggregation levels).
