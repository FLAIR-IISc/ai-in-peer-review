Apologies for the delayed review—technical issues with OpenReview prevented me from posting earlier.

This paper introduces a method for learning to make predictions from sets of data points. The approach is a hierarchical VAE whose top layer contains an abstract context unit that summarizes an entire dataset. The experiments indicate that the model effectively “learns to learn,” acquiring the ability to infer distributions from small numbers of examples.

Overall, this is a solid contribution to the one-/few-shot learning literature. The method is conceptually simple and elegant, and it appears to perform well. Compared to other recent one-shot learning approaches, it is simpler and grounded in unsupervised representation learning. The paper is clearly written and enjoyable to read.

The title feels overly grand given the scope of the work; the method does not seem closely related to what a statistician actually does, unless by “statistician” one means merely “someone who invents statistics.”

The experiments are well chosen, and the few-shot results look quite strong given the simplicity of the approach.

The spatial MNIST dataset is interesting and could serve as a useful toy benchmark. However, the inputs in Figure 4 seem fairly dense; shouldn’t the method be able to recognize the underlying distribution with fewer samples? A minor point: the red points in Figure 4 do not appear to correspond to the meaningful points as claimed in the text.

Will the authors release the code?
