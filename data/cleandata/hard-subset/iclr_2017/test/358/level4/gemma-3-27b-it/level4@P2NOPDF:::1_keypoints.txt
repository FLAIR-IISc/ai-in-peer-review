- Introduces a hierarchical generative model with a “double” variational bound: higher-level latents for datasets and lower-level latents for individual examples.  
- Hierarchical modeling for collections of datasets is an important, underexplored challenge; this work is a valuable contribution.  
- Reported few-shot learning results are promising.  
- The “double” variational bound is well-motivated, clearly presented, and conceptually sound.  
- Unclear whether the statistic network is trained using minibatches.  
- If minibatches are used, it must be clarified whether they give an unbiased estimate of the full gradient.  
- Concern that minibatch training may prevent learning dataset-level features that depend on presence across examples, limiting expressiveness.  
- Authors should justify the minibatch approach or explore alternatives/mitigations.  
- Suggest evaluating the model on hierarchical forecasting tasks (e.g., electricity demand or sales) as a practical application.
