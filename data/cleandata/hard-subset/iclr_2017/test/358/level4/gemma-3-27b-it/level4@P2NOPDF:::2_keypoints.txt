- Introduces a novel method for predicting distributions from sets using a hierarchical VAE with an abstract context vector summarizing the dataset.  
- Demonstrates effective "learn to learn" behavior and strong few-shot generalization from very small samples.  
- Method is conceptually simple, elegant, and relies on unsupervised representation learning, performing competitively with recent approaches.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Title seems overly ambitious and the claimed connection to statistical learning is tenuous.  
- Experimental setup is well-considered and the few-shot results are promising given the method's simplicity.  
- The new spatially-transformed MNIST dataset is a useful contribution and potential benchmark.  
- Figure 4 appears to use a higher sample density than necessary to represent the learned distribution.  
- Red points in Figure 4 are not clearly matched to the “meaningful points” described in the text.  
- Authors should release their code to benefit the research community.
