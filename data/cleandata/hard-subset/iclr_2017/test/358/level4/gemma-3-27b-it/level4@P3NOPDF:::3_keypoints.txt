- Introduces dataset-level latent-variable VAE that models an entire dataset with a single latent vector; motivation addresses instance-level VAE limitations.  
- Moves beyond standard VAE graphical models, expanding architectural possibilities; potentially significant contribution.  
- Presentation and experimental evaluation need improvement.  
- Terminology critique: calling the network a “statistician” (or “statistic network”) is misleading and restrictive; prefer the standard term “approximate posterior.”  
- One-shot generation explanation (end of page 6 / Fig. 5) is unclear and requires clarification.  
- Clarification requested (a): are data-point latents z generated by the forward model conditioned on context c, or sampled directly from q(c|X)?  
- Recommend quantitative evaluation (b): report log p(x|c) with c from the approximate posterior or log p(x) on held-out data instead of relying solely on visual samples.  
- Expectation that log-probability metrics will show substantial improvement over a standard VAE; include these results to support generative claims.  
- Overall: interesting and promising work; addressing terminology and adding rigorous log-probability evaluations will substantially strengthen the paper.
