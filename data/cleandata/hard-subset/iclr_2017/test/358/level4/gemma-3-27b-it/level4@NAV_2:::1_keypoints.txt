- Proposes a hierarchical generative model with a double variational bound: higher-level latents for datasets and lower-level latents for individual examples.
- Highlights hierarchical modeling as an important, under-explored problem in deep learning.
- Reviewer recommends rejection due to concerns about training methodology and unclear experimental design.
- Major concern: training the statistic network on minibatches may bias learned dataset summaries and limit expressive capacity.
- Paper does not fully address whether minibatch training prevents learning globally representative dataset features.
- Lack of analysis on how batch size affects performance and robustness; results may depend on batch-size choices.
- Experimental results are promising but their validity depends on properly training the statistic network.
- Request to explicitly clarify whether full datasets or minibatches are used for statistic network training and to justify the choice.
- Ask for theoretical justification for using the sample mean as the pooling operation and discussion of its limitations.
- Recommend exploring and reporting alternative pooling strategies and their impact on performance.
- Recommend ablation studies to quantify contributions of components like skip connections and multiple stochastic layers.
- Suggest demonstrating practical applications (e.g., hierarchical forecasting for electricity or sales) to show utility.
- Algorithm 4 is unclear: need definition of q(C|x;φ) and precise computation of the KL divergence.
- Ask how the model handles datasets of varying sizes, whether performance degrades, and possible mitigation strategies.
