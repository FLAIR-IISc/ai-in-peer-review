- Introduces a VAE extension that models dataset-level latent variables to learn dataset representations rather than individual datapoints.  
- Clear motivation and articulation for learning summary statistics that encapsulate per-dataset generative models.  
- Novel contribution in extending VAE structures to more complex graphical models, enriching deep learning tools.  
- Recommendation: Reject due to insufficient quantitative rigor and weak theoretical justification for some terminology.  
- Primary critique: experimental evaluation lacks quantitative assessment of generative quality (no log-probabilities or standard metrics reported).  
- Visual samples are appealing but insufficient without quantitative measures to support generative claims.  
- Terminology (“neural statistician” / “statistic network”) is potentially misleading and not well-justified statistically.  
- The model learns an approximate posterior over summary statistics, which is not the only or necessarily most principled summarization approach.  
- Limited quantitative results: OMNIGLOT few-shot gains are marginal; YouTube Faces results lack quantitative analysis.  
- Missing comparisons to simpler baselines (e.g., per-dataset VAE, simple feature aggregation), making benefits unclear.  
- Generation process needs clarification, specifically the role and origin of data-point latent variables z.  
- Requested improvements: strengthen theoretical justification for learning an approximate posterior over context vectors.  
- Requested improvements: report quantitative generative metrics (log-likelihood, FID, etc.) for OMNIGLOT and YouTube Faces.  
- Requested improvements: include baseline comparisons and reconsider misleading terminology.  
- Specific reviewer questions to address: (a) are z in Figure 5 from p(x|z,c) or q(z|x,c)? (b) why were log-likelihood metrics omitted and what are the computational trade-offs?  
- Suggested addition: discuss limitations such as hyperparameter sensitivity and computational cost.
