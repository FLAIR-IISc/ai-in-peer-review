- Decision: Reject; encourage resubmission after addressing training-procedure concerns affecting representational capacity.
- Contribution: Proposes a hierarchical generative “neural statistician” with dataset-level and example-level latents via a double variational bound.
- Strength: Conceptually interesting and addresses an underexplored hierarchical modeling problem; shows promising few-shot learning results.
- Major concern: Training the statistic network on minibatches of datasets (subsamples) is unjustified and may prevent learning features that depend on entire datasets.
- Inconsistency: Minibatch training undermines the desired global summary and conflicts with the graphical model’s implication of a global dataset statistic.
- Exchangeability issue: The minibatch approach may violate exchangeability and produce biased or incomplete summary statistics.
- Missing justification: No theoretical or empirical analysis is provided to validate the minibatch approximation or its consequences.
- Interpretability: Without resolving the minibatch issue, observed performance gains are difficult to interpret confidently.
- Related work: Coverage is good but should compare more directly to set-summarization approaches (e.g., set-to-sequence models).
- Ablation needs: Additional studies required to quantify effects of architectural choices (skip connections, number of stochastic layers).
- Broader applicability: Recommend exploring other applications (e.g., hierarchical forecasting) to demonstrate generality.
- Notation/math: Generally clear but some equations (notably the variational lower bound, Eq.4) need more explanation and assumptions clarified.
- Question — minibatch rationale: Why use minibatches of datasets rather than full datasets; theoretical justification for unbiased/representative gradients?
- Question — minibatch size: How does model performance change with different numbers of datasets per minibatch; effect of larger batches?
- Question — pooling methods: Have alternative pooling strategies (max-pooling, learned attention) been explored for robustness?
- Question — dataset-size dependence: How does performance scale with per-dataset size; is there a minimum dataset size needed?
- Question — computational cost: What are training and inference costs compared to related approaches, especially ones not using minibatch statistic training?
