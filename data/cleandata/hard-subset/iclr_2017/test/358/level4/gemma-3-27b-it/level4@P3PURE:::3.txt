The authors introduce a variant of the variational autoencoder (VAE) that models dataset-level latent variables. The idea is clearly motivated and well described. In my opinion, the greatest contribution of this paper is the movement beyond the relatively simple graphical model structure of traditional VAEs and the introduction of more interesting structures to the deep learning community.

Comments:

- The justification for the term “statistician” is unclear. Learning an approximate posterior over summary statistics is not the only conceivable method for summarizing a dataset with a neural network; a maximum likelihood approach, for instance, could also be considered. Generally, the paper would benefit from avoiding the coinage of new terms like “statistic network” and instead relying on the more precise “approximate posterior.”
- The experiments are well-executed, and I appreciate the response to my question regarding “one-shot generation.” However, the language surrounding this topic requires clarification, particularly at the end of page 6. My understanding of Figure 5 is as follows: given an input set, compute the approximate posterior over the context vector, then generate from the forward model using samples drawn from the approximate posterior. I would like clarification on the following:
(a) Are the data point-dependent vectors *z* generated from the forward model or sampled directly from the approximate posterior?
(b) While the samples are visually of high quality, this is a qualitative assessment. A key advantage of VAEs over GANs lies in the availability of natural methods for computing log-probabilities. Therefore, a more rigorous evaluation of “one-shot generation” performance would be to report log *p(x | c)* (where *c* is sampled from the approximate posterior) or log *p(x)* for held-out datasets. I suspect the log probability performance of these networks, relative to a vanilla VAE without the context latent variable, will be compelling. I still see no compelling reason not to include this analysis.
