The authors present a novel extension to the variational autoencoder (VAE) framework, incorporating latent variables that operate at the dataset level. The motivation for this work is sound and the approach is well-articulated. The primary strength of this paper lies in its departure from the standard, relatively simple graphical model structure of conventional VAEs, and the introduction of a more sophisticated architecture to the deep learning field.

However, the terminology used could be improved. The designation of this component as a "statistician" feels unnecessarily restrictive; learning an approximate posterior over summary statistics represents just one potential method for dataset summarization via neural networks, and alternatives like a maximum likelihood estimation could also be considered. The paper would benefit from greater clarity and a reliance on established terms like “approximate posterior” rather than introducing novel phrasing like “statistic network.”

The experimental results are encouraging, and I appreciate the authors’ response to my previous inquiry regarding “one-shot generation.” Nevertheless, the description of this process requires further refinement, particularly at the end of page 6. My interpretation of Figure 5 is as follows: given an input dataset, the approximate posterior over the context vector is computed, and then samples are drawn from this posterior to generate data via the forward model. To ensure complete understanding, I request clarification on the following points:

(a) Are the data point-specific latent vectors, *z*, generated by the forward model, or are they directly sampled from the approximate posterior?

(b) While the generated samples appear visually compelling, this observation is subjective. A key advantage of VAEs over generative adversarial networks (GANs) is the availability of natural log-probability calculations. Therefore, a rigorous evaluation of the “one-shot generation” capability would involve reporting metrics such as log p(x | c) – where *c* is sampled from the approximate posterior – or log p(x) for held-out datasets. I anticipate that the log-probability performance of this model, compared to a standard VAE without the context latent variable, would be substantial. I strongly recommend including such quantitative analysis.
