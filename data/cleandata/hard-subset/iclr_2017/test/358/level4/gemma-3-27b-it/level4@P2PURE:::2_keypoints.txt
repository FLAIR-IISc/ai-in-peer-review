- Introduces a hierarchical VAE for set prediction with an abstract context unit that summarizes an entire dataset.  
- Demonstrates the model’s "learn to learn" ability to rapidly infer distributions from limited data (few-shot learning).  
- Represents a valuable, conceptually straightforward and elegant contribution to one-/few-shot learning with strong empirical performance.  
- Approach is comparatively simpler than recent one-shot methods and grounded in unsupervised representation learning.  
- Paper is well-written and enjoyable to read.  
- Title seems overly ambitious; the link to statistical learning beyond developing statistical techniques is unclear.  
- Experimental design is appropriate and the few-shot results are robust given the method’s simplicity.  
- Using spatial MNIST is interesting and may serve as a useful benchmark.  
- Figure 4 visualizations use relatively dense sampling; the method should be shown to recognize distributions with fewer samples, and the red points in Fig. 4 appear misaligned with described data points.  
- Asks whether the authors will make their code publicly available.
