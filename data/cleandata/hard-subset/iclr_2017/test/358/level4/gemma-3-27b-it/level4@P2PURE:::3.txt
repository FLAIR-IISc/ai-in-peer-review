The authors present a novel variational autoencoder (VAE) variant that incorporates dataset-level latent variables. The motivation for this work is clear and the approach is well explained. The primary contribution of this paper, in my view, lies in its departure from the comparatively simple graphical model structure of traditional VAEs and its introduction of more complex structures to the deep learning community.

Comments:
- The justification for the term "statistician" is unclear. Learning an approximate posterior over summary statistics represents only one possible method for dataset summarization using neural networks; alternative approaches, such as maximum likelihood estimation, are conceivable. Generally, the paper would benefit from greater clarity and should avoid introducing new terminology like "statistic network," instead consistently using the more precise term "approximate posterior."
- The experiments are well-executed, and I appreciate the response to my question regarding “one-shot generation.” However, the associated language requires further clarification, particularly at the end of page 6. My understanding of Figure 5 is as follows: given an input set, the approximate posterior over the context vector is computed, and then samples from this posterior are used to generate data via the forward model. I request clarification on the following points: 
(a) Are the data point-dependent vectors *z* generated by the forward model, or are they sampled directly from the approximate posterior?
(b) While the generated samples appear visually appealing, this observation lacks quantitative support. A key benefit of VAEs over GANs is the availability of natural metrics for evaluating generative performance through log-probabilities. Therefore, a rigorous evaluation of “one-shot generation” performance would involve reporting log *p(x | c)* (where *c* is sampled from the approximate posterior) or log *p(x)* for held-out datasets. I anticipate that the log-probability performance of these networks, relative to a standard VAE lacking the context latent variable, will be strong. I still believe including this type of evaluation is crucial.
