- Decision: Reject.

- Proposal: Hierarchical generative model with dataset-level and example-level latents using a “double” variational bound to learn unsupervised dataset summary statistics.

- Potential: Interesting idea with promising results on Spatial MNIST and Omniglot.

- Main verdict: Experimental validation and clarity are insufficient for acceptance.

- Experimental detail lacking: Architectures, hyperparameter tuning, and baseline comparisons are not adequately described.

- Comparative analysis weak: Table 1 and few-shot comparisons are brief and don’t contextualize performance against established baselines.

- YouTube Faces experiment under-detailed: Minimal description and no rigorous evaluation of generated sample quality.

- Minibatch training concern: Training the statistic network on minibatches may prevent capturing true dataset-level statistics and biases representations.

- Missing justification for minibatches: The paper does not explain or analyze why minibatching is acceptable despite potential drawbacks.

- Clarity issues: Some model components (e.g., skip-connections) and transitions between equations and explanations are described abstractly and are hard to follow.

- Theoretical gaps: Lacks rigorous analysis of when learned statistics are meaningful and what information the context variable captures.

- Pooling choices limited: Only sample mean pooling considered; alternative pooling methods should be explored.

- Computational complexity omitted: No discussion of scaling or costs with dataset size.

- Error analysis absent: No detailed error analysis of few-shot experiments to reveal limitations.

- Future application suggestion: Hierarchical forecasting proposed as an interesting direction.

- Key questions for authors: Impact of minibatch size, justification for minibatches, challenges of training on whole datasets, comparison to SOTA including computational cost, and details of hyperparameter tuning.
