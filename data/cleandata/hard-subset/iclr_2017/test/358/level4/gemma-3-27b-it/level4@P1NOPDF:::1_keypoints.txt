- Proposes a hierarchical generative model with dataset-level and example-level latents using a “double” variational bound.  
- Highlights hierarchical modeling as an important, under-explored problem in deep learning.  
- Few-shot learning results appear promising.  
- The double variational bound concept is well-presented and seems broadly applicable.  
- Question: Are minibatches used to train the statistic network (i.e., subsets of examples)?  
- Question: If minibatches are used, do they provide an unbiased estimator of the full gradient, and might minibatching prevent learning dataset-level features that depend on any single example’s presence?  
- Suggestion: Evaluate on hierarchical forecasting tasks (e.g., electricity or sales prediction).
