- Proposes a hierarchical generative model: lower level models points within datasets, higher level models collections of datasets as unordered sets, using a “double” variational bound with dataset- and point-level latents.

- Notes hierarchical modeling is important and relatively underexplored in deep learning.

- Strength: Few-shot learning performance appears promising.

- Strength: The “double” variational bound is clearly explained and likely broadly applicable.

- Question: Is the statistic network trained using minibatches or full datasets?

- Concern: If minibatches are used, does that provide an unbiased estimator of the full gradient, and might minibatch training prevent capturing dataset-level features (limiting expressiveness)?

- Suggestion: Evaluate on hierarchical forecasting tasks (e.g., electricity demand, sales) to show practical utility.
