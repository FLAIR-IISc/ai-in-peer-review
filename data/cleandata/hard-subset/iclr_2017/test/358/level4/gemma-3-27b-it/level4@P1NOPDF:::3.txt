The authors introduce a variant of the variational autoencoder (VAE) that models dataset-level latent variables. The idea is clearly motivated and well described. In my opinion, the greatest contribution of this paper is its move beyond the relatively simple graphical model structure of traditional VAEs and the introduction of more complex structures to the deep learning community.

Comments:

- The term "statistician" is not entirely clear to me. Learning an approximate posterior over summary statistics is only one possible way to summarize a dataset using a neural network; a maximum likelihood approach, for instance, could also be considered. More generally, the paper would benefit from avoiding coined terms like “statistic network” and instead consistently using the more precise “approximate posterior.”

- The experiments are well-executed, and I appreciate the response to my question regarding “one-shot generation.” However, the language surrounding this still needs clarification, particularly at the end of page 6. My understanding of Figure 5 is as follows: given an input set, the approximate posterior over the context vector is computed, and then data is generated from the forward model using samples drawn from this posterior. I would appreciate clarification on the following points:
(a) Are the data point-dependent vectors *z* generated by the forward model, or are they sampled directly from the approximate posterior?
(b) While the generated samples are of high quality, this is a qualitative assessment. A key advantage of VAEs over GANs is the availability of natural methods for computing log-probabilities. Therefore, a more rigorous evaluation of “one-shot generation” performance would be to report log *p(x | c)* (where *c* is sampled from the approximate posterior) or log *p(x)* for held-out datasets. I suspect the log-probability performance of these networks, compared to a vanilla VAE without the context latent variable, would be compelling. I still see no compelling reason why these metrics were not included.
