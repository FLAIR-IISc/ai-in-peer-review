This paper proposes a hierarchical generative model with points within datasets at the lower level and unordered sets of datasets modeled at the higher level. The core idea is to employ a “double” variational bound, where a higher-level latent variable describes datasets and a lower-level latent variable describes individual examples.

Hierarchical modeling is an important and potentially high-impact problem, and I believe it is currently under-explored within the deep learning literature.

**Pros:**

*   The few-shot learning results appear promising, though I am not an expert in this specific area.
*   The concept of utilizing a “double” variational bound in a hierarchical generative model is well-presented and seems broadly applicable.

**Questions:**

*   During training of the statistic network, are minibatches used (i.e., subsets of the examples)?
*   If not, does using minibatches provide an unbiased estimator of the full gradient (as if all examples were used)? For example, consider a scenario where the statistic network aims to identify a dataset characteristic based on the presence of a particular feature in *any* example within the dataset. If the statistic network is trained on minibatches, it may be unable to learn this type of characterization, as a single minibatch will inevitably miss some examples. Using minibatches – as opposed to the entire dataset – to train the statistic network appears to potentially limit the model’s expressive power.

**Suggestions:**

*   Hierarchical forecasting tasks (e.g., electricity or sales prediction) could represent an interesting and practical application of this model.
