This paper introduces a novel approach to learning from limited data, specifically focusing on predicting distributions from sets of data points. The proposed method builds upon the Variational Autoencoder (VAE) framework with a hierarchical structure, incorporating an abstract context vector at the top level to summarize the input dataset. Experimental results demonstrate the model’s ability to effectively “learn to learn,” exhibiting strong performance in few-shot learning scenarios where it can generalize from very small sample sizes.

Overall, this work represents a valuable contribution to the growing field of one- and few-shot learning. The method is both conceptually straightforward and elegant, and the reported results suggest it performs competitively. Compared to other recent approaches in this area, the presented technique distinguishes itself through its relative simplicity and reliance on unsupervised representation learning. The paper is well-written and a pleasure to read. 

However, the title feels somewhat ambitious given the scope of the work. The connection to statistical learning, beyond the natural application of statistical principles, appears tenuous. 

The experimental setup is well-considered, and the few-shot learning performance is promising, particularly considering the method's simplicity. The introduction of the spatially-transformed MNIST dataset is a positive aspect, offering a potentially useful benchmark for future research. Regarding Figure 4, the density of samples used to represent the distribution appears higher than necessary; one might expect the method to accurately capture the distribution with fewer points. Furthermore, a minor point: the red data points highlighted in Figure 4 do not clearly correspond to the “meaningful points” referenced in the accompanying text.

Finally, it would be beneficial for the research community if the authors were to release their code.
