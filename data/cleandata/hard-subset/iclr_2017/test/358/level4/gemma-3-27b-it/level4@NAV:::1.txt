This paper introduces a hierarchical generative model with a novel structure: the lower level models points within individual datasets, while the higher level models collections of datasets as unordered sets. The core concept involves a “double” variational bound, employing a higher-level latent variable to represent datasets and a lower-level latent variable to describe individual data points.

Hierarchical modeling represents a significant and potentially impactful area, currently receiving comparatively limited attention within the Deep Learning community.

**Strengths:**
  -The few-shot learning performance appears promising, though my expertise in this specific domain is limited.
  -The proposed “double” variational bound within this hierarchical generative framework is clearly articulated and appears broadly applicable.

**Questions:**
  -During training of the statistic network, is the process conducted using minibatches (subsets of the examples)?
  -If minibatches *are* used, does this approach yield an unbiased estimator of the full gradient, as would be obtained if all examples were utilized? Specifically, consider a scenario where the statistic network aims to identify datasets containing a particular feature, as potentially reflected in the graphical model shown in Figure 1. Minibatch training might prevent the network from capturing such a dataset-level characterization, given the incomplete representation of the entire dataset within each batch. Consequently, employing minibatches instead of the full dataset for statistic network training could potentially constrain the model’s expressive capacity.
