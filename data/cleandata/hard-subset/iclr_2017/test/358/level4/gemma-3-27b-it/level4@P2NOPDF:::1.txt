This paper introduces a hierarchical generative model with a novel structure: the lower level models individual data points within datasets, while the higher level models unordered collections of these datasets. The core concept involves a “double” variational bound, employing higher-level latent variables to represent datasets and lower-level latent variables to represent individual examples within them.

Hierarchical modeling represents a significant and impactful challenge, and its exploration within the deep learning literature appears currently limited. This work offers a valuable contribution in this direction.

**Strengths:**

*   The reported few-shot learning results are promising, though my expertise in this specific area is limited.
*   The application of a “double” variational bound within a hierarchical generative model is a well-articulated and potentially broadly applicable idea. The presentation is clear and the approach appears conceptually sound.

**Questions/Concerns:**

*   Regarding the training of the statistic network, could the authors clarify whether minibatches are utilized?
*   If minibatches *are* used, the authors should address whether this provides an unbiased estimate of the full gradient (as would be obtained by using the entire dataset). Specifically, I am concerned about scenarios where the statistic network aims to identify dataset-level characteristics based on the presence of a feature in *any* example within the dataset (as suggested by the graphical model depicted on the right side of Figure 1). Minibatch training may prevent the network from learning such characteristics, as any given minibatch may not contain all relevant examples. Consequently, using minibatches instead of the complete dataset for training the statistic network could restrict the model’s expressive power and its ability to capture subtle, dataset-wide properties. Further justification for the minibatch approach, or exploration of alternatives, would be beneficial. 

**Suggestions:**

*   The authors might consider investigating hierarchical forecasting tasks – such as those arising in electricity demand or sales prediction – as a compelling and practical application of this model. These domains naturally lend themselves to hierarchical structure and could highlight the benefits of the proposed approach.
