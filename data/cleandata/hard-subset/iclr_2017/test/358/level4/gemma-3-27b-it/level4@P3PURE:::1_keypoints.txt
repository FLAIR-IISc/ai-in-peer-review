- Proposes a hierarchical generative model: lower level models points, higher level models unordered sets of datasets.  
- Core idea is a “double” variational bound using a higher-level latent for datasets and a lower-level latent for examples.  
- Hierarchical modeling is important and relatively under-explored in deep learning.  
- Pros: Few-shot learning results look promising (reviewer has limited domain expertise).  
- Pros: The double variational bound concept is clearly presented and seems broadly applicable.  
- Question: Are minibatches used when training the statistic network (i.e., subsets of examples)?  
- Question/concern: If minibatches are used, do they provide an unbiased estimator of the full gradient—minibatching may prevent learning dataset-level features that require seeing all examples (e.g., “any example has feature X”).  
- Suggestion: Apply the model to hierarchical forecasting tasks (e.g., electricity or sales prediction) to demonstrate practical impact.
