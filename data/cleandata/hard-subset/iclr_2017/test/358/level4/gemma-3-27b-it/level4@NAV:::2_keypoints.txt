- Reviewer apologized for delayed submission due to OpenReview technical difficulties.
- Paper proposes a hierarchical Variational Autoencoder with an abstract top-level context unit to summarize input datasets.
- Method demonstrates “learning to learn,” effectively modeling distributions from limited data (few-shot).
- Work is a valuable contribution to one- and few-shot learning, being conceptually straightforward and elegant.
- Empirical results appear strong, especially given the method’s simplicity.
- Compared to recent related work, the approach is simpler and leverages unsupervised representation learning.
- Manuscript is well-written and engaging.
- The paper title seems overstated and the connection to statistics is tenuous.
- Experimental design is well-considered and few-shot learning experiments are compelling.
- Use of spatial MNIST is insightful and could serve as a useful benchmark.
- Figure 4 shows an unnecessarily high sample density and red points may not align with described meaningful data points (minor issue).
- Reviewer strongly encourages public release of the code.
