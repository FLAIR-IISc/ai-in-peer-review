This paper proposes a hierarchical generative model with a novel structure: the lower level models points within datasets, while the higher level models unordered sets of datasets. The core idea is to employ a "double" variational bound, leveraging a higher-level latent variable to describe datasets and a lower-level latent variable to describe individual examples. Hierarchical modeling is an important and potentially high-impact area, and appears to be relatively under-explored within the deep learning literature.

**Pros:**
  -The few-shot learning results appear promising, though I lack specialized expertise in this domain.
  -The concept of utilizing a “double” variational bound within a hierarchical generative model is clearly presented and seems broadly applicable.

**Questions:**
  -During training of the statistic network, are minibatches used (i.e., subsets of the examples)?
  -If not, does using minibatches provide an unbiased estimator of the full gradient (as would be obtained using all examples)? Specifically, consider a scenario where the statistic network aims to identify datasets containing *any* example with a particular feature, treating this as a dataset characterization – a situation that aligns with the graphical model depicted on the right side of Figure 1. Minibatch training could hinder learning such a characterization, as a given minibatch may not include all relevant examples from the dataset. Consequently, relying on minibatches (instead of the complete dataset) for statistic network training may limit the model’s expressive power.

**Suggestions:**
  -Exploring hierarchical forecasting applications, such as in electricity or sales prediction, could demonstrate a compelling and practical use case for this type of model.
