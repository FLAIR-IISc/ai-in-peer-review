This paper introduces a novel extension to the variational autoencoder (VAE) framework by incorporating dataset-level latent variables. The motivation for this approach is clear and the concept is well-explained. A significant contribution of this work lies in its departure from the standard, relatively simple graphical model structure of traditional VAEs, and the exploration of more complex and potentially fruitful architectures for deep learning models.

However, I have some concerns regarding the terminology and experimental evaluation. The designation of the network learning summary statistics as a “statistician” feels unnecessarily restrictive. While the authors employ an approximate posterior to summarize a dataset, this is not the sole possible approach using neural networks; maximum likelihood estimation, for instance, represents a viable alternative. The paper would benefit from greater clarity by avoiding the introduction of new terms like “statistic network” and consistently using the more precise “approximate posterior” throughout.

The experimental results are encouraging, and I appreciate the authors’ response to my prior question concerning “one-shot generation.” Nevertheless, the description of this process requires further clarification, particularly on page 6. As I interpret Figure 5, the method involves computing an approximate posterior over a context vector from an input set, then generating data using the forward model conditioned on samples drawn from this posterior. I request clarification on the following points:

(a) Are the data point-dependent latent vectors *z* generated by the forward model, or are they directly sampled from the approximate posterior?

(b) While the generated samples appear visually compelling, this observation lacks quantitative support. A key strength of VAEs, differentiating them from generative adversarial networks (GANs), is the inherent ability to compute log-probabilities. Therefore, a more rigorous evaluation of “one-shot generation” performance would involve reporting metrics such as log *p(x | c)* (where *c* is sampled from the approximate posterior) or log *p(x)* for held-out datasets. I anticipate that the log-probability performance of this model, in comparison to a standard VAE without the dataset-level context latent variable, would be substantial. The inclusion of these standard VAE evaluation metrics seems crucial and I do not see a strong justification for their omission.
