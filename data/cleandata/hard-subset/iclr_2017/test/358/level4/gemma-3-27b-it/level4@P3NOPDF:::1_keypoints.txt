- Introduces a hierarchical generative model with a “double” variational bound that models distributions over datasets (high-level) and examples (low-level).  
- Core innovation: higher-level latent variable for datasets plus lower-level latent for individual examples, clearly illustrated by Figure 1.  
- Promising experimental results in few-shot learning, indicating the model’s potential.  
- Well-presented and broadly applicable framework with clear exposition and graphical model.  
- Framework’s focus on learning distributions over datasets suggests strong potential for generalization to new datasets.  
- Major concern: training of the statistic network—unclear whether mini-batches or full datasets are used, and whether mini-batching yields an unbiased gradient estimator.  
- Risk that mini-batch training may miss rare but dataset-defining examples, limiting the statistic network’s ability to produce true whole-dataset representations.  
- Request authors to clarify design choice and compare full-dataset versus mini-batch training impacts.  
- Suggest exploring additional practical applications (e.g., hierarchical forecasting for electricity demand or sales) to demonstrate broader utility.  
- Recommend a detailed discussion of computational complexity for training and inference, and comparisons with other hierarchical models.  
- Advise ablation studies to isolate the contributions of the double variational bound and the statistic network architecture.  
- Overall: novel and potentially impactful work that warrants acceptance if concerns about statistic network training and broader validation are addressed.
