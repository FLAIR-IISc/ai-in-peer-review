- Reviewer apologized for delayed review due to OpenReview technical difficulties.
- Paper proposes a method for predicting from sets using a hierarchical VAE architecture.
- Main innovation is a top-level abstract context unit that summarizes an entire dataset.
- Experiments show the model can “learn to learn,” inferring distributions from few examples.
- Work is a valuable contribution to one-/few-shot learning: conceptually simple, elegant, and effective.
- Method stands out for its simplicity and use of unsupervised representation learning compared to other one-shot approaches.
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- The paper title feels overly ambitious; links to broader statistical principles are tenuous unless interpreted very broadly.
- Experiments are thoughtfully designed and few-shot results are encouraging; spatial MNIST benchmark is promising.
- Figure 4 concerns: input samples appear dense (expectation of fewer points), and red points don’t align with “meaningful points” referenced in text.
- Reviewer asks whether the authors will release their code for reproducibility and further research.
