The authors present a novel variational autoencoder (VAE) variant incorporating dataset-level latent variables. The motivation for this work is sound and the approach is well-explained. The primary strength of this paper lies in its departure from the standard, relatively simple graphical model structure of traditional VAEs, and its introduction of a more complex and potentially impactful framework for the deep learning community.

However, the terminology used could be improved. The designation of the network as a “statistician” feels unnecessarily restrictive; learning an approximate posterior over summary statistics is only one possible method for dataset summarization with neural networks, and alternatives like maximum likelihood estimation exist. The paper would benefit from greater clarity by avoiding the introduction of new terms like “statistic network” and instead consistently using the more precise term “approximate posterior.”

The experimental results are promising, and I appreciate the authors’ response to my previous question concerning “one-shot generation.” However, the description of this process requires further clarification, particularly at the end of page 6. My current understanding of Figure 5 is that, given an input set, the approximate posterior over the context vector is computed, and then samples from this posterior are used to generate data via the forward model. To ensure complete understanding, I request clarification on the following points:
(a) Are the data point-dependent vectors *z* generated by the forward model, or are they directly sampled from the approximate posterior?
(b) While the generated samples appear visually compelling, this observation is subjective. VAEs offer a key advantage over GANs in their ability to naturally compute log-probabilities. Therefore, a rigorous evaluation of “one-shot generation” performance would involve reporting metrics such as log *p(x | c)* (where *c* is sampled from the approximate posterior) or log *p(x)* for held-out datasets. I anticipate that the log-probability performance of this model, compared to a standard VAE lacking the context latent variable, would be substantial. The inclusion of such a quantitative analysis would significantly strengthen the paper.
