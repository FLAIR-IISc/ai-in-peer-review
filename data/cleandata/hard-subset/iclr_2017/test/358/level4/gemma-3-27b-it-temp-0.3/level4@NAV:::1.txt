This paper introduces a hierarchical generative model with a novel structure: a lower level representing points within individual datasets, and a higher level modeling collections of these datasets as unordered sets. The core concept involves a “double” variational bound, utilizing higher-level latent variables to characterize datasets and lower-level latent variables to represent individual data instances. 

Hierarchical modeling represents a significant and impactful challenge, and this work addresses a relatively under-investigated area within the deep learning field.

**Strengths:**
  -The reported few-shot learning performance appears promising, although my expertise in this specific area is limited.
  -The application of a “double” variational bound within this hierarchical generative model is clearly articulated and demonstrates broad potential applicability.

**Questions:**
  -During the training of the statistic network, is the process conducted using minibatches of examples?
  -If minibatches *are* employed, does this approach yield an unbiased estimate of the full gradient (as would be obtained using the complete dataset)? Specifically, consider a scenario where the statistic network aims to identify a characteristic present in *any* example within a dataset. As illustrated by the graphical model in Figure 1, this requires considering the entire dataset. Training on minibatches might preclude learning such characteristics, as individual minibatches may not contain all relevant examples. Consequently, the use of minibatches, rather than the full dataset, could potentially restrict the model’s representational capacity.

**Suggestions:**
  -Exploring hierarchical forecasting tasks, such as those found in electricity demand or sales prediction, could provide a compelling and practical demonstration of this model’s capabilities.
