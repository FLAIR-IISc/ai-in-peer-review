- Summary: Proposes the Neural Statistician, a VAE-based model that learns dataset-level context vectors for generative modeling and few-shot tasks.  
- Decision: Reject — interesting idea and results but presentation, rigor, and analysis are insufficient.  
- Lack of rigorous comparison: Experiments lack consistent, strong baselines and standardized splits; some results (e.g., Omniglot, YouTube Faces) are not fairly compared.  
- Limited theoretical depth: Paper does not formally justify advantages like parameter efficiency or scalability versus traditional parameter-estimation approaches.  
- Clarity and motivation issues: Architectural choices (multiple stochastic layers, skip connections) are under-motivated and the term “statistic” is used without strong ties to classical estimators.  
- Reproducibility concerns: Use of different frameworks (Theano/TensorFlow) and incomplete implementation details make replication and fair comparison harder.  
- Strengthen baselines: Run rigorous, standardized comparisons against established methods with matched splits and hyperparameters.  
- Theoretical analysis needed: Provide formal analysis quantifying benefits, parameter efficiency, and effects of priors.  
- Connect to statistics: Clarify how learned context vectors relate to traditional estimators (means, moments, etc.) and interpretability.  
- Ablation studies: Evaluate contributions of architectural components (skip connections, stochastic layers) via controlled ablations.  
- Detailed result analysis: Visualize context vectors, report error bars, and provide deeper empirical insights into behavior and variability.  
- Clarify notation: Resolve ambiguous symbols (e.g., ‘c’ used for context and class) and improve consistency.  
- Question — network capacity: How does statistic network capacity affect its ability to approximate distribution moments and what are remedies?  
- Question — quantitative metrics for faces: Propose using FID, SSIM, or other metrics instead of only visual inspection for YouTube Faces.  
- Question — scaling/data efficiency: Provide theoretical or empirical evidence on performance scaling as number of datasets and per-dataset data vary.  
- Question — matching networks integration: Could matching-network-style mechanisms be incorporated to improve 20-way classification performance?  
- Question — alternative info-flow: Were alternative architectures to skip-connections tried, and how did they perform?
