- Introduces the "neural statistician": a statistic network that learns dataset-level summary vectors and a hierarchical VAE generative model for unordered collections, enabling unsupervised, data- and parameter-efficient few-shot learning.

- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.

- Addresses an underexplored problem (representations of datasets) and is well-motivated and placed within prior work.

- Experimental validation is strong and comprehensive (synthetic 1D clustering, spatial MNIST, OMNIGLOT, YouTube Faces) showing meaningful dataset representations and competitive few-shot results.

- Technical contributions are sound: hierarchical variational formalism, justified statistic network design, and links to classical statistics (e.g., sample mean).

- Improvement 1: More explicitly contrast with other set representation methods (e.g., Set2Seq) to clarify differences in supervision and approach.

- Improvement 2: Bolster the computational-efficiency claim with concrete metrics (e.g., inference time comparisons to alternatives).

- Improvement 3: Make the "dataset hungry" limitation more specific (how many datasets needed) and discuss mitigation (data augmentation, transfer learning).

- Improvement 4: Streamline and summarize YouTube Faces architecture details from the appendix in the main text.

- Question 1: Provide theoretical justification for the claim that the sample mean plus an expressive instance encoder can approximate useful statistical moments.

- Question 2: Explain how using a shared Gaussian variance in YouTube Faces affects modeling capacity and whether per-datapoint variance would help.

- Question 3: Elaborate on the "sample dropout" technique used for OMNIGLOT and whether it is necessary for the statistic network to work effectively.

- Overall: Significant, well-written contribution with strong empirical evidence; minor revisions would make it an excellent ICLR paper.
