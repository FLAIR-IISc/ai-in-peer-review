The paper proposes an extension of the variational autoencoder (VAE) model, referred to as the Neural Statistician, which learns to compute summary statistics of datasets in an unsupervised fashion. The model is designed to work with datasets as the key objects to model, rather than individual datapoints. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on various tasks, including clustering datasets, transferring generative models to new datasets, selecting representative samples of datasets, and classifying previously unseen classes.

I recommend accepting this paper with one or two key reasons for this choice. The first reason is that the paper tackles a well-defined and interesting problem, which is to learn summary statistics of datasets without supervision. The authors provide a clear and well-motivated approach to this problem, which is to extend the VAE model to work with datasets. The second reason is that the paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed approach on various tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness and versatility.

The paper supports its claims through a combination of theoretical and empirical results. The authors provide a clear and well-written description of their approach, including the model architecture and the training procedure. They also provide a thorough evaluation of their approach on various tasks, including clustering datasets, transferring generative models to new datasets, selecting representative samples of datasets, and classifying previously unseen classes. The results demonstrate the effectiveness and versatility of the proposed approach.

One potential limitation of the paper is that it assumes that the datasets are i.i.d. samples from some distribution. This assumption may not always hold in practice, and it would be interesting to see how the proposed approach can be extended to handle more complex datasets.
