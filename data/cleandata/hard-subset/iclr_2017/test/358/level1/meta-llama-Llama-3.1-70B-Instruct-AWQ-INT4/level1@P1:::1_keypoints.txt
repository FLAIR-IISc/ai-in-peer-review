- Paper proposes "Neural Statistician," extending VAEs to learn unsupervised dataset-level representations (statistics).
- Demonstrated effectiveness on clustering, generative model transfer, representative sample selection, and few-shot classification.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on solid motivation/placement in literature and convincing experimental support.
- Clear motivation linking to transfer learning and few-shot learning, with detailed related-work comparison.
- Treating datasets as primary objects in a VAE extension is natural and well-explained.
- Experiments are thorough, include state-of-the-art comparisons and ablation studies, and show visually compelling results.
- Major weakness: limited analysis of method limitations beyond noting it is "dataset hungry."
- Missing discussion on how approach scales or extends to more complex datasets and tasks.
- Implementation details are sparse—statistic network and inference network architectures and design choices are insufficiently described.
- Suggested improvements: deeper analysis of limitations, performance vs. dataset count, and exploration of extensions to complex tasks.
- Specific questions for authors: architectural details and choices for statistic/inference networks, how performance degrades with fewer datasets, and possible extensions/applications.
