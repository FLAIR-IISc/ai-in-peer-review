- Paper proposes the Neural Statistician: a VAE-based model that learns dataset-level summary representations rather than per-datapoint embeddings.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novelty: Introduces a new idea of learning representations of datasets with potential for few-shot and transfer learning.  
- Technical soundness: Well-motivated VAE formulation with clear model architecture and training procedure.  
- Related work: Provides a thorough review connecting to topic models, graphical models, and transfer learning.  
- Empirical evaluation: Demonstrates effectiveness on clustering, transferring generative models to new datasets, representative sample selection, and classifying unseen classes.  
- Presentation: Model and experimental results are clearly and well-written.  
- Limitation: Requires a large number of datasets to learn useful dataset-level representations.  
- Interpretability: More insight is needed into the interpretability and practical use of the learned summaries.  
- Baselines: Paper would benefit from a more detailed comparison with other few-shot and transfer learning approaches.  
- Questions for authors: Clarify architecture and hyperparameter choices.  
- Questions for authors: Explain plans to mitigate the large-dataset requirement.  
- Questions for authors: Provide details on experimental setup (hardware/software).  
- References suggested: Cite additional few-shot/transfer learning and set-to-set function work (e.g., Matching Networks, memory-augmented nets, order-matters for sets).
