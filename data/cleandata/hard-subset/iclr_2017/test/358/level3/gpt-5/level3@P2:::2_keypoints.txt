- Proposes a hierarchical VAE that models entire datasets via a dataset-level latent context c plus instance-level latents z to capture shared and per-example variation.
- Uses a statistic network q(c|D) with an exchangeable pooling operation to amortize inference over sets and learn unsupervised dataset summary statistics.
- Demonstrates clustering of dataset families and disentangling of statistics on synthetic 1D data.
- Shows transfer of generative structure and representative-subset selection on Spatial MNIST.
- Achieves few-shot generation and competitive 1- and 5-shot classification on Omniglot and MNIST; underperforms Matching Networks in 20-way tasks.
- Provides proof-of-concept few-shot conditioned face generation on YouTube Faces.
- Methodologically sound: standard variational objectives, exchangeable architecture, and sufficient implementation detail to reproduce trends.
- Decision: Accept; key reasons are a simple, well-motivated unsupervised framework and strong, diverse empirical demonstrations.
- Suggest softening the “Neural Statistician” naming to avoid overstating scope beyond learned set summaries.
- Request clarification on Spatial MNIST visualizations and verification of representative-point overlays and the greedy KL selection algorithm.
- Recommend quantifying dataset efficiency with learning curves over number of training datasets and per-dataset sample sizes.
- Ask for ablations on pooling choice (mean/sum/max), context dimensionality, number of stochastic layers, and skip connections and their effects on accuracy and sample quality.
- Request discussion/evaluation of KL direction used in classification (DKL(Ni||Nx) vs DKL(Nx||Ni) or symmetrized divergences) and inductive vs transductive protocol details.
- Encourage releasing code, data-processing scripts (Spatial MNIST, dilation/sample-dropout), and pretrained checkpoints for reproducibility.
- Ask targeted questions: will code/checkpoints be released; why mean pooling and sensitivity to pooling/test-set size; did you try alternative KL directions; how do c dimension and z depth affect performance; smallest data regime with meaningful statistics; verify Spatial MNIST representative points; robustness to test-time set size mismatch and effect of sample-dropout; and whether conditioning on the whole support set was explored to improve 20-way performance.
