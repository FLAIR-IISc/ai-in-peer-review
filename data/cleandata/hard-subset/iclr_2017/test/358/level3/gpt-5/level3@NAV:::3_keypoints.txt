- Proposes a hierarchical VAE with dataset-level latent variables (contexts) and a permutation-invariant set encoder to amortize inference over sets.
- Motivated by few-shot/transfer scenarios where modeling p(x|c) for an unseen dataset from few examples is desired.
- ELBO derivation and the exchangeability-preserving set encoder are clearly described.
- Empirical results show dataset clustering, transfer of generative models, subset selection, and few-shot generation/classification on synthetic 1D, spatial MNIST, Omniglot, and YouTube Faces.
- Decision: Accept — timely modeling contribution with clean inference machinery and strong empirical demonstrations, including one-shot generation.
- Strengths: well-situated relative to topic models/structured VAEs/few-shot baselines; hierarchical factorization and skip connections are thoughtfully adapted; set encoder respects exchangeability.
- Main empirical weakness: one-shot generation is under-quantified — missing log p(x|c) or held-out log p(x) (e.g., IWAE) comparisons to vanilla/conditional VAE baselines.
- Terminology critique: calling the method “neural statistician/statistic network” is misleading; prefer “approximate posterior over context” or “set encoder for q(c|D).”
- Clarify generation procedure: explicitly state whether c is set to E[q(c|D)] or sampled, and whether datapoint z are drawn from p(z|c) or from q(z|x,c).
- Recommended experiments: report log-likelihoods for one-shot generation, calibrate q(c|D) vs set size, and ablate pooling choices.
- Add baselines: include a vanilla VAE trained per dataset or a conditional VAE, and discuss the 20-way accuracy gap relative to matching networks.
- Question: Are the data-point-dependent z used in one-shot generation sampled from p(z|c) or taken from q(z|x,c)?
- Question: How exactly was Figure 5 produced — deterministic c vs sampled, number of ancestral samples, any truncation/temperature?
- Question: Why were log p(x|c) or held-out log p(x) not reported, and can you provide these vs a vanilla/conditional VAE?
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the number of shots at test time relative to training set sizes?
