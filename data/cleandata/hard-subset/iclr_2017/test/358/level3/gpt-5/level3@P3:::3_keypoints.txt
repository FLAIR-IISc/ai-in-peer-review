- Introduces a hierarchical VAE with a dataset-level latent c and amortized inference q(c|D) over unordered sets, enabling dataset-level reasoning beyond standard i.i.d. VAEs.  
- Architecture enforces exchangeability via a statistic network that pools instance encodings and uses multiple stochastic layers with skip connections in inference and generation.  
- Evaluated on synthetic 1D, Spatial MNIST, Omniglot, and YouTube Faces; supports clustering, dataset summarization, few-shot conditional generation, and few-shot classification with strong empirical transfer to unseen classes/domains.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reasons: principled, timely contribution (dataset-level latents with amortized set inference) and solid empirical demonstrations with convincing qualitative and competitive quantitative results.  
- Motivation: fills gap between pointwise representation learning and sequence models with a probabilistically sound hierarchical latent formulation related to topic models and one-shot learning.  
- Methodological soundness: correct ELBO derivations, justified factorization and skip connections; pooling-based statistic network is simple, exchangeable, and effective.  
- Empirical highlights: c clusters synthetic distributions sensibly; Spatial MNIST shows compelling conditional generation/summarization; Omniglot yields strong few-shot classification and transfer to MNIST; YouTube Faces demonstrates flexible identity generation.  
- Presentation: Overall clear, with helpful author response, but two procedural clarifications needed for one-shot generation.  
- Terminology concern: avoid potentially misleading names like “neural statistician”/“statistic network”; prefer “dataset-level approximate posterior” or “dataset-level inference network q(c|D)”.  
- Clarify one-shot generation procedure: specify whether c is posterior mean/sample/MAP and whether z are drawn from p(z|c) or from q(z|x,c), and whether multiple c samples were used to show variability.  
- Quantify one-shot generation: report conditional ELBOs (log p(x|c) lower bounds), unconditional ELBOs (log p(x) lower bounds) on held-out datasets, and compare to a vanilla VAE baseline.  
- Additional recommended ablations: effect of pooling choice (mean/max/sum) and sample dropout, sensitivity to c dimensionality and number of stochastic layers/skip connections, and scaling with number of context examples at test time.  
- Questions for authors: specifics of Figure 5 (context size, disjointness, c fixed vs sampled, additional conditioning), whether both p(z|c) and q(z|x,c) were tried for generation and how results differ.  
- Questions for authors: will you report conditional/unconditional ELBO comparisons to baselines; comment on computational cost/batching and practical dataset minibatch sizes; did you try hybrid conditioning for few-shot classification (e.g., matching-network-style support conditioning with q(c|D))?  
- Overall assessment: well-motivated, clearly executed, and broadly relevant work; recommend acceptance with minor clarifications and a simple quantitative comparison for one-shot generation.
