Summary and contribution: The paper proposes a hierarchical VAE with dataset-level latent variables (contexts) and a permutation-invariant “statistic” network that amortizes inference over sets. This moves beyond the pointwise graphical structure of standard VAEs and is well motivated by few-shot/transfer scenarios where we wish to model p(x|c) for an unseen dataset from a handful of examples. The derivation of the ELBO and the exchangeability-preserving set encoder are clearly described. Empirically, the method supports clustering of datasets, transfer of generative models, subset selection, and few-shot generation/classification across synthetic 1D data, spatial MNIST, Omniglot, and YouTube Faces. The idea is clear, general, and convincingly presented.

Decision: Accept. Key reasons: (1) A timely, well-placed modeling contribution—dataset-level latent structure in VAEs—with clean inference machinery; (2) Broad and generally strong empirical demonstrations, including compelling one-shot generation.

Supporting arguments: The approach is well situated against topic models, structured VAEs, and few-shot baselines (matching networks, memory-augmented nets). The hierarchical factorization and skip connections are standard yet thoughtfully adapted to contexts, and the set encoder respects exchangeability. Claims about learning reusable dataset representations and enabling few-shot adaptation are mostly supported by qualitative samples and competitive few-shot classification (notably on 5-way tasks). However, the paper under-quantifies one-shot generation: it should report log p(x|c) or held-out log p(x) (e.g., via importance-weighted bounds) and compare to a vanilla VAE and a conditional VAE baseline; this omission weakens the empirical rigor but is straightforward to fix.

Additional feedback (to improve the paper): 
- Terminology: Calling the method a “neural statistician/statistic network” is misleading. Prefer “approximate posterior over context” or “set encoder for q(c|D).”
- Clarify one-shot generation (end of p.6) and Figure 5: explicitly specify whether c is set to E[q(c|D)] or sampled, and whether datapoint-specific z are drawn from p(z|c) (forward model) or from q(z|x,c).
- Quantify one-shot generation as suggested above; also report calibration of q(c|D) vs set size and ablate pooling choices.
- Include a vanilla VAE baseline trained per dataset or with conditional inputs, and discuss 20-way accuracy gap relative to matching networks.

Questions for the authors:
- Are the data-point-dependent z used in one-shot generation sampled from p(z|c) or taken from q(z|x,c)? 
- How exactly was Figure 5 produced (deterministic c vs sampled; number of ancestral samples; any truncation/temperature)?
- Why was log p(x|c) or held-out log p(x) not reported, and can you provide these vs a vanilla/conditional VAE?
- How sensitive is performance to the number of shots at test time relative to training set sizes?
