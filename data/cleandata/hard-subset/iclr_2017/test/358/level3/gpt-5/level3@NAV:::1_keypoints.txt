- Proposes a hierarchical generative model over datasets with a dataset-level latent c and per-example latents z.  
- Introduces a "double" ELBO: a dataset-level variational term over c nested with per-example terms over z, amortized by a permutation-invariant statistic network.  
- Presentation is clear, principled, and the architecture respects exchangeability over sets.  
- Method is general and likely reusable beyond the specific instantiations shown.  
- Empirical demonstrations: few-shot adaptation, dataset clustering, summarization, and classification on Omniglot, spatial MNIST, and YouTube Faces.  
- Few-shot results are strong (competitive on 5-way, not SOTA on 20-way) and qualitative results are compelling.  
- Decision: Accept — timely, well-motivated formulation with a clean variational treatment and promising empirical support.  
- Main methodological concern: using minibatches of examples per dataset to train the statistic network may yield a biased gradient because q(c|D) is nonlinear and sample means are used.  
- Request to clarify training: whether examples are subsampled for q(c|D), whether the gradient estimator is unbiased, and how variance scales with set size.  
- Suggest exploring more expressive pooling (max, log-sum-exp, attention, learned set encoders) since mean pooling may miss "existential" features.  
- Ask for ablations: pooling choice, number of stochastic layers, skip connections, and sample-dropout effects.  
- Recommend evaluating calibration/uncertainty: how the posterior over c narrows as number of shots increases.  
- Propose practical applications such as hierarchical forecasting (e.g., electricity or retail sales across related series).  
- Questions for authors: are minibatches used within datasets for the statistic network and is the resulting gradient unbiased?  
- Questions for authors: how sensitive are results to the pooling operator and to train/test dataset size mismatch?  
- Questions for authors: can the model capture "any-instance" features and would alternative pooling/attention address this?  
- Questions for authors: provide computational cost per dataset and how it scales with set size.
