- Proposes the "neural statistician": a VAE extension that learns representations of entire datasets via a dataset-level context variable c.
- Introduces a statistic network (feedforward) that maps a dataset to an approximate posterior over c and enforces exchangeability within datasets.
- Claims benefits: enables transfer learning, few-shot learning, dataset clustering, selecting representative samples, and transferring generative models.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on varied domains (1D distributions, spatial MNIST, Omniglot, YouTube Faces).
- Recommendation: accept — novel, well-motivated, well-executed, with convincing experiments.
- Main technical contribution: modeling datasets (not points) with a hierarchical generative model that handles varying dataset sizes and generalizes to new datasets/classes without retraining.
- Concern: terminology is misleading — “neural statistician”/“statistic network” implies classical statistics; should be renamed (e.g., “context posterior network”).
- Concern: missing crucial baseline — no comparison to a vanilla VAE on few-shot tasks (reporting log p(x|c) or log p(x) on held-out datasets).
- Concern: few-shot generation procedure is unclear — ambiguous whether z is sampled from the forward model p(z|c) or from the approximate posterior q(z|x,c).
- Suggestion: revise terminology to avoid confusion with statistical terminology.
- Suggestion: include a vanilla VAE baseline for few-shot experiments to isolate benefits of dataset-level modeling.
- Suggestion: clarify the few-shot generation procedure (explicitly state source of z variables).
- Question: are z variables for few-shot generation drawn from p(z|c) or q(z|x,c)?
- Question: how would a standard VAE trained on the entire dataset perform on the reported few-shot classification tasks?
- Question: how is "reasonable likeness" in the YouTube Faces results quantified — any metrics (e.g., FID, perceptual similarity) to support this claim?
- Overall: strong and promising direction; with the requested clarifications and baseline comparisons it would be an excellent contribution.
