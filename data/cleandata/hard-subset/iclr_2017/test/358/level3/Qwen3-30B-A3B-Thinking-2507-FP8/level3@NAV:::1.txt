

This paper introduces the "neural statistician," a novel hierarchical generative model that learns to compute summary statistics of datasets in an unsupervised manner. The authors propose a "double" variational bound where a higher-level latent variable describes entire datasets while lower-level latents model individual examples within those datasets. This approach enables efficient learning from new datasets for both unsupervised and supervised tasks, with demonstrated capabilities in clustering datasets, transferring generative models, selecting representative samples, and few-shot classification.

I recommend acceptance. The paper tackles an important, under-explored problem in deep learning with strong theoretical motivation. The core idea of the "double" variational bound is well-motivated, clearly presented, and appears widely applicable across different domains. The experimental results, particularly in few-shot learning, are impressive and well-supported by thorough ablation studies across multiple datasets including Omniglot, spatial MNIST, and YouTube Faces.

The main concern relates to the training procedure for the statistic network. The paper states minibatches of datasets are used during training, but it's unclear whether this leads to an unbiased gradient estimator or potentially limits the model's ability to learn true dataset-wide features (e.g., "any example has a feature"). This is critical for the model's expressiveness and generalization capabilities.

Additional feedback: The paper would benefit from a clearer explanation of how the minibatch training strategy affects the model's ability to capture dataset-level properties. A comparison of training with full datasets versus minibatches would strengthen the empirical analysis. The suggestion to explore hierarchical forecasting applications (e.g., electricity or sales) is promising and worth pursuing.

Questions for authors: 
1. Can you clarify whether minibatch training of the statistic network provides an unbiased gradient estimator of the full dataset gradient?
2. How does the model ensure it captures dataset-wide features rather than just per-example patterns when trained on minibatches?
