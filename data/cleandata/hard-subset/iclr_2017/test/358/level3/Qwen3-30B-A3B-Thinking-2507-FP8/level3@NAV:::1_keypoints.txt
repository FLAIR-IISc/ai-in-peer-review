- Introduces the "neural statistician," a hierarchical generative model that learns unsupervised dataset summary statistics via a higher-level latent for datasets and lower-level latents for examples.  
- Proposes a "double" variational bound to model dataset-level and example-level structure.  
- Enables efficient adaptation to new datasets for unsupervised and supervised tasks, including clustering, generative model transfer, representative sample selection, and few-shot classification.  
- Strong theoretical motivation; tackles an important, under-explored problem in deep learning.  
- Core idea and double variational bound are well-motivated, clearly presented, and broadly applicable.  
- Experimental results are impressive, especially in few-shot learning, with thorough ablation studies.  
- Evaluation conducted across multiple datasets (Omniglot, spatial MNIST, YouTube Faces).  
- Main concern: minibatch training of the statistic network is unclear—may yield biased gradient estimates or hinder learning true dataset-wide features.  
- Paper should better explain how minibatch strategy affects capturing dataset-level properties and include comparisons to full-dataset training.  
- Suggested future direction: explore hierarchical forecasting applications (e.g., electricity or sales).  
- Questions for authors: (1) Does minibatch training provide an unbiased gradient estimator of the full-dataset gradient? (2) How does the model ensure it captures dataset-wide features rather than only per-example patterns when trained on minibatches?
