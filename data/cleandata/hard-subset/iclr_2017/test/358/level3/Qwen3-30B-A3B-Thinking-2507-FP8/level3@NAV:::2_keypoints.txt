- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
- Contribution: introduces a hierarchical VAE ("Neural Statistician") that learns dataset-level representations via a statistic network.
- Key innovation: unsupervised learning of summary statistics for datasets enabling few-shot "learning to learn".
- Strength: conceptually simple and elegant compared to many recent one-shot methods.
- Empirical results: solid performance on few-shot learning, dataset clustering, and generative transfer across multiple domains.
- Experiments: well-chosen domains (synthetic distributions, Spatial MNIST, OMNIGLOT, YouTube Faces).
- Writing: clear, enjoyable, and technically well-founded.
- Criticism: the title and "statistician" claim are overgrandiose and misleading regarding actual statistical reasoning.
- Issue: Figure 4 (Spatial MNIST) appears problematic—point density and red-point correspondences do not match the text, raising performance questions for small datasets.
- Requested revisions: soften title/claims, clarify Spatial MNIST setup, and address the Figure 4 discrepancy.
- Requested additional detail: specify dataset size requirements for effective learning and how performance scales with dataset size.
- Reproducibility question: please clarify plans to release code.
- Specific probe: explain model behavior with significantly fewer samples (e.g., 10 vs. 50) and how the statistic network handles varying dataset sizes.
