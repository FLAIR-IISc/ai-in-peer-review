- Proposes the "Neural Statistician": a VAE extension with a dataset-level latent variable (context) and a statistic network that outputs context vectors summarizing whole datasets.  
- Decision: accept with minor revisions.  
- Main contribution: reframes datasets as the fundamental modeling unit, capturing shared structure across data points via a context latent.  
- Model capability: enables learning generative models per-dataset, clustering datasets, selecting representatives, transfer, and few-shot classification.  
- Empirical evidence: thorough experiments on 1D distributions, spatial MNIST, OMNIGLOT, and YouTube Faces showing effective dataset summarization and generalization with few examples.  
- Strengths highlighted: well-motivated, clearly written, good literature discussion, impressive one-shot generation, unsupervised and data-/parameter-efficient.  
- Terminology issue: "statistician" and "statistic network" are misleading; recommend renaming to "approximate posterior" or "context encoder."  
- Clarification needed: unclear if data-point latent z in one-shot generation comes from p(z|c; θ) or q(z|x,c; φ) (Algorithm 2 / Figure 5).  
- Quantification requested: report log p(x|c) or log p(x) on held-out datasets and compare to a vanilla VAE for one-shot generation.  
- Question (a): Are z vectors in one-shot generation sampled from p(z|c; θ) or taken from q(z|x,c; φ)?  
- Question (b): Can the authors provide held-out log-likelihoods versus a vanilla VAE to show the model learns dataset distributions rather than memorizing?
