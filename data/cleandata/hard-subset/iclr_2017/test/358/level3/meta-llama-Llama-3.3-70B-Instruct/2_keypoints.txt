- Proposes Rectified Factor Networks (RFNs) for biclustering, overcoming limitations of FABIA.  
- RFNs construct sparse, non-linear, high-dimensional representations to detect rare and small events.  
- Method evaluated on synthetic and real datasets (gene expression, 1000 Genomes), outperforming 13 other methods.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Key reason: well-motivated, conceptually simple, and a significant addition to biclustering literature.  
- Key reason: strong empirical performance identifying biclusters across varied datasets.  
- Presentation: clear mathematical formulation, implementation details, and thorough experimental evaluation.  
- Suggestion: provide more details on computational complexity and scalability to large datasets.  
- Suggestion: study robustness to different types of noise and outliers.  
- Suggestion: consider applying RFNs to other domains (e.g., image or text analysis).  
- Question: please clarify hyperparameter choices (number of hidden units, dropout rate).  
- Question: how do RFNs handle unknown or varying numbers of biclusters across datasets?  
- Question: are there plans to release the RFN implementation as open-source for replication?
