- Proposes Rectified Factor Networks (RFNs) for biclustering, addressing limitations of existing methods like FABIA.  
- RFNs produce sparse, non-linear, high-dimensional representations enabling detection of rare and small events.  
- Authors evaluate RFNs on synthetic and real-world datasets, including gene expression and 1000 Genomes Project data.  
- Experimental results show RFNs outperform existing biclustering methods, including FABIA.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing significant improvement in biclustering performance.  
- RFNs are seen as having potential to become the new state-of-the-art due to scalability, sparse coding, and distinct membership assignment.  
- Paper provides a clear, well-motivated introduction and is well-placed in the literature with theoretical and empirical support.  
- Suggest exploring practical applications (e.g., hierarchical forecasting for electricity or sales) to demonstrate utility.  
- Recommend adding details on RFN computational complexity and comparisons with existing methods.  
- Request more visualizations of biclustering results to aid interpretation.  
- Questions for authors: provide details on RFN training process, optimization algorithm, and convergence criteria.  
- Questions for authors: explain hyperparameter selection (hidden units, dropout, learning rate) and interpretation of results for the 1000 Genomes Project.
