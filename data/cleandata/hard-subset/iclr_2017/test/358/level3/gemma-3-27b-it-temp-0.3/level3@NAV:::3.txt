## Review of "Neural Statistician"

This paper proposes a novel extension to variational autoencoders (VAEs) by introducing dataset-level latent variables, effectively shifting the focus from modeling individual data points to modeling entire datasets. This is a well-motivated idea, grounded in the principle of efficient learning through knowledge reuse and recognizing datasets as fundamental objects. The authors clearly articulate the benefits of this approach, including unsupervised learning of dataset summaries, data efficiency, parameter efficiency, and potential for few-shot learning. The paper is well-written and the core concept is readily understandable.

**Reject** – While the idea is promising and the initial experiments are encouraging, the paper lacks a crucial comparative evaluation that would solidify its contribution. Specifically, a quantitative comparison against a vanilla VAE on the one-shot generation task is missing.

The primary reason for rejection stems from the lack of a baseline comparison. The authors demonstrate the ability to generate samples from new datasets, but without quantifying the performance relative to a standard VAE, it’s difficult to assess the true value of their approach. The claim of improved few-shot learning relies heavily on the quality of the learned dataset representations, and this quality needs to be objectively measured. The experiments presented are generally well-designed and the authors’ response regarding one-shot generation is appreciated, but this doesn’t substitute for a direct quantitative comparison. 

Furthermore, the terminology used throughout the paper is somewhat misleading. Referring to the model as a "statistician" and using the term "statistic network" is unnecessarily evocative and obscures the fact that they are learning an *approximate* posterior distribution. Adopting more standard terminology would improve clarity and precision.

**Additional Feedback:**

*   The language surrounding one-shot generation on page 6 and the procedure depicted in Figure 5 require further clarification. Specifically, how are the context variables *c* obtained for new datasets? Are they sampled from the prior, or is there a more sophisticated inference procedure?
*   To address my primary concern, I request the authors to quantify one-shot generation by reporting log p(x|c) or log p(x) on held-out datasets and comparing these results to a vanilla VAE. This comparison is essential to demonstrate the benefits of their approach.
*   **Question (a):** Are the data-point-dependent vectors *z* generated from the forward model (p(z|c; θ)) or are they sampled from the approximate posterior (q(z|x, c; φ))? This clarification is important for understanding the generative process.
*   **Suggestion (b):** Consider providing ablation studies to evaluate the contribution of different components of the model, such as the skip connections or the specific pooling mechanism used in the statistic network.
