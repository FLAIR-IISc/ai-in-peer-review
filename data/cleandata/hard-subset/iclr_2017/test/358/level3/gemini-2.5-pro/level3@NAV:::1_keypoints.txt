- Proposes the "neural statistician," a hierarchical generative model that learns dataset-level representations in an unsupervised way.
- Uses a two-level generative process with a higher-level "context" latent for datasets and lower-level latents for individual data points.
- Main technical contribution is a "double" variational bound extending the VAE framework to the hierarchical setting.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on tasks including dataset clustering, conditional sample generation from few examples, and few-shot classification.
- Recommends Accept, citing the importance of hierarchical modeling and the principled, potentially widely applicable variational bound.
- Motivation to model distributions over datasets is clear and compelling, enabling sharing statistical strength across related tasks.
- Technical execution is sound; the statistic network plus context variable are well presented and empirically supported.
- Qualitative results on synthetic and image data are illustrative; quantitative Omniglot few-shot results are strong and competitive.
- Concern: the statistic network’s simple mean-pooling may miss dataset-wide properties (variance, multimodality, rare examples); discuss limitations and alternatives.
- Suggest adding discussion of practical applications (e.g., hierarchical forecasting for related time series like retail or electricity).
- Clarification requested on scalability: whether the instance encoder processes entire large datasets or uses minibatch approximations, and whether minibatch estimates give unbiased gradients for the context posterior.
