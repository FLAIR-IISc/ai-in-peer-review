- Proposes the "Neural Statistician," a hierarchical VAE that learns dataset-level latent contexts (c) and a permutation-invariant "statistic network" to represent entire datasets.  
- Trains the model unsupervised to learn summary statistics that define a generative model per dataset.  
- Demonstrates applicability on clustering 1D distributions, representative sampling in Spatial MNIST, and few-shot generation/classification on Omniglot and YouTube Faces.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main reason: Novel, well-motivated extension of VAEs to dataset-level representations with clear exposition.  
- Main reason: Empirically effective across diverse tasks with strong qualitative results.  
- Strength: Conceptual novelty and elegant hierarchical formulation with an exchangeable pooling statistic network.  
- Experimental highlights: 1D experiments show meaningful context space; Spatial MNIST shows effective dataset summarization; few-shot generation on Omniglot/YTF produces convincing samples; few-shot classification is competitive.  
- Major weakness: No quantitative evaluation for few-shot generative performance (e.g., held-out log-likelihood), hindering rigorous comparison to baselines.  
- Feedback: The term "Neural Statistician" and "statistic network" may be misleading; recommend clearer, more standard terminology (e.g., inference network for context posterior).  
- Feedback: Clarify the one-shot generation procedure, specifically how data-point latent variables z are handled when conditioning on a new dataset.  
- Question: During few-shot generation, are z variables drawn from the prior p(z|c) or inferred from examples—please clarify and update Algorithm 2.  
- Question: Please provide quantitative one-shot generation metrics (average log-likelihood on unseen classes) and comparisons to simple baselines (e.g., VAE conditioned on mean support embedding); why were these omitted?
