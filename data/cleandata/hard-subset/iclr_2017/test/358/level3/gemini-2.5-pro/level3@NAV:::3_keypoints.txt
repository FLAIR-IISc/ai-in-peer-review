- Proposes a novel VAE extension that models collections of datasets using a hierarchical latent "context" inferred by a statistic network.  
- Demonstrates model on clustering datasets, one-shot generation, and few-shot classification with results on Omniglot and YouTube Faces.  
- Reviewer recommends Accept.  
- Key contribution: incorporating dataset-level latent variables is a significant advance for generative modeling and meta-learning.  
- Strength: principled framing that treats datasets as objects, enabling transfer and few-shot learning; clear motivation and compelling qualitative results.  
- Main weakness: quantitative evaluation of one-shot generative performance is incomplete.  
- Missing evidence: no log-likelihood (e.g., log p(x|c) or log p(x)) comparisons on held-out datasets against a vanilla VAE baseline.  
- Suggestion: clarify terminology—avoid misleading names like "neural statistician" and prefer standard terms (e.g., approximate posterior over dataset-level latent).  
- Suggestion: clarify the one-shot generation procedure described on page 6 and in Figure 5.  
- Question: during generation, are datapoint latents z drawn from the model p(z|c) or sampled from the approximate posterior q(z|x,c)?  
- Recommendation: add quantitative one-shot generation metrics (held-out log-likelihoods) compared to standard VAE to strengthen empirical claims.
