- Proposes a hierarchical generative model that learns dataset-level representations using a higher-level latent variable and per-example lower-level latents.  
- Introduces a “double” variational bound as the core theoretical contribution for dataset-level modeling.  
- Motivated by the need for reuse of knowledge across tasks and learning dataset-wide abstractions.  
- Demonstrates capabilities on clustering datasets, generative model transfer, and few-shot learning (results appear promising).  
- Recommends accepting the paper, citing novelty and well-defined theoretical framework.  
- Paper is clearly written with a solid theoretical derivation and helpful appendices (pseudocode and architecture details) for reproducibility.  
- Experimental results and ablation studies (notably OMNIGLOT comparisons) provide initial validation of learned dataset representations.  
- Major concern: training the statistic network with minibatches of datasets may yield a biased gradient estimator if datasets in a minibatch are correlated.  
- Suggests evaluating practical real-world applications (e.g., hierarchical time-series forecasting) to demonstrate utility beyond experiments.  
- Asks authors to clarify statistical properties of the gradient estimator and provide theoretical/empirical evidence about any bias.  
- Asks whether alternative training strategies (larger/more diverse batches or bias-mitigation techniques) were considered.  
- Requests discussion of computational cost and scalability compared to other dataset-representation methods and possible optimizations.
