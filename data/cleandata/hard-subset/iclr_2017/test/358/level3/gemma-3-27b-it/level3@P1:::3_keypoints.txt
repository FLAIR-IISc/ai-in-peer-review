- Proposes extending VAEs to learn dataset-level representations via a dataset latent variable summarizing each dataset.  
- Decision: Accept, based on novelty and potential impact.  
- Method is well-motivated to enable transfer and efficient learning across related datasets.  
- Theoretical grounding is sound, building on variational inference with clear math.  
- Experiments across synthetic 1D, spatial MNIST, Omniglot, and YouTube Faces show effectiveness and diverse applicability.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations like dataset hunger and room to improve few-shot performance.  
- Suggest renaming “neural statistician” to a more precise term (e.g., approximate posterior or dataset embedding network).  
- Request clarification of the one-shot generation procedure: whether z for sampling comes from p(x|z,c) or q(z|x,c).  
- Recommend more thorough comparisons to existing methods, especially quantitative metrics (e.g., log likelihood) vs. standard VAE.  
- Ask whether alternative pooling methods (sum, max, geometric mean) were tried instead of sample mean in the statistic network.  
- Ask for strategies to mitigate dataset hunger, such as data augmentation or meta-learning.
