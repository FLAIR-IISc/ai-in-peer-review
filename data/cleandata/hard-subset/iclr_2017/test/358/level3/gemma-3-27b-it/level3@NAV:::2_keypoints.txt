- Proposes a hierarchical VAE with a novel “statistic network” to learn dataset-level summary representations for clustering, transfer, and few-shot learning.  
- Conceptually elegant, moves toward dataset-centric modeling; validated on synthetic 1D, Spatial MNIST, Omniglot, and YouTube Faces; paper is clearly written.  
- Recommendation: Reject with revision — the title “Neural Statistician” is overly ambitious/misleading given the method’s scope.  
- Major strength: solid few-shot learning results (especially on Omniglot) and a simpler approach than many one-shot methods; YouTube Faces results are preliminary but promising.  
- Concern: Spatial MNIST experiment may use unusually dense inputs; unclear if model handles truly sparse samples and the “meaningful” red points in Fig. 4 lack strong visual support.  
- Requested improvements: revise title/framing, provide more Spatial MNIST analysis with sparser visualizations, clarify/justify which points in Fig. 4 are meaningful, and add ablation studies (e.g., skip-connections).  
- Asks whether the authors will release code to aid reproducibility and further research.
