- Proposes a “neural statistician”: a VAE extension that learns dataset-level latent variables (“contexts”) to capture whole-dataset statistics for unsupervised learning and knowledge reuse.  
- Motivated as enabling dataset clustering, transfer learning, sample selection, and few-shot classification via learned dataset representations.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — strong core idea, well-executed, significant contribution despite some shortcomings.  
- Main strength: elegant, principled extension of VAEs to dataset-level modeling that moves beyond point-wise representations.  
- Benefits claimed: data efficiency, parameter efficiency, and suitability for few-shot learning.  
- Empirical evaluation: comprehensive experiments on synthetic 1D distributions, spatial MNIST, Omniglot, and YouTube Faces; Omniglot and spatial MNIST results are especially compelling.  
- YouTube Faces is a promising proof-of-concept for generation from unseen individuals; authors addressed reviewer queries about one-shot generation in their response.  
- Terminology concern: terms like “statistician” and “statistic network” are misleading—should describe learned representations as approximate posteriors over dataset-level latents.  
- One-shot generation description unclear: need explicit clarification whether z vectors are generated from p(x|z; θ) given sampled c or sampled from q(z|x,c; φ), and a step-by-step Figure 5 explanation.  
- Missing quantitative generative evaluation: should report held-out log likelihoods (log p(x|c) or log p(x)) and compare to a vanilla VAE trained on individual datasets to demonstrate benefits.  
- Appendix/architecture details: architectural specifics are essential and should be better integrated into the main experiment descriptions (e.g., key layer sizes).  
- Questions for authors: (a) clarify the one-shot z sampling procedure, and (b) explain omission of quantitative comparison to a standard VAE and provide log-likelihood results.
