- Proposes the "neural statistician," a hierarchical VAE that learns dataset-level representations (dataset latent) and example-level latents.
- Extension is well-motivated, novel, and potentially impactful for representing datasets rather than individual points.
- Decision: Accept.
- The hierarchical VAE formulation is principled with a solid theoretical foundation (double variational bound).
- Empirical results are promising across tasks: synthetic 1D, spatial MNIST, OMNIGLOT, and YouTube Faces.
- Few-shot learning on OMNIGLOT is competitive with state-of-the-art.
- YouTube Faces results are presented as a proof-of-concept but show meaningful dataset-level generation.
- Authors acknowledge limitations, including dataset hunger and the trade-off between fast adaptation and slow training.
- Recommend more detail on network architecture choices (layers, activations) for each task.
- Recommend a more thorough ablation study to isolate effects of components like skip-connections and sample dropout.
- Raises concern about minibatch training of the statistic network possibly hindering learning of dataset-wide features; asks whether full-dataset training improves performance.
- Questions how pooling choice (sample mean vs max or weighted average) affects the ability to capture different dataset-level statistics.
- Suggests exploring additional applications such as hierarchical forecasting across multiple time series.
