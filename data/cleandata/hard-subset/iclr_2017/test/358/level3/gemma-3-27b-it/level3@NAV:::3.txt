## Review of “Neural Statistician”

This paper proposes a novel extension to variational autoencoders (VAEs) by introducing dataset-level latent variables, effectively moving beyond traditional data-point focused modeling. The core idea—learning representations of entire datasets to facilitate transfer learning and efficient generalization—is well-motivated and clearly presented. The authors demonstrate the model's potential in clustering datasets, transferring generative models, selecting representative samples, and classifying new classes.

I recommend **Accept** with minor revisions. The contribution is significant and the experimental results are promising. The approach has the potential to improve learning efficiency, particularly in scenarios with limited data per class or dataset. However, some clarifications and a crucial comparison are needed to fully validate the claims.

The primary strength of the work lies in its conceptual leap – explicitly modeling datasets rather than individual data points. This is a compelling idea with a strong theoretical basis, and the authors articulate this motivation well. The experimental setup appears sound, and the results in the spatial MNIST and OMNIGLOT datasets are particularly encouraging. The authors’ response regarding one-shot generation in the rebuttal also demonstrates a good understanding of the method's capabilities. 

However, the terminology used (“neural statistician,” “statistic network”) is somewhat misleading. Referring to these components as an “approximate posterior” would be more precise and align better with standard statistical language.  Further, the description of the one-shot generation procedure (end of p.6) and the visual representation in Figure 5 require clarification – it’s not entirely clear how the samples are being generated from the approximate posterior. 

Specifically, **(a)** are the data-point dependent vectors *z* generated from the forward model *p(x|z;θ)* or sampled directly from the approximate posterior *q(z|x, c; φ)*?  Moreover, **(b)** a critical comparison is missing: quantify one-shot generation by reporting log *p(x|c)* or log *p(x)* on held-out datasets and compare the performance to a vanilla VAE. I expect this comparison to be significant, and its omission weakens the argument for the proposed method’s superiority.

Finally, while the ablation studies could be expanded, they are adequate for this initial exploration. It would be valuable to see performance with different pooling strategies in the statistic network.
