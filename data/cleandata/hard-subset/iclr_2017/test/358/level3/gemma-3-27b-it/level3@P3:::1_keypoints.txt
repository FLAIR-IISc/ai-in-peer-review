- Decision: Accept — paper presents a novel hierarchical model learning dataset-level representations with promising results.
- Core idea: Introduces a “double” variational bound with higher-level dataset latent variables and lower-level example latents.
- Contribution: Extends variational autoencoders to model datasets as fundamental objects for transfer and few-shot learning.
- Strength: Strong motivation for dataset-level representations enabling knowledge reuse in low-data regimes.
- Clarity: Mathematical formulation and variational bound derivation are clear and well-presented.
- Results: Demonstrates versatility across tasks (1D distributions, spatial MNIST, Omniglot few-shot, YouTube faces) with especially compelling spatial MNIST and competitive Omniglot performance.
- Limitation acknowledgment: Authors discuss weaknesses and future work, adding credibility.
- Statistic network detail needed: More discussion required on instance encoder capacity and architectural choices impacting dataset representation.
- Minibatch training bias: The use of minibatches of datasets raises concerns about biased gradient estimates and needs explicit treatment.
- Dataset-wide feature learning: Potential limitation that minibatch training may hinder learning of global dataset features; mitigation strategies should be discussed.
- Ablation studies recommended: Evaluate contributions of components (e.g., skip connections, multiple latent layers) to strengthen claims.
- Baseline comparisons: Include simpler baselines (e.g., averaging embeddings) in few-shot experiments to contextualize gains.
- Question — gradient estimation: Request details/theoretical justification on unbiasedness of gradient estimation when training on minibatches of datasets.
- Question — pooling sensitivity: Ask how sensitive performance is to pooling choice (mean, sum, max) in the statistic network and whether alternatives were tested.
- Question — time-series application: Request elaboration on adapting the framework for hierarchical forecasting (e.g., defining datasets as consecutive time windows) and potential advantages.
