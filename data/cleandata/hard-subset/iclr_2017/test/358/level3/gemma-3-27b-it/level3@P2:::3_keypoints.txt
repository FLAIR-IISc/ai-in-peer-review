- Paper proposes a dataset-level VAE ("neural statistician") to learn representations of datasets rather than individual datapoints.
- Experiments cover dataset clustering, generative transfer, representative sampling, and few-shot classification with promising qualitative results.
- Recommendation: Reject due to insufficient empirical evidence and unclear comparative advantage.
- Main empirical concern: experiments show ability but not clear effectiveness versus baselines or simpler alternatives.
- Missing baseline: no comparison to a standard VAE in one-shot generation (Omniglot, YouTube Faces).
- Subjective claims about sample quality need quantitative evaluation (e.g., likelihoods, precision/recall).
- Experimental setup lacks crucial detail: data splits, hyperparameters, training procedures, and exact evaluation metrics.
- Terminology is misleading: calling the model a "neural statistician" and component a "statistic network" obscures that it learns approximate posteriors, not classical statistics.
- Descriptions of the generative process and latent roles are imprecise and confusing (e.g., Figure 5 and one-shot generation).
- Specific ambiguity: unclear whether datapoint-dependent z are sampled from p(x|z,c) or from q(z|x,c), and how context c is chosen.
- Recommendation: replace misleading terms with precise names like "dataset-level VAE" or "approximate posterior network."
- Recommendation: provide quantitative metrics (log-likelihoods, precision/recall) for all experiments.
- Recommendation: include baseline comparisons to standard VAE for one-shot generation to demonstrate benefits of dataset-level latent variables.
- Recommendation: add detailed experimental description and ablation studies (e.g., skip connections, instance pooling layer).
- Recommendation: clarify Figure 5 and sampling procedure, and expand discussion of limitations (sensitivity to hyperparameters, computational cost).
- Question: In one-shot experiments, are z drawn from p(x|z,c) or q(z|x,c), and how is c chosen?
- Question: Why omit comparison to a standard VAE, and under what conditions would the proposed method outperform it?
- Question: Provide details for few-shot learning splits (number of classes for train/test, selection criteria).
- Question: Why use sample mean for instance pooling; were other pooling methods tested and how did they compare?
- Question: The model is described as “dataset hungry” — quantify required number of datasets for effective learning.
