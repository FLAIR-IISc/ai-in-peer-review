- Proposes extending VAEs with dataset-level latent variables to model entire datasets rather than individual datapoints.  
- Idea is well-motivated and clearly presented, enabling transfer learning and better generalization.  
- Recommends accept with minor revisions; contribution is significant and experiments are promising.  
- Potential to improve learning efficiency, especially with limited data per class/dataset.  
- Clarifications and a crucial quantitative comparison are needed to fully validate claims.  
- Main strength is the conceptual leap to explicitly modeling datasets, with a strong theoretical basis.  
- Experimental setup appears sound; spatial MNIST and OMNIGLOT results are encouraging.  
- Authors’ rebuttal on one-shot generation shows good understanding of the method’s capabilities.  
- Terminology (“neural statistician,” “statistic network”) is misleading; should be framed as an “approximate posterior.”  
- One-shot generation description and Figure 5 are unclear—need clarification on how samples are generated from the approximate posterior.  
- Specific question: are datapoint-dependent z vectors generated from p(x|z; θ) or sampled from q(z|x,c; φ)?  
- Missing critical comparison: report log p(x|c) or log p(x) on held-out data and compare to a vanilla VAE.  
- Ablation studies are adequate for initial exploration but could be expanded, e.g., testing different pooling strategies in the statistic network.
