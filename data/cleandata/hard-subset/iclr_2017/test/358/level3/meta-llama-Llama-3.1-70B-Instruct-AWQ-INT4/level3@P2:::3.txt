**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel extension of the Variational Autoencoder (VAE) framework, which learns to represent datasets rather than individual datapoints. The authors introduce a dataset-level latent variable, referred to as the "context," which captures the underlying structure of the data. This allows the model to learn a generative process for each dataset, enabling efficient learning from new datasets for both unsupervised and supervised tasks. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of this approach on various tasks, including clustering datasets, transferring generative models to new datasets, selecting representative samples of datasets, and classifying previously unseen classes.

**Decision**

Based on the review, I decide to **Accept** the paper. The main reason for this decision is that the paper proposes a well-motivated and clearly described extension of the VAE framework, which addresses a significant limitation of traditional VAEs. The experiments are also generally well-executed and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper's main contribution is the introduction of a dataset-level latent variable, which allows the model to learn a generative process for each dataset. This is a significant improvement over traditional VAEs, which only model individual datapoints. The authors provide a clear and well-motivated explanation of the need for this extension and demonstrate its effectiveness on various tasks.

The experiments are also well-executed and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The authors provide a range of experiments, including clustering datasets, transferring generative models to new datasets, selecting representative samples of datasets, and classifying previously unseen classes. These experiments demonstrate the flexibility and effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Additional Feedback**

While the paper is generally well-written, there are a few areas that could be improved. Firstly, the terminology used in the paper is sometimes misleading. For example, the authors refer to the "statistic network" as a "statistician," which is not accurate. The paper should prefer the more accurate term "approximate posterior."

Secondly, the language around one-shot generation (end of p.6) and the procedure in Figure 5 should be clarified. The authors should provide a clear explanation of how the one-shot generation process works and how it is evaluated.

Finally, the authors should quantify one-shot generation by reporting log p(x|c) or log p(x) on held-out datasets and compare to a vanilla VAE. This comparison is expected and provides a clear evaluation of the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Questions for the Authors**

1. Are the data-point-dependent vectors z generated from the forward model or taken from the approximate posterior?
2. Can you provide more details on the one-shot generation process and how it is evaluated?
3. Can you report log p(x|c) or log p(x) on held-out datasets and compare to a vanilla VAE?
