- Proposes a hierarchical VAE with a top-layer context unit that summarizes datasets to perform few-shot learning.
- Method effectively "learns to learn" and infers distributions from small numbers of examples (few-shot).
- Conceptually simple, elegant, and unsupervised, simpler than many recent one-shot methods.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and enjoyable to read.
- Empirical performance is good with well-chosen experiments demonstrating effectiveness.
- Results are solid and provide a useful contribution to machine learning.
- Paper title/name "Neural Statistician" is overly grandiose and potentially misleading.
- Presentation issues: Spatial MNIST inputs in Figure 4 appear denser than expected and may not reflect few-sample scenarios.
- Figure 4’s red points do not seem to correspond to the claimed meaningful points.
- Reviewer requests code release to facilitate further research and experimentation.
- Decision: Accept.
- Reasons for decision: novel and effective approach; conceptually simple and unsupervised.
- Suggested additions: more implementation details (network architectures, hyperparameters) and more experiments on varied tasks/datasets.
- Questions for authors: provide implementation details, report results on other datasets/tasks, and state plans/timeline for releasing code.
