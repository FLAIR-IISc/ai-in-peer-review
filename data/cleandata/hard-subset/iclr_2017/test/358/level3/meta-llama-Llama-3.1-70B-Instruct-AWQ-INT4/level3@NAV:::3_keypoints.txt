- Proposes a VAE variant with dataset-level latent variables ("neural statistician") to learn unsupervised dataset summary statistics.  
- Enables applications such as dataset clustering, generative model transfer, representative sample selection, and classification of unseen classes.  
- Decision: Accept — model is well-motivated, clearly described, and addresses an important problem.  
- Experimental results demonstrate effectiveness on tasks like few-shot learning and dataset summarization.  
- Paper gives a thorough introduction and situates the approach well in related work.  
- Model architecture and training procedure are clearly and concisely described.  
- Experiments are comprehensive and support the method’s effectiveness.  
- Terminology concern: the term "statistician" is misleading and should be replaced (e.g., "approximate posterior").  
- Clarify language and procedure around one-shot generation (end of page 6 and Figure 5).  
- Clarify whether data-point-dependent vectors z are sampled from the generative model or taken from the approximate posterior.  
- Request detailed explanation of how log p(x|c) or log p(x) on held-out datasets is computed and how it compares to a vanilla VAE.  
- Suggest quantifying one-shot generation by reporting log-likelihoods on held-out data and comparing to a vanilla VAE.  
- Suggest providing more details on hyperparameter tuning and computational resources required for training.
