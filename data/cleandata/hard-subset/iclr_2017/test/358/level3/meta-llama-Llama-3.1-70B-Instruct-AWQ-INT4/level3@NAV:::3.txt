**Summary of the Paper's Contribution**

The paper proposes a variant of the Variational Autoencoder (VAE) that incorporates dataset-level latent variables, extending the traditional VAE's simple graphical structure. This approach, referred to as the "neural statistician," enables the learning of summary statistics of datasets without supervision. The model can be used for various tasks, including clustering datasets, transferring generative models to new datasets, selecting representative samples of datasets, and classifying previously unseen classes.

**Decision**

Based on the review, I decide to **Accept** the paper, with the main reasons being:

1. The paper proposes a well-motivated and clearly described extension of the VAE model, which addresses a significant problem in machine learning.
2. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on various tasks, including few-shot learning and dataset summarization.

**Additional Feedback**

While the paper is well-written and the results are impressive, there are a few areas that require clarification:

1. The terminology used in the paper, specifically the term "statistician," is misleading and should be replaced with a more accurate term, such as "approximate posterior."
2. The language around one-shot generation (end of page 6) and the procedure in Figure 5 should be clarified.
3. The authors should provide more details on the data-point-dependent vectors z, specifically whether they are generated from the forward model or taken from the approximate posterior.
