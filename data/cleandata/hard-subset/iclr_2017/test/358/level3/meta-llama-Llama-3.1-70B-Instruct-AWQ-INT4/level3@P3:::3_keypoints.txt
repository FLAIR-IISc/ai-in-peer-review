- Proposes a novel VAE extension ("neural statistician") with dataset-level latent variables to learn representations of entire datasets.  
- Well-motivated for few-shot learning and transfer learning by capturing dataset similarities and differences.  
- Model description, graphical structure, and approximate inference are clearly and thoroughly presented.  
- Extensive experiments on synthetic data, MNIST, OMNIGLOT, and YouTube Faces demonstrate effectiveness in clustering, few-shot learning, and image generation.  
- Authors provided a clarifying response and extra results on one-shot generation, but reviewer still has concerns.  
- Terminology ("statistician"/"statistic network") may be misleading; recommend clearer terms like "approximate posterior" or "dataset representation network."  
- Request more quantitative evaluation of one-shot generation (e.g., log likelihood/log probability) for objective comparison.  
- Ask to clarify whether data-point-dependent vectors z come from the forward model or the approximate posterior.  
- Suggest reporting log p(x|c) or log p(x) on held-out datasets and comparing to a vanilla VAE to quantify one-shot performance.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions due to novelty, solid experiments, and potential impact.
