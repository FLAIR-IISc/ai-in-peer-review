- Proposes a hierarchical generative model ("neural statistician") that learns dataset-level representations using a higher-level latent and example-level latents.  
- Introduces a "double" variational bound to capture structure within and across datasets.  
- Builds on the VAE framework and extends it from modeling datapoints to modeling entire datasets.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on clustering, transfer learning, and few-shot learning tasks.  
- Shows strong performance on complex, structured data (e.g., images) with promising few-shot results and thorough comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Mathematical derivations and presentation of the double variational bound are clear and sound.  
- Main concern: unclear training details for the statistic network—whether minibatches are used and if they yield an unbiased gradient estimator.  
- Potential issue: using minibatches might prevent learning true dataset-wide features and limit model expressiveness.  
- Suggests exploring additional applications (e.g., hierarchical forecasting for electricity or sales) to further validate the approach.  
- Recommends clarifications and additional experiments to better characterize strengths and limitations.  
- Overall assessment: significant and widely applicable contribution to deep learning; decision: Accept.  
- Primary reasons for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach; broadly applicable double variational bound; promising few-shot learning results.  
- Supporting points: clear methodology and derivations, empirical effectiveness across tasks, and potential for broad applicability.  
- Questions for authors: clarify statistic network training/minibatch use and impact, address need for many datasets to learn representations, and discuss interpretability and downstream use of learned representations.
