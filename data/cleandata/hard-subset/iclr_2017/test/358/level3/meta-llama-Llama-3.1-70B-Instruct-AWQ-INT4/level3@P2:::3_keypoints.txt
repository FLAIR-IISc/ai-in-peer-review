- Proposal: Introduces a VAE extension with a dataset-level latent variable ("context") to represent datasets rather than individual datapoints.  
- Benefit: Context captures dataset structure and enables learning a generative process per dataset for transfer and few-shot tasks.  
- Empirical claims: Demonstrated on clustering datasets, transferring generative models to new datasets, selecting representative samples, and classifying unseen classes.  
- Decision: Accept — well-motivated, clearly described extension addressing a key VAE limitation with generally solid experiments.  
- Main contribution: Dataset-level latent variable as a significant improvement over traditional per-datapoint VAEs.  
- Experiments appraisal: Experiments are diverse and generally well-executed, supporting the method’s flexibility and effectiveness.  
- Terminology issue: Calling the "statistic network" a "statistician" is misleading; prefer "approximate posterior" or "statistic network."  
- Clarity request: One-shot generation description (end of p.6) and the procedure in Figure 5 need clearer explanation and evaluation details.  
- Quantitative evaluation request: Report log p(x|c) or log p(x) on held-out datasets and compare to a vanilla VAE.  
- Questions for authors: (1) Are datapoint-dependent z sampled from the generative (forward) model or taken from the approximate posterior? (2) Provide more details on the one-shot generation procedure and its evaluation. (3) Please report log p(x|c) / log p(x) on held-out sets and compare to a vanilla VAE.
