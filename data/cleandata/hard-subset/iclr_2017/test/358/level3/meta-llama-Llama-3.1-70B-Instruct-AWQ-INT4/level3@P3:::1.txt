**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel hierarchical generative model, called the neural statistician, which can learn to compute summary statistics of datasets without supervision. The model consists of a lower level that models individual datapoints within a dataset and a higher level that models unordered sets of datasets. The core idea is to use a "double" variational bound, where a higher-level latent variable describes datasets and a lower-level latent variable describes individual examples. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on various tasks, including clustering datasets, transferring generative models to new datasets, selecting representative samples of datasets, and classifying previously unseen classes.

**Decision**

I recommend accepting this paper. The key reasons for this choice are:

1. The paper tackles an important and high-impact problem in deep learning, namely hierarchical modeling of datasets.
2. The approach is well-motivated and well-placed in the literature, drawing on ideas from variational autoencoders and transfer learning.

**Supporting arguments**

The paper presents a clear and well-structured argument for the need for hierarchical modeling of datasets. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on various tasks, including clustering datasets, transferring generative models to new datasets, selecting representative samples of datasets, and classifying previously unseen classes. The results, particularly the few-shot learning results, look impressive and demonstrate the potential of the approach.

The "double" variational bound idea is well-presented and appears widely applicable. The authors provide a clear explanation of the model and its components, including the statistic network, which is a key innovation of the paper.

**Additional feedback**

One potential concern is that training the statistic network on minibatches may prevent learning dataset-wide features, limiting the model's expressiveness. The authors should consider addressing this concern in future work.

Another suggestion is to explore hierarchical forecasting (e.g., electricity or sales) as a practical application of the neural statistician. This could provide a useful demonstration of the model's capabilities in a real-world setting.

**Questions for the authors**

1. Are minibatches used when training the statistic network, and if so, do they give an unbiased estimator of the full gradient?
2. How do the authors plan to address the potential concern about training the statistic network on minibatches limiting the model's expressiveness?
3. Can the authors provide more details on the experimental setup and hyperparameter choices for the few-shot learning tasks?

**Additional references**
