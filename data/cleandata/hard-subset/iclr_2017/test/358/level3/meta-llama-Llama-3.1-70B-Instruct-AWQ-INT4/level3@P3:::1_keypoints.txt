- Proposes the neural statistician, a hierarchical generative model that learns dataset summary statistics without supervision.  
- Model comprises a lower level for individual datapoints and a higher level for unordered sets, using a "double" variational bound.  
- Demonstrated on clustering datasets, transferring generative models to new datasets, selecting representative samples, and classifying unseen classes.  
- Recommendation: accept—addresses an important, high-impact problem in hierarchical dataset modeling.  
- Approach is well-motivated and well-placed in the VAE/transfer learning literature.  
- Empirical results, particularly few-shot learning, are impressive and indicate strong potential.  
- The double variational bound and the statistic network are key innovations and are clearly presented.  
- Concern: training the statistic network on minibatches may impede learning dataset-wide features and limit expressiveness.  
- Suggestion: explore practical applications such as hierarchical forecasting (e.g., electricity or sales) to demonstrate real-world utility.  
- Question: Are minibatches used for training the statistic network, and do they provide an unbiased estimator of the full gradient?  
- Question: How will the authors address the minibatch expressiveness concern, and can they provide more experimental/hyperparameter details for the few-shot tasks?  
- Recommendation to cite relevant prior work: Kingma et al. (2014), Rezende et al. (2014), and Vinyals et al. (2016).
