**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

This paper proposes a hierarchical generative model for learning representations of datasets, rather than individual datapoints. The model, called the Neural Statistician, uses a "double" variational bound to learn a lower-level latent space for individual datapoints and a higher-level latent space for datasets. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on several tasks, including clustering, transferring generative models to new datasets, selecting representative samples of datasets, and few-shot learning.

**Decision: Accept**

I decide to accept this paper for several reasons. Firstly, the paper tackles an important and high-impact problem in deep learning, namely hierarchical modeling of datasets. The authors provide a well-motivated approach that is grounded in the literature, and their experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of their method.

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper's contributions are significant, and the authors provide a clear and well-written explanation of their approach. The use of a "double" variational bound is a novel and interesting idea that appears to be widely applicable. The experimental results, particularly the few-shot learning results, are impressive and demonstrate the potential of the Neural Statistician model.

**Additional Feedback**

One potential concern with the paper is the use of minibatches when training the statistic network. The authors should clarify whether minibatches are used and, if so, whether they provide an unbiased estimator of the full gradient. Additionally, the authors may want to consider exploring hierarchical forecasting as a practical application of their approach, as suggested in the reviewer guidelines.

**Questions for the Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how the minibatches are used when training the statistic network, and whether they provide an unbiased estimator of the full gradient?
2. How do you plan to address the potential limitation of the model requiring a large number of datasets to learn useful representations?
3. Can you provide more information on the computational resources required to train the Neural Statistician model, and whether it can be scaled to larger datasets?
