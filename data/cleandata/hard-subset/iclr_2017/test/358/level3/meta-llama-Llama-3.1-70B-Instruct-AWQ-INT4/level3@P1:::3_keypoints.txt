- Proposes a novel VAE extension that learns dataset-level representations via a dataset latent variable called the "context."
- Introduces the "neural statistician" with an inference network to approximate the posterior over the dataset-level latent.
- Main contribution: a hierarchical generative model that represents datasets as wholes, grounded in VAE and graphical model literature.
- Evaluated on clustering, transfer learning, and one-shot generation across multiple datasets.
- Terminology concern: calling the method a "statistician" and the inference a "statistic network" is misleading; better to use "approximate posterior network."
- Experimental evaluation is generally strong, and author responses clarified one-shot generation aspects.
- Clarity needed on one-shot generation wording (end of p.6) and the procedure depicted in Figure 5.
- Requests more quantitative one-shot metrics (report log p(x|c) or log p(x) on held-out sets) and comparison to a vanilla VAE.
- Implementation ambiguity: unclear whether data-point-dependent vectors z come from the generative model or the approximate posterior.
- Suggests exploring applications to more complex, multimodal, or nonlinear datasets in future work.
- Recommends investigating more powerful inference networks (e.g., normalizing flows) for improved posterior approximation.
- Decision: Accept — significant contribution, clear motivation, and generally well-done experiments.
- Questions for authors: clarify z generation implementation and how they will address limitations like needing many datasets or handling complex datasets.
