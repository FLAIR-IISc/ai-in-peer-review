- Proposes the Neural Statistician, a hierarchical generative model that represents and generates entire datasets rather than individual datapoints.  
- Introduces a "double" variational bound to model both example-level latents and dataset-level latents for unordered sets.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on few-shot learning, clustering, and generating new datasets.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on the paper’s contributions and evaluation.  
- Tackles an important, under-explored problem (hierarchical modeling of datasets) with potential for significant impact.  
- The double variational bound is well-presented and appears broadly applicable.  
- Contributions are substantial, clearly motivated, and a natural extension of point-wise generative models.  
- Experimental results are impressive, especially for few-shot learning; clustering and dataset generation results are promising.  
- Request for more details on the training procedure, particularly the minibatching scheme for the statistic network and whether it yields an unbiased gradient estimator.  
- Suggests exploring practical applications like hierarchical forecasting (e.g., electricity or sales forecasting).  
- Requests more comprehensive comparisons to existing few-shot learning and clustering methods.  
- Asks authors to clarify minibatching bias, report any experiments on hierarchical forecasting, and compare performance and computational efficiency to prior methods.
