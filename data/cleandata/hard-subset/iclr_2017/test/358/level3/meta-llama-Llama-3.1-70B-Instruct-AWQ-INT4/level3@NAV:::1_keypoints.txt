- Proposes the Neural Statistician: a hierarchical generative model that learns dataset-level representations using a "double" variational bound.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on clustering, transferring generative models to new datasets, selecting representative samples, and few-shot learning.
- Decision: Accept.
- Tackles an important, high-impact problem—hierarchical modeling of datasets.
- Method is well-motivated and grounded in prior work.
- The "double" variational bound is a novel idea with broad applicability.
- Experimental results are strong and especially impressive for few-shot learning.
- Writing and exposition are clear and well presented.
- Concern: unclear whether minibatches are used to train the statistic network and if they yield an unbiased estimator of the full gradient.
- Suggestion: consider exploring hierarchical forecasting as a practical application.
- Questions for authors: clarify minibatch usage/unbiasedness; how to handle requirement for many datasets; computational resources and scalability.
