This paper proposes a novel approach to learning representations of datasets using a hierarchical variational autoencoder (VAE) with a top-layer context unit that summarizes a dataset to predict from sets of points. The method effectively "learns to learn," acquiring the ability to infer distributions from small numbers of examples, a phenomenon known as few-shot learning. The approach is conceptually simple, elegant, and unsupervised, making it simpler than many recent one-shot methods.

The paper is well-written and enjoyable to read, with a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method and its underlying principles. The empirical performance of the method is good, with well-chosen experiments that demonstrate its effectiveness in various tasks, including few-shot learning. The results are solid, and the paper provides a useful contribution to the field of machine learning.

However, I have some minor issues with the paper. The title and name of the proposed method, "Neural Statistician," seem overly grandiose and misleading. While the method is indeed able to learn summary statistics of datasets, the name suggests a level of sophistication and generality that may not be entirely accurate.

Additionally, I have some nitpicks with the presentation of the results. For example, the Spatial MNIST dataset is a useful toy benchmark, but the inputs in Figure 4 appear denser than expected, and the model might be expected to recognize distributions with fewer samples. Furthermore, the red points in Figure 4 do not appear to correspond to the meaningful points claimed in the text.

Overall, I believe that this paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of machine learning and that the proposed method has the potential to be widely applicable. I would like to see the authors release the code for the method to facilitate further research and experimentation.
