- Proposes a hierarchical VAE with a top-layer context unit that summarizes datasets for set-to-point prediction (few-shot learning).
- Method effectively "learns to learn," enabling inference of distributions from small example sets.
- Approach is conceptually simple, elegant, unsupervised, and simpler than many recent one-shot methods.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on strong experimental support and clear presentation.
- Paper is well-motivated and well-placed in literature, building on VAEs and transfer learning with thorough related-work discussion.
- Empirical results are strong across multiple datasets (synthetic 1-D, spatial MNIST, OMNIGLOT, YouTube Faces).
- Demonstrates abilities to cluster datasets, transfer generative models, select representative samples, and classify unseen classes.
- Minor weakness: the title "neural statistician" may be overly grandiose or slightly misleading.
- Minor issues with spatial MNIST visuals (denser inputs than expected; red points not matching claimed meaning).
- Suggested improvements: release code for reproducibility and evaluate on more challenging, high-dimensional datasets.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution to few-shot learning, well-motivated, executed, and evaluated.
- Paper specifically tackles few-shot learning; approach is well-motivated; claims are supported by experiments.
