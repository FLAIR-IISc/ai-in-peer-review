- Introduces Doc2VecC, a novel document representation method that averages word embeddings with corruption-based regularization.  
- Claims Doc2VecC outperforms Word2Vec, Paragraph Vectors, and Skip-thought Vectors on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing strong motivation and literature grounding.  
- Corruption model is well-explained and argued to provide data-dependent regularization favoring informative/rare words.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation across multiple tasks showing significant performance gains over baselines.  
- Analysis shows the corruption mechanism dampens embeddings of common, non-discriminative words.  
- Suggests adding details on hyperparameter tuning procedures.  
- Requests more detailed comparisons with LSTM-based methods for semantic relatedness.  
- Recommends analysis of learned word embeddings and document representations to interpret model behavior.  
- Asks for clarification on how corruption rate q affects performance and what optimal q values are per task.  
- Queries how out-of-vocabulary words are handled and how vocabulary size impacts performance.  
- Requests information on computational resources and efficiency compared to other methods.
