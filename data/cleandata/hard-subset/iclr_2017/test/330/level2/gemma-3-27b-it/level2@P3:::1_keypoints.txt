- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Proposal: Doc2VecC — learn document representations by averaging word embeddings with a corruption mechanism as a regularizer.
- Motivation: Decouple model complexity from corpus size and improve test-time efficiency compared to methods like Paragraph Vectors.
- Corruption mechanism: Data-dependent regularizer that suppresses less informative words and improves embedding quality.
- Empirical performance: Strong results on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness, often matching or exceeding state-of-the-art.
- Efficiency: Significant reported training and testing speed gains.
- Comparisons and validation: Thorough comparisons to baselines (Paragraph Vectors, Skip-thought) and ablation studies support claims.
- Strengths: Clear motivation, sound methodology, and convincing empirical evaluation.
- Missing justification: No rationale given for choosing vocabulary cutoff of 10 on the Wikipedia dataset.
- Missing analysis: No discussion of limitations or dataset/document types where Doc2VecC might underperform.
- Unclear explanation: Need deeper analysis of why Paragraph Vectors perform poorly on the word analogy task beyond high capacity.
- Reproducibility concern: Hyperparameter settings for each experiment are not clearly reported; should be added to main text or supplementary.
