- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as averaged word embeddings with a corruption model for regularization.  
- Builds on Word2Vec/Paragraph Vectors and addresses scaling issues of Paragraph Vectors.  
- Claims simplicity, fast training, and scalability for large-scale applications.  
- Demonstrates competitive or superior performance on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.  
- Recommends accept based on novelty, justification, and empirical results.  
- Strong experimental support: comprehensive comparisons to baselines and useful ablation studies.  
- Ablation highlights the impact of the corruption model and compares to denoising autoencoders.  
- Training-time comparisons further substantiate efficiency claims.  
- Theoretical insight: connection to data-dependent regularization explains performance gains.  
- Weakness: needs clearer explanation of why Doc2VecC outperforms Skip-thought on long documents beyond RNN limitations.  
- Weakness: more detail required on hyperparameter tuning, especially the explored range for corruption rate q and justification for q=0.9.  
- Question: how much does performance depend on initializing word embeddings with pre-trained GloVe vs random initialization?
