- Paper proposes Doc2VecC: average word-embedding document representation with a corruption model that randomly removes words to induce data-dependent regularization and claims improved embeddings, accuracy, and efficiency.

- Recommendation: reject — approach is simple and efficient but evidence for core claims is insufficient and theoretical justification is weak.

- Theoretical justification for the corruption mechanism is inadequate; derivation (Eq.7) does not convincingly show why the regularization improves document representations over standard techniques like Word2Vec+subsampling.

- Comparison to Paragraph Vectors is misleading because Paragraph Vectors’ poor word-analogy performance (attributed to high capacity) undermines the claim that Doc2VecC is fundamentally better rather than exploiting an implementation weakness.

- The "data-dependent regularization" claim is overstated: suppression of common words looks similar to subsampling effects in Word2Vec and the paper does not show this suppression actually improves document representations.

- Experimental design is limited: missing direct comparisons to simple averaging baselines (e.g., Word2Vec+AVG with the same settings) and insufficient evidence that efficiency gains justify the new model.

- Need a rigorous empirical comparison between Doc2VecC and Word2Vec+AVG with subsampling to validate the claimed advantages.

- Theoretical section should be simplified and focused to clearly explain how corruption specifically improves document representation beyond standard Word2Vec techniques.

- The claim that Doc2VecC yields significantly better word embeddings than Word2Vec is not fully supported; improvements in word-analogy tasks are shown but their relevance to document tasks is not demonstrated.

- Clarify relationship to denoising autoencoders (DEA): paper states Doc2VecC reduces to DEA in a special case but treats DEA as a separate baseline, creating confusion about novelty.

- Question: how does corruption rate q affect performance and why was q=0.9 chosen, given no sensitivity analysis is provided?

- Question: why does Paragraph Vectors perform poorly on word analogy and how does that relate to document representation performance?

- Question: how does Doc2VecC’s data-dependent regularization compare to subsampling in Word2Vec in terms of performance and computational cost?

- Question: why does Doc2VecC outperform Skip-thought on IMDB but underperform on SICK — is paragraph length a limiting factor and how significant is this limitation?
