- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as averaged word embeddings with a corruption model providing data-dependent regularization.  
- Claims state-of-the-art performance on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness while being highly efficient at train/test time.  
- Key innovations: decouples model complexity from corpus size, suppresses common non-discriminative words via regularization, enables efficient test-time representation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over baselines, e.g., ~15% better than BoW on sentiment tasks, and much faster training/testing.  
- Theoretical analysis of the corruption model as a data-dependent regularizer is solid and explains model behavior.  
- Main limitation: insufficient exploration of how the corruption rate q affects performance; reviewer requests a table/figure across q values.  
- Needs clearer discussion of trade-offs versus LSTM/sequence-based methods: outperforms Skip-thought on long docs (IMDB) but slightly underperforms on short sentences (SICK).  
- Paper is well-motivated, well-positioned in related work, reproducible, and practically advantageous due to simplicity.  
- Suggestion: add brief analysis of corruption-rate impact and clarify strengths/weaknesses for varying document lengths.  
- Question for authors: how does the corruption model interact with subsampling of frequent words (sec 4.2), and does the model’s regularization reduce the need for subsampling?
