- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as simple averages of word embeddings trained with a corruption mechanism that randomly removes words.

- Corruption acts as data-dependent regularization, amplifying informative/rare words and suppressing common/non-discriminative words.

- Addresses Paragraph Vectors’ drawbacks (high parameter complexity, expensive inference) and bag-of-words’ lack of semantics with a simpler, efficient model.

- Empirical results show Doc2VecC matches or outperforms state-of-the-art on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.

- Demonstrates substantial efficiency gains in training and test-time representation generation, with timing comparisons supporting the claim.

- Theoretical justification framing corruption as data-dependent regularization is persuasive and supported by experiments.

- Recommendation: paper should be accepted — core contribution is well-motivated and empirically validated across multiple tasks.

- Suggest clarifying the relationship to standard dropout and explicitly explaining how the unbiased corruption in eq.(1) differs from dropout.

- Request a clearer rationale or sensitivity analysis for choosing q = 0.9 as the corruption rate.

- Ask for an explicit discussion distinguishing Doc2VecC’s corruption from Denoising Autoencoder corruption and elaboration on the claimed reduction to DEA.

- Recommend deeper analysis of the learned word embeddings to show how corruption affects their semantic properties beyond analogy results.

- Encourage explaining why Paragraph Vectors perform surprisingly poorly on a dataset beyond attributing it to model capacity.

- Ask for concrete evidence connecting the improved word embeddings to improved document representations (how averaging + corruption yields better docs).
