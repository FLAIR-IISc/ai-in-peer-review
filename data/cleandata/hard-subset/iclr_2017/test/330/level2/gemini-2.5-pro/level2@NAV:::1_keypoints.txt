- Introduces Doc2VecC: represents a document as the average of its word embeddings with this enforced via a corruption mechanism.  
- Theoretically motivated: random word-dropping acts as a data-dependent regularizer that penalizes common words and favors rare, informative ones.  
- Main contributions: high efficiency, a principled corruption-based regularization technique, and competitive/state-of-the-art performance on downstream tasks.  
- Recommendation: Accept—based on the novelty of the corruption-as-regularizer idea and robust experimental validation.  
- Strength: simplicity and clear theoretical grounding (Section 3.1 shows approximate equivalence to adding a specific penalty term).  
- Experiments: comprehensive evaluation across benchmarks; shows competitive accuracy and substantial training/inference efficiency gains over Paragraph Vectors and Skip-thought (Table 2).  
- Validation: qualitative embedding analysis (Table 3) and word analogy results support the regularization claims.  
- Suggestion: fix title typo ("MENTS THROUGH CORRUPTION").  
- Suggestion: include a small sensitivity analysis for the corruption rate q (currently fixed at 0.9) to guide practitioners.  
- Suggestion: clean up inconsistent reference formatting.  
- Question: clarify how the high regularization factor q/(1-q) (9 when q=0.9) affects training stability and final embedding norms.  
- Question: clarify whether frequent-word subsampling was disabled or used alongside the corruption model in experiments to isolate effects.
