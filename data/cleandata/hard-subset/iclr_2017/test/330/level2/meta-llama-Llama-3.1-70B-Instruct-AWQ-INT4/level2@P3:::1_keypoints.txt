- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as simple averages of word embeddings with a corruption model.  
- Corruption introduces data-dependent regularization that favors informative/rare words and pushes common/non-discriminative word embeddings toward zero.  
- Claims Doc2VecC produces better word embeddings than Word2Vec and outperforms several state-of-the-art document representation methods.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Key reason: novel, efficient framework that outperforms baselines on multiple tasks.  
- Key reason: corruption-based regularization is a desirable property for document representations.  
- Empirical support: strong results on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.  
- Theoretical support: analysis showing corruption induces data-dependent regularization.  
- Comparative support: shown to outperform Word2Vec, Paragraph Vectors, and Skip-thought Vectors.
