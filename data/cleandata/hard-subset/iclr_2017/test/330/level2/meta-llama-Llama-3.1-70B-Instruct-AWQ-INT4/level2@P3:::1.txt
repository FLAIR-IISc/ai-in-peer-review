**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper introduces an efficient document representation learning framework called Document Vector through Corruption (Doc2VecC). This framework represents each document as a simple average of word embeddings, which captures the semantic meanings of the document during learning. A corruption model is included, which introduces a data-dependent regularization that favors informative or rare words while forcing the embeddings of common and non-discriminative ones to be close to zero. The authors claim that Doc2VecC produces significantly better word embeddings than Word2Vec and outperforms several state-of-the-art document representation learning algorithms on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness tasks.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

Based on the review, I would **accept** this paper. The key reasons for this decision are:

1. The paper introduces a novel and efficient document representation learning framework that outperforms several state-of-the-art algorithms on multiple tasks.
2. The corruption model used in Doc2VecC provides a data-dependent regularization that favors informative or rare words, which is a desirable property for document representation learning.
