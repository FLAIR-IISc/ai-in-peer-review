- Proposes Doc2VecC: efficient document representation by averaging word embeddings with a corruption mechanism enforcing data-dependent regularization.  
- Corruption favors informative/rare words and shrinks common/non-discriminative word embeddings toward zero.  
- Reports better word embeddings than Word2Vec and superior document representations on sentiment analysis, classification, and semantic relatedness.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Main reasons for acceptance: model is well-motivated with clear literature connections, and evaluation is thorough and demonstrates effectiveness and efficiency.  
- Paper clearly explains the model, corruption mechanism, and regularization.  
- Related work is well-reviewed and situates the method in context.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and compares against strong baselines.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of the corruption mechanism’s impact on learned embeddings.  
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis of which words are affected by corruption and how semantic relationships change.  
- Suggestion: include experiments assessing robustness to different types of noise or input corruption.  
- Suggestion: give more details on hyperparameter tuning ranges and selection criteria.  
- Questions: how is the corruption implemented in practice, how are words chosen and what are corruption probabilities?  
- Questions: how is the optimal corruption rate q determined, and is it task- or dataset-dependent?  
- Questions: have the authors tried Doc2VecC on other tasks (e.g., QA, MT), and what were the results?
