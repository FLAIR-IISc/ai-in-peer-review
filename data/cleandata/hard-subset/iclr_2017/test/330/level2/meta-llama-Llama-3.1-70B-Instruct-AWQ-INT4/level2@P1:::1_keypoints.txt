- Proposes Doc2VecC: a novel, efficient document representation that averages word embeddings with a corruption-based regularization.  
- Corruption model provides data-dependent regularization that favors informative/rare words and suppresses common, non-discriminative ones.  
- Captures global document semantics while reducing noise from non-discriminative words; method is well-motivated and literature-grounded.  
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art methods (Word2Vec, Paragraph Vectors, Skip-thought) on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.  
- Paper provides a clear and thorough explanation of architecture, training objective, and regularization mechanism.  
- Analysis demonstrates the regularization mechanism’s effect in favoring informative words.  
- Experimental results are impressive with detailed comparisons to competing methods.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty, efficiency, strong empirical results, and clarity.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity, including time and memory breakdowns despite claiming high training speed.  
- Suggested improvements: add detailed complexity analysis and more interpretability insights (visualizations or example documents).  
- Specific questions: provide details on the corruption sampling distribution; analyze effects of hyperparameters (corruption rate, embedding dimensionality); offer interpretability examples.
