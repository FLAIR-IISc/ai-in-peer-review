- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as averages of word embeddings with a corruption model that regularizes toward informative/rare words.
- Claims Doc2VecC produces superior word embeddings to Word2Vec and outperforms several document representation methods.
- Experiments on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness show improved accuracy and efficiency over baselines (Word2Vec, Paragraph Vectors, Denoising Autoencoders).
- Corruption model introduces data-dependent regularization that favors informative/rare words and forces common/non-discriminative embeddings toward zero.
- Simple, scalable model enables training on large datasets and efficient representation of unseen documents.
- Paper demonstrates good field knowledge and situates the approach within related work.
- Novel contribution: corruption-based regularization that biases representations toward informative words.
- Provides sufficient experimental and methodological details for reproducibility (setup, hyperparameters, metrics).
- Limitation: potential bias toward rare words and limited evaluation across datasets.
- Suggestion: provide more detailed analysis of the corruption model's effect on embeddings.
- Suggestion: evaluate on additional datasets to demonstrate generalizability.
- Suggestion: compare against transformer-based models such as BERT.
- Reviewer recommends accepting the paper for publication.
- Question: request more detailed analysis of how the corruption model affects word embeddings.
- Question: how does the framework handle out-of-vocabulary words?
- Question: provide details on computational resources needed for training and testing.
