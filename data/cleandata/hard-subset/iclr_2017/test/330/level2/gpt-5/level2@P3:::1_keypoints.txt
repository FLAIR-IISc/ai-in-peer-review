- Proposes Doc2VecC: average word embeddings with unbiased dropout corruption on global bag-of-words to learn document vectors efficiently.  
- Corruption induces a data-dependent regularizer (via second-order Taylor expansion) that downweights frequent non-informative words.  
- Retains Word2Vec-like training efficiency and enables very fast inference for unseen documents.  
- Empirical results: strong/state-of-the-art on IMDB sentiment and Wikipedia classification, competitive on SICK, and better word embeddings on analogy tasks.  
- Demonstrates substantial training and inference speed advantages and provides code for reproducibility.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: combination of simplicity, clear motivation, theoretical grounding, strong empirical performance, and practical efficiency.  
- Positions method between Word2Vec and Paragraph Vectors, distinguishing prior global-context averaging by introducing corruption.  
- Connects analysis to dropout/marginalized autoencoder literature for principled regularization interpretation.  
- Method is straightforward to implement with clear specification of corruption scaling and negative sampling training.  
- Theoretical insight explains why frequent words are downweighted without heuristic IDF.  
- IMDB: matches/outperforms Paragraph Vectors and beats BoW, Word2Vec+IDF, DAE with notable runtime gains.  
- Wikipedia classification: consistent gains across representation sizes and qualitative t-SNE clustering support.  
- SICK relatedness: competitive with complex models (LSTM, skip-thought) while being much faster and simpler.  
- Word analogy: Doc2VecC embeddings outperform Word2Vec, with gains increasing with data/embedding size.  
- Reproducibility: code and key hyperparameters (e.g., q=0.9) provided.  
- Essential clarification needed: specify exact Paragraph Vector variants, settings, and inference details for fair comparison.  
- Essential clarification needed: state negative sampling distribution (unigram^0.75 vs uniform) and number of negatives used across methods.  
- Ablation requests: sensitivity to corruption rate q, window size k, and a no-corruption baseline to isolate gains from corruption.  
- Normalization concern: analyze 1/T scaling of global context for variable-length documents or alternate normalizations.  
- Experimental fairness: clarify pretraining vs dataset-only training (especially for SICK and comparisons to skip-thought).  
- Suggest adding skip-thought baseline with chunking+pooling for long IMDB reviews.  
- Nice-to-have: report wall-clock training details, memory footprint, and hardware/optimizer settings for comparisons.  
- Nice-to-have: evaluate on more datasets (20 Newsgroups, Yelp, AG News) to show domain robustness.  
- Nice-to-have: explore lightweight test-time token-wise weighting derived from the regularizer for potential gains.  
- Questions: exact negative sampling distribution and parity across methods?  
- Questions: which PV variants/hyperparameters were used and how was PV tuned?  
- Questions: sensitivity to q/k and failure modes for very short/very long documents?  
- Questions: does skipping Mikolov & Dean subsampling harm Doc2VecC or does regularizer replace its effect?  
- Questions: how much of SICK gains come from book-corpus pretraining vs SICK-only training?
