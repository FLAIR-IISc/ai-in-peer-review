- Proposes document representation as average of word embeddings with data-dependent regularization that upweights rare/informative words and drives common words toward zero.  
- Reports lowest error rates vs. baseline embedding methods on sentiment analysis and document classification.  
- Reviewer appreciates the motivation but finds the technical contribution limited since many techniques are not novel; main strengths are simplicity and speed.  
- Requests additional evaluation on more than two tasks to be convinced of general superiority.  
- Asks for clarification on the RNN-LM: was it trained as a language model or to minimize classification error, and was the final hidden state or the average of hidden states used as the representation?  
- Suggests comparing against a bidirectional LSTM that concatenates final hidden states, a widely used baseline for document representation.
