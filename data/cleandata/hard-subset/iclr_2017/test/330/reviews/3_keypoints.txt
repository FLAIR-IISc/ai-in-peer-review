- Proposes learning document embeddings as the sum of constituent word embeddings, with joint learning and random dropout of words during training.
- The model components (joint word/document training and sum-of-words representation) are not novel and have prior precedent.
- The corruption mechanism is essentially standard input-layer dropout rather than a new technique.
- Training uses a word2vec-style loss and established training methods, offering little methodological novelty.
- The approach yields very efficient document generation, requiring only an average of word embeddings instead of expensive inference (e.g., Doc2Vec).
- By construction, embeddings capture global document information that helps local-context prediction.
- Empirical results are strong: encouraging performance on sentiment analysis and document classification tasks.
- Despite limited novelty, the simplicity, efficiency, and performance justify wider attention; reviewer recommends acceptance.
