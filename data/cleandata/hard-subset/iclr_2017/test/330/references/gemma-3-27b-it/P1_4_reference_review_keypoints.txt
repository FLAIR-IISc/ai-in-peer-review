- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as the average of word embeddings with random word "corruption" during training.  
- Claims advantages: data-dependent regularization, decoupled model complexity from corpus size, Word2Vec-like efficiency, and fast inference.  
- Empirical results: matches or outperforms state-of-the-art on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.  
- Decision: Recommend acceptance.  
- Novelty: Simple but powerful idea linking averaging with corruption and compositional semantics.  
- Efficiency: Training and inference significantly faster than Paragraph Vectors, suitable for large-scale data.  
- Theoretical grounding: Corruption interpreted as data-dependent regularization with a derived regularization term.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written with clear motivation, related work, and experimental setup.  
- Ablation suggestion: Need detailed study isolating impact of different corruption rates (q).  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Request more detail on sensitivity to embedding size, window size, etc.  
- Regularization analysis: Recommend comparing embeddings with/without corruption (visualizations, norms for frequent vs rare words).  
- Limitations: Ask for explicit discussion of weaknesses (e.g., very short docs, specialized vocabulary).  
- Reproducibility: Encourage well-documented, easily reproducible code release.  
- Questions for authors: clarify experiments with q values other than 0.9; why choose simple averaging over other composition functions; why Paragraph Vectors underperform on analogy tasks; and impact of fine-tuning on SICK dataset performance.
