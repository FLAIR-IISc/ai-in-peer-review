- Summary: Proposes Doc2VecC — represent documents by averaging word embeddings with random-word “corruption” during training, yielding efficient, fast-inference document vectors; frames corruption as data-dependent regularization.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Motivation & novelty: Addresses scalability and inference-speed issues of existing methods; corruption mechanism and its regularization analysis add novelty.
- Empirical results: Strong performance across sentiment, classification, and semantic relatedness tasks, often beating Paragraph Vectors and matching more complex models; includes timing comparisons.
- Theoretical justification: Derives corruption as a form of regularization and explains its effect on word embeddings.
- Writing quality: Clear, concise exposition with helpful notation and figures.
- Ablation study needed: Recommend evaluating performance sensitivity to corruption rate (q).
- Baseline comparison: Suggest adding simple baselines like averaging pre-trained GloVe/fastText embeddings to quantify training benefits.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Ask for discussion/analysis of embedding dimension, window size, etc.
- Computational details: Request more information on resources (GPU type, memory) to aid reproducibility.
- Limitations: Suggest explicitly discussing weaknesses (e.g., losing long-range/word-order information vs. RNNs).
- Q — Corruption intuition: Ask for deeper explanation of why random removal yields more robust/informative embeddings and links to dropout.
- Q — Corpus size impact: Request analysis/plots of performance and training time as corpus size increases (Doc2VecC vs Paragraph Vectors).
- Q — Negative sampling: Ask if alternative negative sampling distributions (e.g., frequency-based) were tested and effects observed.
- Q — Word order: Ask how loss of word order affects tasks that need it and whether positional embeddings or other fixes could help.
