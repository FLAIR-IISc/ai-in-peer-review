- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as averaged word embeddings with random-word "corruption" during training for regularization and speed.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Empirical strength: Competitive or superior results on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness, especially on Wikipedia classification.  
- Efficiency: Much faster at test time than Paragraph Vectors by avoiding costly inference.  
- Theoretical grounding: Corruption acts as data-dependent regularization; Taylor expansion rationale provided.  
- Writing & motivation: Clear exposition and strong motivation building on Word2Vec/Paragraph Vectors.  
- Request more hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (embedding size, window size, etc.) and rationale for choices.  
- Recommend an ablation study comparing with/without corruption to isolate its contribution.  
- Suggest qualitative analyses (e.g., t-SNE/PCA of embeddings) to interpret learned representations.  
- Related work: expand discussion to contextualized embeddings (ELMo, BERT) and contrast with Doc2VecC.  
- Code/reproducibility: clarify preprocessing steps and provide implementation details in the repository.  
- Question: How does corruption interact with OOV words—are they always removed or treated differently?  
- Question: What are limitations when scaling to extremely large datasets (billions of documents)?  
- Question: How does window size affect Doc2VecC performance; were different sizes evaluated?
