- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as the average of word embeddings with random-word corruption during training.  
- Claims Doc2VecC is computationally efficient and introduces a data-dependent regularization effect.  
- Targets limitations of Paragraph Vectors (many parameters, expensive inference) while leveraging word-embedding composition.  
- Shows competitive or superior empirical performance on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness tasks.  
- Includes runtime comparisons demonstrating efficiency and provides code availability.  
- Decision: Accept (Weak Accept) — compelling approach with solid empirical results but incremental novelty.  
- Novelty is incremental but meaningful: averaging embeddings plus corruption is a novel combination with theoretical justification.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly presented.  
- Recommendation: provide deeper empirical analysis of the proposed data-dependent regularization (e.g., visualize embeddings pre/post corruption).  
- Recommendation: include a comprehensive ablation study isolating the effect of corruption and sensitivity to corruption rate q.  
- Recommendation: report hyperparameter sensitivity (embedding dimension, learning rate) to aid reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: add qualitative error analysis showing where Doc2VecC succeeds and fails.  
- Recommendation: compare against the simple baseline of plain averaged word embeddings without corruption.  
- Questions for authors: provide empirical evidence on how corruption affects embedding distributions and norms.  
- Questions for authors: report optimal corruption rate q across datasets and its sensitivity to dataset characteristics.  
- Questions for authors: discuss scaling to massive datasets (e.g., billions of documents) and potential bottlenecks.  
- Questions for authors: quantify the computational overhead of the corruption mechanism (time to generate corrupted representations).
