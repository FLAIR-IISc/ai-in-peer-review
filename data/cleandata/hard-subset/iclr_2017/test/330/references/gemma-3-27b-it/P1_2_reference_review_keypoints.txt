- Proposes Doc2VecC: document vectors as average word embeddings with random-word "corruption" during training.  
- Claims corruption acts as data-dependent regularization leveraging Word2Vec compositionality.  
- Shows strong empirical performance on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.  
- Recommends Accept with minor revisions due to theoretical motivation and efficiency gains.  
- Novelty: insightful combination of embedding composition and corruption, well-justified relative to prior work.  
- Empirics: consistently outperforms strong baselines (e.g., Paragraph Vectors, Skip-thought) on multiple tasks.  
- Efficiency: significantly faster inference and scalability by decoupling model complexity from corpus size.  
- Theory: provides a Taylor-expansion derivation explaining the data-dependent regularization.  
- Request ablation on corruption-rate sensitivity (q values) to show robustness.  
- Ask for details on negative sampling (number of negatives) to improve reproducibility.  
- Suggest comparison to newer models (e.g., BERT/Sentence-BERT) and discussion of advantages (simplicity, efficiency).  
- Recommend visualizing learned word embeddings and comparing them to Word2Vec to assess context effects.  
- Ask for a more intuitive, example-driven explanation of how regularization highlights informative words.  
- Questions: clarify choice of Taylor expansion point; report performance without SICK fine-tuning; provide compute resource details; give concrete word-analogy examples where Doc2VecC succeeds and Word2Vec fails.  
- Overall: strong, innovative, and efficient contribution that would benefit from the suggested clarifications and extra experiments.
