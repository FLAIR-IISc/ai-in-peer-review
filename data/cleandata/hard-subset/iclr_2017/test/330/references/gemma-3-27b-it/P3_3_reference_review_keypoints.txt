- Doc2VecC: represent documents as averages of word embeddings with random word “corruption” during training, claiming efficiency and data-dependent regularization, with strong results on sentiment, classification, and relatedness tasks.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — simple, effective method with strong empirical and theoretical support and clear presentation.
- Empirical performance: Competitive or superior to baselines (BoW, DEA, Word2Vec, Paragraph Vectors, Skip-thought), especially strong on document classification.
- Efficiency: Model complexity doesn’t scale with corpus size; very fast test-time computation (simple averaging) and favorable training times.
- Theoretical justification: Corruption yields a data-dependent regularizer that penalizes frequent uninformative words, explaining performance gains.
- Thorough experimentation: Includes word analogy tests and ablation studies demonstrating method strengths and weaknesses.
- Clear presentation: Well-written, logically organized, with clear motivation and detailed method description.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Authors claim low sensitivity to corruption rate q but should provide more analysis on other hyperparameters (embedding dim, window size, learning rate).
- Comparison to recent methods: Recommend adding comparisons to newer models like BERT or Sentence-BERT to contextualize results.
- Qualitative examples: Suggest adding embedding visualizations or examples of salient words contributing to document vectors for intuition.
- Clarify corruption/training: Need explicit explanation of how corruption is applied during training and how gradients/backpropagation are handled.
- Scalability question: Ask about practical memory limitations when scaling to extremely large datasets despite favorable complexity.
- Pre-trained embeddings impact: Request analysis of sensitivity to initial embedding quality and whether joint training would help.
- Negative sampling choice: Ask whether the number of negative samples was tuned and how it affected performance.
- Alternatives to averaging: Inquire if weighted averaging (TF-IDF) or attention mechanisms were explored and why simple averaging was chosen.
