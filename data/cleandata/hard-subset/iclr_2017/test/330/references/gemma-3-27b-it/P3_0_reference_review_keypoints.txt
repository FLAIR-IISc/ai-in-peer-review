- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as averages of word embeddings with a corruption mechanism, claiming efficiency and competitive performance.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Novelty: Simple averaging plus corruption is a practical, efficient alternative to more complex document embedding methods.
- Theoretical soundness: Corruption is derived as a data-dependent regularizer that downweights common words and favors informative ones.
- Empirical validation: Strong, consistent results across sentiment, classification, and semantic relatedness tasks with useful ablation studies.
- Efficiency: Lower computational cost at test time and training that scales with vocabulary size rather than corpus size.
- Clarify corruption rate selection: sensitivity of performance to q=0.9 should be analyzed (ablation over q).
- Reproducibility: Report details of negative sampling (number of negative samples used).
- Baseline comparison: Include a simple average of pre-trained Word2Vec embeddings without corruption/fine-tuning.
- Qualitative analysis: Provide nearest-neighbor or visualization examples of learned document vectors.
- Question: Elaborate assumptions in the Taylor expansion and their impact on the regularizer’s validity.
- Question: Explain why Skip-thought performs poorly on IMDB but well on SICK—dataset characteristics causing this difference.
- Question: Provide detailed training time breakdowns per algorithm and hardware specifications.
- Question: Discuss scalability to very large vocabularies (>1M) and possible mitigation techniques.
