- Summary: Proposes Doc2VecC — document vectors as averaged word embeddings with random-word “corruption” during training for efficiency and regularization.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Novelty: Averaging embeddings is familiar, but the corruption mechanism and its theoretical regularizer interpretation are novel.  
- Theoretical soundness: Corruption derived as a data-dependent regularizer via Taylor expansion and Hessian analysis.  
- Empirical validation: Comprehensive experiments (sentiment, classification, semantic relatedness) show competitive or superior performance to strong baselines.  
- Efficiency: Training and inference are notably faster; model complexity decoupled from corpus size, especially faster than Paragraph Vectors.  
- Ablation study: Ablation of corruption and regularization is informative and supports claimed benefits.  
- Hyperparameters: Paper lacks details on which hyperparameters were tuned and the specific tuning procedure — needs clarification for reproducibility.  
- Limitations: Should discuss potential weaknesses (e.g., very long documents, sensitivity to corruption rate q).  
- Vocabulary handling: Need details on how vocabulary was extended and how OOV words were handled in semantic relatedness experiments.  
- Code availability: Provide a direct link to the code repository.  
- Figure 1: Improve clarity, e.g., visually highlight corrupted words to make the concept more intuitive.  
- Corruption rate question: Report the range of q values tried, observed performance variation, and scenarios where q is more critical (small datasets, document length).  
- Complexity analysis question: Provide a more formal complexity analysis, especially scaling with vocabulary size v.  
- Relation to DAEs question: Clarify how Doc2VecC’s corruption differs from standard denoising autoencoder noise (position masking vs. word removal).  
- Preprocessing question: Specify preprocessing steps (stemming/lemmatization) applied to the SICK dataset for semantic relatedness.
