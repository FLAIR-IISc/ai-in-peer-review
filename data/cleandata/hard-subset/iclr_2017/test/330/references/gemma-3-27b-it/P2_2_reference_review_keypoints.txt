- Proposed Doc2VecC: represent documents as averaged word embeddings with random-word “corruption” during training for efficient document vectors.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Novelty & motivation: Combines embedding compositionality and corruption to address limitations of Word2Vec/Paragraph Vectors.  
- Theoretical foundation: Corruption is analyzed as a data-dependent regularizer with a derived regularization term.  
- Empirical results: Strong, competitive or superior performance on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness; efficient testing vs. Paragraph Vectors.  
- Presentation: Well-written, clear notation and model description.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Need more detailed exploration of corruption rate q and other hyperparameters (learning rate, embedding size, window).  
- Error analysis: Recommend qualitative analysis of failure cases to identify document types where the method struggles.  
- Relation to other regularizers: Suggest broader discussion of connections to dropout and other regularization techniques.  
- Code availability: Ensure the provided GitHub code is well-documented and reproducible.  
- Question on corruption q: Request clarification on how q influences the regularization type and guidelines for choosing q.  
- Question on scaling: Ask about performance and bottlenecks at extremely large dataset scales (e.g., billions of documents).  
- Question on task trade-offs: Ask whether gains on SemEval 2014 Task 1 trade off performance on sentiment or classification tasks and how such trade-offs were handled.
