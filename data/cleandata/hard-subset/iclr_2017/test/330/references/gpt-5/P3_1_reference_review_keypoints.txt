- Proposes Doc2VecC: predict target word from local CBOW context plus a corrupted average of document word embeddings as global context.  
- Claims: no per-document parameters, Word2Vec-like efficiency, data-dependent regularization via corruption, strong empirical results; code released.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main reason: Limited novelty—closely related to prior averages/global-context and dropout/denoising work; incremental change (replace learned doc vector with corrupted average).  
- Major empirical gap: Missing comparisons to strong, closely related baselines—especially fastText, SIF (Arora et al.), and Sent2Vec.  
- Strength: Clear, practical idea that enforces averaging at training time, enabling fast, memory-light inference.  
- Strength: Taylor-expansion argument gives intuitive, data-dependent regularization that down-weights frequent uninformative words.  
- Strength: Empirical gains over PV and Word2Vec+IDF on some tasks and notable speed advantages; code available.  
- Weakness: Theoretical novelty limited—analysis borrows existing dropout/mDAE techniques and provides intuition rather than new theory.  
- Weakness: Lack of crucial ablations (corruption rate q, local/global context ablation, subsampling effect, window sizes).  
- Weakness: Negative sampling used uniformly over V instead of standard unigram^0.75; this choice is unexplained and could bias comparisons.  
- Weakness: Comparisons to Skip-Thought are unfair due to sentence-length mismatch and other setup differences.  
- Weakness: No variances, multiple runs, or statistical tests reported; hyperparameter tuning details are sparse.  
- Weakness: Presentation issues—formatting, reference problems, informal equation introductions reduce reproducibility and polish.  
- Requested experiments: Add fastText (supervised and averaged embeddings + n-grams), SIF with common component removal, and Sent2Vec baselines.  
- Requested ablations: Systematic curves for q, experiments with/without subsampling, local-only/global-only/both, and negative-sampling distributions.  
- Requested analysis: Clarify approximation limits when training with negative sampling vs analyzed log-likelihood, and justify/clarify DAE comparisons.  
- Implementation requests: Report negatives, window sizes, optimizers, learning rates, schedules, epochs, and corpus details for analogy tasks.  
- Scope/limitations: Acknowledge loss of word order, OOV handling, long-doc bias (1/T scaling), and consider n-gram extensions.  
- Questions for authors: Sensitivity to q, contribution of local vs global context, reason for uniform negative sampling, ability to match fastText on benchmarks, and reproducibility of analogy gains.  
- Overall: Appealing and practical idea with promising results, but feels incremental and needs stronger baselines, ablations, statistical rigor, and clearer presentation to be compelling.
