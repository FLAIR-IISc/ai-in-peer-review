- Proposes Doc2VecC: document vectors formed by averaging word embeddings with a corruption mechanism that randomly removes words during training.  
- Corruption acts as data-dependent regularization, suppressing common words and favoring informative/rare words.  
- Model complexity depends only on vocabulary size, not corpus size.  
- Training and testing are highly efficient with no costly inference at test time.  
- Corruption enforces that meaningful document representations can be formed by averaged word embeddings.  
- Reports state-of-the-art or strong performance on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness tasks.  
- Outperforms Paragraph Vectors, Word2Vec+IDF, and Skip-thought vectors on multiple benchmarks.  
- Provides theoretical justification linking corruption to a regularization term that favors informative words.  
- Empirical results: IMDB 84.3% accuracy (vs 83.1% PV), Wikipedia 30.24% error (h=1000), SICK 11.70% error (better than Skip-thought).  
- Demonstrates learned embeddings suppress common words and improve analogy performance.  
- Timing results show Doc2VecC is significantly faster to train and test than Paragraph Vectors and Skip-thought.  
- Decision: Accept — significant, simple, and effective contribution with strong empirical and theoretical support.  
- Suggestion: include an ablation study comparing averaging with and without corruption to isolate corruption’s contribution.  
- Suggestion: briefly compare or acknowledge relation to more recent methods (e.g., BERT) to situate work historically.  
- Suggestion: justify choice of corruption rate q (q=0.9) and show sensitivity analysis across q values.  
- Suggestion: clarify the relationship to Denoising Autoencoders when local context words are removed.  
- Suggestion: discuss limitations, e.g., performance on extremely short documents or specialized domains.  
- Question: provide ablation details quantifying how much improvement stems from corruption vs. averaging.  
- Question: clarify how Doc2VecC’s enforcement differs from simple averaging approaches in prior work.  
- Question: explain why corruption at the document level improves word embeddings for analogy tasks.  
- Question: how does model complexity and advantages change if vocabulary grows dynamically during training?  
- Question: elaborate why Doc2VecC is much faster than skip-thought despite both learning document representations.
