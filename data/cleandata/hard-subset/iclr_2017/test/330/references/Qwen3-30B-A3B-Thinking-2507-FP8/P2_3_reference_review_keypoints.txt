- Proposes Doc2VecC: average word embeddings with random-word corruption during training for document representation learning.
- Claims benefits: model complexity independent of corpus size, fast training like Word2Vec, implicit data-dependent regularization, simple test-time averaging, and strong task performance.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Strength: Clearly motivates addressing Paragraph Vectors’ scaling and inference issues and links to compositional properties of word embeddings.
- Strength: Strong empirical gains across sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness with significance and large test-time speedups.
- Strength: Corruption mechanism is a novel, well-justified form of data-dependent regularization that downweights common uninformative words.
- Strength: High practical value due to simplicity and no need for document-specific vectors or expensive inference.
- Weakness: Theoretical derivation connecting corruption to the specific regularization term is hand-wavy and needs rigor.
- Weakness: Insufficient contrast with prior averaging methods (e.g., Huang et al., 2012) and lack of evidence that corruption is the key improvement.
- Weakness: Poorly analyzed claim that Skip-thought Vectors fail on IMDB due to paragraph length; needs more testing.
- Feedback: Provide a more rigorous derivation and clearer explanation of how the corruption differs from dropout/denoising autoencoders.
- Feedback: Directly compare Doc2VecC to plain averaging (no corruption) to isolate the corruption effect.
- Feedback: Add visualizations/analysis showing how corruption affects embeddings of common vs. rare words.
- Feedback: Include ablation studies (with/without corruption) to strengthen causal claims.
- Question: How does the corruption mechanism relate to standard dropout on embeddings, and how is it different?
- Question: Why does training with corruption “naturally enforce” document semantics in averaged embeddings versus training word embeddings on a document-level loss?
- Question: Why does simpler Doc2VecC outperform the more complex Paragraph Vectors across vector sizes?
- Question: Clarify the relationship to Denoising Autoencoders when local context words are removed and how absence of local context affects that relationship.
