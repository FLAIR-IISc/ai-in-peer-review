- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as averages of word embeddings trained with a novel corruption mechanism that yields data-dependent regularization.  
- Addresses Paragraph Vectors' issues: model complexity scaling with corpus size and expensive test-time inference.  
- Claim: Doc2VecC matches or outperforms state-of-the-art on sentiment, document classification, and semantic relatedness while being much faster to train and test.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Reason for accept 1: Identifies a clear, practical problem in document representation with strong motivation.  
- Reason for accept 2: Method is well-motivated, theoretically grounded, and empirically validated across multiple tasks.  
- Theoretical contribution: Derives that corruption acts as a data-dependent regularizer favoring rare/informative words.  
- Empirical evaluation: Thorough experiments on three tasks, t-SNE visualizations, ablation on corruption, and comparisons showing efficiency and accuracy gains.  
- Ablation and analysis: Table 3 supports the corruption regularization effect; Doc2VecC beats Word2Vec+IDF in integrated learning.  
- Practical value: Model complexity depends only on vocabulary size, training is efficient, and test-time inference is trivial (averaging embeddings).  
- Suggested improvement 1: Compare to recent transformer-based methods (e.g., BERT) to situate results in current SOTA.  
- Suggested improvement 2: Provide an ablation showing sensitivity to corruption rate q.  
- Suggested improvement 3: Clarify and compare the relationship to bag-of-words and Denoising Autoencoders (DEA).  
- Suggested improvement 4: Add a scalability analysis demonstrating how training time/memory scale with corpus size.  
- Suggested improvement 5: Give concrete examples showing how the regularization affects specific word embeddings.  
- Question 1: Why are gains over Word2Vec+IDF marginal in some cases, and can you provide deeper analysis of the improvements?  
- Question 2: How does Doc2VecC compare empirically to BERT/transformer-based document representations?  
- Question 3: Why is corruption-based regularization better than standard regularizers (e.g., L2), and can you provide evidence?  
- Question 4: How does sentence/paragraph length affect Doc2VecC vs. LSTM-based methods, given SICK results?  
- Question 5: How does the corruption rate q affect the number of backpropagated parameters and overall training efficiency?
