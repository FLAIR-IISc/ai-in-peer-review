- Introduces Doc2VecC: document representations as averaged word embeddings with random-word corruption during training to favor informative words.  
- Claims advantages: model complexity depends only on vocabulary, efficient training/inference, implicit data-dependent regularization, and superior task performance.  
- Recommends Accept: method is novel, well-motivated, and empirically strong.  
- Novelty: addresses Paragraph Vectors' parameter/inference issues and simple averaging's training limitations using a denoising-like corruption mechanism.  
- Theoretical foundation: derives a data-dependent regularization term from a Taylor expansion (Section 3.1, equation 7).  
- Empirical rigor: comprehensive experiments across sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness with multiple baselines and ablations.  
- Efficiency: substantially faster inference and training than Paragraph Vectors and Skip-thought Vectors (examples: 7s vs 4m17s inference; training under 2 hours vs 2 weeks).  
- Strong results: outperforms baselines on IMDB, Wikipedia classification (30.24% vs 33.02% error), and SICK semantic relatedness (11.70% error).  
- Corruption mechanism interpretation: scaling factor 1/(1-q) for "unbiased corruption" is asserted but needs more intuitive explanation.  
- Sensitivity claim: assertion that Doc2VecC isn't sensitive to corruption rate q is weakly supported; requests performance vs q plot.  
- Baseline comparisons: suggests adding other averaging methods (e.g., TF-IDF) beyond Word2Vec+IDF for better context.  
- Theoretical limitations: lacks discussion on handling very short documents (e.g., tweets); should be addressed.  
- Question 1: Clarify how Doc2VecC's corruption differs from standard denoising autoencoders and why it's more effective.  
- Question 2: Provide more detail on which frequent/uninformative word embeddings are dampened and correlation with word frequency.  
- Question 3: Compare Doc2VecC's implicit regularization to Word2Vec subsampling and whether subsampling could still help.  
- Question 4: Explain how document length affects Doc2VecC vs LSTM methods and whether a length threshold exists where Doc2VecC outperforms LSTMs.
