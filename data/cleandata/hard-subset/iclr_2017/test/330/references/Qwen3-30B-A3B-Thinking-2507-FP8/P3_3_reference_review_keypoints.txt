- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as averages of word embeddings with random word corruption during training to regularize toward rare informative words and suppress common ones.  
- Claims Doc2VecC addresses Paragraph Vector issues: parameter growth with corpus size and expensive inference for unseen documents.  
- Reports matching or exceeding state-of-the-art on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness while being much more efficient to train and test.  
- Decision: Weak Accept — novel, well-motivated idea with strong empirical results but weak theoretical justification for the corruption mechanism.  
- Empirical efficiency is a clear strength: much faster training and test-time encoding than Paragraph Vectors and Skip-thought (e.g., 7s vs 4m17s for 25k docs).  
- Strong empirical performance, especially on IMDB sentiment, and better document clustering visualized versus Paragraph Vectors.  
- Theoretical justification (Section 3.1) is tenuous: Taylor expansion approximation and the claim of intrinsic regularization (Equation 7) are not rigorously proven.  
- Comparison to Skip-thought is potentially unfair because Skip-thought was trained on much shorter paragraphs and not on IMDB-length text.  
- The claimed reduction to Denoising Autoencoders when removing local context is interesting but underexplored, and the contribution of local context is not isolated.  
- Recommend strengthening theoretical support and adding ablation studies that remove corruption while keeping other components constant.  
- Recommend training Skip-thought on comparable-length data (e.g., IMDB) for a fairer benchmark.  
- Ask authors to clarify differences from prior work (e.g., Huang et al. 2012) and emphasize the unique role of corruption.  
- Recommend including qualitative error analysis and examples showing where Doc2VecC outperforms baselines and how corruption helps.  
- Questions for authors: how exactly does corruption ensure averaged embeddings capture global semantics versus Paragraph Vectors?  
- Question on theory: how does suppression of frequent words relate to regularizer R(uj) and why does it depend on confidence σw,c,x rather than simple frequency?  
- Question on attribution: how much improvement is due to the corruption mechanism versus using pre-trained Word2Vec embeddings trained on a different corpus?  
- Clarify the counterintuitive statement that backpropagated parameters increase with the number of surviving words in the corrupted document.
