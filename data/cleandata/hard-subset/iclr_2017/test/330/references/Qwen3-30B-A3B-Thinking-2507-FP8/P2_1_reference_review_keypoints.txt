- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as averages of word embeddings with random-word corruption during training.  
- Corruption acts as data-dependent regularization, down-weighting common non-informative words and favoring rare informative words.  
- Model complexity depends only on vocabulary size (not corpus size), avoiding per-document vectors.  
- Training and inference are efficient, comparable to Word2Vec and much faster than Paragraph Vectors at test time.  
- Theoretical justification: corruption can be framed as a regularizer (Section 3.1).  
- Empirical evaluation across sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness shows consistent improvements over baselines.  
- Strong performance on sentiment analysis (≈15% improvement over BoW) and significant gains over Paragraph Vectors.  
- Ablation studies support claims: l2 norm analysis of embeddings, timing comparisons, and representation visualizations.  
- Authors analyze why some baselines (e.g., Skip-thought) underperform on certain datasets (e.g., long IMDB paragraphs).  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and positions itself clearly in related work.  
- Decision: Accept — motivated by theoretical soundness and strong empirical results.  
- Strength: corruption mechanism is novel and theoretically grounded, not just an empirical trick.  
- Strength: practical efficiency for both training and inference.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical rigor and convincing ablations.  
- Suggestion: explicitly state current state-of-the-art baselines for each task to strengthen claims.  
- Suggestion: include hyperparameter sensitivity analysis for the corruption rate q.  
- Suggestion: discuss effects of vocabulary cutoff (min freq = 10) and how it affects comparisons.  
- Suggestion: add analysis of failure cases where Doc2VecC underperforms the best baseline.  
- Suggestion: clarify relationship to Denoising Autoencoders (DEA) and how Doc2VecC differs.  
- Question: explain in detail how corruption replaces or complements Word2Vec subsampling for frequent uninformative words.  
- Question: would fine-tuning Skip-thought vectors on IMDB close the gap, and how would Doc2VecC compare?  
- Question: provide quantitative analysis of how much the implicit regularization improves performance versus other regularizers.  
- Question: supply concrete examples illustrating why Paragraph Vectors performs poorly on word analogy tasks.
