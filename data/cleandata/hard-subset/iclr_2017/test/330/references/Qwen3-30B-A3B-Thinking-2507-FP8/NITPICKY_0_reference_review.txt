

# Review for "DOCUMENTS THROUGH CORRUPTION" (sic)

## 1. Summary of Claims
The paper proposes "Document Vector through Corruption" (Doc2VecC), a novel approach for document representation learning. It claims that by averaging word embeddings learned with a corruption mechanism (randomly removing words during training), the model can efficiently generate document representations that outperform existing methods in sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness tasks. The authors assert that their method offers computational efficiency (decoupled from corpus size), implicitly learns meaningful document representations through the corruption process, and introduces a data-dependent regularization that suppresses common but non-discriminative words.

## 2. Decision
Reject. The paper contains numerous minor but pervasive technical inconsistencies, formatting errors, and unclear claims that undermine the scientific rigor of the work, despite its potentially interesting core idea.

## 3. Supporting Arguments for Decision
The paper suffers from multiple critical issues that collectively prevent me from confidently assessing its contribution:

1. **Title and fundamental formatting error**: The title "MENTS THROUGH CORRUPTION" is clearly a typo for "DOCUMENTS THROUGH CORRUPTION" (or similar). This is a serious formatting error that suggests the authors did not carefully proofread the paper before submission.

2. **Mathematical inconsistencies**: Multiple equations contain inconsistent notation and formatting errors:
   - Equation (1) uses inconsistent formatting: "x̃d = { 0, with probability q xd 1−q , otherwise" should be properly formatted with cases.
   - Equation (2) has messy notation with "local context︷︸︸︷ Uct + global context︷ ︸︸ ︷ 1 T Ux̃" that should not appear in mathematical notation.
   - Equation (6) has a missing closing parenthesis: "Ep(x̃|x)[f(w, c, x̃)] ≈ f(w, c, µx) + 1 2 tr ( E[(x̃− x)(x̃− x)>]∇2x̃f )".
   - Equation (7) has inconsistent formatting and missing parentheses in the regularization term.

3. **Inconsistent terminology**: The paper claims "Doc2VecC reduces to Denoising Autoencoders (DEA) if the local context words are removed" but provides no mathematical derivation or evidence for this claim. The relationship between Doc2VecC and DEA is not clearly established.

4. **Inconsistent experimental details**: The paper states "We used a cutoff of 100 in this experiment" but earlier mentions "We remove words that appear less than 10 times" (Section 4.2), creating confusion about the vocabulary filtering threshold.

5. **Reference formatting**: The reference list contains inconsistent formatting with extra commas, backslashes in "et al\.", and unusual citation styles like "Blei et al\.,? \Q2003\E", which deviates from standard academic conventions.

6. **Missing Figure 1**: The paper refers to "Figure 1" (Section 3) but does not include it, making it impossible to understand the claimed model architecture.

These issues are not merely superficial - they directly impact the ability to understand and verify the technical contributions of the paper. As a reviewer, I cannot assess whether the method is actually novel or whether the claims about its performance are well-supported when the paper's core technical details are presented with inconsistent mathematical notation and formatting.

## 4. Additional Feedback
These points are intended to help improve the paper, though they are not part of my rejection decision:

- Please correct the title to "DOCUMENT REPRESENTATION THROUGH CORRUPTION" (or similar) and ensure all instances of "Doc2VecC" are properly introduced.

- Format all mathematical equations with proper LaTeX notation, ensuring consistent use of parentheses, spacing, and symbols.

- Clarify the relationship between Doc2VecC and Denoising Autoencoders with a clear mathematical derivation or reference.

- Provide consistent experimental details regarding vocabulary filtering thresholds.

- Include Figure 1 to illustrate the model architecture.

- Fix the reference formatting to follow standard academic conventions.

## Questions for Authors
1. The paper claims "Doc2VecC reduces to Denoising Autoencoders (DEA) if the local context words are removed" - please provide a mathematical derivation or clear explanation of this claim.

2. The paper states "We use a cutoff of 100" in Section 4.2 but earlier says "We remove words that appear less than 10 times" - which threshold was actually used in the experiments?

3. Please clarify the relationship between the corruption mechanism in Doc2VecC and standard dropout techniques in neural networks, as the paper seems to claim a novel form of regularization.

4. Why does the paper use "q = 0.9" as the corruption rate throughout experiments without providing an ablation study showing the sensitivity to this hyperparameter?
