This work introduces a method for learning document embeddings by representing them as the sum of their constituent word embeddings, trained jointly and with a dropout ('corruption') mechanism. Although the individual components – joint word and document embedding training, sum-of-embeddings document representation, and dropout – have appeared in prior work (e.g., Lebret & Collobert’s “The Sum of Its Parts”), the combination yields an efficient algorithm for document representation with strong empirical results. 

The paper does not present substantial novelty; the approach utilizes a word2vec-style loss and training procedure alongside established techniques. The corruption strategy is effectively standard dropout applied to the input layer. 

However, the model’s efficiency is a key strength, as it relies on a simple averaging of word embeddings at inference time, unlike more complex methods like Doc2Vec. Furthermore, the summation-based construction inherently focuses the embedding on information relevant to local context prediction, capturing salient global document characteristics. Given its simplicity, the observed performance on sentiment analysis and document classification tasks is promising.

In conclusion, while lacking in novelty, the model’s simplicity, computational efficiency, and encouraging performance justify its publication and further investigation. I recommend acceptance.
