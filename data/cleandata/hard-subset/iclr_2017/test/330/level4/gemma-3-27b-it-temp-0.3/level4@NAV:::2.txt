This paper introduces a framework for generating document representations by averaging word embeddings, incorporating a data-driven regularization strategy. This regularization prioritizes embeddings of informative and infrequent words while driving embeddings of common words towards zero. The authors demonstrate through sentiment analysis and document classification experiments that their method achieves state-of-the-art performance, yielding lower error rates than existing baseline document embedding techniques. 

The motivation behind identifying an effective method for encoding documents as vectors is sound; however, the paper’s technical novelty is limited. The constituent techniques largely build upon existing work, with the primary advantage of this approach being its simplicity and computational efficiency. To strengthen the claims of superiority, I recommend evaluating the method on a broader range of tasks. 

Furthermore, clarification is needed regarding the RNN-LM implementation. Specifically, was the language model trained to directly minimize classification error, or was it a standard language modeling objective? Additionally, please specify whether the document representation was derived from the final hidden state or an average of all hidden states. 

Given the prevalence of bidirectional LSTM networks for document representation – where the final hidden states are concatenated – a comparative analysis against this approach, particularly on document classification and sentiment analysis tasks, would significantly enhance the paper’s contribution and contextualize its performance.
