This paper introduces a document vector representation method leveraging a skip-gram learning approach, enhanced with a global context vector regularizer and dropout techniques. Although each individual component is established, the specific combination presented appears novel. I particularly valued the thorough behavioral analysis provided in Section 3. 

The primary limitation of this work lies in the scope of its empirical evaluation. The chosen tasks – sentiment analysis and k-way classification – may not fully demonstrate the potential of the method, and the space dedicated to t-SNE visualizations could have been better utilized for more in-depth analysis. 

Despite a somewhat limited evaluation, and acknowledging concerns raised by other reviewers regarding baseline comparisons, I recommend acceptance. The algorithm is interesting, well-structured, and computationally efficient, suggesting its potential utility to the broader research community.
