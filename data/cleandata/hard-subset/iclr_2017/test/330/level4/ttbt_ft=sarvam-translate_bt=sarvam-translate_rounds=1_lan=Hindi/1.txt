This paper discusses a method for computing vector representations for documents, using a skip-gram style learning system with an additional regularizer as a global reference vector with various bits of drop-out. Although none of the individual components proposed in this paper are new, I believe the combination of this approach is novel. Furthermore, I appreciated the detailed analysis of model behavior in Section 3.

The main drawback of this presentation is its relative weakness on the empirical front. Arguably, there are more interesting tasks than sentiment analysis and K-means clustering! Similarly, why waste 2/3 of a page on T-SNi estimates instead of using that space for further analysis?

While I am slightly disappointed with this low evaluation and agree with other reviewers regarding the soft baselines, I feel that this paper should be accepted: it is an interesting algorithm, well-written, and very efficient, so it is reasonable to assume that other readers may find it useful for some of the ideas presented here.
