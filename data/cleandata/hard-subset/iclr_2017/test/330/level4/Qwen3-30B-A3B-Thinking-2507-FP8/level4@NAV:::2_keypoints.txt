- Proposes document representation by averaging word embeddings with data-dependent regularization that upweights informative/rare words and suppresses common words.  
- Reports lowest error rates on sentiment analysis and document classification compared to baseline embedding methods.  
- Motivation for optimizing document encoding is compelling but the method lacks substantial technical novelty.  
- Techniques are mostly incremental; main advantages are simplicity and computational efficiency.  
- Reviewer requests additional task evaluations to be convinced this is an optimal document representation approach.  
- Questions RNN-LM details: was it trained as a standard language model or to minimize classification error?  
- Asks how document representation was derived from RNN-LM: final hidden state or average of all hidden states?  
- Recommends comparison with bidirectional LSTM using concatenated final hidden states on classification/sentiment tasks to strengthen evaluation.
