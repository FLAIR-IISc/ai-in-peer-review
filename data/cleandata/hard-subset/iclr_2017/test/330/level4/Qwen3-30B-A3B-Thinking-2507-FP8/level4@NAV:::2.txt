

This paper introduces a framework for document representation by averaging word embeddings with data-dependent regularization that emphasizes informative or rare words while suppressing common words (driving their weights toward zero). Experiments on sentiment analysis and document classification demonstrate that the method achieves the lowest error rates relative to baseline document embedding approaches. While the motivation for optimizing document vector encoding is compelling, the paper lacks substantial technical novelty. Most techniques employed are incremental rather than novel, with the primary advantage being the method's simplicity and computational efficiency. Consequently, I would require results across additional tasks to be convinced that this approach represents the optimal solution for document representation learning. Regarding the RNN-LM implementation: was the language model trained to minimize classification error or as a standard language model? Additionally, was the document representation derived from the final hidden state or the average of all hidden states? Given that bidirectional LSTM with concatenated final hidden states is a widely adopted document representation technique, a comparative analysis with this approach on tasks like document classification or sentiment analysis would significantly strengthen the evaluation.
