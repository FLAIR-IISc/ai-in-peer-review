

This paper proposes a method for document vector representation using a skip-gram-style learning mechanism enhanced with a global context vector regularizer and dropout techniques. While the individual components are not novel, I believe the specific combination presented is novel and merits attention. I particularly appreciated the detailed analysis of model behavior in Section 3.

The primary limitation of this work is its weak empirical evaluation. The choice of tasks—sentiment analysis and k-way classification—feels limited, especially given the availability of more meaningful benchmarks for document representation. Furthermore, dedicating two-thirds of a page to t-SNE projections seems excessive; that space would have been better utilized for deeper analysis or additional experiments. I’m disappointed by this reduced evaluation scope and align with other reviewers’ concerns regarding the soft baselines.

Despite these shortcomings, I recommend acceptance. The algorithm is interesting, well-structured, and highly efficient, making its core ideas broadly applicable. The technical contributions, though incremental, are clearly articulated and could prove valuable to the community. The method’s simplicity and computational efficiency further strengthen its potential utility. While the empirical section could be expanded, the paper’s theoretical and methodological merits justify acceptance.
