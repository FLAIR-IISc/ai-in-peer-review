- Proposes a document representation method combining skip-gram training with a global context vector regularizer and multiple dropout strategies; integration/regularization is novel.
- Section 3 provides a strong theoretical/analytical treatment, offering clear insights into model behavior and regularizer effects.
- Empirical evaluation is limited—only sentiment analysis and multi-class classification—failing to demonstrate broader utility.
- Lacks comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., state-of-the-art contextual embeddings and recent regularized models), weakening claimed advantages.
- Excessive space devoted to t-SNE visualizations; could be better used for ablation studies, diverse tasks, or hyperparameter sensitivity analyses.
- Method is computationally efficient, well-motivated, and clearly presented, enhancing practical appeal.
- The regularization technique is a simple but effective refinement to document embedding training, though not wholly unprecedented.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance: novelty, analysis, and efficiency outweigh empirical shortcomings, which should be addressed in future work.
