- Proposes learning document embeddings by summing jointly trained word embeddings with random input dropout during training.  
- Constituent ideas are not novel: joint word/document training and sum-to-represent are prior work (e.g., Lebret & Collobert).  
- The corruption mechanism is essentially standard dropout on the input layer.  
- Uses a word2vec-style loss and training methodology, contributing minimal methodological novelty.  
- Method is highly efficient at generation — document vectors are just averaged word embeddings, avoiding Doc2Vec’s costly inference.  
- The learned embedding captures global document information useful for predicting local context.  
- Achieves strong empirical results on sentiment analysis and document classification despite simplicity.  
- Recommendation: accept due to simplicity, efficiency, and strong empirical performance despite limited novelty.
