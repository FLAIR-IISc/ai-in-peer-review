- Method: averages word embeddings with data-dependent regularization that upweights informative/rare words and drives common words' embeddings toward zero.
- Results: reports lowest error rates among baseline document embedding methods on sentiment analysis and document classification.
- Novelty concern: paper offers little technical contribution; techniques are largely not novel and main novelty is simplicity and computational efficiency.
- Evaluation limitation: experiments cover only two tasks; stronger results across more tasks are needed to claim optimality.
- RNN-LM baseline unclear: unclear whether it was trained to minimize classification error or as a standard LM, and whether the final hidden state or average of hidden states was used as the document representation.
- Missing baseline: should directly compare to a bidirectional LSTM with concatenated final hidden states, a widely adopted document representation.
- Suggested action: include comparisons with stronger/better-specified baselines on additional tasks (e.g., document classification, sentiment) to substantiate claims.
