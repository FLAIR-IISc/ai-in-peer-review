

This paper presents a document vector representation method employing a skip-gram-like learning mechanism enhanced with a global context vector regularizer and multiple dropout variants. While the individual components are not novel, the specific integration proposed represents a meaningful contribution. I commend the thorough model behavior analysis in Section 3.  

The primary limitation lies in the empirical evaluation, which is underdeveloped. The focus on sentiment analysis and k-way classification tasks appears less compelling than alternative, more challenging benchmarks. Furthermore, allocating two-thirds of a page to t-SNE visualizations detracts from the opportunity for deeper empirical analysis or comparison.  

Although moderately disappointed by the limited evaluation scope and aligning with other reviewers on the soft baseline concerns, I recommend acceptance. The algorithm is novel in its design, well-structured, and computationally efficient, warranting consideration by the broader research community for its practical utility.
