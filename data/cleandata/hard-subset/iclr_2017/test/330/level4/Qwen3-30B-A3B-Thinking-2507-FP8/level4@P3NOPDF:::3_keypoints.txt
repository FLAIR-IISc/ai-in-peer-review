- Proposes a document embedding method that sums learned word embeddings with dropout during training.  
- Core components (sum-based representation, joint word-document training, dropout) are individually well-established, so novelty is modest.  
- Uses a word2vec-style loss to train embeddings, without introducing new theoretical or algorithmic constructs.  
- Main strength is efficiency: document vectors are computed in linear time by summing pre-trained word embeddings, avoiding costly inference like Doc2Vec.  
- Training objective focuses on local context prediction, which yields meaningful global document semantics without explicit feature engineering.  
- Empirical results on sentiment analysis and document classification are competitive, often matching or exceeding baselines.  
- Method is simple to implement, requires minimal hyperparameter tuning, and has low computational overhead.  
- Offers scalability, interpretability, and practicality for large-scale applications where inference speed matters.  
- Overall assessment: despite limited novelty, the clear presentation, thorough evaluation, and strong practical value justify acceptance (recommendation: accept).
