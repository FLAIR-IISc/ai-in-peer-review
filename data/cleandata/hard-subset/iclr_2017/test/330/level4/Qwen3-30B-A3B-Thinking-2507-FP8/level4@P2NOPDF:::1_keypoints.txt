- Proposes a document representation method combining skip-gram learning with a global context vector regularizer and dropout.
- Individual components are not novel, but their specific integration is a meaningful contribution.
- Section 3 provides a detailed, valuable analysis of model behavior.
- Primary weakness is limited empirical evaluation focused narrowly on sentiment analysis and k-way classification.
- Overuse of t-SNE visualization that could have been replaced with more substantive analysis or comparisons.
- Lacks strong baselines, notably missing comparisons to recent contextual embedding approaches.
- Algorithm is well-motivated, elegantly composed, computationally efficient, and simple.
- Method appears broadly applicable to NLP tasks beyond those evaluated.
- Paper merits acceptance despite empirical scope limitations and provides a solid foundation for future work.
