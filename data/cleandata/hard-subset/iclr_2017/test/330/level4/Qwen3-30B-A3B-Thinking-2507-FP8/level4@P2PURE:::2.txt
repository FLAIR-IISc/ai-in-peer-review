

This paper introduces a framework for document representation by averaging word embeddings with data-dependent regularization that prioritizes informative or rare words while driving common words' embeddings toward zero. Experiments on sentiment analysis and document classification demonstrate that the proposed method achieves the lowest error rates among baseline document embedding approaches. While the motivation for optimizing document vector encoding is compelling, the paper fails to provide significant technical contributions. Most techniques employed are not novel, and the primary novelty lies solely in the method's simplicity and computational efficiency. Consequently, the authors should provide strong results across more than two tasks to substantiate the claim that this approach represents the optimal way to learn document representations. Additionally, for the RNN-LM baseline, it is unclear whether the language model was trained to minimize classification error or as a standard language model, and whether the final hidden state or the average of all hidden states was used as the document representation. Given that a widely adopted document representation method involves using a bidirectional LSTM and concatenating its final hidden states, a direct comparison with this approach on tasks like document classification or sentiment analysis would be highly valuable.
