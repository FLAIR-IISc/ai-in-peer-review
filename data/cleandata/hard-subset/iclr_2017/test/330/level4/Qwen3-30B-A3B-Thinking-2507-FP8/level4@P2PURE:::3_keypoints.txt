- Proposes document embeddings formed by summing jointly learned word embeddings with random dropout ("corruption") during training.  
- Joint training of word and document embeddings and sum-of-words representation are not novel (cites Lebret & Collobert).  
- The proposed corruption is equivalent to standard input-layer dropout.  
- Combined with a word2vec-style loss and training, the method offers little novelty over existing approaches.  
- Method is computationally efficient, requiring only an average/sum of word embeddings and avoiding Doc2Vec-style inference.  
- Learned embeddings capture salient global document information relevant for local-context prediction.  
- Shows strong empirical performance on sentiment analysis and document classification tasks.  
- Despite limited novelty, the model’s simplicity, efficiency, and results merit broader attention and further study.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.
