- Proposes a document vector method using a skip-gram-like learning mechanism with a global context vector regularizer and multiple dropout variants.  
- Individual components are not novel, but their specific integration is a meaningful contribution.  
- Section 3 provides thorough analysis of model behavior.  
- Main limitation: empirical evaluation is underdeveloped.  
- Evaluation tasks (sentiment analysis and k-way classification) are less compelling than more challenging benchmarks.  
- Excessive space spent on t-SNE visualizations (two-thirds of a page) reduces room for deeper empirical analysis or comparisons.  
- Shares reviewers' concerns about weak/soft baselines in experiments.  
- Recommendation: accept — algorithm is novel in design, well-structured, computationally efficient, and practically useful.
