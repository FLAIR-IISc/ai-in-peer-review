- Proposes document representation by averaging word embeddings with data-dependent regularization that upweights rare/informative words and suppresses common words (pushes their embeddings toward zero).
- Experiments on sentiment analysis and document classification show the method attains the lowest error among baseline document embedding approaches.
- Motivation for optimizing document encoding is sound, but the paper offers limited technical novelty; main merits are simplicity and computational efficiency.
- To claim superiority, evaluation should include results on more than two tasks.
- Clarify RNN-LM baseline training objective: trained as a standard language model or to minimize classification error?
- Clarify how document representation is extracted from the RNN-LM: final hidden state or average of all hidden states?
- Recommend direct comparison to bidirectional LSTMs (concatenated final hidden states), a common standard for document representation, on classification/sentiment tasks.
