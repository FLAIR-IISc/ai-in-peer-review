

This paper proposes a framework for document representation based on averaging word embeddings, enhanced by a data-dependent regularization that favors informative or rare words while suppressing common words (forcing their embeddings toward zero). Experiments on sentiment analysis and document classification demonstrate that the method achieves the lowest error rates among baseline document embedding approaches. While the motivation for optimizing document encoding is sound, the paper fails to offer significant technical contributions. Most techniques employed are not novel, and the primary advantage lies in the method's simplicity and computational efficiency. To establish this as a superior approach, the authors should provide compelling results across more than two tasks. Additionally, key technical details require clarification: For the RNN-LM baseline, was the language model trained to minimize classification error or as a standard language model? Furthermore, was the document representation derived from the final hidden state or the average of all hidden states? Given that bidirectional LSTMs (using concatenated final hidden states) represent a widely adopted standard for document representation, a direct comparison of the proposed method against this approach on tasks like document classification or sentiment analysis would be highly valuable.
