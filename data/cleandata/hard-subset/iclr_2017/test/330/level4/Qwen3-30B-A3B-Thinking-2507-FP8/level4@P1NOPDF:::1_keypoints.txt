- Proposes a document vector method using a skip-gram-style learner with a global context vector regularizer and dropout.  
- Individual components are not novel, but their specific combination appears novel and noteworthy.  
- Section 3 provides a detailed and appreciated analysis of model behavior.  
- Primary limitation: weak empirical evaluation.  
- Evaluation tasks (sentiment analysis and k-way classification) are limited and miss more meaningful benchmarks.  
- Excessive space devoted to t-SNE projections (two-thirds of a page) that could be used for deeper analysis or experiments.  
- Concerns about using weak/soft baselines, echoed by other reviewers.  
- Recommends acceptance despite weaknesses.  
- Algorithm is interesting, well-structured, and highly efficient.  
- Technical contributions are incremental but clearly articulated and potentially valuable.  
- Method’s simplicity and computational efficiency increase its practical utility.
