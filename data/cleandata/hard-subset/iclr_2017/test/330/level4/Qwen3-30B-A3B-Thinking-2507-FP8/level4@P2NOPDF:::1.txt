

This paper introduces a document representation method based on skip-gram learning enhanced with a global context vector regularizer and dropout techniques. While the individual components (skip-gram, global context, dropout) are not novel, their specific integration constitutes a meaningful contribution. The detailed analysis of model behavior in Section 3 is particularly valuable and well-executed.

The primary weakness lies in the limited empirical evaluation. The study focuses narrowly on sentiment analysis and k-way classification tasks, overlooking more relevant or challenging benchmarks that would better demonstrate the method's utility. Furthermore, the extensive t-SNE visualization consumes significant space that could have been better utilized for additional analysis or comparisons with stronger baselines. The evaluation section would benefit from deeper exploration of the model's performance across diverse tasks and datasets.

While I share the other reviewers' concerns regarding the lack of strong baselines (e.g., the absence of comparisons to recent contextual embedding approaches), I believe the paper's core contribution merits acceptance. The proposed algorithm is well-motivated, elegantly composed, and computationally efficient. Its simplicity and effectiveness suggest it will be broadly applicable to various NLP tasks beyond those tested here. The method's practicality and clear presentation outweigh the current limitations in empirical scope, making it a useful addition to the literature. The authors have provided a solid foundation for future work, and the ideas presented are likely to be adopted and extended by the community.
