

This paper proposes a method for learning document vector representations using a skip-gram style objective enhanced with a global context vector regularizer and dropout variants. While the individual components are not novel, the specific combination presented constitutes a meaningful contribution. I also commend the thorough analysis of model behavior in Section 3.  
The primary limitation lies in the empirical evaluation, which is underdeveloped. The focus on sentiment analysis and k-way classification tasks is narrow; more diverse or challenging tasks would strengthen the claims. Additionally, dedicating two-thirds of a page to t-SNE visualizations detracts from the opportunity to present deeper analysis or additional experiments.  
While I share disappointment in the limited empirical scope and concur with other reviewers regarding the need for stronger soft baselines, I recommend acceptance. The algorithm is interesting, well-motivated, and computationally efficient, making its ideas valuable to the broader community.
