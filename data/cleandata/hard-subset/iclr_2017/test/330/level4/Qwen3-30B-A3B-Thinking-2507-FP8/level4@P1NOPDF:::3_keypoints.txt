- Proposes document embeddings as the sum of constituent word embeddings with jointly trained, randomly masked word vectors.  
- Individual components (joint training, sum constraint) are not novel and have prior precedent.  
- The corruption mechanism is essentially standard input-layer dropout.  
- Use of a word2vec-inspired loss further limits methodological novelty.  
- Overall contribution on theory/method is limited.  
- Method is highly efficient at inference—just average word embeddings, no complex optimization.  
- Embeddings capture global document semantics relevant for predicting local context.  
- Strong empirical performance on sentiment analysis and document classification tasks.  
- Simple architecture and computational efficiency make it a useful, robust baseline.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on utility and empirical validation.
