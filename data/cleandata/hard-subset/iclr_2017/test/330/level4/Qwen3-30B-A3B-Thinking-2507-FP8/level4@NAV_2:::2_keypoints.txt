- Paper introduces Doc2VecC: document vectors formed by averaging word embeddings with a corruption-based regularizer that downweights common words and favors informative/rare words.
- Claims competitive performance on sentiment analysis and document classification with faster training and inference than existing methods.
- Recommendation: reject due to lack of substantial technical novelty.
- Averaging word embeddings as document representation is not new; corruption regularization is a straightforward extension rather than a major advance.
- Empirical claims are insufficiently supported because experiments cover only two tasks.
- Experimental validation is limited and does not adequately compare to current state-of-the-art methods.
- Paper fails to compare meaningfully with recent deep learning baselines, particularly bidirectional LSTMs commonly used for document representation.
- Reported performance gains are not convincingly large enough to justify the claims given the method’s simplicity.
- Suggested improvements: expand evaluation to other tasks (e.g., semantic relatedness, topic modeling, question answering) to show broader applicability.
- Suggested improvements: provide thorough comparisons with recent SOTA methods, especially bidirectional LSTMs.
- Suggested improvements: include ablation studies to separate the effects of corruption regularization from simple averaging.
- Clarification requested: For the RNN-LM baseline, was it trained to minimize classification error or as a standard language model, and was the document representation the final hidden state or the average of hidden states?
- Clarification requested: How does Doc2VecC specifically compare to bidirectional LSTM approaches on the evaluated tasks?
