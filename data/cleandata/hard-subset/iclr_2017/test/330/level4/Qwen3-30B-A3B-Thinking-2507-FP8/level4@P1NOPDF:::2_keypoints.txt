- Proposes document representation by averaging word embeddings with data-dependent regularization that upweights informative/rare words and suppresses common words.  
- Empirical results on sentiment analysis and document classification show lowest errors among the baseline document embedding methods tested.  
- Motivation for improving document encoding is sound, but the paper lacks substantial technical novelty.  
- Core techniques are largely derivative; the primary advantages are simplicity and computational efficiency.  
- To claim this as an optimal approach, the method needs validation across more than the two tasks presented.  
- RNN-LM component is underspecified: unclear whether the language model was trained as a standard LM (minimizing perplexity) or directly optimized for classification.  
- Unclear how document representations from the RNN-LM were formed: final hidden state or average of hidden states?  
- The paper omits comparison to the widely used BiLSTM approach (concatenating final forward/backward states), a crucial baseline for document classification and sentiment tasks.  
- A direct BiLSTM comparison would materially strengthen the paper’s claims and contextualize the proposed method’s performance.  
- Without benchmarking against BiLSTM and more tasks, the empirical claims of superiority are under-supported given the representational power of established sequence models.
