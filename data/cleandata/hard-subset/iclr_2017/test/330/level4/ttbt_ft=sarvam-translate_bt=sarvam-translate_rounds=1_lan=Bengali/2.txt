This paper presents a framework for creating document representations.

The main idea is to represent a document as the average of its word embeddings, a data-dependent regularization that supports informative or rare words and forces common words to be close to 0.

Experiments related to sentiment analysis and document classification show that the proposed method has the lowest error compared to the baseline document embedding method.

Although I prefer the motivation for finding the best way to encode a document into vectors, the paper does not provide significant technical contributions.

Most of the techniques are not new, and the simplicity and speed of the proposed method are the main selling points.

For this reason, I would like to see better results than two works that lead to the belief that this is the best way to learn document representation.

For RNN-LM, is the LM trained to reduce classification error, or is it trained as a language model? Did you use the final hidden state as the representation, or the average of all hidden states?

One of the most commonly used methods for document representation now is to have a bidirectional LSTM and attach the final hidden state as the document representation.

I think it is important to know how the proposed method compares to this method for tasks like document classification or sentiment analysis.
