This paper discusses a method for computing vector representations of documents using a skip-gram style learning mechanism. In this case, a global context vector is used as a regularizer with various bit drops. Although none of the individual elements proposed in this paper are new, I believe this type of combination is. Furthermore, I was impressed by the detailed analysis of the model's behavior in section 3.

The main drawback of this submission is its empirical weakness. Arguably, there are more interesting works from sentiment analysis and K-way classification! Similarly, instead of wasting 2/3 of a page on T-sine projections, that space should be used for further analysis.

Although I am somewhat disappointed with this reduced assessment and agree with other reviewers about the soft foundation, I believe this paper should be accepted: it is an interesting algorithm, well-written, and very efficient, so it is reasonable to assume that other readers may find some of the ideas presented here useful.
