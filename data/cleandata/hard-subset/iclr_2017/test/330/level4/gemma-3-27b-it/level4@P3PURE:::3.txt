This paper proposes learning document embeddings as a weighted sum of constituent word embeddings, where the weights are jointly learned and randomly dropped out (“corrupted”) during training. While the individual components of this model are not particularly novel, the result is an efficient learning algorithm for document representation with strong empirical performance.

Joint training of word and document embeddings is a known approach, as is the constraint that a document’s representation should be a function of its constituent word embeddings (e.g., Lebret and Collobert’s “The Sum of Its Parts”: Joint Learning of Word and Phrase Representations with Autoencoders). Furthermore, the corruption mechanism employed is effectively standard dropout applied to the input layer. Combined with a word2vec-style loss function and training procedure, this paper presents limited novelty.

However, the model is computationally efficient at generation time, requiring only an averaging of word embeddings rather than a complex inference step as is often the case with Doc2Vec. Moreover, by design, the resulting embedding captures salient global information about the document – specifically, information that is predictive of local context. Given its simplicity, the performance achieved on sentiment analysis and document classification tasks is quite encouraging.

Overall, despite the lack of significant novelty, the model’s simplicity, efficiency, and performance merit wider readership and study. I recommend acceptance.
