- Proposes Doc2VecC: learns document vectors by averaging word embeddings with a skip-gram-style objective and a corruption-based regularizer.  
- Enforcing document-as-average during training is a compelling core idea.  
- Section 3.1’s analysis of the corruption mechanism as a data-dependent regularizer is a strong contribution.  
- Components are not wholly novel, but their combination and resulting computational efficiency are valuable.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance: method is sound, efficiently implemented, and yields competitive results.  
- Motivation aligns with prior Word2Vec/Paragraph Vector work and addresses Paragraph Vector’s large parameter space and inference costs.  
- Experiments (sentiment, document classification, semantic relatedness) show Doc2VecC matches or outperforms state-of-the-art.  
- Ablation study (Section 4.3) strengthens claims about the regularizer’s effect.  
- Reported speed improvements (Table 2) are significant and support efficiency claims.  
- Baseline comparisons are somewhat weak but still demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Suggestion: expand empirical evaluation to more challenging tasks that require nuanced semantics.  
- Suggestion: improve t-SNE visualizations to better show inter-category/semantic clustering.  
- Suggestion: clarify hyperparameter tuning details and final values to aid reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: explicitly discuss limitations (e.g., averaging-induced biases, handling very long documents).  
- Question: Was corruption rate q=0.9 tuned, and does optimal q vary by dataset/task?  
- Question: Can authors show specific words whose embeddings were significantly affected by the regularization and the downstream impact?  
- Question: Why does Doc2VecC lag LSTM methods on semantic relatedness, and how might this be addressed?  
- Question: Any analysis of vocabulary size effects—does performance degrade with very large vocabularies?
