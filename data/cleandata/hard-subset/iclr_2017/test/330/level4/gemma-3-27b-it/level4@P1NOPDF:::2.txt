This paper presents a framework for learning document representations. The core idea is to represent a document as the average of its word embeddings, incorporating a data-dependent regularization term that prioritizes informative or rare words while driving the embeddings of common words towards zero. Experiments on sentiment analysis and document classification demonstrate that the proposed method achieves lower error rates than several baseline document embedding techniques. 

While the motivation – finding an effective way to encode documents as vectors – is sound, the paper doesn’t present substantial novel technical contributions. Many of the techniques employed are not new, and the primary strength of the approach appears to be its simplicity and speed. Consequently, I would like to see more compelling results across a broader range of tasks to be convinced that this is a superior method for learning document representations. 

Regarding the RNN-LM, was the language model trained to directly minimize classification error, or was it trained as a standard language model? Furthermore, did you utilize the final hidden state as the document representation, or the average of all hidden states?

A currently popular and effective method for document representation involves employing a bidirectional LSTM and concatenating the final hidden states to form the document vector. It would be beneficial to understand how the proposed method compares to this widely-used approach, particularly on tasks such as document classification and sentiment analysis.
