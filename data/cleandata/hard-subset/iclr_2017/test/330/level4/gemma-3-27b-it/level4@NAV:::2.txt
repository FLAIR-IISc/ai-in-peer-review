This paper introduces a framework for generating document representations by averaging word embeddings, incorporating a data-dependent regularization technique. This regularization prioritizes embeddings of informative and infrequent words, while driving embeddings of common words towards zero. The authors demonstrate through sentiment analysis and document classification experiments that their method achieves state-of-the-art performance, exhibiting lower error rates than several baseline document embedding approaches. 

The motivation behind identifying effective document vectorization techniques is sound; however, the technical novelty of this work is limited. The core components largely rely on established techniques, and the primary advantage presented is the method’s simplicity and computational efficiency. To better establish the efficacy of this approach as a preferred method for learning document representations, I recommend evaluating its performance on a broader range of tasks. 

Further clarification regarding the RNN-LM implementation would be helpful. Specifically, was the language model trained to directly optimize classification performance, or as a standard language model? Additionally, please specify whether the document representation was derived from the final hidden state or an average of all hidden states. Finally, given the widespread adoption of bidirectional LSTM networks for document representation – typically concatenating the final hidden states – a comparative analysis against this approach on document classification or sentiment analysis tasks would significantly strengthen the presented results.
