- Proposes averaging word embeddings with a data-dependent regularizer that upweights informative/infrequent words and drives common-word embeddings toward zero.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on sentiment analysis and document classification with lower error rates than several baselines.  
- Motivation for improving document vectorization methods is sound.  
- Technical novelty is limited; core components largely rely on established techniques.  
- Primary advantages are simplicity and computational efficiency.  
- Recommends evaluating the method on a broader range of tasks to better establish its general efficacy.  
- Requests clarification whether the RNN-LM was trained to optimize classification performance or as a standard language model.  
- Asks whether the document representation from the RNN-LM was taken from the final hidden state or an average of all hidden states.  
- Suggests comparing against bidirectional LSTM representations (concatenated final states) on classification/sentiment tasks to strengthen results.
