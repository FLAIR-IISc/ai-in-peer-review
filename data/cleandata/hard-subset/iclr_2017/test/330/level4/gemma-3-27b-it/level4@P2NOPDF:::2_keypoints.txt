- Proposes averaging word embeddings with a novel data-dependent regularizer that emphasizes informative/infrequent words and suppresses common words by driving their embeddings toward zero.

- Reports state-of-the-art results on sentiment analysis and document classification compared to several baseline document embedding methods.

- Motivation for effective document encoding is sound, but technical novelty is limited since components are largely established.

- Main strengths are simplicity and computational efficiency rather than methodological innovation.

- Needs stronger empirical validation across a broader range of tasks to convincingly establish superiority.

- Clarify RNN-LM baseline setup: was it trained for classification or standard language modeling, and is the document representation the final hidden state or an average over time steps?

- Missing a key comparison to bidirectional LSTM document representations (concatenated final hidden states); including this would significantly strengthen the evaluation.
