- Proposes document embeddings formed by summing constituent word embeddings, with word and document embeddings learned jointly.
- Employs a dropout-based input corruption mechanism during training.
- Uses a word2vec-style loss and training procedure.
- Individual components (joint training, sum-of-words representation, dropout) are not novel on their own.
- Builds on prior work (e.g., Lebret & Collobert) and uses effectively standard dropout.
- Overall algorithmic novelty is limited, given reliance on established techniques.
- The simplicity yields a major practical advantage: extremely efficient inference (just average word embeddings).
- Model design encourages embeddings to capture global document information relevant for local context prediction.
- Empirical results are competitive on sentiment analysis and document classification despite the minimal design.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, highlighting clarity, efficiency, performance, and practical value.
