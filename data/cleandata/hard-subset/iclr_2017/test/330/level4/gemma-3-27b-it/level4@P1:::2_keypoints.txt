- Proposes representing documents as the average of word embeddings with a data-dependent corruption regularization favoring rare/informative words.  
- Shows competitive or state-of-the-art error rates on sentiment analysis and document classification benchmarks.  
- Main advantage is simplicity and computational speed of the proposed method.  
- Reviewer decision: Reject due to limited novelty and scope of experiments.  
- Technical contribution is viewed as incremental—largely an application of existing ideas like Word2Vec and denoising autoencoders.  
- The corruption-based regularization feels more like engineering than a fundamentally new approach.  
- Experimental scope is narrow (standard sentiment and classification tasks), weakening claims of broad effectiveness.  
- Claim of significant outperforming existing methods is not fully convincing given current experiments.  
- Paper would be stronger with broader task evaluation or deeper theoretical analysis of the regularization.  
- Writing is generally clear but could be more concise in places.  
- The link between the corruption mechanism and the data-dependent regularization needs a more intuitive explanation.  
- Ablation study (Table 3) demonstrating corruption effects is useful and appreciated.  
- Related work discussion is thorough and provides good context.  
- Missing comparison to a bidirectional LSTM baseline, which should be added.  
- Visualization in Figure 3 is helpful for understanding learned representations.  
- Questions for authors include details on RNN-LM training and representation extraction, comparison to bidirectional LSTM, choice and sensitivity of corruption rate q, impact of removing subsampling across methods, and reasons for weaker SICK semantic relatedness performance.
