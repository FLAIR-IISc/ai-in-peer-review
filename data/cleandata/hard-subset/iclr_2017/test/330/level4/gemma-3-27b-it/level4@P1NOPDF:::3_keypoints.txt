- Proposes document embeddings learned as a weighted sum of jointly trained word embeddings with random input dropout during training.  
- Components are not novel: joint word/document training and summation of word vectors previously explored (e.g., Lebret & Collobert).  
- The corruption mechanism is essentially standard input-layer dropout.  
- Combined with a word2vec-style loss and training, the approach offers limited methodological novelty.  
- Inference is very efficient—only averaging word embeddings—unlike more complex procedures such as Doc2Vec.  
- Resulting embeddings capture global document information useful for predicting local context.  
- Despite limited novelty, the model’s simplicity, efficiency, and strong empirical performance on sentiment and classification tasks justify acceptance.
