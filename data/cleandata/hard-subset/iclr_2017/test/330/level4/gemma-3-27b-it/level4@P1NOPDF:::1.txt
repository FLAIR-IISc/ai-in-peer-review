This paper presents a method for learning document vector representations using a skip-gram approach, enhanced with a regularizer in the form of a global context vector and dropout. While the individual components of this method are not novel, their combination appears to be. I particularly appreciated the detailed analysis of model behavior presented in Section 3.

The main weakness of this submission lies in its limited empirical evaluation. The choice of sentiment analysis and k-way classification tasks feels somewhat uninspired; more challenging benchmarks might have better demonstrated the method’s strengths. Furthermore, the inclusion of extensive t-SNE visualizations (occupying roughly two-thirds of a page) feels disproportionate and would be better replaced with additional analysis. 

Despite a somewhat reduced evaluation scope – and I concur with other reviewers regarding the lack of strong soft baseline comparisons – I recommend accepting this paper. It introduces an interesting and well-designed algorithm that is also computationally efficient. I believe other researchers would likely find the ideas presented here valuable and applicable to their own work.
