This paper presents a framework for learning document representations. The core idea is to represent a document as the average of its word embeddings, incorporating a data-dependent regularization term that prioritizes informative or rare words while driving the embeddings of common words towards zero. Experiments on sentiment analysis and document classification demonstrate that the proposed method achieves state-of-the-art error rates when compared to baseline document embedding techniques. While the motivation—finding an effective way to encode documents as vectors—is sound, the paper does not present substantial novel technical contributions. The techniques employed are largely established, and the primary advantage highlighted is the method’s simplicity and computational efficiency. Consequently, I would like to see results on a wider range of tasks to strengthen the claim that this represents a superior approach to learning document representations. Regarding the RNN-LM baseline, it is unclear whether the language model was trained to directly minimize classification error, or as a standard language model. Furthermore, please clarify whether the final hidden state, or the average of all hidden states, was used as the document representation. A particularly common and strong baseline for document representation is to employ a bidirectional LSTM and concatenate the final hidden states. It would be valuable to understand how the proposed method compares to this widely-used approach on tasks such as document classification and sentiment analysis.
