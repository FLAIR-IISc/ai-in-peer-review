- Introduces Doc2VecC: efficient document representation by averaging word embeddings with a corruption-based regularizer.  
- Builds on Word2Vec and Paragraph Vectors; not radically novel but well-motivated and effective.  
- Strong theoretical analysis, especially on the corruption mechanism acting as data-dependent regularization.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Main empirical concern: evaluation tasks (sentiment analysis, document classification) are conventional and may not showcase full potential.  
- Excessive t-SNE visualizations consume space without offering strong empirical value.  
- Baseline comparisons could be stronger; needs inclusion of more recent/state-of-the-art methods.  
- Suggest expanding experiments to harder tasks requiring deeper semantic understanding or reasoning.  
- Recommend refining visualizations to reveal why Doc2VecC yields better representations (semantic feature clustering).  
- Request a detailed ablation study on the corruption rate (q) to show its impact on performance.  
- Ask for a dedicated limitations section discussing datasets or document types where Doc2VecC might underperform.  
- Questions for authors: provide runtime comparisons (training/inference) versus other methods.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to quality/type of underlying word embeddings (GloVe, FastText, etc.).  
- Questions for authors: have alternative corruption strategies been explored beyond random masking?  
- Questions for authors: clarify why Paragraph Vectors performs poorly on word analogy tasks and whether this generalizes.  
- Questions for authors: give intuitive examples of how data-dependent regularization affects embeddings of frequent vs. rare or informative vs. non-discriminative words.
