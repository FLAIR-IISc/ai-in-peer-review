This paper presents a method for learning document vector representations using a skip-gram model augmented with a global context vector regularizer and dropout. While the individual components of this approach are not novel, their combination appears to be a unique contribution. I particularly valued the thorough analysis of model behavior presented in section 3.

The primary weakness of this submission lies in the limited scope of its empirical evaluation. The choice of sentiment analysis and k-way classification tasks feels somewhat uninspired, and the extensive use of t-SNE visualizations (occupying two-thirds of a page) would have been better utilized for more in-depth analysis. 

Despite a somewhat reduced evaluation, and agreeing with other reviewers regarding the lack of strong baseline comparisons, I recommend acceptance. The proposed algorithm is interesting, well-designed, and computationally efficient; therefore, it is likely that other researchers would find the presented ideas valuable.
