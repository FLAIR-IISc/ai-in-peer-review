This paper introduces a method for learning document embeddings using a skip-gram model enhanced with a global context vector regularizer and dropout. Although the individual components – skip-gram, global context vectors, and dropout – are established techniques, their combination represents a novel approach. I particularly valued the thorough behavioral analysis presented in Section 3, which provides valuable insight into the model’s operation.

The primary weakness of this submission lies in the limited scope of its empirical evaluation. While the results on sentiment analysis and k-way classification are presented, these tasks feel somewhat conventional and may not fully demonstrate the potential of the proposed method. The extensive use of t-SNE visualizations (occupying approximately two-thirds of a page) feels disproportionate; that space could have been better utilized for more in-depth analysis or additional experiments. 

I share the concerns of other reviewers regarding the lack of comparison to stronger, more recent baselines. However, despite this somewhat limited evaluation, I believe the paper warrants acceptance. The algorithm itself is interesting and well-designed, and its reported efficiency is a significant advantage. The ideas presented are likely to be useful to other researchers in the field, offering a practical and performant method for document representation learning.
