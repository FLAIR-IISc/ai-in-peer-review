- Proposes representing documents as the average of word embeddings with a data-dependent regularizer that upweights rare/informative words and drives common-word embeddings toward zero.  
- Reports state-of-the-art error rates on sentiment analysis and document classification compared to baseline document embedding methods.  
- Motivation is reasonable, but the paper lacks substantial novel technical contributions — methods are largely established.  
- Main advantages emphasized are simplicity and computational efficiency rather than methodological novelty.  
- Requests experiments on a wider range of tasks to better support claims of overall superiority.  
- RNN-LM baseline details are unclear: was it trained to minimize classification error or only as a standard language model?  
- Need clarification on whether the document representation from the RNN-LM is the final hidden state or the average of all hidden states.  
- Recommends comparing against a strong, common baseline — a bidirectional LSTM with concatenated final hidden states — on classification and sentiment tasks.
