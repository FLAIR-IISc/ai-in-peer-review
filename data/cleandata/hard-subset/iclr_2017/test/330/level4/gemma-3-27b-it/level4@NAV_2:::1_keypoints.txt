- Introduces Doc2VecC: average of word embeddings with a corruption model for data-dependent regularization.  
- Leverages Word2Vec efficiency while improving document representation quality.  
- Clear motivation and solid connection to existing word/document embedding literature.  
- Section 3 provides a detailed and insightful analysis of the corruption mechanism.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Empirical results on sentiment analysis and document classification are competitive given the model’s simplicity.  
- Major strength: significant speed and test-time efficiency.  
- Main limitation: narrow empirical evaluation that may not showcase full potential.  
- Excessive use of t-SNE visualizations consumes space that could be used for deeper analysis or experiments.  
- Baseline comparisons could be strengthened by including more recent, competitive methods.  
- Needs more detail on computational resources, scalability, parameter choices (e.g., corruption rate q), and which word types are most affected by corruption.  
- Suggested improvements: evaluate on harder tasks (topic modeling, information retrieval), compare to newer SOTA methods, report scalability/compute metrics, explore q’s impact, and analyze which parts-of-speech or semantic categories benefit most.
