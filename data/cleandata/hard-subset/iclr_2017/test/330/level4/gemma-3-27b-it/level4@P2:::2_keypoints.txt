- Introduces Doc2VecC: represent documents by averaging word embeddings with a corruption model for data-dependent regularization.  
- Corruption encourages informative/rare words to have stronger embeddings and suppresses common, less discriminative words.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance on sentiment analysis and document classification tasks.  
- Main strengths are simplicity, efficiency, and scalability compared to Paragraph Vectors.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection: contributions seen as incremental and insufficient given existing methods.  
- Experimental comparisons are limited; missing comparison to bidirectional LSTM baselines (a common SOTA).  
- Performance gains are present but not substantial enough to displace current best practices.  
- Detailed analysis of regularization (e.g., Table 3) is informative but not groundbreaking.  
- Suggested expand experiments to more tasks (e.g., topic modeling, information retrieval) and include BiLSTM comparisons.  
- Ask for clarification of RNN-LM baseline training objective and how document representations were extracted (final vs. averaged hidden states).  
- Recommend an ablation study quantifying the effect of corruption vs. no corruption.  
- Suggest providing a more formal theoretical justification of the regularization effect.  
- Advise explicitly discussing limitations, such as sensitivity to underlying word embedding quality.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to corruption rate q, computational resources/training time on large datasets, whether regularization is solely from corruption, and how averaging handles negation/contextual nuances.
