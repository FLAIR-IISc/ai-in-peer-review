- Proposal: represent a document as the average of its word embeddings with a data-dependent regularizer that emphasizes informative/rare words and drives common-word embeddings toward zero.  
- Empirical results: reports lower error rates than several baseline document embedding methods on sentiment analysis and document classification.  
- Novelty concern: motivation is sound but technical contributions are limited and many employed techniques are not new; main strengths appear to be simplicity and speed.  
- Validation concern: reviewer wants more compelling results across a broader range of tasks to be convinced the method is superior.  
- RNN-LM clarification: was the language model trained to directly minimize classification error or as a standard language model?  
- Representation extraction clarification: did you use the final hidden state or the average of all hidden states as the document vector?  
- Comparison request: compare against a bidirectional LSTM that concatenates final hidden states (a popular baseline) on document classification and sentiment tasks.
