- Proposes a document embedding method using a skip-gram/CBoW-style objective with a global context vector and dropout-based regularizer.  
- Individual components aren’t novel, but their combination is a worthwhile contribution.  
- Section 3.1 provides a detailed and appreciated analysis of model behavior.  
- Main limitation: empirical evaluation is weak—tasks (sentiment, document classification) are conventional and not sufficiently challenging.  
- Stronger, more recent baselines are needed for a rigorous comparison.  
- t-SNE visualizations (Figure 3) consume space that could be used for more substantive analysis or experiments.  
- Recommendation: accept—the algorithm is interesting, efficient, and clearly explained.  
- Simplicity and efficiency make the method likely to be useful to other researchers.  
- Theoretical justification of the corruption model as a data-dependent regularizer is a major strength.  
- Experimental results are competitive and show notable efficiency gains (Table 2).  
- Ablation study (Section 4.3) supports the benefit of the data-dependent regularization.  
- Suggested improvement: include stronger/deeper baselines (state-of-the-art document classification/semantic similarity models).  
- Suggested improvement: evaluate on tasks more sensitive to document-level understanding beyond sentiment/classification.  
- Suggested improvement: replace or supplement t-SNE with quantitative analyses of learned embeddings and semantic relationships.  
- Suggested improvement: discuss hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., corruption rate q).  
- Suggested improvement: explain how vocabulary size affects performance.  
- Suggested improvement: report computational resources (e.g., RAM) used for experiments.  
- Question for authors: why choose CBoW specifically—were other architectures (e.g., Skip-gram) explored?  
- Question for authors: provide detailed computational complexity analysis for training and inference compared to other methods.  
- Question for authors: how much does not retraining Skip-thought vectors affect baseline comparisons?  
- Question for authors: how does the method perform on very long documents—any special considerations or adjustments?  
- Question for authors: provide a more intuitive explanation of the data-dependent regularization mechanism.
