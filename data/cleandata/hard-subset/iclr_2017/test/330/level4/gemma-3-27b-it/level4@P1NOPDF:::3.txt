This paper proposes learning document embeddings as a weighted sum of constituent word embeddings, jointly trained and randomly dropped out (‘corrupted’) during training. While none of the individual components of this model are particularly novel, the result is an efficient learning algorithm for document representation with strong empirical performance.

Joint training of word and document embeddings is not a new idea, nor is representing a document as the sum of its word embeddings (see, for example, “The Sum of Its Parts”: Joint Learning of Word and Phrase Representations with Autoencoders by Lebret and Collobert). Furthermore, the corruption mechanism is simply traditional dropout applied to the input layer. Combined with the word2vec-style loss function and training methods, this paper offers limited novelty.

However, the model is very efficient at inference time, requiring only the averaging of word embeddings, unlike more complex inference procedures such as those found in Doc2Vec. Moreover, by design, the resulting embedding captures salient global document information – specifically, the information most useful for local-context prediction. Given its simplicity, the performance on sentiment analysis and document classification tasks is quite encouraging. 

Overall, despite its lack of significant novelty, the model’s simplicity, efficiency, and performance make it a worthwhile contribution that deserves wider readership and further study. I recommend acceptance.
