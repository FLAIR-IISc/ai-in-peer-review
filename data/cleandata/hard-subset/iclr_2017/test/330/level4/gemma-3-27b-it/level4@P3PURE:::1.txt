This paper presents a method for learning document vector representations using a skip-gram model with a novel regularizer: a global context vector combined with dropout techniques. While the individual components of this approach are not new, the combination appears to be a worthwhile contribution. I particularly appreciated the detailed analysis of model behavior presented in section 3.

The primary weakness of this submission lies in its limited empirical evaluation. The chosen tasks – sentiment analysis and k-way classification – feel somewhat uninspired, and more challenging benchmarks would have strengthened the results. Additionally, the inclusion of extensive t-SNE projections (occupying roughly two-thirds of a page) feels like an inefficient use of space, which could have been better utilized for further analysis or experimentation.

Despite a somewhat reduced evaluation, and agreeing with other reviewers regarding the importance of stronger soft baseline comparisons, I believe this paper warrants acceptance. It introduces an interesting and well-designed algorithm that is also computationally efficient. Consequently, it’s reasonable to anticipate that other researchers could find value in the ideas presented herein.
