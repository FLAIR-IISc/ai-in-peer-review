- Proposes learning document embeddings as the sum/average of jointly trained word embeddings with input dropout ('corruption').
- Components (sum-of-embeddings, joint training, dropout, word2vec-style objective) are not novel and have precedents.
- The combined approach yields an efficient algorithm for document representation.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on sentiment analysis and document classification.
- Major advantage is inference-time efficiency: only averaging word vectors, simpler than models like Doc2Vec.
- Model design helps embeddings capture global information useful for local context prediction.
- Despite limited originality, the paper’s simplicity, efficiency, and results justify acceptance.
