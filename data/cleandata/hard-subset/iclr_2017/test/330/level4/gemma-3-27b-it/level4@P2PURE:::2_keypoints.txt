- Proposes document representations by averaging word embeddings with a data-dependent regularizer that downweights frequent words and emphasizes rare/informative words.  
- Regularizer drives embeddings of frequent words toward zero to suppress their contribution.  
- Reports state-of-the-art performance on sentiment analysis and document classification, achieving the lowest error rates among compared baselines.  
- Motivation (efficient document encoding) is sound and the method is simple and computationally efficient.  
- Technical novelty is limited; employed techniques are largely established rather than novel.  
- Claiming the method as optimal requires evaluation on a broader range of tasks beyond the presented datasets.  
- RNN-LM experiment details are unclear: specify whether the language model was trained to minimize classification error or as a standard language model.  
- Need clarification on how the document representation from the RNN was derived: final hidden state vs. average of all hidden states.  
- Recommend comparing against a bidirectional LSTM that concatenates final hidden states, a common strong baseline, on classification and sentiment tasks.
