- Proposes Doc2VecC: learn word and document embeddings jointly and represent documents as the average of word embeddings with randomized word “corruption” during training.
- The individual components (averaging words, joint learning, dropout) are not novel and have precedents (e.g., Lebret & Collobert; standard input-layer dropout).
- Main contribution is the pragmatic combination of existing ideas rather than deep methodological novelty.
- Method is computationally efficient, especially at inference, since document vectors are simple averages of word embeddings.
- Learns embeddings that emphasize global document information by training to predict local context.
- Achieves strong empirical results on sentiment analysis and document classification despite its simplicity.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on simplicity, efficiency, and performance.
- Suggests adding a detailed Big-O comparison of training and inference costs with other methods.
- Asks for a clearer, intuitive explanation of how the corruption/dropout acts as data-dependent regularization.
- Recommends elaborating why Paragraph Vectors perform poorly on word-analogy tasks (possibly due to document-level focus).
- Requests an ablation study on different dropout rates (q) to show sensitivity and effect on embeddings.
- Wants discussion of limitations of averaging word embeddings for documents where word order or complex syntax is important.
