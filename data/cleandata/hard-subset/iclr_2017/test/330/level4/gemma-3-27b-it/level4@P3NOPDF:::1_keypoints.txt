- Proposes learning document vectors via skip-gram augmented with a global context vector and dropout regularization.
- Combination of established components is novel and well-presented.
- Section 3 provides a detailed analysis of model behavior and insightful interpretation of representations.
- Main weakness: empirical evaluation is narrow (only sentiment analysis and k-way classification).
- Excessive space devoted to t-SNE visualizations that could be reallocated to experiments, analysis, or related work.
- Lacks comparisons to stronger, more recent baselines, weakening claims of superiority.
- Algorithm is interesting, well-designed, and computationally efficient.
- Regularization strategy (global context + dropout) is promising and supported by ablation studies.
- Current results are sufficient for publication, though more comprehensive evaluation would strengthen the paper.
