This paper introduces a framework for generating document representations by averaging word embeddings, incorporating a data-dependent regularization technique. This regularization aims to emphasize informative and rare words while suppressing the contribution of frequent words by driving their corresponding embeddings towards zero. The method demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on sentiment analysis and document classification tasks, achieving the lowest error rates when compared to existing document embedding baselines. While the motivation – to efficiently encode documents into vector representations – is sound, the paper’s technical novelty appears limited. The techniques employed are largely established, and the primary advantage of the proposed method lies in its simplicity and computational efficiency. To substantiate its claim as an optimal approach to learning document representations, I would appreciate seeing results on a broader range of tasks. Regarding the RNN-LM experiments, clarification is needed on the training objective: was the language model trained to directly minimize classification error, or as a standard language model? Furthermore, please specify whether the document representation was derived from the final hidden state or an average of all hidden states. A common and highly effective approach to document representation involves utilizing a bidirectional LSTM and concatenating the final hidden states; it would be beneficial to see a comparative analysis between this approach and the proposed method on tasks such as document classification or sentiment analysis.
