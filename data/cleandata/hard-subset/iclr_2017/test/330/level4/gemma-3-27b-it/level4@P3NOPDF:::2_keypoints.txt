- Introduces a document representation method that averages word embeddings with a novel data-dependent regularization emphasizing rare/informative words and suppressing common words.  
- Regularization drives components of common-word embeddings toward zero to reduce their influence.  
- Shows competitive and sometimes state-of-the-art results on sentiment analysis and document classification benchmarks.  
- Motivation is sound and the approach is conceptually clear and computationally efficient.  
- Technical contribution is incremental: builds on established techniques and mainly innovates by combining averaging with data-dependent regularization.  
- Claims of significant advancement need stronger empirical evidence.  
- Evaluation is limited to two tasks; broader benchmarks are recommended to demonstrate generalizability and robustness.  
- Experimental details are unclear for the RNN-LM baseline: training objective (direct classification vs. standard LM + classifier) needs clarification.  
- Need clarification on how RNN document representations were extracted (final hidden state vs. averaged hidden states).  
- Important missing comparison: bidirectional LSTM concatenation (a strong baseline); include accuracy and computational cost comparisons.  
- Overall: a simple, efficient method with promising results but constrained by limited evaluation and incremental novelty; addressing the above issues would strengthen the paper.
