- Proposes document embeddings formed by summing constituent word embeddings, with word vectors learned jointly with document vectors.  
- Applies random dropout to word embeddings during training (akin to standard input-layer dropout).  
- Individual components (joint training, sum-of-words representation, dropout) are not novel on their own.  
- Overall novelty is limited, as the method relies on a word2vec-style loss and standard training procedures.  
- The combination yields a computationally efficient algorithm for generating document embeddings (simple averaging, no complex inference).  
- Summation-based construction encourages embeddings to capture global document information useful for predicting local word context.  
- Achieves strong empirical performance on sentiment analysis and document classification tasks.  
- Simplicity and efficiency make the model well-suited for scenarios requiring fast document embedding generation.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, noting the work’s practical value and broad interest to the representation learning community.
