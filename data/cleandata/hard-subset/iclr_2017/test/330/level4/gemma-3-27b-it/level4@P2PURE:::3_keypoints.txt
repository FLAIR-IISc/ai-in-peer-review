- Introduces a method that represents documents as the sum of word embeddings learned jointly with random input dropout during training.
- Each component (joint word/document training, sum-of-word representation, input-dropout) has prior work and is not novel individually.
- Cites related work (e.g., Lebret and Collobert) showing similar joint learning approaches.
- The corruption mechanism is effectively standard input-layer dropout combined with a word2vec-style loss and training procedure.
- Overall methodological novelty is limited due to reuse of established techniques.
- The combination yields an efficient algorithm that achieves strong empirical performance on sentiment analysis and document classification.
- Inference is computationally cheap (just averaging word embeddings) compared to more complex methods like Doc2Vec.
- Embeddings capture salient global document information useful for local-context prediction.
- Despite limited novelty, the model’s simplicity, efficiency, and strong results justify acceptance.
