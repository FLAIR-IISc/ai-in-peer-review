- Proposes Doc2VecC: document represented as average of word embeddings with a corruption-based data-dependent regularization that downweights common words and favors informative/rare words.  
- Demonstrates competitive or state-of-the-art results on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.  
- Strengths: clear motivation, good summary of related work, well-designed experiments across multiple tasks, statistically significant gains, insightful analysis of the regularization, and notable training/inference efficiency.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form despite promising results.  
- Main critique: technical novelty is limited—averaging embeddings is not new and the corruption regularizer builds on existing techniques.  
- Empirical critique: evaluation does not convincingly show a clear advantage over strong existing methods to justify acceptance at a selective conference.  
- Suggested improvement: provide more extensive experiments or deeper theoretical analysis to more compellingly demonstrate benefits.  
- Specific baseline missing: comparison to strong current models such as bidirectional LSTMs (concatenated final hidden states) on classification and sentiment tasks.  
- Unclear RNNLM baseline details: whether it was trained to minimize classification error or only as a language model, and whether final hidden state or average of hidden states was used as representation.  
- Hyperparameter concern: need clarification on corruption rate q (why 0.9 was chosen and sensitivity to this parameter).
