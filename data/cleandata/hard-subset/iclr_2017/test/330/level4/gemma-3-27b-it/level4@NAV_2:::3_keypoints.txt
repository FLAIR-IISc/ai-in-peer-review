- Proposes learning document embeddings as the sum of jointly learned word embeddings with random word dropout during training.
- Model components (joint word/document training, sum-of-words representation) are not novel and echo prior work (e.g., Lebret & Collobert).
- The corruption mechanism is analogous to standard input-layer dropout and, combined with a Word2Vec-style loss, offers limited methodological novelty.
- Despite limited novelty, the method yields an efficient generation-time algorithm (simple averaging vs. costly inference like Paragraph Vectors).
- The construction promotes embeddings that capture global document information useful for predicting local context.
- Empirical results are encouraging on sentiment analysis and document classification given the model’s simplicity.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on simplicity, efficiency, and performance.
- Requests a more detailed ablation study on the corruption/dropout mechanism, specifically performance when dropout rate = 0.
- Asks for clearer characterization of which document types/tasks benefit most from this approach versus more complex models (e.g., Skip-thought, LSTM).
