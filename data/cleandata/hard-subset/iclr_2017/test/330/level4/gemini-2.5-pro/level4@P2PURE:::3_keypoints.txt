- Proposes learning document embeddings by summing jointly learned word embeddings with a random "corruption" (dropout) during training.
- The method's components (joint training and sum composition) are not novel and mirror prior work like Lebret & Collobert.
- The corruption mechanism is effectively standard input-layer dropout.
- Combined with a word2vec-style loss and training, the paper offers limited novelty.
- Practically advantageous: very efficient inference (simple averaging vs. optimization required by models like Doc2Vec).
- By design, document vectors capture global information useful for predicting local word contexts.
- Empirically strong performance on sentiment analysis and document classification tasks.
- Conclusion: despite limited novelty, the model’s simplicity, efficiency, and strong results justify acceptance.
