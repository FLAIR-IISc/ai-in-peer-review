- Proposes learning document embeddings by jointly training word embeddings and a document vector represented as the sum of its word embeddings with input-word corruption (dropout).
- Core components are not novel individually; sum-of-word embeddings and joint word/document learning have prior work (e.g., Lebret & Collobert 2014).
- The corruption mechanism is essentially dropout on input words and the objective is heavily inspired by word2vec.
- The specific combination of these elements is unique but does not introduce a fundamentally new concept.
- Method is simple, efficient, and yields strong empirical performance on sentiment analysis and document classification.
- Major practical advantage: fast and scalable inference—new document embeddings require only averaging pre-trained word vectors, unlike Doc2Vec’s costly inference step.
- Training objective gives an intuitive justification for why summed compositional vectors capture global semantic context beyond bag-of-words.
- Performance is competitive with more complex models, making this a strong, practical baseline.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, arguing the trade-off of limited novelty for gains in simplicity and efficiency is valuable to the community.
