This paper proposes a method for computing document vector representations by extending a skip-gram model with a global context vector. This global vector acts as a regularizer, and dropout is applied to its components during training. While the individual techniques—skip-gram learning, global context vectors, and dropout—are well-established, their novel combination in this context is a key contribution. A significant strength of the paper is the detailed analysis of the model's behavior presented in Section 3, which offers valuable insights.

The primary weakness of this submission lies in its empirical evaluation. The chosen tasks, primarily sentiment analysis and k-way classification, are somewhat limited and may not fully demonstrate the method's capabilities compared to more challenging benchmarks. Furthermore, the extensive space (approximately two-thirds of a page) dedicated to t-SNE visualizations could have been more effectively utilized for deeper quantitative analysis or ablation studies, which would have strengthened the paper's empirical claims.

Despite these concerns regarding the scope of the evaluation and the use of relatively weak baselines—an issue also noted by other reviewers—I recommend acceptance. The proposed algorithm is interesting, well-presented, and highly efficient. The core ideas are clearly articulated and are likely to be valuable to other researchers in the community. Therefore, the paper's conceptual contributions and potential for inspiring future work outweigh its empirical shortcomings.
