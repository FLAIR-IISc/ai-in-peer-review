- Proposes document vector representations by extending skip-gram with a global context vector that acts as a regularizer.  
- Applies dropout to components of the global context vector during training.  
- Combination of skip-gram, global context vectors, and dropout is novel in this setting despite each technique being well-known.  
- Section 3 provides a detailed analysis of model behavior and yields valuable insights.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: tasks focus mainly on sentiment analysis and k-way classification.  
- Chosen benchmarks are not sufficiently challenging to fully demonstrate the method's capabilities.  
- Too much space (~2/3 page) is devoted to t-SNE visualizations instead of deeper quantitative analysis or ablation studies.  
- Baselines used are relatively weak, an issue also noted by other reviewers.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — algorithm is interesting, well-presented, efficient, and likely useful to the community despite empirical shortcomings.
