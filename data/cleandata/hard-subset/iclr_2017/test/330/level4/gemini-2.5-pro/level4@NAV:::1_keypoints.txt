- Proposes a method for learning document vectors using a skip-gram objective.  
- Regularizes the model by introducing a global context vector with dropout.  
- Constituent components are not novel individually, but their synthesis appears novel.  
- Section 3 offers insightful analysis of the model’s behavior.  
- Main limitation: empirical evaluation is narrow, limited to standard tasks (sentiment analysis and k-way classification).  
- Stronger evidence would come from testing on more challenging benchmarks.  
- Excessive space devoted to t-SNE projections (two-thirds of a page) could be better used for quantitative analysis.  
- Uses non-competitive baselines, a concern shared by other reviewers.  
- Reviewer leans toward acceptance despite reservations.  
- Algorithm is interesting, elegant, computationally efficient, and likely practically useful to researchers.
