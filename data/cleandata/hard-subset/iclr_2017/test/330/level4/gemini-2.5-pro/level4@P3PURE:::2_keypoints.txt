- Proposes document representation as a weighted average of word embeddings with a data-dependent regularizer that upweights informative/rare words and pushes common-word embeddings toward zero.  
- Evaluated on sentiment analysis and document classification, showing lower error rates than the selected baselines.  
- Motivation and goal (efficient, effective document encoding) are well-founded; method is simple and computationally efficient.  
- Primary weakness: limited technical novelty—techniques are largely established and contribution is mainly their combination.  
- Empirical weakness: evaluation is not sufficiently comprehensive; needs demonstration across a broader range of tasks.  
- Clarification needed on RNN-LM baseline: was it fine-tuned for classification or pre-trained, and was the document representation the final hidden state or an average of hidden states?  
- Suggestion: compare against stronger baselines (e.g., bidirectional LSTM with concatenated final states) to better assess performance.
