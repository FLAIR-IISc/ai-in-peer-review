- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as the average of word embeddings with random-word corruption during training.  
- Corruption acts as a data-dependent regularizer that upweights informative/rare words and shrinks common non-discriminative word embeddings.  
- Claims high efficiency, scalability, simplicity, and strong performance on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.  
- Recommendation: reject.  
- Primary reason for rejection: lacks significant technical novelty—combines existing ideas rather than introducing a fundamentally new approach.  
- Supporting: model builds on Word2Vec CBOW, uses a dropout-like corruption, and averages word vectors (a common baseline).  
- Secondary reason for rejection: experimental evidence is not comprehensive enough to establish general superiority.  
- Experiments need stronger and more contemporary baselines to validate claims.  
- Missing important baseline: bidirectional LSTM (Bi-LSTM) document encoders for sentiment/document classification.  
- Suggested improvement: include Bi-LSTM comparisons to contextualize speed/simplicity vs. expressive power trade-offs.  
- Question for authors: Was the RNN-LM trained only as a language model or fine-tuned for classification, and how was the document representation extracted (final hidden state, average of hidden states, etc.)?
