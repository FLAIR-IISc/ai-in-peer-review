This paper presents a method for computing document vector representations by augmenting a skip-gram learning mechanism with a regularizer. This regularizer takes the form of a global context vector to which dropout is applied. While the individual components of this model are not new, their combination in this specific manner appears to be a novel contribution. A particular strength of the paper is the detailed analysis of the model's behavior in Section 3.

The primary weakness of this submission is its empirical evaluation. The chosen tasks, sentiment analysis and k-way classification, are somewhat limited; the paper would have been strengthened by evaluation on more diverse and challenging benchmarks. Similarly, the significant space (two-thirds of a page) dedicated to t-SNE projections could have been more effectively used for further analysis.

Despite the limited evaluation and my agreement with other reviewers about the weakness of the baselines, I recommend that this paper be accepted. The algorithm itself is interesting, well-presented, and very efficient. As such, it is reasonable to expect that the ideas presented here will be useful to other researchers in the community.
