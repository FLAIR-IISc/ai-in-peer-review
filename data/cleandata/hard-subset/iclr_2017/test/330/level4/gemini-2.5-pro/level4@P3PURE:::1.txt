This paper presents a method for learning document vector representations using a skip-gram style learning mechanism. The model is regularized by a global context vector that incorporates dropout. While the individual components are not novel, their combination in this manner appears to be. A key strength of the paper is the detailed analysis of model behavior presented in Section 3.

The primary weakness of this submission lies in its empirical evaluation. The chosen tasks, sentiment analysis and k-way classification, are arguably not the most compelling or challenging benchmarks. Furthermore, the inclusion of t-SNE projections, which occupy two-thirds of a page, seems to be a suboptimal use of space that could have been better utilized for more substantial analysis.

While I am disappointed by the limited evaluation and concur with other reviewers regarding the weakness of the baselines, I recommend that this paper be accepted. The proposed algorithm is interesting, well-presented, and highly efficient. Therefore, it is reasonable to assume that the ideas presented here will be of use to the research community.
