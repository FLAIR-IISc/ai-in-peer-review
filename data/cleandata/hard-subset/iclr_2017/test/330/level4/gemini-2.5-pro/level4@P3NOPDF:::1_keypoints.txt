- Proposes document vectors by adapting skip-gram with a global document context vector regularized by dropout.  
- Novel combination: applying dropout to a global context vector is an interesting, intuitive idea.  
- Section 3 offers a detailed analysis that provides valuable insights into model behavior.  
- Paper is well-written, conceptually clear, and computationally efficient.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited in scope, focusing mainly on sentiment analysis and k-way classification.  
- Baselines are weak; comparisons to more recent or state-of-the-art embedding methods are missing.  
- Excessive use of page space on t-SNE visualizations; better spent on quantitative analysis or ablations.  
- Lacks ablation studies (e.g., impact of dropout rate) and results on additional, more challenging tasks (IR, semantic textual similarity).  
- Recommendation: leans toward acceptance because the core idea is interesting, practical, and likely useful to the community despite experimental shortcomings.
