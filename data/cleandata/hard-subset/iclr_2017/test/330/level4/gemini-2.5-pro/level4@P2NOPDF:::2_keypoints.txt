- Proposes weighted average of word embeddings with data-dependent regularization to up-weight informative words and push common words toward zero.  
- Shows lower error rates on sentiment analysis and document classification compared to reported baselines.  
- Strength: very simple, efficient, and fast way to produce fixed-size document vectors.  
- Strength: clear motivation to filter out non-essential words.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance on the two presented tasks.  
- Weakness: limited technical novelty — uses well-known components (embeddings, weighted averaging, regularization) in a simple combination.  
- Weakness: empirical validation is narrow; needs evaluation on a broader set of tasks to establish generality.  
- Weakness: missing comparison to bidirectional RNN/LSTM baselines (e.g., BiLSTM with pooled or attention-weighted states).  
- Question: Was the RNN-LM baseline trained only as a language model or fine-tuned on the downstream classification task?  
- Question: How was the final document representation from the RNN produced — final hidden state, average of all hidden states, or another pooling method?
