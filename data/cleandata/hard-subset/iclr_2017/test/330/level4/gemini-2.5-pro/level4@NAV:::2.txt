This paper proposes a method for generating document embeddings. The core concept involves representing a document as a weighted average of its constituent word vectors, guided by a data-driven regularization term. This regularization amplifies the influence of informative or infrequent words while pushing the embeddings of common words toward the zero vector. On sentiment analysis and document classification tasks, the authors report that their approach achieves lower error rates than the baseline methods evaluated.

While the motivation to find an optimal document encoding is commendable, the paper's technical contributions appear limited. The framework is primarily a combination of existing techniques, with its primary strengths being simplicity and computational efficiency. For this reason, the claim that this is the best approach to learning document representations would be substantially strengthened by demonstrating strong performance across a broader range of tasks beyond the two presented.

Further clarification is needed regarding the RNN-LM baseline. Was the model's training objective language modeling, or was it fine-tuned to minimize classification error for the downstream task? Additionally, was the document vector derived from the final hidden state or an average of all hidden states? A highly relevant baseline in contemporary research involves using a bidirectional LSTM and concatenating the final hidden states to represent the document. The evaluation would be significantly more compelling if it included a comparison against this strong approach for the reported tasks.
