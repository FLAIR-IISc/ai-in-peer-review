This paper presents a technique for learning document vector representations based on a skip-gram learning objective. The model is regularized through the inclusion of a global context vector that is subjected to dropout. While the constituent elements of the model are not new in themselves, their synthesis in the proposed manner appears novel. Furthermore, the paper's strength is enhanced by the insightful analysis of the model’s behavior provided in Section 3.

The submission's main limitation, however, lies in its empirical evaluation. The experiments are confined to relatively standard tasks—namely sentiment analysis and k-way classification—whereas more challenging benchmarks could have provided stronger evidence for the method's effectiveness. Similarly, the allocation of two-thirds of a page to t-SNE projections seems suboptimal, as this space might have been better utilized for deeper quantitative analysis.

Despite my reservations regarding the limited scope of the evaluation and in agreement with other reviewers about the use of non-competitive baselines, I lean towards acceptance. The proposed algorithm is interesting, elegantly constructed, and computationally efficient. As such, it is reasonable to conclude that the ideas presented will be of practical value to other researchers in the field.
