- Proposes learning document vectors via a skip-gram style method regularized by a global context vector with dropout.  
- Individual components are not novel, but their specific combination appears novel.  
- Section 3 provides a detailed and valuable analysis of model behavior.  
- Primary weakness: empirical evaluation is limited; chosen tasks (sentiment analysis and k-way classification) are not very compelling or challenging.  
- Excessive use of space for t-SNE projections (two-thirds of a page) is a suboptimal allocation; could be replaced with more substantive analysis.  
- Baselines used in experiments are weak (concurs with other reviewers).  
- Recommendation: accept — the algorithm is interesting, well-presented, highly efficient, and likely useful to the community.
