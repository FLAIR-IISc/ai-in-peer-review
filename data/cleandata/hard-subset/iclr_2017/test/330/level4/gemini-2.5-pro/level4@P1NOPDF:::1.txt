This paper proposes a method for computing vector representations for documents using a skip-gram style learning objective. The model is regularized by a global context vector, which is combined with several forms of dropout. While the individual components are not novel in themselves, their combination in the proposed manner appears to be. Furthermore, the detailed analysis of the model's behavior in Section 3 is a significant strength of the paper.

The main weakness of this submission is the empirical evaluation. The chosen tasks—sentiment analysis and k-way classification—are arguably not the most challenging or insightful benchmarks available. Similarly, the considerable space (nearly two-thirds of a page) dedicated to t-SNE projections could have been more productively used for deeper analysis.

Despite my reservations about the limited evaluation, and in agreement with other reviewers concerning the relatively weak baselines, I lean towards acceptance. The paper introduces an interesting, well-presented, and highly efficient algorithm. Given these qualities, it is likely that other researchers will find the ideas presented here useful.
