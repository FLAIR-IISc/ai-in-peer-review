- Proposes representing a document as a weighted average of word embeddings with a data-dependent regularizer that up-weights informative/rare words and pushes common-word embeddings toward zero; reports lower error on sentiment and classification tasks.
- Technical novelty is limited; method's value is mainly its simplicity and speed, so empirical evidence must be strong to be convincing.
- Current experiments are insufficiently broad (only two tasks); need wider task coverage to claim general superiority.
- Clarify RNN-LM baseline training: was it trained only with a language-model objective or fine-tuned for the downstream classification task?
- Clarify RNN-LM representation: was the document embedding taken from the final hidden state or the average of hidden states?
- Recommend comparing against a stronger contemporary baseline, e.g., a bidirectional LSTM using concatenated final hidden states, on the reported tasks.
