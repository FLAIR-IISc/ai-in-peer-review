- Proposes learning document representations by summing constituent word embeddings with jointly learned word vectors.  
- Introduces a random dropout-like "corruption" of inputs during training.  
- Individual components (co-training words/docs, summed word vectors) are not novel.  
- Summation approach similar to prior work (e.g., Lebret and Collobert's "The Sum of Its Parts").  
- The proposed "corruption" is functionally equivalent to standard input-layer dropout.  
- Training uses a word2vec-inspired loss and methodology.  
- Overall theoretical novelty is marginal.  
- Practical advantages: extremely efficient inference (simple averaging) compared to heavier methods like Doc2Vec.  
- Learned embeddings capture global semantics useful for local context prediction.  
- Strong empirical performance on sentiment analysis and document classification benchmarks.  
- Conclusion: simplicity, efficiency, and performance make the model a valuable contribution; recommend acceptance.
