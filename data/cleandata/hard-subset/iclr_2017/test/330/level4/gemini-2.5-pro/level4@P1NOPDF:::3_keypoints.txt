- Proposes learning document embeddings by averaging jointly learned word and document vectors with random word dropout during training.
- Model components and averaging representation are not novel (e.g., Lebret & Collobert 2014).
- The 'corruption' dropout is equivalent to standard input-layer dropout and, with a word2vec-style loss, offers limited technical novelty.
- Very efficient at inference compared to Doc2Vec since embeddings are computed by simple averaging rather than optimization.
- Embeddings capture salient global document information useful for predicting words in local contexts.
- Shows strong empirical performance on sentiment analysis and document classification despite model simplicity.
- Overall: simple, efficient, strong baseline; worthwhile contribution and recommended for acceptance.
