- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as the average of word embeddings trained with an input corruption (dropout) mechanism.
- Frames corruption as a data-dependent regularizer that downweights common, non-discriminative words and preserves informative rare words.
- Model is extremely efficient at training and inference—document vectors are computed by simple averaging at test time.
- Achieves strong empirical performance (sentiment, document classification, semantic relatedness) and competes with or outperforms complex baselines.
- Joint training with corruption also yields higher-quality word embeddings (better word analogy results).
- Contribution is primarily an effective synthesis of known ideas plus a principled theoretical analysis, not a new architectural paradigm.
- Recommendation: Accept, based on practicality/efficiency and strong empirical results.
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the corruption rate q (suggest ablation across q values like 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 0.9)?
- Request: Add a "Word2Vec + Average" baseline to isolate gains from joint training and corruption versus simple composition.
- Request: Provide empirical validation of the regularization effect (e.g., plotting L2 norms of frequent vs. rare word embeddings during training).
