- Proposes a simple document embedding method: sum/average word embeddings, trained jointly with word vectors and input-word "corruption" (dropout).
- Individual components (sum-of-words, joint training) are not novel and overlap with prior work (e.g., Lebret & Collobert 2014).
- The proposed "corruption" is functionally equivalent to standard input-layer dropout.
- Overall contribution is an effective synthesis of existing techniques rather than a new paradigm.
- Key advantage is efficiency at inference—no per-document optimization needed (unlike Doc2Vec).
- Training by predicting local word contexts from a global document vector incentivizes salient semantic capture.
- Delivers surprisingly strong empirical results on sentiment analysis and document classification benchmarks.
- Trade-off: limited technical novelty but significant gains in simplicity, efficiency, and competitive performance.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance and considers the model a valuable, practical baseline.
