This paper proposes a framework for creating document representations by averaging word embeddings with a data-dependent regularization term. This term favors informative or rare words while forcing common words to have values close to 0.

Experiments on sentiment analysis and document classification demonstrate that the proposed method achieves the lowest error rates compared to baseline document embedding methods. 

While the motivation to find an effective way to encode a document into a vector is compelling, the paper does not offer significant technical contributions. Most of the techniques presented are not new, and the primary advantage of the proposed method lies in its simplicity and speed.

To be convinced that this is a superior approach to learning document representations, I would like to see strong results on more than two tasks. 

Regarding the RNN-LM baseline, it is unclear whether the language model was trained to minimize classification error or as a standard language model. Additionally, was the final hidden state used as the representation, or was the average of all hidden states used?

One widely used method for representing documents is to employ a bidirectional LSTM and concatenate the final hidden states as the document representation. I believe it would be useful to compare the proposed method to this approach for tasks such as document classification or sentiment analysis, to provide a more comprehensive understanding of its effectiveness.
