This paper proposes a novel method for computing vector representations of documents by leveraging a skip-gram style learning mechanism, augmented with a global context vector regularizer and incorporating various dropout techniques. Although the individual components presented in this paper are not new, the specific combination of these elements appears to be an original contribution. I particularly appreciated the detailed analysis of model behavior presented in Section 3, which provides valuable insights into the proposed approach.

The primary drawback of this submission lies in its relatively limited empirical evaluation. The choice of sentiment analysis and k-way classification tasks may not be the most compelling, as there are potentially more interesting and challenging tasks that could have been explored. Furthermore, dedicating two-thirds of a page to t-SNE projections seems unnecessary, and this space could have been utilized more effectively for additional analysis or results.

While I share some disappointment with the limited evaluation and concur with the other reviewers regarding the use of soft baselines, I believe that this paper merits acceptance. The proposed algorithm is intriguing, well-composed, and efficient, making it reasonable to assume that other readers may find the ideas presented here useful. Overall, the paper's strengths outweigh its weaknesses, and its potential contribution to the field warrants publication.
