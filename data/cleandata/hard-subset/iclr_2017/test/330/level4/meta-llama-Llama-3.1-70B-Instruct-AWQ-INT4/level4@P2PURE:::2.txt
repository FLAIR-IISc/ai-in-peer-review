This paper introduces a framework for generating document representations by averaging word embeddings with a data-dependent regularization scheme that prioritizes informative or rare words while suppressing common words. The proposed method is evaluated on sentiment analysis and document classification tasks, yielding lower error rates compared to baseline document embedding methods.

Although the motivation to find an optimal method for encoding documents into vectors is commendable, the paper lacks substantial technical contributions. Most of the techniques employed are not novel, and the primary advantage of the proposed method lies in its simplicity and efficiency. To be convinced of the method's efficacy, I would like to see its performance on a more diverse range of tasks.

Regarding the RNN-LM, it is unclear whether the language model is trained to minimize classification error or as a traditional language model. Additionally, it would be helpful to know whether the final hidden state or the average of all hidden states is used as the representation.

A widely used approach for document representation involves employing a bidirectional LSTM and concatenating the final hidden states. I believe it would be beneficial to compare the proposed method to this approach on tasks such as document classification or sentiment analysis to further assess its effectiveness.
