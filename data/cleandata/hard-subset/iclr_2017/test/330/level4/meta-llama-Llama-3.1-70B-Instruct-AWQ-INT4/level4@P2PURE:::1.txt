This paper presents a method for computing vector representations of documents using a skip-gram style learning mechanism, augmented with a global context vector regularizer and various dropout rates. Although the individual components are not novel, the specific combination of these elements appears to be a new contribution. I appreciated the detailed analysis of model behavior in Section 3, which provides valuable insights.

The primary drawback of this submission lies in its limited empirical evaluation. The choice of sentiment analysis and k-way classification tasks may not be the most compelling, and alternative tasks might have better demonstrated the method's capabilities. Additionally, dedicating two-thirds of a page to t-SNE projections seems unnecessary, and this space could have been utilized for more in-depth analysis.

While I share the disappointment of the other reviewers regarding the reduced evaluation and the use of soft baselines, I believe this paper warrants acceptance. The algorithm is interesting, well-composed, and efficient, making it a reasonable assumption that other readers may find the presented ideas useful.
