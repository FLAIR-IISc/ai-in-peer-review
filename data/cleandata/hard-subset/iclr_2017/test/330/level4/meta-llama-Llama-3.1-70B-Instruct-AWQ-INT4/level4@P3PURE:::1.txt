This paper proposes a method for computing vector representations of documents using a skip-gram style learning mechanism, augmented with a global context vector regularizer and various dropout rates. Although the individual components are not novel, the specific combination presented in this paper appears to be new. The detailed analysis of model behavior in Section 3 is also noteworthy.

The primary drawback of this submission lies in its relatively weak empirical evaluation. The choice of sentiment analysis and k-way classification tasks may not be the most compelling, and alternative tasks could have provided more insight. Additionally, dedicating two-thirds of a page to t-SNE projections seems unnecessary, as this space could have been utilized for further analysis.

Despite being somewhat disappointed by the limited evaluation and concurring with other reviewers regarding the soft baselines, I believe this paper warrants acceptance. The proposed algorithm is interesting, well-composed, and efficient, making it reasonable to assume that other readers may find value in the ideas presented here.
