**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes an efficient document representation learning framework, Document Vector through Corruption (Doc2VecC). The framework represents each document as a simple average of word embeddings, ensuring that the representation captures the semantic meanings of the document during learning. A corruption model is included, which introduces a data-dependent regularization that favors informative or rare words while forcing the embeddings of common and non-discriminative ones to be close to zero. The authors demonstrate that Doc2VecC produces significantly better word embeddings than Word2Vec and achieves state-of-the-art performance on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness tasks.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

I decide to accept this paper, with the following two key reasons:

1. The combination of a skip-gram style learning mechanism with a global context vector and various bits of drop out is novel and interesting.
2. The paper provides a detailed analysis of model behavior in section 3, which is appreciated.

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper's main contribution is the introduction of a corruption model that acts as a data-dependent regularization, which favors informative or rare words while forcing the embeddings of common and non-discriminative ones to be close to zero. This approach is shown to be effective in capturing the semantic meanings of documents during learning. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of model behavior in section 3, which helps to understand the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed approach.

**Additional Feedback**

While the paper is well-written and the ideas are interesting, I have some suggestions for improvement:

* The empirical evaluation is limited to sentiment analysis and k-way classification tasks. It would be beneficial to include more diverse tasks to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
* The use of t-SNE projections takes up a significant amount of space in the paper. Consider replacing this with further analysis or results to strengthen the paper.
* Some of the baselines used in the evaluation are soft, as noted by other reviewers. Consider including more robust baselines to provide a more comprehensive evaluation.

**Questions for the Authors**

* Can you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process for the proposed approach?
* How does the corruption rate q affect the performance of the model, and what is the optimal value for q?
* Have you considered applying the proposed approach to other tasks, such as text summarization or question answering?
