- Proposes learning document embeddings as the sum of constituent word embeddings, with jointly learned word vectors and random dropout ('corruption') during training.
- The building blocks are not novel: joint training of word and document embeddings and sum-of-words document representation have prior work (e.g., Lebret & Collobert).
- The corruption mechanism is essentially standard input-layer dropout.
- Combined with a word2vec-style loss and training, the method offers limited novelty.
- The model is very efficient at generation/inference, requiring only averaging word embeddings versus costly inference in Doc2Vec.
- By design the embedding captures salient global document information that aids local-context prediction.
- Empirical results on sentiment analysis and document classification are encouraging given the model's simplicity.
- Conclusion/recommendation: despite limited novelty, the simplicity, efficiency, and performance merit acceptance and wider readership.
