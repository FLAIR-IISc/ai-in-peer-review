This paper presents a method for computing vector representations of documents using a skip-gram style learning mechanism, augmented with a global context vector regularizer and various dropout rates. Although the individual components are not new, the combination of these elements appears to be novel. I appreciated the detailed analysis of model behavior in Section 3, which provides valuable insights into the proposed approach.

The main limitation of this submission lies in its relatively weak empirical evaluation. The choice of sentiment analysis and k-way classification as the primary tasks may not be the most compelling, as there are arguably more interesting and challenging tasks that could have been explored. Furthermore, dedicating two-thirds of a page to t-SNE projections seems unnecessary, and this space could have been utilized more effectively for further analysis or additional experiments.

While I share the disappointment of the other reviewers regarding the reduced evaluation and the use of soft baselines, I believe this paper should be accepted. The algorithm is interesting, well-composed, and efficient, making it reasonable to assume that other readers may find some of the ideas presented here useful.
