- Proposes learning document embeddings as the sum of jointly learned word embeddings with random dropout ("corruption") during training.
- Individual components lack novelty: joint word-document training and sum-of-words representation have prior work (e.g., Lebret & Collobert).
- The corruption mechanism is essentially standard input-layer dropout.
- Combined with a word2vec-style loss and training, the method offers limited novel contributions.
- Method is highly efficient at generation time, requiring only averaging word embeddings instead of complex inference like Doc2Vec.
- Embeddings capture salient global information that aids local-context prediction.
- Simple model shows strong empirical performance on sentiment analysis and document classification.
- Despite limited novelty, the paper’s simplicity, efficiency, and performance warrant acceptance.
