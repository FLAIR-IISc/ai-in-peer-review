- Paper proposes averaging word embeddings with data-dependent regularization that upweights rare/informative words and shrinks common words; reports lowest error on sentiment and document classification baselines.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection.  
- Main reason 1: Limited technical novelty — techniques are mostly not new; main contribution is simplicity and speed.  
- Main reason 2: Limited empirical validation — experiments only on two tasks, insufficient to claim general applicability.  
- Supporting concern: Claims of efficiency are unclear because computational complexity is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Supporting concern: Reviewer not convinced the method is optimal for document encoding.  
- Question about RNN-LM baseline: Was it trained as a classifier or as a standard language model, and which hidden-state aggregation was used (final vs. average)?  
- Request to compare with more recent approaches (e.g., bidirectional LSTM with concatenated final states) on the same tasks.  
- Suggestion: Provide detailed computational complexity analysis and comparisons to existing methods.  
- Suggestion: Evaluate on more datasets/tasks to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Suggestion: Add interpretability analyses of learned word embeddings and document representations to show what the model captures.
