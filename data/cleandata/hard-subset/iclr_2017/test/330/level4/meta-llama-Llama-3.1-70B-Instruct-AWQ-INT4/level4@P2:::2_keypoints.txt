- Paper proposes Doc2VecC: document vectors formed by averaging word embeddings with a data-dependent corruption regularizer that suppresses common/non-discriminative words.  
- Authors claim Doc2VecC outperforms several state-of-the-art methods on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.  
- Reviewer accepts the paper, citing simplicity, efficiency, and high-quality representations with much lower training time and compute.  
- Main technical contribution: a corruption model acting as data-dependent regularization to favor informative/rare words.  
- Reviewer notes most techniques are not novel; the primary novelty is simplicity and speed rather than new algorithms.  
- Suggests broader evaluation on more tasks (e.g., QA, summarization, machine translation) to show generalizability.  
- Requests more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, especially bidirectional LSTM-based approaches.  
- Asks specific methodological questions: for the RNN-LM baseline, was it trained for classification or language modeling, and which hidden-state summary was used?  
- Requests details on hyperparameter tuning for both the proposed method and baselines.  
- Asks how the corruption rate q influences performance and what the optimal q is.  
- Overall: promising, useful, and efficient method that would benefit from additional experiments and comparisons.
