1. Proposes averaging word embeddings with a data-dependent regularizer that upweights informative/rare words and forces common words toward zero.  
2. Shows lower error rates on sentiment analysis and document classification compared to baseline document embedding methods.  
3. Main advantages are simplicity and computational speed.  
4. Lacks significant technical novelty; most techniques are not new.  
5. Needs stronger empirical validation across more than the two reported tasks.  
6. Comparison to RNN-LM is unclear—uncertain whether the LM was trained for classification or as a language model, and whether the representation used was the final hidden state or the average of hidden states.  
7. Recommends comparing against a bidirectional LSTM with concatenated final hidden states on document classification/sentiment tasks.
