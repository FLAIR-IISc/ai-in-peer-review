This paper proposes a framework for creating document representations by averaging word embeddings with a data-dependent regularization term. This term favors informative or rare words, while forcing common words to be close to zero. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on sentiment analysis and document classification, achieving lower error rates compared to baseline document embedding methods.

Although the motivation to find the best way to encode a document into a vector is compelling, the paper lacks significant technical contributions. Most of the techniques employed are not new, and the primary advantage of the proposed method lies in its simplicity and speed. To be convinced of the method's efficacy, I would like to see strong results on more than two tasks.

The authors compare their approach to RNN-LM, but it is unclear whether the language model was trained to minimize classification error or as a traditional language model. Additionally, it would be helpful to know whether the final hidden state or the average of all hidden states was used as the representation.

A widely used approach to representing documents involves using a bidirectional LSTM and concatenating the final hidden states. I believe it would be useful to compare the proposed method to this approach on tasks such as document classification or sentiment analysis to further establish its effectiveness.
