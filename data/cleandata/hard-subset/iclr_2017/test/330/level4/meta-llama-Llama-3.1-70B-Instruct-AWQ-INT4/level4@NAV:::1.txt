This paper presents a novel approach to computing vector representations for documents by integrating a skip-gram style learning mechanism with a global context vector regularizer and variable dropout rates. Although the individual components are not groundbreaking, their combined application is a unique contribution. The in-depth analysis of model behavior in section 3 is particularly noteworthy.

However, the paper's empirical evaluation is somewhat limited, relying heavily on sentiment analysis and k-way classification tasks. More diverse and challenging tasks could have been explored to demonstrate the method's effectiveness. Additionally, the extensive use of t-SNE projections could be replaced with more in-depth analysis to provide further insights.

Despite some disappointment with the evaluation and agreement with other reviewers regarding the use of soft baselines, I recommend accepting this paper. The proposed algorithm is well-composed, efficient, and offers potentially useful ideas for readers, making it a valuable contribution to the field.
