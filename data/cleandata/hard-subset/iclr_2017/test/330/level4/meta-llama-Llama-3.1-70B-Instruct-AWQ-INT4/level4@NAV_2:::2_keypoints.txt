- Paper proposes document representation via averaged word embeddings with data-dependent regularization favoring rare/informative words and suppressing common words.
- Demonstrates effectiveness across sentiment analysis, document classification, word analogy, and semantic relatedness tasks versus baselines.
- Reviewer inclined to accept; method is novel, efficient, simple, fast, and effective.
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and thorough evaluation against multiple baselines.
- Data-dependent regularization is highlighted as a key innovation that improves performance.
- Technical contributions extend existing work rather than being entirely groundbreaking.
- Suggestion to provide more details on experimental setup and per-task hyperparameters.
- Recommend reporting error bars or confidence intervals to show performance variability.
- Questions about RNN-LM setup: whether LM is trained for classification or as a language model, and whether final hidden state or average of hidden states was used as representation.
- Suggest comparing proposed method to bidirectional LSTM concatenated final hidden states for classification/sentiment tasks.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor revisions needed before publication.
