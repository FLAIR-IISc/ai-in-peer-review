- Introduces Doc2VecC: represent documents as the average of word embeddings with data-dependent regularization that favors informative/rare words and drives common words toward zero.
- Empirical results show Doc2VecC achieves lower error rates on sentiment analysis and document classification compared to baseline document embedding methods.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing simplicity and speed as significant advantages over existing methods.
- Approach is well-motivated and well-situated in the literature on word embeddings and document representation learning.
- Experimental evaluation is convincing across multiple tasks.
- Paper includes a thorough analysis, explaining the data-dependent regularization and its effects on embeddings.
- Suggestion: evaluate on additional tasks (e.g., semantic relatedness, question answering) to further establish effectiveness.
- Suggestion: provide more details on the RNN-LM baseline training setup (trained as LM vs. for classification; use of final hidden state vs. average of hidden states).
- Suggestion: compare against bidirectional LSTM representations (concatenated final hidden states) for classification and sentiment tasks.
- Question: clarify the corruption model used in Doc2VecC, including the probability distribution for removing words.
- Question: analyze the effect of the corruption rate q on performance and report optimal q for different tasks.
- Question: provide deeper insight into the data-dependent regularization term and how it promotes informative/rare words while suppressing common words.
