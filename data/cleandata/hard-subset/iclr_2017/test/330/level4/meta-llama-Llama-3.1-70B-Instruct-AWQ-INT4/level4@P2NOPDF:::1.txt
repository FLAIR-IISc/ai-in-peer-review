This paper proposes a novel method for computing vector representations of documents by leveraging a skip-gram style learning mechanism, augmented with a global context vector regularizer and incorporating various dropout techniques. Although the individual components presented in this work are not new, the combination of these elements in this manner appears to be a unique contribution. The detailed analysis of model behavior in Section 3 is also noteworthy and appreciated.

The primary drawback of this submission lies in its relatively limited empirical evaluation. The choice of sentiment analysis and k-way classification tasks may not be the most compelling, and alternative, more interesting tasks could have been explored. Furthermore, dedicating two-thirds of a page to t-SNE projections seems unnecessary, and this space could have been utilized for more in-depth analysis.

While I share the disappointment with the reduced evaluation and concur with the other reviewers regarding the use of soft baselines, I believe this paper warrants acceptance. The algorithm is interesting, well-composed, and efficient, making it reasonable to assume that other readers may find value in the ideas presented.
