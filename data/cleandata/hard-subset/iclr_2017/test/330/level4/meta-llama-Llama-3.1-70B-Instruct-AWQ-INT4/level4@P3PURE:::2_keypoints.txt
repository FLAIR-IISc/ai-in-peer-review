- Proposes document representations by averaging word embeddings with a data-dependent regularizer that upweights informative/rare words and pushes common words toward zero.  
- Reports lower error rates on sentiment analysis and document classification compared to baseline embedding methods.  
- Lacks significant technical novelty; techniques are largely not new and the main benefits are simplicity and speed.  
- Needs stronger empirical validation across more than the two reported tasks to be convincing.  
- Requests clarification on the RNN-LM baseline: was it trained to minimize classification error or as a traditional language model, and was the final hidden state or the average of hidden states used as the document representation?  
- Recommends comparison to widely used alternatives (e.g., bidirectional LSTM with concatenated final hidden states) on classification/sentiment tasks to better assess performance.
