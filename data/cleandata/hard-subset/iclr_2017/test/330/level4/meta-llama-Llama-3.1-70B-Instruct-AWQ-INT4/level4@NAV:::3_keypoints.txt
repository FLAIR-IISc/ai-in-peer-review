- Proposes learning document embeddings by summing jointly learned word embeddings with random dropout, yielding an efficient representation and strong empirical results.  
- Individual components lack novelty; joint training and sum-of-word representations have prior work (e.g., Lebret & Collobert).  
- The corruption mechanism is essentially standard input-layer dropout and, with a word2vec-style loss, adds little new conceptually.  
- Model is highly efficient at generation time—only averaging word embeddings—unlike Doc2Vec’s heavier inference.  
- Resulting embeddings capture global information useful for local-context prediction and perform well on sentiment and document classification tasks.  
- Despite limited novelty, the paper’s simplicity, efficiency, and performance merit acceptance and wider dissemination.
