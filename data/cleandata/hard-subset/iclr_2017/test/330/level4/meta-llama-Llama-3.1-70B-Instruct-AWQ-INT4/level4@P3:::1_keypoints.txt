- Proposes Doc2VecC: document representations as simple averages of word embeddings learned with a corruption model.  
- Corruption model acts as data-dependent regularization, emphasizing informative/rare words and shrinking common/non-discriminative ones.  
- Combines skip-gram style learning with a global context vector and dropout-like corruption—novel and interesting.  
- Claims significantly better word embeddings than Word2Vec and strong results on sentiment, document classification, and semantic relatedness.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty and detailed analysis of model behavior (Section 3).  
- Paper provides a thorough analysis of model behavior, clarifying strengths and weaknesses.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited (mainly sentiment analysis and k-way classification); broader tasks recommended.  
- t-SNE visualizations occupy substantial space; consider replacing with additional analysis or results.  
- Some evaluation baselines are weak; include more robust baselines for a comprehensive comparison.  
- Requests more details on hyperparameter tuning and the effect/optimal value of corruption rate q.  
- Asks whether the method has been or could be applied to other tasks (e.g., summarization, QA).
