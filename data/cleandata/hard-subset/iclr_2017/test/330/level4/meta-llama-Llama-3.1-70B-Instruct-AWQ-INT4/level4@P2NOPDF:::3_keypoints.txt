- Proposes a document embedding model that jointly trains word and document embeddings, representing documents as the sum of their word embeddings with random dropout of constituent words during training.
- Individual components are not novel; similar ideas exist (e.g., Lebret & Collobert) and the corruption is standard dropout with a word2vec-style loss.
- The method is computationally efficient at generation time, requiring only the average of word embeddings versus complex inference in models like Doc2Vec.
- By design the embedding captures salient global document information that aids local-context prediction.
- Demonstrates encouraging empirical performance on sentiment analysis and document classification tasks despite its simplicity.
- Conclusion: lacks novelty but merits acceptance due to simplicity, efficiency, strong performance, and broad interest.
