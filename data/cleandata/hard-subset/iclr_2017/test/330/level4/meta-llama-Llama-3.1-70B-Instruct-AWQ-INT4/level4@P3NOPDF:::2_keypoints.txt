- Proposes averaging word embeddings with a data-dependent regularizer that upweights rare/informative words and suppresses common words; shows lower error rates on sentiment and document classification vs baseline embeddings.
- Motivation for improved document encoding is compelling, but the paper lacks significant technical novelty.
- Main advantage appears to be simplicity and speed rather than new methodology.
- Results are limited; stronger evidence on a more diverse set of tasks is needed to establish superiority.
- Experimental details are unclear: how was the RNN-LM baseline trained (language model vs classification), and was the representation the final hidden state or an average of hidden states?
- Recommends comparisons to stronger/standard baselines such as bidirectional LSTM with concatenated final hidden states for a more informative evaluation.
- Overall conclusion: efficient and simple approach but limited novelty and limited experimental evaluation reduce the paper’s impact; further experiments and clearer comparisons would strengthen it.
