- Proposes learning document embeddings as the sum of jointly learned word embeddings with random dropout corruption during training.  
- Individual components (joint word/document training, sum-of-words representation) are not novel and have prior art (e.g., Lebret & Collobert).  
- The corruption mechanism is essentially standard input-layer dropout, offering limited novelty.  
- Combining these elements with a word2vec-style loss does not constitute a significant methodological advance.  
- The model is highly efficient at generation time, requiring only a simple average of word embeddings vs. Doc2Vec’s complex inference.  
- Learned embeddings capture salient global document information useful for local-context prediction.  
- Empirical results on sentiment analysis and document classification are strong given the model’s simplicity.  
- Despite limited novelty, the simplicity, efficiency, and performance justify wider dissemination; recommendation: accept.
