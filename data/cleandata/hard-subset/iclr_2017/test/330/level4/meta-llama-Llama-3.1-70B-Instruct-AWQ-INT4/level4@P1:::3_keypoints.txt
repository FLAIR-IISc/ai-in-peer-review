- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as the simple average of word embeddings with a corruption model that randomly removes words during training.  
- Corruption acts as data-dependent regularization, favoring informative/rare words and forcing embeddings of common/non-discriminative words toward zero.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance primarily for the model's simplicity and computational efficiency.  
- Empirical performance is strong on sentiment analysis and document classification given the model’s simplicity.  
- Method lacks strong novelty: joint word/document training, sum-of-word representations, and input dropout have prior art.  
- The combination of components yields an efficient learning algorithm and fast document generation (no complicated inference step like Doc2Vec).  
- Embeddings capture salient global information useful for local-context prediction.  
- Suggests additional analysis of the corruption mechanism (e.g., varying corruption rates) to explain its effects on embeddings.  
- Requests more experimental detail and hyperparameter tuning info to improve reproducibility.  
- Specific questions: how corruption rate affects performance and task-dependence, why corruption suppresses common words, impact of embedding dimension and trade-offs, and precise experimental settings.
