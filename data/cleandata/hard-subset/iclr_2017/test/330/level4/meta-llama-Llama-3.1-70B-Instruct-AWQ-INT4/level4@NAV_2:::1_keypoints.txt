- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as simple averages of word embeddings with a corruption model.  
- Motivation: linear operations on word embeddings can retain syntactic and semantic meaning.  
- Corruption model acts as data-dependent regularization, favoring informative/rare words and suppressing common non-discriminative ones.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Reason 1: Well-motivated method that efficiently combines existing techniques and achieves state-of-the-art performance on several tasks.  
- Reason 2: Provides detailed analysis of model behavior, helping clarify strengths and limitations.  
- Empirical results: Outperforms several SOTA document representation methods on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.  
- Efficiency: Can train on billions of words per hour on a single machine.  
- Analysis: Thorough study of corruption model effects showing suppression of common words.  
- Suggested improvement: Evaluate on more diverse and challenging tasks beyond sentiment analysis and k-way classification.  
- Suggested improvement: Replace t-SNE projections with additional analyses or insights.  
- Questions for authors: provide corruption hyperparameter details and tuning procedure; explain how corruption rate q affects performance and selection guidelines; report any applications/results on other NLP tasks (e.g., QA, MT).
