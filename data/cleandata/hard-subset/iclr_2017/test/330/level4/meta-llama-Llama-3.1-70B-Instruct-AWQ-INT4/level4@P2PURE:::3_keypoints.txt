- Proposes a document embedding model that represents documents as the sum of jointly learned word embeddings with random dropout/corruption during training.
- Individual components are not particularly novel; joint training and summing word embeddings have prior work (e.g., Lebret & Collobert).
- The corruption mechanism is essentially standard input-layer dropout.
- Combined with word2vec-style loss and training, the paper offers limited methodological novelty.
- Major advantage is very efficient generation at inference time—just the average of word embeddings versus complex Doc2Vec inference.
- By design, the embeddings capture salient global document information useful for local-context prediction.
- Despite simplicity, the model shows encouraging empirical performance on sentiment analysis and document classification.
- Recommendation: accept due to simplicity, efficiency, and strong empirical results warranting wider readership.
