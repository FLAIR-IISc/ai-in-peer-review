- Proposes document vector method using skip-gram style learning with a global context vector regularizer and varied dropout rates; combination appears novel though components are not.
- Section 3 offers a detailed analysis of model behavior that provides valuable insights.
- Main weakness is limited empirical evaluation.
- Evaluation tasks (sentiment analysis and k-way classification) are not the most compelling; other tasks could better showcase the method.
- Devoting two-thirds of a page to t-SNE projections is unnecessary and could be replaced by deeper analysis.
- Reviewer and others disappointed by reduced evaluation and the use of weak/soft baselines.
- Despite issues, paper merits acceptance: algorithm is interesting, well-composed, efficient, and likely useful to readers.
