This paper proposes a framework for creating document representations by averaging word embeddings with a data-dependent regularization term. This term favors informative or rare words while forcing common words to be close to zero.

The experimental results on sentiment analysis and document classification demonstrate that the proposed method achieves lower error rates compared to baseline document embedding methods. However, while the motivation of finding effective ways to encode documents into vectors is compelling, the paper does not offer significant technical contributions. Most of the techniques employed in this work are not new, and the primary advantage of the proposed method lies in its simplicity and speed.

To be convinced of the proposed method's efficacy, I would like to see strong results on more than two tasks. Additionally, some clarifications on the experimental setup would be beneficial. Specifically, for the RNN-LM baseline, was the language model trained to minimize classification error, or was it trained as a traditional language model? Furthermore, was the final hidden state used as the document representation, or was it the average of all hidden states?

It would also be useful to compare the proposed method to more widely used approaches, such as employing a bidirectional LSTM and concatenating the final hidden states as the document representation. Evaluating the proposed method's performance relative to this approach on tasks like document classification or sentiment analysis would provide a more comprehensive understanding of its strengths and limitations.
