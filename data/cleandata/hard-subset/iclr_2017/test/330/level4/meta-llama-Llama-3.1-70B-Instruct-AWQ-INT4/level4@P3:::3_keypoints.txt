- Proposes Doc2VecC: document representations as simple averages of word embeddings with a corruption-based training scheme.
- Corruption model randomly removes words during learning, acting as data-dependent regularization that favors informative/rare words.
- Builds on idea that linear operations on word embeddings preserve syntactic and semantic meanings.
- Joint training of word and document embeddings using a word2vec-style loss and training methods.
- Extensive experiments (sentiment, classification, semantic relatedness) show Doc2VecC outperforms Word2Vec, Paragraph Vectors, and Skip-thought Vectors.
- Method is more efficient in training and testing time compared to baselines.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing simplicity, efficiency, and strong performance.
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and a comprehensive related work comparison.
- Suggested improvements: provide more intuition/analysis of why corruption helps, add more visualizations of learned embeddings, and discuss model limitations and future work.
