This paper proposes a novel document representation learning framework, Document Vector through Corruption (Doc2VecC), which represents each document as a simple average of word embeddings. The approach is well-motivated, building upon the idea that linear operations on word embeddings can sustain substantial syntactic and semantic meanings. The inclusion of a corruption model introduces a data-dependent regularization that favors informative or rare words while suppressing common and non-discriminative ones.

I recommend accepting this paper with two key reasons: (1) the proposed approach is well-motivated and efficiently combines existing techniques to achieve state-of-the-art performance on several document representation learning tasks, and (2) the paper provides a detailed analysis of the model's behavior, which is valuable for understanding the strengths and limitations of the approach.

The supporting arguments for the decision include the fact that Doc2VecC outperforms several state-of-the-art document representation learning algorithms on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness tasks. The approach is also efficient, allowing for training on billions of words per hour on a single machine. Additionally, the paper provides a thorough analysis of the corruption model's effect on the word embeddings, demonstrating its ability to suppress common and non-discriminative words.

To further improve the paper, I suggest the authors consider adding more diverse and challenging tasks to the evaluation, as well as providing more in-depth analysis of the model's behavior on these tasks. The current evaluation is limited to sentiment analysis and k-way classification, and the inclusion of more tasks would strengthen the paper's contributions. Additionally, the authors could consider using the space currently occupied by t-SNE projections to provide further analysis or insights into the model's behavior.

1. Can you provide more details on the corruption model's hyperparameters and how they were tuned?
