- Proposes document representations by averaging word embeddings with a data-dependent regularizer that emphasizes rare/informative words and drives common words toward zero.

- Experiments on sentiment analysis and document classification show the proposed method achieves the lowest error rates compared to baseline document embedding methods.

- Motivation is compelling, but the paper lacks significant technical novelty; techniques are largely not new and the main benefits are simplicity and speed.

- Results are limited to two tasks; stronger evidence across more tasks is needed to establish superiority.

- Unclear details about the RNN-LM baseline: was it trained to minimize classification error or as a standard language model, and was the final hidden state or the average of hidden states used as the representation?

- Recommend comparing against a bidirectional LSTM that concatenates final hidden states for document representation to provide a more comprehensive evaluation.
