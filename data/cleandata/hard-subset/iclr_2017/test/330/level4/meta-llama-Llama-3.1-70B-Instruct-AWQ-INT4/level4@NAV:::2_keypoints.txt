- Proposes document embeddings by averaging word embeddings with a data-driven regularizer that upweights informative/rare words and suppresses common words.
- Evaluated on sentiment analysis and document classification, reporting lower error rates than baseline methods.
- Objective of optimizing document encoding is sound, but the paper lacks substantial technical novelty; most techniques are not new.
- Primary strengths are simplicity and computational efficiency.
- Recommends adding evaluations on additional tasks beyond sentiment analysis and document classification to better establish efficacy.
- Requests clarification on the RNN-LM: whether it’s trained to minimize classification error or as a traditional language model, and whether the representation is the final hidden state or the average of all hidden states.
- Recommends comparing against a bidirectional LSTM that concatenates final hidden states as a baseline for document/sentiment tasks.
