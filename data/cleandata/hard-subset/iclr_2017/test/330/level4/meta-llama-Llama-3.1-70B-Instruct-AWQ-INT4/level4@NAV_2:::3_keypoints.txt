- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as simple averages of word embeddings with a corruption model.  
- Motivation: linear operations on Word2Vec embeddings preserve syntactic and semantic information.  
- Corruption model acts as data-dependent regularization, boosting informative/rare words and downweighting common/uninformative ones.  
- Recommendation: accept—simplicity, efficiency, and strong performance justify publication despite limited novelty.  
- Empirical evidence: extensive experiments (sentiment, document classification, semantic relatedness) show Doc2VecC outperforms Word2Vec, Paragraph Vectors, and Skip-thoughts.  
- Provides analysis showing the corruption model suppresses embeddings of frequent, non-discriminative words.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough comparison to related methods using similar techniques (e.g., Lebret & Collobert).  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of model limitations and directions for future work.  
- Suggested improvements: add detailed comparisons to similar algorithms, discuss limitations/future work, and expand analysis of the corruption model (visualizations, varying corruption rates).  
- Questions for authors: specify corruption implementation details (dropout rates and their effects), explain handling of out-of-vocabulary words and implications for corruption, and provide more visualization/analysis of corruption effects at different rates.
