- Proposes Doc2VecC: simple, efficient document representation as average of word embeddings with corruption-based regularization.  
- Well-motivated by linear compositionality of Word2Vec embeddings preserving syntax and semantics.  
- Main contribution is simplicity and training/generation efficiency compared to complex models.  
- Jointly learns word and document embeddings to capture global information useful for local-context prediction.  
- Corruption mechanism acts as regularization, favoring informative/rare words and damping common/non-discriminative words.  
- Experimental results show competitive or superior performance on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.  
- Generation is very fast—requires only averaging word embeddings without additional inference.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance despite limited novelty of individual components.  
- Suggest further analysis of how corruption affects learned word embeddings and document representations.  
- Recommend exploring different corruption rates and their impact on performance.  
- Minor fixes: clarify Section 3.1 explanation of corruption, provide more detailed comparisons with other methods, and add visualizations/examples of learned representations.
