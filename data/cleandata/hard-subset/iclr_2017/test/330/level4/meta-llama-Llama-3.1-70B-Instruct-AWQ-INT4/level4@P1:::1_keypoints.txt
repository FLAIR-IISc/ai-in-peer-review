- Proposes a novel document embedding method using a skip-gram style loss plus a global context vector regularizer with dropout.  
- Individual components are not novel, but their combination is innovative and valuable.  
- Section 3 provides a detailed and insightful analysis of model behavior.  
- Main empirical weakness: evaluation limited to sentiment analysis and k‑way classification.  
- Recommends broader, more challenging evaluations (e.g., topic modeling, information retrieval, question answering).  
- t-SNE projections are visually appealing but offer little substantive insight into model performance.  
- Suggest replacing or supplementing t-SNE with ablation studies, sensitivity analysis, or comparisons to state‑of‑the‑art methods.  
- Calls for more detailed comparisons with other methods, discussing relative strengths and weaknesses.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — algorithm is interesting, efficient, well-presented, and has potential with revisions.
