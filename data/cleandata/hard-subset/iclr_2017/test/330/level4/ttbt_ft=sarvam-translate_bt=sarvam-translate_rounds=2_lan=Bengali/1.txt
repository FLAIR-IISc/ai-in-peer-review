This paper discusses a method for computing vector representations of documents using a skip-gram style learning mechanism. In this case, a global context vector is used as a regulator with various bit drops. Although none of the individual elements proposed in this paper are new, I believe such a combination is new. Furthermore, I was impressed by the detailed analysis of the model's behavior in Section 3.

The main drawback of this submission is its empirical weakness. Arguably, there are more compelling works from sentiment analysis and K-way classification! Similarly, instead of wasting 2/3 of the page on T-sign projections, that space should be used for further analysis.

Although I am somewhat disappointed with this reduced assessment and agree with other reviewers about the weak foundation, I believe this paper should be accepted: it is an interesting algorithm, well-written, and very efficient, so it is reasonable to assume that other readers may find some of the ideas presented here.
