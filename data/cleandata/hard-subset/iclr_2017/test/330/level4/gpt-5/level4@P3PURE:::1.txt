This paper presents a method for computing document vector representations using a skip-gram–style learning mechanism augmented with a global context vector as a regularizer and various forms of dropout. While none of the individual components are new, their particular combination appears to be, and I appreciated the detailed analysis of model behavior in Section 3.

The main weakness is on the empirical front. There are arguably more informative evaluation settings than sentiment analysis and k-way classification. Similarly, dedicating two-thirds of a page to t-SNE projections seems unnecessary; that space would be better used for further analysis. I also agree with the other reviewers’ concerns regarding soft baselines.

Despite these limitations, I recommend acceptance. The algorithm is interesting, well composed, and very efficient, and it is reasonable to expect that readers will find some of the ideas presented here useful.
