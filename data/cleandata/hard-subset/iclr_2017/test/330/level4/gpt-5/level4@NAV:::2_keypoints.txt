- Paper introduces a framework for constructing document representations.
- Core approach: model a document as the mean of its word embeddings with data-driven regularization that emphasizes informative/rare words and shrinks common words toward zero.
- Experiments on sentiment analysis and document classification report the lowest error rates relative to baseline document-embedding methods.
- Reviewer feels the paper does not provide substantial technical contributions.
- Many components are not novel; main selling points are simplicity and speed.
- Reviewer requests stronger results on more than two tasks to be convinced of the method’s general superiority.
- Asks whether the RNN-LM was optimized to minimize classification error or trained as a language model.
- Asks whether the representation from the RNN-LM is the final hidden state or the average over all hidden states.
- Recommends comparing to a widely adopted bidirectional LSTM approach (concatenated final hidden states) on document classification and sentiment tasks.
