- Paper proposes averaging word embeddings with a data-dependent regularizer that suppresses common words and up-weights informative/rare ones; experiments on sentiment/classification show improved error rates and method is simple and fast.
- Strength: clear motivation to keep architecture minimal while emphasizing informative words.
- Strength: practical — method is fast, scalable, and easy to integrate.
- Strength: empirically promising on two standard tasks.
- Strength: potential interpretability via learned word weights.
- Weakness: limited technical novelty; resembles weighted averaging/IDF with shrinkage and lacks strong theoretical contribution.
- Weakness: narrow empirical scope — only two tasks; broader validation needed to support strong claims.
- Weakness: missing comparisons to strong neural baselines (bidirectional LSTMs, CNN encoders, hierarchical models).
- Weakness: unclear baseline implementations (RNN training objective, representation extraction), which could bias comparisons.
- Weakness: limited analysis — no ablations isolating regularizer benefit vs. simple averaging or TF-IDF, and no study of learned weights vs. frequency/IDF.
- Clarify RNN-LM baseline: was it trained as LM or fine-tuned for classification, and which representation (final state, pooled states, bidirectional concat) was used?
- Clarify embeddings: were they pre-trained, trained from scratch, or fine-tuned; how are OOV/rare words handled?
- Clarify regularizer: exact definition, parameterization of per-word weights, optimization, and choice/tuning of regularization strength and mitigation of spurious rare-word upweighting.
- Clarify experimental protocol: which baselines were comparably tuned, and are improvements statistically significant across seeds?
- Suggested experiments: compare to BiLSTM (concat and pooled), CNN and hierarchical attention encoders, TF-IDF+logistic regression, paragraph vectors/doc2vec, and fastText n-gram baselines.
- Suggested ablations: uniform average vs. regularized weighting, TF-IDF vs. learned weights, sensitivity to regularization coefficient and vocab pruning, and correlation between learned weights and IDF with qualitative examples.
- Suggested broader evaluation: more tasks/domains (topic classification, long-form sentiment), robustness to document length/domain shift, runtime/memory comparisons to RNN/CNN, and stability across seeds and dataset sizes.
- Suggested practical details: report hyperparameters, optimization, early stopping, preprocessing, and ideally release code or minimal implementation for reproducibility.
- Overall assessment: method is simple, efficient, and promising but lacks novelty and sufficiently broad/rigorous empirical evidence to justify strong claims.
- Recommendation: Weak reject; would reconsider if authors add rigorous comparisons to strong baselines (including bidirectional LSTMs), thorough ablations/interpretability analyses, and expanded evaluation with efficiency and statistical significance.
