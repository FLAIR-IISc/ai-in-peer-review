This paper proposes a method for learning document vector representations using a skip-gram–style objective augmented with a global context vector that acts as a regularizer, along with several forms of dropout. While none of the individual components are new, the particular combination appears novel. I also appreciated the detailed analysis of model behavior in Section 3.

The main weakness of the submission is its empirical evaluation. The experiments focus on sentiment analysis and k-way classification, but more challenging and informative tasks would better showcase the method’s strengths. Similarly, devoting roughly two-thirds of a page to t-SNE projections seems like a poor use of space compared with deeper analysis.

Despite being disappointed by the limited evaluation and, in agreement with other reviewers, the relatively soft baselines, I lean toward acceptance. The algorithm is interesting, well composed, and very efficient, and I expect that readers may find its ideas useful.
