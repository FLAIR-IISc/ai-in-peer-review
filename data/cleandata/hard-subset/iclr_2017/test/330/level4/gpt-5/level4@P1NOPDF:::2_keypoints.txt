- Proposes document representation as average of word embeddings with data-dependent regularization that upweights informative/rare words and pushes common words toward zero.  
- Reports lowest error rates versus baseline document embedding methods on sentiment analysis and document classification.  
- Author commends motivation, simplicity, and computational speed of the method.  
- Major criticism: limited technical novelty; mostly a streamlined, efficient instantiation of existing ideas.  
- Requests additional evaluation on more than two tasks to demonstrate general superiority.  
- Asks clarification about the RNN-LM baseline: was it trained for classification or as a pure language model, and was the document vector the final hidden state or an average of hidden states?  
- Recommends comparing against a bidirectional LSTM using concatenated final hidden states on the same tasks.
