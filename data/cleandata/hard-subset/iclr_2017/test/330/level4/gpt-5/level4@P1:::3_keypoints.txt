- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as the average of word embeddings, jointly learned with a word2vec-style objective and training-time random dropout of input words.  
- Training-time corruption (unbiased dropout) is analyzed as a data-dependent regularizer that shrinks embeddings of frequent, non-discriminative words.  
- Method is simple, scales well, and enables very fast test-time inference (just averaging).  
- Empirically matches or outperforms strong baselines on sentiment analysis and document classification, and is competitive on semantic relatedness.  
- Decision: Accept — strong empirical performance and practical efficiency outweigh limited novelty.  
- Practical value: combination of averaging and input corruption is well-motivated and effective, with analytical intuition provided.  
- Problem/approach aligns with compositionality of embeddings; corruption emphasizes informative words and diminishes stopwords.  
- Novelty is limited: components overlap with prior work (sum/average embeddings, marginalized/denoising AEs, word2vec/CBoW, PV variants).  
- Paper is well-positioned in related work and the analysis linking corruption to regularization is useful.  
- Empirical evaluation is convincing (IMDB, Wikipedia) and speed/scalability claims are well-supported.  
- Request to clarify novelty relative to Lebret & Collobert, marginalized/denoising AEs, and PV-DBOW/PV-DM; add a comparison table of objectives and inference costs.  
- Ask for ablation studies: no corruption vs corruption; with/without local context; IDF weighting vs pure averaging; subsampling frequent words.  
- Request sensitivity analyses for corruption rate q, embedding dimension, number of negative samples, context window size, and document length.  
- Ensure fairness of baselines by matching preprocessing (tokenization, vocab cutoff, subsampling); note Skip-thought may be disadvantaged on long documents.  
- Suggest experiments on PV word vectors (fine-tune without document vectors) and fuller PV-DBOW vs PV-DM comparisons to support the word-embedding capacity hypothesis.  
- Reproducibility: include code (already linked), full hyperparameters, hardware specs for timing, and training tricks (gradient clipping, normalization).  
- Question: what is the precise conceptual difference between Doc2VecC and applying standard input dropout to a bag-of-embeddings model trained with CBoW? Any novel architectural/training nuance?  
- Question: do you average uniformly at test time or apply weighting (e.g., IDF)? are embeddings normalized before averaging?  
- Question: which embeddings form document vectors — input U only, output V, or a combination?  
- Question: provide performance and training-speed vs corruption rate q.  
- Question: ablate removing local context (DEA-like variant) to isolate contributions.  
- Question: how does performance vary with document length (short sentences vs long reviews); does it degrade on very short inputs relative to LSTM/Skip-thought?  
- Question: clarify IMDB unsupervised protocol to ensure no label leakage and confirm matched training conditions for word analogy and other comparisons.
