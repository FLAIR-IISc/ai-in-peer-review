- Method: document embedding = sum of constituent word embeddings; word and document vectors trained jointly with random corruption during training.
- Many components are not novel; joint training and sum-constrained document vectors are established (cf. Lebret & Collobert).
- The corruption step is essentially standard input-layer dropout.
- Using a word2vec-style objective and optimization yields limited methodological novelty.
- Inference is very efficient: document representation is just the average of word embeddings, unlike Doc2Vec’s more complex inference.
- The representation captures salient global document information that facilitates local-context prediction.
- Given the model’s simplicity, the empirical results on sentiment analysis and document classification are compelling.
- Overall judgment: despite the novelty gap, simplicity, efficiency, and strong performance merit acceptance.
