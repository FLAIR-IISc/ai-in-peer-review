- Proposes learning document embeddings as the sum of jointly trained word and document vectors with random dropout (“corruption”) during training.
- Approach lacks novelty: joint word-document training and sum-of-words representations have been done before (e.g., Lebret & Collobert).
- Corruption is standard input-layer dropout and training uses a word2vec-style loss, offering little methodological innovation.
- Very efficient at generation time, requiring only the average of word embeddings rather than expensive Doc2Vec inference.
- By construction, embeddings capture salient global information that supports local-context prediction.
- Achieves encouraging empirical results on sentiment analysis and document classification despite model simplicity.
- Recommendation: accept—simplicity, efficiency, and strong performance justify broader attention despite limited novelty.
