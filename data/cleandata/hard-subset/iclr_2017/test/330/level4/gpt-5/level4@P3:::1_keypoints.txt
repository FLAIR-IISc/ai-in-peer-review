- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents by averaging word embeddings learned with a CBOW-style objective that conditions on local context and a corrupted global document context.
- Corruption masks words with unbiased rescaling, which the authors show induces a data-dependent regularizer that downweights frequent, non-informative words.
- Provides a second-order Taylor expansion derivation explaining how corruption produces the regularization effect.
- Decouples parameter size from corpus size, trains quickly with negative sampling, and yields test-time document vectors by a single average.
- Empirically matches or outperforms strong baselines on sentiment (IMDB), document classification (Wikipedia), semantic relatedness (SICK), and word analogy tasks.
- Produces word embeddings that perform well on analogy evaluations.
- Primary reasons to accept: elegant, well-motivated objective with solid analytical justification and strong practical appeal (speed, simplicity, accuracy).
- Novel combination of known components (averaging, corruption, negative sampling) presented insightfully so averaging is an architectural constraint, not a heuristic.
- Theory section clearly links corruption to penalizing frequent words, aligning with dropout intuitions but specialized to document averaging.
- Demonstrates significant speed and inference advantages; practical for large-scale use due to parameter-corpus decoupling.
- Experimental breadth could be improved with stronger contemporary baselines (e.g., FastText/Bag of Tricks) and IDF/SIF-weighted comparisons for sentences.
- Lacks systematic ablations: effect of removing global document vector, removing corruption, varying corruption rate q, different negative sampling distributions, context window size, and embedding dimension.
- Suggests reallocating space from t-SNE figures to deeper quantitative analyses (frequency-binned performance, clustering metrics, error analyses).
- Needs clearer hyperparameter fairness and details for baselines (Paragraph V, Skip-thought) and clarification of negative sampling distribution (unigram^0.75 vs uniform).
- Requests clarification on negative sampling choice and its effect on analogy and downstream tasks.
- Requests sensitivity study for corruption rate q (e.g., q in {0.5,0.7,0.9}) and ablations for local vs global context.
- Asks how subsampling frequent words affects results and whether IDF weighting at test time adds value.
- Seeks details on Paragraph Vector training/inference budgets, PV-DM vs PV-DBOW comparisons, and any PV regularization attempts.
- Requests clarity on pretraining corpora and domain mismatch effects for SICK and IMDB, especially relative to Skip-thought.
- Asks whether input/output embeddings are tied or separate, and whether averaging U, V, or (U+V)/2 helps.
- Requests tokens/sec and wall-clock times with hardware specs to substantiate efficiency claims.
- Questions how performance scales with very long or very short documents and interactions with 1/T normalization and rescaling.
- Requests reproducibility materials: scripts, seeds, full hyperparameters, and preprocessed data splits for all baselines.
