- Proposes learning document embeddings as the sum/average of constituent word embeddings with jointly trained word and document vectors and input dropout corruption.  
- None of the individual components (joint training, sum-of-words constraint, input dropout) is particularly novel.  
- Similar prior work exists (e.g., Lebret and Collobert’s “The Sum of Its Parts”), and the corruption is standard dropout.  
- Uses a word2vec-style loss and training procedure, further limiting conceptual novelty.  
- Method yields very efficient inference: just average word embeddings, avoiding Doc2Vec’s more expensive inference.  
- By design, document embeddings capture global information useful for predicting local context.  
- Empirical results on sentiment analysis and document classification are strong given the model’s simplicity.  
- Overall assessment: limited novelty but simple, efficient, empirically solid—recommend acceptance.
