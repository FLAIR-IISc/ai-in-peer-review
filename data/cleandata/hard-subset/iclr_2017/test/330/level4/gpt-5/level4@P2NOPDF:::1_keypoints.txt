- Proposes learning document vectors with a skip-gram–style objective plus a global context vector regularizer and dropout variants.
- Individual components are not novel, but their particular integration appears new and conceptually clean.
- Section 3 provides careful and appreciated behavioral analysis.
- Primary weakness is the limited empirical evaluation (only sentiment analysis and k-way classification).
- Recommends broader and more challenging benchmark tasks to demonstrate generality and usefulness.
- Criticizes spending ~two-thirds of a page on t-SNE visualizations instead of quantitative analyses or ablations.
- Baselines are too weak/soft; stronger, more competitive comparisons are needed to contextualize gains.
- Overall positive leaning toward acceptance: algorithm is interesting, simple, and computationally efficient.
- Strengthening the empirical section with tougher tasks and baselines would substantially improve the paper, though it is already a worthwhile contribution.
