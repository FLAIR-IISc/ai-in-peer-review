- Proposes learning document embeddings as the sum of constituent word embeddings with joint training and random input “corruption” during training.
- Joint training and sum-constrained document representations are not novel (e.g., Lebret & Collobert).
- The corruption mechanism is essentially standard input-layer dropout.
- Using a word2vec-style loss and training procedure offers limited methodological novelty.
- Method is very efficient at generation time—only averages word embeddings, avoiding Doc2Vec’s complex inference.
- By design, embeddings capture global document information useful for local-context prediction.
- Despite simplicity, the approach yields strong empirical results on sentiment analysis and document classification.
- Overall recommendation: accept for readership due to simplicity, efficiency, and empirical performance despite limited novelty.
