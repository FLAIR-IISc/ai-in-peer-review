- Proposes Doc2VecC: learn document vectors by averaging word embeddings with word-mask corruption and reweighting; corruption acts as a data-dependent regularizer that downweights common words and preserves rare/informative ones, enabling Word2Vec-like scalability and fast inference.

- Decision: Reject.

- Primary reason for rejection: Limited novelty — averaging embeddings and dropout/denoising-style corruption are well-known; technical contribution is incremental.

- Secondary reason for rejection: Empirical validation is weak — key strong neural baselines are missing and task breadth is limited.

- Problem & motivation: Addresses efficient, compact document representations and reasonably positions the method relative to Word2Vec and Paragraph Vectors.

- Technical novelty & rigor: Method and theoretical link to regularization follow established analyses (marginalized denoising, dropout); incremental technical delta.

- Empirical evidence: Reported accuracy gains and speed improvements are encouraging, but comparisons may be incomplete or unfair.

- Baseline gap: Missing strong baselines such as bidirectional LSTM encoders (concatenated final states and mean/max pooling) and possibly CNN document encoders.

- RNN-LM baseline unclear: Need clarification on whether it was trained solely as a language model or fine-tuned for classification, which representation was used, and full hyperparameters.

- Broader evaluation needed: Recommend more tasks (multi-domain classification, entailment/paraphrase, retrieval) and statistical significance testing to support generality.

- Ablations & sensitivity: Require experiments varying corruption rate q, document length, and ablations removing corruption and local context to quantify contributions.

- Interaction with subsampling: Investigate whether the proposed regularizer subsumes frequent-word subsampling and report results with/without subsampling.

- Practical details missing: Provide runtime comparisons with hardware, vocabulary and corpus sizes, inference-time costs, and OOV handling.

- Questions for authors: clarify RNN-LM training/representation, compare to standard biLSTM encoder on tasks, report sensitivity to q and document length, show impact of removing subsampling, and supply significance tests and hyperparameter tuning details.

- Overall assessment: Well-motivated and empirically promising in efficiency, but contribution is incremental and would be substantially strengthened by stronger baselines and expanded experiments.
