- Proposes Doc2VecC: average word embeddings with corruption (mask-out) during training to form document vectors.
- Corruption acts as a data-dependent regularizer that shrinks common/non-discriminative word embeddings toward zero while preserving informative words.
- Method is simple, scales to very large corpora, and is efficient at test time (document vector = average embeddings).
- Provides a clear second-order Taylor expansion derivation explaining the regularization effect (Section 3).
- Empirical results are competitive on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.
- Produces stronger word embeddings than Word2Vec on analogy benchmarks.
- Reviewer decision: Accept.
- Primary reasons: novel combination in this specific form, high efficiency, and solid analytical insight.
- Motivation: combining averaging, corruption, and negative sampling to learn meaningful averaged document embeddings is well motivated.
- Empirical support is plausible given architecture, but experimental scope is relatively limited and conservative.
- Some baselines used are considered soft and stronger neural baselines should be included.
- Suggested experiments: broaden tasks (long-document retrieval, domain-shift topic classification, low-supervision transfer) to better stress test representations.
- Ablations/sensitivity analyses requested: vary corruption rate q, remove corruption/local context, and test negative sampling rate, context window, and embedding dimensionality.
- Investigate test-time weighting (e.g., IDF) vs learned downweighting and quantify subsampling vs corruption interaction.
- Replace heavy reliance on t-SNE with quantitative analyses (nearest-neighbor coherence, clustering metrics, error analysis).
- Clarify efficiency comparisons (standardize hardware; separate training, encoding, inference costs) and detail subsampling handling.
- Reproducibility: provide a short recipe, default hyperparameters, and code instructions to reproduce main tables.
- Questions for authors: clarify novelty over prior work (Huang et al. 2012, Paragraph Vectors, marginalized denoising AEs) and what's new about pooling+corruption in a predictive objective.
- Questions: is training closer to CBOW or Skip-gram and does it matter; how sensitive is performance to q and can q be learned.
- Questions: quantify reliance on subsampling when corruption is used; did test-time IDF or learned weights help or hurt.
- Questions: provide exact hardware, data sizes, hyperparameters, and negatives per update for timing claims.
- Questions: when is Doc2VecC preferable (long docs vs short sentences) and do hybrid approaches (averaging within RNN/CNN) help.
- Questions: do improved word embeddings on analogy tasks translate to better downstream document performance or are there trade-offs with the document objective.
