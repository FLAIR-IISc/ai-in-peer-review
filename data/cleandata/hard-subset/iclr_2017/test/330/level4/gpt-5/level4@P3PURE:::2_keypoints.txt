- Method: document vector = average of word embeddings with data-dependent regularization that upweights informative/rare words and pushes frequent words toward zero.
- Empirical claim: achieves lowest error rates on sentiment analysis and document classification relative to baseline document embedding methods.
- Strengths: well-motivated, simple, and fast implementation.
- Main critique: limited technical novelty—techniques are mostly existing and the paper is a streamlined instantiation rather than a new method.
- Evaluation concern: only two tasks tested; broader empirical validation is needed to support the claim that this is the best document representation.
- Clarification requested: for the RNN-LM baseline, was it trained for classification or only as a language model, and which hidden-state representation was used (final state or average)?
- Comparison requested: include results comparing the method to a bidirectional LSTM (concatenated final forward/backward states) on the same tasks.
- Overall: empirically strong on reported tasks but modest technical contribution; needs more comprehensive evaluation and clearer comparisons to strong RNN baselines.
