- Proposes skip-gram–style document representations augmented with a global context vector and dropout-based regularization; integration is original though components are not novel individually.  
- Paper is clearly written with careful analysis of model behavior (Section 3).  
- Method is computationally efficient and easy to implement, making it practically relevant.  
- Coherent algorithmic design that combines well-understood ideas into a compact, efficient method.  
- Thoughtful qualitative and quantitative analysis supports model insights.  
- Regularization via a global context vector and targeted dropout is well-motivated to balance local and global signals.  
- Simplicity and efficiency make the method a useful fast baseline or system component.  
- Empirical evaluation is narrow (mainly sentiment and k-way topic classification), limiting evidence of generality.  
- Missing or weak baselines (e.g., PV-DBOW/DM, fastText, NBSVM, skip-thought, SIF/Sent2Vec, bag-of-ngrams), making gains hard to assess.  
- Overuse of t-SNE visualizations that are hard to interpret and could be replaced by more rigorous experiments.  
- Limited ablation studies; unclear contributions of global vector, regularizer, and dropout variants.  
- Statistical rigor is light: lacks variance across runs, hyperparameter sensitivity, and significance testing.  
- Suggestions: clarify relation to Paragraph Vector and whether the global vector is used at inference; compare with matched training budgets.  
- Suggestions: strengthen baselines, expand tasks (retrieval, STS, clustering, duplicate detection, out-of-domain transfer), and report efficiency metrics (training time, memory, throughput).  
- Suggestions: provide ablations for components, dropout placements/rates, negative sampling, and context window effects; replace t-SNE with learning curves, confusion matrices, and significance tests.  
- Suggest expanding analysis of how the global context vector evolves and what information it captures; include probing tasks.  
- Reproducibility: provide code, pseudocode, hyperparameters, and preprocessing details.  
- Minor: tighten exposition of the objective and regularizer with explicit equations and discuss optimization choices (negative sampling vs hierarchical softmax) and complexity.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept contingent on addressing evaluation gaps with stronger baselines, broader tasks, and thorough ablations.
