This paper presents a technique for computing document embeddings using a skip-gram–style training setup augmented with a global context vector that is subjected to stochastic masking/dropout. While the individual components are not new, their particular integration appears novel. I also appreciated the thorough examination of model behavior in Section 3.

The main weakness is the relatively limited empirical evaluation. There are arguably more informative benchmarks than sentiment analysis and flat k-way classification. Similarly, dedicating roughly two-thirds of a page to t-SNE visualizations seems like space that could have been used for deeper analysis.

Despite my disappointment with the narrower evaluation and in agreement with other reviewers about somewhat soft baselines, I recommend acceptance: the method is interesting, well composed, and highly efficient, and it is reasonable to expect the community will find the ideas useful.
