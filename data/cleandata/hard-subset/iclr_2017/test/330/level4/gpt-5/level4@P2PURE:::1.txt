This paper presents a method for learning document representations using a skip-gram–style objective regularized by a global context vector, with dropout applied in several ways. While none of the individual components are novel, the particular combination appears to be. I also appreciated the detailed analysis of model behavior in Section 3.

The main weakness is the empirical evaluation. More compelling tasks than sentiment analysis and k-way classification would strengthen the paper. Likewise, devoting two-thirds of a page to t-SNE projections seems unnecessary; that space would be better used for additional analysis.

Although I am disappointed by the limited evaluation and agree with the other reviewers regarding the soft baselines, I recommend acceptance: the algorithm is interesting, well-composed, and very efficient, and I expect readers will find some of the ideas here useful.
