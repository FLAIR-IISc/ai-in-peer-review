This paper proposes a framework for constructing document representations. The core idea is to represent a document as the average of its word embeddings, with a data-dependent regularization that emphasizes informative or rare words and shrinks common words toward zero. Experiments on sentiment analysis and document classification indicate that the proposed method achieves lower error rates than baseline document-embedding approaches.

While I appreciate the motivation to identify an effective vector encoding for documents, the paper offers limited technical novelty. Most techniques are not new; the main advantages are simplicity and speed. For this reason, I would like to see strong results on more than two tasks to be convinced that this is the best approach for learning document representations.

Regarding RNN-LM: is the model trained to minimize classification error, or strictly as a language model? Did you use the final hidden state as the representation, or the average over all hidden states? One widely used approach is a bidirectional LSTM that concatenates the final forward and backward hidden states as the document representation. It would be helpful to know how the proposed method compares to this approach on tasks such as document classification or sentiment analysis.
