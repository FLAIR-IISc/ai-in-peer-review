- Proposes a simple document representation: average word embeddings with data-dependent regularization that downweights frequent words and emphasizes rare/informative ones.  
- Experiments on sentiment analysis and document classification show lower error rates than several baseline embedding approaches.  
- Motivation (fast, effective document encoding) is compelling and results are promising.  
- Technical novelty is limited; components are mostly known and the main contribution is the combination’s simplicity and speed.  
- Claims of general-purpose utility require broader empirical validation across more tasks and datasets.  
- Need clarification on the RNN-LM baseline: was it trained purely as a language model or optimized for classification?  
- Need clarification on which RNN representation was used (final hidden state, average of hidden states, or another pooling strategy).  
- Recommend direct comparison to a strong baseline: bidirectional LSTM with concatenated final hidden states on the same benchmarks (and ideally additional tasks).  
- Paper would be strengthened by broader validation, clearer baseline training/objective details, and direct biLSTM comparisons.  
- Overall: attractive for simplicity and speed with competitive results on two tasks, but additional experiments and clarifications are needed to support broader claims.
