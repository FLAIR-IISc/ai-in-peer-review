This paper presents a method for deriving document vector representations using a skip-gram-style training objective, augmented by a regularizer implemented as a global context vector alongside several forms of dropout. While none of the individual elements are novel, the way they are combined here appears to be. I also appreciated the detailed examination of model behavior in Section 3.

The main weakness of this submission is its relatively modest empirical evaluation. There are arguably more compelling tasks than sentiment analysis and k-way classification. Similarly, allocating two-thirds of a page to t-SNE projections seems wasteful compared to using that space for deeper analysis.

Although I am somewhat disappointed by the limited evaluation and agree with the other reviewers about the soft baselines, I still recommend acceptance: the algorithm is interesting, well-crafted, and very efficient, so it is reasonable to expect that other readers will find some of the ideas presented here useful.
