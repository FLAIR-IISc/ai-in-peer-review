- Proposal: Doc2VecC learns document vectors by averaging jointly-trained word embeddings using a stochastically corrupted bag-of-words as global context during training.  
- Test-time simplicity: At inference a document vector is just the average of word embeddings, enabling extremely fast encoding.  
- Corruption as regularizer: Stochastic masking acts as a data-dependent regularizer that shrinks embeddings of frequent non-informative words while preserving informative ones.  
- Scalability: Method decouples model size from number of documents and trains as quickly as Word2Vec-style methods.  
- Empirical performance: Matches or outperforms strong baselines on IMDB (sentiment), Wikipedia (classification), and is competitive on SICK (semantic relatedness).  
- Word embeddings: Learned embeddings show substantial gains over Word2Vec on word-analogy benchmarks.  
- Theoretical analysis: Taylor-expansion derivation makes the regularization effect explicit and links to dropout/denoising ideas.  
- Decision: Accept — motivated by strong empirical results, extreme simplicity, test-time efficiency, and principled regularization explanation.  
- Problem fit: Addresses compact, semantically meaningful, and efficient document representations that scale and are trivial to compute.  
- Literature placement: Combines known components (CBOW/negative sampling, averaging, corruption) into a principled compositional encoder and relates to Word2Vec, Paragraph Vectors, SDA/DAE, Skip-Thought.  
- Claims rigor: Efficiency and inference speed convincingly reported; effectiveness supported by experiments; regularization explanation aligns with empirical observations.  
- Novelty: Conceptually moderate (builds on known ideas) but offers a coherent formulation, analysis, and practical benefits.  
- Clarify Paragraph Vectors: Need to specify which PV variants (PV-DM vs PV-DBOW) were used and include competitive PV-DBOW comparisons if missing.  
- Negative sampling: Question choice of uniform negative sampling vs unigram^0.75; request ablations and confirmation that baselines use standard settings.  
- Ablations requested: Show incremental value of global vs local context, vary corruption rate q with plots, and test with subsampling disabled to isolate corruption effect.  
- Baseline fairness: Suggest adding hierarchical/CNN/LSTM baselines tuned for long documents because Skip-Thought may be disadvantaged on long texts.  
- Analysis clarity: Tighten notation in Taylor expansion, state assumptions (e.g., independence/diagonal Σ), and add an intuitive summary.  
- Word-embedding inspection: Include cosine-neighbor examples for sentiment/topic words to illustrate shrinkage of non-informative words.  
- Reproducibility: Provide full hyperparameters, training epochs, early stopping, and compute resources; fix reference formatting.
