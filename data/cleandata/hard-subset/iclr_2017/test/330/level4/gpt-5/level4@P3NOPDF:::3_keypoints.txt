- Proposes learning document embeddings as the sum/average of word embeddings, trained jointly with a word2vec-style objective and input-layer dropout.
- Individual components (sum-of-parts document vectors, joint training, input dropout) are not novel; overall contribution is mainly a simple, well-engineered recipe.
- Inference-time efficiency: document vectors computed by averaging word embeddings, avoiding costly inference like Doc2Vec.
- Conceptual clarity: optimizing local-context prediction yields document representations that capture global information useful for downstream tasks.
- Empirical strength: simple model achieves compelling performance on sentiment analysis and document classification.
- Needs stronger empirical comparisons to Doc2Vec variants (PV-DM, PV-DBOW), fastText, bag-of-ngrams, and tf–idf baselines.
- Should include ablation studies isolating input corruption (dropout rate) and comparing joint training vs. fixing word embeddings.
- Should report training/inference speed, memory footprint, and wall-clock comparisons to common baselines.
- Should analyze sensitivity to document length, vocabulary size, and domain shift; clarify handling of rare and OOV words and potential benefit of subword modeling.
- Should discuss limitations of purely additive composition and the impact of simple weightings (e.g., tf–idf) or normalization.
- Overall judgement: modest methodological novelty but practical value due to simplicity, efficiency, and strong empirical results; recommend acceptance.
