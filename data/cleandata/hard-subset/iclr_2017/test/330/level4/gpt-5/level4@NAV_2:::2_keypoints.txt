- Documents encoded as mean of word embeddings with data-dependent regularization that emphasizes rare/informative words and drives frequent words toward zero.  
- Motivated by simplicity and efficiency, performing averaging during training to preserve document-level semantics.  
- Empirically reports lowest error among compared document-embedding baselines on sentiment and document classification, with fast training/deployment.  
- Decision: Reject, mainly due to limited technical novelty and insufficient empirical breadth to support the central claim.  
- Many components are established (embedding averaging, corruption/dropout-style regularization, negative sampling); contribution is primarily computational simplicity, not a new modeling idea.  
- Stronger, broader empirical evidence (more tasks) is needed to justify claiming this as the preferred document-representation method.  
- Suggest expanding experiments beyond sentiment and document classification to demonstrate generality.  
- Request clarification and consistent reporting for sequential baselines to ensure fair, apples-to-apples comparisons.  
- Recommend adding a competitive bidirectional LSTM baseline (concatenated final forward/backward states) for context.  
- Question: For the RNN-LM baseline, was it trained for classification or only as a language model, and was the downstream representation the final hidden state or an average of hidden states?  
- Question: How does the proposed method compare directly to a bidirectional LSTM concatenating final hidden states on sentiment and document-classification tasks?
