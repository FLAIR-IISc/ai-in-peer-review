- Proposes a document representation where a document vector is the sum/average of jointly trained word and document embeddings, with words randomly dropped during training (input-layer dropout).
- Components are well-known, but their combination yields an efficient method with strong empirical results.
- Novelty is limited: joint training and additive composition previously explored (e.g., Lebret & Collobert); corruption is standard dropout and training follows the word2vec paradigm—methodological contribution is incremental.
- Practical advantages: extremely cheap inference (just average word embeddings), avoids Doc2Vec’s costly inference step, and encodes global signals predictive of local context.
- Empirical results are notably strong on sentiment analysis and document classification.
- Overall assessment: simple, efficient, and empirically effective despite limited conceptual novelty; recommendation to accept.
