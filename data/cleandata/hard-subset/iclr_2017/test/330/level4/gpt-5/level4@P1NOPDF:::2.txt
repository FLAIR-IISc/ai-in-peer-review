This paper presents a framework for constructing document representations. The core idea is to represent a document as an average of its word embeddings with a data-dependent regularization that emphasizes informative or rare words while pushing common words toward zero.

Experiments on sentiment analysis and document classification show that the proposed method achieves the lowest error rates compared with baseline document embedding methods. I appreciate the motivation of finding an effective way to encode a document as a vector, and the method’s simplicity and speed are appealing. However, the paper offers limited technical novelty; most of the techniques are not new, and the main contribution appears to be a streamlined, efficient instantiation of known ideas. Given this, I would like to see results on more than two tasks to be convinced that this is a generally superior approach to learning document representations.

Regarding the RNN-LM baseline: was the model trained to minimize classification error, or purely as a language model? For the document vector, did you use the final hidden state or the average over all hidden states?

Additionally, one of the most widely used approaches for document representation is a bidirectional LSTM with the concatenated final hidden states as the document vector. It would be helpful to see how the proposed method compares to this approach on tasks such as document classification and sentiment analysis.
