- Proposes document representation learning using a skip-gram–style objective regularized by a global context vector, with several dropout variants.  
- Individual components are not novel, but their specific combination appears novel.  
- Section 3 contains a detailed and appreciated analysis of model behavior.  
- Main weakness: empirical evaluation is limited and unconvincing.  
- Tasks chosen (sentiment analysis and k-way classification) are not sufficiently compelling; stronger tasks needed.  
- t-SNE projections take up unnecessary space that could be used for additional analysis.  
- Baseline comparisons are weak/soft, but the reviewer still recommends acceptance due to the method’s efficiency and usefulness.
