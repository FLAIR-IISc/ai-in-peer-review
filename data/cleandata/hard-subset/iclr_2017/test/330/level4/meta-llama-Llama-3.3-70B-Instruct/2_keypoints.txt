- Proposes document representations by averaging word embeddings with a data-dependent regularizer that downweights common words and emphasizes informative ones.
- Core mechanism: represent documents as the mean of word embeddings while pushing common words toward zero and favoring rare/informative words.
- Experimental results on sentiment analysis and document classification claim the lowest error rates compared to baseline methods.
- Positive note: exploring optimal document encoding methods is a worthwhile motivation.
- Main criticism: the approach lacks substantial technical novelty; techniques are not particularly innovative.
- Primary advantage appears to be simplicity and computational efficiency rather than new methodology.
- Needs broader evaluation: require robust performance evidence across more tasks beyond the two presented.
- Requests clarification on RNN-LM training (classification objective vs. language modeling) and whether the final hidden state or average of hidden states is used as the document representation.
- Recommends comparing to a bidirectional LSTM that concatenates final hidden states for document/sentiment classification.
