- Proposes learning document embeddings by summing jointly learned word embeddings with random corruption during training.  
- Individual components are not highly novel, but their combination yields an efficient document representation algorithm with strong empirical results.  
- Joint training of word and document embeddings and sum-of-words representations have prior art (e.g., Lebret & Collobert).  
- The corruption mechanism is essentially input-layer dropout combined with a word2vec-style loss, offering little methodological novelty.  
- Major advantage is efficiency at generation time: document vectors are computed by averaging word embeddings, avoiding Doc2Vec-like inference.  
- Learned embeddings capture global document information that supports local-context prediction.  
- Despite simplicity, the method performs notably well on sentiment analysis and document classification tasks.  
- Overall: not groundbreaking but a valuable, practical contribution—recommended for acceptance.
