This paper discusses a method for computing vector representations for documents, using a skip-gram style learning system as a global reference vector with an additional regularizer that has various bits of drop-out. Although none of the individual components proposed in this paper are new, I believe the combination of these approaches is novel. Furthermore, I appreciated the detailed analysis of model behavior in Section 3.

The main drawback of this presentation is its relative weakness on the empirical front. Arguably, sentiment analysis and K-means clustering are more interesting tasks! Similarly, why waste 2/3 of a page on T-SNI estimates instead of using that space for further analysis?

Although I am slightly disappointed with this low assessment and agree with other reviewers regarding the soft baselines, I think this paper should be accepted: it is an interesting algorithm, well-written, and very efficient, so it is reasonable to assume that other readers may find it useful for some of the ideas presented here.
