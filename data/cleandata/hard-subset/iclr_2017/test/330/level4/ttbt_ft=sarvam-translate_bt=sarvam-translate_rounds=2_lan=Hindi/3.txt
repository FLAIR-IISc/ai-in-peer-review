This paper proposes a learning document embedding algorithm that combines word and document embedding, which are learned jointly during training and randomly dropped ('corrupt'). Although no part of this model is particularly new, the result is an efficient learning algorithm for document representation with good empirical performance.

The combined training of words and document embedding is not a new idea, nor is the idea of representing a document by the sum of its word embeddings (for example, see "The Sum of Its Parts": Combined Teaching of Word and Phrase Representation with Autoencoders by Lebrite and Kolbert). Furthermore, the corruption mechanism is nothing more than the traditional dropout at the input layer. Combined with Word2Vec-style loss and training methods, this paper offers very little on the front of novelty.

On the other hand, it is very efficient in terms of generation time, requiring only the average of word embeddings, not a complex estimation step like Doc2Vec. Moreover, by construction, embedding captures significant global information about the document – this specifically captures information that assists in local-context prediction. For such a simple model, the performance on sentiment analysis and document classification is quite encouraging.

Overall, despite the lack of novelty, the simplicity, efficiency, and performance of this model make it worthy of a broad readership and study, and I recommend acceptance.
