- Paper proposes a simple document representation: average word embeddings with data-dependent regularization that upweights informative/rare words and shrinks frequent words toward zero.  
- Empirical results on sentiment analysis and document classification show the method achieves the lowest error rates compared to baseline embedding methods.  
- Reviewer appreciates the motivation but finds limited technical novelty.  
- Most techniques used are not original; main strengths are simplicity and computational efficiency.  
- Reviewer recommends evaluating the framework on a broader range of tasks to better establish its superiority.  
- Clarification requested: is the RNN language model trained to minimize classification error or optimized purely as a language model?  
- Clarification requested: is the document representation taken as the final hidden state or the average of all hidden states?  
- Suggestion: compare against a bidirectional LSTM that concatenates final hidden states, a commonly adopted baseline for document representation.  
- Recommendation: include comparisons to the bi-LSTM baseline on tasks like document classification and sentiment analysis for a more comprehensive evaluation.
