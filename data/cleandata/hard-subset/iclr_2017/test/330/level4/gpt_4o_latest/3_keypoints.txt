- Introduces learning document embeddings by summing jointly trained word embeddings with input-layer dropout ("corruption") during training.  
- Model components (joint training, sum-of-words representation) are not novel and have prior work (e.g., Lebret & Collobert).  
- The corruption mechanism is essentially standard dropout applied to inputs.  
- Uses a word2vec-style loss and training framework, limiting methodological novelty.  
- Very efficient at generation time—only averaging word embeddings needed, unlike Doc2Vec’s inference.  
- Learned embeddings capture salient global document information that supports local-context prediction.  
- Achieves strong empirical performance on sentiment analysis and document classification despite simplicity.  
- Overall assessment: limited novelty but valuable due to simplicity, efficiency, and strong results; recommendation to accept.
