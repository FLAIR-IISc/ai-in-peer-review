- Proposes Doc2VecC: learn document embeddings by averaging word embeddings with a corruption mechanism during training.  
- Corruption serves as data-dependent regularization, downweighting frequent uninformative words and highlighting rare informative ones.  
- Method is simple, scalable, and computationally efficient for both training and inference.  
- Achieves competitive or superior performance on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness tasks.  
- Avoids parameter explosion of methods like Paragraph Vectors by relying on averaging instead of many document-specific parameters.  
- Builds on Word2Vec/Paragraph Vectors; averaging is not novel but the integrated corruption mechanism is a meaningful contribution.  
- Experimental evaluation is rigorous with strong baselines and analyses of regularization effects and scalability.  
- Conference decision: Accept.  
- Suggestion: Better clarify novelty relative to denoising autoencoders and weighted averaging approaches.  
- Suggestion: Add more theoretical analysis or ablation studies beyond the provided Taylor expansion explanation.  
- Suggestion: Include comparisons or discussion of transformer-based contextual models.  
- Suggestion: Explore broader applicability to other NLP tasks (e.g., summarization, QA).  
- Question: How does the corruption rate (q) affect performance across datasets and is there an optimal range?  
- Question: Have you considered using contextual embeddings (e.g., BERT) instead of static Word2Vec embeddings?  
- Question: How does Doc2VecC handle extremely long documents or documents with very sparse content?
