- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as the average of word embeddings and jointly train embeddings for this compositional task using a corrupted global context.  
- Trains with a Word2Vec CBoW-like objective augmented by a corrupted global context (random subset of document words) to predict target words.  
- The corruption acts as a data-dependent regularizer that downweights frequent, non-informative words (theoretical analysis supports this).  
- Empirically matches or outperforms complex baselines (Paragraph Vectors, Skip-thought) on sentiment analysis, document classification, and word analogy tasks.  
- Achieves much faster inference: generating embeddings for unseen documents requires only averaging, making it far more practical than methods needing per-document optimization.  
- On IMDB, Doc2VecC attains 11.70% error, comparable to Paragraph Vectors and better than Word2Vec+IDF and Skip-thought.  
- Inference speed example: Doc2VecC takes 7s for IMDB test set vs. over 4 minutes for Paragraph Vectors.  
- Word analogy results indicate Doc2VecC learns higher-quality word vectors than Word2Vec and Paragraph Vectors.  
- The main novelty is the effective combination and joint training of known techniques (averaging + input dropout), not a wholly new corruption mechanism.  
- Suggestions: better position the corruption as input-layer dropout, include an ablation study without corruption, and empirically show sensitivity to corruption rate q.  
- Questions for authors: clarify novelty of the corruption model, test Skip-thought performance on truncated reviews to validate the long-document hypothesis, and explore non-uniform corruption (e.g., IDF-based).
