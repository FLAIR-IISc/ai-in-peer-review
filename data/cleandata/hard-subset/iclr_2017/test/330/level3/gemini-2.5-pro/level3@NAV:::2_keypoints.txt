- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as the average of word embeddings trained with a corruption-based, data-dependent regularizer.  
- Regularizer behavior: upweights rare informative words and shrinks common, non-discriminative word embeddings toward zero.  
- Empirical claim: achieves state-of-the-art or competitive error rates on sentiment analysis and a document classification task.  
- Efficiency claim: significantly faster to train and deploy than methods like Paragraph Vectors.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Primary reasons for rejection: limited novelty of the technical contribution and insufficiently comprehensive empirical evaluation.  
- Strength: method is simple and computationally efficient, clearly demonstrated.  
- Novelty concern: averaging embeddings and corruption/dropout are established techniques; contribution is viewed as incremental.  
- Empirical limitation: evaluations confined to sentiment analysis and a single classification benchmark, insufficient to support general-purpose superiority.  
- Suggested additional experiments: test on broader tasks (e.g., information retrieval, semantic textual similarity) to demonstrate wider applicability.  
- Recommended baseline: include a bidirectional LSTM using concatenated final forward and backward hidden states.  
- Clarification requested about RNN-LM baseline: was it trained only as a language model or fine-tuned for classification, and was the document representation the final hidden state or an average over timesteps?
