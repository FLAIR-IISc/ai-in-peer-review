- Presents Doc2VecC: represent documents as the average of word embeddings learned jointly via a CBOW-style objective using a corrupted global context.  
- Corruption (randomly dropping words) acts as a data-dependent regularizer, shrinking embeddings of common non-informative words and preserving rare informative ones.  
- Achieves strong empirical performance, matching or outperforming Paragraph Vectors and Skip-thought on sentiment, classification, and semantic relatedness tasks.  
- Highly efficient: extremely fast training and especially fast inference (e.g., 25k documents in seconds vs minutes/hours for baselines).  
- The main contribution is an effective synthesis of simple ideas (averaging, dropout, CBOW) rather than a single novel component.  
- Theoretical analysis (Sec 3.1) explains the regularization effect of corruption and is supported by qualitative evidence.  
- Doc2VecC yields higher-quality word embeddings than Word2Vec, improving word analogy performance and indicating better composability.  
- Despite limited methodological novelty, the combination of simplicity, speed, and performance makes it a valuable, practical baseline.  
- Suggest explicitly framing “corruption” as input dropout to clarify novelty and relation to existing techniques.  
- Recommend an ablation/sensitivity study on the corruption rate q (paper uses q=0.9) to substantiate robustness claims.  
- Request experimental comparison of Doc2VecC vs Word2Vec+AVG with/without frequent-word subsampling to quantify implicit regularization benefits.  
- Suggest testing the Skip-thought length hypothesis by training/evaluating on documents truncated to shorter lengths.  
- Ask for examples of very frequent words whose embeddings are not pushed to zero to illustrate when common words remain informative.
