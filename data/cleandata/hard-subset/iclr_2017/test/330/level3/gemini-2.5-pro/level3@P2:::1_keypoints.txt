- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as averaged word embeddings with random word-dropout corruption during training.  
- Theoretical analysis (Section 3.1) shows corruption acts as a data-dependent regularizer that penalizes frequent non-discriminative words and favors rare informative ones.  
- Method is simple, elegant, and well-motivated, elevating a heuristic to a principled approach.  
- Extremely efficient to train and infer—orders of magnitude faster than Skip-thought and avoids costly inference required by Paragraph Vectors.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art or competitive results on IMDB sentiment, Wikipedia classification, and semantic relatedness benchmarks.  
- Learns higher-quality word embeddings than Word2Vec, supported by improved word analogy performance.  
- Analysis of embedding norms demonstrates suppression of stop words and retention of informative rare words.  
- Empirical evaluation is broadly supportive but could be strengthened by deeper error analysis instead of extensive t-SNE visualizations.  
- Baseline comparison fairness questioned—Skip-thought’s poor IMDB performance may need stronger application (e.g., chunking + sentence averaging) or justification.  
- Claim that model is insensitive to corruption rate q (used q=0.9) should be backed by an ablation study to guide practitioners.  
- Asks authors to try alternative strategies for Skip-thought baseline on long documents and to clarify how implicit regularization relates to Word2Vec sub-sampling.
