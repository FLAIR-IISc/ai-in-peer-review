- Proposes Doc2VecC: represents a document as the simple average of word embeddings with a corruption model that randomly removes words during learning.
- Corruption introduces data-dependent regularization that favors informative/rare words and shrinks common/non-discriminative word embeddings toward zero.
- Claims Doc2VecC yields better word embeddings than Word2Vec and outperforms several state-of-the-art document representation methods on sentiment, classification, and semantic relatedness tasks.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on simplicity, efficiency, and strong empirical results; notes components are not individually novel but effectively combined.
- Method is efficient to train and test and avoids expensive inference required by Paragraph Vectors.
- Corruption mechanism helps the model capture global document semantics by focusing on informative words.
- Empirical results are strong across multiple evaluation tasks.
- Suggests adding a more detailed analysis of the corruption mechanism and its effect on learned embeddings.
- Recommends providing more insight into hyperparameter choices (e.g., corruption rate) and their performance impact.
- Encourages testing Doc2VecC on additional tasks (e.g., machine translation, question answering) to demonstrate versatility.
- Asks for details on how the corruption rate affects model performance and learned embeddings.
- Asks how hyperparameters were chosen and the effects of different values.
- Asks whether Doc2VecC has been applied to tasks like machine translation or QA and what the outcomes were.
