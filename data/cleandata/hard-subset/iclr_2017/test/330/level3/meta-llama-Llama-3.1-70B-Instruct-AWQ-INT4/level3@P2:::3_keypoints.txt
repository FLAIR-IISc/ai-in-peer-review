- Proposes Doc2VecC: document representation as simple average of word embeddings with a corruption-based regularizer.
- Corruption mechanism acts as data-dependent regularization, favoring informative/rare words and shrinking common/non-discriminative words.
- Avoids expensive inference (e.g., Doc2Vec), making training and inference much faster.
- Empirically outperforms Word2Vec and several state-of-the-art document representation methods on sentiment, classification, and semantic relatedness.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance primarily for the model's simplicity/efficiency and strong empirical results.
- Model components and training are not novel individually, but their combination and application are well-executed and clearly explained.
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis of the corruption mechanism’s impact and sensitivity to corruption rate.
- Suggestion: compare against other efficient document models (e.g., CNN- or RNN-based approaches).
- Suggestion: evaluate on additional tasks (e.g., text classification, information retrieval) to demonstrate broader effectiveness.
- Question: how does corruption rate affect performance and what are optimal rates across tasks?
- Question: how does the model handle out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words and are there plans to extend OOV handling?
- Question: provide details on computational resources, and how performance scales with training data size and model parameters.
