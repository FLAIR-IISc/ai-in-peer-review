- Proposes Doc2VecC: document as average of word embeddings with a corruption model that regularizes toward informative/rare words and shrinks common words.
- Corruption model is a novel contribution that improves model performance.
- Evaluated on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness; achieves state-of-the-art in some cases.
- Claims simplicity and high speed in training/testing compared to existing methods.
- Decision: accept, primarily due to simplicity, efficiency, and competitive performance.
- Paper is well-motivated with clear critique of bag-of-words and paragraph vector limitations.
- Experiments are thorough and include analysis showing Doc2VecC embeddings are more informative/discriminative than Word2Vec.
- Requests more evaluations on additional tasks/datasets (e.g., broader text classification, clustering, topic modeling).
- Requests a more detailed analysis of the corruption model’s impact, strengths, and limitations.
- Asks for clarification on the RNN-LM used: training objective (language model vs classification) and which hidden-state representation was used.
- Asks how the corruption model affects performance across different tasks and where it is particularly effective or ineffective.
- Requests comparison with a bidirectional LSTM baseline that concatenates final hidden states.
