This paper proposes a novel document representation learning framework, Document Vector through Corruption (Doc2VecC), which represents each document as a simple average of word embeddings learned through a skip-gram style objective. The authors introduce a corruption model that randomly removes words from a document during learning, which acts as a data-dependent regularization that favors informative or rare words while dampening the embeddings of common and non-discriminative words. This corruption mechanism enables the model to capture the global semantics of the document during learning and significantly boosts test efficiency.

I recommend accepting this paper. The proposed method is interesting, well-composed, and efficient, and is likely to be useful to others in the field of natural language processing. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the model's behavior and demonstrate its effectiveness on several tasks, including sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a thorough review of the existing literature on document representation learning. They clearly explain the limitations of existing approaches and demonstrate how their proposed method addresses these limitations. The authors also provide a detailed description of their method, including the corruption model and the data-dependent regularization, which enables readers to understand the underlying mechanics of the model.
