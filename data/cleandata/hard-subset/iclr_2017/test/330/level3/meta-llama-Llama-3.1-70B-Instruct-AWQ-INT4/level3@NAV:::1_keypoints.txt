- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as the average of word embeddings with a corruption model for regularization.  
- Corruption model yields data-dependent regularization that upweights informative/rare words and downweights common/non-discriminative words.  
- Authors claim Doc2VecC improves testing efficiency and representation expressiveness over existing methods.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Key reason 1: novel and useful combination of existing techniques.  
- Key reason 2: empirical results show promising testing efficiency.  
- Strength: paper provides a detailed analysis of model behavior.  
- Limitation: empirical evaluation is narrow (sentiment analysis and k-way classification only).  
- Concern: baselines used are weak (bag-of-words, Word2Vec+IDF), possibly insufficient to demonstrate superiority.  
- Concern: excessive emphasis on t-SNE visualizations that add little insight into performance.  
- Suggested improvements: broader empirical evaluation with stronger, more challenging baselines and tasks.  
- Suggested improvements: add theoretical analysis of the corruption model and report hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity.  
- Questions for authors: clarify the corruption model and how the corruption rate q affects performance.  
- Questions for authors: explain strategies to prevent overfitting related to the corruption model.  
- Questions for authors: provide comparative results and insights on semantic relatedness performance versus state-of-the-art.
