- Proposes Doc2VecC, an efficient document representation learning framework using corruption.
- Claims Doc2VecC yields high-quality document vectors that outperform several state-of-the-art methods on sentiment analysis, classification, and semantic relatedness.
- Model is simple, fast to train/test, and scalable to large datasets.
- Novelty: combines a skip-gram style objective with a global context vector regularizer and corruption/dropout.
- Strength: detailed analysis in Section 3 of the corruption model’s regularization effects.
- Empirical results demonstrate effectiveness but the evaluation is limited.
- Concern: evaluation lacks a comprehensive set of competitive baselines.
- Concern: t-SNE visualizations occupy too much space and should be replaced with deeper empirical analyses.
- Suggestion: include more state-of-the-art baselines and stronger comparisons.
- Suggestion: analyze hyperparameter effects, robustness across datasets, and replace or reduce t-SNE usage.
- Question: provide details on hyperparameter tuning for the proposed method and baselines.
- Question: report performance on additional datasets (e.g., 20 Newsgroups, Reuters).
- Question: give more insight into how the corruption regularizer compares to dropout and L1/L2 regularization.
- Overall recommendation: accept — method is interesting, well-composed, efficient, and likely useful with additional evaluation.
