- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent a document as the average of word embeddings with a corruption model that yields data-dependent regularization favoring rare/informative words.
- Reports strong results: competitive or state-of-the-art performance on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness (e.g., IMDB, Wikipedia).
- Decision: accept — main reasons are the method’s simplicity, ease of implementation, and training efficiency on large datasets.
- Supporting argument: Doc2VecC is computationally efficient and simpler than many alternatives, yet achieves competitive performance.
- Supporting argument: Empirical results show state-of-the-art or very competitive accuracy on several benchmark datasets.

- Question for authors: Explain hyperparameter selection and offer practical tuning guidelines for Doc2VecC.
