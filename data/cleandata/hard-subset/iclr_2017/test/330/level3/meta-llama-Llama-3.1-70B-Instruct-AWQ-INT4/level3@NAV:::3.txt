**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper proposes a novel document representation learning framework called Document Vector through Corruption (Doc2VecC). The framework represents each document as a simple average of word embeddings and incorporates a corruption model that randomly removes words from a document during training. The authors claim that this approach captures the semantic meanings of the document during learning and introduces a data-dependent regularization that favors informative or rare words. The paper also presents empirical results demonstrating the effectiveness of Doc2VecC on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness tasks.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

I recommend accepting this paper. The two key reasons for this decision are:

1. **Strong empirical results**: The paper presents strong empirical results on multiple tasks, including sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness. The results demonstrate that Doc2VecC outperforms several state-of-the-art document representation learning algorithms.
2. **Efficient and simple model architecture**: The paper proposes a simple and efficient model architecture that can be trained on large datasets. The authors demonstrate that Doc2VecC can be trained on billions of words per hour on a single machine, making it a scalable solution for document representation learning.

**Supporting Arguments**

While the paper's approach is not entirely novel, the combination of joint word/document training and the corruption model is a unique contribution. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the corruption model and its effect on the learned word embeddings. The paper also presents a comprehensive evaluation of Doc2VecC on multiple tasks, including a comparison with several state-of-the-art algorithms.
