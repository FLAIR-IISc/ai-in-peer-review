- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as averages of word embeddings learned with a skip-gram objective plus random word corruption.
- Introduces a corruption model that randomly removes words during training to regularize and emphasize informative/rare words while downweighting common words.
- Claims corruption helps capture global document semantics and greatly improves test-time efficiency.
- Method is novel, well-composed, efficient, and likely useful to NLP practitioners.
- Authors provide a clear motivation, thorough literature review, and detailed method description.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on tasks including sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.
- Paper includes detailed analysis of model behavior but empirical evaluation is limited in scope.
- Main weakness: evaluation uses only a few datasets; needs broader benchmarks and tasks.
- Lacks deeper performance analysis such as error analysis and ablation studies.
- Needs more in-depth comparison and discussion of relationships with Paragraph Vectors and Word2Vec.
- Suggested improvements: expand evaluations, add error/ablation studies, deepen method comparisons, and include visualizations (e.g., t-SNE).
- Questions for authors: implementation details of corruption model and how hyperparameters (corruption rate) are chosen.
- Requests more analysis of corruption model’s effect on performance and computational efficiency comparisons with Paragraph Vectors/Word2Vec.
- Requests clearer details on datasets and preprocessing used in experiments.
- Reviewer recommendation: accept, contingent on additional analysis and evaluations to strengthen the paper.
