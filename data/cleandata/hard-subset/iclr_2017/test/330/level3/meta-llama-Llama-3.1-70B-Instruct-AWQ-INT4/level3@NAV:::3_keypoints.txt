- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as averaged word embeddings with a corruption model that randomly removes words during training.  
- Claims the corruption induces data-dependent regularization that favors informative/rare words and captures document semantics.  
- Reports strong empirical results outperforming state-of-the-art on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.  
- Recommends acceptance, citing strong empirical performance and a simple, scalable architecture.  
- Notes the model is efficient and scalable (trained on billions of words per hour on a single machine).  
- Contribution is not wholly novel but the joint word/document training combined with corruption is a unique aspect.  
- Authors provide thorough analysis of the corruption model’s effect and a comprehensive evaluation against baselines.  
- Suggest providing more details on hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity.  
- Suggest adding visualizations to better illustrate learned document representations.  
- Suggest discussing limitations and future work (e.g., handling OOV words and varying document lengths).  
- Asks for clarification on corruption model implementation, selection and effect of corruption rate q.  
- Asks how out-of-vocabulary words are handled (special token vs. ignored).  
- Requests insights on how learned word embeddings differ from Word2Vec or Paragraph Vectors.
