- Proposes Doc2VecC: a document representation framework combining skip-gram objective, a global context vector regularizer, and dropout/corruption.
- Combination likely novel though individual components are not.
- Section 3 offers a thorough analysis of model behavior.
- Corruption acts as data-dependent regularization, suppressing embeddings of frequent/uninformative words.
- Empirical evaluation is limited (sentiment analysis and k-way classification only).
- Comparisons to baselines (Word2Vec, Paragraph Vectors, Skip-thought) show Doc2VecC outperforms some methods in places.
- Evaluation could be strengthened with more tasks, datasets, and stronger baselines.
- Too much space devoted to t-SNE projections, which are not very informative.
- Use of weak/“soft” baselines (e.g., Word2Vec+IDF) weakens the evaluation.
- Method is interesting, efficient, well-composed, and likely useful to others.
- Model and components are clearly explained and easy to implement.
- Code is made available (positive).
- Decision: Accept.
- Recommendations: include more tasks/datasets, stronger baselines (e.g., LSTM-based), deeper empirical analyses (ablation, document-type behavior), and report hyperparameter tuning and computational resources.
- Questions for authors: details on hyperparameter tuning and compute; how the technique combination was chosen and alternatives tried; behavior on short vs. long or different-genre documents; performance on other tasks (IR, QA).
