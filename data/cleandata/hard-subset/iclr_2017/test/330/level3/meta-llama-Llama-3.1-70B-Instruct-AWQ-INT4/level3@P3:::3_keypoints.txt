- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as the average of word embeddings with random word corruption during training.  
- Corruption model provides data-dependent regularization that favors informative/rare words and pushes common, non-discriminative words toward zero.  
- Strong empirical results: outperforms several state-of-the-art methods on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.  
- Efficient document generation: simple averaging removes the need for expensive inference.  
- Combination of joint word/document training and dropout-style corruption is an effective, though not wholly novel, approach.  
- Evaluation is thorough with comprehensive comparisons to multiple baselines.  
- Request for more details on the implementation and hyperparameter tuning of the corruption model.  
- Suggest comparison to additional algorithms, including deep learning–based document representation methods.  
- Suggest broader evaluation on more tasks to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Question: How are corruption-model hyperparameters selected and tuned across different tasks?  
- Question: How does the data-dependent regularization specifically affect learned word embeddings?  
- Question: How does Doc2VecC handle out-of-vocabulary words in training and evaluation?
