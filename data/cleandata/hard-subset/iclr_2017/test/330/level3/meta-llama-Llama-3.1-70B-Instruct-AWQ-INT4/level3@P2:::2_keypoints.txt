- Proposes Doc2VecC: represents documents as averages of word embeddings with a corruption model that regularizes toward informative/rare words.  
- Recommendation to accept based on simplicity and efficiency, suitable for large-scale document representation.  
- Strong experimental results on sentiment analysis and document classification demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Clear motivation and well-executed, though the technical contribution is not entirely novel.  
- Corruption model is an interesting data-dependent regularizer that suppresses common/non-discriminative words.  
- Experiments are thorough versus several baselines but need broader task coverage to prove general superiority.  
- Request clarification on RNN-LM baseline: trained as language model or for classification, and whether final or averaged hidden states were used.  
- Suggest adding a bidirectional LSTM baseline that concatenates final hidden states.  
- Minor suggestion to analyze the effect of corruption rate on performance.  
- Minor suggestion to improve t-SNE visualization with more categories or alternative techniques.  
- Minor suggestion to report computational resources used for training and testing.  
- Overall well-written; with additional evaluations and experimental clarifications, it could make a significant contribution.
