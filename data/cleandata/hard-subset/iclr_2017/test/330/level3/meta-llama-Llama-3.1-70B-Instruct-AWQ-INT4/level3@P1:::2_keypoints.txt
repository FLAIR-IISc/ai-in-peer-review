- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as simple averages of word embeddings with a corruption-based regularizer.  
- Corruption model yields data-dependent regularization that upweights rare/informative words and drives common/non-discriminative words toward zero.  
- Authors claim Doc2VecC outperforms state-of-the-art methods on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing strong motivation and solid experimental evidence.  
- Strength: clear, concise motivation and thorough related-work review.  
- Strength: method is simple, effective, and offers a convincing explanation for the corruption mechanism.  
- Strength: experimental results are impressive with detailed comparative analysis across tasks.  
- Concern: technical novelty is limited; many employed techniques are not new and specific contributions could be clearer.  
- Concern: evaluation is somewhat narrow; more tasks are needed to support general superiority claims.  
- Concern: lacks comparison to widely used baselines such as bidirectional LSTM.  
- Question: Clarify RNN-LM details—was it trained as a language model or for classification, and was the final hidden state or average of hidden states used as the representation?  
- Suggestion: Provide more implementation details on the corruption model and how it regularizes embeddings.  
- Suggestion: Analyze how varying the corruption rate affects performance.  
- Suggestion: Compare Doc2VecC to other data-dependent regularization methods (e.g., dropout).  
- Suggestion: Report computational resources and software/hardware used to aid reproducibility.
