- Proposes Doc2VecC: jointly learn word/document embeddings with document vector as average of corrupted-word embeddings, enabling fast training and test-time averaging without inference.
- Uses unbiased input corruption (dropout) during training to speed learning and induce data-dependent regularization that downweights common words.
- Training uses CBoW-style negative sampling; test-time document vectors are simple averages of word embeddings.
- Empirically strong on IMDB sentiment, Wikipedia classification, SICK relatedness, and improves word analogy accuracy over word2vec.
- Decision: Accept — justified by strong empirical performance, simplicity/efficiency, and a clear analysis of corruption as regularization.
- Problem/significance: Addresses scalable global document embeddings with fast inference, avoiding costly per-document parameters/inference like Paragraph Vectors.
- Approach/motivation: Natural synthesis of known components (CBoW, averaging, input dropout); novelty is limited but the combination is practical and purposeful.
- Theoretical analysis: Uses second-order Taylor expansion to connect corruption to an adaptive regularizer that shrinks frequent/uninformative word embeddings.
- Correctness/rigor: Follows established training practices (negative sampling, subsampling); corruption is unbiased and analytic approximations are reasonable and predictive.
- Empirical support: Consistently competitive/better than Doc2Vec, Word2Vec+IDF, DAE, Skip-Thought; much faster training and trivial test-time inference.
- Positioning: Compares to relevant baselines and situates corruption analysis within prior dropout/regularization literature.
- Novelty/framing feedback: Explicitly acknowledge precedents and frame contribution as a clean, unified recipe with strong empirical/analytic support.
- Ablation requests: Report no-corruption baseline, varying corruption rates q, average vs weighted-average at test time, and effect of removing local context.
- Regularization analysis requests: Add plots of embedding norm vs term frequency/IDF and discuss limits of the second-order approximation.
- Baseline fairness requests: Ensure comparable embedding sizes/training budgets, tune PV-DBOW, and include strong tuned averaging baselines (IDF/SIF).
- Practical details requests: Provide hyperparameters, sensitivity analyses, OOV/morphology handling, and effect of phrase detection or subword models.
- Scope/limitations requests: Discuss behavior on very short vs very long texts and applicability to multilingual/highly inflected languages.
- Question: How much of the gain comes from corruption — provide ablations: no corruption, corruption-only global term, and varying q with accuracy/training time?
- Question: Sensitivity to negative sampling distribution and number of negatives?
- Question: Does Doc2VecC still outperform strong pretrained word2vec averaging baselines with IDF/SIF weighting and de-biasing?
- Question: Can you quantify the regularization effect directly (correlation between embedding norm and IDF/document frequency; stopword norm reduction)?
- Question: Have you tried subword/character n-gram models for OOV/morphology, and what is the impact?
- Question: For SICK, were all methods pretrained similarly or fine-tuned on the same data — what happens if trained only on SICK?
- Question: How does performance vary with document length (tweets vs full articles) and is there an optimal q/window size dependent on length?
