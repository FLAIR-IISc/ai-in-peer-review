- Proposal: Doc2VecC averages word embeddings learned with an unbiased mask-out corruption that induces a data-dependent regularizer to downweight common words and preserve informative ones.  
- Strengths: Computationally efficient, decouples model size from corpus size, shows strong results on sentiment analysis and document classification, with analyses on word analogies and SICK.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Primary concern 1: Limited technical novelty—averaging, corruption/dropout, and negative sampling are known; the regularization link is incremental relative to dropout/mDAE.  
- Primary concern 2: Experimental validation is insufficient—missing stronger baselines and broader tasks to support claims of general superiority.  
- Problem/motivation: Clear and well-motivated; making average-of-embeddings work via training-time regularization is appealing.  
- Placement in literature: Method lies between Word2Vec+pooling and PV/mDAE but is conceptually incremental.  
- Evidence/rigor positive: Reported gains on IMDB/Wikipedia and speedups are persuasive.  
- Evidence/rigor negative: Missing standard neural baselines (BiLSTM, CNN), under-specified RNN-LM baseline, limited significance testing and ablation studies.  
- Specific ablations requested: Vary corruption rate q; remove local context; compare unbiased vs biased corruption; vary window size and negative sampling distribution; correlate learned norms with IDF/informativeness.  
- Baselines to add: BiLSTM (concatenated final states), BiLSTM with average pooling, CNN text classifiers, and Word2Vec/SIF-style weighted averaging.  
- Evaluation expansion: Test more datasets/tasks (additional topic/sentiment datasets, multi-label news, cross-domain transfer) and report statistical significance.  
- Fairness/detailing: Match subsampling and preprocessing across baselines and avoid representation learning on test data (or separate settings).  
- Clarify RNN-LM: State training objective (LM vs classification), representation used (final vs averaged hidden states), and uni- vs bidirectional setup.  
- Questions about experiments: Did you try BiLSTM concat final states? How sensitive are results to q and unbiased scaling? What negative sampling distribution and number of negatives were used and are results robust? Do learned word norms correlate with IDF or mutual information?
