- Proposes Doc2VecC: learn document vectors by averaging word embeddings plus a corrupted global bag-of-words context enforced during training.
- Training enforces that the mean of word embeddings suffices to predict target words, combining local CBoW context with a corrupted global context.
- Uses unbiased dropout corruption and shows via second-order Taylor expansion that this induces a data-dependent regularizer.
- The regularizer attenuates embeddings of frequent/non-informative words while preserving informative/rare words, explaining why averaging works.
- Empirically matches or outperforms baselines on IMDB and Wikipedia classification and is competitive on SICK semantic relatedness.
- Method is highly efficient at training and inference, scales to large corpora, and code is released.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reasons for acceptance: conceptually clean, well-motivated, likely novel combination, and theoretically grounded corruption mechanism.
- Practical significance: simple, reproducible, no per-document parameters, fast inference, and useful as a strong baseline.
- Positioning: clearly contrasted with Word2Vec, Paragraph Vectors, denoising autoencoders, and sentence encoders.
- Technical correctness: Taylor approximation analysis is sound in spirit and aligns with empirical observations (e.g., reduced norms for stopwords).
- Empirical support is solid but somewhat narrow; results and efficiency comparisons are favorable.
- Recommendation: strengthen baselines by adding TF-IDF+linear, fastText-style bag-of-ngrams, supervised CNN/LSTM, and both PV-DM/PV-DBOW with fair tuning.
- Suggest broader datasets and deeper ablations: additional corpora, corruption-rate sweeps, removing corruption/local context, varying window size and 1/T scaling, and dimensionality controls.
- Replace some qualitative t-SNEs with quantitative analyses (clustering metrics, cosine distributions) and include error analysis.
- Reproducibility requests: specify negative sampling distribution, number of negatives, learning rates/schedules, epochs, subsampling thresholds, vocab cutoff, preprocessing, and random seeds.
- Theory/exposition requests: discuss limits of the second-order approximation and diagonal Hessian, and provide intuition/alternatives for 1/T scaling.
- Ask authors to report sensitivity to negative sampling (uniform vs unigram^0.75), context window sizes, and provide performance vs corruption-rate plots and noise-model comparisons.
- Requested ablations: remove local/global context, remove subsampling to isolate regularizer, and test tying/input-output embedding choices.
- Implementation questions: epochs, learning rates, whether U and V are both updated, embedding tying, and SICK fine-tuning details.
- Fairness/scope questions: compare to strong supervised baselines (NBSVM, CNNs), and discuss multi-label Wikipedia pages vs single-label setup.
- Generalization questions: try Skip-gram variant, report cross-domain robustness, and note interactions between proposed regularizer and Word2Vec subsampling.
