- Contribution: Proposes Doc2VecC, averaging word embeddings with data-dependent regularization via corruption to upweight rare/informative words.  
- Strength: Simple, fast implementation with significant reported speed advantages (billions of words/hour).  
- Strength: Shows improved performance over baselines on sentiment analysis and document classification.  
- Weakness: Technical novelty limited — components (embedding averaging, data-dependent regularization, dropout-like corruption) are not fundamentally new.  
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is narrow — only two main tasks plus SICK, insufficient to prove general superiority.  
- Analysis: Insightful analysis of learned embeddings and regularization effects, but lacks deeper explanation of which specific words are up/downweighted and why.  
- Needed clarification: Describe RNN-LM baseline training objective and how document representations were extracted (final vs. averaged hidden states).  
- Suggested baseline: Compare against a bidirectional LSTM (concatenated final hidden states) for a stronger evaluation.  
- Ablation: Perform ablation to isolate the corruption mechanism’s contribution and test varying corruption rates (q).  
- Hyperparameters: Report sensitivity to embedding dimension, learning rate, and corruption rate to assess robustness.  
- Vocabulary: Discuss impact of vocabulary cutoff on performance and whether an optimal vocabulary size exists.  
- Questions for authors: Clarify RNN-LM details, provide BiLSTM comparison results, list example words up/downweighted and reasons, and show performance across different q values.
