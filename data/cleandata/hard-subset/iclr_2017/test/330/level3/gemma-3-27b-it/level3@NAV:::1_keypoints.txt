- Introduces Doc2VecC: average word embeddings with skip-gram objective, global context regularizer, and corruption/dropout.
- Corruption (random word removal) is framed as data-dependent regularization that suppresses uninformative words.
- Section 3 offers a detailed theoretical analysis of the regularization effect.
- Reviewer recommends accept due to the novel combination and insightful analysis.
- No single component is groundbreaking, but the combination is practically valuable and efficient.
- Method addresses parameter size and test-time efficiency issues relative to Paragraph Vectors.
- Shows competitive performance on sentiment analysis and document classification tasks.
- Empirical evaluation is limited to standard tasks and should include more nuanced semantic tasks (e.g., QA, summarization).
- Comparisons use relatively weak/“soft” baselines; should include stronger and recent (transformer) baselines.
- Heavy reliance on t-SNE visualizations is insufficient; quantitative analyses of embeddings are needed.
- Question: Have you tried alternative corruption strategies (e.g., POS- or TF-IDF-based masking)?
- Question: Can you quantify the stronger regularization (e.g., compare L2 norms of learned embeddings)?
- Question: Have you considered incorporating weighting (TF-IDF or similar) during training rather than only post-processing?
