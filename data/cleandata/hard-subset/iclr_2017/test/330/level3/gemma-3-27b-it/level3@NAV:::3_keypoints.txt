- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents by averaging word embeddings trained with random word dropout (corruption).
- Combines simple ideas (averaging + dropout); individual components not novel but their combination is effective.
- Strong empirical performance on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness tasks.
- Consistently outperforms several baselines and is competitive with more complex models.
- Offers a substantial efficiency advantage at inference time compared to methods like Doc2Vec.
- Paper clearly motivates averaging embeddings and frames corruption as a regularizer favoring informative words.
- Experimental setup is comprehensive, including diverse baselines and useful ablation studies.
- Authors acknowledge limitations and relate their work to prior literature.
- Reviewer requests deeper theoretical/intuitive justification of why corruption functions as a data-dependent regularizer.
- Reviewer asks for more detailed analysis of sensitivity to the corruption rate q.
- Reviewer recommends quantifying how performance scales with document length, particularly versus LSTM methods.
- Reviewer requests an intuitive, concrete example showing how corruption downweights less informative words in learned embeddings.
- Reviewer asks about sensitivity to the vocabulary cutoff (currently 10) and its impact on performance.
- Reviewer asks for computational cost details (training time and memory) as document size increases, compared to Doc2Vec.
