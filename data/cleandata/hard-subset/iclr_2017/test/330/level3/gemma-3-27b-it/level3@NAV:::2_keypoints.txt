- Proposes Doc2VecC: average word embeddings with data-dependent regularization that upweights rare words and suppresses common words.
- Core idea is well-motivated by semantic preservation under linear operations on embeddings.
- Demonstrates competitive, efficient performance with lowest errors on sentiment analysis and document classification baselines.
- Recommends Reject: technical contribution seen as incremental since averaging and regularization are not novel alone.
- Empirical evaluation is limited (only two tasks), so generalizability and true superiority are unclear.
- Places work appropriately in literature and cites relevant prior work.
- Experiments are clearly described and improvements over baselines are reported comprehensively.
- Main weakness: need broader evaluation (e.g., topic modeling, information retrieval) to strengthen claims.
- Clarify RNN-LM baseline setup: training objective and which document representation was used (final hidden state vs. averaged hidden states).
- Add a bidirectional LSTM baseline concatenating final hidden states for a stronger comparison.
- Provide an ablation study to isolate the effect of the corruption/regularization mechanism.
- Analyze sensitivity to the corruption rate q and discuss its effect on embeddings and downstream performance.
- Justify using simple averaging versus weighted averages (e.g., TF-IDF) for the document representation.
