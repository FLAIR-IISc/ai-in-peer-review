- Proposal: Doc2VecC — represent documents as averaged word embeddings trained with a corruption model that regularizes toward informative words.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novelty/strength: Compelling combination of averaging and corruption; enforces averaging during training rather than post-hoc.  
- Theoretical contribution: Strong mathematical analysis (Section 3) showing corruption acts as a data-dependent regularizer.  
- Empirical results: Competitive performance on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.  
- Efficiency: Training is computationally efficient and fast.  
- Limitation — scope: Experimental evaluation is somewhat limited; should include more diverse datasets and tasks (e.g., document similarity/retrieval).  
- Limitation — qualitative visuals: Overreliance on t-SNE visualizations; these are sensitive to parameters and not a substitute for quantitative analysis.  
- Suggested empirical improvement: Replace some t-SNE space with rigorous analyses such as ablation studies over corruption rates.  
- Baselines: Comparisons use some relatively weak baselines; need stronger, more recent baselines (notably transformer-based methods).  
- Question — corruption rate q: Provide a detailed sensitivity analysis (performance vs. q).  
- Question — vocabulary cutoff: Analyze sensitivity to vocabulary cutoff and when it harms performance.  
- Question — subsampling: Clarify whether removing subsampling from Word2Vec+AVG removes its benefits or if subsampling has other advantages.  
- Question — embedding size h: Offer practical guidelines or heuristics for selecting embedding dimensionality.  
- Question — complexity: Provide a detailed computational-complexity comparison to Paragraph Vectors and other methods.
