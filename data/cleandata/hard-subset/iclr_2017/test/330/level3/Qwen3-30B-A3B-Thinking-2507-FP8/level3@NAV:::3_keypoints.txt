- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as averages of constituent word embeddings.  
- Introduces a corruption mechanism during training that acts as data-dependent regularization to favor informative words and suppress common ones.  
- Ensures representations obtained by averaging during training remain effective at test time.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness tasks.  
- Achieves significant efficiency gains in training and inference compared to prior methods.  
- Recommends acceptance primarily for (1) simplicity and high efficiency enabling billions of words/hour training and fast inference.  
- Recommends acceptance secondarily for strong empirical results that match or outperform state-of-the-art across multiple tasks.  
- Provides convincing experimental evidence across multiple datasets supporting the claims.  
- Offers theoretical justification of the corruption mechanism via Taylor expansion analysis.  
- Suggests clarifying novelty relative to prior sum-of-words methods.  
- Requests a detailed comparison between the corruption mechanism’s regularization effects and standard dropout.  
- Advises discussing limitations, especially handling of very long documents beyond evaluated scope, and explicitly how corruption differs from input dropout in practice.
