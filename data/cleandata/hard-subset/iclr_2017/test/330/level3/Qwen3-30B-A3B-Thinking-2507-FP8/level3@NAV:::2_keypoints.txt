- Proposes Doc2VecC: average word embeddings with corruption-based regularization to upweight rare words and suppress common ones.
- Claims superior semantic document vectors without document-specific parameters, showing improved performance on sentiment analysis and document classification.
- Recommendation: reject.
- Main reason 1: Limited technical novelty—averaging embeddings and corruption mechanism closely build on prior work (denoising autoencoders, Word2Vec).
- Main reason 2: Insufficient evaluation—only two tasks tested, so general superiority is unsubstantiated.
- Strength: Simple and fast method with strong empirical results on the two evaluated tasks.
- Weakness: Missing critical baseline comparisons, notably a bidirectional LSTM concatenating final hidden states.
- Unclear methodology: RNN-LM implementation details are not specified (training objective and which representation was used).
- Fails to address generalization to other tasks beyond the two evaluated.
- Recommended fixes: expand evaluation to ≥3 tasks, clarify RNN-LM details, include bidirectional LSTM baseline, and discuss limitations of the corruption-based regularization.
