- Recommendation: Strong accept based on empirical performance and efficiency.

- Proposal: Doc2VecC represents documents as averages of word embeddings with random corruption during training.

- Empirical results: Matches or outperforms baselines (Paragraph Vectors, Skip-thought, Word2Vec+IDF) on IMDB, Wikipedia classification, and SICK semantic relatedness.

- Specific results: IMDB 87.9% accuracy vs 86.5% (Paragraph Vectors); Wikipedia error 30.24% vs 33.02%; SICK error 11.70% near LSTM performance.

- Efficiency: Extremely fast training (billions of words/hour on one machine) and much faster test-time inference (7s vs 4m17s for 25k docs).

- Mechanism rationale: Corruption acts as data-dependent regularization that downweights common words and preserves rare informative words, akin to dropout applied to words.

- Novelty: Methodological novelty is modest since averaging embeddings is known, but the corruption regularizer is presented as a useful practical innovation.

- Experimental rigor: Strong experimental design, thorough baselines, reproducible code, and plausible explanations for comparative performance differences.

- Requested clarifications: Authors should better acknowledge prior averaging work (Huang et al., 2012) and precisely state the novelty of the corruption mechanism.

- Additional analysis requested: More explicit comparison to standard dropout and a detailed study of how corruption rate q affects performance.

- Scope questions/future work: Clarify whether corruption targets the curse of dimensionality or improves word embeddings, and evaluate Doc2VecC on tasks sensitive to document structure (e.g., summarization, QA).
