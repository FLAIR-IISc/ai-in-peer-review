- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as simple averages of word embeddings with a corruption-based regularizer favoring rare/informative words.  
- Claims high training efficiency (billions of words/hour) and cheap test-time inference for unseen documents.  
- Reports matching or outperforming state-of-the-art on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main critique: limited novelty—averaging embeddings is well-known and the corruption regularizer parallels dropout/denoising autoencoders.  
- Empirical evaluation considered insufficient—only three tasks and no comparison to strong baselines like bidirectional LSTM variants.  
- Missing comparison to commonly used baselines (e.g., BiLSTM concatenated states) makes performance gains unclear or task-specific.  
- Strength acknowledged: simplicity and computational efficiency, but not enough for acceptance at a top-tier venue given limited novelty and evaluation.  
- Recommendation 1: Expand experiments to more diverse tasks and stronger baselines, especially bidirectional LSTM methods.  
- Recommendation 2: Provide sensitivity analysis on the corruption rate q and its effect on performance.  
- Recommendation 3: Include ablation studies to separate the impact of corruption regularization from simple averaging.  
- Recommendation 4: Clarify differences between Doc2VecC and Word2Vec+IDF and how Doc2VecC enforces semantics during training.  
- Clarification Q1: Was the RNN-LM baseline trained as a language model or for classification, and which hidden-state aggregation was used for document representation?  
- Clarification Q2: Could differences versus Skip-thought be due to training corpus/domain and dataset characteristics rather than inherent method superiority?  
- Clarification Q3: How does Doc2VecC compare to the PV-DBOW variant of Paragraph Vectors?  
- Clarification Q4: How does the corruption model’s increase in backpropagated parameters (surviving words) affect computational complexity versus standard Word2Vec?
