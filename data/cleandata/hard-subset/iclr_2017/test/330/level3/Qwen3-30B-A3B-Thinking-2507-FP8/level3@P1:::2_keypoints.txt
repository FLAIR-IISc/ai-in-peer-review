- Paper proposes Doc2VecC: document averages of word embeddings with a corruption model to down-weight common words, justified via Taylor expansion.
- Strength: method is simple, efficient, and shows good empirical performance on evaluated tasks.
- Novelty is limited: averaging embeddings is not new (Huang et al., 2012); corruption is a minor variant of dropout/denoising.
- Claims of “significantly better” performance are overstated given marginal gains (often 1–2%).
- Empirical evaluation is insufficient: only three tasks assessed and lacking analysis of when Doc2VecC succeeds or fails.
- Comparisons to RNN-based baselines are unclear and potentially unfair (RNN-LM training objective and representation unspecified).
- Missing comparison to standard bidirectional LSTM baselines that concatenate final hidden states.
- Theoretical analysis of corruption echoes prior work (Wager et al., 2013; Chen et al., 2014) and lacks clear novelty.
- Reviewer recommends rejection.
- Suggested improvements: better position relative to averaging methods, broader task evaluations, clarify RNN-LM details, and include bidirectional LSTM comparisons.
- Reviewer questions: Was the RNN-LM trained for language modeling or classification and which representation was used? Can results be provided on at least one additional task? Please compare against a bidirectional LSTM that concatenates final states.
