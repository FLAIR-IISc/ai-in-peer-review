- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as averages of word embeddings learned via a CBoW-like model using a corrupted-document global context.  
- Corruption scheme is theoretically equivalent to a data-dependent regularizer that down-weights frequent/non-informative words while preserving rare/discriminative ones.  
- Strong empirical performance across sentiment analysis, document classification, word analogy, and semantic relatedness, often matching or surpassing Paragraph Vectors and Skip-thought vectors.  
- Far more efficient and scalable than Paragraph Vectors; inference for unseen documents is a trivial average, avoiding expensive per-document optimization.  
- Provides a principled theoretical analysis (second-order Taylor expansion) linking corruption to regularization rather than treating it as a heuristic.  
- Learning document-level composition improves word embeddings, yielding superior analogy task performance compared to Word2Vec.  
- t-SNE visualizations and classification results show superior class separability for Doc2VecC representations.  
- Explains reasonable SICK performance and hypothesizes RNNs may excel on short sentences while Doc2VecC excels on longer documents.  
- Reports dramatic efficiency gains in both training and especially inference time (Table 2).  
- Paper is well-written and reproducible code is provided.  
- Title contains a typo ("MENTS THROUGH CORRUPTION") and should be corrected.  
- The Taylor approximation’s validity at high corruption rates (q=0.9) should be discussed; robustness/limitations need clarification.  
- Suggests plotting error vs. document length to strengthen the hypothesis about performance differences with Skip-thought vectors.  
- Recommends an ablation study removing the local context term (Uct) to quantify its contribution.  
- Asks for intuition on why document-level compositional objectives yield better word-level semantics than local-context-only training.  
- Requests confirmation whether main experiments were run without Word2Vec frequent-word subsampling to show corruption-based regularization suffices.  
- Requests a performance vs. corruption-rate (q) plot to demonstrate sensitivity and trade-offs.
