- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as the average of word embeddings and enforce compositionality during training via corruption.  
- Uses a corruption mechanism that forms a global context from a random subset of words when predicting a target word.  
- Theoretical analysis shows the corruption is equivalent to a data-dependent regularizer.  
- Regularizer suppresses embeddings of frequent, non-informative words while preserving rarer, discriminative ones.  
- Empirical results: matches or outperforms Paragraph Vectors, Denoising Autoencoders, and averaged Word2Vec on multiple tasks.  
- Produces word embeddings that outperform Word2Vec on the standard word analogy task.  
- Demonstrates large efficiency gains: training and inference far faster than Paragraph Vectors and Skip-thought Vectors.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Key reason: elegant simplicity and efficiency—compositional document vectors avoid per-document parameters and enable instant inference.  
- Key reason: strong theoretical grounding (Section 3.1) with empirical validation of the regularizer’s effect.  
- Model is a clever evolution of CBoW, retaining local+global context while sidestepping Paragraph Vectors’ scalability issues.  
- t-SNE visualizations and downstream tasks (IMDB, Wikipedia) provide qualitative and quantitative support for representation quality.  
- Timing experiments show dramatic inference speedup (e.g., 7s vs ~4.5min on IMDB).  
- Authors acknowledge limits: slight underperformance vs LSTMs on short-sentence SICK dataset and hypothesis about strengths for long vs short texts.  
- Suggest clarifying the statement about frequent-word subsampling and whether reported results use it or not.  
- Suggest adding an ablation/plot showing sensitivity to corruption rate q (e.g., 0.5, 0.7, 0.9).  
- Suggest preliminary analysis of performance by document length (e.g., bin IMDB reviews) to test the long-vs-short hypothesis.  
- Request to fix the truncated title in the submitted text.  
- Question: why normalize the global context term by original document length T rather than by number of non-zero elements in the corrupted vector x̃?  
- Question: clarify intuition for why enforcing compositional document representations improves word-level analogy performance.  
- Question: explain how negative sampling was used with a Kullback-Leibler divergence reconstruction error in the Denoising Autoencoder baseline.
