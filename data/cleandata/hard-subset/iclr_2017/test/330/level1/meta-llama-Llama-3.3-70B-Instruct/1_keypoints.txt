- Presents Doc2VecC: represents documents as average word embeddings with a corruption model that emphasizes informative/rare words.
- Claims improved testing efficiency and more expressive document representations compared to several state-of-the-art methods.
- Decision: accept, based on strong motivation grounded in literature and convincing empirical evidence.
- Empirical evaluation shows superiority on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness in both accuracy and efficiency.
- Paper is clearly written, with detailed model description, corruption mechanism, and thorough result analysis.
- Experimental setup and evaluation are comprehensive and well-designed.
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis of the corruption mechanism’s effect on specific word embeddings and model performance.
- Suggestion: include more comparisons with alternatives such as RNN- and CNN-based document models.
- Questions for authors: how corruption rate impacts performance and optimal rates per task.
- Questions for authors: clarify the data-dependent regularization effects from corruption on embeddings.
- Questions for authors: explain handling of out-of-vocabulary words and implications for low-data scenarios.
