- Proposes Doc2VecC: learn document vectors by averaging word embeddings with random-word corruption during training.
- Corruption removes words stochastically, acting as data-dependent regularization that favors informative/rare words and suppresses common ones.
- Claims match or exceed state-of-the-art on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness while being much more efficient than Paragraph Vectors.
- Recommendation: Accept — method is novel, technically sound, and empirically strong.
- Well-motivated positioning relative to Word2Vec and Paragraph Vectors, addressing complexity and costly inference for unseen documents.
- Provides theoretical justification: Taylor expansion shows corruption induces a specific regularization on word embeddings.
- Thorough empirical evaluation across IMDB, Wikipedia classification, and SemEval tasks with consistent improvements or parity with baselines.
- Ablation (Table 3) demonstrates corruption’s effect in suppressing embeddings of common non-discriminative words.
- Reports strong efficiency: training on billions of words per hour on one machine and much faster test-time encoding than Paragraph Vectors.
- Request for sensitivity analysis of the corruption rate q (used q=0.9) to show how performance varies.
- Suggests a fairer Skip-thought comparison by training Skip-thought on the same dataset rather than using a pre-trained model.
- Ask to clarify relation to Huang et al. (2012) that used average word embeddings, and to distinguish the corruption-based regularization contribution.
- Recommend more explanation of why corruption is especially effective for document-level (vs. word-level) representation.
- Question: How does suppression of common words affect semantic meaning—provide examples where it yields meaningful improvements?
- Question: How does training time scale with corpus size, and does Doc2VecC maintain its efficiency advantage as corpus grows?
- Question: Which specific task or dataset shows the most significant improvement—highlight the most compelling results.
