- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as averages of word embeddings with a corruption mechanism to favor informative words and improve scalability and test-time efficiency.
- Claims Doc2VecC yields better word embeddings than Word2Vec and matches/outperforms state-of-the-art on several tasks while being extremely fast to train.
- Recommendation: reject.
- Main rejection reasons: the averaging idea is not novel (Huang et al. 2012) and efficiency claims are not convincingly demonstrated against proper baselines.
- Novelty concern: paper does not clearly differentiate Doc2VecC from prior averaging-based document representations.
- Efficiency concern: claim of training “billions of words per hour” is also true for Word2Vec; no clear head-to-head training-time comparison is provided.
- Theoretical justification for the corruption mechanism is overly complex and lacks clear intuition for why it helps.
- Empirical results show only marginal improvements and fail to isolate the effect of the corruption mechanism versus simple averaging.
- Comparison with Word2Vec+IDF and other baselines is insufficient to attribute gains specifically to corruption.
- Requested improvements: explicitly demonstrate how corruption adds value beyond prior averaging methods (Huang et al. 2012).
- Requested improvements: provide detailed, same-hardware training-time comparisons between Doc2VecC and Word2Vec for the same tasks and data.
- Requested improvements: simplify and clarify the theoretical explanation and provide intuitive rationale for the corruption mechanism.
- Requested improvements: add controlled experiments comparing Doc2VecC to plain averaging (no corruption) to isolate the corruption effect.
- Requested improvements: clarify the relationship between Doc2VecC and Denoising Autoencoders and explain differences/advantages.
- Questions for authors: how exactly does Doc2VecC differ from Huang et al. (2012) and what specific improvements does corruption bring?
- Questions for authors: how does the claimed reduction to Denoising Autoencoders relate given DEA’s reconstruction objective, and what benefits does Doc2VecC’s corruption provide over DEA?
- Questions for authors: provide detailed training-time comparisons vs Word2Vec (same corpus and hardware).
- Questions for authors: given similar performance to Paragraph Vectors, what practical advantages (beyond claimed efficiency) make Doc2VecC preferable?
