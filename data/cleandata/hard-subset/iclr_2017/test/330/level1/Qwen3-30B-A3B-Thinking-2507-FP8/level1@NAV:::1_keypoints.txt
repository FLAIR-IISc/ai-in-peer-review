- Introduces Doc2VecC: represent documents as averages of word embeddings with a corruption model providing data-dependent regularization.
- Claims Doc2VecC captures semantic meaning more effectively than existing methods while being significantly more efficient.
- Shows Doc2VecC outperforms or matches state-of-the-art on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness tasks.
- Demonstrates substantially faster training and inference compared to Paragraph Vectors.
- Reviewer recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
- Key acceptance reasons: approach is well-motivated and properly situated within the literature, addressing clear limitations of prior document representation methods.
- Provides strong empirical evidence and a theoretical analysis interpreting the corruption mechanism as regularization.
- Presents comprehensive experiments across multiple tasks with convincing efficiency comparisons.
- Strengths include clear theoretical justification, well-designed experiments with appropriate baselines, and compelling efficiency metrics.
- Ablation study (Table 3) shows the corruption model suppresses embeddings of common, non-discriminative words, a novel contribution.
