- Proposes Doc2VecC: document vectors computed as averaged word embeddings with a corruption-based regularizer, claimed to be efficient and effective.  
- Recommendation: Reject.  
- Comparison to Paragraph Vectors is incomplete—only PV-DM evaluated; PV-DBOW (more efficient/often better) was omitted.  
- Sentiment analysis used the test set during representation learning, causing data leakage and inflated results.  
- Theoretical analysis (Taylor expansion) is overly complex and provides little insight into why corruption is superior to standard techniques.  
- Claim that averaging with corruption "naturally" captures document semantics is not convincingly demonstrated; representation quality vs. PV/others is not shown.  
- Need ablation studies to separate effects of corruption from simple averaging of embeddings.  
- Regularization effect of corruption is weakly evidenced and not clearly distinguished from subsampling frequent words or dropout.  
- Experimental protocol should use standard train/validation/test splits for fair evaluation.  
- Authors should include PV-DBOW in comparisons to provide a fair baseline.  
- Clarify how corruption differs from and improves upon standard techniques like subsampling and dropout.  
- Questions for authors: why omit PV-DBOW; evidence for semantic meaningfulness beyond task accuracy; how corruption improves performance; tuning/impact of corruption rate q; justification for claiming stronger regularization than existing subsampling.
