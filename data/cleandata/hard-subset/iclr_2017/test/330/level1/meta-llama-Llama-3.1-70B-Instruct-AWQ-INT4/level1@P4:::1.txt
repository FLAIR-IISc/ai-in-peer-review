**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes an efficient document representation learning framework called Document Vector through Corruption (Doc2VecC). This framework represents each document as a simple average of word embeddings, ensuring that the representation captures the semantic meanings of the document during learning. A corruption model is introduced, which randomly removes words from a document during learning, providing a data-dependent regularization that favors informative or rare words while forcing the embeddings of common and non-discriminative ones to be close to zero. The authors claim that Doc2VecC produces significantly better word embeddings than Word2Vec and outperforms several state-of-the-art document representation learning algorithms in sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness tasks.
