- Proposes Doc2VecC: document representations as averages of word embeddings learned with a corruption model that drops words during training.
- Corruption model provides data-dependent regularization, favoring informative/rare words and shrinking common/non-discriminative word embeddings.
- Authors claim Doc2VecC yields better word embeddings than Word2Vec and outperforms several state-of-the-art document representation methods across tasks.
- Decision: Accept.
- Reason 1: Novel, efficient framework that outperforms multiple state-of-the-art algorithms on several tasks.
- Reason 2: Thorough analysis including theoretical foundations, experiments, and comparisons.
- Technical soundness: Clear explanation, comprehensive experiments, and reproducible dataset/experimental details.
- Suggestion: Provide time and space complexity analysis for the framework and corruption model.
- Suggestion: Add experiments testing robustness to hyperparameter settings and noise levels.
- Suggestion: Improve interpretability with visualizations or qualitative analyses of embeddings and document representations.
- Questions: Clarify initialization of word embeddings and document representations, selection of corruption rate q, and trade-offs in computational complexity and memory versus other methods.
