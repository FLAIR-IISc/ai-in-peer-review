- Proposal: Doc2VecC represents documents as averages of word embeddings trained with a corruption model that randomly removes words to emphasize informative terms.
- Result claim: Doc2VecC outperforms several state-of-the-art document representation methods on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.
- Decision: Reviewer recommends Accept.
- Reason 1: Tackles a well-defined and important problem in document representation learning.
- Reason 2: Method is well-motivated, building on Word2Vec and Paragraph Vectors with a novel corruption model.
- Reason 3: Thorough experiments and evaluations demonstrate effectiveness across multiple tasks.
- Support 1: Corruption model acts as effective regularization, favoring rare/informative words and downweighting common words.
- Support 2: Empirical results show strong performance on several benchmark tasks.
- Support 3: Analysis includes visualizations of document and word representations to elucidate strengths and weaknesses.
- Feedback 1: Needs more detailed analysis of how the corruption model impacts word embeddings.
- Feedback 2: Should provide more insight into hyperparameter choices, e.g., corruption rate and embedding dimensionality.
- Feedback 3: Could include more related work, especially recent transformer-based document representation advances.
- Question 1: How was the corruption rate chosen and what is its effect on embeddings?
- Question 2: How does embedding dimensionality influence Doc2VecC performance?
- Question 3: What are the computational resource requirements and how do they compare to other state-of-the-art methods?
