**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel document representation learning framework, called Document Vector through Corruption (Doc2VecC). This framework represents each document as a simple average of word embeddings, ensuring that the representation captures the semantic meanings of the document during learning. A corruption model is introduced, which randomly removes words from a document during learning, favoring informative or rare words while dampening the embeddings of common and non-discriminative words. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of Doc2VecC on various tasks, including sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness, outperforming several state-of-the-art document representation learning algorithms.

Based on the review, I decide to **Accept** this paper.

**Reasons for Acceptance**

1. The paper tackles a specific and well-defined problem in document representation learning, which is a crucial task in natural language processing.
2. The approach is well-motivated, building upon the success of Word2Vec and Paragraph Vectors, and introducing a novel corruption model to improve the representation learning process.
3. The paper provides thorough experiments and evaluations, demonstrating the effectiveness of Doc2VecC on various tasks and outperforming several state-of-the-art algorithms.

**Supporting Arguments**

* The corruption model introduced in Doc2VecC is a novel and effective way to regularize the word embeddings, favoring informative or rare words while dampening the embeddings of common and non-discriminative words.
* The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of Doc2VecC on various tasks, including sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.
* The paper provides a thorough analysis of the results, including visualization of document representations and word embeddings, which helps to understand the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed approach.
