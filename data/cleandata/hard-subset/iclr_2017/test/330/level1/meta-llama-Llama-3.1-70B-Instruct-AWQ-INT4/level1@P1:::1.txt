The paper proposes an efficient document representation learning framework, Document Vector through Corruption (Doc2VecC), which represents each document as a simple average of word embeddings. The authors introduce a corruption model that randomly removes words from a document during learning, which acts as a data-dependent regularization that favors informative or rare words while suppressing words that are common but not discriminative. The paper claims that Doc2VecC produces significantly better word embeddings than Word2Vec and outperforms several state-of-the-art document representation learning algorithms on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness tasks.
