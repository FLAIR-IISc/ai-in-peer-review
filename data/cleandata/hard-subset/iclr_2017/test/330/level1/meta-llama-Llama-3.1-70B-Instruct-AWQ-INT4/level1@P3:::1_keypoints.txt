- Proposes Doc2VecC: represent documents as averages of word embeddings with a corruption model for learned regularization.
- Corruption model is data-dependent, favoring informative/rare words and suppressing common/non-discriminative words.
- Empirical results show Doc2VecC outperforms state-of-the-art on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.
- Decision: Accept.
- Reason 1: Well-motivated approach that addresses limitations of existing document representation methods.
- Reason 2: Strong empirical performance with thorough presentation and analysis.
- Supporting: Comprehensive related-work review and clear motivation/explanation of the corruption model.
- Supporting: Thorough comparisons to prior methods with explanations for observed improvements.
- Suggestion: Provide more implementation details on the corruption model and its impact on performance.
- Suggestion: Compare the corruption-based regularization to other techniques (dropout, L1/L2).
- Suggestion: Include deeper analysis of the learned word embeddings and their semantic properties.
- Questions for authors: clarify corruption implementation and effects, compare to other regularizers, and analyze semantic meaning of word embeddings.
