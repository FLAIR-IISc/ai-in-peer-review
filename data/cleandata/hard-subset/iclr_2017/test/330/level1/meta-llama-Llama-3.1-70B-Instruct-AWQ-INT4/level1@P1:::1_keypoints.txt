- Proposes Doc2VecC: document represented as average of word embeddings with a corruption model that randomly removes words during training.  
- Corruption model acts as data-dependent regularization, favoring informative/rare words and suppressing common non-discriminative words.  
- Claims Doc2VecC yields better word embeddings than Word2Vec and outperforms state-of-the-art document representation methods.  
- Accept recommendation based on strong motivation and clear positioning within related work.  
- Accept recommendation also based on extensive experimental validation across multiple tasks and datasets.  
- Authors clearly explain limitations of existing methods (e.g., Word2Vec, Paragraph Vectors) and motivate their solution.  
- Experimental results show consistent improvements on sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness.  
- Detailed analyses include effects of embedding dimension and training data size.  
- Suggestion: provide a more detailed explanation of the corruption model and its impact on learning.  
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis of experimental results, limitations, and future work directions.  
- Questions for authors: clarify corruption model mechanics and trade-offs; detail experimental setup and chosen evaluation metrics; justify selection and limitations of comparison algorithms.
