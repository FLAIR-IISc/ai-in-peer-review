**Summary of Contributions**

The authors propose a novel document representation learning framework, Document Vector through Corruption (Doc2VecC), which represents each document as a simple average of word embeddings. The framework incorporates a corruption model that introduces data-dependent regularization, favoring informative or rare words while suppressing common and non-discriminative words. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of Doc2VecC on several tasks, including sentiment analysis, document classification, and semantic relatedness, outperforming state-of-the-art document representation learning algorithms.
