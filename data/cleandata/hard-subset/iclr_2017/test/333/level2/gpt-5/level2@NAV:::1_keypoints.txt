- Claim: Depth and supervised pretraining are unnecessary — a single convolutional layer with ReLU (even random filters) can match Gram statistics and rival VGG-based texture synthesis.
- Multi-scale single-layer models improve perceptual quality over single-scale variants.
- Performed ablations over filter sets (Fourier, k-means/PCA, random), filter sizes, and nonlinearities.
- Proposes using VGG Gram-loss as a quantitative surrogate for human judgment; provides MaxEnt perspective and finite-size considerations.
- Decision: Accept.
- Reason: Presents a strong, surprising minimal baseline with compelling empirical evidence that advances understanding of texture synthesis essentials.
- Reason: Careful ablations isolate roles of scale, number of channels, and nonlinearities, offering actionable insights.
- Literature positioning is appropriate, contrasting prior deep pretrained-feature approaches (Gatys, Portilla–Simoncelli, GAN/PixelCNN).
- Experimental support is substantial: many single-layer comparisons, qualitative results on ~20 textures, and convincing ablations showing multi-scale and ReLU necessity.
- VGG-based loss is pragmatic and reproducible but carries a risk of circularity with perceptual evaluation.
- Shortcoming: Lack of human psychophysics; a small-scale human study would strengthen claims.
- Suggested improvement: Add a small-scale human study (e.g., 2AFC discrimination or inpainting) to validate the VGG-loss proxy and measure variability.
- Suggested improvement: Quantify diversity across multiple seeds; report runtime and memory and compare to VGG-based synthesis and hierarchical speedups.
- Suggested improvement: Provide statistical summaries across the texture set (means/variances of VGG-loss) and test sensitivity to loss normalization and gradient scaling.
- Suggested improvement: Release code and a fixed evaluation suite for reproducibility.
- Question: How sensitive are results to the number of feature maps and per-scale weighting in the multi-scale model?
- Question: Does the main conclusion hold under alternative perceptual metrics (different pretrained backbones or pooling choices)?
- Question: What is the seed-to-seed variability in quality/diversity, and are there notable failure cases?
- Question: Can circularity in the VGG-loss be mitigated (e.g., cross-model evaluation)?
- Question: What are the exact compute costs per texture, and how do hierarchical approximations change the quality vs. speed trade-off?
