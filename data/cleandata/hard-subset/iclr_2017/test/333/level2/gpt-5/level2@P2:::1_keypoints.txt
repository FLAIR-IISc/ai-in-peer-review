- Main finding: Single-layer CNNs with random filters + ReLU + Gram-matrix constraints can synthesize high-quality natural textures, often competitive with VGG-based multi-layer methods.  
- Core baseline: A minimal model (single conv layer, random filters, ReLU, L-BFGS optimization) is presented as a strong reference.  
- Ablations: Thorough single-layer experiments over filter types (random, Fourier, k-means, PCA), whitening, sampling, and channel counts (e.g., 363 vs 3267) are performed.  
- Multi-scale benefit: A random multi-scale model (up to 55×55 filters) captures small-, mid-, and long-range structure better than single-scale alternatives.  
- Nonlinearity: ReLU is shown to be critical; linear models fail to match texture quality.  
- Quantitative eval: Uses VGG-based loss as a proxy for perceptual similarity and provides qualitative comparisons on ~20 textures.  
- Theoretical framing: Discusses MaxEnt modeling for finite-size textures and trade-offs between perceptual similarity and variability.  
- Decision: Weak accept — novelty and relevance of showing depth/pretraining are not indispensable, careful ablations, and a useful minimal baseline.  
- Main weakness: Evaluation relies heavily on VGG loss without a human study; claims like “rivals SOTA” are texture-dependent.  
- Suggested evaluation improvements: Run a user study (AB or 2AFC), report diversity across random seeds and multiple syntheses, and expand the texture dataset (e.g., DTD).  
- Baseline comparisons: Add or discuss scattering-transform and Portilla–Simoncelli baselines to contextualize random vs designed shallow features.  
- Optimization/control experiments: Try initializing VGG synthesis from random-model outputs to disentangle representation vs optimization effects.  
- Multi-scale clarity: Specify filter sizes, maps per scale, normalization, concatenation, parameter counts, FLOPs, and runtime/memory comparisons vs VGG synthesis.  
- Further ablations: Test other nonlinearities (leaky ReLU, ELU), modest pooling or dilations, and performance vs number of feature maps to show saturation and compute trade-offs.  
- Method details: Clarify loss scaling, L-BFGS termination, preprocessing (mean removal/color handling), and provide code/filters for reproducibility.  
- MaxEnt follow-up: Formalize the ε-environment perspective and demonstrate schemes (e.g., noise-injected objectives) to improve diversity.  
- Open questions for authors: sensitivity to random seed, exact multi-scale configuration and allocation, runtime/memory scaling, how often random outputs have lower VGG loss than VGG-optimized outputs and whether initializing VGG from those outputs helps, failure modes on non-stationary/perspective textures, and comparisons to scattering-based synthesis.  
- Overall judgment: Compelling, minimalist empirical contribution that would be stronger with improved perceptual evaluation, broader datasets, and more complete methodological/runtime reporting.
