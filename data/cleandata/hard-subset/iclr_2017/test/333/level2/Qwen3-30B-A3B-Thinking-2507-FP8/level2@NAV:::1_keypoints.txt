- Shows natural texture synthesis can be achieved with a single convolutional layer with random filters, no pooling, and no pre-training.  
- Demonstrates such minimal models produce textures rivaling state-of-the-art methods, challenging the need for deep hierarchical representations.  
- Main contribution: establishes a strong minimal baseline that reveals fundamental requirements for texture synthesis.  
- Provides thorough empirical evaluation across multiple filter types (Fourier, K-means, PCA, random) and architectures (single-scale vs. multi-scale).  
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Experimental support is robust, including comprehensive comparisons to SOTA and a VGG-based quantitative evaluation metric.  
- Finds random filters perform as well or better than trained filters, and multi-scale models excel at capturing long-range structure.  
- Suggests adding a quantified discussion of computational efficiency trade-offs between single-layer and multi-layer approaches.  
- Recommends explicitly stating scenarios where the proposed approach underperforms compared to VGG-based methods.  
- Requests clarification on how the optimization process differs from VGG-based approaches to explain reduced susceptibility to local minima.  
- Asks for more insight into why random filters outperform trained filters, which contradicts common assumptions.
