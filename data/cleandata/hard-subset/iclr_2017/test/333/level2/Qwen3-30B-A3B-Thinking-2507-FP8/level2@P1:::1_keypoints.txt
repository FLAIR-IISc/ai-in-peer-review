- Paper claims high-quality texture synthesis is possible with a single-layer conv net with random filters, rivaling pre-trained hierarchical models.  
- Key contributions: systematic comparison of filter types (Fourier, K-means, random), VGG-based quantitative evaluation, and analysis of filter scale and non-linearities.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main concern: Insufficient validation that random filters outperform pre-trained ones due to missing critical baselines.  
- Missing baseline comparison: No evaluation against pre-DNN methods like Portilla & Simoncelli (2000), weakening novelty and progress claims.  
- VGG loss limitations: The proposed quantitative metric is acknowledged to fail on regular textures (e.g., grids) but failure frequency and impact are unreported.  
- Lack of human evaluation: No user studies or correlation tests to confirm VGG loss rankings match perceptual quality.  
- Optimization tuning ambiguity: Claims about refined optimization lack detailed hyperparameters, leaving unclear whether gains come from filters or tuning.  
- Reproducibility issue: Insufficient detail on optimization settings (e.g., L-BFGS-B, learning rates, scaling) compared to Gatys et al. (2015a).  
- Variability argument under-supported: Discussion of perceptual similarity vs. sample variability lacks empirical metrics (e.g., diversity/entropy measurements).  
- Suggested improvement: Include non-DNN baselines (Portilla & Simoncelli) to establish genuine advancement.  
- Suggested improvement: Quantify VGG loss reliability with statistics and human evaluations, and report failure cases.  
- Suggested improvement: Provide a clear table of hyperparameters and optimization differences versus prior work for reproducibility.  
- Questions for authors: How does perceptual quality compare to Portilla & Simoncelli on standard datasets?  
- Questions for authors: For textures where VGG fails, what do human evaluations indicate about synthesized samples?  
- Questions for authors: Can you provide comparative hyperparameter tables (filter sizes, optimization steps) to clarify the "refined tuning" claim?
