- Single-layer CNN with random filters can generate high-quality textures, challenging the need for deep supervised features.
- Contributions: (1) minimal parametric texture synthesis baseline using a single-layer random-filter network.
- Contributions: (2) systematic comparison of single-layer architectures with varying filter sizes and types.
- Contributions: (3) quantitative texture quality measure based on VGG synthesis loss.
- Recommendation: accept.
- Key reasons: well-motivated question, well-placed in literature, and thorough empirical analysis.
- Quantitative evaluation: uses VGG-based synthesis loss as a metric for texture quality.
- Novelty: presents a strong, surprising baseline that questions conventional wisdom in texture synthesis.
- Improvement: provide more details on the optimization procedure used to tune the single-layer network.
- Improvement: discuss broader implications for other computer vision tasks (e.g., recognition, segmentation).
- Improvement: include more visualizations of synthesized textures to illustrate results.
