**Summary of the paper's claims and contributions**

This paper investigates the essential elements required for natural texture synthesis using deep neural networks. The authors challenge the conventional wisdom that deep hierarchical representations and supervised training of feature spaces are crucial for natural image generation. They demonstrate that a single-layer convolutional neural network with random filters can generate high-quality textures that rival state-of-the-art approaches. The paper's main contributions include: (1) a strong minimal baseline for parametric texture synthesis using a single-layer network and random filters, (2) a comparison of various single-layer architectures with different filter sizes and types, and (3) a quantitative texture quality measure based on the synthesis loss in the VGG-based model.
