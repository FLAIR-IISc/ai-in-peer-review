- Contribution: A single-layer convolutional network with random filters can synthesize natural textures comparable to state-of-the-art methods, challenging the need for deep supervised feature hierarchies.
- Decision: Recommend accept due to the paper's novelty and convincing empirical support.
- Main rationale: The work boldly and plausibly challenges conventional wisdom and stimulates discussion.
- Experimental evidence: Thorough, well-executed experiments convincingly back the claims.
- Strength — exposition: Clear, concise introduction that motivates the research question.
- Strength — context: Comprehensive related-work review that grounds the contribution.
- Strength — methodology: Well-designed experiments and in-depth analysis that illuminate strengths and limitations.
- Minor issue — implications: Paper needs a more detailed discussion of broader implications for natural image generation.
- Minor issue — presentation: Some figures and tables require improved readability and clarity.
- Minor issue — reproducibility: More details on the optimization procedure and tuning are needed for reproducibility.
- Questions for authors: Plans for extending to object/scene generation, relationship between feature-map count and texture quality, and handling variability in generated textures.
