This paper presents a surprising and thought-provoking contribution to the field of natural image generation, specifically in the context of texture synthesis. The authors challenge the conventional wisdom that deep hierarchical representations and supervised training of feature spaces are essential for generating high-quality textures. Instead, they demonstrate that a single-layer convolutional neural network with random filters can produce textures that rival the state-of-the-art approaches.

I decide to accept this paper, with the main reasons being:

1. The authors' bold and well-motivated challenge to the conventional wisdom in the field, which sparks interesting discussions and debates.
2. The paper's thorough and well-executed experiments, which provide convincing evidence to support the authors' claims.

The paper's strengths include:

* A clear and concise introduction that sets up the problem and motivates the research question.
* A thorough review of the related work, which provides a solid foundation for the authors' contributions.
* Well-designed and executed experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
* An in-depth analysis of the results, which provides insights into the strengths and limitations of the proposed approach.

However, there are some minor issues that the authors could address to further improve the paper:
