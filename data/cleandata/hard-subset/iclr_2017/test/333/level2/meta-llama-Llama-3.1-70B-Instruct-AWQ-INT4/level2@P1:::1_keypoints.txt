- Proposes a novel single-layer CNN with random filters for natural texture synthesis.  
- Demonstrates the minimal model generates high-quality textures comparable to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Challenges the assumption that deep hierarchical representations and supervised training are necessary for texture generation.  
- Paper is well-motivated, well-executed, and clearly positioned in the literature.  
- Experiments show the model captures stationary texture structure effectively.  
- Introduces a quantitative texture quality measure based on VGG synthesis loss for objective evaluation.  
- Achieves a good balance between perceptual similarity to references and variability across samples.  
- Raises important questions about the true role of depth and supervision in image generation.  
- Requests discussion on extending the approach to more complex images with multiple objects or scenes.  
- Asks for more insight into why random filters perform so well and which filter properties matter.  
- Seeks comparisons to other generative methods such as GANs and VAEs.  
- Minor issues: add more figures and visualizations to illustrate results.  
- Minor issues: clarify notation and terminology in texture modeling and synthesis sections.  
- Minor issues: tighten the text to remove repetitive or overly detailed sections.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
