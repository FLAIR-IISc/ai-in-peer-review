- Paper proposes single-layer CNN with random filters for natural texture synthesis.
- Claims single-layer random-filter CNN can produce textures rivaling state-of-the-art deep methods.
- Contribution: shows single-layer random filters suffice to synthesize high-quality textures.
- Contribution: argues depth and pre-training are not indispensable for natural image generation.
- Contribution: introduces a quantitative texture quality metric based on VGG synthesis loss.
- Decision: Accept.
- Reason: method is novel and challenges conventional wisdom about deep hierarchical necessity.
- Reason: extensive, fair experimental comparisons demonstrate effectiveness.
- Supporting: methodology is well-motivated and clearly explained.
- Supporting: experiments are thorough, well-presented, and limitations are discussed.
- Suggestion: provide more details on the optimization procedure for the single-layer CNN.
- Suggestion: explore different filter types and their impact on synthesis quality.
- Suggestion: investigate applications to other image generation tasks (e.g., denoising, super-resolution).
- Question: How were the number of feature maps and filter sizes selected?
- Question: What is the computational efficiency compared to state-of-the-art methods?
- Question: How will you address variability in generated textures?
