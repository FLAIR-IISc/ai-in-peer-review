- Investigates what is necessary for natural texture synthesis, challenging the need for depth and pre-training.  
- Shows high-quality textures can be synthesized with a single-layer network using random filters.  
- Provides a minimal, compelling baseline and contributes a quantitative evaluation metric.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Core argument is well-supported by experiments and analysis, offering a clear challenge to prevailing assumptions.  
- Uses VGG-based loss for quantitative evaluation, providing more objective assessment than human inspection.  
- Strong empirical validation: systematic exploration of single-layer architectures and multi-scale approach rivaling SOTA.  
- Helpful ablation studies comparing filter types (Fourier, K-means, PCA, random) and filter sizes.  
- Thorough visual and quantitative comparison to VGG-based models and reasonable optimization tuning justification.  
- Architecture descriptions (e.g., Fourier-363, Kmeans-3267) enable reproducibility.  
- Theoretical MaxEnt connection is intriguing but underdeveloped and needs further explanation.  
- The 10^7 scaling factor in optimization is unexplained and its sensitivity should be explored.  
- Reliance on VGG-based loss could be broadened by testing other metrics to assess diversity/originality.  
- Claims of “rivaling” SOTA would be strengthened with statistical significance tests, error bars, or p-values.  
- Further analysis needed on VGG-based loss failures for textures with strong regular structures.
