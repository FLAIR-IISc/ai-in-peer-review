- Paper studies whether depth and pre-training are necessary for texture synthesis, showing single-layer CNNs with random filters can produce good textures.
- Authors evaluate various single-layer architectures against a state-of-the-art method and introduce a VGG-based synthesis loss as a quantitative metric.
- Recommendation: Reject.
- Main critique: Limited novelty—simpler models for texture synthesis have been hinted at in prior work.
- Main critique: Improvements over state-of-the-art are modest and claims of “rivaling SOTA” are overstated.
- Main critique: Quantitative results are inconsistent and reliance on VGG-based loss as a proxy for perceptual quality is problematic.
- Main critique: Limited impact—paper shows complexity isn’t absolutely necessary but doesn’t fundamentally advance texture synthesis.
- Main critique: Paper lacks mechanistic insight into why random filters work or which filter properties are crucial.
- Main critique: Experimental setup feels exploratory and ad-hoc; choices of filter sizes and feature counts are insufficiently justified.
- Main critique: Multi-scale model shows promise but performance gains aren’t convincingly explained or causally linked to scale combination.
- Main critique: Discussion of the MaxEnt framework is interesting but tangential to the paper’s core contribution.
- Suggestion: Better contextualize and contrast with prior work on simplified texture synthesis models.
- Suggestion: Strengthen quantitative evaluation by using alternative metrics, user studies, and rigorous statistical comparisons.
- Suggestion: Analyze properties of random filters (e.g., distributions, recurring patterns) to identify what enables good synthesis.
- Suggestion: Narrow and focus experiments on a smaller set of well-motivated architectural choices.
- Suggestion: Provide a detailed analysis explaining why the multi-scale approach improves results (e.g., capture of different frequency components).
- Suggestion: Address and discuss computational cost of large filters and potential optimizations.
- Question: Which specific prior works suggested using random filters and how does this work extend them?
- Question: Why was VGG-based loss chosen, what are its limitations, and were other metrics or user studies considered?
- Question: What are the details of the optimization procedure and measures taken to avoid local minima?
- Question: Is there a correlation between random filter distribution and texture quality; were different distributions tested?
- Question: How does the computational cost of the single-layer multi-scale model compare to SOTA and can it be optimized?
