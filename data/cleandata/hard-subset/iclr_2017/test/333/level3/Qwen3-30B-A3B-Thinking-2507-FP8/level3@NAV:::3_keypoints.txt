- Challenges the assumption that deep, pre-trained networks are necessary for high-quality texture synthesis.  
- Shows shallow, single-layer networks with random filters can produce textures perceptually rivaling VGG-19-based methods.  
- Systematic experiments compare random, hand-crafted, and unsupervisedly learned single-layer architectures.  
- Finds neither depth nor pre-training is required for effective texture modeling.  
- Key result: shallow random networks sometimes outperform VGG-19 on regular textures.  
- Initializing VGG optimization with shallow-network results further improves synthesis outcomes.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution, well-motivated, rigorous empirical validation — recommendation: accept.  
- Evidence: Fig. 4 and supplementary results show shallow networks can achieve lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG optimization.  
- Authors appropriately discuss limitations, notably diversity measurement and the need for psychophysical evaluation.  
- Suggested additions: include psychophysical assessment and discuss applicability to other texture synthesis tasks.  
- Questions for authors: why does VGG loss sometimes favor shallow-generated textures over VGG-generated ones?  
- Question on mechanism: is the multi-scale shallow network’s success due to receptive field size or filter diversity, and how would that generalize?
