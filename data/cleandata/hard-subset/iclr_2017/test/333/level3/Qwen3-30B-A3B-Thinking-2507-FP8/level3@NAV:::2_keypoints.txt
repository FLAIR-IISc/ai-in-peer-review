- Paper challenges prevailing assumption that hierarchical multi-layer representations and supervised training are necessary for natural-looking texture synthesis.  
- Shows high-quality textures can be synthesized from a single convolutional layer with random filters, no pooling, and no training.  
- Result is a significant conceptual advance with implications for understanding texture representation.  
- Recommendation: accept, based on rigorous empirical support and surprising, assumption-challenging findings.  
- Experimental strength: comprehensive comparisons across Fourier, K-means, PCA, and random filters, multiple filter counts and sizes.  
- Multi-scale random-filter model consistently matches state-of-the-art, highlighting the importance of filter scale combination over depth or pre-training.  
- Evaluation weakness: over-reliance on VGG-based loss as a proxy for perceptual quality, which fails for highly regular textures.  
- Suggested improvements: add quantitative metrics (e.g., LPIPS) and broader human evaluations across diverse textures.  
- Clarification requested: explain why random filters outperform trained filters here and reconcile with cases where random weights fail in image generation.
