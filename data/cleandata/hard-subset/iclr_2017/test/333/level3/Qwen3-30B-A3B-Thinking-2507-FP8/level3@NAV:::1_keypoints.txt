- Single-layer CNN with random filters can synthesize high-quality textures, challenging the need for deep/pretrained features.  
- Multi-scale random filter approach often rivals VGG-based models while being computationally simpler.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; work is conceptually important and empirically rigorous.  
- Key strength: shows simple, random filters can match complex pre-trained networks and includes thorough evaluation across filter types and scales.  
- Main limitation: insufficient validation of the claim that hierarchical representations are unnecessary; comparisons limited to specific architectures.  
- Additional concern: both VGG and single-layer models produce imperfect samples and perform worse than non-parametric methods; this needs more discussion.  
- Clarification request 1: Does using multiple filter sizes constitute a form of hierarchy different from traditional deep networks?  
- Clarification request 2: Why does the VGG-based model perform better on inpainting (Fig. 7), apparently contradicting the main claim?  
- Clarification request 3: Could a hierarchical model with fewer parameters than the multi-scale model achieve better results while remaining computationally efficient?
