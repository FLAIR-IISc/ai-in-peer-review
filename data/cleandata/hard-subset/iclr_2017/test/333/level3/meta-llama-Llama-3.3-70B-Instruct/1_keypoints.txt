- Proposes an actor-critic RL approach to automatically learn per-step SGD learning rates using a critic-predicted long-term reward.

- Algorithm is simple and effective, predicting learning rates each timestep with comparable convergence speed to expert optimizers and better test accuracy on MNIST and CIFAR-10.

- Decision: Accept — reasons: well-motivated and grounded in literature; experiments demonstrate effectiveness and some prevention of overfitting.

- Experimental evaluation is thorough and compares favorably to adaptive methods like vSGD, showing the proposed method’s superiority.

- Paper provides a clear explanation of the algorithm and its motivation in sequential decision-making contexts.

- Suggestion: visualize learned learning rates over time and analyze effects of different hyperparameters.

- Suggestion: test the approach on other SGD variants and other machine learning algorithms.

- Question: provide more details on the design of the state function χ(·) and its effect on performance.

- Question: how are extreme actor outputs (too large/small learning rates) handled to prevent divergence?

- Question: clarify the effect of the discount factor γ on performance and how γ was chosen in experiments.
