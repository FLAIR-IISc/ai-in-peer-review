- Proposes an RL-based method (actor-critic) to automatically learn per-step learning rates for SGD.  
- Designs a policy network (actor) to choose learning rates and a value network (critic) to evaluate those choices.  
- Experiments show good SGD convergence, some mitigation of overfitting, and performance better than human-designed baselines.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Well-motivated, interesting analysis with surprising and thorough results (mention of natural-looking texture analysis).  
- Key reason 2: Method is well-situated in literature and constitutes a significant contribution by leveraging RL to learn learning rates.  
- Supporting argument: Clear, well-structured approach with thorough related-work comparison.  
- Supporting argument: Empirical results show similar convergence speed to baselines and slightly better test accuracy.  
- Suggested improvement: Provide more details on hyperparameter tuning and computational resources for training actor/critic.  
- Suggested improvement: Include more experiments on additional datasets and tasks to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Questions for authors: Clarify the state function χ(·), selection of discount factor γ and actor/critic learning rates, and applicability to SGD variants (momentum, RMSprop).
