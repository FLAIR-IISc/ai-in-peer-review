- Proposes an actor-critic RL algorithm to automatically learn SGD learning rates using a policy (actor) and value (critic) network.  
- Claims the method improves convergence and reduces overfitting, yielding better test performance than human-designed schedules.  
- Decision: accept — experiments on MNIST and CIFAR-10 show comparable convergence speed and improved test accuracy versus expert-designed optimizers.  
- The approach is well-motivated, framing learning-rate control as a reinforcement learning problem.  
- Experiments provide clear comparisons to existing adaptive learning-rate methods and demonstrate superior performance.  
- Suggest adding detailed analysis of the learned learning-rate schedules and direct comparisons to human-designed schedules.  
- Recommend more experiments across additional datasets and tasks to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Suggest discussing potential real-world applications of the proposed algorithm.  
- Request clarification on the design and captured information of the state function χ(·).  
- Ask for experiments on the effect of feeding different examples to actor and critic, and plans to extend to other SGD variants and per-parameter learning rates.
