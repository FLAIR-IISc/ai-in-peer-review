- Main claim: Single-layer CNNs with random filters and Gram-matrix matching can synthesize natural textures at perceptual quality comparable to VGG-based methods.  
- Contribution: Introduces a minimal strong baseline (single-layer random filters) and a multi-scale extension combining different receptive field sizes.  
- Experimental scope: Systematic comparison of random, Fourier, k-means, and PCA filters at two capacities (363 vs 3267 maps), plus ablations on scale and nonlinearity.  
- Evaluation: Proposes using VGG Gram-matrix loss as a quantitative proxy for perceptual quality and reports results across 20 textures.  
- Theoretical discussion: Connects texture synthesis to maximum-entropy modeling and analyzes the trade-off between perceptual similarity and variability.  
- Key empirical findings: (a) Single-layer random filters produce convincing textures; (b) multi-scale filters improve long-range structure; (c) ReLU is essential; (d) more channels improve quality; (e) random filters often match/outperform unsupervised/hand-crafted banks; (f) VGG loss generally aligns with human perception for many textures.  
- Practical observation: Initializing VGG-based synthesis with the single-layer result can improve final outputs.  
- Decision: Accept — the paper provides a surprising, well-supported, and useful minimal baseline.  
- Strength: Conceptual novelty — challenges assumptions about depth, pooling, and supervised pretraining being necessary.  
- Strength: Thorough empirical analysis with careful ablations and consistent optimization/evaluation.  
- Limitation: Evaluation relies heavily on VGG-based loss and lacks psychophysical validation, risking mis-ranking for some textures.  
- Limitation: Fairness of comparison to VGG baselines needs clarification (pooling, iterations, initialization, tuning parity).  
- Limitation: Limited statistical analysis across textures and absence of formal significance testing.  
- Limitation: Computational inefficiency of very large single-layer kernels compared to deep small-kernel networks.  
- Limitation: Scope restricted to texture synthesis; implications for style transfer and other tasks are not empirically tested.  
- Suggested improvements: Provide aggregate quantitative summaries (mean/median losses, variability, significance tests) across textures.  
- Suggested improvements: Report runtime, memory, and iteration counts for all models to contextualize trade-offs.  
- Suggested improvements: Expand capacity ablation (quality vs number of feature maps) to show diminishing returns.  
- Suggested improvements: Clarify multi-scale architecture details and sensitivity to design choices.  
- Suggested improvements: Ensure VGG baseline parity in pooling, iterations, optimizer, and initialization, and report impact of initializing from the random model.  
- Suggested improvements: Add a small user study or psychophysical proxy (e.g., inpainting discrimination) for a subset of textures.  
- Suggested improvements: Discuss failure cases and texture families where the method underperforms.  
- Suggested improvements: Release code and configurations to establish the baseline for the community.  
- Questions for authors: Specify exact multi-scale filter sizes and channel allocations and sensitivity to receptive field.  
- Questions for authors: Report performance variability across numbers of feature maps and random seeds (mean/variance).  
- Questions for authors: How sensitive are results to loss/gradient scaling, iteration budget, and optimizer choice (L-BFGS-B vs Adam)?  
- Questions for authors: Did VGG baselines match pooling type, iteration count, and initialization; provide side-by-side numbers with identical settings.  
- Questions for authors: Beyond ReLU, were leaky-ReLU or tanh tested, and is improvement due to rectification sparsity or general nonlinearity?  
- Questions for authors: Why do random filters outperform Fourier and k-means at equal capacity — any diagnostics on activations or Gram spectra?  
- Questions for authors: Can you quantify sample variability (inter-sample VGG loss, pixel diversity) and relate to stopping criteria?  
- Questions for authors: Provide details and results for the hierarchical small-kernel implementation regarding speed and quality.  
- Questions for authors: Do conclusions generalize to style transfer with content constraints or to non-stationary textures?
