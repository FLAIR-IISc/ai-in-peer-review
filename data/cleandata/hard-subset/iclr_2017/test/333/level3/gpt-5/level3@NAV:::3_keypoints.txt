- Paper asks which ingredients (depth, pooling, supervised pretraining) are necessary for Gram-matrix-based texture synthesis.
- Main contribution: careful empirical study showing very shallow architectures, including single-layer random multi-scale filter banks, can synthesize high-quality textures and sometimes rival/outperform VGG-19 on simple/regular textures.
- Optimization insight: optimizing shallow filter banks can yield lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG optimization; initializing VGG optimization with shallow-model outputs improves VGG results.
- Empirical finding: multi-scale filters and many channels help; random filters are surprisingly strong.
- Decision: Accept — surprising, well-motivated, and impactful result that challenges assumptions about depth and pretraining; optimization analysis is especially informative.
- Strengths: solid grounding in prior work, thorough ablations across filter types/sizes/scales, and extensive qualitative evidence.
- Limitation: lacks systematic, quantitative multi-metric comparisons and rigorous assessment of sample diversity; claims about modeling underlying texture distribution are tentative.
- Suggested improvement: add diversity/coverage evaluation (multi-sample LPIPS/MMD in Gram space, patch-level novelty, variability across random seeds).
- Suggested improvement: run a small psychophysical study (2AFC discrimination or inpainting) to validate perceived quality and diversity.
- Suggested improvement: report dataset-level statistics and split results by texture categories (regular vs stochastic).
- Suggested improvement: control for optimization choices (compare L-BFGS-B vs Adam, ablate gradient/loss scaling, ensure fair iteration budgets).
- Suggested improvement: clarify failure cases and provide quantitative summaries rather than only cherry-picked visuals.
- Questions: how consistent are gains across texture types; quantify performance by regular vs stochastic categories.
- Questions: how sensitive are results to nonlinearity (ReLU vs leaky/ELU) and to feature/Gram normalization?
- Questions: with equal parameter counts, what is the contribution of multi-scale vs single large filters; report VGG-initialization statistics (final loss, convergence speed, success rate) and whether shallow models ever exactly reproduce references (memorization).
