- Proposes a minimal single-layer conv model with ReLU, multi-scale random filters, Gram-matrix statistics, optimized with L-BFGS-B as a strong texture-synthesis baseline.
- Systematically contrasts random filters vs learned/Fourier/PCA and single-scale vs multi-scale, showing multi-scale random filters often match or rival VGG-based models in perceptual quality.
- Claims depth, pooling, and supervised pretraining are not indispensable for texture synthesis; multi-scale coverage and nonlinearity are more critical.
- Introduces a VGG-based loss as a quantitative proxy for perceptual evaluation and frames results with a maximum-entropy (variability vs similarity) perspective.
- Practical finding: ReLU is essential; single-layer optimization is easier and can be used to warm-start VGG optimization.
- Decision: Weak accept because the paper is a simple, well-executed baseline that challenges a dominant narrative and offers broad, actionable ablations.
- Supporting argument: Well-motivated by Gatys et al. and prior literature; empirical results largely support claims but are tempered by evaluation limitations.
- Strength: Conceptual clarity in isolating what matters (multi-scale + nonlinearity) vs what does not (depth/pretraining) for this task.
- Strength: Methodological thoroughness with many filter constructions and controlled ablations (scales, map counts, nonlinearity).
- Strength: Practical insight that multi-scale random features are robust, competitive, and useful for initialization.
- Strength: Thoughtful discussion of variability–fidelity trade-offs and MaxEnt framing.
- Limitation: Primary quantitative metric is VGG-based loss, risking circularity and possibly misestimating perceptual quality; no human studies.
- Limitation: Lacks systematic comparison to modern non-parametric patch-based methods; some outputs are worse than strong non-parametric approaches for complex/man-made textures.
- Limitation: Appendix inpainting results suggest VGG-based models outperform single-layer variants, weakening the universality of the “depth not needed” claim.
- Limitation: Efficiency concerns—multi-scale random model uses very large kernels (up to 55×55), which is computationally inefficient; sparse parameter-matched comparisons to hierarchical models.
- Suggestion: Add a small psychophysical study (2AFC/Turing-test) with effect sizes and inter-subject agreement.
- Suggestion: Quantify variability explicitly (VGG-loss distributions, nearest-neighbor analysis, diversity–fidelity curves, per-texture metrics with CIs).
- Suggestion: Include a modern non-parametric baseline (tuned) and report qualitative and quantitative comparisons.
- Suggestion: Provide a controlled quantitative inpainting benchmark and, if VGG wins, qualify claims about depth accordingly.
- Suggestion: Provide parameter- and compute-matched comparisons between large-kernel single-layer and hierarchical/dilated models; explore dilated convolutions for efficiency.
- Suggestion: Reduce metric circularity by evaluating with alternative feature spaces (scattering, steerable pyramids) and human ratings.
- Suggestion: Report sensitivity to optimization details (gradient scaling, iteration budget, initialization), detail multi-scale architecture, and release code/seeds.
- Question: Provide quantitative inpainting results across textures comparing single-layer multi-scale vs VGG, including user studies or classifier discrimination?
- Question: How sensitive are conclusions to number of feature maps—does a scaling law exist where single-layer random models only catch up beyond a certain capacity?
- Question: Can you include parameter- and compute-matched comparisons between multi-scale large-kernel and hierarchical (possibly dilated) models to test efficiency vs quality?
- Question: How robust are results to random filter distribution and preprocessing (per-channel mean subtraction); any failure cases where structured filters outperform random ones?
- Question: Can you add controlled comparisons to strong non-parametric methods and report where parametric models still fall short?
- Question: How many random seeds were used per texture, and what is the variability (mean ± std) of VGG-loss and human preference across seeds?
