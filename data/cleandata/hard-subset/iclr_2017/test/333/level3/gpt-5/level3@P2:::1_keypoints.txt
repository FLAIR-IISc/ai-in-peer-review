- Proposal: single-layer convolutional model with ReLU, random filters, no pooling, extended to a multi-scale variant, using Gram matrices for texture synthesis.  
- Main claim: depth, pooling, and pretraining are not essential; nonlinearity and multi-scale coverage are the crucial ingredients.  
- Empirical finding: single-layer random-filter models, especially multi-scale, achieve high perceptual quality and often rival VGG-based results.  
- Ablation: comprehensive comparisons across filter types (random, Fourier, k-means/PCA, whitened) and scales support the central claim.  
- Quantitative evaluation: uses a VGG-based loss as a proxy for perceptual distance, supplemented by qualitative visual comparisons.  
- Theoretical connection: paper links Gram-based synthesis to maximum-entropy modeling and discusses the similarity–variability trade-off.  
- Reproducibility and clarity: work is clearly written and easy to reproduce.  
- Decision: accept — justified by a strong, well-executed insight and broad empirical ablation.  
- Strength: convincing qualitative results across many textures; ReLU shown necessary; multi-scale captures long-range structure.  
- Strength: VGG-loss comparisons sometimes show the random multi-scale model outperforming VGG-optimized synthesis, highlighting optimization and variability trade-offs.  
- Strength: Thorough and surprising ablation showing learned filters often unnecessary.  
- Limitation: heavy reliance on VGG-based loss, which is an imperfect and potentially biased perceptual proxy; no human psychophysics.  
- Limitation: computational inefficiency for large filters noted but not rigorously quantified; hierarchical variants mentioned briefly.  
- Limitation: some textures remain imperfect and non-parametric patch-based methods can be better for inpainting/extension.  
- Suggestion: add a human perceptual study (e.g., 2AFC/Turing test) to validate perceptual claims.  
- Suggestion: quantify sample diversity vs similarity (distributions of VGG-loss among samples, cross-sample distances, SSIM).  
- Suggestion: report runtime/FLOPs/memory and details of hierarchical architectures to clarify efficiency–quality trade-offs.  
- Suggestion: include non-parametric patch-based and scattering baselines for contextual comparison.  
- Suggestion: temper absolute claims about indispensability to specify scope (Gram-based parametric texture synthesis).  
- Suggestion: provide systematic ReLU and scale ablations (plots vs filter size and feature count).  
- Suggestion: add controlled inpainting benchmarks to compare approaches.  
- Questions for authors: request human study, diversity metrics, metric robustness checks, exact hierarchical architectures and FLOPs, filter-count-controlled comparisons, failure-case analysis by texture class, inpainting quantification, and code/seeds for reproducibility.
