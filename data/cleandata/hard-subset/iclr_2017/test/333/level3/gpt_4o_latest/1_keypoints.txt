- Proposes a minimal single-layer CNN with random filters as a robust baseline for parametric texture synthesis.
- Challenges the belief that deep hierarchical representations and pretrained features are required for high-quality textures.
- Shows the method often rivals VGG-based models in perceptual quality, especially for regular textures.
- Acknowledges the method is computationally inefficient due to large filter sizes.
- Raises the question whether hierarchical models could match quality more efficiently.
- Notes both the proposed and VGG-based models produce imperfect samples inferior to non-parametric methods.
- Reports VGG-based models outperform the proposed method in inpainting tasks.
- Decision: Reject.
- Main rejection reasons: no significant improvement over existing methods and insufficient evidence to claim hierarchical representations are dispensable.
- Novelty: Thought-provoking simple baseline but lacks consistent superiority and practical applicability due to inefficiency.
- Empirical rigor: Thorough experiments but overreliance on VGG-based perceptual metrics and weak support for broad claims.
- Broader impact: Raises trade-off questions between perceptual similarity and variability but offers limited actionable solutions.
- Suggestion: Add stronger quantitative comparisons (e.g., human studies, SSIM) and test hierarchical alternatives for efficiency.
- Suggestion: Quantify variability of generated textures and balance it with perceptual quality.
- Suggestion: Temper or better substantiate claims about the necessity of hierarchical representations with systematic comparisons.
- Questions for authors: Provide evidence for hierarchical dispensability on complex textures, performance on other tasks (style transfer/inpainting), and ways to reduce computational inefficiency.
