- Paper shows single-layer CNNs with random filters can synthesize textures comparable to VGG-19-based methods.
- Challenges the necessity of depth, pooling, and supervised pre-training for high-quality texture synthesis.
- Demonstrates optimization advantages of shallow networks, especially for regular and simple textures.
- Proposes initializing VGG-19-based synthesis with shallow network outputs to improve results.
- Decision: Accept.
- Novelty: Introduces a minimal, impactful baseline that questions prevailing assumptions in texture synthesis.
- Experiments: Extensive comparisons across single-layer architectures; uses VGG-19 loss as an imperfect quantitative measure.
- Practical implication: Simpler models may offer competitive quality with lower computational cost.
- Limitation: Lacks systematic evaluation metrics beyond VGG-19 loss (e.g., entropy, other quantifiable objectives).
- Visual quality concern: Reliance on VGG-19 loss is insufficient without human evaluations or alternative perceptual metrics.
- Psychophysics: Paper suggests psychophysical validation but provides no preliminary experiments.
- Computational analysis missing: Needs deeper analysis of trade-offs between filter size, computation, and synthesis quality.
- Question: How well does the method handle complex, non-regular textures and what are its limitations?
- Question: What is the variability/diversity of textures produced compared to Gatys et al.?
- Question: Can the authors provide quantitative or visual evidence for improvements from initializing VGG-19 with shallow results?
- Conclusion: Strong, paradigm-challenging contribution with remaining validation and evaluation gaps to address.
