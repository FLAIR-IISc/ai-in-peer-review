- Paper studies components of deep nets for texture synthesis, finding very shallow random-filter nets can match or outperform VGG-19 on simple/regular textures.
- Main contribution challenges the assumed necessity of deep hierarchical representations for natural texture synthesis.
- Decision: Accept.
- Novelty/significance: The core finding is novel and significant.
- Thorough analysis: Compares effectiveness of shallow vs. deep architectures and different filter types.
- Well-structured discussion: Clearly outlines strengths, limitations, and future directions for Gram-matrix-based methods.
- Suggests adding systematic, quantifiable comparisons across methods to strengthen claims.
- Recommends focusing on modeling texture distributions instead of matching single samples.
- Notes entropy is impractical for measuring diversity and suggests exploring alternative measures (e.g., psychophysical evaluation).
- Calls for more quantitative evaluation (e.g., image quality assessment metrics) to complement qualitative results.
- Question: How will the authors address the lack of systematic performance comparisons?
- Question: What methods do the authors propose for modeling texture distributions?
- Question: How do the authors plan to explore alternative diversity measurements such as psychophysical evaluation?
- Question: Are there plans to incorporate quantitative image quality metrics for evaluation?
