- Proposes a simple, strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis using multi-scale random filters.  
- Shows high-quality natural textures can be generated with a single-layer, no pooling, random-filter network.  
- Demonstrates depth and pre-training of multi-layer representations are not essential for natural texture generation.  
- Main contributions: minimal baseline, high-quality synthesized textures, and comparisons of single-layer architectures to Gatys et al.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Tackles a well-defined problem, is well-motivated, and well-placed in the literature.  
- Key reason 2: Empirical results support the claims and experiments are scientifically rigorous.  
- Clever methodological choice: isolates architectural effects by using single-layer random filters in a controlled texture synthesis setting.  
- Evaluation is thorough, including visual inspection, quantitative measures, and comparison to state-of-the-art (Gatys et al.).  
- Suggest adding a more detailed discussion relating the approach to other texture synthesis methods (e.g., non-parametric methods).  
- Recommend more insight into why multi-scale random networks outperform single-scale networks and implications for texture synthesis.  
- Ask for more example textures to better demonstrate the method's capabilities.  
- Questions for authors: how to extend to complex/multi-scale or non-stationary textures; more optimization procedure details; potential practical uses (e.g., CAD, games).  
- Overall: well-written, impressive results, significant contribution; minor revisions suggested.
