- Proposes a simple baseline: a single-layer CNN with random, multi-scale filters for parametric texture synthesis.  
- Shows this baseline can generate high-quality textures that often rival VGG-based, pre-trained deep models.  
- Contribution: a strong minimal baseline for parametric texture synthesis.  
- Contribution: empirical evidence suggesting deep hierarchical representations and learned feature maps may not be necessary for texture synthesis.  
- Contribution: a quantitative texture-quality measure based on VGG-model synthesis loss.  
- Decision: accept.  
- Key reason: novel approach that challenges the need for deep hierarchical/pre-trained features.  
- Key reason: impressive empirical results that sometimes match state-of-the-art VGG models.  
- Supporting: thorough evaluation, clear presentation, and thoughtful discussion of implications including perceptual similarity and variability.
