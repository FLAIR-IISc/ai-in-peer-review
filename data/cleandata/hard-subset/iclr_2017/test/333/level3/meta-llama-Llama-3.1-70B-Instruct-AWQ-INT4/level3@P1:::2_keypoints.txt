- Proposes a generative model for natural textures using a single-layer CNN with completely random, data-independent filters.  
- Demonstrates that this minimal model qualitatively captures perceptual differences between natural textures and often rivals state-of-the-art methods.  
- Shows that deep hierarchical representations and trained feature maps are not necessary for effective texture synthesis.  
- Contributions: presents a strong minimal baseline, shows high-quality synthesis rivaling SOTA, and evaluates many single-layer architectures and filter types/sizes.  
- Provides a thorough analysis of conditions enabling natural-looking texture generation, supported by experimental results.  
- Uses a quantitative texture quality measure based on VGG synthesis loss but acknowledges this metric is imperfect.  
- Notes the challenge of balancing perceptual similarity and variability in evaluation.  
- Recommends acceptance because the results are novel, surprising, and provide valuable analysis that challenges existing assumptions.  
- Suggests the paper should include more details on the optimization algorithm used for training.  
- Requests additional analysis of the role and impact of non-linearities in the model.  
- Recommends adding more evaluation metrics to better assess synthesized texture quality and comparisons to SOTA.  
- Asks authors to clarify: the optimization algorithm details, how non-linearities contribute and can be improved, and how the model compares under more evaluation metrics.
