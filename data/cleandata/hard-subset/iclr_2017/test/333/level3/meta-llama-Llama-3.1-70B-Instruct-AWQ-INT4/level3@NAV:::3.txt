**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

The paper "What Does it Take to Generate Natural Textures?" presents a novel approach to natural texture synthesis using single-layer convolutional neural networks (CNNs) with random filters. The authors demonstrate that these simple models can generate high-quality textures that rival state-of-the-art approaches, including the VGG-based model. The paper's main contributions include: (1) a strong minimal baseline for parametric texture synthesis using single-layer CNNs with random filters, (2) a quantitative texture quality measure based on the synthesis loss in the VGG-based model, and (3) a discussion on the importance of balancing perceptual similarity and variability in texture synthesis.

**Decision: Accept**

I recommend accepting this paper for publication in ICLR. The paper presents a well-motivated and well-executed study that challenges the conventional wisdom that deep hierarchical representations are necessary for natural texture synthesis. The authors provide a thorough analysis of their results, including a discussion of the strengths and limitations of their approach.

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper's main strength lies in its ability to demonstrate that simple single-layer CNNs can generate high-quality textures. The authors provide a thorough analysis of their results, including a comparison with state-of-the-art approaches. The paper also raises important questions about the importance of balancing perceptual similarity and variability in texture synthesis.

**Additional Feedback**

To further improve the paper, I suggest that the authors consider the following:

1. Provide more details on the optimization procedure used to train the single-layer CNNs. This would help to clarify the role of optimization in achieving high-quality textures.
2. Consider adding more experiments to evaluate the robustness of the proposed approach to different types of textures and image sizes.
3. Provide more discussion on the implications of the paper's findings for the field of texture synthesis and beyond.

**Questions for the Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the optimization procedure used to train the single-layer CNNs?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of variability in texture synthesis, which is currently not well captured by the proposed approach?
3. Have you considered applying the proposed approach to other image synthesis tasks, such as image-to-image translation or image generation?
