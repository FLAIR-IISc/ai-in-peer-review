- Proposal: single-layer CNN with multi-scale random filters for parametric texture synthesis that can sometimes rival VGG-based models.  
- Main contribution: argues texture synthesis may not require deep hierarchical or learned feature maps.  
- Acceptance rationale: insightful analysis on the role of deep representations and clear, well-organized presentation.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation including direct comparison to state-of-the-art VGG-based model.  
- Strength: detailed analysis showing multi-scale structure can be captured by a single-layer model.  
- Strength: reasoned discussion of VGG limitations and potential benefits of single-layer architectures.  
- Limitation: conclusions need further validation via more systematic comparisons with other architectures and techniques.  
- Limitation: evaluation relies mainly on visual inspection, which may be insufficient to assess sample quality.  
- Limitation: both VGG and single-layer outputs are still substantially worse than non-parametric methods, indicating room for improvement.  
- Suggested improvement: provide more details on the optimization procedure used for the single-layer model.  
- Suggested improvement: evaluate on a wider range of textures and datasets to show generalizability.  
- Suggested improvement: expand discussion on broader implications for texture synthesis and related fields.  
- Questions for authors: request optimization details and comparison to VGG optimization.  
- Questions for authors: ask how the single-layer model performs on more complex, multi-scale, or non-stationary textures.  
- Questions for authors: inquire about evaluations on other datasets or tasks (e.g., denoising, super-resolution).  
- Questions for authors: ask why VGG performs better on inpainting and whether that relates to multi-scale structure capture.
