- Proposes a simple, effective baseline for parametric texture synthesis using multi-scale random filters in a single-layer, no-pooling network.  
- Demonstrates that this minimal model produces high-perceptual-quality natural textures.  
- Shows the baseline often rivals VGG-based state-of-the-art, questioning the necessity of deep hierarchical or learned feature maps.  
- Main contributions: a strong minimal baseline, high-quality synthesized textures, and comparisons of various single-layer architectures against SOTA.  
- Decision: accept with reservations.  
- Reviewer finds the approach well-motivated with convincing experimental results and thorough literature analysis.  
- Concern: conclusions about deep hierarchical representations not being necessary are not fully convincing, especially for more complex images.  
- Requests more rigorous validation of the conclusions and analyses of the method's limitations and when deeper networks help.  
- Asks for explanation of why the multi-scale model outperforms single-scale models in capturing long-range structures.  
- Requests plans to address limited variability in synthesized textures.  
- Wants more discussion on benefits and limitations of random filters versus learned feature maps.  
- Overall: interesting and thought-provoking; with additional experiments/analysis it could make a significant contribution.
