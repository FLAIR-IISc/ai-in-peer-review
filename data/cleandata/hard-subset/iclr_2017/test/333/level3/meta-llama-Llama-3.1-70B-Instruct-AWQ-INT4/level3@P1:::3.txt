**Summary of the paper's claims and contributions**

This paper investigates the essential components of deep neural networks for natural texture synthesis. Building upon the work of Gatys et al., which uses deep-feature Gram matrix statistics to characterize visual textures, the authors study the effectiveness of shallow and deep networks within this texture-synthesis framework. The main finding is that very shallow nets, consisting of a single random filter bank, can perform surprisingly well, sometimes visually outperforming the VGG-19 model on simple and regular textures. The paper provides an informative discussion of the strengths and limitations of Gram-matrix-based texture synthesis methods and highlights the importance of balancing perceptual similarity and variability in texture synthesis.

**Decision: Accept**

I decide to accept this paper due to its novel findings, thorough analysis, and well-structured discussion. The paper's main contribution, demonstrating that shallow nets can effectively capture texture statistics, challenges previous assumptions about the necessity of deep hierarchical representations for natural texture synthesis. Additionally, the authors provide a comprehensive discussion of the limitations and potential future directions of Gram-matrix-based texture synthesis methods.

**Supporting arguments for the decision**

1. **Novelty and significance**: The paper's main finding is novel and significant, as it challenges previous assumptions about the necessity of deep hierarchical representations for natural texture synthesis.
2. **Thorough analysis**: The authors provide a thorough analysis of the effectiveness of shallow and deep networks within the texture-synthesis framework, including a comparison of different architectures and filter types.
3. **Well-structured discussion**: The paper's discussion is well-structured and informative, highlighting the strengths and limitations of Gram-matrix-based texture synthesis methods and providing potential future directions.

**Additional feedback to improve the paper**

1. **Systematic comparison**: While the paper provides a qualitative comparison of different methods, a systematic, quantifiable comparison across methods would strengthen the claims and provide a clearer understanding of the methods' performance.
2. **Texture distribution modeling**: The paper highlights the importance of modeling texture distributions, rather than just matching one sample. Further discussion on this topic and potential methods for achieving this goal would be beneficial.
3. **Diversity measurement**: The authors suggest entropy as a measure of diversity, but acknowledge its impracticality. Alternative methods for measuring diversity, such as psychophysical evaluation, should be explored in future work.
4. **Quantitative evaluation**: The paper relies heavily on qualitative evaluation, which may be subjective. Incorporating quantitative evaluation metrics, such as those used in image quality assessment, would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the methods' performance.

**Questions for the authors**

1. How do you plan to address the limitation of not systematically comparing the performance of different methods?
2. Can you provide more insight into the potential methods for modeling texture distributions, rather than just matching one sample?
3. How do you plan to explore alternative methods for measuring diversity, such as psychophysical evaluation, in future work?
4. Are there any plans to incorporate quantitative evaluation metrics, such as those used in image quality assessment, to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the methods' performance?
