This paper presents a novel approach to generating natural textures using a single-layer convolutional neural network with random filters. The authors demonstrate that this simple model can produce high-quality textures that often rival the state-of-the-art approaches. The paper thoroughly analyzes the conditions that enable the generation of natural-looking textures and provides a strong minimal baseline for parametric texture synthesis.

I recommend accepting this paper, primarily due to its surprising results and thorough analysis. The paper's main contribution is the demonstration that a single-layer network with random filters can generate high-quality textures, challenging the conventional wisdom that deep hierarchical representations are necessary for natural image generation.

The paper's approach is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem they are trying to solve. The paper is also well-placed in the literature, as it builds upon and challenges previous work on texture synthesis.

The results presented in the paper are correct and scientifically rigorous. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their model, including a comparison with state-of-the-art approaches and an analysis of the importance of different components of their model.

However, I do have some additional feedback to help improve the paper. One area for improvement is the evaluation methods used in the paper. While the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their model, the evaluation is primarily qualitative, and it would be beneficial to include more quantitative measures of texture quality. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed approach and potential avenues for future work.

Some questions I would like the authors to answer to clarify my understanding of the paper and provide additional evidence to support their claims include:

* Can the authors provide more details on the optimization procedure used to train their model, and how it compares to other optimization procedures used in the field?
* How do the authors plan to address the issue of variability in their texture synthesis model, and what measures do they propose to use to evaluate the variability of their model's output?
* Can the authors provide more details on the computational efficiency of their model, and how it compares to other texture synthesis models?
