- Proposes single-layer CNNs with random filters as a novel approach for natural texture synthesis.
- Demonstrates these simple models can generate textures comparable to VGG-based state-of-the-art.
- Main contributions: a strong minimal baseline, a quantitative texture-quality measure using VGG synthesis loss, and discussion on perceptual similarity vs. variability.
- Recommendation: Accept for ICLR.
- Strength: thorough analysis and comparisons that challenge the necessity of deep hierarchical representations.
- Limitation: insufficient detail on the optimization procedure used for training/synthesis.
- Recommendation: add experiments testing robustness across different texture types and image sizes.
- Recommendation: expand discussion on broader implications for texture synthesis and related fields.
- Question: provide more details on the optimization procedure.
- Question: how will the variability issue in texture synthesis be addressed?
- Question: have you considered applying this approach to other image synthesis tasks (image-to-image translation, image generation)?
