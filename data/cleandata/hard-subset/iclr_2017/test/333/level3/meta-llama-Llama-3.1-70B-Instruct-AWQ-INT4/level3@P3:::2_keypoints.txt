- Main contribution: strong minimal baseline for parametric texture synthesis using a single-layer network with random, data-independent filters.
- Key finding: natural textures of high perceptual quality can be generated without deep hierarchies or supervised pre-training.
- Result challenges conventional wisdom that depth and trained feature spaces are necessary for natural image generation.
- Recommendation: accept, based on thorough analysis and surprising empirical results.
- Experimental evidence: single-layer model (no pooling, random filters) often rivals Gatys et al. (2015a) in perceptual quality.
- Quantitative evaluation: authors use a VGG-based synthesis loss as a texture quality measure.
- Strength: rigorous comparison and well-motivated experimental setup.
- Evaluation weakness: reliance on human inspection; recommend adding objective metrics such as SSIM or VIF.
- Methodology gap: need more detailed description of the optimization procedure and hyperparameter tuning that enabled the results.
- Model behavior issue: single-layer model favors perceptual similarity over variability; discussion of this trade-off is needed.
- Question raised: request for clarification on hyperparameter selection and optimization choices.
- Question raised: ask authors to comment on potential applications and specific use cases for the single-layer model.
