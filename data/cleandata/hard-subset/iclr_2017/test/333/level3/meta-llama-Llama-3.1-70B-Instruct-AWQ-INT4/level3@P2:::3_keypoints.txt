- Paper studies natural texture synthesis and shows very shallow networks, including single random filter banks, can generate high-quality textures, sometimes outperforming VGG-19 on simple/regular textures.
- Main contribution: a strong minimal baseline—single-layer, data-independent random filters—for parametric texture synthesis that challenges the need for deep pretrained networks.
- Authors analyze Gram-matrix-based texture synthesis and highlight the trade-off between perceptual similarity and variability.
- Experiments include comparisons with VGG-based models and a proposed quantitative texture quality measure based on VGG synthesis loss.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on the significance of the minimal baseline and the informative discussion of Gram-matrix methods.
- Major limitation: lack of systematic, quantifiable comparisons across methods, leaving uncertainty about whether models capture true texture distributions.
- Major limitation: difficulty measuring diversity—entropy is impractical and fails to capture visual quality; psychophysical evaluation suggested but deferred.
- Suggestion: add more quantitative comparisons using metrics such as MSE and PSNR.
- Suggestion: explore alternative diversity measures and generative models (e.g., GANs, VAEs) to better capture texture distributions.
- Suggestion: provide more details on the optimization procedure, including hyperparameters and number of iterations.
- Question: please provide full details of the optimization procedure, hyperparameters, and iteration counts used.
- Question: how will you address the limitation of measuring diversity in texture synthesis and what alternative methods will you use?
- Question: can you provide additional quantitative comparisons across methods using metrics like MSE or PSNR?
- Question: how can Gram-matrix-based synthesis be extended to more complex textures with irregular structures or multiple scales?
- Additional feedback: include more visualizations of generated textures and side-by-side comparisons with the VGG-based model.
- Additional feedback: report computational efficiency of the single-layer model with comparisons to VGG-based approaches.
- Additional feedback: expand discussion on the relationship between the Gram matrix and the underlying texture distribution and synthesis implications.
