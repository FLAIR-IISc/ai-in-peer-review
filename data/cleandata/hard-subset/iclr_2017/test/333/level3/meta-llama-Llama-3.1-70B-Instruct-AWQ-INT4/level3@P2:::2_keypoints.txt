- Paper analyzes conditions for generating natural-looking textures, showing surprising results that single-layer networks with random filters can rival state-of-the-art.  
- Well-motivated and well-placed in the literature with a clear, concise overview of parametric texture modeling.  
- Detailed description of methodology, including single-layer architecture and optimization-based synthesis procedure.  
- Thorough experimental analysis across multiple factors (depth, pooling, training on natural images) supporting the paper's claims.  
- Evaluation uses several metrics including a VGG-based quantitative synthesis-loss measure and extensive visual comparisons.  
- Heavy reliance on visual inspection makes evaluation somewhat subjective.  
- The quantitative texture-quality measure used is imperfect and could be improved.  
- Paper is well-written and aligns with other reviewers; recommendation is to accept.  
- Suggested improvements: provide more details on the optimization procedure and refine evaluation metrics.  
- Open questions for authors: clarify optimization details, plans to improve evaluation methods, and the impact of the number of feature maps on texture quality.
