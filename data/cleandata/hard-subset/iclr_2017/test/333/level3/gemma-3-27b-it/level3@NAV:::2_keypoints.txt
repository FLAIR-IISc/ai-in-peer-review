- Paper challenges need for depth/pre-training: shows natural textures can be synthesized with single-layer, no-pooling, even random-filter networks.
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends accept due to surprising, well-supported results.
- Motivation and positioning: clearly articulates gap in understanding and situates work within parametric texture modeling and Gatys et al.
- Main contribution: systematic exploration of single-layer architectures and comparison to state-of-the-art.
- Experimental thoroughness: comprehensive ablation studies and comparisons (Figures 1–4) convincingly demonstrate method capabilities.
- Quantitative evaluation: introduces a VGG-based synthesis loss as an objective texture quality measure.
- Insightful discussion: addresses trade-off between perceptual similarity and variability and outlines future research directions.
- Evaluation weakness: VGG-based metric is imperfect and evaluation could be strengthened with additional perceptual metrics or user studies.
- Optimization transparency: “more elaborate tuning” of the optimization procedure is insufficiently detailed.
- Computational cost: reviewer requests quantitative comparison of synthesis cost between the multi-scale model and the VGG-based model.
- MaxEnt/finite-size concern: asks for discussion on how finite-size effects in the MaxEnt framework may affect interpreted variability in synthesized textures.
