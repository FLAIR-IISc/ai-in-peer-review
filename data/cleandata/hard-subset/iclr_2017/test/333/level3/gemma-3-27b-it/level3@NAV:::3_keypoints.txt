- Paper challenges the need for depth and pre-training in texture synthesis, showing core components can be simple.  
- Demonstrates strong performance of a single-layer convolutional network with random filters, often rivaling deeper pre-trained networks.  
- Finds that optimizing with shallow random filters can produce lower VGG-19 loss than directly optimizing the VGG-19 objective.  
- Suggests a more tractable optimization landscape for shallow networks, supported by analysis (Fig. 4 and supplement).  
- Discusses limitations of Gram-matrix-based texture synthesis and the difficulty of measuring true texture distributions.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance for ICLR, citing the counterintuitive and significant core finding.  
- Praises clear presentation and the insightful analysis of optimization behavior.  
- Notes a limitation: lack of systematic, quantitative comparisons across methods beyond visual examples.  
- Requests discussion on sensitivity to hyperparameter tuning and different optimization initializations.  
- Asks for clarification on which specific textures shallow networks fail on and the distinguishing characteristics of those textures.  
- Requests more detail on the selection process to ensure diversity of example textures shown.
