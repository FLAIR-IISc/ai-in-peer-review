- Decision: Accept.

- Main claim: Single-layer networks with random filters can generate natural textures competitive with deep, pre-trained models.

- Contribution: Challenges the belief that depth and pre-training are necessary, providing a strong minimal baseline.

- Experiments: Thorough comparison of single-layer architectures (Fourier, K-means, PCA, random) against Gatys et al. (2015).

- Key empirical finding: Multi-scale single-layer models perform especially well by combining different spatial scales.

- Optimization insight: Shallow models can yield lower VGG-19 loss and may be easier to optimize than deeper networks.

- Practical tip: Initializing VGG-19 synthesis with outputs from the single-layer model improves performance.

- Evaluation caveat: Current metrics (VGG-based loss) imperfectly capture perceptual quality and diversity; authors recommend psychophysical evaluation.

- Suggested improvement: Explore additional quantitative metrics beyond VGG loss.

- Suggested improvement: Develop preliminary diversity metrics (e.g., entropy of feature activations) to assess variability.

- Suggested improvement: Perform ablation studies on optimization hyperparameters (learning rate, regularization) to clarify their role.

- Suggested improvement: Include a larger and more diverse set of visual examples to strengthen qualitative claims.

- Question: Request elaboration on specific optimization difficulties for deeper models (e.g., vanishing gradients, local minima).

- Question: Ask for recommendations on experimental design and critical parameters for psychophysical studies of texture quality/diversity.

- Question: Request clarification on the scaling factor (1e7) used for loss/gradients and sensitivity to its value.

- Question: Ask whether similar single-layer successes might transfer to other tasks (image completion, style transfer) and what adaptations would be needed.
