- Main claim: Single-layer convolutional networks with random filters can synthesize textures competitive with deep, pre-trained VGG-based methods.
- Novelty: Successfully uses random filters and emphasizes the importance of multi-scale analysis within a simple single-layer framework.
- Empirical support: Extensive experiments convincingly back the claim and provide a strong minimal baseline against state-of-the-art methods.
- Quantitative contribution: Introduces a VGG-based synthesis loss as a quantitative texture quality measure.
- Strength: Clear demonstration that multi-scale random networks outperform single-scale variants for capturing structures at different resolutions.
- Limitation: Generated samples are not flawless and exhibit known texture-synthesis shortcomings (see Figure 3).
- Nuance: VGG-based models retain an advantage for inpainting tasks (Figure 7).
- Suggested validation: Further tests needed to confirm whether deep hierarchical representations are truly unnecessary for texture synthesis.
- Open question: Could carefully designed hierarchical models outperform the multi-scale single-layer approach on multi-scale textures?
- Method details requested: Clarify the optimization procedure and specific tuning applied, and how this compares to earlier reports.
- Metric concerns: Investigate limitations of the VGG-based loss as a perceptual metric and whether it underestimates diverse sample quality.
- Practical trade-off: Analyze the relationship between number of feature maps and computational cost to strengthen practical implications.
