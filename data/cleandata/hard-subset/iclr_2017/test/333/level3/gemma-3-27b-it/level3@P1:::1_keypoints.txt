- Paper shows high-quality texture synthesis can be achieved with a simple single-layer network with random filters, challenging the need for deep pre-trained models.
- Authors propose a multi-scale single-layer model that often rivals VGG-based approaches in performance.
- Introduces a quantitative evaluation metric based on VGG synthesis loss to provide a more objective assessment than human inspection.
- Reviewer recommends rejection, arguing the paper overstates claims about the non-necessity of depth and pre-training.
- Single-layer and multi-scale models still fall short of state-of-the-art and existing non-parametric methods according to the reviewer.
- VGG loss is acknowledged as limited—especially for regular textures—and may not reliably reflect perceptual quality.
- Visual inspection (Figures 1 & 4) shows noticeable aesthetic and realism differences favoring the VGG model despite similar VGG-loss scores.
- Lack of comparisons to established non-parametric texture synthesis methods (e.g., Portilla & Simoncelli) weakens the evaluation.
- Results may depend heavily on the chosen optimization procedure (L-BFGS-B), and sensitivity to optimizer and its parameters is unclear.
- Reviewer suggests including comparisons to non-parametric methods, conducting user studies for perceptual evaluation, and analyzing optimizer sensitivity.
- Recommends expanding discussion on perceptual similarity vs. variability, and whether deep hierarchical models might more efficiently capture multi-scale structure.
- Advises detailing failure cases of single-layer models to provide further insight.
- Questions for authors request sensitivity analysis of optimization, statistical significance for claimed rivalry with VGG, specific texture types where VGG loss fails, computational trade-offs vs. deep models, and quantitative inpainting comparisons.
