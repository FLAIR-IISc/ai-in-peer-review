- Paper shows a single convolutional layer with random, untrained filters can generate high-quality parametric textures.
- Challenges the necessity of network depth, hierarchical representations, and supervised pre-training for texture synthesis.
- Finds shallow random-filter models can visually outperform deep VGG-19 on certain regular textures.
- Analyzes the optimization landscape, arguing shallow models have simpler objectives that ease optimization.
- Observes shallow-model outputs can yield lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization in some cases.
- Demonstrates VGG-19 synthesis improves when initialized from a shallow model’s output.
- Recommendation: Accept, based on the surprising core result and insightful optimization analysis.
- Strength: provides a strong, simple baseline and prompts re-evaluation of why deep pre-trained models work.
- Limitation: lacks systematic quantitative evaluation of synthesis quality and diversity; relies on visuals and VGG-loss proxy.
- Authors note the need to model texture distributions (not just match one image) and suggest psychophysical studies as future work.
- Reviewer asks for clarification on the trade-off between perceptual quality and sample diversity, especially for shallow models.
- Reviewer asks about practical quantitative diversity metrics (e.g., average distance between generated samples) as alternatives to entropy.
