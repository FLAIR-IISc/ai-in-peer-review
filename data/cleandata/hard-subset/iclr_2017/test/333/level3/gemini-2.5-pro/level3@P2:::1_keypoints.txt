- Paper challenges the assumption that deep, pre-trained networks are required for high-quality parametric texture synthesis.  
- Main contribution: a simple single-layer convolutional model with multi-scale random filters serves as a strong baseline.  
- Empirical results show this minimal model often matches or outperforms the 19-layer pre-trained VGG-based method on many textures.  
- Systematic comparisons of filter types (random, k-means, Fourier) and architectures support the central claim.  
- Key insight: matching Gram-matrix (second-order) statistics in an overcomplete feature space can be sufficient for texture synthesis.  
- Multi-scale filters are crucial because different filter sizes capture different spatial frequencies; their combination models full texture statistics.  
- Visual results are particularly strong on highly regular patterns (e.g., brick walls, grids), where the random model can appear superior.  
- Limitations: synthesized samples still show artifacts and are qualitatively worse than some non-parametric methods.  
- VGG-based model may better capture non-local structure and contextual information (evident in inpainting results).  
- Claim that deep hierarchies are unnecessary should be nuanced: large single-layer filters (up to 55×55) are computationally expensive, while depth can provide efficiency and large receptive fields.  
- Authors should report more details on hierarchical random-network experiments (architectures, results) to substantiate depth-related claims.  
- The introduced VGG-loss is a useful quantitative metric but fails to align with human perception in some cases (e.g., regular grids); its limitations should be stated.  
- The trade-off between perceptual similarity and sample variability is an important point and a valuable direction for future work.  
- Questions for authors: provide architecture details for hierarchical random networks tested.  
- Questions for authors: clarify whether VGG’s better inpainting implies learned features are needed for complex spatial/contextual modeling.  
- Questions for authors: elaborate on the relationship between the multi-scale single-layer model and deep hierarchical models regarding efficiency and parameterization.
