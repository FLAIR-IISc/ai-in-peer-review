- Main finding: a single convolutional layer with random, untrained filters can synthesize high-quality textures, rivaling deep VGG-19 for many patterns.
- The paper systematically compares single-layer models (Fourier, K-means, PCA, random) and studies the effect of filter scale.
- Surprising result: shallow random multi-scale models produce visually cleaner, more globally coherent outputs for regular textures (bricks, grids, scales).
- Key conceptual claim: much of Gram-matrix-based texture synthesis success may stem from general convolutional filter bank properties, not learned hierarchical features.
- Empirical insight: optimizing the shallow model’s objective can yield images with lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG optimization, suggesting a simpler optimization landscape.
- Practical contribution: shallow-model outputs make superior initializations for VGG-based optimization, improving final results.
- Strong discussion: authors candidly address evaluation limitations and emphasize modeling the texture distribution rather than reproducing a single exemplar.
- The paper connects practical issues to the Maximum Entropy framework and advocates psychophysical evaluation for assessing sample variability.
- Decision: Accept, based on the surprising empirical result and the paper’s thoughtful problem framing and analysis.
- Supporting evidence: compelling visual comparisons in figures and supplementary material that back the claims.
- Limitation noted: lack of systematic quantitative evaluation to objectively support visual superiority claims.
- Suggested improvement: include a small-scale user study (e.g., 2AFC) to provide objective perceptual evidence.
- Suggested improvement: show sample variability visually (e.g., 3x3 grids of different syntheses) for both shallow and VGG models.
- Suggested improvement: analyze failure modes to identify texture classes where deep, trained features are necessary.
- Question: Have you analyzed loss landscapes or gradient properties to support the smoother optimization hypothesis beyond initialization experiments?
- Question: Can you detail conditions (layers/filters/texture complexity) under which deeper random networks overfit and trivially reconstruct the reference?
- Question: Do you have an intuition why VGG-based perceptual loss fails on very regular, man-made structures—might VGG’s training bias toward natural scenes explain this?
