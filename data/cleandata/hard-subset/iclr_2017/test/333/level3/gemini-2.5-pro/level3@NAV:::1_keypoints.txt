- Proposes a simple baseline: a single convolutional layer with random, multi-scale filters for parametric texture synthesis.
- Shows matching second-order statistics (Gram matrices) of a rich random filter bank can capture perceptual essence of many natural textures.
- Empirical results often rival VGG-based state-of-the-art texture synthesis.
- Reviewer recommends accepting the paper.
- Main contribution forces re-evaluation of whether deep, pre-trained hierarchical networks are essential for texture synthesis.
- Strength: compelling empirical evidence that a minimal model can match complex models, isolating statistical matching as a core mechanism.
- Claim that deep hierarchical representations are unnecessary may be too strong and needs further validation.
- VGG-based model outperforms the proposed model on inpainting (Fig. 7), indicating hierarchical models may better capture non-local context and structure.
- Both the proposed model and VGG baseline produce samples that are still imperfect and worse than leading non-parametric methods.
- Suggestion to nuance claims about the role of deep hierarchies; depth might provide other advantages.
- Question: Could a hierarchical model achieve multi-scale receptive fields more efficiently, making depth primarily a computational-efficiency benefit?
- Question: How to reconcile the paper’s conclusions with VGG’s stronger inpainting performance, which suggests hierarchical representations help tasks requiring contextual understanding?
