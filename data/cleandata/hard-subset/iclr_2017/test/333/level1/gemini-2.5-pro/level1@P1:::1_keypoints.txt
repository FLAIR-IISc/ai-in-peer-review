- Paper demonstrates a single convolutional layer with random filters and ReLU can synthesize high-quality textures.  
- Claims depth, pooling, and supervised pre-training (e.g., VGG) are not required for natural texture synthesis.  
- A "minimal baseline" (random multi-scale single layer) often rivals or exceeds VGG-based synthesis quality.  
- Systematic comparison of filter types (random, Fourier, K-means, PCA) shows random filters are highly effective.  
- Introduces a VGG-based reconstruction loss as a quantitative metric for texture quality.  
- Discusses trade-off between perceptual similarity and sample variability and frames it in a generalized maximum-entropy view.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: The finding is significant and counter-intuitive, challenging prevailing assumptions about necessary components.  
- Key reason 2: Rigorous, thorough experimentation with ablations and controls supports the claims.  
- The paper disentangles contributions of depth, nonlinearity, pooling, and learned features; visual results are compelling.  
- Authors identify and fix optimization issues from prior random-network attempts by scaling the loss.  
- Experiments show multi-scale filters are crucial to capture both fine and long-range texture structure.  
- Ablation without ReLU confirms nonlinearity is necessary.  
- Section 7 offers a thoughtful discussion and proposes a perceptual-distance/epsilon-environment framework for finite images.  
- Suggestion: Clarify construction and scale-distribution of the 1024 random multi-scale filters and how they are combined for Gram computation.  
- Suggestion: Provide quantitative comparison of computational cost (synthesis time/FLOPS) between large single-filter layers and multi-layer alternatives.  
- Suggestion: Include failure cases where random multi-scale model fails but VGG succeeds to clarify limitations.  
- Question: How sensitive are results to the 1e7 loss/gradient scaling hyperparameter and how was it chosen?  
- Question: Can authors provide examples (appendix) of deeper random CNNs exactly reconstructing reference textures?  
- Question: How does performance scale with the number of random filters and is there a point of diminishing returns compared to VGG-sized layers?
