- Main claim: A single convolutional layer with random, untrained filters can synthesize high-quality textures rivaling deep VGG-based models.  
- Contribution: Comprehensive empirical study comparing single-layer architectures (random, Fourier, K-means/PCA, learned), filter counts, and scales.  
- Finding: Random multi-scale filters often outperform or match VGG-based synthesis despite far simpler models.  
- Metric: Introduces a quantitative texture-quality metric using the VGG synthesis loss as a proxy for perceptual quality.  
- Theoretical connection: Links empirical results to Maximum Entropy texture modeling and discusses limitations for finite images.  
- Decision: Reviewer recommends accept, citing surprise value and strong empirical support.  
- Key reasons to accept: Challenges assumptions about necessity of depth/pretraining; presents rigorous, clear ablation-style evidence.  
- Technical rigor: Systematic exploration, controlled experiments, and identification of an important optimization detail (loss scaling).  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written with clear motivation, methods, and effective figures; Section 7 offers valuable conceptual discussion.  
- Evaluation caveat: Uses VGG loss as a pragmatic quantitative proxy while acknowledging its limitations and discussing them.  
- Suggested improvement: Formally define the "Random multi-scale" model (filter counts and sizes) in Section 2 for clarity.  
- Suggested improvement: Include qualitative results for hierarchical random networks (e.g., in appendix) to support claims about depth.  
- Suggested improvement: More tightly integrate the theoretical metric d(x,y) discussion with empirical findings on multi-scale filters.  
- Question for authors: How sensitive is synthesis quality to the loss-scaling factor and what does that imply about the optimization landscape?  
- Question for authors: How are the 1024 filters in the multi-scale model distributed across scales, and could single-scale models be under-resourced?  
- Question for authors: Did you try alternative optimizers or multiple initializations for VGG-based synthesis to confirm reported local-minimum behavior?
