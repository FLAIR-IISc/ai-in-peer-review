- Decision: Accept.  
- Main contribution: Proposes a minimal single-layer CNN with random filters that rivals deep pre-trained CNNs for high-quality texture synthesis.  
- Novelty/insight: Challenges the assumption that depth and pre-training are necessary for texture generation.  
- Experimental rigor: Thorough experiments and extensive comparisons across architectures, filter types, and metrics.  
- Quantitative evaluation: Introduces VGG-loss as an objective alternative to human inspection for texture quality assessment.  
- Multi-scale & non-linearity: Demonstrates that multi-scale filters and non-linearities are crucial for high-quality synthesis.  
- Comparison to prior work: Directly contrasts results with Gatys et al. (2015a) and shows competitive perceptual quality.  
- Perceptual vs. variability trade-off: Discusses the balance between producing perceptually similar textures and maintaining output variability.  
- Theoretical connection: Links findings to maximum entropy models for deeper analysis.  
- Practical limitation: Notes inefficiency of large-scale filters in single-layer models and calls for optimized implementations.  
- Suggestion — variability: Recommends more detailed assessment of variability in synthesized textures.  
- Suggestion — human studies: Advises incorporating psychophysical experiments to validate perceptual claims and metrics.  
- Question — texture types: Asks how the model performs on highly irregular or non-repetitive textures and potential limitations.  
- Question — adaptive multi-scale: Queries whether filter sizes could be made adaptive to texture characteristics.  
- Question — random filter sensitivity: Inquires about sensitivity to random filter initializations and resulting quality variance.
