- Proposes a generative model for texture synthesis using a single-layer CNN with random filters that often rivals Gatys et al.  
- Main contributions: (1) minimal baseline with single-layer random filters; (2) quantitative texture quality measure based on VGG synthesis loss; (3) discussion on balancing perceptual similarity and variability.  
- Recommendation: Accept for ICLR 2017.  
- Well-motivated approach with clear discussion of limitations and benefits of single-layer random filters.  
- Rigorous evaluation using an objective quantitative texture quality measure rather than only qualitative inspection.  
- Significant contribution demonstrating high-quality texture synthesis achievable with a simple model.  
- Suggestion: further elaborate limitations, e.g., inability to capture long-range dependencies.  
- Suggestion: provide more comprehensive comparisons with other parametric synthesis methods and optimization strategies.  
- Suggestion: evaluate on additional datasets with varied texture types and complexity.  
- Question: provide details on the optimization algorithm, hyperparameters, and number of iterations.  
- Question: how will the issue of variability in texture synthesis be addressed?  
- Question: clarify the relationship between the number of feature maps and perceptual quality of synthesized textures.
