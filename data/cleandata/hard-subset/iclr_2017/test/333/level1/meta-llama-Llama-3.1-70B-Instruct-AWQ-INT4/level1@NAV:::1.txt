**Summary**

This paper proposes a generative model for natural texture synthesis using a single-layer convolutional neural network (CNN) with completely random filters. The authors demonstrate that this minimal model can generate high-quality textures that often rival the state-of-the-art approach by Gatys et al. (2015a), which relies on a deep neural network with features trained on natural images. The paper explores the importance of various aspects of the feature representation, such as depth, pooling, and training on natural images, and shows that none of these are indispensable for natural texture synthesis.

**Decision**

I decide to **Accept** this paper with two key reasons:

1. The paper tackles a specific and well-motivated question, namely, what aspects of feature representation are crucial for natural image generation, and provides a clear and well-supported answer.
2. The paper presents a strong minimal baseline for parametric texture synthesis and demonstrates its effectiveness through extensive experiments and comparisons with state-of-the-art approaches.

**Supporting arguments**

The paper is well-motivated by the current state of the art in natural image generation and texture synthesis. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and the relevant background. The proposed model is simple yet effective, and the experiments are well-designed and thorough. The results are presented in a clear and easy-to-understand manner, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the findings.

The paper also makes a significant contribution to the field by showing that a single-layer CNN with random filters can be a strong baseline for texture synthesis, which challenges the conventional wisdom that deep neural networks are necessary for natural image generation.
