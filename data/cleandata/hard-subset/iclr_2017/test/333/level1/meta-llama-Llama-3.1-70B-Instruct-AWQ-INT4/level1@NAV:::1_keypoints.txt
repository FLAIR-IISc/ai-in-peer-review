- Proposes a generative texture synthesis model using a single-layer CNN with completely random filters.  
- Demonstrates the minimal model can generate high-quality textures often rivaling Gatys et al. (2015a).  
- Shows depth, pooling, and training on natural images are not indispensable for natural texture synthesis.  
- Tackles the question of which feature-representation aspects are crucial for natural image generation.  
- Provides a clear, well-supported answer and a strong minimal baseline for parametric texture synthesis.  
- Experiments are extensive, well-designed, and results are presented and analyzed clearly.  
- Challenges the conventional wisdom that deep, pretrained networks are necessary for natural image generation.  
- Decision: Accept — based on solid motivation/answer and the strong minimal baseline demonstrated.  
- Suggestion: Provide more details on the optimization procedure and its tuning.  
- Suggestion: Add comparisons with other state-of-the-art approaches (e.g., GANs, VAEs).  
- Suggestion: Discuss limitations of the proposed model and potential future research directions.  
- Questions for authors: detail optimization/tuning, possible extensions to image generation or style transfer, and how to address model limitations.
