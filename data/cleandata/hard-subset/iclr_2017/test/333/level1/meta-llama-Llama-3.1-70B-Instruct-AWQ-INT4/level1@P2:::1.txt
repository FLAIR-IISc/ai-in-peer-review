**Summary of the paper's contributions**

This paper proposes a generative model for natural texture synthesis based on a single-layer convolutional neural network with completely random filters. The authors demonstrate that their model can capture the perceptual differences between natural textures, often rivaling the current state-of-the-art approach by Gatys et al. (2015a). The paper's main contributions include: (1) a strong minimal baseline for parametric texture synthesis using a single-layer network with random filters, (2) a quantitative texture quality measure based on the synthesis loss in the VGG-based model, and (3) a discussion on the importance of balancing perceptual similarity and variability in texture synthesis.

**Decision: Accept**

I recommend accepting this paper for publication in ICLR 2017. The paper addresses a well-defined question in the field of natural image generation, namely, what aspects of feature representation are crucial for natural texture synthesis. The authors provide a thorough analysis of their approach, including a comparison with the state-of-the-art method and an evaluation of their model's performance using a quantitative texture quality measure.

**Supporting arguments**

1. **Well-motivated approach**: The authors provide a clear motivation for their approach, discussing the limitations of current methods and the potential benefits of using a single-layer network with random filters. They also provide a thorough analysis of their model's performance, including a comparison with the state-of-the-art method.
2. **Rigorous evaluation**: The authors evaluate their model's performance using a quantitative texture quality measure, which provides a more objective assessment of their model's performance compared to qualitative human inspection.
3. **Significant contribution**: The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of natural image generation, demonstrating that a single-layer network with random filters can be used for high-quality texture synthesis.

**Additional feedback**

1. **Discussion on limitations**: While the authors discuss the limitations of their approach, they could further elaborate on the potential limitations of their model, such as its inability to capture long-range dependencies in textures.
2. **Comparison with other methods**: The authors could provide a more comprehensive comparison with other methods, including parametric texture synthesis models that use different types of filters or optimization algorithms.
3. **Evaluation on more datasets**: The authors could evaluate their model's performance on more datasets, including datasets with different types of textures or images with varying levels of complexity.

**Questions for the authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the optimization algorithm used in your approach, including the choice of hyperparameters and the number of iterations?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of variability in texture synthesis, which is an important aspect of texture modeling?
3. Can you provide more insights on the relationship between the number of feature maps and the perceptual quality of the synthesized textures?
