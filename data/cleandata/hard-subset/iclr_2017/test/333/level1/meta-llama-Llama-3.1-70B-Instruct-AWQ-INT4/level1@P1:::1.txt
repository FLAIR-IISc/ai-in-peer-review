**Summary**

The paper "What does it take to generate natural textures?" presents a novel approach to natural texture synthesis using a single-layer convolutional neural network with random filters. The authors demonstrate that this minimal baseline can generate high-quality textures that often rival state-of-the-art approaches, suggesting that the depth and pre-training of multi-layer image representations are not as indispensable for natural image generation as previously thought.

**Decision**

I recommend accepting this paper with minor revisions.

**Reasons for the decision**

1. The paper tackles a specific and well-defined problem in the field of natural texture synthesis, and the authors provide a clear and well-motivated approach to addressing this problem.
2. The authors provide extensive experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach, including comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and a range of different filter types and sizes.

**Supporting arguments**

* The paper provides a thorough review of the current state of the art in parametric texture modeling and clearly explains the limitations of existing approaches.
* The authors' use of a single-layer convolutional neural network with random filters is a novel and interesting approach that challenges the conventional wisdom that deep hierarchical representations are necessary for natural image generation.
* The experimental results are extensive and well-presented, and the authors provide a clear and detailed description of their methods and results.

**Additional feedback**

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the implications of the authors' findings for the broader field of natural image generation. In particular, the authors could explore the potential applications of their approach to other areas, such as artistic style transfer or image synthesis.
* The authors could also provide more detail on the optimization procedure used to generate the textures, including the choice of hyperparameters and the convergence criteria.
* Some of the figures could be improved for clarity and readability. For example, Figure 1 could be rearranged to make it easier to compare the different models and textures.

**Questions for the authors**

* Can the authors provide more detail on the computational efficiency of their approach compared to state-of-the-art methods? How does the computational cost scale with the size of the input image and the number of filters?
* How do the authors plan to address the issue of variability in the generated textures? Is there a way to incorporate additional constraints or regularization terms to encourage more diverse and realistic textures?
* Can the authors provide more insight into the relationship between the Gram matrix and the perceptual quality of the generated textures? Is there a way to use the Gram matrix to predict the perceptual quality of the textures, or to optimize the texture synthesis process?
