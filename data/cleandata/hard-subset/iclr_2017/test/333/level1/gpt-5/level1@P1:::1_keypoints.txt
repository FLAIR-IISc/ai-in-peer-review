- Minimalist baseline: Single-layer CNNs with ReLU and Gram-matrix matching, even with random filters, can produce high-quality textures rivaling VGG-based models.  
- Multi-scale random filters: Combining receptive fields at multiple scales is particularly effective and often outperforms large single-scale filters.  
- Systematic comparisons: The paper compares random, Fourier, PCA, and k-means filters, showing learned filters do not consistently beat random ones.  
- Ablation findings: Nonlinearity (ReLU) and multi-scale receptive fields are critical; depth and supervised pretraining are not necessary.  
- Quantitative evaluation: Uses VGG Gram-based loss as a proxy for perceptual similarity alongside qualitative results on 20 textures.  
- MaxEnt framing: The paper situates the method within maximum-entropy texture modeling and argues for allowing small constraint mismatches to capture variability.  
- Decision: Accept — novel and impactful evidence that depth and supervised training aren’t required for Gram-based texture synthesis.  
- Empirical rigor: Thorough empirical ablations and consistent visual evidence across many textures support the claims.  
- Optimization insight: Initializing VGG synthesis from random single-layer outputs suggests optimization issues confound deeper models.  
- Metric caveat: VGG loss is a relevant but imperfect perceptual metric; random models outperforming under this metric strengthens claims.  
- Evaluation robustness suggestion: A small psychophysical study (e.g., 2AFC discriminations or inpainting test) would strengthen perceptual claims.  
- Diversity analysis suggestion: Quantify sample diversity with intra-set VGG distances, pixel-space diversity, and report failure cases of near-copying.  
- Reproducibility request: Provide exact multi-scale configuration, filter counts per scale, initialization ranges, optimization hyperparameters, and runtime/memory profiles.  
- Baseline comparisons request: Include Portilla & Simoncelli and scattering-based synthesis for triangulation, and comment on color-space preprocessing effects.  
- Theoretical link suggestion: Relate results to random features/kernel approximations and the scattering transform to explain why random features suffice.  
- Failure-mode characterization: Describe textures where the method underperforms (e.g., long-range structure, non-stationarity) and whether simple constraints help.  
- Question — multi-scale design: What exact filter sizes and allocation produced 1024 features, and how sensitive are results to that allocation?  
- Question — optimization: How critical is the 1e7 scaling and [0,1] box constraint; were other optimizers (Adam, unscaled L-BFGS) or budgets tried?  
- Question — color handling: Were different color spaces or channel-wise vs. cross-channel Gram matrices tested; does decorrelation/whitening change outcomes?  
- Question — diversity: How diverse are samples across seeds; report intra-sample VGG losses and examples of overfitting/near-copying.  
- Question — metrics for regular structure: For cases where visuals beat VGG loss, can alternative metrics (SSIM, LPIPS) or a user study quantify the gap?  
- Question — efficiency: Provide concrete speed/memory numbers for hierarchical vs. single-layer synthesis and whether dilated convolutions help.  
- Question — additional constraints: Have spectrum or histogram constraints been tried to fix failures without adding depth or training?  
- Question — generalization: Do conclusions extend to style transfer, or is trained content-preserving feature necessary; any preliminary results?
