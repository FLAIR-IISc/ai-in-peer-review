- Main claim: A single-layer convolutional network with random filters (no depth or pre-training) can generate high-quality natural textures, challenging the necessity of depth and pre-training.
- Decision: Reviewer recommends rejection due to insufficient evidence of robust, generalizable improvements.
- Primary concern: Claimed SOTA parity is shown mainly via visual comparisons and lacks rigorous statistical validation.
- VGG loss circularity: Using a VGG-based metric biases evaluation because the competing SOTA method is VGG-based, risking evaluation that rewards matching VGG features rather than perceptual quality.
- Optimization tuning unclear: The paper cites “elaborate tuning” as key but fails to specify what was changed, making it unclear whether gains are architectural or optimization artifacts.
- Unjustified scaling: The unusually large scaling factor (10^7) applied to loss/gradients is not justified and raises sensitivity concerns.
- Lack of ablation: The contributions of components (multi-scale, ReLU, filter types) are not systematically ablated to isolate what drives performance.
- Quantitative evaluation lacking: No user study or robust metrics (e.g., FID) are provided to statistically support perceptual claims.
- Generalization not demonstrated: Tests are limited in scope; performance consistency across diverse texture categories/datasets is not shown.
- Computational cost: The multi-scale model is computationally inefficient but trade-offs between cost and quality are not thoroughly discussed.
- Theory–experiment gap: The maximum entropy generalization is interesting but insufficiently connected to empirical results or used to motivate experiments.
- Filter size/receptive field unclear: The role of filter size and receptive field in texture quality needs clearer explanation and visualization.
- Questions for authors: Provide detailed optimization parameters and sensitivity analyses, conduct user studies, perform systematic ablations, and report how VGG-based evaluation handles textures unlike those in ImageNet.
