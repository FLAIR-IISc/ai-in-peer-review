- Paper shows single-layer networks with random filters can generate high-quality textures, challenging need for depth and pre-training.  
- Authors compare single-layer architectures (Fourier, K-means, PCA, Random) to state-of-the-art and introduce a VGG feature-space reconstruction loss as a quantitative metric.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection: findings are interesting but paper fails to justify experimental choices and does not demonstrate clear superiority beyond parity.  
- Weak justification for chosen filter size (11x11) and number of feature maps; calls for ablation studies on these hyperparameters.  
- Key claim that an “elaborate” optimization procedure drives success is under-detailed, harming reproducibility.  
- Inconsistency between relying on VGG-based quantitative metric and continued emphasis on visual/perceptual assessments.  
- MaxEnt framework discussion is valuable but feels disconnected from the experimental results and needs tighter integration.  
- Claim that optimization is “less prone to local minima” requires more empirical evidence and metrics.  
- Paper lacks a comparison of computational costs despite the proposed model’s simplicity.  
- Reviewer questions for authors: detail the optimization tuning (which parameters and why); justify/experiment with 11x11 filters; specify when VGG loss fails to reflect perceptual quality.
