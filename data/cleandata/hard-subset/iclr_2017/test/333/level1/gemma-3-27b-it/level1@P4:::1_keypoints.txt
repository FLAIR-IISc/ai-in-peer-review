- Main claim: Single-layer networks with random filters can synthesize high-quality textures, challenging the need for depth and pre-training.  
- Decision: Reviewer recommends rejection due to insufficient rigor in evaluation and comparisons.  
- Quantitative evaluation is weak; heavy reliance on a flawed VGG-based loss undermines objective comparison to prior work.  
- VGG-loss limitations acknowledged by authors and can fail for regular textures, so it’s an unreliable sole metric.  
- Human perceptual studies are needed to validate perceptual quality claims versus Gatys et al. (2015a).  
- Ablation study on filter types is extensive but lacks details on hyperparameter optimization for each model.  
- Unclear whether all models were tuned to their full potential; more detail on optimization procedures is required.  
- The statement about "more elaborate tuning" needs specificity: which parameters were changed and what impact they had.  
- Missing concrete proposals or metrics for quantifying variability and improving perceptual measures.  
- Filter size selection is unexplained; generalization of results across textures is unclear.  
- Computational efficiency of the multi-scale model is noted but not analyzed for trade-offs or optimizations.  
- Suggestion: provide thorough statistical comparisons (e.g., average VGG loss, significance tests) and an ablation study on optimization parameters (learning rate, iterations).
