- Random single-layer convolutional networks can synthesize natural textures matching or surpassing state-of-the-art deep models.  
- Random filters outperform or match trained filters (K-means, PCA) in texture synthesis.  
- A multi-scale model (filters of different sizes) captures both small- and long-range structures and yields superior results.  
- Network depth and supervised pre-training are not necessary for high-quality texture synthesis.  
- The authors introduce a VGG-based loss as a quantitative evaluation metric alternative to human inspection.  
- Experiments are thorough across filter types (Fourier, K-means, PCA, random), scales, and filter counts.  
- Visual results are compelling and backed by quantitative analysis.  
- Paper offers theoretical insight connecting random filters’ success to optimization tuning and maximum entropy frameworks.  
- The multi-scale finding shows scale combination matters more than filter size alone.  
- Improved optimization tuning likely explains prior failures of random filters to produce good textures.  
- The authors acknowledge limitations where the VGG metric does not correlate with human perception (e.g., very regular textures).  
- Recommendation: Accept, because the paper addresses a fundamental overlooked question and presents robust, comprehensive results.  
- Major concern: No formal human evaluation to validate the VGG-based metric.  
- Major concern: Need more analysis of why random filters work beyond attributing it to better optimization tuning.  
- Major concern: Computational efficiency of the multi-scale approach is not quantified against VGG-based methods.  
- Major suggestion: Conduct a small-scale human study comparing VGG loss to human judgments.  
- Major suggestion: Provide a systematic analysis of performance as a function of number of filters (plot/filter-count analysis).  
- Major suggestion: Better detail the optimization tuning (specific parameters beyond iterations and scaling).  
- Minor suggestion: Include runtime comparisons between the single-layer random model and VGG-based models.  
- Minor suggestion: Discuss potential generalization of the approach to other image types (scenes, objects).  
- Minor suggestion: Specify the exact random initialization distribution used for filters (e.g., Glorot uniform).  
- Question: Have you compared models with the same number of filters (e.g., 363 random vs. 363 K-means) to isolate filter type effects?  
- Question: Can you explain in more detail why multi-scale filter combinations effectively capture local and global texture statistics?  
- Question: How will you address VGG-based loss failures for regular textures, and what more robust perceptual metric could be used?  
- Question: How do you reconcile instances where the multi-scale model achieves lower VGG loss than the VGG-based model—metric limitation or true superiority?  
- Question: Have you tested whether random filters generalize to other tasks (style transfer, image generation), and what were the results?
