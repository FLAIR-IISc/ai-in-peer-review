- Main claim: A single convolutional layer with random filters (large number of feature maps and careful optimization) can synthesize natural textures rivaling state-of-the-art deep models.
- Key contribution: Introduces a strong minimal baseline for texture synthesis using a single-layer network with random filters.
- Key contribution: Shows depth and supervised pre-training are not strictly necessary for high-quality texture generation.
- Key contribution: Systematically compares single-layer architectures with various filter types (random, hand-crafted, unsupervised).
- Key contribution: Proposes a quantitative texture quality metric based on the VGG-based synthesis loss.
- Key contribution: Provides theoretical discussion on maximum-entropy texture modeling and finite-size effects.
- Decision: Recommend Accept due to the paper's counterintuitive claim and compelling experimental evidence.
- Supporting evidence: Demonstrates random filters can match trained filters, contradicting prior claims that random weights fail.
- Experimental thoroughness: Compares filter types, feature map counts (363 vs 3267), and a multi-scale model with multiple filter sizes.
- Multi-scale model: Combining filter sizes (3x3, 11x11, 55x55) effectively captures multiple spatial scales and improves results.
- Theoretical insight: Highlights differences between practical finite-size texture synthesis and ideal MaxEnt formulations.
- Quantitative evaluation: VGG-based loss is a useful objective proxy for texture quality beyond qualitative assessment.
- Improvement request: Clarify and analyze sensitivity of the large loss/gradient scaling factor (1e7) used in optimization.
- Improvement request: Add human perceptual evaluations to validate that VGG loss correlates with human judgments.
- Improvement request: Provide ablation to separate effects of filter type versus number of feature maps (e.g., Random-363 vs Random-3267).
- Improvement request: Address computational inefficiency of large multi-scale filters and discuss alternatives (e.g., stacked small filters).
- Improvement request: Report statistical significance tests to confirm performance differences.
- Question: How was the 1e7 loss-scaling chosen and is performance sensitive to it or masking optimization issues?
- Question: Did you replicate Gatys et al.'s random-weight experiments using your optimization settings, and how do results compare?
- Question: Can you show examples where VGG loss fails (e.g., regular man-made patterns) and explain the failure modes?
- Question: Why were filter sizes 3x3, 11x11, 55x55 chosen and how would different sizes affect results?
- Question: How will you address the reduced variability of naive synthesis and what metrics will measure variability in future work?
