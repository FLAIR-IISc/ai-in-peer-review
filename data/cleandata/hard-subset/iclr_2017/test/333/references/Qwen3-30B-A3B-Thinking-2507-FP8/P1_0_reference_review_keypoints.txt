- Proposes a minimal baseline: single-layer CNN with random filters for texture synthesis.  
- Shows single-layer random-filter synthesis often rivals or exceeds VGG-based state-of-the-art.  
- Systematically tests single-layer architectures with random, hand-crafted, and unsupervisedly learned filters.  
- Introduces a quantitative texture-quality measure based on the VGG synthesis loss.  
- Provides theoretical insights linking summary statistics, perceptual similarity, and texture variability.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Reason: Addresses a fundamental unchallenged assumption in texture synthesis.  
- Reason: Robust, comprehensive, well-organized experimental evidence.  
- Reason: Challenges common assumptions and provides new insights into necessary network properties.  
- Empirical support: Thorough experiments comparing multiple single-layer configs to VGG-based model.  
- Key insight: Single-layer random filters are sufficient for high-quality texture synthesis.  
- Key insight: Depth and supervised pre-training are not as essential as previously thought.  
- Key insight: Multi-scale filter combinations are crucial to capture small- and long-range texture statistics.  
- Key insight: Nonlinearities (ReLUs) are essential to capture texture statistical dependencies.  
- Evaluation note: VGG-based loss is a useful objective measure but has limitations and should be balanced with variability.
