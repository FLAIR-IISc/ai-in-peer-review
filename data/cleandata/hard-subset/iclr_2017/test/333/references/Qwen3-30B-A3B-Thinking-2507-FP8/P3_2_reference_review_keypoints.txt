- Challenges the assumption that deep, supervised networks are necessary for high-quality natural texture synthesis.
- Shows a single-layer CNN with random, data-independent filters can synthesize textures rivaling Gatys et al. (2015a).
- Proposes a strong minimal baseline: single-layer random-filter texture synthesis.
- Systematically compares single-layer architectures with different filter types (random, Fourier, K-means, PCA).
- Finds random filters often outperform filters trained on natural image statistics.
- Introduces a quantitative texture quality measure based on VGG loss to replace subjective evaluation.
- Provides a theoretical discussion linking results to maximum entropy texture modeling for finite-size images.
- Attributes the success of random filters to improved optimization/tuning rather than network architecture per se.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Key reason for acceptance: addresses a fundamental question about depth and pre-training in generative models.
- Key reason for acceptance: thorough experimental design with extensive comparisons and qualitative/quantitative evidence.
- Strength: significant conceptual contribution that may reshape thinking about generative texture models.
- Strength: rigorous experimental evaluation, including a multi-scale approach with up to 1024 random filters.
- Strength: objective, reproducible quantitative evaluation using VGG-based loss.
- Strength: solid theoretical grounding connecting empirical findings to established statistical frameworks.
- Weakness: lacks statistical significance testing for claimed performance differences.
- Weakness: insufficient comparisons to pre-deep-learning baselines (e.g., Portilla & Simoncelli 2000, Liu et al. 2016).
- Weakness: insufficient details about the “elaborate tuning” of the optimization procedure, hindering reproducibility.
- Suggested improvement: add statistical tests (e.g., t-tests with p-values) for VGG-loss differences.
- Suggested improvement: expand comparisons to include classical texture synthesis methods.
- Suggested improvement: provide specific optimization parameters and tuning procedures used.
- Suggested improvement: include computational cost analysis (time and memory) versus prior methods.
- Suggested improvement: clarify and detail the multi-scale implementation.
- Clarifying question: specify random filter initialization and optimization parameters and how they differ from prior work.
- Clarifying question: explain why VGG loss fails on very regular man-made textures and propose better metrics for those cases.
- Clarifying question: explain artifacts in multi-scale samples (e.g., brick wall) and compare to Gatys et al. artifacts.
- Clarifying question: is there an upper bound or diminishing returns when increasing the number of feature maps?
- Clarifying question: how does the method balance perceptual similarity versus variability, and how can variability be improved?
