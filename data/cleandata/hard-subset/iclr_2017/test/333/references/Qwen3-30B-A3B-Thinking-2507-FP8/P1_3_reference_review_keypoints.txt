- Core claim: A single convolutional layer with random filters can synthesize textures that often rival state-of-the-art methods.
- Minimal baseline: Paper introduces a simple, single-layer, data-independent filter baseline for texture synthesis.
- Depth/pre-training not essential: Shows depth and pre-trained weights are not strictly necessary for high-quality texture generation.
- Systematic comparisons: Compares random, hand-crafted, and unsupervisedly learned single-layer filters across many filter sizes and textures.
- Quantitative metric: Introduces a VGG-based synthesis loss as a quantitative texture quality measure.
- Key mechanics: Demonstrates multi-scale filters and non-linearities are crucial for good synthesis performance.
- Empirical rigor: Experiments are thorough, combining qualitative human inspection and quantitative evaluation.
- Robust analysis: Provides explanations for why random filters work and attributes earlier contradictory findings to optimization differences.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
- Requested clarification 1: Provide a deeper theoretical discussion of why random filters succeed (e.g., statistical properties, CLT, convolution effects).
- Requested clarification 2: Include concrete synthesis time vs. quality comparisons to address computational efficiency of large filters.
- Requested clarification 3: Make the MaxEnt framework discussion more concretely tied to the empirical results.
- Requested clarification 4: Improve figure clarity and captions (notably Figure 3) for easier interpretation.
- Question 1: Why do random single-layer models sometimes outperform VGG-based models—optimization or constraint differences?
- Question 2: Can the authors show examples or experiments that demonstrate single-layer models are less prone to local minima than deep models?
- Question 3: Provide examples where VGG loss fails on very regular textures and explain why perceptual advantages are missed.
- Question 4: How might these findings generalize to other image generation tasks (style transfer, object generation)?
