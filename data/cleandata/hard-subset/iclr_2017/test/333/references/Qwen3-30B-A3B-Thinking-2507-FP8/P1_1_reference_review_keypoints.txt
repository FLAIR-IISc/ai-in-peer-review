- Challenges the assumption that deep pre-trained networks are necessary for high-quality natural texture synthesis.  
- Shows a single-layer CNN with random filters can generate textures rivaling Gatys et al. (2015a).  
- Systematically compares single-layer architectures with various filter types (random, Fourier, K-means, PCA).  
- Finds random filters often outperform filters trained on natural images.  
- Introduces a multi-scale model combining different filter sizes that improves texture quality.  
- Proposes a VGG-based loss as an objective quantitative measure of texture quality.  
- Provides theoretical discussion linking results to the Maximum Entropy framework for texture modeling.  
- Decision: accept with minor revisions.  
- Main reasons for acceptance: strong, well-motivated challenge to a common assumption and rigorous experimental design.  
- Empirical claims are supported by extensive visual comparisons across textures and model configurations.  
- Multi-scale model effectively captures both small-scale and long-range structures missing in single-scale models.  
- Paper is well-situated in literature and builds on/challenges Gatys et al. (2015a).  
- VGG-based loss is a useful step toward objective evaluation, despite limitations.  
- Strength: thorough, systematic exploration of filters and scales.  
- Strength: multi-scale approach is particularly impressive and justified.  
- Suggestion: add more quantitative metrics (e.g., SSIM, LPIPS) and statistical analysis of VGG loss.  
- Suggestion: analyze and provide examples where VGG-based loss fails (e.g., regular man-made structures) and explain why.  
- Suggestion: provide runtime and memory comparisons to Gatys et al. and discuss practical computational impact.  
- Suggestion: explicitly discuss limitations—handling of non-stationary textures, strong directional features, and comparison to other recent methods (Aittala et al., Liu et al.).  
- Suggestion: improve reproducibility by detailing optimization parameters and differences from Gatys et al.'s implementation.  
- Question: provide quantitative evidence beyond visuals that random filters outperform trained filters (SSIM/LPIPS/etc.).  
- Question: give specific examples and analysis where VGG loss fails for certain textures.  
- Question: compare computational requirements (time/memory) of the multi-scale model versus VGG-based model and justify cost.  
- Question: provide evidence that the single-layer model is more robust to local minima (optimization trajectories/convergence rates).  
- Question: clarify how the approach handles non-stationary textures or transitions between patterns.
