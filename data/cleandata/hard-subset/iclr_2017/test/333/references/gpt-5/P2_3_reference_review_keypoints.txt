- Paper investigates which elements are necessary for natural texture synthesis and proposes a minimal baseline: single-layer CNN with random filters, ReLU, and Gram-matrix statistics.
- Key findings: single-layer (even random) models can synthesize high-quality textures; a multiscale variant (filters up to 55×55) often rivals VGG-based multi-layer models.
- Training filters (k-means/PCA) is not essential—random features match or outperform given enough channels.
- ReLU nonlinearity is necessary; depth improves efficiency but not synthesis quality.
- Introduces a VGG-based reconstruction loss as a proxy for perceptual similarity and frames results in a MaxEnt perspective (similarity vs. variability trade-off).
- Decision: reject.
- Primary reason for rejection: insufficient quantitative and perceptual evaluation to support “often rivals state of the art” claims.
- Secondary reason: incomplete methodological details and underspecified presentation (multiscale model, optimization, comparisons).
- Evaluation issues: reliance on qualitative figures and a VGG-based loss that may bias comparisons; no human perceptual study or cross-network evaluation.
- Lack of systematic quantification: no datasets, multiple seeds, or statistical analyses to back claims of superiority or parity.
- Variability concerns: experiments optimize for similarity only and do not quantify sample diversity or provide diversity–similarity trade-offs.
- Methodological clarity problems: missing details on multiscale model (scales, filter counts, initialization, Gram aggregation) and optimizer tuning (loss scaling, steps, iterations).
- Nonlinearity ablation incomplete: alternatives to ReLU (leaky ReLU, tanh, modulus) and padding schemes not explored.
- Fairness/efficiency concerns: no runtime or memory comparisons to VGG-based synthesis despite large filters and thousands of channels in single-layer models.
- Missing comparisons: no scattering-transform baseline and no side-by-side results with Portilla & Simoncelli.
- Presentation/writing issues: incomplete references, placeholders, overreliance on figures without robust quantification.
- Suggested evaluations: add human perceptual tests (e.g., 2AFC), cross-network metrics, quantify variability via multiple samples, and expand texture set with statistical summaries.
- Suggested method ablations: fully specify multiscale model, optimizer comparisons (L-BFGS-B vs Adam), convergence plots, and test nonlinearity variants.
- Suggested comparisons: include scattering transform and Portilla & Simoncelli under the same protocol.
- Suggested theory/framing: clarify MaxEnt discussion with an epsilon-tolerant objective or principled stochastic sampling, and discuss channels/spatial overlap effects on constraint redundancy.
- Reproducibility requests: release code, seeds, texture list, and detailed preprocessing and loss normalizations.
- Writing fixes: correct broken references, polish exposition, and move key appendix evaluation into the main text.
- Questions for authors: specify multiscale filter sizes, counts, independence across scales, and Gram aggregation method.
- Questions: report sensitivity to loss scaling, optimizer choice, iteration count, initialization, and provide convergence traces/failure cases.
- Questions: quantify diversity of multiple samples per texture and present diversity–similarity trade-offs.
- Questions: provide runtime and memory costs for single-layer multiscale vs VGG-based synthesis on standard sizes.
- Questions: do conclusions hold under evaluation with non-VGG networks (e.g., ResNet-50) or other metrics?
- Questions: how does the method compare to scattering-based synthesis on the same textures and metrics?
- Questions: do results generalize to larger textures, out-of-distribution patterns (stochastic vs regular), and grayscale vs color?
