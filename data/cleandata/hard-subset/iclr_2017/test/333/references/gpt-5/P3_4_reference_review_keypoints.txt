- Main claim: A single convolutional layer with random filters, ReLU, and Gram-matrix matching can synthesize convincing textures without depth or supervised pretraining.  
- Empirical finding: Multi-scale random CNNs (concatenating filters up to 55×55) often rival or approach VGG-based multi-layer synthesis.  
- Feature learning: Unsupervised or handcrafted filters (k-means, PCA, Fourier) do not outperform random filters; number of feature maps is more critical.  
- Key ingredients: Nonlinearity (ReLU), multi-scale receptive fields, and many channels are essential for good results.  
- Quantitative evaluation: Introduces VGG texture loss as a proxy for perceptual similarity to replace anecdotal visual inspection.  
- Conceptual framing: Provides a relaxed MaxEnt perspective explaining finite-size variability and the trade-off between perceptual similarity and sample diversity.  
- Overall contribution: Offers a strong, simple baseline that clarifies what matters in Gram-based texture synthesis.  
- Decision: Accept — conceptually significant and likely to influence future work and evaluation practices.  
- Empirical support: Broad comparisons and careful ablations across filter types, scales, channel counts, and nonlinearities.  
- Clarity: The main finding is clearly presented and often matches or beats Gatys VGG model in VGG-loss.  
- Ablation thoroughness: Systematically isolates number of channels, filter design, scale, and nonlinearity to support conclusions.  
- Methodology: Uses established Gram-matrix matching with robust optimization (L-BFGS-B, loss scaling, box constraints); initialization from shallow model improves VGG synthesis.  
- Broader implications: Reframes texture synthesis as balancing perceptual similarity and variability with practical implications for evaluation.  
- Eval bias concern: VGG-based loss may favor Gram-like statistics and miss perceptual failures, especially on man-made regular textures.  
- Suggestion — perceptual tests: Add small-scale human 2AFC or inpainting psychophysics, or report LPIPS with multiple backbones and nearest-neighbor analysis.  
- Diversity metrics: Recommend quantitative diversity measures across seeds (distribution of VGG-loss, inter-sample distances, color/texture histogram coverage).  
- Optimization sensitivity: Study sensitivity to large loss/gradient scaling (1e7), box constraints, iterations, optimizers (Adam vs L-BFGS), and initialization schemes.  
- Architectural clarity: Precisely specify multi-scale layer details (filter sizes, filters per scale, channel mixing) and report runtime/memory vs VGG.  
- Comparisons: Add scattering-based (Bruna & Mallat) and feedforward baselines (TextureNet/Ulyanov) to situate results among non-learned and fast methods.  
- Color handling: Report how color coupling vs per-channel processing affects results and whether color histogram matching post-processing helps.  
- Reproducibility: Release code, filter initialization seeds, and trained k-means/PCA filters or detail computational costs for reproduction.  
- Question — multi-scale specifics: What filter sizes and filter counts per scale were used, and are filters depthwise or full RGB?  
- Question — nonlinearity variants: Were leaky-ReLU, absolute value, or squaring tested and how do they affect performance/diversity?  
- Question — optimization details: How critical are 1e7 loss scaling and [0,1] box constraints; provide convergence curves and success rates.  
- Question — diversity quantification: For ~10 seeds per texture, what are VGG-loss variances and inter-sample distances; do samples collapse for regular textures?  
- Question — initialization effects on VGG: Can VGG-based synthesis match the lower VGG-loss of random models with different initializations/optimizers or longer runs?  
- Question — scattering comparison: How does the multi-scale random model compare to fixed wavelet/scattering features under the same optimization?  
- Question — exact reconstructions: Under what conditions (feature count, image size) do deeper random CNNs give exact reconstructions and how does DOF vs constraints trade-off behave?
