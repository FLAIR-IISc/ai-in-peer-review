- Main claim: depth and supervised pretraining are not essential for convincing texture synthesis; a single-layer CNN with Gram matrices and random ReLU filters can work, and a multiscale single-layer often rivals VGG-based multi-layer methods.
- Systematic comparison: evaluates single-layer architectures with random, Fourier, k-means/PCA filters and different channel couplings and feature map counts.
- Key findings: multiscale filters and ReLU nonlinearity are crucial; random filters match or outperform learned/hand-crafted banks given enough channels.
- Quantitative proposal: introduces a VGG-based average Gram-matrix reconstruction loss as a quantitative texture-quality metric and discusses MaxEnt and the similarity–variability trade-off.
- Decision: Weak accept — the work provides a surprising, practical baseline that challenges assumptions and is likely influential.
- Motivation and positioning: well motivated to strip down Gatys et al. and well placed in texture literature with thoughtful MaxEnt connections.
- Methodological clarity: model, optimization, and normalization/scaling details are described precisely and the filter-bank comparisons are diverse and fair.
- Multiscale ablation: shows multiscale filters capture small-, mid-, and long-range statistics, explaining their advantage over single-scale.
- Empirical support: evaluates many textures and variants; qualitative results support claims that more channels help, random ≈ learned, multiscale improves, and ReLU is necessary.
- Optimization insight: initializing VGG synthesis from single-layer results suggests optimization, not just representation, affects VGG outcomes.
- Metric limitation: the primary VGG-based metric is not independent of the baseline and may not correlate with human perception for regular man-made textures.
- Evaluation gaps: no psychophysical user study, no diversity/entropy measures, and limited aggregate statistics (means/variances) across textures.
- Compute analysis: compute/memory costs for very large spatial filters are discussed qualitatively but not rigorously benchmarked.
- Suggested evaluation improvements: report mean/median VGG-loss with confidence intervals across the full set, add a small psychophysical test, and show diversity across random seeds.
- Complementary metrics: recommend additional perceptual proxies beyond SSIM and pixel L2 (e.g., other feature-space distances).
- Reproducibility requests: release code and exact configurations (filter shapes/counts, padding, normalizations, seeds) and runtime/memory comparisons.
- Normalization clarity: request clarification on filter normalization, per-feature-map normalization, and Gram normalization constants.
- Further ablations requested: number of channels vs quality, alternative nonlinearities (abs, leaky ReLU), color spaces, strides/pooling, and DC removal effects.
- Optimization sensitivity: quantify iterations vs loss, effect of the 1e7 scaling, and whether early stopping trades variability for acceptable quality.
- Theory/analysis: tighten discussion on why random multiscale filters + Gram suffice (links to random feature theory/JL) and provide evidence for VGG local minima causing paradoxical results.
- Scope caveat: suggest reframing “minimal” to emphasize “structurally simple and training-free” rather than minimal in parameter count or receptive field size.
- Multiscale details question: what exact filter sizes and counts per scale were used and how sensitive are results to this allocation?
- Filter normalization question: were filters normalized (unit L2) and was any activation normalization applied before Gram computation?
- Nonlinearity question: were absolute value or other nonlinearities tested and is ReLU sparsity the key factor?
- Optimization robustness question: how robust are results to L-BFGS-B settings and the 1e7 scaling, and could Adam/SGD match performance?
- Variability question: how diverse are samples across seeds at matched VGG-loss and can this be quantified/visualized?
- Generalization and failure modes: how does the method handle non-stationary textures or images with global structure and what are typical failure cases?
- Efficiency question: can shallow hierarchical multiscale designs (e.g., dilated convs) retain quality while reducing large-kernel cost?
- Metric dependence question: do conclusions (random ≥ learned; multiscale > single-scale) hold under different perceptual proxies or scattering-based metrics?
