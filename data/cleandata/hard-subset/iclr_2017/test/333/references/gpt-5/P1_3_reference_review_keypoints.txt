- Paper proposes a minimal texture synthesis baseline: single convolutional layer + ReLU + Gram matrix over random (and multi-scale) filters, claiming results often rival multi-layer VGG Gram models.
- Decision: Reject (borderline).

Main criticisms
- Insufficient quantitative, unbiased evaluation to support “rivals state-of-the-art” claims; over-reliance on VGG-based loss risks bias.
- Limited novelty: approach is close in spirit to Gatys et al. and prior fixed-filter methods, offering a useful baseline but modest theoretical/algorithmic advance.
- Primary metric (multi-layer VGG Gram loss) can favor overfitting and does not measure perceptual quality or sample diversity.
- Missing direct comparisons to scattering/wavelet or spectrum-constrained fixed-basis methods that would contextualize contributions.
- Several important factors are not systematically quantified: feature-count vs quality, compute vs quality, and sensitivity to initialization/optimization/hyperparameters.
- Diversity vs similarity trade-off is discussed but not measured; no inter-sample diversity metrics or psychophysical tests are provided.
- Some architectural/experimental details are under-specified (multi-scale config, maps per scale, image sizes, runtime/memory), and references/formats have issues.

Suggested improvements
- Add unbiased perceptual evaluation (small-scale human study such as 2AFC or inpainting discrimination) or multiple objective metrics (LPIPS, DISTS) to support claims.
- Measure and report diversity (e.g., inter-sample VGG distances, patch statistics, variability visualizations).
- Include comparisons to scattering-transform and spectrum-constrained synthesis baselines.
- Provide ablation/scaling curves quantifying performance vs number of feature maps and receptive field size, and test alternative nonlinearities and pooling.
- Analyze optimization sensitivity (optimizer choice, loss/gradient scaling, convergence/stability), and report run times and memory for variants.
- Clarify and detail the multi-scale design, normalization of Gram matrices, and release code and dataset for reproducibility.
- Make the finite-size MaxEnt discussion concrete by specifying/testing an epsilon-tolerance scheme linking epsilon to diversity/similarity.

Questions for authors (to address in revision)
- What exact multi-scale configuration was used (filter sizes, maps per scale, feature normalization across scales)?
- How sensitive are results to gradient/loss scaling and to optimizer choice (L-BFGS-B vs Adam); is the method stable across runs?
- How many textures were evaluated, what sizes/content diversity, and can results be reported averaged with variance?
- Can you provide inter-sample diversity metrics (pairwise VGG-loss distances) and compare them to VGG-based synthesis?
- How do your models compare quantitatively and qualitatively to scattering-transform and spectrum-constrained methods?
- What are the computational costs (time/memory) per synthesis for single-scale, multi-scale, and hierarchical variants?
- Did you try other nonlinearities (absolute value/modulus, leaky ReLU) or pooling strategies, and what were the effects?
- How does performance scale with image size and number of feature maps, and are there failure modes (e.g., non-stationary or highly structured patterns)?
