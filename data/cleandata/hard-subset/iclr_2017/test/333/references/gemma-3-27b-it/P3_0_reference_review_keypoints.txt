- Summary: Single-layer CNNs with random filters can generate high-quality natural textures rivaling state-of-the-art; paper compares single-layer architectures and introduces a VGG-based quantitative loss.

- Decision: Recommend accept with minor revisions — convincing experiments and valuable contributions.

- Novelty & Impact: Challenges the need for deep, pre-trained features and proposes a minimal, influential baseline for texture synthesis.

- Strong Empirical Evidence: Comprehensive visual and quantitative comparisons show the random multi-scale model often matches or surpasses VGG-based results; ablations confirm importance of multi-scale filters and non-linearities.

- Sound Methodology: Experiments are reproducible, use L-BFGS-B optimization with careful parameter tuning, and benchmark against established methods.

- Theoretical Discussion: Provides insight into parametric texture synthesis, MaxEnt connections, and the trade-off between perceptual similarity and variability.

- Optimization Details (feedback): Request clearer explanation of loss/gradient scaling (choice of 10^7) and the tuning procedure.

- Computational Cost (feedback): Ask for runtime and computational cost comparisons of the multi-scale model versus VGG-based approaches.

- Qualitative vs Quantitative Discrepancies (feedback): Request more examples and discussion where VGG-based loss diverges from human perception and potential causes.

- Generalization to Other Domains (feedback): Suggest discussing whether findings extend to style transfer, image completion, or other generative tasks.

- Human Evaluation (feedback): Recommend a small-scale user study to validate perceptual quality beyond VGG-based metrics.

- Questions for Authors: Clarify L-BFGS-B tuning and scaling factor; provide synthesis runtimes; give examples where VGG loss misaligns with perception and speculate reasons; discuss implications for other generative tasks.

- Overall Conclusion: Well-written, impactful paper with counterintuitive results; accept with minor revisions.
