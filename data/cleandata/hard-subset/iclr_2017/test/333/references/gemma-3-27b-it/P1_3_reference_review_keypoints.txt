- Single-layer CNNs with random filters can generate high-quality textures rivaling deeper, pre-trained models.
- Authors systematically compare filter types and sizes and advocate a multi-scale random-filter approach.
- Introduces a VGG-based quantitative evaluation metric and links results to maximum entropy models.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Novelty: Challenges the belief that deep pre-training is necessary for texture synthesis.
- Strong empirical evidence with comprehensive experiments and informative ablations.
- VGG-based loss provides objective metrics but can sometimes favor synthesized images, indicating limitations.
- Theoretical discussion connects findings to maximum entropy frameworks and future directions.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and visually supported by helpful figures.
- Request more detail on optimization tuning (exact parameters and changes) for reproducibility.
- Ask for quantitative comparisons of computational cost (training/synthesis time) across architectures.
- Recommend a small-scale human study to validate the VGG-based metric against perceived quality.
- Suggest discussing failure cases: which textures single-layer models struggle with and why.
- Recommend expanding related-work discussion to include GANs and diffusion models (quality, speed, cost).
- Questions for authors: specifics of optimization differences from Gatys et al., how filter choices affect perceptual similarity vs variability, why VGG loss can be misleading, whether code will be released, and potential generalization beyond textures.
