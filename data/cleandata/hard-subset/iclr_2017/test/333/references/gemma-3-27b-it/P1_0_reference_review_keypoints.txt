- Paper shows single-layer CNNs with random filters can synthesize high-quality textures, challenging the need for deep, pre-trained networks.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Main result is strong and counter-intuitive, warranting publication.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing multiple single-layer architectures, filter sizes, and a multi-scale random CNN.
- Introduces a novel quantitative texture-quality metric based on VGG synthesis loss as an objective alternative to human evaluation.
- Provides an insightful theoretical discussion linking results to maximum entropy models and the trade-off between perceptual similarity and variability.
- Implementation details are reasonably clear, suggesting good reproducibility.
- Request more clarity on the optimization procedure and what specific tuning improved simpler models over Gatys et al.
- Suggest increasing the number and diversity of visual texture comparisons (Fig. 4).
- Ask to quantify computational cost (training and synthesis time) versus VGG-based methods.
- Recommend an error analysis describing texture types where single-layer models fail.
- Propose exploring simple methods to increase variability in generated samples (e.g., noise, stopping criterion).
- Question: Provide specific details on optimization tuning and whether L-BFGS-B itself was modified or only hyperparameters.
- Question: Explain how scales were combined in the multi-scale model (concatenation vs. more sophisticated fusion).
- Question: Report whether K-means filter results generalize when pre-trained on different datasets.
- Question: Describe specific texture types where the VGG-based loss fails and suggest alternative objective metrics.
- Question: Clarify whether and when the authors will release code.
