- Summary: Shows single-layer CNNs with random or simple filters can synthesize natural textures rivaling deep pre-trained VGG-based methods; proposes multi-scale random filters and a VGG-based reconstruction loss; relates findings to maximum entropy modeling.

- Decision: Accept — surprising, well-supported result that challenges assumptions about needing deep pre-trained networks for texture synthesis.

- Strong empirical results: Single-layer, especially multi-scale random filter models, produce visual quality comparable to Gatys et al. (2015a), supported by compelling figures.

- Systematic exploration: Thorough comparison of Fourier, K-means, PCA, and random single-layer architectures clarifies the role of filter design and capacity.

- Multi-scale insight: Multi-scale random filters particularly effective, suggesting scale diversity is key to texture quality.

- Novel quantitative evaluation: Adoption of a VGG-based reconstruction loss as an objective metric improves reproducibility and comparability; supplemental SSIM comparisons add support.

- Theoretical discussion: Thoughtful connection to maximum entropy frameworks and trade-offs between perceptual similarity and image variability.

- Clarity and presentation: The paper is well-written, with clear methodology and concise results.

- Ablation request: Need more detailed ablation on optimization (L-BFGS-B parameters) to assess sensitivity of results, especially for random filters.

- Computational cost: Provide quantitative comparisons of computational cost (training/synthesis time) versus VGG-based approaches, as multi-scale model may be inefficient.

- Generalization: Evaluate on a broader set of natural texture datasets (e.g., material databases) to test generality beyond the limited textures used.

- Filter size rationale: Explain or justify the choice of 11x11 filters and whether a principled method exists to select optimal filter sizes per texture.

- Question on scaling factor: Clarify the rationale and necessity of the 10^7 scaling factor in the loss and any numerical stability concerns.

- Question on VGG loss metric: Discuss potential improvements to better align the VGG-based metric with human perception, possibly via additional statistical texture features.

- Question on MaxEnt and optimization: Explain whether observed constraint mismatches stem from the optimization algorithm (L-BFGS-B) or are inherent to the random filter approach.

- Future directions: Recommend outlining next steps and specific application areas (e.g., procedural texture generation, style mixing) where these insights could be impactful.

- Conclusion: Strong, rigorous paper with a compelling message whose acceptance would be a valuable contribution.
