- Summary: Single-layer CNNs with random or simple filters (especially multi-scale) can synthesize natural textures competitive with deep, pre-trained models; includes VGG-based quantitative metric and MaxEnt discussion.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions due to strong, surprising results and solid evaluation.  
- Novelty/Impact: Demonstrates that depth and pre-training may be unnecessary for texture synthesis, challenging common assumptions.  
- Thorough Evaluation: Comprehensive comparisons across single-layer architectures, controlling filter size and learning method.  
- Quantitative Evaluation: Introduces a VGG-based loss as an objective metric (supplemented by SSIM comparisons).  
- Strong Baseline: Provides a valuable, simple baseline for future texture synthesis work.  
- Writing/Structure: Clear and well-organized manuscript.  
- Suggestion — Optimization Details: Provide more detail and sensitivity analysis on the “elaborate tuning” of optimization (learning rates, schedules, regularization).  
- Suggestion — Computational Cost: Include quantitative wall-clock comparisons (training/synthesis time) between multi-scale random filters and VGG-based approaches.  
- Suggestion — Visuals of Optimization: Add side-by-side intermediate optimization images (e.g., L-BFGS-B iterations) for random-filter vs VGG models.  
- Suggestion — VGG-Loss Limitations: Expand discussion of VGG-loss limitations and consider alternative perceptual metrics.  
- Suggestion — MaxEnt Connection: More explicitly link MaxEnt “mismatch in constraints” to the empirical behavior observed.  
- Question 1: What specific optimization parameters were tuned relative to Gatys et al. (2015a), and how did they affect synthesis quality?  
- Question 2: What is the wall-clock time to synthesize a 256×256 texture for the multi-scale random model versus the VGG model?  
- Question 3: Which alternative perceptual metrics were considered and why was VGG-loss chosen for quantitative evaluation?  
- Question 4: Why do random filters work so well—large filter count vs inherent properties of randomness?  
- Question 5: Can you give a concrete example of how allowing constraint “mismatch” in the MaxEnt framework manifests in the generated textures?
