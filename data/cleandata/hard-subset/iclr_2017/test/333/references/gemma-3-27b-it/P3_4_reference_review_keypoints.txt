- Main finding: A single-layer CNN with random filters can generate high-quality natural textures, often rivaling VGG/Gatys results.  
- Methodology: Systematic comparison of single-layer architectures (Fourier, K-means, PCA, random) with a VGG-based loss for quantitative evaluation.  
- Optimization: Demonstrates that careful optimization tuning is crucial for simpler models; multi-scale models explored.  
- Theory: Connects results to maximum entropy models and discusses the trade-off between perceptual similarity and variability.  
- Decision: Accept with Minor Revisions — significant, counter-intuitive contribution backed by compelling experiments.  
- Novelty/Impact: Challenges the assumption that deep pre-trained networks are required for texture synthesis and simplifies the approach.  
- Evaluation strength: Introduces a quantitative VGG-based metric and compares against the established Gatys et al. baseline.  
- Experimental breadth: Extensive ablation over filter types, sizes, and multi-scale configurations.  
- Theoretical shortcoming: MaxEnt connection is promising but treated superficially and needs deeper analysis.  
- Optimization concern: The role and universality of the 1e7 scaling factor and hyperparameter sensitivity are insufficiently explained.  
- Statistical concern: VGG-loss comparisons lack statistical tests or confidence intervals to demonstrate significance.  
- Computational concern: No clear discussion of computational cost of the multi-scale model versus VGG-based approaches.  
- Presentation issue: Some figures (e.g., Fig. 2) need clearer labeling and larger images for easier visual comparison.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to scaling factor; statistical validation of VGG-loss differences; detailed MaxEnt deviation explanation; analysis of random filter properties; generalization to diverse texture domains.
