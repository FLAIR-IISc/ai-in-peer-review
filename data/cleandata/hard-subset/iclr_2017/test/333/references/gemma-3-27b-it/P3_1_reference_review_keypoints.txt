- Main claim: A single-layer CNN with random filters can produce texture synthesis results competitive with deep, pre-trained networks.  
- Methods: Systematic comparison of single-layer variants (Fourier, k-means, PCA, random) and a multi-scale model; VGG-based reconstruction loss used as a quantitative metric.  
- Theoretical angle: Discusses Maximum Entropy framing and the trade-off between perceptual similarity and variability in texture modeling.  
- Decision: Recommend accept with minor revisions due to strong, counterintuitive findings and solid experiments.  

- Novelty/impact: Challenges the assumption that deep pre-training and hierarchical features are essential for generative texture quality.  
- Empirical strength: Comprehensive comparisons and convincing visual/quantitative results showing random filters work well.  
- Methodological rigor: Careful control of experiments, reasonable choice of VGG loss proxy, and proper optimization (L-BFGS-B) tuning.  
- Broader discussion: Paper goes beyond engineering to discuss theoretical implications and limitations.
