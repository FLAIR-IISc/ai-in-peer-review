- Core finding: single-layer CNNs with random filters can synthesize high-quality natural textures, challenging the necessity of deep, pre-trained networks.  
- Experimental scope: extensive comparison of single-layer architectures (Fourier, k-means, PCA, random) against Gatys et al. (2015a).  
- Metric contribution: introduces a VGG-based quantitative evaluation metric and discusses a generalization of maximum entropy models.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — result is surprising, significant, and well-supported.  
- Strong empirical evaluation: systematic tests over filter types/sizes, compelling visual comparisons (Figures 1 and 4), and objective quantitative loss.  
- Literature placement: well-motivated context and clear contrasts with GANs, PixelCNNs, and style transfer methods.  
- Methodological rigor: detailed architectures, optimization procedure (L-BFGS-B with tuning), implementation details, and justification for optimization scaling.  
- VGG-loss advantage: reduces reliance on subjective judgment but has acknowledged limitations.  
- Requested improvement: deeper discussion of what optimization parameters were tuned and sensitivity to those choices.  
- Requested improvement: further analysis of VGG-loss limitations and failure cases; consider alternative learned perceptual metrics.  
- Requested improvement: more detailed computational cost analysis across architectures (multi-scale inefficiency noted).  
- Requested improvement: report statistical significance for quantitative comparisons (e.g., paired t-tests).  
- Requested improvement: clarify some mathematical notation for readability.  
- Question for authors: detail which optimization parameters (learning rates, momentum, etc.) were adjusted and their impact.  
- Question for authors: any empirical evidence for finite-size effects causing exact reconstruction in deeper models?  
- Question for authors: have you tried optimization/regularization to trade off perceptual similarity for greater variability?  
- Question for authors: besides SSIM and VGG-loss, were other perceptual metrics evaluated or compared?
