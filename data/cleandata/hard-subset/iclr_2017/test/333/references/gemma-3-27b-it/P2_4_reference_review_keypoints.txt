- Single-layer CNNs with random filters can synthesize natural textures nearly as well as deep, pre-trained models.
- The authors systematically evaluate single-layer variants (Fourier, K-means, PCA, Random) across filter sizes/numbers and propose a VGG-based synthesis loss as a quantitative metric.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — strong empirical results, but MaxEnt theoretical discussion needs strengthening.
- Novelty: Challenges the necessity of depth and pre-training for texture synthesis, suggesting simpler, cheaper alternatives.
- Experiments: Thorough comparisons to Gatys et al., extensive ablations (e.g., multi-scale vs single-scale) and insightful analysis.
- Quantitative evaluation: VGG-based loss provides objective comparison and reproducibility, though its perceptual limits are acknowledged.
- Presentation: Well-written paper with clear explanations and informative figures.
- Theory concern: MaxEnt discussion feels disconnected from experiments; needs clearer treatment of finite-size effects and relation to “soft constraints.”
- Optimization concern: The use of a 1e7 scaling factor on loss/gradients requires justification and sensitivity analysis.
- Visuals suggestion: Include more example syntheses and failure cases (e.g., in an appendix) to illustrate method limits and variability.
- Computational cost: Add comparisons of compute requirements across architectures and versus VGG-based methods.
- Questions for authors: justify the 1e7 scaling factor and its sensitivity; explain why multi-scale outputs can have lower VGG loss yet differ perceptually; report sensitivity to random filter initialization; clarify implications of allowing small mismatches in MaxEnt constraints.
