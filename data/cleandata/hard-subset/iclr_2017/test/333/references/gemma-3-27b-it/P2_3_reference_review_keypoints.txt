- Main claim: A single-layer CNN with random filters can generate high-quality natural textures comparable to deep pre-trained methods.  
- Scope: Systematic comparison of single-layer architectures (Fourier, k-means, PCA, random) and emphasis on optimization tuning.  
- Metric/Modeling: Introduces a VGG-based quantitative loss for texture evaluation and a MaxEnt generalization to model texture variability.  
- Decision: Recommend accept with minor revisions due to strong, counterintuitive results and thorough experiments.  
- Novelty/Impact: Challenges the assumption that depth and pre-training are necessary for texture synthesis; significant for generative modeling.  
- Experimental rigor: Comprehensive architecture comparisons, detailed configurations, and inclusion of a quantitative evaluation metric.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and includes clear figures and methodology descriptions.  
- Theoretical grounding: Links empirical findings to maximum entropy framework and acknowledges finite-image limitations.  
- Optimization feedback: Request clearer explanation of loss/gradient scaling and rationale for the 10^7 factor.  
- Filter-size feedback: Suggest visualizations of receptive fields overlaid on textures to support multi-scale filter claims.  
- Computational cost feedback: Ask for discussion or relative comparison of computational cost, especially for large multi-scale models.  
- VGG-loss limitations feedback: Recommend expanding on which textures show discrepancies between perceptual quality and VGG-loss.  
- Related work feedback: Advise adding discussion comparing to simpler generative/Markov Random Field texture models.  
- Q1 for authors: Clarify sensitivity to the 10^7 scaling factor and effects of alternative values.  
- Q2 for authors: Report computational time comparison between multi-scale, VGG-based, and other single-layer models.  
- Q3 for authors: Provide specific examples (e.g., grids) where VGG-loss fails and explain why.  
- Q4 for authors: Describe any methods tried to explicitly balance perceptual similarity and variability.  
- Q5 for authors: Discuss implications of findings for other generative tasks (image completion, super-resolution).  
- Overall recommendation: Addressing these points would strengthen the paper and increase its impact.
