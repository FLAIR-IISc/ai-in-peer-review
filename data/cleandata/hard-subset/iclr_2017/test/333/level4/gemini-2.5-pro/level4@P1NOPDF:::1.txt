This paper proposes a simple yet powerful baseline for parametric texture synthesis. In empirical experiments, samples generated by this baseline—which is composed of multi-scale random filters—sometimes rival those from VGG-based models that use multi-layer, pre-trained filters. Based on these results, the authors conclude that texture synthesis does not necessarily depend on deep hierarchical representations or learned features.

This work is interesting and insightful. However, its conclusions require further investigation, especially regarding the claim about deep hierarchical representations. 

First, the samples generated by both the VGG model and the proposed single-layer model are imperfect and are of significantly lower quality than results from non-parametric methods. This suggests that neither model is fully capturing the necessary statistics for high-fidelity synthesis.

Second, the VGG-based model appears to perform better on the inpainting task, as shown in Figure 7, indicating that the learned, hierarchical features may still offer advantages in certain applications.

Finally, a key question remains unaddressed: could a hierarchical model handle multi-scale information more efficiently than the proposed method, which relies on a large bank of filters of varying sizes? This point warrants further discussion and comparison.
