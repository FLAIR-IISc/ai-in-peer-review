- Proposes a simple parametric baseline (multi-scale random filters) that sometimes rivals VGG-based models.
- Authors conclude texture synthesis may not require deep hierarchical or learned features.
- Reviewer finds the conclusion premature and needing further investigation.
- Both VGG and the proposed model produce imperfect samples that are significantly worse than non-parametric methods, implying neither captures necessary statistics for high-fidelity synthesis.
- VGG-based model appears to perform better on inpainting (Fig.7), suggesting learned hierarchical features can still be advantageous.
- Key unaddressed question: could hierarchical models handle multi-scale information more efficiently than using a large bank of varied filter sizes—needs further comparison and discussion.
