This work proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis. In empirical experiments, samples generated by the baseline, which is composed of multi-scale random filters, sometimes rival those from a VGG-based model that utilizes multi-layer, pre-trained filters. Based on these results, the authors conclude that texture synthesis does not necessarily depend on deep hierarchical representations or learned features.

This work is interesting and insightful. However, the paper's conclusions, particularly regarding the necessity of deep hierarchical representations, require further validation.

First, the samples generated by both the VGG-based and the single-layer models are imperfect and appear qualitatively inferior to results from non-parametric methods. This makes it difficult to draw strong conclusions by comparing two models that are both far from the state-of-the-art. Second, the VGG-based model appears to perform better on the inpainting task, as shown in Figure 7, which contradicts the main claim. Finally, it is worth considering whether a hierarchical model could handle multi-scale information more efficiently than the proposed approach, which relies on a large number of filters of varying sizes.
