- Proposes a simple multi-scale random-filter baseline that sometimes rivals VGG-based pre-trained multi-layer models and concludes deep hierarchical/learned features may be unnecessary for texture synthesis.
- The work is interesting and insightful but its conclusions require additional validation.
- Both the VGG and single-layer models produce imperfect samples that are qualitatively inferior to non-parametric methods, weakening comparisons between them.
- The VGG-based model appears to perform better on the inpainting task (Figure 7), which contradicts the main claim.
- A hierarchical model might handle multi-scale information more efficiently than the proposed approach, which relies on many filters of varying sizes.
