- Proposal: A simple baseline for parametric texture synthesis using a single layer of random, multi-scale filters can produce samples competitive with a VGG-based model.  
- Contribution: The paper is interesting and insightful, challenging the prevailing reliance on complex, pre-trained deep networks through a strong, simple alternative.  
- Overstated claim: The conclusion that deep hierarchical and learned features are unnecessary may be too strong and needs more justification.  
- Quality concern: Both the proposed baseline and the VGG model show visible artifacts and lag behind state-of-the-art non-parametric methods, complicating claims of equivalence.  
- Inpainting gap: The VGG-based model performs noticeably better on inpainting, suggesting hierarchical features capture longer-range or semantic information useful for related tasks.  
- Efficiency/scalability question: The baseline’s large bank of multi-scale filters may be less parameter- or compute-efficient than hierarchical models; authors should discuss whether deep architectures offer a more efficient way to capture multi-scale features.
