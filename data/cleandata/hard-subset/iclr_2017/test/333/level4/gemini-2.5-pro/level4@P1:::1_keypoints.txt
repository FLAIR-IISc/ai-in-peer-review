- Paper proposes a simple baseline: a single convolutional layer with random, multi-scale filters for parametric texture synthesis, claiming it rivals deep pre-trained VGG methods and questions the need for hierarchical/learned features.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection because the central claims are not sufficiently supported by evidence.  
- Major critique: the comparison fails to isolate the role of hierarchy since models differ in both depth and filter type; a controlled comparison (e.g., deep model with random filters) is needed.  
- Authors’ brief mention of hierarchical random models is insufficient; detailed experiments and analysis should be included in the main paper.  
- Qualitative results are mixed: the single-layer model is sometimes impressive but not consistently superior to VGG, and VGG appears better on the inpainting task, suggesting it captures longer-range structure.  
- Generated samples show noticeable artifacts and do not reach the quality of top non-parametric texture methods, weakening claims of superiority.  
- Quantitative concern: the single-layer model sometimes attains lower VGG loss than the VGG model itself, raising doubts about the reliability of the VGG-based metric and possible optimization issues.  
- Requested clarifications: which hierarchical architectures were tried, and could optimization difficulty for deeper random models explain lack of improvement?  
- Suggested improvement: provide deeper analysis of the VGG-loss metric, optimization behavior, and controlled experiments isolating hierarchy vs. learned filters.
