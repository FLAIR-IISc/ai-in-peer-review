- Study compares deep vs. shallow networks within Gatys et al. Gram-matrix texture synthesis framework.  
- Key finding: very shallow, single-layer random-weight filter banks can perform surprisingly well for texture synthesis.  
- For simple/regular textures, shallow networks can produce visually superior results to data-trained networks like VGG-19.  
- Figure 4: optimizing with a shallow filter bank can yield textures with lower VGG-19 loss than directly optimizing the VGG-19 objective.  
- Explanation: VGG-19’s highly non-linear cost is difficult to optimize, which may favor shallower objectives.  
- Supplementary: initializing VGG-19 optimization from the shallow-network output produces improved results.  
- Main limitation: lack of systematic, quantitative comparisons against an objective that measures capturing the texture distribution.  
- Concern: shallow statistics can trivially reproduce a reference texture, but that doesn’t prove they capture the texture distribution or diversity.  
- Proposed metrics (entropy) are reasonable but hard to implement and don’t capture visual quality; psychophysical evaluation is suggested as necessary future work.  
- Overall: empirical strengths justify ICLR publication, but psychophysical assessment would substantively strengthen the claims.
