This work proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis. In empirical experiments, samples generated by the baseline model, which is composed of a single layer of multi-scale random filters, are shown to be competitive with those from a VGG-based model that utilizes multiple layers of pre-trained filters. From these results, the authors conclude that texture synthesis does not necessarily depend on deep hierarchical representations or learned features.

This work is interesting and insightful. However, its conclusions, particularly concerning the necessity of deep hierarchical representations, require further validation. Firstly, the samples generated by both the VGG-based and the single-layer models are visually imperfect and are of considerably lower quality than results from non-parametric methods. Secondly, the VGG-based model appears to perform better on the inpainting task presented in Figure 7. Lastly, it is worth considering whether a hierarchical model might handle multi-scale structures more efficiently than an approach that relies on a large bank of filters with varying sizes.
