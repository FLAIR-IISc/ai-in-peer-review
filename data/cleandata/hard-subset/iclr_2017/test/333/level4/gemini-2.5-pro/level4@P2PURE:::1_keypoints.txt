- Proposes a simple, strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis using a single layer of multi-scale random filters.  
- Empirical results show this baseline is competitive with a VGG-based model that uses multiple layers of pre-trained filters.  
- Authors conclude texture synthesis may not require deep hierarchical representations or learned features.  
- Review finds the work interesting and insightful.  
- Reviewer argues the conclusions need further validation.  
- Both VGG-based and single-layer samples are visually imperfect and notably lower quality than non-parametric methods.  
- The VGG-based model appears to perform better on the inpainting task (Figure 7).  
- A hierarchical model might handle multi-scale structures more efficiently than using a large bank of varied-size filters.
