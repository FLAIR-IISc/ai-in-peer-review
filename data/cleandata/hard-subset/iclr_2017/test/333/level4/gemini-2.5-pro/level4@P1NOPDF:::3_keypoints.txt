- Gatys et al. framework: Gram matrices of deep features are excellent texture descriptors.  
- Paper studies which deep or shallow networks work best within that framework.  
- Key finding: very shallow networks with a single random filter bank perform surprisingly well.  
- For simple/regular textures, shallow models can produce results visually superior to VGG-19.  
- Paper provides an informative discussion of strengths and limitations of these methods.  
- Fig. 4: optimizing with shallow filter banks can yield lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization, attributed to VGG-19’s difficult non-linear optimization.  
- Supplementary: initializing VGG-19 optimization from shallow-network output improves results.  
- Main limitation: no systematic comparison against a quantifiable objective for texture distribution matching.  
- Trivial shallow statistics could exactly reproduce reference textures, but the true goal is capturing a distribution, which remains unmeasured.  
- Authors propose entropy for diversity measurement but note practical difficulties and that entropy misses visual quality; psychophysical tests suggested but deferred.  
- Overall: empirical findings and analysis merit ICLR acceptance, but central claims need psychophysical evaluation to be strengthened.
