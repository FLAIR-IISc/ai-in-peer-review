- Presents a thorough, insightful analysis of conditions for generating naturalistic textures with surprising, well-supported findings.  
- Main strength is a clear, detailed dissection proposing sufficient conditions for high-quality texture synthesis.  
- Claims are substantiated by comprehensive experiments demonstrating the central thesis.  
- Counter-intuitive result: certain statistical properties matter more for perceptual quality than generator architectural complexity.  
- Exposition is clear and makes the core ideas easy to follow.  
- Evaluation is weak: relies heavily on qualitative visual inspection and needs more quantitative metrics.  
- Recommends adding established perceptual metrics or a larger-scale, standardized user study for more objective validation.  
- Limitation does not significantly detract; the analysis opens promising avenues for future research.  
- Insights could lead to more efficient and principled generative models beyond textures.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper, recommend acceptance.
