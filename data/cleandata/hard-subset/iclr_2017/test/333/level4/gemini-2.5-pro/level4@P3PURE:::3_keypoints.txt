- Gatys et al.’s framework shows Gram-matrix correlations of deep features characterize visual textures; this paper studies which deep/shallow networks work within that framework.
- Main finding: very shallow networks (single random filter bank) perform surprisingly well for texture synthesis.
- For simple/regular textures, shallow random filters can produce results visually superior to complex, data-adapted filters like VGG-19.
- The paper discusses strengths and limitations of these texture synthesis methods in an informative way.
- Figure 4: optimizing with shallow filter banks can yield lower VGG-19 loss than directly optimizing the VGG-19 objective.
- Explanation: VGG-19’s highly non-linear cost is harder to optimize; initializing VGG-19 optimization with shallow results improves outcomes (shown in supplement).
- Main limitation: no systematic, quantifiable comparison across methods; hard to verify capture of texture distributions versus trivial exact-copy statistics.
- Diversity measurement (e.g., entropy) is suggested but difficult to implement and still wouldn’t capture visual quality; psychophysical assessment is proposed but deferred.
- Overall recommendation: paper is strong enough for ICLR publication, but a psychophysical evaluation would better substantiate the claims.
