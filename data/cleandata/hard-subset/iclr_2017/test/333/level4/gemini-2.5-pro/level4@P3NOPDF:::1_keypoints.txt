- Proposes a simple baseline for parametric texture synthesis using a single layer of random, multi-scale filters.
- Empirical results show this model often produces textures qualitatively competitive with a VGG-based, multi-layer pretrained model.
- Authors conclude that deep hierarchical representations and learned feature maps may not be necessary for texture synthesis.
- Paper is insightful and provides a valuable, carefully controlled sanity-check baseline for the community.
- Concern: the conclusion that hierarchical representations are unnecessary may be overstated and needs more investigation.
- Both the proposed and VGG-based models show visible artifacts and lag behind non-parametric state-of-the-art, weakening the claim.
- Inpainting results indicate the VGG-based model produces superior structured/large-scale patterns, which counters the main claim.
- The paper should acknowledge VGG’s benefits for tasks needing larger receptive fields or semantic understanding.
- The baseline achieves multi-scale analysis via a large filter bank; hierarchical CNNs may offer more efficient, scalable multi-scale representation.
- Recommend moderating claims and adding a nuanced discussion on the role and efficiency of hierarchical representations.
