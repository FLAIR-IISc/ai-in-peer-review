- Paper proposes a simple yet powerful baseline for parametric texture synthesis using multi-scale random filters.
- Empirical results show the baseline can sometimes be competitive with a VGG-based, pre-trained multi-layer model.
- Authors claim texture synthesis may not inherently require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.
- Reviewer finds the contribution thought-provoking and insightful.
- Reviewer argues the conclusions—especially about deep hierarchies—not fully validated and need more evidence.
- Generated samples from both the proposed and VGG models show notable imperfections and are qualitatively worse than non-parametric methods.
- VGG-based model appears to perform better on the inpainting task shown in Figure 7.
- Reviewer suggests a hierarchical model might handle multi-scale information more efficiently than using many filter sizes.
