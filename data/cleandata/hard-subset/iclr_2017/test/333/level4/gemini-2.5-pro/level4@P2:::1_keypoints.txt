- Single-layer convolutional network with random, multi-scale filters (no pooling) can produce high-quality textures, sometimes rivaling/surpassing Gatys et al. (VGG-based) results.  
- Proposal to use the VGG-based synthesis loss as a quantitative, perceptual metric for texture quality.  
- Decision: Weak Accept.  
- Strong and surprising finding: a minimal baseline challenges the presumed necessity of depth and pre-training for texture synthesis.  
- Claim that deep hierarchical representations are unnecessary is overstated; evidence is not conclusive.  
- Hierarchies offer clear efficiency benefits (large receptive fields) that are underemphasized in the paper.  
- Missing comparison to a deep network with random filters to isolate the effect of depth versus pre-training.  
- Model excels on regular and semi-regular textures and captures long-range correlations in some cases.  
- Inconsistent visual superiority: VGG-based model produces better results on some textures and inpainting (conditional generation) examples.  
- All parametric methods discussed (including the proposed model and VGG) still trail leading non-parametric texture synthesis methods in visual quality.  
- Request for more details on hierarchical experiments (which architectures, were filters random) to strengthen claims about hierarchy.  
- Suggest authors comment on why VGG may perform better on inpainting and nuance the main conclusion to reflect roles of efficiency and conditional tasks.
