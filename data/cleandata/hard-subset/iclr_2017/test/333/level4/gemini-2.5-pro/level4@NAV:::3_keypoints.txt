- Builds on Gatys et al.; evaluates deep vs. shallow networks for texture synthesis using empirical Gram matrices of feature responses.  
- Main finding: very shallow networks, even single randomly weighted filter banks, perform remarkably well for texture synthesis.  
- For simple, regular textures, shallow/random filters can produce results visually superior to complex, data-adapted filters like VGG-19.  
- Paper provides an insightful and informative discussion of strengths and limitations of these texture synthesis methods.  
- Figure 4 shows optimizing with shallow filter banks can yield textures with lower VGG-19 loss than directly optimizing the VGG-19 objective.  
- Authors attribute this to the difficulty of optimizing VGG-19’s highly non-linear cost function.  
- Supplementary material shows initializing VGG-19 synthesis with shallow-network output improves optimization results.  
- Primary limitation: no systematic comparison against a quantifiable objective; trivial shallow statistics can perfectly reproduce a reference without proving distributional capture.  
- Measuring how well methods capture the underlying texture distribution remains an open problem; current empirical results are suggestive but inconclusive.  
- Diversity via entropy is proposed but hard to implement and doesn’t address visual quality; a psychophysical assessment is suggested but left for future work.  
- Overall judgment: paper’s strengths justify publication, but a formal psychophysical evaluation would strengthen the claims.
