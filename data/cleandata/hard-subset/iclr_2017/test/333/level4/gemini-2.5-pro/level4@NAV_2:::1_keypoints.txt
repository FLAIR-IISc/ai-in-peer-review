- Paper claims high-quality texture synthesis can be achieved with a single convolutional layer of random, data-independent filters.  
- Authors systematically remove depth, pooling, and pre-training to establish a strong minimal, multi-scale random-filter baseline.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection primarily because the main conclusion (depth not necessary) is not fully substantiated.  
- Evidence in the paper (e.g., Appendix Fig. 7 inpainting) suggests the VGG-based model better captures broader spatial context and produces more coherent fills.  
- Claiming depth is dispensable requires demonstrating little-to-no advantage across a wide range of challenging textures, which is not shown.  
- All tested parametric models (including VGG and the proposed model) produce samples with visible artifacts and limited quality, weakening general conclusions.  
- The similar, low performance of all methods could indicate shared fundamental limitations rather than proving depth is unnecessary.  
- The assertion that extensive experiments with hierarchical architectures were conducted lacks supporting details and qualitative comparisons.  
- Request for a concrete efficiency analysis: compare synthesis time/computational cost of the single-layer multi-scale model versus deeper hierarchical models with similar receptive fields.  
- Suggest investigating whether VGG’s shortcomings are due to optimization difficulties (initializing VGG with single-layer results improves quality), and whether better optimization could reveal deeper models’ advantages.
