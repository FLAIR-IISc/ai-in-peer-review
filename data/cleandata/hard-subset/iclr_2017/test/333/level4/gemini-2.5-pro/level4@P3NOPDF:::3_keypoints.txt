- Paper studies which network architectures work best for the Gatys et al. texture synthesis framework, showing Gram-matrix statistics remain effective.  
- Key finding: very shallow networks—even a single random filter bank—can synthesize textures effectively.  
- For simple and regular textures, shallow models can produce visually superior results to deep, data-adapted networks like VGG-19.  
- Surprising result: optimizing an image with respect to a shallow filter bank can yield a lower VGG-19 loss than directly optimizing the VGG-19 objective; initializing VGG optimization with the shallow result improves outcomes.  
- Main limitation: no systematic quantitative comparison; paper relies on visual inspection and may conflate high-fidelity reconstruction with modeling the underlying texture distribution.  
- Authors discuss evaluation challenges: entropy could measure diversity but is impractical and misses visual quality; a psychophysical study would be more appropriate but is left for future work.  
- Overall assessment: insightful analysis and compelling empirical results justify publication at ICLR, though a preliminary psychophysical evaluation would have strengthened the claims.
