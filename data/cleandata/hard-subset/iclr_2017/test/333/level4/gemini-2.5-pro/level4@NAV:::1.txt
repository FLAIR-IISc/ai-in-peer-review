This paper introduces a straightforward yet powerful baseline for parametric texture synthesis. Empirical results demonstrate that textures generated by this baseline, which utilizes multi-scale random filters, can at times be competitive with those from a VGG-based model featuring pre-trained, multi-layer filters. Based on these findings, the authors posit that texture synthesis may not inherently depend on deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

This is a thought-provoking and insightful contribution. However, the study's conclusions, particularly regarding the necessity of deep hierarchical representations, warrant further validation. First, the generated samples from both the proposed model and the VGG baseline exhibit notable imperfections and are qualitatively inferior to results from non-parametric approaches. Furthermore, the VGG-based model appears to yield superior performance on the inpainting task illustrated in Figure 7. Finally, it is worth considering whether a hierarchical model might handle multi-scale information more efficiently than the proposed method of employing a large set of filters with different sizes.
