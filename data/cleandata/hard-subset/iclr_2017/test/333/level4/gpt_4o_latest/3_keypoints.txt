- Builds on Gatys et al.: Gram-matrix correlations of deep features are strong texture representations.  
- Paper studies which deep or shallow networks work best within this texture-synthesis framework.  
- Key finding: very shallow networks with random single-filter-bank weights perform surprisingly well.  
- For simple, regular textures, shallow networks can produce visuals superior to complex, data-trained models like VGG-19.  
- Provides a detailed discussion of strengths and limitations of these methods for texture synthesis.  
- Fig.4 shows optimizing with shallow filter banks can yield lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization.  
- Explanation: VGG-19’s highly non-linear objective is harder to optimize; shallow results can serve as better initializations.  
- Supplementary material shows using shallow-optimized images to initialize VGG-19 improves VGG optimization outcomes.  
- Main limitation: lacks systematic comparison against a quantifiable objective for quality and diversity.  
- Warning: trivial shallow statistics can exactly reproduce a reference texture but do not capture the texture distribution.  
- Authors suggest entropy to measure diversity, but it is hard to implement and ignores visual quality.  
- Psychophysical evaluation is recommended to validate visual quality and diversity but is considered out of scope.  
- Conclusion: paper is publishable at ICLR given evaluation challenges, though psychophysical testing would strengthen validation.
