This paper proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis. In empirical experiments, samples generated by a single-layer baseline using multi-scale random filters sometimes rival those from a VGG-based model with multiple pre-trained layers. The authors thus conclude that texture synthesis does not necessarily depend on deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

The work is interesting and insightful. However, the conclusions require further validation, especially the claim regarding the necessity of deep hierarchical representations. First, the samples produced by both the VGG-based model and the single-layer baseline are imperfect and remain notably worse than results from non-parametric methods. Second, the VGG-based model appears to perform better on the inpainting task in Figure 7, which complicates the central claim. Finally, would a hierarchical model—rather than a flat set of filters of different sizes—handle multi-scale structure more efficiently?

Overall, the proposed baseline is valuable and thought-provoking, but the evidence presented does not yet fully justify the broader conclusions. Clarifying these points would strengthen the paper.
