- Proposes a simple strong baseline: single-layer, multi-scale randomly initialized filters that sometimes rival VGG-based multi-layer pre-trained methods.
- Authors claim successful texture synthesis does not require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.
- The paper is interesting and insightful, but that broad conclusion needs further validation.
- Generated samples from both the VGG-based method and the single-layer baseline are imperfect and substantially worse than non-parametric methods.
- The VGG-based model performs better on the inpainting task (Fig. 7), which complicates the claim that deep learned hierarchies are unnecessary.
- Raises the question whether a true hierarchical model (instead of many filter sizes) would more efficiently handle multi-scale structure.
- Overall the baseline is a valuable contribution and thought-provoking, but conclusions should be tempered or supported with additional evidence about hierarchical representations.
