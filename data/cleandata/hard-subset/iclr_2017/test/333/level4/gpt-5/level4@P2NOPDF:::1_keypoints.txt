- Paper proposes a simple single-layer parametric texture synthesis baseline using multi-scale random filters that sometimes rivals pretrained multi-layer VGG features.
- Authors claim effective texture synthesis does not require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.
- Reviewer finds central claims under-supported and calls for stronger evidence.
- Both the VGG-based and single-layer outputs are imperfect and notably worse than non-parametric methods, weakening the no-depth conclusion.
- VGG-based model appears to outperform the baseline on inpainting, indicating hierarchical learned features may still help in some tasks.
- Question whether a hierarchical model could capture multi-scale structure more efficiently than many filter sizes; a direct discussion or comparison is needed.
- Overall: baseline is valuable and thought-provoking but requires additional experiments and analysis to substantiate broader claims about depth and learned features.
