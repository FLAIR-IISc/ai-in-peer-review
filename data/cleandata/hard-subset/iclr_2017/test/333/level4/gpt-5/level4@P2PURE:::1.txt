This paper proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis. Empirically, the baseline—built from multi-scale random filters—sometimes rivals a VGG-based model that relies on multiple layers and pretrained filters. From these results, the authors argue that successful texture synthesis does not necessarily require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

The work is interesting and insightful, but the conclusions—especially regarding the role of deep hierarchical representations—require further validation. First, the samples produced by both the VGG-based and the single-layer models are imperfect and remain clearly inferior to those from non-parametric methods. Second, the VGG-based model appears to perform better on the inpainting task (Figure 7). Finally, would a hierarchical architecture, rather than a large set of filters of varying sizes, offer a more efficient approach to modeling multi-scale structure?
