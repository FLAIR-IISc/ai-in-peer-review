This paper proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis. Using multi-scale, randomly initialized filters in a single layer, the method sometimes rivals a VGG-based approach that relies on multiple layers with pre-trained filters. From these results, the authors conclude that successful texture synthesis does not necessarily require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

The paper is interesting and insightful. However, the strength of its conclusions—particularly regarding the necessity of deep hierarchical representations—requires further validation. In particular:
- The generated samples from both the VGG-based method and the single-layer baseline are imperfect and substantially worse than those produced by non-parametric methods.
- The VGG-based model appears to perform better on the inpainting task (Figure 7), which complicates the claim that deep, learned hierarchies are unnecessary.
- Would a hierarchical model (rather than many filters of different sizes) handle multi-scale structure more efficiently?

Overall, the baseline is a valuable contribution and the empirical findings are thought-provoking, but the conclusions should be tempered or supported with additional evidence, especially concerning the role of hierarchical representations.
