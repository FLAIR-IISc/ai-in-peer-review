This work proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis. In experiments, samples produced by the baseline comprising multi-scale and random filters sometimes rival those from the VGG-based model, which employs multi-layer, pre-trained filters. The authors conclude that texture synthesis does not necessarily depend on deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

This work is indeed interesting and insightful. However, the conclusions require further validation (especially regarding deep hierarchical representations). First, the generated samples from both the VGG and single-layer models are imperfect and substantially worse than results from non-parametric methods. Moreover, the VGG-based model appears to perform better on the inpainting task in Figure 7. Finally, would a hierarchical model (instead of many filters with different sizes) handle multi-scale structure more efficiently?
