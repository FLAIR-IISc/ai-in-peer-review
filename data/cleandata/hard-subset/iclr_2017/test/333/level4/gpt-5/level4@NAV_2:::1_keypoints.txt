- Paper proposes a minimal, strong baseline: a single-layer, multi-scale parametric texture synthesis model with random filters.
- Empirical finding: the single-layer multi-scale model sometimes matches the performance of multi-layer, pre-trained VGG-based approaches.
- Authors conclude that deep hierarchical representations and learned feature maps may not be necessary for effective texture synthesis.
- Reviewer finds the study interesting and insightful but believes the central conclusions require further validation, especially about the role of deep hierarchies.
- Both VGG and single-layer model samples remain imperfect and often fall short of non-parametric methods.
- In the inpainting evaluation (Figure 7), the VGG-based model appears to outperform the single-layer approach.
- Reviewer raises the question whether a true hierarchical architecture (rather than many filters at multiple scales) could provide a more efficient multi-scale solution.
