- Paper revisits Gram-matrix texture synthesis and compares deep vs. shallow architectures within this framework.  
- Key surprising finding: very shallow networks with a single random convolutional filter bank perform remarkably well.  
- For simple and regular textures, shallow models can produce visually superior results to complex, data-adapted filters like VGG-19.  
- Paper discusses strengths and limitations of Gram-based methods for texture synthesis.  
- Notable result: images optimized with shallow filter banks can achieve lower VGG-19 Gram losses than directly optimizing the VGG-19 objective.  
- Authors attribute this to the difficult, highly non-linear VGG-19 optimization landscape.  
- Supplementary material shows initializing VGG-19 optimization with shallow-network solutions improves results.  
- Practical insight: shallow models can serve as effective initializers or priors for optimizing deeper models.  
- Main limitation: lack of systematic, quantitative evaluation across methods to validate claims about quality and diversity.  
- Challenge: defining and measuring diversity is hard; entropy is proposed but practically difficult and imperfect.  
- Diversity metrics alone do not capture perceptual quality; psychophysical evaluation is recommended.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing conceptual clarity, empirical evidence, and optimization insights.  
- Suggested improvements: add a small psychophysical study (e.g., pairwise preference tests).  
- Suggested improvements: provide feasible proxy measures of diversity and transparently report their limitations.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify optimization setup and hyperparameters for reproducibility, especially VGG-19 vs. shallow comparisons.
