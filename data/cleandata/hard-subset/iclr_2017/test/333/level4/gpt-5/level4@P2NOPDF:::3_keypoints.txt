- Paper studies which network architectures (deep vs shallow) work best for Gatys-style texture synthesis using Gram-matrix statistics.  
- Key finding: very shallow models with a single random-weight filter bank perform surprisingly well.  
- For simple, regular textures, shallow random filters can produce results visually better than VGG-19.  
- Paper offers a thoughtful discussion of strengths and limitations of Gram-based texture synthesis methods.  
- Figure 4: optimizing images with shallow filter banks can yield lower VGG-19 loss than directly optimizing the VGG-19 objective.  
- Authors plausibly attribute that result to optimization difficulties of the highly nonconvex VGG-19 loss.  
- Supplementary: initializing VGG-19 optimization with the shallow-model result improves performance.  
- Main limitation: no systematic, quantitative comparison across methods; trivial statistics can reproduce exact images but don’t model texture distributions.  
- Measuring how well methods capture texture distributions remains an open problem and is not resolved here.  
- Proposed diversity metric (entropy) is reasonable but hard to implement and doesn’t address perceptual quality.  
- Psychophysical evaluation is suggested as the most practical way to assess diversity and fidelity but is left to future work.  
- Overall: empirical insights are strong enough for ICLR publication, but adding psychophysical assessment would substantively strengthen the claims.
