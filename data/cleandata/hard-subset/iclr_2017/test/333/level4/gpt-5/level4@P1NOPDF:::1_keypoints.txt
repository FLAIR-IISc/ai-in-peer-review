- Proposes a simple, strong single-layer baseline for parametric texture synthesis.  
- Empirically, multi-scale random filters in a single-layer model sometimes rival VGG-based multi-layer results.  
- Authors conclude deep hierarchical representations or learned features are not necessary for texture synthesis — reviewer says this claim needs further validation.  
- Both the VGG-based model and the single-layer baseline produce imperfect samples and are notably worse than non-parametric methods.  
- VGG-based model appears to perform better on the inpainting task (Fig. 7), which complicates the central claim.  
- Question whether a true hierarchical model (vs. a flat set of multi-scale filters) would handle multi-scale structure more efficiently.  
- Overall the baseline is valuable and thought-provoking, but current evidence does not fully justify the broader conclusions; clarification and additional validation are needed.
