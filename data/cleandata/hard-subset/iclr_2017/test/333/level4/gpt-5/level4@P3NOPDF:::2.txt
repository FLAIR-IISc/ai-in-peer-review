Summary
The paper presents a careful analysis of the conditions under which generative models produce natural-looking textures. The main findings are both clear and somewhat surprising, and the analysis is executed with commendable thoroughness. The work is timely and addresses a question of practical and theoretical importance for generative modeling.

Strengths
- Clear problem framing: The paper precisely identifies the factors hypothesized to affect texture realism and investigates them systematically.
- Thorough analysis: The authors provide a detailed exploration of design choices and conditions, leading to concrete insights rather than anecdotal observations.
- Surprising findings: Several results challenge common intuitions and could influence how future texture generation systems are built.
- Potential impact: The study opens an interesting line of inquiry into principled evaluation and design of texture generators, with implications beyond the specific setting considered.

Weaknesses and suggestions for improvement
- Evaluation methodology: While the analysis is strong, the evaluation could be more rigorous. Consider:
  - Including more comprehensive quantitative metrics that capture realism and diversity, and reporting them consistently across experiments.
  - Strengthening human perceptual evaluations (if any) with clearer protocols (e.g., number of raters, randomization, statistical significance) and reporting inter-rater reliability.
  - Expanding comparisons to strong contemporary baselines, and ensuring fair comparison settings (training data, parameter counts, compute budgets).
  - Providing diagnostics for overfitting or memorization (e.g., nearest-neighbor analyses in feature space, train/test splits, coverage/diversity measures).
- Scope and generalization: It would help to demonstrate robustness across a broader set of textures, resolutions, and generation settings, or to clearly articulate the current scope and limitations.
- Reproducibility: If not already provided, releasing code, trained models, and evaluation scripts would make the findings more actionable and allow the community to build on this work.
- Clarity of takeaways: The paper would benefit from a concise set of design recommendations or “best practices” distilled from the analysis, ideally summarized in a short checklist or decision guide.

Overall assessment
This is a well-motivated and carefully executed study with insightful and, in places, counterintuitive results. Despite the limitations in evaluation, the core contributions—identifying key conditions for realistic texture generation and validating them through systematic analysis—are valuable and likely to influence subsequent work. The evaluation gaps are addressable and do not undermine the main claims; if anything, they highlight a promising avenue for follow-up research focused on standardized metrics and broader comparative studies.
