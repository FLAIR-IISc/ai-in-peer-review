- Builds on Gatys et al.: uses Gram-matrix feature correlations to characterise textures and studies which deep/shallow networks work within this framework.  
- Main finding: very shallow networks (single random filter bank) perform surprisingly well and can outperform complex learned filters (e.g., VGG-19) on simple/regular textures.  
- Provides an informative discussion of strengths and limitations of deep-feature Gram-based texture synthesis methods.  
- Fig. 4: optimising images w.r.t. shallow filter banks can yield lower VGG-19 loss than directly optimising the VGG-19 objective, likely due to optimisation difficulty of VGG-19’s non-linear cost.  
- Supplementary material: initialising VGG-19 optimisation with shallow-network results yields improved VGG-19 optimisation outcomes.  
- Main limitation: no systematic, quantifiable comparison of methods; trivial/shallow statistics can trivially reproduce a reference texture, so capturing the full texture distribution remains unresolved.  
- Measuring diversity (e.g., via entropy) is proposed but acknowledged as impractical and insufficient alone because it doesn’t capture visual quality.  
- Psychophysical evaluation is suggested as likely necessary to assess visual quality/diversity but is deferred to future work.  
- Overall judgment: despite evaluation challenges, the paper has sufficient strengths to merit publication at ICLR.
