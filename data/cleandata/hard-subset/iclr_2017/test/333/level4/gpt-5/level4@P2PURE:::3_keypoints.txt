- Paper studies which deep or shallow networks work within the Gatys et al. texture-synthesis framework.
- Key finding: very shallow networks (single random filter bank) perform surprisingly well, sometimes outperforming VGG-19 on simple/regular textures.
- Provides an informative discussion of strengths and limitations of these methods for texture synthesis.
- Figure 4 shows shallow-filter optimization can produce textures with lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization, attributed to optimization difficulty of VGG-19.
- Supplementary results: initializing VGG-19 optimization with shallow-network results improves final outcomes.
- Main limitation: no systematic, quantifiable comparison across methods — hard to confirm claims about capturing texture distributions versus reproducing single images.
- Pointed out that trivial shallow statistics can reproduce exact textures, so measuring distributional fidelity remains unresolved.
- Authors propose entropy-based diversity measures (difficult in practice) and psychophysical evaluation; entropy won’t address visual quality alone.
- Overall recommendation: paper is ICLR-worthy despite evaluation challenges, but psychophysical assessment would strengthen the claims.
