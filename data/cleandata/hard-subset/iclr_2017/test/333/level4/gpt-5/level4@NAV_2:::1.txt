This paper presents a minimal yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis. Empirically, the single-layer, multi-scale model with random filters sometimes matches the performance of the VGG-based approach that uses multiple layers and pre-trained filters. The authors conclude that effective texture synthesis may not require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

The study is interesting and offers useful insights. However, the central conclusions need further validation (especially regarding the role of deep hierarchies). First, the samples produced by both the VGG and single-layer models are still imperfect and, in many cases, fall short of non-parametric methods. Moreover, in the inpainting evaluation (Figure 7), the VGG-based model appears to perform better. Finally, could a hierarchical architecture (as opposed to using many filters at different scales) deliver a more efficient solution for multi-scale modeling?
