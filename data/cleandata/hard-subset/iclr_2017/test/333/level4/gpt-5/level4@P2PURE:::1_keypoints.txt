- Proposes a simple, strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis using multi-scale random filters that sometimes rivals a VGG-based model.
- Authors argue that successful texture synthesis may not require deep hierarchical representations or pretrained feature maps.
- The work is interesting and insightful but the conclusions about hierarchy need further validation.
- Samples from both the VGG-based and single-layer models are imperfect and clearly inferior to non-parametric methods.
- The VGG-based model appears to perform better on the inpainting task (Figure 7).
- Raises the question whether a hierarchical architecture would be a more efficient way to model multi-scale structure than using many filter sizes.
