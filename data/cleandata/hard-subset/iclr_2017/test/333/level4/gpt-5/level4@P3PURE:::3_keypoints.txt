- Builds on Gatys et al.: uses Gram-matrix deep feature correlations to characterize textures and studies which deep/shallow networks work within this framework.  
- Key finding: very shallow networks (single random filter bank) perform surprisingly well and can visually outperform VGG-19 on simple/regular textures.  
- Provides an informative discussion of strengths and limitations of Gram-based texture synthesis methods.  
- Fig. 4 shows optimizing images w.r.t. shallow filter banks yields textures with lower VGG-19 loss than directly optimizing the VGG-19 objective.  
- Authors attribute this to optimization difficulty of the highly non-linear VGG-19 cost; plausible explanation.  
- Supplementary results: VGG-19 optimization improves when initialized from the shallow network’s output.  
- Main limitation: lacks a systematic, quantitative comparison against a well-defined objective, making claims about quality/diversity hard to confirm.  
- It is trivial to design shallow statistics that exactly reproduce a reference texture; the true goal is capturing the texture distribution, for which measurement remains an open problem.  
- Proposed diversity measures (entropy) are reasonable but hard to implement and do not address visual quality; psychophysical evaluation is suggested as practical future work.  
- Overall: contributions and insights merit publication at ICLR, though a psychophysical assessment would strengthen the paper.
