This paper introduces a straightforward yet effective baseline for parametric texture synthesis. Empirical results demonstrate that textures generated using this multi-scale, random filter approach can achieve comparable quality to those produced by a more complex VGG-based model employing multi-layer, pre-trained filters. The authors interpret these findings as evidence that effective texture synthesis may not fundamentally require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

This is a compelling and insightful contribution. However, the conclusions regarding the necessity of deep hierarchical representations would benefit from further investigation. Specifically, the generated samples from both the VGG model and the single-layer baseline exhibit imperfections and generally fall short of the quality achieved by non-parametric methods. Furthermore, the VGG-based model appears to perform superiorly in the inpainting task presented in Figure 7. Finally, it remains an open question whether a truly hierarchical model – as opposed to a collection of filters at varying scales – would offer a more efficient approach to handling multi-scale texture features.
