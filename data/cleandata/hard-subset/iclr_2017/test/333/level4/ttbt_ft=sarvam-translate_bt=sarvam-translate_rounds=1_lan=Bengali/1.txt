This work proposes a simple yet robust foundation for parametric texture synthesis. In empirical tests, samples generated by foundations composed of multi-scale and random filters sometimes compete with VGG-based models, which have multi-layer and pre-trained filters. The authors conclude that texture synthesis does not rely on deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

This work is indeed interesting and insightful. However, the conclusions need further verification (especially for deep hierarchical representations). Firstly, not all samples generated by both VGG and single-layer models are perfect and are much worse than the results of non-parametric methods. Also, VGG-based models seem to perform better in the inpainting task in Figure 7. Finally, will a hierarchical model (instead of a large number of filters of different sizes) be able to handle multi-scale more efficiently?
