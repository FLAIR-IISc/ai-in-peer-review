This research proposed a simple yet robust baseline for parametric texture synthesis. In empirical experiments, samples generated by a baseline created by multi-layer and random filters sometimes compete with VGG-based models, which have multi-layer and pre-trained filters. The authors concluded that texture synthesis does not necessarily rely on deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

This research is indeed interesting and insightful. However, the findings require further validation (especially for deep hierarchical representations). Firstly, not all samples generated by both VGG and single-layer models are correct and are much worse than results obtained by non-parametric methods. Furthermore, in Figure 7, the VGG-based model appears to perform better in the inpaving task. Finally, will a hierarchical model (instead of multiple filters of different sizes) handle multi-layer more efficiently?
