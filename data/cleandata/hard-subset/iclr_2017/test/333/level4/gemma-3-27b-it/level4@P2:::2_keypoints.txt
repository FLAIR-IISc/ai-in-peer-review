- Central finding: High-quality textures can be synthesized using a single-layer network with random filters.
- Main implication: Depth and pre-training are not indispensable for successful texture generation.
- Evidence: Thorough experimental evaluation and clear presentation supporting the claims.
- Comparative analysis: Comprehensive comparisons across single-layer architectures (Fourier, K-means, PCA, Random) and VGG-based models.
- Evaluation metric: Introduces a VGG-based quantitative texture quality measure, acknowledged as imperfect but a step toward objective evaluation.
- Ablation studies: Filter size and multi-scale analyses provide insight into factors crucial for texture synthesis success.
- Suggested improvements: Add human perceptual studies to strengthen evaluation and explore non-Euclidean distance metrics for Gram matrix comparison.
- Efficiency concern: Clarify computational cost of the multi-scale model and compare efficiency with deeper networks.
- Request for details: Provide specifics on optimization tuning strategies and their impact on results.
- Validation question: Report any preliminary human studies correlating VGG loss with perceived quality.
- Further experiments: Explain any initial attempts with deeper architectures and why they didn’t improve perceptual quality.
