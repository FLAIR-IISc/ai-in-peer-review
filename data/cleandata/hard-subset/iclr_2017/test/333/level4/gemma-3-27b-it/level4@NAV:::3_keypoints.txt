- Builds on Gatys et al.: empirical Gram matrices of deep features effectively characterize visual textures.  
- Thoroughly evaluates various network architectures (deep and shallow) within the Gram-matrix texture framework.  
- Key finding: very shallow, single randomly-weighted filter banks perform surprisingly well and can outperform VGG-19 on simple/regular textures.  
- Provides valuable discussion of strengths and limitations of using correlation statistics for texture synthesis.  
- Fig.4 shows shallow-filter optimization can yield lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization, attributed to optimization difficulty of the VGG-19 objective.  
- Supplementary material demonstrates improved VGG-19 optimization when initialized from shallow-network results.  
- Major limitation: lack of systematic comparison using quantifiable metrics for quality and diversity.  
- Shallow statistics can reproduce reference textures but may fail to capture the broader texture distribution; measuring this remains an open problem.  
- Authors suggest entropy as a diversity measure (practically difficult) and defer psychophysical evaluation as a more definitive assessment.  
- Conclusion: paper is a valuable contribution deserving publication, but inclusion of psychophysical evaluation would strengthen and validate the claims.
