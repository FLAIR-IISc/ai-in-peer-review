- Recommendation: accept — novelty of findings and rigorous exploration of single-layer architectures.
- Main claim: high-quality natural textures can be generated with single-layer networks, no pooling, and random filters.
- Challenge to assumptions: depth and pre-training are not indispensable for texture synthesis.
- Extensive experiments: comparison of Fourier bases, K-means, PCA, and random filters against state-of-the-art.
- Contribution: introduction of a quantitative VGG-based texture quality measure beyond qualitative human evals.
- Clarification needed: relationship between VGG-based loss and perceptual quality, since VGG loss sometimes favors less similar images.
- Efficiency concern: multi-scale model is computationally inefficient; trade-offs between efficiency and quality should be discussed.
- Question: need intuition on why random filters perform well and which properties of random filters matter.
- Question: possible reasons for discrepancy with Gatys et al. (2015a) beyond optimization tuning, including implementation or hardware differences.
- Question: request for specific examples where VGG loss and human perception diverge and hypotheses for those divergences.
- Question: elaboration requested on extending the MaxEnt framework to model variability in summary statistics and its implementation/benefits.
