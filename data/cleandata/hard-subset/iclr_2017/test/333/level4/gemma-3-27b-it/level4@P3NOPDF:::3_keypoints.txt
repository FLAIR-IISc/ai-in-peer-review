- Paper compares texture synthesis across architectures from shallow random filter banks to deep data-trained networks (e.g., VGG-19) using Gram-matrix framework.
- Key finding: very shallow networks with a single random filter bank can produce high-quality textures, sometimes outperforming complex trained filters for simple/regular textures.
- Figure 4 shows optimizing images with shallow filter banks can yield lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization.
- Authors plausibly attribute this to the difficulty of optimizing the highly non-linear VGG-19 objective.
- Supplementary results show initializing VGG-19 optimization with outputs from shallow networks improves VGG-19 optimization outcomes.
- Main limitation: no systematic, quantifiable evaluation against competing methods is provided.
- Core challenge identified: matching image statistics is straightforward, but capturing the texture’s underlying distribution and measuring it remains unresolved.
- Entropy is noted as a potential diversity measure but is practically difficult to apply.
- Psychophysical evaluation is proposed as the most practical solution but left as future work; including even limited psychophysics would strengthen the paper.
- Overall assessment: the paper makes a strong, publishable contribution by highlighting shallow networks’ effectiveness and optimization challenges in complex models.
