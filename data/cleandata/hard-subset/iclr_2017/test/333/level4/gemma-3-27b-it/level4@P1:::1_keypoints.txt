- Paper proposes a simple, strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis using a single convolutional layer with multi-scale random filters.
- Empirical results show the single-layer random-filter model sometimes rivals the VGG-based multi-layer, pre-trained model.
- Authors conclude deep hierarchical representations and learned feature maps are not strictly necessary for texture synthesis.
- Reviewer finds the contribution interesting and insightful but believes conclusions need further investigation on hierarchical representations.
- Generated samples from both models are limited and noticeably worse than non-parametric methods.
- VGG-based model appears to perform better on the inpainting task (Figure 7).
- Question remains whether a hierarchical model could handle multi-scale analysis more efficiently than many filter sizes.
- Reviewer recommends accepting the paper, calling the argument counter-intuitive and well-supported.
- Strong supporting points: clear research question, systematic ablation to a minimal model, and comprehensive experimental comparisons.
- Contribution of a quantitative VGG-based evaluation metric is valuable beyond subjective inspection.
- Theoretical discussion linking results to maximum entropy (MaxEnt) models is thoughtful and relevant.
- Additional feedback: include computational cost comparisons (training/synthesis time) for different architectures.
- Additional feedback: discuss impact of different random filter initializations (distributions, orthogonalization).
- Additional feedback: report sensitivity of results to optimization hyperparameters (learning rate, regularization).
- Additional feedback: provide more diverse visual texture examples to strengthen empirical claims.
- Questions for authors: detail texture types where VGG-based loss fails perceptually and why.
- Questions for authors: clarify whether deeper hierarchical models were trained from scratch or fine-tuned from pre-trained weights.
- Questions for authors: outline how MaxEnt could be extended to allow variability in summary statistics and potential benefits.
- Questions for authors: elaborate how the reported “finite size effect” in MaxEnt constraints might be mitigated in practice.
