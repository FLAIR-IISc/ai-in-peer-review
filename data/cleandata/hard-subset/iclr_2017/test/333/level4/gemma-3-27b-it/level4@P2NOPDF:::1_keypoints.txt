- Proposes a simple, multi-scale random-filter baseline for parametric texture synthesis that often matches VGG-based model quality.
- Claims that successful texture synthesis may not require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.
- Reviewer finds the insight interesting but believes the claim about hierarchy’s necessity needs more evidence.
- Notes both the VGG model and the single-layer baseline produce imperfect samples and generally underperform non-parametric methods.
- Observes the VGG-based model outperforms the baseline on the inpainting task (Fig. 7).
- Raises concern that the multi-scale approach uses many independent filters and could become computationally expensive as resolution or texture complexity grows.
- Suggests a truly hierarchical model might represent scales more compactly and robustly than a large collection of filters.
- Recommends further experiments comparing the proposed method to hierarchical parametric models to better clarify the role of hierarchy in texture synthesis.
