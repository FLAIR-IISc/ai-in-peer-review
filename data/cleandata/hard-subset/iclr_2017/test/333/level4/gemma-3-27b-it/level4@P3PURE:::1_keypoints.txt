- Proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis using multi-scale random filters.
- Empirical results show this baseline sometimes rivals a VGG-based multi-layer pre-trained model.
- Authors conclude texture synthesis may not require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.
- Reviewer finds the work interesting and insightful but believes the conclusions need further investigation.
- Generated samples from both the VGG and single-layer models are imperfect and notably worse than non-parametric methods.
- The VGG-based model appears to perform better on the inpainting task (Figure 7).
- Raises the question whether a true hierarchical model would handle multi-scale textures more efficiently than many filters of varying sizes.
