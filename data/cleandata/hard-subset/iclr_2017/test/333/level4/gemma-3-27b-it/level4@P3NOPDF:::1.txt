This work presents a simple and surprisingly effective baseline for parametric texture synthesis. The proposed method, leveraging multi-scale and random filters, achieves results that, in empirical evaluation, frequently approach the quality of a more complex VGG-based model employing multi-layer, pre-trained filters. This leads the authors to a compelling conclusion: high-quality texture synthesis does not necessarily require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

The core contribution of this work is indeed interesting and insightful, challenging the prevailing assumption that deep learning is essential for strong performance in texture synthesis. However, the strength of this conclusion warrants further investigation, particularly regarding the necessity of deep hierarchical representations. 

Currently, the generated samples from *both* the VGG-based model and the proposed single-layer approach exhibit imperfections and, notably, fall short of the visual fidelity achieved by established non-parametric methods. While the authors demonstrate competitive performance relative to another *parametric* approach, a stronger comparison to state-of-the-art non-parametric techniques would better contextualize the results. Furthermore, the inpainting results presented in Figure 7 suggest that the VGG-based model may possess advantages in tasks beyond pure texture synthesis, hinting at the potential benefits of learned representations in related areas.

A critical question arises regarding scalability and efficiency. The proposed method relies on a large number of filters of differing sizes to capture multi-scale features. It is reasonable to ask whether a truly hierarchical model – one explicitly designed with hierarchical layers – could achieve comparable or superior performance with a more efficient parameterization. Exploring this direction would strengthen the argument that deep *hierarchical* representations are not essential, as opposed to simply demonstrating that a deep *network* isn’t required. 

In summary, this paper presents a valuable contribution by providing a strong, simple baseline for texture synthesis and prompting a re-evaluation of the necessity of complex deep learning architectures. While the results are promising, further investigation, particularly a more robust comparison to non-parametric methods and an exploration of hierarchical model alternatives, is needed to fully substantiate the authors’ claims.
