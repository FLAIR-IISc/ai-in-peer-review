- Proposes a simple, strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis using multi-scale random filters that sometimes rivals a VGG-based model.  
- Claims that texture synthesis may not require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.  
- Reviewer finds the work interesting and insightful.  
- Reviewer argues the conclusions need more substantiation regarding the necessity of deep hierarchical representations.  
- Notes both the VGG and single-layer models produce imperfect samples that are noticeably worse than non-parametric methods.  
- Observes the VGG-based model appears to perform better on the inpainting task (Figure 7).  
- Questions whether a genuinely hierarchical model would handle multi-scale features more efficiently than many filters of varying sizes.
