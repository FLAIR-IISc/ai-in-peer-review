- Proposes a simple, effective parametric texture synthesis baseline using multi-scale random filters that often approaches VGG-based performance.
- Main claim: high-quality texture synthesis may not require deep hierarchical or learned feature representations.
- Contribution is insightful and challenges the assumption that deep learning is essential for texture synthesis.
- Conclusion requires further investigation to confirm the non-necessity of deep hierarchical representations.
- Both the proposed method and the VGG-based model produce imperfect results and underperform compared to established non-parametric methods.
- The paper lacks a strong comparison to state-of-the-art non-parametric texture synthesis techniques.
- Inpainting results indicate the VGG-based model may have advantages in tasks beyond texture synthesis, suggesting benefits of learned features.
- Scalability and efficiency are a concern because the method depends on many filters of varying sizes to capture multi-scale features.
- It remains unclear whether a true hierarchical model could match or exceed performance with a more efficient parameterization.
- Overall, the paper provides a valuable baseline and prompts re-evaluation of deep architectures, but needs more robust comparisons and exploration of hierarchical alternatives.
