- Proposes a simple, effective baseline for parametric texture synthesis using multi-scale random filters.
- Empirical results show this baseline sometimes matches the quality of a VGG-based model with pre-trained multi-layer filters.
- Authors conclude deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps are not necessary for successful texture synthesis.
- Review finds the work interesting and insightful but says the conclusion requires further investigation.
- Generated samples from both the VGG-based and single-layer models are imperfect and generally worse than non-parametric methods.
- The VGG-based model appears to perform better on the inpainting task (Figure 7).
- Suggests a hierarchical model might handle multi-scale texture more efficiently than many filters of varying sizes.
