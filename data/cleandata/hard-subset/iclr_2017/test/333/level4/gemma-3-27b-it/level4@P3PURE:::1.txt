This work proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis. Empirical experiments demonstrate that samples generated by this baseline, composed of multi-scale and random filters, sometimes rival the performance of a VGG-based model employing multi-layer, pre-trained filters. The authors conclude that texture synthesis does not necessarily require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

This work is indeed interesting and insightful. However, the conclusions warrant further investigation, particularly regarding the necessity of deep hierarchical representations. Firstly, the generated samples from both the VGG-based model and the single-layer model are imperfect and noticeably inferior to results obtained from non-parametric methods. Furthermore, the VGG-based model appears to perform better in the inpainting task shown in Figure 7. Finally, it is worth considering whether a hierarchical model – as opposed to numerous filters of varying sizes – would handle multi-scale texture more efficiently.
