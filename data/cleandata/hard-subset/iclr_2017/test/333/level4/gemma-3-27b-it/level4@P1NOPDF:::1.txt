This work proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis. Empirical experiments demonstrate that samples generated by this baseline – composed of multi-scale and random filters – sometimes rival those produced by a VGG-based model employing multi-layer, pre-trained filters. The authors conclude that texture synthesis does not necessarily require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

This work is indeed interesting and insightful. However, the conclusions require further substantiation, particularly regarding the necessity of deep hierarchical representations. Firstly, the generated samples from both the VGG model and the single-layer model are imperfect and considerably worse than results obtained from non-parametric methods. Secondly, the VGG-based model appears to perform better on the inpainting task shown in Figure 7. Finally, one might question whether a truly hierarchical model (rather than a large number of filters with varying sizes) would handle multi-scale features more efficiently.
