- Paper challenges need for depth/pretraining, showing single-layer random-filter convnets can synthesize natural textures comparable to Gatys et al. (2015a).
- Demonstrates strong results from various single-layer architectures (Fourier, K-means, PCA, Random), with multi-scale model sometimes having lower VGG loss.
- Claims simpler models are less prone to optimization local minima and can serve as better initializations for deeper models.
- Emphasizes limitations of current evaluation metrics and the need to consider both perceptual similarity and variability.
- Decision: Reject due to insufficient robust quantitative evaluation beyond VGG-based loss.
- Main weakness: reliance on qualitative visual inspection and VGG loss, which is not a definitive proxy for human perception.
- Needs stronger evidence via psychophysical experiments or alternative metrics that better correlate with human judgments.
- Recommends additional ablation studies to quantify contributions of filter size, number of feature maps, and ReLU non-linearity.
- Requests inclusion/discussion of failure cases to identify textures that the single-layer approach cannot handle.
- Calls for clearer definition and measurement of "diversity" or variability in generated textures.
- Asks for more optimization details (L-BFGS-B parameters, sensitivity to initialization) for reproducibility.
- Question: sensitivity of results to the optimization scaling factor (10^7) and performance variance with other values.
- Question: whether textures where multi-scale model has lower VGG loss indicate optimization issues or capture different texture statistics.
- Question: whether computational cost of the multi-scale model can be reduced (filter pruning, dimensionality reduction) without losing performance.
- Suggests considering reporting FID as a complementary metric despite its limitations for textures.
- Question: any preliminary ideas for incorporating a measure of variability directly into the loss during optimization.
