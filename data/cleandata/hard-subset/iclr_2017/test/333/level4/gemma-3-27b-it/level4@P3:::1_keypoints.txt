- Decision: Accept.
- Main finding: High-quality natural texture synthesis can be achieved with a minimal single-layer convolutional network using random filters.
- Challenge to assumption: Depth and pre-training are surprisingly unnecessary for strong texture generation performance.
- Empirical comparison: Evaluated single-layer architectures including Fourier, K-means, PCA, and random filters.
- Quantitative contribution: Proposes a VGG-based synthesis-loss metric for objective, repeatable texture quality evaluation.
- Results: Single-layer, multi-scale models often match or exceed established VGG-based methods on synthesis quality.
- Ablation insight: Combining filters across multiple scales is crucial; single-scale models are insufficient.
- Writing and position: Well-written, thorough literature review, and clear articulation of the research question.
- Limitations noted: Generated samples are not perfect and can be inferior to non-parametric methods in some cases.
- Inpainting observation: VGG-based model appears to outperform single-layer models on inpainting, raising representational questions.
- Reproducibility concern: Paper references “elaborate tuning of the optimization procedure” without sufficient detail.
- Efficiency concern: Multi-scale approach is computationally inefficient; need discussion of optimizations or alternatives to enlarge receptive fields.
- Trade-off noted: Must balance perceptual similarity and variability; paper acknowledges this and suggests future work.
- Requested clarification: Provide specifics on the optimization tuning (parameters, schedules, techniques).
- Question on initialization: Do different random initialization schemes (Glorot/Xavier vs. He, etc.) significantly affect texture quality?
- Question on inpainting: Why does VGG outperform single-layer models—limited long-range dependency capture or evaluation artifact?
- Question on diversity control: What framework modifications (loss/optimization changes) could better balance perceptual similarity and sample variability?
