- Paper studies deep vs. shallow network architectures within the Gatys et al. Gram-matrix texture synthesis framework.  
- Main finding: very shallow networks (single, random filter bank) can achieve surprisingly strong texture synthesis results.  
- For simple and regular textures, shallow networks can outperform complex, data-adapted filters like VGG-19.  
- Figure 4 shows shallow-filter optimization can yield lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization, likely due to VGG-19’s difficult non-linear optimization.  
- Supplementary results: initializing VGG-19 optimization from shallow-network outputs improves VGG-19 performance.  
- Major limitation: no systematic, quantifiable evaluation against competing methods.  
- Replicating a reference texture is easy; the harder goal is capturing the underlying texture distribution, and objective measures for this are lacking.  
- Authors discuss entropy as a diversity metric but note practical issues and that entropy doesn’t capture visual quality; they propose psychophysical tests as future work.  
- Overall recommendation: paper merits ICLR publication, but including even a limited psychophysical evaluation would strengthen the claims.
