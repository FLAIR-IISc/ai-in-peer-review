- Gatys et al. framework: empirical Gram matrices of deep feature responses effectively characterize visual textures.  
- This paper studies which network architectures (deep vs. shallow) work well within that framework.  
- Key finding: very shallow networks — a single random filter bank — can synthesize textures surprisingly well.  
- For simple and regular textures, shallow networks can produce visuals superior to complex, data-adapted filters like VGG-19.  
- The paper offers an informative discussion of the strengths and limitations of these texture synthesis methods.  
- Figure 4 shows optimizing against shallow filter banks can yield lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization, attributed to optimization difficulty of VGG-19.  
- Supplementary results: initializing VGG-19 optimization with shallow-network outputs improves VGG-19 optimization outcomes.  
- Main limitation: lack of systematic, quantifiable comparison — no agreed objective to measure how well methods capture the texture distribution.  
- Trivial/shallow statistics can exactly replicate a reference texture but do not necessarily capture the underlying texture distribution; empirical claims are hard to conclusively confirm.  
- Authors propose entropy to evaluate diversity (reasonable but hard to implement) and note it wouldn’t fully address visual quality.  
- They propose psychophysical assessment as the most practical solution but defer it to future work; reviewer recommends including such validation.  
- Overall: despite evaluation challenges, the paper has sufficient merit for ICLR publication.
