- Decision: Reject with request for major revisions despite interesting contributions.  
- Main contribution: Introduces a simple single-layer convolutional network with random filters as a strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis.  
- Key claim: Suggests depth and pre-training may not be essential for natural texture generation.  
- Positive result: Demonstrates that this simple model can generate high-quality textures that sometimes rival VGG-based methods.  
- Weakness: Conclusions are not fully supported; comparisons to VGG are inconsistent and VGG sometimes outperforms the proposed method (notably in inpainting).  
- Visual quality concern: Overall texture quality remains limited, even for the multi-scale random model, and may not surpass non-parametric methods.  
- Overstated claim: Assertion of surpassing methods like Portilla & Simoncelli (2000) lacks sufficient visual comparison evidence.  
- Metric issue: Quantitative evaluation relies on VGG-based loss, which is biased toward VGG features and problematic for regular/man-made textures.  
- Evaluation gap: Need for more objective assessments—recommend larger datasets, stronger quantitative metrics, and human studies.  
- Hierarchical models: Authors briefly note hierarchical models are more efficient but not better in quality; more thorough investigation and architectural details are needed.  
- Optimization clarity: Optimization procedure and hyperparameter sensitivity for single-layer models are under-described and should be clarified.  
- Randomness sensitivity: Request analysis of sensitivity to random filter initializations and variability across seeds.  
- Suggested experiments: Expand visual comparisons to non-parametric and more diverse texture methods and include head-to-head human evaluations.  
- Questions for authors: Provide detailed tuning parameters, architectural choices and learning rates for hierarchical tests, analysis of VGG-outperforming cases (e.g., inpainting), seed sensitivity, and alternative metrics under consideration.
