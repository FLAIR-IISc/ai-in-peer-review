- Gatys et al. framework: Gram matrices of deep feature responses effectively characterize visual textures.  
- Paper goal: examine which deep or shallow networks work well within the Gram-matrix texture synthesis framework.  
- Main finding: very shallow single-layer networks with random filters perform surprisingly well, sometimes visually outperforming VGG-19 on simple/regular textures.  
- Broader contribution: provides an informative discussion on strengths and limitations of Gram-matrix-based texture synthesis methods.  
- Optimization insight: optimizing with shallow filter banks can produce images with lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization, likely due to VGG-19’s highly non-linear cost being harder to optimize.  
- Supplementary result: initializing VGG-19 optimization with shallow-network results yields better VGG-19 optimization outcomes.  
- Key limitation: no systematic comparison against a quantifiable objective, so claims about capturing texture distributions and diversity remain inconclusive.  
- Caveat: trivial shallow statistics can replicate a reference texture exactly, but the real goal is modeling the texture distribution (diversity), which isn’t addressed quantitatively.  
- Diversity measurement: entropy is suggested but difficult in practice and insufficient for assessing visual quality alone.  
- Evaluation recommendation: psychophysical (human) assessment is likely the only practical way to evaluate visual quality and diversity, but is left for future work.  
- Recommendation: reviewer supports acceptance—results are surprising, well-supported, and experimentally thorough.  
- Supporting argument: novelty—challenges assumption that depth and pretraining are necessary for natural image generation.  
- Supporting argument: thoroughness—wide exploration of single-layer architectures and rigorous comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Supporting argument: justification—plausible explanation for discrepancies with prior work backed by experiments.  
- Supporting argument: clarity—paper is well-written with clear technical details.  
- Suggested improvement: include a deeper discussion of filter-size trade-offs and multi-scale filter importance.  
- Suggested improvement: provide a precise definition or clarification of “stationary structure.”  
- Suggested improvement: discuss computational cost of the multi-scale model compared to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Suggested improvement: expand on difficulties quantifying texture diversity and mention alternative approaches beyond entropy.  
- Questions for authors: detail sensitivity of results to optimization parameters (learning rate, iterations, box constraints).  
- Questions for authors: provide specific examples where VGG loss is misleading and analyze why it fails.  
- Questions for authors: discuss potential for combining approaches, e.g., using shallow-network initialization for deeper-network optimization.
