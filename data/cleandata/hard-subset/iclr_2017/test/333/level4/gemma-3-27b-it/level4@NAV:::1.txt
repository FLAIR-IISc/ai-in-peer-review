This paper introduces a straightforward yet effective baseline for parametric texture synthesis. Experimental results demonstrate that textures generated using this multi-scale, random filter approach are competitive with those produced by a more complex VGG-based model employing multi-layer, pre-trained filters. The authors suggest this indicates that deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps may not be essential for texture synthesis.

This is a valuable and thought-provoking contribution. However, the conclusions regarding the necessity of deep hierarchical representations warrant further investigation. Firstly, the quality of samples generated by both the VGG model and the single-layer baseline remains below that achievable with non-parametric methods. Furthermore, the VGG-based approach appears to perform superiorly in the inpainting task illustrated in Figure 7. Finally, it is worth considering whether a genuinely hierarchical model – as opposed to a collection of filters at varying scales – could offer a more efficient solution for handling multi-scale textures.
