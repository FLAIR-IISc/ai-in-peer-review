- Paper examines which deep or shallow networks work well with Gatys et al.'s Gram-matrix texture synthesis framework.  
- Key finding: very shallow networks (single random filter bank) can achieve surprisingly effective texture synthesis.  
- For simple and regular textures, shallow networks can produce results visually superior to complex, learned filters like VGG-19.  
- Paper provides an informative discussion of strengths and limitations of these methods for texture synthesis.  
- Figure 4 shows optimizing images w.r.t. shallow filter banks can yield lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization, attributed to optimization difficulty of VGG-19.  
- Supplementary material demonstrates that initializing VGG-19 optimization with shallow-network results improves optimization outcomes.  
- Main limitation: no systematic comparison against a quantifiable objective to measure how well methods capture texture distributions.  
- While shallow statistics can reproduce a reference texture exactly, assessing capture of the full texture distribution remains an open, challenging problem.  
- Authors propose entropy to measure diversity—reasonable but hard to implement and insufficient to assess visual quality.  
- Authors suggest psychophysical evaluation as a practical way to assess quality/diversity; reviewer recommends this as future or additional work.  
- Overall judgment: paper has sufficient strengths for ICLR publication, but would benefit from psychophysical corroboration.
