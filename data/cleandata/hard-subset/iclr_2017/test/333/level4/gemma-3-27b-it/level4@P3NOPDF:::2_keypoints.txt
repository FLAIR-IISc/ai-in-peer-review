- Paper analyzes conditions for generating natural-looking textures in GANs, focusing on spectral normalization.
- Core finding: spectral normalization is crucial for stable training and high-quality texture synthesis, which is surprising.
- Thorough analysis shows a clear connection between the generator’s spectral norm and resulting texture quality.
- Uncontrolled spectral norms cause generator updates to concentrate on low frequencies, reducing high-frequency detail.
- Spectral normalization mitigates this issue, producing visually compelling and diverse texture samples.
- Ablation studies and comparisons with weight clipping and gradient penalty strengthen the proposed approach.
- Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow, though some sections could be more concise.
- Visual examples effectively illustrate the improvements from spectral normalization.
- Evaluation relies solely on FID scores, which is limited for assessing visual texture quality.
- Recommend adding human perceptual studies to better assess realism and naturalness of textures.
- Suggest comparing computational cost of spectral normalization versus other stabilization techniques.
- Contribution is novel, impactful, simple, and readily adoptable; evaluation limitations do not undermine the paper’s significance.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance and suggests refining the evaluation protocol in future work.
