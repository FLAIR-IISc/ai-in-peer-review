- Builds on Gatys et al.: Gram-matrix statistics of deep features effectively characterize visual textures.  
- Study investigates which deep or shallow network architectures work within this Gram-based texture synthesis framework.  
- Main finding: very shallow networks (single random filter bank) perform surprisingly well for texture synthesis.  
- For simple and regular textures, single-layer random filters can produce results visually superior to VGG-19.  
- Paper provides an informative discussion on the strengths and limitations of Gram-based texture synthesis methods.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Challenges the assumption that depth and pre-training are necessary for high-quality texture synthesis.  
- Demonstrates a minimal, effective baseline using single-layer networks with random filters.  
- Offers a comprehensive comparison of various single-layer architectures, highlighting key components for texture generation.  
- Shows shallow filter-bank optimization can yield textures with lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization (attributed to optimization landscape).  
- Supplementary material shows improved VGG-19 optimization when initialized from single-layer results.  
- Thorough ablation studies and comparative analyses support the paper’s claims.  
- Acknowledges subjective evaluation of texture quality is challenging and proposes future psychophysical assessments.  
- Requests clarification on sensitivity to optimization hyperparameters (e.g., scaling factor in Section 4).  
- Suggests evaluating generalizability to more complex and less structured textures.  
- Asks about computational cost of the multi-scale model versus VGG-based synthesis and possible optimizations.  
- Questions the role of ReLU non-linearities and whether other non-linearities are equally effective.
