This work proposes a simple yet effective baseline for parametric texture synthesis. Empirical results demonstrate that samples generated using this baseline – composed of multi-scale and random filters – sometimes achieve comparable quality to a VGG-based model employing multi-layer, pre-trained filters. The authors conclude that successful texture synthesis does not necessarily require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

This work is interesting and provides valuable insights. However, the conclusions warrant further investigation, particularly regarding the necessity of deep hierarchical representations. First, the generated samples from both the VGG-based and single-layer models are imperfect and generally inferior to results obtained with non-parametric methods. Second, the VGG-based model appears to perform better on the inpainting task, as illustrated in Figure 7. Finally, it is worth considering whether a hierarchical model – rather than numerous filters of varying sizes – could handle multi-scale texture more efficiently.
