This study proposes a straightforward yet robust baseline for parametric texture synthesis, demonstrating competitive results against the VGG-based model, which utilizes multi-layer and pre-trained filters, in certain empirical experiments involving multi-scale and random filters. The authors infer that texture synthesis may not necessarily rely on deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

The work offers valuable insights and is indeed thought-provoking. Nevertheless, further validation of the conclusions is required, particularly with regards to deep hierarchical representations. Notably, the generated samples from both the VGG and single-layer models exhibit imperfections and fall short of the quality achieved by non-parametric methods. Moreover, the VGG-based model appears to outperform in the inpainting task, as illustrated in Figure 7. Additionally, it is worth investigating whether a hierarchical model, as opposed to employing numerous filters of varying sizes, could more efficiently handle multi-scale textures.
