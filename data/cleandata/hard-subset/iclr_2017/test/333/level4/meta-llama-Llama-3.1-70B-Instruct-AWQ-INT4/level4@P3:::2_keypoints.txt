- Main claim: Single-layer networks with random filters can generate high-quality, natural-looking textures.  
- Surprising result: This finding challenges the belief that deep, supervised feature hierarchies are necessary for texture synthesis.  
- Scope: Paper addresses a well-defined question about which feature-representation aspects are critical for natural texture generation.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance based on clarity of answer and strong motivation grounded in literature.  
- Experimental support: Extensive comparisons show single-layer random-filter networks often rival Gatys et al.'s state-of-the-art texture model.  
- Ablations: Thorough analysis of key components—number of feature maps, filter sizes, and non-linearities—and their importance.  
- Evaluation weakness: Reliance on visual inspection and a VGG-based synthesis-loss metric, which has acknowledged limitations.  
- Suggested improvement 1: Provide more details on the optimization procedure and how hyperparameters were chosen for each single-layer network.  
- Suggested improvement 2: Discuss potential applications beyond texture synthesis (e.g., image generation or image-to-image translation).  
- Suggested improvement 3: Use additional quantitative evaluation metrics (e.g., FID, Inception Score) to better assess realism and diversity.  
- Overall judgment: Paper makes a valuable contribution and offers a new perspective on conditions for generating natural textures.
