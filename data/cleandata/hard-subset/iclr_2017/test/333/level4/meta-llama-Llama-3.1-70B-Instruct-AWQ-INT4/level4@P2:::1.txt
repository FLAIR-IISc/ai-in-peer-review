**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper proposes a simple yet effective baseline for parametric texture synthesis using a single-layer convolutional neural network with random filters. The authors demonstrate that their approach can generate high-quality textures that rival the state-of-the-art VGG-based model, which relies on a deep hierarchical representation and pre-trained filters. The main contributions of the paper are: (1) a strong minimal baseline for parametric texture synthesis, (2) the demonstration that texture synthesis does not necessarily depend on deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps, and (3) the introduction of a quantitative texture quality measure based on the synthesis loss in the VGG-based model.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

I recommend accepting this paper, with the following key reasons:

1. **Novelty and Impact**: The paper presents a novel and counterintuitive finding that challenges the conventional wisdom in the field of texture synthesis. The results have significant implications for the design of texture synthesis models and the understanding of what makes a good texture representation.
2. **Technical Soundness**: The paper is technically sound, with a clear and well-motivated approach, thorough experiments, and a detailed analysis of the results.

**Supporting Arguments**

1. **Empirical Evaluation**: The authors conduct an extensive empirical evaluation of their approach, comparing it to the state-of-the-art VGG-based model and other baselines. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach and provide insights into the strengths and limitations of different texture synthesis models.
2. **Theoretical Analysis**: The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the proposed approach, discussing the role of Gram matrices in capturing the stationary structure of textures and the importance of non-linearities in texture synthesis.

**Additional Feedback**

1. **Comparison to Non-Parametric Methods**: While the paper compares the proposed approach to other parametric methods, it would be interesting to see a comparison to non-parametric methods, which are often considered state-of-the-art in texture synthesis.
2. **Inpainting Task**: The paper mentions that the VGG-based model performs better in an inpainting task, but it would be useful to provide more details on this experiment and the results.
3. **Hierarchical Model**: The authors could explore the use of a hierarchical model instead of a single-layer model with multiple filters, to see if this would handle multi-scale texture synthesis more efficiently.

**Questions for the Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the optimization procedure used in the paper, and how it was tuned for the different models?
