- Builds on Gatys et al.: Gram matrices of deep feature responses effectively characterize visual textures; this paper studies which deep/shallow networks work within that framework.
- Key finding: very shallow networks (single random filter bank) perform surprisingly well and can outperform complex networks (e.g., VGG-19) on simple/regular textures.
- Provides an informative discussion of strengths and limitations of shallow vs. deep approaches for texture synthesis.
- Figure 4 shows optimizing shallow filter banks can produce textures with lower VGG-19 loss than directly optimizing the VGG-19 objective.
- Authors attribute that result to the difficulty of optimizing the highly non-linear VGG-19 cost function.
- Supplementary material shows initializing VGG-19 optimization from shallow-network results yields improved optimization outcomes.
- Main limitation: no systematic, quantifiable comparison between methods; trivial shallow statistics can overfit single textures, so assessing capture of the texture distribution remains unresolved.
- Proposed metrics (entropy for diversity) are reasonable but hard to implement and do not capture visual quality; psychophysical evaluation is suggested but left for future work.
- Overall recommendation: paper has enough merit for ICLR acceptance, but a psychophysical assessment would strengthen the claims.
