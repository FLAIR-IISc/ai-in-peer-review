- Proposes a novel, simple single-layer CNN with random filters for high-quality natural texture synthesis.  
- Claims method rivals state-of-the-art VGG-based deep hierarchical models despite using random, single-layer filters.  
- Key contribution: challenges the belief that deep hierarchical learned features are necessary for texture synthesis.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear, concise explanation of the approach.  
- Empirical results are impressive, producing textures comparable to VGG-based models.  
- Provides thorough analysis on the importance of multi-scale features and non-linearities for texture statistics.  
- Reviewer accepts the paper but recommends minor revisions.  
- Concern that the conclusion dismissing deep hierarchical representations may be overstated.  
- Suggests more comparisons to hierarchical models with fewer filters and to non-parametric methods.  
- Recommends experiments on performance with fewer filters and comparisons to other state-of-the-art approaches.  
- Requests more optimization details (hyperparameters, number of iterations).  
- Asks for additional visualizations of generated textures, especially for the multi-scale model.  
- Suggests discussing practical applications in computer vision and graphics.  
- Recommends a more comprehensive related-work review covering other texture synthesis and analysis methods.
