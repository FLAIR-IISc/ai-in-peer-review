- Builds on Gatys et al.: empirical Gram matrices of deep feature responses characterize visual textures.  
- Paper studies which network types (deep vs shallow) work for this framework.  
- Key finding: very shallow nets with a single random filter bank perform surprisingly well and can outperform VGG-19 on simple/regular textures.  
- Provides an informative discussion of strengths and limitations of Gram-based texture synthesis methods.  
- Fig. 4 shows shallow-net optimization can yield lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization, attributed to optimization difficulty of the non-linear VGG-19 objective.  
- Supplementary material: initializing VGG-19 optimization with shallow-net results improves VGG-19 optimization outcomes.  
- Main limitation: no systematic, quantifiable comparison; trivial shallow statistics could reproduce a reference texture exactly, so measuring true capture of a texture distribution remains unresolved.  
- Diversity via entropy is suggested but hard in practice and doesn’t capture visual quality; psychophysical evaluation is recommended but left for future work.  
- Overall verdict: paper has sufficient merits for ICLR acceptance, though a psychophysical assessment would strengthen empirical claims.
