- Proposed method: single-layer CNN with random filters as a simple baseline for parametric texture synthesis that produces high-quality textures comparable to VGG-based models.
- Main contributions: a strong minimal baseline; evidence that deep hierarchical or learned features are not necessary for texture synthesis; a quantitative texture-quality measure based on VGG synthesis loss.
- Recommendation: Accept — presents a novel, counterintuitive finding with significant implications for texture-model design and representation understanding.
- Technical soundness: method is well-motivated, experiments are thorough, and analysis is detailed.
- Empirical evaluation: extensive comparisons to VGG-based and other baselines demonstrating effectiveness and revealing strengths and limitations.
- Theoretical analysis: explains Gram matrices’ role in capturing stationary texture structure and highlights the importance of non-linearities.
- Feedback — compare to non-parametric methods, which are often state-of-the-art in texture synthesis.
- Feedback — provide more details and results for the inpainting experiment where VGG performs better.
- Feedback — consider exploring hierarchical models instead of a single-layer multi-filter approach for multi-scale textures.
- Question — provide more details on the optimization procedure and how it was tuned across models.
- Question — how could the approach be extended to handle more complex textures or larger images?
- Question — were other filter types or feature extractors tried, and what were the outcomes?
