- Proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis using multi-scale random filters that sometimes rivals VGG-based models.  
- Authors conclude texture synthesis may not require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.  
- Reviewer finds the work interesting and insightful.  
- Reviewer says the conclusions require further validation, particularly about deep hierarchical representations.  
- Generated samples from both the VGG-based and single-layer models are imperfect and substantially worse than non-parametric methods.  
- VGG-based model appears to perform better on inpainting (Figure 7).  
- Reviewer suggests evaluating whether a true hierarchical model could handle multi-scale textures more efficiently than using many filters of different sizes.
