This paper provides an interesting analysis of the conditions that enable the generation of natural-looking textures. The results are quite surprising, and the analysis is thorough.

I recommend accepting this paper, with the main reason being that it presents a well-motivated and well-executed study that challenges existing assumptions about the requirements for natural texture synthesis. The authors' approach to using a single-layer convolutional neural network with random filters is innovative and leads to impressive results.

One key reason for my decision is that the paper tackles a specific question/problem in a well-defined manner. The authors clearly articulate the research question and provide a thorough analysis of the conditions necessary for natural texture synthesis. The paper is well-placed in the literature, building upon existing work on texture synthesis and deep neural networks.

The paper supports its claims through a combination of theoretical analysis, experimental results, and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods. The authors provide a detailed description of their methodology, including the architecture of their single-layer convolutional neural network, the optimization procedure, and the evaluation metrics used.

One area for improvement is the evaluation methods, as mentioned by other reviewers. While the authors provide a quantitative evaluation of their method using the VGG-based texture model, they also rely on qualitative human inspection. Developing more robust evaluation metrics that can capture the nuances of texture synthesis would strengthen the paper's contributions.

To further improve the paper, I suggest the authors consider the following:

* Provide more details on the optimization procedure, including the choice of hyperparameters and the convergence criteria.
* Discuss the limitations of their approach, including the potential drawbacks of using a single-layer convolutional neural network and the impact of the random filters on the results.
* Explore the relationship between the number of feature maps and the quality of the synthesized textures in more depth.
* Consider using additional evaluation metrics, such as the Structural Similarity Index (SSIM) or the Multi-Scale Structural Similarity Index (MS-SSIM), to provide a more comprehensive assessment of the method's performance.

Questions for the authors:

* Can you provide more insights into the choice of the 11x11 filter size and the number of feature maps (363 and 3267)?
* How do you plan to address the issue of variability in the synthesized textures, which is an important aspect of texture synthesis?
* Have you considered using other types of neural networks, such as recurrent neural networks or generative adversarial networks, for texture synthesis?
