- Paper investigates which aspects of deep feature representations (hierarchy, pooling, training) are necessary for high-quality natural texture synthesis.  
- Main finding: a single convolutional layer with random filters can synthesize textures rivaling state-of-the-art methods.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing a thorough, well-motivated analysis and strong placement in the literature.  
- Primary contributions: (1) a minimal parametric texture-synthesis baseline, (2) demonstration of high-quality textures from that baseline, (3) exploration of many single-layer architectures, (4) use of a quantitative quality measure based on VGG synthesis loss.  
- Key result: hierarchical deep networks, spatial pooling, and learned filters are not required for natural texture synthesis.  
- Multiscale network design is important for modeling small-, mid-, and long-range texture correlations.  
- Discussion highlights the trade-off between perceptual similarity and variability; measuring diversity is challenging and psychophysics is proposed.  
- Limitation: lacks systematic, quantifiable comparisons across methods; authors acknowledge this as an open problem.  
- Suggested improvements: include systematic method comparisons, deeper analysis of limitations, and a psychophysical assessment of generated textures.  
- Reviewer questions: request details on architecture/hyperparameter selection for single-layer networks.  
- Reviewer questions: ask how authors will measure diversity and visual quality in texture synthesis.  
- Reviewer questions: request further insight into why combining scales in the multi-scale network improves synthesis.
