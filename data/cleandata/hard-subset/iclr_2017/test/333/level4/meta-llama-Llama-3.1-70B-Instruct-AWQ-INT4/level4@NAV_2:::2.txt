This paper presents a fascinating examination of the necessary conditions for generating natural-looking textures. The findings are quite surprising, and the analysis is thorough and well-supported.

I recommend accepting this paper, with the primary reason being its thought-provoking and well-executed exploration of the requirements for natural texture synthesis. The secondary reason is that the paper's contributions, including the presentation of a strong minimal baseline for parametric texture synthesis and the demonstration of the effectiveness of single-layer networks with random filters, are significant and well-supported.

The paper's approach to addressing the question of what aspects of feature representation are crucial for natural image generation is well-motivated and well-placed in the literature. The authors provide a clear and concise overview of the current state of the art in parametric texture modeling and effectively situate their work within this context.

The paper's main contributions are well-supported by the results, which demonstrate that textures synthesized from the proposed baseline are of high quality and often rival state-of-the-art approaches. The authors also provide a thorough evaluation of their method, including a comparison with the VGG-based texture model and an analysis of the importance of different filter sizes and types.

One area for improvement is the evaluation methods, which could be further developed and refined. However, as noted by other reviewers, this is an interesting line of work that could be explored in future research and does not detract from the current paper's contributions.

Additional feedback:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the implications of the results, particularly in terms of the broader context of natural image generation.
* The authors may want to consider providing more information about the optimization procedure used in the texture synthesis algorithm, as this appears to be a crucial component of the method.
* The use of a quantitative texture quality measure based on the synthesis loss in the VGG-based model is a useful contribution, but the authors may want to explore other evaluation metrics in future work.

Questions for the authors:

* Can you provide more information about the optimization procedure used in the texture synthesis algorithm, including the specific parameters and hyperparameters used?
* How do you think the results of this paper could be extended to other areas of natural image generation, such as object recognition or image segmentation?
* Are there any plans to explore other evaluation metrics or methods for assessing the quality of the synthesized textures?
