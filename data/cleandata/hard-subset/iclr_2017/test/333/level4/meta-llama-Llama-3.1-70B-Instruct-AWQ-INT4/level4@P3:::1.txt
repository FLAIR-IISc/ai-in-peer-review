This paper proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis, leveraging a single-layer convolutional neural network with completely random filters. The authors demonstrate that this approach can generate high-quality textures that often rival the state-of-the-art VGG-based model, which relies on a deep hierarchical representation with pre-trained filters. This finding challenges the conventional wisdom that texture synthesis necessitates deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

I decide to accept this paper, with the primary reasons being its novelty, thorough experimentation, and insightful conclusions. The authors' contributions are threefold: (1) they present a strong minimal baseline for parametric texture synthesis, (2) they show that textures synthesized from this baseline are of high quality and often rival state-of-the-art approaches, and (3) they provide a quantitative texture quality measure based on the synthesis loss in the VGG-based model.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear and concise overview of the current state of the art in parametric texture modeling. They also thoroughly discuss the limitations of their approach and potential avenues for future research. The experimental results are comprehensive, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the performance of their proposed model.

One potential concern is that the generated samples by both the VGG-based model and the single-layer model are not perfect and may be outperformed by non-parametric methods. Additionally, the VGG-based model seems to perform better in the inpainting task, as shown in Figure 7. However, these limitations do not detract from the overall significance of the paper's contributions.

To further improve the paper, I suggest that the authors consider the following questions:

1. How do the results change when using a hierarchical model with a smaller number of filters, but with different sizes?
2. Can the authors provide more insights into why the random filters perform on par with or even better than the filters trained on the statistics of natural images?
3. How do the results generalize to other types of images, such as natural scenes or objects?

Overall, this paper presents a significant contribution to the field of parametric texture synthesis, and its findings have the potential to impact the development of future texture synthesis models.
