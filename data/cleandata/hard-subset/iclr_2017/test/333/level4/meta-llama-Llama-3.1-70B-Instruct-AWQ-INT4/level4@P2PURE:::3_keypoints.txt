- Paper studies which deep or shallow networks work in the Gatys et al. texture synthesis framework.  
- Main finding: very shallow nets (single random filter banks) perform surprisingly well for texture synthesis.  
- For simple and regular textures, shallow random filters can look visually superior to complex, data-adapted networks like VGG-19.  
- Paper offers an informative discussion on strengths and limitations of Gram-matrix-based texture synthesis methods.  
- Figure 4: optimizing with shallow filter banks can yield images with lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization.  
- Authors attribute this to optimization difficulty of the highly non-linear VGG-19 objective.  
- Supplementary: initializing VGG-19 optimization from shallow-net results improves VGG-19 optimization outcomes.  
- Main limitation: no systematic comparison using a quantifiable objective to evaluate texture distribution capture.  
- Measuring how well methods capture texture distributions remains an open and difficult problem.  
- Authors suggest entropy-based diversity metrics but note practical implementation difficulties and that it misses visual quality.  
- They propose psychophysical assessments as a practical way to evaluate quality/diversity, but leave this to future work.  
- Overall judgement: paper has sufficient strengths for ICLR publication, though a psychophysical evaluation would strengthen the claims.
