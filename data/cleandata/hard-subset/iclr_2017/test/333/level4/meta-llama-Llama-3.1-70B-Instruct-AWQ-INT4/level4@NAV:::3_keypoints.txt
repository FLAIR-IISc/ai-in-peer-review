- Builds on Gatys et al.'s texture-synthesis framework using correlation statistics from deep feature responses.  
- Key finding: extremely shallow networks (single random filter bank) perform surprisingly well for texture synthesis.  
- Simple shallow networks can produce visually superior results for regular/simple textures, sometimes outperforming VGG-19.  
- Figure 4 shows optimization with shallow filter banks can yield images with lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 objective optimization.  
- Authors attribute this to optimization difficulties of the highly non-linear VGG-19 cost function.  
- Supplementary material shows initializing VGG-19 optimization from shallow-network results improves final optimization.  
- Primary limitation: no systematic, quantifiable comparison across methods to evaluate capturing a texture distribution.  
- Claim that simple statistics suffice for quality and diversity is plausible but not conclusively demonstrated.  
- Proposed diversity metric: entropy, but it is practically difficult to estimate and ignores visual quality.  
- Suggest psychophysical evaluation as a remedy, but this is left for future work.  
- Overall assessment: paper is strong enough for ICLR publication, though psychophysical validation would strengthen the claims.
