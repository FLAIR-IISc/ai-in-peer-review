- Builds on Gatys et al.: Gram matrices of deep feature responses effectively characterize visual textures.  
- Paper investigates which network architectures work in this framework, finding very shallow, random-filter banks perform surprisingly well.  
- For simple and regular textures, shallow random filters can produce visually superior results to complex data-adapted networks like VGG-19.  
- Paper provides an informative discussion of strengths and limitations of Gram-based texture synthesis methods.  
- Figure 4: optimizing images w.r.t. shallow filter banks can yield lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization, likely due to VGG-19’s difficult non-linear optimization landscape.  
- Supplementary material: initializing VGG-19 optimization from shallow-network results improves VGG-19 optimization outcomes.  
- Main limitation: no systematic comparison against a quantifiable objective, making it hard to conclude simple statistics truly capture texture distributions.  
- Warning: trivial shallow statistics can exactly reproduce a reference texture without capturing the full texture distribution.  
- Authors propose entropy-based diversity measures (difficult to implement) and psychophysical evaluation (practical but left to future work).  
- Recommendation: paper is publishable at ICLR, but a psychophysical assessment would strengthen and validate the claims.
