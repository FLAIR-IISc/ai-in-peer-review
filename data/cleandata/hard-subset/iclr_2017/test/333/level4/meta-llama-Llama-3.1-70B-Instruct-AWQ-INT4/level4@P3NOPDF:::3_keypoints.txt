- Paper studies which deep or shallow networks work within the Gatys et al. Gram-matrix texture synthesis framework.  
- Main finding: very shallow nets with random filters perform surprisingly well and can outperform complex networks (e.g., VGG-19) on simple/regular textures.  
- Includes a useful discussion of strengths and limitations of Gram-matrix-based texture synthesis methods.  
- Fig. 4: optimizing with shallow filter banks can produce textures with lower VGG-19 loss than directly optimizing the VGG-19 objective, likely due to VGG-19 optimization difficulty.  
- Supplementary: initializing VGG-19 optimization from shallow-net results yields improved optimization outcomes.  
- Major limitation: no systematic, quantifiable comparisons between methods to evaluate how well they capture the texture distribution.  
- Empirical results suggest simple statistics may suffice for quality and diversity, but this conclusion is not conclusively demonstrated.  
- Authors propose entropy-based diversity measures (theoretically reasonable but hard in practice) and psychophysical testing (practical but deferred).  
- Reviewer recommends ICLR publication but suggests adding psychophysical evaluation to validate the claimed intuitions.
