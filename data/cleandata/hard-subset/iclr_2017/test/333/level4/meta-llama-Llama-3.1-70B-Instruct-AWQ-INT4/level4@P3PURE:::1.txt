This work proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis. In empirical experiments, samples generated by this baseline, composed of multi-scale and random filters, sometimes rival those produced by the VGG-based model, which utilizes multi-layer and pre-trained filters. The authors conclude that texture synthesis does not necessarily depend on deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

This work is indeed interesting and insightful. However, the conclusions require further validation, particularly with regards to deep hierarchical representations. Firstly, the generated samples from both the VGG-based model and the single-layer model are imperfect and significantly worse than the results from non-parametric methods. Additionally, the VGG-based model appears to perform better in the inpainting task, as shown in Figure 7. Lastly, it is worth considering whether a hierarchical model, rather than using numerous filters of varying sizes, could handle multi-scale textures more efficiently.
