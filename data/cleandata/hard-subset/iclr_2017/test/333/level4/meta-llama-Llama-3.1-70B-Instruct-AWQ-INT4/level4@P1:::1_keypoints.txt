- Proposes a simple but strong baseline: single-layer network with random filters for parametric texture synthesis.  
- Claims texture synthesis may not require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.  
- Paper is well-motivated with a thorough review of prior parametric texture modeling.  
- Experiments are well-presented and include clear comparison to VGG-based models.  
- Introduces a quantitative texture quality measure based on VGG synthesis loss for objective evaluation.  
- Concern: single-layer model results are not perfect and often worse than non-parametric methods.  
- Concern: VGG-based model performs better in the inpainting task, questioning single-layer generalizability.  
- Unclear whether a hierarchical model with fewer filters could handle multi-scale textures more efficiently.  
- Requests additional experiments: direct comparison between single-layer and non-parametric methods.  
- Requests broader evaluation of the VGG-based model across more texture types.  
- Suggests exploring hierarchical architectures with fewer filters to compare performance.  
- Minor suggestions: reorganize paper flow (intro, related work, methodology) and improve figure/table clarity.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution; decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Additional feedback: discuss implications for hierarchical representations, explore other applications (denoising, super-resolution), evaluate the perceptual validity of the quantitative metric, and detail model limitations and future work.  
- Questions for authors: detail optimization procedure and hyperparameter tuning; possible model improvements and application domains; computational efficiency comparison vs VGG (train/test time); plans to release code/models.
