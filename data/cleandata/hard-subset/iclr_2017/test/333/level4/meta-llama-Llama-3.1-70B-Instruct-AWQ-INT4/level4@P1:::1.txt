This paper proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis, demonstrating that high-quality textures can be generated using a single-layer network with random filters. The authors' conclusion that texture synthesis does not necessarily depend on deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps is intriguing and warrants further investigation.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a thorough review of the current state-of-the-art in parametric texture modeling. The experimental results are also well-presented, with a clear comparison between the proposed single-layer model and the VGG-based model. The use of a quantitative texture quality measure based on the synthesis loss in the VGG-based model is a welcome addition, as it provides a more objective evaluation of the texture models.

However, I have some reservations regarding the conclusions drawn by the authors. Firstly, while the single-layer model with random filters is able to generate high-quality textures, the results are not perfect and are often worse than those obtained using non-parametric methods. This suggests that there may still be room for improvement in the proposed model. Secondly, the VGG-based model appears to perform better in the inpainting task presented in Figure 7, which raises questions about the generalizability of the single-layer model. Finally, it is unclear whether a hierarchical model with a smaller number of filters would be able to handle multi-scale textures more efficiently.

To further strengthen the paper, I would like to see additional experiments that address these concerns. Specifically, I would like to see a more detailed comparison between the single-layer model and non-parametric methods, as well as an evaluation of the VGG-based model's performance on a wider range of textures. Additionally, it would be interesting to see an exploration of hierarchical models with a smaller number of filters to determine whether they can achieve similar performance to the single-layer model.

In terms of minor suggestions, I would recommend reorganizing the paper to make it easier to follow. The current structure jumps abruptly between the introduction, related work, and methodology, which can make it difficult to understand the context of the paper. Additionally, some of the figures and tables could be improved for clarity and readability.

Overall, this is a solid paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of parametric texture synthesis. With some additional experiments and revisions to address the concerns mentioned above, I believe it has the potential to be even stronger.

Decision: Accept with minor revisions.

Additional feedback:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the implications of the results. For example, what do the findings suggest about the importance of hierarchical representations in texture synthesis?
* The authors may want to consider exploring other applications of the proposed single-layer model, such as image denoising or super-resolution.
* The use of a quantitative texture quality measure is a welcome addition, but it would be interesting to see a more detailed evaluation of its effectiveness in capturing the perceptual differences between textures.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the proposed model and potential avenues for future research.

Questions for the authors:

* Can you provide more details about the optimization procedure used in the single-layer model? How did you tune the hyperparameters, and what was the effect of different optimization algorithms on the results?
* How do you think the proposed single-layer model could be improved or extended? Are there any potential applications or domains where the model could be particularly useful?
* Can you provide more information about the computational efficiency of the single-layer model compared to the VGG-based model? How do the models compare in terms of training and testing time?
* Are there any plans to release the code or models used in the paper? This could be useful for other researchers who want to build on the work or evaluate the models on their own datasets.
