- Paper presents a thorough, surprising analysis of conditions that enable natural-looking texture generation.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing a well-motivated and well-executed study that challenges existing assumptions.  
- Novelty: using a single-layer CNN with random filters is an innovative approach producing impressive results.  
- The research question is clearly articulated and tackled in a well-defined manner.  
- Paper is well-situated in the literature, building on prior work in texture synthesis and deep nets.  
- Claims are supported via theoretical analysis, experiments, and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Methodology is described in detail, including network architecture, optimization procedure, and evaluation metrics.  
- Evaluation methods need improvement: reliance on qualitative human inspection alongside VGG-based metrics is a weakness.  
- Suggestion: provide more optimization details (hyperparameters, convergence criteria).  
- Suggestion: discuss limitations of single-layer CNN and impact of random filters.  
- Suggestion: analyze the relationship between number of feature maps and synthesis quality more deeply.  
- Suggestion: include additional evaluation metrics (e.g., SSIM, MS-SSIM) for more comprehensive assessment.  
- Questions for authors: justify choice of 11x11 filter size and feature map counts (363, 3267).  
- Question: how will you address variability in synthesized textures?  
- Question: have you considered other network types (e.g., RNNs, GANs) for texture synthesis?
