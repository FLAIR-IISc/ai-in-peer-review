- Main contribution: shows high-quality natural textures can be generated from single-layer, no-pooling networks with random filters, challenging need for deep supervised features.  
- Decision: reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty and significance.  
- Strength: experiments and analysis are thorough and results are surprising, often rivaling state-of-the-art methods.  
- Evaluation concern: VGG-based synthesis-loss metric may not fully capture perceptual differences between reference and synthesized textures.  
- Suggestion: provide more details on the optimization procedure and tuning that enabled the single-layer random-filter success.  
- Suggestion: discuss limitations such as increased computational cost and potential lack of generalizability to other image-generation tasks.  
- Suggestion: add more comparisons with other state-of-the-art texture synthesis approaches (e.g., GANs, VAEs).  
- Question: request detailed description of the optimization procedure and how it was tuned for single-layer random filters.  
- Question: ask how changing the non-linearity (e.g., sigmoid or tanh vs ReLU) would affect results.  
- Question: ask whether the method has been or could be applied to object/scene generation, and what challenges/limitations are expected.
