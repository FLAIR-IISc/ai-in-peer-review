This work proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis, demonstrating that samples generated by the baseline, which combines multi-scale and random filters, can sometimes rival those produced by the VGG-based model, which utilizes multi-layer and pre-trained filters. The authors conclude that texture synthesis may not necessarily depend on deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

This work is indeed interesting and insightful; however, the conclusions require further validation, particularly with regards to deep hierarchical representations. Firstly, the generated samples from both the VGG-based model and the single-layer model are imperfect and significantly inferior to those obtained through non-parametric methods. Additionally, the VGG-based model appears to perform better in the inpainting task, as shown in Figure 7. Furthermore, it is worth considering whether a hierarchical model, rather than relying on numerous filters of varying sizes, could more efficiently handle multi-scale texture synthesis.
