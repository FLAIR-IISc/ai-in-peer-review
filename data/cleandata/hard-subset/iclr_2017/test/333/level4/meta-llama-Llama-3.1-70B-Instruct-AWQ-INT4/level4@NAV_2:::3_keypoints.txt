- Paper investigates which representation aspects (depth, pooling, training) are essential for natural texture synthesis.
- Shows a single-layer network with random filters can produce textures rivaling state-of-the-art multisite models.
- Claims depth and pre-training of multi-layer representations are less crucial than previously believed.
- Offers a strong, novel minimal baseline for parametric texture synthesis using random single-layer filters.
- Empirical comparisons to Gatys et al. (2015a) demonstrate comparable or superior perceptual texture quality.
- Analyzes effects of model components such as number of feature maps and filter types.
- Recommends acceptance based on novelty and thorough investigation.
- Main limitation: lacks quantitative evaluation, relying on visual inspection.
- Suggest adding more details on the optimization procedure used to generate textures.
- Suggest investigating learned filters and different filter sizes to potentially improve quality.
- Suggest adopting an objective evaluation metric to quantify texture quality.
- Suggest expanding discussion of implications, applications, and limitations of current state-of-the-art models.
- Questions: request details on optimization tuning, exploration of alternative filters, plans for quantitative evaluation, and broader discussion of implications.
