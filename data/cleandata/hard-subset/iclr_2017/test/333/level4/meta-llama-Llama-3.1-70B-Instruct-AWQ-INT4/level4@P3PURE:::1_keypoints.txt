- Proposes a simple, strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis using multi-scale random filters.  
- Empirical results show this baseline sometimes rivals the VGG-based (multi-layer, pre-trained) model.  
- Authors conclude texture synthesis may not require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.  
- Reviewer finds the work interesting and insightful.  
- Reviewer argues the conclusions need further validation, especially about deep hierarchical representations.  
- Both the VGG-based and single-layer models produce imperfect samples that are noticeably worse than non-parametric methods.  
- The VGG-based model appears to perform better on the inpainting task (Figure 7).  
- Suggests a hierarchical model might handle multi-scale textures more efficiently than using many filter sizes.
