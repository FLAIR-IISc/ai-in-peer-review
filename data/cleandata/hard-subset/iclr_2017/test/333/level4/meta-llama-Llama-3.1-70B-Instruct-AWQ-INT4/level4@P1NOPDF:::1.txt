This work proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis. In empirical experiments, samples generated by the baseline, which combines multi-scale and random filters, sometimes rival those produced by the VGG-based model, which utilizes multi-layer and pre-trained filters. The authors conclude that texture synthesis does not necessarily depend on deep hierarchical representations or the learned feature maps.

This work is indeed interesting and insightful. However, the conclusions require further validation, particularly with regards to deep hierarchical representations. Firstly, all generated samples from both the VGG-based model and the single-layer model are imperfect and significantly inferior to the results obtained from non-parametric methods. Furthermore, the VGG-based model appears to perform better in the inpainting task, as shown in Figure 7. Lastly, it is worth considering whether a hierarchical model, as opposed to using numerous filters of varying sizes, could handle multi-scale textures more efficiently.
