This work proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis. In empirical experiments, samples generated by this baseline, which combines multi-scale and random filters, occasionally rival those produced by the VGG-based model, which utilizes multi-layer and pre-trained filters. The authors conclude that texture synthesis does not necessarily rely on deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

This work is indeed interesting and insightful, offering a fresh perspective on texture synthesis. However, the conclusions drawn by the authors require further validation, particularly with regards to deep hierarchical representations. Firstly, the generated samples from both the VGG-based model and the single-layer model are imperfect and significantly inferior to the results obtained from non-parametric methods. Furthermore, the VGG-based model appears to perform better in the inpainting task, as demonstrated in Figure 7. Lastly, it is worth considering whether a hierarchical model, rather than relying on numerous filters of varying sizes, could handle multi-scale texture synthesis more efficiently.

The authors' claim that texture synthesis does not necessitate deep hierarchical representations is intriguing, but it would be beneficial to provide more comprehensive evidence to support this assertion. A more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art non-parametric methods would help to solidify the authors' argument. Additionally, exploring the potential benefits of hierarchical models in handling multi-scale texture synthesis could lead to a more nuanced understanding of the relationship between model architecture and texture synthesis capabilities.

In terms of presentation, the paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their proposed baseline. The experimental results are also well-presented, with informative figures and tables. However, some of the figure captions could be more descriptive, providing additional context for the results being presented.

Overall, this work contributes a valuable perspective to the field of texture synthesis, and with some additional validation and exploration of the conclusions drawn, it has the potential to make a more significant impact.
