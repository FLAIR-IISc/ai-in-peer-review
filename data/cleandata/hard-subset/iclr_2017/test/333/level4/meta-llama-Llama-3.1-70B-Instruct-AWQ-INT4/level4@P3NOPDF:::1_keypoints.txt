- Proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis using multi-scale random filters that occasionally rival VGG-based outputs.
- Authors claim texture synthesis may not require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.
- Review finds the work interesting and offers a fresh, insightful perspective.
- Conclusions about non-necessity of hierarchical representations need further validation.
- Generated samples from both the VGG-based and single-layer models are imperfect and notably worse than non-parametric methods.
- VGG-based model performs better on the inpainting task (Figure 7).
- A hierarchical model might handle multi-scale texture synthesis more efficiently than many filter sizes.
- Recommends a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art non-parametric methods to strengthen claims.
- Suggests exploring hierarchical architectures to better understand architecture–synthesis relationships.
- Paper is well-organized with clear explanation of the baseline and well-presented experimental results.
- Some figure captions lack sufficient descriptive context.
- Overall contribution is valuable but requires additional validation and exploration to have greater impact.
