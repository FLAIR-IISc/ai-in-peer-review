This work proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis, demonstrating that samples generated by a multi-scale and random filter baseline can rival those produced by a VGG-based model, which utilizes multi-layer and pre-trained filters. The authors conclude that texture synthesis does not necessarily rely on deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

The work is indeed interesting and insightful, offering valuable perspectives on texture synthesis. However, the conclusions require further validation, particularly with regards to deep hierarchical representations. Firstly, the generated samples from both the VGG-based model and the single-layer model are imperfect and significantly inferior to those produced by non-parametric methods. Furthermore, the VGG-based model appears to perform better in the inpainting task, as shown in Figure 7. Lastly, it is worth exploring whether a hierarchical model, rather than relying on numerous filters of varying sizes, could handle multi-scale tasks more efficiently.
