- Proposes a simple yet strong baseline: a single-layer CNN with random filters for parametric texture synthesis.  
- Shows this random-filter baseline generates high-quality textures often rivaling VGG-based state-of-the-art.  
- Challenges the assumption that deep hierarchical or pre-trained feature maps are necessary for texture synthesis.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance due to novelty, thorough experimentation, and insightful conclusions.  
- Main contributions: (1) a minimal strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis.  
- Main contributions: (2) empirical demonstration that the baseline produces high-quality textures comparable to SOTA.  
- Main contributions: (3) a quantitative texture quality measure based on VGG synthesis loss.  
- Paper is well-motivated and provides a clear, concise overview of current SOTA in parametric texture modeling.  
- Authors thoroughly discuss limitations and propose avenues for future research.  
- Experimental results are comprehensive with detailed performance analysis.  
- Concern: generated samples from both models are imperfect and can be outperformed by non-parametric methods.  
- Concern: VGG-based model outperforms the single-layer model on inpainting tasks (Figure 7).  
- Suggested experiments: test hierarchical models with fewer filters and varied filter sizes.  
- Suggested analysis: provide insights into why random filters match or exceed trained filters on texture tasks.  
- Suggested validation: evaluate generalization to other image types such as natural scenes or objects.  
- Overall: paper is a significant contribution with potential impact on future texture synthesis models.
