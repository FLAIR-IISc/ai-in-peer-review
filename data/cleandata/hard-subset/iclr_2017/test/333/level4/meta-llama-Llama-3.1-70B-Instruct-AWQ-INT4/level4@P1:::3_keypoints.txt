- Investigates what aspects (hierarchy, pooling, training data) are required for natural texture generation.
- Proposes a minimal parametric texture synthesis baseline: single-layer network with random, data-independent filters.
- Demonstrates this single-layer random-filter method can produce high-quality textures comparable to state-of-the-art.
- Argues that depth and pre-training on natural images may not be necessary for high-quality texture synthesis.
- Provides a quantitative texture quality metric based on VGG synthesis loss for more objective evaluation.
- Shows non-linearities and multi-scale combinations are crucial to capture small-, mid-, and long-range texture correlations.
- Recommends acceptance because the strong minimal baseline challenges conventional assumptions.
- Limitation: lacks systematic comparisons of methods against a quantifiable objective; measuring diversity and visual quality remains unresolved.
- Suggests the paper should expand discussion on implications and potential applications for broader natural image generation.
- Requests concrete plans from authors for measuring diversity and visual quality in texture synthesis.
- Asks for concrete evaluation strategies to compare texture synthesis methods and applicability to other image-generation tasks.
