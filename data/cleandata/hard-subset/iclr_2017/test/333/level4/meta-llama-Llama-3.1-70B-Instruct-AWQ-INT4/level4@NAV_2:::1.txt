This paper proposes a novel approach to natural texture synthesis, leveraging a single-layer convolutional neural network with random filters to generate high-quality textures. The authors demonstrate that their method can rival the state-of-the-art VGG-based model, which relies on a deep hierarchical representation with pre-trained filters. The key contribution of this work lies in its simplicity, as it challenges the conventional wisdom that deep hierarchical representations and learned feature maps are essential for natural texture synthesis.

The paper is well-structured, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The empirical results are impressive, showcasing the effectiveness of the proposed method in generating textures that are comparable to those produced by the VGG-based model. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of their results, discussing the importance of multi-scale features and the role of non-linearities in capturing the statistical dependencies of textures.

I accept this paper, as it presents a significant contribution to the field of natural texture synthesis. The authors' approach is innovative, and their results are well-supported by empirical evidence. However, I do have some minor suggestions for improvement.

One potential concern is that the authors' conclusion that deep hierarchical representations are not necessary for natural texture synthesis might be overstated. While their results are impressive, it is possible that a hierarchical model with a smaller number of filters could achieve similar results. Additionally, the authors' comparison to non-parametric methods is limited, and it would be beneficial to include more comprehensive evaluations.

To further strengthen their argument, the authors could consider exploring the following questions: (1) How does the performance of their method change when using a smaller number of filters? (2) Can a hierarchical model with a smaller number of filters achieve similar results? (3) How does their method compare to other state-of-the-art texture synthesis approaches?

Overall, this paper presents a significant contribution to the field of natural texture synthesis, and with some minor revisions, it has the potential to make an even more substantial impact.

Additional feedback:

* The authors could provide more details about their optimization procedure, including the choice of hyperparameters and the number of iterations.
* It would be beneficial to include more visualizations of the generated textures, particularly for the multi-scale model.
* The authors could discuss the potential applications of their method in more detail, including its use in computer vision and graphics.
* The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive review of related work, including other approaches to texture synthesis and analysis.
