- Proposes a simple parametric texture synthesis baseline using multi-scale random filters.  
- Key insight: texture synthesis may not require deep hierarchical representations or pre-trained features.  
- Empirically, the baseline sometimes produces samples visually comparable to VGG-based models.  
- Primary claim that hierarchical representations are unnecessary lacks sufficient evidence.  
- All evaluated parametric models (including the proposed one) are visibly inferior to non-parametric methods like PatchMatch.  
- Figure 5 shows parametric outputs suffer artifacts (checkerboard patterns, inconsistent textures) absent in non-parametric results.  
- The paper does not explain why the gap to non-parametric methods exists or whether its results actually refute the necessity of hierarchy.  
- Figure 7 (inpainting) shows VGG-based models outperform the single-layer random filter baseline, contradicting the main claim.  
- Dismissing inpainting as a "side task" undermines the broader argument, since inpainting requires contextual understanding related to synthesis.  
- Efficiency concern: using many filter sizes may be less parameter- and compute-efficient than a hierarchical (stacked-layer) model, but no such comparison is made.  
- Evaluation lacks quantitative metrics (e.g., FID, LPIPS, texture-specific scores) to substantiate visual comparisons.  
- Recommended revisions: add quantitative metrics, compare to hierarchical architectures, and clearly discuss limitations (artifacts and inferiority to non-parametric methods); current conclusions are overstated and need further validation.
