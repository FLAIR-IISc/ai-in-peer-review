

This paper presents a compelling analysis of the conditions enabling the generation of natural-looking textures, offering valuable theoretical insights. The findings are surprising and well-supported by a thorough examination of the underlying mechanisms, making a significant contribution to the field of texture synthesis.  

While the methodological rigor of the evaluation could be strengthened—particularly regarding comparative benchmarks and perceptual metrics—the identified limitations are acknowledged by other reviewers and represent a promising direction for future work. Crucially, these considerations do not undermine the paper’s core contributions or the validity of its primary results. The proposed analysis is sufficiently robust to stand on its own merits, and the suggested improvements are more appropriately addressed in subsequent research rather than as a prerequisite for this work.  

The paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and advances our understanding of texture generation beyond existing empirical approaches. Given the novelty of its theoretical framework and the depth of its analysis, the work merits acceptance. The minor evaluation shortcomings noted do not detract from the paper’s overall quality or significance.
