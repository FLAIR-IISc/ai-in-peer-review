- Proposes a simple, effective baseline for parametric texture synthesis using multi-scale, randomly initialized single-layer filters.  
- Empirical results show the baseline occasionally matches VGG-based models, implying deep pre-trained hierarchical features may not be strictly necessary.  
- The insight is valuable and the baseline well-motivated, but the authors’ conclusions require further validation.  
- All generated samples (both baseline and VGG) remain imperfect and are significantly inferior to non-parametric synthesis, questioning the practical utility of parametric methods.  
- Figure 7 shows the VGG-based model outperforms the baseline on inpainting, contradicting the claim that hierarchical representations are unnecessary for such tasks.  
- The proposed use of many multi-scale filters is ad-hoc and may be inefficient; it’s unclear whether a properly designed hierarchical model could match or exceed performance more efficiently.  
- Addressing these concerns would strengthen the paper’s central argument.
