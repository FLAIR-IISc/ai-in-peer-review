- Introduces a simple parametric baseline using multi-scale random filters that sometimes rivals VGG-based models.  
- Authors claim texture synthesis may not require deep hierarchical representations — a conclusion that overreaches.  
- Both the baseline and VGG-based samples are imperfect and consistently worse than state-of-the-art non-parametric (exemplar-based) methods.  
- VGG-based model outperforms the baseline on inpainting (Fig. 7), contradicting the claim that hierarchical features are unnecessary for structurally coherent tasks.  
- Unclear whether the multi-scale filter approach is efficient compared to hierarchical architectures that might use fewer parameters and less computation.  
- It is not demonstrated whether gains come from multi-scale design or simply avoiding deep learned filters; hierarchical models might still offer scalability or quality benefits.  
- Experiments lack comparisons to non-parametric baselines and miss rigorous ablation studies on hierarchical components.  
- Recommended future work: systematically evaluate against non-parametric methods across diverse textures, analyze why VGG excels at inpainting, and directly compare multi-filter vs hierarchical architectures.  
- Overall: provides a useful baseline and raises important questions, but lacks the empirical depth to dismiss hierarchical representations; revisions addressing these points are needed.
