

This paper proposes a simple yet effective baseline for parametric texture synthesis using multi-scale, randomly initialized filters. Empirical results show that samples generated by this baseline occasionally match those produced by VGG-based models—which rely on deep, pre-trained hierarchical features—suggesting texture synthesis may not strictly require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps. While this insight is valuable and the baseline is well-motivated, the authors' conclusions require further validation. Specifically:  
First, all generated samples (from both the VGG-based model and the single-layer baseline) remain imperfect and significantly inferior to non-parametric synthesis methods, raising questions about the practical utility of parametric approaches overall. Second, Figure 7 demonstrates that the VGG-based model outperforms the baseline in the inpainting task, contradicting the claim that hierarchical representations are unnecessary for such applications. Finally, the authors propose using numerous filters of varying scales to handle multi-scale information; however, it remains unclear whether a properly designed hierarchical model (e.g., a deep network with multi-scale pooling or skip connections) could achieve similar or better results with greater efficiency than this ad-hoc filter ensemble. Addressing these points would strengthen the paper’s central argument.
