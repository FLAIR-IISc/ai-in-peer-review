

This work presents an interesting and insightful contribution by proposing a simple yet effective baseline for parametric texture synthesis. The empirical results demonstrate that samples generated using the multi-scale random filter baseline occasionally match or rival those produced by VGG-based models, which employ multi-layer pre-trained filters. The authors argue that texture synthesis may not inherently require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.  

While the findings are noteworthy, the conclusions require further validation, particularly concerning the role of deep hierarchical representations. Firstly, all generated samples from both the VGG-based model and the single-layer baseline remain suboptimal and substantially inferior to non-parametric synthesis methods. Secondly, the VGG-based model exhibits superior performance in the inpainting task (Figure 7). Last but not least, it remains unclear whether a hierarchical architecture (as opposed to multiple filters of varying scales) would achieve more efficient multi-scale handling.
