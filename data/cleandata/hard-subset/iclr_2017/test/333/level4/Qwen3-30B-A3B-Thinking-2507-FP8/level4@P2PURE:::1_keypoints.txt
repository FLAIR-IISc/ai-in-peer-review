- Proposes a simple, strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis using multi-scale, random filters.  
- Empirical results show the baseline sometimes rivals VGG-based models that use multi-layer, pre-trained filters.  
- Authors conclude texture synthesis may not require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.  
- Reviewer says these conclusions need further validation, particularly about the role of hierarchical representations.  
- Both the baseline and VGG-based samples remain suboptimal and are significantly inferior to non-parametric methods.  
- Figure 7 indicates the VGG-based model outperforms the baseline on the inpainting task.  
- A proper hierarchical model (not just multiple filter sizes) might handle multi-scale synthesis more efficiently and warrants investigation.
