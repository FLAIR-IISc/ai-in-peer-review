- Builds on Gatys et al.: Gram matrix statistics of deep feature responses characterize visual textures.  
- Paper investigates which deep or shallow networks work within this texture-synthesis framework.  
- Key finding: very shallow networks with a single random-weight filter bank perform surprisingly well.  
- For simple, regular textures, shallow random filters can produce visually superior results to data-adapted filters (e.g., VGG-19).  
- Paper includes an informative discussion of strengths and limitations of these texture synthesis methods.  
- Fig. 4: optimizing with shallow filter banks can yield lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization, likely due to optimization difficulty of the VGG-19 objective.  
- Supplementary: initializing VGG-19 optimization from shallow-network results improves VGG-19 optimization outcomes.  
- Main limitation: no systematic comparison against a quantifiable objective for evaluating texture distribution capture.  
- Authors propose entropy to measure diversity, but it is practically challenging and does not address visual quality.  
- Recommend psychophysical (human) evaluation to validate visual-quality and diversity claims; left as future work.  
- Overall: paper’s contributions justify ICLR publication, though a psychophysical assessment would strengthen validation.
