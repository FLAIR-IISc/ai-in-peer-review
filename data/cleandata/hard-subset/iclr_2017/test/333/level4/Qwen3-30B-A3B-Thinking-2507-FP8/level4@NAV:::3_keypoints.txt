- Builds on Gatys et al.: empirical Gram matrices of deep features effectively characterize visual textures.  
- Paper systematically investigates which deep or shallow network architectures work within this Gram-matrix texture framework.  
- Key finding: extremely shallow networks (single random-weight filter banks) often perform surprisingly well and can visually outperform complex, data-adapted filters (e.g., VGG-19) on simple/regular textures.  
- Provides an insightful discussion of strengths and limitations of these texture synthesis approaches.  
- Figure 4: shallow filter banks can yield textures with lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization, likely due to the difficulty of optimizing VGG-19’s highly non-linear objective.  
- Supplementary result: initializing VGG-19 optimization from shallow-network outputs significantly improves VGG-19 results.  
- Main limitation: no systematic comparison using a quantifiable evaluation metric, making it hard to conclusively show capture of the underlying texture distribution.  
- Authors propose using entropy to measure diversity—reasonable in principle but practically difficult and insufficient for assessing visual quality.  
- Recommend psychophysical evaluation to validate visual quality and diversity, but this is deferred to future work.  
- Overall: despite evaluation shortcomings, the empirical contributions merit ICLR publication; a psychophysical study would strengthen validation.
