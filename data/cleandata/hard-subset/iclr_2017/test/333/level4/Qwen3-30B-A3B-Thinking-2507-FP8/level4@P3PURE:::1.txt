

This work proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis. In empirical experiments, samples generated by the baseline—composed of multi-scale random filters—sometimes rival those of VGG-based models, which employ multi-layer and pre-trained filters. The authors conclude that texture synthesis does not necessarily depend on deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.  

While the work is interesting and insightful, the conclusions require further validation, particularly regarding deep hierarchical representations. First, samples generated by both VGG-based and single-layer models are of suboptimal quality and significantly inferior to results from non-parametric methods. Second, the VGG-based model appears to outperform the single-layer model in the inpainting task (Figure 7). Finally, it remains unclear whether a hierarchical model would handle multi-scale processing more efficiently than using numerous filters of varying sizes.
