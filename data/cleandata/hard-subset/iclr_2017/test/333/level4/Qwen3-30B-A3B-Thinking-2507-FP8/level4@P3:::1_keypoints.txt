- Summary: Challenges the need for deep pretrained features — shows single-layer conv nets (random, Fourier, K-means, PCA filters) can synthesize high-quality natural textures; multi-scale model often rivals VGG; introduces VGG loss and thorough ablation studies.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Key reason 1: Rigorously tests whether deep hierarchical representations are necessary for texture synthesis.  
- Key reason 2: Robust empirical evidence across multiple architectures and datasets; multi-scale model consistently challenges prevailing assumptions.  
- Supporting argument: Systematic ablations isolate depth, filter type, and scale to show neither hierarchy nor pretraining is indispensable.  
- Supporting argument: Multi-scale combination — not just larger receptive fields — enables modeling of both local and global texture statistics.  
- Supporting argument: Random filters often match or outperform learned filters, attributed plausibly to improved optimization rather than a methodological error.  
- Supporting argument: VGG loss provides a quantitative alternative to subjective evaluation, strengthening methodological rigor.  
- Limitation noted: Synthesized textures sometimes show artifacts on highly structured patterns (e.g., grids), a general limitation of texture synthesis.  
- Suggestion 1: Briefly contextualize why the method struggles with highly regular textures — optimization vs inherent single-layer limitations.  
- Suggestion 2: Expand the theoretical discussion on why random filters work (e.g., implicit capture of statistical regularities).  
- Suggestion 3: Clarify the perceptual-similarity vs variability trade-off and its relation to observed artifacts.  
- Question 1: Is the K-means-363 model’s occasional outperformance of random filters systematic or dataset-dependent?  
- Question 2: Could a hierarchical stacked small-filter model achieve similar multi-scale performance more efficiently, and why are hierarchical architectures inferior in quality here?  
- Question 3: How should VGG loss’s failure on very regular man-made structures inform future perceptual metrics for texture synthesis?
