

This work proposes a simple yet strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis. In empirical experiments, samples generated by this baseline—which employs multi-scale and random filters—sometimes rival those produced by VGG-based models (which utilize multi-layer, pre-trained filters). The authors conclude that texture synthesis does not necessarily require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.  

While this work is interesting and insightful, its conclusions require further validation, particularly regarding deep hierarchical representations. First, all generated samples from both the VGG-based model and the single-layer baseline remain suboptimal and significantly inferior to results from non-parametric methods. Second, as shown in Figure 7, the VGG-based model demonstrates superior performance in the inpainting task. Finally, a hierarchical model (as opposed to multiple filters of varying sizes) may handle multi-scale synthesis more efficiently—this question warrants investigation.
