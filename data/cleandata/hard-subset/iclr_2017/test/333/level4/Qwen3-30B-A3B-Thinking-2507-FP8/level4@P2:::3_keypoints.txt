- Main contribution: A single-layer convolutional network with random filters (no depth, pooling, or pre-training) can synthesize natural textures at perceptual quality rivaling state-of-the-art.  
- Core finding: Texture synthesis success depends on the number of feature maps and multi-scale filtering, not network depth or trained filters.  
- Empirical approach: Systematic experiments across diverse filter banks (Fourier, K-means, random) isolate the effects of depth, training, and pooling.  
- Quantitative metric: Introduces a VGG-based reconstruction loss to complement human evaluation and provide an objective benchmark.  
- Optimization result: The shallow model often achieves lower VGG loss than VGG-based synthesis, attributed to more robust optimization and fewer local minima.  
- Perceptual outcomes: Multi-scale random filter model produces high-quality textures, often outperforming Gatys et al. for structured textures (e.g., bricks, grids).  
- Theoretical implication: Challenges the assumption that deep hierarchical networks are essential for texture synthesis, providing a strong minimal baseline.  
- Trade-off discussion: Analyzes the trade-off between perceptual similarity and sample variability, noting current metrics imperfectly capture human perception.  
- Limitations acknowledged: Texture diversity remains hard to quantify and VGG loss is an imperfect proxy for perceptual quality.  
- Proposed evaluation: Recommends a psychophysical assessment (Appendix A) as a practical step toward better evaluation.  
- Suggested clarification: Authors should explicitly state scope is texture synthesis only (avoid overgeneralizing "natural textures").  
- Suggested improvements: Elaborate how to adjust optimization bias (loss scaling, stopping criteria) to increase synthesis variability.  
- Suggested addition: Quantify the VGG loss advantage (e.g., percent reduction) to emphasize significance of results.  
- Key questions: How would results compare to a deep network with equivalent receptive fields, and can you give examples where VGG loss misaligns with human preference and how to address it?
