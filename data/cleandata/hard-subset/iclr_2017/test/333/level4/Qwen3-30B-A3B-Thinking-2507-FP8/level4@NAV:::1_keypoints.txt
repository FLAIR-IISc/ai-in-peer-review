- Proposes a simple, effective multi-scale random filter baseline for parametric texture synthesis.  
- Empirical results show the baseline occasionally matches or rivals VGG-based multi-layer pre-trained models.  
- Authors argue texture synthesis may not require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.  
- Reviewer says conclusions need further validation about the role of deep hierarchical representations.  
- Both VGG-based and single-layer baseline samples are suboptimal and substantially worse than non-parametric methods.  
- VGG-based model outperforms the baseline on the inpainting task (Figure 7).  
- It remains unclear whether a true hierarchical architecture (vs. multiple filters at different scales) would more efficiently handle multi-scale structure.
