- Paper challenges the assumption that depth, pooling, and supervised pre-training are necessary for high-quality texture synthesis.  
- Shows high-quality textures can be synthesized with a single-layer CNN of random filters, no pooling, and no pre-training.  
- Key enabler is a well-tuned optimization (L-BFGS-B with scaled gradients) that rivals prior deep methods.  
- Ablation study compares eight single-layer architectures (Fourier, K-means, PCA, random with 363/3267 maps).  
- Finding: a large number of feature maps is crucial for perceptual quality.  
- Finding: random filters often outperform trained filters like K-means.  
- Finding: multi-scale filter combinations (e.g., 3×3, 11×11, 55×55) capture long-range correlations better than single-scale models.  
- Finding: non-linearities matter—ReLU-less models fail to match performance.  
- Paper uses a quantitative VGG-based loss for evaluation, acknowledging limitations on highly regular textures.  
- Results are reproducible and comparisons to Gatys et al. (2015a) are fair and comprehensive.  
- Decision: Accept. Two main reasons—(1) rigorously addresses what aspects of deep nets are indispensable, and (2) exceptionally thorough experimental design.  
- Suggested improvement: provide deeper analysis of why random filters succeed (statistical reasoning or theoretical insight).  
- Suggested improvement: discuss variability vs. similarity trade-off and practical tuning (e.g., early stopping) to increase sample diversity.  
- Suggested improvement: include qualitative comparison of VGG loss vs. human judgment for failure cases like the "wall" texture.  
- Questions for authors: did you test a single-scale model with 1024 filters all of size 55×55 to isolate scale diversity vs. receptive field size?  
- Question: could performance differences be due to filter count (363 vs. deep 19-layer features); how would a 19-layer net with 363 filters compare?  
- Question: what happens if the VGG loss is directly optimized on the single-layer Gram matrix—does the metric favor single-layer synthesis inherently?
