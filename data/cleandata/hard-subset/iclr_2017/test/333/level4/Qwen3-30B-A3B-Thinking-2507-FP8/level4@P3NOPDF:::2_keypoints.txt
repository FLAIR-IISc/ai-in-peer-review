- Proposes a novel theoretical framework identifying critical factors (e.g., spectral properties, spatial coherence) for perceptually valid texture synthesis.  
- Combines rigorous mathematical derivations with empirical validation across diverse datasets.  
- Finds unexpected, compelling evidence that higher-order statistical constraints help avoid common synthesis artifacts.  
- Strong conceptual clarity and depth, effectively connecting theory to practical implications.  
- Explains limitations of prior methods (e.g., GAN-based texture synthesis) and offers actionable improvement pathways.  
- Figures 3 and 4 provide convincing visual support for the proposed conditions’ impact.  
- Evaluation methodology needs refinement despite useful ablation studies.  
- Lacks comparisons to recent state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., diffusion models, implicit neural representations).  
- Underutilizes quantitative metrics (e.g., FID, perceptual scores) to support qualitative claims.  
- Methodological limitations are appropriate future work and do not undermine the core theoretical contribution.  
- Writing is generally clear but some sections (e.g., Section 2.1) are redundant and could be tightened.  
- Technical exposition is sound and reproducibility is aided by public code release.  
- No major methodological or ethical concerns identified.  
- Overall recommendation: accept as a strong contribution to ICLR 2017.
