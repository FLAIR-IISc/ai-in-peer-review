- Paper studies which network architectures (deep vs shallow) are best for Gram-matrix–based texture synthesis.  
- Main finding: very shallow networks (single random-weight filter bank) perform remarkably well on simple, regular textures, sometimes visually outperforming VGG-19.  
- Authors provide a thoughtful discussion of the strengths and limitations of shallow vs complex texture synthesis approaches.  
- Figure 4: optimizing textures with shallow filter banks can yield lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization, due to optimization difficulty of the non-linear VGG-19 objective.  
- Supplementary material: initializing VGG-19 optimization with shallow-network results improves performance, offering a practical refinement.  
- Primary limitation: no systematic comparison against a quantifiable objective measuring how well methods capture the underlying texture distribution.  
- Concern that trivial statistics might produce high visual quality without capturing the true texture distribution; evaluation remains an open challenge.  
- Empirical results indicate shallow statistics provide quality and diversity comparable to complex alternatives, but lack of standardized metrics prevents conclusive validation.  
- Authors propose entropy-based diversity metrics but note practical implementation difficulties and that entropy alone does not capture visual quality.  
- They suggest psychophysical assessments as the most viable evaluation, but this is deferred to future work.  
- Overall assessment: paper makes a significant, well-supported contribution; publication is justified, with recommendation to pursue psychophysical validation in future work.
