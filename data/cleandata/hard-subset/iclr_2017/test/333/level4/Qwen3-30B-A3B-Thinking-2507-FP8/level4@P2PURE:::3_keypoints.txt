- Places work in Gatys et al. framework showing Gram-matrix statistics characterize visual textures.  
- Investigates which deep or shallow networks work effectively within that texture-synthesis framework.  
- Key finding: very shallow networks with a single random filter bank perform surprisingly well.  
- For simple, regular textures, shallow/random filters can produce visually superior results to VGG-19.  
- Paper provides an informative discussion of strengths and limitations of these texture-synthesis methods.  
- Figure 4: optimizing with shallow filter banks can yield lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization.  
- Explanation: VGG-19 objective is highly non-linear and difficult to optimize directly.  
- Supplementary result: initializing VGG-19 optimization from shallow-network outputs improves VGG results.  
- Primary limitation: no systematic comparison against a quantifiable objective for measuring texture distribution fidelity.  
- Proposal to measure diversity via entropy is reasonable but practically challenging and insufficient for visual quality.  
- Recommends psychophysical evaluation as the most viable future approach to validate visual quality and diversity.  
- Overall assessment: paper merits publication at ICLR despite evaluation limitations.
