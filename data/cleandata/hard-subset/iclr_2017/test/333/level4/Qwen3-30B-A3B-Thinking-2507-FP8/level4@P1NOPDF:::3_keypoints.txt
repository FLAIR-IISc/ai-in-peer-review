- Builds on Gatys et al.: Gram matrices of deep features characterize visual textures.  
- Paper studies which deep or shallow networks work within this Gram-matrix framework.  
- Key finding: very shallow networks (single random filter bank) perform surprisingly well for texture synthesis.  
- Shallow random filters often produce visually superior results for simple, regular textures compared to VGG-19.  
- Figure 4: optimizing images with shallow filter banks can yield lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization.  
- Authors attribute that result to the difficulty of optimizing the highly non-convex VGG-19 objective.  
- Supplementary: initializing VGG-19 optimization with shallow-network results improves final performance.  
- Main limitation: lack of systematic comparison against a robust, quantifiable objective for texture distribution modeling.  
- Diversity via entropy is proposed but practically difficult and does not guarantee visual quality.  
- Authors recommend psychophysical assessment for objective evaluation; it remains future work.  
- Overall: empirical results and insights into shallow networks are compelling and justify publication, though psychophysical validation would strengthen the claims.
