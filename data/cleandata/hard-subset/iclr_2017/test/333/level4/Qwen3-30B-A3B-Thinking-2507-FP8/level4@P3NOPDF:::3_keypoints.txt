- Paper investigates texture synthesis via feature correlation (Gram matrices), comparing shallow vs deep networks.  
- Key finding: single-layer random filter banks often outperform complex data-adapted networks (e.g., VGG-19) on simple, regular textures.  
- Shows shallow methods can be computationally efficient and produce high visual quality for specific textures.  
- Empirical support: Figure 4 shows shallow-filter optimization can yield lower VGG-19 perceptual losses than direct VGG-19 optimization.  
- Supplementary result: initializing VGG-19 optimization with shallow-network outputs substantially improves VGG results.  
- Main weakness: lack of a systematic, quantitative evaluation framework to distinguish trivial pixel copying from true texture synthesis.  
- Authors propose entropy-based diversity metrics and psychophysical tests but do not perform psychophysical validation.  
- Entropy is a plausible proxy for diversity but has practical limitations and unclear correlation with human perception.  
- Without objective or human-subjective evaluation, the claim that shallow statistics suffice remains inconclusive.  
- Contributions are novel and practically useful (challenge conventional wisdom; useful initialization technique), but methodological validation is needed.  
- Recommendation: prioritize psychophysical validation and standardized metrics in future work; paper is nonetheless strong and suitable for publication.
