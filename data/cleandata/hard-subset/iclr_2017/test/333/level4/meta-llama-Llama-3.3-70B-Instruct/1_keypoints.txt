- Presents a simple, robust baseline using multi-scale random filters that can sometimes match VGG-based, multi-layer pre-trained filter performance.
- Authors claim texture synthesis may not require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.
- The work is interesting and offers valuable insights.
- Conclusions need stronger validation, especially regarding the role of deep hierarchical representations.
- Generated samples from both VGG and single-layer models have visible imperfections and lag behind non-parametric methods in quality.
- VGG-based model appears to outperform the proposed method on the inpainting task (Figure 7).
- Suggests investigating whether a true hierarchical model could handle multi-scale processing more efficiently than many differently sized filters.
