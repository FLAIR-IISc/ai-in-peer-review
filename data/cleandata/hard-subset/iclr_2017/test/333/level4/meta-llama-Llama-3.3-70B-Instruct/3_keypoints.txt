- Builds on Gatys et al.: correlation statistics (Gram matrices) of deep features capture visual textures and this paper examines which networks work best for that framework.  
- Key finding: very shallow networks (single random-weight filter banks) perform surprisingly well for texture synthesis.  
- For simple and regular textures, shallow nets can produce visually superior results to complex, data-adapted networks like VGG-19.  
- Provides an engaging discussion of strengths and limitations of texture-synthesis methods.  
- Figure 4: optimizing images for shallow filter banks can yield lower VGG-19 loss than direct VGG-19 optimization, likely due to VGG-19’s highly non-linear, harder-to-optimize cost.  
- Supplementary results: initializing VGG-19 optimization with shallow-network outputs leads to improved VGG-19 optimization results.  
- Primary limitation: no systematic, quantifiable comparison of methods against an objective that measures how well a method captures the texture distribution.  
- Empirical results hint simple statistics suffice for quality and diversity, but this cannot be conclusively confirmed.  
- Authors propose using entropy to measure diversity but note practical difficulties and that entropy ignores visual quality.  
- Recommend psychophysical evaluation as a practical way to judge visual quality and diversity, but this is left for future work.  
- Reviewer stance: paper is strong enough for ICLR publication but would benefit from including psychophysical assessment.
