This research proposed a simple yet robust baseline for parametric texture synthesis. In empirical experiments, samples generated by a baseline created by multi-scale and random filters sometimes rivaled VGG-based models, which have multi-layer and pre-trained filters. The authors concluded that texture synthesis does not necessarily rely on deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.

This research is indeed interesting and insightful. However, the findings need further validation (especially for deep hierarchical representations). Firstly, not all samples generated by both VGG and single-layer models are perfect and are far worse than results obtained from non-parametric methods. Furthermore, in Figure 7, the VGG-based model appears to perform better in the inpainting task. Finally, will a hierarchical model (rather than many filters of varying sizes) handle multi-scale more efficiently?
