- Builds on Gatys et al.: deep-feature Gram matrix statistics effectively characterize visual textures.  
- Paper studies which deep or shallow networks work within this texture-synthesis framework.  
- Main finding: very shallow nets (single random filter bank) perform surprisingly well, sometimes visually outperforming VGG-19 on simple/regular textures.  
- Provides an informative discussion of strengths and limitations of Gram-matrix-based texture synthesis methods.  
- Figure 4: optimizing images w.r.t. shallow filter banks can yield lower VGG-19 loss than directly optimizing the VGG-19 objective, attributed to VGG-19’s harder optimization landscape.  
- Supplementary: initializing VGG-19 optimization with shallow-network results improves VGG-19 optimization outcomes.  
- Major limitation: no systematic, quantifiable comparison across methods — hard to conclude methods capture the true texture distribution rather than trivially reproducing examples.  
- Trivial, shallow statistics could perfectly reproduce a reference texture, highlighting that the real goal is modeling texture distributions, not just matching one sample.  
- Measuring diversity is open and difficult; entropy is suggested but impractical, and it doesn’t capture visual quality.  
- Psychophysical evaluation is recommended as the practical way to assess visual quality and diversity but is deferred to future work.  
- Overall judgment: paper has sufficient strengths for ICLR publication, though a psychophysical assessment would strengthen its claims.
