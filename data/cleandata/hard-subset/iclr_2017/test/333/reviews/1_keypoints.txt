- Proposes a simple but strong baseline for parametric texture synthesis using multi-scale random filters.  
- Empirical results show the baseline sometimes rivals VGG-based (multi-layer, pre-trained) models.  
- Authors conclude texture synthesis may not require deep hierarchical representations or learned feature maps.  
- Review finds the work interesting and insightful.  
- Calls for further validation of the conclusions, especially about deep hierarchical representations.  
- Notes generated samples from both VGG and single-layer models are imperfect and substantially worse than non-parametric methods.  
- Observes the VGG-based model seems to perform better on inpainting (Figure 7).  
- Asks whether a hierarchical model (rather than many filter sizes) might handle multi-scale structure more efficiently.
