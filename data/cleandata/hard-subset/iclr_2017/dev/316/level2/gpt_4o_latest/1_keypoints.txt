- Introduces PATE: teacher ensemble trained on disjoint sensitive subsets with noisy aggregation to label a student under differential privacy.
- Student model trained on non-sensitive/public data inherits teacher knowledge while preserving strong privacy guarantees.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art privacy/utility trade-offs on MNIST (98%) and SVHN (90.66%) with reported (ε, δ) = (2.04, 10⁻⁵) for MNIST.
- Key contributions: moments accountant privacy analysis, integration of GAN-based semi-supervised learning, and algorithm-agnostic applicability.
- Decision: Accept — paper is well-motivated, methodologically sound, and a significant improvement in privacy-preserving ML.
- Well-motivated problem statement with clear real-world relevance and grounding in prior work.
- Strong empirical results that improve upon previous methods in accuracy and privacy.
- Novelty and generality: black-box, applicable across model types (deep nets, random forests); GANs enhance semi-supervised use.
- Rigorous privacy analysis using the moments accountant yields tighter privacy bounds than earlier approaches.
- Suggestion: provide clearer discussion of applicability limits (e.g., imbalanced tasks, limited non-sensitive data).
- Suggestion: include scalability analysis for computational overhead of large teacher ensembles, many classes, or small per-teacher data.
- Suggestion: broaden empirical evaluation to more diverse datasets (text, medical) to support generality claims.
- Suggestion: compare student performance against non-private baselines trained on the same data to contextualize utility loss.
- Question: How does performance scale with number of teachers and dataset size; are there diminishing returns?
- Question: Can the moments accountant be further optimized for larger output spaces or more complex data distributions?
- Question: How does PATE perform when public/non-sensitive unlabeled data is scarce or unavailable?
- Conclusion: PATE is a practical, theoretically sound framework with strong empirical results and clear avenues for further exploration.
