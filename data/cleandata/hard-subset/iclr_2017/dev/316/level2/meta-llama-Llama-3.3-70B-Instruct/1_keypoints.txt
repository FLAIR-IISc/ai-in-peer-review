- Proposed method (PATE): train teacher ensemble on disjoint sensitive subsets, aggregate noisy teacher votes to label unlabeled non-sensitive data, train a student with differential-privacy guarantees.  
- Main contribution: a general, black-box strategy for providing differential privacy for training data.  
- Improvement over prior work: demonstrates better protection of training data while maintaining model utility.  
- Student dependence reduction: explores methods to reduce how much the student relies on teachers.  
- Privacy analysis: introduces a novel application of the moments accountant to improve DP analysis of knowledge transfer.  
- Empirical evaluation: state-of-the-art privacy/utility trade-offs on MNIST and SVHN; shows applicability to different model structures and datasets.  
- Overall assessment: well-motivated, clearly written, impressive results — justifies acceptance.
