- Proposes privacy-preserving training using multiple teacher models on disjoint datasets that label a student via noisy voting.
- Theoretical contributions are solid and intuitive, arguing the student is unlikely to replicate teacher behavior due to noise.
- Privacy/probabilistic guarantees depend on several empirical parameters, making it hard to definitively assess full security.
- Experiments on MNIST and SVHN are well-executed.
- Suggest adding one or two experiments on sensitive domains (e.g., medical records) to demonstrate real-world applicability.
