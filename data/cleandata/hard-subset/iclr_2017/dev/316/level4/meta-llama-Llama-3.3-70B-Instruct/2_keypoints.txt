- Proposes training a student model using noisy voting among multiple teacher models trained on disjoint datasets to protect training-data privacy.  
- Theoretical findings are appealing and intuitive: noisy teacher voting reduces the chance the student replicates teacher behavior, improving privacy.  
- Privacy guarantees are probabilistic and rely on several empirical parameters, making absolute security assurances unclear.  
- Experimental evaluation on MNIST and SVHN is well-executed and supportive.  
- Claiming suitability for sensitive data (e.g., medical records) is not empirically validated in the paper.  
- Additional experiments on real-world sensitive datasets are needed to substantiate the approach’s applicability.
