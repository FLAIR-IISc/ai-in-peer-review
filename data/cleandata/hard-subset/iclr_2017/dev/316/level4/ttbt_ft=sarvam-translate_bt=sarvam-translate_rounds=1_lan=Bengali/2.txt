This paper discusses how to ensure the confidentiality of training data. In the proposed method, multiple models trained on isolated datasets are used as "teacher" models, which will train a "student" model to predict an output chosen through noisy voting among all the teachers.

The theoretical results are excellent, but also intuitive. Since the results of the teachers are provided through noisy voting, the student model may not perfectly mimic the teacher's behavior. However, there are several empirical parameters within the probabilistic limit that make it difficult for me to decide whether the security is 100% guaranteed.

The experiments on MNIST and SVHN are good. However, as the paper claims, the proposed method is probably more suitable for sensitive data like medical history; it would be good to conduct one or two experiments on such applications.
