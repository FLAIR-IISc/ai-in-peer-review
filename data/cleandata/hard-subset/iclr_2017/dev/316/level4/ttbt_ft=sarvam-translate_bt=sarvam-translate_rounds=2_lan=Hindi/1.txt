This paper addresses the problem of achieving differential privacy in a very common scenario where a group of teachers is trained on an unrelated subset of sensitive data, and the student makes predictions based on public data labeled by the teachers through noisy voting. I found this approach to be entirely commendable and very clearly explained by the authors. Further discussion on the limit (and its tightness) will be appreciated from Theorem 1 onwards. A simple idea of adding the error of miscalculation to the calculations known from differential-privacy literature has been beautifully reused by the authors and elegantly applied in a much broader (non-convex setting) and practical context compared to differential-privacy and other related papers. The generality of the approach, the clear improvement over predecessors, and the clarity of the writing make this method publishable.
