This research paper discusses how to guarantee privacy for training data. In the proposed approach, multiple models trained on unrelated datasets are used as "teacher" models, which will train a "student" model to predict the output chosen by noisy voting among all the teachers.

The theoretical results are good but also intuitive. Since the results of the teachers are provided through noisy voting, the student model cannot replicate the teacher's behavior. However, there is a significant number of empirical parameters in the probabilistic limit, making it difficult for me to determine whether security is 100% guaranteed.

Experiments on MNIST and SVHN are good. However, as claimed in the paper, the proposed approach may be useful for mostly sensitive data such as medical history; it would be good to conduct one or two experiments on such applications.
