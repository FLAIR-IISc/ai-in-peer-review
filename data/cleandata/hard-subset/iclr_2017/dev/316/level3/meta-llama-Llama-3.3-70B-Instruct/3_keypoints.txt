- Proposes Higher Order RNNs (HORNNs): novel recurrent structure using multiple memory units to capture longer-term dependencies; paper is well-written with thorough related work.
- Reviewer recommends rejection primarily due to lack of theoretical guarantees about HORNNs' learning performance.
- Empirical results show effectiveness on language modeling but no theoretical analysis of model capabilities is provided.
- Concern that the approach may not generalize to other architectures; more experiments on different architectures are encouraged.
- Claims significant contribution to differentially-private deep learning, but reported privacy epsilons are not privately releasable.
- A technique is needed to produce privately releasable epsilons for meaningful comparison with related work.
- Doubt that the approach will work across other natural data types; experiments on more datasets strongly encouraged.
- Paper needs clarifications in specific sections: last paragraph of 3.1, section 4.1, and the discussion of Figure 3.
- Related work is thorough but missing a survey of differentially-private semi-supervised learning, which should be added.
- Section C’s comparison must ensure an apples-to-apples quantitative comparison with GAN results.
- Reviewer questions for authors: (1) provide theoretical guarantees and comparison to other RNNs; (2) explain how to obtain privately releasable epsilons and techniques to use; (3) provide more experiments on other datasets and architectures to demonstrate generalizability.
