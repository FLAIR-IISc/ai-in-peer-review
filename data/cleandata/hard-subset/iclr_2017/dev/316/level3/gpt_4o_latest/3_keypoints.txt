- Proposes PATE: train teacher models on disjoint sensitive-data subsets and train a student on noisy aggregated teacher outputs to achieve differential privacy in deep learning.  
- PATE is broadly applicable to various models, including non-convex deep networks, and provides intuitive and formal privacy guarantees.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art privacy-utility trade-offs on MNIST and SVHN using semi-supervised learning with GANs and an improved moments-accountant privacy analysis.  
- Decision: Accept — significant contribution advancing differentially-private deep learning and offering a generalizable framework.  
- Empirical results are strong, showing high student accuracy under strict privacy guarantees and improvements over prior work (e.g., Abadi et al. 2016).  
- The paper is well-written and thorough, with a comprehensive related-work discussion and an innovative use of semi-supervised GANs to reduce privacy loss.  
- Generality supported by experiments with convolutional nets and random forests across MNIST, SVHN, UCI Adult, and Diabetes datasets.  
- Lacks theoretical guarantees for learning performance; future work should provide bounds or conditions ensuring PATE’s effectiveness.  
- Reported epsilon values are not privately releasable; authors should adopt a differentially-private mechanism for releasing privacy budgets.  
- Experiments are limited to image and tabular data; testing on text, time-series, or other data types is needed to strengthen generality claims.  
- Clarity issues to fix: rephrase Section 3.1 last paragraph, formalize the Section 4.1 trade-off on number of teachers, and revise the discussion of Figure 3 for consistency.  
- Related work omission: include differentially-private semi-supervised teacher-learner methods from the 2013 literature.  
- Open questions for authors: generalization to complex data types (NLP/sequential), provision of a DP mechanism to release epsilon, and scalability limits for larger datasets or many output classes.  
- Conclusion: PATE is a promising, strong contribution to DP deep learning despite areas needing improvement.
