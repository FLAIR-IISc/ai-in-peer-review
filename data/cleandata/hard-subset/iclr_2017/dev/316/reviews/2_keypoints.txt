- Proposes training multiple teacher models on disjoint datasets and using noisy voting among teachers to label examples for a student model to preserve training-data privacy.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound and intuitive, providing probabilistic privacy bounds.  
- Privacy bounds depend on many empirical parameters, making it unclear whether the guarantees amount to full/absolute security in practice.  
- Because labels are chosen via noisy voting, the student may not perfectly replicate teacher behavior.  
- Experiments on MNIST and SVHN are solid, but the paper lacks evaluations on truly sensitive domains (e.g., medical records) that are the target application.
