- Proposes PATE: aggregate knowledge from teacher ensembles trained on disjoint data, transfer to a student via noisy voting to ensure differential privacy.  
- Main contributions: general DP learning strategy; improved privacy analysis using the moments accountant; semi-supervised GANs to reduce privacy loss; strong privacy/utility on MNIST and SVHN.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novelty & impact: introduces a practical, generalizable, theoretically sound approach for differential privacy in ML.  
- Scientific rigor: provides detailed privacy analysis with moments accountant and empirical validation on benchmarks.  
- Empirical strength: demonstrates superior privacy/utility trade-offs versus prior methods like noisy SGD.  
- Generality: algorithm-agnostic approach applicable to varied models (including deep nets and random forests).  
- Well-motivated: builds on prior DP and knowledge transfer work to address protecting sensitive training data.  
- Use of semi-supervised learning with GANs to reduce the number of private queries and privacy loss.  
- Suggestion — clarity: privacy analysis sections are dense and need more figures/examples for non-experts.  
- Suggestion — evaluation: expand experiments beyond MNIST/SVHN to diverse, real-world privacy-sensitive datasets (e.g., healthcare).  
- Suggestion — scalability: discuss scaling PATE to larger tasks and complex output spaces where many teachers may be required.  
- Suggestion — comparisons: compare PATE-G to other state-of-the-art semi-supervised methods on utility and privacy.  
- Question — multi-class scaling: how does PATE handle tasks with many output classes and mitigations for large teacher counts?  
- Question — computational overhead: details on overhead from the moments accountant and noisy aggregation mechanisms?  
- Question — noise sensitivity: how sensitive are results to Laplace noise scale (γ) and can it be adaptively tuned?  
- Question — sequence models: can PATE be applied to RNNs/transformers, and what challenges arise?  
- Overall conclusion: significant contribution to privacy-preserving ML and suitable for conference acceptance.
