- Paper proposes a heuristic training method for deep directed generative models where each layer samples from the same conditional distribution, replacing the posterior with a heuristically constructed approximation each step.
- The approximating distribution is not updated to improve a variational lower bound and conditionals are optimized greedily rather than following the joint log-likelihood gradient.
- The approach is interesting and worth exploring but may have limited impact given existing methods that are theoretically stronger, similarly efficient, and practical (e.g., Rezende & Mohamed, 2015).
- Major concern: empirical evaluation is limited and needs strengthening to demonstrate practical value.
- Positive: inclusion of proper log-likelihood estimates for continuous MNIST enables future comparisons.
- Caveat: reported comparisons to Wu et al. (2016) are misleading since those numbers don’t represent a VAE with more flexible observation models.
- Missing comparisons for inpainting tasks to other methods (e.g., diffusion approaches); evaluating practicality and inpainting performance is a missed opportunity.
- Minor issues: missing citation for “ordered visible dimension sampling.”
- Minor issues: typos and frequent incorrect use of \citet and \citep.
