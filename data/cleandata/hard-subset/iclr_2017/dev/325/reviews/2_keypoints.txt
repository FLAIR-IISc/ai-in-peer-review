- Paper proposes training a generative model via iterative denoising initialized from a crude data approximation and improved by a Markov-chain blending procedure.
- Presentation is clear, the approach is simple and interesting, and the paper is enjoyable to read.
- Method is novel but closely related to prior work (Sohl-Dickstein, DAEs) and shares conceptual ties to energy-based/MCMC perspectives.
- Promising direction because it may avoid GAN convergence/mode-collapse issues and problems from restrictive inference models paired with powerful generators.
- Missing direct comparisons to related methods (e.g., HMC+variational inference and Sohl-Dickstein) in terms of sampling steps and generation quality.
- Key training details are omitted: how alpha and omega are set and how alpha affects generator capabilities; more experiments varying alpha are needed.
- No infusion or generating chain visualizations are shown for complex datasets, which would be informative.
- Evaluation is strong, including multiple metrics and a log-likelihood bound.
- Lacks theoretical guarantees and a clear statement about for which alpha choices the procedure works or its relation to MCMC/energy models; a brief note in conclusion would help.
- Minor issues: broken reference, Figure 3 starts at 100 epochs (poorly informative), confusing description of convolutional architecture relative to "Improved GANs", unusual variance parametrization via sigmoid, and several typos/grammatical errors.
- Update: Authors’ response addressed open issues; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance but notes the generator architecture may be sub-optimal though not crucial.
