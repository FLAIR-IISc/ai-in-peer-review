- Trains a generative model that maps noise to samples via a gradual denoising process similar to diffusion models.  
- Uses only a small number of denoising steps, making it far more computationally efficient than diffusion.  
- Inference chain jumps to q(z(0)|x) then runs in the same direction as the generative model, acting as a perturbation toward the data (akin to ladder networks).  
- There is no tractable variational bound on the log-likelihood.  
- Reviewer liked the idea and found short-chain visual sample quality impressive.  
- Inpainting results are strong and enable one-shot inpainting not usually possible in other generative frameworks.  
- Requests a more convincing log-likelihood comparison that does not rely on Parzen estimates.  
- Asks how sensitive results are to the infusion rate.  
- Claims in Sec.5 that the method “appears to provide more accurate models” are unsupported due to no direct comparison with Sohl-Dickstein et al.  
- Lists several minor typos/phrasing fixes in Sec.2, Sec.3.3, and Sec.4.  
- Fig. 4 is noted as neat.
