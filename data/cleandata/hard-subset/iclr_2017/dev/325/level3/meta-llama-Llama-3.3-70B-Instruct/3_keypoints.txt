- Proposes PATE, a novel approach to provide strong privacy guarantees for training data.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on MNIST and SVHN, claiming state-of-the-art privacy/utility trade-offs.  
- Well-motivated approach building on knowledge aggregation/transfer and framed within differential privacy.  
- Provides detailed privacy analysis and empirical comparisons to Abadi et al. (2016) and Shokri & Shmatikov (2015).  
- Concern about uncertain impact given other theoretically sound, similarly complex practical approaches.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited and needs more comparisons to assess practicality and effectiveness.  
- Suggests more detailed comparisons with other privacy-preserving techniques, including differential privacy and federated learning.  
- Recommends deeper analysis of privacy/utility trade-offs and applicability to other domains/datasets.  
- Asks how the number of teachers affects privacy cost and what the optimal number is for given tasks/datasets.  
- Requests more detailed analysis of the moments accountant technique and its role in bounding privacy cost.  
- Wants a direct comparison of PATE to other privacy-preserving methods in terms of privacy/utility trade-offs.  
- Concludes the paper has significant potential but needs more analysis and comparisons.  
- Decision: reject for now due to limited empirical evaluation, but may accept after revisions addressing concerns.
