- Proposes a novel iterative denoising method for training generative models; clearly written and interesting.  
- Method connects to recent denoising-based sampling work and is potentially novel in sidestepping issues of directed generative model training.  
- Claims potential to address convergence, mode coverage, and multi-modal distribution modeling problems.  
- Paper’s stated problem: providing strong privacy guarantees for training data, especially for sensitive data.  
- Approach is well-motivated and grounded in literature, building on differential privacy and knowledge transfer.  
- Supports claims with theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation on benchmark datasets showing PATE balances privacy and utility.  
- Recommendation: Accept, based on a well-motivated, novel approach and effective results.  
- Strengths: clear presentation, addresses important ML problems, strong empirical evaluation.  
- Weaknesses: lacks direct comparisons to other methods and omits detailed training/hyperparameter information.  
- Suggested improvements: add more comparisons, provide experimental setup/hyperparameter details, and test PATE on other domains/datasets.  
- Minor issues to clarify: unclear convolutional network description and unusual variance parameterization via a sigmoid output.  
- Question: How does the number of teachers affect privacy cost and what is the optimal teacher count per dataset?  
- Question: How does PATE compare to Abadi et al.’s differential privacy method?  
- Question: Can PATE be applied to other model families (e.g., RNNs, transformers)?
