- Contribution: Proposes "infusion training" to learn a Markov chain transition operator that progressively denoises noise into data samples by biasing the chain with target exemplars.
- Positive novelty: Creative, simpler alternative to variational optimization that avoids GAN/VAE complexities and is computationally efficient.
- Empirical results: Demonstrates competitive samples and inpainting on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CelebA, and provides log-likelihood estimates for future comparisons.
- Efficiency claim: Generates high-quality samples in fewer steps than diffusion-based methods like Sohl-Dickstein et al.
- Empirical limitations: Evaluation is insufficient to establish robustness, generalizability, and practical impact.
- Missing comparisons: No thorough comparisons with state-of-the-art methods (e.g., GANs/VAEs) for inpainting and sample-quality metrics.
- Theoretical concerns: Heuristic approach lacks theoretical grounding and insufficient justification/analysis of the surrogate loss.
- Suggested metrics: Include rigorous quantitative metrics (e.g., FID, precision-recall) and SOTA baselines to strengthen evaluation.
- Clarify limitations: Explicitly discuss limits of the approach, especially how heuristic posterior approximation affects latent-variable inference.

- Broader applicability: Needs exploration on more diverse datasets and modalities (e.g., text, speech) to demonstrate generalizability.
