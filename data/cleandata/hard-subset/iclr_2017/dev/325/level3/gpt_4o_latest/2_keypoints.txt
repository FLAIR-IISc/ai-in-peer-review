- Introduces "infusion training": an iterative denoising Markov chain that biases the chain toward the target distribution to learn a small-step denoising transition operator.  
- Claims a simpler alternative to GANs and diffusion methods by using a single network and avoiding adversarial training instability.  
- Demonstrates competitive empirical performance on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CelebA with sharp, diverse samples.  
- Validates model with multiple metrics (log-likelihood bounds, Inception scores) and shows successful inpainting results.  
- Decision: Accept — significant, practical, and conceptually simple contribution deserving publication.  
- Novelty: Extends denoising-based generative models with fewer denoising steps and an adaptive training chain compared to prior work.  
- Importance: Addresses efficient sampling and multimodal distribution learning, relevant to generative modeling challenges.  
- Missing theoretical guarantees on Markov chain convergence and effects of hyperparameters like infusion rate.  
- Lacks direct quantitative comparisons to certain state-of-the-art alternatives (e.g., HMC with variational inference).  
- Training details unclear: roles and impact of parameters (alpha, omega) need fuller explanation and ablation studies for reproducibility.  
- Visualization shortcomings: should include infusion/sampling chain visualizations for complex datasets (CIFAR-10, CelebA).  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to infusion rate and schedule, scalability to higher resolutions/architectures, and extension to conditional tasks.  
- Additional fixes: correct reference errors, clarify variance parameterization, discuss computational efficiency versus GANs/diffusion models, and show failure cases/biases.
