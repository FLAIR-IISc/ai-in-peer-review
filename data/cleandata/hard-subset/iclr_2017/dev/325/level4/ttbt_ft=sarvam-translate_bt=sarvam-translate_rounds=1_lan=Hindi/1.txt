This paper trains a generative model that converts noise into model samples through a sequential denoisement process. It is similar to a diffusion-based generative model. Unlike the diffusion approach:
- It uses only a few denoisement steps, and is therefore computationally more efficient.
- Instead of having a reverse trajectory, the conditional chain q(z(0) | x) jumps to the predicted posterior, and then moves in the same direction as the generative model. This allows the prediction chain to behave like a disturbance around the generative model, pulling it towards the data. (This also seems somewhat related to a ladder network.)
- There is no easy variance bound on the log likelihood.
I liked the idea, and the visual sample quality looking at a small series was impressive. The inprenting results were particularly good, as a shot inprenting is not possible under most generative modeling frameworks. It would be more convincing to compare the log likelihood that does not depend on the Poisson likelihood.
