Summary:

This paper introduces a heuristic approach for deep-learning generative model training, where each layer collects samples from the same conditional distribution, which is the transition operator of a Markov chain. The core idea of this approach is to approximate the gradient using another distribution instead of the latent background, such as optimizing a variable lower bound. However, the approximate distribution is not updated to improve the lower bound; rather, it is created declaratively at each step. Another difference from variable optimization is that the conditional distributions are optimized greedily, instead of following the joint log-probability gradient.

Review:

The proposed method is interesting and seems to me worth exploring further. Since there are similar methods for training similar models that are 1) theoretically more reliable, 2) have approximately the same computational complexity, and 3) work well in practice (e.g., Rezende and Mohamed, 2015), I am still unsure about its potential to create an impact. However, my main concern is that its empirical evaluation is still quite limited.

I am grateful to the authors for their accurate estimates of the log-probability. This will enable and encourage future comparisons of this method with MNIST. However, the authors should keep in mind that the numbers taken from Wu et al (2016) do not represent the performance of VAE. (From the paper: "Therefore, the log-probability values we report should not be directly compared with networks that have more flexible observation models. Such observation models can easily achieve much higher log-probability scores, [...].")

It would have been good to compare the inpainting results using other methods. How practical is the proposed method compared to other methods? Like the diffusion method by Soh-Dickstein et al (2015), the proposed method seems to be both efficient and effective for inpainting. It seems like missing an opportunity to make an accurate assessment without exaggerating it.

Minor flaws:

- The citation for "Ordered Visible Dimension Sampling" is missing.
- Typos and incorrect use of \citet and \citep.
