This paper trains a generative model that gradually converts noise into the model's sample through a denoise process. It is similar to a diffusion-based generative model. In contrast to the diffusion method:

- It uses only a small number of denoise steps and is therefore much more computationally efficient to date.
- Instead of a backward trajectory, the approximate backward conditional chain jumps in the same direction as q (z (0) | x) and then moves in the same direction as the generative model. This allows the inference chain to behave as a perturbation around the generative model, pulling it towards the data. (It also seems somewhat related to a ladder network.)
- There is no simple variational bound on the log likelihood.

I liked the idea, and the visual sample quality seemed impressive given the short chain. The inpainting results were particularly beautiful, as one-shot inpainting is not possible under most generative modeling frameworks. It would have been more convincing if a log likelihood comparison had been done that does not depend on the Poisson probability.
