Summary:

This paper introduces a heuristic approach for deep-learning generative model training, where each layer collects samples from the same conditional distribution as the transition operator of a Markov chain. Similar to optimizing a variational lower bound, the core idea of this method is to approximate the gradient using another distribution instead of the background on the latent. However, the approximate distribution is not updated to improve the lower bound; rather, it is created declaratively at each step. Another difference from variational optimization is that the conditional distributions are optimized greedily, rather than following the gradient of joint log-likelihood.

Review:

The proposed method is interesting and seems more worth exploring to me. Since there are similar methods for training similar models that are 1) theoretically more reliable, 2) have approximately the same computational complexity, and 3) work well in practice (e.g., Rezende & Mohammed, 2015), I am still unsure about its potential to create an impact. However, my main concern is that its empirical evaluation is still quite limited.

I am grateful to the authors for their accurate estimates of log-likelihood. This will enable and encourage future comparisons of this method with continuous MNIST. However, the authors should note that the numbers taken from Wu et al. (2016) do not represent the performance of VAE. (From the paper: "Therefore, the log-likelihood values we report should not be directly compared with networks that have more flexible observation models. Such observation models can easily achieve much higher log-likelihood scores, [...].")

It would have been good to compare the inpainting results using other methods. How practical is the proposed method compared to other methods? Like the diffusion method by Sohl-Dickstein et al. (2015), the proposed method seems to be both efficient and effective for inpainting. It seems like missing an opportunity to make an accurate assessment without exaggerating it.
