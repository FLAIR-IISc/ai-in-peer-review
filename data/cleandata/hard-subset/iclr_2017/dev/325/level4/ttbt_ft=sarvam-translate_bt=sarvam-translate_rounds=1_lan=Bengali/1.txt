This paper trains a generative model that gradually converts noise into model samples through a denoise process. It is similar to a diffusion-based generative model. Unlike the diffusion method:

- It uses only a small number of denoise steps, and is therefore much more computationally efficient to date.
- Instead of a backward trajectory, the approximate posterior conditional chain jumps in the same direction as q(z(0) | x) and then moves in the same direction as the generative model. This allows the inference chain to behave like a perturbation around the generative model, pulling it towards the data. (This also seems somewhat related to a ladder network.)
- There is no simple variational bound on the log likelihood.

I liked the idea, and the visual sample quality seemed impressive given the short chain. The inpainting results were particularly beautiful, as one-shot inpainting is not possible under most generative modeling frameworks. It would have been more convincing if there was a comparison of a log likelihood that did not depend on the Poisson likelihood.
