- Proposes a novel, simple iterative denoising method that starts from a crude data-distribution sample and refines it via multiple denoising steps.  
- Training frames a Markov chain that blends the model's proposals with real data and updates the model to reproduce the improved blended samples.  
- Paper is well-written, enthusiastic, and presents an intriguing, clearly motivated approach inspired by prior denoising-based generative work (e.g., Sohl-Dickstein, DAEs).  
- The research direction is important for addressing GAN issues like convergence and mode coverage and for modeling multi-modal distributions.  
- Notes that alternative methods (e.g., combining Hamiltonian Monte Carlo with variational inference) might also address these problems and could outperform the proposed approach.  
- Recommends direct empirical comparison to Sohl-Dickstein’s procedure (sampling steps and generation quality) due to their close relation.  
- Suggests broader comparisons between different MCMC-like inference methods, though this may be out of scope.  
- Some training details are missing—specifically how alpha and omega are set and how alpha influences generator capability.  
- The absence of infusion chains or generating chains for more complex data distributions is unfortunate and worth examining.  
- Empirical evaluation is strong, with multiple metrics and a provided bound on log-likelihood.  
- The approach currently lacks theoretical guarantees; the impact of alpha and connections to MCMC or energy-based models remain unclear.  
- Minor issues: a broken reference, Figure 3 starts at 100 epochs limiting insight, unclear convolutional network description, odd variance parametrization via sigmoid, and typographical errors (footnote 1 and pages 1 and 4).  
- The author’s response addressed open issues; despite a sub-optimal generator architecture the reviewer strongly supports acceptance and values the paper’s contributions.
