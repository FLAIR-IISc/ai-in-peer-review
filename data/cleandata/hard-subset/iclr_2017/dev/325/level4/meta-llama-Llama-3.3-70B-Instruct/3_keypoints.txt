- Proposes a heuristic training method for deep directed generative models where each layer samples from the same conditional distribution, akin to a Markov chain transition operator.  
- Approximates gradients by replacing the true posterior with a heuristically constructed alternative distribution, resembling a variational lower-bound approach but without updating the approximating distribution to improve the bound.  
- Optimizes conditional distributions greedily instead of following the gradient of the joint log-likelihood, differing from standard variational optimization.  
- Method is intriguing and worth exploring but may have limited impact given existing alternatives that are theoretically cleaner and similarly efficient (e.g., Rezende & Mohamed 2015).  
- Major concern: empirical evaluation is limited.  
- Positive: includes proper log-likelihood estimates for continuous MNIST, enabling future comparisons.  
- Critique: reported numbers from Wu et al. (2016) are not directly comparable to VAE results and need a more nuanced discussion.  
- Missing comparisons for inpainting against other methods and insufficient emphasis/evaluation of practicality and efficiency (e.g., relation to diffusion methods like Sohl-Dickstein et al. 2015).  
- Minor issues: missing citation for "ordered visible dimension sampling."  
- Minor issues: typos and frequent incorrect usage of \citet and \citep.
