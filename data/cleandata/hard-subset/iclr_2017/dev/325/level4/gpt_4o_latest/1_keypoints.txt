- Proposes a progressive denoising generative model similar to diffusion models but using only a limited number of denoising steps, yielding much lower computational cost.  
- The inference chain directly transitions to q(z(0)|x) and proceeds in the same direction as the generative model, acting as a perturbation that guides generation toward the data.  
- This inference design has conceptual overlap with ladder networks.  
- There is no tractable variational bound on the log likelihood for the proposed method.  
- Visual sample quality is impressive even with a short chain; one-shot inpainting results are particularly noteworthy.  
- The paper should include a log-likelihood comparison that does not rely on Parzen estimates.  
- The claim that the method “appears to provide more accurate models” is not demonstrated and needs direct comparison (e.g., to Sohl-Dickstein et al.).  
- How sensitive are the results to the infusion rate? experimental sensitivity analysis is requested.  
- Minor editorial/typo fixes needed: Sec.2 (“theta(0) the” → “theta(0) be the”, “theta(t) the” → “theta(t) be the”, “what we will be using” → “which we will be doing”, paragraph break after “q.”), Sec.3.3 (“learn to inverse” → “learn to reverse”), Sec.4 (“For each experiments” → “For each experiment”).
