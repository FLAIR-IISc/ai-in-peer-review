- Proposes a novel iterative denoising training method that refines samples from a rough prior via a Markov chain blending model samples with real data.
- Training updates the denoising model to reproduce improved blended samples, producing high-quality outputs over multiple denoising steps.
- Paper is well-written, simple, and conceptually appealing, with parallels to Sohl-Dickstein and denoising autoencoder-based generative methods.
- The approach is significant because it could mitigate GAN issues (convergence, mode collapse) and difficulties modeling multi-modal distributions with restrictive inference.
- Reviewer notes similarity to HMC+variational inference (Salimans et al.); unclear whether the proposed method outperforms that approach, though it may be simpler to train.
- Questions the authors’ claim that MCMC-based MC-EM sampling is infeasible—suggests it might work on simple datasets like MNIST and calls for more direct comparisons.
- Expects a direct comparison to Sohl-Dickstein’s method in terms of sampling steps and generation quality given the close relationship.
- Major missing training details: unclear how alpha and omega are set and how alpha affects generator performance and failure modes.
- Recommends experiments varying alpha (including extreme values like alpha=1) to clarify when the procedure succeeds or fails.
- Lacks experiments showing infusion or generating chains on more complex data distributions.
- Evaluation is strong: multiple metrics and a bound on log-likelihood are provided.
- Method lacks theoretical guarantees and a clear connection to MCMC or energy-based models; suggests addressing this in the conclusion.
- Minor issues: broken second reference.
- Figure-related critique: Figure 3 starts at 100 epochs—should show the full training curve and consider a log-scale x-axis.
- Architectural clarity: description of convolutional/generator architecture is unclear and likely incorrect compared to the referenced Improved GANs setup.
- Questionable design choice: variance parameterized with a sigmoid output unit—motivation not explained.
- Typos and grammar errors in footnote and on Pages 1 and 4 that should be corrected.
- Update: authors’ response resolved open issues; reviewer strongly supports acceptance, noting only that a suboptimal generator architecture may limit empirical performance.
