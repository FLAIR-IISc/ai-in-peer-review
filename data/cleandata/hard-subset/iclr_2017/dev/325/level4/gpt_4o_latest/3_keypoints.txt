- Proposes a heuristic training method for deep directed generative models where each layer samples from the same conditional distribution (like a Markov chain transition).  
- Approximates the gradient by replacing the true posterior with an alternative distribution constructed heuristically at each step, rather than variationally optimizing it.  
- Optimizes conditional distributions greedily instead of following the gradient of the joint log-likelihood.  
- Method is intriguing but impact is uncertain given existing alternatives that are theoretically more robust and similarly efficient (e.g., Rezende & Mohamed 2015).  
- Primary concern: empirical evaluation is limited.  
- Positive: authors provide proper log-likelihood estimates, enabling future comparisons on continuous MNIST.  
- Clarify that log-likelihoods cited from Wu et al. (2016) are not representative of VAE performance due to more flexible observation models.  
- Missed opportunity: no comparisons of inpainting results with other methods; the approach may be efficient/effective for inpainting (cf. Sohl-Dickstein et al. 2015) and should be evaluated.  
- Minor: missing citation for "ordered visible dimension sampling."  
- Minor: typos and frequent incorrect usage of \citet and \citep.
