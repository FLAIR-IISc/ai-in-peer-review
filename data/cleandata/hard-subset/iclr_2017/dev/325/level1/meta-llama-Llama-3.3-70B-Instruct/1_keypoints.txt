- Proposes "infusion training": learn a generative Markov chain that progressively denoises noise into samples by infusing information from training examples during training.
- Training samples from a slightly different chain than the generation chain, where target-example information is injected into the chain.
- Claims: produces high-quality, diverse samples in a small number of steps.
- Demonstrated on MNIST, Toronto Face Database, CIFAR-10, and CelebA.
- Decision: Accept.
- Reason 1: Tackles a well-motivated problem of learning efficient transition operators for generative sampling.
- Reason 2: Method is well-situated within prior work (denoising autoencoders, GSNs, MCMC).
- Reason 3: Paper gives a clear, detailed explanation of the infusion training procedure and experimental setup.
- Supporting argument: Provides theoretical analysis including a lower bound on the model log-likelihood.
- Supporting argument: Experimental results show effectiveness across multiple datasets.
- Supporting argument: Compares favorably and competitively to prior approaches like GANs and VAEs.
- Requested improvement: Provide a more detailed analysis of the infusion rate schedule and how to choose it.
- Requested improvement: Evaluate scalability with experiments on larger datasets.
- Requested improvement: Include more comparisons to other generative families (normalizing flows, autoregressive models).
- Questions for authors: How is the optimal infusion rate schedule chosen for a dataset?
- Questions for authors: Why does infusion training yield high-quality samples in few steps?
- Questions for authors: How does this approach relate to recent denoising diffusion models?
