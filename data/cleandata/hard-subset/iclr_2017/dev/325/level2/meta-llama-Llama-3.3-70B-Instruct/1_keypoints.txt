- Proposes learning a generative model as a Markov chain where a learned transition operator progressively denoises noise into data.  
- Introduces a "target-infusion" technique: sample from a slightly different chain and infuse training-target information into the chain.  
- Claims significant improvement over GANs and VAEs using a single network and a simpler training objective.  
- Claims fewer denoising steps and more accurate models than previous methods.  
- Decision: accept the paper.  
- Reason 1 for acceptance: well-motivated, novel approach that is well-situated in the literature and addresses an important problem.  
- Reason 2 for acceptance: thorough evaluation with numerical results and generated samples demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Paper provides detailed model and training procedure descriptions and comparisons to existing methods.  
- Reviewer requests more discussion of limitations, potential applications, and avenues for future work.  
- Asks authors to clarify how the infusion rate affects performance and what its optimal value is.  
- Asks authors to clarify how the number of denoising steps affects performance and what the optimal number is.  
- Requests more details on the neural network architecture used and its design choices.  
- Requests clearer comparison to GANs and VAEs regarding sample quality and diversity.
