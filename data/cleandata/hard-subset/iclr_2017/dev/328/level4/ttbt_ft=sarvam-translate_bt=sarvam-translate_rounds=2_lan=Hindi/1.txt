This paper explores the potential of nonlinear recurrent neural networks to explain the neural response properties of distant models. A multilevel RNN is trained to mimic the stimulus-response mapping measured from real retinal ganglion cells in response to a sequence of natural images. The RNN performs significantly better than traditional LNM/GLM models, especially considering transient responses.

This work is an important step in understanding the nonlinear response properties of visual neurons. Recent results have shown that even the responses of retinal ganglion cells in response to natural movies are difficult to explain in terms of standard receptive field models. Therefore, this presents a significant challenge for the field. If we even have a model that works, it would be a starting point. Therefore, this work should be viewed in that light. The challenge now is certainly to distinguish what the RNN is doing. Perhaps it can now be simplified by narrowing down which parts are important for performance. It would be good to see such an analysis. Nevertheless, this result is a good start, and I think it is important for people to know about it.
