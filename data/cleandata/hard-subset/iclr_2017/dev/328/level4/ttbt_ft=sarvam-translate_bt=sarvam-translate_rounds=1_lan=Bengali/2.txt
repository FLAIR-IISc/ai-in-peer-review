Here's a clearly written research paper with a promising, yet simple, result: RNNs (Recurrent Neural Networks) could be a good predictive model for retinal neuron firing rates.

On one hand, the initial scientific contribution seems to have only been to confirm that the method works. The gains from using RNNs in this particular stimulus-bound task were relatively small, and it didn't teach us anything new about biology.

On the other hand, it (along with contemporary work by Macintosh and others) is introducing neural network modeling into a field where it is currently not being used, and where it will prove very effective.

I think it would be very interesting to see the results of applying such a framework using LFP and other neurons as input, and on a smaller discretization time scale.

I believe that further work based on the proof of this concept will be even more exciting.
