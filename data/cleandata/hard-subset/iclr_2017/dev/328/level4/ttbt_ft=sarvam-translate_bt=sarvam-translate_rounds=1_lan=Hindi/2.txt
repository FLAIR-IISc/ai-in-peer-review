This is a clearly written paper with a promising result, albeit straightforward: RNNs can be good predictive models of neuron firing rates in the retina.

On one hand, the primary scientific contribution seems to be merely confirming that this approach works. The benefits of using RNNs on this particular stimulus-locked task appear relatively minor, and it hasn't taught us anything new about biology yet.

On the other hand, it (along with the concurrent work of McIntosh et al.) is introducing neural network modeling into a field that currently doesn't use it, and where it should prove very effective.

I think it would be very interesting to see the results of applying such a framework to LFP and other neurons as input and on a lower discretization time scale.

I suspect that follow-up work based on evidence of this concept will be increasingly exciting.
