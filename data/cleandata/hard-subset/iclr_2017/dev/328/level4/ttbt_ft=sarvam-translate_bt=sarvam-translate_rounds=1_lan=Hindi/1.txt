This paper explores the ability of nonlinear recurrent neural networks to explain the neural response properties of otherwise distant models. A multilayer RNN is trained to mimic the stimulus-response mapping measured from real retinal ganglion cells in response to a sequence of natural images. The RNN performs significantly better than traditional LN/GLM models, especially considering transient responses.

This work is a significant step in understanding the nonlinear response properties of visual neurons. Recent results have shown that even the responses of retinal ganglion cells in response to natural movies are difficult to explain in terms of standard receptive field models. Therefore, this presents a significant challenge for the field. If we even have a model that works, it would be a starting point. So, this work should be viewed in that light. The challenge now is certainly to isolate what the RNN is doing. Perhaps it can now be pruned and simplified to see which parts are important for performance. It would be good to see such an analysis. Nevertheless, this result is a good start, and I think it is important for people to know about it.
