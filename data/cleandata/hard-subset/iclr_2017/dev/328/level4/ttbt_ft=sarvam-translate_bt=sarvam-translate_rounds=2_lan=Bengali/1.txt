This paper discusses the ability of nonlinear recurrent neural networks to explain neural response characteristics that other models have failed to do. A multilayer RNN is trained to simulate the stimulus-response mapping from actual retinal ganglion cells in response to a sequence of natural images. Compared to conventional LN/GLM models, RNNs perform significantly better in transient responses.

This work is an important step in understanding the nonlinear response characteristics of visual neurons. Recent results have shown that the responses of retinal ganglion cells in response to natural images are also difficult to explain through standard receiver field models. Therefore, this is an important challenge for this field. If we even have a model that works, it will be a starting point. So this work should be looked at in that light. Now the challenge must be to see what the RNNs are doing separately. Perhaps now it can be seen by pruning and simplifying its parts to see which parts are important for performance. It would be nice to see such analysis. Nevertheless, this result is a good start and I think people need to know.
