- Paper shows RNNs can predict retinal neuron firing rates; presentation is clear and results straightforward.
- Main scientific contribution is confirmation that RNNs work here, with only modest gains for this stimulus-locked task and no new biological insight.
- Together with McIntosh et al., the work could introduce neural-network modeling to an underutilized field and bring significant benefits.
- Extending the framework to include LFPs and other neurons as inputs could make results more compelling.
- Exploring shorter discretization time scales may yield stronger findings.
- Follow-up studies building on this foundation are likely to produce more interesting outcomes.
- Clarify the purpose of the 0.833 ms bins described in Section 3.2.
- Use the term "epoch" consistently instead of alternating with "pass through data."
- Plot Figure 4 with a logarithmic x-axis to improve visualization and interpretation.
