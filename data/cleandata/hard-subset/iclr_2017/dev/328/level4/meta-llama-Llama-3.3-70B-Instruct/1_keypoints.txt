- Paper studies multilayer RNNs for modeling neural responses that other models fail to capture.  
- RNN trained to replicate stimulus-response mapping of retinal ganglion cells to sequences of natural images.  
- RNN substantially outperforms LN and GLM models, especially in modeling transient responses.  
- Work is a foundational step toward understanding nonlinear response properties of visual neurons.  
- Prior difficulty of standard receptive-field models to explain responses to natural movies makes this functional model valuable.  
- Missing but important next step: dissect RNN mechanisms (e.g., via pruning/simplification) to identify critical components.  
- Reviewer unclear about term "movie"—seems to be unrelated 1 s images; frame-rate mention (1/8.33 ms) appears confusing or tied to monitor refresh.  
- Reviewer speculates that using real dynamic natural scenes would likely increase deviations from LN models and amplify RNN advantages.
