- Suggestion to add the word "activity" to the title for formal precision.
- Paper introduces an RNN for time-series spike prediction and compares it to the prior-standard GLM.
- Underlying premise: improved spike prediction can enable insights into neural processing mechanisms.
- Results (Fig. 2): RNN achieves slightly better spike prediction than the GLM, but discrepancies with real data remain.
- Reviewer unconvinced the work yields significant new biological insights and asks "what comes next?"
- Authors should elaborate on specific insights gained from the improved predictive model.
- RNN outperforming GLM is not surprising given known retinal recurrence and multilayer structure.
- LSTMs and more advanced recurrent architectures did not improve performance, but dataset size may limit such comparisons.
- With more data or more detailed retinal-structure modeling, deeper/more complex models might further improve accuracy.
- Surprised all neurons share identical parameters; neuron-specific parameters could outperform given sufficient training data and merit exploration.
