- Multilayer RNN trained to reproduce retinal ganglion cell responses to sequences of natural images.  
- RNN shows marked improvement over conventional LN/GLM models, especially in capturing transient responses.  
- Represents a significant advance in modeling nonlinear visual neuron responses that standard receptive field models struggle with.  
- Provides a valuable starting point for tackling the challenge of explaining responses to natural movies.  
- Missing mechanistic dissection: pruning or simplifying the network to identify critical components would strengthen the work.  
- Terminology ambiguity: the term "movie" is unclear—needs clarification whether it means 1s sequences of unrelated images versus continuous frames and how the 1/8.33 ms frame rate is used.  
- Reviewer suspects using real dynamic natural scenes would amplify deviations from LN models and further highlight the RNN’s advantages.
