- Proposes a novel multitask RNN framework to model parasol ganglion cell spiking responses to natural images.
- RNN-based models consistently outperform traditional GLMs in predictive accuracy, including with limited data.
- Introduces a hybrid GLM-RNN that separates spatial and temporal processing for insight into RNN-captured computations.
- Multitask approach shares features across neurons, improving predictive performance and model stability.
- Demonstrates deep networks’ utility for capturing complex nonlinearities, with relevance to retinal prosthetics and higher sensory modeling.
- Decision: Accept — paper makes a significant contribution to computational neuroscience.
- Strong empirical results: robust performance across datasets, metrics, and ablation studies.
- Claims are well-supported and use explainable variance as an appropriate evaluation metric.
- Well-grounded in prior literature and established neuroscience practices (receptive field estimation, spike-triggered averaging).
- Provides sufficient methodological detail for reproducibility (data preprocessing, architectures, training).
- Suggests improving interpretability by visualizing temporal filters and analyzing specific recurrent units.
- Recommends comparisons with other deep architectures (CNNs, transformers, feedforward nets).
- Advises expanding discussion of limitations, especially RNN black-box nature and lack of population-level correlation modeling.
- Key questions: How do simpler deep models compare? Can the GLM-RNN capture inter-neuron correlations? Can the framework generalize to other modalities or brain regions?
- Additional feedback: Paper is well-written; recommend discussing broader implications and releasing code/pretrained models to boost impact.
