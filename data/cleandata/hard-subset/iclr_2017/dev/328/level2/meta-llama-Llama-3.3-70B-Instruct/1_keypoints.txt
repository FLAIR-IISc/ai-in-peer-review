- Proposes a multitask RNN framework to model sensory neurons, applied to primate parasol ganglion cells responding to natural images.  
- Claims the RNN outperforms traditional GLMs in predicting spiking responses.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing strong motivation and scientific rigor.  
- Methodology clearly explained: multitask setup shares features across neurons.  
- Experiments show consistent RNN improvements and more stable predictions under the multitask regime.  
- Analysis highlights ability to capture nonlinear spatial and temporal interactions and to inform simpler model development.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations, notably the need to better understand recurrent dynamics.  
- Suggestion: add more interpretability details and visualization of learned RNN features.  
- Suggestion: include comparisons to other state-of-the-art models (e.g., CNNs, LSTMs).  
- Query: plans and anticipated challenges for extending the approach to higher sensory areas.  
- Query: potential uses and benefits of the model for retinal prosthetics research.  
- Query: proposed future work to dissect RNN dynamics and how that could boost model performance.
