- Proposes a multitask recurrent neural network (RNN) framework to model sensory neurons’ computations.  
- Claims improved accuracy and flexibility over traditional generalized linear models (GLMs) for predicting primate retinal ganglion cell spikes.  
- Introduces a GLM–RNN hybrid model to reveal which aspects of retinal processing are better captured by RNNs.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reasons for acceptance: addresses an important neuroscience problem and is well-motivated with rigorous empirical support.  
- Empirical findings: multitask RNNs outperform GLMs and single-task RNNs in predictive performance and are more robust with limited training data.  
- Presentation: paper is clear and well-structured in motivation, approach, and results.  
- Requested improvements: provide more detailed analyses, visualizations, and interpretations of learned filters and dynamics.  
- Open questions: how learned features/dynamics relate to known retinal ganglion cell properties.  
- Open questions: whether the framework generalizes to other sensory neurons or brain areas.  
- Open questions: implications of results for retinal prosthetics and neural interfaces.
