- Title should include the word "activity" to be formally correct.  
- Paper proposes a recurrent network to predict retinal ganglion cell spike trains, compared to a generalized linear model (GLM).  
- Stated paradigm: good spike prediction lets you inspect the model to learn neural processing.  
- Reviewer finds results plausible but not very enlightening about retinal computation.  
- Figure 2 shows the RNN predicts spikes somewhat better than the GLM, but differences are modest and some real-data variability remains.  
- The reviewer questions what biological insights were gained from the improved predictive performance.  
- Not surprised RNN outperforms GLM given retina’s layered recurrent structure.  
- LSTMs and more complex recurrent models reportedly did not improve performance, but comparisons are limited by data requirements.  
- Expectation that deeper/more detailed models could improve predictions with more data.  
- Concern that the network shares identical parameters across neurons; models with neuron-specific parameters might outperform given sufficient training data.
