- Trains a multilayer RNN to reproduce retinal ganglion cell responses to sequences of natural images.  
- RNN outperforms conventional LN/GLM models, especially in capturing transient responses.  
- Important advance for modeling nonlinear response properties of visual neurons where standard receptive-field models fail.  
- Lacks analysis to interpret the RNN’s computations — pruning/simplification to identify critical components is needed.  
- Terminology confusion: “movie” appears to mean sequences of unrelated images shown for 1 s; “frame rate” of 1/8.33 ms likely refers to monitor refresh and needs clarification.  
- Predicts stronger deviations from LN models with true dynamic natural movies; testing on real movies could be more informative.  
- Overall a promising, important first step that the field should know about.
