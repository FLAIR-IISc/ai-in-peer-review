- Novel application: use of RNNs to model spiking responses of primate parasol ganglion cells to natural images.  
- Performance: RNNs outperform traditional GLMs in predictive accuracy, even with limited training data.  
- Multitask framework: sharing parameters across neurons improves predictive stability and accuracy.  
- Hybrid model: GLM-RNN provides insights into temporal and spatial nonlinearities captured by RNNs.  
- Biological insight: study does not yet produce new biological findings but is a significant methodological advance.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Experimental rigor: well-designed experiments with appropriate baselines (GLMs, LNs) and metrics (fraction of explainable variance).  
- Future impact: approach enables future work on other neural systems, shorter timescales, and LFPs.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, reproducible, and acknowledges limitations.  
- Clarification needed: explicitly explain the role of the 0.833 ms bins and use “epoch” consistently.  
- Figure suggestion: plot Figure 4 x-axis on a log scale to better show training-data vs. performance.  
- Interpretability: recommend deeper exploration of how learned features map to known biological mechanisms.  
- Limitations discussion: more explicitly frame modest performance gains and lack of immediate biological insights as future work.  
- Questions for authors: clarify 0.833 ms bins’ impact on temporal resolution; robustness of the multitask framework to neuron count; feasibility and challenges of applying the framework to other neurons/brain regions.
