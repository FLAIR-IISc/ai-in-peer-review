- Proposal and result: RNN framework predicts retinal ganglion cell spike trains from natural images, outperforming GLMs and capturing spatial and temporal nonlinearities.  
- Model design: Multitask RNN with shared parameters across neurons enables accurate prediction even with limited training data.  
- Decision: Paper rejected because it provides limited insight into neural processing despite improved predictive performance.  
- Concern about complexity: LSTM variants do not improve performance, questioning the need for increased model complexity.  
- Concern about realism: Sharing parameters across all neurons may be unrealistic; a model with free parameters could perform better with more data.  
- Requested improvements: Authors should analyze and report the specific nonlinearities and neural-processing insights learned by the RNN.  
- Suggested extensions: Explore more complex models and more data, and compare against models with free parameters.  
- Questions for authors: (1) What specific neural-processing insights does the RNN provide? (2) How will you address the shared-parameter limitation? (3) What are applications for retinal prosthetics and extensions to higher sensory areas?
