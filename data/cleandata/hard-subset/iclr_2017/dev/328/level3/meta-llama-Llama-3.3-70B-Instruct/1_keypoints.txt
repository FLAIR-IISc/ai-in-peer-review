- Demonstrates nonlinear RNNs can model neural response properties and transient responses, outperforming GLMs for primate retinal ganglion cell spikes to natural images.
- Accepted because the approach is well-motivated and addresses shortcomings of standard receptive-field models for natural movies.
- Acceptance also based on rigorous empirical results showing RNN superiority over GLMs.
- Methodology is clearly explained, using a multitask framework with shared parameters across neurons.
- RNNs capture ~80% of the explainable variance across experiments and cell types.
- Multitask training leads to better and more stable predictions.
- Suggestion to prune/simplify trained RNNs to identify critical components and reduce black-box concerns.
- Requested clarification on the definition of "movie" (sequence of unrelated images vs. dynamic natural scenes).
- Question 1: Provide more details on the characteristics of the natural images used and their relation to RNN performance.
- Question 2: How will this approach be extended to higher sensory areas and what challenges are expected?
- Question 3: What computational features important for simpler models are revealed by the trained networks?
- Overall assessment: significant advance with potential to enable richer models of complex nonlinear spiking computations in other brain areas.
