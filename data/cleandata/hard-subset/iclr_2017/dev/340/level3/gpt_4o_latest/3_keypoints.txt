- Proposal: Introduces Domain Transfer Network (DTN), an unsupervised domain transfer method using a fixed perceptual function f and a compound loss (multiclass GAN + f-preserving + regularization).  
- Results: Shows visually appealing transfers (SVHN→MNIST, photo→emoji) and achieves state-of-the-art unsupervised domain adaptation on SVHN→MNIST.  
- Decision: Accept — judged interesting and well-executed with strong empirical results despite some limits in novelty and generalization.  
- Strength: Using a fixed perceptual function f to enforce cross-domain semantic consistency is practical and effective.  
- Strength: Qualitative results are strong, capturing domain-specific characteristics while preserving identity.  
- Strength: Quantitative performance on SVHN→MNIST surpasses prior methods, demonstrating DTN’s potential for domain adaptation.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written with clear methodology and experiments.  
- Weakness: Main novelty centers on the f-constancy term, limiting conceptual contribution.  
- Weakness: Reliance on a fixed f trained on the source may hurt performance when source and target domains are highly dissimilar.  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is narrow (primarily SVHN→MNIST), raising questions about generality.  
- Weakness: Visual quality assessment may be misleading because original model outputs are relegated to the appendix.  
- Suggestion: Move original model outputs from the appendix into the main paper for transparent visual evaluation.  
- Suggestion: Broaden experiments to more domain pairs to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Suggestion: Discuss limitations of the f-constancy constraint, especially when f performs poorly on the target domain.  
- Question: How sensitive are results to the choice of perceptual function f and its training data/task?  
- Question: Have adaptive techniques for improving f on the target domain been considered, and how would they integrate into DTN?  
- Question: Can the method be extended to more complex or multimodal transfers (e.g., text→image, audio→video)?
