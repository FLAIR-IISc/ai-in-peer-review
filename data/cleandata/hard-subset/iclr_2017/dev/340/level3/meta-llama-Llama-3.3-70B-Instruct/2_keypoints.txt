- Proposes Domain Transfer Network (DTN) that maps source to target while preserving a representation function f (f-constancy).
- Provides extensive experimental results on two visual domain adaptation tasks demonstrating effectiveness.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty and potential impact.
- Paper tackles a well-motivated problem and offers a clear, well-structured solution.
- Strength: clear explanation and detailed description of DTN and its components.
- Strength: convincing experimental presentation with thorough comparisons to baselines and related work.
- Concern: forcing G = g ∘ f may lose information and raises recoverability issues.
- Concern: f is learned for a specific task (e.g., identity), so DTN may need retraining for other tasks (e.g., expression recognition).
- Suggested improvement: analyze why the baseline performs poorly.
- Suggested improvement: compare DTN to style-transfer approaches for tasks like generating emojis from photos.
- Questions for authors: how does the choice of f affect DTN performance?
- Questions for authors: can DTN handle other modalities (text, audio) and differing source/target distributions/statistics?
- Overall: well-written work with potential for significant impact pending additional clarifications and comparisons.
