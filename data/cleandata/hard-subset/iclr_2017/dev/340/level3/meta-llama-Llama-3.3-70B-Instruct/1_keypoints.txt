- Proposes Domain Transfer Network (DTN): unsupervised image transformation mapping samples from a source domain to a target domain without aligned training pairs.
- DTN uses a compound loss composed of a multiclass GAN loss, an f-preserving term, and a regularizer that encourages the generator to map target-domain samples to themselves.
- Generator is decomposed into an encoder and decoder to identify a common feature space and generate target-domain samples.
- Method is GAN-based.
- Experiments demonstrate effectiveness on digits and face images with visually impressive results.
- Quantitative evaluations and careful ablation studies support the method’s effectiveness.
- Decision: Accept — due to tackling a novel/important problem and a well-motivated method with supported results.
- Suggestion: explore DTN on other domains (e.g., text ↔ image) and additional applications.
- Suggestion: analyze preservation of facial attributes when mapping to the target domain.
- Question: How does the choice of the function f affect DTN performance?
- Question: Can DTN be used for other tasks like image-to-image translation or image generation?
- Question: How does DTN handle cases where source and target domains have different distributions or styles?
