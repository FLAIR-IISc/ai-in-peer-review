- Proposes an unsupervised Domain Transfer Network (DTN) that transfers samples between related domains using a fixed perceptual function f.  
- DTN optimizes a compound loss combining a multiclass GAN loss, an f-preserving term, and a regularizing component.  
- Produces visually appealing results on digits and face datasets and shows improved SVHN→MNIST domain adaptation performance.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Key reason 1: Insufficient domain adaptation experiments to establish DTN as a general alternative to existing methods.  
- Key reason 2: Limited novelty—main contribution is the f-constancy term, which may not generalize to more dissimilar domains.  
- Limitation: Reliance on a fixed f trained on the source domain may discard important target-domain information.  
- Limitation: Use of super-resolved outputs may create a misleading impression of visual quality; original outputs should be presented in the main paper.  
- Recommendation: Add more comprehensive domain adaptation experiments and address the method’s limitations.  
- Question: How will the approach be adapted for more dissimilar domain pairs?  
- Question: What is the rationale for the chosen perceptual function f and how does it affect results?  
- Question: How can the method be extended to other domains and tasks beyond visual domains?
