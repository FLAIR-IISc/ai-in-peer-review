This paper presents an unsupervised image transformation method that maps a sample from the source domain to the target domain. Its main contribution is that it does not require aligned training pairs from the two domains. The model is based on GANs. To make it work in an unsupervised setting, the paper divides the generation function into two modules: an encoder that identifies a common feature space between the two domains and a decoder that generates samples in the target domain. To avoid trivial solutions, the paper proposes two additional losses that penalize 1) the feature difference between the source sample and its transformed sample, and 2) the pixel difference between the target sample and its regenerated sample. The paper presents extensive experiments on transferring SVHN digit images to MNIST style and face images to emoji style.

+The proposed learning method enables unsupervised domain transfer, which can be impactful in a wide range of problem contexts.
+The paper presents careful ablation studies to analyze the effects of various components of the system, which is helpful in understanding this paper.
+The transferred images are visually impactful, and quantitative results also show that the image recognition is preserved to some extent in the domain.
-It would be more interesting to show the results in other domains such as text and images.
-In addition to face recognition, it is also very important to analyze how well facial features are preserved when mapping to the target domain.
