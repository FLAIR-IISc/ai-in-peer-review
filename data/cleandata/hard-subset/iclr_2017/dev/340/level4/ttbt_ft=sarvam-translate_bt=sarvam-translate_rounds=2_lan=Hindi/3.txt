Update: After reading the rebuttal comments and the revised paper, I am keeping the rating the same as before.

This paper proposes an unsupervised algorithm for transferring samples from one domain to another (related domain), under the constraint that some predefined f gives the same result for input and output.

Pros:
1. The paper presents an interesting idea of comparing samples from different domains using a specific conceptual function f.
2. The proposed method generates visually appealing results on several datasets.
3. The authors demonstrate how their approach can be used for domain optimization and achieve better results on the SVHN->MNIST task.
4. The paper is well-written and easy to read.

Cons:
1. The novelty of the method is relatively minor (I consider the term "f-stability" to be the main contribution).
2. It seems that the proposed approach will break down for more dissimilar domains. This method depends on a fixed f that is trained on the source domain. This f may potentially miss important information 1) to obtain better reconstruction in the target domain 2) more tightly related x and g(f(x)). I think the authors should consider training all modules of the model end-to-end or including the target samples in the training of f.
3. A single domain optimization experiment is certainly not enough to consider the proposed method as a universal alternative to existing DA approaches.

I would also like to point out that using a super-resolved output as opposed to the output of the actual model can create a false perception of the visual quality of the transferred samples. I would suggest moving the original output from the appendix to the main body.
