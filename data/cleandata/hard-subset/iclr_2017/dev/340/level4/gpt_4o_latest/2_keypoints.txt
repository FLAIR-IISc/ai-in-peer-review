- Reviewer updated score: authors addressed most concerns after additional experiments and explanations.  
- Paper studies learning G mapping source→target while preserving representation f (f-constancy); evaluated on two visual domain-adaptation tasks.  
- Manuscript is clear and presents extensive experimental results.  
- Primary novelty claimed is the f-constancy concept.  
- Concern: constraining G to g∘f is counter-intuitive because f may have discarded information needed for reconstruction.  
- Unclear whether source/target inputs can be reconstructed by G as implied by equations (5) and (6).  
- f is task-specific (trained for digits/identities), so the pipeline may need to be repeated for different target tasks, limiting generality.  
- Question: why does the baseline method in equations (1) and (2) perform so poorly?  
- Figure 5 comparison with style transfer is ambiguous; suggestion to apply style transfer to generate emojis from photos and replicate Table 4 experiments for a clearer comparison.
