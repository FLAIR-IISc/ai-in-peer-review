- Introduces an unsupervised cross-domain sample transfer method that enforces a fixed perceptual function f to be constant between input and transformed output.  
- Novel concept of comparing samples across domains using a fixed perceptual function f.  
- Produces visually compelling results across multiple datasets.  
- Demonstrates usefulness for domain adaptation with improved SVHN→MNIST performance.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Limited novelty: main contribution seems to be the f-constancy term.  
- Method may fail on more dissimilar domains because f is fixed and trained on the source, risking loss of information for target reconstructions and alignment; recommend end-to-end training or using target samples for f.  
- Only a single domain adaptation experiment, insufficient to claim generality over existing techniques.  
- Concern about presenting super-resolved outputs instead of original model outputs; recommend moving originals from appendix to main text for transparency.
