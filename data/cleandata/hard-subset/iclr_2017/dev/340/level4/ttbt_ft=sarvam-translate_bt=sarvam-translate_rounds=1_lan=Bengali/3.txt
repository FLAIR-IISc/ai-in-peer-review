Update: After reading the critical comments and the revised paper, I am keeping the rating the same as before.

This paper proposes an unsupervised algorithm designed to transfer samples from one domain to another (related) domain. In this case, some predefined function f provides the same results for input and output.

Pros:
1. The paper presents an interesting idea of comparing samples from different domains using a specific perception function f.
2. The proposed method produces visually appealing results on several datasets.
3. The authors show how their method can be used for domain adaptation and improved results can be obtained in the SVHN->MNIST task.
4. The paper is well-written and easy to read.

Cons:
1. The novelty of the method is relatively low (I consider the f-consistency term as a major contribution).
2. It seems the proposed method will break down for more different domains. The method depends on a specific f trained in the source domain. This f could potentially miss important information that would help in 1) better reconstruction in the target domain and 2) obtaining more closely related x and g(f(x)). I think the authors should consider end-to-end training of all modules in the model or including the target sample in the training of f.
3. A single domain adaptation test is not enough to consider the proposed method as a universal alternative to existing DA methods.

I would also like to mention that using super-resolved output against the original model's output can create a false impression about the visual quality of the transferred sample. I would advise removing the original outputs from the appendix to the main body.
