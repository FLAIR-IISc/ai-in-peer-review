This paper presents an unsupervised image transformation method that maps a sample from the source domain to the target domain. Its main contribution is that it does not require aligned training pairs from the two domains. The model is based on GANs. To make it effective in an unsupervised setting, this paper divides the generation function into two modules: an encoder that identifies a common feature space between the two domains and a decoder that generates the sample in the target domain. To avoid trivial solutions, this paper proposes two additional losses that penalize 1) the difference in features between a source sample and its transformed sample, and 2) the difference in pixels between a target sample and its recreated sample. This paper presents extensive tests on transferring SVHN digit images in the MNIST style and facial images in the emoji style.

+ The proposed learning method enables unsupervised domain transfer, which can be impactful in a wide range of problem contexts.
+ This paper carefully presents ablation studies to analyze the impact of various components of the system, which is helpful in understanding the paper.
+ The transformed images are visually appealing, and the quantitative results also show that image detections are preserved across domains to some extent.
- It would be more interesting to show the results on other domains such as text and images.
- Along with facial recognition, it is also of great interest to analyze how well facial features are preserved when mapping to the target domain.
