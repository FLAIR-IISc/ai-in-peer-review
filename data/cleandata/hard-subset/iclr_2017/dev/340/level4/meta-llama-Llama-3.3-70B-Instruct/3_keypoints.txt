- Reviewer’s evaluation remains unchanged after rebuttal.
- Paper proposes unsupervised domain transfer enforcing that a fixed perceptual function f gives the same output for input and translated sample.
- Novel idea of comparing cross-domain samples via a fixed perceptual function f.
- Method produces visually appealing results across multiple datasets.
- Demonstrated applicability to domain adaptation with improved SVHN→MNIST performance.
- Paper is clear and readable.
- Limited novelty: the f-constancy term is the primary new contribution.
- Robustness concerns: relying on a fixed f trained on the source may lose information for dissimilar domains; end-to-end training or including target samples in f’s training is suggested.
- Insufficient domain adaptation experiments to claim broad applicability.
- Presentation concern: showing super-resolved outputs instead of raw model outputs may overstate visual quality; move original outputs from appendix to main text.
