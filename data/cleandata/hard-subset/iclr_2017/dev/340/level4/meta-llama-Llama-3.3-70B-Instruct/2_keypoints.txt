1. Reviewer revised evaluation after authors added experiments and explanations.  
2. Paper studies learning a generator G that maps source to target while preserving representation f (f-constancy), tested on two visual domain-adaptation tasks.  
3. Manuscript is well-structured with extensive experiments; f-constancy is a notable novelty.  
4. Concern that constraining G = g∘f is counterintuitive because f may have lost crucial information, casting doubt on input recoverability (eqs. 5–6).  
5. Concern that f is task-specific (e.g., identity/digit recognition), so changing the task would require redoing the whole procedure.  
6. Request for explanation of why the baseline in equations (1) and (2) performs poorly.  
7. Figure 5’s comparison between style transfer and the proposed method is inconclusive; reviewer suggests applying style transfer to generate emojis and repeating Table 4 experiments for a clearer comparison.
