- Reviewer updated score after authors ran more experiments and added explanations; most concerns addressed.  
- Paper proposes learning a generator G mapping source to target such that a fixed representation f is unchanged (f-constancy).  
- Method evaluated on two visual domain-adaptation tasks with extensive experimental results.  
- Paper is easy to follow; f-constancy is the main novelty.  
- Concern: forcing G = g ∘ f is counter-intuitive because f may have already lost information needed for reconstruction.  
- Concern: f is learned for a specific task (e.g., digit ID or face identity), so the method may not generalize to other tasks and would need re-training.  
- Unclear whether inputs from source/target can be recovered using G as defined in equations (5) and (6).  
- Question why the baseline methods in equations (1) and (2) perform so poorly.  
- Figure 5 comparison to style transfer is inconclusive; asks whether style transfer could generate emojis from photos and be used to repeat the Table 4 experiments.
