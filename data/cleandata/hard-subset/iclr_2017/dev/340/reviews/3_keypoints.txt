Update: reviewer keeps the original rating after reading rebuttal and revised paper.

Pros:
- Novel idea of comparing cross-domain samples using a fixed perceptual function f.
- Produces visually appealing results on several datasets.
- Shows improved domain adaptation performance on SVHN->MNIST.
- Paper is well-written and easy to read.

Cons / concerns:
- Novelty is limited; main contribution is the f-constancy term.
- Approach may break for more dissimilar domains because f (trained on source) can drop target-relevant information.
- Recommend training modules end-to-end or incorporating target samples into f's training.
- Single domain-adaptation experiment is insufficient to claim generality over existing DA methods.
- Using super-resolved outputs may misrepresent visual quality; original outputs should be moved from appendix to main text.
