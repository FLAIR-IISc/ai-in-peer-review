- Proposes Domain Transfer Network (DTN) to transfer samples between domains while preserving a given function’s representation (identity).
- Introduces a compound loss combining a multiclass GAN loss, an f-preserving term, and a regularizing self-mapping term for target-domain samples.
- Demonstrates DTN on visual domains (digits and faces), showing convincing novel images that preserve identity.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on strong motivation and proper placement in the literature.
- Claims supported by scientifically rigorous experiments and clear explanations of method and results.
- Authors provide a thorough related-work review and clearly differentiate their approach from existing methods.
- Paper includes detailed analysis of each loss component’s contribution to overall performance.
- Suggested improvements: add implementation details (generator/discriminator architectures, hyperparameters) and more visual examples for faces.
- Requests clarification on hyperparameter selection for loss-term trade-offs and the specific impact of the f-preserving component.
