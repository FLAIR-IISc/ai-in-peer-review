- Presents a novel Domain Transfer Network that transfers samples between domains while preserving a given function's representation.  
- Proposes a compound loss combining a multiclass GAN loss, an f-preserving term, and a regularizer encouraging identity mapping on the target domain.  
- Applied to visual domains (digits and faces) and shown to generate convincing novel images that preserve identity.  
- Supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Experiments include SVHN→MNIST and face→emoji transfers, demonstrating high-quality results.  
- Provides ablation/analysis of each loss component and shows their importance for performance.  
- Limitation: insufficient comprehensive comparison to other state-of-the-art domain/style transfer methods.  
- Suggestion: add implementation details (generator/discriminator architectures, hyperparameters).  
- Suggestion: analyze robustness to noise and perturbations and explore other domains/tasks.  
- Question: how does the method handle cases where the representation function f is inaccurate on the target domain?  
- Question: can the method be extended to multiple source/target domains and how does it compare in computational efficiency and scalability?
