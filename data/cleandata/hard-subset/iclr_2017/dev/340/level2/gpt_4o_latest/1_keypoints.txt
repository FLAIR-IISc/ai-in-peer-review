- Proposes Domain Transfer Network (DTN) for unsupervised mapping S -> T while preserving a perceptual function f.
- Introduces a compound loss combining GAN adversarial loss, f-preservation loss, and identity regularization for T samples.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on visual tasks (SVHN->MNIST, photo-to-emoji) with convincing, identity-preserving outputs.
- Claims applicability to unsupervised domain adaptation and style transfer.
- Decision: Accept due to novelty and distinct problem formulation from prior style transfer/domain adaptation work.
- Main novelty: meaningful combination of f-preservation with GAN-based losses within the DTN framework.
- Experimental validation: quantitative and qualitative results with ablation studies showing contribution of loss components.
- Strong theoretical foundation: builds on GANs and f-preserving mappings with a well-motivated compound loss.
- Empirical rigor: thorough experiments and reported state-of-the-art performance in unsupervised domain adaptation.
- Practical usefulness: enables personalized emoji generation and label-free domain adaptation; shows versatility.
- Suggestion: improve clarity, especially in mathematical formulations for broader accessibility.
- Suggestion: include comparisons with additional state-of-the-art baselines in domain/style transfer.
- Suggestion: provide a more detailed discussion of limitations and failure cases (e.g., highly dissimilar domains, limited T data).
- Question: how sensitive is performance to the choice of perceptual function f and to using different f’s?
- Question: can DTN generalize to non-visual domains (text/audio), and what modifications would be needed?
- Question: how does DTN handle high intra-class variability in the target domain (e.g., diverse artistic styles)?
