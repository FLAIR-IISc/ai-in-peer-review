- Proposes learning chess-like game concepts under weak supervision with CNNs, using tic-tac-toe as a case study.  
- Demonstrates CNNs can discover game concepts: board grid, winning rule, and two sides.  
- Shows cross-modal supervision works for higher-level semantics and that CAM can activate non-salient regions.  
- Decision: accept with reservations due to clarity problems and insufficiently rigorous support for some conclusions.  
- Main contribution: novel, well-motivated demonstration that CNNs can learn game concepts under weak supervision.  
- Experiments are generally well-designed and CAM-based visualizations are a useful addition.  
- Major weakness: paper clarity is poor and some results/conclusions need stronger, more rigorous evidence.  
- Recommendation: provide deeper analysis of learned representations and their relation to game concepts.  
- Recommendation: adopt more advanced evaluation protocols (from NLP/CV) for representation assessment.  
- Recommendation: expand discussion on implications and applicability to more complex games or real-world settings.  
- Question for authors: how do learned representations change when game rules or supervision signals are modified?  
- Question for authors: how does CAM activate non-salient regions and what does that imply about CNN behavior?  
- Question for authors: how do these results relate to prior work on learning game concepts/representations and future research directions?
