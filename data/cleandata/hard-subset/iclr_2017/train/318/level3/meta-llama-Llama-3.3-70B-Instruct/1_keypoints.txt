- Paper studies learning game concepts under weak supervision using CNNs on rendered tic-tac-toe boards and CAM.
- Proposes a protocol to train a classification network and analyze learned representations with class activation mapping.
- Empirical results show the network discovers complex concepts: board grid, winning rule, and the notion of two sides.
- Decision: accept — method is novel, well-motivated, and the paper is well-organized.
- Experiments are carefully designed and thoroughly analyzed, providing strong evidence for the claims.
- Use of CAM effectively demonstrates the network’s ability to gather contextual information.
- Builds on prior work in cross-modal supervision and CAM.
- Suggestion: add discussion on implications and applicability to more complex games or real-world scenarios.
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis of learned representations (e.g., visualize feature maps, analyze weights).
- Question: How will the approach be extended to more complex games like chess or Go?
- Question: Can the authors explain how the network leverages context and which factors enable this ability?
- Question: How can this method be applied to real-world scenarios such as learning concepts from images or videos?
