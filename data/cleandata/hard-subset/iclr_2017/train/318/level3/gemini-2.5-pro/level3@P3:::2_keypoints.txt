- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN): a differentiable, dynamically constructed graph memory for representing and reasoning over text.  
- Core contribution: a set of differentiable graph transformations that build and modify an explicit entity–relation graph on-the-fly.  
- Learning setup: transformation parameters and sentence encoder are learned long-term; graph structure is inferred per example at runtime.  
- Empirical results: solves 19/20 bAbI tasks and succeeds on rule-discovery tasks (cellular automata, Turing machines).  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novelty: Significant conceptual advance—explicit, differentiable graph memory versus less-structured memory architectures.  
- Demonstrated potential: Competitive with state-of-the-art on bAbI and especially strong on inherently graph-structured tasks (e.g., pathfinding, induction).  
- Main strength: Extends gated graph sequence networks to dynamically construct graphs from unstructured text with modular transformations.  
- Evaluation quality: Thorough bAbI evaluation showing near-perfect accuracy on most tasks, supporting the architecture’s viability.  
- Limitations: Substantial model complexity and reliance on strong, step-by-step graph supervision limit immediate real-world applicability.  
- Technical soundness and clarity: Method appears sound and the paper (with appendix) explains the complex model well, likely to inspire follow-ups.  
- Improvement suggestion 1: Provide concrete complexity/scalability analysis (typical graph sizes, training times, comparison to MemN2N).  
- Improvement suggestion 2: Emphasize and expand discussion on reliance on strong supervision and propose concrete mitigation strategies (e.g., semi-supervised or curriculum learning).  
- Improvement suggestion 3: Include ablation/justification for the chosen five graph transformations and consider alternatives (e.g., node deletion).  
- Question 1 for authors: Any preliminary experiments with weak supervision (partial graph supervision across data/timesteps)?  
- Question 2 for authors: Clarify the failure on Task 17 (Positional Reasoning)—fundamental representation limit or optimization/training issue?  
- Question 3 for authors: Deeper intuition on which structural task properties favor GGT-NN versus memory-slot-based models?
