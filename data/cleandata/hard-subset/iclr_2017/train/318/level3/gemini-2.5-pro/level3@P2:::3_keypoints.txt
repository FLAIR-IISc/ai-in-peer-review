- Proposes GGT-NN, a fully differentiable model that dynamically builds and modifies an internal graph from sequential input using five graph transformations.  
- Demonstrates strong results on bAbI (solves 19/20 tasks) and on learning dynamics for 1D cellular automata and Turing machines.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Primary weakness: requires ground-truth graph states at every timestep, limiting real-world applicability and making comparisons to end-to-end models less meaningful.  
- Presentation is dense and complex, making it hard to assess individual components and reproduce the work.  
- The supervision requirement frames the model as mimicking a known symbolic process rather than a general QA system.  
- No clear path shown to reduce supervision, leaving the approach niche rather than widely applicable.  
- Missing ablation studies to justify the necessity of each of the five transformations.  
- Quadratic complexity noted but not sufficiently analyzed; need concrete graph sizes, parameter counts, and training times.  
- Recommend exploring weaker supervision strategies (final-graph supervision, semi-supervised, or reinforcement learning).  
- Suggest specific ablations (e.g., remove Edge Update, test pre-allocated graphs vs Node Addition) to clarify component contributions.  
- Presentation improvement: include a running example (e.g., “Mary went to the kitchen”) showing transformations step-by-step.  
- Question on fairness of Table 2 comparisons given differing supervision levels across models.  
- Request for practical computational details: maximum nodes and per-epoch training time compared to baselines like MemN2N.  
- Ask whether learned graphical representations for Turing machines correspond to actual rules and whether edge matrices C were visualized to confirm state transitions.
