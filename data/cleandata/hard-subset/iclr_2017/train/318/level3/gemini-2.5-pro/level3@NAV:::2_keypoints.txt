- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN): a novel architecture that builds an explicit, differentiable graph-structured memory from textual input.  
- Graph memory is created on-the-fly via learned differentiable transformations and used to represent/manipulate world state.  
- Empirical results show success on most bAbI QA tasks and learning rules of cellular automata and Turing machines, demonstrating versatility.  
- Claimed novelty: first implementation of a fully differentiable memory explicitly represented as a graph, enabling structured, interpretable internal states.  
- Recommendation: Accept, based on conceptual novelty and competitive empirical proof-of-concept results.  
- Strength: explicit graph structure is a more natural fit for relational problems and yields strong performance on pathfinding (bAbI Task 19).  
- Caveat: does not consistently outperform simpler methods and is considerably more complex, though matching established baselines is a valuable result.  
- Paper is technically sound and the current draft is clear, making the complex approach understandable.  
- Main limitations: quadratic complexity in node count and heavy reliance on strong graph-level supervision.  
- Suggestion: emphasize limitations and speculate on mitigation strategies (e.g., sparse graph updates, semi-supervised learning).  
- Suggestion: include analysis of failure cases to show where the model struggles or which graph structures are problematic.  
- Questions for authors: can reliance on ground-truth graph supervision be reduced, and were weaker supervision/regularization strategies tried?  
- Questions for authors: how sensitive is performance to the initial graph representation and to manual choices in converting bAbI knowledge objects to graphs?
