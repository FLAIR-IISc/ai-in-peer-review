- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that learns dynamic, graph-structured internal state via differentiable graph transformations.  
- Extends GGS-NNs by allowing the graph to serve as a mutable intermediate representation constructed from sequential input.  
- Core technical contribution: differentiable graph-to-graph transformations enabling end-to-end training of models that build and modify knowledge graphs.  
- Empirical result: achieves state-of-the-art on 19 of 20 bAbI tasks, with especially strong performance on pathfinding (Task 19) and induction (Task 16).  
- Demonstrates generality by learning the dynamics of cellular automata and a family of Turing machines; rule-discovery experiments are promising but not perfect.  
- Offers a more interpretable alternative to implicit-memory models (e.g., DNCs, Memory Networks).  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Major limitation: relies on strong, step-by-step graph supervision; this caveat should be emphasized when comparing to weakly-supervised baselines.  
- Efficiency concern: quadratic complexity noted; average graph sizes and runtimes for bAbI should be reported to assess scalability.  
- Suggested improvement: analyze failure cases on Rule 30 to explain why performance lags compared to near-perfect Turing machine results.  
- Clarification requested: have authors tried weaker supervision (e.g., only final graph) to reduce dependence on ground-truth trajectories?  
- Clarification requested: how sensitive is performance to the hand-crafted schema used to represent bAbI tasks as graphs?
