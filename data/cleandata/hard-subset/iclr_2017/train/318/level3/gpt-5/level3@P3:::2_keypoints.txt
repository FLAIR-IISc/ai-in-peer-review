- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Networks (GGT-NNs) that build an explicit, differentiable graph memory on-the-fly from text for reasoning and QA.
- Jointly learns long-term parameters for sentence encoding and graph transformations, plus per-example inference-time graph states.
- Claims first concrete instantiation of explicit differentiable graph memory, generalizing simpler memories (stacks/lists).
- Evaluated on bAbI (QA and sequence), 1D cellular automata, and 4-state Turing machines, showing learned graph machinery can model nontrivial state transitions and sometimes generalize to longer horizons.
- Final version improves clarity, is technically accurate, and raises interesting ideas about structured, interpretable memory.
- Decision: Accept (borderline) for novelty and potential impact.
- Novelty: on-the-fly learned graph memory expands design space beyond Memory Networks/DNCs and offers interpretable internal states.
- Empirics caveat: bAbI performance is comparable to strong memory networks but not decisively better; some tasks still outperformed by rule induction.
- Strength: conceptual novelty and modular library of differentiable graph operations.
- Strength: interpretability — internal graph state can be inspected and reused by downstream systems.
- Strength: breadth of demonstrations across QA and rule-discovery tasks, not tied to one modality.
- Strength: technical care in decomposition of transformations, training for unordered nodes, and complexity discussion.
- Weakness: added model complexity with many components and quadratic scaling in nodes/edges.
- Weakness: reliance on strong supervision for bAbI (full graphs and direct-reference signals); end-to-end QA without graph labels not convincingly demonstrated.
- Weakness: empirical results feel preliminary with limited gains and some worse-than-baseline outcomes.
- Weakness: limited QA generalization to longer contexts/more entities (e.g., task 17).
- Weakness: fairness of comparisons is unclear and runtime/memory costs are not reported.
- Suggested improvement: include end-to-end QA results without graph supervision and ablate the direct-reference contribution.
- Suggested improvement: report wall-clock time, memory footprint, and scaling vs. number of nodes/edges; compare to MemN2N/DNC at matched accuracy.
- Suggested improvement: ablate contributions of each transformation (Tadd, Th, TC, Tprop, Trepr), propagation depth, and the permutation-invariant node-matching loss.
- Suggested improvement: test robustness/generalization on longer stories, more entities, OOV names, and noisy lexical matches.
- Suggested improvement: add broader baselines (GCN with gold graphs, neural stack/queue memories, modern relational memory modules).
- Suggested improvement: tighten notation, add an earlier concrete worked example of graph construction, and summarize Algorithm 1 with a running example.
- Key questions: performance on bAbI without graph supervision/direct reference and learning curves/final accuracies?
- Key questions: exact training/inference-time complexity (time and space) as function of nodes and edge types and any sparsity tricks used?
- Key questions: sensitivity to propagation steps and fset/freset parameterization; stability of max-over-permutations node loss and alternatives like Sinkhorn?
- Key questions: handling of OOV entities and multi-word references beyond string matches; any curriculum or semi-supervised setups tested?
- Key questions: on task 17, is the bottleneck representational or data efficiency, and can authors provide qualitative examples of learned graphs and failure cases?
