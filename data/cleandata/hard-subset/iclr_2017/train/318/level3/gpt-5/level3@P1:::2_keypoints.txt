- Proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Networks (GGT-NNs): an explicit, differentiable graph as persistent working memory with composable graph transformation primitives.  
- Architecture supports node addition, node-state update (optional direct-reference), edge update, message propagation, and aggregation under a controller to build/modify graphs from text.  
- Extends prior GNN/GGS-NN work from fixed-input-graph→output to text→learned-intermediate-graph→output, enabling on-the-fly graph construction at inference.  
- Empirical result on bAbI: with strong intermediate-graph supervision and direct reference, achieves ≥95% on 19/20 tasks and excels on graph-suited tasks (e.g., pathfinding).  
- Empirical result on algorithmic tasks: learns transition rules for Rule 30 cellular automaton and 2-symbol 4-state Turing machines and generalizes to longer rollouts.  
- Claims novelty as the first end-to-end differentiable memory that is explicitly and persistently a graph; provides modular, reusable graph transformation library.  
- Revised draft is clearer, technically sound, and thought-provoking.  
- Decision: Weak accept based primarily on conceptual novelty and modularity of explicit graph memory.  
- Secondary acceptance reason: diverse, solid demonstrations showing both graph construction from text and learned state transitions.  
- Problem addressed: unifies structured prediction and algorithmic manipulation by learning to build/transform graph-structured state from unstructured input for reasoning.  
- Positioned well relative to GNNs/graph convnets and implicit-memory architectures (MemNN, DNC); related work coverage is broad and fair.  
- Results largely support claims but with caveats: bAbI gains not uniformly superior to best end-to-end models and often rely on strong supervision and direct reference.  
- Acknowledged underperformance on bAbI task 17 with plausible explanations; comparative baselines with matched supervision are limited.  
- Rigour: training/losses (including set-matching loss) are clearly described; ablations demonstrate role of direct reference and propagation; code available.  
- Key limitations: quadratic time/space scaling with nodes, reliance on strong supervision for best bAbI performance, and only preliminary generalization evidence beyond presented tasks.  
- Feedback — supervision: separate and clearly report results for strong-graph-supervision/direct-reference vs end-to-end; include matched-supervision baselines and semi-supervised experiments.  
- Feedback — scalability: provide runtime/memory scaling curves, discuss sparse adjacency/neighborhood sampling, and test on larger graphs or more entities.  
- Feedback — ablations/analysis: quantify each transformation’s contribution, propagation steps, direct-reference effect, sensitivity to vocab sizes and entity-linking errors.  
- Feedback — generalization: show length-generalization curves and failure modes for CA/TM; test bAbI robustness to paraphrases, OOVs, and multi-word entities.  
- Feedback — interpretation: visualize learned graphs and edge evolution during training/test, especially for pathfinding/induction tasks.  
- Feedback — clarity/claims: calibrate “first” claim and cite related efforts if any; tighten notation and fully specify hyperparameters/datasets.  
- Questions — supervision: how does performance vary with fraction of supervised steps (0/10/50%) and can semi-supervised training work?  
- Questions — inference discretization: how are continuous node/edge strengths turned into discrete structures at test time and how sensitive are results?  
- Questions — node growth/halting: how many nodes are added per step, is there a learned halting criterion or fixed max, and what if entities exceed the cap?  
- Questions — direct reference robustness: how does model handle noisy/ambiguous mentions, coreference, and multi-word entities without pre-normalization?  
- Questions — scalability experiments: any experiments with sparse message passing (k-NN, attention-based pruning) or batching tricks to reduce O(|V|^2)?  
- Questions — generalization for TMs: how well does the model extrapolate to longer runs/larger tapes and what are main failure modes?  
- Questions — comparisons: include additional structured-reasoning baselines (DNC/EntNet variants) and a GGS-NN baseline with precomputed graphs to isolate text-to-graph benefits.
