- Proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Networks (GGT-NNs): differentiable graph edit operations enabling end-to-end graph→graph learning.
- Extends GGS-NN to produce and manipulate an explicit, evolving graph state usable in text→graph→text pipelines.
- Testbed: learns rule systems as graph-form cellular automata and Turing machines; demonstrates capability but struggles with long-horizon Rule 30 dynamics.
- Achieves strong bAbI performance using graphs and direct reference, often outperforming baselines on pathfinding and induction under strong supervision.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: novel, well-executed framework bridging GNNs and memory architectures via differentiable graph transformations.
- Empirical support: explicit graph states improve text QA, especially for inherently graphical tasks.
- Problem addressed: learning to construct, transform, and reason over explicit graph states from unstructured inputs for downstream prediction.
- Positioning: clearly situated relative to GNN/GGS-NN, Memory Networks/DNC/HAM, and LRNNs, with fair discussion of benefits and quadratic scaling trade-offs.
- Results detail: 19/20 bAbI tasks high accuracy with direct reference; Turing machine generalization within evaluated horizons; Rule 30 highlights remaining limitations.
- Methodology: appropriate set-prediction and edge losses, useful ablations, and released code support reproducibility.
- Strengths: modular reusable differentiable graph operations, interpretability of explicit graph state, and demonstration on discrete algorithmic updates.
- Weaknesses: heavy reliance on full graph supervision, O(n^2) scaling, Rule 30 long-horizon/robustness issues, and incomplete reporting of parameter counts/training stability.
- Requested ablations: vary supervision strength (partial/noisy labels), report compute/memory costs and scaling, and explore sparse/local propagation variants.
- Direct-reference analysis requested: whether content-based attention can replace naming and sensitivity to OOV or obfuscated entity names.
- Diagnostics requested for rule learning: error modes on Rule 30, divergence horizon, and whether learned local rules match ground truth.
- Fairness of comparisons: include MemNN/DNC variants with comparable supervision where possible.
- Questions for authors: performance with only final-answer supervision or partial graph labels; sensitivity to entity vocab/OOVs; parameter counts and compute costs; adaptive node-addition/halting; generalization to larger/denser graphs; specific failure modes on Rule 30; comparisons to contemporary graph attention/message-passing networks.
