- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) and differential graph transformations enabling gradient-based learning for graph-to-graph tasks.  
- Frames the problem as learning a cellular automaton with sequences of growing graphs, neighbor pointers, and 0/1 value nodes.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance: 95%+ accuracy on 19/20 bAbI tasks and improvements over state-of-the-art on several benchmarks.  
- Shows promising, though incomplete, results on Rule 30 cellular automaton and Turing machine tasks.  
- Recommends acceptance based on the paper’s novel capability to learn graph-to-graph transformations via gradient descent.  
- Argues the contribution fills a significant gap in graph neural network literature and is well-motivated from prior GGS-NN work.  
- Praises experimental rigor: appropriate baselines, thorough analysis, and focus on strengths of graphical representations (e.g., pathfinding).  
- Suggests further analysis of why Rule 30 remains unsolved and exploration of potential architectural limitations for that task.  
- Requests a detailed comparison of computational complexity to clarify trade-offs between expressive power and computational demands.  
- Asks for clarification on scalability: how to extend the model to handle much larger graphs (beyond ~20 nodes) and what architectural changes are needed.  
- Requests deeper insight into why the direct reference mechanism substantially improves performance beyond empirical effectiveness.
