- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Networks (GGT-NNs), extending GGS-NNs to learn differentiable graph transformations as internal representations.  
- Proposes five differentiable transformation types: node addition, node state update, edge update, propagation, and aggregation.  
- Enables iterative construction and modification of evolving graph states conditioned on textual input while remaining end-to-end differentiable.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results: >95% accuracy on 19/20 bAbI tasks and successful rule discovery for cellular automata and Turing machines.  
- Recommends rejection primarily because training requires ground-truth graph states at each timestep, limiting real-world applicability.  
- Authors acknowledge the supervision limitation but do not provide adequate strategies to overcome it.  
- Paper is dense and technically complex, better suited for a journal than a conference, which harms readability and accessibility.  
- Strong supervision requirement undermines the practical contribution despite compelling empirical results.  
- Comparison with memory networks and neural Turing machines is appropriate but lacks discussion of generalization to settings without explicit graph supervision.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss ways to reduce supervision requirements.  
- Suggested improvements: simplify presentation for broader accessibility.  
- Suggested improvements: include a more detailed analysis of computational complexity.  
- Reviewer requests clearer statement of the specific problem GGT-NN solves that existing approaches cannot.  
- Questions for authors: how to adapt the model to tasks without ground-truth graph states (e.g., real-world QA on unstructured text)?  
- Questions for authors: provide a concrete practical application where learned graph transformations are essential versus other approaches.
