- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that extends GGS-NNs to learn differentiable, evolving graph transformations.  
- Proposes five differentiable graph transformation types (node addition, node state update, edge update, propagation, aggregation) to build/modify graphs incrementally from text.  
- Demonstrates strong performance on bAbI tasks (95%+ on most) and can learn cellular automata and Turing machine rules.  
- Contribution: enables neural networks to operate on evolving graph structures rather than just fixed graphs.  
- Recommendation: reject.  
- Primary reason for rejection: model requires ground-truth graph states at every timestep for training, limiting real-world applicability.  
- Secondary reason for rejection: paper is overly dense and complex, reducing readability and suitability for a conference.  
- Key limitation: no compelling evidence the approach works without strong supervisory graph-state annotations.  
- bAbI results likely rely on strong supervision, undermining practical utility.  
- Paper presentation: too formal/long (feels like a journal paper), needs simplification for conference readers.  
- Suggested improvement: better justify why graph representations outperform alternatives beyond pathfinding.  
- Suggested improvement: propose concrete methods to reduce or remove need for ground-truth graph states (e.g., self-supervision).  
- Suggested improvement: include qualitative visualizations of produced graphs to illustrate internal representations.  
- Questions for authors: how to reduce supervision (unsupervised/self-supervised options), show detailed graph examples and their evolution, explain why direct reference helps, and handle tasks without pre-known graph structure.
