- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) with differential graph transformations to learn graph-to-graph mappings via gradient descent.
- Frames problems as learning cellular automata using sequences of growing graphs with neighbor pointers and binary-value nodes.
- Model can generate graph sequences from text and use the graph to produce textual outputs.
- Strong empirical results: >95% accuracy on 19/20 bAbI tasks; promising but imperfect results on Rule 30 and Turing machine tasks.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Fills a gap by enabling neural models to construct and modify graph structures rather than relying on fixed input graphs.
- Well-motivated and clearly positioned relative to GNNs, GGS-NNs, Memory Networks, DNCs, emphasizing interpretability of graph-structured internal state.
- Empirical validation is rigorous with careful experimental setup and appropriate baselines.
- Suggestion: analyze Rule 30 experiment limitations more thoroughly to explain why it remains unsolved.
- Suggestion: provide concrete time/space examples to clarify computational complexity and practical limits.
- Suggestion: include concrete bAbI examples showing how the direct reference mechanism operates in practice.
- Question: does the model learn partial Rule 30 patterns or fail systematically, and which transition aspects are most challenging?
- Question: how much of the performance gain is due to strong supervision (full graph information) versus the graph representation/architecture?
- Question: how does the fixed maximum-node design handle tasks that require more nodes, and what are the practical scalability limits?
