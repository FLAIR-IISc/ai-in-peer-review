- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), extending GGS-NNs to learn complex, differentiable graph transformations as internal representations.  
- Proposes five differentiable transformation types: node addition, node state update, edge update, propagation, and aggregation.  
- Maintains evolving graph states that are fully differentiable and trainable by gradient descent, mixing continuous and symbolic representations.  
- Uses learned graph transformations to iteratively construct/modify graphs from textual input for downstream tasks.  
- Strong empirical results on bAbI: model reaches ~95% accuracy on 19/20 tasks, with 11 tasks at 100% accuracy.  
- Successfully models rule-discovery tasks including a cellular automaton and Turing machines, showing capability to learn complex graph dynamics.  
- Clearly positioned relative to related work (Memory Networks, NTM, DNC) by explicitly modeling internal states as labeled graphs.  
- Decision: accept — novel framework, well-motivated, technically sound, and empirically compelling.  
- Limitation: requires ground-truth graph states at each timestep; supervision requirement needs further discussion and potential mitigation.  
- Limitation: computational complexity is quadratic in graph size and should be contextualized with trade-offs against expressiveness.  
- Presentation could be denser than ideal for a conference; recommend more intuitive explanations, visualizations, and a worked example for Algorithm 1.  
- Clarifying questions requested on: why Task 17 (Positional Reasoning) failed, why direct reference greatly improves performance and whether alternatives exist, and how performance scales with more than 1,000 training examples.
