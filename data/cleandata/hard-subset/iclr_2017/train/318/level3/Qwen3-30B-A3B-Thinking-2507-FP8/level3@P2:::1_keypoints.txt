1. Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that learns to construct and modify graphs as intermediate representations from textual input.  
2. Key technical contribution: differentiable graph transformations (node addition, node state update, edge update, propagation, aggregation).  
3. Evaluated on bAbI QA and rule-discovery tasks (cellular automata and Turing machines).  
4. Strong bAbI results: 95%+ accuracy on 19/20 tasks with direct reference, 11 tasks at 100%.  
5. Outperforms several state-of-the-art models on tasks where graph representations are advantageous.  
6. Fills a gap in literature by enabling graph-structured intermediate representations rather than only graph-structured input.  
7. Demonstrates ability to learn complex update rules for cellular automata and Turing machines.  
8. Rule 30 experiment remains far from solved and needs clearer reporting and analysis.  
9. Decision: Accept — significant contribution to neural networks for structured data.  
10. Recommend providing detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability (noting quadratic time/space growth).  
11. Recommend clarifying Rule 30 accuracy and why it is more challenging than bAbI tasks.  
12. Recommend comparing training time and resource requirements with other architectures (e.g., DNCs, memory networks).  
13. Ask authors to report exact Rule 30 accuracy and specific challenges faced.  
14. Ask how the model can scale to much larger graphs (thousands of nodes) and potential techniques to address quadratic complexity.  
15. Ask for more insight into why direct reference substantially improves training and whether that approach generalizes to other graph architectures.
