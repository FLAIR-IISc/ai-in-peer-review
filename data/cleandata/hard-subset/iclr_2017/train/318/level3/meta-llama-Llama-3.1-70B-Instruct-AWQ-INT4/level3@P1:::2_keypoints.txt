- Proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), extending GGS-NN to allow graph-structured intermediate representations and outputs.  
- GGT-NN can learn to construct and modify graphs from textual input and use them to produce diverse outputs.  
- Main contributions: introduction of GGT-NN, application to bAbI tasks, and learning update rules for a cellular automaton and 4-state Turing machines.  
- Approach is well-motivated, building on prior graph neural networks and seq2seq models.  
- Experiments show GGT-NN is effective on bAbI and rule-discovery tasks, achieving high accuracy on most tasks.  
- Model outperforms some existing methods but offers only marginal gains compared to memory networks; results remain preliminary.  
- Paper is technically sound, thought-provoking, and considered publishable by the reviewer (Decision: Accept).  
- Supporting strengths: significant extension of GGS-NN, demonstrated ability to model complex graph update rules, and strong empirical performance on several tasks.  
- Suggested improvements: clearer explanation for non-experts and more detailed analysis/comparison on bAbI tasks.  
- Practical concern: authors should report computational resources required for training, as scalability may be a limitation.  
- Questions for authors: how will training resource requirements be addressed for larger graphs?  
- Questions for authors: details on hyperparameter choices and tuning process?  
- Questions for authors: plans to extend GGT-NN to more complex scenarios like multiple graphs or graph sequences?
