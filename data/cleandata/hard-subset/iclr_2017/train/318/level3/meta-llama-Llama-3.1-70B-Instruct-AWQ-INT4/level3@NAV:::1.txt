**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

This paper introduces a novel neural network architecture, called the Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), which enables learning graph-to-graph tasks using gradient descent. The GGT-NN model combines ideas from prior work on Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) and Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks (GGS-NNs) to produce textual output via graphs as intermediate representations. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the GGT-NN model on the bAbI tasks, a set of simple natural-language tasks, and show that it outperforms state-of-the-art models on these tasks. Additionally, the authors apply the GGT-NN model to two additional tasks, a cellular automaton task and a Turing machine task, and demonstrate its ability to learn complex update rules.
