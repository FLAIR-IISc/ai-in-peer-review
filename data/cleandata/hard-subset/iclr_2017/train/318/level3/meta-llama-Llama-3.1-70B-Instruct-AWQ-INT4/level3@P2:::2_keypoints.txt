- Proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN): a novel, technically sound method for learning graph-structured state transitions using graphs as differentiable memory.  
- Demonstrates GGT-NN on bAbI tasks, showing the model can construct/modify graphs from text and produce varied outputs.  
- GGT-NN is a significant extension of GNNs and GGS-NNs, enabling graph-structured intermediate representations and outputs.  
- Achieves high accuracy on most bAbI tasks and outperforms some state-of-the-art methods on certain tasks.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing novelty and broad potential applicability.  
- Performance is comparable to top memory networks but still worse than traditional rule induction on these tasks.  
- Model complexity is substantially higher than prior methods (e.g., memory networks), which may hinder training and optimization.  
- Paper lacks sufficient analysis of why the model succeeds on some tasks and fails on others.  
- Reviewer requests more detailed analysis: ablation studies and comparisons with other approaches.  
- Suggests exploring model simplification to reduce complexity while retaining performance.  
- Recommends providing more examples and real-world applications to demonstrate practical usefulness.  
- Asks authors to discuss integration with other approaches (memory networks, rule induction) and plans to address the above concerns.
