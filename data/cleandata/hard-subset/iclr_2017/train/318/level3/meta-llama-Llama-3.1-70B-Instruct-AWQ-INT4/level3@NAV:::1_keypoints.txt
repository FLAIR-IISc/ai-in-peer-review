- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) for learning graph-to-graph tasks with gradient descent.  
- Combines ideas from Graph Neural Networks and Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks to generate text via intermediate graph representations.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on bAbI tasks, cellular automaton, and Turing machine tasks, reportedly outperforming state-of-the-art.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Key reason 1: well-motivated approach addressing limitations of existing graph-to-graph methods.  
- Key reason 2: strong empirical results across multiple tasks.  
- Paper includes thorough related-work discussion and detailed description of graph transformation operations and architecture.  
- Empirical analysis highlights both benefits and limitations of the approach.  
- Suggestion: provide more implementation details of graph transformation operations and network architecture.  
- Suggestion: discuss potential applications beyond the evaluated tasks.  
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis into factors contributing to GGT-NN’s success.  
- Questions for authors: more implementation details, plans for scaling to larger/more complex graphs, and further analysis of success factors.
