- Proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN): a graph-based differentiable memory that processes unstructured input and updates a graph representation of dialog.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on bAbI tasks, a simple cellular automaton, and a Turing machine; claims to be the first differentiable graph memory with advantages like distributed processing and explicit internal-state modeling.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.

Supporting arguments
- Tackles a well-motivated problem with clear placement in the literature and distinction from prior work.
- Clearly motivated approach that argues benefits of representing dialog as a graph and using differentiable graph memory for NLP.
- Experimental results support claims and provide comparisons with existing architectures.

Minor revisions
- Clarify the GGT-NN architecture and how its components interact.
- Provide more detailed analysis of experimental performance across tasks and datasets.
- Expand discussion of limitations and concrete future work to address them.

Additional feedback
- Add more detailed visualizations showing how learned graph structures relate to input data.
- Provide a more thorough analysis of computational complexity and ways to reduce it.
- Discuss potential real-world applications in more depth.

Questions for the authors
- Can you provide more detailed visualizations of the learned graph structures?
- How do you plan to address the high computational complexity of GGT-NN?
- Can you provide more detailed experimental analyses and comparisons with existing architectures?
- How will you extend GGT-NN to more complex tasks and larger datasets?
- What concrete real-world applications do you envision for GGT-NN?
