- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), a novel architecture for graph-to-graph learning with differentiable graph transformations.
- Extends GNNs and GGS-NNs by enabling graph-structured intermediate representations and outputs.
- Tackles a well-motivated question: how to handle graph-structured intermediate representations and outputs.
- Clear, well-structured presentation with detailed description of GGT-NN architecture and components.
- Provides strong empirical evidence, with experiments on bAbI tasks and rule discovery tasks supporting the model’s claims.
- Thorough explanation of the differentiable graph transformations and the training procedure, including supervised guidance.
- bAbI experiments demonstrate GGT-NN’s ability to generate graphs that capture underlying data structure.
- Rule discovery results show capacity to learn complex rules for graph evolution over time.
- Limitation: Rule 30 experiment remains far from solved; model struggles to learn correct evolution rules, acknowledged and discussed by authors.
- Suggested improvement: provide more implementation details for the differentiable graph transformations and specific architectures used.
- Suggested improvement: include more analysis of bAbI results and a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art models.
- Questions for authors: clarify implementation details of the differentiable graph transformations.
- Questions for authors: explain how architectures for the differentiable transformations were chosen and design considerations.
- Questions for authors: provide more analysis and comparisons of bAbI task results with SOTA.
- Questions for authors: discuss potential real-world applications and envisioned uses of GGT-NN.
- Overall assessment: strong paper making a significant contribution to graph neural networks with promising potential for complex graph-to-graph tasks.
