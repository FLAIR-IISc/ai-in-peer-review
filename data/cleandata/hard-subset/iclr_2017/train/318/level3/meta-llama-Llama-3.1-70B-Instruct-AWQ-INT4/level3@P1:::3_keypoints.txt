- Proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) to learn complex, differentiable graph transformations as internal representations.  
- Extends Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks (GGS-NNs) to construct and modify graphs based on textual input.  
- Well-motivated and grounded in literature, drawing on graph neural nets, RNNs, and differentiable neural computers.  
- Demonstrates empirical success on bAbI and rule discovery tasks with high accuracy.  
- Compares favorably to several state-of-the-art methods and outperforms many on certain tasks.  
- Key strength: maintains evolving, complex graph states while remaining fully differentiable and trainable by gradient descent.  
- Effectively mixes continuous and symbolic representations, offering more generality than fixed-shape memory models.  
- Major concern: requires ground-truth graph states at each timestep, limiting applicability to supervised settings.  
- Paper is dense and may be better suited to a journal than a conference, reducing readability.  
- Suggested improvement: provide more intuitive explanations of the model and its components.  
- Suggested improvement: add more visualizations to illustrate graph transformations and model behavior.  
- Suggested improvement: explore reducing supervision needs, e.g., fewer graph states or alternative supervision methods.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Questions for authors: clarify how graph transformations are learned/applied, plans to address timestep supervision requirement, and provide more visualizations.
