- Proposes an extension to Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks (GGS-NNs) to learn complex, differentiable graph transformations as internal representations.  
- Introduces the Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that combines differentiable graph transformations with a recurrent input model to build graphs from natural language.  
- GGT-NN maintains evolving, complex graph states while remaining fully differentiable and trainable by gradient descent.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on bAbI tasks and shows the model can learn update rules for a 1D cellular automaton and arbitrary 4-state Turing machines.  
- Strength: effectively mixes continuous and symbolic representations, making it more general than fixed-shape memory models (e.g., Memory Networks, Neural Turing Machines).  
- Strength: supports distributed per-node processing, which can benefit complex graph tasks such as pathfinding.  
- Major limitation: training requires ground-truth graph states at each timestep, limiting applicability to supervised settings.  
- Presentation concern: paper is dense and may be better suited to a journal than a conference due to readability issues.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address the noted concerns.  
- Revision request: provide more details on the training process, including optimization algorithm and hyperparameter tuning.  
- Revision request: explicitly discuss limitations (e.g., need for ground-truth states) and possible remedies.  
- Revision request: add more visualizations or examples to illustrate graph transformations and the model architecture.  
- Questions for authors: compare GGT-NN to other GNNs (GCNs, GATs).  
- Questions for authors: can GGT-NN handle temporal-reasoning tasks (e.g., bAbI task 14)?  
- Questions for authors: how can GGT-NN be extended to multi-graph tasks like graph alignment or matching?
