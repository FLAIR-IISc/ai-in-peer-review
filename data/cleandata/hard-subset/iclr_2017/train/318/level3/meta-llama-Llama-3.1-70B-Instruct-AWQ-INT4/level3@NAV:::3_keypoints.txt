- Summary: Proposes Gated Graph Transformer NN (GGT-NN), extending GGS-NNs with five differentiable graph transformations to learn complex, trainable graph-state transitions; evaluated on bAbI, cellular automata, and Turing-machine tasks.

- Decision: Recommend acceptance conditional on addressing readability and applicability concerns.

- Well-motivated: Clearly argues limitations of existing GNNs and need for flexible, differentiable graph transformations.

- Effective evaluation: Demonstrates the model can learn complex transformations and achieves strong / state-of-the-art results on several tasks.

- Novel contribution: Introduces a new, more flexible differentiable approach to graph transformations in GNNs.

- Readability concern: Paper is dense and may be better formatted with clearer sections, headings, and more intuitive explanations.

- Applicability concern: Method relies on ground-truth graph states at each timestep, which may restrict use to tasks with such supervision.

- Additional experiments requested: Should test robustness across varied graph structures, node types, and edge types.

- Question 1: How will the authors address limited applicability from requiring ground-truth intermediate graph states?

- Question 2: Can the authors justify and provide more insight into the design/choice of the five differentiable transformation types?

- Question 3: How do the authors plan to extend the approach to more complex settings, e.g., multiple graphs or dynamic graph structures?
