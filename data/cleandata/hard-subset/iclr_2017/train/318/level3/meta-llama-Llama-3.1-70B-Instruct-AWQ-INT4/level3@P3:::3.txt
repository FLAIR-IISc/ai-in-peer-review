This paper proposes an extension to Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks (GGS-NNs) that enables learning complex, differentiable graph transformations as internal representations. The authors introduce the Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) model, which combines a series of differentiable graph transformations with a recurrent input model to incrementally construct a graph given natural language input.

The GGT-NN model is designed to maintain evolving, complex graph states while remaining fully differentiable and trainable by gradient descent. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the model on the bAbI tasks, a set of simple natural-language tasks, and show that it can learn complex update rules by training it to model a simple 1D cellular automaton and arbitrary 4-state Turing machines.

The paper's strengths include its ability to effectively mix continuous and symbolic representations, making it more general than fixed-shape memory models such as Memory Networks and Neural Turing Machines. Additionally, the GGT-NN model can process data in a distributed fashion, allowing each node to independently process its surroundings, which can be beneficial for complex tasks such as pathfinding on a graph.

However, the paper also has some limitations. One major concern is that training the model requires ground-truth graph states at each timestep, limiting its applicability to tasks where such supervision is available. Additionally, the paper is dense and may be better suited to a journal than a conference, which can hinder readability.

Based on these factors, I would recommend accepting this paper, but with some revisions to address the concerns mentioned above.
