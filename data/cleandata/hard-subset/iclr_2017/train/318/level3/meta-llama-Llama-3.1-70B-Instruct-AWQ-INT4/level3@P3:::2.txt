This paper proposes a novel approach to learning graphical state transitions, introducing the Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) model. The GGT-NN model extends previous work on Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) by allowing graph-structured intermediate representations and outputs. The model is designed to learn complex update rules and manipulate graphical internal states based on textual input. The author demonstrates the effectiveness of the GGT-NN model on the bAbI tasks and two additional tasks: a 1-dimensional cellular automaton and simulating an arbitrary 2-symbol 4-state Turing machine.

I decide to accept this paper, primarily due to its novelty and the potential impact of the proposed approach on the field of natural language processing and graph-based learning. The paper is well-motivated, and the author provides a thorough review of related work, highlighting the advantages of the GGT-NN model over existing approaches.

The paper supports its claims through a series of experiments, demonstrating the effectiveness of the GGT-NN model on various tasks. The author provides a detailed description of the model architecture, including the differentiable graph transformations, and the experimental setup. The results show that the GGT-NN model can learn complex update rules and achieve high accuracy on the bAbI tasks and the additional tasks.

However, I would like to see more analysis on the limitations of the model and potential areas for improvement.
