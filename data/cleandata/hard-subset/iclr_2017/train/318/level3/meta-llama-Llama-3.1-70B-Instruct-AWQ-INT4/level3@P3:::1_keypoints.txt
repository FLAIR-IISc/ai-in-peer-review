- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), an extension of GGS-NNs that uses graph-structured intermediate representations to construct/modify graphs from text and produce outputs.
- Shows GGT-NN is effective on bAbI tasks, a 1D cellular automaton, and arbitrary 4-state Turing machines.
- Recommendation: accept the paper.
- Novel, well-motivated architecture that extends prior work in NLP and graph neural networks.
- Strong empirical evidence with thorough evaluations and comparisons to state-of-the-art models.
- Demonstrates ability to learn complex update rules and reason about temporal sequences.
- Provides clear explanation of model components, differentiable graph transformations, supervision scheme, and detailed result analysis.
- Scalability concern: training time and memory grow quickly with task complexity; needs optimization.
- Supervision concern: current results rely on strong supervision; potential to explore weaker or no graph-level supervision.
- Question: how will authors address scalability and what optimizations could be explored?
- Question: can GGT-NN be trained with weaker or no graph-level supervision, and what are the trade-offs?
- Question: how will the model be extended to more complex tasks involving multiple temporal sequences or multiple graphs?
