- Proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) to learn graphical state transitions with graph-structured intermediate representations and outputs.  
- Extends prior GNN work to allow manipulation of graphical internal states conditioned on textual input.  
- Demonstrates the model on bAbI tasks, a 1D cellular automaton, and a 2-symbol 4-state Turing machine, achieving high accuracy.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance citing novelty and potential impact on NLP and graph-based learning.  
- Paper is well-motivated and includes a thorough related-work review highlighting advantages over existing approaches.  
- Provides detailed model architecture, including differentiable graph transformations, and describes the experimental setup.  
- Requires substantial supervision to extract meaningful graph-structured data, posing a limitation for real-world use.  
- Training time and memory grow quickly with task complexity, limiting scalability to larger or more complex graphs.  
- Reviewer requests more details on experimental setup: hyperparameter tuning and computational resources.  
- Reviewer asks for deeper analysis of learned graph structures and update rules to clarify model behavior and applicability.  
- Reviewer requests plans for addressing supervision needs and extending the model to larger/complex tasks.  
- Overall positive: GGT-NN shows promise and the reviewer looks forward to further developments and applications.
