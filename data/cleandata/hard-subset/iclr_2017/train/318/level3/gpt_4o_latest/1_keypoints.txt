- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) with differentiable graph transformations for graph-to-graph classification.  
- Enables construction, modification, and use of graph-structured intermediate representations via gradient descent.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on bAbI tasks and success on various tasks including rule discovery for cellular automata and Turing machines.  
- Struggles to learn Rule 30 in the cellular automaton task, indicating limitations in capturing some complex rules.  
- Extends GGS-NN ideas to generate textual outputs using graphs as intermediate representations.  
- Decision: Accept, based on novelty, empirical results, and potential impact.  
- Novelty/impact: Differentiable graph transformations open new possibilities for structured-data tasks and more interpretable intermediate states.  
- Empirical validation: Strong bAbI results and generalization to rule-discovery tasks, supported by ablation studies.  
- Scientific rigor: Detailed experiments and ablations strengthen claims, though Rule 30 failure warrants further investigation.  
- Clarity/reproducibility: Paper includes clear descriptions, pseudocode, and diagrams aiding reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: Provide deeper analysis of the Rule 30 failure and test alternative architectures or supervision.  
- Suggestion: Address scalability concerns from quadratic time/space complexity via sparse edges or selective node processing.  
- Suggestion: Evaluate generalization to much longer sequences (e.g., 50+ timesteps) to assess robustness.  
- Suggestion: Include more comparison with non-graph models (e.g., transformers) to highlight advantages of graph representations.  
- Question: Did you try alternative architectures or training strategies to overcome Rule 30 failure?  
- Question: How does the model scale to significantly larger graphs or longer sequences, and what are the bottlenecks?  
- Question: Can the graph transformations be extended to dynamic graphs with changing node/edge types over time?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to hyperparameters like propagation steps and hidden-state size?
