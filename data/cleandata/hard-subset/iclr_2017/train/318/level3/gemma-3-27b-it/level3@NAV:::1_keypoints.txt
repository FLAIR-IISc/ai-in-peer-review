- Introduces a novel Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) with differentiable graph transformations for graph-structured data.
- Core idea: learn cellular-automaton-like update rules and represent data as evolving sequences of graphs with node/edge manipulations.
- Combines with prior GGS-NN work to form a flexible framework for dynamic state representation.
- Shows strong performance on bAbI tasks, which is a significant contribution.
- Reviewer rejects the paper due to weak empirical validation and limited demonstration on complex tasks.
- Rule 30 experiment, intended as a proof-of-concept for learning complex rules, showed limited success and undermines confidence in generalization.
- Improvement on bAbI may be driven by the sequence modeling component rather than learned graph transformations.
- Lack of comparable results on tasks explicitly designed to test graph transformation learning is a major drawback.
- Recommendation: include ablation studies to isolate contributions of graph transformations and the direct reference mechanism.
- Recommendation: analyze hyperparameter sensitivity, especially for graph-transformation-related settings.
- Recommendation: provide visualizations of learned graphs (Rule 30, Turing machine) to clarify what the model learns.
- Recommendation: provide more detailed scalability analysis and experiments on larger graphs beyond current complexity discussion.
- Questions for authors: specify metrics and quantitative results for Rule 30 and what “far from solved” means.
- Questions for authors: report attempts to scale Rule 30 (larger initial states, longer simulations) and limitations observed.
- Questions for authors: detail network architecture and optimization parameters used per experiment.
- Question for authors: compare GGT-NN to a standard seq2seq model on graph-preprocessed bAbI inputs to isolate benefits of learned graph transformations.
