- Proposal: Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) extending GGS-NNs to learn differentiable graph transformations as internal representations.
- Core contribution: Enables models to manipulate and reason about graph-structured data via learned graph-state transitions.
- Experiments: Demonstrated on bAbI tasks, a cellular automaton, and Turing machines, showing learning of complex rules and evolving graph states.
- Strength: Seamlessly integrates continuous and symbolic representations, more general than fixed-shape memory models.
- Implementation: Technical implementation appears sound.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends rejecting the paper.
- Main limitation: Heavy reliance on complete graph-state supervision at each timestep is a significant drawback.
- Practicality concern: Supervision requirement makes the approach impractical for many real-world settings lacking such labels.
- Claim limitation: The claim of learning “graphical formulations” is weakened if graphs are provided rather than discovered from unstructured input.
- Missing baselines: Insufficient comparison to unsupervised or weakly-supervised methods for learning graph structures.
- Analysis gap: Needs deeper analysis of what the network learns, especially on the Turing machine task.
- Improve supervision discussion: Paper should discuss avenues to reduce or remove the need for ground-truth graph states.
- Improve readability: Restructure for clearer narrative, expand introduction, provide more intuitive explanations, and break up large paragraphs.
- Clarify direct reference: Explain why direct reference improves performance and under what conditions it helps most.
- More visuals: Provide detailed visualizations/examples of learned graph transformations to illustrate model behavior.
- Question — cost: What is the computational cost of the graph transformations and how does it scale with graph size?
- Question — flexibility: Can the GGT-NN be adapted to handle graphs with varying node and edge types more flexibly?
- Question — initialization: What strategies could initialize graph transformations to accelerate learning?
- Question — transformation importance: Is there an analysis of which graph transformations are most critical for performance on different tasks?
