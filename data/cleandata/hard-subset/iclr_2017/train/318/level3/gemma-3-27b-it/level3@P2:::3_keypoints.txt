- Proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), extending GGS-NNs with learnable graph-structured intermediate representations.
- Core contribution: a set of differentiable graph transformations (node addition, state update, edge update, propagation, aggregation) to construct and modify graphs from input.
- Demonstrates strong performance on bAbI tasks, a cellular automaton, and Turing machines, especially when graphical representations are natural.
- Decision: Reject.
- Primary reason: heavy reliance on complete graph-level supervision (ground-truth graphs at each timestep) that is unrealistic for many real-world scenarios.
- Authors acknowledge supervision limitation but do not convincingly explore weakly supervised or unsupervised alternatives.
- Performance gains may be partially attributable to the strong supervisory signal, making true generalization unclear.
- Paper is dense and detailed, hindering readability; needs clearer, more intuitive presentation of core ideas.
- Organization could be improved and extensive appendix suggests content may be more suitable for a journal.
- Related-work comparison is comprehensive but claimed advantages are weakened by the supervision requirement.
- Distributed nature and computational cost are a valid disadvantage and need further exploration and optimization strategies.
- Suggest exploring weak supervision approaches (pretraining on unlabeled data, fine-tuning with limited labels, contrastive learning).
- Recommend ablation studies to quantify each graph transformation’s contribution.
- Request a detailed scalability analysis: how cost scales with graph size and strategies to mitigate quadratic complexity.
- Recommend qualitative analysis and visualizations of learned graphs (especially for cellular automaton and Turing machine tasks).
- Recommend simplifying presentation by focusing on core contributions and moving technical detail to the appendix.
- Question: what is the computational cost of training and inference as a function of graph size n, and practical graph size limits?
- Question: have alternative training strategies been explored to reduce reliance on full graph supervision, and with what results?
- Question: can authors provide examples of learned graph structures for the cellular automaton and Turing machine tasks?
- Question: how sensitive is the model to the initial graph structure, and can it learn from a completely empty initial graph?
