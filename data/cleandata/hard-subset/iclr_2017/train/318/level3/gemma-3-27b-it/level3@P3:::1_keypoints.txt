- Decision: Accept — paper presents a strong, novel contribution.

- Core contribution: Introduces Gated Graph Transformer NN (GGT-NN) that learns differentiable graph transformations for graph-to-graph tasks.

- Novelty: Learns and modifies graph structure (not limited to fixed graphs), increasing adaptability.

- Mechanism: Learns additions, updates, propagations, and aggregations as differentiable transformations.

- Empirical results: State-of-the-art performance on bAbI tasks with consistent high accuracy.

- Demonstrations: Successfully learned simple cellular automata and Turing machines; Rule 30 remained unsolved.

- Presentation: Well-written with detailed appendix aiding reproducibility.

- Improvement — Rule 30: Paper should provide more detail on what the model learned and failure modes for Rule 30.

- Improvement — Scalability: Need deeper discussion and experiments on addressing quadratic scaling (beyond sparse/partial processing).

- Improvement — Ablations: More ablation studies to isolate the contribution of direct reference and robustness to noise.

- Improvement — Comparison to GGS-NN: Include direct comparative experiments to quantify advantages over GGS-NN.

- Question: Describe observed training behavior on Rule 30 — approximations learned, convergence issues, and visualizations of learned graphs.

- Question: Which scalability mitigations were tried or are most promising (e.g., graph coarsening, attention, sparse ops)?

- Question: How sensitive are results to initialization and hyperparameters; were extensive searches performed and what were optimal settings?

- Question: Why does the extended graph-sequence input model perform slightly worse — could the recurrent combiner be a bottleneck or is overfitting the cause?

- Question: Clarify choices of activation functions and architectures within graph transformations — empirical vs. theoretical justification.
