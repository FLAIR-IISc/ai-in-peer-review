- Paper introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) to learn differentiable graph transformations for dynamic graph construction and manipulation.  
- Decision: Reject — interesting idea with promising results but key claims insufficiently supported.  
- Main critique: Claims of learning cellular automata and Turing machines are not backed by quantitative evidence; visual inspection (Figure 3) is insufficient.  
- Recommend reporting quantitative metrics (e.g., per-cell/prediction accuracy, loss) for CA and Turing machine tasks.  
- bAbI results are competitive but novelty there appears incremental given strong existing baselines.  
- Ablation study is limited; interactions and individual contributions of node/edge/state/propagation/aggregation transformations are not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Authors note "direct reference" improves performance but provide insufficient analysis of why that is the case.  
- Experimental setup lacks detail on hyperparameter tuning, architecture specifics (hidden sizes, GRU units) and training procedures, hurting reproducibility.  
- Need deeper analysis of which transformations are critical and whether simpler models could suffice.  
- Suggest hyperparameter sensitivity analysis to assess robustness.  
- Suggest richer visualizations and interpretability attempts to explain learned rules for CA and Turing machines.  
- Recommend comparing to more relevant baselines specifically for CA and Turing machine tasks.  
- Request clearer formal definition of the graph structure (what nodes/edges represent).  
- Clarify Algorithm 1 & 2: do all transformations occur every timestep or are operations sequential?  
- Questions for authors: provide quantitative CA/TM results, computational cost vs alternatives, sensitivity to initial graph and convergence behavior, limitations and failure modes, and the hyperparameters used for TM and CA training.
