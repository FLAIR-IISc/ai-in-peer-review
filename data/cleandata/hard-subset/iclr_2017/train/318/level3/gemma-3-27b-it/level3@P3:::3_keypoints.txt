- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that extends GGS-NNs to manipulate evolving graph-structured internal representations.  
- Shows ability to learn complex graph transformations from text and solve bAbI tasks.  
- Demonstrates discovery of rules for simple computational systems (cellular automata, Turing machines) using predefined graph encodings.  
- Core contribution: fully differentiable maintenance of evolving graph states enabling end-to-end gradient training versus fixed-shape memory models.  
- Notable combination of continuous and symbolic representations for flexible knowledge representation and reasoning.  
- Recommendation: Reject due to heavy reliance on ground-truth graph state supervision at every timestep, limiting real-world applicability.  
- Main limitation: model learns transformations on pre-defined structures rather than inferring graph structure from raw data.  
- Presentation is dense, notation-heavy, and sometimes convoluted; inconsistent equation formatting hinders accessibility.  
- Technical implementation is solid: well-defined differentiable graph transformations and logically sound architecture.  
- Experimental results are encouraging and comparisons to other memory architectures are fair; supplementary transformation details are helpful.  
- Suggest exploring methods to reduce supervision dependency (semi-supervised, unsupervised, or structure inference).  
- Recommend simplifying presentation, adding intuitive explanations, diagrams, and moving some details to appendices.  
- Ask for expanded discussion of computational cost and scalability (quadratic scaling concerns).  
- Request ablation studies to isolate contributions of individual graph transformations and the direct reference mechanism.  
- Propose including qualitative visualizations of learned graph structures for intuition.  
- Questions for authors: potential for RL/unsupervised graph-structure learning; memory/training complexity and optimizations; sensitivity to story graph encodings in bAbI; concrete target applications and adaptation challenges; justification of hyperparameter choices.
