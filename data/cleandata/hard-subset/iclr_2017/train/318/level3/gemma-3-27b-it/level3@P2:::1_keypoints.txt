- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), extending GGS-NNs with differentiable graph transformations (node add, state update, edge update, propagation, aggregation).

- Demonstrates GGT-NN on bAbI, Rule 30 cellular automaton, and Turing machines; bAbI results competitive but use strong supervision.

- Decision: Reject — experimental validation insufficiently convincing and presentation lacks clarity.

- Strength: Conceptually appealing to learn graph structure rather than rely on predefined graphs.

- Weakness: Rule 30 and Turing machine results are underreported — only accuracy given, no insight into rule quality or exactness.

- Weakness: No thorough ablation study to assess contribution of each graph transformation (e.g., effect of removing edge update).

- Weakness: Presentation problems — dense notation, convoluted descriptions, Figures 1–2 hard to interpret, Algorithm 1 notation unclear.

- Request: Provide more detailed quantitative evaluation for Rule 30 and Turing machine tasks, examples of learned graphs, and metrics beyond accuracy.

- Request: Conduct a comprehensive ablation study to isolate component contributions.

- Request: Discuss computational complexity and scalability, particularly quadratic scaling with graph size.

- Request: Offer a more nuanced comparison with related work (e.g., differentiable neural computers) on capacity and efficiency.

- Request: Report hyperparameter sensitivity and identify critical parameters for performance.

- Request: Include clearer visualizations of learned graphs, especially for Rule 30 and Turing experiments.

- Request: Clarify Algorithm 1 variables such as i(k), D(k), and query-related notation.

- Questions for authors: (1) Explain limitations on bAbI Task 17 — data vs model capacity. (2) What maximum Turing-machine complexity and rulesets were learned? (3) How does GGT-NN compare to GGS-NN when input is already a graph? (4) Why does direct reference input improve performance? (5) Why use GRU cells and were alternatives (e.g., LSTMs) tried?
