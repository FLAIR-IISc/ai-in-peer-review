- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), an extension of GGS-NNs.  
- Core novelty: differentiable graph construction and manipulation during end-to-end training.  
- Model dynamically builds and updates graph-structured intermediate representations from text.  
- Demonstrated on bAbI QA tasks, cellular automata, and Turing machines to learn complex state transitions.  
- Does not achieve state-of-the-art on bAbI but shows promising new approach and technical soundness.  
- Recommendation: Accept, due to novelty and future potential outweighing current performance limits.  
- Addresses important problem of representing and reasoning about stateful information beyond fixed memories.  
- Learns flexible graph representations that can adapt internal state based on input and task.  
- Experimental results show capability to learn complex rules and internal dynamics, competitive with strong baselines.  
- Ablation studies (with/without direct reference) provide useful insight into component importance.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and includes appendices that aid reproducibility.  
- Performance analysis lacking: need deeper investigation into why it underperforms on some bAbI tasks.  
- Scalability concern: model scales quadratically with graph size; needs techniques like sparsity or summarization.  
- Generalization limited: Turing machine experiments are encouraging but scope of rule-based tasks should be expanded.  
- Visualization gap: more visual examples of learned graphs would clarify model behavior on tasks like bAbI.  
- Comparison gap: discuss relationship between learned graphs and established knowledge graph representations.  
- Question: Why did task 17 (Positional Reasoning) perform poorly and were alternate graph structures/training tried?  
- Question: Is summing partial vectors into D(k) the only direct-reference method tried, or were attention-based methods considered?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to activation functions and hyperparameters in GRUs and graph transformations?  
- Question: Are pre-trained models available alongside the released code for reproducibility?  
- Question: Given computational cost, are there plans for parallelization or distributed training strategies?
