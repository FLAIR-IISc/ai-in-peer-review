- Proposes representing dialogue states as graphs used as differentiable memory in a Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN).
- Novelty: first implementation of a differentiable graph-structured memory and learning graph transformations during inference.
- Demonstrated on bAbI tasks and shown to learn rules for cellular automata and Turing machines.
- Recommendation: accept, based on conceptual novelty and promising potential despite limited performance gains on bAbI.
- Significance stems from conceptual contribution and potential to model complex relationships beyond simpler memory structures.
- Technical soundness: paper is rigorous and revised version improved clarity and readability.
- Experimental setup is solid; inclusion of rule-discovery tasks strengthens claims beyond question answering.
- Authors position work well within memory network and graph neural network literature, highlighting unique contributions.
- Weakness: increased model complexity raises concerns about interpretability and scalability.
- Suggested improvements: visualize learned graphs (e.g., from bAbI) to analyze representations and reasoning.
- Suggested improvements: perform a more thorough ablation study to measure component impacts (direct reference, propagation steps).
- Questions for authors: scalability and strategies to mitigate quadratic cost; examples and analysis of learned graph structures; generalization to new rules and limits in learning new rules.
