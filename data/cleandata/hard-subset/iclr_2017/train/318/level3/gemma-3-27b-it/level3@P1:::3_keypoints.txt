- Proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), extending GGS-NNs with differentiable graph additions, updates, propagations, and aggregations.  
- Trains the model with supervised graph states at every timestep to learn constructing and manipulating internal graph representations.  
- Demonstrates success on most bAbI tasks and in discovering rules for simple cellular automata and Turing machines.  
- Decision: Reject — overall interesting but full timestep supervision and paper density outweigh contributions for conference acceptance.  
- Primary limitation: requires precise ground-truth graph state at every timestep, severely limiting real-world applicability.  
- Authors note the supervision issue but do not provide a convincing approach to relax this requirement.  
- Paper is dense and mathematically heavy, making it hard to digest in a conference format.  
- Empirical results are positive but not clearly superior to existing methods given the strong supervision.  
- Notable strength on task 19 (pathfinding) but weakness on task 17 (positional reasoning), indicating scalability or capacity limits.  
- Scalability concerns: performance may degrade as number of entities, edges, and types increases.  
- Recommend exploring weakly supervised or unsupervised approaches to reduce reliance on full graph supervision.  
- Recommend a detailed scalability analysis varying graph size and complexity.  
- Recommend ablation studies to quantify the contribution of each graph transformation component.  
- Recommend simplifying presentation and expanding comparisons to related graph neural architectures.  
- Questions for authors: what are training/inference time and memory costs compared to other methods as graph size grows?  
- Questions: have you explored different graph-state initialization methods or ways to learn edge/node types?  
- Questions: what regularization techniques were used to prevent overfitting under strong supervision?  
- Questions: how could GGT-NN be applied to real-world tasks lacking fully supervised graph data (e.g., knowledge graph construction from text)?
