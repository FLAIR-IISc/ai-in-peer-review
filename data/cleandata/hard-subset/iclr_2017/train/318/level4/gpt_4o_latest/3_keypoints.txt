1. Introduces an enhancement to Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network enabling complex, learnable graph transformations.  
2. Core idea: construct or modify an internal graph representation to solve tasks, demonstrated on question-answering.  
3. Defines five differentiable transformations that are learned, typically in a supervised setting with state annotations.  
4. Presents a specific model instance that processes input sequences and iteratively updates an internal graph to produce predictions.  
5. Demonstrates effectiveness on QA tasks (e.g., bAbI) with promising empirical results.  
6. Method compelling because it keeps complex graph state differentiable and trainable via gradient descent.  
7. Viewed as a successful integration of continuous and symbolic representations.  
8. More general than prior neural-symbolic efforts (e.g., Memory Networks, NTM) since the state structure can evolve dynamically.  
9. Primary concern: training requires providing the graph state at each timestep, which is not scalable to more complex problems.  
10. Additional concern: paper is dense and hard to read; content may be better suited for a journal than a conference.
