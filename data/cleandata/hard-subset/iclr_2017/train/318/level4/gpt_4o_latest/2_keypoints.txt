- Introduces an on-the-fly method to represent dialog as a graph memory and evaluates it on bAbI tasks.
- Integrates graph learning into inference and uses long-term representation learning to optimize graph transformations and sentence encodings.
- Claimed to be the first implementation of a differentiable memory represented as a graph.
- Method is significantly more complex than prior approaches (e.g., memory networks).
- Does not show substantial performance improvements on bAbI tasks compared to simpler models.
- Work is preliminary but the graph-based memory concept appears to have greater potential than simpler structures like stacks.
- Major concern is clarity, though the manuscript has been substantially improved from an earlier, less readable version.
- Reviewer finds the paper original, technically sound (within their understanding), and intellectually stimulating—worthy of publication.
- Preliminary results do not yet demonstrate superior learning or generalization versus other methods; performance matches top memory networks but lags behind rule-induction approaches.
