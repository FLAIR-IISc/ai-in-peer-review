This research paper proposes an extension of the gated graph sequence neural network, incorporating the ability to generate complex graph transformations within this model. The idea behind this is to propose a method that would enable the construction/modification of a graph structure as an internal representation to solve a problem, and specifically to solve question-and-answer problems in this paper. The author proposes 5 different possible differentiable transformations that will be learned on a training set, typically in a supervised manner where the graph's state is given at each time-step. A specific implementation of the model is presented that takes a sequence as input, repeatedly updates an internal graph state into a final prediction, and can be applied to solve QA tasks (such as BaBi), with interesting results.

The approach in this paper is indeed interesting because the proposed model is able to maintain its current state representation as a complex graph, but still retains the property of being differentiable and can therefore be easily learned through gradient-descent techniques. This can be seen as a successful attempt to combine continuous and symbolic representations. Furthermore, it seems more general that recent efforts have been made to add some 'symbolic' things to differentiable models (memory networks, NTM, etc...) because the size of the state is not fixed here and can evolve. My main concern is how the model is trained, i.e., by providing the graph's state at each time-step, which can only be done for specific tasks (such as Babi), and may not be a solution for more complex problems. My second concern is about the entire content of the paper, which would probably be best suited for a journal format and not a conference format, making the article still difficult to read due to its density.
