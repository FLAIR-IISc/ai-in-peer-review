- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that builds and modifies differentiable graph-structured intermediate representations from text.  
- Uses these graphs to produce outputs; demonstrated on bAbI QA, cellular automata, and Turing machine rule discovery.  
- Achieves 95%+ accuracy on 19/20 bAbI tasks and notably outperforms others on the pathfinding task (Task 19).  
- Key innovation: differentiable graph transformations enabling incremental graph construction and modification.  
- Decision: Weak Accept — technically sound, novel, and promising results, especially for tasks suited to graphical representations.  
- Well-motivated extension of prior graph neural network work and properly situated within related literature.  
- Addresses the limitation of prior GNNs that require fixed graph inputs by allowing graph-structured intermediate representations.  
- Strong empirical support including ablation studies showing the importance of the direct reference mechanism.  
- Claimed advantages over memory networks and related approaches need clearer articulation and quantification.  
- Computational complexity scales quadratically with input size; paper should discuss optimizations (e.g., sparse edges) and impact.  
- The “direct reference” mechanism needs clearer explanation of implementation details and guidance on when to use it.  
- Recommend a more detailed performance comparison with GGS-NN (Li et al., 2016), especially for textual bAbI tasks.  
- Ask authors for more details on computational efficiency (training time and memory) versus other approaches.  
- Ask which specific bAbI tasks benefit most from direct reference and why.  
- Ask how model performance scales on rule-discovery tasks as rule complexity or number of states increases.
