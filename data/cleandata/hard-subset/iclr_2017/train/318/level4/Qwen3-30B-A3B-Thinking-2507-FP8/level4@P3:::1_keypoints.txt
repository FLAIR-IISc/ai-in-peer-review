1. Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) for differentiable graph construction and modification from text.  
2. Shows strong empirical performance: 95%+ on 19/20 bAbI tasks, 98.3% on Rule 30, and models arbitrary Turing machines.  
3. Recommendation: accept — well-motivated extension with strong results across tasks.  
4. Key advance over prior work: enables dynamic graph construction instead of requiring fixed graph inputs.  
5. Empirical results outperform or match state-of-the-art on several bAbI tasks, especially those benefiting from graph structure (e.g., pathfinding).  
6. Ablation studies indicate the direct reference mechanism substantially improves performance and sample efficiency.  
7. Theoretical foundation and implementation details are clearly defined and sound (Section 3, Appendix B).  
8. Contribution: learns graphical intermediate representations from raw textual input without preprocessed graphs.  
9. Suggestion: clarify how sentences map to graph structure in bAbI with a concrete example.  
10. Suggestion: include visual comparisons of predicted vs. ground-truth graphs for the Rule 30 task.  
11. Suggestion: investigate computational/scaling limitations (quadratic complexity) with experiments on larger graphs.  
12. Question: how are entity names handled when they appear as substrings of other words (e.g., “green” vs “greenhouse”)?  
13. Question: provide exact generalization results for Rule 30 on longer sequences (20 and 30 timesteps).  
14. Question: why does direct reference help so much, and can it be integrated into other graph architectures?
