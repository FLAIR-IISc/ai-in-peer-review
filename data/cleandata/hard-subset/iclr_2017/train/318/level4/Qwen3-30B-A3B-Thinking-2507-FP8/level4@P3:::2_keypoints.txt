- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that learns differentiable graph construction and modification from textual input.  
- GGT-NN achieves strong empirical results: 95%+ on 19/20 bAbI tasks (with direct reference), learns Rule 30 cellular automaton rules, and models Turing machines.  
- Key innovation: differentiable graph transformations enabling end-to-end learning of dynamic graph-structured internal representations, extending GGS-NNs.  
- Decision: Accept — meaningful, well-motivated contribution with strong experimental support.  
- Addresses the clear problem of moving from fixed-input graphs to architectures that can dynamically build and manipulate graph representations.  
- Well-placed in literature, comparing to memory networks, neural Turing machines, and graph convolutional nets, and clarifying how GGT-NNs differ.  
- Provides sufficient architectural, training, and evaluation details to support claims.  
- Major concern: computational complexity — graph propagation scales quadratically and practical optimizations are not explored.  
- Suggests strengthening empirical comparisons by including more recent models and deeper analysis of why GGT-NNs outperform on specific tasks.  
- Recommends more analysis of when and which bAbI tasks particularly benefit from graph-based representations.  
- Calls for a more detailed discussion of limitations, especially scalability to larger graphs and more complex tasks.  
- Asks authors to specify which bAbI tasks gain most from direct reference and explain why.  
- Requests elaboration on why the graph-based approach excels at pathfinding (task 19) and whether that advantage holds for larger pathfinding problems.  
- Asks how model performance scales with increasing complexity of cellular automata and Turing machines (more states/symbols).  
- Requests discussion of strategies to mitigate quadratic time/space scaling, e.g., sparse representations or selective node processing.
