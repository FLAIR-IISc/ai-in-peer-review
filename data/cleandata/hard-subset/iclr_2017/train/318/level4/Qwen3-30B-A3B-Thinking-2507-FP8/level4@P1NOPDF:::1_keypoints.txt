- Proposes a novel framework for graph-to-graph classification using differential graph transformations to learn sequences of evolving graphs via gradients.  
- Naturally models cellular automata by representing states as nodes, neighbor relations as edges, and binary values via specialized nodes.  
- Architecture can learn graph sequences but fails to solve Rule 30, indicating substantial room for improvement on that task.  
- Integrates GGS-NN to use graphs as an intermediate representation for text generation, achieving state-of-the-art on bAbI QA tasks.  
- Failure on Rule 30 weakens the claim that differential transformations robustly capture complex dynamics; authors should clarify if limitations are architectural, training-related, or task-inherent.  
- Description of the graph transformation mechanism is under-specified—missing precise update rules and clear interaction with cellular-automaton transitions.  
- Lack of mathematical formulations or pseudocode makes it hard to assess novelty relative to prior graph neural network work.  
- Integration with GGS-NN needs stronger justification; no ablation studies isolate the graph-based representation from other architectural choices.  
- Paper’s scope feels disjointed: CA example motivates the method but primary empirical validation focuses on non-graph bAbI tasks, weakening coherence.  
- Overall: idea is novel and promising and bAbI results are notable, but incomplete Rule 30 validation, insufficient technical detail, and missing ablations limit the paper’s impact.
