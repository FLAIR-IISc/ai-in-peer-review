

1. The paper introduces the Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), a novel extension of GGS-NNs that enables the model to learn and manipulate graph-structured intermediate representations based on textual input. The authors demonstrate that this architecture can successfully solve most bAbI tasks (achieving 95% accuracy on 19 of 20 tasks), discover rules for cellular automata, and model Turing machines. The key contribution lies in the introduction of differentiable graph transformations that allow the network to dynamically construct and modify graph structures during inference.

2. I recommend acceptance. The primary reasons for this decision are that the paper presents a genuinely novel approach to modeling structured data with a differentiable graph representation, and the empirical results demonstrate competitive performance on standard benchmarks while providing a more interpretable internal state than previous approaches.

3. The GGT-NN model's ability to learn graph structures from textual input represents a significant advancement over previous graph-based neural network approaches. The paper provides thorough experimental validation across multiple domains (bAbI tasks, cellular automata, and Turing machines), showing that the model can learn complex rules and achieve high accuracy on diverse tasks. The architectural innovations in differentiable graph transformations are well-motivated and properly placed within the literature, building upon existing work while introducing meaningful extensions. The paper's claims are well-supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical results.

4. To further strengthen the paper, I suggest clarifying the relationship between the different graph transformation operations in the main text, perhaps with a more detailed schematic. Additionally, the authors could provide more explicit discussion of the computational trade-offs between GGT-NNs and other approaches, including a more detailed analysis of training time and memory requirements. The comparison with the pre-existing GGS-NN model could be expanded to include a more direct analysis of the advantages gained by the new graph transformation capabilities.

5. Could the authors provide more insight into how the model handles cases where the optimal graph structure changes dramatically between timesteps? How does the model's performance scale with increasingly complex graph structures, and what are the computational limitations of the current implementation? Finally, could the authors elaborate on how the direct reference mechanism specifically contributes to the model's ability to handle complex relational reasoning tasks?
