- Proposes extending GGNNs to learn complex, differentiable graph transformations as part of an internal, dynamically changing graph-structured state.  
- Core idea: allow the model to build/modify its graph state during computation for tasks like question answering (bAbI).  
- Introduces five distinct differentiable graph transformations learned in a supervised way, with graph state provided at each training timestep.  
- Presents an instantiated model that iteratively updates an internal graph from sequence input and yields strong/promising results on bAbI tasks.  
- Conceptually interesting: combines gradient-based learning with evolving symbolic graph representations and advances over fixed-structure memory models.  
- Major concern: training requires ground-truth graph states at every timestep, which is impractical for complex tasks where graph evolution is unknown.  
- Presentation issue: paper is overly dense and hard to follow; better suited for a journal with clearer exposition than a conference.
