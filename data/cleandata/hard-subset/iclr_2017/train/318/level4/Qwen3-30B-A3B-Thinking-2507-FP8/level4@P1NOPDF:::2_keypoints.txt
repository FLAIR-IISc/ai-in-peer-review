- Introduces on-the-fly learning to represent dialogues as graphs used as memory, evaluated on bAbI tasks.
- Graph structure is learned during inference; long-term learning optimizes graph transformation parameters and sentence encodings.
- First implementation of a graph-based differentiable memory for dialogue representation.
- Graph-based memory offers greater expressive power than simpler structures like stacks.
- Complexity of the proposed method seems disproportionate to the marginal performance gains on bAbI versus Memory Networks.
- Preliminary results do not demonstrate clear superiority in learning or generalization over existing alternatives.
- Performance on bAbI is comparable to state-of-the-art Memory Networks and underperforms traditional rule-based methods.
- Original draft suffered major clarity issues, but substantial revisions made the paper much more readable and coherent.
- Work is technically sound, conceptually novel, and thought-provoking; core idea merits publication.
- Authors should clarify practical benefits of graph expressiveness and validate on larger, more challenging datasets.
