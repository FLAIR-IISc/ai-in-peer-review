- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) to construct and modify graph-structured internal representations from text.  
- Proposes five differentiable graph transformations: node addition, node state update, edge update, propagation, and aggregation.  
- Demonstrates high performance on bAbI tasks (95% on 19/20) and rule discovery for cellular automata and Turing machines.  
- Main decision: Reject.  
- Strength: Addresses an important problem and is well-motivated, building on prior GNN and sequence-to-sequence work.  
- Major concern: Claims of learning graph construction/modification are undermined by heavy reliance on strong supervision (correct graph provided at each timestep).  
- Empirical weakness: No experiments showing performance when graph structure is not provided during training.  
- Baseline comparison lacking: No direct comparison to GGS-NN (the claimed predecessor) or other most relevant baselines.  
- Theoretical weakness: Proposed differentiable graph transformations lack justification or analysis of necessity/sufficiency and failure modes.  
- Recommendation: Provide results on tasks without graph supervision to show learned graph construction from text.  
- Recommendation: Include a detailed comparison with GGS-NN to quantify benefits of graph construction capability.  
- Recommendation: Add theoretical justification for the chosen graph transformations and discuss limitations.  
- Recommendation: Simplify presentation to focus on core contribution and empirical validation rather than task-specific graph format details.  
- Key questions for authors: Provide bAbI results without per-timestep graph supervision and clarify performance when graph structure is not given during training.  
- Key questions for authors: How would the model learn graph structure for cellular automata/Turing machines without explicit graph supervision, and how does GGT-NN compare to Memory Networks/NTMs when no graph supervision is available?
