- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), extending GGS-NN to learn and modify graph structures as intermediate representations for text.
- Proposes five differentiable graph transformations: node addition, node state update, edge update, propagation, and aggregation.
- Demonstrates strong empirical results: 95% accuracy on 19/20 bAbI tasks (with direct reference) and successful rule discovery on cellular automata and Turing machine tasks.
- Strength: Bridges continuous learning with symbolic representations, allowing dynamic, differentiable graph-structured internal states.
- Strength: Offers interpretability via labeled graph states that can be visualized and used downstream.
- Decision: Reject.
- Primary rejection reason: Training requires full graph supervision at each timestep, limiting applicability to tasks where ground-truth graphs are available.
- Secondary rejection reason: Paper is dense and technically deep, better suited for a journal than a conference presentation.
- Supporting critique: Reliance on full graph supervision prevents learning graph construction from scratch, reducing real-world utility.
- Supporting critique: Related-work comparison is superficial and lacks discussion of trade-offs (interpretability vs. computational efficiency).
- Additional feedback: Recommend exploring semi- or weakly-supervised approaches to reduce dependence on full supervision.
- Additional feedback: Model has quadratic time complexity in graph size; scalability experiments and a deeper complexity analysis are needed.
- Additional feedback: Appendix description of bAbI graph format is useful but derivation and task modifications (e.g., history nodes) need clearer justification.
- Questions for authors: How would training be modified to reduce reliance on full graph supervision, and have partial-supervision experiments been tried?
- Questions for authors: Provide detailed analysis and empirical scaling of the model’s quadratic complexity with graph size.
- Questions for authors: How would the model handle more complex rule-discovery tasks without predefined graph structures, and what adaptations would be required?
