1. Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), extending GGS-NNs to learn and modify graph-structured intermediate representations from text via a differentiable framework (node addition, state/edge update, propagation, aggregation) and applies it to text answers and rule learning.

2. Recommends acceptance, praising the paper as a significant, flexible contribution with strong empirical validation.

3. Empirical results: >95% accuracy on 19/20 bAbI tasks (notably strong on pathfinding) and high accuracy on cellular automata and Turing machine rule-discovery; graph-structured representations give a clear advantage for graphical tasks.

4. Requests more detailed analysis of computational complexity and practical limitations/optimizations for larger graphs beyond the paper's mention of quadratic scaling.

5. Seeks clarification on performance with more complex graph structures (more node types/edges) and how the model handles dynamic graph changes beyond the described transformations.
