- Extends GGNN by introducing differentiable graph transformations to dynamically build/modify internal graph structures.
- Proposes five learnable, differentiable graph transformations trained supervisedly with ground-truth graph states at each timestep.
- Provides a concrete instantiation that iteratively updates an internal graph while processing input sequences to produce final predictions.
- Demonstrates promising results on QA tasks (e.g., bAbI).
- Core innovation: maintains a complex, evolving graph state while preserving differentiability for gradient-based optimization.
- Bridges continuous and symbolic representations and offers a flexible alternative to fixed-state models (Memory Networks, NTMs).
- Strength: dynamic adaptation of structural representations is compelling.
- Major concern: training requires explicit ground-truth graph states at every timestep, feasible only for highly structured tasks.
- This training dependency severely limits generalizability to broader, real-world applications where graph states are unobservable.
- Paper is dense and detailed—better suited to a journal than a conference—and impairs readability for quick comprehension.
- Recommendation: address feasibility of training without pre-specified graph states and streamline the manuscript for a conference audience.
