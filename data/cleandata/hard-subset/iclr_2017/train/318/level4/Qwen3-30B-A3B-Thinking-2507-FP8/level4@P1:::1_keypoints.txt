- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) with differentiable graph transformations (node addition/update, edge update, propagation, aggregation) to build/modify graphs from text.  
- Extends prior GNNs by enabling dynamic, graph-structured intermediate representations instead of requiring fixed input graphs.  
- Strong empirical results: 95%+ accuracy on 19/20 bAbI tasks, successful learning of Rule 30 cellular automaton and Turing machines with good generalization.  
- Decision: Accept — significant, well-motivated contribution with solid experimental support.  
- Clearly identifies the limitation of fixed-graph GNNs and proposes a principled solution via differentiable graph transformations.  
- Paper is well-situated in literature with clear explanation of how GGT-NN extends GG-NN/GGS-NN.  
- Experimental design is sound with appropriate baselines and thorough result analysis.  
- Correction: draft review’s claim that Rule 30 was “far from solved” is incorrect — the paper reports near-perfect performance on Rule 30 and Turing tasks.  
- Suggestion: analyze why direct reference substantially improves performance (e.g., visualize which nodes receive updates).  
- Suggestion: provide a more thorough time and space complexity analysis for larger graphs and task sizes.  
- Suggestion: discuss trade-offs between the strong per-timestep supervision used and end-to-end learning, and ways to reduce supervision.  
- Question for authors: clarify whether direct reference improves primarily node selection or overall graph construction.  
- Question for authors: how might required per-timestep graph supervision be reduced while maintaining performance?  
- Question for authors: provide quantitative time/space complexity comparisons against memory networks/DNCs on bAbI.  
- Overall conclusion: strong, meaningful paper that warrants acceptance.
