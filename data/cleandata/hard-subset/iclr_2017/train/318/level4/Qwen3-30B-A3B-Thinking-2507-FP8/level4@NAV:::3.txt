

The paper introduces an extension to the Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network by enabling the model to generate complex graph transformations as part of its internal representation. This approach allows the model to dynamically construct or modify graph structures during problem-solving, specifically targeting question-answering tasks. The authors propose five distinct differentiable transformations, learned via supervised training where the graph state is provided at each timestep. A specific instantiation processes sequential inputs, iteratively updating an internal graph state to produce predictions, demonstrating effective application to QA benchmarks like BaBi.  

The methodology is notably compelling, as it maintains a complex graph-based state representation while preserving differentiability—enabling efficient gradient-based learning. It represents a successful integration of continuous and symbolic reasoning. Furthermore, it surpasses recent symbolic-integration efforts (e.g., memory networks, NTMs) by permitting the state’s structural evolution, unlike fixed-shape architectures. However, two concerns arise: first, the training paradigm relies on providing ground-truth graph states at every timestep, which is feasible only for specific tasks (e.g., BaBi) but not scalable to broader problems. Second, the paper’s content and density appear better suited for a journal venue than a conference, potentially hindering accessibility for the intended audience.
