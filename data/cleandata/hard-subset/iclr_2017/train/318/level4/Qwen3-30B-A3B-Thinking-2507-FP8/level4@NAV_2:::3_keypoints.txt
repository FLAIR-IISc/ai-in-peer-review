- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that learns to construct and modify internal graph structures from text.  
- Proposes five differentiable graph transformations: node addition, node state update, edge update, propagation, and aggregation.  
- Demonstrates GGT-NN on bAbI, cellular automata, and Turing machines with strong empirical results.  
- Reports bAbI performance: 95% accuracy on 19/20 tasks and 100% on 11 tasks.  
- Shows clear advantage on pathfinding (task 19) where graphical representation is natural.  
- Represents a meaningful advancement over existing GNNs by enabling dynamic graph construction instead of fixed graphs.  
- Provides a differentiable blend of symbolic and continuous representations, addressing limits of memory networks and NTMs.  
- Offers thorough comparisons showing the graph-based approach outperforms alternative representations on specific tasks.  
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Requests more discussion on computational complexity, notably quadratic scaling with graph size and scalability limits.  
- Asks for elaboration on potential applications and concrete real-world adaptations.  
- Raises concern: How does the model handle graphs exceeding the maximum node count used in training, and implications for dynamic-size tasks?  
- Raises concern: What limitations arise from requiring full graph supervision at each timestep, and how can this be relaxed for tasks without such supervision?  
- Raises concern: Need more details on generalization to substantially different tasks, e.g., complex real-world knowledge graphs.
