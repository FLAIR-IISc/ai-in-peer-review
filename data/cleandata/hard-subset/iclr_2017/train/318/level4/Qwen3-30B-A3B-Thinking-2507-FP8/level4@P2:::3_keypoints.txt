- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), which learns to construct and modify graph-structured internal representations.  
- Proposes five differentiable graph transformations: node addition, node state update, edge update, propagation, and aggregation.  
- Applies GGT-NN to bAbI question-answering and discovering rules for cellular automata and Turing machines.  
- Achieves strong bAbI results: 95% accuracy on 19/20 tasks and 100% on 11 tasks with direct reference; notably strong on pathfinding (task 19).  
- Successfully learns underlying rules for cellular automata and Turing machines with good generalization to longer sequences.  
- Decision: Accept — significant contribution enabling dynamic graph-structured internal states.  
- Strength: Maintains an interpretable, evolving graph internal state that aligns naturally with structured tasks.  
- Strength: Thorough comparison to prior work (GNNs, GGS-NNs, Memory Networks, DNCs) highlighting advantages.  
- Limitation: Training currently requires full graph supervision at each timestep, limiting real-world applicability.  
- Suggestion: Discuss or experiment with weaker supervision methods (self-supervision or using model predictions).  
- Suggestion: Reduce paper density for conference format; move detailed math to appendix and add intuitive examples of graph evolution.  
- Suggestion: Clarify how specific graph transformations are used per task (e.g., node addition in bAbI vs cellular automata).  
- Question: Why does direct reference improve performance on 14 tasks, and how does it affect learning entity relationships?  
- Question: What architectural/training modifications enabled generalization to longer sequences, and how do they scale to larger graphs?  
- Question: Given quadratic time/space scaling, what are theoretical/practical bounds on graph size and implications for real-world use?
