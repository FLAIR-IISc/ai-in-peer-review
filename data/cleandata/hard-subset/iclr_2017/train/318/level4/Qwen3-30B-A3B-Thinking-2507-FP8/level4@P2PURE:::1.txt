

The primary contribution of this paper is the introduction of differential graph transformations, enabling graph-to-graph classification tasks to be learned via gradient descent. This framework naturally models the learning of cellular automata as sequences of evolving graphs, where the graph state iteratively expands, with nodes representing binary values (0/1) and adjacency defining neighborhood relationships. While the proposed architecture facilitates learning such graph sequences, the Rule 30 task—used as an experimental benchmark—remained challenging and was not fully solved. The approach integrates concepts from prior work (GGS-NN) to generate textual outputs through graphs as an intermediate representation, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the bAbI tasks.
