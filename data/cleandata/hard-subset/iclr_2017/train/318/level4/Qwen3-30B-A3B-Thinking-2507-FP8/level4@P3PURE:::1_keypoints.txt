- Introduces differential graph transformations enabling end-to-end learning of graph-to-graph classification via gradient descent.  
- Frames tasks as learning a cellular automaton represented by a sequence of growing graphs with neighbor connections and special 0/1-labeled nodes.  
- Proposes an architecture to learn these graph sequences.  
- Experimental limitation: fails to solve the Rule 30 cellular automaton task.  
- Builds on prior GGS-NN ideas to produce text via graphs and achieves state-of-the-art results on BaBi tasks.
