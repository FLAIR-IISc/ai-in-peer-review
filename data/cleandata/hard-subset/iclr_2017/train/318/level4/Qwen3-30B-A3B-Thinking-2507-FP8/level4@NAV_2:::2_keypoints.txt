2. Recommends acceptance based on novelty, differentiable graph representation, competitive benchmarks, and improved interpretability.

4. Suggests clarifying relationships among graph transformation operations (e.g., with schematics) and expanding analysis of computational trade-offs, training time, memory, and direct comparison to GGS-NN.

5. Requests more detail on handling dramatic graph-structure changes between timesteps, scalability and computational limits for complex graphs, and how the direct reference mechanism aids relational reasoning.
