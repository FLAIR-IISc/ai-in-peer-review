

The main contribution of this paper is the introduction of a set of differential graph transformations that enable learning graph-to-graph classification tasks via gradient descent. This approach naturally maps to learning a cellular automaton as a sequence of graphs, where the graph of nodes grows iteratively, with nodes connected to neighbors and special nodes labeled 0 or 1 representing values. The proposed architecture enables learning this sequence of graphs; however, in the experiments, the Rule 30 cellular automaton task remains unsolved. The method integrates ideas from prior work (GGS-NN) to produce textual output via graphs as an intermediate representation, achieving state-of-the-art performance on BaBi tasks.
