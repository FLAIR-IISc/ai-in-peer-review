Paper introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) to learn and modify graph-structured intermediate representations from text.  
Decision: Reject.  
Strong empirical results on bAbI (95%+ on 19/20 tasks) but insufficient evidence for claimed rule-discovery capabilities.  
Poor performance on Rule 30 cellular automaton (76.1% test accuracy) undermines the claim of learning complex graphical rules.  
Unclear how much performance gains come from learned graph structure versus other model components.  
Comparison to MemNN is potentially unfair because of differing supervision levels.  
Insufficient theoretical justification for the chosen differentiable graph transformations.  
Related-work discussion is superficial and does not clearly distinguish GGT-NN from existing graph neural network approaches.  
Recommendation: investigate and honestly report reasons for Rule 30 failures or improve results (more data, hyperparameters, architecture).  
Recommendation: run controlled ablations to isolate the contribution of the graph structure (e.g., fixed-graph vs learned-graph).  
Recommendation: provide theoretical motivation and clearer explanation for the specific transformation formulations.  
Recommendation: strengthen positioning and detailed comparisons to prior graph representation learning work.  
Questions for authors: explain specific challenges on Rule 30 and how to address them; compare learned graph rules to true system rules and whether they can be extracted/verified; provide evidence that graph construction is truly sophisticated rather than memorization; clarify why direct reference improves graph construction and its relation to graph structure.
