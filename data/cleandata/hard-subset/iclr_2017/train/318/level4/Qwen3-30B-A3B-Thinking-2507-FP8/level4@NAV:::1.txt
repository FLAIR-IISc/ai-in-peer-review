

The primary contribution of this paper is the introduction of differential graph transformations enabling gradient-based learning for graph-to-graph classification tasks. This framework naturally models the learning of cellular automata as sequences of evolving graphs, where node sets expand iteratively with adjacency relationships and binary-valued nodes (0/1) encoding state. While the proposed architecture successfully learns such graph sequences, the specific Rule 30 task remains challenging in the experiments. The approach extends prior work (GGS-NN) by utilizing graphs as intermediate representations to generate textual outputs, thereby outperforming state-of-the-art methods on the bAbI question-answering tasks.
