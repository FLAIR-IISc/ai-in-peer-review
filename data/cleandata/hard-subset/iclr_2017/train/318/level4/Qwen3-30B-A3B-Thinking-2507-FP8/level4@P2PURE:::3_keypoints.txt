- Proposes extending GGNNs to generate and modify internal graph structures during computation for problem solving, focused on question-answering.
- Introduces five differentiable graph transformations learned in a supervised manner using provided graph states at each timestep.
- Presents an instantiation that iteratively updates an internal graph state to produce final predictions and shows promising results on QA benchmarks like bAbI.
- Successfully maintains a complex, dynamically evolving differentiable graph representation, blending continuous and symbolic computation.
- Represents a notable advancement over prior differentiable-symbolic efforts (e.g., Memory Networks, NTMs) because the state shape can change during processing.
- Major concern: training requires per-timestep graph state supervision, which is infeasible for most complex tasks beyond datasets like bAbI.
- Paper is technically dense and long, reducing readability and suggesting it may be more appropriate for a journal than a conference venue.
