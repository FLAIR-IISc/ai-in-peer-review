- Proposes an extension to Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks to enable complex graph transformations.  
- Core idea: build and modify an internal graph structure as a problem-solving representation, applied here to question answering.  
- Introduces five differentiable transformations that can be learned from data, typically supervised with graph state at each timestep.  
- Presents a model that iteratively updates an internal graph from sequence input to produce final predictions, with promising results on QA tasks (e.g., BaBi).  
- Strength: keeps an evolving, complex graph state while remaining fully differentiable, bridging continuous and symbolic representations.  
- Claimed generality: more flexible than prior symbolic-differentiable models (Memory Networks, NTM) because the state’s structure can change dynamically.  
- Main concern: training requires providing the graph state at every timestep, which is unlikely to scale to more complex, realistic tasks.  
- Secondary concern: paper is dense and possibly better suited for a journal than a conference due to readability and scope.
