This paper introduces a novel approach to dialog representation through on-the-fly graph learning, utilizing the graph structure as a differentiable memory. The method is initially evaluated on the bAbI tasks, with graph learning integrated into the inference process alongside long-term learning of graph transformation parameters and sentence encodings. To the best of my knowledge, this work represents the first implementation of a differentiable memory based on a graph structure; while significantly more complex than previous memory network approaches, the performance gains on bAbI tasks are currently modest. Nevertheless, this is a promising and preliminary exploration, and the graph-based memory representation holds substantial potential beyond simpler stack-based alternatives. The paper underwent substantial improvement in clarity between versions, evolving from a computationally readable form to a much more accessible presentation. Overall, this technically sound and conceptually innovative work warrants publication.

However, the current results do not yet demonstrate a clear advantage in learning or generalization capacity for this complex graph-based memory compared to existing methods.
