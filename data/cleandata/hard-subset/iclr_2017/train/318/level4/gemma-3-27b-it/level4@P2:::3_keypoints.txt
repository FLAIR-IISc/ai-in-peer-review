- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), extending GGS-NNs with differentiable graph transformations to learn and manipulate internal graph representations.  
- Proposes five differentiable graph transformations: node addition, state update, edge update, propagation, and aggregation.  
- GGT-NN iteratively constructs and modifies a graph from textual input to produce final predictions.  
- Demonstrates strong results on bAbI tasks and the ability to learn rules for cellular automata and Turing machines.  
- Reviewer recommendation: Reject—paper lacks sufficient justification for conference presentation.  
- Primary concern: heavy reliance on strong supervision (ground-truth graph at each timestep) limits real-world applicability.  
- Paper is long and dense, more like a journal article, making core contributions hard to quickly grasp.  
- Motivation and positioning within GNN and memory-augmented literature are solid; end-to-end differentiable transformations are a clear advantage.  
- Results are presented clearly with helpful comparisons to existing methods.  
- Authors acknowledge supervision limitation but offer no compelling path to address it; proposed solutions are speculative.  
- Suggested improvement: investigate methods to reduce reliance on ground-truth graph states (alternative objectives, regularization, curriculum learning).  
- Suggested improvement: simplify presentation and move extensive background/technical detail to the appendix.  
- Suggested improvement: add ablation studies to quantify each transformation’s contribution and the impact of direct reference.  
- Suggested improvement: expand empirical evaluation to more challenging, less-structured tasks with limited supervision.  
- Questions for authors: how sensitive is performance to the accuracy of the initial graph structure in bAbI?  
- Questions: what concrete strategies beyond brief mentions have been tried to mitigate the supervision requirement?  
- Questions: provide a detailed analysis of computational cost as graph size increases.  
- Questions: what are the limitations of using GRU cells for transformation outputs and would other alternatives help?
