- Paper proposes extending Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network with five differentiable graph transformations to build and modify internal graph structures for QA tasks.  
- Model iteratively updates an internal graph state from sequential input and achieves promising results on tasks like bAbI.  
- Training requires full graph state supervision at each timestep (supervised graph evolution).  
- Recommendation: reject, due to reliance on full supervision and overly extensive presentation for a conference.  
- Strength: enables representing and manipulating complex, dynamic state via differentiable graphs—more flexible than fixed-size memory methods.  
- Contribution: the set of proposed differentiable graph transformations is a solid technical contribution.  
- Major limitation: requirement of complete graph state supervision limits applicability beyond datasets with explicit supervision (e.g., bAbI).  
- Paper is overly dense and detailed, resembling a journal article; background material detracts from core contributions.  
- Suggested improvement: discuss/experiment with training under partial or no graph supervision (e.g., RL or unsupervised structure learning).  
- Suggested improvement: streamline presentation and move detailed transformation descriptions to the appendix.  
- Suggested improvement: evaluate generalization on more diverse tasks that require learning graph structure from unstructured input.  
- Suggested improvement: perform ablation studies to measure each transformation’s contribution and the effect of direct reference.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity of performance to errors in graph-state supervision during training?  
- Questions for authors: computational costs and scaling of the graph transformations with graph size?  
- Questions for authors: details and rationale for implementation choices of the transformation functions (architectures of fset, f_fwd_y, etc.)?  
- Questions for authors: potential benefits of using sparse edge connections to improve scalability?
