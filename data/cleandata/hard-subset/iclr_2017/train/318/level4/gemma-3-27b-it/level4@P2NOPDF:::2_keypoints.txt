- Proposes a novel dialog representation: a dynamically constructed graph used as a differentiable memory.  
- Model learns to build the graph on the fly and optimizes graph-transformation and sentence-encoding parameters via long-term representation learning.  
- Claimed to be the first implementation of a differentiable graph-based memory, a more complex architecture than prior memory networks.  
- Empirical gains on bAbI tasks are modest—comparable to strong memory-network baselines and worse than traditional rule induction methods.  
- Conceptually significant and promising despite limited empirical improvements.  
- Initial submission had clarity problems; the revised paper is substantially clearer.  
- Graph memory potentially offers greater representational power and flexibility to capture complex dialog relationships.  
- Current experiments do not conclusively demonstrate that added complexity improves learning or generalization.  
- Paper appears technically sound and the architectural complexity is justified by potential benefits.  
- Authors were responsive to feedback; paper merits publication but requires further investigation on harder dialog tasks and comparative advantages.
