- Main contribution: introduces differentiable graph transformations enabling gradient-based learning for graph-to-graph classification and graph sequence modeling.  
- Maps naturally to learning cellular automata: graphs evolve each iteration with nodes pointing to neighbors and designated 0/1 nodes as cell values.  
- Proposed architecture can learn graph sequences, but the Rule 30 experiment shows the task is still challenging and not fully solved.  
- Combines with GGS-NN ideas to produce text from graphs as intermediate representation, achieving state-of-the-art on BaBi tasks.  
- Novel and insightful bridge between discrete graph manipulation and continuous optimization, with the cellular automata connection as strong motivation.  
- Rule 30 results indicate possible limitations in transformation expressiveness or training procedure—more refinement needed.  
- Reviewer requests deeper analysis of learned transformations and identification of performance bottlenecks.  
- Suggests evaluating scaling (e.g., increasing hops in BaBi) and comparing to more recent QA architectures to strengthen empirical claims.  
- Paper is well-written and clear, but notation should be made more consistent and symbols explicitly defined.  
- Recommends adding discussion of computational cost of transformations, particularly for large graphs.  
- Overall verdict: promising work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points above.
