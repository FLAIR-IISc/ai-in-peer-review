The paper proposes an extension to the Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network, augmenting it with the capability to perform complex, differentiable graph transformations. The core idea is to enable the model to build and modify a graph structure as an internal representation for problem solving, specifically focusing on question-answering tasks in this work. The authors propose five distinct differentiable transformations, learned in a supervised manner from training data, where the graph’s state is typically provided at each timestep. A specific instantiation of the model iteratively updates an internal graph state from a sequence input to reach a final prediction, demonstrating promising results on QA tasks such as BaBi.

This approach is compelling because the proposed model maintains a representation of its current state as a complex graph while remaining differentiable, and therefore readily trainable via gradient-descent. It represents a successful attempt to integrate continuous and symbolic representations. Furthermore, it appears more general than recent efforts to incorporate ‘symbolic’ components into differentiable models (e.g., Memory Networks, NTMs), as the shape of the state is not fixed and can evolve dynamically. My primary concern lies with the training methodology – requiring the graph’s state at each timestep – which limits applicability to tasks like BaBi and may not generalize to more complex problems. A secondary concern is the paper’s scope and density; the content might be better suited for a journal publication than a conference format, currently making the article challenging to follow.
