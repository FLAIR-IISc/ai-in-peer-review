The primary contribution of this work is a novel set of differentiable graph transformations enabling gradient-based learning for graph-to-graph classification. This approach establishes a direct correspondence to learning a cellular automaton, effectively represented as a sequential evolution of graphs where nodes expand with neighbor connections and designated nodes (0/1) indicate state values. While the proposed architecture facilitates learning such graph sequences, experimental results on Rule 30 demonstrate the task remains challenging.

Furthermore, the authors integrate concepts from prior work (GGS-NN) to enable textual output generation, utilizing graphs as an internal representation. This combination yields state-of-the-art performance on BaBi reasoning tasks.
