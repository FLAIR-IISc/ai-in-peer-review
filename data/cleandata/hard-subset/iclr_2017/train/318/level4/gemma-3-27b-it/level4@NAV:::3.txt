This paper introduces a compelling extension to the Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network, augmenting its capabilities with differentiable graph transformation operations. The central concept is to enable the model to dynamically construct and refine a graph structure as an internal representation for problem-solving, demonstrated here through question answering. The authors define five distinct, learnable, differentiable transformations, typically trained in a supervised manner with known graph states at each time step. A specific instantiation of this model iteratively updates an internal graph representation from sequential input to arrive at a final prediction, achieving promising results on QA tasks such as BaBi.

The proposed approach is noteworthy for its ability to maintain a complex graph-based state representation while retaining differentiability, facilitating learning via gradient descent. This work represents a successful integration of continuous and symbolic representations, and appears more flexible than recent efforts to incorporate symbolic elements into differentiable models (e.g., Memory Networks, NTMs) due to the dynamic, evolving nature of the graph structure. However, a key concern is the training methodology, which relies on providing the complete graph state at each time step – an approach limited to specific tasks like BaBi and unlikely to generalize to more complex, real-world problems. Furthermore, the paper’s depth and scope may be better suited for a journal publication than a conference setting, presently resulting in a dense and challenging read.
