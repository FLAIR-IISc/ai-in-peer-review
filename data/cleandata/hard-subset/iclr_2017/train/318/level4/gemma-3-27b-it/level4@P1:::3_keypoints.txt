- Proposal: extend Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network to perform complex, learnable graph transformations for building/modifying an internal graph representation for problem-solving (QA).
- Method: introduces five differentiable transformations learned during training, typically supervised with the graph state provided at each timestep.
- Model: iteratively updates an internal graph state from input sequences to produce final predictions; shows promising results on bAbI QA tasks.
- Strength: maintains an evolving complex graph representation while remaining fully differentiable and trainable by gradient descent.
- Contribution: integrates continuous and symbolic representations and is more general than fixed-shape symbolic augmentations (e.g., Memory Networks, NTMs) because state shape can evolve.
- Primary concern: reliance on strong supervision (graph state at each timestep) severely limits applicability beyond tasks where such supervision is available.
- Presentation concern: paper is overly long and dense, resembling a journal article and making it hard to read in a conference setting.
- Decision: Reject — technically interesting but unsuitable for ICLR due to strong supervision requirement and excessive length/density.
- Supporting argument (supervision): paper lacks convincing strategies to train without full graph-state supervision, reducing long-term impact.
- Supporting argument (length): extensive background, transformation details, and many experiments obscure the core contributions.
- Suggested improvement: streamline the paper to emphasize the core GGT-NN architecture and key bAbI results; move detailed transformations and Turing machine experiments to appendices.
- Suggested improvement: investigate weakly supervised, reinforcement learning, or self-supervised approaches to reduce reliance on full graph supervision.
- Suggested improvement: address scalability concerns (quadratic scaling) with proposed optimizations or approximations for larger graphs.
- Suggested improvement: more explicitly clarify novelty and key differences between GGT-NNs and existing graph neural network variants (GNNs, GG-NNs, GGS-NNs).
- Suggested improvement: include ablation studies to evaluate the importance of components (e.g., direct reference mechanism, individual transformations).
- Question: what strategies will you explore for training GGT-NN when the complete graph state is not available at each timestep?
- Question: can you detail the computational complexity of each transformation and the overall model, and planned optimizations for scaling?
- Question: can you provide a concise summary of the key differences between GGT-NN and prior GNN architectures in the abstract/introduction?
- Question: how sensitive are results to hyperparameters (learning rate, batch size, propagation steps) and was a thorough hyperparameter search performed?
