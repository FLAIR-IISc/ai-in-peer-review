- Proposes extending Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks with differentiable graph transformation to dynamically build/modify internal graph representations for QA.
- Introduces five supervised, differentiable graph transformation operations that let the model evolve its graph state over time.
- Model can maintain a complex, evolving graph while remaining end-to-end differentiable, blending continuous and symbolic reasoning.
- Flexibility of the graph structure is a key strength compared to fixed-structure memory-augmented models.
- Demonstrates promising performance on QA benchmarks like the bAbI dataset.
- Major concern: training requires providing the complete graph state at each timestep, which limits applicability to real-world tasks lacking such supervision.
- Paper lacks a clear solution or discussion for training without full state supervision; addressing this is crucial for broader impact.
- Presentation is dense and more suited to a journal; needs streamlining for conference readership.
- Motivation and intuitive explanation for each of the five transformations are insufficiently clear.
- Would benefit from more visual/step-by-step examples showing how the graph evolves during a QA example.
- Recommendation: weak accept contingent on addressing training-methodology concerns and significantly improving clarity and conciseness.
- Suggests adding a detailed discussion of alternative or preliminary training strategies to strengthen the paper.
