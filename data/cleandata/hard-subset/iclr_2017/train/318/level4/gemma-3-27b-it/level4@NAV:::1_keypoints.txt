- Primary contribution: a novel set of differentiable graph transformations enabling gradient-based learning for graph-to-graph classification.  
- Method establishes a direct correspondence to learning a cellular automaton via sequential graph evolution.  
- Graph evolution modeled as node expansion with neighbor connections and designated 0/1 nodes representing state values.  
- Proposed architecture can learn such graph sequences, but experiments on Rule 30 show the task remains challenging.  
- Authors integrate ideas from prior GGS-NN work to produce textual outputs using graphs as internal representations.  
- The combined approach achieves state-of-the-art performance on the bAbI reasoning tasks.
