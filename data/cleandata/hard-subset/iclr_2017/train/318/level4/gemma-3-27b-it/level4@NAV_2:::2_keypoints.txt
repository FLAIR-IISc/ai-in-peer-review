- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that uses graph-structured intermediate representations for learning and reasoning.  
- Core idea: dynamically construct and modify graphs from textual input for internal state representation and output generation.  
- Demonstrated on bAbI tasks, a cellular automaton, and Turing machines, showing ability to learn complex rules and relationships.  
- Recommendation: accept — presents a compelling and original approach to structured knowledge in neural architectures.  
- Key contribution: learning graph transformations directly from data rather than relying on predefined graphs.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-motivated, and positioned within graph neural nets, gated recurrent units, and memory networks literature.  
- Experimental results are competitive on bAbI (not clearly state-of-the-art) and show strong potential via automaton and Turing machine experiments.  
- Detailed, differentiable descriptions of graph transformations are a valuable technical contribution.  
- Suggestion: conduct a more comprehensive ablation study to assess impact of each graph transformation component.  
- Suggestion: analyze and report computational complexity and compare runtime/cost with other memory-based architectures.  
- Suggestion: expand related work discussion to include more recent advances in graph representation learning.  
- Suggestion: provide visualizations of learned graph structures for bAbI, cellular automaton, and Turing machine examples to improve intuition.  
- Question: how does GGT-NN scale with increasing graph size/complexity and what strategies mitigate computational bottlenecks?  
- Question: were additional regularization techniques explored to address potential overfitting, especially for longer sequences?  
- Question: please elaborate on activation choices and network architectures within each graph transformation and report any hyperparameter optimization findings.  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to Turing machine rule choices and initial tapes, and does the model generalize to unseen rule sets?
