- Paper proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) with differentiable graph transformations (node addition, state/edge update, propagation, aggregation) and evaluates on bAbI, a cellular automaton, and Turing machines.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main concern: Limited empirical validation—bAbI results don’t justify model complexity and no clear baselines for cellular automaton/Turing tasks; Rule 30 performance is poor.  
- Requests deeper failure analysis for Rule 30 and visualization of learned graphs to explain model shortcomings.  
- Implementation details are unclear—insufficient specifics on architectures, hyperparameters, and dense appendix with many unexplained abbreviations.  
- Unclear motivation for several design choices (e.g., ∑xv,j = 1 constraint, node strengths) and how they affect performance/differentiability.  
- Suggest running ablation studies to quantify the contribution of individual graph transformations.  
- Recommend baseline comparisons (e.g., RNN/LSTM on sequential graph representations) for automaton and Turing tasks.  
- Ask for visualizations of learned graphs to illustrate representation and manipulation.  
- Request discussion of computational cost (memory, training time) and scaling with graph size.  
- Advise simplifying notation and reducing abbreviations to improve readability.  
- Questions for authors: detailed analysis of Rule 30 failures and learned-graph deviations.  
- Questions for authors: provide computational cost measurements and scaling behavior.  
- Questions for authors: justify the ∑xv,j = 1 constraint and its impact.  
- Questions for authors: specify network architectures and hyperparameters for each graph transformation.  
- Questions for authors: explain why direct reference consistently improves performance and what benefits it provides.
