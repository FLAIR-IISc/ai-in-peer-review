Presents an extension to Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks by introducing differentiable graph transformations for dynamic graph construction and modification.

Core idea: learn an evolving internal graph representation to facilitate problem solving, demonstrated on question answering.

Proposes five distinct, learned differentiable transformations that operate on the graph state each timestep, typically trained with supervised target graph states.

Concrete instantiation iteratively updates an internal graph from sequential input and achieves promising results on QA tasks like bAbI.

Strength: maintains a complex, evolving, fully differentiable graph-based state enabling end-to-end gradient learning.

Contribution bridges continuous and symbolic representations and is more general than prior hybrids (e.g., Memory Networks, Neural Turing Machines) because the graph structure is not fixed.

Primary concern: training relies on providing the complete graph state at each timestep, limiting applicability to tasks lacking such supervision.

Scaling to scenarios without explicit intermediate graph-state supervision is a significant challenge.

Presentation/scope concern: the paper’s depth and density are more suited to a journal than a conference, making it hard to follow.

Suggestion: streamline presentation and focus on the most crucial contributions to improve accessibility.
