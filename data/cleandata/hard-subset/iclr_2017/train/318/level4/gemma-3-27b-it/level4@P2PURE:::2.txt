This paper introduces a novel approach to dialog representation learning, employing a dynamically constructed graph as a differentiable memory. The method is initially evaluated on the bAbI tasks, where graph learning occurs during inference, while long-term representation learning optimizes graph transformation parameters and sentence encodings used as graph inputs. To the best of my knowledge, this is the first implementation of a differentiable memory based on a graph structure. Although significantly more complex than previous memory network approaches, the performance gains on bAbI tasks are currently modest. Nevertheless, this work represents a promising and preliminary investigation; the graph-based memory representation appears to offer greater potential than simpler stack-based alternatives. Clarity was initially a concern, but the authors have substantially improved the manuscript through revisions, making it significantly more accessible. Overall, this technically sound and thought-provoking paper is worthy of publication. 

Currently, the preliminary results do not demonstrate a clear advantage in learning or generalization capacity for the complex graph-based differentiable memory compared to existing methods.
