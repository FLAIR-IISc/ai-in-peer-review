- Introduces differentiable graph transformations to enable gradient-based learning for graph-to-graph classification.  
- Frames the task as learning a cellular automaton: a sequence of expanding, neighbor-connected graphs with designated 0/1 nodes representing values.  
- Proposes an architecture that can learn the graph sequence, though experiments on the Rule 30 task show it remains challenging.  
- Combines this approach with ideas from GGS-NN to generate text via graphs as intermediates, achieving state-of-the-art on the bAbI tasks.
