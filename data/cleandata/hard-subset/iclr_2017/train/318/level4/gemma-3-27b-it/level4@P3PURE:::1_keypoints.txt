- Introduces differentiable graph transformations to apply gradient descent to graph-to-graph classification tasks.
- Frames cellular automata learning as sequences of evolving graphs with nodes as cells, edges as neighborhoods, and designated nodes for cell values.
- Proposes an architecture for learning graph sequences, but Rule 30 experiments show a complete solution remains elusive.
- Extends the core idea by integrating GGS-NN concepts to enable generation of textual output using graphs as intermediate representations.
- The combined approach achieves state-of-the-art performance on BaBi tasks.
