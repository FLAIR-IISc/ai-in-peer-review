- Extends Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network with differentiable graph transformation operations.  
- Enables dynamic construction and refinement of an internal graph representation for problem-solving (demonstrated on QA).  
- Defines five learnable, differentiable transformations, typically trained with supervised known graph states at each time step.  
- Implements an iterative model that updates an internal graph from sequential input to produce final predictions, showing promising results on bAbI QA tasks.  
- Strength: maintains complex graph-based state while preserving differentiability, allowing end-to-end gradient-based learning.  
- Successful integration of continuous and symbolic representations; appears more flexible than Memory Networks and NTMs due to dynamic, evolving graph structure.  
- Major concern: training requires full graph state supervision at every time step, limiting applicability to simple tasks and hindering generalization to real-world problems.  
- Presentation concern: paper is dense and may be more appropriate for a journal than a conference, making it a challenging read.
