- Proposes an extension to Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks that enables complex, differentiable graph transformations.  
- Core idea: let the model build and modify an internal graph structure for problem solving, demonstrated on question-answering.  
- Introduces five distinct differentiable transformations learned in a supervised manner, with the graph state provided at each timestep.  
- Implements an iterative model that updates an internal graph state from a sequence input to produce final predictions.  
- Shows promising results on QA benchmarks such as bAbI.  
- Strength: maintains a complex, evolving graph representation while remaining fully differentiable and trainable via gradient descent.  
- Strength: integrates continuous and symbolic representations effectively.  
- Strength: more general than prior differentiable models with symbolic components because the state shape can change dynamically.  
- Primary concern: training requires the graph’s state at every timestep, limiting applicability beyond synthetic tasks like bAbI.  
- Secondary concern: paper is dense and broad in scope, potentially better suited for a journal than a conference and currently hard to follow.
