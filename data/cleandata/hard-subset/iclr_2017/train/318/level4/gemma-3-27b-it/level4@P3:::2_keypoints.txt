- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), an extension of GGS-NNs that dynamically constructs and modifies differentiable graph structures.  
- Core idea: learn to represent and manipulate information via a differentiable graph for reasoning and knowledge representation.  
- Demonstrates capabilities on bAbI tasks, a cellular automaton, and Turing machines, showing learning of complex rules and structured reasoning.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—novel, technically sound, and worthy of publication.  
- Strength: offers a flexible alternative to fixed-memory methods by learning graph construction and transformation.  
- Differentiable graph transformations enable end-to-end training and adaptation.  
- Versatility shown across symbolic reasoning tasks and a natural language benchmark (bAbI).  
- Clear, detailed description of node/edge updates, propagation, and aggregation provides a solid foundation for future work.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations and suggest promising future research directions.  
- Clarity issue: dense notation; needs more intuitive explanations for readers less familiar with graph neural networks.  
- Comparative analysis lacking: more comprehensive comparison to memory network architectures and trade-offs (compute, scalability) needed.  
- Ablation studies are useful but further analysis required on graph transformation configurations (e.g., intermediate propagation) and graph size vs. performance.  
- Broader impact: paper should discuss potential applications beyond presented tasks.  
- Reproducibility: Turing Machine setup description should be expanded for wider reproducibility.  
- Question: quadratic scaling with graph size—can sparse connections or pruning mitigate scalability?  
- Question: sensitivity to initial graph structure—performance differences between empty vs. pre-defined graphs?  
- Question: challenges on bAbI Task 17 (Positional Reasoning) and whether alternative graph representations/transformations could help.  
- Question: role of tanh activation in GRU units and graph transformations; were other activations tested?  
- Question: extended sequence-of-graphs model performed slightly worse—were specific hyperparameters tuned to improve it?
