The primary contribution of this paper is the introduction of differentiable graph transformations, enabling the application of gradient descent to graph-to-graph classification tasks. This approach maps naturally to learning cellular automata, which can be represented as a sequence of graphs where the graph structure evolves iteratively, with nodes representing cells and edges defining neighborhood relationships, and designated nodes representing cell values (0 or 1). The proposed architecture facilitates learning such graph sequences; however, the experimental results on the Rule 30 task indicate that a complete solution remains elusive.

This core idea is further extended by integrating concepts from prior work (specifically, GGS-NN) to enable the model to generate textual output, utilizing graphs as an intermediate representation. This combination allows the model to achieve state-of-the-art performance on BaBi tasks.
