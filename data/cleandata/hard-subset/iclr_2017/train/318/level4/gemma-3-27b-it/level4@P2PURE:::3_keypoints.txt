- Introduces an extension to Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks that enables differentiable graph transformations.  
- Central idea: learn to construct and modify internal graph structures for problem solving (demonstrated on question answering).  
- Proposes five differentiable transformation operations learned in a supervised way with graph states provided at each timestep.  
- Presents an instantiation that iteratively updates an internal graph from sequential input to produce final predictions.  
- Shows promising results on question-answering benchmarks such as bAbI.  
- Strength: maintains a complex, fully differentiable graph-based state amenable to gradient-based learning.  
- Strength: successful integration of continuous and symbolic representations with dynamically evolving state structure, more general than fixed-structure approaches (e.g., Memory Networks, NTMs).  
- Major concern: training requires supervision of graph state at every timestep, limiting applicability beyond tasks like bAbI.  
- Presentation concern: paper is dense and broad in scope, perhaps better suited for a journal than a conference; currently hard to follow.
