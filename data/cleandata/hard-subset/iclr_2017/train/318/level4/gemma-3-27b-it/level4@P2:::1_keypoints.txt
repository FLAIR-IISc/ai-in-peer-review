- Paper introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) enabling differentiable graph transformations for graph-to-graph mappings and intermediate graph representations.  
- Core contributions: node addition, state update, edge update, propagation, and aggregation as differentiable operations for learning graph-structured data.  
- Builds on GGS-NNs and relates appropriately to Memory Networks and DNCs.  
- Strong performance on bAbI tasks but relies on strong supervision (correct graph at each timestep), limiting claimed generality.  
- Experimental results on the Rule 30 cellular automaton are weak and described as “far from solved,” undermining claims about learning dynamical systems.  
- Turing machine graphical representation is described too briefly to assess effectiveness.  
- Comparisons to baselines on bAbI are reasonable and help assess effectiveness.  
- Paper needs a more thorough ablation study to isolate contributions (e.g., direct reference, propagation).  
- Request for more detailed cellular automaton experiment reporting: metrics, architectures, hyperparameter sweeps.  
- Suggest adding a visual example and more detail for the Turing machine graph encoding.  
- Discuss implications of strong supervision and report performance under reduced supervision.  
- Address scalability concerns from quadratic complexity of graph transformations and propose mitigation strategies.  
- Clarify definitions and architectures for fset and freset in Appendix B.3, including activation functions and network structure.  
- Questions for authors: effects of architecture/hyperparameters on Rule 30, detailed Turing machine encoding, strategies to reduce transformation complexity, and sensitivity to supervision levels.
