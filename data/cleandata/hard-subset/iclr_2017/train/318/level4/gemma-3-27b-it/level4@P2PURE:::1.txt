The paper’s primary contribution is the introduction of differentiable graph transformations, enabling gradient-based learning for graph-to-graph classification tasks. This approach naturally frames the problem as learning a cellular automaton, which can be represented as a sequence of graphs where nodes expand with each iteration, connected to neighbors, and utilizing designated nodes (0/1) to represent values. The proposed architecture facilitates learning this graph sequence, although the experimental results on the Rule 30 task indicate it remains challenging. 

This concept is further combined with ideas from prior work (GGS-NN) to generate textual output via graphs as an intermediate representation, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the BaBi tasks.
