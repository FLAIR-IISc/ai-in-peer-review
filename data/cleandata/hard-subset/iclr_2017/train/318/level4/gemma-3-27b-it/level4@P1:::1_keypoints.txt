- Main contribution: introduces differentiable graph transformations to learn graph-to-graph mappings with gradient descent.  
- Application example: applied to modeling cellular automata (e.g., Rule 30) as sequences of graphs with node values.  
- Extension: combines with GGS-NN ideas to produce textual output via graphs and attains competitive bAbI performance.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Primary reason: insufficient empirical evidence of clear advantages over existing methods given the approach’s complexity.  
- bAbI results: competitive but not consistently superior; often marginal gains and sometimes outperformed by MemNN.  
- Rule discovery results: cellular automaton and Turing machine experiments are weak and not convincingly mastered.  
- Lack of ablation: no thorough ablation study to show importance of individual components (e.g., direct reference, specific transformations).  
- Complexity: architecture has many components and hyperparameters, making behavior and debugging difficult.  
- Experimental clarity: needs more details on hyperparameters, optimization, and training procedures.  
- Baselines: should compare against stronger, more recent baselines on bAbI.  
- Analysis: provide detailed examples/visualizations of learned graphs and their temporal evolution for rule discovery tasks.  
- Generalization: include quantitative evaluation of generalization to longer sequences in rule tasks.  
- Computational cost: discuss runtime and scaling with graph size and task complexity, and compare to alternatives.  
- Simplification: consider whether some graph transformations can be removed without significant performance loss.  
- Questions for authors: explain low accuracy on bAbI task 17, sensitivity to hyperparameters, scaling of computational cost, show learned graph examples, and clarify contributions of each graph transformation.
