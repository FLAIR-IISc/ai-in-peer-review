- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) using differentiable graph transformations (node addition, state update, edge update, propagation, aggregation).  
- Shows strong performance on most bAbI tasks and partial success discovering rules for a cellular automaton and Turing machines.  
- Recommendation: Reject.  
- Major weakness: experimental validation lacks depth and clarity, especially for rule discovery experiments.  
- Cellular automaton (Rule 30) results appear limited and not clearly quantified for multi-step prediction.  
- Turing machine experiment lacks crucial details on complexity and scale of learned machines; performance needs further scrutiny.  
- bAbI success may depend heavily on strong supervision from provided graph structures.  
- No comparative analysis versus existing rule-learning methods, reducing impact.  
- Ablation studies (direct reference and intermediate propagation) are insufficiently comprehensive.  
- Suggestion: provide detailed quantitative evaluation for CA and Turing machine tasks, including multi-timestep prediction and analysis of learned TM complexity.  
- Suggestion: include comparative analysis against established rule-learning algorithms.  
- Suggestion: perform systematic ablation studies to evaluate each graph transformation and the direct reference mechanism.  
- Suggestion: clarify role of initial node strengths and the effect of the normalization constraint (∑ x_v,j = 1).  
- Suggestion: discuss computational complexity and strategies for scaling to larger graphs.  
- Clarifying question: what is the maximum number of states and symbols the network can reliably learn for Turing machines?  
- Clarifying question: how sensitive is the model to initial node strengths?  
- Clarifying question: provide implementation details of fset and freset in the edge update transformation.  
- Clarifying question: is the normalization constraint on node annotations essential for stable learning?  
- Clarifying question: what are the current GGT-NN limitations and potential future improvement directions?
