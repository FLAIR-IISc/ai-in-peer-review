This paper introduces an extension to the Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network, augmenting it with the capability to perform differentiable graph transformations. The central idea is to learn a method that constructs and modifies graph structures as an internal representation for problem solving, demonstrated here through question answering. The authors propose five differentiable transformation operations, learned in a supervised manner with graph states provided at each timestep. A specific instantiation of this model iteratively updates an internal graph state from a sequential input to arrive at a final prediction, achieving promising results on question answering tasks like BaBi.

The approach is compelling, as the model maintains a complex graph-based representation of its state while remaining fully differentiable and thus amenable to gradient-based learning. This can be viewed as a successful integration of continuous and symbolic representations, and appears more general than recent efforts to incorporate 'symbolic' elements into differentiable models (e.g., Memory Networks, NTMs), as the state’s structure is not fixed and can evolve dynamically. However, a significant concern is the training paradigm – requiring the state of the graph at each timestep – which limits applicability to specific tasks like BaBi and may not generalize to more complex problems. Furthermore, the paper’s scope and density might be better suited to a journal publication than a conference format, currently making it challenging to follow.
