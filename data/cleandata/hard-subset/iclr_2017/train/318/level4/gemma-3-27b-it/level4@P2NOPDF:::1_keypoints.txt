- Proposes a novel differentiable graph transformation approach enabling gradient-based learning for sequential graph modifications.
- Frames the method as learning a cellular automaton with iterative graph evolution (nodes as states, edges as neighborhoods).
- Architecture supports learning sequences of graph transformations but struggles to fully automate complex dynamics.
- Rule 30 experiment shows the framework's potential but reveals a substantial performance gap and training difficulty.
- Paper lacks detailed analysis on why the model fails on Rule 30 (expressiveness vs. optimization vs. architectural limits).
- Effectively integrates with GGS-NNs to map graph computations to textual outputs.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on several bAbI reasoning tasks, demonstrating practical utility.
- Needs a more thorough comparison and ablation study to isolate the impact of differentiable transformations versus GGS-NN components.
- Missing discussion of computational costs and scalability of the differentiable graph transformations.
- Overall promising and innovative work that merits acceptance if limitations (Rule 30 analysis, comparisons, cost) are addressed.
