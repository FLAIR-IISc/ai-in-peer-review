- Introduces differentiable graph transformations for graph-to-graph learning, framed as learning cellular automata.  
- Enables gradient-based learning for sequential graph manipulation by representing cellular automaton evolution as graph sequences.  
- Uses a learned transformation to iteratively evolve graph structure and learn automaton rules.  
- Integrates GGS-NN ideas to generate textual output via graphs, achieving state-of-the-art results on BaBi tasks.  
- Motivates approach by addressing discrete-operation limitations and enabling end-to-end differentiable graph modification.  
- Cellular automaton analogy provides an intuitive, visual demonstration of the method’s capabilities.  
- Experimental result: model learns Rule 30 only approximately and fails to perfectly reproduce its dynamics.  
- Imperfect Rule 30 results raise concerns about the expressiveness and capacity of the graph transformation mechanism.  
- Strong BaBi performance suggests the graph-based intermediate representation effectively captures logical structure.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly explains core concepts.  
- Lacks sufficient implementation detail on how node and edge attributes are updated and what constraints ensure stable graph evolution.  
- Hyperparameter settings for Rule 30 and BaBi experiments are under-documented, hindering reproducibility.  
- Overall recommendation: promising and novel work, accept with minor revisions to address implementation and analysis gaps.  
- Requests for authors: provide implementation details, complexity/scaling analysis, discussion of limitations and extensions, and a more comprehensive BaBi ablation study.
