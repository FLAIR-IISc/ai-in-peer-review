- Introduces differential graph transformations to enable gradient-based learning for graph-to-graph classification.  
- Conceptually frames the approach as learning a cellular automaton: a sequence of expanding, interconnected graphs with special nodes encoding values.  
- Proposed architecture can learn the graph sequence, but experiments on the Rule 30 task did not achieve a full solution.  
- Integrates elements from prior work (GGS-NN) to enable generation of textual output using graphs as intermediate representations.  
- This integration yields state-of-the-art performance on BaBi tasks.
