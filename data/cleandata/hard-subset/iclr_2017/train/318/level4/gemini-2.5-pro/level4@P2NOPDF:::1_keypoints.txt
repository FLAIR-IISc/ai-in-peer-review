- Introduces differentiable graph transformations enabling end-to-end learning of graph-to-graph mappings with gradient descent.  
- Framework is suited for modeling dynamic systems as sequences of graphs.  
- Demonstrated on a cellular automaton (Rule 30) by modeling evolution as sequential graph growth, neighbor connections, and special nodes for binary states.  
- Experimental results on Rule 30 were limited—the model did not fully learn the target dynamics.  
- Combines the transformations with Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network ideas to produce textual output from dynamic graphs.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on several bAbI QA tasks, showing practical value of using graphs as internal reasoning states.
