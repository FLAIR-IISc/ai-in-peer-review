- Proposes representing dialog as a dynamically constructed graph at inference that serves as the system's memory.  
- Demonstrated approach on the bAbI tasks; graph structure is determined at inference while transformation/encoding parameters are learned during training.  
- Claims to present the first differentiable, graph-based memory implementation.  
- Model is considerably more complex than existing approaches like memory networks.  
- Does not show a significant performance gain on bAbI; performance is comparable to state-of-the-art memory networks.  
- Still falls short of traditional rule induction methods on bAbI.  
- Core idea is compelling and potentially more powerful than simpler memory structures (e.g., a stack).  
- Initial submission had major clarity issues, but the revised paper is vastly improved and more readable.  
- Overall assessment: original, technically sound, thought-provoking, and merits publication.  
- Preliminary results do not yet establish superior learning or generalization capacity versus alternative approaches.
