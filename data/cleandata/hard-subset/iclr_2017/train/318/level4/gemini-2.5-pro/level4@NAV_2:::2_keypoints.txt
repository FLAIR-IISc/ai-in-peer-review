- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN): a differentiable, explicitly graph-structured internal state that is dynamically constructed from sequential input.  
- Core contribution: a set of differentiable graph transformations enabling the model to build and modify graph structure on the fly.  
- Empirical demonstration: solves most bAbI tasks and excels on graphical tasks (pathfinding, induction), and discovers rules for a cellular automaton and Turing-machine family.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Key strength: novel, conceptually significant explicit graph memory that contrasts with fixed-structure GNNs and slot-based memories.  
- Key strength: versatile ability to learn complex, rule-based and algorithmic dynamics beyond standard QA.  
- Comparative advantage: learns the graph structure itself, offering potential for more interpretable and powerful reasoning.  
- Empirical caveat: overall bAbI performance comparable to SOTA but particularly strong on inherently graphical tasks.  
- Notable achievement: success on rule-discovery tasks indicates learning of abstract transformations rather than mere memorization.  
- Main weaknesses: high computational (quadratic) complexity and reliance on strong, step-by-step graph supervision limiting practical applicability.  
- Feedback — clarity: main paper needs a high-level, example walkthrough of transformations (Tadd, TC, Tprop) to aid comprehension.  
- Feedback — supervision: discuss possibilities for relaxing full graph supervision (partial supervision or curriculum learning).  
- Feedback — complexity: include empirical runtime and memory comparisons to models like MemN2N or DNC to quantify trade-offs.  
- Feedback — visualization: add visualizations of learned graphs and transformations over timesteps for rule-discovery tasks.  
- Question: How is the node-addition loss (max over permutations) implemented differentiably — is a relaxation/approximation used?  
- Question: How does the model behave when the direct-reference mechanism is ambiguous or incorrect — does it overly rely on it or can it override it?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the schema used to convert bAbI knowledge into a graph (different edge types or node definitions)?
