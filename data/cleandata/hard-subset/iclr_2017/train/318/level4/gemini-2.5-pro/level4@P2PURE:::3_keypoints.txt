- Proposes an extension to Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks to enable complex, learnable graph transformations as internal state.  
- Core idea: build and iteratively modify an internal graph structure to solve tasks, demonstrated on question-answering.  
- Introduces five differentiable graph transformation operators learned in a supervised fashion requiring ground-truth graph states at each timestep.  
- Presents a model instance that consumes a sequence, updates an internal graph state iteratively, and produces accurate QA predictions (e.g., bAbI).  
- Strength: maintains a complex, fully-differentiable internal graph, effectively integrating continuous and symbolic representations.  
- Strength: more general than fixed-shape memory models (Memory Networks, NTMs) because the internal graph’s shape can evolve.  
- Primary concern: training requires full intermediate graph supervision at every timestep, which is unrealistic/scalability-limited for many tasks.  
- Secondary concern: paper is dense and hard to read; presentation may be better suited for a journal-length format.
