The paper's primary contribution is the introduction of a set of differentiable graph transformations that enable the use of gradient descent for graph-to-graph learning tasks. This approach finds a natural application in learning cellular automata, which are modeled as a sequence of evolving graphs. In this setup, the graph grows at each step, with nodes representing cells, edges indicating neighbors, and specific nodes encoding binary values (0/1). The proposed architecture is capable of learning this graph sequence, yet the experiments demonstrate that it is far from solving the presented task (Rule 30).

Furthermore, this core idea is combined with methods from previous work (GGS-NNs) to adapt the model for text generation instead of graph output. By employing graphs as an internal, intermediate representation, this integrated model successfully surpasses the state-of-the-art on the bAbI tasks.
