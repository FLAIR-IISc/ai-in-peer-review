The central contribution of this paper is the introduction of a set of differentiable graph transformations, which enables end-to-end learning of graph-to-graph mapping tasks using gradient descent. This framework is well-suited for modeling dynamic systems that can be represented as a sequence of graphs. The authors demonstrate this concept on the task of learning a cellular automaton (specifically, Rule 30). In this formulation, the automaton's evolution is modeled as a sequence of graph modifications where the graph grows at each time step, nodes are connected to their neighbors, and special nodes are used to represent the binary cell states (0 or 1).

While the proposed architecture is capable of learning these graph sequences, the experimental results show that its performance on the Rule 30 task was limited, indicating that the model did not fully master the target dynamics.

The authors also combine their proposed transformations with concepts from Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks (GGS-NNs). This hybrid approach allows the model to produce textual output by using dynamic graphs as an intermediate representation. By leveraging graphs as an internal state for reasoning, the model achieves state-of-the-art results on several bAbI question-answering tasks, demonstrating the practical value of the proposed techniques.
