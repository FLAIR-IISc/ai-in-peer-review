The primary contribution of this paper is the introduction of a set of differentiable graph transformations that enable the learning of graph-to-graph mappings via gradient descent. This framework is naturally applied to the task of learning a cellular automaton, where the automaton's evolution is represented as a sequence of graphs. In this application, the graph expands at each iteration, with nodes representing cells, edges connecting neighbors, and cell states represented by pointers to special nodes corresponding to the values 0 and 1. While the proposed architecture is designed to learn this sequence of graphs, the experiments demonstrate that for the Rule 30 task, the model was far from a complete solution.

This core idea is also combined with concepts from previous work (Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks) to use graphs as an intermediate representation while producing textual output. This hybrid architecture achieves state-of-the-art performance on the bAbI question-answering tasks.
