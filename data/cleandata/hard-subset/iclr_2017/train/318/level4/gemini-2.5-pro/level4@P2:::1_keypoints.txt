- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), which dynamically constructs and updates an explicit graph-structured internal state from sequential inputs.  
- Core contribution is a set of differentiable graph transformations (node addition, edge updates, propagation) enabling end-to-end gradient-based learning of graph manipulation.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results: solves 19/20 bAbI tasks by building and querying knowledge graphs.  
- Shows rule-discovery capabilities by learning dynamics of a cellular automaton and a family of Turing machines.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novelty/significance: offers a compelling, interpretable alternative to implicit-memory models (e.g., DNCs) by learning explicit, modifiable graph memories.  
- Empirical validation: competitive or superior performance on many bAbI tasks, especially graph-suited tasks like pathfinding and induction.  
- Strength: can learn and apply complex, algorithm-like transitional rules, not just store facts.  
- Primary weakness: heavy reliance on strong, step-by-step ground-truth graph supervision during training limits practical applicability.  
- Practical concern: model complexity scales quadratically with node count, posing scalability challenges.  
- Suggestion: emphasize the supervision caveat earlier (intro/abstract) to set reader expectations.  
- Suggestion: discuss possible mitigation for quadratic scaling (e.g., local neighborhood updates or attention-based subset updates).  
- Questions for authors: analyze error types in longer rollouts (e.g., Rule 30 drop from 93.4% to 82.5%), test robustness to weaker supervision (only final graph), and consider replacing direct reference string-matching with learned attention for node routing.
