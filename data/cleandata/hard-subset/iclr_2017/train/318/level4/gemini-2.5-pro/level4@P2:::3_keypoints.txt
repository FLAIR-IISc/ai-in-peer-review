- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that dynamically constructs and manipulates a differentiable graph-structured internal state from unstructured inputs.  
- Proposes five differentiable graph transformations: node addition, node state update, edge update, propagation, and aggregation.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on bAbI QA tasks and on rule-discovery tasks (cellular automata, Turing machines), achieving competitive or state-of-the-art results.  
- Key novelty: explicit, interpretable, symbolic-like graph memory learned from data, contrasting with opaque vector memories (NTM/DNC).  
- Strength: conceptual advance showing GNNs can learn to create and manipulate internal graph structure, with clear advantages on inherently graphy problems (e.g., pathfinding).  
- Major limitation: relies on strong, step-by-step graph supervision for training, limiting applicability to real-world data.  
- Decision: Accept — contributions and foundational operations justify publication despite supervision constraint.  
- Suggestion: Reduce paper density by moving technical details to the appendix and add a diagram showing transformation flow per sentence/query.  
- Suggestion: Discuss quadratic node-pair complexity earlier and speculate on scalability fixes (sparse updates, attention, etc.).  
- Suggestion: Clarify/generalize the “direct reference” mechanism beyond string matching, e.g., learned routing or attention.  
- Question: Any experiments/ideas to reduce reliance on full graph supervision (weak/semi-supervised or final-answer-only training)?  
- Question: For bAbI Task 17 (Positional Reasoning), did failures come from incorrect graph construction or from subsequent reasoning/propagation?  
- Question: Have you considered two-stage or candidate-edge proposals to avoid the quadratic edge-update computation for larger graphs?
