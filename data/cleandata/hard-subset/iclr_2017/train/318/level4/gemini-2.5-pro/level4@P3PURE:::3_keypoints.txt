- Proposes an extension to Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network to perform complex, learned graph transformations.
- Method builds and modifies an internal graph representation for problem-solving.
- Applied to question-answering tasks, introducing five distinct differentiable transformations learned from data.
- Training is typically supervised with ground-truth graph states provided at each timestep.
- Presents a model instantiation that processes sequence inputs and iteratively updates an internal graph to produce final predictions.
- Achieves interesting results on QA benchmarks such as bAbI.
- Strength: maintains a complex, dynamically evolving graph state while remaining fully differentiable and trainable by gradient descent.
- Strength: successfully integrates continuous and symbolic representations and is more general than fixed-shape memory models (e.g., Memory Networks, NTMs).
- Concern: per-timestep ground-truth supervision is impractical for complex, real-world tasks and limits scalability.
- Concern: paper is very dense and difficult to read; scope and density may be better suited to a journal format than a conference paper.
