The primary contribution of this paper is the introduction of a set of differentiable graph transformations that enable learning for graph-to-graph tasks using gradient descent. The authors apply this framework to the task of learning a cellular automaton, which is represented as a sequence of graphs. In this formulation, the graph expands at each iteration, with nodes representing cell values (0 or 1) and edges defining their neighbors. While the proposed architecture is designed to learn this sequence of graphs, the experiments demonstrate that it is far from solving the specific task of learning Rule 30.

The paper then combines this method with ideas from prior work (GGS-NNs) to produce textual output instead of a graph. By using graphs as an intermediate representation, this hybrid model achieves state-of-the-art performance on the bAbI tasks.
