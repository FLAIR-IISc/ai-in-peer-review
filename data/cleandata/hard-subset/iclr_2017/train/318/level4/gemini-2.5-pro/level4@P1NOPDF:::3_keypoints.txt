- Proposes extending Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks to perform complex, learned graph transformations.  
- Core idea: build and iteratively modify a graph-structured internal representation to solve tasks.  
- Introduces five differentiable graph transformations learned from data, typically with full supervision of intermediate graph states.  
- Implements a model that takes a sequence input, updates an internal graph iteratively, and outputs a final prediction.  
- Applied to question-answering tasks (e.g., bAbI) and achieves promising results.  
- Strength: maintains a complex, dynamic graph state while remaining fully differentiable and trainable end-to-end.  
- Strength: better generality than fixed-structure symbolic-like models (e.g., Memory Networks, NTMs) because internal structure can evolve.  
- Primary concern: requires full timestep-level supervision of graph state, which is unavailable for most real-world/complex tasks.  
- Secondary concern: paper is dense and broad; presentation may be too heavy for a conference format and is hard to read.
