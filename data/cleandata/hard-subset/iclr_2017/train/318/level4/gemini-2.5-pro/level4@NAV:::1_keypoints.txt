- Introduces differentiable graph transformations that enable gradient descent for graph-to-graph learning.
- Demonstrates a natural application to learning cellular automata modeled as sequences of evolving, growing graphs.
- Experimental setup: nodes represent cells, edges represent neighbors, and specific nodes encode binary (0/1) values.
- The proposed architecture can learn the graph sequence but fails to solve the presented Rule 30 task in experiments.
- Integrates methods from GGS-NNs to adapt the model for text generation rather than graph output.
- Using graphs as an internal intermediate representation, the integrated model surpasses state-of-the-art on the bAbI tasks.
