- Proposes a novel dialogue modeling approach that represents dialogue history as a dynamic, differentiable graph learned during inference.  
- Graph-based memory is a significant departure from flat memory structures and could better capture complex relationships and entities in dialogue.  
- The implementation appears to be the first of its kind and opens a promising new direction for memory-augmented neural networks.  
- The authors substantially improved clarity from an earlier draft, making the current manuscript more readable and technically clear.  
- Empirical results on bAbI are competent but do not outperform state-of-the-art Memory Networks or simpler rule-based methods.  
- The added model complexity is not yet shown to yield clear gains in learning capacity or generalization.  
- Evaluation is preliminary and should be extended to tasks where graph memory is likely to provide clear advantages.  
- Despite limited empirical gains, the work is technically interesting and likely to inspire future research, warranting acceptance.
