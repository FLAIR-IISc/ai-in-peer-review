This paper proposes an extension of the gated graph sequence neural network, incorporating the ability to create complex graph transformations. Its core idea is to propose a method that can create/modify a graph structure as an internal representation for solving a problem, and specifically for solving question-and-answer problems. The author proposes 5 different possible differentiable transformations that will be learned on a training set, typically supervised where the state of the graph is given at each timestep. A specific case of the model is presented that takes a sequence as input and recursively updates an internal graph state for final prediction, and which can be applied to solve QA tasks (such as BaBi) and provide interesting results.

The method in this paper is indeed interesting, as the proposed model is able to maintain a representation of its current state as a complex graph, while still retaining the property of being differentiable and thus easily learned through gradient-descent techniques. This can be seen as a successful attempt to mix continuous and symbolic representations. Furthermore, it seems more general than recent attempts to add some 'symbolic' things (memory networks, NTMs, etc...) to differentiable models, because here the shape of the state is not fixed and it can evolve. My main concern is the method of training the model, i.e., providing the state of the graph at each timestep, which can only be done for specific tasks (such as Babi) and cannot be used to solve more complex problems. My other concern is the overall content of the paper, which would probably be best suited for a journal format, rather than a conference format, which would make the article difficult to read due to its density.
