- Proposes learning, on the fly, to represent a dialog as a graph memory, demonstrated on bAbI tasks.  
- Graph construction is part of inference; representation learning estimates graph-transformation parameters and sentence encoders.  
- Claimed first implementation of a differentiable memory in graph form.  
- Substantially more complex than prior approaches (e.g., Memory Networks, stacks).  
- Does not yet yield significant performance gains on bAbI, though graph memory may be more powerful.  
- Clarity was a major issue originally but the revised paper is much clearer.  
- Paper is original, technically accurate, thought-provoking, and worth publishing.  
- Preliminary results do not demonstrate greater learning/generalization capacity; performance comparable to Memory Networks but worse than rule-induction methods.
