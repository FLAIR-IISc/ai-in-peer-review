- Extends Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network to let the model build and modify a graph-structured internal representation while solving tasks.
- Proposes five differentiable transformation types that are learned from data, typically supervised with graph states provided at each time step.
- Implements a sequence-to-graph instance that iteratively updates an internal graph state and produces final predictions; applied to QA (e.g., bAbI) with promising results.
- Strength: maintains a rich, evolving graph-based state while remaining fully differentiable and trainable by gradient descent, bridging continuous and symbolic representations.
- Claimed advantage over prior “symbolic”–differentiable work (Memory Networks, NTM): the form of the state is not fixed and can evolve over time.
- Main concern: training paradigm relies on complete intermediate graph-state supervision, which is unlikely to scale to realistic problems without weaker- or latent-state training strategies.
- Secondary concern: presentation is dense and more suited to a journal than a conference paper, hurting accessibility and reproducibility.
