- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Networks (GGT-NNs), extending GGS-NNs to construct, maintain, and transform an explicit differentiable graph state while processing text/graphs.  
- Core contribution: a suite of differentiable graph operations — node addition, node state update (including direct-reference), edge update, message-passing propagation, and graph-level aggregation.  
- Presents a QA configuration that incrementally builds an internal graph from sentences and uses it to answer queries.  
- Demonstrates rule-learning on 1D cellular automata and Turing machines, showing learned complex transitions and generalization to longer sequences.  
- On bAbI, achieves strong results (notably pathfinding) but mainly when provided “direct reference” supervision.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Primary concern: Heavy reliance on strong supervision (full graph states and direct references) limits generality and makes comparisons to end-to-end baselines unfair.  
- Secondary concern: Paper is dense with key technical details deferred to appendices, reducing clarity and conference suitability.  
- Motivation and positioning: Addresses a timely problem and builds well on GG-NNs/GGS-NNs and memory-augmented models; task choices (bAbI, rule-discovery) are appropriate.  
- Novelty: Differentiable graph transformations are sensible, but the advance over GGS-NNs is incremental — mainly making the graph an evolving differentiable latent and adding parameterized operators.  
- Empirical evidence: Promising results but depend on strong supervision; ablations show large performance drops without direct reference.  
- Missing analyses: runtime/memory complexity, scalability to larger graphs, robustness to supervision noise, hyperparameter sensitivity, and systematic ablation of the five transformations and supervision components.  
- Recommended experiments: results under weaker supervision (answer-only, partial graph, noisy/learned direct reference) to quantify minimal supervision needed.  
- Recommended baselines: include models given the same strong supervision (e.g., GGNN or MemNN with oracle entity linking) to isolate benefits of structural updates.  
- Further ablations: remove each transformation, vary propagation steps, avoid teacher-forcing of predicted nodes/edges, and test the permutation-invariant node-addition loss.  
- Scalability suggestions: provide theoretical time/memory complexity in |V|, Y, T, empirical runtimes, and explore sparse connectivity or neighborhood sampling to mitigate quadratic scaling.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: move key algorithmic details from appendices to main text, tighten notation, add a running toy example, more bAbI graph visualizations, and consolidate hyperparameters/training details and code/scripts.  
- Open questions for authors: minimal supervision needed; loss weighting and exposure bias; sensitivity to direct-reference errors and feasibility of learned entity linker; computational footprint and max graph size; node over-generation/pruning; diversity and failure modes in rule tasks; comparison to GGNN/GGSNN baselines with identical supervision.
