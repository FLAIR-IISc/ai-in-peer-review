The paper’s central contribution is a suite of differentiable graph transformations that enable learning graph-to-graph classification/mapping using gradient descent. This framework aligns naturally with tasks like learning the evolution of a cellular automaton represented as a sequence of graphs: at each step the graph grows, nodes link to their neighbors, and dedicated 0/1 nodes encode cell values. The proposed architecture can learn such sequences of graphs; however, in the reported experiment on Rule 30, the task appears far from fully solved.

The method is combined with prior GGS-NN ideas to permit textual outputs in addition to graph outputs, using graphs as an intermediate representation, which allows the model to surpass state-of-the-art results on the bAbI tasks.
