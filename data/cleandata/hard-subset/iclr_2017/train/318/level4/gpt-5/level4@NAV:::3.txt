The paper presents an extension of the Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network that equips the model with the ability to carry out complex graph transformations. The core idea is to introduce a method capable of constructing/modifying a graph-structured internal representation for problem solving, with a particular focus on question answering in this work. The author proposes five distinct differentiable transformations to be learned from a training set, typically in a supervised setting where the graph state is provided at each timestep. A concrete instantiation of the model is described that takes a sequence as input and iteratively updates an internal graph state to produce a final prediction, and it can be applied to QA tasks (e.g., bAbI) with interesting results.

The approach is compelling because the proposed model maintains a rich graph-based state while remaining differentiable and thus trainable via gradient descent. It can be viewed as a successful attempt to blend continuous and symbolic representations. Moreover, it appears more general than recent efforts to add “symbolic” components to differentiable models (Memory Networks, NTM, etc.), since the shape of the state is not fixed and can evolve. My main concern is the training procedure—providing the graph state at each timestep—which may be feasible only for particular tasks (e.g., bAbI) and is unlikely to extend to more complex problems. My other concern is that the breadth and density of the content may be better suited to a journal format than a conference paper, which makes the article somewhat difficult to read.
