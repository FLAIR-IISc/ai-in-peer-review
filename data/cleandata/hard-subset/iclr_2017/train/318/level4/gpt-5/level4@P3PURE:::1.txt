The main contribution of this paper is a set of differentiable graph transformations that enable learning graph-to-graph classification tasks via gradient descent. This maps naturally to learning a cellular automaton represented as a sequence of graphs. In that setting, the graph grows at each iteration, with nodes pointing to neighbors and special 0/1 nodes representing the values. The proposed architecture can learn this sequence of graphs; however, in the experiment on Rule 30, the task remained far from solved.

The paper further combines this idea with prior work (GGS-NN) to enable the model to produce textual output rather than graph output, using graphs as an intermediate representation. This leads to state-of-the-art results on the bAbI tasks.
