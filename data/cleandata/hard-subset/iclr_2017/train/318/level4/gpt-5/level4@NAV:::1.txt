The paper’s primary contribution appears to be a suite of differentiable graph transformations that enable learning graph-to-graph classification tasks via gradient descent. This maps naturally to learning a cellular automaton represented as a sequence of graphs. In that setting, the node set expands at each step, nodes connect to neighbors, and special 0/1 nodes encode the values. The proposed architecture can learn such sequences of graphs, although in the experiments this task (Rule 30) was far from solved.

This idea is combined with prior work (GGS-NN) to enable the model to produce textual output rather than graph output, using graphs as an intermediate representation, which allows it to surpass the state of the art on the BaBi tasks.
