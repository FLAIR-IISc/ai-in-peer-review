- Introduces on-the-fly learning to represent dialogue as a graph memory, demonstrated on the bAbI tasks.
- Integrates graph induction into inference and uses longer-term representation learning to learn graph transformation parameters and sentence encoders.
- Claims to be the first implementation of a differentiable memory instantiated as a graph.
- Method is substantially more complex than prior approaches (e.g., memory networks) without clear performance gains on bAbI.
- Work is preliminary, but a graph-based memory appears considerably more expressive than a stack.
- Clarity was a significant concern in the initial version, though the author produced a much-improved later version.
- Paper appears technically correct and thought-provoking, and merits publication.
- Preliminary results do not establish that the complex graph memory offers greater learning or generalization capacity than other methods.
- Performance is comparable to the strongest memory networks but still inferior to traditional rule-induction approaches.
