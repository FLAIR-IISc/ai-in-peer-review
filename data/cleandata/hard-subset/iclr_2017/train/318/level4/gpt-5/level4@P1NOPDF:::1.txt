The paper’s main contribution is the introduction of a family of differentiable graph transformations that enable end-to-end learning of graph-to-graph classification tasks via gradient descent. This maps naturally to learning a cellular automaton represented as a sequence of graphs: at each iteration the graph grows, edges connect nodes to their neighbors, and special nodes encode the binary values 0 and 1. The proposed architecture can, in principle, learn this evolving sequence of graphs; however, in the reported experiment on Rule 30, the task remained far from solved.

The authors also combine this idea with prior work on Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks (GGS-NN) to produce textual outputs rather than graph outputs, using graphs as an intermediate representation. This extension allows the model to outperform the state of the art on the bAbI tasks.
