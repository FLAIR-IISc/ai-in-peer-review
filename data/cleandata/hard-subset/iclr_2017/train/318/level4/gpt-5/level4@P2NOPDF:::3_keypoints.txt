- Extends Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network to support complex, learned graph transformations.  
- Central idea: allow the model to build and modify a graph-structured internal representation while solving tasks (focus on question answering).  
- Introduces five differentiable transformation operations that can be learned from data, typically supervised with graph state at each timestep.  
- Presents a concrete model that consumes an input sequence, iteratively updates an internal graph, and produces a final prediction.  
- Demonstrates promising empirical results on QA benchmarks such as bAbI.  
- Approach is compelling: maintains a rich, dynamic graph representation while remaining fully differentiable and trainable by gradient methods.  
- Thoughtful integration of continuous and symbolic computation; more general than prior fixed-shape memory models (e.g., Memory Networks, NTM).  
- Primary concern: reliance on supervision of intermediate graph states—feasible for synthetic datasets but unlikely to scale to complex, real-world problems.  
- Suggests need to discuss and evaluate alternative training strategies (weak supervision, latent structure learning, reinforcement learning).  
- Secondary concern: presentation and scope are dense and hard to follow; material breadth may be better suited to a journal-length treatment.  
- Calls for clearer exposition, more ablation studies, and deeper analysis of when each transformation is necessary.  
- Overall: innovative and potentially influential work with encouraging results, but limited practical applicability due to reliance on fully supervised intermediate graph annotations.
