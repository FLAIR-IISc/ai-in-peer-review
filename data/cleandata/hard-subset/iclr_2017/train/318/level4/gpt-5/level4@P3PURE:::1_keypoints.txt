- Introduces differentiable graph transformations enabling end-to-end gradient-based learning for graph-to-graph classification.
- Frames the problem as learning cellular automata represented by sequences of growing graphs.
- Graphs grow each iteration with nodes pointing to neighbors and special 0/1 nodes encoding cell values.
- Proposed architecture can learn to produce these graph sequences, but fails to solve Rule 30 in experiments.
- Combines the approach with prior GGS-NN work to map intermediate graphs to textual output.
- Uses graphs as an intermediate representation to generate text.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the bAbI question-answering tasks.
