- Introduces an on-the-fly, graph-based differentiable memory for dialog, first validated on bAbI tasks.
- Graph construction is integrated into inference; a separate long-term learning process optimizes graph transformations and sentence encodings.
- Claimed novelty: first implementation of an explicitly structured differentiable graph memory.
- Concept is original and potentially powerful—graphs can capture richer relational dependencies than simpler memories.
- Method is considerably more complex than prior memory-network architectures.
- Empirical results on bAbI do not show clear gains over strong memory-network baselines and lag behind some rule-induction methods.
- Presentation clarity was initially poor but the revised version is substantially clearer; technical content appears sound.
- Despite complexity and mixed empirical advantages, the paper is original, thought-provoking, and merits publication.
