- Proposes learning, on the fly, to represent a dialog as a graph that serves as memory, demonstrated on bAbI tasks.
- Graph learning is part of inference, while long-term representation learning trains graph-transformation parameters and sentence encoders.
- Appears to be the first implementation of a differentiable memory represented as a graph.
- Approach is substantially more complex than prior methods such as memory networks.
- Preliminary results do not yet show significant performance gains on bAbI compared to best memory networks.
- Representing memory as a graph seems more powerful than a stack-based memory.
- Clarity was a major issue in the initial version, but the revised paper is greatly improved.
- The paper is original, technically accurate (as understood), thought-provoking, and worth publishing.
- It remains unclear whether the complex graph-based memory affords greater learning or generalization capacity than alternatives.
- Performance is comparable to the best memory networks but still worse than traditional rule induction approaches.
