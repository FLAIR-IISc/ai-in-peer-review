The paper’s main contribution is the introduction of differentiable graph transformations that enable graph-to-graph prediction using gradient descent. This framework maps naturally to modeling cellular automata as sequences of graphs: at each iteration, the node set grows, edges connect neighboring cells, and special nodes encode binary (0/1) values. The proposed architecture is designed to learn such evolving graph sequences; however, in experiments on Rule 30, the task remained far from solved.

The authors further combine this idea with prior work (GGS-NN) to produce textual outputs rather than graph outputs, treating graphs as an intermediate representation. In this setting, the model attains state-of-the-art results on the bAbI tasks.
