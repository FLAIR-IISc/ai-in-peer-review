- Extends Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network to enable complex graph transformations.  
- Introduces a method to construct/modify a graph-structured internal representation for problem solving, with focus on QA.  
- Proposes five differentiable transformations learned from training data, typically supervised with graph state provided at each timestep.  
- Describes a concrete model that iteratively updates an internal graph state from sequence input to produce predictions; applied to QA tasks (e.g., bAbI) with promising results.  
- Strength: maintains a rich, differentiable graph-based state, effectively blending continuous and symbolic representations.  
- Advantage over prior work (Memory Networks, NTM): the state’s shape can evolve rather than being fixed.  
- Main concern: training requirement of providing graph state at every timestep may only work for limited tasks and likely won’t scale to more complex problems.  
- Secondary concern: breadth and density of content suit a journal more than a conference paper, making the article somewhat hard to read.
