- Introduces differentiable graph transformations enabling end-to-end learning of graph-to-graph mappings via gradient descent.
- Proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), extending GGS-NN to use graphs as intermediate representations and produce text or graphs.
- Bridges graph-based reasoning and sequence prediction by composing graph transformations into a model that maps text to evolving graphs and graphs to outputs.
- Demonstrates applicability to evolving-state problems with a cellular automaton (Rule 30) experiment where graphs grow over time.
- Reports strong empirical performance on the bAbI suite, claiming state-of-the-art results using graph-structured intermediate states.
- Decision: Accept (weak).
- Key rationale: a clear, useful extension of GGS-NN that makes differentiable graph construction and transformation first-class.
- Key rationale: strong empirical results on bAbI support the proposed architecture’s effectiveness.
- Problem/motivation: addresses need to track and update relational structure over time, unlike GNN/GGS-NN that assume fixed graphs.
- Approach: defines five differentiable transformations (node add, node update, edge update, propagation, aggregation) and composes them into GGT-NN.
- Approach: coherent text-to-graph and graph-to-output mapping enabling end-to-end training with direct references to node types.
- Empirical support: bAbI results and the cellular automaton experiment show the intended use case and promise of the operators.
- Limitation: performance on Rule 30 is far from solving the task, indicating issues with stability, training signals, or model capacity for complex graph dynamics.
- Request to clarify supervision: specify what supervision is available per experiment, whether full intermediate graphs are provided, and how losses are combined.
- Ablation requests: test with/without direct reference on bAbI, with/without intermediate propagation, and remove individual transformations (e.g., Tadd, TC) on Rule 30.
- Error analysis request: provide qualitative failure cases for bAbI (e.g., positional reasoning) and Rule 30 and hypothesize root causes (graph growth, edge typing, propagation depth).
- Scalability concern: discuss time/space complexity as graphs grow (e.g., O(|V|^2) edge updates) and potential mitigations like sparse ops or pruning.
- Training details request: report propagation steps, hidden sizes, learning rates, regularization, and other hyperparameters critical for convergence in graph-growing settings.
- Comparison request: add baselines that operate on evolving graphs (fixed-topology GNN with provided updates, rule-based updates) to isolate benefits of learned construction.
- Generalization request: report rule-discovery generalization beyond training horizons and to larger graphs (accuracy vs. steps/graph size).
- Supervision questions: whether full ground-truth graphs are provided per sentence in bAbI and how critical that is; how node strength/type distributions are supervised and sensitivity to node ordering.
- Direct-reference questions: how node types map from ambiguous/multiword entities, contribution of direct reference versus learned attention, and whether the model can learn without direct references.
- Rule 30 questions: what limits performance (propagation depth, edge typing, optimization), and whether curriculum learning or stronger regularization was tried.
- Propagation capacity question: how many propagation steps are used per sentence and at query time, and how accuracy trades off with depth and hidden size.
- Efficiency question: provide practical memory and runtime costs as graph size increases and evidence whether sparse connectivity or sampling preserve performance.
- Reproducibility question: release code and exact bAbI graph schemas to facilitate comparison and follow-up work.
