- Extends Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network to support complex, learnable graph transformations for tasks like question answering.
- Core idea: enable a model to build and iteratively modify a graph-structured internal representation to solve problems.
- Proposes five differentiable graph transformations that are learned from data, typically with supervised graph-state supervision at each timestep.
- Presents a concrete instantiation that processes a sequence, iteratively updates an internal graph state, and produces a final prediction.
- Demonstrates promising results on QA benchmarks such as bAbI.
- Strength: model maintains a rich, evolving graph representation while remaining fully differentiable and trainable by gradient methods.
- Strength: effectively bridges continuous and symbolic representations and is more general than fixed-structure “symbolic” augmentations (e.g., Memory Networks, NTM).
- Main concern: reliance on providing the graph state at each timestep during training is impractical for more complex, real-world tasks.
- Secondary concern: paper’s scope and density are more appropriate for a journal than a conference, making it hard to read.
