- Proposes extending Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks to learn complex, differentiable graph transformations for internal graph-structured representations.  
- Introduces five differentiable transformation operators learned from data, typically under supervised training with full graph-state supervision at each step.  
- Presents an instantiation that iteratively updates an internal graph from a sequence input and shows promising QA results (e.g., bAbI).  
- Strength: maintains a complex, trainable graph-structured state while remaining fully differentiable.  
- Strength: successfully integrates continuous and symbolic representations.  
- Strength: more general than recent symbolic-augmented differentiable models because the state shape can evolve over time.  
- Concern: training requires supervision of the full graph state at every time step, which is unlikely to scale to realistic problems.  
- Concern: paper is very dense and reads more like a journal article, making it difficult to follow for a conference submission.  
- Overall: an interesting and ambitious advancement in differentiable graph reasoning, but limited by reliance on step-by-step supervision and needing clearer, less dense presentation.
