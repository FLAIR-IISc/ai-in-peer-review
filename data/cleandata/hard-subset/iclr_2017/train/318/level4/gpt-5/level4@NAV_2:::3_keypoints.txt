- Extends Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks with learned, differentiable graph transformations.
- Central idea: enable the model to construct and modify a graph-structured internal state for problem solving, focused on question answering.
- Introduces five differentiable transformation primitives that are trained on data, typically with strong supervision (target graph provided at each timestep).
- Presents a concrete instantiation that ingests a sequence and iteratively updates an internal graph state to produce final predictions, showing promising results on QA benchmarks (e.g., bAbI).
- Strength: maintains an explicit, richly structured graph state while remaining fully differentiable and trainable via gradient methods—a useful blend of continuous and symbolic representations.
- Strength: appears more general than previous hybrid models (Memory Networks, NTM) because the internal structure is not fixed and can evolve.
- Primary concern: heavy reliance on full graph-state supervision at every timestep, which is unlikely to scale to complex, real-world problems.
- Secondary concern: paper is dense and broad, making it hard to fit within conference constraints; might be better suited for a journal.
