The paper’s main contribution is a set of differentiable graph transformations that enable learning graph-to-graph classification tasks via gradient descent. This framework naturally maps to learning a cellular automaton represented as a sequence of graphs. In that setting, the graph grows at each iteration, with edges connecting neighboring nodes and special 0/1 nodes encoding cell values. The proposed architecture can, in principle, learn this evolving sequence of graphs; however, in experiments the specific task (Rule 30) remained far from solved.

The approach is further combined with ideas from prior work (GGS-NN) to generate textual output instead of graph output, using graphs as an intermediate representation. In this configuration, the model achieves state-of-the-art results on the bAbI tasks.
