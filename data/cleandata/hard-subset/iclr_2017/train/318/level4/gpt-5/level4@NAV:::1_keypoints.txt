- Primary contribution: a suite of differentiable graph transformations that enable learning graph-to-graph classification via gradient descent.  
- Natural application: models cellular automata as sequences of expanding graphs with neighbor connections and special 0/1 nodes encoding values.  
- Empirical result on cellular automaton (Rule 30): the architecture can learn such sequences but the task remains far from solved in experiments.  
- Extension: combines the idea with prior work (GGS-NN) to produce textual output from graphs as an intermediate representation.  
- Outcome: using graphs as intermediates allows the model to surpass state-of-the-art performance on the bAbI tasks.
