- Proposes an on-the-fly learned graph representation of dialog as a differentiable memory, first evaluated on bAbI tasks.
- Graph construction is performed during inference while longer-term learning estimates graph-transformation parameters and sentence encoders.
- Claimed to be the first implementation of a differentiable, graph-structured memory.
- Substantially more complex than prior methods (e.g., Memory Networks) without clear performance gains on bAbI.
- Preliminary work suggests graph memory may be more powerful than simpler structures like stacks, but this is not yet proven.
- Clarity was a major issue in the initial submission; the authors’ revision substantially improved readability.
- Paper is original, technically sound (as far as the reviewer could assess), and thought-provoking, meriting publication.
- Current results do not demonstrate superior learning or generalization capacity compared to alternatives.
- Performance matches top Memory Networks on bAbI but lags behind traditional rule-induction methods.
