- Main contribution: a set of differentiable graph transformations for learning graph-to-graph classification via gradient descent.
- Natural mapping: framework can represent cellular automata as a sequence of evolving graphs.
- Graph dynamics: graphs grow each iteration, with edges connecting neighboring nodes and special 0/1 nodes encoding cell values.
- Theoretical capability: the architecture can, in principle, learn the evolving graph sequence.
- Empirical limitation: on the specific cellular-automaton task (Rule 30) the model performed far from solved.
- Extension: combined with GGS-NN ideas to produce text from graphs, using graphs as intermediate representations.
- Empirical success: this configuration achieves state-of-the-art results on the bAbI tasks.
