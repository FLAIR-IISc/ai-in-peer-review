The main contribution of this paper is the introduction of a set of differentiable graph transformations, which enables learning graph-to-graph classification tasks using gradient descent. This approach naturally maps to tasks like learning a cellular automaton represented as a sequence of graphs. In such tasks, the graph of nodes grows at each iteration, with nodes pointing to neighbors and special nodes representing values. The proposed architecture allows learning this sequence of graphs, although the experiment on the Rule 30 task was not fully successful.

This idea is combined with concepts from previous papers, specifically the Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network (GGS-NN), to enable the model to produce textual output rather than graph output and use graphs as intermediate representations. This combination allows the model to achieve state-of-the-art results on the bAbI tasks.

**Decision:** Accept

**Key reasons:**

1. The paper introduces a novel and useful set of differentiable graph transformations, which can be applied to various graph-to-graph classification tasks.
2. The proposed architecture combines these transformations with existing ideas from GGS-NN to achieve state-of-the-art results on the bAbI tasks.

**Supporting arguments:**

* The paper provides a clear and well-motivated explanation of the proposed approach, including the introduction of differentiable graph transformations and their combination with GGS-NN.
* The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed architecture on the bAbI tasks, achieving state-of-the-art results.
* The paper also explores the application of the proposed approach to other tasks, such as learning a cellular automaton, which shows promise for future research.

**Additional feedback:**

* The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the differentiable graph transformations and their implementation.
* The experiments on the Rule 30 task could be improved by providing more detailed results and analysis.
* The paper could also explore the application of the proposed approach to more complex tasks and domains.

**Questions for the authors:**
