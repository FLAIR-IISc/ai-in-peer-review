- Extends Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network to generate complex graph transformations as internal representations.  
- Method builds or modifies a graph structure to solve problems, demonstrated for question-answering tasks.  
- Introduces five differentiable transformations that are learned from training data, typically supervised with graph states provided at each timestep.  
- Example model processes a sequence, iteratively updates an internal graph state, and produces a final prediction with promising QA performance.  
- Strength: maintains a rich, learnable graph representation while preserving differentiability, merging continuous and symbolic aspects.  
- Claimed advantage: greater generality compared to recent attempts to add symbolic elements to differentiable models.  
- Main concern: training requires supervision of the graph state at every timestep, limiting applicability to tasks where such supervision is available.  
- Presentation issues: paper is dense and may be better suited for a journal; readability is challenging.
