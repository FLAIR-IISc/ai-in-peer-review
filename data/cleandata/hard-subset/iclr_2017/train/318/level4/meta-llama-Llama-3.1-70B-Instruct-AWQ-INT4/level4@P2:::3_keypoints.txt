- Proposes an extension of Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network to enable complex graph transformations.  
- Introduces five differentiable, supervised transformations with the graph state provided at each time step.  
- Model maintains an evolving, complex graph-structured internal representation while remaining differentiable and trainable by gradient descent.  
- Successfully combines continuous and symbolic representations, offering more generality than fixed-shape models like Memory Networks or Neural Turing Machines.  
- Main concern: training requires the graph state at every time step, which may not scale to more complex problems.  
- Paper is dense and may be better suited to a journal than a conference.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reasons for acceptance: novel extension with promising QA results and valuable continuous-symbolic integration.  
- Supporting strength: clear, well-motivated approach and thorough explanation of transformations and training.  
- Empirical support: impressive results on bAbI tasks demonstrating learning and generalization of complex graph structures.  
- Suggestion: improve readability with smaller sections and more visual aids (diagrams/illustrations).  
- Suggestion: discuss training limitations and future research directions (alternative training methods, harder problems).  
- Suggestion: include more comparisons to related work (Memory Networks, Neural Turing Machines).  
- Questions for authors: provide training hyperparameters and computational resource details.  
- Questions for authors: how will training limitations be addressed and what alternative methods are considered?  
- Questions for authors: have you applied the model to more complex domains, and what were the results?
