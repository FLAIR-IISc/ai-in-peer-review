- Proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) to learn and manipulate graph-structured intermediate representations and outputs.  
- Extends prior GNN/GGS-NN work by enabling construction and modification of graphs conditioned on textual input.  
- Demonstrates capability to produce diverse outputs using learned graph representations.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty, potential, and thorough evaluation.  
- Clear, well-motivated presentation with detailed architecture and component descriptions.  
- Thorough evaluation on bAbI tasks showing learning of complex graph structures and state-of-the-art performance on several tasks.  
- Additional experiments on two rule discovery tasks indicate generalization of graph-structured representations.  
- Writing and clarity substantially improved from initial submission.  
- Limitation: bAbI results are comparable but not clearly superior to other state-of-the-art methods; advantage of graph-based differentiable memory not fully demonstrated.  
- Suggests evaluating on more complex tasks/datasets to better show benefits of the approach.  
- Recommends providing more details on rule discovery tasks and datasets.  
- Recommends adding visualizations/examples of learned graphs, and discussing limitations (scalability, interpretability) and concrete applications/use cases.
