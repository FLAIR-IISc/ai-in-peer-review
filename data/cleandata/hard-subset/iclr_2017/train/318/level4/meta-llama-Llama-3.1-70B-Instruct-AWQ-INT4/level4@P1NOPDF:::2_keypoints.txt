- Proposes a novel dialog-as-graph differentiable memory with graph learning integrated into inference and long-term representation learning for graph transforms and sentence encoding.
- Appears to be the first implementation of differentiable memory modeled as a graph, offering a more complex alternative to memory networks.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on bAbI tasks, but performance gains are not significant compared to existing methods.
- Performance is comparable to the best memory networks but lags behind traditional rule-induction methods.
- Major limitation is clarity of presentation, though the revised manuscript is substantially more readable.
- Preliminary results do not yet show superior learning or generalization capacity; further experiments are required.
- Overall assessment: original, technically sound, and thought-provoking work that merits publication with continued refinement and evaluation.
