- Proposes an extension of Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks to produce complex, learnable graph transformations.  
- Method builds/modifies an internal graph representation to solve problems, particularly question answering.  
- Introduces five differentiable transformations learned from supervised training where graph states are provided at each time step.  
- Presents a model instance that iteratively updates an internal graph from a sequence input to produce final predictions.  
- Demonstrates promising results on QA tasks like bAbI.  
- Strength: maintains a complex, evolving graph state while remaining fully differentiable, integrating continuous and symbolic representations.  
- More general than models like Memory Networks or Neural Turing Machines because the state shape can evolve.  
- Major concern: training requires ground-truth graph state at each time step, which may not scale to more complex tasks.  
- Secondary concern: paper’s density and complexity may be better suited to a journal than a conference format.
