- Main contribution: introduces differential graph transformations to enable learning graph-to-graph classification via gradient descent.  
- Extends idea to learning cellular automata as a sequence of expanding graphs.  
- In the CA setup, graphs expand each iteration with nodes pointing to neighbors and special 0/1 nodes representing values.  
- Proposed architecture can learn sequences of graphs, but experiments on the Rule 30 task did not achieve a complete solution.  
- Combines this approach with prior ideas (e.g., GGS-NN) to allow the model to produce textual output rather than graph output.  
- Using graphs as intermediate representations yields state-of-the-art performance on the bAbI tasks.
