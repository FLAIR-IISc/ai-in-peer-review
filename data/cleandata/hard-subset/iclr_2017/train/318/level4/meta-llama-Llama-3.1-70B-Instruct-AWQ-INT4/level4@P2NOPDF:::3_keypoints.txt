- Proposes an extension of Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network to produce complex, learnable graph transformations.  
- Aims to build/modify an internal graph representation for problem solving, focused on question-answering tasks.  
- Introduces five differentiable transformations that can be learned, typically supervised with graph state provided at each time step.  
- Presents a specific model instance that takes a sequence input and iteratively updates an internal graph state to make a final prediction.  
- Demonstrates applicability to QA tasks (e.g., bAbI) with promising empirical results.  
- Model preserves a complex, evolving graph representation while remaining fully differentiable for gradient-based learning.  
- Represents a successful integration of continuous and symbolic representations.  
- Arguably more general than Memory Networks and Neural Turing Machines because the state shape is not fixed and can evolve.  
- Major concern: training requires providing the graph state at every time step, which may not scale to more complex or realistic tasks.  
- Questions whether the manuscript is better suited for a journal than a conference due to its density and readability issues.  
- Suggests improving clarity by expanding key concepts, adding intuitive explanations, and including visual aids to aid understanding.
