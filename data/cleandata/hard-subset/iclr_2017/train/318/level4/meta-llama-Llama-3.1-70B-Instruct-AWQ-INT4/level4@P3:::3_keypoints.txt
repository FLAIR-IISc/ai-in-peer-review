- Paper extends Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network to enable learned complex graph transformations as internal representations.
- Goal is to build/modify a graph-structured internal state for tasks, demonstrated on question-answering.
- Author proposes five differentiable graph transformations learned in a supervised setting with graph states provided at each timestep.
- Presents an instantiation that takes a sequence input and iteratively updates an internal graph to produce final predictions.
- Experiments on bAbI tasks show promising and interesting results.
- Strength: model combines continuous and symbolic representations while remaining differentiable and trainable by gradient descent.
- Strength: approach appears more general than prior differentiable “symbolic” models since the internal state shape can evolve.
- Concern: training requires providing the graph state at each timestep, which limits applicability beyond tasks like bAbI.
- Concern: scalability and real-world applicability are unclear due to reliance on timestep supervision.
- Paper is dense and sometimes hard to follow; would benefit from clearer sectioning and more concise language.
- Reviewer recommends adding discussion of limitations and future research directions.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: clarify training process and how timestep graph states are provided, break text into clearer sections, and use more concise explanations.
- Questions for authors: detail how graph state is provided during training, plans to address scalability/applicability, and expanded discussion of limitations and future work.
