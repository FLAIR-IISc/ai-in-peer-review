- Main contribution: introduces differentiable graph transformations to learn graph->graph classification via gradient descent, framing cellular automata as sequences of graphs.  
- Proposed task: models growing-graph cellular automaton (nodes grow each iteration, special 0/1 nodes indicate values).  
- Architecture: presents Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) and detailed explanation of differentiable graph ops.  
- Experimental result: shows strong performance on bAbI tasks and can learn some cellular automaton rules, but Rule 30 task was far from solved.  
- Overall judgment: accept—novel, well-motivated approach that combines GNNs and seq2seq ideas and demonstrates effectiveness on multiple tasks.  
- Strength: clear motivation and detailed presentation of model and differentiable graph transformations.  
- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art results on bAbI tasks.  
- Weakness: cellular automaton experiment not fully solved; insufficient analysis of failure modes.  
- Suggested improvements: add more analysis of limitations and failure cases and discuss future improvements.  
- Suggested improvements: include more comparisons to other state-of-the-art models on bAbI tasks.  
- Suggested improvements: provide implementation details—hyperparameters and computational resources.  
- Questions for authors: explain why the model failed to fully learn the cellular automaton task.  
- Questions for authors: how will the approach be improved and what are its potential limitations?  
- Questions for authors: supply detailed implementation info, including hyperparameters and compute used.
