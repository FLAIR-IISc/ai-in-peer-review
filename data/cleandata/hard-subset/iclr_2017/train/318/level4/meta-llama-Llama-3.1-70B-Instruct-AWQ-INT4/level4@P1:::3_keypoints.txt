- Proposes extending Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks (GGS-NN) to produce complex, learnable graph transformations.
- Introduces five differentiable transformations that can be learned, typically with supervised state annotations at each time step.
- Presents a specific model that takes a sequence input and iteratively updates an internal graph state to make predictions.
- Demonstrates applicability to question-answering tasks (e.g., bAbI) with promising results.
- Key contribution: maintains a complex, evolving graph representation while remaining end-to-end differentiable and trainable by gradient descent.
- More general than some prior differentiable-symbolic hybrids (Memory Networks, Neural Turing Machines) because the state shape can change.
- Main concern: training requires providing the graph state at every time step, which is task-specific and may not scale to complex problems.
- Paper density and scope may be better suited for a journal than a conference, making it hard to read in current form.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Suggested improvements: break content into smaller focused sections and add more visual aids to clarify architecture and training.
- Suggested improvements: discuss training limitations and potential solutions for scaling to more complex tasks.
- Suggested improvements: provide more performance analysis, comparisons to state-of-the-art, and evaluation of generalization.
- Questions for authors: clarify how graph state supervision is provided at each time step during training.
- Questions for authors: explain plans to overcome training limitations for more complex problems and provide more comparative/generalization analysis.
