- Proposes a novel graph-based differentiable memory for dialog, integrated into inference with long-term representation learning; claimed first implementation of graph-based differentiable memory.  
- Applied to bAbI tasks, but the method is significantly more complex than prior approaches like memory networks.  
- Does not show substantial performance gains on bAbI tasks compared to existing methods.  
- Graph representation appears more powerful than stack-based memory.  
- Paper clarity was poor initially but the revised version is much improved and more readable.  
- Preliminary results are inconclusive about superior learning or generalization; performance matches top memory networks but lags behind traditional rule induction methods.  
- Further experiments and evaluation are needed to fully assess benefits and limitations.  
- Overall: original, technically sound, thought-provoking work worth publishing and likely to stimulate further research.
