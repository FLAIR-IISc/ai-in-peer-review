This paper proposes a novel approach to learning graph-structured representations of data, introducing the Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) model. The GGT-NN model extends previous work on Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) and Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks (GGS-NNs) by allowing graph-structured intermediate representations and outputs. The model can learn to construct and modify graphs in sophisticated ways based on textual input and use the graphs to produce a variety of outputs.

I decide to accept this paper, with the main reasons being the novelty and potential of the proposed approach, as well as the thorough evaluation on the bAbI tasks and additional rule discovery tasks.

The paper provides a clear and well-motivated explanation of the proposed approach, including a detailed description of the GGT-NN model architecture and its components. The author also provides a thorough evaluation of the model on the bAbI tasks, demonstrating its ability to learn complex graph-structured representations and achieve state-of-the-art performance on several tasks. Additionally, the paper includes results on two rule discovery tasks, showcasing the model's ability to learn and generalize graph-structured representations.

The paper is well-written, and the author has made a significant effort to improve clarity and readability since the initial submission. The text is now much easier to follow, and the author provides a clear and concise explanation of the proposed approach and its evaluation.

However, there are some areas that could be improved. One potential limitation of the paper is that the results on the bAbI tasks are comparable to, but not significantly better than, those of other state-of-the-art approaches. While the author argues that the graph-based differentiable memory has more learning and generalization capacity than other approaches, this is not entirely clear from the results presented. Further evaluation on more complex tasks or datasets could help to demonstrate the advantages of the proposed approach.

Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential applications of the proposed approach. While the author mentions some potential uses for the GGT-NN model, such as extracting graph-structured information from unstructured textual descriptions, more concrete examples and use cases could help to illustrate the potential impact of this work.
