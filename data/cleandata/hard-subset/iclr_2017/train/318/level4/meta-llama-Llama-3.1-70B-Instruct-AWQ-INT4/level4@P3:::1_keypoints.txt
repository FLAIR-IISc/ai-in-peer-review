- Main contribution: introduces differentiable graph transformations enabling end-to-end learning for graph-to-graph classification via gradient descent.  
- Applicability: naturally maps to sequence-of-graphs tasks like cellular automata where graphs grow iteratively.  
- Empirical note: architecture can learn graph sequences but Rule 30 experiment was not fully successful.  
- Innovation: combines differentiable graph transformations with Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network (GGS-NN) ideas.  
- Output capability: enables producing textual output while using graphs as intermediate representations.  
- Results: achieves state-of-the-art performance on the bAbI question-answering tasks.  
- Decision: paper recommended for acceptance.  
- Key reason 1: novel and useful set of differentiable graph transformations applicable to many graph-to-graph tasks.  
- Key reason 2: effective combination with GGS-NN yields strong empirical results on bAbI.  
- Support: paper is clearly motivated and explains the approach well.  
- Support: experiments demonstrate effectiveness on bAbI and show promise on cellular automaton tasks.  
- Feedback: needs more detailed explanations and implementation details of the differentiable graph transformations.  
- Feedback: Rule 30 experiments require more detailed results and analysis.  
- Feedback: should explore applications to more complex tasks and domains.  
- Questions: request specifics on neural architectures used for the graph transformations.  
- Questions: ask how authors will address limitations like strong supervision needs and potential overfitting.  
- Questions: ask about plans to extend the approach to more complex or multi-graph settings.
