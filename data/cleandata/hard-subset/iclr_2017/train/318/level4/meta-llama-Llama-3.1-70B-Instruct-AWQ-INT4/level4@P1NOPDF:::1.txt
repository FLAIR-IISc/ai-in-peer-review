The main contribution of this paper appears to be the introduction of a set of differential graph transformations, enabling the use of gradient descent to learn graph-to-graph classification tasks. This concept naturally extends to learning a cellular automaton represented as a sequence of graphs. In this context, the graph of nodes expands at each iteration, with nodes pointing to their neighbors and special nodes 0/1 representing the values. The proposed architecture allows for learning this sequence of graphs, although the experiment falls short of fully solving this task, as evidenced by the results on Rule 30.

This idea is combined with concepts from previous papers, such as GGS-NN, to enable the model to produce textual output instead of graph output, utilizing graphs as intermediate representations. This approach allows the model to achieve state-of-the-art performance on the BaBi tasks.
