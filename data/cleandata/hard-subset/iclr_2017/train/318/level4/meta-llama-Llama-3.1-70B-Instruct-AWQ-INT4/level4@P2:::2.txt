This paper proposes learning on the fly to represent a dialog as a graph (which acts as the memory), and is first demonstrated on the bAbI tasks. Graph learning is part of the inference process, though there is long term representation learning to learn graph transformation parameters and the encoding of sentences as input to the graph.

**Decision:** Accept
**Key reasons:**
1. The paper presents a novel and powerful approach to representing memory as a graph, which has the potential to be more expressive than previous approaches.
2. The author has made significant efforts to improve the clarity and readability of the paper, making it more accessible to a wider audience.

**Supporting arguments:**

1. The paper tackles the specific question of learning graph-structured representations of memory, which is a well-motivated problem in the context of natural language processing and artificial intelligence.
2. The approach is well-placed in the literature, building on previous work on graph neural networks and memory-augmented neural networks.
3. The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the proposed method, including the differentiable graph transformations and the training procedure.
4. The experimental results on the bAbI tasks demonstrate the potential of the approach, although more work is needed to fully evaluate its performance and generalization capacity.

**Additional feedback:**

1. While the paper has improved significantly in terms of clarity, there are still some sections that could be further simplified and clarified.
2. The author could provide more discussion on the potential limitations and challenges of the approach, such as scalability and interpretability.
3. It would be helpful to include more visualizations and examples to illustrate the graph-structured representations learned by the model.
4. The author could consider providing more comparisons with other approaches, such as memory networks and neural Turing machines, to better evaluate the performance and generalization capacity of the proposed method.

**Questions for the authors:**

1. Can you provide more details on the computational resources required to train the model, and how you plan to address scalability issues?
2. How do you plan to evaluate the interpretability and explainability of the graph-structured representations learned by the model?
3. Can you provide more discussion on the potential applications of the approach beyond the bAbI tasks, and how you plan to adapt the method to more complex tasks?
