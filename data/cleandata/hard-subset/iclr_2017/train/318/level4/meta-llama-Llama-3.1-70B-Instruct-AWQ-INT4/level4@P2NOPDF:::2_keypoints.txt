- Proposes a novel dialog representation that learns a graph on the fly as a differentiable memory component.  
- Demonstrates the approach on bAbI tasks, using graph learning as part of inference.  
- Method includes long-term representation learning for graph transformation parameters and sentence encoding as graph input.  
- Using a graph as differentiable memory is a unique and potentially more powerful alternative to traditional memory networks.  
- Results on bAbI do not show significant performance gains over existing methods.  
- Preliminary nature of work suggests graph-based representations merit further exploration despite modest results.  
- Revised paper has substantially improved clarity and readability compared to the original submission.  
- Remaining questions about whether the approach offers superior learning or generalization compared to alternatives.  
- Performance is comparable to top memory networks but still lags behind traditional rule induction methods.  
- Overall: technically accurate, original, thought-provoking contribution that warrants further investigation.
