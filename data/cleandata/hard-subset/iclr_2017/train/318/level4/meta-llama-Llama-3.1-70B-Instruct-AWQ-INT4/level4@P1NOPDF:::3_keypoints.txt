- Extends Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network to enable complex, differentiable graph transformations.  
- Proposes building/modifying an internal graph representation for problem solving, with a focus on question answering.  
- Defines five differentiable transformations that are learned, typically supervised with the graph state provided at each timestep.  
- Presents a model instance that iteratively updates an internal graph from a sequence and shows promising QA performance (e.g., bAbI).  
- Keeps the model fully differentiable, allowing training via gradient descent despite complex graph states.  
- Effectively combines continuous and symbolic representations.  
- More general than Memory Networks/Neural Turing Machines because the state’s shape can evolve dynamically.  
- Major concern: reliance on per-timestep graph-state supervision may not scale to more complex, realistic tasks.  
- Presentation density raises readability issues and prompts the question of whether a journal format would be more appropriate than a conference paper.
