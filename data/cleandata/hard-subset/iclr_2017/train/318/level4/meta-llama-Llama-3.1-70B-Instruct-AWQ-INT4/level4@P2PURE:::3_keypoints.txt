- Proposes an extension to Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks to enable complex, learnable graph transformations.  
- Aims to build or modify an internal graph representation for problem solving, focusing on question-answering tasks.  
- Introduces five differentiable transformations that can be learned, typically with supervised graph-state supervision at each time step.  
- Presents a concrete model that takes a sequence input, iteratively updates an internal graph state, and outputs final predictions.  
- Shows promising results on QA benchmarks such as bAbI.  
- Strength: preserves differentiability while maintaining a complex, evolving graph representation, bridging continuous and symbolic methods.  
- More general than previous differentiable-symbolic models (e.g., Memory Networks, NTM) because the state shape can change over time.  
- Main concern: training requires providing graph states at every time step, which may be impractical or unscalable for many tasks.  
- Secondary concern: paper is dense and possibly better suited for a journal than a conference, making it hard to read.
