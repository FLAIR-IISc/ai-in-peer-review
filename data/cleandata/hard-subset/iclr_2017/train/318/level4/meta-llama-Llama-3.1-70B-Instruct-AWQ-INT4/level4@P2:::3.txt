The paper proposes an extension of the Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network by incorporating the ability to produce complex graph transformations. The underlying idea is to develop a method that can build or modify a graph-structured internal representation for solving problems, particularly question-answering tasks. The author introduces five differentiable transformations that can be learned in a supervised manner, where the graph state is provided at each time step.

The approach presented in this paper is interesting, as the proposed model can maintain a representation of its current state as a complex graph while remaining differentiable and easily learnable through gradient-descent techniques. This can be seen as a successful attempt to combine continuous and symbolic representations. The model appears to be more general than recent attempts to incorporate symbolic elements into differentiable models, such as Memory Networks and Neural Turing Machines, as the shape of the state is not fixed and can evolve.

However, my main concerns are about the training process and the overall content of the paper. The model is trained by providing the graph state at each time step, which may be feasible for specific tasks like bAbI but may not be suitable for more complex problems. Furthermore, the paper's content is dense and may be better suited for a journal format rather than a conference format.

**Decision:** Accept
