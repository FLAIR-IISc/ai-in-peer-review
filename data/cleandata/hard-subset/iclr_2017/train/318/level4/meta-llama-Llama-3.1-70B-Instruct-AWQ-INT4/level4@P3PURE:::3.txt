The paper proposes an extension of the Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network, incorporating the ability to produce complex graph transformations. The underlying idea is to devise a method that can build or modify a graph structure as an internal representation for solving problems, particularly question-answering problems. The authors propose five differentiable transformations that can be learned from a training set, typically in a supervised fashion where the state of the graph is given at each time step. A specific instance of the model is presented, which takes a sequence as input and iteratively updates an internal graph state to produce a final prediction. This model can be applied to solve QA tasks, such as BaBi, with promising results.

The approach presented in this paper is interesting, as the proposed model maintains a representation of its current state as a complex graph while preserving the property of being differentiable and thus easily learnable through gradient-descent techniques. This can be seen as a successful attempt to integrate continuous and symbolic representations. Furthermore, this approach appears more general than recent attempts to incorporate symbolic elements into differentiable models (e.g., Memory Networks, Neural Turing Machines), as the shape of the state is not fixed and can evolve.

However, my primary concern lies in the training methodology, which involves providing the state of the graph at each time step. While this approach may be feasible for specific tasks, such as BaBi, it may not be applicable to more complex problems. Additionally, I question the suitability of the paper's content for a conference format, as it may be more appropriate for a journal format due to its density and complexity, making the article challenging to read.
