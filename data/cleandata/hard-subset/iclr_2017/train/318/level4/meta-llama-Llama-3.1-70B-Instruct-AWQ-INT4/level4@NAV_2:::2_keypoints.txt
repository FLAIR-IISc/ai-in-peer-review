- Proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that learns to construct and modify graphs from text as a differentiable memory.
- Demonstrated on bAbI tasks, showing promising results particularly for temporal sequence reasoning.
- Claimed to be the first implementation of a differentiable memory represented as a graph.
- Graph memory is more expressive than a stack but the approach is substantially more complex than prior memory networks.
- Complexity does not yield significant performance gains on bAbI tasks; work is still preliminary.
- Reviewer decision: accept, based on novelty and preliminary positive results.
- Paper is well-motivated and well-placed in the literature with appropriate evaluation on bAbI.
- Presentation issues: notation and terminology are sometimes confusing and rely on appendices for clarity.
- Suggested improvements: provide more intuition and explanations for model components (graph transformation layers, attention).
- Suggested improvements: add visualizations/diagrams to clarify model structure and operations.
- Suggested improvements: include more analysis of results and discussion of generalization across tasks.
- Reviewer questions: request implementation details of graph transformation layers and attention, and plans for extending GGT-NN to more complex tasks/domains.
