This paper presents an extension of the Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network, enabling the model to generate complex graph transformations. The core concept revolves around developing a method that constructs or modifies a graph structure as an internal representation to solve problems, with a specific focus on question-answering tasks. The author introduces five distinct differentiable transformations that are learned from a training set, typically in a supervised manner where the graph state is provided at each time step. A notable instance of the model is showcased, where it takes a sequence as input, iteratively updates an internal graph state, and yields a final prediction, demonstrating its applicability to question-answering tasks with promising results.

The proposed approach is intriguing, as the model successfully maintains a complex graph representation of its current state while preserving differentiability, allowing for efficient learning through gradient-descent techniques. This can be seen as a successful fusion of continuous and symbolic representations, offering greater generality compared to recent attempts at incorporating symbolic elements into differentiable models. However, my primary concerns lie in the training methodology, which relies on providing the graph state at each time step, a limitation that may only be feasible for specific tasks. Additionally, the paper's content may be more suited to a journal format, and its density makes it challenging to read.
