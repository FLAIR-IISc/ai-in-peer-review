- Proposes an extension of Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network to support complex, learnable graph transformations.
- Introduces five differentiable graph transformation operators learned in a supervised setting with graph states provided at each timestep.
- Presents a sequence-to-graph instance applied iteratively to QA tasks (e.g., bAbI) with promising results.
- Recommends acceptance based on novelty and potential impact combining neural networks with graph-structured representations.
- Major concern: training requires supervising the graph state at every timestep, which may not scale to more complex or realistic problems.
- Paper is dense and may be better suited to a journal format; clarity/readability is an issue.
- Requests more experiments on additional tasks and datasets to strengthen empirical support.
- Asks for a more detailed analysis of the model’s performance, limitations, and comparisons to stronger baselines.
- Wants a clearer, more detailed explanation of the differentiable graph transformations and how they are learned.
- Requests a more thorough discussion of potential applications and possible extensions of the approach.
- Specific author questions: how to extend to problems without per-timestep graph supervision, precise learning details for the transformations, and plans for applying the approach to other AI areas.
