The primary contribution of this paper appears to be the introduction of a novel set of differential graph transformations, enabling the application of gradient descent to graph-to-graph classification tasks. This innovation has a natural affinity with learning cellular automata represented as a sequence of graphs, where the graph of nodes expands iteratively, with nodes referencing neighboring nodes and special nodes denoting values. Although the proposed architecture facilitates learning across this sequence of graphs, the experimental results fell short of solving the task at hand (Rule 30). This concept is integrated with established ideas from prior works (GGS-NN), allowing the model to generate textual outputs while utilizing graphs as intermediate representations, ultimately yielding state-of-the-art performance on BaBi tasks.
