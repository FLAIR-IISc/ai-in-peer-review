This paper introduces a novel extension to the Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network, equipping it with the capability to perform differentiable graph transformations. The core concept is to enable the model to construct and refine a graph structure as an internal representation for problem-solving, demonstrated here through question answering. The authors present five distinct, learnable differentiable transformations, typically trained in a supervised manner with known graph states at each time step. A specific instantiation of this model iteratively updates an internal graph based on sequential input, ultimately yielding a prediction suitable for QA tasks like BaBi, achieving promising results.

The proposed approach is compelling due to its ability to maintain a complex graph-based state representation while remaining fully differentiable, facilitating learning via gradient descent. This represents a successful integration of continuous and symbolic reasoning paradigms, and appears more flexible than recent efforts to incorporate ‘symbolic’ elements into differentiable models (e.g., Memory Networks, NTMs), as the state’s structure is not fixed. However, a key limitation lies in the training methodology – requiring the graph state at each time step – which restricts its applicability to tasks like BaBi and may not generalize to more complex, real-world scenarios. Furthermore, the paper’s scope and density suggest it would be more appropriately suited for a journal publication rather than a conference format, currently hindering readability.
