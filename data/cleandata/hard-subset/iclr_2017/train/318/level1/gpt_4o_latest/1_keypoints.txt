- Introduces GGT-NN, an extension of GGS-NNs that constructs, modifies, and uses graph-structured intermediate representations from textual input.  
- Shows strong empirical performance: state-of-the-art on bAbI (especially pathfinding and induction), plus success on cellular automata and Turing machine tasks, with generalization to larger inputs.  
- Novel contribution: differentiable graph transformations enabling dynamic graph construction/modification not addressed by prior GNNs, GGS-NNs, or memory networks.  
- Modularity and flexibility: proposed transformations allow adapting the architecture to various structured-data tasks.  
- Rigorous methodology: detailed model, training, experimental setup, wide benchmarking, and ablation studies (e.g., with/without direct reference).  
- Recommendation: paper accepted for significant contribution, novelty, empirical results, and future application potential.  
- Scalability concern: quadratic time/space complexity due to distributed node processing; suggests exploring sparse edges or selective node processing.  
- Limited analysis of failures: model struggles on task 17 (Positional Reasoning); needs deeper analysis and remedies.  
- Comparison gap: more discussion needed on trade-offs between interpretability (explicit graph structure) and efficiency (implicit memory models like DNCs).  
- Broader applications: paper should discuss real-world uses such as knowledge graph construction and NLP reasoning.  
- Question: How does the model handle noisy or incomplete input where graph structure is ill-defined?  
- Question: Can GGT-NN be extended to dynamic graphs with changing node/edge types, and what modifications are required?  
- Question: How does performance scale with larger graphs or more complex tasks, and what are the practical computational limits?
