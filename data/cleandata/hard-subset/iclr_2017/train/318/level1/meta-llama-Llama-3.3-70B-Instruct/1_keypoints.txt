- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), extending GGS-NN to support graph-structured intermediate representations.
- GGT-NN can learn to construct and modify graphs from textual input and use those graphs to produce varied outputs.
- Evaluated on bAbI tasks, achieving high accuracy on most tasks.
- Applied to rule-discovery tasks (1D cellular automaton and 2-symbol 4-state Turing machine) and successfully learned evolution rules.
- Decision: Accept — approach is well-motivated and results are impressive.
- Builds on existing GGS-NN and graph neural network work and tackles a clear problem.
- Paper provides clear, detailed explanations of the model and components.
- Suggests additional analysis of computational complexity and scalability to larger graphs and harder tasks.
- Recommends more comparisons with other graph-and-text models/architectures.
- Suggests including more visualizations and example graphs to illustrate capabilities and limitations.
- Questions for authors: handling varying graph sizes/structures; applicability to multi-graph or multi-type node/edge tasks; performance comparisons to other models; plans to release code and data.
