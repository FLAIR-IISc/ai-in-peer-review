- Paper introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) to learn and modify graph-structured intermediate representations for downstream tasks.  
- Demonstrates strong/competitive results on bAbI tasks and positive results on cellular automaton and Turing machine rule-learning tasks.  
- Recommendation: Reject.  
- Main critique: Limited novelty—builds on GG-NNs/GGS-NNs and similar ideas exist (e.g., NTMs, DNCs), so contribution not clearly distinct.  
- Gains on bAbI are marginal and may not justify added architectural complexity.  
- Experimental concerns: reliance on strong supervision (providing correct graph each timestep) questions ability to learn without explicit guidance.  
- Rule-discovery experiments are interesting but underexplored and lack comparisons to other methods that could learn rules without explicit graph representations.  
- Computational cost: model scales quadratically with graph size and paper does not adequately address mitigation strategies.  
- Suggest clearer articulation of unique contributions and stronger comparisons with related work (NTMs, DNCs, LRNNs).  
- Presentation issues: Section 3 (graph transformations) is dense and hard to follow; needs more intuitive explanation, diagrams, and simpler notation.  
- Recommend ablation studies to quantify contributions of components (e.g., direct reference, intermediate propagation).  
- Recommend investigating reduced/weak supervision to test whether the model can learn without explicit graph-level labels.  
- Recommend exploring scaling solutions (sparse edges, graph coarsening) to address quadratic complexity.  
- Recommend more comprehensive evaluation/comparisons on rule-discovery tasks against non-graph-based learners.  
- Questions for authors: what is the primary advantage of graph-structured intermediate states vs vectors/matrices/trees?  
- Questions: how does performance degrade with increasing graph size and have mitigation techniques been tested?  
- Questions: how sensitive are results to the choice of graph transformations?  
- Questions: provide implementation details of neural networks within transformations (layers, activations).  
- Questions: what is the effect of removing the direct reference mechanism—does the model still learn effectively?
