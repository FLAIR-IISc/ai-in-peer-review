- Paper introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) to learn and manipulate graph-structured internal representations.  
- Core idea of using graphs as flexible internal state for relational tasks is promising.  
- Demonstrated successful application to bAbI tasks, a cellular automaton, and Turing machines; notably solved almost all bAbI tasks and learned Turing machine rules.  
- Recommendation: Reject, but with strong encouragement to revise and resubmit.  
- Major concern: insufficient implementation and analysis detail to judge robustness and generalizability.  
- Comparative analysis is limited and cursory; lacks deep explanation of why GGT-NN outperforms or underperforms on specific tasks.  
- Missing ablation studies—especially on the contribution of “direct reference” for tasks like pathfinding.  
- Reproducibility issues due to absent hyperparameter and training details for Turing machine and cellular automaton experiments.  
- Suggested improvements: detailed ablation on bAbI (direct reference), full training details (learning rate, batch size, architecture), discussion of graph representation choices and alternatives, and qualitative examples of learned graphs.  
- Questions for authors: What are hidden-state sizes and input/output dimensions, initialization, and optimizer for the Turing machine experiments?  
- Questions for authors: Explain the claimed modularity of GGT-NN and provide examples of alternative configurations for different tasks.  
- Questions for authors: What is the computational cost and scalability of GGT-NN compared to baseline methods, and was any specific effort made to address failure on bAbI task 17?
