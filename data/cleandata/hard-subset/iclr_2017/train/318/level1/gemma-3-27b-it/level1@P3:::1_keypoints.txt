- Contribution: Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) to learn and manipulate graph-structured intermediate representations from text; results are compelling (near-perfect on many bAbI tasks, learns rule-based systems).
- Recommendation: Reject.
- Reproducibility: Insufficient implementation details (missing layer sizes, activations, initializations, and detailed graph transformation descriptions); GitHub link alone is inadequate.
- Comparative analysis: Weak and superficial comparisons to prior work; lacks statistical analyses and clear demonstration of advantage given model complexity.
- Experiment scale: Some experiments use small training sets (e.g., 1000 examples), raising overfitting and generalizability concerns.
- Ablations: Missing comprehensive ablation studies to isolate the impact of key components beyond the “direct reference” comparison.
- Architectural justification: Choices like GRU cells and specific graph-transformation designs are not well motivated and may be arbitrary.
- Definition of graph structure: The notion of “graph-structured intermediate representations” is under-specified and needs a formal/practical definition.
- Loss function: Description is too brief; requires a more detailed explanation and rationale for each component.
- Error analysis: Paper lacks analysis of failure cases and reasons for model errors.
- Computational cost: Quadratic scaling noted but mitigation strategies (sparse graphs, approximations) are not discussed.
- Related work: Discussion should be expanded to include more recent advances in graph neural networks and knowledge representation learning.
- Significance of tasks: The paper should better explain why success on rule-discovery tasks (cellular automata, Turing machines) is important.
- Questions for authors: Provide hyperparameters and training details; report computational cost vs baselines; report sensitivity to graph-transformation architectures; report experiments with alternative graph structures; justify choice of GRU versus other recurrent units.
