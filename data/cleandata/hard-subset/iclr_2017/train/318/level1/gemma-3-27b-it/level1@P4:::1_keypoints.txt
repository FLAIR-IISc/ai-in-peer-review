- Contribution: Proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) to learn/manipulate graph-structured intermediate representations.
- Demos: Evaluated on bAbI tasks, a cellular automaton, and Turing machines with competitive but not decisive results.
- Main technical weakness: Description of transformations (Node Addition, State Update, Edge Update, Propagation, Aggregation) and their interplay is vague.
- Missing implementation details: Internal architectures for functions (e.g., f_fwd, f_bwd, f_add) are unspecified.
- Overreliance on prior work: Heavily references Li et al. (2016) without clearly explaining integration of GRUs/GG-NNs into GGT-NN.
- Novelty concerns: Graph-as-intermediate-representation idea is not new; key claim rests on differentiable graph transformations whose impact is not convincingly shown.
- Empirical impact: Performance gains on bAbI are modest relative to model complexity.
- Supervision mismatch: Comparisons to other models are problematic because baselines were trained with different levels of supervision (full graph info).
- Rule-learning experiments: Cellular automaton and Turing machine tasks are interesting but lack comparisons to specialized algorithms.
- Lack of ablation studies: No analysis quantifying contributions of components like direct reference or intermediate propagation.
- Task diversity: Evaluation is limited; broader and more diverse datasets would strengthen claims.
