- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that constructs and modifies graphs from textual input and uses them to produce outputs.
- Achieves strong empirical results: ~95% accuracy on most bAbI tasks and learns rules for cellular automata and Turing machines.
- Recommends acceptance; work is well-motivated and well-situated within GNN literature.
- Identifies a meaningful gap: models that can construct/modify graph structures from unstructured input.
- Proposes a principled architecture providing differentiable graph transformations, building on GNN/GG-NN/GGS-NN prior work.
- Empirical evaluation is rigorous and shows the model outperforms strong baselines, especially on graph-suited tasks (e.g., pathfinding).
- Rule-discovery experiments demonstrate the model’s ability to learn complex graph dynamics.
- Ablation study on direct reference provides clear evidence of its importance for performance.
- Authors thoughtfully acknowledge and discuss model limitations in the conclusion.
- Recommendation: add a more detailed discussion of computational complexity and scalability (notably the quadratic complexity).
- Recommendation: provide concrete examples/visualizations of the learned graph representations.
- Question: How do the authors envision using the model’s graph representations in downstream applications beyond demonstrated tasks?
- Question: Could modifying the graph representation improve performance on task 17 (Positional Reasoning)?
- Question: How does performance scale with the number of node types or greater graph complexity beyond the bAbI tasks?
