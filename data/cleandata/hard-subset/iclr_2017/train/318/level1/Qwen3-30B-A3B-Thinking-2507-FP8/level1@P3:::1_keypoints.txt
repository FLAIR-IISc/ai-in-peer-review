- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that dynamically constructs and modifies graph-structured intermediate representations from text.  
- Shows strong empirical performance on bAbI tasks (95%+ on most tasks) and success on cellular automata and Turing machine rule tasks.  
- Decision: reject due to insufficient theoretical justification and limited empirical evaluation.  
- Main theoretical concern: claims of full differentiability (especially node addition and edge updates) are asserted without rigorous proofs or detailed analysis.  
- Lacks comparison to existing differentiable graph operations (e.g., GCNs), making novelty unclear.  
- Mathematical descriptions are hand-wavy (e.g., unsupported statement about fadd outputting sv = 0 to control node creation).  
- Empirical concerns: training on only 1,000 bAbI examples (vs. 10,000 typically) and using strong graph-level supervision may make comparisons misleading.  
- No ablation studies to isolate contributions of direct reference, node addition, intermediate propagation, etc.  
- Rule-discovery claims for cellular automata and Turing machines are unsubstantiated; model may be memorizing rather than learning true rules.  
- Requested improvements: provide rigorous differentiability proofs/analysis and run ablation studies.  
- Suggested baselines: compare to a model using the same graph structure without graph construction components.  
- Address computational complexity (quadratic scaling) and provide optimizations.  
- Provide stronger evidence that learned graphs reflect underlying rules, not just training-example matching.  
- Key questions for authors: formal proof of differentiability for node addition; performance without strong graph supervision; examples of learned graphs that correspond to true rules.
