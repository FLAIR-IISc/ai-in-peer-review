- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) to construct and modify graph-structured intermediate representations from text.
- Provides a set of differentiable graph transformations (node addition, node update, edge update, propagation, aggregation) composing the GGT-NN.
- Demonstrates strong empirical results: 95% accuracy on 19/20 bAbI tasks with direct reference and high accuracy on cellular automata and Turing machine rule discovery with good generalization.
- Decision: Accept — technically sound contribution addressing a clear gap with strong empirical evidence.
- Novelty: Extends GGS-NNs to allow dynamic construction of graph representations from unstructured input rather than requiring fixed graph inputs.
- Technical soundness: Well-defined transformations, clear architecture, and rigorous experimental methodology including ablations and baselines.
- Experiments: Thorough evaluation with appropriate baselines, detailed ablation studies, and additional tasks showing broader applicability.
- Limitation: Model has quadratic time/space complexity, limiting scalability.
- Suggestion: Include a more detailed computational complexity analysis with specific runtime measurements as graph size grows.
- Suggestion: Provide visualizations and discussion connecting learned graph representations to underlying task structures (e.g., pathfinding).
- Suggestion: Clarify the extent and nature of supervision used for training GGT-NN versus other models (what graph information was provided).
- Suggestion: Analyze why and when direct reference improves performance, with example cases where it gives significant gains.
- Question: Exactly which graph aspects were supervised in bAbI experiments (node/edge types, node strengths, etc.) and how this compares to supervision for models like MemNN?
- Question: Which specific bAbI tasks benefit most from direct reference, and why?
- Question: How does the model generalize to longer sequences in rule-discovery tasks and are there patterns in generalization performance across lengths?
- Question: How might the quadratic time/space complexity be addressed in future work for large-graph applications?
