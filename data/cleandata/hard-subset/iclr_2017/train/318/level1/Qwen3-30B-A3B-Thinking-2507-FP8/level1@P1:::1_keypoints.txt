- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) to learn construction and modification of graph structures from textual input.  
- Uses differentiable graph transformations (node addition, node state update, edge update, propagation, aggregation) to incrementally build/manipulate graphs.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on bAbI QA (95% on 19/20 tasks), learning Rule 30 cellular automaton, and simulating 4-state Turing machines.  
- Explicitly models internal state as labeled graphs, improving interpretability and structured reasoning over prior approaches.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Key reasons for acceptance: fills a gap by enabling dynamic graph construction from unstructured input and shows strong empirical results across domains.  
- Paper is well-motivated and situates clearly relative to GNNs, GGS-NNs, Memory Networks, and DNCs.  
- Empirical results are strong and well-presented, with notable gains on graph-suited tasks (e.g., pathfinding) and successful rule-discovery experiments.  
- Methodology is rigorous with appropriate baselines, clear metrics, and thorough ablation studies (including direct reference comparisons).  
- Provides analysis explaining why the model succeeds on some tasks and struggles on others (e.g., task 17).  
- Major concern: training uses strong supervision (correct graph at each timestep), which may not reflect realistic scenarios; authors acknowledge this.  
- Suggested improvements: add more visual examples of learned graphs to illustrate state transitions.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss quadratic complexity in more depth and propose concrete mitigations (e.g., sparsification, selective processing).  
- Suggested improvements: clarify why direct reference aids handling multiple entity types and how it improves graph-based reasoning.  
- Suggested improvements: show a concrete alternative configuration of transformations for tasks that don’t require node addition.  
- Questions for authors: how does the model perform without per-timestep supervision (only final-answer supervision)?  
- Questions for authors: why is direct reference so beneficial and how could it be integrated into other graph models?  
- Questions for authors: can the model scale to larger or sparser graphs, and could techniques like sparsification or selective connectivity mitigate quadratic costs?
