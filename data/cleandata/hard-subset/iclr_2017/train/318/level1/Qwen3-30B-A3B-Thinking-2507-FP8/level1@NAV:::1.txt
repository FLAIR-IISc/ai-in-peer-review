

This paper introduces the Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), an extension of GGS-NNs that uses graph-structured data as an intermediate representation. The model can learn to construct and modify graphs based on textual input, then use these graphs to produce various outputs. The authors demonstrate the model's effectiveness on the bAbI tasks (achieving 95% accuracy in most tasks) and its ability to learn the rules governing graphical formulations of a cellular automaton and Turing machines.

I recommend acceptance of this paper. The work is well-motivated and properly situated within the literature on graph neural networks. The authors clearly build on previous work (GNNs, GG-NNs, GGS-NNs) while identifying a meaningful gap: the need for models that can construct and modify graph structures based on unstructured input. The proposed GGT-NN architecture provides a principled solution to this problem through its differentiable graph transformations.

The empirical results are scientifically rigorous and compelling. The bAbI task results show the model outperforms several strong baselines on specific tasks (particularly those well-suited to graph representations like pathfinding), and the rule discovery experiments demonstrate the model's ability to learn complex graph dynamics. The ablation study on direct reference provides clear evidence of its importance for model performance. The authors also thoughtfully address the model's limitations in the conclusion.

To strengthen the paper further, I recommend:
1) Adding a more detailed discussion of computational complexity and scalability, particularly regarding the quadratic complexity mentioned in the conclusion.
2) Providing more concrete examples of the graph representations the model learns, perhaps through visualization.

I have a few questions to clarify the work:
1) How do the authors envision the model's graph representations being used in downstream applications beyond the tasks demonstrated?
2) Could the model's performance on task 17 (Positional Reasoning) be improved by modifying the graph representation to better capture the entity relationships?
3) How does the model's performance scale with the number of node types or graph complexity beyond the bAbI tasks?
