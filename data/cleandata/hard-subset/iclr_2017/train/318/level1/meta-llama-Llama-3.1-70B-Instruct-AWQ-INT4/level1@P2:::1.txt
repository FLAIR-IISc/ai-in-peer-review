This paper proposes a novel neural network architecture called the Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), which extends the Gated Graph Sequence Neural Network (GGS-NN) by allowing graph-structured intermediate representations. The GGT-NN model can learn to construct and modify graphs in sophisticated ways based on textual input, and also use the graphs to produce a variety of outputs.

**Decision:** Accept

**Key Reasons:**

1. The paper tackles a specific and well-defined problem of learning graph-structured state transitions from textual input, which is a challenging and relevant task in the field of natural language processing.
2. The approach is well-motivated and builds upon existing work on graph neural networks and sequence-to-sequence models, providing a clear and logical extension of these ideas.

**Supporting Arguments:**

* The paper provides a clear and detailed explanation of the GGT-NN model, including its components, architecture, and training procedure.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the GGT-NN model on a range of tasks, including the bAbI tasks, a simple cellular automaton, and a family of Turing machines.
* The results show that the GGT-NN model is able to learn complex graph-structured representations and achieve high accuracy on these tasks, often outperforming other state-of-the-art models.
* The paper also provides a thorough analysis of the model's performance, including an examination of the effects of different hyperparameters and training procedures.

**Additional Feedback:**

* One potential limitation of the paper is that the GGT-NN model requires a significant amount of supervision, including full graph information, to train effectively. It would be interesting to explore ways to reduce the level of supervision required, such as using semi-supervised or unsupervised learning techniques.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other related work, such as graph convolutional networks and lifted relational neural networks.
* The authors may also want to consider exploring the application of the GGT-NN model to more complex and real-world tasks, such as question answering or text summarization.

**Questions for the Authors:**

* Can you provide more details on how the GGT-NN model handles out-of-vocabulary words or unseen entities in the input text?
* How do you plan to address the issue of scalability, as the model's time and space complexity increase with the size of the input graph?
* Have you considered using other types of graph neural networks, such as graph attention networks or graph convolutional networks, as part of the GGT-NN model?
