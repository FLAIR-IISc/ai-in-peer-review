- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), extending GGS-NN to allow graph-structured intermediate representations.  
- Combines differentiable graph transformations with a recurrent input model to incrementally construct graphs from natural language.  
- Model can output a final graph or use the intermediate graph to generate natural language.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, primarily due to the novelty and potential impact of GGT-NN.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on multiple tasks, including bAbI and rule discovery tasks.  
- Paper gives a clear, detailed explanation of the architecture and differentiable transformations.  
- Provides thorough result analysis and comparisons with state-of-the-art models.  
- Requests additional experiments and evaluations on more complex tasks and larger datasets.  
- Asks for a detailed analysis of computational complexity, scalability, and time/space efficiency.  
- Suggests a clearer discussion of relationships with other graph-based models (GNNs, GCNs).  
- Requests more insight into hyperparameter choices and model sensitivity to them.  
- Recommends more visualizations and examples of graphs produced, especially for complex tasks (cellular automaton, Turing machine).  
- Suggests elaborating potential applications (NLP, computer vision, graph-based ML).  
- Asks whether GGT-NN can handle tasks requiring reasoning about multiple graphs (graph-to-graph translation, graph QA).  
- Questions how the model handles graphs of varying sizes and complexities.  
- Asks whether GGT-NN can generate graphs from scratch versus only modifying existing graphs.  
- Inquires about the model’s robustness to noisy or incomplete graph data.
