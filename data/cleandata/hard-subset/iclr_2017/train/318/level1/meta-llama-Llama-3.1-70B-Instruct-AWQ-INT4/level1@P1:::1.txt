This paper introduces the Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), an extension of Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks (GGS-NNs) that uses graph-structured data as an intermediate representation. The model can learn to construct and modify graphs in sophisticated ways based on textual input, and also to use the graphs to produce a variety of outputs.

I decide to **accept** this paper. The two key reasons for this choice are:

1. The paper tackles a specific and well-defined problem, namely learning graph-structured state transitions from textual input. This problem is well-motivated and relevant to the field of natural language processing and graph-based machine learning.

2. The paper provides a clear and well-supported approach to solving this problem, including a detailed description of the GGT-NN model and its components, as well as experimental results demonstrating its effectiveness on a range of tasks.

Supporting arguments for the reasons for the decision:

* The paper provides a thorough review of the relevant literature, including previous work on graph neural networks and sequence-to-sequence models. This helps to situate the GGT-NN model within the broader context of the field.
* The GGT-NN model itself is well-defined and easy to understand, with a clear explanation of its components and how they interact. This makes it easy for readers to follow the paper and understand the approach.
* The experimental results are strong, with the GGT-NN model achieving high accuracy on a range of tasks, including the bAbI tasks and the cellular automaton task. This demonstrates the effectiveness of the approach and suggests that it has the potential to be widely applicable.
