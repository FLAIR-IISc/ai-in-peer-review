- Paper proposes Gated Graph Transformer NN (GGT-NN) that builds and reasons over an explicit, differentiable graph constructed from text.  
- Key technical ideas: soft node-type distributions, node/edge strengths, differentiable graph transforms (add, update, edge update, propagate, aggregate), and strong supervised training with permutation-invariant node addition loss.  
- Empirically strong on bAbI (95%+ on 19/20 with direct reference) and can learn transitions for synthetic systems (Rule 30 CA, small TMs) with some generalization.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main reason 1: Core results rely on privileged, task-specific strong supervision (ground-truth graphs, engineered schemas), so end-to-end effectiveness is unclear.  
- Main reason 2: Paper lacks experimental rigor — missing ablations, hyperparameters, training details, compute/memory costs, seed variance, and fair baselines, undermining claims of generality/superiority.  
- Strength: Problem motivation and modular graph-construction view are timely and well articulated; extension beyond fixed graphs is valuable.  
- Positioning: Qualitative comparison to GNN/GGS-NN, MemNN, DNC; important quantitative baselines (e.g., GGS-NN with text-to-graph parser, supervised MemNN) are missing.  
- bAbI critique: Uses converted “Knowledge” graphs and task-specific history engineering plus direct-reference entity linking, deviating from standard end-to-end QA and inflating comparisons.  
- Without direct reference performance drops substantially (95% on only 9/20 tasks); no experiments trained end-to-end from answers only are provided.  
- Rule-discovery critique: Synthetic tasks use constrained schemas and short horizons, with no baselines or failure-mode analysis and limited rollout-generalization evidence.  
- Rigor critique: Key implementation details (hidden sizes, node-add limits, propagation steps, optimizer, training time/memory) and ablations (transformation/supervision contributions) are missing.  
- Claim vs evidence: The claim that internal graph construction from text is effective is only supported under privileged supervision, limiting impact.  
- Recommended experiments: End-to-end training (with/without direct reference), semi-supervised variants, detailed ablations, and comparisons to equally supervised baselines.  
- Complexity/scalability suggestions: Report time/space as functions of |V|,|E|, actual graph sizes, throughput, memory, and explore sparse/top-k edge updates.  
- Robustness suggestions: Provide accuracy vs sequence/graph size, multiple seeds, error analyses, and sensitivity to history-linked-list engineering.  
- Reproducibility/clarity suggestions: Consolidate hyperparameters, propagation steps, node/edge vocabularies, stopping criteria for Tadd, and number of nodes added per step.  
- Visualization suggestion: Show qualitative learned graph trajectories for bAbI to support interpretability claims.  
- Key questions for authors: limits on nodes added per sentence and parsimony, definition/learning of node/edge types, handling of OOV/paraphrases with direct reference, end-to-end results, main hyperparameters/training cost, sensitivity to history engineering, node identity stability, train/test tape lengths for CA/TM, seed averaging, and feasibility of sparse edge updates.
