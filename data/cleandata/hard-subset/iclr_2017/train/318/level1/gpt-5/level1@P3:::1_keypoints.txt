- Proposal: Gated Graph Transformer Neural Networks (GGT-NNs) that make graphs differentiable/editable with soft node types, existence strengths, and multi-type edge strengths.
- Core contributions: a differentiable graph formalism, composable graph transformations (Tadd, Th, Th,direct, TC, Tprop, Trepr), and a text-to-graph training strategy with permutation-invariant supervised loss.
- Empirical claims: high accuracy on many bAbI tasks (notably pathfinding and induction) with strong graph supervision; learns CA and small Turing-machine dynamics with some horizon generalization.
- Decision: Reject.
- Key reason 1: Heavy reliance on strong supervision (gold graphs and direct entity matching) undermines the significance and comparability of bAbI results to end-to-end baselines.
- Key reason 2: Empirical evaluation lacks ablations and controlled comparisons to convincingly show editable graph state advantages beyond task-specific supervision.
- Problem importance: Tackles learning to construct/update graph-structured latent states from text — timely and relevant for relational reasoning.
- Novelty: Incremental over GGS-NN — main novelty is making the graph itself differentiable and editable plus text-to-graph integration, but experimental dissection is insufficient.
- bAbI concerns: Strong supervision (knowledge objects, direct reference, teacher-forcing) simplifies grounding and precludes fair comparison to end-to-end models; no partially-supervised or supervision-controlled baselines shown.
- Rule-discovery concerns: CA and Turing experiments are promising but lack per-component ablations, error analysis, and details on dataset/rule distributions and train/test difficulty.
- Training mismatch: Replacing predicted nodes/edges with gold values during forward passes creates exposure-bias and train/test mismatch that is not analyzed.
- Loss/optimization: The set-to-sequence hard permutation assignment is non-smooth; alternatives and stability analyses are not explored.
- Complexity/scalability: Tprop is O(|V|^2Y); scalability is acknowledged but not empirically evaluated (no time/memory vs. |V| curves).
- Reproducibility: Many architectural/hyperparameter details are missing from the main text; code is referenced but not assessed.
- Suggested ablations: Isolate contributions of Tadd, TC, Th,direct, Tprop, Trepr; test effects of disabling propagation or fixing nodes/edges.
- Suggested supervision experiments: Evaluate no-graph, partial-graph, and varying supervision regimes to quantify gaps and sample efficiency.
- Baseline controls: Train MemNN/MemN2N/DNC with equivalent strong supervision to fairly compare where advantages originate.
- Robustness: Replace direct string matching with learned/noisy entity linking to test beyond bAbI’s toy setting.
- Reporting requests: Provide compute and memory requirements vs. |V| and sequence length; include timing/throughput.
- Generalization analysis: Measure performance vs. horizon, rule complexity, states/symbols, and analyze failure modes in rule-discovery tasks.
- Training fixes: Address gold-substitution exposure bias (e.g., scheduled sampling or fully predictive rollouts with auxiliary losses).
- Questions for authors: performance without graph supervision and with/without Th,direct; baselines trained with same supervision; sensitivity to max nodes/edge types; distribution and generalization of Turing-machine rules; empirical scaling (time/memory) and max practical graph size; ablations showing necessity of Tprop vs. TC+Th only.
