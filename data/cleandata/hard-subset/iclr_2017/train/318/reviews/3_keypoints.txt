- Proposes extending Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks to learn complex, differentiable graph transformations as internal representations.  
- Introduces five differentiable transformation types learned in a supervised setting with graph states provided at each timestep.  
- Presents a model instance that iteratively updates an internal graph from a sequence input and applies it to QA tasks (e.g., bAbI) with promising results.  
- Strength: maintains evolving, complex graph states while remaining fully differentiable and trainable by gradient descent.  
- Strength: effectively mixes continuous and symbolic representations and is more general than fixed-shape memory models (Memory Networks, NTM).  
- Main concern: training requires ground-truth graph states at each timestep, limiting applicability to tasks where such supervision is available.  
- Additional concern: paper is dense and may be better suited to a journal than a conference, hindering readability.
