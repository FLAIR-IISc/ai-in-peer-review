- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) with differentiable graph transformations for dynamically constructed and modified graph-structured internal states.  
- Demonstrated on bAbI tasks, cellular automata, and Turing machines, showing ability to learn complex state update rules.  
- Core contribution: flexible, learnable graph-based representation and manipulation of knowledge from data.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — novel, promising, and empirically compelling.  
- Novelty: moves beyond fixed-graph GNNs by learning to construct and modify graphs dynamically.  
- Strong empirical results: competitive/exceeding SOTA on bAbI, successful rule learning on cellular automata and Turing machines, notably strong on pathfinding (task 19).  
- Presentation: paper is well-written with clear architecture explanation and helpful appendix.  
- Ablation studies: comparisons with/without direct reference provide useful insight into that component’s effectiveness.  
- Scalability concern: needs deeper discussion of computational costs and mitigation strategies (e.g., sparsity, node sampling) due to quadratic graph-operation scaling.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: paper lacks analysis of sensitivity to learning rates, hidden dimensions, and transformation configurations.  
- Error analysis: more detailed error analysis is needed, especially for lower performance on task 17 (positional reasoning).  
- Visualizations: recommend showing learned graph visualizations for cellular automaton and Turing machine experiments.  
- Comparison to alternatives: suggest discussing relationships to graph autoencoders and graph VAEs that also learn graph representations.  
- Question on scalability: what specific training/inference bottlenecks arise with larger graphs and were any mitigation techniques tried?  
- Question on task 17: could different graph representations or node-type structures improve positional reasoning performance?  
- Question on direct reference: does its benefit vary across bAbI tasks or ever hurt performance?  
- Question on rule discovery interpretability: are learned graphs interpretable with regard to underlying rules, and how to assess correctness beyond output matching?  
- Question on applications: what additional real-world domains do authors envision for GGT-NN?
