- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) enabling dynamic graph structures via differentiable graph transformations.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — novel, well-motivated, and empirically promising.  
- Novelty: Learns and modifies graph structures rather than relying on predefined graphs.  
- Motivation: Clear rationale and applicability to diverse data types (maps, data structures, etc.).  
- Technical soundness: Differentiable transformations with GRU-based state updates and attention aggregation are reasonable and flexible.  
- Empirical validation: Strong results on bAbI, cellular automata, and Turing machines; competitive with SOTA on small datasets.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written, though some transformation descriptions and appendices are dense.  
- Ablation studies: Needed to measure contribution of components (e.g., direct reference, propagation).  
- Scalability: Authors should address bottlenecks and discuss strategies (sparse graphs, efficient propagation).  
- Visualization: More examples of learned graphs (especially Turing machine and cellular automaton) would aid interpretation.  
- Comparison: Needs discussion of relation to other graph-construction methods (e.g., knowledge graph embeddings).  
- Appendix organization: Recommend consolidating and clarifying headings to streamline details.  
- Question — scalability: What specific bottlenecks prevent scaling to larger graphs and how might they be addressed?  
- Question — transformations: Were combinations of transformations systematically explored; which are most crucial per task?  
- Question — loss sensitivity: How sensitive is performance to weighting between edge loss and node-adjustment loss?  
- Question — direct reference: Could direct reference be replaced by a general attention mechanism without explicit node-type labels?  
- Question — Turing machine: How was the Turing machine initial state represented in the graph; please provide more details.
