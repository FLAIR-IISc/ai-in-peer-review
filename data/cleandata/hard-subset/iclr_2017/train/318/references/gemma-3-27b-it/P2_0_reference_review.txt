## Review of "LEARNING GRAPHICAL STATE TRANSITIONS"

**1. Summary of Claims**

This paper introduces the Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), an extension of GG-NNs and GGS-NNs that allows for dynamically constructed and modified graph-structured intermediate representations. The core idea is to utilize differentiable graph transformations (node addition, state update, edge update, propagation, and aggregation) to build and manipulate a graph representing the state of a system based on input data. The authors demonstrate the GGT-NN's capabilities on the bAbI task suite, a 1D cellular automaton, and Turing machines, showcasing its ability to learn complex state update rules and reason about structured data.

**2. Decision & Key Reasons**

**Reject.** While the idea of dynamically learning graph structures is interesting, the paper lacks sufficient novelty to justify publication at ICLR. The experimental results, though promising, don’t convincingly outperform existing strong baselines in a significant manner, and the improvements seem heavily reliant on task-specific customizations and strong supervision. The presentation of the approach is also unnecessarily dense and difficult to follow.

**3. Supporting Arguments**

* **Limited Novelty:** The core contribution – learning graph structures – is not entirely new. Several works (cited in the paper) already explore graph neural networks and methods for manipulating graph structures. The differentiability of the transformations, while important, feels incremental. The key claim of *building* graphs from unstructured input is not thoroughly demonstrated to be superior to other approaches.
* **Incremental Improvement & Strong Supervision:** The performance gains on the bAbI tasks are marginal, and often achieved with strong supervision (providing the correct graph structure during training).  The comparison to existing state-of-the-art methods, while provided, doesn't demonstrate a clear and substantial advantage. While the authors acknowledge this, the lack of significant improvement relative to other strong baselines raises concerns. The strong reliance on direct reference feels like a significant dependency and reduces the generality of the approach.
* **Presentation & Clarity:** The paper is dense and difficult to follow. The introduction of numerous transformations and their intricate details, coupled with the complex mathematical notation, creates a significant barrier to understanding. The organization is somewhat disjointed, making it hard to grasp the overall contribution quickly. The figures, while present, are often unhelpful and lack clear labels.
* **Lack of Ablation Studies:** The paper presents results with and without direct reference, but lacks thorough ablation studies to understand the contribution of each transformation component.  How much does each transformation individually contribute to the overall performance? This information is crucial for assessing the effectiveness of the proposed architecture.

**4. Additional Feedback (for improvement)**

* **Focus on Novelty:** The authors should better highlight the specific and unique aspects of their approach compared to existing work. What makes the GGT-NN fundamentally different and more powerful than existing graph neural network architectures?
* **Simplify Presentation:** The paper needs significant streamlining. Reducing mathematical notation, providing clearer explanations, and using more intuitive language would significantly improve readability. Focus on conveying the core ideas without getting bogged down in implementation details.
* **More Rigorous Evaluation:** Conduct a more thorough comparison with strong baselines. Consider evaluating on more challenging datasets that require true graph construction from unstructured input.  Provide a detailed ablation study to assess the contribution of each component of the GGT-NN.
* **Explain the Trade-offs:** Discuss the computational cost of the GGT-NN (quadratic scaling with graph size) and potential strategies for mitigating this issue.
* **Clarify Supervision:**  Explicitly state the degree of supervision used in each experiment.  Explore weakly supervised or unsupervised learning scenarios to demonstrate the true potential of the approach.

**5. Questions for the Authors**

* You mention the quadratic scaling of the GGT-NN as a potential limitation. Have you explored any strategies to address this, such as sparse edge connections or node sampling?
* The performance on Task 17 (Positional Reasoning) is significantly lower than other tasks. What are the specific challenges of this task that hinder the GGT-NN’s performance?
* How sensitive is the performance of the model to the initialization of the graph structure?  Does pre-training the graph transformations help?
* Can you provide a more intuitive explanation of the role of each graph transformation in the context of the bAbI tasks? For example, how does node addition help with reasoning about new entities?
* The direct reference mechanism appears to be crucial for performance. What are the limitations of this approach, and how could it be extended to handle more complex forms of textual input?
