- Paper proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) for differentiable graph construction and manipulation.  
- Demonstrated on bAbI, 1D cellular automata, and Turing machines to learn state update rules and structured reasoning.  
- Recommendation: Reject — novelty insufficient for ICLR and empirical gains not convincingly strong.  
- Limited novelty: learning graph structures and differentiable transformations seen in prior work; claim of building graphs from unstructured input not clearly superior.  
- Incremental improvements and heavy reliance on strong supervision (direct reference) reduce generality and advantage over baselines.  
- Experimental results show marginal gains and lack clear outperforming of strong baselines.  
- Presentation is dense, with complex notation and disjoint organization that hinders clarity; figures lack helpful labels.  
- Missing thorough ablation studies to quantify each transformation’s contribution.  
- Suggest clarifying unique contributions relative to existing GNNs and emphasizing what makes GGT-NN fundamentally different.  
- Recommend simplifying presentation, reducing notation, and improving intuitive explanations and figure clarity.  
- Advise more rigorous evaluation: stronger baselines, harder datasets requiring true graph construction, and detailed ablations.  
- Request discussion of computational trade-offs (quadratic scaling) and mitigation strategies.  
- Ask to clarify supervision level per experiment and explore weakly/unsupervised settings.  
- Questions: have you tried sparse edges or node sampling to address scaling?  
- Questions: why poor performance on Task 17 (Positional Reasoning) and sensitivity to graph initialization or pretraining?  
- Questions: provide intuitive roles for each transformation (e.g., node addition) and limitations/extensions of the direct reference mechanism.
