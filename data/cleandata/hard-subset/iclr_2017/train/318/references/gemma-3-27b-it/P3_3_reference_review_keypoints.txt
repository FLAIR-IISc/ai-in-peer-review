- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that uses differentiable graph transformations (node add, state update, edge update, propagation, aggregation) to build and modify graphs from text and graphs.
- Evaluated on bAbI, a 1D cellular automaton, and Turing machines, often outperforming existing methods, especially on tasks naturally suited to graphs.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions due to novelty and strong results.
- Novelty: Enables learning to construct and modify graphs, extending beyond fixed-structure GNNs.
- Empirical strength: Competitive or superior performance on bAbI and demonstrated ability to learn complex procedural rules on CA and Turing tasks.
- Ablation study: Comparison of models with and without direct reference is insightful.
- Method clarity: Detailed architecture, graph operations, training procedure, and helpful pseudocode provided.
- Literature positioning: Good overview and clear articulation of how GGT-NN differs from prior work.
- Computational complexity: Quadratic scaling with graph size; needs discussion of mitigation strategies (e.g., sparsity, approximations).
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Paper lacks analysis of hyperparameter tuning and its impact on performance.
- Visualizations: More dynamic visualizations of learned transformations (edge add/remove over time) would clarify model behavior.
- Error analysis: Deeper analysis needed for failures on bAbI task 17 (Positional Reasoning) to diagnose limitations.
- Knowledge representation: Should discuss what types of “knowledge” the learned graph structures encode and how that supports reasoning.
- Scalability question: What is the largest graph size the implementation can handle and plans for scaling?
- Transformation selection question: How are transformation combinations/order determined (fixed, learned, or manual) and could a learned policy help?
- Direct reference question: Is there a quantifiable correlation between direct-reference quality (e.g., entity recognition accuracy) and overall performance?
- Generalization question: How readily can the architecture adapt to other domains beyond QA and rule discovery, and what modifications would be needed?
- Activation functions question: Why use tanh; were alternatives like ReLU explored and what were the results?
