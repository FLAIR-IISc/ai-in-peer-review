- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) enabling differentiable graph-structured intermediate representations and dynamic graph transformations.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Novelty: Key contribution is learning differentiable graph transformations rather than relying on fixed graphs.
- Technical soundness: Architecture and mathematical formulation are well-defined, integrating GRUs and attention effectively.
- Empirical validation: Competitive bAbI results and strong performance on cellular automaton and Turing machine rule-discovery tasks.
- Presentation: Paper is clear and includes helpful implementation details in the appendix.
- Ablation studies: More comprehensive ablations (e.g., removing node addition or propagation) are needed to isolate component contributions.
- Scalability: Paper acknowledges quadratic scaling; authors should discuss strategies like sparse representations or sampling.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Report sensitivity to learning rates, hidden sizes, and propagation steps to strengthen empirical claims.
- Visualization: Provide learned graph visualizations (especially for Turing machine and cellular automaton tasks) to illustrate representations.
- Practical connections: Discuss links between learned transformations and graph-database operations (e.g., Cypher) to emphasize real-world relevance.
- Q1 — node/edge strengths: How sensitive is performance to constraints on node/edge strengths and would relaxing them help?
- Q2 — computational cost: Provide detailed complexity analysis per transformation and identify the most expensive operations.
- Q3 — generalization: Do learned representations vary systematically across different rules, and can the model generalize to unseen rules in the same class?
- Q4 — direct reference: What negative effects does direct reference introduce (e.g., overreliance on surface features vs. abstract reasoning)?
