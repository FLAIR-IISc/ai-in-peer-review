- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that builds and manipulates differentiable graph-structured internal representations (node add, state/edge update, propagation, aggregation).

- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — novel approach with strong empirical results.

- Novelty: GGT-NN can construct/modify internal graphs rather than operating on a fixed graph, which is a genuine contribution.

- Strong empirical validation: Successful results on bAbI tasks, a 1D cellular automaton, and Turing machines; improved performance on pathfinding (Task 19).

- Well-motivated: Clear rationale for using graphs as state representations and a natural extension of GG-NNs/GGS-NNs.

- Clear technical description: Detailed, mathematically sound presentation with helpful pseudocode (Algorithm 1).

- Suggestion: Add ablation studies to quantify the impact of each graph transformation (e.g., remove propagation or edge updates).

- Suggestion: Provide a deeper discussion of scalability limits and mitigation strategies (e.g., sparse representations, hierarchical transformations).

- Suggestion: Include more visualizations of learned graphs and their temporal evolution, especially for the Turing machine experiments.

- Suggestion: Clarify relation to dynamic graph neural networks that learn graph structure and other related methods.

- Question: Have you explored methods to reduce propagation cost (limiting hops, attention-based focus, etc.)?

- Question: Why is performance on Task 17 (Positional Reasoning) notably lower, and can architectural or training changes address it?

- Question: How was the initial Turing machine tape encoded as a graph — manually engineered or learned end-to-end?

- Question: Provide concrete downstream applications that would benefit from graph-structured internal states beyond the presented tasks.
