- Paper proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) to learn and manipulate graph-structured internal states and outputs via differentiable graph transformations.  
- Decision: Reject — interesting idea but experimental validation and presentation are insufficient to support claims.  
- Comparisons to existing methods are limited and not comprehensive, making claimed improvements unconvincing.  
- Performance likely benefits from strong supervision (providing correct graph at each timestep), which may overstate model advantages.  
- The model introduces many graph transformations and parameters without clear justification for each, raising complexity concerns.  
- Paper is verbose and includes excessive background that obscures the core novelty; figures and descriptions could be clearer.  
- Recommend ablation studies to measure the contribution of each graph transformation.  
- Suggest experiments with reduced supervision or reinforcement learning to test learning without strong graph-level labels.  
- Advise comparisons against more recent GNNs (e.g., GAT, GraphSAGE) with standardized setups and possible pre-training.  
- Request deeper analysis and visualization of learned graphs to interpret the model’s internal reasoning.  
- Ask for clearer motivation of when GGT-NN is preferable and which tasks specifically benefit from dynamic graph state construction.  
- Question sensitivity to supervision level and expected performance drop with less graph supervision.  
- Ask about scalability given quadratic costs and potential optimizations for larger graphs.  
- Inquire how transformation selection affects performance and whether a principled selection method exists.  
- Request methods for interpreting learned transformation parameters and evidence of generalization to unseen tasks without retraining.
