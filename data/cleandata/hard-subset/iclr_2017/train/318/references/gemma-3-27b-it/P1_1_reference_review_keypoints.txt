- Proposal of Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that learns and manipulates dynamic graph-structured internal states via differentiable graph transformations.  
- Accept with minor revisions; novel approach and promising empirical results.  
- Core contribution: differentiable graph transformations (node add, state update, edge update, propagation, aggregation) enabling end-to-end learning of graph manipulation.  
- Strong empirical performance: competitive on bAbI and successful rule discovery for cellular automata and Turing machines.  
- Well-written and organized manuscript with useful appendix implementation details.  
- Modularity of transformations promotes reusability and adaptability to new tasks.  
- Recommend more extensive ablation studies to isolate contributions of individual graph transformations.  
- Request a deeper scalability analysis, potential optimizations, and computational complexity discussion.  
- Ask for hyperparameter search details and sensitivity analysis to improve reproducibility.  
- Suggest visualizations of learned graphs for qualitative insight into internal representations.  
- Ask for a comparison and discussion of relations/tradeoffs with recent Graph Transformer work.  
- Question about impact of arbitrary node ordering during node adjustment and exploration of alternatives.  
- Question on robustness to noise or incomplete inputs in cellular automaton and Turing machine experiments.  
- Request elaboration on combining supervised and unsupervised training and regularizers to encourage meaningful graph structure.  
- Ask for memory footprint details and comparison to other GNN approaches for larger graphs.
