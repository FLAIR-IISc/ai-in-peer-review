- Paper proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) for dynamic internal graph representations and differentiable graph transformations.
- Decision: Reject — interesting idea but results don’t convincingly advance the state of the art given strong supervision.
- bAbI results show limited, task-specific improvements over strong baselines (MemNN, DMN+), not justifying added complexity.
- Heavy supervision: providing correct graph at each timestep undermines claim that the model learns graph structure from unstructured data.
- Complexity and scalability: model scales quadratically with graph size, limiting practical applicability to large graphs.
- Lack of ablation studies: individual component contributions (e.g., direct reference) are not systematically evaluated.
- Presentation is overly long and detailed, making it hard to extract the core contributions.
- Suggest exploring weaker supervision strategies (edge/node-level, reinforcement learning) to reduce reliance on full graph supervision.
- Recommend addressing scalability with sparse representations or approximate propagation methods.
- Ask for deeper analysis of failure cases beyond Task 17 to better understand model limitations.
- Suggest simplifying the manuscript and trimming appendices to focus on core ideas and results.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to graph/node embedding initialization?
- Questions for authors: detailed computational cost comparison versus baselines as graph size grows?
- Questions for authors: why GRUs for node updates/input processing and were other architectures tried?
- Questions for authors: how were initial states and rules chosen/tuned in cellular automaton and Turing machine experiments?
- Questions for authors: how to extend the approach to handle continuous node or edge attributes?
