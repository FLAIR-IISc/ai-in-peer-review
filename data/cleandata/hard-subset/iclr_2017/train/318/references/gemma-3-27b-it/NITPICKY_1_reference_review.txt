This paper introduces the Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), an extension of GG-NNs and GGS-NNs that allows for graph-structured intermediate representations and outputs. The core idea is to use differentiable graph transformations to manipulate an internal graph state, enabling the model to process both graph-structured and textual data and perform tasks like question answering, cellular automaton modeling, and Turing machine simulation. The authors demonstrate the model's capabilities on the bAbI tasks, a 1D cellular automaton, and 4-state Turing machines, showcasing its ability to learn complex update rules and achieve strong performance.

**Reject.** While the core idea of using differentiable graph transformations is interesting, the paper suffers from a multitude of minor flaws and inconsistencies, coupled with a lack of rigorous analysis and justification for many design choices. The sheer number of these issues undermines confidence in the results and the validity of the claims.

Here’s a breakdown of my concerns:

**1. Lack of Rigor and Justification:** Many design choices appear arbitrary. For instance, the specific architecture used in Algorithm 1 feels ad-hoc. Why this particular configuration of transformations? What experiments were done to explore alternative configurations? The choice of GRUs within the transformations is also not deeply justified. The authors state results “indicate” the benefit of direct reference, but a more systematic ablation study is needed. The claim that the chosen graphical format for the cellular automaton and Turing machine tasks is "natural" feels unsubstantiated. There's a general lack of analysis regarding the computational complexity of the proposed model; acknowledging the quadratic scaling is insufficient without exploring potential mitigations.

**2. Numerous Minor Flaws & Inconsistencies:**
*   **Notation:** Throughout the paper, notation is inconsistent. For example,  "h(t)v" and "h(t-1)v" are used inconsistently and it’s unclear when indices are subscripts vs. superscripts. There's also ambiguity in the use of Cv,v′,y – does this represent the belief in an edge *of type y* from v to v′, or the overall strength of the edge?
*   **Mathematical Details:** Equation 5 in section 3 lacks proper explanation of the functions ffwdy and fbwd y. What are their inputs and outputs? The constraints on the connectivity matrix C are stated but not fully leveraged in the described transformations. How is the normalization ∑N j=1 xv,j = 1 enforced?
*   **Implementation Details:** The appendix describes the transformations, but the implementation details are often vague. For example, "a simple 2-layer fully connected network" lacks specifics. What activation functions are used? How many hidden units are there?
*   **Experimental Setup:** The experimental details are sparse. What hyperparameters were used for training? How was the validation set used? Were any regularization techniques applied? The comparison to other models is presented without sufficient context. Different models were trained with different levels of supervision; this needs to be addressed more explicitly.
*   **Typos and Grammatical Errors:** Numerous minor typos and grammatical errors throughout the manuscript detract from its professionalism.

**3. Overstated Claims & Lack of Critical Discussion:** The claims regarding the performance on the bAbI tasks are somewhat overstated. While the model achieves good results, the results are not significantly better than existing state-of-the-art methods, especially considering the strong supervision used. The authors downplay the limitations of the model, particularly its scalability issues. The discussion of related work is superficial and doesn't fully acknowledge the strengths and weaknesses of competing approaches.

**Additional Feedback (for potential revision):**

*   **Clarify the contribution:** Focus on the novelty of the differentiable graph transformations. The GGT-NN architecture is a consequence of this, but the transformations themselves are the key contribution.
*   **Conduct a thorough ablation study:** Systematically evaluate the impact of different design choices, such as the use of GRUs, the direct reference mechanism, and the specific graph transformations.
*   **Provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity:** Explore techniques to mitigate the quadratic scaling issue.
*   **Improve the writing and presentation:** Address the numerous inconsistencies, typos, and grammatical errors. Provide more detailed experimental details and a more critical discussion of the results.
*   **Justify the graphical representations:** Explain why the chosen graphical representations for the cellular automaton and Turing machine tasks are effective.



**Questions for the Authors:**

*   What is the computational cost of applying the graph transformations to a graph with *n* nodes and *m* edges?
*   How sensitive are the results to the choice of hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate, hidden unit size, number of propagation steps)?
*   Could you provide more detail on the implementation of the functions ffwdy and fbwd y in Equation 5?
*   What alternative graph transformations were considered, and why were the ones presented in the paper chosen?
*   How do the results change if the model is trained with less supervision (e.g., without providing the correct graph at each timestep)?
