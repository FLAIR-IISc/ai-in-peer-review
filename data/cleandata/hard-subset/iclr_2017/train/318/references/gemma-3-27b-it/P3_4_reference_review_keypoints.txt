- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) to learn dynamic graph-structured intermediate representations and operations.  
- Demonstrated on bAbI tasks, a 1D cellular automaton, and Turing machines to show rule-learning and graph-construction abilities.  
- Decision: Reject — contributions and empirical evidence not strong enough for ICLR.  
- bAbI improvements are modest and sometimes worse than strong baselines, so gains aren’t clearly significant.  
- Model relies heavily on strong supervision (correct graph at each timestep), limiting generalizability and fair comparison.  
- Ablation study is limited; more granular analysis needed on the role of each graph transformation (node add vs edge update, etc.).  
- Paper glosses over reasons for failure on Task 17; deeper analysis of graph representation limits is needed.  
- GGT-NN is architecturally complex and the paper doesn’t justify complexity relative to performance gains.  
- Computational cost discussion is brief and lacks exploration of optimizations or detailed scalability analysis.  
- Cellular automaton and Turing machine experiments feel like proofs-of-concept rather than rigorous demonstrations of generalization.  
- Needs comparison to more recent graph architectures (e.g., Graph Transformers) as stronger baselines.  
- Should evaluate weaker supervision or unsupervised settings to show practical applicability.  
- Recommend visualizing learned graphs/transformations to improve explainability and trust.  
- Urges a thorough error analysis on bAbI to pinpoint reasoning failure modes.  
- Concern about reliance on perfect “direct reference” (named entities); suggest alternatives for ambiguous language.  
- Questions for authors: alternatives to GRU for transformations, deeper analysis of Task 17 failures, sensitivity to initial graph structure, memory footprint and bottlenecks for large graphs, and specifics/estimates for proposed computational optimizations.
