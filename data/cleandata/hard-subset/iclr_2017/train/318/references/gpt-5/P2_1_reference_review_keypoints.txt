- Paper proposes learning and updating an explicit, differentiable graph-structured state from text for reasoning/generation, allowing dynamic nodes/edges/strengths.
- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) with differentiable operators: node addition, node update (including direct reference), edge update, propagation, and aggregation.
- Nodes/edges carry continuous existence/strengths; sentences processed by a GRU and graph transforms applied per sentence; training uses strong supervision with permutation-invariant node assignment.
- Empirically, GGT-NN achieves high accuracy on bAbI with access to gold generator knowledge and shows rule-learning on synthetic CA and Turing-machine tasks with some generalization.
- Decision: Reject.
- Major reason: Heavy reliance on strong, engineered supervision (full gold graphs and string-match direct references) undermines claim of learning graphs from unstructured input.
- Comparisons are unfair: GGT-NN uses stronger supervision than baselines like MemN2N/DNC, so end-to-end performance without graph supervision is unclear.
- Ablation and robustness analyses are missing; model without direct reference struggles on many tasks, indicating dependency on privileged signals.
- Experimental reporting lacks variance, hyperparameter sensitivity, and detailed settings (propagation steps, hidden sizes, edge types).
- Novelty is incremental: dynamic differentiable graph construction is a natural extension of prior GNNs, with design choices feeling like engineering variants rather than a fundamental advance.
- Related work engagement is incomplete; some contemporary dynamic-graph and structured-state works are missing or poorly cited/formatted.
- Reproducibility concerns: essential training details are omitted from main text and code/documentation status unclear.
- Practicality/scalability concerns: O(|V|^2) time/space costs, no empirical scaling analysis, and fragility to natural-language variation due to direct-reference reliance.
- Suggested improvement: evaluate end-to-end learning on bAbI with only answer supervision and ablate each transformation (node add, edge reset, propagation depth, direct reference).
- Replace string-matching direct reference with learned mention grounding (e.g., attention) and test robustness to paraphrase/multiword entities.
- Provide fair baselines given same supervision (e.g., GGS-NN over gold graphs, seq2seq graph parser) and include qualitative visualizations/failure analyses.
- Report full training details, multiple seeds, variance, and sensitivity to hyperparameters; clarify constraint enforcement on node/edge strengths and types.
- Address scalability via sparse adjacency, neighborhood sampling, or other strategies and report runtime/memory vs. graph size.
- Questions for authors: performance on bAbI with only answer supervision and learning curves?
- How are simplex and [0,1] constraints enforced (sigmoid/softmax or projection)?
- Exact hyperparameters: hidden sizes, propagation steps, nodes added per step, MLP sizes, and sensitivity?
- How many edge types per task and how were they designed for bAbI?
- In the Turing-machine experiment, how is generalization to longer rollouts measured and do rule graphs remain fixed?
- Can the method handle ambiguous/noisy mentions (coreference, paraphrase) without direct reference?
- What are runtime and memory footprints versus graph size and the maximum practical graph handled?
