- Paper proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Networks (GGT-NNs) that make graph structure a differentiable, trainable state via differentiable graph operations.  
- Core contributions: differentiable node addition, node-state update (with direct-reference), edge update, message-passing propagation, and graph-level aggregation on a soft graph parameterization.  
- Demonstrated on bAbI QA with supervision of intermediate graphs and on rule-discovery tasks (Rule 30 and simple Turing machines) showing learned internal graph updates and some rollout generalization.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Major concern: main bAbI results rely on strong, timestep-level supervision and handcrafted direct-reference signals, undermining claims of learning graph extraction end-to-end.  
- Strong supervision effect: large performance gap between “with direct reference” and “without,” indicating fragility without privileged signals.  
- Comparability issues: task preprocessing and exposure of generator “Knowledge” as targets (history nodes, token collapsing) likely make tasks easier and hinder fair comparison to prior models.  
- Presentation and empirical rigor are lacking: missing full per-task tables/figures, hyperparameters, model/optimizer details, runtime/memory, and variance across runs.  
- Limited ablations and sensitivity analyses: unclear contributions of individual transformations, propagation steps, sparsity, and no multiple-seed reporting.  
- Scalability concerns: quadratic time/space in node count acknowledged but no empirical profiling or sparse/batched solutions demonstrated.  
- Rule-discovery tasks are synthetic and not compared to strong baselines; generalization claims are not fully quantified.  
- Terminology issue: using “Transformer” may mislead readers since it is unrelated to attention-based Transformer architectures.  
- Suggested improvements: provide complete experiments, separate supervision regimes, stronger ablations, multiple seeds, and quantify training/settings and failure cases.  
- Suggested directions: reduce supervision (semi/weak supervision), replace lexicon direct-reference with learned grounding, implement sparse/batched message passing, and test on larger/realistic tasks without ground-truth graphs.  
- Questions for authors sought: end-to-end bAbI performance without graph supervision, sensitivity to propagation steps/node limits/set-matching loss, handling of ambiguous names/coreference, full rollout generalization metrics, exact model/hardware/training specs, and targeted ablations removing specific modules or supervision changes.
