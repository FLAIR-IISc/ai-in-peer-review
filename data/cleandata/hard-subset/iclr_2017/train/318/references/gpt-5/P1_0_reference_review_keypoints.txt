- Proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Networks (GGT-NNs) to incrementally construct and manipulate latent graphs from text using differentiable node/edge transformations and propagation.  
- Uses a GRU text encoder and a query reader; trains with strong supervision on intermediate graphs and teacher forcing of gold nodes/edges.  
- Evaluated on bAbI (text-to-answer), Rule 30 cellular automaton, and simple Turing-machine tasks; shows high bAbI accuracy (with direct reference) and good synthetic generalization.  
- Decision: reject.  
- Main concern: heavy reliance on strong supervision and dataset modifications undermines interpretability and practical impact of empirical claims.  
- Main concern: incremental novelty over GG-NN/GGS-NN and other existing modules; conceptual contribution modest.  
- Scalability concern: dense pairwise edge MLPs are O(|V|^2), limiting practicality and deployment.  
- Motivation/positioning critique: gold graph supervision at every timestep and exact string-matched direct references simplify the problem relative to end-to-end learning.  
- Comparability issue: baselines mix models trained with strong supervision and those without, making superiority claims ambiguous.  
- bAbI evaluation issues: dataset modifications (collapsing multi-word entities, added history lists) weaken comparability and may change task difficulty.  
- Ablation gaps: limited ablations (only direct-reference and propagation); missing analyses on individual transformations, propagation steps, and representation sizes.  
- Synthetic task critique: CA/TM experiments show rule learning but are synthetic; exact-match metric is harsh and lacks error-mode diagnosis or finer-grained metrics.  
- Rigor/clarity issues: under-specified training choices (constraint enforcement, teacher forcing at test time), hyperparameters, capacity, and compute; inconsistent references and brief dataset modification descriptions.  
- No evaluated mitigations for O(|V|^2) edge updates (e.g., sparsification, learned proposals, locality).  
- Suggested improvements: reduce supervision (end-to-end or semi-supervised training), quantify gap vs. full supervision.  
- Suggested improvements: include fair, matched baselines and graph-based parsers (dependency/AMR + GGNN) to separate parsing vs. reasoning.  
- Suggested improvements: evaluate on stronger, more realistic datasets (narrative reasoning, temporal KG construction, AMR).  
- Suggested improvements: run thorough ablations (remove Tadd/TC/Tprop, sparse edges, sensitivity to entity count), report per-edge precision/recall, visualize learned vs. gold graphs, and explore scheduled sampling/self-consistency.  
- Suggested improvements: address efficiency via sparsity, top-k proposals, locality-aware propagation, or hierarchical construction.  
- Key questions for authors: which bAbI tasks were modified and can results be reported on unmodified data?  
- Key questions for authors: how is exposure bias handled at test time; was scheduled sampling tried?  
- Key questions for authors: how are constraints enforced for node existence/edges (softmax/sigmoid/renormalization), and what are typical N, Y, D, T, parameter counts, training time, and memory?  
- Key questions for authors: are edge updates computed for all pairs or sparsified, and is fset/freset computed for all (v,v')?  
- Key questions for authors: how robust is direct reference to noisy mentions/coreference/synonyms, and can it be learned end-to-end rather than exact string matches?  
- Key questions for authors: can you report per-timestep and edge-level metrics and provide qualitative error analysis for CA/TM and graph accuracy on bAbI without substituting gold?
