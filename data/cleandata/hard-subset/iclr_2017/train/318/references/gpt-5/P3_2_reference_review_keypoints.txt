- Paper proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Networks (GGT-NN): differentiable graph construction/update operations composed to build/interact with a labeled graph state from text and answer queries.

- Decision: Reject.

- Major concern: Heavy reliance on strong supervision (gold intermediate graphs and exact string direct references) undermines the claim of learning to induce structure from text.

- Major concern: Limited novelty—mostly standard neural components (GRU, MLPs, attention) composed in a straightforward way; “Transformer” name is misleading.

- Empirical weakness: bAbI performance depends critically on direct references (95% with, 9/20 without), indicating poor end-to-end entity/structure induction.

- Empirical weakness: Comparisons to baselines are confounded by different supervision regimes and dataset/format modifications; no reported variances across runs.

- Missing analyses: No ablations of the five transformations beyond limited toggles, and no graph-level accuracy metrics on bAbI despite training with graph supervision.

- CA/TM experiments: Good fit and some generalization, but narrow TM setup (2-symbol, 4-state, 6-step) with unclear rule distribution and no baselines or robustness tests.

- Claims vs. evidence: Central claim (learning to construct/exploit graph state from unstructured input) is only partially supported due to dependence on full structural supervision and exact lexical matches.

- Scalability concern: Quadratic time/space complexity noted but no runtime, memory, or scaling experiments provided.

- Clarity/reproducibility: Transformation definitions are clear and appendix has details, but important training hyperparameters and dataset modification extents are missing.

- Suggested improvement: Evaluate under weaker supervision (supporting facts only, partial/no graph supervision, or consistency losses).

- Suggested improvement: Report graph-level metrics (precision/recall of nodes/edges) on bAbI in addition to QA accuracy.

- Suggested improvement: Add ablations removing node addition, edge update, direct reference, varying propagation depth, and report effects.

- Suggested improvement: Report training/inference time, memory footprint, scaling with |V|/|E|, and explore sparse updates or neighborhood attention.

- Suggested improvement: Strengthen baselines (text-to-graph parsers, GG-NN on fixed graphs, algorithmic baselines) and ensure fair bAbI comparisons (e.g., 10k-example parity).

- Suggested improvement: Clarify permutation-matching algorithm for node supervision, and how maximum new nodes per step is set.

- Suggested improvement: Replace hard direct reference with a learned pointer/mention mechanism to reduce reliance on exact string matching.

- Questions for authors: Is graph construction fully predicted at test time on bAbI and what is test-time graph reconstruction accuracy?

- Questions for authors: How is permutation-invariant node matching implemented (Hungarian?) and what is its training cost?

- Questions for authors: Typical |V|, |E|, and number of edge types per task, plus training/inference runtimes and memory usage; how does performance degrade with larger graphs?

- Questions for authors: Can you provide results with partial or no graph supervision to show end-to-end structure induction?

- Questions for authors: How sensitive is performance to noisy/ambiguous direct references and can a learned mention detector replace the hard lexicon?

- Questions for authors: For Turing machines, what is the distribution over rules, do results generalize to unseen rule sets, and how do results scale with more states/symbols or longer rollouts?

- Questions for authors: Please provide standard deviations across seeds and more ablations of the transformation components.
