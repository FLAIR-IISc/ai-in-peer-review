- Proposal: Gated Graph Transformer Neural Networks (GGT-NNs) — differentiable graphs with soft node types/existence, dense multi-edge tensor, and graph transforms (Tadd, Th, TC, Tprop, Trepr) for incremental text→graph→QA processing.
- Empirical claims: strong supervision on bAbI with teacher-forced intermediate graphs (≥95% on 19/20 tasks with direct reference) and synthetic rule-discovery results (Rule-30 CA, small Turing machines) with some length-generalization.
- Positives noted: clear modular formulation, set-matching training for unordered node creation, convincing qualitative fits, detailed appendices and released code.
- Decision: Reject.
- Core critique: central results depend on heavy supervision (teacher-forced graphs + dictionary direct reference), undermining claim of learning to build graphs from raw text.
- Experimental rigor concerns: no end-to-end (no graph supervision) bAbI results, few ablations, lack of fair baselines on rule tasks, and no scaling analysis.
- Supervision realism: model is trained at every step with gold node/edge states and gold graphs are injected subsequently; direct reference is implemented via dictionary lookup — making comparisons to end-to-end models unfair.
- Missing ablations: no analysis of teacher forcing, Tadd vs pre-instantiated nodes, TC vs propagation-only, effect of direct reference, or discrete vs continuous edge representations.
- Algorithmic-task baselines lacking: no comparisons to NTM/DNC/EntNet/transformers and missing per-timestep/length extrapolation curves and detailed metrics.
- Scalability issue: O(|V|^2·Y) compute/memory from dense TC/Tprop; no empirical scaling study, sparsity strategies, or experiments on larger graphs.
- Clarity/positioning problems: “Transformer” naming is ambiguous, related work on differentiable structure learning/neural relational inference omitted, references inconsistent, and many training/hyperparameter details missing.
- Suggested improvements: report end-to-end/semi-supervised bAbI results, comprehensive ablations, stronger algorithmic baselines and length-generalization plots, runtime/memory vs |V| analysis and sparse update strategies, clearer naming and full training details, and qualitative visualizations/failure analysis.
- Questions for authors requested: performance without ground-truth graph injection (none/partial/full), typical |V|,|E|,Y and empirical scaling, hyperparameter table and validation protocol, per-timestep/length metrics and baselines for rule-learning, robustness to noisy/absent direct reference, whether edges discretize at inference, and feasibility of sparse candidate-edge adaptations.
