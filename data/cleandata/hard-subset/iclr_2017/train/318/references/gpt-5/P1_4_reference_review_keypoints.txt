- Proposal: GGT-NNs — a modular framework for differentiable graph transformations enabling dynamic node/edge creation and joint text-to-graph/graph-to-text reasoning with claimed interpretability and flexibility.  
- Decision: Reject — primary reasons are heavy reliance on strong supervision, insufficient empirical rigor/ablations and limited evaluation, and incremental novelty over prior graph/memory models.  
- Supervision/fairness concern: bAbI results use full ground-truth intermediate graphs and direct lexical alignment, an unrealistic advantage compared to end-to-end baselines.  
- Novelty/positioning concern: Core propagation/aggregation follow GG-NN/GGS-NN; additions (soft adjacency, node addition, controller) resemble differentiable memory controllers (e.g., DNC) and lack clear superiority justification.  
- Missing ablations: No systematic study of individual transformations (Tadd, Th, TC, Tprop), node/edge strengths, direct reference, intermediate propagation, or permutation-invariant loss.  
- Reproducibility gaps: Key training details (hyperparameters, hidden sizes, propagation steps T/K, learning rates, regularization) are underspecified despite code link.  
- Efficiency analysis missing: No runtime/memory benchmarks beyond noting quadratic scaling and no evidence of sparse/efficient implementations.  
- Synthetic-evaluation limits: Rule-discovery tasks are simple/synthetic and not stress-tested for OOD generalization (larger tapes, more states/symbols, different topologies) or compared to relevant baselines.  
- Data-leakage risk: Training injects gold graph structures and substitutes ground-truth edges/nodes during training, conflating parsing with reasoning and easing the task.  
- Clarity issues: Several technical choices underspecified (max nodes per step, thresholds on strengths, stability of fset/freset, selection of edge types Y) and inconsistent references.  
- Recommendation — reduce supervision: Evaluate with only answer supervision, semi-supervised mixes, and on realistic datasets where string matching fails (co-reference, paraphrase, unseen entities).  
- Recommendation — perform ablations/diagnostics: Remove/replace transformations, compare soft vs discrete edges, analyze node strengths, intermediate propagation, and the permutation-invariant node-add loss.  
- Recommendation — report efficiency and scaling: Provide hyperparameters, training times, memory footprints, scaling behavior, and explore sparsity or neighborhood sampling.  
- Recommendation — strengthen baselines: Include GGS-NN with text preprocessing, pointer/seq2graph baselines, and modern graph-attention mechanisms with aligned supervision.  
- Recommendation — improve clarity: Formalize controller architecture, stopping criteria for node addition, node-count decision mechanisms, and provide bAbI preprocessing code.  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to availability of graph-level supervision — results with only answer supervision or partial supervision (edges-only, nodes-only)?  
- Question: What are the exact hyperparameters (hidden sizes, propagation steps T, K, learning schedules) used in main experiments?  
- Question: How is the maximum number of nodes per Tadd chosen, how does the model decide when to stop adding nodes, and what thresholding/rounding is used at inference?  
- Question: Why use separate fset/freset for edge updates instead of a single parametric function or discrete relaxations (e.g., Gumbel-Softmax); were alternatives tried?  
- Question: Can you provide runtime/memory benchmarks vs GG-NN/GGS-NN, MemN2N, and DNC, and evidence that sparsifying C or limiting neighborhoods preserves accuracy?  
- Question: Does the bAbI pipeline require co-reference/entity aliasing resolution beyond exact string match, and how would direct reference generalize beyond bAbI?  
- Question: For the Turing-machine task, how does performance scale to more states/symbols or longer rollouts without retraining, and what are observed failure modes?
