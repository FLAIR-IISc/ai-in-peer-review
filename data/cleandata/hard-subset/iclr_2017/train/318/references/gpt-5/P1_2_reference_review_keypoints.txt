- Proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Networks (GGT-NNs): differentiable graph state updated by learnable transformations (add, update, edge update, propagate, aggregate) with soft nodes/edges.  
- Implements a configuration that parses sentences with a GRU to incrementally build/update a graph and answer queries from the final graph.  
- Training uses strong structural supervision: gold edges and newly added nodes at each step, with set-like loss for unordered nodes and substitution of gold structure during training.  
- Reports strong bAbI results (≥95% on 19/20 tasks with direct references) and demonstrations on Rule 30 cellular automata and simple Turing machines with some horizon generalization.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main reason 1: Heavy reliance on strong, task-specific supervision and direct referencing undermines claim of learning graph construction from text.  
- Main reason 2: Limited novelty relative to GG-NN/GGS-NN; transformations largely repackage known components without theoretical or algorithmic advances.  
- Training protocol concern: Replacing model predictions with gold structures during training breaks end-to-end learning, risks exposure bias, and obscures test-time stability.  
- Comparison fairness: Most baselines are weakly supervised end-to-end while GGT-NN used full gold graphs, making comparisons not apples-to-apples.  
- Novelty/positioning: Differentiable graph elements and modular transforms are sensible engineering but conceptually close to prior work; direct-reference is essentially an attention mask.  
- Experimental scope: bAbI relies on generator-derived gold graphs; Turing/CA tasks are engineered and small-scale with limited metrics and analysis.  
- Analysis gaps: Little inspection of learned structures, sparsity, edge calibration, hyperparameter sensitivity, or failure modes.  
- Scalability concern: O(|V|^2) complexity noted but no empirical runtime/memory characterization, sparsification attempts, or parameter/count details.  
- Suggestion: Provide weakly/semi-supervised and fully end-to-end training results to quantify impact of structural supervision.  
- Suggestion: Add ablations (remove direct-reference, vary supervision strength, analyze each transform, quantify exposure bias).  
- Suggestion: Strengthen baselines (GGS-NN with preprocessing, neural semantic/AMR parsers, DNC/HAM ablations for pathfinding).  
- Suggestion: Report compute/memory footprints, experiment with sparsity (top-k, learned sparsifiers), and include hyperparameters/training details.  
- Suggestion: Evaluate on more realistic datasets without gold structures (e.g., KB population, AMR) to support motivation.  
- Questions for authors: How does training behave without gold substitutions? Can you train fully end-to-end and report stability/accuracy?  
- Questions: Do gradients flow through substituted gold structures? What is the impact on learned dynamics at test time?  
- Questions: How many node/edge types per task, are types fixed, and how handle open-vocabulary entities/coreference?  
- Questions: Provide practical computational/memory complexity (D, Y, |V|) with wall-clock and peak memory plots.  
- Questions: For Turing experiments, how are rules sampled and do models generalize to unseen rule tables and longer horizons (per-step error rates)?  
- Questions: Do learned graphs become sparse/binary at convergence and do you use regularization to encourage discreteness?  
- Questions: Can you compare to GGS-NN on pathfinding with equivalent supervision to isolate effects of dynamic graph construction?
