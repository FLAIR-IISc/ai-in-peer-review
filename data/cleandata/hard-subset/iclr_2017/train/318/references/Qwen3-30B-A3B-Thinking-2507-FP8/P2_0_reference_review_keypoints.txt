- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that can build and modify graph-structured internal states from unstructured input.  
- Proposes five differentiable graph transformations: node addition, node state update, edge update, propagation, and aggregation.  
- Demonstrates strong performance on bAbI QA tasks (95%+ on 19/20 tasks) and significant improvement on pathfinding compared to prior models.  
- Shows capability on rule-discovery: learns Rule 30 cellular automata (95%+) and 4-state Turing machines (98%), generalizing to longer sequences.  
- Provides thorough literature positioning and distinguishes GGT-NN from memory networks, DNCs, HAM, and prior GGS-NN approaches.  
- Ablation studies indicate direct reference substantially improves training efficiency and final performance.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations: quadratic complexity and reliance on strong supervision.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on clear gap identification, well-motivated solution, and strong empirical results.  
- Suggests adding concrete computational cost numbers to quantify the quadratic complexity impact on training and memory.  
- Recommends evaluating performance under reduced supervision to assess practicality.  
- Proposes including a more complex rule-discovery task to better demonstrate scalability of learned graph transformations.  
- Asks authors to explain why direct reference aids learning graph structure more effectively.  
- Requests justification for differing dataset sizes (1,000 vs 20,000) used for Rule 30 and Turing machine experiments.  
- Requests exact DMN+ accuracy on bAbI task 19 for a precise comparison.
