- Paper introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) with differentiable graph transformations to build and modify graph structure during inference.  
- Demonstrated on bAbI QA, cellular automata, and Turing machine tasks, claiming advantages over implicit-memory models.  
- Decision: Reject — novelty limited and experimental validation insufficient to support claimed advantages.  
- Novelty concern: Idea of learning graph structure is not new and appears derivative of GGS-NN; paper doesn't clearly distinguish GGT-NN from prior work.  
- Experimental rigor concern: bAbI experiments use strong graph-level supervision, making comparisons to end-to-end models (e.g., MemNN) misleading.  
- Missing standard evaluation: No results reported under the standard bAbI protocol where graph structure must be learned from text.  
- Generalization concern: High accuracies on rule tasks may reflect memorization; no evidence of generalizing to unseen rules or substantially larger graph sizes.  
- Methodological clarity lacking: Important details on computational complexity, scalability, training time, and memory are omitted.  
- Scalability issue: Method has quadratic complexity in graph size (Section 6) which is not adequately addressed.  
- Incomplete comparisons: Paper claims benefits of explicit graphs but provides no visualizations or downstream-task demonstrations of learned graphs.  
- Suggested revision: Explicitly clarify how GGT-NN differs from GGS-NN and justify the new framework/name.  
- Suggested revision: Re-run bAbI experiments without graph-level supervision and report results on the standard protocol.  
- Suggested revision: Provide stronger generalization tests to larger graphs and unseen rule sets.  
- Suggested revision: Include a clear computational complexity analysis and discuss sparse-connection or distributed processing implications.  
- Questions for authors: How does GGT-NN perform on standard bAbI without graph supervision? Can it generalize to unseen Turing machine rules? How would sparse edges affect differentiability? How is claimed distributed processing reconciled with quadratic complexity?
