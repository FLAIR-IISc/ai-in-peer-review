- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that builds and modifies graph-structured intermediate representations and outputs via differentiable graph transformations.
- Key transformations: node addition, node state update, edge update, propagation, and aggregation (Tadd, Th, TC, Tprop, Trepr).
- Evaluated on bAbI QA, Rule 30 cellular automaton, and 4-state Turing machines, claiming strong performance and generalization to larger inputs.
- Empirical result highlight: 95%+ accuracy on 19/20 bAbI tasks and particularly strong pathfinding performance.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; addresses a clear limitation of fixed-structure GNNs and is well-motivated.
- Technical strength: modular, well-described mathematical formulation and implementation of differentiable graph transformations.
- Main concern: heavy supervision in bAbI experiments (providing correct graph at each timestep) makes comparisons to end-to-end models uneven.
- Request to provide results with reduced/no intermediate graph supervision to assess the model's true learning capability.
- Ask for expanded scalability analysis with concrete training time and memory scaling and possible optimizations (e.g., sparsity).
- Recommend a more thorough ablation study to determine which transformations/configurations are critical for which tasks.
- Request clearer explanation of how graph representations were modified for bAbI tasks and task-specific adaptations.
- Question: how does performance change when graph supervision is removed entirely?
- Question: which specific bAbI tasks fail without direct reference, and why is direct reference crucial for them?
- Question: were special adaptations needed to represent cellular automata and Turing machines as graphs?
- Question: provide concrete comparisons of training time and memory usage versus other models on the same hardware.
