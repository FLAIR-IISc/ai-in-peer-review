- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer NN (GGT-NN) that incrementally constructs/updates graph structure from text via differentiable graph transformations, evaluated on bAbI, Rule 30, and a 4-state Turing machine.  
- Recommendation: weak acceptance.  
- Fills a gap: enables learning graph structure incrementally rather than requiring a fixed input graph.  
- Key innovation: differentiable graph transformations (node addition, state update, edge update, propagation, aggregation).  
- Strong empirical performance: >95% on many bAbI tasks and improvements on tasks 5, 7, 8, 17, 18, and 19 versus baselines.  
- Ablation study validates importance of the direct reference mechanism.  
- Shows capability to learn update rules for cellular automata and Turing machines, demonstrating learning of complex dynamics for trained rules.  
- Major limitation: insufficient detail and experimental evidence on computational complexity and scalability (training time and memory vs graph size).  
- Overstated claim: results only show simulation of trained rules, not demonstrated generalization to arbitrary unseen rules.  
- Missing qualitative analysis: no visualizations of graphs produced by the model to assess learned representations.  
- Suggested improvement: add experiments/section on computational complexity scaling (time and memory).  
- Suggested improvement: include visualizations of learned graph structures for tasks (e.g., bAbI, automaton steps).  
- Suggested improvement: evaluate generalization to unseen rules (e.g., train on Rule 30, test on Rule 110).  
- Suggested improvement: clarify how GGT-NN extends and differs from prior GNN architectures.  
- Suggested improvement: provide more training details and hyperparameter tuning procedures.  
- Question for authors: how well does the model generalize to rules not seen in training (e.g., Rule 30 → Rule 110)?  
- Question for authors: how does training time and memory scale with number of graph nodes?  
- Question for authors: what concrete approaches to “less supervision” are being considered and how might they affect performance?
