- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that builds and modifies graph-structured internal states from unstructured input via differentiable graph transformations (node add/update, edge update, propagation, aggregation).  
- Evaluated on bAbI QA, a 1D cellular automaton (Rule 30), and 4-state Turing machines, showing strong performance and generalization.  
- Main contribution: extends GNNs to dynamic graph construction, addressing the limitation that most prior GNNs require fixed graph-structured input.  
- Recommendation: accept — clear contribution, addresses a meaningful gap, and shows strong empirical results, especially on graph-suited tasks like bAbI pathfinding.  
- Empirical highlights: 95%+ accuracy on 19/20 bAbI tasks with 1,000 examples, 64% improvement over MemNN on pathfinding task 19, and high accuracy/generalization on CA and Turing machine tasks.  
- Technical clarity: well-explained methods, clear pseudocode (Algorithm 1), thorough appendix, and informative ablation studies.  
- Concern: computational complexity is mentioned but not quantified; need analysis of how graph size affects training time and memory.  
- Concern: strong supervision (full graph information) was used for training, which may limit real-world applicability and should be discussed.  
- Concern: the paper shows direct reference improves performance but lacks deeper analysis of why and how it helps learning relationships.  
- Question: how does quadratic scaling with graph size affect performance on larger graphs and practical limits for real-world problems?  
- Question: can the authors provide specific examples showing how direct reference improves learning of entity relationships?  
- Question: how would the model be trained without full graph supervision and what performance degradation is expected?  
- Conclusion: significant, well-written, and technically sound contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing complexity, supervision, and direct-reference analysis.
