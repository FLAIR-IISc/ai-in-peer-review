- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), which learns and manipulates graph-structured internal states via differentiable graph transformations.  
- Extends GNNs and GGS-NNs by enabling dynamic construction and modification of graph structures during processing.  
- Implements five transformation types: node addition, node state update, edge update, propagation, and aggregation.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on bAbI QA tasks, a 1D cellular automaton (Rule 30), and arbitrary 4-state Turing machines.  
- Shows especially strong results on graph-suited tasks (e.g., pathfinding) and outperforms many baselines on several bAbI tasks.  
- Ablation studies highlight the importance of direct reference, improving accuracy from 9/20 to 19/20 tasks at 95% accuracy.  
- Most compelling result: on bAbI task 19 (pathfinding), GGT-NN with direct reference significantly outperforms MemNN baselines and most other models.  
- Makes a strong argument for explicit graph-structured internal representations and provides visualizable internal states.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions due to meaningful contribution, sound technical foundation, and clear empirical advantages.  
- Request clearer explanation of differentiability and how losses are backpropagated through graph structure modifications (esp. node ordering).  
- Ask for a computational complexity analysis and discussion of scalability to larger graphs (e.g., 100+ nodes).  
- Recommend a hyperparameter/data-sensitivity ablation showing performance scaling with different amounts of training data.  
- Suggest tightening background sections (GRUs and GG-NNs) to focus on novel contributions and improve conciseness.  
- Advise adding a brief comparison to recent graph-transformer architectures to situate the work in current literature.

- Question: How can the model be extended to handle much larger numbers of entities (e.g., for positional reasoning)?
