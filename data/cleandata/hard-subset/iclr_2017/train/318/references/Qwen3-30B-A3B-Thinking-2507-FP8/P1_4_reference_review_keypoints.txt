- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) enabling differentiable construction and modification of graph-structured internal states from unstructured input.  
- Shows GGT-NN solves graphical reasoning tasks (bAbI QA, cellular automata, Turing machine) better than sequence-based approaches.  
- Key contribution: differentiable graph transformations for dynamic graph building and reasoning.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Addresses important gap: most GNNs require fixed input graphs; GGT-NN fills this by learning graph structure.  
- Strong experimental validation: state-of-the-art bAbI results (11 tasks at 100%, 19 tasks ≥95%), major improvement on pathfinding (task 19, +64% vs MemNN).  
- Analysis links task difficulty (e.g., task 17) to number of entity types and story length.  
- Demonstrates robustness and generalization: perfect outputs on extended cellular automaton and Turing machine timesteps.  
- Thorough comparison with related work and clear differentiation from Memory Networks and DNCs.  
- Paper is well-written with reproducibility details in appendices.  
- Suggest adding discussion of computational complexity and mitigation strategies (sparsity, selective processing).  
- Recommend comparing and citing more recent GNN work (GraphSAGE, GAT, GIN).  
- Advise placing the GitHub implementation link in the main text for easier access.
