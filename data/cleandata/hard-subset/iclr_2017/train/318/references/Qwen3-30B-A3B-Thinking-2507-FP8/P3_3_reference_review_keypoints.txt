- Summary: Proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) for differentiable graph transformations to build/modify graphs from unstructured input, formalizing five graph operations and showing strong results on bAbI, cellular automata, and Turing machine tasks.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Novelty concern: Differentiable graph transformations are seen as a straightforward extension of prior GNN work and not clearly distinct from related methods.  
- Motivation gap: Paper does not convincingly distinguish its approach from existing dynamic/differentiable graph methods.  
- Unfair experimental comparison: GGT-NN was trained with full graph-level supervision on bAbI while baselines were trained end-to-end, creating an uneven comparison.  
- Supervision issue unresolved: Authors acknowledge stronger supervision but fail to show comparable performance with reduced or no graph-level supervision.  
- Ablation insufficient: The ablation on the "direct reference" mechanism is superficial and does not quantify its contribution relative to the graph representation.  
- Computational limitations: Model has quadratic complexity, but paper provides no empirical scaling results or mitigation strategies (e.g., sparsity or sampling).  
- Practicality claim questioned: Claim that the model can be used "as-is" is unlikely given unaddressed computational constraints for larger graphs.  
- Rule discovery experiments under-specified: Cellular automaton and Turing machine setups lack detail on graph design, making it unclear whether the model learns rules or memorizes transformations.  
- Lack of error analysis/baselines: No error analysis or comparisons to baselines that use similar graphical representations for rule discovery tasks.  
- Clarify novelty: Must compare thoroughly to recent differentiable graph methods (e.g., GraphSAGE, GAT, learnable-edge GNNs) to establish novelty.  
- Address supervision: Provide experiments training GGT-NN without full graph-level supervision or study how much supervision is necessary.  
- Quantify direct reference: Run proper ablations measuring performance drop when the direct reference mechanism is removed.  
- Discuss computational scaling: Include experiments on performance and training time vs. graph size and propose solutions to quadratic complexity.  
- Detail graphical representations: Explain and visualize graph constructions for rule-discovery tasks to show what the model is learning.  
- Compare to recent methods: Empirically compare to more recent GNN approaches to demonstrate relative advantages.  
- Questions for authors: How does GGT-NN compare to a fixed-graph GNN baseline using the same representation?  
- Questions for authors: What ablations would prove the model learns underlying rules rather than memorizing transformations?  
- Questions for authors: Can the model learn to build graphs without the full graph-level supervision used in bAbI experiments?
