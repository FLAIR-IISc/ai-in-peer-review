- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that dynamically constructs and modifies graphs via differentiable transformations (node/edge operations, propagation, aggregation).

- Shows strong empirical results on bAbI (95%+ on 19/20 tasks), Rule 30 cellular automata, and Turing machines, arguing for effective structured reasoning.

- Decision: Weak Accept — meaningful contribution but requires major revisions.

- Key technical advance: addresses fixed-graph limitation of prior GNNs by enabling graph-structured state representations to be built during processing.

- Empirical highlight: large accuracy gain on pathfinding (task 19) and ability to learn complex rule-based transformations.

- Major concern: comparisons are unfair because GGT-NN uses strong supervision (full graph info per timestep) while baselines are end-to-end.

- Major concern: paper lacks computational complexity analysis and practical mitigation strategies for the model’s quadratic time/space scaling.

- Required revision: provide results on the standard bAbI benchmark without graph supervision for fair comparison.

- Required revision: include scalability experiments (performance and runtime across graph sizes) and discuss optimizations (e.g., sparsity, selective processing).

- Required revision: compare and cite recent dynamic graph generation work (e.g., Dai et al., ICML 2018) to situate the contribution.

- Required revision: clarify and analyze the direct reference mechanism to explain why it improves performance.

- Suggestion: provide examples of alternative transformation orderings to demonstrate the model’s modularity.

- Suggestion: analyze failure on task 17 (Positional Reasoning) to identify limitations and causes.

- Questions for authors: report bAbI results without graph supervision, plans to address quadratic complexity, clarify relationship with GGS-NN, and explain failure on task 17.
