- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), enabling differentiable graph transformations to build/modify graphs from unstructured input.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance: 95%+ on most bAbI tasks, large improvement on pathfinding, learns Rule 30 CA and 4-state Turing machines.  
- Recommends paper for acceptance.  
- Theoretical contribution: formalizes node addition/state/edge updates, propagation and aggregation to overcome fixed-graph limitations in GNNs.  
- Empirical validation is comprehensive with ablations showing benefits of direct reference and detailed analyses of task-specific performance.  
- Well-positioned in literature with fair comparisons to GNNs, memory networks, and related work.  
- Suggestion: clarify how graph-level “strong supervision” differs from sentence-level supervision in competing models.

- Suggestion: discuss expected generalization to other graph domains (e.g., proteins, social networks).  
- Suggestion: more explicitly relate GGT-NN to other graph architectures like Graph Attention Networks (GATs).
