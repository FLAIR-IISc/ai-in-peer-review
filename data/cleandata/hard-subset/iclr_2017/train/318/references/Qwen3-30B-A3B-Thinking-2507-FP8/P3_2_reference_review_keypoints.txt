- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that learns to build and modify graph-structured internal states from unstructured input via differentiable graph transformations (node add/update, edge update, propagation, aggregation).  
- Evaluated on bAbI QA, Rule 30 cellular automata, and 4-state Turing machines, achieving strong results (95%+ on most bAbI tasks).  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — novel contribution, technically sound, and well-validated experimentally.  
- Novelty: Differentiable graph transformations make the graph structure itself learnable, advancing beyond fixed-structure GNNs.  
- Empirical strength: Outperforms prior methods on graph-structured tasks (notably large gains on pathfinding task 19).  
- Related work: Clear distinction from GNNs, Memory Networks, DNCs; positions GGT-NN as modeling modifiable graph structure.  
- Improvement — complexity: Needs deeper discussion of quadratic computational complexity and practical scaling/trade-offs.  
- Improvement — direct reference: Requires analysis of why direct reference yields large performance gains and how it aids entity tracking.  
- Improvement — generalization: Needs stronger analysis to determine whether the model learns underlying rules or memorizes patterns.  
- Question: Provide full per-task bAbI accuracy table (with and without direct reference).  
- Question: Quantify performance drop without “strong supervision” and identify minimal graph-level supervision required.  
- Question: For Turing machines, clarify whether the model learned transition rules or memorized I/O and show internal state evolution.  
- Question: Show an example of reconfiguring the transformation sequence for a different task (e.g., graph classification).  
- Conclusion: Significant advance bridging unstructured input to graph-structured processing; address complexity and direct-reference analyses to strengthen the paper.
