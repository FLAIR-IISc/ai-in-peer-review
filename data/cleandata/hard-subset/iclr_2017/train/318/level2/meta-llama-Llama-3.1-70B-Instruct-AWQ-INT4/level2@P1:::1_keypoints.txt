- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), an extension of GGS-NNs using graph-structured intermediate representations.  
- GGT-NN learns to construct and modify graphs from textual input and to use those graphs to produce outputs.  
- Evaluated on bAbI tasks plus cellular automaton and Turing machine tasks, showing effectiveness.  
- Reviewer recommends accepting the paper.  
- Novel and well-motivated approach addressing an important problem in modeling graph-structured data.  
- Model is well-designed with a clear, intuitive architecture combining graph transformations and a recurrent input model.  
- Paper provides a clear introduction and motivation, highlighting limitations of existing approaches.  
- Detailed description of model components, graph transformation operations, and supervision mechanism.  
- Evaluation is thorough and well-executed, with detailed analysis of results and implications.  
- Suggests more discussion of relationships to other methods like GNNs and GCNs.  
- Requests more insight into choice of graph transformation operations and the supervision mechanism’s contribution.  
- Recommends strengthening evaluation with additional baselines and state-of-the-art comparisons on bAbI.  
- Asks for implementation details on how graph transformations and supervision are optimized during training.  
- Raises concerns about scalability and efficiency for large, complex graphs.  
- Requests discussion of potential applications and how GGT-NN could be adapted or extended to other domains.
