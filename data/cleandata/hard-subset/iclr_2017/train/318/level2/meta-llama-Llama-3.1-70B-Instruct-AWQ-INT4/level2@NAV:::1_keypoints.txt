- Summary: Proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) to learn constructing/modifying graph-structured outputs from text using differentiable graph transformations.
- Decision: Recommend accept.
- Well-motivated approach: Clearly motivates GGT-NN by highlighting limitations of prior graph-processing methods and situating work in related literature.
- Clear explanation: Paper gives a detailed, well-organized description of the GGT-NN architecture and its components.
- Effective evaluation: Demonstrates GGT-NN on bAbI tasks, a cellular automaton, and Turing-machine family, showing capability to learn graph construction/modification.
- Need complexity analysis: Authors should provide a detailed time/space computational-complexity analysis to assess scalability.
- Discuss limitations: Paper should include a more thorough discussion of limitations (e.g., strong supervision requirement) and practical constraints.
- More experiments: Recommend broader experimental validation on additional, diverse tasks to assess generalizability.
- Question — implementation details: Request specifics on how the differentiable graph transformations are implemented and the neural architectures used.
- Question — supervision: Ask how the authors plan to mitigate the need for strong supervision during training.
- Question — complexity specifics: Request concrete measurements of training time and memory usage across tasks to clarify computational demands.
