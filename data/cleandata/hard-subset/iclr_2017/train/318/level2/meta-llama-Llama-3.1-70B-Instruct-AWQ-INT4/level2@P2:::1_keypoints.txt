- Paper introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), extending GGS-NNs to learn and modify graph-structured intermediate representations.  
- Reviewer recommends accept, citing novelty and significance of GGT-NN and thorough evaluation.  
- Model is well-motivated, addressing fixed-graph limitations of prior work.  
- Paper gives clear description of differentiable graph transformations, GGT-NN architecture, and supervision methods.  
- Experiments are thorough, covering bAbI tasks, rule discovery tasks, and comparisons to related work.  
- Paper discusses advantages (distributed processing, flexibility) and limitations of GGT-NN.  
- Potential applications across domains are noted, though not deeply explored.  
- Minor issue: notation and terminology can be confusing and need clearer explanation.  
- Minor issue: paper lacks sufficient visualizations/illustrations of graph structures and transformations.  
- Minor issue: paper should expand discussion of potential applications and future directions.  
- Suggestion: add more experiments/evaluations to demonstrate robustness and generalizability.  
- Request for clarification: details of implementation for differentiable graph transformations (node addition, state update, edge update, propagation, aggregation).  
- Request for clarification: how the model handles empty input graphs or graphs with no nodes.  
- Request for clarification: more discussion/examples of real-world applications (NLP, computer vision, robotics).  
- Request for clarification: plans to address computational complexity and scalability limitations.
