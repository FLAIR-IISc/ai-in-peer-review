- Introduces GGT-NN, an extension of GGS-NN that uses graph-structured intermediate representations and can produce graph-structured outputs from text.  
- Demonstrates model effectiveness on bAbI tasks, a simple cellular automaton, and a family of Turing machines.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Reason: addresses the specific problem of learning graphical state transitions from textual input.  
- Reason: approach is well-motivated, building on graph neural networks and sequence-to-sequence models.  
- Reason: provides a clear and detailed description of the model, training procedure, and experiments.  
- Supporting: paper details components such as graph transformer, node addition, node state update, edge update, propagation, and aggregation.  
- Supporting: experimental setup is described thoroughly, including datasets, evaluation metrics, and results.  
- Result: GGT-NN outperforms baselines including seq2seq LSTM and a modified Memory Network.  
- Improvement: paper needs deeper analysis of results, discussing model strengths and weaknesses.

- Improvement: should discuss potential applications in NLP, computer vision, and other areas.
