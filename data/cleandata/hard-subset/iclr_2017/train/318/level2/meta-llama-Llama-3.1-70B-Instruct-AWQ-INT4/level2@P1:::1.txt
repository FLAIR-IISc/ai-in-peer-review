The paper "Learning Graphical State Transitions" introduces the Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) model, an extension of Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks (GGS-NNs) that uses graph-structured data as an intermediate representation. The model can learn to construct and modify graphs in sophisticated ways based on textual input, and also to use the graphs to produce a variety of outputs. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the GGT-NN model on the bAbI tasks and two additional tasks, including a cellular automaton and a Turing machine.

I recommend accepting this paper.

My decision is based on the following reasons:

1. The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to modeling graph-structured data, which is an important problem in many applications.
2. The GGT-NN model is well-designed and effectively evaluated on a range of tasks, demonstrating its ability to learn complex graph structures and transitions.

Supporting arguments for the reasons:

1. The paper provides a clear and concise introduction to the problem of modeling graph-structured data and the limitations of existing approaches. The authors motivate the need for a new approach that can learn to construct and modify graphs in a flexible and efficient manner.
2. The GGT-NN model is well-designed, with a clear and intuitive architecture that combines graph transformations with a recurrent input model. The authors provide a detailed description of the model and its components, including the graph transformation operations and the supervision mechanism.
3. The evaluation of the GGT-NN model is thorough and well-executed, with a range of experiments on the bAbI tasks and two additional tasks. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of the model in learning complex graph structures and transitions, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the results and their implications.

Additional feedback:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the relationship between the GGT-NN model and other approaches to modeling graph-structured data, such as Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) and Graph Convolutional Networks (GCNs).
* The authors could provide more insight into the choice of graph transformation operations and the supervision mechanism, and how these components contribute to the overall effectiveness of the model.
* The evaluation could be strengthened by including more baselines and comparisons to other state-of-the-art models, particularly on the bAbI tasks.

Questions for the authors:

* Can you provide more detail on the implementation of the graph transformation operations and the supervision mechanism, and how these components are optimized during training?
* How do you plan to address the issue of scalability and efficiency in the GGT-NN model, particularly for large and complex graphs?
* Can you discuss the potential applications of the GGT-NN model in other domains and tasks, and how the model could be adapted and extended to address these applications?
