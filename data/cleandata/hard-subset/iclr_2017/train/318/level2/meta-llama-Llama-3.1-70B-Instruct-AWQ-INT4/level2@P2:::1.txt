This paper introduces the Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) model, an extension of Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks (GGS-NNs) that uses graph-structured data as an intermediate representation. The model can learn to construct and modify graphs in sophisticated ways based on textual input, and also to use the graphs to produce a variety of outputs.

I decide to **accept** this paper, with the main reasons being the novelty and significance of the proposed GGT-NN model, as well as the thorough evaluation of the model on various tasks.

The GGT-NN model is well-motivated, building upon existing work on GGS-NNs and addressing the limitation of fixed graph structures. The paper provides a clear and detailed description of the model, including the differentiable graph transformations, the GGT-NN architecture, and the supervision methods. The experiments are thorough and well-designed, demonstrating the effectiveness of the GGT-NN model on various tasks, including the bAbI tasks, rule discovery tasks, and comparison with related work.

The paper also provides a good discussion of the advantages and limitations of the GGT-NN model, including its ability to process data in a distributed fashion, its flexibility in handling different types of input and output, and its potential applications in various domains.

However, I do have some minor issues with the paper. Firstly, the notation and terminology used in the paper can be confusing at times, and it would be helpful to provide a clearer explanation of the notation and terminology used. Secondly, the paper could benefit from more visualizations and illustrations of the graph structures and transformations, to help readers better understand the model and its applications. Finally, the paper could provide more discussion on the potential applications and future directions of the GGT-NN model, beyond the tasks and experiments presented in the paper.

To improve the paper, I would suggest the following:

* Provide a clearer explanation of the notation and terminology used in the paper.
* Include more visualizations and illustrations of the graph structures and transformations.
* Provide more discussion on the potential applications and future directions of the GGT-NN model.
* Consider adding more experiments or evaluations to demonstrate the robustness and generalizability of the GGT-NN model.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of graph neural networks, and I believe it has the potential to inspire new research directions and applications in this area.

I would like to ask the authors to clarify the following points:

* Can you provide more details on the implementation of the differentiable graph transformations, including the node addition, node state update, edge update, propagation, and aggregation transformations?
* How do you handle the case where the input graph is empty or has no nodes?
* Can you provide more discussion on the potential applications of the GGT-NN model in real-world domains, such as natural language processing, computer vision, or robotics?
* How do you plan to address the limitation of the GGT-NN model in terms of its computational complexity and scalability?
