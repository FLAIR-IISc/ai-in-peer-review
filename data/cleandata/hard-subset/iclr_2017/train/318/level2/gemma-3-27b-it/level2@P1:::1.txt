This paper introduces the Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), an extension to existing Gated Graph Sequence Neural Networks (GGS-NNs) that leverages graph-structured data as an intermediate representation and allows for graph transformations. The model aims to learn to construct and modify graphs based on input, and then utilize these graphs for various tasks, demonstrated through bAbI tasks, a cellular automaton, and Turing machines. The approach is well-motivated by the prevalence of graph structures in various data types and builds upon prior work in GNNs and GGS-NNs.

I recommend **rejecting** the paper in its current form. While the idea of learning graph transformations is interesting and the empirical results are promising, the paper lacks sufficient clarity in explaining the numerous components and configurations, making it difficult to fully assess the technical contribution and reproducibility of the results. Additionally, the comparison to existing work, while extensive, doesn’t fully highlight the unique advantages of GGT-NN beyond the ability to learn graph transformations from scratch.

Here are the supporting arguments for my decision:

1. **Lack of Clarity & Excessive Detail:** The paper is extremely dense and includes a significant amount of detailed mathematical notation and description of various transformations. While thoroughness is appreciated, the presentation feels overwhelming and makes it challenging to grasp the core contributions and overall architecture. For example, the five graph transformations (Node addition, Node state update, Edge update, Propagation, and Aggregation) are each described in considerable detail, which obscures the broader picture of how they work together within the GGT-NN framework. The numerous equations, particularly in Appendix B, detract from readability. The discussion of direct reference, while potentially beneficial, adds another layer of complexity without clear justification of its necessity.

2. **Insufficient Evaluation & Ablation Studies:** The experiments demonstrate that the GGT-NN can achieve good performance on the bAbI tasks and rule discovery tasks. However, there's a lack of comprehensive ablation studies to isolate the impact of the key innovations – the graph transformations and the ability to learn the graph structure. For example, how much does performance degrade if the graph structure is pre-defined instead of learned? What is the contribution of each individual transformation? Without such analyses, it's hard to determine which aspects of the model are truly driving the results. The comparison with strong baselines is positive, but a more focused comparison highlighting the specific benefits of GGT-NN’s learned graph structure would be more convincing.

3. **Reproducibility Concerns:** While the authors state that the code is available, the sheer complexity of the model and the numerous hyperparameters make reproduction a significant challenge. A more streamlined implementation or a more detailed explanation of the key hyperparameters and training procedures would greatly improve reproducibility. The various task-specific configurations described throughout the paper also add to this concern.

Here's some additional feedback aimed at improving the paper:

* **Streamline the Presentation:** Prioritize clarity and conciseness. Consider moving much of the detailed mathematical notation and implementation specifics to appendices. Focus the main body of the paper on the core ideas and high-level architecture. Visual diagrams illustrating the data flow and transformation process would be extremely helpful.
* **Focus on the Key Contributions:** Clearly articulate what distinguishes GGT-NN from existing approaches like GGS-NNs and other graph neural networks. What specific problem does GGT-NN solve that others cannot?
* **Conduct Ablation Studies:** Perform systematic ablation studies to evaluate the impact of each component of the GGT-NN. This will help identify the key drivers of performance and justify the complexity of the model.
* **Provide More Detailed Training Information:** Include details about the hyperparameters, optimization algorithms, and training procedures used in the experiments.
* **Clarify the Role of Direct Reference:** Provide a clearer justification for the use of direct reference and demonstrate its impact on performance through ablation studies.
* **Address the Scalability Issues:** Discuss the limitations of the model in terms of scalability and potential approaches to address these limitations.

I have the following questions for the authors:

* Could you provide a simplified diagram illustrating the overall architecture of the GGT-NN and the flow of information between different components?
* What is the computational cost of the graph transformations compared to traditional graph neural network operations?
* What specific design choices were made to ensure the differentiability of the graph transformations? Were there alternative approaches considered?
* Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the types of graphs learned by the GGT-NN on the bAbI tasks and the rule discovery tasks? What patterns or structures emerged?
* How does the performance of the GGT-NN vary with the size and complexity of the graph? Is there a point at which the model becomes computationally intractable?
