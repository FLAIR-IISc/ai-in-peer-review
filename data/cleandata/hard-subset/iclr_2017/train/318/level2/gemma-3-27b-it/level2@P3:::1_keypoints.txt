- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that builds and modifies graph-structured intermediates from text and is evaluated on bAbI, cellular automata, and Turing machines.  
- Recommendation: Reject.  
- Main critique: Insufficient novelty relative to existing graph neural and memory network models.  
- Empirical gains are limited and do not clearly outperform state-of-the-art methods to justify added complexity.  
- Results on bAbI largely reiterate performance of models like MemNN and DMN+; improvements on tasks 5, 8, 16, and 19 are modest.  
- Performance on task 17 is notably weak, indicating limitations in relational reasoning capacity.  
- Experiments on rule discovery (cellular automaton, Turing machines) are interesting but lack comparison to specialized alternative methods.  
- Paper lacks thorough comparative analysis, including statistical significance testing.  
- Computational cost and scalability (quadratic complexity) are not sufficiently analyzed and raise concerns for larger datasets.  
- Need for ablation studies to quantify contributions of model components (e.g., direct reference, intermediate propagation).  
- Request clearer articulation of novelty, especially the unique contribution of differentiable graph transformations.  
- Suggest discussion of potential optimizations to address quadratic scaling and applicability to realistic datasets.  
- Improve clarity of appendices by making them more concise and removing repetition.  
- Clarify Algorithm 1 (define i(k) and D(k) within the algorithm).  
- Explain why the extended model using a sequence of graphs performs slightly worse and propose remedies.
