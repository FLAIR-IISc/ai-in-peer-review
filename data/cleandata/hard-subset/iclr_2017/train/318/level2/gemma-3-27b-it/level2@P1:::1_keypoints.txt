- Paper introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) to learn and apply graph construction and transformations for downstream tasks (bAbI, cellular automata, Turing machines).  
- Recommendation: reject in current form despite interesting idea and promising results.  
- Main critique: presentation is overly dense with heavy mathematical detail that obscures core contributions and architecture.  
- Specific clarity issue: extensive descriptions of five graph transformations and Appendix equations make it hard to see how components interact.  
- Evaluation concern: lacks comprehensive ablation studies isolating the impact of learned graph structure and individual transformations.  
- Comparative claim: comparisons are extensive but do not clearly demonstrate unique advantages of GGT-NN beyond learning graphs from scratch.  
- Reproducibility worry: model complexity, many hyperparameters, and task-specific configurations make reproduction difficult despite code availability.  
- Suggested fix: streamline presentation, move detailed math to appendices, and add clear diagrams of the architecture and data flow.  
- Suggested fix: clearly state and emphasize the unique contributions relative to GGS-NNs and other GNNs.  
- Suggested fix: run systematic ablation studies to quantify contributions of each component, including direct reference.  
- Suggested fix: provide detailed training/hyperparameter information and discuss scalability limits and mitigation strategies.  
- Questions for authors: request for simplified architecture diagram and info on computational cost of graph transformations vs. standard GNN ops.  
- Questions for authors: ask which design choices ensure differentiability of transformations and whether alternatives were considered.  
- Questions for authors: request analysis of learned graph structures on tasks and performance scaling with graph size/complexity.
