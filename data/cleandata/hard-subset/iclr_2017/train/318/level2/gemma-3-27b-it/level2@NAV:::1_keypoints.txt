- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that constructs and modifies graphs for downstream tasks.  
- Core idea is promising and shows capability on bAbI, cellular automata, and Turing machine tasks.  
- Reviewer recommends rejecting the paper in current form but encourages revision and resubmission.  
- Presentation lacks sufficient clarity and rigor to convincingly demonstrate significance and generalizability.  
- Major concern: insufficient or inappropriate comparative baselines, lacking comparisons to recent dynamic graph neural network methods.  
- Ablation study (direct reference vs. no direct reference) is superficial and doesn’t thoroughly analyze mechanisms or impact.  
- Experimental setup, especially for the Turing machine task, is underexplained (unclear success criteria for “learning” rules).  
- Authors should clarify advantages of GGT-NN’s graph transformations over existing techniques.  
- Need more detailed hyperparameter settings, training procedures, and computational resource information for reproducibility.  
- Appendix B implementation details are useful but should be streamlined for readability.  
- Paper should discuss computational complexity and scalability, given the mentioned quadratic scaling.  
- Questions raised: limitations of the current graph transformation set and types of relationships or structures the model struggles to represent.  
- Suggests exploring attention mechanisms within graph transformations to potentially improve performance.  
- Requests a detailed analysis of learned graph structures to reveal patterns and model reasoning.
