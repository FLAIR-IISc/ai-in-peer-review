- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that builds and uses graph-structured intermediate representations from text.  
- Recommendation: Reject due to incremental novelty and insufficient clarity/comparative analysis for a top-tier venue.  
- Main critique: Lack of comparison to sequence-to-sequence models (LSTMs, Transformers) that do not enforce graph structure.  
- Needs a thorough ablation showing performance with and without the graph-structured intermediate representation.  
- State-of-the-art / competitive performance claims are not convincingly contextualized against existing approaches.  
- Description of graph transformations is buried in the appendix and needs clearer visual explanations and a more intuitive narrative.  
- The connection between the graph transformations and the GRU mechanism (especially fadd) is unclear and unjustified.  
- The benefits of “direct reference” (explicit node-type referencing) are asserted but not well explained.  
- Rule-discovery experiments lack strong comparative baselines (e.g., LSTM on sequential data).  
- Claims of generalization to longer sequences are stated with insufficient supporting evidence.  
- Paper organization is disjointed; transitions between background, transformations, and model are awkward.  
- Recommended ablation studies: remove/disable specific transformations (edge updates, direct reference) to isolate component contributions.  
- Visualization request: show learned graphs for bAbI and cellular automaton tasks to aid understanding.  
- Provide more hyperparameter-tuning details (learning rate, batch size, propagation steps).  
- Discuss computational cost and complexity, particularly quadratic scaling with graph size.  
- Provide error analysis, especially for failures on task 17, to guide improvements.  
- Move crucial implementation details from the appendix into the main text for clarity.  
- Question: What is the computational overhead of the graph-structured intermediate representation versus standard seq-to-seq on bAbI?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to choices of f_fwd_y and f_bwd_y in propagation; were alternatives explored?  
- Question: Can you provide concrete examples/visualizations of graphs learned for specific bAbI tasks?  
- Question: How does the extended model (Algorithm 2) perform versus the original model given the same training data on bAbI?  
- Question: Why were GRU units chosen for node updates and fadd instead of other recurrent architectures?
