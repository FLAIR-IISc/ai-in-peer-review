- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) with differentiable graph transformations for building and modifying internal graph states.  
- Demonstrates SOTA on 19/20 bAbI QA tasks, showing strong text-to-relational-structure parsing and reasoning.  
- Shows the model can learn dynamics of cellular automata and a family of Turing machines, indicating discovery of complex rule-based transitions.  
- Recommendation: Accept — judged a significant, novel contribution bridging fixed-graph GNNs and memory-augmented networks.  
- Key strength: novel, interpretable, dynamically changing graph formulation that learns to construct its own graph rather than assuming fixed structure.  
- Empirical validation is thorough and diverse, with particularly strong results on graph-suited bAbI tasks (e.g., pathfinding, induction).  
- Limitation: training relies on strong supervision with ground-truth graphs, which is an important caveat.  
- Limitation: quadratic complexity of the edge update transformation poses potential scalability bottlenecks.  
- Suggested improvement: discuss avenues to relax strong supervision (e.g., semi-supervised methods or reinforcement learning with final-answer reward).  
- Suggested improvement: mention potential solutions to edge-update complexity such as sparse or attention-based edge modifications.  
- Question: How robust is the model to imperfect or partially missing supervision graphs during training?  
- Question: Have you considered learning the "direct reference" mechanism (e.g., via attention) instead of relying on word matching?  
- Question: Were the five graph transformations chosen empirically as sufficient, and did you test larger or smaller sets of primitive operations?
