- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), extending GGS-NN to learn and modify graph-structured intermediate representations.  
- Claims GGT-NN can solve diverse tasks (bAbI, rule discovery) and generalize to larger inputs.  
- Argues advantages over existing models: handles unstructured input, modifies graphs, and distributes computation across nodes.  
- Experimental results show high accuracy on most tasks and often outperform state-of-the-art baselines.  
- Provides detailed performance analysis, limitations, and potential application discussion.  
- Highlights usefulness for extracting graph-structured information from text and for parallelizing complex computations.  
- Situates work within related literature; novelty is permitting graph-structured intermediate representations.  
- Paper is comprehensive in model description, components, and experimental analysis.  
- Acknowledges limitations: high training computational cost and need for extra supervision to obtain meaningful graphs.  
- Suggests mitigation strategies like sparse edge connections and reduced supervision requirements.  
- Recommends acceptance, calling the model a significant contribution with thorough presentation and empirical support.  
- Requests for improvement: more implementation details, deeper discussion of applications, and richer analysis/comparisons.  
- Questions for authors: specify architectures for graph transformations/output, plans to reduce training cost, and detailed application scenarios.
