- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) that constructs and modifies graph structures from textual input to produce outputs.  
- Demonstrates strong performance on bAbI (95%+ accuracy on 19/20 tasks), a Rule 30 cellular automaton, and a family of Turing machines.  
- Reviewer recommends accept; sees a significant contribution in enabling graph construction from unstructured input vs requiring fixed graphs.  
- Empirical results are strong and state-of-the-art on several bAbI tasks, showing advantages of graphical representations for reasoning.  
- Paper is well-motivated and well-placed in the literature, clearly explaining limitations of existing fixed-graph approaches.  
- Section 6 provides a thorough comparison with related work, emphasizing explicit graph representations versus implicit memory structures.  
- Evaluation is comprehensive and scientifically rigorous, including analyses with and without direct reference and multiple ablations.  
- Ablation studies show direct reference greatly improves performance (95% accuracy on 19/20 tasks vs 9/20 without it).  
- Success on cellular automata and Turing machines provides compelling evidence the model can learn complex update rules from unstructured input.  
- Paper acknowledges failure on Task 17 (Positional Reasoning) but should provide deeper analysis of causes (e.g., larger entity set or shorter stories).  
- Suggests adding a concrete example showing how textual phrases map to learned graph structures to clarify the graph-learning process.  
- Notes computational complexity is quadratic in graph size and recommends discussing practical mitigation strategies (e.g., sparse connections, attention, graph sparsification).  
- Requests clearer comparison of required training data vs other state-of-the-art methods, given the model’s use of strong supervision (full graph info).  
- Clarification question: How does the model handle ambiguous references to node types when multiple candidate nodes exist?  
- Clarification question: In the cellular automaton task, how does the model learn the correct initial graph (e.g., number of initial cells like 13)?  
- Clarification question: Which aspects of the direct reference mechanism drive the large improvement on Task 19 (pathfinding), and how can that inform future work?
