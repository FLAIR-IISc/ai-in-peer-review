- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN), extending GGS-NNs to learn construction and modification of graph-structured internal representations from text.  
- Main technical contribution: a set of differentiable graph transformations (node addition, state update, edge update, propagation, aggregation) to incrementally build and use graphs.  
- Addresses a gap in GNN research by enabling graph construction from unstructured input rather than requiring pre-existing graphs.  
- Strong empirical results on bAbI tasks: 95% accuracy on 19/20 tasks.  
- Successful rule-discovery experiments (cellular automata, Turing machines), showing the model can learn underlying update rules without explicit supervision.  
- Recommendation: accept — paper addresses a meaningful problem and empirical results robustly support claims.  
- Clear motivation and literature placement, distinguishing GGT-NNs from GNNs, GGS-NNs, memory networks, and DNCs.  
- Model architecture and differentiable transformations are well-defined and clearly described.  
- Outperforms strong baselines on several bAbI tasks, notably task 19 (pathfinding), due to natural fit of graphical representations.  
- Rule-discovery tasks further validate the model’s ability to learn complex graph-based update dynamics.  
- Suggested improvement: provide a more detailed discussion of scalability limits and concrete examples of how quadratic propagation complexity affects larger graphs.  
- Suggested improvement: clarify the implementation and necessity of the “direct reference” update mechanism.  
- Suggested improvement: include direct computational comparisons (training time, memory) with GGS-NNs, beyond noting GGS-NNs’ preprocessing requirement.  
- Two author questions: how much supervision is actually required for bAbI (could learning occur with weaker or final-timestep-only supervision?), and how does the model handle significant structural changes between timesteps (e.g., node addition)—explicit mechanism or implicit via transformations?
