- Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) to build and modify graph-structured intermediate representations from text.  
- Claims the model can learn to produce internal graphs from natural language, use them for question answering, and discover rules for cellular automata and Turing machines.  
- Key contributions: a novel graph transformation framework (node addition, state updates, edge modifications, propagation, aggregation) plus empirical demonstrations across multiple domains.  
- Recommendation: accept; the paper makes a clear, significant contribution by enabling dynamic construction/modification of graph structures rather than only fixed graph inputs.  
- Strong empirical results: 95%+ accuracy on 19/20 bAbI tasks (with direct reference improving performance) and successful learning of Rule 30 cellular automata and 4-state Turing machine rules.  
- Experiments are well-designed with appropriate baselines and thorough analysis.  
- Positions the work well within literature, clearly differentiating GGT-NN from Memory Networks and DNCs via explicit graph-structured internal state.  
- Demonstrates particular value for tasks naturally suited to graphs (e.g., pathfinding in bAbI task 19).  
- Suggestion: add discussion of when graph-structured intermediate representations are necessary versus when simpler approaches suffice.  
- Suggestion: include concrete analysis/examples of the quadratic time/space complexity limitation and graph sizes where the model becomes impractical.  
- Suggestion: clarify whether the proposed graph transformations apply to other GNN architectures beyond the specific GGT-NN implementation.  
- Paper provides sufficient detail for reproducibility and appropriately acknowledges limitations (computational complexity and supervision requirements).  
- Overall judgment: a meaningful advancement in neural network architectures for structured data processing.
