- Paper proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Network (GGT-NN) to build/modify graph-structured intermediate representations from text and apply them to bAbI, cellular automata, and Turing machine tasks.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main reason: Novel contribution relative to prior GGS-NNs (Li et al., 2016) is unclear and claims lack rigorous comparisons/analyses.  

Supporting arguments:
- The paper fails to clearly distinguish how GGT-NN extends GGS-NN despite citing GGS-NN as the basis.  
- bAbI evaluation is ambiguous about training set size (1k vs 10k) and compares results inconsistently across different training regimes.  
- Claims of rule discovery for cellular automata and Turing machines are unsubstantiated; high accuracy may reflect memorization rather than learning generalizable rules, with no representation or generalization analyses.  
- Limitations are insufficiently addressed—quadratic complexity is mentioned but its practical impact and scaling behavior are not evaluated or demonstrated.  

Additional feedback:
- Clarify specific limitations of GGS-NN that GGT-NN addresses and the new capabilities GGT-NN provides.  
- Provide analyses showing the model learns underlying rules and generalizes to unseen examples.  
- Ensure fair, rigorous comparisons with prior work using the same number of training examples.  
- Discuss scalability and practical applicability given quadratic complexity, with empirical evidence.  
- Explicitly state whether reported bAbI accuracies are based on 1k or 10k training examples.  

Questions for authors:
- How exactly does GGT-NN differ from GGS-NN in capability and design, and which GGS-NN limitations are resolved?  
- Can you provide concrete evidence that the model learns rules for cellular automata and Turing machines rather than memorizing training data?  
- What are the training time and memory requirements for your tasks, and how do these scale with graph size?  
- Which experimental results correspond to 1,000 versus 10,000 training examples?
