- Proposal: Introduces Gated Graph Transformer Neural Networks (GGT-NNs) — a modular, differentiable framework with soft node/edge existence, trainable add/update/propagate/aggregate transformations, and end-to-end pipelines for text→graph→answer or graph→graph tasks.
- Results summary: With strong supervision and optional direct reference, achieves ≥95% on 19/20 bAbI tasks, succeeds on Rule 30 CA and 2-symbol 4-state Turing machine rule-discovery with apparent generalization.
- Decision: Reject.
- Key reason 1: Heavy reliance on strong supervision and dataset modifications undermines evidence the approach works in realistic, weakly supervised settings.
- Key reason 2: Empirical evaluation lacks crucial ablations and fair, apples-to-apples comparisons to dynamic-memory baselines under identical supervision, so advantages beyond bAbI are not convincingly established.
- Motivation/placement: Learning dynamic graph-structured latent states from text is timely and well-motivated, and the decomposition into differentiable graph transformations is clear and useful.
- Novelty: Meaningful incremental extension of GGS-NN to dynamic graph construction (soft nodes/edges, direct reference, modular operators), but many components resemble standard gated updates and attention.
- bAbI concerns: Main bAbI gains come from full graph supervision, direct reference, and dataset alterations (exposing generator knowledge, history nodes, collapsing entities), making comparisons to end-to-end baselines unfair.
- bAbI variant weakness: Non–direct-reference model performs substantially worse on many tasks, indicating dependence on direct matching.
- Rule-discovery strengths: Demonstrates learning of structured transition rules and generalization to longer rollouts in synthetic CA and Turing machine settings.
- Rule-discovery limitations: Lacks baselines and ablations to identify which transformations are essential for success.
- Missing analyses: No systematic ablations of Tadd/Th/Th,direct/TC/Tprop/Trepr, intermediate propagation, or supervision granularity.
- Robustness gaps: Limited investigation of robustness to vocabulary mismatch for direct reference and sensitivity to thresholds for soft connectivity.
- Scaling concerns: Quadratic time/space complexity noted, but empirical scaling curves and sparse/message-passing mitigations are not explored.
- Fairness recommendation: Train baselines with equivalent strong supervision or provide GGT-NN variants under end-to-end regimes for fair comparison.
- Dataset transparency: Clarify exactly what information was exposed from the bAbI generator and results on the unmodified dataset.
- Ablation recommendations: Evaluate which operators are necessary (e.g., can tasks be solved with only TC+Tprop?) and sensitivity to number of node/edge types and node caps.
- Efficiency suggestions: Provide runtime/memory vs |V|, Y and explore sparse top-k or learned sparsity to reduce O(|V|^2) cost.
- Generalization suggestion: Evaluate on real datasets without graph supervision (e.g., IE or KB completion) and test robustness to imperfect direct reference (synonyms/paraphrases).
- Interpretability suggestion: Visualize learned graphs and node/edge evolution across sentences to illustrate interpretability and mechanism for tasks like pathfinding.
- Training/implementation notes: Mathematical descriptions are mostly clear and code availability is a plus.
- Questions — node/edge types: How are N and Y chosen per task and do they scale with vocabulary/entities or are fixed a priori?
- Questions — permutation matching cost: What is the computational cost of node permutation matching during training and its scaling with max nodes per step?
- Questions — node addition stats: For bAbI, how many nodes added per sentence on average and sensitivity to the cap?
- Questions — non-direct-reference mechanism: In the non–direct-reference setting, what substitutes for hard type matches and can learned attention replace it?
- Questions — soft connectivity at test: How are soft connectivity values C thresholded or discretized at test time and does calibration matter?
- Questions — ablation requests: Can you report ablations removing Tadd or TC for tasks that should only need one of those?
- Questions — rule-discovery failure modes: In rule tasks, how often do learned graphs deviate transiently yet recover, and are there failure modes when generalizing to much longer horizons (e.g., 100 steps)?
