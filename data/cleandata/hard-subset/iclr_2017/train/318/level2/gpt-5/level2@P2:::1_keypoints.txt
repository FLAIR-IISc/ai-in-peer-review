- Proposes Gated Graph Transformer Neural Networks (GGT-NNs): differentiable graph transformations to construct/modify/query an explicit graph state from sequential text.  
- Strong results on bAbI (≥95% on 19/20) and success on synthetic Rule 30 and 2-symbol 4-state Turing machine tasks.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Major flaw: heavy reliance on strong supervision and direct reference undermines the central claim of learning graphs from raw text.  
- Comparisons are not apples-to-apples: many baselines are trained end-to-end without the graph supervision used here.  
- Novelty is incremental over GGS-NN/GNN/message-passing architectures (adds continuous node/edge strengths and trainable add/update transforms).  
- bAbI setup exposes generator’s ground-truth graphs and uses graph-level losses plus permutation alignment, effectively circumventing structure induction from text.  
- Without direct reference, performance drops substantially (≥95% on only 9/20), so no evidence the model can learn useful graph structure from answer-only supervision.  
- Synthetic experiments are capability demos but small-scale; Turing-machine experiment mainly executes provided rules rather than discovering them.  
- Missing/unclear technical details: permutation-max procedure (solver and cost), complexity and scaling (O(|V|^2Y)), discretization thresholds for nodes/edges, and limited ablation analysis.  
- Generality and significance unclear: no real-world graph-centric evaluations and questionable scalability to larger graphs or noisy entity mentions.  
- Suggested improvements: run end-to-end/no-graph-supervision variants or semi-supervised mixes, report runtime/memory/graph-size/ sparsity, detail permutation/thresholding, add ablations, and evaluate at least one non-synthetic graph task.  
- Request for fairer comparisons: include GGS-NN baselines with comparable supervision or train GGT-NN without graph supervision for baseline parity.  
- Key questions posed to authors: how permutation max is computed and its overhead; how thresholds for discretization are chosen and sensitivity; results without graph supervision; max feasible graph size and profiling; whether rules can be inferred from trajectories; GGS-NN baseline comparison; robustness to noisy/entity variations.
