Summary of claims and contributions
The paper reframes single-image super-resolution as amortised MAP inference and derives a principled objective: minimizing the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the distribution of network outputs and the HR image prior. A key architectural contribution is an affine projection layer using A+ that guarantees exact LR–HR consistency (Aŷ = x), turning the constrained MAP problem into unconstrained training. Three training routes are proposed: (i) AffGAN, a GAN variant with “instance noise” that approximately minimizes KL[qθ||pY]; (ii) denoiser-guided SR (AffDG) that backpropagates score estimates from a DAE; and (iii) a likelihood-guided baseline (AffLL) using a PixelCNN/MCGSM prior. Experiments on a 2D toy problem, textures, CelebA, and ImageNet show the affine projection helps training and that AffGAN yields the most perceptually plausible results; a connection to amortised variational inference is provided.

Decision: Accept
Key reasons: (1) Conceptual novelty and clarity: a clean reduction of SR MAP to matching output marginals via an elegant affine projection that enforces measurement consistency exactly. (2) Practical impact: the projection layer and instance-noise-stabilized GAN provide a usable recipe that produces sharp results without paired supervision.

Supporting arguments
- The derivation from constrained MAP to H[qθ, pY] is sound and insightful; the affine projection is simple, general, and demonstrably preserves LR consistency (zero error) while not hurting MSE training.  
- On the Swiss-roll, AffGAN/AffDG approach the MAP cross-entropy, supporting the main claim.  
- On real images, AffGAN delivers sharper, plausible outputs; qualitative gains align with the objective (plausible priors over HR).  
- Literature positioning is strong (GANs, f-divergences, denoising-score links), and the VI connection is a valuable theoretical add-on.

Additional feedback to help improve the paper (not decisive for acceptance)
- Add quantitative perceptual evaluation (LPIPS, FID/KID, user studies) and broader SR baselines; report runtime and parameter counts.  
- Provide an ablation of instance noise (levels, annealing) vs. alternatives (one-sided label smoothing, WGAN-GP).  
- Quantify sensitivity to A/A+ mismatch and degradation errors; discuss blind SR and noisy LR.  
- Strengthen AffDG/LL with modern score/diffusion priors or improved density models; include more thorough training/stability analysis.  
- Clarify the extent of “unpaired” training: how is pX obtained in practice?

Questions for the authors
1) How do you obtain pX during training—by downsampling HR data or from an independent LR dataset—and does the method truly avoid paired supervision?  
2) How sensitive is performance to inaccuracies in A+ and to mismatch between assumed A and the true degradation?  
3) Can you quantify the impact of instance noise vs. baseline GAN training and vs. WGAN-GP?  
4) Why did AffDG/LL underperform—capacity, estimator bias, or optimization—and could score-based/diffusion priors fix this?  
5) Beyond exact LR consistency, how can semantics drift (e.g., snake→water) be controlled?  
6) What are the computational costs and convergence characteristics relative to MSE-trained SR?
