Summary and contributions
The paper tackles single-image super-resolution (SR) by recasting it as amortised MAP inference under a known observation model x = Ay. The key idea is to enforce exact data-consistency via an affine projection layer gθ(x) = (I − A+A)fθ(x) + A+x, ensuring Aŷ = x for any network output. Under this architecture, training for MAP reduces to minimising the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the model’s output distribution qθ and the HR data distribution pY, enabling training without paired LR/HR data. The paper proposes three practical approaches to approximate this objective: (1) AffGAN, a GAN variant with a generator fed by LR images and an analysis suggesting it minimises KL[q‖p]; (2) AffDG, which uses gradients from a denoiser to estimate ∇y log pY(y) and backpropagate through the affine-projected generator; and (3) AffLL, which uses a differentiable likelihood model (PixelCNN-like with MCGSM) as a density-guided baseline. The paper also introduces “instance noise” to stabilise GAN training, and draws a connection between stochastic AffGAN and amortised variational inference. Empirically, the affine projection improves consistency and often speeds training; AffGAN produces the most visually appealing results on textures, CelebA, and ImageNet; and toy experiments support the cross-entropy minimisation view.

Decision: Reject
Key reasons: While the architectural and theoretical framing are interesting and potentially useful, the empirical validation is limited. There is no comparison against strong SR baselines beyond the paper’s own variants, no perceptual evaluation, and the “unpaired training” claim is not demonstrated. The denoiser/density-guided methods underperform, and the AffGAN results show notable artifacts.

Supporting arguments
- Problem and motivation: The paper clearly articulates the mismatch between pixel-wise MSE training and perceptual plausibility for ill-posed SR. Framing SR as amortised MAP under a known A and targeting the prior pY is well motivated and grounded in literature on GANs and score-based denoising.

- Methodological novelty:
  - The affine projection layer is a clean and practical mechanism to enforce exact consistency Aŷ = x and can be dropped into many architectures. The numerical approach to approximate A+ is reasonable.
  - Casting amortised MAP as cross-entropy minimisation H[qθ, pY] and feeding the generator with LR images (rather than noise) is a useful perspective; it clarifies why adversarial training can be appropriate for SR.
  - The instance noise idea is coherent with recent theory on stabilising adversarial learning by smoothing distributions.
  - The Appendix F derivation linking AffGAN to amortised VI (KL over qY|X to pY|X) is insightful.

- Correctness and clarity:
  - The derivations around constrained MAP leading to Eq. (9) are sound. The interpretation that training minimises a marginal cross-entropy over ŷ(x) is correct, though “amortised MAP” is slightly stronger phrasing than what is optimised (see below).
  - The GAN objective variant intended to target KL[q‖p] is plausible (in line with Huszár, 2016), but the exact generator loss is difficult to parse in the main text. A clearer statement of the implemented loss and a brief proof sketch in the main paper would help.
  - The instance noise discussion is well reasoned; however, only limited ablations are shown.

- Empirical support:
  - The 2D toy example is useful and shows cross-entropy improvements consistent with MAP-like behaviour.
  - For images, results are mostly qualitative; the MSE/SSIM numbers align with expectations but do not assess perceptual realism. No human studies (e.g., MOS or 2AFC) or even proxy perceptual metrics are reported. There is no comparison to contemporary SR methods (e.g., SRCNN/VDSR/ESPCN/Johnson et al./SRGAN), nor to plug-and-play/regularisation-by-denoising baselines that also leverage priors.
  - The strong claim that paired LR/HR data are not needed is not empirically validated; all experiments appear to use synthetic LR from known A applied to HR images.
  - The observation model is fixed to Gaussian blur + stride-4 decimation; generalisation to bicubic or unknown/downstream degradations is not tested. The approach relies on a known A and a good approximation to A+, which may limit applicability.
  - AffDG and AffLL underperform and exhibit training instabilities; while the discussion is honest, these methods are subsequently dropped, reducing the paper’s practical impact to the AffGAN variant.

Additional feedback and questions to improve the paper
These comments are intended to help strengthen the work; they are not all decision-critical.
- Clarify “amortised MAP”: Minimising Ex[−log pY(ŷ(x))] under the affine constraint does not guarantee ŷ(x) equals the per-x MAP mode; it optimises an average objective over x. Please discuss when this aligns with per-instance MAP and when it may trade off across inputs. The Appendix F connection suggests an alternative stochastic formulation that targets the posterior family more directly—can you empirically explore it?

- Generator loss and KL: Please state the exact generator objective used, its gradient, and provide a concise derivation in the main text showing the link to KL[q‖p]. As written, Eq. (10) is hard to interpret.

- Instance noise: Provide ablations on noise variance schedules, and comparisons to label smoothing and to not fully training D, to quantify stability and final quality. Does instance noise harm sharpness if not annealed?

- Unpaired training: Demonstrate training with unpaired pX and pY (e.g., LR from one dataset, HR from another), and test on real LR inputs with unknown degradations. This would substantiate the “no paired data” claim.

- Baselines and evaluation: Include comparisons to strong SR baselines (e.g., SRCNN, VDSR/DRRN/ESPCN, SRGAN) with standard datasets (Set5/Set14/BSD100/Urban100) and bicubic degradation. For perceptual assessment, consider human studies (2AFC/MOS) or proxy metrics (LPIPS, NIQE/BRISQUE). Report consistency Aŷ − x for all methods.

- Robustness to A: Discuss how to handle unknown or spatially varying A; can A+ be learned jointly, or can the projection be relaxed while preserving most benefits?

- Artifacts and failure modes: Some AffGAN outputs exhibit high-frequency noise and semantic drift (snake to water). Can you mitigate via multi-scale discriminators, feature matching losses, or an entropy penalty? Reporting failure rates would be helpful.

- DAE and density-guided variants: Given their conceptual appeal, can you improve stability (e.g., better score estimators, noise annealing schedules, modern density models) and provide stronger evidence? Alternatively, narrow scope to AffGAN and go deeper.

- Minor: Provide implementation details for computing A+ and sensitivity to kernel sizes; show that training with the correct A+ fixed vs learnable yields similar results across datasets.

Overall, the architectural idea of an affine projection for exact data consistency and the theoretical framing are promising and could influence future SR methods. Strengthening the empirical validation, clarifying the loss/derivations, and substantiating unpaired training would make the paper much more compelling.
