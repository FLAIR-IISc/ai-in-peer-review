- Paper reframes single-image SR as amortised MAP inference under x = Ay and enforces exact data consistency via an affine projection layer.  
- Training reduces to minimising cross-entropy H[qθ, pY], enabling learning without paired LR/HR data in principle.  
- Three practical methods proposed: AffGAN (GAN with affine projection), AffDG (denoiser-based score estimator), and AffLL (likelihood model baseline).  
- Introduces instance noise to stabilise GAN training and links stochastic AffGAN to amortised variational inference.  
- Affine projection empirically improves consistency (Aŷ = x) and can speed training; AffGAN produces visually appealing textures on some datasets.  
- Decision: reject due to limited empirical validation and unsubstantiated claims.  
- No comparisons to strong SR baselines (e.g., SRCNN/VDSR/ESPCN/SRGAN) or standard SR datasets.  
- No perceptual evaluation (human studies or perceptual metrics like LPIPS/NIQE) to support visual quality claims.  
- The “unpaired training” claim is not demonstrated; all experiments use synthetic LR from known A applied to HR images.  
- AffDG and AffLL underperform and are unstable, leaving practical value concentrated in AffGAN.  
- Observation model limited to known Gaussian blur + decimation; generalisation to unknown/bicubic degradations not tested.  
- AffGAN outputs show artifacts and semantic drift in some cases (high-frequency noise, semantic errors).  
- The affine projection and numerical A+ approximation are practical and can be inserted into architectures, but reliance on known A may limit applicability.  
- Theoretical framing and derivations (constrained MAP, Appendix F link to VI) are sound and insightful, but “amortised MAP” needs clarification about per-instance vs average objectives.  
- The GAN generator loss intended to target KL[q‖p] is plausible but poorly presented; the exact implemented loss and derivation should be clearly stated in the main text.  
- Instance noise idea is reasonable, but ablations (variance schedules, comparisons to label smoothing or partial discriminator training) are missing.  
- Empirical support: useful 2D toy experiments, but image results are mostly qualitative and lack rigorous metrics and baselines.  
- Recommend experiments: unpaired training, comparisons to SR baselines on standard datasets, perceptual evaluations (MOS/2AFC or LPIPS), and consistency metrics for all methods.  
- Suggested methodological fixes: clarify amortised MAP interpretation, clearly state generator objective and gradients, ablate instance noise, explore robustness/learning of A or relaxed projections.  
- For AffDG/AffLL: improve stability with better score/density estimators, noise annealing, or focus paper scope on AffGAN with deeper evaluation.  
- Minor: provide implementation details for computing A+, sensitivity to kernel size, and whether A+ should be fixed or learnable.
