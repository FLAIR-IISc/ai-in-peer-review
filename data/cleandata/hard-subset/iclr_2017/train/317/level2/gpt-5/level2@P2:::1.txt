Summary and contributions:
The paper tackles single-image super-resolution (SR) from a Bayesian perspective and argues for amortized MAP inference rather than empirical risk minimization with convex pixel losses. The key technical contribution is an affine projection layer that guarantees consistency with the downsampling operator A (i.e., A ŷ = x) by projecting network outputs onto the affine subspace of valid HR reconstructions via the Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse A+. With this constraint, training for MAP reduces to minimizing the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the model’s output distribution and the HR data distribution, enabling training without paired LR–HR examples. The paper proposes three training strategies: (1) AffGAN, a GAN variant with a generator loss approximating KL[q‖p] and a stabilization technique (“instance noise”); (2) AffDG, which backpropagates denoiser-derived score estimates; and (3) AffLL, using a learned likelihood (PixelCNN+MCGSM). Experiments on a 2D toy problem, textures, faces (CelebA), and ImageNet suggest AffGAN yields sharper, more plausible images and strictly enforces input–output consistency; the other two methods underperform visually. An appendix draws a connection between a stochastic AffGAN and amortized variational inference.

Decision: Reject.

Key reasons: (1) The empirical evaluation is not yet sufficiently rigorous for ICLR: there are limited quantitative perceptual metrics, no human studies, no comparison against strong contemporary SR baselines (e.g., SRGAN/perceptual-loss methods), and no results on standard SR benchmarks; (2) Two of the three proposed methods (AffDG, AffLL) fail to deliver competitive results, leaving the main empirical evidence to AffGAN, whose advantage over prior adversarial SR approaches is not convincingly established beyond the (valuable) exact-consistency constraint.

Supporting arguments:
- Problem and motivation: The critique of MSE and the case for MAP in underdetermined SR is well motivated, with a clear geometric picture (affine slices) and a principled desire to pick high-prior-probability solutions. The affine projection layer is a neat, well-placed contribution relative to prior “soft constraint” approaches, and enforcing A ŷ = x exactly is practically important.
- Placement in literature: The work connects to GANs, denoising/score matching, and PixelCNNs. The instance noise idea is timely and related to concurrent efforts on stabilizing adversarial training. However, empirical comparisons to SRGAN and VGG-feature-based perceptual losses are missing, which weakens claims of significance for SR practice.
- Do results support claims?
  • The toy Swiss-roll experiment clearly supports the MAP/cross-entropy objective and shows that AffGAN/AffDG approximate the mode better than MSE/MAE.
  • The MSE proof-of-concept shows the affine projection can speed training and enforce consistency without hurting (and sometimes improving) MSE/SSIM—a useful engineering contribution.
  • On images, evidence is mostly qualitative. PSNR/SSIM favor MSE as expected; the paper argues for perceptual quality but does not present quantitative perceptual evaluations or user studies. The texture and face examples suggest AffGAN can generate plausible detail, but occasional artifacts and “dreamed” content (e.g., snake→water) indicate a mismatch between “plausible under pY” and “faithful to x”, which deserves deeper analysis.
  • AffDG and AffLL underperform; the paper offers plausible reasons (difficulty of high-dim score and density estimation) but without turning them into strong baselines.
  • The theoretical claims (AffGAN minimizing KL[q‖p] under optimal D; connection to amortized VI) are interesting but rest on assumptions (unique optimal D, overlap via instance noise) and are not validated experimentally (e.g., ablations on noise, convergence diagnostics).

Additional feedback to improve the paper (helpful, not necessarily decision-critical):
- Strengthen empirical evaluation:
  • Include comparisons to SRGAN and feature-perceptual losses on standard SR benchmarks (Set5/14, BSD100, Urban100, DIV2K), using common degradations (bicubic A). Report metrics that better reflect perception (e.g., user study, NIQE/BRISQUE, or deep perceptual similarity when feasible for 2017).
  • Provide ablations: with/without affine projection; with/without instance noise; different generator losses; sensitivity to A+ initialization and kernel size.
  • Show consistency error distributions vs. soft constraints across datasets and training stages.
- Clarify the “no paired data” claim: In practice, training requires that pX corresponds to applying the same A to samples from pY. Discuss domain shift and sensitivity to mismatch between the LR dataset and A pY.
- Theoretical clarity:
  • Give conditions under which minimizing H[qθ, pY] yields per-x conditional modes along the affine slice (i.e., amortized MAP), not just high-prior outputs. Discuss uniqueness of modes and the impact of the entropy term in AffGAN (H[q]) on selecting among multiple modes.
  • Tighten the KL-minimization derivation for the generator loss and clarify how instance noise alters the divergence and affects the MAP interpretation.
- Denoiser/density guided methods: Either improve them (e.g., modern score matching with annealed noise; multi-scale score networks) or reposition them as exploratory. Report quantitative training stability and guidance on σ annealing schedules.
- Practicality: Discuss how to compute A+ for common degradations, computational overhead, and how to adapt to unknown A (blind SR).

Questions for the authors:
1. How sensitive is AffGAN to mismatch between the true downsampling operator and the A used for training/inference? Can you show robustness or blind-SR adaptations?
2. Can you provide ablations demonstrating the contribution of instance noise (e.g., stability, diversity, and consistency) and how you choose/anneal σ in practice?
3. Does the affine projection degrade performance when A has non-local inverses (very wide A+)? How does kernel size of A+ affect results?
4. Under what assumptions does minimizing H[qθ, pY] with the affine constraint recover the per-x MAP solution? Can you formalize this for log-concave priors along the slice or for mixture priors?
5. Could you compare to SRGAN on identical datasets/degradation and report a perceptual evaluation (even a small user study)?
6. How diverse are outputs conditioned on the same x (given AffGAN’s implicit preference for higher-entropy qθ)? Do you observe mode collapse, and if so, how is it mitigated?
