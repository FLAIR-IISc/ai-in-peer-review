- Main contribution: Reframes single-image SR as amortised MAP inference and proposes minimizing H[qθ, pY] between network outputs and the HR prior.  
- Architectural novelty: Introduces an affine projection layer (using A+) that enforces exact LR–HR consistency (Aŷ = x), converting the constrained MAP problem into unconstrained training.  
- Training methods: Proposes three routes—AffGAN (GAN with instance noise approximating KL[qθ||pY]), AffDG (denoiser-guided SR using DAE score estimates), and AffLL (likelihood-guided baseline with PixelCNN/MCGSM).  
- Experimental findings: On toy data, textures, CelebA, and ImageNet the affine projection aids training and AffGAN yields the most perceptually plausible, sharp outputs.  
- Theoretical link: Provides a connection to amortised variational inference and positions work within GANs, f-divergences, and denoising-score literature.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reasons for acceptance: Clear conceptual novelty (clean reduction to matching output marginals via affine projection) and practical impact (projection + instance-noise GAN produce sharp results without paired supervision).  
- Supporting evidence: Derivation is sound; affine projection preserves LR consistency and does not hurt MSE training; Swiss-roll results approach MAP cross-entropy; qualitative gains on real images align with the HR prior.  
- Literature positioning: Strong coverage of related work and valuable VI connection.  
- Requested empirical additions: Add quantitative perceptual metrics (LPIPS, FID/KID, user studies), broader SR baselines, and report runtime/parameter counts.  
- Ablation and robustness suggestions: Ablate instance-noise settings and alternatives (one-sided smoothing, WGAN-GP); quantify sensitivity to A/A+ mismatch and noisy or blind LR degradations.  
- Model improvements suggested: Strengthen AffDG/LL with modern score/diffusion priors or better density models and provide more training/stability analysis.  
- Clarification needed: Explain how “unpaired” pX is obtained in practice.  
- Questions for authors: How is pX obtained and does training avoid paired supervision? How sensitive is performance to inaccuracies in A+ and degradation mismatch? Can you quantify instance noise’s impact vs baseline and WGAN-GP? Why did AffDG/LL underperform and could score/diffusion priors fix this? How can semantics drift be controlled beyond LR consistency? What are computational costs and convergence behavior vs MSE-trained SR?
