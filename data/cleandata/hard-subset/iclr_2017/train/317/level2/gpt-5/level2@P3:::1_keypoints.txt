- Proposes amortised MAP inference for single-image SR with an affine projection layer g(x) = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) to guarantee exact data consistency Afθ(x)=x.  
- Implements A as strided convolution and A+ as deconvolution estimated numerically.  
- Reformulates amortised MAP as minimising H[qθ, pY], turning SR into a generative modelling problem matching qθ to pY.  
- Introduces three training strategies: AffGAN (affine-projected GAN), AffDG (denoiser-guided using a DAE), and AffLL (density-model-guided using PixelCNN/MCGSM).  
- Proposes instance noise (convolving pY and qθ with noise) to regularise and stabilise GAN training.  
- Theoretical claim: AffGAN minimises KL[qθ‖pY], with a stochastic variant performing amortised variational inference.  
- Empirical: Swiss-roll toy shows AffGAN/AffDG approach MAP in cross-entropy; CelebA proof-of-concept shows projection does not hurt MSE training.  
- Empirical: Qualitative 4× SR results (textures, faces, ImageNet) show AffGAN yields sharper, more plausible images than MSE/MAE.  
- Decision: Weak accept.  
- Primary reason 1: Affine projection layer and amortised-MAP formulation are principled and novel, ensuring guaranteed data consistency.  
- Primary reason 2: Theoretical grounding of GAN-based SR and instance-noise stabilisation are insightful and broadly relevant.  
- Primary reason 3: Empirical results demonstrate perceptual benefits and support theory on controlled tasks, though not exhaustive.  
- Motivation: Addresses mismatch between pixel-wise losses and perceptual plausibility; well positioned relative to MSE/MAE and GAN SR prior work.  
- Novelty: Exact data consistency via affine projection contrasts with soft penalties; connects SR to generative modelling without paired data.  
- Technical correctness: Projection constraint, derivations linking GAN objective to KL, A+ numerical estimation, and denoiser→∇ log p link are sound.  
- Empirical rigor: Toy experiment convincing; real-image results qualitatively good but lack standard benchmarks, perceptual studies, and comparisons to strong SR baselines.  
- Essential improvement: Add standard SR benchmarks (Set5/Set14/BSD100/Urban100), distortion and perception metrics, and comparisons to SRResNet/SRGAN/VDSR plus a small human study.  
- Essential improvement: Evaluate robustness to model mismatch when true degradation differs from A (blur/noise) and consider relaxed/learned constraints.  
- Optional improvement: Ablate instance-noise levels/schedules, compare to label smoothing/gradient penalties, and quantify training stability.  
- Optional improvement: Demonstrate truly unpaired training or clarify pairing assumptions and domain-mismatch handling.  
- Optional improvement: Release implementation details for A+ estimation and analyse sensitivity to kernel support and convergence criteria.  
- Optional improvement: Diagnose AffDG/AffLL underperformance (gradient fields, likelihood calibration) and test multiscale/noise curricula.  
- Optional improvement: Characterise AffGAN high-frequency artifacts (spectral analysis) and explore simple priors to mitigate them.  
- Clarification needed: Were LR inputs generated by applying A to HR images or drawn independently (unpaired training)?  
- Clarification needed: How is A chosen for real data, how sensitive are results to misspecification, and can A/A+ be learned while retaining projection guarantees?  
- Clarification needed: Details of instance-noise annealing schedule, discriminator training protocol (D/G steps, convergence).  
- Clarification needed: Does AffGAN’s KL objective lead to higher-entropy reconstructions vs strict MAP, and is there evidence of multimodality for the same LR input?  
- Clarification needed: Runtime and memory overhead of affine projection vs standard architectures and whether projection accelerates convergence.  
- Overall: Paper offers a principled architectural idea and theoretical insights with promising qualitative results; stronger empirical validation and robustness analysis are needed.
