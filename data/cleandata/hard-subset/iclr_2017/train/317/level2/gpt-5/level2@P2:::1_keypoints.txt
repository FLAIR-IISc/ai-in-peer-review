- Paper proposes amortized MAP inference for single-image SR with an affine projection layer enforcing A ŷ = x and reducing training to minimizing H[qθ, pY], enabling training without paired LR–HR examples.  
- Introduces three methods: AffGAN (GAN variant with instance noise), AffDG (denoiser/score backprop), and AffLL (learned likelihood using PixelCNN+MCGSM).  
- Experiments on toy data, textures, faces, and ImageNet show AffGAN produces sharper, plausible images and enforces exact consistency; AffDG and AffLL underperform visually.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main empirical critique: evaluation lacks rigorous perceptual metrics, no human studies, no comparison to strong SR baselines (e.g., SRGAN/perceptual-loss), and no results on standard SR benchmarks.  
- Method critique: two of three proposed methods fail to deliver competitive results, leaving AffGAN as the primary evidence without convincingly establishing advantage over prior adversarial SR beyond exact-consistency.  
- Strength: clear motivation against MSE, solid geometric affine-slice view, and a neat affine-projection layer that enforces consistency exactly.  
- Placement: connects to GANs, score matching, and PixelCNNs; instance noise is timely for stabilizing adversarial training.  
- Missing literature comparisons: no experiments against SRGAN or VGG-feature perceptual-loss methods, weakening practical significance claims.  
- Toy results: Swiss-roll experiment supports MAP/cross-entropy objective and shows AffGAN/AffDG approximate modes better than MSE/MAE.  
- MSE experiment: affine projection speeds training and enforces consistency without harming MSE/SSIM—useful engineering contribution.  
- Real-image results: mainly qualitative; PSNR/SSIM favor MSE as expected; no perceptual metrics or user studies to substantiate perceptual claims.  
- Artifacts: AffGAN sometimes hallucinates content (plausible under pY but unfaithful to x), indicating a tradeoff between plausibility and faithfulness needing deeper analysis.  
- AffDG/AffLL shortcomings: underperform likely due to difficulty of high-dim score/density estimation; not turned into strong baselines.  
- Theory: claims (AffGAN minimizing KL[q‖p], connection to amortized VI) are interesting but rest on assumptions (unique optimal D, instance-noise overlap) and lack experimental validation or ablations.  
- Suggested empirical improvements: compare to SRGAN and perceptual losses on standard SR benchmarks (Set5/14, BSD100, Urban100, DIV2K) with bicubic A and perception-oriented metrics (user study, NIQE/BRISQUE, deep perceptual similarity).  
- Suggested ablations: with/without affine projection, with/without instance noise, different generator losses, sensitivity to A+ initialization and kernel size.  
- Suggested analyses: show consistency error distributions vs. soft constraints across datasets and training stages.  
- Clarify "no paired data" claim: discuss requirement that pX = A pY, domain shift, and sensitivity to mismatch between LR dataset and A pY.  
- Theoretical clarifications requested: conditions where minimizing H[qθ, pY] yields per-x MAP along affine slice, uniqueness of modes, and effect of H[q] in AffGAN on mode selection.  
- Tighten derivations: clarify KL-minimization derivation for generator loss and how instance noise alters divergence and MAP interpretation.  
- Denoiser/density methods: recommend improving (annealed noise, multi-scale score networks) or repositioning them as exploratory; provide training-stability details and σ schedules.  
- Practicality: discuss computing A+ for common degradations, computational overhead, and adaptation to unknown A (blind SR).  
- Key questions for authors: sensitivity to mismatch between true downsampling and training A / blind-SR adaptations?  
- Key question: ablations on instance noise—its contribution to stability, diversity, consistency, and σ annealing strategy?  
- Key question: does affine projection degrade performance when A+ is non-local; how does A+ kernel size affect results?  
- Key question: under what assumptions does minimizing H[qθ, pY] recover per-x MAP (e.g., log-concave priors or mixtures)?  
- Key question: can you compare to SRGAN on identical datasets/degradations with a perceptual evaluation or user study?  
- Key question: how diverse are outputs conditioned on the same x, is there mode collapse, and how is it mitigated?
