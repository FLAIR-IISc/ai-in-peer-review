- Proposes an amortized MAP inference approach for image super-resolution that enforces consistency between low- and high-resolution images via an affine projection layer.  
- Introduces three solution methods: Affine Projected GAN (AffGAN), denoiser-guided SR (AffDG), and density-guided SR (AffLL).  
- AffGAN yields visually superior, sharper SR results compared to MSE-based methods in both synthetic and real-image experiments.  
- Demonstrates with a 2D Swiss-roll toy that AffGAN and AffDG minimize the MAP objective while MSE/MAE produce low-probability outputs.  
- Real-image experiments (grass, CelebA, ImageNet) show perceptually better outputs from AffGAN despite lower PSNR/SSIM for MSE methods.  
- Establishes a theoretical connection between AffGAN and variational inference, strengthening the paper’s conceptual contribution.  
- Decision: accept.  
- Suggests stronger comparisons with recent state-of-the-art SR methods using perceptual losses (e.g., Ledig et al., 2016).  
- Requests a clearer explanation for why AffDG and AffLL produced blurry results and discussion of their limitations.  
- Recommends better justification and comparison of the instance noise stabilization technique versus alternatives like label smoothing.  
- Asks for a concrete example showing how the affine projection layer is implemented in a typical CNN to clarify practicality.  
- Question: How to prevent MAP reconstructions that are visually plausible but factually incorrect (e.g., wrong object identities)?  
- Question: How does the AffLL density model quality compare to other SR density models, and why did AffLL fail despite competitive log-likelihood?  
- Question: How will the authors address MAP estimates being atypical in high dimensions and the limitations of MAP inference as dimensionality grows?
