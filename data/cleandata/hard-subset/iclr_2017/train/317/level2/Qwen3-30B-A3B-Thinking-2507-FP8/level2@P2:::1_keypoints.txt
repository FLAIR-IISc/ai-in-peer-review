- Proposes amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution using an affine projection layer to enforce LR–HR consistency and reduce the problem to minimizing cross-entropy with a natural image prior.  
- Introduces three approaches: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided).  
- Strong theoretical development highlighting the affine subspace structure of SR and a clear derivation of the method.  
- Proposes "instance noise" to stabilize GAN training and notes a connection between AffGAN and variational inference.  
- Decision: Reject — theory is interesting but empirical evidence and comparisons are insufficient.  
- Fails to properly compare to state-of-the-art SR (e.g., Ledig et al. 2016) despite citing it.  
- AffGAN yields lower PSNR than MSE methods, contradicting expectations and raising concerns about quantitative evaluation.  
- Claims of producing "more plausible" images lack human evaluations or perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS).  
- AffDG and AffLL perform poorly (blurry, non-improving) and the paper does not explain their failure.  
- The unexplained failure of AffDG/AffLL undermines the overall theoretical framework and claims of multiple viable approaches.  
- Recommend clearer differentiation from prior GAN-SR work (Ledig et al.) and explanation of novel contributions.  
- Recommend adding human studies or perceptual metrics to validate perceptual-quality claims.  
- Recommend either thoroughly analyze why AffDG/AffLL fail or remove them from main comparisons.  
- The variational-inference connection in the appendix is interesting but should be integrated into the main narrative.  
- Advise a more nuanced discussion of MAP limitations (coordinate dependence) and a justification for performing MAP in RGB space.  
- Clarification questions requested: why AffGAN has lower PSNR despite sharper images; why AffDG fails while AffGAN succeeds; and why the VI connection is relegated to the appendix.
