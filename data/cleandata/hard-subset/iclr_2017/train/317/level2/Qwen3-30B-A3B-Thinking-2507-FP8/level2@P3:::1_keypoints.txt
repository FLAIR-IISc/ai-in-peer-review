- Proposes amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution to overcome MSE-induced blurriness.
- Introduces a novel neural architecture with an affine projection layer that enforces LR–HR consistency.
- Recasts MAP inference as minimizing cross-entropy between the model’s output distribution and the natural image prior.
- Proposes three optimization methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser guidance), and AffLL (density models).
- Strong theoretical motivation: MAP targets posterior modes (more plausible) vs MSE targeting the mean (blurry).
- Affine projection is a clever technical solution to the SR consistency problem.
- Derivation connecting MAP to cross-entropy is well-justified; Appendix F links the approach to variational inference.
- Toy experiments show AffGAN and AffDG minimize the intended cross-entropy, while MSE/MAE yield low-probability outputs.
- Real-image experiments show AffGAN produces sharper, more plausible reconstructions with supporting quantitative metrics.
- Ablation study indicates the affine projection layer does not hurt performance and may improve training speed and quality.
- Recommends acceptance due to strong theory and clear experimental evidence of perceptual improvements.
- Suggests adding detailed computational cost and training-time comparisons with existing methods.
- Requests deeper analysis of why AffDG and AffLL underperform relative to AffGAN, including specific failure modes.
- Advises broader comparisons to recent state-of-the-art SR methods (e.g., perceptual-loss approaches) beyond Ledig et al. (2016).
- Recommends expanding the discussion of MAP limitations with concrete examples of representation-dependent failures.
- Notes the instance-noise technique for GAN stabilization is promising but needs stronger theoretical justification and convergence analysis.
