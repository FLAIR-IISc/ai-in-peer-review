**Summary of the paper's claims and contributions:**

This paper proposes a new approach for image super-resolution (SR) using amortized Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference. The authors argue that traditional SR methods using empirical risk minimization often produce blurry and over-smoothed outputs, and instead propose to use a MAP approach that prefers solutions with high posterior probability. To achieve this, they introduce a novel neural network architecture that performs a projection to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions, ensuring that the high-resolution output is always consistent with the low-resolution input. They also propose three methods to approximate the MAP objective: Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), denoiser-guided SR, and a baseline method using a maximum-likelihood-trained image prior. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on various image datasets, including natural images and faces.
