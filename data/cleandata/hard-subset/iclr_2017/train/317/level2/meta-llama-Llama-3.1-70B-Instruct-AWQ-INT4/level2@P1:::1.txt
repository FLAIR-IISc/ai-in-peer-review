**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

The paper proposes a novel approach to image super-resolution (SR) using amortised Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference. The authors introduce a convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture that projects the model output to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions, ensuring that the high-resolution output is always consistent with the low-resolution input. They then propose three methods to approximate the MAP estimate: (1) Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), (2) denoiser-guided SR, and (3) a baseline method using a maximum-likelihood-trained image prior. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of these methods on both toy data and real image datasets.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

I recommend accepting this paper. The key reasons for this decision are:

1. The paper proposes a novel and well-motivated approach to image SR, addressing the limitations of empirical risk minimization methods.
2. The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of their approach, including a connection between GANs and amortised variational inference.
3. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, particularly the GAN-based approach, on various datasets.
