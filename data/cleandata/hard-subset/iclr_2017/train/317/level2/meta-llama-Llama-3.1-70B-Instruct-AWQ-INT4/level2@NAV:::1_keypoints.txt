- Paper proposes amortised MAP inference for image super-resolution using a CNN that projects outputs to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions, with three MAP approximations (GAN, denoiser-guided, density-guided) and claims GANs give the best perceptual results.
- Decision: Accept.
- Novelty: Introduces a new SR approach using amortised MAP and a projection CNN to enforce LR consistency — a novel contribution.
- Theoretical soundness: Provides a thorough theoretical derivation of the MAP objective and a stochastic AffGAN variant, well-supported by prior work.
- Experimental results: Demonstrates effectiveness on a 2D toy dataset, grass textures, CelebA faces, and ImageNet, especially for GAN-based models.
- Comparison to state-of-the-art: Reviewer requests explicit comparisons to current SOTA SR methods to clarify advantages and limitations.
- Limitations: Reviewer asks for a more detailed discussion of limitations (e.g., assumption that distribution mode depends on representation) and directions for future work.
- Code and data/reproducibility: Reviewer requests more details about code, datasets, preprocessing, and hyperparameter tuning to facilitate reproducibility.
- Question 1: What is the computational complexity of your approach compared to other SOTA SR methods?
- Question 2: How will you address limitations such as the assumption that the mode depends on the representation?
- Question 3: Please provide more information on code, data, preprocessing, and hyperparameter tuning used in experiments.
