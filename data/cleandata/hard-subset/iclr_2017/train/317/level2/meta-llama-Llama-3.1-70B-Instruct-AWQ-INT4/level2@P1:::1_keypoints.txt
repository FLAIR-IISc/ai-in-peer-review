- Proposes amortised MAP inference for image super-resolution using a CNN that projects outputs to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions to ensure LR consistency.
- Introduces three MAP approximation methods: GANs, denoiser-guided SR, and a maximum-likelihood-trained image prior baseline.
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis and links GANs to amortised variational inference.
- Affine projection layer highlighted as a key innovation enforcing HR–LR consistency.
- Experimental results on toy and real datasets demonstrate effectiveness, with GAN-based approach performing best.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty, solid theory, and convincing experiments.
- Presentation is clear and well-structured; contributions are significant and well-connected to prior work.
- Requests more implementation details for the affine projection layer (hyperparameters, optimization).
- Suggests discussing limitations, especially computational complexity and application scope.
- Recommends adding more visualizations, particularly for grass texture and ImageNet results.
- Asks to clarify connections to existing SR work, especially regarding GANs and variational inference.
- Question: provide more detail on hyperparameter choices for the affine projection layer and GAN approach.
- Question: plans for extending the methods to denoising, deblurring, or other applications?
- Question: discuss potential limitations regarding computational complexity and applicability.
- Question: how will the GAN-based approach handle mode collapse in high-dimensional image spaces?
