- Proposes amortised MAP inference for image super-resolution using a CNN that projects outputs to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer to enforce high-resolution outputs consistent with the low-resolution input.  
- Presents three optimisation methods: GAN-based, denoiser-guided SR, and a maximum-likelihood-trained image prior baseline.  
- Experiments show the GAN-based approach performs best on real image data.  
- Decision: paper accepted.  
- Key reason: novel and well-motivated amortised MAP approach, a significant contribution to SR.  
- Key reason: thorough theoretical analysis, including derivation of affine projection and GAN–amortised variational inference connection.  
- Key reason: experiments demonstrate effectiveness, especially for the GAN method.  
- Supporting: paper is well-written and clearly explains the approach and theory.  
- Supporting: provides a comprehensive review of related work in SR and GANs.  
- Supporting: experiments are well-designed and include comparisons to existing methods.  
- Suggestion: discuss limitations, especially the Gaussian prior assumption.  
- Suggestion: provide more implementation details for the affine projection layer and GAN method.  
- Suggestion: include more visualisations for grass texture and CelebA results.  
- Question: provide hyperparameter details for the GAN method (learning rate, batch size).  
- Question: plans for extending the approach to multi-frame SR or non-Gaussian priors?  
- Question: provide deeper insights into the theoretical implications of the GAN vs amortised variational inference connection.
