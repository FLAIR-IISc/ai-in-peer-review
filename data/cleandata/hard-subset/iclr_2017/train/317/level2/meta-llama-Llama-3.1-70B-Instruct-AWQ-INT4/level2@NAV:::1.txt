**Summary of the Paper's Claims**

The paper proposes a novel approach to image super-resolution (SR) using amortised Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference. The authors introduce a convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture that projects the model output to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions, ensuring that the high-resolution output is consistent with the low-resolution input. They then present three methods to approximate the MAP objective: Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), denoiser-guided SR, and density-guided SR. The authors claim that their approach produces perceptually superior SR results, especially when using GANs.

**Decision: Accept**

I accept this paper because it presents a novel and well-motivated approach to image super-resolution. The authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of their method, including the theoretical background and experimental results. The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise.
