**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to image super-resolution (SR) using amortised Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference. The authors introduce a convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture that projects the model output to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions, ensuring that the high-resolution output is always consistent with the low-resolution input. They then present three methods to solve the optimisation problem: Generative Adversarial Networks (GAN), denoiser-guided SR, and a baseline method using a maximum-likelihood-trained image prior. The experiments show that the GAN-based approach performs best on real image data.

**Decision**

Based on the review, I decide to **accept** this paper.

**Reasons for the Decision**

1. The paper proposes a novel and well-motivated approach to image SR using amortised MAP inference, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of their approach, including a derivation of the affine projection layer and the connection between GANs and amortised variational inference.
3. The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, particularly the GAN-based method, on real image data.

**Supporting Arguments**

3. The paper could include more visualisations of the results, particularly for the grass texture and CelebA datasets.
