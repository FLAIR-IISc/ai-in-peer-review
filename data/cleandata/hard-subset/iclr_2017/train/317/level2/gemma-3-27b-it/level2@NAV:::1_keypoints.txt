- Proposes amortised MAP inference for image super-resolution to avoid MSE-induced blurriness.  
- Introduces a CNN with an affine projection to enforce LR–HR consistency, simplifying to cross-entropy minimization.  
- Explores three solution methods (GANs, denoiser-guided, density-guided), finding GAN-based (AffGAN) most effective.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection despite sound motivation and clear theoretical framing.  
- Main criticism: experimental validation is insufficient to show clear improvement over existing GAN-based SR methods.  
- Reported performance gains over standard GANs (SoftGAN) are marginal and qualitative results are not convincingly superior.  
- Affine projection adds complexity, and its practical benefits appear modest based on presented ablations.  
- Comparisons to Ledig et al. (2016) and other state-of-the-art methods are indirect or missing.  
- Important benchmarks and metrics (Set5/Set14/BSD100, PSNR, SSIM, perceptual metrics) are not thoroughly evaluated.  
- Denoiser-guided and density-guided approaches performed poorly and reasons for this are not fully explored.  
- Reviewer asks for more extensive ablations on affine projection across architectures/datasets and its computational cost.  
- Requests clearer articulation of AffGAN’s specific advantages, including claimed stability in Section 3.2.1.  
- Suggests a more thorough discussion of limitations of MAP inference in this context.  
- Recommends releasing code to enable independent verification and further research.  
- Questions for authors: what is the computational overhead of the affine projection, why were non-GAN methods not competitive, and can you provide more perceptual comparison examples?
