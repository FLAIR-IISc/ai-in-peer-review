- Decision: Reject — ideas are interesting but empirical gains don’t justify added complexity.
- Proposed approach: amortized MAP inference for SR using CNNs with an affine projection to enforce LR–HR consistency.
- Methods evaluated: GAN-based SR, denoiser-guided SR, and a maximum-likelihood trained image-prior baseline.
- Presentation: paper is well-written and motivation for MAP vs MSE is clearly explained.
- Limited empirical gains: real-image results show only marginal improvement and PSNR/SSIM don’t demonstrate clear advantage.
- Visual results: some sharper outputs but also artifacts and inconsistencies across datasets.
- GAN instability and complexity: GANs perform best but are unstable, hyperparameter-sensitive, and require instance-noise fixes.
- Computational cost vs benefit: GAN approach adds significant tuning and compute without clear payoff.
- Lack of robustness/generalization: denoiser- and density-guided methods struggle to converge and are not thoroughly evaluated across datasets.
- Ablation studies needed: isolate effect of affine projection (performance with/without projection using same loss).
- Comparison to SOTA needed: evaluate against recent SR methods (e.g., EDSR, RCAN) for meaningful benchmarks.
- Perceptual evaluation lacking: recommend user studies or rigorous subjective assessments to support perceptual quality claims.
- Discuss limitations in depth: provide practical details on computational scaling, runtime, and memory requirements.
- Clarify affine projection: improve intuitive explanation of how projection enforces consistency and its benefits.
- Questions for authors: provide computational/memory analysis, hyperparameter sensitivity and tuning strategies, other techniques tried for convergence, and elaboration on AffGAN’s connection to amortized variational inference and its practical implications.
