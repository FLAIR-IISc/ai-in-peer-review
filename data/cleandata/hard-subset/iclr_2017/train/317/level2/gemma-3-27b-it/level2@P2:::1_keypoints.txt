- Proposes an amortized MAP approach to image super-resolution using a CNN that projects outputs to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions to enforce LR–HR consistency.
- Explores three training methods: GAN-based, denoiser-guided, and a maximum-likelihood-trained image prior baseline.
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and addresses the blurring problem from pixel-wise losses.
- Decision: Reject due to insufficient empirical depth to support claimed benefits.
- Empirical comparisons are mainly against basic baselines, not state-of-the-art SR methods.
- Reported gains (especially for GAN) are visible but not substantial enough for a top conference.
- Ablation studies are limited and do not fully isolate the contributions of components.
- The connection to amortized variational inference is intriguing but feels tacked on and underexplored.
- Strength: theoretical grounding and novelty of the affine projection constraint.
- Strength: convincing MAP argument and clever architectural enforcement of LR–HR consistency.
- Strength: 2D Swiss-roll example effectively illustrates MSE limitations.
- Strength: useful theoretical justification for using GANs in SR.
- Weakness: experiments focus on simple datasets (grass textures) and lack real-world generalization analysis.
- Weakness: ImageNet results are presented but lack detailed analysis and metrics.
- Weakness: limited comparison to recent SOTA SR methods (e.g., Ledig et al. 2016; EDSR/RCAN/RDN).
- Weakness: denoiser- and density-guided methods were abandoned quickly without deep investigation into causes.
- Weakness: insufficient study of architectural/hyperparameter variations and the true impact of the affine projection layer.
- Suggestions: add stronger baselines (EDSR, RCAN, RDN) and more comprehensive ablations on affine projection, losses, and architectures.
- Suggestions: provide detailed ImageNet qualitative/quantitative analysis across downsampling levels and investigate failure cases.
- Suggestions: expand on the variational inference connection and its practical implications or use for regularization.
- Suggestions: include more compelling qualitative comparisons (zoomed-in regions) to highlight texture/detail differences.
- Questions: why were denoiser-/density-guided methods abandoned and were hyperparameter searches performed?
- Questions: what is the computational cost of the affine projection layer versus a standard CNN?
- Questions: how sensitive is performance to the choice of downsampling kernel A?
- Questions: can the affine projection layer be incorporated into other SR architectures (e.g., residual networks)?
- Questions: clarify limitations and potential drawbacks of focusing on the MAP/mode of the posterior (Section 5.6).
