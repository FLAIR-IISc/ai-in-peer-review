- Decision: Reject — novel MAP framing and affine projection are interesting but empirical evidence is insufficient to justify acceptance.
- Limited empirical gains: Strong result on 2D Swiss-roll but only marginal or inconsistent improvements on complex image datasets; PSNR/SSIM often favor MSE baseline.
- Perceptual claims unsupported: Claimed superior perceptual quality (AffGAN) lacks a proper perceptual study or consistent quantitative backing.
- Instability and tuning: Methods (AffLL, AffDG) require careful stabilization (instance noise, annealing) and appear fragile and sensitive to hyperparameters.
- Lack of comparison to SOTA: No thorough benchmarking against modern SR methods (e.g., EDSR, RCAN, RDN); unclear if affine projection adds value beyond optimized architectures/losses.
- Need ablation studies: More detailed experiments needed to isolate the contribution of the affine projection across losses and architectures.
- Failure-case analysis missing: Deeper analysis required for observed failures (e.g., generating “water” instead of “snake”) to understand limitations.
- Clarify variational inference link: The connection to variational inference (Appendix F) is underdeveloped and needs clearer implications and practical implementation details.
- Computational cost unclear: Paper should discuss overhead of affine projection and proposed training procedures relative to standard SR methods.
- Quantitative rigor lacking: Recommend statistical tests and more rigorous quantitative justification of claimed advantages.
- Questions for authors — affine projection limits: What are cases where affine projection might hinder performance?
- Questions for authors — A+ kernel and noise sensitivity: How does the choice of deconvolution kernel (A+) and instance-noise hyperparameters affect results?
- Questions for authors — SOTA and VI intuition: How does AffGAN compare to state-of-the-art SR on standard benchmarks, and can you give a clearer, practical explanation of the AffGAN ↔ amortized variational inference connection?
