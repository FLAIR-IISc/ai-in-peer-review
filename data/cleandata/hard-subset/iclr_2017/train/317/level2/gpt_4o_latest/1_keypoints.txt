- Paper proposes amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution using a CNN with an affine projection layer to ensure HR–LR consistency.  
- Three MAP optimization methods are evaluated: GANs (AffGAN), denoiser-guided SR, and density-guided SR with ML-trained priors.  
- AffGAN achieves the best perceptual quality among the methods and exhibits sharper, more plausible outputs.  
- The paper establishes a theoretical connection between GANs and variational inference.  
- Novelty: the affine projection layer is a significant architectural contribution not present in prior SR methods.  
- Experimental validation is comprehensive, covering synthetic data, textures, faces (CelebA), and natural images (ImageNet).  
- Affine projection yields faster convergence and better performance in experiments.  
- Use of instance noise improves GAN training stability and is a practical contribution.  
- Limitation: MAP inference issues (dependence on representation, atypicality of mode) are acknowledged but not fully explored.  
- Denoiser- and density-guided approaches produced blurrier results and lack detailed analysis explaining their underperformance.  
- Visual artifacts: AffGAN sometimes produces high-frequency noise that needs further mitigation.  
- Reproducibility: providing open-source code or pre-trained models would strengthen adoption and reproducibility.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity of AffGAN to noise distribution and annealing schedule during training.  
- Questions for authors: applicability and required modifications of the affine projection layer to other inverse problems.  
- Questions for authors: consider user studies or advanced perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS) to better evaluate visual quality.
