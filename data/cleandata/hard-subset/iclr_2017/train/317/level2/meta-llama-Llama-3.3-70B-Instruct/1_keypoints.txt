- Proposes a novel amortized MAP inference approach for image super-resolution (SR).
- Introduces a CNN that projects outputs to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions, ensuring LR-HR consistency.
- Proposes three MAP approximation methods: GANs (AffGAN), denoiser-guided SR, and density-guided SR.
- Argues MAP-based SR avoids blurry, over-smoothed outputs common to empirical risk minimization.
- Claims MAP approach favors solutions with high posterior probability and more plausible image priors, improving perceptual quality.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing focus on a specific SR problem and strong motivation.
- Paper provides clear theoretical background and detailed explanation of proposed methods.
- Comprehensive experiments are presented, showing effectiveness especially of the GAN-based AffGAN.
- AffGAN outperforms MSE/MAE methods in cross-entropy and visual quality metrics.
- Authors analyze limitations, including dependence on representation and the assumption that MAP estimates appear plausible.
- Reviewer suggests adding implementation details for denoiser-guided and density-guided methods, which underperformed.
- Recommends more experiments on robustness to different noise types and degradations.
- Requests clarifications on why denoiser/density methods underperformed, how to address MAP limitations (representation dependence), and computational complexity/real-world applicability.
