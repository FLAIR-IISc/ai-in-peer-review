- Paper frames single-image super-resolution as amortised MAP inference to produce perceptually plausible HR images.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer enforcing that downsampled output equals the low-resolution input.  
- Affine projection turns the constrained MAP objective into an unconstrained cross-entropy minimisation problem.  
- Proposes and compares three solution methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided baseline).  
- Experiments show AffGAN yields the sharpest and most visually appealing results.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Reason 1: Affine projection is a principled, novel architectural contribution that models the physical SR constraint better than soft penalties.  
- Reason 2: Strong experimental support, including a toy Swiss-roll demonstration validating the MAP objective and convincing real-image qualitative results.  
- Core strength: elegant mathematical formulation that reduces to matching generated and real-image distributions, justifying GAN use.  
- Swiss-roll experiment provides quantitative validation that the methods optimize the intended objective.  
- Qualitative results (grass, faces) highlight AffGAN’s ability to recover plausible high-frequency details absent in MSE-trained methods.  
- Weakness: Denoser- and density-guided methods’ failures are discussed briefly and mainly relegated to the appendix; need fuller analysis of their limitations.  
- Weakness: AffGAN can introduce high-frequency artifacts; paper should discuss mitigation strategies for GAN artifacts.  
- Questions for authors: Any preliminary experiments or challenges related to the theoretical link between stochastic AffGAN and amortised variational inference?  
- Question for authors: How sensitive is AffGAN’s performance to the choice of downsampling kernel A, e.g., would bicubic downsampling work as well?
