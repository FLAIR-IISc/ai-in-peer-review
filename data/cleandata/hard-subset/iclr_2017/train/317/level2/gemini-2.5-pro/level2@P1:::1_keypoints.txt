- Proposes amortised MAP inference for single-image super-resolution to avoid MSE-induced blurriness and produce plausible HR outputs.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces exact downsampling consistency, turning SR into an unconstrained generative modeling problem.  
- Frames objective as minimizing cross-entropy between network outputs and true HR image distribution, reducing reliance on paired LR-HR data.  
- Explores three optimization methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided score estimation), and AffLL (density-guided with PixelCNN).  
- AffGAN yields significantly sharper, more perceptually convincing results than AffDG, AffLL, and MSE baselines on toy, texture, face, and ImageNet datasets.  
- Proposes "instance noise" to stabilize GAN training, shown useful especially on ImageNet.  
- Provides theoretical connection between a stochastic AffGAN variant and amortised variational inference.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: principled, novel formulation and powerful affine projection layer that outperforms soft-penalty approaches and reframes conditional SR as unconditional generation.  
- Empirical evidence is strong: qualitative results show improved perceptual quality and the affine projection aids training speed and final performance even with MSE loss.  
- Thorough experimental exploration and honest reporting of AffDG/AffLL failures; visual results on grass and CelebA are particularly compelling.  
- Appendix F derivation and discussion of MAP limitations add theoretical depth and nuance.  
- Suggestion: summarize failure analysis of AffDG/AffLL in main paper rather than only in Appendix E.  
- Suggestion: provide an ablation on instance noise to quantify its effect on stability and image quality.  
- Suggestion: mention MAP inference limitations earlier in the introduction to better frame the approach.  
- Questions for authors: computational cost and overhead of precomputing A+ and using the projection in training; why affine projection helps MSE-trained networks (regularization/consistency intuition); any experiments or initial results with stochastic AffGAN for diversity and reduced artifacts?
