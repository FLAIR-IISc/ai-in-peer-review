- Recasts single-image super-resolution as amortised MAP inference to avoid MSE-induced blurriness.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces the high-resolution output to downsample exactly to the low-resolution input.  
- With the affine constraint, the MAP objective reduces to minimizing cross-entropy between the network output distribution and true high-resolution images.  
- Proposes three training methods: AffGAN (GAN-based generator), AffDG (denoiser-guided using a pre-trained denoising autoencoder), and AffLL (density-guided using a PixelCNN-style density model).  
- Empirical results: AffGAN produces sharp, perceptually convincing images; AffDG and AffLL produce blurry outputs.  
- Presents a practical "instance noise" technique to stabilize GAN training, especially on ImageNet.  
- Provides a theoretical connection between a stochastic variant of AffGAN and amortised variational inference.  
- Recommendation: Accept — paper is novel, well-motivated, and theoretically grounded.  
- Key strength: Clear conceptual derivation showing how the affine projection turns a constrained MAP problem into an unconstrained generative modeling objective.  
- Strong empirical validation: Swiss-roll toy and natural image experiments (grass, CelebA, ImageNet) demonstrating AffGAN’s perceptual superiority over MSE.  
- Architectural contribution is independently useful: affine projection can improve MSE-trained models.  
- Authors candidly discuss limitations, including empirical failures of AffDG/AffLL and potential pitfalls of MAP (representation dependence, mode atypicality).  
- Weakness: AffDG and AffLL failures are not fully analyzed; unclear if better auxiliaries or optimization could rescue them.  
- Suggested improvements: deeper analysis of AffDG/AffLL gradient/variance issues and OOD behavior, an instance-noise ablation study, and direct comparisons with perceptual/VGG-based losses.  
- Clarification questions: sensitivity of results to precision of learned pseudo-inverse A+; whether denoiser annealing schedule or curriculum strategies were explored for AffDG; and whether stochastic f_theta(x,z) was trained to produce diverse reconstructions.
