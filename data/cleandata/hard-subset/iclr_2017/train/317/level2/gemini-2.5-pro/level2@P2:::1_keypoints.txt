- Reformulates single-image super-resolution as amortised MAP inference to produce perceptually plausible HR images instead of MSE-blurred outputs.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces the hard constraint that the downsampled output matches the LR input.  
- Shows amortised MAP inference reduces to minimizing the cross-entropy between generated-image distribution and true HR distribution.  
- Proposes three methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (likelihood-guided with PixelCNN).  
- AffGAN yields the most visually appealing and sharp results on natural images.  
- Presents “instance noise” as a novel, practical technique to stabilize GAN training.  
- Derives a theoretical connection between a stochastic AffGAN variant and amortised variational inference.  
- Decision: Accept — justified by a principled theoretical framework and a novel, effective architecture.  
- Uses a Swiss-roll toy example to clearly motivate why MSE fails and MAP is preferable for perceptual SR.  
- Affine projection is implementable with standard conv/deconv operations and improves training speed and results, even for MSE tasks.  
- Comparative study is valuable even though only AffGAN succeeded on complex datasets.  
- Paper is transparent about AffDG and AffLL failures but provides only a brief analysis of why they failed.  
- Lacks quantitative perceptual evaluation (e.g., user studies) to substantiate qualitative claims of perceptual superiority.  
- Comparison between AffGAN and SoftGAN is inconclusive; trade-offs (sharper vs. more high-frequency noise) need clearer articulation.  
- Discussion of MAP limitations (representation dependence, atypical mode issue) is strong but should be linked to AffGAN’s behavior and KL/diversity effects.  
- Asks for more intuition on AffDG’s failure modes and sensitivity to denoiser noise-annealing and data multimodality.  
- Requests clarification on how critical instance noise was for AffGAN training, signs of mode collapse, and sensitivity to noise schedules.  
- Seeks practical details on computing the pseudo-inverse A+: computational cost and dependence on downsampling kernel properties.
