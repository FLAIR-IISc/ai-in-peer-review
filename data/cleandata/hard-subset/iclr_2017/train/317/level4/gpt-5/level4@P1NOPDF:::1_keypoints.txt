1) Novel amortized MAP + GAN yields high-quality SR but unclear incremental theoretical/empirical advantages over prior GAN-based methods (e.g., SRGAN).  
2) Affine projection claimed to remove need for paired HR–LR data, but projection layer seems to require pairs—clarify which components need paired data and the practical benefits of the decomposition.  
3) Evaluation limited to ImageNet; include standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) using common protocols/metrics for fair comparison.  
4) Experiments limited to 128×128 outputs; discuss scalability to larger images, memory/runtime behavior, and any architectural/training changes needed for high-resolution inference.  
5) Clarify use of stochastic latent noise in the generator/inference: were noise vectors tried, and how do they affect diversity, fidelity, and training stability versus the deterministic amortized MAP objective?  
6) Overall: promising and theoretically grounded work, but necessity and comparative advantage versus existing GAN-based SR are not fully demonstrated; more experiments on benchmarks and higher resolutions are needed.
