Summary
The paper formulates single-image super-resolution (SR) as amortized MAP estimation and introduces a projection step to enforce exact consistency between high-resolution (HR) reconstructions and their corresponding low-resolution (LR) observations under the known degradation model. This projection is well-motivated and empirically shown to improve results across settings. The authors further propose and evaluate three different strategies to optimize the cross-entropy objective in Eq. 9. Experiments include both carefully chosen toy problems and realistic SR scenarios, with a consistent emphasis on visual quality; GAN-based training emerges as the most perceptually compelling approach.

Strengths
- Clear, well-organized, and well-written paper. The problem formulation is sound, and the projection step is intuitively appealing and practically effective.
- The experimental design is thoughtful, combining diagnostic toy examples with real-world applications. The results are compelling and consistently support the claims about HR-LR consistency and perceptual gains.
- The contributions specific to SR in Sections 3.2–3.4 appear novel. The insight that GAN-based training tends to yield the most visually appealing reconstructions is valuable and likely to influence subsequent SR research.
- The paper’s framing through amortized MAP offers a unifying perspective that can extend beyond SR, potentially benefiting other inverse problems.

Weaknesses and Questions
- Some components related to GAN training may overlap with existing variants. Please clarify the novelty and precise connections to prior GAN-based SR or perceptual-loss methods, ideally with a brief positioning paragraph and an explicit comparison table or citations.
- The three optimization strategies for Eq. 9 would benefit from a deeper comparative analysis. Concretely:
  - What are their computational costs and convergence behaviors?
  - How sensitive are they to hyperparameters?
  - Under what conditions does each method tend to perform best or fail?
  Including ablations and runtime/memory metrics would strengthen the case.
- While perceptual quality is emphasized, it would help to also report HR-LR consistency quantitatively (e.g., degradation-model reconstruction error) and to present standard distortion metrics (PSNR/SSIM) alongside perceptual metrics, to make trade-offs explicit.
- Please discuss robustness to mismatch in the assumed degradation model (blur kernel, noise level). How does the projection behave under model misspecification or heavy noise?
- Minor: The manuscript contains a few typos; a careful proofreading pass is recommended.

Clarity and Presentation
- Very good. The exposition is accessible, and figures/examples are informative. The notation around the projection and Eq. 9 is mostly clear; consider adding a brief intuition box or schematic for the projection operator and its interaction with training.

Reproducibility
- The paper would benefit from releasing code and pretrained models. Please include full training details for each of the three Eq. 9 strategies (architectures, losses, optimization settings, and seeds), plus any data preprocessing and degradation-model specifications.

Significance
- High. The amortized MAP viewpoint and the explicit consistency projection target a central SR challenge and convincingly improve practical outcomes, especially when combined with GAN-based training for perceptual quality. These ideas should be relevant to future SR work and potentially to other inverse problems.

Recommendation
- Accept. Strong, well-presented paper with meaningful contributions and solid empirical support. Minor revisions: clarify novelty versus prior GAN literature, expand the comparison among the three optimization methods, and proofread for typos.
