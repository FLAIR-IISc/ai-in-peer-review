The paper proposes framing Super-Resolution as amortized MAP estimation. It introduces a projection step to enforce consistent HR-LR dependencies, which is empirically validated and leads to improved results throughout. Additionally, three different approaches to solve the resulting cross-entropy objective in Eq.9 are proposed and evaluated.

Summary: This is a very strong paper, clearly written and well presented. The experimental results are sufficient, with well-chosen toy examples and real-world applications. To my understanding, the contributions to super-resolution are novel (3.2, 3.3, 3.4), while components specific to GAN training may have appeared in variant forms elsewhere (see discussion). I expect this work to be relevant for future research on super-resolution; the finding that GAN-based training produces the most visually appealing outputs suggests further exploration in this direction.

The manuscript would benefit from another proofreading pass; there are a few minor typos worth correcting.
