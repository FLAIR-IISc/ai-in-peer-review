This paper presents an amortized MAP estimation method for super-resolution (SR). The core idea is to train a neural network that projects inputs onto an affine subspace of SR solutions that are consistent with the low-resolution (LR) observation model. Within this subspace, the method can recover valid solutions using a variety of strategies, including GAN-based priors, noise-assisted optimization, and density-assisted optimization. Results are demonstrated clearly on several datasets.

Overall, I like the paper. The approach is conceptually appealing: enforcing LR consistency via a learned projection while allowing flexible downstream optimization is elegant and practically useful. The empirical results are convincing and help illustrate the benefits of the framework.

That said, the writing and presentation could be improved substantially. I found the paper hard to follow at times, and given the complexity of the subject, clearer exposition would significantly strengthen the work. In particular, tighter organization, more precise definitions, and a clearer description of the training procedure and how the affine subspace is parameterized would help readers understand the method without repeatedly backtracking through the text.

I would also like to see more analysis of the learned networks. What kinds of features do they capture, and how does this relate to the structure of the affine subspace and LR consistency? Some qualitative and quantitative insights into what the projection network learns would make the contribution more interpretable and easier to assess.
