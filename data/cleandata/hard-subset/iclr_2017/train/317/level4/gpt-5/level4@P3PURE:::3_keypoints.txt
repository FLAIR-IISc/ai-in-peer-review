- Proposes an amortized MAP estimation method for super-resolution that learns to project inputs onto an affine subspace of LR-consistent HR solutions.
- The learned affine subspace enables recovery of plausible high-resolution outputs via multiple strategies (GAN-based, noise-assisted, density-assisted).
- Empirical results are demonstrated across several datasets and are generally convincing.
- Strength: principled amortized framework that enforces LR-consistency through projection to an affine subspace.
- Strength: flexible design allowing different optimization criteria to be plugged in for diverse outputs.
- Weakness: exposition is often hard to follow—needs a more structured narrative, clearer notation, and tighter explanations.
- Weakness: lacks deeper analysis of the learned networks—missing qualitative visualizations or quantitative probes of intermediate representations and relation to SR priors.
- Overall assessment: positive about the contribution but improvements in clarity and additional analysis of learned representations are required.
