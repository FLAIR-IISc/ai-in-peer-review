- Proposes an amortized MAP approach for super-resolution that maps observations to an affine subspace of high-resolution solutions consistent with low-resolution measurements.  
- Within that affine subspace, supports multiple inference strategies: GAN-based, noise-assisted, and density-assisted optimization to produce plausible SR reconstructions.  
- Empirical demonstrations on multiple datasets yield convincing results; reviewer finds idea interesting and results solid.  
- Major issue: exposition needs substantial polishing—core intuition, affine-subspace construction, and relationships among inference modes are hard to follow.  
- Request clearer problem setup, more precise notation, and a step-by-step description of training and inference (preferably with schematic figure or pseudocode).  
- Related work section should be tightened to better situate the contribution relative to amortized inference and existing SR methods.  
- Wants deeper analysis of learned networks: what features/structures are captured and visualizations of learned filters or affine-subspace basis elements.  
- Suggests visualizing example trajectories within the subspace during optimization to illustrate behavior of different inference modes.  
- Ask for sensitivity analyses: effect of subspace dimensionality and choice among GAN-, noise-, and density-assisted objectives.  
- Requests ablation studies and error analyses, especially under mismatch between assumed and true degradation models, to clarify when each inference mode is preferable.  
- Minor fixes: correct typos and terminology (e.g., “amortized,” “proper,” consistent LR notation), standardize hyphenation, and improve figure captions and cross-references.  
- Summary: promising framework with compelling results, but needs improved clarity and deeper analysis to fully convey its contribution.
