- Proposes amortized MAP inference for SR with a pre-learned affine projection enforcing LR consistency and three strategies for minimizing cross-entropy.  
- Novelty: amortized MAP is new and yields high-quality GAN results, but authors should clarify concrete advantages over existing GAN-based SR (e.g., SRGAN).  
- Affine projection means the main model can train without paired HR-LR, but pretraining the affine layer uses pairs — does this simply shift the supervision requirement?  
- Ask for results on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) to enable fair comparison with prior work.  
- Concern about scalability: current results are limited to 128×128 — need evidence the method works at larger/higher resolutions and for arbitrary input sizes.  
- Question whether injecting a noise latent vector was tried to better model output distributions in the GAN.  
- Overall: strong, theoretically grounded paper with multiple solution strategies likely to inspire follow-up work, but requires clarifications and more empirical validation.
