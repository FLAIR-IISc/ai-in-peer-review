1) Novel amortized MAP + GAN approach produces plausible SR results but it's unclear what it adds over recent GAN-based methods (e.g., SRGAN).  
2) Affine projection lets the main model train without HR–LR pairs, but training the affine layer itself still requires paired data—possibly just shifting supervision.  
3) Lacks evaluation on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) for fair comparison with prior work.  
4) Experiments are limited to 128×128; unclear how the method scales to larger or arbitrary-sized images used in practice.  
5) Unclear whether a noise latent vector was incorporated into the GAN to model output variability; authors did not report experiments with noise.  
6) Strong theoretical contribution and novel ideas with several variants explored, but necessity of some components is questionable and additional experiments are needed.
