1. Summary of contributions
The paper tackles single-image super-resolution as an amortized MAP inference problem. The key idea is to enforce exact consistency between the high-resolution (HR) output and the low-resolution (LR) input via an affine projection layer that implements the Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse of the downsampling operator. This converts amortized MAP into minimizing the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the model’s implied HR output distribution and the true HR data distribution, enabling training without paired LR–HR examples.

On top of this, the paper presents three training strategies:
- AffGAN: a GAN-based method (with an instance-noise trick) that effectively minimizes KL[qθ‖pY] and provides theoretical grounding for adversarial SR in this setting.
- AffDG: denoiser-guided training that backpropagates estimated score-function gradients from a denoising autoencoder.
- AffLL: density-guided training using a differentiable PixelCNN/MCGSM prior to optimize cross-entropy directly.

Empirically, the affine projection is shown to preserve input–output consistency exactly and can even improve MSE/SSIM and training speed compared to unconstrained CNNs. On a toy Swiss-roll problem, AffGAN and AffDG achieve cross-entropy close to brute-force MAP. On images (textures, faces, ImageNet), AffGAN produces the most visually plausible, sharp results, while AffDG and AffLL tend to be blurry. The paper also discusses important caveats of MAP (representation dependence and atypicality of modes) and connects a stochastic AffGAN variant to amortized variational inference.

2. Decision
Accept. The paper offers a well-motivated reframing of SR as amortized MAP with a principled architectural constraint, and provides both theory (for GAN training and VI connection) and convincing empirical evidence that the approach yields perceptually superior SR, especially with the AffGAN variant.

3. Supporting arguments
- Problem framing and motivation: The move away from MSE/PSNR toward statistically plausible reconstructions is clearly argued and well grounded in the underdetermined nature of SR. The affine projection elegantly resolves the likelihood constraint exactly, avoiding the pitfalls of soft penalties and yielding an unsupervised marginal-matching objective.
- Novelty and placement: While GANs have been applied to SR, the contributions here are novel for SR: (i) the affine projection architecture that guarantees Afθ(x)=x, (ii) the amortized MAP derivation reducing to cross-entropy minimization, (iii) denoiser- and density-guided training in this context, and (iv) the “instance noise” stabilization with a clear divergence interpretation. The theoretical link between AffGAN and amortized VI is a valuable conceptual contribution.
- Empirical support: The Swiss-roll study directly verifies cross-entropy minimization toward MAP. The ablation on affine projection shows no loss in capacity and even better MSE/SSIM training dynamics compared to unconstrained models, while guaranteeing exact consistency (soft constraints do not). On challenging textures and natural images, AffGAN consistently yields sharp, plausible results; the observed artifacts (e.g., high-frequency noise, occasional hallucination such as “snake→water”) are acknowledged and consistent with the chosen priors/objective. Results on CelebA align with expectations: MSE excels in PSNR/SSIM, while AffGAN improves perceptual quality.

4. Additional feedback (to help improve the paper; not all points affect my decision)
- Comparisons: Add stronger baselines where possible (e.g., SRCNN/ESPCN or SRGAN settings) to contextualize gains, and clarify how your AffGAN differs from Ledig et al. (e.g., exact affine projection vs. soft constraints, objective, and instance noise).
- Evaluation: Since PSNR/SSIM do not reflect perceptual quality, consider including human studies or perceptual metrics (e.g., SSIM/MS-SSIM variants, or deep-feature distances) to quantify the perceptual improvements claimed for AffGAN.
- Degradation model: All experiments assume a known Gaussian downsampling kernel A and its pseudoinverse A+. Please discuss sensitivity to mismatch between training/test A, and how the method might adapt when A is unknown or variable (common in real-world SR).
- Instance noise: Provide more guidance on the annealing schedule and magnitude, and a brief comparison to other stabilization methods (e.g., label smoothing, WGAN) to highlight trade-offs.
- AffDG and AffLL: The paper transparently reports that these methods produced blur and exhibited training instability. Briefly analyze failure modes and whether stronger priors (e.g., improved PixelCNN variants) or alternative noise schedules might help, to better delimit when these approaches are viable.
- Proofreading: A final pass to fix the few remaining typos would improve polish.

5. Questions for the authors
- A+ estimation: Could you report the sensitivity of results to the numerical quality of A+ (e.g., kernel size, objective residuals in Appendix B)? In your ablations, how large is the performance drop when A+ is initialized incorrectly or learned freely?
- Stability and instance noise: How sensitive is AffGAN’s convergence to the instance-noise variance and annealing schedule? Did you try training D to (near-)convergence per iteration vs. limited updates, and how did this interact with noise?
- Prior mismatch and hallucinations: In failure cases like “snake→water,” can conditioning be strengthened (e.g., conditional discriminators using x) without breaking the affine constraint? Do you observe systematic biases toward high-prior-density textures?
- AffDG gradients: How do you select/anneal σ for the denoiser to balance coverage vs. accuracy? Did you try score-matching or Stein-based estimators as alternatives?
- Density model: How would a more expressive density (e.g., PixelCNN variants with continuous likelihoods) change AffLL performance? Is the main bottleneck model capacity, optimization, or mismatch between learned density support and qθ?
- Generalization to unknown A: Could the affine projection be generalized by learning A jointly (or estimating A per image) while maintaining consistency? What are the practical obstacles?

Overall, this is a strong, well-argued, and practically useful paper. The affine projection and amortized MAP perspective are valuable, and the AffGAN results are compelling. I recommend acceptance.
