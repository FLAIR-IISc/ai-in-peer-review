- Proposes amortised MAP inference for single-image SR to avoid blur from MSE/MAE by learning a function that outputs MAP estimates for any LR input.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces strict LR–HR consistency by mapping outputs to the affine subspace consistent with the LR observation using a learned Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse A+.  
- Reformulates training as matching the marginal output distribution qθ to the HR data distribution pY (minimizing H[qθ, pY]).  
- Proposes three training strategies: AffGAN (affine-projected GAN with instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-guided using DAE gradients as score estimates), and AffLL (density-guided with differentiable PixelCNN/MCGSM likelihood).  
- Empirically, AffGAN yields perceptually sharper, more plausible SR results than MSE/MAE on textures, faces, and ImageNet crops.  
- The affine projection maintains exact LR-consistency and does not harm—and sometimes improves—MSE/SSIM and convergence when used with MSE training.  
- Toy Swiss-roll experiments show the proposed methods approach brute-force MAP in terms of cross-entropy.  
- Paper sketches a connection between stochastic AffGAN and amortised variational inference, offering theoretical grounding.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main strengths: methodological novelty and clarity (affine projection and amortised MAP framing) with solid links to GANs and score matching.  
- Strong empirical support: perceptually superior SR from AffGAN, exact consistency, and ablations showing benefits over soft constraints and instance noise for stability.  
- Well-motivated and well-placed in literature; improves over SRGAN/perceptual losses by enforcing exact consistency and distribution-matching framing.  
- Limitations: perceptual evaluation is mostly qualitative; quantitative perceptual metrics or user studies are limited.  
- AffDG and AffLL underperform in high-dimensional image settings.  
- Some failure modes acknowledged (hallucinations/high-frequency noise) and risk of semantic hallucination when the prior dominates.  
- Suggest clearer exposition: add high-level overview figures contrasting empirical risk vs amortised MAP and schematic of affine projection with pseudo-code.  
- Evaluation suggestions: add human preference studies, deep-feature distances, ablations for instance noise and A+ training vs fixed, and comparisons to perceptual SR baselines like SRGAN under identical downsampling.  
- Report runtime and memory at training/inference and discuss throughput advantages of amortisation.  
- Robustness suggestions: analyze sensitivity to mismatched/unknown downsampling operator A and discuss blind SR or learning A jointly.  
- Test other scales (×2, ×3) and operators to demonstrate generality.  
- Quantify hallucination risk and whether uncertainty/confidence can be estimated to detect semantic drift.  
- Alternative formulations: consider stochastic AffGAN outputs for posterior samples and discuss diversity–quality trade-offs.  
- For AffDG/AffLL, explore why they struggle in high dimensions and whether curriculum strategies or modern score models could help.  
- Questions for authors include sensitivity to misspecified A, whether A or A+ can be learned end-to-end, instance-noise annealing schedules, presence of user studies, effect of fixing vs training A+, density-model NLL on HR data, and experiments sampling with z for diversity.
