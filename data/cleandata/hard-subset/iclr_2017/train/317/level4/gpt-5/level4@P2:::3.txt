Summary and contributions
- The paper tackles single-image super-resolution as an underdetermined inverse problem and proposes amortised MAP inference instead of empirical risk minimization with MSE/MAE. The central idea is to enforce exact consistency with the low-resolution input by projecting network outputs onto the affine subspace of valid HR reconstructions defined by the downsampling operator.
- The authors introduce an affine projection layer (using the Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse of the downsampler) that can be plugged into CNNs so that outputs always satisfy the observation model. With this architecture, training for MAP reduces to matching the marginal HR output distribution to the HR data distribution (minimizing cross-entropy).
- They explore three approaches: (1) AffGAN, a GAN-based method tailored to this setting (with “instance noise” to stabilize training); (2) AffDG, which uses gradients from a denoiser to guide optimization; and (3) AffLL, which relies on a tractable density model trained by maximum likelihood (PixelCNN-style with continuous likelihood).
- Experiments on a toy Swiss-roll, grass textures, CelebA, and ImageNet show that AffGAN yields the best perceptual results and that the affine projection improves consistency and often training speed. The paper also draws a connection between AffGAN and amortized variational inference.

Decision: Accept
Key reasons
- A principled and well-motivated formulation of SR as amortised MAP with an elegant architectural constraint that guarantees consistency.
- Strong empirical evidence that AffGAN improves perceptual quality, coupled with useful theoretical insights (instance noise, AffGAN–VI connection).

Supporting arguments
- Problem and motivation: The paper clearly identifies the limitations of MSE/MAE for multimodal image distributions and motivates MAP as a way to produce plausible, high-prior-probability reconstructions. Recasting amortised MAP as matching marginals is a clean, literature-aware step bridging SR and generative modeling.
- Placement in literature: The work is well grounded, relating to GANs, denoising-as-score estimation, and likelihood-based generative models. The affine projection provides a concrete alternative to common soft constraints used in SRGAN-like models.
- Claims vs. evidence: 
  - The toy example convincingly shows that AffGAN/AffDG approach the MAP cross-entropy, while MSE/MAE do not.
  - On real images, AffGAN produces sharper, more plausible textures and faces than MSE. The projection ensures exact LR-HR consistency, which is a notable practical advantage over soft penalties.
  - The paper candidly reports that AffDG and AffLL underperform perceptually and can be unstable, which strengthens the credibility of the overall empirical narrative.
- Novelty: The affine projection layer for exact observation consistency, instance noise for stabilization, and the derivation linking AffGAN to amortized VI are meaningful contributions.

Additional feedback to improve the paper (not critical to the accept decision)
- Clarity and structure: The exposition can be tightened. Summarize the core optimization (cross-entropy minimization via affine projection) early with a high-level figure and a brief algorithmic overview of AffGAN/AffDG/AffLL. Reduce notation where possible and move some derivations to the appendix with clear pointers.
- Empirical analysis:
  - Provide ablations isolating the effect of the affine projection vs. soft constraints on both perceptual quality and LR-HR consistency, keeping all else equal.
  - Report stability metrics and training curves with and without instance noise; detail the noise schedule and sensitivity.
  - Include more quantitative perceptual metrics or a small-scale user study, since PSNR/SSIM favor MSE and do not track perceptual realism well.
  - Compare more directly to concurrent SRGAN-style baselines using the same downsampling operator and training data.
- Methodological details:
  - Elaborate on computing A+ for typical downsamplers, kernel sizes, and boundary handling. Discuss computational overhead and whether A+ must be recomputed for different A, scales, or blur kernels.
  - Analyze robustness to model mismatch in A (unknown or approximate downsampling). How do errors in A+ affect results?
  - Provide run-time and memory comparisons vs. standard SR CNNs.
- Understanding learned representations: Add qualitative/quantitative analysis of features learned with and without projection and under different objectives (e.g., frequency spectra, texture statistics, intermediate activations).
- Hallucination vs. fidelity: Discuss mechanisms to control hallucinations (e.g., conditional priors, stronger content losses, or uncertainty estimates), given examples where plausible but incorrect content appears.

Questions for the authors
1. How sensitive is AffGAN to mismatch in the assumed downsampling operator A? Can the method handle unknown or spatially varying degradations, and how would you adapt A+ in such cases?
2. What is the computational overhead of the affine projection in practice (both forward and backward), and does it impact inference speed compared to standard SR CNNs?
3. Can you provide ablations quantifying the contribution of instance noise (e.g., FID-like scores, discriminator loss behavior) and the sensitivity to the annealing schedule?
4. For AffDG and AffLL, what are the main failure modes during training, and do curriculum strategies or improved density/denoiser models narrow the performance gap to AffGAN?
5. How do you suggest balancing perceptual realism against content fidelity to avoid plausible but incorrect reconstructions? Could a hybrid objective (e.g., weak content loss plus affine projection) mitigate artifacts without reverting to over-smoothing?
6. The paper argues for MAP solutions but also notes potential atypicality of modes in high dimensions. Do you see practical benefits in a stochastic extension (sampling from q(y|x)) for SR, and can your AffGAN–VI connection be used to produce calibrated posterior samples?

Overall, this is a timely, well-motivated paper with solid theoretical framing and compelling empirical results for AffGAN. Strengthening clarity and adding the suggested analyses would further increase its impact.
