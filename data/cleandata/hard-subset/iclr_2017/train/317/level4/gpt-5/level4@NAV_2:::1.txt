1. Summary of claims and contributions
The paper tackles single-image super-resolution as an ill-posed inverse problem and proposes amortized MAP inference as a principled alternative to empirical risk minimization with MSE. The core architectural contribution is an affine projection layer that projects network outputs onto the affine subspace of solutions consistent with the LR observation, guaranteeing exact data consistency (Aŷ = x) and turning training into matching the marginal output distribution qθ to the HR data distribution pY. This reframes training as cross-entropy minimization H[qθ, pY] without requiring paired {LR, HR} data. Three instantiations are developed: (i) AffGAN, a GAN variant (with “instance noise”) that effectively minimizes KL[qθ‖pY]/H[qθ, pY]; (ii) AffDG, which backpropagates score estimates from a denoiser to follow ∇y log pY(y); and (iii) AffLL, a likelihood-based baseline using a differentiable PixelCNN/MCGSM density model. Empirically, on a 2D Swiss-roll the methods approach the MAP cross-entropy; on images (grass textures, CelebA, ImageNet, 4× SR), AffGAN yields the most perceptually plausible results. The paper also shows that affine projection can improve stability/training speed even under MSE, and it establishes a connection between (stochastic) AffGAN and amortized variational inference.

2. Decision: Accept
Key reasons:
- A clear, principled formulation of SR as amortized MAP with an elegant data-consistency projection that removes the need for paired data and grounds adversarial SR in a well-motivated objective.
- Solid theoretical framing and ablations, plus compelling empirical results (especially AffGAN) that address the perceptual shortcomings of MSE-based SR.

3. Supporting arguments
- Problem specificity: The work directly addresses the multimodality and averaging pathologies of MSE in SR, aiming to recover plausible, high-posterior solutions via MAP.
- Motivation and placement in the literature: The limitations of pixel-wise losses and the appeal of priors/likelihoods are well argued. The approach is positioned relative to SRGAN-style methods by enforcing exact data consistency via affine projection (vs soft penalties) and by providing a training objective tied to cross-entropy/KL. The denoiser and likelihood-guided variants are grounded in established connections between denoising and score estimation, and in state-of-the-art density modeling.
- Support for claims/rigor: On the toy problem, AffGAN/AffDG achieve cross-entropy near brute-force MAP; affine projection gives exactly zero LR-consistency error and tends to reduce H[qθ, pY] vs soft constraints. On real images, AffGAN qualitatively improves perceptual realism compared to MSE and soft-constrained GANs; the affine projection reduces color drift and enforces fidelity. The analysis of instance noise provides a plausible rationale for stabilizing GAN training. The connection to amortized VI (Appendix F) is theoretically interesting. Some evaluation gaps remain (standard SR benchmarks, larger images), but do not undermine the core contributions.

4. Additional feedback (to help improve the paper; not necessarily part of the accept decision)
- Benchmarking: Include results on Set5/Set14/BSD100 with standard 4× protocols to facilitate direct comparison with prior SR work (both PSNR/SSIM and perceptual assessments). Even if the focus is perceptual quality, these datasets are widely used and would clarify trade-offs.
- Comparison to SRGAN: Provide a controlled comparison to “Photo-Realistic Single Image Super-Resolution Using a GAN” under matched backbones/training, highlighting what AffGAN’s exact data consistency and objective add (e.g., fewer artifacts, better LR consistency, training stability via instance noise).
- Resolution/generalization: Demonstrate performance on higher resolutions and arbitrary input sizes, and discuss runtime/memory scaling. Clarify any boundary handling in the affine projection for large images.
- Data requirements: The paper states paired {LR, HR} data are not needed. Please clarify the practical requirements for learning/setting A and A+. Appendix B suggests A+ can be obtained numerically without pairs—spelling this out would preempt confusion.
- Stochastic outputs: Since Appendix F links stochastic AffGAN to amortized VI, it would be valuable to show empirical results with an explicit latent z (e.g., sample diversity conditioned on x, uncertainty visualization) and discuss if this reduces “overconfident” high-frequency noise.
- Reporting perceptual metrics: Consider adding user studies or learned perceptual metrics to complement PSNR/SSIM and better reflect the paper’s goals.
- Instance noise ablation: Provide an ablation of noise schedule/variance and its effect on stability and quality; this would make the proposed trick more reproducible.

Questions for the authors
- What, concretely, does AffGAN’s formulation and affine projection add over SRGAN beyond exact LR consistency and the theoretical objective? Can you provide side-by-side quantitative and perceptual comparisons under identical architectures/training budgets?
- Do you require any paired {LR, HR} data at any stage? How is A chosen in practice, and how is A+ trained/estimated? Can A+ be learned entirely from synthetic probes without pairs as suggested in Appendix B?
- Can the method handle arbitrary image sizes at inference with the same performance characteristics? Are there boundary or tiling artifacts when scaling to, say, 512×512 or larger?
- Have you experimented with adding a latent noise vector z during training (beyond the deterministic AffGAN)? If so, how does it affect diversity, artifact levels, and alignment with LR constraints?
- Will you report results on Set5/Set14/BSD100 to enable fair comparisons with prior SR methods?
- How sensitive is AffGAN to mismatch between the assumed downsampling operator A and the true degradation in real images? Could you comment on robustness or potential extensions to unknown kernels?
