The paper presents an amortised MAP estimation framework for super-resolution (SR). It learns a neural network that projects onto an affine subspace of SR solutions that are consistent with the LR observations, enabling the recovery of proper solutions using a variety of approaches: GAN-based, noise-assisted, and density-assisted optimisation. The results are clearly demonstrated on several datasets.

Overall, I like the paper. However, the writing and presentation could be significantly polished; at times it was hard to follow, and given the complexity of the topic, clearer exposition would help. I would also like to see more analysis of the learned networks—what kinds of features do they capture?
