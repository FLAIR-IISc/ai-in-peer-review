- Proposes amortized MAP inference for single-image SR and an affine projection layer enforcing exact LR–HR consistency (Afθ(x)=x), reframing training as matching the HR marginal distribution.
- Shows that with the affine projection the amortized MAP objective reduces to minimizing H[qθ, pY] (matching marginal HR distribution) rather than supervised LR–HR risk.
- Introduces three training strategies: AffGAN (GAN-based with instance-noise stabilization), AffDG (uses denoiser gradients to approximate ∂y log pY), and AffLL (uses tractable likelihood models like PixelCNN/MCGSM).
- Empirical results on toy Swiss-roll, textures, CelebA, and ImageNet indicate AffGAN gives the most visually plausible outputs and affine projection guarantees perfect LR–HR consistency.
- Provides a theoretical connection between a stochastic variant of AffGAN and amortized variational inference.
- Decision: Weak Accept.
- Primary positives: principled and novel SR formulation with affine projection and solid theoretical grounding; strong qualitative results and useful analysis of GAN stability via instance noise.
- Main concerns: lack of comparisons on standard SR benchmarks and unclear differentiation from concurrent GAN-based SR (e.g., SRGAN).
- Paper well-motivated: replacing pixelwise risk with amortized MAP is argued to better capture perceptual fidelity and natural image statistics.
- Literature relation: GAN framing and denoising-gradient connections are sound; instance noise addresses known GAN instability issues.
- Empirical limitations: missing results on standard SR datasets (Set5/Set14/BSD100), image sizes capped at 128×128, and weaker/underexplored AffDG and AffLL results.
- Suggestion: explicitly position contributions versus SRGAN and clarify what amortized MAP and affine projection enable beyond prior work.
- Suggestion: add standard benchmark results (PSNR/SSIM, LPIPS, user studies) to enable fair comparisons and illustrate perceptual vs distortion trade-offs.
- Suggestion: demonstrate scalability to higher resolutions, analyze runtime/memory, and discuss tiling/fully-convolutional and boundary handling.
- Suggestion: clarify how the affine projection A+ is obtained/learned and whether paired LR–HR data are required or assumed A is known; discuss robustness to A mismatch.
- Suggestion: include ablations (with/without affine projection, instance-noise schedules, downsampling kernel sensitivity, impact of latent noise z).
- Suggestion: analyze why AffDG/AffLL underperform and whether alternatives (curriculum, annealing, score matching/diffusion models) could help.
- Questions to authors: concrete advantages over SRGAN—does affine projection or the KL/cross-entropy view yield measurable differences?
- Questions: does training truly avoid paired LR–HR data given A+/A estimation needs, or does supervision shift to learning A/A+?
- Questions: can you report Set5/Set14/BSD100 results for direct comparison?
- Questions: how does the method scale to larger images and arbitrary sizes—any architectural/training changes needed?
- Questions: did you try adding latent noise z to the generator to capture posterior uncertainty and improve diversity/stability?
- Overall assessment: original, thoughtful work with solid theory and promising perceptual results; addressing benchmarking and clarification points would substantially strengthen the contribution.
