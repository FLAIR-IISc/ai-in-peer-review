1. Summary of claims and contributions (positive)
The paper reframes single-image super-resolution (SR) as amortised MAP inference and introduces an architecture that enforces exact LR–HR consistency via an affine projection layer derived from the downsampling operator (A) and its Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse (A+). This reduces learning to matching the model’s output distribution qθ to the HR data distribution pY by minimising cross-entropy, eliminating the need for paired LR–HR training data. The authors propose and study three training strategies: (i) an affine-projected GAN (AffGAN) with a specific generator update that targets KL[qθ‖pY] and the “instance noise” trick to stabilise training; (ii) denoiser-guided SR (AffDG) that backpropagates score estimates from a DAE to ascend log pY; and (iii) a likelihood-guided baseline (AffLL) using a differentiable density model (PixelCNN+MCGSM). Experiments on a 2D Swiss-roll toy problem, textures, CelebA faces, and ImageNet suggest the approach is effective; the affine projection yields exact consistency (zero LR error), and AffGAN produces the most visually plausible reconstructions. The paper also draws a link between (stochastic) AffGAN and amortised variational inference.

2. Decision
Accept, because:
- The architectural and problem formulation contributions (affine projection for exact consistency; amortised MAP via cross-entropy) are novel and well-motivated for SR, and
- The empirical evidence and analysis convincingly support improved perceptual quality and provide practical guidance (AffGAN performs best).

3. Supporting arguments
- Problem tackled: SR is ill-posed; MSE-trained models produce posterior means that look blurry. The paper targets high-posterior, prior-consistent reconstructions via amortised MAP.
- Motivation and placement: The work builds on known limitations of MSE/PSNR and prior GAN-based perceptual SR, but adds a principled architectural constraint (exact LR–HR consistency via affine projection) and a derivation that recasts amortised MAP as cross-entropy minimisation over pY—more akin to generative modelling. Compared to prior GAN SR with soft LR constraints, the affine projection enforces the observation model exactly.
- Claims supported:
  - Theory: Derivation of the affine-projected objective H[qθ,pY]; GAN update tied to KL; “instance noise” rationale; connection to amortised VI (Appendix F).
  - Empirics:
    • Swiss-roll: AffGAN/AffDG approach MAP cross-entropy; MSE/MAE do not; affine models achieve zero LR error, soft constraints do not.
    • MSE proof-of-concept: Affine projection does not hinder performance; it speeds training, improves MSE/SSIM, and guarantees consistency.
    • Textures/Faces/ImageNet: AffGAN gives sharper, more plausible images; occasional high-frequency noise and rare semantic hallucinations are acknowledged. The qualitative findings align with the stated objectives.
- Novelty: For SR, the affine projection formulation and the three amortised MAP training routes (Sections 3.2–3.4) are novel. Some GAN training elements echo ideas appearing elsewhere, as the authors note, but the integration for SR with exact consistency is new and valuable.

4. Additional feedback (to help improve the paper; not decision-critical)
- Writing/typos: A final proofreading pass would help fix minor typographical issues.
- Evaluation: Consider reporting perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS, FID) and/or a small-scale user study to complement PSNR/SSIM and visual inspection.
- Stability details: Expand on “instance noise” (schedule, variance, stopping criterion), and compare empirically to other stabilisation methods (e.g., gradient penalties, spectral norm) to position the trick among alternatives.
- Degradation robustness: Discuss sensitivity to mismatch between assumed A and real camera pipelines (e.g., non-Gaussian blur, unknown kernels, compression, noise). A brief robustness study would strengthen practical relevance.
- AffDG/AffLL: The paper candidly shows these are blurrier/less stable. It would be valuable to try stronger modern priors (score-based/consistency models or diffusion models) as denoisers/densities, or to include an ablation analyzing why the current DAE/density choices underperform (e.g., gradient locality, prior sharpness).
- Ablations: Provide more results isolating (i) fixing vs learning A+, (ii) soft vs affine constraints under identical backbones, (iii) discriminator update ratio, and (iv) effect of entropy (H[qθ]) implicit in AffGAN.
- Unpaired training: Clarify practical setups for sampling pX and pY independently in real applications; discuss domain mismatch risks and mitigation (e.g., cycle constraints, domain adaptation).
- Hallucinations: Consider simple content-preserving regularisers or semantic constraints to reduce rare but plausible hallucinations when fidelity is critical, or provide uncertainty indicators.

Questions for the authors
- How sensitive is performance to inaccuracies in A (kernel width/shape, stride) and to real-world degradations (noise, compression)? Can the method adapt when A is unknown or spatially varying?
- A+: You numerically approximate the pseudoinverse kernel. How large are the learned kernels in practice, and how does fixing vs training A+ affect results across datasets? Could you share hyperparameters and convergence diagnostics for Appendix B’s optimisation?
- Instance noise: What precise annealing schedule/variance worked best? Did you verify discriminator convergence per step, and how does the method compare empirically to gradient penalties or spectral normalisation?
- AffDG/AffLL: Do you attribute their underperformance primarily to prior quality or to optimisation (e.g., vanishing/peaked gradients near the data manifold)? Would a modern score network (e.g., NCSN/diffusion) change the outcome?
- Objective details: In practice, does your AffGAN update behave closer to KL or JS minimisation, and how crucial is “training D to (near) optimality” versus using a fixed number of D steps?
- Unpaired data: In your experiments, are pX samples obtained by applying A to HR images from the same corpus as pY, or do you use independently collected LR data? How robust is training if pX and pY come from slightly different domains?
- Perceptual validation: Did you run any human preference tests or compute LPIPS/FID to corroborate visual quality claims?
- Diversity/entropy: Since AffGAN implicitly encourages higher entropy, can you quantify output diversity versus SoftGAN or MSE baselines while maintaining LR consistency?
- Reproducibility: Could you provide pseudocode or additional implementation details for integrating the affine projection into common SR backbones and for the A+ estimation routine?
