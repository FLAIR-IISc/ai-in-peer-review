The paper introduces a new framework for SR based on amortized MAP inference and employs a pre-learned affine projection layer to enforce consistency with the LR image. It also proposes three distinct strategies for minimizing cross-entropy. Overall, this is a strong paper. That said, I have several comments:

1) The proposed amortized MAP inference is novel relative to prior SR methods. When combined with a GAN, the framework yields plausible, high-quality results. However, compared to other GAN-based SR approaches such as “Photo-Realistic Single Image Super-Resolution Using a Generative Adversarial Network,” it would be helpful to clarify what additional advantages or substantive differences this new formulation offers over the current state of the art.

2) With the affine projection architecture acting as a constraint, the main model does not require paired {HR, LR} images for training. Yet, training the affine projection layer itself still relies on {HR, LR} pairs. Does this effectively shift the supervision requirement to the pretraining of the affine projection?

3) The paper reports extensive results, including on natural images from ImageNet. Could the authors also provide results on Set5, Set14, and BSD100, the standard SR benchmarks, to enable fair comparison with prior work?

4) The natural-image results shown are limited to 128×128 outputs. Can this framework scale effectively to larger resolutions? SR systems often face inputs of arbitrary size, so evidence of performance at higher resolutions would be valuable.

5) Classical GANs include a noise latent vector to better model a distribution. Did the authors experiment with injecting a noise vector?

Overall, the paper presents a novel SR framework with solid theoretical underpinnings and explores multiple solution strategies. While questions remain regarding necessity and additional empirical validation, this work is likely to inspire further research in the community.
