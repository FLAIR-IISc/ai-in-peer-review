- Reframes single-image SR as amortized MAP inference, arguing MSE yields blurry, low-probability outputs while MAP produces plausible reconstructions with a natural image prior.
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces exact LR–HR consistency by projecting outputs onto the affine subspace defined by the downsampling operator.
- Converts the amortized MAP objective into matching the model output distribution to the HR data distribution, enabling training without paired LR–HR data.
- Proposes three training strategies: AffGAN (affine-projected GAN), AffDG (denoiser-guided SR), and AffLL (affine-projected maximum-likelihood baseline).
- Argues AffGAN effectively minimizes KL(q||p) and stabilizes training using “instance noise.”
- Uses denoising autoencoders to backpropagate score estimates in the AffDG approach.
- Uses a maximum-likelihood density model for the AffLL affine-projected baseline.
- Provides theoretical motivation for affine projection, justification for GANs in this setting, and links stochastic AffGAN to amortized variational inference.
- Empirically, AffGAN best matches objectives and yields the most perceptually convincing results across toy and real datasets.
- Denoiser- and density-guided approaches tend to produce blurrier results compared to AffGAN.
- Affine projection enforces zero LR–HR inconsistency by construction and can improve standard MSE training.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Well-motivated reformulation of SR as amortized MAP with a principled architectural constraint enforcing LR–HR consistency.
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical evidence and analysis showing AffGAN’s superior perceptual quality, with theoretical grounding and practical stabilization via instance noise.
- Paper addresses the averaging/mode-collapse problem of MSE training and seeks plausible, high-probability HR reconstructions consistent with LR inputs.
- Positioned within MAP inference, GAN-based perceptual SR, denoising-as-score-estimation, and autoregressive density modeling literature; improves on soft LR-consistency penalties.
- Theoretical support: affine projection to distribution matching, AffGAN approximates KL minimization, and instance noise motivates discriminator uniqueness and stable gradients.
- Toy data: AffGAN/AffDG approach brute-force MAP cross-entropy; MSE/MAE do not; affine projection yields exact LR consistency.
- Real images: AffGAN produces sharper, more plausible textures and faces; MSE yields higher PSNR/SSIM but is oversmoothed; soft-constraint GANs show color drift and weaker consistency.
- Ablations: Affine projection improves training dynamics and consistency; initialization of A+ is important.
- Rigor: Experiments include controlled toy problems, diverse real datasets, qualitative and quantitative comparisons, ablations, and training details; underperforming methods are transparently discussed.
- Suggest proofreading to fix typos/formatting.
- Recommend adding human studies or learned perceptual metrics (deep feature distances) since PSNR/SSIM correlate poorly with perception.
- Ask for more guidance on instance noise schedules, discriminator update ratios, and failure modes for reproducibility.
- Suggest exploring robustness to mismatch between assumed downsampling A and real-world degradations, or learning A.
- Request expanded ablations on fixed vs trainable A+ and quantification of numerical A+ approximation effects across kernels/strides.
- Ask authors to discuss diversity vs. fidelity trade-offs given AffGAN’s KL(q||p) behavior and role of stochastic generators.
- Recommend brief empirical checks on MAP’s representation dependence (alternate color or perceptual feature spaces).
- Question 1: Sensitivity to errors in A+ and whether A/A+ can be learned jointly.
- Question 2: Assumptions about LR distribution pX during training vs test time and robustness to distribution mismatch.
- Question 3: Details and criticality of instance noise schedules and whether other noise families or adaptive schemes were tried.
- Question 4: Best practices for stabilizing AffDG/AffLL and whether stronger density models or score estimators narrow the gap to AffGAN.
- Question 5: Whether user studies or deep-feature metrics were used to quantify perceptual gains beyond PSNR/SSIM.
- Question 6: Practical benefits of stochastic AffGAN for uncertainty quantification or sampling diverse plausible HR outputs.
- Question 7: Characterization of failure cases (e.g., semantic shifts like snake→water) and whether regularizers or content losses can mitigate them without losing sharpness.
