- Reframes single-image SR as amortised MAP inference and introduces an affine-projection layer (from A and its pseudoinverse A+) to enforce exact LR–HR consistency.
- Reduces learning to matching model output distribution qθ to HR data pY by minimising cross-entropy, enabling unpaired training of SR models.
- Proposes three training strategies: AffGAN (affine-projected GAN with a KL-targeted generator update and instance-noise stabilisation), AffDG (denoiser-guided using DAE score estimates), and AffLL (likelihood-guided with a differentiable density model).
- Experiments on Swiss-roll, textures, CelebA, and ImageNet show affine projection yields zero LR error and AffGAN produces the most visually plausible reconstructions.
- Draws a theoretical connection between stochastic AffGAN and amortised variational inference.
- Decision: Accept — novelty in architecture and formulation plus convincing empirical evidence, with AffGAN performing best.
- Motivation: Addresses SR ill-posedness and MSE-induced blurriness by targeting high-posterior, prior-consistent reconstructions.
- Placement: Improves on prior GAN-SR work by enforcing exact observation-model consistency rather than soft LR constraints.
- Theory support: Provides derivations for the affine-projected objective, ties GAN updates to KL, justifies instance noise, and links to amortised VI (App. F).
- Empirical support: Swiss-roll shows MAP behavior; affine models achieve zero LR error; affine projection does not hinder and can speed MSE training while improving MSE/SSIM.
- Image results: AffGAN yields sharper, more plausible images but can produce high-frequency noise and rare semantic hallucinations.
- Novelty claim: Affine projection formulation and the three amortised-MAP training routes are novel for SR despite overlapping GAN training ideas elsewhere.
- Writing: Paper needs a final proofreading pass to fix minor typographical issues.
- Evaluation suggestion: Report perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS, FID) and/or conduct a small human preference study to supplement PSNR/SSIM and visual inspection.
- Stability suggestion: Provide details and ablations on instance-noise (schedule, variance, stopping) and compare to other stabilisation methods like gradient penalties or spectral normalization.
- Robustness suggestion: Discuss sensitivity to mismatches between assumed A and real imaging pipelines (non-Gaussian blur, unknown kernels, compression, noise) and include a brief robustness study.
- AffDG/AffLL critique: These methods are blurrier/less stable; recommend trying stronger modern priors (score-based/diffusion) or ablations to diagnose underperformance (e.g., gradient locality, prior sharpness).
- Ablation requests: Isolate effects of fixing vs learning A+, soft vs affine constraints with identical backbones, discriminator update ratios, and the implicit entropy effect in AffGAN.
- Unpaired training clarification: Explain practical sampling setups for pX and pY, discuss domain-mismatch risks, and propose mitigations (cycle constraints, domain adaptation).
- Hallucination mitigation: Suggest simple content-preserving regularisers, semantic constraints, or uncertainty indicators to reduce rare but critical hallucinations.
- Question: How sensitive is performance to inaccuracies in A (kernel width/shape, stride) and to real-world degradations; can the method adapt to unknown or spatially-varying A?
- Question: How large are the numerically approximated A+ kernels in practice, and how does fixing vs training A+ affect results; share hyperparameters and convergence diagnostics for A+ optimisation.
- Question: What precise instance-noise annealing schedule/variance was used; was discriminator convergence verified per step; how does instance noise compare empirically to gradient penalties or spectral normalization?
- Question: Do AffDG/AffLL underperform due to prior quality or optimisation issues (vanishing/peaked gradients); would modern score/diffusion models change outcomes?
- Question: In practice, does the AffGAN update behave closer to KL or JS minimisation, and how crucial is training the discriminator to (near) optimality versus fixed-step updates?
- Question: In experiments, are pX samples obtained by applying A to HR images from the same corpus as pY or from independent LR data; how robust is training to domain mismatch between pX and pY?
- Question: Were human preference tests or LPIPS/FID computed to corroborate visual quality claims?
- Question: Can the authors quantify output diversity/entropy for AffGAN versus SoftGAN or MSE baselines while preserving LR consistency?
- Question: Request pseudocode and additional implementation details for integrating the affine projection into common SR backbones and for the A+ estimation routine to aid reproducibility.
