The paper introduces a new framework for SR based on amortized MAP inference and employs a pre-learned affine projection layer to enforce consistency with the LR input. It also proposes three different methods for minimizing cross-entropy. Overall, this is a strong paper, but I have several comments:

1) The proposed amortized MAP inference is novel and distinct from prior SR approaches. When combined with a GAN, the framework yields plausible, high-quality results. However, compared with another GAN-based SR method (Photo-Realistic Single Image Super-Resolution Using a Generative Adversarial Network), it is unclear what additional benefits this new formulation provides over the current state of the art.

2) With the affine projection architecture used as a constraint, the model does not require corresponding {HR, LR} image pairs to train the SR component. Yet training the affine projection layer itself still requires {HR, LR} pairs. Does this effectively just shift the need for paired supervision to the training of the affine projection?

3) The paper reports extensive results, including on natural images from ImageNet. Could the authors also provide results on Set5, Set14, and BSD100, which are standard SR test sets, to enable fair comparison with prior work?

4) The natural image results shown are limited to 128x128. Can the framework handle larger images effectively, given that SR systems must operate on inputs of arbitrary size?

5) Standard GANs include a stochastic latent (noise) input to better capture a distribution. Did the authors experiment with a noise vector?

Overall, the paper presents a new SR framework with solid theoretical analysis. The idea is novel and the authors explore multiple methods. Nonetheless, questions remain about necessity, and additional experiments are needed. I believe this work will provide valuable inspiration to the community.
