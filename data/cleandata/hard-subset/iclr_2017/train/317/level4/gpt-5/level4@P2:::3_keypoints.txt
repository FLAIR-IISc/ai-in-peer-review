- Central idea: Recast single-image SR as amortised MAP inference and enforce exact LR-HR consistency by projecting network outputs onto the affine subspace defined by the downsampling operator.
- Architectural contribution: Introduce an affine projection layer using the Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse of the downsampler that guarantees outputs satisfy the observation model.
- Training reduction: With affine projection, MAP training reduces to matching the marginal HR output distribution to the HR data distribution (minimizing cross-entropy).
- Methods explored: Present three approaches — AffGAN (GAN-based with instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-guided gradients), and AffLL (tractable density model / maximum likelihood).
- Empirical summary: AffGAN produces the best perceptual results on toy and real datasets; affine projection improves LR-HR consistency and often speeds training.
- Theoretical insight: Draw a connection between AffGAN and amortised variational inference (AffGAN–VI link) and justify instance noise for stabilization.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reasons for acceptance: Principled, well-motivated formulation of SR as amortised MAP and an elegant architectural constraint guaranteeing consistency.
- Empirical support: Strong evidence that AffGAN improves perceptual quality and useful theoretical insights on stabilization and VI connection.
- Motivation validity: Correctly identifies limitations of MSE/MAE for multimodal distributions and motivates MAP for plausible, high-prior reconstructions.
- Literature placement: Well grounded with connections to GANs, denoising-as-score estimation, and likelihood-based generative models; affine projection is a concrete alternative to soft constraints.
- Claims vs. evidence — toy example: AffGAN/AffDG approach MAP cross-entropy while MSE/MAE do not.
- Claims vs. evidence — real images: AffGAN yields sharper, more plausible textures and faces; projection enforces exact LR-HR consistency unlike soft penalties.
- Credibility: Authors candidly report AffDG and AffLL underperform perceptually and can be unstable.
- Novelty: Affine projection layer, instance noise stabilization, and derivation linking AffGAN to amortised VI are meaningful contributions.
- Clarity suggestion: Tighten exposition, summarize core optimization early with a high-level figure and brief algorithmic overview; move derivations to appendix.
- Empirical ablations requested: Isolate effect of affine projection vs. soft constraints on perceptual quality and LR-HR consistency with controlled ablations.
- Stability analysis requested: Report training curves and stability metrics with/without instance noise, and provide noise schedule and sensitivity analysis.
- Perceptual metrics: Include more quantitative perceptual metrics or a small user study since PSNR/SSIM favor MSE but not perceptual realism.
- Baseline comparisons: Compare directly to SRGAN-style baselines using the same downsampling operator and training data.
- Method details requested: Explain computation of A+ for typical downsamplers, kernel sizes, boundary handling, computational overhead, and whether A+ must be recomputed for different A or scales.
- Robustness question: Analyze sensitivity to model mismatch in A and how errors in A+ affect results.
- Efficiency: Provide run-time and memory comparisons versus standard SR CNNs.
- Representation analysis: Add qualitative/quantitative analysis of learned features with/without projection and under different objectives (e.g., frequency spectra, texture statistics).
- Hallucination control: Discuss mechanisms to balance hallucination vs. fidelity (conditional priors, content losses, uncertainty estimates) and potential hybrid objectives.
- Question 1: How sensitive is AffGAN to mismatch in the assumed downsampling operator A and can it handle unknown or spatially varying degradations?
- Question 2: What is the computational overhead of the affine projection in practice (forward/backward) and impact on inference speed?
- Question 3: Can you provide ablations quantifying the contribution of instance noise and sensitivity to the annealing schedule?
- Question 4: What are the main failure modes of AffDG and AffLL, and can curriculum strategies or better models close the gap to AffGAN?
- Question 5: How to balance perceptual realism and content fidelity to avoid plausible but incorrect reconstructions; would a hybrid objective help?
- Question 6: Given concerns about mode atypicality in high dimensions, are stochastic extensions (sampling from q(y|x)) practical, and can the AffGAN–VI link produce calibrated posterior samples?
