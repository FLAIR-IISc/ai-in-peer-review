- Summary: Reframes single-image SR as amortized MAP using an affine-projection layer (A+) enforcing exact LR–HR consistency and reducing training to minimizing cross-entropy between model and data, enabling training without paired LR–HR examples.
- Methods: Proposes three training strategies — AffGAN (GAN with instance-noise, minimizes KL[q‖pY]), AffDG (denoiser-guided score estimates), and AffLL (density-guided with differentiable PixelCNN/MCGSM).
- Empirical claims: Affine projection preserves exact consistency, can improve MSE/SSIM and training speed, and AffGAN yields the most visually plausible, sharp SR while AffDG/AffLL tend to be blurrier.
- Theoretical contributions: Provides theory grounding adversarial SR (instance-noise interpretation) and links a stochastic AffGAN variant to amortized variational inference.
- Decision: Recommend accept — well-motivated reframing, principled architectural constraint, theoretical analysis, and convincing empirical results (especially AffGAN).
- Motivation strength: Convincingly argues moving beyond MSE/PSNR to statistically plausible reconstructions and shows affine projection avoids soft-penalty pitfalls by enforcing the likelihood constraint exactly.
- Novelty: Novel SR contributions include guaranteed Afθ(x)=x affine architecture, amortized MAP → cross-entropy derivation, denoiser/density-guided training in this context, and instance-noise stabilization with divergence interpretation.
- Swiss-roll and ablation: Toy experiments show cross-entropy minimization toward MAP; ablations show no capacity loss from affine projection and better training dynamics than unconstrained models.
- Visual results and caveats: AffGAN produces sharp, plausible outputs but can introduce high-frequency artifacts or hallucinations (e.g., “snake→water”); MSE-based methods still dominate PSNR/SSIM.
- Comparisons suggestion: Add stronger SR baselines (e.g., SRCNN/ESPCN, SRGAN) and clarify differences from Ledig et al. (affine projection vs. soft constraints, objective, instance-noise).
- Evaluation suggestion: Include human studies or perceptual metrics (deep-feature distances, MS-SSIM) since PSNR/SSIM poorly reflect perceptual quality.
- Degradation model concern: Discuss sensitivity to mismatch in downsampling operator A and how to handle unknown or variable A in real-world SR.
- Instance-noise guidance: Provide practical details on annealing schedule and magnitude and compare to other stabilizers (label smoothing, WGAN).
- AffDG/AffLL failure analysis: Analyze failure modes and whether stronger priors or alternative noise schedules could mitigate blur and instability.
- Proofreading: Fix typos for polish.
- Q: A+ estimation sensitivity: Report how performance varies with numerical quality of A+ and performance when A+ is initialized incorrectly or learned.
- Q: AffGAN stability: Detail sensitivity to instance-noise variance/annealing, and effects of discriminator update frequency (convergence vs. limited updates).
- Q: Prior mismatch/hallucinations: Can conditioning be strengthened (e.g., conditional discriminators using x) without breaking affine constraint, and are there biases toward high-prior-density textures?
- Q: AffDG σ selection: How is denoiser noise σ selected/annealed to balance coverage vs. accuracy, and were score-matching or Stein estimators tried?
- Q: Density model capacity: Would more expressive densities (continuous PixelCNN variants) improve AffLL, and is the bottleneck capacity, optimization, or support mismatch?
- Q: Unknown A generalization: Can affine projection be extended by learning A jointly or estimating A per image while preserving consistency, and what practical obstacles exist?
