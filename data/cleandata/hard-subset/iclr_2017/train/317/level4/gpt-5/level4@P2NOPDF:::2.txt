This paper frames super-resolution as amortized maximum a posteriori (MAP) estimation and introduces a projection step to enforce consistent high- and low-resolution dependencies. This projection is empirically validated and shown to improve results across experiments. The authors further propose three distinct methods for addressing the resulting cross-entropy objective in Eq. 9, and evaluate them thoroughly.

Overall assessment: This is a strong paper—clearly written, well organized, and supported by solid experiments. The empirical evaluation combines well-chosen toy problems with realistic applications, and is sufficient to substantiate the claims. From my reading, the contributions specific to super-resolution (Sections 3.2–3.4) are novel, while some GAN-related training elements may overlap with prior variants in the literature, as the authors also discuss. The work is likely to influence future research on super-resolution; in particular, the finding that GAN-based training produces the most visually compelling reconstructions points to a promising direction for further exploration.
