Summary of contributions
- The paper proposes amortized MAP inference for single-image super-resolution (SR), arguing that MAP solutions better reflect natural image statistics than MSE/MAE-trained predictors. A key architectural contribution is an affine projection layer that enforces exact consistency with the downsampling operator (Afθ(x) = x), turning the amortized MAP objective into matching the marginal HR distribution (minimizing H[qθ, pY]) rather than supervised risk on paired LR–HR data.
- Three training strategies are developed: (1) AffGAN, a GAN-based approach with an objective related to KL[q‖p] and a stabilization technique termed instance noise; (2) AffDG, which backpropagates gradients from a denoiser to approximate ∂y log pY; and (3) AffLL, which uses a tractable likelihood model (PixelCNN/MCGSM) to guide training via cross-entropy.
- Experiments on a toy Swiss-roll, textures, faces (CelebA), and natural images (ImageNet) show that AffGAN yields the most visually plausible results, while the affine projection ensures perfect LR–HR consistency. The paper also draws a theoretical link between a stochastic variant of AffGAN and amortized variational inference.

Decision: Weak Accept
- Key reasons: (1) A principled and novel formulation of SR as amortized MAP with a compelling affine projection mechanism and clear theoretical grounding; (2) strong qualitative results and an insightful analysis of GAN stability (instance noise). However, the paper lacks comparisons on standard SR benchmarks and a clear differentiation from concurrent GAN-based SR (e.g., SRGAN).

Supporting arguments
- Problem significance and framing: The paper tackles the underdetermined nature of SR by replacing pixelwise EMP risk with amortized MAP, which is well-motivated for perceptual fidelity. The derivation showing that, with the affine projection, training reduces to minimizing H[qθ, pY] is elegant and places SR in an unsupervised/generative learning setting.
- Relation to the literature: The GAN perspective is well-situated and the connection to denoising gradients is technically sound. The instance noise rationale addresses known GAN instabilities. That said, relative novelty over SRGAN (Ledig et al., 2016) needs to be clarified beyond the affine projection and the KL/cross-entropy interpretation.
- Empirical support: The 2D Swiss-roll convincingly demonstrates that AffGAN and AffDG move toward MAP-like solutions and lower cross-entropy than MSE/MAE. On real images, AffGAN clearly improves perceptual sharpness while preserving consistency via the affine projection. However, the experimental evaluation misses standard SR datasets (Set5/Set14/BSD100), and the image sizes are capped at 128×128, which limits assessment of scalability and practical impact. AffDG/AffLL results are weaker and not fully explored, which leaves the generality of the amortized MAP framework less substantiated.

Additional feedback to improve the paper (helpful suggestions; not all part of the decision)
- Clearly position your contributions versus SRGAN: what is enabled or improved by amortized MAP and the affine projection that SRGAN did not provide (e.g., exact LR consistency, training without paired LR–HR, theoretical objective)?
- Provide results on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) with PSNR/SSIM and, if possible, perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS) and user studies. This will allow fair comparison with prior work and clarify the trade-off between perceptual quality and distortion metrics.
- Discuss and demonstrate scalability: show results at higher resolutions and analyze runtime/memory for larger images. Since SR often operates on arbitrary-sized inputs, including tiling/fully-convolutional behavior and boundary handling would be valuable.
- Clarify the training of the affine projection (A+). If A+ is learned numerically, detail whether any paired LR–HR data are required or whether A is assumed known (e.g., Gaussian downsampling). If A is unknown or mismatched to real degradations, discuss robustness and how to learn A/A+ jointly or adaptively.
- Report ablations: (i) with/without affine projection; (ii) effect of instance noise schedules; (iii) sensitivity to the downsampling kernel, and mismatches between assumed and true A; (iv) the impact of adding a latent noise vector to the generator.
- For AffDG and AffLL, include more analysis on why training struggled (e.g., gradient magnitude/variance, density model sharpness) and whether curriculum/annealing or alternative priors (e.g., score matching, diffusion models) could improve performance.

Questions for the authors
- Novelty vs SRGAN: Beyond qualitative similarities, what concrete advantages does your amortized MAP formulation (and the KL/cross-entropy perspective) bring over SRGAN? Is the affine projection the main differentiator, or does the objective lead to measurably different behavior?
- Training without pairs: You claim paired LR–HR data are unnecessary due to the affine projection, yet A+ must be instantiated. Does training or estimating A+ require paired {HR, LR} data, or is A assumed known and A+ computed numerically without supervision? Does this merely shift the need for supervision to learning A/A+?
- Standard benchmarks: Can you report results on Set5, Set14, and BSD100 to enable direct comparison with prior SR work?
- Scalability: How does the approach perform on larger images and arbitrary input sizes? Are there architectural or training changes needed to maintain perceptual quality at higher resolutions?
- Latent noise: Did you try adding a stochastic latent input z to the generator (in addition to x) to capture posterior uncertainty and increase sample diversity? If so, what was the impact on perceptual quality and stability?

Overall assessment
This is an original and thoughtful paper with solid theory and promising perceptual results, especially via AffGAN and the affine projection layer. Addressing the benchmarking and clarification points above would make the contribution substantially stronger.
