- Reframes single-image super-resolution as amortized MAP inference instead of MSE-based empirical risk minimization.
- Proposes learning a direct LR→HR MAP estimator that enforces data fidelity.
- Introduces an affine projection layer that guarantees LR–HR consistency (Aŷ = x) by projecting outputs onto the valid subspace.
- Training becomes matching the model’s marginal output distribution to the HR data distribution (minimize cross-entropy H[qθ, pY]).
- Removes requirement for paired LR–HR training data for learning the SR mapping under the formulation.
- Presents three training strategies: AffGAN (GAN with affine projection), AffDG (uses denoiser gradients to approximate ∇y log pY), and AffLL (uses a learned likelihood model).
- Adds instance noise to stabilize GAN training and connects AffGAN objective to amortized variational inference.
- Empirically, AffGAN produces visually sharper and more plausible images than MSE/MAE on toy and real data.
- The affine projection yields perfect LR–HR consistency by construction and does not hinder (and may speed) MSE training.
- Decision: Accept — novel, well-motivated reframing with a principled architectural mechanism and strong theoretical grounding.
- Strengths: principled hard constraint via affine projection, theoretical links (cross-entropy, KL, denoiser gradients, VI), and compelling qualitative perceptual gains.
- Limitation: missing evaluations on standard SR benchmarks (Set5/Set14/BSD100) and broader quantitative perceptual metrics.
- Limitation: assumes a known, noiseless downsampling operator A; robustness to mismatch or unknown A is not evaluated.
- Limitation: experiments limited to outputs up to 128×128; scalability to larger resolutions and arbitrary input sizes is not demonstrated.
- Suggestion: include benchmark results (PSNR/SSIM and perceptual metrics) for fair comparison with prior SR methods.
- Suggestion: evaluate robustness to degradation-model mismatch (blur/noise, unknown A) or include an adaptive/learned A variant.
- Suggestion: demonstrate scaling to larger outputs (256×256, 512×512) and discuss memory/computation or tiling strategies.
- Suggestion: provide ablation on instance noise (with/without, annealing) and compare to one-sided label smoothing.
- Suggestion: explore stochastic AffGAN with latent noise to study posterior sampling vs MAP and perceptual diversity.
- Clarity request: explain how A+ (pseudo-inverse) is obtained in practice and whether it requires paired data; move practical details from appendix to main text.
- Questions: quantify added value over prior GAN SR (e.g., Ledig et al., 2016) via side-by-side comparisons and ablations with/without projection.
- Questions: clarify training data requirements and whether learning A+ needs paired LR–HR images.
- Questions: report results on standard datasets or provide bicubic-downsampling compatibility results.
- Questions: report scalability, artifacts at tile boundaries, and generalization to arbitrary input sizes.
- Questions: have you tried adding latent noise to the generator and how does it affect perceptual quality and LR consistency?
- Questions: how sensitive is the method to deviations from assumed A or to additive sensor noise, and can the framework learn/adapt A?
