The paper presents a new framework for solving the super-resolution (SR) problem via amortized MAP inference and adopts a pre-learned affine projection layer to ensure the output is consistent with the LR input. It also proposes three different methods to minimize cross-entropy. Overall, this is a strong paper. However, I have several comments:

1) The proposed amortized MAP inference is novel and distinct from previous SR methods. Combined with a GAN, this framework produces plausible and high-quality results. However, compared with another GAN-based SR method, “Photo-Realistic Single Image Super-Resolution Using a Generative Adversarial Network,” questions remain about what this new formulation adds beyond the latest state of the art.

2) By using an affine projection layer as a constraint, the model does not require corresponding {HR, LR} image pairs for training. However, training the affine projection layer itself still requires {HR, LR} pairs. Does this simply shift the supervision requirement from the main model to the affine projection layer?

3) The paper presents many results for natural images from ImageNet. Please also provide results on Set5, Set14, and BSD100—conventional SR test sets—so that we can make a fair comparison with prior work.

4) The natural-image results shown are limited to 128×128 resolution. Can this framework perform well on larger images? In practice, SR systems encounter inputs of arbitrary size.

5) A standard GAN includes a noise term as a latent variable to model a distribution. Did the authors try incorporating a noise vector?

Overall, this paper proposes a new SR framework with solid theoretical analysis. The idea is novel and the authors explore several methodological variants. That said, questions remain about the necessity of some components, and additional experiments are needed. I believe this work will provide valuable inspiration to the community.
