- Novel amortized MAP + GAN approach yields plausible SR but it's unclear what concrete advantages it provides over prior GAN-based SR; request head-to-head comparisons and ablations isolating amortized MAP benefits.  
- Affine projection removes paired {HR,LR} requirement for the main model but itself requires paired data for pretraining—clarify data needs, training pipeline, retraining frequency, and effects on generalization.  
- Results only on ImageNet; include standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) with corresponding metrics and recent baselines for fair comparison.  
- Qualitative results limited to 128×128; discuss scalability to larger/arbitrary input sizes, architectural/computational constraints, and provide performance/runtime evidence at higher resolutions.  
- Did the authors experiment with injecting latent noise? Report effects on perceptual quality, output diversity, and training stability within the amortized MAP framework.  
- Overall: promising and theoretically solid, but addressing the above empirical and clarification points—especially incremental contribution vs GAN-SR, affine projection supervision impact, and benchmark comparisons—would strengthen the paper.
