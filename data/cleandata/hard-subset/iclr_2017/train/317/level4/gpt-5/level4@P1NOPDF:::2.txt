This paper proposes addressing super-resolution as amortized MAP estimation. It introduces a projection step to enforce consistent high-resolution–low-resolution (HR–LR) dependencies, and demonstrates experimentally that this yields improved results throughout. In addition, the authors propose and evaluate three different methods for solving the resulting cross-entropy problem in Eq. 9.

Summary: This is a very strong paper—well written, clearly presented, and convincingly argued. The experimental results are sufficient and well chosen, spanning both illustrative toy examples and real-world applications. To the best of my understanding, the core contributions to super-resolution (Sections 3.2, 3.3, and 3.4) are novel. Some components that are specific to GAN training may have appeared in related forms elsewhere (see also the authors’ discussion), but the integration within the proposed framework and the emphasis on amortized MAP appear original and valuable.

I expect this paper to be influential for future work in super-resolution. In particular, the empirical finding that GAN-based training yields the most visually appealing outputs is noteworthy and suggests a promising direction for further research in this domain.
