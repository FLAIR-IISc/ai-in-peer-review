Apologies for the late review.

This paper proposes treating super-resolution as amortized MAP estimation. It introduces a projection step to ensure consistent HR–LR dependencies, which is experimentally validated to yield improved results throughout. The paper further proposes and evaluates three different methods for solving the resulting cross-entropy problem in Eq. 9.

Overall assessment: This is a very good paper—clearly written and well presented. The experimental results are sufficient, with well-chosen toy examples and real-world applications. To my understanding, the contributions to super-resolution are novel (Sections 3.2, 3.3, 3.4), while parts that are specific to GAN training may have appeared in different variants elsewhere (as discussed in the paper). I believe this work will be relevant to future research on super-resolution. The finding that GAN-based model training yields the most visually appealing results is especially compelling and suggests further work in this direction.
