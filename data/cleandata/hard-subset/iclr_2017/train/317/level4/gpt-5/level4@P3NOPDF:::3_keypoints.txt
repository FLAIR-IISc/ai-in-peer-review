- Proposal summary: learns an affine subspace of HR images that are LR-consistent under a known degradation to enable amortized MAP inference for single-image SR.
- Strength — novelty: separating measurement consistency from a learned prior via an LR-consistent affine subspace is conceptually compelling.
- Strength — flexibility: same parameterization supports GAN-, noise-, and density-assisted training/inference for different goals (perception vs. fidelity).
- Strength — empirical promise: results on multiple datasets show visually plausible reconstructions that adhere to the degradation model.
- Weakness — exposition: paper is hard to follow; key definitions and consistency enforcement should be presented earlier and more clearly.
- Weakness — method details unclear: unclear how the affine subspace is parameterized or how projections and gradient flow are implemented.
- Weakness — empirical gaps: lacks quantitative measurement-consistency metrics, subspace-dimension/projection ablations, perception–fidelity trade-off analysis, and runtime/scalability comparisons.
- Weakness — learned representation not analyzed: missing insights into what the basis/U captures (frequencies, textures, structures).
- Weakness — positioning: insufficient comparison and discussion relative to MAP with learned priors, SRGAN/ESRGAN, deep image prior, plug-and-play/RED, and data-consistent generative models.
- Suggestion — clarify model: state forward operator/noise model, define affine form x=x0+Uz with Hx=y, and explain how x0 and U are obtained and enforced.
- Suggestion — provide algorithms: include concise pseudocode for GAN, noise-assisted, and density-assisted variants specifying losses, projection steps, and optimization.
- Suggestion — expand evaluation: report PSNR/SSIM and perceptual metrics (LPIPS/NIQE), LR-consistency error, ablations on subspace dimension and projection strength, and runtime/memory comparisons.
- Suggestion — robustness tests: evaluate degradation mismatch (unseen kernels, noise levels), different scales, and compare to strong data-consistent baselines (iterative projection, PnP, generative priors).
- Suggestion — analyze learned basis: visualize basis columns, frequency responses, spectrum/conditioning, diversity of samples for a fixed LR input, and feature attributions.
- Suggestion — improve readability: tighten notation, add an illustrative figure of the affine subspace and LR manifold, group objectives, and proofread for typos/consistency.
- Questions — construction: is there an explicit projection operator, and does training guarantee exact LR consistency or only penalize deviations?
- Questions — dimensionality: what is the effective subspace dimension and how sensitive is performance to that choice?
- Questions — unknown degradations: can the method handle unknown or spatially varying degradations and how is the operator adapted or identified?
- Questions — variant comparison: how do GAN/noise/density variants compare across the fidelity–perception trade-off (e.g., Pareto frontier)?
- Questions — mode collapse: does amortization reduce or increase mode collapse relative to purely adversarial SR models?
- Overall assessment: promising and elegant idea with encouraging results, but requires clearer exposition, stronger positioning, and deeper quantitative and representation analyses before acceptance.
