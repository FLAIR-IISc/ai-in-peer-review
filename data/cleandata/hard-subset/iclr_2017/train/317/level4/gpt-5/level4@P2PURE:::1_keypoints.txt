1) Novel amortized MAP inference combined with GANs produces high-quality results, but unclear what advantages it offers over existing GAN-based SR (e.g., SRGAN).  
2) Affine projection lets SR training avoid paired HR–LR data, yet the affine projection layer itself requires HR–LR pairs—possibly just shifting paired supervision.  
3) Request to report standard benchmark results (Set5, Set14, BSD100) for fair comparison with prior work.  
4) Experiments are limited to 128×128 images—need evidence the method scales to larger, arbitrary-size inputs.  
5) Standard GANs use stochastic latent noise to model output distributions—did the authors experiment with a noise vector?  
6) Overall assessment: novel framework with solid theory and multiple methods explored, but questions remain on necessity and more experiments are needed; potentially valuable to the community.
