- Reframes single-image SR as amortised MAP inference with an affine projection enforcing exact LR–HR consistency.
- Affine projection layer ensures Aŷ = x, turning objective into minimising cross-entropy between model output distribution and true HR distribution.
- Introduces three training strategies: AffGAN (affine-projected GAN with KL-targeted update and instance noise), AffDG (uses denoiser-estimated score ∇y log pY), and AffLL (trains against a tractable density model).
- Provides a stochastic extension linking AffGAN to amortised variational inference.
- Experiments on Swiss-roll, textures, CelebA, and ImageNet show affine projection preserves LR consistency and accelerates training.
- AffGAN produces the sharpest, most plausible reconstructions; AffDG/AffLL are weaker and have stability/sharpness issues.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: well-motivated formulation and novel, practical architectural contribution enforcing exact affine consistency.
- Key reason: strong empirical and conceptual support with toy evidence, perceptual results, and theoretical link between GANs and amortised VI.
- Targets known MSE/PSNR limitation (blurriness under multimodal posteriors) and justifies MAP-like solutions for perceptual SR.
- Affine projection avoids soft penalties that fail to guarantee consistency and reduces learning to HR distribution matching.
- GAN update targeting KL[q‖p] and instance-noise stabilisation are timely given GAN instability concerns.
- Swiss-roll results show AffGAN/AffDG approach MAP better than MSE/MAE; affine projection yields zero LR–HR error.
- Texture/face/ImageNet results show sharper images with AffGAN; limitations of AffDG/AffLL acknowledged.
- Theory is clear about assumptions (e.g., Bayes-optimal discriminator) and motivates instance noise for support overlap.
