Introduces an amortized MAP estimation method for super-resolution by training a network that projects inputs onto an affine subspace of LR-consistent solutions.

Allows recovery within this subspace via multiple strategies (GAN-based priors, noise-assisted optimization, density-assisted optimization).

Presents clear empirical results on several datasets; reviewer finds the results convincing.

Conceptually appealing and practically useful: enforces LR consistency through a learned projection while supporting flexible downstream optimization.

Writing and presentation are unclear and need substantial improvement; paper is sometimes hard to follow.

Needs tighter organization, more precise definitions, and a clearer description of the training procedure and how the affine subspace is parameterized.

Lacks analysis of what the learned projection networks capture and how that relates to the affine subspace and LR consistency.

Requests additional qualitative and quantitative evaluation of the projection network to improve interpretability and assessment.
