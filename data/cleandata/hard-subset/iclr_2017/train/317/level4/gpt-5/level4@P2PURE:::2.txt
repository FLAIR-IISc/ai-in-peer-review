Sincere apologies for the delayed review.
This paper proposes framing super-resolution as amortized MAP estimation. It introduces a projection step to maintain consistent HR–LR dependencies, and shows experimentally that this yields improved results throughout. In addition, it proposes and evaluates three different methods for solving the resulting cross-entropy objective in Eq. 9.

Summary: This is a very good paper—well written and clearly presented. The experimental results are sufficient, with well-chosen toy examples and real-world applications. To my understanding, the super-resolution contributions in Sections 3.2, 3.3, and 3.4 are novel; components specific to GAN training may have appeared in variant forms elsewhere (see also the discussion). I believe this paper will be relevant to future work on super-resolution. The finding that GAN-based model training produces the most visually appealing results suggests further exploration in that direction.

The manuscript should be proofread once more; there are a few minor typos worth fixing.
