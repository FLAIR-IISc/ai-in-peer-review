- Proposes amortized MAP inference for single-image super-resolution with an affine projection layer that enforces exact data consistency (Aŷ = x) and removes need for paired LR-HR data by matching output distribution qθ to pY.  
- Introduces three instantiations: AffGAN (GAN variant with instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-based score backprop), and AffLL (likelihood-based PixelCNN/MCGSM baseline).  
- AffGAN yields the most perceptually plausible results on real-image tasks (grass textures, CelebA, ImageNet, 4× SR).  
- On a 2D Swiss-roll toy, methods approach the MAP cross-entropy; affine projection yields zero LR-consistency error and reduces H[qθ, pY] versus soft constraints.  
- Affine projection can improve stability and training speed even for MSE-trained models.  
- Provides a theoretical connection between stochastic AffGAN and amortized variational inference (Appendix F).  
- Instance noise is analyzed and shown to plausibly stabilize GAN training.  
- Decision: Accept — motivated by a clear, principled formulation, elegant data-consistency projection, solid theoretical framing, ablations, and compelling empirical results.  
- Addresses SR multimodality and MSE averaging pathologies by aiming for high-posterior MAP solutions rather than pixel-wise averaging.  
- Positions work relative to SRGAN by enforcing exact LR consistency (affine projection) and tying training to cross-entropy/KL objectives.  
- Some evaluation gaps remain (missing standard SR benchmarks and larger images), but these do not undermine core contributions.  
- Suggestion: Add results on Set5/Set14/BSD100 (PSNR/SSIM and perceptual assessments) for direct comparison with prior SR work.  
- Suggestion: Provide a controlled comparison to SRGAN under matched backbones/training to quantify benefits (fewer artifacts, better LR consistency, training stability).  
- Suggestion: Demonstrate performance on higher resolutions/arbitrary input sizes and discuss runtime/memory scaling and boundary handling for affine projection.  
- Suggestion: Clarify practical data requirements and how A and A+ are obtained or learned (Appendix B hints at numeric estimation without pairs).  
- Suggestion: Empirically evaluate stochastic outputs with an explicit latent z to show conditional sample diversity and uncertainty behavior.  
- Suggestion: Add perceptual metrics or user studies to better reflect perceptual quality goals beyond PSNR/SSIM.  
- Suggestion: Provide instance-noise ablation (schedule/variance) to improve reproducibility and understand stability-quality trade-offs.

- Question: Do you require any paired LR-HR data at any stage; how are A and A+ chosen or trained; can A+ be learned from synthetic probes without pairs?  
- Question: Can the method handle arbitrary image sizes at inference without boundary/tiling artifacts, and how does performance scale to e.g., 512×512 images?  
- Question: Have you tried adding a latent noise vector z during training; how does it affect diversity, artifacts, and LR alignment?
