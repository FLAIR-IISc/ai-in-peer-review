- Proposes amortised MAP inference for single-image SR using an affine projection that enforces exact data fidelity (Aŷ = x) and reframes training as matching reconstructed-image marginals to a natural-image prior without paired LR–HR data.  
- Introduces three training strategies: AffGAN (GAN with discriminator and instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-guided using estimated score ∇y log pY), and AffLL (density-guided using a differentiable likelihood prior).  
- Shows affine projection does not harm and can improve MSE-trained SR, and reports experiments on Swiss-roll, textures, CelebA, and ImageNet with AffGAN giving the sharpest, most plausible outputs.  
- Links a stochastic variant of AffGAN to amortised variational inference, providing theoretical connections to GANs and VI.  
- Decision: Accept, because of a novel, well-motivated formulation with an elegant affine projection and solid empirical evidence, especially for AffGAN.  
- Addresses the ill-posed SR problem by approximating MAP to avoid MSE averaging/blurriness while retaining amortised inference efficiency.  
- Affine projection is a principled alternative to soft fidelity penalties, exactly enforcing LR–HR consistency; instance noise is used to stabilise GAN training.  
- Denoiser-guided approach leverages known denoising–score estimation theory to provide gradients for training.  
- Swiss-roll: AffGAN and AffDG approach brute-force MAP in cross-entropy; MSE/MAE produce low-density/blurry solutions.  
- Affine projection guarantees zero LR-consistency error; soft constraints fail to guarantee this.  
- Affine-projected networks train faster and reach better MSE/SSIM on CelebA while maintaining exact consistency.  
- Textures: AffGAN yields sharp plausible textures; AffDG and AffLL converge but produce blurry results, indicating limitations of denoiser/density guidance for textures.  
- Faces and ImageNet: AffGAN produces sharper images with good global structure but sometimes high-frequency noise or semantic drift; PSNR/SSIM still favour MSE as expected.  
- Overall experiments and analyses support the paper’s central claims, with AffGAN showing clear advantages and other methods offering useful insights into limitations.  
- Suggest tightening exposition around affine projection (define A and A+ early, explain ΠAx and null-space projection) and moving key appendix material (e.g., instance noise rationale) into the main text.  
- Recommend deeper analysis of learned models (visualise null-space residuals, intermediate features, frequency spectra; study instance noise effects on discriminator features).  
- Advise adding perceptual metrics or human studies and more ablations (with/without affine projection, soft-constraint strengths, instance-noise schedules, sensitivity to A/A+).  
- Request more reproducibility details: training stability tricks, hyperparameters (instance-noise annealing, learning rates, discriminator updates), A+ estimation procedure/code, and computational cost.  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to mis-specification of A (blur kernel mismatch), and can the method handle unknown degradations or jointly estimate A?  
- Question: Is A+ fixed after estimation or can it be learned safely, and are there results across different downsampling kernels or scales?  
- Question: How is instance noise variance set and annealed in practice, were other stabilisation methods compared quantitatively, and does instance noise affect final sharpness or introduce high-frequency noise?  
- Question: Why were AffDG/AffLL blurry on textures—due to poor gradient estimates early in training or limitations of the priors—and would multi-scale priors or better density models help?  
- Question: How robust are denoiser/density methods to choices of noise levels and annealing schedules?  
- Question: Have alternative color or feature spaces for MAP been explored, and how do results change?  
- Question: Can the stochastic AffGAN variant be used to sample from p(Y|X) rather than just produce MAP-like reconstructions?  
- Question: How does AffGAN compare to perceptual-loss SR methods that combine feature-space distances with adversarial losses under both fidelity and perceptual metrics, and are there results for other upscaling factors or standard SR benchmarks?
