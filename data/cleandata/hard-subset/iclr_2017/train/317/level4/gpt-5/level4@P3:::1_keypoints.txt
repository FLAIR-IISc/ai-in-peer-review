- Proposes amortized MAP inference for single-image SR with an affine projection enforcing Aŷ = x.
- Shows amortized MAP reduces to minimizing cross-entropy H[qθ, pY], reframing SR as generative modeling without paired data.
- Introduces three training strategies: AffGAN (GAN on affine-projected outputs with instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-guided using ∇y log pY), and AffLL (density-guided with a differentiable likelihood model).
- Designs an affine projection layer implementable via deconvolution that guarantees exact LR-HR consistency.
- Provides a novel theoretical connection between stochastic AffGAN and amortized variational inference / KL minimization.
- Empirical results (toy, textures, CelebA, ImageNet) show AffGAN yields sharper, more plausible images than MSE/MAE and soft-constraint baselines while preserving LR consistency.
- Decision: Weak accept.
- Main reasons: principled reformulation of SR, elegant affine-projection mechanism, and insightful theoretical analysis likely to influence future work.
- Correctness: Derivations (cross-entropy objective, KL view for AffGAN, instance noise role, null-space projection) are sound and well argued.
- Toy experiments validate that AffGAN/AffDG approach MAP and affine projection achieves zero LR-consistency error.
- Empirical support is qualitative and shows sharper outputs, but lacks comparisons on standard SR benchmarks and broader quantitative metrics.
- Suggestion: Include standard SR benchmarks (Set5/Set14/BSD100) under bicubic downsampling for direct comparison (PSNR/SSIM).
- Suggestion: Report inference speed and model size versus common baselines (SRCNN/ESPCN/SRGAN) for practical relevance.
- Suggestion: Add perceptual metrics (e.g., MS-SSIM) and a small human study to substantiate “plausibility.”
- Suggestion: Demonstrate scaling to larger images and arbitrary resolutions, and clarify boundary handling and non-multiple-of-stride inputs.
- Suggestion: Discuss sensitivity to mismatch between assumed downsampling operator A and real degradations, and whether A can be learned/adapted.
- Suggestion: Provide head-to-head comparisons against SRGAN with identical data and downsampling to isolate contributions (affine projection, instance noise).
- Suggestion: Detail instance-noise schedule, sensitivity, failure modes, and include training curves for D/G losses.
- Clarify claim of unpaired training: specify required relationships between pX and pY and sensitivity to mismatched LR distributions.
- Note: AffDG and AffLL produce blur on real images; suggest diagnostics (gradient spectra, likelihood landscapes) to explain failures and whether better denoisers/density models help.
- Suggestion: Experimentally evaluate stochastic AffGAN with latent z to assess conditional sample diversity and posterior coverage.
- Questions: What datasets are used for pX and pY, and must pX be generated by the same operator A applied to pY samples?
- Questions: How is A+ estimated in practice; does this require HR-LR pairs or only knowledge of A; what kernel/support is used for large images?
- Questions: Under identical settings, how does AffGAN compare to SRGAN in PSNR/SSIM, perceptual quality, and LR-consistency?
- Questions: What instance-noise levels and annealing schedules were used; how robust is convergence to these choices and were other noise types tried?
- Questions: Were stochastic generators (latent z) tried in experiments; if so, how diverse are conditional samples and do they reduce artifacts?
- Questions: Can the affine projection be applied convolutionally to arbitrary-size images at test time without retraining, and what boundary handling is needed?
- Overall: Compelling, principled perspective with a simple powerful constraint and solid theory; stronger experimental comparisons and stochastic inference demos would increase impact.
