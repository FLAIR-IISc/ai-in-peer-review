- Summary: Proposes amortized MAP for single-image SR with a learned affine projection enforcing LR-consistency, three cross-entropy minimization strategies, and adversarial training yielding visually plausible outputs; conceptually novel and theoretically motivated.

- Strength: Novelty—amortized MAP + affine projection is a fresh, elegant approach relative to regression-based SR.

- Strength: Theoretical grounding—the projection constraint directly encodes the forward image formation model.

- Strength: Promising visual quality—adversarially trained models produce realistic textures.

- Concern 1: Relation to prior GAN-based SR—unclear benefits over SRGAN; needs head-to-head comparison and ablation isolating amortized MAP and the projection layer.

- Concern 2: Training without paired data vs projection pre-training—projection is learned from paired data, raising questions about shifted supervision, sensitivity to degradation mismatch, and required data volume/diversity.

- Concern 3: Benchmarking—missing quantitative and visual comparisons on standard SR datasets (Set5, Set14, BSD100, Urban100) using PSNR/SSIM and perceptual metrics.

- Concern 4: Resolution and scalability—results limited to 128×128; need discussion and empirical evidence for larger/arbitrary sizes, memory/use of tiling, and runtime profiling.

- Concern 5: Role of stochasticity—unclear if noise input is used or beneficial; clarify determinism from MAP objective and effects on diversity and LR-consistency.

- Concern 6: Cross-entropy strategies—three proposed methods lack clear comparison, motivation, computational trade-offs, and thorough ablation.

- Concern 7: Stability and training dynamics—need analysis of how amortized MAP + projection affects GAN stability, training curves, seed variance, and hyperparameter sensitivity.

- Concern 8: Clarity of projection constraint—require details on affine projection architecture/parameterization, whether LR-consistency is exact or approximate, and projection error analysis.

- Suggestions: Add SRGAN and strong non-adversarial baselines on standard benchmarks, component-wise ablations, large-image experiments with profiling, clarify projection training protocol and robustness, and explore stochastic variants.

- Overall assessment: Interesting, well-motivated, and promising qualitatively, but requires rigorous benchmarking, ablations, and scalability evidence to substantiate advantages and impact.
