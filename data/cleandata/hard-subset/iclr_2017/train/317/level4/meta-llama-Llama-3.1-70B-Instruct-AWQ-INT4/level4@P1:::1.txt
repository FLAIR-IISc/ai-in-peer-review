The paper presents a new framework to solve the single image super-resolution (SR) problem using amortized Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference. The authors adopt a pre-learned affine projection layer to ensure the output is consistent with the low-resolution (LR) input. They also propose three different methods to solve the problem of minimizing cross-entropy. 

The proposed amortized MAP inference is novel and different from previous SR methods. When combined with Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), this framework can obtain plausible and good results. However, it is unclear what this new formulation adds to the latest state-of-the-art, particularly when compared to other GAN-based SR methods such as Photo-Realistic Single Image Super-Resolution Using a Generative Adversarial Network.

The authors propose using an affine projection architecture as a constraint, which eliminates the need for corresponding high-resolution (HR) and LR image pairs for training. However, it is not clear whether this approach simply transfers the training procedure to the training of the affine projection layer, which still requires HR and LR image pairs.

The paper presents many results of the framework, including the results of natural images from ImageNet. To perform a fair comparison with previous work, it would be beneficial to provide results on conventional test datasets for SR, such as Set5, Set14, or BSD100.

The size of the results of natural images presented in this paper is limited to 128*128. It is unclear whether this framework can perform well on images with larger sizes, as SR will encounter inputs with arbitrary sizes.

A typical GAN has a noise term as a latent space, which allows it to better learn a distribution. It is unclear whether the authors have tried using a noise vector in their framework.

Overall, this paper provides a new framework for SR with solid theoretical analysis. The idea is novel, and the authors explore many methods. However, there are still some questions regarding the necessity of this approach and the need for more experiments. This work has the potential to provide good inspiration to the community.

Decision: Accept (with minor revisions)

Reasons for the decision: The paper presents a novel framework for SR with solid theoretical analysis. The idea is innovative, and the authors explore many methods. Although there are some questions and areas for improvement, the paper has the potential to contribute to the community.
