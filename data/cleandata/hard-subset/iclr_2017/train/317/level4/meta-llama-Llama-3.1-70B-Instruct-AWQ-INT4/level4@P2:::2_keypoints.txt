- Novel formulation: image super-resolution posed as amortised MAP inference with a projection step enforcing HR–LR consistency that improves results.
- Methodology: three methods proposed to solve the resulting cross-entropy problem and evaluated experimentally.
- Affine-projected GANs: introduced and shown to be particularly promising for SR.
- Theoretical contribution: establishes a connection between GANs and amortised variational inference.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, clearly structured, and provides thorough explanations of theory and methods.
- Experiments: results are sufficient, balanced between toy examples and real-world datasets, and well-presented.
- Possible overlap: some GAN-training ideas may have appeared in variants elsewhere, though the authors clarify their contributions.
- Minor edits: paper needs brief proofreading to fix a few typos.
- Recommendation: accept.
- Decision reasons: novel, well-motivated SR approach; affine-projected GANs show strong potential.
- Supporting points: clear theoretical exposition; adequate experimental validation; valuable GAN–VI connection.
- Suggested improvements: add more discussion of limitations and future work; expand related-work coverage on GAN training.
- Requests for authors: provide computational-complexity comparisons to SOTA; address limitations of assuming a fixed image prior; give more insights into extending the GAN–amortised VI connection.
