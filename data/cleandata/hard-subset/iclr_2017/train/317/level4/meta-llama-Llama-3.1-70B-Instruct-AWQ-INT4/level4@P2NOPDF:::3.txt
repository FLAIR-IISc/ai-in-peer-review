This paper introduces an amortized MAP estimation method for super-resolution (SR) problems, which learns to project onto an affine subspace of SR solutions that are consistent with the low-resolution (LR) input. By leveraging a neural network, the method enables finding proper solutions using various techniques, including GANs, noise-assisted, and density-assisted optimization.

The results are convincingly demonstrated on several datasets, showcasing the method's effectiveness. Overall, I appreciate the paper's contributions, but I believe the writing can be significantly improved for clarity and readability. At times, the text was difficult to follow, and given the complexity of the subject matter, clearer presentation would greatly benefit the reader.

Specifically, I would suggest reorganizing certain sections and providing more intuitive explanations for the key concepts. Additionally, I would love to see a more in-depth analysis of the resulting networks, such as the types of features they learn and how they contribute to the overall performance. This would provide valuable insights into the method's strengths and limitations, further enhancing the paper's impact.
