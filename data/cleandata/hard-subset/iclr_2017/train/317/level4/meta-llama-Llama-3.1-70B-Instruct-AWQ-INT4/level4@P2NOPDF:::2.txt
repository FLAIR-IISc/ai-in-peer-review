This paper proposes an innovative approach to Super-Resolution by formulating it as amortized MAP estimation. A key contribution is the introduction of a projection step to maintain consistent high-resolution (HR) and low-resolution (LR) dependencies, which is experimentally validated to yield improved results. Furthermore, the authors present three distinct methods to solve the cross-entropy problem in Eq. 9 and provide experimental evaluations of these approaches.

Summary: Overall, this is a well-written and well-presented paper. The experimental results are sufficient, and the authors provide well-chosen toy examples and real-world applications to illustrate their approach. In my opinion, the contributions to the field of super-resolution (Sections 3.2, 3.3, and 3.4) are novel. While some aspects related to the training of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) may have been explored in different variants elsewhere (also discussed later), I believe that this paper will have a significant impact on future work in super-resolution. The finding that GAN-based model training produces the most visually appealing results suggests that further research in this domain is warranted.
