This paper presents a novel approach to Super-Resolution by formulating it as amortized MAP estimation. The authors propose a projection step to maintain consistent high-resolution (HR) and low-resolution (LR) dependencies, which is experimentally validated to yield improved results. Furthermore, they introduce and evaluate three distinct methods to solve the cross-entropy problem presented in Equation 9.

Summary: Overall, this is an excellent paper, well-written and effectively presented. The experimental results are comprehensive, featuring well-selected toy examples and real-world applications. In my opinion, the contributions to the field of super-resolution (sections 3.2, 3.3, and 3.4) are innovative, whereas the aspects specific to GAN training may have been explored in various forms elsewhere (also discussed later). I firmly believe that this paper will have a lasting impact on future super-resolution research, and the finding that GAN-based model training produces the most visually appealing results warrants further investigation in this area.

Before publication, I recommend a thorough proofread to address the few minor typos that were encountered.
