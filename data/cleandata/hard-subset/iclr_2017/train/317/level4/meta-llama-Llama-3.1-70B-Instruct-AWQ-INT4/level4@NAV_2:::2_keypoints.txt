- Proposes super-resolution as amortised MAP estimation with a novel projection step to enforce consistent HR-LR dependencies.  
- Introduces and empirically validates a projection step that improves HR-LR consistency.  
- Presents three distinct methods to solve the resulting cross-entropy problem and compares their performance.  
- Experimental evaluation includes both toy examples and real-world applications, showing sufficient and well-chosen results.  
- Finds GAN-based training produces the most visually appealing outputs, highlighted as important for future work.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and easy to follow.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution to super-resolution; recommendation: accept.  
- Key reasons for acceptance: novel amortised MAP formulation, adequate experiments, and clear presentation.  
- Supporting argument: projection step is a key innovation that enforces correct dependencies.  
- Supporting argument: the three proposed solution methods are well-motivated and effectively tested.  
- Supporting argument: empirical evidence that GAN-based models yield superior visual quality.  
- Suggested improvement: provide more context on current super-resolution literature and positioning.  
- Suggested improvement: clarify/expand mathematical notation and equations for accessibility.  
- Suggested improvement: discuss limitations and challenges, especially scalability and computational cost.  
- Questions for authors: explain selection and evaluation criteria for the cross-entropy solution methods.  
- Questions for authors: describe how the approach would integrate into practical super-resolution pipelines.  
- Questions for authors: any plans to extend the method to other image-processing or computer-vision tasks?
