- Proposes a novel amortised MAP estimation method for image super-resolution.  
- Learns a neural network projecting outputs to an affine subspace to ensure LR–HR consistency.  
- Framework allows use of GANs, noise-assisted, and density-assisted optimisation to find solutions.  
- Motivated as an improvement over MSE-based empirical risk minimisation for SR.  
- Argues amortised MAP yields solutions with higher posterior probability under an image prior.  
- Well-situated in literature, building on GANs, denoising, and density models.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis of the method, including affine projection rationale.  
- Experimental results show effectiveness across datasets, including challenging grass textures.  
- Quantitative and qualitative comparisons indicate sharper, more plausible images than MSE and soft-constrained GANs.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing novelty, effectiveness, and thorough evaluation.  
- Suggested writing improvements to enhance clarity and readability.  
- Requests more analysis of learned network features and visualisations (e.g., affine subspace plots).  
- Recommends additional comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Questions for authors: how the affine projection layer affects learned features, sensitivity to different noise/density choices, and applicability to other inverse problems (deblurring, inpainting).
