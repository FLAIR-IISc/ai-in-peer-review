This paper presents a novel approach to image super-resolution (SR) by formulating it as amortised Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference. The authors propose a projection step to ensure consistency between high-resolution (HR) and low-resolution (LR) images, which is experimentally verified to improve results. Three methods are introduced to solve the resulting cross-entropy problem, and their performance is evaluated on various datasets.

The paper is well-written and clearly structured, making it easy to follow. The authors provide a thorough explanation of their approach, including the theoretical background and the proposed methods. The experimental results are sufficient and well-presented, with a good balance between toy examples and real-world applications.

The contributions of this paper are novel and relevant to the field of super-resolution. The proposed methods, particularly the affine projected Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), show promising results and suggest further work in this domain. The connection established between GANs and amortised variational inference is also an interesting contribution.

One potential criticism is that some of the ideas related to GAN training may have appeared in different variants elsewhere. However, the authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of their approach, making it easy to understand the contributions of their work.

Overall, I believe that this paper will be relevant to future work on super-resolution and GANs. The writing is clear, and the experimental results are well-presented. With some minor proof-reading to fix a few typos, this paper is ready for publication.

Decision: Accept

Reasons for decision:

1. The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to image super-resolution, which is a relevant and timely contribution to the field.
2. The proposed methods, particularly the affine projected GANs, show promising results and suggest further work in this domain.

Supporting arguments:

* The paper provides a thorough explanation of the theoretical background and the proposed methods, making it easy to follow and understand.
* The experimental results are sufficient and well-presented, with a good balance between toy examples and real-world applications.
* The connection established between GANs and amortised variational inference is an interesting contribution that suggests further work in this domain.

Additional feedback:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the proposed approach and potential future work.
* Some of the related work could be more thoroughly discussed, particularly in the context of GAN training.
* The writing is clear, but some minor proof-reading is needed to fix a few typos.

Questions for the authors:

* Can you provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed methods and how they compare to other state-of-the-art methods?
* How do you plan to address the potential limitations of the proposed approach, particularly in terms of the assumption of a fixed image prior?
* Can you provide more insights on the connection between GANs and amortised variational inference, and how this contribution can be further developed in future work?
