- Proposes a novel amortized MAP inference framework for image super-resolution (SR).
- Introduces an affine projection architecture to enforce LR-HR consistency without paired data.
- Motivates approach by clearly explaining limitations of empirical risk minimization in SR.
- Presents three methods to minimize cross-entropy: GANs, denoiser-guided SR, and a max-likelihood image-prior baseline.
- Highlights a GAN-based approach with a stochastic extension for amortized variational inference.
- Provides a theoretical proof that the GAN-based method minimizes KL divergence between model and data distributions.
- Offers thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed methods and affine projection properties.
- Empirically evaluates methods on a 2D toy problem, grass textures, and ImageNet natural images.
- Paper is well-written and judged to be novel and inspirational for the community.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reasons for accept: framework is well-motivated/theoretically sound and empirical evaluation is thorough.
- Supporting points: clear explanation of prior limitations, strong analysis of affine projection, and informative empirical insights into strengths/weaknesses.
- Suggested improvements: include more detailed comparisons with existing SR methods, especially GAN-based SR.
- Recommended additional experiments: report results on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) for fair comparison.
- Request more analysis of computational complexity and memory requirements relative to existing methods.
- Ask for more implementation details on the affine projection layer and its training.
- Specific author questions: details on training/usage of affine projection, complexity/memory comparisons, results on conventional SR testsets, and consideration of other generative models (e.g., VAEs).
