This paper argues to approach Super-Resolution as amortised MAP estimation. A projection step to keep consistent HR-LR dependencies is proposed and experimentally verified to obtain better results throughout. Further three different methods to solve the resulting cross-entropy problem in Eq.9 are proposed and tested.

Summary: Very good paper, very well written and presented. Experimental results are sufficient, the paper presents well chosen toy examples and real world applications. From my understanding the contributions for the field of super-resolutions are novel (3.2,3.3,3.4), parts that are specific for the training of GANs may have appeared in different variants elsewhere (see also discussion). I believe that this paper will be relevant to future work on super-resolution, the finding that GAN based model training yields most visually appealing results suggests further work in this domain.

Decision: Accept.

Reasons for the decision:
1. The paper proposes a novel approach to super-resolution, which is well-motivated and grounded in theory.
2. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, particularly the GAN-based approach, in producing visually appealing results.

Supporting arguments:
The paper provides a clear and concise introduction to the problem of super-resolution and the limitations of current methods. The proposed approach of amortised MAP estimation is well-motivated and theoretically grounded. The introduction of a projection step to keep consistent HR-LR dependencies is a novel contribution that is experimentally verified to obtain better results.

The three proposed methods to solve the resulting cross-entropy problem in Eq.9 are well-explained and tested. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, particularly the GAN-based approach, in producing visually appealing results.

Additional feedback:
The paper is well-written and presented, but there are a few minor typos that should be corrected in the final version.

Questions for the authors:
1. Can you provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed methods, particularly the GAN-based approach?
2. How do you plan to extend the proposed approach to more general image restoration tasks?
3. Can you provide more insights on why the GAN-based approach yields more visually appealing results compared to the other proposed methods?
