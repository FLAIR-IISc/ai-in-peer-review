- Novel amortized MAP inference framework with a pre-learned affine projection layer enforces LR-consistency and has a solid theoretical foundation.  
- Combining the framework with GANs produces plausible outputs, but the added value over existing GAN-based SR (e.g., SRGAN) is unclear and needs direct comparison.  
- The affine projection is claimed to remove the need for HR–LR pairs during training, yet training the affine layer itself requires such pairs, possibly shifting rather than eliminating the burden.  
- Provide quantitative results on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) for fair comparison with prior work.  
- Experiments are limited to 128×128 images, leaving open whether the method scales to larger or arbitrary-size inputs.  
- The GAN formulation appears to omit an explicit noise/latent vector, which could help model output diversity and should be considered.  
- Overall well-written, theoretically solid, novel, and potentially inspiring, but requires additional experiments and clarification of certain components.
