- Amortized MAP inference is novel for SR and, combined with GANs, produces plausible results, but its advantage over recent GAN-based SR (e.g., SRGAN) is unclear.  
- The affine projection enforces LR consistency and enables training without HR–LR pairs, but training the affine layer itself requires HR–LR pairs, raising concerns it merely shifts the supervised requirement.  
- The paper explores three methods for minimizing cross-entropy, showing thorough methodological investigation.  
- Results are shown on natural images (ImageNet), but conventional SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) are missing for fair comparison.  
- Experiments are limited to 128×128 images; it’s unclear how the framework handles larger or arbitrary-sized inputs.  
- The role of a GAN noise latent vector was not explored; using noise could better capture output distributions.  
- Solid theoretical analysis and promising empirical results motivate acceptance.  
- More direct comparisons with state-of-the-art SR methods are needed to quantify improvements.  
- Authors should provide more detail on how the affine projection layer is trained and its impact on overall performance.  
- Suggest exploring other generative approaches (e.g., VAEs) as alternative solutions for SR.  
- Overall: novel, well-analyzed framework with good potential, but requires additional experiments and clarifications.
