This paper proposes an approach to Super-Resolution as amortized MAP estimation. A novel projection step is introduced to maintain consistent high-resolution (HR) and low-resolution (LR) dependencies, which is experimentally verified to yield improved results throughout. Furthermore, the authors present three distinct methods to solve the resulting cross-entropy problem in Eq. 9 and evaluate their performance.

Summary: Overall, this is a well-written and well-presented paper. The experimental results are sufficient, and the authors provide well-chosen toy examples and real-world applications. In my opinion, the contributions to the field of super-resolution (Sections 3.2, 3.3, and 3.4) are novel, whereas the parts specific to the training of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) may have appeared in different variants elsewhere (also discussed later). I believe this paper will be relevant to future work on super-resolution, and the finding that GAN-based model training yields the most visually appealing results suggests further research in this domain.

The manuscript is well-organized, but a final proofread would be beneficial to correct the few typos that were noticed.
