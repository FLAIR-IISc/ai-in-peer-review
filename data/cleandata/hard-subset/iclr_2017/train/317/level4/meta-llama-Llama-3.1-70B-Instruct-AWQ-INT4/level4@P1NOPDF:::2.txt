This paper presents a novel approach to Super-Resolution by formulating it as amortized MAP estimation. A key contribution is the introduction of a projection step to maintain consistent dependencies between high-resolution (HR) and low-resolution (LR) images, which is experimentally validated to yield improved results. Furthermore, the authors propose and test three distinct methods for solving the cross-entropy problem in Eq. 9.

Summary: Overall, this is a very good paper that is well-written and effectively presented. The experimental results are sufficient, and the paper includes well-chosen toy examples and real-world applications. In my opinion, the contributions to the field of super-resolution (Sections 3.2, 3.3, and 3.4) are novel, although some aspects related to the training of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) may have been explored in similar forms elsewhere (see discussion below). I believe this paper will be relevant to future work on super-resolution, and the finding that GAN-based model training produces the most visually appealing results suggests further exploration in this domain is warranted.

Before finalizing the manuscript, I recommend a thorough proofread to address the few typos that were encountered, which may improve overall readability.
