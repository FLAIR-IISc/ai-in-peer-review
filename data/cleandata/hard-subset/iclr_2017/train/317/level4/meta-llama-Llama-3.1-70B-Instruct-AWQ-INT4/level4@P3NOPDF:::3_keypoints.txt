- Proposes an amortized MAP estimation method for super-resolution that learns a neural network projecting to an affine subspace of solutions consistent with the LR input.  
- Enables multiple optimization strategies (GANs, noise-assisted, density-assisted) to find SR solutions within that subspace.  
- Empirical results across several datasets demonstrate the method's effectiveness.  
- Paper is well-motivated and the approach is promising.  
- Writing is unclear in places; sections are hard to follow and require multiple readings.  
- Recommend revising organization, flow, and definitions to improve clarity and readability.  
- Request deeper analysis of learned networks—what features they capture and how those features drive performance.  
- Minor suggestions: provide specific architectures, hyperparameter settings, and more visualizations/illustrations (e.g., of the affine subspace and learned projections).  
- Conclusion: compelling approach but needs clearer exposition and additional analysis to increase impact.
