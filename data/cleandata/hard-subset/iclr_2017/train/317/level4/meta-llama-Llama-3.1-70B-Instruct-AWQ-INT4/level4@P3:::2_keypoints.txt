- Proposes amortised MAP inference for image super-resolution to avoid blurring from MSE-based empirical risk minimisation.  
- Introduces a CNN architecture with an affine projection layer to enforce HR–LR consistency.  
- Presents three approximations for the MAP cross-entropy: GANs, denoiser-guided SR, and density-guided SR.  
- Establishes a theoretical connection between GANs and amortised variational inference.  
- Experimental results show the GAN-based method produces the most visually appealing outputs.  
- Provides a thorough analysis of the limitations of MAP inference.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and concise with suitable toy examples and real-world experiments.  
- Minor proofreading needed to fix a few typos.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Key reason: novel, well-motivated approach addressing limitations of traditional methods.  
- Key reason: proposed methods, especially GAN-based, achieve state-of-the-art visual quality.  
- Suggestion: give more context/explanation of the GAN–variational inference connection for non-expert readers.  
- Suggestion: offer guidance on addressing MAP inference limitations in future work.  
- Suggestion: strengthen experiments with more comparisons to SOTA methods and more diverse datasets.  
- Question: provide implementation details of the affine projection layer and its integration with the CNN.  
- Question: how will you address MAP’s mode dependence on representation?  
- Question: potential applications beyond super-resolution (other inverse problems or vision tasks)?
