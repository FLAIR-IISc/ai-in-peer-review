- Proposes amortized MAP inference for SR with a pre-learned affine projection layer and three methods for minimizing cross-entropy; well-written with solid theory.  
- When paired with GANs the method produces plausible results, but its advantage over recent GAN-based SOTA (e.g., SRGAN) is unclear.  
- The affine projection removes the need for HR–LR pairs for the main model, yet the affine layer itself still requires pairs, so supervised training may just be shifted.  
- Results should include standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) to allow fair comparison with prior work.  
- Experiments are limited to 128×128 images, leaving scalability and performance on larger/arbitrary image sizes uncertain.  
- Unclear whether a noise latent vector was used or tested (important for GANs to learn a distribution).  
- Overall: novel and potentially impactful work with solid analysis, but questions remain about necessity, generality, and additional required experiments.
