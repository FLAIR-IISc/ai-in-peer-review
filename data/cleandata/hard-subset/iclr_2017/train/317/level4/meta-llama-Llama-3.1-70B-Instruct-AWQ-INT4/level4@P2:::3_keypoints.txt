- Proposes an amortized MAP estimation method for SR that learns a network projecting to an affine subspace of solutions consistent with the LR input, enabling GAN-, noise-, and density-assisted optimization.  
- Motivates the approach as an alternative to pixel-wise MSE training, which causes blurry, over-smoothed SR outputs.  
- Situates the method well in literature, building on GANs, denoising, and density estimation and providing theoretical analysis (affine projections, GANs, density models).  
- Experimental results are strong across 2D toy data, grass textures, CelebA, and ImageNet, showing perceptually superior, sharper SR and better texture/detail reconstruction.  
- Writing and presentation are at times dense and hard to follow; needs clearer structure, shorter paragraphs, and more intuitive explanations.  
- Lacks deeper analysis of the learned networks—what features they capture and how they compare qualitatively/quantitatively to other SR methods.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept with revisions.  
- Primary reasons for decision: well-motivated method and impressive experimental validation.  
- Revision request 1: improve clarity and accessibility of the writing and presentation.  
- Revision request 2: provide more analysis of the learned networks and feature representations.  
- Question 1 for authors: clarify implementation details of the affine projection layer in practice.  
- Question 2 for authors: compare computational complexity and training time versus other SR methods.  
- Question 3 for authors: provide more insight into the features learned by the networks and their relation to SR performance.
