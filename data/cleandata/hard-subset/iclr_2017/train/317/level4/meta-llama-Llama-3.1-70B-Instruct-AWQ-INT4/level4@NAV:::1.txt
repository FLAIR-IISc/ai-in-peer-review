This paper introduces a novel framework for solving the Super-Resolution (SR) problem through amortized MAP inference, incorporating a pre-learned affine projection layer to maintain consistency with Low-Resolution (LR) outputs. Furthermore, it proposes three distinct methods for minimizing cross-entropy. Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field.

However, I have several comments to further enhance the paper:

1) The proposed amortized MAP inference framework is innovative and distinct from previous SR methods. When combined with Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), this approach yields plausible and promising results. A comparison with another GAN-based SR method, Photo-Realistic Single Image Super-Resolution Using a Generative Adversarial Network, raises the question of what new insights this formulation brings to the current state-of-the-art.

2) By utilizing an affine projection architecture as a constraint, the model eliminates the need for corresponding High-Resolution (HR) and Low-Resolution (LR) image pairs during training. However, the training of the affine projection layer still requires these pairs. Does this imply that the training procedure is merely being transferred to the affine projection layer?

3) The paper showcases numerous results from the proposed framework, including those from natural images in ImageNet. To facilitate a fair comparison with previous work, it would be beneficial to include results from conventional SR test datasets, such as Set5, Set14, or BSD100.

4) The paper primarily presents results from natural images with a limited size of 128*128. Can the proposed framework effectively handle images of larger sizes, as SR often encounters inputs of arbitrary sizes?

5) Typically, a standard GAN includes a noise term as a latent space, enabling it to learn a distribution more effectively. Did the authors explore the use of a noise vector in their framework?

In conclusion, this paper presents a novel SR framework with robust theoretical analysis. Although some questions remain regarding its necessity and the need for additional experiments, this work has the potential to inspire the community with its innovative ideas and thorough exploration of various methods.
