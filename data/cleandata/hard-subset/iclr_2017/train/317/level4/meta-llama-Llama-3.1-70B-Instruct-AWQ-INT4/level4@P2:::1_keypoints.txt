- Proposes an amortized MAP inference framework for image super-resolution using a CNN that projects outputs to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions to ensure LR–HR consistency.
- Introduces three methods to minimize cross-entropy: GAN-based, denoiser-guided SR, and a maximum-likelihood-trained image prior baseline; GAN performs best perceptually.
- Novelty: amortized MAP inference and affine projection constraint allow training without paired HR–LR image data.
- Provides solid theoretical analysis and derivation of the optimization problem and proposed methods.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and shows the methods (especially GAN) produce plausible, perceptually superior SR results, with comparisons to other GAN-based SR.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty, theory, and experimental quality.
- Requests more details on training the affine projection layer, hyperparameters, and computational cost.
- Suggests adding results on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) for fair comparison.
- Recommends investigating scalability to larger or arbitrary image sizes.
- Suggests adding a noise latent input to the GAN-based method to better represent distributional learning.
- Asks how the framework handles highly degraded or noisy low-resolution inputs.
- Requests more insight into the choice of the affine projection architecture and its impact on optimization.
- Asks for comparisons of computational cost and efficiency versus other SR methods.
