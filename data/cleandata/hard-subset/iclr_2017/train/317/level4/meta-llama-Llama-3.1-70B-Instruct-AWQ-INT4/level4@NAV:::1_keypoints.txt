- Novel amortized MAP framework with an affine projection layer is innovative and yields promising GAN-based SR results, but it’s unclear what new insights it provides beyond prior GAN-SR work (e.g., SRGAN).

- The affine projection is claimed to remove the need for HR–LR pairs during training, yet the affine layer itself is trained with such pairs, raising the concern that training is merely shifted rather than eliminated.

- The paper proposes three methods for minimizing cross-entropy and provides solid theoretical analysis, representing a substantive contribution.

- Evaluation is limited to ImageNet natural images; the paper should report results on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) for fair comparison.

- Experiments use small images (128×128); it’s unclear whether the framework scales to larger or arbitrary-sized inputs common in SR tasks.

- Methodological question: did the authors include or explore a GAN latent noise vector to better model output distributions?

- Overall assessment: promising and thorough work that could inspire the community, but needs clarifications and additional experiments to validate its necessity and empirical advantages.
