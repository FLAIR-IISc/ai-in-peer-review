The paper presents an amortized MAP estimation method for super-resolution (SR) problems, which is a significant contribution to the field. By learning a neural network that projects onto an affine subspace of SR solutions consistent with the low-resolution (LR) method, the approach enables the discovery of proper solutions using various methods, including Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), noise-assisted, and density-assisted optimization.

The results are nicely demonstrated on several datasets, showcasing the effectiveness of the proposed method. Overall, I find the paper well-motivated and the ideas presented to be valuable.

However, I feel that the writing could be polished further to improve clarity and readability. At times, I found it challenging to follow the narrative, and given the complexity of the subject matter, clearer presentation would be beneficial. To enhance the paper's impact, I would suggest reorganizing certain sections and providing more intuitive explanations for the key concepts.

Additionally, I would love to see more in-depth analysis of the resulting networks. Specifically, it would be interesting to know what kind of features they learn and how these features contribute to the overall performance of the method. Providing such insights would not only strengthen the paper but also offer a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed approach.

Minor suggestions for improvement include providing more detailed descriptions of the datasets used and the experimental setup. Furthermore, adding more visualizations or illustrations to support the text would aid in comprehension and make the paper more engaging.

In summary, while the paper presents a compelling method for SR problems, refining the writing and adding more analysis would significantly enhance its overall quality and impact.
