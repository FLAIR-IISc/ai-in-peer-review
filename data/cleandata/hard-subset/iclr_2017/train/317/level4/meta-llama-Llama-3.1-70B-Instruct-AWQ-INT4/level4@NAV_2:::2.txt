This paper proposes an approach to Super-Resolution as amortised MAP estimation. The authors introduce a projection step to maintain consistent HR-LR dependencies and experimentally verify its effectiveness in obtaining better results. Furthermore, they present three different methods to solve the resulting cross-entropy problem and test their performance.

The paper is well-written and presented, with a clear structure and concise language. The experimental results are sufficient and well-chosen, including both toy examples and real-world applications. The contributions to the field of super-resolution are novel, particularly in sections 3.2, 3.3, and 3.4. While some aspects of GAN training may have been explored elsewhere, the authors' approach to super-resolution is distinct and relevant to future work in this area.

The paper's findings, which suggest that GAN-based model training yields the most visually appealing results, are particularly noteworthy and warrant further investigation. Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of super-resolution.

Decision: Accept

Reasons for decision:
1. The paper proposes a novel approach to super-resolution as amortised MAP estimation, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. The authors provide sufficient experimental results, including both toy examples and real-world applications, to support their claims.
3. The paper is well-written and presented, making it easy to follow and understand.

Supporting arguments:
1. The authors' use of a projection step to maintain consistent HR-LR dependencies is a key innovation in their approach.
2. The three methods proposed to solve the resulting cross-entropy problem are well-motivated and effectively tested.
3. The paper's findings on the effectiveness of GAN-based model training are particularly noteworthy and warrant further investigation.

Additional feedback:
1. The authors may want to consider providing more context on the current state of super-resolution research and how their approach fits into this broader landscape.
2. Some of the mathematical notation and equations could be clarified or expanded upon for readers who may not be familiar with the specific techniques used.
3. The authors may want to discuss the potential limitations and challenges of their approach, particularly in terms of scalability and computational resources.

Questions for the authors:
1. Can you provide more information on how you selected the specific methods for solving the cross-entropy problem, and how you evaluated their performance?
2. How do you envision your approach being used in practice, particularly in terms of integrating it with existing super-resolution pipelines?
3. Are there any plans to extend this work to other areas of image processing or computer vision?
