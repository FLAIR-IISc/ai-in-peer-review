- Proposes a novel amortized MAP inference framework for single-image super-resolution (SR) using a pre-learned affine projection layer.  
- Introduces three different methods to address minimization of cross-entropy in the framework.  
- Combining the amortized MAP approach with GANs yields plausible and visually good results.  
- Novelty is claimed, but the incremental contribution over recent GAN-based SR methods (e.g., SRGAN) is unclear.  
- Affine projection is presented as a constraint that purportedly removes the need for paired HR–LR training data.  
- Unclear whether affine projection merely shifts the pairing requirement to training the affine projection layer (which may still need HR–LR pairs).  
- Experimental evaluation focuses on ImageNet natural images but lacks results on standard SR test sets (Set5, Set14, BSD100) for fair comparison.  
- Presented image results are limited to 128×128, leaving scalability and performance on larger, arbitrary-size inputs uncertain.  
- The framework does not discuss or report using a noise latent vector typical in GANs to model output variability.  
- Paper provides solid theoretical analysis and explores multiple methods, offering potential inspiration to the community.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested clarifications and experiments: clarify advantages over other GAN-based SR, report results on standard SR benchmarks, test larger images, and consider/experiment with a noise vector.
