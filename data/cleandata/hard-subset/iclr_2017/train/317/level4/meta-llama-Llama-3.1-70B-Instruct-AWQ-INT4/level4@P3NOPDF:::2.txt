This paper proposes a novel approach to Super-Resolution by formulating it as amortized MAP estimation. A key contribution is the introduction of a projection step to maintain consistent dependencies between high-resolution (HR) and low-resolution (LR) images, which is experimentally verified to yield improved results. Furthermore, the authors propose and evaluate three different methods to solve the resulting cross-entropy problem in Eq. 9.

Summary: This is a well-written and well-presented paper, with sufficient experimental results that effectively demonstrate the proposed approach through carefully chosen toy examples and real-world applications. In my opinion, the contributions to the field of super-resolution, specifically sections 3.2, 3.3, and 3.4, are novel and significant. While some aspects related to Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) training may have been explored in different variants elsewhere (as discussed below), I believe this paper will be relevant to future work on super-resolution. The finding that GAN-based model training produces the most visually appealing results suggests a promising direction for further research in this domain.

The manuscript is generally well-organized and clear. However, I recommend a final proofread to correct a few minor typos that were noticed during the review process.
