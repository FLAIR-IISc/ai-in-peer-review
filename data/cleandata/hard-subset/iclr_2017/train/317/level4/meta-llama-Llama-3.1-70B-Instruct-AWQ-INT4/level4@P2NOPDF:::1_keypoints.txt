1. Introduces a novel SR framework using amortized MAP inference and a pre-learned affine projection layer, plus three methods for minimizing cross-entropy; paper is well-written and contributive.

2. Concern about added value over current GAN-based SR methods (e.g., SRGAN)—unclear what the new formulation contributes compared to SOTA.

3. Affine projection allows training without paired HR/LR data, but training the affine projection layer still requires pairs—unclear if the pairing requirement is merely shifted.

4. Requests inclusion of results on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) for fair comparison with prior work.

5. Experiments are limited to 128×128 images; needs demonstration on varying and larger image sizes since SR should handle arbitrary sizes.

6. Asks whether a noise latent vector was used or tested to let the model learn a distribution, as in typical GANs.

7. Overall positive: solid theoretical analysis and multiple methods explored, but some component necessity and experimental validation remain unresolved.
