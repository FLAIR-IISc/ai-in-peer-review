- Novel amortized MAP inference with a pre-trained affine projection is innovative and, when combined with GANs, yields plausible high-quality SR results.
- Clarify the specific contributions and advantages of this formulation compared to prior GAN-based SR methods (e.g., Photo-Realistic SR using GANs).
- Affine projection architecture removes the need for {HR,LR} pairs for the main model, but the affine projection layer itself requires such pairs—does this simply shift the training-data requirement?
- Include evaluations on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) to enable fair comparison with prior work.
- Results are limited to 128×128 images; clarify whether the framework scales effectively to larger or arbitrary image sizes.
- Did the authors experiment with adding a noise vector to the latent space, as is common in GANs, and what were the outcomes?
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution and creative methods, but additional experiments and clarifications are needed to fully establish necessity and state-of-the-art improvements.
