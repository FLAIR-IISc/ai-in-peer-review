This work reframes Super-Resolution as an amortized Maximum A Posteriori (MAP) estimation problem. A novel projection step is introduced to enforce consistency between High-Resolution (HR) and Low-Resolution (LR) dependencies, and its effectiveness is demonstrated through experimentation, leading to improved performance. The paper further proposes and evaluates three distinct approaches for addressing the resulting cross-entropy problem detailed in Eq. 9.

Summary: This is a strong paper, exceptionally well-written and clearly presented. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, featuring insightful toy examples and relevant real-world applications. The contributions to the field of super-resolution, specifically sections 3.2, 3.3, and 3.4, appear novel. While techniques related to GAN training may have been explored in various forms previously (as discussed in the paper), the overall approach is compelling. I anticipate this work will be influential in future super-resolution research, and the observation that GAN-based training produces the most visually pleasing results warrants further investigation.
