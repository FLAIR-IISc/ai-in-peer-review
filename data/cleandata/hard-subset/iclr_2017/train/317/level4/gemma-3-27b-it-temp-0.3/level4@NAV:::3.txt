This paper introduces an amortized Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) estimation approach for Super-Resolution (SR) problems. The core idea – training a neural network to project SR solutions onto an affine subspace constrained by a Low-Resolution (LR) reconstruction – is compelling and allows for flexible optimization strategies, including the use of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), noise-assisted, and density-assisted methods. 

The experimental results are well-demonstrated across multiple datasets.

Overall, I find this to be a strong contribution. However, the manuscript would benefit significantly from improved clarity and polish in its writing and presentation. The complexity of the topic necessitates a more accessible explanation, as I found portions difficult to follow. Furthermore, additional analysis of the learned network representations – specifically, investigating the features the network learns to identify – would be a valuable addition to the work.
