Sincere apologies for the late review.

This paper frames the problem of single-image super-resolution as amortized MAP estimation. A key contribution is a projection step designed to maintain consistency between the high-resolution output and the low-resolution input, which is experimentally verified to improve results. Furthermore, the authors propose and evaluate three different methods for solving the resulting cross-entropy objective function (Eq. 9).

**Summary and Assessment:**

This is a high-quality paper that is very well-written and clearly presented. The experimental evaluation is thorough, featuring well-chosen toy examples that build intuition as well as compelling results on real-world applications.

The core contributions to the field of super-resolution (Sections 3.2, 3.3, 3.4) appear to be novel. While some elements specific to the training of GANs may have appeared in different variants elsewhere (as acknowledged in the discussion), their application and integration within this framework are original. I believe this paper will be highly relevant to future work on super-resolution. In particular, the finding that GAN-based model training yields the most visually appealing results highlights a promising direction for further research in this domain.

The manuscript would benefit from a final proofreading pass to correct a few minor typos.
