### Summary

This paper presents a novel approach to single-image super-resolution by framing the problem as amortized MAP estimation. The core technical contribution is a projection step designed to enforce consistency between the generated high-resolution (HR) output and the original low-resolution (LR) input. This ensures that downsampling the super-resolved image recovers the initial LR image. The authors propose and experimentally validate this projection method, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving results. Furthermore, the paper investigates three distinct methods for solving the resulting optimization problem, which is formulated as a cross-entropy minimization (Equation 9).

Overall, this is a very strong paper that is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented. The experimental evaluation is thorough, leveraging well-chosen toy examples to build intuition alongside comprehensive results on real-world applications. I believe the paper makes a significant and novel contribution to the field of super-resolution and will be highly relevant to future work.

### Strengths

1.  **Novel and Sound Formulation:** The conceptualization of super-resolution as amortized MAP estimation provides a solid theoretical foundation for the work. The proposed projection step (Section 3.2) is an elegant and practical solution for maintaining HR-LR consistency, a crucial aspect often overlooked in generative approaches. The exploration of different solutions to the cross-entropy objective (Sections 3.3, 3.4) is also a valuable contribution.

2.  **Compelling Experimental Results:** The empirical validation is comprehensive and convincing. The authors demonstrate the superiority of their method through both quantitative metrics and qualitative examples. A particularly significant finding is that training the model within a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) framework yields the most visually appealing and perceptually realistic results. This observation strongly suggests that GAN-based training is a promising direction for future super-resolution research.

3.  **Clarity and Presentation:** The paper is written with exceptional clarity. The motivation is well-established, the methods are described in sufficient detail, and the results are presented effectively. The inclusion of both simple, illustrative examples and complex, real-world results makes the paper accessible and its contributions easy to appreciate.

### Minor Points for Improvement

1.  **Novelty of GAN Training:** While the application of the proposed framework to super-resolution is novel, the specific techniques for training GANs may have parallels in other domains. The authors do a good job of contextualizing their work, but a slightly more detailed discussion on how their specific GAN formulation compares to concurrent or prior work in other computer vision tasks could further clarify the precise novelty of their training scheme.

2.  **Proofreading:** The manuscript is of high quality, but it would benefit from one final proofread to correct a few minor typographical errors.

### Recommendation

I strongly recommend accepting this paper. It addresses an important problem with a well-motivated and technically sound approach. The findings are significant, particularly the demonstration that GAN-based objectives can produce state-of-the-art results that are visually superior to those optimized for traditional metrics like PSNR. The paper is poised to have a considerable impact on the field and will undoubtedly inspire future research in generative models for super-resolution and other image restoration tasks.
