- Reformulates single-image super-resolution as amortised MAP inference to avoid MSE-induced blurriness.
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces perfect consistency with the low-resolution input.
- Affine projection simplifies the MAP objective to a cross-entropy minimisation between generated and true image distributions.
- Proposes a new CNN architecture incorporating the affine projection as the core technical contribution.
- Develops and evaluates three solution methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (explicit density-guided).
- AffGAN yields the most visually appealing and sharp results in experiments.
- Toy 2D Swiss-roll experiment validates that GAN and denoiser-guided methods optimize the intended objective where ground-truth MAP is computable.
- Qualitative results on grass textures, faces, and ImageNet support AffGAN’s perceptual superiority over MSE-based methods.
- Affine projection also improves performance and training speed for standard MSE-trained models, showing standalone value.
- Theoretical derivation (Eq. 9) is elegant and the appendix links a stochastic AffGAN to amortised variational inference.
- Practical contribution: use of "instance noise" helps stabilize GAN training.
- Recommendation to accept based on principled framing and thorough experimental validation.
- Minor issues: paper needs final proofreading to fix typos and grammar.
- Suggest expanding discussion on why AffDG and AffLL underperformed on real images (fundamental limitation vs difficulty learning denoisers/density models).
- Suggest reporting sensitivity of results to the instance-noise annealing schedule for reproducibility and practitioner guidance.
- Asks authors to discuss practical trade-offs of moving from deterministic AffGAN to a stochastic, sampling-based variant (training stability, compute cost, and sample quality).
