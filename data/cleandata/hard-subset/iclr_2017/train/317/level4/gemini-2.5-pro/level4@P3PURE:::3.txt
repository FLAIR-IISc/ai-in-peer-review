This paper presents an amortized Maximum A Posteriori (MAP) estimation method for super-resolution (SR) problems. The core contribution is a neural network trained to project an input onto the affine subspace of all high-resolution solutions that are consistent with the given low-resolution image. This framework enables the subsequent use of various optimization methods—such as Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), noise-assisted optimization, and density-assisted optimization—to find a final, high-quality solution within this constrained space. The effectiveness of the proposed approach is demonstrated on several datasets.

Overall, this is a strong paper with an interesting contribution. However, its primary weakness lies in its presentation. The writing could be significantly polished, and the overall exposition made clearer. I found the paper difficult to follow in places; given the complexity of the subject matter, improving the clarity would greatly strengthen the submission.

Additionally, the paper would benefit from an analysis of the resulting networks. For instance, what kinds of features do these projection networks learn? Providing such insights would add significant value.
