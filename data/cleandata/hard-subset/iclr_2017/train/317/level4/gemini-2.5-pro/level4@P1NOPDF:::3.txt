This paper presents an amortized MAP estimation method for super-resolution (SR) problems. The core of this approach is a neural network trained to project solutions onto an affine subspace of high-resolution images that are consistent with a given low-resolution input. This framework enables the generation of high-quality solutions through various techniques, including GAN-based, noise-assisted, and density-assisted optimization. The effectiveness of the method is well-demonstrated on several datasets.

Overall, I believe this is a strong paper with a valuable contribution. However, its primary weakness is the quality of the writing and presentation. The manuscript would benefit significantly from polishing to improve clarity and flow. I found the paper difficult to follow at times, and given the complexity of the subject matter, a clearer explanation would greatly enhance its impact.

Furthermore, I would suggest including a more in-depth analysis of the resulting networks. For instance, what kinds of features does the learned projection capture? Such an analysis would provide valuable insights into the model's behavior and could further strengthen the paper's contributions.
