This work frames the task of Super-Resolution as a problem of amortized MAP estimation. The authors introduce a projection step to ensure consistent dependencies between high-resolution and low-resolution images, which is experimentally demonstrated to yield improved results. Additionally, the paper puts forward and evaluates three distinct methods for solving the cross-entropy problem presented in Equation 9.

Summary: This is an excellent manuscript, notable for its clarity and strong presentation. The experimental validation is thorough, featuring a well-chosen mix of illustrative toy examples and practical, real-world applications. The contributions to the super-resolution field (specifically sections 3.2, 3.3, and 3.4) appear to be novel, although components related to GAN training may share similarities with prior work, a point noted in the discussion. I anticipate this work will be influential for subsequent studies on super-resolution. In particular, its conclusion that GAN-based training produces the most visually compelling results highlights a promising avenue for further investigation.
