- Decision: Accept.

- Proposes a novel, principled single-image SR framework using amortized MAP inference to avoid MSE-induced blurriness.

- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces exact downsampling consistency (Af(x)=x), turning a constrained problem into an unconstrained generative one.

- Reformulates the objective as matching the network output distribution to the true HR image distribution (minimizing cross-entropy).

- Presents three solution variants: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (explicit density baseline); AffGAN performs best.

- Theoretical justification explains why generative approaches (e.g., GANs) are appropriate for SR under this formulation.

- Empirical results: a 2D Swiss-roll toy and real-image experiments (textures, faces) show AffGAN yields sharper, more plausible details than MSE-trained models.

- Proposes instance noise to stabilize GAN training and connects the method to amortized variational inference in the appendix.

- Suggests a clearer comparison with concurrent GAN-based SR work (e.g., Ledig et al. 2016) to highlight advantages of the hard affine constraint vs adversarial+soft consistency losses.

- Requests clarification on data requirements for pre-training the affine projection pseudo-inverse A+: whether paired HR–LR data are needed or synthetic data suffices given known A.

- Recommends reporting results on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) and conventional metrics (PSNR, SSIM) for broader comparability.

- Asks about scalability to larger, arbitrary-sized images beyond the 128×128 patches tested and any associated computational or architectural issues.

- Queries whether a latent noise vector z was used in the generator, and if so how it affected diversity and quality of outputs for a single LR input.
