1) Novel amortized MAP inference for SR that, when combined with a GAN, produces plausible high-quality results but its advantage over existing GAN-based SR (e.g., SRGAN) is unclear.  
2) Claim of not requiring paired {HR,LR} training is questionable because the affine projection layer must be pre-trained on paired data, effectively shifting the paired-data requirement.  
3) Evaluation limited to ImageNet; should report results on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) for fair comparison.  
4) Experiments use 128×128 images only; need analysis of performance and scalability to larger, arbitrary-sized images.  
5) Unclear whether a noise vector was used in the generator and how it affects output diversity and quality; report any experiments and effects.  
6) Overall positive: solid theoretical contributions and innovative core idea, but requires clearer SOTA advantages and more extensive experimental validation.
