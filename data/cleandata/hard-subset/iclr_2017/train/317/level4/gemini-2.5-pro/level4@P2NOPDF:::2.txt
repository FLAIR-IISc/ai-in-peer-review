### Review

**Summary**

This paper formulates the problem of single-image super-resolution as amortized MAP estimation. The core technical contribution is a projection step that enforces consistency between the high-resolution (HR) output and the original low-resolution (LR) input. The authors demonstrate experimentally that this step improves results. Furthermore, the paper proposes and evaluates three different methods for solving the resulting cross-entropy optimization problem outlined in Eq. 9.

This is a high-quality paper that is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented. The experimental validation is thorough, featuring well-chosen toy examples that build intuition before demonstrating strong performance on real-world applications. The primary contributions to the field of super-resolution (Sections 3.2, 3.3, 3.4) appear to be novel. While some components related to GAN training may have precedents in other domains (as noted in the paper's discussion), their application and synthesis within this super-resolution framework are novel and impactful. This work is likely to be influential for future research in super-resolution. In particular, the finding that GAN-based training yields more visually pleasing results is an important observation that will likely stimulate further investigation in this area.

**Strengths**

*   **Clarity and Presentation:** The paper is very well-written and easy to follow. The inclusion of simple toy examples is highly effective for illustrating the core concepts before tackling more complex, real-world scenarios.
*   **Novel Contributions:** The proposed framework, particularly the projection step for maintaining HR-LR consistency and the comparative analysis of different optimization strategies, represents a significant contribution to the super-resolution literature.
*   **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The experimental results are convincing and sufficiently support the paper's claims. The qualitative and quantitative analyses are well-executed.
*   **Potential for Impact:** The conclusion that GAN-based models produce more visually appealing results, even when not achieving the highest scores on traditional metrics like PSNR, is a key insight that addresses a known limitation in the field and encourages a promising direction for future work.

**Weaknesses**

The paper is of high quality and has no major weaknesses.

**Minor Comments**

*   The manuscript is generally well-polished, though a final proofread is recommended to correct a few minor typographical errors.
