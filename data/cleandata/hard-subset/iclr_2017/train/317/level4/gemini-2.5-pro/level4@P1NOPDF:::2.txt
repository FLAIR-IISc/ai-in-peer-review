This paper frames the problem of single-image super-resolution as amortized MAP estimation. To this end, the authors propose a projection step to enforce consistency between the high-resolution (HR) and low-resolution (LR) images, which is experimentally verified to yield improved results. Furthermore, the paper proposes and evaluates three different methods for solving the cross-entropy minimization problem presented in Equation 9.

**Summary**

Overall, this is an excellent paper that is well-written and clearly presented. The experimental evaluation is thorough, featuring a good selection of both toy examples and real-world applications.

The core contributions to the field of super-resolution (Sections 3.2, 3.3, and 3.4) appear to be novel. While some elements related to GAN training may have appeared in other forms elsewhere, the authors appropriately acknowledge this in their discussion.

This paper will likely be highly relevant to future work on super-resolution. In particular, the finding that GAN-based model training yields the most visually appealing results points to a promising direction for further research in this domain.

The manuscript could benefit from one final proofread to correct a few minor typos.
