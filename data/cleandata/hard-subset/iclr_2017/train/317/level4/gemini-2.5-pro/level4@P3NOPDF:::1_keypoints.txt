- Novel amortized MAP inference formulation for single-image super-resolution that reframes SR in a principled probabilistic way.  
- Pre-learned affine projection layer enforces data consistency by ensuring downsampling the HR output reconstructs the LR input.  
- Exploration of three methods for minimizing the cross-entropy objective and solid theoretical derivation underpin the approach.  
- Combining the framework with a GAN produces visually plausible, high-quality results with realistic textures and fine details.  
- Insufficient direct comparison to recent GAN-based SR methods (e.g., Ledig et al., 2017) and unclear practical/theoretical advantages over them.  
- Ambiguity about training data requirements: the affine projection layer needs pre-training with {HR, LR} pairs, so the method may not be fully unsupervised.  
- Lack of benchmarking on standard SR datasets (Set5, Set14, BSD100) to allow quantitative comparison using PSNR/SSIM.  
- Experiments only on 128x128 images—scalability and performance on larger, real-world image sizes are not demonstrated.  
- Unclear role of stochasticity: the generator appears deterministic and it’s not reported whether adding input noise affects diversity or quality.  
- Overall recommendation: novel, well-motivated, and strong work with a recommendation for acceptance despite the noted experimental and clarification gaps.
