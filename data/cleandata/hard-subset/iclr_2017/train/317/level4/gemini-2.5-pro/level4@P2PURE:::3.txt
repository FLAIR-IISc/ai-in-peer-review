This paper presents an amortized MAP estimation method for super-resolution (SR) problems. The approach trains a neural network to project an input onto an affine subspace of high-resolution solutions that are consistent with the corresponding low-resolution image. This projection enables the discovery of high-quality solutions using various techniques, including GANs, noise-assisted methods, and density-assisted optimization. The results are effectively demonstrated on several datasets.

Overall, this is a promising paper. However, its primary weakness lies in its presentation. The writing could be significantly polished to improve clarity. Given the complexity of the subject matter, the paper was difficult to follow at times, and a clearer exposition would greatly enhance its impact.

Additionally, the paper would be strengthened by an analysis of the learned networks. For instance, providing insight into what kinds of features the networks learn to represent the solution subspace would be a valuable addition.
