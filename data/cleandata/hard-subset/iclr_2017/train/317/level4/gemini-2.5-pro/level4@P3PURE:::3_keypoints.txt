- Proposes an amortized MAP estimation method for super-resolution that trains a neural network to project inputs onto the affine subspace of high-resolution solutions consistent with a low-resolution image.
- Framework allows subsequent use of various optimization techniques (GANs, noise-assisted, density-assisted) within the constrained solution space to obtain final high-quality outputs.
- Empirical results demonstrate the approach’s effectiveness across several datasets.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with an interesting and valuable contribution.
- Main weakness: presentation and exposition are unclear and need significant polishing for readability.
- Recommendation: include analysis of the learned projection networks (e.g., what features they capture) to provide additional insight and value.
