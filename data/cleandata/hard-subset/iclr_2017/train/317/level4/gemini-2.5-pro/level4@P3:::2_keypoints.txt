- Frames single-image SR as amortised MAP inference to avoid MSE-induced blurring from averaging multiple solutions.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces downsampled HR output equals LR input, turning a constrained problem into an unconstrained one.  
- Casts the amortised MAP task as cross-entropy minimisation between generated-image and true-HR distributions.  
- Proposes and evaluates three solution methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (explicit density-guided baseline).  
- Empirically shows AffGAN yields the sharpest, most perceptually convincing SR results versus MSE and the other proposed methods.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Novel, principled formulation moving beyond MSE with an elegant affine projection that simplifies the learning objective.  
- Key reason 2: Comprehensive, convincing empirical validation on toy and real-image experiments demonstrating perceptual improvements.  
- Motivation: Swiss-roll toy effectively illustrates why MSE averages produce implausible, blurry solutions and motivates MAP.  
- Technical soundness: Clear derivation from MAP to cross-entropy and a principled use of affine projection to handle the likelihood term.  
- Experimental rigor: Swiss-roll quantitative results, proof-of-concept MSE experiment showing no harm (possibly speedup) from affine projection, and strong qualitative results on textures, faces, and ImageNet.  
- Transparency: Paper honestly reports failure modes of AffDG and AffLL, strengthening empirical claims.  
- Suggestion: Summarize reasons for AffDG/AffLL failures in the main paper (instability, inaccurate densities) rather than relegating to appendix.  
- Suggestion: Emphasise the instance-noise GAN stabilization technique more in the main text and contrast with other stabilization methods.  
- Clarify whether the pseudo-inverse A+ is a one-time precomputation for a given downsampling kernel or adds significant runtime overhead.  
- Minor: final proofreading recommended to fix a few typos.  
- Question: How does AffGAN training time compare to a similarly sized CNN trained with standard MSE loss?  
- Question: How robust is the affine-projection approach when the downsampling operator A is unknown or only approximately known?  
- Question: Any preliminary experiments with the stochastic AffGAN variant (amortised variational connection) to generate diverse plausible reconstructions?
