Sincere apologies for the late review.

This paper frames the problem of Super-Resolution as amortized MAP estimation. The authors propose a projection step to maintain consistency between high-resolution (HR) and low-resolution (LR) image pairs, and they experimentally demonstrate that this step consistently improves results. Furthermore, the paper introduces and evaluates three distinct methods for solving the resulting cross-entropy optimization problem (Eq. 9).

Summary:
This is an excellent, well-written, and clearly presented paper. The experimental evaluation is thorough, featuring well-chosen toy examples alongside real-world applications. The primary contributions to the field of super-resolution (Sections 3.2, 3.3, and 3.4) appear to be novel. While some aspects of the GAN training methodology may overlap with existing work, this is appropriately discussed by the authors. I believe this paper will be influential for future research in super-resolution. In particular, the finding that GAN-based model training yields the most visually appealing results is significant and will likely encourage further work in this domain.

The manuscript would benefit from a final proofreading to correct a few minor typos.
