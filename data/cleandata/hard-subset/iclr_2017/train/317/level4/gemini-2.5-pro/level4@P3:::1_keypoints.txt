- Recasts single-image SR as amortised MAP inference to produce perceptually plausible HR images instead of MSE-blurry outputs.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces the hard constraint that the downsampled output equals the LR input.  
- Reduces MAP inference to minimizing cross-entropy between generated and true HR distributions, enabling training without paired LR–HR data.  
- Proposes three solution methods: AffGAN (GAN-based, KL-minimizing), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-model-guided).  
- Experiments on toy and real images show AffGAN yields the sharpest and most visually compelling results.  
- Provides a proof that their GAN objective minimizes KL-divergence and proposes "instance noise" to stabilize GAN training.  
- Establishes a connection between a stochastic version of AffGAN and amortised variational inference.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: The principled amortised MAP formulation gives strong theoretical justification for perceptual SR and use of GANs.  
- Key reason: The affine projection layer is an elegant architectural constraint that enforces consistency, accelerates training, and improves solutions.  
- Swiss-roll toy experiments demonstrate MAP methods (AffGAN/AffDG) find solutions closer to true MAP than MSE/MAE baselines.  
- Affine-projected networks outperform standard CNNs even on MSE tasks, showing the layer’s broader utility.  
- The three-method exploration is thorough; denoiser- and density-guided approaches underperform on complex images but yield useful insights.  
- Contributions beyond SR include GAN-KL analysis, the instance-noise trick, and the amortised VI connection.  
- Request: Provide explicit comparison to concurrent SRGAN work to clarify theoretical and architectural advantages.  
- Request: Report results on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) and PSNR/SSIM for comparability.  
- Request: Discuss scalability to larger image resolutions and potential computational/memory constraints.  
- Request: Report experiments with a stochastic generator to evaluate diversity and address the "atypical mode" issue.  
- Request: Clarify that learning the pseudo-inverse A+ depends only on the known downsampling operator A and requires no paired LR/HR data.
