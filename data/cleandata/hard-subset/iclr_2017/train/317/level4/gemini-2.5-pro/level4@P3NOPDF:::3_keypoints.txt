- Introduces an amortized MAP framework for single-image SR that learns a network projecting inputs onto an affine subspace of high-resolution solutions.  
- Ensures all solutions in the subspace are consistent with the given LR image (they downsample to the original LR).  
- Decouples the consistency constraint from the prior, enabling flexible optimization for plausible solutions.  
- Supports use of different priors/selection methods (GANs, noise-assisted, density-assisted) within the subspace.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on several standard SR datasets.  
- Strength: Novel, powerful formulation that cleanly separates physical constraints from perceptual priors.  
- Strength: Modular and adaptable framework, allowing future extensions with different priors.  
- Weakness: Paper presentation is unclear and dense; needs more intuitive explanation, better flow, and illustrative figures.  
- Weakness: Lacks in-depth analysis of the learned projection network and its internal representations.  
- Conclusion: Valuable and strong contribution deserving acceptance, contingent on substantial revision for clarity and additional analysis.
