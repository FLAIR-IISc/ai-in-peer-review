- Decision: Accept.

- Main contribution: Frames image super-resolution as amortised MAP estimation, offering a principled alternative to empirical risk minimisation.

- Architectural innovation: Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces that HR outputs downsample exactly to the LR inputs, restricting solutions to the valid affine subspace.

- Theoretical claim: With the affine constraint, MAP inference reduces to minimising cross-entropy between generated and real HR image distributions.

- Methods evaluated: Proposes and compares three approaches — AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (likelihood-guided).

- Empirical validation: Thorough experiments on a 2D Swiss-roll toy problem and real datasets (grass textures, CelebA, ImageNet) validate the framework.

- Key empirical finding: AffGAN yields the sharpest, most visually appealing results; AffDG and AffLL produced blurrier outputs on real images.

- Benefit of affine projection: Improves theoretical correctness, practical performance, and training stability, even for standard MSE models.

- GAN training: Uses stabilization techniques like instance noise; application and analysis within SR is novel despite parallels to other work.

- Comparative value: The three-way comparison offers a comprehensive exploration of optimising the cross-entropy objective for SR.

- Writing and presentation: Well-written manuscript with clear arguments; minor typos remain.

- Suggested improvement: Provide deeper discussion on why AffDG and AffLL underperformed (e.g., denoiser gradient estimation or high-dimensional density inaccuracies).

- Question 1 for authors: Clarify practical difficulties with AffDG/AffLL — optimization issues or fundamental limitations of denoising score-matching/explicit density models in high dimensions?

- Question 2 for authors: How sensitive are results to numerical accuracy of the affine pseudo-inverse A+?

- Question 3 for authors: How sensitive is model performance to the annealing schedule and magnitude of instance noise used for GAN stabilization?
