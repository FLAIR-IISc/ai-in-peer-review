- Recasts single-image SR as amortised MAP inference to produce a single plausible HR image rather than MSE-averaged blurry outputs.  
- Argues pixel-wise losses like MSE produce perceptually unsatisfying, blurry results by averaging plausible HRs.  
- Contribution: introduces an affine projection layer that enforces exact consistency between network HR outputs and the LR input.  
- Contribution: formulates amortised MAP as minimizing cross-entropy between generated HR distribution and real HR distribution.  
- Contribution: proposes and evaluates three solution methods — AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided).  
- Experiments on toy and real images show AffGAN gives perceptually sharper and more plausible results than MSE-trained models.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: principled, novel formulation connecting MAP inference to cross-entropy minimization and motivating generative approaches.  
- Key reason 2: effective architectural innovation — affine projection elegantly enforces a fundamental SR constraint and improves performance.  
- Supporting evidence: solid theoretical development, convincing Swiss-roll toy experiment, and proof-of-concept showing affine projection improves MSE-trained networks.  
- Authors transparently discuss limitations and failure modes of AffDG and AffLL and critique MAP inference in Section 5.6.  
- Suggestion: minor proofreading to fix typos and grammatical errors.  
- Suggestion: summarize Appendix E insights (issues with denoiser/density-guided models) in the main text for clarity.  
- Suggestion: expand main-text discussion and comparisons for the instance-noise technique used to stabilize GAN training.
