- Proposes an amortised MAP framework for single-image super-resolution as a principled alternative to empirical risk minimisation.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer that guarantees any high-resolution output downscales exactly to the input low-resolution image.  
- Reframes SR as minimising cross-entropy between the generated and true image distributions.  
- Evaluates three optimisation strategies: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-model baseline).  
- AffGAN produces perceptually superior, visually sharp results on challenging natural-image datasets.  
- Recommends acceptance based on theoretical novelty and strong empirical validation.  
- Main strength: principled theoretical approach shifting from pixel-wise losses to MAP inference, with a rigorous architectural solution for the downsampling constraint.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: 2D Swiss-roll sanity check, grass textures, CelebA, and ImageNet experiments validate the framework and highlight AffGAN’s advantages.  
- Systematic comparison of AffGAN, AffDG, and AffLL is a valuable contribution, showing practical benefits of adversarial training.  
- Suggestion: improve clarity and add intuitive explanations/simpler notation in the theory section for accessibility.  
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis of why AffDG and AffLL fail on complex textures (e.g., local minima or imprecise gradient estimates).  
- Suggestion: visualize and analyze the learned residual (I − A+A)fθ(x) to understand high-frequency details added by the network.  
- Question: elaborate on practical training difficulties and sensitivity for AffDG and AffLL, including dependence on denoiser/density model quality and schedules.  
- Question: give intuition and details on the instance-noise selection and annealing schedule used to stabilise GAN training.  
- Question: report whether alternative color spaces (e.g., YCbCr vs RGB) were tested and how representation choice affects AffGAN visual quality.
