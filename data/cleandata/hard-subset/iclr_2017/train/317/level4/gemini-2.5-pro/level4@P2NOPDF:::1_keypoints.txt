- Novel framework: proposes amortized MAP inference with a pre-learned affine projection layer to enforce LR-HR consistency and explores three methods for minimizing cross-entropy; theoretically sound.

- Clarify advantage over SRGAN: need explicit comparison and discussion of benefits relative to recent GAN-based SR methods (e.g., Ledig et al.).

- Paired-data concern: affine projection must be pre-trained on LR–HR pairs, so clarify whether paired-data requirement is eliminated or merely shifted and discuss practical implications.

- Evaluation gap: strong qualitative ImageNet results but should include quantitative benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) for direct comparison to prior work.

- Scalability question: experiments limited to 128×128 images; authors should address whether the method scales to arbitrary/higher-resolution images and note architectural or computational limits.

- Latent noise omission: framework appears not to use a generator noise vector; report experiments (if any) on adding noise and its effects on output diversity and quality.

- Overall verdict: method is novel, interesting, and promising; addressing the above points would substantially strengthen the paper.
