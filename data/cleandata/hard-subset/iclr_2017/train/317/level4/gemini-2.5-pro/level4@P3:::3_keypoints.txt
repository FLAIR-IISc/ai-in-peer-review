- Proposes framing single-image super-resolution as amortised MAP inference to avoid MSE-induced blurring.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer that guarantees output consistency with the low-resolution input (A * f_theta(x) = x).  
- Reformulates SR as maximizing the log-probability (minimizing cross-entropy) of generated HR images under the real data distribution.  
- Presents three training strategies: AffGAN (GAN with affine-projected generator), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-model-guided using PixelCNN).  
- Empirical results across toy data, textures, faces, and natural images show AffGAN produces the sharpest, most perceptually convincing results.  
- Provides a theoretical link showing a stochastic version of AffGAN performs amortised variational inference.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Main strength: principled framework and the novel affine projection architecture enforcing data consistency by design.  
- Strong empirical validation, with the Swiss-roll example and real-image qualitative results (grass, faces) convincingly demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Affine projection lets the network focus on hallucinating high-frequency details in A's null-space and improves even MSE-trained models.  
- Thorough exploration of optimization approaches; empirical finding that AffGAN outperforms AffDG and AffLL on complex images.  
- Honest reporting of AffDG and AffLL limitations increases credibility and highlights future research challenges.  
- Paper discusses MAP limitations and situates contributions within broader theoretical context (Appendix F).  
- Presentation is dense; a clearer walkthrough or diagram showing low-frequency fixed component and learned high-frequency residual would help readability.  
- Instance noise is an important practical stabilization technique for GAN training but is underemphasized in the main text.  
- Would benefit from analysis of learned features (filters/activations) to show what high-frequency priors the generator captures.  
- Question: How sensitive is final image quality to the accuracy of the pseudo-inverse A+ approximation versus simpler upsampling methods?  
- Question: Why did AffDG and AffLL produce blurry results on real images — noisy/bias in denoiser gradients or poor/ hard-to-optimize density model?  
- Question: How sensitive are training stability and final performance to the instance-noise annealing schedule, and is the proposed discriminator-loss heuristic easy to implement and tune?
