- Reframes single-image SR as amortised MAP inference, enforcing LR-HR consistency via an affine projection layer.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer that hard-constrains downsampled outputs to match the LR input.  
- Shows that with this constraint the objective reduces to maximizing the log-prior, i.e., minimizing cross-entropy to the real-image distribution.  
- Proposes and evaluates three solutions: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (explicit density model).  
- Empirically, AffGAN produces the most perceptually sharp and plausible SR results, outperforming MSE-based methods.  
- Validates core claims with a 2D toy problem and real-image experiments on faces and natural scenes.  
- Affine projection is theoretically sound and even improves MSE-trained networks when added.  
- AffDG and AffLL failed to produce sharp images; their inclusion highlights practical optimization difficulties with proxy gradients/density models.  
- AffGAN results are convincing on textures (e.g., grass) but can exhibit high-frequency artifacts/noise.  
- Paper includes useful discussions on instance noise for GAN stability, MAP limitations, and links to amortised variational inference.  
- Suggests improving clarity with a diagram of the affine projection and clearer step from Eq.8 to Eq.9.  
- Recommends expanding analysis of why AffDG and AffLL fail (noisy gradients vs. poor density estimates).  
- Suggests analyzing learned features, e.g., visualizing the residual (I - A+A)f_θ(x), to understand added high-frequency details.  
- Asks authors to clarify whether AffDG/AffLL failures are fundamental or tunable, and to report sensitivity to instance-noise schedules/distributions.  
- Requests comparison of computational cost (training/inference) to standard MSE SR and cost/complexity of computing A+ for different downsampling kernels.
