- Novel framework: SR formulated as amortized MAP inference, reframing SR as minimizing cross-entropy between generated and real HR image distributions.  
- Key architectural contribution: affine projection layer that enforces exact LR-HR consistency (A f(x) = x) rather than a soft penalty.  
- Methods explored: three approaches to optimize the objective — AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (explicit likelihood-guided).  
- Empirical finding: GAN-based AffGAN produces perceptually sharp and consistent results, outperforming the other proposed variants qualitatively.  
- Theoretical support: clear derivation linking the objective to distribution matching and a connection between stochastic AffGAN and amortized variational inference.  
- Strength vs MSE: principled approach addresses shortcomings of MSE-based SR that yield overly smooth/blurry outputs.  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty, theory, and empirical results.  
- Request for comparison: ask for direct comparison to GAN-based SR prior work (e.g., SRGAN) and clarification of differences in formulation and performance.  
- Benchmarking: suggest adding results on standard SR datasets (Set5, Set14, BSD100) for broader contextual comparison.  
- Scalability concern: ask whether the approach and affine projection/GAN training scale to larger-than-128x128 images and any computational/stability issues.  
- Data requirement clarification: question whether learning the pseudo-inverse A+ requires paired HR-LR data or can be done from unpaired data given A.  
- Stochastic generator: encourage experiments with stochastic f_theta(x,z) to sample from p(y|x) and produce diverse plausible reconstructions.
