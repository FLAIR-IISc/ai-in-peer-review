- Proposes framing single-image super-resolution as amortized MAP inference, shifting from pixel-wise regression to finding the most probable HR image consistent with the LR input.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces the downsampled output equals the LR input, handling the likelihood term in the MAP objective.  
- Shows that with the affine constraint, training reduces to matching the generated HR distribution to the true HR distribution (via cross-entropy).  
- Explores three training methods: GAN-based, denoiser-guided, and a baseline using a pre-trained density model.  
- Empirical results indicate the GAN-based approach produces perceptually plausible and sharp images.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Strength: Provides a clear theoretical foundation for using generative models in SR and a rigorous architectural enforcement of consistency.  
- Request: Needs explicit comparison to prior GAN-based SR (e.g., Ledig et al. 2016) to clarify whether novelty is theoretical or due to the affine projection.  
- Question: Clarify whether paired {HR,LR} data is truly unnecessary, given the pseudo-inverse A+ is pre-trained (apparently using paired data).  
- Suggestion: Add evaluations on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) for fair quantitative comparison.  
- Suggestion: Discuss scalability to larger and variable-sized images and performance at higher output resolutions.  
- Suggestion: Consider a stochastic generator fθ(x,z) to capture multi-modal p(y|x) and empirically explore the link to amortized variational inference mentioned in Appendix F.  
- Overall: Excellent, well-written paper with novel, well-motivated ideas likely to be a valuable contribution.
