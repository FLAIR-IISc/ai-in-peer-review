This paper proposes a method for amortized MAP estimation in the context of super-resolution (SR) problems. The core idea is to train a neural network that learns a projection onto an affine subspace of SR solutions, where all solutions within this subspace are consistent with the low-resolution input. This framework enables the generation of high-quality solutions via multiple optimization strategies, including GAN-based, noise-assisted, and density-assisted approaches.

The efficacy of the proposed method is demonstrated effectively across several datasets.

Overall, I find this to be a valuable contribution. However, I believe the paper's impact could be significantly enhanced by improving the quality of the writing and presentation. The exposition was challenging to follow at times, and given the complexity of the subject matter, greater clarity would be highly beneficial. Furthermore, the paper would be strengthened by a deeper analysis of the resulting neural networks. An investigation into the types of features the projection network learns, for example, would offer valuable insights into the model's behavior.
