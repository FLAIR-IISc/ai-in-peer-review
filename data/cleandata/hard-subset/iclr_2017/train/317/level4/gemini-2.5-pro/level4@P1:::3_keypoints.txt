- Frames single-image SR as amortized MAP inference to avoid MSE-induced blurriness by seeking a single high-probability HR solution.
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces the hard constraint that generated HR images downsample to the original LR input (x = Ay).
- Shows that enforcing this constraint reduces amortized MAP to minimizing cross-entropy between generated and true image distributions.
- Proposes three optimization methods: AffGAN (GAN minimizing KL), AffDG (denoiser-guided gradient estimation), and AffLL (likelihood-guided using a PixelCNN variant).
- Empirical results on toy data, textures, faces, and natural images indicate AffGAN yields the sharpest, most realistic outputs.
- Decision: Accept — paper offers strong, novel conceptual and technical contributions.
- Key reason 1: Principled reframing (amortized MAP) directly addresses the primary weakness of pixel-wise losses.
- Key reason 2: The affine projection layer is an elegant input–output consistency constraint and, with GANs, produces substantial perceptual improvements.
- Supporting intuition: Swiss-roll example illustrates why MAP finds modes while MSE/MAE produce low-probability averages.
- Empirical support: Affine projection accelerates training and improves final performance even with conventional MSE loss.
- Thorough exploration: Comparing GAN, denoiser-guided, and density-guided approaches adds depth; theoretical links to KL and amortized variational inference are helpful.
- Presentation feedback: Section 3 is dense and needs a clearer roadmap and more intuitive exposition (e.g., Eq.8→Eq.9).
- Analysis feedback: Add systematic analysis of what textures/features the generator learns and of failure modes (e.g., hallucinated “snake into water”).
- Methodology feedback: Discuss instance noise (Appendix C) in the main text since it’s critical for GAN stability on complex datasets.
- Miscellaneous: Final proofreading needed for minor grammatical errors and typos.
- Question 1: Why did AffDG and AffLL produce blurry results on textures — optimization issues or fundamental estimator limitations?
- Question 2: How sensitive is AffGAN to instance-noise hyperparameters and annealing schedule for stable ImageNet training?
- Question 3: Would a stochastic AffGAN (amortized variational interpretation) help sample the posterior and alleviate atypical-mode/diversity problems?
