- Paper proposes amortised MAP inference for image super-resolution via a CNN that projects outputs to the affine subspace consistent with the low-resolution input.  
- Training framed as minimizing cross-entropy between model output and true image prior, enabling GANs, denoising-guided, and density-model-based training.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection in current form despite promising core idea and improved experimental results.  
- Primary reason for rejection: poor clarity and presentation — dense writing, jumps between theory and experiments, and hard-to-decipher theoretical contributions.  
- The affine projection concept is introduced but not sufficiently explained or illustrated beyond a Swiss-roll toy example.  
- Connections and relative strengths/weaknesses of the GAN, denoising, and density-guided methods are unclear and not well articulated.  
- Theoretical motivation feels disconnected from the practical implementation and needs clearer mapping to methods used.  
- Limited analysis of learned models: paper relies on quantitative metrics (PSNR, SSIM, cross-entropy) but lacks qualitative inspection of what the network learns.  
- Missing investigations: visualizations of learned filters/activation maps, ablation studies, and sensitivity analyses for hyperparameters.  
- Perceptual benefits expected from a MAP-based approach are not sufficiently demonstrated or discussed.  
- Promising aspects: affine projection idea is intriguing and GAN-based results are encouraging.  
- Suggestion: substantially rewrite the manuscript for clarity, breaking complex concepts into digestible parts and adding illustrative examples.  
- Suggestion: explicitly strengthen and explain the connection between theoretical motivations and practical implementation.  
- Suggestion: provide more thorough qualitative analysis, ablation studies, and hyperparameter sensitivity experiments.  
- Suggestion: clearly compare advantages and limitations of GAN, denoising, and density-guided approaches.  
- Suggestion: expand discussion of MAP inference limitations and potential remedies/future work.  
- Suggestion: include more visualizations, demonstrations of the affine projection in action, and comparisons across SR methods.  
- Reviewer question: provide a more intuitive explanation or visual analogy for the affine projection beyond the Swiss-roll example.  
- Reviewer question: what is the computational overhead of incorporating the affine projection into the network?  
- Reviewer question: how sensitive are results to the noise distribution and annealing schedule in the denoising-guided approach?  
- Reviewer question: why did the AffDG and AffLL models perform poorly on the grass texture dataset?  
- Reviewer question: elaborate on potential drawbacks of MAP inference and ways to address them in future work.  
- Reviewer question: how does this method compare to state-of-the-art SR approaches that use perceptual loss (e.g., SSIM-based) rather than GANs?
