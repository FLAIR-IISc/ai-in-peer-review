1) Introduces novel amortized MAP inference for SR with GANs, but unclear what new contributions it offers over existing GAN-based SR (e.g., SRGAN).  
2) Affine projection architecture is claimed to remove need for paired {HR,LR} data, yet training the affine layer still requires pairs—possible mere transfer of standard training.  
3) Requests evaluation on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) for fair comparison with prior work.  
4) Results are limited to 128×128 images; asks for analysis of performance and scalability to larger, arbitrary-sized inputs.  
5) Questions whether a noise/latent vector was included or explored to explicitly model output distribution as in traditional GANs.  
6) Overall: solid theoretical contribution and promising results, but some components’ necessity and experiments require clarification and additional comparisons.
