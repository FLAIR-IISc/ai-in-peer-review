The paper presents a new framework for single image super-resolution (SR) based on amortized MAP inference, and employs a pre-learned affine projection layer to ensure consistency between high-resolution (HR) and low-resolution (LR) outputs. It also proposes three methods for minimizing cross-entropy. Overall, this is a strong paper. However, I have several comments and questions for the authors.

1) The proposed amortized MAP inference is novel and distinguishes itself from previous SR approaches. When combined with a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN), this framework achieves plausible and compelling results. However, in comparison to other GAN-based SR methods, such as Photo-Realistic Single Image Super-Resolution Using a Generative Adversarial Network, it is unclear what novel contributions this new formulation offers to the current state-of-the-art.

2) By utilizing an affine projection architecture as a constraint, the model ostensibly eliminates the need for paired {HR, LR} training data. However, the training of the affine projection layer itself still requires {HR, LR} image pairs. Does this simply represent a transfer of the standard training procedure to the affine projection layer's optimization?

3) The paper presents extensive results, including those obtained on natural images from ImageNet. To facilitate a fair comparison with existing SR techniques, I request that the authors also provide results on standard benchmark datasets such as Set5, Set14, or BSD100.

4) The presented results for natural images are limited to a size of 128x128. Given that SR methods often encounter inputs of arbitrary size, can the authors comment on the framework’s performance with larger images and demonstrate its scalability?

5) Traditional GANs incorporate a noise term representing a latent space, allowing for a more explicit modeling of a distribution. Did the authors explore the inclusion of a noise vector in their framework?

Overall, this paper offers a novel framework for SR with a solid theoretical foundation. The core idea is innovative, and the authors explore a variety of approaches. While questions remain regarding the necessity of certain components and further experimentation is warranted, I believe this work will provide valuable inspiration to the research community.
