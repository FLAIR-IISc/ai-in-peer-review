- Paper proposes amortised MAP estimation for image super-resolution using a learned affine projection to ensure LR consistency and optimises with GANs, denoising, or density-based methods; results shown on several datasets.  
- Recommendation: reject due to unclear presentation and insufficient analysis to support claims.  
- Strength: core idea of amortised MAP inference and affine projection is novel and potentially valuable.  
- Main flaw: writing is dense and hard to follow, with complex concepts insufficiently explained.  
- Theoretical motivation for the affine projection is given but practical implications and benefits beyond consistency are not explored.  
- Three optimisation methods (GAN, denoising, density modeling) are introduced rapidly without comparative analysis of strengths, weaknesses, or trade-offs.  
- Empirical results lack insight into why methods perform as they do and do not convincingly support perceptual-quality claims.  
- Perceptual quality claims not backed by user studies or compelling qualitative comparisons; PSNR/SSIM limitations acknowledged but not addressed experimentally.  
- Ablation studies are limited (e.g., AffGAN vs SoftGAN helpful but insufficient); more extensive analyses needed.  
- Connection to variational inference is relegated to the appendix and not adequately discussed in terms of implications and differences from VAEs.  
- Recommend rewriting for clarity, adding visuals (e.g., to illustrate affine projection), and restructuring complex sections.  
- Recommend systematic comparative evaluation of the three optimisation methods across datasets and metrics, plus deeper ablations.  
- Recommend adding perceptual/user studies and feature visualisations (e.g., activation maps) to support qualitative claims.  
- Clarifying questions requested: choice and sensitivity of deconvolution kernel A+; conditions and mitigation of MAP limitations; theoretical justification for instance noise vs other GAN stabilisers; computational cost of affine projection vs other SR methods; limitations of the MCGSM density model and their effect on performance.
