This paper introduces an amortized Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) estimation approach to the Single Image Super-Resolution (SR) problem. The core idea – training a neural network to map low-resolution inputs to an affine subspace constrained by solutions consistent with traditional SR methods – is promising and facilitates the application of diverse optimization techniques, including GAN-based, noise-assisted, and density-assisted approaches. 

The experimental results are well-demonstrated across multiple datasets.

Overall, this is a strong contribution. However, the manuscript would benefit from significant improvements in clarity and polish. The complexity of the subject matter necessitates particularly clear exposition, and I found sections difficult to follow. Furthermore, additional analysis of the learned network representations – specifically, investigating the features learned by the network – would be a valuable addition.
