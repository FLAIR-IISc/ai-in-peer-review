This paper proposes a novel approach to super-resolution, framing it as amortized Maximum A Posteriori (MAP) estimation. A key contribution is a projection step designed to maintain consistency between high-resolution (HR) and low-resolution (LR) dependencies, which is experimentally demonstrated to improve performance. The authors further propose and evaluate three distinct methods for addressing the resulting cross-entropy problem outlined in Equation 9.

Summary: This is a very good paper, exceptionally well written and presented. The experimental results are comprehensive, and the paper includes well-chosen toy examples alongside applications to real-world scenarios. To the best of my understanding, the contributions to the field of super-resolution are novel – particularly sections 3.2, 3.3, and 3.4. While techniques specific to GAN training may have appeared in various forms previously (as discussed below), the overall approach feels fresh and insightful. I believe this paper will be highly relevant to future work on super-resolution, and the finding that GAN-based model training produces the most visually appealing results suggests a promising direction for further research.

The manuscript would benefit from a final proofread to correct a few minor typos.
