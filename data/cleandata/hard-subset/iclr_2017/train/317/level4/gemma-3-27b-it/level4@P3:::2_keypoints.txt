- Frames super-resolution as amortised MAP estimation and introduces an affine projection layer to enforce LR–HR consistency.  
- Proposes and evaluates three optimization strategies under this framework: GAN-based, denoiser-guided, and density-guided methods.  
- Experimental validation includes a 2D Swiss-roll toy and natural image datasets, showing effectiveness of the projection and methods.  
- GAN-based models produce the most visually appealing (perceptual) results.  
- Ablation studies demonstrate the importance of the affine projection for performance.  
- Theoretical connection established between AffGAN and amortised variational inference (Appendix F).  
- Paper is well-written, novel, and presents a significant advance for SR; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Suggests final proofreading to fix minor typos and grammar.  
- Recommends expanding discussion of limitations, especially the computational cost and scalability of the affine projection.  
- Requests discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity (learning rate, noise levels) to strengthen analysis.  
- Suggests more direct comparisons to the latest state-of-the-art SR methods.  
- Asks for details on sensitivity to the instance noise annealing schedule used in AffGAN.  
- Questions whether incorporating variational techniques (e.g., KL regularization) could mitigate atypical MAP estimates in high dimensions.  
- Requests concrete measurements or discussion of the affine projection’s computational overhead for higher-resolution images.  
- Asks which aspects of the density model limit AffLL’s performance and what improvements might help.
