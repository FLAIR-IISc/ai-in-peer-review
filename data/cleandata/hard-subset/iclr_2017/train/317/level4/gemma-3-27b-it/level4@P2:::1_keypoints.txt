- Introduces an amortized MAP framework for single-image SR using a CNN with an affine projection layer and MAP approximations via GANs, denoiser guidance, and a learned prior.  
- Claims the approach addresses MSE-blurring by producing more perceptually realistic SR outputs.  
- Recommends rejection due to limited evaluation scope and perceived incremental contribution versus existing GAN-based SR methods.  
- Primary empirical concern: no quantitative comparison to standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) or state-of-the-art methods (e.g., SRGAN/EDSR).  
- ImageNet experiments use low resolution (128×128); scalability to larger, realistic images is not demonstrated.  
- The claim that affine projection removes need for {HR,LR} pairs is misleading because the affine projection layer itself requires paired data for initial training.  
- The Appendix connection to variational inference is interesting but underexplored and lacks empirical validation.  
- The model omits a noise latent vector for the generator, raising questions about latent-space exploration and output diversity.  
- Suggest adding benchmarked quantitative results (PSNR, SSIM) on standard datasets.  
- Suggest evaluating scalability to higher-resolution images to show practical applicability.  
- Request a more thorough ablation study to isolate the effects of the affine projection and the different optimization methods (GAN, denoiser, density model).  
- Request clarification and sensitivity analysis of the affine projection training procedure and its impact on the unsupervised claim.  
- Recommend exploring a stochastic/noise-input version of AffGAN and further developing the VAE/variational-inference connection.  
- Specific questions: quantitative comparison to state-of-the-art SR methods, scalability limits, sensitivity to affine projection initialization, results when including a noise vector, and practical benefits of the amortized-VI perspective.
