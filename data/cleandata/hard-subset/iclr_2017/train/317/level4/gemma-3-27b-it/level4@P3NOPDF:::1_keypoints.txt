- Proposes an amortized MAP inference framework for single-image SR with a pre-learned affine projection layer to enforce LR–HR consistency.  
- Explores three distinct methods for minimizing cross-entropy within this framework.  
- Shows GAN-combined outputs that are visually plausible but needs clearer articulation of advantages over SRGAN and other GAN-based SR methods.  
- Affine projection allows core SR training without paired data, yet its own training requires paired HR–LR—clarify whether this merely shifts the paired-training step and quantify its unique benefit.  
- Presents extensive ImageNet results but omits standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) needed for fair comparison.  
- Experiments are limited to 128×128 images; scalability and performance on larger, arbitrary-size images should be evaluated.  
- Raises the question of including a latent noise vector in the GAN—ask whether it was tested or justified, since it could improve diversity and realism.  
- Overall assessment: novel and theoretically sound with promising potential, but requires clearer comparative advantages and more comprehensive experimentation.
