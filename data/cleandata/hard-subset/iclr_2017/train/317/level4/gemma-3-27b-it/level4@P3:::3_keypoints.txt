- Proposes framing image super-resolution as amortised MAP inference using neural networks.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer to enforce LR–HR consistency and reduce ill-posedness.  
- Explores three optimization strategies: GAN-based (AffGAN), denoising-guided (AffDG), and density-model (AffLL).  
- Draws a theoretical connection between GANs and amortised variational inference.  
- Decision: Reject — interesting theory but insufficient empirical gains and clarity issues.  
- Shows strong results on a synthetic Swiss-roll dataset and validates affine projection theoretically.  
- Performance on real datasets (CelebA, ImageNet) is marginal and not consistently better by PSNR/SSIM.  
- GAN-based models improve perceptual quality in some visuals but not dramatically over recent SR methods.  
- Paper over-emphasizes AffGAN; AffDG and AffLL results are weaker and underexplored.  
- Presentation is dense, notation-heavy, and sometimes hard to follow, hurting evaluation.  
- Needs more intuitive explanations, visual aids, and better narrative flow.  
- Lacks direct, comprehensive comparisons to state-of-the-art SR methods (e.g., EDSR, SRGAN) with statistical testing.  
- Requires ablation studies to isolate contributions (affine projection vs. optimization strategy).  
- Recommends feature visualization to analyze learned patterns and network behavior.  
- Suggests a deeper discussion of limitations, failure cases, and future directions.  
- Question: How sensitive are results to the instance-noise annealing schedule and were other noise types tried?  
- Question: Is the CelebA color drifting a known GAN issue and how can it be mitigated?  
- Question: Why did AffDG and AffLL underperform on grass texture—what are their bottlenecks?  
- Question: Have benefits of the variational-inference connection been leveraged for sampling or uncertainty estimation?
