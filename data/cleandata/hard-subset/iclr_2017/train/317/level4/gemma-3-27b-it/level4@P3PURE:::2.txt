This paper proposes a novel approach to super-resolution by framing it as amortized Maximum A Posteriori (MAP) estimation. A key contribution is a projection step designed to maintain consistency between high- and low-resolution dependencies, which is experimentally demonstrated to improve performance. Furthermore, the authors propose and evaluate three distinct methods for addressing the resulting cross-entropy problem presented in Eq. 9.

Summary: This is a very strong paper, exceptionally well written and presented. The experimental results are comprehensive, and the inclusion of both carefully chosen toy examples and real-world applications effectively illustrates the proposed method. To the best of my understanding, the contributions to the field of super-resolution – specifically sections 3.2, 3.3, and 3.4 – are novel. While techniques relating to GAN training may have appeared in alternative forms elsewhere (as discussed within the paper), the combination and application here are compelling. I believe this work will be highly relevant to future research in super-resolution, and the finding that GAN-based model training yields the most visually appealing results suggests a promising avenue for further investigation.

The manuscript would benefit from a final proofread to address a small number of typographical errors.
