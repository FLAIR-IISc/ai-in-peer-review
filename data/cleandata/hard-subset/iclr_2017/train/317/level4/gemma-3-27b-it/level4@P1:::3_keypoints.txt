- Proposes amortised MAP estimation for image SR by learning a network that projects solutions to an affine subspace consistent with the LR input, enabling GAN-, denoiser-, and density-guided HR generation across datasets.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Addresses an important SR problem—producing more plausible, less blurry outputs—with a novel, theoretically grounded approach.
- Affine projection constraint enforces LR–HR consistency and reduces the problem to minimizing a cross-entropy.
- Analysis linking AffGAN to amortised variational inference is a valuable theoretical contribution.
- Ablation studies (e.g., Table 1; AffGAN vs SoftGAN) convincingly show the benefit of the affine projection.
- Swiss-roll toy and real-image experiments demonstrate MAP’s advantages over MSE and practical impact, especially for AffGAN.
- Writing is dense; the paper needs improved clarity and readability, particularly in theoretical sections.
- Introduction should include concrete examples of perceptual shortcomings in existing SR methods.
- Theory section needs more intuitive and visual explanations of key equations (e.g., affine projection and eq. (7)).
- Experiments require clearer descriptions of the experimental setup and the network architectures for reproducibility.
- More detailed feature analysis of learned representations would strengthen empirical insights.
- Discussion should more deeply address limitations of the MAP approach and outline future directions.
- Question: Explain how atypical MAP estimates might appear in practice and possible mitigation strategies.
- Question: Describe the criteria and alternatives considered for the instance-noise annealing schedule and their effects.
- Question: Why did density-guided AffLL underperform relative to AffGAN—was it density-model capacity or other factors, and could stronger density models help?
- Question: How sensitive is the affine projection to inaccuracies in the downsampling kernel A, and how would slight errors affect performance?
