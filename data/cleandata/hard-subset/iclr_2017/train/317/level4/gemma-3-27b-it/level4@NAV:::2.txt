This work frames Super-Resolution as an amortized Maximum A Posteriori (MAP) estimation problem. A projection step designed to maintain consistency between High-Resolution (HR) and Low-Resolution (LR) dependencies is introduced and demonstrated through experiments to improve performance. Furthermore, the paper proposes and evaluates three distinct approaches to address the resulting cross-entropy problem detailed in Eq. 9.

Summary: This is a strong paper, exceptionally well-written and presented. The experimental validation is adequate, featuring insightful toy examples alongside applications to real-world data. The contributions to the field of super-resolution – specifically sections 3.2, 3.3, and 3.4 – appear novel. While aspects relating to GAN training may have precedents in existing literature (as discussed), the overall approach is compelling.
