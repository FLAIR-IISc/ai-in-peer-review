- Frames super-resolution as amortised MAP estimation and introduces an affine projection step to enforce HR–LR consistency, improving results.  
- Proposes three methods (GAN-based, denoiser-guided, density-guided) to solve the resulting cross-entropy minimisation; GAN-based training gives the most visually pleasing outputs.  
- Paper is well-written, novel (Sections 3.2–3.4), and experimentally thorough with both toy and real-world validations.  
- Connects AffGAN to amortised variational inference, a noteworthy theoretical contribution.  
- Manuscript requires a final proofread to correct minor typographical errors.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Supporting strengths: motivates MAP over MSE, shows affine projection simplifies optimization, and provides valuable comparative analysis across methods.  
- Additional feedback: discussion of MAP limitations is insightful; ablation study confirms affine projection benefit; Appendix D details experiments for reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: include discussion of computational complexity and training time for each method.  
- Questions for authors: provide the instance-noise annealing schedule used; clarify affine projection applicability to other downsampling operators; discuss how to address MAP’s potential to yield atypical estimates (e.g., sampling or alternative priors); and elaborate on leveraging variational inference within AffGAN and any preliminary results.
