- Proposes a novel SR formulation as amortized MAP estimation via a neural network projecting solutions onto an affine subspace constrained by LR consistency.  
- Learned affine subspace enables application of diverse optimization techniques (GANs, noise-assisted, density-assisted) to refine SR outputs.  
- Framework is flexible and unifies multiple optimization strategies without being tied to a specific method.  
- Demonstrates visually compelling high-resolution results on benchmark datasets.  
- Well-motivated approach addressing SR ill-posedness by incorporating a learned prior to guide solutions toward realism and consistency.  
- Presentation suffers from clarity issues, especially the explanation of the affine subspace and its LR consistency relationship.  
- Unclear how each optimization technique (e.g., GAN discriminator, noise-assisted steps) specifically leverages the learned subspace.  
- Recommends more intuitive/visual explanations of the subspace and clearer articulation of method components.  
- Quantitative evaluation is limited to PSNR/SSIM; should include perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS) to reflect visual improvements.  
- Lacks ablation studies isolating the contributions of the affine subspace, optimization choices, and LR consistency constraint.  
- Calls for analysis and visualization of the learned network’s weights/activations and testing robustness to different LR degradations.  
- Overall promising and potentially impactful work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions focused on clarity and additional analyses.
