The paper presents an amortized MAP estimation method for super-resolution (SR) problems. By learning a neural network that projects to an affine subspace of SR solutions consistent with a low-resolution (LR) input, the method enables the discovery of plausible solutions using a variety of optimization techniques, including GANs, noise-assisted optimization, and density-assisted optimization. 

Results are nicely demonstrated on several datasets. 

Overall, I find this paper promising, though the writing could benefit from significant polishing and the presentation could be made clearer. The paper was difficult to follow at times, and given the complexity of the subject matter, increased clarity would be beneficial. Furthermore, I would appreciate more analysis of the learned network representations – specifically, what kind of features do they learn?
