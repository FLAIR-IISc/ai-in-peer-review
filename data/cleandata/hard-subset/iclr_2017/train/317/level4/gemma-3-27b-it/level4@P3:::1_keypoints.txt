- Proposes an amortized MAP inference framework for image SR using a CNN with an affine projection layer and optimizations via GANs, denoiser guidance, and a maximum-likelihood prior.  
- Reviewer decision: Reject — interesting and theoretically sound but empirical evaluation is insufficient.  
- Main concern: Limited comparative evaluation and no direct comparison to recent GAN-based SR methods (e.g., Ledig et al. 2016).  
- Lacks quantitative metrics on ImageNet and no results on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) with PSNR/SSIM.  
- Experiments confined to a 2D toy problem, grass textures, and 128×128 images — scalability to larger, practical image sizes is unclear.  
- Unclear role and training details of the affine projection layer: it requires {HR, LR} pairs for initial training and the efficiency/benefit needs clarification.  
- Recommend investigating incorporation of a noise vector into AffGAN to explore latent space and improve diversity.  
- Request an ablation study to isolate contributions of the affine projection and each optimization method.  
- Ask for a more detailed comparison and discussion of how this work builds on and differs from Ledig et al. (2016) and other GAN-based SR approaches.  
- Need clarity on whether the affine projection provides substantial performance or stability gains over soft constraints like L1 loss.  
- Questions for authors: provide PSNR/SSIM on standard benchmarks vs SOTA; detail data/computational cost of affine projection training; report experiments with noise in AffGAN; and list limitations or failure cases.
