This paper proposes a novel framework for Super-Resolution, formulating it as amortized Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) estimation. A projection step is introduced to enforce consistency between high-resolution (HR) and low-resolution (LR) image dependencies, and is demonstrated experimentally to improve performance. The authors further explore three distinct methods for addressing the resulting cross-entropy problem, as defined in Eq. 9, and provide comparative results.

Summary: This is a strong paper, exceptionally well-written and presented. The experimental evaluation is thorough, including insightful toy examples and applications to real-world data. The contributions to the field of super-resolution, specifically sections 3.2, 3.3, and 3.4, appear novel. While techniques for training Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) may have been explored in various forms previously (as discussed in the paper), the application within this MAP estimation framework is significant. I anticipate this work will be influential in future super-resolution research; the observation that GAN-based training produces visually compelling results warrants further investigation. 

The manuscript would benefit from a final proofread to correct a small number of typographical errors.
