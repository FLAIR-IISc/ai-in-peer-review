1. Proposes a novel amortized MAP inference framework for image super-resolution.  
2. Introduces an affine projection layer to enforce consistency between HR outputs and LR inputs.  
3. Presents three methods (GAN-based, denoising-based, density-model-based) to minimize cross-entropy.  
4. Combining amortized MAP with GANs yields plausible, visually appealing SR results.  
5. Reviewer asks for clarification of concrete advantages over state-of-the-art GAN SR (e.g., Ledig et al.).  
6. Concern that training the affine projection layer may still require paired {HR, LR} data, shifting rather than removing data reliance.  
7. Experiments are promising but lack evaluation on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100).  
8. Current results are limited to 128×128 images; scalability to larger, arbitrary image sizes is unclear.  
9. Question whether a latent noise vector was incorporated or tested in the GAN formulation to improve diversity.  
10. Theoretical contributions (e.g., AffGAN–variational inference connection) and GAN analysis are valuable.  
11. Overall recommendation: Accept — novel, theoretically sound, and promising, but needs additional validation.  
12. Suggested improvements: clarify advantages over existing GAN methods, detail affine projection training, add benchmarks, test larger images, explore noise vectors, and expand on MAP limitations.  
13. Specific author questions: advantages vs Ledig, affine layer training interactions, scaling challenges and remedies, experiments with latent noise, and future directions to address MAP limitations.
