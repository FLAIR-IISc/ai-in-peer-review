- Proposes treating super-resolution as amortised MAP estimation with an affine projection step to enforce LR–HR consistency.  
- Introduces a projection step that improves consistency and experimentally yields better results.  
- Presents three methods to solve the resulting cross-entropy problem (Eq. 9) and evaluates them.  
- Well-written, clear, and well-structured presentation despite technical complexity.  
- Novel theoretical contribution linking amortised MAP, the affine projection layer, and connections between GANs and variational inference.  
- Strong empirical validation with toy (2D Swiss-roll) and real-world (grass, CelebA, ImageNet) experiments.  
- GAN-based method (AffGAN) produces the most visually appealing results and outperforms alternatives.  
- Decision: Accept — novel approach with solid theory and compelling experiments addressing MSE-induced blurriness.
