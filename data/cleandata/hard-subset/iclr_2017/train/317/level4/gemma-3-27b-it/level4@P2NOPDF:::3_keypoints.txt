- Proposes an amortized MAP estimation approach for single-image super-resolution using a neural network projection onto an affine subspace constrained by an LR prior.  
- Core idea: train a network to project SR solutions to an affine subspace defined by the lower-resolution prior to guide plausible high-resolution reconstructions.  
- Framework is flexible and can integrate with various optimization strategies, including GAN-based, noise-assisted, and density-assisted methods.  
- Demonstrates compelling experimental results on several benchmark datasets, showing effectiveness of the method.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with promising results and notable flexibility.  
- Major presentation issue: writing is often dense and hard to follow, reducing accessibility.  
- Needs clearer motivation for using an affine subspace and more precise explanation of how the LR prior is incorporated.  
- Would benefit from a more intuitive explanation of underlying principles to improve reader understanding.  
- Requests further analysis of learned network representations (e.g., visualizing filters, activation patterns, and ablation studies) to assess what the network learns, generalization, and sensitivity.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions focused on clearer exposition and deeper analysis of learned representations.
