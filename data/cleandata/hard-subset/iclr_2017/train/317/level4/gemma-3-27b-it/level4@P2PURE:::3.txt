The paper introduces an amortized maximum a posteriori (MAP) estimation approach to address single image super-resolution (SR) problems. The method learns a neural network to project SR solutions onto an affine subspace, ensuring consistency with a lower-resolution (LR) input. This projection enables the use of diverse optimization strategies, including those leveraging generative adversarial networks (GANs), noise-assisted optimization, and density-assisted optimization, to recover plausible high-resolution solutions.

The authors demonstrate compelling results on multiple datasets.

Overall, I find this paper promising. However, the writing could benefit from significant polishing and the presentation needs improvement. I occasionally found the paper difficult to follow, and given the complexity of the topic, increased clarity would be highly beneficial. Furthermore, I would appreciate additional analysis of the learned network representations – specifically, an investigation into the features they capture.
