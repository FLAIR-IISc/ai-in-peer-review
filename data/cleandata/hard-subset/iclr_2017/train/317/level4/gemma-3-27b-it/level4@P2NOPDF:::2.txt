This paper presents a novel approach to super-resolution by framing it as amortized Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) estimation. The authors introduce a projection step designed to enforce consistency between high-resolution (HR) and low-resolution (LR) image dependencies, and demonstrate its effectiveness through experimentation. Furthermore, the paper explores three distinct methods for addressing the resulting cross-entropy problem (Eq. 9), providing a comprehensive empirical evaluation of each.

Overall, this is a strong paper, exceptionally well-written and clearly presented. The experimental validation is thorough, supported by insightful toy examples and compelling applications to real-world scenarios. The core contributions related to super-resolution – specifically the projection step (Section 3.2) and the alternative methods for solving the cross-entropy problem (Sections 3.3 and 3.4) – appear novel. While techniques for training Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), which are leveraged within this framework, have been explored in various forms previously (see discussion section), their application within this novel MAP estimation context is significant. 

I believe this work will be highly relevant to future research in super-resolution. The finding that GAN-based training consistently produces the most visually pleasing results strongly suggests a promising avenue for further investigation. This work provides a solid foundation for exploring the benefits of incorporating GANs into MAP estimation-based super-resolution pipelines.

I recommend a final proofread of the manuscript to address a few minor typographical errors. These are infrequent but would benefit from correction to enhance the overall polish of the paper.
