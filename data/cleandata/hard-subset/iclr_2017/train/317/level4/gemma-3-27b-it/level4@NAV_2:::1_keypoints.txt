- Overall decision: Reject — novel ideas but lacking sufficient quantitative comparison to state-of-the-art and needing further justification.

- Main contribution: Proposes amortised MAP inference for single-image SR and introduces an affine projection layer to enforce HR–LR consistency.

- Methods: Introduces three MAP-approximation approaches — GAN-based, denoiser-guided, and density-model-guided SR.

- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation and a useful connection between GANs and amortised variational inference.

- Strength: Affine projection layer is a clever constraint that ensures valid solutions and simplifies learning.

- Major weakness: Insufficient quantitative comparison to recent high-performing GAN-based SR methods (e.g., ESRGAN, Real-ESRGAN) on standard benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100).

- Misleading claim: The paper’s statement that paired HR–LR data aren’t needed is misleading — the affine projection layer’s initial training appears to require paired data; clarify its effect on the unsupervised claim (is it transfer learning?).

- Limited experiments: Evaluation mostly on texture patches and 128×128 images; need results on standard SR benchmarks and tests of scalability to larger/arbitrary image sizes.

- Missing investigation: No exploration of a latent noise vector to enable diverse plausible HR solutions and richer output diversity.

- Discussion gap: The paper briefly mentions limitations of MAP inference; this should be expanded with concrete failure scenarios.

- Questions for authors: computational cost and scaling of affine projection training; robustness to degraded LR inputs (noise/blur); rationale and influence of the chosen density-model architecture; sensitivity to noise distribution and annealing schedule in instance noise.
