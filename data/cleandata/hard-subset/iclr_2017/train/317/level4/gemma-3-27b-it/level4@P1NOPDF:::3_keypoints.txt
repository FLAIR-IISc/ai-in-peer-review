- Proposes an amortized MAP estimation method for super-resolution using a neural network that projects outputs onto an affine subspace consistent with the LR input.  
- Leverages multiple optimization strategies (GANs, noise-assisted, density-assisted) within this affine-subspace framework.  
- Experimental results are convincing and demonstrated on several datasets.  
- Constraining solutions to an affine subspace is an interesting, potentially impactful core idea and shows flexibility.  
- Writing and presentation require significant polishing; paper is sometimes difficult to follow.  
- Paper lacks sufficient analysis of what the learned networks/features represent.  
- The connection between the affine subspace and the LR input is not clearly explained; definition and justification of the subspace are needed.  
- Recommend adding visual examples illustrating projection onto the affine subspace.  
- Motivation for using amortized MAP estimation is insufficiently explained; needs explicit comparison to traditional MAP in quality and computational cost.  
- Experiments need more detailed ablation studies, e.g., performance of optimization techniques without the affine constraint.  
- Suggest testing a wider range of SR factors (e.g., x8) to assess generalizability.  
- Recommend visualizing feature maps or performing feature ablation studies to better interpret network behavior.  
- Overall assessment: promising contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing clarity and additional analysis.
