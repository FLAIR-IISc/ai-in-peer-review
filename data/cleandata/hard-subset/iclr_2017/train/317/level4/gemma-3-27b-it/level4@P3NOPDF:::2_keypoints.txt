- Novel formulation: reframes single-image super-resolution (SISR) as amortized MAP estimation with a projection step enforcing HR–LR consistency.  
- Main contribution: introduces the projection mechanism and evaluates three methods for the resulting cross-entropy problem.  
- Presentation quality: exceptionally clear writing, notation, and effective visual examples.  
- Experimental strength: consistent improvements over baselines across datasets and metrics, with helpful ablation studies isolating projection and loss impacts.  
- Practical relevance: GAN-based training produces the most perceptually pleasing results, highlighting real-world applicability.  
- GAN stability concern: training stability for the GAN approach is acknowledged and needs more discussion of mitigation techniques (e.g., spectral normalization, gradient penalty).  
- Related work gap: should better contrast with existing GAN-based SR methods (e.g., SRGAN) and clarify why the projection adds benefits beyond prior GAN techniques.  
- Minor issues: manuscript contains a few typographical errors; recommend a final proofread.  
- Discussion takeaway: novelty is the principled MAP + projection formulation rather than merely using GANs; projection is the key differentiator.  
- Overall verdict: high-quality, significant contribution with easily addressable minor weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
