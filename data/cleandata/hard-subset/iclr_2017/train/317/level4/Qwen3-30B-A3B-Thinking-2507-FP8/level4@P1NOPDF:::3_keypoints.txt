- Introduces an amortized MAP estimation approach for super-resolution that learns to project observations onto an affine subspace of SR solutions.
- Framework supports efficient solution finding via diverse optimization strategies (GANs, noise-assisted, density-assisted).
- Demonstrates strong empirical results across multiple standard SR benchmarks.
- Technical contribution is promising and empirically well-validated.
- Writing requires significant polishing to improve clarity and readability.
- Exposition sometimes obscures the method’s complexity, making key steps hard to follow without rereading.
- Recommends a more structured presentation to improve accessibility given the approach's intricacy.
- Lacks deeper analysis of the learned networks and their internal representations.
- Suggests investigating what features networks encode during projection and how these features relate to SR quality and diversity.
- Overall technically sound and addresses a meaningful SR challenge, but would benefit from clearer exposition and feature-analysis to increase impact.
