- Proposes reframing image super-resolution as amortized MAP inference with a novel affine projection layer that enforces Aŷ = x.  
- Introduces three implementations: AffGAN (affine-projected GAN), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided), with AffGAN producing the best visual results.  
- Affine projection transforms SR into a generative modeling problem, allowing training to match reconstructed-image marginals to a natural image prior instead of using MSE.  
- The affine projection layer is mathematically well-motivated and guarantees HR-LR consistency.  
- AffGAN is theoretically grounded and empirically shown to minimize the desired cross-entropy objective, producing sharper, more perceptually plausible images than MSE-based methods.  
- Experiments are thorough, including a 2D Swiss-roll toy and multiple real-world datasets, with AffGAN achieving cross-entropy close to theoretical MAP (Table 1).  
- Paper establishes a novel connection between GANs and amortized variational inference, a potentially significant theoretical contribution.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing strong theory and convincing experiments addressing MSE blurriness.  
- Suggest expanding limitations discussion (representation dependence) with concrete examples of how different representations affect SR results.  
- Recommend explicitly contrasting the affine projection with prior soft-constraint approaches and clarifying why exact consistency matters.  
- Note minor grammatical/typo fixes (e.g., consistent use of "amortized").  
- Ask for explanation why AffDG and AffLL yield blurrier results despite theoretical ties between denoising and gradient estimation.  
- Pose three author questions: effect of minimizing KL[q‖p] (incl. entropy) on quality vs. diversity; how affine projection interacts with representation dependence (color spaces/features); and how MAP outputs can be semantically “wrong” (prior bias vs. high-probability outputs).
