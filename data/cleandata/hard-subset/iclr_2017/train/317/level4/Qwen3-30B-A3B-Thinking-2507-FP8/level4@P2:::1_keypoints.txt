- Proposes an amortized MAP inference framework for image super-resolution, replacing ERM/MSE approaches.  
- Introduces a CNN with an affine projection layer that enforces outputs consistent with the low-resolution input.  
- Reframes SR training as minimizing cross-entropy between model outputs and a natural image prior, turning SR into a generative modeling task.  
- Proposes three solution methods: Affine Projected GAN (AffGAN), denoiser-guided SR (AffDG), and density-guided approach (AffLL).  
- AffGAN yields superior perceptual quality on real-world images compared to MSE-based baselines.  
- Provides theoretical contribution connecting GANs to amortized variational (MAP) inference (Appendix F).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions based on strong theoretical and empirical contributions.  
- Successfully identifies MSE limitations (blurriness from averaging multimodal solutions) and motivates MAP as more appropriate.  
- Well situated in literature, building on GAN advances (Ledig et al., 2016) and strengthening theoretical justification.  
- Claims supported by theoretical analysis (Section 3) and empirical results (Section 5), with cross-entropy metrics and visual comparisons favoring AffGAN.  
- Needs explicit quantitative comparison with Ledig et al. (2016) to isolate gains from the theoretical framework versus engineering differences.  
- Ambiguity: affine projection training requires HR-LR pairs, contradicting claim of not needing corresponding pairs—clarify whether this is a one-time or per-dataset requirement.  
- Experiments should include standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) for direct community comparison.  
- Scalability concern: current results limited to 128×128—address whether framework handles larger resolutions and arbitrary input sizes.  
- Question on diversity: AffGAN omits a latent noise vector—clarify impact on diversity of super-resolved outputs versus standard GANs.  
- Suggestion to include human perceptual evaluation beyond PSNR/SSIM to substantiate perceptual quality claims.  
- Request clarification on how AffGAN mitigates known limitations of MAP inference compared to conventional MAP approaches.
