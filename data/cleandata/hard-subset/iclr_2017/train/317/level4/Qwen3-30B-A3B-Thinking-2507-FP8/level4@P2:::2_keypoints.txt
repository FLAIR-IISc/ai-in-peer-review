- Proposes amortized MAP inference for single-image super-resolution instead of MSE-based empirical risk minimization.  
- Argues MSE-based SR produces blurry outputs by averaging over plausible HR solutions.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces LR–HR consistency by projecting outputs to the valid SR affine subspace.  
- Presents three amortized MAP methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided).  
- Shows theoretical grounding and a variational-inference connection for using GANs in SR (Appendix F).  
- Empirically demonstrates AffGAN yields visually superior, sharper images on toy and real datasets.  
- Recommends acceptance: novel theory plus compelling empirical results constitute a strong contribution.  
- Theoretical argument: MAP selects high-probability solutions while MSE produces low-probability averaged outputs.  
- Affine projection converts the constrained SR problem into an unconstrained optimization elegantly and effectively.  
- Swiss-roll toy experiments show AffGAN/AffDG minimize cross-entropy; MSE/MAE fail to produce plausible outputs.  
- On real images AffGAN improves perceptual quality even when PSNR is slightly lower than MSE models.  
- Ablation study indicates the affine projection improves training dynamics and final performance.  
- Instance noise is proposed to stabilize GAN training and is a practically useful technique.  
- Suggest expanding the discussion of MAP limitations and how the affine projection interacts with them.  
- Suggest explicitly relating improvements to perceptual metrics (SSIM, PSNR) and explaining PSNR trade-offs.  
- Recommend summarizing key implementation details in the main text for clarity.  
- Recommend adding a brief comparison to Ledig et al. (2016) to contextualize the work.  
- Ask authors to provide concrete examples of AffGAN’s output diversity, especially on challenging textures (e.g., grass).  
- Ask how the affine projection mitigates MAP’s representation dependence compared to other SR methods.  
- Ask for elaboration on applying the instance noise stabilization technique to other image-generation tasks.
