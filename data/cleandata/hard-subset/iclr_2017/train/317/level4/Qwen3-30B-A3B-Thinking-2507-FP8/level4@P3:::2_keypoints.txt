- Proposes amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution using an affine projection layer to enforce LR–HR consistency and avoid MSE-induced blurriness.  
- Reformulates MAP inference as minimizing cross-entropy between model output and natural image prior, turning SR into a generative modeling task.  
- Introduces three solution methods: AffGAN (affine-projected GAN), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided baseline).  
- Provides strong theoretical grounding (Section 3) and a novel connection between GANs and amortized variational inference (Appendix F).  
- Experimental validation on toy and real datasets shows AffGAN yields visually superior, more natural outputs despite lower MSE/PSNR.  
- Ablation studies show the affine projection layer improves training dynamics, consistency, and final performance.  
- Recommendation: accept — novel, well-motivated approach with convincing empirical results, especially for AffGAN.  
- Suggests clearer discussion of limitations of existing perceptual metrics (SSIM, etc.) and how MAP addresses them.  
- Requests more detailed comparison to recent perceptual-loss/GAN SR methods (e.g., Larsen et al., Ledig et al.) to clarify differences and improvements.  
- Recommends strengthening the critique of MAP inference by giving concrete examples of how affine projection mitigates concerns.  
- Asks authors to explain why AffDG and AffLL produced blurry results and whether architectural changes could salvage them.  
- Asks for intuition on connecting deterministic AffGAN to variational inference and implications for stochastic extensions.  
- Asks how affine projection leads AffGAN to minimize cross-entropy (including entropy term) versus standard GANs minimizing KL, and whether this explains AffGAN’s superior performance.
