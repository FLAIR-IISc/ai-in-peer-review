- Proposes amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution instead of empirical risk minimization to avoid blurring from posterior averaging.
- Introduces an affine projection layer to enforce consistency between low- and high-resolution images, turning constrained MAP into an unconstrained cross-entropy problem.
- Presents three solution methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided).
- AffGAN yields visually superior, sharper results on synthetic and real datasets (grass textures, faces, natural images).
- Recommends acceptance based on theoretical grounding and perceptual improvements over MSE-based methods.
- The affine projection idea and mathematical analysis (Section 3) are strong and clearly justified.
- Empirical validation: Swiss-roll experiment shows AffGAN/AffDG minimize the target objective; real-data experiments and ablation study support effectiveness and the importance of the affine layer.
- Provides a novel theoretical link between GANs and amortized variational inference (Appendix F).
- Suggests clearer, more intuitive presentation of the affine projection (e.g., diagram) to aid reader understanding.
- Recommends deeper analysis of learned features/activations to explain what the networks prioritize.
- Advises expanding the discussion of limitations and failure cases of MAP inference versus sampling-based approaches.
- Asks how minimizing KL[q‖pY] vs. cross-entropy H[q,pY] affects diversity versus sharpness of outputs.
- Asks why AffGAN produces sharper results than AffDG/AffLL despite using the same affine projection architecture.
- Requests more intuition and details on the choice and numerical optimization of the Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse A+ for the projection.
