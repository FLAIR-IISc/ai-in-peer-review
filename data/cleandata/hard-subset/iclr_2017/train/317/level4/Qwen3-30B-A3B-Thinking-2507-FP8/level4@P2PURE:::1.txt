

The paper introduces a novel framework for solving the super-resolution (SR) problem through amortized MAP inference, incorporating a pre-learned affine projection layer to ensure output consistency with the low-resolution (LR) input. It further proposes three distinct approaches to minimize cross-entropy. Overall, the work presents a well-motivated contribution with a solid theoretical foundation. However, several points require clarification:  

1) While the amortized MAP inference framework is novel compared to prior SR methods and yields plausible, high-quality results when combined with GANs, it raises the question of its specific contribution relative to the state-of-the-art, particularly in comparison to *Photo-Realistic Single Image Super-Resolution Using a Generative Adversarial Network*.  

2) The affine projection layer enables training without paired {HR, LR} images, yet its training requires such pairs. This implies a transfer of the paired training procedure to the affine projection layer—does the framework truly eliminate the need for paired data, or merely relocate this requirement?  

3) The paper demonstrates results on natural images from ImageNet but omits standard SR benchmarks like Set5, Set14, or BSD100. Including results on these conventional datasets is essential for a fair comparison with prior work.  

4) The presented natural image results are limited to 128×128 resolution. Given that SR must handle arbitrary input sizes, the framework's scalability to larger images requires validation.  

5) Standard GANs incorporate a noise vector to model distribution learning. The authors should address whether they considered integrating a noise vector into their framework.  

While the paper offers a novel SR framework with rigorous theoretical analysis and explores multiple solution methods, some questions regarding the necessity of the approach and the need for additional experiments remain. This work provides valuable inspiration for the community.
