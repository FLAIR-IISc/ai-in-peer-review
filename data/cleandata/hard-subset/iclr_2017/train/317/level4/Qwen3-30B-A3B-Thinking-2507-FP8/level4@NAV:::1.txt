

The paper introduces a novel framework for the super-resolution (SR) problem, employing amortized MAP inference and incorporating a pre-trained affine projection layer to ensure output consistency with the low-resolution (LR) input. It further proposes three distinct approaches to minimize cross-entropy. Overall, this is a strong contribution. However, the following points require clarification:  

1) While the amortized MAP inference is novel compared to prior SR methods and yields high-quality results when combined with GANs, it is unclear how this formulation advances beyond the latest state-of-the-art, particularly relative to the Photo-Realistic Single Image Super-Resolution Using a Generative Adversarial Network approach.  

2) The affine projection architecture eliminates the need for paired {HR, LR} training data. However, training the affine projection layer itself requires {HR, LR} pairs. Does this imply the training procedure for the affine layer is merely transferred to the overall model?  

3) The framework demonstrates results on natural images from ImageNet, but results on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, or BSD100) are missing. Including these would enable fairer comparisons with prior work.  

4) The presented natural image results are limited to 128×128 resolution. Can the framework maintain performance on larger input resolutions, as SR typically handles arbitrary-sized inputs?  

5) Standard GANs utilize a noise vector in the latent space to model distributions. Did the authors explore incorporating such a noise term?  

Despite these points, the paper offers a compelling new SR framework with rigorous theoretical grounding. The novelty and breadth of explored methods are noteworthy. Addressing the above questions and providing additional experiments would significantly strengthen the contribution. This work holds substantial potential to inspire the community.
