- Frames image super-resolution as amortized MAP inference, a principled and significant contribution.  
- Proposes an affine projection layer that enforces outputs lie in the affine subspace of valid SR solutions, ensuring LR consistency.  
- Introduces three solution methods: GAN-based (AffGAN), denoiser-guided, and density-guided approaches for cross-entropy minimization.  
- Provides thorough experimental validation on toy problems and real-world image datasets.  
- Decision: Accept — novel theoretical framework and clear visual improvements over existing methods.  
- Theoretical insight: MAP inference addresses blurriness inherent to MSE-based SR methods.  
- Affine projection layer is a technically sound innovation claimed to be computationally efficient.  
- AffGAN shows particularly strong visual quality; Appendix F links GANs to amortized variational inference strengthening theory.  
- Manuscript is well-written and technically sound, with a few minor typos to correct.  
- Reviewer requests more details about computational cost (inference time and memory) of the affine projection layer versus standard SR networks.  
- Reviewer asks why denoiser-guided and density-guided methods produced blurrier results than AffGAN and whether they can be improved.
