- Novelty unclear: paper does not convincingly show how amortized MAP (with GANs) improves over SRGAN or other SOTA in theory or practice.  
- Need ablation: require direct comparisons/ablations isolating amortized inference vs iterative optimization and other core innovations.  
- Training-data claim inconsistent: affine projection layer is pre-trained with HR/LR pairs, so the model may still depend on paired data; clarify if affine layer is fixed or jointly trained.  
- Missing standard benchmarks: must report quantitative metrics (PSNR, SSIM) on Set5, Set14, BSD100 for fair comparison.  
- Limited resolution experiments: results only at 128×128; demonstrate scalability to larger images (e.g., 256×256+) and analyze computational/memory costs.  
- Latent-space exploration absent: investigate use of noise vectors in the GAN component and their effect on diversity and reconstruction quality.  
- Vague wording: replace imprecise phrases like "plausible and good results" with specific metrics and comparisons.  
- Clarify methods: succinctly summarize the three proposed cross-entropy minimization methods and their key differences.  
- Terminology consistency: use consistent terms such as "HR/LR pairs" throughout.  
- Overall assessment: theoretically grounded and promising methodology but currently under-justified due to missing comparisons, benchmarks, and clarifications.  
- Recommendation: reject (with minor revisions); request authors to add SRGAN/EDSR/RCAN comparisons, include Set5/Set14/BSD100 results, clarify affine layer training dependency, demonstrate scalability, and test latent noise effects.
