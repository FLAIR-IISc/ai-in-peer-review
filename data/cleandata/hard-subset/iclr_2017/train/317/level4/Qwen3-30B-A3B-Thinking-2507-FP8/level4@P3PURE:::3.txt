

The paper introduces an amortized maximum a posteriori (MAP) estimation approach for super-resolution (SR) tasks. The method employs a neural network to project onto an affine subspace of SR solutions consistent with the low-resolution (LR) observation model, thereby enabling the discovery of proper solutions through diverse optimization strategies—including GANs, noise-assisted, and density-assisted techniques. Results are effectively demonstrated across multiple datasets.  

While the technical contribution is promising, the manuscript requires significant polishing to enhance clarity and readability. The exposition is occasionally difficult to follow, particularly given the complexity of the underlying methodology; a more structured presentation would greatly improve accessibility. Additionally, the authors should include deeper analysis of the learned network features to elucidate what characteristics the model identifies and leverages for SR performance.
