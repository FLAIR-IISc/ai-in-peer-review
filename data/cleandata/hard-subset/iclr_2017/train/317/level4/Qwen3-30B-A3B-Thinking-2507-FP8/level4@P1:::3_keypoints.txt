- Proposes amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution with a novel affine-projection layer enforcing LR–HR consistency, enabling MAP without paired LR–HR data.  
- Introduces three amortized MAP methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (likelihood-guided).  
- AffGAN yields perceptually superior, state-of-the-art results on real datasets and connects GANs to variational inference.  
- Decision: Accept — strong theoretical contribution addressing blur from MSE and strong empirical perceptual gains.  
- Clearly identifies core problem: ERM/MSE averages plausible HRs producing blur; MAP favors high-probability natural-image solutions.  
- Well-motivated and well-placed in literature, with solid theoretical foundations and links to generative modeling.  
- Claims are well-supported: affine projection derivation and validation; AffGAN empirical superiority; theoretical variational connection.  
- Minor weakness: AffDG and AffLL underperform compared to AffGAN, acknowledged by authors.  
- Clarity could be improved (e.g., simplify Section 3.1 with an intuitive explanation).  
- Recommend adding a diagram showing integration of the affine projection layer into SR networks.  
- Suggest analyzing why AffGAN produces high-frequency noise and discuss sharpness vs. artifact trade-offs.  
- Propose trimming less critical appendices (e.g., Appendix C) to improve conciseness.  
- Questions: Why does AffGAN output deviate from the true mode in Fig.1b — sampling vs. GAN limitation?  
- Questions: How does affine projection mitigate MAP pitfalls like mode collapse compared to standard MAP inference?  
- Questions: What causes AffDG training divergence — noise-annealing schedule or fundamental gradient-based MAP limitation?
