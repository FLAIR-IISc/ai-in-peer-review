

The paper introduces an amortized MAP estimation approach for super-resolution (SR), where a neural network learns to project observations onto an affine subspace of SR solutions consistent with the low-resolution observation. This framework enables efficient solution finding through diverse optimization strategies—including GANs, noise-assisted, and density-assisted methods—and demonstrates strong results across multiple standard benchmarks.  

While the technical contribution is promising and the empirical validation is well-executed, the writing requires significant polishing to enhance clarity. The exposition occasionally obscures the method’s complexity, making it challenging to follow key steps without repeated rereading. Given the intricate nature of the approach, a more structured presentation would substantially improve accessibility.  

Additionally, the paper lacks deeper analysis of the learned networks. Specifically, it would be valuable to investigate: (1) what features the networks encode during projection, and (2) how these features correlate with the quality or diversity of reconstructed SR outputs. Such analysis would strengthen the interpretability of the method and clarify its operational principles.  

Overall, the work addresses a meaningful challenge in SR, but refining the presentation and including network feature analysis would elevate its impact. The current version is technically sound but would benefit from greater precision in exposition.
