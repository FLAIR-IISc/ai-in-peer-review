- Proposes an amortized MAP approach for single-image super-resolution using a neural projector onto an affine subspace of solutions consistent with the LR model.  
- Integrates projector with GAN-based, noise-assisted, and density-assisted optimization for efficient solution finding.  
- Empirical results on multiple benchmarks are competitive and demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Manuscript needs significant refinement for clarity and accessibility.  
- Technical exposition is occasionally convoluted, especially the affine subspace projection mechanism and its integration with auxiliary optimization methods.  
- The relationship between the learned network, the affine subspace, and specific optimization strategies (GAN/noise/density) is ambiguous and under-explained.  
- Lacks clear differentiation from or comparison to existing amortized inference frameworks for SR.  
- Paper structure and narrative should be improved; key contributions (theory for affine subspace, network design, projector–optimizer interplay) must be articulated precisely.  
- Figures and algorithm descriptions should explicitly show information flow and component roles.  
- Needs deeper analysis of learned network features: spatial/frequency features, correlation with reconstruction quality/noise robustness, and whether it learns priors distinct from standard SR models.  
- Contains minor grammatical errors and redundant phrasing that should be corrected.  
- Abstract and introduction should better contextualize the work within amortized inference for inverse problems and state the specific gaps addressed.  
- Overall, the idea is novel and empirically strong; clearer exposition and deeper analysis would substantially increase the paper’s impact.
