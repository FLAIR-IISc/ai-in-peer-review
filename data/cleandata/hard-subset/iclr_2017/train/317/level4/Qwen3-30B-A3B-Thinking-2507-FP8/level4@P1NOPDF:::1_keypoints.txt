- Novel amortized MAP framework with affine projection yields plausible SR and strong theoretical grounding, but unclear how it advances beyond recent GAN-based SR (e.g., Johnson et al.).

- Affine projection is claimed to remove need for paired {HR,LR} data, yet it itself requires {HR,LR} pairs for initial training—does this simply shift the supervised requirement to that component?

- Paper shows ImageNet results but lacks comparisons on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) for fair evaluation against baselines.

- Experiments use fixed 128×128 inputs; the paper must demonstrate scalability and performance on larger, arbitrary image sizes.

- It's unclear whether a latent noise vector (as in standard GANs) was used or tested in the generator to model HR image variability—authors should clarify or justify omission.

- Overall: novel and promising with solid theory and multiple solution paths, but needs stronger justification of broader impact and additional experiments; merits acceptance with revisions.
