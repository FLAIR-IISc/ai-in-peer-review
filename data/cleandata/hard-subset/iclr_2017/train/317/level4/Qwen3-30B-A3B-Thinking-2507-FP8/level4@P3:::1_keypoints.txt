- Introduces an amortized MAP framework for SR with an affine projection layer and three methods (AffGAN, AffDG, AffLL); AffGAN yields visually superior results on real images.  
- Decision: Reject — interesting theory and solid technical work but insufficient novelty justification and weak empirical evaluation.  
- Main critique: Insufficient differentiation from Ledig et al. (2016) GAN-based SR; affine projection’s claimed advantage not shown to give measurable performance gains.  
- Experiments are limited: tested on ImageNet only, missing standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) and quantitative metrics (PSNR/SSIM).  
- Visual comparisons are promising but lack corresponding quantitative evaluation on conventional datasets.  
- Improvement 1: Add comprehensive quantitative comparisons on Set5, Set14, BSD100 with PSNR and SSIM alongside visual results.  
- Improvement 2: Clarify practical necessity of the affine projection layer and provide an ablation comparing AffGAN to standard GAN-based SR.  
- Improvement 3: Show scalability to larger image resolutions beyond the presented 128×128 results.  
- Question 1: How does AffGAN specifically improve upon Ledig et al.’s GAN-SR, and what measurable gains stem from the affine projection?  
- Question 2: Clarify the claim of reduced paired-data requirement given Appendix B indicates the affine projection needs paired HR-LR data.  
- Question 3: Why was no noise latent vector used in the GAN framework; was this considered to better model the distribution?  
- Question 4: Why did AffDG and AffLL produce blurry results compared to AffGAN; are there theoretical reasons for their underperformance?  
- Overall: Theoretical perspective is interesting but the paper lacks clear empirical evidence of superiority over state-of-the-art; addressing the above issues could make it a valuable contribution.
