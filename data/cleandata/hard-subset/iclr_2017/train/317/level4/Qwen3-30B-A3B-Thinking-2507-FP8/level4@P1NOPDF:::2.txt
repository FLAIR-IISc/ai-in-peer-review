

Apologies for the delayed review.  
This paper frames super-resolution as amortized maximum a posteriori (MAP) estimation, introducing a projection step to preserve high-resolution (HR)–low-resolution (LR) dependencies. The authors demonstrate through experiments that this approach consistently improves results across benchmarks. Additionally, three distinct methods for solving the optimization problem in Equation 9 are proposed and validated.  

Summary: This is a strong, well-written paper with compelling experiments. The authors present well-chosen toy examples and meaningful real-world applications. The contributions in Sections 3.2, 3.3, and 3.4 appear novel to the super-resolution field, though certain aspects of the GAN training methodology may resemble prior work in different contexts (as noted in the discussion). The finding that GAN-based models yield superior visual quality suggests promising directions for future research.  

The manuscript requires one final proofread to address a few minor typos.
