- Introduces amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution to overcome MSE-induced blurriness.  
- Argues MSE averages the posterior while MAP selects the mode, better matching natural image priors.  
- Proposes a CNN with an affine projection layer to enforce LR-HR consistency in outputs.  
- Reformulates MAP as minimizing cross-entropy between model output distribution and the natural image prior, enabling training without paired HR-LR data.  
- Presents three optimization methods: AffGAN (GAN + affine projection), AffDG (denoising-gradient estimates), and AffLL (maximum-likelihood density model).  
- Empirical results show AffGAN achieves the best perceptual quality on real-image datasets.  
- Establishes a theoretical connection between GANs and amortized variational inference.  
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Highlights the affine projection layer as an elegant solution to the SR consistency constraint.  
- Demonstrates convincingly that AffGAN outperforms MSE-based methods in perceptual quality.  
- Suggests polishing writing and restructuring dense theoretical derivations for clarity.  
- Requests deeper analysis of features learned by the networks to justify reliance on natural image statistics.  
- Calls for a more thorough discussion of MAP limitations, especially mode dependence on representation and the role of affine projection.  
- Asks for more analysis explaining why AffGAN produces sharper results than AffDG and AffLL.  
- Seeks an intuitive explanation why the optimal deconvolution A+ is not simply the transpose of A and its architectural implications.  
- Requests elaboration on the snake→water example: why the model favors water as a more probable HR solution.  
- Asks why AffDG and AffLL produced blurry results and whether this is fundamental or due to implementation.  
- Overall assessment: strong, theoretically grounded contribution with compelling results, needing clarity and additional analysis.
