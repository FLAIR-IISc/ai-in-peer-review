- Paper proposes an amortized MAP framework for image super-resolution with an affine projection layer to ensure LR-HR consistency.  
- Authors present three optimization variants: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided), with AffGAN producing the most visually pleasing outputs.  
- Claim: affine projection transforms SR into minimizing cross-entropy with the natural image prior and enables training without paired HR-LR data.  
- Recommendation: reject — interesting theory and solid visuals but insufficient novelty and incomplete empirical evaluation.  
- Main criticism: approach is not well-distinguished from prior GAN-based SR (Ledig et al. 2016); AffGAN is nearly identical in practice.  
- The affine projection layer is interesting theoretically but the paper does not clearly demonstrate measurable improvements over existing methods.  
- The claimed connection between GANs and amortized variational inference is presented as a minor theoretical extension rather than a substantial contribution.  
- Empirical evaluation omits standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100), preventing fair comparison to prior work.  
- Experiments are limited to 128×128 images; scalability to larger, real-world image sizes (e.g., 256×256+) is not demonstrated.  
- Paper lacks analysis on whether adding noise vectors (as in standard GANs) would improve performance.  
- Request to authors to clearly articulate the technical novelty over Ledig et al. and provide quantitative benchmark comparisons.  
- Request to include results on Set5, Set14, BSD100 and larger image sizes to evaluate practical impact and scalability.  
- Request for perceptual metrics or human evaluation to substantiate claims that AffGAN produces “sharper” images.  
- Clarification needed on whether the affine projection layer truly removes the need for paired HR-LR data, since appendix suggests paired data may be required for training.
