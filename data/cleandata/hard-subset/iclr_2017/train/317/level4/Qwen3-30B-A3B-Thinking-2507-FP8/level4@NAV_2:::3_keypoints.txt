1. Proposes a novel SR method framing super-resolution as amortized MAP inference with a network that projects outputs to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions, yielding three implementations (GAN-, denoiser-, density-guided) and showing AffGAN gives perceptually superior results to MSE.

2. Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — significant contribution addressing MSE blurriness via principled use of natural image statistics and a useful theoretical GAN ↔ amortized variational inference connection.

3. Theory and experiments are well-motivated: affine projection enforces LR–HR consistency, methods minimize cross-entropy (Table 1), AffGAN approaches MAP closely, and results are strong across multiple datasets with Appendix F supporting the theory.

4. Presentation needs clearer technical exposition of the affine projection and the three methods, plus more visualizations and a more systematic explanation to aid accessibility.

5. Requests additional analysis of features learned by AffGAN versus MSE-based models, clarifying which image-prior aspects the affine projection captures and how those features drive perceptual improvements.
