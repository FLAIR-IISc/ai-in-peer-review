

This paper proposes a novel perspective on super-resolution by framing it as amortized maximum a posteriori (MAP) estimation. The authors introduce a projection step to preserve high-resolution (HR) and low-resolution (LR) dependencies, which they rigorously demonstrate improves performance across multiple benchmarks. Additionally, three distinct approaches for solving the cross-entropy optimization problem (Eq. 9) are developed and empirically evaluated.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented. Experimental results are comprehensive, supported by well-chosen toy examples and compelling real-world applications. The core contributions (Sections 3.2–3.4) represent meaningful advances for the super-resolution field. While certain GAN-specific training techniques may have appeared in prior work, their novel application within the SR context, particularly the proposed projection mechanism, constitutes a significant contribution. The finding that GAN-based training yields superior visual quality provides a strong foundation for future research directions in perceptual super-resolution.

The manuscript is of high quality, but a final proofread is recommended to address a few minor typos and grammatical inconsistencies. These are trivial corrections that would further enhance the paper's professionalism.

Overall, this is a strong submission that advances the state of the art in super-resolution. The theoretical insight, methodological innovation, and thorough evaluation make it highly relevant to the ICLR community and likely to influence future work in the field. I recommend acceptance.
