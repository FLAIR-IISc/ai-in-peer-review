- Proposes a novel SR framework based on amortized MAP inference, moving beyond pixel-wise empirical risk minimization.
- Introduces a neural architecture with an affine projection layer to ensure consistency between LR inputs and HR outputs.
- Frames SR as cross-entropy minimization between the model output distribution and a natural image prior.
- Presents three approximation approaches: affine-projected GAN (AffGAN), denoiser-guided SR (AffDG), and density-guided SR (AffLL).
- AffGAN achieves superior perceptual quality on real-world datasets and lower cross-entropy than MSE-based methods.
- Provides theoretical connections to variational inference and MAP, with rigorous analysis.
- Toy 2D Swiss-roll experiments show the methods find the posterior mode; natural image results are visually superior.
- Recommends acceptance due to significant theoretical contribution and clear empirical advantages of AffGAN.
- Suggests explicitly comparing to recent SOTA SR methods (e.g., ESRGAN, RCAN) on standard benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100).
- Requests clarification of the affine projection layer training procedure and whether it requires HR/LR pairs distinct from the main SR network.
- Recommends evaluating scalability on larger image sizes beyond the presented 128×128 results.
- Asks how AffGAN advances beyond Ledig et al. (2016) both theoretically and practically.
- Queries whether instance noise in AffGAN biases the learned distribution compared to other GAN stabilization techniques.
- Requests results on standard SR benchmarks to enable direct comparison with prior work.
