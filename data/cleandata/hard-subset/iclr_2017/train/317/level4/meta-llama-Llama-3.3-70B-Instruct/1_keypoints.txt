- Proposes an innovative amortized MAP inference framework with a pre-learned affine projection layer to enforce LR consistency; combining it with GANs yields plausible SR results.
- Clarify novelty: need explicit comparison and explanation of what new insights this formulation adds relative to existing GAN-based SR methods (e.g., SRGAN).
- The affine projection architecture claims to remove the need for HR–LR pairs overall, but training the affine projection layer itself still requires pairs — unclear if this merely shifts the training burden.
- Provide results on standard SR testsets (Set5, Set14, BSD100) to enable direct comparison with prior work.
- Assess scalability: current natural-image results are limited to 128×128 — evaluate performance on larger, arbitrary-sized images common in SR applications.
- Investigate and report whether a noise latent vector was used in the GAN component, and if not, explore its effect on modeling output distributions.
- Overall assessment: paper is well-written, theoretically rigorous, and innovative, but needs clarifications and additional experiments to fully validate impact.
