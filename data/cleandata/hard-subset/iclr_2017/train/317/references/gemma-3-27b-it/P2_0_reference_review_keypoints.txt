- Proposes framing image super-resolution as amortized MAP inference using an affine projection layer and three MAP-approximation methods (GAN-, denoising-, density-guided).  
- Decision: Reject — interesting theory and initial results but insufficient experimental rigor and unclear practical gains.  
- Limited quantitative validation: image SR metrics (PSNR/SSIM) show small or inconsistent improvements that don't justify added complexity.  
- Insufficient baseline comparison: lacks comparisons to strong recent SR methods like EDSR, RCAN, or RDN.  
- Weak ablation and hyperparameter analysis: affine projection shown helpful but sensitivity to hyperparameters, architectures, and instance noise is not explored.  
- Overreliance on qualitative results: visual comparisons are subjective and not backed by robust perceptual metrics.  
- Theory–practice gap: theoretical MAP/cross-entropy framing doesn’t clearly translate to substantial improvements in high-dimensional image tasks.  
- Suggest including stronger baselines (EDSR/RCAN/RDN) in experiments.  
- Recommend additional perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS) beyond PSNR/SSIM.  
- Request a comprehensive ablation study for each component (affine projection, loss variants, instance noise) and network configs.  
- Ask for detailed hyperparameter tuning procedures and robustness checks.  
- Advise analyzing failure cases and limitations (e.g., plausible but incorrect reconstructions).  
- Ask to clarify and expand the connection to variational inference—whether it constitutes true amortized VI or a related observation.  
- Q1: Why were recent state-of-the-art SR architectures (EDSR, RCAN, RDN) omitted from comparisons?  
- Q2: What are the computational costs (training/inference time) versus standard MSE-trained SR CNNs?  
- Q3: How were training instabilities for AffDG and AffLL mitigated in practice?  
- Q4: How exactly does the AffGAN objective relate to the ELBO in variational inference and what approximations are used?  
- Q5: How sensitive is AffGAN to the choice and annealing schedule of instance noise?
