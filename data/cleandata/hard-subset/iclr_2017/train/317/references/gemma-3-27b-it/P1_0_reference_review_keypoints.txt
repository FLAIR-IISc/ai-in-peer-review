- Proposal: Amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution using an affine-projection CNN and three MAP-approximation methods (AffGAN, AffDG, AffLL), with AffGAN performing best.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Motivation: MSE-based SR yields blurry outputs; MAP prioritizes high-posterior solutions and avoids blurriness (demonstrated on a toy problem).
- Novelty: Introduction of an affine projection layer that enforces LR–HR consistency and simplifies optimization.
- Methods: Three approaches to approximate MAP — GAN-based (AffGAN), denoising-guided (AffDG), and density-guided (AffLL).
- Empirical results: AffGAN improves perceptual quality and achieves cross-entropy close to theoretical MAP; validated on 2D Swiss-roll and real-image SR datasets with PSNR/SSIM and visual comparisons.
- Theoretical grounding: Strong connections to MAP and variational inference; links to GANs and justification of instance noise are well-explained.
- Clarify affine projection: Request clearer implementation details and an intuitive explanation of how the pseudoinverse A+ is computed relative to the downsampling kernel A.
- Ablation studies: Recommend isolating the effect of instance noise (e.g., without affine projection) and other component-wise ablations.
- Discussion of limitations: Ask for expanded discussion on when MAP may be suboptimal and potential drawbacks of the affine projection.
- Visual comparisons: Suggest side-by-side comparisons with more recent state-of-the-art SR methods.
- Computational cost: Request analysis of training/inference cost compared to standard SR approaches.
- Question on A+: How sensitive is performance to inaccuracies in the pseudoinverse A+, and is there a degradation threshold?
- Question on AffDG/AffLL: What specific instabilities were encountered, why were they not pursued further, and were alternative optimization strategies tried?
- Question on instance noise: How sensitive are results to the annealing schedule of instance noise and are there dataset-specific guidelines?
- Question on posterior sampling: Have methods for sampling from the posterior (vs. MAP) been explored and compared to AffGAN’s MAP estimate?
- Question on scaling: Is AffGAN’s performance consistent across downsampling factors (e.g., 2x to 8x), and are there limitations as downsampling increases?
