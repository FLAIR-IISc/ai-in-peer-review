- Summary: Frames super-resolution as amortised MAP inference, introduces an affine projection layer, and proposes AffGAN, AffDG, and AffLL to approximate MAP; AffGAN yields visually appealing results.  
- Decision: Recommend accept with minor revisions due to solid theory and convincing qualitative results.  
- Strong theoretical motivation: Clear argument that MSE causes blurriness and that MAP inference with affine projection is well-founded.  
- Novelty: Combining affine projection with GAN-, denoising-, and density-based approaches is a new direction.  
- Convincing experiments: Swiss-roll toy and real-image experiments show benefits of MAP and AffGAN’s sharper, plausible outputs.  
- Insightful analysis: Thorough comparison of methods and discussion of strengths/weaknesses.  
- Variational link: Identifies a useful connection between AffGAN and amortised variational inference.  
- Quantitative comparisons lacking: PSNR/SSIM sometimes not competitive; recommend more comparisons with perceptual-loss SR models and standard datasets (Set5, Set14, BSD100).  
- Perceptual metrics: Suggest evaluating with modern perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS) to better reflect visual quality.  
- Instance noise explanation: Instance noise stabilisation is promising but needs clearer description and justification of annealing schedule.  
- Ablation studies needed: More detailed ablations to isolate contributions of affine projection, instance noise, and other components.  
- Computational cost: Missing analysis of runtime and resource cost, especially for AffGAN versus standard SR models.  
- Limitations: Expand discussion on potential mode collapse, how affine projection might reduce diversity or introduce artifacts.  
- Sensitivity questions: Ask for sensitivity analysis on instance-noise hyperparameters and annealing schedules.  
- Affine projection questions: Request elaboration on scenarios where affine projection could hinder performance or create artifacts.  
- Future work / stochastic AffGAN: Ask for details and preliminary results on the stochastic/variational interpretation and benefits.  
- Failure cases: Request examples and characterization of cases where AffGAN fails to produce plausible SR results.
