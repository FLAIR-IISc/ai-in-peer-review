- Paper frames image super-resolution as amortized MAP inference and proposes an affine projection layer plus three MAP-approximation methods (AffGAN, AffDG, AffLL).  
- Main contributions claimed: affine projection, theoretical link between GANs and MAP inference, and empirical demonstration favoring AffGAN for perceptual quality.  
- Decision: Reject due to incomplete empirical validation and insufficient evidence of practical benefits relative to complexity.  
- Limited empirical validation: quantitative results are inconclusive and rely too much on subjective visual comparisons.  
- AffGAN shows improvement on a texture dataset but comparable or worse PSNR/SSIM on CelebA versus standard methods.  
- Inconsistent claims: perceptual superiority is not fully supported by metrics and outputs exhibit high-frequency noise and semantically incorrect “dreamed” details.  
- Complexity vs. benefit: affine projection and theoretical additions increase system complexity without clear, decisive gains.  
- Unclear viability of alternatives: AffDG and AffLL produced blurry results and were insufficiently analyzed or salvaged.  
- Recommendation: compare to stronger SR baselines (EDSR, RCAN, SwinIR) and report LPIPS and other perceptual metrics.  
- Recommendation: run a perceptual user study to substantiate perceptual quality claims.  
- Recommendation: perform a more detailed ablation isolating components (instance noise, affine projection, etc.) and address high-frequency noise.  
- Recommendation: provide deeper analysis of why AffDG/AffLL failed and explore potential fixes.  
- Recommendation: clarify and contextualize the variational-inference connection and detail training hyperparameters and compute.  
- Questions for authors: how to prevent GAN from generating semantically incorrect outputs, sensitivity to noise distribution/annealing, reasons AffDG/AffLL were abandoned, computational overhead of affine projection, and whether advanced GAN training techniques were considered.
