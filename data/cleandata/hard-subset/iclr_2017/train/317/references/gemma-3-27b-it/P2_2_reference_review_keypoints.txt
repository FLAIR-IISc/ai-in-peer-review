1. Proposes amortised MAP inference for SR using an affine projection layer and three methods (AffGAN, AffDG, AffLL).  
2. Decision: Reject.  
3. Empirical gains are limited: AffGAN shows some perceptual improvement but not consistently reflected in PSNR/SSIM.  
4. Comparisons are incomplete; lacks benchmarking against state-of-the-art SR methods (e.g., EDSR, RCAN).  
5. AffDG and AffLL suffer from significant training instability that undermines practicality.  
6. Theoretical contributions are interesting but do not convincingly translate to practical improvements.  
7. The 2D Swiss Roll toy experiment is insufficient to demonstrate generalization to complex natural images.  
8. No detailed ablation studies to isolate the impact of the affine projection across architectures and training regimes.  
9. Needs more robust evaluation on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100, Urban100).  
10. Requires systematic subjective evaluation (e.g., user study) to validate perceptual quality claims.  
11. More analysis is needed on causes of instability and potential mitigation strategies.  
12. The connection to VAEs in the appendix is intriguing but underdeveloped and needs expansion.  
13. Limitations discussion should be expanded to cover computational cost and scalability of the affine projection.  
14. Reviewer asks for detailed training time and compute comparison between AffGAN and comparable GANs without affine projection.  
15. Reviewer requests which hyperparameters were tuned for AffDG/AffLL and sensitivity analysis of those settings.  
16. Reviewer asks whether other stabilization techniques (gradient clipping, spectral normalization) were tried beyond annealing.  
17. Reviewer asks how the affine projection interacts with convolutional receptive fields and whether it limits long-range dependency modeling.
