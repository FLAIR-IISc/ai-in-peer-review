## ICLR 2024 Review: Amortised MAP Inference for Image Super-Resolution

**1. Summary:**

This paper tackles the problem of image super-resolution (SR) by framing it as an amortized Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference problem. The authors argue that minimizing pixel-wise losses like MSE leads to blurry results due to averaging over plausible solutions, and propose methods to directly optimize for high posterior probability. They introduce an affine projection layer to enforce consistency between LR and HR images and then explore three approaches for MAP inference: a GAN-based method (AffGAN), a denoising-guided method (AffDG), and a density-guided method (AffLL). The paper provides both theoretical grounding and empirical validation on synthetic and real image datasets, demonstrating promising results, particularly with the AffGAN approach.

**2. Decision:**

**Reject.** While the paper presents an interesting and theoretically sound approach, the empirical results, particularly on real image datasets, are not convincingly superior to existing methods. The improvements demonstrated are often marginal and the paper lacks a comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art SR techniques. Furthermore, the experimental section feels somewhat fragmented and lacks a clear, unifying narrative.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

* **Limited Empirical Advantage:** The primary justification for the proposed methods should be a clear perceptual or quantitative improvement over existing SR techniques. While the 2D Swiss-roll experiment provides a nice illustration of the MAP principle, the results on real image datasets (CelebA, ImageNet) are not compelling. Figures 4 and 5 show improvements, but they appear subtle, and the quantitative results in Table 2 show that the MSE-trained model often performs better in terms of PSNR/SSIM.
* **Insufficient Comparison to State-of-the-Art:** The paper primarily compares against MSE-trained models.  A more rigorous evaluation requires a comparison with recent, high-performing SR models that utilize perceptual losses (e.g., Enhanced Deep Residual Networks for Single Image Super-Resolution – EDSR, Real-ESRGAN, or similar). Without this, it's difficult to assess the true value of the proposed methods.
* **Fragmented Experimental Narrative:** The experiments feel disjointed. The progression from the toy problem to grass textures, CelebA, and ImageNet isn’t clearly justified, and the explanation of why AffDG and AffLL were largely abandoned is brief and unconvincing. More thorough analysis of their failures would strengthen the paper.
* **Overly Complex Presentation:** The theoretical sections are dense and difficult to follow without significant background knowledge. While the mathematical formulation is appreciated, the explanations could benefit from more intuitive explanations and visual aids.

**4. Additional Feedback (for potential improvement):**

* **Strengthen Empirical Evaluation:** Perform a comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art SR models using standard evaluation metrics (PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS) *and* perceptual studies (e.g., user studies).
* **Investigate AffDG and AffLL Further:**  Provide a more in-depth analysis of why these methods underperformed. Was it a training instability issue, architectural limitations, or something else?
* **Clarify the Role of the Affine Projection:** While the theoretical justification for the affine projection is sound, its practical impact is not fully explored.  Ablation studies examining the effect of removing the projection layer would be helpful.
* **Simplify the Presentation:**  Break down the theoretical sections into smaller, more digestible chunks.  Use more visual aids to illustrate key concepts.
* **Address the Limitation of MAP Inference:** The discussion in Section 5.6 regarding the potential limitations of MAP inference is good, but could be expanded.  Discuss potential strategies for mitigating these limitations (e.g., using a sampling-based approach).



**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  Why was a more extensive comparison to state-of-the-art SR methods not included in the experimental evaluation?
2.  Could you provide more insight into the reasons for the poor performance of the AffDG and AffLL models? Were there specific hyperparameter settings or training procedures that could have improved their performance?
3.  How sensitive are the results to the choice of the Gaussian kernel used in the affine projection?  Have you explored alternative kernel functions?
4.  In the discussion of MAP inference limitations, you mention the potential for the mode to be atypical.  How do you address this concern in the context of image super-resolution?
5.  The paper briefly mentions a connection to variational inference. Could you elaborate on how AffGAN can be interpreted as performing amortized variational inference, and what the implications of this connection are?
