- Paper proposes amortized MAP inference for super-resolution using an affine-projected CNN and three MAP approximations (GAN-, denoising-, and density-based).  
- Decision: Reject due to limited empirical gains and training instability.  
- Main claim of superior MAP-based SR is not convincingly supported on real datasets; improvements are modest beyond a 2D toy.  
- Qualitative results are not consistently better than state-of-the-art and sometimes worse (e.g., Figure 5 snake).  
- Quantitative results are competitive but do not surpass standard methods (Table 2).  
- Training instability is a major issue, especially for denoising and density-guided methods; instance noise helps GANs but not clearly others.  
- Heavy hyperparameter tuning and annealing schedules are required, raising practicality concerns.  
- Variational inference connection in the appendix is interesting but underdeveloped and lacks empirical validation.  
- Paper lacks comparisons to strong modern SR baselines using perceptual losses and advanced GAN architectures (e.g., ESRGAN).  
- Need clearer discussion and analysis of the affine projection’s practical impact, artifacts, and receptive field effects.  
- Request more comprehensive ablation studies to isolate contributions of affine projection, instance noise, and loss choices.  
- Recommend adding perceptual-correlated metrics (e.g., LPIPS) alongside PSNR/SSIM for evaluation.  
- Authors should report computational cost and practicality compared to existing SR methods.  
- Suggest expanding discussion on MAP limitations and exploring alternatives like posterior sampling.  
- Questions: why does AffGAN produce consistently sharper images than AffDG/AffLL, and which GAN training aspects cause this?  
- Questions: sensitivity to noise distribution and annealing schedule for instance noise; have other noise types been tried?  
- Questions: provide thorough comparisons to state-of-the-art SR methods with perceptual losses and advanced GANs.  
- Questions: what practical benefits does the VI connection offer for sampling or uncertainty estimation in SR?
