- Frames image super-resolution as amortized MAP inference using an affine projection and proposes AffGAN, AffDG, and AffLL.  
- Decision: Reject — theoretically interesting but empirically unconvincing and insufficiently compared to SOTA.  
- AffGAN shows promise, but improvements on real datasets are marginal and subtle.  
- MSE-trained models often outperform the proposed methods on PSNR/SSIM.  
- Lacks comparison to state-of-the-art perceptual SR methods (e.g., EDSR, Real-ESRGAN).  
- Experimental narrative is fragmented and dataset progression (toy → textures → CelebA → ImageNet) is not well justified.  
- AffDG and AffLL were largely abandoned without thorough analysis of why they failed.  
- Theoretical sections are dense and need more intuitive explanations and visual aids.  
- Recommend strengthening empirical evaluation with PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS and perceptual/user studies.  
- Recommend deeper investigation into AffDG/AffLL failure modes (training stability, hyperparameters, architecture).  
- Recommend ablation studies to clarify the practical impact of the affine projection layer.  
- Suggest simplifying presentation by breaking theory into smaller parts and adding illustrations.  
- Suggest expanding discussion of MAP inference limitations and mitigation strategies (e.g., sampling).  
- Question: Why was there no extensive comparison to state-of-the-art SR methods?  
- Question: What specifically caused poor AffDG/AffLL performance—could hyperparameters or training procedures help?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to the choice of Gaussian kernel in the affine projection, and were alternatives explored?  
- Question: How is the concern that MAP may select atypical modes addressed in the SR context?  
- Question: Can you elaborate on interpreting AffGAN as amortized variational inference and its implications?
