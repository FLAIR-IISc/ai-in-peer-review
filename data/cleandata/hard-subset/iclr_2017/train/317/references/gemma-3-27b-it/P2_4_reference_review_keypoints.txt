- Summary: Proposes amortized MAP inference for super-resolution with a CNN containing an affine projection layer and three MAP-approximation methods (AffGAN, AffDG, AffLL), backed by theory and experiments.  
- Decision: Reject — interesting theory but empirical gains and practical benefits are insufficient for acceptance.  
- Modest empirical gains: Quantitative improvements are marginal and visual claims lack objective perceptual metrics.  
- High-frequency artifacts: AffGAN results exhibit notable high-frequency noise that undermines perceptual quality.  
- Unclear benefit of affine projection: Experiments do not convincingly show the necessity or practical advantage of enforcing the affine constraint.  
- Complexity vs. gain: Theoretical development is heavy but does not translate to substantial improvement over existing SR methods.  
- Dismissal of other approaches: AffDG and AffLL are underperforming and largely sidelined in favor of AffGAN without clear analysis.  
- Inconsistent results: Improvements appear dataset-dependent and failure modes (e.g., hallucinated water) indicate potential mode collapse or bias.  
- Request for perceptual metrics: Include LPIPS, NIQE or human studies to objectively validate perceptual quality claims.  
- Need ablation studies: Isolate contributions of affine projection, instance noise, and other components to quantify their impacts.  
- Compare to SOTA: Benchmark against recent strong SR methods (e.g., RCAN, ESRGAN) for a competitive baseline.  
- Clarify affine projection rationale: Provide clearer explanation and stronger empirical evidence for why exact affine constraint helps.  
- Investigate AffDG/AffLL: Analyze why these methods fail and explore improvements or reasons for their poor performance.  
- Expand variational connection: Integrate and elaborate the link to variational inference in the main text rather than only the conclusion.  
- Questions for authors: What is the computational overhead of the affine projection? How sensitive are results to noise distribution/annealing? Why did AffDG/AffLL underperform? Any quantitative human/perceptual evidence for plausibility?
