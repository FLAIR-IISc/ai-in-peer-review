- Paper frames super-resolution as amortised MAP inference using affine projections and proposes AffGAN, AffDG, and AffLL.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Empirical gains are limited; improvements are moderate and not clearly perceptually superior.  
- MSE-trained model still achieves best PSNR/SSIM, and perceptual claims lack robust evidence.  
- Affine projection adds architectural/theoretical complexity without clear benefit on real images.  
- AffGAN shows promise but training instability and high-frequency noise raise robustness concerns.  
- Comparison is incomplete — lacks evaluation against modern perceptual SR methods (e.g., ESRGAN).  
- The theoretical link to Variational Inference is interesting but its practical implications are unclear.  
- Recommend stronger perceptual evaluation (user study or LPIPS) to substantiate visual quality claims.  
- Recommend ablation studies to isolate the affine projection’s contribution (e.g., GAN/denoising without projection).  
- Suggest analyzing hyperparameter sensitivity, especially for affine projection and instance noise.  
- Recommend broader dataset and scaling factor evaluation (e.g., 2x, 8x) to test generalization.  
- Ask authors to clarify how the VI connection can be leveraged for improvements or insights.  
- Request discussion of computational overhead introduced by the affine projection (training/inference time).  
- Question: provide detailed analysis and mitigation strategies for AffGAN’s high-frequency noise.  
- Question: how sensitive are results to the choice of kernel for the affine projection?  
- Question: what is the computational cost compared to standard SR models?  
- Question: what instance noise parameters/annealing schedule helped stabilize AffGAN training?  
- Question: why was the density-guided approach (AffLL) deprioritized and what challenges were encountered?
