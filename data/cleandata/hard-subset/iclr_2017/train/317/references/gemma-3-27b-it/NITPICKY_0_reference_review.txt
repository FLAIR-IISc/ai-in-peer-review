This paper proposes methods for image super-resolution (SR) based on amortized Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference, arguing that minimizing cross-entropy between the HR data distribution and the model's output distribution leads to more plausible results than traditional pixel-wise loss functions like MSE. The core idea is to exploit the affine subspace structure of the SR problem through a specifically designed CNN architecture with an affine projection layer. The authors present three approaches: a GAN-based method (AffGAN), a denoising-guided method (AffDG), and a density-guided method (AffLL). They demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on both a 2D toy dataset and real image datasets.

**Reject.** While the theoretical motivation for MAP inference in SR is sound, and the introduction of the affine projection layer is a potentially valuable contribution, the paper suffers from a multitude of minor flaws, inconsistencies, and a lack of thoroughness in experimental validation. The extensive list of issues, despite the potentially interesting core idea, leads to a lack of confidence in the reliability and impact of the presented work.

**Supporting Arguments:**

1.  **Inconsistent Reporting and Lack of Statistical Rigor:** Table 1 presents cross-entropy values with varying levels of precision (e.g., 3.15 vs. 1.25e-02). Furthermore, the lack of standard deviations or error bars for these results raises concerns about the reproducibility and statistical significance of the reported improvements. The "0.0" values are particularly suspicious and require clarification.
2.  **Insufficient Justification for Architectural Choices:** The paper claims the affine projection layer improves performance, but the reasoning for the specific deconvolution kernel chosen (Figure 2) is not adequately explained. Why is the transposed convolution used as the Moore-Penrose inverse instead of a simpler, more direct implementation? The claim about initialisation speed advantage is not convincingly demonstrated.
3.  **Unclear Experimental Setup & Ablation Studies:** The experimental details in Section 4 are lacking. What specific network architectures were used for each method (GAN, Denoising, Density)? What were the hyperparameter settings? The ablation study comparing AffGAN and SoftGAN is insufficient – a more detailed analysis of the impact of the affine projection is needed. Why were the denoiser and density guided models dropped from further experimentation?
4.  **Ambiguous and Overstated Claims:** Phrases like "AffGAN produces clearly the sharpest images" (Section 5.3) are subjective and lack quantitative support. Similarly, claiming the AffGAN model produces "slightly sharper images" (Section 5.4) requires a more rigorous perceptual evaluation beyond PSNR/SSIM.
5.  **Theoretical Section is Dense and Difficult to Follow:** While the mathematical derivation in Section 3 is ambitious, it’s presented in a very dense and challenging manner. The connection to variational inference (Appendix F) is mentioned briefly but not fully explored or demonstrated empirically. The discussion on the limitations of MAP inference (Section 5.6) feels tacked on and insufficiently developed.
6.  **Formatting and Presentation Issues:** Numerous minor formatting issues throughout the paper (e.g., inconsistent use of boldface, equation numbering) detract from the overall professionalism and readability.

**Additional Feedback:**

*   **Clarify the Noise Distribution:** The choice of Gaussian instance noise (Section 3.2.1) is not sufficiently justified. Explain why this distribution was chosen and whether other distributions were considered.
*   **Detailed Architectural Specifications:** Provide a complete and detailed description of all network architectures used, including the number of layers, filter sizes, activation functions, and other relevant parameters.
*   **Quantitative Perceptual Evaluation:** Incorporate a perceptual quality metric (e.g., LPIPS) to provide a more reliable assessment of the visual quality of the generated images.
*   **Expand the Ablation Study:** Conduct a more comprehensive ablation study to systematically evaluate the impact of different components of the proposed method, including the affine projection layer, the instance noise, and the choice of loss function.
*   **Address the Concerns about the MAP Assumption:** Elaborate on the potential limitations of MAP inference, especially in the context of natural images.
*   **Fix Formatting Errors:** Carefully proofread the paper to correct all formatting errors and ensure consistency.

**Questions for the Authors:**

*   Can you provide standard deviations or error bars for the results presented in Table 1?
*   Why was the transposed convolution chosen as the Moore-Penrose inverse, and what are the implications of this choice?
*   What were the specific hyperparameter settings used for all experiments?
*   Can you provide a more detailed justification for the choice of Gaussian instance noise?
*   What is the computational cost overhead of the affine projection layer, and how does it affect training time?
*   Regarding the snake-to-water super-resolution example (Section 5.5), can you explain why the model made this specific hallucination and what implications it has for the reliability of the method?
