## ICLR 2024 Review: Amortised MAP Inference for Image Super-Resolution

**1. Summary:**

This paper tackles the problem of image super-resolution (SR), arguing that relying solely on empirical risk minimization with pixel-wise losses (like MSE) leads to blurry results due to ignoring the underlying image prior. The authors propose a shift towards amortised Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference, favoring solutions with higher posterior probability. They introduce a CNN architecture leveraging affine projections to ensure consistency between LR and HR images, and then explore three methods to approximate MAP inference: a GAN-based approach (AffGAN), a denoising-guided method (AffDG), and a density-guided method (AffLL).  The paper demonstrates, through theoretical analysis and experiments, that AffGAN achieves results competitive with state-of-the-art while offering advantages in consistency and plausibility. A particularly interesting connection is established between AffGAN and variational inference.

**2. Decision:**

**Reject.** While the paper presents an interesting theoretical framework and demonstrates promising results, particularly with AffGAN, the empirical validation feels incomplete and lacks a sufficiently compelling demonstration of superiority over existing GAN-based SR methods. The results, while promising, are not consistently superior and the theoretical justifications, while clever, are not fully translated into practical gains demonstrated through rigorous quantitative evaluation.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

* **Limited Comparative Analysis:** The primary comparison is against MSE and MAE, which are already known to be suboptimal for perceptual quality. A stronger evaluation would involve a comprehensive comparison against state-of-the-art GAN-based SR methods (e.g., ESRGAN, Real-ESRGAN) using standard metrics *and* perceptual quality assessments (e.g., LPIPS, NIQE). The comparisons provided in Figures 4 and 5 don't provide a convincing advantage for AffGAN over stronger GAN baselines.
* **Inconsistent Performance:**  The results on grass textures are presented as a strong win for AffGAN, but the DAE and density guided approaches are quickly dismissed. The reasons for their failure are briefly mentioned but not thoroughly investigated. Furthermore, the reported high-frequency noise in the ImageNet results, while acknowledged, isn't adequately addressed or mitigated. This raises concerns about the robustness of the proposed approach.
* **Theoretical Claims vs. Practical Impact:** The theoretical justification for MAP inference is well-presented, and the connection to variational inference is intriguing. However, the paper doesn't fully explain *why* AffGAN achieves the observed performance gains based on these theoretical foundations.  How does the affine projection specifically enable better exploration of the posterior distribution, and how does this translate into improved perceptual quality?
* **Implementation Details & Reproducibility:**  While Appendix D provides some details, the implementation is complex with several hyperparameters. It would be extremely valuable to provide code or at least more detailed hyperparameter settings to enable reproducibility and facilitate further research.

**4. Additional Feedback (For Improvement):**

* **More Rigorous Evaluation:**  A comprehensive comparison against state-of-the-art GAN-based SR methods is crucial. Include quantitative metrics (PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS, NIQE) and subjective evaluations (user studies).
* **Ablation Studies:**  Perform ablation studies to quantify the contribution of the affine projection.  What is the performance gain achieved solely by enforcing the consistency constraint?
* **Address High-Frequency Noise:** Investigate and mitigate the high-frequency noise observed in the ImageNet results.  Explore techniques like spectral normalization or alternative GAN training strategies.
* **Expand on Failure Modes:** Investigate why the DAE and density guided approaches failed to produce competitive results. This could reveal valuable insights into the limitations of the proposed framework.
* **Clarify Theoretical Link to Performance:** Elaborate on the connection between the theoretical advantages of MAP inference and the observed empirical results.  Specifically, explain how the affine projection enables better posterior exploration and perceptual quality.
* **Reproducibility:** Provide code or detailed hyperparameter settings to improve reproducibility.

**5. Questions for the Authors:**

* Could you provide a more detailed comparison of AffGAN against state-of-the-art GAN-based SR methods, including perceptual quality metrics like LPIPS and NIQE?
* What specific modifications were attempted to address the high-frequency noise observed in the ImageNet results, and why were they unsuccessful?
* Could you provide a more detailed explanation of how the affine projection specifically facilitates better exploration of the posterior distribution compared to standard GAN training?
* What computational cost is incurred by adding the affine projection layer to the CNN architecture? Is there a trade-off between performance and computational efficiency?
* The paper briefly mentions a stochastic version of AffGAN connecting it to variational inference. Could you provide more details about this connection and the potential benefits of this stochastic approach?
