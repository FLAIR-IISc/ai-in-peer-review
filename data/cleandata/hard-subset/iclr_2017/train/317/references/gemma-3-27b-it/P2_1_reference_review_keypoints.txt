- Proposal: Move SR from pixel-wise losses to MAP inference using a natural image prior; methods include an affine-projection CNN, AffGAN, AffDG, and AffLL with theoretical and empirical support.
- Decision: Reject — interesting theory but empirical improvements are incremental and not convincing for real images.
- Limited empirical advantage: Strongest results are on a toy 2D Swiss-roll; gains on CelebA/ImageNet are modest and not consistently superior to SRGAN.
- Incomplete baselines: Lacks comparison to strong modern SR methods (EDSR, RCAN, transformer-based models), making contribution unclear.
- Perceptual vs distortion metrics: PSNR/SSIM are worse than MSE baselines, and the claim of better perceptual quality is not well quantified or explained.
- Theory–implementation gap: Heavy theoretical derivations but unclear connection to practical choices (architecture, annealing schedules, hyperparameters).
- Instability and sensitivity: AffDG/AffLL show unstable training; AffGAN requires instance noise for stabilization, suggesting hyperparameter sensitivity and reproducibility issues.
- Clarify advantage: Need clearer explanation of why the approach should outperform existing methods and how affine projection addresses GAN mode collapse.
- More thorough evaluation requested: Add comparisons to stronger baselines and ablation studies to isolate contributions of components (affine projection, instance noise).
- Quantitative perceptual metrics: Recommend adding metrics like LPIPS to objectively assess perceptual quality.
- Explain artifacts: Address observed high-frequency noise in AffGAN outputs and whether it is an inherent limitation.
- Reproducibility: Provide detailed training settings (hyperparameters, optimizers, learning rate schedules).
- Question — expressiveness: Does the affine projection constraint limit network expressiveness and how does it compare to other consistency constraints?
- Question — stabilization: What specific modifications stabilized AffDG/AffLL and how sensitive are results to these changes?
- Question — qualitative comparisons: Provide more ImageNet examples where AffGAN produces clearly more plausible results than standard GAN SR.
- Question — variational link: Clarify the exact variational objective being approximated and how AffGAN relates to VAEs/amortized variational inference.
