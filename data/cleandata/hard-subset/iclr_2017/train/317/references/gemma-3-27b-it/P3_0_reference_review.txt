## ICLR 2024 Review: Amortised MAP Inference for Image Super-Resolution

**1. Summary:**

This paper tackles the problem of image super-resolution (SR), arguing that current methods relying on pixel-wise losses (like MSE) fall short due to their inability to capture the multimodal nature of plausible SR solutions and the underlying image prior. The authors propose a novel approach based on amortised Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference, aiming to find solutions with high posterior probability. They introduce a CNN architecture incorporating an affine projection to enforce consistency between LR and HR estimates, and explore three methods to approximate the MAP objective: a GAN-based approach (AffGAN), a denoising-guided approach (AffDG), and a density-guided approach (AffLL). The paper presents experiments on both a 2D toy dataset and real image datasets, demonstrating promising results, particularly with AffGAN.  A particularly interesting theoretical connection is made to variational inference in the stochastic AffGAN setting.

**2. Decision:**

**Reject** with a strong encouragement to resubmit after substantial revisions. While the paper presents a conceptually interesting and theoretically grounded approach, the empirical evaluation is not sufficiently convincing, and several aspects of the methodology and results require further clarification. The paper feels exploratory and lacks the depth and rigor expected for acceptance at ICLR.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

* **Limited Empirical Validation:** The experiments, while exploring different datasets, do not decisively demonstrate a significant perceptual improvement over existing state-of-the-art methods. The quantitative metrics (PSNR/SSIM) often favour the MSE-trained baseline, which contradicts the core argument of the paper about the limitations of MSE.  The visual examples, while suggestive, are not systematically compared to established SR methods (e.g., EDSR, RCAN, Real-ESRGAN). A more thorough comparative analysis is crucial.
* **Inconsistent Results & Unclear Advantages:** The performance of AffDG and AffLL appears significantly weaker than AffGAN, yet the reasoning for this discrepancy is not explored in detail. The paper acknowledges these issues but doesn’t offer sufficient insight into why AffGAN consistently outperforms the other approaches.  The claimed advantage of affine projection – faster training – is not robustly demonstrated, and the benefit seems limited to the initial training phase.
* **Lack of Ablation Studies:** The impact of the ‘instance noise’ trick on the stability of GAN training isn't adequately substantiated.  A more detailed ablation study, quantifying the effect of noise levels and comparing performance with and without it, is needed.  Similarly, a systematic exploration of different network architectures, loss function weights, and optimization strategies would strengthen the findings.
* **Theoretical Claims Require Strengthening:** While the connection to variational inference is intriguing, it’s presented as a “stochastic version” and requires a more rigorous mathematical derivation. The assertion that AffGAN directly minimizes a variational lower bound needs stronger justification. The discussion around the limitations of MAP inference is valid but feels somewhat tacked on at the end.

**4. Additional Feedback (for improvement):**

* **Detailed Comparison to SOTA:** Include a comprehensive comparison against current state-of-the-art SR methods (e.g., EDSR, RCAN, Real-ESRGAN) using standard evaluation protocols. This is essential to establish the true value of the proposed approach.
* **Perceptual Metrics:** Beyond PSNR/SSIM, incorporate perceptual metrics like LPIPS to assess the perceptual quality of the generated images more accurately.
* **Clarify Affine Projection Benefits:**  Provide a clearer explanation of *why* the affine projection consistently improves performance, particularly in relation to the GAN-based approach.  What specific properties of the affine constraint contribute to better generation?
* **Investigate AffDG/AffLL Performance:** Conduct a more in-depth investigation into the inferior performance of AffDG and AffLL. Is it due to gradient issues, density model limitations, or other factors?
* **Expand Ablation Studies:**  Include ablation studies for the instance noise trick, network architecture choices, and optimization parameters.
* **Strengthen Variational Inference Connection:** Provide a more detailed mathematical derivation of the connection between AffGAN and variational inference.
* **Address Potential Mode Collapse:** GANs are prone to mode collapse. How does the proposed method mitigate this issue, and is there evidence of mode collapse in the generated images?
* **Discuss Computational Cost:** While faster training is mentioned, provide a more detailed analysis of the computational cost of the proposed methods compared to existing approaches.



**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  The paper states that AffGAN achieves cross-entropy values close to the MAP solution on the 2D toy problem. Could you provide a more detailed explanation of how the MAP solution was obtained in this case for direct comparison?
2.  The results on natural images (Figure 5) show some artifacts.  Could you analyze these artifacts and discuss potential strategies to mitigate them?
3.  What specific architectural choices or hyperparameters were found to be most critical for achieving stable training and good performance with AffGAN?
4.  Why do the quantitative metrics (PSNR/SSIM) sometimes *worse* for AffGAN compared to the MSE baseline, despite the qualitative improvements observed in some examples?
5.  Could you elaborate on the choice of the MCGSM as the generative model in AffLL?  Were other density models considered, and why was MCGSM chosen?  What are its limitations in the context of this application?




In conclusion, the paper explores an interesting direction for improving image super-resolution. However, the lack of comprehensive empirical validation and the unclear advantages of the proposed methods prevent its acceptance in its current form. Addressing the concerns outlined above would significantly strengthen the paper and increase its potential for a successful resubmission.
