- Proposes amortised MAP inference for image super-resolution using an affine-projected CNN and three MAP approximations: AffGAN, AffDG, and AffLL.  
- AffGAN shows the most promising results, with a theoretical link to variational inference in its stochastic form.  
- Decision: Reject; encourage resubmission after substantial revisions due to insufficient empirical rigor.  
- Experiments do not convincingly show perceptual improvements; PSNR/SSIM often favour MSE baselines.  
- Visual comparisons are not systematically made against established SOTA SR methods (e.g., EDSR, RCAN, Real-ESRGAN).  
- AffDG and AffLL perform significantly worse than AffGAN, with limited analysis of why.  
- Claimed benefit of affine projection (faster training) is not robustly demonstrated and seems limited to early training.  
- Ablation studies are lacking, particularly on the role and tuning of instance noise for GAN stability.  
- Network architecture, loss weighting, and optimization choices are not systematically explored.  
- Theoretical claims—especially that AffGAN minimizes a variational lower bound—need stronger mathematical derivation.  
- Discussion of MAP inference limitations is underdeveloped and feels tacked on.  
- Recommend adding comprehensive comparisons to SOTA SR methods and standard evaluation protocols.  
- Suggest incorporating perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS) in addition to PSNR/SSIM.  
- Request clearer explanation of why affine projection improves performance and how it interacts with GAN training.  
- Advise deeper investigation into AffDG/AffLL failures (gradient issues, density model limits, etc.).  
- Recommend expanded ablation studies on instance noise, architectures, and optimization parameters.  
- Ask for stronger derivation of the AffGAN–variational inference connection.  
- Raise concerns about potential GAN mode collapse and request evidence/mitigation strategies.  
- Ask for a detailed analysis of computational cost compared to existing methods.  
- Specific author questions: how MAP was computed on the 2D toy problem; analysis of artifacts in natural images; critical architectural/hyperparameter choices for AffGAN; reasons PSNR/SSIM can be worse despite qualitative gains; and rationale for using MCGSM in AffLL and its limitations.
