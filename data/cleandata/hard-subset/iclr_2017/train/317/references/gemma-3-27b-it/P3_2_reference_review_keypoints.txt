- Paper proposes MAP-based SR using natural image priors and three amortised inference methods: AffGAN, AffDG, AffLL, with an affine projection layer to enforce LR-HR consistency.  
- Empirically strong on a 2D toy (Swiss-roll) but gains on natural images (CelebA, ImageNet) are marginal.  
- AffGAN shows improved perceptual quality qualitatively, but quantitative metrics (PSNR, SSIM) do not consistently outperform MSE-trained nets.  
- Review decision: Reject due to insufficiently convincing empirical improvements and training challenges.  
- Major concern: Limited empirical advantage over existing SR methods and lack of comparisons to SOTA (EDSR, RCAN, Real-ESRGAN).  
- Training instability: AffDG and AffLL are difficult to train and sensitive to hyperparameters; AffGAN requires tricks like instance noise.  
- Complexity and practicality concerns: method needs careful tuning, annealing schedules, and specific architectures, reducing accessibility and robustness.  
- Ablation shortcomings: more detailed studies needed to isolate the impact of affine projection, instance noise, discriminator design, and other components.  
- Novelty concern: applying GANs to SR is not new; paper must better justify what makes AffGAN uniquely effective beyond affine projection.  
- Suggested improvement: include stronger baselines and direct quantitative comparisons against leading SR models.  
- Suggested metrics: report perceptual measures (e.g., LPIPS, NIQE) in addition to PSNR/SSIM to better capture image quality.  
- Suggested analysis: add detailed ablations, failure-case analysis, and deeper discussion of failure examples like the snake-to-water case.  
- Theoretical clarity: expand and clarify the connection between AffGAN and amortised/variational inference and its implications.  
- Address MAP criticism: discuss limitations of MAP inference and propose mitigation strategies.  
- Questions for authors: provide PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS comparisons to SOTA on CelebA/ImageNet, detail stabilization choices and sensitivity analysis for AffGAN.  
- Questions for authors: quantify computational cost and impact of the affine projection on training/inference speed.  
- Questions for authors: explain why AffDG/AffLL are hard to train and what mitigations were attempted.  
- Questions for authors: justify affine projection versus enforcing soft L1/L2 constraints and provide theoretical rationale for any improvement.
