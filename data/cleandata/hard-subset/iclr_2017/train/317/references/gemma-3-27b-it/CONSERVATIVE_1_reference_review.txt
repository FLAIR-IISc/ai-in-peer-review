This paper proposes a novel approach to image super-resolution (SR) based on amortized Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference, arguing that minimizing cross-entropy between the model output distribution and the true data distribution yields more plausible results than traditional pixel-wise loss functions like MSE. The authors introduce an affine projection layer to enforce consistency between LR and HR images and explore three methods – GAN-based, denoising-based, and density-guided – to approximate the desired cross-entropy minimization.

**Reject.** While the motivation for moving beyond MSE is well-established, and the theoretical arguments for MAP inference are sound, the paper lacks sufficient empirical evidence to support its claims of significant improvement, particularly concerning perceptual quality. The experiments, while including a helpful 2D toy problem, feel largely exploratory and don’t convincingly demonstrate a consistent advantage over established methods, especially in the more complex image SR tasks.

The primary concern lies with the reliance on visual inspection and limited quantitative evaluation. The comparison to standard methods (MSE) is present, but feels cursory. The reported PSNR and SSIM scores, while shown in tables, do not uniformly favor the proposed methods. Furthermore, the claim of perceptual superiority relies heavily on subjective assessments, which are insufficient without more robust metrics (e.g., learned perceptual image patch similarity - LPIPS) and/or user studies. The results on the grass texture dataset are presented as a success, but the blurry results from AffDG and AffLL are dismissed without substantial analysis. The issues with stability during training (particularly for AffDG and AffLL, as highlighted in Appendix E) raise concerns about the robustness of the approach. 

The theoretical connection drawn between AffGAN and amortized variational inference (Appendix F) is interesting, but feels tacked on and doesn’t significantly strengthen the core contribution of the paper. The extensive discussion of instance noise, while potentially valuable, feels somewhat disconnected from the main thrust of the SR application and introduces further complexity without a clear demonstration of its necessity.

**Additional Feedback (for potential improvement):**

*   **More Comprehensive Evaluation:** Perform a more thorough quantitative evaluation using standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100, Urban100) and compare against state-of-the-art SR methods (e.g., EDSR, RCAN, SRGAN). Include LPIPS scores as a more perceptually aligned metric.
*   **Ablation Studies:** Conduct ablation studies to isolate the impact of the affine projection layer. Quantify the benefits of the projection, rather than relying on qualitative observations.
*   **Robustness Analysis:** Investigate the sensitivity of the proposed methods to hyperparameters and different network architectures. Address the training instability issues, particularly for AffDG and AffLL.
*   **Qualitative Analysis:** Provide more systematic qualitative comparisons with clear visual explanations of why the proposed methods produce more desirable results. Highlight specific details that demonstrate perceptual improvements.
*   **Streamline Presentation:** The paper is quite dense and could benefit from a more focused presentation. Some sections (e.g., detailed derivations in the theory section) could be moved to the appendix.
*   **Clarify connection to existing work**: Position the work more clearly in relation to existing GAN-based SR methods (e.g., SRGAN). What specific challenges does this work address that aren't already tackled by existing solutions?

**Questions for the Authors:**

*   Why were LPIPS or other perceptual metrics not used for evaluating the SR results?
*   Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the training instability observed with AffDG and AffLL? What specific factors contribute to this instability, and what steps have you taken to mitigate it?
*   How does the performance of AffGAN compare to SRGAN on standard SR benchmarks, particularly in terms of both PSNR/SSIM and perceptual quality metrics (e.g., LPIPS)?
*   What is the computational cost of adding the affine projection layer, and does it significantly impact training or inference speed?
*   The conclusion states that the stochastic extension of AffGAN performs amortized variational inference. Has this been empirically validated? If so, can you provide details on the experimental setup and results?
