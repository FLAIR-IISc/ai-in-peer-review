- Proposes shifting SR from empirical risk minimization to amortized MAP inference to favor higher-probability, more plausible reconstructions.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer to enforce consistency between low- and high-resolution images.  
- Presents three MAP-approximation methods: AffGAN (GAN variant), AffDG (denoising-guided), and AffLL (density-guided).  
- Demonstrates theoretical advantages on a 2D toy (Swiss-roll) and shows qualitative improvements on CelebA and ImageNet.  
- Decision: Reject — ideas are interesting but empirical results and presentation are insufficient for acceptance.  
- Limited empirical gains on real images: only slightly sharper outputs with no clear PSNR/SSIM improvements.  
- Insufficient ablation and baseline comparisons, missing evaluations against modern SR methods (e.g., EDSR, RCAN, SRGAN) and isolation of affine layer effects.  
- Architectural choices (layer counts, filter sizes, activations) are not well-justified or systematically explored.  
- Density model (MCGSM) may be limited; log-likelihood may not capture data distribution fully, weakening AffLL.  
- Training instability and high sensitivity to hyperparameters (instance noise, annealing schedules) are practical concerns.  
- Paper is dense and theoretically heavy; needs clearer explanations and visual aids for accessibility.  
- Mathematical notation is inconsistent and some equations need more explanation.  
- Experimental details (hyperparameters, schedules, hardware) are insufficient for reproducibility.  
- Releasing code would strengthen the paper and aid verification.  
- Discussion of MAP limitations should be expanded, especially trade-offs between MAP and other estimates in SR.  
- Question: Provide objective perceptual evaluations (e.g., LPIPS or user studies) beyond qualitative claims.  
- Question: How sensitive are results to noise distribution and annealing schedule; provide ablation on these choices.  
- Question: Why does the affine projection layer sometimes reduce PSNR — is there a perceptual vs. pixel-accuracy trade-off?  
- Question: Clarify the practical advantages/disadvantages of the connection to variational inference and implications for future work.  
- Question: Compare computational cost of proposed methods to state-of-the-art SR techniques like EDSR or RCAN.
