

# Review of "AMORTISED MAP INFERENCE FOR IMAGE SUPER-RESOLUTION"

## 1. Summary of Contributions
The paper proposes a novel approach to image super-resolution (SR) based on amortized Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference. The authors argue that current SR methods using MSE or similar losses produce blurry outputs because they average over the posterior distribution rather than finding the mode. To address this, they introduce an affine projection layer ensuring outputs are consistent with LR inputs, and propose three methods for amortized MAP inference: AffGAN (affine projected GAN), AffDG (denoiser guided), and AffLL (density guided). The paper demonstrates that these methods produce sharper, more perceptually appealing results than MSE-based approaches.

## 2. Decision
Reject. The paper contains significant methodological inconsistencies, misrepresentation of prior work, and insufficient empirical support for its claims. While the core idea of using MAP inference for SR is interesting, the paper fails to properly establish its theoretical foundation or provide rigorous empirical evidence.

## 3. Supporting Arguments
The paper contains several critical flaws that undermine its claims:

**Theoretical inconsistencies:**
- Equation (7) defines the affine projection as Π_Ax fθ(x) = (I - A+A)fθ(x) + A+x, but the notation is inconsistent with standard mathematical conventions for projection operators. The expression (I - A+A) should be a projection matrix, but the paper doesn't clarify this.
- The paper claims AffGAN minimizes KL[qθ||pY], but in Section 3.2.1 they introduce instance noise, which actually minimizes dσ(qθ, pY) = KL[pσ*qθ||pσ*pY], not the standard KL divergence. This is a significant theoretical misrepresentation.
- In Section 3.3, they reference "First shown in (Huszár, 2016)" but don't provide a proper citation, and the reference list doesn't contain a 2016 Huszár paper.

**Misrepresentation of prior work:**
- The paper claims "to our knowledge this work is the first time that the output of a denoiser is explicitly back-propagated to train another network," but this is incorrect. The connection between denoising and gradient estimation was established in Vincent et al. (2008) and Alain & Bengio (2014), which the paper cites but doesn't properly credit for this specific contribution.
- The paper incorrectly positions their "instance noise" as a novel technique, when it's closely related to the one-sided label smoothing introduced by Salimans et al. (2016), which they cite but don't properly acknowledge as the foundation for their approach.

**Insufficient empirical support:**
- In Section 5.3, the authors claim AffDG and AffLL produce "blurry results" but don't provide visual examples of these results (Figure 3 only shows AffGAN, MSE, and Affinit). Without visual evidence, this claim is unsubstantiated.
- The paper doesn't compare to state-of-the-art SR methods beyond MSE-based approaches, making it impossible to assess whether their methods actually improve upon the current state of the art.
- In Table 2, they report PSNR and SSIM scores but don't provide statistical significance tests, making it difficult to determine if the differences are meaningful.

**Presentation issues:**
- The paper contains multiple grammatical errors and inconsistent notation (e.g., using "y" for both HR images and generator outputs).
- The reference list contains formatting inconsistencies (e.g., some entries have "et al." and others don't).
- The paper claims to "leave the empirical risk minimisation framework" but then uses GANs, which are also empirical risk minimization methods with a different objective.

## 4. Additional Feedback
- Clarify the mathematical formulation of the affine projection layer and its relationship to standard projection operators.
- Provide a more accurate literature review that properly credits prior work on denoising and gradient estimation.
- Include visual examples of the AffDG and AffLL results to substantiate claims about their blurry outputs.
- Explain how the MAP solution is calculated for real image SR (not just the 2D toy example).
- Provide comparisons to state-of-the-art SR methods beyond MSE-based approaches.

## 5. Questions for Authors
1. How exactly do you calculate the MAP solution for real image SR, given that the posterior is intractable in high dimensions? The paper only demonstrates this for the 2D toy example.
2. Why do you claim AffGAN is "more resilient to coordinate transformations" without providing any theoretical or empirical evidence for this claim?
3. Can you provide a more rigorous theoretical justification for why MAP inference produces more perceptually appealing results than sampling from the posterior, given the limitations you mention in Section 5.6?
4. The paper claims that "water will likely have a higher density under the image prior than snakes" in the snake example, but this is a hand-wavy argument. How do you quantitatively support this claim?
5. Why did you choose to use instance noise instead of the more established label smoothing technique for GAN stabilization, given that they're both addressing similar issues?
