- Proposes amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution to avoid MSE-induced blurriness.  
- Argues MSE/empirical risk minimization averages the posterior (Bayes-optimal for MSE) instead of selecting its mode.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer to ensure model outputs are consistent with the low-resolution input.  
- Proposes three methods: Affine Projected GAN (AffGAN), Denoiser Guided SR (AffDG), and Density Guided SR (AffLL).  
- Provides strong theoretical grounding and transforms constrained MAP into an unconstrained optimization problem.  
- Establishes a novel connection between GANs and variational inference (Appendix F).  
- AffGAN is particularly compelling, building on GAN-based SR with theoretical justification.  
- Uses instance noise to stabilize GAN training and argues it outperforms label smoothing.  
- 2D toy experiments (Swiss-roll) show MSE/MAE produce low-probability/blurry outputs; AffGAN/AffDG minimize the MAP objective.  
- Real-image experiments (grass, faces) show AffGAN yields sharper, more plausible images despite worse PSNR/SSIM.  
- Emphasizes that PSNR/SSIM correlate poorly with human perception and perceptual quality matters more.  
- Recommends comparing to modern perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS) to strengthen claims.  
- Requests explanation for why AffDG and AffLL underperformed and produced blurry results.  
- Asks for expanded discussion and implementation details of instance noise and why it beats label smoothing.  
- Suggests discussing scalability to higher downscaling factors (8×, 16×).  
- Recommends explicitly comparing AffGAN to Ledig et al. (2016) and other GAN-based SR methods.  
- Questions: expected AffGAN performance on LPIPS metric.  
- Questions: evidence/examples for AffGAN’s claimed resilience to coordinate transformations via H[qθ].  
- Questions: how performance would change with larger downscaling factors and what modifications are needed.  
- Questions: why AffDG/AffLL failed to capture high-probability posterior regions while AffGAN succeeded.  
- Questions: how the variational-inference perspective might guide future SR method design.  
- Decision: Recommend acceptance based on strong theoretical contribution and compelling empirical evidence.
