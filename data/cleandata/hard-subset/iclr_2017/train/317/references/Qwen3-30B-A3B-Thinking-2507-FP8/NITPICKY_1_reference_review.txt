

# Review of "AMORTISED MAP INFERENCE FOR IMAGE SUPER-RESOLUTION"

## 1. Summary of Claims and Contributions

The paper proposes an approach to image super-resolution (SR) based on amortized Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference, arguing that it produces more perceptually pleasing results than standard MSE/MAE-based methods. The authors introduce an affine projection layer to ensure consistency between input LR images and output HR images, and present three methods to minimize the cross-entropy between the model's output distribution and the natural image prior: AffGAN (affine projected GAN), AffDG (denoiser guided), and AffLL (density guided). The paper claims these methods produce sharper, more plausible images compared to standard SR approaches, and establishes a connection between GANs and variational inference.

## 2. Decision

**Reject**. The paper presents an interesting theoretical framework but contains multiple technical inconsistencies, formatting errors, and unverified claims that undermine the validity of the results.

## 3. Supporting Arguments for Rejection

**Technical inconsistencies in core theoretical claims**: The paper contains fundamental mathematical errors that compromise its theoretical foundation. In Section 3.1, Equation (9) states "argmax θ Ex log pY (ΠAx fθ(x)) = argmax θ Eŷ∼qθ log pY (ŷ) = argmin θ H[qθ, pY ]". However, H[qθ, pY] = E_y~qθ[-log pY(y)], so maximizing E_y~qθ log pY(y) is equivalent to minimizing H[qθ, pY], not maximizing it as the paper states. This is a critical error that affects the entire theoretical framework.

**Inconsistent notation and formatting**: The paper uses inconsistent mathematical notation throughout. For example, "pY" is used instead of "p_Y" or "p_Y(y)" in many places, and "y" is sometimes used as a random variable and sometimes as a specific value. In Table 1, the cross-entropy values (4.10 for AffGAN) are implausibly low for image data, suggesting a possible calculation error or misinterpretation of the metric. Cross-entropy values for image distributions are typically much higher (e.g., 10+ bits for 256x256 images).

**Unverified claims with insufficient evidence**: The paper claims "the AffGAN model produces clearly the sharpest images" in Section 5.3, but provides no quantitative metrics to support this assertion. Similarly, in Section 5.4, the paper states "the AffGAN model produces slightly sharper images" without providing any statistical evidence of significance. The paper relies on subjective visual inspection rather than quantitative metrics, which is insufficient for a rigorous ML paper.

**Theoretical limitations not adequately addressed**: The paper acknowledges in Section 5.6 that MAP estimates can be highly atypical (e.g., mode of a normal distribution has norm 0 while samples have norm ~√d), yet fails to provide a convincing counterargument or propose a solution. This is a fundamental criticism of MAP inference that the authors do not adequately address.

**Formatting and presentation issues**: The paper contains numerous formatting errors, including inconsistent citation styles (e.g., "? \Q2014\E" instead of proper citations), inconsistent use of mathematical notation (e.g., "pY" vs "p_Y"), and missing figure references (e.g., "Figure 1a" is mentioned but not provided). These errors suggest a lack of attention to detail that is unacceptable for a top-tier conference.

## 4. Additional Feedback for Improvement

**Clarify the core theoretical claim**: The paper must correct the fundamental error in Equation (9) and properly explain the relationship between the objective function and the cross-entropy. The current formulation is mathematically incorrect and undermines the entire theoretical contribution.

**Provide quantitative evidence**: The paper should replace subjective visual claims with quantitative metrics (PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS, etc.) to support claims about image quality. The paper should also report statistical significance for performance improvements.

**Address the MAP criticism**: The paper should either provide a compelling argument against the criticism in Section 5.6 or propose a method to overcome this limitation (e.g., by sampling from the posterior rather than using MAP).

**Fix formatting issues**: The paper should standardize all mathematical notation, fix citation formatting, and ensure all figures and tables are properly referenced and included.

**Clarify "Aff" in AffGAN/AffDG**: The paper uses "Aff" in model names but never explicitly defines what it stands for. This should be clarified early in the paper.

**Questions for Authors**:

1. Can you clarify the exact relationship between the objective function and cross-entropy in Equation (9), given the current mathematical formulation appears incorrect?

2. How do you explain the implausibly low cross-entropy values reported in Table 1 (e.g., 3.81 for AffDG)? What is the unit of this metric?

3. Can you provide quantitative evidence (not just visual inspection) to support the claim that AffGAN produces "sharper" images?

4. How do you address the criticism in Section 5.6 that MAP estimates can be highly atypical, making them potentially less perceptually plausible than samples from the posterior?

5. Why does the paper claim that "training becomes more akin to training generative models" when the method still requires LR images as input (unlike standard generative models that use noise)?
