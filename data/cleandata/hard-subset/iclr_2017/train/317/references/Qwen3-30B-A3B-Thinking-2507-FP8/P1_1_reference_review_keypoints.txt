- Proposes amortized MAP inference for super-resolution to avoid MSE-induced blurriness by targeting posterior modes rather than the mean.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer ensuring outputs are exactly consistent with the low-resolution input when downsampled.  
- Presents three MAP-approximation methods without paired data: AffGAN (affine-projected GAN), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided).  
- Provides theoretical justification and connections between GANs and variational inference (Appendix F).  
- Demonstrates empirical results on multiple datasets (2D Swiss-roll, textures, CelebA, ImageNet) showing sharper outputs from AffGAN.  
- AffGAN produces visually sharper, more perceptually plausible images while maintaining input consistency.  
- Uses a 2D Swiss-roll example to convincingly illustrate why MSE favors low-probability mean outputs and MAP finds high-probability modes.  
- Affine projection layer is a general, novel architectural contribution applicable to any SR method for enforcing consistency.  
- Quantitative cross-entropy measurements and qualitative comparisons support AffGAN’s objective minimization and visual gains.  
- Introduces "instance noise" with annealing to stabilize GAN training, a potentially broadly useful technique.  
- Recommendation: Accept — paper is theoretically sound, well-motivated, and empirically compelling.  
- Suggests adding a human perceptual evaluation since PSNR/SSIM correlate poorly with human judgments.  
- Recommends comparison to more recent SR methods (e.g., ESRGAN, RDN) to better contextualize results.  
- Asks for clarification and discussion on why AffDG and AffLL produce blurrier outputs and whether their limitations can be addressed.  
- Requests more implementation details about instance-noise schedules and sensitivity for reproducibility.  
- Asks for a simple illustrative example showing AffGAN’s resilience to coordinate transformations due to the entropy term H[qθ].  
- Requests quantified perceptual metrics or human scores for texture results and clarification on whether AffGAN’s “dreamed” outputs generalize across image classes and relate to the MAP objective, including the trade-off between sharpness and high-frequency noise.
