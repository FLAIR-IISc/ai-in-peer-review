

# Review of "AMORTISED MAP INFERENCE FOR IMAGE SUPER-RESOLUTION"

## Summary
This paper proposes a novel approach to image super-resolution (SR) by leveraging amortized Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference instead of conventional empirical risk minimization with MSE/MAE loss. The authors argue that current SR methods produce blurry outputs because they minimize the mean of the posterior distribution, whereas MAP inference favors high-probability solutions that align better with human perception. The key contributions include: 1) an affine projection layer that ensures model outputs are consistent with the LR input; 2) three methods for MAP inference using GANs (AffGAN), denoising (AffDG), and density modeling (AffLL); and 3) theoretical connections between GANs and variational inference. The paper provides compelling theoretical motivation and promising empirical results on toy and real-world datasets.

## Decision
Reject. The paper contains a critical contradiction between its main empirical claims and the data presented in Table 1, which undermines the core contribution of the work.

## Supporting Arguments
1. **Critical contradiction in empirical results**: The paper claims "the AffGAN and AffDG achieves cross-entropy values close to the MAP solution" (Section 5.1, Table 1), yet Table 1 shows AffDG has a lower cross-entropy (3.81) than AffGAN (4.10), while the MAP solution is 3.15. This means AffDG's performance is closer to the optimal MAP solution than AffGAN. However, the paper consistently presents AffGAN as the superior method throughout the paper (e.g., "AffGAN produces the most visually appealing results" in Section 6), creating a fundamental contradiction that is never resolved. This inconsistency invalidates the paper's main empirical claim.

2. **Inadequate discussion of AffGAN's limitations**: The paper acknowledges AffGAN produces "slightly sharper images" but also "contain slightly more high frequency noise" (Section 5.4). However, it fails to adequately address this significant drawback, especially since the paper's entire premise is about producing perceptually superior results. The high-frequency noise in AffGAN results (Figure 4, 5) appears to be a serious practical limitation that the authors do not properly contextualize or mitigate.

3. **Minor but pervasive formatting inconsistencies**: The paper contains numerous minor but distracting inconsistencies, such as:
   - Inconsistent use of "x" vs "X" for variables (e.g., "x ∈ [−8, 8]" vs "x" in equations)
   - Inconsistent spacing around operators (e.g., "log pY |X(y|x)" vs "log pY|X(y|x)")
   - Inconsistent notation for the affine projection (e.g., "ΠAx fθ(x)" vs "ΠA x fθ(x)")
   - Inconsistent capitalization of terms (e.g., "MAP" vs "Map")
   These suggest a lack of attention to detail that would be unacceptable in a high-quality ICLR submission.

## Additional Feedback
1. **Clarify the AffGAN vs AffDG performance discrepancy**: The authors must either revise the claims about AffGAN being superior or provide a clear explanation for why AffDG's lower cross-entropy does not translate to better visual results. The paper currently presents AffGAN as the best method without addressing this contradiction.

2. **Address high-frequency noise**: The paper should include a thorough discussion of the high-frequency noise issue in AffGAN results, including possible causes and potential mitigations. Without addressing this limitation, the paper's claim that AffGAN produces "visually appealing" results is questionable.

3. **Improve theoretical consistency**: The paper claims "the optimal deconvolution A+ is not simply the transpose of A" but doesn't provide a clear mathematical explanation for this. The paper also contains a minor error in Eqn. (4) where it writes "Ex log pY |X(fθ(x)|x)" instead of the correct "Ex log pY |X(fθ(x)|x)".

4. **Strengthen the connection to variational inference**: The paper claims to establish a connection between GANs and variational inference in Appendix F, but the connection is not sufficiently developed or empirically validated. The authors should provide more concrete evidence for this claim.

5. **Improve presentation of results**: The paper should include statistical significance tests for the performance improvements claimed in Section 5.2 (e.g., MSE and SSIM improvements with affine projection). Without these, the claims about "better solution" and "train faster" are not fully supported.

## Questions for Authors
1. Can you clarify why AffDG achieves a lower cross-entropy than AffGAN (Table 1) but the paper consistently presents AffGAN as the superior method? What is the basis for this discrepancy?

2. The paper states "the AffGAN and AffDG achieves cross-entropy values close to the MAP solution" but AffDG's value (3.81) is 22% higher than the MAP solution (3.15), while AffGAN's value (4.10) is 32% higher. Is this "close" by the authors' definition, or should the paper revise its claim?

3. How does the paper reconcile the high-frequency noise observed in AffGAN results (Figures 4 and 5) with the claim that AffGAN produces "visually appealing" results? What specific perceptual metrics were used to make this judgment?

4. In Appendix F, the paper claims "a stochastic version of AffGAN performs amortised variational inference." What empirical evidence supports this claim, and how does it differ from standard variational inference methods?

This paper has interesting theoretical ideas but is undermined by a critical contradiction in its main empirical claim and lacks sufficient attention to detail in its presentation. The authors should address these fundamental issues before the paper can be considered for publication.
