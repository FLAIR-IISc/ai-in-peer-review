- Proposal: Amortised MAP inference for image super-resolution using an affine projection layer to enforce LR-HR consistency and three implementation variants (AffGAN, AffDG, AffLL).  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Major theoretical contribution: Rigorous connection between MAP inference and SR, with a clear derivation of the affine projection layer.  
- Affine projection: Ensures downsampling the SR output recovers the LR input and aligns with MAP objective via cross-entropy minimization.  
- Empirical claim: MAP-based methods (notably AffGAN) produce sharper images than MSE-based methods.  
- Toy experiment support: Swiss-roll experiment shows AffGAN and AffDG find posterior modes while MSE/MAE produce low-probability outputs.  
- Real-image results: AffGAN yields visually sharper outputs than MSE baselines while maintaining input-output consistency.  
- Weakness 1: AffDG and AffLL are dropped early without quantitative analysis or explanation for their poor performance.  
- Weakness 2: Perceptual quality claims lack human studies or rigorous perceptual metrics beyond PSNR/SSIM.  
- Weakness 3: Insufficient positioning relative to prior GAN-based SR work (e.g., Ledig et al. 2016) and unclear comparative advantages.  
- Suggested experiments: Provide quantitative perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS, FID) and human preference studies to support visual-quality claims.  
- Suggested analysis: Deeper investigation into why AffDG/AffLL fail, including quantitative comparisons and diagnostic experiments.  
- Clarification request: Explain specifically how the affine projection layer improves over existing GAN-based SR methods.  
- Broader discussion: Expand on MAP inference limitations and how the affine projection interacts with coordinate transformation issues.  
- Questions for authors: Why do AffDG/AffLL fail vs. AffGAN, how exactly does affine projection improve over Ledig et al., and can you show human preference evidence for AffGAN outputs.
