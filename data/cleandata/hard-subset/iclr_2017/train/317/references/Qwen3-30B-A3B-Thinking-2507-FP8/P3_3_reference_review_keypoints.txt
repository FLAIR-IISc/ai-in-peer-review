- Proposes amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution to avoid MSE-induced blurriness from posterior averaging.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer ensuring SR outputs downsample exactly to the LR input (consistency constraint).  
- Presents three training variants: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided).  
- Theoretical result: MAP inference corresponds to minimizing cross-entropy between model outputs and the natural image prior.  
- Empirical evidence: 2D Swiss-roll shows AffGAN/AffDG find posterior modes while MSE/MAE produce low-probability outputs.  
- Real-image experiments: AffGAN yields sharper, more perceptually plausible images than MSE and baselines.  
- Novel theoretical connection drawn between GANs and variational inference (Appendix F).  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions—solid theoretical grounding and convincing empirical results.  
- Request to add quantitative perceptual validation (e.g., user study or LPIPS) beyond PSNR/SSIM.  
- Ask for stronger comparison to recent state-of-the-art SR methods, especially GAN-based approaches (e.g., Ledig et al., 2016).  
- Recommend clarifying how MAP limitations affect the proposed methods and when MAP might fail for SR.  
- Need technical clarification on why AffDG and AffLL produced blurry results and whether architectural/training changes could fix this.  
- Question: elaborate specific challenges with AffDG/AffLL and potential mitigations.  
- Question: how to trade off AffGAN’s increased sharpness versus higher high-frequency noise and methods to control it.  
- Question: does the affine projection layer change coordinate-dependence or uniqueness issues of MAP estimates?  
- Question: how the AffGAN–variational inference connection could guide future work on mode-seeking versus sampling approaches.
