- Proposes shifting from ERM/MSE to amortized MAP inference for super-resolution to avoid blurry averaged outputs.
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces output consistency with the low-resolution input (downsampling returns the input).
- Presents three amortized MAP methods: AffGAN, AffDG (Denoiser Guided), and AffLL (Density Guided).
- Theoretically shows MAP inference minimizes cross-entropy between model output distribution and the natural image prior.
- Establishes a novel connection between AffGAN and amortized variational inference, linking GANs to variational methods.
- Demonstrates strong theoretical analysis and a convincing 2D toy experiment validating objectives.
- Empirical results across datasets show AffGAN yields perceptually superior, sharper images vs MSE/MAE and some GAN baselines.
- Ablation study confirms the affine projection provides practical performance gains beyond theory.
- Grass texture experiment highlights cases where MSE/MAE fail catastrophically while AffGAN succeeds.
- Introduces an "instance noise" trick to stabilize GAN training, a practical contribution for GANs.
- Recommends accepting the paper due to novelty, theoretical soundness, and empirical validation.
- Requests clearer differentiation of AffGAN from prior GAN-based SR methods (e.g., Ledig et al., Garcia).
- Asks for expanded discussion of computational cost and inference-time impact of the affine projection.
- Suggests addressing MAP limitations (atypicality of modes) and how they may affect results.
- Raises questions: why AffGAN avoids blurriness vs AffDG/AffLL; how AffGAN's higher-entropy MAP manifests visually; interpretation of ImageNet failure (snake → water) as probable reconstruction vs model hallucination.
