- Proposes amortized MAP inference for super-resolution, reframing SR as minimizing cross-entropy between model output and natural image distributions without paired HR-LR data.  
- Introduces an "affine projection layer" to enforce LR-HR consistency by projecting outputs to the affine subspace of valid solutions.  
- Develops three practical methods: AffGAN (affine projected GAN), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided), with AffGAN showing strongest empirical performance.  
- Provides theoretical grounding for using GANs in SR and introduces an "instance noise" trick to stabilize GAN training.  
- Establishes a connection between AffGAN and variational inference, offering new theoretical insight into GANs.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation, rigorous theoretical analysis, and comprehensive empirical validation.  
- Recommendation: Accept — method yields perceptually superior results to MSE-based SR with strong theoretical and empirical support.  
- Key motivation: MSE-based SR averages solutions and produces blurry outputs; MAP approach addresses this by modeling multimodal natural image distribution.  
- Affine projection layer improves convergence and final results without hurting performance; implementation and optimal deconvolution kernel are well-detailed.  
- Empirical results: AffGAN and AffDG match MAP on toy tasks; AffGAN yields sharper, more plausible images on real datasets compared to MSE/MAE baselines.  
- Ablation studies support importance of affine projection; theoretical link to variational inference is noteworthy.  
- Suggest adding quantitative comparisons to recent perceptual-loss/GAN-based SR methods (e.g., Ledig et al., 2016).  
- Recommend discussing computational complexity and providing training/inference time comparisons to standard SR methods.  
- Suggest expanding discussion on practical limitations of MAP inference, especially for highly ambiguous LR inputs.  
- Request clearer visualization of affine projection’s effect on specific images to aid intuition.  
- Question: Why did AffDG and AffLL produce blurry results while AffGAN succeeded — implementation issue or fundamental limitation?  
- Question: How does instance noise compare to other GAN stabilization methods (label smoothing, spectral norm) and has it been tested beyond SR?  
- Question: Can authors provide more intuition on why AffGAN minimizes KL in an amortized variational inference framework and its relation to VAE objectives?  
- Question: How is "plausibility" of reconstructions determined or quantified beyond visual inspection (e.g., the snake→water example)?
