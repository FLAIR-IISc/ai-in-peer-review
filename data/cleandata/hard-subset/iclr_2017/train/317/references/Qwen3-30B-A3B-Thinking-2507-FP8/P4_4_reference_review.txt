

# Review for "AMORTISED MAP INFERENCE FOR IMAGE SUPER-RESOLUTION"

## 1. Summary of Contributions
The paper proposes a novel approach to image super-resolution (SR) based on amortized Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference. The authors argue that current SR methods using MSE loss produce blurry outputs because they average over multiple plausible HR solutions, whereas MAP inference would select the mode of the posterior distribution, corresponding to high-probability solutions under natural image priors. The paper introduces an affine projection layer that ensures consistency between LR inputs and HR outputs, and proposes three methods to approximate MAP inference: AffGAN (using GANs), AffDG (using denoising), and AffLL (using density modeling). The authors provide theoretical justification for their approach, demonstrate it on a toy Swiss-roll problem, and show promising results on natural image datasets, with AffGAN producing sharper, more perceptually pleasing images than MSE-based methods.

## 2. Decision
Accept. The paper makes a strong theoretical contribution to understanding why GAN-based SR methods work, and provides a principled approach to SR that better aligns with perceptual quality. The AffGAN method shows clear improvements in perceptual quality over MSE-based methods while maintaining computational efficiency.

## 3. Supporting Arguments
The paper successfully addresses a well-known limitation of current SR methods: MSE-based approaches produce blurry outputs due to averaging over multiple plausible solutions. The authors provide a clear theoretical motivation for using MAP inference, supported by the Swiss-roll toy example that effectively demonstrates the problem with MSE.

The affine projection layer is a significant technical contribution that ensures consistency between LR inputs and HR outputs, which is a major advancement over previous soft-constrained approaches. The paper's theoretical derivation showing that MAP inference for SR reduces to minimizing the cross-entropy between the model's output distribution and the HR image distribution is sound and well-justified.

The empirical results demonstrate that AffGAN produces visually superior results compared to MSE, MAE, and other proposed methods, with quantitative results supporting the qualitative observations. The ablation studies in Section 5.2 provide strong evidence that the affine projection layer is beneficial for SR, not just a theoretical curiosity.

The connection between GANs and variational inference established in Appendix F is a valuable theoretical contribution that extends the paper's impact beyond SR.

## 4. Additional Feedback
- The paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of computational complexity. While AffGAN produces superior perceptual quality, it would be helpful to quantify the additional computational cost compared to MSE-based methods, especially for real-time applications.

- The paper mentions that AffDG and AffLL produced blurry results but doesn't provide sufficient explanation for why they underperformed compared to AffGAN. A more detailed analysis of the differences between these methods would strengthen the paper.

- While the paper demonstrates the effectiveness of AffGAN on several datasets, it would be valuable to include comparisons with more recent GAN-based SR methods beyond Ledig et al. (2016) to better position the contribution.

## 5. Questions for Authors
1. The paper mentions that AffGAN produces slightly sharper images but with more high-frequency noise. Could you provide more details on how this noise manifests visually, and whether it is perceptually acceptable compared to the blur in MSE-based methods?

2. The paper states that AffGAN is more resilient to coordinate transformations than MAP inference. Could you elaborate on this claim and provide evidence from your experiments?

3. The paper mentions that AffLL achieved a log-likelihood of -4.10 bits per dimension, comparable to Theis & Bethge (2015). Could you provide more context on what this means for the quality of the density model, and why it might still not be sufficient for training AffLL effectively?
