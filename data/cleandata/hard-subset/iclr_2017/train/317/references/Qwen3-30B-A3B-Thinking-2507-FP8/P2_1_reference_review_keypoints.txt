- Paper proposes amortized MAP inference for super-resolution, arguing MAP yields more perceptually realistic (less blurry) outputs than MSE-based ERM.
- Introduces a CNN with an affine projection layer that enforces input-output consistency (downsampling output equals input).
- Proposes three amortized MAP methods: AffGAN (affine-projected GAN), AffDG (affine-projected denoiser-guided), and AffLL (affine-projected density-guided).
- Provides theoretical derivation that these methods minimize cross-entropy between the model output distribution and the natural image prior.
- Empirically validates on a 2D toy problem and real datasets (grass textures, CelebA, ImageNet), showing AffGAN produces sharper, more realistic results than MSE/MAE methods.
- Establishes a connection between AffGAN and amortized variational inference, offering theoretical insight into GANs.
- Positions work well in related literature and contrasts exact affine projection consistency with prior soft-constraint SR methods.
- Decision: Accept — significant contribution and theoretically grounded alternative to MSE-based SR.
- Key reason for acceptance: identifies fundamental limitation of MSE (blurriness) and provides a principled MAP-based remedy.
- Key reason for acceptance: affine projection layer is novel and empirically beneficial for consistency.
- Suggestion: more thorough theoretical discussion of MAP's representation-dependence and how affine projection mitigates this.
- Suggestion: include perceptual quality metrics (e.g., LPIPS, FID, human studies) rather than relying mainly on PSNR/SSIM.
- Suggestion: report computational efficiency (training and inference time) compared to other methods.
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis of the introduced instance noise and compare to other GAN stabilization techniques.
- Questions for authors: how does AffGAN address MAP estimates that may be visually atypical; why did AffDG and AffLL produce blurrier results despite theoretical ties to MAP; how to quantify plausibility of “dreamed-up” outputs like the snake→water example?
