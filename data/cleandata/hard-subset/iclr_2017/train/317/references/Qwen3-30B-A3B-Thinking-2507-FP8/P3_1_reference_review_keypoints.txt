- Paper proposes amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution as an alternative to MSE-based ERM to avoid blurry mean outputs.  
- Introduces an affine projection CNN layer to ensure high-resolution outputs are exactly consistent with low-resolution inputs (x = Ay).  
- Proposes three amortized MAP methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoising-based), and AffLL (density-model-based).  
- Provides theoretical justification linking the objective to minimizing cross-entropy between the model output distribution and the natural image prior.  
- Empirical results show sharper, more perceptually plausible SR outputs compared to MSE, MAE, and soft-constrained approaches.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, calling the contribution significant and well-motivated.  
- Strong theoretical motivation illustrated by a 2D Swiss-roll toy example showing MAP finds high-probability modes while MSE finds low-probability means.  
- Affine projection layer is a clever architectural innovation and empirically enforces exact LR–HR consistency (error = 0) versus soft constraints.  
- AffGAN is particularly compelling with theoretical GAN–MAP connections and visually sharper results in experiments.  
- Paper is well-situated in related work, discussing GAN-based SR, perceptual losses, and denoising connections.  
- Suggestion: address representation dependence of MAP more thoroughly and provide empirical robustness to coordinate transformations (e.g., color space).  
- Suggestion: compare against recent state-of-the-art perceptual SR methods (e.g., ESRGAN, RDN).  
- Suggestion: provide concrete implementation details for instance noise (schedule, annealing) used in GAN training.  
- Suggestion: analyze why AffGAN outperforms AffDG and AffLL to clarify performance differences.  
- Questions for authors: quantify the sharpness-versus-noise trade-off for AffGAN relative to SOTA; explain the snake→water failure case given the LR cues; and provide empirical evidence that AffGAN is resilient to coordinate transforms (H[qθ] claim).
