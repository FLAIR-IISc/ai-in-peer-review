- Proposes amortized MAP inference for SR, arguing MSE produces blurry means rather than MAP modes.
- Shows MAP in SR equals minimizing cross-entropy between model outputs and a natural image prior.
- Introduces an affine projection layer to enforce LR–HR consistency.
- Proposes three amortized MAP methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided).
- Introduces "instance noise" to stabilize GAN training.
- Provides empirical validation on synthetic and real datasets.

- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.

- Strong theoretical derivation linking MAP inference to cross-entropy minimization.
- Affine projection is a clever architectural solution enabling input–output consistency without paired data.
- AffGAN theoretically justifies GAN use in SR; instance noise is a practical contribution to GAN training stability.
- Experiments (toy and real-world) show AffGAN yields sharper, more perceptually pleasing results than MSE/MAE.
- Ablations show affine projection improves training speed and performance even with MSE objectives.

- Key strengths: identifies a core SR limitation, strong theory, well-motivated methods, consistent empirical support, useful instance-noise technique.

- Minor weaknesses: no comparison to recent SOTA (e.g., ESRGAN, RCAN); lacks quantitative perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS, FID); MAP limitations acknowledged but not fully addressed.

- Suggestion: add quantitative perceptual metrics like LPIPS/FID.
- Suggestion: compare with recent SOTA SR methods (ESRGAN, RCAN).
- Suggestion: clarify and better evaluate AffLL (PixelCNN/MCGSM) and why it underperforms.
- Suggestion: expand on the AffGAN–variational inference connection in the main text.
- Suggestion: provide more implementation details on pseudo-inverse optimization and sensitivity to downsampling kernel.

- Questions: How does AffGAN compare to ESRGAN/RCAN in PSNR/SSIM and perceptual quality?
- Question: Why do AffDG and AffLL produce blurrier results than AffGAN—fundamental limitation or fixable?
- Question: Why is the optimal deconvolution A+ not simply A^T, and how is A+ optimized numerically?
- Question: How do MAP inference limitations show up empirically, and how can AffGAN mitigate them?
- Question: Can instance noise generalize to other GAN applications, and what insights exist for such generalization?
