- Novel amortized MAP framework for super-resolution with an affine projection layer enforcing LR–HR consistency.  
- Three proposed MAP methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoising-based), and AffLL (density-modeling-based).  
- Theoretical argument that MSE averages the posterior causing blurriness; MAP selects the posterior mode (illustrated by Swiss-roll example).  
- Derivation of a cross-entropy minimization objective (Equation 9) grounding the approach.  
- Affine projection layer shown to improve training speed and final performance; includes practical numerical estimation of the Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets (Swiss-roll, grass, CelebA, ImageNet) show AffGAN yields sharper, more perceptually plausible outputs than MSE/MAE and soft-constrained GANs.  
- Cross-entropy measurements (Table 1) quantitatively support the method minimizing the intended objective.  
- Appendix F connects AffGAN to variational inference, providing additional theoretical grounding.  
- Main limitation: insufficient comparison to recent SOTA SR methods (e.g., ESRGAN, RCAN) in the main text.  
- Unclear why AffDG and AffLL underperform relative to AffGAN; paper lacks theoretical explanation for this gap.  
- Criticism of MAP inference (Section 5.6) is noted but could be made more concrete with specific failure examples.  
- Suggested minor revisions: add SOTA comparisons, discuss why AffGAN outperforms other proposed methods, and provide concrete MAP failure cases.  
- Questions for authors: compare to ESRGAN/RCAN, explain theoretical/empirical reasons for AffGAN’s superior performance over AffDG/AffLL, and give concrete examples where MAP inference fails.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — well-motivated, technically sound, and significant contribution to SR.
