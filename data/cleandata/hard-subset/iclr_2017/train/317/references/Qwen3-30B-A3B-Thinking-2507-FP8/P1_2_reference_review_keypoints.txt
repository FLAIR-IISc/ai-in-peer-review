- Contributions: Proposes amortized MAP inference for SR with (1) an affine projection layer, (2) three cross-entropy minimization methods (AffGAN, AffDG, AffLL), and (3) theoretical link showing AffGAN minimizes KL[q||pY], explaining GAN success in SR.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Motivation: Strongly motivated critique of MSE-based SR producing blurry means; Swiss-roll toy illustrates MAP is preferable.  
- Theoretical analysis: Rigorous, novel derivations showing affine projection enables cross-entropy minimization and linking GANs to MAP inference.  
- Empirical results: AffGAN yields perceptually superior, sharper SR on toy and real datasets, aligning with theory.  
- Ablations: Affine projection improves training speed and enforces LR consistency exactly; soft constraints do not.  
- Limitations acknowledged: Notes MAP issues with coordinate transformations and mode atypicality.  
- Clarify KL vs cross-entropy: Paper should explain why minimizing KL[q||pY] (AffGAN) is preferable to minimizing H[q,pY] for SR.  
- Explain AffDG/AffLL underperformance: Need deeper analysis whether blurriness is due to model choices or fundamental issues.  
- Computational efficiency: Discuss inference time and memory impact of affine projection vs standard SR methods.  
- Variational inference connection: Expand Appendix F to better relate AffGAN to existing variational SR work.  
- Q1 for authors: How robust is the method to coordinate/color-space transformations and have experiments been run across representations?  
- Q2 for authors: How does minimizing KL[q||pY] vs H[q,pY] affect practical behavior and why is KL preferable for SR?  
- Q3 for authors: How does instance noise differ from stabilization techniques like label smoothing, and why is it unbiased for the optimal discriminator?  
- Q4 for authors: How would computing MAP via brute-force Monte Carlo scale to high-dimensional real images where brute-force is infeasible?
