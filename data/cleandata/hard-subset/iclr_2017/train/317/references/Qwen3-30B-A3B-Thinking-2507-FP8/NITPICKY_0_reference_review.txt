

# Review of "AMORTISED MAP INFERENCE FOR IMAGE SUPER-RESOLUTION"

## Summary
The paper proposes using amortized Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference for image super-resolution (SR) as an alternative to standard MSE-based approaches. The authors argue that MAP inference produces sharper, more perceptually plausible results by preferring solutions with high posterior probability under the natural image prior. They introduce three methods based on GANs (AffGAN), denoisers (AffDG), and density models (AffLL), all incorporating an affine projection layer to ensure consistency between low-resolution inputs and high-resolution outputs. The paper demonstrates their approach on a 2D toy problem and several image datasets.

## Decision
Reject

## Supporting Arguments for Rejection

1. **Critical Methodological Omissions**: The paper lacks essential implementation details for the affine projection layer, which is central to their approach. While they reference Appendix B for details on computing the Moore-Penrose pseudoinverse A+, the main text fails to explain how this is implemented for convolutional layers (which is non-trivial). This omission makes the approach difficult to reproduce and evaluate.

2. **Inconsistent Notation and Terminology**: The paper contains multiple notational inconsistencies that undermine clarity. For example:
   - In Equation 4, they write "log pY |X(fθ(x)|x)" which is confusing notation; it should be log pY|X(y|x) evaluated at y=fθ(x)
   - They inconsistently use "HR" and "high-resolution" and "LR" and "low-resolution"
   - The phrase "shown ( )" in Figure 1a appears to be a formatting error

3. **Insufficient Empirical Validation**: The paper claims AffGAN produces "visually appealing" results but provides no quantitative perceptual metrics (e.g., user studies, perceptual metrics beyond PSNR/SSIM). In Table 2, they report PSNR and SSIM scores but fail to provide statistical significance tests for these comparisons. For the grass texture experiments (Section 5.3), they claim AffDG and AffLL "produced blurry results which we were unable to improve upon" but don't provide evidence of sufficient optimization attempts.

4. **Theoretical Claims Without Support**: The paper claims "It can be shown that this amounts to minimising KL[qG‖pY ]" but refers to Appendix A for the proof, which is not included in the provided text. The theoretical grounding appears incomplete without this supporting detail.

5. **Problematic Literature Positioning**: The paper cites "unpublished work by Garcia (2016)" which is inappropriate for a conference paper. Additionally, they fail to clearly distinguish their approach from Ledig et al. (2016), which also used GANs for SR, making it difficult to assess the novelty.

## Additional Feedback for Improvement

1. **Clarify Key Implementation Details**: Provide a clear, concise explanation in the main text of how to implement the affine projection layer for convolutional networks, not just a reference to Appendix B.

2. **Provide Statistical Evidence**: Include statistical significance tests for all comparative results (e.g., t-tests for PSNR/SSIM differences) to support claims about performance improvements.

3. **Expand Empirical Evaluation**: Add perceptual metrics beyond PSNR/SSIM (e.g., user studies, structural similarity metrics) to support claims about "visually appealing" results.

4. **Fix Formatting and Consistency**: Correct all notation inconsistencies, ensure consistent terminology, and fix the placeholder text "(shown ( )" in Figure 1a.

5. **Reposition Against Prior Work**: Clearly distinguish your approach from Ledig et al. (2016) and other GAN-based SR methods, explaining why your theoretical grounding is superior.

## Questions for Authors

1. The paper claims "the affine projected models generally found a lower cross-entropy H[qθ, pY ]" but doesn't provide statistical significance tests. Can you provide these tests to support this claim?

2. In Section 5.5, you state "water will likely have a higher density under the image prior than snakes." Can you provide evidence for this claim, or explain how you determined this?

3. The paper mentions "unpublished work by Garcia (2016)" - can you provide a citation for this work or explain why it's not properly published?

4. Why did you choose to focus only on AffGAN for further experiments after observing poor performance of AffDG and AffLL on grass textures? Did you try any specific optimization techniques to improve these methods?

5. How do you explain the difference between your approach and Ledig et al. (2016) in terms of theoretical justification for using GANs in SR?
