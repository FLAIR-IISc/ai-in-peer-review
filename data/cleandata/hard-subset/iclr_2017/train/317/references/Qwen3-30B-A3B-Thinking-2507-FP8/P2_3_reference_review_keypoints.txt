- Proposes amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution as an alternative to MSE training, arguing MSE yields blurry averaged outputs.  
- Main contributions: (1) affine projection layer enforcing LR–HR consistency, (2) three amortized MAP methods (AffGAN, AffDG, AffLL), (3) theoretical link between AffGAN and variational inference.  
- Strong theoretical motivation illustrated by a 2D toy (Swiss-roll) showing MSE blurring vs MAP mode recovery.  
- Affine projection layer is a clever architectural fix enabling consistency and training without paired data.  
- AffGAN produces the most compelling empirical results: sharper, plausible textures and lower cross-entropy while preserving input-output consistency.  
- The paper provides a novel theoretical connection between GANs and variational inference (Appendix F).  
- Introduces an instance-noise technique to stabilize GAN training.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions based on solid theory and promising results.  
- Main weakness: lacks rigorous quantitative evidence of perceptual superiority beyond visual inspection.  
- Suggestion: add perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS) or a user study to validate perceptual quality.  
- Suggestion: explain why AffDG and AffLL underperform relative to AffGAN and whether architectural/training fixes could help.  
- Suggestion: analyze and mitigate AffGAN’s increased high-frequency noise.  
- Suggestion: clarify the relationship and potential disconnect between MAP solutions (high probability) and perceptual quality.  
- Questions for authors: how to improve AffDG/AffLL, results on LPIPS or other perceptual metrics, whether high-frequency noise is inherent to MAP and how to mitigate it, and how noise trade-offs affect downstream tasks.
