- Proposes amortized MAP inference for super-resolution and introduces an affine projection layer to ensure LR consistency.  
- Presents three amortized MAP methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided), claiming sharper, more perceptual results.  
- Recommendation: reject.  
- Main rejection reason: central claim that MAP yields superior perceptual results is insufficiently supported.  
- Theoretical flaw: authors acknowledge MAP modes are atypical in high dimensions, undermining the claim that MAP produces plausible reconstructions.  
- Lack of rigorous perceptual evaluation: relies on PSNR/SSIM and qualitative examples, with no LPIPS/FID or human studies.  
- Example failure: Figure 5 shows plausible-but-incorrect reconstructions (snake → water), highlighting risk of MAP producing wrong but plausible outputs.  
- Inadequate comparisons: fails to compare with modern perceptual SR methods (e.g., ESRGAN, Ledig et al. 2016) and unclear distinction from prior GAN SR work.  
- Methodological concerns: AffDG and AffLL produce blurry results with no explanation for failures.  
- Instance noise stabilization is presented without rigorous justification or ablation studies.  
- Suggestion: conduct perceptual evaluations using LPIPS, FID, or human studies.  
- Suggestion: resolve or justify the contradiction between MAP atypicality and claimed perceptual plausibility, or compare to posterior sampling.  
- Suggestion: include comprehensive comparisons to state-of-the-art perceptual SR methods.  
- Suggestion: explain why AffDG/AffLL fail and whether modifications can salvage them.  
- Suggestion: clarify how AffGAN differs from and improves on Ledig et al. (2016).  
- Key question: how will you validate AffGAN's perceptual superiority with metrics that correlate with human perception?  
- Key question: how do you reconcile MAP atypicality in high dimensions with claims of plausible reconstructions?  
- Key question: how do you evaluate the trade-off between perceptual plausibility and reconstruction accuracy (e.g., snake→water)?  
- Key question: can you provide empirical evidence that AffGAN is more resilient to coordinate transformations beyond theoretical discussion?  
- Key question: why did AffDG and AffLL fail, and how would you modify them to avoid blurry outputs?
