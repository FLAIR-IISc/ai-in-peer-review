Summary
The paper proposes “amortised MAP” inference for single-image super-resolution (SR). The core idea is to enforce measurement consistency via an explicit affine projection layer Π_Ax(fθ(x)) that guarantees Afθ(x)=x for a known linear downsampling operator A, and then learn fθ by maximizing the average log-prior log pY of the projected outputs. This leads to minimizing the cross-entropy H[qθ,pY], where qθ is the distribution of model outputs at random LR inputs. Since direct access to pY is unavailable, the paper explores three surrogates: (i) an affine-projected GAN (AffGAN) with a modified generator update and with “instance noise” for stability; (ii) a denoiser-guided approach (AffDG) using score estimates from a DAE; and (iii) a density-guided approach (AffLL) using a differentiable PixelCNN-like model with MCGSM likelihood. A toy 2D Swiss-roll example suggests AffGAN/AffDG approximate MAP, and qualitative SR results on grass textures, CelebA, and ImageNet show sharper images for AffGAN compared to MSE/MAE baselines, albeit with some high-frequency noise. The paper also claims a connection between (a stochastic version of) AffGAN and amortized variational inference.

Decision: Reject
Key reasons: (1) The theoretical connection between minimizing H[qθ,pY] (or KL[qθ||pY]) and amortized MAP per input x is not rigorously established and, as stated, is at best heuristic; (2) Empirical validation is limited, controlled to an idealized forward model A known and matched at training/evaluation, with weak baselines and largely qualitative comparisons; (3) Numerous methodological, technical, and presentation issues (equation inconsistencies, missing details, reference errors) undermine confidence in correctness and reproducibility.

Supporting arguments
- Amortized MAP vs cross-entropy minimization: The objective Ex log pY(Π_Ax fθ(x)) equals H[qθ,pY] up to a constant, but this ensures only that the marginal output distribution matches the high-density regions of pY. It does not guarantee per-x MAP behavior (i.e., selecting the mode of pY restricted to {y:Ay=x}). Multiple θ can minimize H without realizing the per-input mode; the paper provides no theorem linking the global optimum of H to per-x MAP under capacity constraints. This conceptual gap is central to the claimed contribution.
- Known and matched degradation model: All experiments use a fixed, known A (Gaussian blur + stride) and evaluate consistency with that same A. This makes exact consistency unsurprising and favors the proposed projection. Real-world SR frequently has mismatch/unknown A; the paper neither studies robustness to A-mismatch nor compares to methods designed for unknown degradations.
- Affine projection benefit: The “faster training and better MSE/SSIM” for affine-projected networks trained with MSE are expected given that the architecture hard-wires consistency to the very A used to generate the LR data. This is not a fair test of modeling capacity vs an unconstrained CNN; rather, it shows an architectural bias aligned with the data-generation process.
- GAN objective and claims: The modified GAN generator objective claimed to minimize KL[q||p] is not clearly or correctly stated. Equation (10) appears malformed (missing parentheses/ratio), and Appendix A’s derivation is terse and relies on an ideal Bayes-optimal discriminator and support-overlap—which the paper itself argues often fails. The “instance noise” idea is very close to contemporaneous work advocating noise to ensure overlap (and to label smoothing), so novelty is limited; the paper does not ablate its contribution beyond anecdotal remarks.
- Weak empirical evidence: 
  - Grass textures: AffGAN is preferred qualitatively, but AffDG/AffLL are abandoned after reporting blur. No quantitative perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS, NIQE, human studies) are provided. 
  - CelebA/ImageNet: Qualitative only for AffGAN vs an MSE baseline; PSNR/SSIM favor MSE as expected; perceptual superiority is asserted without rigorous evaluation. Some outputs exhibit noise/semantic hallucinations (“snake → water”), which questions faithfulness even under the known A.
  - No comparisons to contemporaneous SR perceptual methods (e.g., SRGAN) beyond brief mentions; no ablations (effect of A+, instance noise schedule, capacity).
- Appendix F claim: The asserted “first” connection between adversarial methods and amortized VI is overstated; similar connections and divergences relating implicit models and variational bounds were being developed concurrently. Moreover, the main model is deterministic (no z), so the VI interpretation applies only to an unstated stochastic extension; no empirical evaluation of that extension is offered.
- Technical/presentation issues:
  - Numerous typos and inconsistencies: “Shannon-Jensen” vs Jensen-Shannon; inconsistent notation (yA vs Ay), undefined or overloaded symbols (Π_Ax), malformed equations (generator loss), dangling footnotes, and garbled figure/table references.
  - Reference list contains incomplete/broken entries (missing years, duplicated authors, stray commas); some citations are non-standard (“Open source code”).
  - Critical implementation details are ad hoc or unclear: Why 42 kernels for A+? How is A+ guaranteed to be a true pseudoinverse with finite kernels? How are σ schedules chosen in instance noise/DAE annealing? Training data filtering (“remove images <20kB”) is unusual and unexplained.

Additional feedback (to help improve the paper)
- Provide a rigorous statement (with assumptions) relating minimization of Ex[−log pY(Π_Ax fθ(x))] to per-x MAP solutions; or reframe the claim as “mode-seeking amortized inference” with clear caveats.
- Evaluate robustness to A mismatch (e.g., train with one A and test with bicubic; unknown blur; noise), and compare to methods that handle unknown degradations.
- Strengthen empirical evaluation: include perceptual metrics (LPIPS, DISTS), user studies, and stronger baselines (e.g., SRGAN or other perceptual SR models of the time). Perform ablations: no projection vs projection; with/without instance noise; varying A+ accuracy.
- For AffDG/AffLL, either develop training so they are competitive or omit them. If kept, use modern score models/diffusion models or stronger density models; show that the negative results are not due to suboptimal training or poor models.
- Clarify and correct the GAN objective and derivation; relate to f-GAN and to prior KL/JSD generator objectives precisely.
- Clean up notation, equations, figures, and references. Provide code and exact hyperparameters/schedules.

Questions for the authors
1. Under what assumptions does minimizing H[qθ,pY] imply that for almost all x, fθ(x) equals argmax_y:Ay=x log pY(y)? Can you provide a formal result or counterexample?
2. How sensitive is the method to misspecification of A? What happens if the true LR is bicubic or noisy but you project with your Gaussian A?
3. Please precisely specify the generator loss used in AffGAN and reconcile it with the KL[q||p] claim. Did you try standard non-saturating GAN, least-squares GAN, or other f-GAN variants?
4. Can you quantify the effect of instance noise (vs label smoothing, vs no noise) on stability and final quality, with controlled ablations?
5. How is A+ learned/validated, and how close is AA+ to the identity on the LR space for your chosen finite kernels?
6. Do you have quantitative perceptual evaluations (LPIPS/NIQE/FSIM) or small-scale human studies supporting the claimed perceptual gains?
7. For the stochastic AffGAN/VI connection, did you train with z? If so, how do results differ from the deterministic case?
