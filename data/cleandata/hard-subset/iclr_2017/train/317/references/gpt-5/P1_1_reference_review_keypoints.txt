- Paper proposes amortized MAP for single-image SR, arguing MSE/MAE produce blurry averages under multimodal priors.
- Introduces an affine projection layer enforcing exact consistency Afθ(x)=x using A+ to avoid soft penalties.
- Reformulates amortized MAP as minimizing cross-entropy H[qθ, pY], reframing SR as matching model output marginal to the HR prior.
- Proposes three training methods to minimize H[qθ,pY]: AffGAN (GAN variant), AffDG (denoising-score based), and AffLL (differentiable density model).
- Provides theory: generator objective minimizing KL[q||p], instance-noise analysis, and a connection between stochastic AffGAN and amortized VI.
- Empirically validates on a toy Swiss-roll (AffGAN/AffDG recover posterior modes) and shows affine projection yields exact consistency without MSE loss degradation.
- Real-image results (grass, CelebA, ImageNet) are qualitative: AffGAN produces sharper but sometimes noisy or prior-dominant (hallucinated) outputs.
- Decision: Reject.
- Primary reason 1: Insufficient empirical rigor—largely qualitative evaluation, missing perceptual metrics, human studies, and comparisons to strong SR baselines (e.g., SRGAN/perceptual losses).
- Primary reason 2: Two proposed routes (AffDG, AffLL) underperform and are abandoned, leaving AffGAN whose novelty/benefit over existing GAN-based SR is not convincingly shown.
- Literature placement is reasonable; theoretical connections to prior GAN/denoising work are noted but practical assumptions are delicate.
- Theoretical decomposition to cross-entropy is elegant, but KL-minimizing generator relies on strong discriminator optimality and support-overlap conditions.
- Instance-noise analysis is a theoretical strength addressing support overlap issues.
- Swiss-roll experiment supports the cross-entropy objective; affine projection ablation shows exact consistency and importance of correct A+ initialization.
- AffGAN real-image outputs show sharpness but high-frequency noise; AffDG/AffLL yield blurry, unstable training with no remedies provided.
- Major empirical gaps: no LPIPS/FID/KID, no user studies, no matched-backbone comparisons to perceptual/SRGAN methods, and restrictive noiseless observation model.
- Clarity issues: formatting, inconsistent notation, fragile appendix assumptions, though experimental details are extensive.
- Suggested improvements: add modern perceptual metrics, user studies, benchmark SR datasets, realistic degradations, and ablations on instance-noise and discriminator budget.
- Suggested methodological directions: try modern score/density estimators (diffusion/score matching), improve noise/likelihood curricula, and empirically explore amortized VI/stochastic generators for diversity.
- Practicality suggestions: test robustness to A-mismatch and noise, consider learning A, and release code for A+ and affine projection.
- Key questions for authors include sensitivity to A-mismatch/noise, LPIPS/user study comparisons to SRGAN, instance-noise scheduling and D:G training details, projection benefits on standard SR backbones, score estimator accuracy for AffDG, and quantitative evidence that H[qθ,pY] yields posterior-mode outputs per input.
