Summary
This paper proposes amortised MAP inference for single-image super-resolution (SR). The key idea is to enforce exact LR-HR consistency via an affine projection layer ŷ = Π_Ax fθ(x) = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x, where A is the downsampler and A+ its Moore-Penrose pseudoinverse. Under this constraint, training for amortised MAP reduces to minimizing the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the model’s output distribution qθ (induced by LR inputs) and the HR data distribution pY, eliminating the need for paired LR-HR supervision. Three practical instantiations are explored: (1) AffGAN, a GAN variant whose generator is the affine-projected SR network and whose update approximates minimizing KL[qθ||pY], with “instance noise” to stabilize training; (2) AffDG, which uses a denoiser to estimate score ∇y log pY(y) for direct gradient-based optimization; and (3) AffLL, which uses a differentiable density model (PixelCNN/MCGSM) to guide training via likelihood. The paper also connects a stochastic AffGAN to amortised variational inference. Empirically, the affine projection improves stability/consistency; on Swiss-roll, AffGAN/AffDG recover MAP-like solutions; on textures, faces, and ImageNet crops, AffGAN yields visually sharp outputs, sometimes hallucinating plausible content. 

Decision: Accept
Key reasons: Strong conceptual novelty and a bold, principled reframing of SR as amortised MAP under an exact affine projection, together with a theoretically motivated GAN objective (KL[q||p]) and a simple, broadly applicable stabilization technique (instance noise). The affine projection insight is elegant and potentially impactful beyond SR. While some empirical validations are incomplete, the core ideas are sufficiently novel and promising.

Supporting arguments
- Motivation and placement: The paper makes a compelling case against MSE/MAE for perceptual SR and argues for MAP under a natural image prior. The shift from supervised ERM to matching qθ to pY using only HR statistics (thanks to exact projection) is original and well motivated. Related work is fairly covered, including SRGAN and f-GAN, with a clear distinction due to the exact affine constraint and the amortised MAP framing.
- Theory: 
  - The derivation that amortised MAP with exact LR-HR consistency reduces to minimizing H[qθ, pY] is clean and insightful (Eq. 9).
  - The AffGAN generator update corresponds to minimizing KL[q||p] (Appendix A), which is naturally mode-seeking and aligns with MAP.
  - Instance noise is well motivated: it makes discriminators unique/well-conditioned by ensuring overlap via convolution with noise, aligning with later principles in stabilizing GANs.
  - The stochastic AffGAN connection to amortised VI (Appendix F) is interesting and, to my knowledge, novel at the time.
- Empirics:
  - Swiss-roll: AffGAN/AffDG approach MAP cross-entropy; constrained models satisfy LR-HR consistency exactly and outperform soft penalties.
  - Affine projection ablation: Improves MSE/SSIM and training dynamics even under standard MSE training, while guaranteeing zero LR mismatch.
  - Qualitative results: AffGAN produces sharper images than MSE and soft-GAN, with reasonable global consistency and plausible textures. The snake→water example illustrates the prior-driven nature of the approach (and its risks), but also demonstrates the method’s strong prior utilization.
- Scope and impact: The projection layer is simple to adopt; the amortised MAP lens plus instance noise can influence how SR and other inverse problems are approached.

Areas of concern (not blocking, but important)
- Validation depth: There is limited quantitative evaluation on modern perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS, FID/KID) and no user studies, making it hard to substantiate perceptual gains rigorously.
- Baselines: Comparisons to SRGAN or strong contemporary SR architectures/losses are missing.
- “Unpaired” claim: While the framework theoretically removes the need for paired LR-HR data, experiments are conducted with synthetic LR via known A. No results on real, unknown degradations or truly unpaired settings are shown.
- Alternative priors: AffDG/LL underperform and are dropped; this weakens the claim that the framework broadly supports three effective routes. More analysis is needed to understand the failure modes and to fairly represent their promise.
- Faithfulness vs plausibility: The framework is explicitly mode-seeking and may hallucinate content—beneficial for perception but risky for applications needing fidelity.

Suggestions to improve the paper
- Add perceptual metrics (LPIPS, FID/KID), and, if possible, a small-scale human MOS study. Report the fidelity–perception trade-off (e.g., PSNR vs LPIPS curves).
- Include stronger baselines (e.g., SRGAN or VGG-feature/perceptual loss variants), and ablate: affine projection vs soft constraint; instance noise schedules; impact of A+ accuracy.
- Demonstrate unpaired training: train with HR images from a different dataset and LR inputs from another domain or with real camera/downloader degradations; evaluate robustness to mismatched/unknown A.
- Explore stochastic AffGAN empirically: add latent z to allow posterior sampling vs deterministic MAP; analyze diversity vs consistency trade-offs.
- Clarify and release the A+ estimation procedure/code; assess sensitivity to kernel size/stride and to mismatches between assumed and true downsamplers.
- Discuss connections to plug-and-play/RED denoising priors and how your affine projection compares in guarantees and practicality.

Questions for the authors
1) How sensitive is performance to inaccuracies in A and A+? Can your method tolerate unknown or spatially varying degradation operators, and can A+ be learned jointly?
2) With instance noise and finite-capacity discriminators, what divergence is effectively minimized in practice? How do you set/anneal σ, and how sensitive are results to this schedule?
3) Can you provide empirical results for the stochastic AffGAN (with latent z) demonstrating posterior sampling vs MAP behavior and its impact on perceptual quality and LR consistency?
4) Why do AffDG and AffLL yield blurry outputs? Is it due to weak gradients early in training, model capacity, or density model calibration? Could score-based methods with modern denoisers improve AffDG?
5) Beyond synthetic Gaussian downsampling, how does the approach perform on real LR images where A is unknown or non-convolutional?
