- Paper reframes single-image SR as amortised MAP inference with an affine projection ΠA ensuring outputs satisfy Ay = x exactly.
- Under the affine constraint the objective reduces to minimising cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between model outputs and the HR data distribution, enabling unpaired training.
- Proposes three training strategies: AffGAN (GAN variant with modified loss + instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-guided score backpropagation), and AffLL (differentiable density model providing log-density gradients).
- Empirically, on a 2D Swiss-roll toy AffGAN/AffDG match posterior modes and approach brute-force MAP cross-entropy.
- On images, affine projection does not harm MSE training and may help; AffGAN produces sharper perceptual results for 4× SR while AffDG/AffLL produce blurry outputs.
- Appendix links a stochastic AffGAN to amortised variational inference.
- Decision: Reject.
- Key reason 1: Insufficient empirical rigor and missing comparisons on standard SR benchmarks.
- Key reason 2: Only the GAN variant shows compelling results; denoiser/density-guided methods underperform and lack strong evidence.
- Key reason 3: Important claims (GAN→KL minimisation, instance-noise benefits) lack thorough ablation and validation.
- Motivation: The amortised MAP framing and affine projection are well-motivated and connect cleanly to GANs, DAEs, and density models.
- Technical soundness: The decomposition to prior maximisation and use of ΠA are correct; GAN objective plausibly targets KL under strong assumptions but needs empirical support.
- Novelty: Affine projection layer and theoretical unification are notable, but overlap exists with perceptual SR work (e.g., SRGAN); main novelty is projection and amortised-MAP view.
- Empirical weaknesses: No evaluation on Set5/Set14/BSD100/Urban100 or against strong supervised baselines; perceptual metrics (LPIPS/FID/NIQE) and user studies are missing.
- More empirical gaps: No ablations for projection vs soft penalty, instance-noise schedules, GAN loss variants, or robustness to A/A+ mis-specification.
- Scope/assumptions: Assumes known Gaussian downsampling kernel A; real SR often has unknown or spatially varying kernels and this is not explored.
- Constructive: Strengthen evaluations with standard benchmarks, perceptual metrics, ablations, and user studies.
- Constructive: Improve or clarify AffDG/AffLL (stronger score/density models, training curricula) or explain practical limits.
- Constructive: Precisely state theoretical conditions for GAN→KL claims, study H[q] effects, and provide empirical evidence for claimed behaviours.
- Presentation: Fix formatting, move key hyperparameters into main text, and provide algorithm boxes for AffGAN/AffDG/AffLL.
- Questions for authors: Provide SOTA comparisons and perceptual metrics; evaluate sensitivity to A/A+ and kernel mismatch; ablate instance noise; test stronger score/density estimators; clarify theoretical guarantees for per-x MAP and unpaired training benefits.
