Summary and contributions
- The paper tackles single-image super-resolution by framing it as amortised MAP inference. The key idea is to enforce exact data-consistency with the downsampling operator via an affine projection layer gθ(x) = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x, where A is the downsampler and A+ its Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse. This converts the amortised MAP objective into minimising the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the distribution of outputs qθ and the natural image prior pY.
- Three training strategies are proposed to approximate this objective without paired LR–HR supervision: (i) AffGAN, a GAN variant with a modified generator update claimed to minimise KL[q||p] and stabilised by “instance noise”; (ii) AffDG, which uses scores estimated by a denoising autoencoder to provide gradients of log pY; and (iii) AffLL, using a tractable density model (PixelCNN-like with MCGSM) to directly optimise log pY.
- Empirically, the affine projection is shown not to hurt MSE training, and AffGAN produces perceptually sharper results on grass textures, CelebA, and ImageNet (4× SR), while AffDG and AffLL underperform.

Decision: Reject
Primary reasons: (1) Numerous methodological, theoretical, and presentation issues (many minor but collectively substantial) that reduce confidence and reproducibility; (2) Empirical evaluation does not sufficiently support the central claims beyond a toy example and qualitative images; key baselines and metrics are missing.

Supporting arguments
- Novelty and positioning: The affine data-consistency projection is elegant and aligns with long-standing inverse-problem practice (exact data-fidelity constraints). However, the paper does not convincingly position this relative to existing SR literature that uses exact/soft consistency or proximal/projected architectures. The assertion that this can be “added to any CNN” is plausible but the practicalities (e.g., boundary handling, kernel support, aliasing) are under-specified.
- Theory:
  - The reduction from amortised MAP to cross-entropy minimisation via the affine projection is clear and useful.
  - The claim that the modified GAN generator update minimises KL[q||p] relies on a unique Bayes-optimal discriminator and ignores the effect of instance noise on the divergence actually optimised. Appendix A sketches the argument but mixes objectives (Eq. 10 vs Eq. 17) and suffers from typos; it does not formally show that with instance noise the generator minimises KL of the smoothed distributions.
  - The connection to amortised VI (Appendix F) is interesting but high-level; it assumes exact consistency and deterministic A+, and seems close in spirit to prior work on implicit inference models. The “first time” claim is too strong.
- Empirics:
  - The only quantitative support for approximating the MAP objective is on a 2D Swiss-roll. On real images, there is no estimate of cross-entropy (understandable), but also no perceptual evaluation (user studies, LPIPS), and no comparison to strong SR baselines (e.g., SRGAN, perceptual-loss methods). PSNR/SSIM are reported only to show MSE is better there; they do not validate perceptual gains.
  - The denoiser- and density-guided methods are presented as core contributions but are dropped after producing blurry outputs. This weakens the multi-pronged claim and suggests practical challenges not addressed (e.g., score network training stability, density model sharpness vs support mismatch). The annealing curricula are heuristic and not ablated.
  - The affine projection’s benefit under MSE is shown, but potential confounds remain: initialisation advantages, capacity differences, and whether A+ is fixed vs trainable. Statistical significance/error bars are missing.
  - The downsampling model A is Gaussian+stride, not bicubic (standard in SR), and the ImageNet/CelebA preprocessing choices simplify the task. Sensitivity to A-mismatch (inevitable in practice) is not evaluated.
- Reproducibility and clarity:
  - Many typos, broken equations, and reference issues: e.g., L(G;D) in Eq. (10) is malformed; the GAN objective in Eq. (17) differs; “42 kernels” appears to mean 4^2; “Affinit” in Fig. 3; multiple references have missing years and LaTeX artifacts; footnotes bleed into text; numerous formatting glitches. These hinder verification.
  - Critical details are underspecified: boundary conditions for A/A+, exact kernel supports, λ for soft constraints, noise schedule for instance noise (beyond a heuristic), training time and compute, and randomness control.

Additional feedback to improve the paper (helpful, not all decisive)
- Clarify and rigorously derive the GAN update and divergence with and without instance noise; reconcile Eq. (10) and Appendix A; specify when the method minimises JS vs KL of smoothed distributions; discuss entropy term H[q] and its effect on “MAP-ness.”
- Provide an ablation on instance noise (variance schedule, effect on D optimality, uniqueness) and its sensitivity.
- Expand experiments:
  - Use standard SR setups (bicubic x4), and compare against strong baselines (e.g., SRGAN, perceptual/VGG loss, ESRGAN-style if allowed) with both distortion and perception metrics (LPIPS, MOS/2AFC).
  - Evaluate robustness to A-mismatch and boundary handling. Show that the exact consistency survives approximate A+ (finite kernels), and quantify residual errors.
  - Include statistical analysis over multiple seeds; report training stability and failure rates.
- Strengthen AffDG/AffLL or scope them down. For AffDG, consider modern score matching (noise-conditioned score networks) and SDE/annealed approaches; for AffLL, compare to modern continuous autoregressive flows or diffusion models with tractable likelihoods.
- Clarify what “unpaired” supervision means here: you still need x ∼ pX; how is pX obtained in practice? Spell out the data pipeline.
- Improve presentation: fix equations, references, figure captions; standardize notation; remove LaTeX artifacts; ensure all claims are supported and appropriately hedged.

Questions for the authors
- What exactly is the generator loss used in practice (please give the precise formula)? How does it relate to Eq. (17), and does instance noise change the divergence being minimised?
- How is A+ handled at image boundaries, and what is the kernel support used? How sensitive are results to the accuracy of A+ and to the choice of A (e.g., bicubic vs Gaussian)?
- For SoftGAN/SoftDG, what weight(s) were used on the consistency penalty? Did you tune λ to achieve near-zero residuals? How does performance vary with λ?
- Can you provide quantitative perceptual evaluations (LPIPS, user studies)? How does AffGAN compare to SRGAN/perceptual-loss baselines under identical A and data?
- For AffDG, what noise levels and network capacities were used? Did you try noise-conditioned score networks or improved annealing schedules? Can you show that the learned score approximates ∇ log pY (sanity checks)?
- How diverse is qθ? Does the entropy term in AffGAN lead to multimodal outputs for the same x (if stochastic), and how does that relate to the MAP target?
- Will you release code and pretrained models to facilitate reproducibility?
