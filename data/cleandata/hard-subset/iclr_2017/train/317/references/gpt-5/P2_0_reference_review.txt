Summary and contributions
- The paper reframes single-image super-resolution (SR) as amortised MAP inference: maximise the expected log-posterior over outputs subject to the exact downsampling constraint Ay = x. Using an affine projection layer ΠAx = (I − A+A)· + A+ x with the Moore-Penrose inverse A+ ensures outputs always lie in the affine subspace consistent with the LR input.
- Under this constraint, the amortised MAP objective reduces to minimising the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the distribution of model outputs and the HR data distribution. This enables training without paired LR–HR supervision and connects SR to generative modelling.
- Three training strategies are proposed to reduce H[qθ, pY]: (i) AffGAN, a GAN variant claimed to minimise KL[qθ‖pY] with an alternative generator loss and “instance noise” to stabilise training; (ii) AffDG, which backpropagates score estimates from a denoiser (DAE) to follow ∇y log pY(y); (iii) AffLL, which uses a differentiable density model (PixelCNN-like with MCGSM likelihood) to provide log-density gradients.
- Empirically: on a 2D Swiss-roll toy problem, AffGAN/AffDG match the posterior mode and achieve cross-entropy close to brute-force MAP; on images, the affine projection does not hurt (and may help) MSE training; for textures and faces/natural images with 4× SR, AffGAN produces sharper, perceptually plausible results, while AffDG/AffLL yield blurry outputs. An appendix connects a stochastic AffGAN to amortised variational inference.

Decision: Reject
Key reasons: (1) Insufficient empirical rigor and limited comparisons on standard SR benchmarks; (2) Only the GAN-based variant delivers compelling results, while the denoiser/density-guided approaches—central to the paper’s “amortised MAP” thesis—underperform and lack solid evidence; (3) Several claims (KL minimisation via GAN objective, instance-noise benefits) are not validated with thorough ablations.

Supporting arguments
- Motivation and placement: Viewing SR as amortised MAP with an explicit affine projection is a clean and well-motivated framing. The connection to cross-entropy minimisation and to score/density models is conceptually appealing and well placed in the literature (GANs, DAEs, PixelCNNs). The critique of MSE/PSNR for perceptual quality is appropriate.
- Technical soundness: The decomposition from expected log-posterior to the constrained prior maximisation, and the use of ΠAx to satisfy Ay = x exactly, are correct and useful. The equivalence to minimising H[qθ, pY] over the output distribution is sound; with sufficient capacity, this recovers per-x MAP by maximising the prior along each affine slice. The GAN objective variant plausibly targets KL[q‖p] when D is optimal, and instance noise addresses the non-overlapping-support pathology; however, these come with strong assumptions (optimal D, annealing schedule), and empirical validation is minimal.
- Novelty: The affine projection layer that enforces exact LR consistency is a practical and notable contribution compared with soft LR penalties. The theoretical unification (GAN/DAE/density) for amortised MAP is interesting. That said, contemporaneous work (e.g., SRGAN, perceptual losses) already targets perceptual SR; the primary novelty here rests on the projection-layer formulation and the amortised-MAP view.
- Empirical support: The 2D toy results neatly validate the objective. On images, evidence is thin:
  - No evaluation on standard SR benchmarks (Set5/Set14/BSD100/Urban100) or against strong supervised baselines (EDSR/RCAN and SRGAN-like methods). Perceptual metrics (LPIPS, NIQE, FID/KID) and user studies are absent; only PSNR/SSIM are reported where GANs are expected to underperform.
  - The affine projection’s benefit is shown only under MSE on CelebA with limited analysis; no ablation of A/A+ mis-specification or robustness.
  - AffDG and AffLL are central to the thesis but produce blurry outputs and exhibit training instabilities; they are then de-emphasised. This undercuts the claim that amortised MAP via score/density guidance is practically effective.
  - Instance noise is stated as crucial, but there is no systematic ablation (with/without noise, different noise schedules) or quantitative stability evidence.
- Scope/assumptions: The method assumes known A with a Gaussian downsampling kernel. Many real SR settings have unknown or spatially varying kernels; the paper does not explore misspecification. The advantage of “unpaired” training is muted because training still relies on LR obtained via A from HR images (as is standard in SR).

Constructive feedback to improve the paper
- Strengthen empirical evaluation:
  - Compare against strong supervised and perceptual baselines on standard datasets; include PSNR/SSIM for fidelity and perceptual metrics (LPIPS, NIQE/PI, FID/KID), and ideally a small-scale user study.
  - Provide ablations: with/without affine projection; soft LR penalty vs exact projection; instance noise schedules and magnitudes; GAN loss variants; capacity controls.
  - Evaluate robustness to kernel mismatch and to unknown A; consider learning A or using plug-and-play priors.
- Make AffDG and AffLL competitive or clarify limits:
  - Improve score/density models (modern score-based models, better PixelCNN variants) and training curricula; show cases where they help or clearly explain why amortised-MAP via these routes struggles.
  - Quantify when the cross-entropy objective is well approximated in high dimensions.
- Clarify theory and claims:
  - Precisely state conditions under which the GAN objective minimises KL[q‖p], and relate to f-GAN and regularisation by instance noise. Provide empirical evidence that the modified loss leads to KL-like behaviour.
  - Discuss the effect of the H[q] term (cross-entropy vs KL) on the per-x MAP property and diversity.
- Improve clarity and presentation:
  - Fix formatting issues, improve figure readability, and move key hyperparameters and training details from the appendix into the main text where needed.
  - Provide a succinct algorithm box for AffGAN/AffDG/AffLL with the affine projection.

Questions for the authors
- Can you provide quantitative perceptual metrics and SOTA comparisons on standard SR benchmarks? How does AffGAN fare against SRGAN and recent supervised methods?
- How sensitive is performance to the accuracy of A and A+? What happens under kernel mismatch or spatially varying blur?
- Can you ablate instance noise (amount, schedule) and show training stability curves and final quality differences?
- For AffDG and AffLL, can stronger score/density estimators (e.g., modern score models or improved PixelCNNs) recover sharpness? Is the limitation fundamental or due to model/training choices?
- Does minimising H[qθ, pY] with the affine constraint provably recover per-x MAP under model capacity limits? If not, what failure modes arise?
- How does the method perform when trained only on HR images (unpaired) versus paired synthetic LR-HR? Is there a practical advantage in typical SR pipelines?
