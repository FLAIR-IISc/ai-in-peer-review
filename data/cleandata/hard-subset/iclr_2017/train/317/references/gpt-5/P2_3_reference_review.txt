Summary and contributions
- The paper frames single image super-resolution (SR) as amortised MAP inference under a known linear degradation x=Ay. It proposes an affine projection layer ŷ = ΠAx fθ(x) = (I−A+A)fθ(x)+A+x that guarantees data-consistency (Aŷ=x) by construction, turning the amortised MAP objective into minimising the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the model’s marginal output distribution and the HR data distribution. This obviates per-pixel supervised losses and supports training via natural image statistics.
- Three training strategies are proposed: (i) AffGAN, a GAN variant with a modified generator objective that is argued to minimise KL[q‖p] (plus entropy), combined with “instance noise” (adding noise to inputs of D) to stabilise training; (ii) AffDG, backpropagating denoiser gradients as ∇y log p(y) estimates via a DAE; and (iii) AffLL, training against a learned tractable density model (PixelCNN/MCGSM).
- The paper further argues that AffGAN corresponds to amortised variational inference in a stochastic extension, and empirically shows: (a) on a 2D Swiss-roll toy problem, AffGAN/AffDG approach MAP; (b) an affine projection layer does not harm and may accelerate MSE training; (c) on grass textures, faces (CelebA), and ImageNet, AffGAN yields sharper, more plausible images than MSE or soft-constraint GANs.

Decision: Reject
Key reasons:
- Insufficient empirical validation against strong SR baselines and perceptual metrics; experiments are limited and largely qualitative, with no human study or modern perceptual metrics, and AffDG/AffLL fail to produce competitive results.
- The approach critically assumes a known, fixed A (Gaussian blur + strided downsampling) and numerically approximated A+, which limits realism and generality; there is no evaluation under degradation mismatch, noise, or unknown A typical of practical SR.

Supporting arguments
- Motivation and positioning: The motivation to move beyond MSE/PSNR to perceptually plausible SR is sound and well-situated. The idea to enforce exact data-consistency via an affine projection is natural for linear inverse problems and valuable. The cross-entropy reformulation is elegant and clarifies why adversarial/score-based priors can be used without paired losses.
- Theoretical claims: The derivations that affine projection turns amortised MAP into H[qθ, pY] minimisation are clear. The modified GAN generator update to approximate KL[q‖p] is interesting, though the main text relies on appendix-level arguments and practical training uses instance noise, which shifts the divergence to a smoothed dσ; this weakens the claim about which divergence is actually optimised. The amortised VI connection for a stochastic generator is insightful but remains primarily conceptual; there is no empirical demonstration of posterior sampling or calibration.
- Empirical evidence:
  - Toy Swiss-roll: convincing sanity check; AffGAN/AffDG lower cross-entropy and resemble MAP.
  - Affine projection with MSE: showing zero consistency error and slightly better MSE/SSIM is useful; however, performance likely benefits from perfect knowledge of A and may not generalise if A is mismatched.
  - Real images: Visuals suggest AffGAN is sharper than MSE/soft-GAN; however, evaluation is narrow. There is no comparison with contemporary SR methods (e.g., SRGAN/SRResNet, EDSR, RCAN) on standard benchmarks (Set5/14, BSD100, Urban100, DIV2K) using standard bicubic A. Quantitatively, PSNR/SSIM favour MSE as expected; there is no complementary perceptual metric (LPIPS, FID, NIQE) or user study to substantiate the perceptual claim. The noted high-frequency noise and hallucinations (e.g., snake→water) raise the need for more controlled evaluation (fidelity vs realism trade-off).
  - AffDG and AffLL: These methods underperform and are abandoned; the paper speculates on optimisation issues but provides no fixes or competitive results. This weakens the broader claim that amortised MAP can be achieved via multiple principled routes.
- Generality: The method presumes a known, fixed A and computes A+ numerically; in practice A is often unknown, non-Gaussian, spatially varying, or includes noise. No experiments test robustness to A mismatch or observation noise. The claim that paired HR/LR is unnecessary is nuanced: training still uses x sampled by downsampling HR images with the same A; the setup effectively assumes knowledge of the exact forward operator.

Additional feedback to improve the paper (helpful, not decisive)
- Benchmarks and baselines:
  - Evaluate on standard SR datasets (Set5/14, BSD100, Urban100, DIV2K) with bicubic degradation; compare to SRResNet/SRGAN and strong MSE baselines (e.g., EDSR/RCAN).
  - Include perceptual metrics (LPIPS, FID/KID, NIQE/PI) and, ideally, a small-scale user study to support perceptual claims.
  - Report ablations: with/without affine projection; varying A+/kernel sizes; instance noise schedule vs label smoothing vs WGAN-GP; generator loss variants.
- Robustness:
  - Test mismatch: train with one A and evaluate with another; add realistic sensor noise; evaluate data-consistency under approximate A+.
  - Consider unknown A: joint estimation of A or plug-and-play consistency modules; discuss feasibility.
- Theory/clarity:
  - Clarify the exact divergence optimised with instance noise (dσ) and its implications; provide empirical evidence that instance noise improves stability/convergence beyond ablations.
  - Expand the amortised VI connection with a stochastic generator: show posterior sample diversity per x and calibration (e.g., coverage under synthetic degradations).
  - Discuss coordinate dependence of MAP more concretely and whether entropy term in AffGAN meaningfully mitigates it in practice (empirical test with colour-space changes or feature-space MAP).
- Practical aspects:
  - Provide details on computational cost, training stability (failure rates), and inference speed.
  - Release code for computing A+ and the affine projection layer; document kernel sizes and numerical precision issues.

Questions for the authors
- How sensitive is performance to mismatch between the assumed A used in training/inference and the true degradation (kernel width, stride, downsampler type), and to additive noise? Can you report results under controlled mismatch/noise?
- What is the quantitative effect of the affine projection on perceptual quality for GAN-based SR when evaluated with LPIPS/NIQE or a MOS study?
- Can you provide ablations showing the benefit of instance noise over standard stabilisation tricks (e.g., one-sided label smoothing, spectral norm, gradient penalty)? How do results change if you train D to near-convergence vs a few steps?
- For AffDG/AffLL, can you stabilise training with modern score-based/diffusion denoisers or improved density models? Are the failures due to model capacity or optimisation?
- In the VI view, can you demonstrate meaningful conditional diversity for fixed x (multiple ŷ samples) and assess whether samples cover the posterior’s plausible modes without violating data-consistency?
- How is A+ chosen when the exact Moore-Penrose inverse has non-local support? How large must the deconvolution kernel be to keep consistency error negligible, and what is the runtime impact?
