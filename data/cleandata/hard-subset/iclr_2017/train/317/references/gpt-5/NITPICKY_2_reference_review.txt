Summary
The paper proposes “amortised MAP inference” for single-image super-resolution (SR). The key idea is to enforce exact data consistency by projecting network outputs onto the affine subspace {y: Ay = x} using gθ(x) = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x, where A is the known downsampling operator and A+ its Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse. Under this construction, maximizing Ex log pY(gθ(x)) reduces to minimizing the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the distribution of projected outputs and the HR data distribution, removing the need for paired LR–HR supervision. Three training strategies are proposed: (i) AffGAN, a GAN variant claimed to minimize KL[qθ||pY] (with “instance noise” to stabilize training); (ii) AffDG, which uses a denoiser to estimate the score ∇y log pY and backpropagate it through the generator; and (iii) AffLL, which uses a tractable density model (PixelCNN with MCGSM) to provide log-likelihood gradients. Experiments on a 2D Swiss roll, grass textures, CelebA, and ImageNet suggest AffGAN yields the visually sharpest outputs; affine projection improves training stability and exactly enforces LR consistency.

Decision: Reject
Key reasons: (1) Significant issues with rigor, clarity, and correctness (broken equations/notation, missing details, and over-claims about theory and supervision). (2) Empirical evaluation is limited and selectively reported; two of three proposed approaches underperform and are largely abandoned. The paper’s stated goal (amortized MAP SR) is not convincingly demonstrated beyond a toy problem and qualitative examples.

Supporting arguments
- Theory/claims:
  - “Amortised MAP”: The objective Ex log pY(gθ(x)) = −H[qθ, pY] does not by itself guarantee per-x MAP solutions; it encourages outputs to lie in high-density regions of pY subject to Ay=x. With sufficient capacity the per-x decisions decouple, but this is neither proven nor analyzed. The failure case “snake→water” underscores that the method can choose globally likely y (under pY) that are semantically inconsistent with the specific x.
  - AffGAN minimizing KL[q||p]: Equation (10) appears incorrect (generator loss mixes log D and D; missing logit term). Appendix A’s derivation depends on a Bayes-optimal unique discriminator and well-behaved likelihood ratios—assumptions violated when supports don’t overlap (acknowledged later). The proposed instance noise addresses this, but the theoretical link to KL minimization in the practical setting remains tenuous, and experiments do not quantify divergence reduction.
  - Affine projection: Using P = I − A+ A as a projection to null(A) depends on A+ being the true Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse. Here A+ is approximated by a local transposed convolution learned via Monte Carlo objectives (Appendix B). No proof that the learned A+ satisfies MP properties; boundary conditions and stride-induced aliasing not discussed. Claims of “exact to numerical precision” need quantitative evidence (e.g., spectral norm bounds, idempotence tests).
  - “No paired data needed”: In practice the method still requires HR samples from pY and LR samples from pX generated via the same A (and often from the same HR dataset). The practical advantage over standard supervised SR (which also constructs pairs via A) is not convincingly demonstrated.

- Empirical evaluation:
  - Two of three methods (AffDG, AffLL) are reported to produce very blurry outputs; the paper then largely drops them. This undermines the central claim that the framework provides three viable routes to amortized MAP SR.
  - Quantitative evaluation is thin. For faces, PSNR/SSIM favor MSE, as expected; no human studies, LPIPS, or other perceptual metrics are reported to support claims of superior perceptual quality. No ablations to isolate the effects of affine projection, instance noise, or the specific generator/discriminator architectures.
  - The 2D Swiss roll supports the objective equivalence but relies on KDE approximations; the HR experiments are largely qualitative. The “snake→water” example is an explicit failure mode for SR fidelity.
  - The “affine vs soft” comparison is promising but incomplete: soft constraints’ weight, convergence behavior, and failure modes are not systematically explored.

- Presentation/clarity:
  - Numerous typographical/formatting issues: broken sentences, missing/garbled equations (e.g., Eq. 10), inconsistent notation, figures/tables referenced but not shown, and many malformed references (“? \Q2016\E”). This severely impedes reproducibility and confidence in the results.
  - Important implementation details are missing or vague (e.g., exact boundary conditions for A, precise A+ kernel sizes vs downsampling kernel widths, noise annealing schedule and its sensitivity).

Additional feedback (to help improve the paper)
- Clarify and correct all equations (especially GAN objectives) and unify notation. Provide a precise statement: under what conditions does minimizing Ex[−log pY(gθ(x))] produce per-x MAP? Include a proof or counterexamples and practical implications.
- Provide rigorous analysis of the affine projection: assumptions on A (Toeplitz/circulant, boundary handling), justification that the learned A+ approximates the MP inverse, and empirical tests (idempotence, projection error vs kernel size).
- Evaluate perceptual quality with modern metrics (LPIPS) and/or user studies; report FID/KID between qθ and pY to support distributional alignment claims. Add ablations for instance noise (on/off, schedules), fixing vs learning A+, and capacity of fθ.
- Strengthen the claim “no paired data” by training with unpaired HR and LR sets (e.g., HR from dataset A, LR from a different source) and demonstrating robustness.
- Revisit AffDG and AffLL: if they are part of the contribution, provide stronger models (modern score models or diffusion-based scores; improved density estimators) and stabilize training, or clearly scope them as exploratory.
- Discuss real-world deployment: mismatch in A at test time, robustness to unknown blur/aliasing, and failure modes. Consider stochastic generators to approximate sampling from pY|X rather than point estimates.
- Release code and pre-trained models; detail hyperparameters, training schedules, and computational costs.

Questions for the authors
1) Under what precise conditions (on model capacity and A+) does minimizing Ex[−log pY(gθ(x))] recover per-x MAP? Can you show a counterexample where it doesn’t?
2) Is A+ exactly MP with your learned local kernel? Please report projection idempotence errors, ‖(I − A+ A)^2 − (I − A+ A)‖, and data-fidelity errors across datasets/boundaries.
3) How critical is instance noise? Provide quantitative stability metrics and performance with/without noise and with different schedules.
4) Can you demonstrate training with genuinely unpaired HR/LR sets? What are the results relative to paired training?
5) How sensitive is AffGAN to mismatch between the true downsampling kernel and the assumed A during training/testing?
6) For AffDG/AffLL, can stronger modern score/density models remedy the blurriness? If not, why?
7) Beyond qualitative samples, can you provide LPIPS/human studies to substantiate perceptual superiority claims?
