Summary and contributions
- The paper proposes amortised MAP inference for single-image super-resolution (SR), arguing that MAP, rather than MSE/MAE risk minimization, better aligns with natural image priors and perceptual plausibility. The key idea is to enforce exact data-consistency with the known downsampling operator A by inserting an affine projection layer ŷ = ΠAx fθ(x) = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x, where A+ is the Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse. This turns the constrained MAP problem into minimizing the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY], where qθ is the marginal distribution of model outputs when fed LR inputs, thereby removing the need for paired LR–HR supervision.
- Three training routes to approximate cross-entropy minimization are developed: (1) an affine-projected GAN (AffGAN) with a modified generator loss that targets KL[qθ || pY], plus a stabilization trick termed instance noise; (2) a denoiser-guided approach (AffDG) using score estimates from a DAE to backpropagate ∂y log pY; (3) a density-guided approach (AffLL) using a differentiable PixelCNN-like model with MCGSM likelihoods.
- The paper provides a theoretical analysis connecting the modified GAN loss to KL minimization and shows that with a stochastic generator, AffGAN performs amortised variational inference minimizing Ex KL(qθ(y|x) || p(y|x)).
- Empirically, the affine projection layer ensures exact consistency (Ax = x) and speeds training under MSE. On a 2D Swiss-roll toy problem, AffGAN/AffDG approximate MAP solutions and achieve low cross-entropy. On textures, faces (CelebA), and natural images (ImageNet), AffGAN produces perceptually sharper SR than MSE/MAE or soft-constraint baselines; AffDG/AffLL underperform perceptually. Instance noise is critical for stable GAN training.

Decision: Accept
Key reasons: (1) Clear, principled formulation of SR as amortised MAP with an elegant, architectural projection ensuring exact data-consistency; (2) solid theoretical links tying the objective to cross-entropy, KL, and amortised VI; (3) practically relevant training innovation (instance noise) with convincing qualitative improvements for perceptual SR.

Supporting arguments
- Novelty and motivation: The affine projection is a simple but powerful idea that codifies known physics of the forward operator and prevents “cheating” via soft penalties. Casting amortised MAP as cross-entropy minimization aligns SR with generative modeling and avoids brittle perceptual losses. The paper is well positioned relative to GAN-based SR (SRGAN) and denoiser priors, and it clarifies the role of exact vs soft consistency.
- Theory: The decomposition of the MAP objective and its reduction to cross-entropy over qθ is clean. The modified GAN generator objective and its connection to KL[q||p] is plausible under standard GAN assumptions (optimal D). The amortised VI interpretation for the stochastic AffGAN is a refreshing conceptual link. The instance noise analysis correctly identifies manifold-disjoint support as a root of instability and frames instance noise as minimizing KL of convolved distributions.
- Empirical support: The Swiss-roll study demonstrates the objective alignment (cross-entropy) and the failure modes of MSE/MAE. The MSE-based experiments with and without affine projection show that enforcing exact consistency helps even in MSE training (better/faster). On real images, AffGAN produces sharper, plausible results; qualitative examples illustrate that the method can “dream” plausible high-frequency details consistent with a natural image prior.

Caveats and weaknesses (do not block acceptance)
- MAP vs KL/cross-entropy mismatch: The paper’s overarching narrative is amortised MAP (minimizing cross-entropy), but the practical AffGAN training minimizes KL[q||p], which includes an H[q] term and generally favors higher-entropy solutions than strict MAP. While this can be beneficial (diverse, less mode-seeking), the conceptual gap should be acknowledged more prominently, and the implications for “MAP-ness” clarified.
- Empirical evaluation scope: The real-image evaluation is mostly qualitative. There is no human perceptual study or modern perceptual metric (e.g., LPIPS). Comparisons to contemporary perceptual SR baselines (e.g., SRGAN with VGG feature loss) are limited, and benchmarks (e.g., Set5/14, DIV2K) with standard bicubic degradations are not reported. The degradation model is a fixed Gaussian-strided convolution; robustness to mismatch in A (common in practice) is not evaluated.
- AffDG and AffLL underperform and are dropped early. This weakens the claim that they are viable alternatives; more analysis is needed (e.g., stronger density models, better score estimation, annealing schedules).
- Clarity and presentation: The manuscript has formatting issues, some truncated sentences, and a few reference glitches. The “no paired data” claim is nuanced: you still need pX consistent with A; in practice the paper downscales HR images, essentially creating pairs.

Suggestions to improve the paper
- Clarify the relationship between amortised MAP (cross-entropy) and AffGAN’s KL target; discuss when they coincide or diverge and how this affects perceptual outcomes.
- Add quantitative perceptual evaluation: user studies and/or perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS, FID on crops), and include comparisons to feature-perceptual losses (VGG-based), SRGAN, and strong PSNR models (e.g., VDSR/EDSR) to illustrate the perception–distortion trade-off.
- Evaluate on standard SR benchmarks and degradations (bicubic), and study robustness to A mismatch. Include ablations for instance noise (schedule, variance), discriminator capacity, and the effect of fixing vs learning A+.
- Provide deeper analysis of AffDG/AffLL failures: try stronger autoregressive or flow models, improved score models, and principled noise/likelihood annealing; connect to plug-and-play/RED literature.
- Improve clarity and polish: fix typos and clipped sentences; better highlight the conditions under which “no pairs needed” holds; release code for A+ estimation and the affine projection layer.

Questions for the authors
1. Training data pairing: Do you ever train with completely unpaired LR and HR sets (e.g., LR from one source, HR from another) and still succeed? If not, please clarify the practical meaning of “no matched pairs needed.”
2. Degradation model: How sensitive is AffGAN to misspecification of A at test time? Can the method be extended to learn A jointly or to handle unknown blur/noise?
3. MAP vs KL: Given AffGAN minimizes KL[q||p], do you view AffGAN as amortised MAP or a different target? Can you quantify the entropy of q in practice and its effect on perceived realism?
4. Instance noise: Please provide ablations on the noise schedule and comparisons to other stabilization methods (e.g., one-sided label smoothing, gradient penalty, spectral norm).
5. Stochastic generator: Can you demonstrate posterior sampling (multiple ŷ per x) with the stochastic variant and assess diversity vs fidelity?
6. AffDG/AffLL: What are the main failure modes you observed (e.g., vanishing gradients near the data manifold, mode collapse to blurry means)? Could improved score/density estimators (e.g., modern score-based models) remedy this?
