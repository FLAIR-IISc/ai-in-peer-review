- Reframes single-image SR as amortised MAP inference with observation model x = Ay, reducing under linear A to maximising a prior with hard constraint Afθ(x)=x.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer ŷ = ΠAx fθ(x) that enforces exact LR-HR consistency, can be inserted into CNNs, and empirically speeds training without hurting MSE/SSIM.  
- Shows the training objective is equivalent to minimising H[qθ,pY] (cross-entropy between model outputs and HR data) and proposes three approximations: AffGAN, AffDG, and AffLL.  
- AffGAN: GAN variant targeting KL[q||p] with instance noise for stability; AffDG: uses denoiser-based score estimates; AffLL: trains against a tractable continuous density model.  
- On a 2D toy, AffGAN/AffDG approach the MAP solution; on images AffGAN yields sharper, more plausible outputs while AffDG/LL are blurry; affine projection enforces consistency and speeds training.  
- Decision: Reject (borderline) — empirical evidence is limited and does not convincingly show amortised MAP improves perceptual SR at scale.  
- Reason 1: Missing key baselines and perceptual metrics; AffGAN shows artifacts and depends on modestly novel training tricks.  
- Reason 2: Conceptual distinctions are blurred (MAP vs distribution matching; “no paired data”), rely on strong assumptions (known A, accurate priors), and DG/LL variants fail empirically.  
- Novelty/positioning: Affine projection is a useful, practical contribution, but the objective and ideas overlap with plug-and-play, deep generative priors, and conditional generative modeling and need tighter positioning vs SRGAN/score-based methods.  
- Stabilization/generator-loss contributions are incremental relative to f-GAN/WGAN-GP/etc., and no empirical comparisons are provided.  
- Variational inference connection is noted but similar to conditional VAEs and not a distinct breakthrough.  
- Correctness: The derivation linking Ex log pY(ΠAx fθ(x)) to H[q,p] is correct, but the GAN generator loss relies on strong, often-unmet assumptions about discriminator optimality.  
- Minimising H[q,p] encourages high-density outputs but does not guarantee per-x MAP and can hallucinate globally common textures for ambiguous inputs.  
- The claim of “no paired data” is overstated — training still requires known A and sampling x consistent with A.  
- Empirical evaluation is mostly qualitative: lacks LPIPS/FID/KID/DISTS, MOS/human studies, and comparisons to modern SR baselines (SRGAN/ESRGAN/RCAN) on standard benchmarks with bicubic ×4.  
- Use of a Gaussian A downsampling prevents easy comparison to established literature and protocols.  
- Reported GAN artifacts (high-frequency noise, color drift) and AffDG/LL failures indicate prior-driven approaches are fragile; only affine-projection ablations with MSE are convincing.  
- Cross-entropy improvement is only shown in 2D; no high-dimensional quantitative proxy is provided.  
- Recommended experiments: compare to SRGAN/ESRGAN with perceptual metrics and user studies; ablate projection (hard vs soft), instance noise vs other GAN stabilizers, and plot training diagnostics.  
- Additional analysis requested: robustness to misspecified A and real LR inputs; formal conditions when H[q,p] yields per-x MAP; empirical/theoretical support for coordinate-transformation/entropy claims.  
- For AffDG/AffLL: quantify score-estimation accuracy vs σ, consider modern score/diffusion or flow models, and provide density-model calibration for AffLL.  
- Practical requests: report compute/training costs and inference speed, clarify projection test-time overhead, and release code/models for reproducibility.  
- Overall: promising perspective and a useful architectural tool, but insufficient empirical validation, positioning, and ablations prevent acceptance in current form.
