Summary and contributions
- The paper proposes amortised MAP inference for single image super-resolution (SR). The key idea is to avoid empirical risk minimisation with convex pixel losses (MSE/MAE) and instead maximise posterior probability by matching the model’s output distribution to the natural image prior.
- A practical and elegant architectural device is introduced: an affine projection layer gθ(x) = (I−A+A)fθ(x) + A+x that guarantees data-consistency A ŷ = x for any generator fθ and known downsampling operator A. This turns the constrained MAP objective into minimising the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the model’s output distribution qθ and the HR data distribution pY, enabling training without paired LR-HR data.
- Three routes to approximate this objective are explored: (i) AffGAN, a GAN variant that—with a modified generator objective—seeks to minimise KL[q||p] and is paired with “instance noise” to stabilise training; (ii) AffDG, which uses denoising autoencoder gradients as score estimates to guide the generator; (iii) AffLL, which uses a differentiable likelihood model (PixelCNN-like with MCGSM) as a learned prior.
- The paper also makes conceptual connections: AffGAN plus affine projection performs amortised inference over pY|X, and a stochastic AffGAN is linked to amortised variational inference.

Decision: Reject (borderline). Key reasons:
- Insufficient empirical support for the breadth of claims; only the GAN variant yields compelling results, while the denoising- and density-guided approaches underperform and are effectively abandoned.
- Experimental evaluation lacks rigor relative to SR standards: no comparisons on canonical benchmarks/degradations, minimal perceptual evaluation, and limited quantitative analysis of the proposed benefits (e.g., “unpaired” training, robustness to operator mismatch).

Supporting arguments
- Motivation and positioning. The critique of MSE for SR and the appeal of MAP under natural image priors are well motivated, and the affine projection is a simple, well-justified way to enforce data consistency. The literature review is largely appropriate, and the discussion of f-divergence/GANs and denoising-as-score is timely. The representation-dependence critique of MAP is refreshingly candid.
- Theory. The reduction from amortised MAP to H[qθ, pY] is clear given the linear observation model and projection. The modified GAN objective’s link to KL[q||p] is plausible under optimal/unique discriminators and with added instance noise, but remains heuristic in practice; nonetheless, the instance noise argument is thoughtful and addresses a real failure mode. The VI connection in the stochastic case is interesting, albeit high-level.
- Novelty. The affine projection layer for SR (exact A ŷ = x) is a neat contribution that could be broadly useful, and applying score-based gradients from a separate denoiser to train an SR generator is a reasonable extension. Instance noise for stabilising adversarial learning was concurrent with other work; the paper’s framing adds insight but may not be unique.
- Empirical validation. Strengths:
  - A toy Swiss-roll study convincingly shows that AffGAN/AffDG track the posterior mode where MSE/MAE fail, and cross-entropy estimates support the claim.
  - A careful ablation shows the affine projection does not hurt (and often helps) MSE training and enforces exact consistency.
  - AffGAN produces sharper results on grass/CelebA/ImageNet, with qualitative plausibility.
  Weaknesses:
  - The two non-GAN approaches (AffDG/AffLL) do not yield strong results; the paper acknowledges instability and blurriness but then de-emphasises them. This undercuts the central claim that amortised MAP can be trained by these alternative routes.
  - Evaluation is not aligned with SR practice: no results on standard benchmarks (Set5/14, BSD100, Urban100), no bicubic degradation comparisons, and few or no comparisons to strong contemporaneous SR methods beyond a basic MSE baseline and a soft-constrained GAN. Perceptual metrics (LPIPS) or user studies are absent; PSNR/SSIM are included but not the focus (reasonable), yet then the perceptual claims lack objective support.
  - The claim of training without paired data is appealing, but experiments appear to use synthetically generated LR via known A. There is no demonstration of truly unpaired learning when pX is not exactly the pushforward of pY under A, nor robustness to operator mismatch.
  - Failure modes are visible (e.g., snake→water). While plausible under the prior, they highlight the risk of hallucination; no calibration/uncertainty or sampling-based alternatives are provided.
  - The affine inverse A+ is learned numerically and assumed known A (Gaussian + stride). Realistic degradations (bicubic, unknown blur, noise) are not explored; sensitivity to mis-specified A or kernel size is not quantified.

Additional feedback to improve the paper
- Strengthen empirical validation:
  - Report results on standard SR benchmarks under bicubic x4 and compare against SRGAN-like baselines and MSE/MAE models with similar capacity. Include LPIPS and a small-scale user study if possible.
  - Quantify the benefit of the affine projection with ablations (fixed vs trainable A+, wrong A, soft penalty weights).
  - Evaluate robustness to kernel mismatch and mild noise; many SR settings do not know A exactly.
  - Provide a thorough ablation of instance noise (none vs annealed σ; impact on D overfitting, mode collapse, FID/LPIPS).
- For AffDG/AffLL:
  - Consider modern score-based priors or diffusion models to obtain better gradients; experiment with multi-noise-level training and preconditioning.
  - Diagnose why training diverges (step sizes, mismatch between projection geometry and prior gradients).
- Clarify the “unpaired” claim: demonstrate training with independent HR and LR datasets not derived via A, and discuss identifiability.
- The VI connection is intriguing; include preliminary experiments for the stochastic variant, even small-scale, or move this to future work more explicitly.

Questions for the authors
- Can you demonstrate training without paired LR-HR by using HR from dataset A and LR from dataset B (generated with a known A), and report performance?
- How sensitive is AffGAN to mismatch in A (blur kernel width/shape, stride misalignment), and to additive observation noise?
- What is the empirical effect of instance noise vs one-sided label smoothing in your SR setting (training curves, stability metrics, FID/LPIPS)?
- Can you compare to SRGAN (or similar) trained with a soft consistency term under the same architecture/capacity to isolate the value of affine projection?
- For AffDG, did you try multi-noise score matching or denoisers trained with noise schedules closer to score-based generative modeling? Does preconditioning the gradient by a Fisher-like metric help?
- How large/local is the learned A+ kernel in practice, and does truncation introduce artifacts?
- Could you report diversity metrics over outputs conditioned on the same x (especially for the stochastic variant)?
