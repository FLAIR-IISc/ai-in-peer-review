- Proposal: Amortised MAP inference for single-image SR using an affine projection layer with A+ (Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse) to enforce exact LR data consistency.
- Objective: Turns MAP into maximizing prior log-probability under affine constraint, yielding minimisation of cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between model outputs and true HR distribution.
- Methods: Three training routes — AffGAN (GAN variant with modified generator loss and instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-guided score estimation via DAE), and AffLL (train against tractable density model).
- Practical contributions: Construction of A+ for strided convolutions and demonstration that affine projection can match/improve MSE-trained CNNs while ensuring exact LR consistency.
- Theoretical note: Elegant reduction of amortised MAP to H[qθ, pY] and plausible AffGAN derivation, but typographical issues make some update rules ambiguous.
- Empirical: Swiss-roll experiment shows AffGAN/AffDG approximate MAP and lower cross-entropy vs MSE/MAE as a sanity check.
- Empirical: Affine projection speeds MSE training and guarantees LR consistency in practice.
- Empirical: On CelebA/ImageNet, AffGAN yields sharper, perceptually plausible outputs but with high-frequency noise and occasional semantic hallucinations.
- Empirical shortcomings: Evaluation lacks strong SR baselines (e.g., SRGAN, VDSR/EDSR), perceptual metrics (LPIPS, FID/KID), and human studies.
- Method robustness: Assumes known downsampling operator and noiseless observations; robustness to mismatch or noise is unexplored and is a critical limitation.
- Stability/performance: Denoiser- and density-guided methods largely fail (blurry/unstable) and were dropped, weakening the claim that all three routes are practical.
- Novelty: Affine projection and instance noise are neat contributions and the amortised MAP framing is useful, but lack of comparative experiments reduces empirical novelty.
- Presentation issues: Equation correctness, missing figure references, broken placeholders, and incomplete citations hinder reproducibility and clarity.
- Recommendation: Reject due to limited empirical validation and clarity/presentation problems undermining central claims.
- Fixes requested: Clarify/correct ambiguous equations (AffGAN generator loss, combined objective Eqn. 17) and fix typos/figures/references.
- Empirical improvements requested: Compare to SRGAN and strong baselines, add perceptual metrics and a small user study, and evaluate on standard SR benchmarks (Set5/14, Urban100, DIV2K).
- Ablations needed: Instance noise schedule, A+ variants (fixed vs trainable vs incorrect), and robustness to mismatch/noise in A.
- DAE/density routes: Either stabilize with modern score/flow models and multiscale training or clearly label them exploratory given current failures.
- Data-claim clarity: Clarify “no paired data” claim and discuss genuinely unpaired HR/LR training scenarios.
- Amortised optimisation caveat: The objective encourages high-prior feasible outputs on average but does not guarantee per-input posterior modes; consider stochastic generators for multimodality.
- Reproducibility: Release code and detailed A+/A construction to improve reproducibility.
- Questions for authors: Provide exact AffGAN generator objective and clean derivation; quantify sensitivity to A mismatch and noise; detail A+ parameterisation and artifacts.
- Questions for authors: Quantify instance noise impact; report attempts to stabilise AffDG/AffLL and their failure modes; report results with stochastic generators and provide perceptual metrics or human studies.
