Summary and contributions
- The paper tackles single-image super-resolution (SR) as amortised MAP inference under a known linear degradation A. The key architectural idea is an affine projection layer ŷ = ΠAx fθ(x) = (I − A+A)fθ(x) + A+x that guarantees exact data-consistency Afθ(x) = x for any fθ. This turns amortised MAP learning into minimising the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the model’s output distribution qθ and the true HR distribution pY, removing the need for paired LR-HR supervision.
- Three training strategies to approximate this objective are proposed:
  1) AffGAN: a GAN variant whose generator is the affine-projected SR network and whose objective approximates minimising KL(qθ‖pY). The paper introduces “instance noise” (additive noise to both real and generated samples with annealing) to stabilise GAN training and argues it makes the discriminator’s optimum unique and the divergence well-behaved.
  2) AffDG: denoiser-guided SR that uses a DAE to estimate ∂y log pY(y) (score) and backpropagates it through ΠAx fθ(x) to maximise Ey∼qθ log pY(y).
  3) AffLL: density-guided SR that uses a differentiable PixelCNN-like model with MCGSM likelihood to directly minimise −Ey∼qθ log p̂Y(y).
- Theory: (i) reduction of amortised MAP to minimising H[qθ, pY]; (ii) modified GAN generator loss leading to KL(q‖p); (iii) analysis and motivation for instance noise; (iv) connection between a stochastic version of AffGAN and amortised variational inference EpX KL(qY|X‖pY|X).
- Empirics: (a) on a 2D Swiss-roll inverse problem, AffGAN and AffDG approach MAP and achieve cross-entropy close to ground-truth MAP; (b) affine projection does not hurt and often speeds up MSE training, and enforces exact data consistency; (c) on textures, faces (CelebA), and ImageNet 4× SR, AffGAN yields sharper, more plausible images than MSE/SSIM-oriented models; denoiser/density-guided methods tend to be blurry or unstable. Qualitative results show strengths and some hallucinations.

Decision: Reject
Key reasons:
- Empirical evaluation on real images is limited and not rigorous enough for ICLR: few quantitative or perceptual metrics beyond PSNR/SSIM, no user studies or strong baselines (e.g., SRGAN/SRResNet variants), and no evaluation on standard SR benchmarks with known protocols. AffDG/AffLL underperform; the case for the broader “amortised MAP” framework rests almost entirely on the GAN variant and qualitative visuals.
- Theoretical-experimental mismatch and assumptions: the training objective for AffGAN minimises KL(q‖p), not the cross-entropy directly; the MAP link (minimising H[q, p]) is therefore indirect. Claims hinge on idealised Bayes-optimal discriminators and known, fixed A constructed synthetically; generalisation to realistic unknown/mismatched degradations is not demonstrated.

Supporting arguments
- Novelty and motivation: Strong and well-motivated. Enforcing exact affine data-consistency via A+ in a deep SR network is elegant and practically useful; showing amortised MAP reduces to matching qθ to pY (thus enabling unpaired training) is a nice conceptual contribution. The instance noise discussion is timely and clearly reasoned, with a divergence perspective.
- Correctness: The derivations are generally sound (cross-entropy reformulation, score-based gradient via DAE, KL objective for the modified GAN). However, the central claim “amortised MAP” is only approximated by AffGAN via KL(q‖p) and not validated on real image domains (no estimate of H[q, pY] or its surrogates).
- Empirical support: The Swiss-roll experiment convincingly validates the theory. For images, the affine projection’s benefit (training speed, consistency) is well demonstrated. Yet, the main empirical claims—superior perceptual SR via amortised MAP—are not backed by rigorous comparisons, perceptual metrics (e.g., NIQE/PI/LPIPS) or user studies, and AffDG/AffLL results are weak with limited diagnosis.

Additional feedback to improve the paper (helpful suggestions)
- Benchmarks and baselines: Include comparisons to SRResNet/SRGAN (Ledig et al., 2016) and strong MSE/MAE baselines on standard datasets (Set5/Set14/BSD100/Urban100/Div2K) with established protocols. Add ablations of the affine projection (soft vs exact), and report data-consistency errors.
- Perceptual evaluation: Beyond PSNR/SSIM, use perceptual metrics (e.g., NIQE/PI/LPIPS) and, ideally, a small-scale human study. Report FID/KID between qθ and pY to support distributional matching claims.
- Instance noise: Provide quantitative stability analysis and an ablation (no noise vs instance noise vs one-sided label smoothing; and vs WGAN-GP) and report sensitivity to the annealing schedule.
- Unknown/mismatched A: Demonstrate robustness when A is misspecified, learned, or spatially varying. Real-world SR rarely has exact A; discuss or experiment with blind/plug-and-play variants, or integrate A-estimation.
- AffDG/AffLL: Consider more recent score-based/diffusion estimators and modern density models; clarify training pathologies and present additional stabilisation strategies (e.g., noise-conditioned scores, multiscale guidance). If they are secondary, tighten the narrative to focus on AffGAN + projection.
- Clarity: Fix typos/equation formatting (e.g., generator loss), make assumptions explicit (paired vs unpaired training, how pX is obtained), and release code for reproducibility.

Questions for the authors
- How sensitive is performance to the approximation quality of A+ (kernel size/support)? Does Afθ(x) remain exactly equal to x in practice for all images and strides? Please quantify residuals.
- Can your method train and infer with real LR images not generated by your A (i.e., no paired data) and still perform well? Any results on naturally downsampled or camera-degraded images?
- What is the empirical impact of adding stochasticity z to the generator (Appendix F)? Does the amortised VI perspective lead to measurable gains (diversity/perceptual quality) without harming consistency?
- How does AffGAN compare to SRGAN trained with a strong data-consistency term or with your exact projection? Any head-to-head comparisons?
- Could you report perceptual metrics (e.g., NIQE/PI/LPIPS) and user-study outcomes to substantiate visual gains?
- For AffDG, what were the exact noise annealing schedules and DAE capacities? Did you try modern score estimators or diffusion-based guidance to mitigate the blurriness?
- Have you tested alternative divergence formulations (e.g., WGAN-GP) instead of instance noise? How do stability and quality compare?
