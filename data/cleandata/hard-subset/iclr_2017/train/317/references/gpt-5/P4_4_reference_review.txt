1) Summary of claims and contributions
The paper proposes amortised MAP inference for single-image super-resolution (SR). The core idea is to enforce the exact observation model x = Ay via an affine projection layer ŷ = Π_Ax fθ(x) = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x, where A is the downsampling operator and A+ its Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse. Under this architecture, amortised MAP reduces to minimising the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the distribution qθ of outputs and the HR data distribution pY, obviating the need for paired LR/HR supervision. The paper develops three training strategies for this objective: (i) AffGAN, a GAN variant aimed at minimising KL[qθ || pY] with an “instance noise” stabilisation technique; (ii) AffDG, using a denoising autoencoder to estimate ∇y log pY(y) and backpropagating through the affine projection; (iii) AffLL, using a tractable density model (MCGSM-PixelCNN-like) to provide gradients of log pY(y). Experiments on a 2D toy Swiss-roll and on images (grass textures, CelebA, ImageNet) show: (a) the affine projection preserves input–output consistency and often speeds training; (b) AffGAN yields sharper perceptual results than MSE/MAE and soft penalty baselines; (c) the denoiser/density-guided variants underperform visually (blur). The paper also presents a variational inference interpretation of a stochastic AffGAN (Appendix F).

2) Decision: Reject
Key reasons: (1) Empirical support is insufficient and lacks rigorous comparisons to strong baselines; (2) Novelty over existing GAN-based SR (e.g., SRGAN) appears incremental, with the main practical gains hinging on the affine projection and an instability fix similar to concurrent ideas.

3) Supporting arguments
- Soundness of the core derivation: The constrained MAP objective under exact x = Ay reduces, with the affine projection, to maximising Ex log pY(Π_Ax fθ(x)), which is equivalent to minimising H[qθ, pY]. This is technically sound and clarifies why paired LR/HR data are not required if A is known and enforced. The link between the proposed GAN update and KL[qθ || pY] is consistent with f-GAN formulations.
- Affine projection: Enforcing exact consistency via Π_Ax is a principled, practically useful architectural choice. The numerical construction of A+ and demonstrations that Afθ(x) ≈ x (to numerical precision) are convincing. Showing that affine projection does not harm—and can even help—MSE optimisation is valuable.
- Empirical evaluation: While the toy example supports the cross-entropy objective and MAP intuition, the image experiments are limited. There is minimal quantitative evaluation beyond PSNR/SSIM for CelebA (where MSE unsurprisingly wins) and no user studies or perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS, FID) to substantiate claims about perceptual quality. Comparisons to established SR baselines (e.g., SRGAN, perceptual-loss methods, strong non-adversarial SR models) are absent. The most novel components beyond SRGAN—the denoiser-guided and density-guided schemes—do not produce competitive results, and their instability limits the support for the broader “amortised MAP” framing.
- Instance noise: Adding noise to inputs for GAN stabilisation is reasonable and well-motivated; however, similar ideas appeared concurrently (and subsequently matured, e.g., gradient penalties, spectral norm). The paper presents no comprehensive ablation to show its contribution over standard stabilisation techniques.
- Scope and generality: The approach assumes the observation operator A is exactly known and fixed. There is no analysis of robustness to misspecification of A, nor results for different downsampling kernels (beyond Gaussian blur) or real-camera degradations, which is important for practical SR.
- Novelty: The paper’s main distinct elements are (i) the exact affine projection layer in SR networks, and (ii) a clean derivation that training the generator under this layer corresponds to matching pY via cross-entropy/KL. Given prior GAN-based SR work, these are interesting but not sufficient, in this reviewer’s view, to meet the acceptance bar without stronger empirical validation.

4) Additional feedback to improve the paper (helpful suggestions, not necessarily decisive)
- Stronger baselines and metrics: Include comparisons to SRGAN and non-adversarial SOTA (e.g., VDSR/EDSR-like) on common benchmarks, and evaluate perceptual metrics (LPIPS, FID/KID) and, ideally, a small-scale user study. Report diversity/entropy of outputs to align with the KL vs cross-entropy discussion.
- Ablations: (a) Affine projection vs soft penalty across multiple As and datasets, with quantitative data-consistency measures; (b) instance noise schedules vs other stabilisation methods; (c) impact of A+ accuracy (kernel size/locality) on performance.
- Robustness: Evaluate sensitivity to mismatch in A and to real degradations (e.g., different blur kernels, JPEG, noise). Discuss training when pX at training differs from test pX.
- Denoiser/density variants: Provide clearer diagnostics of failure modes (e.g., gradient scale, denoiser noise levels, density peakiness) and explore techniques known to help score estimation (e.g., multi-noise score matching, modern flow/score models). Consider recent diffusion/score-based priors for more stable gradients.
- Variational connection: Demonstrate the stochastic AffGAN and show empirical evidence for amortised posterior approximation (e.g., diversity along the affine subspace conditioned on x).
- Clarity: Fix typographical/formatting issues, streamline the narrative around the central claims, and provide code/reproducibility details for A+ estimation.

Questions for the authors
- How is pX obtained in practice? Do you train with LR images generated by A(y) from the same HR dataset or with independently collected LR data? How sensitive is performance to mismatch between training pX and test-time LR distributions?
- How robust is the approach to misspecification of A (kernel shape/stride), and how does performance degrade as the A+ approximation becomes more local?
- Can you provide quantitative ablations on instance noise (schedule, magnitude), and comparisons to other stabilisers (e.g., gradient penalty, spectral normalisation)?
- For AffDG/AffLL, can you report training diagnostics (gradient norms, effective step sizes, variance) and try recent score-based models as priors? What prevents these methods from matching AffGAN on images?
- Do you observe mode-collapse or limited diversity along the affine subspace for a fixed x? Can the stochastic AffGAN (Appendix F) sample multiple plausible y per x, and does this align with the variational interpretation?
- Have you evaluated perceptual metrics (LPIPS/FID) and user preference tests to substantiate the perceptual improvements claimed for AffGAN?
