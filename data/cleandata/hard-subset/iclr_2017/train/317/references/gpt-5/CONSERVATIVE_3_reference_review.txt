Summary and claimed contributions
The paper proposes amortized MAP inference for single-image super-resolution (SR). The core ideas are:
- Enforce exact data consistency via an affine projection layer ΠAx that guarantees Afθ(x)=x, with A the known downsampling operator and A+ its Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse. This permits optimizing only the prior term and recasts training as matching the marginal distribution qθ of outputs to the HR data distribution pY.
- Show that amortized MAP reduces to minimizing the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY], making paired LR–HR supervision unnecessary in principle.
- Provide three training approaches: (i) AffGAN, a GAN with the affine projection that (the authors claim) minimizes KL[qθ‖pY], stabilized with “instance noise”; (ii) AffDG, using a denoiser (DAE) to estimate ∇y log pY and backpropagate through ΠAx; (iii) AffLL, using a differentiable density model (PixelCNN-like with MCGSM) to provide log pY gradients.
- Empirical results on a 2D Swiss-roll toy problem, grass textures, CelebA 4× SR, and ImageNet 4× SR. AffGAN yields sharper images and exact LR–HR consistency; AffDG/LL tend to be blurry and unstable. The affine projection reportedly does not harm MSE-based SR and can accelerate training.

Decision: Reject
Key reasons:
- Insufficient empirical validation against established and stronger SR baselines and on standard benchmarks; perceptual claims are not substantiated with appropriate metrics or human studies.
- Methodological clarity and rigor are lacking: theoretical claims (e.g., KL minimization with the proposed GAN objective) are not convincingly supported; two of the three proposed routes (denoiser- and density-guided) fail in high dimensions, yet are presented as contributions.

Supporting arguments
- Experimental comparisons are weak. The paper primarily compares against MSE/MAE-trained CNNs and soft LR consistency. There is no comparison to strong SR methods using perceptual losses or adversarial training (e.g., SRGAN/ESRGAN, EDSR/RCAN with perceptual loss, modern score-based priors). No evaluation on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100, Urban100, DIV2K) or degradation models used in the literature. Reported PSNR/SSIM is on CelebA only, and the conclusions (MSE wins PSNR/SSIM; GAN looks sharper) are already known.
- Perceptual quality is asserted but not measured. There is no LPIPS/FID/NIQE, no user study, and no controlled study of hallucinations. The anecdotal “snake becomes water” example underscores the risk of hallucination; without rigorous evaluation, it is difficult to accept that the approach improves perceptual quality reliably.
- The “paired data not needed” claim is not convincingly demonstrated. While the objective reframes training via pY and pX, experiments appear to rely on synthetic downsampling A applied to HR images (i.e., effectively paired). There is no study of real LR distributions, unknown/mismatched A, or robustness to kernel mismatch—central issues in SR.
- Theoretical arguments are underdeveloped and sometimes inconsistent. The generator loss in the main text appears malformed; the KL derivation is relegated to an appendix and hinges on idealized assumptions (unique optimal discriminator, convergence per iteration), which the authors themselves argue do not hold in practice. The cross-entropy objective H[qθ,pY] vs the KL minimized by AffGAN differs by H[qθ], yet the implications are not analyzed empirically (e.g., does AffGAN truly approximate MAP solutions?).
- Two of the three proposed training routes (AffDG and AffLL) underperform or diverge on real images. The paper acknowledges this and abandons them for later experiments. Presenting methods that fail to deliver in the main application domain, without remediation, weakens the contribution.
- Novelty is incremental relative to established lines: enforcing data-consistency via known forward models and projections nulling the measurement operator’s range is standard in model-based deep learning and plug-and-play/score-based methods. The “instance noise” stabilization is close to contemporaneous/noise-regularized GAN training and is not evaluated against alternative stabilizers. The claimed MAP framing is interesting but not unique, and related work (e.g., PnP/RED/score-based SR) is not discussed or compared.

Additional feedback to improve the paper
- Evaluate on standard SR benchmarks with standard degradations and include strong baselines (SRGAN/ESRGAN, EDSR/RCAN with perceptual loss, score-based/pseudolikelihood priors). Report PSNR/SSIM and perceptual metrics (LPIPS, FID, NIQE) and, ideally, a small human study.
- Clarify and correct the GAN objective in the main text; align it with the appendix derivation. Empirically probe the difference between minimizing H[qθ,pY] and KL[qθ‖pY], including entropy effects and mode-seeking behavior (and whether this aligns with MAP).
- Substantiate the “unpaired” claim by training with real LR data without access to paired HR and demonstrating robustness to unknown/mismatched A. Analyze sensitivity to A/A+ mis-specification.
- Strengthen the denoiser/density routes or omit them. If retained, consider modern score models (score matching/SDEs) for ∇ log pY, and show working SR with them. For the density model, assess gradient quality and smoothness; ablate mixture components and architecture depth.
- Provide ablations: with/without affine projection; accuracy of A+; effect on training stability and runtime; instance noise schedule vs label smoothing and other GAN stabilizers.
- Improve clarity and writing: fix formatting errors, incomplete references, and missing figures/equations; provide implementation details and code for reproducibility.

Questions for the authors
- What exact generator loss did you implement for AffGAN? The main text and appendix differ; please provide the explicit formula used and its gradient.
- How does the method perform with unknown or mismatched degradations A (e.g., different blur kernels/strides)? How sensitive is the affine projection to errors in A+?
- Can you demonstrate training without paired LR–HR (i.e., real LR pX from a camera pipeline) and quantify performance?
- How does AffGAN compare quantitatively to SRGAN/ESRGAN and perceptual-loss baselines on DIV2K/Set14/Urban100 in both distortion (PSNR/SSIM) and perception (LPIPS/FID/NIQE)?
- Can you report LPIPS and a small user study to support perceptual claims and quantify hallucinations?
- For AffDG/LL, what prevented success in high dimensions? Would modern score models (e.g., NCSN/Score-SDE) address the gradient pathologies you observed?
- What is the computational overhead of the affine projection (including A+ estimation), and does it generalize to non-convolutional A (e.g., spatially varying degradations)?
