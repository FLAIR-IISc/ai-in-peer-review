Summary and contributions
- The paper argues for amortised MAP inference for single-image super-resolution (SR) under a deterministic observation model x = Ay. It shows that, with an explicit affine projection layer ŷ = ΠAx fθ(x) = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x, the likelihood constraint Aŷ = x is satisfied exactly, reducing training to minimising the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the distribution of outputs and the HR data prior.
- Three ways to approximate this objective are proposed: (i) AffGAN, a GAN variant intended to minimise KL(qθ‖pY), with “instance noise” to stabilise training; (ii) AffDG, backpropagating score estimates ∇y log pY(y) from a denoising autoencoder; and (iii) AffLL, a likelihood-guided approach using a differentiable PixelCNN-like prior (MCGSM).
- The affine projection is presented as a general architectural element that guarantees LR-HR consistency and can be attached to any SR network. The paper also outlines a connection between a stochastic AffGAN and amortised variational inference.
- Experiments: a 2D Swiss-roll toy shows AffGAN and AffDG approximating MAP; an MSE study suggests the affine projection does not harm (and may help) training; on grass textures and CelebA/ImageNet 4× SR, AffGAN yields sharper, more plausible images than MSE, albeit with more high-frequency noise; AffDG/LL results are blurry in high dimensions.

Decision: Reject (borderline)
Key reasons: (1) The empirical validation is limited and does not convincingly support the breadth of claims—only the GAN variant performs well in realistic settings; (2) The setting assumes a known degradation operator A and matched HR/LR domains, with no evaluation under realistic and mismatched degradations where the approach’s core assumptions are most fragile.

Supporting arguments
- Novelty and motivation: The architectural affine projection for exact consistency is a neat and useful idea. Framing SR as amortised MAP via cross-entropy minimisation provides a coherent probabilistic motivation for adversarial SR and offers a unifying view across GAN/DAE/density-based priors. The “instance noise” argument is sensible and aligns with later insights on support mismatch.
- Theoretical correctness and scope: The equivalence from amortised MAP to minimising H[qθ, pY] under the hard constraint is sound; the derivation that AffGAN minimises KL(q‖p) with the proposed generator objective follows Huszár’s analysis. However, both depend on technical caveats: qθ is singular (manifold support), so KL and differential entropy are ill-defined without smoothing; the paper relies on added noise and annealing. The amortised VI connection (Appendix F) is interesting but high-level; rigorous conditions under which it holds (and its practical benefits over deterministic AffGAN) are not demonstrated.
- Empirical rigor:
  - The 2D toy and MSE-with/without projection experiments support the architectural claim and the MAP framing.
  - For real images, only AffGAN produces compelling visuals. AffDG and AffLL fail to scale, and the paper relegates them after the texture experiment. This weakens the claim of “three methods” for amortised MAP in practice.
  - Evaluation is primarily qualitative. PSNR/SSIM unsurprisingly favour MSE; there are no perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS), no human studies, and no distributional metrics (e.g., FID/KID) to substantiate “perceptually superior.” Comparisons to contemporary perceptual SR baselines (e.g., SRGAN/perceptual/VGG-feature losses) are missing or minimal.
  - The observation that a snake becomes water underscores a core risk of prior-driven MAP: the prior can overwhelm the conditional, hallucinating plausible but semantically incorrect content. This is a critical failure case for SR that needs systematic analysis and mitigation.
- Assumptions and generality: The method assumes a known A and constructs A+ numerically. Real SR often involves unknown, spatially varying degradations and camera pipelines; hard-projection to a mis-specified A can yield systematically wrong reconstructions. The paper does not evaluate robustness to kernel/scale mismatch or unknown degradations. Although the authors claim paired HR/LR are not required, all experiments generate LR via the same A applied to the HR training data; the more interesting unpaired setting (domain-mismatch between pY and pX) is not shown.

Constructive feedback to improve the paper
- Strengthen empirical evaluation:
  - Include comparisons to strong perceptual SR baselines (e.g., SRGAN with VGG/perceptual loss, ESRGAN-like variants) using perceptual metrics (LPIPS, DISTS) and a small-scale user study.
  - Report distributional metrics (e.g., FID/KID) on HR patches to assess qθ vs pY more directly.
  - Provide ablations on the affine projection: learned vs fixed A+, kernel size, and robustness to A mis-specification (blur width mismatch, different downsampling kernels, bicubic vs Gaussian, and mild noise).
  - Evaluate in unpaired settings (distinct HR corpus and LR corpus generated differently) to validate the “no paired data needed” claim.
- Clarify and solidify theory:
  - Precisely state assumptions for the KL-minimisation claim and for Appendix F (absolute continuity via instance noise, uniqueness of optimal D, etc.). Discuss the effect of entropy H[qθ] in deterministic generators and the role of instance noise for making quantities well-defined.
  - Clean up Equation (10) (generator objective formatting) and ensure notation is consistent throughout.
- Instance noise:
  - Compare systematically against other stabilisation methods (label smoothing, gradient penalty, WGAN-GP, spectral norm) on the same SR setup. Provide an ablation of noise schedules and magnitudes.
- AffDG and AffLL:
  - If kept as contributions, include experiments where they are competitive (e.g., smaller scales, class-conditional priors, patch-based tasks), or reposition them as negative results with analysis. Consider stronger priors (modern autoregressive/flow/score models) and score-matching-based training to address the “peaky prior” issue.
- Discuss practical deployment:
  - Analyze failure modes (semantic hallucinations) and propose controls: stochastic AffGAN to sample from an approximate posterior; uncertainty estimates; or hybrid objectives that nudge away from pure MAP (e.g., tempered priors or feature-space constraints).
  - Address checkerboard artifacts and numerical stability of A+ (especially with strided deconvs) and show that the projection does not introduce artifacts.

Questions for the authors
1. How robust is the affine projection to mis-specified A? Can you quantify degradation when the true blur/stride differ from the assumed A? Any results on standard bicubic SR benchmarks?
2. You claim paired HR/LR are not required. Do you have an experiment where the HR prior and LR inputs come from different datasets (unpaired), still achieving good SR?
3. Can you provide a direct quantitative measure of qθ approaching pY (e.g., FID/KID on patches) and an ablation demonstrating the effect of instance noise vs label smoothing vs WGAN-GP?
4. For AffDG/LL, can you show conditions (noise levels, model capacity) where they outperform or complement AffGAN? Would a stochastic generator and denoiser-guided training alleviate the blurriness?
5. In Appendix F, does the amortised VI interpretation lead to a practical stochastic SR method? Can you sample diverse ŷ conditioned on x, and how does that compare perceptually to deterministic MAP?
