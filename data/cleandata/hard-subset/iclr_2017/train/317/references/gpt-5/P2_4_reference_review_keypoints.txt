- Core idea: enforce LR observation constraint via an affine projection layer gθ(x) = (I − A+ A)fθ(x) + A+ x to guarantee consistency Ax = ŷ.  
- Reformulation: amortized MAP under the projection reduces to minimizing cross-entropy H[qθ, pY], matching the HR distribution while keeping LR consistency.  
- Proposed methods: AffGAN (GAN variant with instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-guided gradients), AffLL (trainable density model minimizing −log pY).  
- Empirical claims: AffGAN and AffDG approximate MAP on a 2D toy; AffGAN produces sharper outputs on textures/faces while MSE scores highest in PSNR/SSIM.  
- Decision: Reject — affine projection and derivation interesting, but empirical validation insufficient and novelty over GAN-based SR limited.  
- Main critique: AffGAN is essentially SRGAN with an exact observation constraint; denoiser/density approaches are not convincingly effective beyond toy tasks.  
- Theoretical strengths: derivation from MAP to cross-entropy is sound under assumptions; amortized VI link is noted but mostly formal.  
- Theoretical caveats: results rely on idealized assumptions (unique optimal discriminator, overlapping support); AffGAN minimizes KL(q‖p) not pure MAP, so “amortized MAP” claim is overstated.  
- Benchmark gaps: experiments omit standard SR datasets (Set5/14, BSD100, Urban100, DIV2K) and comparisons to SRGAN/ESRGAN or perceptual-loss baselines.  
- Metric gaps: no perceptual metrics (LPIPS, FID, NIQE) or user studies; reliance on PSNR/SSIM and qualitative visuals weakens perceptual claims.  
- Hallucination risk: strong prior + only Ax = x can produce plausible-but-wrong outputs (e.g., “snake becomes water”) and this tradeoff is not systematically studied.  
- AffDG/AffLL weakness: these two routes underperform on real images and lack analysis explaining why (gradient quality, density mismatch).  
- Observation model realism: method assumes known A and zero noise; robustness to A mismatch or noise is not evaluated.  
- Reproducibility issues: many architectural/hyperparameter details missing, no code release, and sparse quantitative tables.  
- Suggested empirical fixes: include standard datasets, compare to SRGAN/ESRGAN, report perceptual metrics/MOS, and ablate projection and instance noise.  
- Suggested robustness tests: evaluate mismatched/unknown A, observation noise, and consider learned/blind SR variants or A+ mis-specification effects.  
- Clarify theory: explicitly distinguish H[qθ, pY] optimization from KL minimization and state conditions when amortized minimization yields per-x MAP.  
- Improve DG/LL: provide diagnostics (gradient fields, noise schedules), try stronger density/score estimators, or reposition these methods as exploratory if weak.  
- Study fidelity vs perception: quantify LR consistency, semantic fidelity, and introduce a controlled trade-off knob between prior and data fidelity.  
- Key author questions: sensitivity to A/noise; formal conditions for recovering per-x MAP; role of instance noise vs other GAN stabilizers; provide LPIPS/FID and SRGAN comparisons; characterize hallucination frequency; noise schedule choices for AffDG and modern score-matching attempts; AffLL density quality and calibration.
