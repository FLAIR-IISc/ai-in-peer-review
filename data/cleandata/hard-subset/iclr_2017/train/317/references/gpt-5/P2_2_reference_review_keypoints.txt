- Reframes single-image SR as amortised MAP with an affine projection layer (using A and A+) enforcing Afθ(x)=x, turning MAP into minimising H[qθ, pY].
- Proposes three training strategies: AffGAN (GAN on projected outputs), AffDG (denoiser-based score estimation), and AffLL (differentiable density model), plus instance noise to stabilise GANs.
- Experiments: Swiss‑roll toy (cross-entropy estimable), SR on textures/CelebA/ImageNet (4×), and affine-projection ablation under MSE; AffGAN yields qualitatively sharper images and exact LR–HR consistency.
- Decision: Reject (borderline).
- Key reason 1: Empirical support for amortised MAP via cross-entropy is weak on real images—only AffGAN is competitive and evaluation is mainly qualitative and lacks strong baselines.
- Key reason 2: Matching the marginal qθ(y) to pY(y) does not guarantee faithful conditional reconstructions pY|X and can produce hallucinations; the paper does not quantify the faithfulness–plausibility trade-off.
- Motivation/placement: Moving beyond MSE and enforcing exact data consistency is well motivated; affine projection is elegant and potentially widely useful.
- Theory praise and caveat: Derivation linking affine constraint to H[qθ,pY] is sound, but AffGAN actually minimises KL(q||p) (not cross-entropy), which is mode-seeking and may reduce diversity.
- The marginal-matching reduction is a weak surrogate for conditional MAP—generators can add x‑independent nullspace residuals that match the prior and cause semantic hallucinations.
- Variational interpretation (Appendix F) is interesting but depends on stochastic generators and affine constraint; main experiments use deterministic AffGAN and do not validate this view.
- Swiss‑roll provides a good sanity check showing cross-entropy reduction for AffGAN/AffDG.
- Real-image evaluation is limited: no human studies or learned perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS/FID), and no comparison to strong perceptual SR baselines (SRGAN/ESRGAN or VGG perceptual losses).
- AffDG and AffLL underperform (blurry/unstable), weakening the claim that H[qθ,pY] minimisation is practical with current denoisers/density models.
- Affine projection as an MSE ablation is useful (faster training, exact consistency), but the practical pseudoinverse for strided blur-conv is approximate and sensitivity to kernel/stride mismatch is unquantified.
- Instance noise is a reasonable stabiliser for GAN training, but ablations on schedules and alternatives are limited.
- Clarity/reproducibility: Core ideas and derivations are clear, but there are typos, unevenness, and no released code/data.
- Suggestion: Evaluate perceptual quality rigorously with user studies or learned metrics (LPIPS, FID/KID) and compare to SRGAN/ESRGAN and VGG-based perceptual losses with/without LR-consistency.
- Suggestion: Quantify conditional fidelity beyond PSNR/SSIM using metrics that test whether high-frequency content correlates with the LR input (e.g., patch-wise or feature-space consistency).
- Suggestion: Analyse the nullspace residual dependence on x (e.g., mutual information, shuffling experiments) to measure how much hallucinated detail is conditioned on the input.
- Suggestion: Test robustness to mismatch between assumed A and true degradation; consider jointly estimating A or measuring sensitivity to kernel mismatch.
- Suggestion: Strengthen AffDG/AffLL using stronger density/denoiser models (modern flows, autoregressives, or score-based diffusion guidance) and improved training schedules.
- Suggestion: Provide ablations on A+ (exact vs learned), kernel sizes, and quantify how close the projection is to the true pseudoinverse in practice.
- Suggestion: Broaden related work discussion on data-consistency constraints in inverse problems.
- Question: How often do conditional failures (plausible-but-wrong semantics) occur—can you quantify hallucination rate and the faithfulness–plausibility trade-off?
- Question: How sensitive are results to mismatch between assumed A (blur kernel/stride) and true degradation; can the method adapt to jointly estimate A?
- Question: In AffGAN, does the nullspace residual meaningfully depend on x—can you measure this (e.g., by shuffling x and observing changes in high-frequency content)?
- Question: What is the practical impact of the entropy gap between KL(q||p) and H[q,p]; were objectives closer to cross-entropy (e.g., f‑GAN variants) tried and did they differ?
- Question: Can you report LPIPS/FID or small-scale human studies to substantiate the perceptual gains?
- Question: For AffDG/AffLL, what were the main failure modes—do stronger denoisers/density models or alternate schedules make them viable?
