Summary and contributions
- The paper proposes amortised MAP inference for single-image super-resolution (SR). The core idea is to enforce exact LR-HR consistency by inserting an affine projection layer gθ(x) = ΠAx fθ(x) = (I − A+A)fθ(x) + A+ x, where A is the known linear downsampler and A+ its (approximated) Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse. This restricts outputs to the affine subspace {y: Ay = x}.
- Under this constraint, maximising the expected log-posterior reduces to minimising the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the marginal distribution of model outputs qθ and the HR data distribution pY. Training thus resembles generative modelling and, in principle, does not require paired LR/HR.
- Three training routes are explored: (i) a GAN variant (AffGAN) argued to minimise KL[qθ‖pY] with an “instance noise” trick for stability; (ii) denoiser-guided (AffDG) training using score estimates from a DAE; (iii) density-guided (AffLL) training using a differentiable PixelCNN-like density model.
- Experiments on a 2D Swiss-roll toy problem, grass textures, CelebA faces, and ImageNet qualitatively suggest AffGAN produces sharper images than MSE/MAE baselines, while the affine projection enforces exact LR consistency.

Decision: Reject
Key reasons:
- Insufficient empirical validation against established SR baselines on standard benchmarks and degradations; no perceptual metrics or user studies to substantiate “perceptual quality” claims.
- Theoretical framing is appealing but relies on idealised assumptions (known A, perfect A+, perfect discriminators, well-specified priors) and is not convincingly connected to the actual empirical behaviour on real images.
- The alternative training routes (AffDG, AffLL) largely fail; the paper pivots to AffGAN without thorough analysis, weakening the central “amortised MAP” narrative.

Supporting arguments
- Empirical rigor and baselines: The paper omits comparisons to strong, established SR methods (e.g., VDSR/LapSRN/EDSR, and SRGAN/Perceptual-loss baselines). Datasets and settings (Gaussian strided A) do not reflect common SR practice (bicubic or realistic degradations, DIV2K/Set5/Set14/BSD100/Urban100). Reported quantitative results are limited (mostly PSNR/SSIM on CelebA), with no modern perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS, NIQE, FID) or user studies. The qualitative results include clear semantic failures (e.g., snake→water), which undermines the MAP-inference motivation.
- Novelty relative to prior art: Enforcing data consistency via a projection or pseudoinverse in inverse problems is not new; iterative back-projection and data-consistency layers are well-known in SR and related imaging. The “instance noise” idea is similar to contemporaneous/known GAN stabilisation techniques (noise-convolution, regularisation, WGAN variants); the novelty and necessity over established alternatives (WGAN-GP, spectral norm) are not empirically justified.
- Theoretical claims vs. practice: 
  - The reduction from amortised MAP to cross-entropy minimisation of qθ vs. pY hinges on exact knowledge of A and exact projection. In realistic SR, A is seldom known and A+ often has infinite support; here A+ is approximated numerically with local kernels, but the impact of approximation error is not analysed.
  - The link between AffGAN’s KL minimisation and per-x MAP solutions is asserted but not demonstrated in high dimensions. Minimising H[qθ, pY] or KL[qθ‖pY] matches output marginals to the prior, not necessarily the conditional modes for each x. The observed semantic hallucinations highlight this gap.
  - The amortised VI connection (Appendix F) is interesting but remains informal and untested; no stochastic generator experiments are shown.
- Scope and robustness: AffDG and AffLL do not work satisfactorily; they are largely abandoned after grass textures. This weakens the central premise that amortised MAP can be achieved via generic prior modelling/score estimation. Training instability and the need for annealed instance noise suggest sensitivity; no ablations or robustness analysis are provided.
- Practical relevance: The “no paired LR/HR needed” claim is of limited practical value in SR, where pairs can be generated from HR images with the same A used here. The more pressing challenge is unknown/mismatched degradations, not addressed.

Additional feedback to improve the paper
- Evaluation:
  - Benchmark on standard datasets (DIV2K, Set5/14, BSD100, Urban100) with bicubic and realistic degradations, report PSNR/SSIM and perceptual metrics (LPIPS/NIQE/PI) and conduct a user study.
  - Compare against strong supervised SR and perceptual SR baselines (VDSR/LapSRN/EDSR, SRGAN/perceptual-loss methods), as well as plug-and-play priors or score-based priors for inverse problems.
  - Provide ablations on the affine projection: accuracy of A+, effect of fixing vs training, kernel size/support, robustness to mismatch between training/inference A.
- Theory/analysis:
  - Clarify conditions under which minimising H[qθ, pY] leads to per-x MAP estimates, and discuss how the marginal matching objective can fail (e.g., producing plausible but wrong semantics).
  - Quantify the approximation error introduced by finite-support A+, and its effect on LR consistency and reconstruction quality.
  - Situate the affine projection in the context of classic iterative back-projection and modern data-consistency layers; discuss pros/cons and computational trade-offs.
- Methods:
  - For GANs, compare instance noise against WGAN-GP, spectral normalisation, and gradient penalties; provide stability metrics and convergence diagnostics.
  - For AffDG, ensure the DAE is sufficiently strong and validated as a score estimator; consider modern score matching/diffusion models and pose the method as plug-and-play/Score-SR for a fairer, stronger baseline.
  - For AffLL, use state-of-the-art likelihood models and quantify calibration; if the model’s fit is weak, the training signal will be misleading—acknowledge and fix or drop the claim.
- Reproducibility: Release code, pretrained models, and the numerical procedure for A+; provide full hyperparameters and annealing schedules.

Questions for the authors
- How does the method perform on standard SR benchmarks and degradations? Can you report results against EDSR/SRResNet/SRGAN on DIV2K/Set14/Urban100?
- How accurate is the numerically learned A+ relative to the true pseudoinverse for bicubic downsampling? What is the impact of finite kernel size on exact LR consistency and ultimate image quality?
- How robust is the approach to A mismatch between training and test time (realistic SR)? Does small blur/sharpening or noise break the guarantees or significantly degrade performance?
- Can you quantify the benefit of instance noise over WGAN-GP or spectral normalisation in your setting? What is the sensitivity to the noise schedule?
- For AffDG, how close is your DAE to the Bayes-optimal denoiser? Did you try modern score networks or diffusion-model scores, which are designed for accurate ∇y log pY(y)?
- For AffLL, can you demonstrate that the density model is calibrated and expressive enough to provide meaningful gradients? If not, is the negative result informative about the limits of likelihood-based guidance for SR?
- Does the affine projection reduce model capacity in a way that harms high-frequency detail? Can you quantify the trade-off vs. a soft consistency loss under strong baselines?
- Can you provide perceptual metrics and/or user studies to support the claim of improved perceptual quality, and analyse semantic errors like the “snake→water” example?
