- Tackles single-image SR as amortised MAP with an affine-projection layer guaranteeing exact data consistency and enabling unpaired training by matching qθ to pY.
- Introduces three training strategies: AffGAN (affine-projected GAN + instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-guided using DAE score), and AffLL (density-guided with PixelCNN-like likelihood).
- Theoretical contributions: amortised MAP → minimizing H[qθ, pY], modified GAN loss approximating KL(q||p), analysis/motivation for instance noise, and link from stochastic AffGAN to amortised VI.
- Empirics: Swiss-roll validates theory; affine projection enforces data consistency and can speed MSE training; AffGAN yields sharper SR images on textures/faces/ImageNet qualitatively.
- Main decision: Reject — due to limited and insufficiently rigorous empirical evaluation on real images and a theoretical–experimental mismatch.
- Principal empirical criticisms: few quantitative/perceptual metrics beyond PSNR/SSIM, no user studies, missing strong baselines (e.g., SRGAN/SRResNet), and no standard SR benchmark protocols.
- Principal theoretical criticism: AffGAN optimizes KL(q||p) rather than cross-entropy directly, relies on ideal discriminator assumptions, and uses synthetic known A, so generalization to real/mismatched degradations is unproven.
- Novelty/motivation: affine projection and amortised MAP framing are elegant and well-motivated; instance-noise perspective is timely and well-reasoned.
- Correctness: derivations are generally sound (cross-entropy reformulation, DAE score use, GAN KL objective), but the amortised MAP claim is only approximated and not empirically validated on real images.
- Empirical support verdict: Swiss-roll is convincing and projection benefits are shown, but main claims about perceptual SR lack rigorous comparisons and metrics; AffDG/AffLL perform weakly and need diagnosis.
- Benchmark recommendation: compare to SRResNet/SRGAN and strong MSE/MAE baselines on Set5/Set14/BSD100/Urban100/Div2K with standard protocols and projection ablations.
- Perceptual evaluation recommendation: report NIQE/PI/LPIPS and FID/KID, and run a small-scale human study to substantiate visual gains.
- Instance-noise recommendation: provide quantitative stability analysis and ablations (no noise vs instance noise vs one-sided smoothing vs WGAN-GP) and sensitivity to annealing schedule.
- Robustness recommendation: test misspecified/learned/spatially varying A or blind/plug-and-play variants to show real-world applicability.
- AffDG/AffLL recommendation: try modern score-based/diffusion estimators, clarify training pathologies, explore stabilisation strategies, or focus the narrative on AffGAN if others are secondary.
- Reproducibility/clarity: fix typos/equation formatting, make assumptions explicit (paired vs unpaired, how pX is obtained), and release code.
- Question: How sensitive is performance to approximation quality of A+ (kernel size/support) and what are the quantifiable residuals for Afθ(x)=x in practice?
- Question: Can the method train and infer on real LR images not generated by the known A and still perform well on natural degradations?
- Question: What is the empirical impact of adding stochasticity z to the generator — does the amortised VI perspective improve diversity or perceptual quality without harming consistency?
- Question: How does AffGAN compare head-to-head with SRGAN trained with a strong data-consistency term or with the exact projection?
- Question: Will the authors report perceptual metrics (NIQE/PI/LPIPS) and any user-study outcomes to substantiate visual improvements?
- Question: For AffDG, what were the exact noise annealing schedules and DAE capacities, and were modern score/diffusion estimators attempted?
- Question: Have alternative divergence formulations (e.g., WGAN-GP) been tested instead of instance noise, and how do stability and quality compare?
