Summary
This paper proposes “amortised MAP” for single-image super-resolution (SR): rather than empirical risk minimization with MSE/MAE, the authors enforce exact observation consistency via an affine projection layer and train the SR network to maximize the prior probability of its outputs. This recasts SR training as minimizing the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the distribution of reconstructions and the distribution of natural images, removing the need for paired LR–HR supervision. They instantiate three training routes:
- AffGAN: a GAN variant with an affine projection (hard observation constraint) and a generator loss targeting KL[q‖p]; they introduce “instance noise” to stabilize training.
- AffDG: use a denoiser to estimate score ∇y log pY(y) and backpropagate through the SR network (affine-projected).
- AffLL: maximize log-likelihood under a differentiable density model (PixelCNN + MCGSM) for prior guidance.
They show the affine projection can be implemented with strided conv A and learned pseudo-inverse A+ as a deconv, guaranteeing Afθ(x)=x and reducing training to matching priors. Experiments on a toy Swiss-roll, textures, CelebA, and ImageNet indicate AffGAN produces perceptually sharper results than MSE/MAE and soft-constrained GANs, while AffDG/AffLL tend to be blurry. They also show the projection helps even for MSE training, and derive a connection between a stochastic AffGAN and amortised variational inference.

Decision: Accept
Key reasons:
- Conceptual novelty: a clean, principled reframing of SR as amortised MAP with an affine projection that enforces the observation model exactly, turning SR into prior matching without paired supervision.
- Methodological boldness: three distinct realizations (GAN, score-based denoising, explicit likelihood) and the instance noise stabilization; plus a thoughtful connection to amortised VI.

Supporting arguments
- The derivation from constrained MAP to minimizing H[qθ, pY] via affine projection is elegant and practically actionable. It cleanly separates the likelihood (handled exactly) from the prior (learned), and justifies adversarial/perceptual training beyond heuristics.
- The affine projection layer is a simple architectural idea with outsized impact: exact LR–HR consistency, faster training, and no penalty terms; the pseudo-inverse learning procedure is well motivated and empirically stable.
- Instance noise is a well-argued fix for GAN instability when supports don’t overlap; the analysis is clear and the heuristic annealing is reasonable.
- The toy Swiss-roll convincingly validates that AffGAN/AffDG approximate the MAP mode and reduce cross-entropy close to the brute-force MAP baseline, while MSE/MAE fail as expected.
- On CelebA/ImageNet, AffGAN gives plausibly sharper textures and content than MSE, and affine projection improves LR-consistency versus soft constraints. The paper is transparent about failure cases and limitations of MAP (representation dependence, mode atypicality).

Concerns and weaknesses (tempered by the novelty)
- Empirical evaluation is limited for a modern SR paper: no human study, no LPIPS or FID/NIQE/PI scores, and no comparison to strong contemporaries (e.g., SRGAN/EDSR-like baselines). The qualitative gains are promising but not comprehensively validated.
- AffDG and AffLL underperform and sometimes diverge; while negative results are valuable, the paper leaves open whether better score/density models or schedules could make these routes competitive.
- The hard observation model assumes a known downsampling operator A and exact consistency; in real settings A is unknown or mismatched, and exact consistency may be harmful. Robustness to operator mismatch is not explored.
- GAN artifacts/high-frequency noise remain in some outputs; instance noise helps but a systematic ablation is missing.
- The KL-minimization claim for the GAN variant relies on idealized assumptions; more empirical evidence (e.g., effect of instance noise on discriminator uniqueness/stability) would strengthen it.

Suggestions to improve the paper (helpful, not all tied to my decision)
- Add perceptual metrics (LPIPS) and a small user study; include comparisons to SRGAN and a strong MSE/EDSR-like baseline.
- Evaluate robustness to operator mismatch (wrong A, blur kernel variation) and to real degradation pipelines (e.g., camera ISP).
- Report ablations: with/without affine projection; instance noise schedule; effect of learning vs fixing A+; entropy/diversity (H[qθ]).
- For AffDG: try modern score models (e.g., NCSN/ScoreSDE-like training) or multiscale noise schedules; for AffLL: stronger likelihoods and tempering.
- Demonstrate the “unpaired” virtue: train prior on a different HR dataset from LR sources, or show cross-domain viability.
- Explore the stochastic AffGAN/posterior sampling angle empirically (e.g., diversity conditioned on x).

Questions for the authors
- How sensitive is performance to misspecification of A? Can the affine projection be relaxed or learned jointly to handle real-world degradations?
- Can you quantify the stability gains from instance noise (e.g., success rate, discriminator loss trajectories) and provide an ablation versus label smoothing/gradient penalty?
- For AffDG, did you try modern score-matching/denoising setups (multi-noise training with exact score matching) and preconditioning? What limits the quality—score bias or SR optimization?
- How does the affine projection affect capacity/expressivity? Any cases where it hurts compared to soft constraints when A is imperfect?
- In practice, can you train without paired LR–HR by sampling x from a real LR dataset whose pX differs from A∗pY? Does the theory or practice break?
- For the VI connection, can you clarify the role of entropy H[qθ] in AffGAN versus pure cross-entropy minimization, and whether this leads to systematically different perceptual trade-offs?

Overall, this paper offers a fresh, principled perspective on SR and compelling architectural and algorithmic ideas. Despite imperfect empirical breadth, the conceptual advances justify acceptance.
