Summary and claimed contributions
- The paper proposes amortised MAP inference for single-image super-resolution by enforcing exact data consistency via an affine projection layer gθ(x) = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x, where A models the downsampling operator and A+ is its pseudoinverse. This guarantees Aŷ = x.
- Under this projection, training reduces to matching the marginal distribution qθ of reconstructed HR images to the true HR distribution pY, i.e., minimising H[qθ, pY].
- Three training routes are explored: (i) AffGAN, a GAN variant argued to approximate KL[qθ||pY] with an “instance noise” trick to stabilise training; (ii) AffDG, using gradients from a denoising autoencoder to approximate ∇y log pY(y); and (iii) AffLL, using a differentiable density model (PixelCNN+MCGSM) to provide log-likelihood guidance.
- Empirically, on a 2D Swiss roll toy example, AffGAN/AffDG are close to the brute-force MAP solution. On images (grass textures, CelebA, ImageNet), AffGAN yields sharper, more “perceptual” outputs than MSE/SSIM-optimised models; the affine projection maintains exact LR-HR consistency. The paper also discusses theoretical links between (stochastic) AffGAN and amortised variational inference.

Decision: Reject
Primary reasons: (1) Insufficient empirical validation against established SR baselines and metrics on standard benchmarks; (2) Heavy reliance on an idealised and known downsampling operator A (Gaussian blur + striding) with a numerically optimised A+, which limits practicality and may inflate gains; (3) The main novelty relative to prior SRGAN-style approaches is modest (a projection layer and a GAN loss variant) and the paper does not provide rigorous quantitative evidence (human studies, perceptual metrics) to support its claims.

Supporting arguments
- Baselines and benchmarks: The paper omits comparisons with strong and well-established SR baselines on standard datasets (e.g., SRGAN, VDSR/SRCNN, EDSR, Urban100/Set5/Set14/BSD100/DIV2K under bicubic). Reported quantitative results show that MSE training still dominates on PSNR/SSIM, and perceptual advantages of AffGAN are argued qualitatively. Without rigorous comparisons on standard protocols and robust perceptual metrics, it is difficult to assess the contribution.
- Observation model realism: All experiments assume a fixed, known A (Gaussian downsampling) and optimise a matched A+. Real-world SR often faces unknown kernels, mismatch, and noise; exact data consistency Aŷ = x is then neither realistic nor necessarily desirable. The claimed benefits of the projection may not hold under mismatch; the paper does not study robustness to kernel errors, noise, or bicubic downsampling.
- Affine projection novelty and scope: The projection gθ(x) = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x is a straightforward data-consistency construction for linear inverse problems. While incorporating it in SR CNNs is reasonable, the architectural novelty seems limited, and related “consistency” ideas exist in adjacent inverse problems. The paper does not show that this constraint improves perceptual quality beyond enforcing consistency; even in the authors’ results, AffGAN introduces high-frequency noise, and PSNR/SSIM are lower than MSE.
- GAN objective and stability: The theoretical argument for minimising KL[qθ||pY] relies on a modified generator loss and near-optimal discriminators; in practice, strong assumptions are needed. The “instance noise” trick is plausible but not well ablated: the paper states training was unstable without it, but provides no systematic study of noise schedules, sensitivity, or comparisons to other stabilisation methods (e.g., gradient penalties, Wasserstein). The connection to amortised VI is interesting but purely theoretical here; no empirical evidence is provided.
- The denoiser/density-guided routes: These are positioned as alternatives but are then largely abandoned because they yield blurry outputs or unstable training. Presenting three methods but only developing one undermines the overall contribution. If the MAP framing is central, stronger evidence that alternatives can work in challenging settings is needed—or a tighter focus on the single effective method.

Constructive feedback to improve the paper
- Provide thorough comparisons on standard SR benchmarks (Set5/Set14/BSD100/Urban100/DIV2K), under bicubic and, if desired, Gaussian kernels, with established baselines (e.g., SRGAN, ESRGAN later, EDSR). Include both distortion metrics (PSNR/SSIM) and perceptual metrics (e.g., no-reference IQA; if possible, MOS). Qualitative figures alone are not enough.
- Evaluate robustness to kernel mismatch and observational noise. Show how performance degrades when A+ is approximate or when A differs from training. This is critical for deployment.
- Ablate the affine projection: compare AffGAN vs SoftGAN with equal capacity; test “projected SRGAN” baselines. Quantify the trade-off between exact consistency and perceptual quality.
- Ablate instance noise: report convergence curves, FID/precision-recall-type measures, and failure modes with and without noise; compare to alternative stabilisation (e.g., label smoothing, gradient penalty).
- Tighten the theory: clarify conditions under which the affine projection genuinely converts the objective to H[qθ, pY] in high dimensions with approximate A+. Discuss how entropy terms (H[qθ]) interplay with MAP-like behaviour. Bridge the theory with empirical diagnostics beyond the 2D toy.
- If maintaining the three-methods framing, bring AffDG/AffLL to a competitive level or narrow scope to the single effective approach. The present results suggest they are not viable for high-dimensional SR as instantiated.

Questions for the authors
- How does AffGAN perform under bicubic downsampling (standard SR setting) and unknown kernels? What happens with additive noise in x?
- Can you compare directly to SRGAN (Ledig et al., 2016) with identical architectures, with and without your affine projection? Is your method simply SRGAN plus projection?
- How sensitive is performance to errors in A+? What filter sizes are needed; what is the computational overhead?
- Can you provide quantitative perceptual evidence (e.g., user study, no-reference IQA) supporting that AffGAN improves perceived quality despite lower PSNR/SSIM?
- What is the impact of the instance noise schedule on stability and final image quality? Any principled way to set/anneal σ?
- The ImageNet split states “104 samples” in test/validation; is this 104 or 10^4? Please clarify dataset sizes and training details for reproducibility.
- Can you release code/models for the affine projection layer (A, A+ estimation) and AffGAN training?
