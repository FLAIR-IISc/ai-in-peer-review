Summary of contributions
- The paper proposes amortised MAP inference for single-image super-resolution (SR). It argues that, because many HR images are consistent with a given LR input, MAP is preferable to MSE/MAE which yield low-probability, over-smoothed estimates under natural image statistics.
- A key architectural contribution is an affine projection layer gθ(x) = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x that enforces exact data-consistency Afθ(x) = x for a given downsampling operator A, with A+ the Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse. This reframes training as minimising the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the distribution of outputs and the HR image prior, enabling training without paired LR–HR targets.
- Three training strategies are developed: (i) AffGAN, a GAN variant with the SR network as generator and an objective shown to approximate minimisation of KL[qθ‖pY]; a stabilisation technique “instance noise” is introduced and motivated; (ii) AffDG, which uses a pre-trained denoiser to estimate ∂y log pY(y) and backpropagate it through the SR network; (iii) AffLL, which uses a tractable density model (PixelCNN + MCGSM) to guide training by maximising log pY of generated outputs.
- Theory includes: equivalence of amortised MAP to cross-entropy minimisation under the affine constraint; proof that the modified GAN generator loss approximates KL[q‖p]; a principled rationale for instance noise; and a derivation showing a stochastic AffGAN minimises an amortised VI objective Ex KL[qY|X;θ‖pY|X].
- Experiments: (a) a 2D Swiss-roll toy problem where AffGAN/AffDG match the MAP mode and achieve near-optimal cross-entropy; (b) MSE SR with/without affine projection, showing the projection does not hurt and enforces exact consistency; (c) qualitative SR results on grass textures, CelebA, and ImageNet, with AffGAN producing sharper, more plausible images (sometimes with high-frequency noise/hallucinations). AffDG/AffLL produce blur and are unstable.

Decision: Reject
Key reasons: (1) Empirical support for the central claims is limited and largely qualitative on real images; rigorous quantitative, perceptual, and comparative evaluations are missing. (2) Two of the three proposed amortised-MAP training routes (denoiser- and density-guided) fail to deliver good SR in practice, undermining the generality of the approach and leaving the contribution primarily as an AffGAN variant with limited ablations.

Supporting arguments
- Problem and motivation: The underdetermined nature of SR and the mismatch between MSE/MAE and perceptual plausibility are correctly identified. Casting SR as amortised MAP with a prior over natural images is well motivated and placed in the literature, with appropriate connections to GANs, denoising, and density models.
- Technical soundness:
  - The affine projection construction is correct and useful. It guarantees Afθ(x)=x, reframes training as minimising H[qθ, pY], and is implementable via convolution/deconvolution with a numerically estimated A+. The derivations for cross-entropy, and the null-space residual interpretation, are sound.
  - The modified GAN objective and its link to KL[q‖p] follow prior analyses and are consistent. The instance noise argument addresses discriminator degeneracy when supports are disjoint; the divergence dσ and annealing rationale are reasonable.
  - The denoiser-gradient connection to ∂y log pY is standard; using it to backpropagate into the SR network is theoretically valid.
  - The amortised VI equivalence for stochastic AffGAN under the affine constraint is a neat and correct derivation.
- Evidence:
  - The toy Swiss-roll convincingly shows that AffGAN/AffDG target the MAP mode and achieve low cross-entropy; this supports the theoretical claims.
  - On real images, the strongest empirical finding is that AffGAN yields sharper images and exact consistency via the affine layer. However, quantitative metrics (PSNR/SSIM) favour MSE as expected; there are no perceptual metrics or human studies to corroborate “plausibility.” Comparisons to established baselines (e.g., SRGAN/perceptual-loss methods) are missing.
  - AffDG and AffLL underperform (blurry, unstable), with limited diagnostic analysis beyond training difficulties. This raises concerns about the practicality of amortised MAP via these routes and weakens the paper’s broader claim of training without paired LR–HR data.
  - Realistic observation models (noise, unknown/mismatched A) are not addressed; the method assumes noiseless known A and exact A+.

Additional feedback (to help improve the paper; not all part of the decision)
- Expand empirical evaluation:
  - Include stronger baselines (e.g., SRGAN/perceptual-loss networks) and more comprehensive metrics (perceptual metrics and/or small-scale human MOS). Report diversity/entropy of outputs to discuss the KL vs cross-entropy distinction.
  - Provide ablations: effect of affine projection (fixed vs learned A+), sensitivity to A+ approximation error, instance noise schedule, and the modified GAN loss vs the standard non-saturating loss.
  - Assess robustness to observation noise and downsampling-kernel mismatch; consider a soft data-consistency term to handle real-world degradations and compare to the exact affine layer.
- Strengthen AffDG/AffLL:
  - Diagnose why they blur: analyse gradient magnitudes and alignment from the DAE/density model near and far from the data manifold; explore noise/likelihood annealing schedules more systematically; consider score-matching diffusion models or improved continuous autoregressive density models.
  - For AffLL, check whether the density model is sufficiently expressive at the scales used and whether the likelihood gradients are well conditioned.
- Clarify training without paired data: in experiments, pX is obtained by applying A to the HR dataset; discuss settings where unpaired LR/HR datasets are available and how to estimate pX without relying on the same HR set.
- Discuss the MAP representation dependence more concretely for SR (e.g., RGB vs perceptual feature space), and whether incorporating a learned invertible feature transform could mitigate this.
- Provide computational cost and training stability statistics, and consider releasing code for reproducibility.

Questions for the authors
- How sensitive is performance to inaccuracies in A+? What is the effect of using an approximate pseudoinverse (finite kernel) on both data-consistency and perceptual quality?
- Can your approach handle observation noise or unknown A? Would combining the affine projection with a learned noise model (soft constraint) alleviate this?
- For AffGAN, how critical is instance noise versus partial discriminator training or gradient penalties? Please provide quantitative ablations.
- For AffDG, what are the gradient norms and cosine similarity between estimated ∂y log p and true updates (on the toy task) across noise levels? Can score-based models improve this component?
- For AffLL, how do you verify that the density model’s gradients are informative for training SR? Do you observe mode-collapse toward high-likelihood but blurry regions?
- Can you quantify distribution matching on real images (e.g., statistics of gradients, patch histograms) to support the claim of minimising cross-entropy beyond qualitative visuals?
