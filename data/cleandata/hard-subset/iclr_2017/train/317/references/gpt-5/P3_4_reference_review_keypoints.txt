- Proposes amortised MAP inference for single-image SR using an affine projection layer ΠA to enforce exact data-consistency via ŷ = ΠA fθ(x), removing need for paired LR–HR supervision.  
- Develops three training approaches: AffGAN (affine-projected GAN with instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-guided using DAE score estimates), and AffLL (density-guided with PixelCNN-like likelihoods).  
- Provides theory linking the modified GAN loss to KL[q||p] and shows stochastic AffGAN performs amortised variational inference minimizing Ex KL(qθ(y|x) || p(y|x)).  
- Empirically shows affine projection enforces exact consistency (Ax=x) and speeds/facilitates MSE training.  
- Swiss-roll toy: AffGAN/AffDG approximate MAP and achieve low cross-entropy; MSE/MAE fail.  
- On textures, CelebA, and ImageNet, AffGAN yields perceptually sharper SR than MSE/MAE and soft-constraint baselines; AffDG/AffLL perform worse perceptually.  
- Instance noise is critical for stable GAN training and addresses manifold-disjoint support instability.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Clear, principled formulation of SR as amortised MAP with elegant affine-projection ensuring exact data-consistency.  
- Key reason 2: Solid theoretical links tying objectives to cross-entropy, KL, and amortised VI.  
- Key reason 3: Practical training innovation (instance noise) with convincing qualitative perceptual improvements.  
- Novelty: Affine projection is simple yet powerful, preventing “cheating” via soft penalties and aligning SR with generative modeling.  
- Theory strength: Clean reduction of MAP to cross-entropy and plausible GAN-KL connection under standard assumptions; useful amortised VI interpretation.  
- Empirical support strength: Swiss-roll validates objectives; real-image examples show plausible high-frequency detail generation.  
- Caveat: Conceptual mismatch—AffGAN minimizes KL[q||p] (includes entropy) which can diverge from strict MAP and should be more clearly acknowledged.  
- Caveat: Real-image evaluation is mostly qualitative; lacks human studies and modern perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS), and limited comparisons to SRGAN/VGG losses and standard SR benchmarks.  
- Caveat: AffDG and AffLL underperform and are not fully analyzed, weakening claims about those alternatives.  
- Caveat: Presentation issues—formatting, truncated sentences, and nuanced “no paired data” claim that in practice uses downscaled HR images.  
- Suggestion: Clarify relation between amortised MAP (cross-entropy) and AffGAN’s KL objective and implications for perceptual outcomes.  
- Suggestion: Add quantitative perceptual evaluations (user studies, LPIPS, FID) and comparisons to SRGAN/VGG, VDSR/EDSR, and standard benchmarks (Set5/14, DIV2K).  
- Suggestion: Evaluate robustness to A mismatch, test bicubic degradations, and include ablations for instance noise schedule, discriminator capacity, and A+ estimation.  
- Suggestion: Analyze AffDG/AffLL failures and try stronger density/score models or annealing strategies; connect to plug-and-play/RED literature.  
- Suggestion: Improve clarity, fix typos, better qualify “no pairs needed,” and release code for A+ and projection layer.  
- Question: Do you ever train with completely unpaired LR and HR sets and still succeed—clarify practical meaning of “no matched pairs needed.”  
- Question: How sensitive is AffGAN to misspecification of A at test time; can A be learned or handled when unknown?  
- Question: Given AffGAN minimizes KL[q||p], do you view it as amortised MAP; can you quantify q’s entropy and its effect on realism?  
- Question: Provide instance-noise ablations and comparisons to other stabilizers (label smoothing, gradient penalty, spectral norm).  
- Question: Can the stochastic generator produce multiple samples per x and what is the diversity vs fidelity trade-off?  
- Question: What specific failure modes occurred for AffDG/AffLL and could modern score/density estimators remedy them?
