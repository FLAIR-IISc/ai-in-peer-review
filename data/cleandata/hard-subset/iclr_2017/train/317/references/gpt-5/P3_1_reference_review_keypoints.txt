- Paper proposes amortised MAP for single-image SR with an affine projection layer ŷ = ΠAx fθ(x) enforcing exact data-consistency A ŷ = x.  
- Shows amortised MAP reduces to minimizing cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] with qθ the marginal of outputs, allowing training without paired LR–HR examples.  
- Proposes three instantiations: AffGAN (GAN generator = ΠAx fθ(x) with instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-based score backpropagation), and AffLL (fit a density model and maximize Eŷ log p̂Y).  
- AffGAN yields sharper, more plausible images than MSE/MAE and soft constraints but can hallucinate content inconsistent with x; AffDG and AffLL produce blurry or unstable results.  
- Experiments include Swiss roll toy, textures, CelebA, and ImageNet; affine projection improves reconstruction consistency and training dynamics.  
- Decision: Reject. Main reasons: limited/selective empirical support, lack of convincing validation on realistic SR benchmarks, and incremental novelty over concurrent GAN-based SR works.  
- Technical framing and affine projection are elegant and clarify the role of discriminative priors; instance noise rationale is well motivated.  
- The averaged objective Ey log pY(ŷ) does not guarantee per-input MAP due to function-class coupling and the generator actually minimizing KL[qθ||pY], not true cross-entropy.  
- Swiss roll experiment is informative but too simple to validate per-input MAP claims in realistic SR tasks.  
- Using an unconditional discriminator (not conditioned on x) can encourage hallucinations that preserve low-frequency consistency but alter semantics; a conditional discriminator baseline is missing.  
- Empirical comparisons omit standard SR benchmarks (Set5/Set14/BSD100/Urban100), SRGAN-like baselines, LPIPS or human perceptual studies, and robustness tests.  
- AffDG and AffLL are presented as core contributions but underperform and lack thorough hyperparameter/architecture exploration or quantitative analysis.  
- The method assumes known A and exact A+, which may not hold in real pipelines (bicubic, camera ISP); robustness to A mismatch is untested.  
- The claim of “no paired data needed” is misleading because pX is generated by applying A to HR samples during training.  
- Theoretical derivation that generator loss → KL[q||p] under optimal discriminator is correct; instance noise helps but the variational interpretation is not empirically validated.  
- Recommendation: compare to conditional GANs with D(y,x), SRGAN/perceptual+adversarial losses, and report LPIPS/human studies on standard benchmarks.  
- Recommendation: test robustness to A mismatch, analyze per-input MAPness (e.g., MCMC along affine slices), and ablate instance noise and discriminator conditioning.  
- Recommendation: strengthen AffDG/AffLL with modern density/score models (flows, diffusion), report systematic negative results, and clarify training details.  
- Recommendation: clarify the “unpaired” claim, release code and precomputed A+ kernels, and quantify training stability across seeds.  
- Key questions for authors: Did you try a conditional discriminator D(y,x)? How sensitive is AffGAN to downsampling kernel mismatch? Does KL minimization outperform non-saturating GAN loss? Can you quantify per-input MAP-ness? How does σ scheduling affect AffDG? What is the effect of instance noise choice and annealing?
