- Paper frames single-image SR as amortised MAP under known linear degradation x = Ay and enforces data-consistency via an affine projection layer ŷ = ΠAx fθ(x).
- Reformulates the objective as minimising H[qθ, pY], turning perceptual SR into matching the model’s marginal to the HR data distribution without per-pixel supervised losses.
- Proposes three training strategies: AffGAN (GAN variant with modified generator and instance noise), AffDG (use denoiser gradients as ∇y log p(y)), and AffLL (train against a learned density model).
- Claims AffGAN approximates KL[q‖p] (plus entropy) and connects AffGAN to amortised variational inference under a stochastic generator extension.
- Empirical sanity checks: 2D Swiss-roll shows AffGAN/AffDG approach MAP and lower cross-entropy on toy data.
- Affine projection with MSE yields exact consistency (Aŷ=x) and slightly improved MSE/SSIM, suggesting no harm and possible acceleration for MSE training.
- On real images (textures, CelebA, ImageNet) AffGAN produces visually sharper images than MSE or soft-constraint GANs, but results are largely qualitative.
- AffDG and AffLL underperform in practice and are abandoned without fixes, weakening claims of multiple viable amortised-MAP routes.
- Major decision: Reject due to insufficient empirical validation against strong SR baselines and lack of perceptual metrics or user studies.
- Key weakness: Method assumes a known, fixed A and numerically approximated A+, limiting realism and generality; no tests for degradation mismatch, noise, or unknown A.
- Theoretical derivations are clear about the affine-projection→cross-entropy link, but claims about the exact divergence optimised are weakened by instance noise (optimises a smoothed dσ).
- The amortised VI connection is conceptually interesting but not empirically demonstrated (no posterior sampling or calibration shown).
- Real-image evaluation lacks comparisons to contemporary SR methods (SRGAN/SRResNet, EDSR, RCAN) on standard benchmarks and lacks modern perceptual metrics (LPIPS, FID, NIQE) or MOS.
- Visual artifacts and hallucinations observed (e.g., structure confusions) indicate a need for controlled fidelity-vs-realism evaluation.
- Paper fails to test robustness to A mismatch, spatially-varying/non-Gaussian A, or additive noise; practical feasibility of computing A+ and its numerical issues are not explored.
- Reviewer recommends extensive benchmarks, perceptual metrics, user studies, and ablations (affine projection, A+ kernel sizes, instance noise schedules, loss variants).
- Suggests robustness tests: train/evaluate with different As, add sensor noise, and consider unknown-A strategies or joint estimation.
- Requests clarity on which divergence is optimised with instance noise and empirical ablation showing instance noise benefits over other stabilisation methods.
- Asks for demonstrations of conditional diversity for fixed x under the VI view and empirical tests of MAP coordinate dependence and entropy effects.
- Practical feedback: report computational cost, training stability statistics, inference speed, and release code for A+ computation and affine projection implementation.
- Specific questions for authors include sensitivity to A mismatch/noise, quantitative perceptual effects of affine projection, ablations for instance noise, stabilisation of AffDG/AffLL, conditional sample diversity, and details on A+ kernel size/runtime trade-offs.
