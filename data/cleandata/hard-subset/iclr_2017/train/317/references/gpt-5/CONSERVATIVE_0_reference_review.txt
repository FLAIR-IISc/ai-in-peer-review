Summary and contributions
- The paper targets single-image super-resolution via amortised MAP inference, arguing that MSE-trained models yield perceptually implausible averages. The key architectural idea is an affine projection layer that enforces exact consistency with the downsampling operator x = Ay by using gθ(x) = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x, so outputs lie in the affine subspace of valid HR solutions.
- Building on this, the paper reframes training as minimising cross-entropy H[qθ, pY], and proposes three ways to approximate it: (i) AffGAN, a GAN variant argued to minimise KL[q‖p] and employing “instance noise” for stability; (ii) AffDG, which uses a denoiser to estimate score ∇y log pY and backpropagate through the affine projection; (iii) AffLL, which trains a differentiable density model (PixelCNN-like with MCGSM) and minimises negative log-likelihood of outputs.
- Empirical evidence spans a toy Swiss-roll, grass textures, CelebA, and ImageNet, with qualitative gains in perceptual sharpness for AffGAN. The paper also discusses theoretical caveats of MAP (coordinate dependence) and sketches a connection between a stochastic AffGAN and amortised variational inference.

Decision: Reject
Key reasons: (1) The empirical validation is insufficient and not rigorous against established baselines under realistic SR settings; (2) The core assumptions (exact, noiseless, known downsampling A) and reliance on adversarial priors yield hallucinations and questionable fidelity, with no robustness analysis.

Supporting arguments
- Unrealistic observation model and lack of robustness: The method enforces exact data-consistency for x = Ay with a numerically estimated A+. Real-world SR involves unknown/approximate degradations and sensor noise. The paper neither evaluates sensitivity to A mismatch/noise nor demonstrates robustness. The qualitative “snake becomes water” example highlights hallucination risk, which is unacceptable in many SR applications.
- Limited empirical evaluation: Results heavily rely on qualitative inspection. There are no comparisons to strong contemporary SR baselines using perceptual losses (e.g., SRGAN, EDSR/RCAN with VGG/LPIPS losses), nor to classical MAP/regularized priors. Quantitative metrics are sparse (PSNR/SSIM shown mainly for MSE baselines), and no perceptual metrics (LPIPS, FID/KID) or human studies are provided. Claims about minimising cross-entropy are only validated on a 2D toy problem; there is no surrogate for H[q,p] in images beyond anecdotes.
- “Unpaired” training claim is not convincingly demonstrated: Training requires samples x ∼ pX. In practice, x appears to be generated by applying the same A to HR images; this is effectively a synthetic paired pipeline and does not test the purported benefit of not requiring HR-LR pairs. No results are shown on genuinely unpaired LR data or with unknown degradations.
- GAN objective and stability: The proposed generator loss that aims at KL minimisation relies on optimal discriminators and specific update rules. There is no ablation comparing to standard GAN losses, nor clear evidence that the KL-oriented variant yields better MAP-like behaviour. “Instance noise” is closely related to existing ideas (label smoothing, noise injection, later formalised in works like WGAN-GP/regularisation), but the contribution is incremental and the analysis informal.
- AffDG/AffLL underperform and are unstable: The paper acknowledges that the denoiser- and density-guided variants produce blurry or diverging results. This undermines the central narrative that amortised MAP with learned priors is effective beyond GANs, and it is unclear whether the failure is fundamental (e.g., poor score estimates) or implementation-related. No comparison is made to more recent score-based/EBM estimators that could strengthen this path.
- Novelty and positioning: The affine projection/data-consistency layer is useful, but similar ideas are well-known across inverse problems (MRI, compressed sensing, plug-and-play, deep data-consistency). The paper does not sufficiently position against this body of work, nor compare to strong “soft consistency” baselines that include explicit fidelity terms tuned for realistic noise.
- Presentation/reproducibility issues: Several references/figures are incomplete or garbled; key implementation details, datasets/splits, and evaluation protocols are not fully specified; there is no code. This impedes assessment and reproducibility.

Additional feedback (to help improve the paper)
- Evaluate under realistic degradations: include noise/blur, kernel mismatch, and unknown A. Compare with and without the affine projection to quantify robustness.
- Strengthen baselines and metrics: report PSNR/SSIM along with LPIPS and distributional metrics (FID/KID). Include user studies where perceptual quality is the goal. Compare against SRGAN/ESRGAN/EDSR/RCAN and feature-based perceptual losses.
- Fidelity vs perceptual trade-off: add measures of content fidelity beyond low-frequency consistency (e.g., LR-reconstruction error, identity preservation for faces). Consider hybrid losses that regularise hallucinations.
- Clarify the “unpaired” setting: demonstrate training when only HR data define pY and LR x come from a separate distribution not generated by the same A. Otherwise temper the claim.
- Ablations: (i) A+ fixed vs trainable; (ii) instance noise schedule and magnitude; (iii) projection strength vs soft constraint; (iv) effect of discriminator optimality and different GAN losses.
- For AffDG, consider modern score models (denoising score matching/NCSN/ScoreSDE) to improve gradient quality; for AffLL, try state-of-the-art continuous likelihoods and calibrate the density’s sharpness to avoid vanishing gradients.
- Discuss computational cost and training stability, with learning curves and variance across seeds.

Questions for the authors
1) How are LR samples x obtained during training? Are they strictly generated as x = Ay from the HR training set, or do you use real LR data? If the former, how does the method behave when the test-time LR images are produced by a different or noisy degradation?
2) Sensitivity to A: What happens if A is misspecified (kernel width/stride/boundary conditions) or noise is added? Please provide quantitative robustness studies.
3) Can you provide quantitative comparisons on standard SR benchmarks (e.g., DIV2K, Set14, Urban100) versus SRGAN/ESRGAN/EDSR/RCAN, including LPIPS/FID and, if possible, MOS?
4) Does the KL-oriented GAN loss outperform standard non-saturating GAN objectives in practice for SR? Any ablation supporting the rationale?
5) How is A+ computed at image boundaries, and what is the effect of finite kernel support on exactness of the projection?
6) The stochastic AffGAN/variational inference connection is intriguing. Can you show empirical benefits (e.g., sampling multiple ŷ per x to characterise posterior variability) and quantify diversity/fidelity?
7) Why do AffDG and AffLL fail to produce sharp images—insufficient priors, optimisation issues, or theoretical limitations? Could stronger score/density models remedy this?
