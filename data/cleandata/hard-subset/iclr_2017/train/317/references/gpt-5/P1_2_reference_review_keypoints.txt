- Reframes single-image SR as amortized MAP inference under a natural image prior, replacing pixel-wise MSE/MAE objectives.
- Introduces an affine projection layer ŷ = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x that guarantees exact data fidelity A ŷ = x using a learned pseudoinverse A+.
- Shows amortized MAP reduces to minimizing cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between model output distribution qθ and HR data distribution pY, decoupling training from paired LR–HR supervision.
- Proposes three H[qθ, pY] minimization methods: AffGAN (affine-projected GAN), AffDG (denoiser-guided gradients), and AffLL (train against a tractable density model).
- Argues AffGAN approximately minimizes KL[qθ‖pY] under Bayes-optimal discriminator and connects to amortized variational inference for a stochastic generator.
- Introduces “instance noise” (adding noise to real and generated samples with annealing) to stabilize GAN training by ensuring overlapping supports.
- Toy experiments (2D Swiss-roll) show AffGAN/AffDG closely match tractable MAP and achieve low cross-entropy.
- Affine projection does not harm and can improve MSE training on CelebA while enforcing perfect consistency A ŷ = x.
- On textures and faces/natural images, AffGAN produces perceptually sharper results than MSE/MAE baselines, though PSNR/SSIM are lower.
- Decision: Weak accept — affine projection is a principled, broadly useful contribution; amortized MAP framing and instance-noise insight are valuable despite limited empirical breadth.
- Strength: Clear motivation and positioning relative to SRGAN and feature losses, highlighting exact vs soft data constraints.
- Strength: Technical clarity in affine projection derivation and equivalence to H[qθ, pY]; viable theoretical links to GAN/KL and amortized VI.
- Strength: Principled justification for instance noise as more than a heuristic GAN trick.
- Strength: Empirical demos (toy problem, CelebA, qualitative SR results) support claims and show improved training dynamics.
- Weakness: Empirical rigor is limited — largely qualitative on real images, missing standard SR benchmarks, perceptual metrics (LPIPS/NIQE/PI), SRGAN comparisons, and user studies.
- Weakness: Method assumes known, noise-free downsampling A; exact consistency may break under real camera degradations and misspecification.
- Weakness: The theoretical leap from minimizing H[qθ, pY] to per-x MAP estimates is intuitive but not fully formalized; MAP’s representation dependence unresolved.
- Weakness: Some clarity/notation issues and limited details on boundary handling and practical computation of A+.
- Recommended experiments: add standard SR benchmarks, perceptual metrics, user study, direct SRGAN/feature-loss comparisons, and ablations isolating affine projection and instance noise.
- Recommended analysis: quantify robustness to A mismatch/noise, detail A+ computation (boundaries, kernel sizes), provide AA+A and A+AA+ errors on real images, and release code.
- For AffDG/AffLL: analyze failure modes, visualize DAE gradient fields at different σ, evaluate density calibration, and try modern score-based models or score-matching.
- Clarify generator loss choice (log-odds vs non-saturating), divergences targeted (KL vs JS), and conditions for each objective’s minimization.
- Questions for authors include sensitivity to A mismatch/noise, ablations on instance-noise magnitude/annealing, generalization across upscaling factors/kernels, DAE σ selection and behavior, and quantifying the consistency vs perceptual sharpness trade-off.
