1) Summary of contributions
The paper proposes amortised MAP inference for single-image super-resolution (SR) by enforcing exact data-consistency with the downsampling model and optimizing only for high prior probability under natural image statistics. The key ideas are:
- Affine projection layer: A principled architectural constraint ŷ = Π_Ax fθ(x) = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x, which guarantees Afθ(x) = x for any x and any fθ. This encodes the SR constraint exactly (as opposed to soft L1/L2 penalties), decouples likelihood from the prior, and turns amortised MAP into minimization of the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the model’s output distribution and the HR image distribution.
- Three learning instantiations to approximate this objective without paired LR-HR data: (i) AffGAN, a GAN variant where the generator is the affine-projected SR network; (ii) AffDG, which backpropagates denoiser-estimated score ∇y log pY(y); and (iii) AffLL, which uses a tractable density model (PixelCNN+MCGSM) to directly minimize cross-entropy.
- “Instance noise” for stabilizing GAN training by convolving data and model distributions with noise to ensure overlapping support.
- Empirical studies: a 2D Swiss roll toy problem (showing outputs align with the posterior mode and reduced cross-entropy); proof-of-concept MSE training showing affine projection improves training speed and consistency; qualitative SR on grass textures, CelebA, and ImageNet, where AffGAN yields sharper results than MSE/MAE baselines and the soft-constraint variants.

Overall, the paper is an interesting re-framing of SR as amortised MAP with a clean architectural mechanism to enforce data consistency, and it connects GANs and denoising-based score estimation to this objective.

2) Decision: Reject
Primary reasons:
- Empirical support is limited and mixed: only AffGAN yields compelling results; AffDG and AffLL largely fail to produce good images. Quantitative evaluation lacks modern perceptual metrics or user studies, and comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., SRGAN, perceptual losses) are minimal.
- Novelty/rationale are promising, but the practical gains beyond existing adversarial SR methods are not convincingly demonstrated; the main unique element (affine projection) is not thoroughly ablated against established consistency/back-projection strategies or across realistic degradation mismatches.

3) Supporting arguments
- On motivation and placement: The critique of MSE for multi-modal posteriors is sound, and the MAP framing with exact consistency is well motivated. The literature coverage is generally appropriate; the links to f-GANs and denoising-as-score estimation are correct and timely. The discussion of label smoothing vs instance noise is thoughtful and consistent with theory on support mismatch.
- On theory: The derivation that, under exact data consistency, amortised MAP reduces to minimizing H[qθ, pY] is clear and valuable. The alternative GAN generator objective approximating KL[q||p], and the denoising gradient estimator, are plausible; however, the overall theoretical claims hinge on idealized conditions (optimal discriminators, infinitesimal noise, well-specified A and A+) and are not stress-tested.
- On experiments/rigor:
  - The Swiss roll experiment supports the central claim but is a toy setting.
  - On images, the strongest results come from AffGAN; outputs are often sharp but sometimes contain high-frequency noise or semantic hallucinations. No human study, LPIPS, NIQE/BRISQUE, or FID/KID are reported, making it difficult to substantiate “perceptually superior” claims.
  - The affine projection layer seems beneficial for training speed and consistency, but ablations do not compare to alternative exact/iterative back-projection or plug-and-play priors; robustness to A mis-specification is not studied.
  - The promise of unpaired training is not convincingly validated; all LR images are generated from HR via the same A, and no cross-domain (unpaired) experiments are shown.
  - AffDG and AffLL underperform substantially; while the paper offers reasons, the negative results weaken the broader amortised-MAP narrative.

4) Suggestions for improvement (constructive, not all part of the decision)
- Strengthen empirical validation:
  - Evaluate on standard SR benchmarks (Set5/Set14/B100/Urban100/DIV2K) and report PSNR/SSIM for completeness plus perceptual metrics (LPIPS, NIQE/BRISQUE, FID/KID) and a small-scale user study.
  - Compare against SRGAN and feature-perceptual losses (e.g., VGG) with matched architectures/training budgets. Include recent back-projection or data-consistency-enforcing SR models as baselines.
  - Provide a thorough ablation of the affine projection: exact vs soft constraints, projection accuracy vs kernel size, training with fixed vs learned A+, and computational overhead. Compare to known back-projection iterative schemes.
  - Demonstrate robustness to A mis-specification (e.g., train with assumed Gaussian/bicubic, test with different/downsampling kernels and mild sensor noise).
  - Substantiate the “unpaired” claim by training with HR and LR drawn from different datasets and/or with unmatched degradations.
- Clarify theory and methodology:
  - Discuss the difference between minimizing H[q,p] and KL[q||p] in terms of mode-seeking/covering behavior and its impact on MAP-like solutions; reconcile with the entropy term in AffGAN.
  - Provide more details and sensitivity analyses for instance noise (schedule, magnitude, effect on D optimality), and compare to other stabilizers (gradient penalty, spectral norm, WGAN-GP).
  - For AffDG, report the learned denoiser’s fidelity (denoising MSE vs σ), the impact of noise annealing schedules, and experiments with modern score-based models.
  - For AffLL, consider stronger likelihood models and multi-scale or latent-variable density estimators; report calibration and log-likelihood on held-out HR data.
- Address hallucinations/failure modes:
  - Add semantic regularizers or conditional priors to mitigate plausible-but-wrong reconstructions; assess content fidelity (e.g., identity preservation on faces).
  - Explore stochastic extensions (Appendix F) empirically, comparing MAP-like point estimates vs posterior sampling.

Questions for the authors
- How robust is the method to mis-specification of A and A+? What happens when the real degradation deviates from the assumed Gaussian+stride model (e.g., bicubic, unknown blur, sensor noise)?
- Can you demonstrate training with truly unpaired LR and HR datasets (different sources/domains), and quantify performance?
- How sensitive is AffGAN to the instance noise schedule and magnitude? Did you try alternative stabilizers (e.g., gradient penalties, spectral normalization), and how do they compare?
- Could you report perceptual metrics (LPIPS, NIQE/BRISQUE/FID) and/or a small user study to support the “perceptually superior” claim?
- For AffDG, what are the denoiser’s score estimation errors across σ, and how do these errors translate to SR performance? Have you tried more expressive score models?
- For AffLL, is the weak performance due to a mismatch between the density model and true pY, or optimization issues? Can a stronger continuous likelihood model improve results?
- Do you observe consistent gains from the affine projection when combined with modern perceptual losses or adversarial losses, beyond training speed and consistency error?
