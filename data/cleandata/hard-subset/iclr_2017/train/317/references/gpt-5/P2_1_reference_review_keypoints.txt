- Proposes amortised MAP inference for single-image SR by matching output distribution to a natural image prior instead of minimising pixel losses.
- Introduces an affine projection layer gθ(x) that guarantees data-consistency A ŷ = x and converts the constrained MAP objective into minimising H[qθ, pY].
- Explores three approximation routes: AffGAN (GAN variant minimising KL[q||p] with instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-guided score estimates), and AffLL (likelihood-based prior like PixelCNN/MCGSM).
- Presents conceptual links: AffGAN+affine projection as amortised inference over pY|X, and a stochastic AffGAN connection to variational inference.
- Decision: Reject (borderline) due to insufficient empirical support for broad claims.
- Main empirical shortcoming: only AffGAN yields compelling results; AffDG and AffLL underperform and are effectively abandoned.
- Evaluation lacks SR rigor: no canonical benchmarks/degradations, minimal perceptual evaluation, and limited quantitative analyses of claimed benefits.
- Motivation and literature positioning are strong; affine projection and critique of MSE are well argued.
- Theoretical reduction to H[qθ, pY] is clear under the linear observation model; GAN-KL link and instance-noise rationale are plausible but heuristic.
- Affine projection layer is a neat, potentially broadly useful novelty; applying denoiser gradients to SR is a reasonable extension.
- Toy Swiss-roll and cross-entropy estimates convincingly show AffGAN/AffDG track posterior modes where MSE/MAE fail.
- Ablations show affine projection enforces consistency and often helps MSE training without harm.
- AffGAN yields qualitatively sharper results on grass/CelebA/ImageNet.
- Weakness: AffDG/AffLL produce blurry/unstable results and are de-emphasised despite being central claims.
- Missing standard SR comparisons (Set5/14, BSD100, Urban100), bicubic x4 baselines, LPIPS/user studies, and comparisons to strong contemporaneous SR methods.
- The “unpaired” training claim is not demonstrated in realistic mismatched pX scenarios; experiments use synthetic LR via known A.
- No evaluation of robustness to operator mismatch, unknown blur/kernels, additive noise, or mis-specified A.
- Visible failure modes (hallucinations) highlight risk; no uncertainty calibration, sampling, or mitigation provided.
- Affine inverse A+ is learned numerically with limited exploration of realistic degradations or sensitivity to kernel size/truncation.
- Suggested empirical improvements: benchmark comparisons, LPIPS/user studies, ablations on A+/A mismatch and instance-noise schedules, and robustness tests to kernel/noise mismatch.
- For AffDG/AffLL: try modern score-based/diffusion priors, multi-noise-level training, and diagnose divergence causes (step sizes, geometry mismatch).
- Clarify and demonstrate true unpaired training (independent HR and LR datasets) and discuss identifiability; include stochastic VI experiments or relegate to future work.
- Key questions for authors: can you show true unpaired training, sensitivity to A/kernel/noise mismatch, instance-noise vs label smoothing effects, comparison to SRGAN with soft consistency, improved score-matching for AffDG, learned A+ kernel size/artifacts, and diversity metrics for conditioned outputs?
