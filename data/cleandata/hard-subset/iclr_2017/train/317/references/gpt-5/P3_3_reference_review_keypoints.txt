- Proposes amortised MAP for single-image SR with an affine projection layer that enforces Aŷ = x exactly, reducing training to minimising cross-entropy between output distribution and HR prior.  
- Introduces three approximations: AffGAN (GAN with instance noise), AffDG (denoising autoencoder backprop of score), and AffLL (likelihood-guided using a PixelCNN-like prior).  
- Presents the affine projection as a general architectural element attachable to any SR network and links stochastic AffGAN to amortised variational inference.  
- Experiments: 2D Swiss-roll toy, MSE ablation, and real-image 4× SR on textures/CelebA/ImageNet; AffGAN yields sharper images, AffDG/AffLL are blurry in high dimensions.  
- Decision: Reject (borderline) due to limited empirical validation and unrealistic assumption of known/matched degradation operator A.  
- Strength: The affine projection is a neat, useful idea that guarantees LR-HR consistency.  
- Strength: Framing SR as amortised MAP unifies GAN/DAE/density-based priors and motivates adversarial SR.  
- Theoretical claim that AffGAN minimises KL(q‖p) is plausible but relies on smoothing/noise because qθ is supported on a low-dimensional manifold.  
- Amortised VI connection (Appendix F) is interesting but high-level and lacks rigorous conditions or demonstrated practical benefits.  
- Empirically, only AffGAN scales to realistic images; AffDG and AffLL fail in high dimensions, weakening the “three methods” claim.  
- Evaluation is mainly qualitative; missing perceptual metrics (LPIPS/DISTS), distributional metrics (FID/KID), user studies, and comparisons to perceptual SR baselines (SRGAN/VGG-feature losses).  
- The prior can hallucinate semantically incorrect content (e.g., snake→water), a critical SR failure mode that is not systematically studied.  
- Method assumes known A and constructs A+ numerically; this is unrealistic for real SR with unknown or spatially varying degradations.  
- No robustness tests to kernel/scale mismatch, bicubic vs Gaussian, or unknown degradations; ”paired data not required” claim is unsupported because training used matched A.  
- Recommend stronger empirical evaluation: compare to strong perceptual baselines, report LPIPS/FID/KID, and run a user study.  
- Recommend ablations on affine projection: learned vs fixed A+, kernel size, and robustness to A mis-specification.  
- Recommend unpaired experiments to validate the claim that paired HR/LR are not required.  
- Theory fixes: state assumptions for KL-minimisation and Appendix F, discuss absolute continuity via instance noise and the role of entropy for deterministic generators.  
- Instance-noise analysis should be compared systematically to other stabilisers (label smoothing, WGAN-GP, spectral norm) with ablations on schedules/magnitudes.  
- For AffDG/AffLL, either show competitive settings (smaller scales, patch tasks, stronger priors) or present them as negative results with analysis.  
- Practical deployment: analyze failure modes and propose controls (stochastic sampling, uncertainty estimates, tempered priors, hybrid objectives) to avoid harmful hallucinations.  
- Address numerical stability and artifacts from A+ (checkerboard artifacts, strided deconvs) and show the projection does not introduce artifacts.  
- Key questions for authors: robustness to mis-specified A and results on standard bicubic benchmarks; evidence for unpaired HR/LR success; quantitative measures (FID/KID) and instance-noise ablations; conditions where AffDG/LL can work; and whether Appendix F yields a practical stochastic SR sampler.
