Summary and contributions
- The paper tackles single-image super-resolution by reframing it as amortised MAP inference under a natural image prior. It proposes an affine projection layer that enforces exact consistency with the LR observation (Aŷ = x) via ŷ = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x, where A is the downsampling operator and A+ its Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse (implemented by a deconvolution/up-convolution).
- With this architecture, maximising the posterior reduces to minimising the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY], where qθ is the marginal distribution of outputs on LR inputs and pY is the HR data distribution. Three training strategies to approximate this are proposed:
  1) AffGAN: a GAN variant claimed to minimise KL[qθ‖pY] (via a modified generator objective) plus an “instance noise” trick to stabilise training.
  2) AffDG: backpropagating score estimates ∂y log pY(y) from a denoising autoencoder.
  3) AffLL: using a differentiable density model (PixelCNN + MCGSM) to provide log-likelihood gradients.
- Experiments on a 2D Swiss roll toy problem, grass textures, CelebA, and ImageNet 4× SR show that the affine projection enforces consistency, that MSE-trained affine models train faster and achieve lower MSE/SSIM than unconstrained counterparts, and that AffGAN produces perceptually sharper images (albeit sometimes with high-frequency noise).

Decision: Reject
Key reasons:
1) Insufficient empirical validation and comparisons: no evaluation on standard SR benchmarks, limited metrics, and incomplete baselines; two of the three proposed MAP-training routes (AffDG, AffLL) are abandoned due to poor performance without thorough investigation.
2) Numerous technical and presentation issues (derivation clarity, incorrect/ambiguous equations, broken references/figures, and formatting errors) that undermine confidence in correctness and reproducibility.

Supporting arguments
- Novelty/significance: The affine projection that guarantees Aŷ = x is a neat architectural idea, and the connection from amortised MAP to cross-entropy with pY is conceptually appealing. The instance noise discussion is interesting and aligns with known issues in GAN training. The variational inference view for a stochastic AffGAN variant is also thought-provoking. However, the primary empirical takeaway is that AffGAN (a variant of GAN training) with affine projection gives sharp results—this is incremental relative to prior SR-GAN work, and the other two proposed MAP-training strategies are not convincingly validated.
- Empirical rigor:
  - No standard SR datasets (e.g., Set5/14, BSD100, Urban100, DIV2K) or realistic degradations; training/testing use synthetic Gaussian-blur + strided downsampling identical to the model’s A, making the task easier and tightly matched to the inductive bias.
  - Perceptual quality is asserted via visual inspection; there is no LPIPS, FID/KID, user study, or any perceptual metric beyond anecdotal comments. PSNR/SSIM reported only for CelebA; unsurprisingly MSE wins.
  - Comparisons are limited. There is no head-to-head with strong SR baselines (SRResNet/EDSR/RFDN, perceptual-loss models, SRGAN variants), nor with soft-constraint consistency approaches tuned to near-zero error.
  - AffDG and AffLL are dropped after producing blurry results; the paper does not explore hyperparameters, architecture variants, stronger denoisers/density models, or curriculum designs beyond a brief annealing heuristic. This weakens the claim that amortised MAP via score/density guidance is viable.
- Theoretical clarity/correctness:
  - Eq. (10): the generator objective is written as L(G;D) = −Ez log D(G(z)) − D(G(z)), which is dimensionally and conceptually odd. The appendix later uses Ez[log D(G) − log(1 − D(G))] to argue minimisation of KL[q‖p]. Please fix the main text to match the derivation and clearly state the training update used in practice. Also, emphasise assumptions (unique Bayes-optimal discriminator, sufficient capacity, support overlap ensured by noise).
  - The projection ΠAx requires x ∈ range(A) to guarantee Aŷ = x. In practice, with real LR images (and noise/MTF/camera pipeline), this often fails. The paper assumes synthetic x = Ay; this limits real-world applicability and should be discussed.
  - The claim that dσ is a Bregman divergence for Gaussian noise is stated without proof or citation; at least provide a reference and conditions.
  - The amortised VI view (Appendix F) is interesting, but only for the stochastic generator; the main experiments appear to use deterministic fθ(x). Clarify what is actually used in experiments and do not overstate the VI connection for the deterministic case.
- Reproducibility and details:
  - Many missing training details: optimizer(s), batch sizes, LR schedules, number of discriminator/generator steps, exact instance noise annealing schedule, regularisation, compute budget.
  - The A+ estimation method is described, but no quantitative check on how close A+ is to the true pseudoinverse (beyond values of `1 + `2) nor analysis of kernel support vs aliasing.
  - Ambiguities: “42 kernels” for A+ likely means 4^2; please correct. Sigmoid at output may hinder dynamic range; justify.
- Presentation quality:
  - The manuscript has numerous broken equations, figure/table references, and typos (e.g., “Shannon-Jensen Divergence” vs Jensen–Shannon, “varational,” “emperical,” dangling “shown ( )”, “AffGAN( )”, etc.), incomplete references with placeholders (e.g., “? \Q2016\E”), and grammar issues. This significantly detracts from readability and confidence.

Additional feedback (to help improve the paper)
- Strengthen experiments: evaluate on standard SR benchmarks and realistic degradations; report PSNR/SSIM for distortion fidelity and LPIPS/FID for perceptual quality; include user studies if feasible.
- Provide ablations:
  - Affine projection vs strong soft constraints matched to zero LR error.
  - GAN training: standard vs your KL-variant; with vs without instance noise; annealing schedules.
  - Impact of A+ kernel size/accuracy on results; robustness when x ∉ range(A).
- For AffDG and AffLL, try stronger score/density estimators (multi-scale DAEs, modern flow/score-based models), stabilisation (e.g., gradient clipping, curriculum), and provide thorough negative results rather than abandoning the paths.
- Clarify the exact generator/discriminator losses used, update schedules, and training heuristics. If the discriminators are not trained to convergence, discuss the impact on the KL interpretation.
- Discuss domain gap: how would the method handle unknown camera pipelines/noise? Consider learning A jointly or estimating it from data.
- Improve writing/formatting: fix all broken references/equations; unify notation; add missing citations (e.g., score matching literature).

Questions for the authors
1) What exact generator loss was used in practice (please provide the explicit formula and code snippet)? Was it the ratio log D − log(1 − D) as in the appendix, or something else?
2) Did you actually use a stochastic generator (fθ(x, z)) anywhere? If not, how should we interpret the VI connection for the deterministic case?
3) How do you ensure x ∈ range(A) at test time? What happens when the LR input is not exactly produced by your A?
4) Is “42 kernels” in A+ a typesetting error for 4^2? Please clarify the A+ architecture and kernel sizes.
5) Can you provide quantitative ablations demonstrating the benefit of instance noise vs standard GAN tricks, and the sensitivity to the annealing schedule?
6) Why are AffDG and AffLL so much blurrier? Is it a limitation of the estimators or of the training dynamics? Any quantitative diagnostics (e.g., score norm, likelihood curvature) to support your hypotheses?
7) Please compare AffGAN against a strong perceptual SR baseline (e.g., SRGAN/SRGAN+perceptual, ESRGAN) under matched training data and report both distortion and perceptual metrics.
