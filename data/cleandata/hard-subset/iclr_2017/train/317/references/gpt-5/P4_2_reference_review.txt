Summary of contributions
- Proposes amortised MAP inference for single-image super-resolution by enforcing exact data-consistency via an affine projection layer. The projection uses the Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse A+ of the known downsampling operator A, yielding outputs ŷ in the affine subspace {y : Ay = x} and turning amortised MAP into minimising cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the model’s output distribution and the HR data distribution.
- Instantiates three training strategies to approximate the cross-entropy: (i) AffGAN, a GAN variant argued to minimise KL[qθ‖pY], with a new “instance noise” regularisation to stabilise training; (ii) AffDG, which backpropagates score estimates from a denoiser (DAE) to ascend log pY; (iii) AffLL, which uses a differentiable likelihood model (PixelCNN/MCGSM) to train by (approximate) maximum likelihood.
- Provides a clear theoretical formulation linking amortised MAP to cross-entropy minimisation under the affine projection and relating a stochastic AffGAN to amortised variational inference (Appendix F).
- Empirical evidence: on a 2D Swiss-roll toy problem AffGAN/AffDG approach MAP and achieve low H[qθ, pY]; affine projection preserves exact consistency Afθ(x)=x; in image SR (CelebA, ImageNet, texture patches) AffGAN yields sharper, more plausible images than MSE/MAE baselines; the affine projection does not harm (and sometimes helps) MSE training; instance noise helps stabilise training.

Decision: Reject
Key reasons: (1) Insufficient empirical support on real-image SR to substantiate the central MAP/cross-entropy claims and superiority over strong baselines; (2) Novelty over contemporaneous GAN-based SR is moderate, and several contributions (GAN objective, instance noise) rely on assumptions with limited ablations and quantitative evaluation.

Supporting arguments
- Technical soundness: The reduction from amortised MAP with exact data consistency to minimising H[qθ, pY] is correct and insightful. The affine projection parameterisation gθ(x)=(I−A+A)fθ(x)+A+x is well-motivated and ensures exact Afθ(x)=x given a known A and accurate A+. The GAN-to-KL derivation holds under an optimal discriminator and overlapping support; the paper recognises the pathologies (non-overlap, instability) and motivates instance noise as a remedy.
- Scope and assumptions: The approach assumes exact knowledge of A and a noiseless observation model, which is restrictive for real SR where blur/noise are often unknown. The paper compares to “soft constraints,” but does not explore robustness to A mismatch or observation noise, making real-world applicability unclear.
- Empirical evidence: 
  - The strongest quantitative validation of minimising H[qθ, pY] is on the 2D toy. For natural images, the paper largely relies on qualitative comparisons and PSNR/SSIM, which are known to penalise perceptual methods. There is no human evaluation or perceptual metric (e.g., LPIPS), and no comparisons to state-of-the-art perceptual SR (e.g., SRGAN/Relativistic GAN variants) on standard benchmarks.
  - AffDG and AffLL underperform (blur), with limited analysis beyond training difficulties. This weakens the multi-pronged “amortised MAP” narrative in high dimensions.
  - Instance noise is a promising idea, but there is no systematic ablation (e.g., with/without noise, comparison to label smoothing, WGAN/gradient penalties), nor quantitative stability metrics or convergence analyses.
- Clarity: The paper is generally clear and well-structured, with helpful appendices. Some notation and claims (e.g., “no paired LR-HR needed”) are conceptually true but in practice training still uses A to generate x from y, effectively providing pairs.
- Novelty: The affine projection/data-consistency layer for SR is a solid architectural contribution; the amortised MAP perspective is neat. However, GAN-based perceptual SR is established, the specific generator update targeting KL is related to f-GAN, and “instance noise” is close to contemporaneous ideas. The empirical section does not convincingly demonstrate unique advantages over existing perceptual SR methods.

Additional feedback to improve the paper (non-decisive suggestions)
- Strengthen empirical evaluation:
  - Compare against strong perceptual SR baselines (e.g., SRGAN/SRResNet) on standard datasets (Set5/14, BSD100, Urban100, DIV2K) with perceptual metrics (LPIPS, NIQE, PI) and user studies.
  - Provide ablations for affine projection vs soft constraint in GAN training (convergence speed, stability, data consistency error, perceptual quality).
  - Systematically evaluate instance noise (noise schedule sensitivity, training stability, FID/KID, mode coverage), and compare to alternatives (label smoothing, WGAN(-GP), spectral norm).
- Robustness and realism:
  - Evaluate under observation noise and blur mismatch; include a probabilistic observation model (e.g., Gaussian noise) and discuss how to relax exact constraints (projected proximal steps, learned A).
  - Study sensitivity to errors in A+ and discuss computational cost/memory of the projection for large images.
- Denoiser/density routes:
  - Revisit AffDG with modern score-based estimators or sliced/denoising score matching, and consider multiscale noise schedules; provide diagnostics on score accuracy.
  - For AffLL, quantify the gap between the density model’s likelihood and true data; try stronger continuous likelihoods or flow models to avoid the “peaked manifold” issue.
- Theory/claims:
  - Clarify the “no paired data needed” claim and the practical training pipeline (sampling x from pX without y, or generating x via A).
  - Provide tighter conditions or empirical evidence that instance noise leads to a unique near-optimal discriminator and better-behaved gradients.

Questions for the authors
- How sensitive is AffGAN to mismatch in A and to observation noise? Can you report performance and stability when the downsampler at test time deviates from the training A, or when LR inputs are noisy?
- What quantitative impact does the affine projection have on GAN training stability and final perceptual quality compared to soft constraints? Can you provide controlled ablations and metrics beyond PSNR/SSIM?
- How does instance noise compare empirically to WGAN-GP or spectral normalization on the same SR task (stability, quality, diversity)?
- Can AffDG be improved using modern score models (e.g., noise-conditioned score networks) and do you observe better gradients and visual quality with them?
- Do you obtain meaningful posterior samples with a stochastic AffGAN (Appendix F)? If so, how do they compare perceptually to MAP outputs?
- How is A+ computed for larger, realistic A (e.g., non-separable blurs), and can you provide guarantees or bounds on Afθ(x)−x under finite kernels?
