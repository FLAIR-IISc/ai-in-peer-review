Summary and contributions
The paper proposes amortized MAP inference for single-image super-resolution (SR). The key ideas are:
- Enforcing exact consistency with the downsampling operator via an affine projection layer gθ(x) = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x. This guarantees Aŷ = x and removes the need for a soft consistency loss.
- Reframing amortized MAP as minimizing the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the model’s output distribution at test-time and the HR data distribution. This casts SR training as matching the marginal image prior subject to the affine constraint.
- Three training strategies to approximate cross-entropy minimization: (i) AffGAN, a GAN variant with a modified generator update that targets KL(q||p) and the introduction of “instance noise” to stabilize training; (ii) AffDG, using a denoiser to estimate ∇y log pY(y) and backpropagate to the SR network; and (iii) AffLL, using a tractable density model (PixelCNN+MCGSM) to provide log-density gradients.
- Empirical demonstrations on a 2D Swiss-roll toy problem, textures, CelebA faces, and ImageNet. The affine projection is shown not to hurt MSE-based training and to ensure exact measurement consistency. AffGAN produces sharper, perceptually more plausible results than MSE/MAE and soft constraints. The paper also discusses limitations of MAP and outlines a stochastic extension connecting AffGAN to amortized variational inference.

Decision: Reject
Key reasons:
- Insufficient empirical validation and comparisons for SR, especially against strong perceptual baselines (e.g., SRGAN and feature/perceptual losses) and without human studies or modern perceptual metrics.
- Only one of the three proposed approaches (AffGAN) works well on natural images; the other two largely fail to deliver, limiting the support for the broader “amortized MAP” program.

Supporting arguments
- Problem and motivation: The paper identifies a real issue—pixel MSE training yields blurry outputs in underdetermined SR—and provides a principled motivation for preferring high-prior solutions (MAP). The affine projection that enforces Aŷ = x is elegant, practical, and broadly useful. Positioning SR training as marginal prior matching is an interesting perspective and relates to implicit modeling and score matching.
- Literature placement: The links to GANs, f-divergences, denoising/score estimation, and autoregressive density models are appropriate. The use of a hard affine constraint contrasts with widespread soft penalties in SR. The “instance noise” idea is reasonable and aligns with concurrent work on GAN stability. However, the empirical comparison to concurrent SR methods leveraging GANs and deep feature content losses is limited; the paper does not quantitatively benchmark against SRGAN or perceptual/feature-loss approaches.
- Support for claims:
  - Theory: The derivations connecting the affine constraint to cross-entropy minimization and to AffGAN’s KL objective are clear at a high level but rely on standard idealizations (optimal discriminator, overlapping supports). The instance noise rationale is plausible, but analysis remains qualitative.
  - Empirics: The Swiss-roll experiments are instructive and show the intended MAP behavior. On images, evidence that the affine projection does not reduce MSE performance is helpful. For perceptual quality, results are primarily qualitative; PSNR/SSIM are reported where MSE is expected to win, but no perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS, VIQ/IQMs) or user studies are provided. AffDG and AffLL either blur or diverge; the paper does not convincingly demonstrate amortized MAP beyond AffGAN. No ablations quantify the contribution of instance noise or of the exact A+ vs trainable transpose conv. Failure cases (e.g., snake→water) point to mode-seeking hallucination without clear guidance on control.

Additional feedback and suggestions (to help improve the work)
- Strengthen empirical evaluation:
  - Include comparisons with strong baselines: SRGAN (Ledig et al.) and feature/perceptual losses (e.g., VGG-based content metrics), modern diffusion/score-based SR if possible, and recent realistic degradations.
  - Report perceptual metrics (LPIPS, DISTS, NIQE/PI, FID/KID) and, ideally, a small-scale human study to substantiate perceptual gains.
  - Provide ablations on instance noise (schedule, magnitude) and discriminator/generator update ratios to quantify stability and quality trade-offs.
  - Evaluate robustness to mismatch in A (e.g., train with Gaussian+stride, test on bicubic/downsample variants) and report how the affine projection behaves in realistic degradations or blind SR.
- Broaden the scope of degradations: The Gaussian+strided downsampling is convenient but narrow. Demonstrate with bicubic, camera ISP-like kernels, and anti-aliasing variants; discuss boundary conditions and A+ estimation with finite kernels.
- Clarify diversity and multimodality: A deterministic amortized MAP is inherently mode-seeking. Show the stochastic extension (Appendix F) in practice: can you sample diverse high-prior reconstructions consistent with x? How does diversity vs fidelity trade off?
- Improve AffDG/AffLL:
  - For AffDG, relate to modern score-based models; try multiscale noise levels and preconditioning, and analyze why gradients mislead the generator at small σ. Consider guiding with a pretrained score network (NCSN/EDM-style).
  - For AffLL, use a stronger continuous likelihood model or a flow-based prior to avoid the mismatch in differentiability and excessive peaking; provide diagnostics for gradient quality and conditioning.
- Theory clarifications: Tighten the conditions under which the KL-minimizing generator update holds with instance noise; discuss the effect of H[qθ] (entropy term) on “approximate MAP” vs diversity and how affine constraints interact with change of variables (coordinate dependence critique).

Questions for the authors
1. What is the exact form, size, and boundary handling of A and A+? How sensitive are results to kernel size and to errors in A+? Did you try bicubic A at test-time?
2. How is the instance noise variance scheduled/selected in practice? Can you provide quantitative evidence that it reduces mode collapse and improves stability (e.g., training loss landscapes, discriminator calibration)?
3. For CelebA/ImageNet, can you report LPIPS/FID or a user study to substantiate perceptual improvements over MSE/MAE and soft constraints?
4. How does the method perform under mismatch between assumed A and the actual degradation (e.g., camera demosaicing, compression)? Can the affine projection be relaxed or adapted for blind SR?
5. Can you demonstrate the stochastic AffGAN variant (Appendix F) with multiple diverse outputs per x and analyze diversity vs consistency?
6. For AffDG/AffLL, did you attempt stronger priors (flows, diffusion models) or preconditioned score estimation? What prevented them from working on natural images beyond textures?
7. How do you control hallucinations (e.g., snake→water) at test-time? Are there mechanisms to bias toward content preservation while retaining high-prior details?
