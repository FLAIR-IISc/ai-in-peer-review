Summary and contributions
The paper proposes “amortised MAP” for single-image super-resolution, reframing SR away from empirical risk minimization towards directly maximizing posterior probability subject to the observation constraint. The key ideas are:
- Affine projection layer: a simple, principled architectural constraint ŷ = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x that enforces Afθ(x) = x exactly, for any fθ. This turns the likelihood into a hard constraint and reduces training to maximizing the image prior on the affine subspace. It also removes the need for paired LR-HR supervision (given a known A and HR samples).
- Objective reformulation: amortised MAP reduces to minimizing cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the model’s output distribution and the HR data distribution.
- Three training strategies to approximate H[qθ, pY]: (i) AffGAN, a GAN variant argued to minimize KL(qθ‖pY) with a new “instance noise” stabilization; (ii) denoiser-guided gradients (AffDG) using score estimates from a DAE; (iii) density-guided (AffLL) using a differentiable PixelCNN/MCGSM log-likelihood.
- Theory: derivations connecting the affine projection to the MAP objective; a training rule leading to KL(q‖p) for the GAN variant; and an appendix showing a stochastic AffGAN minimizes an amortized variational objective Ex KL(q(y|x)‖p(y|x)).
- Empirics: 2D Swiss-roll where AffGAN/AffDG approach brute-force MAP cross-entropy; proof-of-concept that the affine projection does not hurt MSE training and improves consistency/stability; qualitative SR results on textures, CelebA, and ImageNet where AffGAN produces sharper, plausible images; soft constraints are inferior in input-output consistency.

Decision: Accept
Key reasons:
- Conceptual novelty and bold reframing: the affine projection + amortized MAP perspective cleanly unifies SR, prior matching, and exact data consistency, enabling unpaired training. This is a fresh direction relative to standard MSE/GAN losses.
- Practical impact and plausible evidence: the AffGAN variant yields high-quality, perceptually convincing results with exact consistency; instance noise is a simple, broadly useful stabilization trick.

Supporting arguments
- Soundness of the setup: For linear downsampling A, imposing Afθ(x) = x via a learnable A+ is elegant and computationally simple. The reduction to minimizing Ey log pY(ŷ) under this constraint convincingly motivates learning to map each x to a high-prior solution on its affine slice (i.e., amortized MAP).
- Theoretical grounding for GAN-based SR: The modified generator update tied to KL(q‖p) (building on Huszár) gives a clearer target than the usual JS divergence narrative, and the derivation is carefully outlined. The instance noise argument addresses the manifold-support mismatch and discriminator non-uniqueness with a principled divergence dσ.
- Empirical signals: On Swiss-roll, AffGAN/AffDG closely approach the MAP cross-entropy, unlike MSE/MAE. The affine projection improves training speed and exact consistency without degrading MSE/SSIM. Qualitative SR examples (textures, faces, natural images) are sharp and visually plausible, consistent with the stated goal of improving perceptual realism rather than PSNR.
- Broader connections: The amortized VI view of stochastic AffGAN is insightful and may stimulate follow-up work that bridges adversarial and variational inference in inverse problems.

Caveats (not blocking)
- Experimental rigor is limited: no human studies or perceptual metrics (LPIPS), few strong baselines (e.g., SRGAN/ESRGAN/EDSR), and AffDG/AffLL underperform in practice. Still, the paper is transparent about these limitations and focuses on the most promising AffGAN.
- Some claims and presentation need tightening: the generator loss formula for KL minimization needs clearer exposition; the “first connection” to variational inference should be contextualized w.r.t. ALI/BiGAN; references are messy and some are incomplete.
- Assumptions on A: Numerical A+ for Gaussian-stride downsampling is fine, but sensitivity to kernel mismatch/unknown A is not studied.

Feedback to improve the paper (helpful suggestions)
- Strengthen empirical evaluation:
  - Add perceptual metrics (LPIPS, FID/KID on crops) and user study on a subset.
  - Compare to strong supervised SR baselines (e.g., SRGAN/ESRGAN/EDSR) and a GAN with soft LR penalties under the same training data.
  - Provide ablations on instance noise (schedule, magnitude) and on the necessity of exact projection vs soft LR loss.
- Clarify and tighten theory:
  - Present the exact generator loss used to target KL(q‖p) in the main text and reconcile with Appendix A, including assumptions (optimal D, noise settings).
  - Calibrate the “VI connection” claim by discussing ALI/BiGAN and how your amortized posterior over y|x differs.
- Robustness and practicality:
  - Analyze sensitivity to A misspecification and to real-world degradations (bicubic, blur+noise, unknown kernels). Consider a learned A or a marginalization over A.
  - Discuss computational overhead of the projection and A+ estimation; release code for A+ estimation.
  - Explore the stochastic AffGAN empirically to enable posterior sampling and quantify diversity vs consistency.
- Presentation:
  - Clean up equations (some typos in the generator loss), references, and figures; add more quantitative tables; include failure cases (e.g., “snake→water”) and discuss why MAP may prefer high-prior but semantically wrong reconstructions.

Questions for the authors
- Generator loss: Can you provide the exact generator objective you use to target KL(q‖p) and a concise proof sketch under realistic (non-optimal D) training? How critical is that specific loss vs the standard non-saturating GAN loss?
- Instance noise: How sensitive are results to the noise schedule and distribution? How does instance noise compare empirically to gradient penalties (e.g., WGAN-GP) in your setting?
- Affine projection: How robust is the method to A mismatch (e.g., bicubic downsampling, unknown blur, sensor noise)? Can A or A+ be learned jointly?
- Amortized MAP vs per-sample MAP: Do you observe cases where the amortized solution ignores minority modes for certain x? Would a stochastic generator alleviate this by approximating the full posterior p(y|x)?
- Data requirements: Do you truly train without paired LR-HR? In practice, are LR inputs x always produced by applying A to HR samples during training?
- Metrics: Can you report LPIPS/FID and a small user study to substantiate perceptual gains?
