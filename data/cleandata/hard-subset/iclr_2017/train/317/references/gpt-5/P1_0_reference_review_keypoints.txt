- Paper proposes amortized MAP SR enforcing exact LR-consistency via an affine projection using A+ to map network outputs to valid HR solutions.
- Reformulates constrained MAP as minimizing cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between model output distribution and HR data distribution.
- Introduces three training strategies: AffGAN (affine-projected GAN with instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-guided using DAE scores), and AffLL (density-guided using differentiable PixelCNN/MCGSM).
- Empirically evaluated on a toy Swiss-roll (where MAP is tractable) and images (grass textures, CelebA, ImageNet).
- AffGAN yields sharp, plausible images while satisfying exact LR-consistency; AffDG and AffLL are blurrier/unstable.
- Affine projection can improve optimization even when training with MSE.
- Decision: Reject — contributions are promising conceptually but lack sufficient empirical validation and comparisons.
- Main reason 1: Empirical evidence is limited and lacks comparisons to strong, contemporary SR baselines (e.g., SRGAN, perceptual-loss methods) and perceptual evaluations.
- Main reason 2: Only the GAN-based variant works well, weakening the claim that amortized MAP broadly improves SR.
- Main reason 3: Assumes a known, noise-free forward operator A and enforces it exactly, a strong and untested assumption for practical SR.
- Motivation and placement: Critique of MSE/PSNR is well-motivated; affine projection is a clean architectural idea linking outputs to the HR prior.
- Theoretical correctness: Decomposition to constrained MAP and projection construction are sound (given accurate A+); GAN update targeting KL[q||p] and DAE connections are theoretically justified.
- Toy example: Demonstrates AffGAN/AffDG approximate posterior modes and show exact LR-consistency for affine models.
- Image experiments: Visuals suggest better perceptual details for AffGAN, but evaluations are qualitative with PSNR/SSIM favoring MSE and no perceptual metrics or user studies.
- Missing comparisons: No head-to-head with SRGAN or perceptual-content losses on same datasets; limited evidence comparing affine projection to soft LR penalties beyond consistency.
- AffDG/AffLL issues: Negative results and instability acknowledged but without a convincing resolution, undermining two of three proposed routes.
- Assumptions and scope: Exact Af=y consistency ignores noise/kernel mismatch; robustness to A inaccuracies or noise is not evaluated and may hurt real-world performance.
- Suggested empirical improvements: Compare to SRGAN/perceptual losses, add perceptual metrics/user studies, include ablations (projection, soft penalties, instance noise), and test robustness to noise/kernel mismatch.
- Suggested theoretical/clarity improvements: State KL-minimization assumptions, explain instance noise role, empirically demonstrate stochastic AffGAN/VI connection, fix typos and clarify A+ estimation.
- Application trade-offs: Discuss when strict LR-consistency is preferable versus perceptual fidelity and potential harms of hallucinations in sensitive domains.
- Key open questions for authors: quantitative/qualitative comparison to SRGAN, sensitivity to A inaccuracies/noise and accommodation of probabilistic likelihoods, perceptual metrics/human MOS, instance noise hyperparameters and annealing, remedies for AffDG/AffLL instability, and diversity vs. fidelity of stochastic AffGAN.
