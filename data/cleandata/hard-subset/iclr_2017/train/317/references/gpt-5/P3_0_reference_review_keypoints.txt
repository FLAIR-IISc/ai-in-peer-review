- Paper proposes amortised MAP SR under known noiseless degradation x = Ay using an affine projection layer with A+ to guarantee Afθ(x)=x and learns by minimising cross-entropy between reconstructions and an HR prior.
- Methods: AffGAN (GAN targeting KL[q||p] with instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-guided using DAE score), AffLL (density-guided using PixelCNN-like prior); theoretical link between stochastic AffGAN and amortised variational inference.
- Decision: Reject.
- Main reason 1: Empirical validation is limited; only AffGAN works well beyond the toy task, AffDG and AffLL underperform.
- Main reason 2: Unrealistic, noiseless-known-A setup with synthetic LR; unpaired training and practical utility under realistic degradations/noise not demonstrated or compared to strong SR baselines.
- Strength: Affine projection layer is a principled architectural contribution that enforces LR consistency and the practical numerical A+ approximation is useful.
- Strength: Clear derivation reducing amortised MAP (delta likelihood) to minimising H[qθ,pY], motivating HR-only unpaired learning.
- Strength: GAN analysis (KL-targeted update, instance noise) and discussion of support mismatch/discriminator instability are insightful.
- Strength: Novel conceptual link between stochastic AffGAN and amortised variational inference.
- Weakness: All theory and experiments assume p(x|y)=δ(x−Ay); no robustness tests to additive noise or mismatched A, risking poor performance under misspecification.
- Weakness: The unpaired-training claim is not substantiated since LR were generated from HR with the same A; no experiments with independent LR/HR domains.
- Weakness: Limited empirical rigor—no quantitative H[q,p] evaluation on images, perceptual quality judged mostly visually, missing NR-IQA/user studies and comparisons to strong modern perceptual baselines.
- Weakness: AffDG and AffLL produce blurry outputs and are unstable, weakening the claim of three viable amortised-MAP approaches.
- Weakness: Instance-noise idea lacks thorough ablation vs contemporaneous stabilisation methods (noise schedules, discriminator steps, alternatives).
- Weakness: Presentation issues—references/formatting glitches and important experimental details are buried in the appendix.
- Suggested experiments: Test robustness to noise and A mismatch, compare hard projection vs soft penalties, and evaluate faithfulness beyond LR consistency.
- Suggested experiments: Demonstrate truly unpaired training with disjoint LR/HR datasets and compare paired vs unpaired performance.
- Suggested evaluations: Add NR-IQA metrics, user studies, and perception–distortion tradeoff plots; compare to SRGAN/ESRGAN and back-projection baselines.
- Suggested ablations: A+ initialisation, fixed vs learnable A+, projection vs no projection, and instance noise schedules vs other stabilisers.
- Suggested improvements: For AffDG use modern score-matching/annealed DAEs; for AffLL test continuous priors and temperature scaling to avoid overconcentration.
- Questions for authors (summary): How does projection behave under noise or misspecified A; can you demonstrate truly unpaired training; what are NR-IQA/user-study results and hallucination frequency?
- Questions for authors (continued): How do noise annealing/DAE architecture affect AffDG stability; how sensitive is AffGAN to instance-noise schedules and alternative stabilisers?
- Questions for authors (continued): Can A+ be learned end-to-end with regularisation for AA+≈I and does that help when A is approximate; what receptive field size is required for accurate A+ and do truncation effects cause artifacts?
