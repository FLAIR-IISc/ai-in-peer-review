1. Summary and contributions
This paper reframes single-image super-resolution (SR) as amortised MAP inference under a known linear downsampling operator A, and proposes an elegant architectural and algorithmic pipeline to implement it. The key ingredients are:
- Affine projection layer: a principled projection ŷ = Π_Ax fθ(x) = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x ensuring exact data-consistency Aŷ = x. This turns the amortised MAP objective into minimising the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the distribution of network outputs and the HR data prior, removing the need for paired LR–HR training data.
- Three training strategies to approximate cross-entropy minimisation:
  1) AffGAN: a GAN variant where the generator is the affine-projected SR network and an alternative generator loss approximately minimises KL[qθ || pY]; includes “instance noise” to stabilise training when supports do not overlap.
  2) AffDG: denoiser-guided training using a DAE to estimate score ∇y log pY(y) and backpropagate it through the projection.
  3) AffLL: density-guided training with a differentiable PixelCNN-like continuous likelihood (MCGSM).
- Theory: derivation that affine projection reduces the amortised MAP objective to cross-entropy minimisation; analysis of instance noise as minimising KL of noise-convolved distributions; and an appendix connecting a stochastic AffGAN to amortised variational inference (minimising Ex KL(qY|X || pY|X)).
- Empirics: On a 2D Swiss roll toy problem, AffGAN/AffDG attain cross-entropy close to brute-force MAP; on images, affine projection improves even MSE-trained baselines in training speed and consistency; for textures, faces (CelebA), and ImageNet, AffGAN yields perceptually sharp, plausible outputs with exact LR consistency, whereas AffDG/AffLL tend to be blurry/unstable.

2. Decision: Accept
Primary reasons:
- Novel, principled formulation of SR as amortised MAP with an explicit, differentiable, exact data-consistency projection that enables training without paired LR–HR data.
- A practical algorithm (AffGAN + instance noise) that delivers clear perceptual improvements and comes with useful theoretical grounding (KL objective, VI connection).

3. Supporting arguments
Strengths
- Conceptual clarity and novelty: Casting SR training as H[qθ, pY] minimisation via an affine projection is a compelling and broadly applicable idea for linear inverse problems. It cleanly decouples the observation model from the prior and aligns with how one would do MAP under a known A.
- Architectural contribution: The A+/projection layer is simple, general, and enforces exact consistency—something often only approximated with soft penalties. The numerical estimation of A+ for strided conv downsampling is pragmatic and well explained.
- Theoretical insights: The generator loss leading to KL minimisation, instance noise rationale, and the amortised VI view of stochastic AffGAN are timely and informative.
- Empirical signals: 
  - Toy example validates the objective directly via cross-entropy estimates.
  - Affine projection helps even MSE training (faster, better SSIM/MSE, zero consistency error).
  - On textures/faces/ImageNet, AffGAN produces sharp, plausible outputs often superior to soft-constraint GANs; exact consistency is a strong positive.

Limitations and risks
- Evaluation is primarily qualitative; PSNR/SSIM are expectedly worse for GANs and no human studies or learned perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS) are reported. Claims about perceptual quality would be stronger with such measures or user studies.
- Among the three routes, only AffGAN consistently works well on images; AffDG and AffLL are either unstable or produce blur. The paper is honest about this, but it narrows practical impact to AffGAN at present.
- The assumption of exact known A and zero observation noise may limit robustness in real pipelines (unknown/downstream kernel mismatch, sensor noise). The snake-to-water failure illustrates a prior-dominant hallucination risk when conditioning is weak.
- Some theory (e.g., KL minimisation with GAN) rests on idealised discriminators; in practice, results hinge on the instance noise schedule and other stabilisation details.

Overall, despite imperfect validations and some negative results for the DAE/density paths, the core idea is fresh and promising, with a working instantiation that improves perceptual SR and a clean data-consistency mechanism. This aligns with valuing bold new directions.

4. Feedback to improve the paper (helpful, not necessarily decision-critical)
- Perceptual evaluation: Add LPIPS/MS-SSIM and, if feasible, a small-scale MOS study. Include a calibration showing that improved perceptual metrics coincide with qualitative gains.
- Comparisons: Report against SRGAN/perceptual-loss baselines matched for backbone and with hard data-consistency (e.g., your projection layer) to isolate the contribution of the prior/objective.
- Robustness: Study kernel/noise mismatch (train/test A mismatch, additive observation noise) and possibly extend the projection to a probabilistic consistency (e.g., projection onto Ay ≈ x with σx).
- Ablations: Sensitivity to the accuracy/size of A+; instance noise schedule (variance, annealing) and discriminator update ratio; entropy H[qθ] and diversity across x.
- Stochastic AffGAN: Include preliminary results where z induces diverse outputs conditioned on x (MAP vs sampling) and report how diversity impacts perceptual plausibility vs faithfulness.
- Release code and pre-trained A+ kernels; this will facilitate adoption for other inverse problems.

Questions for the authors
- How sensitive is performance to inaccuracies in A+ and to train–test kernel mismatch? Can you quantify degradation and any mitigation (e.g., learning A jointly)?
- Can your framework accommodate explicit observation noise models by relaxing the hard constraint to a probabilistic one? How would the projection change?
- Did you try feature-space (e.g., VGG) priors for MAP to address representation-dependence of modes, and how did that compare?
- For AffGAN with noise input z, what is the conditional entropy H[qθ(y|x)] in practice? Do you observe meaningful multi-modality conditioned on the same x?
- What is the computational overhead of the projection vs unconstrained SR? Any runtime/memory trade-offs?
- For AffDG, have you tried modern score networks or annealed noise schedules closer to score matching/diffusion training to improve stability/precision?

I recommend acceptance for its conceptual novelty, principled data-consistency mechanism, and a practically useful AffGAN instantiation with thoughtful theoretical insights.
