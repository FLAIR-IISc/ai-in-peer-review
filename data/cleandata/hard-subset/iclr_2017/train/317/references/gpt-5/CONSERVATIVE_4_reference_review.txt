Summary and contributions
- The paper proposes amortized MAP inference for single-image SR. The key idea is to enforce exact consistency with the LR input via an affine projection layer gθ(x) = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x, where A models downsampling and A+ is its pseudoinverse. This converts training into matching the marginal distribution of outputs to the HR data prior pY, equivalently minimizing H[qθ, pY].
- Three training strategies are offered: (i) AffGAN, a GAN variant intended to minimize KL[q‖p] using a modified generator loss and “instance noise” to stabilize training; (ii) denoiser-guided (AffDG), using a DAE to estimate ∇y log pY; and (iii) density-guided (AffLL), using a differentiable PixelCNN+MCGSM likelihood as a surrogate prior.
- Empirical evidence is provided on a 2D Swiss-roll toy problem and 4× SR for textures, CelebA, and ImageNet; AffGAN appears most visually appealing. The paper also claims a conceptual link between a stochastic AffGAN and amortized variational inference.

Decision: Reject
Key reasons:
1) Empirical validation is insufficient and not rigorous against established SR baselines; evaluation relies largely on qualitative images, with limited and misaligned metrics.
2) The approach assumes a known, fixed degradation operator A and demonstrates results only under synthetic A; this substantially weakens claims about practical SR and amortized MAP.

Supporting arguments
- Baselines and metrics: For faces, PSNR/SSIM are reported only for MSE vs GAN variants; for textures and ImageNet, assessment is qualitative. There are no comparisons with strong SR baselines using supervised losses (e.g., contemporary high-PSNR models) nor with perceptual SR methods beyond a soft L1/L2 constraint. No human studies, LPIPS, FID/KID, or task-based evaluations are provided to support the “perceptual” claims.
- Synthetic degradation: All LR inputs are produced using the same A used by the model, and A+ is numerically tuned for that A. Real-world SR rarely has a known A (and often differs from Gaussian+decimation). The method’s central premise—amortized MAP without paired data—hinges on pX being induced by the same A from pY. The paper does not evaluate robustness to A mismatch or unknown degradations, which is critical for applicability.
- Theoretical claims: The derivation that the modified GAN update minimizes KL[q‖p] depends on strong assumptions (optimal, unique discriminator, overlapping supports). The paper itself notes instability and introduces “instance noise,” but provides no formal convergence guarantees. The equivalence to amortized VI in the appendix is suggestive but not operationally exploited (no stochastic posterior sampling experiments).
- Alternative routes underperform: AffDG and AffLL yield blurry results and training instabilities. This weakens the broader claim that amortized MAP via principled priors is a practical training route; success hinges on AffGAN, which is closer in spirit to established perceptual SR via adversarial training.
- Affine projection: Enforcing exact consistency with LR via ΠAx is a sensible architectural constraint and a clear contribution. However, its novelty and practical benefit are not convincingly isolated: ablations on projection quality (effect of approximate A+), generality across different A (e.g., bicubic), and comparisons to modern upsampling modules are limited. The MSE “proof-of-concept” shows faster convergence and exact consistency, but the gain over strong unconstrained architectures remains unclear.

Additional feedback (to improve the paper; not all affect the accept/reject)
- Evaluation:
  - Include rigorous comparisons to strong SR baselines at the time (and perceptual SR such as SRGAN-style methods) using both distortion (PSNR/SSIM) and perceptual metrics (LPIPS, FID/KID), plus a small-scale user study.
  - Report LR-HR consistency error across methods; for GANs, quantify hallucination vs faithfulness (calibrated on edge maps or task accuracy).
  - Provide ablations on instance noise level/schedule, discriminator updates per generator step, and the effect of A+ accuracy.
- Robustness:
  - Test under unknown/mismatched degradation (e.g., bicubic, mild noise, kernel shifts) and with learned A/A+ to reflect practical SR.
  - Evaluate generalization when pX is not generated by your assumed A.
- Methodology:
  - Clarify the generator loss in Eqn. (10) (there seem to be typos/inconsistencies with the ratio form used later in the KL argument).
  - For AffDG/AffLL, detail hyperparameter search, stabilization tricks, and why they failed beyond qualitative impressions; consider multi-scale priors, noise-level schedules, and preconditioning of gradients to mitigate peaked densities.
  - Discuss computational overhead and throughput of the affine projection vs standard SR pipelines.
- Scope/claims:
  - Temper claims that the method “does not need paired LR-HR data”—it still requires that LR be sampled from A applied to pY. Discuss how to proceed when this assumption fails.
  - Address the MAP mode-seeking limitation more thoroughly; consider posterior sampling variants and uncertainty calibration.

Questions for the authors
1) How sensitive is performance to mismatch between the true degradation and the modeled A? Can you show results under bicubic downsampling, mild noise, or kernel perturbations?
2) How accurately must A+ be estimated? What is the impact of truncating its support and of errors in A+ on both consistency and perceptual quality?
3) Can you provide quantitative perceptual metrics (LPIPS, FID/KID) and a user study comparing AffGAN to a strong perceptual SR baseline?
4) What is the exact generator loss used in practice (please correct Eqn. 10 if needed), and how critical is the instance noise schedule? Any ablations?
5) For AffDG and AffLL, were multi-scale priors or alternative denoising likelihoods tried? Could you report quantitative progression beyond the training curves, and do you believe the failure is due to model capacity, optimization, or the surrogate prior?
6) In the “amortized VI” view, can you demonstrate stochastic outputs conditioned on x (e.g., sampling diverse plausible HR reconstructions) and evaluate their calibration/coverage?

Overall, the affine projection idea is neat and the formulation linking SR with marginal distribution matching is interesting, but the current empirical evidence and positioning against established SR practice are not yet sufficient for acceptance.
