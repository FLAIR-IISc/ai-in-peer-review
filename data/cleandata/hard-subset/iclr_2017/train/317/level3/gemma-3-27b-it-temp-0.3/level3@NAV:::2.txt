## Review of "Amortised MAP Inference for Image Super-Resolution"

This paper presents a compelling reframing of image super-resolution (SR) as an amortised Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) estimation problem. The authors introduce a novel network architecture incorporating an affine projection to enforce consistency between LR and HR spaces, and explore three methods – GANs, denoiser-guided SR, and a density-model approach – to solve the resulting cross-entropy minimization. The work is well-motivated, clearly written, and demonstrates promising results, particularly with the GAN-based approach.

**Decision:** Accept. The paper presents a novel and well-supported approach to SR, offering a theoretically grounded and empirically validated alternative to traditional methods. The introduction of the affine projection is a significant contribution, and the thorough exploration of different training strategies strengthens the overall impact of the work.

**Supporting Arguments:**

The paper successfully addresses a key limitation of existing SR techniques – the tendency to produce blurry results due to reliance on pixel-wise loss functions. By framing SR as MAP inference, the authors directly address the issue of plausibility and prior consistency. The affine projection is a clever mechanism to ensure valid HR solutions, and the experimental results convincingly demonstrate its effectiveness. The comparison of GAN, denoiser-guided, and density-model approaches provides valuable insights into the practical challenges and benefits of each method, with the GAN-based approach consistently delivering the most visually appealing results. The experiments are well-designed, including both toy examples for theoretical validation and real-world image datasets for practical evaluation.

**Additional Feedback:**

While the paper is generally excellent, a final proofread to address minor typos would be beneficial. The connection to variational inference established in the appendix is intriguing but could be further elaborated upon in the main body of the paper to highlight its potential implications. It would also be helpful to discuss the computational cost associated with the affine projection, particularly for high-resolution images. 

**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  The paper mentions potential limitations of MAP inference regarding representation dependence. Could you elaborate on how the affine projection mitigates this issue in the context of SR, and whether this mitigation is empirically validated?
2.  The instance noise technique appears crucial for GAN training stability. Could you provide more details on the annealing schedule used for the noise level and the sensitivity of the results to this schedule?
3.  The density-guided approach (AffLL) showed less promising results. Do you believe that improvements in the density model itself (e.g., using a more expressive architecture) could significantly improve the performance of this method?
