- Proposes a novel distributed stochastic optimization approach that combines synchronous and asynchronous benefits.  
- Introduces a synchronous algorithm with backup workers to reduce straggler effects while avoiding gradient staleness.  
- Provides a thorough analysis of trade-offs between synchronous and asynchronous methods.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness via extensive experiments on models including Inception and PixelCNN.  
- Decision: Accept the paper.  
- Key reason 1: Novelty and significance—backup workers are an innovative solution to stragglers with potential system performance gains.  
- Key reason 2: Experimental rigor—comprehensive evaluations and detailed result analysis across multiple models.  
- Supporting argument: Clear motivation, thorough literature review, and detailed algorithmic description.  
- Supporting argument: Experimental results are well-presented and show superiority over existing methods.  
- Suggested improvements: Provide more implementation details on backup worker selection and gradient aggregation.  
- Suggested improvements: Add experiments on robustness to different straggler types and network conditions and discuss theoretical implications (convergence, limitations).  
- Questions for authors: How are backup workers selected and their gradients aggregated? How is a backup that becomes a straggler handled? What are scalability and robustness limitations?
