- Proposal: Introduces synchronous stochastic optimization with backup workers to mitigate stragglers while avoiding asynchronous staleness.  
- Empirical results: Validated on ImageNet and CIFAR-10, showing faster convergence and better test accuracy than asynchronous optimization.  
- Decision: Accept — approach is well-motivated, innovative, and supported by thorough empirical evaluations.  
- Strengths: Clear problem introduction and motivation, well-explained method, and convincing empirical evidence of improved speed and accuracy.  
- Suggested improvements: Add analysis of learned networks (e.g., feature visualization, impact of backups) and more implementation details (hardware/software) for reproducibility.  
- Questions for authors: Explain backup-worker selection and gradient aggregation; how severe straggler cases are handled if backups are insufficient; applicability/results with other optimizers (Adam/Adagrad).
