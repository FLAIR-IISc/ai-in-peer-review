- Proposes a novel synchronous distributed stochastic optimization framework using backup workers to mitigate stragglers while avoiding gradient staleness.  
- Empirical results on models like Inception and PixelCNN show better test accuracy and faster convergence than asynchronous methods.  
- Addresses the important problem of distributed stochastic optimization and is well-motivated in the literature.  
- Clear explanation of weaknesses of pure synchronous and asynchronous optimization.  
- Use of backup workers is a novel and interesting idea.  
- Empirical evaluations are rigorous and analyze the trade-off between convergence speed and test accuracy.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Main strengths: novel, well-motivated approach and strong empirical validation.  
- Concern: No detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method.  
- Concern: Lack of guidance on how to choose the number of backup workers, a crucial hyperparameter.  
- Suggestion: Provide computational complexity analysis.  
- Suggestion: Study the effect of varying the number of backup workers and test on additional models/datasets to demonstrate generality.  
- Questions for authors: How is the number of backup workers chosen and how does it affect performance?  
- Questions for authors: Provide detailed complexity comparison with other distributed stochastic optimization algorithms.  
- Questions for authors: Have you applied the method to other models/datasets, and what are the challenges or limitations?
