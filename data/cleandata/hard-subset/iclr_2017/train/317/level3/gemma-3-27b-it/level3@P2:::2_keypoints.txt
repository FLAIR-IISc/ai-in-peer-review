- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Main contribution: Frame single-image super-resolution as amortised MAP estimation using a novel affine projection enforcing LR–HR consistency.
- Motivation: Argues MSE-based training yields blurry results; MAP with a learned prior produces more perceptually plausible images.
- Methods: Explore three optimization strategies for the cross-entropy objective — GANs (AffGAN), denoiser-guided training, and a density-model approach.
- Architectural novelty: The affine projection is a key component that constrains solutions to valid SR outputs and reduces the problem to tractable cross-entropy minimization.
- Experimental validation: Thorough evaluation starting from a 2D toy problem to real datasets (grass, CelebA, ImageNet) showing practical effectiveness.
- Baselines and metrics: Appropriate comparisons to MSE-trained networks and GAN variants, reporting PSNR/SSIM alongside visual results.
- Results: AffGAN consistently yields superior perceptual quality compared to other approaches.
- Theory: Connects AffGAN to amortised variational inference, offering a significant theoretical insight and future research directions.
- Reproducibility: Detailed experimental setup and appendices (including Appendix D and F) aid reproducibility and theoretical understanding.
- Minor issues: Manuscript contains typos/grammatical errors requiring proofreading.
- Clarification needed: Provide a more intuitive explanation of instance noise and its role in stabilizing GAN training in the main text.
- Practical concern: Discuss computational cost, especially overhead introduced by the affine projection.
- Further work: Recommend additional ablation studies (e.g., skip connections, network depth) to isolate component effects.
- Questions for authors: Effects of affine projection on representation dependence; sensitivity to DAE quality in denoiser-guided approach; exploration of non-Gaussian instance noise; practical benefits from the variational-inference connection.
