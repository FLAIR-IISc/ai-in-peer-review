- Proposes amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution using CNNs to avoid blur from MSE-based methods.
- Introduces an affine projection layer to ensure LR-HR consistency in the output.
- Explores three optimization strategies: GAN-based, denoiser-guided, and maximum-likelihood trained prior; GAN performs best on real images.
- Establishes a theoretical connection between GANs and amortized variational inference.
- Decision: Reject — promising theory but insufficient empirical evidence and comparisons to state-of-the-art.
- Main empirical weakness: evaluations limited to simple datasets (Swiss-roll, grass textures) and 128×128 images.
- Critically missing comparisons on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) and against top SR methods (e.g., EDSR, RCAN).
- Possible contradiction: affine projection claimed to remove need for paired data, but training A+ in Appendix B appears to require paired HR–LR examples.
- Perceptual quality claims are based on visual inspection and lack stronger quantitative perceptual metrics beyond PSNR/SSIM.
- Recommend adding evaluations on standard SR benchmarks for fair comparison to existing methods.
- Recommend demonstrating scalability to larger, realistic image sizes beyond 128×128.
- Suggest exploring latent noise vector z in GAN generator to improve diversity and control hallucinated details.
- Recommend thorough ablation studies to quantify contributions of affine projection, GAN techniques, and loss choices.
- Ask authors to clarify data requirements for training the affine projection (A+) and its impact on the unpaired-data claim.
- Recommend including advanced perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS) to better quantify perceptual quality.
- Questions for authors: why standard benchmarks were omitted, does A+ require paired data, was latent z used in GAN, why only PSNR/SSIM were reported, and can the method handle arbitrary scaling factors?
