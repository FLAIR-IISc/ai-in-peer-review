- Reframes image super-resolution as amortised MAP estimation, offering a novel theoretical perspective.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer to enforce consistency between low- and high-resolution representations.  
- Evaluates three optimization approaches (GANs, denoiser-guided training, baseline density model) for the cross-entropy objective.  
- Finds the GAN-based method produces the most visually appealing results.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly presented, and strongly grounded theoretically.  
- Experimental validation is comprehensive, from a 2D toy case to complex natural images.  
- Ablation studies (e.g., affine-projected vs. soft-constrained) are insightful and support the claims.  
- Correctly identifies limitations of MSE-based SR and shows benefits of aligning outputs with an image prior.  
- Appendix establishes a useful connection between AffGAN and amortised variational inference.  
- Minor typos should be corrected.  
- Suggests adding a more intuitive explanation of why instance noise stabilizes GAN training for less expert readers.  
- Recommends expanding the limitations discussion to address potential biases from learned priors and generalization concerns.  
- Advises visualizing the affine projection and constrained solution space for representative LR inputs.  
- Questions for authors: implications of affine projection with severely degraded LR inputs and whether it may discard useful information.  
- Questions for authors: whether other noise distributions (e.g., Laplacian, uniform) were tried and how they compared to Gaussian instance noise.  
- Questions for authors: how the variational-inference perspective could enable future techniques from the VAE literature to improve SR.
