- Proposes novel image super-resolution via amortized MAP inference with outputs constrained to the affine subspace consistent with the low-resolution input.

- Explores three training strategies within this constrained space: GAN-based (AffGAN), denoising-guided (AffDG), and density-guided (AffLL).

- The approach is theoretically well-motivated and addresses MSE-induced blurriness by favoring plausible solutions aligned with image priors.

- Empirically promising results across datasets, with visual improvements in textures (grass) and faces; 2D Swiss-roll shows alignment with MAP solutions.

- Decision: Accept — significant core contribution despite presentation issues.

- Motivations for moving beyond pixel-wise MSE are clearly articulated; MAP inference argued as a principled alternative.

- Affine subspace projection is a clever consistency enforcement that simplifies MAP estimation.

- Comparing three strategies gives a comprehensive evaluation of the framework.

- Theoretical link between AffGAN and amortized variational inference strengthens the conceptual foundation.

- Presentation is dense and needs clearer, more intuitive explanations of mathematical formulations, especially the affine projection-to-cross-entropy connection.

- Experiments should be expanded: visualize learned convolutional filters and analyze how affine constraint shapes features.

- Recommend exploring effects of different noise levels in denoising-guided and density models to find optimal configurations.

- Discussion of MAP limitations is insightful but should be expanded with scenarios where MAP estimates are undesirable or misleading.

- Notation and equation formatting need consistency and clearer variable explanations.

- Q1: Request elaboration on label-smoothing bias vs instance noise and how instance noise avoids that bias, ideally with a visual.

- Q2: Ask why AffDG and AffLL underperform relative to AffGAN — is training stability or noisy gradient estimation the cause?

- Q3: Request high-level overview of the stochastic AffGAN extension that performs amortized variational inference and pointer to exact equations (Appendix F).

- Q4: Ask how often ImageNet yields plausibly incorrect but high-probability outputs (e.g., snake → water) and request quantification of such failures.
