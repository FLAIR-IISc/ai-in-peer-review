- Proposes framing image super-resolution as amortized MAP estimation with a neural network projecting onto an affine subspace consistent with the LR input.  
- Affine projection idea is principled and helps address blurriness common in traditional SR methods.  
- Explores multiple optimization/training strategies (GAN-based, noise-assisted, density-assisted) showing thorough investigation.  
- Empirical results across datasets are well-presented and visually favor the GAN-based implementation.  
- Paper is accepted: approach is innovative, well-motivated, and shows promising results.  
- Strong theoretical grounding and clear connection to MAP inference, moving beyond empirical risk minimization.  
- Swiss-roll toy experiment effectively illustrates the core principle of finding plausible solutions within a subspace.  
- Real-image improvements are visually noticeable though quantitative gains are not always large.  
- Exposition lacks clarity; writing and presentation need substantial polishing for better flow and understanding.  
- Mathematical derivations and implementation details require more detailed explanations.  
- Learned representations are not thoroughly analyzed; paper shows what is achieved but not how.  
- Requests visualization of learned filters in initial convolutional layers to understand learned features.  
- Suggests a more detailed ablation study on the affine projection (varying strength, layer-wise application, or softening).  
- Recommends reporting perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS) in addition to PSNR and SSIM for stronger quantitative evaluation.  
- Asks about computational overhead of the affine projection layer compared to standard convolutions.  
- Questions sensitivity of the denoising-guided approach (AffDG) to training noise level and how optimal noise level was chosen.
