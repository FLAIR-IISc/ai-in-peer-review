- Decision: Accept.  
- Novelty: Frames super-resolution as amortized MAP inference with a novel affine-projection architecture.  
- Affine projection: Enforces LR-HR consistency and addresses the ill-posed SR problem.  
- Optimization strategies: Explores GAN-based, noise-assisted, and density-assisted training within the framework.  
- Empirical performance: Promising results across datasets; AffGAN yields visually plausible reconstructions.  
- Toy example: 2D Swiss-roll shows MAP estimation advantage over MSE.  
- Quantitative vs perceptual tradeoff: Metrics not always better than MSE but align with improved perceptual quality.  
- Ablations: Tests (affine-projected vs soft-constrained) support the proposed design choices.  
- Theoretical insight: Connects AffGAN to amortized variational inference, bridging adversarial methods and Bayesian inference.  
- Clarity/exposition: Paper is dense and needs clearer explanations and intuitive examples.  
- Feature learning: Lacks analysis/visualization of learned features, activations, or filters.  
- GAN training stability: Needs more detailed analysis of instance noise and other stabilization techniques.  
- Comparison to SOTA: Should include stronger comparisons to current SR leaders (e.g., EDSR, RCAN).  
- Failure cases: More investigation and discussion of failure examples (e.g., snake reconstructed as water) are needed.  
- Questions for authors: Request intuition for affine projection benefits, feature visualizations, instance-noise annealing strategy, computational overhead of projection, and scalability to higher resolutions/datasets.
