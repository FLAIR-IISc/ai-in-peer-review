- Reframes image super-resolution as amortised MAP inference to avoid blurriness from pixel-wise MSE averaging.  
- Introduces an affine projection in a CNN to enforce LR–HR consistency and reduce MAP inference to minimizing cross-entropy.  
- Proposes and evaluates three optimisation approaches: AffGAN (GAN), denoiser-guided gradient estimates, and a maximum-likelihood image-prior baseline.  
- Demonstrates AffGAN performs best on real image data and connects AffGAN to amortised variational inference.  
- Decision: Accept — novel, theoretically sound approach with convincing experimental support.  
- Strong motivation: empirical risk minimization struggles when multiple plausible HR solutions exist for a LR input.  
- Theoretical contribution: affine projection and cross-entropy formulation are clearly presented and well-justified.  
- Architectural contribution: affine-projection CNN enforces consistency and is innovative.  
- Experimental rigor: Swiss-roll toy experiments, image datasets (grass, CelebA, ImageNet), ablations (AffGAN vs SoftGAN), and appendix variational link strengthen claims.  
- Request to discuss computational cost/complexity of the affine projection relative to other SR methods.  
- Ask for sensitivity analysis of key hyperparameters (learning rate, denoiser noise, GAN parameters).  
- Suggest visual comparisons with state-of-the-art SR methods (e.g., EDSR, SRGAN) and fixing minor typos.  
- Ask authors to clarify the intuitive role of instance noise in stabilising training beyond Appendix C.  
- Questions for authors: how representation choice affects MAP estimates; experiments with other instance-noise distributions; why AffDG/AffLL were blurrier than AffGAN; limitations when downsampling is non-linear; effect and tuning of the Gaussian kernel size in the affine projection.
