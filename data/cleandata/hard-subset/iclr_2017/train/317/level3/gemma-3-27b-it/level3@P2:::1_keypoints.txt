- Proposes amortized MAP inference for image SR using an affine projection to enforce LR consistency and learn an implicit prior with CNNs+GANs.  
- Introduces three methods: GAN-based, denoiser-guided, and density-model-guided; GAN approach shows the most promise.  
- Decision: Reject — theoretically sound but limited empirical validation and unclear practical impact.  
- Strength: Clear motivation for MAP over MSE and a clever affine-projection constraint simplifying problem formulation.  
- Strength: Insightful theoretical connections to GANs and variational inference.  
- Empirical: Swiss-roll 2D results convincingly show ability to find MAP estimates.  
- Empirical gap: Natural-image evaluation is limited and lacks comparisons to standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100).  
- Missing direct comparison to relevant prior work (e.g., Ledig et al. / SRGAN, ESRGAN).  
- Experiments use small images (≤128×128); scalability to realistic resolutions is unclear.  
- Concern: Computational cost of the affine projection may grow substantially with image size.  
- GAN training instability acknowledged; instance noise introduced but its effectiveness and comparison to other stabilization methods not thoroughly explored.  
- Unclear whether training the projection (A+) requires paired data and whether this simply shifts the pairing requirement elsewhere.  
- Recommendation: Add comprehensive benchmark evaluations with PSNR, SSIM, and perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS).  
- Recommendation: Analyze scalability and computational cost on larger images (e.g., 512×512).  
- Recommendation: Provide detailed ablation studies isolating affine projection, loss types, and instance noise effects.  
- Recommendation: Explore latent-vector inputs for the generator to increase control/diversity.  
- Recommendation: Clarify training procedure for the initial A+ projection and its use of paired data.  
- Recommendation: Expand discussion of MAP limitations, prior choice sensitivity, and dependence on architecture/training hyperparameters.  
- Question: Why were standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) omitted from experiments?  
- Question: How does the computational cost of the affine projection scale with resolution and can it be mitigated?  
- Question: How is the initial A+ projection trained, and does that stage rely on paired data?  
- Question: Were other GAN stabilization techniques (e.g., spectral normalization, gradient penalty) tried and how did they compare to instance noise?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to the choice of image prior in the density-guided approach?
