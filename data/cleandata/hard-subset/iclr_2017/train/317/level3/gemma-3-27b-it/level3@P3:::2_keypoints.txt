- Decision: Accept.  
- Main contribution: Reframe image super-resolution as amortized MAP inference with a novel affine-projection network enforcing LR–HR consistency.  
- Methods explored: Three approaches to cross-entropy minimization — GANs, denoiser guidance, and a density-model (AffGAN, AffDG, AffLL).  
- Empirical result: GAN-based method yields superior perceptual quality and outperforms existing techniques on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Theoretical insight: Affine projection simplifies the problem to cross-entropy minimization and links AffGAN to amortized variational inference.  
- Experiments: Well-designed evaluation including a 2D toy problem and real-world image datasets; quantitative (PSNR, SSIM, cross-entropy) and visual comparisons support claims.  
- Writing quality: Exceptionally well-written and clearly presented; complex theory made accessible.  
- Limitations noted: MAP inference limitations discussed; reviewer suggests expanding discussion on how prior choice affects results.  
- Minor issues: Some typos and awkward phrasings — a careful proofread recommended.  
- Question 1: Request details on the instance-noise annealing schedule, sensitivity, and alternative noise distributions.  
- Question 2: Ask for specifics on AffDG DAE parameter loading schedule and switching criteria between noise levels.  
- Question 3: Request comparison of the reported −4.10 bits/dim density-model log-likelihood to state-of-the-art and whether density modeling limits AffLL performance.  
- Question 4: Ask whether the downsampling kernel A significantly impacts performance and if kernels beyond Gaussian were tested.
