- Introduces amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution to avoid blurring from pixel-wise loss averaging.  
- Enforces LR-HR consistency via projection onto an affine subspace, steering outputs toward high-prior regions.  
- Explores three optimization strategies: GAN-based, denoiser-guided, and maximum-likelihood trained image prior.  
- GAN-based method yields the most promising perceptual results in experiments.  
- Draws a theoretical connection between the proposed GAN approach and amortized variational inference.  
- Experimental validation includes Swiss-roll toy and grass texture datasets, with cross-entropy and consistency-error analyses.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Writing needs clarity: introduction dense and theoretical sections are mathematically rigorous but hard to follow.  
- Recommend breaking down equations and adding more intuitive explanations in theory sections.  
- Experimental presentation could be stronger with larger qualitative comparisons and more visual examples.  
- Feature analysis of affine-projected CNNs is missing; authors should report qualitative/quantitative differences vs. MSE-trained CNNs.  
- Ablation studies are limited; more analysis of affine projection choices, architectures, and hyperparameters is needed.  
- Discussion of MAP limitations should be expanded with alternatives or mitigation strategies.  
- Appendix D experimental settings are very detailed and thorough.  
- Questions for authors: details on learned features, comprehensive ablations, computational cost of method and affine projection, generalization to diverse datasets, and specifics of the stochastic AffGAN–variational inference connection.
