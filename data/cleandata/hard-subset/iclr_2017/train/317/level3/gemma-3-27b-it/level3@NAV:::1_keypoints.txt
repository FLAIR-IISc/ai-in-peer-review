- Proposes amortized MAP inference with GANs for SR, using affine projection to enforce LR–HR consistency — theoretically interesting and well-motivated.  
- Explores multiple optimization methods (GANs, denoising-guided SR, baseline), showing thorough methodological investigation.  
- Main strength: solid theoretical justification for MAP and elegant use of affine projections; notable connection between GANs and amortized variational inference.  
- Major weakness: empirical evaluation is limited and lacks comparisons to established SR benchmarks and state-of-the-art GAN SR methods (e.g., ESRGAN, Real-ESRGAN); “best” claims are unsubstantiated.  
- Experiments are restricted to 2D, 128×128 patches, so scalability and real-world SR performance are unclear.  
- The claim that affine projection reduces need for paired HR/LR data is weakened because the projection itself is trained with paired data — need clarity on whether projection is learned independently or jointly and sensitivity to paired-data quality.  
- Reviewer asks whether a GAN latent noise vector was used/tried to better model distributions — not addressed in the paper.  
- Paper does not report the computational complexity/impact of the affine projection layer.  
- Recommendations: provide quantitative benchmarking (PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS) on standard datasets, test scalability/computational efficiency, detail projection training procedure, explore latent-space conditioning, run ablations for each component, and discuss MAP limitations (mode uniqueness/representativeness).  
- Clarification needed: how does the affine projection layer’s computational cost scale with image size and network depth?  
- Clarification needed: was the affine projection initialized randomly or pre-trained, and how does that choice affect training stability and final performance?  
- Clarification needed: were other generative models besides GANs explored for minimizing cross-entropy, and what were the results?
