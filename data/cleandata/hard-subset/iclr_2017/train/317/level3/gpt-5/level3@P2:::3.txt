Summary and contributions
The paper tackles single-image super-resolution (SR) as an underdetermined inverse problem and proposes amortized MAP inference as an alternative to the prevalent empirical risk minimization with convex losses (e.g., MSE). The key idea is to enforce exact consistency with the low-resolution (LR) input by projecting the network output onto the affine subspace of valid SR solutions defined by the known downsampling operator A. This “affine projection” turns amortized MAP into the problem of minimizing the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the model’s output distribution (over projected predictions) and the high-resolution (HR) data distribution, enabling training without paired LR-HR data. The paper instantiates this idea with three training strategies: (1) a GAN-based method (AffGAN) that, aided by “instance noise,” effectively minimizes KL[qθ‖pY]; (2) a denoiser-guided method (AffDG) that backpropagates gradient estimates of log pY from an independently trained denoiser; and (3) a density-guided baseline (AffLL) using a differentiable likelihood model (MCGSM/PixelCNN-like). Experiments on a toy Swiss roll, grass textures, CelebA faces, and ImageNet show that the affine projection yields exact LR-consistency and often faster training; AffGAN produces the most perceptually pleasing results; and the approach provides a principled justification for adversarial SR. The paper also draws an interesting connection between AffGAN and amortized variational inference.

Decision: Accept
Key reasons:
- Conceptual novelty and clarity: reframing SR as amortized MAP with an architectural affine projection is elegant, practical, and well-motivated; it provides a principled bridge between SR and generative modeling.
- Empirical promise: AffGAN with affine projection produces perceptually convincing results that are LR-consistent, and instance noise is a useful stabilization technique likely of broader interest.

Supporting arguments
- Addresses a real limitation of MSE/PSNR-driven SR (blurriness in multimodal settings) with a prior-driven, MAP-oriented objective that better aligns with perceptual goals.
- The affine projection is simple, compatible with standard CNNs, and guarantees zero LR-inconsistency, a property many perceptual methods only enforce softly.
- The theoretical decomposition from amortized MAP to cross-entropy minimization is clean; the GAN and denoiser formulations are well grounded (KL/JSD view; denoising-score connection).
- The instance noise idea is well motivated (support overlap, discriminator uniqueness) and practically helpful; it contributes to stability beyond SR.
- Experiments are diverse: a controlled toy problem shows that AffGAN/AffDG approach MAP solutions (via cross-entropy estimates); real-image results show strong perceptual quality on textures and faces; quantitative consistency is clearly superior with affine projection.
- The connection to amortized variational inference (Appendix F) is insightful and may inspire stochastic extensions yielding posterior samples for SR.

Limitations (do not preclude acceptance)
- Writing and exposition are at times dense and hard to follow; definitions and objectives (e.g., variants of GAN losses) could be streamlined and standardized.
- Quantitative evaluation favors perceptual inspection; PSNR/SSIM naturally prefer MSE baselines. A richer set of perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS) or user studies would strengthen claims.
- Comparisons with strong contemporary SR baselines (e.g., SRGAN, perceptual-loss methods) are limited; the downsampling model A is assumed known (Gaussian+stride), which differs from standard bicubic protocols and real degradations.
- AffDG and AffLL underperform perceptually; analysis of why (e.g., gradient quality, density sharpness) is mostly qualitative.

Suggestions to improve the paper (for the camera-ready)
- Clarify and polish exposition: provide a concise overview figure of the affine projection pipeline; present the amortized MAP-to-cross-entropy derivation prominently; standardize GAN loss notation; summarize the instance-noise schedule and rationale in the main text.
- Expand empirical evaluation:
  - Compare to standard SR baselines on common benchmarks (Set5/14, BSD100, Urban100) under bicubic degradation, and report PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS; include a small user study if feasible.
  - Add perceptual metrics (LPIPS), naturalness measures (NIQE), and FID/KID between generated HRs and ground-truth HRs.
  - Ablations: with/without affine projection; hard vs soft consistency; A+ fixed vs trainable; instance noise on/off; noise schedule sensitivity.
- Analyze what the networks learn: visualize filters/feature activations; characterize frequency spectra; report patch statistics and texture descriptors; probe how (I − A+A) shapes gradients and high-frequency synthesis.
- Robustness: study sensitivity to mismatch in A (kernel width/stride), unknown degradations, and multiple scale factors (×2/×3/×4); report LR-consistency errors where applicable.
- AffDG/AffLL: provide diagnostics of score estimate quality and density sharpness; quantify their gradient variance; discuss whether modern score-based models could improve AffDG.
- Reproducibility: release code and the numerically estimated A+ kernels; include algorithmic pseudocode for AffGAN with instance noise.

Questions for the authors
- Downsampling operator:
  - How sensitive is performance to mismatch between the assumed A and the true degradation (e.g., bicubic, camera ISP)? Can the method handle unknown A or learn A jointly?
  - What is the practical footprint of A+? How does truncating the deconvolution kernel affect LR-consistency and perceptual quality?

- Instance noise:
  - Please detail the annealing schedule and selection criteria (e.g., keeping discriminator loss constant). How does it compare empirically to label smoothing, spectral normalization, or gradient penalties (e.g., WGAN-GP)?
  - Does instance noise bias the solution (e.g., oversmoothing) if not annealed to zero?

- AffGAN behavior:
  - Do you observe mode collapse or reduced diversity in qθ? Can you quantify entropy/diversity across images and within-image stochastic variants?
  - For the “snake→water” failure, can you bound semantic drift while preserving LR-consistency (e.g., via content or feature losses)?

- AffDG and AffLL:
  - How sensitive are results to the denoiser noise schedule and architecture? Have you tried modern score-matching/score-DSM models for better ∇y log pY estimates?
  - For AffLL, could a stronger likelihood model (e.g., flow-based, diffusion likelihoods) improve optimization stability and perceptual outcomes?

- Evaluation:
  - Can you report LPIPS and/or a small-scale user study to substantiate perceptual gains?
  - Could you add comparisons to SRGAN/perceptual-loss baselines under the same degradation and training data?

Overall, this is a timely, principled, and practically relevant contribution. With improved exposition and expanded evaluation, it could have strong impact.
