- Problem and approach: Treats single-image SR as an underdetermined inverse problem and proposes an amortized MAP framework that enforces exact data-consistency with the LR input.

- Main contributions: (i) affine projection layer guaranteeing Afθ(x)=x via numerically estimated A+; (ii) casting amortized MAP as minimizing H[qθ,pY]; (iii) three training routes—AffGAN, AffDG, AffLL; (iv) theoretical link between AffGAN and amortized variational inference.

- Affine projection: Elegant, plug-and-play layer that enforces exact consistency, speeds training, and can be applied to any SR backbone.

- Amortized MAP formulation: Clean unification linking SR to generative modeling by matching model output distribution to the HR data distribution.

- AffGAN and instance noise: GAN-based realization targeting KL[q‖pY] with an “instance noise” stabilization trick; produces perceptually sharper outputs in experiments.

- Empirical strengths: Convincing toy-task behavior (Swiss roll) and improved perceptual quality on textures, faces, and ImageNet patches; affine projection improves MSE training too.

- Empirical weaknesses: Lacks standard SR benchmarks and metrics (Set5/Set14/BSD100, PSNR/SSIM, LPIPS, FID, MOS) and broader-scale experiments to substantiate perceptual claims.

- Unpaired training claim: Mostly theoretical—experiments appear to use paired LR generated from HR via known A, so unpaired training is not empirically demonstrated.

- AffDG/AffLL limitations: Denoiser- and density-guided variants underperform empirically and need deeper analysis of failure modes.

- Resolution and scalability: Need experiments and discussion on scaling to larger resolutions (256–512 px), different scale factors, and discriminator architectures (e.g., PatchGAN).

- Latent stochasticity: Should evaluate stochastic generator ŷ=ΠAx fθ(x,z) to produce diverse consistent HR samples and quantify diversity vs. fidelity.

- Ablations needed: (i) affine projection vs. soft consistency losses; (ii) instance noise ablation and schedule vs. other stabilization methods; (iii) A+ fixed vs. learned; (iv) robustness to mismatch between assumed A and true downsampling.

- Hallucination risk: Paper should address plausible-but-wrong textures (e.g., snake→water) via weak content losses, patch identity constraints, or cycle consistency to preserve content fidelity.

- Denoiser/density improvements: Try stronger priors/score estimators (PixelCNN++, modern score-based models), annealing schedules, and analyze score-quality dependence on σ.

- Benchmarking recommendation: Include standard SR datasets with bicubic downsampling, report PSNR/SSIM for completeness and perceptual metrics (LPIPS, NIQE/BRISQUE, FID) and, if possible, a small MOS.

- A estimation and sensitivity: Clarify how A is chosen/estimated in practice, sensitivity to mismatch, and whether A or A+ can be learned jointly.

- Instance-noise analysis: Quantify its effect on stability and metrics (FID/LPIPS), compare to label smoothing/feature matching, and detail noise scheduling.

- Questions for authors: Did you train with truly unpaired HR/LR sets? How is A chosen and how sensitive is performance to mismatch? Can you quantify instance-noise benefits and schedules? Did you train with latent z and produce multiple reconstructions? Can you report standard benchmark results and discuss scalability limits? What are observed failure modes for AffDG/AffLL and can better estimators help?

- Overall decision and rationale: Weak accept — principled amortized MAP with exact data-consistency and GAN realization plus instance-noise stabilization are valuable and novel, but broader empirical validation and clarifications are needed.
