- Paper reframes single-image super-resolution as amortized MAP inference and enforces LR-consistency via an affine projection onto the valid solution subspace defined by downsampling operator A.  
- Affine projection converts the problem to minimizing cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between projected model outputs and the HR data distribution, enabling training without paired LR–HR data.  
- Three training instantiations: AffGAN (GAN-based with instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-guided using ∇y log pY), and AffLL (density-guided with a differentiable likelihood model).  
- Experiments (toy Swiss roll, textures, CelebA, ImageNet) show affine projection yields exact LR-consistency and often faster training; AffGAN gives the most perceptually pleasing results.  
- The approach provides a principled justification for adversarial SR and connects AffGAN to amortized variational inference.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: conceptual novelty and clarity—affine projection is an elegant, practical bridge between SR and generative modeling.  
- Key reason: empirical promise—AffGAN with affine projection produces perceptually convincing, LR-consistent results; instance noise stabilizes training.  
- Addresses blurriness of MSE/PSNR-driven SR by using a prior-driven MAP objective aligned with perceptual goals.  
- Affine projection is simple, compatible with CNNs, and guarantees zero LR-inconsistency (hard constraint).  
- Theoretical decomposition to cross-entropy minimization is clean; GAN and denoiser formulations are well grounded (KL/JSD and denoising-score links).  
- Instance noise is well motivated (support overlap, discriminator uniqueness) and practically helpful beyond SR.  
- Diverse experiments: controlled toy validates MAP behavior; real images show strong perceptual quality and superior LR-consistency.  
- Appendix connection to amortized variational inference is insightful and suggests stochastic/posterior-sampling extensions.  
- Limitation: writing and exposition are sometimes dense and could be streamlined (definitions, GAN losses).  
- Limitation: quantitative evaluation relies on perceptual inspection; PSNR/SSIM favor MSE baselines—need richer perceptual metrics or user studies.  
- Limitation: comparisons to strong SR baselines (SRGAN, perceptual-loss) are limited and degradation model A differs from standard bicubic protocols.  
- Limitation: AffDG and AffLL underperform perceptually; analyses of their shortcomings are mostly qualitative.  
- Suggestion: clarify exposition with an overview figure, prominent derivation of amortized MAP→cross-entropy, standardized GAN notation, and instance-noise schedule summary.  
- Suggestion: expand evaluation—compare to standard SR baselines on common benchmarks, report PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS, add user studies and FID/KID/NIQE.  
- Suggestion: include ablations (with/without affine projection, hard vs soft consistency, A fixed vs trainable, instance noise variations).  
- Suggestion: analyze learned representations (filters, spectra, patch stats) and how (I − A+A) affects high-frequency synthesis.  
- Suggestion: test robustness to mismatch in A, unknown degradations, multiple scale factors, and report LR-consistency under mismatch.  
- Suggestion: for AffDG/AffLL, provide diagnostics of score/density quality, gradient variance, and consider modern score-based models or stronger likelihoods.  
- Suggestion: improve reproducibility—release code, A+ kernels, and algorithmic pseudocode for AffGAN with instance noise.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to assumed A vs true degradation, ability to learn A jointly, and practical footprint/truncation effects of A+.  
- Questions: details of instance-noise annealing schedule, comparisons to label smoothing/spectral norm/WGAN-GP, and whether noise biases solutions if not annealed.  
- Questions: does AffGAN suffer mode collapse or reduced diversity; can semantic drift be bounded while preserving LR-consistency?  
- Questions: sensitivity of AffDG to denoiser schedule/architecture and whether modern score-matching improves results; could stronger likelihoods improve AffLL?  
- Questions: can authors provide LPIPS and/or a small user study and comparisons to SRGAN/perceptual-loss baselines under the same degradation and data?
