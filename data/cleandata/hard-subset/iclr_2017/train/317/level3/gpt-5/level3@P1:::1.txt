The paper tackles single-image super-resolution (SR) as an underdetermined inverse problem and proposes an amortized MAP framework that seeks HR reconstructions with high posterior probability while strictly enforcing data-consistency with the LR input. The main contributions are: (i) an affine projection layer that guarantees Afθ(x)=x by projecting to the affine subspace of valid solutions via a numerically estimated A+; (ii) a reduction of amortized MAP to matching the model output distribution qθ to the HR data distribution pY (minimizing cross-entropy H[qθ,pY]); (iii) three training routes—AffGAN (GAN with an update targeting KL[qθ‖pY ] and an “instance noise” trick), AffDG (denoiser-guided using score estimates), and AffLL (density-guided using a PixelCNN/MCGSM prior); and (iv) a theoretical connection between AffGAN and amortized variational inference. Empirically, AffGAN yields sharper, more plausible reconstructions on a toy Swiss roll, grass textures, CelebA, and ImageNet; affine projection helps both perceptual methods and even MSE training by ensuring exact consistency and faster training.

Decision: Weak accept, for the principled amortized MAP formulation with exact data-consistency and its GAN-based realization, plus the stabilizing “instance noise” trick. The framing is novel relative to contemporaneous SR methods, and the theory is thoughtful; however, broader empirical validation and clarifications are needed.

Supporting arguments
- Problem and motivation: The paper squarely targets the perceptual shortcomings of MSE/MAE SR under multimodal uncertainty and argues for MAP-style solutions that land in high-prior regions. The approach is well situated vs. SRGAN and perceptual-feature losses, with a clear differentiator: hard data-consistency via affine projection and a training objective grounded in cross-entropy to the HR distribution, potentially allowing unpaired training.
- Novelty and relation to literature: The affine projection layer is elegant and practically useful (guaranteed consistency, plug-and-play with any SR backbone). Casting amortized MAP as H[qθ,pY] minimization is a clean unification that links SR to generative modeling; the AffGAN derivation (KL[qθ‖pY ] update) and instance noise provide theoretical and algorithmic contributions beyond prior SRGAN-style work. The denoiser/density guided routes are conceptually interesting even if empirically weaker.
- Empirical support and rigor: Results on the toy task convincingly show MAP-like behavior and near-optimal cross-entropy. The affine projection improves training dynamics and exactly enforces consistency. AffGAN produces perceptually sharper images on textures and faces. That said, claims of perceptual superiority would be strengthened by evaluations on standard SR benchmarks and perceptual metrics, larger-scale images, and ablations for the proposed tricks. The “unpaired training” claim is mostly theoretical; experiments appear to use paired degradations (downsampling via known A). Denoiser/density variants underperform, and their negative results could be analyzed more deeply.

Additional feedback to improve the paper (helpful, not necessarily decision-critical)
- Benchmarking: Report results on standard SR datasets (Set5, Set14, BSD100) with bicubic downsampling for 2×/3×/4× to enable fair comparisons to SRCNN/VDSR/ESPCN and SRGAN. Include PSNR/SSIM (for completeness) and perceptual metrics (LPIPS, NIQE/BRISQUE, FID) and, if feasible, a small MOS study.
- Unpaired training: Demonstrate training with disjoint HR and LR corpora (e.g., real camera LR, HR from another dataset), leveraging only knowledge of A. This would substantiate the “no paired {HR,LR}” claim.
- Scale and resolution: Show inference on larger/arbitrary image sizes (e.g., 256–512 px, patch-based if needed) and different scale factors. Discuss discriminator architecture choices to make training resolution-agnostic (e.g., PatchGAN).
- Latent stochasticity: Evaluate a stochastic generator ŷ=ΠAx fθ(x,z) (Appendix F) to produce diverse yet consistent HR solutions. Quantify diversity vs. fidelity (e.g., conditional LPIPS over samples) and discuss when sampling the posterior may be preferable to MAP.
- Ablations: (i) With/without affine projection vs. soft L1/L2 consistency at multiple weights; (ii) instance noise on/off and schedule vs. label smoothing/feature matching; (iii) A+ fixed vs. learned; (iv) sensitivity to mismatch between assumed A and the true downsampling (robustness to kernel/camera pipeline).
- Hallucination control: The “snake→water” example underscores the risk of plausible-but-wrong textures. Consider adding weak content losses (VGG features), patch-wise identity constraints, or cycle consistency to bias toward content fidelity while retaining the prior’s benefits.
- Denoiser/density routes: Provide further analysis/ablations (annealing schedules, score quality vs. σ, stronger priors like PixelCNN++/WaveNet-like models, or modern score-based estimators) to understand failure modes and future promise.

Questions for the authors
1) Unpaired training: In your experiments, did you ever train with truly unpaired HR and LR datasets, or were LR inputs generated by downsampling the HR set with A? If unpaired, please detail datasets, protocol, and results. If not, please clarify the claim and what additional assumptions (knowledge of A, domain shift) are required.
2) Downsampler: How is A chosen in practice, and how sensitive is performance to mismatch between the assumed A and the true imaging pipeline? Could A (and/or A+) be learned jointly from data?
3) Instance noise: Can you quantify its effect (stability, FID/LPIPS, failure rate) and provide an ablation vs. label smoothing or other stabilization baselines? How is the noise schedule selected in practice?
4) Latent z: Did you try adding a latent input to the generator during training (as in Appendix F)? If so, what was the impact on diversity and perceptual quality? Can you produce multiple plausible reconstructions per LR input?
5) Benchmarks and metrics: Can you report results on Set5/Set14/BSD100 with bicubic downsampling and include perceptual metrics or MOS to corroborate “perceptual quality” claims?
6) Resolution and scalability: What limits current training/inference to ≤128×128? Can you discuss memory/runtime and any architectural changes needed to scale to higher resolutions typical in SR?
7) AffDG/AffLL: What are the main observed failure modes (e.g., inaccurate scores off-manifold, peaked densities)? Could more recent score estimators or better likelihood models mitigate the blur/divergence you observed?

Overall, this is a thoughtful, well-motivated paper with a clean architectural idea and a principled learning objective. With stronger empirical validation and clearer evidence for unpaired training, it would make a compelling contribution.
