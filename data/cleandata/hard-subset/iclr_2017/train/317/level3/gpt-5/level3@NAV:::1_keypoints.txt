- Proposes amortized MAP SR using an affine projection layer to enforce LR–HR consistency, turning MAP learning into marginal HR distribution matching.  
- Develops three training routes (AffGAN, denoiser-guided, density-guided), with AffGAN performing best, and introduces instance noise to stabilize GANs.  
- Provides theoretical links between AffGAN, KL minimization, and amortized variational inference.  

- Decision: Reject.  
- Main reasons: insufficient empirical validation against recent GAN-based SOTA and limited practical advantage over SRGAN-style methods.  

- Lacks head-to-head comparisons on standard SR benchmarks (Set5/Set14/BSD100) and perceptual metrics (LPIPS/NIQE/PI) or user studies.  
- “Unpaired” training claim is unclear: pX is obtained by downsampling and A+ is learned with synthetic paired constraints, possibly shifting pairing assumptions.  
- Experiments limited to small scales (64→16, 128→32); unclear scalability to larger/arbitrary resolutions and common SR scale factors.  
- AffGAN outputs can be noisy and suffer semantic drift (e.g., object identity changes), indicating need for perceptual/semantic evaluation and comparisons.  
- Theory (affine projection and VI connection) is strong but needs broader standardized experiments to substantiate practical claims.  

- Requests: report Set5/Set14/BSD100 results (×2/×3/×4) with PSNR/SSIM and perceptual metrics and compare to SRGAN/perceptual-loss methods.  
- Clarify what data are required to estimate pX and train A+; evaluate performance with misspecified A and whether A can be learned from unpaired data.  
- State whether the model runs fully convolutionally on arbitrary sizes and provide memory/runtime for 720p/1080p.  
- Try stochastic generators with latent z or input noise, and provide ablations on instance noise and the necessity of affine projection.  
- Provide failure-case analysis and remedies for high-frequency noise and semantic drift (e.g., perceptual regularizers, content losses, posterior sampling).
