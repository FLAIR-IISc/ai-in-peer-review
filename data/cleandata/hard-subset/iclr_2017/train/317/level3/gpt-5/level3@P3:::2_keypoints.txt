- Reframes single-image super-resolution as amortised MAP inference, aiming to produce MAP estimates by maximising the log-posterior rather than minimising pixel loss.
- Converts the MAP objective into cross-entropy minimisation H[qθ, pY], removing the need for paired HR–LR data.
- Introduces an affine projection layer ŷ = ΠAx fθ(x) = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x to enforce exact LR–HR consistency (Aŷ = x).
- Proposes three optimisation methods: AffGAN (GAN-based with instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-guided via DAE score backprop), and AffLL (density-guided with PixelCNN/MCGSM prior).
- Empirical results on a Swiss-roll toy and images (grass, CelebA, ImageNet) show affine projection improves consistency and AffGAN yields the most visually convincing outputs.
- Paper connects AffGAN to amortised variational inference and frames instance noise as convolved-divergence minimisation.
- Overall assessment: well-written, conceptually clear, practically relevant; projection step and amortised-MAP reframing are compelling.
- Decision: Accept — key reasons are the clear principled reformulation and thorough empirical demonstrations showing perceptual gains.
- Problem addressed: overcomes MSE/PSNR-driven blurriness by targeting MAP-consistent, high-prior reconstructions while amortising inference.
- Literature placement: well-motivated relative to SRGAN and perceptual losses; affine projection enforces hard data constraint unlike soft penalties.
- Swiss-roll results: AffGAN/AffDG match brute-force MAP cross-entropy; soft constraints underperform; projection gives exact LR consistency.
- CelebA/ImageNet: AffGAN produces sharper, more perceptually convincing results with more high-frequency noise; MSE still wins PSNR/SSIM, illustrating perceptual–distortion tradeoff.
- Grass textures: AffGAN clearly outperforms MSE; AffDG/AffLL lag due to optimisation/density challenges.
- Ablations: affine projection outperforms soft constraints, doesn't reduce capacity, and can speed training when A+ is correctly initialised.
- Novelty: affine projection architecture, amortised-MAP casting, and three optimisation routes are novel; theoretical framing and VI connection add value.
- Scientific rigor: clear derivations, explicit assumptions (known linear A), careful experiments, and candid discussion of limitations (MAP representation dependence).
- Suggest clarifying robustness when A is misspecified/non-Gaussian and whether A+ generalises across blur kernels/strides or can be learned.
- Recommend perceptual evaluation additions (human study or LPIPS) to complement PSNR/SSIM metrics.
- Ask for ablations on GAN stability: training curves with/without instance noise, noise schedule sensitivity, and comparison to other stabilisers (gradient penalty, spectral norm).
- Discuss diversity vs MAP: clarify whether AffGAN collapses to a single mode per x and show stochastic AffGAN samples to assess posterior coverage.
- Diagnose AffDG/AffLL underperformance and suggest whether better score/density models or diffusion priors could close the gap.
- Minor fixes: proofread to remove typos and duplicate references.
- Specific questions: robustness to degradation mismatch, whether A can be learned jointly, sensitivity to A+ initialisation and training stability, instance-noise schedule and comparisons, unpaired training/domain-shift sensitivity, stochastic AffGAN samples and posterior coverage, perceptual metric results, and MAP failure modes and mitigation. 
- Final assessment vs review questions: clearly defined problem (perceptual SR via amortised MAP), strong motivation and literature placement, and theoretical plus experimental support for the claims.
