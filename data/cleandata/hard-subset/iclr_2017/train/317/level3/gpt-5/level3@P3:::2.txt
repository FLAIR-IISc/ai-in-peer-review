1. Summary of what the paper claims to do/contribute
- Reframes single-image super-resolution (SR) as amortised MAP inference. Rather than minimising a pixel loss, the goal is to directly produce the MAP estimate for each LR input by maximising the log-posterior.
- Converts the MAP objective into a cross-entropy minimisation H[qθ, pY] (Eq. 9), where qθ is the distribution of projected network outputs for random LR inputs and pY is the HR image distribution. This removes the need for paired HR–LR data.
- Introduces an affine projection layer ŷ = ΠAx fθ(x) = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x that ensures exact LR–HR consistency (Aŷ = x) for a known linear downsampler A. This is simple, principled, and gives zero LR reconstruction error while leaving high-frequency degrees of freedom to the network.
- Proposes and evaluates three methods to optimise the cross-entropy: (i) AffGAN (GAN-based, with an instability-mitigating “instance noise” trick), (ii) AffDG (denoiser-guided, backpropagating scores from a DAE), and (iii) AffLL (density-guided with a differentiable PixelCNN/MCGSM prior).
- Provides empirical evidence on a 2D toy Swiss-roll and on images (grass textures, CelebA faces, ImageNet) that the affine projection improves consistency and that AffGAN yields the most visually convincing SR outputs. The paper also connects AffGAN to amortised variational inference (Appendix F).

Overall, this is a well-written, well-presented paper with strong conceptual clarity and practical relevance. The projection step and the amortised-MAP reframing are particularly compelling.

2. Decision
Accept. Key reasons: (1) clear, principled reformulation of SR as amortised MAP with a simple projection layer that enforces exact LR–HR consistency; (2) thorough empirical demonstrations (toy and real data) showing perceptual gains, with GAN-based training producing the most visually appealing results and likely influencing future SR work.

3. Supporting arguments for the decision
- Problem tackled: The paper addresses the mismatch between standard MSE/PSNR-driven SR (which yields blurry averages under multimodal posteriors) and perceptually plausible SR. The goal is to produce MAP-consistent reconstructions that lie in high-probability regions of the image prior while amortising inference.
- Motivation and placement in literature: The motivation is strong and well grounded. The analysis explains why convex pixel losses fail under uncertainty and motivates the MAP objective. The work is well placed relative to SRGAN and perceptual losses: unlike soft LR-consistency penalties, the affine projection enforces the data constraint exactly. The denoiser gradient approach leverages known score–denoising connections, and AffLL provides a tractable density-model baseline. The instance-noise idea is timely and addresses GAN instability with a clean divergence interpretation.
- Do the results support the claims?
  • Swiss-roll: AffGAN/AffDG achieve cross-entropy close to brute-force MAP; soft constraints underperform; affine projection yields exact LR consistency. This directly supports the amortised-MAP objective.
  • CelebA/ImageNet: AffGAN reconstructions are sharper and perceptually more convincing than MSE baselines, with slightly more high-frequency noise; MSE yields higher PSNR/SSIM as expected, aligning with the paper’s position on perceptual-vs-distortion trade-offs.
  • Grass textures: AffGAN is clearly best perceptually on a difficult case for MSE; AffDG/AffLL lag, consistent with the authors’ analysis of optimisation/density challenges.
  • Ablations: Affine projection vs soft constraints shows tangible gains in LR–HR agreement and often better cross-entropy; MSE experiments suggest the projection neither harms nor reduces capacity and may speed training when A+ is correctly initialised.
- Novelty: The affine projection architecture, the amortised-MAP objective casting and its three optimisation routes (Secs. 3.2–3.4) are novel in SR. Some GAN training aspects overlap with prior variants, but the theoretical framing (KL target, instance noise as convolved-divergence minimisation) and the VI connection add value.
- Scientific rigor: The derivations are clear; the constraints and assumptions (known linear A, learned A+) are explicit; experiments are sufficient and carefully chosen; limitations are candidly discussed (representation dependence of MAP, potential atypicality of modes).

4. Additional feedback to improve the paper (helpful, not decisive)
- Clarify assumptions about A: Emphasise how robust the projection is when A is misspecified or non-Gaussian, and whether A+ learned numerically generalises across blur kernels/strides. A study with unknown/estimated A would broaden applicability.
- Perceptual evaluation: Consider a small human study or a learned perceptual metric (LPIPS) to complement PSNR/SSIM and better quantify the perceptual advantage of AffGAN.
- GAN stability and instance noise: Provide an ablation showing training curves with/without instance noise, and a sensitivity analysis of the noise schedule. Comparing to alternative stabilisers (e.g., gradient penalty) would strengthen the case.
- Diversity vs MAP: While the method targets MAP, the AffGAN objective differs by an H[q] term. Discuss practical implications: does AffGAN encourage multiple plausible reconstructions or still collapse to a single mode per x? Show variations from a stochastic AffGAN (Appendix F) on the same LR input.
- AffDG/AffLL: Briefly diagnose why they underperform on images and what advances (better score models, diffusion-based priors, improved density models) might close the gap.
- Minor: Another proofread will fix residual typos and some duplicated references.

5. Questions for the authors
- Robustness to degradation mismatch: How does performance degrade if the true downsampling/blur differs from A used in ΠAx? Can A be learned jointly from data, or estimated from LR/HR statistics?
- Sensitivity to A+: You show correct initialisation of A+ matters. If A+ is learned end-to-end, does training remain stable and is LR consistency preserved in practice?
- Instance noise: What annealing schedule and noise levels worked best? Could you report discriminator losses and FID-like measures over time to evidence stability? How does instance noise compare to gradient penalty or spectral norm in this setting?
- Unpaired training: Since Eq. 9 removes the need for paired data, did you attempt training with pX and pY from different datasets (e.g., LR from one domain, HR from another)? How sensitive is AffGAN to domain shift between pX and pY?
- Stochastic AffGAN: Can you provide qualitative results for multiple samples ŷ per x using the stochastic variant, and any quantitative measure of posterior coverage vs sharpness?
- Evaluation beyond PSNR/SSIM: Do AffGAN outputs score better on perceptual metrics (LPIPS, DISTS) or in user preference tests? Any plans to include such evaluations?
- Failure modes: The “snake→water” example is illustrative. Can MAP be made more posterior-aware (e.g., conditional priors or semantic guidance) to avoid such plausible-but-wrong hallucinations for specific categories?

Assessment against key review questions
1. Specific problem: Produce perceptually plausible SR by amortising MAP inference with exact LR–HR consistency.
2. Motivation/literature: Strong and well-situated; clear limitations of MSE, principled MAP target, connections to GANs/denoising/density modelling.
3. Support for claims: Yes. Theory is sound; experiments convincingly back the main claims, with honest discussion of limits.
