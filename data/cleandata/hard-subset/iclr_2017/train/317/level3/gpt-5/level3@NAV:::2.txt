1) Summary of contributions
The paper reframes single-image super-resolution as amortised MAP estimation and shows that, under a linear degradation model, training reduces to minimising the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the model’s output distribution and the HR data distribution. A key architectural contribution is an affine projection layer that enforces exact HR-LR consistency (y s.t. Ay = x) via A+, ensuring all outputs satisfy the observation model. Building on this, the paper proposes and evaluates three optimisation strategies for Eq. 9: (i) AffGAN (a projected GAN variant with “instance noise”), (ii) AffDG (denoiser-guided using score estimates), and (iii) AffLL (likelihood-guided with a tractable density model). Experiments on a toy Swiss-roll, textures, CelebA, and ImageNet indicate the GAN-based approach yields the most visually compelling results; the projection improves consistency and sometimes even MSE/SSIM. Theoretical links to KL minimisation and amortised variational inference are provided.

2) Decision
Accept. Key reasons: (a) strong, well-motivated reframing with a simple, effective projection mechanism that guarantees consistency; (b) convincing experimental and theoretical support, with clear practical impact for SR.

3) Supporting arguments (addressing the three key questions)
- Problem tackled: How to perform amortised MAP inference for SR to avoid MSE-induced blur, without paired HR-LR supervision, while enforcing exact HR-LR consistency.
- Motivation and placement: The limitations of MSE/PSNR are clearly articulated; the approach connects to GANs, denoising score estimation, and likelihood models, and distinguishes itself via the affine projection and cross-entropy objective. Some GAN training aspects resemble prior work, but the MAP framing and projection are novel for SR.
- Support for claims: Toy data show cross-entropy near ground-truth MAP; projection yields zero LR-consistency error and can aid MSE training; GAN yields superior perceptual quality on real images. Theoretical derivations (Eq. 9, KL link, instance noise rationale, VI connection) are sound.

4) Suggestions and questions (to help improve the paper; not conditions for acceptance)
- Clarify how A and A+ are chosen in practice across datasets; report sensitivity to kernel mismatch and to errors in the numerically estimated A+.
- Provide ablations: with/without projection; with/without instance noise; effect on stability and final quality.
- Compare more directly to SRGAN/ESRGAN-style baselines under identical degradations and add perceptual metrics or a small user study.
- Discuss robustness to unknown/realistic degradations and potential extension to blind SR.
- On AffDG/AffLL: can improved score/density models (e.g., modern diffusion/flow models) close the gap to GAN?
- Minor: please proofread to fix typos and minor formatting inconsistencies.

Questions for authors
- How sensitive is AffGAN to the accuracy of A+ and to the assumed downsampling kernel?
- Did you observe mode collapse without instance noise, and how does its annealing schedule affect diversity/entropy H[qθ]?
- Can the approach be extended to stochastic posterior sampling (beyond MAP) while retaining the projection constraint?
- What is the computational overhead of the projection relative to standard SR generators?
