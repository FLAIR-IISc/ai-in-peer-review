Summary of contributions
- The paper proposes amortized MAP inference for single-image super-resolution (SR). The key idea is to train a network to directly output the MAP estimate by maximizing the log-posterior averaged over LR inputs, which reduces to minimizing the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the distribution of reconstructions and the HR data distribution.
- A novel affine projection layer ensures consistency between the LR input and the downsampled output (Aŷ = x) by projecting to the affine subspace of valid HR solutions using the Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse A+. This enforces data fidelity exactly and allows training with unpaired HR and LR images since only the marginals need to match.
- Three training strategies are explored: (i) AffGAN, a GAN variant that (approximately) minimizes KL[qθ||pY], with “instance noise” to stabilize training; (ii) AffDG, which uses gradients from a denoiser to approximate ∇y log pY; and (iii) AffLL, which uses a learned density model (PixelCNN/MCGSM) to provide tractable gradients of log pY.
- Empirically, on a 2D “Swiss roll” toy task, AffGAN and AffDG approach the MAP solution. On images (grass textures, CelebA, ImageNet), AffGAN produces visually sharper, more plausible results than MSE/MAE-trained baselines and outperforms soft constraints in LR/HR consistency.

Decision
Reject. While the framework is conceptually appealing and the affine projection is a neat architectural contribution, the empirical validation is limited relative to the SR literature and it is unclear what the method adds over concurrent GAN-based SR approaches beyond the projection and interpretation.

Supporting arguments
- Positioning and novelty: The amortized-MAP perspective and the exact data-consistency projection are interesting and well motivated. However, the strongest empirical gains come from the GAN training, which overlaps with SRGAN-era work. The paper argues theoretical grounding and a connection to amortized VI, but the main practical advantage over SRGAN-like baselines is not convincingly demonstrated.
- Comparisons and benchmarks: The experiments omit standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) and widely used baselines (e.g., SRCNN, VDSR, SRResNet/SRGAN). Reported results focus on grass textures, CelebA, and up to 128×128 ImageNet crops, which makes it hard to assess competitiveness and generality. No human study or strong perceptual metric evaluation is provided.
- Scale and generalization: Results are limited to 4× upscaling and relatively small outputs (mostly 128×128). It is unclear how the approach scales to larger resolutions or arbitrary image sizes that SR typically targets. Although the projection is convolutional and therefore nominally size-agnostic, the paper does not show such evidence.
- Affine projection and “unpaired” training: The method claims no need for paired HR/LR, but the training pipeline still relies on samples from pX, produced by a known A. In the experiments, LR inputs are created by downsampling HR images with A. It is not fully clear whether the approach truly removes the dependence on paired datasets in practice, or simply shifts it to knowing and calibrating A and A+; and how it fares when LR images come from unknown degradations.
- Baselines within the framework: AffDG and AffLL underperform and are not pursued further; this weakens the case that amortized MAP with estimated ∇ log pY or learned densities is practically viable today. The paper’s strongest results rely on AffGAN with instance noise, which resembles prior GAN SR pipelines with an additional data-consistency layer.
- Rigor and claims: The theory is solid and carefully presented. The empirical evidence supports data consistency and improved perceptual sharpness, but does not substantiate broader claims about MAP advantages in realistic SR settings (unknown degradations, diverse content, large-scale benchmarks).

Additional feedback (to help improve the paper)
- Add evaluations on Set5, Set14, BSD100 (and Urban100 if possible), with PSNR/SSIM and perceptual measures (e.g., NIQE/BRISQUE; or a small-scale MOS study). Compare against SRCNN, VDSR, SRResNet/SRGAN and other contemporaneous methods. Include runtime and model size.
- Show results on higher resolutions and variable image sizes. Since the architecture is convolutional with a fixed A and A+, demonstrate inference on larger images and other scales (×2, ×3, ×4, potentially ×8).
- Clarify the “unpaired” training claim. Detail how pX is obtained in practice, whether true unpaired datasets were used (distinct HR and LR corpora), and whether any implicit pairing remains. Discuss robustness when A is misspecified or unknown; include experiments with realistic camera degradations and noise.
- Explore and report the stochastic generator variant (input noise z) to capture posterior multimodality and improve diversity/artifact trade-offs. Even a short experiment could substantiate the amortized VI connection and mitigate GAN ringing/noise.
- Provide stronger ablations: with/without affine projection; soft vs hard constraints; effect of instance noise schedule; different A/A+ designs. Analyze PSNR drop vs perceptual gain trade-offs.
- Discuss how A+ is computed, how sensitive the results are to its accuracy, and whether A+ could be learned jointly without violating consistency.
- Consider reporting learned perceptual similarity (feature-space metrics) and qualitative failure modes, including the “snake→water” example, to calibrate expectations.

Questions for the authors
- How exactly did you obtain pX during training when claiming unpaired HR/LR? Did you use distinct datasets for HR (for D) and LR (for G inputs), or did you downsample the same HR corpus? In a fully unpaired scenario, how would you calibrate A?
- Does the method handle unknown or spatially varying degradations? Can A and/or A+ be estimated from data and still preserve the hard consistency constraint?
- Can your current AffGAN operate with a latent noise input z to produce diverse solutions for the same x? If tried, how did it affect sharpness, artifacts, and data-consistency?
- What prevents scaling to larger images or different upscaling factors? Are there memory or discriminator stability issues? Any results beyond 128×128?
- Could you provide results on Set5/Set14/BSD100 and comparisons to SRResNet/SRGAN and MSE-trained strong baselines?
- How sensitive is performance to the accuracy of A+? If A+ is imperfect, does the constraint meaningfully degrade, and can joint fine-tuning recover performance?
- Can you quantify the benefit of instance noise versus label smoothing in AffGAN on your datasets (stability, FID-like measures, or discriminator loss curves)?
