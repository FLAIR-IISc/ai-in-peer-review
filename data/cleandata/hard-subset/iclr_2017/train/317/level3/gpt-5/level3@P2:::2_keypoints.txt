- Reframes single-image SR as amortised MAP inference and enforces exact LR-HR consistency via an affine projection layer.  
- Affine projection: gθ(x) = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x guarantees Afθ(x) = x and converts MAP to minimising cross-entropy between implied HR distribution and true HR distribution.  
- Removes need for paired LR-HR supervision by targeting high-probability reconstructions under an image prior.  
- Proposes three amortised MAP training strategies: AffGAN (GAN variant with affine projection), AffDG (denoiser-guided via score estimates), and AffLL (density-guided using differentiable likelihood model).  
- AffGAN includes an “instance noise” stabilisation trick with clear theoretical motivation and approximately minimises KL[qθ||pY].  
- AffDG backpropagates gradient estimates of log pY using a pre-trained denoiser (score estimation).  
- AffLL uses a PixelCNN-like differentiable likelihood (MCGSM) to directly minimise cross-entropy.  
- Theory: derives affine projection, amortised MAP objective, links GAN objective to KL minimisation, and connects to amortised variational inference (stochastic AffGAN).  
- Empirics: validated on a Swiss roll toy, textures, CelebA, and ImageNet; affine projection enforces exact consistency and speeds MSE optimisation.  
- AffGAN yields the most perceptually convincing SR results among the proposed methods.  
- Overall assessment: well written, well motivated, novel architectural constraint, and practically compelling — recommendation to accept.  
- Key reasons for acceptance: principled reframing of SR as amortised MAP and a theoretically motivated, effective AffGAN with instance-noise stabilisation.  
- Suggest direct comparisons to SRGAN/perceptual-loss baselines to isolate the benefit of exact affine projection vs. soft consistency.  
- Recommend reporting perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS) or a small user study to complement PSNR/SSIM and qualitative results.  
- Ask for ablations: with/without affine projection, instance noise schedules, discriminator capacity and update ratio.  
- Request robustness tests to mismatch between assumed A and true degradation, and experiments with unknown/estimated A or mild noise.  
- Ask authors to discuss computational overhead of computing A+ and whether a learned A+ (properly initialized) is practical.  
- Clarify how pX is obtained in unpaired settings and behavior when only HR data are available.  
- Provide explicit instance noise annealing schedule and compare to other stabilisation methods (gradient penalty, spectral norm).  
- For AffDG, quantify score quality vs. noise level and explain why it leads to blur; consider newer score-matching methods.  
- For AffLL, detail likelihood calibration, overfitting checks, and whether the density model biases toward low-variance regions.  
- Suggest stochastic extension experiments to demonstrate diversity (sampling from qY|X;θ) and multi-scale affine projections or other inverse problems.  
- Minor: proofread for typos/formatting and consider releasing code for A+ estimation and instance noise.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to errors in A or A+ and results under kernel mismatch or sensor noise.  
- Questions on training: instance noise schedule, performance without noise, alternatives compared, and discriminator-to-generator update ratio.  
- Questions on AffGAN: empirical evidence for KL approximation, and whether AffGAN yields higher-entropy (more diverse) solutions vs. MAP fidelity.  
- Questions on AffDG/AffLL: quantify denoiser-derived score accuracy and whether alternative likelihoods or normalising flows were tried.  
- Request evaluation details: human preference results, LPIPS metrics, failure-mode statistics, and mitigation strategies.
