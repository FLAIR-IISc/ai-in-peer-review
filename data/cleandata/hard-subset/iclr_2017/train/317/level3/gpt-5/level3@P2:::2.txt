1. Summary of contributions
The paper reframes single-image super-resolution (SR) as amortised MAP inference and proposes an architecture and training procedures that directly target high-probability reconstructions under an image prior while strictly enforcing LR-HR consistency:
- Affine projection layer: A simple but elegant architectural constraint gθ(x) = (I − A+ A) fθ(x) + A+ x that guarantees Afθ(x) = x. This converts the MAP objective into minimising cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the model’s implied HR output distribution and the true HR distribution, removing the need for paired LR-HR supervision.
- Three training strategies for amortised MAP:
  1) AffGAN: a GAN variant that, with the affine projection, approximately minimises KL[qθ||pY]; includes a practical “instance noise” trick for stabilising training and a clear theoretical motivation.
  2) AffDG: denoiser-guided SR that backpropagates gradient estimates of log pY via a pre-trained denoiser (score estimation).
  3) AffLL: density-guided SR using a differentiable likelihood model (PixelCNN-like with MCGSM) to directly minimise cross-entropy.
- Theory: Clear derivation of the affine projection, the amortised MAP objective, a link between the proposed GAN objective and KL minimisation, and a conceptual connection to amortised variational inference (stochastic AffGAN).
- Empirics: Demonstrations on a 2D Swiss roll toy problem, grass textures, CelebA faces, and ImageNet. The affine projection preserves exact LR consistency, improves optimisation with MSE, and AffGAN delivers the most perceptually convincing SR among proposed methods.

Overall, the paper is well written, the problem is well motivated, and the core idea—amortised MAP with an architectural constraint—is both novel in SR and practically compelling.

2. Decision
Accept.
Key reasons: (i) Strong, clear conceptual reframing of SR as amortised MAP with a principled architectural projection that enforces physical consistency; (ii) Practical and theoretically motivated AffGAN method that yields state-of-the-art perceptual quality (with a useful, general training stabilisation via instance noise), supported by well-chosen experiments.

3. Supporting arguments
- What problem is tackled? The ambiguity of SR under MSE/PSNR training, which produces blurry averages. The paper aims to recover HR reconstructions that are plausible under the image prior through amortised MAP, while ensuring exact consistency with the LR input.
- Is the approach well motivated and well placed in the literature? Yes. The limitations of MSE/PSNR are well established; connections to SSIM/perceptual metrics, SRGAN, perceptual losses, and denoising/score estimation are appropriate. The distinction between soft LR consistency (common in prior SRGAN-like work) and the proposed exact affine projection is a meaningful contribution. The theoretical perspective on GANs (KL minimisation variant, instance noise) is timely and relevant.
- Do the results support the claims with rigor? The Swiss roll experiments directly evaluate cross-entropy and show the proposed methods approach MAP. On images, the affine projection demonstrably enforces exact consistency and speeds MSE optimisation; AffGAN produces sharper and more plausible results on textures, faces, and ImageNet. The paper is candid about the relative underperformance of AffDG and AffLL and provides reasonable diagnosis. While human studies and broader baselines would further strengthen the case, the evidence presented is sound for ICLR.

4. Additional feedback (to help improve the paper)
- Comparisons and evaluation:
  - Include a direct comparison to SRGAN/perceptual-loss baselines with soft consistency penalties and VGG-feature losses to isolate the impact of exact projection vs. soft regularisation.
  - Consider perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS) or a small-scale user study to complement PSNR/SSIM and qualitative figures.
  - Report ablations: with/without affine projection; instance noise schedules; discriminator capacity and update ratio.
- Practicality and robustness:
  - Evaluate robustness to mismatch between assumed A (Gaussian + stride) and true degradation kernels; include experiments with unknown/estimated A or mild blur/noise mismatch.
  - Discuss computational overhead of computing/using A+, and whether a learned A+ (initialized correctly) is acceptable in practice.
- Methodological clarity:
  - Clarify how pX is obtained in unpaired settings when real LR images are not generated by your A. What happens when only HR data is available (common in SR benchmarks)?
  - Expand on how instance noise is annealed (provide an explicit schedule or controller) and its sensitivity; contrast with other stabilisation methods (e.g., gradient penalty, spectral norm).
  - For AffDG, provide more quantitative analysis of the score quality vs. noise level and why it leads to blur in practice; consider recent score-matching methods for improved gradients.
  - For AffLL, detail the log-likelihood calibration, overfitting checks, and whether the learned density overemphasises low-variance regions (leading to blur).
- Broader discussion:
  - The mode-typicality caveat is important; consider a stochastic extension experiment (sampling from qY|X;θ) to demonstrate diversity without sacrificing plausibility.
  - Explore multi-scale affine projections and generalisation to other inverse problems (deblurring, inpainting) in a brief experiment or discussion.
- Minor: A careful proofread to fix typos and formatting glitches; consider releasing code for A+ estimation and instance noise.

Questions for the authors
- Affine projection:
  - How sensitive is performance to errors in A or A+? Can you report results under kernel mismatch or modest additive sensor noise?
  - In practice, does fixing A+ vs. fine-tuning it change results materially when initialised correctly?
- Training/stability:
  - Please specify the instance noise annealing schedule and any adaptive criteria used. How much does performance degrade without instance noise, and how does it compare to alternatives like gradient penalties?
  - How many D updates per G update do you use, and do you ever train D “to convergence” per iteration as suggested theoretically?
- AffGAN objective:
  - You argue the generator update approximates minimising KL[qθ||pY]. Can you provide empirical evidence (e.g., likelihood-ratio diagnostics) or discuss conditions under which the approximation breaks?
  - Given that H[qθ, pY] = KL[qθ||pY] + H[qθ], does AffGAN tend to higher-entropy solutions than MAP? Can you illustrate diversity vs. fidelity trade-offs for fixed x?
- AffDG and AffLL:
  - Could you quantify the accuracy of the denoiser-derived score (e.g., Stein’s unbiased risk estimator or score matching diagnostics) and its effect on SR quality?
  - For AffLL, did you try alternative continuous likelihoods or normalising flows to improve gradient fidelity?
- Evaluation:
  - Do you have any human preference results or LPIPS metrics? Can you share failure statistics (e.g., frequency of “snake→water” hallucinations) and ways to detect/mitigate them?

Overall, this paper offers a compelling, principled reframing with a simple architectural idea that materially improves SR practice. I recommend acceptance.
