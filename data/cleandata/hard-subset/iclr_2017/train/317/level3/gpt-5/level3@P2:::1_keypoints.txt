- Proposes amortized MAP for single-image SR by training a network to output MAP estimates via maximizing log-posterior / minimizing H[qθ, pY].
- Introduces an affine projection layer using A+ (Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse) to enforce exact LR/HR consistency (Aŷ = x).
- Claims this projection allows training with unpaired HR and LR images by matching marginals only.
- Studies three training strategies: AffGAN (GAN with instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-gradient estimator), and AffLL (learned density model).
- Empirically, AffGAN and AffDG approach MAP on a 2D toy; AffGAN yields visually sharper reconstructions than MSE/MAE baselines on selected datasets.
- Decision: Reject — conceptually appealing but empirical validation is limited and practical gains over GAN-based SR are unclear.
- Main novelty overlaps with SRGAN-era approaches; strongest gains stem from GAN training rather than the amortized-MAP framing.
- Experiments omit standard SR benchmarks and widely used baselines (Set5, Set14, BSD100; SRCNN, VDSR, SRResNet/SRGAN), limiting assessment of competitiveness.
- Evaluation lacks human studies and strong perceptual metrics; reported datasets are limited (grass textures, CelebA, small ImageNet crops).
- Scale/generalization concerns: only 4× upscaling and mostly 128×128 outputs; unclear behavior on larger images or other scales.
- "Unpaired" training claim is unclear because LR samples are produced by a known A in experiments, so dependence on knowing A remains.
- AffDG and AffLL underperform in practice, weakening the claim that amortized MAP with estimated ∇ log pY or learned densities is viable today.
- Theory is solid and well presented, but empirical evidence does not substantiate broad MAP advantages in realistic SR settings (unknown degradations, diverse content).
- Recommend adding evaluations on Set5/Set14/BSD100/Urban100 with PSNR/SSIM, perceptual metrics (NIQE/BRISQUE) and comparisons to SR baselines, plus runtime/model size.
- Recommend demonstrating higher-resolution and variable-size inference and other upscaling factors (×2, ×3, ×8) to show scalability.
- Recommend clarifying unpaired training details: how pX is obtained, whether distinct LR/HR corpora are used, and robustness to misspecified or unknown A.
- Recommend exploring stochastic generator variants (latent z) to capture multimodality and mitigate GAN artifacts, supporting amortized VI claims.
- Recommend stronger ablations: with/without affine projection, soft vs hard constraints, instance noise schedule, and different A/A+ computations.
- Recommend analyzing computation and sensitivity of A+, and whether A+ can be learned jointly without breaking consistency.
- Recommend reporting feature-space perceptual metrics, qualitative failure modes, and detailed ablations on artifact trade-offs.
- Question: How was pX obtained in training — distinct LR dataset or downsampled same HR corpus; how to calibrate A for truly unpaired data?
- Question: Can the method handle unknown or spatially varying degradations; can A and/or A+ be estimated from data while preserving the hard constraint?
- Question: Can AffGAN operate with latent noise z for diverse outputs, and how does that affect sharpness, artifacts, and data consistency?
- Question: What prevents scaling to larger images or different upscaling factors; are there memory or discriminator stability issues; any results beyond 128×128?
- Question: Please provide results on Set5/Set14/BSD100 and comparisons to SRResNet/SRGAN and strong MSE-trained baselines.
- Question: How sensitive is performance to inaccuracies in A+; does an imperfect A+ degrade results and can joint fine-tuning recover performance?
- Question: Can you quantify the benefit of instance noise versus label smoothing in AffGAN (stability metrics, FID-like measures, discriminator loss curves)?
