Summary of contributions
- The paper reframes single-image super-resolution (SR) as amortized MAP inference, aiming to produce high-resolution outputs that lie in high-probability regions under a natural image prior rather than the mean solutions encouraged by MSE/MAE.
- A key architectural contribution is an affine projection layer ŷ = Π_Ax fθ(x) = (I − A⁺A)fθ(x) + A⁺x that guarantees exact consistency with the LR observation (Afθ(x)=x) while letting the network learn only the null-space residual. This turns training into minimising the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] without requiring paired LR–HR training data.
- Three training strategies are developed to approximate this objective: (i) AffGAN, an affine-projected GAN with a generator fed by LR inputs and a discriminator over HR images, together with “instance noise” to stabilise training; (ii) denoiser-guided (AffDG), which backpropagates score estimates ∇y log pY(y) from a denoising model; and (iii) density-guided (AffLL), which uses a differentiable autoregressive density model (PixelCNN+MCGSM) to provide log-likelihood gradients.
- A useful theoretical connection is made between the AffGAN objective and minimising KL[qθ||pY], and in Appendix F between a stochastic extension of AffGAN and amortized variational inference.
- Empirically, the approach is demonstrated on a 2D Swiss-roll toy problem (where cross-entropy to the true prior can be computed), grass textures, CelebA faces, and ImageNet. AffGAN yields the most visually plausible, sharp results; affine projection enforces exact LR consistency and often improves training speed and stability versus soft constraints.

Decision: Accept
Key reasons: (1) Novel, well-motivated reformulation of SR as amortized MAP with an elegant affine projection that guarantees LR consistency and obviates paired supervision; (2) Solid theoretical grounding and convincing empirical demonstrations across multiple datasets, with AffGAN clearly outperforming MSE/MAE in perceptual quality.

Supporting arguments
- Problem tackled: The paper targets the underdetermined nature of SR and the known shortcomings of MSE/MAE training (blurry averages), proposing amortized MAP to favor high-prior-probability outputs while retaining fast test-time inference.
- Motivation and placement: The approach is well positioned relative to SRGAN and perceptual-loss SR: unlike soft LR consistency penalties, the affine projection enforces exact consistency and reduces the learning problem to matching the HR marginal distribution. The use of instance noise addresses a concrete failure mode of GAN training (non-overlapping supports). The denoiser- and density-guided variants are principled alternatives grounded in score matching and likelihood modeling.
- Empirical/theoretical support: 
  - Toy Swiss-roll: AffGAN/AffDG achieve cross-entropy near the brute-force MAP optimum; soft constraints underperform and violate LR consistency.
  - CelebA/ImageNet: AffGAN yields sharper, more plausible textures and details, while MSE has higher PSNR/SSIM as expected. Affine projection reduces LR inconsistency to zero and often trains faster.
  - Theory: Clear derivations show the constrained MAP objective reduces to H[qθ,pY]; the specific GAN update approximates KL[qθ||pY]; instance noise is justified to ensure discriminator uniqueness/overlap; the VI connection is interesting and new.
- Limitations: 
  - Perceptual validation is largely qualitative; no human studies or learned perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS). Some GAN artifacts and occasional semantic hallucinations (snake→water) are reported. 
  - The AffDG/AffLL variants underperform on natural images; analyses suggest training/peakedness issues, but the paper provides limited quantitative ablations beyond convergence plots. 
  - The approach assumes a known, fixed downsampling operator A and numerically tuned A⁺; robustness to kernel mismatch or noise is not evaluated.
  - Exposition is dense; some derivations and notation could be streamlined.

Additional feedback (to improve the paper; not critical to the accept decision)
- Clarity and organization
  - Introduce A and A⁺ earlier and summarise the projection intuition with a compact figure; move numerical A⁺ estimation details to the main text briefly (with kernel sizes, constraints), leaving proofs in the appendix.
  - Clean up notation for distributions qθ, qY|X;θ and make the mapping between the amortized MAP objective, cross-entropy, and the GAN/denoiser/density surrogates explicit in a one-page “map of objectives.”
  - Tighten the GAN objective derivation (Eq. 10) and clarify the link to KL minimization and the role of the extra H[qθ] term wrt MAP bias.
- Evaluation
  - Add perceptual metrics (LPIPS, DISTS) and, if feasible, a small human study. Report FID/KID between qθ and pY on held-out data to quantify marginal matching.
  - Provide ablations on instance noise (level and annealing), discriminator update ratio, and the effect of the affine projection (fixed vs trainable A⁺, random vs MP initialisation).
  - Demonstrate robustness to mismatch in A (unknown blur kernel, small observation noise) and discuss how to integrate kernel/ noise estimation.
  - For AffDG/AffLL, include more diagnostics: gradient field visualisations, sensitivity to noise level/likelihood sharpness, and whether multi-scale DAEs or tempered densities help.
- Analysis of what is learned
  - Visualise the null-space residual (I−A⁺A)fθ(x) separately from A⁺x; show its spectra and spatial statistics to illustrate “what details” the model hallucinates.
  - Probe discriminator features (e.g., via linear probes/activation maximisation) and generator filters to understand texture vs structure priors learned.
  - Measure entropy/diversity H[qθ] (where possible) and show how affine projection influences it compared to soft LR penalties.
- Reproducibility
  - Clarify whether training used paired LR-HR or unpaired sampling; if paired, include an unpaired setting to validate the claimed independence from pairs.
  - Release code for A⁺ estimation and instance-noise scheduling; provide exact dataset splits and hyperparameters.

Questions for the authors
1. Unpaired training: In the image experiments, did you train strictly without paired LR-HR samples (sampling x from pX and y from pY independently), or were LR images generated from HR via A? If the latter, can you show results with unpaired x and y to validate the amortized setup?
2. Robustness to A mismatch: How sensitive is performance to errors in A or mild observation noise? Can the affine projection be extended to jointly estimate A or accommodate noise (e.g., via a slack variable)?
3. Instance noise: How did you set and anneal σ in practice (heuristic target loss, schedule)? Can you provide an ablation showing training stability and final quality vs σ schedule?
4. AffDG/AffLL: What were the most effective configurations (noise levels, multi-scale DAEs, density temperature)? Could preconditioning or score-matching at multiple σ alleviate the training issues?
5. Stochastic AffGAN: Do you have preliminary empirical results with a z-conditioned generator (sampling from qY|X)? Does it improve plausibility/avoid “atypical mode” concerns highlighted in Section 5.6?
6. Learned features: Can you visualise the residual components the model adds (e.g., frequency bands, texture dictionaries) and discriminator activations to elucidate which priors are captured?
7. Failure cases: Beyond the snake→water example, what patterns of semantic drift arise, and how often? Any simple mitigation (content-aware losses, conditional discriminators)?
