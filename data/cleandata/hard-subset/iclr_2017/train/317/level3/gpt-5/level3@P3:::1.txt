Summary of contributions
- The paper proposes amortized MAP inference for single-image SR, implemented via a CNN whose output is projected onto the affine subspace of solutions consistent with the LR input. This guarantees exact consistency (Aŷ = x) and reframes training as minimizing cross-entropy H[qθ, pY], enabling unpaired training in principle.
- Three training strategies are explored: AffGAN (an adversarial variant argued to minimize KL[qθ‖pY] with “instance noise” for stability), denoiser-guided SR (AffDG, using score estimates from a DAE), and a maximum-likelihood density-guided baseline (AffLL).
- Theoretical contributions include: (i) the affine projection construction and its properties; (ii) analysis connecting the proposed GAN objective to KL minimization under instance noise; and (iii) a link between a stochastic extension of AffGAN and amortized variational inference.
- Empirically, AffGAN yields visually plausible SR on grass textures, CelebA, and ImageNet up to 128×128, and enforces exact LR–HR consistency by design.

Decision: Reject
Key reasons: (1) Empirical validation is limited and does not compare on standard SR benchmarks; (2) Incremental value over contemporaneous GAN-based SR methods (e.g., SRGAN) is unclear without strong ablations and head-to-head comparisons.

Supporting arguments
- Problem tackled: Learn SR mappings that produce plausible high-frequency details by approximating amortized MAP, avoiding MSE-induced blur and enforcing LR–HR consistency.
- Motivation and placement: The motivation to move from MSE/PSNR to priors and MAP is sound. The affine projection is elegant and addresses a practical weakness of soft LR consistency penalties. The paper provides a clean probabilistic objective, and the “instance noise” perspective on stabilizing GANs is well reasoned. However, the paper does not clearly articulate what is gained over recent GAN-based SR (e.g., Ledig et al., 2016), beyond exact LR consistency and the amortized MAP framing. Without comparative experiments, the added value is speculative.
- Do the claims hold?
  - Theory: The derivations for turning amortized MAP into cross-entropy minimization with affine projection are clear. The KL connection for the generator update and the smoothed divergence under instance noise are plausible under standard GAN assumptions. The amortized VI connection in Appendix F is interesting but not empirically validated.
  - Empirics: AffGAN is the only practical success; AffDG and AffLL underperform. Results are on non-standard datasets and small resolutions (mostly 4× SR to 64 or 128), with limited quantitative perceptual metrics. The snake-to-water failure illustrates the risk of hallucination under a strong prior. Overall, the experiments are insufficient to support broad claims about SR quality relative to state-of-the-art.

Additional feedback to improve the paper (helpful suggestions, not all decision-driving)
- Clarify the incremental contribution over GAN-based SR:
  - Provide head-to-head comparisons against SRGAN and perceptual-loss baselines (e.g., SRResNet/SRGAN) with and without the affine projection.
  - Ablate the affine projection (hard vs soft), instance noise, and the proposed generator update rule. Show effects on stability, LR–HR consistency, and perceptual quality.
- On “training without pairs”:
  - In principle, your objective needs unpaired HR and LR datasets if A is known. In practice, all image experiments downsample HRs with A to form x (paired). Please demonstrate unpaired training: use LR images sourced independently (e.g., camera LR or a different dataset), HRs from another corpus, and verify the approach still works.
  - Estimation of A+: Appendix B optimizes A+ using synthetic Gaussian vectors, not HR/LR pairs, which is good. But in real settings A is unknown and spatially varying. Discuss and/or demonstrate robustness to kernel mismatch, blind SR scenarios, or learning A jointly.
- Benchmarking and scale:
  - Evaluate on Set5, Set14, BSD100 (and ideally Urban100, DIV2K) with bicubic degradation to enable fair comparison. Report PSNR/SSIM for fidelity, and perceptual scores (LPIPS, NIQE, FID) and MOS for quality.
  - Go beyond 128×128. Since the model is fully convolutional with a fixed A/A+, it should handle arbitrary sizes; demonstrate runtime/memory scaling and results at higher resolutions (e.g., 720p crops).
- Stochastic generator and multimodality:
  - The amortized MAP framing favors single-mode outputs. Please experiment with the stochastic extension (adding z) to generate multiple plausible HRs per LR, and compare perceptual realism and fidelity trade-offs. Quantify diversity (e.g., LPIPS across samples) and user preference.
- AffDG and AffLL:
  - Consider stronger score estimators (e.g., modern score-matching/denoising diffusion models) for AffDG and improved density models for AffLL. Your analysis correctly identifies training pathologies; more modern techniques may overcome them.
- Evaluation of LR–HR consistency:
  - Report exact LR consistency errors and show that the hard projection maintains perfect consistency across datasets, contrasting with soft penalties.
- Failure analysis and use-case guidance:
  - Provide a systematic analysis of hallucinations and domain shifts. For applications sensitive to semantics (e.g., faces, medical), discuss safeguards or calibrated uncertainty (e.g., multiple samples or confidence maps).

Questions for the authors
1) What, concretely, does AffGAN add over SRGAN beyond exact LR consistency and the amortized MAP interpretation? Can you show consistent perceptual gains or stability improvements in controlled ablations?
2) Did you run truly unpaired training in any experiment? If so, how were pX and pY sourced, and what was the impact on quality? If not, can you add such an experiment?
3) How sensitive is performance to kernel mismatch between the true downsampling operator and A used in training? Can A (and A+) be learned jointly in a blind SR setting?
4) Can your model process arbitrary image sizes at test time with the same A/A+? Are there boundary artifacts or tiling issues? Please report timings and memory for larger inputs.
5) Did you try adding a latent z in AffGAN (as in Appendix F) to sample multiple plausible HRs for a fixed LR? How did this affect performance and training stability?
6) Could you provide results on Set5/Set14/BSD100 (4×, bicubic) with PSNR/SSIM and perceptual metrics (LPIPS, NIQE) and, ideally, a small-scale MOS study?
7) How critical is instance noise vs other stabilization methods (e.g., gradient penalty)? Please include an ablation and a practical schedule for annealing σ.
