- Reframes single-image SR as amortised MAP inference to produce posterior modes (perceptually plausible reconstructions) instead of MSE-averaged outputs.
- Introduces an affine projection layer enforcing exact LR consistency Afθ(x)=x using the Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse A+ of the downsampler A.
- Affine projection allows minimising cross-entropy H[qθ,pY] and removes the need for paired LR–HR supervision in training.
- Proposes three training strategies: AffGAN (affine-projected GAN), AffDG (denoiser-guided using score estimates), and AffLL (density-guided with a differentiable PixelCNN/MCGSM prior).
- Theoretically grounds AffGAN as approximately minimising KL[qθ‖pY] with instance noise improving GAN stability.
- Experiments (Swiss-roll, textures, CelebA, ImageNet) show AffGAN yields the most visually plausible results; affine projection speeds training and enforces zero LR mismatch.
- Affine projection can also improve MSE/SSIM when used with an MSE objective.
- Decision: Accept — clear reformulation, simple general affine projection, strong theoretical and empirical support, and novel links between GAN training and variational inference.
- Addresses the underdetermined nature of SR and justifies moving away from MSE/PSNR toward perceptual priors.
- Derivations connecting training objectives to cross-entropy and KL are sound and well situated in prior GAN/denoising literature.
- Results support claims on toy tasks and show trade-offs on natural images (sharper perceptual results but lower PSNR/SSIM); authors transparently discuss instance-noise necessity and failure cases.
- AffDG and AffLL underperform visually in high dimensions; paper offers plausible explanations (score/density sharpness and instability).
- Suggests adding perceptual evaluations (human studies or NIQE/BRISQUE) since PSNR/SSIM are misaligned with perception.
- Requests direct comparison with SRGAN and ablations on instance noise vs other stabilization methods to isolate contributions of affine projection and objectives.
- Asks for discussion and experiments on sensitivity to downsampler A mismatch, feasibility of learning A, and effects of model misspecification.
- Requests clarification on numerical estimation of A+ (support, kernel sizes, boundary conditions, anti-aliasing), and runtime/memory/training-stability metrics.
- Proposes demonstration of truly unpaired LR/HR training to support the unpaired claim.
- Raises concerns about hallucination vs faithfulness and suggests exploring weak content-consistency terms or stochastic sampling to present multiple plausible outputs.
- Recommends improvements for AffDG/AffLL (multi-scale DAEs, multi-noise-level score matching, stronger priors) and diagnostics on gradient quality/mode coverage.
- Asks for theoretical/empirical analysis of entropy bias (KL vs cross-entropy) and measures of qθ entropy with/without affine projection.
- Calls for reproducibility materials: code, instance-noise schedules, annealing heuristics, and sensitivity analyses for GAN/discriminator capacity.
