1) Summary of claims and contributions
The paper proposes amortized MAP inference for single-image super-resolution by training a network that projects outputs onto the affine subspace consistent with the LR input, guaranteeing exact observation consistency. It recasts amortized MAP as cross-entropy minimization between the model’s HR output distribution and the true HR distribution, and instantiates three learning mechanisms: an affine-projected GAN (AffGAN), a denoiser-guided approach (AffDG), and a density-guided approach (AffLL). The paper also introduces “instance noise” to stabilize GAN training and draws a connection between AffGAN and amortized variational inference. Empirically, AffGAN delivers sharp, plausible reconstructions on textures, faces, and ImageNet, while the affine projection improves consistency and sometimes optimization.

2) Decision
Accept. Key reasons: (i) a well-motivated, novel formulation of amortized MAP with an elegant affine projection ensuring LR-consistency, and (ii) solid theoretical framing plus compelling empirical evidence, especially for AffGAN.

3) Supporting arguments
Q1: Problem tackled? Addressing the underdetermined nature of SR to produce plausible HR reconstructions via amortized MAP rather than MSE, while enforcing exact LR consistency.
Q2: Motivation and placement? Strong. The affine projection is principled for linear downsampling; the MAP-as-cross-entropy view connects naturally to GANs and denoising gradients; the VI link is insightful; discussion of limits of MAP is honest and nuanced.
Q3: Do results support claims? Largely yes. Swiss-roll experiments directly validate cross-entropy minimization. The affine projection yields zero consistency error. AffGAN qualitatively outperforms MSE/MAE and soft constraints on textures and improves perceptual sharpness on faces/ImageNet. However, AffDG/AffLL underperform, and perceptual evaluation is mostly qualitative; broader metrics/user studies would strengthen claims.

4) Additional feedback (to improve the paper; not decisive for acceptance)
- Clarify exposition: earlier, crisper definition of A and A+, schematic of the projection pipeline, and algorithm boxes for AffGAN/AffDG/AffLL (with instance-noise schedule).
- Provide deeper analysis of learned networks: visualize residuals in the null space, frequency responses, feature maps, and what the discriminator focuses on.
- Add perceptual metrics (LPIPS, DISTS, FID) and, if possible, a small user study; report runtime and stability statistics; compare against strong perceptual SR baselines (e.g., SRGAN/ESRGAN, EDSR/RCAN with perceptual losses).
- Study sensitivity to misspecification of A, unknown degradations, and A+ training/fixing; ablate instance noise and projection.

Questions for authors
- How sensitive is performance to errors in A/A+ and to unknown blur/downsampling kernels?
- What is the training/inference cost versus standard SR methods?
- How does a stochastic AffGAN variant behave empirically (diversity vs plausibility)?
- Can you quantify/estimate H[q] or diversity to complement MAP-focused objectives?
- What features are learned in the residual/null-space branch; any visualizations?
- How was the instance-noise schedule chosen; how robust is AffGAN to this choice?
