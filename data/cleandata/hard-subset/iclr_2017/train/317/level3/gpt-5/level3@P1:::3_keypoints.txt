- Reframes single-image SR as amortized MAP inference instead of empirical risk minimization with pixel losses, arguing MAP aligns better with perceptual plausibility.
- Introduces an affine projection layer using A and a learned pseudoinverse A+ to guarantee LR-HR consistency (Afθ(x)=x).
- Affine projection turns the constrained MAP problem into an unconstrained maximization over projected network outputs.
- Shows amortized MAP reduces to minimizing H[qθ, pY], recasting SR as unsupervised generative modeling that in principle does not require paired LR-HR samples.
- Proposes three training strategies: AffGAN (GAN variant approximating KL[q||pY] with instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-guided using score estimates), and AffLL (density-guided using tractable continuous density models).
- Provides theoretical analysis: proof sketch linking modified GAN updates to KL minimization and a connection between stochastic AffGAN and amortized variational inference.
- Toy and real-data experiments: Swiss-roll shows AffGAN/AffDG approach MAP cross-entropy; CelebA, grass, and ImageNet (4×) show affine projection improves consistency and training speed.
- AffGAN yields the most perceptually plausible outputs among methods, producing sharp details though sometimes introducing high-frequency noise or semantic mistakes.
- Ablations demonstrate affine projection outperforms soft LR-consistency penalties and that instance noise stabilizes GAN training by ensuring support overlap.
- Decision: Accept — originality of affine projection and amortized MAP plus strong theoretical and empirical support justify acceptance.
- Motivation: SR is underdetermined and MSE biases toward blurred posterior means; MAP is a principled alternative favoring high-probability image solutions.
- Placement: situates work relative to GAN and perceptual-loss SR methods, highlighting exact LR-HR consistency versus soft constraints.
- Affine projection empirical benefits: zero LR-consistency error, faster convergence, and improved MSE/SSIM relative to unconstrained CNNs trained with MSE.
- Cross-entropy objective validated on Swiss-roll where MSE/MAE fail to reach MAP, supporting the proposed training view.
- Instance noise rationale: ensures discriminator/generator support overlap and clearly improves GAN stability in experiments.
- Honesty about limitations: authors note MAP representation dependence and atypicality issues in high dimensions.
- Rigor: derivations are clear, assumptions stated (e.g., discriminator uniqueness), and VI connection is insightful for broader impact.
- Exposition feedback: paper is dense — recommend clearer signposting, algorithm boxes for AffGAN/AffDG/AffLL, and a figure summarizing the affine projection pipeline.
- Clarify “unpaired” claim: discuss how pX would be obtained in real settings when A is unknown and robustness to A mismatch.
- Recommend reporting perceptual metrics (VGG-based distances, user studies, or classifier two-sample tests) in addition to PSNR/SSIM.
- Ablation suggestions: report instance noise schedules/magnitudes, compare to label smoothing/WGAN, test sensitivity to A+ accuracy and A mismatches, and compare deterministic vs stochastic generators.
- Representation analysis: visualize filters/feature maps, residual spectra ((I−A+A)fθ(x)), and how the network uses the null-space versus A+x.
- Diagnostics for AffDG/AffLL: visualize score fields, likelihood landscapes, and gradient norms to explain underperformance or optimization limits.
- Failure-mode analysis: catalog typical hallucinations/semantic errors and discuss mitigations such as weak content losses or conditional priors.
- Question 1: How would pX be obtained without paired data in real settings, and can A be learned if only approximately known?
- Question 2: What instance-noise annealing schedules were used per dataset, and how sensitive are results to these schedules?
- Question 3: Can you quantify stability gains from instance noise (e.g., discriminator loss behavior, collapse frequency)?
- Question 4: For AffDG, what σ values were learned and how does score quality ((fσ(y)−y)/σ2) correlate with improvements in H[qθ,pY]?
- Question 5: For AffLL, how close is the density model to pY (bits/dim vs SOTA) and do better likelihoods translate to better perceptual SR?
- Question 6: Does AffGAN’s deterministic mapping approximate the posterior mode per x, or does the KL+entropy objective favor a different solution; how sensitive is it across initializations?
- Question 7: What are training/inference runtime and memory costs versus standard SR CNNs, does affine projection add overhead, and can you release code and A/A+ parameters for reproducibility?
