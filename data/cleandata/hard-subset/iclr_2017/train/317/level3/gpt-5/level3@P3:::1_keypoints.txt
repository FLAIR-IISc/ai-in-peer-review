- Proposal: Amortized MAP inference for single-image SR via a CNN whose output is projected onto the affine subspace consistent with the LR input, guaranteeing exact LR–HR consistency.
- Training objective: Reframe training as minimizing cross-entropy H[qθ, pY], enabling (in principle) unpaired training.
- Methods: Three strategies — AffGAN (adversarial, argued to minimize KL[qθ‖pY] with instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-guided using DAE score estimates), and AffLL (maximum-likelihood density-guided baseline).
- Theory: Formalization of the affine projection and its properties.
- Theory: Analysis linking the GAN generator update under instance noise to KL minimization of smoothed divergences.
- Theory: Connection between a stochastic extension of AffGAN and amortized variational inference (Appendix F).
- Empirics: AffGAN produces visually plausible SR on grass textures, CelebA, and ImageNet up to 128×128 and enforces exact LR–HR consistency.
- Empirics: AffDG and AffLL underperform compared to AffGAN.
- Decision: Reject.
- Primary reasons for rejection: Limited empirical validation and lack of comparisons on standard SR benchmarks.
- Primary reasons for rejection: Incremental novelty over recent GAN-based SR methods (e.g., SRGAN) is unclear without strong ablations and head-to-head comparisons.
- Motivation/placement: Moving from MSE/PSNR to priors and MAP is well-motivated; affine projection is an elegant solution to hard consistency.
- Claims — theory: Theoretical derivations are clear and plausible under standard GAN assumptions, though some links (amortized VI) lack empirical validation.
- Claims — empirics: Experiments use non-standard datasets, small resolutions, limited perceptual metrics, and include examples of problematic hallucinations (snake-to-water).
- Suggested experiments: Head-to-head comparisons with SRGAN/SRResNet, ablations for hard vs soft projection, instance noise, and generator update rule.
- Suggested experiments: Demonstrate true unpaired training with independent LR and HR corpora and robustness to unknown or spatially varying A (blind SR).
- Suggested benchmarks: Evaluate on Set5/Set14/BSD100/Urban100/DIV2K with PSNR/SSIM and perceptual metrics (LPIPS, NIQE, FID) and MOS; scale beyond 128×128.
- Suggested model extensions: Test stochastic generator with latent z to produce multiple plausible HRs and quantify diversity (e.g., LPIPS).
- Suggested improvements for AffDG/AffLL: Use stronger score estimators (modern score-matching/diffusion models) and improved density models to address pathologies.
- Suggested reporting: Exact LR–HR consistency errors to show hard projection benefits over soft penalties.
- Suggested analysis: Systematic failure analysis for hallucinations/domain shifts and guidance for sensitive applications (faces, medical).
- Questions for authors (selection): What concrete gains does AffGAN provide over SRGAN beyond exact consistency and formulation? Did you run truly unpaired training and what were the results?
- Questions for authors (selection): How sensitive is performance to kernel mismatch between true A and training A, and can A/A+ be learned jointly for blind SR?
- Questions for authors (selection): Can the model process arbitrary image sizes at test time without artifacts, and what are runtime/memory characteristics for larger inputs?
- Questions for authors (selection): Did you try adding latent z in AffGAN to sample multiple HRs, and how did it affect performance and stability?
- Questions for authors (selection): Can you provide results on standard benchmarks (Set5/Set14/BSD100 4× bicubic) with PSNR/SSIM and perceptual metrics, plus an MOS study?
- Questions for authors (selection): How critical is instance noise versus other stabilization methods (e.g., gradient penalty), and please include an annealing schedule for σ.
