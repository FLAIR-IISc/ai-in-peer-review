1. Summary of contributions
The paper proposes amortized MAP inference for single-image super-resolution via a novel affine projection layer that guarantees consistency between the LR input and the generated HR output. This converts MAP learning into matching the marginal HR distribution, enabling training without explicit {LR,HR} pairs in the loss. Three practical training routes are developed: a GAN-based approach (AffGAN), denoiser-guided training, and density-guided training; AffGAN performs best. The work also introduces “instance noise” to stabilize GANs and provides theoretical links between AffGAN, KL minimization, and amortized variational inference. Overall, the framework is elegant, principled, and produces visually plausible results.

2. Decision: Reject
Key reasons: (i) Insufficient empirical validation against recent GAN-based SOTA on standard SR benchmarks; (ii) Limited scope (up to 128×128, restricted datasets) and unclear practical advantage over SRGAN-style methods.

3. Supporting arguments
- Positioning vs SOTA: While amortized MAP with an affine projection is conceptually appealing, the paper does not demonstrate clear improvements over recent GAN-based SR (e.g., SRGAN/perceptual-loss methods). No head-to-head on Set5/Set14/BSD100, no perceptual metrics (LPIPS/NIQE/PI), and no user study.
- “Unpaired” training claim: The affine projection allows training without paired supervision in the loss, but in practice pX is obtained by downsampling HR data with a known A, and A+ is learned using (synthetic) paired constraints. The paper should clarify whether this merely shifts the pairing assumption to knowing A and to A+ optimisation, and discuss realism when A is unknown/mismatched.
- Scalability: Experiments are limited to 64→16 and 128→32 setups; it remains unclear whether the approach handles larger/arbitrary resolutions and scale factors common in SR.
- AffGAN behavior: Results are plausible but sometimes noisy and semantically drift (e.g., snake→water), underscoring the need for perceptual/semantic metrics and comparisons.
- Solid theory: The affine projection and VI connection are strong and novel, but the claims would be much more convincing with broader, standardized experiments.

4. Suggestions and questions for the authors (to help improve the paper)
- Please report results on Set5/Set14/BSD100 (×2/×3/×4), with PSNR/SSIM and perceptual metrics (LPIPS, NIQE/PI), and compare to SRGAN and recent perceptual-loss SR.
- Clarify the “no pairs” claim: what data are required to estimate pX and train A+? How does the method perform if A is misspecified? Can A be learned jointly from unpaired data?
- Can the model run fully convolutionally on arbitrary image sizes at test time? What are memory/runtime characteristics for 720p/1080p?
- Did you try stochastic generators with a latent z (as motivated in Appendix F) or input noise to better capture pY|X diversity? Any ablation on instance noise and the affine projection’s necessity?
- Provide failure-case analysis and strategies to reduce high-frequency noise and semantic drift (e.g., perceptual regularizers, content losses, or posterior sampling variants).
