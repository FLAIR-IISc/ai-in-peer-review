- Proposes amortized MAP for single-image SR using a network that projects outputs onto the affine subspace consistent with the LR input, guaranteeing exact observation consistency.  
- Recasts amortized MAP as cross-entropy minimization between model HR distribution and true HR distribution.  
- Instantiates three learning mechanisms: affine-projected GAN (AffGAN), denoiser-guided (AffDG), and density-guided (AffLL).  
- Introduces “instance noise” to stabilize GAN training and links AffGAN to amortized variational inference.  
- Empirically, AffGAN produces sharp, plausible reconstructions on textures, faces, and ImageNet; affine projection improves consistency and sometimes optimization.  
- Recommendation: Accept, based on a novel, well-motivated formulation and strong theoretical framing plus compelling empirical results for AffGAN.  
- Problem addressed: tackles SR’s underdetermined nature by producing plausible HR reconstructions via amortized MAP rather than MSE, while enforcing exact LR consistency.  
- Motivation/placement: affine projection is principled for linear downsampling; MAP-as-cross-entropy links naturally to GANs and denoising gradients; VI connection is insightful; discussion of MAP limits is honest.  
- Empirical support: swiss-roll validates cross-entropy minimization; affine projection achieves zero consistency error; AffGAN qualitatively outperforms MSE/MAE and soft constraints on textures and improves perceptual sharpness.  
- Limitations in results: AffDG/AffLL underperform; evaluations are mostly qualitative—need broader metrics and user studies.  
- Clarify exposition: define A and A+ earlier, add schematic of projection pipeline, and include algorithm boxes with instance-noise schedules.  
- Analyze learned networks: visualize null-space residuals, frequency responses, feature maps, and discriminator attention.  
- Add quantitative perceptual metrics (LPIPS, DISTS, FID), a small user study, runtime and stability stats, and comparisons to strong perceptual SR baselines (SRGAN/ESRGAN, EDSR/RCAN with perceptual losses).  
- Ablations needed: sensitivity to misspecified A, unknown degradations, A+ training/fixing, and the effects of instance noise and projection.  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to errors in A/A+ and unknown blur/downsampling kernels?  
- Question: What are training and inference costs compared to standard SR methods?  
- Question: How does a stochastic AffGAN behave empirically in terms of diversity vs plausibility?  
- Question: Can you quantify or estimate H[q] or other measures of output diversity to complement MAP-focused objectives?  
- Question: What features are learned in the residual/null-space branch—any visualizations?  
- Question: How was the instance-noise schedule chosen and how robust is AffGAN to that choice?
