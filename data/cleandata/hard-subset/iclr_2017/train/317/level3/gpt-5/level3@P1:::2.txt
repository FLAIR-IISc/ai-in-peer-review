1. Summary and contributions
The paper reframes single-image super-resolution (SR) as amortised MAP inference: rather than minimising a pointwise distortion (e.g., MSE), the goal is to produce the mode of the posterior p(y|x), which lies in a high-probability region under the natural image prior and is thus more perceptually plausible. The key architectural contribution is an affine projection layer that enforces exact consistency between the output high-resolution ŷ and the input low-resolution x via Afθ(x)=x, implemented using the Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse A+ of the downsampling operator A (a strided convolution). With this, amortised MAP reduces to minimising the cross-entropy H[qθ,pY], where qθ is the marginal distribution of model outputs induced by x~pX, eliminating the need for paired LR-HR supervision.

Building on this, the paper proposes three training strategies:
- AffGAN: an affine-projected GAN that, with a specific generator objective and instance noise, approximately minimises KL[qθ‖pY], providing theoretical grounding for GAN-based SR and improved stability via instance noise.
- AffDG: denoiser-guided SR, leveraging the score estimate from a denoising autoencoder to backpropagate ∂y log pY(y) through the affine-projected SR network.
- AffLL: density-guided SR using a differentiable PixelCNN/MCGSM prior trained by maximum likelihood to optimise cross-entropy directly.

Experiments on a 2D Swiss-roll toy problem, grass textures, CelebA faces, and ImageNet show that AffGAN yields the most visually plausible results, while the affine projection itself improves training speed, guarantees input-output consistency, and can improve MSE/SSIM when used with an MSE objective.

2. Decision: Accept
Primary reasons:
- Clear, well-motivated reformulation of SR as amortised MAP with a simple, general affine projection that enforces exact LR consistency and connects SR training to unsupervised density matching.
- Convincing theoretical and empirical support, especially for AffGAN, including stability improvements (instance noise) and a novel view relating GAN training to variational inference.

3. Supporting arguments
- Problem tackled: SR is underdetermined; MSE promotes averaging over modes and yields blurry outputs. The paper targets producing plausible, high-prior-probability reconstructions by amortising MAP inference while preserving computational benefits and exact LR consistency.
- Motivation and placement in literature: The limitations of MSE/PSNR and better alignment with perceptual quality are well argued and supported by prior work. The affine projection offers a principled alternative to soft LR consistency penalties used in prior GAN-based SR. The derivations connecting the training to cross-entropy minimisation and relating the proposed GAN objective to KL are sound and contextualised against f-GAN and recent GAN stability techniques. The denoiser-guided connection to score estimation is well grounded in prior denoising literature.
- Do the results support the claims? On the toy Swiss-roll, AffGAN and AffDG approximate MAP closely by measured cross-entropy; MSE/MAE do not. The affine projection demonstrably speeds training and enforces zero LR mismatch; soft penalties leave residual inconsistencies. On textures and natural images, AffGAN produces sharper, plausible details compared to MSE baselines, with honest acknowledgment that PSNR/SSIM may be worse (expected for perceptual methods). The need for instance noise and observations about hallucination/failure cases are transparent. AffDG and AffLL underperform visually in high dimensions; the paper provides plausible explanations (score/density sharpness and training instability), though this is an area where further work would help.

4. Feedback and questions for the authors
Aimed to help improve the paper; not all affect my decision.
- Perceptual evaluation: Consider adding human studies or no-reference metrics (e.g., NIQE/BRISQUE). PSNR/SSIM are acknowledged as misaligned with perception; a complementary metric would strengthen claims.
- Comparisons: Include a direct comparison with SRGAN (Ledig et al., 2016) using identical backbones/training data, isolating the effect of the affine projection and the AffGAN objective. An ablation on instance noise vs. other stabilisation (e.g., label smoothing, gradient penalties) would strengthen the stability story.
- Generality across downsamplers: In practice, A can be unknown or mismatched. Please discuss sensitivity to A and A+ mismatch and feasibility of learning or estimating A jointly. Does the affine projection help or hurt under mild model misspecification?
- Affine projection details: Clarify the support and accuracy of the numerically estimated A+. How large are the learned kernels; how do boundary conditions and anti-aliasing affect results? Provide runtime/memory overhead and training-time stability benefits quantitatively.
- Unpaired training claim: While the framework does not require paired LR-HR data, experiments appear to simulate LR via A applied to HR. Can you demonstrate training with genuinely unpaired LR and HR collections (e.g., using different datasets), and report performance?
- Hallucinations vs. faithfulness: The “snake→water” example highlights a perceptual/faithfulness trade-off. Would adding a weak content or feature consistency term mitigate objectionable hallucinations without reintroducing blur? How would you incorporate uncertainty (e.g., stochastic AffGAN sampling) to present multiple plausible SR outputs?
- AffDG/AffLL underperformance: The score/density guidance is elegant but underwhelming experimentally. Could multi-scale DAEs, score matching at multiple noise levels, or recent autoregressive/flow priors improve AffDG/AffLL? Any diagnostics on gradient quality or mode coverage?
- Theory: The KL vs. cross-entropy distinction implies an entropy bias in AffGAN. Can you characterise how this affects MAP approximation in high dimensions? Any empirical probe of entropy of qθ with and without affine projection?
- Reproducibility: Please provide code, details on the instance noise schedule/annealing heuristic, and sensitivity analyses for GAN/discriminator capacity.

Overall, the paper is well written and clearly presented; a careful proofread to fix minor typos and formatting glitches would help. The proposed affine projection and amortised MAP perspective are valuable contributions likely to influence future SR research, and the empirical evidence for AffGAN is strong.
