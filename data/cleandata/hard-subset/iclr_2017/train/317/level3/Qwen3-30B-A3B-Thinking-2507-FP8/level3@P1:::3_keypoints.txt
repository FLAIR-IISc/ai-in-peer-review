- Proposes using amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution to avoid blurring from MSE-based averaging.  
- Argues MAP prefers high-probability natural-image solutions under an image prior.  
- Introduces a CNN architecture that projects outputs onto the affine subspace of valid SR solutions to ensure LR consistency.  
- Reformulates MAP inference as cross-entropy minimization between generated and true HR image distributions.  
- Proposes three optimization methods: affine-projected GAN (AffGAN), denoiser-guided SR (AffDG), and density-guided approach (AffLL).  
- AffGAN yields visually superior, sharper SR results across multiple datasets compared to MSE and other SR methods.  
- The paper draws a novel theoretical connection between GANs and amortized variational inference.  
- Recommends acceptance with a strong positive recommendation.  
- The affine projection layer is a clever, well-justified technical solution enabling MAP inference.  
- Empirical validation is thorough: synthetic and real datasets (Swiss-roll, textures, CelebA, ImageNet) with convincing qualitative results.  
- Theoretical sections are dense and sometimes difficult to follow; derivations need clearer intuition.  
- Paper lacks analysis of what features the learned networks capture and how they differ from MSE-trained networks.  
- Description and motivation of the instance noise technique (Section 3.2.1) need clearer practical explanation.  
- Requests clarification on: differences in features learned by AffGAN vs MSE SR, stronger perceptual quality evaluation (user study or perceptual metrics beyond PSNR/SSIM), and comparison of stochastic AffGAN to standard variational inference in performance and efficiency.
