- Proposes amortized MAP inference for single-image super-resolution using a network that projects outputs onto an affine subspace to ensure downsampled consistency with the LR input.  
- Reformulates SR as minimizing cross-entropy between the model output distribution and the natural image prior.  
- Introduces three training methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided).  
- Empirically, AffGAN yields sharper, more visually plausible results than MSE-based methods across synthetic 2D tasks, textures, faces (CelebA), and ImageNet.  
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Correctly identifies MSE-based SR’s tendency to produce blurry averages and motivates MAP as a more plausible alternative.  
- The affine-projection architecture and theoretical framing are sound and well-motivated.  
- The paper provides thorough experiments and a valuable theoretical link between AffGAN and amortized variational inference (Appendix F).  
- Writing and exposition are poor and dense, especially in Sections 3 and 4, and need substantial polishing for clarity.  
- The paper lacks analysis of the learned features; visualizations of intermediate representations comparing AffGAN and MSE models are recommended.  
- The GAN–variational inference connection should be moved from the appendix into the main text for greater impact.  
- Asks for more detail on learned AffGAN features and how they differ from MSE models.  
- Requests clarification and evidence for AffGAN’s claimed resilience to coordinate transformations.  
- Seeks comparison of the reported AffLL density log-likelihood (-4.10 bits/dim) to state-of-the-art density models and justification for its insufficiency in training.
