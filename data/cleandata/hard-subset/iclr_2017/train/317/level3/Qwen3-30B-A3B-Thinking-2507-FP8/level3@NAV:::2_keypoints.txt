- Reframes image super-resolution as amortised MAP estimation, moving beyond MSE-based empirical risk minimisation.  
- Introduces a novel neural architecture with an affine projection layer to ensure HR-LR consistency.  
- Shows the problem reduces to minimising cross-entropy between distributions, providing a rigorous theoretical foundation.  
- Proposes and evaluates three solution methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided).  
- AffGAN produces the most visually appealing SR results across toy and real-world datasets.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, including a 2D Swiss-roll toy example and tests on grass textures, CelebA, and ImageNet.  
- Contributions in Sections 3.2–3.4 appear novel for super-resolution, though GAN training aspects resemble prior work.  
- Manuscript is well-written and motivates MAP over MSE convincingly, but needs a final proofread to fix minor typos.  
- Suggest discussing computational complexity of the proposed methods versus standard SR approaches for practical context.  
- Recommend expanding the AffGAN–amortised variational inference connection (Appendix F) into the main text to highlight broader significance.  
- Overall recommendation: accept; paper makes a significant theoretical and practical contribution.
