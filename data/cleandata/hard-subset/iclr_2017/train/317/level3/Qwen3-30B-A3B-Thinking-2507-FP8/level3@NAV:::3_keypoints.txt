- Proposes a novel amortized MAP estimation method for image super-resolution to overcome MSE-induced blurriness.  
- Introduces a neural network that projects outputs onto an affine subspace of SR solutions consistent with the low-resolution input.  
- Recasts MAP inference as minimizing cross-entropy between the model output distribution and the natural image prior.  
- Proposes three solution methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (noise-assisted), and AffLL (density-assisted).  
- AffGAN yields the best perceptual quality on real-image datasets.  
- Empirical results on 2D toy, grass textures, CelebA, and ImageNet convincingly show AffGAN outperforms MSE/MAE baselines visually.  
- Provides theoretical grounding linking GANs to amortized MAP inference, offering insight into GAN-based SR success.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, noting the work is well-motivated and properly situated in related literature.  
- Main weakness is presentation: unclear exposition and difficult-to-follow theoretical sections.  
- Requests more analysis of learned features and which image statistics the model captures to explain perceptual improvements.  
- Suggests more explicit comparisons with recent perceptual loss-based methods.  
- Recommends improvements: clearer theoretical exposition, visualizations of learned features/activations, and detailed ablation studies on architectural choices.
