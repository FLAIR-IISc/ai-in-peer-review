- Proposal: Amortised MAP inference for single-image SR using an affine projection layer to enforce LR consistency, turning SR into minimizing cross-entropy with natural image statistics; three methods introduced (AffGAN, AffDG, AffLL), AffGAN performs best; theoretical links to GANs and variational inference.

- Decision: Reject — framework is theoretically sound and novel but empirical evaluation is insufficient to justify acceptance.

- Concern 1: Paper does not clearly differentiate from recent GAN-based SOTA (e.g., Ledig et al., 2016), making the novel contribution unclear.

- Concern 2: Evaluation uses custom datasets (grass textures, CelebA, ImageNet) and omits standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100), preventing fair comparison with prior work.

- Concern 3: Experiments are limited to 128×128 images with no evidence of scalability to larger or arbitrary image sizes typical in SR applications.

- Concern 4: Affine projection layer appears to require paired training data, contradicting the claim that paired data is unnecessary.

- Suggested fixes: Run comprehensive comparisons on standard SR benchmarks, demonstrate scalability to larger image sizes, clarify relationship to GAN-based SR methods, and justify advantages over simpler alternatives.

- Author question 1: How does AffGAN quantitatively and perceptually improve over Ledig et al.'s GAN-based SR?

- Author question 2: Why not include a GAN latent noise vector z to better model the distribution of plausible high-resolution images?

- Author question 3: How would the framework handle arbitrary image sizes beyond 128×128 and what modifications are needed?
