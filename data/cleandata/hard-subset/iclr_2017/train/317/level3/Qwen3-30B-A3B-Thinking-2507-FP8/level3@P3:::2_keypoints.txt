- Reframes image super-resolution as amortized MAP inference with an affine projection layer to ensure HR outputs are consistent with LR inputs.  
- Reduces the task to minimizing cross-entropy between model outputs and the natural image prior and proposes three methods: AffGAN, AffDG, AffLL.  
- Provides strong theoretical justification, including a connection between GANs and amortized variational inference.  
- Empirical results show AffGAN produces visually superior, sharper outputs compared to MSE-based baselines on both toy (Swiss-roll) and real datasets (grass, CelebA, ImageNet).  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Key strength: principled framework explaining MSE blurriness (posterior mean vs MAP mode) and the affine projection as a critical technical contribution.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and well-chosen; AffGAN is the most effective of the proposed methods.  
- Manuscript is well-written and organized but needs minor proofreading for typos.  
- Suggestion: emphasize the GAN–amortized-variational-inference connection in the conclusion and discuss AffGAN’s computational cost.  
- Questions for authors: how to address the sharpness vs high-frequency noise trade-off in AffGAN; how to reconcile MAP’s representation dependence with observed visual improvements; why AffDG/AffLL produced blurry results and whether they can be modified to improve visuals.
