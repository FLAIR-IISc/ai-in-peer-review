- Reframes image super-resolution as amortized MAP inference, moving beyond MSE-based empirical risk minimization.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces LR-HR consistency in network outputs.  
- Shows the projection reduces SR training to minimizing cross-entropy with a natural image prior, aligning SR with generative modeling.  
- Proposes and evaluates three optimization methods: AffGAN, AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided).  
- Strong theoretical motivation and clear connection of AffGAN to amortized variational inference (Appendix F).  
- Elegant architectural innovation (affine projection implemented via Moore-Penrose pseudoinverse) validated by ablation studies.  
- Thorough empirical validation on synthetic (2D Swiss-roll) and real-world datasets demonstrating practical relevance.  
- AffGAN produces the most visually appealing, perceptually superior results compared to MSE/MAE baselines.  
- Affine-projected models outperform soft-constrained alternatives in maintaining LR-HR consistency and perceptual quality.  
- Use of instance noise as a GAN training stabilizer is a valuable practical contribution.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and experimentally well-analyzed.  
- Minor issues: a few typographical errors needing final proofreading.  
- Suggested clarification: relationship between the affine projection layer and prior "consistent" SR work (e.g., Garcia 2016).  
- Suggested addition: discuss computational overhead of the affine projection layer versus standard SR networks.  
- Suggested comparison: include recent perceptual-loss SR methods (e.g., Ledig et al., 2016) for positioning.  
- Question: how sensitive is AffGAN to the choice and level of instance noise across different datasets?  
- Question: AffDG shows training stability issues—why does this occur and is annealing sufficient for all datasets?  
- Question: how does the theoretical framework account for plausible-but-incorrect reconstructions (e.g., snake→water) and how might this be mitigated?  
- Overall recommendation: accept — significant contribution addressing blurred outputs from MSE and providing a principled MAP-based alternative.
