- Paper proposes amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution using a CNN with an affine projection layer to enforce LR consistency.
- Authors replace MSE-based ERM with minimizing cross-entropy to the HR prior (MAP) and present three approximation methods: AffGAN, AffDG, AffLL.
- AffGAN produces visually superior results on textures and natural images compared to MSE and some baselines.
- Recommendation: reject.
- Major criticism: core contribution lacks sufficient novelty and resembles Ledig et al. (2016) GAN-based SR.
- Unclear advantage of the affine projection layer over existing GAN-based SR; results are not substantially better than prior work.
- Claim that the method “doesn't require paired HR-LR data” is misleading because the affine projection component (A+) is pre-trained with paired data.
- Experimental validation is inadequate and not comprehensive for a top-tier venue like ICLR.
- Datasets tested are limited (grass textures, CelebA, ImageNet) and standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) are missing.
- Quantitative metrics are limited to PSNR and SSIM, which poorly reflect perceptual quality; no perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS, FID) or statistical significance testing provided.
- Experiments use only 128×128 images, raising concerns about scalability to standard larger SR benchmarks and practical applications.
- Paper omits discussion and experiments on the GAN latent noise vector, leaving generative modeling potential underexplored.
- Theoretical foundation and affine projection motivation are sound, but these alone don’t overcome novelty and empirical shortcomings.
- Recommended improvements: evaluate on standard benchmarks and perceptual metrics, clarify benefits of affine projection, investigate latent noise, demonstrate scalability, and disclose paired-data requirements.
