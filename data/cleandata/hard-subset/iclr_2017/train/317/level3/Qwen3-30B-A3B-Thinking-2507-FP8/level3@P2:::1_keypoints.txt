- Proposes amortized MAP inference for image SR using an affine projection layer and three training methods (AffGAN, AffDG, AffLL); claims better perceptual results than MSE for challenging textures.

- Decision: Reject — strong theory but insufficient empirical validation and unclear necessity vs recent GAN SOTA.

- Missing evaluations on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100), preventing fair comparison to prior work.

- Claim of eliminating paired HR/LR training data is undermined because the affine projection layer needs paired data for numerical optimization (Appendix B).

- No clear quantitative or qualitative comparison showing improvement over recent GAN-based SR methods (e.g., Ledig et al. 2016).

- Experiments are limited to 128×128 images; scalability to typical larger SR resolutions is not addressed.

- Run experiments on standard SR benchmarks and report quantitative metrics (PSNR, SSIM) alongside qualitative results.

- Clarify the training procedure for the affine projection layer and resolve the apparent paired-data requirement.

- Provide detailed comparisons with recent GAN-based SR methods, explicitly highlighting advantages.

- Explore scalability to larger image sizes and report computational/resource requirements.

- Consider using a latent noise vector in the GAN to better model the distribution of plausible HR images.

- Question: How does AffGAN specifically improve upon Ledig et al.'s GAN-based SR, and what quantitative evidence supports this?

- Question: How does the affine projection layer's paired-data requirement reconcile with the paper's claim of removing the need for paired HR/LR data?

- Question: Have you tried a latent noise vector (beyond instance noise) to better model plausible HR images, and what were the results?

- Question: How would the framework handle larger image sizes (e.g., 512×512+), and what modifications would be necessary?
