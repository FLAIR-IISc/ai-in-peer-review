

**Review**

This paper reframes image super-resolution as amortised Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) estimation, moving beyond standard empirical risk minimisation with MSE loss that produces blurry, over-smoothed outputs. The authors introduce a novel neural network architecture with an affine projection layer ensuring consistent HR-LR dependencies, reducing the problem to minimising cross-entropy between distributions. They propose and evaluate three methods to solve this: AffGAN (using GANs), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided). The experimental results demonstrate that AffGAN yields the most visually appealing SR results across multiple datasets, including a compelling 2D toy example and real-world applications on grass textures, CelebA faces, and ImageNet.

I recommend **acceptance**. The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution by establishing a clear connection between super-resolution and MAP inference, with the affine projection layer providing an elegant solution to maintain HR-LR consistency. The experimental evaluation is thorough, with well-chosen toy examples and real-world applications demonstrating the superiority of the proposed methods, particularly AffGAN.

The paper is well-motivated, with a compelling explanation of why MAP inference would produce more plausible results than MSE-based approaches, illustrated through the 2D Swiss-roll example. The theoretical foundation is rigorous, showing how the problem reduces to cross-entropy minimisation, and the experiments support the claims with both quantitative metrics and qualitative visual results. The contributions in Sections 3.2-3.4 appear novel for super-resolution, though the GAN training aspects have similarities to prior work.

The manuscript is well-written but requires one final proofread to fix minor typos. I suggest briefly discussing the computational complexity of the proposed methods compared to standard SR approaches, as this would provide valuable practical context for readers. Additionally, the connection between AffGAN and amortised variational inference established in Appendix F is intriguing and could be expanded in the main text to highlight its broader significance for the field.
