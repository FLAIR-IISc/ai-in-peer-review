- Introduces amortized MAP inference for single-image SR with an affine projection layer that enforces LR–HR consistency and three solution methods (AffGAN, AffDG, AffLL); strong theoretical link between GANs and variational inference; AffGAN yields visually sharper results than MSE.

- Decision: Reject — contributions not sufficiently justified against recent GAN-based SOTA and critical experiments are missing.

- AffGAN resembles Ledig et al. (2016) but replaces soft constraints with an affine projection; the paper fails to clearly show what concrete advantages this provides.

- Affine projection may still require paired data (to compute A+), so the claim of eliminating paired-data requirements is not demonstrated.

- No evaluation on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) with quantitative metrics, preventing fair comparison to prior work.

- Experiments only on 128×128 images, whereas SR methods typically handle arbitrary/resizable images; scalability is not demonstrated.

- Theoretical framework is strong and results are visually promising, but lack of benchmarks and scalability tests undermine the practical contribution.

- Requested fixes: add benchmark results (PSNR/SSIM), demonstrate image-size scalability and how affine projection scales, clearly articulate advantages over soft-constraint approaches, clarify paired-data requirements for A+, explore using latent vector z in AffGAN, and compare to more recent SOTA (e.g., ESRGAN, RCAN).
