- Introduces amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution to avoid MSE-induced blurry averages and produce sharper, more plausible images.  
- Proposes a neural architecture that projects outputs to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions to ensure LR consistency.  
- Presents three solution methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided).  
- Establishes a theoretical connection between GANs and amortized variational/MAP inference.  
- Empirically shows AffGAN yields visually superior, sharper results compared to MSE/MAE methods on toy and real datasets.  
- Cross-entropy measurements quantitatively support that AffGAN and AffDG minimize the intended objective better than MSE/MAE.  
- Visual comparisons (Figures 3–5) demonstrate improved texture and natural image fidelity for AffGAN.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong theoretical contribution with clear empirical validation.  
- Writing and presentation need substantial polishing for clarity and accessibility.  
- Suggest reorganizing the paper, adding intuitive explanations (e.g., affine projection, MAP benefits), and clarifying implementation details like pseudo-inverse estimation.  
- Recommend more analysis of learned features to explain why the method works.  
- Suggest explicitly comparing theoretical justification to prior GAN-based SR work (Ledig et al., 2016).  
- Question: Why does density-guided AffLL underperform compared to AffGAN/AffDG — model limitation or general issue?  
- Question: How sensitive is AffGAN to coordinate transformations (e.g., color/feature spaces) relative to other methods?  
- Question: Can the authors provide more rigorous perceptual evaluation (human studies or perceptual metrics beyond PSNR/SSIM)?  
- Question: How does the affine projection layer affect network capacity and what features does the network learn for high-quality reconstructions?
