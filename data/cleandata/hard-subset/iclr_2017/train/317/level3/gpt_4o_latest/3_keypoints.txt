- Proposes amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution using an affine projection to ensure HR outputs match LR inputs.  
- Introduces three optimization methods: GAN-based (AffGAN), denoiser-guided SR, and density-guided SR.  
- AffGAN produces the most visually appealing results on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Presents a novel theoretical connection between GANs and variational inference.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main reason 1 for rejection: writing and presentation are unclear and dense, hindering comprehension.  
- Main reason 2 for rejection: insufficient analysis of learned network features limits interpretability.  
- Clarity issue: affine projection mechanism and its network integration lack intuitive explanation and visual aids.  
- Feature-analysis issue: paper does not examine how affine projection affects representations or why AffGAN outperforms others.  
- Empirical validation issue: comparisons limited to MSE/MAE baselines; lacks comparison to state-of-the-art SR methods.  
- Suggestion: improve writing with diagrams and intuitive explanations alongside math.  
- Suggestion: add in-depth feature analysis, visualizations, and ablation studies to explain method behavior.  
- Suggestion: compare against perceptual-loss and leading GAN-based SR methods (e.g., SRGAN/ESRGAN).  
- Suggestion: provide more detail on GAN training stability and impact of instance noise versus other techniques.  
- Questions for authors: how affine projection influences features and can be visualized?  
- Questions for authors: why AffGAN outperforms other proposed methods and when alternatives might be preferable?  
- Questions for authors: how does the method compare to SRGAN/ESRGAN in perceptual quality and efficiency?  
- Questions for authors: can the affine projection mechanism generalize to other inverse problems?
