- Paper frames image super-resolution as amortized MAP estimation with a projection step enforcing HR-LR consistency.  
- Introduces three methods to optimize the cross-entropy objective: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (maximum-likelihood prior baseline).  
- AffGAN produces the most visually appealing, perceptually superior results across datasets like CelebA and ImageNet.  
- Affine projection (architectural constraint) is a novel contribution that ensures HR-LR consistency and speeds convergence versus unconstrained models.  
- The work connects GANs to amortized variational inference, a notable theoretical contribution.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, including quantitative metrics (cross-entropy, PSNR, SSIM) and qualitative results on toy and real-world datasets.  
- Minimizing cross-entropy is presented as a theoretically grounded alternative to traditional MSE losses for SR.  
- Some GAN-specific training aspects overlap with existing literature, especially regarding stabilization techniques.  
- AffDG and AffLL underperform compared to AffGAN and would benefit from deeper analysis and potential improvements.  
- Use of instance noise for GAN stabilization is interesting but requires more detail on annealing schedule and convergence effects.  
- Minor typographical errors should be corrected in the final manuscript.  
- Questions for authors include generalization of the affine projection to other inverse problems, exploration of alternative denoisers/density models for AffDG/AffLL, and plans to empirically validate the AffGAN–amortized variational inference connection.  
- Overall decision: Accept.
