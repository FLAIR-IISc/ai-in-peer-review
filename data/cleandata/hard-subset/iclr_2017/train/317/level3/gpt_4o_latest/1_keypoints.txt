- Introduces an amortized MAP inference SR framework with an affine projection layer enforcing HR-LR consistency and reducing paired-data requirements in some training stages.
- Proposes three optimization methods (AffGAN, denoiser-guided SR, density-guided baseline), with AffGAN producing the most visually plausible outputs.
- Provides a theoretical connection between GANs and amortized variational inference.
- Decision: Reject due to insufficient empirical validation and lack of clarity in critical areas.
- Major omission: No evaluation on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100), preventing fair comparison to state-of-the-art.
- Scalability concern: Experiments are limited to 128×128 images, leaving applicability to larger, real-world images untested.
- Training challenge: The affine projection layer itself requires paired HR-LR data, casting doubt on the claimed reduced dependence on paired datasets.
- Diversity limitation: GAN architecture lacks a noise term, potentially restricting exploration of the HR image distribution and result diversity.
- Suggestion: Include evaluations on standard SR datasets (Set5, Set14, BSD100) for fair SOTA comparison.
- Suggestion: Extend experiments to larger image sizes to demonstrate real-world applicability and scalability.
- Suggestion: Investigate adding a noise vector to the GAN to better model HR distribution and increase diversity.
- Suggestion: Clarify the affine projection layer’s training procedure and how it aligns with reduced paired-data claims.
- Suggestion: Conduct ablation studies to quantify contributions of the affine projection layer and each optimization method.
- Question: Why were standard SR benchmarks omitted and how does the method compare to SOTA GAN-based SR on those datasets?
- Question: How does the framework scale to larger images, and are there computational or architectural limitations?
- Question: Was a noise term in the GAN explored; if so, what were the findings, and if not, why was it omitted?
- Question: Can the affine projection layer be trained without paired HR-LR datasets; please elaborate on the training process and practical implications.
- Conclusion: Novel and theoretically strong work but limited immediate impact due to lack of comprehensive empirical validation and clarity; addressing these issues could substantially strengthen the paper.
