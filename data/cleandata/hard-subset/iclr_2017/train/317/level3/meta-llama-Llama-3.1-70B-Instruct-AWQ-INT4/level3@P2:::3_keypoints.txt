- Proposes an amortized MAP estimation method for super-resolution that trains a network to project onto an affine subspace of solutions consistent with the low-resolution input.  
- Supports multiple solution-finding strategies: GAN-based, noise-assisted, and density-assisted optimization.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results across several datasets, showing method effectiveness.  
- Recommendation: accept for publication based on contributions and results.  
- Key contribution: a novel amortized MAP approach for super-resolution.  
- Evidence: impressive empirical performance and evaluations.  
- Paper includes theoretical foundations, empirical evaluations, and discussion of limitations and future directions.  
- Writing is generally clear but can be hard to follow; needs more intuitive explanations.  
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis of what the learned networks capture and how those features relate to SR.  
- Suggestion: clarify and more explicitly compare the relationship to existing methods.  
- Suggestion: add more visualizations of SR outputs and learned network behavior.  
- Question: provide implementation details and performance impact of the affine projection layer.  
- Question: how are GAN hyperparameters chosen and how sensitive are results to them?  
- Question: discuss potential applications beyond SR, e.g., other inverse problems or image-processing tasks.
