- Introduces a novel amortised MAP inference framework for image super-resolution using affine projections combined with GANs.  
- Affine projection layer enforces that high-resolution outputs are consistent with the low-resolution input, enabling training without paired images in principle.  
- Proposes three amortised MAP methods: GANs (AffGAN), denoiser-guided SR, and a maximum-likelihood image prior baseline.  
- Provides theoretical analysis and a connection between GANs and amortised variational inference.  
- New neural architecture that projects outputs to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions.  
- Experiments show AffGAN produces the most visually appealing results, especially on natural images.  
- Affine projection does not harm CNN SR performance and can improve it.  
- Lacks clear comparison with recent SOTA GAN-based SR methods, making added value unclear.  
- Affine projection appears to be trained with paired HR-LR images, which may not eliminate the need for pairs as claimed.  
- Experiments are limited to 128×128 images; scalability to larger or arbitrary SR sizes is untested.  
- No evaluations on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) for fair comparison with prior work.  
- Unclear whether a GAN latent noise vector was used or tested to better model output distributions.  
- Requests more implementation details (affine projection, GAN architecture, training protocols) and further experiments to justify necessity.  
- Paper is well-written, theory is solid, and the GAN–variational inference connection is an interesting contribution.
