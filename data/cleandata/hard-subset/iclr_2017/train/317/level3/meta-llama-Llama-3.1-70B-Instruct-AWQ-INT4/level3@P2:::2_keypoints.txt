- Novelty: Reframes image super-resolution as amortised MAP estimation and introduces a projection step to enforce HR-LR consistency.
- Key technical contribution: Affine/projection step that maintains consistent high- and low-resolution dependencies, shown to improve results.
- Methods compared: Proposes and evaluates three solutions to the cross-entropy problem in Eq. (9): GANs (AffGAN), denoiser-guided SR, and a maximum-likelihood-trained prior baseline.
- Main empirical finding: The GAN-based (AffGAN) approach yields the most visually appealing super-resolved images.
- Experiments: Thorough evaluation on a 2D toy problem, grass textures, CelebA, and ImageNet demonstrating method effectiveness.
- Acceptance rationale: Paper is novel, effective, well-written, and supported by sufficient experiments.
- Motivation: Addresses limitations of empirical risk minimisation (blurry/over-smoothed outputs) with a principled MAP approach.
- Suggestions (minor): Manuscript needs another proofread to fix minor typos.
