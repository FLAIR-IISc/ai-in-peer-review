- Proposes an amortized MAP estimation method for image super-resolution using a neural network projecting onto an affine subspace consistent with the LR input.  
- Introduces three optimization variants: GAN-based, noise-assisted, and density-assisted.  
- Empirical results across toy and real datasets demonstrate effectiveness of the proposed methods.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Approach is well-motivated and scientifically rigorous, addressing desire for high-posterior SR solutions.  
- Affine subspace projection is a novel contribution ensuring HR outputs remain consistent with LR inputs.  
- GAN-based method performs best on real image data.  
- Affine-projected models generally achieve lower cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] than soft-constrained versions.  
- Writing and exposition could be clearer—the theory section is dense and may be hard to follow.  
- Paper lacks deeper analysis of the learned networks (features learned and relation to image prior).  
- Needs more experimental details (hyperparameters per method and computational resources).  
- Requests from reviewers: clarify affine projection mechanism, explain learned network features relative to the image prior, and provide detailed experimental/hardware setup.
