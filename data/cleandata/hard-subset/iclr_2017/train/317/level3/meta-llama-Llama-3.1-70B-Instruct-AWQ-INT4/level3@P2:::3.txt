**Summary**

This paper proposes an amortized MAP estimation method for super-resolution (SR) that trains a neural network to project onto an affine subspace of SR solutions consistent with the low-resolution (LR) input. The authors support finding solutions via multiple strategies, including GAN-based, noise-assisted, and density-assisted optimization. They demonstrate empirical results across several datasets, showcasing the effectiveness of their approach.

**Decision**

Based on the content and contributions of the paper, I recommend accepting this paper for publication. The main reasons for this decision are:

1. **Novel approach**: The paper proposes a novel amortized MAP estimation method for SR, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Empirical results**: The authors demonstrate impressive empirical results across several datasets, showcasing the effectiveness of their approach.

**Supporting arguments**

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including theoretical foundations and empirical evaluations. The authors also discuss the limitations of their approach and potential future directions.

The writing and presentation of the paper are clear, but sometimes hard to follow due to the complex subject matter. The authors could benefit from polishing their writing and providing more intuitive explanations of their methods.

**Additional feedback**

To further improve the paper, I suggest the following:

1. **Provide more analysis of the learned networks**: It would be interesting to see what features the networks learn and how they relate to the SR task.
2. **Clarify the relationship between the proposed method and existing work**: While the authors discuss related work, it would be helpful to provide a more detailed comparison of their approach with existing methods.
3. **Consider providing more visualizations**: Additional visualizations of the SR results and the learned networks could help to better understand the proposed method and its effectiveness.

**Questions for the authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how the affine projection layer is implemented and how it affects the performance of the network?
2. How do you choose the hyperparameters for the GAN-based optimization method, and what is the sensitivity of the results to these hyperparameters?
3. Can you discuss the potential applications of the proposed method beyond SR, such as in other inverse problems or image processing tasks?

Overall, this is a well-written paper that proposes a novel approach to SR. With some minor revisions to address the above comments, it has the potential to make a significant impact in the field.
