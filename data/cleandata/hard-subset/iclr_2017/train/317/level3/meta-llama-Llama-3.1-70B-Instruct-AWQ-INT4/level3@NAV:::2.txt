**Paper Review: AMORTISED MAP INFERENCE FOR IMAGE SUPER-RESOLUTION**

**Summary:**
This paper proposes a novel approach to image super-resolution (SR) by reframing it as amortized Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) estimation. The authors introduce a projection step to maintain consistent high-resolution (HR) and low-resolution (LR) dependencies, which leads to improved results experimentally. They also propose and evaluate three methods to solve the cross-entropy problem in the context of SR.

**Decision:**
Accept

**Reasons for Acceptance:**

1. The paper presents a well-motivated and novel approach to SR, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of their approach, including a connection to amortized variational inference.
3. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, particularly the Generative Adversarial Network (GAN)-based approach.

**Supporting Arguments:**

* The paper provides a clear and concise introduction to the problem of SR and the limitations of current approaches.
* The authors present a well-structured and easy-to-follow theoretical analysis of their approach, including a detailed derivation of the cross-entropy problem.
* The experimental results are thorough and well-presented, including a comparison of the proposed methods on various datasets.
* The paper includes a discussion of the limitations of the approach and potential future directions, which demonstrates the authors' awareness of the potential challenges and opportunities.

**Additional Feedback:**

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the relationship between the proposed approach and other existing methods for SR.
* The authors may want to consider including more visual examples of the results, particularly for the GAN-based approach.
* The paper assumes a certain level of background knowledge in machine learning and SR, which may make it challenging for non-experts to follow. Consider adding more introductory material or explanations to make the paper more accessible.

**Questions for the Authors:**

* Can you provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed methods, particularly in comparison to other existing approaches?
* How do you plan to address the potential issue of mode collapse in the GAN-based approach?
* Can you discuss the potential applications of the proposed approach beyond SR, such as image denoising or deblurring?
