- Novel formulation: reframes super-resolution as amortised MAP estimation, a well-motivated and original approach.  
- Projection step: introduces a projection to enforce consistent HR–LR dependencies and shows empirical improvement.  
- Methods evaluated: proposes and tests three solutions to the cross-entropy problem—GANs, denoiser-guided SR, and an ML-trained image-prior baseline.  
- Experimental design: experiments are thorough and well-chosen, including toy examples and real-world tasks.  
- Results: GAN-based training produces the most visually appealing outputs and is likely relevant for future SR work.  
- Acceptance rationale: paper accepted because of its novel approach and sufficient, well-designed experiments.  
- Presentation quality: paper is well-written with clear explanations and connections to prior work.  
- Minor issues: manuscript needs another proofread to fix typos.  
- References: some citations should be updated to include more recent GAN and SR literature.  
- Open questions: request comparisons to other recent SR methods (e.g., deep nets or alternative losses).  
- Analysis requests: ask for more insight into why GANs outperform other methods and how instance noise helps, plus discussion of potential limitations and future fixes.
