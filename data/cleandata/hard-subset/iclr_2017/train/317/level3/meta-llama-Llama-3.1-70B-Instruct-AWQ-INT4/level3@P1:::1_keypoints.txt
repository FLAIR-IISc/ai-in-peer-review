- Paper proposes a novel amortized MAP inference framework for image super-resolution with a CNN that projects outputs onto an affine subspace to ensure LR-HR consistency and three MAP approximations (GAN, denoiser-guided, ML prior); GAN performs best on real images.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Novelty/impact: Framework combines MAP estimation benefits with amortized inference efficiency and could advance SR state-of-the-art.

- Theoretical soundness: Solid theoretical foundation, derivation of affine projection layer, and link to GANs provided.

- Experiments: Promising results across multiple datasets, with strongest performance from the GAN-based method.

- Clarify contribution vs recent GAN-based SOTA: Need more discussion on what the framework adds over existing GAN SR methods.

- Evaluation on standard benchmarks: Include results on Set5, Set14, BSD100 for fair comparison with prior work.

- Scalability: Discuss how the method scales to larger or arbitrary image sizes common in SR.

- GAN latent noise: Clarify whether a GAN noise latent vector was used or tested to better model output distributions.

- Affine projection: Provide more discussion on limitations and possible extensions of the affine projection layer.

- Denoiser-guided SR: Give more implementation/details and explicitly connect it to the MAP estimate.

- Baseline ML-prior method: Discuss limitations, implementation details, and potential extensions of the maximum-likelihood-trained image prior baseline.

- Question — affine projection & GAN: Please expand on the connection between the affine projection layer and the GAN framework.

- Question — large images: How does the framework handle larger/arbitrary SR image sizes?

- Question — latent vector experiments: Did you try using a GAN noise latent vector to better model output distributions?
