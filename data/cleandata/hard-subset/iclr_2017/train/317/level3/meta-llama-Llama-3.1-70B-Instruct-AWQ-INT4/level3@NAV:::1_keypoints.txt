- Proposes amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution using a CNN that projects outputs to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions, ensuring LR consistency.  
- Introduces three MAP-approximation methods: GANs, denoiser-guided SR, and an ML-trained image prior baseline.  
- Shows effectiveness across datasets, with GAN-based method producing the most visually appealing results.  
- Decision: Accept with Minor Revisions.  
- Concern: Unclear what the approach adds over recent GAN-based SOTA—needs more detailed comparison.  
- Concern: Necessity of the affine projection layer is unclear; it may simply transfer the need for paired HR-LR data to the projection.  
- Request: Provide more analysis or experiments to justify the affine projection layer.  
- Positive: The use of amortized MAP inference is novel and well-motivated.  
- Positive: Experiments are thorough and results are promising.  
- Suggestion: Evaluate on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100) for fair comparison with prior work.  
- Suggestion: Analyze limitations and performance on larger or arbitrary image sizes typical in SR.  
- Question: Provide more details on necessity of the affine projection layer and comparisons to approaches without it.  
- Question: How will the authors address the affine projection limitation and extend the framework to other restoration tasks (denoising, deblurring)?
