This paper reframes the super-resolution (SR) problem as amortised Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) estimation, which is a novel and well-motivated approach. The authors introduce a projection step to maintain consistent high-resolution (HR) and low-resolution (LR) dependencies, showing improved results experimentally. They propose and evaluate three methods to solve the cross-entropy problem in Eq. (9), including Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), denoiser-guided SR, and a baseline method using a maximum-likelihood-trained image prior.

I accept this paper, with two key reasons for this choice: (1) the paper presents a well-motivated and novel approach to the SR problem, and (2) the experiments are sufficient and well-designed to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.

The paper is well-written and presented, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and their connections to prior work. The experiments are thorough and well-chosen, including toy examples and real-world applications. The findings indicate that GAN-based training yields the most visually appealing results, which will be relevant for future SR work.

However, I do have some minor suggestions for improvement. The manuscript could benefit from another proofread to fix a few typos. Additionally, some of the references could be updated to include more recent work on GANs and SR.

To improve the paper, the authors could consider addressing the following questions:

* How does the proposed approach compare to other recent methods for SR, such as those using deep neural networks or alternative loss functions?
* Can the authors provide more insights into why the GAN-based method performs better than the other two methods, and how the instance noise technique contributes to its success?
* Are there any potential limitations or drawbacks to the proposed approach, and how might they be addressed in future work?

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of SR. With some minor revisions, it has the potential to be even more impactful and influential.
