- Proposes amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution with a CNN that projects outputs onto an affine subspace to ensure LR consistency and three optimization methods (GAN, denoiser-guided, density-guided).  
- Demonstrates methods on a 2D toy problem, grass textures, CelebA faces, and ImageNet natural images.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Novelty/impact: Introduces a new approach with potential to improve state-of-the-art SR.  
- Technical soundness: Provides a solid theoretical foundation and a novel CNN architecture.  
- Optimization methods: Presents three well-motivated, technically sound optimization approaches (GAN, denoiser-guided, density-guided).  
- Empirical results: Convincing experimental results across multiple datasets.  
- Theoretical clarity: Clear explanation of amortized MAP inference applied to SR.  
- Architecture quality: CNN design is well-explained and appears well-constructed.  
- Writing/presentation: Needs substantial polishing to improve accessibility.  
- Analysis of learned models: Paper should include more analysis/visualizations of learned filters and feature maps.  
- Comparisons: Requires more comprehensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art SR methods.  
- Question — robustness: How do the methods handle noisy or poor-quality LR inputs?  
- Question — interpretability: Can authors provide deeper insights into learned features and representations?  
- Question — efficiency: How do the methods compare to SOTA in computational complexity and runtime efficiency?
