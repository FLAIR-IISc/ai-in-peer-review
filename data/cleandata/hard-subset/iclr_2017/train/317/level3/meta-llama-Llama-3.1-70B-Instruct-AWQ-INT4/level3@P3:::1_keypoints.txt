- Introduces a novel amortized MAP inference method combined with GANs for image super-resolution that produces plausible SR results.  
- Uses an affine projection layer to guarantee the high-resolution output is consistent with the low-resolution input.  
- Proposes three optimization approaches: GAN-based, denoiser-guided SR, and a maximum-likelihood-trained image prior baseline.  
- Well-motivated paper with clear explanation of current SR limitations and benefits of MAP inference; literature placement and related-work discussion are thorough.  
- Experimental results show the GAN-based approach yields visually appealing outputs and include comparisons and analysis against other methods.  
- Unclear what concrete advantages the method offers over recent GAN-based state-of-the-art approaches.  
- Affine projection layer requires paired HR–LR training data, which may limit practical applicability.  
- Experiments are limited to 128×128 images, leaving it unclear whether the framework scales to larger or arbitrary SR image sizes.  
- Paper does not clarify whether a GAN latent noise vector was used or tested to better model output distributions.  
- Accepted because of the novel amortized MAP+GAN approach and thorough empirical evaluation.  
- Requests: clearly explain advantages over recent GAN SOTA, clarify affine projection applicability and pairing requirement, evaluate on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100).  
- Requests continued: investigate handling of larger/arbitrary image sizes and consider using a GAN latent noise vector to better model distributions.  
- Questions for authors: provide details on affine projection training and its performance impact.  
- Questions for authors: compare the proposed method to other MAP inference approaches (e.g., variational inference, MCMC).  
- Questions for authors: supply experimental details—generator/discriminator architectures, training procedure, and evaluation metrics.
