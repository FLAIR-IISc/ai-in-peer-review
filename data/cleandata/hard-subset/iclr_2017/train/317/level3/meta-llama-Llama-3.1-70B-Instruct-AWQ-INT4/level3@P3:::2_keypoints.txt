- Novel approach: reframes image super-resolution as amortized MAP estimation.
- Introduces an affine projection step to maintain consistent HR–LR dependencies.
- Proposes and evaluates three solutions to the cross-entropy problem: GANs, denoiser-guided SR, and an ML-trained image prior baseline.
- Experimental result: GAN-based method produces the most visually appealing outputs.
- Decision: accept the paper based on novelty and strong evaluation.
- Strength: well-motivated, clearly written, and easy to follow.
- Strength: thorough and well-explained theoretical analysis (Section 3).
- Strength: affine projections are a clever idea that improves results.
- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation across datasets and metrics (Section 5).
- Requested improvement: provide more implementation details for the affine projection layer and its integration.
- Requested improvement: strengthen experiments with more baselines and additional datasets.
- Requested improvement: discuss limitations, especially computational complexity and training stability.
- Questions: specify GAN hyperparameters (learning rate, batch size).
- Questions: how will you address potential GAN mode collapse?
- Questions: discuss potential applications beyond SR (e.g., denoising, deblurring).
- Overall: strong paper with minor revisions and more experiments could make it top-tier.
