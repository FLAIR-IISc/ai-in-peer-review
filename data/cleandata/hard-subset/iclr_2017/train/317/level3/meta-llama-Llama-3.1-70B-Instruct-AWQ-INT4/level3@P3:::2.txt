**Summary of the Paper**

This paper presents a novel approach to image super-resolution (SR) by reframing it as amortized MAP estimation. The authors introduce a projection step to maintain consistent high-resolution (HR) and low-resolution (LR) dependencies, which leads to improved results experimentally. They propose and evaluate three methods to solve the cross-entropy problem, including Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), denoiser-guided SR, and a baseline method using a maximum-likelihood-trained image prior. The experiments demonstrate that the GAN-based approach yields the most visually appealing results.

**Decision**

I decide to accept this paper. The main reasons for this choice are:

1. The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to image super-resolution, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis and experimental evaluation of their methods, which demonstrates their effectiveness.

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper is well-written and presented, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand their contributions. The introduction provides a clear motivation for the work, highlighting the limitations of current SR methods and the potential benefits of using amortized MAP estimation.

The theoretical analysis in Section 3 is thorough and well-explained, providing a solid foundation for the proposed methods. The authors' use of affine projections to maintain consistent HR-LR dependencies is a clever idea that leads to improved results.

The experimental evaluation in Section 5 is comprehensive and well-designed, covering a range of datasets and evaluation metrics. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, particularly the GAN-based approach.

**Additional Feedback**

To further improve the paper, I suggest the following:

* The authors could provide more details on the implementation of the affine projection layer and how it is integrated with the SR network.
* The experimental evaluation could be strengthened by including more baseline methods and evaluating the proposed methods on additional datasets.
* The authors could discuss the potential limitations and challenges of their approach, particularly in terms of computational complexity and training stability.

**Questions for the Authors**

* Can you provide more details on the choice of hyperparameters for the GAN-based approach, particularly the learning rate and batch size?
* How do you plan to address the potential issue of mode collapse in the GAN-based approach?
* Can you discuss the potential applications of your approach beyond image super-resolution, such as image denoising or deblurring?

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of image super-resolution. With some minor revisions and additional experimentation, it has the potential to be a top-tier publication.
