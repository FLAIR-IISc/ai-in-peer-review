**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

The paper proposes a novel approach for image super-resolution (SR) using amortized Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference. The authors introduce a convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture that projects the model output onto an affine subspace of valid SR solutions, ensuring consistency with the low-resolution (LR) input. They then present three methods to optimize this architecture: Generative Adversarial Networks (GAN), denoiser-guided SR, and density-guided SR. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of these methods on various datasets, including a 2D toy problem, grass textures, CelebA faces, and natural images from ImageNet.
