**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

This paper introduces a novel amortized MAP inference framework for image super-resolution (SR), which combines the benefits of Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) estimation with the efficiency of amortized inference. The authors propose a convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture that projects the model output to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions, ensuring that the high-resolution output is always consistent with the low-resolution input. They then present three methods to approximate the MAP estimate: Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), denoiser-guided SR, and a baseline method using a maximum-likelihood-trained image prior. The experimental results show that the GAN-based approach performs best on real image data.

**Decision: Accept with Minor Revisions**

I recommend accepting this paper with minor revisions. The paper presents a novel framework for image SR, which is well-motivated and grounded in theory. The experimental results are promising, and the authors have made a good effort to evaluate their method on various datasets. However, I have some minor concerns and suggestions for improvement, which I outline below.

**Supporting Arguments for the Decision**

1. **Novelty and Impact**: The paper presents a novel amortized MAP inference framework for image SR, which combines the benefits of MAP estimation with the efficiency of amortized inference. This framework has the potential to improve the state-of-the-art in image SR.
2. **Theoretical Soundness**: The paper provides a solid theoretical foundation for the proposed framework, including a derivation of the affine projection layer and the connection to GANs.
3. **Experimental Results**: The experimental results are promising, and the authors have made a good effort to evaluate their method on various datasets.

**Minor Concerns and Suggestions for Improvement**

1. **Clarity on the Contribution**: While the paper presents a novel framework, it is not entirely clear what it adds over recent GAN-based state-of-the-art methods. The authors should provide more discussion on this point.
2. **Evaluation on Standard Benchmarks**: The paper should include evaluations on standard SR benchmarks (e.g., Set5, Set14, BSD100) to facilitate fair comparison with prior work.
3. **Scalability**: The paper should discuss the scalability of the framework to larger/arbitrary image sizes typical in SR.
4. **GAN Noise Latent Vector**: The authors should clarify whether a GAN noise latent vector was used or tried to better model distributions.

**Additional Feedback**

1. **Affine Projection**: The authors should provide more discussion on the affine projection layer, including its limitations and potential extensions.
2. **Denoiser-Guided SR**: The authors should provide more details on the denoiser-guided SR method, including its connection to the MAP estimate.
3. **Baseline Method**: The authors should provide more discussion on the baseline method using a maximum-likelihood-trained image prior, including its limitations and potential extensions.

**Questions for the Authors**

1. Can you provide more discussion on the connection between the affine projection layer and the GAN framework?
2. How does the proposed framework handle larger/arbitrary image sizes typical in SR?
3. Did you try using a GAN noise latent vector to better model distributions?
