- Proposes a novel amortised MAP framework for single-image super-resolution to avoid MSE-induced blur.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer to enforce exact LR-HR consistency by projecting outputs into the affine subspace defined by the downsampling operator.  
- Recasts SR training as generative modelling (cross-entropy minimization), enabling training without paired LR-HR examples for the main generator.  
- Explores three solution methods: AffGAN (GAN-guided), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (likelihood-guided), with AffGAN performing best.  
- Provides theoretical motivation linking the GAN objective to KL-divergence minimization and MAP inference.  
- Proposes "instance noise" as a practical technique to stabilize GAN training.  
- Decision: Weak Accept.  
- Main positive: elegant, principled formulation and strong theoretical contributions (affine projection, MAP rationale).  
- Main negative: empirical evaluation is insufficient to prove superiority over existing GAN-SR methods.  
- Lacks experiments on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100), making comparisons to prior work unclear.  
- Omits standard quantitative metrics (PSNR, SSIM) and rigorous perceptual evaluations (user studies or established perceptual metrics).  
- Experiments are limited to relatively small images (up to 128×128); scaling to larger, arbitrary sizes is untested.  
- Praises the "Criticism and Future Directions" section for acknowledging MAP limitations and advocating posterior sampling work.  
- Suggests an ablation comparing instance noise to other GAN stabilization methods (e.g., one-sided label smoothing).  
- Requests clarification/comparisons: practical advantage of hard affine projection vs SRGAN's soft content loss; sensitivity to mismatched downsampling operator A and pseudo-inverse A+; and whether a latent noise z was tried to capture multimodal posteriors.
