- Novel framework: reformulates single-image SR as amortised MAP inference with an affine projection layer enforcing LR-HR consistency.  
- Affine projection reduces the learning objective to matching the generated HR distribution to the real HR distribution, enabling training without strictly paired data.  
- Proposes three solution methods; the GAN-based approach (AffGAN) produces the most perceptually convincing results.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: provides principled justification for using GANs in SR by connecting MAP inference and generative modeling.  
- Decision: Weak Accept (borderline) — novelty and theory are compelling but empirical evidence is insufficient.  
- Main empirical weakness: no evaluations on standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100), preventing fair comparison to prior work and SOTA methods like SRGAN.  
- Unclear practical advantage over GANs trained with soft consistency penalties (e.g., L1/MSE) since direct comparisons to external baselines are missing.  
- Experiments are limited to small 128×128 images; scalability and computational feasibility on larger, practical resolutions are not addressed.  
- Generator appears deterministic; authors should clarify whether a latent noise vector z was used to model the multimodal posterior and generate diverse details.  
- Question: clarify whether learning the pseudo-inverse A+ is entirely unsupervised or requires paired data/assumptions that reintroduce dependency on paired HR-LR data.  
- Request: provide quantitative and qualitative results on standard benchmarks (Set5, Set14) to enable fair comparison and validate practical impact.
