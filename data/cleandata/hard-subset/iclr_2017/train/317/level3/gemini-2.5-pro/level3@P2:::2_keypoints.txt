- Frames single-image super-resolution as amortised MAP inference to avoid MSE-induced blurriness.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces exact consistency between downsampled output and input.  
- Shows amortised MAP with the architecture is equivalent to minimizing cross-entropy between generated and real HR image distributions.  
- Proposes and evaluates three solutions to the cross-entropy objective: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (explicit density model).  
- Empirically demonstrates AffGAN produces visually sharp, perceptually superior results on textures, faces, and ImageNet.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: principled reframing from MSE to MAP with a sound derivation linking to cross-entropy minimization.  
- Affine projection layer is a significant, practical architectural advance that enforces data consistency and accelerates training.  
- Experiments validate the affine layer improving MSE and SSIM even under MSE loss, showing general applicability.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation including a 2D Swiss-roll toy that illustrates conceptual differences between MSE and MAP.  
- Honest reporting of negative results: AffDG and AffLL failed to produce sharp images, and this is acknowledged.  
- AffGAN qualitative results are convincing, producing plausible high-frequency detail though not pixel-perfect matches.  
- Introduces instance noise as an effective GAN stabilization technique used in ImageNet experiments.  
- Suggested improvements: proofreading to fix minor typos and grammar.  
- Suggested improvements: expand discussion (from appendix) on why AffDG and AffLL failed to produce sharp images.  
- Suggested improvements: compare instance noise to other GAN stabilization methods in main experiments.  
- Suggested improvements: include a small qualitative example of stochastic AffGAN to illustrate diverse outputs for a single LR input.  
- Questions for authors: clarify failure modes of AffDG and AffLL—optimization vs. fundamental guidance limitations.  
- Questions for authors: report sensitivity of AffGAN training to instance noise hyperparameters and annealing schedule across datasets.  
- Questions for authors: provide intuition why MAP modes for natural image posteriors appear perceptually typical rather than atypical.
