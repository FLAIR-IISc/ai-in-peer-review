- Proposes amortised MAP inference for single-image SR to avoid MSE-induced blurring and produce high-probability HR solutions.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces exact data consistency between HR outputs and LR inputs.  
- Reformulates the training objective as minimizing cross-entropy between the network output distribution and the true HR image distribution.  
- Explores three optimization strategies: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided).  
- Extensive experiments (Swiss-roll toy, textures, faces, natural images) demonstrate the framework's effectiveness; AffGAN yields the sharpest, most perceptually convincing results.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novel and well-motivated framework; affine projection is a principled improvement over prior soft-penalty approaches.  
- Empirical validation is thorough and systematic, providing strong evidence and practical insights across datasets.  
- Affine projection alone improves performance even under standard MSE training (Section 5.2 proof-of-concept).  
- Swiss-roll experiment effectively validates that methods minimize the intended cross-entropy in a measurable setting.  
- Paper presentation is dense; Section 3 (derivation to Eq.9) needs clearer intuition and step-by-step explanation.  
- Recommend a summary table comparing requirements, pros/cons of AffGAN, AffDG, and AffLL, plus editorial cleanup for readability.  
- Suggest more analysis of learned representations (e.g., visualize residual (I−A+A)f_theta(x) and discriminator activations) to explain why the method works.  
- Request clarification on instance-noise annealing schedule used in ImageNet experiments and sensitivity to its parameters.  
- Ask for hypotheses on why AffDG and AffLL produce blurry results on real images — optimization issues or inaccurate auxiliary gradient/likelihood estimates?  
- Ask for comparison of computational costs: pretraining denoiser/density model vs end-to-end AffGAN training.  
- Ask about robustness to numerical pseudo-inverse A+ accuracy and impact of using simpler upsampling kernels (e.g., bilinear, nearest) in the affine projection.
