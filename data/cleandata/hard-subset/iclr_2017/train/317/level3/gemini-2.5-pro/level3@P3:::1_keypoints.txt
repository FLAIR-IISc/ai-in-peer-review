- Reframes single-image super-resolution as amortized MAP inference to avoid blurring from MSE averaging over multimodal posteriors.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer to enforce hard data-consistency that the downsampled output matches the LR input.  
- Shows that with the affine projection, MAP inference reduces to matching generated HR distribution to real HR distribution (cross-entropy), enabling generative-model training.  
- Proposes and evaluates three optimization strategies: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided); AffGAN yields the best visual results.  
- Decision: Accept, based on novel theoretical framing and architectural contribution, but empirical validation needs strengthening.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical motivation and principled alternative to ad-hoc perceptual losses for SR.  
- Strength: Affine projection is a significant technical contribution that restricts solutions to valid HR images and allows unpaired training.  
- Weakness: Insufficient empirical validation and lack of rigorous, comparative experiments to substantiate practical benefits.  
- Weakness: No direct comparison to SRGAN or other contemporary GAN-based state-of-the-art methods.  
- Weakness: Experiments omit standard SR benchmarks (Set5, Set14, BSD100), preventing fair quantitative comparison.  
- Weakness: Experiments are limited to small images (e.g., 32x32→128x128); scalability and GAN stability at larger resolutions are not demonstrated.  
- Negative result: AffDG and AffLL failed empirically (produced blurry outputs); more analysis of these failures is needed.  
- Suggestion: Explore and provide preliminary results for the stochastic generator variant to model multimodal HR outputs and avoid MAP mode collapse.  
- Suggestion: Discuss limitations when the downsampling kernel A is unknown (blind SR) and potential extensions to that setting.  
- Questions for authors: clarify practical advantages of affine projection vs. soft perceptual/content losses, confirm dependence on known A, explain omission of standard benchmarks, comment on scaling to larger resolutions, and report any experiments with the stochastic generator.
