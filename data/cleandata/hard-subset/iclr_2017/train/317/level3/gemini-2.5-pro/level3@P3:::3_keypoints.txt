- Problem: MSE-based SR yields perceptually blurry results; authors reframe single-image SR as amortized MAP inference to enforce plausibility under a natural-image prior.
- Contribution 1: Novel network architecture with an affine projection layer that enforces LR-HR consistency by projecting outputs onto the affine subspace satisfying x = A*y_hat.
- Contribution 2: Reformulation showing amortized MAP inference reduces to minimizing cross-entropy between the network output distribution and the true HR image distribution.
- Contribution 3: Development and comparison of three optimization strategies: affine-projected GAN (AffGAN), denoiser-guided (AffDG), and density-guided (AffLL).
- Empirical validation: Strong results on Swiss-roll toy, textures, CelebA, and ImageNet; AffGAN produces the sharpest, most perceptually convincing outputs.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Theoretical novelty and principled framework (MAP + affine projection) that directly enforces the data-consistency constraint.
- Key reason 2: Convincing empirical improvements, especially AffGAN’s ability to generate plausible high-frequency details over MSE baselines.
- Strengths: Elegant derivation connecting MAP to cross-entropy and comprehensive comparison of three methods demonstrating GAN superiority in high dimensions.
- Weakness: Dense, hard-to-follow exposition with important implementation details (e.g., numerical pseudo-inverse A+) relegated to the appendix.
- Suggestion 1: Improve clarity and pedagogy of theoretical development and move core affine-projection implementation details into the main text.
- Suggestion 2: Provide analysis/visualization of the learned residual (I - A+A)f_theta(x) to understand what high-frequency details the generator adds.
- Suggestion 3: Expand discussion of why AffDG and AffLL failed on real images (e.g., denoiser gradient accuracy, optimization or density-model issues).
- Question 1: Are AffDG/AffLL failures fundamental to denoiser/density-based score estimation in high dimensions or primarily due to optimization/training choices?
- Question 2: How sensitive is final image quality to numerical approximation of A+; did learning A+ end-to-end help or hurt?
- Question 3: How might one guide MAP-based models toward semantically correct but lower-probability reconstructions in ambiguous cases (e.g., snake → water example)?
