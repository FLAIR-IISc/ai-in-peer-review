- Frames single-image super-resolution as amortised MAP inference to generate outputs consistent with LR input and likely under the natural image prior.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces LR–HR consistency mathematically.  
- Proposes and evaluates three methods to minimise cross-entropy: AffGAN (GAN-based), denoiser-guided (AffDG), and a maximum-likelihood-trained prior baseline.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Paper presents a novel, principled contribution to the SR literature with a solid theoretical foundation.  
- Effectively addresses the blurriness of MSE-trained SR models.  
- Swiss-roll toy experiment shows AffGAN and AffDG approximate the MAP solution well.  
- On real images, the GAN-based approach produces visually superior, sharper results.  
- Affine projection layer alone can accelerate training and improve results even under MSE.  
- Manuscript is well-written but needs a final proofread to fix minor typos.  
- Recommend strengthening discussion of GAN training stability (e.g., instance noise) with comparisons to broader GAN literature.  
- Question: trade-off between sharpness and high-frequency noise in AffGAN—can it be controlled via instance-noise annealing or architecture?  
- Question: why did denoiser-guided and density-guided methods fail on image data—optimization difficulties or fundamental high-dimensional limitation?
