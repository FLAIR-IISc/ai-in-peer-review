- Proposes framing single-image super-resolution (SR) as amortised MAP inference.
- Argues MSE-based empirical risk yields overly smooth, averaged, perceptually poor results.
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces data consistency (downsampling HR output recovers LR input).
- The affine constraint reduces the MAP objective to matching the generated image distribution to the real HR distribution.
- Proposes three distribution-matching methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (explicit density model).
- Empirical results indicate AffGAN is most effective, producing sharp, perceptually plausible images.
- Decision: Accept, based on theoretical novelty and improved perceptual outputs.
- Strength: provides a principled probabilistic foundation linking SR to generative modeling.
- Strength: affine projection layer elegantly encodes the forward downsampling model into the architecture.
- Qualitative examples (grass, faces, ImageNet crops) show marked perceptual improvement over MSE methods.
- Limitation: lacks comparison to state-of-the-art GAN-based SR methods (e.g., SRGAN) and standard SR benchmarks.
- Concern: datasets/custom processing (CelebA, ImageNet crops) make it hard to assess relative performance.
- Ambiguity: learning the pseudo-inverse A+ may still require paired HR/LR data or exact knowledge of A—needs clarification.
- Recommendation: evaluate on standard benchmarks with quantitative metrics (PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS or user studies).
- Question: scalability to higher resolutions (experiments limited to 128×128) should be tested and discussed.
- Suggestion: investigate stochastic generator fθ(x,z) to model posterior multimodality and produce diverse reconstructions.
- Request: clarify differences vs SRGAN and merits of hard affine projection versus soft penalty approaches.
