- Reframes single-image super-resolution as amortised MAP estimation to avoid blurry MSE-averaged outputs.
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces exact downsampling consistency (x = Ay) in the SR network.
- Shows amortised MAP with the affine constraint is equivalent to minimising cross-entropy between generated and true HR distributions.
- Proposes and evaluates three optimisation methods: AffGAN (adversarial), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (explicit likelihood-guided).
- AffGAN produces the sharpest, most perceptually appealing results and outperforms MSE-based baselines in experiments.
- Comprehensive experiments (2D Swiss-roll toy and real datasets: textures, faces, ImageNet) validate the theoretical claims and practical benefits.
- Theoretical derivation (Section 3) is clear and significant, connecting SR to generative modeling and MAP inference.
- The affine projection improves convergence, training stability, and MSE/SSIM compared to unconstrained networks.
- AffDG and AffLL underperform on complex images, but their inclusion makes the study more complete.
- Introduces an "instance noise" trick to stabilise GAN training, with practical and theoretical motivation.
- Recommends minor proofreading to fix typos and grammar.
- Suggests expanding the main-text discussion on why AffDG/AffLL failed on complex data (e.g., score estimation or density-model limits).
- Recommends an ablation to quantify the impact of instance noise on training stability.
- Highlights the important connection between stochastic AffGAN and amortised variational inference; suggests this be mentioned more prominently.
- Asks authors to clarify practical difficulties with AffDG and AffLL (optimization vs fundamental limits) and whether they tried a stochastic generator f_θ(x,z) to sample the posterior.
