- Frames single-image super-resolution as amortised MAP inference to avoid MSE-induced blurriness.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces the hard constraint that the downsampled output equals the low-resolution input.  
- Casts the amortised MAP objective as minimising cross-entropy between generated and real high-resolution image distributions.  
- Evaluates three solution methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided).  
- Empirically, AffGAN produces the sharpest, most perceptually convincing results compared to MSE-trained baselines.  
- Recommendation: Accept, based on the principled reframing and the effective affine-projection architecture.  
- Theoretical contribution: clear derivation from MAP to cross-entropy unifying SR with generative modeling.  
- Architectural benefit: affine projection outperforms soft penalties, yields faster training, better MSE/SSIM, and guaranteed consistency.  
- Experimental validation: thorough experiments (Swiss-roll toy, textures, CelebA, ImageNet) that support claims and show AffGAN’s superiority.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written with clear motivation, stepwise theory, informative figures, and thoughtful discussion of MAP limitations.  
- Minor fixes suggested: final proofread to correct typos and inconsistent capitalization.  
- Suggestion: add a main-paper discussion on why AffDG and AffLL fail on real images (e.g., gradient instability or insufficient model capacity).  
- Suggestion: highlight and possibly ablate the instance-noise trick to demonstrate its impact on GAN stability/performance.  
- Question: request details on sensitivity and principled scheduling of the instance-noise annealing.  
- Question: ask whether stochastic AffGAN / amortised variational inference was explored and whether it might mitigate “atypical mode” issues.  
- Question: ask for further intuition on whether denoiser/density-guided underperformance is due to model capacity or fundamental gradient stability in high dimensions.
