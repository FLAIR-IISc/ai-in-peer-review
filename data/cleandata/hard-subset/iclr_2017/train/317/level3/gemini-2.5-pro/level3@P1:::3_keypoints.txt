- Novel formulation: frames single-image SR as amortised MAP inference with an affine projection layer enforcing LR consistency.  
- Affine projection: enforces A*y = x and converts optimization into maximizing the image prior / minimizing cross-entropy between generated and real HR images.  
- Three solution methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided using denoiser gradients), and AffLL (guided by an explicit pre-trained density model).  
- Empirical finding: AffGAN yields perceptually superior, much sharper results than MSE baselines and the other proposed techniques.  
- Toy and real-data validation: 2D Swiss-roll and datasets (textures, CelebA, ImageNet) demonstrate the methods approximate MAP and scale to realistic images.  
- Honest reporting: authors acknowledge denoiser- and density-guided methods’ poorer performance despite theoretical promise.  
- Secondary contribution: affine projection can accelerate and improve training even with standard MSE loss.  
- Practical trick: introduce "instance noise" to stabilize GAN training.  
- Theoretical link: appendix connects a stochastic version of AffGAN to amortised variational inference.  
- Decision: Accept — justified by the novel, well-motivated formulation and comprehensive experimental validation.  
- Clarity suggestion: improve exposition, make Eq.(9) → methods connection explicit, and move dense derivations to the appendix.  
- Representation analysis: recommend visualizing the learned residual (I − A+A)fθ(x) to show what the network captures (textures, edges, etc.).  
- Failure-mode analysis: request deeper discussion of hallucinations (e.g., snake→water) and when the prior causes plausible but incorrect details.  
- Question on A+: how sensitive is performance to numerical estimation of A+ and how to handle unknown/variable downsampling kernels in practice?  
- Question on AffDG/AffLL: why did they produce blurry results — noisy/bias denoiser gradients or insufficient density models?  
- Question on instance noise: how does it compare to other GAN stabilization methods (weight clipping, gradient penalty) and is it complementary or alternative?
