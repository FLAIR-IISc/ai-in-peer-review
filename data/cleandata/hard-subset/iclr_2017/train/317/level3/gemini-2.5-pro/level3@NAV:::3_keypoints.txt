- Presents a principled framework for single-image SR via amortized MAP inference with a neural architecture featuring an affine projection layer that enforces HR→LR consistency.  
- Affine projection layer reduces the MAP objective to matching the distribution of generated images to real data.  
- Proposes and evaluates three solution strategies: GAN-based (AffGAN), denoiser-guided, and density-guided methods.  
- Experiments (2D toy problem and datasets like CelebA and grass textures) show AffGAN produces sharp, perceptually plausible results vs. MSE blurriness.  
- Affine projection layer improves performance and training stability and helps even under MSE training.  
- Provides strong theoretical motivation linking MAP inference to minimizing cross-entropy.  
- Empirical validation is methodical, starting with a toy problem that clarifies the method’s correctness.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
