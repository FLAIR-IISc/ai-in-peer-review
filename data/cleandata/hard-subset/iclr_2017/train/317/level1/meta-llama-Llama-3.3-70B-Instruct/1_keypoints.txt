- Proposes AffGAN: amortised MAP inference for image super-resolution using a CNN with an affine projection layer to enforce LR-HR consistency.  
- Argues AffGAN yields more plausible, less blurry outputs than empirical risk minimisation by preferring high-posterior solutions.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance; approach is well-motivated and backed by theoretical arguments.  
- Paper presents a clear problem statement and situates the method well in the literature.  
- Experiments on natural images and faces show AffGAN outperforms MSE/MAE methods on visual quality and cross-entropy.  
- Authors provide a thorough analysis of limitations and outline potential future directions.  
- Suggest adding implementation details for the AffGAN algorithm, including generator and discriminator architectures.  
- Suggest providing a detailed computational complexity analysis (time and memory requirements).  
- Suggest including more visual comparisons and examples of results versus other methods.  
- Request clarification on the choice and practical implementation of the affine projection layer.  
- Ask how the method handles or approximates cases where the downsampling kernel A has infinite support.  
- Ask for more insight into the connection between AffGAN and amortised variational inference and how that could improve performance.
