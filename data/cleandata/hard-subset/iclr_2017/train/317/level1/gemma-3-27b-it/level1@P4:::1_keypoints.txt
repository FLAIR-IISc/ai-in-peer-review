- Tackles image super-resolution by casting it as amortized MAP inference and proposing an affine-projected CNN to enforce LR–HR consistency.  
- Explores three solution methods: GAN-based (AffGAN), denoiser-guided SR (AffDG), and a maximum-likelihood image-prior baseline.  
- Recommendation: Reject — theoretical framework is interesting but empirical results are not compelling for acceptance.  
- Best results come from the GAN-based approach, yet gains are inconsistent and often marginal compared to state-of-the-art.  
- 2D Swiss-roll experiment shows AffGAN/AffDG can find MAP in a toy setting but this success doesn’t translate clearly to complex natural images.  
- Grass texture experiment is promising but lacks strong quantitative evidence and broader comparisons.  
- CelebA and ImageNet results show only modest improvements with AffGAN.  
- Evaluation relies on PSNR/SSIM; more perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS) should be included for robust visual-quality assessment.  
- Missing comprehensive comparisons to strong SR baselines (e.g., SRGAN, ESRGAN, recent GAN-based SR advances).  
- Need more ablation studies to isolate the affine projection’s effect, especially comparing correct initialization of A+ versus learning it.  
- Paper omits discussion of computational cost, notably the extra overhead of training the denoiser in the denoiser-guided approach.  
- Explanation and analysis of instance noise are unclear; a clearer description and its impact on training stability are needed.  
- Some notation is ambiguous (multiple q variants); clearer, more explicit notation would improve readability.  
- The limitations of MAP inference and potential drawbacks of the proposed methods require a more thorough discussion.  
- Questions for authors include: AffGAN vs. ESRGAN performance on CelebA/ImageNet; computational overhead of denoiser-guided SR; intuitive explanation and mitigation of instance noise benefits; ways to prevent AffGAN “hallucinating” incorrect details; and results from stochastic extensions of AffGAN.
