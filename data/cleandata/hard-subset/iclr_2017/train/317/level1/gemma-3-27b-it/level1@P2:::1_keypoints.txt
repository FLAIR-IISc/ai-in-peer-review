- Paper frames image super-resolution as amortized MAP inference and proposes a CNN with an affine projection to enforce LR–HR consistency.  
- Authors explore three MAP inference methods: GAN-based, denoiser-guided, and a maximum-likelihood-trained image prior baseline.  
- Recommendation: reject.  
- Main novelty: affine projection is a clever idea, and the MAP framing is interesting.  
- Empirical results do not convincingly show superiority, especially for the GAN approach claimed as best.  
- 2D Swiss-roll experiment shows MAP recovery in a simple setting but does not translate to compelling natural-image results.  
- Quantitative metrics (PSNR, SSIM) often favor the MSE-trained baseline, contradicting perceptual-quality claims.  
- GAN outputs exhibit high-frequency noise and some implausible reconstructions (e.g., snake → water).  
- Presentation is dense and offloads crucial implementation and experimental details to the appendix, hindering evaluation and reproducibility.  
- Lack of sufficient ablation studies to isolate the contributions of affine projection, loss choices, and instance noise.  
- Reliance on instance noise to stabilize GAN training suggests a fundamental training issue that is not adequately addressed.  
- DAE and density-guided methods are dismissed without sufficient investigation or optimization attempts.  
- Connection to variational inference in the appendix is interesting but underdeveloped and feels like an afterthought.  
- Suggested improvements: stronger perceptual evaluation (e.g., LPIPS, user studies).  
- Suggested improvements: comprehensive ablation studies for model components and training choices.  
- Suggested improvements: move key implementation details into the main text and justify architecture/hyperparameter choices.  
- Suggested improvements: analyze failure cases and limitations explicitly.  
- Suggested improvements: compare to stronger, recent SR baselines (e.g., EDSR, RCAN).  
- Suggested improvements: thoroughly analyze the role and effects of instance noise on GAN stability and outputs.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to Gaussian kernel width in the affine projection?  
- Questions for authors: detailed analysis and mitigation strategies for GAN high-frequency noise?  
- Questions for authors: computational cost of the affine projection vs. standard convolutions?  
- Questions for authors: why did denoiser- and density-guided methods fail and were further optimizations attempted?  
- Questions for authors: what specific user-study protocol would you use to validate perceived image quality?
