- Novel framing: Proposes amortised MAP inference for super-resolution, arguing MSE-driven SR is implausible and leads to blur.  
- Architectural idea: Introduces a CNN with an affine projection to enforce LR–HR consistency.  
- Methods explored: Evaluates three approaches to inference — GANs (AffGAN/SoftGAN), denoiser guidance, and a learned density prior.  
- Theoretical contribution: Well-motivated theoretical justification linking MAP, affine projection, and GAN-based inference.  
- Promising direction: Explicit image prior + consistency constraint is a compelling idea for SR.  
- Main verdict: Recommendation to reject pending major revisions due to incomplete experimental validation.  
- Experiment gap: Jump from toy cross-entropy results to perceptual gains on real images is not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Quantitative shortcomings: Lacks comprehensive evaluation with standard SR metrics (PSNR, SSIM) and perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS) and comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Qualitative shortcomings: Visual comparisons are limited and do not include established SR baselines (e.g., EDSR, RCAN) or diverse image content.  
- Ablation needed: More extensive ablation of AffGAN architecture and training hyperparameters is required.  
- Methods comparison lacking: Paper doesn't fully analyze relative strengths/weaknesses and trade-offs of GAN, denoiser, and density approaches.  
- Premature dismissal: Denoiser- and density-guided approaches were dropped too quickly without detailed investigation of their limitations.  
- Instance noise: Instance noise looks promising but needs deeper justification and sensitivity analysis of noise levels.  
- Questions for authors: How does affine projection mitigate MAP’s representation dependence? Provide quantitative metrics for the grass experiment. Why were denoiser/density methods abandoned (specific challenges)? Clarify architecture choices (layers, filters) and their effect on performance.
