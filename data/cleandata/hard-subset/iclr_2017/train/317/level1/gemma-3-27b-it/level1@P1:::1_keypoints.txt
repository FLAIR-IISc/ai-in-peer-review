- Proposal: learn an amortised MAP estimator for image SR via a CNN that includes an affine-projection layer to enforce LR–HR consistency, trained with GAN, denoiser-guided, or density-guided methods.
- Decision: Reject.
- Strong theoretical motivation but practical realization is challenging and empirical gains are unconvincing.
- Limited empirical gains: main successes are on a Swiss-roll toy; CelebA/ImageNet improvements are marginal and not consistently better by PSNR/SSIM.
- Comparisons to existing SR methods (e.g., Ledig et al. 2016) lack rigor and identical-architecture benchmarks.
- Inconsistent results and weak ablation: denoiser- and density-guided models reportedly diverged, but causes and tuning details are insufficiently explored.
- Ablation study shows benefit of affine projection but is not comprehensive (e.g., impact of instance noise not isolated).
- Lack of quantitative robustness: low cross-entropy in toy tasks doesn’t convincingly correlate with perceptual quality or standard SR metrics on real images.
- Recommend more rigorous comparisons with state-of-the-art (EDSR, SRGAN, RDN) using identical architectures/training where only loss/strategy varies.
- Recommend sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (learning rate, batch size, instance noise level).
- Recommend better qualitative evaluation: carefully selected comparisons to baselines and/or a user study for perceptual quality.
- Recommend detailed divergence analysis and stabilization strategies for denoiser- and density-guided training (optimizers, schedules, regularization).
- Recommend reporting computational cost and overhead of the affine projection relative to standard SR networks.
- Question: explain and mitigate cases where AffGAN produces plausible but semantically incorrect content (e.g., snake→water).
- Question: provide detailed instance-noise annealing schedule and its impact on training stability.
- Question: clarify whether denoiser- and density-guided failures were due to intrinsic limitations or hyperparameter/implementation issues.
- Question: compare AffGAN’s performance directly to SRGAN with comparable architecture and training settings.
- Question: quantify the computational overhead introduced by the affine projection layer.
