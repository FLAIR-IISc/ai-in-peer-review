- Shift from MSE-based empirical risk minimization to amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution with an affine projection layer ensuring LR-HR consistency.  
- Reformulates MAP inference as minimizing cross-entropy between the model output distribution and the natural image prior.  
- Proposes three solution methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided).  
- Theoretically and empirically shown to produce sharper, more plausible SR results than MSE-based approaches.  
- Decision: Accept — significant theoretical contribution with strong empirical validation.  
- Technical soundness: Rigorous theoretical foundation explaining blurriness from MSE averaging and well-motivated affine projection.  
- Transformation to cross-entropy and derivations are technically justified and grounded in prior work.  
- Novelty: Affine projection for SR is new; first theoretical justification linking GANs to MAP inference for SR.  
- Additional theoretical novelty: Connection between AffGAN and amortized variational inference (Appendix F).  
- Empirical validation: Extensive experiments (toy, textures, CelebA, ImageNet) showing AffGAN/AffDG minimize cross-entropy and outperform MSE/MAE perceptually.  
- Ablation: Affine projection does not degrade performance and can improve MSE training speed/quality.  
- Suggestion: Provide analysis of computational complexity and training time of AffGAN vs. standard SR methods.  
- Suggestion: Include quantitative metrics (PSNR, SSIM) for AffGAN alongside qualitative comparisons.  
- Suggestion: Clarify relation between instance noise technique and prior GAN stabilization methods, especially vs. label smoothing.  
- Suggestion: Expand discussion on MAP inference limitations with concrete experimental failure cases.

- Question: Provide empirical/theoretical support for the claim that AffGAN is more resilient to coordinate transformations.
