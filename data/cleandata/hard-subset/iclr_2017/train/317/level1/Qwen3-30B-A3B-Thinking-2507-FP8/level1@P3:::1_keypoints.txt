- Proposes amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution with an affine projection layer and three methods (AffGAN, AffDG, AffLL); connects GANs to amortized variational inference and shows AffGAN yields sharper, more plausible outputs.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions for solid theory and empirical gains.  
- Theoretical claim: MAP inference is preferable to MSE since it finds posterior modes in high-probability regions and reduces to minimizing cross-entropy with the natural image prior.  
- Architectural innovation: affine projection using the Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse enforces perfect LR–HR consistency and aids training.  
- Ablation: affine projection does not hurt performance and may improve convergence and final quality.  
- Empirical results: Swiss-roll and real-image experiments show AffGAN produces sharper textures and visually superior reconstructions, with quantitative results supporting qualitative findings.  
- Novel contribution: derivation linking AffGAN and variational inference offers a new perspective on GANs.  
- Suggestion: add deeper discussion relating the affine projection to prior constrained SR methods to better position the work in literature.  
- Suggestion: provide more detailed analysis explaining why AffDG and AffLL underperform compared to AffGAN (denoiser/density limitations).  
- Suggestion: strengthen Appendix F with a more rigorous derivation of the AffGAN–variational inference connection.  
- Question: why do affine-projected networks train faster and achieve better MSE/SSIM—does the constraint regularize the optimization landscape?  
- Question: why does the denoiser-based/log-density approach fail in practice—is it denoiser quality or optimization issues?  
- Question: how sensitive is AffGAN to instance-noise level and annealing schedule, and is instance noise generally effective for stabilizing GAN training beyond SR?
