- Introduces amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution as an alternative to MSE-based empirical risk minimization.
- Proposes a network architecture that projects outputs to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions to ensure LR–HR consistency.
- Frames MAP inference as minimizing cross-entropy between the model output distribution and the natural image prior.
- Develops three methods to solve the MAP objective: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided).
- Provides strong theoretical grounding and a novel connection between GANs and variational inference.
- Demonstrates method performance on a 2D toy problem and multiple real-world image datasets.
- Shows that AffGAN and AffDG correctly minimize the MAP objective in the toy experiment.
- Reports perceptually superior outputs compared to MSE-based baselines on real data.
- Claims rigorous theoretical derivations and comprehensive, well-designed experiments across datasets.
- Positions the contribution clearly within existing literature and highlights the limitation of blurry SR outputs from averaging.
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
- Requests clarification of the method’s computational cost relative to standard SR models (e.g., EDSR, RCAN).
- Suggests adding a detailed discussion on why AffDG and AffLL produced blurry results despite theoretical soundness.
- Asks two specific questions: (1) How does AffGAN’s computational complexity compare to EDSR/RCAN? (2) Why did AffDG and AffLL underperform AffGAN in practice despite theoretical equivalence?
