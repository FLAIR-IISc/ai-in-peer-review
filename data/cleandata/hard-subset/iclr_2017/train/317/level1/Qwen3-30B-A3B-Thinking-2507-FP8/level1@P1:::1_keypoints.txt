- Summary: Identifies MSE-based SR produces blurry means; proposes amortized MAP inference with an affine-projection network and three methods (AffGAN, AffDG, AffLL); AffGAN yields the best visual results and can train without paired data; connects to variational inference.

- Decision: Recommend accept due to strong theory, novel affine-projection architecture, and compelling empirical results.

- Supporting — clarity/motivation: Clear problem statement and a 2D toy example showing why MAP is preferable to MSE for perceptual plausibility.

- Supporting — related work: Well-situated in literature with thorough discussion of GAN-based SR, perceptual metrics, and variational inference links.

- Supporting — theory: Theoretical proofs show affine projection makes MAP reduce to cross-entropy minimization.

- Supporting — empirical: AffGAN achieves lowest cross-entropy and produces the sharpest, most plausible SR images (Figures/Tables); instance noise helps stabilize GAN training.

- Feedback 1: Provide deeper analysis of the perceptual-quality vs. PSNR/SSIM trade-offs and why AffGAN beats PSNR despite lower scores.

- Feedback 2: Analyze why AffDG and AffLL produce blurry results to illuminate failure modes and guide future work.

- Feedback 3: Report computational complexity and training-time comparisons versus standard SR methods for practical adoption.

- Feedback 4: Expand the theoretical variational-inference connection to discuss practical benefits and implications for future SR designs.

- Question 1: What causes the grass-texture artifacts in AffGAN outputs — inherent to MAP or implementation-specific?

- Question 2: Is the extra high-frequency noise from the affine projection or the GAN itself, and how might it be mitigated?

- Question 3: How does the AffGAN–variational-inference connection inform implementation choices, and what advantages would a stochastic AffGAN bring?
