- Frames single-image super-resolution as amortised MAP inference to produce perceptually plausible HR images instead of MSE-averaged blurs.  
- Shows amortised MAP objective is equivalent to minimizing cross-entropy between generated and true image distributions.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer that analytically enforces A * f(x) = x, turning a constrained problem into an unconstrained one.  
- Proposes three optimization methods: AffGAN (GAN with affine projection), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-model-guided).  
- Empirically demonstrates AffGAN produces the sharpest, most perceptually convincing results across toy data, textures, faces, and ImageNet.  
- Provides a theoretical link between a stochastic AffGAN variant and amortised variational inference, connecting GANs and VAEs.  
- Decision: Accept — motivated by a principled framework and strong empirical results.  
- Affine projection layer is an elegant, technically sound solution that removes the need to balance reconstruction and prior terms.  
- Clearly motivates replacing MSE with a MAP framework due to MSE’s tendency to average multimodal posteriors.  
- Honest reporting of negative results for AffDG and AffLL is scientifically valuable and thorough exploration of alternatives is included.  
- Visual results (Figures 3–5) convincingly show perceptual improvements over MSE-trained models.  
- The paper contributes an alternative GAN update minimizing KL-divergence and appendix material linking to variational inference.  
- Suggests deeper analysis of why AffDG and AffLL fail (noisy/bias in denoiser gradients or complex log-likelihood surfaces).  
- Recommends ablation on the instance-noise technique to quantify its effect on GAN stability and performance.  
- Proposes highlighting the amortised variational inference connection in the main text for greater visibility.  
- Asks whether jointly training or fine-tuning the denoiser with the SR network was tried to improve AffDG.  
- Questions if AffLL’s failure might stem from optimization dynamics or a highly non-convex gradient field despite good bits-per-dimension.  
- Asks how to control MAP-induced “hallucinations” (plausible but incorrect textures) versus faithfulness to subtle LR features.
