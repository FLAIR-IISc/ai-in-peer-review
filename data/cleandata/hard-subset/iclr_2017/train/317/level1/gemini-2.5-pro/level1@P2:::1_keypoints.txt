- Reframes single-image SR as amortised MAP inference, arguing MSE averages modes and yields blurry outputs while MAP produces sharper, more realistic images.
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces the downsampling constraint, turning constrained MAP into an unconstrained cross-entropy minimization.
- Proposes three solution methods: AffGAN (GAN-based), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided).
- Empirical results show AffGAN is most effective, producing visually sharper results than MSE baselines on toy and real images.
- Provides a theoretical link between a stochastic version of AffGAN and amortised variational inference.
- Decision: Accept — paper makes significant conceptual and technical contributions.
- Strength: Presents a novel, principled framework shifting SR from pixel-wise losses to MAP-based generative modeling.
- Strength: Affine projection layer is an elegant architectural innovation that enforces hard data consistency without harming standard metrics.
- Strong motivation and clear exposition, illustrated by a simple 2D Swiss-roll example.
- Theoretical derivation in Section 3 convincingly connects MAP objective to cross-entropy minimization via the affine projection.
- Thorough empirical validation: 2D toy experiments demonstrate AffGAN/AffDG approximate MAP better than MSE/MAE.
- Sanity check shows affine projection does not degrade MSE-trained network performance and may improve it.
- Qualitative results on textures, faces, and natural images show AffGAN outputs are perceptually superior; authors transparently report AffDG/AffLL failures.
- Instance noise for GAN stabilization and the variational-inference connection in the appendix are valuable additional contributions.
- Suggestion: Provide deeper analysis of why AffDG/AffLL failed (e.g., difficulty training denoisers/density models or optimization/backprop issues).
- Suggestion: Add quantitative perceptual metrics (NIQE, BRISQUE) or a human study to support perceptual quality claims.
- Suggestion: Include an ablation for instance noise to quantify its effect on GAN stability and image quality.
- Suggestion: Clarify differences to concurrent SRGAN work, highlighting the principled MAP formulation and affine projection vs heuristic VGG loss.
- Question: How sensitive is performance to numerical precision of the pseudo-inverse A+, and how does the method handle unknown downsampling kernels A in practice?
- Question: Have you tried stochastic AffGAN to sample from p(y|x)? If so, how do sample quality and diversity compare to the single MAP estimate?
