- Frames single-image SR as amortised MAP inference, arguing MAP yields more perceptually realistic outputs than MSE averaging.
- Core contributions: affine projection layer enforcing LR↔downsampled-HR consistency; three MAP approximation methods (AffGAN, AffDG, explicit density); theoretical GAN–amortised variational inference connection.
- Empirical result: AffGAN produces sharp, high-quality reconstructions across multiple image datasets.
- Decision: Accept.
- Strength: Affine projection layer is an elegant, principled architectural solution that outperforms soft-penalty approaches.
- Strength: Strong theoretical framing supported by convincing empirical comparisons, notably GAN-based perceptual improvements.
- Supporting evidence: 2D Swiss-roll validates MAP optimization; ablation shows affine projection helps under MSE; qualitative results highlight AffGAN advantages on textures and faces.
- Authors honestly report shortcomings of denoiser- and density-guided methods on complex images.
- Improvement request: discuss reasons why denoiser- and density-guided approaches failed (e.g., inaccurate gradients in high dimensions, optimization challenges).
- Improvement request: analyze sensitivity of the instance-noise annealing schedule and compare its stabilization effectiveness to other GAN techniques.
- Clarification request: discuss the trade-off between achieving sharpness and introducing high-frequency artifacts, and whether this trade-off can be controlled.
- Clarification request: speculate on practical benefits of sampling from the posterior (stochastic generator) versus finding the mode for SR tasks.
