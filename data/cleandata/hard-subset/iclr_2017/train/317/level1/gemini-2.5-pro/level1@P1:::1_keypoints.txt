- Introduces an affine projection layer that analytically enforces LR–HR consistency by projecting outputs onto the affine subspace of valid solutions.  
- Recasts amortised MAP inference for SR as minimizing cross-entropy between model outputs and real HR images, turning supervised SR into a generative modeling problem.  
- Proposes three methods to optimize this objective: AffGAN (GAN-based, with instance noise and KL-modified objective), AffDG (denoiser-guided using score estimation), and AffLL (density-guided using a pre-trained PixelCNN-like model).  
- Empirical results show AffGAN produces perceptually superior, much sharper SR results than MSE-trained models.  
- Swiss-roll toy experiment demonstrates AffGAN/AffDG approximate MAP modes, unlike MSE/MAE which return mean/median.  
- Affine-projected network trained with MSE passes a proof-of-concept sanity check and can match standard metrics.  
- Qualitative results on textures, CelebA, and ImageNet are compelling; paper honestly reports poorer AffDG/AffLL performance.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Affine projection layer is a significant, broadly applicable architectural contribution for enforcing hard data-consistency in inverse problems.  
- Key reason 2: Provides a strong theoretical framework connecting MAP inference, cross-entropy minimization, and generative modeling, justifying GAN-based perceptual SR.  
- Methodology is well-derived, and "instance noise" is a useful GAN stabilization trick with theoretical motivation.  
- Empirical evaluation is thoughtful and convincing, including low-dimensional visualization, sanity checks, and real-image qualitative comparisons.  
- Suggest deeper analysis of why AffDG and AffLL fail on real images—e.g., difficulties in high-dimensional score/density estimation producing noisy/bias gradients.  
- Recommend highlighting the Appendix F connection to amortised variational inference (GAN–VAE link) more prominently in the main text/conclusion.  
- Recommend an ablation showing practical impact of instance noise (training stability/performance with vs. without).  
- Questions for authors: clarify the generator loss typo in Section 3.2 and state the exact KL-corresponding objective used.  
- Questions for authors: explain practical challenges and potential remedies for AffDG and AffLL on real images (score/density model capacity, optimization).  
- Questions for authors: report sensitivity and verification of numerical pseudo-inverse A+ and how well Ax = A(g_theta(x)) was satisfied in practice.  
- Questions for authors: comment on "hallucination" bias (e.g., LR ambiguity leading to common textures like water) and other observed examples.
