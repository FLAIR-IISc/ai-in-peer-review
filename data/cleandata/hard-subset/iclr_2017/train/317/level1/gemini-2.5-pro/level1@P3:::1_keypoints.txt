- Proposes framing single-image super-resolution as amortised MAP inference to avoid MSE-induced blurring.  
- Introduces an affine projection layer that exactly enforces the downsampling constraint Ay = x by projecting network outputs onto the affine subspace of valid solutions.  
- Reframes supervised SR as a generative modelling problem by turning MAP inference into minimising cross-entropy between generated and real HR image distributions.  
- Proposes and evaluates three methods to approximate the MAP objective: AffGAN (GAN with affine projection), AffDG (denoiser-guided), and AffLL (density-guided with PixelCNN).  
- AffGAN produces sharp, perceptually convincing results on toy and real images, outperforming MSE baselines.  
- Introduces "instance noise" as a practical technique to stabilise GAN training.  
- Provides a theoretical connection between a stochastic variant of the framework and amortised variational inference (in Appendix F).  
- Decision: Accept — motivated by a novel, principled formulation and strong empirical validation.  
- Strength: Clear writing and intuition, with an effective Swiss-roll example illustrating why MAP is preferable to MSE.  
- Strength: The affine projection layer is a standout, general-purpose contribution that enforces data consistency and can improve MSE training convergence.  
- Strength: Thorough exploration of three approaches to amortised MAP, even if only one works well in practice.  
- Strength: Insightful discussion of MAP limitations and future directions, including posterior sampling and variational inference links.  
- Weakness: AffDG and AffLL fail on real images, producing blurry outputs; investigation into causes is brief.  
- Weakness: Limited direct comparison or discussion relative to concurrent SRGAN work, despite conceptual differences.  
- Feedback: Expand Appendix E to better analyse why denoiser- and density-guided methods fail in high dimensions.  
- Feedback: Further relate instance noise to other GAN stabilisation methods (e.g., discriminator input noise, label smoothing).  
- Feedback: Move or summarise the amortised variational inference connection into the main text to highlight its importance.  
- Questions: Are AffDG/AffLL failures due to local properties of the learned log-likelihood (noisy/peaky gradients) rather than overall density accuracy?  
- Questions: How sensitive are results to numerical approximation of A⁺, and did you compare fixed A⁺ to a learned A⁺ within the projection framework?  
- Questions: Does enforcing the affine constraint improve GAN training stability compared to a soft-penalty SoftGAN baseline?
