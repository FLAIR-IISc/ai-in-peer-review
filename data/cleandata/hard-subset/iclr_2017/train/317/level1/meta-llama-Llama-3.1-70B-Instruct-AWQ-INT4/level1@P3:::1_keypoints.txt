- Proposes amortized MAP inference for image super-resolution with a CNN that enforces outputs lie in the affine subspace of valid SR solutions.
- Introduces three MAP approximation methods: GAN-based, denoiser-guided SR, and a maximum-likelihood image-prior baseline.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on toy data and real datasets (grass textures, CelebA, ImageNet).
- Decision: Accept.
- Novelty/impact: Amortized MAP and the affine projection layer are novel and potentially influential for SR.
- Thorough evaluation: Extensive experiments show especially strong perceptual results for the GAN-based approach.
- Clear exposition: Methods and links to prior work are well-motivated and explained.
- Instance noise: New technique proposed to stabilize GAN training but needs more analysis of effectiveness and limits.
- Comparisons: Paper compares to MSE-based methods but should include other state-of-the-art deep SR methods.
- Limitations/future work: More discussion needed on assumptions (e.g., mode representativeness) and future directions.
- Questions for authors: Provide deeper analysis of instance noise effectiveness; compare methods to other SOTA SR approaches; expand on limitations and future work.
