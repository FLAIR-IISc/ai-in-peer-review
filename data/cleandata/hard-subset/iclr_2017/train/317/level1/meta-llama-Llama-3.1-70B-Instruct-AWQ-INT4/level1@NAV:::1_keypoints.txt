- Proposes amortised MAP inference for image super-resolution using a CNN that enforces output consistency by projecting to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions.  
- Presents three amortised MAP methods: GAN-based SR, denoiser-guided SR, and density-guided SR.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness across datasets (natural images, faces, textures).  
- Recommendation: Accept due to novelty and thorough evaluation.  
- Novelty/impact: Introduces a significant new SR approach addressing limitations of traditional methods.  
- Technical soundness: Provides clear mathematical formulation and implementation details.  
- Evaluation/results: Comprehensive experiments show improved perceptual quality.  
- Clarity/organization: Well-organized overall, but some sections (e.g., introduction) need clearer context on traditional SR limitations.  
- Comparison gap: Should include comparisons to state-of-the-art SR methods.  
- Future work: Mentions stochastic extensions but needs more detail on those extensions and applications.
