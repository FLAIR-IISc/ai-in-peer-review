- Proposes amortised MAP inference for image super-resolution to avoid blur from MSE-based empirical risk minimisation.  
- Introduces a CNN architecture that projects outputs to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions, ensuring LR-HR consistency.  
- Well-motivated and well-situated in literature with a clear explanation of limitations of traditional SR and MAP benefits.  
- Provides a thorough related-work review, including perceptual-quality methods using deep image representations.  
- Novel approach: affine-projection CNN architecture is interesting and original.  
- Presents three optimisation strategies: GAN-based, denoiser-guided SR, and a maximum-likelihood image-prior baseline.  
- Experiments are well-designed and show the GAN-based method performs best on real images.  
- Shows the affine projection layer does not degrade CNN SR performance.  
- Recommendation: paper should be accepted.  
- Feedback: paper needs more discussion of limitations, e.g., representation dependence of distribution modes.  
- Feedback: provide more implementation details for the affine projection layer and the GAN approach.  
- Feedback: include more visualisations and examples of super-resolved images.  
- Questions: how is the affine projection layer implemented and how does it affect CNN performance?  
- Questions: how are GAN hyperparameters chosen and how sensitive is performance to them?  
- Questions: provide more discussion on limitations and comparison to other methods.
