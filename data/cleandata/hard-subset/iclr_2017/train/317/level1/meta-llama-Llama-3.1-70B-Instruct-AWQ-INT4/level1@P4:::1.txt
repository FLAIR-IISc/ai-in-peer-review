**Summary of the paper's contributions**

The paper proposes a new approach to image super-resolution (SR) using amortised Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference. The authors introduce a novel neural network architecture that performs a projection to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions, ensuring that the high-resolution output of the network is always consistent with the low-resolution input. They then propose three methods to solve the optimisation problem: (1) Generative Adversarial Networks (GAN), (2) denoiser-guided SR, and (3) a baseline method using a maximum-likelihood-trained image prior.
