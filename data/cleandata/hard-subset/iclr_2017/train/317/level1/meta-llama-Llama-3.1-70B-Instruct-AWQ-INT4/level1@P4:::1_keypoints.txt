- Proposes amortised MAP inference for image super-resolution with a neural architecture that projects outputs to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions, ensuring LR-HR consistency.  
- Introduces three optimisation/solution methods: (1) GAN-based, (2) denoiser-guided SR, and (3) a maximum-likelihood-trained image prior baseline.  
- Decision: accept, primarily because the approach is novel and well-motivated for SR.  
- Decision reason: provides thorough theoretical analysis, notably connecting GANs with amortised variational inference.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and includes a comprehensive related-work review explaining prior limitations.  
- Methods are well-motivated and experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach.  
- Theoretical analysis is solid and the GAN–amortised-variational-inference connection is an important insight.  
- Suggest adding more experimental details (network architectures, hyperparameters) for reproducibility.  
- Recommend more visualizations (images/plots) to better illustrate results.  
- Suggest discussing potential limitations and outlining future research directions.  
- Ask authors to clarify computational cost of the proposed methods and comparisons to existing approaches.  
- Ask how methods handle noisy or corrupted low-resolution inputs and for deeper insight into the GAN–amortised-VI relationship relative to other methods.
