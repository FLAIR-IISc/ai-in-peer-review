- Introduces amortised MAP inference for image super-resolution to avoid blurriness from MSE/MAE.
- Proposes a CNN that projects outputs to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions, enforcing LR-HR consistency.
- Presents three MAP approximation methods: GANs, denoiser-guided SR, and a maximum-likelihood-trained image prior baseline.
- Experimental results indicate the GAN-based approach performs best on real images.
- Decision: accept.
- Clearly motivated problem formulation advocating amortised MAP over empirical risk minimisation.
- Well-situated in prior work on GANs, denoising, and density models.
- Provides a thorough theoretical foundation supporting the methods.
- Methods are well-explained and offer a comprehensive approach to approximating the MAP estimate.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and compares proposed methods to traditional approaches.
- Limitation: reliance on an assumed Gaussian noise distribution needs further discussion.
- Requests more detail on hyperparameter choices and sensitivity of results to them.
- Notes the interesting connection between GANs and amortised variational inference warrants further exploration.
- Suggests a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art image super-resolution methods.
- Asks for clarification on hyperparameter selection and sensitivity analysis.
- Asks how the Gaussian noise assumption will be addressed or relaxed.
- Asks for more insight into the relationship between GANs, amortised variational inference, and SOTA SR methods.
- Asks about plans to extend the approach to other tasks like denoising or segmentation.
