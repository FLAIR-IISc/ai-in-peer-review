**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

The paper proposes a novel approach for image super-resolution (SR) using amortised Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) inference. The authors introduce a convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture that projects the model output to the affine subspace of valid SR solutions, ensuring consistency between the input and output. They then propose three methods for amortised MAP inference: Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), denoiser-guided SR, and density-guided SR. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of these methods on various image datasets, including natural images, faces, and textures.
