- Paper proposes casting single-image SR as amortised MAP under a noiseless model x = Ay with an affine projection layer enforcing Afθ(x)=x via ΠAx fθ(x).
- Main training idea: minimise cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between model output marginal and HR data, enabling learning without paired LR–HR samples.
- Proposes three training routes: AffGAN (GAN variant targeting KL[q||pY] with instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-guided score backpropagation), and AffLL (likelihood-guided using PixelCNN/MCGSM).
- Theoretical contributions: MAP→cross-entropy decomposition under affine constraint, derivation that their GAN update targets KL, and a link between stochastic AffGAN and amortised variational inference.
- Experiments (2D toy, textures, CelebA, ImageNet) show AffGAN yields sharper outputs than MSE baselines and affine projection improves training speed and consistency.
- Decision: Reject (borderline) because empirical validation is insufficient and practical assumptions are restrictive.
- Major critique: Empirical evidence does not substantiate the amortised-MAP claim on real images; comparisons and evaluation protocols are limited.
- Two proposed routes (AffDG, AffLL) underperform and are not explored beyond a single dataset, weakening the generality of the approach.
- Practicality concern: method requires known, noiseless, convolutional A and accurate A+ (rare in real SR); robustness to mismatch/noise is not evaluated.
- Theory praise: derivations are sound and the GAN/KL analysis clarifies the intended optimisation, though GAN update remains heuristic in practice.
- 2D Swiss‑roll toy validates AffGAN/AffDG approach MAP under controlled conditions, supporting the MAP proxy in ideal settings.
- Image results are largely qualitative; AffGAN can hallucinate semantically implausible content, exposing fidelity risks of the prior and divergence targeted.
- Claims about unpaired training are not convincing because pX is synthesized from HR corpus using A; real LR degradations/noise are not considered.
- Baselines are limited: no comparisons to strong SR methods (SRResNet/SRGAN), perceptual/feature losses, or classical priors; metrics focus on PSNR/SSIM that favor MSE.
- No human study or perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS, user preference) are reported, leaving plausibility claims anecdotal.
- AffDG and AffLL largely fail to produce sharp images; instability/peakedness issues are acknowledged but not resolved, undermining the amortised-MAP claim beyond GANs.
- Affine projection exactness may hurt when x is noisy or A is misspecified; approximation error from finite-support A+ is not quantified.
- Reviewer recommends robustness experiments: additive noise, blur-kernel mismatch, non-convolutional/unknown degradations, and soft vs hard consistency.
- Suggests perceptual evaluation: human study or perceptual metrics and comparisons to SRGAN-like baselines.
- Suggests stronger baselines and ablations: SRResNet/SRGAN, VGG feature loss, affine projection ablation, instance noise schedule, and generator update variants.
- Clarify unpaired training: demonstrate truly unpaired LR data or learn/estimate A jointly for blind SR.
- For AffDG/AffLL, analyze why they blur (σ-annealing, score calibration) and test improved score/density models (multi-scale DAEs, flows).
- Propose conditioning the prior on x (e.g., conditional discriminators or perceptual matching) to reduce semantic hallucinations while keeping sharpness.
- Request more implementation details: receptive field/support and approximation error of A+, runtime/memory, and whether A+ should be fixed or trainable.
- Questions for authors requested: sensitivity to kernel/noise mismatch; whether LR data were independent in “unpaired” experiments; impact of A+ kernel size; quantified benefit of instance noise vs other regularisers; reconciliation of KL-targeting with MAP objectives and observed entropy artifacts; potential improvements for AffDG; experiments with conditional discriminators.
