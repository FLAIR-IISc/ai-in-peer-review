- Proposes amortised MAP inference for single-image SR by training a deterministic mapping with an affine projection that enforces Afθ(x)=x, reducing to minimising cross-entropy H[qθ,pY].
- Introduces an affine projection ΠAx implemented via the Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse A+ to ensure exact LR consistency and turn the MAP objective into unconstrained optimisation.
- Proposes three optimisation routes: AffGAN (GAN variant approximating KL[qθ‖pY] with instance noise), AffDG (denoiser-based score estimation), and AffLL (tractable density model gradients).
- Experiments: 2D Swiss-roll toy shows AffGAN/AffDG approach brute-force MAP; image results (textures, CelebA, ImageNet, 4× SR) show sharper outputs than MSE baselines while preserving LR consistency.
- Draws a sketchy connection between a stochastic AffGAN and amortised variational inference.
- Decision: Reject.
- Primary reason 1: Empirical evaluation lacks rigor, strong baselines, and quantitative perceptual evidence to support central claims.
- Primary reason 2: Core amortised-MAP claim is only partially justified—GAN training minimises KL (or smoothed KL), not cross-entropy, and there is no guarantee the deterministic mapping yields per-x MAP solutions.
- Clarity positive: High-level narrative and affine projection motivation are clear; appendices contain derivations and implementation details.
- Clarity negative: Notational inconsistencies and typographical errors (e.g., generator objective) and key claims are only sketched, not rigorously proven.
- Technical soundness positive: Affine projection layer and numerical A+ estimation are conceptually sound and produce near-zero LR-consistency errors.
- Technical soundness positive: Score-based guidance via denoising is consistent with known theory and backpropagating score estimates is reasonable.
- Technical soundness positive: Instance noise rationale for support overlap and discriminator uniqueness is coherent and aligns with GAN stability ideas.
- Technical soundness negative: Minimising KL[qθ‖pY] differs from minimising cross-entropy due to entropy term H[qθ], and paper does not establish when this discrepancy preserves per-x MAP optimality.
- Technical soundness negative: Training objective maximises Ey log pY(gθ(x)) averaged over x, which need not produce per-x MAP solutions without further conditions.
- Novelty: Affine projection for exact LR-consistency and reduction of amortised MAP to distribution matching are novel and insightful.
- Novelty caveat: Instance noise analysis and VI connection are interesting but presented as brief sketches rather than full contributions.
- Empirical support positive: Toy experiments quantify cross-entropy and LR consistency and support claims.
- Empirical support negative: Image evaluation is mostly qualitative, lacks perceptual metrics or user studies, and reports PSNR/SSIM only to show expected MSE superiority.
- Empirical support negative: Missing comparisons to strong contemporaneous baselines (SRResNet/SRGAN, perceptual-loss methods); AffDG/AffLL are underexplored and dropped early.
- Empirical support negative: “Unpaired” training claim is untested in truly unpaired settings since LR images were generated by applying A to HR images.
- Suggested improvements (empirical): Add perceptual metrics/user studies, compare to SRResNet/SRGAN under identical downsampling, and run ablations on instance noise and affine projection robustness.
- Suggested improvements (theory): Provide clean derivations showing GAN update targets KL, relate KL to cross-entropy, and state assumptions for per-x MAP optimality; expand the amortised VI connection.
- Suggested improvements (practical): Give more details on computing A+ (kernel sizes, convergence), quantify Afθ(x)=x residuals, report computational cost and release code.
- Key questions for authors: Provide typo-free proof that generator update minimises KL[qθ‖pY] and sensitivity to non-saturating loss.
- Key questions for authors: Under what assumptions does Ey log pY(gθ(x)) produce per-x MAP solutions, and can you show counterexamples?
- Key questions for authors: How does performance degrade if test-time A differs from training A, and can affine projection be adapted or learned?
- Key questions for authors: Have you compared AffGAN to SRGAN with identical setup, and does affine projection improve perceptual quality and LR consistency?
- Key questions for authors: Can you demonstrate true unpaired training where pX ≠ A∗pY, and what fails when distributions differ?
- Key questions for authors: Can you quantify stability gains from instance noise (discriminator trajectories, mode collapse frequency) and provide annealing guidance?
