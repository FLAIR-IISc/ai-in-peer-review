Summary and contributions
The paper tackles single-image super-resolution (SR) as an underdetermined inverse problem and proposes amortised MAP inference as a principled alternative to MSE-based empirical risk minimisation. The key architectural contribution is an affine projection layer that guarantees exact LR-HR consistency by projecting outputs onto the affine subspace defined by the downsampling operator. This recasts amortised MAP into matching the generator’s marginal output distribution to the HR data distribution via cross-entropy minimisation. Three optimisation routes are explored: an affine-projected GAN (AffGAN) with an instance-noise stabilization trick, a denoiser-guided approach (AffDG) leveraging score estimates, and a density-guided baseline (AffLL) using a continuous PixelCNN-like prior. The paper provides theoretical grounding (links to KL minimisation and a stochastic variant connecting to amortised variational inference) and empirically shows that AffGAN yields sharper, more plausible images while strictly enforcing LR consistency.

Decision: Reject
Primary reasons: (1) Insufficient empirical validation against strong baselines and lack of standardized perceptual evaluation; (2) Limited evidence that amortised MAP, beyond toy settings, improves SR in practice under realistic degradations and benchmarks.

Supporting arguments
- Evaluation largely relies on qualitative results, PSNR/SSIM (where MSE models unsurprisingly win), and a 2D toy cross-entropy estimate. There is no human study, LPIPS, FID, or recent perceptual metrics on standard SR benchmarks (e.g., Set5/14, BSD100, DIV2K), nor head-to-head comparisons with SRGAN/ESRGAN or modern regression+perceptual-loss methods.  
- The degradation model A is a Gaussian-stride convolution learned pseudo-inverse; generalization to common bicubic kernels or unknown/real degradations is not demonstrated.  
- Two of the three proposed MAP routes (AffDG, AffLL) underperform and are deprioritized; their limitations are discussed but not resolved, weakening the “amortised MAP” thesis.  
- AffGAN exhibits artifacts (high-frequency noise) and clear semantic failure in at least one example (snake→water), underscoring the need for stronger priors/evaluation.

Feedback to improve (helpful, not decisive)
- Add rigorous perceptual evaluation (LPIPS, FID/KID, DISTS) and a small-scale human study; include comparisons with SRGAN/ESRGAN and regression+VGG/perceptual losses on standard datasets and bicubic downsampling.  
- Test robustness to mismatched/unknown A, blind SR, and multiple scales (2×, 3×, 4×).  
- Provide ablations on the affine projection (fixed vs trainable A+), instance noise schedule, and discriminator capacity; quantify stability gains.  
- Strengthen AffDG/LL via improved score/density models (score-based diffusion, modern autoregressive/flow priors) and curricula.  
- Report computational cost and training dynamics; release code.

Questions for authors
- How sensitive is performance to the choice of A and inaccuracies in A+? Can the method handle unknown or spatially varying degradations (blind SR)?  
- Can you report LPIPS/FID and comparisons on DIV2K with bicubic downsampling?  
- What is the effect of instance noise magnitude/annealing on stability and perceptual metrics?  
- Can you quantify H[qθ] or diversity to substantiate the KL vs cross-entropy discussion?  
- For AffDG/LL, could modern score/density estimators fix the observed blurriness and instability?
