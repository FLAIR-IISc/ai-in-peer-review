- Proposes amortised MAP inference for single-image SR to avoid over-smoothed MSE/MAE outputs by learning MAP-consistent reconstructions under a natural image prior.
- Introduces an affine projection layer enforcing exact LR-HR consistency ŷ = Π_Ax fθ(x), guaranteeing Aŷ = x and reframing training as minimising cross-entropy H[qθ, pY].
- Develops three training approaches: AffGAN (GAN variant with affine projection and instance noise), AffDG (uses a denoiser to estimate score ∂y log pY), and AffLL (maximum-likelihood via a learned density model).
- Shows on a 2D Swiss-roll toy that AffGAN/AffDG approximate brute-force MAP and lower cross-entropy while MSE/MAE fail.
- Demonstrates on images (grass, CelebA, ImageNet, 4×) that affine projection does not harm expressivity and can improve MSE training.
- Finds AffGAN yields sharper, more plausible images than MSE and soft-constraint GAN baselines; AffDG/AffLL tend to produce blurrier outputs in high dimensions.
- Connects the modified GAN generator objective to approximating KL[qθ||pY] and relates stochastic AffGAN to amortised variational inference.
- Uses instance noise as a principled stabiliser when supports of qθ and pY don't overlap.
- Decision: Weak Accept — novelty in amortised MAP framing and affine projection, with insightful theory and credible empirical ablations.
- Strong problem framing: identifies posterior averaging as the cause of blurriness and motivates MAP for underdetermined inverse problems.
- Positions work relative to prior SR GANs by enforcing exact LR consistency instead of soft penalties and leveraging denoising/score-matching links.
- Theoretical contribution: clean decomposition to cross-entropy and justification for modified GAN updates and instance noise.
- Empirical support: quantitative H[qθ,pY] estimates on toy data, strict LR consistency from projection, and visual improvements from AffGAN on natural images.
- Limitations: perceptual evaluation limited to PSNR/SSIM (which favor MSE); no user studies or broader perceptual metrics reported.
- Limitations: AffDG and AffLL underperform in high dimensions and the paper largely focuses on AffGAN for large-scale results.
- Limitations: comparisons to strong contemporaries (e.g., SRGAN with content loss) are limited; the image prior pY is implicit and training claims depend on data assumptions.
- Suggested improvement: add perceptual user studies or surrogate perceptual metrics (MS-SSIM, NIQE/BRISQUE) and report discriminator stability metrics with/without instance noise.
- Suggested improvement: compare against stronger baselines (SRGAN/content losses), report runtime and parameter counts, and evaluate projection overhead.
- Suggested improvement: test robustness to A mismatch, noise, blind-SR scenarios, other scale factors and color spaces, and consider learned feature spaces (e.g., VGG) for MAP discussion.
- Suggested improvement: analyse why AffDG/AffLL blur in high dimensions, report denoiser schedules/capacity effects, and try other continuous likelihoods or flows.
- Suggested improvement: formalise instance-noise annealing, provide ablations on noise level vs stability, and relate to other stabilisation techniques.
- Reproducibility suggestion: release code for computing A+, and pretrained models.
- Questions for authors: clarify whether training avoided paired supervision (discriminator only sees HR and generator conditions on independent LR) or used paired downsampled LR images.
- Questions for authors: report sensitivity to A mismatch at test time, tests on unknown blur kernels or real LR images with noise/compression.
- Questions for authors: detail A+ approximation (kernel sizes, optimisation), effects of learning A+ jointly, and any finite-support artifacts.
- Questions for authors: provide instance-noise settings/annealing schedule, discriminator/generator step ratios, and whether D was fully optimised per iteration.
- Questions for authors: give AffDG denoiser architectures/noise schedules, correlation between score estimation and SR quality, and possibility of combining AffDG with AffGAN.
- Questions for authors: report preference studies or recognition-based identity preservation tests on faces and other failure modes beyond noted examples.
- Questions for authors: demonstrate stochastic AffGAN sampling diversity per x and compare perceptually to MAP outputs.
