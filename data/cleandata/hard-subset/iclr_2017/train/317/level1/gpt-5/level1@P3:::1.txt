Summary and contributions
The paper proposes to cast single-image super-resolution (SR) as amortised MAP inference under a noiseless observation model x = Ay. The key idea is an affine projection layer that guarantees consistency Afθ(x) = x by parameterising the output as ΠAx fθ(x) = (I − A+A)fθ(x) + A+x, where A is the downsampling operator and A+ its Moore–Penrose pseudoinverse implemented as a deconvolution. Under this architecture, training to approximate MAP reduces to minimising the cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the model’s marginal output distribution and the HR data distribution, enabling learning without paired LR–HR samples. The paper proposes three practical training routes: (i) AffGAN, a variant of GAN training with a generator update that targets KL[qθ||pY] and introduces “instance noise” to stabilise training; (ii) AffDG, which backpropagates score estimates from a denoiser to follow ∇y log pY(y); and (iii) AffLL, which uses a differentiable likelihood model (PixelCNN/MCGSM) to provide −log pY. Theoretical contributions include the decomposition from MAP to cross-entropy under the affine constraint, a derivation that their GAN update targets KL, and a connection between stochastic AffGAN and amortised variational inference. Experiments on a 2D toy problem, grass textures, CelebA, and ImageNet show that AffGAN produces sharper, more plausible outputs than MSE-trained baselines and that the affine projection improves training speed and enforces consistency.

Decision: Reject (borderline)
Key reasons:
- Empirical validation is insufficient to substantiate the central amortised-MAP claim on real images; comparisons and evaluation protocols are limited, and two of the three proposed training routes (AffDG, AffLL) underperform and are not pursued beyond a single dataset.
- The practicality hinges on a known, noiseless, convolutional A and an accurate A+, assumptions that are rarely met in real SR; robustness to mismatch/noise is not evaluated.

Supporting arguments
- Motivation and placement: The critique of MSE/PSNR and the case for MAP/plausibility are well argued. The affine projection is a clean way to enforce data consistency, and the reduction to matching marginals is conceptually appealing. The work connects GANs, denoising-as-score-matching, and explicit density models under this lens; the “instance noise” idea aligns with later advances that regularise the discriminator by smoothing distributions.
- Theory: The derivation from constrained MAP to minimising H[qθ, pY] under ΠAx is sound. The KL-targeting GAN generator update is plausible under an optimal discriminator but remains heuristic in practice; nonetheless, the analysis clarifies what their objective optimises. The amortised VI connection for stochastic AffGAN is interesting.
- Empirics: 
  - The 2D Swiss-roll study nicely demonstrates that AffGAN/AffDG approach the MAP solution and achieve low estimated cross-entropy, validating the MAP proxy under controlled conditions.
  - On images, evidence is largely qualitative. AffGAN produces sharp textures and faces but also hallucinates semantically implausible content (snake→water), highlighting fidelity risks of MAP under the assumed prior and the divergence targeted. Claims about training without paired LR–HR are not convincingly demonstrated: pX is obtained by applying A to the HR corpus; real-world LR come from unknown degradations and noise.
  - Baselines are limited. There is no comparison to strong SR methods available at the time (e.g., SRResNet/SRGAN), to feature/perceptual losses, or to TV/MRF priors. Quantitative metrics are mainly PSNR/SSIM (unsurprisingly favouring MSE); there is no human study or perceptual metric (e.g., user preference, pairwise Turing tests), leaving plausibility claims anecdotal.
  - The denoiser-guided and density-guided variants largely fail to produce non-blurry results on images; the paper acknowledges instability/peakedness issues but then abandons them, weakening the “amortised MAP” story beyond GANs.
- Practicality and assumptions: The approach assumes a fixed, known A and no observation noise. Enforcing Afθ(x) = x exactly may hurt when x is noisy or when A is misspecified; the paper does not study robustness to kernel mismatch, additive noise, or compression artifacts—common in SR. The numerical approximation of A+ with finite-support deconvolutions is sensible, but the impact of approximation error and kernel size on performance is not quantified.

Additional feedback (to help improve the paper; not all are decision-critical)
- Evaluate robustness: Add experiments with (i) moderate additive noise, (ii) blur-kernel mismatch between training and test, and (iii) non-convolutional or unknown degradations. Compare hard affine projection vs soft consistency under noise.
- Perceptual evaluation: Include a small-scale human study or modern perceptual metrics; report preference rates against SRGAN-like baselines; measure LPIPS or similar (if available).
- Baselines and ablations: 
  - Compare to SRResNet/SRGAN and feature-loss methods (e.g., VGG-based) on the same setup.
  - Ablate the affine projection (hard vs soft), instance noise (on/off, schedule), and the generator update rule (standard vs KL-targeting).
- Clarify “unpaired” training: Demonstrate training on truly unpaired LR data drawn from a different process than A(y); or learn/estimate A jointly from HR↔LR (e.g., blind SR).
- Score/density guidance: Provide deeper analysis why AffDG/AffLL blur (e.g., effect of σ-annealing, score magnitude calibration, density sharpness), and whether improved score estimators (e.g., multi-scale DAEs) or flow models could help.
- Consistency vs semantics: Consider mechanisms to condition the prior on x’s content (e.g., conditional discriminators or perceptual feature matching) to mitigate semantic hallucinations while preserving sharpness.
- Details: Report the receptive field/support of A+, its approximation error (`1 + `2), and runtime/memory overhead; discuss whether A+ should remain fixed vs trainable.

Questions for the authors
- How sensitive is AffGAN to mismatch between the true downsampling kernel/noise and the assumed A? Can you report results with kernel mismatch and with moderate LR noise?
- In your “unpaired” setting, are LR images ever sourced independently (e.g., web LR) rather than synthesized via A? If not, how do you envision deploying the method when A is unknown?
- What is the impact of the finite kernel size used to approximate A+ on reconstruction quality and on the exactness of Afθ(x) = x? How large must the support be for typical 4× downsampling?
- Can you quantify the benefit of instance noise compared to (i) no noise, (ii) label smoothing, and (iii) gradient penalty, in terms of stability and final quality?
- Your generator update targets KL[q||p], which is mode-covering relative to MAP’s mode-seeking flavour. How do you reconcile this with the MAP objective H[q, p]? Do you observe entropy-driven artifacts (e.g., excessive high-frequency noise)?
- Could AffDG be improved with better score models (e.g., multi-scale score networks) or by normalising the score magnitude? What failure modes did you observe during training?
- Have you tried conditional discriminators that see both x and ŷ to better enforce content fidelity beyond the affine constraint?
