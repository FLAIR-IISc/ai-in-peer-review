- Proposes "amortised MAP" for single-image SR, training a network to map LR inputs to MAP estimates under a natural image prior rather than using MSE regression.
- Introduces an affine projection layer that enforces exact consistency with the imaging model x = Ay by projecting outputs onto {y: Ay = x}.
- Shows that under affine projection the amortised MAP reduces to minimising cross-entropy H[qθ, pY] between the reconstruction marginal and the HR data distribution.
- Develops three training schemes to approximate this objective: AffGAN (adversarial with instance noise), AffDG (backpropagating denoiser gradients), and AffLL (using a likelihood-based image model).
- Empirically, the affine projection improves optimisation and guarantees LR–HR consistency versus soft-constraint baselines.
- AffGAN produces perceptually sharper and more plausible images (textures, faces, ImageNet) compared to MSE, though it can hallucinate plausible but incorrect content and add high-frequency noise.
- Provides theoretical connections: a GAN-style generator update relates to KL minimisation and a stochastic AffGAN links to amortised variational inference.
- Technical derivations (projection decomposition, AffGAN loss, denoiser-gradient estimator) are sound and well-motivated.
- AffDG and AffLL underperform empirically and are less stable, suggesting optimisation or model-misspecification issues.
- Quantitative evaluation is limited: PSNR/SSIM favor MSE but do not capture perceptual quality; no user studies or strong perceptual metrics reported.
- Comparisons to strong perceptual SR baselines (e.g., SRGAN, perceptual-loss methods) are mostly qualitative and incomplete.
- Method assumes a known downsampling operator A; robustness to unknown/spatially-varying A or observation noise is not evaluated.
- The "no paired data" claim depends on having LR pX and HR pY consistent with the same A; domain mismatch concerns are unaddressed.
- Ablations on instance noise, noise schedules, discriminator/generator update ratios, and A+ initialization are limited.
- Recommendations: add perceptual user studies (2AFC/MOS), compare to SRGAN and feature-loss methods, report diversity and LR-consistency metrics, and run ablations on instance noise and training schedules.
- Suggest improving AffDG/LL with modern score-matching/denoising techniques and stronger density models to test whether failures are optimisation or model-related.
- Recommend exploring robustness to misspecified A, joint/estimated A or spatially-varying degradations, and potential weak content or feature-matching losses to reduce artefacts.
- Key open questions for authors: sensitivity to misspecified A and observation noise; quantitative impact of instance noise versus alternatives; direct comparisons to SRGAN and human studies; whether entropy terms or stochastic generators increase diversity; robustness to domain mismatch between pX and pY; and computational/behavioural trade-offs when computing or learning A+.
