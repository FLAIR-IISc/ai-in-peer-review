- Problem: Image super-resolution is an underdetermined inverse problem and MSE loss yields blurry, implausible outputs.  
- Proposed approach: Amortized MAP inference via a CNN with affine subspace projections to enforce LR–HR consistency.  
- MAP approximation methods: Three proposed methods — GANs (AffGAN), denoiser-guided SR, and a density model trained by maximum likelihood.  
- Theoretical contribution: Provides theoretical grounding and links GANs to variational inference.  
- Empirical result: AffGAN produces perceptually superior, sharper SR results on natural images, grass textures, and faces.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Supporting argument 1: Well-motivated and situated in literature with thorough justification for MAP and affine projections.  
- Supporting argument 2: Comprehensive experiments across diverse datasets showing robust advantages of the methods, especially AffGAN.  
- Supporting argument 3: Novelty and impact from affine projections and the GAN–variational connection.  
- Suggestion 1: Provide clearer discussion of MAP inference limitations and their practical effects.  
- Suggestion 2: Compare with sampling-based methods (e.g., diffusion models) to strengthen evaluation.  
- Suggestion 3: Include ablation studies to quantify the contribution of affine projections.  
- Suggestion 4: Address high-frequency noise seen in AffGAN outputs and propose mitigation strategies.  
- Question 1: How does AffGAN compare to state-of-the-art diffusion or other sampling-based SR methods?  
- Question 2: Can the affine projection layer be generalized to other inverse problems, and what modifications are needed?  
- Question 3: Can high-frequency noise from AffGAN be reduced via additional regularization or post-processing?  
- Conclusion: The paper is a significant, theoretically grounded, and empirically validated contribution that warrants acceptance.
