- Proposes incorporating unsupervised auxiliary tasks into deep RL agents (e.g., A3C).  
- Introduces a set of auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks.  
- Evaluates the approach on Labyrinth and Atari environments.  
- UNREAL agent achieves significantly better performance than A3C and learns faster.  
- Contribution is valuable but somewhat expected; serves as a proof-of-concept that auxiliary tasks aid feature learning.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and accessible to readers with deep RL background.  
- Reviewer asks for more information on computational resources required to train UNREAL.  
- Reviewer notes the architecture is complex and requests the authors release source code for reproducibility and extension.
