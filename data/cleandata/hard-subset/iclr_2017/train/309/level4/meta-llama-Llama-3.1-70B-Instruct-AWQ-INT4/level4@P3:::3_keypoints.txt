- Proposes novel auxiliary control and reward-prediction tasks to improve learning efficiency and robustness in deep RL.  
- Claims traditional unsupervised methods may not learn useful representations in sparse-reward environments; control-based tasks can complement policy learning.  
- Introduces two pseudo-control tasks: controlling pixel-intensity changes and controlling latent feature activations.  
- Adds a supervised immediate-reward prediction task trained offline with skewed replay sampling to balance reward/non-reward examples.  
- The UNREAL agent substantially outperforms A3C on Labyrinth and exceeds current state-of-the-art on Atari.  
- Paper is novel, well-written, and accessible to readers not familiar with deep RL.  
- Reviewer requests more experimental analysis of auxiliary tasks beyond aggregate performance.  
- Wants investigation of how auxiliary tasks affect learning dynamics, convergence rate, and training stability.  
- Requests detailed analysis of the importance of each auxiliary task and how they interact (synergies or conflicts).  
- Asks how the specific auxiliary tasks were chosen and whether other tasks could be effective.  
- Suggests considering other auxiliary task types, such as model-based learning or imitation learning.  
- Decision: Accept, based on novelty and strong empirical improvements.  
- Supporting arguments: novel control-based idea, clear presentation, and impressive experimental results.
