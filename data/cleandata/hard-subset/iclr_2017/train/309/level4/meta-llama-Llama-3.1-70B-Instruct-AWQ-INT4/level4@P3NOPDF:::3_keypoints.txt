- Proposes training RL agents with auxiliary tasks alongside the primary control task to learn stronger features via multi-task learning.  
- Introduces three auxiliary tasks: two pseudo-control tasks (control change in pixel intensity and latent feature activations) and a supervised regression task predicting immediate reward.  
- The supervised reward task is learned offline using skewed sampling of the replay buffer to balance reward/no-reward examples.  
- Achieves large gains in discrete-action-continuous-space RL, reaching baseline performance in roughly 10× fewer iterations.  
- Argues this active control-based approach complements policy learning and avoids pitfalls of passive unsupervised or model-based objectives under partial observability.  
- Novel and intuitively explained; paper is clear for deep RL readers but would benefit from exposing lower-level details earlier (e.g., L_PC before appendix).  
- Hyperparameter tuning approach is reasonable (considering top-3 performers) but distributions of best hyperparameters may differ between A3C and UNREAL.  
- Experimental analysis is limited—insufficient investigation of each auxiliary task’s individual contribution and lack of metrics beyond RL performance.  
- Pixel/feature control appears most impactful (A3C+PC outperforms others in Labyrinth); recommends deeper isolated and combined analyses of this task.  
- Overall a promising, effective approach that would be strengthened by additional experiments and analysis.
