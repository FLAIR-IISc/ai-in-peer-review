- Paper proposes augmenting policy optimization with domain-independent auxiliary tasks (control, immediate reward prediction, value function replay) sharing CNN+LSTM features, and shows improved data efficiency on Atari and Labyrinth vs A3C.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance: paper is well-written, ideas are sound, and results are convincing.  
- Request discussion of extra computational cost from optimizing auxiliary tasks: impact on training speed and which components are most costly.  
- Ask to explicitly state in abstract/introduction that separate policies are learned for each auxiliary task (to avoid misunderstanding).  
- Concern that the "feature control" auxiliary is not empirically validated: Fig.5 gives only slight initial benefit and may induce instability/convergence issues because tasks change as features are modified.  
- Suggest continuing training of steadily improving agents to evaluate longer-term performance.  
- Question why auxiliary-task weight parameters (lambda_*) were fixed to 1 rather than treated as hyperparameters; request experiments validating that choice.  
- Note that auxiliary tasks are not trained with "true" Q-learning but off-policy with multi-step empirical rewards (per OpenReview discussion).  
- Minor wording: rephrase "Policy gradient algorithms adjust the policy to maximise the expected reward, L_pi = -..." to reflect it is a loss to be minimized.  
- Equation/formula corrections: lambda_c should be inside the sum in Eq.1; r_t^(c) just below Eq.1 should be r_{t+k}^{(c)}.  
- Point out missing/incorrect references: Figure 2 is not referenced; Figure 1(d) should be referenced in Section 3.3.  
- Grammar fix: "the features discovered in this manner is shared" → "the features discovered in this manner are shared."  
- Clarify loss terms: text around Eq.2 refers to L_PC which is not defined or included in Eq.2.  
- Explain terminology/legend: define "Clip" for dueling networks in Figure 3 legend.  
- Request more ablation studies on Atari to verify if individual contribution patterns match those on Labyrinth.  
- Clarify that percentages in Figure 3 legend refer to Labyrinth (currently unclear).  
- Corrections to figure references in Section 4.1.2: replace "Figure 3 (right) shows..." with "the top-left plot of the figure" and "Figure 3 Top" with "Figure 3 Top Right."  
- Clarify Figure 5 caption/text: it claims top 5 hyperparameter settings on three Labyrinth levels but plots appear to show only two levels.  
- Fix punctuation in Section 4.2: add comma or punctuation after "for all three methods".  
- Appendix: specify where supplementary materials can be found.  
- Ask to report the value of lambda_PC (presumably 1).
