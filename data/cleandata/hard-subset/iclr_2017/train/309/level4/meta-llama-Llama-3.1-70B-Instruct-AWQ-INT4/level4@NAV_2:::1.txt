This paper proposes a novel approach to augmenting deep reinforcement learning agents with unsupervised auxiliary tasks. The authors introduce a range of auxiliary control tasks and reward prediction tasks, which they evaluate on both the Labyrinth and Atari environments. The proposed UNREAL agent achieves significant performance gains over the baseline A3C agent, both in terms of final performance and learning speed.

The paper is well-written and clear, making it accessible to readers with a background in deep reinforcement learning. The authors provide a thorough explanation of their approach, including the architecture and training procedure for the UNREAL agent.

One of the key strengths of this paper is its ability to demonstrate the effectiveness of auxiliary tasks in improving the performance of deep reinforcement learning agents. The results presented in the paper are impressive, with the UNREAL agent achieving state-of-the-art performance on both Labyrinth and Atari.

However, it is worth noting that the idea of using auxiliary tasks to improve the performance of deep reinforcement learning agents is not entirely new. Previous work has demonstrated the effectiveness of auxiliary tasks in various domains, and it is not surprising that the authors' approach yields similar benefits.

In terms of potential improvements, the authors could provide more details on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent. This information would be useful for readers who are interested in implementing the approach in their own work.

Additionally, the overall architecture of the UNREAL agent is quite complex, and it would be beneficial for the authors to release the source code for their model. This would allow other researchers to more easily build upon and extend the authors' work.

Overall, I believe that this paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of deep reinforcement learning, and I recommend its acceptance.
