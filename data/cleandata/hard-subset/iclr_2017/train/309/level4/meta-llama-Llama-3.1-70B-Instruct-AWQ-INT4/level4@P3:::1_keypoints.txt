- Proposes UNREAL: integrating unsupervised auxiliary tasks into A3C for deep RL.
- Introduces auxiliary control and auxiliary reward tasks, evaluated on Labyrinth and Atari.
- UNREAL achieves significant gains in final performance and learning speed over standard A3C.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, accessible to deep RL readers.
- Provides thorough explanation of auxiliary tasks and UNREAL architecture, grounded in prior work.
- Experimental results are well-presented with clear visualizations and direct A3C comparisons.
- Ablation studies quantify the contribution of each auxiliary task to performance improvements.
- Convincing answer to whether auxiliary tasks improve deep RL: yes, they do.
- Criticism: improvements are not entirely surprising since relevant auxiliary tasks are expected to help.
- Improvement suggestion: provide more details on computational resources (GPUs, wall-clock training time) beyond the 32 async threads.
- Request that authors release source code to facilitate further research and experimentation.
- Questions for authors: exact GPUs and training time used; will the source code be released?
