- Proposes training RL agents on auxiliary tasks to learn stronger features that complement the main control policy.  
- Introduces two pseudo-control tasks: controlling change in pixel intensity and controlling activation of latent features.  
- Adds a supervised regression auxiliary task to predict immediate reward, learned offline with skewed replay sampling to balance reward occurrence.  
- Reports strong empirical gains: reaches baseline performance in roughly 10× fewer iterations on discrete-action continuous-space tasks.  
- Argues this active, control-based auxiliary learning is preferable to passive unsupervised or model-based objectives under partial observability.  
- Presents a novel and intuitive alternative to unsupervised representation learning by leveraging what the agent can control.  
- Exposition is clear at a high level, but some low-level details (e.g., L_PC) should be introduced earlier rather than only in the appendix.  
- Methodology and hyperparameter search are sound; using top-3 helps ensure fair comparison across methods despite differing hyperparameter distributions.  
- Main weakness: limited analysis of how auxiliary tasks themselves affect learned representations beyond aggregate performance; pixel/feature control merits deeper, isolated study.
