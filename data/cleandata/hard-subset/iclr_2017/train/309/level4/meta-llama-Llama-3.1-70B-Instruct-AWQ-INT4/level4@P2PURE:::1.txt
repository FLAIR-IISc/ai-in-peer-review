This paper introduces a method for incorporating unsupervised auxiliary tasks into a deep reinforcement learning (RL) agent, specifically building upon the A3C framework. The authors propose a set of auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks, which they evaluate in the Labyrinth and Atari environments. The proposed UNREAL agent demonstrates significant performance improvements over A3C, achieving both better results and faster learning. This contribution is undoubtedly valuable to the conference. However, the outcome is somewhat expected, as the addition of relevant auxiliary tasks typically facilitates more efficient and effective feature learning. In essence, this paper serves as a proof-of-concept for this idea.

The paper is well-written and accessible to readers with a background in deep RL. To provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed approach, could the authors please comment on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent? Furthermore, due to the complexity of the overall architecture, would the authors be willing to release the source code for their model, facilitating easier replication and extension of their work?
