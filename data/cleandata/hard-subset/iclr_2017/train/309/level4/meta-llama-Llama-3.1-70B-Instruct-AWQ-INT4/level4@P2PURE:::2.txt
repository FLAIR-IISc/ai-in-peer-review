This paper proposes an approach to improve feature learning in deep reinforcement learning by augmenting the main policy's optimization problem with terms corresponding to domain-independent auxiliary tasks. These tasks involve control (learning other policies that attempt to maximally modify the state space), immediate reward prediction, and value function replay. Except for the latter, these auxiliary tasks are used to shape the features by sharing the CNN+LSTM feature extraction network. The experimental results demonstrate the benefits of this approach on Atari and Labyrinth problems, with a notable improvement in data efficiency compared to A3C.

The paper is well-written, and the ideas are sound, making a strong case for acceptance. I have a few minor suggestions and questions to further improve the paper:

- It would be beneficial to discuss the extra computational cost of optimizing these auxiliary tasks. What is the impact on training speed, and which components are the most costly?
- To avoid potential confusion, it would be helpful to clarify in the abstract and introduction that the agent learns different policies for each task. Initially, the phrase "the agent also maximises many other pseudo-reward functions simultaneously by reinforcement learning" suggested that a single policy optimizes all rewards together, which was later corrected upon reaching equation 1.
- The "feature control" idea is intriguing, but the preliminary experiment in Figure 5 is not entirely convincing, as it only shows a slight initial improvement. I appreciate the idea, but I am concerned about potential stability and convergence issues, as the task is changing during learning due to the modification of extracted features.
- Given that the performance of the agents is still improving, it would be interesting to continue training them to see how far they can go, at least for the best-performing ones.
- It is unclear why the auxiliary tasks' weight parameters (the lambda_*) are not treated as hyperparameters to be optimized. Were there any experiments to validate the choice of using 1?
- It would be helpful to mention that the auxiliary tasks are not trained with "true" Q-Learning, as they are trained off-policy with more than one step of empirical rewards, as discussed in the OpenReview comments.

Minor points:

- The sentence "Policy gradient algorithms adjust the policy to maximise the expected reward, L_pi = -..." should be rephrased, as L_pi is actually a loss to be minimized.
- In equation 1, lambda_c should be included within the sum.
- The text below equation 1 should read "rt+k^(c)" instead of "rt^(c)".
- Figure 2 is not referenced in the text, and Figure 1(d) should be referenced in section 3.3.
- The sentence "the features discovered in this manner is shared" should be corrected to "are shared".
- The text surrounding equation 2 refers to the loss L_PC, but this term is not defined and is not explicitly included in equation 2.
- It would be helpful to explain what "Clip" means for dueling networks in the legend of Figure 3.
- Additional ablated versions on Atari would be beneficial to see if the same patterns of individual contribution as on Labyrinth are observed.
- The legend of Figure 3 mentions percentages for Labyrinth, which is not clear from the text.
- In section 4.1.2, the sentence "Figure 3 (right) shows..." should be corrected to "the top left plot of the figure." Similarly, "This is shown in Figure 3 Top" should be corrected to "Figure 3 Top Right."
- The sentence "Figure 5 shows the learning curves for the top 5 hyperparameter settings on three Labyrinth navigation levels" likely refers to the left and middle plots of the figure, showing results for only two levels.
- In section 4.2, a comma is missing after "methods" in the sentence "The left side shows the average performance curves of the top 5 agents for all three methods the right half shows..."
- The appendix mentions "Further details are included in the supplementary materials," but it is unclear where these materials are located.
- What is the value of lambda_PC? (It is likely 1, but confirmation would be helpful.)

Note: I am aware that some of these questions were already addressed in the comments, and I do not expect re-answers to those points.
