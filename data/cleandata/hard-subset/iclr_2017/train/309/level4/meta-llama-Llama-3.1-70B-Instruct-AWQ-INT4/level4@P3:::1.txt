This paper proposes a novel approach to deep reinforcement learning by incorporating unsupervised auxiliary tasks into a deep RL agent, specifically A3C. The authors introduce a range of auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks, which they evaluate in both the Labyrinth and Atari environments. The proposed UNREAL agent demonstrates significant performance improvements over the standard A3C agent, both in terms of final performance and learning speed.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, making it accessible to readers with a background in deep RL. The authors provide a thorough explanation of their approach, including the various auxiliary tasks and the UNREAL agent architecture. The experimental results are also well-presented, with clear visualizations and comparisons to the standard A3C agent.

The key question addressed by this paper is: Can the addition of unsupervised auxiliary tasks improve the performance of a deep RL agent? The authors provide a convincing affirmative answer, demonstrating that their UNREAL agent can learn faster and achieve better performance than the standard A3C agent.

The approach is well-motivated, building on existing work in deep RL and auxiliary tasks. The authors provide a clear explanation of how their auxiliary tasks are designed to improve the agent's performance and how they are integrated into the UNREAL agent architecture.

The paper supports its claims through extensive experimental results, including comparisons to the standard A3C agent and ablation studies to evaluate the contribution of each auxiliary task. The results demonstrate that the UNREAL agent can achieve significant performance improvements over the standard A3C agent, both in terms of final performance and learning speed.

One potential criticism of the paper is that the results are not entirely surprising, as the addition of relevant auxiliary tasks is likely to improve the agent's performance. However, the paper still provides a valuable contribution to the field by demonstrating the effectiveness of this approach and providing a clear explanation of how to integrate auxiliary tasks into a deep RL agent.

To further improve the paper, I would like to see more discussion on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent. The authors mention that the agent is trained using 32 asynchronous threads, but it would be helpful to provide more details on the computational resources required, such as the number of GPUs used and the training time.

Additionally, I would like to see the authors release the source code for their model, as this would facilitate further research and experimentation with the UNREAL agent.

Questions for the authors:

1. Can you provide more details on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent, such as the number of GPUs used and the training time?
2. Are you willing to release the source code for your model to facilitate further research and experimentation with the UNREAL agent?
