- Proposes novel RL approach using auxiliary control and reward-prediction tasks to improve data efficiency and robustness.  
- Argues traditional unsupervised methods may not learn useful representations; instead learn to control local and internal features.  
- Auxiliary tasks (pixel and feature control) are intuitive, well-explained, and methodologically sound.  
- Combines benefits of two state-of-the-art methods into a new approach.  
- Demonstrates significant performance gains on discrete-action, continuous-space RL tasks.  
- Achieves baseline performance with roughly 10x fewer iterations.  
- Reviewer accepts paper primarily for its novelty and strong empirical improvements.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and balances high-level intuition with low-level details.  
- Lacks experimental analysis of how auxiliary tasks affect learned representations beyond performance metrics.  
- Requests deeper analysis of pixel vs feature control effects and explanation of why these tasks are effective.  
- Asks for discussion of extensions to more complex environments and potential limitations or challenges.
