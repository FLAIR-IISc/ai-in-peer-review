This paper presents an innovative approach to enhance feature learning in deep reinforcement learning by incorporating auxiliary tasks into the main policy's optimization problem. These domain-independent tasks encompass control, immediate reward prediction, and value function replay, which, except for the latter, are utilized to shape the features by sharing the CNN+LSTM feature extraction network. Experimental results on Atari and Labyrinth problems demonstrate the effectiveness of this method, showcasing improved data efficiency compared to A3C.

The paper is well-written, with sound ideas and convincing results, making it a strong candidate for acceptance. However, I have a few minor suggestions for improvement:

- It would be beneficial to discuss the additional computational cost of optimizing the auxiliary tasks and their impact on training speed. Identifying the most costly components would also be helpful.
- To avoid potential misinterpretation, consider rephrasing the abstract and introduction to explicitly state that the agent learns different policies for each task. This clarification would prevent readers from assuming a single policy optimizes all rewards simultaneously.
- The "feature control" concept, although intriguing, lacks empirical validation. The preliminary experiment in Fig. 5 is not conclusive, and the changing task during learning may raise stability and convergence concerns.
- Given the agents' performance is still improving, it would be valuable to continue training to observe their full potential.
- The choice of weight parameters (lambda_*) for auxiliary tasks seems arbitrary; were there any experiments to validate the use of 1 as a good choice?
- Please note that auxiliary tasks are not trained with "true" Q-Learning, as they are trained off-policy with more than one step of empirical rewards.
