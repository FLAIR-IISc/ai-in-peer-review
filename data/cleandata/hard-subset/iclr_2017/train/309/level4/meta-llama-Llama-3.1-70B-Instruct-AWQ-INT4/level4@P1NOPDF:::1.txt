This paper proposes a method for incorporating unsupervised auxiliary tasks into a deep reinforcement learning (RL) agent, such as A3C. The authors introduce a set of auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks, and evaluate the agent's performance in the Labyrinth and Atari environments. The proposed UNREAL agent achieves significantly better results than A3C and exhibits faster learning. This is undoubtedly a valuable contribution to the conference. However, the outcome is not entirely unexpected, as adding relevant auxiliary tasks to the primary goal should generally facilitate improved and accelerated feature learning. This paper serves as a proof of concept for this idea.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow for readers with a background in deep RL. The authors' presentation of their work is clear and concise, making it accessible to those familiar with the field.

One aspect that warrants further discussion is the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent. Could the authors provide more information on this topic?

Furthermore, the overall architecture of the UNREAL agent is quite complex. Would the authors be willing to release the source code for their model, allowing other researchers to build upon and extend their work?
