- Proposes training RL agents with auxiliary tasks that complement the main control objective via multi-task learning to learn stronger features.  
- Introduces two pseudo-control tasks: controlling changes in pixel intensity and controlling activation of latent features.  
- Adds a supervised regression task to predict immediate rewards after event sequences, trained offline.  
- Reward predictor is trained using skewed sampling from the experience replay buffer to balance reward vs. non-reward examples.  
- Shows agents with these auxiliary tasks reach baseline performance roughly 10× faster on discrete-action, continuous-space RL tasks.  
- Contrasts with unsupervised/model-based methods by leveraging agent control to learn useful local and internal feature representations under partial observability.  
- Method is novel and intuitively explained, but some low-level details (e.g., explicit mention of L_PC outside the appendix) are missing.  
- Hyperparameter tuning approach is reasonable, using top-3 performing configurations to find good settings for A3C and UNREAL.  
- Experimental weakness: limited analysis of how each auxiliary task individually contributes beyond overall performance gains.  
- Calls for deeper investigation into pixel/feature control effects, especially given A3C+PC’s strong Labyrinth results relative to other methods.
