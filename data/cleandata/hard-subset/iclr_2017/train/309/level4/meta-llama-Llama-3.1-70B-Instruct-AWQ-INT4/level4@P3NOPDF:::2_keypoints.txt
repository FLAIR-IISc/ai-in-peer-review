- Paper proposes augmenting policy optimization with domain-independent auxiliary tasks (control, immediate reward prediction, value replay) to improve feature learning in deep RL.
- Experiments on Atari and Labyrinth show improved data efficiency over A3C; paper is well-written and results are convincing—strong candidate for acceptance.
- Authors should quantify extra computational cost of optimizing auxiliary tasks, including training speed loss and most expensive components.
- Abstract/introduction must clearly state the agent learns separate policies for each task to avoid implying a single policy optimizes all rewards jointly.
- The "feature control" auxiliary task lacks convincing empirical validation; preliminary results show only slight early improvements and may cause instability due to changing features during learning.
- Continue training top-performing agents longer, since learning curves are still improving, to observe their full potential.
- Treat auxiliary task weight parameters (λ*) as tunable hyperparameters and provide experiments validating the choice (λ* = 1 may not be optimal).
- Acknowledge that auxiliary tasks are not trained with "true" Q-Learning—training is off-policy with multi-step empirical rewards (as noted in OpenReview).
- Minor correction: L_pi is presented as maximized but is actually a loss to be minimized.
- Equation 1 formatting: λ_c should be inside the summation.
- Below equation 1, rt^(c) should be rt+k^(c).
- Figure references: Figure 2 is not referenced; Figure 1(d) should be referenced in section 3.3.
- Grammar: "the features discovered in this manner is shared" → "are shared."
- Equation 2/text mismatch: L_PC is referenced but not defined or included in equation 2.
- Figure 3 legend should explain what "Clip" means for dueling networks and clarify that percentage labels refer to Labyrinth.
- Request additional ablated versions on Atari to see if per-task contribution patterns match Labyrinth results.
- Figure 3 caption/refs: correct "Figure 3 (right) shows..." to "Figure 3 (top left) shows..." and "Figure 3 Top" to "Figure 3 Top Right."
- Figure 5 caption: clarify it likely shows only two Labyrinth levels (left and middle plots), not three.
- Section 4.2 punctuation: add comma after "methods" in the sentence about left/right sides of the figure.
- Appendix: clarify where supplementary materials are located.
- Value of λ_PC is not specified in the paper (likely 1)—state it explicitly.
