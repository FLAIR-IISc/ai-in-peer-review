- Proposes augmenting deep RL agents (e.g., A3C) with unsupervised auxiliary tasks.  
- Introduces auxiliary control and auxiliary reward tasks, evaluated on Labyrinth and Atari.  
- UNREAL shows significant improvements in final performance and learning speed over A3C.  
- Contribution is valuable to the conference.  
- Result not entirely unexpected—acts as a proof-of-concept that auxiliary tasks aid feature learning.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible to deep RL experts.  
- Requests more detail on computational resources required to train UNREAL.  
- Architecture is complex; recommends releasing source code to improve reproducibility.
