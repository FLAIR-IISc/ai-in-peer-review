This paper introduces an approach to integrating unsupervised auxiliary tasks into a deep reinforcement learning (RL) framework, specifically with the A3C algorithm. The authors propose and evaluate several auxiliary control and reward tasks within the Labyrinth and Atari environments. The UNREAL agent demonstrates substantial performance improvements over A3C, achieving faster learning and better outcomes. This contribution is undoubtedly valuable to the conference.

While the results are noteworthy, they are not entirely unexpected, as the incorporation of relevant auxiliary tasks generally facilitates improved feature learning and shaping. This study primarily serves as a proof of concept, validating the effectiveness of this approach.

The paper is well-structured and accessible to readers with a background in deep RL. However, to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method, the authors should discuss the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent.

Furthermore, due to the complexity of the overall architecture, it would be beneficial for the authors to consider releasing the source code for their model to facilitate reproducibility and further research.
