- Proposes training RL agents with auxiliary tasks to learn stronger features: two pseudo-control tasks (control pixel-intensity change and latent feature activation) plus a supervised regression task to predict immediate reward.  
- Uses skewed offline sampling from the replay buffer to balance reward/no-reward examples for the reward-prediction task.  
- Novel approach that achieves state-of-the-art results on challenging benchmarks (Labyrinth and Atari).  
- Clear motivation and strong positioning within related work.  
- Thorough and sound empirical evaluation showing significant performance gains over prior methods.  
- Paper is well-organized and easy to follow with a detailed methodology.  
- Weakness: lacks experimental analysis of how auxiliary tasks influence the learning dynamics and learned features.  
- Suggestion: explore effects of different auxiliary task types and combinations, and provide implementation details including hyperparameters.  
- Open questions: how auxiliary tasks change features/learning, effects of task combinations, scalability to more complex environments, and limitations/future work.
