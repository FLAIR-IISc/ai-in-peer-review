**Summary of the Paper**

This paper introduces a novel approach to deep reinforcement learning by augmenting the main policy's optimization problem with terms corresponding to domain-independent auxiliary tasks. These tasks are designed to improve feature learning and include control tasks, immediate reward prediction, and value function replay. The authors demonstrate the benefits of this approach on Atari and Labyrinth problems, achieving significant improvements in data efficiency and robustness to hyperparameter settings.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

I recommend accepting this paper. The key reasons for this choice are:

1. The paper presents a well-motivated and novel approach to improving feature learning in deep reinforcement learning.
2. The experimental results demonstrate significant improvements in data efficiency and robustness to hyperparameter settings on challenging problems.

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper is well-written, and the ideas are sound. The authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed approach and its relationship to existing work in the field. The experimental results are convincing, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the benefits and limitations of their approach.

**Additional Feedback**

To further improve the paper, I suggest addressing the following points:

* Provide more information about the extra computational cost of optimizing the auxiliary tasks and how it affects training speed.
* Clarify in the abstract and introduction that the agent is learning different policies for each task.
* Validate the "feature control" idea empirically and discuss potential stability and convergence issues.
* Consider continuing training the agents to see how far they can improve.
* Justify the choice of hyperparameters for the auxiliary tasks and consider optimizing them.
* Mention that the auxiliary tasks are not trained with "true" Q-Learning since they are trained off-policy with more than one step of empirical rewards.
* Address minor issues with notation, figure references, and text clarity.

**Questions for the Authors**

* Can you provide more information about the computational cost of optimizing the auxiliary tasks and how it affects training speed?
* How did you choose the hyperparameters for the auxiliary tasks, and did you consider optimizing them?
* Can you provide more empirical evidence to validate the "feature control" idea and discuss potential stability and convergence issues?
