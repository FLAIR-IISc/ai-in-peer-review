- Introduces a novel method that augments policy optimization with domain-independent auxiliary tasks (control tasks, immediate reward prediction, value replay).
- Aims to improve feature learning in deep reinforcement learning.
- Demonstrates significant improvements in data efficiency and hyperparameter robustness on Atari and Labyrinth.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty and empirical gains.
- Key reasons: well-motivated idea and strong experimental results showing data-efficiency and robustness.
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation and relation to prior work.
- Experimental analysis is thorough, discussing benefits and limitations.
- Request more detail on the extra computational cost and impact on training speed.
- Clarify in abstract/introduction that the agent learns separate policies for each task.
- Empirically validate the "feature control" idea and discuss potential stability and convergence issues.
- Suggest continuing training longer to assess further improvements.
- Justify and possibly optimize hyperparameter choices for auxiliary tasks.
- Note that auxiliary tasks are trained off-policy with multi-step empirical rewards (not "true" Q-learning).
- Fix minor issues in notation, figure references, and text clarity.
- Questions for authors: provide more info on computational cost, hyperparameter selection/optimization, and empirical evidence plus stability analysis for feature control.
