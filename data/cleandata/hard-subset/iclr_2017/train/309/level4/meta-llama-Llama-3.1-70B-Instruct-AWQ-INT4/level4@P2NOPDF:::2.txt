This paper proposes an approach to improve feature learning in deep reinforcement learning by incorporating domain-independent auxiliary tasks into the main policy's optimization problem. These tasks include control, immediate reward prediction, and value function replay, which help shape the features by sharing the CNN+LSTM feature extraction network. The experiments demonstrate the benefits of this approach on Atari and Labyrinth problems, showing significant improvements in data efficiency compared to A3C.

The paper is well-written, and the ideas are sound. The results are convincing, making this a clear acceptance. However, I have a few minor suggestions for improvement:

1. The authors should discuss the extra computational cost of optimizing the auxiliary tasks. How much does this impact training speed, and which components are the most costly?

2. The abstract and introduction could be clarified to indicate that the agent learns different policies for each task. Initially, the phrase "the agent also maximises many other pseudo-reward functions simultaneously by reinforcement learning" suggested that a single policy was being learned to optimize all rewards together.

3. The "feature control" idea, although intriguing, is not empirically validated. The preliminary experiment in Figure 5 shows only minor initial improvements, and the changing task during learning may raise stability and convergence concerns.

4. Given the agents' steady performance improvement, it would be interesting to continue training to see how far they can go, at least for the best-performing ones.

5. The auxiliary task weight parameters (λ*) could be hyperparameters to optimize. Were there any experiments to validate the choice of using 1?

6. The authors should mention that auxiliary tasks are not trained with "true" Q-Learning, as they are trained off-policy with more than one step of empirical rewards.

Minor points:

- The sentence "Policy gradient algorithms adjust the policy to maximise the expected reward, L_pi = -..." should be rephrased, as it is a loss to be minimized.

- In equation 1, λ_c should be within the sum.

- Figure 2 is not referenced in the text, and Figure 1(d) should be referenced in section 3.3.

- The sentence "the features discovered in this manner is shared" should be corrected to "are shared".

- The text around equation 2 refers to the loss L_PC, but it is not defined and not explicitly included in equation 2.

- The legend of Figure 3 should explain what "Clip" means for dueling networks.

- More ablated versions on Atari would be helpful to see if the same patterns of individual contribution as on Labyrinth are observed.

- The legend of Figure 3 should clarify that the percentages mentioned are for Labyrinth.

- In section 4.1.2, "Figure 3 (right) shows..." should be corrected to "the top left plot of the figure." Also, "This is shown in Figure 3 Top" should be corrected to "Figure 3 Top Right".

- The sentence "Figure 5 shows the learning curves for the top 5 hyperparameter settings on three Labyrinth navigation levels" should be corrected to refer to the left and middle plots of the figure, showing results for only two levels.

- In section 4.2, a comma is missing after "methods".

- The appendix should specify where the supplementary materials are included.

- The value of λ_PC should be provided (presumably 1).
