This paper proposes a method for incorporating unsupervised auxiliary tasks into a deep reinforcement learning (RL) agent, such as A3C. The authors introduce several auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks, and evaluate the agent's performance in the Labyrinth and Atari environments. The proposed UNREAL agent demonstrates significant improvements over A3C, both in terms of performance and learning speed. This contribution is undoubtedly valuable to the conference.

However, the outcome is not entirely unexpected, as the addition of relevant auxiliary tasks should generally facilitate better and faster feature learning. In essence, this paper serves as a proof of concept for this idea.

The paper is well-written and easily accessible to readers with expertise in deep RL. Nevertheless, I would appreciate it if the authors could provide more information about the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent.

Furthermore, the overall architecture of the UNREAL agent is quite complex. I would like to know whether the authors plan to release the source code for their model, which would facilitate further research and experimentation.
