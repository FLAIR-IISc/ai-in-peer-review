- Paper proposes using domain-independent auxiliary tasks (control, immediate reward prediction, value replay) sharing CNN+LSTM features to improve deep RL feature learning.
- Experiments on Atari and Labyrinth show significant data-efficiency improvements over A3C; writing and ideas are sound and merit acceptance.
- Discuss the extra computational cost of optimizing auxiliary tasks: impact on training speed and which components are most expensive.
- Clarify abstract/introduction to state that separate policies are learned per auxiliary task rather than a single policy optimizing all rewards.
- "Feature control" idea lacks strong empirical validation; Figure 5 shows only minor early gains and raises stability/convergence concerns due to changing tasks.
- Suggest running longer training for the best agents to see ultimate performance limits given steady improvements.
- Treat auxiliary-task weight parameters (λ*) as tunable hyperparameters and report experiments validating the choice of 1.
- Note that auxiliary tasks are trained off-policy with multi-step empirical rewards and are not "true" Q-Learning; mention this explicitly.
- Rephrase the policy gradient sentence to reflect that L_pi is a loss to be minimized, not directly maximized.
- Fix equation formatting: include λ_c inside the sum in equation 1 and correct the reward reference below equation 1 to r_{t+k}^{(c)}.
- Reference Figure 2 in the text and reference Figure 1(d) in section 3.3 where appropriate.
- Grammar correction: change "the features discovered in this manner is shared" to "are shared".
