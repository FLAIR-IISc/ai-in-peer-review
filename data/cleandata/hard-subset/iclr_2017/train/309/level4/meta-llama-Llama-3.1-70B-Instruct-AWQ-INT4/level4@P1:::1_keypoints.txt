- Proposes UNREAL: augment deep RL (A3C) with unsupervised auxiliary control and reward tasks, evaluated on Labyrinth and Atari.
- UNREAL significantly outperforms A3C, learns faster, and is more robust to hyperparameter settings.
- Contribution is valuable as a proof-of-concept, though the result is unsurprising since relevant auxiliary tasks aid feature shaping.
- Paper is well written and easy to follow for readers with deep RL expertise.
- Acceptance decision based on strong empirical performance and clear exposition.
- Authors provide clear explanations of auxiliary tasks and the UNREAL architecture.
- Reviewer requests clarification on computational resources required to train UNREAL.
- Reviewer suggests releasing source code due to the architecture’s complexity and asks if authors are willing to do so.
