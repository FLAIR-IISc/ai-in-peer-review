This paper proposes a novel approach to enhancing deep reinforcement learning (RL) agents, such as A3C, by incorporating unsupervised auxiliary tasks. The authors introduce a range of auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks, which they evaluate in the Labyrinth and Atari environments. The proposed UNREAL agent demonstrates significant performance improvements over A3C, both in terms of final performance and learning speed. This contribution is undoubtedly valuable to the conference.

However, the results are not entirely unexpected, as the addition of relevant auxiliary tasks should generally facilitate better and faster feature learning. In this sense, the paper can be seen as a proof of concept, validating the idea that unsupervised auxiliary tasks can be a useful augmentation to deep RL agents.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow for readers with a background in deep RL. The authors present their ideas clearly, making the paper accessible to experts in the field.

To further strengthen the paper, it would be beneficial for the authors to provide information on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent. This detail would be helpful in assessing the practicality and scalability of the proposed approach.

Additionally, the overall architecture of the UNREAL agent is quite complex. To facilitate reproducibility and further research, the authors may consider releasing the source code for their model.
