- Proposes augmenting policy optimization with domain-independent auxiliary tasks (control, immediate reward prediction, value replay) sharing a CNN+LSTM feature extractor.
- Experiments on Atari and Labyrinth show significantly improved data efficiency vs. A3C.
- Paper is well-written, ideas are sound, and results are convincing — clear acceptance recommendation.
- Request to report extra computational cost: impact on training speed and which components are most costly.
- Clarify abstract/introduction to state that different policies are learned per task (avoid implying a single policy optimizes all pseudo-rewards).
- "Feature control" idea lacks convincing empirical validation; Figure 5 shows only slight initial improvements and raises stability/convergence concerns due to changing tasks during learning.
- Recommend longer training for best-performing agents since learning curves were still improving.
- Treat auxiliary task weights (lambda_*) as tunable hyperparameters and report any validation/experiments for choosing value 1.
- Note auxiliary tasks are not trained with "true" Q-learning but off-policy with multi-step empirical rewards (as discussed in OpenReview).
