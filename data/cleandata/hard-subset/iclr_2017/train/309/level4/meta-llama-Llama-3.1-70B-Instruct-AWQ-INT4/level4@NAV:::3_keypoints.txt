- Introduces a novel RL training approach that adds auxiliary tasks to encourage development of more robust features.  
- Proposes three auxiliary tasks: two pseudo-control tasks (control of pixel intensity changes and latent feature activations) and one supervised regression task predicting immediate rewards.  
- The reward-prediction task is trained offline using skewed sampling from the replay buffer to balance reward/non-reward examples.  
- Empirical results show agents with these auxiliary tasks significantly outperform baselines on discrete-action, continuous-space RL tasks and reach baseline performance in fewer iterations.  
- Approach departs from traditional unsupervised or model-based methods by focusing on controllable local/internal features rather than full input or environment modeling.  
- Novelty lies in leveraging the agent’s control over the environment to improve learning; the high-level explanations make the intuition accessible.  
- Suggests clarifying terminology (explicitly introducing L_PC before the appendix) for readers familiar with deep RL.  
- Methodology is sound; optimal hyperparameter distributions may differ between A3C and UNREAL, and using top-3 performance helps identify best hyperparameters within search ranges.  
- Limitation: lacks deeper experimental analysis isolating the specific contributions of each auxiliary task beyond overall performance gains.  
- Observation/recommendation: pixel/feature control appears most influential (A3C+PC excels in Labyrinth); further isolated or in-depth study of this effect is warranted.
