This paper proposes an approach to augmenting deep reinforcement learning (RL) agents, such as A3C, with unsupervised auxiliary tasks. The authors introduce a set of auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks, which they evaluate in the Labyrinth and Atari environments. The proposed UNREAL agent demonstrates significant performance improvements over A3C, both in terms of final performance and learning speed. This contribution is undoubtedly valuable to the conference.

However, the result is not entirely unexpected, as incorporating relevant auxiliary tasks should generally facilitate better and faster feature learning. In this sense, the paper can be viewed as a proof-of-concept for this idea.

The paper is well-written and accessible to readers with expertise in deep RL. To further enhance the paper's impact, it would be beneficial for the authors to provide additional details on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent.

Furthermore, the overall architecture of the UNREAL agent is quite complex. To facilitate reproducibility and further research, the authors may consider releasing the source code for their model.

After reviewing the authors' rebuttal, I did not find any new information that would alter my initial assessment.
