This paper proposes a novel approach to deep reinforcement learning by incorporating unsupervised auxiliary tasks into a deep RL agent, such as A3C. The authors introduce a range of auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks, which they evaluate in both the Labyrinth and Atari environments. The proposed UNREAL agent demonstrates significant performance improvements over the standard A3C agent, both in terms of final performance and learning speed.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow for readers with expertise in deep reinforcement learning. The authors provide a clear explanation of their methodology and present their results in a well-organized manner.

However, the outcome of the paper is not entirely surprising, as adding relevant auxiliary tasks to the learning process can be expected to enhance feature shaping and improve overall performance. This paper can be seen as a proof-of-concept for this idea, demonstrating its effectiveness in a practical setting.

To further strengthen the paper, the authors could provide additional information on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent. This would be particularly useful for readers looking to replicate the results or apply the method to their own projects.

Additionally, considering the complexity of the overall architecture, it would be beneficial for the authors to release the source code for their model. This would facilitate further research and experimentation, allowing others to build upon the work presented in this paper.

Overall, the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of deep reinforcement learning, and with some additional details, it has the potential to be even more impactful.
