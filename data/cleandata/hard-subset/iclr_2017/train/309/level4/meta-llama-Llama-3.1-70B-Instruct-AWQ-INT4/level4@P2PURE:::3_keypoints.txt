- Proposes training RL agents on auxiliary tasks alongside primary objectives to develop stronger features.  
- Introduces two pseudo-control tasks: controlling pixel-intensity changes and controlling activations of latent features.  
- Proposes a supervised reward-prediction regression task learned offline via skewed replay sampling to balance reward occurrences.  
- Agents with these auxiliary tasks achieve substantial gains, reaching baseline performance ~10× faster on discrete-action continuous-space tasks.  
- Approach contrasts with passive unsupervised/model-based learning by leveraging controllable, local/internal feature learning under partial observability.  
- Novel and intuitive alternative to unsupervised learning that capitalizes on the agent’s ability to control the environment.  
- Exposition is clear at a high level but would benefit from more low-level detail (e.g., explicitly naming L_PC before appendix).  
- Methodology for measuring top-3 performance is sound, assuming optimal hyperparameters lie within explored ranges.  
- Caveat: best-hyperparameter distributions may differ between A3C and UNREAL, which could affect comparisons.  
- Limited experimental analysis beyond task performance; more investigation needed into why pixel/feature control yields the largest gains (e.g., additional metrics).
