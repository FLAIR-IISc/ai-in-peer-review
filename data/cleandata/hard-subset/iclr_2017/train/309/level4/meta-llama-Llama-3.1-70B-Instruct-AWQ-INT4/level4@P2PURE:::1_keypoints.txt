- Introduces a method for incorporating unsupervised auxiliary tasks into deep RL, built on the A3C framework.  
- Proposes a set of auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks.  
- Evaluates the approach on Labyrinth and Atari environments.  
- The UNREAL agent shows significant performance improvements over A3C, with better results and faster learning.  
- Contribution is valuable to the conference.  
- Outcome is somewhat expected since relevant auxiliary tasks generally aid feature learning — essentially a proof-of-concept.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible to readers with deep RL background.  
- Reviewer asks authors to report computational resources required to train UNREAL.  
- Reviewer requests that authors release source code to facilitate replication and extension.
