- Proposes UNREAL: integrating unsupervised auxiliary control and reward tasks into deep RL agents (e.g., A3C).  
- Evaluated the auxiliary tasks on Labyrinth and Atari benchmarks.  
- UNREAL yields significant gains in final performance and learning speed over standard A3C.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible to deep RL experts.  
- Methodology and results are clearly explained and well-organized.  
- Results are not entirely surprising; the work functions as a proof-of-concept that auxiliary tasks improve feature shaping.  
- Suggestion: report computational resources required to train UNREAL for reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: release source code to help replication and further research given the architecture complexity.  
- Overall a valuable contribution that would be more impactful with additional details.  
- Reviewer rating: 8/10; decision: Accept.  
- Main reasons for acceptance: novel/effective approach and substantial improvements over A3C.  
- Questions for authors: provide details on compute resources and whether they will release the source code.
