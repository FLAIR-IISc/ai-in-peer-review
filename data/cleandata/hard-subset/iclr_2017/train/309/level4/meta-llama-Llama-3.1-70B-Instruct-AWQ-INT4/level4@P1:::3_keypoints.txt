- Proposes training RL agents on auxiliary pseudo-control tasks (pixel intensity control, latent feature activation) plus a supervised reward-prediction regression task using skewed replay sampling.
- Novel alternative to unsupervised methods that complements policy learning by controlling local and internal environment features.
- Particularly useful in partially observed environments where model-based methods struggle.
- High-level explanations are clear and intuitive, but some technical details (e.g., definition of L_PC) should be moved from appendix into main text.
- Methodology appears sound and comparisons to UNREAL and A3C are made fairly by evaluating top-3 hyperparameters per method.
- Main weakness: limited experimental analysis on why auxiliary tasks help and the individual role of each task.
- Noted strong impact of pixel/feature control in Labyrinth—needs deeper investigation and ablation studies.
- Reviewer is inclined to accept but requests addressing weaknesses and adding experimental support.
- Request for more architecture and training details for the auxiliary tasks, especially the supervised regression task.
- Suggestion to include visualizations or examples of features learned by the UNREAL agent.
- Recommend comparing UNREAL to other state-of-the-art methods (e.g., Prioritized Dueling DQN) to contextualize performance.
- Suggest exploring UNREAL on other domains (continuous control, multi-agent) to assess generality.
- Questions for authors: provide hyperparameter choices and tuning procedure for auxiliary tasks.
- Questions for authors: plans for extending UNREAL to more complex environments/tasks.
- Questions for authors: provide insights into what features UNREAL learns versus the A3C baseline.
