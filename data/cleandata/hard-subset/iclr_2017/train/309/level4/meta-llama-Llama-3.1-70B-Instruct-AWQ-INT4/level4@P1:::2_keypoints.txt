- Proposal: Augment policy optimization with domain-independent auxiliary tasks (control, immediate reward prediction, value replay) sharing a CNN+LSTM feature extractor.  
- Results: Demonstrates significant data-efficiency improvements on Atari and Labyrinth compared to A3C.  
- Overall assessment: Well-written, sound ideas, convincing results — clear acceptance.  

- Clarify in abstract/introduction that the agent learns different policies for each task (not a single policy optimizing all rewards).  
- Discuss extra computational cost: impact on training speed and which components are most expensive.  
- Feature-control validation: Fig. 5 is preliminary and unconvincing; feature-control may cause stability or convergence issues during learning.  
- Training duration: Continue training best agents to assess longer-term performance since learning curves are still improving.  
- Hyperparameters: Treat auxiliary-task weights (lambda_*) as tunable hyperparameters and justify the choice of 1 or provide tuning experiments.  
- Q-learning note: Clarify that auxiliary tasks are not trained with “true” Q-learning because they are trained off-policy with multi-step empirical rewards.  

- Minor fixes: rephrase policy-gradient loss sentence; move lambda_c inside sum in Eq.1; change rt^(c) to rt+k^(c); reference Figure 2 and 1(d) correctly; fix grammar (“are shared”); define L_PC explicitly; explain “Clip” in Fig.3 legend; clarify Fig.3 percentages refer to Labyrinth; add more Atari ablations; correct figure references and punctuation; specify lambda_PC value; indicate where Appendix/supplementary materials can be found.  

- Decision: Accept.  

- Reasons: Novel, well-motivated approach with clear methodological exposition and strong empirical gains in data efficiency.  

- Supporting arguments: Natural extension using auxiliary tasks; significant A3C improvements; clear and detailed methods/experiments.  

- Additional feedback: Add more Atari ablations, quantify computational cost, clarify abstract, continue training top agents, and validate lambda_* choices.  

- Questions for authors: Provide details on computational cost of auxiliary-task optimization; how was lambda_* chosen and validated (any experiments for value=1)?; provide more details on feature-control and potential stability/convergence issues.
