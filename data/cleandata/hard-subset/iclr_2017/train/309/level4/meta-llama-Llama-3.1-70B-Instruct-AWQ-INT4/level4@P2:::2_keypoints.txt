- Paper proposes augmenting policy optimization with domain-independent auxiliary tasks (control, immediate reward prediction, value replay) to improve feature learning in deep RL.

- Writing and ideas are sound; experiments show convincing, significant data-efficiency gains on Atari and Labyrinth compared to A3C.

- Sharing the CNN+LSTM feature extractor across auxiliary tasks is a good design choice and appears effective.

- Need to report extra computational cost: training speed impact and which auxiliary components are most costly.

- Clarify in abstract/introduction that the agent learns separate policies for each task to avoid misinterpretation.

- The "feature control" auxiliary task is intriguing but not empirically validated; Fig. 5 result is weak and needs deeper investigation of stability/convergence.

- Suggest continuing training for the best agents to assess longer-term performance since learning curves are still improving.

- Auxiliary-task weight hyperparameters (lambda_*) should be treated as tunable; provide experiments or justification for setting them to 1.

- Request more ablation studies on Atari to isolate individual contributions of each auxiliary task.

- Minor wording/math fixes: rephrase policy-gradient loss sentence to indicate minimization.

- Equation corrections: move lambda_c inside the sum in eq.1; change rt^(c) to rt+k^(c) just below eq.1.

- Clarify notation/definitions: define L_PC (referenced around eq.2) and explicitly state the value of lambda_PC.

- Figure and reference fixes: reference Figure 2 in text; reference Figure 1(d) in section 3.3; correct several figure-caption/legend clarifications.

- Figure 3 legend should explain what "Clip" means for dueling networks and clarify that the percentages shown refer to Labyrinth.

- Rephrase several figure/text pointers for clarity (e.g., Figure 3 (right) → Figure 3 (top left); Figure 5 caption to specify left/middle plots).

- Fix grammar: "the features discovered in this manner is shared" → "are shared".

- Clarify Appendix location and include missing supplementary details.
