- Proposes training RL agents on auxiliary tasks (pseudo-control tasks) to learn stronger features.  
- Introduces two pseudo-control tasks: controlling pixel-intensity changes and controlling latent feature activations.  
- Adds a supervised regression task that predicts immediate reward after event sequences.  
- Reward-prediction is learned offline using skewed sampling of the replay buffer to balance reward/no-reward to 1/2.  
- Agents with these auxiliary tasks achieve large gains, reaching baseline ~10× faster on discrete-action continuous-space RL tasks.  
- Approach contrasts with passive unsupervised/model-based learning by leveraging controllability of local/internal features.  
- Novel and intuitively explained, but some low-level details are missing (e.g., L_PC should be introduced before the appendix).  
- Methodology is sound; top-3 hyperparameter measurement helps find per-method best settings across A3C and UNREAL.  
- Experimental analysis is limited: the paper shows strong performance gains but lacks deeper investigation of how/why auxiliary tasks help.  
- Pixel/feature control appear most impactful (A3C+PC outperforms others in Labyrinth except UNREAL); this deserves more in-depth study beyond task performance.
