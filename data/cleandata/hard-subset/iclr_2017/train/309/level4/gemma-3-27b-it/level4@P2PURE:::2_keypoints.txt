- Paper proposes augmenting policy optimization with auxiliary tasks (control, reward prediction, value replay) sharing a CNN+LSTM, improving data efficiency vs A3C on Atari and Labyrinth.  
- Overall judgement: well-written, sound ideas, convincing results — recommend acceptance.  
- Ask for discussion of computational overhead: how much training time increases and which components are most expensive.  
- Clarify abstract/introduction to state agents learn separate policies for each auxiliary task (current phrasing misleading).  
- Concern that empirical validation of “feature control” is weak (Figure 5 shows only slight initial benefit) and that changing tasks during learning may cause stability/convergence issues.  
- Suggest continuing training best-performing agents since performance is still improving to assess ultimate potential.  
- Question why auxiliary task weights (lambda_*) were fixed at 1 instead of tuned as hyperparameters; request experiments or justification.  
- Request explicit acknowledgement that auxiliary tasks are not trained with standard Q-learning due to off-policy multi-step empirical rewards (as noted in OpenReview).  
- Minor correction: “maximize” vs “minimize” for policy gradient loss wording.  
- Minor correction: include lambda_c inside the summation in equation 1.  
- Minor correction: rt^(c) should be rt+k^(c) just below equation 1.  
- Figure and reference issues: Figure 2 not referenced; Figure 1(d) should be referenced in section 3.3.  
- Grammar correction: “the features discovered in this manner is shared” → “are shared.”  
- Clarify that loss L_PC is referenced but not defined or explicitly shown in equation 2.  
- Explain meaning of “Clip” for dueling networks in Figure 3 legend.  
- Request more ablation studies on Atari to see if task contributions from Labyrinth generalize.
