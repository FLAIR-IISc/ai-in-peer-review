- Proposes training RL agents on auxiliary tasks to encourage learning of stronger features.  
- Introduces two pseudo-control tasks: controlling pixel intensity changes and controlling activations of latent features.  
- Adds a supervised regression task to predict immediate reward, trained offline with skewed replay sampling to balance reward/non‑reward 50/50.  
- Agents with these auxiliary tasks significantly improve performance, reaching baseline in ten times fewer iterations on discrete-action, continuous-space tasks.  
- Contrasts with passive unsupervised/model-based learning by using the agent’s control to learn useful representations under partial observability.  
- Reviewer finds the approach novel and an interesting alternative that leverages the agent’s inherent control capacity.  
- Paper explains tasks at a high level, but should present the L_PC loss details earlier (not only in the appendix) for clarity.  
- Methodology appears sound; using top‑3 performance measurements helps find per‑method optimal hyperparameters across A3C and UNREAL.  
- Key weakness: limited experimental analysis of the auxiliary tasks’ effects beyond primary RL performance—more in‑depth investigation is needed.  
- Noted observation: pixel/feature control seems most impactful (A3C+PC often outperforms other configurations in Labyrinth), meriting further isolated study.
