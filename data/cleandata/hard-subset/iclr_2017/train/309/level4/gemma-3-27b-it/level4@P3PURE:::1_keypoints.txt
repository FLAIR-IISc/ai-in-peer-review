- Proposes UNREAL: enhance deep RL agents (e.g., A3C) by incorporating unsupervised auxiliary tasks.
- Introduces auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks as the auxiliary learning signals.
- Evaluates on Labyrinth and Atari environments.
- UNREAL yields significant performance gains over A3C and learns faster.
- Contribution is valuable but serves mainly as a proof-of-concept for a generally expected effect.
- Observed benefits are not entirely surprising since relevant auxiliary tasks aid feature shaping and acceleration.
- Paper is well-written and accessible to readers with deep RL background.
- Requests authors comment on computational resources required to train UNREAL.
- Notes overall architecture appears complex and asks whether authors will release source code.
