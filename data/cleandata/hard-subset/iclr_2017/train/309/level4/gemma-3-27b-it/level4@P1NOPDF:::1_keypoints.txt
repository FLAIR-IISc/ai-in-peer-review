- Proposes UNREAL: augmenting deep RL agents (e.g., A3C) with unsupervised auxiliary tasks.  
- Introduces a set of auxiliary control and reward tasks.  
- Evaluated on Labyrinth and Atari, showing significant performance improvements and faster learning vs A3C.  
- Considered a valuable conference contribution and a compelling proof of concept.  
- Result not entirely surprising—relevant auxiliary tasks generally aid feature learning.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible to readers familiar with deep RL.  
- Reviewer requests information on computational resources needed to train UNREAL.  
- Notes the overall architecture is complex and asks whether authors will release source code.  
- After rebuttal, reviewer made no change to their assessment.
