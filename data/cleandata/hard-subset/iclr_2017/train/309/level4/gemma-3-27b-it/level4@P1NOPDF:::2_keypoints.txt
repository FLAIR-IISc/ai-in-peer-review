- Paper proposes augmenting policy optimization with domain-independent auxiliary tasks (feature control, reward prediction, value replay), sharing a CNN+LSTM; shows improved data efficiency on Atari and Labyrinth; recommends acceptance.

- Please discuss additional computational cost of optimizing auxiliary tasks: impact on training speed and which components are most expensive.

- Clarify abstract/introduction to explicitly state the agent learns different policies for each auxiliary task (initial phrasing implied a single policy).

- Feature-control idea lacks strong empirical validation (Figure 5 shows only slight initial improvement); concern that changing tasks during learning may harm stability/convergence.

- Since agents’ performance is still improving, consider continuing training best agents to assess longer-term gains.

- Why weren’t auxiliary-task weights (λ_*) treated as tunable hyperparameters? Any experiments justifying using value 1?

- Acknowledge that auxiliary tasks are not trained with “true” Q-learning but off-policy with multi-step empirical rewards (as noted in OpenReview comments).
