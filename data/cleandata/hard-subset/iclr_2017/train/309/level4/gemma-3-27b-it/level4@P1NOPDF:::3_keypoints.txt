- Proposes training RL agents with auxiliary tasks to encourage more robust feature representations.  
- Introduces two pseudo-control tasks: controlling pixel-intensity changes and controlling latent feature activations.  
- Adds a supervised regression task to predict immediate reward using skewed 1:1 sampling from replay to balance reward/non-reward events.  
- Agents with these auxiliary tasks achieve substantially faster learning, reaching baseline performance ~10× fewer iterations.  
- Offers a novel, active alternative to passive unsupervised or model-based representation learning by leveraging agent control.  
- Presentation is intuitive and high-level, but some details (e.g., pixel control loss L_PC) should be moved into the main text.  
- Methodology appears sound; top-3 hyperparameter reporting likely finds per-method optima across A3C and UNREAL.  
- Key weakness: limited ablation/analysis of individual auxiliary-task contributions beyond aggregate performance.  
- Pixel/feature control seem most impactful (notably in Labyrinth where A3C+PC outperforms others), warranting deeper investigation and broader metrics.
