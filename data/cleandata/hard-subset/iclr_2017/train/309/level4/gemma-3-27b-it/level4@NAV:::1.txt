This paper introduces UNREAL, a deep reinforcement learning agent extending A3C with unsupervised auxiliary tasks – both control and reward-based – and demonstrates its effectiveness in Labyrinth and Atari environments. The results show a clear performance improvement and accelerated learning compared to the baseline A3C agent, representing a valuable contribution to the field. While the observed benefits of incorporating relevant auxiliary tasks to enhance feature shaping are largely consistent with existing understanding, this work provides a strong proof-of-concept demonstration. 

The paper is clearly written and accessible to readers familiar with deep reinforcement learning.

I would appreciate it if the authors could elaborate on the computational resources required for training the UNREAL agent. Furthermore, given the architectural complexity of the model, I encourage the authors to consider releasing the source code to facilitate reproducibility and further research.
