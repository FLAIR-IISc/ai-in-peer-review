- Decision: Accept — paper introduces UNREAL, augmenting policy learning with auxiliary tasks to improve feature learning and data efficiency.

- Contribution: Proposes auxiliary tasks (pixel-control, reward prediction, value replay) that share a CNN+LSTM feature extractor with the main policy.

- Empirical result: Demonstrates substantial improvements on Atari and Labyrinth, including reported ~10× speedup in Labyrinth versus A3C.

- Motivation: Well-motivated approach addressing representational learning in RL by leveraging sensorimotor stream information.

- Experimental strength: Thorough evaluation with ablation studies isolating auxiliary task contributions and reported robustness to hyperparameters.

- Computational cost concern: Request to quantify the training-time and compute overhead introduced by the auxiliary tasks and identify the most expensive tasks.

- Clarify multi-policy phrasing: Abstract/introduction should explicitly state that separate policies are learned for each auxiliary task to avoid misinterpretation.

- Feature-control validation weak: Empirical validation of the feature-control task is limited and needs a more comprehensive, dedicated evaluation.

- Stability concern: Ask for discussion of potential stability/convergence issues when modifying the shared feature extractor during training.

- Continued training suggestion: Recommend extending training runs, especially for top configurations, since performance appears still improving.

- Lambda weights rationale: Request explanation for fixed auxiliary-task weight choices and whether any optimization was performed over these values.

- Off-policy clarification: Ask authors to acknowledge and clarify that auxiliary tasks use off-policy multi-step empirical updates and are not "true" Q-learning.

- More Atari ablations: Suggest including additional ablated experiments on Atari to analyze individual auxiliary task contributions as done for Labyrinth.

- Figure and text corrections: Multiple minor edits requested (equation fixes, figure references, legend clarifications, typos, and explicit definition of terms like Clip and L_PC).

- Presentation fixes: Clarify figure captions/legends (e.g., what percentages refer to, correct figure references), and state the value of lambda_PC.

- Appendix/supplement location: Request specification of where supplementary materials are located.

- Questions for authors: (1) Provide computational-cost breakdown per auxiliary task; (2) Report whether lambda weights were optimized and results; (3) Propose strategies to mitigate stability issues from feature-control; (4) Explain how auxiliary tasks are trained off-policy with multi-step empirical rewards and why this differs from true Q-learning.
