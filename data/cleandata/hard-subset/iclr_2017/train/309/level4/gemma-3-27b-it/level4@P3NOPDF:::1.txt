## Review of UNREAL: Leveraging Unsupervised Auxiliary Tasks for Deep Reinforcement Learning

This paper presents UNREAL, a novel approach to enhance deep reinforcement learning agents – specifically, Asynchronous Advantage Actor-Critic (A3C) – by incorporating unsupervised auxiliary tasks. The authors introduce both auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks, demonstrating improved performance and faster learning in the Labyrinth and Atari environments. The results clearly show that UNREAL outperforms the baseline A3C agent, representing a positive contribution to the conference.

The core idea of leveraging relevant auxiliary tasks to improve feature learning in reinforcement learning is intuitively sound. Adding tasks that encourage the agent to learn useful representations, even without direct reward signals tied to the primary goal, is a logical step towards more efficient and robust learning. The experimental validation provided supports this intuition, effectively demonstrating the benefits of the UNREAL framework. While not necessarily a groundbreaking departure from established principles, this work provides a concrete and effective instantiation of this idea, serving as a compelling proof of concept. 

The paper is well-written and accessible to readers with a background in deep reinforcement learning. The authors clearly articulate the motivation, methodology, and experimental setup. The figures and tables effectively communicate the results, and the presentation is generally concise and easy to follow.

However, while the improvements demonstrated are significant, the result feels somewhat expected. The benefit of shaping the learning process with relevant auxiliary information is well-recognized within the RL community. Therefore, the novelty lies more in the *implementation* – the specific choice of auxiliary tasks and their integration into the A3C framework – than in the fundamental idea itself. A more detailed discussion of related work exploring similar concepts (e.g., intrinsic motivation, auxiliary tasks in diverse RL settings) would further contextualize the contribution. 

I have two specific questions for the authors:

1. **Computational Resources:** Could the authors provide more detail regarding the computational resources (e.g., number of CPUs, GPUs, training time) required to train the UNREAL agent, particularly compared to the baseline A3C agent? This information is crucial for assessing the practical feasibility of the proposed approach.

2. **Code Release:** Given the complexity of the UNREAL architecture, and to facilitate reproducibility and further research, are the authors willing to release the source code for their implementation? Open-sourcing the code would significantly benefit the community and allow for a more thorough evaluation and extension of this work.



**Overall Score:** 7/10

**Confidence:** 4/5
