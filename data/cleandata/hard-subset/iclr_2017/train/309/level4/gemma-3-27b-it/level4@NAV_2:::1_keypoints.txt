- Introduces UNREAL: augmenting deep RL with auxiliary tasks that learn multiple pseudo-reward policies alongside the main task.  
- Uses a shared representation that can develop even without extrinsic rewards.  
- Proposes a mechanism to focus the shared representation on extrinsic rewards for rapid adaptation.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on Atari (avg. 880% human expert) and Labyrinth (87% human, 10× faster learning).  
- Recommends acceptance based on significant performance improvements and clear exposition.  
- Addresses sample efficiency and generalization challenges in deep RL via sensorimotor control auxiliary tasks.  
- Well-motivated and properly positioned relative to prior work (Horde, UVFA, successor representations).  
- Provides strong empirical support with comprehensive experiments and effective ablation studies.  
- Experimental methodology is sound and results are clearly presented.  
- Requests clarification on computational resources (CPU/GPU hours and memory) for reproducibility.  
- Asks that authors release source code given architectural complexity to facilitate follow-up work.  
- Questions the choice and sensitivity of log-uniform sampling for weighting terms (λVR, λPC, λRP).  
- Requests a more quantitative analysis of robustness to hyperparameters in Labyrinth (e.g., performance variance).
