- Proposes training RL agents with auxiliary tasks to encourage learning of more robust features.  
- Introduces two pseudo-control tasks: controlling pixel intensity changes and controlling activations of latent features.  
- Adds a supervised regression task to predict immediate reward, trained offline with biased sampling to ~50% reward/no-reward balance.  
- Agents using these tasks show large gains on discrete-action, continuous-space tasks, reaching baseline in ~10× fewer iterations.  
- Offers a distinct, active-control alternative to passive unsupervised or model-based representation learning under partial observability.  
- The approach is novel and presents a compelling complementary strategy to learning optimal control policies.  
- Exposition is clear but would benefit from earlier, more detailed presentation of the L_PC loss instead of deferring to the appendix.  
- Methodology appears sound; reporting top-3 hyperparameter results helps ensure good hyperparameter selection across A3C and UNREAL.  
- Experimental analysis is limited: auxiliary tasks’ effects are shown mainly via overall performance without deeper ablation or insight.  
- Pixel/feature control seems particularly impactful (e.g., A3C+PC strong in Labyrinth), warranting further focused investigation.
