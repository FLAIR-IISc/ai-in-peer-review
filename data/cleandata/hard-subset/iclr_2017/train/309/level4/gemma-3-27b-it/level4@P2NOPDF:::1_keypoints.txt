- Introduces UNREAL: an A3C-based agent augmented with unsupervised auxiliary tasks.
- Proposes and evaluates auxiliary control and auxiliary reward tasks in Labyrinth and Atari.
- Empirical results show UNREAL significantly outperforms A3C, with faster learning and better final performance.
- Contribution is valuable but improvement is not entirely surprising—auxiliary tasks generally aid feature learning and speed training.
- Paper functions as a compelling proof-of-concept for auxiliary-task augmentation in DRL.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and accessible to readers familiar with deep reinforcement learning.
- Reviewer requests details on computational resources required to train UNREAL.
- Reviewer asks that authors release source code to facilitate reproducibility and further research.
- No changes were made following the rebuttal.
