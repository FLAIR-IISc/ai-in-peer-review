- Introduces a novel RL approach that augments training with auxiliary tasks to learn robust, useful feature representations by actively controlling internal states and environment aspects.  
- Proposes two pseudo-control tasks: pixel intensity control (L_PC) and control of latent feature activations.  
- Adds a supervised reward-prediction regression task trained offline with skewed replay sampling to provide dense signals in sparse-reward settings.  
- Experimental results show agents with these auxiliary tasks reach baseline performance roughly ten times faster on discrete-action, continuous-space RL problems, especially in challenging environments.  
- Offers a compelling alternative to unsupervised or model-based representation learning, avoiding irrelevant representations and intractable environment modeling under partial observability.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible; high-level explanations convey intuition effectively.  
- Presentation critique: L_PC is introduced too late; its loss function should be presented earlier rather than in the appendix.  
- Methodology is sound; hyperparameter concerns are mitigated by reporting the top-3 configurations to identify best-performing settings.  
- Limitation: limited analysis of individual auxiliary-task contributions beyond overall RL performance improvements.  
- Recommendation for future work: evaluate L_PC in isolation, provide metrics for representation quality and auxiliary-task control to better understand mechanisms.
