This paper introduces a method for enhancing deep reinforcement learning agents, such as A3C, through the incorporation of unsupervised auxiliary tasks. The authors propose and evaluate a set of auxiliary control and reward tasks within the Labyrinth and Atari environments. The resulting UNREAL agent demonstrates significantly improved performance and learning speed compared to A3C. This represents a valuable contribution to the conference. However, the observed benefits are not entirely unexpected, as the addition of relevant auxiliary tasks generally facilitates improved and faster feature learning. As such, this work serves as a strong proof of concept for this approach. 

The paper is well-written and accessible to readers with a background in deep reinforcement learning.

I would appreciate it if the authors could provide details regarding the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent. 

The overall architecture is complex. Would the authors be willing to release their source code to facilitate reproducibility and further research?
