- Proposes UNREAL: augment deep RL with auxiliary control and reward-prediction tasks to improve representation learning.  
- Demonstrates large gains over A3C on Labyrinth and Atari: faster learning, higher final performance (e.g., 880% human on Atari; 10x speedup and 87% human on Labyrinth).  
- Decision: Accept — compelling approach with strong empirical results and clear impact.  
- Core idea well-motivated and positioned relative to prior work (unsupervised learning, options, UVFA, Horde, successor representations); key distinction is using auxiliary tasks solely for representation.  
- Empirical evidence strengthened by ablation studies, robustness/hyperparameter analysis, and a demonstration video.  
- Request for more detail on weighting of auxiliary losses (λVR, λPC, λRP) and the hyperparameter search process.  
- Architecture is complex; recommend a more detailed breakdown (especially the deconvolutional pixel-control network) for implementers.  
- Suggest discussing why pixel-reconstruction baselines perform worse than control-based auxiliary tasks despite both leveraging visual input.  
- Question: provide computational resources and training time required for UNREAL on Labyrinth to assess practical feasibility.  
- Question: will the authors release source code given the architecture complexity?  
- Question: guidance/principled methods for selecting appropriate auxiliary tasks for a given environment.  
- Question: how well will the approach scale to more complex, higher-dimensional real-world tasks (e.g., robotic manipulation) and what modifications might be needed?
