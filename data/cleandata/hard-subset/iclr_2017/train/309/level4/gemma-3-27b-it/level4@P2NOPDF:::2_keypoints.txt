- Proposes auxiliary tasks (control, immediate reward prediction, value replay) sharing a CNN+LSTM to improve feature learning in deep RL; shows improved data efficiency on Atari and Labyrinth.

- Overall assessment: strong paper with sound ideas and compelling results; recommend acceptance.

- Request quantification of computational overhead from optimizing auxiliary tasks, including training speed impact and most expensive components.

- Clarify in abstract/introduction that the agent learns separate policies for each auxiliary task (current wording led to confusion).

- Concern that the feature control auxiliary task lacks strong empirical validation; Figure 5 shows only marginal initial benefit and dynamic features may cause instability.

- Suggest training best-performing agents for longer since performance appears still improving.

- Question choice of fixed auxiliary weights (λ*): were these tuned as hyperparameters? justify selecting value 1.

- Clarify that auxiliary tasks are not trained with traditional Q-learning but off-policy using multi-step empirical rewards.

- Minor correction: change "maximise the expected reward, L_pi = -..." to "minimize the loss L_pi = -...".

- Minor correction: include λc within the summation in Equation 1.

- Minor correction: just below Equation 1, rt^(c) should be rt+k^(c).

- Figure reference issue: Figure 2 is not referenced in the text; Figure 1(d) should be referenced in section 3.3.

- Grammar correction: "the features discovered in this manner is shared" → "are shared".

- Clarify L_PC: loss term L_PC is referenced around Equation 2 but not explicitly defined.

- Clarify what “Clip” refers to in the dueling networks legend of Figure 3.

- Request more ablation studies on Atari to verify whether individual task contributions match Labyrinth findings.
