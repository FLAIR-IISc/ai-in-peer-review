This paper presents a method for enhancing deep reinforcement learning agents, such as A3C, by incorporating unsupervised auxiliary tasks. The authors introduce a set of auxiliary control and reward tasks and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach—dubbed UNREAL—on the Labyrinth and Atari environments. The UNREAL agent significantly outperforms A3C and exhibits faster learning. This represents a valuable contribution to the conference. However, the result isn’t entirely surprising; adding relevant auxiliary tasks should generally facilitate improved and accelerated feature learning. As such, this paper serves as a compelling proof of concept.

The paper is well-written and accessible to readers with a background in deep reinforcement learning. 

I would appreciate it if the authors could comment on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent.

The overall architecture is quite complex. Would the authors be willing to release the source code for their model?
