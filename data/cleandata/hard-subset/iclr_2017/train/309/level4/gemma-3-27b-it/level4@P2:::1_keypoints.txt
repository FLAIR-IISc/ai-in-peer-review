- Introduces UNREAL: an RL agent that augments primary RL with control-based and reward-focused auxiliary tasks to learn richer representations.  
- UNREAL maximizes multiple pseudo-reward functions concurrently to improve adaptability.  
- Evaluated on 3D Labyrinth and Atari, showing significant performance gains over A3C.  
- Demonstrates faster learning and greater robustness to hyperparameter settings.  
- Paper is well-motivated, building on representation learning, auxiliary tasks, and multi-objective RL.  
- Architecture is clearly presented and experimentally well-supported.  
- Ablation studies effectively show the contribution of each UNREAL component.  
- Reported speedups in learning are notable and practically encouraging.  
- Decision: Accept, primarily due to substantial and consistent empirical gains across diverse environments.  
- Request for more detailed comparison of computational costs (training time, GPU hours) versus A3C.  
- Concern that the architecture’s complexity may hinder adoption; recommend releasing source code for reproducibility.  
- Suggest investigating transferability of learned representations to related tasks.  
- Question: Provide detailed breakdown of computational resources and training time compared to A3C.  
- Question: Do the authors plan to release source code, and when?  
- Question: Have the authors tested representation transfer to other tasks, or plan to?  
- Question: Please clarify the replay buffer skewing strategy for reward prediction and rationale for equal zero/non-zero reward sampling.
