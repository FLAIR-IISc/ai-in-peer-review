This paper introduces UNREAL, a deep reinforcement learning agent that enhances the A3C algorithm through the addition of unsupervised auxiliary tasks. The authors propose and evaluate both auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks within the Labyrinth and Atari environments. Results demonstrate that UNREAL significantly outperforms A3C, achieving both improved performance and faster learning speeds. This represents a valuable contribution to the field. However, the observed improvement isn’t entirely unexpected; incorporating relevant auxiliary tasks generally facilitates more efficient feature learning and accelerates training. Consequently, this work can be viewed as a compelling proof of concept demonstrating the benefits of this approach.

The paper is well-written and accessible to readers familiar with deep reinforcement learning. The presentation is clear and easy to follow. 

I have two questions for the authors. First, could they provide details regarding the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent? This information would be helpful for assessing the practical applicability of the method. Second, the overall architecture appears quite complex. Would the authors be willing to release their source code to facilitate reproducibility and further research?
