This paper presents a method for improving feature learning in deep reinforcement learning by augmenting the policy optimization problem with auxiliary tasks. These tasks – control (learning policies to modify the state space, specifically pixels), immediate reward prediction, and value function replay – are designed to shape the learned features (shared via a CNN+LSTM network). The paper demonstrates the benefits of this approach on Atari and Labyrinth environments, achieving notably improved data efficiency compared to A3C.

Overall, the paper is well-written, the ideas are sound, and the results are convincing, leading me to recommend acceptance. I have a few minor comments and suggestions for improvement:

*   **Computational Cost:** It would be beneficial to discuss the additional computational cost associated with optimizing the auxiliary tasks. Specifically, how much does this impact training speed, and which components are the most computationally demanding?
*   **Clarification of Multi-Policy Learning:** The abstract and introduction could be clarified to explicitly state that the agent learns distinct policies for each task. My initial reading of the abstract – “the agent also maximises many other pseudo-reward functions simultaneously by reinforcement learning” – led me to believe a single policy was being optimized for all rewards, a misinterpretation corrected only upon reaching equation 1.
*   **Feature Control Validation:** The empirical validation of the “feature control” idea is currently weak (the preliminary experiment in Figure 5 offers only slight initial improvement). While I find the idea appealing, I am concerned that the changing nature of the task during learning might introduce stability or convergence issues as the extracted features are concurrently modified.
*   **Extended Training:** Given the authors’ observation that “the performance of our agents is still steadily improving”, I suggest continuing training of the best performing agents to determine their ultimate potential.
*   **Auxiliary Task Weights:** The auxiliary task weights (λ<sub>*</sub>) are currently fixed at 1. I question whether these weights should be treated as hyperparameters and optimized. Were any experiments conducted to justify this choice?
*   **Off-Policy Training of Auxiliary Tasks:** Please acknowledge that the auxiliary tasks are not trained using “true” Q-Learning, as they are trained off-policy with multi-step empirical rewards (as discussed in the OpenReview comments).
