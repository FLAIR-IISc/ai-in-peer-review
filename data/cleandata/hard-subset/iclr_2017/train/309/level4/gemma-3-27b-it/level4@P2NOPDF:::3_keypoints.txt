- Introduces a novel RL approach using auxiliary tasks to encourage robust feature representations.  
- Core idea: control both external (pixel intensity) and internal (latent activations) aspects alongside the primary RL objective to boost learning.  
- Proposes a supervised regression task to predict immediate reward from past event sequences, using biased replay sampling to balance positives/negatives.  
- Demonstrates substantial gains: reaches baseline performance in roughly ten times fewer iterations on discrete-action, continuous-state environments.  
- Distinguishes itself from unsupervised/model-based methods by leveraging the agent’s control to learn locally relevant, useful features.  
- Approach is novel and promising, providing a compelling alternative to purely unsupervised representation learning.  
- High-level explanations effectively convey intuition, but specific loss details (e.g., L_PC) should be presented earlier rather than only in the appendix.  
- Paper is clearly written and accessible to readers familiar with deep RL.  
- Methodology is sound; authors address potential hyperparameter distribution differences via robust search and top-3 performance selection.  
- Major weakness/future work: limited analysis of individual contributions of each auxiliary task beyond their combined effect.  
- Pixel control (PC) appears especially impactful—A3C+PC often outperforms other configurations in Labyrinth except UNREAL.  
- Recommendation: more detailed exploration of PC (in isolation and via representation-analysis metrics) would strengthen the work.
