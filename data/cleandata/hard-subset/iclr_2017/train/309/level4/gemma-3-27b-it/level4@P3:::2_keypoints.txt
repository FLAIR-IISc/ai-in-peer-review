- Introduces auxiliary tasks (control, immediate reward prediction, value replay) that share a CNN+LSTM feature extractor to improve representation learning in deep RL.  
- Auxiliary tasks shape representations but are not used directly for action selection.  
- Empirical results on Atari and Labyrinth show significant sample-efficiency gains and overall performance improvements vs A3C.  
- Decision: Accept—method is well-motivated, technically sound, and makes a significant empirical contribution.  
- Core idea is grounded in RL and unsupervised learning literature and effectively combines auxiliary-task learning with A3C.  
- Experimental validation is strong, with consistent UNREAL outperformance and impressive learning speedups.  
- Ablation studies provide some insight into each auxiliary task’s contribution, though more analysis is desirable.  
- Experimental setup and multiple hyperparameter settings are well-documented, increasing result robustness.  
- Request to quantify computational overhead (training time/resources) and identify costly components.  
- Clarify wording to emphasize that separate auxiliary policies are learned while sharing the feature extractor to avoid confusion.  
- Feature-control auxiliary task lacks convincing empirical support; more experiments or targeted analysis needed to validate and address stability.  
- Suggest investigating long-term performance by extending training since performance appears to continue improving.  
- Recommend optimizing auxiliary-task weights (λs) via hyperparameter search or adaptive methods rather than fixed arbitrary values.  
- Explicitly state that auxiliary tasks are trained off-policy and clarify multi-step reward usage.  
- List of minor edits: fix loss notation, ensure consistent equation notation, correct typos and figure/equation references, clarify “Clip” in Figure 3, expand Atari ablations to match Labyrinth, label percentage values and number of levels in figures, confirm λ_PC value.  
- Ask authors to include the promised supplementary materials.  
- Questions for authors: quantify computational overhead; report any experiments on adaptive λ weighting; describe methods to stabilize feature-control (regularization/gradual introduction); justify choice of λPC and λVR = 1.
