- Proposes UNREAL: augment A3C with unsupervised auxiliary control and reward tasks to improve representation learning.
- Introduces specific auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks and integrates them into the agent.
- Evaluates UNREAL on Labyrinth and Atari environments.
- UNREAL significantly outperforms A3C, showing much faster learning and higher final scores.
- Reports large empirical gains (≈10× speedup on Labyrinth, 880% human on Atari, 87% on Labyrinth).
- Paper provides a strong proof of concept, though the core idea is intuitive.
- Well-written and accessible to readers familiar with deep RL.
- Recommendation: Accept based on strong empirical results despite intuitive novelty.
- Robustness analysis indicates the method is not overly sensitive to hyperparameters (supplementary Fig. 8).
- Ablation studies in Labyrinth demonstrate the contribution of each auxiliary task.
- Request more detail on computational cost (CPU/GPU counts, training time) for reproducibility.
- Suggest releasing source code to facilitate community adoption and follow-up work.
- Recommend discussing failure cases or environments where UNREAL shows minimal or no improvement.
- Ask for clearer comparison to related work (UVFA and successor representations) regarding pseudo-rewards.
- Ask for stronger justification of chosen auxiliary tasks (pixel changes, network features, reward prediction).
- Specific questions for authors: detailed computational breakdown, code release plans/timeline, any negative or null-result environments, and clarification of differences from UVFA/successor representations.
