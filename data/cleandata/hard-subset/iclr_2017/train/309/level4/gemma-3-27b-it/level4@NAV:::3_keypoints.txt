- Paper studies training RL agents with auxiliary tasks to encourage more robust feature representations.  
- Introduces two pseudo-control tasks: controlling pixel intensity changes and controlling latent feature activations.  
- Adds a supervised offline reward-prediction regression task trained with biased replay sampling to balance rewarded vs non-rewarded experiences (~50/50).  
- Experiments show large performance gains: baseline-level performance reached in about ten times fewer iterations on discrete-action, continuous-space benchmarks.  
- Approach departs from passive unsupervised or full-modeling methods by leveraging the agent’s ability to actively control local and internal features.  
- Methodology is novel and a promising alternative to standard unsupervised representation learning.  
- Paper is clearly written and accessible, but the loss L_PC and related details should be explained earlier rather than only in the appendix.  
- Experimental design is rigorous; acknowledges hyperparameter distribution differences (A3C vs UNREAL) and uses a top-3 selection strategy to find per-method optima.  
- Future work needed: deeper analysis of the auxiliary tasks themselves, especially the notable impact of pixel/feature control (e.g., A3C+PC performance in Labyrinth).
