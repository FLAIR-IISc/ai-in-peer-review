- Proposed method: augment policy optimization with auxiliary tasks (feature control, immediate reward prediction, value replay) using separate policies that share a CNN+LSTM representation.  
- Decision: Accept — novel, well-motivated, and empirically strong.  
- Main contribution: auxiliary tasks improve feature learning and tackle sparse rewards via representation shaping.  
- Architecture choice: separate policies per auxiliary task with shared representation is sensible and effective.  
- Empirical results: significant gains in sample efficiency and final performance on Atari and Labyrinth versus A3C.  
- Ablations: studies show each auxiliary task contributes to the overall improvement.  
- Writing: clear exposition of method and experiments.  
- Computational cost concern: quantify overhead and trade-off between sample efficiency and added compute; identify costliest auxiliary tasks.  
- Clarity issue: abstract/introduction should explicitly state that separate policies are learned for each task.  
- Stability concern: feature-control task may have learning stability/convergence issues and needs more empirical validation and stabilization strategies.
