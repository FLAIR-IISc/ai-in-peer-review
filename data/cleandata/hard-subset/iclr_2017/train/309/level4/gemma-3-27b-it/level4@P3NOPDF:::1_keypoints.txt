- Proposal: Introduces UNREAL, which augments A3C with unsupervised auxiliary control and reward tasks.  
- Empirical results: UNREAL yields faster learning and better performance than baseline A3C on Labyrinth and Atari.  
- Conceptual strength: Leveraging auxiliary tasks to improve representation learning is intuitive and effectively demonstrated.  
- Novelty: Contribution is more in implementation/choice/integration of auxiliary tasks than a fundamental new idea.  
- Writing and presentation: Paper is well-written, clear, and supported by effective figures and tables.  
- Related work: Would benefit from a more detailed comparison to prior work (e.g., intrinsic motivation, other auxiliary-task approaches).  
- Question 1 (resources): Reviewer requests details on computational resources and training time, especially versus A3C.  
- Question 2 (reproducibility): Reviewer asks whether the authors will release source code to facilitate reproducibility.  
- Meta: Overall score 7/10, confidence 4/5; no changes after rebuttal.
