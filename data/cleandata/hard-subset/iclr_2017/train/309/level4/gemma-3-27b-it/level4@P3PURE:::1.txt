This paper proposes a method for enhancing deep reinforcement learning agents, such as A3C, by incorporating unsupervised auxiliary tasks. The authors introduce auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks, evaluating the resulting agent – UNREAL – on the Labyrinth and Atari environments. UNREAL demonstrates significant performance improvements over A3C and exhibits faster learning. This represents a valuable contribution to the conference. However, the observed benefits are not entirely unexpected, as incorporating relevant auxiliary tasks generally aids in improved and accelerated feature shaping. This work effectively serves as a proof-of-concept for this principle.

The paper is well-written and accessible to readers with a background in deep reinforcement learning. 

I would appreciate it if the authors could comment on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent. 

The overall architecture appears complex. Would the authors be willing to release the source code for their model?
