- Introduces method to enhance deep RL agents (e.g., A3C) by incorporating unsupervised auxiliary tasks.  
- Proposes and evaluates auxiliary control and reward tasks in Labyrinth and Atari environments.  
- The UNREAL agent achieves significantly improved performance and faster learning compared to A3C.  
- Represents a valuable contribution and a strong proof of concept for using auxiliary tasks to improve RL.  
- Benefits are not entirely surprising, since relevant auxiliary tasks generally facilitate faster and better feature learning.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible to readers familiar with deep reinforcement learning.  
- Reviewer requests details on computational resources needed to train the UNREAL agent.  
- Reviewer notes the overall architecture is complex and asks whether the authors will release source code to aid reproducibility.  
- No change in the review after the authors' rebuttal.
