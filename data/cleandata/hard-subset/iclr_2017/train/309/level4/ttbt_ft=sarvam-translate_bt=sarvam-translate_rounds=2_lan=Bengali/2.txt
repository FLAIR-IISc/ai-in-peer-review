This paper discusses the improvement of learning features in deep reinforcement learning, specifically focusing on optimizing the core principles with (domain-dependent) auxiliary tasks. These tasks relate to control (learning other policies that try to maximize the state space, such as pixels here), immediate reward prediction, and value function replay. Except for the last, these auxiliary tasks are only used to shape the features (CNN+LSTM share feature extraction networks). Experiments demonstrate the advantages of this approach in Atari and Labyrinth problems, especially with much more data efficiency than A3C.

The paper is well-written, the concepts are accurate, and the results are quite convincing, so I consider it a clear recognition. I have a few points to make at a high level, none of which are major concerns:

- I think you should say something about the additional computational cost of optimizing these auxiliary tasks. How much do you lose in terms of training speed? What are the most expensive components?
- If possible, please be clearer in the abstract/introduction section that the agent is learning different policies for each task. When I read in the abstract that the agent "simultaneously maximizes many other pseudo-reward functions through reinforcement learning, such as pixels here," my first impression was that it was learning a single policy to optimize all the rewards together, and I realized my mistake only when I reached equation 1.
- The concept of "feature control" has not been empirically verified (the initial test in Figure 5 is not very convincing because it initially seems to help little). I like the idea, but I am concerned that the task is changing as the learning progresses, as the extracted features are changing. There may be stability/convergence problems here.
- As you mentioned, "the performance of our agents is still constantly improving," so why aren't they run to see how far they go? (at least the best agents)
- Why aren't the weight parameters of the auxiliary tasks (λ*) hyperparameters to optimize? Was there any testing to verify whether using 1 is a good choice?
- Please note that the auxiliary tasks are not trained with "real" Q-learning, as they are off-policy trained with multiple steps of empirical reward (as discussed in the OpenReview comment).
