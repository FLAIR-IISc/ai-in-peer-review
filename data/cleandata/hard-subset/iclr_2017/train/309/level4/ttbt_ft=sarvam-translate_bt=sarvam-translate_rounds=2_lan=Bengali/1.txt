This paper proposes a way to incorporate unsupervised assistive tasks into a deep RL agent like A3C. The authors propose assistive control tasks and assistive reward tasks and evaluate the agent in the lab and in the field. The proposed UNREAL agent performs significantly better than A3C and can learn faster. This is certainly a good contribution to the conference. However, this is not a surprising result, as incorporating additional assistive tasks relevant to the goal always helps to create faster features. This paper is proof of this concept.

The paper is well-written and easy for any reader with deep RL skills to follow.

Could the authors comment on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent?

The overall structure is quite complex. Are the authors willing to release the source code for their model?
