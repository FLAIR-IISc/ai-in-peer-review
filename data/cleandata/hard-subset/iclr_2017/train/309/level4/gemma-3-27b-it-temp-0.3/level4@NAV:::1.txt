This paper introduces UNREAL, a deep reinforcement learning agent extending A3C through the incorporation of unsupervised auxiliary tasks – both control and reward-based. The method is evaluated on the Labyrinth and Atari environments, demonstrating significant performance improvements and accelerated learning compared to the baseline A3C agent. This represents a valuable contribution to the conference. However, the observed benefits are largely expected; leveraging relevant auxiliary tasks to enhance feature learning is a well-established principle. Consequently, this work functions as a strong proof-of-concept demonstration. 

The paper is clearly written and accessible to readers familiar with deep reinforcement learning.

I would appreciate it if the authors could provide details regarding the computational resources required for training the UNREAL agent. 

Given the architectural complexity of the proposed system, would the authors consider releasing their source code to facilitate reproducibility and further research?
