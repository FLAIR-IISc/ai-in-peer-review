This paper proposes a way to incorporate unsupervised auxiliary tasks into a deep RL agent like A3C. The authors propose auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks and evaluate the agent in Labyrinth and Atari. The proposed UNREAL agent performs significantly better than A3C and can learn faster. This is certainly a good contribution to the conference. However, this is not a surprising result, as incorporating additional auxiliary tasks relevant to the goal always helps in building faster features. This paper is proof of this concept.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow for any reader with deep RL skills.

Could the authors comment on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent?

The overall structure is quite complex. Are the authors willing to release the source code for their model?
