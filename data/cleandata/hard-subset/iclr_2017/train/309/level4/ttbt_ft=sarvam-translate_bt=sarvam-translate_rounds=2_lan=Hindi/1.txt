This paper proposes a method for incorporating unsupervised auxiliary tasks into deep RL agents like A3C. The authors propose a set of auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks and evaluate the agent in a maze and a tower. The proposed UNREAL agent performs significantly better and learns faster than A3C. This is certainly a good contribution to the conference. However, this is not a surprising result because including additional auxiliary tasks relevant to the goal should always help with better and faster feature size. This paper is a proof-of-concept for this idea.

This paper is well-written and easy to understand for any reader with deep RL expertise.

Could the authors comment on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent?

The overall architecture is quite complex. Are the authors ready to release the source code for their model?
