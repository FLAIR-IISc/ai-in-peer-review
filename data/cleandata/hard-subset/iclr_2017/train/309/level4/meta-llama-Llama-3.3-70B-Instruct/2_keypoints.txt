- Paper introduces domain-independent auxiliary tasks (control, immediate reward prediction, value replay) that share CNN+LSTM features to improve data efficiency in deep RL, showing strong Atari and Labyrinth results.
- Reviewer finds the paper well-written with sound ideas and convincing results, recommending acceptance.
- Request to discuss additional computational cost of optimizing auxiliary tasks and impact on training speed.
- Suggest clarifying in abstract/introduction that the agent learns distinct policies for each task to avoid implying a single-policy optimizing all rewards.
- Expresses concern that the "feature control" concept lacks empirical validation; Figure 5’s preliminary experiment is unconvincing and raises stability/convergence questions.
- Recommends continuing training agents to observe their long-term/asymptotic performance since learning curves are still improving.
- Questions the choice of fixed auxiliary-task weights (lambda_*) and asks for experiments or validation for that choice.
- Notes that auxiliary tasks are not trained via traditional Q-learning but off-policy with multi-step empirical rewards—this should be stated.
- Minor correction: policy gradient algorithm description should state it minimizes a loss function.
- Equation 1 needs lambda_c inside the sum and r_t^(c) should be r_{t+k}^{(c)} (subscript correction).
- Figure 2 is not referenced in the text, and Figure 1(d) should be referenced in Section 3.3.
- Several grammatical errors and missing definitions (e.g., "the features discovered in this manner is shared" and undefined L_PC) need fixing.
- Figure 3 legend must explain the "Clip" term for dueling networks; more ablation studies on Atari are requested.
- Multiple inconsistencies and incorrect figure references/unclear descriptions should be corrected for clarity.
- Appendix mentions supplementary materials that are not provided, and the value of lambda_PC is not specified.
