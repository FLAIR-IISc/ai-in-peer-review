

This work introduces a novel approach for training reinforcement learning (RL) agents by incorporating auxiliary tasks designed to enhance feature learning. Specifically, the authors propose two pseudo-control tasks: regulating pixel intensity changes and controlling latent feature activations, alongside a supervised regression task predicting immediate reward following event sequences. The latter task is trained offline using a skewed sampling strategy applied to the experience replay buffer to achieve a 50% probability of observing reward signals. Agents trained with these auxiliary tasks demonstrate significant performance gains on discrete-action continuous-space RL benchmarks, achieving baseline performance in 10× fewer iterations compared to baselines.  

The method diverges from conventional "passive" unsupervised or model-based learning paradigms. Rather than compelling models to learn potentially irrelevant input representations or attempt intractable environment modeling (due to partial observability), this approach leverages the agent’s inherent capacity to control local and internal environmental features, thereby complementing optimal policy learning. The core idea represents a compelling and novel alternative to existing unsupervised learning techniques, effectively harnessing the agent’s controllability over the environment.  

The proposed tasks are described at a high level, which aids intuitive understanding but would benefit from greater implementation detail. For instance, the notation L_PC should be explicitly introduced before the appendix. Overall, the paper is well-structured and accessible to readers with Deep RL familiarity. The methodology is sound; while the distribution of optimal hyperparameters may differ between A3C and UNREAL, the use of top-3 performance metrics ensures robust identification of per-method best hyperparameters within the explored ranges.  

A critical limitation is the lack of experimental analysis into the individual contributions of the auxiliary tasks beyond their substantial impact on final RL performance. For example, in Labyrinth, A3C+PC alone outperforms all other variants except UNREAL. A deeper investigation—either isolating the effects of pixel/feature control or analyzing these tasks more thoroughly—would significantly strengthen the work, particularly given the prominence of these auxiliary tasks in the results.
