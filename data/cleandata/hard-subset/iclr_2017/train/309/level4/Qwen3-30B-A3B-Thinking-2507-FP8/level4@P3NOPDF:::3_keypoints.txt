- Introduces auxiliary control tasks for representation learning in RL: pixel control (L_PC), latent feature control, and immediate reward prediction.  
- Auxiliary tasks are trained concurrently with the main RL objective using only agent-environment interactions (no extra supervision or data).  
- Reward prediction uses skewed replay sampling to balance reward events roughly 1:1.  
- Demonstrates large empirical gains on discrete-action, continuous-state benchmarks (Atari, Labyrinth).  
- Agents with auxiliaries reach baseline performance ≈10× faster in training iterations than standard RL baselines.  
- Pixel control (L_PC) is the most impactful auxiliary and often outperforms other combinations (except UNREAL) in Labyrinth.  
- Core conceptual shift: active feature control (agent-driven) vs. passive unsupervised or model-based methods; representations become policy-relevant.  
- Method is particularly valuable for sample-constrained settings and avoids learning “useless” or unachievable models.  
- Paper is well-structured and implementation appears clean and robust across environments.  
- Minor issue: L_PC is defined only in the appendix and should be introduced earlier in the main text for clarity.  
- Missing deeper analysis: lacks ablation/mechanistic studies explaining how each auxiliary improves performance (e.g., state representation vs. exploration vs. convergence).  
- Unclear role of the reward-prediction task—paper doesn’t clarify whether it’s necessary or merely supplementary to L_PC’s gains.  
- Recommendation: Accept — significant contribution, rigorous methodology, and compelling results; addressing the minor analytical and structural gaps would strengthen the paper.
