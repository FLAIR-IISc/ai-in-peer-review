

This paper introduces UNREAL, a deep reinforcement learning agent that incorporates unsupervised auxiliary tasks—both control-based and reward-based—into the A3C framework. The authors evaluate UNREAL on Labyrinth and Atari environments, demonstrating significantly improved performance and faster learning compared to A3C. While this represents a valuable contribution to the field, the core idea—that relevant auxiliary tasks enhance feature learning and accelerate training—is unsurprising. The results serve primarily as a proof of concept for this established principle rather than introducing a novel methodological insight.  

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, making it accessible to readers with expertise in deep reinforcement learning.  

To strengthen the submission, we request clarification on two points:  
1. The computational resources required for training UNREAL (e.g., GPU hours, memory usage) should be explicitly reported, as this is critical for reproducibility and assessing scalability.  
2. Given the complexity of the proposed architecture, the authors should confirm their willingness to release the source code upon publication. This would greatly benefit the community’s ability to validate and extend their work.  

Overall, the technical contribution is modest but valid, and the writing is strong. The computational details and code availability are the primary considerations for acceptance.
