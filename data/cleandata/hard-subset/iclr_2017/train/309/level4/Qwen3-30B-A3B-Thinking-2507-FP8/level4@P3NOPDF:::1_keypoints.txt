- Introduces UNREAL: an A3C extension that adds unsupervised auxiliary tasks (control- and reward-based) to improve deep RL.  
- Demonstrates superior performance and faster learning of UNREAL versus A3C on Labyrinth and Atari benchmarks.  
- Empirically validates that auxiliary tasks aligned with the main objective improve feature representation and sample efficiency.  
- Contribution is practical and systematic, though the core idea is not entirely novel.  
- Paper is clearly written with accessible explanations and a well-structured argument.  
- Experimental design is thorough and results convincingly support the claims.  
- Performance gains are notable across challenging environments, including consistent learning speedups without harming final performance.  
- Paper lacks deeper theoretical analysis linking why the chosen auxiliary tasks (frame prediction, entropy estimation) are effective and non-interfering.  
- Two reproducibility/resource concerns remain: (1) unclear computational requirements and whether speedups translate to wall‑clock time or only sample efficiency.  
- (2) Code availability is not confirmed; releasing source code is important given the architecture’s complexity.  
- Rebuttal did not address these reproducibility points, which is concerning for ICLR standards.  
- Overall judgment: strong, practical contribution likely to be adopted; recommendation to accept if computational/code concerns are addressed for camera-ready.
