- Introduces UNREAL: an A3C agent augmented with unsupervised auxiliary tasks (control- and reward-based).  
- Evaluated on Labyrinth and Atari, showing significantly better performance and faster learning than A3C.  
- Contribution is valuable but unsurprising—auxiliary tasks improving feature learning is already established; this is largely a proof of concept.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Requests explicit reporting of computational resources (GPU hours, memory) for reproducibility and scalability assessment.  
- Asks authors to confirm willingness to release source code upon publication to aid validation and extension.  
- Overall judgment: technically modest but valid; acceptance hinges mainly on computational details and code availability.
