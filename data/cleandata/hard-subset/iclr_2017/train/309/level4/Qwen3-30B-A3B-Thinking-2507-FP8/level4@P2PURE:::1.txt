

This paper proposes incorporating unsupervised auxiliary tasks into a deep reinforcement learning agent such as A3C. The authors introduce a set of auxiliary control and reward tasks, evaluating the resulting UNREAL agent on Labyrinth and Atari environments. The UNREAL agent achieves significantly superior performance and faster learning compared to A3C, representing a valuable contribution to the field. However, this outcome is not unexpected, as the integration of relevant auxiliary tasks should inherently enhance feature shaping and accelerate learning, making this work primarily a proof of concept for the approach. The paper is well-written and accessible to readers with deep reinforcement learning expertise. Could the authors provide details on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent? Additionally, given the complexity of the overall architecture, will the authors release the source code for their model?
