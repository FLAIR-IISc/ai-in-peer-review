

This paper introduces an approach to integrate unsupervised auxiliary tasks into deep reinforcement learning agents such as A3C, proposing multiple auxiliary control and reward tasks. The authors evaluate the resulting UNREAL agent on Labyrinth and Atari benchmarks, demonstrating substantially superior performance and faster learning compared to A3C. While this constitutes a valuable contribution to the field, the observed improvements are not unexpected, as incorporating relevant auxiliary tasks inherently enhances feature learning efficiency—a principle this work validates as a proof-of-concept. The paper is clearly written and accessible to readers with deep RL expertise. The authors should clarify the computational resources required for training the UNREAL agent. Given the architecture's notable complexity, the authors are encouraged to commit to releasing the source code for reproducibility.
