- Introduces UNREAL: an RL agent that learns auxiliary control tasks (pixel and feature control) plus reward prediction to improve representations alongside reward maximization.
- Demonstrates large gains in data efficiency and performance on Atari and Labyrinth, reporting 880% human-normalized on Atari and 87% on Labyrinth with a 10× mean learning speedup.
- Recommendation: Accept — the paper makes a strong technical contribution.
- Shows auxiliary control tasks (maximizing pixel/feature changes) outperform passive unsupervised methods (e.g., pixel reconstruction) and substantially accelerate learning.
- Experimental validation is compelling: diverse environments (Atari, Labyrinth) and robust hyperparameter analysis.
- Core innovation: framing auxiliary objectives as control tasks that align with goal-directed behavior rather than passive reconstruction.
- Ablation studies indicate each auxiliary task contributes meaningfully, with A3C+PC notably strong in Labyrinth.
- Reward prediction trained via skewed replay balances sparse rewards and stabilizes learning without biasing the main policy.
- Methodological placement: contrasts with Horde/UVFA by sharing representations for auxiliary tasks and avoids model-based issues in partially observable settings.
- Suggested improvement: explicitly quantify marginal contribution of each auxiliary task in isolation (pixel control vs. feature control).
- Suggested improvement: perform activation/feature analyses to show how each auxiliary task affects representation learning, not just final performance.
- Suggested improvement: include a brief formal definition of L_PC in the main text (Section 3.1) for reproducibility.
- Suggested improvement: clarify whether the reported 10× speedup accounts for A3C’s asynchronous parallelism or is relative to a single-threaded baseline.
