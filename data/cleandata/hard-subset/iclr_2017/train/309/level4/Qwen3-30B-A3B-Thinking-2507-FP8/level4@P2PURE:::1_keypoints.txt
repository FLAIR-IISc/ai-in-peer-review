- Proposes incorporating unsupervised auxiliary tasks into a deep RL agent (A3C) to improve learning.  
- Introduces a set of auxiliary control and reward tasks and evaluates the approach on Labyrinth and Atari.  
- The resulting UNREAL agent achieves significantly superior performance and faster learning compared to A3C.  
- Contribution is valuable but not surprising—primarily a proof-of-concept since relevant auxiliary tasks naturally improve feature shaping and learning speed.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible to readers with deep RL expertise.  
- Reviewer requests details on computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent.  
- Reviewer asks whether the authors will release the source code given the architecture’s complexity.  
- No change in the review after the authors’ rebuttal.
