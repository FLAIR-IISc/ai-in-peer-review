- Proposes training RL agents on auxiliary tasks to learn stronger, more useful features.  
- Introduces two pseudo-control tasks: controlling change in pixel intensity and controlling activation of latent features.  
- Proposes a supervised regression task to predict immediate reward after a sequence, learned offline with skewed replay sampling to balance reward observation at 50%.  
- Agents with these auxiliary tasks achieve large gains, reaching baseline performance in ~10x fewer training iterations on discrete-action, continuous-state tasks.  
- Contrasts with passive unsupervised/model-based learning by leveraging the agent’s capacity for control, which better complements policy learning under partial observability.  
- Method is novel and presents a compelling alternative to unsupervised learning.  
- Auxiliary tasks are well-motivated and intuitive; however, L_PC’s implementation should be introduced earlier (not relegated to the appendix) for accessibility.  
- Paper is clear and accessible to readers familiar with deep RL.  
- Methodology and experimental setup are sound; evaluating the top-3 hyperparameter configurations helps ensure fair comparison despite different hyperparameter distributions.  
- Major limitation: lacks deeper experimental analysis isolating individual contributions of auxiliary tasks (e.g., ablations, feature quality, learning dynamics).  
- Deeper analysis is important future work given space constraints, but its absence does not invalidate the current contribution.
