- Proposes improving feature learning in deep RL by augmenting policy optimization with domain-independent auxiliary tasks (state-control policies, immediate reward prediction, value-function replay).

- Auxiliary tasks (except value-function replay) shape features via shared CNN+LSTM feature extractor.

- Reports significant data-efficiency and performance gains over A3C on Atari and Labyrinth.

- Paper is well-written, ideas are sound, results convincing — recommends acceptance.

- Computational overhead: request to quantify training speed impact and identify which components add most cost.

- Policy-structure clarity: abstract implies a single policy maximizing many pseudo-rewards, but method uses separate policies per task—needs clarification in abstract/introduction.

- Feature-control validation: empirical support is weak (Fig.5 shows marginal initial gains); concerns about dynamic feature modification affecting stability and convergence—stronger validation requested.

- Extended training: recommend training top agents longer to evaluate ultimate performance potential.

- Auxiliary-task weights: question why λ_* are fixed at 1; request ablations or hyperparameter tuning to justify this choice.

- Off-policy training note: clarify that auxiliary tasks use off-policy multi-step returns (not true Q-learning), as mentioned in OpenReview.
