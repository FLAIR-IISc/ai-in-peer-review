

This paper introduces UNREAL, an extension of A3C that integrates unsupervised auxiliary tasks—including control tasks (e.g., predicting future states) and reward tasks (e.g., predicting intrinsic rewards)—to enhance deep reinforcement learning. Evaluated on Labyrinth and Atari benchmarks, UNREAL achieves superior performance and faster convergence compared to A3C, demonstrating that auxiliary tasks can effectively shape feature representations for improved learning. While the empirical results are compelling and well-supported, the core insight—that relevant auxiliary tasks generally accelerate learning—builds directly on established principles in representation learning, making the contribution more of an empirical validation than a conceptual novelty. The paper remains a valuable practical demonstration for the community, however, as it systematically tests and confirms this intuition across diverse environments.

The manuscript is exceptionally well-written, logically structured, and accessible to readers with standard deep RL expertise. The methodology and results are clearly presented, with thorough ablation studies supporting the claims. 

Two critical points warrant clarification for reproducibility and resource transparency:  
1. **Computational Requirements**: The authors should specify the computational resources (e.g., GPU hours, hardware specifications) needed to train UNREAL, as this impacts the accessibility of their approach for other researchers.  
2. **Source Code Availability**: Given the complexity of the architecture, the authors should confirm whether they intend to release the source code, which would facilitate independent validation and extension of their work.  

The authors’ rebuttal did not address these points, but the paper’s technical execution and clear presentation remain strong. While the novelty is limited to empirical confirmation rather than theoretical innovation, the work provides a robust, well-executed case study for auxiliary task integration in RL. This represents a solid, contribution to the field’s practical toolkit, even if the underlying principle is not unexpected. The paper’s clarity and thorough evaluation make it suitable for publication, provided the requested details on computational resources and code availability are addressed in the final version.
