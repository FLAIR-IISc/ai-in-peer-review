- Proposes auxiliary tasks (domain-independent control, immediate reward prediction, value function replay) to improve feature learning in deep RL, integrated into main policy optimization.
- Auxiliary tasks (except value function replay) shape features via a shared CNN+LSTM feature extractor rather than producing separate policies.
- Experiments show improved data efficiency over A3C on Atari and Labyrinth benchmarks.
- Paper is well-written, ideas are sound, and results are convincing—recommends clear acceptance.
- Request to quantify computational overhead of auxiliary tasks: report training speed impact and identify the most costly components.
- Clarify in abstract/introduction that the agent learns distinct policies per task, not a single policy optimizing all pseudo-rewards.
- Feature-control mechanism lacks strong empirical validation (Fig. 5 is inconclusive) and may risk stability/convergence during learning.
- Suggest extending training for best agents to assess longer-term performance trends.
- Justify fixing auxiliary task weights (λ_*) at 1 rather than tuning them as hyperparameters.
- Point out auxiliary tasks use off-policy Q-learning with multi-step rewards (not strict one-step Q-learning); clarify in paper.
