- Introduces UNREAL: an RL agent that learns multiple auxiliary policies for different pseudo-rewards while sharing a continually developing representation.  
- Proposes a mechanism to focus the shared representation on task-relevant aspects, improving data efficiency and performance.  
- Reports strong empirical gains: Atari (880% expert human) and Labyrinth (87% expert human, 10× faster learning).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor concerns—method is well-motivated, technically sound, and rigorously evaluated.  
- Key contribution: combining auxiliary control tasks (pixel/feature control), reward prediction, and value replay substantially improves data efficiency and robustness.  
- Empirical and theoretical grounding: convincing results across diverse environments and good contextualization with related work (auxiliary tasks, UVFAs, successor representations).  
- Clarification requested: explicitly state that separate policies are learned for each auxiliary task (not a single policy optimizing all rewards).  
- Validation requested: provide stronger empirical evidence for the feature control auxiliary task beyond the preliminary Figure 5.  
- Complexity/efficiency concern: discuss the computational overhead and impact on training speed from adding auxiliary tasks.  
- Hyperparameter question: explain why auxiliary task weights (λPC, λVR, λRP) are fixed to specific values instead of being tuned/optimized.  
- Implementation detail: clarify how auxiliary tasks are trained off-policy with multi-step rewards (per OpenReview comments).  
- Specific questions to answer: (1) exact computational overhead vs base A3C, (2) how feature control remains stable while features change, (3) rationale for chosen λ values, (4) whether continued training would yield further improvement and why training stopped when it did.
