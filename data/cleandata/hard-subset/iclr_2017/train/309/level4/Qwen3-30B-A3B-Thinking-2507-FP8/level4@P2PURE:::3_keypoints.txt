- Proposes training RL agents on auxiliary tasks to learn stronger feature representations.
- Introduces two pseudo-control tasks: controlling pixel-intensity changes and controlling latent-feature activations.
- Adds a supervised regression task to predict immediate rewards, learned offline with skewed replay sampling to balance reward occurrence to 50%.
- Agents with these auxiliary tasks achieve significantly higher performance, reaching baseline in roughly 10× fewer training iterations.
- Contrasts with passive unsupervised/model-based learning by avoiding learning useless input representations or infeasible environment models under partial observability.
- Argues that controlling local and internal environmental features complements learning the optimal control policy.
- Presentation is clear and intuitive, but explicit definitions (e.g., L_PC) should appear earlier in the main text rather than the appendix.
- Methodology and evaluation are sound; evaluating the top three hyperparameter configurations likely identifies per-method optimal hyperparameters within explored ranges.
- Critical limitation: lacks detailed experimental analysis isolating the specific effects of individual auxiliary tasks (e.g., pixel control’s disproportionate influence in Labyrinth), which is an important direction for future work.
