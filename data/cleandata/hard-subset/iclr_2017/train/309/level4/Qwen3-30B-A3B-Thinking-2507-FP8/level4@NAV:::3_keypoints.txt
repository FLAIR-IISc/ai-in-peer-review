- Introduces a novel RL training approach that uses auxiliary tasks to enhance feature learning.  
- Proposes two pseudo-control tasks (regulating pixel intensity changes and controlling latent feature activations) plus a supervised regression task predicting immediate reward after event sequences.  
- Trains the reward-prediction task offline with skewed replay-buffer sampling to obtain a 50% probability of reward observations.  
- Agents with these auxiliary tasks achieve large gains, reaching baseline performance in ~10× fewer iterations on discrete-action continuous-space benchmarks.  
- Diverges from passive unsupervised/model-based methods by leveraging the agent’s ability to control local and internal environmental features.  
- Presents a compelling, novel alternative that complements optimal policy learning by harnessing agent controllability.  
- Task descriptions are high-level and intuitive but need more implementation detail (e.g., introduce L_PC notation before the appendix).  
- Paper is well-structured, accessible to Deep RL readers, and the methodology is sound.  
- Using top-3 performance metrics is a robust approach to identify best hyperparameters across A3C and UNREAL differences.  
- Major limitation: lacks experimental analysis isolating the individual contributions of the auxiliary tasks (pixel vs feature control) and deeper task-specific investigation.  
- Empirical note: in Labyrinth, A3C+PC alone outperforms most variants except UNREAL, highlighting the need for ablation studies.
