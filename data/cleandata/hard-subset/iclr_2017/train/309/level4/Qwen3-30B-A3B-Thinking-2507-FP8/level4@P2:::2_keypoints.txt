- Introduces UNREAL: an agent architecture that augments deep RL with auxiliary tasks that control sensorimotor aspects.  
- Trains multiple auxiliary control policies (pixel changes, network-feature control) plus reward prediction and value replay.  
- Auxiliary tasks share the main feature extractor so representations generalize across objectives.  
- Strong empirical gains: 880% human-normalized on Atari (2.5× prior work), 87% on Labyrinth, and ~10× faster learning.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with high confidence, citing strong contribution and execution.  
- Paper clearly addresses the problem of improving feature learning by jointly learning auxiliary control policies.  
- Novelty: shapes features via auxiliary control policies without using them directly for action selection; connects to Horde/UVFA/successor representations.  
- Empirical validation is rigorous across two domains, with ablation studies and statistical significance reported.  
- Paper is clear and well-presented; main contribution and experimental design are well articulated.
