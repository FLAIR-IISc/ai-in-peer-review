- Introduces UNREAL: an A3C extension that integrates unsupervised auxiliary tasks (control and reward prediction) to improve deep RL.  
- Evaluated on Labyrinth and Atari, showing superior performance and faster convergence compared to A3C.  
- Demonstrates that auxiliary tasks effectively shape feature representations to improve learning.  
- Empirical results are compelling and well-supported by experiments.  
- Core insight is largely an empirical validation of established representation-learning principles rather than a novel theoretical contribution.  
- Provides a valuable, systematic practical demonstration across diverse environments.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written, logically structured, and accessible to standard deep RL readers.  
- Methodology and results are clearly presented with thorough ablation studies.  
- Two critical reproducibility/resource clarifications requested: specify computational requirements (GPU hours, hardware) and confirm source code availability.  
- Authors’ rebuttal did not address the computational and code availability concerns.  
- Overall: strong technical execution and presentation; limited novelty but suitable for publication if resource and code details are added.
