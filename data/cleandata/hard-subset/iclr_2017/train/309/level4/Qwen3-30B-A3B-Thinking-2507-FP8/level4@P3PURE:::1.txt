

This paper introduces the UNREAL agent, which enhances deep reinforcement learning (RL) agents like A3C through the incorporation of unsupervised auxiliary tasks—including both control and reward tasks. The authors evaluate UNREAL on Labyrinth and Atari benchmarks, demonstrating significant performance improvements over A3C alongside faster learning convergence. While the contribution is valuable and well-executed, the core idea—that relevant auxiliary tasks facilitate better feature learning and accelerate training—is not unexpected; this work serves as a compelling proof of concept for the established principle. The paper is well-written and accessible to readers with deep RL expertise. To aid reproducibility, the authors should address two points: (1) the computational resources required to train UNREAL, and (2) their willingness to release the source code for the model. No changes were made to this review following the rebuttal.
