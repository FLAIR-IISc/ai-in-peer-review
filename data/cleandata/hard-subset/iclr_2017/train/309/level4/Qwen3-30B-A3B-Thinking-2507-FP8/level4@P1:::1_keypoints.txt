- Paper proposes UNREAL: augmenting A3C with auxiliary control and reward-prediction tasks to learn richer representations.  
- Strong empirical claims: 87% human-normalized on Labyrinth (vs. A3C 54%) with 10× learning speedup; 880% mean human performance on Atari.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main critique: novelty gap — auxiliary control tasks echo prior work (Horde, UVFA, successor representations) and are incremental.  
- UNREAL does not exploit Horde/UVFA strengths (e.g., planning or transfer), so contribution is limited.  
- Key claimed advantage (pixel control > reconstruction) is not adequately justified or compared to prior RL-based auxiliary methods.  
- Empirical rigor issues: Atari 880% lacks context/benchmarks against recent SOTA (e.g., Prioritized Dueling DQN, Rainbow, IMPALA).  
- Labyrinth 10× speedup may be confounded by replay usage; paper does not isolate source of gains.  
- Technical clarity problems: unified loss weights (λVR, λPC, etc.) lack justification and sensitivity analysis.  
- Ablation findings are incomplete—feature vs. pixel control difference is unexplained and not analyzed.  
- Reviewer suggestions: directly compare to UVFA/Horde, benchmark against modern SOTA, analyze why pixel control outperforms feature control, and report computational costs.  
- Questions for authors: how does UNREAL compare to Rainbow/IMPALA; why does reconstruction harm performance here versus prior positive results; and what are the training time/memory costs and scalability?  
- Overall: empirically strong but incremental and insufficiently contextualized, so not suitable for ICLR.
