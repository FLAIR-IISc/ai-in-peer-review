- Introduces training RL agents with three auxiliary tasks: pixel control (PC), latent feature control (L_PC), and supervised reward prediction.  
- Reward prediction is learned offline using stratified replay sampling to maintain a 50:50 ratio of reward to non-reward transitions.  
- Agents with these auxiliary tasks achieve large gains, reaching baseline performance in roughly 10× fewer training iterations.  
- Core contribution: reframes unsupervised learning as active/self-supervised control of local and internal features rather than passive observation or model learning.  
- Argues that controlling features the agent can influence is more actionable and effective, especially under partial observability.  
- Novelty is compelling — moves beyond static representation learning to leverage agent-environment interactions.  
- Paper is generally well-written and accessible to deep RL readers.  
- Clarity issue: L_PC should be explicitly defined earlier in the main text rather than in the appendix.  
- Methodology is sound and robustness to hyperparameters is addressed by reporting top-3 results across the explored space for A3C and UNREAL.  
- Major experimental gap: no ablation isolating the individual contributions of PC, L_PC, and reward prediction.  
- Open questions: relative contributions of PC vs L_PC, their effects on representation quality/learning speed, and possible diminishing returns or task interactions.  
- Current evaluation focuses only on final RL performance; stronger evidence requires analyses of feature dynamics, representation quality, and per-task learning effects.  
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to clarify task definitions and to address the lack of per-task experimental dissection.
