- Summary: Introduces UNREAL, an RL agent that jointly learns auxiliary control and prediction tasks (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction) to produce better representations and accelerate learning.  
- Decision: Accept — compelling contribution with rigorous empirical validation and novel approach.  
- Core innovation: Treats auxiliary tasks as control problems (not reconstruction), aligning representation learning with the agent’s actionable goals.  
- Atari results: Achieves 880% mean human-normalized score across 57 Atari games, outperforming A3C and Prioritized Dueling DQN.  
- Labyrinth results: Reaches 87% human-normalized performance and converges 10× faster than A3C.  
- Quantitative rigor: Experiments use standard protocols and extensive hyperparameter sweeps (e.g., 50 runs per game).  
- Robustness: Performance is less sensitive to hyperparameters and ablations show each auxiliary task contributes.  
- Pixel control dominance: Pixel control is the largest contributor to gains; A3C + pixel control outperforms other combinations on Labyrinth.  
- Theoretical positioning: Places UNREAL between unsupervised learning and model-based RL, arguing control-oriented tasks better leverage agency.  
- Reward prediction tweak: Uses skewed replay for reward prediction to address sparsity — a sound technical solution.  
- Methodology soundness: Well-designed ablations and isolation of auxiliary task impacts support the claims.  
- Minor improvement 1: Clarify the pixel control loss weight (L_PC) in the main text rather than only the appendix.  
- Minor improvement 2: Add brief analysis on why pixel control is most effective (e.g., alignment with spatiotemporal visual dynamics).  
- Minor improvement 3: Discuss scalability to continuous/high-dimensional action spaces.  
- Question 1: How were Labyrinth’s 20×20 pixel control grid boundaries chosen and would coarser grids (e.g., 10×10) retain benefits with less cost?  
- Question 2: Why use a 3-class reward prediction (pos/neg/zero) instead of continuous regression (MSE), and were performance differences observed?  
- Question 3: Why is feature control slightly worse than pixel control — due to feature selection (layer choice) or does pixel control inherently capture more useful dynamics?
