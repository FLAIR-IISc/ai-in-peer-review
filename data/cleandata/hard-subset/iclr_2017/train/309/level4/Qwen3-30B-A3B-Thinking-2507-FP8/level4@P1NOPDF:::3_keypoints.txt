- Proposes auxiliary tasks (pixel control, latent feature control, reward prediction) to improve representation learning in RL agents.  
- Reward prediction is learned offline using skewed replay sampling to ensure a 50% chance of observing rewards.  
- Agents with these auxiliary tasks reach baseline performance in roughly 10× fewer training iterations on discrete-action, continuous-state benchmarks.  
- Distinguishes itself from passive unsupervised/model-based methods by leveraging the agent’s ability to control local environmental features.  
- The dual focus—learning control policies while manipulating internal features—complements the primary RL objective and offers a novel alternative to standard unsupervised strategies.  
- Core idea is compelling and well-motivated, with thorough empirical validation.  
- Exposition is generally clear, but technical details (e.g., explicit definition of L_PC) should appear before the appendix for reproducibility.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity is addressed by reporting top-3 performance across the search space, improving robustness for A3C and UNREAL.  
- Major limitation: insufficient mechanistic analysis of how auxiliary tasks yield performance gains (e.g., feature quality vs. exploration).  
- Lacks isolation of individual task effects and ablation studies to explain why pixel control outperforms feature control in some settings.  
- Overall assessment: novel, well-executed, accessible to Deep RL practitioners, and a meaningful step toward efficient and interpretable representation learning.
