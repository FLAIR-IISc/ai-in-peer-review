

This paper introduces a novel reinforcement learning agent that learns multiple auxiliary policies for maximizing various pseudo-reward functions simultaneously, sharing a common representation that continues to develop even without extrinsic rewards. The authors propose a mechanism to focus this representation on relevant aspects of the actual task, significantly improving data efficiency and performance. The UNREAL agent outperforms previous state-of-the-art methods on Atari (880% expert human performance) and Labyrinth tasks (87% expert human performance, 10× faster learning).

I recommend acceptance with minor concerns. The paper presents a well-motivated, technically sound approach that is clearly supported by rigorous experiments across multiple challenging environments.

The work makes a strong contribution to reinforcement learning by demonstrating that auxiliary control tasks (pixel control and feature control) combined with reward prediction and value function replay substantially improve data efficiency and robustness. The empirical results are convincing, showing clear improvements over baseline methods across diverse environments. The theoretical foundation is solid, with appropriate contextualization within related work on auxiliary tasks, UVFAs, and successor representations.

For further improvement, please address the following points:
- Clarify in the abstract and introduction that the agent learns separate policies for each auxiliary task (not a single policy optimizing all rewards simultaneously), as this was initially confusing.
- Provide empirical validation of the feature control auxiliary task beyond the preliminary experiment in Figure 5, as the current results are not sufficiently convincing.
- Discuss the computational cost of implementing these auxiliary tasks and whether training speed is impacted.
- Explain why the auxiliary task weights (λ) are fixed to specific values rather than being optimized as hyperparameters.
- Clarify the technical details of training auxiliary tasks off-policy with multi-step rewards as mentioned in the OpenReview comments.

Please answer these questions to help clarify the paper:
1. What is the computational overhead of implementing the auxiliary tasks compared to the base A3C agent?
2. How does the feature control task maintain stability when the extracted features are continuously modified during learning?
3. Why were the specific values for λPC, λVR, and λRP chosen rather than optimizing them as hyperparameters?
4. Could the agent continue to improve with additional training time, and if so, why was the training stopped at the current point?
