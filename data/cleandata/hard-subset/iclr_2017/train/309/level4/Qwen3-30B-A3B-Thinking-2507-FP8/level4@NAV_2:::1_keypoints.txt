- Introduces UNREAL, an RL agent that learns multiple auxiliary tasks simultaneously to improve representations and task performance.  
- Framework trains separate policies to maximize pseudo-reward functions (e.g., pixel control, feature control) while sharing a common representation.  
- Incorporates reward prediction as an auxiliary task to bias representations toward task-relevant aspects of the environment.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains: Labyrinth — 87% human-normalized performance with 10× faster learning; Atari — 880% mean human performance.  
- Recommends acceptance, citing a meaningful contribution to improving performance and learning efficiency via auxiliary tasks.  
- Claims scientific rigor with comprehensive experiments across domains and thorough ablation studies.  
- Positions work well in literature, building on auxiliary-task prior work (Horde, UVFA, successor representations) and introducing novel integration mechanisms.  
- Provides a theoretical foundation arguing auxiliary control tasks and reward prediction shape useful representations without biasing primary objective.  
- Empirical results are compelling, showing consistent improvements, faster learning, and robustness to hyperparameters.  
- Suggested improvement: include deeper analysis of the specific features learned via auxiliary tasks and their transfer across environments.  
- Suggested improvement: provide a more thorough discussion of computational training costs for practical adoption.  
- Requests clarification on computational resources required (GPU hours, memory) versus baseline A3C and whether the UNREAL source code will be released.
