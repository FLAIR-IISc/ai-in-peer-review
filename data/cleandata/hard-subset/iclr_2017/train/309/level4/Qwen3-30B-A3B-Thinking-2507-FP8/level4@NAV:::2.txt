

This paper proposes enhancing feature learning in deep reinforcement learning through auxiliary tasks—specifically, domain-independent control (learning policies to maximally modify state space), immediate reward prediction, and value function replay—integrated into the main policy's optimization. The auxiliary tasks (except value function replay) solely shape features via shared CNN+LSTM feature extraction. Experiments demonstrate improved data efficiency over A3C on Atari and Labyrinth benchmarks. The paper is well-written, ideas are sound, and results are convincing, warranting clear acceptance. Minor points for consideration:

- Address the computational overhead of auxiliary tasks: quantify training speed impact and identify most costly components.
- Clarify in the abstract/introduction that the agent learns *distinct policies* per task (not a single policy optimizing all pseudo-rewards simultaneously), as the current phrasing caused initial misinterpretation.
- The "feature control" mechanism lacks empirical validation (Fig. 5's preliminary results are inconclusive). The dynamic feature modification during learning risks stability/convergence issues.
- Extend training for the best agents to assess long-term performance trends, given their steady improvement.
- Justify why auxiliary task weights (λ_*) are fixed at 1 rather than optimized as hyperparameters.
- Note that auxiliary tasks use off-policy Q-learning with multi-step rewards (not true Q-learning), as highlighted in OpenReview.
