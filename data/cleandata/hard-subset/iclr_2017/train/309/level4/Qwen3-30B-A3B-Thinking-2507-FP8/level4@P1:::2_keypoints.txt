- Introduces UNREAL, an RL agent that improves data efficiency and robustness by using auxiliary tasks.  
- Key innovation: train multiple policies for different pseudo-rewards (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction) while sharing a common representation.  
- Learning to control sensorimotorstream aspects yields representations that accelerate the main task.  
- Strong empirical gains: Atari (880% expert human), Labyrinth (87% expert human), and ~10× faster learning.  
- Recommendation: accept — auxiliary tasks are a general, effective representation-learning approach for RL.  
- Paper is well-structured and provides sufficient theoretical grounding for the method.  
- Extensive experiments show consistent improvements and robustness to hyperparameters compared to A3C.  
- Ablation studies indicate each auxiliary task contributes meaningfully, with the combination performing best.  
- Comparison with alternative unsupervised approaches (pixel reconstruction, immediate prediction) supports the method’s advantages.  
- Suggest clarifying in abstract/introduction that separate policies are learned for each auxiliary task to avoid misunderstanding.  
- Feature control needs more empirical validation or tempered claims, as Figure 5 shows only marginal/short-term gains.  
- Address computational cost: report additional training time and which auxiliary components are most expensive.  
- Report asymptotic performance by continuing training to show final performance beyond steady improvement.  
- Justify auxiliary task weights (λ values) or include sensitivity experiments to show robustness to these choices.  
- Clarify training details: explicitly state auxiliary tasks are trained off-policy and whether true Q-learning is used.  
- Fix minor technical issues: define L_PC in Eq.2, correct λC placement in Eq.1, and grammar ("is shared" → "are shared").  
- Correct figure references/legends: specify percentages are for Labyrinth, and change "Figure 3 (right)" to "Figure 3 (Top Right)".  
- Add more ablation studies on Atari to verify whether the contribution pattern (pixel control dominance) holds across domains.  
- Properly reference supplementary materials and indicate where they can be found.
