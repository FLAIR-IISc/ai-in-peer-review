

This paper proposes enhancing feature learning in deep reinforcement learning by augmenting the main policy's optimization with domain-independent auxiliary tasks: state-modifying control policies, immediate reward prediction, and value function replay. The auxiliary tasks (except value function replay) solely shape features through shared CNN+LSTM feature extraction. Results demonstrate significant data efficiency gains over A3C on Atari and Labyrinth, with compelling performance improvements. The paper is well-written, ideas are sound, and results are convincing—this warrants acceptance. I have minor points for clarification, none critical:

1. **Computational overhead**: Please quantify the training speed impact of auxiliary tasks. Which components contribute most to the added cost?

2. **Clarity on policy structure**: The abstract states the agent "maximizes many pseudo-reward functions simultaneously." This suggests a single policy optimizing all rewards, but the method uses separate policies per task (per Equation 1). Clarify this in the abstract/introduction to avoid initial misinterpretation.

3. **Feature control validation**: The "feature control" approach (Section 3.2) lacks convincing empirical support. Figure 5 shows only marginal initial gains, and the dynamic feature modification during learning raises stability/convergence concerns. Provide stronger validation.

4. **Extended training**: Since agent performance "steadily improves" (Section 4.1), why not train the top agents further to assess ultimate potential?

5. **Auxiliary task weights**: Why are λ_* fixed at 1? Were ablations tested to validate this choice? Optimizing these as hyperparameters would strengthen the approach.

6. **Off-policy training note**: Clarify that auxiliary tasks use off-policy training with multi-step rewards (not true Q-learning), as noted in OpenReview comments.

- Equation 2 references undefined L_PC; clarify or remove.
