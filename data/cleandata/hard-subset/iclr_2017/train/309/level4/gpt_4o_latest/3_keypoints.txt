- Introduces training RL agents with auxiliary tasks to learn more robust features.  
- Proposes two pseudo-control tasks: controlling pixel intensity changes and controlling latent feature activations.  
- Adds a supervised regression task to predict immediate reward after event sequences, trained offline with skewed replay sampling (balanced 50/50 reward vs. no-reward).  
- Demonstrates significant performance gains: reaches baseline with ~10× fewer iterations on discrete-action continuous-space tasks.  
- Contrasts with passive unsupervised/model-based methods by leveraging the agent’s control to learn relevant representations under partial observability.  
- Novel and compelling alternative to unsupervised learning; auxiliary tasks presented at a high level that aid intuition.  
- Clarity issue: L_PC loss should be introduced earlier in the main text rather than placed in the appendix.  
- Methodology seems sound; using top-3 performance metrics helps identify optimal hyperparameters for A3C and UNREAL.  
- Limitation: lacks detailed experimental analysis of the auxiliary tasks themselves beyond overall performance impact.  
- Pixel/feature control appears most influential (e.g., A3C+PC in Labyrinth), and deserves deeper, isolated analysis with additional metrics.
