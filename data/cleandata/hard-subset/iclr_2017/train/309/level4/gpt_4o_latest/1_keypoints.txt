- Introduces a method to incorporate unsupervised auxiliary tasks into deep RL agents (e.g., A3C).
- Presents a variety of auxiliary control and reward tasks.
- Evaluates the approach on Labyrinth and Atari environments.
- UNREAL agent demonstrates notable improvements over A3C in both performance and learning speed.
- Considered a valuable contribution to the conference.
- Results not particularly surprising, since relevant auxiliary tasks are expected to enhance and accelerate feature learning.
- Serves primarily as a proof of concept for that principle.
- Paper is well-written and accessible to readers with deep RL expertise.
- Requests information on computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent.
- Notes the overall architecture appears quite complex and asks whether the authors will release the source code.
- After rebuttal, the reviewer made no change to their evaluation.
