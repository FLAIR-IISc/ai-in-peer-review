- Summary: Paper augments policy learning with domain-independent auxiliary tasks (control, reward prediction, value replay) to improve shared feature learning; shows strong data-efficiency gains on Atari and Labyrinth.
- Recommendation: Well-written, robust ideas, compelling results — clear acceptance from reviewer.

Major concerns/questions:
- Report additional computational cost: how much do auxiliary tasks slow training and which components are most expensive?
- Clarify in abstract/introduction that separate policies are learned per auxiliary task (current wording suggests a single policy optimizing many rewards).
- Empirical validation of "feature control" is weak; Figure 5 shows only slight initial gains and raises stability/convergence concerns as tasks evolve.
- Let agents train longer since performance was still improving; report eventual performance for best agents.
- Why weren’t auxiliary task weights (lambda_*) treated/tuned as hyperparameters? Any experiments justifying setting them to 1?
- Explicitly state auxiliary tasks are not trained with "true" Q-learning — they are off-policy trained using multi-step empirical rewards.

Minor/issues to fix:
- Correction: policy gradient loss written as L_pi = -... is presented as maximizing but is actually a loss to minimize.

- Equation 2 references L_PC which is not defined or shown explicitly.
- Specify the value of lambda_PC (presumably 1) explicitly.
- Figure and text cross-references: Figure 2 not referenced; Figure 1(d) should be referenced in Sec. 3.3; multiple incorrect figure references in Sec. 4.1.2 and 4.2 (top/left/right labels and punctuation).
- Figures/legends: explain "Clip" for dueling networks in Figure 3 legend; clarify that percentages in Figure 3 legend refer to Labyrinth.
- Clarify caption/text for Figure 5 which seems to show only two Labyrinth levels despite text saying three.
- Grammar/typo fixes: "The features discovered in this manner is shared" → "are shared."
- Request more Atari ablation studies to verify if individual contributions mirror Labyrinth results.
