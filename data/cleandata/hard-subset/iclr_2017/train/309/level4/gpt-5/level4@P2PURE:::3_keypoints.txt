- Paper proposes training RL agents on auxiliary pseudo-control tasks (pixel intensity control and latent feature control) plus a supervised reward-prediction head to improve representations.
- Reward-prediction head is trained offline with skewed sampling from replay to balance reward/no-reward cases at 50/50.
- Agents achieve strong performance on discrete-action, continuous-state tasks and reach baseline performance roughly ten times faster.
- Approach contrasts with passive unsupervised/model-based methods by using control-based auxiliary tasks that complement policy learning under partial observability.
- Reviewer finds the idea novel and compelling as an alternative to unsupervised representation learning.
- Some auxiliary-task details are high-level; specific technical definitions (e.g., L_PC) should be moved into the main text.
- Overall paper is clear and accessible to readers familiar with deep RL.
- Methodology appears sound; reporting top-3 performance helps mitigate differences in optimal hyperparameters across methods.
- Key weakness: limited experimental analysis of how auxiliary tasks affect representations beyond aggregate performance.
- Pixel/feature control seems to drive most gains (A3C+PC outperforms most variants), so further isolated or deeper analysis with additional metrics is recommended.
