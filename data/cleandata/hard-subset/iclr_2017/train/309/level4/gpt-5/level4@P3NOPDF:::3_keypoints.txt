- Proposal: augment RL with auxiliary control tasks (pixel-intensity and latent-feature control) plus a replay-based reward-prediction task to shape representations.  
- Empirical result: large sample-efficiency improvements, reaching baseline performance in roughly an order of magnitude fewer iterations, especially on Labyrinth.  
- Conceptual claim: leverages the agent’s ability to act (control-based objectives) rather than passive unsupervised or brittle model learning.  
- Strength: clear, compelling idea and motivation that is practical and accessible to deep RL readers.  
- Strength: strong empirical gains and sensible engineering (replay balancing) that stabilizes reward-prediction learning.  
- Strength: methodology is generally sound with fair hyperparameter selection via top-k evaluation.  
- Weakness: objective-level details (exact losses, normalizations, discounting, weighting coefficients) are relegated to the appendix and should be in the main text for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of why each auxiliary task helps; needs systematic ablations isolating pixel control, feature control, and reward prediction.  
- Weakness: sensitivity analyses missing—vary auxiliary-loss weights and update proportions to assess robustness and impact on stability/variance.  
- Weakness: replay-balancing mechanism is underspecified—clarify sampling method, buffer segmentation, bias correction, and whether targets are binary/real-valued; include ablations with different reward ratios.  
- Weakness: scope limited to discrete-action, continuous-state domains; provide evidence or discussion for continuous-action control (e.g., MuJoCo) and explicit POMDP benchmarks.  
- Weakness: computational overhead not reported; need wall-clock speedups/slowdowns and frames-to-threshold to contextualize the “10x fewer iterations” claim.  
- Clarity: elevate crucial definitions (pixel-control objective, feature-control architecture, reward-prediction target) from appendix to main text and add a schematic of loss integration and update schedules.  
- Methodology note: evaluation is reasonable but should confirm hyperparameter ranges cover community norms and include at least one competitive unsupervised baseline (e.g., reconstruction pretraining).  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to spatial/temporal resolution of pixel control and choice of latent features for feature control.  
- Questions for authors: do auxiliary tasks ever conflict with long-horizon main objectives, and how is that mitigated?  
- Questions for authors: could intrinsic rewards from control replace or complement auxiliary losses, and what are the trade-offs?  
- Overall assessment: strong, practical contribution with convincing sample-efficiency gains; recommend acceptance pending added objective-level detail and deeper ablations.
