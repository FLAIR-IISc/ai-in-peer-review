This paper proposes augmenting a deep RL agent such as A3C with unsupervised auxiliary tasks. The authors introduce a set of auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks, and evaluate the approach on Labyrinth and Atari. The resulting UNREAL agent performs significantly better than baseline A3C and also learns faster. This is a solid contribution to the conference.

That said, the result is not particularly surprising: adding auxiliary tasks that are relevant to the main objective should generally improve representation learning and accelerate training by shaping features more effectively. In this sense, the paper serves as a proof of concept for this idea.

The paper is well written and easy to follow for readers with expertise in deep RL.

Questions and concerns:
- Could the authors comment on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent?
- The overall architecture is quite complex. Are the authors willing to release the source code for their model?
