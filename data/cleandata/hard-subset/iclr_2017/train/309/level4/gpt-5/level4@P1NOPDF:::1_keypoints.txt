- Proposes augmenting deep RL agents (e.g., A3C) with unsupervised auxiliary tasks (UNREAL).
- Introduces auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks.
- Evaluated on Labyrinth and Atari benchmarks.
- UNREAL performs significantly better than baseline A3C and learns faster.
- Considered a solid conference contribution.
- Result not surprising—auxiliary tasks naturally improve representations and speed training; serves as a proof of concept.
- Paper is well written and easy to follow for deep RL experts.
- Question: what computational resources are required to train UNREAL?
- Concern: overall architecture is quite complex; will the authors release the source code?
