This paper introduces UNREAL, which augments an A3C-style deep RL agent with unsupervised auxiliary tasks. The authors propose multiple auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward-prediction tasks, and evaluate the approach on DeepMind Lab (Labyrinth) and Atari. The UNREAL agent consistently outperforms the A3C baseline and also learns faster. This is a strong contribution to the conference. However, the improvements are not especially surprising: well-chosen auxiliary tasks that are relevant to the main objective are generally expected to improve representation learning and accelerate training. As such, the paper serves primarily as a compelling proof of concept for this idea.

The paper is clearly written and should be easy to follow for readers with deep RL expertise.

Questions for the authors:
- Please detail the computational resources required to train UNREAL (e.g., hardware configuration, number of parallel actors, total environment frames, and wall-clock time).
- The overall architecture appears fairly complex. Do you plan to release the source code to facilitate replication?
