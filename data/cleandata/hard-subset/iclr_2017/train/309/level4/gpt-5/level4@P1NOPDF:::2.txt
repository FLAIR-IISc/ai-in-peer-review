This paper proposes improving feature learning in deep reinforcement learning by augmenting the main policy’s objective with domain-agnostic auxiliary tasks. These include control (learning additional policies that try to maximally modify the state, here the pixels), immediate reward prediction, and value-function replay. Except for the latter, the auxiliary tasks are used solely to shape the representation via a shared CNN+LSTM feature extractor. Experiments on Atari and Labyrinth show clear benefits, especially much better data efficiency than A3C.

The paper is well written, the ideas are sound, and the results are convincing; I recommend acceptance. I have a few high-level comments, none of which are major concerns:
- Please discuss the additional computational cost of optimizing the auxiliary tasks. How much training speed is lost, and which components are most expensive?
- In the abstract/intro, please make it explicit that the agent learns separate policies for each auxiliary task. When the abstract says the agent “also maximises many other pseudo-reward functions simultaneously by reinforcement learning,” I initially thought a single policy was optimizing all rewards jointly, and only realized the correct interpretation at Eq. 1.
- The “feature control” idea is not convincingly validated empirically. The preliminary result in Fig. 5 seems to provide only a slight initial benefit. I like the idea, but I’m concerned that the task itself changes during learning as the representation evolves, which could introduce stability/convergence issues.
- Since “the performance of our agents is still steadily improving,” why not run the best agents longer to show their eventual performance?
- Why aren’t the auxiliary-task weights (the λ_*) treated as hyperparameters? Were any experiments run to validate that using 1 is a good choice?
- Please note that the auxiliary tasks are not trained with “true” Q-learning, since they are off-policy with multi-step empirical returns (as discussed on OpenReview).

Minor comments:
- “Policy gradient algorithms adjust the policy to maximise the expected reward, L_pi = - ...”: that expression is a loss to be minimized.
- In Eq. 1, λ_c should be inside the summation.
- Just below Eq. 1, r_t^(c) should be r_{t+k}^(c).
- Figure 2 does not seem to be referenced in the text; also Figure 1(d) should be referenced in Section 3.3.
- “the features discovered in this manner is shared” should be “are shared.”
- The text around Eq. 2 refers to the loss L_PC, but that term is not defined and does not appear (explicitly) in Eq. 2.
- Please explain what “Clip” means for dueling networks in the Figure 3 legend.
- I would like to see more ablations on Atari, to check whether the individual-contribution patterns match those observed on Labyrinth.
- In the Figure 3 legend, the percentages refer to Labyrinth; this should be made explicit in the text.
- In Section 4.1.2: “Figure 3 (right) shows...” actually refers to the top-left plot. Later, “This is shown in Figure 3 Top” should be “Figure 3, top right.”
- “Figure 5 shows the learning curves for the top 5 hyperparameter settings on three Labyrinth navigation levels” seems to refer to the left and middle plots only (i.e., two levels). The surrounding text may also need correction.
- In Section 4.2: “The left side shows the average performance curves of the top 5 agents for all three methods the right half shows...” needs punctuation after “methods.”
- Appendix: “Further details are included in the supplementary materials.” Please indicate where these details can be found.
- What is the value of λ_PC? (I assume 1.)
