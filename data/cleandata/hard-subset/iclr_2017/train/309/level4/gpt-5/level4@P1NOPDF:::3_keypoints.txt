- Proposes training RL agents with auxiliary pseudo-control objectives: controlling pixel intensity changes and latent feature activations, plus a supervised reward-prediction head.
- Reward-prediction head is trained offline using skewed sampling from replay to balance rewarding vs non-rewarding samples (~50/50).
- Agents achieve strong empirical results on discrete-action, continuous-state tasks, reaching baseline performance in roughly ten times fewer iterations.
- Approach contrasts with passive unsupervised/model-based learning by explicitly exploiting the agent’s control to learn useful representations.
- Reviewer finds the approach novel and a compelling alternative to unsupervised representation learning.
- Exposition is clear at a high level, but some lower-level details (e.g., explicit definition of pixel-control loss L_PC) should be moved into the main text.
- Methodology appears sound; reporting top-3 performance helps account for differing optimal hyperparameter distributions across methods.
- Major weakness: limited experimental analysis of how each auxiliary task affects learning beyond aggregate performance.
- Pixel/feature control seems to drive most gains (e.g., pixel control alone outperforms all but UNREAL on Labyrinth); more ablations and measures (learning speed, representation quality, stability) are needed to explain when and why each task helps.
