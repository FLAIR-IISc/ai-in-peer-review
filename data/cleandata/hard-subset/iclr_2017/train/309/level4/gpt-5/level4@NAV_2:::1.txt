1. Summary of contributions
The paper introduces an approach for augmenting a deep RL agent (built on A3C) with unsupervised auxiliary tasks. The authors propose a set of auxiliary control tasks (e.g., policies that maximize pseudo-rewards such as pixel or feature changes) and auxiliary reward-focused tasks (e.g., immediate reward prediction), trained largely off-policy from replay while sharing representations with the main policy/value networks. The resulting UNREAL agent is evaluated on Labyrinth and Atari and is shown to attain substantially higher scores than A3C while learning more quickly. The paper is clearly written and accessible to readers familiar with deep RL.

2. Decision
Accept. The work delivers strong empirical improvements in both performance and learning speed over a widely used baseline, and provides a clear, practical instantiation of how auxiliary tasks can shape more effective representations for RL.

3. Supporting arguments
- The empirical results convincingly demonstrate that augmenting with auxiliary control and reward prediction tasks yields substantial gains over A3C across two diverse domains (Labyrinth and Atari), and improves data efficiency.
- The methodology is straightforward to integrate with standard deep RL agents and leverages experience replay effectively for both efficiency and stability.
- While the outcomes are not entirely surprising—auxiliary tasks that align with the main objective can be expected to enhance representation learning—the paper serves as a solid proof of concept that validates this intuition at scale with careful engineering.

4. Additional feedback (to help improve the paper; not necessarily part of the decision)
- The overall architecture has many moving parts. A more streamlined description and a higher-level ablation of which components matter most for which settings could improve readability for practitioners.
- Practical guidance on compute requirements (e.g., wall-clock time, hardware configuration) would help others reproduce the results and gauge feasibility.
- If possible, releasing the implementation would significantly aid adoption and reproducibility.

Questions for the authors
- What computational resources (e.g., number/type of GPUs/CPUs, wall-clock training time per game/level) are required to train the UNREAL agent to the reported performance?
- Given the architectural complexity, are you planning to release source code or reference implementations?
