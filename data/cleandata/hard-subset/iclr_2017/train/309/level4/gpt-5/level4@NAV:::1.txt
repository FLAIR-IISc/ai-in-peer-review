This paper introduces a method for augmenting a deep RL agent such as A3C with unsupervised auxiliary tasks. The authors propose several auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks and evaluate the agent on Labyrinth and Atari. The resulting UNREAL agent performs markedly better than A3C and also learns more quickly. This is a strong contribution to the conference. However, the outcome is not particularly surprising, since adding goal-relevant auxiliary tasks should generally aid in faster and better feature shaping. This work serves as a proof of concept for that idea.
The paper is clearly written and easy to follow for readers with deep RL expertise.
Can the authors comment on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent?
The overall architecture is fairly complex. Are the authors willing to release the source code for their model?
