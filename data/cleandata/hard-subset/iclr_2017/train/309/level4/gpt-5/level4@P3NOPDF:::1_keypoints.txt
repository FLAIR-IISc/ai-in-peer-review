- Proposes UNREAL: augmenting A3C with unsupervised auxiliary control and reward-related tasks, showing large improvements on DeepMind Lab and Atari in both final performance and learning speed.
- Strength: Clear and compelling empirical gains over a strong A3C baseline across two challenging domains.
- Strength: Demonstrates faster learning and better asymptotic performance, suggesting improved representations and exploration.
- Strength: Paper is well-written with an intuitively appealing motivation for auxiliary objectives.
- Concern: Core idea is sensible and expected; contribution is primarily strong empirical validation/systemization rather than a surprising theoretical or algorithmic breakthrough.
- Concern: Architecture and training setup are relatively complex with multiple components and losses, raising implementation burden, hyperparameter sensitivity, and reproducibility questions.
- Concern: Insufficient analysis isolating the impact and relative weighting of each auxiliary task; thorough ablations are needed to attribute gains to specific choices.
- Question: Please provide compute details (CPU/GPU counts, wall-clock time per environment, total environment frames, parallelization) and comparison to vanilla A3C.
- Question: How sensitive is performance to choice and weighting of auxiliary losses; provide per-task ablations and variance across runs.
- Question: How general are the auxiliary tasks across domains—how domain-specific are the control/reward tasks for Labyrinth vs. Atari?
- Question: Will the authors release code and detailed training scripts/configurations to facilitate replication given the system complexity?
- Overall: Solid, timely contribution with substantial empirical improvements likely to influence RL representation learning; clarity on compute, ablations, and code would strengthen the paper.
