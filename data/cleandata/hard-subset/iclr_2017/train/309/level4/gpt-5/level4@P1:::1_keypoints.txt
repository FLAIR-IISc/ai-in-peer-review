- Paper augments A3C with unsupervised auxiliary tasks (pixel/feature control, immediate reward prediction) trained off-policy via replay to shape shared representations and accelerate learning.
- UNREAL combines on-policy A3C updates with auxiliary control and reward tasks that share CNN/LSTM features.
- Evaluated on Labyrinth and Atari, UNREAL substantially outperforms A3C and learns much faster.
- Reviewer decision: Accept.
- Primary reasons: strong empirical gains over A3C across domains and a convincing proof-of-concept that auxiliary tasks improve representation learning.
- Problem addressed: learning robust representations under sparse/delayed extrinsic rewards by leveraging sensorimotor signals as auxiliary objectives.
- Motivation and prior work: well-grounded in literature (options, Horde, UVFAs, successor representations); novelty lies in using auxiliary tasks to shape shared features rather than their policies.
- Method instantiation is thoughtful (off-policy Q-learning for control tasks, replay with skewed sampling for reward prediction, shared architecture).
- Empirical rigor: extensive comparisons, ablations, multiple levels/games, seeds, and hyperparameter robustness; pixel/feature control shown more effective than reconstruction/input-change prediction.
- Reproducibility feedback: report computational resources, wall-clock training times, and engineering choices to aid replication.
- Open-source suggestion: release code or pseudo-code/reference implementations for auxiliary heads and replay pipelines.
- Clarity suggestion: include a concise training loop diagram detailing on-policy vs off-policy updates, update cadence, and replay buffer partitioning/sampling.
- Questions for authors: what hardware/number of actors/threads and training time are required, and will you release source code or a reference implementation?
- After rebuttal: reviewer’s decision unchanged.
