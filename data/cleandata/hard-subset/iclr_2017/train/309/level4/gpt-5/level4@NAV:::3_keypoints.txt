- Proposes training RL agents on auxiliary tasks to learn stronger features that complement the control policy.
- Introduces two pseudo-control tasks (controlling pixel intensity changes and latent feature activations) and a supervised regression task predicting immediate reward.
- Reward prediction is trained offline with biased sampling from replay to balance reward vs no-reward observations (~1/2).
- Agents achieve strong performance on discrete-action, continuous-space RL problems, reaching baseline in ~10× fewer iterations.
- Contrasts with passive unsupervised/model-based learning by leveraging the agent’s control to learn useful representations.
- Reviewer finds the approach novel and a compelling alternative to unsupervised representation learning.
- Auxiliary tasks are described at a high level for intuition, but lacking some low-level details (e.g., L_PC should be introduced before the appendix).
- Methodology appears sound; reporting top-3 hyperparameter results helps identify best settings across A3C and UNREAL.
- Key weakness: limited experimental analysis of how auxiliary tasks influence learning beyond overall performance.
- Pixel/feature control seems most influential (A3C+PC in Labyrinth outperforms others); merits deeper, isolated analysis with additional metrics.
