- Paper proposes augmenting policy learning with domain-agnostic auxiliary tasks (feature-control, immediate reward prediction, value-function replay) that share CNN+LSTM features.  
- Experiments on Atari and Labyrinth show improved sample efficiency vs A3C; reviewer views paper as a clear accept.  
- Please report computational overhead of optimizing auxiliary tasks: training speed loss and which components are most expensive.  
- Clarify in abstract/intro that the agent learns distinct policies for each auxiliary task (not a single policy maximizing all pseudo-rewards).  
- Feature-control idea lacks strong empirical support and may raise stability/convergence concerns because features (and thus tasks) evolve during learning.  
- If performance is still improving, run longer experiments for best agents to show longer-term behavior.  
- Treat auxiliary-task weights (lambda_*) as tunable hyperparameters or justify setting them to 1; report any tests.  
- Explicitly state auxiliary tasks are not trained with “true” Q-learning but off-policy using multi-step empirical returns.  
- Minor correction: policy gradient loss sign — L_pi = -... is a loss to be minimized.  
- Eq. 1: lambda_c should be inside the sum.  
- Just below Eq. 1: r_t^(c) should be r_{t+k}^{(c)}.  
- Figure 2 is not referenced; cite Figure 1(d) in Section 3.3.  
- Grammar: “the features discovered in this manner is shared” => “are shared.”  
- Eq. 2 references L_PC which is undefined and does not explicitly appear in Eq. 2.  
- Figure 3 legend: explain what “Clip” means for dueling networks.
