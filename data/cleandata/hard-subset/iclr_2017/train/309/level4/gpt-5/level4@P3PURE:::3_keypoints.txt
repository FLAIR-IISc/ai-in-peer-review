- Proposes training RL agents on auxiliary tasks to encourage learning stronger feature representations.  
- Introduces two pseudo-control tasks: controlling changes in pixel intensity and controlling activations of latent features.  
- Adds a supervised regression auxiliary that predicts immediate reward, trained offline with skewed sampling to balance reward/no-reward to 1/2.  
- Empirically, agents perform strongly on discrete-action, continuous-space tasks and reach baseline performance with roughly 10× fewer iterations.  
- Conceptually contrasts with passive unsupervised/model-based learning: controlling local/internal features complements optimal policy learning.  
- The approach is novel and offers an interesting alternative that leverages the agent’s ability to control the environment.  
- Clarity is generally good for Deep RL readers; auxiliary tasks are explained at a helpful high level.  
- Some lower-level details are missing (e.g., L_PC should be explicitly defined in the main text rather than deferred to the appendix).  
- Methodology appears sound; reporting top-3 hyperparameter results helps mitigate differences between A3C and UNREAL.  
- Key weakness: limited experimental analysis of the auxiliary tasks beyond aggregate downstream performance.  
- Pixel/feature control seems most influential (A3C+PC in Labyrinth outperforms most variants); calls for isolated/deeper study and additional measures beyond RL performance.
