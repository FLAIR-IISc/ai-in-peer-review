- Paper proposes auxiliary-domain-independent tasks (control, reward prediction, value replay) to shape shared CNN+LSTM features and shows improved data efficiency over A3C on Atari and Labyrinth.
- Overall writing, ideas, and results are strong; reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Please discuss additional computational cost of optimizing auxiliary tasks: training speed loss and dominant overhead components.
- Clarify in abstract/introduction that distinct policies are learned for each auxiliary control task (not a single policy).
- Feature-control auxiliary task lacks strong empirical validation; Fig.5 results are weak and may introduce stability/convergence issues as features evolve.
- Continue training longer for top agents, since performance is still improving, to report eventual performance.
- Explain why auxiliary-task weights (λ_*) were not treated as tunable hyperparameters and whether experiments justify setting them to 1.
- Note that auxiliary tasks are not trained with “true” Q-learning because they use off-policy data with multi-step targets.
- Minor: policy gradient loss sign—L_pi as written is a loss to be minimized, not maximized.
- Minor: In Eq.1, λ_c should appear inside the summation.
- Minor: Just below Eq.1, r_t^(c) should be r_{t+k}^(c).
- Minor: Figure 2 is not referenced in the text; Figure 1(d) should be referenced in Section 3.3.
- Minor: Grammar—“the features discovered in this manner is shared” should be “are shared.”
- Minor: Eq.2 references L_PC, but L_PC is neither defined nor included in Eq.2.
- Minor: Explain what “Clip” denotes for dueling networks in Figure 3 legend.
