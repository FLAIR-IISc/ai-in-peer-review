- Proposal: UNREAL augments A3C with auxiliary tasks (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction, value replay) that share a common representation and are trained off-policy via replay.

- Pixel control: learn policies to maximize local pixel-intensity changes over a spatial grid.

- Feature control: learn policies to maximize activations of internal network units as pseudo-rewards.

- Reward-focused auxiliaries: immediate reward prediction with skewed replay to oversample rare rewards, and value-function replay.

- Training regime: main policy/value learned on-policy (A3C); auxiliary tasks trained off-policy from replay; unified loss specified.

- Empirical gains: substantially faster learning (≈10× fewer steps on Labyrinth), higher final performance (≈87% human on Labyrinth, 880% human on Atari), and improved hyperparameter robustness.

- Ablations: auxiliary tasks—especially pixel and feature control—are the primary drivers of improvement over baseline A3C.

- Decision: Accept, based on novelty of control-based auxiliaries and strong, broad empirical evidence.

- Motivation: addresses sparse rewards by shaping representations through control signals instead of reconstruction or full environment modeling.

- Positioning: distinct from options/Horde/UVFA/successor reps because auxiliary policies shape features rather than act as temporally extended actions.

- Methodological soundness: coherent combination of on-policy and off-policy updates, entropy regularization, n-step Q-learning from replay, and skewed replay for reward prediction.

- Experimental breadth: evaluations across 13 Labyrinth levels and 57 Atari games, with ablations, robustness sweeps, and reasonable top-k reporting for fair comparison.

- Clarity and reproducibility: high-level intuition and architecture are clear; appendices provide implementation specifics.

- Recommend moving key definitions (auxiliary loss L_PC, pixel-control reward, deconvolutional head) from appendix into main text for reproducibility.

- Request deeper analysis: quantify per-task contributions and interactions, probe representation quality (linear probes, generalization/transfer), and provide sensitivity analyses (grid resolution, replay size, skew ratio, loss weights).

- Ask for computational-cost reporting: training time, memory overhead, and wall-clock vs. sample-efficiency tradeoffs relative to A3C.

- Concern about bias leakage: shared encoder for reward prediction may indirectly bias policy/value; suggest discussion or control experiment with separate encoder.

- Suggest investigating whether auxiliary policies aid exploration or can be composed into options for planning.

- Question: which auxiliary task consistently contributes most across Atari and Labyrinth—per-domain and per-game breakdown requested.

- Question: sensitivity of performance to reward-prediction replay skew (e.g., 50/50 split) and behavior under highly imbalanced rewards.

- Question: evidence that sharing the convolutional encoder with reward prediction does not introduce bias or instability; have separate-encoder tests been run?

- Question: how large is the computational overhead from auxiliary heads and replay relative to A3C; are improvements mainly sample-efficient or also wall-clock efficient?

- Question: robustness of pixel-control design choices (grid size, definition of “pixel change”) and their effect on results.

- Question: feature control performance on Atari and which network layer’s activations work best as pseudo-rewards.

- Question: can learned auxiliary policies be leveraged for exploration/options, and if tried, what were the outcomes?

- Question: applicability to continuous-action domains and any required methodological adaptations.
