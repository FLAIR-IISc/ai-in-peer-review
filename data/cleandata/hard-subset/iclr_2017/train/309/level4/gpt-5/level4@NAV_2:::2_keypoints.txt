- Proposes augmenting policy optimization with domain-agnostic auxiliary objectives (pixel-control, feature-control, reward prediction, value-function replay) to shape shared CNN+LSTM representations.  
- Empirical results on Atari and DeepMind Lab show strong gains, especially in data efficiency relative to A3C; paper is well written and recommended for acceptance.  

Main concerns:
- Quantify additional computational overhead from training auxiliary tasks: wall-clock slowdown and dominant cost components.  
- Clarify in abstract/introduction that distinct policies are learned for each auxiliary control task (phrase initially ambiguous until Eq. 1).  
- Feature-control is under-validated: only slight early boost shown, and nonstationary evolving targets may raise stability/convergence concerns.  
- Run top agents longer to characterize asymptotic performance since learning was still improving.  
- Treat auxiliary loss weights (λ_*) as tunable hyperparameters or justify fixing them to 1.  
- Auxiliary control updates are not “true” Q-learning: they are off-policy with multi-step targets—needs technical accuracy.
