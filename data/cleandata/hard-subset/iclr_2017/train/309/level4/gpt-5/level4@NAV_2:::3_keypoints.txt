- Summary: Proposes an RL agent that jointly learns the main task plus auxiliary pseudo-reward control (pixel-intensity and latent feature control) and reward-prediction tasks using off-policy n-step Q-learning from a shared representation and skewed replay for efficiency.
- Key mechanism: Uses experience replay to efficiently update many auxiliary tasks and focuses auxiliaries on controllable sensorimotor aspects instead of passive reconstruction/prediction.
- Empirical claim: Achieves strong gains on Labyrinth and Atari, reaching baseline performance in roughly 10× fewer iterations, with pixel/feature control driving much of the benefit.

- Decision: Accept — method is compelling, well-motivated, and shows large, consistent empirical improvements and data efficiency.

- Novelty/motivation: Framing auxiliaries as controllable pseudo-rewards (pixel/feature control) is a clear conceptual advance over reconstruction/short-horizon prediction, aligning representations with agent influence.
- Methodological soundness: Off-policy auxiliary updates from a single experience stream and replay-based rebalancing for reward prediction are technically sensible and designed not to bias policy/value learning.
- Empirical strength: Consistent speedups and higher final performance across tasks; pixel/feature control particularly impactful (A3C+pixel-control performs very well).
- Clarity: High-level exposition is clear and intuitive for deep RL readers.

- Reproducibility request: Add low-level definitions (e.g., explicit L_PC, targets, normalization) in the main text rather than only in the appendix.
- Deeper analysis: Isolate pixel vs. feature control effects, track auxiliary losses vs. returns, analyze representations (similarity/saliency), and report success/failure modes.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Report robustness to λ_PC, λ_RP, replay buffer size, and skew ratio to show stability of gains.
- Explain pixel-control impact: Discuss why pixel/feature control yields large benefits (exploration, exposure to reward, credit assignment, etc.).
- Clarify metrics: Define “iterations/steps” precisely (environment frames vs. learner updates across actors) to make data-efficiency claims unambiguous.

- Question — L_PC details: Please define L_PC precisely (targets, bootstrapping, dueling head, per-cell reward normalization) and justify grid/crop choices and sensitivity.
- Question — reward-prediction skewing: Provide evidence that replay rebalancing for reward prediction does not bias learned policy/value (ablation with/without rebalancing).
- Question — sensitivity/robustness: How sensitive are results to λ_PC, λ_RP, and replay buffer size; does value-function replay alone explain gains?
- Question — auxiliary policies usage: Are pseudo-control policies ever executed for exploration, or only training signals; does executing them improve sample efficiency?
- Question — attribution in Labyrinth: Share qualitative behavioral differences (path coverage, object interaction) explaining why A3C+pixel-control is so effective.
- Question — data-efficiency metric: Clarify whether the ~10× reduction refers to aggregated environment interaction steps or learner updates and how normalization across methods was performed.
