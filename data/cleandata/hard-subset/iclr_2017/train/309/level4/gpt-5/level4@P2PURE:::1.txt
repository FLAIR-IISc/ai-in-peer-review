This paper introduces a method for augmenting a deep RL agent such as A3C with unsupervised auxiliary tasks. The authors present a set of auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward-related tasks, and evaluate the approach on Labyrinth and Atari. The proposed UNREAL agent performs significantly better than A3C and also learns faster. This is a solid contribution to the conference. However, the results are not particularly surprising: adding goal-relevant auxiliary tasks should generally lead to better and faster representation shaping. This paper primarily serves as a proof of concept for that idea.
The paper is well written and easy to follow for readers with deep RL expertise.
Could the authors comment on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent?
The overall architecture is fairly complex. Are the authors willing to release the source code for their model?
