- Proposal: UNREAL augments A3C with auxiliary tasks (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction, value replay) that share convolutional and recurrent representations.  
- Training: Auxiliary control tasks use off-policy n-step Q-learning from replay; reward prediction is trained on a skewed replay to de-sparsify signals.  
- Shared representation: All tasks share CNN/LSTM so learning continues without extrinsic reward and features focus on reward-relevant aspects.  
- Empirical claim: Substantial gains in final performance, learning speed, and hyperparameter robustness on 13 Labyrinth levels and 57 Atari games.  
- Ablations: Control-oriented auxiliary tasks outperform prediction/reconstruction; hierarchy observed: control > prediction > reconstruction.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main reasons for accept: Strong, consistent empirical improvements and well-motivated, carefully integrated auxiliary objectives addressing sparse rewards.  
- Positioning: Distinct from Horde/UVFAs by using pseudo-rewards to shape representation rather than for planning/control.  
- Methodological soundness: Combining on-policy A3C with off-policy Q-learning plus replay is technically sensible; architectures and heads are clearly described.  
- Empirical specifics: Labyrinth shows ~10× speedup and large human-normalized gains (~87%); Atari shows improvements over A3C and Prioritized Dueling DQN.  
- Rigor: Multiple baselines, ablations, hyperparameter sweeps, and documentation of replay/schedules/loss weights provided.  
- Novelty caveat: Conceptually incremental—integrates known components; main contribution is an engineered, empirically validated instantiation.  
- Complexity caveat: Many components (aux heads, replay, loss weights) may hinder reproducibility and attribution of gains.  
- Metric caveat: Human-normalized means can be skewed by outliers; clearer wall-clock and compute reporting would strengthen data-efficiency claims.  
- Suggested improvements: Report compute budgets, wall-clock times, and overhead; release code and detailed hyperparameters; provide sensitivity analyses for key hyperparameters and grid sizes.  
- Evaluation clarity: Clarify Atari and Labyrinth evaluation protocols (episodes, life-loss handling, no-op starts, termination conditions).  
- Additional experiments: Add transfer/generalization tests and analyses of learned representations (saliency, feature-control visualizations).  
- Stability concerns: Discuss mixing on-policy A3C with off-policy Q-learning, replay freshness, and use of target networks.  
- Resource questions: Report memory footprint of replay buffers and auxiliary heads and scalability limits.  
- Author questions: Request hardware/runtime, sensitivity sweeps for λ and grid size, off-policy instability observations, reward-prediction bias checks, feature vs pixel control performance, use of auxiliary policies for exploration, and memory costs.  
- Post-rebuttal: Review unchanged after rebuttal.
