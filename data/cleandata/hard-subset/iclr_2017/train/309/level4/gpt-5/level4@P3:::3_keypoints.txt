- Introduces UNREAL: augment A3C with auxiliary tasks (pixel control and feature control) to shape representations and speed learning.  
- Auxiliary control tasks: learn policies/value functions for pseudo-rewards from the agent’s sensorimotor stream (pixel-intensity change and internal-feature activation).  
- Reward-focused auxiliaries: immediate reward prediction with skewed replay and value replay to reduce reward sparsity and improve value estimates.  
- Joint training: combine on-policy A3C for the base task with off-policy n-step Q-learning for auxiliary control using a shared CNN-LSTM.  
- Empirical gains: Labyrinth shows ~10× speedup and 87% human-normalized performance; Atari-57 shows 880% mean and 250% median human-normalized performance.  
- Robustness: improved robustness to hyperparameters and extensive ablations indicate consistent benefits.  
- Ablations: each auxiliary component contributes, with pixel/feature control particularly impactful.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Primary rationale: reframes “unsupervised RL” as control-oriented representation learning and demonstrates broad empirical improvements.  
- Motivation: addresses sparse extrinsic rewards by learning to control perceptually/internal meaningful signals rather than passive reconstruction.  
- Positioning: related to Horde/UVFA/successor representations but novel in using auxiliary policies for representation shaping rather than behavior.  
- Methodology appraisal: combined loss and shared representation are sensible; off-policy auxiliaries improve data efficiency and decouple control from behavior.  
- Empirical rigor: strong, consistent results across domains with reasonable baselines, ablations, and robustness analyses.  
- Reproducibility concerns: some implementation specifics are in the appendix and should be moved/clarified in the main text.  
- Suggestion: define pixel/feature control rewards and auxiliary Q-loss (LPC) mathematically in the main text with deconvolution head details.  
- Suggestion: clarify off-policy stability measures (target networks, update schedules, double Q or bias-reduction) and explain why importance sampling isn’t used.  
- Suggestion: provide replay details (buffer size rationale, sampling ratios for value/control/reward prediction) and sensitivity analyses.  
- Suggestion: include analyses of learned representations (visualizations, saliency, unit selectivity, temporal credit assignment).  
- Suggestion: more systematic comparisons of pixel vs. feature control across tasks and report interactions (additive vs. redundant).  
- Suggestion: discuss/apply to continuous control or non-visual modalities to broaden impact.  
- Suggestion: report exact training frames, wall-clock compute, number of seeds, and variance bars for key curves.  
- Questions for authors (summarized): normalization and scaling of pixel-change rewards; best layers/features for feature control and multi-layer control; use of target networks and off-policy stability; role/need for importance sampling given skewed replay; replay buffer size sensitivity; per-task Atari ablations; effects of reward-prediction skew in dense rewards; transfer/generalization of learned features; applicability to continuous-control domains; provide compute/hyperparameters/code for reproducibility.
