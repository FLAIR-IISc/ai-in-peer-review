- Introduces method to augment deep RL agents (e.g., A3C) with unsupervised auxiliary tasks.
- Proposes multiple auxiliary control and auxiliary reward tasks; evaluates on Labyrinth and Atari.
- UNREAL outperforms A3C and learns faster.
- Strong contribution, though not surprising—adds evidence that goal-relevant auxiliary tasks improve feature shaping.
- Serves as a proof of concept for using auxiliary tasks to speed and improve learning.
- Paper is clearly written and easy to follow for readers with deep RL expertise.
- Reviewer asks for details on computational resources required to train UNREAL.
- Reviewer notes the architecture is fairly complex and asks if the authors will release source code.
- After rebuttal: reviewer made no change to their evaluation.
