- Paper proposes UNREAL: A3C augmented with auxiliary tasks (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction, value replay) sharing CNN/LSTM to improve representation learning.
- Auxiliary control tasks use off-policy n-step Q-learning on pseudo-rewards (pixel/feature control); reward prediction head uses replay biased toward rare rewards; value replay adds extra value regression.
- Empirical results: large gains in Labyrinth (mean ~10× speedup) and Atari (880% mean human-normalized), with improved robustness to hyperparameters.
- Decision: Accept.
- Primary reason 1: Strong, consistent empirical improvements with careful ablations on Labyrinth and meaningful Atari comparisons.
- Primary reason 2: Well-motivated, literature-grounded approach unifying auxiliary control and reward prediction with on-policy A3C, yielding clear data-efficiency benefits.
- Problem addressed: Learning useful representations when extrinsic rewards are sparse by leveraging control- and reward-aligned auxiliary signals.
- Positioning/novelty: Connects to options/Horde/UVFA/successor reps; novelty in many off-policy control tasks + biased reward prediction while keeping main policy on-policy and recurrent.
- Results support claims: Labyrinth ablations show contributions from pixel control, reward prediction, and value replay; Atari shows strong average improvements and robustness.
- Caveat: Auxiliary Q-learning is off-policy with n-step returns, which may introduce bias and should be explicitly acknowledged and discussed.
- Suggestion: Quantify extra compute and memory (wall-clock slowdown per env step) and which auxiliary components dominate cost.
- Suggestion: Clarify early that separate policies/values are learned per auxiliary control task to avoid confusion.
- Suggestion: Discuss stability of feature control given moving targets; report any stabilizing techniques and expand empirical validation beyond preliminary results.
- Suggestion: Continue training top agents to asymptote to verify persistence of relative gains.
- Suggestion: Explain why auxiliary loss weights (λVR, λPC, λRP) were fixed rather than tuned; provide sensitivity analysis and ranges used per domain.
- Suggestion: Explicitly note off-policy multi-step bootstrapping without importance sampling for auxiliary control tasks and discuss bias/variance trade-offs.
- Suggestion: Add Atari ablations (remove pixel control / reward prediction / value replay) to assess component contributions on Atari.
- Suggestion: Report replay buffer sizes, sampling ratios per auxiliary head, and any prioritization beyond reward-balancing.
- Editorial: Clarify sign/conventions for policy gradient loss and fix Equation (1) notation for λC and rewards.
- Editorial: Fix subscripts/superscripts below Eq. (1), define LPC explicitly in Eq. (2), and correct notation/grammar issues (e.g., "are shared").
- Editorial: Clarify "Clip" in Figure 3 legend, correct multiple figure references and legends (Figure 2, Fig 1(d), Fig 3 positions), and reconcile Figure 5 caption with plotted panels.
- Editorial: Add punctuation in Section 4.2 sentence and provide pointer or remove claim about supplementary materials.
- Editorial: Report default/values of λPC per domain in the paper.
- Questions for authors: What are wall-clock training time and throughput relative to A3C for Labyrinth and Atari, and how do compute costs scale with number of auxiliary tasks?
- Questions for authors: How sensitive are results to λVR, λPC, λRP; were per-task weights or normalization methods explored?
- Questions for authors: For feature control, were target-network slowing, layer normalization, or other stabilization methods tried; is there evidence of instability?
- Questions for authors: What are replay buffer sizes, update frequencies per env step, sampling ratios, and was prioritized replay beyond reward-balancing tested?
- Questions for authors: How does performance vary with pixel-control grid resolution and normalization of pixel-change magnitudes across Atari games?
- Questions for authors: Were importance sampling or Retrace-style corrections for off-policy n-step Q-learning tried, and with what effect?
- Questions for authors: If training is extended further, do UNREAL’s gains persist and what is the asymptotic gap versus A3C?
