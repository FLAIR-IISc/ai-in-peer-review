- Proposes augmenting RL agents with auxiliary tasks to learn more robust representations.
- Introduces two pseudo-control objectives: pixel-control (changes in pixel intensity) and feature-control (activations of latent features).
- Adds a supervised reward-prediction head trained offline from replay with skewed sampling to balance reward/no-reward ~50/50.
- Achieves strong results on discrete-action, continuous-state tasks and matches baseline performance in roughly one-tenth the iterations.
- Contrasts with passive unsupervised or full model-based objectives by focusing on controllability of local and internal features.
- The controllability-focused idea is novel and complements the main control objective under partial observability.
- Presentation is overall clear but omits some concrete details needed for reproducibility (e.g., pixel-control loss left in appendix).
- Experimental methodology is sound; using top-3 performance across hyperparameter settings helps mitigate selection bias if ranges include near-optimal settings.
- Key limitation: limited analysis of each auxiliary task’s individual contribution beyond headline returns.
- Evidence suggests pixel/feature control drive most gains (e.g., pixel control alone outperforms alternatives in Labyrinth).
- Recommends deeper ablations and diagnostics (isolate tasks, examine interactions, measure representation effects, feature diversity, stability, data efficiency).
- Overall assessment: well-executed, innovative, and practically beneficial; would be stronger with clearer task definitions and richer auxiliary-task analysis.
