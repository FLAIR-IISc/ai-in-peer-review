Summary
The paper proposes improving representation learning in deep reinforcement learning by augmenting a main policy’s objective with domain-agnostic auxiliary tasks. These include control tasks (learning separate policies to maximize changes in perceptual features/pixels), reward prediction, and value function replay. Except for value replay, these tasks serve solely to shape the shared CNN+LSTM features used by the main policy. Experiments on Atari and DeepMind Lab show substantial gains in data efficiency and overall performance over A3C and related baselines. The paper is clearly written, technically sound, and empirically convincing. I recommend acceptance.

Strengths
- Clear, well-motivated idea: leveraging auxiliary control and prediction tasks to enrich shared features.
- Strong empirical results on two challenging domains, with notable data-efficiency improvements.
- Careful engineering enables stable joint training of policy and auxiliary heads.
- The approach is conceptually simple and broadly applicable.

Concerns and suggestions for clarification
- Computational cost and throughput. Please quantify the extra wall-clock and compute cost of the auxiliary tasks relative to a comparable A3C baseline. Which components dominate cost (e.g., auxiliary control heads, replayed updates, reward prediction)? Report frames-per-second and total training time to achieve key score thresholds, so readers can assess the cost–benefit tradeoff.
- Clarify multiple policies in abstract/introduction. The phrasing “maximises many other pseudo-reward functions simultaneously” suggests a single policy optimized for a combined reward. Please state upfront that separate policies are learned for the auxiliary control tasks, sharing a common feature encoder with the main policy.
- Validation of feature-control. The empirical evidence for feature-control benefits is limited; Fig. 5 suggests only a small initial gain. Given the non-stationarity (targets depend on evolving features), discuss potential stability issues and any mitigation (e.g., target networks, lagged features, regularization). If available, add stronger ablations or diagnostics (e.g., effect on representation diversity, gradient norms, or training variance).
- Asymptotic performance. Since learning curves were still improving at the training cutoff, consider extending training for the best agents to show final asymptotic performance and whether gains persist.
- Auxiliary task weights. The choice to set all λ parameters to 1 seems strong. Were alternatives tried? Please discuss sensitivity and whether a small hyperparameter search for λ values changes conclusions or improves stability.
- Off-policy Q-learning details. Please explicitly note that auxiliary control tasks are not trained with “true” Q-learning; updates are off-policy and use multi-step returns. Summarize any practical implications (bias, stability) and mitigations.
- Some of these points were discussed in the online comments; it would help to incorporate the clarifications into the paper so readers do not need to consult the discussion.

Minor comments and nits
- Terminology: “Policy gradient algorithms adjust the policy to maximise the expected reward, L_pi = - …” The expression shown is a loss minimized during training. Consider rephrasing to avoid confusion.
- Eq. 1: λ_c should be inside the sum over control tasks. Also, the reward term just below Eq. 1 should be r_{t+k}^{(c)}, not r_t^{(c)}.
- Around Eq. 2: The text references L_PC, but this loss is not defined and does not explicitly appear in Eq. 2. Please define it and ensure consistent notation.
- Grammar: “the features discovered in this manner is shared” → “are shared.”
- Figures and references:
  - Figure 2 is not referenced in the text; please add a reference. Also, Figure 1(d) should be cited in Section 3.3.
  - Figure 3: clarify in the legend that the reported percentages refer to Labyrinth. Explain what “Clip” means for dueling networks. The caption/text references are inconsistent: “Figure 3 (right)” appears to refer to the top-left panel; later “Figure 3 Top” should be “Figure 3 Top Right.”
  - Figure 5: the sentence “learning curves for the top 5 hyperparameter settings on three Labyrinth navigation levels” appears to refer only to the left and middle plots (two levels). Please correct the text and ensure all panels are described.
- Section 4.1.2: “Figure 3 (right) shows …” should reference the correct subpanel. Also fix “This is shown in Figure 3 Top” to “Figure 3, top right.”
- Section 4.2: “The left side shows the average performance curves of the top 5 agents for all three methods the right half shows …” Add a comma or revise for clarity.
- Ablations: It would be valuable to include more Atari ablations mirroring the Labyrinth analysis, to see if the same pattern of auxiliary task contributions holds.
- Appendix: “Further details are included in the supplementary materials.” Please provide a pointer or include them in the appendix.
- Hyperparameters: Please state the value of λ_PC (presumably 1) and any tuning done.

Overall assessment
This is a strong paper: the idea is elegant and general, the empirical results are compelling, and the writing is clear. Addressing the points above—especially computational cost, clearer exposition on multiple policies, and stronger validation or discussion of feature-control—would further strengthen the contribution. I recommend acceptance.
