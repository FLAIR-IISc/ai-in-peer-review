- Proposes augmenting deep RL agents (e.g., A3C) with unsupervised auxiliary tasks (UNREAL).
- Introduces a suite of auxiliary control and reward-prediction tasks.
- Evaluates the method on DeepMind Labyrinth and Atari benchmarks.
- Reports that UNREAL significantly outperforms the A3C baseline and learns faster.
- Considers the work a solid contribution to the conference.
- Notes the primary result is unsurprising—auxiliary tasks are known to aid feature learning; this serves as a proof of concept in deep RL.
- Paper is well-written and accessible to experts in deep reinforcement learning.
- Requests clarification on computational resources required to train UNREAL.
- Asks whether the authors will release source code to facilitate reproducibility.
