- Introduces training RL agents with auxiliary tasks to encourage more robust feature representations.
- Proposes two pseudo-control tasks: maximizing pixel intensity changes and controlling hidden-layer activations.
- Adds a supervised regression task to predict the immediate upcoming reward.
- Trains the reward predictor offline with biased sampling from replay to balance positive and zero rewards 1:1.
- Agents achieve substantial gains on discrete-action, continuous-state RL tasks, matching baselines with ~10× fewer training iterations.
- Contrasts with passive unsupervised or model-based approaches by leveraging the agent’s control to learn useful representations.
- Approach is novel and presents a compelling alternative to standard unsupervised learning techniques.
- Auxiliary tasks are described conceptually and convey intuition well, but low-level details (e.g., explicit pixel control loss L_PC) should appear in the main text, not just the appendix.
- Paper is well-written and accessible to readers familiar with deep RL.
- Experimental methodology is robust; using top-3 performing runs helps identify good hyperparameters for each method within the search range.
- Weakness: limited analysis into why the auxiliary tasks are effective beyond improved final performance.
- Pixel and feature control tasks appear to drive most gains—pixel control alone (A3C+PC) nearly matches UNREAL in Labyrinth—warranting deeper, focused investigation and additional metrics.
