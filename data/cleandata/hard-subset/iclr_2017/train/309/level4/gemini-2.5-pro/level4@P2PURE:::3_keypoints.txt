- Proposal: train RL agents with auxiliary tasks to learn more effective feature representations.
- Methods: introduce two pseudo-control tasks (control pixel-intensity change and control latent-feature activations) plus a supervised reward-prediction task.
- Reward prediction is trained offline with skewed replay sampling to enforce a 1:1 balance of rewarding vs non-rewarding events.
- Result: UNREAL achieves significantly improved performance, reaching baseline levels with roughly 10× better data efficiency on discrete-action, continuous-state tasks.
- Key insight: move away from passive auxiliary objectives toward active control of local/internal features, leveraging the agent’s ability to exert control.
- Novelty and impact: the active-control auxiliary objectives are a compelling alternative to unsupervised or model-based objectives, especially under partial observability.
- Clarity: paper is generally clear for DRL readers but omits some low-level details (e.g., the pixel-control loss L_PC should be defined in the main text).
- Experimental methodology: sound; averaging the top-3 runs helps ensure fair comparison and likely finds good hyperparameters for each method.
- Main weakness: limited analysis of why the auxiliary tasks are effective beyond their impact on final performance.
- Important observation: A3C+PixelControl nearly matches UNREAL on Labyrinth, suggesting control-based tasks are the primary drivers of improvement.
- Recommendation: deeper analysis of learned representations and additional metrics (beyond task performance) is needed to explain the auxiliary tasks’ benefits.
