- Presents a method that improves representation learning in deep RL by training auxiliary control, reward-prediction, and value-replay tasks alongside the main extrinsic reward objective.
- Uses a shared CNN-LSTM feature extractor trained by these tasks, showing stronger, more general representations.
- Strong empirical results on Atari and Labyrinth, with significant gains in final performance and data efficiency over A3C.
- Recommendation: Accept — ideas are well-motivated, technically sound, clearly written, and experimentally convincing.
- Strength: Novel combination of auxiliary tasks with compelling core idea of using control as an unsupervised objective.
- Strength: Large empirical improvements (e.g., ~10x speedup, 87% human performance on Labyrinth) and informative ablation studies.
- Major: Discuss computational cost — impact on wall-clock training time vs A3C and which auxiliary components are most expensive.
- Major: Clarify abstract/introduction to state the agent learns separate policies for each auxiliary task (avoid implying a single multi-objective policy).
- Major: Feature-control task appears preliminary with only minor benefit and may introduce non-stationarity and stability concerns — authors should comment.
- Major: Report longer training runs to better characterize asymptotic performance since agents are still improving.
- Major: Explain choice/validation of auxiliary loss weights (λ_*) — were they set to 1 for principled or empirical reasons?
- Major: Clarify that auxiliary tasks are trained off-policy using n-step returns (a variant of single-step Q-learning).
- Minor: Background formulates policy gradient as a reward to be maximized rather than a loss to be minimized.
- Minor: In Equation 1, λ_C placement and the return notation r_{t+k}^{(c)} should be fixed.
- Minor: L_PC is mentioned but missing from the combined loss function.
- Minor: Figures 2 and 1(d) are not explicitly referenced in the main text.
- Minor: Grammar: "the features discovered... is shared" should be "are shared."
- Minor: Figure 3 legend should explain "Clip" and clarify percentages are for Labyrinth.
- Minor: Incorrect plot references in Section 4.1.1 (e.g., "Figure 3 (right)" and "Figure 3 Top" misreferenced).
- Minor: Figure 5 description claims three Labyrinth levels but appears to show only two.
- Minor: Atari experiments need a more detailed ablation study to test consistency of each auxiliary task’s contribution across domains.
