This paper proposes improving feature learning in deep reinforcement learning by augmenting the main policy's optimization objective with auxiliary, domain-independent tasks. These tasks involve learning separate control policies to maximize change in the pixel space, predicting immediate rewards, and replaying value function estimates. While the auxiliary policies are discarded after training, their gradients help shape a shared feature representation (a CNN+LSTM network), leading to more robust learning. The experiments on Atari and Labyrinth domains compellingly demonstrate the benefits of this approach, particularly in terms of data efficiency compared to the A3C baseline.

The paper is well-written, the ideas are sound, and the results are convincing. I recommend this paper for acceptance. I have a few suggestions and questions for the authors, none of which are major concerns.

### Major Comments

1.  **Computational Cost:** The use of auxiliary tasks inevitably introduces computational overhead. Could the authors comment on the additional cost? Specifically, how much does this approach slow down wall-clock training time compared to the baseline, and which auxiliary components are the most computationally expensive?
2.  **Clarity on Multiple Policies:** The abstract states that the agent "also maximises many other pseudo-reward functions simultaneously by reinforcement learning." This could be misinterpreted as learning a single policy for a multi-objective reward function. It only becomes clear in Equation 1 that the agent learns separate policies for each auxiliary task. I suggest clarifying this distinction earlier in the paper, perhaps in the abstract or introduction, to avoid potential confusion.
3.  **Validation of "Feature Control":** The empirical validation for the pixel control auxiliary task appears weak. The preliminary experiment in Figure 5 suggests only a slight initial benefit, making its contribution unclear. Furthermore, I have a conceptual concern about this task's stability: since the policy is trying to control features that are themselves being modified during training, the task is non-stationary. Could the authors comment on whether this non-stationarity poses convergence or stability challenges?
4.  **Training Duration and Final Performance:** The authors note that "the performance of our agents is still steadily improving" at the end of training. This suggests that the reported scores may not represent the agent's full potential. Have the authors considered running the experiments for longer to see the final converged performance, at least for the best-performing models?
5.  **Auxiliary Task Weights (lambda_*):** The weights for the auxiliary losses are set to 1. Were these values chosen arbitrarily, or were they validated experimentally? It seems they could be treated as important hyperparameters, and some discussion on their sensitivity would be valuable.
6.  **Off-Policy Auxiliary Learning:** The auxiliary control and value prediction tasks are trained off-policy using multi-step returns. For technical precision, it would be beneficial to explicitly mention that this is a departure from standard one-step Q-learning.

### Minor Comments and Typos

*   **Ablation Studies:** It would be insightful to see an ablation study on Atari similar to the one performed for Labyrinth (Figure 3, top left), to verify if the individual contributions of the auxiliary tasks follow the same pattern across different domains.
*   **Policy Gradient Loss:** In the introduction to policy gradients, the text states: "Policy gradient algorithms adjust the policy to maximise the expected reward, L_pi = -...". The equation shown is a loss to be minimized, not a quantity to be maximized. Please clarify the wording.
*   **Equation 1:** The weight `lambda_c` should be inside the summation over the control tasks.
*   **Text below Equation 1:** `r_t^(c)` should be `r_{t+k}^(c)` to match the n-step return formulation.
*   **Equation 2:** The text preceding the equation refers to a loss `L_PC`, but this term is not defined or explicitly present in the equation itself.
*   **Figure References:** Figure 2 does not appear to be referenced in the main text. Also, Figure 1(d) should be referenced in Section 3.3 (Value Function Replay).
*   **Figure 3 Legend:**
    *   The term "Clip" for the dueling network baseline is not defined. Please explain what this refers to.
    *   The percentages reported (e.g., "A3C 41%") appear to refer to the Labyrinth results, but this is not explicitly stated. Please clarify.
*   **Figure 3 Text Reference:** In Section 4.1.2, "Figure 3 (right)" appears to refer to the top-left plot. Later, "This is shown in Figure 3 Top" should probably be "Figure 3 Top Right."
*   **Figure 5 Text Reference:** In Section 4.1.2, the text states "Figure 5 shows the learning curves for the top 5 hyperparameter settings on three Labyrinth navigation levels." However, the figure seems to show results for only two levels (the left and middle plots).
*   **Grammar:**
    *   "...the features discovered in this manner is shared..." should be "...are shared."
    *   In Section 4.2, the sentence "The left side shows the average performance curves of the top 5 agents for all three methods the right half shows..." is missing punctuation. A comma is needed after "methods".
*   **Hyperparameter Value:** What is the value of the weight `lambda_PC` for the pixel control loss? I assume it is 1, but it should be stated.
*   **Appendix:** The appendix states, "Further details are included in the supplementary materials." It is unclear where these materials can be found.
