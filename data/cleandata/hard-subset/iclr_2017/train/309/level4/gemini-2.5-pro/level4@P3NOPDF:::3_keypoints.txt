- Proposes auxiliary-task-driven RL (UNREAL) where self-supervised pseudo-control tasks improve representation learning and primary policy performance.
- Introduces Pixel Control (maximize pixel-intensity change) and Feature Control (control latent activations) as novel self-supervised tasks.
- Adds a supervised Reward Prediction task learned from a replay buffer with skewed sampling to rebalance rewarding vs non-rewarding experiences.
- Integrates auxiliary tasks with A3C to form UNREAL, achieving state-of-the-art results on DeepMind Lab and reaching baseline performance up to 10× faster.
- Core contribution: using the agent’s ability to exert control as a supervisory signal is a novel, elegant alternative to passive unsupervised methods.
- Empirical strengths: significant improvements in sample efficiency and final performance, backed by strong experimental results.
- Experimental methodology is sound, with fair comparisons and hyperparameter searches for both A3C and UNREAL and top-3 run reporting.
- Weakness: auxiliary tasks are described at a high level—concrete definitions (e.g., Pixel Control loss) are relegated to the appendix, reducing self-contained clarity.
- Weakness: limited analysis of why/how auxiliary tasks help; ablations do not fully disentangle individual contributions or reveal what representations are learned.
- Suggestion: deeper investigation into learned representations and isolated evaluation of auxiliary policies would strengthen the paper.
- Conclusion: overall a strong, novel contribution to deep RL with convincing empirical gains; recommend accept pending deeper mechanistic analysis.
