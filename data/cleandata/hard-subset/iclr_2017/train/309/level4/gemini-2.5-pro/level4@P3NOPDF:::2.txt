### **Summary**

This paper proposes a method to improve feature learning and data efficiency in deep reinforcement learning by augmenting the standard A3C agent with several auxiliary tasks. These unsupervised, domain-independent tasks are jointly trained with the main policy objective and include: (1) auxiliary control tasks, where separate policies learn to maximize change in the pixel space; (2) reward prediction from a sequence of recent frames; and (3) value function replay, where the agent re-trains on high-value states. These auxiliary tasks share the underlying convolutional and recurrent network layers with the main policy, thereby shaping the learned representations to be more informative. The experiments, conducted on Atari and the 3D Labyrinth environment, demonstrate that this approach (termed UNREAL) achieves significantly better data efficiency and performance compared to the A3C baseline.

### **Overall Assessment and Recommendation**

The paper is well-written, the proposed ideas are sound and well-motivated, and the experimental results are quite convincing. The UNREAL agent's ability to learn much more quickly than A3C represents a significant step forward in sample efficiency for deep RL agents. For these reasons, I believe this is a strong paper and recommend a clear acceptance. My comments below are intended to help improve the final version of the manuscript.

### **Major Comments**

1.  **Computational Overhead:** The introduction of multiple auxiliary tasks presumably increases the computational cost of training. The paper would benefit from a discussion on this overhead. How much is the training speed (in terms of wall-clock time or frames per second) affected compared to the A3C baseline? It would also be insightful to know which of the auxiliary components are the most computationally expensive.

2.  **Clarity on Multiple Policies:** To improve clarity, especially in the abstract and introduction, it would be helpful to explicitly state that the agent learns distinct policies for each auxiliary control task. The abstract's phrasing that the agent "also maximises many other pseudo-reward functions simultaneously by reinforcement learning" could be misinterpreted as learning a single policy to optimize a weighted sum of all rewards. I only realized this was not the case upon reaching Equation 1. A brief clarification early on would prevent this potential confusion.

3.  **Validation of Feature Control:** The empirical validation for the "feature control" auxiliary task appears somewhat limited. The experiment in Figure 5 shows only a marginal benefit, and primarily during the initial phase of training. I find the idea compelling, but I am concerned about the non-stationarity of the task, as the features being controlled are themselves changing during training. This could introduce potential stability or convergence issues. A more in-depth discussion of this dynamic, or a more thorough ablation, would strengthen the paper's claims regarding this component.

4.  **Experiment Duration:** The authors note that "the performance of our agents is still steadily improving" at the end of training. Given this, it would be interesting to see how far the top-performing agents could go if training were extended. This could provide valuable insight into the asymptotic performance of the UNREAL agent and how it compares to state-of-the-art methods when granted a larger training budget.

5.  **Auxiliary Task Weighting:** The weights for the auxiliary tasks (λ_*) are set to 1. It would be valuable to clarify whether these were considered hyperparameters to be tuned. A brief discussion on the sensitivity of the agent's performance to these weights, or the justification for fixing them to unity, would be an informative addition.

6.  **Off-Policy Auxiliary Learning:** As has been discussed in the comments, the auxiliary tasks are trained off-policy using multi-step returns. To ensure full methodological clarity, it would be beneficial to explicitly state in the main text that this approach deviates from standard one-step Q-learning.

### **Minor Comments and Typos**

*   In the description of policy gradients: "Policy gradient algorithms adjust the policy to maximise the expected reward, L_pi = -..." This is technically a loss to be minimized.
*   In Equation 1, the weight λ_c should be inside the summation over the control tasks.
*   Just below Equation 1, r_t^(c) should likely be r_{t+k}^(c) to align with the n-step return formulation.
*   Figure 2 does not appear to be referenced in the main text. Additionally, Figure 1(d) should be referenced in Section 3.3 (Value Function Replay).
*   Grammar: "the features discovered in this manner is shared" should be "are shared".
*   The text around Equation 2 refers to the loss L_PC, but this term is not explicitly defined in or alongside the equation.
*   In the legend for Figure 3, please clarify the meaning of "Clip" in the context of dueling networks.
*   The paper would be strengthened by including more extensive ablation studies on the Atari suite to see if the same patterns of individual task contributions observed on Labyrinth hold.
*   In the Figure 3 legend, please clarify that the performance percentages shown correspond to the Labyrinth experiments.
*   In Section 4.1.2: "Figure 3 (right) shows..." refers to the top-left plot of the figure. Later, "This is shown in Figure 3 Top" should probably be Figure 3 Top Right.
*   In Section 4.2: "Figure 5 shows the learning curves for the top 5 hyperparameter settings on three Labyrinth navigation levels." This appears to refer to the left and middle plots, which cover only two levels. The corresponding text may also need adjustment.
*   In Section 4.2: "The left side shows the average performance curves of the top 5 agents for all three methods the right half shows..." This sentence is missing punctuation after "methods".
*   Appendix: "Further details are included in the supplementary materials." Please clarify where these materials can be found.
*   What is the value of λ_PC? It is presumably 1, but this should be stated.
