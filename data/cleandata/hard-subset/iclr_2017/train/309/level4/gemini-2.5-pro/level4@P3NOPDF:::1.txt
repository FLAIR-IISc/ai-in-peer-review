**Summary**

This paper introduces UNREAL (Unsupervised Reinforcement and Auxiliary Learning), an agent that enhances a deep reinforcement learning framework like A3C by incorporating a suite of unsupervised auxiliary tasks. The authors propose several auxiliary control tasks (e.g., pixel control) and auxiliary reward tasks (e.g., value function replay, reward prediction), which are trained jointly with the main policy. The goal is to accelerate the learning of meaningful representations and improve data efficiency. The effectiveness of the UNREAL agent is demonstrated on the DeepMind Lab (Labyrinth) environment and a set of Atari 2600 games.

**Strengths**

The primary strength of the paper lies in its strong empirical results. The UNREAL agent significantly outperforms the A3C baseline, achieving substantially higher scores and demonstrating faster learning across the tested domains. This provides convincing evidence for the utility of incorporating auxiliary objectives into deep RL agents. The paper is well-written and clearly structured, making the proposed architecture and experimental setup easy to understand for readers with expertise in deep reinforcement learning.

**Critique and Weaknesses**

While the performance gains are impressive, the core finding is not entirely surprising. The underlying hypothesis—that incorporating relevant auxiliary tasks should guide the agent towards learning more robust and generalizable features—is an intuitive one. In this respect, the paper serves as a valuable large-scale empirical validation and a strong proof-of-concept for this principle. The main contribution is the specific instantiation and successful integration of these diverse tasks into a single, effective agent.

**Questions and Suggestions for the Authors**

1.  **Computational Resources:** The addition of multiple auxiliary tasks presumably increases the computational cost of training. The paper would be strengthened by a discussion of the computational overhead. Could the authors provide a comparison of the resources (e.g., wall-clock training time, GPU/CPU usage) required for the UNREAL agent versus the A3C baseline?

2.  **Reproducibility and Code Release:** The overall UNREAL architecture is quite complex, integrating numerous components. This complexity may pose a challenge for reproducibility. To benefit the community and facilitate future work building on these ideas, I strongly encourage the authors to release their source code. Are they planning to do so?

**Overall Recommendation**

In summary, this paper presents a solid and well-executed contribution to the field of deep reinforcement learning. It convincingly demonstrates that auxiliary unsupervised tasks can drastically improve the performance and sample efficiency of RL agents. Despite the intuitive nature of the core idea, the strong empirical evidence makes this a valuable paper for the conference.
