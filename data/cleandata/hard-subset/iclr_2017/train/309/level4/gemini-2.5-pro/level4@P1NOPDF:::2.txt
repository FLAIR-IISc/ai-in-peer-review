This paper introduces a method for improving feature learning in deep reinforcement learning by augmenting the primary optimization objective with several auxiliary, domain-independent tasks. These auxiliary tasks include predicting immediate rewards, replaying and learning value functions for past experiences, and a novel "feature control" task, where the agent learns policies that aim to maximally change its sensory input (i.e., the pixel values). With the exception of value function replay, these tasks serve purely to shape the feature representations learned by a shared network, which are then used by the main policy. The experiments, conducted on Atari and Labyrinth domains, demonstrate the benefits of this approach, most notably a significant improvement in data efficiency compared to A3C.

The paper is well-written, the ideas are sound, and the results are convincing. I recommend this paper for acceptance. I have a few high-level comments and questions, none of which are major concerns.

**Major Comments**

1.  **Computational Cost:** The paper would be strengthened by a discussion of the computational overhead introduced by the auxiliary tasks. What is the impact on training speed (e.g., in terms of wall-clock time or frames per second)? Which of the auxiliary components are the most computationally expensive?

2.  **Clarity on Multiple Policies:** The abstract and introduction could be clarified to state that the agent learns separate policies for the different control tasks. The phrase "maximises many other pseudo-reward functions simultaneously" could be misinterpreted to mean that a single policy is learned to optimize a composite reward. I initially had this interpretation and only understood that multiple policies were being learned upon reaching Equation 1.

3.  **Validation of Feature Control:** The empirical validation for the "feature control" concept is not entirely convincing. The preliminary experiment in Figure 5 suggests only a marginal benefit, and primarily in the early stages of training. While the idea is interesting, I am concerned that the non-stationarity of this task—learning to control features that are themselves changing during training—could introduce stability or convergence issues.

4.  **Asymptotic Performance:** Given that the paper notes "the performance of our agents is still steadily improving" at the end of the reported training period, it would be interesting to see results from longer training runs for the best-performing agents to understand their asymptotic performance.

5.  **Auxiliary Task Weights:** The paper would benefit from a discussion on the choice of weights for the auxiliary tasks (λ_*). Were these values treated as hyperparameters and tuned, or were they fixed at 1? If the latter, could you provide some justification or ablation experiments supporting this choice?

6.  **Off-Policy Training:** For clarity, it would be helpful to explicitly state that the auxiliary tasks are trained using an off-policy, multi-step Q-learning variant, rather than standard Q-learning.

**Minor Comments and Corrections**

*   The description of the policy gradient states that it maximizes expected reward, but the corresponding equation L_π represents a loss to be minimized.
*   In Equation 1, λ_c should be placed inside the summation.
*   Just below Equation 1, r_t^(c) should be r_{t+k}^(c).
*   Figure 2 does not appear to be referenced in the main text. Additionally, Figure 1(d) should be referenced in Section 3.3.
*   In the phrase "the features discovered in this manner is shared," the verb should be "are shared."
*   The text surrounding Equation 2 refers to a loss L_PC, but this term is not explicitly defined in the equation.
*   Please clarify the meaning of "Clip" in the legend of Figure 3, specifically in the context of dueling networks.
*   An ablation study on the Atari domain, similar to the one performed on Labyrinth, would be a valuable addition. It would be interesting to see if the individual contributions of the auxiliary tasks follow the same patterns.
*   It should be clarified that the percentages mentioned in the legend of Figure 3 refer to performance on Labyrinth.
*   There appear to be incorrect references to subplots in Section 4.1.2. "Figure 3 (right)" seems to refer to the top-left plot, and "This is shown in Figure 3 Top" should likely be "Figure 3 Top Right."
*   The caption for Figure 5 states that it shows results on three Labyrinth levels, but it appears to only show results for two levels (in the left and middle plots). The caption and corresponding text may need correction.
*   In Section 4.2, the sentence "The left side shows the average performance curves of the top 5 agents for all three methods the right half shows..." is missing punctuation after "methods".
*   The appendix states that "Further details are included in the supplementary materials," but these materials do not seem to be available.
*   What is the value used for λ_PC? I assume it is 1.
