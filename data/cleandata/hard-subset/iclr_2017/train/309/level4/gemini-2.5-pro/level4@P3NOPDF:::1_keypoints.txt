- Introduces UNREAL: augment A3C with unsupervised auxiliary tasks to improve representation learning and sample efficiency.  
- Proposes specific auxiliary tasks like pixel control, value function replay, and reward prediction, trained jointly with the policy.  
- Aims to accelerate learning of meaningful features and improve data efficiency in deep RL.  
- Evaluated on DeepMind Lab (Labyrinth) and a suite of Atari 2600 games.  
- Strong empirical results: substantially outperforms A3C and learns faster across tested domains.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly describes the architecture and experimental setup.  
- Main critique: core idea is intuitive and not surprising; contribution is mainly a large-scale empirical validation and specific integration of tasks.  
- Suggestion: report computational overhead (wall-clock time, GPU/CPU usage) comparing UNREAL to A3C.  
- Suggestion: release source code to aid reproducibility given the architecture’s complexity.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, valuable contribution with convincing evidence despite the intuitive premise.
