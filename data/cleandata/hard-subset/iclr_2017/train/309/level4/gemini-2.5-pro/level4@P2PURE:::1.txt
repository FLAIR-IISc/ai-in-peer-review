This paper proposes a method for augmenting deep reinforcement learning agents, such as A3C, with unsupervised auxiliary tasks. The authors introduce several auxiliary control and reward prediction tasks, which are evaluated on the DeepMind Labyrinth and Atari benchmarks. The proposed agent, UNREAL, is shown to significantly outperform the A3C baseline in both final performance and learning speed.

This work is a valuable contribution. The paper is well-written and easy to follow for readers with expertise in deep reinforcement learning. However, the primary finding—that incorporating relevant auxiliary tasks can accelerate and improve representation learning—is conceptually intuitive, making the results not entirely surprising. The paper thus serves as an effective proof of concept for this idea.

Questions for the authors:
*   Could the authors comment on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent?
*   Given the complexity of the overall architecture, are the authors willing to release the source code to facilitate reproducibility?
