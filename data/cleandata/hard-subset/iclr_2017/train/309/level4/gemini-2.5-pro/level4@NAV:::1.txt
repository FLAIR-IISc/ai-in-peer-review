This paper presents a methodology for augmenting deep reinforcement learning agents, such as A3C, with unsupervised auxiliary tasks. The authors introduce a collection of auxiliary control and reward objectives and demonstrate their effectiveness on the Labyrinth and Atari benchmark environments. The proposed UNREAL agent not only achieves significantly higher performance than the A3C baseline but also exhibits faster convergence. This work is a valuable contribution to the conference. Nevertheless, the positive outcome is not entirely unexpected; it is intuitive that incorporating auxiliary tasks relevant to the main goal would facilitate more efficient and effective feature learning. This paper provides a compelling proof of concept for this hypothesis.

The manuscript is well-structured and clearly written, making it accessible to readers with expertise in deep reinforcement learning.

Could the authors elaborate on the computational requirements for training the UNREAL agent?
Considering the intricate nature of the overall architecture, would the authors be willing to make their source code publicly available to promote reproducibility?
