- Introduces UNREAL: augmenting A3C with unsupervised auxiliary tasks to accelerate learning and improve data efficiency.
- Integrates auxiliary control and reward-prediction tasks (pixel control, value replay, reward prediction) trained concurrently with the policy.
- Evaluated on Atari 2600 and 3D Labyrinth, showing substantially faster learning and higher final performance than A3C.
- Primary strength is strong empirical results demonstrating improved sample efficiency and performance across domains.
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and accessible to deep RL experts.
- Contribution is a robust, well-engineered validation of the intuitive hypothesis that dense auxiliary signals improve representation learning.
- UNREAL combines multiple diverse tasks into a cohesive framework and demonstrates practical benefits at scale.
- Architecture is considerably more complex than A3C, adding multiple network heads and off-policy mechanisms.
- Practical concern: lacks analysis of computational cost (training time, memory, hardware) compared to baseline.
- Reproducibility concern: unclear whether source code will be released to facilitate verification and follow-up work.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution setting a new standard for sample efficiency in RL.
- After rebuttal: reviewer made no change to their evaluation.
