- Proposes training RL agents on multiple auxiliary tasks to learn more robust and useful features (multi-task learning).  
- Introduces two pseudo-control tasks: pixel control (maximize pixel intensity changes) and feature control (maximize specific hidden-unit activations).  
- Adds a supervised reward-prediction task trained via skewed replay sampling to ensure ~50% of sampled sequences contain a reward.  
- Combines these into the UNREAL agent, which shows large performance gains and can reach baseline performance with about one-tenth the training experience.  
- Frames the approach as a compelling alternative to passive unsupervised or model-based representation learning by leveraging the agent’s ability to control its environment.  
- Paper is generally clear for Deep RL readers, but auxiliary-task implementation details are high-level; key items (e.g., the pixel-control loss L_PC) should appear in the main text.  
- Experimental methodology is sound; reporting top-3 hyperparameter performances helps address concerns about differing optimal hyperparameter ranges.  
- Limited analysis of why the auxiliary tasks help beyond improved final scores; ablations indicate pixel and feature control drive most gains.  
- Recommends further work: study control tasks in isolation, analyze learned representations, and use additional metrics to explain mechanisms.
