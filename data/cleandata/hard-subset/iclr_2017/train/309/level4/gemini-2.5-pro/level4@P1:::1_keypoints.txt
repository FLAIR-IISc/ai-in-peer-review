- Introduces UNREAL: augments A3C with auxiliary tasks to accelerate and improve deep RL learning.
- Proposes two auxiliary task classes: control tasks (pixel/feature control) and reward prediction from short observation sequences.
- Trains auxiliary tasks in parallel with main policy, sharing representation and using off-policy Q-learning from a replay buffer.
- Evaluated on 57 Atari games and a new 3D Labyrinth environment.
- UNREAL significantly outperforms A3C and prior SOTA in final performance, data efficiency, and hyperparameter robustness.
- Decision: Accept.
- Major empirical contribution: state-of-the-art Atari results and substantial 10x speedup on Labyrinth.
- Effective integration: combines on-policy A3C for the main task with off-policy value learning for auxiliary tasks to shape representations.
- Ablation studies show each component (Pixel Control, Reward Prediction, Value Replay) contributes positively, with the combination best.
- Pixel control encourages learning controllable environment aspects and outperforms reconstruction-based objectives.
- Reward prediction with skewed replay focuses on rare rewards and aids temporal credit assignment in sparse-reward settings.
- Strong empirical numbers: average 880% human on Atari and super-human results with faster learning on Labyrinth, robust across hyperparameters.
- Questions for authors: report computational overhead of auxiliaries vs A3C; plan to release code and Labyrinth; how were auxiliary loss weights (λVR, λPC, λRP) chosen and how sensitive are they?
