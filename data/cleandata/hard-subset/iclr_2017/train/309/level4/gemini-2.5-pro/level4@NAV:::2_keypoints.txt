- Proposes augmenting policy learning with domain-agnostic auxiliary tasks (pixel control, reward prediction, value replay) sharing a CNN-LSTM feature extractor.
- Pixel control and reward prediction shape features only; value replay also affects the value estimate.
- Empirical results on Atari and Labyrinth show substantial data-efficiency gains over A3C.
- Manuscript is well-written, ideas are sound, results are compelling; reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Authors should analyze computational overhead and identify the most expensive auxiliary components.
- Clarify in abstract/introduction that distinct policies are learned for each auxiliary task (not a single multi-objective policy).
- Limited empirical validation for feature control: only marginal early benefit shown and potential stability concerns as features change.
- Run training longer (at least for top agents) to assess asymptotic performance since learning was still improving.
- Explain why auxiliary task weights (λ_*) were fixed to one rather than optimized or tuned.
- Clarify that auxiliary tasks are trained off-policy with multi-step returns and thus deviate from standard Q-Learning.
