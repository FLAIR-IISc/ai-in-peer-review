This paper proposes a method for augmenting deep reinforcement learning agents, such as A3C, with unsupervised auxiliary tasks. The authors introduce a suite of auxiliary control and reward prediction tasks and evaluate their proposed UNREAL agent on the DeepMind Labyrinth and Atari benchmarks. The results demonstrate that the UNREAL agent significantly outperforms the A3C baseline and exhibits a faster learning rate.

This work is a solid contribution to the conference. However, the primary result is not surprising; it is a well-understood principle that adding relevant auxiliary tasks should facilitate more effective and rapid feature learning. In this respect, the paper serves as a strong proof of concept for applying this idea in the context of deep RL.

The paper is well-written and should be easy to follow for any reader with expertise in deep reinforcement learning.

Could the authors comment on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent?

Given the complexity of the overall architecture, will the authors release the source code for their model to facilitate reproducibility?
