- Proposal: UNREAL augments A3C with auxiliary tasks (auxiliary control and auxiliary reward prediction) to improve representation learning and data efficiency.
- Auxiliary tasks: auxiliary control learns policies to change sensory/internal features (e.g., pixel control); auxiliary reward predicts onset of immediate rewards.
- Integration: auxiliary objectives are trained off-policy from an experience replay buffer alongside on-policy A3C updates.
- Empirical claims: UNREAL achieves state-of-the-art results—average 880% human performance on 57 Atari games and 87% on Labyrinth—while learning ~10× faster than A3C on Labyrinth.
- Decision: Accept.
- Primary reason 1: Substantial, convincing performance and data-efficiency gains over A3C across two distinct domains, with robustness to hyperparameters.
- Primary reason 2: Thorough empirical investigation and ablation studies showing complementary value of multiple auxiliary objectives.
- Supporting evidence: Extensive experiments (Figure 3) demonstrate each auxiliary task improves performance and their combination is best; pixel control outperforms pixel prediction/reconstruction (Figure 5).
- Conceptual note: The paper is mainly empirical; framing why this particular combination of control, reward prediction, and value replay works could be clearer.
- Environment-analysis suggestion: More analysis on which auxiliary tasks help which environment types (e.g., visual dynamics vs. sparse rewards) would be valuable.
- Reproducibility question 1: What is the additional computational overhead (memory and wall-clock time) of UNREAL versus A3C?
- Reproducibility question 2: Are there plans to release the source code and Labyrinth environment to facilitate reproduction?
