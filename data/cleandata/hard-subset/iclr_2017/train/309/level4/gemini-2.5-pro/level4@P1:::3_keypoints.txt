- Proposes UNREAL: augment RL with auxiliary control and prediction tasks to learn richer, more useful representations.
- Auxiliary control tasks: off-policy value functions for pixel control (maximize local pixel change) and feature control (maximize hidden unit activations).
- Auxiliary prediction task: predict immediate extrinsic reward from short observation sequences using a replay buffer skewed toward rewarding events.
- Integrates these into A3C, producing substantial gains on 57 Atari games and Labyrinth, with up to 10x faster learning.
- Core contribution: shift from passive reconstruction objectives to active, control-oriented auxiliary objectives aligned with agent goals.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Novel and compelling formulation that encourages learning cause–effect representations via control-oriented auxiliaries.
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical results—large improvements in final performance and data efficiency, notably the 10x speedup.
- Supporting argument: Thorough experimental evaluation and ablations show each auxiliary component contributes; broad hyperparameter search reduces tuning confounds.
- Additional feedback: Paper shows that auxiliaries help but lacks deeper analysis of why they are effective, especially pixel control’s dominant role.
- Additional feedback: Some crucial implementation details (e.g., exact pixel control pseudo-reward) are tucked in the appendix and should be moved to the main text.
- Question: Why is pixel control so much more effective than reward prediction or value replay in isolation—does actively influencing visuals provide a more general skill?
- Question: Have you compared against modern passive representation methods (e.g., contrastive learning) rather than just pixel reconstruction?
- Question: What behaviors does feature control encourage—do specific neurons map to high-level concepts or policies to activate them?
