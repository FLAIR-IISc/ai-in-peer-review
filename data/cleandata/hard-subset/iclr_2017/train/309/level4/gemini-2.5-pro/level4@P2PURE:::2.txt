This paper proposes a method to improve feature learning in deep reinforcement learning by augmenting the primary optimization objective with several auxiliary, domain-independent tasks. These tasks include learning control policies to maximize change in the pixel space, predicting immediate rewards, and replaying value function estimates. With the exception of value function replay, these tasks contribute to the loss solely to improve the representation learned by a shared feature extraction network (a CNN+LSTM). The experimental results on Atari and Labyrinth domains demonstrate the benefits of this approach, most notably a significant improvement in data efficiency compared to the baseline A3C agent.

The paper is well-written, the core ideas are sound, and the results are convincing. I recommend acceptance. I have a few high-level comments and questions for the authors, none of which are major concerns:

-   The authors should comment on the additional computational cost incurred by optimizing the auxiliary tasks. How does this affect overall training speed, and which components are the most computationally expensive?
-   The abstract and introduction could be clarified to state that the agent learns a separate policy for each auxiliary control task. The current phrasing, that the agent "also maximises many other pseudo-reward functions simultaneously by reinforcement learning," could be misinterpreted as learning a single policy to optimize a composite reward. I made this misinterpretation myself until reaching Equation 1.
-   The empirical validation for the "feature control" task appears weak; the preliminary experiment in Figure 5 shows only a slight initial benefit. Furthermore, I am concerned about the stability of this approach, as the control task's objective (the features) changes concurrently with the policy being learned. The authors should comment on potential stability or convergence issues.
-   Since the paper notes that "the performance of our agents is still steadily improving," it would be interesting to see how far the performance of the best agents can be pushed with longer training.
-   The weights for the auxiliary tasks (the λ coefficients) are fixed at 1. Were these treated as hyperparameters? It would be valuable to know if any experiments were conducted to validate this choice over tuning them.
-   It should be explicitly mentioned that the auxiliary tasks are trained using off-policy learning with multi-step returns, which deviates from standard one-step Q-Learning.

Minor issues:
-   In the description of policy gradients, `L_pi` is described as maximizing the expected reward, but the subsequent equation defines a loss to be minimized.
-   In Equation 1, `λ_c` should be inside the summation.
-   Just below Equation 1, `r_t^(c)` should be `r_{t+k}^(c)`.
-   Figure 2 does not appear to be referenced in the main text. Additionally, Figure 1(d) should be referenced in Section 3.3.
-   In the sentence "the features discovered in this manner is shared," the verb should be plural: "are shared."
-   The text surrounding Equation 2 refers to a loss `L_PC`, but this term is not explicitly defined in the equation.
-   In the legend for Figure 3, the meaning of "Clip" in the context of dueling networks should be explained.
-   The ablation studies on Labyrinth are informative. For completeness, it would be beneficial to see similar ablations on the Atari domain to verify if the same patterns of contribution from individual tasks hold.
-   The legend of Figure 3 mentions percentages that correspond to the Labyrinth experiments; this should be explicitly stated.
-   In Section 4.1.2, the reference "Figure 3 (right)" appears to point to the top-left plot. Later, "Figure 3 Top" should be "Figure 3 Top Right."
-   The caption for Figure 5 states it shows results on "three Labyrinth navigation levels," but it appears to only show two (left and middle plots). The preceding text may also need correction.
-   The sentence in Section 4.2 beginning "The left side shows..." is missing punctuation after "methods."
-   The appendix states, "Further details are included in the supplementary materials," but it is unclear where these materials are located.
-   The value for `λ_PC` is not specified; I assume it is 1.
