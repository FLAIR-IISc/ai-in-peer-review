- Introduces UNREAL: augments A3C with auxiliary self-supervised tasks to accelerate and stabilize deep RL.
- Uses auxiliary control (pixel/feature control) and reward prediction to build rich, reward-focused representations.
- Hybrid learning: on-policy A3C for main task combined with off-policy Q-learning from replay for auxiliary tasks.
- Validated on 57 Atari games and new 3D Labyrinth environment with state-of-the-art performance.
- Substantially outperforms A3C in final score, data efficiency (up to 10x speedup), and hyperparameter robustness.
- Decision: Accept.
- Significant performance and efficiency gains represent a notable advance for deep RL.
- Novel integration of auxiliary tasks and learning methods; using RL to solve auxiliary control tasks is powerful.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough with ablation studies showing each auxiliary component helps and the combination is best.
- Pixel control auxiliary is more effective than prediction or reconstruction alternatives, supporting the control-based hypothesis.
- Paper is well-written and Labyrinth provides a valuable, more complex evaluation environment.
- Request for clarification on computational cost: memory footprint and training time per step versus A3C.
- Question on generality: have other control objectives (e.g., game-specific features) been explored beyond low-level visual features?
- Request for code/environment release plans to enable reproducibility and wider adoption.
