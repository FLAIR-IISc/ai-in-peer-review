- Introduces UNREAL: a deep RL agent that uses auxiliary tasks to speed learning and improve final performance.  
- Moves beyond passive unsupervised objectives by framing representation learning as active control problems.  
- Proposes two auxiliary task classes: auxiliary control (pixel control, feature control) and auxiliary reward prediction.  
- Trains auxiliary tasks in parallel with the main policy using off-policy experience replay and biased sampling for sparse rewards.  
- Builds on A3C and shows state-of-the-art results on the 57-game Atari suite.  
- Demonstrates ~10x faster learning and large performance gains on a visually rich 3D Labyrinth environment.  
- Decision: Accept — considered a strong, well-motivated paper with compelling empirical evidence.  
- Key reason 1: Novel conceptual contribution by reframing representation learning as controllable objectives rather than reconstruction.  
- Key reason 2: Significant empirical improvements over a strong A3C baseline across distinct domains, with better data efficiency and robustness.  
- Ablation studies show each auxiliary component contributes positively to final performance.  
- Experimental methodology is sound, with thorough hyperparameter search and reporting of top-performing runs.  
- Suggest deeper analysis of what the auxiliary tasks learn (e.g., visualizations of pixel control policies).  
- Recommend further investigation of pixel control’s properties and potential failure modes (e.g., distractions from irrelevant controllable elements).  
- Request clearer presentation of auxiliary loss formulations in the main text for readability.  
- Question for authors: quantify computational overhead — how does UNREAL compare to A3C in wall-clock time or FLOPs per training step?
