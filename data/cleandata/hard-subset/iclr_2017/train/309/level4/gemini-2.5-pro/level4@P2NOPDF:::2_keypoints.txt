- Summary: Proposes UNREAL—augmenting A3C with domain-independent auxiliary tasks (Pixel Control, Reward Prediction, Value Replay) to improve feature learning and data efficiency, evaluated on Atari and Labyrinth with strong gains.

- Overall assessment: Strong, well-written paper with intuitive idea and compelling empirical improvements; clear accept.

- Computational overhead: Need discussion/comparison of wall-clock training speed and which auxiliary tasks add most compute.

- Clarity on multiple policies: Clarify earlier that separate policies are learned for auxiliary control tasks to avoid implying a single composite-policy.

- Validation and stability of feature control: Feature-control results look preliminary; need more ablation (especially on Atari) and discussion of non-stationarity/stability.

- Training duration: Report or extend longer training for top models to see final potential and possibly establish new SOTA.

- Auxiliary task weighting: Explain choice/sensitivity of λ coefficients (all set to 1) and whether they were tuned.

- Off-policy auxiliary updates: Clarify that auxiliary control and reward prediction use off-policy n-step Q-learning-style updates and distinguish from one-step Q-learning.
