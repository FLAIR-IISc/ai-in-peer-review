This paper proposes a method for augmenting deep reinforcement learning agents, such as A3C, with unsupervised auxiliary tasks. The authors introduce several auxiliary control and reward prediction tasks and evaluate their proposed agent, UNREAL, on the DeepMind Labyrinth and Atari 2600 domains. The results demonstrate that the UNREAL agent significantly outperforms the A3C baseline in terms of both final performance and learning speed. This represents a strong contribution and the work is a good fit for the conference.

The paper is well-written and should be easy to follow for readers with expertise in deep reinforcement learning.

However, the core finding is not surprising. It is generally expected that adding relevant auxiliary tasks will improve and accelerate representation learning for the main task. In this sense, the paper serves as a strong proof of concept for this principle within a deep RL context, rather than presenting a fundamentally new insight.

I have a few questions for the authors:
1.  Could you comment on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent compared to the A3C baseline?
2.  The overall architecture is quite complex. Are you planning to release the source code to facilitate reproducibility?
