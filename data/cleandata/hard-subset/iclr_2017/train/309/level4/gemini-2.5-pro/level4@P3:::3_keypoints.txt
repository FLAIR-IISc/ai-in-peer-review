- Proposes UNREAL: augment A3C with auxiliary tasks to learn a powerful shared representation for RL.  
- Introduces two auxiliary objective classes: Auxiliary Control Tasks and Auxiliary Reward/Value Tasks.  
- Auxiliary Control Tasks: pixel control (maximize pixel changes) and feature control (maximize hidden-unit activations), learned off-policy via Q-learning on replay.  
- Auxiliary Reward/Value Tasks: reward prediction (oversampled rewarding events) and value-function regression on replay to address reward sparsity.  
- Combines on-policy A3C for main task with off-policy auxiliary tasks in a single agent.  
- Strong empirical gains on Labyrinth: 87% of human expert, more than doubling A3C and up to 10x faster learning.  
- Strong empirical gains on Atari: mean score 880% of human, outperforming prior state-of-the-art.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Significant conceptual contribution—shifts representation learning from passive (reconstruction) to active control-based objectives.  
- Key reason 2: State-of-the-art empirical results with extensive experiments, ablations, and hyperparameter robustness analysis.  
- Pixel control forces learning of controllable, causal aspects of the environment and outperforms predictive/reconstruction objectives.  
- Labyrinth benchmark demonstrates improvements in data efficiency, robustness, and stability; ablations show complementary benefits of auxiliary tasks.  
- Suggestion: clarify exact formulation of auxiliary control loss (L_PC) earlier, showing the n-step Q-learning loss for a single control task.  
- Suggestion: provide qualitative analysis of behaviors learned by pixel control policies to explain why they help main tasks.  
- Suggestion: expand motivation and experiments for feature control, as it appears more general and promising than pixel control.  
- Questions: provide intuition/qualitative examples of pixel-control behaviors and their contribution to navigation/combat.  
- Questions: have other control objectives been considered and what principles should guide their design?  
- Questions: why was feature control tested only on one level, and is it a more general alternative to pixel control?
