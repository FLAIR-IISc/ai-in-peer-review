### Review

This paper introduces UNREAL (Unsupervised Reinforcement and Auxiliary Learning), a method for augmenting deep reinforcement learning agents with unsupervised auxiliary tasks to accelerate learning and improve data efficiency. The authors integrate a suite of auxiliary control and reward prediction tasks into a base A3C agent. These tasks, which include pixel control, value function replay, and reward prediction, are trained concurrently with the main policy. The proposed UNREAL agent is evaluated on the Atari 2600 suite and 3D Labyrinth environments, where it demonstrates significantly improved performance and learning speed compared to the A3C baseline.

The primary strength of this work lies in its strong empirical results. The UNREAL agent not only learns substantially faster but also achieves a higher level of final performance across a variety of challenging domains. This provides compelling evidence for the efficacy of using auxiliary tasks to shape the feature representations learned by the agent. The paper is well-written, clearly structured, and easy to follow for readers with expertise in deep reinforcement learning.

The contribution can be seen as a powerful and well-engineered validation of an intuitive concept. The hypothesis that adding relevant, dense learning signals through auxiliary tasks should improve the agent's representation learning is not in itself surprising. However, this paper provides a robust and comprehensive implementation that successfully combines multiple diverse tasks into a single, cohesive framework and demonstrates its practical benefits on a large scale.

While the results are impressive, the UNREAL architecture is considerably more complex than the A3C baseline, integrating multiple additional network heads and off-policy learning mechanisms. This raises a few practical questions:

1.  Could the authors comment on the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent compared to the baseline? An analysis of the training time, memory usage, and hardware requirements would be valuable for the community to understand the trade-offs involved and for other researchers aiming to build upon this work.

2.  Given the model's complexity, reproducibility is a key concern. Do the authors intend to release the source code for their agent? This would greatly facilitate further research and allow the community to verify the results and explore variations of the proposed architecture.

In summary, this paper presents a significant contribution to the field of deep reinforcement learning. By successfully integrating a variety of auxiliary tasks, the UNREAL agent sets a new standard for sample efficiency and performance. The empirical validation is thorough, and the work provides a strong foundation for future research into representation learning in RL.
