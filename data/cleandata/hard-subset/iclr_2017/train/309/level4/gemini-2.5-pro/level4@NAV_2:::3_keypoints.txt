- Proposes UNREAL: a deep RL agent that augments A3C with off-policy auxiliary control tasks (pixel/feature control) and an auxiliary reward prediction trained from a skewed replay buffer.  
- Trains auxiliary value functions and reward prediction to improve representation learning and data efficiency.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results: ~880% human on 57-game Atari suite and large gains (≈10x speedup) on a 3D Labyrinth task.  
- Demonstrates substantial improvements in final performance, learning speed, and hyperparameter robustness vs. a strong A3C baseline.  
- Conceptual contribution: shifts representation learning from passive reconstruction to active control, focusing on controllable aspects of the environment.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough with robust hyperparameter search and ablation studies showing positive contributions from each auxiliary component.  
- Pixel and feature control contribute most to performance gains, with pixel control particularly dominant in Labyrinth.  
- Recommends acceptance based on novelty, strong motivation, and compelling empirical results.  
- Suggests moving key implementation details (e.g., explicit definition of L_PC) from appendix to main text for clarity.  
- Requests more analysis/qualitative visualization explaining why pixel/feature control are so effective.  
- Notes potential failure modes: pixel control may be distracted by stochastic or irrelevant dynamic textures.  
- Asks whether smaller contributions from reward prediction and value replay are due to task tuning (λ weights) or inherent limits of those signals.
