- Introduces UNREAL: augmenting A3C with concurrent auxiliary tasks (pixel control, reward prediction) sharing a representation to improve learning speed and performance.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on Atari and Labyrinth, with strong empirical evidence.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novel, well-motivated architecture addressing representation learning in RL, especially for sparse rewards.  
- Strong empirical results show large gains in final performance and data efficiency.  
- Reports a 10× mean speedup on Labyrinth and major improvement in human-normalized scores.  
- UNREAL more than doubles mean Labyrinth score (54% → 87%) and achieves 880% average expert human performance on Atari.  
- Ablations show each auxiliary task contributes positively and the method is more robust to hyperparameters.  
- Auxiliary tasks are domain-agnostic and effective across 2D and 3D environments, suggesting broad applicability.
