- Proposes auxiliary, domain-independent tasks (pixel control, immediate reward prediction, value replay) to shape shared CNN+LSTM features for better RL learning; auxiliary policies discarded after training.  
- Experiments on Atari and Labyrinth show improved data efficiency over A3C; paper is well-written and convincing; reviewer recommends acceptance.  

Major comments:
- Computational cost: asks for measured wall-clock overhead and identification of most expensive auxiliary components.  
- Clarity on multiple policies: clarify earlier that separate policies are learned for each auxiliary task, not a single multi-objective policy.  
- Validation of pixel control: empirical benefit appears weak and possibly unstable due to non-stationarity of features; request discussion on convergence/stability.  
- Training duration and final performance: agents still improving at training end—request longer runs to assess final converged performance.  
- Auxiliary task weights (lambda_*): weights set to 1—ask whether chosen arbitrarily or validated and request sensitivity analysis.  
- Off-policy auxiliary learning: note that auxiliary control and value prediction are trained off-policy with multi-step returns—request explicit statement that this departs from standard one-step Q-learning.

Minor comments and typos:
- Ablation studies: request Atari ablation comparable to Labyrinth to verify per-task contributions across domains.  
- Policy gradient loss wording: clarify that the shown L_pi is a loss to be minimized, not a maximized quantity.  
- Equation 1: weight lambda_c should be inside the summation over control tasks.  
- Text below Equation 1: r_t^(c) should be r_{t+k}^(c) to match n-step returns.  
- Equation 2: L_PC referenced but not defined in the equation—define explicitly.  
- Figure references: Figure 2 not cited; Figure 1(d) should be referenced in Section 3.3.  
- Figure 3 legend: explain term "Clip" for dueling baseline and clarify what reported percentages refer to (Labyrinth?).  
- Figure 3 text references: fix incorrect references (e.g., "Figure 3 (right)" vs top-left, "Figure 3 Top" vs top right).  
- Figure 5 reference: text claims three Labyrinth levels but figure appears to show only two—please correct.  
- Grammar fixes: "the features discovered in this manner is shared" → "are shared"; add comma in the long sentence in Section 4.2.  
- Hyperparameter value: state lambda_PC value explicitly (assumed 1).  
- Appendix: clarify where supplementary materials are located.
