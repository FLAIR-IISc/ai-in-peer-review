- Proposes training RL agents with auxiliary tasks to learn stronger feature representations.
- Introduces two pseudo-control tasks: control change in pixel intensity and control activation of latent features.
- Adds a supervised regression task to predict immediate reward after a sequence, learned offline via skewed replay sampling to balance reward/no-reward.
- Empirical results: agents perform strongly on discrete-action-continuous-space tasks and reach baseline performance in ~10x fewer iterations.
- Contrasts with passive unsupervised/model-based learning by using controllable local/internal feature learning to complement policy learning under partial observability.
- Approach is novel and leverages the agent's ability to influence the environment as a learning signal.
- Presentation critique: auxiliary tasks are described at a high level; more low-level detail is needed (e.g., introduce L_PC before the appendix).
- Methodology generally sound; hyperparameter differences between A3C and UNREAL acknowledged and top-3 reporting helps mitigate this.
- Weakness/future work: insufficient experimental analysis of auxiliary tasks themselves—pixel/feature control appears most impactful and warrants deeper, isolated study and metrics beyond RL performance.
