

# Review of "UNREAL: Training a Single Agent to Control Multiple Tasks"

## 1. Summary of Contributions

The paper introduces UNREAL, an agent that improves feature learning in deep reinforcement learning by adding domain-independent auxiliary tasks (control, reward prediction, value replay) that share CNN+LSTM features. The key contribution is demonstrating that these auxiliary tasks substantially improve data efficiency and robustness over A3C on Atari and Labyrinth environments. The authors show UNREAL achieves 880% human-normalized performance on Atari (more than doubling previous state-of-the-art) and 87% on Labyrinth, with a 10× speedup in learning. The approach is well-motivated by the idea that learning to control features of the sensorimotor stream leads to more useful representations than traditional unsupervised learning.

## 2. Decision

Accept. The paper makes a strong contribution to the field with convincing empirical results demonstrating improved data efficiency and robustness across multiple environments. The core idea of using auxiliary tasks to improve representation learning is well-supported by the experiments.

## 3. Supporting Arguments

The paper makes a compelling case for the value of auxiliary tasks in deep RL. The experiments on both Atari and Labyrinth are thorough, with multiple ablation studies showing that each auxiliary task contributes to performance. The results on Labyrinth are particularly impressive, showing a 10× speedup in learning and significant performance gains. The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the methodology and results.

The key strength is the empirical validation showing that the auxiliary tasks lead to more efficient learning across multiple environments. The authors demonstrate that the UNREAL agent not only achieves better final performance but also learns significantly faster than A3C, which is critical for practical applications.

## 4. Additional Feedback for Improvement

While the paper is strong, several points would enhance its clarity and scientific rigor:

1. **Clarify separate policies**: The abstract and introduction should explicitly state that separate policies are learned for each auxiliary task (not a single policy optimizing all rewards), as this is a key aspect of the approach.

2. **Quantify computational cost**: Please provide metrics on the extra computational cost (training speed loss) and which auxiliary components are most expensive. This is crucial for understanding the practical trade-offs.

3. **Feature-control validation**: The feature-control auxiliary task lacks strong empirical validation. Figure 5 is unconvincing and raises stability/convergence concerns as tasks change during training. Please provide stronger evidence for its effectiveness.

4. **Training duration**: The agent performance was still improving at the end of training; please report how far the best agents went and suggest training longer.

5. **Auxiliary task weights**: Why were auxiliary-task weights (λ*) fixed at 1 rather than treated as hyperparameters or validated experimentally? This should be addressed.

6. **Off-policy training**: Please explicitly state that auxiliary tasks are trained off-policy (not with "true" Q-learning due to multi-step empirical rewards).

7. **Equation corrections**: 
   - Eq. 1: λ_c should be inside the summation
   - Below Eq. 1: r_t^(c) should be r_{t+k}^{(c)}
   - Eq. 2: L_PC is referenced but not defined

8. **Figure and reference issues**:
   - Figure 2 is not referenced in the text
   - Figure 1(d) should be referenced in Section 3.3
   - Figure 3 legend: Explain what "Clip" means for dueling networks
   - Clarify that percentages in Figure 3's legend refer to Labyrinth
   - Correct figure references (4.1.2 "Figure 3 (right)" actually refers to the top-left plot)
   - Fix Figure 5 caption/text mismatch (claims top 5 on three levels but seems to show two)

9. **Minor corrections**:
   - "the features discovered in this manner is shared" → "are shared"
   - 4.2 sentence punctuation: Add comma after "methods"
   - Appendix: Specify where supplementary materials are located
   - Report λ_PC explicitly (assumed to be 1)

10. **Additional ablation**: Provide more ablation studies on Atari to see if per-component contributions match Labyrinth patterns.

## Key Questions for Authors

1. Could you quantify the extra computational cost (training speed loss) for each auxiliary component?
2. Why were auxiliary-task weights fixed at 1 rather than treated as hyperparameters?
3. The feature-control task's empirical validation in Figure 5 appears weak; could you provide stronger evidence for its effectiveness?
4. Could you clarify the off-policy nature of the auxiliary task training in the main text?
5. The agent performance was still improving at the end of training; what was the final performance after longer training?

The paper makes a significant contribution to deep RL with well-supported results. Addressing these points would substantially strengthen an already strong paper. The reviewers' concerns are largely about clarification and additional evidence rather than fundamental flaws, making this a clear accept.
