- Proposes UNREAL: an agent that learns auxiliary control (pixel change, feature activation) and reward-prediction tasks alongside the primary RL objective to improve representations.
- Demonstrates large empirical gains: on Labyrinth reaches 87% human performance and learns ~10× faster than A3C; on Atari achieves 880% human performance.
- Argues auxiliary tasks provide complementary learning signals that improve feature extraction and data efficiency.
- Presents careful ablation studies showing individual and combined benefits of each auxiliary task and robustness across hyperparameter settings.
- Results are a persuasive proof-of-concept validating the intuition that relevant auxiliary tasks aid feature shaping.
- Paper is well-written and situates contributions within prior work on auxiliary tasks in RL.
- Notes architecture is relatively complex, which may affect practicality and reproducibility.
- Recommends acceptance as a solid, practical extension to deep RL with rigorous empirical evaluation.
- Suggests adding detailed analysis of computational resources required (GPU hours, number of machines).
- Requests clarification on source-code release plans and timing to aid reproducibility.
