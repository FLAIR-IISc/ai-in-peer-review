- Introduces UNREAL: augments A3C with unsupervised auxiliary tasks to improve representation learning and performance.
- Proposes two auxiliary task types: auxiliary control tasks (pixel and feature control) and auxiliary reward prediction.
- Shares a common representation across tasks to accelerate learning and improve final performance.
- Empirical gains: strong improvements on Labyrinth (87% human, 61% relative gain) and 57 Atari games (avg 880% human), ~10× faster learning and more hyperparameter-robust.
- Decision: Accept — solid contribution with rigorous results.
- Addresses an important problem: leveraging sensorimotor stream structure to learn better representations beyond sparse extrinsic rewards.
- Positioning: thorough related work discussion and clear distinction that auxiliary tasks aid representation learning, not separate policies.
- Empirical rigor: extensive ablation studies, multiple environments, learning curves, human-normalized scores, and averaging over hyperparameters/seeds.
- Notable result: 10× speedup on Labyrinth.
- Suggestion: discuss computational overhead of auxiliary tasks (inference time and memory) in more detail.
- Suggestion: provide a deeper comparison of auxiliary tasks’ relative contributions across environments (e.g., pixel vs feature control differences).
- Suggestion: give more intuition for why reward prediction helps beyond “removing perceptual sparsity.”
- Question: provide detailed computational resources used (GPUs, total training time) beyond “32 asynchronous threads.”
- Question: will the authors release source code to aid reproducibility?
- Question: explain why reconstruction improved initial learning but worsened final scores compared to vanilla A3C.
- Question: clarify how the 116% human-normalized score on lt hallway slope compares to the human experts who provided the benchmark.
