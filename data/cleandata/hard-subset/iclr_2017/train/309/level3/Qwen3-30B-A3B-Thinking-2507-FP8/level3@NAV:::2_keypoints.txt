- Introduces UNREAL: trains separate policies for multiple domain-independent auxiliary tasks that share a CNN+LSTM feature representation to improve feature learning in deep RL.
- Demonstrates large improvements in data efficiency and performance over A3C on Atari (880% human expert) and Labyrinth (87% human expert).
- Decision: Accept — strong contribution with well-motivated approach and rigorous results.
- Situates work relative to UVFA/Horde and introduces novel mechanisms for shared representation learning.
- Empirical results are robust across multiple environments and hyperparameter settings, showing clear superiority.
- Request to quantify computational cost and identify which auxiliary components are most expensive.
- Clarify in abstract/intro that separate policies are learned for each auxiliary task (not a single policy optimizing all rewards).
- Strengthen empirical validation of the feature-control task (Fig. 5 is unconvincing and raises stability concerns).
- Report how far the best agents progressed and consider training longer since performance was still improving.
- Justify why auxiliary-task weights (λ_*) were fixed at 1 instead of being treated as hyperparameters.
- Explicitly state that auxiliary tasks are trained off-policy (not using "true" Q-learning due to multi-step empirical rewards).
- Fix minor technical issues: Eq.1 formatting (λ_c inside summation), reference Fig.2, grammar fixes, and clarify figure references.
