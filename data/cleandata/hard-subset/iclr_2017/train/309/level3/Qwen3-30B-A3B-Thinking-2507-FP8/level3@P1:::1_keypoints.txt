- Introduces UNREAL: augments deep RL with auxiliary control and reward prediction tasks to improve representation learning.
- Proposes learning separate policies for multiple pseudo-rewards (pixel changes, network features) and a mechanism to focus representations on extrinsic rewards.
- Integrates these auxiliary tasks within the A3C parallel learning framework for improved data efficiency and performance.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Well-motivated contribution: auxiliary control tasks (controlling features vs. reconstructing) substantially improve representations.
- Strong empirical results: large gains over A3C on Atari and Labyrinth, including a 10× speedup on Labyrinth.
- Exceptional Atari performance: reported 880% human-normalized performance and surpassing state-of-the-art across 57 games.
- Clear positioning relative to prior work (Horde, UVFA, successor representations) and distinction that auxiliaries are used for representation learning, not as direct task solutions.
- Compelling, well-explained motivation (e.g., baby example) for learning to control features.
- Thorough, rigorous empirical evaluation with comprehensive ablation studies showing each auxiliary component contributes.
- Demonstrates improved robustness to hyperparameters, important for practical use.
- Paper is well-written and accessible; architecture and experiments are clearly explained.
- Supplementary visualizations provide useful intuition about how representations develop.
- Request to clarify computational requirements (number of GPUs, total training time, memory usage) for practitioners.
- Suggests authors state whether they will release source code to aid reproducibility given architectural complexity.
- Two specific questions for authors: exact computational overhead per iteration vs. A3C, and a detailed comparison to prior 3D auxiliary-prediction work (Lample & Chaplot 2016; Mirowski et al. 2016) in theory and implementation.
