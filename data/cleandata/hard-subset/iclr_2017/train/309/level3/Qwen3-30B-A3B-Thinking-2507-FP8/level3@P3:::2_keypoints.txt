- Introduces UNREAL: an agent using domain-independent auxiliary tasks (control, reward prediction, value replay) sharing CNN+LSTM features.  
- UNREAL substantially improves data efficiency and robustness over A3C (880% human-normalized on Atari, 87% on Labyrinth) and achieves ~10× faster learning.  
- Decision: Accept — strong contribution with convincing empirical results across multiple environments.  
- Experiments are thorough with ablations showing each auxiliary task contributes; Labyrinth results particularly compelling.  
- UNREAL yields better final performance and significantly faster learning than A3C, important for practical applications.  
- Clarify in abstract/introduction that separate policies are learned for each auxiliary task.  
- Quantify extra computational cost (training speed loss) and identify the most expensive auxiliary components.  
- Feature-control auxiliary task lacks strong empirical validation; Figure 5 is unconvincing and raises stability/convergence concerns.  
- Report longer training runs since agent performance was still improving at the end of reported training.  
- Explain why auxiliary-task weights (λ*) were fixed at 1 instead of being treated/validated as hyperparameters.  
- Explicitly state auxiliary tasks are trained off-policy (not true Q-learning because of multi-step empirical rewards).  
- Equation corrections needed: λ_c placement in Eq.1, r_t^(c)→r_{t+k}^{(c)} below Eq.1, and define L_PC referenced in Eq.2.  
- Fix figure and reference issues: reference Figure 2; cite Figure 1(d) in Section 3.3; clarify "Clip" in Figure 3 legend and that legend percentages refer to Labyrinth; correct mislabeled plots and Figure 5 caption/text mismatch.  
- Minor edits: grammar ("are shared"), punctuation, specify supplementary materials location, and explicitly report λ_PC.  
- Provide additional ablations on Atari to verify per-component contributions mirror Labyrinth patterns.  
- Key questions for authors: quantify per-component computational cost; justify fixed auxiliary weights; provide stronger evidence for feature-control task; clarify off-policy training in main text; report final performance after longer training.
