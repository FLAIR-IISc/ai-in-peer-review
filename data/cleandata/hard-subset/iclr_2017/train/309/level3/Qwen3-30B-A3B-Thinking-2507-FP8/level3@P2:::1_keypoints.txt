- Introduces the UNREAL agent using auxiliary control and reward prediction tasks to improve representation learning in deep RL.  
- Auxiliary control tasks maximize pseudo-rewards (pixel changes and feature activations); reward prediction anticipates immediate rewards.  
- Integrates these auxiliary tasks with A3C to produce the UNREAL agent.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results: 880% mean human on Atari and 87% human-normalized on Labyrinth, with ~10× faster learning.  
- Well-motivated hypothesis that controlling sensorimotor features yields more useful representations than traditional approaches.  
- Decision: Accept — significant contribution demonstrating improved performance and data efficiency.  
- Strong empirical validation: comprehensive ablation studies and robustness analysis across hyperparameters.  
- Properly situated in prior work (Horde, UVFA, successor representations) with novel use of auxiliaries for representation learning.  
- Quantitative results: UNREAL outperforms A3C by ~45% on Labyrinth and substantially improves Atari performance.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible to deep RL researchers.  
- Additional feedback: need more detailed discussion of computational cost and absolute memory requirements (replay buffer, auxiliary networks) versus A3C.  
- Recommendation to release source code and provide a specific timeline for reproducibility.  
- Question: please provide concrete computational resource comparisons (GPU hours, memory) between UNREAL and A3C.  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to the deconvolutional auxiliary network architecture; would simpler architectures suffice?  
- Question: provide quantitative measures of robustness to hyperparameters (e.g., standard deviation across settings).
