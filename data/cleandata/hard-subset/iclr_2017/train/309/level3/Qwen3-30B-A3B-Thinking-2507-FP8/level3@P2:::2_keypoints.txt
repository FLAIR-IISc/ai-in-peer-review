- Introduces UNREAL: learns multiple pseudo-rewards with a shared representation using three auxiliary tasks (auxiliary control, reward prediction, value replay), yielding large gains (880% Atari, 87% Labyrinth).

- Decision: Accept — strong contribution with significant empirical improvements over prior work.

- Tackles sparse rewards by showing auxiliary control tasks produce better representations than reconstruction; ablations show each component helps and combined gives largest gains.

- Clarify abstract/introduction that each auxiliary task learns a separate policy (not one policy optimizing all rewards).

- Feature-control auxiliary task needs stronger empirical validation; Figure 5 results and training stability are unconvincing.

- Report the value of λ_PC explicitly (assumed 1) and discuss whether it should be tuned.

- Clarify the claim of using "true" Q-learning for auxiliary tasks versus using empirical environment rewards.

- Fix grammar: "the features discovered in this manner is shared" → "are shared."

- Correct equation formatting: λ_C should be inside the summation in Eq.1 and define L_PC referenced in Eq.2.

- Explicitly reference the location of supplementary materials.

- Clarify how features are shared with the primary LSTM (weight sharing, concatenation) and provide implementation details.

- Provide details on how off-policy experience replay affects stability and convergence of auxiliary tasks compared to on-policy training.

- Explain why feature-control was only slightly worse than pixel control and whether that difference is statistically significant.

- Clarify the "human-normalized scores" legend in Figure 3 (Labyrinth or Atari).
