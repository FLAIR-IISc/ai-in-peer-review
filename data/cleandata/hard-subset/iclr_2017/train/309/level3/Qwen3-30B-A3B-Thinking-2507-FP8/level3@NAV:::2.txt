

**Review**

This paper introduces UNREAL, an agent that improves feature learning in deep RL by training separate policies for multiple domain-independent auxiliary tasks (control, reward prediction, value replay) that share a CNN+LSTM feature representation. The authors demonstrate significant improvements in data efficiency and performance over A3C on Atari (880% expert human performance) and Labyrinth (87% expert human performance), with convincing results and clear presentation.

**Decision:** Accept. The paper makes a strong contribution to the field with well-motivated approach and scientifically rigorous results. The minor issues identified below are not fundamental to the core contribution.

**Supporting Arguments:** The paper tackles the important problem of improving data efficiency in deep RL through auxiliary tasks. The approach is well-placed in the literature, building on related work like UVFA and Horde while introducing novel mechanisms for shared representation learning. The empirical results are robust across multiple environments and hyperparameter settings, with clear evidence of the proposed method's superiority.

**Additional Feedback:** The paper would benefit from addressing the following points to enhance clarity and scientific rigor:

1. Quantify the computational cost (training speed loss) and identify which auxiliary components are most expensive.
2. Clarify in the abstract/intro that separate policies are learned for each auxiliary task (not a single policy optimizing all rewards).
3. Strengthen empirical validation of the feature-control task (Fig. 5 is unconvincing and raises stability concerns).
4. Report how far the best agents went (suggest training longer since performance was still improving).
5. Justify why auxiliary-task weights (λ_*) were fixed at 1 rather than treated as hyperparameters.
6. Explicitly mention that auxiliary tasks are trained off-policy (not with "true" Q-learning due to multi-step empirical rewards).
7. Fix minor technical issues: Eq.1 formatting (λ_c inside summation), Fig.2 not referenced, grammar fixes, and clarify figure references.

These are minor revisions that would substantially improve the paper's clarity and scientific rigor without affecting the core contribution. The results are compelling and the approach is promising for future work in deep RL.
