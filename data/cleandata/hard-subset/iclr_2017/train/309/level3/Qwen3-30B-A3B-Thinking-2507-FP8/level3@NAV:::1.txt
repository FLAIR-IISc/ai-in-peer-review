

# Review of "Unsupervised Reinforcement Learning"

The paper proposes UNREAL, an agent that learns multiple auxiliary tasks alongside the primary reinforcement learning objective. Specifically, it introduces auxiliary control tasks (maximizing pixel changes and network feature activations) and auxiliary reward prediction tasks to shape the agent's representation more effectively. The authors demonstrate that UNREAL significantly outperforms standard A3C on both Labyrinth (reaching 87% human performance, 10× faster learning) and Atari (880% human performance) across multiple benchmarks. The paper presents a well-structured argument for how these auxiliary tasks provide complementary learning signals that improve feature extraction and data efficiency.

I recommend acceptance. The paper makes a solid contribution to the field by demonstrating a practical extension to deep RL that consistently improves performance and data efficiency across diverse environments. The results are scientifically rigorous, with careful ablation studies showing the individual and combined benefits of each auxiliary task, and thorough evaluation across multiple hyperparameter settings.

The paper is well-written and clearly situates its contribution within the broader literature on auxiliary tasks in reinforcement learning. While the results aren't entirely unexpected (as relevant auxiliary tasks should aid feature shaping), the paper serves as an important proof-of-concept that validates this intuition with compelling empirical evidence.

For future improvements, I suggest including a more detailed analysis of the computational resources required to train UNREAL, as this would help readers assess the practical implications of the approach. Additionally, while the architecture is complex, the authors should strongly consider releasing their source code to facilitate reproducibility and further research.

I have two specific questions: 1) Could you provide more details on the computational resources (e.g., GPU hours, number of machines) required to train UNREAL on the Atari and Labyrinth benchmarks? 2) Will the authors release the source code for UNREAL, and if so, when? These details would help readers better understand the practical viability of the approach.
