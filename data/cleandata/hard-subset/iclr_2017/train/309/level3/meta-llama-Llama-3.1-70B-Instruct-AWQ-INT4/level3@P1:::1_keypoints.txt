- Proposes adding unsupervised auxiliary tasks to A3C, creating the UNREAL agent.
- Introduces two auxiliary task types: control tasks and reward prediction tasks.
- Control tasks learn separate policies for pseudo-rewards (e.g., pixel changes, network features).
- Reward prediction tasks predict immediate reward onset from recent history.
- UNREAL combines these auxiliary tasks with A3C to form a single agent.
- Shows significant improvements in performance and learning speed on Labyrinth and Atari.
- Thorough evaluation against A3C and several ablations, demonstrating faster learning and hyperparameter robustness.
- Reports more than double the final human-normalised mean performance of A3C on Labyrinth and state-of-the-art results on Atari.
- Reviewer recommends accept based on novelty, thorough evaluation, and strong results.
- Requests more information on computational resources required for training.
- Suggests clearer explanation of the overall UNREAL architecture and component interactions.
- Advises releasing source code to facilitate further research and development.
- Asks authors for details on training resources, component interactions/bottlenecks, and plans/timeline for code release.
