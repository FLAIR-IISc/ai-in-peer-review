**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

This paper proposes a novel approach to training reinforcement learning (RL) agents by incorporating auxiliary tasks to learn stronger feature representations. The authors introduce two types of pseudo-control tasks: control change in pixel intensity and control activation of latent features. Additionally, they add a supervised regression task to predict immediate reward after a sequence, learned offline via skewed replay sampling. The proposed UNREAL agent combines these auxiliary tasks with the A3C algorithm, resulting in improved performance and data efficiency on discrete-action-continuous-space tasks, including the challenging Labyrinth and Atari environments.

**Decision: Accept**

I decide to accept this paper based on two key reasons:

1. **Novelty and Impact**: The paper proposes a novel approach to training RL agents, which has the potential to significantly improve performance and data efficiency in complex environments. The results demonstrate a substantial improvement over state-of-the-art methods, making it a valuable contribution to the field.
2. **Sound Methodology and Empirical Results**: The paper presents a well-motivated approach, grounded in the literature, and provides extensive empirical results to support the claims. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the results, including ablation studies and comparisons to baseline methods.

**Supporting Arguments**
