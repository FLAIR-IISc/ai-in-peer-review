- Proposes UNREAL, a deep RL architecture combining A3C with unsupervised auxiliary tasks.
- Introduces auxiliary control and reward tasks to improve feature shaping and learning efficiency.
- Evaluates UNREAL on 3D Labyrinth and 57 Atari games, showing large improvements over A3C.
- Reports a mean 10× speedup in learning efficiency and a mean human-normalized score of 87% on Labyrinth.
- Finds UNREAL to be more robust to hyperparameter settings than A3C.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing a solid contribution.
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow for deep RL readers.
- Results are unsurprising but valuable as a proof-of-concept for combining auxiliary tasks with A3C.
- Requests details on computational resources (GPUs, training time, memory usage).
- Requests whether the authors will release source code and a link to the repository.
- Asks for more detail on how auxiliary tasks were chosen and selected.
- Asks how the optimal weighting terms for individual loss components were determined.
- Asks whether other auxiliary tasks (e.g., input reconstruction or feature prediction) were explored.
