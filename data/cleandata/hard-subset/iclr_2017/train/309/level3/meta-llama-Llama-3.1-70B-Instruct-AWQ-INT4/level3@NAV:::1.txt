**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper proposes a novel deep reinforcement learning (RL) agent, called UNREAL, that significantly outperforms the state-of-the-art A3C algorithm on both the Labyrinth and Atari game platforms. The UNREAL agent is designed to learn separate policies for maximizing many other pseudo-reward functions simultaneously, in addition to the primary reward function. These auxiliary tasks are used to develop a more effective representation of the environment, which is shared across all tasks. The authors demonstrate that the UNREAL agent achieves a mean speedup of 10x in learning and reaches a mean human-normalized score of 87% on Labyrinth, and 880% on Atari.
