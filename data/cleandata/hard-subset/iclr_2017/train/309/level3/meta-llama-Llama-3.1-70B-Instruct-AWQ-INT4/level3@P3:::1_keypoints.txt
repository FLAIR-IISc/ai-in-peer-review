- Proposes incorporating unsupervised auxiliary tasks into A3C to improve deep RL.
- Introduces two auxiliary task types: control tasks (maximize pseudo-rewards) and reward tasks (predict immediate reward onset).
- Presents the UNREAL agent combining auxiliary tasks, showing significant gains in performance and learning speed over A3C.
- Paper is well-written and clear for deep RL experts, with a clear explanation of architecture and training.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and convincing, covering Labyrinth and Atari environments.
- Reviewer accepts the paper, citing solid contribution and well-motivated approach.
- Criticism: results are not entirely surprising since auxiliary tasks can be expected to aid feature shaping; paper serves as proof-of-concept.
- Requests additional details on computational resources and specific hardware/software used for training (32 asynchronous threads with shared RMSprop mentioned).
- Asks whether the authors will release the UNREAL source code to aid reproducibility and further work.
- Suggests exploring other types of auxiliary tasks beyond control and reward tasks to assess broader impact.
