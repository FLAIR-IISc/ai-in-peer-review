- Proposes training RL agents with auxiliary tasks to learn stronger feature representations.
- Introduces two pseudo-control tasks: control of pixel intensity and control of latent feature activations.
- Adds an offline supervised regression task to predict immediate reward using skewed replay sampling to balance reward/no-reward.
- Empirical results: strong performance on discrete-action, continuous-space tasks and reaches baseline in ~10× fewer iterations.
- Recommendation: accept — approach is novel, leverages agent influence as a learning signal, and is well presented.
- Methodology is generally sound; authors acknowledge A3C vs UNREAL hyperparameter differences and use top-3 reporting to mitigate bias.
- Main weakness: auxiliary tasks are described at a high level; more low-level implementation detail (e.g., introduce L_PC earlier) is needed.
- Need more experimental analysis isolating the auxiliary tasks themselves; pixel/feature control seems most impactful and deserves deeper study.
- Request 1: provide detailed implementation of auxiliary tasks including architectures and hyperparameters.
- Request 2: analyze how auxiliary tasks affect learning dynamics and identify key success factors.
- Request 3: report performance breakdowns for individual auxiliary tasks (pixel control vs feature control) and their contributions.
- Request 4: discuss plans/extensions for applying the approach to more complex tasks and environments.
- Additional suggestions: add more visualizations/illustrations, expand discussion of implications and relation to prior work, and include fuller experimental setup and robustness analyses.
