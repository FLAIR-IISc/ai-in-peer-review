**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel deep reinforcement learning architecture, UNREAL, which combines the benefits of two state-of-the-art approaches: Asynchronous Advantage Actor-Critic (A3C) and unsupervised auxiliary tasks. The authors introduce auxiliary control and reward tasks to improve feature shaping and learning efficiency. The UNREAL agent is evaluated on the challenging 3D Labyrinth environment and 57 Atari games, achieving significant improvements over the A3C baseline.

**Decision**

I recommend accepting this paper, with two key reasons for this choice:

1. **Solid contribution**: The paper proposes a novel architecture that combines unsupervised auxiliary tasks with A3C, demonstrating significant improvements in learning efficiency and performance on challenging environments.
2. **Well-written and easy to follow**: The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach, making it easy to follow for readers with deep RL expertise.

**Supporting Arguments**

The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, comparing the UNREAL agent to the A3C baseline on two challenging environments: Labyrinth and Atari. The results show that the UNREAL agent performs significantly better than A3C, achieving a mean speedup of 10× in learning efficiency and a mean human-normalized score of 87% on Labyrinth. The authors also demonstrate that the UNREAL agent is more robust to hyperparameter settings than A3C.

While the results are not surprising, as relevant auxiliary tasks should aid feature shaping, the paper serves as a proof-of-concept, demonstrating the effectiveness of combining unsupervised auxiliary tasks with A3C. The authors provide a clear explanation of their approach, making it easy to follow for readers with deep RL expertise.
