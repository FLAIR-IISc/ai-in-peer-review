- Proposes UNREAL, an RL agent that learns auxiliary pseudo-reward tasks to build shared representations, claiming 10× learning speedup and strong human-normalized scores (87% Labyrinth, 880% Atari) versus A3C.
- Decision: Accept — paper addresses a well-motivated problem and clearly demonstrates substantial gains over A3C.
- Supporting: Clear, thorough presentation of architecture, training, experiments, and analysis; effective results on Labyrinth and Atari; comprehensive related work and limitations discussion.
- Requests more details on computational resources used for training (GPUs/CPUs/memory).
- Suggests releasing source code to facilitate reproducibility and follow-up work.
- Asks for detailed description of hyperparameter tuning (ranges and selection method).
- Requests specifics on auxiliary tasks: what pseudo-rewards were used and why.
- Asks how UNREAL would extend to more complex settings (multi-agent or dynamic environments).
- Requests details of the experience replay mechanism (buffer size and sampling strategy).
