- Proposes UNREAL: domain-independent auxiliary tasks sharing CNN+LSTM to improve feature learning in deep RL, achieving SOTA on Atari and Labyrinth.
- Approach is well-motivated, combining insights from reinforcement learning and unsupervised learning.
- Empirical results are convincing: UNREAL outperforms A3C on Atari and Labyrinth.
- Ablation studies are provided to show each auxiliary task’s contribution.
- Paper recommended for acceptance due to strong empirical gains and broad applicability.
- Lacks clear quantification of extra computational cost from auxiliary tasks (training speed loss and expensive components).
- Feature-control auxiliary task has weak empirical validation; Figure 5 results are not fully convincing.
- Recommend training longer to see continued improvements and report distance to optimal performance.
- Need explanation why auxiliary-task weights (λ*) were fixed at 1 instead of being tuned or validated.
- Request more ablation studies on Atari to verify per-component contributions match Labyrinth patterns.
- Clarify that percentages in Figure 3’s legend refer to Labyrinth (text is unclear).
- Minor correction: Equation 1 formatting — λc should be inside the summation.
- Minor correction: Figure 3 legend should explain what "Clip" means for dueling networks.
- Ask authors to quantify extra computational cost, justify fixed λ* values, provide more evidence for feature-control, report best-agent optimality, and add Atari ablations.
- Suggest more discussion of how auxiliary tasks influence the main policy’s representation learning.
- Encourage exploration of other auxiliary task types to improve feature learning.
- Recommend analysis of UNREAL’s robustness to hyperparameter settings and sensitivity.
