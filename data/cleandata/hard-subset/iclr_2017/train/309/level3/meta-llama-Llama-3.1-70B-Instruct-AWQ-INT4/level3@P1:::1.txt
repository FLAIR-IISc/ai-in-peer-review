This paper proposes a novel approach to deep reinforcement learning (RL) by introducing unsupervised auxiliary tasks to the Asynchronous Advantage Actor-Critic (A3C) algorithm. The authors design two types of auxiliary tasks: control tasks and reward prediction tasks. The control tasks aim to learn separate policies for maximising different pseudo-reward functions, such as pixel changes and network features. The reward prediction tasks focus on predicting the onset of immediate reward given some historical context. The proposed UNREAL agent combines these auxiliary tasks with the A3C algorithm and demonstrates significant improvements in performance and learning speed on both the Labyrinth and Atari environments.

The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, comparing the UNREAL agent with the A3C algorithm and several ablated versions of UNREAL. The results show that the UNREAL agent achieves more than twice the final human-normalised mean performance of A3C on the Labyrinth environment and surpasses the current state-of-the-art agents on the Atari environment. Additionally, the UNREAL agent learns faster and is more robust to hyperparameter settings.

Overall, this paper is a solid conference contribution that provides a well-written and easy-to-follow explanation of the proposed approach. The results are convincing, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their method.

I decide to **accept** this paper, with the main reasons being the novelty of the approach, the thorough evaluation, and the significant improvements in performance and learning speed.

The supporting arguments for this decision are:

* The proposed approach is novel and well-motivated, building upon the idea of using auxiliary tasks to improve RL performance.
* The evaluation is thorough, comparing the UNREAL agent with several baselines and ablated versions of UNREAL.
* The results are significant, demonstrating improvements in performance and learning speed on both the Labyrinth and Atari environments.

Additional feedback to improve the paper:

* The overall architecture of the UNREAL agent is quite complicated, and it would be helpful if the authors could provide a more detailed explanation of how the different components interact.
* The authors should consider releasing the source code for the UNREAL agent to facilitate further research and development.
