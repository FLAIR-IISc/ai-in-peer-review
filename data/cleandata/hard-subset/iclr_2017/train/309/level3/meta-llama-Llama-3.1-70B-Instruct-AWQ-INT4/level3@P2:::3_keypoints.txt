- Proposes UNREAL: train RL agents with auxiliary tasks (pixel control, feature control) plus a supervised reward-prediction task to learn stronger representations.
- Introduces two pseudo-control tasks (control of pixel intensity and control of latent feature activations) and an offline skewed-replay reward regression.
- Combines auxiliary tasks with primary policy learning, yielding improved performance and data efficiency on Labyrinth and Atari.
- Recommendation: accept.
- Reason 1: Novel, well-motivated approach leveraging the agent’s ability to influence the environment as a self-supervised signal.
- Reason 2: Strong empirical results—state-of-the-art on Labyrinth and Atari and faster convergence than baselines.
- Supporting: Clear motivation that reconstruction/model-based unsupervised methods may fail in partially observed settings.
- Supporting: Auxiliary tasks provide complementary, controllable learning signals to policy learning.
- Supporting: Thorough empirical analysis including ablations and baseline comparisons.
- Suggestion: Provide more low-level implementation details for the pixel and feature control architectures.
- Suggestion: Include deeper analysis of auxiliary tasks (visualizations of learned features, metrics beyond RL performance, effects on exploration).
- Suggestion: Report hyperparameter tuning procedures and sensitivity analyses, especially differences between A3C and UNREAL.
- Question: How are auxiliary-task losses weighted/combined with the policy objective, and how sensitive are results to these weights?
- Question: How do auxiliary tasks affect exploration, particularly early in training?
- Question: Can you provide visualizations/analysis of the learned feature representations for pixel and feature control?
- Question: How well do the results generalize to other environments and different observation/action types?
