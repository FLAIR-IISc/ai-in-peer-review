- Proposes novel auxiliary-task approach (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction) to learn stronger representations for RL agents.
- Introduces pixel control and feature control trained off-policy, plus an auxiliary reward prediction task to focus on extrinsic rewards.
- UNREAL algorithm combines these auxiliary tasks with primary policy learning, improving performance and data efficiency.
- Empirical results show UNREAL outperforms state-of-the-art on Atari and yields significant improvements on Labyrinth.
- Provides extensive experiments and ablation studies demonstrating effectiveness of each auxiliary task and their combination.
- Strength: clear presentation of the UNREAL algorithm, auxiliary tasks, experience replay, and optimization process.
- Weakness: missing low-level details on auxiliary tasks in the main text (e.g., L_PC introduced only in appendix).
- Methodology broadly sound; authors acknowledge hyperparameter differences between A3C and UNREAL and use top-3 reporting to mitigate this.
- Reviewer requests more analysis on robustness of results to hyperparameter choices (learning rate, entropy cost).
- Weakness: lack of deeper experimental analysis of the auxiliary tasks themselves and metrics beyond RL performance.
- Suggested improvement: provide more architecture and optimization details for the auxiliary tasks.
- Suggested improvement: include thorough robustness analysis to hyperparameter choices.
- Suggested improvement: discuss potential applications and extensions beyond Atari and Labyrinth.
- Questions for authors: details on auxiliary-task architectures/optimization; plans for extending UNREAL to continuous or high-dimensional environments; robustness analysis to hyperparameters; applicability to real-world problems (robotics, autonomous driving).
