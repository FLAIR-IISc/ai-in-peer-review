- Proposes an agent that learns multiple pseudo-rewards simultaneously with a shared representation that adapts to extrinsic tasks.  
- Introduces a mechanism to focus representation on extrinsic rewards for rapid task adaptation.  
- Evaluated on Atari and Labyrinth, showing state-of-the-art results, improved data efficiency, and hyperparameter robustness.  
- Decision: accept.  
- Key reason 1: novel, well-motivated approach to improving feature learning in deep RL.  
- Key reason 2: strong experimental improvements in data efficiency and robustness over A3C.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of auxiliary control tasks, reward prediction, and value replay.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive across Atari and Labyrinth and convincingly supports claims.  
- Request to quantify extra computational cost and training-speed impact of auxiliary tasks.  
- Ask to clarify that separate policies are learned for each auxiliary task (not a single policy).  
- Request stronger empirical validation for the feature-control auxiliary task addressing stability and convergence.  
- Suggest training agents longer to report extended performance trajectories.  
- Ask to justify fixed auxiliary weights (lambda_*) or validate them as hyperparameters experimentally.  
- Note that auxiliary tasks are trained off-policy and not with true Q-learning due to multi-step empirical rewards; request explicit mention.  
- Minor corrections requested: formatting, grammar, figure references, captions, and explicit figure annotations.  
- Ask for more ablation studies on Atari to compare per-component contributions to Labyrinth results.  
- Request clarification that percentages in Figure 3's legend refer to Labyrinth and to correct figure reference mismatches.  
- Ask to report the value of lambda_PC (assumed 1) explicitly.  
- Request location and specification of supplementary materials in the appendix.  
- Questions for authors: details on computational cost impact, stability/convergence fixes for feature-control, validation of fixed auxiliary weights, plans to extend to other domains, and implementation details/effects of the reward prediction task.
