- Proposes adding auxiliary tasks to improve feature learning in deep reinforcement learning and introduces a "Read-Again" model that reads input twice for better word representations.  
- Decision: paper rejected.  
- Primary reason for rejection: no clear analysis of the extra computational cost and impact on training speed from optimizing auxiliary tasks.  
- Secondary reason for rejection: the "feature control" idea lacks empirical validation and sufficient experimental evidence.  
- Strength: the approach is interesting and aims to improve data efficiency via auxiliary tasks.  
- Weakness: missing thorough analysis of computational overhead that could negate benefits.  
- Weakness: insufficient experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of feature control across scenarios.  
- Requested improvement: provide detailed analysis of computational cost and its training-speed implications.  
- Requested improvement: run more experiments to empirically validate feature control and show effectiveness in varied settings.  
- Requested improvement: clarify the relationship and interaction between auxiliary tasks and the main policy optimization.  
- Reviewer questions: provide specifics on computational cost and training-speed impact, proposed experiments to validate feature control, and insights into how auxiliary tasks interact with policy optimization.
