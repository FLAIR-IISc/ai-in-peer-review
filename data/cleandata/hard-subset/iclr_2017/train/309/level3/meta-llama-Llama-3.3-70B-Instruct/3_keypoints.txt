- Proposes a Read-Again encoder and a copy mechanism for sequence-to-sequence text summarization.
- Read-Again model reads the input twice to better capture each word’s meaning in full-sentence context.
- Copy mechanism enables generation of out-of-vocabulary words by copying from the input.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on Gigaword and DUC2004 benchmarks.
- Decision: accept—method is well-motivated and experimentally effective.
- Paper is clearly presented with detailed descriptions of the Read-Again model and copy mechanism.
- Provides thorough experimental analysis, including how the copy mechanism reduces decoder vocabulary and improves performance.
- Situates the work appropriately within existing sequence-to-sequence and summarization literature.
- Suggests further analysis of the Read-Again model, especially the impact of varying the number of reading passes.
- Recommends exploring application to other sequence-to-sequence tasks (e.g., machine translation) and handling larger inputs (documents/articles).
- Requests more examples and analysis of the types of words most commonly copied by the copy mechanism.
- Asks authors to clarify plans for scaling to larger inputs, insights on commonly-copied word types, and extensions to other tasks.
