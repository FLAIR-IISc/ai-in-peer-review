- Proposes a novel "Read-Again" model that reads the input twice to form improved word representations for seq2seq tasks.
- Introduces a principled copy mechanism to handle out-of-vocabulary words, reducing decoder vocabulary size and speeding inference.
- Approach is well-motivated, inspired by human reading behavior, and addresses limitations of standard encoder-decoder models.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on Gigaword and DUC2004, which is a key reason for acceptance.

- Provides visualizations of importance weights and examples illustrating the copy mechanism in action.
- Outperforms existing methods despite using a significantly reduced decoder vocabulary.
