- Proposal: UNREAL augments A3C with unsupervised auxiliary tasks (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction, value replay) to shape a shared representation.  
- Architecture/training: Auxiliary control tasks use off-policy n-step Q-learning with replay; combined loss balances on-policy A3C and off-policy auxiliary objectives.  
- Design details: Shared CNN/LSTM state, deconvolutional Q-heads for spatial control, and prioritized-like replay for reward prediction.  
- Conceptual framing: Reframes unsupervised RL as predicting/controlling sensorimotor features rather than reconstruction.  
- Empirical results (Labyrinth): On 13 3D tasks, achieves 87% human-normalized performance and ~10× data-efficiency vs A3C.  
- Empirical results (Atari): On 57 games reports 880% mean and 250% median human-normalized performance, outperforming A3C and Prioritized Dueling DQN.  
- Robustness: Shows improved hyperparameter robustness and consistent gains across tasks.  
- Ablations: Each auxiliary task contributes; control-based auxiliaries outperform reconstruction and immediate-pixel-change prediction.  
- Positioning: Novelty is incremental but noteworthy for using many auxiliary control tasks purely for representation shaping and integrating them with an on-policy recurrent agent plus off-policy updates.  
- Decision: Accept — justified by strong, broad empirical gains and a well-motivated integration likely to influence practice.  
- Motivation: Sparse extrinsic rewards hinder representation learning; pseudo-rewards capturing controllable structure are well motivated.  
- Methodological soundness: Combined loss and off-policy auxiliary training from a single stream are reasonable and appropriate.  
- Evidence strength: Large experimental scope, multiple seeds/sweeps, and ablations strengthen claims despite predictability of the headline.  
- Clarity: Paper is well written with implementation details provided.  
- Limitation — novelty: Conceptual novelty is incremental relative to prior RL frameworks.  
- Limitation — complexity: Architecture has many components/hyperparameters, which may hinder reproducibility.  
- Limitation — reporting: Comparisons and compute budgets need more complete documentation for fairness.  
- Reproducibility request: Release code, default hyperparameters, task weights, normalization and buffer settings, and a minimal reference implementation.  
- Compute transparency request: Report wall-clock time, hardware, number of actors, frames per run, and energy usage where feasible.  
- Evaluation detail request: Specify Atari protocols (no-op/human starts, life-loss termination, action repeat/sticky actions) and provide per-game mean±std and confidence intervals.  
- Off-policy concerns: Discuss absence of importance sampling, stability of auxiliary Q-updates, loss balancing, and sensitivity to replay size/rebalancing.  
- Negative transfer/analysis request: Analyze when auxiliaries hurt, sensitivity to λPC/reward scaling, feature visualizations, and FPS/wall-clock overhead vs gains.  
- Broader baselines: Compare to stronger self-supervised/intrinsic control baselines (predictive coding, forward models).  
- Questions for authors: Request details on compute resources, code release timeline, hyperparameter fairness, replay/rebalancing sensitivity, loss normalization/instability, Atari evaluation protocol, feature-control layer choice, cross-task transfer, and failure cases.  
- Final stance: Reviewer unchanged after rebuttal — strengths remain compelling; requested clarifications would further strengthen the work.
