- Proposal: UNREAL augments on-policy A3C+LSTM with off-policy auxiliary tasks to learn stronger, task-relevant representations.
- Auxiliary control tasks: pixel control and feature control policies trained off-policy via n-step Q-learning that share the main representation.
- Reward-focused auxiliary task: supervised immediate-reward predictor trained from replay with skewed sampling to densify reward signals.
- Value-function replay: additional value regression updates from replay to exploit newly shaped features.
- Combined objective: L_UNREAL = L_A3C + λ_VR L_VR + λ_PC Σ_c L_Q^(c) + λ_RP L_RP.
- Empirical gains: ~10× faster to A3C’s best on 13 Labyrinth levels and substantially higher final returns (87% vs 54% human-normalized).
- Atari results: outperforms prior state-of-the-art across 57 games with robustness across hyperparameter choices.
- Framing: positions controllable auxiliary tasks as complementary to model-based and passive unsupervised objectives, focusing on controllability under partial observability.
- Decision: Accept — justified by strong empirical improvements and a clear, novel auxiliary-control framing.
- Problem addressed: accelerating and stabilizing deep RL in visually rich, partially observed, sparse-reward environments via auxiliary signals.
- Literature placement: clearly contrasted with reconstruction/prediction and model-based work; related to Horde/UVFAs but differs in auxiliary use for representation shaping.
- Empirical support: ablations indicate pixel control, reward prediction, and value replay each meaningfully contribute to improvements.
- Method details: replay sizes, off-policy updates, deconvolutional heads, and skewed sampling are reported and training appears stable.
- Clarity suggestion: define and explain L_PC and pixel-control/feature-control architectures earlier in the main text for reproducibility.
- Diagram request: provide a compact diagram showing which components are trained on-policy vs off-policy and which weights are shared.
- Ablation request (pixel control): deeper study varying grid resolution, reward scaling, deconv capacity, and comparisons to immediate-change prediction/reconstruction.
- Ablation request (feature control): extend beyond preliminary results to identify which layers/units help and how controlled-layer choice affects performance.
- Representation analysis: add metrics beyond returns (linear probes, controllability scores, mutual information with future rewards, value prediction error).
- Reporting robustness: include mean±SEM over seeds/hyperparameters and wall-clock runtime comparisons to account for extra computation.
- Sensitivity analysis: expand tuning for λ_PC, λ_RP, λ_VR and report failure modes, e.g., when off-policy Q-learning destabilizes shared representations.
- Generality: discuss extension to continuous action spaces and whether auxiliary Q-heads scale or actor-critic-style auxiliaries are preferable.
- Transfer: evaluate whether learned representations transfer across related tasks or Labyrinth levels.
- Questions for authors: pixel-control defaults and sensitivity; feature-control layer/unit selection and normalization; replay skew sensitivity and prioritization by TD-error.
- Questions for authors (stability & compute): any interference between off-policy Q and on-policy actor-critic, stability tricks used (target networks/update schedules), and wall-clock overhead vs A3C.
- Questions for authors (test-time use): are auxiliary policies/heads used at test time and does disabling them affect performance?
- Overall conclusion: clear, novel, practical contribution to RL representation learning with strong empirical evidence; recommendation to accept.
