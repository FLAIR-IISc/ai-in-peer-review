Summary
The paper proposes UNREAL, an A3C-based deep RL agent that augments feature learning with domain-independent auxiliary tasks: (i) pixel-control and feature-control auxiliary policies trained off-policy via n-step Q-learning; (ii) reward prediction to desparsify extrinsic signals; and (iii) value replay. These tasks share the CNN+LSTM representation with the base policy. On Atari and DeepMind Lab/Labyrinth, UNREAL substantially improves data efficiency and final performance over A3C, with strong writing and convincing experiments.

Decision: Accept
Reason: The method is simple, well-motivated, and delivers large, robust gains in both learning speed and final score across two challenging benchmarks.

Supporting arguments
- Clear conceptual motivation: leveraging abundant pseudo-rewards to shape representation, while keeping the base policy on-policy and unbiased.
- Strong empirical evidence: large mean/median gains on Atari (including robustness across hyperparameters) and 10× average data-efficiency gains on Labyrinth; ablations demonstrate the value of the components.
- Sound integration: parallel on-policy A3C with off-policy replay for auxiliary tasks; shared features drive practical benefits without complex algorithmic changes.

Suggestions and questions to improve the paper (helpful, not all decision-critical)
- Compute cost: please quantify wall-clock training speed vs A3C (throughput, GPU-hours), and break down which auxiliary components are most expensive.
- Clarify early (abstract/intro) that separate policies are learned for each auxiliary control task, not a single policy optimizing all rewards.
- Feature-control validation: Fig. 5 is unconvincing; provide stronger evidence, discuss stability as the controlled features evolve, and any convergence issues.
- Training horizon: many curves are still improving; train longer and report how far the best agents go.
- Hyperparameters: explain why λVR, λRP (and λPC) are fixed to 1 (or to the sampled ranges); discuss sensitivity or include validation. Report the exact λPC used per domain and the value of λPC in Eq. 2 explicitly.
- Off-policy details: state clearly that auxiliary tasks are trained off-policy and that the n-step targets are multi-step bootstrapped, i.e., not “true” 1-step Q-learning.
- Notational/formatting fixes: Lπ is a maximization term but shown as a loss; Eq. 1 should place λC inside the summation; r(c)t:t+n should use r(c)t+k; Eq. 2 references LPC—ensure it is explicitly defined; grammar: “features … are shared.”
- Figures: reference Fig. 2 in text; reference Fig. 1(d) in Sec. 3.3; correct Figure 3 left/right/top annotations; clarify that Figure 3 percentages refer to Labyrinth; explain “Clip” for dueling networks; Figure 5 claims three levels but shows two—please reconcile.
- Ablations: include Atari ablations per component to check if contributions mirror Labyrinth patterns.
- Minor: missing comma after “methods” (Sec. 4.2); specify where supplementary materials are located.
