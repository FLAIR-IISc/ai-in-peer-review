- Proposal: UNREAL augments A3C with domain-independent auxiliary tasks to shape a shared CNN+LSTM representation for better data efficiency and performance.
- Auxiliary tasks: off-policy control heads for pseudo-rewards (pixel-control, feature-control), reward-prediction from short histories, and value-function replay.
- Training: joint optimization of combined loss with on-policy A3C updates and off-policy replay for auxiliary heads.
- Empirical results: large gains on 13 Labyrinth levels (mean ~10× speedup) and improved mean/median human-normalized scores and robustness on 57 Atari games versus A3C and Prioritized Dueling DQN.
- Decision: Accept — idea is clear, simple to apply, and supported by broad empirical evidence and ablations.
- Main strengths: well-motivated representation-learning approach, strong cross-benchmark improvements, and appropriate methodology.
- Main weaknesses: limited empirical validation of feature-control relative to pixel-control and lack of computational cost / wall-clock overhead analysis.
- Clarify that auxiliary control trains separate Q-heads/policies per pseudo-reward which are not executed at test time.
- Explicitly state that auxiliary control uses off-policy n-step Q-learning from replay and discuss stability considerations.
- Discuss non-stationarity of feature-control targets (changing hidden activations) and any stabilizing measures used (e.g., target networks or slower updates).
- Quantify computational overhead: memory/compute cost of auxiliary heads and replay, and wall-clock slowdown vs A3C.
- Report wall-clock training speedups/slowdowns in addition to sample-efficiency gains.
- Run longer training curves on representative tasks to demonstrate asymptotic behavior.
- Treat λRP, λVR, λPC (and per-task λc) as explicit hyperparameters; report chosen values and sensitivity analyses.
- Discuss whether reward-skewed replay biases the shared representation and how this was mitigated.
- Provide Atari ablations analogous to Labyrinth to verify per-component contributions generalize.
- For feature-control, supply stronger evidence (more tasks, stability metrics, or visualizations) given limited Fig. 5 results.
- Justify replay design choices: buffer size (2k steps), auxiliary update cadence, and consider sensitivity or alternatives (larger buffers, prioritized replay).
- Explain pixel-control reward normalization across Atari games and sensitivity to that normalization.
- Notational and typo fixes: clarify λ placement in Eq.(1), use r(t+k)^(c), consistent A3C sign conventions, consistent Eq.(2) notation, and correct figure references/captions.
- Fix figure-specific issues: explain “Clip” in dueling context, correct Fig. 5 caption/content mismatch, and clarify legend percentages.
- Minor grammar/presentation fixes and indicate where supplementary materials are available.
- Questions for authors: what is UNREAL’s wall-clock overhead vs A3C and which auxiliaries dominate cost?
- Questions for authors: exact hyperparameter values for λRP, λVR, λPC and sensitivity to per-task weighting?
- Questions for authors: were there stability issues with off-policy n-step Q-learning and were techniques like target networks, different n, or importance corrections tried?
- Questions for authors: how stable are feature-control heads with moving targets and were lagging targets or other tricks used?
- Questions for authors: can you provide Atari per-component ablations and checks that reward-prediction replay doesn’t harm exploration/generalization?
- Questions for authors: do UNREAL and A3C converge to the same asymptote with longer training or does the gap persist/widen?
