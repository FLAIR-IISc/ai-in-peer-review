1. Summary and contributions
The paper proposes UNREAL, an A3C-based agent augmented with unsupervised auxiliary tasks: (i) auxiliary control via off-policy Q-learning for pixel/feature control, (ii) auxiliary reward prediction to reduce reward sparsity in representation learning, and (iii) value replay. These tasks share a representation with the policy/value LSTM and are trained from a small replay buffer. On 3D Labyrinth and 57 Atari games, UNREAL substantially improves both final performance and data efficiency over A3C (e.g., ~10× speedup on Labyrinth; ~87% human on Labyrinth; ~880% mean human on Atari), and shows improved robustness to hyperparameters. The paper is clearly written and well organized, with ablations indicating the impact of each auxiliary component.

2. Decision: Accept
Key reasons: (1) Strong empirical gains in two challenging benchmarks with careful ablations and robustness analysis. (2) A well-motivated, broadly applicable idea—using relevant auxiliary tasks to shape representations in deep RL—executed convincingly.

3. Supporting arguments
- Problem: How to exploit unsupervised signals to improve representation learning and sample efficiency in deep RL under sparse rewards.
- Motivation and placement: Well grounded in prior work (options, Horde, UVFA, successor representations, model prediction, prioritized replay). While the high-level idea is expected—that relevant auxiliary tasks aid feature shaping—the formulation and integration into an on-policy A3C agent with off-policy auxiliary training and replay is a solid and useful contribution.
- Evidence: Results are consistent, sizable, and include ablations (pixel control vs prediction vs reconstruction; reward prediction; value replay), robustness plots, and multiple environments. Methodological details are sufficient for expert readers, and claims are supported by empirical data.

4. Additional feedback (to help improve, not gating the decision)
- Please report compute: number/type of GPUs/CPUs, number of actor threads, total environment frames per experiment, wall-clock time to reach A3C’s final performance and to convergence, and the cost of auxiliary updates relative to baseline A3C.
- The architecture is fairly complex; code release (including training scripts and hyperparameter sweeps) would greatly aid reproducibility.
- Clarify sensitivity to λPC, λVR, λRP and interactions with entropy cost; a small hyperparameter sweep table would help.
- Discuss generalization: do pixel/feature control tasks help in reward-sparse, visually static games? Any negative transfer cases?
- Fairness: confirm tuning budgets for baselines vs UNREAL and whether A3C+replay or A3C+reward-pred-only were tuned equivalently.

Questions for authors
- Exact computational resources and wall-clock training times per domain? What is the overhead (%) of auxiliary heads and replay?
- How stable is training to the choice of replay buffer size and reward rebalancing for prediction?
- How do results change if auxiliary tasks are disabled after a burn-in phase?
- Will you release code and pre-trained models? If not, can you provide training logs and seeds?
- Post-rebuttal note: Responses were appreciated but did not change my overall assessment (accept).
