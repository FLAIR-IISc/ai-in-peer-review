- Proposes UNREAL: augment A3C with auxiliary tasks (pixel/feature control, reward prediction, value replay) sharing a visual encoder to improve representation learning and data efficiency.  
- Auxiliary control uses off-policy Q-learning for maximizing pixel changes or feature activations to produce task-relevant signals.  
- Reward prediction uses skewed replay to balance non-zero vs zero rewards and bias features toward reward-relevant information.  
- Value function replay performs extra value regression updates from recent experience to stabilize learning.  
- Losses from all tasks are combined into a single objective with shared encoder/LSTM for faster, more robust learning.  
- Empirical gains: large speedups and performance improvements on Labyrinth (mean human-normalized 54%→87%, ~10× data efficiency) and Atari (880% mean, 250% median).  
- Ablations show each auxiliary component (control, reward prediction, value replay) contributes and control > predictive reconstruction baselines.  
- Decision: Accept, based on strong, consistent empirical improvements and careful ablations across domains.  
- Novelty: Using multiple control tasks as auxiliary objectives trained off-policy with replay to shape representations is a clear contribution.  
- Experiments: Broad coverage (13 Labyrinth levels, 57 Atari games), ablations, predictive baseline comparisons, and hyperparameter robustness analyses.  
- Evidence supports claim that control-oriented auxiliary signals yield more task-relevant features than pure prediction/reconstruction.  
- Caveat: UNREAL uses extra replay-based updates absent in baseline A3C, complicating attribution between better signal vs more updates.  
- Caveat: Deeper isolated analysis of pixel/feature control (controllability metrics, sensitivity to grid/normalization) is missing.  
- Caveat: Some key component definitions and architectural details are relegated to the appendix, reducing clarity.  
- Suggestion: Report both environment steps and total gradient updates, and include a matched-update A3C baseline for fairness.  
- Suggestion: Expand pixel control analysis (grid resolution, cropping, reward normalization) and study failure modes (noisy visuals).  
- Suggestion: Provide more feature control results, compare controlling earlier vs later layers, and check redundancy with pixel control.  
- Suggestion: Quantify effects of reward prediction skewing and test different skew ratios or no skew to check for covariate shift.  
- Suggestion: Provide diagnostics on Q-learning stability (TD errors), replay buffer size/recency effects, and off-policy stability with recurrence.  
- Suggestion: Test generalization to continuous-control domains or other 3D platforms to assess transferability.  
- Suggestion: Add qualitative visualizations linking auxiliary Q-maps/feature activations to policy behavior and competence of auxiliary policies.  
- Questions: How many auxiliary/off-policy updates per environment step, wall-clock and update-normalized comparisons to A3C, and matched-update A3C results?  
- Questions: Sensitivity of pixel-control grid size/cropping/normalization and selection/placement of feature-control units; any gradient conflicts?  
- Questions: Impact of 50/50 reward skew on convergence and potential negative transfer through shared features?  
- Questions: Effects of replay buffer size/recency, use of prioritized replay, and mechanisms to prevent divergence in off-policy Q-learning with recurrence?  
- Questions: Are there domains where pixel control hurts (dynamic irrelevant textures), and how to adapt UNREAL to continuous actions or non-image observations?  
- Questions: Hyperparameter selection protocol for top-3 reporting, number of seeds per setting, and robustness across seeds/settings.  
- Overall: Strong, well-motivated paper with compelling empirical results; suggested clarifications and deeper analyses would further strengthen the work.
