- Introduces UNREAL: an A3C agent augmented with unsupervised auxiliary tasks (pixel/feature control and reward-focused tasks) to shape representation learning.  
- Proposes two auxiliary objective families: auxiliary control (pixel/feature control via off-policy n-step Q-learning on replay) and reward-focused tasks (reward prediction and value replay from a skewed replay buffer).  
- Shows large empirical gains on Labyrinth: UNREAL more than doubles A3C’s mean human-normalized performance (87% vs 54%) and ≈10× faster to reach A3C’s best.  
- Shows strong results on Atari: UNREAL outperforms A3C and Prioritized Dueling DQN (880% mean, 250% median human-normalized performance) and is more hyperparameter-robust.  
- Paper is clearly written, well organized, with ablations demonstrating pixel control benefits over reconstruction/change prediction and benefits from combining auxiliary tasks.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: strong empirical performance and data-efficiency improvements across domains with supporting ablations and robustness analyses.  
- Key reason: well-motivated idea that auxiliary tasks can shape useful features, offering a convincing large-scale proof-of-concept.  
- Problem tackled: accelerating and stabilizing deep RL by exploiting unsupervised sensorimotor signals beyond sparse extrinsic rewards.  
- Related work and motivation: well positioned with links to options/pseudo-rewards, Horde, UVFAs, successor representations, and recent auxiliary prediction work; motivates control-focused objectives.  
- Empirical rigor: extensive evaluation across 13 Labyrinth levels and 57 Atari games with ablations and hyperparameter sweeps, though some implementation/sensitivity details are underreported.  
- Request to report compute/resources and wall-clock training time, throughput, and environment frames to reproduce main results.  
- Suggest releasing code and training scripts due to architectural complexity and implementation details.  
- Questions about auxiliary design sensitivity: grid resolution, crop size, reward definition for pixel control; color vs grayscale; more feature-control ablations and Atari results.  
- Ask for loss-weight sensitivity analyses (λPC, λRP, λVR) and interactions with learning rate/entropy cost.  
- Request replay/off-policy details: update frequencies, target networks, replay buffer size impact, and whether prioritized replay was tried.  
- Concern about combining on-policy A3C with off-policy Q-learning: ask for diagnostics on instabilities (loss scales, gradient norms).  
- Robustness reporting request: per-game means and confidence intervals across seeds for fixed hyperparameters, and exploration metrics for Labyrinth.  
- Scope/generalization concerns: discuss failure cases (noisy/distractor visuals), negative transfer, and representation transfer to changed tasks.  
- Minor clarifications requested: preprocessing, frame-skip/stacking vs LSTM memory, action repeat for Atari, reward predictor normalization, and sticky-action/evaluation protocol.  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer appreciates responses but retains the accept decision; overall assessment unchanged.
