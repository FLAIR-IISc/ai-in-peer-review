- Proposal: UNREAL augments A3C with auxiliary control (pixel/feature control) and reward-prediction tasks plus value replay to shape representations for sparse, partially observable RL.
- Architecture: Shared convolutional and recurrent representation with on-policy A3C and off-policy Q-learning for auxiliary control heads and replayed value regression.
- Pixel-control: Off-policy pseudo-reward maximizing local pixel-intensity change on a spatial grid to encourage controllable visual changes.
- Feature-control: Off-policy pseudo-reward maximizing internal unit activations to shape internal representations.
- Reward prediction: Supervised immediate-reward prediction trained from replay with skewed sampling to overrepresent rewarding events.
- Empirical gains: Substantially faster learning and higher final performance on Labyrinth (≈10× data-efficiency; 87% human-normalized) and state-of-the-art on 57 Atari games (mean 880%, median 250%).
- Ablations: Each auxiliary component contributes; pixel/feature control are especially impactful.
- Robustness: Improved robustness to hyperparameters demonstrated with sweeps and reporting of top agents to reduce cherry-picking.
- Novelty/positioning: Conceptual shift from passive prediction to many control-based auxiliary tasks purely for representation shaping; distinctions made from Horde, UVFA, successor features, and model-based/reconstruction approaches.
- Methodological soundness: Coherent combination of on-policy A3C and off-policy auxiliary Q-learning; skewed replay densifies reward supervision without biasing main estimators.
- Empirical rigor: Extensive evaluation across 13 Labyrinth levels and 57 Atari games, with ablations and comparisons to reconstruction and immediate-change prediction.
- Acknowledged limitations: Domain-specific λPC tuning, some low-level details deferred to appendix, and need for deeper analyses of auxiliary contributions.
