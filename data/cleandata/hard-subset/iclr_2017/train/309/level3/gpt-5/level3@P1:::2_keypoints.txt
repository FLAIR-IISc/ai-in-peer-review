- Proposes UNREAL: A3C augmented with domain-independent auxiliary tasks (pixel control, feature control) sharing a CNN+LSTM to improve representation learning under sparse rewards.  
- Auxiliary control tasks: off-policy n-step Q-learning for separate policies that maximize local pixel-change and hidden-unit activations, trained with deconvolutional heads from a shared replay buffer.  
- Reward-focused auxiliaries: skewed-replay reward prediction (3-way immediate reward classification) and value-function replay to speed up value learning off-policy.  
- Unified loss: combines on-policy A3C updates with off-policy auxiliary updates to continue representation learning even without extrinsic rewards.  
- Strong empirical gains: ~10× speedup and 87% human-normalized on Labyrinth; state-of-the-art on 57 Atari games (880% mean, 250% median expert human performance).  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reasons for acceptance: consistent empirical improvements across domains with clear ablations, and a simple broadly applicable method to enhance RL representation learning.  
- Problem/motivation: addresses representation learning when extrinsic rewards are sparse by using pseudo-reward control/prediction objectives instead of reconstruction.  
- Placement in literature: well connected to Horde/UVFAs/successor representations/model prediction; novelty is off-policy auxiliary control purely to shape shared representation plus reward prediction/value replay.  
- Evidence and rigor: convincing empirical results across 13 Labyrinth levels and 57 Atari games, ablations show contributions of pixel control, reward prediction, and value replay; good implementation/evaluation detail.  
- Minor empirical weakness: limited validation of the feature-control task and desire for more Atari ablations per component.  
- Clarity/correctness edits requested: clarify at abstract/intro that auxiliary policies are separate and unused at test time; detail that Q-learning uses n-step targets from replay.  
- Notation/loss fixes: clarify policy loss sign, move λC inside summation or make task-specific, fix reward indexing, explicitly define LPC, and correct grammar.  
- Figure/caption fixes: add/correct figure references, unify left/right/top annotations, explain “Clip,” clarify legend percentages, fix caption/figure mismatches, add comma in Section 4.2, and point to appendix materials.  
- Experimental scope requests: quantify wall-clock/GPU/CPU overhead per auxiliary, report which tasks dominate compute, and provide longer-run/asymptotic performance.  
- Hyperparameter/sensitivity requests: justify or analyze λRP, λVR, λPC and replay buffer size, report λPC chosen per domain and sensitivity to reward-skew and annealing.  
- Ablation and stability requests: provide per-component Atari ablations, more evidence on feature-control stability/convergence, and discussion of co-evolving features and pseudo-rewards.  
- Questions for authors: quantify wall-clock slowdown and extra updates/FLOPs per auxiliary; sensitivity to λs, buffer size, and skew; evidence of instability from feature-control; utility on low pixel-dynamics games and normalization of pixel-change rewards; off-policy bias/divergence with n-step targets and any use of importance sampling/target networks; whether auxiliaries remain beneficial asymptotically; and whether auxiliary policies could be used as options for exploration.  
- Overall: significant, practically valuable contribution that leverages auxiliary control and reward prediction to improve deep RL data efficiency and robustness.
