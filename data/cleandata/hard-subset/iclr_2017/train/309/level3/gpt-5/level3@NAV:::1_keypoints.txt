- Proposes UNREAL: A3C augmented with unsupervised auxiliary tasks (pixel/feature control via off-policy Q-learning, reward prediction, value replay) sharing representation with policy/value LSTM.  
- Demonstrates large empirical gains and data-efficiency improvements over A3C on Labyrinth and Atari (e.g., ~10× speedup on Labyrinth; strong human-normalized scores).  
- Includes ablations and robustness analysis showing the impact of each auxiliary component.  
- Decision: Accept, based on strong empirical gains and convincing execution of a broadly applicable idea.  
- Problem framed: improve representation learning and sample efficiency in deep RL under sparse rewards using unsupervised signals.  
- Positioning: Well-grounded in prior work (options, Horde, UVFA, successor representations, model prediction, prioritized replay) and integrates off-policy auxiliary training into on-policy A3C.  
- Evidence: Consistent, sizable results across multiple environments with methodological details sufficient for experts.  
- Request to report compute: number/type of GPUs/CPUs, actor threads, total frames, wall-clock times to reach baselines and convergence, and auxiliary update cost.  
- Recommend releasing code, training scripts, and hyperparameter sweeps to aid reproducibility given architectural complexity.  
- Ask for clarification on hyperparameter sensitivity (λPC, λVR, λRP) and interactions with entropy cost, ideally via a small sweep table.  
- Suggest discussing generalization and potential negative transfer, especially in reward-sparse or visually static games.  
- Raise fairness concerns: confirm tuning budgets for baselines vs UNREAL and whether baseline variants were tuned equivalently.  
- Specific questions: exact computational resources and overhead percentage; stability w.r.t. replay buffer size and reward rebalancing; effect of disabling auxiliary tasks after a burn-in; and plans to release code, pretrained models, logs, and seeds.  
- Post-rebuttal: author responses were appreciated but did not change the accept decision.
