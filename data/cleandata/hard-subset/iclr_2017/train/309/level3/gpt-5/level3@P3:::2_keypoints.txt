- Proposal: UNREAL augments A3C with domain-independent auxiliary tasks (pixel/feature-control via Q-learning, reward prediction, value-function replay) sharing CNN+LSTM features and separate heads per auxiliary control task.
- Empirical gains: Large improvements in data efficiency and final performance on Labyrinth (~10× speedup, mean 87% human) and strong gains on Atari (mean ≈880% human, median ≈250%) with solid ablations.
- Clarity: Paper is well written, simple and well-motivated, with thorough architecture and training details and a single combined loss.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reasons for acceptance: strong, consistent empirical gains across two domains from a simple design; clear evidence auxiliary tasks improve feature learning in deep RL.
- Methodological placement: Situates work relative to A3C, DQN/replay, UVFA, successor representations, and Horde; novelty is in effective integration and shared representation with off-policy auxiliary training.
- Empirical strength: Labyrinth ablations isolate component effects; Atari results show improved performance and robustness over baselines.
- Reproducibility: Architecture/training details (CNN/LSTM, replay, pixel-control) are thorough and combined loss components are clear.
- Clarification requested: State prominently that separate auxiliary control policies/heads are learned rather than a single policy maximizing a weighted sum of rewards.
- Clarification requested: Explicitly note auxiliary control tasks are trained off-policy from replay with n-step targets (not 1-step Q-learning).
- Hyperparameter request: Report λPC values used for top results, justify fixing λVR and λRP = 1 vs tuning, and provide sensitivity analysis.
- Compute/memory request: Quantify UNREAL vs A3C costs (wall-clock slowdowns per env step/update, replay and heads memory, component-wise cost breakdown).
- Training horizon request: Extend training or report asymptotic/best-agent performance since many curves were still improving at time limit.
- Feature-control analysis: Evidence is limited; request stability/convergence analyses (e.g., frozen vs changing feature targets, annealing schedules).
- Atari ablations: Request more per-component and combination ablations on Atari to verify Labyrinth findings generalize.
- Robustness reporting: Clarify whether percentage improvements/robustness plots refer to Labyrinth or Atari and make figure captions explicit.
- Notation/equations corrections: Fix policy loss phrasing (loss vs maximization), place λC inside summation in Eq.1 and correct r(c)t:t+n notation, define LPC consistently in Eq.2 and text, and clarify replay episode/truncation handling for n-step targets.
- Figure and caption fixes: Reference Figure 2 in text, explicitly cite Fig.1(d) for value replay, clarify “Clip” in Figure 3 legend, correct left/right/top/bottom annotations, and ensure percentage labels refer to correct domain.
- Figure 5 mismatch: Caption claims “top 5” on three Labyrinth levels but shows only two—correct caption or include missing panel.
- Writing/formatting fixes: Grammar fixes (e.g., “features … are shared”), punctuation in Section 4.2, specify where supplementary materials are accessed, and clean malformed references.
- Questions for authors: report wall-clock slowdowns and which components dominate compute/memory per domain.
- Questions for authors: how sensitive are results to λVR and λRP; were they tuned, annealed, or normalized?
- Questions for authors: provide stronger empirical evidence and stability analyses for feature-control or downscale its claimed importance.
- Questions for authors: do Atari ablations corroborate Labyrinth component contributions?
- Questions for authors: for pixel-control, did you evaluate alternative grids, reward normalizations, or value-head designs and which choices matter most?
- Questions for authors: clarify what “Clip” denotes in the dueling baseline.
- Questions for authors: report exact λPC values used for best runs and summarize their distribution across seeds/games.
