1) Summary and contributions: The paper proposes an RL agent that augments policy learning with auxiliary objectives designed to learn stronger, more controllable feature representations. Two pseudo-control tasks are introduced: (a) pixel-control, which learns policies that maximize local changes in pixel intensity, and (b) feature-control, which learns policies that activate latent network features. In addition, the agent trains an immediate-reward prediction head offline using skewed replay to balance reward/no-reward events, and performs value-replay updates. The combined loss is optimized jointly, leveraging off-policy Q-learning for auxiliary control and on-policy A3C for the main policy. Empirically, the agent achieves large gains on discrete-action, visually rich 3D tasks and Atari, reaching baseline performance roughly 10x faster and substantially improving robustness to hyperparameters. The work is positioned as an alternative to passive unsupervised/model-based approaches by exploiting the agent’s ability to influence its inputs as a learning signal under partial observability.

2) Decision: Accept. Key reasons: (i) Clear, novel formulation of controllable auxiliary tasks that materially improve data efficiency and robustness; (ii) Strong empirical support across diverse benchmarks with careful reporting (top-3 across hyperparameters).

3) Supporting arguments: The motivation is well grounded in the literature (options, Horde, UVFA, depth/reward prediction), and the shift from passive prediction/reconstruction to controllable local/internal features is compelling. The methodology is sound: off-policy auxiliary control with shared representation, replay-based reward prediction with skewed sampling, and value replay are coherently integrated with A3C. Results on Labyrinth (≈10x sample-efficiency speedups) and Atari (substantial human-normalized improvements) support the central claims. Reporting across many seeds/hyperparameters, plus acknowledgment of A3C vs UNREAL differences, mitigates selection bias.

4) Suggestions to improve (not central to the decision): 
- Clarify and detail the auxiliary control losses earlier (define LPC, pixel-control rewards, deconv heads, and λPC in the main text rather than deferring to the appendix). 
- Provide a deeper, isolated analysis of the auxiliary tasks themselves. Pixel/feature control appear most impactful; quantify their contributions with diagnostics beyond RL return (e.g., controllability metrics, linear probe performance on downstream signals, representation mutual information, stability under off-policy updates).
- Discuss sensitivity to skew ratio in reward replay and to λVR/λPC/λRP.

Questions for authors:
- How sensitive are gains to λPC and the grid resolution for pixel control, and which layer is best for feature control?
- Does skewed replay for reward prediction improve sample efficiency uniformly, or mainly in sparse-reward regimes?
- What interference, if any, occurs between on-policy A3C updates and off-policy Q-learning on shared features?
- Can the approach extend to continuous-action settings, and does feature control retain its benefits there?
- Are auxiliary control policies ever used at execution (e.g., options) or solely for representation shaping?
