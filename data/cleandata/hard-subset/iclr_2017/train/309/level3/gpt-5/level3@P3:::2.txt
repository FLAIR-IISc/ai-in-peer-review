Summary of contributions
- The paper proposes UNREAL, an A3C-based agent augmented with domain-independent auxiliary tasks that share CNN+LSTM features: (i) auxiliary control via Q-learning (pixel-control and feature-control policies), (ii) auxiliary reward prediction to de-sparsify reward-related features, and (iii) value-function replay for additional value updates. Importantly, separate policies/heads are learned for each auxiliary control task, all sharing a common representation.
- The approach yields large gains in data efficiency and final performance on Labyrinth (≈10× speedup to A3C’s best, mean 87% human) and strong improvements and robustness on 57 Atari games (mean ≈880% human, median ≈250%), with convincing ablations (especially on Labyrinth).
- The paper is clearly written, the design is simple and well-motivated in the context of auxiliary tasks and off-policy replay for representation learning, and the empirical support is strong.

Decision: Accept
- Key reasons: (1) Strong, consistent empirical gains in data efficiency and final performance across two challenging domains with a simple, well-motivated design. (2) Clear demonstration that auxiliary tasks focusing on control and reward shaping can substantially improve feature learning in deep RL.

Supporting arguments
- Methodological placement: The work is well situated relative to A3C, DQN/replay, UVFA, successor representations, and Horde; the novelty is not the specific auxiliary ideas per se, but the effective integration and shared representation with off-policy training to enhance on-policy actor-critic.
- Empirical strength: The Labyrinth ablations isolate the impact of pixel-control, reward prediction, and value replay; the speedups and final scores are substantial. Atari results show both performance and robustness improvements over strong baselines.
- Clarity and reproducibility: Architecture and training details are thorough (e.g., CNN/LSTM specifics, replay settings, pixel-control head). A single combined loss is optimized with clear components.

Additional feedback and questions to improve the paper (not all affect my accept decision)
A. Clarifications and methodological details
- Separate policies: Please state prominently in the abstract/intro that separate auxiliary control policies/heads are learned (rather than one policy jointly maximizing a weighted sum of all rewards).
- Off-policy and multi-step Q-learning: Explicitly note that auxiliary control tasks are trained off-policy from replay with n-step targets (i.e., not 1-step “true” Q-learning), to avoid confusion.
- Auxiliary weights: In Eq. 2 you fix λVR = 1 and λRP = 1, and sample λPC; please (i) report the λPC values used for the reported top results per domain, (ii) justify fixing λVR and λRP to 1 versus treating them as tunable/validated hyperparameters, and (iii) provide sensitivity where feasible.
- Computational cost: Quantify added cost of UNREAL vs. A3C: (i) wall-clock training speed slowdowns (per environment step and per update), (ii) memory footprint of replay and extra heads, and (iii) component-wise cost breakdown (deconv pixel-control head, feature-control head, reward-prediction net, value replay), ideally with ablations.
- Training horizon: Many curves are still improving at the time limit. Please extend training (or at least report asymptotic/best-agent performance) to show whether gaps persist, and how far the best agents go on each domain.

B. Empirical analysis
- Feature-control validation: Evidence for feature-control is limited/preliminary; Fig. 5 does not convincingly establish benefits or stability. Since controlled features are non-stationary during training, please discuss/measure stability and convergence (e.g., learning curves under frozen vs. changing feature targets, or a schedule that anneals this objective).
- Atari ablations: Provide more ablations on Atari (per-component and combinations) to assess whether contributions mirror the Labyrinth patterns.
- Robustness reporting: Clarify whether the percentage improvements and robustness plots in Fig. 3 refer to Labyrinth or Atari (and ensure this is explicit in the figure legend/caption).

C. Notation, equations, and minor correctness
- Policy loss sign: Lπ = −E[log π(a|s)·A] is a minimization loss that corresponds to maximizing the policy objective; please phrase consistently (do not call it a “maximization” if presented as a loss).
- Eq. 1: λC should be inside the summation; below Eq. 1, r(c)t:t+n uses ∑k r(c)t+k, so the term should read r(c)t+k (not r(c)t).
- Eq. 2: Ensure LPC is explicitly defined in the equation (you use ∑c L(c)Q but also refer to LPC in text); keep notation consistent across text and equation.
- Auxiliary-task indices: In L(c)Q the n-step target is based on empirical multi-step rewards; be explicit about how episodes/truncations are handled in replay samples.

D. Figures, captions, and referencing
- Figure 2 (Labyrinth screenshots) is not referenced in the main text; please add a pointer when introducing the environment. Section 3.3 should explicitly reference Figure 1(d) when describing value replay and replay buffers.
- Figure 3 legend: Explain what “Clip” means for the dueling baseline (reward clipping? gradient clipping?).
- Figure 3 references: In Section 4.1.1, the text mentions “Figure 3 (right)” when it appears to be the top-left plot; please correct left/right/top/bottom annotations throughout (e.g., “Figure 3 Top”).
- Percentages in Figure 3 legend: Clarify they refer to Labyrinth.
- Figure 5 mismatch: The caption/text claims “top 5” on three Labyrinth levels, but only two levels appear to be shown; please correct the caption or include the missing panel(s).

E. Writing and formatting
- Grammar: “the features discovered in this manner is shared” → “are shared.”
- Section 4.2 punctuation: Add a comma after “methods” in the sentence introducing Atari results.
- Appendix: “Further details are included in the supplementary materials.” Please specify where/how to access these materials (URL or appendix section).
- References: A few entries appear malformed (e.g., repeated author tokens); please clean.

Questions for the authors
- What are the wall-clock slowdowns from A3C to UNREAL per domain, and which auxiliary components dominate compute/memory?
- How sensitive are results to λVR and λRP? Did you try tuning them, annealing them, or normalizing targets?
- Can you provide stronger empirical evidence (and stability analyses) for feature-control, or otherwise deprioritize it in claims?
- Do the Atari ablations corroborate the Labyrinth component contributions?
- For pixel-control, did you evaluate alternative grids, reward normalizations, or value-head architectures? Which choices matter most?
- What precisely does “Clip” denote in the dueling baseline?
- Please report the exact λPC values used for the best runs and summarize their distribution across seeds/games.
