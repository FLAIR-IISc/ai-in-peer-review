- Proposes an RL agent that augments policy learning with controllable auxiliary objectives: pixel-control and feature-control, plus an offline skewed-replay reward-prediction head and value-replay, jointly optimized via off-policy Q-learning for auxiliaries and on-policy A3C for the main policy.

- Empirical claim: large gains on visually rich 3D tasks and Atari, achieving baseline performance ~10× faster and greater hyperparameter robustness.

- Decision: Accept — novel controllable auxiliary tasks materially improve data efficiency and robustness with strong empirical support.

- Motivation: Well grounded in prior work; shift from passive prediction/reconstruction to controllable local/internal feature learning is compelling.

- Methodology: Sound integration of off-policy auxiliary control with shared representations, skewed replay reward prediction, and value replay within A3C.

- Results support: Labyrinth ≈10× sample-efficiency speedups and substantial human-normalized improvements on Atari; careful reporting across seeds/hyperparameters reduces selection bias.

- Suggestion: Define auxiliary control losses (LPC), pixel-control rewards, deconv heads, and λPC in the main text rather than the appendix.

- Suggestion: Provide deeper, isolated analyses of auxiliary tasks’ contributions using diagnostics beyond return (e.g., controllability metrics, linear probes, mutual information, off-policy stability).

- Suggestion: Analyze sensitivity to skew ratio in reward replay and to hyperparameters λVR/λPC/λRP.

- Question: How sensitive are gains to λPC, pixel-control grid resolution, and which network layer is best for feature control?

- Question: Does skewed replay for reward prediction improve sample efficiency uniformly or mainly in sparse-reward regimes?

- Question: What interference, if any, occurs between on-policy A3C updates and off-policy Q-learning on shared features?

- Question: Can the approach extend to continuous-action settings, and does feature control retain benefits there?

- Question: Are auxiliary control policies used at execution (e.g., as options) or solely for representation shaping?
