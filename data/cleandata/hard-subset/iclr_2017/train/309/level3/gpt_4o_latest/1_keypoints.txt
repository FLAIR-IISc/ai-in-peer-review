- Introduces UNREAL agent: augments A3C with unsupervised auxiliary tasks (pixel/feature control, reward prediction) to improve representations, data efficiency, and robustness.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains: outperforms A3C on Labyrinth and Atari, with ~10× mean speedup in Labyrinth and 880% average human-normalized score on Atari.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Major contribution: clear improvement over state-of-the-art A3C via auxiliary tasks.  
- Empirical validation: extensive, robust experiments and ablation studies across diverse environments.  
- Well-motivated and grounded in prior work; introduces novel mechanisms like pixel control and reward prediction.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written and accessible with clear architecture and experimental details.  
- Missing detail: insufficient information on computational resources required for training (GPU hours, memory).  
- Concern: added architectural complexity raises questions about scalability and computational overhead.  
- Reproducibility concern: no statement about source code or hyperparameter release.  
- Question to authors: specify computational requirements for Labyrinth and Atari training (GPU hours, memory).  
- Question to authors: how does the architecture scale to higher-dimensional state/action spaces?  
- Question to authors: will source code and hyperparameter configurations be released?
