- Introduces UNREAL agent that augments deep RL with domain-independent auxiliary tasks (pixel control, reward prediction, value replay) sharing a CNN+LSTM backbone.
- Claims auxiliary tasks improve representation learning, especially with sparse rewards, and boost data efficiency.
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains: 10× faster learning on Labyrinth and average 880% human performance on Atari, outperforming A3C and prior work.
- Decision: Accept.
- Strength: Well-motivated and novel integration of multiple pseudo-reward auxiliary tasks with shared representation learning.
- Strength: Convincing empirical results across diverse tasks, including complex 3D environments.
- Strength: Methodology is sound with clear task/loss descriptions and useful ablation studies.
- Weakness: Computational overhead of optimizing auxiliary tasks is not quantified.
- Weakness: Auxiliary task weights (λ_*) are fixed; rationale and hyperparameter tuning are not justified or explored.
- Weakness: Feature-control auxiliary task lacks strong empirical validation on stability and convergence.
- Suggestion: Extend training runs for top agents to explore long-term performance on challenging tasks.
- Suggestion: Improve clarity—explicitly state separate policies for auxiliary tasks and fix figure legends, references, and supplement details.
- Questions for authors: What are the computational costs and scaling with number of tasks; why fix λ_* values; can you provide Atari ablations; how does feature control affect stability and in which environments does it fail?
- Minor corrections: Fix grammar, ensure equations/figure references are consistent, and include missing parameter values (e.g., λ_PC) in main text or supplement.
- Conclusion: Significant contribution showing auxiliary tasks improve RL data efficiency and performance, but requires further analysis on costs, hyperparameters, and feature-control validation.
