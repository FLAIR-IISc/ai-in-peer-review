- Decision: Accept.  
- Contribution: Proposes augmenting A3C with auxiliary tasks (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction) to learn better representations.  
- Core idea: Train agent to maximize extrinsic reward plus control aspects of sensory input and predict immediate rewards as auxiliary objectives.  
- Results: Strong empirical gains with state-of-the-art performance on Labyrinth (87% human-normalised) and Atari (880% human-normalised), and ~10x faster learning.  
- Motivation: Addresses sparse extrinsic rewards by using feature/control-based pseudo-rewards as a more effective representation learning signal than reconstruction.  
- Method clarity: UNREAL architecture is well-described and integrates auxiliary tasks with A3C seamlessly.  
- Experimental rigor: Thorough experiments including ablation studies, comparisons to alternatives (e.g., pixel reconstruction), and hyperparameter robustness analyses.  
- Limitations: Authors acknowledge potential downsides and limitations, increasing trust in the work.  
- Request for clarity: Expand descriptions of pixel control and feature control (introduce key equations/parameters like L_PC earlier).  
- Ablation analysis: Provide deeper analysis on why specific auxiliary tasks (e.g., pixel control) are particularly effective.  
- Reward prediction sampling: Discuss potential biases introduced by skewed sampling for reward prediction.  
- Hyperparameter tuning: Give a more detailed account of hyperparameter tuning procedures across A3C, UNREAL, and ablations.  
- Visualization suggestion: Include a figure showing the pixel control grid overlaid on environment frames rather than relying only on a video.  
- Question 1: Clarify computational cost of auxiliary tasks and how per-step overhead balances with overall learning speedup.  
- Question 2: Provide more details and possible explanations for preliminary results where feature control outperformed reward prediction.  
- Question 3: Report sensitivity of results to the skewed sampling ratio for reward prediction and explore alternative ratios.  
- Question 4: Discuss applicability and expected benefits of the approach for policy-gradient methods beyond A3C.  
- Question 5: Share more granular Atari results, e.g., per-game or by game category, to contextualize overall performance gains.
