- Presents UNREAL, a deep RL agent using unsupervised auxiliary control and reward tasks to improve learning speed and performance.  
- Core idea—learning auxiliary control tasks alongside extrinsic reward—is well-motivated and intuitively appealing.  
- Demonstrates substantial empirical gains: ~10x speedup on Labyrinth and averaged 880% human performance on Atari, outperforming A3C.  
- Paper is well-written with clear architecture and experimental setup, accessible to deep RL readers.  
- Recommends acceptance based on significant performance improvements and robust implementation.  
- Strength lies in strong empirical results, consistent improvements across hyperparameters, and supporting ablation studies.  
- Notes complexity of the UNREAL architecture with many components and weighting parameters, making interplay nontrivial to understand.  
- Requests more details on computational resources and training time (hardware, GPUs/CPUs) for reproducibility.  
- Suggests releasing source code to aid reproducibility and community uptake.  
- Asks for more detailed ablation analysis of individual auxiliary tasks (e.g., pixel control vs. reward prediction) across more environments.
