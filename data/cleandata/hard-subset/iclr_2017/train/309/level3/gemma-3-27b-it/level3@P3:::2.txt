## Review for ICLR 2017 Submission

**1. Summary:**

This paper presents UNREAL, a deep reinforcement learning agent that enhances feature learning through the addition of domain-independent auxiliary tasks – control, reward prediction, and value replay – all sharing a CNN+LSTM feature representation. The core idea is to leverage a multitude of pseudo-rewards to develop a more robust and efficient representation for the primary reinforcement learning task. The authors demonstrate significant performance improvements over A3C on both Atari games and a challenging suite of 3D Labyrinth environments, showcasing substantial gains in data efficiency and robustness. The paper is generally well-written and the experimental results are convincing.

**2. Decision:**

**Accept.** The paper presents a significant contribution to the field of deep reinforcement learning. The proposed approach demonstrably improves data efficiency and performance on challenging environments. The results are compelling and the paper is well-structured.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

The experimental validation is strong. The gains achieved on Labyrinth (10x speedup, 87% human performance) are particularly impressive, and the improvements on Atari are also noteworthy. The ablation studies presented in Figure 3 effectively demonstrate the contribution of each auxiliary task. The authors clearly articulate the motivation for their approach and position it well within the existing literature. The inclusion of a video demonstrating agent performance is a valuable addition. The authors also correctly identify and discuss limitations of related work.

**4. Additional Feedback:**

The following points, while not directly impacting the acceptance decision, would improve the paper's clarity and impact:

*   **Abstract/Introduction:** Clarify that separate policies are learned for each auxiliary task, rather than a single policy optimizing all rewards simultaneously. This distinction is crucial for understanding the architecture.
*   **Computational Cost:** The paper highlights improved data efficiency but lacks quantification of the extra computational cost introduced by the auxiliary tasks. Reporting training speed loss (e.g., wall-clock time per epoch) and identifying which auxiliary components are most expensive (in terms of computation) would be valuable.
*   **Feature-Control Validation:** The empirical validation for the feature-control auxiliary task (Section 3.1) is weak. Figure 5 is not entirely convincing, and the changing nature of the tasks during training raises concerns about stability and convergence. Further investigation and clearer results are needed.
*   **Training Duration:** The authors mention that agent performance was still improving. Reporting how far the best agents progressed (e.g., final scores, episode lengths) would provide a more complete picture of their capabilities.
*   **Auxiliary Task Weights:** The fixed value of lambda_* = 1 is a questionable design choice. Treating these as hyperparameters and performing a sensitivity analysis, or providing a justification for the fixed value, would strengthen the paper.
*   **Off-Policy Training:** Explicitly state that the auxiliary tasks are trained off-policy and not with “true” Q-learning, due to the use of multi-step empirical rewards.
*   **Minor Correction:** In Section 3.3, “L_pi = -…” is presented as a maximization but written as a loss to be minimized. This is a minor inconsistency but should be addressed.
*   **Equation Formatting:** Eq.1 should have lambda_c *inside* the summation. Additionally, below Eq.1, r_t^(c) should be r_{t+k}^{(c)}.
*   **Figure References:** Figure 2 is not referenced in the text. Section 3.3 should reference Figure 1(d).
*   **Grammar:** “the features discovered in this manner is shared” should be “are shared”.
*   **Equation 2:** Eq.2 references L_PC which is not explicitly defined within the equation.
*   **Figure 3 Legend:** Explain what “Clip” means for dueling networks in the legend of Figure 3.
*   **Ablation Studies (Atari):** Conduct more ablation studies on Atari to determine if the per-component contributions align with the patterns observed in the Labyrinth experiments.
*   **Percentage Clarification:** Clarify that the percentages in Figure 3’s legend refer to Labyrinth, as this is not immediately apparent from the text.
*   **Figure References (Text):** Correct the figure references in the text. For example, 4.1.2 “Figure 3 (right)” refers to the top-left plot. All mentions like “Figure 3 Top” need correct left/right/top/right annotations.
*   **Figure 5 Caption/Text:** The caption and text related to Figure 5 contain a discrepancy – the paper claims top 5 on three Labyrinth levels but the figure seems to show only two.
*   **Section 4.2 Punctuation:** A comma is missing after “methods” in Section 4.2.
*   **Appendix:** Specify the location of the “supplementary materials” mentioned in the Appendix.
*   **lambda_PC Value:** Report the value of lambda_PC (assumed to be 1) explicitly.



I appreciate the authors’ response to comments, and believe that addressing the suggestions above will further strengthen this already compelling work.
