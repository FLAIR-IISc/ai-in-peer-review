- Decision: Accept — compelling, well-supported approach with substantial empirical gains and improved robustness.  
- Core idea: Augment A3C with auxiliary tasks (control of pixels/activations and reward prediction) to improve representation learning.  
- Motivation: Addresses limitations of relying solely on sparse extrinsic rewards by learning richer state representations.  
- Auxiliary tasks: Includes control-based tasks (maximize pixel/activation changes) and immediate reward prediction.  
- Architecture: Thoughtfully designed but complex, combining parallel learning with experience replay.  
- Empirical results: Large gains on Labyrinth (≈87% human, 10× speedup) and Atari (880% of expert human), with faster learning and hyperparameter robustness.  
- Ablation studies: Each auxiliary task contributes meaningfully; control-based tasks outperform pixel reconstruction baselines.  
- Writing and reproducibility: Paper is clear and provides extensive experimental and hyperparameter details.  
- Implementation concern: Complexity and interacting optimization schemes may hinder replication and extension; code release recommended.  
- Resource transparency: Paper should report hardware and training time to assess practical feasibility.  
- Further research: Recommend systematic study of task combinations/weightings and analysis/visualization of learned features.  
- Questions for authors: Request details on computational resources, plans to release code, sensitivity to loss weights, and insights into features/activations learned.
