- Proposes auxiliary tasks (controlling sensory stream and predicting immediate rewards) to learn stronger feature representations for RL agents.
- Novel, intuitively appealing use of active interaction signals versus passive learning.
- Empirical results are strong: significant performance gains on Atari and 3D Labyrinth, with reported ~10x learning speedup.
- Claims that auxiliary tasks help with partial observability by promoting controllable internal features.
- Recommendation: Accept — approach is novel, effective, and a valuable contribution to RL.
- Methodological strengths: solid experimental design, thorough baselines, and mitigation of confounds (hyperparameter controls, top-3 reporting).
- Pixel control analysis is promising and provides a foundation for future work.
- Paper needs clearer, earlier presentation of implementation details (e.g., introduce L_PC sooner rather than in appendix).
- Experimental analysis of auxiliary tasks is incomplete; lacks isolated studies on each task’s specific impact on learned features.
- Suggests providing metrics beyond RL performance (feature visualization, transfer experiments) to validate representation quality.
- Clarifying questions: sensitivity to 0.5 weighting in skewed replay sampling for reward prediction.
- Clarifying questions: why feature control underperformed pixel control and how to improve it.
- Clarifying questions: details on computational cost of auxiliary tasks beyond reported speedup, including per-iteration training time.
