- Proposes auxiliary tasks (control + reward prediction) to learn shared representations that improve sample efficiency and robustness in deep RL.
- Shows strong empirical gains over prior methods on Atari (880% human) and Labyrinth (10x speedup), with good ablation analysis.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.

- Strength: Well-motivated approach addressing sparse extrinsic rewards by framing unsupervised-like control tasks as RL problems.
- Strength: Convincing comparisons to A3C and Prioritized DQN and demonstration of generalization across Atari and Labyrinth.
- Strength: Ablation studies provide insight into each auxiliary task’s contribution.

- Request: Quantify additional computational cost of auxiliary tasks, their impact on training speed, and which components are most expensive.
- Clarify that separate policies are learned for each auxiliary task rather than a single policy optimizing all rewards.
- Concern: Feature-control auxiliary task shows weak/unstable empirical validation (Fig. 5); needs stronger analysis or evaluation.
- Request: Report longer training results and convergence behavior since performance was still improving at end of runs.
- Request: Systematically explore auxiliary task weight hyperparameters (λ) instead of fixing them at 1.
- Clarify that auxiliary tasks are trained off-policy and note deviation from “true” Q-learning due to multi-step empirical rewards.
- Minor correction: Present L_pi consistently (loss vs. maximization).
- Minor correction: In Eq. 1, lambda_c should be inside the summation.
- Minor correction: Below Eq. 1, r_t^(c) should be r_{t+k}^(c).
- Minor correction: Figure 2 is not referenced in the text.
- Minor correction: Reference Figure 1(d) in Section 3.3.
- Minor correction: Fix grammar “the features discovered in this manner is shared” → “are shared”.
- Minor correction: Eq. 2 references L_PC which is not defined or included.
- Clarify legend item “Clip” in Figure 3.
- Request: More Atari ablation studies to verify per-component contributions generalize from Labyrinth.
- Clarify that percentages in Figure 3 legend refer to Labyrinth.
- Fix incorrect figure references in the text (e.g., 4.1.2 “Figure 3 (right)” actually top-left).
- Fix inconsistency between Figure 5 caption and text about how many Labyrinth levels are shown.
- Expand appendix/supplementary-materials pointers and specify where additional details can be found.
- Explicitly report the value of lambda_PC (assumed to be 1).

- Question: Provide the computational overhead per auxiliary task and how it scales with environment complexity.
- Question: Explain potential causes for poor feature-control performance and possible modifications to improve it.
- Question: How sensitive are results to the reward-prediction skewing factor (e.g., 0.5 probability of non-zero reward)?
- Question: What experiments informed fixing lambda values, and would an adaptive schedule change results?
