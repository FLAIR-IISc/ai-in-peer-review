- Proposes augmenting A3C with unsupervised auxiliary tasks (control-based and reward-prediction) to improve representation learning in DRL.  
- Hypothesis: learning to control sensorimotor features and predict rewards speeds learning and improves performance.  
- Strong empirical results: UNREAL outperforms A3C on Labyrinth and Atari (≈10x learning speedup, 87% human-level on Labyrinth, 880% expert human on Atari).  
- Well-motivated idea with practical integration into the A3C framework.  
- Ablation studies effectively show contributions of individual auxiliary tasks.  
- Comparisons to related methods (pixel reconstruction, input-change prediction) provide useful insights.  
- Includes some robustness analysis to hyperparameters, strengthening empirical claims.  
- Overall assessment: solid conference contribution — Accept (addresses sample efficiency and robustness).  
- Missing detail: computational resources (number of CPUs/GPUs, training time, memory) for reproducibility.  
- Missing detail: source code not provided; releasing code would increase impact and facilitate follow-up work.  
- Missing analysis: limited visualization/analysis of learned features to explain why auxiliary tasks help.  
- Missing analysis: more detailed sensitivity study of auxiliary task weights (λVR, λPC, λRP) is needed.  
- Missing detail: rationale for framing reward prediction as three-class classification vs regression and specifics of reward-balancing.  
- Question: provide a detailed breakdown of compute resources and training time for Labyrinth and Atari.  
- Question: do you plan to release the source code, and if so, when?  
- Question: can you provide examples/visualizations of features learned by the agent and their semantic meaning?  
- Question: how sensitive are results to the loss weightings; was a systematic hyperparameter search performed?  
- Question: why choose three-class reward prediction and how was the 50/50 zero vs non-zero reward balance achieved?
