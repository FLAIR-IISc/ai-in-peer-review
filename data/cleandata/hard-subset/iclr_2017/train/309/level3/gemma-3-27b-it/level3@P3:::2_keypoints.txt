1. Paper introduces UNREAL, an RL agent using domain-independent auxiliary tasks (control, reward prediction, value replay) sharing a CNN+LSTM representation to improve feature learning.  
2. Core idea: use multiple pseudo-rewards to learn a more robust representation that improves primary task performance and data efficiency.  
3. Empirical claim: UNREAL significantly outperforms A3C on Atari and Labyrinth, showing large gains in data efficiency and robustness.  
4. Decision: Accept — significant contribution with compelling results and clear writing.  
5. Strong experimental validation, especially Labyrinth results (10x speedup, 87% human performance).  
6. Ablation studies effectively show the contribution of each auxiliary task.  
7. Paper situates itself well in related work and includes a useful demonstration video.  
8. Clarify in abstract/introduction that separate policies are learned for each auxiliary task, not a single policy optimizing all rewards.  
9. Quantify extra computational cost from auxiliary tasks (report wall-clock training speed and identify expensive components).  
10. Feature-control auxiliary task validation is weak; Figure 5 is unconvincing and raises stability/convergence concerns.  
11. Report final training progress (final scores, episode lengths) since agents were still improving.  
12. Lambda_* = 1 is questionable; treat auxiliary weights as hyperparameters or justify the fixed choice and provide sensitivity analysis.  
13. Explicitly state auxiliary tasks are trained off-policy and not with true Q-learning due to multi-step empirical rewards.  
14. Minor inconsistency: present L_pi consistently as a maximization or as a loss to be minimized.  
15. Equation formatting issues: lambda_c should be inside the summation in Eq.1, and r_t^(c) should be r_{t+k}^{(c)} below Eq.1.  
16. Figure 2 is not referenced; Section 3.3 should reference Figure 1(d).  
17. Grammar fix: change “the features discovered in this manner is shared” to “are shared.”  
18. Eq.2 references L_PC which is not explicitly defined within the equation.  
19. Clarify legend in Figure 3 by explaining what “Clip” means for dueling networks.  
20. Perform more ablation studies on Atari to confirm component contributions match Labyrinth patterns.  
21. Clarify that percentages in Figure 3’s legend refer to Labyrinth.  
22. Correct figure references and positional descriptors (left/right/top) throughout the text, e.g., Figure 3 and 4.1.2.  
23. Resolve discrepancy in Figure 5 caption/text regarding top-5 results across Labyrinth levels.  
24. Add missing comma after “methods” in Section 4.2.  
25. Specify location of “supplementary materials” in the Appendix.  
26. Explicitly report the value of lambda_PC (assumed to be 1).
