- Proposes augmenting deep RL with auxiliary tasks (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction) to learn stronger representations.  
- Trains agents to maximize extrinsic reward plus control of sensory stream changes and predict immediate rewards.  
- Demonstrates significant performance gains and faster learning on Atari and 3D Labyrinth, achieving state-of-the-art results.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Contribution is novel, technically sound, and potentially high-impact, especially for complex environments.  
- Major strength: substantial learning speedup on challenging Labyrinth benchmark.  
- Approach is well-motivated by using agent agency as a dense learning signal versus passive unsupervised methods.  
- Experimental setup is robust with comparisons to A3C baselines, ablation studies, and top-3 reporting across hyperparameters.  
- Acknowledges and accounts for hyperparameter differences between A3C and the proposed UNREAL method.  
- Inclusion of both Atari and Labyrinth benchmarks supports generalizability of results.  
- Paper lacks low-level implementation detail of auxiliary tasks (e.g., L_PC placement before appendix).  
- Needs clearer intuition for why pixel changes and feature activations were chosen as control targets.  
- Analysis of which auxiliary tasks drive performance is limited; more isolated studies and additional metrics are suggested.  
- Recommends visualizations showing what agents do when maximizing auxiliary rewards (e.g., areas of pixel change).  
- Questions remain on why input reconstruction harmed performance and whether reconstruction was on raw pixels or features.  
- Asks why multiclass cross-entropy was used for reward prediction instead of MSE and requests analysis/visualization of the learned reward predictor.  
- Requests clarification of computational costs (memory and training time) introduced by auxiliary tasks.
