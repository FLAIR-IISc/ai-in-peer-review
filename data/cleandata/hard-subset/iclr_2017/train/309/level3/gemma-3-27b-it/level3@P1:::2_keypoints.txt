- Decision: Accept with high confidence.  
- Contribution: Proposes UNREAL, an agent that jointly learns main reward and multiple auxiliary pseudo-reward tasks to improve representations.  
- Core idea: Train separate control problems for different sensorimotor features to produce more robust, efficient representations.  
- Empirical results: Strong gains on Atari and Labyrinth, including 880% expert-human on Atari and 87% on Labyrinth.  
- Learning speed: Demonstrates much faster learning, e.g., ~10× speedup on Labyrinth.  
- Ablations: Ablation studies show individual auxiliary tasks contribute meaningfully to performance.  
- Baselines: Outperforms A3C and prioritized dueling DQN in comparisons.  
- Writing: Paper is well-written and experiments are thorough and compelling.  
- Computational cost concern: Needs quantification of extra training time and which auxiliary tasks are most expensive.  
- Clarification needed: State explicitly that separate policies are learned for each auxiliary task (not a single policy).  
- Feature-control task: Empirical support for the feature-control auxiliary is weaker; results raise stability and convergence concerns and need more analysis.  
- Training duration: Report longer-run final performance since learning was still improving at the end of reported training.  
- Auxiliary weights: Fixed auxiliary task weights (λ*) at 1; should explore treating them as hyperparameters and tuning them.  
- Off-policy updates: Clarify that Q-learning updates for auxiliary tasks are off-policy and based on empirical n-step returns (not exact Q-learning).  
- Minor corrections: Fix policy-gradient sign, move lambda_c inside summation in Eq.1, correct r_t^(c) → r_{t+k}^{(c)}, reference Figure 2, reference Fig.1(d) in Sec 3.3, grammar fixes, define L_PC in Eq.2, clarify “Clip” in Fig.3 legend.  
- Question — overhead: Provide a breakdown of computational overhead per auxiliary task.  
- Question — λ* rationale: Explain why auxiliary weights were fixed at 1 and whether tuning was attempted.  
- Question — off-policy details: Provide implementation details for off-policy auxiliary learning and clarify why these updates aren’t “true” Q-learning.  
- Question — λ_PC value: Report the actual lambda_PC used and how it might vary across environments.  
- Question — extended training: Indicate how far agents would progress with extended training beyond reported runs.
