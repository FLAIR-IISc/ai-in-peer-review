- Proposes UNREAL: an RL agent using auxiliary tasks to accelerate and improve representation learning via off-policy auxiliary policies.  
- Auxiliary control tasks: learn to control pixel regions or hidden features as parallel RL problems to capture cause-effect.  
- Auxiliary reward prediction: supervised prediction of immediate reward using skewed replay to over-sample rare rewards.  
- Empirical results: achieves 880% human on Atari and ~10x learning speedup on 3D Labyrinth vs A3C.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novelty: reframes auxiliary tasks as active control (not passive prediction), a significant conceptual shift.  
- Practical impact: combines top performance with large improvements in data efficiency.  
- Motivation: addresses sparse rewards by generating dense internal training signals through control objectives.  
- Literature placement: correctly connects to Horde, UVFAs, successor representations while distinguishing its contribution.  
- Experimental rigor: uses A3C baseline and ablations showing each component (pixel control, reward prediction, value replay) helps.  
- Generality: Labyrinth suite shows applicability to 3D navigation, exploration, and fine-grained motor control.  
- Active vs passive: active pixel-control outperforms pixel reconstruction/prediction, which can harm final performance.  
- Robustness: analysis indicates increased robustness to hyperparameter settings.  
- Suggestion: move key architectural details (e.g., deconv Q-network, explicit L_PC definition) into main text for clarity.  
- Suggestion: add qualitative analysis/visualizations of what auxiliary control policies actually learn.  
- Suggestion: expand experiments and discussion on feature-control (which features are learned/controlled).  
- Question: request qualitative examples of pixel-control policies and whether they yield interpretable sub-policies.  
- Question: ask sensitivity of performance to the 50/50 reward/non-reward replay ratio and risk of biasing representations.  
- Question: ask for explanation why reconstruction baseline learns faster initially but degrades final performance (distracting encoder?).
