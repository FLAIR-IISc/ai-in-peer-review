- Introduces UNREAL: augments A3C with domain-independent auxiliary tasks to accelerate and enrich representation learning.  
- Auxiliary tasks include pixel control, feature control, reward prediction (with skewed replay), and value function replay.  
- Auxiliary tasks learn off-policy value functions for pseudo-rewards (pixel/feature control) and predict immediate future rewards.  
- Shares convolutional and recurrent layers between the main policy and auxiliary tasks to produce more robust representations.  
- Strong empirical results: averages 880% of human on Atari, 87% on Labyrinth, and ~10× faster learning on Labyrinth.  
- Decision: Accept — paper is well-written, well-motivated, and experimentally convincing.  
- Method is novel and generalizable: unsupervised auxiliary control/prediction tasks are broadly applicable for representation learning.  
- Ablation studies on Labyrinth show each auxiliary component (pixel control, reward prediction, value replay) contributes positively.  
- Comparison to alternative unsupervised methods (e.g., pixel reconstruction) favors the proposed auxiliary control tasks.  
- Labyrinth environment is a valuable new benchmark with richer visuals and more complex physics.  
- Clarify early that the agent learns separate policies for each auxiliary task rather than a single weighted-sum policy.  
- Feature control validation is preliminary: shows slight underperformance versus pixel control and potential instability; request discussion on stability/convergence.  
- Off-policy learning nuance: auxiliary control is trained with Q-learning but uses n-step returns—should be explicitly stated.  
- Training curves often still trend upward at the end; recommend reporting longer training runs to show asymptotic behavior.  
- Recommend ablation experiments on a subset of Atari to test whether auxiliary contributions generalize across domains.  
- Minor corrections requested: fix ambiguous figure references, grammar ("is/are"), explicitly write the full loss in Equation 2, resolve Figure 5 level mismatch, and specify location of supplementary materials.  
- Questions for authors: quantify computational overhead (wall-clock slowdown and costliest components); justify fixed auxiliary loss weights or treat them as tunable; clarify what "Clip" means in the Dueling DQN legend.
