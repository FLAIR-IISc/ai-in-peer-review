- Introduces UNREAL: augments A3C with auxiliary tasks (pixel/feature control, reward prediction, value replay) to learn richer representations and improve RL performance.  
- Combines on-policy A3C for the main task with off-policy auxiliary learning via an experience replay buffer and novel architecture.  
- Shows strong empirical gains on a new 3D Labyrinth environment and the 57-game Atari benchmark, improving final performance and data efficiency.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Well-motivated, technically sound, and novel integration addressing representation learning in RL, especially for sparse rewards.  
- Key reason 2: Comprehensive empirical evidence—state-of-the-art Atari results and large Labyrinth improvements (e.g., 880% human on Atari, ~10x speedup on Labyrinth).  
- Situates work in literature (Horde, successor representations) and distinguishes control objectives from reconstruction-based auxiliary tasks.  
- Technical merit: A3C + off-policy Q + replay is an elegant, stable solution; auxiliary tasks (pixel control, reward prediction, value replay) are complementary and intuitive.  
- Experiments are thorough: ablations, comparisons to pixel reconstruction, and hyperparameter robustness analyses strengthen claims.  
- Additional feedback: Architecture complexity and multiple loss-weight hyperparameters (λVR, λPC, λRP) could hinder adoption—report sensitivity and guidance.  
- Additional feedback: Report computational trade-offs (wall-clock time, memory) versus A3C to inform implementers.  
- Questions for authors: What is the approximate increase in wall-clock training time per million environment steps compared to A3C?  
- Questions for authors: Will you release source code to facilitate reproducibility and community adoption?
