- Proposal: UNREAL augments A3C with auxiliary tasks to improve sample efficiency and final RL performance.  
- Core auxiliary control tasks: pixel control (maximize local pixel-intensity changes) and feature control (maximize hidden-unit activations).  
- Additional tasks: supervised reward prediction trained on rebalanced experience and a value-function replay task.  
- Empirical results: state-of-the-art on 57 Atari games and the Labyrinth 3D environment, ~10× faster learning than A3C, and greater hyperparameter robustness.  
- Decision: Strong Accept.  
- Novelty/significance: Active control-based auxiliary tasks represent a meaningful shift from passive unsupervised methods for representation learning in RL.  
- Empirical support: Extensive, convincing experiments showing large speedups and substantial performance gains (e.g., 880% human on Atari).  
- Methodological transparency: Authors report hyperparameter differences and evaluate over top-3 settings to demonstrate robustness.  
- Improvement 1: Main text should more clearly define auxiliary task losses (e.g., L_PC) and include concise architectural diagrams for auxiliary heads.  
- Improvement 2: Deeper analysis of individual auxiliary-task contributions and qualitative differences in learned representations (e.g., t-SNE of LSTM states) would strengthen understanding of why the method works.  
- Question 1: Request for detailed per-task ablation results (A3C + each auxiliary alone) on representative environments.  
- Question 2: Ask about sensitivity to pixel-control grid granularity (is 20×20 critical?).  
- Question 3: Request information on computational overhead (wall-clock training time per million frames) compared to A3C.
