- Paper introduces UNREAL: an A3C-based agent augmented with domain-independent auxiliary tasks to improve representation learning and speed up main-policy RL.
- Auxiliary tasks: auxiliary control (pixel control and feature control), auxiliary reward prediction, and value function replay, all sharing convolutional and recurrent layers with the main agent.
- Auxiliary control tasks learn separate policies to maximize changes in pixels or hidden activations, transferring learned representations to the main task.
- Reward prediction task predicts immediate reward onset from recent history to improve reward sensitivity.
- Value function replay provides additional off-policy n-step value updates from a replay buffer to stabilize learning.
- Evaluated on 3D Labyrinth and 57 Atari games, showing substantial improvements over A3C and prior baselines.
- Empirical highlights: ~10x learning speedup on Labyrinth and mean human-normalized score increase from 54% (A3C) to 87%.
- Atari results: mean 880% of expert human performance, outperforming previous state-of-the-art.
- Ablations on Labyrinth show each auxiliary component contributes positively to final performance.
- Auxiliary tasks increase robustness to hyperparameter settings, a notable practical benefit.
- Pixel control is well-validated and appears particularly effective compared to traditional unsupervised objectives like reconstruction.
- Feature control auxiliary task is less convincingly validated; results are preliminary and show only slight improvement, raising convergence/stability concerns as hidden units change during training.
- Recommendation: Accept, based on strong empirical gains, clear writing, and novel domain-agnostic auxiliary control idea.
- Requests for clarification: quantify computational overhead of auxiliary tasks (wall-clock or updates/sec) and which components are most expensive.
- Questions about hyperparameter choices: rationale for fixing λVR = 1 and λRP = 1, and whether these should be tuned like λPC.
- Suggestion to train longer or report best-agent performance, since learning curves are still trending upward at final reported steps.
- Request for an Atari ablation study to confirm component contributions transfer across domains.
- Clarify wording that separate value functions/policy heads are learned per auxiliary task rather than a single policy optimizing a weighted sum.
- Minor corrections: clarify off-policy vs. off-policy n-step updates wording; place λC inside summation in Eq.1 and correct r(c)t+k; clarify L_PC and λPC usage in Eq.2; make A3C policy loss sign consistent.
- Fix figure references and legends (Figure 2 missing ref, Figure 1(d) ref, Figure 3 panel references and "Clip" label, Figure 5 level count) and correct noted grammar/typos and appendix/supplement location.
