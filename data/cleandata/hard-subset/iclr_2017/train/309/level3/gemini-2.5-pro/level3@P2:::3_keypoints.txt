- Introduces UNREAL: augment A3C with auxiliary tasks to improve representation learning, data efficiency, and final performance.  
- Proposes two auxiliary task classes: auxiliary control tasks and auxiliary reward tasks.  
- Auxiliary control tasks: pixel control (maximize pixel changes in screen regions) and feature control (maximize hidden-unit activations).  
- Reframes unsupervised learning as active control to learn affordances rather than passive prediction.  
- Auxiliary reward tasks: reward prediction from short observation sequences with skewed sampling to over-represent rewarding events.  
- Value function replay: additional off-policy updates from replayed data to strengthen the main value estimate.  
- Evaluated on 57 Atari games and the Labyrinth 3D environment, achieving state-of-the-art results (880% human on Atari, 87% on Labyrinth).  
- Demonstrates major data-efficiency gains, reaching baselines’ peak performance up to ~10× faster.  
- Decision: Accept — method is novel, impactful, and empirically strong.  
- Novelty/high-level motivation: auxiliary control tasks are a significant conceptual advance over passive unsupervised objectives.  
- Empirical support: rigorous experiments, ablations show each component contributes, and results are robust across hyperparameters.  
- Recommendation: include key implementation details (e.g., deconv architecture, L_PC loss) in the main text for clarity.  
- Recommendation: add direct analyses of learned representations (e.g., visualize pixel-control policies or t-SNE of LSTM states).  
- Question: sensitivity of pixel-control grid resolution and potential trivial strategies (e.g., camera shaking) and whether safeguards exist.  
- Question: why feature-control wasn’t more broadly tested/integrated—were there stability or computational-cost issues?  
- Question: comment on dynamics and stability of mixing on-policy A3C updates with off-policy replay for auxiliary tasks (distribution mismatch concerns).
