- Introduces UNREAL agent that augments A3C with unsupervised auxiliary tasks to improve representation learning.
- Central hypothesis: predicting and controlling sensory features without extrinsic rewards yields richer, more versatile representations.
- Proposes two auxiliary task classes: auxiliary control (policies to maximize pixel/hidden-unit changes) and auxiliary reward prediction (predicting immediate reward onset).
- Combines off-policy auxiliary tasks with experience replay and on-policy A3C.
- Achieves substantial gains on 3D Labyrinth and 57 Atari games.
- Strong empirical results: ~10x learning speedup in Labyrinth and 880% of expert human performance on Atari.
- Recommendation to accept based on empirical strength and value as a comprehensive proof-of-concept.
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and experimentally convincing.
- Contribution is primarily empirical/architectural rather than theoretical but is valuable for the field.
- Concern: overall UNREAL architecture is complex, raising reproducibility and adoption issues.
- Request for information on computational resources (CPU/GPU hours) compared to A3C to assess cost–benefit.
- Asks whether authors will release source code and environments to facilitate further research.
