- Introduces UNREAL: an A3C-based agent augmented with auxiliary tasks to improve representation learning from sensorimotor streams.  
- Proposes two auxiliary task classes: auxiliary control (off-policy value functions to control features) and auxiliary reward prediction (predicting immediate reward onsets).  
- Combines on-policy A3C for the main task with off-policy Q-learning and supervised reward prediction, trained from a shared replay buffer.  
- Demonstrates large empirical gains: state-of-the-art results on Atari (880% human average) and 10× faster mean learning on 3D Labyrinth.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Reason 1 for acceptance: well-motivated, technically sound integration of unsupervised/self-supervised objectives into deep RL.  
- Reason 2 for acceptance: extensive, compelling empirical evidence across two challenging domains with ablations.  
- Key novelty: pixel control encourages learning controllable, causal features rather than passive reconstruction/prediction.  
- Strong empirical evaluation: thorough tests on 57 Atari games and new Labyrinth environment with significant performance and robustness gains.  
- Ablation studies decompose contributions of each auxiliary component and justify the UNREAL architecture.  
- Comparison shows control-based objectives outperform reconstruction/prediction for useful representations.  
- Paper is well-written, well-situated in prior literature, and easy to follow for RL researchers.  
- Concern: UNREAL is a complex synthesis of many components; clearer discussion of relative importance of each part would help.  
- Suggestion: extend pixel control vs. prediction/reconstruction comparisons to representative Atari games if possible.  
- Questions for authors: quantify computational overhead (wall-clock per million steps) versus A3C.  
- Questions for authors: will code for UNREAL and Labyrinth be released to aid reproducibility?
