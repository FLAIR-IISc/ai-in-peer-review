- Proposes accelerating RL by training agents on auxiliary tasks alongside the main reward-maximization objective.  
- Core idea: controlling and predicting sensory inputs produces richer, more robust feature representations that benefit the main policy.  
- Introduces two pseudo-control tasks (pixel change control and latent feature activation control) plus a supervised reward prediction task.  
- Empirical results: UNREAL achieves state-of-the-art on Atari and a 10x learning speedup on a 3D Labyrinth environment.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Main reasons for acceptance: novelty of active, self-supervised control tasks and extensive, compelling empirical evidence of large sample-efficiency and performance gains.  
- Key strength: reframes auxiliary learning from passive prediction to active control, encouraging causal, goal-aligned representations.  
- Empirical validation: thorough ablations showing each auxiliary task’s contribution, substantial gains, robustness to hyperparameters, and transparency about architectural differences.  
- Suggested improvement: move formal definitions/implementation details of central auxiliary tasks (e.g., pixel control and loss L_PC) from appendix to main text for clarity and reproducibility.  
- Suggested improvement: include deeper analysis of how auxiliary tasks produce benefits—isolated studies of pixel/feature control and task-specific metrics beyond overall RL score.  
- Question 1: Have you visualized or analyzed the learned pixel control policies and correlated their quality with downstream performance?  
- Question 2: How sensitive is reward-prediction performance to the 50/50 zero/non-zero skewed replay sampling, and were other prioritization schemes considered?
