- Introduces UNREAL: an A3C-based agent augmented with domain-independent auxiliary tasks (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction, value replay).  
- Auxiliary tasks share a CNN-LSTM representation to improve feature learning and data efficiency, especially with sparse rewards.  
- Strong empirical results on 57 Atari games and 3D Labyrinth show substantial improvements in learning speed and final performance.  
- Recommendation: Clear Accept due to novel, impactful method and solid empirical gains.  
- Auxiliary tasks produce substantial gains over a strong A3C baseline; experimental methodology is rigorous.  
- Ablation studies validate that individual auxiliary components contribute positively to performance.  
- Reported results include 880% average human expert performance on Atari and ~10x speedup on Labyrinth.  
- Clarify in paper that separate policies are learned for each auxiliary task and that auxiliary tasks are trained off-policy with multi-step empirical returns.  
- Feature-control task’s empirical support is weaker; Figure 5 is unconvincing and raises stability concerns due to non-stationary target features.  
- Minor corrections needed: move λ_c inside summation in Eq.1, reference Figure 2, define L_PC in Eq.2, fix incorrect figure references and grammar.  
- Question: quantify additional computational overhead (wall-clock training time) and identify the most expensive components.  
- Question: justify choice of auxiliary task weights (λ_VR, λ_RP = 1) and whether they were tuned as hyperparameters.  
- Question: consider running experiments longer to report peak performance since learning was still improving in some plots.  
- Question: provide more per-component ablation studies on the Atari suite to verify consistency with Labyrinth findings.
