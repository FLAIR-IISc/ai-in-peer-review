- Introduces UNREAL: augments A3C with unsupervised auxiliary tasks to learn richer representations.  
- Proposes auxiliary control tasks (pixel control, feature control) and auxiliary reward prediction with oversampling of rewarding events.  
- Trains auxiliary objectives off-policy from a replay buffer alongside on-policy A3C updates.  
- Demonstrates large empirical gains: ~10x learning speedup on Labyrinth and strong Atari results (average 880% human).  
- Ablation studies clearly show the individual and combined benefits of pixel control, reward prediction, and value replay.  
- New Labyrinth environment offers a challenging, visually rich 3D testbed and is a valuable contribution.  
- Architecture cleverly combines on-policy actor-critic with off-policy auxiliary Q-learning to improve data efficiency without destabilizing learning.  
- Main drawback: increased architectural and computational complexity may hinder reproducibility and adoption.  
- Asks for details on computational overhead (wall-clock time and CPU/GPU requirements) compared to A3C for fixed environment steps.  
- Requests public release of code and Labyrinth to facilitate verification and community use.  
- Decision: Accept — strong, well-written paper with significant and rigorously validated contributions.
