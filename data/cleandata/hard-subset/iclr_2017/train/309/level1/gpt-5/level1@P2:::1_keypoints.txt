- Proposal: UNREAL augments A3C with unsupervised auxiliary tasks to improve representation learning, data efficiency, and robustness.
- Auxiliary control tasks: off-policy n-step Q-learning from replay for pixel control (maximize local pixel change) and feature control (maximize internal unit activation) to shape shared representations.
- Reward-focused auxiliary tasks: skewed-replay immediate reward prediction to combat sparsity and value function replay for extra off-policy value updates.
- Architecture/loss: combined loss mixing on-policy A3C updates with off-policy auxiliary updates sharing a CNN-LSTM trunk; deconvolutional heads exploit spatial structure.
- Empirical gains: large improvements on 13 Labyrinth levels (mean 87% human-normalized, ~10× faster to best A3C) and 57 Atari games (mean 880% human, median 250%) with ablations showing each component’s contribution.
- Decision: Accept.
- Main reason for acceptance: strong, broad empirical evidence that auxiliary control and reward-focused tasks substantially improve data efficiency and robustness over A3C and Dueling DQN.
- Novelty: clear, well-motivated integration of off-policy auxiliary tasks with on-policy actor-critic for practical performance gains in visually rich, partially observed domains.
- Research question addressed: how to exploit abundant non-reward sensorimotor signals to learn representations that accelerate/stabilize learning of sparse extrinsic rewards.
- Motivation: unsupervised reconstruction may not align with control; predicting/controlling features better aligns with downstream goals.
- Relation to prior work: differs from options/Horde/UVFAs/successor representations by using many auxiliary RL tasks purely to sculpt a shared representation for the extrinsic policy.
- Design choice justification: off-policy Q-learning for auxiliaries improves data efficiency without biasing the on-policy policy gradient; skewed reward prediction shapes features without policy/value bias.
- Evaluation breadth: diverse benchmarks (13 Labyrinth tasks, 57 Atari games) and strong quantitative gains with robustness analyses and ablations.
- Method description: implementation details (network, losses, replay, hyperparameter ranges) are sufficiently described; mixing on/off-policy updates is empirically stable with small replay windows.
- Caveat: Atari evaluation protocol details (training frames, starts, termination rules, clipping, etc.) are under-specified, affecting fairness comparisons.
- Caveat: per-game top-3 hyperparameter selection may advantage UNREAL if baselines weren’t given equal tuning; robustness plots somewhat mitigate this.
- Caveat: mean scores can be dominated by outliers; median and per-game analyses would strengthen claims.
- Additional feedback: clarify Atari evaluation protocol and reproducibility details (frames, seeds, starts, sticky actions, clipping, termination).
- Additional feedback: run full ablations on Atari (each auxiliary alone) to confirm generality across domains.
- Additional feedback: analyze gradient interference between auxiliary and policy/value losses and consider adaptive loss weighting.
- Additional feedback: report sensitivity to auxiliary loss weights (λPC, λRP, λVR) and replay buffer size with guidelines for heterogeneous games.
- Additional feedback: provide per-game Atari results and mark outliers in the main paper or appendix.
- Additional feedback: report computational cost (wall-clock time, throughput) relative to A3C to complement data-efficiency claims.
- Additional feedback: investigate transferability of learned representations to new reward functions or similar environments.
- Minor fixes: correct reference formatting, typos, and clarify that “deconvolution” means transposed convolution.
- Questions for authors: specify exact Atari evaluation settings and whether they match baselines.
- Questions for authors: report training frames used and number of evaluation episodes/seeds for scores.
- Questions for authors: how were “top 3” hyperparameters chosen and were baselines given equal tuning budget?
- Questions for authors: sensitivity of results to λPC and pixel-control grid/cell size; comparative performance of feature control.
- Questions for authors: do auxiliary tasks ever hurt performance on specific games and under what conditions?
- Questions for authors: provide wall-clock speedups and compute overhead of auxiliary heads and replay.
- Questions for authors: does representation from auxiliary control transfer to new reward functions or similar environments?
