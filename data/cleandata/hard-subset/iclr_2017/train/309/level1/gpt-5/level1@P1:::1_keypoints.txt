- Proposes UNREAL: A3C agent augmented with auxiliary tasks (pixel/feature control, reward prediction, value replay) to shape representations while training on extrinsic rewards.
- Introduces two auxiliary task families: control tasks maximizing pseudo-rewards off-policy and reward-focused tasks that densify signal without biasing policy.
- Architecturally shares a CNN-LSTM backbone with deconv heads for control and a feedforward head for reward prediction; uses off-policy n-step Q-learning for auxiliaries and on-policy A3C for the main objective.
- Empirically demonstrates substantial gains in data efficiency, robustness, and final performance on 13 Labyrinth tasks and 57 Atari games (mean 880% human, median 250%).
- Provides ablations (pixel control, reward prediction, value replay), comparisons to reconstruction/change-prediction baselines, and robustness analyses across hyperparameters.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reasons for acceptance: strong, consistent empirical improvements with careful ablations and a novel, well-motivated use of auxiliary control tasks plus an effective training recipe.
- Problem tackled: improving data efficiency and robustness of deep RL under sparse rewards by leveraging richer training signals beyond extrinsic rewards.
- Motivation/literature placement: argues control-oriented pseudo-tasks better shape representations than pixel reconstruction and connects appropriately to prior work while making distinct contributions.
- Results rigor: strong, consistent evidence across domains with human-normalized scores and ablations, though some evaluation/compute caveats remain.
- Feedback: report wall-clock training time, total parameter updates, and compute-budget comparisons to quantify auxiliary-task overhead.
- Feedback: clarify Atari evaluation protocol details (no-op starts, life-loss termination, action repeat, episode length, definition of “expert human”).
- Feedback: include per-component ablations on Atari to verify generalization of improvements beyond Labyrinth.
- Feedback: expand experiments on feature control across Labyrinth and Atari to better establish its value and interaction with pixel control.
- Feedback: explain stability of sharing parameters between on-policy A3C and off-policy auxiliary Q-learning and list any special tricks used.
- Feedback: report how λPC and other auxiliary loss weights impact stability and whether weights were tuned per domain.
- Feedback: analyze replay buffer sensitivity (size, reward rebalancing ratio, n-step targets) for performance and stability effects.
- Feedback: include transfer/generalization experiments to test whether learned representations help new tasks or changed reward functions.
- Feedback: add more visualizations/interpretability analyses (saliency, success maps) to illustrate representation-shaping effects.
- Questions: quantify extra wall-clock time and gradient steps added by auxiliary tasks and whether gains persist under equal compute.
- Questions: specify exact Atari evaluation settings and definition/source of “expert human” scores.
- Questions: report whether A3C and auxiliary Q-learning gradients interfered and whether different learning rates/normalization were used per loss.
- Questions: how sensitive is reward prediction balancing (P(r≠0)=0.5, three classes) to results and were alternatives tried?
- Questions: which feature-layer(s) were most effective to control and how does feature control combine with pixel control?
- Questions: report robustness across random seeds for UNREAL vs A3C under fixed hyperparameters.
- Questions: does freezing the CNN after auxiliary pretraining speed downstream policy learning versus training from scratch?
- Questions: rationale for 2k-step replay buffer and effect of larger/smaller buffers on value replay and control.
