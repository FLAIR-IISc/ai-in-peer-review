- Proposes UNREAL: an A3C agent augmented with off-policy auxiliary tasks to shape shared representations for better sample efficiency and robustness under sparse rewards.  
- Introduces two auxiliary families: control tasks (pixel-control, feature-control) that learn pseudo-reward policies (not executed at test) and reward-focused tasks (immediate reward prediction with replay).  
- Uses value-function replay and a single joint loss combining on-policy A3C and off-policy Q-learning for auxiliary control.  
- Empirical gains: ~10× speedup on Labyrinth, 87% human-normalized performance there, and strong mean/median human-normalized scores across 57 Atari games with improved hyperparameter robustness.  
- Ablations show control auxiliaries contribute most, then prediction, then reconstruction.  
- Decision: Accept — due to significant, consistent empirical improvements and a well-motivated, general framework.  
- Problem framing: clear and timely focus on representation learning and exploration under sparse rewards via multi-task pseudo-rewards.  
- Literature placement: differentiates from options/Horde/UVFA/successor representations and prior prediction work by using learned control purely for feature shaping.  
- Methodology: sound combination of on-policy A3C with off-policy Q-learning, task-specific heads, deconv for spatial control, and prioritized sampling for reward prediction.  
- Evidence: strong aggregate metrics, robustness sweeps, and informative ablations; control-based auxiliaries add clear value.  
- Minor concerns: need clarity on evaluation protocol (Atari settings, seeds, compute), fairness versus matched-capacity baselines, and stability/justification of off-policy auxiliary updates.  
- Suggested improvements: report compute/training time and sample-efficiency curves versus matched-capacity baselines (A3C+replay/prioritized replay).  
- Suggested improvements: provide sensitivity analyses for λPC, λRP, λVR and replay buffer size.  
- Suggested improvements: expand feature-control ablations and analyze learned representations (e.g., probing tasks).  
- Suggested improvements: discuss failure modes and domain dependence of pixel-change pseudo-rewards and potential for transfer.  
- Question: Which Atari evaluation protocol was used (no-op vs human starts, sticky actions, training frames, number of seeds, and top-3 model selection)?  
- Question: Do auxiliary off-policy updates use corrections (e.g., importance sampling) and how sensitive is performance to this?  
- Question: How is pixel-change reward normalized across games with different visual dynamics, and how critical is λPC scaling?  
- Question: Does reward-prediction replay risk overfitting or unintended policy bias; any diagnostics?  
- Question: Can learned auxiliary control policies be leveraged for exploration or transfer beyond representation shaping?
