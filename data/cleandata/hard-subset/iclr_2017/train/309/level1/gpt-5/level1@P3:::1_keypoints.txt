- Proposal: UNREAL augments A3C with unsupervised auxiliary tasks to shape a shared CNN+LSTM representation and improve data efficiency and performance.
- Core auxiliary control tasks: off-policy Q-learning for “pixel control” (per-cell pixel change) and “feature control” (maximize internal unit activations).
- Reward-focused auxiliary task: immediate reward prediction trained from a rebalanced replay to mitigate reward sparsity.
- Value function replay: off-policy replay to stabilize value estimates and exploit improved features.
- Training: combined loss integrates on-policy A3C with off-policy auxiliary heads sharing the trunk.
- Empirical results: large gains on 13 Labyrinth tasks (mean 10× speedup to A3C-best, 87% human-normalized) and 57 Atari games (mean 880%, median 250% human-normalized).
- Robustness: improved robustness to hyperparameters reported; ablations indicate pixel control is particularly beneficial.
- Decision: Accept — due to strong empirical advances, careful ablations, and practical, well-motivated synthesis of auxiliary objectives.
- Positioning: well situated in literature vs options, Horde, UVFA, predictive/model-based methods; novelty is using many off-policy control tasks as representation-only auxiliaries plus rebalanced reward prediction and spatial deconv head.
- Reproducibility: implementation details largely sufficient (architecture, loss weights, replay, schedules).
- Limitation: off-policy training with a shared recurrent trunk is under-specified (handling of LSTM hidden states, sequence length, burn-in, target networks).
- Limitation: fairness of comparisons — UNREAL uses replay and extra compute per env step; no normalization by total updates or wall-clock time.
- Limitation: reward rebalancing may bias shared features and indirectly affect policy/value despite not directly changing objectives.
- Limitation: Atari ablations are limited (only “no pixel control”); more granular ablations (no reward prediction, no value replay) needed for generality.
- Suggested clarification: detail how LSTM hidden states are handled during off-policy replay and whether auxiliary gradients flow through recurrent state.
- Suggested experiment: report per-frame update counts, wall-clock, and a matched-update baseline (e.g., A3C with extra value replay) to isolate auxiliary-task benefits from extra compute.
- Suggested ablations: Atari-wide removal of reward prediction and value replay to show which components drive gains across domains.
- Suggested analysis: sensitivity plots for auxiliary loss weights (λPC, λVR, λRP) and guidance for setting them.
- Suggested analysis: failure modes and tasks where pixel control may hurt; per-game breakdown of gains/losses.
- Suggested baseline: compare to simple intrinsic-reward/curiosity methods to contrast representation-only auxiliaries vs behavior-driving intrinsic rewards.
- Minor suggestion: expand or relegate preliminary feature-control experiments to appendix to keep narrative focused.
- Key questions for authors: details on LSTM state handling during replay, use and update frequency of target networks for Q-heads, compute budget vs A3C, Atari ablations for reward prediction/value replay, sensitivity to λ hyperparameters, tasks where pixel control hurts, and impacts of reward rebalancing on long-horizon credit assignment.
