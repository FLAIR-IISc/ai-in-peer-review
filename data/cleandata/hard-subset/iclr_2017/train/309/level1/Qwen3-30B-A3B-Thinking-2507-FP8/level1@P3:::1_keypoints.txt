- UNREAL proposes learning multiple auxiliary pseudo-reward tasks (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction) alongside extrinsic rewards with experience replay, yielding large gains on Atari and Labyrinth (880% and 87% human performance; 10× faster learning).

- Decision: Accept — strong empirical improvements in data efficiency and robustness with clear motivation.

- Motivation: Environments contain abundant learning signals; auxiliary control tasks better support representation learning than traditional unsupervised objectives.

- Pixel/feature control vs prediction: Learning to control features/pixels outperforms pixel reconstruction or simple prediction for representation learning.

- Experimental rigor: Thorough ablation studies, multiple baselines, 57 Atari games and 13 Labyrinth levels, human-normalized metrics showing higher final performance, faster learning, and hyperparameter robustness.

- Literature placement: Clear relation and distinction from Horde, UVFA, and successor representations by using shared representations rather than separate value functions per auxiliary task.

- Experience replay & reward prediction: Integrating replay with auxiliary tasks enables efficient learning without disrupting policy; reward prediction shapes features without biasing policy.

- Improvement suggestion 1: Provide stronger theoretical justification linking pixel/feature control to properties of an optimal representation.

- Improvement suggestion 2: Analyze why feature control is only slightly worse than pixel control and characterize the specific network features being controlled.

- Improvement suggestion 3: Discuss potential limitations and scalability to more complex environments or tasks where auxiliaries may be less effective.

- Question 1: Why does a control objective for pixel changes yield better representations than a prediction objective — is this a fundamental control vs prediction difference?

- Question 2: Why is pixel control particularly crucial for exploration-heavy Labyrinth levels, and can feature control compensate when pixel control is removed?

- Question 3: Clarify the meaning of 116% human-normalized score on lt hallway slope — how does this reflect actual performance relative to humans and any normalization artifacts?
