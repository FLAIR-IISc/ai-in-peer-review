- Introduces UNREAL: an A3C-based agent that jointly learns auxiliary control tasks and reward prediction to improve representations for RL.
- Claims that controlling sensorimotor signals (pixel/feature control) yields better representations than reconstructing them.
- Integrates pixel control, feature control, and reward prediction auxiliary tasks into a unified architecture.
- Reports strong empirical gains: 880% human on Atari and 87% human on Labyrinth with ~10× faster learning.
- Recommendation: Accept — significant contribution showing auxiliary tasks improve data efficiency and robustness.
- Well-motivated and well-positioned relative to prior work (Horde, UVFA, successor representations).
- Ablation studies demonstrate each UNREAL component meaningfully contributes to performance.
- Comparison to unsupervised baselines shows pixel control outperforms prediction/reconstruction baselines.
- Labyrinth results highlight both higher final performance and much faster learning than A3C.
- Demonstrates robustness to hyperparameter choices, addressing common sensitivity concerns in deep RL.
- Needs clearer discussion of additional computational cost of auxiliary tasks versus A3C and whether learning speedup offsets overhead.
- Could analyze how the choice of which hidden units to control affects performance.
- Requests more detail (diagram/description) of the "deconvolutional network" used for auxiliary value prediction.
- Clarify how the "negative reward" class is defined in reward prediction training.
- Question how skewed sampling for reward prediction (overrepresenting rewards) affects feature shaping and whether it biases policy learning.
- Ask for clarification of the 880% human Atari metric: how it’s computed and whether it’s averaged/normalized across the 57 games.
