- Paper introduces UNREAL: an agent that learns multiple pseudo-reward auxiliary tasks (auxiliary control + reward prediction) alongside A3C to develop shared representations even without extrinsic rewards.
- Auxiliary tasks: (1) auxiliary control tasks (maximize pixel or feature changes) and (2) immediate reward prediction; combined with A3C and off-policy Q-learning for auxiliaries.
- Empirical claim: large performance and data-efficiency gains on Atari and Labyrinth by adding auxiliary tasks.
- Decision: Accept.
- Strong empirical contribution with rigorous results demonstrating auxiliary tasks’ effectiveness.
- Addresses meaningful RL problem—sparse extrinsic rewards and limits of reconstruction-based unsupervised learning.
- Thorough evaluation: Atari (57 games) and Labyrinth (13 levels) with multiple ablation studies.
- Reported results: 880% human-normalized on Atari, 87% on Labyrinth, and ~10× faster learning/data efficiency.
- Ablations show each component contributes; pixel control and reward prediction are particularly important.
- Well-placed in related work; distinguishes from Horde, UVFA, successor representations, and prior auxiliary prediction tasks.
- Methodologically sound: A3C main policy + off-policy Q-learning for auxiliaries; experience replay for reward prediction to handle sparsity.
- Implementation details and hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (Figures 3, 7–8) support reproducibility and robustness claims.
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis of why pixel/feature control outperform alternatives and how representations develop.
- Suggestion: include visualizations/analysis of representation evolution with and without extrinsic rewards.
- Suggestion: discuss rationale and trade-offs for not using pseudo-reward-maximizing policies directly (future direction).
- Suggestion: more explicit connection between auxiliary tasks and biological/cognitive learning mechanisms.
- Question: why does pixel control slightly outperform feature control, and would that hold in more complex environments?
- Question: how sensitive are results to reward-prediction sampling skew (50% non-zero rewards); would different ratios help?
- Question: how would auxiliary deconvolutional network performance change with different architectures (fully connected or alternative conv nets)?
