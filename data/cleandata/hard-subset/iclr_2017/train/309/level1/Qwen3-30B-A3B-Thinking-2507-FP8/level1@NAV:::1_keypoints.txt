- Introduces UNREAL: simultaneously learns multiple policies for pseudo-rewards (pixel changes, feature activations) while predicting extrinsic rewards to shape representations.  
- Claims this representation shaping yields faster learning and better performance than standard RL.  
- Clearly motivated by abundance of learning signals in environments beyond primary reward.  
- Distinguishes from related work (Horde, UVFA, successor representations) by using auxiliary tasks to shape representation rather than as policy components.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Main reasons for acceptance: novel approach justified by theory and empirical evidence.  
- Strong empirical results: Labyrinth 87% human (vs. A3C 54%) with 10× speedup; Atari 880% human, outperforming prior work.  
- Robust experimental validation: ablations show each auxiliary component helps and hyperparameter sensitivity is addressed.  
- Comparison to unsupervised methods (pixel reconstruction vs. control) supports the core hypothesis.  
- Suggested improvements: more explicit explanation of why controlling features beats predicting them, and deeper analysis of which pseudo-rewards help which tasks.  
- Open questions raised: scalability when pseudo-reward space is large, and whether the agent can learn to identify/select the most relevant pseudo-rewards rather than using all equally.
