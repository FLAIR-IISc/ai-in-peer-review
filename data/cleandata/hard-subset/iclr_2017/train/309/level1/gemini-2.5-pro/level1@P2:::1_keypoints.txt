- Introduces UNREAL, an agent architecture that accelerates deep RL by training auxiliary tasks alongside the main task to learn richer representations.  
- Proposes auxiliary "control" tasks (pixel control, feature control) framed as RL problems and trained off-policy via Q-learning.  
- Adds an auxiliary reward prediction task using rebalanced replay to improve sensitivity to reward-relevant states, aiding sparse-reward problems.  
- Combines on-policy A3C for the main policy with off-policy replay updates for auxiliary tasks and value learning in a hybrid architecture.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on 57 Atari games and a new 3D Labyrinth environment, showing state-of-the-art performance and large speedups (e.g., ~10x on Labyrinth).  
- Shows substantial improvements in data efficiency, final performance, and robustness to hyperparameters compared to strong baselines.  
- Ablation studies isolate the contribution of each auxiliary task and show they are complementary.  
- Framing auxiliary tasks as control problems outperforms reconstruction/prediction baselines (e.g., pixel control beats change prediction and reconstruction).  
- The hybrid on-/off-policy approach leverages stability from A3C and data efficiency from replay-based updates.  
- Experiments cover diverse domains (Atari and Labyrinth), supporting the generality of the approach.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation, architecture diagrams, and experimental setup.  
- Recommendation: Accept, based on novelty, conceptual importance, and exceptional empirical evidence.  
- Terminology concern: calling the method "Unsupervised" may be misleading — auxiliary tasks are self-supervised rather than truly unsupervised.  
- Feature control results are preliminary; more thorough investigation could show broader applicability beyond pixel-based tasks.  
- Claim that pixel reconstruction harms performance needs stronger direct evidence (e.g., feature visualizations or attention maps).  
- Question about sensitivity and principled selection of auxiliary loss weights (λPC, λRP, λVR) and whether better tuning methods exist.  
- Suggestion to explore using learned auxiliary policies for exploration or as options for hierarchical planning.  
- Question about replay buffer size: why 2,000 transitions, and how performance changes with larger buffers or different replay strategies.
