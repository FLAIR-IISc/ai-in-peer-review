- Introduces UNREAL: an RL agent using auxiliary tasks to improve representation learning and efficiency beyond extrinsic rewards.  
- Proposes auxiliary control tasks (pixel control, feature control) via off-policy value functions to learn controllable aspects of the environment.  
- Introduces auxiliary reward prediction trained from a balanced replay buffer to mitigate reward sparsity.  
- Combines on-policy A3C for the main task with off-policy Q-learning for auxiliary tasks, sharing experience via a replay buffer.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on 57 Atari games and the Labyrinth 3D environment, outperforming A3C in final performance, data efficiency (10x on Labyrinth), and hyperparameter robustness.  
- Decision: Accept — judged a significant and high-quality contribution.  
- Primary reason 1: Novel, impactful representation learning by framing learning-as-control rather than passive prediction.  
- Primary reason 2: Comprehensive, rigorous experiments including ablations and comparisons to alternative unsupervised methods.  
- Key empirical finding: Pixel control outperforms pixel prediction/reconstruction, supporting the claim that control-based auxiliary tasks yield better features.  
- Reported results: average 880% human expert on Atari and 87% on Labyrinth; ablations show each auxiliary component contributes positively.  
- Suggested improvement: report computational overhead (training time/memory) relative to A3C.  
- Suggested improvement: provide guidance and sensitivity analysis for auxiliary loss weights (λVR, λPC, λRP).  
- Suggested improvement: clarify or expand preliminary feature control experiments.  
- Question: Why choose a 20×20 grid for pixel control and how do different grid sizes affect performance and cost?  
- Question: How is non-stationarity handled when auxiliary tasks depend on the agent’s evolving hidden activations?  
- Question: Was balanced sampling for reward prediction compared to prioritized sampling (e.g., TD-error)?  
- Question: Why does pixel reconstruction hurt performance here—architecture, loss weighting, or focus on irrelevant details?
