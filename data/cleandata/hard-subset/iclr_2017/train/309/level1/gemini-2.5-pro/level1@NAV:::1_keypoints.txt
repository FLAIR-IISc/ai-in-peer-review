- Introduces UNREAL: augment A3C with off-policy auxiliary tasks (pixel/feature control and reward prediction) trained from replay to improve representation learning.  
- Core idea: combine stable on-policy policy learning with data-efficient off-policy auxiliary objectives.  
- Auxiliary tasks include control to maximize changes in pixels/features and reward prediction from short experience sequences.  
- Claims richer environment understanding leading to improved performance and robustness.  
- Empirical results: state-of-the-art on 57 Atari games (880% human) and 10× learning speedup on the Labyrinth 3D domain.  
- Strong experimental validation with extensive ablation studies showing each auxiliary component contributes to gains.  
- Strengths: elegant/effective synthesis of recent deep RL advances and clear empirical improvements.  
- Suggestion: provide more analysis of sensitivity and stability to auxiliary loss weights (λ).  
- Insight: comparison between pixel control and pixel reconstruction is valuable; recommend expanding on why control may yield more causal representations.  
- Question 1: quantify computational overhead (wall-clock time/FLOPs) of auxiliary tasks versus A3C baseline.  
- Question 2: have pixel control and feature control been combined, and do they offer complementary benefits?
