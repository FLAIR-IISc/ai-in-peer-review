- Introduces UNREAL: augments A3C with simultaneous self-supervised auxiliary tasks to build richer representations and improve speed, performance, and robustness.
- Proposes auxiliary control tasks (pixel control, feature control) trained off-policy via Q-learning to maximize pseudo-rewards for representation learning.
- Proposes auxiliary reward task (reward prediction) trained as supervised classification with replay rebalancing to oversample rewarding events.
- Architecture combines on-policy A3C for the main task with off-policy auxiliary updates and an added value regression loss from a single experience stream.
- Empirical results: state-of-the-art average 880% human score on Atari and 87% on Labyrinth, with ~10x learning speedup over A3C.
- Decision: Accept — contribution is significant and well-motivated, reframing representation learning as control problems is novel and effective.
- Strong, rigorous empirical support with extensive experiments across Atari and Labyrinth and thorough ablation studies isolating each auxiliary component.
- Hybrid on-policy/off-policy design is technically sound and leverages data efficiency of off-policy updates while optimizing the main policy on-policy.
- Ablations show pixel control is more beneficial than prediction/reconstruction; UNREAL is more stable and robust to hyperparameters than A3C.
- Terminology concern: “Unsupervised Reinforcement Learning” phrasing could be clarified to avoid confusion with traditional unsupervised learning.
- Hyperparameter concern: pixel-control loss weight (lambda_PC) was tuned per domain; a normalization scheme for pseudo-rewards could improve generality.
- Feature control results are preliminary and need fuller integration and analysis within the full UNREAL agent.
- Question: Was the deconvolutional head critical for pixel control vs simpler alternatives (e.g., shared MLP + linear heads)?
- Question: How would reward-prediction replay rebalancing behave in dense-reward environments—still effective or necessary?
- Question: Have you considered using auxiliary control policies directly (intrinsic motivation or options) rather than solely for representation shaping?
- Question: How sensitive is overall performance to auxiliary loss weights (lambda_VR, lambda_PC, lambda_RP) and how critical is their tuning?
