- Introduces UNREAL: agent that jointly learns multiple pseudo-reward (auxiliary) tasks alongside the primary reward to shape richer representations.
- Core idea: encourage control of sensorimotor stream features to create more informative, adaptable representations.
- Demonstrates significant improvements in learning speed and performance on Atari and 3D Labyrinth, claiming SOTA results.
- Approach is well-motivated and well-positioned relative to related work (UVFA, successor representations, auxiliary prediction tasks).
- Architectural design (A3C + auxiliary tasks + replay) is sensible and justified.
- Labyrinth experiments are comprehensive and convincing, showing clear improvements and a reported ~10x speedup.
- Decision: Reject — strong contribution but Atari results lack sufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning and ablations.
- Concern that Atari hyperparameters may have been heavily retuned for these games, making the 880% human performance claim suspect.
- Atari results lack per-game performance breakdowns and variability analysis, limiting assessment of robustness.
- The presented average performance curves and small table provide limited insight into result variability.
- Need targeted ablation studies within Atari to determine individual contributions and interactions of each auxiliary task.
- Require statistical significance testing (e.g., t-tests) to validate performance gains on both Labyrinth and Atari.
- Replay buffer skewing toward rewarding events needs explicit analysis to determine its impact on Atari results.
- Request detailed hyperparameter search documentation (ranges, selection methodology) for Labyrinth and Atari.
- Ask for Atari-specific ablation studies removing pixel control, reward prediction, etc., with performance outcomes.
- Request per-game tables/plots comparing UNREAL and baselines to show robustness across Atari titles.
- Ask for comparison results with and without the skewed replay buffer to quantify its effect.
- Request discussion of additional computational cost and resource requirements of UNREAL versus A3C.
- Suggest visualizations of learned features/policies from auxiliary tasks to explain representational gains.
- Specific questions for authors: detailed Atari hyperparameter search process and ranges explored.
- Specific question: additional computation (training time, GPU hours) required for UNREAL vs A3C in both domains.
- Specific question: any cases where an auxiliary task degraded performance on particular Atari games, and mitigation strategies.
- Specific question: rationale for using three reward prediction classes (zero, positive, negative) and alternatives considered.
- Specific question: what percentage of replay buffer samples came from rewarding events after skewing?
