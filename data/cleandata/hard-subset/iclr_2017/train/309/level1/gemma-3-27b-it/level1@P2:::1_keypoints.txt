- Introduces augmenting RL with auxiliary sensorimotor-control tasks to improve representation learning.
- Well-motivated core idea: controlling diverse aspects of the environment yields more robust and efficient learning.
- Strong empirical results on Atari and 3D Labyrinth, substantially outperforming prior state of the art.
- Recommendation: Reject, despite promising approach and results.
- Main reason for rejection: insufficient detail about hyperparameter tuning undermines confidence in robustness claims.
- Authors report 50 random hyperparameter settings but don’t specify sampling method or ranges for each hyperparameter.
- Reporting best performance across runs may mask sensitivity to particular hyperparameter choices or “lucky” selections.
- Ablation studies are present but lack granularity; relative contributions of pixel control, feature control, and reward prediction are unclear.
- Robustness claims need more rigorous justification given system complexity and limited experimental detail.
- Suggestion: provide full hyperparameter search spaces, ranges, and result variability (e.g., standard deviations) across runs.
- Suggestion: present more detailed ablations removing each auxiliary task individually and in combinations, summarized in a table.
- Suggestion: add qualitative analyses (visualizations of learned policies/activations) to illustrate what auxiliary tasks teach the agent.
- Suggestion: report computational cost of UNREAL vs. A3C (training time, memory) to assess cost–benefit tradeoff.
- Suggestion: explicitly define “robustness” (to hyperparameters, initial conditions, or environment perturbations).
- Questions for authors: detail hyperparameter search methodology, quantify each auxiliary task’s contribution, provide computational overhead, explain Labyrinth level selection and potential bias, and note any cases where auxiliary tasks hurt performance.
