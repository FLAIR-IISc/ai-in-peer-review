## Review of "Deep Reinforcement Learning with Auxiliary Control Tasks"

This paper introduces a compelling approach to deep reinforcement learning by augmenting standard RL with auxiliary tasks designed to learn a richer representation of the environment. The core idea – simultaneously learning policies for multiple pseudo-reward functions alongside the primary task – is well-motivated by the observation that environments offer far more learning signals than just extrinsic rewards. The authors convincingly argue that this multi-objective learning can accelerate learning and improve performance, particularly in visually complex environments. The results presented on both Atari and the challenging Labyrinth tasks are highly impressive, significantly outperforming state-of-the-art methods and demonstrating substantial improvements in data efficiency.

**Reject.** While the paper presents strong empirical results, the description of the experimental setup and ablation studies lacks sufficient detail to fully understand *why* the proposed approach works so well. Specifically, the contributions of each auxiliary task aren't clearly disentangled. 

The paper demonstrates impressive performance gains, but the explanation of *how* these gains are achieved remains somewhat opaque. The authors demonstrate the superiority of their approach relative to standard RL and some related baselines (like reconstruction loss), however, it is less clear how the individual auxiliary tasks contribute and interact with each other. The ablation studies, while present, could be more granular to isolate the impact of specific components. The dependence on hyperparameters and the robustness to these parameters is mentioned but not sufficiently explored in the supplementary materials.  The specific details of the hyperparameter sweeps and the effect of different weighting schemes for the auxiliary losses are crucial for reproducibility and understanding the sensitivity of the approach.

To further strengthen this work, I recommend the authors:

*   **Provide a more detailed analysis of the learned representations.** Visualizing the activations of the network, particularly in response to different auxiliary tasks, would provide valuable insights into what the agent is learning.
*   **Conduct more extensive ablation studies.** A more fine-grained analysis of the impact of each auxiliary task, potentially including combinations of two or three tasks, would help clarify their individual contributions.
*   **Clarify the weighting of auxiliary losses.** Discuss the rationale behind the chosen weighting schemes and explore the sensitivity of the results to different weightings.
*   **How does the performance scale with the number of auxiliary tasks?** Is there a point of diminishing returns, or even negative impact, as more tasks are added?
