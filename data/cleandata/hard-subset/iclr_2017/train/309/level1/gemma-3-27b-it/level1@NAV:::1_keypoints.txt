- Introduces auxiliary control tasks added to standard deep RL to learn richer environment representations.  
- Core idea: simultaneously learn policies for multiple pseudo-reward functions alongside the primary task.  
- Motivation: environments provide more learning signals than extrinsic rewards, enabling multi-objective learning.  
- Claims multi-objective learning accelerates learning and improves performance, especially in visually complex domains.  
- Empirical results on Atari and Labyrinth are strong, significantly outperforming state-of-the-art and improving data efficiency.  
- Recommendation: Reject due to insufficient detail in experimental setup and ablation studies to explain why it works.  
- Main weakness: contributions of individual auxiliary tasks are not clearly disentangled or analyzed.  
- Ablation studies are present but not granular enough to isolate specific component impacts.  
- Hyperparameter dependence and robustness are mentioned but not thoroughly explored or documented for reproducibility.  
- Missing details on hyperparameter sweeps and the effects of different auxiliary-loss weighting schemes.  
- Suggested improvements: visualize learned representations, run finer-grained ablations (including task combinations), clarify loss weighting rationale and sensitivity, and evaluate scaling/diminishing returns as more auxiliary tasks are added.
