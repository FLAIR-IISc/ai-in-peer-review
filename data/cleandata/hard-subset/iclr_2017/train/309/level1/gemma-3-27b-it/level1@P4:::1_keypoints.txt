- Introduces UNREAL: an agent that jointly learns cumulative reward and multiple pseudo-reward functions to build better representations, showing strong gains on Atari and Labyrinth.
- Recommendation: Reject — strong empirical results but insufficient clarity and weaker-than-claimed novelty.
- Main critique: Paper lacks clear description of how the various components interact and the rationale for key design choices.
- Novelty concern: Approach appears incremental, combining prior ideas (UVFA, Horde, successor representations) without a clearly articulated novel contribution.
- Weak justification for auxiliary tasks: No compelling argument why pixel/control/reward-prediction tasks were chosen or how they specifically improve representations.
- Insufficient ablation: Ablation studies are limited; a more systematic evaluation of each component’s contribution is needed.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Weighting of loss terms (λVR, λPC, λRP) is chosen empirically and sensitivity analysis is inadequate beyond Figure 8.
- Environment generalization: Large Labyrinth gains may reflect environment specifics; results should be validated across more Atari games and diverse tasks.
- Deconvolutional network description: Architecture choices (filter sizes, strides) and motivations are unclear and under-explained.
- Reward-prediction network design: Rationale for a separate feedforward reward-prediction network rather than integrating it with the LSTM is not provided.
- Policy connection: The mechanism by which auxiliary tasks (pixel/control/features) translate to improved primary-task policies is not fully elucidated.
- Suggestion: Explicitly clarify novelty and differences from UVFA/Horde with a concise comparison table.
- Suggestion: Provide detailed analysis/visualizations of learned representations to demonstrate improved feature quality or disentanglement.
- Suggestion: Conduct a thorough, systematic ablation over auxiliary tasks and hyperparameters to quantify contributions.
- Suggestion: Test transferability of learned representations to new tasks/environments to demonstrate generality.
- Suggestion: Expand experiments to a wider range of environments to validate robustness of improvements.
- Suggestion: Fully document and motivate the deconvolutional architecture choices.
- Suggestion: Explain why reward prediction is implemented separately and whether integration with recurrent components was considered.
- Suggestion: Report computational cost and training overhead of UNREAL versus baseline agents.
- Question 1: What theoretical/practical criteria guided selection of auxiliary tasks (pixel changes, network features, reward prediction)?
- Question 2: How sensitive are results to loss-weighting (λVR, λPC, λRP) and could an automatic weighting scheme be used?
- Question 3: Can you show examples of learned features and how they relate to the primary task?
- Question 4: What is UNREAL’s computational overhead relative to standard A3C in training time and resources?
- Question 5: Under what conditions might reconstruction-based objectives be beneficial (e.g., noisy or incomplete observations)?
