- Decision: Accept the paper.  
- Significant improvement over state-of-the-art on Labyrinth and Atari, demonstrating the approach's effectiveness.  
- Well-motivated and well-placed in the literature with a thorough related-work review.  
- Effective use of auxiliary control tasks (e.g., pixel/feature control); combining tasks yields further gains.  
- Greater robustness to hyperparameters than A3C, attributed to auxiliary tasks and experience replay.  
- Well-designed experiments across Labyrinth and Atari with multiple evaluation metrics and comparisons.  
- Paper is well-written overall but some sections (notably experience replay) need clearer implementation details.  
- More analysis is needed to explain why UNREAL outperforms A3C.  
- Suggestion to discuss future directions, such as other domains or additional auxiliary tasks.  
- Request for details on experience replay implementation: replay buffer size and sampling strategy.  
- Question on how auxiliary tasks were selected and whether other tasks could further improve performance.  
- Request for more granular Labyrinth analysis: per-level performance and types of learned behaviors.
