**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

This paper introduces a novel deep reinforcement learning agent that learns to maximize cumulative rewards while also learning separate policies for multiple pseudo-reward functions. The agent uses a shared representation that continues to develop in the absence of extrinsic rewards, and a mechanism to focus this representation on extrinsic rewards. The authors claim that their agent, called UNREAL, significantly outperforms the previous state-of-the-art on Atari games, achieving an average of 880% expert human performance, and a challenging suite of first-person, three-dimensional Labyrinth tasks, leading to a mean speedup in learning of 10× and averaging 87% expert human performance.

**Decision: Accept**

I decide to accept this paper due to its significant contributions to the field of deep reinforcement learning. The authors have demonstrated a novel approach that combines the benefits of two state-of-the-art approaches, A3C and auxiliary tasks, to achieve impressive results on challenging tasks.

**Supporting Arguments**

1. **Well-motivated approach**: The authors have clearly motivated their approach by highlighting the limitations of traditional reinforcement learning methods, which focus solely on maximizing cumulative rewards. They have also provided a thorough review of related work, demonstrating a deep understanding of the field.
2. **Strong empirical results**: The authors have presented extensive experimental results on two challenging domains, Atari and Labyrinth, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach. The results show significant improvements over the previous state-of-the-art methods, both in terms of performance and learning speed.

**Additional Feedback**

1. **Clarity and organization**: The paper is well-organized, but some sections could be clarified for better understanding. For example, the section on related work could be condensed, and the section on experiments could be expanded to provide more details on the experimental setup.
2. **Theoretical analysis**: While the authors have provided some theoretical insights into their approach, a more in-depth analysis of the theoretical properties of their method would strengthen the paper.
3. **Broader impact**: The authors could discuss the broader implications of their work, such as potential applications in robotics, game playing, or other areas where reinforcement learning is used.
