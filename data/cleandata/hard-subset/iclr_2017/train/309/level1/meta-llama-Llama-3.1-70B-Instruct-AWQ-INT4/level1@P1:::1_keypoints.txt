- Introduces UNREAL: learns separate policies for many pseudo-rewards with a shared, continually developing representation and a mechanism to focus that representation on extrinsic rewards; achieves strong results (≈880% human on Atari, ≈87% on Labyrinth, ~10× learning speedup).
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Well-motivated approach addressing efficient and robust learning by using auxiliary pseudo-reward tasks.
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical support with extensive experiments showing performance, data efficiency, and robustness across Atari and Labyrinth.
- Supporting argument: Clear, well-structured presentation with thorough related work and ablation studies clarifying component contributions.
- Feedback 1: Report computational resources required for training to aid reproducibility.
- Feedback 2: Add visualizations of learned policies and pseudo-reward evolution to clarify agent behavior.
- Feedback 3: Provide more insight into hyperparameter choices and sensitivity for practitioners.
- Question 1: How were pseudo-reward functions chosen and selected per task?
- Question 2: How is the method affected when pseudo-reward functions are ill-defined or highly correlated?
