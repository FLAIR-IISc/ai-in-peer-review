- Paper introduces UNREAL: a deep RL agent that learns cumulative-reward policy plus separate policies for multiple pseudo-rewards using a shared representation and an extrinsic-focus mechanism.  
- Claims strong empirical gains: UNREAL reportedly achieves 880% human expert on Atari and ~87% on Labyrinth with ~10× learning speedup.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Motivation: Well-motivated approach with clear discussion of limitations of standard RL and good literature review.  
- Method novelty: Combines A3C with auxiliary tasks to improve representation learning and performance.  
- Empirical evidence: Extensive experiments on Atari and Labyrinth showing significant improvements over prior state-of-the-art in performance and learning speed.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-organized but some sections (related work, experiment details) need condensation/expansion for clarity.  
- Theory: Provides some theoretical insight but would benefit from deeper theoretical analysis of properties.  
- Broader impact: Authors should discuss potential applications (robotics, game playing) and broader implications.  
- Question—hyperparameters: Request for more details on UNREAL hyperparameter tuning process.  
- Question—extensions: How to extend to more complex settings like multi-agent or hierarchical RL?  
- Question—limitations: Request discussion of potential limitations, e.g., reliance on many auxiliary tasks or overfitting risk.
