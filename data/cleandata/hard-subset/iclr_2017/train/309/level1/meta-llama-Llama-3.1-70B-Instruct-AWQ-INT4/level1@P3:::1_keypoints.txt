- Introduces UNREAL, a deep RL agent that learns many pseudo-reward functions alongside extrinsic reward.  
- Combines auxiliary control tasks, reward prediction, and experience replay to learn better environment representations.  
- Claims large state-of-the-art gains: 880% expert human on Atari and 87% on Labyrinth.  
- Decision: Accept — novel, well-motivated approach with strong empirical results and clear writing.  
- Supporting: Well-motivated critique of extrinsic-only RL and justification for learning auxiliary controllers.  
- Supporting: Significant experimental improvements with comparisons to baselines and ablation studies.  
- Feedback: Request more detailed discussion of related work, especially other auxiliary-task or reward-prediction methods.  
- Feedback: Ask for deeper analysis/visualization of the learned representations and which features are controlled.  
- Feedback: Request discussion of UNREAL’s limitations and environments where it may fail.  
- Question: How are auxiliary control tasks selected and designed; what criteria or heuristics are used?  
- Question: How can UNREAL scale to more complex environments with many possible pseudo-reward functions?  
- Question: How does the learned representation relate to the extrinsic reward and enable generalization to new tasks?
