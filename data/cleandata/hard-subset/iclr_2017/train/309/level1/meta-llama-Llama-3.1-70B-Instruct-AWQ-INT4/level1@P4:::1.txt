**Summary of the Paper**

This paper introduces a novel deep reinforcement learning agent, called UNREAL, that learns to predict and control features of the sensorimotor stream by treating them as pseudo-rewards for reinforcement learning. The agent uses a combination of auxiliary control tasks, auxiliary reward prediction tasks, and experience replay to improve its performance and data efficiency. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on the challenging 3D Labyrinth environment and 57 Atari games, achieving state-of-the-art results on both tasks.

**Decision: Accept**

I recommend accepting this paper due to its significant contributions to the field of deep reinforcement learning and its well-motivated approach to addressing the challenges of learning from sparse rewards. The paper provides a clear and thorough explanation of the proposed method, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness on two challenging tasks.

**Supporting Arguments**

1. **Well-motivated approach**: The authors provide a clear motivation for their approach, highlighting the limitations of traditional reinforcement learning methods that focus solely on maximizing extrinsic rewards. They argue that by learning to predict and control features of the sensorimotor stream, their agent can develop a more robust and generalizable representation of the environment.
2. **Technical soundness**: The paper provides a thorough explanation of the proposed method, including the architecture of the agent, the auxiliary tasks, and the experience replay mechanism. The authors also provide a clear description of the experimental setup and the evaluation metrics used to assess the performance of their agent.
3. **Significant contributions**: The paper makes significant contributions to the field of deep reinforcement learning, achieving state-of-the-art results on two challenging tasks. The authors also provide insights into the benefits of using auxiliary tasks and experience replay to improve the performance and data efficiency of reinforcement learning agents.

**Additional Feedback**

1. **More analysis of the auxiliary tasks**: While the authors provide a clear explanation of the auxiliary tasks, it would be helpful to see more analysis of their effectiveness and how they contribute to the overall performance of the agent.
2. **Comparison to other methods**: The authors compare their approach to A3C and Prioritized Dueling DQN, but it would be helpful to see comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods in deep reinforcement learning.
3. **More details on the experimental setup**: While the authors provide a clear description of the experimental setup, it would be helpful to see more details on the hyperparameter tuning process and the computational resources used to train the agents.

**Questions for the Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process and how you selected the values for the weighting terms (λVR, λPC, λRP)?
2. How do you think the performance of your agent would be affected if you used a different type of auxiliary task, such as learning to predict the next state or the reward function?
3. Can you provide more insights into the benefits of using experience replay and how it contributes to the overall performance of the agent?
