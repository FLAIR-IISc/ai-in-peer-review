This paper introduces a novel deep reinforcement learning agent that learns to predict and control various aspects of the sensorimotor stream, in addition to maximising cumulative reward. The agent uses a combination of auxiliary control tasks, reward prediction tasks, and experience replay to improve its performance and robustness. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on the challenging 3D Labyrinth environment and 57 Atari games, achieving state-of-the-art results on both domains.

**Decision: Accept**

The main reasons for my decision are:

1. **Significant improvement over state-of-the-art**: The UNREAL agent achieves significant improvements over the current state-of-the-art agents on both the Labyrinth and Atari domains, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
2. **Well-motivated and well-placed in the literature**: The paper provides a clear motivation for the proposed approach, highlighting the limitations of traditional reinforcement learning methods and the benefits of using auxiliary tasks and experience replay. The authors also provide a thorough review of related work, placing their approach in the context of existing research.

**Supporting arguments:**

1. **Effective use of auxiliary tasks**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of using auxiliary control tasks, such as pixel control and feature control, to improve the agent's performance and robustness. They also show that combining different auxiliary tasks leads to further improvements.
2. **Improved robustness to hyperparameters**: The UNREAL agent is shown to be more robust to hyperparameter settings than the A3C agent, demonstrating the benefits of using auxiliary tasks and experience replay.
3. **Well-designed experiments**: The authors conduct a thorough evaluation of their approach on both the Labyrinth and Atari domains, using a range of evaluation metrics and comparing their results to state-of-the-art agents.

**Additional feedback:**

1. **Clarity and organization**: The paper is well-written and easy to follow, but some sections could be improved for clarity and organization. For example, the section on experience replay could be expanded to provide more details on the implementation.
2. **More analysis of the results**: While the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, more analysis of the results could be provided to help understand why the UNREAL agent performs better than the A3C agent.
3. **Future directions**: The authors could provide more discussion on future directions for their research, such as applying the UNREAL agent to other domains or exploring new auxiliary tasks.

**Questions for the authors:**

1. Can you provide more details on the implementation of the experience replay mechanism, including the size of the replay buffer and the sampling strategy used?
2. How did you select the specific auxiliary tasks used in the UNREAL agent, and are there other tasks that could be used to improve performance?
3. Can you provide more analysis of the results on the Labyrinth domain, including the performance of the UNREAL agent on individual levels and the types of behaviors learned by the agent?
