- Proposes UNREAL: an A3C agent augmented with off-policy auxiliary RL objectives (pixel-control, feature-control, reward prediction, value replay) sharing a common representation.  
- Two auxiliary task families: control tasks (pixel/feature control via n-step Q-learning from replay) and reward-focused tasks (immediate reward prediction on a skewed replay buffer + value function replay).  
- Combined loss integrates on-policy A3C with off-policy auxiliary objectives to keep shaping representations when extrinsic rewards are sparse.  
- Empirical results: large gains on 13 Labyrinth tasks (mean 10× fewer frames to A3C’s best) and improved mean/median human-normalized scores on 57 Atari games.  
- Demonstrates improved robustness to hyperparameters compared to baselines.  
- Ablations in Labyrinth indicate auxiliary control outperforms pixel reconstruction and immediate pixel-change prediction.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Primary reason 1: Strong, consistent empirical improvements across two challenging domains with careful ablations and robustness analyses.  
- Primary reason 2: Clear, well-motivated integration of auxiliary control and reward-focused prediction within a unified on/off-policy framework addressing sparse rewards and representation learning.  
- Problem/motivation: Targets representation learning under sparse/delayed rewards and argues control/prediction shapes better features than reconstruction.  
- Methodological soundness: Off-policy Q-learning heads and pixel-control deconv head are appropriate and efficient; reward prediction is balanced and decoupled from policy/value updates.  
- Empirical support: Strong Labyrinth results, competitive Atari performance, robustness sweeps, and targeted ablations (most component analyses on Labyrinth).  
- Rigor and clarity: Implementation details and baselines are described sufficiently for reproduction; ablations test central hypotheses.  
- Tempering point: Compute vs data efficiency not fully quantified—needs parameter-update/wall-clock cost comparisons to control for extra replay updates.  
- Tempering point: Full component-wise ablations mostly on Labyrinth; similar breakdown on Atari would strengthen generality claims.  
- Tempering point: Atari evaluation protocol details (training frames, start/termination conditions, averaging over seeds) need clearer specification for fair comparison.  
- Requested clarification: Report wall-clock training time and parameter-update counts per frame; consider A3C with extra replay-based value regression as a control.  
- Requested clarification: Specify Atari training/evaluation protocol (frames, no-op/human starts, life-loss treatment) and provide fuller robustness statistics (averages/medians across all hyperparameter settings).  
- Requested clarification: Detail stability measures for mixed on/off-policy training (target networks, update frequency, importance sampling) and any failure modes.  
- Requested clarification: Pixel-control head design choices (ReLU on Qaux, sensitivity to grid size/crop) and observed estimation effects.  
- Requested clarification: More experiments/intuition for feature control vs pixel control, including multi-layer or sparsity constraints to avoid degeneracy.  
- Requested clarification: Sensitivity analysis for auxiliary loss weightings (λPC, λVR, λRP) and consideration of adaptive weighting schemes.  
- Requested clarification: Replay buffer design choices (size, balancing schemes) and their effects; alternatives or curricula for reward prediction.  
- Requested clarification: Evidence of representation transfer across tasks or faster fine-tuning on new levels to demonstrate generalization.
