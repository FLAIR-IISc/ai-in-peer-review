- Proposed method: UNREAL — auxiliary-task-augmented deep RL combining pixel/feature control and reward-prediction with a shared A3C backbone, skewed reward-prediction, and off-policy replay.  
- Claimed contributions: unified auxiliary control + reward-prediction framework; mechanism to focus representations on extrinsic rewards via rebalanced prediction and value replay; state-of-the-art data efficiency and robustness on Labyrinth and Atari.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: strong, broadly convincing empirical gains across two challenging domains supported by ablations.  
- Key reason: novel, well-motivated integration of auxiliary control and reward-focused prediction, clearly positioned in literature and practically useful.  
- Problem addressed: learning richer representations for sparse/partial rewards by exploiting abundant sensorimotor signals beyond extrinsic reward.  
- Literature positioning: solid coverage (Horde, UVFA, successor reps, model prediction, prioritized replay, dueling nets, A3C); using pseudo-reward control only as auxiliary training is a clear differentiator.  
- Empirical highlights: Labyrinth — human-normalized mean improved from 54% to 87% over 13 levels with ~10× speedups; Atari — mean 880% and median 250% of expert human across 57 games.  
- Ablations: pixel vs prediction vs reconstruction and value replay vs reward prediction ablations substantiate origins of gains.  
- Usefulness: practical improvements in data efficiency and hyperparameter robustness, particularly in sparse-reward settings.  
- Completeness: substantial architecture and training details provided (losses, λ ranges, replay design); code not provided but appendix aids reproduction.  
- Limitation: no statistical-significance or variance reporting; mean metrics possibly skewed by outliers.  
- Limitation: unclear compute budget and exact training-frame parity/guarantees vs baselines.  
- Requested improvement: provide per-game training frames, wall-clock times, and compute budgets; ensure fair comparison to Dueling DDQN (frames, sticky actions).  
- Requested improvement: report variance over seeds, run significance tests, and emphasize median/interquartile metrics.  
- Requested improvement: extend ablations on feature control and analyze learned representations (e.g., probing tasks).  
- Requested improvement: discuss failure cases and provide sensitivity plots for λPC/λRP beyond current hyperparameter analyses.  
- Question: how are “expert human” baselines defined (number of participants and trials)?  
- Question: were Atari evaluations run with sticky actions and standardized no-op starts?  
- Question: what was the exact training-frame budget per method and were top hyperparameters selected post hoc on test games?  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to pixel grid size and to λPC across games?  
- Question: does reward rebalancing (P(r≠0)=0.5) harm calibration or induce representation bias, and were alternative reweightings tried?  
- Question: what is the wall-clock and compute overhead of auxiliary tasks relative to baseline A3C?
