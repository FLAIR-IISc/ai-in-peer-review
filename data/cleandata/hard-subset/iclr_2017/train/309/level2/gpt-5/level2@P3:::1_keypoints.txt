- Proposes UNREAL: A3C augmented with off-policy auxiliary tasks (pixel/feature control, reward prediction, value replay) sharing a CNN+LSTM representation.  
- Auxiliary objectives use n-step Q-learning on replay for control, rebalanced replay for reward prediction, and value function replay to stabilize learning.  
- Achieves large empirical gains: 87% mean human-normalized on 13 Labyrinth levels (vs. 54% A3C) and strong improvements on 57 Atari games (880% mean, 250% median).  
- Shows robustness to hyperparameters and a ~10× reduction in samples to reach A3C best performance on Labyrinth.  
- Ablations indicate each auxiliary component contributes and pixel-control outperforms input-change prediction and reconstruction.  
- Decision: accept.  
- Key reason 1: Well-motivated unification of on-policy RL with off-policy auxiliary control and reward prediction to tackle sparse rewards and representation learning.  
- Key reason 2: Strong, broad empirical evidence with careful ablations and robustness analyses across diverse benchmarks.  
- Key reason 3: Methodological novelty in using many learned control tasks purely as auxiliary objectives with shared representation and explicit focus on extrinsic rewards.  
- Problem/motivation: Auxiliary control aligns training signals with controllable future aspects, which is better than reconstruction for agent goals; reward rebalancing addresses perceptual reward sparsity.  
- Placement in literature: Distinguishes from options/Horde/UVFA by using shared representation and auxiliary-only control tasks without goal conditioning.  
- Empirical support: Comprehensive experiments, ablations, and reproducible methodological details (replay design, deconv heads, etc.).  
- Suggestion: Clarify stability and interaction of on-policy A3C with off-policy Q-learning in a recurrent architecture, including backprop through LSTM and stale hidden states handling.  
- Suggestion: Provide systematic sensitivity analysis of loss weights (λPC, λRP, λVR) and justify Atari-specific λPC tuning.  
- Suggestion: Report compute and wall-clock training time vs. A3C and Dueling DQN to contextualize sample-efficiency gains.  
- Suggestion: Expand Atari ablations (reward prediction only, value replay only, combinations) similar to Labyrinth analysis.  
- Suggestion: Discuss limitations (reliance on visual dynamics, potential distraction from main objective if weights poorly chosen).  
- Suggestion: Add representation analysis (linear probes, t-SNE/CCA) to support claims that auxiliaries learn goal-aligned features.  
- Suggestion: Comment on potential for transfer or reuse of learned auxiliary policies/values, even if not exploited.  
- Question: Confirm ALE evaluation protocol details (action repeat, no-op starts, life termination) and alignment with Dueling DQN comparisons.  
- Question: Specify human reference scores and evaluation episodes used for human-normalized scoring.  
- Question: How are initial LSTM states set for replayed sequences and are there state-reset heuristics or auxiliary losses to mitigate mismatch?  
- Question: What is the empirical effect of 50/50 reward rebalancing on prediction and downstream performance; any introduced distributional shift?  
- Question: Sensitivity of performance to pixel-control grid size/crop and feature-control layer choice?  
- Question: Provide final-score distributions across seeds for robustness (representative tasks).  
- Question: Were very sparse games (e.g., Montezuma’s Revenge) evaluated; if not, comment on expectations and failure modes.  
- Question: For Q-learning auxiliaries, what target network update schedule was used and how sensitive is performance to it?
