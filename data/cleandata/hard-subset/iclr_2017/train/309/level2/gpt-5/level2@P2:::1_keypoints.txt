- Proposes UNREAL: A3C enhanced with auxiliary tasks (pixel-control, feature-control, reward prediction) plus value replay and shared conv+LSTM with deconv heads.
- Auxiliary control tasks use off-policy n-step Q-learning to maximize pseudo-rewards (pixel-change per cell emphasized).
- Reward-prediction auxiliary balances zero/non-zero rewards to densify supervision for reward-relevant features without biasing policy updates.
- Value function replay re-uses experience to exploit newly discovered features and improve value learning.
- Integrated architecture shares representation across tasks and uses deconvolutional heads for spatial control objectives.
- Empirical gains: large improvements on DeepMind Labyrinth (mean 87% human-normalized vs 54% for A3C; ~10× fewer steps) and strong results on 57 Atari games (mean 880%, median 250% of human).
- Ablations show each auxiliary component contributes; comparisons to reconstruction/input-change prediction support superiority of control-based auxiliaries.
- Decision: Accept — strong empirical gains, well-motivated, simple to implement, and convincingly improves RL representation learning.
- Motivation: Targets representation learning in sparse/complex visual domains and ties well to prior work (Horde, UVFA, successor features).
- Novelty: Novel combination of off-policy control auxiliaries, deconv spatial heads, reward-rebalanced prediction, and value replay integrated with A3C.
- Empirical rigor: Extensive Labyrinth experiments (13 levels), ablations, robustness checks, and Atari comparisons including UNREAL without pixel control.
- Method clarity: Loss decomposition, training protocol, and implementation details (architecture, buffer, task weights, sampling) are adequately described for replication by resourced groups.
- Evaluation fairness concern: Atari evaluation uses many hyperparameter settings per game and reports top-3 curves, which may inflate comparisons to prior work.
- Reproducibility concern: Labyrinth/DeepMind Lab is nonstandard and not publicly available, limiting external verification of Labyrinth results.
- Computational cost concern: Sample-efficiency gains shown, but added per-step computation from auxiliary losses and wall-clock training time vs A3C are not reported.
- Unclear role of feature control: Feature-control appears preliminary and it’s unclear if it’s included in final reported UNREAL across all domains.
- Atari comparability issues: Uses RGB inputs (84×84) and other eval conventions (no-op/human starts, reward clipping, life handling) need clarification to ensure fair comparison.
- Suggested improvements: report wall-clock time and compute breakdown; standardize Atari protocol; show per-component Atari ablations; clarify feature-control role; analyze reward-prediction oversampling effects.
- Additional suggested analyses: sensitivity to replay buffer size and prioritization, effect of reward-balancing ratio, and probing/saliency visualizations of learned representations.
- Questions for authors: total frames and exact Atari evaluation protocol; wall-clock time vs A3C; per-game vs single global hyperparameter settings; final agent composition (pixel vs feature control); sensitivity to reward prediction ratio; conflicts between pixel control and tasks requiring stillness; whether auxiliaries can be turned into reusable behaviors/options.
