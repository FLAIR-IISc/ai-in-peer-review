- Introduces a novel deep RL method augmenting reward maximization with auxiliary tasks predicting and controlling sensorimotor features.  
- Core idea of learning representations via multiple pseudo-rewards is well-motivated, especially for sparse-extrinsic-reward environments.  
- Demonstrates significant performance improvements on Atari and 3D Labyrinth, achieving state-of-the-art results and faster learning.  
- Recommendation: Reject, due to unclear experimental details and a superficial ablation study that leave key reasons for success opaque.  
- Primary concern: vague description of auxiliary tasks, especially pixel and feature control.  
- Deconvolutional network for auxiliary values is used without sufficient justification beyond “spatial nature.”  
- Hyperparameter sampling (λVR, λPC, λRP) ranges and motivations are not fully explained; impact on performance is unexplored.  
- Ablation study lacks depth and systematic analysis of task importance and interactions.  
- Need experiments on combinations of tasks and varying weights to reveal which components drive gains.  
- Comparison to pixel reconstruction and input-change prediction is a start but doesn’t explain why pixel control outperforms alternatives.  
- Claim that pixel reconstruction harms final performance requires stronger justification.  
- Robustness analysis (Figures 7–8) feels disconnected and should be integrated with main results to show how auxiliary tasks improve robustness.  
- Notation inconsistencies in equations (e.g., θ vs. θ-) reduce clarity.  
- Despite flaws, core idea is strong and results are promising; clearer presentation and deeper analysis could make a significant contribution.  
- Clarify motivation for using an LSTM and why it’s necessary or advantageous over alternatives.  
- Provide more detailed description of the Labyrinth environment, levels, and challenges.  
- Discuss computational cost and practical implications of adding auxiliary tasks.  
- Explore alternative loss functions for reward prediction beyond multiclass cross-entropy.  
- Visualize learned representations (e.g., hidden-unit activations) to show how auxiliary tasks shape the agent’s understanding.  
- Questions for authors: rationale for deconvolutional architecture and alternative architectures considered.  
- Questions: justification for chosen hyperparameter ranges (learning rate, entropy cost, λ values).  
- Questions: how do auxiliary tasks interact; any evidence of negative transfer?  
- Questions: computational overhead of auxiliary tasks and scaling with environment complexity.  
- Questions: generalizability of the approach to RL domains beyond Atari and Labyrinth?
