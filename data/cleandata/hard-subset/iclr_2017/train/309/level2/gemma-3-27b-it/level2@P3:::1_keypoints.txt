- Proposes augmenting RL with auxiliary tasks to learn richer, more generalizable environment representations.  
- Simultaneously trains policies for multiple pseudo-reward functions alongside the primary task to improve sensorimotor understanding.  
- Demonstrates significant performance and learning-speed improvements on Atari and a 3D Labyrinth benchmark.  
- Decision: accept with minor revisions—approach is compelling and empirically well-supported.  
- Strength: thorough exploration of auxiliary tasks with clear empirical benefits.  
- Strength: combination of pixel control, feature control, and reward prediction is particularly effective.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental setup including ablation studies that isolate component contributions.  
- Strength: analysis of robustness to hyperparameters is a valuable contribution.  
- Weakness: unclear mechanisms of how different auxiliary tasks interact and improve performance; representation-level analysis is lacking.  
- Weakness: choice of auxiliary tasks seems somewhat arbitrary; needs more systematic justification or exploration.  
- Weakness: insufficient sensitivity analysis for the auxiliary-task weighting factor λ.  
- Weakness: comparisons limited to A3C and Prioritized Dueling DQN; should include more recent sample-efficient or distributional RL baselines.  
- Weakness: unclear what specific properties make Labyrinth more demanding than VizDoom or Minecraft—needs clarification.  
- Specific requested clarifications: visualizations of learned feature maps, criteria for task selection and omitted alternatives, λ sensitivity analysis, comparisons to newer SOTA methods, and explicit differences/challenges of the Labyrinth environment.
