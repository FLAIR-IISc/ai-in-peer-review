- Paper introduces UNREAL: an agent that learns separate policies to maximize multiple pseudo-reward functions using auxiliary control and reward prediction tasks.  
- Claims substantial gains: 880% human expert performance on Atari and 87% on Labyrinth.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Reason 1: Well-motivated approach addressing RL limitations with auxiliary tasks to boost performance and data efficiency.  
- Reason 2: Strong empirical results across Atari and Labyrinth demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Thorough related-work discussion and clear explanation of architecture and training, aiding reproducibility.  
- Ablation studies show the importance of individual components, increasing confidence in results.  
- Suggestion: provide more details on hyperparameter tuning and selection.  
- Suggestion: compare against additional state-of-the-art methods (e.g., PPO, TRPO) beyond A3C.  
- Suggestion: include deeper analysis/visualizations of the learned representations.  
- Question: how were the specific pseudo-reward functions selected?  
- Question: how could UNREAL be extended to more complex domains (robotics, real-world)?  
- Question: can the authors provide more analysis/visualizations of features and how representations are used by the agent?
