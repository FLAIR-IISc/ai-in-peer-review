- Claim: Introduces UNREAL, a deep RL agent that learns separate policies for multiple pseudo-rewards alongside the global reward, claiming SOTA improvements on Labyrinth and 57 Atari games with faster learning, better data efficiency, and hyperparameter robustness.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Reason 1: Clear, well-motivated approach that addresses limitations of existing deep RL methods.  
- Reason 2: Strong experimental results showing significant improvements on Labyrinth and Atari, with comparisons to ablations and other SOTA methods.  
- Support 1: Clear explanation of UNREAL architecture and components (auxiliary control tasks, reward prediction, experience replay).  
- Support 2: Thorough analysis of experimental results, including learning curves, final metrics, and robustness to hyperparameters.  
- Support 3: Comprehensive related work review highlighting UNREAL’s contributions and differences.  
- Feedback 1: Paper needs more detailed analysis of computational complexity of the UNREAL architecture and components.  
- Feedback 2: Authors should provide more insight into the specific role and contribution of each auxiliary task.  
- Feedback 3: More visualizations of agent behavior and learned representations would clarify strengths and weaknesses.  
- Question 1: Please provide details on the computational complexity of UNREAL and its components.  
- Question 2: How do auxiliary control tasks and reward prediction tasks interact, and what is the impact of removing one or both?  
- Question 3: What is the role of the experience replay mechanism in performance gains, and how does it compare to other replay methods?
