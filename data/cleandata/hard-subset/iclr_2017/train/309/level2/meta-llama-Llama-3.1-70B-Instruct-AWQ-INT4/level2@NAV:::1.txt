**Summary of the Paper's Claims**

The paper introduces a novel deep reinforcement learning agent, UNREAL, that significantly improves upon the state-of-the-art results on the challenging 3D Labyrinth levels and 57 Atari games. The agent learns separate policies for maximizing many other pseudo-reward functions simultaneously, in addition to the global reward function. The authors claim that their approach leads to faster learning, improved data efficiency, and increased robustness to hyperparameter settings.

**Decision: Accept**

**Reasons for the Decision**

1. The paper presents a clear and well-motivated approach to improving deep reinforcement learning agents. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the limitations of existing approaches and introduce a novel architecture that addresses these limitations.
2. The experimental results demonstrate significant improvements over the state-of-the-art agents on both Labyrinth and Atari games. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, including comparisons to ablated versions of their agent and other state-of-the-art methods.

**Supporting Arguments**

1. The paper provides a clear and concise explanation of the UNREAL architecture and its components, including the auxiliary control tasks, reward prediction tasks, and experience replay mechanism.
2. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the experimental results, including learning curves, final performance metrics, and robustness to hyperparameter settings.
3. The paper includes a comprehensive review of related work, highlighting the contributions of the UNREAL architecture and its differences from existing approaches.

**Additional Feedback**

1. The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the UNREAL architecture and its components.
2. The authors could provide more insights into the role of each auxiliary task in improving the performance of the agent.
3. The paper could include more visualizations of the agent's behavior and the learned representations to provide a better understanding of the UNREAL architecture's strengths and weaknesses.

**Questions for the Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the computational complexity of the UNREAL architecture and its components?
2. How do the auxiliary control tasks and reward prediction tasks interact with each other, and what is the impact of removing one or both of these components on the agent's performance?
3. Can you provide more insights into the role of the experience replay mechanism in improving the agent's performance, and how it compares to other experience replay mechanisms in the literature?
