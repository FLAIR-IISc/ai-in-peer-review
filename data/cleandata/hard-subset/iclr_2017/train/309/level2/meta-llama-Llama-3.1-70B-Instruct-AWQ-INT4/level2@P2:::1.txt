**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper introduces a novel deep reinforcement learning agent that learns separate policies for maximising multiple pseudo-reward functions simultaneously. The agent uses a combination of auxiliary control tasks and reward prediction tasks to improve its performance and data efficiency. The authors claim that their approach, called UNREAL, significantly outperforms the previous state-of-the-art on Atari and Labyrinth tasks, achieving 880% expert human performance on Atari and 87% expert human performance on Labyrinth.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

I recommend accepting this paper. The key reasons for my decision are:

1. **Well-motivated approach**: The authors provide a clear motivation for their approach, highlighting the limitations of traditional reinforcement learning methods and the benefits of using auxiliary tasks to improve performance and data efficiency.
2. **Strong empirical results**: The authors present impressive empirical results on two challenging domains, Atari and Labyrinth, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach in improving performance and data efficiency.
