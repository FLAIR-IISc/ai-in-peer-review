**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper introduces an agent that learns to maximize cumulative reward while also learning separate policies for maximizing many other pseudo-reward functions simultaneously through reinforcement learning. The agent shares a common representation that continues to develop in the absence of extrinsic rewards. The authors propose a novel mechanism for focusing this representation on extrinsic rewards, allowing for rapid adaptation to the most relevant aspects of the actual task. The agent achieves state-of-the-art results on Atari and Labyrinth, outperforming the previous state-of-the-art by a significant margin.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

Based on the review, I recommend **accepting** the paper. The two key reasons for this decision are:

1. **Significant improvement in performance**: The authors demonstrate a substantial improvement in performance over the previous state-of-the-art on both Atari and Labyrinth, with an average human-normalized score of 87% on Labyrinth and a mean performance of 880% on Atari.
2. **Novel and well-motivated approach**: The paper introduces a new approach to reinforcement learning that combines auxiliary control tasks and reward prediction to improve performance and robustness. The authors provide a clear and well-motivated explanation of their approach, which is grounded in the literature.
