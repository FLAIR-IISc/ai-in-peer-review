- Paper introduces an agent that learns cumulative reward and many pseudo-reward policies simultaneously with a shared representation that develops without extrinsic rewards.  
- Proposes a novel mechanism to focus the representation on extrinsic rewards for rapid task adaptation.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results, outperforming prior methods on Atari and Labyrinth.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Key reason 1: substantial performance improvement (87% human-normalized on Labyrinth; 880% mean on Atari).  
- Key reason 2: novel, well-motivated approach combining auxiliary control tasks and reward prediction.  
- Supporting argument: thorough evaluation with baselines and ablations shows auxiliary tasks and reward prediction improve performance, data efficiency, and hyperparameter robustness.  
- Feedback 1: provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process (specific ranges/distributions).  
- Feedback 2: include more visualizations of learned representations and their relation to tasks.  
- Feedback 3: discuss potential limitations and future work (e.g., more complex tasks, other auxiliary tasks).  
- Question 1: how are auxiliary control tasks selected and how are their weights assigned?  
- Question 2: how is misalignment between auxiliary and main tasks handled to prevent overfitting to auxiliaries?  
- Question 3: provide more details on the reward prediction task and how it shapes the agent’s features.
