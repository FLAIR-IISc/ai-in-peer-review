**Summary**

This paper introduces a novel deep reinforcement learning agent that learns separate policies for maximising multiple pseudo-reward functions simultaneously. The agent uses a combination of auxiliary control tasks, reward prediction tasks, and experience replay to improve its performance and robustness. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on the challenging Labyrinth and Atari environments, achieving state-of-the-art results.

**Decision**

I recommend accepting this paper.

**Reasons for decision**

1. **Well-motivated approach**: The authors provide a clear motivation for their approach, highlighting the limitations of traditional reinforcement learning methods and the potential benefits of learning multiple pseudo-reward functions simultaneously.
2. **Strong empirical results**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on two challenging environments, Labyrinth and Atari, achieving state-of-the-art results and outperforming previous methods.

**Supporting arguments**

* The authors provide a thorough analysis of their approach, including a detailed description of their architecture and training procedure.
* The use of auxiliary control tasks and reward prediction tasks is well-justified, and the authors provide a clear explanation of how these tasks help to improve the agent's performance.
* The authors also provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, including a comparison to several baselines and an analysis of the robustness of their method to hyperparameter settings.

**Additional feedback**

* One potential limitation of the paper is that the authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost of their approach. It would be helpful to include a discussion of the computational resources required to train the agent and the potential scalability of the method.
* The authors could also provide more insight into the types of pseudo-reward functions that are most effective for different environments. This could help to inform the design of future reinforcement learning systems.
* In some cases, the writing could be clearer and more concise. For example, the section on experience replay could be condensed and made more accessible to readers who are not familiar with the concept.

**Questions for the authors**

* Can you provide more information about the computational cost of your approach and the potential scalability of the method?
* How did you select the specific pseudo-reward functions used in your experiments, and are there any general guidelines for choosing effective pseudo-reward functions for different environments?
* Are there any potential applications of your approach to real-world problems, and if so, what are the potential benefits and challenges of using your method in these contexts?
