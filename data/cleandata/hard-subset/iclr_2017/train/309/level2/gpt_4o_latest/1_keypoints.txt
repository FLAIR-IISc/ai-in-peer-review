- Introduces UNREAL, an RL architecture that integrates auxiliary tasks to improve data efficiency, robustness, and performance.  
- Proposes learning separate policies for pseudo-reward functions and a mechanism to focus representation learning on extrinsic rewards.  
- Uses auxiliary tasks such as pixel control and reward prediction to shape representations.  
- Achieves strong empirical gains: ~10× speedup on Labyrinth tasks.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art Atari performance (880% mean human-normalized score).  
- Decision: Accept — recommended due to strong contributions and compelling results.  
- Novelty: Integration of auxiliary tasks into RL is a significant and useful innovation.  
- Experimental rigor: Extensive experiments on Labyrinth and Atari with ablation studies showing component contributions.  
- Practical usefulness: Architecture yields faster learning and better performance for real-world RL needs.  
- Robustness and reproducibility: Detailed implementation and hyperparameter reporting; analysis of hyperparameter robustness.  
- Presentation suggestion: Clarify and simplify some mathematical formulations and auxiliary task descriptions.  
- Related work: Provide a more detailed comparison to architectures like Horde and UVFA to highlight unique advantages.  
- Limitations: Paper should discuss computational overhead of auxiliary tasks and scaling challenges.  
- Future work: Explore combining pixel control with feature control and applicability to continuous actions or robotics.  
- Questions for authors: Compare computational cost (wall-clock/resource) to A3C; can auxiliary tasks be dynamically prioritized; applicability to continuous action or multi-agent settings?
