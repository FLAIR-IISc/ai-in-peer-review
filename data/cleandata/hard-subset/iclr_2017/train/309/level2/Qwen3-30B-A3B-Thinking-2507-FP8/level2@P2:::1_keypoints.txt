- Proposes UNREAL: an agent learning to predict and control multiple sensorimotor features via auxiliary control tasks, reward prediction, and experience replay.
- Argues control-based auxiliary tasks produce more useful representations than reconstruction-based unsupervised learning.
- UNREAL architecture integrates pixel control, feature control, reward prediction, and replay into A3C.
- Reports strong empirical gains: 880% mean human-normalized on Atari, 87% on Labyrinth, and ~10× faster learning than A3C.
- Decision: Accept with major revisions — promising results but needs key clarifications.
- Strength: Well-motivated by limitations of sparse extrinsic rewards and input-reconstruction unsupervised learning.
- Strength: Impressive Labyrinth performance and ablation studies that show each component’s contribution.
- Weakness: The 880% Atari claim is unusual and lacks clear explanation of the human-normalized metric and baselines.
- Weakness: Paper does not establish prior state-of-the-art on Labyrinth beyond comparison to A3C; literature comparison is insufficient.
- Weakness: Theoretical justification for why controlling features generalizes better than predicting them is superficial.
- Additional feedback: Clarify exactly how Atari human-normalized performance was computed and contextualize against other Atari results.
- Additional feedback: Expand related work and more thoroughly contrast with auxiliary-task RL (e.g., Lample & Chaplot 2016; Mirowski et al. 2016).
- Additional feedback: Provide detailed computational-cost analysis comparing UNREAL to A3C (per-step and total compute vs. learning speedup).
- Additional feedback: Strengthen the theoretical link between the sensorimotor data-stream claim and the proposed approach.
- Additional feedback: Explain and analyze why pixel-control outperforms immediate pixel-prediction for representation learning.
- Question: What human baseline and normalization procedure produced the 880% Atari result?
- Question: What was the prior state-of-the-art on Labyrinth and how does UNREAL compare to related 3D-environment work?
- Question: Why use Q-learning for auxiliary tasks rather than policy-gradient methods?
- Question: Can the authors provide deeper analysis on why controlling pixel changes yields better representations than predicting them?
