- Introduces UNREAL, an RL agent that maximizes multiple pseudo-reward functions simultaneously while sharing a common representation.  
- Proposes a framework combining auxiliary control tasks (pixel control, feature control) with reward prediction to build robust representations.  
- Key claim: approach significantly improves learning speed, data efficiency, and robustness versus prior methods.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; paper makes a strong, well-supported contribution.  
- Strong experimental results: Atari — 880% human-normalized performance; Labyrinth — 87% human-normalized and 10× faster learning.  
- Ablation studies effectively show the contribution of each component.  
- Thorough evaluation across multiple hyperparameter settings supports robustness claims.  
- Theoretical foundation is sound, connecting to Horde, UVFAs, and successor representations.  
- Architecture is well-motivated; auxiliary tasks are shown to complement (not replace) primary reward maximization.  
- Implementation details in the appendix are comprehensive enough for reproducibility.  
- Suggested improvement: clarify the relationship between the reward prediction task and standard value function learning/theory.  
- Suggested improvement: provide quantitative comparisons showing feature control vs pixel control on Labyrinth levels.  
- Suggested improvement: clarify whether auxiliary tasks are equally beneficial across environments or vary by task structure.
