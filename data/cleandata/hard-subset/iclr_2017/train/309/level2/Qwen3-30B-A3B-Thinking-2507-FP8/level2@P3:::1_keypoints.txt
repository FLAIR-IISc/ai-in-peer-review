- Introduces UNREAL: auxiliary control tasks (pixel/feature control) plus reward prediction sharing representation with main policy.  
- Motivation: controlling features aligns better with long-term goals than reconstruction, illustrated by a baby analogy.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on Labyrinth and Atari, improving final performance and learning speed.  
- Labyrinth results: 87% human-normalized vs 54% for A3C across 13 levels, with ~10× learning speedup.  
- Ablations show each auxiliary component helps: pixel control gives largest boost, feature control substantial, reward prediction improves robustness.  
- Atari results: 880% mean human performance and improved hyperparameter robustness vs prior state-of-the-art.  
- Comparison to unsupervised baselines indicates control-based objectives outperform prediction/reconstruction for representation learning.  
- Experiments are rigorous and comprehensive: multiple environments, hyperparameter sweeps, levels, and random seeds.  
- Architecture effectively integrates auxiliary tasks with A3C and uses experience replay to balance on-/off-policy learning.  
- Implementation detail praised: skewed replay sampling of reward events is elegant and data-efficient.  
- Claims are well-supported and not overstated; statistical evaluation is appropriate.  
- Paper is practical and likely to influence future RL representation-learning work.  
- Literature placement is strong and clearly differentiates from Horde, UVFA, successor representations, and prediction tasks.  
- Suggested improvement: deeper analysis of why feature control is slightly less effective than pixel control.  
- Suggested improvement: clarify rationale for 3-class reward prediction vs regression and its performance impact.  
- Suggested improvement: expand feature-control ablation across more Labyrinth levels and discuss biological plausibility beyond the baby analogy.
