- Introduces UNREAL: augments A3C with off-policy auxiliary tasks from a replay buffer to improve representations, final performance, and data efficiency.
- Auxiliary tasks split into control (pixel control, feature control) and reward (reward prediction, value replay) to leverage dense sensory signals beyond extrinsic rewards.
- Control tasks frame representation learning as RL problems maximizing pseudo-rewards (e.g., change pixels or activate features) rather than prediction/reconstruction.
- Reward tasks include immediate reward prediction with skewed sampling for sparse rewards and replaying value updates to focus on extrinsic goals.
- UNREAL combines on-policy policy-gradient for the main task with off-policy value-based auxiliary tasks in a unified hybrid framework.
- Extensive empirical validation on 57 Atari games and the new Labyrinth 3D environment demonstrating state-of-the-art results.
- Reported performance: 880% mean human on Atari and 87% on Labyrinth, with a mean learning speedup of ~10x and improved hyperparameter robustness.
- Decision: Accept — framework is compelling and well-motivated; auxiliary control tasks are a significant conceptual contribution.
- Strong, thorough empirical results with detailed ablations that isolate each component’s contribution.
- Hybrid on-policy/off-policy design is a valuable architectural contribution that makes effective use of different experience streams.
- Pixel control shown to outperform pixel prediction and reconstruction, supporting the idea that influence/control drives better representations.
- Labyrinth introduced as a challenging new benchmark with richer visuals and physics, revealing large gains over A3C.
