1. Introduces UNREAL: augments A3C with off-policy auxiliary tasks to improve data efficiency, final performance, and robustness.  
2. Auxiliary control tasks: learn value functions to control pixels (pixel control) or hidden activations (feature control) to capture cause-and-effect.  
3. Auxiliary reward prediction: predict immediate reward from recent observations using a replay buffer rebalanced to oversample rewarding events.  
4. Value function replay: replay recent experiences for extra value regression to accelerate value propagation.  
5. Evaluated on 57 Atari games and Labyrinth; achieves state-of-the-art results (880% Atari mean human-normalized, 87% Labyrinth) and 10x speedup on Labyrinth.  
6. Decision: Accept — paper provides a clear, significant contribution.  
7. Key reason: novel and effective synthesis of on-policy main task with off-policy auxiliary control objectives.  
8. Key reason: extensive, convincing empirical evaluation with ablations, comparisons, and robustness analysis.  
9. Hybrid on-/off-policy design via replay buffer is a core strength, improving learning without destabilizing A3C.  
10. Ablation studies show pixel control, reward prediction, and value replay each provide significant, cumulative benefits.  
11. Active control (pixel control) outperforms passive reconstruction/prediction for learning useful representations.  
12. Paper well-written and technically sound.  
13. Suggestion: report computational overhead (wall-clock time per step) relative to A3C to assess trade-offs.  
14. Suggestion: clarify the role and evaluation of feature control—why it was preliminary and whether it integrates with pixel control.  
15. Suggestion: discuss sensitivity and intuition behind auxiliary loss weighting (e.g., λPC differences across domains).  
16. Question: Have authors tried leveraging auxiliary control policies for intrinsic motivation, exploration, or hierarchical options?  
17. Question: How sensitive is reward-prediction performance to the 50/50 rebalancing, especially in very sparse-reward environments?  
18. Question: Can authors expand on why control yields better representations than prediction—does control induce more causal understanding?
