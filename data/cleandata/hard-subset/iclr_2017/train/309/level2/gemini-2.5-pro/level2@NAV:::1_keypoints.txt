- Introduces UNREAL: augments A3C with off-policy auxiliary tasks to improve data efficiency and performance.
- Auxiliary control tasks: maximize pseudo-rewards (pixel changes, hidden unit activations) to shape behavior.
- Auxiliary prediction task: predict immediate reward onset using a replay buffer.
- Core claim: combined auxiliary tasks enable continuously improving internal representations.
- Empirical results: significant performance gains, faster learning, and improved hyperparameter robustness.
- Evaluation domains: extensive experiments on Atari and the visually complex 3D Labyrinth.
- Quantitative impact: UNREAL drastically outperforms A3C (≈10x mean speedup) with comprehensive ablations isolating component contributions.
- Demonstrates superiority of control-based unsupervised objectives over pixel-reconstruction approaches for representation learning.
- Contribution: introduction and benchmarking on the Labyrinth environment expands DRL evaluation benchmarks.
- Recommended Accept: addresses data efficiency in sparse-reward RL and is well-substantiated by experiments.
- Suggested improvement: provide deeper discussion/theoretical justification for on-policy A3C combined with off-policy auxiliary updates.
- Suggested improvement: analyze selection of auxiliary tasks and how different tasks affect learning across environments.
- Reviewer questions: what is the computational overhead (wall-clock/FLOPs) of auxiliary tasks vs. A3C?
- Reviewer questions: how was the 20×20 pixel-control grid chosen and how sensitive is performance to grid resolution vs. computational cost?
