- Introduces UNREAL agent: A3C augmented with auxiliary tasks (pixel/feature control, reward prediction, value replay) to learn richer representations.
- Achieves state-of-the-art on Atari (880% mean human) and 10x faster learning on Labyrinth (87% mean human).
- Decision: Accept — strongly recommended.
- Novelty: combines on-policy main task with off-policy auxiliary tasks for improved data efficiency and robustness.
- Auxiliary control tasks (pixel/feature control) are a novel approach contrasting prior options/Horde methods.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and demonstrates generality across Atari and the new 3D Labyrinth benchmark.
- Uses strong baseline (A3C) and provides a valuable new environment (Labyrinth) with richer perceptual/physical complexity.
- Ablation studies show each auxiliary task (pixel control, reward prediction, value replay) contributes and their combination yields the best results.
- Pixel control outperforms pixel prediction/reconstruction, supporting the core hypothesis that control is a better auxiliary objective.
- Reported results represent a substantial performance and data-efficiency improvement; hyperparameter robustness is improved.
- Paper is well-written and claims are strongly supported by experiments.
- Suggestion: include discussion of computational overhead (wall-clock, GPU/CPU) compared to A3C.
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis of feature control (which layers to control, layer choice effects, interactions with other tasks).
- Suggestion: discuss sensitivity to pixel control weight (λPC) and guidance for setting it across environments.
- Question: rationale for using a 20x20 grid of 4x4 cells for pixel control and effects of coarser/finer grids.
- Question: why reward prediction is framed as 3-class classification (pos/neg/zero) rather than regression, and whether regression was considered.
- Question: request deeper intuition why control is a better auxiliary objective (e.g., enforces longer-horizon/causal learning vs immediate prediction).
