- Introduces UNREAL, an RL architecture using multiple auxiliary tasks to improve data efficiency, speed, and hyperparameter robustness.
- Combines three auxiliary tasks: auxiliary control (pixel/feature control), reward prediction, and value function replay.
- Claims substantial gains over A3C on Labyrinth and Atari, with UNREAL more than doubling human-normalized performance on Labyrinth.
- Reports large data efficiency improvements (requires <10% of the data to reach A3C’s final performance) and greater robustness to hyperparameters.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Main reason for acceptance: addresses an important RL problem with a principled extension of prior work.
- Main reason for acceptance: strong, comprehensive empirical results across multiple environments and metrics.
- Argues auxiliary tasks effectively improve representation learning and differ from prior temporal-abstraction approaches.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough: challenging Labyrinth environment, Atari-57 benchmark, and detailed ablation studies supporting task choices.
- Ablations show learning to control pixel changes outperforms merely predicting them.
- Results are statistically significant with reported speedups (~10x) and robustness across hyperparameter settings.
- Paper provides clear implementation details and appendices sufficient for reproducibility.
- Suggestion: provide explicit analysis of how auxiliary tasks change learned representations (e.g., feature visualizations or dimensionality reduction over training).
- Suggestion: report computational overhead and wall-clock time to complement environment-step data-efficiency claims.
- Suggestion: clarify theoretical/empirical relationship between reward prediction and standard value learning.
- Suggestion: discuss failure cases or environments where UNREAL performs poorly to present limitations.
- Question: could skewed sampling for reward prediction bias exploration and lead to suboptimal exploration strategies?
- Question: clarify what metric “less than 10% of the data” refers to (env steps, updates, or other).
- Question: explain what human-normalized scores represent in concrete terms—how they map to actual human performance.
- Question: justify using multiclass classification (zero/positive/negative) for reward prediction rather than regression and whether alternatives were tested.
