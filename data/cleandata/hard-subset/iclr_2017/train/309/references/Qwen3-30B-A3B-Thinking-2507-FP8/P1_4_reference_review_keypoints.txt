- Proposes UNREAL: augmenting RL with auxiliary control tasks, reward prediction, and experience replay to improve data efficiency, learning speed, and hyperparameter robustness.
- UNREAL achieves >87% human-normalized on 3D Labyrinth (more than doubling A3C) and 880% mean human-normalized across 57 Atari games, outperforming A3C and Prioritized Dueling DQN.
- Decision: Accept — well-motivated approach with strong empirical evidence across two distinct domains.
- Strong literature review situating UNREAL relative to options, Horde, UVFA, and successor representations; uses auxiliary tasks for representation learning rather than direct policy optimization.
- Robust experimental design with extensive ablation studies, meaningful baselines (including input reconstruction and immediate reward prediction), and evaluation across multiple hyperparameter settings.
- Labyrinth experiments are diverse (13 levels) testing fruit gathering, navigation, exploration, and laser-tag, providing comprehensive behavioral evaluation.
- Demonstrates high data efficiency (reaching A3C’s final performance with <10% of the data) and uses human-normalized scores as a meaningful benchmark.
- Shows robustness across hyperparameters (figures cited), indicating stability and practical applicability.
- Provides a plausible explanation that auxiliary control tasks shape representations to focus on task-relevant visual features, outperforming input reconstruction.
- Suggestion: clarify the relationship between auxiliary control tasks and successor representations.
- Suggestion: quantify computational overhead of auxiliary tasks (e.g., percentage increase in training time vs base A3C).
- Suggestion: discuss limitations and whether the approach generalizes to more complex environments or task types where aux tasks may be less helpful.
- Suggestion: expand on and justify the reward prediction task formulation (three-class classification vs alternatives like regression).
- Question: why did input reconstruction speed initial learning but worsen final Labyrinth scores—did reconstruction focus on non-rewarding or rarely visible features?
- Question: provide more detail on how robustness was measured (e.g., std dev across hyperparameters or % of configs achieving at least X% of best).
- Question: how does UNREAL’s learning process enable more efficient exploration than naive strategies, and which auxiliary tasks specifically drive that improvement?
