- Introduces UNREAL with three auxiliary tasks (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction) to improve representation learning and boost RL performance on Labyrinth and Atari.  
- Empirical gains: 87% human-normalized on Labyrinth (vs. 54% A3C), 10× faster learning, <10% data to match A3C; Atari: 880% mean, 250% median human-normalized, outperforming A3C and Prioritized Dueling DQN.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Strength: Significant contribution showing auxiliary tasks can shape useful representations, improving data efficiency and robustness.  
- Strength: Comprehensive, well-designed experiments with thorough ablation studies and multiple hyperparameter settings.  
- Positioning: Good literature review that distinguishes UNREAL from prior auxiliary-task work by its focus on representation rather than reward maximization.  
- Robustness: UNREAL is notably less sensitive to hyperparameter choices than A3C.  
- Revision 1: Clarify relationship to successor representations (Barreto et al. 2016) and how UNREAL differs.  
- Revision 2: Provide visualizations or analyses (e.g., feature activations, t-SNE) to show how UNREAL’s representations differ from A3C’s.  
- Revision 3: Report computational overhead of auxiliary tasks and per-step cost compared to A3C.  
- Revision 4: Expand discussion of feature control—what features are controlled (high- vs low-level) and how it differs from pixel control.  
- Revision 5: Include more comparisons to recent auxiliary-task work (e.g., Lample & Chaplot 2016; Mirowski et al. 2016).  
- Question 1: Provide evidence (e.g., visualizations) that input reconstruction focuses on irrelevant parts versus pixel control.  
- Question 2: Justify using 3-class reward prediction over regression and report results if regression were used.  
- Question 3: Detail skewed sampling for reward prediction—how it was implemented and whether separate or shared replay buffers were used.  
- Question 4: Report exact performance metrics for feature control on Labyrinth levels.  
- Question 5: Evaluate UNREAL on environments with much sparser rewards (e.g., rewards only every ~1000 steps).
