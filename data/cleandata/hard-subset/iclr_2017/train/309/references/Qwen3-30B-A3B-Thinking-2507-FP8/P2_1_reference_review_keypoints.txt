- Introduces UNREAL: augmenting A3C with auxiliary control (pixel/feature control) and reward-prediction tasks to improve data efficiency, speed, and hyperparameter robustness.  
- Claims strong empirical gains: Labyrinth 87% vs A3C 54% (human-normalized) and 880% mean human-normalized on 57 Atari games.  
- Ablation studies show each auxiliary component contributes to performance improvements.  
- Comparison to unsupervised baselines indicates auxiliary control (learning to control pixel changes) is crucial and outperforms simple prediction/reconstruction.  
- UNREAL achieves substantially greater data efficiency (reaches A3C final performance with <10% of data).  
- Demonstrates robustness across many hyperparameter settings (50 on Atari, 45 on Labyrinth).  
- Provides a plausible explanation: auxiliary tasks shape representations relevant to reward without directly biasing the policy.  
- Decision: reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Key acceptance reason: strong empirical evidence across diverse, challenging environments.  
- Key acceptance reason: well-situated in related work and clearly differentiated from prior auxiliary/representation work.  
- Suggested improvement: add theoretical motivation linking auxiliary tasks to representation learning.  
- Suggested improvement: include visualizations of learned representations (e.g., feature activation maps).  
- Suggested improvement: clarify how the reward predictor’s role in shaping features differs from auxiliary control tasks.  
- Suggested improvement: discuss limitations and generalization to different visual/reward environments and scaling concerns.  
- Suggested improvement: explain how human-normalized scores are computed.  
- Suggested improvement: provide a more detailed performance breakdown across Labyrinth level categories.  
- Question: how does the reward predictor functionally differ from auxiliary control in shaping features and why is that distinction important?  
- Question: why choose a 20×20 pixel control grid and how would different grid sizes affect performance?  
- Question: why sample reward prediction sequences with equal zero/non-zero rewards versus other strategies like prioritized replay?  
- Question: provide more detail on robustness across hyperparameters, especially task weight values (λVR, λPC, λRP).  
- Question: why does input reconstruction speed initial learning but hurt final performance; request deeper analysis.
