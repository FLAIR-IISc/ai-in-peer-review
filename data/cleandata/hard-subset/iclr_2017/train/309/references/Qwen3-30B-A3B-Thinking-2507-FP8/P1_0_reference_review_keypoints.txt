1. Introduces UNREAL: an RL framework using auxiliary tasks to improve data efficiency, learning speed, and robustness.  
2. Proposes two novel auxiliary control tasks: pixel control and feature control to maximize changes in visuals or activations.  
3. Adds a reward prediction auxiliary task to overcome perceptual reward sparsity.  
4. Combines auxiliary tasks with A3C in a unified architecture.  
5. Evaluated on Labyrinth and 57 Atari games, showing large empirical gains.  
6. Reports 87% human-normalized performance on Labyrinth vs 54% for A3C.  
7. Reports 880% mean human-normalized performance on Atari, outperforming prior state-of-the-art.  
8. Claims dramatic data-efficiency improvements (reaching A3C performance with <10% of data).  
9. Claims improved robustness to hyperparameter choices.  
10. Paper is well-written, thoroughly evaluated, and recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
11. Strong experimental design with proper baselines, ablations, and statistical significance.  
12. Key strengths: auxiliary tasks greatly improve data efficiency without altering base RL algorithm.  
13. Key strengths: auxiliary tasks enhance hyperparameter robustness and avoid biasing the main policy.  
14. Key strengths: careful auxiliary task design and thorough ablation studies isolating each component.  
15. Request to clarify why control tasks outperform prediction/reconstruction for representation learning.  
16. Request for quantitative metrics demonstrating hyperparameter robustness (e.g., variance across settings).  
17. Request to clarify calculation and baseline for human-normalized scores for reproducibility.  
18. Suggest more direct comparisons with UVFA and successor representations to clarify differences.  
19. Ask authors to discuss potential limitations, such as specialization of auxiliary tasks or scaling issues.  
20. Specific questions: explain why reconstruction hurts in Labyrinth and whether this generalizes.  
21. Specific questions: provide exact performance differences between pixel control and feature control on Labyrinth.  
22. Specific questions: explain what makes pixel control more effective than input-change prediction.  
23. Specific questions: give detailed comparisons and implementation differences between UNREAL and Prioritized Dueling DQN.  
24. Specific questions: justify using classification for reward prediction over regression and report regression results if tried.
