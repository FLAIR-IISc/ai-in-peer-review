- Introduces UNREAL, which adds auxiliary tasks to deep RL to improve data efficiency and robustness.  
- Proposes two auxiliary task types: auxiliary control (pixel and feature control) and reward prediction.  
- Uses auxiliary tasks purely for representation learning, not direct policy modification.  
- Empirically outperforms A3C on Labyrinth (87% vs 54% human-normalized) and 57 Atari games (880% mean human-normalized).  
- Demonstrates 10–18x greater data efficiency and improved robustness to hyperparameters.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Strong empirical contribution with clear positioning relative to prior work (options, Horde, UVFA, successor reps, models).  
- Ablation studies show each auxiliary task contributes; pixel-reconstruction baseline is inferior.  
- Experimental design is rigorous with multiple hyperparameter settings and clear visualizations.  
- Request for theoretical justification connecting reward prediction to representation improvement.  
- Ask for analysis of how different auxiliary tasks interact and complement each other.  
- Suggest explaining why spatial structure in pixel/feature control matters for representations.  
- Question why reward prediction uses three-class classification instead of regression and whether alternatives were tested.  
- Clarification needed on how the reward predictor is integrated (shared head vs other integration).  
- Ask why pixel control outperformed feature control and how UNREAL could extend to continuous actions or non-pixel settings.  
- Request information on computational overhead of auxiliary tasks and total training time vs A3C.
