1. Introduces UNREAL: augment A3C with auxiliary tasks (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction) to improve RL.

2. Claims large empirical gains: Labyrinth 87% vs 54% human-normalized, Atari 880% mean, 10–18× faster learning, and hyperparameter robustness.

3. Decision: Accept — strong empirical contribution and convincing ablations.

4. Empirical rigor: consistent, substantial improvements across multiple challenging environments.

5. Literature review: clearly distinguishes UNREAL from temporal abstractions, UVFAs, and successor representations.

6. Ablations: each auxiliary task contributes; pixel control and control-based tasks outperform reconstruction/prediction baselines.

7. Reproducibility: sufficient implementation details and extensive hyperparameter robustness analysis (50 settings across 57 games).

8. Suggestion: add theoretical justification and visualizations to explain why these auxiliary tasks shape representations beneficially.

9. Suggestion: provide a more thorough comparison to UVFAs and successor representations to better position the approach.

10. Suggestion: analyze representations learned with UNREAL (transferability, internal activations, how they differ from A3C).

11. Suggestion: report computational overhead of auxiliary tasks to contextualize data-efficiency claims.

12. Suggestion: include a brief discussion of broader impacts and applicability beyond RL domains.

13. Question: how does the LSTM handle temporal structure for auxiliary tasks, especially reward prediction — any special sequence handling?

14. Question: how does equal sampling of zero vs. non-zero rewards affect reward predictor generalization to different reward distributions?

15. Question: request intuition for why learning to control pixel changes outperforms predicting pixel changes (active vs. passive explanation).

16. Question: how does UNREAL behave on procedural navigation levels compared to agents that explicitly learn maze layouts — any qualitative analysis?

17. Question: how sensitive is performance to the deconvolutional pixel-control architecture; were simpler or alternative architectures tested?
