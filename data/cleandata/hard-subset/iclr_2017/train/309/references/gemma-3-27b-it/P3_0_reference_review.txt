## ICLR 2024 Review: UNREAL - Auxiliary Tasks for Reinforcement Learning

**1. Summary:**

This paper presents UNREAL, a novel reinforcement learning architecture that combines Asynchronous Advantage Actor-Critic (A3C) with a suite of auxiliary tasks designed to improve learning efficiency, robustness, and performance. The authors introduce auxiliary control tasks (pixel control and feature control) and an auxiliary reward prediction task, all integrated into a single, optimized loss function.  The core idea is to learn a richer, more informative representation by encouraging the agent to actively control and predict aspects of its environment, alongside maximizing its primary reward.  The paper demonstrates impressive results on both the complex 3D Labyrinth environment and a suite of Atari games, showcasing significant improvements over baseline A3C and state-of-the-art methods.

**2. Decision:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. The paper presents a strong contribution to the field of reinforcement learning, demonstrating a compelling and effective approach to representation learning through auxiliary tasks. The empirical results are convincing, and the ablation studies provide valuable insights into the contributions of each component.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

* **Novelty & Motivation:** The combination of diverse auxiliary tasks, particularly the control-focused pixel and feature control, is a genuinely novel contribution. The motivation behind learning better representations for faster and more robust learning is well-established, and the authors clearly articulate how their approach addresses this.  The idea of separating reward prediction from value learning to avoid bias is insightful.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The results on Labyrinth are particularly impressive, achieving significant performance gains (more than doubling human-normalized scores) and demonstrating a substantial speedup in learning. The strong performance on Atari, exceeding existing state-of-the-art methods, further validates the effectiveness of UNREAL.
* **Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies are crucial for understanding the impact of each component of UNREAL.  Demonstrating that each auxiliary task contributes to overall performance, and that their combination is synergistic, strengthens the claims made by the authors.  The comparison to pixel reconstruction and simple reward prediction is particularly helpful in contextualizing the benefits of the control-based auxiliary tasks.
* **Robustness Analysis:** The analysis of robustness to hyperparameters is a valuable addition, showcasing that UNREAL is less sensitive to hyperparameter choices than A3C, making it more practical for real-world applications.
* **Clear Presentation:**  The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the core concepts and experimental setup. The inclusion of supporting materials (videos, visualizations) is a positive aspect.

**4. Additional Feedback (for improvement):**

* **Deeper Dive into Feature Control:** While the pixel control task is well explained, the feature control task feels slightly less fleshed out.  A more detailed discussion of *which* features are being controlled and *why* they are beneficial would be valuable.  Visualizations of the activations being controlled would further enhance understanding.
* **Computational Cost:**  The paper focuses on performance and efficiency gains in terms of samples. However, a discussion of the computational cost of adding these auxiliary tasks (e.g., increased training time per step) would provide a more complete picture.  Is the increase in computational cost justified by the gains in sample efficiency?
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity Analysis Details:** The robustness analysis mentions varying learning rate and entropy cost. Providing a bit more detail on the range of values tested and how the results were aggregated would be helpful.  A visualization of the performance landscape across these hyperparameters would be excellent.
* **Connection to Intrinsic Motivation:** The work aligns strongly with the broader field of intrinsic motivation. Explicitly framing the contribution within this context and discussing connections to related work (e.g., curiosity-driven exploration) could strengthen the paper.

**Questions for the Authors:**

* Could you elaborate on the choice of the specific hidden layer for feature control?  Was this selection based on empirical results or a specific theoretical motivation?
*  Regarding the deconvolutional network used for pixel control, could you explain the rationale behind this architectural choice?  Are there alternative methods that were considered?
*  What is the computational overhead (e.g., increase in training time per step) introduced by adding the auxiliary tasks?
*  Have you explored different methods for weighting the different loss components (λVR, λPC, λRP)?  Did any specific weighting schemes consistently outperform others?
*  Do you anticipate that the benefits of UNREAL would generalize to other complex environments beyond Labyrinth and Atari? What modifications, if any, would be necessary to adapt the approach to different domains?



Overall, this is a well-executed and impactful piece of research that makes a significant contribution to the field of reinforcement learning. The acceptance recommendation is based on the strength of the empirical results, the novelty of the approach, and the clarity of the presentation. Addressing the minor feedback points would further enhance the quality of the paper.
