- Presents UNREAL: an RL agent using auxiliary control tasks and reward prediction to learn better representations and improve learning efficiency and robustness.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Strong empirical results: substantial performance and sample-efficiency gains on Labyrinth and Atari (≈10x speedups, consistent improvement over A3C).  
- Well-motivated approach: combines control tasks and reward prediction to address perceptual sparsity and learn useful features, grounded in related work.  
- Thorough ablation studies: isolate contributions of pixel control, feature control, and reward prediction, showing pixel control outperforms simple pixel-change prediction.  
- Robustness analysis: UNREAL reduces sensitivity to hyperparameters compared to baselines.  
- Clear presentation: methodology and experiments are well explained with informative figures/tables.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: request for broader visualization (e.g., heatmaps) across learning rates and entropy costs.  
- Computational cost: asks for discussion of added training-time overhead per environment step due to auxiliary tasks.  
- Reward prediction details: asks for expanded rationale of the three-class (zero/positive/negative) scheme and exploration of alternatives (e.g., magnitude).  
- Generalization across environments: request discussion of limits in very sparse-reward domains and potential modifications needed.  
- Figure clarity: suggest simplifying or providing zoomed-in views for crowded architecture diagrams.  
- Q1 (Computational overhead): how much does training time per step increase in Labyrinth and Atari?  
- Q2 (Reward prediction rationale): why choose three-class reward prediction rather than predicting magnitude or other targets?  
- Q3 (Feature control selection): why use the second hidden layer for feature control and were other layers tested?  
- Q4 (Generalization concerns): would UNREAL work in extremely sparse-reward settings, and what changes would help if not?  
- Q5 (Code availability): will the UNREAL code be released to aid reproducibility?
