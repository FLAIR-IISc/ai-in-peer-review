- Paper presents UNREAL: an A3C-based agent using auxiliary control (pixel/feature) and reward-prediction tasks to improve representation learning, sample efficiency, and robustness.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Novelty: Compelling integration of auxiliary tasks into a practical RL framework, well-motivated for improving sample efficiency and robustness.
- Strong empirical results: Substantial improvements over A3C on Labyrinth and Atari, especially in sample efficiency.
- Ablations: Well-designed ablation studies showing the contributions of different auxiliary tasks.
- Evaluation: Thorough experiments including robustness analyses and supporting visualizations/video (though visualizations not included in paper).
- Presentation: Generally clear writing and helpful background/related work.
- Request to include visualizations of learned features, pixel-control outputs, and reward-prediction behavior to clarify how auxiliaries affect representations and behavior.
- Request deeper analysis of the reward-prediction task: prediction accuracy, loss dynamics over training, and its direct impact.
- Suggest sensitivity analysis for auxiliary-specific hyperparameters (e.g., lambda values) to strengthen robustness claims.
- Ask for discussion of computational overhead: how much extra compute UNREAL requires versus A3C.
- Question on feature control: why it matched pixel control in performance and which hidden layers are best targets.
- Question on reward vs. value prediction: why biasing reward prediction is less likely to bias the policy than biasing value estimates.
- Question on scaling: anticipated challenges and necessary modifications for more complex, higher-dimensional environments.
- Question on replay strategy: whether other replay strategies (e.g., prioritized replay by TD error) were tried or could complement the skewed replay for reward prediction.
