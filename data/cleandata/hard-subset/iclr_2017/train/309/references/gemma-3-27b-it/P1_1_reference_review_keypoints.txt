- Proposes UNREAL: A3C augmented with auxiliary control and reward-prediction tasks to improve representations and speed learning.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Auxiliary tasks yield faster learning, greater robustness, and higher performance in complex environments.  
- Uses off-policy learning for auxiliary tasks to efficiently shape the agent’s representation.  
- Clearly motivated problem: improving sample efficiency and robustness in visually complex RL.  
- Strong empirical results: large gains in Labyrinth and substantial, generalizable gains on Atari.  
- Robustness analysis shows performance is less sensitive to hyperparameter choices.  
- Methodology is well-described with detailed algorithmic components and comprehensive implementation details.  
- Ablation studies effectively isolate contributions of each auxiliary task.  
- Comparisons to A3C and other auxiliary-task methods (pixel reconstruction, input-change prediction) are appropriate and informative.  
- Request to clarify hyperparameter search: ranges, distributions, and scope of random sampling.  
- Ask for discussion of computational overhead introduced by auxiliary tasks.  
- Suggest adding visualizations of learned features (e.g., pixel/feature control activations) for intuition.  
- Recommend including error bars and statistical significance testing on performance results.  
- Question on pixel control: why a central 80x80 crop and whether whole-image application was tested.  
- Question on feature control: how the target hidden layer was selected and results from testing other layers.  
- Question on scalability: applicability to more complex 3D environments or multi-agent settings.  
- Question on reward-prediction skewing: rationale for the 0.5 split and sensitivity/justification of that value.  
- Overall conclusion: strong contribution to RL; accept pending minor revisions addressing the points above.
