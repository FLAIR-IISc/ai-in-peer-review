- Paper introduces UNREAL: adds pixel control, feature control, and reward prediction auxiliary tasks to improve RL data efficiency and robustness.
- Demonstrates significant speedups and higher final performance over A3C on DeepMind Labyrinth and Atari.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — strong contribution with compelling experimental support.
- Novelty: Combining auxiliary control tasks with reward prediction is a novel, well-motivated approach grounded in prior representation work.
- Empirical strength: Particularly impressive Labyrinth results and generalization to Atari show effective, practical improvements.
- Ablations: Systematic ablation studies show each auxiliary task contributes meaningfully and their combination is crucial.
- Robustness: UNREAL exhibits a smoother, less hyperparameter-sensitive performance landscape.
- Presentation: Paper is well-written and architecture diagrams aid clarity.
- Request to discuss computational cost: quantify training time and resource overhead of auxiliary tasks versus data efficiency gains.
- Reward prediction detail: justify the three-class (zero/positive/negative) choice versus regression or other formulations.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: provide search space and distributions used across successful runs to substantiate robustness claims.
- Qualitative analysis: include visualizations or examples of features/activations learned by auxiliary tasks.
- Relate to intrinsic motivation literature more explicitly to strengthen framing.
- Clarify how "human-normalized scores" are computed.
- Question on pixel control grid size: did you test sensitivity to different cell sizes?
- Question on feature vs pixel control: elaborate differences observed in learned representations and why feature control was slightly weaker on Labyrinth.
- Request a table summarizing average performance per Labyrinth category for A3C, UNREAL, and ablations.
- Atari hyperparameters: clarify whether hyperparameters were shared or tuned per game and the tuning effort required.
- Replay memory: report memory requirements for experience replay and how this scales with environment complexity.
- Conclusion: Strong, well-motivated paper with minor suggested clarifications; recommend acceptance.
