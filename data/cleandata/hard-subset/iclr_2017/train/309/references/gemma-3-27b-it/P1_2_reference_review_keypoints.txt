- Proposal: UNREAL integrates auxiliary control tasks and reward prediction into A3C to learn richer representations and improve sample efficiency and performance.  
- Decision: Accept — compelling, well-supported approach with strong empirical evidence.  
- Strong empirical results: Significant improvements in Labyrinth and Atari, faster learning, and human-level performance on some Labyrinth levels.  
- Motivation: Auxiliary tasks address perceptual sparsity and improve representation learning; connections to temporal abstractions, UVFAs, and successor representations.  
- Evaluation: Thorough ablation studies, comparisons to other auxiliary methods, and robustness analysis demonstrating stability.  
- Presentation: Well-written, organized, with useful implementation details and supplemental material.  
- Suggestion — Reward prediction: Provide more details on the specific network architecture, hyperparameters, and rationale for choices.  
- Suggestion — Feature control: Analyze which features are learned and how they contribute; include visualizations of activations during gameplay.  
- Suggestion — Generalization: Discuss applicability of auxiliary tasks to other RL algorithms (e.g., PPO, SAC).  
- Suggestion — Computational cost: Report computational overhead and trade-offs between sample efficiency and added computation.  
- Question — Input reconstruction: Is the detrimental effect consistent across Atari games, and why might reconstructing irrelevant pixels harm performance more than pixel-control tasks?  
- Question — Labyrinth gains: Are navigation-level improvements due to better state representation or other task-specific factors?  
- Question — Reward prediction metrics: Besides classification accuracy, what metrics monitored prediction quality and did prediction quality correlate with policy performance?  
- Question — Loss weighting: How were the weighting coefficients (λVR, λPC, λRP) chosen — extensive tuning or a principled method?  
- Overall verdict: Strong paper, valuable contribution to RL, recommended for acceptance.
