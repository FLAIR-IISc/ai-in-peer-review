This paper proposes UNREAL, a reinforcement learning agent that combines A3C with auxiliary control and reward prediction tasks to improve learning efficiency and robustness. The core idea is to learn useful representations by simultaneously optimizing for multiple objectives – the primary task reward and auxiliary tasks like predicting pixel changes, network activations, and immediate rewards. The approach is evaluated on both the Labyrinth 3D environment and a suite of Atari games, demonstrating significant performance gains and increased robustness to hyperparameter settings compared to baseline A3C.

**Decision:** Reject. While the experimental results are promising and the idea of combining auxiliary tasks with A3C is well-motivated, the paper lacks sufficient clarity in several key areas, and the presentation of results feels somewhat inflated. The impact, while potentially significant, isn’t convincingly demonstrated given the presentation.

**Supporting Arguments:**

1. **Lack of Ablation Detail & Justification:** The paper presents numerous ablation studies (A3C + control tasks, + reward prediction, etc.), but the justification for *why* certain ablations were chosen is missing. For example, why specifically pixel control and network feature control? Were other types of auxiliary tasks considered and discarded? The lack of reasoning makes it difficult to assess the contribution of each component.  The benefit of the reward prediction task is stated as "removing the perceptual sparsity of rewards," but this isn't deeply explored or clearly connected to the observed improvements.

2. **Overstated Claims & Presentation of Results:** The claim of "more than twice the final human-normalised mean performance" in Labyrinth sounds impressive, but the definition of "human-normalised" isn’t clear in the main text, and the figures (Fig. 3) are somewhat busy and difficult to interpret.  Presenting median performance alongside the mean would provide a more complete picture. The 10x-18x speedup is also stated very confidently, but without sufficient statistical significance testing or discussion of variance.  Similarly, stating UNREAL “surpasses the current state-of-the-art” on Atari feels strong given the competitive landscape; a more nuanced comparison to recent results is needed. The use of absolute percentage improvements isn’t well contextualized.

3. **Insufficient Detail on Implementation:** The implementation details section (Appendix B) is extensive, but lacks crucial information.  For example, the deconvolutional network architecture used for pixel control is described, but the rationale for these specific choices isn’t provided. How were the hyperparameters for the auxiliary tasks (e.g., λPC) tuned? A more thorough description of the hyperparameter optimization process is required. There’s also a mention of "randomness robustness," but the methodology for assessing this (beyond stating multiple runs were performed) isn’t sufficiently detailed.



**Additional Feedback (for improvement):**

* **Clarify "Human-Normalised" Score:** Provide a precise definition of this metric. How is human performance measured, and how is the agent’s performance normalized?
* **Provide Statistical Significance:** Conduct statistical tests (e.g., t-tests) to demonstrate the significance of the performance improvements reported in the experiments. Report p-values.
* **Expand Ablation Studies:** Investigate a wider range of auxiliary tasks beyond pixel changes and network features. Consider tasks related to environment modeling or more complex high-level goals.
* **Analyze Auxiliary Task Losses:** Include plots showing the evolution of the auxiliary task losses during training. This could provide insights into how these tasks are contributing to the learning process.
* **Visualize Learned Features:** Show examples of the learned features (e.g., activations of hidden units) to provide a qualitative understanding of what the agent is learning.
* **Address the Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the paper claims improved robustness, show how sensitive the UNREAL agent's performance is to the weighting parameters (λVR, λPC, λRP). A sensitivity analysis would be very beneficial.
* **Expand the Related Work:** The related work section could be strengthened by discussing more recent advances in auxiliary task learning and representation learning for reinforcement learning.
* **Explain the intuition behind skewed sampling in Experience Replay**: Briefly explain why using skewed sampling for reward prediction in experience replay improves the learning process.

**Questions for the Authors:**

* Could you provide a more detailed explanation of the rationale behind choosing pixel control and network feature control as auxiliary tasks? What other auxiliary tasks were considered?
* What specific metrics were used to assess the robustness of the agent to hyperparameter settings? Can you provide more details on the hyperparameter optimization process?
* Could you provide examples of the learned features (e.g., activations of hidden units) to illustrate how the auxiliary tasks are influencing the agent’s representation?
* The reward prediction task samples equally from zero and non-zero rewards. Is this choice sensitive to the reward distribution of the environment? Have you experimented with different sampling strategies?
*  How does the performance of UNREAL scale with the number of auxiliary tasks? Is there a point of diminishing returns, or even negative transfer?
