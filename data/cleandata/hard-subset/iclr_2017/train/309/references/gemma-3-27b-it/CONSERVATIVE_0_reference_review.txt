This paper proposes UNREAL, an agent architecture that combines A3C with auxiliary control and reward prediction tasks to improve data efficiency and robustness in reinforcement learning. The core idea is to learn useful representations by simultaneously optimizing for multiple objectives, including both the primary task reward and auxiliary signals derived from pixel changes, network activations, and reward prediction. The authors demonstrate improvements on both the Labyrinth 3D environment and a suite of Atari games.

**Reject.** While the experimental results demonstrate performance gains, the approach feels overly complex and lacks sufficient justification for the specific choices made. The improvements, while notable, do not seem substantial enough to warrant the added architectural complexity and the sheer number of hyperparameters introduced. The paper relies heavily on empirical results without providing sufficient theoretical grounding or ablation studies to understand *why* these auxiliary tasks are effective.

The primary concern is the lack of a compelling argument for the specific auxiliary tasks chosen. Pixel control and feature control feel somewhat arbitrary. While the authors mention intuition about learning useful features, there's little systematic investigation into which features are actually learned or how they contribute to performance. The reward prediction task is better motivated, but the specific implementation details (e.g., skewed sampling, classification loss) appear ad-hoc and lack rigorous justification. 

Furthermore, the paper reports numerous experiments but lacks sufficient detail regarding hyperparameter tuning. While a range of hyperparameters were tested, the process is not clearly described. The claim of robustness to hyperparameters needs to be substantiated with more thorough analysis, potentially showing variance in performance across different hyperparameter settings. The ablation studies, while present, don’t provide a clear understanding of the relative contributions of each auxiliary task. It’s unclear if the gains are due to the combination of tasks or if a single, well-chosen auxiliary task could achieve similar results with less complexity.

The experimental setup, particularly in Labyrinth, also raises concerns. The levels are categorized, but it's not clear how representative these categories are of the broader range of possible Labyrinth environments. The human-normalized scores, while useful, need to be interpreted cautiously, as the skill of the “human” baseline is not defined. 

**Additional Feedback (for improvement):**

*   **Theoretical Justification:** Provide a more theoretical rationale for why these specific auxiliary tasks should be beneficial. Could information-theoretic arguments or representational learning theory be used to support the choices?
*   **Ablation Analysis:** Conduct a more thorough ablation study, systematically evaluating the impact of each auxiliary task and hyperparameter. Include statistical significance tests to ensure that observed improvements are not due to chance.
*   **Feature Visualization:** Visualize the features learned by the agent, particularly those related to the auxiliary tasks. This would provide valuable insights into what the agent is actually learning.
*   **Hyperparameter Tuning Details:** Provide a detailed description of the hyperparameter tuning process, including the search space, optimization algorithm, and computational resources used.
*   **Robustness Analysis:** Quantify the robustness of the agent to hyperparameters by reporting performance variance across different settings.
*   **Comparison to Simpler Baselines:** Compare UNREAL to simpler baselines that incorporate only one or two carefully chosen auxiliary tasks. This would help determine if the complexity of UNREAL is truly necessary.
*   **Generalization:** Assess the generalization ability of the learned representations by transferring them to new environments or tasks.

**Questions for the Authors:**

*   What specific criteria were used to select the pixel control grid size (e.g., 20x20)? Was this optimized, or is it a fixed parameter?
*   Why was a multi-class cross-entropy loss chosen for reward prediction, rather than a regression loss?
*   Can you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process, including the search space and optimization algorithm used?
*   How sensitive is the performance of UNREAL to the weighting of the different loss components (λVR, λPC, λRP)?
*   What are the most salient features learned by the agent in the Labyrinth environment, particularly those related to the auxiliary tasks? Can you visualize these features?
