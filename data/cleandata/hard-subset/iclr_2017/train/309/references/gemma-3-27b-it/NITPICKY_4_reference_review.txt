This paper proposes UNREAL, a reinforcement learning agent that augments A3C with auxiliary control and reward prediction tasks to improve data efficiency and robustness. The core idea is to learn useful representations by simultaneously optimizing for multiple objectives, including controlling pixel changes, network features, and predicting immediate rewards. The authors demonstrate significant performance gains on both the Labyrinth 3D environment and a suite of Atari games, showing improved sample efficiency and hyperparameter robustness.

**Reject.** While the core idea of leveraging auxiliary tasks for representation learning is sound and the results demonstrate improvements, the paper suffers from a multitude of minor flaws, inconsistencies, and a lack of rigorous justification for several design choices. The sheer number of these issues undermines confidence in the overall validity of the results and suggests insufficient attention to detail.

The primary concern lies with the lack of clarity and justification surrounding the hyperparameter selection process. The authors state they "retuned the hyperparameters" for different experiments, but fail to provide any details on *how* this was done. Were grid searches performed? Bayesian optimization? Simply trying a few random values? Without this information, the reported performance improvements could be attributed to lucky hyperparameter tuning rather than the UNREAL architecture itself. The claim of improved "robustness" to hyperparameters is also not convincingly demonstrated; simply showing performance across different settings doesn't equate to robustness – it requires an analysis of variance or similar statistical measure.

Furthermore, several methodological details are ambiguous. For example, the precise architecture of the reward prediction network is described but the justification for choosing a separate feedforward network instead of sharing more parameters with the main agent isn't clearly articulated. The rationale for the specific weighting factors (λVR, λPC, λRP) is also missing. Are these hand-tuned? If so, how? The authors mention a log-uniform distribution for sampling hyperparameters but don't specify the range used.

Minor but pervasive issues include inconsistent notation (e.g., using both θ and θ− for parameters), grammatical errors throughout the text, and a verbose writing style. The inclusion of extensive supplementary material (detailed implementation details, additional figures) does not compensate for the lack of clarity in the main paper. The formatting of the references is also inconsistent. The frequent referencing of arXiv preprints is acceptable but should be noted.

The experiment section, while presenting promising results, also lacks sufficient analysis. The ablation studies are helpful, but a more systematic evaluation of the contribution of each auxiliary task would be beneficial. The comparison to existing methods is limited, and the authors do not adequately address potential limitations of their approach.

**Additional Feedback (for potential revision):**

*   **Hyperparameter Tuning:** Provide a detailed description of the hyperparameter tuning process. Include the search space, optimization algorithm, and number of trials.
*   **Ablation Study:** Conduct a more comprehensive ablation study, systematically evaluating the contribution of each auxiliary task and the impact of different weighting factors.
*   **Statistical Significance:** Perform statistical tests to demonstrate the significance of the observed performance improvements.
*   **Justification of Design Choices:** Provide a clear rationale for all design choices, including the architecture of the reward prediction network, the weighting factors, and the selection of specific auxiliary tasks.
*   **Clarity and Conciseness:** Rewrite the paper to improve clarity and conciseness. Eliminate unnecessary jargon and avoid verbose descriptions.
*   **Notation:** Ensure consistent notation throughout the paper.
*   **Grammar and Proofreading:** Thoroughly proofread the paper to correct grammatical errors and typos.
*   **Formatting:** Standardize the formatting of references and ensure consistency throughout the paper.
*   **Address limitations:** Discuss any potential drawbacks or limitations of the UNREAL approach.

**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  Can you provide a detailed description of the hyperparameter tuning process used in your experiments, including the search space, optimization algorithm, and number of trials?
2.  What is the justification for using a separate feedforward network for reward prediction, instead of sharing more parameters with the main A3C agent?
3.  How were the weighting factors (λVR, λPC, λRP) determined?
4.  Can you provide the exact ranges used for sampling hyperparameters from the log-uniform distribution?
5.  What specific steps were taken to ensure the reproducibility of your results?
