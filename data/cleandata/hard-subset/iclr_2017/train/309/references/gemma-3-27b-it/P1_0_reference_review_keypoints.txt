- Summary: Proposes UNREAL, combining A3C with auxiliary control and reward-prediction tasks to improve representation learning, data efficiency, and robustness, showing strong gains on Labyrinth and Atari.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — significant contribution with solid empirical support.  
- Strong empirical results: Substantial, statistically convincing improvements over A3C and baselines, with useful ablation studies.  
- Well-motivated approach: Clearly motivated use of auxiliary tasks for representation learning, grounded in relevant literature.  
- Sound technical design: Proper integration of auxiliary tasks (off-policy learning, replay skewing, combined loss) with reproducible implementation details.  
- Robustness analysis: UNREAL shows lower sensitivity to hyperparameters than A3C.  
- Computational cost: Needs discussion/comparison of increased training cost from auxiliary tasks versus data-efficiency gains.  
- Auxiliary task contributions: Request for more detailed analysis of which auxiliary tasks help specific levels or games.  
- Reward prediction clarification: Ask whether reward prediction is binary onset or also predicts magnitude and request an example.  
- Intrinsic motivation connection: Suggest explicitly relating auxiliary tasks to intrinsic motivation literature.  
- Hyperparameter tuning question: How sensitive is performance to loss-weighting (λVR, λPC, λRP) and were weights systematically searched or set heuristically?  
- Scalability question: How performance scales with number of auxiliary tasks and whether diminishing or negative returns occur.  
- Generalization question: Ask about UNREAL’s performance in domains beyond Labyrinth and Atari.  
- Replay buffer skewing question: Was an exploration bonus considered alongside skewed replay to encourage visiting rewarding states?
