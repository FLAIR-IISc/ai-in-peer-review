## Review of "AUXILIARY TASKS FOR REINFORCEMENT LEARNING"

**1. Summary:**

This paper presents UNREAL, a novel reinforcement learning agent architecture that combines A3C with auxiliary control and reward prediction tasks. The authors argue that these auxiliary tasks – pixel control, feature control, and reward prediction – promote faster learning, more robust learning, and ultimately higher performance. They demonstrate substantial improvements over A3C on both the complex 3D Labyrinth environment and a suite of Atari games, showing both increased performance and improved robustness to hyperparameter settings. The paper provides a detailed description of the architecture and experimental setup, and includes ablation studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of each auxiliary task.

**2. Decision:**

**Accept.** The paper presents a compelling and well-executed contribution to the field of reinforcement learning. The experimental results are strong, demonstrating a clear and significant advantage of UNREAL over the A3C baseline in both challenging environments. The motivation is solid, and the approach is well-grounded in prior work.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

* **Strong Empirical Results:** The results in Labyrinth are particularly impressive, demonstrating more than a 2x improvement in performance compared to A3C. The improved data efficiency (10x speedup) is also a significant contribution. The Atari results further validate the effectiveness of the approach.
* **Well-Motivated Approach:** The authors clearly articulate the motivation behind using auxiliary tasks – to learn a more effective representation for faster and more robust learning. This aligns with current research trends in representation learning and its application to RL. The choice of auxiliary tasks (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction) appears thoughtful and well-justified.
* **Thorough Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies effectively demonstrate the contribution of each auxiliary task, providing insights into their individual and combined effects.
* **Clear and Detailed Description:** The paper provides a thorough description of the UNREAL architecture and experimental setup, making it easy to understand and reproduce the results.
* **Robustness Analysis:** Showing improved robustness to hyperparameter settings is a significant benefit and demonstrates a practical advantage of the approach.

**4. Additional Feedback:**

* **Discussion of Computational Cost:** While the performance gains are substantial, it would be helpful to discuss the computational cost of adding the auxiliary tasks.  Is there a significant increase in training time or memory requirements? A brief analysis of the trade-offs between performance and computational cost would be valuable.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the paper shows robustness, a more detailed analysis of the hyperparameter sensitivity of each auxiliary task (e.g., λPC, λRP, λVR) could provide further insights.
* **Visualization of Learned Features:** Providing more visualizations of the learned features, perhaps highlighting how the auxiliary tasks shape the internal representations of the agent, could further strengthen the paper's claims.  For example, visualizing the learned Qaux values for pixel control could demonstrate the agent's focus on salient changes in the environment.
* **Connection to Intrinsic Motivation:** The reward prediction task bears a strong resemblance to intrinsic motivation approaches.  Explicitly discussing this connection and how UNREAL differs from existing intrinsic motivation methods would be beneficial.



**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  **Computational Cost:** What is the approximate increase in training time and memory requirements when using the full UNREAL architecture compared to the A3C baseline?
2.  **Hyperparameter Tuning:** Could you elaborate on the strategies used for tuning the weights (λVR, λPC, λRP) for the auxiliary losses? Were there any particular challenges encountered in finding suitable values?
3.  **Learned Feature Analysis:** Do you have any qualitative examples of the features learned by the agent (e.g., activation patterns in hidden layers) that demonstrate the influence of the auxiliary tasks?
4.  **Generalization to Other Environments:** Have you explored the applicability of UNREAL to other reinforcement learning environments beyond Labyrinth and Atari? If so, what were the results?



Overall, this is a strong paper with significant contributions to the field. The authors have presented a well-motivated, well-executed, and thoroughly evaluated approach that significantly improves the performance and robustness of reinforcement learning agents. I recommend it for acceptance to ICLR.
