## Review for ICLR 2024

**Paper Title:** AUXILIARY TASKS FOR REINFORCEMENT LEARNING

**1. Summary:**

This paper presents UNREAL, a reinforcement learning agent that significantly improves data efficiency and robustness by incorporating auxiliary tasks alongside the primary reinforcement learning objective. The authors introduce auxiliary control tasks (pixel changes and network feature activation) and a reward prediction task, integrated into an A3C framework with experience replay. The core idea is to learn a richer representation by forcing the agent to actively explore and understand its environment beyond simply maximizing reward. Extensive experiments on the Labyrinth 3D platform and Atari demonstrate that UNREAL substantially outperforms A3C, achieving higher performance with fewer samples and increased robustness to hyperparameter settings.

**2. Decision:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. The paper addresses a significant challenge in reinforcement learning – sample efficiency and robustness – with a well-motivated and empirically validated approach. The results are compelling, particularly the performance gains on the visually complex Labyrinth environment.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

* **Novelty and Motivation:** The work builds upon existing ideas of auxiliary tasks and representation learning, but provides a compelling combination and integration within a practical RL framework (A3C). The motivation for improving sample efficiency and robustness is clearly articulated and aligns with important open challenges in the field.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The experiments demonstrate substantial improvements over a strong baseline (A3C) on both Labyrinth and Atari. The improvement in sample efficiency is particularly noteworthy, highlighting the benefits of the proposed auxiliary tasks. The ablation studies (comparing different combinations of auxiliary tasks) are well-designed and insightful.
* **Thorough Evaluation:** The experiments are well-conducted and include detailed analyses of robustness to hyperparameters. The inclusion of visualizations (mentioned but not directly included in the submission) and a video further enhance the understanding of the approach.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the proposed method and experimental setup. The inclusion of relevant background information and related work is helpful.

**4. Additional Feedback (Suggestions for Improvement):**

* **Visualizations:** The paper mentions visualizations but does not include them. Including visualizations of learned features, pixel control outputs, and the reward prediction would greatly improve understanding of *how* the auxiliary tasks are impacting the agent’s behavior and learned representations.
* **Reward Prediction Loss Analysis:** While the reward prediction task is presented as beneficial, a more detailed analysis of the performance of the reward predictor itself would be valuable.  What is the accuracy of the reward prediction? How does the loss change over time?
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the paper shows robustness, it would be helpful to provide a more in-depth analysis of the hyperparameters specific to the auxiliary tasks (e.g., lambda values).  A sensitivity analysis for these parameters could further solidify the claims of robustness.
* **Computational Cost:** A brief discussion of the computational overhead introduced by the auxiliary tasks would be beneficial. Does UNREAL require significantly more compute than A3C?

**5. Questions for the Authors:**

* **Regarding Feature Control:**  You mention that feature control yielded promising initial results. Could you elaborate on why it performed similarly to pixel control in your experiments? Are there specific hidden layers that are more effective targets for feature control?
* **Reward Prediction vs. Value Prediction:** You highlight the distinction between reward prediction and value prediction. Could you explain in more detail why biasing the reward predictor is less likely to introduce bias into the policy compared to biasing the value function?
* **Scaling to More Complex Environments:**  The Labyrinth and Atari environments are challenging, but still relatively limited. Do you anticipate any significant challenges in scaling UNREAL to even more complex and high-dimensional environments?  What modifications, if any, would be necessary?
* **Replay Buffer Strategy:** The paper mentions skewing the replay buffer for reward prediction. Did you experiment with other replay buffer strategies, such as prioritized experience replay based on TD error? Would combining these strategies be beneficial?



Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of reinforcement learning. The proposed UNREAL agent demonstrates significant improvements in sample efficiency and robustness, and the experiments are well-designed and executed. Addressing the points raised in the "Additional Feedback" section would further strengthen the paper and enhance its impact.
