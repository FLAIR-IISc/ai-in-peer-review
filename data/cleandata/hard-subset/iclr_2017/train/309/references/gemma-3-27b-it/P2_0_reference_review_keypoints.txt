- Proposes UNREAL: A3C augmented with auxiliary control tasks and reward prediction to learn stronger representations.  
- Demonstrates substantial performance and data-efficiency gains on Labyrinth and Atari, with clear algorithm description and experiments.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — compelling contribution with strong empirical support.  
- Strong empirical results: >2× improvement on Labyrinth, human-level on some levels, and clear Atari gains.  
- Well-motivated: Auxiliaries grounded in prior work (options, UVFA, successor reps) to improve representation and exploration.  
- Comprehensive evaluation: Ablation studies and comparisons to baselines (pixel recon, input change prediction) clarify contributions.  
- Clear, detailed methodology: Architecture, losses, and implementation described sufficiently for replication.  
- Robustness analysis: Shows increased robustness to hyperparameters across settings.  
- Request more detailed discussion of which hyperparameters most affect performance and their optimal settings per environment.  
- Ask for assessment of computational overhead (training time, memory) introduced by auxiliary tasks.  
- Suggest visualizations of learned features (hidden activations, pixel-control outputs) to provide intuition.  
- Recommend further exploration of feature-control choices (different hidden layers, feature selection).  
- Q1: How was λPC (pixel-control weight) fitted for Atari, and can this be automated or learned?  
- Q2: Why does controlling pixel changes help more than merely predicting input changes — any hypothesis?  
- Q3: Were other recurrent architectures (e.g., GRU) tested instead of LSTM, and what were the outcomes?  
- Q4: Have policy-gradient-based auxiliary tasks been considered alongside the value-based (Q-learning) auxiliaries, and could a hybrid help?
