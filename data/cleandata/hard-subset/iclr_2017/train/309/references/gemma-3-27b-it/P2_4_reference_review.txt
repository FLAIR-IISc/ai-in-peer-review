## ICLR 2024 Review

**Paper:** UNREAL: Auxiliary Tasks for Deep Reinforcement Learning
**Reviewer:** [Your Name/ID]

**1. Summary:**

This paper presents UNREAL, a deep reinforcement learning agent that leverages a combination of auxiliary tasks – pixel control, feature control, and reward prediction – to enhance learning efficiency and robustness. The authors argue that these auxiliary tasks promote better feature representation learning, leading to significant performance improvements in both a complex 3D environment (Labyrinth) and a suite of Atari games. The approach integrates these auxiliary losses into a unified A3C framework, showcasing compelling results demonstrating faster learning, higher final performance, and greater resilience to hyperparameter choices compared to baseline A3C and other methods.

**2. Decision:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. The paper presents a strong and well-supported contribution to the field of reinforcement learning. The empirical results are compelling, demonstrating significant improvements in both challenging 3D and 2D environments. The combination of auxiliary tasks is a clever and effective approach to address key challenges in RL.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

*   **Strong Empirical Results:** The results in Labyrinth are particularly impressive, demonstrating a >2x performance gain over A3C. The improvements on Atari are also significant, showing the generalizability of the approach. The consistent performance across hyperparameter settings reported in Figure 3 (top right) is a strong indicator of robustness.
*   **Well-Motivated Approach:** The paper provides a clear and logical justification for the use of auxiliary tasks, connecting them to the need for robust feature representation and efficient exploration. The authors effectively build upon existing work in temporal abstraction and representation learning, clearly positioning their contribution within the broader literature.
*   **Comprehensive Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies (Figure 3, Section 4.1) demonstrate the value of each auxiliary task, and the combination thereof. The comparison to pixel reconstruction and immediate reward prediction shows the superiority of learning to *control* pixels, rather than just predict them.
*   **Clear and Well-Written Presentation:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to understand the proposed approach and the experimental results. The inclusion of a video showcasing the agent's performance is a valuable addition.

**4. Additional Feedback (for Improvement):**

*   **Discussion of Computational Cost:** The paper focuses on data efficiency, which is excellent. However, a brief discussion of the *computational* cost of adding these auxiliary tasks would be valuable. Does the increased complexity of the network and the additional loss calculations significantly impact training time?
*   **Reward Prediction Details:** While the paper describes using a skewed sampling for reward prediction, it would be helpful to provide more details about the specific implementation (e.g., exact sampling ratios, the architecture of the reward prediction network).
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the paper shows robustness, quantifying the sensitivity to λVR, λPC, and λRP in a more systematic way (e.g., a sensitivity analysis) would further strengthen the claims.
*   **Error bars:** Including error bars on the performance curves would aid in the interpretation of results and demonstrate statistical significance.

**5. Questions for the Authors:**

*   You mention that the learning rate and entropy cost were sampled from log-uniform distributions. Why were these distributions chosen, and how were the ranges for these distributions determined?
*   Could you elaborate on the motivation behind using a *deconvolutional* network for generating the auxiliary values in the pixel control task? Why is this approach preferred over a simpler linear projection?
*   How does the performance of UNREAL scale with the number of auxiliary tasks? Is there a point of diminishing returns, or even negative impact, when adding too many auxiliary objectives?
*   You discuss the potential drawback of the reward prediction task introducing bias. How did you ensure that the weighting of this task (λRP) was carefully tuned to avoid detrimental effects on the main policy?



Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of reinforcement learning. The approach is well-motivated, the results are compelling, and the presentation is clear. Addressing the minor suggestions outlined above would further enhance the paper’s impact.
