## Review of "AUXILIARY TASKS FOR REINFORCEMENT LEARNING"

**1. Summary:**

This paper proposes a novel reinforcement learning architecture, UNREAL, which combines Asynchronous Advantage Actor-Critic (A3C) with auxiliary control tasks (pixel changes, network feature activations) and reward prediction. The core idea is to improve representation learning by encouraging the agent to learn useful features through these auxiliary objectives, leading to faster learning, improved data efficiency, and increased robustness. The authors demonstrate significant performance gains on both the complex 3D Labyrinth environment and a suite of Atari games, outperforming standard A3C and other baselines. The paper provides a well-structured explanation of the approach and demonstrates its effectiveness through comprehensive experiments.

**2. Decision:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. The paper presents a significant contribution to the field of reinforcement learning, demonstrating a clear improvement over existing methods in challenging environments. The combination of auxiliary tasks and the thoughtful design of the UNREAL architecture are compelling and well-supported by empirical results.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

* **Strong Empirical Results:** The performance improvements on both Labyrinth and Atari are substantial and statistically convincing. The consistently higher scores and improved data efficiency compared to A3C and other baselines provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The ablation studies demonstrating the individual contribution of each auxiliary task are particularly valuable.
* **Well-Motivated Approach:** The motivation for using auxiliary tasks for representation learning is clearly articulated and grounded in relevant literature (options, UVFA, successor representations, etc.). The authors convincingly argue that learning to control internal features and predict rewards can lead to more robust and efficient learning.
* **Sound Technical Design:** The integration of auxiliary tasks into the A3C framework is well-designed and technically sound. The use of off-policy learning for auxiliary tasks, the replay buffer for skewed sampling, and the combined loss function all contribute to the effectiveness of the approach. The details provided regarding the implementation (network architectures, hyperparameters) are sufficient for reproducibility.
* **Robustness Analysis:** The analysis of the method’s robustness to hyperparameter settings is a valuable addition, showing that UNREAL is less sensitive to hyperparameter choices than A3C, a key benefit in practice.

**4. Additional Feedback (for improvement):**

* **Discussion of Computational Cost:** While the paper demonstrates improved data efficiency, a discussion of the increased computational cost associated with training multiple auxiliary tasks would be beneficial. A comparison of training time between A3C and UNREAL, even qualitatively, would strengthen the overall evaluation.
* **Further Analysis of Auxiliary Task Contributions:** While the ablation studies are good, a more detailed analysis of *which* auxiliary tasks contribute most to performance on *specific* levels or games could offer further insights.  Are pixel control tasks more beneficial in visually complex environments, while feature control is better in scenarios requiring abstract reasoning?
* **Clarification on Reward Prediction:** The reward prediction task is described as predicting the “onset” of reward. Is it purely binary (reward/no reward), or is it also predicting the magnitude? A small example of the input/output relationship would be helpful.
* **Connection to Intrinsic Motivation:** The auxiliary tasks can be seen as a form of intrinsic motivation. Explicitly connecting the work to the broader literature on intrinsic motivation would contextualize the contribution.

**5. Questions for the Authors:**

* **Hyperparameter Tuning:** How sensitive is the performance of UNREAL to the weighting parameters (λVR, λPC, λRP) used in the combined loss function?  Was a systematic hyperparameter search performed to find optimal values, or were they set heuristically?
* **Scalability:**  How does the performance of UNREAL scale with the number of auxiliary tasks? Is there a point of diminishing returns, or even negative impact, from adding too many auxiliary objectives?
* **Generalization:** How well does UNREAL generalize to environments significantly different from Labyrinth and Atari? Have you explored its performance in other domains?
* **Replay Buffer Skewing:** The paper mentions skewing the replay buffer for reward prediction. Was any exploration bonus mechanism considered to further encourage exploration of rewarding states?



Overall, this is a well-written and impactful paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of reinforcement learning. The acceptance recommendation is based on the strength of the empirical results, the clear motivation, and the sound technical design of the UNREAL architecture. Addressing the suggestions above would further strengthen the paper and enhance its impact.
