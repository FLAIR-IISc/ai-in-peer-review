## ICLR 2024 Review

**Paper Title:** AUXILIARY TASKS FOR REINFORCEMENT LEARNING

**Summary:**

This paper proposes the UNREAL algorithm, which augments a standard A3C agent with a combination of auxiliary tasks: pixel control, feature control, and reward prediction. The core idea is to learn richer representations by encouraging the agent to actively control different aspects of its environment and predict immediate rewards.  The authors demonstrate significant improvements in both sample efficiency and final performance on challenging 3D environments (Labyrinth) and a suite of Atari games, also showing increased robustness to hyperparameter settings.

**Decision:** **Accept**

**Key Reasons:**

* **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper consistently demonstrates substantial improvements across diverse environments (3D and 2D) compared to a strong baseline (A3C) and ablated versions of UNREAL. The gains in sample efficiency are particularly impressive.
* **Well-Motivated Approach:** The authors provide a clear rationale for using auxiliary tasks, grounded in the need for better representation learning in sparse reward scenarios. The specific choices of auxiliary tasks (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction) are also well-justified.

**Supporting Arguments:**

The paper provides compelling evidence to support its claims. The experiments in Labyrinth are particularly convincing, showcasing significant performance boosts and robustness to hyperparameters. The Atari results further validate the generalizability of the approach. The ablation studies are crucial in demonstrating the contributions of each auxiliary task, and the comparison to unsupervised baselines (pixel reconstruction and input change prediction) clearly positions UNREAL as a more effective method.  The detailed experimental setup and clear presentation of results contribute to the rigor of the evaluation. The provided code and video further enhance the reproducibility and understandability of the work.

The authors successfully address a core challenge in RL – learning effective representations in complex environments – and offer a practical and effective solution. The combination of auxiliary tasks seems to be synergistic, leading to improvements beyond what can be achieved by any single task alone.  The method is also relatively straightforward to implement, building upon the well-established A3C algorithm.

**Additional Feedback (for improvement):**

* **Deeper Analysis of Auxiliary Task Contributions:** While ablation studies are present, a more granular analysis of how each auxiliary task influences the learned representations could be beneficial. Visualizing the learned features, or analyzing the gradients flowing through different parts of the network during training, might offer deeper insights.
* **Computational Cost:** A discussion of the computational overhead introduced by the auxiliary tasks would be valuable. While the sample efficiency gains likely offset this cost, it's important to quantify the trade-offs.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the paper shows increased robustness to hyperparameters, the range explored could be broadened. Investigating the performance of UNREAL with more extreme hyperparameter settings might reveal potential limitations.
* **Connection to Intrinsic Motivation:** The work implicitly relates to intrinsic motivation.  Explicitly connecting the auxiliary tasks to established theories of intrinsic motivation (e.g., prediction error minimization, empowerment) could strengthen the theoretical grounding of the approach.

**Questions for the Authors:**

1. **Regarding feature control:** What specific hidden layer was chosen for feature control, and why?  Were other layers explored, and if so, what were the results?
2. **Regarding reward prediction:**  The paper mentions skewed sampling to balance zero and non-zero rewards.  Could you elaborate on the reasoning behind this particular balancing strategy? Were other ratios explored?
3. **Regarding the scalability of pixel control:** The pixel control task involves a fixed grid size. How does performance scale with the resolution of the input image, and the size of the grid?  Does this approach become computationally prohibitive for very high-resolution images?
4. **Error Analysis:**  Are there specific scenarios or levels where UNREAL struggles? A more detailed error analysis could provide valuable insights into the limitations of the approach.



Overall, this is a strong paper with significant contributions to the field of reinforcement learning. The proposed UNREAL algorithm is well-motivated, thoroughly evaluated, and demonstrates impressive results. I believe this work is worthy of acceptance to ICLR.
