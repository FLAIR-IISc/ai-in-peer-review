- Proposes UNREAL: A3C augmented with auxiliary control and reward-prediction tasks.  
- Auxiliary tasks (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction) aim to improve representation learning.  
- Empirical gains: large improvements over A3C in Labyrinth (≈2x performance, ~10x data efficiency) and solid Atari gains.  
- Ablation studies show individual and combined contributions of the auxiliary tasks.  
- Architecture and experimental setup are clearly described and reproducible.  
- Demonstrates improved robustness to hyperparameter settings.  
- Reviewer decision: Accept — strong, well-executed contribution to RL.  
- Request to report computational cost trade-offs: training time and memory overhead of UNREAL vs A3C.  
- Suggest deeper hyperparameter-sensitivity analysis for auxiliary loss weights (λPC, λRP, λVR).  
- Recommend visualizations of learned features/Qaux to illustrate how auxiliary tasks shape representations.  
- Ask authors to discuss the connection/differences to intrinsic motivation methods.  
- Questions for authors: quantify training time/memory increase relative to A3C.  
- Questions for authors: describe strategies and challenges for tuning auxiliary-loss weights.  
- Questions for authors: provide qualitative examples of learned features/activations showing auxiliary-task influence.  
- Questions for authors: report results or applicability of UNREAL on other environments beyond Labyrinth and Atari.
