- Proposes UNREAL: an RL agent using auxiliary tasks (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction) to learn better representations.  
- Combines on-policy A3C with off-policy auxiliary learning via experience replay.  
- Demonstrates large empirical gains: ~10x faster learning and >2x final score on Labyrinth, consistent improvements on Atari.  
- Strong experimental validation with extensive hyperparameter sweeps and ablation studies.  
- Architecture and training procedure are clearly described and methodologically sound.  
- Comprehensive literature review situating the work within temporal abstraction and auxiliary-task research.  
- Novel integration of multiple auxiliary tasks into a unified framework with well-justified task choices.  
- Suggest clarifying how auxiliary tasks interact, including visualization of learned features/policies and potential synergies or conflicts.  
- Recommend reporting computational costs (memory, training time) relative to A3C.  
- Request deeper analysis of the reward-prediction task and examples of what it learns during gameplay.  
- Suggest testing generalization on more diverse environments (e.g., MuJoCo, other 3D platforms).  
- Ask why pixel control uses an 80x80 crop and 20x20 grid — empirical vs. theoretical basis.  
- Ask which hidden layer is used for feature control and the rationale for that choice.  
- Ask how loss weights (λVR, λPC, λRP) were chosen and sensitivity to those hyperparameters.  
- Ask for quantitative measures of robustness to hyperparameters (e.g., performance variance).  
- Ask for explanation why input reconstruction performed worse in unsupervised comparisons.  
- Decision: Accept — strong paper with compelling results and significant contribution.
