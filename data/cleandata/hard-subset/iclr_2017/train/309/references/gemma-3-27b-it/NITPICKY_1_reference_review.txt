This paper proposes the UNREAL agent, an extension to the A3C algorithm that incorporates auxiliary control tasks (pixel and feature control) and reward prediction to improve learning efficiency and robustness in both 3D (Labyrinth) and 2D (Atari) environments. The authors argue that these auxiliary tasks promote better feature learning, leading to faster training and more stable performance. They present results demonstrating significant improvements over baseline A3C and other methods on Labyrinth and Atari benchmarks.

**Reject.** While the paper presents a compelling idea and achieves promising empirical results, the sheer volume of minor flaws and inconsistencies, coupled with a lack of rigorous analysis of individual components, ultimately undermines confidence in the overall contribution. The presentation feels rushed and lacks the precision expected of a top-tier conference submission.

The core issue lies in the somewhat haphazard presentation of experiments and results. While the authors demonstrate improvements, the ablation studies lack nuance. For example, simply stating “combining different auxiliary tasks leads to further improvements” without presenting a clear ablation table showcasing the incremental benefit of each added task is insufficient. The impact of varying λVR, λPC, and λRP is also glossed over; a sensitivity analysis is crucial. The claim of a 10x speedup, while impressive, requires far more careful justification. How was “speedup” measured exactly? Is this consistent across all levels? Is it statistically significant? 

Furthermore, the writing style contains numerous instances of imprecision and potential ambiguity. For instance, the description of the deconvolutional network for pixel control is convoluted and difficult to follow. The distinction between “reward-focused auxiliary tasks” and “auxiliary control tasks” remains somewhat unclear. The reward prediction task is described as using a “simpler feedforward network,” but the justification for this architectural choice isn't compelling. Why is a simpler network preferable?  What is the capacity of this network? 

The experimental setup also raises concerns. The hyperparameter search space is mentioned but not fully detailed. How were the 50 random hyperparameter settings chosen? Was there any attempt to optimize these parameters beyond random search? The claim of robustness needs to be substantiated with a more systematic analysis of performance across hyperparameter settings, beyond the scatter plots presented. The replay buffer size (2k) feels arbitrarily chosen. Why this particular value?

The level descriptions in the Labyrinth experiments (Section 4.1) are overly verbose and interrupt the flow. This section could be streamlined significantly. The figure captions are sometimes uninformative, and several references are missing page numbers or specific details. The inclusion of a large table of raw Atari scores in the appendix (D) is unnecessary and clutters the submission.

**Additional Feedback (for potential revision, should the authors choose to resubmit):**

*   **Ablation Studies:** Conduct a thorough ablation study. Present results in a clear table showing the performance of A3C, A3C + pixel control, A3C + feature control, A3C + reward prediction, UNREAL, and variants with different combinations of auxiliary tasks.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Perform a systematic sensitivity analysis of the weighting terms (λVR, λPC, λRP). Visualize the results in a clear manner (e.g., heatmaps).
*   **Statistical Significance:** Provide statistical significance tests (e.g., t-tests) to support the claims of improvement.
*   **Clarity of Writing:** Revise the manuscript to improve clarity and precision. Define key terms clearly and avoid ambiguous language.
*   **Architectural Details:** Provide more detailed descriptions of the network architectures, including the number of parameters and the activation functions used.
*   **Experiment Setup:** Clearly document the hyperparameter search space, the optimization procedure, and the justification for the chosen parameters.
*   **Replay Buffer Size:** Provide a rationale for the chosen replay buffer size.
*   **Section 4.1:** Streamline the descriptions of the Labyrinth levels.
*   **Appendix D:** Remove the raw Atari scores.

**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  Can you provide a more detailed justification for the choice of 2k as the replay buffer size?
2.  What specific metrics were used to measure the “speedup” in learning? Can you provide confidence intervals for this metric?
3.  Could you elaborate on the hyperparameter search procedure and provide the full search space used for each parameter?
4.  Why was a feedforward network chosen for the reward prediction task instead of leveraging the LSTM shared with the main policy? What is the capacity of the reward prediction network?
5.  Can you provide a more quantitative analysis of the robustness of UNREAL to hyperparameter settings, beyond the scatter plots presented?
