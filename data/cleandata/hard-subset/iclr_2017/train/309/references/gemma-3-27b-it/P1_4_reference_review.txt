## ICLR 2024 Review

**Paper Title:** AUXILIARY TASKS FOR REINFORCEMENT LEARNING

**Reviewer:** [Your Name/Identifier]

**1. Summary:**

This paper presents UNREAL, a novel reinforcement learning architecture that combines A3C with auxiliary control and reward prediction tasks to improve learning speed, data efficiency, and robustness. The authors argue that learning useful representations is crucial for effective RL, and that auxiliary tasks can facilitate this process. They propose two types of auxiliary tasks: pixel control (maximizing pixel changes in grid regions) and feature control (maximizing activation of hidden network units), alongside a reward prediction task.  The core idea is to jointly optimize these tasks with the primary reinforcement learning objective, enabling the agent to learn a more robust and informative representation of the environment. Extensive experiments on the Labyrinth 3D game platform and Atari demonstrate that UNREAL significantly outperforms A3C, achieving state-of-the-art results and demonstrating improved hyperparameter robustness.

**2. Decision:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. 

The paper presents a compelling approach to improving RL performance through auxiliary tasks. The experimental results are strong, demonstrating significant improvements in both 3D and 2D environments. The design and implementation of UNREAL appear well-considered and the ablation studies effectively highlight the contributions of each component.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

* **Strong Empirical Results:** The most compelling aspect of this paper is the consistently strong performance of UNREAL across diverse environments.  The significant improvements on Labyrinth (particularly achieving human-level performance) and Atari demonstrate the broad applicability of the proposed method. The data efficiency gains (10x speedup) are particularly noteworthy.
* **Well-Motivated Approach:** The paper provides a clear and convincing motivation for using auxiliary tasks to learn better representations. The authors appropriately position their work within the context of existing research on temporal abstraction, value function factorization, and representation learning.
* **Rigorous Evaluation:** The inclusion of ablation studies is crucial for understanding the contribution of each component of UNREAL. The comparisons to baseline methods (A3C, pixel reconstruction, input change prediction) provide valuable insights.  The hyperparameter robustness analysis adds further confidence to the results.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the proposed approach. The architecture diagrams (Figure 1) are helpful for understanding the different components of UNREAL.



**4. Additional Feedback (for improvement):**

* **Reward Prediction Details:** The section on reward prediction feels slightly less detailed than the descriptions of the control tasks.  Providing more information on the architecture used for reward prediction (beyond “simpler feedforward network”) and the rationale for the specific choices made would be helpful.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the paper demonstrates improved hyperparameter robustness, providing a more granular analysis of the sensitivity to key hyperparameters (e.g., λVR, λPC, λRP) across different environments would be valuable. A visualization beyond the scatter plots in Appendix C could be beneficial.
* **Connection to Intrinsic Motivation:** The authors touch on the idea of shaping features, but could more explicitly connect their work to the broader literature on intrinsic motivation and curiosity-driven exploration.  Discussing how the auxiliary tasks relate to these concepts would strengthen the paper.
* **Computational Cost:** A discussion of the increased computational cost associated with adding the auxiliary tasks would be valuable. Understanding the trade-off between improved performance and increased computational requirements is important for practical applications.

**5. Questions for the Authors:**

* **Regarding reward prediction:** You mention skewing the reward prediction samples to have an equal representation of zero and non-zero rewards. What impact did this skewing have on the overall performance of UNREAL? Did you experiment with different skewing strategies, and if so, what were the results?
* **Regarding pixel control:**  The deconvolutional network used for pixel control seems somewhat complex. Were other approaches considered for generating auxiliary values, and if so, why was this architecture chosen?
* **Generalization to Other Environments:** Beyond Labyrinth and Atari, have you explored the performance of UNREAL in other reinforcement learning environments?  If so, what were the results? If not, do you anticipate any challenges in adapting UNREAL to different domains?
* **Future Work:** You briefly mention future directions. Could you elaborate on the potential benefits of combining feature control with other auxiliary tasks?  Are there any other types of auxiliary tasks that you are considering exploring?



I believe this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of reinforcement learning, and with minor revisions, it would be a valuable addition to the ICLR conference.
