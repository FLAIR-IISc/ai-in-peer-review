- Presents UNREAL: A3C combined with auxiliary tasks (pixel/feature control, reward prediction) to learn richer representations and improve RL performance.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions based on strong contribution and empirical evidence.  
- Novelty: Combining control-focused auxiliary tasks with reward prediction is a novel and well-motivated approach to representation learning.  
- Empirical results: Significant gains on Labyrinth ( >2x human-normalized) and strong improvements on Atari, outperforming baselines.  
- Ablations: Ablation studies show each auxiliary task contributes and their combination is synergistic; comparisons to pixel reconstruction and simple reward prediction are informative.  
- Robustness: UNREAL is less sensitive to hyperparameters than A3C, improving practicality.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written with useful supporting materials (videos, visualizations).  
- Feature control feedback: Feature control needs clearer explanation—specify which features are controlled and why, plus activation visualizations.  
- Computational cost concern: Need discussion of added computational/training time per step from auxiliary tasks vs. sample efficiency gains.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity detail: Provide ranges tested, aggregation methods, and visualizations of performance across learning rate and entropy cost.  
- Intrinsic motivation connection: Explicitly situate the work within intrinsic motivation/curiosity literature and related methods.  
- Q: Why choose that specific hidden layer for feature control—empirical or theoretical basis?  
- Q: Rationale for using a deconvolutional network for pixel control and whether alternatives were considered.  
- Q: What is the computational overhead (e.g., increased training time per step) from auxiliary tasks?  
- Q: Were different loss-weighting schemes (λVR, λPC, λRP) explored and did any consistently perform best?  
- Q: Will UNREAL generalize to other complex environments beyond Labyrinth and Atari, and what adaptations would be needed?
