## ICLR 2024 Review

**Paper Title:** AUXILIARY TASKS FOR REINFORCEMENT LEARNING

**Reviewer:** [Your Name]

**1. Summary:**

This paper presents the UNREAL algorithm, a novel approach to deep reinforcement learning that combines A3C with auxiliary control and reward prediction tasks. The authors argue that these auxiliary tasks promote faster learning, more robust performance, and ultimately higher performance in complex environments. They demonstrate significant improvements over A3C on both the 3D Labyrinth environment and a suite of Atari games, showcasing both increased performance and improved robustness to hyperparameter settings. The key idea is to leverage off-policy learning for auxiliary tasks to efficiently shape the agent's representation and accelerate learning.

**2. Decision:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. The paper presents a well-motivated and effective approach that achieves strong results on challenging benchmarks. The improvements over A3C are substantial and the analysis provides good insight into the benefits of the proposed auxiliary tasks.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

* **Clear Problem & Motivation:** The paper clearly identifies the problem of sample efficiency and robustness in deep RL, particularly in visually complex environments. The motivation for using auxiliary tasks to learn richer representations is well-established in the literature and the authors effectively position their work within this context.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The results presented are compelling. The performance gains in Labyrinth are particularly impressive, exceeding previous state-of-the-art by a significant margin. The improvements on Atari, while less dramatic, are still substantial and demonstrate the generalizability of the approach. The robustness analysis adds further support to the claims.
* **Well-Defined Methodology:** The UNREAL algorithm is clearly described, with a detailed explanation of each component and how they are integrated. The ablation studies effectively isolate the contributions of different auxiliary tasks. The implementation details are sufficiently comprehensive.
* **Effective Comparison:** The comparison to a baseline A3C agent, along with ablated variants, is well-executed and allows for a clear understanding of the benefits of each component. The comparison to other auxiliary task approaches (pixel reconstruction and input change prediction) provides further insight.

**4. Additional Feedback (for improvement):**

* **Clarify the Hyperparameter Search:** While the paper mentions random hyperparameter sampling, it would be helpful to provide more details about the range and distribution used. Understanding the scope of the hyperparameter search will better contextualize the robustness analysis.
* **Discussion of Computational Cost:** While the focus is on sample efficiency, a brief discussion of the computational overhead introduced by the auxiliary tasks would be valuable.
* **Visualizations of Learned Features:** Showing examples of the features learned by the auxiliary tasks (e.g., pixel control activations, feature control activations) would provide a more intuitive understanding of their impact on the agent's representation.  This could be a good addition to the supplementary material.
* **Error Bars/Statistical Significance:** While the average performance curves are informative, including error bars (e.g., standard deviation) would strengthen the conclusions. Also, assessing the statistical significance of the performance differences would be valuable.

**5. Questions for the Authors:**

* **Regarding Pixel Control:** The paper mentions that the pixel control tasks are applied to a central 80x80 crop. Was this size chosen through experimentation, or is there a specific rationale behind it?  Would applying it to the entire image impact performance?
* **Regarding Feature Control:**  Could you elaborate on the criteria used to select which hidden layer to apply the feature control task to?  Did you experiment with different layers, and if so, what were the results?
* **Scalability:**  The Atari experiments cover a broad range of games. Have you explored the scalability of this approach to even more complex environments (e.g., more complex 3D environments or multi-agent settings)?
* **Reward Prediction Skewing:** You mention skewing the reward prediction samples to represent rewarding events equally.  Was the 0.5 split determined empirically, or is there a theoretical justification for this value? How sensitive is performance to this value?



In conclusion, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of reinforcement learning. The UNREAL algorithm is a promising approach for improving the sample efficiency and robustness of deep RL agents. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above.
