## ICLR 2024 Review

**Paper:** UNREAL: Auxiliary Tasks for Deep Reinforcement Learning
**Reviewer:** [Your Name]

**1. Summary**

This paper presents UNREAL, a deep reinforcement learning agent that leverages auxiliary tasks – specifically, pixel control, feature control, and reward prediction – alongside a standard A3C architecture to improve learning speed, data efficiency, and robustness. The authors demonstrate significant performance gains on both the complex 3D Labyrinth environment and a suite of Atari games, showcasing the effectiveness of their approach. The core idea of combining off-policy learning for auxiliary tasks with on-policy learning for the main task, and leveraging experience replay to balance data distribution, is well-motivated and leads to compelling results.

**2. Decision**

**Accept** with minor revisions. The paper presents a strong contribution to the field of reinforcement learning, demonstrating a significant improvement over existing methods. The experimental results are convincing, and the approach is well-motivated and clearly explained. The novelty of combining these specific auxiliary tasks and the observed performance gains warrant acceptance.

**3. Supporting Arguments**

* **Strong Empirical Results:** The results on Labyrinth are particularly impressive, showing a more than doubling of performance compared to the baseline A3C agent. The gains on Atari are also significant and demonstrate the generalizability of the approach. The ablation studies effectively demonstrate the contribution of each auxiliary task.
* **Well-Motivated Approach:** The authors provide a clear rationale for each auxiliary task, linking them to improving the agent's representation learning capabilities. The discussion of why reward prediction shouldn’t bias the policy directly is insightful.
* **Clear Explanation:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the technical details of the UNREAL architecture and the training procedure. The figures and tables are helpful in understanding the experimental setup and results.
* **Robustness Analysis:** The analysis showing improved robustness to hyperparameters is a valuable contribution, suggesting that UNREAL is less sensitive to hyperparameter tuning than existing methods.
* **Comparison to Related Work:** The related work section is thorough and appropriately positions the contribution of this paper within the broader context of reinforcement learning and representation learning.

**4. Additional Feedback (Suggestions for Improvement)**

* **Discussion of Computational Cost:** While the paper demonstrates improvements in data efficiency, it would be helpful to discuss the computational cost associated with training the additional auxiliary tasks. How does the overall training time compare to A3C?
* **Analysis of Learned Features:** It would be interesting to visualize and analyze the learned features from the auxiliary tasks (e.g., what patterns does the pixel control task learn to control?). This would provide further insight into the benefits of the approach.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the paper shows improved robustness, a more detailed analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity for each auxiliary task could be beneficial. What are the key hyperparameters for each task, and how do they impact performance?
* **Ablation Study Depth:** While the ablations are good, exploring combinations of auxiliary tasks beyond simply adding or removing them (e.g. pixel control + reward prediction) would strengthen the analysis.

**5. Questions for the Authors**

*   The paper mentions prioritizing rewarding events in the experience replay for reward prediction. How sensitive is the performance to the specific skewing factor (e.g., equal representation of zero and non-zero rewards)? Have you experimented with different skewing strategies?
*   Regarding the deconvolutional network used for pixel control: what was the rationale for this specific architecture, and were alternative architectures explored?
*   In the Atari experiments, did you observe any particular games where UNREAL performed significantly better or worse than the baseline, and if so, can you speculate on the reasons for these differences?
*   The paper briefly touches on future work involving combining feature control with other tasks. Can you elaborate on potential synergies and challenges in this direction?



Overall, this is a well-executed and impactful paper. The authors have made a significant contribution to the field of reinforcement learning, and I am confident that this work will be of interest to the ICLR community. The suggested improvements are minor and should not detract from the overall quality of the work.
