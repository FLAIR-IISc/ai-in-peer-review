## ICLR 2024 Review: UNREAL - Auxiliary Tasks for Reinforcement Learning

**1. Summary:**

This paper proposes UNREAL, a novel reinforcement learning agent that leverages a combination of auxiliary control tasks (pixel control and feature control) and reward prediction to improve data efficiency and robustness in both 3D (Labyrinth) and 2D (Atari) environments. The core idea is to learn a richer representation by simultaneously optimizing for multiple objectives, thereby aiding in faster learning and more stable performance.  The authors demonstrate significant improvements over A3C and other state-of-the-art methods on challenging benchmarks, showcasing both higher final performance and faster convergence. The work is well-motivated, builds upon a solid foundation of related research, and presents a compelling argument for the benefits of multi-objective learning in RL.

**2. Decision:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. The paper presents a substantial contribution to the field, demonstrating a clear improvement in both performance and data efficiency. The results are compelling, and the methodology is well-explained. While the presentation could be slightly tightened, the overall quality of the work warrants acceptance.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

* **Strong Empirical Results:** The results presented in both the Labyrinth and Atari experiments are very strong. The significant performance gains over A3C, coupled with the observed speedup in learning, provide convincing evidence of the effectiveness of the UNREAL approach. The human-normalized scores are particularly impressive, and the robustness analysis further reinforces the value of the method.
* **Novel Combination of Techniques:** The work doesn’t introduce entirely new concepts but skillfully combines existing ideas (auxiliary tasks, A3C, replay buffers) in a novel and effective way. The insight to combine both control-based *and* prediction-based auxiliary tasks appears to be a key driver of the observed improvements.
* **Clear Motivation & Related Work:** The authors provide a comprehensive review of related work, appropriately positioning their contribution within the existing literature. The motivation for the approach – to address perceptual sparsity and improve representation learning – is well-articulated.
* **Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies effectively demonstrate the contribution of each component of the UNREAL architecture.  Showing the relative benefit of pixel control, feature control, and reward prediction helps to justify the complexity of the full model.
* **Detailed Implementation Details:**  The inclusion of thorough implementation details (Appendix B) is crucial for reproducibility and helps to establish the scientific rigor of the work.


**4. Additional Feedback (Suggestions for Improvement):**

* **Clarity of Loss Function:** While Equation (2) presents the overall loss function, its complexity makes it difficult to quickly grasp the relative weighting of each component.  Perhaps a table summarizing the typical values used for λVR, λPC, and λRP in the experiments would be helpful.
* **Discussion of Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper highlights the increased robustness to hyperparameters, which is excellent. However, a more detailed discussion of *which* hyperparameters were most sensitive and how the authors addressed this sensitivity during tuning would be valuable.
* **Visualizations:** The visualizations in Figures 7 & 8 are helpful, but could be improved. Larger figures, clearer labels, and perhaps a more nuanced representation of the hyperparameter space would enhance their impact.
* **Computational Cost:**  The paper focuses on data efficiency, which is excellent. A brief discussion of the *computational* cost of UNREAL compared to A3C would provide a more complete picture of the method's practicality. Does the increased complexity of the model significantly increase training time?
* **Connection to Intrinsic Motivation:** The use of auxiliary tasks aligns strongly with the field of intrinsic motivation in RL.  Explicitly connecting the work to this broader area could strengthen the paper’s position within the field.

**5. Questions for the Authors:**

* **Regarding Reward Prediction:** You mention skewing the reward prediction samples. What specific method was used for skewing (e.g., equal representation of zero and non-zero rewards)?  Did you experiment with different skewing strategies?
* **Regarding Feature Control:** In section 4.1.1, you state that feature control showed a substantial improvement, but was only tested on one level. Were there any limitations that prevented testing feature control more extensively?
* **Regarding Deconvolutional Networks:**  Why was a deconvolutional network chosen for the pixel control and feature control tasks? Could other methods for generating the auxiliary values (e.g., a simple linear layer) have been used?  What benefit did the deconvolutional approach provide?
* **Scalability:** Given the increased complexity of UNREAL, how do you foresee this architecture scaling to even more complex environments or larger state spaces?  Are there any anticipated bottlenecks that might limit its scalability?
* **Negative Results:** Were there any auxiliary tasks that were explored but ultimately didn’t yield positive results? If so, what were they and why do you believe they were unsuccessful?



Overall, this is a high-quality paper with a significant contribution to the field of reinforcement learning.  Addressing the minor suggestions above would further strengthen the work and enhance its impact.
