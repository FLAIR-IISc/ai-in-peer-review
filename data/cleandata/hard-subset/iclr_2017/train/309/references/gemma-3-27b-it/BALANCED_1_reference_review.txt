This paper proposes UNREAL, a deep reinforcement learning agent that combines A3C with auxiliary control tasks (pixel and feature control) and reward prediction, and experience replay to improve learning efficiency and robustness. The core idea is to leverage multiple auxiliary objectives to learn a richer, more useful representation of the environment, leading to better performance and faster learning. The results on both Labyrinth and Atari demonstrate significant improvements over the A3C baseline, both in terms of final performance and sample efficiency.

**Decision: Reject**

While the paper presents a compelling idea and demonstrates impressive empirical results, I have concerns regarding the clarity of the motivation for *why* these specific auxiliary tasks are chosen and a lack of detailed ablation study exploring the impact of each component, especially regarding hyperparameter sensitivity. The improvements observed are substantial, but the paper doesn’t sufficiently dissect the contributions of each component.

**Supporting Arguments:**

1. **Motivation for Task Selection:** The justification for choosing pixel control and feature control feels somewhat ad-hoc. While the authors state the rationale—pixel changes indicate events and network features capture task-relevant information—the paper doesn’t provide a strong theoretical argument or exploration of *why* these choices are particularly well-suited for the environments tested. A deeper dive into the information content of these signals would strengthen the contribution. Are there other auxiliary tasks that might be equally or more beneficial?

2. **Ablation Study Detail & Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The ablation study presented is not detailed enough. While the paper shows that adding auxiliary tasks improves performance, it doesn’t sufficiently isolate the impact of each task, particularly when combined. The effect of different weighting parameters (λVR, λPC, λRP) isn’t thoroughly investigated. Furthermore, there’s a lack of discussion around the hyperparameter tuning process for each component. How sensitive is the performance to these weights? Were extensive sweeps performed? The robustness analysis, while present, doesn’t delve deep enough into the interplay between the hyperparameters of different tasks.

3. **Limited Novelty of Individual Components:** While the *combination* of these techniques is novel, many of the individual components (auxiliary tasks, replay buffers, A3C) are well-established in the RL literature. The paper needs to more clearly articulate the novel contribution beyond simply combining existing methods. The improvement in robustness is a notable contribution, but needs stronger justification as to why this specific combination achieves it.



**Additional Feedback (for improvement):**

*   **Theoretical Analysis:** Consider adding a brief theoretical discussion regarding the benefits of learning diverse representations. Could information-theoretic concepts be used to justify the choice of auxiliary tasks?
*   **Visualizations:**  More visualizations of the learned representations, particularly the auxiliary task outputs (e.g., heatmaps showing where the agent focuses when performing pixel control), could provide valuable insights.
*   **Computational Cost:** A discussion of the added computational cost associated with the auxiliary tasks would be helpful. Does UNREAL require significantly more resources than A3C?
*   **Code Availability:** Releasing the code would significantly increase the impact of the paper and allow for further scrutiny and replication of the results.
* **Clarify Reward Prediction:** The reward prediction task is described as being skewed. What specific sampling strategy is used, and how does this affect the learned representations? Is there a risk of overfitting to the skewed distribution?
* **Experiment Details:** Please provide further details about the hyperparameter tuning process. What ranges were explored for each parameter? What criteria were used to select the best hyperparameters?

**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  Could you elaborate on the criteria used to select pixel control and feature control as auxiliary tasks? What other potential auxiliary tasks were considered, and why were they rejected?
2.  Could you provide a more detailed ablation study, systematically evaluating the impact of each auxiliary task and different weighting schemes? Specifically, how does performance change with varying values of λVR, λPC, and λRP?
3.  How sensitive is the performance of UNREAL to the choice of hyperparameters for each auxiliary task? Can you provide a table or figure summarizing the results of a hyperparameter sweep?
4.  Could you quantify the additional computational cost associated with the auxiliary tasks? How does the training time of UNREAL compare to A3C?
5.   Can you elaborate on the sampling strategy used for the reward prediction task and how you ensured it avoids overfitting to the skewed distribution?
