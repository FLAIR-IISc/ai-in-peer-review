- Proposes UNREAL: A3C augmented with auxiliary control and reward-prediction tasks to improve representation learning, speed, and robustness.  
- Introduces pixel control and feature control auxiliary tasks plus a reward prediction objective, jointly optimized with the RL loss.  
- Extensive experiments on Labyrinth and Atari show UNREAL substantially outperforms A3C, achieving state-of-the-art results and ~10× data-efficiency gains.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Strong empirical evidence: consistent improvements across diverse environments, including human-level performance on Labyrinth.  
- Well-motivated approach that situates auxiliary tasks within existing work on temporal abstraction and representation learning.  
- Rigorous evaluation with ablation studies, baseline comparisons, and hyperparameter robustness analysis.  
- Clear presentation and helpful architecture diagrams.  
- Request for more details on the reward-prediction architecture and design rationale.  
- Ask for a more granular hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (e.g., λVR, λPC, λRP) and improved visualizations.  
- Suggest explicitly connecting auxiliary tasks to intrinsic motivation and curiosity-driven exploration literature.  
- Recommend discussing the computational cost and trade-offs of adding auxiliary tasks.  
- Question about impact of skewing reward-prediction samples and whether alternative skewing strategies were tested.  
- Question about the choice of the deconvolutional network for pixel control and whether simpler alternatives were considered.  
- Ask whether UNREAL generalizes to other RL environments beyond Labyrinth and Atari, and anticipated adaptation challenges.  
- Request elaboration on future work: combining feature control with other auxiliary tasks and exploring additional auxiliary objectives.  
- Overall view: significant contribution that would benefit from minor clarifications and additional analyses.
