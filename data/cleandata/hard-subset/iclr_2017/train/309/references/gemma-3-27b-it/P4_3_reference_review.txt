This paper introduces UNREAL, a reinforcement learning agent that combines A3C with auxiliary control and reward prediction tasks to improve data efficiency and robustness. The core idea is to leverage auxiliary tasks – pixel control, feature control, and reward prediction – to learn a more effective representation of the environment, leading to faster learning and improved performance in both 3D (Labyrinth) and 2D (Atari) environments. The paper is well-structured and provides a clear explanation of the proposed approach and experimental setup.

**Reject**

The primary reason for rejection is a lack of sufficient detail regarding the hyperparameter search and ablation studies, hindering confidence in the reported gains. While the paper presents improvements over A3C, the extent to which these improvements are robust to different hyperparameter settings, and the specific contribution of each auxiliary task beyond the basic setup, remains unclear.

The paper claims significant performance improvements, particularly in Labyrinth, but the justification for the chosen hyperparameters, and the sensitivity of the results to these choices, isn’t fully explored. The text mentions sampling hyperparameters, but lacks details on the range and method used. The ablation studies, while present, aren't thoroughly analyzed. It’s not entirely clear *why* certain combinations of auxiliary tasks perform better than others. For example, the observation that feature control performed comparably to pixel control is mentioned but not deeply investigated.

The reward prediction task, while intuitively appealing, is described somewhat vaguely. The paper states sampling is skewed towards rewarding events to 50/50 representation, but doesn't detail the criteria or mechanism for identifying rewarding events. How does this skewing affect performance? Further, the choice of a feedforward network for reward prediction, separate from the LSTM used for the main policy, isn’t fully justified.

**Supporting Arguments:**

* **Novelty:** The combination of auxiliary tasks, specifically the pixel and feature control tasks alongside reward prediction, and their integration within an A3C framework, demonstrates a degree of novelty. However, the individual components are not entirely new; the paper builds on existing work in auxiliary task learning and representation learning.
* **Technical Soundness:** The technical details of the A3C implementation and the auxiliary task definitions appear sound. The use of Q-learning for auxiliary control tasks and the proposed loss function for reward prediction are reasonable.
* **Results:** The empirical results presented in both Labyrinth and Atari are promising. The reported speedup in learning and improved performance compared to A3C are significant. However, the lack of robust hyperparameter analysis weakens the validity of these results.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to understand, with a clear explanation of the key concepts and algorithms.

**Additional Feedback:**

* **Hyperparameter Details:** Provide a comprehensive table outlining the hyperparameter ranges explored for each experiment (Labyrinth and Atari). Explain the search method used (e.g., random search, grid search, Bayesian optimization). Include standard deviation/error bars on the reported performance to demonstrate the robustness of the results.
* **Ablation Study Analysis:**  More thoroughly analyze the contribution of each auxiliary task. For example, quantify the improvement gained by adding each task incrementally. Include error bars on the ablation results.
* **Reward Prediction Clarification:** Detail the exact criteria used to identify "rewarding events" for skewed sampling in reward prediction. Explain the rationale behind using a separate feedforward network for reward prediction instead of integrating it within the LSTM.
* **Figure Clarity:** The figures presenting the results (especially Figure 3) are visually busy. Consider simplifying the presentation to improve readability.
* **Replay Buffer Details**: Provide details on the size of the replay buffer and the replay strategy. Is the replay buffer shared across all tasks?
* **Statistical Significance:** Include statistical significance tests to validate the performance differences between UNREAL and A3C.

**Questions for Authors:**

1.  What specific criteria were used to define a “rewarding event” for the skewed sampling in the reward prediction task?
2.  Why was a feedforward network chosen for the reward prediction task instead of leveraging the LSTM used for the main policy?
3.  Can you provide a detailed breakdown of the hyperparameter search space and the method used to select the optimal hyperparameters for each experiment?
4.  How sensitive are the results to the weighting terms (λVR, λPC, λRP) in the combined loss function? Were these weights also subject to a hyperparameter search?
5.  Could you provide more insight into why feature control auxiliary task appeared to perform almost as well as pixel control?
