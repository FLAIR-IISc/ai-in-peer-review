- Proposal: Introduces UNREAL, augmenting A3C with auxiliary tasks (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction) to learn richer representations.
- Main idea: Encourage active control of environment aspects and predict immediate rewards to improve representation learning in sparse-reward settings.
- Decision: Accept.
- Strong empirical results: Substantial improvements in sample efficiency and final performance across Labyrinth and Atari compared to A3C and ablations.
- Well-motivated: Clear rationale for auxiliary tasks and their choice (pixel, feature, reward) to address representation learning needs.
- Ablation studies: Show each auxiliary task’s contribution and demonstrate that the combination is synergistic beyond single tasks.
- Comparison to baselines: Outperforms unsupervised baselines like pixel reconstruction and input change prediction.
- Robustness and reproducibility: Demonstrates hyperparameter robustness, provides detailed experiments, code, and video.
- Simplicity and practicality: Builds on A3C and is relatively straightforward to implement.
- Request for deeper analysis: Recommend more granular investigation of how each auxiliary task shapes learned representations (e.g., feature visualizations, gradient analysis).
- Computational cost: Ask for quantification of the overhead introduced by auxiliary tasks versus sample-efficiency gains.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Suggest broader exploration of hyperparameter ranges to fully characterize robustness.
- Theoretical framing: Recommend explicitly connecting auxiliary tasks to intrinsic motivation theories (prediction error minimization, empowerment).
- Questions for authors: Which hidden layer was used for feature control and were alternatives tested?
- Questions for authors: Rationale and alternatives for skewed sampling in reward prediction; scalability of pixel control with image resolution and grid size; and request for error analysis on failure cases.
