- Paper proposes UNREAL: augment A3C with auxiliary tasks (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction) using off-policy replay to improve learning speed, data efficiency, and robustness.
- Demonstrated large performance gains on Labyrinth (over 2x vs A3C) and significant improvements across Atari suite.
- Core idea: combine off-policy learning for auxiliary tasks with on-policy main task and use experience replay to balance data distribution.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Strong empirical results with convincing ablation studies showing each auxiliary task’s contribution.
- Well-motivated rationale linking auxiliary tasks to improved representation learning and careful handling of reward prediction to avoid policy bias.
- Paper is clearly written with helpful figures/tables and clear description of architecture and training.
- Robustness analysis shows reduced sensitivity to hyperparameters compared to baselines.
- Thorough related work section that situates the contribution within RL and representation learning.
- Suggest discussing computational cost/training time of auxiliary tasks relative to A3C.
- Recommend visualizing and analyzing learned features (e.g., pixel control patterns) to provide deeper insight.
- Request more detailed hyperparameter sensitivity analysis for each auxiliary task and their key hyperparameters.
- Suggest deeper ablation studies exploring combinations of auxiliary tasks (e.g., pixel control + reward prediction).
- Questions: sensitivity to replay skewing factor for reward prediction and alternative skewing strategies.
- Questions: rationale for the chosen deconvolutional architecture for pixel control and whether alternatives were tried.
- Questions: which Atari games showed particularly better or worse performance and possible reasons.
- Questions: potential synergies and challenges in combining feature control with other tasks for future work.
