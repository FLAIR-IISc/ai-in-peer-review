This paper proposes the UNREAL agent, augmenting an A3C agent with auxiliary control and reward prediction tasks to improve data efficiency and robustness in reinforcement learning. The authors demonstrate significant performance gains on both the Labyrinth 3D environment and a suite of Atari games, attributing these gains to improved feature learning and faster adaptation.

**Reject.** While the empirical results are promising, the sheer number of minor methodological inconsistencies, formatting issues, and overly verbose sections detract from the overall clarity and rigor of the work. The paper feels rushed, and a more thorough revision is necessary before acceptance.

The primary concern lies in the lack of meticulousness in presentation. For example, the description of the deconvolutional network used for pixel control (Section 3.1) is convoluted and lacks clarity. The repeated use of "e.g." without a complete example is distracting (e.g., "e.g. Lin & Mitchell (1992); Li et al. (2015)"). The paper suffers from a repetitive writing style, reiterating the same points across different sections. The experimental details in Appendix B, while present, are overwhelming and lack crucial information, such as specific hyperparameter ranges used for each auxiliary task. The appendix also contains multiple figures that are not referred to in the main text (e.g. Figure 7, Figure 8).

Furthermore, while the authors claim increased robustness to hyperparameters, the presentation of these results (Figures 7 and 8 in the appendix) is insufficient. A simple scatter plot lacks the statistical rigor needed to support such a claim. A proper sensitivity analysis is required. The choice of hyperparameters for the reward prediction task seems arbitrary.  The justification for the skewing of the replay buffer towards rewarding events is weak. While intuitively plausible, it lacks a more formal theoretical grounding or ablative analysis demonstrating its necessity. The comparison to baselines in Section 4.1 (pixel reconstruction and input change prediction) feels superficial and lacks a comprehensive hyperparameter search for each baseline to ensure a fair comparison. The statement about why input reconstruction "hurts final performance" is speculative and not convincingly supported by the presented data. The sentence structure in many places is awkward and could benefit from rewriting (e.g., "The input to the agent at each timestep was an 84× 84 RGB image.").

The introduction of equation (1) feels abrupt and lacks sufficient explanation of the motivations behind combining auxiliary tasks in this manner. The definition of st (state) as "st = f(o1, r1, a1, ..., ot, rt)" is overly general and provides little insight into the specific state representation used in the experiments.  The formatting is inconsistent throughout - some citations are bolded, others are not.

**Additional Feedback (for improvement):**

*   **Clarity and Conciseness:** Streamline the writing style and remove redundant information.
*   **Methodological Rigor:** Provide a detailed and clear explanation of all architectural components, particularly the deconvolutional network. Include pseudocode if necessary.
*   **Hyperparameter Analysis:** Perform a thorough hyperparameter search and present the results in a statistically meaningful way.
*   **Ablation Studies:** Conduct more comprehensive ablation studies to isolate the impact of each auxiliary task.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** Provide a stronger theoretical justification for the design choices, such as the skewing of the replay buffer.
*   **Formatting and Proofreading:** Carefully proofread the paper for grammatical errors, inconsistencies in formatting, and awkward phrasing. Ensure all figures are referenced in the main text.
*   **Appendix Clarity:**  Reorganize and clarify the Appendix, removing extraneous content and providing a more concise and informative overview of the experimental setup.

**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the architecture and hyperparameters used for the reward prediction task?
2.  What is the rationale behind choosing a 50/50 split for the reward prediction sampling? Was any other ratio considered?
3.  Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the performance of the baselines (pixel reconstruction and input change prediction) to ensure a fair comparison? Were the hyperparameters tuned for these baselines?
4.  Could you elaborate on the theoretical basis for the observed improvement in robustness to hyperparameters?
5.  Why are certain citations bolded and others not? Is there a specific reason for this formatting choice?
6.  What were the computational resources required to train the UNREAL agent, and how does this compare to the baseline A3C agent?
