This paper tackles the problem of improving sample efficiency and robustness in deep reinforcement learning, particularly in complex 3D environments like Labyrinth and Atari. The authors propose UNREAL, an agent that combines A3C with auxiliary control tasks (pixel and feature control) and reward prediction. The core idea is to learn useful representations by incentivizing the agent to control various aspects of its environment and predict immediate rewards, thereby accelerating learning and making the agent less sensitive to hyperparameter choices.

I believe this is a strong paper with a clear contribution. The experimental results, particularly on Labyrinth, are compelling and demonstrate a significant improvement over the A3C baseline and ablated versions. The authors provide thorough ablation studies and a convincing argument for the benefits of their approach. The combination of auxiliary tasks and the careful design of the auxiliary losses are well-motivated and justified.

My decision is to **accept** the paper. The primary reason is the significant and well-supported performance improvements demonstrated in the experiments, especially in the challenging Labyrinth environment. The consistent gains across different ablation studies strengthen the claim that the proposed approach is indeed effective. A secondary reason is the clear and thorough presentation of the methodology and experimental setup.

The paper’s strengths lie in its robust experimental validation. The improvements demonstrated on both Labyrinth and Atari, coupled with the ablation studies, provide strong evidence that the proposed auxiliary tasks are beneficial. The analysis of robustness to hyperparameters is also a valuable contribution, highlighting a key limitation of many RL algorithms. The authors also clearly delineate the core ideas and provide appropriate context by referencing relevant prior work. 

However, there are a few points that, if addressed, could further strengthen the paper:

* **Reward Prediction Loss Details:** While the reward prediction task is well-motivated, the details regarding the choice of the multiclass cross-entropy loss and the 0.5 probability threshold for balancing zero and non-zero rewards could be elaborated upon. Why was this specific threshold chosen, and were other thresholds explored? How sensitive is performance to this value?
* **Feature Control Selection:** The choice of which hidden layer to use for feature control is not fully explained. A brief justification for this choice, or an exploration of different layer options, would be helpful.
* **Computational Cost:** While the paper emphasizes data efficiency gains, the added computational cost of training the auxiliary tasks is not explicitly discussed. Providing a comparison of training time for UNREAL versus A3C would provide a more complete picture of the overall benefits.
* **Generalization to Other Environments:** Beyond Labyrinth and Atari, exploring the performance of UNREAL in a wider range of environments could strengthen the generalizability claims.
* **Visualizations of Learned Features:** Although mentioned, further visualizations demonstrating *what* the agent learns to control via the auxiliary tasks (beyond the examples in the supplementary video) would be insightful. For example, showing the activation maps corresponding to the learned feature control tasks or visualizing the pixel changes being maximized.



Here are some specific questions for the authors:

*  Regarding the reward prediction task: Was a different loss function considered, like MSE, and if so, what were the results? What is the rationale behind the specific probability threshold of 0.5 for balancing zero and non-zero rewards?
*  For the feature control task: How was the specific hidden layer chosen for control? Was any analysis performed on the information content of different layers?
*  Could you provide a more detailed breakdown of the computational cost associated with training the auxiliary tasks? Specifically, how does the training time of UNREAL compare to A3C?
*  The paper mentions that pixel control is better than simply predicting immediate pixel changes, but how does that compare to other types of prediction of environmental changes/state such as predicting the next state.
* What is the role of the LSTM in learning a good representation for feature control and reward prediction?



Overall, this is a valuable contribution to the field of reinforcement learning. The proposed UNREAL agent demonstrates significant improvements in sample efficiency and robustness, and the paper is well-written and clearly presented. Addressing the suggested points would further enhance the paper’s impact and clarity.
