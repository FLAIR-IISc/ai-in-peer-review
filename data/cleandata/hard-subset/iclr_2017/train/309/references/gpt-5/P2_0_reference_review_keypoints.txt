- Proposal: UNREAL augments A3C with off-policy auxiliary tasks from a short replay buffer to shape representations and speed learning (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction with class-rebalance, value function replay).
- Architecture: Auxiliary control implemented via a deconvolutional head producing spatial Q-values; a single combined loss optimizes on-policy A3C plus off-policy auxiliary losses.
- Empirical claim: Substantial gains in sample efficiency and final performance on 13 Labyrinth levels and 57 Atari games, with improved hyperparameter robustness.
- Ablations: Control-based auxiliary tasks outperform change-prediction and reconstruction; combining auxiliary tasks yields additional improvements.
- Decision: Accept — strong empirical evidence, simple and well-motivated method, novel combination (off-policy pseudo-control for representation + rebalanced reward prediction).
- Motivation: Addresses slow/brittle learning and poor representations in sparse-reward, visually rich RL by densifying signals and exploiting replay for feature learning.
- Literature positioning: Well-situated relative to Horde, UVFA, successor representations, and model learning; novelty is using auxiliary tasks solely for representation, not inference-time control.
- Technical soundness: Combined loss, off-policy Q-learning for auxiliary control, and class-rebalanced reward prediction are appropriate and detailed; sampling and update cadence specified.
- Empirical rigor — breadth: Extensive evaluation across diverse Labyrinth levels and a large Atari suite supports robustness claims.
- Empirical rigor — strength: Reported large improvements in data efficiency (up to ~10x) and human-normalized scores (e.g., Labyrinth ~54%→~87%, Atari mean/median large gains) plus hyperparameter robustness tests.
- Reproducibility: Many implementation and training details provided (network, losses, replay size/frequency, task weights).
- Caveat: Relative contributions of value replay vs. control vs. reward prediction are not fully dissected across the entire task suite.
- Caveat: Atari comparison fairness (training budget and evaluation protocol) needs clearer documentation.
- Caveat: No formal analysis of off-policy bias or gradient interference, though empirical stability is shown.
- Suggestion: Provide fuller ablations isolating LVR, LPC, and LRP with matched update budgets across Labyrinth and Atari.
- Suggestion: Clarify Atari evaluation protocol (frames, clipping, life termination, sticky actions, evaluation episodes) and budget parity with baselines.
- Suggestion: Report compute and wall-clock training overhead from auxiliary heads and replay to contextualize data-efficiency gains.
- Suggestion: Analyze sensitivity to replay buffer size and auxiliary update ratios (include sizes beyond 2k).
- Suggestion: Expand feature control experiments beyond one level and test combinations of pixel and feature control.
- Suggestion: Provide statistical confidence intervals or significance tests over seeds for key benchmarks.
- Suggestion: Measure representational changes (e.g., linear probes, gradient similarity) to support the representation-learning claim.
- Suggestion: Discuss potential failure modes (e.g., low-visual-change environments) and how λPC is tuned relative to pixel-change statistics.
- Questions: Quantify separate effects of LVR, LPC, and LRP on speed and asymptotic performance across more tasks.
- Questions: Provide exact Atari evaluation protocol and confirm training budget matching to A3C and DQN baselines.
- Questions: Do you apply off-policy corrections for value replay or auxiliary control, and was any bias/instability observed?
- Questions: How sensitive are results to replay buffer size and auxiliary update frequency, and is there a trade-off with on-policy stability?
- Questions: How were loss weights (e.g., λPC) selected and are there heuristics making UNREAL “set-and-forget” across environments?
- Questions: For feature control, which layers/units were most beneficial and does depth of controlled features affect outcomes?
- Questions: In games with minimal pixel change, does pixel control help or hurt, and can the agent down-weight unhelpful auxiliary tasks automatically?
- Overall verdict: Well-motivated, clearly presented, and empirically strong paper likely to impact training of data-efficient, robust deep RL agents; recommend acceptance.
