- Proposes UNREAL: A3C augmented with auxiliary control tasks (pixel/feature control), reward-prediction with skewed replay, and value replay, combined in a unified loss.  
- Auxiliary control: off-policy n-step Q-learning on pseudo-rewards for pixel-change and feature activations, sharing CNN/LSTM representations with policy.  
- Reward prediction: skewed-replay classifier oversampling rewarding events to combat perceptual sparsity without biasing policy/value updates.  
- Value replay: off-policy value regression from a short replay buffer to accelerate and stabilize learning.  
- Empirical results: large gains on 13 DeepMind Lab levels (mean/median human-normalized scores and ~10× sample-efficiency improvement) and improved performance/robustness on 57 Atari games.  
- Ablations: cumulative benefits from auxiliary tasks; pixel control outperforms reconstruction/change prediction; feature control shows preliminary promise.  
- Decision: Accept — justified by strong empirical gains, modular and well-motivated integration of auxiliary tasks, and thorough ablations.  
- Significance: Addresses sparse rewards and partial observability by leveraging naturally available auxiliary signals to improve representations.  
- Literature relation: Well-situated relative to options/Horde/UVFAs/successor representations; novelty in using pseudo-reward policies to shape representations without executing them.  
- Experimental rigor: Diverse tasks, hyperparameter robustness checks, and focused ablations supporting the main claims.  
- Practicality: Auxiliary heads and replay are simple to implement and compatible with standard CNN-LSTM RL architectures.  
- Request to report wall-clock and parameter-update counts to clarify compute fairness given extra replay updates.  
- Ask for discussion/mitigations of on-policy/off-policy mixing and details on target network/update schedules for replayed losses.  
- Concern about reward-skewed replay potentially affecting shared features — request ablation showing no bias to on-policy estimates.  
- Ask to justify ReLU on Qaux outputs and the role/necessity of dueling parameterization in auxiliary heads.
