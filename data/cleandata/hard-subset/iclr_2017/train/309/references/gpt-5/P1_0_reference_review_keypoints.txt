- Proposal: UNREAL augments an on-policy A3C CNN-LSTM agent with off-policy auxiliary tasks from a small replay buffer to improve representation, data efficiency, and robustness.
- Auxiliary tasks: pixel-control (per-cell deconv Q-learning), feature-control (maximize internal activations), reward prediction with rebalanced replay, and value-function replay.
- Training: Joint loss combining on-policy A3C and off-policy auxiliary losses; short prioritized replay buffer for rare rewards.
- Empirical results: Substantial gains over A3C on 13 Labyrinth levels and 57 Atari games in final score, learning speed, and hyperparameter robustness.
- Comparison: UNREAL also surpasses Prioritized Dueling DQN on Atari in reported metrics.
- Ablations: Control-based auxiliary tasks outperform prediction/reconstruction; pixel control > immediate pixel-change prediction > reconstruction.
- Practicality: Approach is simple, general, and compatible with existing architectures and computational budgets.
- Decision: Reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Main reasons for accept: strong empirical improvements across domains and a well-motivated, novel integration of off-policy auxiliary control with on-policy recurrence supported by ablations.
- Motivation: Tackles representation learning in visually rich, partially observed, sparse-reward settings by aligning auxiliaries with controllable and rewarding aspects.
- Methodological strengths: Sound separation of on-policy and off-policy losses, effective deconv heads for spatial control, and efficient small replay/prioritization.
- Empirical rigor: Evaluated across many seeds/hyperparameters, reports mean/median, and includes ablations and implementation details.
- Weakness — baselines/fairness: Comparisons to DQN use published numbers with differing architectures/protocols; compute/evaluation protocol details need clarity.
- Weakness — novelty: Contributions are practical integration of known ideas (Horde, UVFA, intrinsic motivation) rather than new theory.
- Weakness — generality/failure modes: Pixel control might emphasize visual dynamics irrelevant to reward; more analysis needed on representation focus.
- Weakness — statistics: Recommend clearer confidence intervals and statistical reporting on final scores and sample-efficiency metrics.
- Suggested improvement: Provide a consolidated ablation table across Labyrinth for A3C, +VR, +RP, +PC, combinations, and full UNREAL with mean/median and speedups.
- Suggested improvement: Clarify off-policy stability details—use of target networks θ− and update cadence for auxiliary Q-learning.
- Suggested improvement: Report compute details—wall-clock training time, environment frames, and overhead of auxiliary heads.
- Suggested improvement: Analyze representations to show auxiliaries focus on task-relevant dynamics (saliency, texture controls, grayscale/temporal smoothing experiments).
- Suggested improvement: Compare to curiosity/intrinsic motivation baselines and other auxiliary tasks (e.g., depth prediction).
- Suggested improvement: Report 95% confidence intervals for final performance and speedup metrics.
- Suggested improvement: Expand feature-control results and test combinations with pixel control across more tasks.
- Reproducibility: Ask to release code and environment configurations.
- Key questions for authors: Do auxiliary Q-learning heads use target networks and what cadence? How much gain comes from each auxiliary (VR, RP, PC) with a full ablation table? Why choose 2k replay size and sensitivity to it? Did pixel-control ever hurt due to dynamic textures or was normalization/flow tried? Were Atari training frames/action-repeat and wall-clock matched to DQN baselines? Which layers/units used for feature control and does combining them help? Will code/env configs be released?
