- Proposes UNREAL: augment on-policy recurrent A3C with off-policy auxiliary tasks from a short replay buffer (pixel/feature control, reward prediction, value replay) sharing representation but not used for action selection.
- Main empirical claims: substantial gains in data efficiency, final performance, and hyperparameter robustness on 13 Labyrinth levels and 57 Atari games; ablations show each auxiliary helps and full UNREAL is strongest.
- Decision: Accept — due to strong, consistent empirical improvements and a clear, well-motivated approach that advances representation shaping via off-policy control tasks and reward-focused prediction.
- Motivation: Addresses slow/brittle learning in sparse-reward, partially observed environments by learning richer representations via auxiliary control and reward prediction.
- Rationale: Using off-policy Q-learning for many parallel pseudo-rewards from a single behavior stream is well justified.
- Novelty/positioning: Straightforward yet novel combination (A3C + replayed value regression + off-policy Q-learning heads + rebalanced reward prediction) distinct from Horde, UVFA, and prior auxiliary prediction work.
- Architecture: Reasonable architectural choices (deconv heads, dueling parametrization, shared CNN+LSTM) and clear descriptions.
- Labyrinth results: UNREAL improves mean human-normalized score (54%→87%) and median (45%→92%), with ~10x speedups to reach A3C’s best; pixel control is most effective among auxiliaries.
- Atari results: On 57 games UNREAL outperforms A3C and Prioritized Dueling DQN on mean/median human-normalized scores and shows improved hyperparameter robustness.
- Robustness analysis: Broad sweeps over learning rate and entropy show smoother performance surfaces and improved robustness for UNREAL.
- Experimental rigor: Good implementation details and ablations; reconstruction and change-prediction baselines are useful comparators.
- Compute/update fairness concern: UNREAL uses additional off-policy updates; a baseline matching total updates or wall-clock time is needed to isolate auxiliary-objective effects.
- Evaluation reporting concern: Some results use top-k hyperparameter averages; clearer reporting with fixed hyperparameters, multiple seeds, and statistical tests would strengthen claims.
- Reproducibility concern: Labyrinth is proprietary and code release is unclear; human-score normalization details are sparse.
- Auxiliary side-effects concern: Pixel-control could focus on visually salient but reward-irrelevant changes (noisy-TV); more analysis to rule out degenerate behaviors is needed.
- Sensitivity concern: Auxiliary loss weights (e.g., λPC) are tuned per domain; more sensitivity analysis and guidance would help practitioners.
- Suggested additional experiment: Provide a compute-normalized baseline (A3C with matched updates/wall-clock) to quantify task-design vs extra updates.
- Suggested reporting: Report per-task sample efficiency in environment frames and wall-clock with confidence intervals over multiple seeds for fixed hyperparameters.
- Suggested ablations: More granular ablations (value replay only, reward prediction only, pixel control only) across more Labyrinth tasks and combine feature+pixel control.
- Suggested diagnostics: Analyze potential oscillatory behaviors, action entropy, and motion statistics to check for pixel-control degeneracies.
- Clarification requests: Explain human-normalization protocol (number of humans, training time, variance) and evaluation episode counts.
- Generalization suggestion: Test transfer by training on some maze seeds and evaluating on held-out seeds to assess representation benefits.
- Reproducibility suggestion: Release code/environments or detailed configuration files to aid verification.
- Key author questions: Provide compute-fairness comparisons, stability details for off-policy Q-heads (target network cadence), sensitivity sweeps for auxiliary weights, pixel-control behavioral diagnostics, effects of reward-predictor rebalancing in dense-reward games, examples of negative transfer, human-score collection details, and replay buffer sizing/prioritization rationale.
