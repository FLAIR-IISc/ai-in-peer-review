- Proposal: UNREAL augments A3C with auxiliary tasks (pixel control, feature control, reward prediction, value replay) sharing a conv encoder and trained from a small replay buffer alongside on-policy A3C.
- Auxiliary control: off-policy n-step Q-learning on pseudo-rewards (pixel-change per grid cell or network feature activations).
- Reward prediction: immediate reward predictor trained with rebalanced sampling to over-represent rare rewards.
- Value replay: additional value-function regression from recent experience.
- Empirical results: large gains in data efficiency and final performance on 13 Labyrinth tasks and 57 Atari games.
- Quantitative gains: mean human-normalized Labyrinth from 54%→87% (median 45%→92%); up to 10–18× reduction in steps to match A3C; outperforms A3C and Prioritized Dueling DQN on Atari and is more hyperparameter-robust.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Strong, well-motivated idea distinct from prior UVFA/SR/Horde/model-based approaches.
- Key reason 2: Compelling empirical evidence with careful ablations and robustness analyses.
- Problem addressed: slow and brittle representation learning in deep RL under sparse rewards and partial observability.
- Positioning: differentiates from options/pseudo-reward controllers, Horde, UVFA/SR, and model-based representation work; design choices aim to avoid policy bias.
- Breadth: evaluation across varied Labyrinth tasks (fruit collection, navigation, procedurally generated mazes, laser-tag) and 57 Atari games supports generality.
- Strength of gains: consistent, substantial improvements in sample efficiency, final score, and hyperparameter robustness.
- Ablations: control-based auxiliary tasks (pixel control) outperform input-change prediction and reconstruction; feature control is promising.
- Methodological care: reward predictor rebalanced but not used in control; auxiliary Q-learning is off-policy making replay appropriate; value replay leverages recent experience without skew.
- Reproducibility: implementation details (architectures, buffer sizes, update schedules, hyperparameter ranges) are provided.
- Caveat: Atari evaluation protocol (no-op starts, frame skip, termination rules) not fully specified, affecting cross-paper comparisons.
- Caveat: Limited statistical reporting—per-configuration variance or confidence intervals for headline numbers are not provided.
- Caveat: Human baseline details for Labyrinth (participants, instructions, training) are lacking.
- Caveat: Computational cost and wall-clock comparisons versus baselines are not reported.
