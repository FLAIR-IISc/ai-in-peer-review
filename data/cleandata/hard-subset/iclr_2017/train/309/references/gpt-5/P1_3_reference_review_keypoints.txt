- Proposes UNREAL: augment recurrent on-policy A3C with off-policy auxiliary tasks from recent replay to improve representations and data efficiency.  
- Auxiliary tasks: pixel/feature control via Q-learning, short-horizon reward prediction with class-rebalanced replay, and value function replay to stabilize learning.  
- Jointly trains shared CNN+LSTM encoders while keeping main policy/value updates on-policy.  
- Experiments on 13 Labyrinth levels and 57 Atari games show large gains in final performance, sample efficiency, and hyperparameter robustness.  
- Ablations indicate each auxiliary contributes, with pixel control especially effective on Labyrinth.  
- UNREAL outperforms A3C and Prioritized Dueling DQN on Atari aggregate metrics.  
- Clear motivation: addresses sample inefficiency and brittle representation learning under sparse rewards and partial observability.  
- Situates work relative to options, Horde, UVFAs, successor representations, and prior auxiliary prediction approaches.  
- Novelty lies in combining off-policy control-based auxiliaries, reward-focused prediction, and value replay with recurrent A3C.  
- Empirical evidence is broad and largely supports claims, including per-level breakdowns and robustness plots.  
- Missing statistical reporting: confidence intervals and significance tests are largely absent.  
- Some evaluation protocol details for Atari are unspecified, affecting comparability.  
- Computational overhead (extra heads, replay, deconv) and wall-clock training cost are not quantified.  
- Feature control results are preliminary and less conclusive than pixel control and reward prediction.  
- Off-policy auxiliary Q-learning without importance sampling is pragmatically acceptable but can bias shared features.  
- Reward-prediction with class-rebalanced replay shapes features without biasing policy gradients—reasonable design.  
- Architecture and loss composition are well described and reproducible with provided hyperparameters and replay details.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify evaluation protocols, report medians and CIs, avoid “top-3” hyperparameter selection bias, and quantify overhead.  
- Recommend deeper Atari ablations, analysis of when pixel control helps/hurts, and exploration of generalization/transfer.  
- Discuss limitations: environments with low pixel change, conflicts between off-policy auxiliaries and on-policy gradients, and sensitivity to auxiliary weights and buffer size.  
- Questions for authors: exact Atari protocol, hyperparameter selection (λs), robustness statistics, wall-clock overhead, pixel vs feature control tradeoffs, buffer size choice, and any off-policy destabilization observed.
