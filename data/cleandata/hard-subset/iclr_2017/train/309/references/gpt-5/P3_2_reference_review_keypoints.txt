- Proposes UNREAL: augment A3C with three auxiliary objectives (pixel/feature-control via n-step Q-learning, skewed immediate reward prediction, and value replay) sharing a common conv encoder and LSTM.  
- Claims auxiliary tasks shape representations to improve data efficiency, robustness, and final performance.  
- Empirical result: UNREAL substantially outperforms A3C on 13 Labyrinth tasks (mean human-normalized 54%→87%, ~10× fewer steps to reach A3C’s best).  
- Empirical result: UNREAL improves over A3C and Prioritized Dueling DQN on 57 Atari games.  
- Ablations indicate pixel/feature control and reward prediction each contribute; reconstruction or simple change prediction underperform control.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: Novel, well-motivated integration of off-policy auxiliary control and reward-focused auxiliary prediction into an on-policy recurrent actor-critic with strong empirical gains.  
- Key reason: Demonstrated improvements in data efficiency and robustness with ablations and multiple seeds/hyperparameters.  
- Problem framing: Auxiliary tasks densify sparse/partial-observability signals for representation learning without biasing policy when used only for representation.  
- Positioning: Clearly relates to options, Horde, UVFAs, successor representations, and model-based/auxiliary prediction; emphasizes auxiliary policies are learning targets, not executed.  
- Methodology: Clear formulation of pixel/feature control (cell-wise pixel-change rewards, deconv heads), skewed reward prediction, and value replay; architecture and hyperparameters specified.  
- Empirical support: Labyrinth is a challenging POMDP benchmark; reported 10× speed-up and robustness improvements are meaningful.  
- Rigor: Use of many random hyperparameter settings and seeds and robustness plots helps address variance/instability concerns.  
- Concern: Fairness of comparisons—A3C baseline lacks replay; need A3C+value-replay and A3C+reward-prediction baselines to isolate effects.  
- Concern: Ablation coverage incomplete—feature control only preliminary on subset of Labyrinth and omitted on Atari; full factorial ablations (PC, FC, RP, VR) needed.  
- Concern: Combining on-policy actor-critic with off-policy Q-learning may introduce gradient interference and instability; lacks analysis of gradient scales, loss normalization, and stability metrics.  
- Concern: Skewed reward prediction’s oversampling of rewarding transitions may bias features and hurt dense-reward tasks; no sensitivity analysis reported.  
- Concern: Missing Atari protocol details (training frames, no-op starts, sticky actions, evaluation episodes), affecting reproducibility and fairness vs prior work.  
- Concern: Labyrinth environment availability is unclear; reproducibility would benefit from releasing environments and code.  
- Additional feedback: Include A3C+replay baseline and a full component ablation on a representative Atari subset.  
- Additional feedback: Report training frames, wall-clock time, and exact Atari evaluation settings; align to ALE best practices.  
- Additional feedback: Provide sensitivity analyses for λPC, λVR, λRP and auxiliary update frequency/ratio and whether auxiliary losses are normalized.  
- Additional feedback: Analyze representation changes (e.g., saliency, value-approximation error) to show how auxiliaries help.  
- Additional feedback: Consider off-policy corrections (Retrace) or target networks for auxiliary Q-learning to improve stability.  
- Additional feedback: Investigate generalization/transfer of auxiliary-control representations across tasks and consider parameterized pseudo-reward embeddings.  
- Question: How much gain comes from value replay alone—can you report A3C+VR and A3C+RP (no PC/FC) baselines on Labyrinth and Atari?  
- Question: What are the exact Atari evaluation protocols and training budgets compared to Prioritized Dueling DQN?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to λPC/λVR/λRP and auxiliary update frequency; are auxiliary losses normalized?  
- Question: Did you observe instability from off-policy Q-learning heads and what mitigations (target networks, gradient clipping) were used?  
- Question: Does skewed reward prediction degrade performance on dense-reward games; was adaptive skewing tried?  
- Question: How robust is pixel control to grid resolution/crop choices and to environments with high exogenous flicker/dynamic textures?  
- Question: Will you release Labyrinth levels and code to facilitate reproducibility?
