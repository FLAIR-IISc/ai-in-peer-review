- Proposal: UNREAL augments A3C with off-policy auxiliary tasks (pixel/feature control, reward prediction, value replay) and deconvolutional spatial heads to improve representations, data efficiency, and robustness.
- Core contributions: (i) auxiliary control tasks optimized via n-step Q-learning on replay, (ii) reward-prediction trained on replay skewed toward rewarding events, (iii) value-function replay to stabilize value estimates.
- Empirical claim: Large gains on 13 Labyrinth levels (≈2× human-normalized over A3C; ~10× fewer steps to match A3C) and SOTA across 57 Atari games, with ablations.
- Decision: Accept — motivated by strong, consistent empirical improvements and principled integration of auxiliary tasks into A3C.
- Motivation strength: Directly targets sparse rewards and partial observability by shaping shared representations via auxiliary tasks rather than using them for control/transfer.
- Novelty: Control-based auxiliary tasks with off-policy Q-learning distinguish this work from prior prediction/model-based and option/Horde literature.
- Methodological soundness: Clear loss decomposition combining on-policy A3C and off-policy Q-learning, with sensible use of replay and spatially grounded control heads.
- Reward-skewed replay rationale: Densifies reward-relevant representation learning while keeping main policy/value updates on-policy to avoid bias.
- Empirical robustness: Results include per-component ablations, hyperparameter sweeps, and multiple seeds, improving confidence in findings.
- Reporting concerns: Atari evaluation details (sticky actions, life handling, eval protocol, frame budgets) need clarification for fair cross-paper comparison.
- Selection bias risk: Some plots emphasize “top-3” agents; fixed-budget comparisons and confidence intervals over all runs would strengthen claims.
- Reproducibility issue: Labyrinth is in-house; public availability or cross-benchmark comparisons would aid reproducibility.
- Feature control limitation: Feature-control experiments are preliminary; need fuller study to determine when pixel vs feature control is preferable or synergistic.
- Off-policy RNN concern: More detail required on reconstructing LSTM hidden states for replayed sequences to assess stability/correctness of off-policy Q-learning.
- Suggested methodological clarifications: Report LSTM replay handling, replay ratios, target networks, and sensitivity analyses for λPC/λVR/λRP and buffer size.
- Suggested analyses/ablations: Test reward-skewing effects in dense-reward games, visualize learned control behaviors, ablate the deconv spatial head, and evaluate combinations of auxiliaries.
- Reproducibility requests: Provide pseudo-code, recommended hyperparameters, and preferably open-source code or training logs.
- Questions for authors: Clarify LSTM state reconstruction for replay, exact training frame budgets and frames-to-threshold, Atari eval protocol specifics, λ selection and sensitivity, effects of reward-skewing, stability measures for auxiliary Q-learning, pixel vs feature control trade-offs, and wall-clock overhead/speedup.
