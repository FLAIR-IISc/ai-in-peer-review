- Proposes UNREAL: augmenting A3C with auxiliary tasks to improve representation learning, data efficiency, and robustness.  
- Auxiliary components: off-policy n-step Q-learning on pseudo-rewards (pixel control and feature control).  
- Auxiliary components: rebalanced replay-based reward prediction to address perceptual reward sparsity.  
- Auxiliary components: value-function replay to further shape the critic.  
- Architecture: shared CNN-LSTM backbone with deconvolutional heads for spatial auxiliary Q-values.  
- Training: unified loss combining on-policy A3C and off-policy auxiliary updates with a small, recent replay buffer.  
- Empirical: substantially outperforms A3C on 13 Labyrinth levels with up to ~10x data-efficiency gains.  
- Empirical: improves mean/median performance and hyperparameter robustness on 57 Atari games versus A3C and Prioritized Dueling DQN.  
- Ablations: compares reconstruction vs change prediction vs control and shows control is key; feature-control is a competitive alternative.  
- Robustness: thorough analyses across many hyperparameters and seeds; replay prioritization limited to reward prediction to reduce bias.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Primary reason 1: Simple, well-motivated auxiliaries that significantly improve sample efficiency and robustness with careful ablations.  
- Primary reason 2: Clear, scalable recipe blending on-policy actor-critic with off-policy auxiliary updates with likely broad impact.  
- Novelty: Distinct use of many off-policy control heads as auxiliary objectives for representation learning, not hierarchical control.  
- Novelty: Reward-prediction with rebalanced replay to break sparsity without biasing policy/critic targets.  
- Novelty: Integration of auxiliaries with a recurrent on-policy actor-critic in a practical framework.  
- Pixel control: simple, effective intrinsic control objective with spatial dueling/deconv head leveraging visual structure.  
- Methodological strength: clear separation of on-policy and off-policy updates and a well-described combined loss/training protocol.  
- Methodological strength: careful ablations and sensitivity analyses, including replay/prioritization choices.  
- Empirical support: Labyrinth results show consistent gains across diverse tasks (navigation, mazes, laser tag) with per-level breakdowns.  
- Empirical support: Atari results show improved human-normalized scores and robustness; reward-prediction and value replay help sparse-reward learning.  
- Concern: Atari baseline comparability — A3C uses LSTM and RGB here, differing from common feedforward grayscale baselines; clarify evaluation and include non-LSTM A3C.  
- Concern: Value-replay off-policy bias — no importance sampling used; discuss potential bias and report ablation showing its incremental benefit.  
- Concern: Auxiliary non-stationarity — feature-control pseudo-rewards depend on changing hidden activations; clarify stability measures and target network usage.  
- Concern: Pixel-control design — may emphasize camera motion/texture flicker; analyze sensitivity to ego-motion vs object motion and normalize choices.  
- Concern: Compute vs data-efficiency — report wall-clock time and gradient-update-to-environment-step ratio to separate compute from data-efficiency.  
- Concern: Reproducibility — Labyrinth is internal; release configs/code/seeds/checkpoints and per-game Atari scores with confidence intervals and hyperparameter grid.  
- Questions: details on n-step return computation for value replay, bootstrap/target network use, and importance sampling.  
- Questions: auxiliary Q-learning stability — target update schedule, sensitivity to target frequency and replay buffer size, any divergence.  
- Questions: pixel-control variants — optical flow, motion-normalized rewards, grid-size sensitivity.  
- Questions: reward-prediction leakage — did rebalancing bias shared value estimates; any experiments with separate encoders.  
- Questions: compute overhead — wall-clock and FLOP comparisons to A3C; does UNREAL win at equal compute.  
- Questions: Atari protocol details — sticky actions, no-ops, life-loss handling, random starts, adherence to ALE best practices.  
- Questions: transferability — zero/few-shot transfer tests of encoder to new reward functions or related tasks.  
- Minor: clarify weighting schedules and update frequencies for auxiliary losses; include sensitivity plots for λVR, λPC, λRP.  
- Minor: report per-task contribution bars for Labyrinth levels and confidence intervals/interquartile ranges for scores and speedups.  
- Minor: tighten related work around GVFs/predictive knowledge and cite intrinsic-motivation literature; fix reference formatting.  
- Overall: well-motivated, practical, and empirically strong contribution advancing deep RL training practice.
