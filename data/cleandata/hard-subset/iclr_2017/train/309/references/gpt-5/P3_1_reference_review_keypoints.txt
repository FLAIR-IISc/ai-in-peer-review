- Proposes UNREAL: an A3C agent augmented with off-policy auxiliary tasks from a small replay buffer to accelerate and stabilize representation learning.

- Auxiliary control tasks: pixel control (maximize local pixel-change on an n×n grid) and feature control (maximize hidden unit activations) trained via off-policy n-step Q-learning.

- Reward prediction task: a feedforward head trained on rebalanced replay that oversamples rewarding transitions to mitigate reward sparsity.

- Value function replay: reuse recent experience to stabilize and speed up value learning.

- Joint loss: shared CNN/LSTM backbone with on-policy A3C updates for policy/value and off-policy auxiliary objectives shaping representation.

- Empirical results: substantial gains in data efficiency, final performance, and hyperparameter robustness on 13 Labyrinth levels and 57 Atari games versus A3C and Prioritized Dueling DQN.

- Ablations: pixel control and reward prediction both contribute; control-based objectives outperform passive prediction/reconstruction.

- Decision: Accept — motivated by strong empirical improvements and a novel, practical integration of off-policy auxiliaries into an on-policy recurrent agent.

- Problem motivation: targeting sparse rewards and slow representation learning in high-dimensional, partially observed settings via auxiliary signals to densify training.

- Method novelty: practical pseudo-rewards for controllability, deconvolutional dueling head for spatial Q-values, and rebalanced sampling for reward prediction without biasing actor-critic.

- Method soundness: clear training protocol combining on-policy A3C with off-policy replay for auxiliary tasks is feasible and well-articulated.

- Relation to prior work: distinguishes from Horde, UVFA/successor representations, and model-prediction auxiliaries by emphasizing representation shaping for a recurrent policy.

- Labyrinth results: large human-normalized gains, ~10× median data-efficiency improvements, and robustness across hyperparameters.

- Atari results: broad mean/median human-normalized score improvements across 57 games with multiple hyperparameter settings per game.

- Request for clarity: specify Atari evaluation protocol (frame budgets, evaluation episodes, start conditions, sticky actions, termination rules) and align comparisons to baselines.

- Reproducibility concern: clarify how LSTM hidden/cell states are initialized and handled for replayed sequences in off-policy Q-learning and value replay.

- Compute fairness: report wall-clock and frame budgets, auxiliary computation overhead, and ensure baselines receive comparable compute; provide per-level sample-efficiency curves for Atari.

- Sensitivity analysis: include studies on auxiliary weights (λPC, λRP, λVR), pixel-control grid resolution, pixel-change normalization, and rationale for architectural choices (e.g., ReLU on Qaux).

- Representation analysis: provide qualitative/quantitative evidence of how auxiliaries alter features, saliency, or value estimation errors beyond performance metrics.

- Generality concerns: discuss applicability to non-visual domains, object-centric signals, and tasks with dense rewards; clarify transfer potential.

- Specific questions for authors: details on LSTM state initialization for replay, target network use for auxiliary heads, exact Atari evaluation settings and frame counts, seed averaging, any adverse effects from rebalanced sampling, fraction of compute spent on auxiliaries, sensitivity to replay buffer size and update frequency, sensitivity of pixel-control grid/cropping/normalization, and whether learned representations transfer when frozen.
