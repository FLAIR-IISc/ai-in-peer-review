**Summary of the Paper's Claims**

The paper proposes a framework for language learning that relies on multi-agent communication, specifically in the context of referential games. The authors claim that their approach allows agents to develop their own language interactively, without relying on large amounts of text data. They demonstrate that two simple neural networks can learn to coordinate in a referential game, and that the emergent language captures general conceptual properties of the objects depicted in the images. The authors also show that by modifying the game environment, they can encourage the agents to develop symbol meanings that are more closely related to human semantics. Finally, they demonstrate a path to grounding the communication in natural language by combining the game with a supervised task.
