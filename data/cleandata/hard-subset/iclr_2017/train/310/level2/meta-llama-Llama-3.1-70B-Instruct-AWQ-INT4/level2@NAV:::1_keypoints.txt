- Proposes a multi-agent communication framework for language learning using referential games.
- Claims agents can develop their own language interactively without large text corpora.
- Demonstrates two simple neural networks can coordinate effectively in a referential game.
- Shows emergent language captures general conceptual properties of pictured objects.
- Modifying the game environment can encourage symbol meanings closer to human semantics.
- Presents a path to ground emergent communication in natural language via a supervised task.
- Decision: Accept — paper is novel, well-motivated, and supported by strong experimental evidence.
- Authors situate work well within existing research and clearly explain contributions and limitations.
- Main contribution: a clear framework for language learning based on multi-agent interaction.
- Experimental results are strong and effective across multiple settings.
- Analysis of emergent language is thorough and shows conceptual structure alignment.
- Limitation: experiments use a relatively simple game environment; scalability is unclear.
- Suggests need for more analysis of relation between emergent language and human language.
- Recommends improving interpretability of the emergent language.
- Questions: how to extend to more complex environments and expected challenges?
- Questions: can you provide deeper analysis of emergent vs. human language and improve interpretability?
- Questions: how will you evaluate real-world effectiveness and which metrics will you use?
- Potential improvements: more analysis of human-language relationships and interpretability, and more details on scaling to complex environments and anticipated challenges.
