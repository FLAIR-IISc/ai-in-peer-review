- Proposes a multi-agent language-learning framework using referential games (sender/receiver choose target image).
- Shows two simple neural agents can coordinate and develop an emergent language mapping symbols to intuitive meanings.
- Explores environment tweaks to induce more semantically intuitive word meanings and a simple grounding strategy to natural language.
- Recommendation: accept.
- Strength: addresses a clear problem—developing machines that can communicate with humans productively.
- Strength: approach is well-motivated and situated within linguistics, cognitive science, and game theory.
- Strength: paper is clearly organized with a well-presented framework, experimental setup, and results.
- Supporting: provides a thorough literature review identifying limitations of prior multi-agent communication work.
- Supporting: experimental design is careful and controlled, isolating effects of different variables.
- Supporting: results convincingly demonstrate coordination and emergent language-like symbol-meaning mappings.
- Supporting: discussion of implications for conversational machines is clear and well-motivated.
- Limitation: game environment is relatively simple, raising questions about generalization to more complex settings.
- Suggestion: explore more sophisticated communication protocols (multi-step interaction, multi-symbol exchange).
- Suggestion: include more detailed analysis of the emergent language’s syntactic and semantic properties.
- Question: how is the agents’ language represented and how would it be used to communicate with humans?
- Question: how will the framework be extended to more complex environments and communication protocols?
- Question: can the authors provide deeper syntactic and semantic analysis of the emergent language?
