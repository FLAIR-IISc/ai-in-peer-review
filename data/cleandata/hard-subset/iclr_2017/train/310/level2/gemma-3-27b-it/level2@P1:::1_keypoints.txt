- Paper studies language emergence in referential games with two agents and grounding via supervised image labeling.  
- Recommendation: reject.  
- Research direction is promising and experimental setup is solid.  
- Contributions feel incremental and lack sufficient novelty over prior work (Sukhbaatar et al., 2016; Foerster et al., 2016).  
- Key design choices are not convincingly justified.  
- Motivation and problem framing are clear and well-articulated.  
- Experimental setup uses standard architectures and datasets and is well-defined.  
- Core results (agents can coordinate; environment changes influence semantics) are unsurprising given prior literature.  
- "Informed sender" yields performance gains but its explanation (dimension-by-dimension image combination) seems ad-hoc and lacks theoretical grounding.  
- Purity improvements tied to class-level properties are interesting but not transformative for the field.  
- Grounding experiment is promising but appears tacked on and under-analyzed.  
- 68% human-interpretability result lacks experimental detail and robustness checks.  
- Mention of “metonymic” links needs deeper qualitative analysis to explain successes and failures.  
- 47% correct label when target is in supervised category reads like a standard supervised outcome, not convincing grounding.  
- Paper needs clearer articulation of what is genuinely novel about the framework or analysis.  
- Ablation studies are needed to isolate contributions (informed sender, vocabulary size, temperature, etc.).  
- Theoretical analysis of game dynamics and why architectures perform differently would strengthen the work.  
- More qualitative examples of emergent symbols and failed communications are required.  
- Comparisons to additional baselines or alternative communication strategies should be included.  
- CrowdFlower experiment details (instructions, participant demographics, evaluation criteria) must be clarified.  
- Question: Why use convolution kernel size 2x1 for the informed sender — tuned or theoretically justified?  
- Question: Does informed sender performance degrade if vocabulary size is reduced to match the agnostic sender?  
- Question: Provide concrete examples of metonymic links and explanations for when grounding succeeds or fails.  
- Question: How sensitive is the human interpretability score to the choice of ImageNet labels for supervised learning?  
- Question: How does this work relate to signaling-game theory (Lewis), especially equilibrium selection and non-convergence?
