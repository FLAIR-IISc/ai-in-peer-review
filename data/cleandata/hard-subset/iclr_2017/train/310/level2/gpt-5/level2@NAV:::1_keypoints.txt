- Proposes a multi-agent referential game where two tabula rasa neural nets develop a discrete communication protocol enabling near-perfect coordination on real images.  
- Contribution 1: Environmental manipulations (e.g., different exemplars to sender/receiver) bias emergent codes toward higher-level, human-like semantics.  
- Contribution 2: Interleaving self-play with supervised image labeling grounds symbols in natural language and improves human interpretability.  
- Empirical findings: fast convergence, increased symbol purity with architectural/object-level biases, and 68% human interpretation accuracy versus 50% chance on a distinct dataset.  
- Decision: Accept—novel, well-motivated focus on aligning emergent protocols with human-understandable semantics and convincing empirical evidence including human evaluation.  
- Strong supporting analyses: clear framing against prior work and multiple quantitative probes (coordination accuracy, purity vs. McRae categories, SVD, t-SNE) and a preregistered-style human test with highly significant results.  
- Supervised grounding is simple yet substantially improves interpretability without harming coordination.  
- Acknowledged limitation: simplified one-shot, single-symbol setting using pretrained visual features, though insights likely transfer to richer dialog.  
- Recommend reporting variability across random seeds and confidence intervals for purity and human accuracy.  
- Recommend stronger baselines (non-communicative receiver, frozen random sender, hand-crafted features) and cross-play between independently trained agents.  
- Recommend analyzing topographic similarity/mutual information between visual/category distances and symbol space and testing context-independence/compositionality.  
- Recommend clarifying RL details (variance reduction, entropy regularization, temperature schedule) and ablating vocabulary size, bottleneck temperature, and feature layers.  
- Recommend assessing zero-shot generalization to new partners and multi-round/sequence communication.  
- Question: How stable are purity and human interpretability across seeds and vocab sizes?  
- Question: Does the informed sender’s advantage persist with larger vocabularies and deeper networks?  
- Question: In the grounding setup, how is leakage controlled between supervised labels and game images, and is there a category-wise breakdown of human accuracy?  
- Question: Can independently trained agents successfully cross-play, and how does supervision affect cross-play?  
- Question: What failure modes did the human study reveal (e.g., word-frequency biases, visually salient distractors)?
