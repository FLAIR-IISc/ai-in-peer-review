- Study of grounded communication emergence in referential games using two sender architectures (agnostic vs. informed) and a receiver on ImageNet-derived features.
- Contributions: (i) demonstrates robust emergence of communication without pre-coded protocols or supervision.
- Contributions: (ii) analyzes emergent symbol semantics, showing alignment with intuitive category structures, especially under an object-level variant.
- Contributions: (iii) proposes joint supervised grounding of the sender to increase symbol purity and partial human interpretability (validated in a ReferIt human study).
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reasons for acceptance: timely, well-motivated work with clear empirical evidence that interaction and light supervision improve semantic alignment.
- Key reasons for acceptance: human evaluation showing nontrivial interpretability (68% accuracy) is valuable and rare for emergent communication papers.
- Problem significance: addresses an important gap by training agents interactively rather than via passive supervised learning, relevant for conversational AI and grounding.
- Placement in literature: well-positioned relative to multi-agent RL and emergent language literature, with a distinctive focus on human-relevant semantics and interpretability.
- Methodological soundness: clean referential game setup; REINFORCE on discrete symbols is appropriate; informed sender shows beneficial inductive bias (faster convergence, more symbols, higher purity).
- Empirical support: near-perfect coordination, analyses of symbol usage (SVD, cluster purity, t-SNE) support claims; object-level variant and mixed supervised training convincingly improve abstraction and interpretability.
- Clarity and usefulness: provides actionable design insights about environment constraints and light grounding improving emergent code naturalness and portability.
- Reproducibility request: specify optimizer, learning rates, initialization, REINFORCE baselines/variance reduction, entropy regularization, and number of random seeds with mean/variance.
- Evaluation breadth requested: report absolute coordination accuracy, actual vocabulary size used, purity with confidence intervals, and generalization to unseen target/distractor concepts.
- Evaluation suggestion: assess referential consistency across diverse distractors and compare to a baseline that emits top-1 ImageNet class from features.
- Architectural analysis suggestion: add ablations to isolate the informed sender’s inductive bias (e.g., MLP with bilinear terms, Siamese encoders) and study sensitivity to temperature τ and bottleneck strength.
- Extensions suggestion: discuss multi-symbol messages, role alternation, or negotiation to position future work on compositionality.
- Human study details requested: report participant screening, inter-participant agreement, effect sizes, failure examples, and selection/balancing of image pairs.
- Semantic analysis suggestion: augment purity vs. McRae categories with WordNet-based hierarchical metrics or linear separability probes.
- Questions for authors: report training stability/variance across seeds, use of baselines/entropy bonus, and sample complexity to reach >95% accuracy.
- Questions for authors: clarify data splits and leakage, ensuring train/test disjointness at the image level and details for the object-level variant.
- Questions for authors: detail purity computation given unused symbols, normalization by effective symbol count, and confidence intervals.
- Questions for authors: quantify synonymy vs. polysemy via symbol entropy per object and object entropy per symbol from SVD analysis.
- Questions for authors: report sensitivity of grounding to proportion of supervised steps, label overlap, and shared vs. partial sender embedding in classifier head.
- Questions for authors: clarify ReferIt human study image pair selection and whether success correlates with semantic relatedness (e.g., WordNet distance).
