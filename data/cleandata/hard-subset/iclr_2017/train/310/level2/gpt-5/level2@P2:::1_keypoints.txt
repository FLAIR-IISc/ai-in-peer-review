- Proposes a simple framework where two tabula-rasa neural agents learn discrete-symbol communication to identify target images in cooperative referential games using ImageNet inputs.  
- Shows agents reliably and quickly develop a discrete communication protocol via REINFORCE across architectures and vocabulary sizes.  
- Analyzes induced symbol meanings and finds they correlate with high-level semantics (e.g., living vs. non-living, category structure), stronger for an “informed sender” architecture.  
- Demonstrates an object-level manipulation (different instance views per agent) that nudges emergent symbols toward higher-level semantics.  
- Introduces a grounding strategy mixing self-play with supervised image-labeling that improves interpretability and enables partial human–agent communication (68% human accuracy).  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Clear, timely, and novel empirical demonstration that discrete communication emerges on realistic visual inputs and can be steered toward human-interpretable semantics.  
- Key reason 2: A simple, scalable grounding mechanism that measurably improves interpretability and yields promising human-in-the-loop results.  
- Quality/correctness: Training setup and analyses (purity vs chance, t-SNE, SVD) support claims that symbols reflect high-level concepts, and object-level manipulation produces expected effects.  
- Novelty: Differs from prior work by using large noisy natural images, enforcing discrete sampling-based bottleneck, studying sender architecture bias, and focusing on controllability/interpretability with human evaluation.  
- Significance: Simple, robust setup is a strong foundation for scaling to richer language/tasks and for building interactive, human-interpretable agents.  
- Recommend adding unsupervised baselines for semantic purity (e.g., k-means on VGG features) and reporting purity variance over seeds.  
- Ask authors to clarify evaluation details: test-time sampling vs argmax, exact accuracies with confidence intervals, and purity mean±std across runs.  
- Request RL training details: variance-reduction baselines, entropy regularization, reward normalization, and sensitivity to τ and vocabulary sizes beyond 10/100.  
- Clarify data splits and leakage: are train/test images disjoint at instance/class level and report generalization to unseen concepts/instances.  
- Suggest ablating informed-sender components and reporting symbol entropy/usage distributions to understand architectural inductive biases.  
- Quantify synonymy/homonymy (many-to-one or one-to-many mappings) with confusion matrices between symbols and categories.  
- Human study: report inter-annotator agreement, per-word performance, and examples where humans systematically fail.  
- Propose harder distractor conditions (semantically similar distractors) to test finer-grained semantic specialization of symbols.  
- Fix reference formatting and consider releasing code and preprocessed splits for reproducibility.  
- Questions for authors include: exact test-time decoding policy; use of variance-reduction techniques and sensitivity to τ; number of random seeds and variance; train/test instance disjointness and performance on unseen classes; effect of semantically similar distractors; baseline purity for clustering VGG features; and how often sender uses supervised labels and whether metonymic mappings arise.
