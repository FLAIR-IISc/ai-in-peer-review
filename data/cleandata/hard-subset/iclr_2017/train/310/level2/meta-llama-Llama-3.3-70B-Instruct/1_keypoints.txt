- Proposes a novel multi-agent communication framework where agents develop language through interactive games.
- Demonstrates that simple neural-network agents can coordinate in a referential game and assign symbols reflecting conceptual object properties.
- Shows grounding in natural language by combining the game with a supervised task.
- Uses reinforcement learning and game-theoretic ideas as a solid theoretical foundation.
- Experimental results support effectiveness in learning to communicate and grounding language.
- Paper is well-motivated, innovative, well-written, and provides a comprehensive related-work review.
- Authors acknowledge limitations and outline clear directions for future research.
- Reviewer decides to accept the paper based on its contribution and thorough evaluation.
