- Proposes a referential-game framework for studying language emergence with sender and receiver agents developing a communication protocol from scratch.
- Shows simple neural agents can learn to communicate and coordinate in the referential game starting from blank slates.
- Finds emergent symbols correspond to high-level semantic object categories rather than low-level visual features (cluster purity and visualizations).
- Demonstrates that removing low-level visual common knowledge nudges agents toward more abstract, conceptual communication.
- Introduces grounding by combining interactive self-play with supervised image-labeling, producing agent protocols interpretable by humans and generalizing to unseen objects.
- Decision: Accept.
- Primary reason 1: Provides a compelling, scalable interactive framework that moves beyond passive language learning.
- Primary reason 2: Novel and effective grounding strategy that yields human-interpretable, generalizable communication.
- Strong motivation and clear positioning within multi-agent, game-theory, and cognitive-science literature.
- Logical experimental progression: emergence → analysis → shaping → grounding.
- Analyses are solid (cluster purity, SVD showing richer vocabulary for informed sender, helpful t-SNE visualizations).
- Human evaluation (68% accuracy) strongly supports flexible grounding and metonymic generalization claims.
