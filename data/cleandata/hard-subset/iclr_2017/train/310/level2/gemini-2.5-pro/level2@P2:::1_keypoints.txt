- Introduces a multi-agent, referential-game framework as an alternative to passive corpus-based language learning.  
- Shows tabula rasa feed-forward agents can learn discrete-symbol communication to coordinate in the referential game.  
- Demonstrates architectural bias ("informed" sender) and task modifications improve semantic purity of emergent protocols.  
- Proposes grounding emergent symbols in natural language by combining interactive play with supervised image labeling.  
- Hybrid training yields human-interpretable symbols that generalize to novel objects (semantic extension/metonymy).  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Reason 1: Strong motivation and interdisciplinary grounding connecting game theory, cognitive science, and deep learning.  
- Reason 2: Novel, significant experiments—especially the grounding results—convincingly support claims.  
- Experimental analysis is thorough, using cluster purity and SVD to probe semantic structure.  
- "Informed" sender architecture appears to produce richer, more semantically coherent protocols.  
- Object-level reference modification encourages abstract representations and increases semantic purity.  
- Human evaluation (ReferItGame) shows 68% accuracy for humans interpreting agent messages, highlighting generalization.
