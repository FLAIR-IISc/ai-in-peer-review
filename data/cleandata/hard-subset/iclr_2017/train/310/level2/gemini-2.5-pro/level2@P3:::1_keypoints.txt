- Framework: proposes a multi-agent referential game where sender and receiver learn a communication protocol from scratch using real images.
- Contribution 1: shows simple feed-forward networks trained with RL can converge to a shared protocol on this task.
- Contribution 2: demonstrates environmental and architectural factors (informed sender, removing instance-level common knowledge) promote more compositional, abstract emergent language.
- Contribution 3: introduces grounding by combining interactive play with supervised image labeling, producing protocols interpretable by humans, including for unseen concepts.
- Decision: Accept.
- Reason 1: compelling, well-motivated alternative to passive supervised language learning; framework is simple, elegant, and effective.
- Reason 2: thorough and insightful experiments that analyze emergent language properties and demonstrate concrete shaping and grounding strategies; human evaluation supports claims.
- Methodological strength: grounded in signaling-game theory and implemented with modern deep learning + RL, with careful experimental design.
- Analysis strength: uses SVD for vocabulary richness and cluster purity for semantic coherence to convincingly characterize emergent protocols.
- Architectural finding: the “informed sender” and object-level training encourage semantically structured, abstract communication.
- Grounding result: mixing self-play with supervised classification produces interpretable words and human-recognizable metaphoric/metonymic usage for novel objects.
- Feedback — clarity: clarify the informed sender’s mathematical formulation (1-D convolutions and filters) for reproducibility.
- Feedback — hyperparameters: discuss sensitivity to hyperparameters (e.g., temperature τ) and their effect on exploration vs. exploitation.
- Feedback — scalability: address challenges and directions for scaling to much larger vocabularies and complex compositional language.
- Feedback — RL credit assignment: acknowledge the credit-assignment issue when both agents share a global reward (sender vs receiver responsibility).
- Clarification Q1: in object-level experiments, clarify whether agents are trained/tested on different unseen images of the same classes to support generalization claims.
- Clarification Q2: expand interpretation of SVD results (e.g., significance of ~50 required dimensions relative to number of concepts/semantic features).
