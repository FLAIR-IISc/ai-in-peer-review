- Introduces a referential game where a sender, given a binary target and two images, sends a message to a receiver who must recover the target; agents rewarded for correct prediction.  
- Agents are neural networks using pre-trained image representations and are trained with REINFORCE; training converges to optimal policies and yields meaningful emergent concepts.  
- A variant with different image classes leads agents to learn even more meaningful concepts.  
- A multi-game setup where the sender alternates between the referential game and a supervised task produces more symbolic, meaningful communications.  
- Framing shared representations for communication and grounding in human language is a novel and valuable alternative to standard sequence-to-sequence approaches.  
- The proposed task, framework, and token-analysis contributions are strong and justify acceptance.
