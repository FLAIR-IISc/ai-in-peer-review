- Training natural language systems via interactive multi-agent referential communication games is an intriguing and promising approach, building on limited prior work.
- Alternating between these games and supervised learning (Sections 5 and 6) is presented as a particularly effective methodology.
- Clarity concerns: Figure 1 appears to omit some network connections and lacks a caption, and Section 3’s customized architecture description is convoluted, so the diagram and explanation need refinement.
- The framework in Section 2 is well-designed, effective, versatile, and yields reasonable results likely to inspire future variations.
- Reviewer caveat: limited familiarity with closely related literature may affect the accuracy of the paper’s contextual assessment.
