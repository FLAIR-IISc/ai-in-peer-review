- Proposal: introduce a referential game with a sender and receiver over two images where the sender receives a binary target t, sends a symbol, and both are rewarded if the receiver predicts t.  
- Method: agents are neural networks using pretrained image features and trained with REINFORCE.  
- Results: agents converge to optimal policies and learned symbols encode meaningful concepts.  
- Variants: a game grounded in different image classes yields even more meaningful concepts.  
- Multi-game: alternating the referential game with supervised image classification further anchors communication and improves conceptfulness.  
- Novelty/significance: learning communication first then grounding in language is novel and a promising alternative to seq2seq; paper is a valuable foundation for future work.  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Clarification requested: explain how the target variable t is incorporated into the sender network.  
- Presentation issues: standardize percentage formatting between Table 1 and Table 2; verify suspiciously small "obs-chance purity" values in Table 1.  
- Typos: fix "assest" → "assess" and "usufal" → "usual."
