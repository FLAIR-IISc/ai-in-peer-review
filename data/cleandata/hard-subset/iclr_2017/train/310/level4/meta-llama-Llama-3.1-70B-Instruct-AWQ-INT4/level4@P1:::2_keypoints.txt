- Proposes a novel framework for training natural language systems via multi-agent interactive referential communication games.  
- Combining these games with supervised learning (Sections 5 and 6) is particularly promising.  
- Framework in Section 2 is well-designed and has potential for various applications.  
- Results are reasonable though not groundbreaking, providing a solid foundation for future work.  
- Figure 1 appears to omit some network connections and Section 3’s description is confusing; the diagram and caption should be revised.  
- Reviewer is not fully familiar with closely related literature, so paper’s positioning is unclear, but the approach and results are compelling.  
- Requests clarifications: more network-architecture details and omitted connections in Figure 1.  
- Requests clarifications: how the framework could be applied to more complex tasks like dialogue systems.  
- Requests clarifications: potential limitations/challenges of using multi-agent games for teaching communication and plans to address them.  
- Overall assessment: promising work that could make a valuable contribution after addressing the noted concerns.
