This paper introduces a referential game involving two agents, each observing two images, where the sender agent receives a binary target variable and must convey a message to the receiver agent, enabling the latter to infer the target. Both agents are rewarded if the receiver accurately predicts the target. The authors propose using neural networks with pre-trained image representations as feature vectors and training them via REINFORCE. The results demonstrate that the agents converge to optimal policies and develop meaningful communication concepts. Furthermore, experiments with variant games grounded in different image classes yield even more meaningful concepts. A multi-game setup is also explored, where the sender agent alternates between playing the game and a supervised learning task, resulting in more meaningful symbolic communications.

Learning shared representations for multi-agent communication is a promising research direction, presenting a more challenging task than standard supervised learning or single-agent reinforcement learning. The approach of learning communication between two agents before grounding it in human language is novel and may offer an alternative to standard sequence-to-sequence models, which often focus on statistical properties rather than functional aspects of language. The contributions of the proposed task and framework, as well as the analysis and visualization of the communicated tokens, provide a valuable foundation for future research. Therefore, I recommend accepting this paper.
