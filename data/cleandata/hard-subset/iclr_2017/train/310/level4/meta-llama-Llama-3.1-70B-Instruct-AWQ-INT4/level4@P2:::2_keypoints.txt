- Proposes a multi-agent communicative framework for language learning focused on referential games.  
- Well-motivated alternative to supervised learning for building interactive machines like conversational agents.  
- Uses simple neural-network agents to learn coordination, showing emergent language capturing conceptual object properties.  
- Paper accepted based on innovation and demonstrated potential of multi-agent communication.  
- Clear and effective framework in Section 2 with useful experimental demonstrations.  
- Good balance between theoretical framework and empirical results.  
- Simplicity of agents and referential-game focus improves interpretability of results.  
- Figure 1’s network connections are unclear and the caption needs improvement.  
- Related-work discussion is too high-level; needs more detailed comparison to prior research.  
- Experimental setup lacks details on hyperparameter tuning and network initialization.  
- More visualizations (performance over time, symbol-usage heatmaps) would strengthen the paper.  
- Should discuss limitations and future directions (scaling agents, complex protocols).  
- Provide more context linking the work to existing literature.  
- Questions for authors: details on initialization/hyperparameter tuning, plans/challenges for scaling agents, potential applications to conversational agents, and how common knowledge issues will be addressed.
