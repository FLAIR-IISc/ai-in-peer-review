- Proposes a novel framework using multi-agent referential games to train natural language systems.
- Two agents (sender and receiver) interact to develop a language for coordination.
- Emergent language captures general conceptual object properties rather than low-level visual features.
- Explores grounding agent communication in human language by combining the game with supervised image labeling.
- Decision: accept—framework is novel, effective, and well-motivated.
- Results are reasonable and demonstrate the approach’s potential.
- Paper presents a clear description of the game setup, agent architectures, and training procedure.
- Section 5 experiment (combining game with supervised labeling) is particularly interesting and yields more interpretable, grounded language.
