The authors' approach to training natural language systems by engaging multiple agents in an interactive referential communication game is innovative and well-motivated. As they acknowledge, there has been some prior work on utilizing multi-agent games to teach communication, and this direction appears to be worth exploring further. The proposed method of switching between these games and supervised learning, as demonstrated in Section 5 and suggested in Section 6, seems particularly promising.

One aspect that could be improved for clarity is the diagram in Figure 1. Some network connections appear to be missing, and the highly customized architecture, combined with the somewhat convoluted description in Section 3, makes the shorthand diagram confusing. To mitigate this, the diagram could be refined, and at the very least, a caption should be added to guide the reader in interpreting the figure.

Overall, the framework presented in Section 2 is well-designed and appears to be effective and useful in various contexts. The results are reasonable, and it is likely that this work will lead to interesting future variations. The authors have demonstrated a solid foundation for their approach, and with further refinement, it has the potential to make a significant impact in the field.
