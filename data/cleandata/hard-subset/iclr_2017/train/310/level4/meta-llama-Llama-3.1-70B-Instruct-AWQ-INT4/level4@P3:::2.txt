**Summary of the Paper**

The authors propose a novel framework for training natural language systems by leveraging multi-agent communication in referential games. In this setup, two agents, a sender and a receiver, interact to develop a language that enables them to coordinate and achieve a common goal. The authors demonstrate that this approach can lead to the emergence of a language that captures general conceptual properties of objects, rather than low-level visual properties. They also explore ways to ground the agents' communication in human language by combining the game with a supervised image labeling task.
