- Proposes a novel multi-agent language-learning framework using a referential game with a sender and receiver.  
- Agents are parameterized as neural networks using pre-trained image feature vectors and trained with REINFORCE.  
- Shows agents can converge to optimal policies and develop meaningful communication protocols.  
- Novel two-step paradigm: first learn inter-agent communication, then ground it in human language, distinct from supervised or single-agent RL.  
- Analysis and token visualizations offer valuable insights into the learned representations.  
- Recommendation: paper should be accepted.
