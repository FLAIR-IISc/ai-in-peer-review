Using multi-agent interactive referential communication games to train natural language systems is a promising approach.

Alternating between these games and supervised learning (demonstrated in Section 5 and suggested in Section 6) appears particularly fruitful.

Figure 1's network connections appear incomplete and the abbreviated diagram adds confusion; it needs refinement and at least a descriptive caption.

Section 3's description is somewhat convoluted and the highly customized architecture increases difficulty of understanding.

The framework in Section 2 is excellent, seemingly effective, and broadly useful.

Results are reasonable and likely to inspire interesting future variations.

Reviewer caveat: not sufficiently familiar with the closest related literature to confidently assess the paper's positioning.
