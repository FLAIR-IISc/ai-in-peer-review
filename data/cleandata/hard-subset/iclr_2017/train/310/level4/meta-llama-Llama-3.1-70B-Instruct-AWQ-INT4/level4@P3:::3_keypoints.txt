- Proposes a novel multi-agent referential game framework where sender and receiver agents learn communication via REINFORCE to identify a target image.
- Shows agents can coordinate and develop meaningful concepts through learned communication.
- Explores variants: class-specific communication and grounding the sender in human language via supervised learning.
- Recommendation: accept — task and framework are novel, experiments and visualizations are insightful, contributions justify acceptance.
- Strength: novel approach to learning and grounding communication with convincing experiments and token visualizations.
- Unclear how the target (t) is incorporated into the sender networks — needs clearer explanation.
- Inconsistent percentage formatting between Table 1 ([0,100]) and Table 2 ([0,1]) — requires correction.
- "obs-chance purity" in Table 1 has extremely small values that may be erroneous and should be checked.
- Paper contains minor typos (e.g., "assest" → assess, "usufal" → usual).
- Suggest providing more details on the sender and receiver neural network architectures.
- Suggest experiments on robustness of learned protocols to environmental changes or noise.
- Questions for authors: clarify incorporation of target t, plans for extending to more complex language tasks, and whether alternative algorithms (policy gradient/actor-critic) were considered.
