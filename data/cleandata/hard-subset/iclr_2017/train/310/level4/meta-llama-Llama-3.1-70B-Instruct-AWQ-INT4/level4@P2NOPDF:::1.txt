**Pros**

This paper tackles a crucial problem of understanding communication between two agents, a topic that gains increasing relevance as more applications of reinforcement learning are being explored. By doing so, the authors bring to the forefront a fundamental question: does the problem-solving approach of machines resemble that of humans? The simplicity of the task makes post-learning analysis intuitive and easy to follow. Furthermore, the results show an interesting distinction in the communication strategies employed by informed and agnostic agents, with the former utilizing multiple symbols to convey messages, while the latter relying on only two symbols.

**Cons**

A significant limitation of the proposed approach is that the task effectively reduces to image classification when the two images sent belong to different categories. In this case, the symbols used can be seen as equivalent to the image class, which the second agent learns to assign to either of the images. This essentially boils down to a transfer learning problem, which could potentially be solved more efficiently using alternative methods, rather than relying on a reinforcement learning algorithm.
