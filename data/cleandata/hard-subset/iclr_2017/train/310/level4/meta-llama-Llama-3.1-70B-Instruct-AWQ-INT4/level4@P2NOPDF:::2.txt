This paper explores a promising approach to training natural language systems by engaging multiple agents in an interactive referential communication game. The authors build upon previous work that utilizes multi-agent games to teach communication, a direction that warrants further investigation. The proposed method of combining these games with supervised learning, as demonstrated in Section 5 and suggested in Section 6, appears to be a fruitful approach.

One aspect that requires clarification is the network architecture depicted in Figure 1. Some connections seem to be omitted, which, combined with the customized architecture and the somewhat convoluted description in Section 3, may lead to confusion. A more detailed diagram or, at the very least, an explanatory caption is necessary to facilitate the reader's understanding of the figure.

The framework presented in Section 2 is well-designed and demonstrates potential for various applications. The results are reasonable, and it is likely that this work will inspire interesting future variations. However, it is essential to acknowledge that this work's position within the broader context of related literature is not entirely clear, as the reviewer is not sufficiently familiar with the most closely related studies.
