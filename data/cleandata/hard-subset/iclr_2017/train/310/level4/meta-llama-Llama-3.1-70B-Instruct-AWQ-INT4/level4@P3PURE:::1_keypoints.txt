- Addresses understanding communication between two agents, a crucial problem as RL applications expand.
- Revisits the fundamental question of whether machines employ problem-solving approaches similar to humans.
- The task is simple, which makes post-learning analysis intuitive.
- Empirical observation: informed agents use multiple symbols, while agnostic agents rely on only two.
- Major criticism: when the two images belong to different categories, the task effectively becomes image classification.
- In that case, symbols correspond to image class and the second agent simply learns to assign classes.
- Thus the problem reduces to a transfer-learning setup that could likely be trained far faster than using reinforcement learning.
