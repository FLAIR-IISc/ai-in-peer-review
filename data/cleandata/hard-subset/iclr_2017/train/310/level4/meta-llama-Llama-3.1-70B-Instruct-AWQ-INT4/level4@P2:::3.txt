This paper proposes a novel framework for language learning through multi-agent communication in a referential game setting. The authors design a game where two agents, a sender and a receiver, observe two images and aim to communicate the target image through a symbolic code. The agents are parametrized as neural networks and trained using REINFORCE to learn optimal policies. The paper demonstrates that the agents can converge to successful communication and develop meaningful concepts in their learned communication protocol.

The authors' approach to learning shared representations for communication in a multi-agent setup is an interesting research direction to explore. This task is significantly more challenging than standard supervised learning or single-agent reinforcement learning tasks, justifying the use of a relatively simple task as a starting point. The idea of first learning communication between two agents and then grounding this communication in human language is novel and may offer an alternative paradigm to standard sequence-to-sequence models, which tend to focus on statistical properties of language rather than their functional aspects.

The paper's contributions, including the proposed task and framework, as well as the analysis and visualization of the communicated tokens, are valuable stepping stones for future work. The authors' experiments on a variant of the game grounded on different image classes and the multi-game setup, where the sender agent alternates between playing the game and a supervised learning task, provide additional insights into the potential of this approach.
