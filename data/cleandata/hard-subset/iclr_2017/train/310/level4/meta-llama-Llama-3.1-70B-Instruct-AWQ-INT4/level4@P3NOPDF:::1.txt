This paper provides an interesting perspective on understanding communication between two agents, a crucial problem in the realm of reinforcement learning. As researchers continue to explore various applications of reinforcement learning, this approach revisits a fundamental question: do machines employ problem-solving strategies similar to those of humans?

The authors have chosen a simple yet effective task that facilitates intuitive post-learning analysis. A notable observation is the distinct communication strategies employed by informed and agnostic agents. Informed agents utilize multiple symbols to convey messages, whereas agnostic agents rely on a limited vocabulary of only two symbols.

However, upon closer examination, the task can be reduced to a straightforward image classification problem when the two images sent belong to different categories. In this context, the symbols used effectively represent the image class, which the second agent learns to assign to either image. This approach essentially boils down to a transfer learning problem, which could potentially be trained much faster than a reinforcement learning algorithm.

To strengthen the paper, the authors could provide more insight into how their approach differs from or improves upon existing transfer learning methods. Additionally, they may want to consider exploring more complex tasks or scenarios that truly necessitate the use of reinforcement learning for communication.
