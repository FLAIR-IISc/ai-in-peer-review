This paper proposes a novel framework for language learning through multi-agent communication, addressing a crucial problem in the development of conversational machines. The authors design a referential game where two agents, a sender and a receiver, must develop a language to coordinate and achieve a common goal. This approach is well-motivated, as it allows agents to learn the functional aspects of communication, which are essential for human-agent interaction.

I decide to accept this paper, with two key reasons for this choice. Firstly, the paper tackles a fundamental problem in AI research, and the proposed framework is innovative and well-designed. Secondly, the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach, showing that agents can learn to communicate and coordinate successfully.

The paper provides a clear and thorough explanation of the framework, including the game setup, agent architectures, and training details. The experimental results are well-presented, and the analysis of the emergent language is insightful. The use of clustering and visualization techniques to evaluate the semantic properties of the language is particularly effective.

One potential limitation of the approach is that it may not generalize well to more complex scenarios, where the number of images and categories increases significantly. Additionally, the reliance on reinforcement learning may make the training process slower compared to other approaches, such as transfer learning.

To improve the paper, I suggest the authors consider the following points:

* Provide more discussion on the scalability of the approach and its potential applications in real-world scenarios.
* Explore the use of other machine learning techniques, such as transfer learning, to improve the efficiency of the training process.
* Consider adding more complexity to the game setup, such as introducing noise or ambiguity, to make the task more challenging and realistic.

Questions for the authors:

* Can you elaborate on the choice of agent architectures and the design of the communication protocol?
* How do you plan to extend the approach to more complex scenarios, such as multi-agent communication or human-agent interaction?
* What are the potential applications of this framework in real-world scenarios, such as robotics or natural language processing?
