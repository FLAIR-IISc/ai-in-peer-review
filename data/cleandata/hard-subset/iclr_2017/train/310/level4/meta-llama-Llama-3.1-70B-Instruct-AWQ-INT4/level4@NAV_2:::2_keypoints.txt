- Proposes training natural language systems via multi-agent communication in referential games.  
- Novel contribution: combines interactive learning with supervised learning within a unified framework.  
- Experimental results show agents learn to communicate and coordinate effectively.  
- Recommendation: accept, motivated by strong grounding in literature and promising results.  
- Framework is clearly explained with a general multi-agent setup and a referential-game implementation.  
- Uses simple neural networks and reinforcement learning, making the approach accessible.  
- Agents maintain effective communication even when the environment is modified to encourage abstraction.  
- Figure 1’s network visualization omits some connections and needs a clearer diagram or caption.  
- Paper lacks a comprehensive comparison and context within related multi-agent communication work.  
- Suggested investigations: how communication protocols evolve and what influences that evolution.  
- Suggested extensions: apply framework to more complex environments and compare with other training methods (supervised learning, RL from human feedback).
