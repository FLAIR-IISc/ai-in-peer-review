This paper provides an interesting read.

Pros:
- The authors tackle a crucial problem of understanding communication between two agents, which is particularly relevant given the increasing exploration of reinforcement learning applications. This approach prompts a fundamental question: do machines employ problem-solving strategies similar to those of humans?
- The task is simple, making post-learning analysis intuitive.
- It is notable to observe that informed agents utilized multiple symbols to convey messages, whereas agnostic agents relied on only two symbols.

Cons:
- The task essentially reduces to an image classification problem when the two images sent belong to different categories. In this context, the symbols used are effectively equivalent to the image class, which the second agent learns to assign to either image. This approach is analogous to a transfer learning problem, which could potentially be trained much faster than a reinforcement learning algorithm.
