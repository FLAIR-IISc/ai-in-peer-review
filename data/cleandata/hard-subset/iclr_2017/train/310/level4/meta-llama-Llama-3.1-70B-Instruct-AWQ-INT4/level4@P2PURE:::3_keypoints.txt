- Paper proposes a referential game where a sender with a binary target sends a symbol to a receiver so the receiver can predict the target; both agents are rewarded for correct prediction.  
- Agents are neural networks using pre-trained image feature vectors and are trained with REINFORCE.  
- Agents converge to optimal policies and learn symbolic communications that exhibit meaningful concepts.  
- Experiments on a variant grounded in different image classes show even more meaningful learned concepts.  
- A multi-game setup alternating between the referential game and supervised image classification further grounds and improves the meaningfulness of the symbols.  
- The approach of learning agent communication first and then grounding it in human language is novel and a useful alternative to seq2seq-focused paradigms.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, noting the task and analyses are a valuable stepping stone for future work.  
- Requests clarification on how the target t is incorporated into the sender network.  
- Points out inconsistencies in percentage formatting across Table 1 and Table 2 and an apparent error in the "obs-chance purity" column.  
- Notes minor typos: "assest" → "assess" and "usufal" → "usual".
