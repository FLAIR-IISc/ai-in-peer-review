This paper tackles a crucial problem of understanding communication between two agents, which is increasingly relevant as reinforcement learning applications continue to expand. By exploring this problem, the authors revisit a fundamental question: do machines employ a problem-solving approach similar to that of humans? 

The task is simple enough to make post-learning analysis intuitive. One notable observation is that informed agents utilized multiple symbols to transmit messages, whereas agnostic agents relied on only two symbols.

However, the task effectively boils down to image classification when the two images sent belong to different categories. In this context, the symbols used are effectively the image class, which the second agent learns to assign to either of the images. This approach essentially reduces to a transfer learning problem, which could likely be trained much faster than a reinforcement learning algorithm.
