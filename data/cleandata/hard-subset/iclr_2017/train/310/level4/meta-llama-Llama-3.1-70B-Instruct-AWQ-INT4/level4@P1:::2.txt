The paper proposes a novel framework for training natural language systems by engaging multiple agents in an interactive referential communication game. This approach is indeed intriguing, and the authors are correct in noting that there has been limited prior work on utilizing multi-agent games for teaching communication. The idea of combining these games with supervised learning, as demonstrated in Section 5 and suggested in Section 6, appears particularly promising.

The paper's framework, presented in Section 2, is well-designed and demonstrates potential for various applications. The results, while not groundbreaking, are reasonable and provide a solid foundation for future work.

However, there are some areas that require attention. Specifically, the network architecture depicted in Figure 1 seems to be missing some connections, which, combined with the somewhat confusing description in Section 3, may lead to misunderstandings. To alleviate this, the diagram should be revised, and a detailed caption should be added to facilitate the reader's comprehension.

In terms of the paper's position within the broader context of related literature, I am not sufficiently familiar with the most closely related work to provide an accurate assessment. Nevertheless, the proposed framework and results are compelling, and I anticipate that this work will lead to interesting future variations.

To further improve the paper, I would like the authors to address the following questions:

1. Could you provide more details about the network architecture and clarify any omitted connections in Figure 1?
2. How do you envision the proposed framework being applied to more complex natural language tasks, such as conversation or dialogue systems?
3. What are the potential limitations or challenges of using multi-agent games for teaching communication, and how do you plan to address them in future work?

Overall, I believe that this paper presents a promising approach to training natural language systems, and with some revisions to address the mentioned concerns, it has the potential to make a valuable contribution to the field.
