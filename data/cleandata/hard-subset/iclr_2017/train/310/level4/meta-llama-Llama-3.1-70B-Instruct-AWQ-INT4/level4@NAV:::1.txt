Thank you for sharing this thought-provoking paper.

Strengths:
- This study addresses a fundamental issue in understanding the communication dynamics between two agents, which is increasingly relevant as reinforcement learning applications continue to expand. By doing so, it raises an essential question: do machines employ problem-solving strategies similar to those of humans?
- The simplicity of the task facilitates an intuitive analysis of the results post-learning.
- It is noteworthy to observe how informed agents utilized multiple symbols to convey messages, whereas agnostic agents relied solely on two symbols.

Weaknesses:
- Upon closer examination, the task essentially reduces to an image classification problem when the two transmitted images belong to distinct categories. In this context, the symbols used can be viewed as image class labels that the second agent learns to assign to either image. Consequently, this approach can be seen as a transfer learning problem, which could potentially be trained more efficiently using alternative methods rather than reinforcement learning.
