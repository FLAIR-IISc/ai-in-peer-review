Thank you for an interesting read.

Pros:
- This paper tackles a crucial problem of understanding communication between two agents, which is particularly relevant as reinforcement learning is being increasingly explored in various applications. By doing so, it raises a fundamental question: do machines approach problem-solving in a way similar to humans?
- The task is simple enough to make post-learning analysis intuitive, allowing for a clear understanding of the agents' behavior.
- It is interesting to note how informed agents utilized multiple symbols to transmit messages, whereas agnostic agents relied solely on two symbols.

Cons:
- The task effectively reduces to image classification when the two images sent belong to different categories. In this case, the symbols used are essentially the image class that the second agent learns to assign to either of the images. This approach can be viewed as a transfer learning problem, which could likely be trained much faster than a reinforcement learning algorithm.
