- Proposes a referential game: sender sees binary target t and two images, sends a symbol to receiver who must predict the target; both rewarded for correct prediction.
- Agents are neural networks using pre-trained image feature vectors and trained with REINFORCE.
- Empirical result: agents converge to optimal policies and develop symbolic communications that capture meaningful concepts.
- Variant experiment with different image classes shows agents learn even more meaningful concepts.
- Multi-game setup (alternating referential game and supervised image classification) further grounds symbols, producing more human-meaningful communications.
- Framing multi-agent shared-representation learning for communication is an interesting, challenging direction and the paper’s approach (learn communication then ground in language) is novel relative to seq2seq.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, viewing contributions and visualizations as a useful stepping stone.
- Requested clarification on how the target t is incorporated into the sender network.
- Noted inconsistency in percentage scales between Table 1 ([0,100]) and Table 2 ([0,1]) and suspiciously small "obs-chance purity" values in Table 1.
- Minor typos to fix: "assest" → "assess" and "usufal" → "usual".
