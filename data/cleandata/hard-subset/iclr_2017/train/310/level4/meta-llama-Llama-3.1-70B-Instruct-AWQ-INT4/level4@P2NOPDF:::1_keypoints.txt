- Tackles the important problem of understanding communication between two agents as RL applications expand.  
- Raises the fundamental question of whether machine problem-solving resembles human problem-solving.  
- Uses a simple task that makes post-learning analysis intuitive and easy to follow.  
- Presents an interesting empirical distinction: informed agents use multiple symbols while agnostic agents rely on only two.  
- Major limitation: the task reduces to image classification when paired images are from different categories, making symbols equivalent to image classes.  
- Consequently, the problem resembles transfer learning and might be solved more efficiently without reinforcement learning.
