- Paper offers an interesting perspective on agent-to-agent communication in RL and asks whether machines adopt human-like problem-solving strategies.  
- Experimental task is simple and well-chosen for intuitive post-learning analysis.  
- Key empirical finding: informed agents use many symbols, while agnostic agents restrict to a two-symbol vocabulary.  
- Critique: when transmitted images are from different categories, the task reduces to a straightforward image-classification problem.  
- Interpretation: the learned symbols effectively encode image class and the receiver learns to map class labels—i.e., this is essentially a transfer-learning setup.  
- Concern: the approach could likely be trained faster with supervised/transfer learning than with RL.  
- Recommendation: authors should compare to/contrast with transfer-learning baselines and evaluate on more complex tasks that truly require RL-based communication.
