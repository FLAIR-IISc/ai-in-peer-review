This paper proposes a novel framework for language learning through multi-agent communication in a referential game setup. The game involves two agents, a sender and a receiver, who observe two images and aim to communicate the target image through a symbolic message. The agents are parametrized as neural networks with pre-trained representations of the images as feature vectors and are trained using REINFORCE. The paper demonstrates that the agents can converge to optimal policies and develop meaningful communication protocols.

I appreciate the authors' efforts to explore this interesting research direction, which is distinct from standard supervised learning or single-agent reinforcement learning tasks. The approach of first learning communication between two agents and then grounding this communication in human language is novel and may offer an alternative paradigm to standard sequence-to-sequence models. The analysis and visualization of the communicated tokens provide valuable insights into the learned representations.

Given the significance of the proposed task and framework, I believe this paper should be accepted.

To further improve the paper, I have a few minor comments:

* The authors should clarify how the target variable (t) is incorporated into the sender networks. This detail is crucial for understanding the model architecture.
* There seems to be a discrepancy in the formatting of percentage values in Table 1 and Table 2. The values in the first table appear to be in the interval [0, 100], while those in the second table are in the interval [0, 1]. The authors should correct this inconsistency. Additionally, the "obs-chance purity" column in Table 1 contains extremely small values, which may be a mistake.
* There are a few typos in the paper: "assest" should be replaced with "assess," and "usufal" should be replaced with "usual."

Overall, the paper presents a compelling framework for language learning through multi-agent communication, and with minor revisions, it has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
