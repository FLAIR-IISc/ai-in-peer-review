- Paper proposes a referential game: sender and receiver each see two images, sender gets binary target t and sends a symbol to help receiver recover t.  
- Agents are parameterized as neural networks using pre-trained image features and trained with REINFORCE.  
- Experiments show agents converge to optimal policies and the learned symbolic communications capture meaningful concepts.  
- A variant grounded on different image classes leads to even more meaningful learned concepts.  
- A multi-game setup alternates the sender between the referential game and a supervised image classification task, which further grounds the symbols.  
- Learning shared communication representations in multi-agent setups is an interesting and challenging direction; starting with a simple task is justified.  
- The approach of learning communication then grounding it in human language is novel and could be an alternative to sequence-to-sequence models.  
- Contributions, analyses, and visualizations of communicated tokens are useful and justify acceptance.  
- Clarification requested: how is the target t incorporated into the sender network?  
- Issue: Table 1 and Table 2 use percentages inconsistently (one in [0,100], the other in [0,1]); "obs-chance purity" in Table 1 seems suspiciously small and may be an error.
