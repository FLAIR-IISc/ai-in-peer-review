- Introduces a novel referential-game approach to study communication between two agents.  
- Tackles a significant reinforcement learning question: can machines learn human-like communication.  
- Strength: simple task setup with intuitive post-learning analysis.  
- Simplicity of referential games makes agent behavior and symbol usage easy to interpret.  
- Interesting comparison between informed and agnostic agents: informed agents use multiple symbols, agnostic agents use fewer.  
- Limitation: the task can be reduced to image classification, making it similar to transfer learning.  
- Raises concerns about scalability and applicability to more complex communication tasks.  
- Suggestion: evaluate generalizability to more complex or dynamic environments.  
- Suggestion: extend framework to multi-agent or more nuanced communication and explore mapping learned symbols to human language.
