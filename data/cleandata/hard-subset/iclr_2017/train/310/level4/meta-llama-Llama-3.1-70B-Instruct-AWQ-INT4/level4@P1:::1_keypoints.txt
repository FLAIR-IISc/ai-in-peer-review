- Tackles an important problem: understanding communication between agents and whether machine problem-solving mirrors humans.
- Simple task design enables intuitive post-learning analysis and clear insight into communication dynamics.
- Strength: provides empirical insight into differences between informed and agnostic agents.
- Observed difference: informed agents use multiple symbols while agnostic agents rely on only two.
- Suggests domain knowledge can enrich agents’ decision-making and communication protocols.
- Limitation: task effectively reduces to image classification when paired images come from different categories.
- Concern: approach may resemble transfer learning and could be trained faster than with reinforcement learning.
- Recommendation: discuss scaling to more complex, realistic tasks and anticipated challenges.
- Suggest exploring applications in human-agent collaboration and natural language processing.
- Key author questions: why informed agents used multiple symbols; how to address the image-classification reduction and increase robustness; consider more complex protocols (symbol sequences, multi-step communication).
