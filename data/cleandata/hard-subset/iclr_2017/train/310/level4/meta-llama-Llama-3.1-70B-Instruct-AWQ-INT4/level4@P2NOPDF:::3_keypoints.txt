- Proposes a referential game with two agents: sender sees a binary target t and two images, sends a symbol; receiver uses the symbol and images to predict t, both rewarded for correct prediction.
- Agents are neural networks using pre-trained image feature vectors and are trained with REINFORCE.
- Experiments show agents converge to optimal policies and learn symbolic communications that convey meaningful concepts.
- A variant grounded on different image classes yields even more meaningful learned concepts.
- A multi-game setup alternates the sender between the referential game and a supervised image classification task, which further grounds symbols in meaningful concepts.
- Learning shared representations for communication in multi-agent setups is an important and challenging research direction, and this two-agent grounding-first approach is novel compared to sequence-to-sequence models.
- The paper’s task, framework, analyses, and visualizations are valuable contributions and justify acceptance.
- Request to clarify how the target variable t is incorporated into the sender network.
- Tables 1 and 2 use inconsistent percentage notation; the "obs-chance purity" column in Table 1 appears to have erroneous extremely small values.
- Minor typos to fix: "assest" → "assess" and "usufal" → "usual."
