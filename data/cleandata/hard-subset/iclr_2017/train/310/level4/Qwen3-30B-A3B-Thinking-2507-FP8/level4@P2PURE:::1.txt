

Thank you for the opportunity to review this work.  
Pros  
- The paper addresses a fundamental challenge in understanding communication between agents, a critical question as reinforcement learning applications expand. It revisits whether machine problem-solving approaches resemble human cognition.  
- The task design enables intuitive post-hoc analysis due to its simplicity.  
- It is insightful to observe that informed agents utilize multiple symbols for message transmission, whereas agnostic agents rely solely on two symbols.  
Cons  
- The task effectively reduces to image classification when the two transmitted images belong to distinct categories. Here, the symbols function as labels corresponding to the image classes that the second agent learns to assign. Consequently, the approach reduces to a transfer learning problem, which could likely be trained significantly faster than the proposed reinforcement learning method.
