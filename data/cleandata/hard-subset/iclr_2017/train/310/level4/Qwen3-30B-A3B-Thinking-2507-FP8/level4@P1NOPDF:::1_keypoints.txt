- Addresses a fundamental challenge in understanding multi-agent communication and whether machine problem-solving mirrors human cognition.  
- Task design is simple enough to allow intuitive post-hoc analysis of communication strategies.  
- Shows informed agents use multiple symbols while agnostic agents use only two, highlighting differences in communication efficiency.  
- Core task essentially reduces to standard image classification when transmitted images are from distinct categories.  
- Symbols effectively become class labels, turning the setup into a transfer learning problem.  
- A non-RL solution (e.g., pretrained classifier) could likely match or outperform the RL approach with much lower training cost.  
- This questions the necessity of the RL methodology and suggests the main contribution is analysis of communication rather than a novel RL method.
