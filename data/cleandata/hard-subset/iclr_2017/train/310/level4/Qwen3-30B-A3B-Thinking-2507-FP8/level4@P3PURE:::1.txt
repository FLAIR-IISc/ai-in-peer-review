

Thank you for the opportunity to review this work. The paper addresses a significant challenge in multi-agent reinforcement learning: understanding communication dynamics between agents. As RL applications expand, this research prompts a fundamental question about whether machine problem-solving strategies align with human cognition.

**Strengths**  
- The paper tackles a crucial problem with clear relevance to advancing RL theory.  
- The task design is sufficiently simple to enable intuitive post-learning analysis of communication patterns.  
- The observation that informed agents utilize multiple symbols for message transmission, while task-agnostic agents rely exclusively on two symbols, provides valuable empirical insight into communication complexity.  

**Weaknesses**  
The core task effectively reduces to image classification: when the two images transmitted belong to distinct categories, the symbols function as class labels that the second agent learns to associate with each image. Consequently, this approach fundamentally amounts to a transfer learning problem rather than emergent communication. Such a setup could likely be solved significantly faster using standard transfer learning techniques, undermining the necessity of the proposed reinforcement learning framework.
