- Introduces a referential game where two agents each see two images; the sender knows a binary target t and sends a symbolic message, and the receiver must predict the target.  
- Both agents receive joint reward when the receiver correctly predicts the target.  
- Agents are neural networks using pretrained image representations and are trained with REINFORCE.  
- Empirical results show agents converge to optimal policies.  
- Learned symbolic communications encode meaningful concepts.  
- A variant using distinct image classes yields even more semantically meaningful concepts.  
- Proposes a multi-task setup where the sender alternates between the referential game and supervised image classification.  
- Grounding communication in the supervised task produces messages with enhanced meaningfulness.  
- Learning shared communication in multi-agent settings is a compelling, more challenging direction than standard supervised or single-agent RL; the simple setup is justified.  
- Novelty: developing agent communication first and then grounding it in human language offers an alternative to sequence-to-sequence approaches.  
- The task framework, analyses, and visualizations provide a valuable foundation; reviewer recommends acceptance.
