- Introduces a referential communication game: sender observes two images + binary target t, transmits a symbolic message, receiver predicts t; both rewarded for correct prediction.  
- Agents are neural networks using pretrained image features and trained with REINFORCE.  
- Results show agents learn optimal communication policies and that transmitted symbols encode meaningful concepts.  
- Grounding on distinct image classes produces even more interpretable symbolic representations.  
- In a multi-game setup where the sender alternates between communication and supervised image classification, symbols become increasingly meaningful due to classification grounding.  
- Novel paradigm: learn agent-to-agent communication first, then ground to human language—distinct from sequence-to-sequence approaches that favor statistical patterns over functional semantics.  
- Contributions: clear experimental framework for grounded communication, rigorous analysis of learned symbols, and insightful semantic visualizations; reviewer recommends acceptance.
