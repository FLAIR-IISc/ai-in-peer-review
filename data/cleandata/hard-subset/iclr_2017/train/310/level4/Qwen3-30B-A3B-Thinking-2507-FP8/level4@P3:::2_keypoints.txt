- Introduces a multi-agent referential game framework to train language models, addressing limits of passive supervised learning.  
- Demonstrates that simple neural agents can develop effective communication protocols via interactive coordination.  
- Shows environmental modifications (e.g., removing low-level visual common knowledge) and supervised image-label grounding improve semantic coherence and human interpretability.  
- Bridges theoretical linguistics, game theory, and practical AI with clear empirical validation.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Strength: First rigorous demonstration that tabula rasa agents can develop communication aligned with high-level semantic properties without pre-coded language.  
- Strength: Grounding via supervised ImageNet labels bridges emergent agent language to human-interpretable terms, validated by crowdsourced evaluation.  
- Agents reach near-perfect coordination within ~10k training rounds; informed sender yields greater symbol diversity and semantic purity than agnostic sender.  
- SVD and t-SNE analyses indicate symbols correlate with high-level categories (e.g., living vs. non-living), with purity far above chance.  
- Grounding increases symbol purity to ~70% and yields ~68% human interpretability; agents generalize to novel images using metonymic reasoning.  
- Methodology is rigorous with ablation studies, appropriate baselines, transparent hyperparameters, and clear positioning relative to prior work.  
- Figure 1 needs a clearer caption mapping the "game-specific embedding" layers, filter operations (especially informed sender), and image-order handling to the text.  
- SVD analysis requires justification: clarify how the 50-dimensional SVD was cross-validated and why this implies non-redundant (vs. synonymous) symbol usage.  
- Human evaluation protocol should report details beyond 68% accuracy (e.g., inter-rater reliability and error analysis).  
- Clarifying Q1: Specify how the shared embedding transfers supervised classification knowledge to the referential game and whether supervised labels are used during play.  
- Clarifying Q2: Explain whether two-symbol agents' living vs. non-living split is a limitation or an expected/acceptable outcome for intuitive meaning assignment.  
- Clarifying Q3: For the ReferItGame extension, clarify whether using all 100 symbols resulted from the larger image set or an adjusted vocabulary size.  
- Overall assessment: A valuable step toward interactive language learning with a promising grounding strategy; minor methodological clarifications would strengthen the paper.
