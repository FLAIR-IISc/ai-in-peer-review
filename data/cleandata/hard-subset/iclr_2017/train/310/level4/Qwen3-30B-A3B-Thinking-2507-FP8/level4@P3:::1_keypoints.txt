- Framework for language emergence using multi-agent referential games where simple neural nets develop communication without pre-defined language.
- Game environment engineering encourages emergent communication with semantic properties.
- Novel grounding strategy: combine referential game with supervised image classification to align emergent symbols with natural language.
- Experiments are rigorous and well-motivated by AI and linguistic literature.
- Decision: Accept — strong contribution toward interactive communication and human-like language capabilities.
- Agents learn to coordinate via reinforcement learning; informed sender produces more diverse symbol usage than agnostic sender.
- Emergent symbols correlate with high-level conceptual categories rather than low-level visual features, suggesting semantic organization.
- Grounded protocols are partially human-interpretable; human evaluation achieved 68% accuracy on target identification.
- Paper effectively bridges theoretical language emergence studies and practical AI implementation/evaluation.
- Additional feedback: need deeper analysis of why the informed sender outperforms the agnostic variant (effects of inductive bias).
- Additional feedback: analyze how communication protocol evolves during training (time-series of symbol usage and semantic purity).
- Question: how to distinguish language reflecting intuitive semantics from correlations with visual data distribution?
- Question: what limits human interpretability (68%) and what training changes could increase it without hurting coordination?
- Question: how generalizable are observed metonymic uses (e.g., "dolphin" for sea) and request for more examples of semantic shifts.
