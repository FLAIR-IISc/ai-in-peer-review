- Proposes a multi-agent communication framework using referential games where sender and receiver coordinate to identify target images.  
- Shows simple neural networks can develop communication protocols through interaction rather than supervised learning.  
- Explores game design choices that encourage emergence of more semantically meaningful language.  
- Introduces a grounding strategy combining interactive gameplay with supervised learning to align agent communication with natural language.  
- Demonstrates agents converge to successful coordination in experiments.  
- Analysis indicates emergent symbols capture high-level semantic/conceptual categories rather than low-level visual features.  
- Grounding approach yields communication protocols that are at least partially interpretable by humans.  
- Well-motivated work that situates itself within literature on language emergence, multi-agent systems, and game theory.  
- Experimental setup is clear, uses appropriate baselines, and provides thorough analysis of emergent protocols.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant contribution showing interactive emergence of language-like communication.  
- Suggested improvement: provide more detail on training dynamics and convergence rates across architectures.  
- Suggested improvement: discuss limitations and scalability of the approach to more complex environments.  
- Requests clarification: how do convolutional layers in the informed sender specifically promote more semantically meaningful symbols versus the agnostic sender?  
- Requests clarification/evidence: will the observed semantic properties generalize to more complex, real-world communication beyond the referential game?
