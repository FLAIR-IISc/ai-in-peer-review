

This paper introduces a referential game between two agents. Both agents observe two images. The sender agent receives a binary target variable (t) and transmits a symbol (message) to the receiver agent, whose goal is to recover the target. Both agents receive a reward if the receiver accurately predicts the target. The authors parametrize the agents as neural networks using pretrained image representations as feature vectors and train them via REINFORCE. The results demonstrate that the agents converge to optimal policies, and their learned communications (e.g., symbolic codes) encode meaningful concepts. The paper further presents experiments on a variant grounded in distinct image classes, where agents learn even more interpretable concepts. Additionally, a multi-game setup is proposed where the sender alternates between the referential game and a supervised image classification task. When anchored to the classification task, the symbolic communications exhibit enhanced meaningfulness.  

The exploration of shared representation learning for communication in multi-agent systems represents a valuable research direction, particularly given its increased complexity relative to standard supervised learning or single-agent reinforcement learning. The paper’s focus on a simplified task is well-justified. To the best of my knowledge, the approach of first learning agent communication and subsequently grounding it in human language is novel. As the authors note, this could offer an alternative paradigm to sequence-to-sequence models, which often prioritize statistical language properties over functional semantics. The proposed task, framework, and the analysis/visualization of communicated token semantics constitute a useful foundation for future work. I therefore recommend acceptance.
