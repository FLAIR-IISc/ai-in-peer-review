

Thank you for this interesting submission.  

**Strengths**  
- The paper addresses a fundamental challenge in understanding multi-agent communication, a critical question as reinforcement learning applications proliferate. It compellingly prompts us to consider whether machine problem-solving approaches mirror human cognition.  
- The task design is sufficiently simple to enable intuitive post-hoc analysis of communication strategies.  
- Notably, the authors demonstrate how *informed* agents leverage multiple symbols to encode messages, while *agnostic* agents rely exclusively on two symbols—a distinction that offers valuable insight into communication efficiency.  

**Concerns**  
The core task reduces to a standard image classification problem: if the two transmitted images belong to distinct categories, the symbols function as class labels that the second agent learns to associate with each image. Consequently, this approach essentially constitutes a transfer learning problem. A non-RL solution (e.g., pretraining a classifier on the same image pairs) could likely achieve equivalent or superior results with significantly reduced training overhead compared to the proposed reinforcement learning framework. This undermines the necessity of the RL methodology for the stated task, suggesting the paper’s contribution may be more about communication analysis than a novel RL approach.
