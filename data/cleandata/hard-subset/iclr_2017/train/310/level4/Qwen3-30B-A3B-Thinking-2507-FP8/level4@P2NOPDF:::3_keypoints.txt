- Introduces a referential game where the sender sees two images and a binary target t, sends one symbol, and the receiver predicts t for a shared reward.  
- Agents are neural networks using pretrained image features and are trained with REINFORCE.  
- Results show agents converge to near-optimal policies and learned symbols encode interpretable visual concepts.  
- Extension with distinct image classes yields more semantically meaningful symbol representations.  
- Multi-game setup alternates the sender between the referential game and supervised image classification, increasing symbol interpretability when grounded in classification.  
- Addresses the hard problem of learning shared communication protocols in multi-agent RL and proposes grounding communication in language as a novel paradigm.  
- Contributions: new task framework, analysis of learned symbols, and visualizations of semantic content—deemed a meaningful stepping stone warranting acceptance.
