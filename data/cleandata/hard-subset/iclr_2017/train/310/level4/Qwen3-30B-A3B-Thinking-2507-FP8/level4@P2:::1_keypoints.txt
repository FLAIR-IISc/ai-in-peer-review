- Introduces a multi-agent referential-game framework showing emergent communication via RL without pre-defined protocols.  
- Demonstrates environment modifications (e.g., removing low-level visual common knowledge) push symbols toward high-level semantic categories.  
- Proposes a supervised grounding strategy to map emergent symbols to human language.  
- Decision: Weak Accept — meaningful contribution with strong empirical support but limited scope and grounding requires deeper validation.  
- Empirical result: tabula rasa agents can develop coordinated communication; informed sender shows higher semantic purity (70% vs 40%) and uses many more symbols (≈88 vs 2).  
- Motivation is well-grounded in linguistics and game theory and distinct from human-in-the-loop or pre-programmed methods.  
- Experiments: symbol clusters align with McRae categories, t-SNE visualizations reflect high-level semantics, and grounding yields 68% human interpretability (vs 1% chance).  
- Evidence robustness: includes appropriate baselines, statistical validation, and ablation studies.  
- Main limitation: gap between simplified referential games and real-world conversational AI (multi-turn, noisy inputs not addressed).  
- Human-grounding is promising but modest (32% failures unexplained); deeper qualitative analysis is needed.  
- Clarification: the claim that the task "boils down to image classification" is misleading — agents learn symbolic communication for high-level categories, not mere labels.  
- Vocabulary concerns: 100-symbol lexicon is small relative to natural language, and handling ambiguity/open-ended references is unexplored.  
- Suggestion: analyze failed crowdsourcing cases with qualitative examples to understand misinterpretations.  
- Suggestion: discuss scalability to multi-turn dialogue and much larger vocabularies (e.g., 1,000+ symbols).  
- Suggestion: compare to strong RL baselines (e.g., Sukhbaatar et al., 2016) to quantify grounding efficiency gains.  
- Suggestion: add a quantitative "semantic naturalness" metric (e.g., human-rated coherence) beyond purity.  
- Clarifying Q1: How do you ensure symbol usage isn't high synonymy (multiple symbols for the same concept)?  
- Clarifying Q2: How did the grounded agent generalize to unseen categories in ReferItGame—did it rely on metonymy and can that be refined?  
- Clarifying Q3: What additional steps would you propose to raise human interpretability from 68% to >90%?
