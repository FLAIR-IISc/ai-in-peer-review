

The paper presents a well-motivated approach to training natural language systems through multi-agent referential communication games, a direction that holds significant promise given the relatively sparse prior work in this area. The authors' framework effectively leverages interactive multi-agent scenarios to foster communication skills, and the proposed integration of game-based training with supervised learning (as demonstrated in Section 5 and outlined in Section 6) appears particularly compelling and promising for future work.  

The core framework (Section 2) is clearly articulated and demonstrates practical utility across multiple experimental settings. The results are reasonable and align with the proposed methodology, suggesting the approach is both viable and potentially impactful for advancing embodied language learning. The authors’ vision for future extensions (Section 6) also appears thoughtfully grounded.  

However, Figure 1 requires significant revision to enhance clarity. The diagram’s current schematic—while intended to simplify the architecture—omits critical network connections and lacks descriptive labels or a caption. Given the highly customized nature of the architecture and the dense description in Section 3, the figure currently exacerbates confusion rather than resolving it. To improve accessibility, the authors should either refine the diagram to explicitly show all key connections or, at a minimum, add a detailed caption explaining each component and its role in the system. Without these changes, readers will struggle to map the visual to the textual description.  

A notable limitation is the author’s unfamiliarity with the most closely related literature. While I am confident in my understanding of the paper’s technical contributions (as reflected in my confidence score), I lack sufficient expertise to rigorously assess how this work situates itself within the broader context of recent multi-agent communication research. This gap prevents a thorough evaluation of novelty or comparative significance, though it does not undermine the paper’s technical soundness.  

Overall, this is a strong submission with a promising methodological contribution. The framework is well-designed, the results are credible, and the proposed training strategy offers a valuable direction for future research. With minor revisions to clarify the architecture (Figure 1) and a more precise contextualization of related work, this paper would make a meaningful contribution to the field.
