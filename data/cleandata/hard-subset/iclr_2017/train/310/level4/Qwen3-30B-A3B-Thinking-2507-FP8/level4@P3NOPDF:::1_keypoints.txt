- Timely investigation into multi-agent communication dynamics and whether machine strategies mirror human cognitive processes.  
- Core contribution: focus on communication efficiency between agents, an underexplored and important area for RL.  
- Strong task design: simple yet rich image-communication task enabling intuitive post-learning analysis.  
- Compelling empirical finding: informed agents use multiple symbols for nuanced distinctions while agnostic agents converge on a binary symbol system.  
- Task effectively isolates communication as the primary experimental variable.  
- Major concern: task reduces to standard image classification—symbols act as proxies for image classes, making communication redundant.  
- RL framework appears unnecessarily complex; supervised classifiers could achieve identical results with far less data and compute.  
- Authors fail to distinguish their approach from conventional classification pipelines or justify the need for RL.  
- Required revisions: redesign the task to prevent direct mapping of symbols to classes (e.g., partial observability/ambiguity) or justify RL by comparing against supervised baselines.  
- Recommendation: reject unless the authors address the reducibility to classification and demonstrate that emergent communication goes beyond mere label transfer.
