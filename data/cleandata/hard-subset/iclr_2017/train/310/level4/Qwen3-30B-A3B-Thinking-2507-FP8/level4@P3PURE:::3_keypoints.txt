- Introduces a referential game: sender gets binary target t, sends a symbolic message, receiver predicts t; both rewarded on success.
- Agents parameterized as neural networks using pretrained image features and trained via REINFORCE.
- Results show agents converge to optimal policies and learn meaningful symbolic communications.
- A variant grounded on distinct image classes produces even more interpretable concepts.
- Multi-game setup (alternating referential game and supervised classification) grounds symbols, making communications more semantically meaningful.
- Addresses the important challenge of learning shared communication protocols in multi-agent settings, beyond supervised or single-agent RL.
- Proposes a novel paradigm of first learning agent communication then grounding it in human language, contrasting with seq2seq approaches.
- Contributions (task, framework, analysis/visualization of tokens) are valuable; reviewer recommends acceptance.
