- Addresses a fundamental challenge in understanding communication dynamics between agents.  
- Revisits whether machine problem-solving strategies mirror human cognition as RL applications expand.  
- Simple task design enables intuitive post-training analysis.  
- Empirical finding: informed agents use multiple symbols while agnostic agents restrict communication to exactly two symbols.  
- Major critique: when transmitted images come from distinct categories, the task reduces to image classification/transfer learning—symbols act as class identifiers and the RL framework may be unnecessarily inefficient.
