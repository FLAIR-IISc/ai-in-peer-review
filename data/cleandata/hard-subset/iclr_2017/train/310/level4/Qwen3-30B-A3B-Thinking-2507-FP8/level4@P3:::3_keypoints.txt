- Introduces a multi-agent referential-game framework to develop interactive language capabilities in AI.  
- Shows simple neural networks trained with reinforcement learning can spontaneously form a shared communication protocol reflecting high-level semantic properties.  
- Demonstrates that removing low-level visual common knowledge and adding supervised image classification grounds the emergent language in human-understandable terms.  
- Argues the approach addresses limitations of language models trained on static text by emphasizing functional, interactive communication.  
- Key contributions: (1) novel paradigm for bootstrapping language via multi-agent interaction, (2) empirical evidence of alignment with human semantic intuition, (3) scalable path to human-interpretable communication.  
- Recommendation: Accept for conceptual novelty and rigorous empirical validation.  
- Empirical finding: tabula rasa agents achieve near-perfect coordination (99%+ accuracy) across architectures and vocabulary sizes within ~10k training rounds.  
- Semantic improvements: removing low-level common knowledge raises symbol purity by ~12%; supervised grounding yields 68% human-interpretable symbols in crowdsourced tests.  
- Results are systematically linked to design choices (informed vs agnostic senders, class-level vs image-level inputs), supporting causal claims.  
- Strong literature positioning: distinguishes from pre-programmed, human-in-the-loop, and scripted approaches and builds on Lewis signaling games and prior emergent-communication work.  
- Revision request: clarify how the sender’s target input "t" is encoded.  
- Revision request: standardize table formatting (consistent [0,1] or [0,100] reporting with clear labels).  
- Revision request: refine the purity metric description and specify how the random baseline is computed (cite Zhao & Karypis 2003).  
- Revision request: expand discussion of the informed sender’s larger vocabulary (“aggressive” usage) and its role in semantic generalization.  
- Clarification needed: report convergence stability across random seeds (variance/standard deviation in test accuracy).  
- Clarification needed: describe how human interpretability/generalization applies to unseen categories outside supervised classes.  
- Conclusion: elegant, powerful framework with rigorous experiments; minor clarifications would strengthen an already strong paper.
