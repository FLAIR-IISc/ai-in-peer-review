- Introduces a referential game where a sender, given a binary target t and two images, sends a symbol to a receiver who must recover the target.  
- Agents are neural networks using pretrained image features and are trained with REINFORCE.  
- Experiments show agents converge to optimal policies and learned symbols encode meaningful concepts.  
- A variant with distinct image classes yields even more interpretable symbolic concepts.  
- A multi-game setup alternates the referential game with supervised classification; anchoring to classification makes communications more meaningful.  
- The paper explores shared representation learning for multi-agent communication and proposes a novel approach to grounding communication in human language, offering an alternative to seq2seq paradigms.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.
