- Addresses an important, timely question about how multi-agent communication in RL aligns with human cognition.  
- Experimental design is simple and interpretable, enabling intuitive post-hoc analysis of communication dynamics.  
- Key finding: informed agents use multiple symbols for nuanced information, while agnostic agents default to a binary symbol system.  
- Major concern: the task effectively reduces to standard image classification, weakening the RL framing.  
- When images map to distinct categories, sender symbols act as direct class labels, meaning the problem can be solved as classification rather than a communication-specific RL challenge.  
- Supervised or transfer learning methods could likely achieve similar or better results with fewer samples and less computation, so RL appears unnecessary without justification.  
- Recommendation: either redesign the task to prevent reduction to classification (e.g., add ambiguity/dynamics) or include explicit comparisons between RL and supervised baselines to justify using RL.
