- Introduces a multi-agent referential game framework where two agents develop a shared communication protocol to identify target images.  
- Shows simple neural networks can learn effective communication without pre-defined linguistic structures.  
- Environmental changes (pixel- to class-level representations) steer emergent language toward higher-level, human-interpretable semantics.  
- Hybrid training with supervised image classification grounds agent symbols in human concepts and improves interpretability.  
- Strong empirical evidence that emergent symbols encode conceptual properties rather than low-level visual features.  
- Grounding experiment maps emergent symbols to human language labels while preserving agent coordination.  
- Decision: Accept — the paper establishes a viable paradigm for functional language acquisition via interaction.  
- Both “agnostic” and “informed” senders reach near-perfect coordination; informed sender converges faster and uses richer symbol sets.  
- Purity metrics and t-SNE visualizations show symbol-object clusters significantly exceed chance and group semantically similar objects.  
- Object-level reference modification raises semantic purity by ~15%, forcing reliance on higher-level categories.  
- Grounding yields ~70% symbol purity and ~68% human interpretability in crowdsourced tests, far above chance.  
- Qualitative “metonymic” symbol usage (e.g., “dolphin” for sea) indicates meaningful generalization beyond supervised labels.  
- Approach avoids scalability/engineering issues of human-in-the-loop or scripted-environment methods and gives a path to human-aligned communication.  
- Request to clarify how the target input t ∈ {L,R} is encoded (visual marker vs separate binary input) in Section 3.  
- Recommend standardizing Table 1/2 formatting to percentages for consistency and readability.  
- Suggest using “emergent language” or “symbolic communication” instead of “symbolic code” for terminology accuracy.  
- Ask for details on whether the 68% human accuracy includes out-of-vocabulary/test pairs or only supervised-category pairs.  
- Key question: How does visual marking of the target affect learning versus a purely symbolic cue, and could agents coordinate without it?  
- Key question: How does the expansion to 88 symbols correlate with the diversity of supervised labels (100 total, 100 used)?  
- Key question: Provide qualitative examples of symbols that generalize to novel image pairs to illustrate metonymic reasoning.
