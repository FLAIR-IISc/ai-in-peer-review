- Addresses a significant challenge in multi-agent RL: understanding communication dynamics and whether machine strategies mirror human cognition.  
- Tackles a crucial problem with clear relevance to advancing RL theory.  
- Uses a simple task design that enables intuitive post-learning analysis of communication patterns.  
- Empirical finding: informed agents use multiple symbols for messages, while task-agnostic agents rely solely on two symbols, highlighting differences in communication complexity.  
- Key weakness: the task reduces to image classification/transfer learning—symbols act as class labels, so standard transfer learning could likely solve it faster, undermining the claim of emergent communication via RL.
