It's great to see that natural language systems are being trained by placing multiple agents within an interactive, referential communication game. As the authors mentioned, there has been some (though not much visible) previous work on using multi-agent games to teach communication, and this certainly seems like a promising direction to pursue. Furthermore, switching between these games and some supervised learning approaches, as described in Experiment 5 and suggested in Experiment 6, seem particularly promising.
