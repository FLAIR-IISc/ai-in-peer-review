This paper proposes an interesting and valuable framework for training natural language systems by situating multiple agents within an interactive, referential communication game. The core idea is compelling, and as the authors note, while there is some precedent for using multi-agent games to develop communication, the area remains ripe for exploration. This work represents a promising step in a worthwhile direction. Furthermore, the hybrid approach of alternating between these communication games and standard supervised learning, as demonstrated in the experiment in Section 5 and proposed in Section 6, seems particularly fruitful.

Overall, the paper has several key strengths. The general framework outlined in Section 2 is well-conceived and appears to be a versatile and effective tool for this line of research. The experimental results are reasonable and provide solid evidence for the efficacy of the proposed method. Finally, the paper successfully opens up several avenues for interesting future work, and it is easy to envision how other researchers might build upon this foundation with their own variations and extensions.

The primary weakness of the paper, however, lies in the clarity of its presentation, specifically regarding the model architecture. Figure 1, which is intended to illustrate the architecture, is a source of considerable confusion. It appears that some network connections have been omitted for simplification, but this choice unfortunately backfires. Instead of clarifying the model, the shorthand diagram becomes ambiguous and difficult to interpret.

This issue is compounded by the description in Section 3. The text describes a highly customized architecture that is, in its own right, somewhat hard to follow. The combination of a complex textual description and an ambiguous diagram makes it challenging for the reader to gain a confident understanding of the model's precise structure. To significantly improve the paper, the authors should revise Figure 1 to be more detailed and accurate. At the absolute minimum, a comprehensive caption must be added to explain the components of the figure, what the various connections represent, and which connections, if any, have been omitted for clarity. This would greatly aid the reader in interpreting the model and appreciating the paper's technical contribution.

In summary, this is a good paper with a valuable central idea. The multi-agent communication framework is a promising research direction that is well worth pursuing. The paper would be significantly strengthened by improving the clarity of the architectural description and, most importantly, the corresponding diagram.
