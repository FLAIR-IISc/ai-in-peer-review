### Review

This paper investigates the interesting and crucial problem of emergent communication between two artificial agents. By situating the work within a simple, cooperative task, the authors explore how agents develop a communication protocol, touching upon the fundamental question of whether machine problem-solving strategies resemble those of humans.

**Strengths**

*   The paper tackles the important and timely problem of understanding how communication can emerge between agents. As reinforcement learning finds broader application, this work brings a welcome focus back to fundamental questions about machine intelligence.
*   The simplicity of the experimental task is a strength, as it allows for an intuitive and clear post-learning analysis of the emergent communication protocol.
*   A particularly interesting finding is the observation that "informed" agents learn to utilize multiple symbols to transmit their message, whereas "agnostic" agents develop a simpler protocol relying on only two symbols.

**Weaknesses**

*   The primary weakness is that the experimental task can be simplified considerably. In the scenario where the two images sent to the agents are from different categories, the task effectively reduces to an image classification problem. The symbols in the communication channel function as class labels that the second agent learns to assign to the correct image. Consequently, the problem can be reframed as a transfer learning task. It is likely that a direct supervised learning approach would be significantly more sample-efficient and train much faster than the reinforcement learning algorithm presented.
