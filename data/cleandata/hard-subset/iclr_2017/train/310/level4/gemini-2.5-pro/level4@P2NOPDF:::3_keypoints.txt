- Proposes a referential game with a sender and receiver: both see two images, sender given binary target t, sender sends a discrete symbol, receiver must identify the target.  
- Agents are neural networks operating on pre-trained image features and trained end-to-end with REINFORCE.  
- Experiments show agents converge to optimal policies under this setup.  
- Learned communication protocols develop meaningful, disentangled concepts related to image properties.  
- Extension 1: grounding the game on distinct image classes yields more interpretable concepts.  
- Extension 2: alternating the sender between the referential game and supervised image classification (multi-game) further anchors representations and produces clearer semantic structure.  
- Authors claim novelty in learning protocol from scratch and grounding it in human-understandable concepts as an alternative to seq2seq approaches.  
- Reviewer deems the task/framework and analyses valuable and recommends acceptance.  
- Request for clarification: how exactly is the target variable t input to the sender network?  
- Presentation issue: inconsistent percentage scales between Table 1 ([0,100]) and Table 2 ([0,1]); please standardize.  
- Data/check issue: "obs-chance purity" values in Table 1 seem extremely small—please confirm correctness or typographical error.  
- Minor typos to correct: "assest" -> "assess", "usufal" -> "usual".
