- Addresses the timely problem of how communication protocols emerge between artificial agents and whether machine problem-solving parallels human cognition.  
- Task design is simple and interpretable, enabling straightforward analysis of learned communication protocols.  
- Key finding: informed agents use multiple symbols to convey information, while agnostic agents converge to a minimal two-symbol system.  
- Main criticism: the experimental task can be reduced to a standard image classification problem when the two images are from different categories.  
- In that framing, communicated symbols effectively act as class labels and the receiver simply learns symbol-image associations.  
- Suggests the setup resembles transfer learning, where one agent generates a feature (the symbol) used by another, and supervised transfer learning might solve it more efficiently than the RL approach.
