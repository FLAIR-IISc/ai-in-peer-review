- Proposes a referential game where sender and receiver agents learn a communication protocol from scratch via reinforcement learning.
- Core contributions: (1) agents can coordinate and develop communication; (2) forcing class-level reference yields more abstract, semantically-structured language; (3) grounding emergent symbols via joint supervised labeling makes language interpretable to humans.
- Decision: Accept — the paper is a valuable, well-motivated contribution with a simple, elegant experimental setup.
- Strong motivation to study interactive, functional language emergence beyond passive supervised learning.
- The referential game is a clean, effective testbed demonstrating emergent coordination from a shared reward.
- Analysis tools (symbol purity, visualizations) provide solid evidence for claims and show benefits of the grounding strategy.
- Informed vs. agnostic sender architectures reveal how inductive biases affect emergent vocabulary richness.
- Showing different instances of the same class forces abstraction and alignment with high-level semantic categories.
- Grounding via joint supervised + interactive training yields conventional and metaphorical usages that humans can interpret (68% accuracy on novel uses).
- Main concern: setup may encourage solving distributed classification rather than true, compositional communication.
- Suggestion: include harder discrimination tasks (e.g., same-class contrasts) to push for attribute-level or compositional symbols.
- Request deeper analysis of the agnostic sender’s 2-symbol living vs. non-living distinction (e.g., purity score, example categorizations).
- Reproducibility requests: report results averaged over multiple seeds with means and standard deviations due to REINFORCE variance.
- Ask for clarification on sensitivity to the Gibbs temperature (set to 10) and any observed training instability.
