- Addresses a fundamental question: how shared symbolic protocols emerge in cooperative multi-agent settings, timely for multi-agent RL research.
- Experimental design is simple and clear, making emergent protocols and symbol meanings transparent and easy to interpret.
- Key empirical finding: prior knowledge shapes protocol complexity — informed agents develop multi-symbol, more expressive protocols; agnostic agents converge to a minimal two-symbol system.
- Major concern: when the two images are from different categories, the task effectively reduces to supervised image classification, weakening the need for an RL emergent-communication setup.
- The problem could be reframed as transfer learning or solved by training a classifier and using class labels as symbols; a supervised baseline would likely converge faster and more reliably.
- Paper should include discussion and empirical comparison against supervised/classifier baselines to justify the choice of reinforcement learning for emergent communication.
- Overall: interesting, well-designed experiment with a clear result, but impact is limited unless task formulation and baseline comparisons are addressed.
