- Proposes a multi-agent referential game where tabula rasa sender and receiver agents learn a communication protocol via reinforcement learning to identify a target image from a pair.  
- Shows agents can converge to an optimal policy and effectively create their own symbolic language.  
- Demonstrates the emergent protocol captures meaningful semantic concepts from images rather than only low-level visual features.  
- Shows environment manipulations (removing low-level details, enforcing object-class coordination) steer the emergent language toward more abstract, generalizable concepts.  
- Proposes grounding emergent agent language in natural language by combining the game with supervised image classification, yielding partially human-interpretable symbols transferable to novel objects.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Key reason: Novel, well-motivated approach that frames language learning as functional, goal-driven multi-agent coordination rather than supervised correlation learning.  
- Key reason: Thoughtfully designed experiments and analyses provide strong empirical support and an effective grounding method.  
- Strength: Frames language learning as cooperative multi-agent coordination, a more realistic and challenging setting than standard supervised tasks.  
- Strength: Thorough experimental validation using purity scores, SVD, and human evaluation to show semantic structure and generalization of grounded language.  
- Strength/novelty: First to let communication protocols emerge between agents then ground them in natural language, providing a stepping stone for human-AI interaction research.
