**Pros**

*   This paper addresses the crucial problem of understanding communication between two agents. Amidst the growing exploration of reinforcement learning applications, this work revisits the fundamental question of whether machines develop problem-solving approaches similar to those of humans.
*   The simplicity of the proposed task facilitates an intuitive post-learning analysis of the agents' behavior.
*   A particularly interesting finding is the contrast between "informed" agents, which utilized multiple symbols to transmit information, and "agnostic" agents, which relied on only two.

**Cons**

*   When the two images shown to the agents are from different categories, the task effectively reduces to an image classification problem. The symbols exchanged between agents function as class labels, which the second agent learns to assign to the corresponding images. Consequently, the proposed method is analogous to a transfer learning problem that could likely be trained more efficiently than the reinforcement learning algorithm employed here.
