The proposal to train natural language systems through an interactive, multi-agent referential communication game is compelling. As the authors note, while there is some prior work on using multi-agent games to teach communication, this research direction is promising and worth pursuing. Furthermore, the hybrid approach of alternating between these games and supervised learning, as demonstrated in Section 5 and proposed for future work in Section 6, seems particularly fruitful.

The clarity of the paper is hampered by Figure 1. While some network connections may have been omitted for simplicity, this abstraction—combined with a complex, highly-customized architecture and a description in Section 3 that is difficult to follow—ultimately creates confusion rather than clarity. The diagram should be revised for better readability. At a minimum, a detailed caption is essential to help the reader interpret the figure and prevent potential misinterpretation.

Overall, the proposed framework is strong and appears to be both effective and broadly applicable. The results are solid, and the paper opens up several promising avenues for future research.

While I am confident in my understanding of the paper's core contributions, I am not sufficiently familiar with the most closely related literature to definitively assess the work's novelty or its precise positioning within the field.
