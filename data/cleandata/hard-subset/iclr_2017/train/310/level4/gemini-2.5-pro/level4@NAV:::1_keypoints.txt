- Tackles the relevant problem of emergent communication between two agents, probing whether machines develop human-like problem-solving strategies.  
- Simple experimental setup allows clear and intuitive analysis of the learned communication protocol.  
- Key finding: informed agents develop a multi-symbol vocabulary, whereas agnostic agents rely on a binary symbolic system.  
- Main limitation: task can be reduced to image classification/transfer learning (symbols act as class labels), so supervised approaches could be more efficient than the RL framework.
