- Proposes a referential game where a sender and receiver observe image pairs; the sender gets a binary target and sends a discrete symbol for the receiver to identify the target.
- Agents are neural networks using pre-trained image features and are trained with REINFORCE using a shared reward for correct identification.
- Empirically, agents converge to optimal policies in this setting.
- Learned communication protocols encode meaningful visual concepts.
- Training with image pairs from different classes encourages more semantically meaningful symbols.
- A multi-game setup alternates the referential game with supervised image classification, which further grounds symbols in clear semantic meanings.
- The approach is novel and significant as it develops communication from scratch and grounds it in human-interpretable concepts, offering an alternative to seq2seq models.
- Recommendation: accept.
