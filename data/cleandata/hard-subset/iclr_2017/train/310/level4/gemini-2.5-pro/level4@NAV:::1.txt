Thank you for this thought-provoking submission.

**Strengths**
- This work addresses the highly relevant and fundamental problem of emergent communication between two agents. In an era of expanding reinforcement learning applications, the paper laudably returns to the foundational question of whether machines develop problem-solving strategies analogous to those of humans.
- The simplicity of the experimental setup is a significant strength, as it permits a clear and intuitive analysis of the learned communication protocol.
- The observation that informed agents developed a multi-symbol vocabulary, in contrast to agnostic agents who relied on a binary symbolic system, is a particularly insightful finding.

**Weaknesses**
- The primary limitation is that the proposed task can be simplified to an image classification problem, particularly when the two presented images belong to distinct categories. In this formulation, the symbols effectively act as class labels that the second agent learns to associate with the corresponding image. Consequently, the framework is analogous to a transfer learning problem, which could potentially be trained with greater efficiency than the chosen reinforcement learning approach.
