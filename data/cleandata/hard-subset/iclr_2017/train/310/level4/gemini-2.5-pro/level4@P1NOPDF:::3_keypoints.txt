- Proposes a referential game where a sender (given target t) and receiver observe two images and the sender transmits a symbolic message so the receiver can pick the target.  
- Agents are neural networks using pre-trained image features and trained end-to-end with REINFORCE to maximize shared reward for correct prediction.  
- Empirically the agents converge to optimal policies and develop a communication protocol that maps to meaningful visual concepts.  
- When the two images are from different classes, the emergent symbols correspond to even more interpretable class-level concepts.  
- Introducing a multi-game setup where the sender alternates between the referential game and supervised image classification further grounds representations and yields clearer visual concepts.  
- Framing: learning a communication protocol first then grounding it in human-interpretable concepts is novel and offers an alternative to sequence-to-sequence approaches.
