- Tackles the important, timely problem of emergent communication between agents and whether machine strategies mirror human problem-solving.  
- Uses a simple cooperative task that enables intuitive, clear post-learning analysis of emergent protocols.  
- Finds that "informed" agents use multiple symbols to convey messages while "agnostic" agents converge on a simpler two-symbol protocol.  
- Critiques that when images are from different categories, the task reduces to image classification and effectively becomes a transfer learning problem.  
- Argues a supervised learning approach would likely be far more sample-efficient and faster to train than the presented reinforcement learning method.
