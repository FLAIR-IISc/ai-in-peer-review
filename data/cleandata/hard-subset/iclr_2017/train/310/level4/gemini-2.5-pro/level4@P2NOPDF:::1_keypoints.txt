- Focuses on emergence of communication protocols in cooperative multi-agent settings, asking if machines develop human-like problem-solving and communication.
- Experimental task is simple and well-designed, allowing clear, intuitive post-hoc analysis of learned communication strategies.
- Key finding: informed agents use multi-symbol protocols for nuanced information while agnostic agents converge to a minimal two-symbol system, showing prior knowledge shapes language evolution.
- The task can be reframed as a simpler ML problem, which may limit the generalizability of conclusions about emergent communication.
- In cases where the two images are from different categories, the problem essentially reduces to supervised image classification with symbols acting as category labels.
- Questions whether reinforcement learning is necessary here, since a supervised or transfer learning approach could likely solve the task more sample-efficiently; authors should justify the RL choice and its unique insights.
