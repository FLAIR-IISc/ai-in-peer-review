- Proposes a multi-agent cooperative referential game where a sender sends one symbol to identify a target image to a receiver; agents are neural nets trained with REINFORCE using pre-trained image features.

- Agents successfully learn to communicate and converge to optimal policies in the task.

- Emergent symbolic language captures meaningful semantic concepts according to analysis.

- Showing sender and receiver different instances of the same class encourages learning of more abstract, generalizable concepts.

- A multi-task setup alternating gameplay with supervised image classification grounds the emergent language in human labels and makes communication more interpretable.

- Combined training enables generalization to novel concepts understandable by human evaluators.

- The approach is novel and compelling as an alternative to seq2seq models by focusing on functional, goal-oriented language emergence.

- Experiments are well-designed and analyses (purity metric, t-SNE visualizations) provide valuable evidence supporting claims.

- Recommendation: accept — problem is important, well-motivated, and a solid foundation for future work.

- Request clarification on how the target indicator t is provided to the sender (input order vs explicit t).

- Minor fixes requested: consistent table formatting/scales, double-check "obs-chance purity" values, and correct minor typos.
