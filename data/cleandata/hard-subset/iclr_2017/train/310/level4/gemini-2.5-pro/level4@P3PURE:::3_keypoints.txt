- Proposes a referential game with a sender and receiver who observe two images; sender knows a binary target t and sends one symbol to help receiver infer t.  
- Both agents are neural networks taking pretrained image features and are trained end-to-end with REINFORCE using a shared reward for correct prediction.  
- Empirical result: agents converge to optimal policies in this setting.  
- Analysis shows the emergent symbolic communication encodes meaningful visual concepts.  
- Grounding the referential task in distinct image classes encourages emergence of more semantically meaningful symbols.  
- Multi-game setup (alternating referential game and supervised image classification) anchors representations and makes the protocol more interpretable and human-aligned.  
- Framing the problem and approach is novel and a valuable alternative to sequence-to-sequence models, motivating future work.
