- Proposes a compelling framework for training NLP systems via multi-agent interactive referential communication games.
- Hybrid training (alternating communication games and supervised learning) appears especially promising and is demonstrated in experiments.
- Section 2’s general framework is well-conceived, versatile, and effective for this research direction.
- Experimental results are reasonable and provide solid evidence for the method’s efficacy.
- Paper opens several clear and useful avenues for future work and extension by other researchers.
- Primary weakness: unclear presentation of the model architecture—Figure 1 is ambiguous and omits connections.
- Section 3’s textual description is complex and, combined with the ambiguous figure, makes the model hard to understand.
- Strong recommendation to revise Figure 1 to be detailed and accurate, and at minimum add a comprehensive caption explaining components and omitted connections.
- Overall: a good paper with a valuable central idea that would be significantly strengthened by clearer architectural description and diagram.
- Reviewer caveat: not an expert in the multi-agent communication subfield, so assessment of novelty/related work may be incomplete.
