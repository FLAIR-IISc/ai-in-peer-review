- Proposal: Introduces a referential game framework where a sender and receiver use discrete symbols and REINFORCE-trained neural policies on pretrained visual features to learn communication.  
- Variants/results: Agents reliably learn to communicate; emergent symbols show semantic structure, become more concept-driven when sender/receiver see different class instances, and more interpretable when grounded with a supervised classification task.  
- Decision: Accept — novel, well-motivated approach that empirically demonstrates interactive emergence of semantically structured and alignable communication.  
- Contribution framing: Addresses how to train agents to develop protocols via interaction and how to nudge those protocols toward human-interpretable semantics in a scalable referential-game testbed.  
- Positioning: Well-situated relative to seq2seq, prior multi-agent communication work, and human-in-the-loop/scripted environments, emphasizing interaction and realistic visual inputs.  
- Empirical evidence: Across configurations agents converge to coordination; analyses indicate symbol usage reflects broad semantic organization rather than low-level cues.  
- Low-level common knowledge: Using different images of the same class for sender and receiver reduces reliance on low-level signals and increases conceptual coherence of symbols.  
- Grounding with supervision: Alternating play with a supervised image classification task grounds emergent codes in natural language and improves interpretability.
