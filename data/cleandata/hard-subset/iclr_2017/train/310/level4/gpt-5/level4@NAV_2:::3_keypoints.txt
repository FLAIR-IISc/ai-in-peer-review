- Introduces a two-agent referential game where sender sees target indicator t and emits one symbol for receiver to identify the target from two images.  
- Agents are neural networks using pretrained image features and trained with REINFORCE; both rewarded for correct identification.  
- Agents converge to high-performing policies and the sender’s symbolic code organizes around semantically meaningful concepts.  
- A variant where sender and receiver see different images from same classes yields more abstract, interpretable symbols.  
- A multi-task setup alternating referential game with supervised classification grounds symbols further in human-interpretable concepts.  
- Framing agent–agent communication and then grounding in language is novel and complementary to seq‑to‑seq distributional approaches.
