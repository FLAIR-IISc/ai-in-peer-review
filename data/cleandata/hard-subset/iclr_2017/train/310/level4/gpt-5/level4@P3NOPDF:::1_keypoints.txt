- Main finding: “Informed” agents with richer priors use more symbols, while “agnostic” agents converge to very few symbols.
- Strength: Addresses a timely, important question about emergent multi-agent communication.
- Strength: Task design is simple and interpretable, enabling post-hoc protocol analysis.
- Strength: The contrast between informed and agnostic agents is intriguing and suggestive about prior-driven code richness.
- Weakness: Task often reduces to image classification, allowing messages to collapse to class labels.
- Weakness: Risk of conflating emergent communication with supervised/transfer learning or simple label transmission.
- Weakness: Lacks strong supervised/transfer baselines to calibrate RL benefits and costs.
- Weakness: Analysis of emergent code is preliminary and missing quantitative metrics (symbol usage, MI, stability, generalization).
- Weakness: “Informed” vs. “agnostic” distinction is imprecise; unclear what prior differences drive symbol diversity.
- Suggestion: Make tasks robust to class-label shortcuts (same-category distractors, attribute/relation-based queries, require discrimination beyond class).
- Suggestion: Evaluate few-shot/zero-shot generalization to probe compositionality.
- Suggestion: Add baselines and ablations (supervised mapping to messages, non-communicative/lookup baselines, vocabulary/message/channel/perceptual ablations).
- Suggestion: Deepen protocol analysis (entropy, type-token ratios, mutual information, cluster purity, cross-run alignment, human guessability).
- Suggestion: Clarify methodology (precise definition of informed/agnostic, dataset composition, vocabulary/message constraints, training stability and compute/time comparisons).
- Suggestion: Consider richer games (multi-step dialogues, multiple distractors with shared categories but differing attributes/relations).
- Minor: Edit for grammar/terminology consistency and motivate when RL is preferable to supervised approaches.
- Overall assessment: Interesting and transparent setup with a notable observation, but current task reduction, missing baselines, and shallow analysis justify a weak reject unless revised.
