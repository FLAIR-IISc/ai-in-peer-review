- Introduces a referential game: both agents see two images, sender gets binary target t and sends one symbol, receiver must identify the target; both rewarded for correct prediction.
- Agents are neural networks using pretrained image features and trained with REINFORCE.
- Agents learn effective policies and the emergent symbol communication reflects meaningful concepts.
- A variant grounded in different image classes yields even more semantically coherent symbols.
- In a multi-task setting where the sender alternates between the referential game and supervised image classification, grounding in labels produces more interpretable symbols.
- Learning shared representations for communication in a multi-agent setting is compelling and more challenging than standard supervised learning or single-agent RL, justifying the simple initial task.
- The approach—inducing communication first, then grounding in human language—appears novel and complements sequence-to-sequence models.
- The task, framework, analyses, and visualizations provide a useful foundation for future work; reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Request to clarify how the target variable t is incorporated into the sender network architecture.
