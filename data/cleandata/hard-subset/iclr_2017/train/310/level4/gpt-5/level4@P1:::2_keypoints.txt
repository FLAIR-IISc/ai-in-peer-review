- Proposes a multi-agent referential game framework where tabula-rasa sender and receiver coordinate using a discrete vocabulary to identify target images.  
- Shows two simple neural agents can learn effective communication with REINFORCE and a discrete bottleneck.  
- Introduces an “informed” sender architecture that converges faster and uses a richer symbol set than an “agnostic” sender.  
- Emergent symbols correlate with human-relevant semantics: cluster purity above chance and small symbol sets capture broad distinctions (e.g., living vs. non-living).  
- Removing shared low-level information (different images of same class) pushes agents toward more abstract, class-level semantics.  
- Mixing self-play with supervised object naming grounds the emergent code in natural language, expanding symbol usage and raising purity (~70%).  
- Human evaluation (ReferItGame) shows humans pick the agent’s intended target 68% of the time, indicating partial interpretability beyond supervised classes.  
- Decision: Accept — framework is clear, well-motivated, and demonstrates alignment of emergent symbols with human semantics.  
- Grounding strategy combining self-play and supervision is practical and yields partial human interpretability on out-of-domain images.  
- Referential game is an appropriate minimal testbed for studying emergent communication that aligns with human semantic intuitions.  
- Empirical results consistently support claims: fast convergence, informed sender advantage, semantic coherence, environment-driven shaping, and improved grounding.  
- Suggest improving Figure 1 and Section 3 with clearer, explicit diagrams and captions to show forward paths and architecture differences.  
- Request clearer architecture details: kernel sizes, channel roles, concatenation vs per-dimension operations, and pseudocode for reproducibility.  
- Ask for reporting variability across random seeds, sensitivity to vocabulary size and temperature τ, and brief ablations (e.g., removing per-dimension bias).  
- Recommend more detail on the human study (pair selection, instructions, controls, sampling per symbol) to support interpretability claims.  
- Suggest stronger comparison to recent neural emergent-communication literature to clarify novelty (scale, discrete bottleneck, environment shaping, human grounding).  
- Questions for authors: precise sender architecture specs (sigmoids, filter operations, logits mapping) and stability across random initializations.  
- Questions on synonymy vs effective dimensionality and whether SVD results reflect redundancy or distinct meanings.  
- Questions on joint effects of temperature τ and vocabulary size on diversity, purity, and convergence.  
- Questions on grounding transfer: which supervised labels transfer best and which metonymic patterns aid humans.  
- Question on supervised/self-play mix: what proportion of supervised steps is required and does increased supervision help or hurt generalization.
