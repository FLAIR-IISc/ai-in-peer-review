- Addresses the important problem of emergent communication between two agents and whether machine problem-solving and communicative strategies resemble humans.  
- Task design is simple enough to allow straightforward, interpretable post-training analysis.  
- Key finding: informed agent uses a broader symbol set while the agnostic agent compresses communication into a two-symbol code.  
- Limitation: when images come from different categories the task reduces to image classification, making it akin to transfer learning and possibly more efficiently solved with supervised methods than RL.
