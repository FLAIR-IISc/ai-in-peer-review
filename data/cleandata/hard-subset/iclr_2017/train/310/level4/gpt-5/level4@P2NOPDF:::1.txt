Pros
- The paper addresses a central problem: understanding communication between two agents. As reinforcement learning applications expand, this work revisits a foundational question: Do machines solve problems in ways comparable to humans?
- The task is sufficiently simple to make post-training analysis clear and intuitive.
- It is interesting that informed agents use multiple symbols to convey messages, whereas agnostic agents rely on only two symbols.

Cons
- When the two images come from different categories, the task effectively reduces to image classification. The symbols act as class labels that the second agent learns to associate with one of the images. In this sense, the approach resembles transfer learning and could likely be trained more efficiently than with a reinforcement learning algorithm.
