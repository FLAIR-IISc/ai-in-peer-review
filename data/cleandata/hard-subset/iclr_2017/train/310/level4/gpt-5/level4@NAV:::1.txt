Review- Thank you for an engaging read.
Pros
- This paper addresses a fundamental problem: understanding communication between two agents. As reinforcement learning sees broader application, this work revisits a foundational question: do machines approach problem solving in a way similar to humans?
- The task is sufficiently simple to make post-learning analysis intuitive.
- It was interesting to observe that informed agents used multiple symbols to convey messages, whereas agnostic agents relied on only two symbols.
Cons
- When the two transmitted images come from different categories, the task effectively reduces to image classification. The symbols essentially represent the image class, which the second agent learns to assign to one of the images. In effect, this becomes a transfer learning problem that could likely be trained much faster than a reinforcement learning algorithm.
