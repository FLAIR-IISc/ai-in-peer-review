Thank you for an engaging submission.

Pros
- The paper addresses the important problem of emergent communication between two agents. As RL applications proliferate, this work usefully returns to a core question: do machines’ problem-solving and communicative strategies resemble those of humans?
- The task design is sufficiently simple to make post-training analysis straightforward and interpretable.
- It is interesting that the informed agent employs a broader set of symbols to convey messages, whereas the agnostic agent effectively compresses communication to a two-symbol code.

Cons
- When the two images come from different categories, the task largely reduces to image classification: the emitted symbol effectively represents the image class, which the receiver learns to associate with one of the images. Consequently, the setup resembles a transfer-learning problem that could likely be trained more efficiently with supervised methods than with reinforcement learning.
