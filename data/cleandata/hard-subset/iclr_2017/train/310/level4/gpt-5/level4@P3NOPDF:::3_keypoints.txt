- Studies emergent communication in a referential game where sender sees binary target t and sends a discrete symbol for receiver to infer t, trained with REINFORCE on pretrained image features.
- Shows learned symbols correlate with semantically meaningful visual concepts across game variants.
- Introduces class-grounded and multi-task (referential + supervised classification) settings; grounding in labels yields clearer, more interpretable symbols.
- Strength: timely, well-motivated problem and controlled experimental setup amenable to analysis.
- Strength: novel angle of learning communication first then grounding in human language, highlighting functional communication aspects.
- Strength: empirical evidence supports nontrivial structure and alignment of codes with high-level concepts.
- Strength: potential impact as a stepping stone for emergent communication and alignment research.
- Weakness: methodological details underspecified (how t is injected into sender architecture, normalization, encoder selection).
- Weakness: unclear message format and vocabulary details (vocabulary size, single token vs. sequence, decoding/training protocol).
- Weakness: missing REINFORCE variance-reduction details (baselines, entropy regularization) affecting stability interpretation.
- Weakness: overstrong claim of convergence to optimal policies—should be tempered to “effective” or “near-optimal” absent proofs or exhaustive baselines.
- Weakness: evaluation could be deeper (e.g., mutual information with classes, compositionality metrics, OOD generalization).
- Weakness: dependence on pretrained image features not analyzed; an ablation with weaker/random features is suggested.
- Minor: table formatting and inconsistent percentage scales; verify “obs-chance purity” values and unify [0,100]% convention.
- Minor: typos to fix (“assest”→“assess”, “usufal”→“usual”).
- Reporting request: include random seeds, number of runs, mean±std, optimizer/learning rates/batch sizes/steps, and release code or pseudocode.
- Overall: clean, convincing framework and analyses demonstrating that grounded/multi-tasking senders yield more interpretable symbols; methodological/reporting clarifications needed.
- Recommendation: Accept.
