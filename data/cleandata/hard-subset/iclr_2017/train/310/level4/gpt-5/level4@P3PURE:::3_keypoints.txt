- Proposes a referential game: both agents see two images; sender receives binary target t and sends a symbol; receiver predicts the target; both rewarded for correct prediction.
- Agents are neural networks operating on pretrained image features and trained with REINFORCE.
- Authors show agents converge to optimal policies and that transmitted symbols encode meaningful concepts.
- Variant experiments grounded in different image classes yield even more meaningful learned concepts.
- Multi-game setup alternates the referential game with supervised image classification, which further grounds symbols in human-label concepts.
- The direction (induce communication then ground in language) is novel and a useful alternative to sequence-to-sequence approaches.
- Paper offers useful analysis and visualizations; reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Request: clarify how the target variable t is incorporated into the sender network(s).
- Issue: inconsistent percentage scales in Table 1 vs Table 2; "obs-chance purity" in Table 1 likely mistaken.
- Minor typos to fix: "assest" → "assess", "usufal" → "usual".
