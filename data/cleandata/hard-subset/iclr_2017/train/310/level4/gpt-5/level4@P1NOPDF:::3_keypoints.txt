- Proposes a referential game where a sender, given a binary target t, sends a discrete symbol to a receiver to identify the target from two images; agents trained end-to-end with REINFORCE on pretrained visual features.  
- Empirically agents converge to near-optimal policies and the learned symbolic communication captures meaningful concepts.  
- In a variant grounded in different image classes, agents learn even more semantically meaningful symbols.  
- In a multi-game setup where the sender alternates between the referential game and supervised image classification, symbols align more closely with human-interpretable concepts.  
- Framing multi-agent emergent communication and then grounding in language is novel and a promising alternative to seq2seq approaches focused on statistical regularities.  
- Starting with this simple task is justified because multi-agent communication is substantially harder than supervised or single-agent RL.  
- Overall assessment: useful stepping stone with solid analyses/visualizations — recommendation to accept.
