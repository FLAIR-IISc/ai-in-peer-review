- Addresses a central problem: understanding communication between two agents and whether machines solve problems comparably to humans.
- Task simplicity makes post-training analysis clear and intuitive.
- Empirical finding: informed agents use multiple symbols to convey messages, while agnostic agents rely on only two symbols.
- When images come from different categories, the task reduces to image classification—symbols act as class labels—so the approach resembles transfer learning and could likely be trained more efficiently than with reinforcement learning.
