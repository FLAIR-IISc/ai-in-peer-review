Review- Training natural language systems by embedding multiple agents in an interactive referential communication game is a very appealing idea. As the authors note, there is some (albeit limited) prior work on using multi-agent games to foster communication, and it certainly looks like a direction worth pursuing. Additionally, alternating between these games and supervised learning—as in the experiment described in Section 5 and proposed in Section 6—seems particularly promising.

Note: For the sake of “clarity,” I believe some network connections in Fig. 1 were omitted. However, given the highly customized architecture and the somewhat hard-to-follow description in Section 3, the shorthand diagram actually adds to the confusion. The figure likely needs refinement; at a minimum (especially if I am misinterpreting it), a caption should still be added to guide the reader.

Overall, the framework (Section 2) is strong and appears effective/useful in several ways, the results are reasonable, and I anticipate interesting future variants of this work as well.
