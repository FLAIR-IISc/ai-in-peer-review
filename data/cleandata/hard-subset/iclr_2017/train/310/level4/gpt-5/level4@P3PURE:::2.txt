Training natural language systems by placing multiple agents in an interactive referential communication game is a compelling idea. As the authors note, there has been some (though seemingly not much) prior work on using multi-agent games to foster communication, and this direction appears well worth pursuing. In particular, the strategy of interleaving these games with supervised learning—as implemented in the experiment in Section 5 and proposed more broadly in Section 6—seems especially promising.
