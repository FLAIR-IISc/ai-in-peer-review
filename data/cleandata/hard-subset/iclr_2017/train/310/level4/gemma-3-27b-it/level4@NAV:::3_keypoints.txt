- Introduces a referential game where a sender receives a binary target and sends a symbol to a receiver to predict the target from two images.  
- Agents are implemented as neural networks using pre-trained image features and trained with REINFORCE.  
- Results show convergence to optimal policies and that the learned symbolic communication captures meaningful concepts.  
- A variant using different image classes promotes learning of more interpretable concepts.  
- Multi-game setup (alternating referential game and supervised classification) grounds communication, producing symbols with enhanced semantic meaning.  
- Contribution: novel two-step approach of establishing agent communication before grounding in human-interpretable concepts, complementary to seq2seq paradigms; reviewer recommends acceptance.
