In this paper, the authors propose a referential game between two agents. Both agents observe two images. The first agent, the sender, receives a binary target variable (t) and must send a symbol (message) to the second agent, the receiver, such that the receiver can accurately predict the target. Both agents receive a reward if the receiver successfully predicts the target. The paper proposes to parameterize the agents as neural networks – utilizing pretrained image representations as feature vectors – and train them using REINFORCE. In this setting, the authors demonstrate that the agents converge to optimal policies and that their learned communications (i.e., the symbolic code transmitted from the sender to the receiver) develop meaningful concepts. Furthermore, the paper presents experiments on a variant of the game grounded in different image classes, where the agents appear to learn even more interpretable concepts. Finally, a multi-game setup is proposed, where the sender agent alternates between playing the referential game and performing a supervised learning task (image classification). As expected, when grounded in the supervised learning task, the symbolic communications exhibit even more meaningful concepts.

Learning shared representations for communication in a multi-agent setting is an interesting and challenging research direction. This is considerably more difficult than standard supervised learning or single-agent reinforcement learning, justifying the choice of a relatively simple initial task. To the best of my knowledge, the approach of first learning communication between two agents and then grounding that communication in human-understandable concepts is novel. As the authors point out, this may represent an alternative paradigm to standard sequence-to-sequence models, which often focus on the statistical properties of language rather than its functional aspects. I believe the contributions of the proposed task and framework, along with the analysis and visualization of the communicated tokens, represent a valuable step forward for future work. For these reasons, I recommend the paper for acceptance.
