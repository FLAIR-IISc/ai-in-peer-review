**Pros**

- This paper addresses a critical problem: understanding communication between two agents. As the applications of reinforcement learning expand, this work returns to a fundamental question – whether machine problem-solving strategies align with those of humans.
- The chosen task is sufficiently simple to facilitate intuitive post-learning analysis.
- It was insightful to observe that informed agents utilized multiple symbols to convey information, while uninformed agents relied on only two.

**Cons**

- The task essentially reduces to image classification when the two presented images belong to different categories. The symbols employed effectively represent the image class, which the receiving agent learns to associate with each image. Consequently, this approach fundamentally resembles a transfer learning problem and could potentially be trained more efficiently than with a reinforcement learning algorithm.
