- Introduces a referential game where sender and receiver each see two images, sender receives a binary target t and sends a symbol so receiver can predict the target.  
- Agents are neural networks using pre-trained image representations and are trained with the REINFORCE algorithm.  
- Results show agents converge to optimal policies and the learned symbolic communication develops meaningful concepts.  
- A variant grounded in different image classes yields even more interpretable concepts.  
- A multi-game setup alternates the referential game with a supervised image-classification task for the sender, which further grounds and clarifies the symbols.  
- Framing: learning shared representations for multi-agent communication is valuable and appropriately tackled with a simple initial task.  
- Novelty: establishing agent communication first and then grounding it in human-understandable concepts is, to the reviewer’s knowledge, a new and promising approach compared to sequence-to-sequence models.  
- Contributions: task, framework, and analysis/visualization of communicated tokens are useful steps for future research; recommendation: accept.  
- Clarification request: explain how the target variable (t) is incorporated into the sender network.  
- Presentation issues: standardize percentage formatting across Tables 1 and 2 and verify unusually small values in the "obs-chance purity" column of Table 1.  
- Minor typos: replace "assest" with "assess" and "usufal" with "usual".
