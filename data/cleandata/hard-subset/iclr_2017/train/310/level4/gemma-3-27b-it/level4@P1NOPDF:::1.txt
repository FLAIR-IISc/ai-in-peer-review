Thank you for an interesting read.

**Pros**

- This paper tackles a crucial problem: understanding communication between two agents. As the applications of reinforcement learning continue to expand, this work returns us to a fundamental question: is the problem-solving approach of machines similar to that of humans?
- The task is simple enough to allow for intuitive post-learning analysis.
- It was interesting to observe that informed agents utilized multiple symbols to transmit their message, whereas agnostic agents relied on only two.

**Cons**

- The task effectively reduces to image classification when the two images sent belong to different categories. The symbols used are effectively image class labels, which the second agent learns to assign to either of the images. This approach, in essence, boils down to a transfer learning problem that could likely be trained much faster than the proposed reinforcement learning algorithm.
