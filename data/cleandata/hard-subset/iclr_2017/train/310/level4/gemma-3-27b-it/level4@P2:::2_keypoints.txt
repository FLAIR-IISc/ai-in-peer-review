- Proposes a novel multi-agent referential game framework for emergent language; idea is compelling and aligned with interactive AI goals.
- Builds on and extends multi-agent and game-theoretic prior work, demonstrating promising initial results.
- Combining game-based learning with supervised grounding yields more human-interpretable communication.
- Agents can learn to coordinate and environmental manipulations influence the semantic properties of the emergent language.
- Grounding experiment (Section 5) is a valuable contribution showing a path toward human-interpretable communication.
- Recommendation: Reject but strongly encouraged to resubmit due to significant presentation issues.
- Major problem: Architecture descriptions and figure captions lack sufficient detail, hindering comprehension and evaluation.
- Figure 1 is insufficiently detailed (missing layer dimensions, caption explanations) making the “informed sender” novelty hard to assess.
- Related work discussion is superficial; needs deeper contextualization and comparison to closely related approaches.
- Reviewer concerned about missing connections to familiar literature, possibly weakening the paper’s positioning.
- Suggested improvement: Expand architectural details (layer sizes, activations, parameters) and revise figures/captions for clarity.
- Suggested improvement: Ensure consistent and precise terminology throughout the paper.
- Suggested improvement: Strengthen related work section and clearly differentiate contributions from prior approaches.
- Suggested improvement: Add ablation studies to isolate contributions of components like the informed sender and supervised grounding.
- Suggested improvement: Provide error analysis to reveal limitations and failure modes of the agents.
- Suggested improvement: Include more quantitative metrics for human evaluation of grounding.
- Questions for authors: Why were specific ImageNet classes chosen and what motivated that choice?
- Questions for authors: What is the computational cost and how does training scale with vocabulary size and image resolution?
- Questions for authors: How sensitive are results to hyperparameters (learning rate, batch size, temperature)?
- Questions for authors: How were the 100 supervised labels selected and does label choice affect performance?
- Questions for authors: Intuitively, why does the “informed sender” outperform the “agnostic sender”; what inductive biases does it introduce?
