- Decision: Reject — primary reasons are lack of novelty and limited scope.  
- Strength: Tackles an important problem; task is simple enough for intuitive post-learning analysis.  
- Empirical observation: Informed agents develop diverse vocabularies; agnostic agents use only two symbols.  
- Main critique: When images are from different categories, the task reduces to image classification.  
- Claim: Learned symbols correspond to image class labels and receiver maps labels to images.  
- Concern: Agents leverage pre-trained VGG features — this looks like transfer learning, not emergent language learned de novo.  
- Method critique: The RL setup appears to fine-tune a classifier rather than bootstrap a new communication system.  
- Authors note transfer learning link but fail to clearly distinguish their contribution from standard transfer/supervised methods.  
- Scope critique: Environments are too simple and do not capture the ambiguity/richness of natural language.  
- Suggestion: Explicitly clarify novelty and how the multi-agent game produces truly emergent communication beyond class-mapping.  
- Suggestion: Test on more challenging/visually similar images to require nuanced descriptions and validate robustness.  
- Suggestion: Add quantitative comparison to standard image classification baselines trained on the same data.  
- Suggestion: Expand analysis of symbol usage — correlate symbols with visual features and analyze error patterns.  
- Suggestion: Investigate impact and optimality of vocabulary size and behavior as vocabulary increases.  
- Question: How does the informed sender’s performance compare to a classifier trained on image features with agent-learned labels?  
- Question: Were embeddings regularized to encourage more interpretable or human-like symbol assignments?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to choice of pre-trained features (e.g., VGG vs others)?  
- Question: What are the limitations of the referential game as a proxy for natural language, and how might future work address them?
