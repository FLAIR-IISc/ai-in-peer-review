- Training language via interactive multi-agent referential games is a compelling and valuable research direction given limited prior work.  
- Alternating between multi-agent games and supervised learning (Sections 5 and 6) is a particularly promising strategy.  
- Recommend acceptance based on the framework’s novelty and encouraging initial results showing emergent interpretable communication.  
- Contributions include exploration of environmental factors influencing language development and attempts to ground emergent language.  
- The proposed research program and framework (Section 2) are well-defined and provide a clear structure for studying language emergence.  
- Experimental results (Sections 4 & 5) show agents can learn to coordinate and produce communication protocols with semantic links to object categories.  
- With supervised grounding, the learned protocols can align with human language, demonstrating interpretability.  
- Demonstrated successful learning in a referential game and initial steps toward interpretable communication signals.  
- Ablation studies (informed vs. agnostic sender) and environment modifications (object-level reference) strengthen the analysis.  
- Suggest improving accessibility of network architecture description (Section 3) and clarifying Fig. 1 and the role of 1D convolutions in the informed sender.  
- Request more details on the crowdsourcing/human evaluation procedure (participant demographics, instructions) to improve reproducibility.  
- Ask authors to clarify hyperparameter choices (Section 3): were they tuned experimentally or taken from prior work?  
- Request more information on vocabulary redundancy in the informed sender (Fig. 2) and its effect on interpretability.  
- Ask for additional examples of “metonymic” links between symbols and image content and discussion of implications for robust, generalizable communication systems.
