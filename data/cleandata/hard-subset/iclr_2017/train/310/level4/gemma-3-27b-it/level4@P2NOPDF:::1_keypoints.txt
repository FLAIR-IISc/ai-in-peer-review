- Investigates whether RL agents’ communication aligns with human problem-solving—an important, timely question.  
- Simplicity and clarity of the task/environment are strengths, enabling intuitive, interpretable post-learning analysis.  
- Key finding: informed agents develop a richer symbolic repertoire while agnostic agents converge to a limited set (two symbols).  
- Suggests a link between an agent’s task understanding and the complexity of its communication.  
- Major concern: the communication task may effectively reduce to image classification when images are from distinct categories.  
- Learned symbols may simply correspond to image classes, with the receiver learning to map them (a transfer-learning interpretation).  
- A supervised classifier could likely map images to classes more efficiently than the RL framework, questioning the necessity and efficiency of RL here.
