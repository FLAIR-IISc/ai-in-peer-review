- Tackles the crucial problem of understanding communication between two agents and whether machine problem-solving resembles human reasoning.

- Uses a simple task that permits intuitive post-learning analysis.

- Observes that informed agents use multiple symbols while agnostic agents rely on only two.

- The task can effectively reduce to image classification when the two images belong to different categories.

- Symbols behave like image class labels, making the setup essentially a transfer learning problem that could likely be solved faster than with the proposed RL approach.
