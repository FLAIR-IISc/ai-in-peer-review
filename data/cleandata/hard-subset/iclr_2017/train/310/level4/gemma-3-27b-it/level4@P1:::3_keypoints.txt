- Proposes a referential game where a sender and receiver observe two images; the sender gets a binary target and sends a symbol so the receiver can recover the target, with reward for correct prediction.  
- Agents are parametrized as neural networks using pretrained image features and trained with REINFORCE.  
- Experiments show agents converge to optimal policies and develop meaningful symbolic communications.  
- Grounding the game on different image classes yields even more meaningful concepts.  
- A multi-task setup (alternating between the game and supervised image classification) further anchors and improves the meaningfulness of the symbols.  
- Learning shared representations for multi-agent communication is an interesting, challenging direction and justifies starting with a simple task.  
- The approach of first learning agent-to-agent communication and then grounding it in human language is novel and a useful alternative to seq2seq paradigms.
