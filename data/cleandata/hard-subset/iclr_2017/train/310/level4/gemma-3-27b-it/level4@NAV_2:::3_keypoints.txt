- Proposes a referential game with two agents (sender/receiver) observing two images; sender gets a binary target and sends a symbol so receiver can recover the target.  
- Both agents receive reward when the receiver correctly predicts the target.  
- Agents are parametrized as neural networks using pretrained image feature vectors and trained with REINFORCE.  
- Empirical results show convergence to optimal policies.  
- Learned communications (symbolic codes) exhibit meaningful, interpretable concepts.  
- Variant experiments grounded on different image classes produce even more meaningful concepts.  
- Multi-game setup alternates communication training with a supervised image-classification task, which further grounds and clarifies symbols.  
- Framing communication learning before grounding in human language is novel and a useful alternative to seq2seq approaches.  
- Reviewer considers the contributions, analyses, and visualizations a valuable stepping stone and recommends acceptance.  
- Request for clarification: how is the target variable incorporated into the sender network?
