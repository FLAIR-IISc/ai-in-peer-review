- Paper proposes a referential game where a sender, given a binary target t, sends a symbol to a receiver so the receiver can recover the target from two observed images.  
- Agents are parametrized as neural networks using pretrained image features and trained with REINFORCE.  
- Authors show agents converge to optimal policies and learn meaningful symbolic communications.  
- Experiments on image-class-grounded variants produce more interpretable concepts.  
- Multi-game setup alternates between the referential game and supervised image classification, which further grounds symbols in meaningful concepts.  
- Learning shared communication representations in multi-agent settings is an interesting, challenging, and justified research direction.  
- The approach of first learning agent communication and then grounding it in human-understandable concepts appears novel and contrasts with standard seq2seq language-focused models.  
- The task, framework, analyses, and visualizations are a valuable stepping stone; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Clarification requested: how is the target t incorporated into the sender network?

- Possible error: “obs-chance purity” values in Table 1 look extremely small and may be incorrect.
