This paper introduces a referential game played between two agents. Each agent observes two images, and the first agent – the sender – receives a binary target variable (t) and must transmit a symbol (message) to the second agent, the receiver, enabling it to predict the target. Both agents receive a reward if the receiver correctly predicts the target. The agents are implemented as neural networks, utilizing pre-trained image representations as feature vectors, and are trained using the REINFORCE algorithm. The results demonstrate that the agents converge to optimal policies, and that the learned communication – the symbolic code exchanged between sender and receiver – develops meaningful concepts. The paper further presents experiments on a variant of this game grounded in different image classes, where the agents learn even more interpretable concepts. Finally, a multi-game setup is proposed, alternating between the referential game and a supervised learning task (image classification) for the sender. As expected, anchoring the communication in the supervised learning task leads to the development of even more meaningful symbolic representations.

Learning shared representations for communication in a multi-agent setting is a valuable and challenging research direction, significantly more difficult than standard supervised or single-agent reinforcement learning, justifying a focus on a relatively simple initial task. To the best of my knowledge, the approach of first establishing communication between two agents and then grounding this communication in human-understandable concepts is novel. As the authors point out, this represents a potentially promising alternative to traditional sequence-to-sequence models, which often prioritize the statistical properties of language over its functional aspects. I believe the contributions of the proposed task and framework, along with the analysis and visualization of the communicated tokens, constitute a useful step toward future research in this area. Therefore, I recommend accepting this paper.
