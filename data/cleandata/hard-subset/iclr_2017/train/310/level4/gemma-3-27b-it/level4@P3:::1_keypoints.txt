- Paper addresses learning grounded communication via interaction using multi-agent referential games.  
- Proposed framework is compelling for moving beyond supervised learning toward conversational agents.  
- Simple game setup enables intuitive analysis and promising initial coordination results.  
- Recommendation: Reject.  
- Primary concern: emergent communication appears tied to implicit image classification, reducing novelty of the “language.”  
- Differences between informed and agnostic senders likely reflect inductive biases, not fundamentally different communication strategies.  
- Interpretability and grounding claims (68% human success) are encouraging but need more scrutiny and evidence.  
- Paper situates problem well within linguistics, cognitive science, and game theory literature.  
- Experimental setup and referential game choice are clearly described and sensible.  
- Informed senders use a more diverse vocabulary; symbol purity indicates some semantic organization.  
- Core issue: task reduces to identifying object categories, so symbols may simply map to pre-existing semantic labels rather than a de novo language.  
- Reliance on pre-trained image embeddings and ImageNet labels raises concerns about genuine semantic grounding.  
- Grounding evaluation lacks detail on which images/symbols succeeded and the nature of observed metonymic links.  
- Recommended ablations: test sensitivity to choice of embeddings, including random or different-dataset embeddings.  
- Recommended analysis: visualize images per symbol to clarify symbol meanings.  
- Recommended baseline: compare to supervised image-to-symbol mapping to quantify RL benefits.  
- Clarify definition of “language” and acknowledge limitations vs. natural human language.  
- Provide detailed human evaluation methodology: participant demographics, instructions, and examples of successes/failures.  
- Questions for authors: effect of visually similar images on results and reliance on classification.  
- Questions for authors: examples of symbols used for semantically diverse images and driving features.  
- Questions for authors: percent of symbols directly associated with ImageNet labels and performance differences.  
- Questions for authors: have alternative rewards or game dynamics been explored to encourage richer communication?  
- Questions for authors: how does performance scale with vocabulary size and are there diminishing returns?
