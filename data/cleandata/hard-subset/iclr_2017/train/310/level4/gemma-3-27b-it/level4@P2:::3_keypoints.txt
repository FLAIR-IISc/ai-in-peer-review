- Introduces a novel multi-agent referential game framework for language emergence between a sender and receiver.
- Agents are parametrized as neural networks and trained with REINFORCE to coordinate and communicate effectively.
- Demonstrates agents develop a communicatory code with discernible semantic properties aligned with image concepts.
- Explores environment modifications to encourage more intuitive “word meanings.”
- Proposes grounding the emergent code in natural language via a combined game-playing and supervised learning setup.
- Recommends paper for acceptance, arguing interaction-driven communication is a valuable research direction.
- Core contribution: shows agents can learn communication from scratch distinct from traditional seq2seq statistical learning.
- Visualizations and analyses provide evidence for the meaningfulness and interpretability of the learned communication.
