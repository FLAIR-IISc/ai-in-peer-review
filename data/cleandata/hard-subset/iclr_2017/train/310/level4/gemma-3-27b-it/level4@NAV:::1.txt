Thank you for submitting this interesting work.

**Pros:**

- This paper addresses a fundamental and increasingly important question in the field of reinforcement learning: how do agents learn to communicate, and does this process mirror human communication strategies? As the application space for RL expands, revisiting these foundational questions is critical.
- The simplicity of the chosen task facilitates clear post-learning analysis and interpretation of results.
- The observed difference in symbol usage between informed and agnostic agents – the former utilizing a richer symbol set, the latter relying on a limited two-symbol vocabulary – is a particularly noteworthy finding.

**Cons:**

- The task structure inherently reduces to an image classification problem when the presented images belong to distinct categories. The employed symbols effectively function as image class labels, which the receiving agent learns to associate with the presented stimuli. Consequently, the presented approach closely resembles a transfer learning problem, potentially amenable to more efficient training methodologies than the utilized reinforcement learning algorithm.
