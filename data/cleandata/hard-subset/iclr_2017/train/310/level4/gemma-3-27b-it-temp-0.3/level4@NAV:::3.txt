This paper introduces a referential game played between two agents. Each agent is presented with two images, and the first agent – the sender – receives a binary target variable and communicates a symbol to the second agent – the receiver – with the goal of enabling the receiver to predict the target. Both agents are rewarded for successful target prediction. The authors propose implementing the agents as neural networks, leveraging pre-trained image representations as feature vectors, and training them via REINFORCE. Results demonstrate convergence to optimal policies, with the learned communication exhibiting interpretable concepts. The work further explores a variant of the game using diverse image classes, leading to the emergence of even more meaningful concepts. Finally, a multi-game setup is presented, alternating between the referential game and a supervised image classification task for the sender, resulting in symbolic communications with enhanced interpretability due to the grounding in the supervised task.

The investigation of shared representations for communication within a multi-agent framework represents a compelling research direction, particularly given its increased complexity compared to traditional supervised or single-agent reinforcement learning. The authors’ approach of initially establishing communication between agents before grounding it in human-interpretable concepts is, to my knowledge, a novel contribution. As they point out, this offers a potentially valuable alternative to sequence-to-sequence models, which often prioritize statistical language properties over functional meaning. I find the contributions of the proposed task and framework, along with the analysis and visualization of the communicated tokens, to be a valuable foundation for future research. Therefore, I recommend acceptance of this paper.
