Thank you for submitting this interesting work.

**Pros**
- This paper addresses a fundamental and increasingly relevant problem: understanding communication between agents. With the expanding applications of reinforcement learning, this work thoughtfully returns to a core question regarding the alignment of machine and human problem-solving strategies.
- The simplicity of the task facilitates clear post-learning analysis and interpretation of results.
- The observed difference in symbol usage – informed agents leveraging multiple symbols versus agnostic agents relying on only two – was a particularly insightful finding.

**Cons**
- The task structure closely resembles an image classification problem when the presented images belong to distinct categories. The employed symbols effectively function as image classes, with the receiving agent learning to categorize images accordingly. Consequently, the approach largely mirrors a transfer learning scenario, potentially achievable with greater efficiency than the current reinforcement learning implementation.
