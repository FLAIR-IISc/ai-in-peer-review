**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

This paper proposes a novel framework for language learning that relies on multi-agent communication in referential games. The authors introduce a game setup where two agents, a sender and a receiver, need to develop a language to coordinate and earn payoffs. The agents start as blank slates and, by playing the game together, they can develop and bootstrap knowledge on top of each other, leading to the emergence of a language. The authors investigate the semantic properties of the emergent communication protocol and explore ways to make the language more interpretable and aligned with human intuitions.
