- Proposes a novel multi-agent communication framework for language learning in referential games.  
- Aims to develop a language portable to new situations and partners, including humans.  
- Uses a simple sender–receiver image coordination game as the experimental setup.  
- Shows agents can learn to communicate successfully in the game.  
- Emergent language captures general conceptual properties of depicted objects.  
- Grounds communication in natural language by combining the game with supervised image labeling.  
- Resulting language is interpretable by humans and generalizes to new images and contexts.  
- Recommendation: accept the paper.  
- Key reason 1: Well-motivated approach with a clear problem statement and proposed solution.  
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical results demonstrating the approach’s effectiveness.  
- Argues interactive, grounded learning addresses limits of traditional supervised approaches.  
- Empirical evidence supports generalization and conceptual structure in the emergent language.
