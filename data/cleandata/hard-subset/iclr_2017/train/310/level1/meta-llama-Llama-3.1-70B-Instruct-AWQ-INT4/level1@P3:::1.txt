**Summary of the Paper**

The authors propose a framework for language learning that relies on multi-agent communication, specifically in the context of referential games. They study the emergence of language in a game where two agents, a sender and a receiver, must communicate to identify a target image from a pair of images. The authors demonstrate that simple neural network architectures can learn to coordinate in this game and develop a language that captures general conceptual properties of the objects depicted in the images. They also explore how to modify the game environment to encourage the development of more interpretable languages and propose a method for grounding the agents' communication in human language.
