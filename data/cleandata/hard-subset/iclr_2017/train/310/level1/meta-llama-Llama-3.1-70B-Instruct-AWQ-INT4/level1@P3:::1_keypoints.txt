- Proposed framework: multi-agent referential game where sender and receiver communicate to identify a target image, showing emergent language capturing object concepts.  
- Decision: Accept — paper offers a novel, significant departure from traditional supervised language learning.  
- Originality/significance: Focus on language emergence via multi-agent communication and game-theoretic setup is novel and important.  
- Experimental thoroughness: Extensive experiments across architectures, vocabulary sizes, and environments demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Theoretical foundations: Solid grounding in game theory, linguistics, and cognitive science with well-motivated critique of supervised learning.  
- Experimental design: Clear progression from simple to complex games and use of varied architectures/vocabularies supports result generality.  
- Analysis and results: Thorough analysis shows agents coordinate and develop languages reflecting general conceptual object properties.  
- Comparison to related work: Paper should include more direct comparisons to other language-learning approaches to better establish novelty.  
- Limitations and future work: Authors should provide a more detailed discussion of limitations and concrete future research directions.  
- Interpretability and evaluation: Need for more detailed evaluation metrics and deeper analysis of the interpretability of the emergent language.  
- Question — scaling: How will the approach extend to more complex game environments and larger vocabularies?  
- Question — baselines: Can the authors provide direct comparisons to supervised and reinforcement learning approaches?  
- Question — interpretability evaluation: How will interpretability and effectiveness be evaluated in more complex scenarios?
