**Summary**

This paper proposes a framework for language learning through multi-agent communication, specifically in the context of referential games. The authors aim to develop a language that is portable to new situations and communication partners, including humans. They design a game environment where two agents, a sender and a receiver, must communicate to identify a target image from a pair of images. The agents develop their own language through interaction, and the authors investigate the emergence of semantic properties in the language. They also explore ways to ground the agents' language in human language by combining the game with a supervised image labeling task.

**Decision**

I recommend accepting this paper, with two key reasons:

1. **Novel approach to language learning**: The paper presents a fresh perspective on language learning, moving away from traditional supervised learning approaches. The use of multi-agent communication and referential games is a promising direction for developing more interactive and human-like language abilities.
2. **Comprehensive evaluation and analysis**: The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, including experiments on language emergence, semantic properties, and grounding in human language. The analysis is well-supported by theoretical and empirical evidence, making the paper a valuable contribution to the field.

**Supporting arguments**

The paper is well-motivated, building on existing work in linguistics, cognitive science, and game theory.
