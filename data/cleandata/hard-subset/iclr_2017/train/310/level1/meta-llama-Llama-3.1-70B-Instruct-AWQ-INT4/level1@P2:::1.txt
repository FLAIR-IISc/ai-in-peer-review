**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

This paper proposes a novel framework for language learning based on multi-agent communication in referential games. The authors introduce a game setup where two agents, a sender and a receiver, need to develop a language to coordinate and refer to objects in images. The agents start as blank slates and learn to communicate through playing the game, leading to the emergence of a language. The paper demonstrates that simple neural network agents can learn to coordinate in the referential game and that the meanings they assign to symbols capture general conceptual properties of the objects depicted in the images. The authors also explore how to modify the game environment to encourage the agents to develop more natural language-like communication and propose a method to ground the agents' communication in human language by combining the game with a supervised image labeling task.

I decide to accept this paper because it presents a well-motivated and novel approach to language learning, and the authors provide convincing empirical evidence to support their claims. The paper is well-written, and the authors do an excellent job of explaining the game setup, the agent architectures, and the experimental results.
