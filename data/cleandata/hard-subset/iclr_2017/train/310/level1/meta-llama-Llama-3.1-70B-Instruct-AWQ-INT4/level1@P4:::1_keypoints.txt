- Proposes a multi-agent referential game framework where sender and receiver agents develop a language via interaction.
- Agents start as blank slates and bootstrap knowledge from each other, leading to emergent communication.
- Investigates semantic properties of the emergent protocol and methods to make it interpretable and human-aligned.
- Decision: Accept.
- Well-motivated critique of mainstream NLP training and a novel framework addressing those limitations.
- Strong empirical support with extensive results demonstrating interpretable, human-aligned emergent language.
- Thorough related-work review and clear explanation of game setup, agent architectures, and training procedure.
- Detailed analysis of results highlighting both strengths and limitations of the approach.
- Suggests providing more implementation details (hyperparameters, exact training procedure).
- Recommends analyzing robustness to different types of noise and perturbations.
- Encourages exploring applications to more complex tasks, such as conversational dialogue systems.
- Requests from authors: more implementation details, plans for extending to complex tasks, and robustness analyses.
