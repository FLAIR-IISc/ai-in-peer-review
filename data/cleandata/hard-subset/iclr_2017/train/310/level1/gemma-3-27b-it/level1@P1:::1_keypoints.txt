- Paper studies language emergence in multi-agent referential games, focusing on communication from scratch and environmental effects on semantics.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main acceptance reason: framework successfully fosters agent communication and coordination.  
- Second acceptance reason: emergent protocol shows sensitivity to object categories and partial alignment with human intuition.  
- Experimental setup is well-defined, using deep learning, reinforcement learning, and referential games.  
- Results are clearly presented with effective tables and figures.  
- Experiments progress logically from coordination to semantic grounding to human interpretability.  
- 'Informed sender' architecture produces more interpretable language, suggesting useful inductive biases.  
- Ablation studies (removing common knowledge, supervised learning) are well-executed and informative.  
- Quantitative metrics (purity, human accuracy) provide objective support for claims.  
- Related work in linguistics, game theory, and AI is appropriately cited and contextualized.  
- Human evaluation is limited in scope but indicates promise for grounding emergent language.  
- Feedback: discuss hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., embedding dimensionality, temperature) and its effects.  
- Feedback: justify vocabulary size choices (10 vs 100) and whether larger vocabularies show diminishing returns.  
- Feedback: add deeper qualitative examples of symbol–image pairings and analysis of emergent language.  
- Feedback: strengthen theoretical connection to game theory by discussing the types of equilibria agents converge to.  
- Feedback: provide more analysis of the receiver architecture and its role in shaping language.  
- Questions for authors: which Nash equilibrium(s) do agents converge to and how do environmental factors affect this?  
- Questions: how do humans interpret symbols for images without direct label correspondences; consider more comprehensive human evaluations.  
- Questions: how does the 'informed sender' compare to other inductive biases or failed attempts to guide emergence?  
- Questions: did Reinforce exhibit instability or variance, and how was it handled?  
- Questions: provide more examples of metonymic links observed in human evaluation beyond the dolphin/fence case.
