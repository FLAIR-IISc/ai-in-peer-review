- Introduces a multi-agent referential game framework where neural agents learn a shared language to identify target images.  
- Demonstrates agents can coordinate successfully in referential games and develop emergent communication.  
- Shows that modifying the game environment encourages emergent language with semantic properties aligned to human concepts.  
- Proposes a grounding strategy combining referential game training with supervised image classification to improve human interpretability.  
- Lists three main contributions: successful communication learning, environment-driven semantic improvement, and a grounding method for interpretability.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Key reason for acceptance: well-motivated approach with strong empirical evidence and rigorous experiments supporting claims.  
- Positions work against supervised and human-in-the-loop approaches and connects to signaling game theory and cognitive science.  
- Empirical results show semantic structure beyond low-level features, notably a living-vs-non-living split emerging even with a 2-symbol vocabulary.  
- Environmental modification experiment effectively forces coordination on higher-level properties and confirms the hypothesis.  
- Grounding approach maintains communication success while substantially improving human interpretability (68% crowdsourced accuracy).  
- Methodological rigor: reinforcement learning training, appropriate metrics (cluster purity, human eval), and controlled experiments enabling reproducibility.  
- Suggestions: add a detailed discussion of limitations and practical implications for real-world agents.  
- Suggestions: report computational requirements (training time, hardware) to assess scalability.  
- Suggestions: more explicit discussion of which equilibrium concepts are observed and their relevance to language emergence.  
- Suggestions: provide a more detailed comparison to Sukhbaatar et al. (2016) and Foerster et al. (2016).  
- Question: explain why the informed sender uses more symbols than the agnostic sender and how architecture influences emergent language.  
- Question: clarify mechanisms by which grounded language generalizes beyond supervised categories.  
- Question: relate observed "metonymic" links to theories of semantic extension and implications for future language learning systems.
