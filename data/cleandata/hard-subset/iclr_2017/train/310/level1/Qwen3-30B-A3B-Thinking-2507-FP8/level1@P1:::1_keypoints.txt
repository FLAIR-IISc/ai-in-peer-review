1. Novel framework for language learning via multi-agent referential games producing emergent communication reflecting high-level semantics.  
2. Shows agents can develop communication without pre-programmed protocols.  
3. Demonstrates that modifying the game environment yields more semantically meaningful communication.  
4. Introduces grounding via interactive learning plus supervised image labeling; grounded agents reach 68% human comprehension.  
5. Recommendation: accept — significant, well-motivated work with strong empirical support.  
6. Strength: well-situated in literature and theoretically grounded in signaling-game and evolutionary perspectives.  
7. Strength: robust experiments with appropriate baselines, quantitative metrics (purity, coordination), t-SNE, and human evaluations showing near-perfect coordination and high semantic purity.  
8. Strength: offers a practical path toward human-interpretable conversational agents through grounding.  
9. Suggestion: discuss limitations, especially scalability and generalization beyond referential games.  
10. Suggestion: provide a more thorough comparison to Sukhbaatar et al. (2016) and Foerster et al. (2016) and clarify improvements in grounding.  
11. Suggestion: expand discussion of the "object-level reference" experiment and why it enhances semantic properties.  
12. Question: why does the informed sender use many more symbols after grounding and does this diversity drive better semantics?  
13. Question: compare the 68% human accuracy to a baseline using random words to quantify the grounding benefit.  
14. Question: provide more examples of metonymic shifts (e.g., "dolphin" for sea) and discuss their implications.  
15. Question: how can this approach scale to complex scenarios like multi-turn dialogues or abstract-concept communication?
