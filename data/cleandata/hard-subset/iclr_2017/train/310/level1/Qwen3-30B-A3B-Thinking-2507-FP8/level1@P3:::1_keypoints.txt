- Contribution: Proposes a multi-agent communication framework where agents develop language through interaction (referential games) rather than passive text learning.  
- Contribution: Shows emergent communication protocols exhibit semantic structure aligned with human conceptual categories.  
- Contribution: Introduces a grounding method combining interactive gameplay and supervised image classification to map emergent symbols to natural language.  
- Theoretical/empirical grounding: Uses game theory (signaling games) and empirical experiments to justify and validate the approach.  
- Decision: Recommend accept—substantial, well-motivated, methodologically sound, and scientifically rigorous.  
- Empirical robustness: Demonstrates convergence across architectures and parameter settings, with the informed sender achieving higher performance and more diverse symbol use.  
- Semantic analysis: McRae category analysis and t-SNE visualizations support that communication reflects human-like semantic categories rather than low-level visual features.  
- Grounding validation: Grounding approach is innovative and effective; human evaluation shows 68% accuracy in interpreting agent communication.  
- Literature placement: Clearly distinguishes contributions from prior multi-agent and neural communication work and connects to linguistic/cognitive literature.  
- Hyperparameter concern: Need clearer description of hyperparameter choices and sensitivity analysis.  
- Prior-work comparison: Should more thoroughly contrast with Sukhbaatar et al. (2016) and Foerster et al. (2016), especially regarding semantic properties.  
- Metonymy analysis: Desires more systematic analysis of observed metonymic communication patterns from human evaluation.  
- Scalability question: How performance scales with larger vocabularies or more complex, multi-object/spatial environments.  
- Computational cost question: What is the computational cost of the grounding procedure versus standard supervised NLP approaches.  
- Extension question: How to extend the framework to multi-turn conversations and what additional challenges that introduces.
