- Presents multi-agent communication games as a solution to language learning beyond passive text exposure.  
- Shows simple neural networks can coordinate in referential games to develop communication protocols.  
- Demonstrates environmental modifications can induce more semantically meaningful language.  
- Contribution 1: Tabula rasa agents can learn to communicate effectively in referential games.  
- Contribution 2: Agents develop symbol meanings aligned with human semantic categories.  
- Contribution 3: Introduces a grounded approach linking agent communication to natural language via supervised learning.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong empirical evidence and a significant step toward human-interpretable machine communication.  
- Experimental design is rigorous, using appropriate metrics (purity, SVD) and careful comparison of sender architectures.  
- Strengths: clear problem formulation, thorough literature positioning, and convincing results on convergence and semantic quality.  
- Grounding strategy is innovative and improves human interpretability when supervised on natural language labels.  
- Suggested improvement: explicitly discuss limitations, particularly scalability to more complex communication scenarios.  
- Suggested improvement: provide deeper analysis of why the informed sender outperforms the agnostic sender.  
- Question: How would results change with more than two agents to better model real-world communication?  
- Question: Beyond vocabulary size, which supervised-learning factors most improved human interpretability?
