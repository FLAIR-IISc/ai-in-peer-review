- Contribution: Demonstrates near-perfect emergent communication between feed-forward Sender/Receiver agents trained with REINFORCE on ImageNet-derived VGG features.
- Contribution: Compares two Sender architectures—agnostic MLP vs. informed per-dimension convolution—finding the informed sender converges faster and uses a richer symbol inventory.
- Contribution: Shows emergent symbols correlate with semantic structure (above-chance purity for 20 human categories, alignment with visual similarity, living vs. non-living split with 2 symbols).
- Contribution: Removing low-level common knowledge (different exemplars per class) nudges symbols toward higher-level, class-level semantics (increased purity).
- Contribution: Interleaving self-play with supervised labeling increases symbol purity and yields interpretable symbols (47% exact match vs. 1% chance).
- Contribution: Human evaluation on ReferIt-derived patches shows humans identify referents 68% of the time using learned words, indicating partial interpretability and metonymic usage.
- Decision: Reject—primary reasons are limited technical depth/rigor and reliance on ad hoc analyses without strong baselines, ablations, or principled generalization tests.
- Critique: RL implementation details and training stability across seeds are sparse, and variance-reduction/regularization techniques are not reported.
- Critique: Semantic alignment claims lack baselines showing how much semantic signal is already present in VGG fc features (e.g., clustering baselines).
- Critique: SVD spectrum and t-SNE visualizations are non-quantitative; living/non-living split with two symbols is under-analyzed.
- Critique: Purity gains from removing common knowledge are described as small and lack statistical testing or effect-size reporting.
- Critique: Grounding benefits are reported without key controls (e.g., random label replacement, nearest-neighbor visual baselines).
- Critique: Human study lacks appropriate baselines and may be biased by selecting only successful Sender–Receiver trials and limited samples per symbol.
- Recommendation: Provide baselines for purity (random assignment, K-means on VGG features, supervised classifier) with effect sizes and confidence intervals.
- Recommendation: Measure topographic similarity between symbol-space distances and visual/semantic distances.
- Recommendation: Run cross-play between independently trained agents to test for idiosyncratic vs. shared conventions.
- Recommendation: Evaluate generalization (zero-shot classes, more distractors, robustness to noise/occlusion).
- Recommendation: Test compositionality/systematicity with multi-object or attribute recombination tasks (color/shape controls).
- Recommendation: Detail REINFORCE implementation (baselines, entropy regularization, temperature schedule), number of seeds, variance across runs, and sample complexity.
- Recommendation: Perform architecture ablations to explain informed sender advantages and report vocabulary entropy and mutual information with categories.
- Recommendation: Strengthen environment manipulations (different viewpoints, occlusions, style-transfer) and report semantic shifts.
- Recommendation: Compare interleaved supervision to pretraining/fine-tuning baselines and nearest-neighbor label assignment; test grounding persistence out-of-domain.
- Recommendation: Improve human study with controls (random label mapping, visual-similarity heuristic, human-human ceiling), per-word/item accuracies, and unbiased sampling.
- Questions for authors: How many random seeds were run and what is the variance in coordination accuracy, purity, and symbol usage?
- Questions for authors: Do independently trained agents cross-play successfully, and can alignment be recovered via fine-tuning?
- Questions for authors: What RL variance-reduction techniques and temperature schedules were used?
- Questions for authors: How is purity computed and how sensitive is it to class imbalance and symbol frequency?
- Questions for authors: What is baseline purity from clustering fc vectors with the same number of clusters/symbols?
- Questions for authors: How do you prevent low-level leakage in the different-exemplar setting (e.g., background biases)?
- Questions for authors: In the human study, how does 68% compare to a nearest-neighbor ImageNet-label baseline based on visual similarity?
- Questions for authors: Does supervised grounding persist when supervised categories are disjoint from self-play categories?
- Questions for authors: How stable are vocabulary size and symbol usage across seeds and training—do synonymy/homonymy dynamics appear?
