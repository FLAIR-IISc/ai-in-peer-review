- Proposed framework: multi-agent referential games over real images where sender/receiver learn discrete communication via REINFORCE.  
- Main contributions: reliable agent coordination, emergent symbols matching high-level semantics, object-level manipulation encouraging natural semantics, and supervised grounding improving interpretability and human use (68%).  
- Decision: Accept (weak accept).  
- Key reason: tackles how to bias emergent communication toward human-like semantics with concrete, testable design choices.  
- Key reason: empirical package is convincing—convergence across settings, semantic analyses, and a human grounding study.  
- Strength: clear problem statement and sensible use of referential games with natural images.  
- Strength: well situated in prior work; novelty lies in explicit focus on interpretability via inductive biases, environment changes, supervised grounding, and human evaluation.  
- Evidence: near-perfect coordination across architectures/vocab sizes, faster convergence for informed sender.  
- Evidence: symbol clusters show above-chance purity with respect to categories, visual coherence in CNN space, and emergence of living/non-living split in a 2-symbol code.  
- Evidence: object-level setup increases purity and aligns symbols with higher-level semantics as hypothesized.  
- Evidence: mixed supervised/self-play grounding boosts symbol usage/purity and yields 68% human interpretability on ReferIt-derived regions.  
- Weakness: evaluation metrics limited (purity only); SVD analysis is suggestive and ad hoc.  
- Weakness: missing baselines and ablations (non-communicative heuristics, continuous-message baselines, message length >1, variance over random seeds).  
- Weakness: human study lacks methodological detail (screening, QC, per-word breakdown, CIs, effect sizes).  
- Weakness: little analysis of out-of-distribution or zero-shot generalization of the learned code.  
- Recommendation: report variability over multiple seeds, convergence speed, and error bars.  
- Recommendation: expand semantic analyses (mutual information with supercategories, symbol entropy, topographic similarity, per-category purity).  
- Recommendation: add stronger baselines (continuous messages, entropy regularization, longer/compositional messages).  
- Recommendation: justify and test the informed sender inductive bias across feature spaces, architectures, and temperatures.  
- Recommendation: explore grounding variants (pretrain vs interleave, supervised proportion, grounding receiver as well).  
- Recommendation: improve human evaluation reporting (CIs, exclusion criteria, demographics, confusion matrices, qualitative failure cases).  
- Recommendation: release code, data splits, and trained models for reproducibility.  
- Questions for authors: report robustness to initialization, temperature, vocab size, and embedding dim (mean±std).  
- Questions for authors: clarify purity chance computation and provide CIs for purity and obs−chance margins.  
- Questions for authors: why does the agnostic sender collapse to 2 symbols; tried entropy bonuses or KL regularization?  
- Questions for authors: in object-level setup, are target and distractors always different classes and what if they share supercategories?  
- Questions for authors: does the learned code transfer to unseen classes or other datasets without extra supervision?  
- Questions for authors: in mixed objective, how often is the supervised label emitted and does grounding improve agent-agent ReferIt performance?  
- Questions for authors: human study details—annotator filtering, per-word accuracies, concentration of successes, and CIs.  
- Questions for authors: have you tried multi-symbol messages or interactive exchanges to probe compositionality?
