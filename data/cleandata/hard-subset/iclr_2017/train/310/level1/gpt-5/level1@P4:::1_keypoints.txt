- Proposes a simple multi-agent referential game with sender/receiver learning discrete communication from scratch on real images.
- Shows tabula rasa agents trained with REINFORCE reliably and rapidly converge to successful communication.
- Analyzes how architectural biases (informed vs agnostic sender) and environment design shape emergent symbol meanings toward higher-level semantics.
- Introduces "object-level reference" (different instances shown to sender/receiver) to encourage more abstract, class-level semantics.
- Adds a grounding strategy interleaving supervised object naming that increases symbol interpretability and enables partial human-agent communication.
- Human study: after grounding, humans identify the target 68% of the time using agent symbols/labels in a 2AFC task.
- Decision: Accept — tackles an important problem with a simple, well-motivated framework and strong empirical evidence.
- Clarity: Experimental setup, architectures, REINFORCE training, and manipulations are clearly described and motivated.
- Technical soundness: REINFORCE with discrete symbols, dot-product scoring, and pretrained VGG features are appropriate and justified.
- Novelty: Distinguishes itself using large natural image sets, semantic alignment metrics (category purity, SVD), common-knowledge manipulation, and a human evaluation.
- Empirical support: Multiple analyses (vocabulary usage, SVD, category purity, t-SNE) and the human experiment back the semantic alignment claims.
- Notable finding: A two-symbol code often captures living vs non-living distinction, reflecting meaningful emergent semantics.
- Suggestion: Report full quantitative results in main text — exact accuracies, training curves, symbol counts, purity with CIs, and significance tests.
- Suggestion: Strengthen baselines — supervised-only sender, continuous channels, random mappings, and end-to-end learned encoders.
- Suggestion: Test generalization — zero-shot classes, out-of-distribution distractors, and robustness to pose/background/occlusion.
- Suggestion: Use more rigorous semantic metrics — adjusted mutual information, clustering stability, correlations with WordNet or human norms.
- Suggestion: Analyze communication bottleneck — sensitivity to vocabulary size, temperature τ, sampling vs argmax, and run variance.
- Suggestion: Clarify interpretability/synonymy — estimate effective vocabulary size (entropy/type-token ratios), and test redundancy vs context-specific synonyms.
- Suggestion: Expand human study details and controls — chance levels, interface, per-word stats, random-label control, and conventional-name calibration.
- Suggestion: Improve reproducibility — full training details (LRs, optimizers, variance reduction), data splits, and code release.
- Questions: How are train/test games constructed to avoid leakage, and are unseen object classes tested?
- Questions: Provide exact purity numbers with CIs/p-values and consistency across seeds.
- Questions: How do results vary with vocabulary sizes beyond 10/100, and do symbols collapse/fragment at extremes?
- Questions: Sensitivity to temperature and sampling vs greedy decoding at test time?
- Questions: Ablation of informed sender — which components drive faster convergence and richer symbol use?
- Questions: In grounding transfer, how much of 68% is true semantics vs dataset bias; report per-word accuracies.
- Questions: Is 68% significantly above random baseline per symbol; how does performance depend on presence of supervised label?
- Questions: Do results hold with different CNN encoders (ResNet) or without pretraining (end-to-end)?
- Questions: How interoperable are languages across runs (cross-play), and does supervision improve cross-run stability?
