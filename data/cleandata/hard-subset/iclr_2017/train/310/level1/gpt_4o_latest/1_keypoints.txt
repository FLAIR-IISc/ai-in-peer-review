- Introduces a multi-agent referential game framework where a sender and receiver learn a communication protocol to identify target images.
- Studies emergence of semantic properties, effects of game design on interpretability, and supervised grounding to human language.
- Shows agents can coordinate, develop semantically meaningful symbols, and align communication with human intuitions via supervised grounding.
- Decision: Accept — novel approach, strong empirical results, and potential impact on AI and cognitive science.
- Novelty/motivation: Emphasizes interactive, function-driven language learning over passive supervised methods; well-grounded in related literature.
- Scientific rigor: Thorough experiments, clear methodology, and robust evaluations demonstrating meaningful communication emergence.
- Referential games are a well-justified, interdisciplinary testbed drawing on linguistics, cognitive science, and game theory.
- Empirical evidence: Agents develop interpretable protocols; supervised grounding notably enhances interpretability and human-agent interaction.
- Broader implications: Offers insights into language emergence and a scalable alternative to human-in-the-loop training for conversational AI.
