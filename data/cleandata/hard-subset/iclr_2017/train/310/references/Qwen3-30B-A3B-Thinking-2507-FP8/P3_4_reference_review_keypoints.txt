- Proposes multi-agent coordination communication games for agents to learn natural-language-like communication without pre-coded protocols.  
- Shows tabula rasa agents can develop communication protocols through interaction in referential games.  
- Emergent protocols capture general conceptual/semantic properties rather than low-level visual features.  
- Introduces grounding of agents' communication in human language via interactive games plus supervised image classification.  
- Provides evidence that grounded communication is interpretable by humans, including for objects unseen during supervised training.  
- Experiments include semantic analyses, human interpretability studies, and ablation studies across game setups.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Key acceptance reasons: addresses a fundamental limitation of supervised language learning and offers a principled alternative.  
- Experimental design is rigorous with appropriate metrics and careful analysis of emergent protocols.  
- Strong positioning within literature and innovative combination of interactive learning and supervised grounding.  
- Theoretical grounding: connects work to Lewis' signaling game and cheap talk literature to explain language emergence.  
- Ablation studies show removing low-level common knowledge encourages agents to coordinate on abstract properties.  
- Suggested improvement: better situate work relative to recent large language models (e.g., GPT-3, BERT) and multi-agent RL.  
- Suggested improvement: explicitly acknowledge limitations (small image set, limited language) and discuss scalability.  
- Suggested improvement: provide deeper theoretical analysis linking game structure to emergent language properties.  
- Suggested improvement: include systematic comparisons to alternative grounding approaches (e.g., video grounding).  
- Question: Why does the informed sender use more symbols than the agnostic sender—explain convolutional architecture's role.  
- Question: How does human 68% accuracy compare to a baseline where humans see the actual ImageNet label instead of the sender's symbol?  
- Question: Define "basic communication" and propose measures for progress toward more complex communication.  
- Question: How would showing class-level properties scale to complex visual domains where class-level info may be insufficient?  
- Question: Clarify how supervised grounding generalizes beyond learned denotations, particularly for metonymic examples (e.g., "dolphin" for sea).
