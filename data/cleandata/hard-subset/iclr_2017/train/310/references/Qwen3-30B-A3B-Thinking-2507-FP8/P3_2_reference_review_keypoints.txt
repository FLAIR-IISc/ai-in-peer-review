- Introduces a novel referential-game framework where blank-slate agents develop natural-language-like communication through interaction.  
- Shows agents achieve near-perfect coordination (≈99.8% success) across configurations.  
- Emergent communication captures higher-level semantic properties rather than low-level visual features (purity metrics and t-SNE evidence).  
- Grounding via combining interactive gameplay with supervised image classification yields interpretable symbols for humans (68% human interpretability).  
- Grounding increases symbol purity to ~70% and yields ~47% direct correspondence with supervised labels.  
- Demonstrates interesting “metonymic” semantic shifts (agents using labels flexibly, e.g., “dolphin” for a stretch of sea).  
- Clearly positions contribution relative to multi-agent, human-in-the-loop, and supervised-language work.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions based on novelty, rigor, and empirical support.  
- Request to clarify the “portability” claim to novel domains, tasks, and partners beyond same-domain images.  
- Ask for discussion on scaling grounding from 100 ImageNet labels to full natural-language vocabularies and complex structures.  
- Suggest more detailed linguistic analysis of emergent phenomena (compositionality, syntax, metonymy vs. other shifts).  
- Recommend strengthening human evaluation (larger dataset than 298 pairs and detailed error analysis).  
- Request deeper analysis of why the informed sender outperforms the agnostic sender (convolutional inductive biases).  
- Question how the observed living-vs-non-living dichotomy maps to cognitive science categories (basic vs. superordinate).  
- Ask to reconcile discrepancies between purity metrics and human interpretability (70% vs. 68% and what drives differences).  
- Query effects of alternative discrete-communication methods (Gumbel-Softmax, straight-through) on emergent semantics.  
- Ask how removing common knowledge or training on different image sets would alter emergent language.  
- Overall assessment: substantial contribution bridging theoretical language emergence and practical grounded communication with humans.
