- Study of emergent communication in a two-agent referential game with natural images, comparing agnostic vs informed sender, object-level variant, and supervised grounding; human interpretability 68%.

- Decision: Reject due to insufficient novelty and weak empirical rigor.

- Positioning: Setup is close to prior work; contributions (natural images, informed sender, supervised grounding) are incremental.

- Object-level manipulation is conceptually interesting but yields modest empirical payoff and limited testing.

- Core semantic evidence relies on weak proxies (category purity, t-SNE); stronger metrics are missing.

- Missing metrics include mutual information, topographic similarity, speaker/listener consistency, entropy/communication rate, and confusion matrices.

- Baselines and controls are inadequate: no capacity-matched ablation for senders, no supervised baselines, and no analysis of temperature, sampling vs argmax, or variance-reduction.

- No generalization or portability tests such as cross-play between independently trained agents or unseen-category evaluation.

- Statistical rigor is lacking: no confidence intervals, significance tests, or results across multiple random seeds.

- The living vs non-living observation is anecdotal and not quantitatively validated.

- Human study potentially biased: pairs sampled where agent succeeded, limited label overlap, and no comparison to ungrounded or supervised baselines.

- Claims of portability and human alignment are overstated given the limited and favorable human evaluation.

- The informed sender’s greater symbol usage may reflect synonymy or instability rather than meaningful semantics; SVD analysis is suggestive but not decisive.

- Reproducibility issues: missing optimizer, learning rates, REINFORCE baseline details, seed counts, exact architecture and parameter counts, and unclear τ definition.

- Suggested semantic quantification: compute mutual information, topographic similarity, speaker/listener consistency, entropy, confusion matrices, and explicit living vs non-living classifier accuracy.

- Suggested robustness tests: cross-play, unseen-category evaluation, harder distractors, varying vocabulary sizes and temperatures, and reporting stability over seeds.

- Suggested ablations/baselines: capacity-matched sender comparisons, remove/alter inductive biases, supervised “emit label if available” baseline, and post-hoc label-alignment baselines.

- Suggested human evaluation improvements: include ungrounded and random-word controls, unbiased sampling, per-word performance, inter-annotator agreement, and systematic analysis of semantic shifts.

- Reporting improvements: provide full hyperparameters, code, τ clarification, learning curves with error bands, and number-of-seeds and variance.

- Questions for authors: report number of seeds and variance in results.

- Questions for authors: specify optimizer, learning rates, REINFORCE variance-reduction, and sensitivity to τ and sampling vs argmax.

- Questions for authors: are informed and agnostic senders capacity-matched, and does matching reduce gaps?

- Questions for authors: provide cross-play results to support portability.

- Questions for authors: do symbols generalize to unseen object classes when categories are held out?

- Questions for authors: quantify binary living vs non-living prediction accuracy from emitted symbols.

- Questions for authors: report human study baselines (ungrounded agent, naive supervised) and describe pair sampling to avoid bias.

- Questions for authors: explain how supervised labels are tied to symbols—any parameter tying or only emergent alignment?

- Questions for authors: provide significance tests and confidence intervals for purity and SVD dimensionality claims.
