- Paper studies emergent communication in a two-agent referential game with discrete single-symbol messages and real ImageNet-derived image features.  
- Main empirical findings: near-perfect coordination with REINFORCE-trained feed-forward agents; informed convolutional sender yields faster convergence and richer symbol use than an agnostic sender.  
- Analysis shows emergent semantics correlate with category structure (category purity, SVD of usage, t-SNE visualizations), especially when sender/receiver see different exemplars (object-level reference).  
- Joint supervised/self-play grounding increases category purity, encourages conventional object names, generalizes to a new dataset, and yields 68% human accuracy in a ReferIt 2AFC test.  
- Paper is well-motivated, timely, and positioned within cheap talk, language evolution, and multi-agent RL literature.  
- Methodology is clear: discrete messages via temperature-scaled softmax, REINFORCE training, two sender architectures differing in inductive bias.  
- Empirical scope: tested design levers (architecture, representation layer, vocab size, object-level constraint, supervised grounding) with measured conclusions about human-aligned semantics.  
- Suggestion: control for context dependence (measure mutual information between symbol and target conditioned on distractor; report symbol stability across distractors).  
- Suggestion: complement purity with topographic similarity or RSA to quantify global alignment between image/semantic space and message space.  
- Suggestion: provide permutation tests/CIs or other significance tests to show purity is above chance.  
- Concern: pretrained ImageNet features may inject category semantics—ablate with lower-level conv or self-supervised features to isolate emergence from feature priors.  
- Concern: REINFORCE variance—report whether a baseline/critic was used, learning curves, number of seeds, variance in convergence and sample efficiency; justify temperature τ=10 and explore sensitivity and entropy regularization.  
- Suggestion: analyze degeneracy/synonymy (message entropy, effective vocabulary size, Zipf behavior, symbol replacement tests) to see if symbols are functionally redundant.  
- Suggestion: evaluate cross-play/zero-shot compatibility across independently trained agent pairs and few-shot partner adaptation to test generality.  
- Suggestion: strengthen human evaluation controls (random/shuffled-message baselines, per-word difficulty, semantically close foils, free-response) to better assess interpretability.  
- Caution: avoid overclaiming "natural language" emergence—single-symbol messages lack compositionality; outline steps toward multi-symbol/sequential interaction for syntax.  
- Key author questions to address: context-invariance of symbols, use of REINFORCE baselines and number of seeds, sensitivity to τ/sampling method (Gumbel-Softmax), effect of different feature layers/self-supervised features, cross-play compatibility, and statistical magnitude/significance of purity gains.
