- Paper studies emergence of natural-language-like communication between tabula rasa sender/receiver agents in referential games using real images.

- Main claims: agents reliably learn to communicate; emergent lexicon aligns with high-level concepts rather than low-level features; supervised grounding + self-play increases human interpretability.

- Introduces an “informed sender” inductive bias, an object-level reference manipulation (limiting common knowledge), and mixed supervised/self-play grounding.

- Decision: Weak accept — novel, well-motivated task linking emergent communication to natural images and human interpretability.

- Strength: Clear, minimal referential game design allows controlled study of environmental and architectural effects.

- Strength: Empirical result that agents quickly reach near-perfect coordination; informed sender converges faster and uses more symbols.

- Evidence: Symbols exhibit above-chance alignment with object categories; a living vs. non-living split emerges even with two symbols.

- Evidence: Object-level reference increases symbol–object purity, supporting the hypothesis that removing low-level common knowledge promotes abstract reference.

- Analyses: t-SNE shows symbol coherence in CNN feature space; SVD indicates multi-dimensional structure beyond trivial synonymy.

- Grounding: Supervised labels substantially increase purity and symbol usage; sender often reproduces conventional labels and shows metonymic usage on out-of-domain ReferIt images.

- Human study: Participants chose intended target 68% of the time (vs. 50% chance), with a significant logistic regression effect.

- Reporting improvement: Include confidence intervals/variance across multiple random seeds for coordination, purity, symbol usage, and SVD dimensionality.

- Analysis detail: Fully describe SVD methodology (data matrix, cross-validation, dimensionality choice) and consider measures like topographic similarity and mutual information.

- Human study rigor: Report effect sizes, confidence intervals, left/right balancing, random-word controls, inter-rater variability, and exclusion criteria; add baselines.

- Clarify train/test splits and whether supervised labels overlap with game concepts; analyze generalization to unseen concepts.

- Baselines/ablations: Compare to non-communicative or trivial protocols, vary message bottleneck size, temperature/entropy regularization, and report REINFORCE variance-reduction and training stability.

- Scalability: Test more distractors, multi-symbol messages, and multi-round interaction to probe compositionality and robustness.

- Scope caution: Temper claims about “natural language resemblance” since codes are single-symbol and task-specific; 68% human success is promising but modest.

- Confound to discuss: Category-level structure may reflect VGG/ImageNet pretraining rather than purely emergent semantics; object-level control helps but discuss explicitly.

- Reproducibility: Provide code, full hyperparameters (optimizer, learning rates, baselines, annealing schedules) and list of the 100 supervised labels.

- Key technical questions: Stability of emergent lexicons across random seeds and permutation alignment of symbols to categories.

- Key metric questions: Exact purity computation (micro vs. macro), chance baseline estimation, and reporting of confidence/significance.

- Training sensitivity questions: What variance-reduction techniques were used with REINFORCE and how sensitive are results to temperature τ and embedding dimensionality?

- Mixed-supervision question: Do agents overfit to supervised classes and how do they perform when test referents are all unseen categories?

- Compositionality questions: How do performance and purity change with more than two distractors or when messages are sequences; is more compositional structure observed?

- Ambiguity quantification: Can you report synonymy/homonymy measures (entropy of symbol|object and object|symbol) and topographic similarity?

- Human study controls: Did the human experiment include random-word or non-grounded-agent controls and proper position balancing to rule out biases?
