- Paper studies emergent communication in a two-agent referential game with real ImageNet-derived images, asking if agents can learn to communicate, what yields interpretable concepts, and if codes can be grounded in human language.
- Compares two sender architectures: agnostic MLP vs an “informed” sender that compares image embeddings dimension-wise, using REINFORCE and a discrete single-symbol bottleneck.
- Empirically, agents reach near-perfect coordination; the informed sender converges faster and uses a richer symbol inventory with symbols correlating with high-level concepts (category purity, t-SNE, living/non-living split).
- Changing common knowledge by showing different images of the same class pushes codes toward class-level semantics; interleaving a supervised naming task grounds symbols and increases purity and symbol usage.
- Human evaluation shows grounded sender words let humans pick the intended image 68% of the time, indicating partial interpretability and metonymic flexibility.
- Decision: Accept (weak) based on importance of the problem, simple compelling setup, and supportive empirical and human-study evidence.
- Motivation and placement: Well-motivated focus on interpretability and realistic visual inputs, clearly positioned relative to prior emergent communication and signaling work.
- Correctness and rigor: Standard, appropriate training setup and plausible architectural claims; interventions are logically motivated and effects align with hypotheses.
- Evidence: Multiple analyses (category purity, SVD, t-SNE) and a sizable human study provide triangulated support for semantic structure in emergent symbols.
- Limitation: Missing variance reporting across random seeds and limited statistical reporting for key metrics (e.g., confidence intervals).
- Limitation: Communication protocol is highly restricted (single-symbol, no compositionality) and lacks analysis of code topology (e.g., topographic similarity).
- Limitation: Lack of empirical baselines (continuous channel, ST-Gumbel, alternative receivers) and under-specified design choices (temperature, variance-reduction baselines).
- Suggestion: Report mean±std over multiple random seeds and provide learning curves to assess stability.
- Suggestion: Include full referenced results tables (fc vs softmax features; vocab sizes) currently missing.
- Suggestion: Quantify interpretability via topographic similarity, mutual information between symbols and categories, and zero-shot generalization tests.
- Suggestion: Analyze the lexicon for confusions, synonymy/polysemy, SVD dimensionality vs vocab size, and show symbol–category heatmaps and cluster labels.
- Suggestion: Run partner-swap experiments to test conventionality and portability of the learned protocol.
- Suggestion: Ablations on temperature, entropy regularization, REINFORCE baselines, annealing, embedding sizes, and message bandwidth to probe effects on diversity and convergence.
- Suggestion: Explore compositional protocols (multi-symbol sequences) and interactive exchanges to see if more complex semantics emerge.
- Suggestion: Strengthen human study controls (random baselines, word frequency effects), report inter-annotator agreement, and analyze per-word performance and errors.
- Suggestion: Clean up incomplete references.
- Question: How robust are results across random seeds for success rate, purity, symbol usage, and SVD dimensionality?
- Question: Did you use any variance-reduction baseline for REINFORCE, and how does adding a baseline or entropy regularization affect symbol diversity and convergence?
- Question: How sensitive are outcomes to temperature τ and sampling vs argmax at test time?
- Question: In the supervised grounding experiment, how are the 100 labels mapped to the 100-symbol vocabulary during self-play (one-to-one or shared embeddings)?
- Question: Can the grounded sender communicate with a newly initialized receiver trained only to imitate messages (partner-swap test)?
- Question: Do agents generalize to unseen object categories or out-of-distribution images, or is there evidence of overfitting to ImageNet classes?
- Question: What happens with multi-symbol messages or longer interactions—do you observe more compositional or fine-grained semantics?
- Question: How do purity and human interpretability compare when using softmax-layer (class-probability) features vs fc features; does low-level class bias inflate purity?
