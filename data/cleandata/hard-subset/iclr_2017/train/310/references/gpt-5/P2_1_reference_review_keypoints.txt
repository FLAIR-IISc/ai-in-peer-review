- Introduces a two-agent referential game over natural-image features with discrete-symbol messages trained via REINFORCE.  
- Compares two sender architectures (agnostic vs. informed) showing both achieve near-perfect coordination; informed sender converges faster and uses a richer, less redundant inventory.  
- Analyzes symbol semantics via cluster purity against McRae categories and visual-space structure, finding above-chance alignment and a living/non-living split with two symbols.  
- Shows that altering “common knowledge” (sender/receiver see different images of same class) promotes object-level semantics and improves alignment.  
- Combines self-play with supervised object naming to ground symbols in natural language, increasing purity, symbol usage, and human interpretability.  
- Human crowdsourced test: participants identify target given agent word 68% of time (vs. 50% chance), with qualitative metonymic evidence.  
- Overall claim: provides a practical path to designing environments and light supervision that foster human-aligned emergent communication.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: well-motivated question with clear, testable interventions (architecture, common-knowledge, mixed training) to steer emergent communication.  
- Empirical support judged convincing and strengthened by a human study demonstrating semantic alignment beyond coordination.  
- Problem clarity: explicitly targets portability of emergent language to new settings and human partners with principled interventions.  
- Positioning: builds on multi-agent communication and signaling-game literature while moving to realistic images and human interpretability.  
- Methodological soundness: clean referential game setup, appropriate use of discrete messages and REINFORCE, and controlled architectural comparisons.  
- Evidence triangulation: multiple metrics (coordination, symbol diversity, SVD, purity, visual coherence) argue against trivial memorization.  
- Scope and realism: use of natural images and large concept sets increases ecological validity relative to synthetic data.  
- Limitation: results are largely descriptive; some quantitative details (purity scores, variance across runs) are underreported.  
- Limitation: uses single-symbol messages only; compositionality and multi-round interaction are unexplored.  
- Limitation: missing baseline comparisons for human interpretability (e.g., supervised classifier labels).  
- Limitation: partner-robustness (cross-play or new agents) is not evaluated.  
- Limitation: human study is modest in size and constrained to pairwise choices.  
- Suggestion: report full quantitative results with variability (seeds, mean±std, confidence intervals, chance baselines, effect sizes).  
- Suggestion: add ablations for temperature, vocabulary size, embedding dim, variance reduction for REINFORCE, and sender inductive bias impact.  
- Suggestion: include partner-robustness tests such as zero-shot communication with new receivers and cross-play between independently trained agents.  
- Suggestion: add baseline human comparison using purely supervised classifier labels or random ImageNet labels to isolate interaction effects.  
- Suggestion: explore longer message sequences and potential compositionality; analyze synonymy/homonymy information-theoretically.  
- Suggestion: analyze the living vs. non-living split more systematically with external ontologies and provide concrete symbol-object examples.  
- Suggestion: clean up references and share code and data splits for reproducibility.  
- Questions: report number of random seeds and variability in purity, symbol usage, and convergence across seeds and architectures.  
- Questions: did you use variance-reduction in REINFORCE; how sensitive are results to temperature τ and sampling vs. argmax at test time?  
- Questions: can receivers trained with one sender decode messages from a different independently trained sender (cross-play); does mixed supervised training improve cross-play?  
- Questions: provide exact purity numbers (observed vs. chance) for all conditions (agnostic/informed; 10 vs. 100 symbols; image- vs. class-level; with supervision).  
- Questions: in the human study, report per-word accuracies, inter-annotator agreement, and accuracy vs. semantic distance between emitted word and target.  
- Questions: does increasing message length or allowing multi-round interaction improve category purity and human interpretability?  
- Questions: how does performance scale when increasing number of candidate images per game?
