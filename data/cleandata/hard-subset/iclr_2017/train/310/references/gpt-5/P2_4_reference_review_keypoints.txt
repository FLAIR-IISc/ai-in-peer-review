- Study of emergent discrete one-symbol communication in a two-agent referential game with real images using REINFORCE.  
- Comparison of two sender architectures: agnostic MLP vs informed sender with per-dimension comparison bias.  
- Agents reliably converge to high coordination accuracy; the informed sender learns faster and uses more of the vocabulary.  
- Probes show emergent symbols align above chance with McRae semantic categories (cluster purity).  
- A living vs. non-living distinction emerges even with only two symbols.  
- Object-level variant (different images of same class shown to each agent) reduces low-level common knowledge and increases semantic purity.  
- Grounding via joint supervised object-labeling (shared embedding) increases symbol purity and yields interpretable symbols.  
- Crowdsourced 2AFC human study: people pick the sender’s intended referent 68% of the time, including for images outside supervised labels.  
- Evidence suggests meaningful generalization and metonymic use of emergent signals.  
- Decision: Weak Accept.  
- Strength: Clear, well-motivated question with a simple, scalable setup using realistic inputs.  
- Strength: Environment design and light supervision push codes toward human-interpretable semantics; includes human evaluation.  
- Methodologically sound and likely to inspire follow-up work on grounding and alignment.  
- Approach: Simple referential game and reasonable inductive bias (informed sender) for discrimination tasks.  
- Object-level manipulation is a neat, game-theoretic intervention to encourage higher-level meanings.  
- Claims of reliable communication are well supported by training results and probes (t-SNE, purity, human study statistics).  
- Rigor: Training details provided and use of pre-extracted VGG features keeps focus on communication.  
- Human study size (~3k judgments) and analysis reported with strong significance.  
- Limitation: Evaluation restricted to 2AFC, which may overestimate language quality; compositionality and sample efficiency not assessed.  
- Limitation: Purity is a coarse metric; synonymy analysis via SVD is heuristic and could be replaced with information-theoretic measures.  
- Limitation: No partner-swap (zero-shot coordination) tests to assess code portability across agents.  
- Limitation: Limited ablation/sensitivity analysis on vocabulary size, temperature, RL variance, and capacity/bias effects.  
- Limitation: Grounding depends on supervised label embedding; robustness to label mismatch, polysemy, and sparse labels unclear.  
- Limitation: Reproducibility issues—citation formatting broken and no statement on code/data release.  
- Actionable: Add partner-swap experiments to test portability and convention stabilization.  
- Actionable: Include principled metrics (mutual information, topographic similarity, message entropy) and report CIs across seeds.  
- Actionable: Probe compositionality and attribute-level generalization with harder distractor sets.  
- Actionable: Provide ablations for capacity-matched senders, vocabulary/temperature, supervision fraction, end-to-end vs fixed features, and continuous relaxations.  
- Actionable: Expand human evaluation (control for frequency/familiarity, add free-text justification, test few-shot interaction).  
- Actionable: Improve presentation—fix references, detail splits, report variance, and clarify decoding at test time.  
- Questions: Report robustness across seeds for purity and symbol usage (mean±std).  
- Questions: Do partner-swap tests maintain coordination, and does grounding help zero-shot coordination?  
- Questions: Sensitivity to vocabulary size and τ; does purity peak at intermediate bottleneck strengths?  
- Questions: In object-level variant, were background correlations controlled to avoid low-level cues?  
- Questions: Provide MI between symbols and semantic classes and topographic similarity measures.  
- Questions: How were ambiguous/polysemous supervised words handled in the human study; do concrete words perform better?  
- Questions: Does the informed sender’s advantage persist when capacity is matched, or is it mainly an inductive bias?  
- Questions: Will code and supervised label lists be released for reproducibility?
