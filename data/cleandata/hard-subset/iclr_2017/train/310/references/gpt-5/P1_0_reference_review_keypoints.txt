- Paper studies emergence of discrete communication between sender/receiver neural agents trained with REINFORCE on referential games using real-image VGG features.
- Compares an “agnostic” MLP sender vs. an “informed” sender (1-D conv across paired embeddings); both achieve coordination, informed converges faster and uses a richer vocabulary.
- Analysis shows learned symbols align with higher-level semantics (cluster purity, t-SNE) rather than low-level visual cues; living vs. non-living splits often emerge.
- Introduces an object-level variant where sender and receiver see different exemplars of the same class, which increases semantic purity by removing low-level common knowledge.
- Proposes grounding by interleaving supervised object naming, which boosts symbol purity and interpretability and yields words humans can interpret.
- Human evaluation: given sender’s symbol-word, humans pick intended referent 68% of the time (vs. 50% chance), showing above-chance interpretability.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reasons for accept: well-motivated study showing environment design nudges emergent communication toward human-aligned semantics; human study and grounding improve interpretability and broader relevance.
- Problem & motivation: addresses when/how language-like, human-interpretable codes arise for coordination; situates well in signaling games and multi-agent communication literature.
- Methodology: simple, transparent experimental design using discrete symbols with RL and precomputed VGG features to isolate communication dynamics.
- Coordination result: near-perfect test accuracy across settings demonstrates the communication problem is solved.
- Semantics result: cluster purity above chance and SVD analysis indicate nontrivial semantic structure; object-level variant further increases semantic alignment.
- Grounding result: supervised naming increases symbol usage/purity and encourages conventional labels; qualitative examples show plausible metonymy.
- Novelty/impact: contribution centers on architectural bias effects, environment design for abstraction, and practical grounding with human evaluation, likely to influence follow-up work.
- Rigor/reporting recommendation: report means/stds and statistical tests, multiple random seeds, and variance for purity, vocabulary usage, and convergence.
- SVD/reporting suggestion: clarify matrix construction, cross-validation for rank selection, and quantitative rank choices vs. baselines.
- Ablations requested: vary vocabulary size, temperature, entropy regularization; report symbol entropy and mutual information with object classes.
- REINFORCE details requested: state reward baseline/variance reduction methods, temperature annealing, entropy bonuses, and whether baselines were used.
- Baselines/controls requested: compare to continuous-communication and Gumbel-softmax relaxations; test with raw pixels or earlier CNN layers to control for pretraining.
- Stronger metrics requested: add topographic similarity, ARI/NMI clustering, and RSA between symbol distributions and visual embeddings.
- Generalization tests requested: zero-shot to unseen categories, partner-swapping, harder negatives, more distractors, multi-symbol sequences, and dialog settings.
- Human study improvements: report per-word difficulty, inter-annotator agreement, stronger baselines (e.g., frequency-biased guesser), and anonymized-symbol controls.
- Clarity improvements: clearer diagram/math for the informed sender; specify VGG feature normalization and quantify differences between sm vs. fc features.
- Questions for authors: what reward baselines/variance reduction were used and were results stable across seeds?
- Questions for authors: how sensitive are results to temperature and sampling vs. argmax at train/test time?
- Questions for authors: how many random seeds per configuration and what is variability in accuracy, purity, and symbol usage?
- Questions for authors: can you quantify symbol redundancy/synonymy (mutual information or clustering of symbol usage)?
- Questions for authors: in object-level setting, do agents maintain coordination with hard negatives from same superordinate category and how does purity change?
- Questions for authors: how does the approach generalize zero-shot and does supervised grounding help more there?
- Questions for authors: in the human eval, was a control mapping symbols to random English words or anonymized symbols tested, and how do results change?
- Request: please release code and human study materials to facilitate replication.
