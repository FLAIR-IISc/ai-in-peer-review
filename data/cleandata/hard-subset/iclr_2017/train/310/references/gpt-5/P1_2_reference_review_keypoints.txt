- Paper studies emergence of grounded communication between two neural agents in a single-symbol referential game over real images, comparing agnostic vs. “informed” sender architectures.  
- Key findings: agents reliably learn to communicate; informed sender converges faster and uses richer vocabularies.  
- Emergent symbols align with high-level semantics (cluster purity above chance vs. McRae categories) and are not trivially low-dimensional.  
- Removing low-level common knowledge (different images for sender/receiver) increases symbol abstraction/purity.  
- Mixing supervised object naming with game-play greatly increases symbol purity (~70%), vocabulary usage, and induces direct name–symbol alignment (~47% exact label use).  
- Crowdsourced human evaluation on ReferItGame shows humans map symbols to targets at 68% accuracy, indicating partial human interpretability.  
- Decision: Accept — addresses an important question with a simple clear framework, compelling empirical findings, and a human study.  
- Strength: advances emergent communication using real images at scale, focuses on interpretability, and situates well within related literature.  
- Empirical rigor: multiple analyses (t-SNE, SVD) support claims though some evaluations are qualitative or heuristic.  
- Suggestion — deeper evaluation: report symbol entropy, mutual information with categories, topographic similarity, and confidence intervals/statistical tests across seeds.  
- Suggestion — generalization tests: evaluate zero-shot on unseen object classes and out-of-distribution distractors.  
- Suggestion — training details: clarify REINFORCE variance-reduction, entropy regularization, temperature, sampling vs. greedy test-time, and stability across seeds.  
- Suggestion — sensitivity/ablations: vary vocabulary size, temperature, embedding dims, filter counts, and ablate the informed inductive bias.  
- Suggestion — stronger baselines: compare to differentiable communication (continuous channels, Gumbel-Softmax), attention-based senders, and non-communicative baselines.  
- Suggestion — improve human study: address selection bias, sample randomly, report per-word performance and inter-subject agreement, control for word frequency.  
- Suggestion — broader probing: test partner-swapping, multi-symbol utterances, and interactive/multi-step communication for compositionality.  
- Reproducibility request: release code, data splits, full hyperparameters, number of runs, and variance bars.  
- Questions for authors: details on REINFORCE baseline/entropy/test-time policy; number of seeds and variance in purity/accuracy; symbol entropy and mutual information with ImageNet/McRae categories; generalization to unseen classes and new receivers; sensitivity to vocabulary size K; exact human study sampling procedure and inter-subject agreement; whether other inductive biases or multi-step setups were tried.  
- Overall: well-motivated and impactful; empirical evidence supports claims and light supervision offers a practical route to human-aligned grounding, but recommended analyses would strengthen the paper.
