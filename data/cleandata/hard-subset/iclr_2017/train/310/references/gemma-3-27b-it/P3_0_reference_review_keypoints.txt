- Paper studies emergence of communication in a referential multi-agent game, focusing on conceptual association and human interpretability, and shows environment manipulations plus supervised naming improve semantic coherence.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — strong contribution with well-defined experiments and compelling results, especially the grounding experiments.
- Clear problem definition and motivation, framed against limits of pure supervised learning and illustrated via Ted Chiang’s “Story of Your Life.”
- Rigorous experimental design comparing agnostic vs informed sender architectures, environment manipulations, and a supervised naming phase.
- Compelling results: consistent coordination, emergent language purity aligned with conceptual categories, and greatly improved human interpretability through supervision.
- t-SNE visualizations and qualitative analyses support claims of semantic coherence.
- Strong connection to prior work in multi-agent systems, linguistics, cognitive science, game theory, Lewis’ signaling game, and Nash equilibria.
- Novelty in designing environments that foster interpretable language and in the grounding experiments linking emergent codes to human labels.
- Suggest deeper analysis of failure cases to identify image pairs or symbols that cause miscommunication.
- Recommend ablation studies on supervised learning parameters (vocabulary size, supervised vs game-play ratio) to quantify their effects.
- Ask for discussion on generalization and scalability to more complex environments and strategies to address those challenges.
- Request more qualitative examples showing which symbols correspond to specific images to improve interpretability.
- Ask authors to report computational cost and training resources required.
- Question why the informed sender achieves higher coordination faster — purely inductive bias or other factors?
- Question sensitivity of results to the quality and coverage of supervised labels (e.g., ImageNet) and robustness to noisy labels.
- Question why metonymic (indirect) mappings appear in human evaluations — limited vocabulary vs intrinsic emergent communication patterns?
- Question whether using separate vs shared embedding layers for game and classification tasks was tried and how it affected performance.
