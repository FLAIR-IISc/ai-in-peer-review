- Paper studies emergence of communication in referential games, comparing sender architectures and grounding emergent language via supervised learning to improve human interpretability.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Clear motivation and problem framing about building AI that communicates with humans and other agents.  
- Strong empirical results showing agents can coordinate from tabula rasa and that inductive biases (informed vs agnostic senders) shape communication.  
- Grounding emergent language with supervised learning yields improved human interpretability.  
- Novelty: designing environments to foster interpretable language and using supervised grounding is a promising, original direction.  
- Rigorous methodology using reinforcement learning, quantitative metrics (purity), t-SNE visualizations, and human evaluations.  
- Request more detailed linguistic analysis of emergent languages, including concrete symbol sequences and ambiguity quantification.  
- Suggest ablation studies on key hyperparameters (embedding dimensionality, temperature) to assess their impact.  
- Section 2 (General Framework) is too abstract and would benefit from a concrete example.  
- Presentation improvements needed: clearer figure captions and highlighted key observations (e.g., Figure 3).  
- Ask for a more thorough discussion of limitations (simple games, potential ImageNet biases).  
- Question: provide specific emergent language examples linking symbols to image pairs and typical triggers.  
- Question: discuss scalability to more complex environments and larger agent populations and required modifications.  
- Question: report whether alternative grounding strategies beyond supervised learning were explored and their outcomes.  
- Question: give more details on the human evaluation crowd-sourcing setup and participant demographics.
