- Explores emergence of communication between agents in a referential game, testing sender architecture and environment effects on interpretability.
- Shows informed sender architecture and a supervised grounding phase substantially improve human interpretability of emergent languages.
- Frames the work in Lewis’ signaling game and connects to linguistics, cognitive science, and game theory.
- Uses ImageNet and pre-trained CNNs in a controlled experimental setup to provide realistic input space.
- Experimental design is simple yet well-defined, enabling controlled investigation of factors influencing language emergence.
- Evaluation includes coordination success rate, symbol usage analysis, and crowdsourced human evaluation.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions due to theoretical grounding and empirical strength.
- Clear problem definition and motivation, emphasizing the need for interactive learning beyond supervised approaches.
- Strong empirical results demonstrating effective agent communication and interpretable symbol use by informed senders.
- Novel contribution in focusing on interpretability and environmental design to facilitate emergent language.
- Rigorous, multi-faceted evaluation strengthens credibility of findings.
- Suggestion to discuss failure cases and analyze instances where communication failed or was challenging.
- Recommend more detailed ablation studies to isolate contributions of specific components (e.g., convolutional filters).
- Request more consistent reporting of statistical significance and p-values across experiments.
- Encourage deeper analysis and examples of the observed “metonymic” links in agent-human mappings.
- Ask authors to report computational resources (GPUs, training time) to aid reproducibility.
- Question whether supervised grounding was tried with datasets beyond ImageNet or richer annotations (e.g., referring expressions).
- Ask whether methods were tried to reduce vocabulary redundancy (regularization, pruning).
- Question generalizability to multi-agent/multi-party communication and anticipated challenges.
- Inquire about any observed non-convergence or oscillatory behavior during training and contributing factors.
- Ask why the Reinforce algorithm was chosen and whether alternative RL algorithms were considered.
