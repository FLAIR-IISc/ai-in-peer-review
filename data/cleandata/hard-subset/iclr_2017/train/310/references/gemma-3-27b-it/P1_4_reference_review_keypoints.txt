- Paper introduces a referential game to study emergent communication and proposes grounding via combined game-playing and supervised learning.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Strength: Clearly motivates need for interactive emergent communication beyond supervised pattern recognition.
- Strength: Sound experimental design using ImageNet and varied sender architectures/environmental manipulations.
- Strength: Compelling results showing coordination, informed sender yields more diverse/interpretable signals, and supervision improves grounding.
- Strength: Thorough related work and strong positioning within multi-agent communication and linguistic theory.
- Suggestion: Provide more details on the Reinforce implementation, baseline, exploration strategy, and learning rate handling.
- Suggestion: Perform deeper ablation studies on the "informed" sender to isolate benefits of convolutional filters versus complexity.
- Suggestion: Visualize learned sender/receiver embeddings (e.g., t-SNE/UMAP) to illustrate representations.
- Suggestion: Discuss failure cases and limitations, including challenging image pairs and conditions causing coordination breakdown.
- Question: Did training exhibit multiple equilibria or convergence to suboptimal strategies before better protocols emerged?
- Question: Did the supervised shared embedding risk overwriting learned protocols, and were alternative gradual-transfer architectures explored?
- Question: For human interpretability, provide symbol-level accuracy distribution and correlation between symbol frequency and interpretability.
- Question: What are the challenges to scaling to more agents, larger vocabularies, and more complex scenarios?
