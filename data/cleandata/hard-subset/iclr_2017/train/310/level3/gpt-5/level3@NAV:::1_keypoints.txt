- Paper studies emergent communication in referential games and shows coordination, richer symbol use by an “informed” sender, modest semantic structure, and improved human-interpretability when mixed with supervised naming.
- Decision: Reject — main reasons are task reduction to image-category discrimination and insufficient evidence that findings require interactive RL.
- Core critique: the game often collapses to class-label encoding when images are from different categories, weakening claims about language-like emergence.
- Missing baselines: no supervised classifier with a discrete bottleneck or other non-RL models to show necessity of interaction/RL.
- Analyses (purity, t-SNE, SVD) suggest structure but are inconclusive without same-category controls and stronger distractors.
- Strength: problem is important and the simple game setup aids interpretability.
- Related work/motivation: well-situated in emergent communication and human-grounding literature.
- Evidence/rigor: need controls with same-class target/distractor pairs to test fine-grained semantics.
- Suggested baselines: frozen VGG + shallow classifier with discrete code, supervised/IB models, straight-through/Gumbel estimators.
- Suggested control tasks: same-category pairs, adversarial nearest-neighbor distractors, held-out classes, and cross-play between independently trained agents.
- Requested metrics: sample efficiency, variance across seeds, vocabulary entropy, topographic similarity, mutual information between symbols and class/attributes.
- Test compositionality by evaluating multi-object scenes.
- Clarify human evaluation by stratifying in- vs out-of-supervised-label sets and analyzing error/metonymic uses.
- Specific question: what fraction of train/test pairs are same-category vs different-category and how do results change when conditioning on same-category pairs?
- Specific question: how does a supervised classifier with a discrete bottleneck perform (accuracy, purity, symbol usage) compared to RL?
- Specific question: do results hold with stronger distractors (nearest neighbors in fc space) and with frozen visual features?
- Specific question: how stable are symbol inventories across runs and does cross-play succeed between independently trained agents?
- Specific question: can you quantify the living/non-living structure (e.g., mutual information) and show generalization to unseen categories?
