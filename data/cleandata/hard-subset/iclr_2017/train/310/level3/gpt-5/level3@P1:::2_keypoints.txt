- Proposal: Introduces a simple multi-agent referential game framework for emergent discrete communication about real images using only success/failure feedback.  
- Main results: Agents reliably and rapidly learn to coordinate.  
- Main results: An “informed sender” architectural inductive bias speeds convergence and yields richer symbol use.  
- Main results: Environment design (removing image-level common knowledge) pushes symbol meanings toward higher-level object semantics.  
- Main results: Mixing self-play with supervised object naming grounds the code in human language, improving interpretability and human transfer.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Clear, well-motivated framework with novel insights on how environment and inductive bias shape emergent communication and human-alignment.  
- Key reason 2: Convincing empirical demonstration of coordination and partial grounding, including a human study.  
- Problem/positioning: Addresses training interactive language agents beyond passive supervised learning and which game designs foster transferable, interpretable language.  
- Approach: Clean referential game using pre-trained vision features, discrete communication via REINFORCE, and two sender variants to probe architectural bias.  
- Approach: Object-level reference and combined supervised+game training are sensible design choices to encourage higher-level semantics and grounding.  
- Empirical support: Near-perfect accuracy across settings; informed sender converges faster and uses more symbols; SVD and cluster purity show nontrivial signal structure.  
- Empirical support: Human study shows 68% correct identification on out-of-domain pairs, indicating partial interpretability and metonymic flexibility.  
- Scope/impact: Framework is extensible (longer messages, multi-turn, role rotation, human-in-the-loop) and relevant for emergent communication and human-AI interaction research.  
- Clarity request: Improve architecture figure and provide detailed tensor shapes, data flow, and mathematical description of sender scoring in both variants.  
- Experimental reporting request: Include variance across seeds, learning curves with confidence bands, and sensitivity analyses for vocabulary size and temperature; report stability of symbol usage and purity.  
- Baselines/ablations request: Compare to continuous communication and Gumbel-Softmax/straight-through estimators, add a non-communicative baseline, and ablate pre-trained vision features.  
- Metrics request: Complement purity with topographic similarity or representational similarity analyses to quantify alignment between visual and signal spaces.  
- Human study request: Provide participant demographics, instructions, quality controls, inter-participant agreement, effect sizes, and error-type analysis.  
- Related work request: Expand discussion of multi-agent differentiable communication and cognitive science signaling games to better position the contribution.  
- Limitations/next steps: Discuss compositionality, multi-symbol messages, attribute-based tasks, role rotation, and multi-step negotiation for conversational competence.  
- Reproducibility/questions: Report stability across seeds, sensitivity to τ/learning rates/vocabulary size, and whether results degrade with larger vocabularies.  
- Discrete sampling question: Did the authors try Gumbel-Softmax or straight-through estimators and how do they compare to REINFORCE?  
- Symmetry question: Could sender/receiver ordering create positional shortcuts; what if both orders are randomized or target index provided separately?  
- Purity/statistics question: How is chance purity computed, are differences significant across runs, and can confidence intervals be reported for purity and SVD diagnostics?  
- Object-level reference question: Beyond modest purity gains, are there clearer semantic clusters or improved topographic similarity; provide qualitative contrasts.  
- Grounding/generalization question: How often are supervised labels used metaphorically vs. literally, and how does transfer perform on non-supervised categories?  
- Compositionality question: Have multi-symbol messages or attribute-based games been tried to test for systematic compositional generalization?  
- Reproducibility commitment: Will code, trained models, and supervised label lists be released to facilitate replication?
