- Summary: paper studies emergent discrete communication in a minimal referential game where a sender sees two images + binary target t and sends one symbol for a receiver to pick the target, trained end-to-end with REINFORCE.  
- Main result: rapid convergence to near-optimal policies across architectures and vocabularies, with learned tokens capturing meaningful high-level concepts.  
- Object-level variant: hiding image-specific details yields more semantically coherent symbols.  
- Grounding via multi-tasking: alternating referential game with ImageNet supervision grounds symbols in human labels, improving interpretability and enabling above-chance human–agent communication on a distinct dataset.  
- Claim of novelty: first learn agent-to-agent communication then ground it in human language as an alternative to seq2seq approaches.  
- Decision: Accept — clear, well-motivated contribution with convincing empirical evidence and elegant grounding method.  
- Positioning: appropriate contrast to prior work (differentiable comms, cheap-talk) and emphasis on semantic interpretability and human evaluation.  
- Empirical support: results consistent across conditions — convergence, increased purity for object-level setup, large gains from supervised grounding (e.g., 47% exact label use; 68% human success).  
- Methodology: use of discrete bottleneck and pretrained visual features is reasonable to isolate the communication question.  
- Ask to clarify sender input: how t is incorporated (ordering vs explicit binary input) and robustness to randomized image order.  
- Request reporting details: variability across seeds, confidence intervals for purity and human studies, and training stability (REINFORCE variance, any baselines/entropy terms).  
- Fix reporting issues: resolve inconsistencies between Table 1 and Table 2 ([0,100] vs [0,1]) and the numerically odd “obs-chance purity” in Table 1.  
- Suggested ablations: vocabulary size effects, fc vs softmax features, and informed vs agnostic sender under equal capacities.  
- Generalization checks: partner-swap (new receiver), unseen categories, and symbol stability across runs.  
- Minor fixes: typos (“assest”→“assess”, “usufal”→“usual”) and reference formatting proofreading.  
- Questions for authors: precise encoding of t and experiments with symmetric/target indicator embeddings; number of runs and mean±sd for coordination/purity; sensitivity to temperature τ and any annealing/Gumbel-Softmax trials; whether supervised grounding helps/hurts zero-shot reference to unseen classes; and whether the code can support multi-symbol messages or multi-turn protocols.
