- Contributions: shows tabula-rasa REINFORCE agents learn near-perfect image communication; analyzes semantics aligning with high-level concepts over low-level features; proposes alternating supervised grounding that improves interpretability and human alignment.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Clear, well-motivated problem and a simple, compelling interactive framework advancing function-driven language learning beyond passive supervision.
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical evidence that communication reliably emerges and modest design choices plus light supervision improve semantic alignment and human interpretability.
- Problem & motivation: Addresses induction of coordination-useful protocols and shortcomings of passive dialogue training, grounding in signaling-game tradition.
- Placement in literature: Differentiates from prior multi-agent work via realistic image inputs, explicit semantic alignment analysis, and pragmatic supervised grounding with a human study.
- Empirical backing: Agents converge quickly and reliably; informed sender learns faster and uses richer, multi-dimensional symbol usage (SVD evidence).
- Semantic findings: Symbols show above-chance category purity and can reflect high-level splits (e.g., living vs. non-living) even with very small vocabularies.
- Object-level reference: Removing shared low-level cues improves semantic alignment as intended.
- Grounding experiment: Alternating supervised labeling raises symbol purity (~70%), yields direct label use (47% on supervised classes), and achieves human interpretability on out-of-domain images (68%).
- Methodology: Simple, sensible ablations and analyses that support the paper's claims.
- Clarity suggestion: Explicitly state how target t is provided to the sender (appears to be fixed-order (target,distractor)); clarify in equations/diagrams.
- Reporting consistency: Standardize scales in tables (percent vs proportion), fix “obs–chance purity” inconsistency, and correct typos.
- Stability/variance request: Report results across multiple seeds with means/SD or CIs for success rate, purity, symbol usage, and SVD spectra; detail variance reduction used with REINFORCE.
- Sensitivity analyses: Report sensitivity to temperature τ and vocabulary size (e.g., K=10 vs K=100) for learning speed, entropy, and purity.
- Controls for shortcuts: Add controls randomizing input order or swapping target position and test same-class image pairs to rule out positional or trivial cues.
- Deeper semantic analyses: Complement purity with NMI/ARI, topographic similarity, RSA between sender embeddings and CNN distances, and report symbol entropy and MI with categories.
- Generalization tests: Evaluate zero-shot generalization to unseen concepts, larger candidate sets (>2 images), and multi-symbol/multi-turn message scalability.
- Human study details: Provide UI/instructions, attention checks, inter-annotator agreement, and calibrated human baselines or relatedness judgments for the 68% result.
- Data overlap concern: Clarify overlap between supervised labels and game/test images; quantify performance split for overlap vs non-overlap (ReferIt partially addresses this).
- Question 1: Is target t conveyed solely by ordering (target first) to the sender, and were variants without explicit target indication tried?
- Question 2: How consistent are symbol semantics and purity across seeds—do similar lexicons emerge or are there multiple equilibria?
- Question 3: How sensitive are results to τ and vocabulary size K; does higher τ trade speed for better alignment?
- Question 4: Can you demonstrate receiver decisions are content-driven (e.g., by shuffling orders at test time)?
- Question 5: In object-level reference, what is the effect size of purity gains and per-category symbol mappings/examples?
- Question 6: In ReferIt human study, how often are words related via hypernymy/metonymy vs visual association; any error taxonomy?
- Question 7: If supervised labels are entirely disjoint from game categories/images, how much interpretability/purity remains?
- Question 8: Have you tried sequences of symbols or multi-turn interactions, and do observed trends scale to those settings?
