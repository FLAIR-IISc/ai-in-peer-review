- Proposed framework: multi-agent referential communication games where sender/receiver learn from real images, producing symbols aligned with high-level semantics rather than low-level features.
- Architecture variants: compared “agnostic” vs “informed” senders and introduced an object-level variant to remove low-level common knowledge and encourage abstract meanings.
- Hybrid training: alternating self-play with supervised object naming yields higher semantic purity and better human interpretability.
- Human study: humans as receivers achieved 68% accuracy (above 50% chance), with appropriate statistical testing.
- Decision: Accept — framework is strong and alternation between game-based and supervised learning is particularly promising for grounding and interpretability.
- Motivation/positioning: timely, well-motivated problem and cites relevant prior work, though closer positioning to contemporaries is needed.
- Empirical support: results show rapid coordination, increased semantic purity (especially with informed sender/object-level), and meaningful interpretability.
- Methodological rigor: appropriate use of REINFORCE and metrics (purity, spectrum/SVD, human study), but some controls and ablations are missing.
