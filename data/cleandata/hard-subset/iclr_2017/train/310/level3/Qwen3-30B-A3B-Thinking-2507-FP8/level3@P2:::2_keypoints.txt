- Introduces a novel multi-agent referential game framework where sender and receiver agents develop a communication protocol without pre-defined language.  
- Shows simple neural networks can evolve communication that reflects image semantics and can be grounded in natural language via game-based and supervised learning.  
- Argues this approach addresses limitations of passive, text-only language models and enables interactive language learning.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Tackles a meaningful problem of developing interactive communication systems rather than passive supervised learning.  
- Situates work well in related fields (linguistics, cognitive science, game theory, multi-agent RL) and references Sukhbaatar et al. (2016) and Foerster et al. (2016).  
- Provides rigorous experiments showing agents learn to communicate (Table 1), emergent language encodes semantics (Figure 3), and grounding improves interpretability (Section 5).  
- Key contribution: demonstrates grounding emergent communication in natural language using combined game-based and supervised learning.  
- Figure 1 needs clearer visual representation, labeled connections, and a detailed caption to clarify the architecture.  
- The "informed sender" architecture description should be made more accessible with an intuitive explanation of the convolutional filters' role.  
- Related work section should more thoroughly compare and contrast with Sukhbaatar et al. (2016) and Foerster et al. (2016).  
- Clarifying question: How does this approach specifically differ from Sukhbaatar et al. (2016) and Foerster et al. (2016) in the communication protocols learned?  
- Clarifying question: What mechanisms enable generalization beyond supervised categories, and how can this be improved or further analyzed?  
- Clarifying question: How would the class-level semantic clustering approach scale to more complex visual domains with greater semantic diversity?
