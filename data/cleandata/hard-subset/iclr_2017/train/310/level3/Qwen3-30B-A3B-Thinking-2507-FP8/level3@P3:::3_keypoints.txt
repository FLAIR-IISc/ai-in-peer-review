- Introduces a multi-agent referential game framework where a sender, given a binary target and two images, sends a symbol to a receiver who must identify the target, producing emergent communication via reinforcement learning.
- Demonstrates neural agents learn communication protocols capturing meaningful semantic concepts rather than only low-level visual features.
- Proposes a multi-game setup alternating referential game and supervised image classification to ground emergent signals in human language.
- Shows empirical evidence of successful learning: near-perfect coordination, higher purity scores, informative t-SNE visualizations, and improved human interpretability.
- Reports a 68% accuracy in crowdsourced human evaluation, indicating the emergent communication can be interpreted by humans.
- Claims novelty as first work to learn agent-to-agent communication and subsequently ground it in human language using the multi-game approach.
- Argues the multi-game grounding is elegant, practical, and scalable without constant human supervision.
- Decision: Accept.
