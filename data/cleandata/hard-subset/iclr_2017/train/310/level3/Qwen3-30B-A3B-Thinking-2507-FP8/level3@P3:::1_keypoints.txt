- Paper proposes a framework for emergent language in referential games where neural agents learn to communicate to identify a target image from a pair, with claims of semantic structure, grounding via supervised learning, and human-evaluation interpretability.

- Decision: Reject — reviewer believes core claims are undermined by fundamental experimental flaws that make findings trivial.

- Main critique: The task effectively reduces to image classification when the two images are from different categories, so communication simply conveys category labels.

- Sender need only transmit the target's category and the receiver picks the matching image, which is not genuine language emergence but class-label transfer.

- Empirical support for this critique: the agnostic sender uses only two symbols (living vs. non-living), and the informed sender mainly encodes image class information.

- The paper fails to distinguish between communication and classification, and the authors themselves note the reduction yet still claim language emergence.

- Improvement suggestion: Explicitly acknowledge that the current setup demonstrates image classification via a communication channel rather than true language emergence.

- Improvement suggestion: Use a more challenging design where target and distractor are from the same category to force richer communication strategies.

- Improvement suggestion: Provide a more nuanced comparison with related work (e.g., Sukhbaatar et al. 2016, Foerster et al. 2016) that shows more sophisticated emergent protocols.

- Improvement suggestion: Clarify how the proposed grounding approach addresses the core issue that protocols may just be transmitting class labels.

- Question for authors: How does your framework differ from simply training a classifier, and what communication challenges does it address beyond standard classification?

- Question for authors: In same-category target/distractor cases, how would agents' protocols change and what specific challenges would require more sophisticated language?

- Question for authors: How do you reconcile the claim of "language emergence" with symbols that merely encode image class, which could be solved without communication?
