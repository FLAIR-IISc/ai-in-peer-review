- Proposes a referential game where tabula rasa sender and receiver learn a discrete communication protocol to identify a target image from a pair.  
- Compares two sender architectures (agnostic vs informed); informed sender converges faster and uses a richer vocabulary.  
- Finds emergent language exhibits semantic structure aligning with human-concept categories (measured by cluster purity).  
- Shows environment manipulations can steer the protocol toward more abstract, general meanings.  
- Introduces grounding by combining the game with supervised image labeling, producing partially interpretable language usable by humans, including for some unseen concepts.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Key reason 1: The referential game often reduces communication to a simple image classification task because target and distractor are from distinct concepts.  
- Key reason 2: Emergent symbols function primarily as class labels, calling into question the novelty and necessity of the multi-agent RL approach versus supervised/transfer learning.  
- Supporting argument: High success rates likely reflect classifier performance on VGG features rather than true communicative emergence.  
- Supporting argument: The informed sender’s larger symbol set may simply indicate a better multi-class classifier versus the agnostic sender’s binary scheme.  
- Supporting argument: The grounding experiment’s reliance on supervised labels reinforces that the underlying problem solved is classification, not compositional communication.  
- Suggestion: Increase task difficulty by sampling target and distractor from the same base-level concept to force descriptive, distinguishing communication.  
- Suggestion: Deeper analysis of why the informed sender’s dimension-wise comparison helps (e.g., visualize learned filters or intermediate representations).  
- Suggestion: Expand human evaluation to analyze correlation between human performance and semantic distance and to systematize metonymic links.  
- Question: What percentage of image pairs in Section 4 are from the same McRae category (i.e., within-category pairs)?  
- Question: What are the advantages of the multi-agent RL approach over training a standard classifier on image features and using its predictions as symbols?  
- Question: In the grounding experiment, was there any overlap between the 100 supervised ImageNet labels and the objects in the ReferItGame test set?
