- Paper studies emergent communication between sender and receiver in a referential image game, showing agents can develop a shared protocol to identify a target image.
- Simple task enables post-hoc analysis: informed sender (with inductive bias) learns richer vocabulary; agnostic sender converges to a two-symbol distinction; symbols cluster semantically.
- Grounding experiment links emergent symbols to natural language via supervised image-labeling, producing interpretable codes and some generalization (e.g., using "dolphin" for sea images).
- Decision: Reject.
- Major concern: Sampling targets and distractors from different high-level categories makes the task trivial and reduces communication to learned class labels rather than compositional/referential language.
- Major concern: Reinforcement learning is an inefficient and unnecessary choice; the task is better framed as supervised/transfer learning with faster convergence and stronger baselines.
- Supporting argument: The sender and receiver can act as decoupled classifiers (sender outputs class label; receiver matches label), so no true interactive negotiation is required.
- Supporting argument: The informed sender’s larger vocabulary is explained by better multi-class classification ability, not emergent compositionality.
- Supporting argument: The success of the supervised grounding experiment reinforces that the problem is classification-driven.
- Suggestion: Increase task difficulty by pairing target and distractor from the same base-level category (e.g., two dogs) to force fine-grained, potentially compositional communication.
- Suggestion: Include stronger baselines, e.g., a supervised sender that passes labels/logits to a receiver, to justify the use of RL and compare performance and convergence.
- Suggestion: Expand and analyze the grounding experiment in depth, measuring metonymic generalization systematically (e.g., semantic distances via WordNet/embeddings).
- Question: How do agents perform when target and distractor are from the same McRae category or the same base-level concept?
- Question: Have you compared the RL approach to a supervised baseline (sender as classifier + receiver matcher) in convergence speed and final accuracy?
- Question: Please clarify the motivation for the informed sender’s 1D convolution and why that inductive bias should encourage a richer vocabulary versus other joint-processing architectures.
