- Presents a referential-game multi-agent framework where sender and receiver learn communication from scratch to identify target images.
- Shows tabula rasa agents can coordinate and reach near-perfect accuracy in the referential game.
- Demonstrates emergent symbols correspond to higher-level conceptual image properties rather than only low-level visual features.
- Shows that altering the game environment (e.g., forcing object-level coordination) promotes more abstract semantics.
- Proposes a hybrid training strategy that interleaves interactive game-play with supervised image-labeling to ground emergent language in natural language.
- Hybrid approach produces a communication protocol interpretable by humans and generalizes to novel objects.
- Decision: Accept — paper is well-motivated, thoughtfully executed, and explores a promising research direction.
- Framework is clean, generalizable, and offers an alternative to standard supervised methods for learning communication.
- Empirical results, including convergence to successful protocols, provide a solid baseline and support the claims.
- Analysis using cluster purity and t-SNE offers preliminary evidence that learned symbols have semantic structure.
- Human evaluation validates grounding: people can interpret agents’ metonymic uses of words (e.g., “dolphin” → sea).
- Hybrid self-play plus supervised learning is a scalable, practical path toward human-understandable emergent communication.
- Suggestion: clarify sender architectures (especially the “informed sender”), improve Figure 1 caption and explanation of 1-D convolutions for reproducibility.
- Suggestion: provide more direct comparisons and explicit novelties relative to related multi-agent communication work (e.g., Foerster et al., Sukhbaatar et al.).
- Question: please elaborate the informed sender architecture and how the two image embeddings are processed by convolutional filters.
- Question: was there any trade-off between supervised classification performance and referential game success, particularly on objects outside the supervised label set?
- Question: have you analyzed compositionality in the emergent language (e.g., symbols consistently referring to attributes like color or shape)?
