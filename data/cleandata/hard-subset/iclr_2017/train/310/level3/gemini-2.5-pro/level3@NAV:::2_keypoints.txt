- Proposes a multi-agent referential communication-game framework for language learning.  
- Shows tabula rasa agents can develop an emergent communication protocol to solve a cooperative image identification task.  
- Explores aligning emergent language with human semantics by modifying common knowledge and via hybrid training.  
- Combines game-based reinforcement learning with supervised learning on natural-language labels to improve grounding.  
- The hybrid approach improves semantic purity and yields codes interpretable by humans, including for novel objects.  
- Recommendation: Accept — paper is well-motivated and presents a promising research direction.  
- Empirical results are convincing: agents coordinate, purity metrics indicate meaningful semantics, and human evaluation supports viability.  
- Situates itself in literature and argues limitations of purely supervised approaches; Section 2 presents a strong, adaptable framework.  
- Main weakness: clarity of experimental setup and agent architecture description (Section 3) is poor.  
- Figure 1 is missing key network connections and a descriptive caption; needs a clearer diagram distinguishing "agnostic" vs "informed" senders.  
- Requests clarification on the "informed sender": how 1-D convolutions operate on two image embeddings and the filter structure that produces vocabulary scores.  
- Questions the high temperature (τ = 10) for Gibbs distributions and asks for sensitivity analysis to this hyperparameter.
