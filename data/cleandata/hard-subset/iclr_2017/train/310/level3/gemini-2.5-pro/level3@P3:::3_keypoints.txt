- Introduces a simple referential game framework to study emergence of grounded communication between a sender and receiver.  
- Sender transmits a single symbol from a vocabulary; receiver must identify the target image from a pair.  
- Agents use pretrained image features, are implemented as simple neural nets, and are trained with REINFORCE to converge to optimal policies.  
- Emergent symbols are shown to capture meaningful semantic concepts via clustering purity against human categories.  
- Changing the environment (common knowledge) encourages more abstract, semantically-rich communication.  
- Novel multi-game setup alternates the referential game with supervised image classification to ground symbols in natural language labels.  
- Grounding generalizes: sender uses words metonymically to refer to unseen objects in ways humans can interpret.  
- Framed as an important step toward interactive agents that learn functional, goal-oriented aspects of language, complementing supervised sequence-to-sequence approaches.  
- Decision: Accept — framework is clear, well-motivated, and elegant; the supervised + interactive grounding experiment is a significant contribution.  
- Strengths: principled first-principles approach, clean testbed, demonstration that simple agents can develop semantically-structured protocols.  
- Experimental progression is compelling: initial learning, environment-driven abstraction, and interpretability via grounding.  
- Clarification requested: specify exactly how the target variable t is provided to the sender (is target identity conveyed solely by input order?).  
- Reporting issues: inconsistent percentage ranges in tables (Table 1 [0,100] vs Table 2 [0,1]) and questionable "obs-chance purity" value of 37% that needs clarification.  
- Minor typos to correct: "assest" → "assess" and "usufal" → "usual" (or check parsing of reference).
