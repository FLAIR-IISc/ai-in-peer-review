- Proposes Deep Variational Bayes Filters (DVBF) to learn state space models from raw non‑Markovian sequence data.  
- Claims DVBF overcomes intractable inference and enables latent dynamic system identification.  
- Approach is well‑motivated and grounded in recent stochastic gradient variational Bayes literature.  
- Method is clearly explained, including transition reparameterization and derivation of the variational lower bound.  
- Empirical results show DVBF recovers meaningful latent states and yields stable long‑term predictions.  
- DVBF outperforms existing methods such as Deep Kalman Filters in the reported experiments.  
- Experiments cover multiple environments: dynamic pendulum, bouncing ball, and two bouncing balls, with latent‑space visualizations and analyses.  
- Reviewer requests more implementation details (model architectures and training hyperparameters).  
- Reviewer asks for broader comparisons to other methods (e.g., Embed to Control).  
- Open questions: how latent dimensionality is chosen and its effect on performance.  
- Open questions: how DVBF handles highly nonlinear or non‑stationary dynamics.  
- Open question: potential real‑world applications (robotics, computer vision).  
- Overall verdict: paper makes a significant contribution and is recommended for acceptance.
