- Introduces Deep Variational Bayes Filters (DVBF) for unsupervised learning/identification of latent Markovian state-space models.
- Uses stochastic gradient variational Bayes to address intractable inference and handle highly nonlinear inputs with temporal and spatial dependencies.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on vision-based experiments: recovers latent states tied to physical quantities and produces stable long-term predictions.
- Decision: Accept.
- Reason 1 for acceptance: Tackles a crucial, well-motivated problem about agent communication and comparing machine vs human problem-solving.
- Reason 2 for acceptance: Empirical results strongly support DVBF’s ability to recover latent states and generate stable long-term forecasts.
- Paper strengths: clear introduction and thorough method explanation; solid literature review highlighting limitations of existing methods.
- Experimental strengths: well-presented results, clear comparisons to methods like Deep Kalman Filters, and effective use of annealing/inverse temperature scheduling.
