This paper introduces a novel framework for language learning through multi-agent communication in referential games. The authors propose a game-based paradigm where agents interactively develop their communication protocol, and demonstrate its potential in training natural language systems. The paper's central contribution is a well-motivated approach to language learning, which is distinct from traditional supervised learning methods.

I recommend accepting this paper, with two key reasons for this choice. Firstly, the approach is well-motivated and grounded in the literature on multi-agent systems, linguistics, and cognitive science. The authors provide a clear explanation of the limitations of traditional supervised learning methods and demonstrate how their approach can address these limitations. Secondly, the paper presents a strong framework and reasonable results, which invite interesting future variations and extensions.

The authors' use of a game-based paradigm to train natural language systems is a promising direction. The results show that agents can learn to coordinate in a referential game and develop a language that captures general conceptual properties of objects. The paper also explores how to ground the agents' communication in human language by combining the game with a supervised task. The results suggest that this approach can lead to more interpretable and human-understandable language.

However, there are some areas that require improvement. The diagram in Figure 1 is unclear and needs clarification, as well as a caption. The bespoke architecture and Section 3 description are hard to follow, and the reviewer would benefit from a clearer explanation of the network connections. Additionally, while the reviewer understands the paper, they are not sufficiently familiar with the closest related literature to fully assess its context.

To improve the paper, the authors could provide more context and comparison to related work on multi-agent communication. They could also clarify the diagram in Figure 1 and provide a more detailed explanation of the network architecture. Furthermore, the authors could explore more variations of the game-based paradigm and evaluate its potential in different scenarios.

Questions for the authors:

1. Can you provide more context and comparison to related work on multi-agent communication, such as the work by Sukhbaatar et al. (2016) and Foerster et al. (2016)?
2. Can you clarify the diagram in Figure 1 and provide a more detailed explanation of the network architecture, including the connections between the different components?
3. How do you plan to extend the game-based paradigm to more complex scenarios, such as multi-step communication or communication with humans?
4. Can you provide more details on the supervised task used to ground the agents' communication in human language, and how it was designed and evaluated?
