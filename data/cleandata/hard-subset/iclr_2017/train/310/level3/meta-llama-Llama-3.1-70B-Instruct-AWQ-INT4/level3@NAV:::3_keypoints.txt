- Proposed method: a novel multi-agent referential game where sender and receiver neural nets develop an emergent language to communicate about images.  
- Findings: agents converge to optimal policies and learn tokens capturing general conceptual properties of objects; grounding via supervised image classification links agent language to human language.  
- Claim: approach may enable conversational machines that can productively communicate with humans.  
- Decision: reviewer recommends acceptance; paper tackles a well-motivated problem, is well-placed in the literature, and presents rigorous results.  
- Strengths: novel and well-motivated approach, clear exposition, well-designed experiments, transparent results, and thorough analysis of convergence and emergent communication.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent percentage scales in Table 1 vs Table 2 ([0,100] vs [0,1]) and a few typos (e.g., "assest", "usufal") to fix.  
- Question 1: clarify how the target variable t is incorporated into the sender network θS(iL, iR, t).  
- Question 2: provide more details on the supervised image classification task and how it affects emergence of meaningful communication tokens.  
- Question 3: provide deeper insights into interpretability—how agent symbols relate to human language and implications for conversational machine development.
