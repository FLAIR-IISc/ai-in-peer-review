- Proposes a novel multi-agent referential game framework where a sender and receiver learn to communicate about images.  
- Two neural agents develop an emergent language through interactive learning to achieve a shared goal.  
- Learned communication tokens capture meaningful visual/conceptual structure.  
- Explores making emergent language interpretable by grounding communications in human language via supervised image labeling.  
- Positions the approach as an alternative to sequence-to-sequence methods with potential for building conversational machines.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Reasons for acceptance: tackles a well-motivated NLP problem and is well-rooted in related literature (linguistics, cognitive science, game theory).  
- Thorough evaluation: clear description of game, architectures, training, and experiments demonstrating effective communication.  
- Grounding experiments show symbols can align with visual/class semantics, improving human interpretability.  
- Minor issues: typos ("assest", "usufal") and inconsistent percentage values in Tables 1 and 2 need fixing.  
- Clarification requested on how target variable t is incorporated into the sender network.  
- Suggestion: add discussion on limitations and scalability to more complex tasks and larger datasets, and expand on potential applications for human-facing conversational systems.
