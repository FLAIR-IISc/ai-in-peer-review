- Proposes a multi-agent referential-game framework where sender and receiver coordinate to identify a target image.  
- Argues that passive text-based training is insufficient for developing interactive language-capable machines.  
- Shows simple neural agents can learn to coordinate and develop an emergent language capturing conceptual object properties.  
- Explores grounding emergent communication in human language via a supervised image-labeling auxiliary task.  
- Decision: Recommend accept.  
- Paper is well-motivated and presents an innovative approach to language learning through multi-agent communication.  
- Experiments demonstrate the paradigm’s potential, including emergence of conceptually meaningful communication.  
- Provides analysis of the semantic properties of the emergent protocol and the effects of grounding.  
- Suggests clearer explanation or diagram of the referential game for readability.  
- Recommends a more comprehensive review of related multi-agent communication and language-learning literature.  
- Calls for more detailed analysis of results, including limitations and potential biases.  
- Asks for details on the neural network architectures and hyperparameter selection used.  
- Asks how the paradigm can be extended to more complex tasks (e.g., conversation or dialogue).  
- Requests more information about the supervised image-labeling task, including label and image selection.
