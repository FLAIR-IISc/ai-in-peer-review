**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper proposes a framework for language learning through multi-agent communication in referential games. The authors argue that current approaches to training natural language systems, which rely on passive learning from large amounts of text, are insufficient for developing interactive machines. They introduce a game-based paradigm where agents learn to communicate by playing a referential game, in which a sender and a receiver must coordinate to identify a target image. The authors demonstrate that simple neural network agents can learn to coordinate in this game and develop a language that captures general conceptual properties of the objects depicted in the images. They also explore ways to ground the agents' communication in human language by combining the game with a supervised image labeling task.

**Decision: Accept**

I recommend accepting this paper because it presents a well-motivated and innovative approach to language learning through multi-agent communication. The authors provide a clear and compelling argument for why current approaches to natural language processing are insufficient for developing interactive machines, and they demonstrate the potential of their game-based paradigm through a series of experiments.
