- Proposes a novel multi-agent referential game where sender and receiver neural networks learn to communicate to identify a target image.  
- Agents successfully coordinate and develop a communicative protocol that captures meaningful concepts (object categories, visual properties).  
- Framework includes grounding learned tokens in human language via a multi-game setup alternating sender roles with supervised image classification.  
- Well-motivated and well-situated in related work with a clear introduction and thorough literature review.  
- Strong empirical support: well-designed experiments whose results are clearly presented and easy to follow.  
- Balanced theoretical and empirical contributions.  
- Decision to accept based on novelty of approach and thoroughness of experiments.  
- Weakness: unclear presentation of the target variable t in the sender network—insufficient detail on how t is incorporated and affects learning.  
- Presentation issues: inconsistent percentage values in Tables 1 and 2 and a likely error in the "obs-chance purity" value in Table 1.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify incorporation of t, fix and clarify result tables/metrics, and expand discussion of implications and applications to more complex tasks.  
- Questions for authors: provide details on t’s role, clarify table metrics, explain plans for extending to conversational agents or translation, and discuss how this approach can improve current language models.  
- Minor typos to fix: "assest" → "assess"; "usufal" → "usual."
