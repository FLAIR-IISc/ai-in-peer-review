**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

This paper introduces a novel framework for language learning through multi-agent communication in a referential game setup. The authors propose a simple task where two agents, a sender and a receiver, must coordinate to identify a target image from a pair of images. The sender is allowed to send a message from a fixed vocabulary to the receiver, who must then guess the target image based on the message. The authors demonstrate that two neural networks with simple configurations can learn to coordinate in this game and develop a language that captures general conceptual properties of the objects depicted in the images. They also show that by tweaking the game setup, the agents can be encouraged to develop symbol meanings that align with human semantic intuition. Furthermore, the authors propose a method to ground the agents' communication in natural language by combining the referential game with a supervised image labeling task.
