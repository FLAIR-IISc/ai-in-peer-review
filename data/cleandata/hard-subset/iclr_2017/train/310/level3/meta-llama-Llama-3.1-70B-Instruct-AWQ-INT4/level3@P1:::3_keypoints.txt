- Proposes a novel multi-agent framework for language learning in referential games.
- Demonstrates two neural agents can coordinate and develop a language to refer to image objects from an arbitrary vocabulary.
- Shows learned communication tokens capture meaningful high-level concepts.
- Shows agents can adapt to new image classes.
- Grounds agent communication in human language via a multi-game setup (alternating game and supervised classification).
- Recommendation: accept — well-motivated, well-executed, clearly written, and results are easy to understand.
- Strength: emergence of meaningful communication that captures general conceptual properties rather than only low-level visual features.
- Strength: multi-game grounding makes tokens more interpretable to humans.
- Minor issue: inconsistent percentage reporting (Table 1 in [0,100] vs Table 2 in [0,1]).
- Clarification needed: unclear how target variable t is incorporated into the sender network (θS(iL, iR, t) not fully explained).
- Suggestion: add more details about neural network architectures and hyperparameters.
- Suggestion: include more discussion of limitations and directions for future work.
- Suggestion: add more visualizations to illustrate the emergence of meaningful communication.
- Questions for authors: clarify how t is integrated into the sender; how limitations will be addressed; provide network architecture details; discuss applicability to more complex tasks (NLP/dialogue).
