- Presents a framework for language emergence via multi-agent referential games as an alternative to static supervised datasets.  
- Motivates importance of functional aspects of language, arguing limits of purely supervised learning.  
- Experimental setup is clearly described and shows agents can learn to coordinate and develop interpretable signals.  
- Grounding emergent language with supervised learning is a promising addition.  
- Decision: Accept—task is important, approach sound, results support claims.  
- Strength: Focuses on a different learning paradigm and analyzes how environment shapes emergent language.  
- Explores semantic alignment of symbols with object categories and demonstrates meaningful grounding.  
- Results indicate successful coordination and semantic interpretability.  
- Critique: Network architecture description and Figure 1 are difficult to follow; need a clearer diagram and explanatory caption.  
- Suggestion: Provide a more detailed comparison to Sukhbaatar et al. (2016) and Foerster et al. (2016) to clarify novelty.  
- Question: Why was the Reinforce algorithm chosen for parameter updates and were alternative RL methods considered?  
- Question: Was the 50/50 split between game playing and supervised learning optimized or heuristic, and how sensitive are results to this ratio?  
- Question: Request more details on CrowdFlower human evaluation demographics, expertise, and any pre-screening for object familiarity.
