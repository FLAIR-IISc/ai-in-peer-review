- Introduces a novel multi-agent framework for learning language via referential games.  
- Shows two neural networks (sender and receiver) can develop a communication protocol from scratch.  
- Explores methods to steer emergent language toward intuitive semantic properties.  
- Grounds agent communication in natural language via combined game-playing and supervised learning.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results, including near-perfect coordination between agents.  
- Reports alignment of emergent language with object categories and human interpretability.  
- Grounding experiment yields encouraging human-evaluation accuracy (~68%).  
- Paper is well-written and situates contributions within multi-agent, linguistics, and game-theory literature.  
- Decision: Accept with high confidence.
