- Paper proposes multi-agent referential game framework where agents can develop a language and environment changes affect emergent semantics.  
- Authors demonstrate coordination between two neural agents and partial grounding of emergent symbols into natural language.  
- Decision: Reject — contribution seen as incremental with insufficiently robust empirical validation for ICLR.  
- Core claim (agents learn communication) is not novel given prior work (Sukhbaatar et al., 2016; Foerster et al., 2016).  
- The environment-driven effects (e.g., informed sender using more symbols) are unsurprising and possibly trivial.  
- Improvements in semantic alignment may largely reflect pre-existing ImageNet structure and pre-trained convolutional features.  
- The informed sender’s superiority may stem from better exploitation of task structure, not genuine emergent linguistic principles.  
- Emergent “language” still heavily relies on the pre-trained feature space, weakening claims of interpretable semantics.  
- Grounding experiment resembles transfer learning; unclear if agents learn compositional/systematic language or just label associations.  
- Human evaluation is limited to supervised vocabulary images, so interpretability claims are narrow.  
- Paper is well-written and situates itself adequately in related work.  
- Suggestion: Test robustness to input diversity (noise, image quality, different datasets) to validate emergent language properties.  
- Suggestion: Provide ablation studies on informed sender architecture (role of 1D convolutions, vocabulary size effects).  
- Suggestion: Use more sophisticated quantitative measures of semantic similarity beyond purity.  
- Suggestion: Investigate compositionality — can agents describe complex scenes via symbol combinations?  
- Suggestion: Evaluate generalization to novel concepts or objects unseen during training.  
- Question: Does the task effectively reduce to image classification when target and distractor are from different classes?  
- Question: How to distinguish genuine language grounding from transfer/memorization from supervised labels?  
- Question: How interpretable is the emergent language on images outside the supervised training categories?  
- Question: Would moving beyond pre-trained feature extractors to end-to-end training yield more grounded and interpretable symbols?
