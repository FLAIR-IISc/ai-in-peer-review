- Core contribution: proposes multi-agent referential games as a strong interactive framework for learning communication beyond passive supervised learning.  
- Empirical finding: simple neural sender/receiver networks can learn to coordinate to identify target images.  
- Environment design: modifying game conditions encourages more semantically meaningful emergent languages.  
- Grounding strategy: combining game-based training with supervised learning helps ground emergent signals in human language.  
- Human evaluation: crowdsourced assessment suggests improved interpretability of learned communication under the proposed setup.  
- Framework advantages: the proposed framework is robust, adaptable, and opens many extensions tied to game theory and linguistics.  
- Decision: reviewer recommends acceptance — contributions are sound and results reasonable despite some clarifications needed.  
- Main weakness: Figure 1 is unclear/incomplete and needs a clearer, fully labeled diagram and walkthrough of data flow.  
- Related work: paper should engage more thoroughly with closely related emergent-communication and multi-agent literature and explicitly contrast approaches.  
- Vocabulary-size question: needs discussion of trade-offs between small and large vocabularies (expressivity vs. learning complexity).  
- Ablation need: additional ablation studies (e.g., effect of 1D convolutions in informed sender) are needed to isolate design choices.  
- Human-eval details: report participant instructions, demographics, exact survey phrasing, and other methodological details for validity.  
- Hyperparameters: clarify choices (embedding size, learning rate, temperature) and whether hyperparameter tuning was performed.  
- Representation analysis: consider visualizing/analyzing learned sender/receiver embeddings to reveal interpretable structure.  
- Combined training specifics: report the relative time/proportion spent on game vs. supervised tasks and how varying it affects results.  
- Future grounding: discuss plans for more sophisticated grounding methods (external knowledge, direct human feedback) beyond “basic grounding.”
