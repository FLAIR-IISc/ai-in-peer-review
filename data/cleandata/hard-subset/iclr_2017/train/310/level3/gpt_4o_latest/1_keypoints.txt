- Presents a novel multi-agent framework for language learning using referential games where sender and receiver networks develop a communication protocol.  
- Shows that modifying the game environment induces more semantically meaningful and interpretable emergent communication.  
- Introduces supervised grounding to align emergent language with natural language.  
- Decision: Accept — paper is well-motivated, novel, and supported by rigorous experiments.  
- Scientific rigor: Thorough experiments with quantitative metrics (purity scores, t-SNE) and human evaluations demonstrating interpretability.  
- Impact: Framework can advance conversational AI and opens promising directions for scaling and more complex tasks.  
- Clarity: Paper needs clearer, more concise presentation of the experimental setup (e.g., informed vs agnostic sender).  
- Broader evaluation: Should discuss generalization beyond referential games to other interactive tasks or domains.  
- Human-agent interaction: Grounding experiments are promising but stronger real-world human-interaction evaluations are needed.  
- Scalability: Lacks discussion on computational scalability for multi-step communication or larger vocabularies.  
- Question — robustness: How does the framework handle ambiguity or noise in input data?  
- Question — multi-agent extension: Can this be extended to setups with more than two agents and what challenges arise?  
- Question — generalization: How does supervised grounding affect agents’ ability to generalize to unseen tasks or domains?  
- Overall: Significant contribution; minor revisions would further strengthen the paper.
