- Introduces a multi-agent referential-game framework where two neural agents develop symbolic communication using pretrained image representations and REINFORCE.  
- Proposes a multi-game setup that combines referential games with supervised image classification to ground communication in human language and improve interpretability.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key contribution: innovative integration of referential games and supervised learning to produce human-compatible, interpretable communication.  
- Strong empirical results showing emergence of meaningful symbolic communication and improved interpretability in the multi-game setup.  
- Evidence supported by purity metrics, t-SNE visualizations, and human evaluations indicating encoding of high-level semantic concepts.  
- Scientific rigor: thorough experiments and analyses that position the work within multi-agent, linguistics, and RL literature.  
- Novelty: emphasis on functional aspects of language and grounding via supervised tasks distinguishes this work from prior sequence-to-sequence approaches.  
- Suggestion: clarify how the target variable t is incorporated into the sender network and influences decisions.  
- Suggestion: fix formatting inconsistencies and errors in Tables 1 and 2 and correct minor typos.  
- Suggestion: provide deeper semantic analyses (e.g., clustering or similarity metrics) beyond purity.  
- Suggestion: expand human evaluation with larger sample size and more detailed failure-case analysis.  
- Question: how exactly is t encoded by the sender and how does it shape emergent signals?  
- Question: what is the impact of varying vocabulary size or embedding dimensionality on semantic quality?  
- Question: can the framework be extended to multi-step or bidirectional communication, and what challenges would that entail?
