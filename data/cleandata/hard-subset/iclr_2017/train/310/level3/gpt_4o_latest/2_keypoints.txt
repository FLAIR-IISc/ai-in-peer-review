- Introduces a framework for training natural language systems via interactive multi-agent referential communication games (sender and receiver identify target images).  
- Agents successfully coordinate and develop semantically meaningful symbol usage; combines reinforcement learning games with supervised grounding for human alignment.  
- Decision: Accept — paper is compelling and innovative.  
- Novelty: Referential games address a gap versus passive, text-based training for emergent communication.  
- Scientific rigor: Well-designed experiments demonstrating effective coordination and semantic alignment.  
- Effective combination: Integrates multi-agent games with supervised learning to ground emergent language in human semantics while fostering functionality.  
- Framework design: General, well-structured, and adaptable to extensions (e.g., multi-step communication, human-in-the-loop).  
- Empirical results: Strong evidence via metrics like purity and human interpretability.  
- Suggestion — Figure 1 clarity: add omitted network connections, annotations, and a descriptive caption.  
- Suggestion — Broader context: expand discussion of related work in multi-agent communication and linguistics.  
- Suggestion — Scalability: address applicability to more complex environments and larger vocabularies.  
- Suggestion — Evaluation metrics: include additional quantitative measures (e.g., semantic similarity scores).  
- Question for authors: How does the framework handle ambiguity or synonymy and resolve conflicts in symbol usage?  
- Question for authors: Can the approach be extended to multi-step communication protocols or tasks needing complex reasoning?  
- Question for authors: How robust is the framework to noise/variability or more diverse, less-structured image datasets?  
- Conclusion: Significant contribution that needs minor clarity and contextualization improvements.
