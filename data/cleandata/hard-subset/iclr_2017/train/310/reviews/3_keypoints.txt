- Proposes a referential game where a sender, given a binary target t and two images, sends a symbol so a receiver can recover the target; both rewarded for correct prediction.
- Agents are neural networks using pretrained image feature vectors and trained with REINFORCE.
- Shows agents converge to optimal policies and that learned communication tokens capture meaningful concepts.
- Experiments with varied image classes lead to even more semantically meaningful communications.
- Multi-game setup alternates the sender between the game and supervised image classification, which further grounds and improves symbol meaning.
- Learning shared communication representations in multi-agent settings is an interesting, challenging direction and justifies starting with a simple task.
- Claimed novelty: first to learn agent-to-agent communication then ground it in human language, offering an alternative to seq2seq approaches; reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Clarification requested: how is the target variable t incorporated into the sender network?
- Reporting issue: Table 1 and Table 2 use percentage values inconsistently (one in [0,100], the other in [0,1]); likely error in "obs-chance purity" in Table 1.
- Minor typos to fix: "assest" → "assess", "usufal" → "usual".
