- Tackles the important problem of understanding communication between two agents and whether machine problem-solving resembles human strategies.
- Uses a simple task that makes post-learning analysis intuitive and interpretable.
- Finds that informed agents use multiple symbols to transmit messages, while agnostic agents rely on only two symbols.
- Critique: the task effectively reduces to image classification when the two images are from different categories.
- Critique: the symbols merely encode image class, making the setup akin to a transfer learning problem that could be solved faster than with reinforcement learning.
