- Proposes a novel knowledge distillation framework that minimizes discrepancy in the sum of statistics across feature maps between teacher and student.
- Empirical results indicate the method outperforms a FitNet-style distillation baseline.
- Methodology is extensively evaluated on multiple computer vision datasets.
- Paper is generally well-structured and clearly written.
- Approach appears constrained to convolutional architectures, limiting broader applicability.
- The term "attention" is misleading since the method actually distills summed squared or Lp-norm activations rather than true attention maps.
- Inclusion of gradient-based attention transfer seems incongruous and lacks direct comparison or joint use with the main "attention-based" transfer.
- The induced 2-norm in equation (2) is unclear and computationally suspect (largest singular value); authors may have intended the Frobenius norm.
- Organizational issues and ambiguous notation detract from the paper’s clarity and impact despite practical effectiveness.
