- Proposes transferring attention maps (rather than activations) for knowledge transfer; idea is appealing and potentially richer.  
- Core claim: attention maps encapsulate more transferable, task-relevant information than raw activations.  
- Methodology is sound and clearly presented, with implementation details described.  
- Uses multiple attention mechanisms (spatial, channel), increasing flexibility of the approach.  
- Empirical evaluation covers CIFAR-10/100 and SVHN but shows only modest gains over standard knowledge distillation.  
- Improvements reported are often small in magnitude, raising questions about whether added complexity is justified.  
- ImageNet results are underwhelming and do not convincingly demonstrate benefit at large scale.  
- Recommend further ImageNet experiments (different architectures, hyperparameters, attention variants) to validate effectiveness.  
- Section 4.2.2 (weighting schemes for attention vs. feature distillation losses) is underdeveloped and needs expansion.  
- Suggest detailed ablation on sensitivity to weighting hyperparameters and exploration of alternative loss functions beyond L2.  
- Paper is well-written, related work is comprehensive, and code availability is a positive.  
- Overall: promising idea but current experimental evidence (especially on ImageNet) is insufficient to claim a substantial contribution; would reconsider score if Section 4.2.2 and ImageNet analysis are expanded.
