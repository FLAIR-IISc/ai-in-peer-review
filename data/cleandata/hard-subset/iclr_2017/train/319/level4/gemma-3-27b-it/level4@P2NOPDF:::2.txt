This paper introduces a novel knowledge distillation framework focused on minimizing the difference between statistical summaries of feature maps in teacher and student networks. The authors demonstrate, through empirical evaluation, that their approach outperforms a standard FitNet-style distillation baseline.

**Strengths:**

*   The proposed methods are evaluated on a diverse set of computer vision datasets, demonstrating broad applicability.
*   The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.

**Weaknesses:**

*   The current implementation appears constrained to convolutional neural network architectures, limiting its potential for broader application.
*   The use of “attention” terminology is potentially misleading. The method primarily focuses on distilling the summed statistics (e.g., squared activations or L<sub>p</sub> norms) of hidden feature maps, rather than transferring attention mechanisms in the conventional sense.
*   The inclusion of gradient-based attention transfer feels somewhat disjointed from the main contribution. This gradient-based approach is presented independently, without direct comparison to, or integration with, the primary “attention-based” transfer method. Its added value to the overall framework is therefore unclear.
*   The computation of the induced 2-norm in equation (2) requires clarification. Given that Q is a matrix in ℝ<sup>H x W</sup>, its induced 2-norm corresponds to its largest singular value, which is computationally expensive to calculate. The authors should clarify if they intended to use the Frobenius norm instead, which would be more practical. 

**Overall:**

The proposed distillation method shows promising practical performance. However, the paper suffers from some organizational issues and instances of unclear notation. Specifically, the relationship between the different proposed approaches (attention-based vs. gradient-based) could be strengthened, and the computational cost of the induced 2-norm should be addressed. Addressing these points would significantly enhance the clarity and impact of the work.
