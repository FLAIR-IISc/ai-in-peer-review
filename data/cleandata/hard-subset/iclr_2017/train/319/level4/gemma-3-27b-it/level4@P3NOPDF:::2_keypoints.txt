- Evaluated on diverse computer vision datasets, showing reasonable generalizability.  
- Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.  
- Method appears tailored to convolutional networks; applicability to RNNs or transformers is unclear.  
- Calling the method “attention” is misleading since it matches aggregated feature statistics, not attention mechanisms.  
- The gradient-based attention transfer feels disjoint, is not compared to the main method, and lacks clear motivation.  
- Equation (2)’s induced 2-norm (largest singular value) is computationally expensive; authors should clarify if they meant the Frobenius norm or justify using SVD.  
- Method empirically outperforms FitNet but the paper suffers from organizational issues and unclear notation/terminology.  
- Clarify the architectural scope and limitations of the proposed approach.  
- Replace or clarify “attention” terminology with “feature statistic matching” or similar.  
- Integrate the gradient-based transfer into the core framework with comparisons, or remove it to focus the paper.  
- Explicitly explain how the 2-norm is computed and justify computational feasibility if using SVD.  
- Discuss potential broader impacts or negative societal consequences of improved model compression and efficiency.
