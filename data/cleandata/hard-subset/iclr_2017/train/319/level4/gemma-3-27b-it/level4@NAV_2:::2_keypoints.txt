- Paper proposes distilling feature-map statistics (sums/maxes of absolute activations, optionally powered) from a teacher to a student via spatial attention-map matching and shows empirical gains across datasets and architectures.  
- Decision: reject due to organizational and technical clarity issues that prevent a strong acceptance recommendation.  
- Positive: idea is well-motivated, experiments are broad (CIFAR, CUB, Scenes, ImageNet; NIN, ResNet), and code/models aid reproducibility.  
- Framing concern: calling the method “attention transfer” is misleading—method distills feature-map statistics rather than transferring attention weights in the usual sense.  
- Tangential material: the gradient-based attention transfer feels disjointed from the main activation-based contribution and is underemphasized in results.  
- Methodological clarity: key details (notably the norm in Eq. (2)) are unclear and need explicit specification.  
- Computational concern: using the spectral (induced 2-) norm for attention maps appears expensive; Frobenius norm may be more practical.  
- Core contribution dilution: exploratory or ancillary experiments weaken the paper’s central message.  
- Suggestion — terminology: reframe method as feature-map-statistic distillation rather than “attention transfer.”  
- Suggestion — focus: streamline the paper to emphasize activation-based attention transfer; shorten or move gradient-based work to future work.  
- Suggestion — loss clarity: explicitly state whether the 2-norm is spectral or Frobenius and justify computational cost if spectral.  
- Suggestion — ablations: provide thorough ablation studies on different attention mapping functions (sum, max, p-norms).  
- Suggestion — layers: investigate the effect of choosing different layers for attention transfer.  
- Suggestion — limitations: discuss applicability challenges to architectures beyond convolutional networks.  
- Questions for authors: why choose the spectral norm in Eq. (2) and what are the practical computational implications vs. Frobenius?  
- Questions for authors: detail the batch-normalization challenges when performing the second backpropagation in gradient-based attention transfer and possible fixes.  
- Questions for authors: why did knowledge distillation (KD) work on CIFAR but not on ImageNet—what might explain this discrepancy?  
- Questions for authors: how sensitive are results to hyperparameter β and was a hyperparameter search performed to choose it?
