- Proposes transferring attention maps instead of activations for knowledge transfer, a novel and intuitive idea.
- Motivation: focusing on where the teacher looks may be more targeted and interpretable than matching activations.
- Methodology is clearly explained and the paper is well written.
- Ablation studies are useful and help isolate component contributions.
- Experimental improvements over standard knowledge distillation are modest in magnitude.
- Imagenet experiments are limited and need a more comprehensive evaluation to demonstrate scalability.
- Suggested Imagenet extensions: test different student/teacher architectures, vary distillation temperatures, combine attention transfer with traditional distillation, and include stronger baselines.
- Recommend an ablation study isolating attention transfer’s specific impact on Imagenet performance.
- Presentation could be improved by showing visual comparisons of teacher vs. student attention maps and examples of success/failure cases.
- Strengths: novel approach, clear writing, and helpful ablation analyses.
- Weaknesses: modest empirical gains, insufficient Imagenet experimentation, and lack of visual attention examples.
- Recommendation: currently a weak reject pending substantially expanded Imagenet experiments and comparisons to stronger baselines.
