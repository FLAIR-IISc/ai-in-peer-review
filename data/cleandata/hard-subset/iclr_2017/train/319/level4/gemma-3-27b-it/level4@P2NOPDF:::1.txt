This paper introduces a novel approach to knowledge transfer, focusing on the transfer of attention maps rather than direct activation values. This is a compelling idea, offering a potentially more targeted and interpretable method for distilling knowledge from a teacher network to a student. The core concept demonstrates promise and represents a worthwhile exploration within the field.

However, the empirical results presented do not demonstrate a substantial advantage over standard knowledge distillation techniques. While the method shows some improvement, the gains are not consistently significant across the reported experiments. Specifically, the results on the ImageNet dataset are of particular concern; the reported improvements appear limited, and a more thorough investigation within this benchmark would strengthen the paper considerably. Expanding the experiments in Section 4.2.2 to include a wider range of network architectures, hyperparameter settings, and potentially different training regimes on ImageNet would be highly beneficial. 

Currently, the paper feels somewhat incomplete. The potential of transferring attention maps feels underexplored given the relatively modest experimental validation. Addressing the points raised concerning the ImageNet experiments, and providing a more comprehensive analysis, would significantly enhance the paper's contribution and justify a higher score. I would be willing to reconsider my evaluation if the authors were to substantially extend Section 4.2.2 with these additional experiments and analyses.
