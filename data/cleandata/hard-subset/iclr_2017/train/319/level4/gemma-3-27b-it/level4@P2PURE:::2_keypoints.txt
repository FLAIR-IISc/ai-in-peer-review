- Proposes a modified knowledge distillation that matches aggregated statistics (e.g., sums of activations) across teacher and student feature maps.
- Empirically outperforms a standard FitNet-style distillation baseline.
- Evaluated on a variety of computer vision datasets.
- Paper is generally well-written.
- Method appears limited to convolutional neural network architectures.
- Calling the method “attention” is misleading—it mainly distills aggregated activation statistics (summed squares, Lp norms), not conventional attention maps.
- Inclusion of gradient-based attention transfer feels disjointed, not directly compared or combined with the main mechanism and seems less impactful.
- Computation of the induced 2-norm in Eq. (2) is unclear; for Q in R^{H×W} the induced 2-norm is the largest singular value and may be expensive.
- Authors may have intended the Frobenius norm instead of the induced 2-norm, but this is not clarified.
- Overall strong empirical performance but the paper has organizational issues and occasional notational ambiguity.
