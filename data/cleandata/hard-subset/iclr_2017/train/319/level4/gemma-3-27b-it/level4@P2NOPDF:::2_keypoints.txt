- Introduces a novel knowledge-distillation method that minimizes differences between statistical summaries of teacher and student feature maps and empirically outperforms a FitNet-style baseline.
- Evaluated across diverse computer vision datasets, showing broad applicability.
- Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.
- Method appears restricted to convolutional neural networks, limiting broader applicability.
- Use of the term “attention” is misleading since the method distills summed feature-map statistics (e.g., squared activations or Lp norms), not conventional attention mechanisms.
- The gradient-based attention transfer component feels disjointed, presented independently without clear comparison or integration with the main approach, so its added value is unclear.
- Computation of the induced 2-norm in equation (2) is unclear and potentially expensive (largest singular value); authors should clarify or consider the Frobenius norm as a practical alternative.
- Paper has organizational and notation issues; stronger linkage between the attention-based and gradient-based approaches and clarification of computational costs would improve clarity and impact.
