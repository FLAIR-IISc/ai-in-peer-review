- Paper proposes transferring activation- and gradient-based attention maps from teacher to student to improve CNN performance.
- Code and models are publicly available, aiding reproducibility.
- Decision: Reject — gains over existing distillation methods, especially on large datasets, are insufficient.
- Idea is interesting and well-motivated by psychology and prior work on attention and distillation.
- Paper is properly situated within related literature on attention, distillation, and visualization.
- Experiments show consistent improvements on CIFAR, CUB, Scenes, and ImageNet, but ImageNet gains are modest.
- Experimental section lacks depth and feels exploratory rather than conclusive.
- Ablation studies (e.g., activation-based mapping functions) are useful but incomplete; more on weighting (β) needed.
- Combining attention transfer with knowledge distillation on ImageNet did not yield strong results, weakening impact.
- Implementation details are clear and reproducible.
- Recommendation: expand ImageNet experiments with more tuning, architectures, and comparisons to SOTA distillation.
- Recommendation: explore which layers (shallow vs deep) benefit most from attention transfer.
- Recommendation: include more qualitative visualizations of teacher vs student attention maps in the main text.
- Recommendation: provide deeper theoretical justification for why attention transfer helps beyond regularization.
- Gradient-based attention results are limited; authors should clarify difficulties scaling to complex setups.
- Questions for authors: elaborate on batch-norm challenges for gradient-based attention and possible workarounds.
- Questions for authors: report computational overhead of attention transfer versus standard training/KD.
- Questions for authors: how sensitive are results to β and is there a principled way to choose it?
- Questions for authors: did you try different KD temperatures or loss weightings when KD did not help on ImageNet?
- Questions for authors: clarify the claim that this is the first knowledge transfer method successfully applied on ImageNet.
