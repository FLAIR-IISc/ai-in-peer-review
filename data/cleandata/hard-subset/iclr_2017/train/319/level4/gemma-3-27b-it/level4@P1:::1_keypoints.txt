- Paper proposes transferring attention maps (activation- and gradient-based) between CNNs, motivated by human visual attention and related work in distillation and visualization.  
- The idea is well-motivated and the exploration of different attention map types and transfer methods is thoughtful.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form despite promising core idea.  
- Main reason: experimental results on large-scale datasets (ImageNet) do not show a compelling advantage over existing techniques like knowledge distillation.  
- Reported improvements are often marginal and lack robust statistical significance.  
- Empirical evidence is limited and sometimes ambiguous; CIFAR results are consistent but come from constrained conditions.  
- ImageNet experiments are inadequately tuned (incomplete hyperparameter search), weakening the claims.  
- Claim of being the “first successful” knowledge transfer on ImageNet is overstated given acknowledged limitations.  
- Presentation is fragmented across tables and appendix, making overall performance gains hard to interpret.  
- Comparisons with Knowledge Distillation are not always direct or comprehensive, hindering fair evaluation.  
- The paper’s claim that KD struggles on ImageNet when combined with AT is not well substantiated experimentally.  
- Suggestion: expand and thoroughly tune ImageNet experiments, including systematic KD comparisons (temperatures, loss weights).  
- Suggestion: report statistical significance for all improvements to strengthen conclusions.  
- Suggestion: perform detailed ablation studies to isolate contributions of attention mappings and loss weights.  
- Suggestion: consolidate results into clearer tables and highlight key findings versus baselines.  
- Suggestion: explicitly discuss limitations (e.g., computational cost of the extra backprop for gradient-based AT).  
- Suggestion: better explain why KD fails on ImageNet—consider architecture, hyperparameter sensitivity, or other factors.  
- Questions for authors: was KD’s poor ImageNet performance consistent across runs and what hyperparameters were tested?  
- Question: provide detailed computational cost comparison of gradient-based AT vs standard training/KD.  
- Question: did you try other attention normalization methods (e.g., l1) and their effects?  
- Question: sensitivity of performance to β in Eq.2 and how β was chosen?  
- Question: why focus attention transfer on the last two residual groups for ImageNet, and would earlier-layer transfers help?
