- Proposes aligning spatial attention maps (statistics of feature activations) between teacher and student to transfer knowledge, showing improvements across datasets and architectures.
- Decision: Reject due to conceptual clarity issues and a disjointed presentation.
- Terminology is misleading: method matches activation statistics rather than demonstrating true attentional mechanism transfer.
- Core contribution’s novelty is obscured by imprecise language, making assessment difficult.
- Gradient-based attention transfer is presented as a disconnected, weakly motivated parallel to activation-based transfer.
- Results for gradient-based transfer are less compelling than for activation-based transfer.
- Ambiguity about the norm in equation (2): unclear if induced 2-norm (largest singular value) or Frobenius norm is intended.
- If induced 2-norm is intended, computing it on large HxW maps is computationally expensive and needs clarification or approximation.
- Recommend reframing the work explicitly as a feature-map-statistics-based knowledge distillation method rather than “attention transfer.”
- Suggest removing or better integrating the gradient-based section with clear comparative analysis and justification.
- Request explicit clarification and justification of the norm choice in equation (2) regarding computation and theoretical properties.
- Recommend expanded ablation studies to isolate contributions of different attention mappings, components, and comparisons to KD.
- Advise discussing limitations, including reliance on convolutional architectures and sensitivity to hyperparameters.
- Ask authors to explain how the hyperparameter β is chosen and its effect on performance.
- Questions for authors: specify exact 2-norm used and comment on computational cost/approximations.
- Questions for authors: why treat gradient-based attention separately and what are its mechanistic differences from activation-based transfer?
- Questions for authors: how does this differ from other feature-based knowledge distillation methods and what are its specific advantages?
- Questions for authors: has the method been evaluated beyond CNNs (e.g., Transformers), and what were the results?
- Questions for authors: how sensitive are results to hyperparameters like β and the p-norm choice?
