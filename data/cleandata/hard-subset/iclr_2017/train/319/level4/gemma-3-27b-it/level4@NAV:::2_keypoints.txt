- Proposes a novel knowledge distillation method that minimizes discrepancy in feature-map aggregate statistics (sum of statistics) between teacher and student.  
- Empirical results show performance gains over a standard FitNet distillation baseline.  
- Evaluated across a diverse set of computer-vision datasets.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly presented.  
- Method appears constrained to convolutional neural network architectures.  
- Using the term “attention” is misleading because the method distills aggregate activation statistics (e.g., summed squares or Lp norms), not attention mechanisms.  
- Inclusion of gradient-based attention transfer feels disjoint and lacks direct comparison or integration with the main method, making it seem underdeveloped and of limited incremental benefit.  
- Equation (2)’s induced 2-norm computation is unclear: for Q in R^{H×W}, the induced 2-norm equals the largest singular value (potentially expensive); authors should clarify if they meant the Frobenius norm.  
- Overall: method is practically effective but would benefit from improved organization and clearer notation and terminology.
