- Proposes transferring knowledge via attention maps (activation-based and gradient-based) from teacher to student networks.  
- Idea is well-motivated, drawing parallels between human and machine attention.  
- Presents several novel attention-transfer methods with consistent improvements across datasets and architectures.  
- Code and models are publicly available, aiding reproducibility.  
- Overall recommendation: reject due to insufficiently substantial/convincing improvements, especially on ImageNet.  
- Improvements are modest relative to the added complexity of the methods.  
- ImageNet results are underdeveloped and feel incomplete.  
- Ablation studies on CIFAR are insightful; attention transfer outperforms full activation transfer.  
- Lack of thorough hyperparameter tuning and direct knowledge distillation (KD) comparisons on ImageNet weakens the contribution.  
- Discussion of why KD failed on ImageNet is speculative and needs deeper investigation.  
- Appendix contains valuable detail but should be better integrated into the main text for clarity.  
- Recommended: expand ImageNet experiments (hyperparameter search, KD baselines, varied teacher-student pairs).  
- Recommended: provide a clearer analysis of computational cost/overhead of attention transfer.  
- Recommended: explore other normalization strategies (e.g., l1) beyond l2-normalization.  
- Recommended: include discussion of failure cases and scenarios where attention transfer does not work.  
- Recommended: streamline presentation with more figures/tables and condense related work.  
- Question: which hyperparameter settings were tried and why did KD not perform well on ImageNet?  
- Question: what is the training-time overhead (e.g., time per epoch) of adding attention transfer losses?  
- Question: does attention transfer effectiveness vary by dataset type (fine-grained vs. general)?  
- Question: were experiments done transferring attention from intermediate teacher layers to multiple student layers, and what were the results?  
- Question: can the authors provide an intuitive explanation for why attention-map transfer outperforms full-activation transfer?
