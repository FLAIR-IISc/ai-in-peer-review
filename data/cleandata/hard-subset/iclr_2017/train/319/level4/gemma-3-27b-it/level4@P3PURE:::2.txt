The paper presents a modified knowledge distillation framework that minimizes the difference between the sum of statistics across feature maps of teacher and student networks. The authors empirically demonstrate that their proposed method outperforms a FitNet-style distillation baseline.

**Pros:**
+ The authors evaluate the proposed methods on a variety of computer vision datasets.
+ The paper is generally well-written.

**Cons:**
- The method appears limited to convolutional architectures.
- The use of “attention” terminology is misleading. The proposed method distills the summed squared (or other statistics, e.g., summed Lp norm) of activations within a hidden feature map, rather than performing attention-based transfer.
- The gradient-based attention transfer feels somewhat out of place. The proposed gradient-based methods are not directly compared to, nor used in conjunction with, the “attention-based” transfer. This appears as a parallel idea added to the paper that does not significantly enhance the overall contribution.
- The computation of the induced 2-norms in Eq. (2) is unclear. Given that Q is a matrix ∈ ℝ<sup>H x W</sup>, its induced 2-norm corresponds to its largest singular value. Computing this as part of the cost function seems computationally expensive. It is possible the authors intended to use the Frobenius norm instead?

**Overall:** The proposed distillation method performs well in practice. However, the paper suffers from some organizational issues and unclear notation.
