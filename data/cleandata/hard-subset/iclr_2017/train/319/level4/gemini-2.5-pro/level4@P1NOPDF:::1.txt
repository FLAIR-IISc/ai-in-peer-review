This paper proposes a novel method for knowledge transfer from a teacher to a student network. I find the core idea of transferring knowledge via attention maps, rather than relying on intermediate feature activations, to be intuitive and promising.

However, the primary weakness of the paper lies in its experimental evaluation. The reported results do not demonstrate a significant improvement over knowledge distillation alone, with performance gains appearing marginal. This is particularly evident in the ImageNet experiments presented in Section 4.2.2. To convincingly establish the effectiveness of the proposed attention transfer mechanism, a more comprehensive experimental evaluation on this large-scale dataset is required.

I would be willing to reconsider my score if the authors can provide a more thorough and extended evaluation in Section 4.2.2, with results that more clearly demonstrate the advantages of their proposed method over existing baselines.
