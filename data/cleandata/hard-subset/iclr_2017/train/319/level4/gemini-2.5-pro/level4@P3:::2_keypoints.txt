- Introduces Attention Transfer (AT): train a smaller student CNN to mimic teacher “attention maps.”
- Defines two attention types: activation-based (spatial summaries of activations) and gradient-based (loss gradient w.r.t. input).
- Provides extensive experiments on CIFAR-10, CUB-200, MIT Scenes, and ImageNet across ResNet, Wide ResNet, and NIN.
- AT consistently improves student accuracy, including a notable +1.1% top-1 on ImageNet (ResNet-18 ← ResNet-34).
- AT outperforms FitNets-style full activation tensor transfer, suggesting summarized attention is a better hint.
- Ablations show sum-based attention mappings generally beat max-based ones (practical implementation guidance).
- Claimed novelty: one of the first KD methods to show clear gains at ImageNet scale.
- Terminology concern: calling static activation summaries “attention” may confuse readers used to learned/dynamic attention mechanisms.
- Gradient-based AT feels disconnected and is evaluated only in limited CIFAR-10 settings (e.g., no batch norm).
- Presentation issue: Equation (2) notation ambiguous about Q being a vector and the norms used.
- Hyperparameter β importance noted but lacks sensitivity analysis; more guidance would aid reproducibility.
- Suggestion to briefly discuss whether the idea (statistical summaries of activations) generalizes beyond CNNs (e.g., Transformers, RNNs).
- Questions for authors: why summarized attention beats full activations; how gradient-based AT interacts with components like batch norm; confirm norm/vector notation in Eq.(2).
