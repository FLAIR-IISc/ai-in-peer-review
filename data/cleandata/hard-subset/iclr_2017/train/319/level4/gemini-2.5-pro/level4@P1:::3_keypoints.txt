- Introduces attention transfer (AT) for knowledge transfer where a student mimics teacher attention maps.  
- Defines attention maps via activation-based summaries and gradient-based sensitivity maps.  
- Proposes an AT loss that minimizes L2 between teacher and student normalized attention maps, added to classification loss.  
- Evaluates AT across datasets (CIFAR-10/100, CUB-200, MIT Scenes, ImageNet) and architectures (NIN, ResNet, WRN).  
- Demonstrates AT consistently improves student performance and complements knowledge distillation.  
- Shows AT outperforms FitNet-style full activation transfer, suggesting attention summaries are more effective than raw feature regression.  
- Method is simple, well-motivated, and easy to implement.  
- Empirical results are thorough and compelling, including successful ImageNet application.  
- Conceptual novelty may be incremental relative to FitNets, but the attention formulation and demonstrated superiority are valuable.  
- Paper is well-written and the authors released their code.  
- Suggest quantitatively measuring attention-task correlation (e.g., IoU with ground-truth bounding boxes).  
- Recommend sensitivity analysis of the balancing hyperparameter β (plot performance vs β).  
- Ask for deeper analysis of different attention map types (F_sum, F_max, power p choices, gradient-based).  
- Request reporting efficiency gains (parameter/FLOP/inference speed reductions) alongside accuracy improvements.  
- Suggest plotting teacher training/validation curves to contextualize student gains.  
- Ask for elaboration on same-architecture transfer benefits (regularization vs faster convergence).
