- Introduces Attention Transfer (AT) to train a student to mimic teacher spatial attention maps (activation- and gradient-based).
- Frames AT as hint-based training like FitNets but using compact attention maps instead of full feature tensors.
- Experiments on CIFAR-10/100, CUB, Scenes, ImageNet and multiple architectures show AT consistently improves students and combines with KD.
- Decision: Weak Reject.
- Primary concern 1: Performance gains over standard Knowledge Distillation (KD) are often marginal; AT alone sometimes underperforms KD.
- Primary concern 2: ImageNet experiments are preliminary and untuned, weakening the paper’s scalability claims.
- Reviewer would reconsider if authors provide thoroughly tuned, complete ImageNet results.
- Notable contribution: Transferring summarized attention maps outperforming full-activation transfer is an interesting, valuable insight.
- CIFAR results: AT+KD yields best results but adds only small benefit over a strong KD baseline (example WRN-40-2 → WRN-16-2).
- ImageNet concern: reported 1.1% top-1 gain is promising but credibility is limited by lack of hyperparameter tuning and incomplete loss application; KD’s reported failure lacks sufficient KD setup details.
- Suggest emphasizing the comparison to full-activation transfer as a paper strength.
- Gradient-based AT is an intriguing idea but currently evaluated only on a simple NIN/CIFAR setting and needs validation on modern architectures.
- Recommend ablation studies on the β coefficient and exponent p to assess hyperparameter sensitivity and give practical guidance.
- Rebuttal requests: (1) completed/tuned ImageNet experiments, (2) full KD configuration on ImageNet (temperature, α, etc.), (3) explanation for task-dependent efficacy (why larger gains on CUB), (4) intuition why attention maps outperform full-activation matching.
