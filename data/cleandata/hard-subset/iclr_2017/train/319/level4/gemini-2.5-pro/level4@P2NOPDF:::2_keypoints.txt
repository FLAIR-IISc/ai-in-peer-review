- Proposes Attention Transfer: distill teacher-to-student by matching spatial "attention maps" (summary statistics of feature maps).
- Strong empirical results showing consistent outperformance of FitNet across CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and ImageNet.
- Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.
- Term "attention" is misleading—method matches activation-map statistics rather than using dynamic attention mechanisms; a more precise name is needed.
- Ambiguity in Eq. (2): unclear whether induced 2-norm (largest singular value) or Frobenius norm is intended; needs clarification due to computational/differentiation concerns.
- The gradient-based attention transfer variant feels disconnected from the main activation-based method and lacks direct comparison or integration.
- Method appears primarily applicable to convolutional architectures; unclear how to adapt to Transformers or MLPs without spatial feature maps.
- Overall: simple, effective distillation approach with strong validation, but weakened by misleading terminology, unclear notation, and a disjointed secondary method.
