- Proposes a novel distillation method aligning statistical properties of teacher and student feature maps via a loss on summed statistics.  
- The loss minimizes discrepancies between summary statistics of activations (e.g., sums of squared values or L_p norms).  
- Empirically outperforms FitNet-style distillation baselines.  
- Thorough validation across multiple computer vision datasets and generally clear, well-written manuscript.  
- Method appears confined to convolutional neural networks.  
- Calling the method "attention" is misleading; it distills summary statistics rather than conventional attention maps.  
- Gradient-based attention transfer section feels disconnected, not compared or integrated with the main activation-based method.  
- Equation (2)’s induced 2-norm needs clarification—largest singular value is expensive to compute; authors might mean Frobenius norm.  
- Strong empirical performance but paper suffers from structural weaknesses and unclear mathematical notation.
