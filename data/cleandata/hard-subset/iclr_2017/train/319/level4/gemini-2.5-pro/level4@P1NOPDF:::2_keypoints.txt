- Proposes a KD variant that trains a student to match summary statistics (e.g., sum of squares) of teacher feature maps across spatial dimensions.  
- Shows empirical gains over a FitNet-style distillation baseline on multiple computer-vision datasets.  
- Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.  
- Method seems limited to convolutional architectures because it relies on spatial feature maps.  
- Calling the method "attention" is misleading; it's feature-statistic matching, not a re-weighting attention mechanism.  
- Gradient-based attention-transfer section is disconnected, not compared to or integrated with the activation-based method.  
- Equation (2) uses the induced 2-norm on H×W feature maps—unclear and potentially expensive; authors should clarify if a different norm (e.g., Frobenius) was intended.  
- Overall: strong empirical performance but organizational issues, unclear notation, and misleading terminology reduce clarity and should be addressed.
