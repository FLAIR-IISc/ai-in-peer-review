- Proposes "attention transfer": force a student CNN to mimic teacher spatial attention maps.  
- Defines two attention types: activation-based (summary stats of feature maps) and gradient-based (loss gradient w.r.t. input).  
- Shows activation-based AT yields significant, consistent gains across datasets and architectures (CIFAR, CUB, Scenes, ImageNet).  
- Activation-based AT outperforms baseline students and FitNet-style full-activation transfer; ImageNet results particularly notable.  
- Recommendation: accept—method is simple, effective, and a valuable contribution.  
- Terminology concern: calling it "attention" is misleading—method distills spatial summary statistics, not learned attention re-weighting.  
- Gradient-based AT feels disconnected and underdeveloped; not directly compared or integrated with activation-based AT.  
- Suggest adding discussion on extending the approach to non-convolutional models (e.g., Vision Transformers).  
- Request clarification that ‖.‖2 in Equation (2) denotes the standard L2 (Euclidean) vector norm.  
- Request more intuition/sensitivity analysis for the hyperparameter β and guidance on setting it.
