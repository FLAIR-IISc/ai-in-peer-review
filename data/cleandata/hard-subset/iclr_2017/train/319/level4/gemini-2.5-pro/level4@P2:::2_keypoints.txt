- Paper proposes "attention transfer" (AT) to improve student CNNs by forcing them to mimic teacher 2D spatial "attention maps" (activation-based and gradient-based) and reports accuracy gains on CIFAR, CUB, MIT Scenes, and ImageNet.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main reason 1: The term "attention" is misleading — the maps are post-hoc channel-wise activation summaries, not learned/dynamic attention mechanisms.  
- Main reason 2: Lack of cohesion — activation-based AT is well-validated but gradient-based AT is underdeveloped, evaluated narrowly, and feels disconnected.  
- Activation-based AT outperforms FitNets-style full-activation transfer in experiments and is lightweight and effective.  
- Reviewer recommends concentrating the paper on the activation-based method for a clearer narrative.  
- Clarify notation in Eq. (2): confirm that ||.||_2 denotes the vector L2 norm on the vectorized attention map.  
- Question fairness of comparison to FitNets since FitNets used regressors/1x1 convolutions that appear omitted here.  
- Request ablation/sensitivity analysis for the loss weight β and how its value was chosen relative to attention map size.  
- Ask about generalizability beyond CNNs (e.g., applicability to Vision Transformers where attention is intrinsic).
