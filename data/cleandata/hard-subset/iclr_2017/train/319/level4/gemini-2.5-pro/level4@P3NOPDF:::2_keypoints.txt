- Proposal: Introduces Attention Transfer (AT) where a student is trained to mimic a teacher's "attention maps" computed as spatial summary statistics (e.g., sum of absolute activations or squares) of feature maps.  
- Empirical strength: Thorough experiments on CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and STL-10 show AT consistently outperforms the FitNet distillation baseline.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.  
- Terminology concern: Calling these summary statistics "attention" is misleading relative to standard attention mechanisms (query-key-value/self-attention) and may overstate novelty.  
- Scope limitation: Method appears tied to convolutional spatial maps; unclear how it would generalize to Transformers or MLPs.  
- Gradient-based transfer issue: The gradient-based variant is presented disjointly, lacks direct comparison or integration with activation-based AT, and reads like an underdeveloped side idea.  
- Notational/reproducibility problem: Equation (2) uses the induced 2-norm (largest singular value), which is expensive; unclear if the authors implemented that or a tractable norm (e.g., Frobenius), requiring clarification.  
- Overall assessment: Simple and effective idea with strong results, but weakened by misleading terminology, fractured presentation, and unclear notation that should be addressed.
