- Proposes a modified knowledge distillation framework that matches aggregated feature‑map statistics between teacher and student and outperforms FitNet-style distillation.
- Evaluated on a variety of computer-vision datasets.
- Paper is generally well-written.
- Method appears restricted to convolutional architectures.
- Use of "attention" terminology is misleading: the method distills summed squared activations or Lp norms, not conventional attention mechanisms.
- Gradient-based attention transfer feels disconnected, not compared or integrated with the main attention-based method, and seems tangential.
- Equation (2) notation is unclear: induced 2-norm for Q ∈ R^{H×W} would imply largest singular value (expensive); possibly the Frobenius norm was intended.
- Overall: strong empirical results but suffers from organizational issues, unclear terminology, and ambiguous notation.
