This paper presents a modified knowledge extraction framework that reduces the difference in the sum of statistics across a feature map within a teacher-student network. The authors empirically show that the proposed methods outperform the baseline extraction of the FitNet style.

Pros:
+ The authors have evaluated the proposed methods on various computer vision datasets.
+ In general, the paper is well-written.

Cons:
- The method seems limited to a convolutional architecture.
- The term "attention" is misleading in the paper. The proposed method actually attempts to extract the summed squared activations (or other statistics such as the summed lp norm) of a hidden feature map.
- The gradient-based attention transfer seems inappropriate. The proposed gradient-based methods are not directly compared to or used jointly with "attentional-based" transfer. It seems to be a parallel concept that has been added to the paper and does not add much value.
- It is also unclear how the induced 2-norms are calculated in equation (2). Q is a matrix \in \mathbb{R}^{H \times W} whose induced 2-norm is its largest singular value. Calculating such a cost function seems computationally expensive. Did the authors really mean to imply the Frobenius norm?

Overall, the proposed extraction method works well in practice, but the paper has some organizational problems and unclear notation.
