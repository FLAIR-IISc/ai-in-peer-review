- Proposes a KD method minimizing differences in summed statistics (e.g., squared activations or norms) across feature maps between teacher and student.  
- Empirically outperforms a FitNet-style distillation baseline across multiple computer vision datasets.  
- Evaluated on diverse CV datasets, providing strong empirical support.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly structured.  
- Method appears restricted to convolutional architectures, limiting broader applicability.  
- Use of "attention" terminology is misleading—method distills summed statistics, not attention mechanisms.  
- Gradient-based attention transfer component seems disconnected, not directly compared or integrated, and adds little demonstrated value.  
- Computation of the induced 2-norm in Eq.(2) is unclear—largest singular value would be expensive; authors may have intended the Frobenius norm.  
- Overall effective in practice but suffers from organizational issues, unclear notation, and misleading terminology.
