- Proposes a novel knowledge transfer method using attention maps instead of activations—promising idea.  
- Attention-based transfer may capture more semantically meaningful features than standard activation distillation.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: reported gains over baseline KD are marginal.  
- Lacks sufficient ablation studies to isolate the effect of attention transfer from other components.  
- ImageNet experiments are underdeveloped, reporting results only for ResNet-50.  
- No exploration of deeper architectures or model depth variation on ImageNet.  
- Missing comparisons against stronger and recent state-of-the-art KD baselines.  
- Reviewer requests expanded ImageNet experiments and rigorous analysis to validate broader applicability.  
- Recommends reconsidering the score after Section 4.2.2 is revised to include those additional experiments and ablations; current evidence is insufficient.
