

This paper investigates attention transfer between teacher and student networks, minimizing the L2 distance between teacher and student attention maps across layers alongside classification loss and optionally a knowledge distillation term. The authors define multiple activation-based attention mechanisms (sum or max of feature values raised to power p) and propose a gradient-based attention mechanism (derivative of loss w.r.t. inputs). Experiments on CIFAR, CUB-200/Scene-15, and ImageNet demonstrate that attention transfer consistently improves student network test performance. However, student networks still underperform compared to the teacher, even with attention transfer.

Key remarks/questions:
- Section 3 claims higher-accuracy networks exhibit stronger spatial correlation between objects and attention maps. While Figure 4 is compelling, quantitative metrics supporting this claim would strengthen the argument.
- The selection of hyperparameters requires clarification. It would be beneficial to include an analysis of how hyperparameter β impacts performance.
- Figure 7b should report both the teacher's training and validation losses to provide a complete performance comparison.
- The experiments do not clearly delineate the relative advantages and limitations of the different attention mechanisms for specific scenarios.
- While attention transfer improves student performance, the student still underperforms the teacher. It would be valuable to characterize the computational speed-up (e.g., inference time) due to the student's reduced parameter count. If the student and teacher share identical architectures, does attention transfer still provide any benefit?

Summary:
Pros:
- Well-motivated and clearly written.
- Consistent performance gains for students when incorporating attention transfer compared to students without attention.

Cons:
- Student networks consistently underperform the teacher, even with attention transfer.
- The choice of attention mechanism (activation-based vs. gradient-based) and its optimal application context remain unclear.
- The novelty is somewhat incremental relative to FitNets.
