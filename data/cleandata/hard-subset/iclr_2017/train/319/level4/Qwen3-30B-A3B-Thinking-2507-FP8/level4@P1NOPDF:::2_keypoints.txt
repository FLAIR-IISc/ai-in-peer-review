- Proposes a modified knowledge distillation that minimizes discrepancy in summed statistics (e.g., summed squared activations or Lp norms) across feature maps.  
- Empirically outperforms a FitNet-style distillation baseline on multiple computer vision datasets.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across diverse vision datasets.  
- Strength: paper is generally well-written and clearly structured.  
- Concern: method appears restricted to convolutional architectures, limiting applicability to transformers and other network types.  
- Concern: the use of the term "attention" is misleading—the method distills summed activations/Lp norms, not attention mechanisms.  
- Concern: the gradient-based attention transfer component is disconnected, not directly compared or integrated with the proposed transfer, and lacks justification.  
- Concern: Equation (2)’s induced 2-norm is unclear—Q∈R^{H×W} implies a spectral norm (expensive); authors may have intended the Frobenius norm.  
- Overall: demonstrates practical effectiveness but suffers from conceptual misalignment, unclear notation, and lack of integration between key components, weakening its technical contribution.
