- Proposes a modified knowledge distillation that minimizes differences in summed statistics across teacher and student feature maps.  
- Empirically outperforms a FitNet-style distillation baseline.  
- Evaluated across multiple computer vision datasets.  
- Paper is generally well-written.  
- Method appears restricted to convolutional architectures.  
- Calling the method "attention" is misleading; it just distills summed statistics (e.g., squared activations or L_p norms).  
- The gradient-based attention transfer component seems tangential, not compared or integrated with the main method.  
- Equation (2) uses an "induced 2-norm" on Q ∈ ℝ^{H×W}, which would be the largest singular value and expensive to compute; authors likely meant the Frobenius norm.  
- Overall strong empirical results, but the paper has organizational issues and unclear notation.
