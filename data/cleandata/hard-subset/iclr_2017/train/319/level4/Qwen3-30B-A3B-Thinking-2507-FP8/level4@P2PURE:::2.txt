

The paper proposes a modified knowledge distillation framework that minimizes the difference in the sum of statistics across feature maps between teacher and student networks. The authors empirically demonstrate that their method outperforms the FitNet-style distillation baseline.  

Pros:  
+ The method is evaluated across multiple computer vision datasets.  
+ The paper is generally well-written.  

Cons:  
- The approach appears restricted to convolutional architectures.  
- The use of "attention" terminology is misleading; the method merely aims to distill summed statistics (e.g., summed squared activations or other L_p norms) of hidden feature map activations.  
- The gradient-based attention transfer component seems tangential. It is neither directly compared against nor integrated with the "attention-based" transfer, appearing as an unrelated addition with limited value.  
- It is unclear how the induced 2-norm in Equation (2) is computed. Given that Q ∈ ℝ^{H×W}, its induced 2-norm corresponds to its largest singular value, which would be computationally expensive. The authors likely intended the Frobenius norm instead.  

Overall, while the proposed distillation method demonstrates strong empirical performance, the paper suffers from organizational issues and unclear notation.
