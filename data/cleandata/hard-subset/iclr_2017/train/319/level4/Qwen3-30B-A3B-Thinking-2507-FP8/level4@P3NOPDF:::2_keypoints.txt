- Proposes distillation of summed feature-map statistics (e.g., ∑|f|² or ∑|f|ᵖ) and shows empirical gains over FitNet on vision benchmarks.  
- Strength: Well-motivated, evaluated across diverse datasets (CIFAR-10, ImageNet) with generally clear writing.  
- Major concern: Misuse of the term "attention" — the method is statistical, not an adaptive attentional mechanism, and terminology should be corrected.  
- Major concern: Gradient-based attention transfer (Section 3.2) is disconnected from the core contribution and lacks comparison or integration with the proposed statistical distillation.  
- Major concern: Ambiguous norm in Eq. (2) — "induced 2-norm" (spectral) is likely incorrect/expensive; authors should clarify/justify using the Frobenius norm.  
- Major concern: Method is tailored to convolutional architectures with no discussion of applicability to transformers, MLPs, or other backbones.  
- Minor issue: Abstract/introduction wording misleads readers by implying attention-like mechanisms.  
- Minor issue: Missing ablation studies to isolate the effect of the statistical distillation from other components.  
- Minor issue: Computational cost of the proposed loss (especially if spectral norm is used) is not addressed.  
- Overall: Idea is promising with solid experiments, but conceptual/notation errors and poor integration weaken the contribution.  
- Recommendation: Reject; revise terminology, clarify norm choice (use/justify Frobenius), and either justify, evaluate, or remove the gradient-based component.
