- Proposes Attention Transfer (AT), a novel distillation method using attention maps rather than feature activations, with a clear theoretical rationale and interpretability benefits.

- Argues AT may better preserve hierarchical visual structure compared to conventional KD.

- Empirical results on CIFAR-10/100 and SVHN show only marginal improvements over KD (e.g., +0.3% on CIFAR-100, +0.1% on SVHN), which are not convincing.

- Paper does not demonstrate improvements in efficiency, performance, or generalizability that justify added complexity.

- Major omission: no quantitative ImageNet-1K evaluation; only qualitative ResNet-50 visuals are provided, undermining claims of broad applicability.

- Ablation study is underdeveloped: lacks comparisons to stronger baselines, statistical significance testing, and sensitivity analyses to hyperparameters.

- Fails to explicitly quantify AT’s contribution versus other components (e.g., loss terms) or benchmark against state-of-the-art attention-based distillation methods.

- Required improvements: full ImageNet-1K experiments (ResNet-50/101) with quantitative comparisons and an expanded, rigorous ablation study.

- Recommendation: major revision—paper has potential if requested experiments and analyses are provided; current evidence does not justify the novelty claim.
