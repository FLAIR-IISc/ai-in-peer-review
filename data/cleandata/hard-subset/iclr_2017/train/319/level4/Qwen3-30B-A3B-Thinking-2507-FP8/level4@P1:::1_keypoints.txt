- Paper proposes attention-based knowledge transfer (activation- and gradient-based attention maps) and shows consistent improvements across datasets and architectures with detailed ablations.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Primary reason: ImageNet results do not convincingly show AT outperforms standard knowledge distillation (KD).  
- Claim that AT is "first successful" on ImageNet is unsupported because KD was not properly evaluated/tuned on ImageNet.  
- AT reports only a modest 1.1% top-1 gain on ImageNet while KD’s ImageNet training was halted due to slow convergence and resource limits.  
- The paper fails to provide rigorous direct comparisons (e.g., convergence curves, final accuracies) between AT and KD on ImageNet.  
- The observed KD failure under CIFAR hyperparameters suggests hyperparameter/architectural mismatch rather than inherent superiority of AT.  
- Missing ablation/explanation for why AT helps on CUB but not convincingly on ImageNet.  
- Feedback 1: Add direct ImageNet experiments comparing AT and KD with identical hyperparameters, convergence plots, and final accuracies.  
- Feedback 2: Clarify the “architecture/depth” mismatch claim and explain why KD fails on ImageNet for ResNet-18/34 despite same architecture family.  
- Feedback 3: Quantify AT’s advantage over KD on CUB with explicit numerical comparisons.  
- Feedback 4: Address limitations of gradient-based AT—explain BN incompatibility and whether AT can be adapted for modern BN-containing networks.  
- Question 1: What is the exact ResNet-18 + KD top-1 accuracy on ImageNet when hyperparameters are tuned specifically for ImageNet?  
- Question 2: Please clarify the contradiction about KD struggling with architecture/depth differences and provide evidence AT resolves depth-mismatch on ImageNet.  
- Question 3: How does AT’s closure of the ResNet-18/34 accuracy gap on CUB compare numerically to KD’s performance?  
- Overall: Conceptually promising work but requires stronger, more rigorous empirical validation to substantiate central claims.
