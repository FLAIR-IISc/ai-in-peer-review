- Novel idea: transfers attention maps instead of raw activations, with clear theoretical merit.  
- Core claim: higher-level semantic attention patterns could be more efficient than activation-based distillation.  
- Empirical gains are marginal overall and do not convincingly beat standard KD.  
- ImageNet results show no statistically significant improvement (e.g., ≤0.3% top-1 difference).  
- Experiments lack depth and necessary ablation studies to isolate attention-map contribution.  
- Section 4.2.2’s claims of superior transferability are under-supported.  
- ImageNet evaluation is underdeveloped: single ResNet-50 run, no error bars, no multiple seeds.  
- Missing comparisons to recent attention-based baselines and analyses of failure cases.  
- Recommended experiments: multiple ImageNet architectures (ResNet-18, ResNet-101), SOTA attention baselines, and statistical significance testing.  
- Recommended analysis: study interactions of attention-map transfer across different teacher–student pairs.  
- Current empirical weaknesses prevent strong endorsement despite the method’s potential.  
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance pending the requested experimental and analysis additions; otherwise the work appears incremental.
