

The paper introduces a novel knowledge transfer approach that leverages attention maps rather than activations. This idea is promising, as attention-based transfer could potentially capture more semantically meaningful features than standard activation distillation. However, the experimental evaluation falls short of demonstrating substantial improvements over baseline knowledge distillation (KD) methods. Specifically, the reported gains on standard benchmarks are marginal, and the paper lacks sufficient ablation studies to isolate the contribution of attention transfer from other components. Crucially, the ImageNet experiments are underdeveloped; the authors only report results on a single architecture (ResNet-50) without exploring deeper architectures or comparing against stronger KD baselines. To validate the method's broader applicability, additional experiments on ImageNet—including comparisons with recent state-of-the-art KD techniques and results across multiple model depths—are essential. I would recommend reconsidering the score upon the authors' revision of Section 4.2.2 to include these expanded ImageNet experiments and a more rigorous analysis of attention transfer's incremental benefit. Without these additions, the current evidence does not sufficiently support the claimed advantages of the proposed approach.
