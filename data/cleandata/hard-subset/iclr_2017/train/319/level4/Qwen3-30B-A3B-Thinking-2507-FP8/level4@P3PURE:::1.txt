

The paper introduces a novel approach for knowledge transfer by leveraging attention maps rather than raw activations. While the core idea of transferring attention representations is conceptually interesting and potentially valuable, the experimental evaluation fails to demonstrate significant improvements over standard knowledge distillation alone. Specifically, the reported gains on ImageNet are marginal and do not convincingly justify the added complexity of the proposed method. Additional ImageNet experiments are required to substantiate the claimed advantages and establish the method's practical utility. The current score may be reconsidered if the authors expand Section 4.2.2 with further empirical analysis, including ablation studies and comparisons against stronger baselines on ImageNet.
