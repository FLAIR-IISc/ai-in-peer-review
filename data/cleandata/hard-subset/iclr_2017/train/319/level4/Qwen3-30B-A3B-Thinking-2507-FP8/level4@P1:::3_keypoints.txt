- Proposes attention transfer for knowledge transfer using activation-based and gradient-based attention maps.  
- Defines activation-based maps (sum/max of |activations|^p) and gradient-based input sensitivity maps.  
- Transfers attention from teacher to student to improve image recognition performance.  
- Provides extensive experiments on CIFAR, CUB, Scenes, ImageNet across ResNet, Wide ResNet, and Network-in-Network, showing consistent student improvements over baselines.  
- Motivation: attention maps correlate with network performance; paper explores different attention mapping functions.  
- Recommendation: Reject — method is technically sound but offers limited novelty and insufficiently compelling benefits over existing knowledge distillation (KD).  
- Primary concern: No clear advantage of attention transfer over KD; students still underperform teachers and gains may not justify extra complexity.  
- Primary concern: Novelty is incremental — activation-based AT resembles FitNets “hints” and gradient-based AT builds on Grad-CAM.  
- Supporting argument: Reported improvements are marginal (e.g., 0.8% top-5 on ImageNet) while students remain substantially worse than teachers.  
- Supporting argument: Paper does not convincingly demonstrate superiority to standard KD; comparisons are not sufficiently meaningful.  
- Supporting argument: Claim that activation-based AT outperforms full-activation transfer is weakly supported; Table 1 differences are marginal and hyperparameters (e.g., β) are not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Feedback: Quantify the relationship between attention map quality and network performance with numeric metrics, not just visuals.  
- Feedback: Provide a more comprehensive comparison between attention transfer and knowledge distillation, particularly for teacher/student architecture mismatch.  
- Feedback: Clarify computational cost with actual timing measurements to support the “very little computational cost” claim.  
- Feedback: Compare against other attention-based methods beyond KD (e.g., VQA, image captioning) to establish broader applicability.  
- Question: Provide detailed analysis of when and why certain attention mapping functions outperform others.  
- Question: Clarify the claim that this is the “first” KT method applied on ImageNet given prior KD work (e.g., Hinton et al., 2015).  
- Question: Have alternative batch normalization behaviors been explored for the second backprop in gradient-based AT?  
- Question: Does attention transfer improve generalization beyond validation accuracy (robustness to adversarial/OOD examples)?
