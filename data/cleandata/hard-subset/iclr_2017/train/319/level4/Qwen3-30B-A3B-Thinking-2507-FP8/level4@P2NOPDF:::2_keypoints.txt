- Proposal: modified knowledge distillation minimizing differences in summed activation statistics across feature maps; outperforms FitNet on multiple vision datasets.  
- Strength: comprehensively evaluated on diverse vision benchmarks, supporting empirical claims.  
- Strength: paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Weakness: scope limited to convolutional architectures with no discussion of applicability to non-CNNs (e.g., transformers).  
- Weakness: terminology misleadingly uses "attention" though the method distills summed activation statistics, risking confusion.  
- Weakness: gradient-based attention transfer is tangential, not integrated or directly compared, and adds little demonstrated value.  
- Weakness: Equation (2) ambiguously defines an induced 2-norm (largest singular value) though the Frobenius norm (sum of squares) is likely intended, causing potential misinterpretation.  
- Overall: method shows empirical promise but presentation flaws (terminology, disconnected component, norm ambiguity) undermine clarity and require substantial revision.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept, conditional on major revisions addressing terminology, norm clarification, and scope.
