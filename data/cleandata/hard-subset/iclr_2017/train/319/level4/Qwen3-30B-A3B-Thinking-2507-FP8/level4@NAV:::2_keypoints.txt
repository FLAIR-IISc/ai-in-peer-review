- Introduces a KD variant that minimizes discrepancy in summed statistics across feature maps between teacher and student.  
- Empirically outperforms a FitNet-style distillation baseline.  
- Evaluated on multiple computer vision datasets.  
- Paper is generally well-structured and clearly written.  
- Method seems restricted to convolutional architectures, limiting generality.  
- Calling it "attention" is misleading — it simply distills summed squared (or other Lp) activations in feature maps.  
- The gradient-based attention transfer term is disconnected, not compared or integrated, and contributes little.  
- Notation/derivation issues: Eq. (2) claims an induced 2-norm for Q ∈ R^{H×W} (largest singular value), but authors likely meant the Frobenius norm; computation is unclear.  
- Overall: strong empirical results but hampered by organizational flaws and ambiguous notation.
