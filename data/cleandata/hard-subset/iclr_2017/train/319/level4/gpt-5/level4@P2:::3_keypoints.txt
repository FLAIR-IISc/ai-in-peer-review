- Paper studies transferring “where to look” (spatial attention) from a teacher CNN to a student via L2 loss on normalized attention maps, defined from activations or gradients, optionally combined with knowledge distillation.

- Defines activation-based attention mappings (sum/max of |features|^p), a gradient-based (double-backprop) attention variant, and a symmetry (horizontal flip) regularizer.

- Activation-based attention transfer (AT) consistently improves student accuracy and often outperforms full-activation regression; AT can be combined with KD.

- Empirical gains reported across CIFAR-10/100, transfer to CUB and MIT Scenes, and ImageNet (≈1% top-1 improvement for ResNet-18 taught by ResNet-34); code released.

- Decision: Weak accept — method is simple, well-motivated, model-agnostic, and yields consistent practical improvements beyond logits.

- Problem framing connects to human/neural attention, saliency/CAM/Grad-CAM, KD, and FitNets, filling a gap by explicitly transferring spatial attention.

- Experimental setup: clear definitions and placement of losses; ablations on attention mappings, layer placement, normalization, and p; gradient AT and symmetry help on CIFAR but are costlier.

- Rigor: Method is precisely described and reproducible, with useful ablations, but some analyses are incomplete (attention-quality metrics, hyperparameter sensitivity, statistical robustness).

- Suggestion: Quantify “better attention” using metrics like pointing game, IoU with bounding boxes, or correlation with CAM/Grad-CAM to substantiate visual claims.

- Suggestion: Provide sensitivity analysis for the AT weight β, normalization choice (l1 vs l2), and the power p used in activation maps; report robust defaults.

- Suggestion: Clarify pros/cons and usage guidance for activation-based versus gradient-based AT (stability, cost, benefits).

- Suggestion: Report practical benefits of student models (parameters, FLOPs, wall-clock inference) and test AT when teacher and student share the same architecture at scale.

- Suggestion: Extend layer-selection ablations to show whether matching more layers always helps or causes over-regularization/diminishing returns.

- Suggestion: Ablate normalization strategies (none, l1, l2) to confirm the importance of normalizing attention maps.

- Suggestion: Report mean and standard deviation across multiple runs, especially for CIFAR and key ImageNet results, to assess robustness.

- Suggestion: For KD on ImageNet, include a brief hyperparameter sweep for temperature/alpha to rule out tuning issues causing KD failure.

- Suggestion: Clarify implementation details of gradient-based AT (handling of batch norm in second backprop) and provide computational overhead estimates versus activation AT.

- Suggestion: Include more qualitative side-by-side visualizations of student vs teacher attention maps before and after AT, particularly for fine-grained datasets.

- Question: How sensitive are AT gains to mismatches in spatial resolution between teacher and student, and which interpolation strategies are best?

- Question: Does AT primarily help specific hard classes or failure modes; any class-wise or calibration analyses?

- Question: Can AT be combined with label smoothing or other regularizers without conflict?

- Question: Does the symmetry regularizer generalize to other transformations (e.g., rotations) and how does it interact with data augmentation?

- Overall take: A clear, practical, broadly applicable attention-transfer method with empirical effectiveness that warrants a weak accept.
