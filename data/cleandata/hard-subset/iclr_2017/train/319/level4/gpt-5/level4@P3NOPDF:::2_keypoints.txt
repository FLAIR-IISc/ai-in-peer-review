- Summary: Proposes distillation by matching spatial activation summaries (Lp norms across channels/spatial dims) and a gradient-based variant; outperforms FitNets-style baseline on vision datasets.

- Strength: Simple, clear idea that’s easy to implement and gives consistent gains over a strong baseline.

- Strength: Evaluated across multiple datasets and architectures, showing robustness within convolutional settings.

- Strength: Writing and empirical presentation are generally clear and practically motivated.

- Weakness: Calling these activation-energy summaries “attention” is misleading without stronger justification or analysis.

- Weakness: Approach seems specific to convolutional spatial feature maps and unclear how to extend to non-convolutional or 1D/sequence models.

- Weakness: Gradient-based attention transfer feels disconnected, under-compared, and not integrated with activation-based transfer.

- Weakness: Notation ambiguity in Eq. (2) regarding the “induced 2-norm” (spectral vs Frobenius) and missing details on computation/approximation and cost.

- Weakness: Empirical analysis lacks important ablations (choice of p in Lp, per-channel vs spatial summaries, normalization schemes, placement/number of transfer points, teacher–student depth mismatch, interaction with logit distillation).

- Question: Which norm is used in Eq. (2), and if spectral, how is it approximated and what is the runtime/memory overhead?

- Question: Precisely how are attention maps formed and normalized (aggregation order, per-sample vs batch normalization)?

- Question: What loss weighting is used between attention transfer and primary/distillation losses, and how sensitive are results to this weight?

- Question: Do you backpropagate through the teacher, or are teacher features/gradients detached?

- Question: How many layers receive the transfer loss, how were they selected, and is there a trade-off with shallow vs deep layers?

- Question: For gradient-based method, what are the targets, how stable is training, and can it be combined with activation-based transfer for additive gains?

- Suggestion: Either justify the “attention” terminology with evidence (e.g., visualizations) or rename to “activation summary transfer.”

- Suggestion: Consolidate or clearly compare the two transfer mechanisms, or move the gradient-based approach to supplementary material with focused experiments.

- Suggestion: Clarify notation and axes of aggregation; replace “induced 2-norm” with the exact norm and implementation details for any expensive ops.

- Suggestion: Report computational cost (FLOPs, wall-clock) and memory overhead for varying numbers of transfer points.

- Suggestion: Add focused ablations on Lp choice, normalization, layer selection, capacity gap, and combination with standard logit distillation.

- Overall assessment: Practical and effective within convolutional vision models but hindered by misleading framing, unclear norm specification, and a disconnected gradient-based section; recommendation—weak accept contingent on clarifications and targeted ablations.
