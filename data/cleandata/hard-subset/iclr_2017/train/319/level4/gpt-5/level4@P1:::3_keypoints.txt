- Paper proposes attention transfer (AT) to train student CNNs by matching teacher attention maps, augmenting classification loss (optionally with KD).  
- Defines activation-based attention (spatial maps from channel-wise stats: sum/max of |activations|^p) and gradient-based attention (input sensitivity maps via double backprop).  
- Introduces a horizontal-flip invariance regularizer on gradient-based attention maps.  
- Experiments on CIFAR-10, CUB-200, MIT Scenes, and ImageNet show consistent student accuracy gains with AT.  
- Activation-based AT generally outperforms transferring full activations and is complementary to knowledge distillation.  
- On ImageNet, AT yields notable improvements (ResNet-18 gets +1.1% top-1, +0.8% top-5 using ResNet-34 teacher).  
- Empirical analysis highlights where AT helps and provides practical implementation details.  
- Decision: Accept (weak accept) due to simplicity, motivation, broad applicability, and consistent empirical gains.  
- Limitation: Students remain weaker than teachers; novelty is incremental relative to FitNets; some analyses are qualitative.  
- Practical concern: double backprop complexity and unclear interaction with batch normalization.  
- Suggest quantifying attention–object correlation (e.g., pointing game, bbox/segmentation overlap, CAM localization metrics).  
- Request hyperparameter sensitivity analyses for beta, p, and normalization choice (l1 vs l2) and layer-wise scaling/decay schedules.  
- Ask to include teacher training curves to contextualize student speed-up and final gap.  
- Recommend summarizing trade-offs between activation- and gradient-based AT (compute, stability, BN issues) and best defaults (e.g., F2sum).  
- Request compute/latency comparisons (parameters, FLOPs, wall-clock inference time) to quantify practical efficiency gains.  
- Suggest experiments on same-architecture transfer for larger datasets (partial ImageNet) and broader validation of flip-invariance and BN-handling strategies; provide KD hyperparameter details for ImageNet experiments.
