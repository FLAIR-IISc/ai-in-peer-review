- Evaluated on multiple computer vision datasets, showing empirical effectiveness.
- Paper is generally clear and readable.

- Method appears limited to convolutional architectures.
- Describing the approach as “attention” is misleading; it actually distills aggregated activation statistics (e.g., sums of squared activations or L_p norms) over spatial feature maps.
- The gradient-based attention transfer component feels disconnected and is neither integrated with nor properly compared to the proposed “attention-based” transfer.
- Definition of the induced 2-norm in Eq. (2) is unclear; if Q is H-by-W the spectral norm would be expensive, so the intended (likely Frobenius) norm and computation details must be clarified.

- Overall: technique is simple and effective but needs clearer terminology, cohesive organization, and precise notation.
- Requested fixes: (i) define “attention” explicitly to avoid conflation with standard attention mechanisms, (ii) clarify the chosen norms and how they are computed efficiently, (iii) either integrate/evaluate the gradient-based transfer with the main method or present it as a separate contribution with comparisons.
