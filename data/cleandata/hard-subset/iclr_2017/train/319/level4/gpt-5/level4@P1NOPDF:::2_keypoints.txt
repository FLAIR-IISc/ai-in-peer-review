- Proposes a knowledge-distillation method that aligns aggregated statistics over feature maps (e.g., summed squared activations or lp norms) between teacher and student.  
- Empirically outperforms a FitNet-style distillation baseline on several computer vision benchmarks.  
- Evaluation covers multiple computer-vision datasets and the paper is generally well written.  
- Method appears restricted to convolutional architectures and unclear how to extend to non-convolutional or sequence models.  
- Calling these aggregated activation norms “attention” is misleading—no learned attention weights/distributions are transferred.  
- The gradient-based attention-transfer component feels disconnected: not directly compared to or combined with the proposed method and its added value is unclear.  
- Definition/computation of the induced 2-norm in Eq.(2) is ambiguous; computing the largest singular value in the loss is costly—authors may have meant the Frobenius (entrywise l2) norm.  
- Paper needs clearer terminology, tighter organization, integration of the gradient-based piece, and precise/unambiguous notation for reproducibility.  
- Overall: a sensible, empirically effective distillation approach that would be strengthened by addressing terminology, integration, and norm-computation clarity.
