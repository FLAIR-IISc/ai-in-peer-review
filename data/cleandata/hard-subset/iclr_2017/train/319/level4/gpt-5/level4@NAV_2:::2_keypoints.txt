- Introduces activation-based attention transfer: student matches spatial attention maps from teacher using channel-wise reductions (sum, sum of powers, max) with l2-normalization; also explores gradient-based double-backprop and a flip-invariance regularizer.  
- Empirical finding: activation-based transfer yields consistent student performance gains across datasets and architectures, outperforming a FitNets-style full-activation regression baseline and combining with standard KD.  
- Decision: Accept (borderline) — motivated by strong, consistent empirical gains and a simple, practical mechanism robust across network families.  
- Problem framing: targets transferring where a teacher “focuses” spatially inside a CNN by matching spatial summaries of internal activations or input gradients.  
- Terminology concern: calling these maps “attention” is misleading since they are statistics of activations/gradients rather than learned attention mechanisms; clearer positioning is needed.  
- Gradient-variant weakness: gradient-based transfer is treated as orthogonal and lacks direct comparisons or integration with activation-based transfer, weakening its presented value.  
- Notation ambiguity: Eq. (2) is unclear about the norm — it likely intends l2 on vectorized maps (Frobenius) rather than an induced/spectral 2-norm; this should be specified.  
- Organization suggestion: either integrate the gradient-based variant with controlled comparisons or move it to the appendix as exploratory work to keep the main narrative focused.  
- Scope clarification: explicitly state the method targets CNNs with spatial feature maps and discuss potential extension (or limitations) to non-convolutional architectures.  
- Ablation requests: include sensitivity analyses for β, choice of pooling function p, selection of layers/groups for the loss, and report computational overhead.  
- Questions for authors: confirm Eq. (2) uses l2 between l2-normalized vectorized maps (no spectral norm); compare gradient- vs activation-based transfer under matched settings and whether they combine additively; justify “attention” terminology vs “spatial activation statistics”; state applicability beyond conv backbones; report sensitivity to β and p; quantify training-time overheads, especially for double backprop.
