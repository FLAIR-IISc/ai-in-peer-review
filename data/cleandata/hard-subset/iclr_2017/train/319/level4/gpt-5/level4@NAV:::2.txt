The paper proposes a modified knowledge distillation framework that reduces the discrepancy between teacher and student by aligning sums of statistics across a feature map. The authors empirically show the method outperforms a FitNet-style distillation baseline. 
Pros:
+ The authors evaluate the proposed methods on multiple computer vision datasets.
+ The manuscript is generally well written.
Cons:  
- The approach appears restricted to convolutional architectures.
- The use of “attention” is misleading: the method essentially distills the summed squared (or other statistics, e.g., summed l_p norm) activations in a hidden feature map.
- The gradient-based attention transfer feels out of place. The gradient-based variants are neither directly compared with nor combined with the “attention-based” transfer, making them seem like a parallel add-on that contributes little.
- It is unclear how the induced 2-norms in Eq. (2) are computed. Q is a matrix \in \mathbb{R}^{H \times W}, whose induced 2-norm is its largest singular value; computing this within the objective seems expensive. Did the authors instead intend the Frobenius norm?
Overall, the proposed distillation method performs well empirically, but the paper has organizational issues and ambiguous notation.
