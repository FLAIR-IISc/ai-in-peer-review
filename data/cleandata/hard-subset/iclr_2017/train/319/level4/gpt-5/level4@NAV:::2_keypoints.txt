- Proposes a modified knowledge distillation that aligns sums of statistics across feature maps to reduce teacher–student discrepancy and outperforms a FitNet-style baseline.
- Evaluated on multiple computer vision datasets.
- Manuscript is generally well written.
- Method appears restricted to convolutional architectures.
- Calling the method “attention” is misleading; it essentially distills summed squared (or other p-norm) activations of a feature map.
- Gradient-based attention transfer feels out of place and is neither directly compared with nor integrated into the attention-based transfer, so its contribution is unclear.
- Eq. (2) is ambiguous: the induced 2-norm of Q ∈ R^{H×W} (largest singular value) would be expensive to compute, so the authors may have meant the Frobenius norm.
- Overall, the method performs well empirically but the paper suffers from organizational issues and ambiguous notation.
