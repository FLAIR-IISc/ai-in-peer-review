- Proposal: attention transfer (AT) trains a student CNN to match teacher spatial attention maps instead of full activations.  
- Attention types: activation-based (channel-wise reductions like sum/sum-of-powers) and gradient-based (input sensitivity maps).  
- Loss: a normalized matching loss aligning attention maps at selected layers; can be combined with knowledge distillation (KD).  
- Experiments: reported consistent gains for activation-based AT on CIFAR, fine-tuning tasks, and some ImageNet results; code/models released.  
- Decision: Reject (borderline) due to insufficient ImageNet validation and unclear advantages over well-tuned KD baselines.  
- Strength: idea is novel, intuitive for CNN spatial structure, and well-positioned relative to FitNets/KD.  
- CIFAR findings: AT often improves over student baseline and sometimes over KD, but gains vs. well-tuned KD are modest and comparisons lack controlled tuning/ablations.  
- ImageNet weakness: evaluation limited (only two attention losses, reused hyperparameters), lacks systematic KD/FitNets baselines and tuning to support broad applicability claims.  
- Gradient-based AT: promising but shown only on a CIFAR NIN variant without BN; double-backprop overhead and BN stability are unresolved.  
- Methodological gaps: key design choices (p in attention, L2 normalization, loss placement, β scheduling) not deeply analyzed; missing statistical significance and runtime/memory reporting.  
- Missing ablations: layer-wise placement, attention resolution effects, alternative attention definitions (e.g., CAM/Grad-CAM) not thoroughly explored.  
- Recommended expansions: controlled KD/FitNets comparisons with tuning and multiple runs; comprehensive ImageNet ablations (all residual groups, β tuning, matched backbones/depths); convergence and compute budgets.  
- Report overhead: quantify compute/memory/wall-clock cost, and scaling of double backprop for gradient-based AT with BN.  
- Clarify BN behavior: explain how gradient-based AT interacts with batch-norm statistics and training stability.  
- Reproducibility/readability: improve references/figure captions and summarize key tables/figures in main text.  
- Key questions for authors: provide thorough KD baselines on ImageNet; sensitivity to β/p/L2; per-layer ablations; compute/memory overhead; quantify attention alignment vs. accuracy; robustness to teacher-student mismatch; gradient-AT with BN implementation details; comparison to class-discriminative attention (CAM/Grad-CAM).
