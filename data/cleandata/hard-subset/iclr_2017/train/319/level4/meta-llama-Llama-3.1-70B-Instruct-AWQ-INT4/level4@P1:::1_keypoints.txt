- Proposes a new method for transferring knowledge via attention maps, a valuable contribution to deep learning.
- Transferring attention maps instead of activations is a promising, more focused approach to knowledge transfer.
- Demonstrates effectiveness across datasets including CIFAR, Scenes, CUB, and ImageNet.
- Experimental results show consistent improvements over baseline but gains are not always significant compared to knowledge distillation.
- More experiments are needed, particularly stronger evidence on ImageNet, to fully demonstrate benefits of attention transfer.
- Comparison with knowledge distillation on ImageNet is insufficient and lacks analysis of why KD hurts convergence with CIFAR hyperparameters.
- Request more experiments investigating the relationship between attention transfer and knowledge distillation on larger datasets.
- Recommend including more visualizations of attention maps and their impact on student network performance.
- Suggest extending Section 4.2.2 and providing more convincing results before acceptance.
- Specific questions to address: why KD hurts ImageNet convergence with same hyperparameters, how attention maps evolve during student learning, and challenges/limitations of applying attention transfer to large datasets.
