The paper proposes a novel approach to knowledge transfer in convolutional neural networks (CNNs) by leveraging attention maps. The idea of transferring attention maps instead of activations is intriguing and has the potential to improve the performance of student networks.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including a comprehensive review of related work and a detailed explanation of the attention transfer process. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on various datasets, including CIFAR, Caltech-UCSD Birds-200-2011, and MIT indoor scenes.

However, I have some concerns regarding the experimental results. While the proposed method shows consistent improvements over the baseline, the margin of improvement is not substantial compared to knowledge distillation alone. I would like to see more experiments, especially on the ImageNet dataset, to further validate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

In particular, I think the ImageNet section (Section 4.2.2) is somewhat lacking. The authors mention that they were unable to achieve positive results with knowledge distillation on ImageNet, but they do not provide a thorough analysis of why this might be the case. Additionally, the experimental results on ImageNet are limited to a single student-teacher pair, and I would like to see more experiments to confirm the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

Overall, I think the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of knowledge transfer in CNNs. However, I would like to see more experiments and a more thorough analysis of the results before considering the paper for acceptance.

To address my concerns, I would like the authors to provide the following:

* More experiments on the ImageNet dataset, including a thorough analysis of why knowledge distillation may not work well on this dataset.
* A comparison of the proposed approach with other knowledge transfer methods, such as FitNets, on the ImageNet dataset.
* A more detailed analysis of the attention maps transferred from the teacher network to the student network, including visualizations of the attention maps and an analysis of how they change during training.

If the authors can provide these additional experiments and analysis, I would be willing to reconsider my evaluation of the paper.
