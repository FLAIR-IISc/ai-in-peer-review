The paper proposes a new method for transferring knowledge from one neural network to another, which is a valuable contribution to the field of deep learning. I appreciate the idea of transferring attention maps instead of activations, as it provides a more focused approach to knowledge transfer. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method on several image classification datasets, including CIFAR, Scenes, CUB, and ImageNet.

However, I have some reservations about the experimental results. While the authors show consistent improvements over the baseline, the gains are not always significant when compared to knowledge distillation alone. I believe that more experiments are necessary to fully demonstrate the benefits of attention transfer, particularly in the ImageNet section.

One potential area for improvement is the comparison with knowledge distillation on ImageNet. The authors mention that knowledge distillation hurts convergence with the same hyperparameters as on CIFAR, but they do not provide a detailed analysis of why this might be the case. I would like to see more experiments that investigate the relationship between attention transfer and knowledge distillation on larger datasets like ImageNet.

Additionally, I think it would be beneficial to include more visualizations of the attention maps and their impact on the student network's performance. This would help to provide a clearer understanding of how attention transfer works and why it is effective.

Overall, I consider the paper to be a good start, but I would like to see more comprehensive experiments and analysis before accepting it. If the authors can extend the last section (4.2.2) and provide more convincing results, I would be willing to update my score.

Some specific questions I would like the authors to address are:

* Can you provide more details on why knowledge distillation hurts convergence on ImageNet with the same hyperparameters as on CIFAR?
* How do the attention maps change as the student network learns to mimic the teacher network?
* Are there any specific challenges or limitations to applying attention transfer to larger datasets like ImageNet?

By addressing these questions and providing more comprehensive experiments, I believe the authors can strengthen their paper and demonstrate the full potential of attention transfer as a knowledge transfer method.
