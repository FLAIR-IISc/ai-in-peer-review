- Proposes a KD framework minimizing differences of summed statistics across feature maps; outperforms FitNet-style baseline.  
- Strong empirical evaluation across multiple computer vision datasets demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.  
- Method appears limited to convolutional architectures, restricting applicability to other network types.  
- Calling the method “attention” is misleading; it actually distills summed squared or lp-norm statistics of activations.  
- Inclusion of gradient-based attention transfer reads like an afterthought and doesn’t add clear value.  
- Gradient-based methods are not directly compared or jointly used with the proposed “attention” transfer.  
- Notation issues: induced 2-norms in Eq. (2) are not clearly defined and may be computationally expensive; Frobenius norm might be intended.  
- Organization and unclear notation reduce the paper’s impact.  
- Decision: Reject (encouraged to resubmit after revisions).  
- Reasons for rejection: limited applicability, misleading terminology, and organization issues (including the gradient-method inclusion).  
- Suggested revisions: reframe as distillation of feature-map statistics, clarify norm definitions/use Frobenius, evaluate on non-convolutional architectures, remove or better integrate the gradient-based method.  
- Questions for authors: clarify the induced 2-norm in Eq. (2) and rationale; how to extend to non-convolutional architectures; why include the gradient-based method and how it relates to the main contribution.
