The paper presents a modified knowledge distillation framework that minimizes the difference in the sum of statistics across feature maps between the teacher and student networks. The authors empirically demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms the FitNet-style distillation baseline.

Pros:
+ The authors evaluate the proposed method on various computer vision datasets.
+ The paper is generally well-written.

Cons:
- The method appears to be limited to convolutional architectures.
- The use of the term "attention" in the paper is misleading. The proposed method essentially distills the summed squared (or other statistics, e.g., summed Lp norm) of activations in a hidden feature map.
- The gradient-based attention transfer seems out of place. The proposed gradient-based method is not compared directly to, nor used jointly with, the "attention-based" transfer. It appears to be a parallel idea that does not add significant value to the paper.
- The computation of the induced 2-norm in Eq. (2) is unclear. Since Q is a matrix in ℝ^{H × W}, its induced 2-norm is its largest singular value. Computing such a cost function seems computationally expensive. It is possible that the authors actually mean the Frobenius norm.

Overall, the proposed distillation method is effective in practice, but the paper has some organizational issues and unclear notation.
