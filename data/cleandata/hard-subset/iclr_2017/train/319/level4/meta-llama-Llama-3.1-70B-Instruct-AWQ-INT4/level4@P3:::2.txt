The paper proposes a novel approach to knowledge distillation by transferring attention information from a teacher network to a student network. The authors define attention as a set of spatial maps that encode the importance of different regions in the input image for the network's output decision. They introduce two methods for attention transfer: activation-based and gradient-based. The activation-based method computes spatial attention maps from the activations of the teacher network and trains the student network to mimic these maps. The gradient-based method, on the other hand, defines attention as the gradient of the loss with respect to the input and trains the student network to have similar gradients.

The authors evaluate their approach on various image classification datasets, including CIFAR, ImageNet, and fine-grained classification datasets. They demonstrate that their method can significantly improve the performance of the student network, especially when combined with knowledge distillation. The results show that attention transfer can be an effective way to distill knowledge from a powerful teacher network to a smaller student network.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach and its underlying motivations. The experimental results are also well-presented, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of their findings.

However, there are a few limitations and areas for improvement. Firstly, the method seems to be limited to convolutional architectures, and it is not clear how it can be extended to other types of neural networks. Secondly, the attention terminology used in the paper is somewhat misleading, as the proposed method is more related to distilling the statistics of activations in a hidden feature map rather than attention in the classical sense. Finally, the gradient-based attention transfer method seems out of place, as it is not directly compared to or used jointly with the activation-based method.

To improve the paper, the authors could provide more clarity on the notation used, especially in Equation (2), where the induced 2-norm is not clearly defined. Additionally, the authors could consider providing more details on the computational cost of their method, especially in terms of computing the cost function.

Overall, the proposed distillation method is effective in practice, but the paper could benefit from some reorganization and clarification to make it more readable and understandable.
