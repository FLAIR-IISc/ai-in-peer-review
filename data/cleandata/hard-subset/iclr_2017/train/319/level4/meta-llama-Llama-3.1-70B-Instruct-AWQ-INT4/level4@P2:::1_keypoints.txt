- Proposes a novel attention-map-based knowledge transfer method that may improve student network performance.
- Transferring attention instead of activations is an intriguing and potentially beneficial idea.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly explained, and provides thorough related-work analysis.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on several image classification datasets.
- Experimental gains are consistent but not always substantial, especially compared to knowledge distillation alone.
- ImageNet evaluation is limited; more comprehensive results and comparisons with distillation and other SOTA methods are needed.
- Recommend additional experiments on ImageNet and other tasks (e.g., object detection, segmentation).
- Suggest analyzing teacher and student attention maps to understand what is being transferred.
- Overall promising contribution but requires more comprehensive experimentation and analysis.
- Recommendation: Reject for now, with willingness to reconsider if authors address concerns.
- Questions for authors: provide detailed experimental hyperparameters; explain how to extend method to detection/segmentation; give more insights into attention maps and their relation to performance.
