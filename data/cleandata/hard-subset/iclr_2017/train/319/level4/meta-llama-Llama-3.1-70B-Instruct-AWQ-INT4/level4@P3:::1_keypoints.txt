- Proposes transferring attention maps for knowledge transfer in CNNs — a novel, promising idea that could improve student networks.
- Provides thorough analysis of the method, with comprehensive related work review and detailed explanation of attention transfer.
- Experimental results show effectiveness on CIFAR, Caltech-UCSD Birds-200-2011, and MIT Indoor Scenes.
- Improvements over baseline are consistent but the margin is not substantial compared to knowledge distillation alone.
- ImageNet experiments are lacking: limited scope and only a single student-teacher pair evaluated.
- Authors report failure to obtain positive knowledge distillation results on ImageNet but do not analyze why.
- Reviewer requests more ImageNet experiments and a thorough analysis of why knowledge distillation may not work there.
- Reviewer asks for comparisons with other transfer methods (e.g., FitNets) on ImageNet.
- Reviewer requests detailed analysis and visualizations of transferred attention maps and how they change during training.
- Overall: paper has significant potential but requires additional experiments and deeper analysis before acceptance.
