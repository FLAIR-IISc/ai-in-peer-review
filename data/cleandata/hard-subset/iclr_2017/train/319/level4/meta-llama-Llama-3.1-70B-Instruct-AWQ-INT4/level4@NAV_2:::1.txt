This paper proposes a novel approach to transferring knowledge from a powerful teacher network to a smaller student network by leveraging attention maps, which is a promising direction in the field of deep learning. The idea of transferring attention maps instead of activations is particularly intriguing, as it has the potential to provide more informative and relevant knowledge to the student network.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including a detailed explanation of the attention mapping functions and the attention transfer process. The experimental results on CIFAR and other datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in improving the performance of the student network.

However, I would have liked to see more significant improvements in the experiments, particularly in comparison to knowledge distillation alone. The results presented in the paper are somewhat incremental, and it is not entirely clear whether the proposed method offers substantial advantages over existing knowledge transfer techniques.

Furthermore, I think the IMAGENET section (4.2.2) is somewhat limited, and more experiments are needed to fully demonstrate the potential of the proposed approach on this challenging dataset. For instance, the authors could have explored different architectures, hyperparameters, or training protocols to further improve the performance of the student network.

Overall, I consider the paper to be a good start, but I would like to see more comprehensive experiments and analysis to fully convince me of the effectiveness of the proposed approach. If the authors can extend the last section (4.2.2) with more experiments and results, I would be willing to reconsider my assessment.
