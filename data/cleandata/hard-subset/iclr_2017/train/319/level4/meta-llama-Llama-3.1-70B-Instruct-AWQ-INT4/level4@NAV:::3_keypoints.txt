- Explores attention transfer from teacher to student to improve student performance.
- Uses L2 minimization between teacher and student attention maps across layers, plus classification loss and optional KD term.
- Introduces activation-based attention maps: sum of |features|^p and max of features^p.
- Proposes a gradient-based attention map from the loss derivative w.r.t. inputs.
- Evaluated on CIFAR, CUB/Scene, and ImageNet; attention transfer improves student test performance.
- Student networks still consistently underperform teacher networks even with attention transfer.
- Claim that higher-accuracy networks have higher spatial correlation between objects and attention maps lacks quantitative support beyond Figure 4.
- Hyperparameter choices (e.g., β) are not reported and their effects are unanalyzed.
- Recommends including teacher training/validation loss in Figure 7b for fuller context.
- Experiments do not clearly elucidate pros and cons of the different attention map variants.
- No characterization of speed-up or compute/parameter trade-offs despite students having fewer parameters.
- Unclear if attention transfer provides benefit when teacher and student share the same architecture.
- Strengths: well-written, well-motivated, and shows consistent student improvement versus baseline.
- Weaknesses: students remain worse than teachers; attention-map selection/application unclear; novelty is incremental compared to FitNet.
