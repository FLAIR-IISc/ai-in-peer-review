- Proposed method: modified knowledge distillation that minimizes differences of summed statistics across feature maps; empirically outperforms a FitNet-style baseline.  
- Evaluations: tested on multiple computer-vision datasets, providing a thorough empirical assessment.  
- Writing: paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.  
- Applicability: method appears limited to convolutional architectures, restricting broader applicability.  
- Terminology: calling the method "attention" is misleading—it simply distills summed squared or Lp-norm statistics of activations, not true attention.  
- Gradient-based component: inclusion of gradient-based attention transfer feels out of place and is neither directly compared to nor combined with the proposed transfer, reducing its contribution.  
- Notation/clarity: Equation (2)’s induced 2-norm is unclear—Q ∈ R^{H×W} would make it the largest singular value (computationally expensive); authors may have meant the Frobenius norm.  
- Overall: method works well in practice but organizational issues and unclear notation weaken the paper’s impact.
