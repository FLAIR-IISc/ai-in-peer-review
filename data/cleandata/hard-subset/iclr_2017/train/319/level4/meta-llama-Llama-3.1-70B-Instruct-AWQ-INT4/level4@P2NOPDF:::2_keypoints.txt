- Proposes a modified knowledge-distillation method that minimizes differences in summed feature-map statistics and empirically outperforms a FitNet-style baseline.
- Evaluated across multiple computer-vision datasets, providing comprehensive empirical validation.
- Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.
- Method appears limited to convolutional architectures, restricting applicability to other network types.
- Use of the term “attention” is misleading—the method actually distills sums of squared (or Lp) activation statistics, not attention maps.
- Inclusion of gradient-based attention transfer feels out of place and is neither compared to nor integrated with the primary “attention-based” transfer.
- Notation and computation of the induced 2-norm in Eq.(2) are unclear; treating Q ∈ R^{H×W} as having an induced 2-norm (largest singular value) seems costly and possibly incorrect—Frobenius norm might have been intended.
- Paper suffers from organizational issues and unclear notation, which weakens clarity despite promising empirical results.
