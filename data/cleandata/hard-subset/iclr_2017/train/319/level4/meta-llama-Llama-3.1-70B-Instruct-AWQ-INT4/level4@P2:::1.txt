This paper proposes a novel approach to knowledge transfer between neural networks by leveraging attention maps. The idea of transferring attention instead of activations is intriguing and has the potential to improve the performance of student networks.

The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the proposed method and its underlying motivations. The authors provide a thorough analysis of related work and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on several image classification datasets.

However, I have some concerns regarding the experimental evaluation. While the results show consistent improvements over the baseline, the gains are not always substantial, especially when compared to knowledge distillation alone. I believe that more experiments are necessary to fully demonstrate the benefits of attention transfer, particularly on larger datasets like ImageNet.

In the ImageNet section (4.2.2), I would like to see more comprehensive results, including a comparison with knowledge distillation and other state-of-the-art methods. The current evaluation is limited, and it is unclear whether the observed improvements are due to the proposed attention transfer method or other factors.

To strengthen the paper, I would suggest the following:

1. Provide more extensive experiments on ImageNet, including a comparison with knowledge distillation and other state-of-the-art methods.
2. Investigate the effectiveness of attention transfer on other datasets and tasks, such as object detection and segmentation.
3. Analyze the attention maps obtained from the teacher and student networks to gain insights into what the student network is learning from the teacher.

Overall, I think the paper has the potential to make a valuable contribution to the field, but it requires more comprehensive experimentation and analysis to fully demonstrate its benefits.

Decision: Reject (but would consider updating the score if the authors address the above concerns)

Questions for the authors:

1. Can you provide more details on the experimental setup, including the hyperparameters used for training the student and teacher networks?
2. How do you plan to extend the attention transfer method to other tasks, such as object detection and segmentation?
3. Can you provide more insights into the attention maps obtained from the teacher and student networks, and how they relate to the performance improvements observed in the experiments?
