- Proposes a modified knowledge distillation that minimizes differences of summed statistics across feature maps and empirically outperforms FitNet-style distillation.  
- Evaluation across several computer vision datasets is comprehensive and supports the method's performance.  
- Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.  
- Method appears limited to convolutional architectures, potentially restricting applicability to other network types.  
- Calling the method "attention" is misleading; it actually distills summed squared/Lp norms of activations, which can confuse readers.  
- Inclusion of gradient-based attention transfer feels out-of-place and is not directly compared or integrated with the main "attention"-based method.  
- Unclear notation and computational details—specifically the induced 2-norm in Eq.(2) is ambiguous and potentially expensive to compute.  
- Authors may have meant the Frobenius norm rather than the induced 2-norm, which would be more efficient and common.  
- Overall promising empirical results, but organization, terminology, and notation need clarification to strengthen the paper.
