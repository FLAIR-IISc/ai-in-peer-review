- Proposes a novel knowledge distillation approach that transfers attention from a teacher to a student network.  
- Defines attention as spatial maps encoding region importance for the network's output.  
- Introduces two attention transfer methods: activation-based and gradient-based.  
- Activation-based method computes spatial attention from teacher activations and trains the student to mimic them.  
- Gradient-based method defines attention as the gradient of the loss w.r.t. input and trains the student to match those gradients.  
- Evaluated on multiple image-classification datasets including CIFAR, ImageNet, and fine-grained datasets.  
- Demonstrates significant student performance improvements, especially when combined with standard knowledge distillation.  
- Shows attention transfer is an effective way to distill knowledge from larger teachers to smaller students.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation of approach and motivations.  
- Experimental results are well-presented and accompanied by thorough analysis.  
- Limitation: method appears restricted to convolutional architectures.  
- Limitation: “attention” terminology may be misleading—method more closely resembles distilling activation statistics.  
- Limitation: gradient-based attention transfer feels out of place and is not directly compared or jointly evaluated with activation-based transfer.  
- Clarity issue: notation in Equation (2) is unclear, specifically how the induced 2-norm is defined.  
- Missing detail: computational cost of the method and cost-function computation are not quantified.  
- Suggestion: reorganize and clarify parts of the paper to improve readability and understandability.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Decision rationale: method is novel and effective, evaluation is thorough, and the paper is clearly written.  
- Requested improvements: provide computational-cost details, clarify Equation (2) and induced 2-norm computation, reorganize for clarity, and discuss extensions to other network types.  
- Questions for authors: how is the induced 2-norm in Eq. (2) computed; how does computational cost compare to other KD methods; how can the method be extended to other network architectures; how will applicability limitations be addressed?
