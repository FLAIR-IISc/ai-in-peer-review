The paper proposes a modified knowledge distillation framework that minimizes the difference in the sum of statistics across feature maps between the teacher and student networks. The authors empirically demonstrate that their method outperforms the FitNet-style distillation baseline.

Pros:
+ The authors evaluate the proposed method on a variety of computer vision datasets.
+ The paper is generally well-written.

Cons:
- The proposed method appears to be limited to convolutional architectures.
- The use of the term "attention" in the paper is misleading, as the method essentially distills the summed squared (or other statistics, such as summed Lp norm) of activations in a hidden feature map.
- The inclusion of gradient-based attention transfer seems unnecessary, as it is not directly compared to nor used in conjunction with the "attention-based" transfer. This addition appears to be a parallel idea that does not significantly enhance the paper.
- The computation of the induced 2-norm in equation (2) is unclear. Specifically, Q is a matrix in \mathbb{R}^{H \times W}, and its induced 2-norm is its largest singular value. Computing this cost function appears to be computationally expensive. It is possible that the authors intend to use the Frobenius norm instead?

Overall, while the proposed distillation method is effective in practice, the paper suffers from organizational issues and unclear notation.
