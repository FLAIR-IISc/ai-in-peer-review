- Paper proposes a modified knowledge distillation that matches summed statistics across feature maps between teacher and student.  
- Empirical results show the method outperforms FitNet-style distillation baselines.  
- Strength: Evaluated across multiple computer vision datasets, demonstrating effectiveness in varied settings.  
- Strength: Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.  
- Limitation: Method appears restricted to convolutional architectures; extension to other network types is unclear.  
- Concern: Use of the term "attention" is misleading; the method distills summed activation statistics rather than classical attention.  
- Issue: Gradient-based attention transfer feels out-of-place and is not compared or integrated with the main "attention-based" transfer.  
- Notation problem: Ambiguity in eq. (2) about the induced 2-norm—unclear whether it refers to largest singular value or Frobenius norm, with computational cost concerns.  
- Overall: Method works well in practice but paper has organization and clarity issues that need addressing.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Reasons for decision: Novel and effective distillation approach with thorough evaluation.  
- Minor revisions requested: Clarify notation in eq. (2); remove or better integrate gradient-based transfer; avoid misleading use of "attention."  
- Questions for authors: Explain how induced 2-norms in eq. (2) are computed; how the method can be extended beyond convolutional networks; why the gradient-based idea was included and how it relates to the main contribution.
