- Proposes attention transfer between networks using spatial attention maps with two methods: activation-based and gradient-based.  
- Shows consistent improvements across datasets (CIFAR, Scene, CUB, ImageNet) and architectures (ResNet, WRN, NiN).  
- Attention transfer outperforms full activation transfer and can be combined with knowledge distillation.  
- Provides thorough experiments, ablation studies (attention mappings, transfer layers), visualizations, multiple runs, and releases code/models.  
- Activation-based attention transfer consistently outperforms gradient-based methods in the reported experiments.  
- Claim that activation-based transfer is the first method successfully applied on ImageNet needs stronger literature justification.  
- Computational overhead of gradient-based method (double backprop) is mentioned but not quantified—needs analysis.  
- Batch normalization challenges for gradient-based transfer are noted but not explored or resolved.  
- Lacks deeper insight/theoretical explanation for why attention maps are more transferable than full activations.  
- Requests: more detailed ImageNet experiment statistics and computational cost comparisons to standard training/KD.  
- Requests: clarification whether activation-based superiority holds across all architectures/datasets or if exceptions exist.  
- Requests: explanation why knowledge distillation hurt convergence on ImageNet and whether hyperparameter tuning could fix it.  
- Requests: additional visualizations to illustrate layer-specific attention behavior across datasets.  
- Decision: Accept.
