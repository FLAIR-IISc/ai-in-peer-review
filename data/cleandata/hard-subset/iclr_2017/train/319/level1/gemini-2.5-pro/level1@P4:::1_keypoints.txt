- Proposes Attention Transfer (AT) to teach a student to mimic teacher spatial attention maps.
- Activation-based AT: attention maps from channel-wise summary statistics (computationally cheap).
- Gradient-based AT: attention as input-gradient saliency maps, requiring double backprop (computationally expensive).
- Core contributions: attention-as-knowledge idea, two AT formulations, extensive experiments, and combining AT with distillation.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: high novelty and a simple, well-motivated method distinct from KD and FitNets.
- Key reason: strong, comprehensive empirical validation across datasets and architectures, including ImageNet improvements.
- AT outperforms full-activation transfer (FitNets-style), e.g., WRN-16-2→WRN-16-1 results cited.
- ImageNet result (ResNet-34→ResNet-18) shows a notable 1.1% top-1 improvement, a significant benchmark success.
- Gradient-based AT is promising but preliminary due to computational challenges; includes a gradient-symmetry self-regularizer.
- Recommend clarifying that their “attention” are post-hoc saliency maps, different from learned attention mechanisms.
- Recommend an ablation showing the effect of l2-normalizing attention maps on transfer performance.
- Note ambiguity of Batch Normalization behavior during second backprop in gradient-based AT; suggest discussing potential approaches.
- Recommend discussing sensitivity to hyperparameters (p in F_sum^p and loss weight β).
- Questions for authors: effect of skipping normalization; impact of adding AT at earlier layers on ImageNet; KD failure modes across heterogeneous architectures; feasibility of approximating second-order derivatives for gradient-based AT.
