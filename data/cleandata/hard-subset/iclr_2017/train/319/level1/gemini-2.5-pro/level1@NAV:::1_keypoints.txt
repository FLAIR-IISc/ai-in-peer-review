- Proposes Attention Transfer (AT) to teach a student network by mimicking teacher spatial attention maps.  
- Formalizes two attention-map definitions: activation-based and gradient-based.  
- Shows transferring distilled spatial attention is more effective than matching full activation tensors.  
- Demonstrates AT can be combined with traditional knowledge distillation.  
- Validates method across datasets (CIFAR, CUB, ImageNet) and multiple modern CNN architectures.  
- Strong recommendation for acceptance based on well-motivated novelty and rigorous empirical validation.  
- Positions attention-map transfer as an intermediate, intuitive hint between full feature-map matching and output distribution matching.  
- Provides extensive, well-designed experiments with consistent performance gains, notably successful on ImageNet.  
- Suggests clarifying how this functional “attention” relates to common self-attention or seq2seq attention mechanisms.  
- Requests more insight or preliminary thoughts on gradient-based attention’s interaction with batch normalization.  
- Recommends including sensitivity analysis for the hyperparameter β.  
- Asks for intuition on why summarized attention maps outperform full activation transfer and whether this acts as regularization.  
- Inquires whether AT is especially suited for tasks needing localization of small discriminative regions (e.g., CUB).
