This paper proposes a novel approach to knowledge transfer in deep neural networks by leveraging attention mechanisms. The authors introduce two types of spatial attention maps, activation-based and gradient-based, and demonstrate their effectiveness in improving the performance of a student network by forcing it to mimic the attention maps of a powerful teacher network. The paper presents a comprehensive study of attention transfer on various image classification datasets, including CIFAR, ImageNet, and fine-grained recognition tasks.

I decide to **accept** this paper, with two key reasons for this choice:

1. **Well-motivated approach**: The authors provide a clear and well-motivated explanation of why attention mechanisms can be useful for knowledge transfer in deep neural networks. They also provide a thorough review of related work on attention mechanisms and knowledge distillation.
2. **Strong experimental results**: The paper presents a comprehensive set of experiments on various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed attention transfer methods. The results show consistent improvements in performance, especially when combined with knowledge distillation.

Supporting arguments for these reasons include:

* The authors provide a clear and intuitive explanation of how attention mechanisms can be used to transfer knowledge from a teacher network to a student network.
* The proposed attention transfer methods are simple to implement and require minimal additional computational cost.
* The experimental results are thorough and well-presented, with clear comparisons to baseline methods and other knowledge transfer techniques.

Additional feedback to improve the paper:

* While the paper provides a good overview of related work on attention mechanisms, it would be helpful to include a more detailed discussion of how the proposed approach differs from existing methods.
* The authors could provide more insight into why certain attention mapping functions (e.g., sum-based vs. max-based) work better than others.
* It would be interesting to see more experiments on other types of tasks, such as object detection or segmentation, to further demonstrate the generality of the proposed approach.

Questions for the authors:

* Can you provide more details on how the attention transfer method is affected by the choice of teacher and student network architectures?
* How do the proposed attention transfer methods compare to other knowledge transfer techniques, such as FitNets or DarkRank?
* Are there any plans to extend the proposed approach to other types of neural networks, such as recurrent neural networks or graph neural networks?
