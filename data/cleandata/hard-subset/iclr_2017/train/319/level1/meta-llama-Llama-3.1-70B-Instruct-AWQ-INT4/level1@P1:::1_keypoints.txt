- Paper proposes attention transfer for knowledge distillation using activation- and gradient-based spatial attention maps to guide a student network.
- Evaluated comprehensively on CIFAR, ImageNet, and fine-grained recognition tasks showing consistent performance gains.
- Recommendation: accept.
- Reason 1: well-motivated approach with clear explanation and thorough related-work coverage on attention and distillation.
- Reason 2: strong experimental results demonstrating consistent improvements, especially when combined with knowledge distillation.
- Supporting: intuitive explanation of attention-based transfer from teacher to student.
- Supporting: methods are simple to implement and incur minimal extra computational cost.
- Supporting: experiments are thorough and comparisons to baselines are clear.
- Suggestion: include a more detailed discussion of how this approach differs from existing methods.
- Suggestion: provide insight into why certain attention mapping functions (e.g., sum vs. max) perform better.
- Suggestion: evaluate on other tasks (e.g., object detection, segmentation) to show generality.
- Question: how sensitive is attention transfer to the choice of teacher and student architectures?
- Question: how do these methods compare quantitatively to other transfer techniques like FitNets or DarkRank?
- Question: are there plans to extend attention transfer to other network types (RNNs, GNNs)?
