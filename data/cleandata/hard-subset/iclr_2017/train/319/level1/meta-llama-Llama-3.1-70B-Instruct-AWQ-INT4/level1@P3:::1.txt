This paper proposes a novel approach to knowledge transfer between neural networks, leveraging attention mechanisms to improve the performance of a student network by mimicking the attention maps of a powerful teacher network. The authors define two types of spatial attention maps, activation-based and gradient-based, and explore their application in various image classification tasks. The experimental results demonstrate consistent improvements across different datasets and architectures, showcasing the effectiveness of the proposed attention transfer methods.

**Decision:** Accept

**Key reasons:**

1. The paper presents a well-motivated and novel approach to knowledge transfer, building upon the concept of attention mechanisms in neural networks.
2. The experimental results are thorough and demonstrate consistent improvements across different datasets and architectures, providing strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Supporting arguments:**

* The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the concept of attention in neural networks, highlighting its importance in human visual experience and its potential applications in computer vision and NLP tasks.
* The proposed attention transfer methods are well-defined and easy to understand, with a clear explanation of the activation-based and gradient-based attention maps.
* The experimental results are extensive and cover a range of datasets and architectures, including CIFAR, ImageNet, and fine-grained recognition tasks.
* The authors provide a detailed analysis of the results, including comparisons with other knowledge transfer methods and ablation studies to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed attention transfer methods.

**Additional feedback:**

* While the paper provides a good introduction to the concept of attention in neural networks, it would be beneficial to include a more comprehensive review of related work in the field, including other knowledge transfer methods and attention mechanisms.
* The authors could provide more details on the implementation of the attention transfer methods, including the specific architectures and hyperparameters used in the experiments.
* It would be interesting to see the application of the proposed attention transfer methods to other tasks, such as object detection or weakly-supervised localization, as mentioned in the conclusion.
* The authors could consider providing more visualizations of the attention maps, particularly for the gradient-based attention transfer method, to help illustrate the concept and its application.

**Questions for the authors:**

* Can you provide more details on the implementation of the attention transfer methods, including the specific architectures and hyperparameters used in the experiments?
* How do you plan to extend the proposed attention transfer methods to other tasks, such as object detection or weakly-supervised localization?
* Can you provide more visualizations of the attention maps, particularly for the gradient-based attention transfer method, to help illustrate the concept and its application?
* How do you think the proposed attention transfer methods can be applied to other domains, such as NLP or robotics?
