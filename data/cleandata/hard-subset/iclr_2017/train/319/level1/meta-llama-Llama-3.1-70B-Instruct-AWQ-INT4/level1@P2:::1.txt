**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

The paper proposes a novel approach to knowledge transfer in convolutional neural networks (CNNs) using attention mechanisms. The authors define attention as a set of spatial maps that encode the importance of different regions in the input image for the network's output decision. They introduce two types of spatial attention maps: activation-based and gradient-based. The paper demonstrates that transferring attention information from a powerful teacher network to a smaller student network can significantly improve the student's performance across various datasets and architectures. The authors also show that combining attention transfer with knowledge distillation can lead to even better results.
