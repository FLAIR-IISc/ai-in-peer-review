- Decision: Accept.  
- Novel contribution: Proposes a well-motivated, novel attention-based knowledge transfer method for student networks.  
- Method: Defines two spatial attention map types—activation-based and gradient-based—for attention transfer.  
- Empirical result: Shows consistent performance improvements across datasets and architectures.  
- Experimental breadth: Thorough experiments on CIFAR, ImageNet, and fine-grained recognition tasks.  
- Clarity: Clear, concise introduction and easy-to-understand method descriptions.  
- Analysis: Includes comparisons with other knowledge transfer methods and ablation studies.  
- Missing related work: Would benefit from a more comprehensive review of related knowledge transfer and attention literature.  
- Implementation details: Needs more specifics on architectures, training details, and hyperparameters.  
- Broader application: Suggests extension to tasks like object detection and weakly-supervised localization.  
- Visualization: Recommends additional attention map visualizations, especially for gradient-based maps.  
- Generalization questions: Asks how methods would be implemented in detail, extended to other tasks, visualized further, and applied to domains like NLP or robotics.
