**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach for transferring knowledge from a powerful teacher network to a smaller student network, leveraging attention mechanisms. The authors define two types of spatial attention maps, activation-based and gradient-based, and explore their use in transferring attention information from the teacher network to the student network. The proposed approach, called attention transfer, is evaluated on various image recognition datasets, including CIFAR, ImageNet, and fine-grained classification tasks. The results show consistent improvements in performance, especially when combined with knowledge distillation.
