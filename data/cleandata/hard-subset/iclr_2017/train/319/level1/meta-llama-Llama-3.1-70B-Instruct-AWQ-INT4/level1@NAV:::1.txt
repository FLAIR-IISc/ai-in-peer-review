**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to knowledge transfer in convolutional neural networks (CNNs) by leveraging attention mechanisms. The authors define attention as a set of spatial maps that capture the importance of different regions in an input image for a CNN's output decision. They introduce two types of attention maps: activation-based and gradient-based. The authors then propose several methods for transferring attention from a powerful teacher network to a smaller student network, with the goal of improving the student's performance. The paper presents experimental results on various image classification datasets, demonstrating consistent improvements in performance using attention transfer.

**Decision**

Based on my review, I decide to **Accept** this paper. The paper tackles a specific and well-motivated problem, namely, how to improve the performance of a student CNN network by leveraging attention mechanisms from a teacher network. The approach is well-placed in the literature, building upon existing work on attention mechanisms and knowledge transfer. The paper provides a clear and thorough explanation of the proposed methods, and the experimental results are convincing and well-presented.

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper's contributions are significant, as it introduces a new approach to knowledge transfer that is both effective and efficient. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed attention transfer methods, including a comparison of different attention mapping functions and an evaluation of the impact of attention transfer on the student network's performance. The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating consistent improvements in performance across various datasets and network architectures.
