- Proposes attention-based knowledge transfer for CNNs using spatial attention maps (activation- and gradient-based) to improve student networks.  
- Introduces methods to transfer teacher attention to students and reports consistent performance gains on image classification datasets.  
- Decision: Accept — well-motivated, well-situated in literature, clearly explained methods, and convincing experiments.  
- Contributions: new effective and efficient attention transfer approach with thorough analysis and comparisons of attention mapping functions.  
- Requests more details on computational cost of the proposed attention transfer methods versus other transfer methods.  
- Recommends evaluating the impact of attention transfer on more challenging tasks (object detection, segmentation).  
- Suggests exploring other attention mechanisms (self-attention, multi-head attention) to potentially improve results.  
- Question: provide implementation details, especially computational cost and memory requirements.  
- Question: how to extend proposed methods to object detection or segmentation?
