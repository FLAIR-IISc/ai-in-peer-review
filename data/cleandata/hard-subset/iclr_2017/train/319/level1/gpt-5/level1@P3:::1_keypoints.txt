- Proposes "attention transfer" (AT) to transfer spatial attention between CNNs using activation- and gradient-based attention maps.  
- Defines activation-based attention via pooled absolute activations (sum, sum of powers, max of powers) and gradient-based attention via input gradients.  
- Trains students to match teacher attention maps with an L2 loss on L2-normalized attention vectors alongside the task loss.  
- Uses double backpropagation for gradient-based AT and introduces a flip-invariance regularizer.  
- Code and models are released.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Novel, simple, broadly applicable formulation of attention as a distillation target.  
- Key reason 2: Consistent, meaningful empirical improvements across datasets and architectures with sensible ablations.  
- Motivation: Aligning spatial "where" information is architecture-agnostic, low-dimensional, and focuses on class-relevant regions.  
- Methodology: Attention pooling family and L2 normalization are sensible; layer pairing per-block/group is practical.  
- Empirical — CIFAR-10: AT consistently improves WRN students and complements KD; sum-based pooling outperforms max-based.  
- Empirical — Gradient AT: Classic gradient-norm penalty and proposed symmetry term improve performance on small models (no BN).  
- Empirical — Transfer learning: AT narrows gap between ResNet-18 and ResNet-34, outperforming KD on CUB.  
- Empirical — ImageNet: ResNet-18 gains ~1.1% top-1 and 0.8% top-5 with only two AT losses, a notable result at scale.  
- Comparison: Activation-based AT outperforms FitNets-style full activation regression and complements KD.  
- Caveat: Gradient-based AT shown only on small models without BN; BN in second backward pass is unresolved.  
- Caveat: ImageNet experiments under-tuned; stronger baselines and full training curves would strengthen results.  
- Caveat: Statistical reporting inconsistent (mix of medians and single runs).  
- Caveat: AT is largely class-agnostic; class-conditional attention transfer might improve multi-object/fine-grained tasks.  
- Suggested ablations: Systematically test normalization (L1 vs L2), exponent p, and spatial resolution matching/interpolation.  
- Suggested reporting: Provide precise compute/memory overhead for activation- and gradient-based AT, including teacher forward cost.  
- Suggested comparisons: Add feature-hint transfer with adapters, tuned KD, and Jacobian regularizers on ImageNet.  
- Suggested extensions: Explore class-conditional attention (Grad-CAM/++), robustness/data-efficiency in low-data regimes, and cross-architecture transfer.  
- Request for clarity: Provide schedule/sensitivity for β (layer-wise scaling/decay) and a simple user recipe.  
- Key questions for authors include sensitivity to normalization/p, which layers contribute most, experiments with class-conditional attention, KD interplay and tuning, BN handling for gradient AT, overhead measurements, effect of teacher quality, and applicability to detection/segmentation.
