- Introduces Attention Transfer (AT): match spatial attention maps between teacher and student during training.  
- Proposes two AT types: activation-based (channel-statistics like squared-sum, l2-normalized, l2 loss) and gradient-based (match input gradients, requires double backprop, with flip-invariance regularizer).  
- AT is architecture-agnostic, low-overhead for activation-based AT, and complementary to Knowledge Distillation (KD).  
- Empirical gains are consistent across CIFAR-10/100 (WRN/NIN/ResNet), transfer learning (CUB-200, MIT Scenes), and ImageNet (ResNet-18 student with ResNet-34 teacher: +1.1% top-1, +0.8% top-5).  
- AT outperforms regressing full activations (FitNet-style) and speeds convergence.  
- Ablations explore attention definitions (sum vs max, p values), loss placement, and normalization choices.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reasons for accept: novel, simple, broadly applicable idea with strong, consistent empirical improvements including a notable ImageNet result.  
- Motivation: targets transferring “where to look” (spatial saliency) rather than full representations, grounded in interpretability/prior visualization work.  
- Method soundness: activation-based AT is simple, differentiable, and scale-robust via l2 normalization; gradient-based AT is theoretically justified and includes a practical regularizer.  
- CIFAR results: meaningful gains for same- and different-depth teacher/student; AT+KD often best; sum-based maps beat max-based.  
- Gradient-based results: improves baseline; flip-invariance is effective; activation-based AT generally performs best in same setup.  
- Transfer learning: AT closes much of the gap between ResNet-18 and ResNet-34 on fine-grained tasks and outperforms KD on CUB.  
- ImageNet: +1.1% top-1 gain with minimal tuning is compelling; KD reported ineffective under same conditions.  
- Reproducibility: code and models released; major implementation details provided.  
- Requested experimental improvements: report mean±std over runs, standardize reporting (medians vs single runs), and provide sensitivity analyses (β scaling, normalization types, layer choices, p in Fpsum).  
- Fairness and baselines: provide detailed ImageNet training/augmentation/schedule info, stronger KD hyperparameter sweeps, include FitNets/KD on CUB/Scenes and modern distillation baselines on ImageNet.  
- Practicality concerns: clarify gradient-based AT computational/memory overhead and BN handling during double backprop (frozen vs updated stats).  
- Qualitative and scope suggestions: add visualizations of teacher/student attentions pre/post AT, test more teacher/student pairs (cross-architecture), and temper claims about being the “first” successful ImageNet transfer or strengthen with more pairs.  
- Specific questions for authors: sensitivity to normalization choices (l2/l1/softmax/KL), effects of number/position of AT losses, performance with weak/similar teachers and negative transfer cases, cross-architecture alignment, exact training-time/memory overhead for gradient-based AT, BN treatment during second pass, thoroughness of KD sweeps on ImageNet, and categories or cases where AT fails or hurts.
