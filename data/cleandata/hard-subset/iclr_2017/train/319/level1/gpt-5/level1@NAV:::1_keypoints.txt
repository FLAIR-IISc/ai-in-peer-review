- Proposes Attention Transfer (AT) to transfer teacher CNN knowledge by aligning spatial attention maps (activation- and gradient-based).  
- Activation-based maps use channel-wise reductions of feature magnitudes with normalization; gradient-based maps use input saliency via double-backprop.  
- Method is architecture-agnostic, applicable at multiple layers, complements knowledge distillation, and code is released.  
- Empirical results show consistent improvements across CIFAR-10, CUB-200, MIT Scenes, and ImageNet on ResNets, NIN, and WRNs.  
- Activation-based AT outperforms full activation regression and yields nontrivial ImageNet gains (e.g., ResNet-18 +1.1% top-1).  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Novel, well-motivated idea treating attention as a transferable inductive bias rather than just visualization.  
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical evidence across datasets and architectures with minimal overhead.  
- Problem/motivation: Addresses what aspects of representation to transfer in KD, arguing spatial attention is discriminative, lower-dimensional, and robust.  
- Methodology: Clean activation-based formulation (normalized spatial maps, sum/max and p choices), sensible layer-wise losses, principled gradient-AT connection to double backprop.  
- Evidence: CIFAR ablations support claims; gains on CUB/Scenes demonstrate breadth; ImageNet improvement shows scalability.  
- Rigor: Results are compelling but limited by some single-run experiments; gradient AT restricted to NIN on CIFAR; ImageNet KD baselines may be under-tuned.  
- Requested improvements: Report mean/std over seeds and hyperparameter sensitivity (β schedule, normalization, p, layer selection).  
- Requested improvements: Provide compute/memory overhead for AT and double backprop and clarify BN handling in second backprop.  
- Requested experiments: Strengthen ImageNet baselines, explore cross-architecture transfer, analyze failure modes, and show attention similarity vs. accuracy correlation with qualitative maps.  
- Questions asked: Sensitivity to l1 vs l2 normalization and exponent p; exact compute overhead per batch; handling of batch norm in second backprop; effect when teacher/student have different downsampling schedules; multi-run ImageNet stats and group-wise ablations; why AT outperforms full activation transfer (e.g., sparsity/low-rank).
