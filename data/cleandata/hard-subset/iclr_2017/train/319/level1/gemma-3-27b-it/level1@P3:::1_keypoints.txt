- Proposes transferring knowledge by matching activation- and gradient-based attention maps between teacher and student CNNs.  
- Core idea is intuitive and experimental results are promising across datasets and architectures.  
- Recommendation: Reject.  
- Experiments lack depth and clarity, weakening claims of robustness and generalizability.  
- Experimental design is ad-hoc with inconsistent hyperparameter tuning and selective result reporting.  
- Ablation studies are limited, making it hard to isolate the contribution of attention transfer versus other factors.  
- Writing is redundant and lacks concise explanations for key decisions.  
- Comparison to full activation transfer is unconvincing due to vague implementation details (e.g., 1x1 convs for shape matching may be a bottleneck).  
- Gradient-based attention experiments are limited to one network/dataset and the batch-norm issue is not convincingly analyzed.  
- ImageNet results are reported without sufficient detail; claims that KD failed lack hyperparameter/training evidence.  
- Relation to existing KD methods is underexplored; interactions and differences with KD are not well explained.  
- Appendix contains useful information but is poorly integrated into the main text.  
- Suggestion: perform rigorous ablations comparing attention mapping functions, combinations with KD, and weighting factors.  
- Suggestion: fully document hyperparameter tuning ranges and selection criteria.  
- Suggestion: release comprehensive reproducible code and detailed experimental instructions.  
- Suggestion: provide clearer experimental setups identifying datasets, architectures, training parameters, and metrics.  
- Suggestion: give deeper analysis of ImageNet experiments (training curves, val/top-5 accuracy) and explain KD failure.  
- Suggestion: explore theoretical justification for why attention transfer should work (e.g., representational similarity).  
- Suggestion: investigate the batch-normalization interaction with gradient-based attention transfer in depth.  
- Questions for authors: provide detailed ImageNet KD hyperparameters and training curves.  
- Questions for authors: confirm capacity of 1x1 convs in activation vs. attention comparisons to rule out confounding.  
- Questions for authors: provide computational cost comparison between attention transfer and full activation transfer.  
- Questions for authors: report experiments with other loss norms (l1, cosine) besides l2 for attention loss.  
- Questions for authors: clarify how attention transfer relates to and differs from feature-based or relation-based KD.
