- Paper studies transferring attention (activation/gradient maps) from teacher to student CNNs to improve student performance.  
- Recommendation: Reject.

- Main critique: experimental results lack clarity and rigor, insufficient to support claims of significant improvement.  
- CIFAR experiments: setup (hyperparameters, training schedule, tuning) not clearly described, hindering reproducibility.  
- Reported gains are often <1% accuracy, so the claim of "significant" improvement is unsupported.  
- ImageNet experiments: insufficient detail and weak presentation of results and comparisons.  
- Comparison to full activation transfer is unclear—implementation specifics and reasons for attention transfer superiority are not well explained.  
- Novelty: method feels incremental relative to existing knowledge distillation and attention work.  
- Differentiation from knowledge distillation (including FitNets) is unconvincing and comparisons lack depth.  
- Observation that KD fails on ImageNet is noted but not analyzed rigorously.

- Reproducibility recommendation: provide full experimental details and release well-documented code.  
- Ablation studies: perform comprehensive ablations (attention mapping functions, loss weights, layer choices).  
- Baselines: include stronger, more recent knowledge distillation baselines and feature-based distillation methods.  
- Statistical significance: report tests to show improvements are not due to chance.  
- Presentation: improve clarity, conciseness of attention mapping descriptions, and figure quality; discuss limitations more directly.  
- Theoretical support: add justification for why attention transfer should work.  
- Gradient-based attention: clarify implementation details and address challenges with batch normalization and the second backpropagation.

- Questions for authors: detail hyperparameter tuning, learning rate schedule, and optimizer for CIFAR.  
- Questions for authors: provide full ImageNet breakdown—teacher/student architectures, schedules, and learning rates.  
- Questions for authors: explain why attention transfer outperforms full activation transfer and what distinct information attention maps capture.  
- Questions for authors: compare to recent, sophisticated distillation losses and feature-based distillation methods.  
- Questions for authors: elaborate on challenges and fixes when applying gradient-based attention transfer to networks with batch normalization.
