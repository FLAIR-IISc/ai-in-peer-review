## Review of "Attention Transfer"

This paper explores the transfer of attention mechanisms between convolutional neural networks (CNNs) to improve student network performance. The authors propose defining attention as spatial maps derived from both activation and gradient information, and then demonstrate various methods for transferring this attention from a powerful teacher network to a student network. The core idea—leveraging the “where to look” information of a well-trained network to guide the learning of another—is compelling and well-motivated, building upon recent work in both attention mechanisms and knowledge distillation. The paper provides a comprehensive experimental evaluation across several datasets and network architectures, demonstrating consistent performance improvements.

**Reject.** While the paper presents an interesting approach and supporting results, the experimental setup lacks sufficient clarity and control for a strong acceptance. Specifically, the hyperparameter tuning process is not well described, and the justification for certain design choices (e.g., network architectures used for gradient-based transfer) is lacking. Furthermore, the claim of being "the first knowledge transfer method to be successfully applied on ImageNet" feels overstated without more extensive comparison to established knowledge distillation techniques.

The authors clearly demonstrate that attention transfer can improve performance, particularly with activation-based methods. The results on CIFAR and the finetuning experiments on CUB and Scenes are encouraging. However, the inconsistency in experimental setup across different attention types (e.g. different normalizations used) makes direct comparison difficult. The gradient-based attention transfer section feels less robust and is hampered by the constraints imposed by the choice of network architecture.  The limited discussion of computational cost and scalability also limits the practical impact of the findings. The paper relies heavily on empirical results, and a more thorough theoretical grounding of the proposed methods would strengthen the contribution.

**Additional Feedback:**

*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** How sensitive are the results to the choice of the β parameter in the loss function? A sensitivity analysis would be valuable.
*   **Ablation Studies:** While the paper compares different attention mapping functions, a more detailed ablation study, investigating the impact of transferring attention from different layers, would be insightful.
*   **Computational Cost:** What is the overhead introduced by the attention transfer mechanism, both during training and inference?
*   **Reproducibility:**  While code is provided, clear instructions for reproducing the results would be extremely helpful.
*   **ImageNet Discussion:** Could you elaborate on why KD didn't work well on ImageNet and how the proposed method overcomes the limitations? A detailed comparison with existing KD methods on ImageNet would be beneficial to validate the claims.
