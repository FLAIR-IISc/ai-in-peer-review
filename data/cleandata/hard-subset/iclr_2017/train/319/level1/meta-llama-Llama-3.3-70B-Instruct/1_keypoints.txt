- Proposes a novel teacher-to-student knowledge transfer method using attention as spatial maps.  
- Defines attention as spatial maps and introduces activation-based and gradient-based attention maps.  
- Demonstrates that transferring attention maps improves student network performance.  
- Presents experiments on CIFAR, ImageNet, and fine-grained recognition showing consistent improvements.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Well-motivated approach with solid literature grounding and a novel perspective.  
- Key reason 2: Strong, robust experimental results indicating generalizability.  
- Supporting argument: Thorough analysis and clear comparisons with other knowledge transfer methods.  
- Request: Provide more implementation details (architectures and tuned hyperparameters).  
- Request: Add more analysis and visualizations of attention maps to interpret model decisions.
