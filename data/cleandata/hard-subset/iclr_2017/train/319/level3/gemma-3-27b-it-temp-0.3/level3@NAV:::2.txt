## Review of "Attention Transfer"

This paper proposes a novel knowledge distillation framework centered around minimizing the differences in summed statistics across feature maps of teacher and student networks. The core idea – transferring “attention” defined as activation patterns – is compelling and the authors demonstrate empirical improvements over FitNet-style distillation across multiple computer vision datasets. The paper is generally well-written and clearly presents the proposed methods.

I recommend **Accept** with minor revisions. The method shows promising results and addresses an interesting problem in knowledge distillation. However, the paper suffers from organizational issues and some conceptual imprecision that need to be addressed.

The primary strength of the work lies in its empirical validation. The authors convincingly demonstrate that transferring attention information, as defined by their summed statistics approach, leads to performance gains. The experiments across CIFAR, CUB, Scenes, and ImageNet provide a good breadth of evaluation. The ablation studies on CIFAR, comparing different attention mapping functions and the inclusion of knowledge distillation, are particularly insightful. 

However, the term “attention” is misleading. The method distills summed activations, not attention *maps* in the traditional sense (e.g., those derived from attention mechanisms). This is a crucial distinction. Furthermore, the gradient-based attention transfer section feels disconnected from the main contribution. While interesting, it’s not directly compared to or integrated with the activation-based transfer, diminishing its impact. The computation of the 2-norm in Eq.(2) is also unclear; the authors should clarify whether they intended the largest singular value or the Frobenius norm, as the former is computationally expensive.

**Additional Feedback:**

*   **Clarify “Attention” Definition:** Reframe the terminology to avoid confusion. Perhaps “activation pattern transfer” or “feature map alignment” would be more accurate.
*   **Integrate or Remove Gradient-Based Section:** Either demonstrate a clear synergy between the gradient-based and activation-based methods or remove the former to streamline the paper.
*   **Elaborate on 2-Norm Computation:** Provide details on how the 2-norm in Eq.(2) is calculated. If it's the largest singular value, justify the computational cost.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Discuss the sensitivity of the results to the β parameter in Eq.(2).
*   **Why does KD struggle on ImageNet?** The authors mention KD struggles on ImageNet. Elaborate on potential reasons and future directions.



**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  Could you provide a computational complexity analysis of calculating the 2-norm in Eq.(2), specifically detailing the cost of computing the largest singular value for a feature map of size H x W?
2.  Why do you believe the gradient-based attention transfer method did not show similar improvements to the activation-based method, and what potential modifications could be explored to improve its performance?
3.  You mention KD struggling on ImageNet. Was this observed across different teacher/student architectures, or was it specific to the ResNet-18/ResNet-34 pairing? What hyperparameters were used for the KD experiments on ImageNet?
