- Proposes Attention Transfer (AT), a method to teach a smaller student network to mimic spatial attention maps of a larger teacher.  
- Defines attention maps via channel-wise activation summaries or input-gradient-based maps.  
- Positions AT as an alternative/complement to knowledge distillation, arguing summarized attention is better than full activation tensor transfer.  
- Shows consistent improvements on CIFAR, CUB, and Scenes datasets.  
- Reviewer recommends Weak Accept: novel, well-motivated idea with promise on smaller datasets.  
- Main strength: elegant framing of knowledge transfer as teaching "where to look" and computationally efficient activation-based maps.  
- Activation-based AT outperforms FitNets-style full-tensor hints on CIFAR.  
- Primary weakness: insufficient and preliminary ImageNet experiments undermine general applicability claims.  
- ImageNet evaluation issues: single student-teacher pair, untuned hyperparameters, and transfer losses applied to only two of four residual groups.  
- Claim that AT is the first successful ImageNet transfer method is unsupported given limited evidence and potentially poorly tuned KD baseline.  
- Recommendation for improvement: expand ImageNet experiments with multiple architectures and a properly tuned KD baseline to strengthen claims.  
- Questions for authors: details on KD hyperparameter search on ImageNet and results (if any) for adding transfer losses to all four residual groups.
