- Proposes "Attention Transfer" (AT): distill spatial attention maps (channel-wise p-norm summaries) from teacher to student via L2 loss on normalized maps.
- AT transfers compressed spatial summaries instead of full activation tensors, reducing complexity.
- Empirically outperforms FitNet-style full activation transfer and combines well with traditional KD.
- Demonstrates consistent improvements across CIFAR-10, CUB-200, MIT Scenes, and ImageNet (notably +1.1% top-1 for ResNet-18).
- Method is simple, intuitive, easy to implement, and computationally efficient with no auxiliary parameters.
- Strong empirical contribution: closes student–teacher gap across architectures, datasets, and tasks (classification and fine-tuning).
- Comparison to full-activation transfer validates that activation summaries can be more effective than raw activations.
- Use of the term "attention" is misleading versus standard, learnable attention mechanisms; alternative names suggested.
- The gradient-based attention transfer section feels disconnected, underdeveloped, and experimentally limited.
- Recommends moving gradient-based material to appendix to keep paper focused on activation-based AT.
- Equation (2) notation for the L2 norm could be clarified to avoid confusion about vector vs. matrix norm.
- Suggests analyzing the effect of the p parameter (controls map "peakiness") on transfer performance.
- Asks authors to justify the "attention" terminology and whether alternative names were considered.
- Requests rationale for including gradient-based AT and plans for more comparable experiments.
- Seeks intuition why sum-based aggregation outperforms max-based aggregation for transfer.
