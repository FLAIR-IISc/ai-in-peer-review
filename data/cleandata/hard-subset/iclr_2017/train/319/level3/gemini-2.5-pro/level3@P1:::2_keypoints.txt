- Introduces Attention Transfer (AT), forcing a student network to mimic teacher attention maps computed from activation summaries or input gradients.  
- Activation-based AT consistently improves student performance across CIFAR-10/100, CUB-200, MIT Scenes, and ImageNet.  
- Activation-based AT outperforms FitNets-style full activation tensor transfer and is more effective as a compressed spatial hint.  
- Demonstrates scalability by improving ResNet-18 top-1 accuracy on ImageNet by ~1.1% when learning from ResNet-34.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Simple, intuitive, and empirically strong method that beats strong baselines across datasets and architectures.  
- Key reason 2: Significant contribution by successfully applying knowledge transfer at ImageNet scale where prior methods struggled.  
- Strength: Summary statistics of activation maps act as efficient and effective "hints" and improve results further when combined with KD.  
- Feedback 1: The term "attention" may be misleading; recommend clarifying or using a more precise term like "activation-based spatial summary transfer."
