- Introduces Attention Transfer (AT): train student to mimic teacher spatial attention maps (activation- and gradient-based) using L2 loss on normalized maps, combined with classification loss; shows consistent gains on CIFAR, CUB, Scenes, ImageNet and with KD.

- Decision: Weak Accept — method is simple and yields consistent improvements, but experiments lack sufficient rigor.

- Strength: Clear motivation and intuitive idea that students should "look" where teachers do; consistent empirical improvements across architectures and datasets; clear writing and easy to implement.

- Weakness: Experimental analysis is shallow; key claims (e.g., higher-accuracy networks have more correlated attention maps) are only supported qualitatively.

- Weakness: No quantitative validation relating attention maps to ground-truth object locations or segmentation.

- Weakness: No ablation or sensitivity study for the crucial AT loss weight β, leaving reproducibility and stability unclear.

- Weakness: No guidance on choosing between attention map variants (sum vs max, choice of p); novelty is incremental relative to FitNets.

- Weakness: Lacks discussion of practical trade-offs since student never exceeds teacher (e.g., inference speed-up vs accuracy loss).

- Suggested improvement: Provide quantitative analysis comparing attention maps to bounding boxes or segmentation masks.

- Suggested improvement: Include ablation on AT loss weight β to show sensitivity and guidance for tuning.

- Suggested improvement: Add teacher training/validation loss curves in comparisons (e.g., Fig. 7b) for context.

- Suggested improvement: Report parameter counts, FLOPs, and inference speeds for teacher and student to quantify efficiency gains.

- Question: Clarify rationale for selecting specific activation-based attention map types (F_sum^2 vs F_max)—principled or empirical choice?

- Question: Have you tested AT when student and teacher share the same architecture to determine if AT is a general regularizer or mainly a cross-architecture distillation tool?
