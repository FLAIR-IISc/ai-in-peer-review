1. Proposes Attention Transfer (AT) to force students to mimic teacher spatial attention maps (activation- and gradient-based) and reports improved accuracy on CIFAR, CUB, MIT Scenes, and preliminary ImageNet results.  
2. Decision: Reject — reviewer finds empirical validation insufficient for acceptance.  
3. Primary concern: Experiments do not convincingly show AT outperforms or complements standard Knowledge Distillation (KD) because KD-only baselines are missing.  
4. Primary concern: ImageNet claims are based on a single, untuned teacher-student run and are therefore too preliminary to support broad claims.  
5. Supporting: CIFAR results lack a "KD only" baseline, making it impossible to attribute gains to AT versus KD in combined AT+KD settings.  
6. Supporting: The authors’ reported failure of KD on ImageNet suggests hyperparameter issues, so their ImageNet comparison is unreliable.  
7. Supporting: Gradient-based AT experiments use nonstandard training (thin NIN, no BN, no random crops), so they are not directly comparable to activation-based AT results.  
8. Suggested fix: Add thorough KD-only comparisons and re-run FitNets with the authors’ implementation for fair benchmarking.  
9. Suggested fix: Complete and tune ImageNet experiments, including multiple teacher-student pairs and hyperparameter searches for both AT and KD.  
10. Suggested fix: Provide deeper analysis/intuitions on why attention-map transfer outperforms full activation transfer (e.g., regularization/localization effects).  
11. Suggested fix: Include sensitivity analysis for the loss-balance hyperparameter beta to show robustness.  
12. Questions for authors: Provide KD-only results for Table 1; explain whether KD’s ImageNet failure is due to inherited CIFAR hyperparameters; hypothesize why summarized attention beats full activations; clarify whether p=2 is stable across datasets/architectures.
