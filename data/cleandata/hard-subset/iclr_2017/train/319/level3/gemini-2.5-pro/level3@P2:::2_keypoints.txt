- Proposes "attention transfer" (AT) to make a student CNN mimic teacher intermediate attention maps (activation-based and gradient-based), showing activation-based AT improves student performance across CIFAR-10, CUB-200, MIT Scenes, and ImageNet.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main concern: Terminology is misleading — the proposed "attention maps" are fixed, post-hoc channel-wise statistics, not learned/dynamic attention mechanisms.  
- Main concern: Paper is structurally disjoint — activation-based and gradient-based methods are not integrated and feel like two separate, incomplete ideas.  
- Activation-based maps are simple deterministic summaries (F(A)=sum(|A_i|^p)); the true contribution is distilling compact feature statistics, not an attention mechanism.  
- Gradient-based AT is underdeveloped, only evaluated on a non-standard NIN model on CIFAR-10, and limited by double backpropagation practicality.  
- The two approaches are never directly compared or combined, leaving unclear their relative merits or generality.  
- Empirical activation-based results are strong and notable, including being reported as the first successful knowledge transfer on ImageNet.  
- Suggestion: Reframe paper to focus on activation-based method with clearer title (e.g., "Distilling Activation Summaries" or "Feature Statistic Transfer").  
- Suggestion: Clarify notation in Eq.(2), specifically which matrix norm (spectral, Frobenius, or vector L2) is used for attention map normalization.  
- Suggestion: Strengthen ablation on which layers to apply transfer loss and provide intuition for why later layers (e.g., ResNet groups) transfer better.  
- Suggestion: Expand ImageNet analysis to explain why activation-based AT succeeds where prior KD methods struggle across different-depth networks.  
- Question 1: Which exact matrix norm is used in Eq.(2) for normalizing attention maps?  
- Question 2: Please justify using the term "attention" for fixed, post-hoc activation summaries and relate it to learned attention mechanisms in the literature.  
- Question 3: What motivated inclusion of the gradient-based transfer given its limited evaluation, and are there scenarios where it is preferable?  
- Question 4: For ImageNet, why does standard Knowledge Distillation "hurt convergence," and why is activation-based transfer more stable/effective in cross-architecture settings?
