- Proposes Attention Transfer (AT): student network trained to mimic teacher attention maps (activation-based and gradient-based).
- Main contributions: attention as transfer modality, several activation-based mappings, a gradient-based alternative, and experiments showing improvements across datasets and architectures.
- Decision: Weak Reject.
- Reason: AT gives consistent improvements but often only marginally better than standard Knowledge Distillation (KD).
- Reason: ImageNet results are preliminary and lack hyperparameter tuning, reducing confidence in claims about AT’s large-scale effectiveness.
- CIFAR-10: AT alone improves over baseline but is weaker than KD; AT+KD yields the best but only a small extra gain (~0.19%).
- Transfer learning: mixed results — MIT Scenes shows AT ≈ KD, while CUB shows substantial AT gains, suggesting AT helps fine-grained/localization tasks.
- ImageNet concern: reported 1.1% top-1 gain for ResNet-18 is promising but may be unreliable without tuned baselines; KD’s reported failure could stem from poor tuning.
- Core idea is intuitive and well-motivated, but empirical superiority over existing methods is not consistently demonstrated.
- Gradient-based AT experiments are limited, underexplored, and use a nonstandard simple network; consider focusing on activation-based AT.
- Activation-based AT may outperform transferring full activations (F-ActT), but comparison details matter.
- Ask for clarification whether F-ActT replication matches FitNets (loss/regressor specifics) or is a simplified L2 on activations.
- Recommend stronger ImageNet experiments with tuned hyperparameters for both AT and KD to substantiate claims.
- Suggest qualitative analysis explaining why AT excels on CUB (e.g., localization of discriminative parts).
- Note that AT and KD combined consistently perform best, implying complementary information transfer.
- Ask for intuition/analysis on why AT and KD are complementary (spatial evidence vs class-probability distribution).
- Request details on KD setup/tuning for ImageNet to determine if KD’s failure was experimental rather than fundamental.
