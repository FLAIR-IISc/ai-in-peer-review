- Introduces Attention Transfer (AT) to train a smaller student network to mimic a larger teacher's spatial attention maps.  
- Defines two attention map types: activation-based (channel summary statistics) and gradient-based (output sensitivity to input).  
- Demonstrates consistent student performance improvements across CIFAR, CUB, MIT Scenes, and ImageNet, and across architectures (NIN, ResNet, WRN).  
- Shows AT outperforms transferring full activation tensors and is among the first distillation methods to succeed on ImageNet.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Reason 1: Strong and consistent empirical results, notably ImageNet improvements.  
- Reason 2: Novel, elegant formulation compressing intermediate activations into spatial attention maps.  
- Tackles model compression and knowledge transfer to make compact students closer to larger teachers.  
- Well-motivated with ties to human visual attention and prior distillation/visualization literature (Hinton, Romero, Simonyan).  
- Claims that mimicking teacher attention improves student performance are strongly supported by multiple tables and ImageNet gains (e.g., +1.1% top-1 for ResNet-18).  
- Transferring summarized attention is empirically more effective than transferring full activations (F-ActT comparison).  
- Weakness: Qualitative claim that better networks have better-focused attention lacks quantitative validation.  
- Weakness: Students improve but do not surpass teachers, framing the work as efficiency improvement rather than new SOTA.  
- Weakness: Novelty may be seen as incremental over FitNets, though results justify contribution.  
- Suggestion: Quantify attention map quality (e.g., IoU with bounding boxes) to support visual claims.  
- Suggestion: Provide sensitivity analysis for loss weight β to guide practitioners.  
- Suggestion: Offer guidance on choosing between activation-based variants and gradient-based maps.  
- Suggestion: Broaden gradient-based AT experiments beyond the single CIFAR-10 setting without batch normalization.  
- Question: Have you tried AT between teacher and student of the same architecture (self-distillation/regularization)?  
- Question: Can you elaborate on batch-normalization-related difficulties in the second backpropagation for gradient-based AT and possible fixes?  
- Question: Have you explored the effect of transfer location/number (e.g., final layers vs. multiple stages) on performance?
