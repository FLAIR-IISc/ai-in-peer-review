- Proposes attention transfer (AT) to improve student CNNs by mimicking teacher attention maps (activation- and gradient-based) and shows consistent gains on CIFAR, CUB/Scene, and ImageNet.
- Recommendation: Reject with major revisions — paper doesn't convincingly establish why and when AT works.
- Primary concern: Lack of a compelling argument for AT's broader impact since student improvements are incremental and never exceed teacher performance.
- Needs characterization of speed-up with smaller students and study of AT when student and teacher share the same architecture to demonstrate performance/efficiency trade-offs.
- No clear guidance on selecting attention map type; insufficient analysis comparing activation-based vs gradient-based attention.
- Supporting: Student networks with AT still consistently underperform teachers, suggesting incomplete knowledge capture.
- Supporting: Hyperparameter sensitivity (attention transfer weight β) is unexplored and critical for practical guidance.
- Supporting: Incomplete results — Fig. 7b omits teacher training/validation loss, preventing assessment of convergence under AT+KD.
- Supporting: Incremental novelty — AT is close to prior knowledge distillation methods (e.g., FitNets) and its advance is not clearly articulated.
- Supporting: Claim of stronger spatial correlation in higher-accuracy networks is visually shown but lacks quantitative/statistical evidence.
- Suggestion: Conduct ablation studies to isolate contributions of L2 normalization, attention map types, and knowledge distillation.
- Suggestion: Provide computational cost analysis, particularly the extra backprop required by the gradient-based AT.
- Suggestion: Add more visualizations of attention maps across layers and datasets to show how AT affects student attention.
- Suggestion: Perform error analysis to identify which error types AT corrects and where it fails.
- Suggestion: Explore alternative loss functions for attention transfer (e.g., cosine similarity, perceptual loss).
- Question: What is the potential upper bound of performance improvement with AT and what prevents surpassing the teacher?
- Question: How does AT perform when student and teacher architectures are identical and what are the implications for model compression?
- Question: Can the authors provide a sensitivity analysis of β and its effect on learning dynamics and final performance?
- Question: Please provide teacher training and validation loss curves for Fig. 7b to complete the analysis.
- Question: Regarding spatial correlation claims, what quantitative metrics were considered and why were they not included?
