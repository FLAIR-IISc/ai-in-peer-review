- Proposal: Introduces attention transfer between teacher and student CNNs using activation- and gradient-based attention maps for knowledge transfer.
- Motivation: Leveraging attention as a transfer mechanism is well-motivated and aligns with interpretability and distillation research.
- Experimental results: Shows consistent improvements on CIFAR and modest positive gains on CUB and Scenes, but not dramatic.
- Decision: Reject — improvements are often marginal and experimental validation, especially on ImageNet, is insufficient for ICLR.
- ImageNet weakness: ImageNet experiments report only a 1.1% top-1 gain with limited hyperparameter tuning, which is not compelling given the cost of training.
- Knowledge distillation concern: Authors report failing to achieve positive results with KD on ImageNet, which is unexpected and requires further investigation.
- Overstated claim: The claim of being “the first knowledge transfer method to be successfully applied on ImageNet” is inaccurate given prior work.
- Comparison: Paper compares attention transfer to full activation transfer, showing benefits of attention but lacks deeper analysis of why it outperforms activation transfer.
- Need for ablations: More targeted ablation studies are needed to isolate contributions of attention mapping functions (Fsum, Fpsum, Fpmax) and loss placement across layers.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: The sensitivity of results to the β parameter in the loss (Eq. 2) should be analyzed and reported.
- Computational cost: The paper should discuss training/inference overhead introduced by attention transfer.
- Qualitative analysis: More qualitative visualizations of student vs teacher attention maps are needed to demonstrate alignment and interpretability.
- Related work: Expand related work to cover recent advances in knowledge distillation and attention visualization techniques.
- ImageNet details requested: Authors should elaborate why KD failed on ImageNet and what specific hyperparameter/troubleshooting steps were attempted.
- Open questions for authors: Why attention transfer outperforms full activation transfer; details on ImageNet KD attempts; β sensitivity; computational cost for deep nets; scenarios where attention transfer may fail.
