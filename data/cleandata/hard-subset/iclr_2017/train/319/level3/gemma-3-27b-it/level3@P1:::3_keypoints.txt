- Proposal: transfer knowledge by aligning teacher and student attention maps (activation- and gradient-based); code provided and experiments show consistent student improvements.
- Decision: Reject.
- Strength: well-motivated, clearly written, and empirically consistent improvements across datasets and architectures.
- Main concern: improvements are modest in magnitude and students still significantly underperform teachers.
- Concern: reported gains (e.g., ~1% on ImageNet) are not a substantial advance for distillation/student–teacher learning.
- Concern: paper lacks analysis of computational overhead and whether gains justify the cost of computing/matching attention maps.
- Recommendation: analyze performance vs efficiency trade-off and show speedup benefits of using a smaller attention-informed student instead of the teacher.
- Weakness: inadequate analysis and justification for when to use activation-based versus gradient-based attention maps.
- Weakness: missing sensitivity analysis of the regularization parameter β; the statement "β usually varies about 0.1" is vague and unjustified.
- Additional: quantify attention map correlation (e.g., IoU or correlation coefficients) instead of relying on visual inspection.
- Additional: include a direct, fair comparison to FitNets under identical experimental conditions.
- Additional: provide full training curves (loss and accuracy) for teacher and student to illustrate learning dynamics.
- Additional: perform an ablation study to isolate contributions of components (activation vs gradient attention, distillation, symmetry constraints).
- Additional: clarify and justify the horizontal flip invariance constraint on gradient attention maps.
- Additional: explicitly state all data augmentation methods used, especially for ImageNet experiments.
- Question: what is the computational overhead of computing and transferring attention maps, and impact on training/inference time?
- Question: detailed analysis of optimal β and its sensitivity across datasets and architectures?
- Question: how does attention transfer compare to FitNets under identical experimental conditions?
- Question: why use L2 normalization of attention maps, and were other normalization schemes tried?
- Question: does attention transfer help when teacher and student share the same architecture/capacity, and if so why?
