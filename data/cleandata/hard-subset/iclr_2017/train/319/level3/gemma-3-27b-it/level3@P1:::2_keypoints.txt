- Decision: Reject.
- Contribution: Proposes transferring spatial “attention” maps (activation- and gradient-based) from teacher to student to improve transfer learning.
- Main critique: Framing as “attention transfer” is misleading; method is essentially transferring statistical summaries of feature activations, not true task-driven attention.
- Organizational issue: Paper is disjoint and lacks clarity in places, weakening the presentation.
- Gradient-based method: Inclusion feels like an afterthought; benefits are not convincingly demonstrated and evaluation is limited.
- Implementation concern: Equation (2) uses a 2-norm whose computation is unclear and potentially computationally expensive (largest singular value vs. Frobenius norm).
- Scalability question: Unclear how the chosen norm/approximation affects runtime and scalability.
- Recommendation: Reframe/rename method to something like “feature map distillation” or “activation-based knowledge transfer.”
- Recommendation: Streamline focus to activation-based approach; present gradient-based method as preliminary or future work unless further justified.
- Ablation request: Provide thorough ablation studies showing contribution of each attention mapping function (Fsum, Fpsum, Fpmax).
- Baseline comparison: Expand comparisons to more recent/distinct knowledge distillation methods beyond FitNets.
- Hyperparameter analysis: Investigate sensitivity to β (Eq. 2) and other hyperparameters.
- Reproducibility: Improve code release with detailed README and example scripts.
- Questions for authors: Why call it “attention transfer” given the method’s nature?
- Questions for authors: What exact computation/approximation is used for the 2-norm and what is its complexity?
- Questions for authors: Why include gradient-based transfer without deeper evaluation; what challenges limited its exploration?
- Questions for authors: How is the second backpropagation implemented, especially with batch normalization?
- Questions for authors: Why does KD allegedly not work on ImageNet and what hyperparameter variations were tried?
