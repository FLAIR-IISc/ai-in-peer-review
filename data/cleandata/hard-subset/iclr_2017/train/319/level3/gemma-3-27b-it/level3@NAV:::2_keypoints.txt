- Paper proposes distilling summed feature-map statistics (attention transfer) as a novel knowledge-distillation framework.
- Empirical results show attention transfer outperforms FitNet-style distillation across several vision datasets.
- Paper is generally well-written with clear motivation for using attention as a transfer signal.
- Recommendation: Reject, with encouragement to revise and resubmit.
- Main conceptual issue: connection to standard “attention” is tenuous — summed activations differ from attention maps/mechanisms.
- Major limitation: method appears restricted to convolutional architectures and lacks discussion of adapting to transformers or other models.
- Gradient-based attention-transfer section feels disjoint and adds little value to the core contribution.
- Unclear math: computation of the 2-norm in Eq.(2) needs clarification — did the authors mean the Frobenius norm?
- Despite concerns, experiments consistently demonstrate performance improvements, warranting further investigation.
- Paper organization and notation require refinement for clarity.
- Questions for authors: justify use of the term “attention” or propose a better label; discuss extensions to non-CNNs; provide detailed complexity/clarity on Eq.(2); explain why gradient-based transfer underperforms and why it was included.
