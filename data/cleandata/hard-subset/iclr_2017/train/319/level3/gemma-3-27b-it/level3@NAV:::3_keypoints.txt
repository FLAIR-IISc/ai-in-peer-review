- Proposes Attention Transfer (AT) to improve student CNNs by mimicking teacher attention maps (activation- and gradient-based).  
- Shows consistent student improvements across CIFAR, CUB/Scene, and ImageNet; paper is well-written and logically motivated.  
- Decision: Reject — main concern is that students still underperform teachers despite AT, questioning AT’s effectiveness.  
- Lack of analysis on speed-up and trade-offs; need characterization of efficiency and experiments where student and teacher share the same architecture.  
- Unclear guidance on which attention map type to use; experiments don’t clarify pros/cons of activation vs. gradient attention.  
- Novelty is incremental (builds on FitNet); needs a more compelling demonstration of advantages over prior work.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (AT weight β) not analyzed; a sensitivity study is needed.  
- Figure 7b should include teacher training/validation loss for a proper comparison with AT and KD.  
- Claim that higher-accuracy networks have higher spatial correlation with attention maps (Fig. 4) lacks quantitative metrics; provide correlation measurements.  
- Ablation studies missing — e.g., contribution and necessity of l2 normalization are not evaluated.  
- Computational cost not discussed, especially the extra backprop in gradient-based AT; report overhead.  
- Unanswered question: why doesn’t AT allow students to surpass teachers — capacity limits, AT limitations, or other causes?
