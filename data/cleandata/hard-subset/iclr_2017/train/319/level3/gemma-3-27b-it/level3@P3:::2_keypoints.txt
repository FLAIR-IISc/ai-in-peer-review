- Decision: Reject with request for major revisions.
- Contribution: Proposes distillation by minimizing differences in summed feature-map statistics to improve student networks.
- Empirical result: Method outperforms FitNet-style distillation on multiple vision datasets.
- Writing: Paper is generally well-written with a clear motivation.
- Reproducibility: Code repository provided.
- Framing issue: Calling the method “attention transfer” is misleading—it distills feature statistics, not spatial attention maps.
- Disconnected component: The gradient-based attention transfer section feels tacked on and not integrated with the main contribution.
- Notation problems: Some symbols (e.g., QjS, QjT) and mathematical derivations are unclear.
- Norm ambiguity: Eq.(2) norm (l2/Frobenius/spectral) is unspecified and computational complexity is unclear.
- Organization: Experimental section is disjoint and subsections do not flow logically.
- Recommendation: Reframe terminology (e.g., “feature statistic transfer” or “representation distillation”).
- Recommendation: Remove or condense the gradient-based section unless directly compared or integrated.
- Recommendation: Explicitly state and justify the norm used in Eq.(2); correct equations if needed.
- Recommendation: Improve and consistently define notation throughout.
- Recommendation: Restructure experiments for a clearer narrative.
- Recommendation: Add detailed ablation studies (norm types, layer selection, etc.).
- Recommendation: Provide a thorough computational-cost analysis versus other distillation methods.
- Question: Clarify which norm is used in Eq.(2) and its computational complexity given QjS/QjT dimensions.
- Question: Explain why the gradient-based attention transfer was included and what benefit it provides; consider removing it if not central.
- Question: Justify layer selection for transfer (e.g., choice of last two residual blocks) and whether systematic layer-selection experiments were done.
- Question: Specify the schedule and rationale for decaying β during training.
