## Review of "Attention Transfer"

This paper proposes a novel knowledge distillation framework centered around minimizing differences in summed statistics across feature maps of teacher and student networks. The authors demonstrate empirically that their approach, focused on attention transfer, outperforms FitNet-style distillation methods across several computer vision datasets. The paper is generally well-written and presents a clear motivation for exploring attention as a means of knowledge transfer. 

I recommend **Reject** with encouragement to revise and resubmit. While the core idea and empirical results are promising, several issues hinder a strong evaluation and understanding of the proposed method. Primarily, the connection to "attention" as commonly understood in the field is tenuous. The method focuses on distilling summed activations – a useful signal, but distinct from transferring attention *maps* or mechanisms. 

The method’s limitation to convolutional architectures is a significant drawback. While acknowledged, the paper doesn't discuss potential adaptations to other architectures like transformers, where attention is fundamental. Additionally, the gradient-based attention transfer section feels disjointed and adds little value. Its inclusion doesn't enhance the core contribution and weakens the paper’s focus. Further, the computation of the 2-norm in Eq.(2) is unclear; the authors should clarify if they intended the Frobenius norm given the potential expense of computing the largest singular value.

Despite these concerns, the experimental results demonstrate a consistent performance boost with the proposed method, justifying further investigation. However, the paper’s organization and notation require refinement for clarity. 

**I have the following questions for the authors:**

*   Given the loose connection to the standard definition of “attention,” could you justify the continued use of this terminology or consider a more descriptive label for the transferred knowledge (e.g., “feature statistic transfer”)?
*   Have you explored potential extensions of this method to non-convolutional architectures, or investigated why it appears limited to CNNs?
*   Could you provide a more detailed explanation and computational complexity analysis of calculating the 2-norm in Eq.(2)? Was the Frobenius norm intended?
*   Why does the gradient-based transfer not improve performance as much as activation-based? Is there a reason the gradient-based section was included if it is not directly compared to the activation-based transfer?
