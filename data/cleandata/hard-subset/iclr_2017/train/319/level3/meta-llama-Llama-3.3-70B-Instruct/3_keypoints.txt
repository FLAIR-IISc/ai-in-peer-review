- Proposes Incremental Sequence Learning: train on initial few steps of sequences, gradually increase sequence length during training.
- Evaluated on MNIST pen stroke sequences and reports a 74% reduction in test error and a 20× training speedup.
- Shows effectiveness of the method for transfer learning to sequence classification.
- Decision: Accept — motivated, novel approach with thorough analysis and strong empirical gains.
- Paper is well-written with a comprehensive related-work review and clear motivation.
- Method is simple yet effective; authors analyze origins of performance gains and compare to multiple baselines.
- Experimental evaluation is rigorous, with thorough result analysis.
- Suggest providing more details on hyperparameter tuning, especially the threshold for increasing sequence length.
- Recommend including more examples of generated sequences and deeper analysis of sequence classification results.
- Advise comparing the approach to other state-of-the-art sequence methods (e.g., attention mechanisms, graph neural networks).
- Questions for authors: how was the threshold chosen and how sensitive are results to it; can more generated sequence examples and detailed classification analyses be provided; were comparisons to other SOTA methods attempted and what were the outcomes?
