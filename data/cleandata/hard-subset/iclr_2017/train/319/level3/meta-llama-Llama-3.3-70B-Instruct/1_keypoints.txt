- Proposal: Introduces Incremental Sequence Learning, gradually increasing sequence length during training.
- Empirical claim: Shows effectiveness on MNIST pen stroke sequences with a 74% reduction in test error and ~20× speedup.
- Decision: Paper rejected.
- Primary reason for rejection: Experimental results are not comprehensive enough to support the authors' claims.
- Secondary reason for rejection: Limited and unclear comparison to other curriculum learning methods.
- Supporting critique: Comparisons limited to only two other methods and results are not consistent across experiments.
- Supporting critique: Insufficient analysis of why the method improves performance.
- Supporting critique: Lack of discussion on potential applications and limitations.
- Suggested improvements: Conduct more comprehensive experiments and broader comparisons with other curriculum methods.
- Suggested improvements: Analyze sensitivity to hyperparameters and investigate origins of performance gains.
- Suggested improvements: Expand discussion of applications, limitations, and related work for better context.
- Questions for authors: How will you address experimental limitations and what additional experiments will you run?
- Questions for authors: Can you provide deeper analysis/insights into why the approach is effective?
- Questions for authors: How will the approach be extended to other sequence tasks and what applications do you foresee?
