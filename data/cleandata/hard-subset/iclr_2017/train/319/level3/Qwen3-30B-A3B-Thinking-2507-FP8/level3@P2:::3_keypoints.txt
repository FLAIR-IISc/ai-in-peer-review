- Proposes attention transfer between teacher and student networks using activation-based (sum/max of |features|^p) and gradient-based attention maps.  
- Shows consistent student performance improvements across CIFAR, CUB, Scene, and ImageNet, and across residual and non-residual architectures.  
- Activation-based attention transfer outperforms full-activation transfer and can be combined with knowledge distillation.  
- Decision: Weak Accept — solid empirical contribution but somewhat incremental and needing clarification.  
- Strength: Well-motivated, properly situated in literature, clear writing, and convincing multi-dataset/architecture experiments.  
- Weakness: Novelty is incremental relative to FitNets (extends hints to spatial maps rather than a fundamentally new idea).  
- Weakness: Claim that higher-accuracy nets have better spatial correlation between objects and attention maps relies on visuals, lacking quantitative evidence.  
- Weakness: No analysis of attention transfer when teacher and student share the same architecture, an important comparison since students still lag teachers.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameter selection (especially β) is unclear and not analyzed, hindering reproducibility and practical use.  
- Weakness: No principled guidance on choosing attention map types (sum vs. max, p values), despite empirical notes that sum-based works better.  
- Recommendation: Provide quantitative metrics for spatial correlation (e.g., correlation coefficients or segmentation overlap).  
- Recommendation: Include hyperparameter sensitivity analysis for β and guidelines based on architecture/dataset.  
- Recommendation: Clarify and report results for same-architecture teacher-student pairs and compare with standard knowledge distillation.  
- Recommendation: Extend experiments to compare knowledge distillation when teacher and student share architectures and include teacher training/validation loss in plots (e.g., Fig. 7b).  
- Questions for authors: How to quantify spatial correlation beyond visuals; sensitivity of performance to β; does attention transfer help for identical architectures and why; why activation-based AT outperforms full-activation transfer; why KD fails on ImageNet for different-architecture pairs but works on CIFAR?
