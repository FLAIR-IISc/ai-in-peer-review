- Introduces an attention-based knowledge distillation framework using activation- and gradient-based attention maps for teacher-to-student CNN transfer.  
- Shows empirical improvements across CIFAR, ImageNet, and fine-grained classification, claiming gains over FitNet-style baselines and compatibility with standard KD.  
- Provides code and models for reproducibility.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Limited applicability: method is explicitly tailored to convolutional architectures and lacks discussion of generalization to non-CNNs (e.g., transformers, RNNs).  
- Misleading terminology: calling the method "attention" conflicts with established meanings (e.g., self-attention), risking reader confusion.  
- Unclear computational details: computation of induced 2-norm (Eq. 2) is poorly explained and may be computationally expensive/scalability-prone.  
- Organizational and notational issues: unclear notation and structure make methodology and experiments hard to follow, especially the distinction between activation- and gradient-based attention.  
- Gradient-based attention feels disconnected from the main method and lacks strong justification or integration.  
- Suggest clarifying terminology (e.g., "feature map alignment" or "spatial focus transfer") to avoid confusion.  
- Suggest discussing or extending applicability beyond CNNs or explicitly justifying the CNN focus.  
- Suggest providing detailed computational cost analysis for Eq. (2) and mitigation strategies for large datasets.  
- Suggest reorganizing the paper to separate methodology, experimental setup, and results with consistent notation.  
- Questions for authors: how does computational cost compare to FitNet and other baselines on large datasets like ImageNet?  
- Questions for authors: can the method be adapted to non-convolutional architectures (transformers, GNNs), and what are the challenges?  
- Questions for authors: why choose the term "attention" despite potential confusion with self-attention?  
- Questions for authors: how does the method perform on tasks beyond classification, such as detection or segmentation?
