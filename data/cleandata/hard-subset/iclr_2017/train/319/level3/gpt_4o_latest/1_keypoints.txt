- Proposes attention-based knowledge transfer using activation- and gradient-based spatial attention maps.  
- Shows consistent, but modest, improvements on CIFAR and ImageNet and releases code/models.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main concern: experimental results are not sufficiently compelling for acceptance.  
- Limited improvement over baselines: only modest gains on CIFAR and about +1.1% top-1 on ImageNet.  
- ImageNet experiments are incomplete: no hyperparameter tuning and attention losses not applied to all residual groups.  
- Section 4.2.2 evaluation is underdeveloped, lacking detailed analysis and SOTA comparisons.  
- Claims of “significant improvement” are not well supported by the reported results.  
- Suggestion: expand and tune ImageNet experiments, applying attention transfer across all residual groups.  
- Suggestion: extend Section 4.2.2 with more detailed experiments and comparisons to other methods.  
- Suggestion: provide statistical significance testing or stronger comparative evidence for improvement claims.  
- Suggestion: evaluate on additional, diverse datasets (e.g., object detection, weakly-supervised localization).  
- Question: how does this method compare to other attention-based transfer techniques in compute cost and performance?  
- Question: why were ImageNet hyperparameters not tuned and how might that have affected results?  
- Question: can the authors explain why attention transfer outperforms full activation transfer in their experiments?
