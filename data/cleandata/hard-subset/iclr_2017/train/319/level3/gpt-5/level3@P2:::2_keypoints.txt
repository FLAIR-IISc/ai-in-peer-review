- Proposes Attention Transfer (AT): aggregate channel activations into normalized spatial attention maps and train students to match teacher attentions at selected layers, optionally with KD.  
- Presents a gradient-based AT variant that matches input-gradient “attentions” using double backprop.  
- Activation-based AT consistently improves student performance across datasets (CIFAR-10, CUB, MIT-Scenes, ImageNet) and architectures (ResNet, WRN, NiN).  
- AT outperforms FitNet-style full-activation matching and is complementary to knowledge distillation in several settings.  
- Paper is well-written, accessible, and releases code/models.  
- Decision: Accept — strong empirical gains, simple broadly applicable method, and notable ImageNet results where prior KD methods often fail.  
- Motivation: targets spatial “where” information as a robust, architecture-friendly transfer signal alternative to logits or full activations.  
- Literature placement: relates to KD, FitNets/hints, CAM/Grad-CAM; AT avoids adapters and brittleness of full-activation regression.  
- Experiments: CIFAR ablations cover teacher/student pairs and attention mappings; CUB/Scenes narrow ResNet-18 vs ResNet-34 gap; ImageNet shows non-trivial top-1/top-5 gains.  
- Ablations: useful analysis of which layers to transfer and attention mapping choices (sum vs max, p); gradient-based AT yields modest, limited results.  
- Caveat: “attention” terminology is misleading — main signal is spatial activation aggregation, not an explicit or class-discriminative attention mechanism.  
- Caveat: gradient-based AT feels orthogonal, isn’t combined with activation-based AT, and adds limited value in current form.  
- Caveat: some notation and implementation details are unclear and need clarification.  
- Suggestion: Clarify Eq. (2) to state ||·||2 is l2 of vectorized map (Frobenius norm), not spectral norm.  
- Suggestion: Tighten terminology (e.g., “spatial activation aggregation (attention) maps”) and explicitly differentiate from CAM/Grad-CAM.  
- Suggestion: Reorganize to foreground activation-based AT; move gradient-based section to appendix or strengthen empirical story and combinations.  
- Suggestion: Provide hyperparameter guidance (β scaling/schedules, choice of p, layer selection, l2 vs l1) and a small sensitivity analysis for reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: Expand ImageNet experiments (apply AT to all residual groups, report compute/wall-clock overhead, compare KD with tuned hyperparameters and same-architecture pairs).  
- Suggestion: Discuss limitations—method is tied to convolutional spatial maps and needs commentary on adaptation to non-convolutional/sequence tasks.  
- Editorial: Define notation (I, interpolation for mismatched resolutions), state whether teacher is frozen, and polish presentation.  
- Questions for authors: confirm norm interpretation and l1 usage; sensitivity to layer selection and p; how β was chosen/scheduled; training overhead and caching teacher attentions; KD interactions and same-architecture KD behavior; BN handling and combinations for gradient-based AT; tests on detection/segmentation and capacity-gap failure modes; quantify attention alignment vs accuracy gains.
