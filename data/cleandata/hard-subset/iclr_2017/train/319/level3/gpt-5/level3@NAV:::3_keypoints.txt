- Proposal: Introduces Attention Transfer (AT) to train students to match teacher attention maps while optimizing classification loss, with activation-based (|A|^p sum/max) and gradient-based attention and an L2 loss on normalized maps.
- Empirical claim: AT yields consistent accuracy improvements across CIFAR-10/100, CUB, MIT Scenes, and ImageNet, often complementing KD and beating full-activation regression; code is provided.
- Decision: Reviewer recommends rejection due to limited novelty over FitNets/KD-style hints and insufficient analysis.
- Strength: Paper is clearly written, well-motivated, and shows consistent empirical gains including ImageNet.
- Novelty concern: AT is viewed as incremental relative to intermediate-hint regression (FitNets); novelty mainly in choice of reduced statistics and normalized L2 loss.
- Lack of quantitative support: Qualitative claims (e.g., higher-accuracy networks have higher object-attention correlation) lack measured evidence.
- Hyperparameter issues: Sensitivity to AT weight β, exponent p, attention type (sum vs max, activation vs gradient), normalization, and loss placement is under-explored.
- Gradient-AT costs: Double backprop required for gradient-based AT; compute, memory overhead, and stability (especially with batch norm) are not characterized.
- Teacher/student gap: Students never surpass teachers in reported experiments; inference speed/latency and practical speedups from smaller students are not reported.
- Suggested analyses: Quantify spatial correlation with metrics (IoU, pointing game, CAM/Grad-CAM) and correlate with accuracy.
- Suggested experiments: Systematically sweep β, p, attention type, and AT placement; provide guidance on when to use activation vs gradient AT.
- Reporting request: Show teacher training/validation losses and student/teacher learning curves for fairness.
- Efficiency reporting: Report training overhead, memory footprint, wall-clock training time, inference latency, parameter and FLOP reductions.
- Additional analysis: Study same-architecture student/teacher and cross-architecture transfer cases.
- KD interactions: Clarify how AT interacts with KD on ImageNet and include broader hyperparameter sweeps.
