- Proposes attention transfer (activation- and gradient-based) to transfer spatial attention maps from teacher to student, with mapping functions, normalization, multi-layer losses; shows results on CIFAR, transfer learning (CUB/Scenes), and initial ImageNet; code released.

- Decision: Reject (borderline) — main reasons: empirical gains marginal/inconsistent and ImageNet evidence too limited.

- Evidence that AT outperforms KD is not robust: CIFAR gains over KD are small and ImageNet KD may be under-tuned, leaving AT’s advantage unclear.

- ImageNet evaluation is narrow: single R34→R18 pair, two AT losses, no hyperparameter tuning, and only ~1.1% top-1 improvement without comparisons to tuned KD, FitNets, or AT+KD.

- Gradient-based AT only tested on CIFAR under constrained settings; scalability, extra computational cost (double backprop), and BN stability are not quantified.

- Claim of being “first” successful knowledge transfer on ImageNet is insufficiently supported.

- Requested experiment improvements: expand Section 4.2.2 with multiple teacher–student pairs (e.g., R50→R18/34), cross-architecture transfers, and thorough KD baselines (temperature, alpha, label smoothing).

- Test AT+KD synergy and include FitNets-style baselines for fair comparison.

- Provide statistical significance (mean±std over ≥3 seeds), matched compute budgets, and report wall-clock/compute overhead for AT and gradient-based AT.

- Run deeper ablations: attention mapping choices (p, sum vs max), normalization, layer placement, β schedules, and sensitivity to spatial resolution.

- Clarify theoretical intuition for why attention maps outperform full activations; analyze failure cases and where AT helps most (fine-grained vs generic, depth/width mismatches).

- Question: can authors provide tuned KD baselines on ImageNet (temperature, α, label smoothing) and compare to AT and AT+KD under matched training budgets?

- Question: how do results change when applying AT losses to all residual groups on ImageNet and for cross-architecture transfers (e.g., R50→MobileNet)?

- Question: what are the training-time and memory overheads of activation-based AT and gradient-based AT (double backprop) on large-scale models?

- Question: how sensitive are results to attention map normalization and the choice of p in Fpsum, and is there qualitative/quantitative evidence that student attentions align better with teacher on ImageNet?

- Question: please report variability across seeds and provide error bars for CIFAR and ImageNet.
