- Proposes "attention transfer" (AT): distill teacher‚Üístudent by matching spatial attention maps (channel-wise statistics over absolute activations) added as an L2 loss to classification, combinable with KD.  
- Introduces a gradient-based AT variant using input gradients (double backprop) exploring gradient-norm regularization, flip-equivariance, and student‚Äìteacher gradient matching.  
- Extensive experiments on CIFAR-10/100, CUB, MIT Scenes, ImageNet and multiple architectures show consistent gains; AT outperforms FitNets-style full activation transfer and improves ResNet-18 top-1 on ImageNet.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Simple, well-motivated method providing consistent, non-trivial gains across multiple settings, including ImageNet.  
- Key reason 2: Empirical evidence supports that matching spatial activation statistics is an effective distillation signal with minimal overhead.  
- Problem significance: Targets an important question‚Äîcan students learn where teachers ‚Äúlook‚Äù‚Äîlinking interpretability and practical distillation.

- Evidence & rigor: Strong CIFAR ablations, layer-placement and mapping-function analyses, KD synergy tests, and ImageNet evaluations with convergence curves.  
- Transparency: Paper acknowledges weaker/mixed results for gradient-based AT and implementation constraints.
