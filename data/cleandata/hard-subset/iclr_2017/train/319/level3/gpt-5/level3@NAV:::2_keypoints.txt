- Proposes distilling spatial attention via normalized summed activation maps across channels to transfer knowledge from teacher to student CNNs.  
- Method consistently improves student accuracy across CIFAR-10, CUB, MIT Indoor, and ImageNet, often outperforming FitNet-style full-activation matching.  
- Complementary to standard knowledge distillation (KD) and easy to implement with minimal computational overhead.  
- Decision: Weak Accept — idea is simple, well-motivated, and empirically strong, including on ImageNet.  
- Targets transferring spatial focus/energy of feature maps, arguing these maps capture discriminative regions better than full activation regression.  
- Empirical rigor: consistent gains over competitive baselines, ablations over statistic choices (p-sum vs max), multi-depth losses, and ImageNet scalability.  
- Results suggest superiority to FitNet-style matching and partial additivity with KD in some settings.  
- Limitation: approach is inherently tied to convolutional feature maps and not evaluated beyond CNNs.  
- Terminology concern: calling summed activation maps “attention” is potentially misleading compared to learned attention mechanisms.  
- Gradient-based transfer section feels orthogonal to main contribution and is not well-integrated or thoroughly compared.  
- Request to clarify Eq. (2) notation—specify whether ‖Q‖2 is vector l2 norm after flattening or the matrix spectral norm and unify norm notation.  
- Suggest renaming/defining “attention” (e.g., “spatial activation energy”) to avoid confusion.  
- Recommend integrating gradient-based transfer with main method or moving it to appendix to keep focus.  
- Ask authors to report wall-clock time and memory overheads, and sensitivity to p, β, layer choices, and normalization.  
- Request stronger FitNet/KD baselines with hyperparameter tuning, multiple seeds, and significance/variance reporting.  
- Question 1: Clarify whether ‖Q‖2 in Eq. (2) is the vector l2 norm or the matrix spectral norm.  
- Question 2: What are the wall-clock and memory overheads of AT per batch, with and without teacher forward pass?  
- Question 3: How sensitive are results to p, β, and the chosen layers; any practitioner guidance?  
- Question 4: Can AT be combined with gradient-based AT for further gains; if not, why?  
- Question 5: How does AT perform on non-residual CNNs, transformers, or MLP-Mixers (via token-wise maps)?  
- Question 6: Are improvements robust across multiple seeds on larger datasets (CUB/Scenes/ImageNet)?  
- Question 7: How does AT compare to using CAM/Grad-CAM-derived maps as the distillation target?
