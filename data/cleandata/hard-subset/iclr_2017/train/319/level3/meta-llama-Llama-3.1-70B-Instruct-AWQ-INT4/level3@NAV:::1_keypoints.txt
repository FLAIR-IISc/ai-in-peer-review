- Proposes attention transfer for CNNs by mimicking teacher attention maps to improve student performance.  
- Defines two spatial attention map types: activation-based and gradient-based, and several transfer methods.  
- Shows consistent improvements across CIFAR and other small datasets and various CNN architectures.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Primary reason: ImageNet experiments are not extensive enough to validate effectiveness.  
- Secondary reason: Improvements over standard knowledge distillation are marginal.  
- Supporting critique: ImageNet evaluation uses only a single student–teacher pair, lacking thorough benchmarking.  
- Suggestion: Run extensive ImageNet experiments with multiple student–teacher pairs and deeper evaluation.  
- Suggestion: Analyze why gains are marginal and perform sensitivity analysis on hyperparameters.  
- Suggestion: Provide more attention-map visualizations to clarify method behavior.  
- Questions for authors: detail student/teacher architectures and implementation, explain hyperparameter selection and sensitivity, and clarify why this outperforms standard knowledge distillation.
