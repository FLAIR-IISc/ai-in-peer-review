**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper proposes a novel method for transferring knowledge from a powerful teacher network to a smaller student network, leveraging the concept of attention. The authors introduce two types of spatial attention maps, activation-based and gradient-based, and demonstrate their effectiveness in improving the performance of the student network. They also show that their approach outperforms existing knowledge distillation methods, such as FitNets, on multiple computer vision datasets. The paper's contributions include: (1) proposing attention as a mechanism for transferring knowledge between networks, (2) introducing activation-based and gradient-based spatial attention maps, and (3) demonstrating significant improvements in performance across various datasets and architectures.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

Based on the review, I decide to **Accept** the paper with minor revisions. The key reasons for this decision are:

1. The paper proposes a novel and well-motivated approach to knowledge distillation, leveraging the concept of attention.
2. The authors provide extensive experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach on multiple datasets and architectures.

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The experimental results are thorough and demonstrate significant improvements in performance. However, there are some minor issues that need to be addressed:

* The terminology "attention" is somewhat misleading, as the method simply distills summed (squared or lp-norm) activations, not attention maps.
* The gradient-based attention-transfer section feels out-of-place and is not directly compared with or combined with the main attention-based transfer.
* The notation is unclear in some places, and the authors should provide more details on the computation of the induced 2-norm in Eq.(2).

**Additional Feedback**

To improve the paper, the authors should:

* Clarify the terminology and notation throughout the paper.
* Provide more details on the computation of the induced 2-norm in Eq.(2).
* Consider combining the gradient-based attention-transfer section with the main attention-based transfer or providing a clearer justification for its inclusion.
* Provide more analysis on the limitations of the method, such as its applicability to non-convolutional architectures.
