- Proposes a novel attention-based knowledge transfer method for CNNs using activation- and gradient-based spatial attention maps.
- Introduces several new mechanisms to transfer teacher attention to a student network to improve student performance.
- Shows consistent empirical improvements across multiple datasets and both residual and non-residual architectures.
- Method is well-motivated by connections to human visual attention and prior attention work.
- Provides detailed definitions of attention mapping functions and the attention transfer process.
- Includes comprehensive evaluations and comparisons with standard knowledge distillation methods.
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and contains a thorough related-work review.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty, motivation, and convincing experimental results.
- Suggests more extensive ImageNet experiments to further strengthen empirical claims.
- Recommends additional visualizations of attention maps and the transfer process for clarity.
- Requests more discussion of limitations and potential future research directions.
- Asks authors to clarify how attention mapping functions are chosen and why some are more effective.
- Asks how the method handles teacher–student architecture or depth mismatches and its effectiveness there.
- Requests analysis of the computational cost of the proposed method versus standard knowledge distillation.
