- Proposes a novel knowledge-distillation method using two types of spatial attention maps (activation-based and gradient-based) to transfer teacher to student and shows performance gains on CNNs across datasets and architectures.

- Recommendation: accept with reservations; method is well-motivated, literature-grounded, clearly explained, and empirically effective.

- Terminology concern: calling summed (squared or lp-norm) activations “attention” is misleading and may confuse readers familiar with attention mechanisms.

- Gradient-based attention-transfer section is out-of-place, not directly compared or combined with the main method, and adds little value; consider removing or shortening it.

- Notation issues: unclear definitions (e.g., reusing symbol “F” for both activation- and gradient-based mapping functions) can cause confusion.

- Insufficient analysis of results: lacks detailed evaluation of computational cost and sensitivity to hyperparameters.

- Suggested improvements: clarify terminology and notation throughout the paper.

- Suggested improvements: remove/shorten the gradient-based section or better integrate/compare it with the main approach.

- Suggested improvements: provide deeper result analysis including computational cost, hyperparameter tuning effects, and more visualizations of the attention maps.

- Questions for authors: how is the induced 2-norm in Eq.(2) computed and what is its computational cost?

- Questions for authors: how can the method be extended to non-convolutional architectures?

- Questions for authors: why was the gradient-based attention-transfer included and how does it relate to the activation-based transfer?
