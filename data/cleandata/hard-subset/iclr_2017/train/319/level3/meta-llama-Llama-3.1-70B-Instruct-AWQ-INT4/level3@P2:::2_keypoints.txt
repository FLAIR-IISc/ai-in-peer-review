- Proposes attention transfer: training a student CNN to mimic teacher spatial attention maps (activation- and gradient-based) to improve performance.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR, ImageNet, and fine-grained tasks, showing consistent improvements over baselines.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address organization and unclear notation.  
- Strengths: well-motivated, novel method, clear writing, thorough analysis with visualizations and ablations.  
- Weakness: gradient-based attention-transfer section feels out of place and not well integrated or compared with the main method.  
- Notation issue: Eq.(2) is unclear—likely intended Frobenius norm rather than 2-norm.  
- Limitation: method appears limited to convolutional architectures; “attention” term may be misleading (distills summed/powered activations).  
- Request to clarify computational cost and scalability to larger datasets.  
- Suggest reorganizing the paper to integrate gradient-based transfer with the main approach and fix unclear notation.  
- Recommend discussing limitations and future directions explicitly.  
- Questions for authors: provide computational cost/scalability details, extension plans to RNNs/transformers, and how attention transfer relates to/combines with knowledge distillation.
