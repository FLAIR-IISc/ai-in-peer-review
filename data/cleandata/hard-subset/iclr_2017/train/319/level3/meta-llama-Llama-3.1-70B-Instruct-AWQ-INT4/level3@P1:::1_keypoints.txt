- Proposes attention-based knowledge transfer between networks using activation- and gradient-based attention maps.  
- Claims attention maps contain valuable information that improves student network performance.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on CIFAR, ImageNet, and fine-grained recognition tasks with experimental results.  
- Main contribution: introducing attention as a mechanism for transferring knowledge between teacher and student.  
- Shows attention transfer yields significant accuracy improvements, especially when combined with knowledge distillation.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, with good figures, tables, and related-work positioning.  
- Experimental evaluation on ImageNet is insufficient; needs more extensive experiments across architectures, depths, and hyperparameters.  
- Requires a more detailed comparison with standard knowledge distillation methods to clarify benefits and trade-offs.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Reasons for decision: novel approach, supporting experimental evidence, and clear presentation.  
- Supporting arguments: current results are promising but more experiments and comparisons are needed.  
- Suggested additions: more results on varied architectures/depths/hyperparameters and richer visualizations of attention transfer.  
- Ask authors to provide implementation details (architectures, hyperparameters) and plans to address limitations like teacher strength and overfitting.  
- Request more insights into the relationship between attention transfer and knowledge distillation, including benefits and trade-offs.
