**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel method for transferring knowledge from a powerful teacher network to a smaller student network, leveraging the concept of attention. The authors introduce two types of spatial attention maps: activation-based and gradient-based, which are used to transfer attention information from the teacher to the student. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach, showing consistent improvements across various datasets and convolutional neural network architectures. The authors also explore the combination of attention transfer with knowledge distillation, achieving better performance than standard knowledge distillation.

**Decision**

I recommend accepting this paper, with the condition that the authors extend and strengthen the experimental results on ImageNet.

**Reasons for the decision**

The paper proposes a well-motivated and innovative approach to knowledge transfer, which is a crucial aspect of deep learning. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the concept of attention and its application to convolutional neural networks. The experimental results on CIFAR, Scenes, and CUB datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, the results on ImageNet are limited and require further investigation.

**Supporting arguments**

The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the proposed method and its theoretical foundations. The authors provide a comprehensive review of related work, highlighting the novelty of their approach. The experimental results are thorough and well-presented, with a clear comparison to baseline methods.

However, the results on ImageNet are limited to a single experiment, and the authors acknowledge that they did not have time to tune hyperparameters. This raises concerns about the robustness of the results and the potential for overfitting. Furthermore, the comparison to knowledge distillation on ImageNet is limited, and the authors should provide more extensive experiments to demonstrate the superiority of their approach.

**Additional feedback**

To strengthen the paper, the authors should:

* Extend the experimental results on ImageNet, including a more thorough hyperparameter search and a comparison to knowledge distillation.
* Provide more visualizations of the attention maps and their impact on the student network's performance.
* Investigate the application of attention transfer to other tasks, such as object detection and weakly-supervised localization.
* Consider providing more theoretical analysis of the proposed method, including a discussion of its limitations and potential pitfalls.

**Questions for the authors**

* Can you provide more details on the implementation of the attention transfer method, including the specific architectures and hyperparameters used?
* How do you plan to address the potential issue of overfitting in the attention transfer method?
* Can you provide more insights into the visualization of attention maps and their impact on the student network's performance?
* Have you considered applying the attention transfer method to other tasks, such as object detection and weakly-supervised localization?
