- Proposes attention transfer method to distill teacher-to-student using activation- and gradient-based spatial attention maps.  
- Demonstrates consistent performance gains across CIFAR, Scenes, CUB and multiple CNN architectures.  
- Shows combining attention transfer with knowledge distillation improves over standard distillation.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance conditional on strengthening ImageNet experiments.  
- Method is well-motivated, clearly explained, and grounded in attention analysis for CNNs.  
- Experimental presentation is thorough with clear baselines and comparisons.  
- ImageNet results are limited (single experiment, no hyperparameter tuning), raising robustness concerns.  
- Limited comparison to knowledge distillation on ImageNet; more extensive experiments needed.  
- Suggests extending ImageNet experiments with thorough hyperparameter search and KD comparisons.  
- Requests more visualizations of attention maps and their effect on student performance.  
- Recommends exploring attention transfer for other tasks (object detection, weakly-supervised localization).  
- Suggests adding more theoretical analysis, limitations, and potential pitfalls of the method.  
- Asks for implementation details: architectures, hyperparameters, and training setup.  
- Requests clarification on handling potential overfitting and deeper insights from attention visualizations.
