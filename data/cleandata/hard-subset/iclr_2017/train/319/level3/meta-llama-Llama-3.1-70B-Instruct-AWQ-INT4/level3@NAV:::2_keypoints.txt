- Proposes attention-based knowledge transfer from teacher to student using activation- and gradient-based spatial attention maps.  
- Claims activation- and gradient-based attention maps improve student network performance over prior distillation methods like FitNets.  
- Contributions: (1) attention as a transfer mechanism, (2) activation- and gradient-based spatial attention maps, (3) demonstrated improvements across datasets and architectures.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Key reason: Novel, well-motivated approach to knowledge distillation.  
- Key reason: Extensive experiments showing effectiveness on multiple datasets and architectures.  
- Writing is clear and concise overall, with thorough experimental results.  
- Terminology concern: calling summed activations "attention" is misleading.  
- Structural concern: gradient-based attention-transfer section feels out-of-place and not integrated or compared to main method.  
- Notation concern: some notation is unclear, especially computation of the induced 2-norm in Eq.(2).  
- Requested fixes: clarify terminology and notation throughout the paper.  
- Requested fixes: provide detailed computation of the induced 2-norm in Eq.(2).  
- Requested fixes: either combine or better justify the inclusion of gradient-based attention-transfer with the main method.  
- Requested analysis: discuss limitations, applicability to non-convolutional architectures, sensitivity to hyperparameters, and applicability to different tasks.  
- Questions for authors: give details on induced 2-norm computation, plans for extending to non-convolutional architectures, and analysis of method limitations and hyperparameter sensitivity.
