**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a new knowledge transfer method that leverages attention maps to improve the performance of a student convolutional neural network (CNN) by mimicking the attention maps of a powerful teacher network. The authors define two types of spatial attention maps: activation-based and gradient-based, and propose several novel methods for transferring attention from the teacher to the student network. The experimental results show that the proposed attention transfer method provides consistent improvements across various datasets and CNN architectures, including residual and non-residual networks.

**Decision**

Based on the review, I would like to **Reject** the paper with the following key reasons:

1. The experiments on ImageNet are not extensive enough to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
2. The improvements over standard knowledge distillation are only marginal.

**Supporting Arguments**

While the paper proposes a novel approach to knowledge transfer, the experimental results are not convincing enough to demonstrate its effectiveness. The results on CIFAR and other smaller datasets are promising, but the experiments on ImageNet, which is a more challenging and widely-used benchmark, are limited. The authors only report results on a single student-teacher pair and do not provide a thorough evaluation of the proposed method on ImageNet.

Additionally, the improvements over standard knowledge distillation are only marginal, which raises questions about the significance of the proposed method. The authors should provide more extensive experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.

**Additional Feedback**

To strengthen the paper, the authors should:

* Provide more extensive experiments on ImageNet, including multiple student-teacher pairs and a thorough evaluation of the proposed method.
* Investigate the reasons behind the marginal improvements over standard knowledge distillation and provide a more detailed analysis of the results.
* Consider providing more visualizations of the attention maps to help understand the behavior of the proposed method.

**Questions to the Authors**

* Can you provide more details on the implementation of the proposed method, including the architecture of the student and teacher networks?
* How did you select the hyperparameters for the proposed method, and did you perform any sensitivity analysis to evaluate their impact on the results?
* Can you provide more insights on why the proposed method is more effective than standard knowledge distillation, and what are the key factors that contribute to its success?
