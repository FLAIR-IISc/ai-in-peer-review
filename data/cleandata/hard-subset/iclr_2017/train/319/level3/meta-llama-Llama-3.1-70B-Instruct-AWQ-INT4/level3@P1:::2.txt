**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

This paper proposes a novel knowledge transfer method, dubbed "attention transfer," which aims to improve the performance of a student convolutional neural network (CNN) by forcing it to mimic the attention maps of a powerful teacher network. The authors define attention as a set of spatial maps that encode the importance of different spatial areas of the input for the network's output decision. They propose two types of spatial attention maps: activation-based and gradient-based. The experimental results show that attention transfer provides significant improvements across various datasets and CNN architectures. The authors also demonstrate that combining attention transfer with knowledge distillation leads to further performance gains.

**Decision: Accept with Reservations**

While the paper presents a well-performing method and provides valuable insights into the importance of attention in CNNs, there are some organizational issues and unclear notation that need to be addressed. Specifically, the gradient-based attention-transfer section feels out-of-place and is not directly compared with or combined with the main attention-based transfer method. Additionally, the notation in Eq. (2) is unclear, and the authors may have meant to use the Frobenius norm instead of the 2-norm.

**Supporting Arguments for the Decision**

1. **Method performance**: The experimental results demonstrate that attention transfer provides significant improvements across various datasets and CNN architectures. This suggests that the method is effective and can be useful in practice.
2. **Novelty**: The paper proposes a novel knowledge transfer method that is different from existing methods, such as FitNet-style distillation. This novelty is a strength of the paper.
3. **Insights into attention**: The paper provides valuable insights into the importance of attention in CNNs and how attention can be used to improve performance.

**Additional Feedback to Improve the Paper**

1. **Reorganize the paper**: Consider reorganizing the paper to better integrate the gradient-based attention-transfer section with the rest of the paper.
2. **Clarify notation**: Clarify the notation in Eq. (2) and specify whether the 2-norm or Frobenius norm is used.
3. **Provide more comparisons**: Provide more comparisons between the proposed attention transfer method and existing knowledge transfer methods, such as FitNet-style distillation.
4. **Discuss limitations**: Discuss the limitations of the method, such as its applicability to non-convolutional architectures.

**Questions for the Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how the gradient-based attention-transfer method is related to the main attention-based transfer method?
2. How did you choose the value of the hyperparameter β in Eq. (2)?
3. Can you provide more insights into why attention transfer is more effective than FitNet-style distillation?
4. Have you considered applying the attention transfer method to other domains, such as natural language processing?
