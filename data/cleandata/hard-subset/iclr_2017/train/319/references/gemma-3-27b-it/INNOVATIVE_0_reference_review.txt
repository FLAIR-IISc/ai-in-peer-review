This paper explores knowledge transfer between convolutional neural networks via attention maps, proposing to use both activation-based and gradient-based attention as a means of improving student network performance. The authors demonstrate improvements across several datasets and architectures, highlighting the benefits of attention transfer over full activation transfer and its compatibility with knowledge distillation.

**Reject.** While the idea of leveraging attention maps for knowledge transfer is interesting, the paper feels somewhat exploratory and lacks the depth of analysis needed for acceptance at ICLR. The improvements reported, while consistent, are not transformative, and the experimental validation feels somewhat superficial, especially concerning the gradient-based attention transfer. The paper presents a collection of observations rather than a deeply investigated phenomenon.

The primary concern lies in the breadth of experiments without sufficient depth. The authors try a lot of different things (different attention mapping functions, combining with knowledge distillation, gradient-based attention, symmetry constraints) but don’t fully dissect *why* certain methods work and others don’t. For example, the observation that activation-based attention transfer outperforms full activation transfer is presented, but the underlying reason is not explored rigorously. Is it because attention maps are more compact representations, focusing on relevant features? Is it related to the sparsity induced by the attention mechanism?  Similarly, the gradient-based attention results are less convincing, and the authors acknowledge the complexity of applying it with batch normalization, raising concerns about the validity of those findings.

The paper also lacks a clear theoretical justification for why attention transfer should work. While the authors draw inspiration from human visual attention, a more formal connection to learning theory or information bottleneck principles would strengthen the argument. The use of l2 normalization on attention maps is mentioned as important for success, but the rationale behind this choice is not clearly explained.

The writing quality is acceptable but could be improved.  The numerous citations feel somewhat overwhelming and detract from the flow of the text. Figures are referenced frequently but aren’t always immediately clear without referring back to the text.

**Additional Feedback (for potential improvement):**

*   **Deeper Analysis:**  Investigate *why* attention transfer works. Conduct ablation studies to determine the importance of different components (e.g., different attention mapping functions, different layers for transfer).
*   **Theoretical Grounding:**  Provide a more theoretical justification for the proposed approach.  Connect it to existing principles in learning theory or information theory.
*   **Gradient-Based Attention:** Either rigorously address the challenges of gradient-based attention with batch normalization or significantly downplay the results.
*   **Error Bars/Statistical Significance:** Provide error bars and statistical significance tests for the reported results.
*   **Visualization:** More compelling visualizations of the attention maps themselves would be helpful, particularly showing how they align with relevant features in the input images.
*   **Related Work:** While the related work section is extensive, it is mostly descriptive. A more critical analysis of existing work and a clearer positioning of the current paper would be beneficial.

**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  What is the computational overhead of calculating and transferring attention maps compared to other knowledge distillation techniques?
2.  You mention that attention maps contain valuable information. Have you explored using these attention maps for other downstream tasks, such as interpretability or data augmentation?
3.  Why do you think the results with KD on ImageNet are negative, while the activation transfer still gives an improvement?
4.  Could you provide a more intuitive explanation of why l2 normalization of the attention maps is crucial for the success of the transfer?
5.  What experiments were performed to determine the optimal value of the beta parameter in Equation 2?
