This paper investigates attention transfer from a teacher to a student convolutional neural network, proposing both activation-based and gradient-based attention maps for this purpose. The authors demonstrate improvements in performance across various datasets and network architectures, suggesting that attention information can be a valuable tool for knowledge transfer. They further explore combining attention transfer with knowledge distillation.

**Reject.** While the idea of attention transfer is intriguing and the experimental results show some improvements, the paper suffers from a multitude of minor flaws, inconsistencies, and a lack of rigor in the experimental setup and analysis. These issues, collectively, overshadow the potential contributions of the work.

The paper is riddled with inconsistencies and a lack of attention to detail. For example, the phrasing throughout is often awkward and repetitive ("attention maps contain valuable information that can be used for significantly improving performance" – tautological). The presentation of equations and figure references is inconsistent (e.g., Fig. 1 is mentioned but not crucial to understanding the core concepts, while Fig. 3 and 4 seem critical but are only briefly referenced). The implementation details are sparse, making reproducibility challenging. Specifically, the hyperparameter settings (particularly β) are described vaguely, and the choice of normalization techniques (ZCA whitening, batch normalization) seems arbitrary and not thoroughly justified. The claim of being the “first knowledge transfer method to be successfully applied on ImageNet” is unsubstantiated and likely incorrect given the extensive literature on knowledge distillation.

The experimental validation feels incomplete. The CIFAR experiments are presented with numerous tables but lack a clear overall narrative. The performance gains are not always substantial, and the ablation studies (e.g., comparing different attention mapping functions) are not always conclusive. The ImageNet experiments, while promising, are hampered by the lack of hyperparameter tuning and a clear explanation of why KD failed. The justification for choosing specific network architectures (NIN, ResNet) and their configurations is weak. Why were certain architectures only used for gradient-based attention transfer? The results from table 3 are presented without adequate explanation; what does the "min-l2" refer to, and how does it relate to the proposed gradient-based attention transfer? The claim of horizontal flip invariance being a regularization effect needs stronger justification and analysis. Figures 6 and 7 (mentioned in the appendix) are vital to understanding the qualitative aspects of the attention transfer and should be included in the main body of the paper.

The related work section is descriptive but lacks critical analysis. It simply lists existing methods without highlighting their limitations or how the current work builds upon them. The connection to the broader field of knowledge transfer and representation learning is superficial.

**Additional Feedback (for potential revision, should the authors choose to address the concerns):**

*   **Clarity and Conciseness:** Rewrite the paper to improve clarity and conciseness. Eliminate redundant phrases and ensure consistent terminology.
*   **Rigor in Experiments:** Provide detailed experimental settings, including all hyperparameters, optimization algorithms, and data augmentation techniques. Report standard deviations and confidence intervals. Conduct more thorough ablation studies to validate the effectiveness of each component of the proposed approach.
*   **Reproducibility:** Release the code and trained models to facilitate reproducibility.
*   **Comparative Analysis:** Compare the proposed approach with state-of-the-art knowledge transfer methods more comprehensively, including a detailed discussion of their strengths and weaknesses.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** Provide a more theoretical justification for the effectiveness of attention transfer. Why does transferring attention lead to improved performance?
*   **Justification of Architectural Choices:** Explain the rationale behind choosing specific network architectures and their configurations.

**Questions for the Authors:**

*   What is the computational overhead of implementing the proposed attention transfer methods, particularly for gradient-based attention transfer with the second backpropagation step?
*   How sensitive is the performance to the choice of the β parameter in the loss function? Did you perform a systematic hyperparameter search to optimize its value?
*   Could you elaborate on why knowledge distillation failed to improve performance on ImageNet, and what steps were taken to address this issue?
*   What are the limitations of the proposed approach, and what are potential directions for future research?
*   Can you provide more insight into the choice of the norm type 'p' in the loss function? Did you explore other norm types or values, and if so, what were the results?
