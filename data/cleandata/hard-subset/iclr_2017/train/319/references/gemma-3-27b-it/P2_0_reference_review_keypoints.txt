- Paper investigates transferring attention maps (activation- and gradient-based spatial maps) from teacher to student CNNs to improve performance.
- Decision: Accept (with minor revisions).
- Novelty: Attention-map transfer is a novel, relevant form of knowledge transfer; exploring activation vs. gradient definitions is original.
- Empirical strength: Consistent performance improvements across CIFAR, CUB, Scenes, and ImageNet; activation-based attention transfer outperforms full activation transfer.
- Combination with KD: Attention transfer can be effectively combined with knowledge distillation.
- Clear definitions: Activation- and gradient-based attention maps and mapping functions (sum, max, power) are well defined and explored.
- Thorough experimentation: Results cover diverse datasets, architectures, and same-depth vs different-depth teacher-student setups.
- Ablation studies: CIFAR ablations (Tables 1 & 2) convincingly show benefits and effects of different mapping functions.
- ImageNet: Preliminary ImageNet results are encouraging and suggest scalability, though tuning was limited.
- Code availability: Authors will release code, which is a strong positive.
- Gradient-based attention: Section is underdeveloped and results less compelling; needs stronger experiments or streamlining and more discussion of double backpropagation issues with batch norm.
- Hyperparameter tuning: Limited tuning on ImageNet; recommend broader search and discussion of computational cost, especially for double backpropagation.
- Visualization: More attention-map visualizations are needed, especially for larger datasets and gradient-based maps.
- Limitations: Paper should discuss limitations explicitly (e.g., architectures or datasets where attention transfer may be less effective).
- Related work: Related work section is comprehensive but could focus more on intermediate-representation knowledge-transfer techniques.
- Question on β sensitivity: How sensitive are results to the β parameter and was a hyperparameter search conducted?
- Question on KD temperature: For ImageNet KD failures, was the temperature parameter varied?
- Question on why attention > full activations: Request clearer explanation of what attention maps retain that full activation tensors lose.
- Question on gradient-based challenges: Ask for elaboration on applying gradient-based attention transfer to complex architectures with batch normalization and future directions.
