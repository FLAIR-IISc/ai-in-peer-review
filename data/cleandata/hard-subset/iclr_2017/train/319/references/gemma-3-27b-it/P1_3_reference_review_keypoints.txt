- Paper proposes transferring activation- and gradient-based spatial attention from teacher to student, improving student performance and combining well with knowledge distillation.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Novelty: Explicit attention-map transfer is a relatively new and valuable form of knowledge transfer focusing on “where” networks look.  
- Strong empirical results: Consistent improvements across CIFAR, CUB, Scenes and preliminary ImageNet results, supported by ablation studies.  
- Clear methodology: Attention definitions, loss functions, and implementation details are well explained and reproducible.  
- Thorough investigation: Explores multiple attention mapping functions, transfer methods, and KD combinations.  
- Related work: Literature review is well-structured and situates the work among attention and distillation papers.  
- ImageNet detail request: Provide exact teacher/student architectures, training schedule, hyperparameters and a clear Top-1/Top-5 comparison table versus other methods.  
- Gradient-based attention request: Experiments limited to NIN on CIFAR; expand to larger architectures/datasets and clarify the second backpropagation step.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Report robustness or tuning requirements for β in the loss function.  
- Visualization improvement: Add more attention-map examples across layers/datasets and overlay them on input images.  
- Limitations: Discuss computational cost of gradient-based attention and risk/instances of negative transfer.  
- Q1: Provide detailed ImageNet results, hyperparameters, and comparisons to state-of-the-art KD methods.  
- Q2: Explain implementation and computational overhead of the second backpropagation for gradient-based attention.  
- Q3: Report experiments with normalization alternatives to L2 for attention maps and their effects.  
- Q4: Describe any observed negative transfer cases and contributing factors.  
- Q5: Analyze how transferring from different layers (early vs. late) or multiple layers affects student performance.
