- Proposes attention transfer using activation- and gradient-based spatial attention maps to transfer knowledge from teacher to student CNNs.  
- Activation-based attention transfer often outperforms transferring full activations and can be combined with knowledge distillation.  
- Shows strong empirical gains across multiple datasets (CIFAR, CUB, Scenes, ImageNet) and architectures (NIN, ResNet).  
- Ablation studies compare attention mapping functions (sum, max) and transfer methods, supporting the approach.  
- Experimental setup is thorough and includes same-depth and different-depth teacher-student scenarios.  
- Paper is clearly written with useful figures and tables.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Request more extensive experiments for gradient-based attention transfer, including symmetry constraints and batch-normalization issues.  
- Ask for a sensitivity analysis of the β hyperparameter controlling attention transfer loss.  
- Request a quantitative analysis of the additional computational cost of attention transfer.  
- Suggest deeper qualitative analysis: visualize and compare teacher vs student attention maps before and after transfer, including difference maps.  
- Question: Were other norms (e.g., L1, cosine) tried for the attention transfer loss, and how did they compare?  
- Question: Is the difficulty of KD on ImageNet with same architecture/depth dataset-specific or observed elsewhere, and why might it occur?  
- Question: In practice, how to obtain a suitable teacher when no pre-trained teacher exists (e.g., self-training or semi-supervised options)?  
- Question: Please provide an estimate of the percentage increase in training time when incorporating attention transfer.
