- Overall decision: Reject.

- Paper summary: Proposes transferring activation- and gradient-based attention maps from teacher to student CNNs, reporting modest gains and compatibility with knowledge distillation.

- Main reason 1: Lacks novelty—approach is incremental application of known attention and distillation ideas with limited novelty in transfer method.

- Main reason 2: Insufficient experimental rigor—minimal hyperparameter tuning and missing crucial ablations and justifications.

- Motivation & related work: Introduction links to cognitive science well, but related work doesn’t critically engage recent attention visualization or transfer advances.

- Technical approach: Activation- and gradient-based attention definitions are reasonable, but the transfer loss (Eq.2) is ad-hoc and theory for l2 normalization is missing.

- Batch normalization issue: Handling of batch norm in gradient-based transfer (double backprop) is inadequately addressed.

- CIFAR experiments: Report modest improvements; claims of superiority over full activation transfer need stronger evidence, especially given architecture limitations.

- ImageNet experiments: Unconvincing due to limited hyperparameter tuning; incorrect claim that KD hasn’t been applied to ImageNet.

- Visualizations: Attention maps are shown but not used to explain why transfer succeeds or fails.

- Presentation & clarity: Generally well-written but notation is dense, experimental details sparse, and appendix disorganized.

- Needed ablations: Test different attention mappings, loss weights, transfer layers, and effects of including/excluding KD.

- Theoretical grounding: Provide justification for loss choice and l2 normalization; explain why attention transfer should help.

- Stronger baselines: Compare against recent feature-matching and adversarial transfer methods.

- Error analysis: Analyze failure cases and limitations of attention transfer.

- Reproducibility: Release code and trained models.

- Question on normalization: Why use l2 norm for attention maps and have alternatives been tried?

- Question on generality: Is activation-based transfer superiority consistent across architectures/datasets and what factors influence it?

- Question on ImageNet tuning: Provide detailed hyperparameter tuning methods for ImageNet experiments.

- Question on stability: What measures ensure gradients remain well-behaved during double backpropagation?
