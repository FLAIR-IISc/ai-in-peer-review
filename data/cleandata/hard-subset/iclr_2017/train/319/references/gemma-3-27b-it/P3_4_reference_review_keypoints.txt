- Paper proposes transferring teacher attention maps (activation- and gradient-based) to a student CNN to improve performance, showing attention transfer outperforms full-activation transfer and can combine with knowledge distillation.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Novelty: Explicit attention-map transfer is a well-motivated, valuable contribution grounded in cognitive-science intuition.  
- Empirical thoroughness: Extensive experiments across datasets (CIFAR, CUB, Scenes, ImageNet) and architectures (NIN, ResNet) compare attention definitions and transfer methods.  
- Results: Strong and consistent performance gains, notably on ImageNet; visualizations show more focused student attention.  
- Presentation: Clear writing, well-organized methodology, equations, and informative figures.  
- Gradient-based attention: Section is underdeveloped; implementation details and challenges (second-order backprop) need deeper discussion and larger-scale evaluation.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Ablation studies (e.g., weight β for attention loss) are needed to show robustness, especially for ImageNet.  
- Computational cost: Paper should discuss added training cost of attention transfer, particularly for gradient-based methods.  
- Relation to existing attention: Clarify how transferred attention differs from intra-network attention mechanisms (self-attention, channel attention).  
- Appendix: Expand with more attention-map examples across layers and datasets.  
- Question — attention functions: Explain why sum-based attention (Fsum/Fpsum) outperforms max-based variants and when max might be preferred.  
- Question — gradient-based attention & BN: Detail challenges with second-order backprop and batch normalization handling or alternatives explored.  
- Question — ImageNet distillation: Explain why knowledge distillation helped on CIFAR but not on ImageNet and hypotheses for this discrepancy.  
- Question — future domains: Suggest additional application areas beyond object detection and weakly supervised localization where attention transfer could help.
