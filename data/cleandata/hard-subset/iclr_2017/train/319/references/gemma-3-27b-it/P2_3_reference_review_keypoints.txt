- Paper proposes transferring activation- and gradient-based attention maps from teacher to student CNNs to improve student learning.
- Methods for attention transfer are presented and evaluated across CIFAR, CUB, Scenes, and ImageNet with architectures like NIN and ResNet.
- Key finding: attention-map transfer outperforms direct activation transfer and can be combined effectively with knowledge distillation.
- Decision: Accept with Minor Revisions.
- Clear problem definition and motivation connecting attention transfer to human visual perception and neural attention benefits.
- Novelty: focusing on transferring attention maps and comparing activation vs. gradient-based approaches is a novel contribution.
- Strong empirical results: consistent improvements across datasets and architectures, including ImageNet (with limited tuning).
- Visualizations of attention maps help illustrate the approach’s effectiveness.
- Paper is well-written, organized, and easy to follow.
- Request more detailed discussion of computational overhead of attention transfer vs. standard training or KD.
- Suggest expanding ablation studies (e.g., ImageNet) and testing impact of varying numbers of attention-transfer layers.
- Ask for sensitivity analysis of the β hyperparameter (attention transfer weight).
- Recommend providing theoretical justification for why attention transfer works (e.g., links to representation learning or information bottleneck).
- Clarify motivation and role of l2 normalization of attention maps beyond scaling.
- Question: Did you try mitigation techniques (adapter layers, intermediate feature matching) to help KD across differing architectures on ImageNet?
- Question: Any intuition why using all attention-transfer layers slightly outperforms using only the most informative layer(s)?
- Question: How sensitive are results to choice of attention mapping function (Fsum, F2sum, etc.), and are certain functions better for specific datasets/architectures?
- Question: What specific challenges arise when combining gradient-based (double backprop) attention transfer with batch normalization?
