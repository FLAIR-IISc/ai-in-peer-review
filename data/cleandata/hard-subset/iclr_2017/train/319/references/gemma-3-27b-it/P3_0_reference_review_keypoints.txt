- Paper studies transferring attention (activation- and gradient-based spatial maps) from teacher to student CNNs and proposes novel transfer methods.
- Empirical results show improvements across CIFAR, CUB, Scenes, ImageNet and multiple architectures, suggesting attention maps carry transferable knowledge.
- Decision: Reject due to insufficient clarity, rigor, and fragmented experimental design that undermine confidence in claims.
- Major critique: Lack of rigorous ablation studies to isolate the effect of attention transfer and compare different attention map definitions.
- Experimental detail is inconsistent (CIFAR experiments detailed, ImageNet and gradient-based studies under-specified) with missing justifications for design choices.
- Improvements on larger datasets (ImageNet ~1.1% top-1) are modest and possibly not practically meaningful without fuller hyperparameter tuning.
- Gradient-based attention results are limited in scope (thin NIN on CIFAR) and their generality to complex architectures/datasets is unclear.
- Comparisons to existing knowledge-distillation and transfer methods are weak; claim of first successful ImageNet transfer is inadequately substantiated.
- Recommend clarifying the notion of “attention” and its connection to human-vision concepts for better intuition.
- Ask for more visualizations showing student attention converging to teacher attention, especially on larger datasets.
- Suggest improving paper organization, notation consistency, and clearer explanations of methods and implementation details.
- Request analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (β loss weight) and guidance for tuning across datasets and architectures.
- Advise expanding related work to include recent attention/transformer advances and efficient attention mechanisms.
- Questions for authors: Why not fully tune ImageNet hyperparameters and would tuning yield larger gains?
- Questions for authors: What is the computational cost of gradient-based (double backprop) attention transfer vs. knowledge distillation?
- Questions for authors: When should attention transfer be used alone vs. combined with knowledge distillation?
- Questions for authors: Why did knowledge distillation fail on ImageNet—any hypotheses?
- Questions for authors: How do different attention-map normalization schemes (L1 vs L2) impact results?
