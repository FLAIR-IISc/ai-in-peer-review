- Paper tackles transferring spatial “attention” between CNNs to improve students using teacher inductive biases.
- Defines activation-based attention maps via channel-wise reductions (sum, max, p-exponent) and gradient-based attention via input saliency.
- Proposes an Attention Transfer (AT) loss that aligns normalized attention maps across corresponding layers, optionally combined with Knowledge Distillation (KD).
- Explores gradient-based AT implemented via double backprop and a symmetry regularizer on gradient attention.
- Reports consistent empirical gains: CIFAR (WRN/NIN), transfer learning improvements on CUB and MIT Scenes (ResNet-18/34), and ImageNet ResNet-18 +1.1% top-1 from ResNet-34.
- Decision: Accept — novelty in framing transfer as attention alignment and strong empirical signal including ImageNet-scale improvement.
- Motivation: Aligning spatial attention guides students toward teacher-like internal representations without full activation matching.
- Literature placement: connects to saliency/CAM/Grad-CAM, FitNets, KD, double backprop, and group equivariance.
- Methodological soundness: normalization and layer-pairing enable cross-architecture alignment; p-exponent and ablations justify design choices.
- CIFAR results: consistent, non-trivial gains; AT outperforms full-activation matching and complements KD on small datasets.
- Gradient-based AT: shows improvements in constrained settings but faces batch-norm complications.
- Transfer learning: AT narrows ResNet-18 vs ResNet-34 gap, especially on fine-grained tasks like CUB.
- ImageNet result: meaningful +1.1% top-1 improvement using two AT losses without hyperparameter tuning.
- Limitations: many results lack variance reporting; compute/memory overhead and teacher-forward reuse details are sparse.
- KD on ImageNet underperforms in places and isn’t deeply analyzed.
- Gradient-based AT experiments are limited to settings without BN and need more practical handling of BN.
- Presentation: needs cleaner citation formatting, clearer figures/appendix, and a compact schematic of the pipeline and layer pairing.
- Experimental reporting: request mean±std, training time/memory overhead, teacher-forward reuse, and ImageNet learning curves/sensitivity to β and loss placements.
- Baseline comparisons: recommend stronger FitNets and contemporary distillation baselines, and more tuning on CIFAR/ImageNet baselines.
- Gradient-AT improvements: report double backprop cost and demonstrate BN handling (freeze vs update) in experiments.
- Analysis requests: provide quantitative attention-similarity metrics, failure cases where AT misguides, and studies on robustness and calibration.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to β and normalization choices; per-layer or learned β; layer matching strategy for differing depths.
- Questions: compute/memory overhead per iteration on ImageNet and whether teacher forward can be precomputed or approximated.
- Questions: why max-based maps underperform sum-based; did KD hyperparameters (temperature, α) or label smoothing get swept on ImageNet?
- Questions: how BN is handled in second backprop; does AT help when teacher is only modestly better, equal capacity, or poorly calibrated?
