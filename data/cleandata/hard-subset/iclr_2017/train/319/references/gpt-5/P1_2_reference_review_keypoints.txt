- Proposes attention transfer (AT): transfer spatial attention maps from teacher to student CNNs as a knowledge-transfer mechanism.
- Defines two attention families: activation-based maps (channel-wise spatial statistics like sum of |A|^p) and gradient-based maps (input sensitivity ∂L/∂x).
- Uses a simple normalized alignment loss to match teacher and student attention at selected layers; AT can be combined with knowledge distillation (KD).
- Activation-based AT generally outperforms full activation regression (FitNets-style hints) and often complements or exceeds KD.
- Empirical evaluation across CIFAR-10, CUB-200-2011, MIT Scenes, and ImageNet shows consistent gains.
- ImageNet result: ResNet-18 gains ~1.1% top-1 and 0.8% top-5 from ResNet-34 teacher using two transfer losses without tuning.
- Activation-based maps: sum-based mappings outperform max-based; using multiple AT layers yields better results than a single layer.
- Gradient-based AT: yields gains in restricted CIFAR settings (no BN, thin NIN) and benefits from double backprop and flip symmetry, but is limited by BN and experimental scope.
- Transfer learning: AT narrows the gap between ResNet-18 and -34 on fine-grained datasets and can outperform KD in some cases.
- Normalization of attention maps (e.g., l2) and the simple reproducible loss are emphasized as important.
- Some experimental details are missing or ad hoc (e.g., ImageNet tuning omitted, gradient-AT limitations, few variance estimates).
- Rigor: breadth of datasets/models and consistent trends lend credibility despite some missing variance reporting.
