- Paper proposes Attention Transfer (AT): train student CNN to match teacher spatial attention maps alongside supervised loss.
- Two attention families: activation-based maps (sum, p-norms, max over channels) and gradient-based saliency maps.
- AT uses normalized losses to align attention across corresponding layers/groups.
- Activation-based AT outperforms regressing full activations (FitNets-style) and complements Knowledge Distillation (KD).
- Demonstrates improvements across CIFAR-10, CUB-200, MIT-Scenes, and ImageNet; notable 1.1% top-1 gain for ResNet-18 from ResNet-34 teacher.
- Activation-based AT is lightweight, architecture-agnostic, easy to integrate, and adds negligible overhead; code released.
- Method is well-motivated and positioned relative to KD, FitNets, and attention/visualization literature.
- Design choices (e.g., l2-normalization of attention vectors, per-block layer selection) are sensible and empirically important.
- CIFAR results: consistent gains across WRN pairs; sum-based maps beat max-based; multi-stage losses help; AT speeds convergence.
- Gradient-based AT and symmetry regularizer show gains on constrained CIFAR setups but are preliminary and under-explored.
- Transfer learning: AT closes much of the ResNet-18 vs ResNet-34 gap on fine-grained tasks and often outperforms/equals KD.
- ImageNet results are meaningful but comparisons to other regularizers and tuned KD settings are limited.
- Limitation: gradient-based AT experiments avoid batch normalization and use narrow networks, leaving BN-handling unresolved.
- Reporting concerns: some results are single-run; more runs/CI (mean/std) would increase rigor.
- Baseline concerns: stronger, well-tuned FitNets-style baselines and KD sweeps are needed to solidify claims.
- Theoretical grounding is limited; choice of absolute activations and reduction functions is heuristic with limited analysis.
- Suggested ablations: normalization types (l1 vs l2), β scheduling, loss placement, exponent p sensitivity, and overhead measurements.
- Suggested stronger baselines: tuned activation regression (FitNets) and KD temperature/α sweeps, plus multiple seeds for key tables.
- Implementation clarifications requested: per-layer β, normalization constants, decay schedules, and BN handling in gradient AT.
- Suggested qualitative analysis: side-by-side teacher/student attention maps across layers/classes with quantitative similarity metrics.
- Suggested broader applicability tests: experiments or discussion on detection/segmentation and weakly supervised localization.
- Questions for authors include normalization choice sensitivity, β scheduling guidelines, choice of absolute activations vs signed/energy, FitNets tuning effort, KD tuning on ImageNet, BN handling for gradient AT, cross-architecture transfer, and actual training-time overhead.
