- Proposes Attention Transfer (AT): align spatial attention maps between teacher and student using activation-based (sum/max of |A|^p) or gradient-based (input gradients) definitions with l2-normalized attention losses.
- Activation-based AT is cheap, simple, and compatible with knowledge distillation (KD).
- Gradient-based AT aligns input gradients via double backprop and includes a flip-symmetry regularizer, but is computationally heavier.
- Empirical gains: activation-based AT consistently improves student accuracy on CIFAR, fine-tuning tasks (CUB, MIT Indoor), and yields +1.1% top-1 on ImageNet for ResNet-18.
- Activation-based AT outperforms full-activation regression (FitNets-style) given equal supervision budget and accelerates convergence.
- AT combines additively with KD on small datasets (CIFAR) but KD was not effective on the reported ImageNet setup.
- Conceptual novelty: aligns “where to look” rather than matching full features, offering an architecture-agnostic spatial prior transfer.
- Broad applicability demonstrated across residual/non-residual nets, same/different depths, and training-from-scratch vs. fine-tuning regimes.
- Gradient-based AT results are preliminary and limited to a no-BN CIFAR setting, weakening claims about that variant.
- Important ablations missing: role of l2-normalization, sensitivity to p and sum vs. max, per-layer loss weighting, and strategies for mismatched spatial resolutions.
- Baseline tuning asymmetry: KD and other baselines on ImageNet were not well-tuned, weakening comparative claims.
- Claim of being first successful distillation on ImageNet may be too strong without broader, tuned comparisons (FitNets, self-distillation, concurrent work).
- Evaluation scope limited: core hypothesis about spatial priors would benefit from testing on localization, detection, or segmentation tasks.
- Scientific rigor: losses and optimization are clear, activation-based overhead is small, but many CIFAR experiments use single seeds—need means ± std and more runs.
- Recommendations: add ablations (normalization, p, layer choices, interpolation), strengthen KD/FitNets baselines on ImageNet, clarify label use in gradient-based AT, and handle BN in double backprop.
- Suggest reporting variability, showing teacher-student attention alignment metrics over training, and exploring alternative attention definitions (Grad-CAM, learned channel weights).
- Open questions for authors: sensitivity to normalization/p, layer selection for different-depth pairs, whether teacher gradients use ground truth or soft labels, tuned KD/activation-regression baselines on ImageNet, runtime/memory overhead, and application to detection/segmentation.
