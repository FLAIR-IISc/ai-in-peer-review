- Proposes attention transfer (AT) to align spatial attention between teacher and student CNNs for knowledge transfer.
- Defines activation-based attention: spatial maps from channel-wise stats (sum, sum/max of |activations|^p).
- Defines gradient-based attention: input-gradient saliency maps, with optional flip-symmetry constraints.
- Adds losses matching l2-normalized attention maps at corresponding layers (with interpolation for resolution mismatch).
- AT can be combined with knowledge distillation (KD) with negligible extra cost.
- Experiments on CIFAR-10 (WRN, NIN), transfer to CUB-200 and MIT Scenes (ResNet-18/34), and ImageNet demonstrate consistent gains.
- Ablation: activation-based AT outperforms full-activation (FitNets-style) transfer despite both speeding convergence.
- Ablation: sum-based attention variants outperform max-based; multi-layer losses help; normalization is critical.
- Gradient-based AT and double backprop give preliminary improvements, but activation-based AT performs best in reported settings.
- ImageNet result: ResNet-18 gains +1.1% top-1 and +0.8% top-5 using only two attention losses, showing scalability.
- Practicality: activation-based AT has small overhead since teacher attention maps are available from the forward pass; code promised.
- Decision: reviewer recommends acceptance for novel, well-motivated idea with strong empirical support and architecture-agnostic transfer signal.
- Suggestion: provide full experiment tables, per-run means and standard deviations, and statistical significance tests.
- Suggestion: expand baselines to include other distillation/hinting variants and temperature sweeps for logit matching.
- Suggestion: report hyperparameters (β schedules, p choices), normalization ablations (l1 vs l2), layer-selection strategy, and sensitivity analyses.
- Suggestion: for ImageNet, try attention losses on all residual groups and perform modest hyperparameter sweeps to strengthen robustness claims.
- Suggestion: clarify BN treatment for gradient-based AT’s second backward pass and provide BN-compatible implementation/results.
- Suggestion: report computational overhead for double backprop and comparative runtime/compute costs with and without AT.
- Suggestion: ensure teacher/student use comparable augmentations/training protocols and explicitly report training time and compute.
- Suggestion: qualify or support claim of being first successful transfer on ImageNet; fix typos, references, and missing figures/tables.
- Question: provide per-run means/stddevs and learning curves to quantify convergence speed differences.
- Question: how sensitive are results to attention function choice (Fsum vs Fpsum) and normalization (l1 vs l2); guidance on p and β?
- Question: how are layers/groups chosen for alignment; have early/mid/late-only AT experiments been tried and which layers dominate gains?
- Question: what is the training-time overhead of AT relative to baseline and KD; does AT change optimal learning-rate schedules?
- Question: can authors expand comparison to FitNets on ImageNet and other distillation variants (logit + intermediate hints)?
- Question: for gradient-based AT with BN, did authors fix BN stats/params during second backward pass and what BN strategy is recommended?
- Question: have authors tried AT for tasks beyond classification (detection, segmentation, weakly supervised localization)?
