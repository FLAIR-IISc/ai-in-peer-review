- Proposes attention transfer (AT) to transfer knowledge from teacher to student via spatial attention maps derived from activations or input gradients.  
- Activation-based AT uses simple channel-wise mapping functions (sum, sum of powers, max of powers), l2-normalizes attention maps, and applies similarity losses at multiple layers; can be combined with KD.  
- Gradient-based AT matches input gradients via a double-backprop-like second-order step and introduces a flip-invariant gradient regularizer.  
- Demonstrates consistent empirical gains: CIFAR-10 (WRN, NIN), finetuning improvements on CUB and MIT-Scenes, and +1.1% top-1 on ImageNet ResNet-18 using a ResNet-34 teacher.  
- Activation-based AT outperforms full activation transfer and accelerates convergence, showing spatial attention is a better transfer target.  
- AT is novel, simple, broadly applicable, and low computational overhead (especially activation-based AT).  
- Ablations support design choices: sum-based mapping and placing losses across layer groups perform best; AT complements KD.  
- Method is clearly specified with realistic discussion of computational costs; code is provided.  
- Statistical rigor is limited: mixed use of medians and single runs, absent error bars, and limited hyperparameter-tuning fairness.  
- Gradient-based AT is only validated on small models without BatchNorm; interaction with BN and BN-compatible approaches remain unresolved.  
- ImageNet experiments were limited (only two layer groups, minimal hyperparameter tuning); fuller exploration and stronger baselines would strengthen claims.  
- Scope excludes detection/segmentation experiments where spatial attention might be most beneficial.  
- Suggested improvements: report means/stddev and learning curves (more seeds), especially for CIFAR and ImageNet.  
- Suggested improvements: expand KD baselines with stronger tuning and include FitNets-style hints on large-scale tasks.  
- Suggested improvements: provide sensitivity analysis for β, p in Fpsum, and number/placement of AT losses, with practitioner guidance.  
- Suggested improvements: include computational cost breakdown (wall-clock, FLOPs) for training with AT and clarify inference cost.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify normalization choice (l2 vs l1) and normalization axis, and explain why normalization is crucial.  
- Suggested improvements for gradient AT: detail BN handling during second backprop (freeze vs update) and explore BN-compatible variants.  
- Minor fixes requested: improve reference formatting, fix typos, and make figure captions/results self-contained.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to β/layer choices and a rule-of-thumb for placement; why sum-based attention outperforms max; evidence on calibration/robustness improvements.  
- Questions for authors: KD comparisons at ImageNet scale with more tuning and AT+KD performance; BN-handling strategies for gradient AT and any preliminary results.  
- Questions for authors: effectiveness of AT for cross-architecture or cross-domain teachers and identification of failure cases where AT may hurt (e.g., mislocalized teacher attention).
