- Proposes "attention transfer" (AT) to transfer spatial attention maps from teacher to student CNNs using activation- or gradient-based maps.
- Activation-based AT aggregates absolute channel activations (sum, powered sum, max) into 2D attention maps and aligns teacher/student via normalized L2 losses at matched stages.
- Gradient-based AT aligns input sensitivity maps via a double-backprop objective and explores a flip-symmetry regularizer.
- Activation-based AT is simple, architecture-agnostic, inexpensive, and compatible with knowledge distillation (KD).
- Empirically, activation-based AT yields consistent gains across CIFAR-10, CUB, MIT Scenes, and ImageNet, often complementing KD.
- Reported improvements include ~0.8–1.1% top-1 on ImageNet for ResNet-34 → ResNet-18 and significant gains on CIFAR with Wide ResNets.
- Activation-based AT outperforms full activation regression and often synergizes with KD.
- Conceptually novel and well-motivated: spatial energy/localization is an informative mid-level supervisory signal that is easier to mimic than full features.
- Methodologically sound: attention maps tie to known visualization tools; normalization and multi-layer loss placement reduce brittleness.
- Comparative baselines include KD, FitNets-style activation regression, and double backprop, with AT showing favorable results.
- Observations that powered sums outperform max and that multi-layer alignment helps are valuable practical guidance.
- Significance: demonstrating reliable ImageNet transfer with minimal tuning is practically meaningful.
- Gradient-based AT is underdeveloped: limited experiments (NIN on CIFAR without BN), single-run results, BN second-derivative issues, and high computational cost.
- ImageNet experiments lack rigor: single teacher-student pair, only two insertion points, minimal hyperparameter search, and no variance estimates.
- Ablation analyses are incomplete: need systematic studies on β across layers, p in powered sums, normalization choices, interpolation effects, and calibration/robustness impact.
- KD comparisons may be unfair: KD baselines on ImageNet could be sensitive to temperature/α, loss placement, and training schedule tuning.
- Paper is primarily empirical with limited discussion of theory, failure modes, or cases where AT could hurt (e.g., teacher spurious focus or domain shift).
- Suggestion: clarify BN handling for gradient-based AT, report compute/memory overhead and wall-clock multipliers, and provide multi-run statistics.
- Suggestion: explore gradient alignment at intermediate feature layers to reduce dimensionality and BN complications.
- Suggestion: add more ablations for activation-based AT (layer-wise β scheduling, cosine vs L2, L1 normalization, interpolation methods).
- Suggestion: compare to class-aware attention (CAM/Grad-CAM) for transfer, especially on fine-grained tasks.
- Suggestion: report calibration (ECE) and robustness (corruption benchmarks) to assess changes in student evidence reliance.
- Suggestion: extend ImageNet tests with more insertion points, different teacher/student pairs, and variance across seeds.
- Suggestion: improve related work positioning and temper claims about being the "first successful" ImageNet transfer.
- Suggestion: improve clarity, references, figure cross-references, and provide qualitative visualizations of attention before/after AT.
- Questions: sensitivity to normalization and loss form (cosine vs L2) and whether per-layer temperature/β schedules were tried.
- Questions: number of ImageNet runs, variance of the +1.1% gain, and whether gains persist with more loss locations or different teachers (e.g., ResNet-50).
- Questions: whether class-conditional attention maps (Grad-CAM) were tried and how they compare to class-agnostic activation maps.
- Questions: quantify training overhead (compute, memory) for activation- and gradient-based AT and BN handling during second backprop.
- Questions: does AT affect calibration or robustness and does the student inherit teacher biases (e.g., background reliance)?
- Questions: how does AT perform under domain shift when teacher is trained on ImageNet and student is fine-tuned on different-stats domains?
