- Proposes attention-based knowledge transfer using activation- and gradient-based attention maps to improve student networks.  
- Shows attention transfer often outperforms traditional knowledge distillation and can be combined with it.  
- Demonstrates consistent accuracy gains across datasets (CIFAR, CUB, Scenes, ImageNet) and architectures (ResNet, WRN, NiN).  
- Finds attention maps are more effective for transfer than full activation tensors, with F²sum mapping performing best.  
- Observes attention maps focus on low-level features in early layers, discriminative regions in middle layers, and full objects in top layers.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions due to novelty, strong experiments, and practical significance.  
- Strength: Well-motivated connection between human and network attention with sound methodology and strong ablation studies.  
- Strength: Notable ImageNet results demonstrating practical applicability.  
- Major revision: Lacks theoretical justification for why attention maps transfer better than full activations.  
- Major revision: Needs clearer positioning and comparison to recent attention-based distillation work (e.g., Wang et al. ICLR 2017, Zhang et al. ICML 2018).  
- Major revision: Gradient-based attention transfer description is unclear regarding handling of batch normalization; clarify or acknowledge limitations.  
- Minor revision: Quantify computational overhead of attention transfer versus standard distillation.  
- Minor revision: Explain why KD converges slower on ImageNet as claimed.  
- Minor revision: Improve reproducibility by providing hyperparameters, training procedures, and compute details for ImageNet.  
- Questions: Please provide a theoretical explanation for attention maps being more transferable than full activations.  
- Questions: Clarify the claim that this is the first method applied successfully on ImageNet and how it differs from prior work.  
- Questions: Detail how batch normalization was handled during gradient-based attention transfer in ImageNet experiments.  
- Questions: Provide comparison of the reported ImageNet improvements (e.g., ResNet-18 from ResNet-34 teacher) to state-of-the-art KD results.  
- Questions: Elaborate why activation-based attention transfer yields better improvements than full-activation transfer.  
- Conclusion: Strong, practical contribution that, with addressed revisions on theory and literature positioning, merits acceptance.
