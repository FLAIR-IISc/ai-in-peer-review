- Proposes attention transfer (AT) for knowledge transfer between networks, using activation-based and gradient-based spatial attention maps.  
- Shows AT improves small student networks across CIFAR-10, CUB, Scenes, and ImageNet, and can be combined with knowledge distillation (KD).  
- Introduces activation-based and gradient-based attention map definitions and compares attention mapping functions and transfer strategies.  
- Claims activation-based AT yields significant improvements, including a 1.1% top-1 gain on ImageNet with ResNet-18.  
- Positions AT as complementary to KD and argues attention maps are more useful than full-activation transfer.  
- Experimental design is rigorous with ablation studies validating mapping choices, loss placements, and AT+KD combinations.  
- Provides empirical evidence that AT outperforms both standard KD and full-activation transfer across architectures.  
- Review recommends acceptance with minor revisions based on novelty, motivation, and experimental strength.  
- Suggests clarifying the novelty claim about being the first successful AT application on ImageNet and comparing to prior methods.  
- Recommends expanding theoretical justification for why attention maps are more transferable than full activations.  
- Asks for quantified computational cost/timing for gradient-based AT (double backpropagation) rather than qualitative claims.  
- Requests more analysis of attention map differences between teacher and student and how they correlate with performance.  
- Asks authors to explain why activation-based AT outperforms full-activation transfer beyond empirical results.  
- Seeks clarification on KD failing on ImageNet—hyperparameter sensitivity vs fundamental limitation, and whether standard KD hyperparameters were tried.  
- Asks for expected gains from applying losses to all four residual block groups on ImageNet vs current two-loss configuration.  
- Inquires about handling batch normalization during the second backpropagation in gradient-based AT and any attempted solutions.
