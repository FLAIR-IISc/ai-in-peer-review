- Introduces attention transfer using activation- and gradient-based spatial attention maps for teacher→student knowledge transfer.
- Key contributions: attention as transferable knowledge, two attention map types, consistent gains across datasets/architectures, activation-based AT outperforms full-activation and complements KD.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
- Well-motivated placement in attention and distillation literature; fills gap for thin deep networks.
- Strong empirical support across CIFAR-10, ImageNet, CUB, Scenes and architectures (ResNet, WRN, NIN).
- Ablations show activation-based AT > full-activation transfer and attention mapping choice (sum vs max) matters; visualizations corroborate results.
- Claims activation-based AT is the first successful KD method applied on ImageNet, supported by experiments and literature review.
- Revision request: clarify theoretically why attention maps are more transferable than full activations.
- Revision request: analyze computational cost, since gradient-based AT uses double backprop and is expensive.
- Revision request: include comparisons with more recent KD variants (e.g., relational or mutual KD).
- Revision request: clarify the ImageNet claim—whether prior methods attempted or failed—and check relevant literature.
- Question: what specific information in attention maps makes them more transferable than full activations?
- Question: why does KD struggle for different-architecture/depth teacher–student pairs on ImageNet but not on CIFAR?
- Question: elaborate on batch normalization behavior during second backprop in gradient-based AT and recommended handling.
- Question: have other KD methods (e.g., FitNets) been successfully applied to ImageNet, or are they absent due to computational limits?
