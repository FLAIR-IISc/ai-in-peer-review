- Proposes attention-based knowledge transfer using activation- and gradient-based attention maps to improve student networks beyond traditional distillation.  
- Demonstrates consistent empirical improvements across datasets (CIFAR, CUB, Scenes, ImageNet) and architectures (ResNet, WRN, NIN).  
- Recommends weak acceptance with major revisions.  
- Strength: Addresses an important problem with a novel, well-motivated attention-transfer idea grounded in literature.  
- Strength: Solid experimental coverage showing attention transfer often outperforms full-activation transfer and complements knowledge distillation.  
- Concern: Overstated claim of being "first" to apply knowledge transfer on ImageNet without sufficient literature support.  
- Concern: No measured computational cost or memory analysis for gradient-based attention transfer’s double backpropagation.  
- Concern: Limited theoretical justification for why attention maps are more informative than full activations; evidence is mainly empirical.  
- Concern: ImageNet results lack a fair, properly tuned knowledge distillation baseline and convergence analysis.  
- Request: Reframe or better support the "first on ImageNet" claim with a thorough literature review.  
- Request: Add training time and memory comparisons between attention transfer and standard KD, especially for gradient-based methods.  
- Request: Provide theoretical rationale explaining why attention maps capture more useful information than full activations.  
- Request: Improve ImageNet experimental setup with tuned KD baselines, training curves, and convergence analysis.  
- Request: Clarify implementation details and challenges for gradient-based attention transfer (e.g., handling batch normalization during second backprop).  
- Request: Add ablation studies on attention mapping functions, β values, and layer choices to show sensitivity and applicability.  
- Question: Can authors substantiate the claim that attention transfer is the first successful knowledge transfer on ImageNet and list reviewed methods?  
- Question: How were β values chosen across datasets and architectures, and how sensitive are results to β?  
- Question: What is the actual computational overhead of gradient-based attention transfer versus standard KD?  
- Question: Why choose F^2sum as the default attention mapping function and how do other mapping functions perform?  
- Question: Can authors analyze how attention maps evolve across layers and tasks (object recognition vs. fine-grained recognition)?
