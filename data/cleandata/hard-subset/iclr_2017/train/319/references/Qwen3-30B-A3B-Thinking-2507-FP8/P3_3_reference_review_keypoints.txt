- Proposes attention transfer (activation- and gradient-based spatial attention maps) for knowledge transfer and shows consistent performance gains across datasets and architectures; activation-based AT outperforms full-activation transfer and complements knowledge distillation.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Motivation: Better networks exhibit more discriminative attention maps; visualizations support transferring attention as useful knowledge.  
- Empirics: Thorough experiments and ablations across CIFAR, ImageNet, CUB, Scenes, and multiple architectures; F²sum mapping is particularly effective.  
- ImageNet result is notable as a successful large-scale knowledge transfer application.  
- Related work: Well connected to attention visualization and KD literature; positioned as complementary to existing KD methods.  
- Missing theoretical justification for why attention maps contain more transferable information than full activations.  
- Needs a clearer, more precise analysis of computational cost for gradient-based AT (double backprop), especially at scale.  
- The claim of being the first KD method on ImageNet should be qualified (FitNets performed KD on ImageNet).  
- Ablation studies should include the effect of transferring attention from different layers (early vs. late).  
- Reproducibility: provide full hyperparameters and ImageNet training details beyond just β values and a GitHub link.  
- Question: request a theoretical or information-theoretic explanation for attention > full activations.  
- Question: ask for KD comparison and explanation why activation-based AT (11.2%) beats gradient-based AT (12.1%) in Table 3.  
- Question: ask to clarify the ImageNet claim relative to FitNets.  
- Question: ask about handling batch normalization during the second backpropagation and any experimental observations.
