- Paper proposes transferring spatial attention maps from a teacher to a student using activation- and gradient-based attention.  
- Contributions: define attention maps from activations and gradients; propose attention transfer methods; show activation-based transfer outperforms full activation transfer and complements knowledge distillation; evaluate on CIFAR, ImageNet, fine-tuning and release code/models.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novelty: Framing knowledge transfer around attention maps is a novel alternative to prior methods like FitNets and KD.  
- Experimental rigor: Extensive experiments across datasets and architectures showing consistent, statistically significant improvements.  
- Practical usefulness: Methods are lightweight, computationally efficient, and easy to implement for deployment-focused practitioners.  
- Reproducibility: Sufficient experimental detail and public release of code/models support reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: Clarify the gradient-based attention method, especially the double-backpropagation computational overhead and practical implications.  
- Suggestion: Add more detailed comparisons with related transfer methods (e.g., FitNets, KD) on large-scale datasets like ImageNet.  
- Suggestion: Provide systematic hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, particularly for ImageNet experiments.  
- Suggestion: Explicitly discuss limitations and scalability to tasks beyond classification (e.g., object detection).  
- Question: What is the actual computational cost of gradient-based vs activation-based attention transfer in practice, and is it feasible at ImageNet scale?  
- Question: Can the proposed attention-transfer methods be extended to non-vision domains (e.g., NLP) that use attention?  
- Question: How robust are the methods to large architecture mismatches between teacher and student (very deep/wide teacher vs small student)?  
- Conclusion: The paper makes a strong, practical contribution to knowledge transfer via attention; authors should address the suggested clarifications and comparisons in the final version.
