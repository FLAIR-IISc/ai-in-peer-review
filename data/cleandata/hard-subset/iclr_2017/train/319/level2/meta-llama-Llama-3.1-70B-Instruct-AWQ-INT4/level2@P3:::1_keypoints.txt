- Proposes "attention transfer," using teacher attention maps to improve student network performance.  
- Defines two spatial attention map types: activation-based and gradient-based.  
- Presents several methods for transferring attention between teacher and student networks.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on CIFAR, ImageNet, and fine-grained classification datasets.  
- Claims contributions: attention as a transfer mechanism, use of both attention map types, and empirical improvements across architectures/datasets.  
- Decision: accept the paper.  
- Key reason — novelty: method is novel, well-motivated, and grounded in attention literature.  
- Key reason — technical soundness: methods and attention map definitions are clearly and coherently presented.  
- Key reason — experiments: thorough experimental evaluation across datasets and architectures.  
- Supporting: clearly articulates transfer learning problem and limitations of existing approaches like knowledge distillation.  
- Supporting: provides a well-motivated rationale for why attention aids transfer learning.  
- Supporting: includes comprehensive comparisons to baselines, showing superiority over knowledge distillation.  
- Suggestion: add more implementation details (architectures used, hyperparameters).  
- Suggestion: investigate attention transfer effects at different network layers, not just outputs.  
- Suggestion: explore applying attention transfer to other tasks (e.g., object detection, segmentation).  
- Questions for authors: provide implementation/hyperparameter details used in experiments.  
- Questions for authors: How would you extend attention transfer to tasks like detection or segmentation?  
- Questions for authors: What insights do you have about attention transfer impact across different layers?
