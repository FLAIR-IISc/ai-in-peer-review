- Proposes a novel method for transferring knowledge from teacher to student using attention information.  
- Defines two types of spatial attention maps: activation-based and gradient-based.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness of attention transfer across multiple datasets and architectures.  
- Paper is well-written with clear and thorough explanation of the approach.  
- Accepted because the approach is novel/well-motivated and experimental results show significant improvements.  
- Well-placed in literature with a thorough review of related work on attention and distillation.  
- Provides detailed experimental analysis including ablation studies and comparisons with other methods.  
- Strength: use of multiple datasets and architectures to validate the method.  
- Raises interesting questions about the role and importance of attention in deep networks.  
- Weakness: lacks a clear theoretical explanation for why the method works.  
- Would benefit from more discussion of limitations and future work directions.  
- Asks authors to clarify computational cost and additional computations required for attention transfer.  
- Requests plans for extending the approach to other domains (e.g., NLP, speech).  
- Requests discussion on the relationship and potential combination of attention transfer with knowledge distillation.  
- Additional feedback: add more visualizations of attention maps, detail hyperparameter tuning, and discuss real-world applications.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution; ready for publication after minor revisions addressing the above points.
