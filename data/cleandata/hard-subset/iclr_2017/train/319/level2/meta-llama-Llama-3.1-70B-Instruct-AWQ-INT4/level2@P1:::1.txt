This paper proposes a novel method for transferring knowledge from a powerful teacher network to a smaller student network by leveraging attention information. The authors define two types of spatial attention maps, activation-based and gradient-based, and demonstrate their effectiveness in improving the performance of the student network across various datasets and architectures. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of their approach.

I decide to accept this paper with two key reasons for this choice: (1) the paper proposes a novel and well-motivated approach to knowledge transfer, and (2) the experimental results demonstrate significant improvements in performance across various datasets and architectures.

The paper is well-placed in the literature, and the authors provide a thorough review of related work on attention mechanisms and knowledge distillation. The proposed approach is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the underlying assumptions and principles. The experimental results are also well-presented, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the results.

One of the strengths of the paper is the use of multiple datasets and architectures to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the results, including ablation studies and comparisons with other methods. The paper also raises interesting questions about the importance of attention in deep neural networks and how it can be used to improve performance.

One potential weakness of the paper is the lack of a clear theoretical understanding of why the proposed approach works. The authors provide some intuition about the role of attention in deep neural networks, but a more rigorous theoretical analysis would be beneficial. Additionally, the paper could benefit from more discussion on the limitations of the proposed approach and potential avenues for future work.

Some specific questions I would like the authors to address in their response:

* Can the authors provide more details on the computational cost of the proposed approach, particularly in terms of the additional computations required for attention transfer?
* How do the authors plan to extend the proposed approach to other domains, such as natural language processing or speech recognition?
* Can the authors provide more discussion on the relationship between attention transfer and knowledge distillation, and how they can be combined to achieve even better performance?

Additional feedback:

* The paper could benefit from more visualizations of the attention maps and how they change during training.
* The authors could provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process and how the optimal values were selected.
* The paper could benefit from more discussion on the potential applications of the proposed approach in real-world scenarios.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. With some minor revisions to address the above comments, I believe the paper is ready for publication.
