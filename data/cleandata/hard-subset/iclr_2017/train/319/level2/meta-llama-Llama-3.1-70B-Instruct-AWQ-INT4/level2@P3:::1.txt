**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

This paper proposes a novel approach to transfer learning, called attention transfer, which leverages attention mechanisms to improve the performance of a student neural network by mimicking the attention maps of a powerful teacher network. The authors define two types of spatial attention maps, activation-based and gradient-based, and propose several methods for transferring attention from a teacher to a student network. They demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on various image classification datasets, including CIFAR, ImageNet, and fine-grained recognition datasets. The paper's contributions include the proposal of attention as a mechanism for transferring knowledge, the use of both activation-based and gradient-based spatial attention maps, and the demonstration of significant improvements across various datasets and network architectures.
