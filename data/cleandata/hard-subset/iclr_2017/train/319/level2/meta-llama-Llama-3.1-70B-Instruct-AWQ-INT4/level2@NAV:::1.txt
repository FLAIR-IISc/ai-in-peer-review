**Summary of the Paper's Claims**

The paper proposes a novel approach to knowledge transfer in deep neural networks, leveraging attention mechanisms to improve the performance of a student network by mimicking the attention maps of a powerful teacher network. The authors introduce two types of spatial attention maps, activation-based and gradient-based, and demonstrate their effectiveness in transferring attention information from a teacher to a student network. The paper claims that attention transfer can significantly improve the performance of the student network, especially when combined with knowledge distillation.

**Decision: Accept**

**Reasons for the Decision**

1. The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to knowledge transfer in deep neural networks, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. The authors provide extensive experimental results, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach on various image recognition datasets, including CIFAR, ImageNet, and fine-grained recognition tasks.

**Supporting Arguments**

1. The paper provides a clear and well-structured introduction to the concept of attention in deep neural networks, highlighting its importance in human visual experience and its potential applications in computer vision and NLP tasks.
2. The authors propose two types of spatial attention maps, activation-based and gradient-based, which are well-motivated and supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.
3. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of attention transfer in improving the performance of the student network, especially when combined with knowledge distillation.
4. The paper provides a thorough analysis of the results, discussing the limitations of the approach and potential future directions.

**Additional Feedback**
