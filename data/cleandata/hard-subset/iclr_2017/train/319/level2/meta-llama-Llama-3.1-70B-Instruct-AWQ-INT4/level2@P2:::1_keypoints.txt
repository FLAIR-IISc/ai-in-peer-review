- Proposes attention-based knowledge transfer using spatial attention maps indicating where networks focus during prediction.  
- Defines two attention map types: activation-based and gradient-based.  
- Introduces several methods to transfer attention from teacher to student networks.  
- Claims significant performance improvements across datasets and CNN architectures.  
- Contributions: proposing attention as a transfer mechanism, new transfer methods, and empirical performance gains.  
- Decision: accept the paper.  
- Key reason: originality and significance—attention as a novel, effective knowledge transfer approach.  
- Key reason: well-motivated and well-situated in existing literature with a clear gap addressed.  
- Strong experimental support across multiple datasets and architectures.  
- Detailed analysis shows attention maps capture meaningful input information.  
- Comparisons indicate superiority over some existing methods (e.g., FitNets, Knowledge Distillation) in certain cases.  
- Suggestion: provide more implementation details (architectures, hyperparameters).  
- Suggestion: discuss limitations and practical challenges of the approach.  
- Suggestion: explore applicability beyond classification (e.g., detection, segmentation).  
- Question: request details on computing attention maps, especially the gradient-based method.  
- Question: ask how attention-transfer loss hyperparameters are chosen and tuned.  
- Question: inquire about combining attention transfer with other methods like Knowledge Distillation.
