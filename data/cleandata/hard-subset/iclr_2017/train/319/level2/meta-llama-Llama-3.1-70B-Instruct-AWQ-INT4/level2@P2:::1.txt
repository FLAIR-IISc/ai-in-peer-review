This paper proposes a novel approach to knowledge transfer in deep neural networks, focusing on the concept of attention. The authors define attention as a set of spatial maps that encode the areas of the input where the network focuses most when making predictions. They introduce two types of spatial attention maps, activation-based and gradient-based, and propose several methods for transferring attention from a powerful teacher network to a smaller student network. The paper claims that this approach leads to significant improvements in performance across various datasets and convolutional neural network architectures. The contributions of this work include the proposal of attention as a mechanism for knowledge transfer, the introduction of novel methods for transferring attention, and the demonstration of consistent improvements in performance.
