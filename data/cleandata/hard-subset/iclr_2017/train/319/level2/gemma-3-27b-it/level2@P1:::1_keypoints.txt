- Decision: Accept — novel, well-motivated attention-transfer method with consistent, significant performance gains.
- Proposal: Transfer attention maps from a teacher CNN to improve student CNN performance using activation- and gradient-based definitions.
- Attention definition: Spatial maps indicating input focus, computed via activation summaries (sum/max of absolute activations) and gradients.
- Methods: Several attention-transfer techniques proposed, including combinations with knowledge distillation and comparisons to full activation transfer.
- Empirical results: Strong, consistent improvements across datasets (CIFAR, CUB, Scenes, ImageNet) and architectures (NIN, ResNet).
- Ablation studies: Thorough analysis of attention mapping functions and KD impact, plus visualizations supporting claims.
- Reproducibility: Code and models provided.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: More discussion needed, especially sensitivity to the attention transfer weight (β).
- Computational cost: Need assessment of overhead, notably for gradient-based transfer requiring an extra backpropagation.
- Theoretical justification: Lacks formal theory explaining why attention transfer works; exploring links to feature representations could help.
- Error analysis: Missing detailed cases where the method fails; such analysis could guide future work.
- Related work: Related work section is broad; should focus more on recent knowledge distillation and attention literature and cite relevant theory (e.g., information bottleneck).
- Question — tuning: How much would ImageNet results improve with fuller hyperparameter tuning, particularly β?
- Question — batch norm: What challenges arose combining gradient-based attention transfer with batch normalization, and how might BN be modified?
- Question — attention vs activation: Why does attention transfer outperform full activation transfer; could attention act as regularization?
- Question — method choice: When is activation-based vs gradient-based attention transfer preferable, and which dataset/architecture traits influence that choice?
