- Investigates transferring attention from a teacher CNN to a student using activation- and gradient-based spatial maps.  
- Proposes several novel methods for transferring attention information.  
- Core idea is promising and intuitive, paralleling human learning and visual perception.  
- Experiments show consistent performance improvements across CIFAR, CUB, Scenes, and ImageNet and multiple architectures.  
- Recommendation: Accept—novel and effective technique with comprehensive experimental support.  
- Strength: clear methodology and thorough experimentation comparing attention map definitions and transfer methods.  
- Ablation studies indicate attention transfer outperforms full activation transfer and highlight effects of different attention mappings.  
- Validation on ImageNet (with limited hyperparameter tuning) suggests generalizability.  
- Availability of code and models enhances reproducibility and future work.  
- Weakness: Paper states KD struggles on ImageNet but does not analyze why (hyperparameters vs architecture incompatibility).  
- Weakness: Gradient-based attention transfer is less developed than activation-based; needs deeper comparison of their pros/cons.  
- Weakness/Request: Paper should discuss computational cost of the additional backpropagation in gradient-based transfer and suggest remedies for KD on ImageNet.
