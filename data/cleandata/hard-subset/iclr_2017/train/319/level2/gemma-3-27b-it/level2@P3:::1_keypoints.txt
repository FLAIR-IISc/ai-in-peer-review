- Proposes transferring teacher attention maps (activation- or gradient-based spatial maps) to student CNNs to improve student performance.  
- Attention maps capture where the teacher focuses processing and can be used as supervisory signals.  
- Explores multiple definitions and transfer methods for attention maps across datasets and architectures.  
- Experimental results show consistent improvements; public code is provided.  
- Decision: accept with minor revisions.  
- Strength: novel, relevant approach well-motivated and positioned within prior work on attention, distillation, and CNN visualization.  
- Strength: clear presentation of definitions, methods, comprehensive experimental setup, and concise results.  
- Revision: clarify relationship, differences, and potential synergies between attention transfer and existing knowledge distillation techniques.  
- Revision: better explain why attention transfer outperforms full-activation transfer, with deeper analysis of information content.  
- Revision: expand discussion of gradient-based attention transfer, especially challenges and remedies related to batch normalization.  
- Revision: provide more detailed ablation and systematic comparison of different attention mapping functions across datasets.  
- Q1: Justify choice of l2 norm for comparing attention maps and whether other distance metrics were considered.  
- Q2: Provide quantitative details on the extra computational cost of the second backpropagation required for gradient-based transfer.  
- Q3: Regarding KD not working on ImageNet, report experiments with different temperatures or hyperparameters.  
- Q4: Investigate applying the horizontal flip invariance constraint to activation-based attention transfer.  
- Q5: Offer initial thoughts on adapting attention transfer to object detection or weakly-supervised localization tasks.
