- Proposes attention transfer (AT): match spatial attention maps from teacher to student during training.
- Defines two attention types: activation-based (aggregate channel activations) and gradient-based (input gradients of loss).
- Introduces a normalized loss to match attention maps and can combine AT with knowledge distillation (KD).
- Experiments on CIFAR-10, CUB-200, MIT Indoor, and ImageNet show consistent improvements and released code.
- ImageNet result: +1.1% top-1 and +0.8% top-5 for ResNet-18 (ResNet-34 teacher) without hyperparameter tuning.
- AT improves convergence, outperforms FitNets-style full activation matching, and complements KD on CIFAR.
- Decision: Accept.
- Primary reason 1: Conceptually novel yet simple and architecture-agnostic method for transfer.
- Primary reason 2: Strong empirical support across datasets and architectures with low overhead.
- Problem/motivation: Attention maps are a compact, salient supervisory signal beyond logits/full features.
- Method soundness: Activation-based functions are differentiable and scale-invariant; gradient-based AT leverages double backprop.
- CIFAR results: Sum-based attention outperforms max-based; AT > full activation matching; AT+KD > KD.
- Gradient-based AT: Shows improvement but evaluated in a limited, controlled setting.
- Transfer learning: AT narrows ResNet-18 vs ResNet-34 gap and can outperform KD on fine-grained tasks.
- Positioning: Well connected to attention visualization, KD/FitNets, and double backprop literature.
- Reproducibility/practicality: Code release, clear losses, and minimal computational overhead noted.
- Limitation: Some experiments lack statistical rigor (single runs or medians without variances).
- Limitation: Gradient-based AT is preliminary and BN handling in second-order backprop is unresolved.
- Limitation: ImageNet KD baselines may be under-tuned, complicating comparisons.
- Limitation: More systematic ablations on layer choices, p, and normalization would strengthen recommendations.
- Suggested improvements: Report mean±std over ≥3 seeds and confidence intervals for ImageNet.
- Suggested improvements: Provide wall-clock overhead for attention computation and double backprop.
- Suggested improvements: Ablate layer selection and consider learned per-layer AT weights or temperature scaling.
- Suggested improvements: Discuss pre/post-activation attention and BN impacts on activation-based attention.
- Suggested improvements: Run fuller ImageNet ablations (more groups, varied β) and tune KD baseline.
- Questions for authors: Sensitivity to p and normalization choices; experiments with l1 or temperature scaling?
- Questions for authors: How were ImageNet AT layers selected and do earlier groups help?
- Questions for authors: What is wall-clock overhead of AT vs baseline and KD on ImageNet?
- Questions for authors: Can CIFAR/transfer results be reported as mean±std and tested for significance?
- Questions for authors: Any cross-architecture transfer experiments (e.g., VGG→ResNet)?
- Questions for authors: Recommended BN handling for gradient-based AT; tried freezing BN stats?
- Questions for authors: Can the claim of first successful transfer on ImageNet be contextualized with prior KD/FitNets work?
