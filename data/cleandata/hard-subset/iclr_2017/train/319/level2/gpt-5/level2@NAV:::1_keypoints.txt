- Proposes "attention transfer" (AT): align student spatial attention maps (from activations or input gradients) to teacher to improve student CNN performance.  
- Core claim: matching teacher attention maps improves student accuracy across datasets/architectures and can complement knowledge distillation.  
- Claims activation-based AT outperforms transferring full activations and provides preliminary ImageNet scaling results.  
- Decision: Accept. Main reasons: clear, simple, novel method with strong empirical gains and practical utility (low overhead, released code).  
- Empirical support: well-specified AT loss, normalization and layer placement guidelines, tested on WRNs and ResNets with consistent improvements (e.g., WRN-16-1 error drops, ResNet-18 +1.1% top-1 on ImageNet).  
- Ablations: compare attention mappings (sum vs max, effect of p), show AT beats full-activation transfer and speeds convergence.  
- Related work: situates method among distillation, FitNets, CAM/Grad-CAM, and double backprop, extending transfer to spatial intermediate representations.  
- Additional techniques: explores gradient-based AT and symmetry regularizer with initial CIFAR gains.  
- Reproducibility: code release supports replication.  
- Suggestion: increase statistical rigor — report mean±std over seeds on CIFAR and CIs on ImageNet/CUB/Scenes; clarify median usage.  
- Suggestion: expand ablations — sensitivity to β/normalization, which layers matter on ImageNet, KD interactions (temperature/α), and robustness to teacher quality.  
- Suggestion: validate gradient-based AT with batch normalization and larger models; report computational/memory cost of second backprop and BN handling.  
- Suggestion: temper or substantiate "first on ImageNet" claim by surveying contemporary work or rephrase as "to our knowledge."  
- Suggestion: add baselines like FitNets-style hints and other saliency/CAM-derived transfers for completeness.  
- Questions: How sensitive is AT to teacher accuracy and are there failure modes with weak/miscalibrated teachers?  
- Questions: What are the wall-clock and memory overheads of AT on ImageNet per step and overall?  
- Questions: Was l1 vs l2 normalization systematically evaluated, and how does β scale with resolution?  
- Questions: How are mismatched spatial resolutions handled (interpolation choices) and do they affect results?  
- Questions: Does AT improve student calibration or localization (e.g., CAM quality) beyond top-1/5 accuracy?
