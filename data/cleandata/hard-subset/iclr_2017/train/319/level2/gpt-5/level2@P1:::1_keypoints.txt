- Proposes Attention Transfer (AT): align spatial attention maps from a teacher to a student as an auxiliary loss.
- Explores two attention types: activation-based maps (sum/sum-of-powers/max-of-powers with l2 normalization) and gradient-based input-sensitivity maps (double-backprop + flip-symmetry regularizer).
- AT can be applied across corresponding layers/groups and combined with Knowledge Distillation (KD).
- Reports consistent accuracy gains across CIFAR, CUB, MIT Scenes, and ImageNet, with low overhead for activation-based AT.
- Notable ImageNet result: ResNet-18 gains +1.1% top-1 accuracy.
- AT outperforms full activation regression (FitNets-style) and can complement KD; code and models released.
- Decision: Accept — simple, well-motivated idea with strong empirical support and broad applicability.
- Novelty: using spatial attention as a distilled signal is intuitive, computationally light, and effective.
- Problem importance: fills a gap in transferring "where to look" inductive biases beyond logits/activations.
- Method clarity: attention mappings and normalization choices are justified and empirically validated.
- Placement of AT losses across residual groups is reasonable and experimentally explored.
- CIFAR experiments: consistent error reductions and ablations show sum-based attention functions outperform max.
- AT accelerates convergence and gives larger final gains than full activation transfer.
- Gradient-based AT: careful baselines and symmetry regularization show improvements despite complexity.
- Transfer learning (CUB/MIT): AT narrows the gap between ResNet-18 and ResNet-34, especially for fine-grained tasks.
- ImageNet results are compelling and achieved with minimal tuning, which is rare for distillation work at this scale.
- Reproducibility: public code and detailed implementation notes increase confidence.
- Suggest quantifying compute and GPU memory overhead vs baseline and full activation transfer, especially on ImageNet.
- Recommend reporting variance/error bars across runs for key results to show stability.
- Ask for detailed KD+AT hyperparameters (β schedules, KD temperature/α) and sensitivity analyses.
- Suggest discussing class-conditional vs class-agnostic attention (e.g., CAM/Grad-CAM) and broader teacher–student architecture mismatches.
- Recommend a BN/dropout handling recipe for gradient-based AT (e.g., frozen BN stats) to reduce implementation ambiguity.
- Propose small-scale experiments on detection/segmentation or weakly supervised localization to show transfer of spatial priors.
- Questions on sensitivity: how results vary with choice/number of layers for AT and weighting strategies.
- Ask why l2 normalization was chosen over l1 or spatial standardization and whether alternatives were compared.
- Request exact β settings and Fpsum p values explored and sensitivity to those hyperparameters.
- Ask why KD+AT hurts convergence on ImageNet and whether KD was tuned sufficiently (temperature/label smoothing interactions).
- Request details of autodiff strategy for second pass in gradient AT and how BN/dropout are handled; ask about numerical stability.
- Curious whether other symmetries (rotations) were tried and whether symmetry regularizer was applied at intermediate maps.
- Ask about overfitting to teacher attention, effects on robustness/OOD/adversarial sensitivity.
- Request clarification of the claim about being the “first successful knowledge transfer on ImageNet” to avoid overclaiming.
