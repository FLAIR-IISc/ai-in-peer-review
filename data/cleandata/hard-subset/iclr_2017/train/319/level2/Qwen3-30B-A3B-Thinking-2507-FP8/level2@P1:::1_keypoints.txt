- Proposes attention transfer for knowledge distillation using activation-based and gradient-based attention maps.  
- Provides formal definition of attention for CNNs and novel attention transfer methods.  
- Shows extensive experiments with consistent student network improvements across CIFAR, CUB, Scenes, and ImageNet.  
- Demonstrates applicability across architectures (ResNet, Wide ResNet, Network-in-Network).  
- Decision: Accept — idea is novel, well-justified, and empirically convincing.  
- Ablation studies indicate attention transfer outperforms full activation transfer (FitNets-style).  
- Finds specific attention mapping functions (especially F^2sum) perform best.  
- Reports the first successful application of activation-based attention transfer on ImageNet (noting this claim should be qualified).  
- Gradient-based attention transfer was only evaluated on CIFAR due to computational and batch-normalization issues.  
- Requests clearer analysis of why attention maps outperform full activations and what unique information they capture.  
- Asks for clarification on how attention transfer complements knowledge distillation (e.g., spatial vs. class-distribution focus).  
- Suggests discussion on why batch normalization hinders gradient-based attention transfer and how to address it.  
- Questions why F^2sum and Fsum yield very similar error rates and when one is preferable.  
- Asks how blurry higher-layer attention maps affect transfer and what mitigation strategies are used.  
- Seeks insight on why attention transfer benefits fine-grained recognition (CUB) more than scene classification.  
- Requests examples/analysis of how attention maps change across layers for different tasks (e.g., object detection vs. fine-grained classification).
