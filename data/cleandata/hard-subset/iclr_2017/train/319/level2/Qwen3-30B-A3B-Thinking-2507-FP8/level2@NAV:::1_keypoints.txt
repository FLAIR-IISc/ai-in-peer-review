- Proposes attention transfer between networks using spatial attention maps for CNNs.
- Introduces activation-based and gradient-based attention transfer methods.
- Shows attention transfer significantly improves student performance and is compatible with knowledge distillation.
- Claims attention transfer outperforms full-activation transfer.
- Provides experimental validation across multiple datasets and architectures, including ImageNet.
- Reports a notable 1.1% top-1 improvement on ImageNet and claims to be the first successful knowledge transfer on ImageNet.
- Experimental design is sound with appropriate baselines, multiple runs, and statistical significance.
- Motivation and placement within related work are well argued.
- Ablation studies support the importance of key components and show consistent improvements for activation-based transfer.
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
- Requests more discussion on why attention maps are more informative than full activations.
- Asks for analysis of computational cost of gradient-based attention transfer, especially the extra backpropagation.
- Wants clarification of the relationship to prior methods like FitNets.
- Suggests expanding on limitations of gradient-based transfer, particularly with batch normalization.
- Requests clarification on generality to other architectures and applicability to non-classification tasks (e.g., object detection).
- Suggests adding more context comparing the ImageNet claim to recent related work.
