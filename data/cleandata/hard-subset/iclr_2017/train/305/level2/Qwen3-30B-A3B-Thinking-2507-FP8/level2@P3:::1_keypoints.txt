- End-to-end optimized image compression jointly learns analysis transform, quantizer, and synthesis transform for rate-distortion performance.  
- Introduces biologically-inspired GDN nonlinearity, shown more effective than linear transforms and pointwise nonlinearities.  
- Uses a continuous relaxation of quantization to enable gradient-based end-to-end training.  
- Outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 across tests, with notable MS-SSIM gains even when trained with MSE.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing a significant problem addressed and a clear contribution.  
- Strong empirical evidence and clear potential for practical application.  
- Well-placed in the literature with clear distinctions from VAEs and other generative models.  
- Theoretical justification for the quantization relaxation is sound and avoids pitfalls of continuous-domain approximations.  
- Robust experimental design: multiple datasets, bit rates, and metrics (PSNR and MS-SSIM).  
- Main contribution: demonstrating that end-to-end nonlinear transform coding yields superior rate-distortion and more natural visual results.  
- GDN is parameter-efficient, achieving comparable or better performance with fewer parameters.  
- Visual quality improvements are consistent across images and bit rates.  
- Lacks detailed quantitative information on computational requirements for training and inference.  
- Needs further exploration of how compression performance scales with the number of GDN layers (current fixed three-stage architecture).  
- Should clarify exact relationship and differences with Toderici et al. (2016) regarding multi-rate optimization.  
- The perceptual quality claim would be strengthened by a user study to complement objective metrics.  
- Questions remain about scalability to higher-resolution images and comparisons to more recent state-of-the-art methods beyond JPEG/JPEG 2000.
