- Paper introduces an end-to-end optimized image compression framework using biologically-inspired nonlinear transforms and a continuous relaxation of quantization to enable gradient-based optimization.

- Method jointly optimizes analysis/synthesis transforms and quantization, showing superior rate-distortion performance and improved visual quality vs JPEG and JPEG 2000 across bit rates (measured by PSNR and MS-SSIM).

- Recommendation: accept — strong, theoretically sound contribution with solid empirical validation.

- Novelty: effectively combines deep learning with rate-distortion theory; continuous relaxation of quantization is well-justified.

- Experiments are thorough and convincing, with clear comparisons to traditional codecs and related deep-learning approaches (Gregor et al., Toderici et al.).

- Visual quality improvements are notable and supported by objective metrics rather than just subjective impressions.

- Paper distinguishes its approach from variational autoencoders, clarifying its unique contribution.

- Suggestion: include a more detailed comparison of computational complexity, particularly explicit training vs encoding/decoding trade-offs and concrete timing numbers.

- Suggestion: expand analysis of GDN/IGDN versus simpler nonlinearities (ReLU, sigmoid) to quantify efficiency and performance differences.

- Request: clarify the exact relationship to Toderici et al. (2016), especially concerning single representation for all rate points.

- Question 1 for authors: provide specific timing comparisons (training, encoding, decoding) versus JPEG and JPEG 2000 on standard hardware.

- Question 2 for authors: elaborate on parameter counts or architectural differences that give GDN/IGDN efficiency advantages and whether this holds for both grayscale and RGB.

- Question 3 for authors: discuss whether GDN nonlinearities inherently capture perceptual quality or whether gains arise primarily from the end-to-end optimization.
