- Novel end-to-end learned lossy image compression using nonlinear transform coding with cascaded convolutional layers and GDN nonlinearities.
- Core technical contribution: continuous relaxation of the quantizer via additive uniform noise to enable gradient-based optimization.
- Insightful connection drawn between the relaxed compression objective and variational autoencoders, noting key similarities and differences.
- Empirical result: outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 on rate-distortion (PSNR) when optimized for MSE.
- Strong perceptual improvement with visual examples and consistently higher MS-SSIM across bitrates.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: elegant, effective framework that tackles non-differentiable quantization and advances deep-learning-based compression.
- Key reason: state-of-the-art results and markedly improved visual quality with fewer blocking/ringing artifacts.
- Validation: Figure 4 shows the relaxed loss is a reasonable proxy for true rate and distortion.
- Evaluation: thorough and fair comparisons, including implementation of an arithmetic entropy coder to report actual bit rates.
- Suggestion: include a formal ablation study comparing GDN to standard activations (e.g., ReLU) to quantify GDN’s contribution.
- Suggestion: provide quantitative analysis of computational complexity and inference times (CPU/GPU) versus JPEG 2000.
- Suggestion: discuss rate adaptability and whether λ could be provided as an input to avoid training per rate point.
- Question: clarify exploration of architecture design space and sensitivity to depth, channel counts, and filter sizes.
- Question: address the biased estimator behavior of the relaxed entropy at low rates and its impact on optimization.
- Question: consider richer entropy/context models to capture spatial and cross-channel dependencies and potential further gains.
