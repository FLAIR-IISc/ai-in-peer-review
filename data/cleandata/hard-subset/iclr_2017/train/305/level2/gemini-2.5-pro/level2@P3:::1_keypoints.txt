- Proposes an end-to-end lossy image compression framework with multi-stage convolutional transforms using GDN, uniform quantization, and joint rate-distortion optimization.  
- Enables gradient-based training through replacing quantization with additive uniform noise as a continuous, differentiable proxy.  
- Draws a connection between the relaxed objective and Variational Autoencoders, clarifying important differences for compression.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results: better PSNR than JPEG/JPEG 2000 at low bitrates and substantially higher MS-SSIM across all bitrates.  
- Qualitative results show dramatic reduction of blocking and ringing artifacts, yielding more natural, structurally preserved reconstructions.  
- Decision: Accept — paper is a significant contribution applying end-to-end deep learning to image compression.  
- Key reason 1: Technical novelty and soundness in handling discrete quantization and linking to VAE theory.  
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical performance and notably improved visual quality over established codecs.  
- Well-motivated critique of traditional transform coding and advantage of joint end-to-end optimization.  
- Empirical validation (Figure 4) indicates the continuous proxy is a reasonable and relatively unbiased estimator of discrete rate and distortion.  
- Choice of GDN is justified by prior work and appears parameter-efficient compared to other nonlinearities.  
- Evaluation is rigorous: comparisons to strong baselines on Kodak and use of practical entropy coder (CABAC) for accurate bitrate reporting.  
- Suggestion: Explore a single model capable of multiple rates (e.g., conditioning on λ) to improve practicality.  
- Suggestion: Provide quantitative computational complexity or runtime comparisons (e.g., MACs or execution time vs JPEG 2000).  
- Suggestion: Discuss sensitivity to architectural choices (stages, channels) to guide future work.  
- Question: How do learned filters/representations differ when optimized for rate-distortion versus pure density estimation with the same architecture?  
- Question: Has transfer learning been explored between models trained with different λ values (fine-tuning between bitrates) to reduce training cost?
