- Presents a novel end-to-end learned framework for lossy image compression with encoder, uniform scalar quantizer, and decoder implemented as deep convnets using GDN.  
- Core technical contribution: training through non-differentiable quantization by replacing quantizer with additive uniform noise to form a differentiable proxy for rate-distortion loss.  
- Thoughtful relation drawn between the proposed objective and Variational Autoencoders, clarifying similarities and differences.  
- Trained models at multiple rate-distortion points and evaluated against JPEG and JPEG 2000 on standard test sets.  
- Strong empirical results: competitive or superior PSNR and dramatic perceptual-quality improvements validated visually and by MS-SSIM.  
- Decision: Accept — reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.  
- Key reason 1: Principled, complete end-to-end framework enabling true R+λD optimization via a clever continuous relaxation of quantization.  
- Key reason 2: State-of-the-art empirical performance with visually superior reconstructions that remove blocking and ringing artifacts.  
- Proxy estimator validated (Fig. 4) as a reasonable and relatively unbiased estimator of true discrete rate-distortion values.  
- Architectural choice of GDN is well-motivated by prior work and argued to be more effective than standard nonlinearities.  
- Rigorous evaluation includes implementation of a full entropy coder (CABAC) and reporting of actual bit rates, making comparisons fair and practical.  
- Paper situates contributions well in the literature and explains why compression differs from generative modeling.  
- Additional feedback: paper lacks concrete computational cost/timing or FLOPS comparisons to JPEG/JPEG 2000 for practical feasibility assessment.  
- Additional feedback: network architecture is somewhat ad-hoc; an ablation study (stages/channels) would clarify architectural trade-offs.  
- Additional feedback: current approach requires separate trained models per rate (λ); single rate-adaptive model is a practical limitation to note/future work.  
- Question: Need clarification on what artifacts arise with standard nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU) and whether GDN’s benefits are due to modeling image statistics or stabilization/normalization effects.  
- Question: Entropy model assumes independence of latent elements — have dependencies (spatial/channel context modeling) been considered to further improve R-D performance?  
- Question: Does the uniform-noise continuous relaxation remain effective across λ values, especially at very low bit rates (coarse quantization), or does it introduce optimization instabilities?
