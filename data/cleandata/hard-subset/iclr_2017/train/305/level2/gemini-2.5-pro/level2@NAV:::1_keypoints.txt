- Proposes a novel end-to-end deep neural framework for lossy image compression using an autoencoder-like architecture with non-linear analysis/synthesis transforms, a uniform quantizer, and GDN non-linearity.  
- Key contribution: a continuous, differentiable proxy for the rate–distortion loss that enables direct optimization via stochastic gradient descent through the non-differentiable quantizer.  
- Achieves compelling empirical results, outperforming JPEG and JPEG 2000, particularly at low bit rates, with notable perceptual quality gains supported by visuals and MS-SSIM.  
- Methodology is sound, rigorous, clearly presented, and motivated by prior work on natural image statistics.  
- Clever, empirically validated technical solution for optimizing through the quantizer.  
- Rigorous evaluation includes a full entropy coding implementation (CABAC) and reports actual bit rates, enabling fair comparisons to standards.  
- Paper clearly differentiates the rate–distortion objective from objectives of related generative models like VAEs.  
- Suggestion: include an ablation comparing GDN to standard activations (e.g., ReLU) to quantify the claimed efficiency advantage.  
- Suggestion: discuss practical limitations of training separate models for each rate point and potential extensions to support variable bit rates in a single network.  
- Question for authors: why does the biased continuous rate approximation in the low-rate regime not negatively impact optimization despite the observed bias?  
- Question for authors: provide intuition on sensitivity of performance to network architecture choices (depth, number of filters).
