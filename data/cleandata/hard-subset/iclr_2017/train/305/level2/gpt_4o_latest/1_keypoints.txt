- Presents a novel image compression method using nonlinear transform coding inspired by biology, optimized end-to-end for rate–distortion.
- Key technical contributions: use of Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN) transforms and a relaxed quantization for tractable optimization.
- Provides a full entropy coding framework enabling practical deployment and reports actual bit rates rather than estimates.
- Empirical results show superior rate–distortion performance compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000.
- Demonstrates significant visual-quality improvements supported by MS-SSIM and PSNR evaluations and qualitative examples.
- Decision: Accept — paper has strong, impactful contributions to image compression.
- Strength: Novel biologically-inspired nonlinearities combined with a rigorous optimization framework.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation on standard datasets with convincing quantitative and qualitative support.
- Strength: Solid positioning against prior work and awareness of related literature (e.g., Toderici, Gregor).
- Suggestion: Provide a more detailed discussion of limitations, especially where the method underperforms JPEG 2000 at high bit rates.
- Suggestion: Include more detailed quantitative comparisons to recent methods like Toderici et al. and Gregor et al.
- Suggestion: Discuss adaptation or optimization for perceptual metrics (e.g., optimizing directly for MS-SSIM).
- Suggestion: Expand on broader applicability (e.g., supervised learning tasks), possibly with preliminary experiments.
- Question: How does the method perform on different-domain datasets (medical, satellite) with different compression needs?
- Question: Can the framework be extended to video compression, and what challenges would that entail?
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the choice of λ, and can a single model handle multiple rate–distortion trade-offs?
