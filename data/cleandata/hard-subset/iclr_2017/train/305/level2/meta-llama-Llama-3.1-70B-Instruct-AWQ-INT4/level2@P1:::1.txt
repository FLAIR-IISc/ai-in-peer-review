**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression method using nonlinear transforms. The method consists of a nonlinear analysis transformation, a uniform quantizer, and a nonlinear synthesis transformation. The transforms are constructed in three successive stages of convolutional linear filters and nonlinear activation functions, inspired by models of biological neurons. The method is optimized for rate-distortion performance over a database of training images using stochastic gradient descent. The authors also introduce a continuous proxy for the discontinuous loss function arising from the quantizer, which allows for efficient optimization.

I recommend accepting this paper.

1. The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to image compression using nonlinear transforms, which is a significant departure from traditional linear transform coding methods.
2. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their method, including comparisons to standard image compression methods (JPEG and JPEG 2000) and an analysis of the visual quality of the compressed images.
