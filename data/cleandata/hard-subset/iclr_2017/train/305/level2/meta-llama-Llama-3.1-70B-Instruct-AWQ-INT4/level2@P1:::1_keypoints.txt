- Proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression scheme using nonlinear analysis and synthesis transforms with a uniform quantizer and a continuous proxy for optimization.  
- Transforms are built in three successive stages of convolutional linear filters and nonlinear activations, inspired by biological neuron models.  
- Method is optimized for rate–distortion performance over training images using stochastic gradient descent.  
- Novel, well-motivated departure from traditional linear transform coding.  
- Thorough evaluation including rate–distortion analysis and comparisons to JPEG and JPEG 2000.  
- Demonstrates significantly improved visual quality over traditional methods in provided examples.  
- Clear exposition of mathematical formulation, optimization procedure, and relation to variational autoencoders.  
- Recommends paper for acceptance.  
- Suggests adding a detailed analysis of computational complexity (training and inference times).  
- Requests more justification for choice of nonlinear activation functions and number of transform stages.  
- Asks for comparisons to other recent learned compression methods (e.g., Toderici et al.) and more implementation details on the entropy coding of quantized values, plus exploration of applying the method to other data types (video/audio).
