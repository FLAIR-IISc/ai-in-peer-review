1. Presents an end-to-end optimized image compression method using cascade of biologically-inspired nonlinear transforms.  
2. Claims better rate–distortion performance than JPEG and JPEG 2000 and more natural-looking images with fewer artifacts.  
3. Method can be optimized for different rate–distortion trade-offs.  
4. Recommendation: accept for publication.  
5. Clear, well-motivated approach with thorough explanations of analysis/synthesis transforms, entropy code, and optimization.  
6. Comprehensive evaluation including comparisons to JPEG/JPEG 2000 and detailed rate–distortion analysis.  
7. Demonstrates ability to produce more natural compressed images.  
8. Paper is well-written and well-organized.  
9. Request more details on the optimization procedure, including algorithm and hyperparameters.  
10. Request analysis of computational complexity, including encoding/decoding time and memory requirements.  
11. Suggest more comparisons to other state-of-the-art image compression methods, especially deep-learning–based approaches.  
12. Questions for authors: provide optimization algorithm/hyperparameters, plans to address computational complexity, and additional SOTA comparisons.  
13. Overall: novel and well-evaluated work but needs more detail on optimization and complexity.
