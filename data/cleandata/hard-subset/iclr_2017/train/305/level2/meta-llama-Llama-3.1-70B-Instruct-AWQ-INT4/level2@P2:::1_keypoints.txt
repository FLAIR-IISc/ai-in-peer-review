- Proposes a novel end-to-end optimized nonlinear transform coding method for image compression using cascaded convolutional filters and nonlinear activations.  
- Uses a continuous proxy for the quantizer and SGD variant to train for rate–distortion performance.  
- Demonstrates superior rate–distortion performance compared to traditional linear methods (JPEG, JPEG 2000).  
- Shows substantial visual quality improvements, especially at low bit rates.  
- Provides a framework to optimize models for specific rate–distortion trade-offs.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clearly explains methods and components.  
- Experimental results are thorough and include convincing comparisons across images and bit rates.  
- Includes detailed performance analysis and discussion of rate–distortion trade-offs.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and implementation challenges.  
- Insufficient discussion of relationships to other recent approaches (generative models, deep neural networks).  
- Recommends more detail on hyperparameter choices (number of filters, downsampling) and their optimization.  
- Requests more implementation details of the entropy coding algorithm and optimizations.  
- Suggests evaluating performance across different image types (textures, structures).  
- Asks for clearer discussion of potential applications, including image/video compression standards.  
- Questions for authors: provide encoding/decoding operation counts, compare to generative/deep methods, detail standardization use cases, and clarify handling/limitations for varied image textures and structures.
