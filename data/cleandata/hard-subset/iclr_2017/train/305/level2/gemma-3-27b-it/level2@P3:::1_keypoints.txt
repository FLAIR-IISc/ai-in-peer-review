- Proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression method using learned nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms inspired by biological neurons (GDN) with uniform quantization.
- Learns transforms and entropy coding jointly to minimize rate-distortion, reporting actual bit rates.
- Extends prior perceptual image modeling work to a complete compression framework including entropy coding.
- Demonstrates improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000 in rate-distortion and visual quality (MS-SSIM).
- Decision: accept with minor revisions.
- Paper is novel, well-motivated, and shows compelling empirical results and notable visual quality improvements.
- Writing is clear; GDN motivation and biological connection are well explained.
- Optimization framework and continuous relaxation for quantization are well-described and linked to variational autoencoders.
- Experimental setup and comparisons to JPEG/JPEG 2000 with MS-SSIM strengthen the claims.
- Ablation study of GDN vs. other activations is preliminary and needs more concrete results (e.g., in an appendix).
- Network architecture choices (filters, stages) seem ad-hoc; discuss sensitivity or attempt systematic optimization.
- Provide a more detailed analysis of the approximation error between continuous relaxation and discrete quantization and its effect on performance across quantization levels.
- Quantify computational cost of training (training time, hardware) to assess practicality.
- Add a pipeline diagram showing analysis transform, quantizer, entropy coding, and inverse operations.
- Ensure the online repository with test images is accessible and well-documented for reproducibility.
- Discuss limitations, such as computational complexity and performance on high-frequency or complex-texture images.
- Expand on entropy coding: current use “crudely exploits spatial adaptation” and could be improved; outline future directions.
- Clarify how initial probability models for the CABAC encoder were derived from continuous densities and whether adaptive updating occurred during encoding.
