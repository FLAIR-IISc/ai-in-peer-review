- Proposes end-to-end optimized image compression using cascaded nonlinear transforms inspired by biological neurons.  
- Jointly optimizes analysis and synthesis transforms plus a uniform quantizer for rate–distortion via stochastic gradient descent.  
- Uses a continuous relaxation of quantization interpreted as a variational autoencoder log-likelihood to enable gradient-based training.  
- Demonstrates improved rate–distortion performance and notably better visual quality than JPEG and JPEG 2000 on diverse images.  
- Recommendation: accept, citing strong motivation and significant visual-quality gains.  
- Builds on prior work in nonlinear transforms and perceptual coding (MSE optimization and GDN).  
- The continuous quantization relaxation is a clever technique with a useful VAE theoretical connection.  
- Experiments are comprehensive (Kodak and other datasets) and supplemental materials/test images are provided.  
- Lacks detailed training procedure information (parameter initialization, learning-rate schedules).  
- Computational cost of training is acknowledged but not quantified.  
- Needs deeper analysis/visualization of learned transforms to understand learned features versus standard CNN filters.  
- Suggests expanding discussion on limitations of using MSE as the training metric despite good visual results.  
- Asks authors for details on training resources (time, hardware), visualizations/characterization of learned filters, exploration of perceptual metrics during training, and sensitivity to hyperparameters (filters, downsampling).
