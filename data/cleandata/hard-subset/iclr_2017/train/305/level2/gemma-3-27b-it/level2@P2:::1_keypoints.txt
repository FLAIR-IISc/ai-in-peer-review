- Proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression method using nonlinear transforms, joint analysis/synthesis, entropy coding, GDN nonlinearity, and continuous relaxation of quantization.
- Claims superior MS-SSIM and visual quality compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000.
- Recommendation: Reject.
- Main rejection reason: insufficient rigorous statistical analysis to support subjective visual improvement claims.
- MS-SSIM improvements are presented without error bars or statistical tests, so significance is unclear.
- Reliance on MS-SSIM alone is insufficient given its known limitations as a proxy for human perception.
- Experimental evaluation is incomplete and relies heavily on subjective assessment.
- Comparison to JPEG/JPEG 2000 may be unfair due to possible implementation-specific biases (e.g., OpenJPEG) and lack of controlled tuning for baselines.
- Computational cost and runtime are not quantified; encoding/decoding timing and FLOP counts are missing despite apparent computational intensity in the appendix.
- Appendix optimization details suggest potentially impractical training/inference complexity for real-time use.
- Suggestion: conduct statistical tests (e.g., paired t-tests) on MS-SSIM and report confidence intervals/p-values.
- Suggestion: perform ablation studies to isolate the contribution of GDN versus simpler activations (ReLU, sigmoid).
- Suggestion: provide detailed timing results for encoding/decoding and analyze computational complexity as a function of image size and bit rate.
- Suggestion: analyze sensitivity to the rate–distortion trade-off parameter λ and how optimal λ varies across images and bit rates.
- Suggestion: elaborate on entropy coding (context modeling, arithmetic coding) and compare implemented coder to optimized implementations.
- Suggestion: discuss potential training-data biases from ImageNet and effects on generalization to other image types.
- Suggestion: clarify the continuous relaxation of the quantizer and quantify the approximation error introduced during training.
- Suggestion: evaluate additional perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS) for a more comprehensive visual-quality assessment.
- Question: provide statistical evidence (p-values, confidence intervals) to support claims of significant visual improvement.
- Question: report encoding/decoding times for typical images/bit rates compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000.
- Question: how sensitive is performance to GDN parameter choices (e.g., exponent values)?
- Question: how was λ selected per image and how does that selection process affect reported results?
