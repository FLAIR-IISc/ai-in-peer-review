- Decision: Accept.

- Novel contribution: End-to-end optimized image compression using cascaded nonlinear transforms inspired by biological vision.

- Key technical innovation: Continuous relaxation of quantization enabling gradient-based joint optimization of analysis, synthesis, and entropy coder.

- Methodology: Joint stochastic-gradient optimization of transforms and entropy model for rate-distortion performance.

- Results: Superior rate-distortion and perceptual quality compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000, demonstrated on Kodak and other datasets with visual comparisons.

- Presentation: Well-written, logically organized, figures informative, related work and limitations properly discussed.

- Request: Provide detailed training time and resource requirements and compare encoding/decoding computational cost to JPEG/JPEG 2000.

- Request: Include ablation studies to quantify contributions of GDN nonlinearity, cascade depth, and other components.

- Request: Expand details on entropy coding (context modeling, arithmetic coding) and compare to simpler coders (e.g., Huffman).

- Request: Analyze sensitivity to trade-off parameter λ and identify ranges with high performance sensitivity.

- Request: Provide stronger theoretical justification or information-theoretic analysis explaining observed gains.

- Question: Quantify bias and variance of the relaxed entropy/distortion estimates and how they depend on sampling fineness in piecewise-linear representation.

- Question: Provide computational-cost comparison between GDN/IGDN and ReLU-based models and clarify whether parameter count drives performance differences.

- Question: Explain motivation for symmetric constraint on GDN weight matrix and whether alternatives or unconstrained variants were tested.

- Question: Describe strategies used when averaging rate-distortion curves to address per-image variability against JPEG/JPEG 2000.

- Question: Clarify how entropy-code parameters and context models are adapted during encoding and how context modeling updates depend on the input image.
