- Proposes a novel nonlinear transform coding image compression method achieving state-of-the-art rate–distortion and improved visual quality versus JPEG/JPEG 2000.  
- Uses an end-to-end optimization framework with a variant of stochastic gradient descent to jointly optimize analysis/synthesis transforms and the entropy code.  
- Claims better rate–distortion performance than JPEG/JPEG2000 across most images and bit rates.  
- Claims compressed images look more natural, with fewer artifacts and improved contour preservation.  
- Supports claims with extensive experimental comparisons across images and bit rates.  
- Builds on prior nonlinear transform coding and generative models; key innovation is biologically inspired nonlinearities (GDN) to model complex image statistics.  
- Provides analysis relating the framework to variational autoencoders, noting similarities and differences.  
- Paper is well-written with clear technical explanations and thorough evaluation, accessible to a broad audience.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing significant improvement over existing methods and strong evaluation.  
- Suggests adding details on computational complexity, including training and encoding/decoding times.  
- Suggests exploring applications to other domains such as video compression and image denoising.  
- Recommends using more advanced, DNN-based evaluation metrics for visual quality.  
- Asks for more detail on hyperparameter choices (number of filters, downsampling factors) and how they were optimized.  
- Asks how the method’s computational complexity will be addressed for large images or video.  
- Asks whether the framework has been considered for other data types (audio, 3D models) and what challenges are expected.
