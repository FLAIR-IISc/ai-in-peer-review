- Proposes an end-to-end trainable image compression system using three cascaded convolutional analysis/synthesis transforms with GDN/IGDN nonlinearities.
- Handles quantization during training via additive uniform-noise relaxation, enabling SGD on a Lagrangian rate–distortion objective for chosen λ.
- Connects the relaxed objective to a VAE view under conditions but emphasizes differences due to discrete coding and full R–D curve operation.
- Models latent marginals non-parametrically per channel, assumes marginal independence, and implements a CABAC-like arithmetic coder to report actual bitrates including side information.
- Empirical results show markedly better visual quality than JPEG/JPEG2000, consistent MS-SSIM gains, and improved PSNR for most images (especially at low rates).
- Validates that the relaxed continuous objective closely tracks discrete R–D behavior empirically.
- Decision: Accept.
- Main justification: a principled, practical, complete learned codec producing actual bitstreams with consistent perceptual gains and competitive PSNR versus strong baselines.
- Conceptual contribution: end-to-end R–D formulation with quantization relaxation, GDN/IGDN transforms, and nonparametric latent modeling is elegant and likely influential.
- Soundness: additive-noise relaxation is applied carefully, bias/variance of relaxed rate/distortion are empirically verified, and VAE correspondence is correctly derived and caveated.
- Positioning: situates work within transform coding, vision normalization, and generative/autoencoding literature; GDN is biologically motivated with prior support.
- Empirical support: strong qualitative/quantitative evidence; reporting actual bitrates (not differential entropy) is a major strength.
- Practicality: efficient encode/decode at test time with sufficient implementation details for reproducibility.
- Limitation: models are trained separately for each λ rather than offering a single variable-rate model.
- Limitation: entropy model assumes independent latents and simple raster-scan context, leaving potential bitrate gains unexploited.
- Limitation: comparisons omit some recent learned compression baselines in R–D terms; evaluation uses a relatively small dataset (Kodak).
- Suggested comparison: include RD comparisons to contemporary learned codecs and report PSNR/MS-SSIM on the same test set.
- Suggested extension: explore single variable-rate models (conditioning on λ, scaling latents, or learning quantization steps) and analyze rate control accuracy.
- Suggested entropy improvements: investigate spatial/context models or hierarchical priors (autoregressive or hyperprior) to reduce independence gap.
- Suggested ablations: quantify contributions of GDN vs pointwise nonlinearities, number of stages, and nonparametric vs parametric marginals.
- Suggested clarity: specify JPEG/JPEG2000 settings (chroma subsampling, color transforms) and report BD-rate summaries over Kodak alongside per-image plots.
- Suggested runtime reporting: add encode/decode throughput on CPU/GPU and memory usage to support efficiency claims.
- Suggested perceptual training: try training directly for MS-SSIM or with a perceptual transform given strong MS-SSIM outcomes.
- Questions — rate control: can quantization step sizes or latent scaling achieve intermediate rates at test time without retraining, and how well do they perform?
- Questions — entropy coding: what bitrate benefit does adaptive CABAC provide vs a fixed non-adaptive code, and what is the gap to empirical discrete entropy (in bpp)?
- Questions — side information: are per-channel marginal models fixed per λ and known to decoder, and what is the side-information overhead in bits/image?
- Questions — spline sensitivity: how sensitive are RD results to spline resolution used for marginal density approximation?
- Questions — GDN ablation: what are quantitative RD deltas (e.g., BD-rate) when replacing GDN with ReLU or other pointwise nonlinearities at matched capacity?
- Questions — generalization: tested on higher-resolution images beyond 256×256 crops, and does performance scale similarly?
- Questions — computational cost: typical encode/decode times for a 512×512 RGB image on CPU and GPU?
