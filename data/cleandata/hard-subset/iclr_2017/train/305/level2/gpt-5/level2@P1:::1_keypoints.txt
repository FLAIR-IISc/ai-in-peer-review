- Proposes an end-to-end learned image codec with convolutional analysis/synthesis transforms, uniform quantizer, and a three-stage GDN/IGDN cascade.  
- Addresses non-differentiable quantization by replacing rounding with additive uniform noise and learning nonparametric marginal priors, enabling SGD on a rate–distortion Lagrangian.  
- Clarifies the connection to VAEs, showing the MSE-based objective corresponds to an ELBO for a specific generative/posterior pair while highlighting differences from generative modeling.  
- Implements a real entropy coder (CABAC) and reports actual bitrates including side information.  
- Empirical results show consistent MS-SSIM and visual-quality improvements over JPEG/JPEG2000 and better PSNR for most images, especially at low rates.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Novel, principled end-to-end formulation that directly optimizes rate–distortion with a theoretically grounded quantization relaxation.  
- Key reason 2: Strong practical impact — a complete codec with real bitrate reporting and clear qualitative/quantitative gains over baselines.  
- Well-motivated problem framing: joint nonlinear optimization addresses higher-order dependencies in natural images versus traditional separated/linear codecs.  
- Literature positioning is thorough, distinguishing transform coding, gain-control models, VAEs, and related neural compression work.  
- Technical soundness: additive uniform noise is an appropriate proxy for quantization and empirical checks show low bias in distortion and entropy estimates at fine quantization.  
- Nonparametric marginal priors (splines) are reasonable, differentiable, and practical for optimization.  
- Use of GDN/IGDN is justified by prior Gaussianization work and appears efficient; architectural/training details show care for stability.  
- Empirical setup: trained on large ImageNet subset, tested on Kodak and classic images, with CABAC-based bitrate measurement and side-information accounted for.  
- Visuals demonstrate reduced ringing/blocking and more natural degradation; continuous relaxation reliably predicts discrete performance.  
- Significance: demonstrates practical viability of learned, rate–distortion-optimized codecs and suggests deployment potential due to GDN efficiency.  
- Suggestion: include aggregated R–D metrics (average PSNR/MS-SSIM, BD-rate/BD-PSNR) for clearer quantitative summaries in the paper.  
- Suggestion: report encoding/decoding runtimes, model size, and FLOPs for typical resolutions and compare to JPEG2000 for practicality assessment.  
- Suggestion: add ablation studies to quantify GDN/IGDN benefits (replace with ReLU, remove joint normalization, vary stages/channels).  
- Suggestion: test richer entropy/context models (e.g., autoregression) to estimate remaining coding headroom beyond independent raster-scan CABAC.  
- Suggestion: explore single multi-rate model conditioned on a rate parameter or learned scales instead of separate models per λ.  
- Suggestion: justify or compare color handling (RGB vs Y’CbCr), since MS-SSIM is computed on luma.  
- Suggestion: expand evaluation to additional test sets (e.g., Tecnick or held-out ImageNet val) to confirm generalization.  
- Q1: Sensitivity to number of stages and channels — are there diminishing returns and runtime trade-offs?  
- Q2: Quantify GDN versus learned linear filters — what's the R–D gap to a pointwise nonlinearity baseline?  
- Q3: How much do adaptive CABAC updates reduce rate versus a fixed learned prior; any test-time mismatch between splines and latents?  
- Q4: Typical encoding/decoding times on CPU/GPU for 512×512 images and scaling with resolution?  
- Q5: Does the relaxation mislead optimization at very low rates (large quantization); any tricks needed for convergence at small λ?  
- Q6: Robustness across training datasets or preprocessing — any overfitting to ImageNet-like statistics?  
- Q7: Attempts to condition/share parameters across λ for a single multi-rate model — results or failure modes?
