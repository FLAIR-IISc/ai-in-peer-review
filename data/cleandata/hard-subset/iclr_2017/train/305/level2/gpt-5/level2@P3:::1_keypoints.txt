- Proposes an end-to-end learned image compression system using cascaded convs + GDN/IGDN, uniform scalar quantization, and a learned entropy model, trained by replacing quantization with additive uniform noise to optimize a rate–distortion Lagrangian.
- Shows the relaxed training objective is equivalent to a VAE under conditions, but explicitly targets multiple rate–distortion operating points via separate λ-trained models and evaluates actual bitrates with CABAC.
- Achieves competitive or superior PSNR versus JPEG/JPEG2000 on Kodak and other images, and notably better perceptual quality (MS-SSIM and visual inspection) across tested rates.
- Empirically validates the additive-noise proxy as a reasonable approximation to discrete quantization, with small distortion bias and diminishing entropy bias at finer quantization.
- Addresses joint optimization of transform, quantizer, and entropy model directly, filling a gap relative to classical codecs and prior neural approaches.
- Uses non-parametric marginal modeling and explicitly states an independence assumption for latents to reduce mismatch with the entropy model.
- Justifies GDN/IGDN nonlinearities based on prior Gaussianization evidence and demonstrates their efficiency in this learned transform coder.
- Distinguishes itself by reporting real bitrates and including an actual entropy coder, making comparisons to standard codecs credible.
- Trains separate models per λ to produce a family of operating points approximating the rate–distortion convex hull for the model class.
- Reports qualitative improvements with fewer objectionable artifacts (less ringing/blocking) compared to linear-transform codecs.
- Requests clarification on what entropy-model parameters are pre-shared versus adapted online and whether any adaptation state is transmitted to the decoder.
- Asks for encode/decode runtime, memory footprint, and model parameter counts (grayscale and RGB) compared to JPEG 2000 to assess practicality.
- Requests more training details (splits, number of updates, early stopping) and discussion of generalization to different image statistics.
- Suggests exploring a unified conditional model to traverse the rate–distortion curve at test time instead of training per λ.
- Recommends ablation studies comparing GDN to pointwise nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU) at matched parameter budgets to strengthen architectural claims.
- Notes the independent-latent entropy model likely limits performance and suggests investigating spatial/channel context models or hierarchical priors for gains.
- Suggests adding comparisons to modern codecs (e.g., intra HEVC/BPG) and commenting on how current entropy coding compares to state-of-the-art context modeling.
- Proposes experimenting with perceptual losses (or mixed objectives) given strong MS-SSIM gains despite MSE training.
- Asks whether rate–distortion is sensitive to non-parametric marginal estimation choices (spline resolution/regularization).
- Inquires if noise annealing or alternative straight-through estimators were tried and whether they aided optimization.
- Asks about failure modes where PSNR underperforms JPEG 2000 at high rates and whether patterns suggest architectural fixes.
- Concludes the work is rigorous, impactful, well-executed, and shows clear potential for further advances; recommends acceptance.
