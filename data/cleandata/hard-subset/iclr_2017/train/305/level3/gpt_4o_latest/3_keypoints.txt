- End-to-end trained image compression system using nonlinear analysis/synthesis achieves superior rate-distortion performance versus JPEG and JPEG 2000.  
- Introduces a differentiable "rate" function that enables effective optimization across rate-distortion trade-offs despite quantization.  
- Uses generalized divisive normalization (GDN), a biologically-inspired nonlinearity, improving efficiency and visual quality.  
- Produces visually superior reconstructions, especially at low bitrates, avoiding blocking and ringing artifacts.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on standard datasets with quantitative (rate-distortion curves, MS-SSIM) and qualitative improvements.  
- Claims simplified computations and maintained high performance compared to more complex networks.  
- Reviewer decision: Accept — paper is well-motivated, rigorous, and makes a significant contribution.  
- Broader impact: differentiable rate function may apply to other ML and signal-processing tasks.  
- Suggestion: clarify broader applicability with concrete examples or experiments in other domains.  
- Suggestion: provide detailed efficiency analysis (runtime, memory) for encoding and decoding.  
- Suggestion: add ablation studies to isolate contributions of components like GDN versus simpler nonlinearities.  
- Suggestion: compare against more recent neural compression methods (e.g., Toderici et al. 2016).  
- Question: How does the system perform on non-natural images (medical, satellite) and can it generalize?  
- Question: Can the method be adapted for video compression, and what challenges would arise?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to choice of training data and is retraining needed for different datasets?
