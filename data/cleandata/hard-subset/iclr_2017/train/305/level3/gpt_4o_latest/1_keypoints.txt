- Novel end-to-end DNN image compression using cascaded conv layers with GDN, jointly optimized with quantization and entropy coding.
- Introduces a continuous relaxation of quantization to enable stochastic gradient descent optimization.
- Empirically outperforms JPEG and JPEG2000 on rate-distortion and perceptual quality (MS-SSIM and visual comparisons).
- Implements entropy coding and reports actual bit rates rather than only entropy estimates.
- Decision: Accept based on technical rigor/novelty and strong empirical results.
- Technical strength: GDN non-linearities effectively Gaussianize image densities; continuous quantization relaxation is a practical innovation.
- Baseline comparisons include JPEG2000, strengthening credibility over papers that only use older baselines.
- High impact potential due to improved perceptual quality at lower bit rates for applications like medical imaging and streaming.
- Suggestion: Include results on standard benchmark images (Lena, Barbara, Baboon) for broader evaluation.
- Suggestion: Add an explicit discussion contrasting neural-network-based methods with classical transform coding.
- Suggestion: Compare against other state-of-the-art neural compression methods (e.g., Toderici et al., 2016).
- Question: How does the method perform on high-frequency-content images (e.g., Baboon) versus classical methods?
- Question: What is the computational efficiency of encoding/decoding, and is it suitable for real-time use?
- Question: Have the authors tried optimizing the framework with perceptual metrics (e.g., MS-SSIM) instead of MSE, and what were the results?
