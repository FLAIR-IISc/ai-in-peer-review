- Proposes an end-to-end image compression framework using GDN, links to VAEs, and reports actual bit rates with MS-SSIM improvements.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Limited novelty: GDN and end-to-end optimization have been previously published; optimizing for MSE rather than perceptual metrics is a regression.  
- Technical issue: Equation 10 appears incorrect—the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ), calling the VAE equivalence into question for non-Euclidean metrics.  
- Unclear contributions: The relative impact of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoders/decoders is not disentangled.  
- Insufficient comparisons to prior work, making it hard to assess novelty and significance of the adaptive coder.  
- Empirical performance: Outperforms JPEG/JPEG2000 and some learned methods by MS-SSIM, but visual quality claims lack broader perceptual validation.  
- Perceptual evaluation is limited to MS-SSIM; no user studies or other perceptual metrics were provided.  
- Suggestion: Explicitly separate and quantify contributions of adaptive entropy coding and encoder/decoder depth with comparative experiments.  
- Suggestion: Optimize for perceptual metrics (e.g., MS-SSIM) and include additional perceptual evaluations or user studies.  
- Suggestion: Correct Equation 10 and validate theoretical VAE connections, especially for non-Euclidean metrics.  
- Suggestion: Provide more implementation details and analysis of the adaptive entropy coder and its performance impact.  
- Questions for authors: Clarify the role and comparative benefit of adaptive entropy coding; justify MSE choice over perceptual metrics; explain how the Equation 10 error affects results and provide a corrected form.
