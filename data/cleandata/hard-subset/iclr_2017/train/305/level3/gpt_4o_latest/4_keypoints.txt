- Introduces an end-to-end learning method for lossy image compression optimizing rate-distortion via a novel continuous relaxation of quantization.  
- Employs nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms inspired by biological neurons (GDN/IGDN) trained on natural images.  
- Demonstrates superior rate-distortion performance and visual quality compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000 across bit rates.  
- Decision: Accept — recommended for novelty, thorough analysis, and compelling results.  
- Novelty: GDN transforms are a key biologically-inspired innovation, with an insightful connection to variational autoencoders.  
- Scientific rigor: Robust empirical evaluation using objective metrics (MSE, MS-SSIM), qualitative visuals, and actual bit rates.  
- Strength: Consistent improvements in RD performance and strengthened validity by reporting real bit rates rather than only entropy estimates.  
- Suggestion: Add broader comparisons to recent neural compression methods (e.g., Toderici et al. 2016) to situate work in learned compression landscape.  
- Suggestion: Include ablation studies to quantify contributions of components like GDN/IGDN versus simpler nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU).  
- Suggestion: Discuss training efficiency and strategies to reduce resource costs or adapt method for real-time use.  
- Question: How does the method generalize to datasets with different statistics (e.g., medical or satellite images)?  
- Question: Can the relaxation technique be adapted to other data types like video or audio?  
- Question: Is there a theoretical explanation for why GDN/IGDN outperform simpler activations in this compression context?  
- Overall summary: Significant contribution with potential to be a landmark learned-compression method after minor additional comparisons and analyses.
