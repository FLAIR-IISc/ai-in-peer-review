- Presents an end-to-end trained image compression system that improves rate–distortion performance over JPEG and JPEG2000.  
- Core technical contribution: a differentiable approximation of the rate function enabling joint optimization of rate–distortion trade-offs.  
- Architecture: cascade of convolutional linear filters and nonlinear activations inspired by neurons, with globally optimized GDN nonlinearity.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept.  
- Clever workaround for quantization non-differentiability: continuous relaxation using uniform noise as a training signal.  
- Authors addressed pre-review concerns and discussed preventing networks from gaming entropy by enforcing output form or using variable-rate encoders.  
- Empirical results convincingly show improved RD performance and better visual quality per MS-SSIM.  
- Links method to variational autoencoders and emphasizes potential broader impact of the differentiable approach.  
- Suggestion: clarify and quantify computational cost of training compared to traditional codecs (training appears demanding; decoding is efficient).  
- Suggestion: analyze sensitivity to hyperparameters (number of stages, filter sizes).  
- Suggestion: provide quantitative analysis of GDN’s contribution versus other activation functions.  
- Question on scaling: how performance scales with image resolution and limits on image size.  
- Question on generalization: performance on datasets substantially different from ImageNet.  
- Question on perceptual metrics: whether using a more sophisticated metric than MS-SSIM during training would yield stronger visual improvements.
