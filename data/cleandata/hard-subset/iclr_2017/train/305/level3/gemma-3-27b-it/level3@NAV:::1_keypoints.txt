- End-to-end optimized image compression using DNNs, jointly optimizing analysis, synthesis, and quantization via a continuous relaxation for rate–distortion.
- Incorporates generalized divisive normalization (GDN) inspired by biological vision; GDN is highlighted as crucial to performance.
- Continuous relaxation of quantization enables gradient-based training and use of stochastic gradient descent to optimize the whole model.
- Demonstrates impressive gains over JPEG and JPEG2000 in both objective metrics and subjective visual quality.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with strong theoretical motivation and clear integration of rate–distortion theory.
- Strength: holistic joint optimization and the clever continuous-relaxation trick for quantization.
- Reviewer recommendation: accept; paper seen as a significant and convincing contribution to image compression with DNNs.
- Suggestion: include results on standard test images (e.g., Lena, Barbara, Baboon) for broader literature comparison.
- Suggestion: explicitly compare against more classical state-of-the-art codecs (BPG/WebP, Daala) with a rate–distortion summary table.
- Suggestion: clarify positioning relative to other neural-network-based compression methods and highlight architectural/empirical advantages.
- Question: clarify the “certain conditions” under which the continuous relaxation approximates the discrete quantization problem and its limitations.
- Question: report ablation studies quantifying GDN’s contribution versus other nonlinearities (ReLU, sigmoid).
- Question: provide detailed computational cost — training time and resources — to assess practical feasibility and reproducibility.
