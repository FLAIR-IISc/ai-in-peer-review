- Recommendation: accept — strong end-to-end optimized image compression method with convincing results.
- Novelty: complete end-to-end optimization of the compression pipeline, differing from component-wise traditional methods.
- Method: cascaded convolutional linear filters with biologically inspired nonlinearities (GDN) for superior transform coding.
- Results: substantial visual-quality improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000, supported by MS-SSIM and objective metrics.
- Motivation: clearly articulated limitations of traditional transform coding and strong connection to probabilistic source modeling and information theory.
- Justification: GDN choice is well-motivated by prior work on Gaussianizing image densities and biological plausibility.
- Literature placement: paper is well-situated in prior work (transform coding, VAEs, perceptual quality assessment) with appropriate citations.
- Experimental support: consistent improvements shown on the Kodak dataset and a set of digital photographs; bit rates reported rather than only entropy estimates.
- Optimization technique: continuous relaxation of quantization for training is included and validated for accuracy.
- Ablation/analysis: comparison to alternative nonlinearities and discussion strengthen the findings; online data and rate-distortion curves aid reproducibility.
- Suggested improvement: include standard test images (Lena, Barbara, Baboon) for broader comparability with published results.
- Suggested improvement: explicitly position and compare to a wider set of classical compression benchmarks beyond JPEG/JPEG2000.
- Suggested improvement: more thorough discussion contrasting this method with Gregor et al. (2016) and Toderici et al. (2016), especially on performance and complexity.
- Question: request for implementation details of the CABAC encoder and context modeling, and comparison to CABAC in JPEG 2000/H.264 in complexity and efficiency.
- Question: clarify whether architectural choices (filters, kernels, downsampling) were set ad-hoc or found via architectural/hyperparameter search.
- Question: provide quantitative bias and variance analysis for the relaxed distortion and entropy estimates, and how accuracy scales with sampling interval for density approximation.
- Question/suggestion: feasibility of publicly releasing code to improve reproducibility and facilitate adoption.
