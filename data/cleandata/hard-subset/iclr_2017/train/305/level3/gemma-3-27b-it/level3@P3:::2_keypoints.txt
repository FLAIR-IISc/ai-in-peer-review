- Paper introduces end-to-end optimized image compression with nonlinear transforms and adaptive entropy coding, drawing parallels to VAEs.  
- Writing is exceptionally clear and the paper is well-structured.  
- Results show visually compelling improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000 and competitive rate-distortion versus other learned codecs.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions due to strong practical results despite incremental theoretical novelty.  
- Good use of generalized divisive normalization (GDN) leveraging prior image density estimation work.  
- Inclusion of a functional entropy coder and reporting of actual bitrates is important for a complete solution.  
- Concern: optimization uses MSE/PSNR rather than perceptual metrics, which conflicts with visually-focused claims.  
- The VAE analogy is weakened because Equation 10 shows the method is not equivalent to a VAE for non-Euclidean metrics.  
- Major weakness: lack of ablation studies isolating the adaptive entropy coder from increased encoder/decoder capacity.  
- Insufficient description of the adaptive entropy coder implementation, context modeling, overhead, and quantitative impact.  
- Request to revisit Equation 10 derivation because the partition function likely depends on g_s(y; θ), affecting the VAE interpretation.  
- Ask for clearer training details, specifically the exact criterion for lowering the Adam learning rate ("stagnation").  
- Request quantification of computational cost (GPU hours) and how training time scales with model capacity.  
- Suggested ablation: replace adaptive coder with a simple fixed coder (e.g., Huffman) to measure the coder’s true contribution.  
- Suggested evaluation: justify MSE choice more strongly or include/experiment with perceptual metrics (e.g., MS-SSIM) during training, especially for color images.
