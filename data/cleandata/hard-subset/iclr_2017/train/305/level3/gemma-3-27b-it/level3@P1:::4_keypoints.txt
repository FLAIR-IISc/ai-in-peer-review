- Novel end-to-end learning approach jointly optimizes analysis, quantization (via continuous relaxation), and synthesis transforms for image compression.
- Continuous relaxation of quantization enables gradient-based optimization and is a non-trivial technical contribution.
- Authors connect the relaxed optimization to variational autoencoders, providing useful theoretical context.
- Empirical results show improved rate-distortion performance and substantially better perceptual quality (MS-SSIM) versus JPEG and JPEG 2000.
- Experiments use Kodak and other photographic images with thorough description of training and entropy coding implementation.
- Online availability of test images and compressed results enhances reproducibility.
- Ablation studies (limited) indicate the chosen GDN/IGDN nonlinearities materially affect performance.
- Presentation is clear, analysis thorough, and the work is judged accept-worthy by the reviewer.
- Paper notes computational cost; more details on resources (GPUs, training time) would help practitioners.
- Entropy coding description could be expanded, e.g., details on context modeling in the CABAC framework.
- The paper does not address extending the approach to video; discussion of challenges/opportunities would be valuable.
- Additional analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (learning rate, filter counts) is recommended.
- Question: why is GDN/IGDN particularly well-suited compared to ReLU or sigmoid?
- Question: under what conditions does the relaxed loss reliably approximate the discrete quantization problem, and when might it fail?
- Question: how does this method compare to more recent learned compression techniques (VAEs, GAN-based methods)?
- Question: were there notable interactions or optimization challenges when jointly training analysis, synthesis, and probability models?
