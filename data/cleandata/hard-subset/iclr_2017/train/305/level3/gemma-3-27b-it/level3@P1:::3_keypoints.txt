- Novel end-to-end optimized image compression using deep CNNs with a biologically-inspired GDN nonlinear transform.  
- Core contribution: a differentiable approximation to the rate function enabling joint optimization of analysis/synthesis transforms and quantizer.  
- Demonstrated superior rate-distortion performance and perceptual quality versus JPEG and JPEG 2000.  
- Conference decision: Accept.  
- Strong empirical support on the Kodak dataset with quantitative (MS-SSIM) and qualitative visual comparisons.  
- Implemented a basic entropy coder and reported actual bitrates, showing feasibility of a complete system.  
- Insightful connection between the relaxed loss and variational autoencoders, linking to generative modeling.  
- Request for more detailed discussion of computational cost and encoding/decoding/training complexity versus JPEG/JPEG2000.  
- Suggestion to analyze sensitivity to network architecture and optimization hyperparameters.  
- Recommendation to evaluate generalization on more diverse datasets with different statistical properties.  
- Call for an ablation study to isolate contributions of GDN components (beta, gamma).  
- Ask for a more thorough discussion of limitations (e.g., performance on very high-frequency or textured images).  
- Question about effects of the continuous quantization relaxation at very low bitrates on optimization and final performance.  
- Question about the choice of piecewise linear marginal density approximation versus more complex parametric or nonparametric models.  
- Request examples of applying the differentiable rate approximation to other tasks (e.g., denoising, super-resolution).  
- Ask how GDN differs from batch normalization in this context and why GDN was chosen.
