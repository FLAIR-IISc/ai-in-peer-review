- Novel end-to-end optimized image compression method using cascaded convolutional linear filters and nonlinear activations, learned from data.
- Addresses joint rate–distortion optimization directly rather than separate transform/quantizer/entropy stages.
- Uses generalized divisive normalization (GDN) inspired by biology as the nonlinear transform, with clear motivation and technical explanation.
- Introduces a continuous relaxation of quantization to enable gradient-based optimization despite discontinuity.
- Connects the approach to variational autoencoders, clarifying theoretical grounding and differences (operating at arbitrary points on the rate–distortion curve).
- Demonstrates superior performance to JPEG and JPEG 2000, especially in visual quality measured by MS-SSIM and also by MSE.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, includes ablation studies, and compares GDN vs. ReLU choices.
- Provides online resources (test images and rate–distortion curves) for reproducibility and community use.
- Decision: Accept — significant, well-motivated contribution with compelling experimental support.
- Suggestion: Provide more detailed analysis of computational cost for training and encoding/decoding.
- Suggestion: Discuss potential extension to video compression and associated challenges.
- Suggestion: Comment on robustness to adversarial attacks given links to generative models.
- Suggestion: Discuss sensitivity to hyperparameters (learning rate, filter sizes, etc.).
- Question: How does the quantization relaxation bias affect choice of λ, and is there a way to compensate systematically?
- Question: How does training dataset composition (e.g., ImageNet vs. medical/satellite images) affect compression performance?
- Question: Why choose DCT coefficient parameterization for filters, and how does this impact convergence and performance?
- Question: Was increasing network capacity for RGB done systematically or heuristically, and how does capacity affect rate–distortion performance?
