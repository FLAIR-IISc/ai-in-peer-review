- Paper proposes end-to-end optimized image compression using cascaded CNNs with a novel GDN nonlinearity and continuous relaxation of quantization for gradient-based training.  
- Authors jointly optimize analysis and synthesis transforms for rate–distortion performance and draw connections to variational autoencoders.  
- Decision: Accept — significant, well-executed contribution with substantial visual improvements over standards.  
- Empirical results show visually superior compression vs JPEG and JPEG 2000, corroborated by MS-SSIM and diverse test images.  
- Adaptive entropy coding is included, strengthening practical relevance, but its exact contribution is unclear.  
- Training details and analysis of the relaxed loss function are thorough and well-presented.  
- Experiments are comprehensive, use standard datasets, and the authors provide test images/results for reproducibility.  
- Concern: choice to optimize for MSE rather than perceptual metrics needs justification and discussion of trade-offs.  
- Suggestion: evaluate with a broader set of perceptual metrics beyond PSNR and MS-SSIM to strengthen visual-quality claims.  
- Possible error in Equation (10); partition function likely depends on g_s(y; θ), affecting the VAE equivalence for non-Euclidean metrics.  
- Unclear how much the adaptive entropy coder contributes versus simply increasing network capacity—an ablation study is needed.  
- Request more implementation details for the entropy coder, including context modeling strategy and computational/bitrate overhead.  
- Suggestion: compare against recent learned compression methods (e.g., NeurIPS/ICML papers) rather than only JPEG and JPEG 2000.  
- Question 1: Rationale for choosing MSE as the distortion metric, given prior work on perceptual metrics?  
- Question 2: Please clarify the correct form of Equation (10) and implications for the VAE relationship with non-Euclidean metrics.  
- Question 3: What is the impact of the adaptive entropy coding component; has an ablation versus increased network capacity been done?  
- Question 4: Provide more details on adaptive entropy coder implementation, context modeling, and computational overhead.  
- Question 5: How does the method compare to more recent learned image compression techniques from NeurIPS/ICML?
