- Presents a novel end-to-end optimized nonlinear transform image compression method with adaptive entropy coding, motivated by biological vision.
- Constructs analysis/synthesis transforms from cascaded convolutional filters and nonlinearities, jointly optimized for rate-distortion via SGD and a continuous quantization proxy.
- Core contribution: extends rate-distortion optimization to deep neural architectures and adds adaptive entropy coding, showing visual improvements over JPEG/JPEG2000.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Strength: Strong empirical results with consistent visual quality improvements and supporting MS-SSIM metrics.
- Strength: Clear, well-written presentation with good experimental detail and online resources.
- Theoretical link to variational autoencoders is useful but limited by the chosen distortion metric.
- Critical issue: Equation 10 appears incorrect — the relaxed model’s PDF should depend on the synthesis transform output g_s(y; θ), not just quantized values.
- Method not strictly equivalent to a VAE when using non-Euclidean distortion metrics; Section 3.1 overstates the equivalence.
- Choice to optimize for MSE is puzzling given authors’ expertise in perceptual metrics; stronger justification and clearer PSNR/MS-SSIM rationale needed.
- Unclear contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper network capacity; an ablation study removing adaptive coding is necessary.
- Need more implementation details of the CABAC-based entropy coder, especially context modeling and its interaction with transform coefficients.
- Experimental coverage is limited: more images and statistical summaries are needed due to wide per-image performance variation.
- Suggested fixes: correct Equation 10, justify MSE optimization and PSNR usage, add ablation for adaptive entropy coding, detail CABAC context modeling, and expand experimental results.
- Questions for authors: (1) training computational cost, memory and time vs. standard CNNs; (2) sensitivity to hyperparameters like learning rate and Adam settings; (3) trade-off between GDN/IGDN complexity and performance, and whether simpler nonlinearities could suffice; (4) overhead of transmitting adaptive entropy coding context models and impact at low bitrates; (5) how performance scales with image resolution and limits for high-resolution images.
