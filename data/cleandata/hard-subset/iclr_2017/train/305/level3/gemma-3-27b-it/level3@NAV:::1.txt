## Review of "END-TO-END OPTIMIZED IMAGE COMPRESSION"

This paper presents a compelling end-to-end optimized image compression method utilizing deep neural networks, achieving impressive results compared to traditional approaches like JPEG and JPEG 2000. The core contribution lies in jointly optimizing the analysis, synthesis transforms, and quantization step for rate-distortion performance, leveraging a continuous relaxation of the quantization loss. The incorporation of generalized divisive normalization (GDN) inspired by biological visual systems is a particularly insightful design choice. The paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly explaining the theoretical underpinnings and experimental setup, and smoothly integrating rate-distortion theory into the objective function. 

I **accept** this paper. This is, frankly, the most convincing paper on image compression with deep neural networks I have read to date. The combination of strong theoretical motivation, clear methodology, and demonstrably superior performance makes a significant contribution to the field. The visual quality improvements, in particular, are striking and represent a substantial advancement over existing methods.

The strength of the work resides in its holistic approach to the compression problem, moving beyond independently optimized components. The use of stochastic gradient descent to jointly optimize the entire model is innovative, and the continuous relaxation trick allowing for gradient flow through the quantization step is clever and effective. The comparison to JPEG2000 is a key strength – it's a more appropriate benchmark than simply comparing to JPEG, as it represents the state-of-the-art in traditional image compression. The empirical results consistently demonstrate an advantage, both in terms of objective metrics and subjective visual quality.

However, to further strengthen the paper, I suggest the following: 
* **Standard Image Set:** Include results on standard images such as Lena, Barbara, and Baboon. This would facilitate direct comparison with a wider range of existing literature.
* **Classical Method Comparisons:** Explicitly compare performance against state-of-the-art *classical* methods beyond JPEG and JPEG2000. Provide a table summarizing rate-distortion performance against methods like BPG/WebP, and Daala.
* **Positional Clarity:** Provide a clearer positioning of this work within the broader landscape of neural network-based image compression. Explicitly compare the performance and architectural choices to previous work, specifically highlighting the advantages of this approach over alternatives.

I have a few clarifying questions for the authors:
1.  The paper mentions the continuous relaxation approximating the discrete problem "under certain conditions." Could you elaborate on these conditions and any limitations of the approximation?
2.  The GDN nonlinearity appears crucial to performance. Were ablation studies performed to quantify the contribution of GDN versus other potential nonlinearities (ReLU, sigmoid)?
3.  Regarding the computational cost, can you provide a more detailed breakdown of the training time and resources required for the models presented? This would help assess the practical feasibility of replicating the results.
