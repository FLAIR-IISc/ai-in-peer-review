- Proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression method using cascaded nonlinear transforms inspired by biological vision and joint learning of transforms plus entropy coding.
- Integrates rate-distortion theory into the objective and uses a continuous relaxation of quantization to enable gradient-based optimization.
- Demonstrates superior perceptual quality (MS-SSIM) and competitive rate-distortion performance versus JPEG and JPEG2000.
- Paper is very well-written and clearly motivates and explains the approach and experiments.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; reviewer finds it the most convincing DNN-based image compression paper seen to date.
- Strength: Holistic end-to-end optimization rather than piecemeal component tuning.
- Strength: Rate-distortion incorporation is fundamental to learning the balance between rate and distortion.
- Strength: Valuable comparison to JPEG2000 (a strong classical baseline) and reporting of actual bitrates increases credibility.
- Strength: Clear discussion of relations to variational autoencoders and solid empirical/visual results.
- Suggestion: Add results on standard test images (Lena, Barbara, Baboon) for easier comparison and reproducibility.
- Suggestion: Include explicit comparisons to strong classical codecs (e.g., BPG, WebP) with their best configurations.
- Suggestion: Provide clearer positioning and an explicit comparison table versus other neural compression approaches (autoencoder-, GAN-based).
- Suggestion: Add an ablation study showing the effect of GDN/IGDN layers versus simpler nonlinearities.
- Question: Quantify the approximation error (bias/variance) of the continuous relaxation for rate-distortion estimates.
- Question: Clarify how much larger the parameter count was when using ReLUs and whether architectural changes were needed.
- Question: Describe any systematic exploration of filter sizes and downsampling factors (methodology and results).
- Question: Would a more sophisticated entropy coding scheme likely improve performance, and if so by how much approximately?
- Question: Provide details on computational resources and training time (hardware, GPUs) used for model optimization.
