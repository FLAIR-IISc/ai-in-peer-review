- Proposes a novel end-to-end optimized image compression method using nonlinear transforms with impressive results.  
- Integrates rate-distortion theory for joint optimization of analysis, synthesis transforms, and quantization.  
- Uses generalized divisive normalization (GDN) inspired by biological neurons, yielding visually superior compression.  
- Demonstrates substantial improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000, especially in MS-SSIM and visual quality.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear motivation, methodology, and results.  
- Connects the method to variational autoencoders, offering useful theoretical insight.  
- Uses actual bit rates rather than only entropy estimates, strengthening practical relevance.  
- Provides a thorough ablation study and justification for design choices (e.g., GDN).  
- Recommendation: reject due to insufficient comparisons with a broader set of modern classical compression methods.  
- Evaluation limited by use of only Kodak dataset and missing standard benchmark images (Lena, Barbara, Baboon).  
- Needs comparisons to more recent, highly optimized classical methods to fully establish significance.  
- Suggests clearer positioning relative to other neural network–based compression methods, potentially via a summary table.  
- Requests reporting of computational cost (training and compression/decompression) for practical assessment.  
- Recommends expanded ablation on nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU, sigmoid) to quantify trade-offs with GDN.  
- Questions for authors: provide computational resources and training time details.  
- Questions for authors: discuss method limitations for specific image types, alternative quantization schemes, use of perceptual metrics in loss, and experiments with other distortion metrics beyond MSE.
