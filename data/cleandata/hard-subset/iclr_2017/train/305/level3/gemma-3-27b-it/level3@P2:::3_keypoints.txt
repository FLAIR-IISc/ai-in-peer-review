- Presents an end-to-end optimized image compression method using nonlinear transforms and a differentiable rate approximation.
- Core contribution: continuous relaxation of quantization via additive uniform noise, enabling joint SGD optimization of analysis transform, quantizer, and synthesis transform across rate–distortion trade-offs.
- Chooses Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN) nonlinearity, motivated by biological vision and prior effectiveness in Gaussianizing image densities.
- Situates work within transform coding, variational autoencoders, and biological vision literature.
- Empirical results on Kodak and standard images show consistent R–D and MS-SSIM improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000.
- Recommendation: Reject — promising and novel but justification for design choices and extent of improvements not fully convincing.
- Concern that claimed substantial visual improvements are somewhat overstated; MS-SSIM evidence is supportive but insufficient without human perceptual studies.
- Explanation for why the method yields better visual quality beyond improved parameter optimization is underexplored.
- Notable modest PSNR gains in some cases raise questions about practical impact; artifacts differ but need deeper analysis.
- Suggestion: include ablation studies to quantify contributions of components, especially the GDN nonlinearity versus simpler activations like ReLU.
- Suggestion: provide detailed computational cost and resource requirements for training and deployment to assess feasibility.
- Suggestion: more thorough comparison of entropy coding (e.g., CABAC) versus JPEG 2000’s coding to separate transform vs coding gains.
- Suggestion: clarify the role and selection strategies for λ since it has no intrinsic meaning.
- Suggestion: explicitly acknowledge limitations (training-data sensitivity, scaling to high resolutions) and outline future directions.
- Questions for authors: impact of varying additive-noise scale during training on final performance; why enforcing specific compressor output forms would force more complex networks; any correlation between optimal λ and image characteristics and potential for dynamic λ adjustment.
