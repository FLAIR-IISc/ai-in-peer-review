## Review of END-TO-END OPTIMIZED IMAGE COMPRESSION

**1. Summary**

This paper presents a compelling end-to-end optimized image compression method based on cascaded nonlinear transformations inspired by biological visual systems. The authors seamlessly integrate rate-distortion theory into the optimization objective, allowing joint learning of analysis and synthesis transforms alongside an entropy coding scheme. The method demonstrably outperforms both JPEG and JPEG 2000 in terms of visual quality, as evidenced by MS-SSIM, and achieves competitive rate-distortion performance. The paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly articulating the motivation, approach, and experimental results. The use of a continuous relaxation of the quantization step to enable gradient-based optimization is a clever and effective technique.

**2. Decision**

**Accept** with minor revisions. This is, in my opinion, the most convincing paper on image compression with deep neural networks that I have read to date. The method’s clear advantages in perceptual quality, combined with its solid theoretical foundation and compelling experimental validation, strongly suggest it will have a significant impact on the field.

**3. Supporting Arguments**

The core strength of this work lies in its holistic approach. Unlike many existing deep learning-based compression schemes which focus on individual components, this paper optimizes the entire compression pipeline end-to-end. The incorporation of rate-distortion theory is not merely superficial; it is fundamental to the optimization process, ensuring that the model learns to effectively balance rate and distortion. 

The comparison to JPEG2000 is particularly valuable. While JPEG is a ubiquitous baseline, JPEG2000 represents a more modern and sophisticated compression algorithm. Demonstrating superior performance against JPEG2000 provides a stronger validation of the proposed method's effectiveness. 

The authors provide a clear and insightful discussion relating their approach to variational autoencoders, highlighting both the similarities and crucial differences. This contextualization enhances the understanding of the work and its potential connections to broader research areas. The empirical results, including the visual comparisons and quantitative metrics, convincingly demonstrate the method’s superiority in terms of perceptual quality. The authors’ decision to report actual bitrates, rather than relying solely on entropy estimates, further strengthens the credibility of their claims.

**4. Additional Feedback**

While the paper is excellent, a few additions would further enhance its impact:

*   **Standard Image Comparisons:** Including results on standard test images like Lena, Barbara, and Baboon would allow for easier comparison with a wider range of existing compression methods and facilitate reproducibility.
*   **Comparison to State-of-the-Art Classical Methods:** The paper mentions several classical methods but lacks explicit comparisons with their best-performing configurations. A more thorough benchmarking against these methods (e.g., BPG, WebP) is recommended.
*   **Positioning and Explicit Comparisons:** The paper would benefit from a clearer positioning of the proposed method *relative* to other neural network-based image compression approaches. An explicit comparison table outlining the strengths and weaknesses of different approaches (e.g., autoencoder-based, GAN-based) would be extremely helpful.
*   **Ablation Study**: It might be interesting to include a small ablation study to show the effect of the GDN/IGDN layers on performance, compared to simpler nonlinearities.

These suggestions are primarily aimed at improving the completeness and comparative analysis of the work and are not critical to the overall positive assessment.

**5. Questions for the Authors**

1.  The paper mentions that the continuous relaxation of the quantization step provides a “good approximation” to the actual rate-distortion values. Could you quantify this approximation error more precisely, perhaps by providing statistics on the bias and variance of the relaxed entropy and distortion estimates?
2.  You mention that preliminary results suggest similar performance with rectified linear units, albeit with a larger number of parameters. Could you elaborate on the magnitude of this parameter increase and whether any specific architectural changes were necessary to achieve comparable results?
3.  The selection of filter sizes and downsampling factors seems somewhat ad-hoc. Was any systematic exploration of these hyperparameters performed? If so, could you briefly describe the methodology and results?
4.  Regarding the entropy code, you acknowledge that it is relatively simple compared to those used in JPEG 2000. Do you anticipate that a more sophisticated entropy coding scheme would further improve the performance of your method? If so, by approximately how much?
5.  The appendix provides details on the optimization process, but doesn't detail the computational resources used. Could you provide information on the hardware (e.g., GPUs) and training time required to optimize the models? This will help readers assess the practicality of applying this method to larger datasets.
