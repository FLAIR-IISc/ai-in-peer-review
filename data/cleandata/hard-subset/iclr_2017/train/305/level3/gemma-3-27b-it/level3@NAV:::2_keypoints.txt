- Recommendation: Strong accept — significant visual quality improvements and promising advance in learned image compression.  
- Contribution: End-to-end optimized compression with deep nonlinear transforms and adaptive entropy coding that outperforms JPEG/JPEG2000 visually.  
- Writing: Exceptionally well-written and accessible; clear connection to variational autoencoders.  
- Empirical strength: Convincing perceptual-quality gains, especially at low bitrates, avoiding linear-transform artifacts.  
- Metric concern: Shift from perceptual metrics to MSE is worrying and may sacrifice perceptual benefits.  
- Evaluation concern: Reliance solely on PSNR is incongruous given the paper’s perceptual focus; SSIM or perceptual metrics should be considered.  
- Ablation missing: No ablation study to separate the effects of adaptive entropy coding versus increased encoder/decoder capacity.  
- Entropy coder detail: Description of the adaptive entropy coder is limited; implementation and performance impact need expansion.  
- Technical error: Equation 10 appears incorrect; partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ) for VAE equivalence with non-Euclidean metrics.  
- Requested experiments: Provide ablation removing the adaptive entropy coder and/or results with a perceptual distortion metric.  
- Willingness to revise score: Reviewer may raise score if authors clarify concerns and show improvements aren’t solely due to the entropy coder.
