- Proposes end-to-end learned image compression using rate–distortion optimization with a continuous relaxation of quantization.  
- Uses cascaded convolutional linear filters and GDN/IGDN nonlinearities for jointly optimized analysis and synthesis transforms.  
- Demonstrates superior performance to JPEG and JPEG 2000 in PSNR, MS-SSIM, and subjective visual quality.  
- Provides thorough analysis linking the method to variational autoencoders and detailing optimization and architectural choices.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with high confidence.  
- Novelty/significance: replaces hand-engineered transforms with learned transforms, enabling gradient-based optimization via continuous relaxation.  
- Strong empirical results on Kodak and additional images, with MS-SSIM gains indicating improved perceptual quality.  
- Paper includes publicly available compressed images for inspection.  
- Analysis is insightful, explaining rationale for GDN, training procedure, and entropy estimation.  
- Suggest reporting training computational cost/resources to clarify expense of model training.  
- Recommend exploring sensitivity to architectural hyperparameters (layers, filter sizes) to show robustness.  
- Request more quantitative assessment of CABAC entropy coding efficiency and any overhead introduced.  
- Suggest evaluating generalization to other image domains (medical, satellite) beyond ImageNet/Kodak.  
- Question: How sensitive is model performance to inaccuracies in the continuous relaxation of entropy?  
- Question: How does the method scale to very high-resolution images and what practical changes are needed?  
- Question: What specific properties of GDN explain its efficiency for compression, and are there theoretical justifications?  
- Question: Which perceptual metrics are considered for future optimization and what are the challenges of incorporating them?
