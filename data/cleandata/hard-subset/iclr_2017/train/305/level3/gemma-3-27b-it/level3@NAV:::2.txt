## Review of "END-TO-END OPTIMIZED IMAGE COMPRESSION"

This paper presents an end-to-end optimized image compression method based on nonlinear transforms, building upon prior work in rate-distortion optimization. The authors successfully extend this approach to deep encoders/decoders and incorporate adaptive entropy coding, demonstrating visually compelling results that outperform JPEG and JPEG 2000. The paper is exceptionally well-written, making the complex concepts accessible. 

I recommend **accepting** this paper, despite some limitations, due to the significant visual quality improvements demonstrated and the clear potential for advancing the field of learned image compression. The visible gains over established codecs are a substantial contribution, and the connection to variational autoencoders provides a valuable theoretical framework.

The core strength of the paper lies in its empirical results. The presented images demonstrate a clear advantage in perceptual quality, even at lower bitrates, a problem often plaguing traditional methods. The authors convincingly show that their approach avoids the typical artifacts associated with linear transform coding. However, the shift from using a perceptual metric during optimization (as in their prior work) to MSE is concerning. While simplifying comparison to existing methods, it potentially sacrifices some of the perceptual benefits achievable with a more tailored distortion metric. Furthermore, the sole reliance on PSNR for quantitative evaluation feels incongruous given the authors’ expertise and the focus on perceptual quality. 

A critical missing component is an ablation study to disentangle the contributions of the adaptive entropy coding and the deeper encoder/decoder capacity. It is unclear whether the improved performance is primarily driven by the increased model complexity or the adaptive entropy coder. An experiment removing the adaptive entropy coding component would be invaluable. Additionally, the description of the adaptive entropy coder is somewhat limited. More details on its implementation and its impact on compression efficiency would be beneficial. Finally, Equation 10 appears to be incorrect as stated; the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ) for the method to remain equivalent to a VAE when using non-Euclidean metrics. 

**To improve the paper, I suggest the following:**

*   **Justification for MSE:** Provide a stronger rationale for choosing MSE over a perceptual metric, or include results evaluating performance with a perceptual metric.
*   **Ablation Study:** Conduct an ablation study removing the adaptive entropy coding to assess its independent contribution.
*   **Entropy Coder Details:** Expand the description of the adaptive entropy coder, including implementation details and analysis of its performance gains.
*   **Equation 10 correction:** Please verify and correct equation 10.
*   **Clarify evaluation methodology:** Explain the rational for using PSNR rather than SSIM, given the emphasis on perceptual quality.



I would be happy to raise my score if the authors can provide further clarification on these points and demonstrate that the observed improvements are not solely attributable to the adaptive entropy coding.
