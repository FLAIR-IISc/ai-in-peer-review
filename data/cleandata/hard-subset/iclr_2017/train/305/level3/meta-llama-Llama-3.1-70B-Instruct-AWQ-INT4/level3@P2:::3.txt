**Summary of the Paper**

This paper presents an end-to-end optimized image compression method based on nonlinear transforms. The proposed method consists of a nonlinear analysis transformation, a uniform quantizer, and a nonlinear synthesis transformation. The transforms are constructed in three successive stages of convolutional linear filters and nonlinear activation functions, inspired by models of biological neurons. The entire model is jointly optimized for rate-distortion performance over a database of training images using a variant of stochastic gradient descent. The authors demonstrate that their method generally exhibits better rate-distortion performance than standard JPEG and JPEG 2000 compression methods, with a dramatic improvement in visual quality for all images at all bit rates.

Based on the paper's contributions and the reviewer's comments, I recommend accepting this paper. The key reasons for this choice are:

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper's main contribution is the development of a differentiable approximation of the "rate" function, enabling effective training across different rate-distortion trade-offs. This approach is likely to have an impact beyond compression, as it provides a general framework for differentiable approximations of similar functions.

The authors' response to the reviewer's pre-review question is thoughtful and convincing. They address the concern that a sufficiently complex network could learn to produce codes within a fixed range that maximize entropy, meeting the upper bound. The authors argue that enforcing a specific form on compressor outputs would force a more complex network or require a separate variable-rate encoder to match the current system's effective compression. This argument is convincing, as it highlights the benefits of the proposed approach in terms of simplicity and effectiveness.
