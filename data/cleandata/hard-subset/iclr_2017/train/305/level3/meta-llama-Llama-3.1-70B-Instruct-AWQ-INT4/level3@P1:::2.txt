This paper proposes a novel end-to-end optimized image compression method based on nonlinear transforms, which demonstrates improved rate-distortion performance over traditional methods like JPEG and JPEG 2000. The authors extend their prior work on rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders and decoders, and relate their approach to Variational Autoencoders (VAEs).

The paper is well-written, and the results show visible improvements over JPEG 2000, particularly in terms of perceptual quality. The authors' use of a cascade of biologically-inspired nonlinear transformations, optimized for the task, is a key contribution. The method's ability to capture the features and attributes of images that are represented in the statistics of the data is impressive.

However, the novelty of the approach is somewhat limited, as the core rate-distortion approach was previously published. The switch from a perceptual metric to Mean Squared Error (MSE) may be seen as a step backward compared to prior work, as MSE is a less informative metric for image quality.

The authors' decision to optimize MSE instead of a perceptual metric is not fully justified, given their backgrounds in image processing and perception. Additionally, the evaluation is limited to PSNR, which may not be the most informative metric for image quality. It would be beneficial to include additional metrics, such as SSIM or MS-SSIM, to provide a more comprehensive evaluation.

Furthermore, the contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoder/decoder capacity is unclear. An ablation study (no adaptation) would be necessary to disentangle the effects of these two components. More details on the adaptive entropy coder and its effects are also required to fully understand the method's performance.

The authors relate their approach to VAEs, but Equation 10 seems incorrect, as the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ), implying the method is not equivalent to a VAE for non-Euclidean metrics. This discrepancy should be addressed.

In conclusion, while the paper presents a well-written and well-executed study, the novelty of the approach is somewhat limited, and the evaluation could be more comprehensive. The authors' decision to optimize MSE instead of a perceptual metric is not fully justified, and the contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoder/decoder capacity is unclear.

Decision: Reject (but with potential for resubmission)
