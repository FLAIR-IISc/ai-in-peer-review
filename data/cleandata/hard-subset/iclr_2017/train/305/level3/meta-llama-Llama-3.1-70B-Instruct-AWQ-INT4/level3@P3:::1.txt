**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel end-to-end optimized image compression method using deep neural networks. The authors introduce a framework for jointly optimizing the entire model for rate-distortion performance over a database of training images. The method consists of a nonlinear analysis transformation, a uniform quantizer, and a nonlinear synthesis transformation. The transforms are constructed in three successive stages of convolutional linear filters and nonlinear activation functions, inspired by models of biological neurons. The authors demonstrate that their method generally exhibits better rate-distortion performance than the standard JPEG and JPEG 2000 compression methods, and more importantly, a dramatic improvement in visual quality for all images at all bit rates.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

I decide to **accept** this paper. The key reasons for this choice are:

1. The paper is very well-written and integrates rate-distortion theory smoothly into the objective.
2. The authors provide a thorough comparison to JPEG 2000, which is a stronger baseline than comparing only to JPEG.

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem they are trying to solve. The approach is well-placed in the literature, and the authors provide a thorough review of related work. The paper supports its claims with empirical results, and the authors provide a detailed description of their methodology.

The authors' use of a continuous relaxation of the probability model to optimize the rate-distortion trade-off is a novel and effective approach. The results demonstrate that the proposed method can achieve better rate-distortion performance than JPEG 2000, and the visual quality of the compressed images is significantly improved.

**Additional Feedback**

To further improve the paper, I would like to see:

1. Results on standard images (Lena, Barbara, Baboon) and references to state-of-the-art classical methods.
2. A clearer positioning and explicit comparisons between neural-network methods and the best previous classical methods.

I would also like to see a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method and a comparison to other neural-network-based image compression methods.

**Questions for the Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method and how it compares to other neural-network-based image compression methods?
2. How do you plan to extend this work to other types of data, such as video or audio?
3. Can you provide more insights into the design choices behind the proposed architecture, such as the use of generalized divisive normalization (GDN) and inverse GDN (IGDN)?

Overall, this is a well-written and well-motivated paper that proposes a novel and effective approach to image compression using deep neural networks. With some additional analysis and comparisons, this paper has the potential to make a significant impact in the field of image compression.
