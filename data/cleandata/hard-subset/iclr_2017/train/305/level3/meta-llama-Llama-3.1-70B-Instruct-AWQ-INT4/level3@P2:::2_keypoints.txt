- Proposes end-to-end optimized image compression using nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms with local gain-control nonlinearities, trained for rate-distortion via SGD.  
- Claims to outperform standard codecs (JPEG, JPEG 2000) and relates approach to VAEs while distinguishing differences.  
- Recommendation: reject, primarily due to limited novelty and unclear separation of contributions.  
- Core approach builds on authors' prior rate-distortion work; extension to deeper nets and adaptive entropy coding may be incremental.  
- Authors failed to clearly highlight novel contributions relative to prior publications.  
- Switching from a perceptual metric to MSE is questionable and needs justification and perceptual evaluation in addition to PSNR.  
- Results are strong and visibly improve over JPEG 2000, but attribution of gains is unclear.  
- Unclear how much improvement comes from adaptive entropy coding versus increased encoder/decoder capacity; an ablation study is needed.  
- Paper is well written with clear method description, but lacks sufficient detail on the adaptive entropy coder and its effects.  
- Reviewer would consider raising score if more information on the adaptive entropy coder is provided.  
- Requests authors to explicitly state novel contributions compared to prior rate-distortion work.  
- Requests justification for choosing MSE over perceptual metrics and inclusion of perceptual evaluations.  
- Requests an ablation showing contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper models and detailed implementation of the adaptive coder.  
- Additional suggestions: provide training hyperparameters and optimization details, more compressed image examples at multiple bitrates, comparisons to other learned methods (e.g., Toderici et al.), and analysis of computational complexity and real-world applicability.
