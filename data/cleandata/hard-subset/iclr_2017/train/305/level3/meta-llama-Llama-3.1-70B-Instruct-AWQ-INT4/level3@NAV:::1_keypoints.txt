- Proposes end-to-end optimized image compression using deep neural networks.  
- Jointly optimizes analysis and synthesis transforms along with the entropy code for rate-distortion performance.  
- Uses generalized divisive normalization (GDN) nonlinearity inspired by biological neurons to model image features.  
- Demonstrates better rate-distortion performance and visual quality than JPEG and JPEG 2000.  
- Decision: recommend acceptance due to well-motivated approach and solid placement in the literature.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and integrates rate-distortion theory into the objective smoothly.  
- Comparison to JPEG 2000 is appropriate and stronger than comparing only to JPEG.  
- Provides clear description of network architecture and optimization procedure.  
- Experimental results are thorough and support the claimed improvements.  
- Suggests adding results on standard test images (Lena, Barbara, Baboon).  
- Recommends citing and comparing to state-of-the-art classical compression methods for clearer positioning.  
- Requests more explicit comparisons between neural-network methods and the best previous classical methods.  
- Asks for more details on the optimization procedure, including hyperparameter choices and convergence.  
- Asks how the method could be extended to other image types (e.g., medical images).  
- Requests insights on the learned features and the role/interpretation of the GDN nonlinearity.
