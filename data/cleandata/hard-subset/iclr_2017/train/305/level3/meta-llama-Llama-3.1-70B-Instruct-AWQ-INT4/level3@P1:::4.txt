This paper proposes an end-to-end learning method for image compression, using a rate-distortion optimization framework with a clever relaxation to learn efficient compression from a natural image database. The authors demonstrate that their method can achieve state-of-the-art performance, both in terms of rate-distortion trade-offs and visual quality, compared to standard image compression methods such as JPEG and JPEG 2000.

**Specific question/problem tackled by the paper:** The paper tackles the problem of image compression, specifically the challenge of jointly optimizing the analysis and synthesis transforms for optimal rate-distortion performance.

**Approach well-motivated and well-placed in the literature:** The authors provide a thorough review of the relevant literature on image compression, including traditional transform coding methods and recent advances in deep learning-based approaches. They clearly motivate the need for an end-to-end learning approach that can optimize the entire compression pipeline, rather than relying on separate optimization of individual components.

**Paper supports its claims:** The authors provide extensive experimental results to support their claims, including comparisons with standard image compression methods and evaluations of the visual quality of compressed images. They also provide a detailed analysis of the rate-distortion trade-offs achieved by their method, demonstrating its effectiveness in achieving a good balance between rate and distortion.

**Decision:** Accept.

**Key reasons for this choice:** The paper presents a well-motivated and well-executed approach to image compression, with compelling experimental results and a thorough analysis of the method's performance. The authors demonstrate a clear understanding of the relevant literature and provide a significant contribution to the field of image compression.

**Supporting arguments for the reasons:**

* The paper presents a novel approach to image compression that leverages the power of deep learning to jointly optimize the analysis and synthesis transforms.
