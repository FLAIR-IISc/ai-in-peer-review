- Proposes an end-to-end learning method for image compression using rate-distortion optimization with a continuous relaxation to enable SGD.  
- Develops a framework for end-to-end optimization of nonlinear transform-based image compression models.  
- Introduces a generalized divisive normalization (GDN) joint nonlinearity inspired by biological neurons to better capture image statistics.  
- Uses a continuous relaxation of the probability model to permit stochastic gradient-based training.  
- Demonstrates improved rate-distortion performance over JPEG and JPEG 2000 for most images and bit rates, especially at low bit rates.  
- Provides thorough experimental results and analyses, including visual comparisons and MS-SSIM evaluations.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing novelty, effectiveness, and thorough experiments.  
- Suggests comparing against more recent image compression methods, particularly deep learning–based approaches.  
- Requests more details on computational resources required for training and testing, and discussion of real-world application scenarios.  
- Asks for clarification on the rationale for choosing GDN and its relation to biological neuron models.  
- Asks how the approach could be extended to other data types (e.g., video or audio).  
- Recommends more detail on optimization (hyperparameters, convergence behavior).  
- Recommends additional examples of compressed images, including out-of-training-set images, to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Encourages a more detailed comparison with recent deep learning compression methods to strengthen the evaluation.
