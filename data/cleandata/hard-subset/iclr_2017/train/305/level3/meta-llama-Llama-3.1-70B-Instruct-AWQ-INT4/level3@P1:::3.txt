**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The authors present an end-to-end optimized image compression method that outperforms traditional methods such as JPEG and JPEG 2000. The key contribution is a differentiable approximation of the "rate" function, which enables effective training across different rate-distortion trade-offs. This approach allows the system to learn a representation that balances rate and distortion, achieving better bit-rate vs. quality trade-offs. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method through extensive experiments, showing improvements in both objective metrics (PSNR and MS-SSIM) and subjective visual quality.
