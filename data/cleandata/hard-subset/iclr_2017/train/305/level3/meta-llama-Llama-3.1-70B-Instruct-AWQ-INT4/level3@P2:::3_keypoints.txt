- Proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression system: nonlinear analysis transform → uniform quantizer → nonlinear synthesis transform.  
- Transforms are built from three successive stages of convolutional linear filters and nonlinear activations inspired by biological neurons.  
- Entire model is jointly optimized for rate–distortion performance using a variant of stochastic gradient descent.  
- Introduces a differentiable approximation of the "rate" function, enabling effective training across rate–distortion trade-offs.  
- Demonstrates better rate–distortion performance and substantially improved visual quality compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000.  
- Authors provide a convincing response to a pre-review concern about networks learning high-entropy codes, arguing constraints would increase complexity or require variable-rate encoders.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept, based on novelty, effectiveness, and authors’ response.  
- Suggestion: give more details on the optimization process (hyperparameters, convergence behavior).  
- Suggestion: discuss limitations such as need for large training datasets and training computational cost.  
- Suggestion: compare performance against recent deep learning–based image compression methods.  
- Suggestion: include more compressed image examples across varied content types (natural scenes, textures, etc.).  
- Question for authors: clarify choice of nonlinear activation functions and the number of transform stages.  
- Question for authors: explain how the trade-off parameter λ was selected and its effect on rate–distortion.  
- Question for authors: discuss potential extensions/applications beyond image compression (e.g., video, audio) and comparisons with other recent approaches.
