- Proposes end-to-end optimized image compression via nonlinear transform coding, extending rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders/decoders and adaptive entropy coding and relating the approach to VAEs.  
- Method is optimized for MSE; authors note optimizing for perceptual metrics could yield visual improvements.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Reason: Well-motivated approach building on prior rate-distortion work and VAEs.  
- Reason: Significant rate-distortion improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000, especially at low bitrates.  
- Paper is clear and well written, with a transparent description of the optimization framework and experimental setup.  
- Results are thoroughly evaluated (PSNR, MS-SSIM) with detailed discussion of advantages and limitations.  
- Request an ablation study to separate the contributions of adaptive entropy coding versus increased encoder/decoder capacity.  
- Ask for more details on the adaptive entropy coder and its impact on compression performance.  
- Ask for a discussion on why MSE was chosen instead of a perceptual metric.  
- Question: Why are GDN/IGDN nonlinearities more efficient for compression than alternatives like ReLU?  
- Question: How will the approach be extended to optimize perceptual metrics, and what challenges/benefits are expected?  
- Question: Provide details on computational complexity, particularly encoding and decoding costs.
