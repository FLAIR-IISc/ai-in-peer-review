This paper proposes a novel end-to-end optimized image compression method based on nonlinear transform coding. The authors extend prior rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders/decoders and adaptive entropy coding, and relate their approach to Variational Autoencoders (VAEs). The paper is well-written, and the results show significant improvements over JPEG 2000 and other learned codecs.

However, I have some concerns regarding the novelty of the approach. The core rate-distortion optimization was previously published, and the switch from a perceptual metric to MSE may be a step backward compared to prior work. Moreover, the paper does not provide a clear contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoder/decoder capacity, and an ablation study (no adaptation) is needed to clarify this.

The paper also raises some questions. Why optimize MSE instead of a perceptual metric, and why evaluate only with PSNR given the authors' backgrounds? Additionally, more details on the adaptive entropy coder and its effects are required. Equation 10 seems incorrect, as the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ), implying the method is not equivalent to a VAE for non-Euclidean metrics.

Despite these concerns, the paper provides a significant contribution to the field of image compression. The results show a dramatic improvement in visual quality for all images at all bit rates, which is supported by objective quality estimates using MS-SSIM. The paper also provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including a comparison with JPEG and JPEG 2000.

Based on these points, I would like to ask the authors to clarify the following:

* Can you provide more details on the adaptive entropy coder and its effects on the compression performance?
* Can you explain why you chose to optimize MSE instead of a perceptual metric, and why you evaluated only with PSNR?
* Can you provide an ablation study (no adaptation) to clarify the contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoder/decoder capacity?
* Can you correct Equation 10 and provide a clear explanation of the method's equivalence to VAEs for non-Euclidean metrics?

Overall, I would recommend accepting this paper, but with revisions to address the above concerns.

Rating: 8/10

Specific question/problem tackled by the paper: The paper tackles the problem of image compression using nonlinear transform coding and adaptive entropy coding.

Approach well-motivated and well-placed in the literature: The paper provides a comprehensive review of the literature on image compression and VAEs, and clearly explains how their approach extends prior work.

Paper supports claims: The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, including a comparison with JPEG and JPEG 2000, and demonstrates significant improvements in visual quality and compression performance.
