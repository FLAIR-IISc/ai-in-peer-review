- Proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression model using nonlinear analysis and synthesis transforms plus uniform quantization, trained for rate-distortion via SGD.  
- Empirical comparison shows significant rate-distortion and visual-quality improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, calling the approach well-motivated, well-executed, and likely to have strong impact.  
- Paper is very well written and integrates classical rate-distortion theory smoothly into the learning objective.  
- Provides a thorough explanation of classical rate-distortion optimization and its adaptation to deep networks.  
- Comprehensive evaluation includes standard codec comparisons and analysis of visual quality.  
- Raises an interesting conceptual point about the relationship between generative models (minimizing differential entropy) and transform coding (trade-off between model and slack explanations).  
- Requests additional results on classic test images (e.g., Lena, Barbara, Baboon) and citations to state-of-the-art classical methods for broader benchmarking.  
- Asks for clearer positioning and explicit comparison with other neural-network-based image compression methods.  
- Requests more details on optimization (hyperparameters, convergence behavior).  
- Asks the authors to address computational complexity concerns relevant to practical deployment.  
- Requests more information on visual quality evaluation, including subjective tests or user studies.  
- Wants discussion of practical applications, potential limitations, and how the method could be used in practice.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with potential to be seminal if additional results and clarifications are provided.
