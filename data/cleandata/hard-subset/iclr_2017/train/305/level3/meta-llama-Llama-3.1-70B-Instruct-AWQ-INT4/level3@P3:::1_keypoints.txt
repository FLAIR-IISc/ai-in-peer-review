- Proposes an end-to-end optimized deep neural network for image compression, jointly optimizing rate–distortion over training images.  
- Model consists of nonlinear analysis transform, uniform quantizer, and nonlinear synthesis transform in three convolutional+nonlinearity stages inspired by biological neurons.  
- Uses a continuous relaxation of the probability model to enable optimization of the rate–distortion trade-off.  
- Demonstrates better rate–distortion performance and markedly improved visual quality compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000 across bit rates.  
- Paper is well-written and integrates rate–distortion theory smoothly into the objective.  
- Provides a thorough literature review and clear methodological description.  
- Thorough empirical comparison to JPEG 2000, a strong classical baseline.  
- Reviewer decision: accept.  
- Requested additional experiments: report results on standard test images (Lena, Barbara, Baboon) and cite state-of-the-art classical methods.  
- Requested clearer positioning and explicit comparisons between neural-network methods and the best previous classical methods.  
- Requested more detailed analysis of computational complexity and comparisons to other neural-network-based compression methods.  
- Questions for authors: detail computational complexity vs other NN methods, plans to extend to video/audio, and rationale for architectural choices such as GDN/IGDN.
