- Proposes a novel end-to-end optimized image compression method using nonlinear transform coding and adaptive entropy coding.  
- Extends prior rate–distortion optimization to deep encoders/decoders and adaptive entropy coding, and relates the approach to VAEs.  
- Paper is well-written with significant improvements over JPEG 2000 and other learned codecs in visual quality and compression.  
- Results show dramatic visual-quality improvements at all bitrates, supported by objective MS-SSIM estimates and comprehensive comparisons (JPEG, JPEG 2000).  
- Concern about novelty: core rate–distortion optimization was previously published.  
- Concern that switching from a perceptual metric to MSE may be a step backward relative to prior work.  
- Unclear contribution split between adaptive entropy coding and increased encoder/decoder capacity; attribution needs clarification.  
- Requests an ablation study (no adaptation) to isolate the effect of adaptive entropy coding versus model capacity.  
- Asks for more details on the adaptive entropy coder and its quantitative effect on compression performance.  
- Questions why the authors optimized MSE and evaluated only with PSNR despite perceptual-metric background.  
- Notes a potential error in Equation 10: partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ), challenging claimed VAE equivalence for non-Euclidean metrics.  
- Requests correction of Equation 10 and a clear explanation of VAE equivalence (or lack thereof) for non-Euclidean metrics.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to address the above concerns.  
- Rating: 8/10.
