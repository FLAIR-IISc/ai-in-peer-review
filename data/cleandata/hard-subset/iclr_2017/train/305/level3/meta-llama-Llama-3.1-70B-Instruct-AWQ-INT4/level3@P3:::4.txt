This paper proposes an end-to-end learning method for image compression, leveraging rate-distortion optimization with a clever relaxation to learn efficient compression from a natural image database. The authors develop a framework for end-to-end optimization of an image compression model based on nonlinear transforms, which offers improvements in rate-distortion performance over JPEG and JPEG 2000 for most images and bit rates.

I recommend acceptance of this paper. The key reasons for this choice are the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach, as well as the thoroughness of the experimental results and analysis.

The authors' approach is well-motivated, building on previous work in image compression and nonlinear transforms. The use of a generalized divisive normalization (GDN) joint nonlinearity, inspired by models of biological neurons, is a key innovation that allows the model to capture the features and attributes of images that are represented in the statistics of the data. The authors also provide a clear and thorough explanation of the optimization process, including the use of a continuous relaxation of the probability model to allow for stochastic gradient descent.

The experimental results are compelling, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach in achieving better rate-distortion performance than JPEG and JPEG 2000 for most test images, especially at lower bit rates. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the results, including a comparison of the visual quality of the compressed images and an evaluation of the performance using the MS-SSIM index.

One potential area for improvement is in the evaluation of the proposed approach. While the authors provide a thorough comparison with JPEG and JPEG 2000, it would be useful to see a comparison with other recent image compression methods, such as those based on deep learning. Additionally, the authors could provide more detail on the computational resources required to train and test the model, as well as the potential applications of the proposed approach in real-world image compression scenarios.

Some questions that I would like the authors to answer to clarify my understanding of the paper are:

* Can the authors provide more detail on the choice of the GDN joint nonlinearity and its relationship to models of biological neurons?
* How do the authors plan to extend the proposed approach to other types of data, such as video or audio?
* Can the authors provide more information on the computational resources required to train and test the model, as well as the potential applications of the proposed approach in real-world image compression scenarios?

Additional feedback to improve the paper:

* The authors could provide more detail on the optimization process, including the choice of hyperparameters and the convergence of the optimization algorithm.
* The authors could provide more examples of the compressed images, including images that are not included in the training set, to demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed approach.
* The authors could provide a more detailed comparison with other recent image compression methods, including those based on deep learning.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of image compression. With some additional detail and analysis, it has the potential to be even more impactful.
