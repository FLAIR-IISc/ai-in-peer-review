**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

The paper proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression method based on nonlinear transform coding. The method achieves better bit-rate vs. quality trade-offs than established methods like JPEG-2000. The key technical contribution is a differentiable approximation of the "rate" function, enabling effective training across different rate-distortion trade-offs. This approach is likely to have an impact beyond compression for tasks needing differentiable approximations of similar functions.

**Decision: Accept**

The paper provides a well-motivated approach to image compression, and the results demonstrate significant improvements over existing methods. The authors have also provided a thoughtful response to the reviewer's pre-review question, addressing concerns and clarifying the methodology.

**Supporting Arguments**

1. The paper tackles a specific problem in image compression, which is a well-established field with a clear set of challenges.
2. The approach is well-motivated, building on previous work in nonlinear transform coding and variational autoencoders.
3. The results are supported by empirical evidence, demonstrating improvements in rate-distortion performance and visual quality compared to existing methods.

**Additional Feedback**

To further improve the paper, the authors could consider the following:

* Providing more details on the optimization process, including the choice of hyperparameters and the convergence of the algorithm.
* Exploring the use of different nonlinearities, such as rectified linear units or sigmoidal functions, to determine their impact on performance and visual quality.
* Investigating the application of this approach to other types of data, such as video or audio, to demonstrate its broader applicability.

**Questions for the Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the computational resources required for training and testing the model, including the number of GPUs and the training time?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of model interpretability, given the complexity of the nonlinear transforms and the lack of a clear understanding of the underlying representations?
3. Have you considered using other metrics, such as SSIM or VIF, to evaluate the visual quality of the compressed images, in addition to PSNR and MS-SSIM?
