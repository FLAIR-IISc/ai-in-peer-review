- Proposes an end-to-end learning method for image compression framed as a rate-distortion optimization with a relaxation to learn from natural images.
- Shows improved rate-distortion performance and visual quality versus standard methods (JPEG, JPEG 2000).
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Approach is well-motivated, drawing on transform coding and generative model literature.
- Novel use of generalized divisive normalization (GDN) joint nonlinearity inspired by biological neurons.
- Provides detailed description of the optimization framework, analysis/synthesis transforms, and entropy coding scheme.
- Experiments are comprehensive across bit rates and datasets and support the method’s effectiveness.
- Thorough comparison and analysis of relationships to existing generative and transform-coding approaches, highlighting advantages.
- Suggests adding more insight into the optimization process, including hyperparameter choices (filters, downsampling, number of stages).
- Requests information on convergence behavior and typical optimization properties.
- Asks for analysis of computational complexity (time and memory for training and testing).
- Inquires about extending the method to other data types such as video or audio.
