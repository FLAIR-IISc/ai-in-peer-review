This paper presents a novel approach to image compression using nonlinear transforms, optimized end-to-end for rate-distortion performance. The authors propose a framework that jointly optimizes the analysis and synthesis transforms, as well as the entropy code, to achieve a better trade-off between rate and distortion. The method is based on a cascade of convolutional linear filters and nonlinear activation functions, inspired by models of biological neurons.

The paper claims to contribute to the field of image compression by providing a more efficient and effective method for compressing images. The authors argue that their approach is more flexible and powerful than traditional transform coding methods, such as JPEG and JPEG 2000, which are based on linear transforms and separate optimization of the transform, quantizer, and entropy code.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the rate-distortion performance of their method, comparing it to JPEG and JPEG 2000 on a variety of images. The results show that the proposed method generally exhibits better rate-distortion performance than the standard methods, especially at lower bit rates.

The reviewer finds this paper to be the most convincing paper on image compression with deep neural networks they have read. The paper is very well written and integrates rate–distortion theory smoothly into the objective. The comparison to JPEG2000 is appropriate and stronger than comparing only to JPEG. The reviewer expects the paper to have strong impact.

However, the reviewer requests inclusion of results on standard images (Lena, Barbara, Baboon) and references to state-of-the-art classical methods. The reviewer also asks for clearer positioning and explicit comparisons between neural-network methods and the best previous classical methods.

To address the reviewer's comments, the authors could provide additional results on standard images and include references to state-of-the-art classical methods. The authors could also provide a clearer positioning of their method in relation to other neural-network methods and classical methods, highlighting the advantages and limitations of their approach.
