This paper proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression method using nonlinear transforms. The method consists of a nonlinear analysis transformation, a uniform quantizer, and a nonlinear synthesis transformation. The transforms are constructed in three successive stages of convolutional linear filters and nonlinear activation functions. The joint nonlinearity is chosen to implement a form of local gain control, inspired by those used to model biological neurons. The entire model is jointly optimized for rate-distortion performance over a database of training images using a variant of stochastic gradient descent.

The paper claims to contribute to the field of image compression by introducing a novel method that outperforms standard compression methods such as JPEG and JPEG 2000. The authors also relate their approach to variational autoencoders (VAEs) and highlight the differences between their method and VAEs.

I recommend rejecting this paper, with the main reasons being the limited novelty of the approach and the lack of clarity on the contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoder/decoder capacity.

The paper builds upon the authors' prior work on rate-distortion optimization, and the core approach was previously published. While the extension to deep encoders/decoders and adaptive entropy coding is a significant improvement, it may not be enough to warrant a new paper. The authors should have clearly highlighted the novel contributions of this paper compared to their prior work.

The switch from a perceptual metric to mean squared error (MSE) may be a step backward compared to prior work. The authors should have provided a clear justification for this change and evaluated the method using a perceptual metric in addition to PSNR.

The results of the paper are strong, and the method visibly improves over JPEG 2000. However, the contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoder/decoder capacity is unclear. An ablation study (no adaptation) is needed to understand the impact of each component.

The paper is very well written, and the authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of their method. However, more details on the adaptive entropy coder and its effects are required. The reviewer may raise the score if this information is provided.

In particular, the authors should address the following questions:

* What are the novel contributions of this paper compared to prior work on rate-distortion optimization?
* Why was MSE chosen as the distortion metric instead of a perceptual metric?
* What is the contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoder/decoder capacity?
* How does the adaptive entropy coder affect the performance of the method?
* Can the authors provide more details on the implementation of the adaptive entropy coder?

Additional feedback:

* The authors should consider providing more details on the training procedure, including the choice of hyperparameters and the optimization algorithm used.
* The authors should provide more examples of compressed images at different bit rates to demonstrate the effectiveness of their method.
* The authors should consider comparing their method to other learned compression methods, such as the one proposed by Toderici et al.
* The authors should provide more details on the computational complexity of their method and its potential applications in real-world scenarios.
