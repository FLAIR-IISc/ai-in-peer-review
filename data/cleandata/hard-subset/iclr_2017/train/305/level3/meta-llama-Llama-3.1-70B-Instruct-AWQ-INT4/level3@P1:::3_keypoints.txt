1. Paper proposes end-to-end optimized image compression that outperforms JPEG/JPEG2000.  
2. Main contribution is a differentiable approximation of the rate function enabling training across rate-distortion trade-offs.  
3. Method learns representations balancing rate and distortion for improved bitrate vs. quality.  
4. Extensive experiments show improvements in objective metrics (PSNR, MS-SSIM) and subjective visual quality.  
5. Recommendation: accept.  
6. Technical novelty: differentiable rate approximation is novel and potentially broadly impactful.  
7. Experimental evaluation: thorough, well-designed comparisons with established methods.  
8. Paper is well-motivated and clearly explained, with thoughtful responses to reviewer queries.  
9. Experiments cover diverse images, bitrates, and metrics, showing consistent improvements.  
10. Suggestion: provide more details on network architecture and optimization process.  
11. Suggestion: explore connections to other compression techniques (generative models, sparse coding).  
12. Request clarification on choice and limitations of the differentiable rate approximation.  
13. Question: how to prevent the network from producing codes in a fixed range to maximize entropy (meeting upper bound)?  
14. Question: provide details on computational complexity compared to traditional compression methods.
