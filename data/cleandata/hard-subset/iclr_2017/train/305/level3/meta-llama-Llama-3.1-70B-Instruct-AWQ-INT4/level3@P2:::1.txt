This paper proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression method based on nonlinear transforms. The authors construct a compression model consisting of a nonlinear analysis transformation, a uniform quantizer, and a nonlinear synthesis transformation, and jointly optimize the entire model for rate-distortion performance using stochastic gradient descent. The method is compared to standard image compression methods, including JPEG and JPEG 2000, and shows significant improvements in rate-distortion performance and visual quality.

I accept this paper because it presents a well-motivated and well-executed approach to image compression using deep neural networks. The paper is very well written and integrates rate-distortion theory smoothly into the objective. The comparison to JPEG 2000 is appropriate and stronger than comparing only to JPEG. I expect this paper to have a strong impact on the field of image compression.

One of the key strengths of this paper is its clear and concise presentation of the rate-distortion theory and its application to image compression. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the classical rate-distortion optimization problem and how it can be adapted to the context of deep neural networks. The paper also presents a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including comparisons to standard image compression methods and an analysis of the visual quality of the compressed images.

The paper also raises some interesting questions about the relationship between generative models and transform coding methods. The authors note that while generative models aim to minimize differential entropy, transform coding methods aim to achieve the best trade-off between having the model explain the data and having the slack term explain the data. This highlights the need for further research on the relationship between these two approaches and how they can be combined to achieve better results.

To improve the paper, I would like to see some additional results on standard images (e.g., Lena, Barbara, Baboon) and references to state-of-the-art classical methods. This would provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method and help to establish its position within the broader field of image compression.

Additionally, I would like to see a clearer positioning of the proposed method within the context of neural-network methods for image compression. The authors mention that their method is related to previous work on generative models, but a more explicit comparison between these approaches would be helpful.

Some specific questions I would like the authors to address in their response are:

* Can the authors provide more details on the optimization process, including the choice of hyperparameters and the convergence of the algorithm?
* How do the authors plan to address the issue of computational complexity, which is a significant concern for many image compression applications?
* Can the authors provide more information on the visual quality of the compressed images, including any subjective evaluations or user studies?
* How do the authors see their method being used in practice, and what are the potential applications and limitations of their approach?

Overall, this is a strong paper that presents a significant contribution to the field of image compression. With some additional results and clarifications, I believe it has the potential to be a seminal work in this area.
