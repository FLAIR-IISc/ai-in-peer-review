- Proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression method using nonlinear transforms with improved rate-distortion vs JPEG/JPEG 2000.
- Extends prior rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders/decoders and relates the approach to VAEs.
- Key contribution: cascade of biologically-inspired nonlinear transformations optimized for compression.
- Results show visible perceptual-quality improvements over JPEG 2000 and good modeling of image statistics.
- Novelty is limited because the core rate-distortion approach was previously published.
- Choice to optimize MSE instead of a perceptual metric is questionable and may be a step backward.
- Evaluation is limited to PSNR and should include perceptual metrics like SSIM or MS-SSIM.
- Unclear how much benefit comes from adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoder/decoder capacity; an ablation (no adaptation) is needed.
- More details are required on the adaptive entropy coder and its effects.
- Equation 10 appears incorrect: the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ), calling into question the claimed VAE equivalence for non-Euclidean metrics.
- Decision: Reject (with potential for resubmission).
- Main reasons for rejection: limited novelty, MSE choice over perceptual metrics, and evaluation limited to PSNR.
- Requested actions/questions: justify MSE optimization, clarify novel contributions, provide adaptive coder details, perform ablation studies, and fix/address Eq.10 discrepancy.
