**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

The paper presents an end-to-end optimized image compression method based on nonlinear transform coding. The authors extend prior rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders/decoders and adaptive entropy coding, and relate their approach to Variational Autoencoders (VAEs). The method is optimized for mean squared error (MSE) as a distortion metric, but the authors note that optimizing for a perceptual metric may lead to additional visual improvements.

**Decision to Accept or Reject**

Based on the evaluation criteria, I decide to **Accept** the paper. The main reasons for this decision are:

1. The paper presents a well-motivated approach to image compression, building on prior work in rate-distortion optimization and VAEs.
2. The experimental results demonstrate significant improvements in rate-distortion performance over JPEG and JPEG 2000, especially at lower bit rates.

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper provides a clear and well-written description of the proposed method, including the optimization framework and the experimental setup. The results are thoroughly evaluated using various metrics, including PSNR and MS-SSIM, and the authors provide a detailed discussion of the advantages and limitations of their approach.

**Additional Feedback**

To further improve the paper, I would like to see:

1. A more detailed analysis of the contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoder/decoder capacity. An ablation study (no adaptation) would help clarify this point.
2. More details on the adaptive entropy coder and its effects on the compression performance.
3. A discussion on why the authors chose to optimize for MSE instead of a perceptual metric, given their backgrounds in image compression and perception.

**Questions for the Authors**

1. Can you provide more insights on why the GDN/IGDN nonlinearities are more efficient for compression than other activation functions, such as rectified linear units?
2. How do you plan to extend your approach to optimize for perceptual metrics, and what are the potential challenges and benefits of doing so?
3. Can you provide more details on the computational complexity of your method, especially during the encoding and decoding processes?
