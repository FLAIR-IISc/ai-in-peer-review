- Proposes a novel end-to-end image compression framework that jointly optimizes analysis/synthesis transforms and the entropy code using cascaded convolutional filters and nonlinear activations.  
- Claims greater flexibility and effectiveness than traditional linear transform coding (e.g., JPEG, JPEG 2000).  
- Paper is well written with a clear method description and integrated rate–distortion theory.  
- Thorough rate–distortion evaluation shows generally better performance than JPEG/JPEG 2000, especially at low bit rates.  
- Reviewer considers this the most convincing neural-network paper on image compression they have read and expects strong impact.  
- Reviewer requests results on standard images (Lena, Barbara, Baboon) for easier comparison.  
- Reviewer asks for references to and comparisons with state-of-the-art classical methods (e.g., JPEG-LS, JPEG 2000).  
- Reviewer asks for clearer positioning relative to other neural-network methods (e.g., Toderici et al.).  
- Reviewer requests explicit comparisons highlighting advantages and limitations versus the best previous classical methods; suggests adding additional results and clearer positioning.
