

**Review of "END-TO-END OPTIMIZED IMAGE COMPRESSION"**

1. **Summary**: The paper presents an end-to-end image compression framework using nonlinear transforms with generalized divisive normalization (GDN) and adaptive entropy coding. It extends prior rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders/decoders, relates the approach to variational autoencoders, and demonstrates superior rate-distortion performance and visual quality compared to JPEG 2000 and other learned codecs. The paper is exceptionally well-written and provides thorough experimental validation.

2. **Decision**: Reject. The key reasons are limited novelty and an unjustified methodological choice.

3. **Supporting Arguments**: 
   - The core rate-distortion optimization approach was previously published by the authors (Ballé et al., 2016), making the novelty limited despite the incorporation of adaptive entropy coding.
   - The switch from perceptual metrics (used in the authors' prior work) to MSE represents a step backward, especially given their demonstrated expertise in perceptual quality. The paper fails to justify why MSE was chosen over perceptual metrics despite the authors' background, and evaluates only with PSNR (not MS-SSIM as claimed in the abstract).
   - Equation 10 appears incorrect: the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ), meaning the method is not equivalent to a VAE for non-Euclidean metrics as claimed.
   - The contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper network capacity is unclear without an ablation study (no adaptation).

4. **Additional Feedback**: 
   - Clarify the relationship to VAEs and correct Equation 10 to address the partition function dependency.
   - Justify the MSE choice and provide MS-SSIM evaluations to align with the paper's visual quality claims.
   - Conduct an ablation study to isolate the contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus network capacity.
   - Provide more detailed analysis of the adaptive entropy coder's implementation and impact.

**Questions for Authors**:
1. Why was MSE chosen over perceptual metrics despite your prior work demonstrating perceptual benefits?
2. How does the paper's claimed VAE equivalence hold when Equation 10's partition function depends on g_s(y; θ)?
3. Can you provide results from an ablation study comparing adaptive entropy coding versus a fixed entropy code with identical network capacity?
