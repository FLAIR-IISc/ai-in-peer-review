- Proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression framework using deep neural networks with adaptive entropy coding.  
- Extends prior rate-distortion optimization to nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms (GDN layers) with uniform scalar quantization and continuous relaxation for gradient-based training.  
- Shows a VAE-like interpretation under specific conditions but notes a caveat about that relationship.  
- Demonstrates superior rate-distortion performance and visual quality versus JPEG and JPEG 2000, supported by MS-SSIM results.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and practically thorough.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong practical contribution and significant empirical improvements despite limited novelty.  
- Key strength: complete, practical compression pipeline that convincingly outperforms standard codecs.  
- Contribution beyond prior work: nontrivial extension to deep networks with adaptive entropy coding and a full implementation.  
- Concern: novelty limited since core rate-distortion approach was previously published (Ballé et al., 2016).  
- Choice of MSE (and PSNR evaluation) is reasonable for codec comparison but raises questions about perceptual quality evaluation.  
- Request: include an ablation study to separate the impact of adaptive entropy coding from increased encoder/decoder capacity.  
- Technical correction: Equation 10 needs clarification — the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ), not just y.  
- Request: justify use of MSE over perceptual metrics given authors' prior work on perceptual compression.  
- Request: provide more implementation details on the adaptive entropy coder (how CABAC is adapted to the learned representation).  
- Questions for authors: why MSE, can you provide the adaptive-coder vs capacity ablation, how does quantization relaxation affect practical implementation, and clarify the VAE/partition-function relationship?
