- Proposes end-to-end image compression using nonlinear transforms with GDN and adaptive entropy coding, relating to VAEs and claiming superior rate-distortion and visual quality vs JPEG2000 and other learned codecs.
- Decision: Reject due to limited novelty and an unjustified methodological choice.
- Core rate-distortion optimization was previously published (Ballé et al., 2016), so novelty is limited despite adding adaptive entropy coding.
- Switching from perceptual metrics to MSE is a step backward; authors do not justify MSE and only evaluate with PSNR, not MS-SSIM as claimed.
- Equation 10 appears incorrect: the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ), calling into question the claimed VAE equivalence for non-Euclidean metrics.
- No ablation study is provided, so it’s unclear whether improvements come from adaptive entropy coding or from increased network capacity.
- Ask authors to clarify the relationship to VAEs and correct Equation 10 to address the partition function dependency.
- Ask authors to justify choosing MSE and to provide MS-SSIM evaluations to substantiate visual quality claims.
- Ask authors to perform an ablation study isolating adaptive entropy coding versus a fixed entropy code with identical network capacity.
- Request more detailed analysis of the adaptive entropy coder’s implementation and its impact.
- Question: Why was MSE chosen over perceptual metrics given the authors’ prior work on perceptual quality?
- Question: How does the claimed VAE equivalence hold when Equation 10’s partition function depends on g_s(y; θ)?
- Question: Can you provide ablation results comparing adaptive entropy coding versus a fixed entropy code with identical network capacity?
