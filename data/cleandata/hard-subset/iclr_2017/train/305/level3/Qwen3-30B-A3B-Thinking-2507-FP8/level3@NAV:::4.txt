

**Review of "END-TO-END OPTIMIZED IMAGE COMPRESSION"**

1. **Summary of Contributions**: This paper presents a novel end-to-end learning framework for image compression that jointly optimizes the analysis transform, quantizer, and synthesis transform for rate-distortion performance. The authors introduce a clever relaxation of the quantization step using additive uniform noise to enable gradient-based optimization, while maintaining a direct connection to the discrete compression problem. Their method, leveraging biologically-inspired GDN/IGDN nonlinearities, demonstrates superior rate-distortion performance and significantly improved visual quality compared to standard JPEG and JPEG 2000 methods across multiple test images.

2. **Decision**: Accept. The paper makes a strong contribution to the image compression literature by providing a theoretically grounded, end-to-end trainable framework that outperforms established methods both quantitatively and qualitatively.

3. **Supporting Arguments**: 
   - The paper clearly addresses the fundamental problem of optimizing the rate-distortion trade-off in image compression, which has traditionally been handled through separate optimization of transform, quantizer, and entropy code components.
   - The approach is well-motivated and well-placed in the literature, with careful distinction from related work on generative models and variational autoencoders. The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of their relaxation technique and its relationship to rate-distortion optimization.
   - The experimental results are compelling, with rigorous comparisons across multiple metrics (PSNR, MS-SSIM) and diverse test images. The visual quality improvements are particularly impressive and consistently observed across all bit rates and image types.

4. **Additional Feedback**: 
   - While the paper demonstrates superior visual quality, it would be valuable to include more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000, particularly for real-time applications.
   - The authors might consider exploring the impact of different entropy coding schemes on the final bit rate, as the current implementation uses a relatively simple CABAC-based approach.

**Clarifying Questions**:
1. Could the authors provide more details about the computational cost of the proposed method during inference compared to standard codecs, particularly for embedded systems?
2. How sensitive is the performance to the specific choice of GDN/IGDN parameters, and would simpler nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU) yield comparable results with reduced model complexity?
