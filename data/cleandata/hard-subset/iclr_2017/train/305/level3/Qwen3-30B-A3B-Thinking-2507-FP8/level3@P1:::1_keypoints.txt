- Presents an end-to-end optimized image compression framework that integrates rate-distortion theory with deep learning.  
- Proposes nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms with cascaded GDN operations and a continuous relaxation of quantization for joint rate-distortion optimization.  
- Implements an effective form of vector quantization via nonlinear transforms.  
- Demonstrates superior rate-distortion performance compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000 across multiple test images.  
- Shows improved PSNR and significantly better visual quality at all bit rates, validated by MS-SSIM despite MSE-based optimization.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with strong enthusiasm.  
- Core contribution is the theoretically grounded integration of rate-distortion principles into end-to-end deep learning optimization.  
- Continuous relaxation of quantization enables gradient-based training; analysis relates the method to variational autoencoders.  
- Comparison to JPEG 2000 is a particularly appropriate and valuable benchmark.  
- Technical execution and clarity are excellent; GDN motivation linked to biological vision and natural image statistics.  
- Implementation details (CABAC entropy coding, thorough evaluation) strengthen credibility.  
- Visual quality improvements are striking and consistent across images and bit rates.  
- Anticipated substantial impact on compression research and broader deep learning; framework is flexible across the rate-distortion curve.  
- Recommendation to include results on standard test images (Lena, Barbara, Baboon) for comparability with prior work.  
- Suggest more explicit positioning against other classical methods (e.g., JPEG XR, WebP).  
- Request clearer comparison and positioning relative to Toderici et al. (2016) and other neural compression work.  
- Technical question: ask for deeper theoretical insight into why GDN/IGDN is more parameter-/stage-efficient than alternatives like ReLU.  
- Technical question: request characterization of approximation errors from the relaxed loss, especially under coarse quantization/very low bit rates, and their effect on final performance.  
- Overall assessment: exceptionally well-written, scientifically rigorous, extensively validated, and a significant advance in image compression.
