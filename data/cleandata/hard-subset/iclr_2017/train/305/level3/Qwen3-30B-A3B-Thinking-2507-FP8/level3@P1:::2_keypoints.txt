- Presents an end-to-end optimized image compression system using a cascade of nonlinear transforms (with GDN/IGDN) and uniform quantization, optimized via a continuous relaxation of quantization.
- Jointly optimizes the model for rate-distortion, showing improved RD performance and visually superior results over JPEG and JPEG 2000 across all bit rates.
- Method is well-motivated by limitations of standard transform coding; continuous relaxation technique is sound and clearly explained.
- Decision: Accept (with minor revisions).
- Key reasons for acceptance: consistent, significant improvements in objective (PSNR) and subjective (MS-SSIM, visual quality) metrics.
- Experimental validation is thorough and well-executed across multiple test images and datasets.
- Supporting evidence: extensive comparisons to JPEG/JPEG 2000, striking visual improvements that avoid blocking and ringing artifacts of linear transforms.
- Authors explain relationship to variational autoencoders (VAEs) and highlight key differences in optimization objectives.
- Implementation and experimental details (including entropy coding) are sufficiently thorough for reproducibility.
- Requested clarification: relationship to VAEs, specifically that Equation 10 appears to incorrectly treat the partition function as independent of g_s(y; θ).
- Suggested ablation: isolate the effect of adaptive entropy coding versus simply increasing network capacity (more channels/stages).
- Requested rationale: explain why MSE was used instead of a perceptual metric and how that choice affects reported visual quality, given prior work on perceptual metrics.
- Contribution: significant practical advance in image compression via deep end-to-end optimization, with the ability to operate at any RD point using a single λ.
- Writing: clear and well-organized; novelty somewhat limited by prior RD work but advances the state of the art through end-to-end approach and adaptive entropy coding.
