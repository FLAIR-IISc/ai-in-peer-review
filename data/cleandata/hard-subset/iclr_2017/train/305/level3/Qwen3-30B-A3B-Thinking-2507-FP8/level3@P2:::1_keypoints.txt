- Presents an end-to-end optimized image compression framework using nonlinear transforms and GDN, with continuous relaxation of quantization for gradient-based training.  
- Jointly optimizes analysis transform, quantizer, and synthesis transform to improve rate–distortion performance.  
- Demonstrates superior rate–distortion and visual quality vs. JPEG and JPEG 2000 across bit rates.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Strong empirical gains in MS-SSIM and qualitative examples; continuous quantization relaxation is elegant and preserves connection to discrete compression.  
- Well-written, technically rigorous paper that connects rate–distortion theory with deep learning and addresses limitations of prior generative-model approaches.  
- Suggestion: Add results on classic test images (Lena, Barbara, Baboon) to align with compression community benchmarks.  
- Suggestion: Provide explicit comparisons to top classical compression methods beyond JPEG/JPEG 2000.  
- Suggestion: Discuss computational complexity of encoding/decoding to clarify practical trade-offs.  
- Suggestion: Expand on comparisons with other activation functions and their impact on compression quality and model complexity.  
- Question: How does the method compare in rate–distortion optimality to Toderici et al. (2016)?  
- Question: Can you provide more visual examples or perceptual metrics for standard test images to substantiate the claimed perceptual advantages?  
- Question: How does the RGB model differ from the grayscale model, and were there notable performance differences?  
- Question: Can you provide specific analysis linking GDN parameters to natural image statistics to support the biological-statistics claim?
