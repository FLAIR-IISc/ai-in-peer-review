

**Review of "END-TO-END OPTIMIZED IMAGE COMPRESSION"**

1. The paper presents an end-to-end trained image compression system that achieves superior rate-distortion trade-offs compared to established methods like JPEG-2000. The key contribution is a differentiable approximation of the "rate" function that enables effective joint optimization of analysis and synthesis transforms across the entire rate-distortion curve. The authors demonstrate significant improvements in visual quality across all bit rates, supported by both objective MS-SSIM metrics and visual comparisons.

2. I recommend acceptance. The paper makes a strong technical contribution with a novel differentiable rate approximation, and provides compelling empirical evidence of its effectiveness across multiple image datasets.

3. Supporting arguments: The differentiable rate approach is well-motivated and addresses a fundamental challenge in end-to-end compression optimization. The authors provide thorough experimental validation showing consistent improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000 across diverse image sets, with visual quality improvements that persist across all bit rates. The paper also thoughtfully addresses the relationship to variational autoencoders while highlighting key differences that make their approach more suitable for compression. The authors' response to the pre-review question was particularly thoughtful.

4. Additional feedback: While the paper demonstrates impressive results, it would benefit from more detailed discussion of computational complexity during training versus standard compression methods. The authors might also clarify why three stages of transforms were chosen rather than a different architecture depth, and how this choice impacts the model's efficiency. The paper could further strengthen its position by explicitly addressing how the GDN/IGDN nonlinearities specifically contribute to performance beyond what simpler activation functions could achieve. Finally, a more detailed comparison of the actual bit rates achieved with the entropy code versus theoretical entropy estimates would enhance the evaluation.
