1. Introduces an end-to-end trained image compression system with a differentiable rate approximation that enables joint optimization and yields superior rate–distortion and visual quality (MS-SSIM and visuals) over JPEG-2000 across bit rates.

2. Recommendation: Accept — novel technical contribution with strong empirical evidence across multiple datasets.

3. Supporting points: The differentiable rate formulation is well-motivated, experiments consistently outperform JPEG/JPEG2000, relation to VAEs is clarified, and authors responded thoughtfully to pre-review questions.

4. Suggested improvements: Clarify training computational complexity, justify the choice of three transform stages and their efficiency impact, explain the specific contribution of GDN/IGDN vs simpler activations, and compare actual entropy-coded bitrates to theoretical entropy estimates.
