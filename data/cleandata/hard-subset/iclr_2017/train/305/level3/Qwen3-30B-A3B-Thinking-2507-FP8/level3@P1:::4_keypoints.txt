- Presents an end-to-end learned image compression system optimizing analysis transform, quantizer, and synthesis transform jointly for rate-distortion.
- Introduces a continuous relaxation of quantization (additive uniform noise) enabling gradient-based optimization through the quantizer.
- Uses generalized divisive normalization (GDN) nonlinearities inspired by biological vision to improve performance.
- Optimizes for MSE and achieves superior rate-distortion performance versus JPEG and JPEG 2000 across images and bit rates.
- Demonstrates particularly strong visual quality improvements, reducing blocking, ringing, and aliasing artifacts.
- Provides theoretical analysis linking the approach to variational autoencoders while highlighting important differences tailored to compression.
- Implements real entropy coding (CABAC) for fair, practical comparisons rather than relying on theoretical rate estimates.
- Reports comprehensive experimental validation with objective metrics (PSNR, MS-SSIM) and extensive qualitative examples.
- Transparently shares full test results online and carefully discusses challenges in comparing rate-distortion curves across images.
- Positions the work well in literature, differentiating from other deep learning and generative compression approaches.
- Recommends acceptance based on theoretical soundness, strong empirical results, and clear motivation.
- Suggests deeper analysis of the entropy coding component and comparisons to more advanced coding used in JPEG 2000.
- Requests discussion of computational complexity for training and inference to assess practical feasibility.
- Proposes additional ablation studies on activation functions beyond GDN/IGDN to validate the role of nonlinearities.
- Recommends reporting specific statistics on which image categories or bit-rate ranges show the largest improvements.
