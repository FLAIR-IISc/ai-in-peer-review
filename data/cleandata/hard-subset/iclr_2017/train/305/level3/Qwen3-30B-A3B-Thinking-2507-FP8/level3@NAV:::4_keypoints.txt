- Proposes an end-to-end learning framework that jointly optimizes analysis transform, quantizer, and synthesis transform using an additive uniform-noise relaxation and GDN/IGDN nonlinearities, outperforming JPEG/JPEG 2000 in rate-distortion and visual quality.  
- Decision: Accept — novel, theoretically grounded, and empirically strong contribution.  
- Addresses the core rate-distortion optimization problem by avoiding separate optimization of transform, quantizer, and entropy code.  
- Well-motivated, clearly positioned relative to related work, with thorough theoretical analysis of the quantization relaxation.  
- Strong experimental results across PSNR and MS-SSIM and diverse images, showing consistent visual improvements at all bitrates.  
- Requests more detailed analysis of computational complexity compared to JPEG/JPEG 2000, especially for real-time applications.  
- Suggests exploring the effect of different entropy coding schemes beyond the currently used CABAC-based approach.  
- Clarifying Q1: Asks for inference computational cost details versus standard codecs, particularly for embedded systems.  
- Clarifying Q2: Asks how sensitive results are to GDN/IGDN parameter choices and whether simpler nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU) could achieve similar performance with lower complexity.
