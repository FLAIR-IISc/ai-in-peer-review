

# Review for "END-TO-END OPTIMIZED IMAGE COMPRESSION"

## 1. Summary
The paper presents an end-to-end optimized image compression framework using deep neural networks. It extends prior rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders/decoders with adaptive entropy coding, building upon the authors' earlier work. The method employs a nonlinear analysis transform (with GDN layers), uniform scalar quantization, and a nonlinear synthesis transform, all jointly optimized for rate-distortion performance. The authors provide a continuous relaxation of the quantization step to enable gradient-based optimization, and they demonstrate that their approach can be related to VAEs under specific conditions. Crucially, the authors show that their method achieves superior rate-distortion performance compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000 across multiple test images, with particularly notable improvements in visual quality that are supported by MS-SSIM metrics. The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly articulates both the technical approach and its practical implications.

## 2. Decision
Accept. The paper makes a strong practical contribution to image compression with clear, well-executed results that advance the state of the art. While novelty is limited (as the core rate-distortion approach was previously published), the paper provides a complete, practical implementation that demonstrates significant improvements over standard methods.

## 3. Supporting Arguments
The key strength of this paper is its practical contribution to image compression. The authors successfully demonstrate a complete end-to-end compression pipeline that outperforms standard codecs (JPEG and JPEG 2000) in both rate-distortion performance and visual quality. Their results are compelling and clearly presented, with additional examples available online for verification. The paper's clarity and thoroughness make it highly valuable to the community.

While the novelty is limited (as the core rate-distortion optimization approach was previously published in Ballé et al., 2016), the paper makes a significant contribution by extending this approach to deep neural networks with adaptive entropy coding. This extension is not trivial, as it requires solving practical challenges of implementing a complete compression pipeline. The authors acknowledge the connection to prior work, which is appropriate.

The paper's decision to use MSE rather than a perceptual metric is reasonable for a practical comparison with standard codecs, though this does raise questions about the visual quality improvements (as noted in the key points). The paper does demonstrate that visual quality improvements hold across all bit rates, which is a strong empirical finding.

## 4. Additional Feedback
To strengthen the paper, I recommend addressing the following points:

1. **Clarify the contribution of adaptive entropy coding**: The paper would benefit from an ablation study showing the impact of adaptive entropy coding versus simply increasing encoder/decoder capacity. This would help distinguish whether the performance gains come from the adaptive coding or from the deeper network architecture.

2. **Address Equation 10**: The current formulation implies the method is equivalent to a VAE for non-Euclidean metrics, but the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ), not just y. This needs clarification to avoid misleading readers about the VAE relationship.

3. **Justify MSE optimization**: Given the authors' background in perceptual metrics (as seen in Ballé et al., 2016), please elaborate on why MSE was chosen over a perceptual metric for this work. The paper should also discuss why PSNR is the primary evaluation metric when the authors previously demonstrated the value of perceptual metrics.

4. **Provide more details on the adaptive entropy coder**: The paper mentions the use of CABAC but doesn't fully explain how it's adapted to the learned representation. More details on the entropy coding process and its impact on the results would strengthen the paper.

**Questions for authors:**
- Why was MSE chosen as the distortion metric instead of a perceptual metric, given the authors' prior work on perceptual image compression?
- Can you provide the ablation study comparing adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoder/decoder capacity?
- How does the continuous relaxation of the quantization step affect the practical implementation of the compression pipeline?
- Could you clarify the relationship between the VAE interpretation and the actual compression method, particularly regarding the partition function dependency in Equation 10?
