- Novel end-to-end image compression that jointly learns analysis transform, quantizer, and synthesis transform using a continuous relaxation of quantization for gradient-based optimization.  
- Employs biologically inspired nonlinearities (GDN/IGDN) to model natural image statistics.  
- Demonstrates substantial gains over JPEG and JPEG 2000 in MS-SSIM and visual quality across bit rates.  
- Decision: Accept — strong contribution with rigorous experimental support.  
- Well-motivated solution to the rate–distortion tradeoff via end-to-end optimization.  
- Properly situated in literature and clearly distinguishes from prior deep-learning compression work (Gregor et al., Toderici et al.).  
- Rigorous experiments on Kodak with PSNR/MS-SSIM, extensive visual examples, and ablation studies highlighting GDN’s role.  
- Provides useful connections between rate–distortion optimization and generative models, clarifying differences from VAEs.  
- Suggestion: include detailed discussion of training vs. inference computational cost for larger network configurations.  
- Suggestion: compare to more sophisticated entropy coding methods beyond the simple CABAC used.  
- Suggestion: discuss potential extensions to video compression.  
- Question: provide specific visualizations showing which image features the method captures better than JPEG/JPEG 2000.
