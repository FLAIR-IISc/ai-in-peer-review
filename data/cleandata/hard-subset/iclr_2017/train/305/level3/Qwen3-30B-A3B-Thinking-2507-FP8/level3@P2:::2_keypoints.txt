- Proposes end-to-end image compression using cascaded conv layers with GDN/IGDN and a continuous relaxation of quantization; reports better rate-distortion than JPEG/JPEG2000, especially MS-SSIM.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Novelty is limited—approach builds directly on authors' prior Ballé et al. (2016), with unclear advancement.  
- Optimizing for MSE rather than a perceptual metric is unjustified and surprising given authors' prior perceptual work; no evidence MSE yields better perceptual quality.  
- Contribution of adaptive entropy coding is unclear due to no ablation separating its effect from increased network capacity.  
- Technical error in Equation 10: the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ), breaking the claimed VAE equivalence for non-Euclidean metrics.  
- Recommend an ablation study comparing adaptive entropy coder to simpler entropy coders.  
- Recommend stronger justification for choosing MSE over perceptual metrics and evidence linking MSE optimization to visual quality.  
- Recommend clarifying the specific contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper network capacity via ablation.  
- Question: Why optimize MSE instead of a perceptual metric, and can you provide evidence MSE improves perceptual quality?  
- Question: How is the continuous proxy for quantization novel compared to Ballé et al. (2016)?  
- Question: Can you provide results comparing the method with and without adaptive entropy coding?  
- Question: How do you address the Equation 10 issue where the partition function depends on g_s(y; θ), invalidating the VAE equivalence for non-Euclidean metrics?
