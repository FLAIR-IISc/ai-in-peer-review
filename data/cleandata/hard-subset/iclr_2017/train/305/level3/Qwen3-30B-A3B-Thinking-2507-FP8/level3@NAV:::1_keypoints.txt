1. Summary: Proposes an end-to-end optimized DNN image compression framework using nonlinear transforms and GDN that integrates rate–distortion into the loss and outperforms JPEG/JPEG 2000 in PSNR, MS-SSIM, and visual quality.

2. Decision: Accept — strong theoretical grounding and robust empirical validation addressing lossy image compression.

3. Supporting arguments: Directly optimizes rate–distortion via end-to-end training, is well-motivated with clear literature placement distinct from classical and DL methods, and shows consistent empirical gains over JPEG 2000 across metrics and datasets.

4. Additional feedback: Include results on standard test images (Lena, Barbara, Baboon), compare to more classical state-of-the-art codecs beyond JPEG 2000, and add a clearer positioning statement versus top classical approaches.

5. Questions for authors: Provide rate–distortion curves for standard images and comparisons to recent classical codecs (e.g., BPG, HEIF), and clarify how the method compares to the best non-neural approaches.
