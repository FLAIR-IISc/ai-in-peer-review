- Presents an end-to-end optimized image compression system that outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 in rate-distortion.
- Introduces a continuous relaxation of quantization enabling differentiable training of analysis/quantizer/synthesis via SGD.
- Provides strong motivation and clear placement within prior work, distinguishing from VAEs and generative models.
- Justifies use of generalized divisive normalization (GDN) with prior image-statistics and density-estimation evidence.
- Demonstrates consistent PSNR and MS-SSIM improvements and visually superior reconstructions across bit rates.
- Implements a proper entropy code and reports actual bit rates rather than relying on approximations.
- Ablation studies show GDN/IGDN nonlinearities outperform simpler activations with fewer parameters.
- Differentiable rate approximation is the key technical contribution with broader applicability to rate-distortion problems.
- Validates that the relaxed loss closely approximates actual rate-distortion empirically and supports end-to-end optimization.
- Decision: ACCEPT.
- Key reasons for acceptance: novel technical solution to a longstanding problem and strong empirical evidence.
- Suggested improvement: include more detailed comparisons with other neural compression work (e.g., Toderici et al., Gregor et al.).
- Suggested improvement: analyze computational complexity during encoding/decoding for resource-constrained deployment.
- Questions for authors: provide details on experiments with ReLU/sigmoidal nonlinearities versus GDN in performance and complexity.
- Questions for authors: discuss extending the framework to video compression to exploit temporal coherence.
- Questions for authors: discuss potential benefits and implications of training with perceptual distortion metrics instead of MSE.
