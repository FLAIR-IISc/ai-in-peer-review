- Proposes a novel DCNN approach with object persistence constraints for perceptual similarity judgment.  
- Introduces Object Persistence Net (OPnet), a Siamese triplet architecture that associates different views of the same 3D object.  
- Learns a view-manifold capturing object persistence and continuity.  
- OPnet outperforms AlexNet and other baselines on instance and categorical retrieval for novel objects.  
- Shows higher correlation with human perceptual similarity judgments.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance for innovation, thorough analysis, and strong results.  
- Paper is well-presented with clear explanation of architecture, training, and evaluation metrics.  
- Provides thorough analysis of the learned view-manifold and its relation to perception.  
- Achieves significant improvements specifically in instance retrieval tasks.  
- Requests more detail on the rendering process for the multi-view datasets.  
- Asks for justification/selection process for the triplet loss margin parameter.  
- Suggests additional experiments on transferability to real-world images/videos and handling complex variations (lighting, scale).  
- Seeks plans for extending OPnet to more complex real-world variations.  
- Requests deeper insights into the hierarchical structure of the view-manifold and its relation to human perception.
