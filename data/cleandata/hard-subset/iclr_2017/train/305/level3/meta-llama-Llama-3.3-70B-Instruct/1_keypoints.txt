- Paper proposes Object Persistence Net (OPnet), a re-trained DCNN using object persistence constraints for improved perceptual similarity.
- OPnet is trained with a Siamese triplet architecture to modify the view-manifold of object representations.
- Evaluated on multiple test sets (novel objects, categories, synthetic objects) showing significant gains in similarity judgment and transferability.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Well-motivated, well-placed approach integrating object persistence into DCNN training.
- Key reason 2: Strong experimental results demonstrating improved similarity judgment and learning transfer.
- Supporting argument: Clear introduction and rationale for perceptual similarity and object persistence constraints.
- Supporting argument: OPnet effectively alters view-manifold and outperforms baselines in experiments.
- Suggestion: Provide deeper analysis of the view-manifold structure and its relation to human perceptual similarity.
- Suggestion: Explore applications to other domains such as image compression and retrieval.
- Suggestion: Investigate other architectures (e.g., generative models) for similarity judgment and transferability.
- Question: Please detail the rendering process for multi-view datasets and selection of 3D object models.
- Question: How will OPnet be extended to handle real-world variations like lighting and scale?
- Question: Provide more insight into how view-manifold structure relates to human perceptual similarity and modeling human perception.
