- Proposes Object Persistence Net (OPnet): a DCNN fine-tuned with object persistence constraints using a Siamese triplet architecture linking different views of the same 3D object.  
- OPnet improves perceptual similarity judgment, showing better instance and categorical retrieval performance on both trained and untrained object categories.  
- OPnet’s feature representations align more closely with human perceptual similarity judgments than AlexNet.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Well-motivated approach grounded in object persistence and visual continuity with clear problem statement and methodology.  
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical results demonstrating effectiveness and generalization across categories.  
- Supporting evidence: Thorough experiments, analysis, and comparisons with AlexNet and a joint embedding model.  
- Suggestion: Provide more details on the multi-view rendering process and selection of 3D object models.  
- Suggestion: Discuss limitations, including reliance on 3D models and potential ShapeNet dataset bias.  
- Suggestion: Explore transferability to real-world images and video domains.  
- Suggestion: Offer deeper insights into neural mechanisms linking OPnet to human similarity judgment.  
- Question: How will the authors address potential overfitting of OPnet to ShapeNet?  
- Question: Provide details on computational resources required for training and testing OPnet.  
- Question: How will the approach be extended to handle complex real-world variations (lighting, scale, etc.)?
