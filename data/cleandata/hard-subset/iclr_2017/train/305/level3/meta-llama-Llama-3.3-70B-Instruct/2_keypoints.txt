- Proposes Object Persistence Net (OPnet): retrains a DCNN using object persistence constraints to learn perceptual similarity.
- OPnet associates different views of the same object and produces similarity judgments that transfer to novel objects and categories.
- Demonstrates OPnet outperforms AlexNet and a joint embedding model on multiple retrieval tasks.
- Shows OPnet’s similarity judgments correlate well with human perception.
- Decision: paper recommended for acceptance due to novelty, strong motivation, and impressive results.
- Methodology is clearly presented, including architecture, loss function, and training procedure.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough with multiple comparisons and human perception benchmarks.
- Suggested improvement: more analysis of learned feature representations and their relation to human perception.
- Suggested improvement: explore applicability of OPnet to other domains (e.g., image classification, object detection).
- Suggested improvement: report computational resources and training time, and compare efficiency to existing methods.
- Questions for authors: clarify how object persistence shapes feature representations relative to human perception; plans for extending OPnet to other tasks; provide detailed compute/training-time comparisons.
