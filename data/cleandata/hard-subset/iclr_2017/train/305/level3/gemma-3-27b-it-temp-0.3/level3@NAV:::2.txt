## Review of "END-TO-END OPTIMIZED IMAGE COMPRESSION"

This paper presents an end-to-end optimized image compression method based on cascaded nonlinear transforms, inspired by biological visual systems. The authors extend prior rate-distortion optimization techniques to deep encoders/decoders and incorporate adaptive entropy coding. A key aspect is the connection drawn between their approach and variational autoencoders (VAEs), providing a theoretical grounding for the optimization process. The work is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains the methodology and results. The empirical results demonstrate visually superior performance compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000, with strong rate-distortion performance.

**Reject** – While the paper presents a solid contribution, the novelty is somewhat limited given the prior publication of the core rate-distortion approach. Furthermore, the choice to optimize for MSE instead of a perceptual metric, despite the authors’ expertise in the area, raises concerns.

The core contribution lies in the successful application and extension of existing rate-distortion optimization to a deeper, more flexible architecture with adaptive entropy coding. However, the foundational rate-distortion framework itself isn’t novel. The authors acknowledge this, but the incremental improvement needs to be substantial to warrant acceptance. The switch from perceptual metrics (used in their prior work) to MSE is a significant decision that isn’t sufficiently justified. While MSE allows for easier comparison, it arguably sacrifices the visual quality improvements demonstrated in their earlier research. The results *are* visually impressive, but the lack of perceptual metric evaluation weakens the claim of superior quality. 

The contribution of the adaptive entropy coding is also unclear. It’s difficult to disentangle its impact from the increased capacity of the deeper encoder/decoder. An ablation study, removing the adaptive entropy coding and comparing performance, is crucial to understand its true value. Additionally, more detailed information about the adaptive entropy coder itself – its implementation, complexity, and how it adapts to the image data – is needed. The equation in section 3.1 (Equation 10) appears incorrect, as the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ) for the method to be equivalent to a VAE when using non-Euclidean metrics.

**Additional Feedback:**

*   **Perceptual Metrics:** Please justify the decision to use MSE. If possible, include results using a perceptual metric (like MS-SSIM) during *training* as well as evaluation.
*   **Ablation Study:** Include an ablation study to isolate the contribution of the adaptive entropy coding.
*   **Entropy Coder Details:** Provide more details on the adaptive entropy coder, including its complexity and adaptation mechanism.
*   **Equation 10:** Please verify and correct Equation 10.
*   **Computational Cost:** Discuss the computational cost of training and encoding/decoding compared to standard codecs.



I would be willing to reconsider a higher score if the authors address the concerns regarding the justification for using MSE, provide a clear assessment of the adaptive entropy coder’s contribution, and clarify the mathematical correctness of Equation 10.
