Summary of contributions
- The paper extends classical rate–distortion optimization to modern deep nonlinear transform coding: a multi-stage convolutional analysis/synthesis pair with GDN/IGDN nonlinearities, uniform scalar quantization, and an adaptive entropy coder.
- It introduces a smooth surrogate for the discrete rate–distortion objective by replacing quantization with additive uniform noise, enabling end-to-end learning via SGD.
- It makes an explicit connection to VAEs by showing the relaxed objective corresponds to optimizing a generative model under certain conditions.
- It evaluates with an actual bitstream (CABAC-style) rather than relying on entropy estimates, and demonstrates consistent visual and quantitative improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000 (especially at low rates), with particularly strong perceptual quality.
- The paper is exceptionally well written and carefully organized; the methodology, architecture, and training details are clearly presented.

Decision: Accept
Key reasons: (1) Strong empirical performance with a complete, practical codec (actual bit rates) and consistently superior visual quality; (2) A clear, principled end-to-end formulation that has already influenced subsequent learned compression work, even if the core rate–distortion idea is incremental relative to prior work.

Supporting arguments (addressing the three review questions)
1) Problem tackled: Design and training of a complete image compression system that jointly optimizes the transform, quantizer surrogate, and entropy model for rate–distortion performance, with practical decoding via a bitstream.

2) Motivation and placement in literature: Well motivated and well placed. It builds directly on prior nonlinear transform coding and on the authors’ earlier work optimizing a one-stage transform with a perceptual metric. The VAE connection is valuable context. That said, the central idea—relax quantization and optimize rate+distortion—was previously explored by the authors; the novelty here lies in deeper analysis/synthesis networks, the adaptive entropy coding instantiation, and the systematic bit-accurate evaluation.

3) Do the claims hold?
- Empirical correctness/rigor: Strong. Using an implemented adaptive arithmetic coder and reporting actual bit rates is a major plus. Extensive visual examples and RD curves vs. JPEG/JP2K substantiate claims, including MS-SSIM gains.
- Limitations: (a) It is unclear how much of the gain comes from the adaptive entropy coding versus the increased capacity/depth/GDN; an ablation without adaptation is needed. (b) The switch from a perceptual metric to MSE may be a step backward relative to prior work; while PSNR is standard, a training objective aligned with perception could further improve results. (c) The VAE equivalence is overstated: the normalization constant in the non-Euclidean metric case appears to depend on gs(y;θ), which would invalidate the neat VAE mapping unless additional conditions hold. Also, Equation 10 as written seems to miss this dependence via the partition function.

Additional feedback and questions to improve the paper (not all are decision-critical)
- Adaptive entropy coding:
  - Please detail the coder: number and design of contexts, update schedule, context features (e.g., channel index, magnitude bins, neighborhood), and precise adaptation rule. What is the per-image overhead? How are initial priors seeded?
  - Provide an ablation: (i) fixed non-adaptive coder (frozen marginals), (ii) adaptive coder with identical transforms, and (iii) stronger spatial/context modeling. Report Δbitrate at matched distortion to quantify the specific contribution of adaptation.
  - Clarify how much raster-scan plus shared-channel modeling leaves on the table relative to more sophisticated context models (e.g., autoregressive priors). The appendix hints the gains are modest (Fig. 10); please quantify across Kodak.

- Objective and metrics:
  - Why optimize MSE given your earlier success with a perceptual metric? Was the choice driven by stability, color images, or comparability? Could you train with MS-SSIM (or a differentiable proxy) and report the trade-off vs. PSNR?
  - Although you do report MS-SSIM, many summary plots emphasize PSNR. Please include set-level MS-SSIM summaries and representative crops to align evaluation with perceptual claims.

- VAE connection and Equation 10:
  - The equivalence to VAEs hinges on the form of px|ỹ. For non-Euclidean or perceptual metrics, the normalization constant Z should depend on gs(ỹ;θ) (through the induced energy), not just on λ; otherwise the likelihood is misnormalized and the mapping to ELBO is invalid. Please correct the derivation, specify the conditions under which Z(λ,θ) reduces to Z(λ) (e.g., affine gp, translation-invariant d, and specific constraints on gs), and clarify whether any dependence on θ would introduce extra terms during learning.
  - Equation 10 claims pỹ(n)=Pq(n) at integer points, but in the relaxed model the partition/normalization aspects in the VAE link suggest additional dependencies; please reconcile these statements and ensure equation numbering matches.

- Architecture and capacity:
  - Provide ablations: (a) GDN/IGDN vs. ReLU/sigmoid with matched parameter counts; (b) depth/width scaling; (c) effect of overcompleteness in the first stage. This will clarify whether gains are primarily from the nonlinearity or from capacity.
  - Report compute: encoding/decoding time and memory on CPU/GPU for typical images.

- Training relaxation:
  - Your empirical analysis shows small bias/variance of the relaxed RD estimates. Please add quantitative plots across λ showing bias vs. actual bitrate and MSE, and discuss regimes where the approximation degrades (coarse quantization).

- Comparisons:
  - Briefly position against contemporaneous learned codecs (e.g., Toderici et al.), ideally adding a qualitative and, if feasible, quantitative comparison on a shared set.

Questions for the authors
- How much bitrate reduction (in %) is attributable solely to adaptive entropy coding, averaged over Kodak, at matched MSE and MS-SSIM?
- What contexts are used in CABAC and how frequently are probabilities updated? Any risk of overfitting adaptation to single images?
- Can you train with a perceptual loss (e.g., MS-SSIM) for RGB and report both PSNR and MS-SSIM? Does the VAE correspondence still hold, and how does Z(λ,θ) behave?
- Under what exact assumptions does the VAE equivalence hold? Please provide a corrected derivation showing whether Z depends on θ via gs.
- How sensitive are results to replacing GDN with standard activations when matching parameter counts and depth?
- Could a single conditional model cover multiple rate points (varying λ) without retraining separate models? If attempted, how does performance change?

Overall, despite limited novelty in the core formulation, the paper delivers a convincing, end-to-end codec with strong results, careful engineering, and clear exposition—worthy of acceptance.
