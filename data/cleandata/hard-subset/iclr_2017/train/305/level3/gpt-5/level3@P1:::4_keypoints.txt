- Proposes an end-to-end image compression system using cascaded conv linear filters + GDN nonlinearities with IGDN inverse and uniform scalar quantization in latent space.
- Trains with a continuous relaxation (additive i.i.d. uniform noise) to bypass quantization nondifferentiability and models marginal latent densities non-parametrically.
- Shows the relaxed objective is VAE-like under assumptions, but training is explicitly rate–distortion optimal with trade-off parameter λ.
- Evaluates with a real entropy coder (CABAC-like) and reports actual bitrates instead of relying on differential entropy estimates.
- Demonstrates substantial perceptual gains (MS-SSIM) over JPEG and JPEG 2000 across rates and generally better PSNR, especially at low rates.
- Paper is well positioned in literature (transform coding, VAEs, neural compression) and provides architectural/training details for reproducibility.
- Recommendation: Accept — principled RD framework, elegant relaxation bridging classic and modern methods, and strong empirical evidence with real bitstreams.
- Tackles joint optimization of transform, quantization, and entropy coding to make uniform scalar quantization effective via learned nonlinear transforms.
- Justifies additive-noise relaxation theoretically and empirically, including equivalence at integer lattice points and bias/variance quantification.
- Implementation includes an entropy coder and accounts for side information, avoiding common pitfalls in bitrate comparisons.
- Visual examples align with metric gains, showing contour preservation and fewer basis-related artifacts.
- Analysis comparing GDN to pointwise nonlinearities argues GDN improves model efficiency.
- Caveat: Requires a separate model per λ; a single conditional model spanning the RD curve would be more practical.
- Caveat: Entropy model assumes independent marginals with limited spatial/context adaptation, leaving potential gains from stronger context models unexploited.
- Caveat: Baselines limited to JPEG/JPEG 2000; newer codecs (HEVC/BPG) would strengthen comparisons.
- Caveat: No reported runtime, complexity, or memory results for encoding/decoding.
- Suggestion: Explore conditioning or gating to train a single model across multiple λ values.
- Suggestion: Improve entropy modeling with spatial/context models or hierarchical priors to capture latent dependencies.
- Suggestion: Provide ablations on GDN vs ReLU/sigmoid, stage counts, latent dimensionality, and include parameter counts.
- Suggestion: Report encoding/decoding runtime on CPU/GPU, memory footprint, and deployment feasibility.
- Suggestion: Add broader baselines, larger test sets, more metrics or user studies, and clarify color handling/chroma subsampling.
- Suggestion: Release code/models, CABAC context details, and exact training/preprocessing scripts for reproducibility.
- Questions: Sensitivity to architecture and GDN; request parameter counts and ablation replacing GDN with pointwise nonlinearities.
- Questions: Encoding/decoding times compared to JPEG 2000 and runtime scaling with image size.
- Questions: How much bitrate reduction comes from learned transform vs entropy coder; potential gains from stronger context modeling.
- Questions: Can a single model trained across λ values match per-λ performance via conditioning or multi-task training?
- Questions: Robustness to out-of-distribution content and any observed failure cases.
- Questions: Results of optimizing with MS-SSIM or perceptual losses for color images and their effect on RD/visual quality.
- Questions: Experiments with annealing or learned noise scales in the relaxation and bias behavior at very low rates.
- Questions: Side-information overhead per image and its scaling; internal color space for RGB and potential gains from learned color transforms.
