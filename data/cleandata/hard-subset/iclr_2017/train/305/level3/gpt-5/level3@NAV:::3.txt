Summary of contributions
The paper proposes an end-to-end trainable image compression system built from cascaded convolutional transforms with GDN/IGDN nonlinearities, trained directly for rate–distortion using a differentiable surrogate of both distortion and rate. The key technical contribution is a principled, differentiable approximation of the discrete rate via additive uniform noise and nonparametric marginal models, enabling effective SGD across λ values to trace the RD curve. The method shows consistent visual improvements and better MS-SSIM and often better PSNR than JPEG and JPEG 2000 at equal or lower bitrates, and reports actual bitrates via an implemented entropy coder. The relaxed objective is carefully related to VAEs, highlighting broader impact for tasks needing differentiable approximations of discrete rates.

Decision: Accept
Reasons: (1) Novel and well-founded differentiable rate formulation that unlocks end-to-end training across RD trade-offs. (2) Strong empirical evidence with actual bitrates and clear perceptual gains over established codecs.

Supporting arguments
1) Problem clarity: The paper tackles joint rate–distortion optimization of a nonlinear transform code, addressing the central difficulty of non-differentiable quantization and rate estimation.
2) Motivation and placement: The approach is well-situated amid transform coding, quantization theory, and VAEs, with clear distinctions from generative modeling and prior neural compressors. The GDN choice is motivated by prior work on Gaussianization.
3) Evidence and rigor: The authors validate the bias/variance of the relaxed objective against quantized evaluation, implement CABAC to report true bitrates, and evaluate on Kodak with both PSNR and MS-SSIM, alongside compelling visual comparisons. The author response was thoughtful; while in principle a sufficiently expressive network could maximize entropy within fixed ranges, the authors’ second argument is convincing: enforcing specific output forms would either force a substantially more complex network or require a separate variable-rate encoder to match the current system’s effective variable-rate behavior.

Suggestions for improvement (non-blocking)
- Compare against stronger modern baselines (e.g., BPG/HEVC-intra, AVIF) and include runtime/complexity analysis.
- Provide ablations isolating the impact of GDN versus common activations, network depth/width, and the marginal model.
- Explore a single variable-rate model (shared weights) across λ, or conditional normalization to traverse rates at test time.
- Discuss generalization across datasets and color spaces; include larger-scale RGB evaluations.

Questions for the authors
- How sensitive is performance to the independence assumption across latents? Have you tried simple autoregressive/context models during coding?
- Can one train a single conditional model over λ (e.g., via FiLM/conditioning) without losing RD optimality?
- What are encoding/decoding times and memory footprints compared to JPEG 2000?
- How does performance change when replacing GDN/IGDN with ReLU or other pointwise nonlinearities at equal parameter budgets?
- Any failure cases (textures, medical/scientific images) where the relaxation mispredicts rate or harms perceptual quality?
