Summary of contributions
The paper tackles end-to-end rate–distortion optimization for lossy image compression, extending prior transform-coding work to deep, cascaded analysis/synthesis transforms with GDN/IGDN, uniform quantization, and an adaptive entropy coder. The authors introduce a continuous relaxation for optimization, relate the relaxed objective to VAEs, and train models per-λ to trace the R–D frontier. Empirically, the method delivers consistent and visible gains over JPEG 2000, with competitive performance vs. contemporary learned codecs, and strong visual quality. The paper is exceptionally well written and thorough, with practical evaluation using actual bitrates.

Decision
Accept. Key reasons: (1) Strong empirical results with a complete, deployable system using actual entropy coding; (2) Clear, well-motivated extension of rate–distortion optimization to modern deep transforms with meaningful gains over established standards.

Supporting arguments
- Problem and motivation: The paper addresses a core problem—joint optimization of transforms, quantization, and coding for lossy compression. It is well placed in the literature (classic transform coding, prior perceptual R–D work, learned compression, VAEs).
- Novelty: The conceptual R–D formulation and relaxation were introduced previously; here the novelty lies in deeper architectures, a practical adaptive entropy coder, and a careful VAE connection. While incremental conceptually, the engineering execution and demonstrated practicality are significant.
- Claims and rigor: The empirical evidence supports claims of improved R–D over JPEG 2000 and improved perceptual quality. Using actual bitrates is a strength. However, the contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus increased model capacity remains unclear; an ablation is needed. Also, in Sec. 3.1, the VAE equivalence is overstated: the normalization constant Z for the induced likelihood under non-Euclidean/perceptual distortions depends on gs(y;θ), so exact equivalence is not guaranteed.

Additional feedback (to help improve the paper)
- Please justify optimizing MSE rather than a perceptual metric, and clarify why PSNR is emphasized; given the authors’ expertise, showing perceptual optimization and making MS-SSIM primary would strengthen the case.
- Provide an ablation isolating adaptive entropy coding: fixed (non-adaptive) coder vs. adaptive, and report its bitrate impact across λ and datasets.
- Expand coder details: context design, adaptation schedule, conditioning on channel/space, and complexity. A plot of actual bitrate vs. entropy estimate (with/without adaptation) would be useful.
- Clarify the VAE connection: note explicitly that Z(λ,θ) depends on θ via gs for general metrics, and delineate the conditions under which equivalence holds.
- Minor: report encoding/decoding runtime; release code/models if possible.

Questions for the authors
1) What is the quantitative gain (bps/image) from adaptive entropy coding versus a fixed model?  
2) How do results change with the same network but a non-adaptive coder?  
3) Why prioritize MSE optimization; do you have results when training with a perceptual metric (e.g., MS-SSIM) using the same architecture?  
4) In Sec. 3.1, can you formalize the dependence of Z on θ and state precise conditions for VAE equivalence?  
5) How sensitive are results to GDN vs. pointwise nonlinearities at fixed parameter count and depth?  
6) What are the encoding/decoding times and memory footprints for typical resolutions?
