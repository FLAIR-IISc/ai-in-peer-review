- Extends end-to-end rate–distortion optimization to deep, cascaded analysis/synthesis transforms with GDN/IGDN, uniform quantization, and an adaptive entropy coder.  
- Introduces a continuous relaxation for training, relates the relaxed objective to VAEs, and trains models per-λ to trace the R–D frontier.  
- Empirical results show consistent, visible gains over JPEG 2000 and competitive performance vs. learned codecs, with strong visual quality and actual bitrate evaluation.  
- Decision: Accept — due to strong empirical results and a complete, deployable system using actual entropy coding.  
- Novelty is incremental conceptually (R–D relaxation known) but significant in engineering: deeper architectures and a practical adaptive coder.  
- Claims are generally well supported by experiments; using actual bitrates is a notable strength.  
- Lack of ablation: unclear how much gain comes from adaptive entropy coding versus increased model capacity—an ablation is needed.  
- Overstated VAE equivalence: the normalization constant Z for the induced likelihood can depend on θ via gs, so exact equivalence is not guaranteed for non-Euclidean/perceptual distortions.  
- Request to justify optimizing MSE/PSNR rather than a perceptual metric (e.g., make MS-SSIM primary or show perceptual training results).  
- Request an ablation isolating adaptive entropy coding (fixed vs. adaptive coder) and report bitrate impact across λ and datasets.  
- Ask to expand coder implementation details: context design, adaptation schedule, conditioning, complexity, and provide bitrate vs. entropy-estimate plots (with/without adaptation).  
- Clarify the VAE connection explicitly: state dependence of Z(λ,θ) on θ and precise conditions under which equivalence holds.  
- Minor requests: report encoding/decoding runtime and memory footprints; release code/models if possible.  
- Specific questions for authors: quantitative bps/image gain from adaptive coding; results with same network but non-adaptive coder; results when training with perceptual losses (MS-SSIM); formalize Z(θ) dependence and VAE conditions; sensitivity to GDN vs. pointwise nonlinearities at fixed capacity; encoding/decoding times and memory for typical resolutions.
