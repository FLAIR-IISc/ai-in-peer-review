- Proposes end-to-end learned image compression optimizing a nonlinear transform coder (conv layers + GDN/IGDN) for rate–distortion.
- Introduces a practical relaxation of quantization by replacing it with additive uniform noise to enable SGD while maintaining a link to discrete coding.
- Implements a full system with CABAC entropy coding and reports real bitrates; shows improved R–D (especially low rates) and perceptual quality vs JPEG/JPEG2000.
- Decision: Accept.

Primary reasons:
- Strong, well-motivated end-to-end R–D optimization with an effective relaxation bridging learning and coding.
- Compelling empirical gains with real bitrates, rigorous analysis, and good linkage to prior work (transform coding, VAEs, GDN).

Supporting arguments:
- Tackles the central problem of jointly optimizing transforms and representation for lossy image compression.
- Method is well grounded in transform coding, probabilistic modeling, and prior GDN work.
- Reports actual bitstreams including side-information and compares PSNR/MS-SSIM across images and rates.
- Validates the relaxation empirically: small approximation bias, unbiased distortion, diminishing entropy bias with finer quantization.
- Demonstrates large, consistent perceptual improvements with fewer artifacts and clear qualitative examples.
- Architecture, training, latent density modeling, and entropy coding are clearly described with useful appendices.
- Relates method to VAEs while articulating differences important for compression.
- Method is practical at inference and encoding/decoding is efficient given trained models; reporting real bitrates is a strength.

Suggestions to improve:
- Add comparison to modern baselines (e.g., HEVC/BPG) or at least discuss expected relative performance.
- Provide systematic ablations quantifying GDN/IGDN vs pointwise nonlinearities, depth, and channel count.
- Discuss and quantify potential gains from lightweight spatial/context modeling and cross-channel coupling in the entropy model.
- Consider or discuss conditioning to cover multiple rates with a single model instead of per-λ training.
- Add an aggregate R–D summary at consistent target rates alongside per-image plots for clearer comparison.
- Report encoding/decoding runtime, memory, and parameter counts per λ to contextualize deployment costs.
- Clarify color handling and fairness relative to JPEG’s chroma subsampling; report luminance-only metrics if needed.

Questions for the authors:
- How sensitive is bitrate to mismatches between learned marginal models and test statistics; what is the coding loss when freezing vs adapting CABAC?
- Do you share probability models only within-channel across spatial positions; would simple causal neighbors yield significant savings?
- How much residual dependency remains in latents after GDN (e.g., mutual information between nearby coefficients)?
- Did you compare additive uniform noise relaxation to straight-through estimators or annealed noise; how sensitive are convergence and final R–D to the choice?
- Provide quantitative ablations replacing GDN with ReLU/PReLU: R–D differences, and how many extra layers/parameters are needed to match performance?
- How critical is the symmetric γ constraint and did you test learned exponents vs fixed?
- How is λ chosen at encode time in practice; do you plan multiple λ-specific models or a conditioned single model, and does per-λ optimality materially outperform shared models?
- How does performance generalize across datasets with different statistics (high-ISO noise, graphics, medical); any failure cases where JPEG2000 wins?
- Have you tried training with MS-SSIM instead of MSE, and if so, how do artifacts and R–D change?
