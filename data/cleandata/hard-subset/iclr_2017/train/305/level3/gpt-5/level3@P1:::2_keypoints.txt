- Presents a complete end-to-end learned image codec with cascaded conv transforms, GDN/IGDN, uniform quantization, and an adaptive arithmetic coder, trained jointly via a continuous relaxation replacing quantization with additive uniform noise.
- Extends prior work with deeper convolutional cascades, jointly learned nonparametric marginal models, color-image optimization, and reporting actual bitrates via an implemented entropy coder.
- Clarifies the relation between the relaxed rate–distortion objective and variational autoencoders, stating when the equivalence holds (Euclidean or translation-invariant metrics with affine perceptual transforms).
- Empirically shows consistent perceptual improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000 at all bitrates, with substantial MS-SSIM gains and competitive or superior PSNR, especially at low rates.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Strong, convincing empirical results from a practical codec that reports true bitrates and improves visual quality over established standards.
- Key reason 2: Principled, well-motivated formulation that advances end-to-end compression optimization and clarifies the VAE relationship.
- Problem significance: Optimizing the full compression pipeline for rate–distortion is central and the choice of GDN/IGDN is well-motivated by prior Gaussianization evidence.
- Novelty: Advances engineering of a practical codec (deeper cascades, joint nonparametric marginals, context-adaptive arithmetic coder, real bitstream evaluation) and elaborates the VAE connection.
- Soundness: Continuous relaxation is appropriate; empirical evidence shows relaxed loss tracks true RD, and comparisons use standard datasets and metrics with supportive visual examples.
- Scope and clarity: Method and architectural/training details are clearly described, with an informative entropy-coder appendix and convincing perceptual gains.
- Ablation requested: Quantify bit savings from the context-adaptive arithmetic coder vs. (i) an ideal non-adaptive model matched to marginals and (ii) a simple non-adaptive coder (Huffman/arithmetic).
- Ablation requested: Compare GDN/IGDN to ReLU/tanh networks of comparable capacity and vary stages/channels to show scaling effects.
- Ablation requested: Compare parametric vs. spline/nonparametric marginal densities and test independence across channels vs. modest context modeling.
- Variable-rate limitation: Currently one model per λ; suggest exploring a single network traversing the RD curve (conditioning on λ or learned step sizes) or discuss practical implications.
- Distortion choice: Train vs. evaluate trade-off—request direct comparison of MSE-trained models vs. MS-SSIM/perceptual-trained models to quantify PSNR/MS-SSIM and visual differences.
- Evaluation breadth: Request runtime (encode/decode), model size, and memory comparisons vs. JPEG 2000 on standard CPU/GPU.
- Evaluation detail: Clarify JPEG 2000 settings used and consider matching per-image target bpp points across methods.
- Perceptual validation: Recommend a small-scale human study or pairwise preference test to substantiate perceptual claims.
- Presentation detail: Clarify VAE equivalence conditions (when normalizer Z depends only on λ) and state additional required conditions explicitly.
- Entropy-coder detail: Expand description of contexts, mode estimation/updates, side information size, and empirical adaptation gains.
- Question 1: Measured bitrate gain of adaptive arithmetic coder vs. (a) non-adaptive coder using learned marginals and (b) fixed Laplacian/Gaussian model, with per-rate breakdowns.
- Question 2: Quantify the contribution of GDN/IGDN vs. ReLU/tanh in equal-capacity networks and provide RD ablations.
- Question 3: Report encode/decode times and model size vs. JPEG 2000 on standard hardware.
- Question 4: Sensitivity to the latent-independence assumption and results when using simple spatial/context models during coding.
- Question 5: Whether a single multi-rate model was trained (conditioning on λ or scaling quantization) and how close it comes to the per-λ frontier.
- Question 6: Precise assumptions under which Z(λ) in Eq.16 is independent of θ and ỹ, and consequences if assumptions are violated.
- Question 7: Rationale for prioritizing MSE training given perceptual gains and request for direct MSE vs. perceptual-trained comparisons on the same architecture.
- Question 8: Fraction of performance gap to JPEG 2000 attributable to entropy coding vs. transform quality; request table with true bitrates for same latents encoded by various coders.
- Question 9: Robustness of nonparametric marginals to overfitting and performance on out-of-distribution textures or synthetic images.
- Question 10: Plans to release code, models, and bitstreams for reproducibility.
