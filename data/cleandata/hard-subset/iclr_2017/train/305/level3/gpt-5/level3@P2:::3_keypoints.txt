- Introduces an end-to-end trainable image compression system that jointly optimizes transforms and an entropy model for rate–distortion.
- Main technical idea: differentiable relaxation of quantization via additive uniform noise and a nonparametric surrogate for rate (differential entropy).
- Uses convolutional transforms with GDN/IGDN nonlinearities motivated by visual neuroscience to Gaussianize image statistics.
- Carefully relates the objective to the VAE ELBO while highlighting practical differences important for compression (discrete R–D points, actual bitrates).
- Implements a practical entropy coder (CABAC) and reports true bitrates including side information.
- Empirically outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 on most images in PSNR (especially low rates) and shows consistent MS-SSIM and perceptual quality gains.
- The differentiable rate approximation is broadly useful for learning with quantized bottlenecks.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason for acceptance: strong end-to-end formulation with an effective, differentiable rate proxy enabling true R–D optimization and bitrate reporting.
- Clear empirical improvements with convincing methodology and ablations validating the relaxed loss.
- Validation: distortion proxy is unbiased with low variance; rate proxy bias shrinks with finer quantization and does not harm end performance.
- Evaluation includes actual quantization plus arithmetic coding, and comparisons on Kodak and other images show superior perceptual artifacts.
- GDN appears parameter-efficient relative to pointwise nonlinearities, with preliminary evidence provided.
- Potential concern about networks “gaming” the rate proxy is mitigated by adaptive nonparametric marginals and evaluation with true coding.
- Suggestion: explore a single variable-rate model controllable by λ or a learned scale/importance map for practicality.
- Suggestion: more systematic ablations comparing GDN/IGDN vs. ReLU/pointwise activations at matched parameter counts and depths.
- Suggestion: discuss or experiment with more expressive entropy models (learned context models or hyperpriors) beyond the independence/CABAC assumption.
- Suggestion: improve fairness and clarity in evaluation—matched target-rate comparisons, Y vs. RGB metrics, and chroma subsampling details.
- Suggestion: report encoder/decoder runtimes, memory, FLOPs, and side-information overhead to assess efficiency.
- Suggestion: evaluate generalization to higher-resolution and diverse datasets and discuss scaling from patch training to full images.
- Suggestion: release code, trained models, and bitstreams to aid reproducibility.
- Questions: sensitivity of final bitrates to the independence assumption and any experiments with simple spatial/context models over latents.
- Questions: fraction and scaling of side-information (λ index, headers) in total bitrate.
- Questions: quantification of the proxy’s bias vs. λ and architecture, and whether training ever yields pathological latents under true quantization.
- Questions: comparisons to straight-through estimators or soft-to-hard annealing for quantization.
- Questions: can a single trained model be steered at test-time (scaling latents or changing quantization) to cover a range of rates?
- Questions: runtime/throughput numbers, FLOP counts, and whether GDN/IGDN induce memory bottlenecks.
- Questions: effect on results when replacing GDN with matched compute/parameter pointwise activations.
- Questions: whether MS-SSIM training was attempted and how artifacts changed, and comparisons to contemporaneous learned codecs on Kodak with actual bitrates.
