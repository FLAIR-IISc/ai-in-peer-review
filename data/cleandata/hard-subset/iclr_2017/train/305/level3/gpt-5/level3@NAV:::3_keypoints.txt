- Proposes an end-to-end trainable image compression system using cascaded convolutional transforms with GDN/IGDN, trained directly for rate–distortion via differentiable surrogates.
- Key technical contribution: principled, differentiable approximation of discrete rate using additive uniform noise and nonparametric marginal models, enabling SGD across λ to trace the RD curve.
- Demonstrates consistent visual improvements and better MS-SSIM (often better PSNR) than JPEG/JPEG 2000 at equal or lower bitrates, reporting actual bitrates with an implemented entropy coder.
- Connects the relaxed objective to VAEs, highlighting broader impact for tasks requiring differentiable approximations of discrete rates.
- Decision: Accept.
- Main reasons: novel, well-founded differentiable rate formulation that enables end-to-end RD training; strong empirical evidence with true bitrates and perceptual gains over established codecs.
- Supporting: Clearly addresses joint rate–distortion optimization and the central difficulty of non-differentiable quantization/rate estimation.
- Supporting: Well-placed relative to transform coding, quantization theory, and VAEs; GDN choice motivated by prior Gaussianization work.
- Supporting: Rigorous validation of bias/variance of the relaxation vs quantized evaluation, implementation of CABAC for true bitrates, Kodak evaluations with PSNR/MS-SSIM, and strong visual comparisons; author response persuasive on entropy-maximization concerns.
- Suggestion: Compare against stronger modern baselines (BPG/HEVC-intra/AVIF) and include runtime/complexity analysis.
- Suggestion: Provide ablations isolating the impact of GDN versus common activations, network depth/width, and the marginal model.
- Suggestion: Explore a single variable-rate model (shared weights) across λ or conditional normalization to traverse rates at test time.
- Suggestion: Discuss generalization across datasets and color spaces and include larger-scale RGB evaluations.
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the independence assumption across latents; have simple autoregressive/context models been tried?
- Question: Can one train a single conditional model over λ (e.g., via FiLM/conditioning) without losing RD optimality?
- Question: What are encoding/decoding times and memory footprints compared to JPEG 2000?
- Question: How does performance change when replacing GDN/IGDN with ReLU or other pointwise nonlinearities at equal parameter budgets?
- Question: Any failure cases (textures, medical/scientific images) where the relaxation mispredicts rate or harms perceptual quality?
