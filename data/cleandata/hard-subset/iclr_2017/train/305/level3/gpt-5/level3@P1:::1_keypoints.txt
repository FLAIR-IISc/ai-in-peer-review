- End-to-end image compression system jointly optimizing analysis transform, uniform scalar quantizer (proxy), entropy model, and synthesis transform for rate–distortion (RD) performance.  
- Key techniques: cascaded convolutional transforms with GDN/IGDN for Gaussianization/decorrelation.  
- Key technique: replace non-differentiable quantizer with additive uniform noise to obtain a differentiable training proxy.  
- Key technique: learn nonparametric marginal models for code-space densities.  
- Key technique: evaluate with a real entropy coder (CABAC) and report actual bitrates.  
- The relaxed objective is related to VAEs but is focused on operating across the RD curve via a trade-off parameter λ.  
- Empirical results show strong RD performance versus JPEG and JPEG2000, with large gains in MS-SSIM and visually superior artifacts.  
- Writing, integration of RD theory, and experimental thoroughness are clear and compelling.  
- Decision: Accept — reviewer considers this the most convincing deep-learning-based image compression approach seen.  
- Paper is well positioned in literature, bridging classical transform coding and modern deep learning, and builds appropriately on prior work (GDN/IGDN, learned compression).  
- GDN/IGDN choice is well motivated by image statistics and prior efficiency gains over pointwise nonlinearities.  
- The additive noise proxy is theoretically sound (quantization-noise model) and empirically shows small distortion/entropy biases that decrease with finer quantization.  
- Scientific support includes per-λ optimization tracing the convex RD hull and use of CABAC for real bitrate reporting.  
- Evaluations on Kodak and classic images show consistent MS-SSIM gains at all bitrates and competitive or better PSNR, especially at low rates.  
- Qualitative improvements include better edge preservation and reduced ringing/blocking artifacts.  
- Impact: clean, general formulation likely to influence future learned compression work and can incorporate other perceptual metrics.  
- Suggest adding comparisons to state-of-the-art classical codecs circa 2016–2017, notably BPG (HEVC-Intra), and discussing other RD-optimized transform coding literature.  
- Recommend including standard benchmark results (Lena, Barbara, Mandrill, Peppers) with PSNR and MS-SSIM in main text or appendix.  
- Request ablation studies comparing GDN/IGDN to ReLU/sigmoid at matched parameter counts/depths.  
- Ask for encoding/decoding speed, model size, and bitstream side-information breakdown to assess practical complexity.  
- Suggest discussing the simple CABAC context model and potential rate improvements from stronger context/adaptive models.  
- Request clarification on λ sensitivity and whether a single model can cover multiple rates (e.g., conditional/scale parameters).  
- For color images, document colorspace handling (RGB vs Y’CbCr), training differences, and any chroma artifacts or rate allocation issues.  
- Encourage releasing pretrained models and evaluation scripts or detailed CABAC/training settings for reproducibility.  
- Questions for authors: add BPG comparisons or comment on expected relative performance given current entropy modeling?  
- Questions: quantitative ablations of GDN/IGDN vs pointwise nonlinearities at matched budgets?  
- Questions: can a single conditional model approximate multiple RD points without retraining, and what is the performance gap?  
- Questions: typical encode/decode times and memory footprint on CPU/GPU, and how complexity scales with channels/stages?  
- Questions: how much gain comes from learned transforms vs learned marginals, and results with stronger spatial context models in the entropy coder?  
- Questions: which colorspace is used internally and are there chroma-specific artifacts or rate-allocation issues?  
- Questions: any systematic failure modes (e.g., periodic textures) where PSNR lags JPEG2000 at high rates and mitigation strategies?
