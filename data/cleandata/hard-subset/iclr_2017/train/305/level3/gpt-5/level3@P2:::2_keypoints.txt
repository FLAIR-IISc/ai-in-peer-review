- Proposes a complete end-to-end learned lossy image codec (analysis/synthesis transforms, uniform quantization, entropy coder) optimized for rate–distortion performance.
- Uses cascaded convolutions with GDN/IGDN, uniform-noise relaxation of quantization for SGD, and learned nonparametric latent marginals.
- Relates the relaxed RD objective to VAEs and evaluates using actual bitstreams with a CABAC-based entropy coder rather than just entropy estimates.
- Shows strong empirical RD performance vs. JPEG/JPEG2000 on Kodak, substantial MS-SSIM gains, competitive PSNR (especially at low rates), and improved visual quality.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — driven by strong empirical results and a solid end-to-end RD training framework.
- Main concerns: limited novelty relative to authors’ prior work and missing ablations isolating the sources of gains.
- Key supporting strength: well-motivated RD formulation, justified uniform-noise relaxation, and fair positioning relative to prior learned codecs.
- Empirical support is strong, but the relative contribution of the adaptive entropy coder versus learned transforms is not disentangled.
- The claimed VAE correspondence is qualified and may omit dependence of the partition function Z on θ (and possibly λ) in general—needs clarification.
- Choice to optimize MSE (rather than perceptual metrics) is questioned given prior perceptual work; evaluation leans on PSNR.
- Recommended ablation (entropy coding): compare adaptive CABAC to nonadaptive arithmetic coding and simple Huffman to quantify online adaptation gains.
- Recommended ablation (architecture): compare GDN/IGDN to ReLU/LeakyReLU at matched parameter budgets, vary stages, and report parameter counts and runtime.
- Recommended ablation (latent marginals): compare learned nonparametric marginals to fixed parametric choices (Gaussian/Laplacian/mixtures).
- Evaluation suggestions: justify MSE optimization, train at least one perceptual (MS-SSIM) model, add MS-SSIM curves for all images, and consider user studies or LPIPS.
- Rate-control concern: training separate models per λ complicates deployment—show a single multi-rate model (e.g., via conditioning) and compare RD.
- Entropy coder practicality: provide context design, adaptation schedule, number of contexts, spatial sharing details, and how quickly adaptation occurs.
- Bitstream overhead: quantify side-information (e.g., image size, λ index) and show it’s negligible across rates.
- Implementation practicality: report encoding/decoding speed, memory footprint, and CPU-only decoding feasibility versus JPEG2000.
- Reproducibility: release code/models or give comprehensive implementation details to facilitate adoption.
- Question 1: Quantify RD gain split between adaptive CABAC, frozen learned marginals, and simple codes (Huffman).
- Question 2: Quantify GDN/IGDN contribution vs. standard pointwise nonlinearities at matched capacity—can ReLU models close the gap?
- Question 3: Clarify whether Z(λ) depends on θ through gs for general metrics and correct equations/conditions for VAE correspondence.
- Question 4: Explain why optimize MSE instead of a perceptual metric and include a perceptually trained variant for comparison.
- Question 5: Report sensitivity and stability of performance to nonparametric marginal modeling choices (spline resolution, range, sharing).
- Question 6: Provide encode/decode runtimes, memory usage (CPU/GPU), and quantify side-information overhead.
- Question 7: Show feasibility and RD performance of a single λ-conditioned model versus per-λ models.
