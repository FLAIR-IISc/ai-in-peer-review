1. Summary of contributions
The paper proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression system built from cascaded convolutional linear filters and generalized divisive normalization (GDN) nonlinearities in the analysis transform, paired with an approximate inverse (IGDN) in the synthesis transform. Uniform scalar quantization in the latent space yields a practical codec. To optimize despite the non-differentiability of quantization, the authors introduce a continuous relaxation by adding i.i.d. uniform noise during training and model marginal latent densities non-parametrically; the relaxed objective coincides with a VAE-style evidence lower bound under specific assumptions, but the training is explicitly rate–distortion (RD) optimal with a trade-off parameter λ. Importantly, the evaluation uses an actual entropy coder (CABAC-like), reporting true bit rates rather than entropy estimates. Experiments on Kodak and additional images show substantial perceptual gains (MS-SSIM) over JPEG and JPEG 2000 at all rates and generally better PSNR, with especially strong improvements at lower bit rates. The paper is carefully positioned in prior literature (transform coding, VAEs, and recent neural compression) and provides architectural/training details for reproducibility.

2. Decision
Accept. Key reasons:
- A principled, well-motivated end-to-end RD optimization framework with an elegant and effective relaxation that bridges classic transform coding and modern variational methods.
- Strong empirical evidence with actual bitstreams showing consistent perceptual gains and generally better RD performance than standard baselines.

3. Supporting arguments
- Problem tackled: The paper addresses joint optimization of analysis/synthesis transforms, quantization, and entropy coding to achieve the best RD trade-off for natural images, overcoming the intractability of high-dimensional quantization by learning a nonlinear transform where uniform scalar quantization is effective.
- Motivation and placement: The work builds on transform coding, local gain control/GDN from vision science, and recent deep models. The connection to VAEs clarifies the role of the relaxed objective while highlighting key differences (discrete coding, operation at arbitrary λ, and evaluation on actual bitrates). Related neural compression works are discussed fairly.
- Evidence and rigor:
  - Optimization: Clear derivation of the relaxed loss, with independence assumptions and non-parametric marginal models. The additive uniform noise relaxation is justified both intuitively and via equivalence at integer lattice points.
  - Implementation: An entropy coder is implemented; side information is accounted for. This avoids common pitfalls of comparing differential entropy to real bitrates.
  - Results: Visual exemplars show artifact profiles characteristic of the proposed method (contour preservation, fewer basis-related artifacts), aligning with MS-SSIM improvements across rates and images; PSNR is also improved for most cases, particularly at low rates. The paper quantifies the bias/variance of the relaxation for both rate and distortion. Both grayscale and RGB models are trained on a sizable ImageNet subset, with Kodak held out for testing.
  - Analysis: The discussion of GDN vs pointwise nonlinearities and the effect on model efficiency is insightful and credible.

Caveats (not decision-critical):
- A separate model is trained per λ; a single conditional model covering the RD curve would be more practical.
- The entropy model assumes independent marginals with limited spatial/context adaptation; modern codecs exploit stronger context models.
- Baselines are JPEG and JPEG 2000; more modern references (e.g., intra HEVC/BPG) would strengthen claims, though at ICLR 2017 this comparison was not yet standard.
- Runtime/complexity results are not reported.

4. Suggestions for improvement (helpful, not necessarily decision-critical)
- Single-model RD control: Explore conditioning the analysis/synthesis transforms (or adding a small gating network) on λ so a single set of weights spans multiple rates. Alternatively, multi-objective or curriculum training over a schedule of λ values.
- Entropy modeling: Incorporate spatial/context models or hierarchical priors over latents to capture dependencies beyond independent marginals, potentially improving rates without sacrificing simplicity.
- Ablations: Provide systematic ablations on GDN vs ReLU/sigmoid, number of stages, and latent dimensionality; include parameter counts and model size.
- Efficiency: Report encoding/decoding runtime on CPU/GPU and memory footprint, and discuss feasibility for practical deployment.
- Broader baselines/metrics: If possible, include comparisons to additional codecs and evaluate with both PSNR and MS-SSIM on larger test sets; add user studies or perceptual metrics tuned for color images.
- Color handling: Clarify color space choices, whether any chroma subsampling is used, and fairness in comparisons to JPEG/J2K.
- Reproducibility: Consider releasing code/models and details of the CABAC contexts; provide exact training splits and preprocessing scripts.

Questions for the authors
- How sensitive is performance to the architecture (depth, channel counts) and GDN specifically? Can you share parameter counts and an ablation replacing GDN/IGDN with pointwise nonlinearities at comparable capacity?
- What are the encoding/decoding times relative to JPEG 2000 on standard hardware? How does runtime scale with image size?
- How much of the final bitrate reduction is due to the learned transform vs. the entropy coder’s adaptation? Have you tried stronger context modeling, and what is the expected gain?
- Can a single model trained across multiple λ values (e.g., via conditioning or multi-task training) achieve competitive performance along the RD curve, avoiding per-λ training?
- How robust is the method to out-of-distribution content (e.g., graphics, medical images)? Any failure cases?
- Did you attempt optimizing with MS-SSIM or a perceptual loss for color images, and if so, how did RD performance and visual quality change?
- In the relaxation, did you experiment with annealing the uniform noise width or using learned noise scales? How does the rate/distortion bias behave at very low rates?
- What is the overhead of side information (λ index, etc.) per image, and how does it scale?
- For RGB, which color space is used internally, and could a learned color transform further improve compression?
