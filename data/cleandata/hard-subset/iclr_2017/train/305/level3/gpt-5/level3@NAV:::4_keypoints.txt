- Tackles lossy image compression via end-to-end learned transform coding optimized for rate–distortion.  
- Uses cascaded convolutional analysis/synthesis transforms with GDN/IGDN, uniform scalar quantization, and a learned nonparametric marginal entropy model.  
- Key technical contribution: principled relaxation replacing quantization with additive uniform noise to enable SGD while remaining faithful to the discrete objective and clarifying links to VAEs.  
- Trained on a large natural image dataset and evaluated with an actual entropy coder (CABAC), reporting real bitrates.  
- Experiments show substantial improvements over JPEG/JPEG 2000 in MS-SSIM and visual quality, with PSNR gains for most images.  
- Decision: Accept — paper is well-motivated, literature-grounded, and supported by rigorous experiments and careful analysis.  
- Problem clarity: Clearly addresses end-to-end optimization of transform, quantizer, and entropy model along the rate–distortion curve.  
- Motivation and placement: Grounded in transform coding and rate–distortion theory; connects to VAEs and contemporary learned compression/generative work.  
- Evidence and rigor: Validates continuous relaxation vs discrete coding, uses real bitstreams, standard datasets, RGB and grayscale, and both objective and perceptual metrics.  
- Ablations and architectural choices (including GDN rationale) are discussed and analyzed thoroughly.  
- Suggestion: Compare to stronger baselines (HEVC intra/BPG, WebP) and report BD-rate summaries.  
- Suggestion: Report model size, encoding/decoding speed, and memory footprint; discuss deployment considerations.  
- Suggestion: Explore a single model operating across rates (e.g., conditioning on λ) and stronger entropy models (spatial/context, hyperpriors).  
- Suggestion: Provide ablations quantifying GDN vs. ReLU/sigmoids and the impact of marginal model resolution.  
- Suggestion: Discuss generalization to higher resolutions and out-of-distribution content; consider releasing code/models.  
- Question: How is λ selected at encode time, and can a single conditional model cover multiple rate points without retraining?  
- Question: What are the parameter counts and runtimes for 1080p images on CPU/GPU?  
- Question: How much coding gain is left on the table due to the independent-marginal assumption; have simple spatial/context models been tried?  
- Question: How consistent is the relaxed vs. discrete rate/distortion gap across bitrates and images?  
- Question: Do results hold without CABAC adaptation (i.e., with a fixed code), and how sensitive is performance to the learned marginal spline resolution?  
- Question: For RGB, any observations versus YCbCr coding or chroma subsampling?
