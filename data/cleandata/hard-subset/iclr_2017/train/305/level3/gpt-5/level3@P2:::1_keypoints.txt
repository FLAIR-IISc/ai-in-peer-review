- Proposes end-to-end lossy image compression using three cascaded convolution + GDN analysis stages, IGDN synthesis, uniform scalar quantizer, and a practical arithmetic entropy coder.
- Uses a continuous relaxation of quantization via additive uniform noise to enable joint SGD optimization of transforms and entropy model, linked to VAEs.
- Implements an actual CABAC-style entropy coder and reports real bitrates, not just entropy estimates.
- Demonstrates strong rate–distortion performance: large perceptual gains (MS-SSIM) and better PSNR than JPEG/JPEG 2000 on most images, especially at low rates.
- Paper is exceptionally well written, conceptually clear, and convincingly integrates rate–distortion theory and quantization treatment.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: Principled end-to-end optimization of rate–distortion with a correct continuous proxy and solid variational-inference connection.
- Key reason: Strong empirical results with an actual bitstream and perceptually superior reconstructions.
- Problem & motivation: Addresses joint optimization of transform, quantizer, and entropy model, learning transforms and per-channel marginals together.
- Placement in literature: Clearly situates method relative to transform coding, VAEs, and contemporary neural approaches, distinguishing generative vs rate–distortion objectives.
- Scientific rigor: Validates continuous relaxation vs discrete outcomes, evaluates on Kodak and classic images, reports MS-SSIM/PSNR curves, and accounts for side-information.
- Suggestion: Add stronger baselines (e.g., HEVC intra/BPG, SPIHT/EBCOT variants) to strengthen comparisons.
- Suggestion: Include PSNR and MS-SSIM curves for standard images (Lena, Barbara, Peppers, Mandrill) in the main paper or appendix.
- Suggestion: Add a subsection contrasting learned transforms with classical designs (DCT/wavelets, EBCOT, HEVC intra) explaining sources of gains.
- Suggestion: Discuss variable-rate operation and, if possible, show a single-model approach supporting multiple rates.
- Suggestion: Report encoder/decoder runtime, FLOPs, and model size (CPU/GPU) to assess complexity and feasibility versus classical codecs.
- Suggestion: Provide ablation or discussion quantifying loss from using per-channel marginals without spatial/context models and whether simple context would help.
- Suggestion: Clarify color handling and fairness in comparisons (e.g., JPEG 4:2:0 vs RGB) in main text.
- Question: Can you report HEVC intra (BPG) comparisons on Kodak and classic images for PSNR and MS-SSIM?
- Question: How sensitive are learned transforms to training data; tested generalization on other datasets/high-resolution images?
- Question: Can you provide an ablation quantifying trade-offs between model capacity and GDN vs ReLU/sigmoid networks?
- Question: Is there a straightforward way to modulate λ at inference (e.g., scaling latents) without retraining, or what changes are needed for single-model rates?
- Question: How many contexts are used in CABAC setup, how much adaptive gain over non-adaptive coding, and can simple spatial contexts be added without retraining?
- Question: What are encode/decode times and memory footprints for 512×512 RGB on modern CPU/GPU and how do they compare to JPEG 2000?
- Question: Will code, trained models for multiple λ values, and entropy coder configuration be released for reproducibility?
