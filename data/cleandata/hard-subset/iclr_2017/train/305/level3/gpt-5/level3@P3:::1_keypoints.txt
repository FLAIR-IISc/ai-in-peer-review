- Presents an end-to-end learned image compression system jointly optimizing analysis/synthesis transforms, scalar quantizer, and entropy model for rate–distortion performance.
- Key technical contribution: cascaded convolutional stages with GDN/IGDN plus a continuous relaxation of quantization using uniform noise to enable SGD on an R + λD objective.
- Carefully relates the relaxation to VAEs while highlighting important differences for compression.
- Evaluation uses an actual arithmetic entropy coder and reports real bitrates rather than differential entropy.
- Compares against JPEG and JPEG 2000, showing consistent MS-SSIM gains and strong PSNR–rate performance, especially at low rates.
- Paper is exceptionally clear, integrating rate–distortion theory, explaining the relaxation, and situating the work relative to transform coding and generative modeling.
- Decision: Accept.
- Reason 1 for accept: Most convincing learned image compression paper seen—optimizes true R–D trade-off, deploys an actual coder, and improves over JPEG 2000 visually and quantitatively.
- Reason 2 for accept: Methodologically sound and clear, with a workable codec suggesting high impact.
- Problem addressed: Learn a nonlinear transform coding scheme jointly optimized end-to-end to minimize R + λD and outperform standard codecs across rates.
- Motivation/positioning: Well grounded in rate–distortion theory, justified continuous relaxation, and properly distinguished from prior VAE-like approaches.
- Results support claims: Real bitstreams, recognized datasets, empirical validation of the relaxation, and strong MS-SSIM/PSNR results.
- Strength: Visual quality is markedly better with more natural artifacts than blocking/ringing of linear codecs.
- Strength: High rigor and reproducible implementation details; anticipated high impact as a learned compression baseline.
- Suggestion: Include canonical standard images (Lena, Barbara, Mandrill) in the paper/appendix for community reference.
- Suggestion: Strengthen comparisons vs classical state-of-the-art (HEVC-intra/BPG, WebP) and cite SPIHT/EBCOT; report BD-rate summaries vs JPEG 2000.
- Suggestion: Provide ablations for GDN vs ReLU/sigmoid and number of stages, and quantify effects of latent independence and simple spatial entropy adaptation.
- Suggestion: Add compact complexity analysis (model size, encode/decode time on CPU/GPU, memory) to assess deployability.
- Suggestion: Show at least one RGB example in the main paper with per-method bitrates and PSNR/MS-SSIM, and report variability across images (median/IQR) and where JPEG 2000 still wins.
- Questions for authors: Can a single model be conditioned on λ for continuous variable-rate control, and any preliminary results?
- Questions for authors: How much gain would more expressive entropy models (context/hyperprior) provide—anticipated BD-rate improvements?
- Questions for authors: Any bounds/statistics on bias between differential and discrete entropy beyond Figure 4, especially at lowest rates?
- Questions for authors: What are encode/decode times and parameter counts for small/large models on typical hardware versus JPEG 2000?
- Questions for authors: Can you quantify perceptual trade-offs by comparing MSE-trained vs MS-SSIM-trained models at matched rates?
