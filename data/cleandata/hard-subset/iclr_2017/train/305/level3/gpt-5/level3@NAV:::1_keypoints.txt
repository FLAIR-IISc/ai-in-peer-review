- End-to-end learned image compression jointly optimizes nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms, scalar quantization, and an entropy model using a smooth relaxation (additive uniform noise) to enable SGD while staying faithful to the discrete RD problem.  
- Architecture uses cascaded convolutions with GDN/IGDN and includes a practical arithmetic entropy coder to report real bitrates.  
- Empirically delivers consistent PSNR gains over JPEG/JPEG2000, large MS-SSIM improvements, and markedly better visual quality across bitrates.  
- Exposition is exemplary, integrating rate–distortion theory and clearly relating the method to variational autoencoders.  
- Reviewer decision: Accept — most convincing demonstration to date of deep learned image compression grounded in RD theory with real-bitstream evaluation.  
- Strong empirical rigor: uses arithmetic coding, standard Kodak evaluation, qualitative suppression of blocking/ringing, and ablations validating the relaxation.  
- Problem addressed: jointly learning RD-optimal transforms and entropy models while handling non-differentiable quantization via a principled relaxation.  
- Motivation/literature placement: connects classic transform coding, GDN from vision science, and modern VAE approaches, and explains differences from generative modeling objectives.  
- Suggestion: Include quantitative results on legacy test images (Lena, Barbara, Baboon) in the main paper and compare to stronger classical codecs (JPEG2000 variants, HEVC intra/BPG, SPIHT/EBCOT).  
- Suggestion: Clarify positioning versus contemporary neural methods with explicit RD curves and discuss single-model-for-multiple-rates vs per-λ models.  
- Suggestion: Report computational complexity—model size, encoding/decoding time, memory—and quantify side-information overhead in the bitstream.  
- Suggestion: Ablate the contribution of GDN/IGDN versus alternative activations and evaluate more powerful context/adaptive entropy models.  
- Questions: How is λ selected in practice and can a single conditional model cover multiple rates?  
- Questions: How robust is performance across datasets and image content (textures vs edges)? What is the bitrate gap between your CABAC implementation and the measured entropy of latents? Do color models show the same advantages as grayscale at matched bitrates?
