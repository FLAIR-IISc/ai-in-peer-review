- Proposes an end-to-end learned image compression system jointly optimizing transforms, quantization (via relaxation), and entropy modeling for R–D performance.  
- Uses cascaded convolutions with GDN nonlinearity and learned IGDN inverse as core transform blocks.  
- Replaces rounding with additive uniform noise to relax the discrete R–D objective, enabling stochastic-gradient optimization and tight rate/distortion approximation.  
- Trains nonparametric per-channel marginal priors jointly with transforms and reports true bitrates using a CABAC-based entropy coder.  
- Frames the method in relation to VAEs, highlighting similarities and differences relevant to compression (discrete coding, fixed λ, actual bits).  
- Empirically outperforms JPEG/JPEG2000 on PSNR for most images and yields consistently higher MS-SSIM and better visual quality across bitrates on standard datasets.  
- Validates the surrogate loss against actual quantized R–D, and provides detailed architectural/training specifics and fairness considerations in averaging R–D.  
- Decision: Accept — motivated by a clean framework, principled relaxation, and strong empirical results with real bitstreams.  
- Paper addresses joint learning of analysis/synthesis transforms and entropy model to eliminate hand-engineered transforms and manual tuning.  
- Well-motivated and well-situated in literature, leveraging transform coding theory, GDN prior work, and clarifying VAE connections.  
- Claims are supported by rigorous evidence: real entropy coding, side-information accounting, extensive qualitative and quantitative evaluations.  
- Suggestion: train a single model conditioned on λ or use progressive coding to cover multiple rates and reduce storage/maintenance.  
- Suggestion: improve entropy modeling beyond independent marginals with spatially adaptive priors, hyperpriors, or autoregressive/context models.  
- Suggestion: add comparisons to modern baselines like BPG/HEVC-intra to strengthen empirical claims.  
- Suggestion: report encoding/decoding runtimes, memory usage, and model size to assess deployment feasibility.  
- Suggestion: provide experiments with perceptual losses (e.g., MS-SSIM) or mixed losses, especially for low-rate operating points.  
- Suggestion: include ablation studies on GDN vs. standard activations, number of stages/filters, and prior parameterization.  
- Suggestion: clarify color handling choices, chroma subsampling, and their impact on R–D and perceptual quality.  
- Asks about sensitivity to independent marginal prior assumption and whether simple spatial context models/hyperpriors were tried and their bit savings.  
- Asks if conditioning on λ or scale was tried to enable one model across rates and how close that got to per-λ optima.  
- Asks whether alternatives to additive noise (straight-through estimators, annealed relaxations) were tested and their effect on convergence/R–D.  
- Asks how the support of piecewise-linear priors is adapted and how tail mismatches affect coding loss.  
- Requests details on constraints ensuring GDN/IGDN invertibility and numerical stability and any observed failure modes.  
- Asks which internal color space and chroma subsampling (if any) were used and potential gains from learned luma/chroma decoupling.  
- Requests encode/decode time and memory for 512×512 and 2K images on CPU/GPU, and whether fast approximations were tested.  
- Asks about generalization to high-resolution photographs and any degradation in prior match or entropy coding efficiency.  
- Asks for average proportion of side information in the bitstream and whether adaptive coding reduces it.  
- Asks about robustness to training corpus choice and data augmentation decisions.
