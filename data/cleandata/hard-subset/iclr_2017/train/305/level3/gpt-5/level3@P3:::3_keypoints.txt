- Proposes an end-to-end learned image compression system using GDN/IGDN transforms, scalar quantization, and a learned nonparametric entropy model optimized for R+λD.  
- Introduces a differentiable rate surrogate by replacing quantization with additive uniform noise and learning marginals to enable backpropagation while approximating the discrete problem.  
- Empirically shows the relaxation tracks discrete rate/distortion well.  
- Demonstrates superior RD performance versus JPEG/JPEG2000 (especially at low bitrates) with improved perceptual quality (MS-SSIM) and fewer transform artifacts.  
- Connects the formulation to VAEs while clarifying differences and reports actual bitrates using an implemented arithmetic coder.  
- Argues the differentiable rate approach is broadly useful for tasks requiring differentiable approximations of discrete coding objectives.  

Decision: Accept
- Reason 1: Strong empirical performance, practical relevance, and real bitstreams with competitive/superior RD and visual quality.  
- Reason 2: Principled, general differentiable rate relaxation that enables end-to-end training and has potential impact beyond this application.  

Supporting arguments
- Tackles learning a globally optimal transform coding system for chosen points on the RD curve rather than optimizing components in isolation.  
- Well-grounded in transform coding and rate–distortion theory, leverages perceptual insights (GDN), and situates itself relative to VAEs with emphasis on discrete coding and real-bit evaluation.  
- Provides multiple forms of evidence: validation of the relaxation, use of a real entropy coder, visual comparisons and MS-SSIM gains, and PSNR improvements with candid discussion of exceptions.  
- Authors' response in pre-review convinced reviewer that per-λ learned transforms plus marginals strike a practical balance between optimality and complexity.  
- Architecture, training, and evaluation are clearly described; relationship to VAEs is insightful; ablations are limited but indicate GDN/IGDN parameter efficiency.  

Additional feedback
- Include comparisons to stronger baselines (e.g., HEVC-intra/BPG) and/or enhance entropy model with more spatial/context modeling.  
- Consider a single variable-rate model conditioned on λ (or per-channel scaling) to trace the RD curve.  
- Expand ablations comparing GDN vs. ReLU/other nonlinearities at matched parameter counts and quantify trade-offs.  
- Discuss impact of independence assumptions and explore small-context autoregressive priors or hyperpriors to tighten rates.  
- Report encode/decode runtimes, parameter counts, memory footprint, and quantify side-information overhead in bpp; clarify color/chroma handling.  

Questions for the authors
- How sensitive is training to the uniform noise width being exactly one, and did you try learning per-channel quantization step sizes or scale parameters?  
- What is the measured gap between differential entropy under the factorized model and the achieved arithmetic-coded rate, and have you tried lightweight spatial/context modeling?  
- Do encoder outputs ever “fill” a bounded range uniformly at any λ; can you show representative latent histograms across rates?  
- Have you experimented with conditioning on λ (or a gain vector) to share one model across RD points, and how does that compare to per-λ training?  
- Did you observe optimization instabilities with GDN/IGDN (e.g., parameter blow-up), and how crucial were the parameterizations/normalizations; guidance on depth/width vs. RD?  
- How does performance scale to high-resolution images and datasets with different statistics; any evidence of overfitting in learned marginals?  
- What is the average bitstream overhead for transmitting λ and model indices, and how does this scale with more operating points?
