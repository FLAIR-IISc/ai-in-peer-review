- Proposes an end-to-end image compression system jointly optimizing analysis/synthesis transforms, a uniform quantizer, and a learned entropy model using GDN/IGDN nonlinearities.
- Introduces a practical relaxation of quantization by replacing it with additive uniform noise during training and learning per-channel marginal densities nonparametrically.
- The relaxation yields a differentiable surrogate for rate and distortion, enabling SGD optimization at arbitrary RD points by varying λ.
- Interprets the method as a variational autoencoder, clarifying modeling assumptions and differences (discrete coding targets, explicit λ trade-offs, measured bits).
- Implements an entropy coder (CABAC-style) and reports measured bitrates rather than only entropy estimates.
- Empirically outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 on Kodak and additional images in MS-SSIM across bitrates and often in PSNR, with visibly fewer artifacts.
- The differentiable rate approximation is broadly useful for learning systems that must optimize discrete coding objectives.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reasons for acceptance: principled continuous relaxation + nonparametric marginals enabling true end-to-end RD optimization with real entropy coding.
- Key reasons for acceptance: strong empirical evidence with careful comparisons showing consistent perceptual gains and competitive/better PSNR.
- Motivates the problem: joint optimization is classic but hard due to quantization non-differentiability and high-dimensional dependencies.
- Positions the work well in literature; VAE connection and focus on measured bitrates and specific RD points are strengths relative to prior work.
- Scientific rigor: validates the relaxation by comparing relaxed differential entropy/distortion to realized discrete metrics and discusses side information and coder adaptivity.
- Scope and impact: learned per-channel marginals and differentiable rate proxy are likely reusable beyond image compression; GDN/IGDN is justified and efficient.
- Theoretical concern about networks gaming fixed-form entropy models is mitigated by learning nonparametric marginals and verifying coder rates vs. entropy.
- Suggests exploring single-model variable-rate operation by conditioning networks on λ or a learned quality input.
- Recommends improving entropy modeling with lightweight spatial/channel contexts to approach optimal coding.
- Requests ablation studies on GDN vs. standard activations and on marginal model resolution to quantify component contributions.
- Asks for complexity metrics: encoding/decoding runtime, model size, and memory vs. JPEG 2000 for deployability assessment.
- Recommends using additional modern datasets, per-image RD variance summaries, BD-rate/BD-PSNR metrics, and qualitative comparisons at matched MS-SSIM.
- Advises tightening the VAE derivation and stating conditions under which the surrogate corresponds to a valid likelihood.
- Encourages releasing code/models or detailed hyperparameters, schedules, and compute budgets for reproducibility.
- Questions on realized vs. relaxed entropy: how close are arithmetic coding bitrates to relaxed estimates (average/worst-case gaps; low-rate behavior)?
- Questions about degenerate behaviors: any mode collapse or exploitation of nonparametric marginals and how these are detected/prevented?
- Questions on sensitivity: robustness to spline resolution and marginal range adaptation when coarsening models?
- Questions on single-model feasibility: can transforms be conditioned on λ to yield variable-rate models without RD loss?
- Questions on runtime/memory: encoding/decoding costs for Kodak-size images on CPU/GPU and scaling with channel counts?
- Questions on color handling: did you try alternative color spaces or no chroma subsampling baselines for RGB comparisons?
- Questions on training stability: stability of additive-uniform relaxation at very low rates and any special regularization used?
- Questions on IGDN: more details on invertibility approximation and its impact on reconstruction error, especially at low rates?
