Summary of contributions
- The paper proposes a complete lossy image compression system trained end-to-end for rate–distortion (RD) performance. The codec comprises a learned nonlinear analysis transform, uniform scalar quantization, an entropy coder, and a learned nonlinear synthesis transform.
- Key technical ingredients include cascaded convolutional stages coupled with generalized divisive normalization (GDN/IGDN), a continuous relaxation of quantization via additive uniform noise to enable SGD, and nonparametric marginal models for the latent code, trained jointly with the transforms.
- The authors relate their relaxed RD objective to variational autoencoders (VAEs), clarifying similarities and differences, and they evaluate with actual bitstreams using a CABAC-based entropy coder rather than just entropy estimates.
- Empirically, the method attains strong RD performance versus JPEG/JPEG2000 on Kodak and delivers markedly improved visual quality, with notable gains in MS-SSIM and broadly competitive PSNR, especially at low bitrates. The paper is clearly written and places the work well in the literature.

Decision
Weak accept. My decision is driven by (1) strong empirical performance with a complete, deployable codec (actual bitrates, high perceptual quality) and (2) a solid, well-motivated end-to-end RD training framework. I am concerned about limited novelty relative to the authors’ prior work and missing ablations that would isolate where the gains come from. If the authors add clarifications and ablations about the entropy coding and the role of architectural choices, I would raise my confidence.

Supporting arguments
- What problem is tackled? Learning an end-to-end image compression pipeline that is RD-optimal in practice (for a chosen λ), including analysis/synthesis transforms, quantization, and entropy coding, with training made feasible via a continuous proxy of the discrete objective.
- Is the approach well motivated and well placed? Yes. The RD formulation, uniform-noise relaxation, and joint optimization are well justified and connected to prior art in transform coding, quantization theory, and VAEs. The paper extends the authors’ earlier work by scaling to deeper convolutional transforms, adopting GDN/IGDN, and implementing an adaptive arithmetic coder with actual bitstreams. The positioning relative to contemporary learned codecs (e.g., Toderici et al., Gregor et al.) is fair and insightful.
- Do the results support the claims? Largely yes. Visual examples consistently favor the proposed method; MS-SSIM improvements are substantial across images and rates; PSNR is competitive and often better at low rates. Reporting actual bitrates adds credibility. However:
  - The contribution of the adaptive entropy coder versus the learned transforms is not disentangled. An ablation with a fixed/nonadaptive coder or a mismatched model would strengthen the claims.
  - The paper attributes some theoretical correspondence to VAEs; the equivalence is qualified, but a technical issue remains (see below).
  - The choice to optimize for MSE (rather than a perceptual distortion) is at odds with earlier evidence from the same group and could limit perceptual optimality. The evaluation also leans on PSNR; broader perceptual evaluation would be valuable.

Additional feedback to improve the paper (helpful, not all decisive)
- Clarify the VAE connection. In Sec. 3.1, for non-Euclidean/perceptual metrics and/or non-affine gp, the normalization constant Z generally depends on θ via gs and may depend on λ; please make this explicit. As written, the equivalence is more limited than suggested. If the earlier claim was tied to Eq. (10), please double-check numbering and ensure the correct statement about the partition function.
- Ablations to isolate sources of gain:
  - Entropy coding: compare the proposed adaptive CABAC setup to (i) a nonadaptive arithmetic coder using frozen marginals and (ii) a simple per-symbol Huffman code. Report the bitrate delta and how much on-line adaptation contributes.
  - Architecture: compare GDN/IGDN to ReLU/LeakyReLU with similar parameter counts; vary number of stages; report parameter count and runtime. This would clarify how much GDN itself contributes versus depth/capacity.
  - Latent marginal modeling: compare learned nonparametric marginals to fixed parametric choices (e.g., Gaussian/Laplacian/mixture).
- Metric choice and evaluation:
  - Please justify optimizing for MSE given your previous perceptual work. Could you include one model trained with MS-SSIM (or your Laplacian-based metric) for a direct perceptual-RD comparison?
  - Expand perceptual evaluation: user studies or additional metrics (e.g., LPIPS), if feasible. At minimum, include MS-SSIM curves for all images alongside PSNR in the supplementary.
- Rate control and model count: Training separate models per λ simplifies RD optimization but complicates deployment. Can you demonstrate a single model that supports multiple rates (e.g., via scale parameters or learned importance maps) and compare RD to the per-λ models?
- Entropy coder details and practicality:
  - Provide more detail on context design, adaptation schedule, and how spatial sharing within channels is implemented. How many contexts? How quickly do the models adapt on an image?
  - Quantify bitstream overhead (image size, λ index, etc.) and show it is negligible across rates.
  - Report encoding/decoding speed, memory footprint, and whether CPU-only decoding is feasible. This will help assess practicality versus JPEG2000.
- Reproducibility: Consider releasing code/models or giving comprehensive implementation details to facilitate adoption.

Questions for the authors
1. How much of the RD gain comes from adaptive entropy coding versus the learned transforms? Please report bitrates with (a) your adaptive CABAC, (b) nonadaptive arithmetic coding using the frozen learned marginals, and (c) a simple code (e.g., Huffman). 
2. What is the quantitative contribution of GDN/IGDN relative to standard pointwise nonlinearities at matched parameter budgets and depth? Can a ReLU-based model with slightly higher capacity close the gap?
3. In Sec. 3.1, does the normalization constant Z(λ) depend on θ through gs for general d and gp? If so, please clarify the precise conditions under which the VAE correspondence holds, and correct any equations if needed.
4. Why optimize for MSE instead of a perceptual metric, given your prior results? Could you include a perceptually trained variant and compare RD under MS-SSIM (and perhaps PSNR)?
5. How sensitive is performance to the nonparametric marginal modeling (spline resolution, range adaptation, per-channel sharing)? Any stability issues during joint training?
6. Can you provide encode/decode runtimes and memory usage (CPU and GPU), and quantify side-information overhead?
7. Is it possible to train a single model that supports multiple λ via a conditioning mechanism, and how close would its RD be to the per-λ envelope?

Overall, this is a strong, well-executed paper with compelling empirical results and a complete codec. Addressing the above clarifications and ablations would substantially strengthen the submission.
