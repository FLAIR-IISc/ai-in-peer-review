1. Summary
This paper presents an end-to-end learned image compression system that jointly optimizes nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms, scalar quantization, and an entropy model for rate–distortion performance. The key technical contribution is a smooth, principled relaxation of the discrete rate–distortion objective by replacing quantization with additive uniform noise, enabling stochastic gradient descent while remaining faithful to the discrete problem. The architecture leverages cascaded convolutions with generalized divisive normalization (GDN/IGDN), and the authors implement a practical arithmetic entropy coder to report actual bitrates. The method delivers consistent PSNR gains over JPEG/JPEG2000 for most images, large MS-SSIM improvements, and strikingly better visual quality across bitrates. The exposition is exemplary; rate–distortion theory is integrated cleanly, and the relationship to variational autoencoders is carefully delineated.

2. Decision
Accept. Reasons: (i) Most convincing demonstration to date of deep learned image compression grounded in rate–distortion theory with real-bitstream evaluation; (ii) strong empirical results and scientific rigor, including ablations validating the relaxation.

3. Supporting arguments
- Problem: Achieve RD-optimal image compression by learning the transform and entropy model jointly while handling non-differentiable quantization. The paper tackles this head-on with a theoretically motivated relaxation and complete codec.
- Motivation/literature: The approach is well-placed, connecting classic transform coding, GDN from vision science, and modern VAEs, while explaining why generative modeling objectives differ from RD optimization.
- Evidence/rigor: Uses actual arithmetic coding, evaluates on standard Kodak images with PSNR and MS-SSIM, and presents qualitative results that convincingly suppress blocking/ringing typical of linear transform codecs. The empirical check that the relaxed objective tracks the discrete RD behavior supports the claims.

4. Suggestions and questions (to help improve the paper; not prerequisites for acceptance)
- Please include quantitative results on standard legacy test images (Lena, Barbara, Baboon) in the main paper, not only online, and add references/comparisons to stronger classical codecs (e.g., JPEG2000 variants, HEVC intra/BPG, SPIHT/EBCOT baselines) to position the gains more clearly.
- Clarify positioning versus contemporary neural methods with explicit RD curves (e.g., Toderici et al., Gregor et al.) and discuss pros/cons (single model for multiple rates vs per-λ models).
- Report computational complexity: model size, encoding/decoding time, and memory; quantify side-information overhead in the bitstream.
- Analyze the contribution of GDN/IGDN vs alternative activations (ablation) and the effect of more powerful context/adaptive entropy models.
- Questions: 
  • How is λ selected in practice, and can a single conditional model cover multiple rates? 
  • How robust is performance across datasets and image content (textures vs edges)? 
  • What is the bitrate gap between your CABAC implementation and the measured entropy of the learned latents? 
  • Do color models show the same advantages as grayscale at matched bitrates?
