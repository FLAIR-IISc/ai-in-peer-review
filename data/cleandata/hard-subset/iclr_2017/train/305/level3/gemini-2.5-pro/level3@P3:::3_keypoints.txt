- Proposes an end-to-end learned lossy image compression system with nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms and a uniform scalar quantizer, trained jointly via SGD.  
- Uses deep convolutional transforms with biologically-inspired generalized divisive normalization (GDN) nonlinearity.  
- Main technical contribution: replace quantization with additive uniform noise during training to create a differentiable proxy for the rate-distortion loss.  
- Connects the method to variational autoencoders, clarifying similarities and key differences (optimizing rate-distortion vs. likelihood).  
- Empirically outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 on PSNR and especially MS-SSIM, producing noticeably better visual quality and fewer artifacts.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: presents a complete, highly effective system that convincingly beats strong, widely-used baselines.  
- Key reason: the differentiable relaxation for the rate term is elegant, addresses a core training challenge, and has broader applicability.  
- Strength: joint end-to-end optimization of transform and quantizer overcomes suboptimal separate-component pipelines.  
- Validation: empirical results (e.g., Fig. 4) show the relaxed loss closely matches true discrete metrics.  
- Justification: modeling marginal distributions p(y_i) is preferable to forcing a simple prior, allowing more flexible, efficient representations.  
- Qualitative advantage: the model discards information in a perceptually pleasing way (smooths textures, preserves contours), aligning with MS-SSIM gains.  
- Improvement suggestion: include quantitative computational complexity/runtime comparisons vs. JPEG/JPEG 2000.  
- Improvement suggestion: perform ablation studies on architectural choices (GDN vs. ReLU, depth, channel counts).  
- Improvement suggestion: explore learned, spatially-aware entropy models to capture dependencies between coefficients.  
- Question for authors: trade-offs between learning p(y) versus matching a fixed prior (e.g., uniform or Gaussian) for simpler entropy coding?  
- Question for authors: can the continuous relaxation of quantization be applied to other domains with discrete bottlenecks (discrete latent models, model compression)?
