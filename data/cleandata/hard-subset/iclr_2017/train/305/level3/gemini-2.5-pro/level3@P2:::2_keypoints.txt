- End-to-end lossy image compression using convolutional transforms with GDN, quantization relaxed via additive noise, trained with rate–distortion SGD; connected to VAE and reports actual bitrates with an entropy coder.  
- Decision: Weak Accept.  
- Strength: Produces visually superior reconstructions and significant MS-SSIM gains over JPEG/JPEG 2000, avoiding blocking/ringing artifacts.  
- Strength: Complete practical pipeline including an adaptive entropy coder and measured bit rates.  
- Weakness: Core training framework largely extends prior work (Ballé et al., 2016), so novelty is limited.  
- Weakness: No ablation study to disentangle gains from deeper architecture versus adaptive entropy coding.  
- Weakness: Optimized for MSE despite MS-SSIM evaluation and authors' background in perceptual modeling, raising questions about objective choice.  
- Theoretical concern: Equation (16) may be incorrect if normalization constant Z depends on gs(ỹ;θ) for non-Euclidean translation-invariant metrics, which would affect the VAE equivalence.  
- Empirical suggestion: Provide quantitative comparisons of GDN vs simpler nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU), even as appendix results.  
- Questions for authors: Why optimize MSE rather than a perceptual metric; provide ablation quantifying adaptive coder contribution; give CABAC implementation/context details and whether adaptation gains are consistent; confirm independence of Z(λ) from gs(ỹ;θ) and implications for VAE correspondence.
