- Introduces an end-to-end image compression framework with convolutional analysis/synthesis transforms using GDN nonlinearities.  
- Key technical contribution: a differentiable proxy for the non-differentiable quantizer by replacing quantization with additive uniform noise during training.  
- Uses the differential entropy of the continuous representation to approximate discrete entropy for rate estimation.  
- Optimizes the full model with SGD for a rate-distortion objective.  
- Models trained for MSE outperform JPEG and JPEG 2000 on standard test sets in PSNR at low bit rates.  
- Shows substantial gains in perceptual quality (MS-SSIM and visual inspection), reducing blocking and ringing artifacts.  
- Learned nonlinear transforms outperform fixed linear transforms (DCT/wavelets) by discarding information in a perceptually pleasant way.  
- Provides a thoughtful comparison to VAEs, clarifying differences and applicability to lossy compression across the rate-distortion curve.  
- Argues that modeling latent entropy directly is more effective and elegant than relying on networks to produce entropy-optimized codes.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Primary reason 1: The differentiable rate approximation overcomes a fundamental barrier and is likely to be influential beyond this application.  
- Primary reason 2: Strong empirical results, including superior rate-distortion and markedly better subjective visual quality.  
- Suggestion 1 (improvement): Perform formal ablation studies comparing GDN to ReLU/tanh with matched capacity/training to validate GDN benefits.  
- Suggestion 2 (improvement): Explore more sophisticated or learned context models for entropy coding beyond raster-scan CABAC.  
- Suggestion 3 (improvement): Investigate methods to support a single model across multiple bit rates (conditioning on λ).  
- Question 1: Are GDN/IGDN parameters shared across spatial locations within a channel?  
- Question 2: Have you studied parameter relationships between models trained for adjacent λ values or fine-tuning across λ?  
- Question 3: How well does the learned representation generalize to out-of-distribution images (medical, technical, CG) and do natural-looking artifacts become a disadvantage?
