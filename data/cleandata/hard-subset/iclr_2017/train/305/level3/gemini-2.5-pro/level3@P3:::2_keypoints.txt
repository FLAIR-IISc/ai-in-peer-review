- End-to-end learned lossy image compression using cascaded convolutions + GDN, a uniform quantizer relaxed with additive uniform noise, trained by minimizing a rate–distortion loss.  
- Authors draw a connection between the rate–distortion objective and Variational Autoencoders and use an adaptive arithmetic entropy coder to produce actual bitstreams.  
- Method yields substantial gains over JPEG 2000 in PSNR and especially perceptual quality (MS-SSIM and visual inspection).  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: A complete, well-engineered system with strong empirical improvements and more natural, less distracting artifacts than traditional codecs.  
- Key reason 2: Clear, principled end-to-end framework with a useful (if imperfect) connection to VAEs.  
- Empirical strengths: consistent outperformance on the Kodak dataset, graceful degradation, preserved contours and edges, and large MS-SSIM margins.  
- Extensions over prior work: deeper multi-stage architecture and integration of a practical CABAC-based adaptive entropy coder make the approach viable for real compressed bitrates.  
- Methodological highlight: additive-noise relaxation of the quantizer is elegant; the VAE discussion helps position the work but compression optimizes a λ-controlled trade-off rather than pure likelihood.  
- Shortcomings: surprising choice to optimize MSE despite emphasis on visual quality, and missing ablation study to disentangle gains from architecture versus adaptive entropy coding.  
- Technical correction: the claimed VAE equivalence for non-Euclidean metrics is likely flawed because the partition function Z depends on the decoder output, so log Z cannot be treated as a constant when optimizing decoder parameters.  
- Suggested improvements: provide an ablation comparing adaptive vs non-adaptive entropy coding to isolate contributions.  
- Suggested improvements: justify the choice of MSE over perceptual metrics and discuss challenges of optimizing perceptual metrics for color images.  
- Suggested improvements: better quantify the entropy coder’s effect (e.g., initial entropy estimate vs final bit rate for examples) and expand discussion/figures.  
- Reviewer note: willing to raise score if authors convincingly address the VAE equivalence concern and provide ablation/insight into the entropy coder’s contribution.
