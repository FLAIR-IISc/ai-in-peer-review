- Novel end-to-end neural image compression framework using cascaded conv layers with GDN nonlinearity, a scalar quantizer, and a learned entropy model.  
- Key technical contribution: replace quantization with additive uniform noise to create a differentiable proxy for rate-distortion, enabling SGD training.  
- Paper connects method to VAEs while clearly distinguishing compression’s λ-based rate-distortion objective from VAE likelihood goals.  
- Empirical results: outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 on Kodak in PSNR and MS-SSIM, with markedly better perceptual quality.  
- Decision: Accept — practical significance due to state-of-the-art performance and substantial visual-quality improvement.  
- Decision: Accept — technical innovation in handling discrete bottlenecks via a differentiable relaxation likely useful beyond compression.  
- Problem framed well: reframes lossy compression as joint end-to-end optimization of nonlinear transforms, quantizer, and entropy model.  
- Motivation and literature placement are strong; GDN choice justified by prior work and natural image statistics.  
- Methodological soundness: continuous relaxation shown to be a low-variance, reasonable proxy for actual quantized rate and distortion.  
- Rigorous evaluation: comparisons on Kodak using PSNR and MS-SSIM, plus implemented CABAC-based entropy coding to report real bitrates.  
- Qualitative results: visual examples show reduced blocking/ringing and more pleasant reconstructions at low bit rates.  
- Suggestion: provide a formal ablation comparing GDN to other nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU) with similar parameter counts.  
- Suggestion: improve entropy model by leveraging spatial context between quantized coefficients for further gains.  
- Suggestion: address practicality of training a separate model per λ and discuss possible rate-adaptive single-model approaches.  
- Question for authors: consider and discuss the alternative of forcing integer-range codes and optimizing for uniform code distributions — potential benefits, trade-offs, and scenarios where it might be competitive.
