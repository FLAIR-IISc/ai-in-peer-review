- Decision: Accept.
- Paper presents an end-to-end learned image compression framework with deep nonlinear analysis/synthesis, uniform quantizer, and context-adaptive arithmetic entropy coder.
- Extends prior rate-distortion optimization to deeper convolutional architectures using generalized divisive normalization (GDN) and adds an adaptive entropy coding scheme.
- Provides a theoretical connection relating the rate-distortion framework to Variational Autoencoders (VAEs).
- Model is trained to minimize a weighted sum of rate (entropy) and distortion (MSE).
- Empirical results show significant improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000 in PSNR, MS-SSIM, and subjective visual quality, especially at low bitrates.
- Strengths: strong state-of-the-art empirical results and a well-written paper that bridges classical rate-distortion theory and modern deep generative models.
- Novelty is limited because the core rate-distortion approach was introduced in prior work; primary new contributions are deeper networks and adaptive entropy coding.
- Concern: reverting to MSE optimization appears a conceptual step backward from prior perceptual-metric work despite visual quality being the paper’s main appeal.
- Potential error in the VAE connection: the partition function in Eq.16 likely depends on the synthesis transform output, so the VAE equivalence may not hold for non-Euclidean distortion metrics.
- Suggested improvement: include an ablation study to separate gains from deeper architecture versus the adaptive entropy coder.
- Suggested improvement: provide more implementation detail and analysis of the adaptive entropy coder’s impact.
- Question 1: Clarify the VAE derivation for non-Euclidean metrics and whether Z(λ) depends on network parameters.
- Question 2: Explain motivation for reverting to MSE and why quantitative evaluation emphasizes PSNR over perceptual metrics.
- Question 3: Provide results using a non-adaptive entropy coder to isolate the adaptive coder’s contribution.
