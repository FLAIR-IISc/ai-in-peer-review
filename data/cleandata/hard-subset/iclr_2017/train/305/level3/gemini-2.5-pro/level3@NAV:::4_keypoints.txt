- Proposes an end-to-end deep learning framework for lossy image compression that jointly optimizes nonlinear analysis transform, quantizer, and nonlinear synthesis transform for a rate–distortion objective.  
- Core technical contribution is a continuous relaxation that replaces nondifferentiable quantization with additive uniform noise, enabling training with stochastic gradient descent.  
- Empirical results show the method outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 in rate–distortion metrics and yields substantially better visual quality (PSNR, MS-SSIM, and qualitative examples).  
- The paper is scientifically rigorous, validates the relaxation empirically, and draws an insightful connection to variational autoencoders while noting practical differences for compression.  
- Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance based on the novel, principled approach and thorough, convincing empirical evaluation.  
- Suggestion: add an ablation study or discussion on sensitivity to architectural choices (number of channels, filter sizes).  
- Suggestion: report comparisons using simpler nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU) and quantify extra parameters or stages needed relative to GDN/IGDN.  
- Suggestion: consider and/or report results using more sophisticated spatial-context models in the latent-space entropy coder beyond the current raster-scan context.
