- Presents an end-to-end learned lossy image compression framework with nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms and uniform quantization.  
- Uses generalized divisive normalization (GDN) nonlinearities in the transforms, motivated by neuroscience and density modeling.  
- Introduces a continuous relaxation of quantization by adding uniform noise, enabling gradient-based training and approximating rate and distortion.  
- Connects the relaxed objective to the VAE framework while highlighting differences, notably explicit optimization of the rate-distortion curve via λ.  
- Empirical results outperform JPEG and JPEG 2000 on the Kodak dataset in PSNR and especially MS-SSIM.  
- Visual examples show fewer blocking/ringing artifacts and smoother, more structurally coherent images at low bit rates.  
- Recommendation: strongly accept based on novelty, principled approach, and strong empirical gains.  
- Central technical contribution is the additive-noise relaxation of rounding, validated as a reasonable proxy (Fig. 4).  
- GDN/IGDN appears parameter-efficient and well-motivated; other nonlinearities might work but GDN is effective.  
- The system is a complete practical pipeline, including an entropy coder (CABAC) and reported actual bit rates.  
- Model tends to discard information in a perceptually graceful way, trading PSNR for better perceptual quality.  
- Suggested ablation: include a quantitative comparison showing GDN vs ReLU models with similar parameter counts.  
- Suggested addition: report concrete encoding/decoding runtimes on CPU/GPU and compare to baseline OpenJPEG.  
- Limitation: separate model trained per λ (rate); recommend discussing how to extend to a single rate-adaptive model (e.g., conditioning on λ or recurrent design).  
- Questions for authors: generalization to out-of-distribution images (medical, line drawings, text); sensitivity to architectural choices (depth, filters, downsampling); expected trade-offs if optimizing MS-SSIM instead of MSE and implications for the VAE view.
