- Proposes an end-to-end trainable deep learning framework for lossy image compression with nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms and a uniform quantizer.  
- Introduces a continuous, differentiable proxy for quantization that enables optimization via stochastic gradient descent.  
- Jointly optimizes a weighted rate–distortion loss for compression.  
- Demonstrates outperforming JPEG and JPEG 2000, particularly at low bit rates.  
- Shows substantial perceptual-quality improvements (natural-looking simplifications vs blocking/ringing), supported by visual examples and MS-SSIM.  
- Decision: Strong accept recommended.  
- Main reasons for accept: principled integration of rate–distortion theory into an end-to-end deep framework, and compelling empirical/perceptual results.  
- Paper is well-written, theoretically motivated, and effectively bridges discrete compression and continuous optimization.  
- Stronger baseline choice (JPEG 2000) and striking visual comparisons highlight limitations of linear transform codes.  
- Suggestion: add PSNR and MS-SSIM tables on standard test images (e.g., Lena, Barbara, Baboon) for broader literature comparison.  
- Suggestion: position and compare against stronger classical codecs like BPG (HEVC-based).  
- Suggestion: provide more direct/quantitative comparisons to other neural-network-based compression methods.  
- Question: what is the computational complexity of encoder/decoder vs a software JPEG 2000 implementation?  
- Question: can a single model be trained to operate across multiple rate–distortion tradeoffs instead of training separate models per λ?
