- Proposes an end-to-end learned lossy image compression framework with nonlinear three-stage convolutional analysis/synthesis transforms and GDN nonlinearity.  
- Uses a uniform scalar quantizer and a continuous relaxation (additive uniform noise) to enable gradient-based joint optimization of transforms and an entropy model.  
- Draws a connection between the relaxed training objective and VAEs while emphasizing the different goals of compression versus generative modeling.  
- Claims superior performance to JPEG and JPEG 2000 in rate-distortion metrics (especially MS-SSIM) and in visual quality, avoiding blocking and ringing artifacts.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Primary strength: novel, principled end-to-end optimization for a rate-distortion objective with a well-justified quantizer relaxation.  
- Secondary strength: convincing empirical results, including a full entropy coder for fair bit-rate comparisons and strong qualitative/quantitative improvements.  
- The quantizer relaxation approximates quantization error and links discrete entropy to differential entropy; validated empirically (Fig. 4).  
- The λ trade-off parameter allows operating at different points on the rate-distortion curve, distinguishing compression from likelihood-focused generative models.  
- Implemented CABAC-based entropy coding to report actual compressed sizes, strengthening comparisons to standards.  
- Visual results show naturalistic simplification: preserved contours/edges and discarded fine textures, reflected in large MS-SSIM gains.  
- Suggestion: report concrete computational costs (encoding/decoding times on specific hardware) to assess practical viability versus optimized codecs.  
- Suggestion: include comparative experiments to quantify GDN’s parameter-efficiency versus standard nonlinearities like ReLU.  
- Suggestion: provide ablation studies (number of stages, channels, filter sizes) to justify architectural choices.  
- Question: training a separate model per λ is impractical—have you considered conditioning the network on λ to cover multiple bit rates with one model?  
- Question: the entropy model assumes marginal independence—could a context-adaptive model that captures spatial dependencies yield further compression gains?
