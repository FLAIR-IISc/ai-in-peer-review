- Presents an end-to-end optimized DNN framework for lossy image compression with analysis transform, quantizer, synthesis transform and GDN nonlinearity.  
- Core contribution: a continuous relaxation of quantization via additive uniform noise enabling direct R+λD optimization with SGD and a connection to variational autoencoders.  
- Demonstrates better performance than JPEG and JPEG 2000 on Kodak in PSNR, especially MS-SSIM, and notably improved subjective visual quality.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Reason 1: Principled, elegant integration of classical rate-distortion theory into a deep learning objective; relaxation is clever and theory/VAEs connection is clear.  
- Reason 2: Strong empirical results against robust baselines, with large gains particularly at low bit rates and visually pleasing artifacts.  
- Strength: Rigorous end-to-end formulation grounded in information theory, enabling tracing of the full rate-distortion curve.  
- Strength: Validation of the continuous approximation and use of a proper arithmetic coder to report actual bit rates adds scientific rigor.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and consistent across bit rates; MS-SSIM supports the subjective improvements shown in visual examples.  
- Feedback: Include broader benchmarking on classic “torture test” images (e.g., Lena, Barbara, Baboon) with per-image RD curves.  
- Feedback: Position the work relative to modern classical codecs (e.g., BPG/HEVC); cite/discuss and, if possible, compare or contextualize performance.  
- Feedback: Provide a clearer statement/table contrasting this method with other neural approaches (e.g., Toderici et al., Gregor et al.) regarding formulation and performance.  
- Rebuttal question: How do you expect your method to perform relative to modern codecs like BPG?  
- Rebuttal question: How critical is the GDN/IGDN architecture for the observed visual quality versus deeper networks with standard activations like ReLU?
