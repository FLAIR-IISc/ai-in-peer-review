- Paper presents an end-to-end learned lossy image compression system with encoder (nonlinear analysis transform), uniform quantizer (relaxed with additive uniform noise), and decoder (nonlinear synthesis transform) built from convolutional layers and GDN nonlinearities.  
- Authors enable end-to-end SGD training by replacing quantization with additive uniform noise and connect the relaxed rate–distortion objective to the VAE loss.  
- The full system includes an adaptive arithmetic entropy coder (CABAC) and is trained to minimize a weighted sum of actual bit rate and MSE distortion.  
- Empirically, the method outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 in rate–distortion and produces substantially better perceptual image quality, supported by visual examples and MS-SSIM.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Primary reason 1: The paper delivers a complete, practical, well-engineered learned codec with state-of-the-art results and striking perceptual improvements over standards like JPEG 2000.  
- Primary reason 2: Methodology is sound and well-motivated; the quantizer relaxation is elegant and the VAE connection unifies classical rate–distortion and generative modeling concepts.  
- Strength: Scaling to deeper multi-stage transforms plus integration of a practical entropy coding backend allows reporting actual bit rates and fair comparisons to standards.  
- Strength: Visual results avoid blocking/ringing artifacts and preserve natural appearance at low bit rates; MS-SSIM quantitatively confirms consistent gains.  
- Strength: Theoretical Section 3.1 provides a clear, useful link between rate–distortion optimization and the ELBO in variational inference.  
- Feedback 1: Optimizing for MSE (rather than a perceptual metric like MS-SSIM) is surprising and warrants either additional perceptual-optimized results or deeper discussion of trade-offs and challenges.  
- Feedback 2: Lack of ablation study—unclear how much gains come from deeper architecture vs. context-adaptive entropy coding; report performance with a non-adaptive coder to quantify CABAC's contribution.  
- Feedback 3: Clarify the VAE equivalence for non-Euclidean metrics—normalization constant Z(λ) may depend on the synthesis network and complicate the claimed proportionality.  
- Question 1: Why optimize for MSE instead of a perceptual metric—were there technical challenges applying perceptual losses to the deeper, color-image model?  
- Question 2: What is the bits-per-pixel performance gain attributable specifically to the context-adaptive CABAC vs. a non-adaptive arithmetic coder using learned marginals?  
- Question 3: In Equation 16, does Z(λ) depend on gs(ỹ;θ), and if so, how does that affect the claimed equivalence between rate–distortion minimization and KL divergence minimization?
