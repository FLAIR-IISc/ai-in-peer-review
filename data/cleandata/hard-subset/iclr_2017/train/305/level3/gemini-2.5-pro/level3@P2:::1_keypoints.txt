- Proposes a complete end-to-end learned framework for lossy image compression using convolutional layers and GDN nonlinearity with a uniform quantizer and entropy coder.  
- Uses additive uniform noise as a continuous proxy to enable joint end-to-end optimization despite quantizer non-differentiability.  
- Connects the method to variational autoencoders while clarifying practical differences needed for compression.  
- Demonstrates a full practical system with an implemented entropy coder reporting actual bit rates.  
- Decision: Accept — considered an excellent and highly impactful paper.  
- Key reason 1: Principled, sound methodology integrating rate-distortion theory with effective handling of quantization for end-to-end training.  
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical results showing significant improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000, especially in perceptual quality (MS-SSIM).  
- Thorough evaluation comparing to strong baselines and using both PSNR and MS-SSIM metrics plus compelling visual examples.  
- Use of a real entropy coder (CABAC) strengthens claims by providing actual bit-rate measurements rather than estimates.  
- Empirical validation that the continuous relaxation is a reliable proxy for discrete rate–distortion during optimization.  
- Suggestion: Mention or cite modern engineered standards (e.g., H.265/HEVC-Intra, VVC-Intra) to contextualize results relative to state-of-the-art classical codecs.  
- Suggestion: Clarify which model components (e.g., GDN vs. ReLU) are most critical to the performance gains.  
- Question for authors: Have you tried directly optimizing an MS-SSIM-based loss, and if so, how does it affect visual quality and PSNR trade-offs?
