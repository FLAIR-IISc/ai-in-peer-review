- Presents an end-to-end lossy image compression framework optimized directly for rate-distortion via learned nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms and quantization.
- Implements transforms as a three-stage convolutional network using Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN) inspired by biological vision.
- Introduces a continuous relaxation of quantization by adding uniform noise during training to enable joint SGD optimization.
- Discusses the relationship to VAEs while clearly distinguishing the different objectives (compression R-D vs. generative modeling).
- Trains separate models for different R-D points and evaluates on standard datasets.
- Demonstrates empirical improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000 in PSNR and substantially better perceptual quality measured by MS-SSIM.
- Shows dramatic visual quality gains with fewer blocking/ringing artifacts compared to traditional codecs.
- Validates the additive-noise approximation to rate-distortion (notably in Figure 4).
- Implements a full entropy coder (CABAC-based) and reports actual bit rates for fair comparison.
- Argues GDN and related architectural choices are well-motivated by prior density modeling and natural image statistics.
- Reports that optimizing for MSE nonetheless yields strong perceptual results due to the learned nonlinear transforms.
- Recommendation: Accept — paper is novel, principled, and highly effective.
- Suggests reporting quantitative computational complexity (e.g., MACs or runtime) vs. JPEG 2000 to assess practicality.
- Recommends ablation studies on stages, channels, and nonlinearity (e.g., ReLU baseline) to justify architectural choices.
- Suggests summarizing dataset-level R-D gains (e.g., Bjontegaard-delta) despite averaging difficulties.
- Asks whether a single model controllable at test-time for multiple bit rates was explored and how it would compare to separate models.
- Questions potential bias of the continuous-rate estimator at low bit rates and its impact on optimization and visual quality.
- Asks about IGDN as a one-iteration inverse of GDN: trade-offs of more iterations and whether a non-symmetric decoder could improve results.
