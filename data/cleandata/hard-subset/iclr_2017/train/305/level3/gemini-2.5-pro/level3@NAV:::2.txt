This paper presents an end-to-end framework for learned image compression. The proposed method consists of a deep, nonlinear analysis and synthesis transform, a uniform quantizer, and a context-adaptive arithmetic entropy coder. The authors extend their previous work on rate-distortion optimization to deeper convolutional architectures featuring generalized divisive normalization (GDN) and introduce an adaptive entropy coding scheme. A key contribution is relating this rate-distortion framework to Variational Autoencoders (VAEs), providing a useful theoretical connection. The model is trained to minimize a weighted sum of rate (entropy) and distortion (MSE). The empirical results are impressive, demonstrating significant improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000, not only in terms of PSNR/MS-SSIM but also in subjective visual quality, where the method produces much more natural-looking images at low bitrates.

**Decision: Accept**

My recommendation is to accept this paper based on two key strengths: 1) The strong empirical results, which demonstrate state-of-the-art performance and a substantial improvement in visual quality over standard codecs. 2) The paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly presenting a principled framework that successfully bridges classical rate-distortion theory and modern deep generative models.

While the paper is strong, its novelty is somewhat limited as the core rate-distortion optimization approach was introduced in prior work by the authors. The primary contributions here are the extension to deeper models and the inclusion of adaptive entropy coding. A significant concern is the decision to optimize for MSE, which seems like a conceptual step backward from the authors' previous work on perceptual metrics, especially given the qualitative results are the most compelling aspect of the paper. Furthermore, there appears to be a potential error in the formulation connecting the method to VAEs. The partition function in Equation 16 should depend on the synthesis transform output, which suggests the equivalence to a VAE does not hold for the general case of non-Euclidean distortion metrics, contrary to the paper's claims.

To improve the paper, I would strongly suggest addressing the following points. These are intended as constructive feedback and are not central to my decision. An ablation study is critically needed to disentangle the performance gains from the deeper network architecture versus the adaptive entropy coding. Without it, the contribution of the adaptive coder is unclear. Additionally, providing more detail on the implementation and impact of the adaptive entropy coder would significantly strengthen the paper and could merit a higher score.

Finally, I would like the authors to answer the following questions to clarify my understanding:
1.  Could you clarify the derivation relating your framework to VAEs for non-Euclidean metrics? Specifically, how do you ensure the normalization constant Z(λ) in Equation 16 does not depend on the network parameters, which would invalidate the correspondence?
2.  What was the primary motivation for reverting to MSE optimization instead of a perceptual metric? Why was the quantitative evaluation focused on PSNR when the visual improvements are the most striking result?
3.  Could you provide results for your model with a non-adaptive entropy coder to isolate the gains from the adaptive component?
