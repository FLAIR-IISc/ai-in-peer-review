### Review of "END-TO-END OPTIMIZED IMAGE COMPRESSION"

**1. Summary of Contributions**

This paper introduces a novel end-to-end learning framework for lossy image compression. The proposed model consists of a nonlinear analysis transform, a uniform scalar quantizer, and a corresponding nonlinear synthesis transform. The transforms are implemented as a three-stage convolutional network, which notably employs a Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN) nonlinearity inspired by models of the human visual system.

The core contribution is a method for jointly optimizing the entire compression pipeline for a rate-distortion objective. To overcome the non-differentiability of the quantization step, the authors propose an elegant continuous relaxation, replacing the quantizer with additive uniform noise during training. This allows the use of stochastic gradient descent to optimize all model parameters—including the transforms and a non-parametric density model for entropy estimation—directly from a database of natural images. The authors draw an insightful connection between their relaxed loss function and the objective of a Variational Autoencoder (VAE), while clearly delineating the different goals of compression (operating along a rate-distortion curve) and generative modeling. The paper claims that this approach outperforms standard codecs like JPEG and JPEG 2000, not only in terms of rate-distortion metrics (PSNR and especially MS-SSIM) but also in producing compressed images that are visually far more appealing and free of common artifacts like blocking and ringing.

**2. Recommendation and Key Reasons**

**Recommendation: Accept**

This is an excellent paper that presents a complete and principled solution to a classic problem using modern deep learning techniques. My recommendation for acceptance is based on two primary reasons:

1.  **Novel and Sound Optimization Framework:** The paper's main strength lies in its formulation of an end-to-end optimization scheme for a rate-distortion objective. The continuous relaxation of the quantizer is a clever and effective method for enabling gradient-based learning, which is rigorously justified and empirically validated.
2.  **Compelling and Thoroughly Analyzed Results:** The experimental results are convincing. The proposed method consistently outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 in terms of visual quality across all bit rates, a claim strongly supported by both qualitative examples and the MS-SSIM perceptual metric. The implementation of a full entropy code to report actual bit rates makes the comparisons to established standards fair and scientifically rigorous.

**3. Supporting Arguments**

The problem of image compression is well-established, but this paper provides a fresh and powerful perspective. By framing it as an end-to-end learning problem, the authors move beyond the traditional approach of optimizing transform, quantization, and entropy coding stages separately. The key technical hurdle—the non-differentiable quantizer—is handled elegantly. Replacing rounding with additive uniform noise is not only practical but also theoretically motivated, as it approximates the quantization error and connects the discrete entropy of the quantized representation to the differential entropy of a continuous variable. The paper thoughtfully validates this approximation in Figure 4, showing it is a reliable proxy for training.

The connection to VAEs is particularly insightful. It grounds the work in a well-understood theoretical framework while simultaneously highlighting the crucial differences required for compression. Specifically, the introduction of the trade-off parameter λ, which allows the model to be optimized for any point on the rate-distortion curve, is a critical distinction from standard generative models that typically aim to maximize data likelihood (equivalent to the low-distortion, high-rate regime).

The empirical evaluation is exemplary. The authors do not rely on mere entropy estimates; they implement a full CABAC-based entropy coder to measure actual compressed file sizes, ensuring a fair comparison with industry standards. The visual results shown in Figures 5 and 6 are striking. Whereas JPEG and JPEG 2000 degrade by introducing visually jarring artifacts, the proposed method learns to simplify images in a much more naturalistic way, preserving smooth contours and sharp edges while gracefully discarding fine textures. This subjective improvement is quantitatively captured by a large gain in MS-SSIM scores, demonstrating that even when optimized for a simple metric like MSE, the learned nonlinear representations capture perceptually relevant image structures.

**4. Additional Feedback and Questions for the Authors**

The following points are intended to help improve the paper and are not critical for my decision to accept.

**Suggestions for Improvement:**
*   **Computational Complexity:** The paper mentions that encoding and decoding are efficient, but it would be beneficial to include concrete numbers on computational cost (e.g., encoding/decoding time for a standard image size on a specific hardware platform) to better assess the practical viability of the method compared to highly optimized codecs like JPEG 2000.
*   **Role of GDN:** The discussion mentions that preliminary results suggest GDN is more parameter-efficient than standard nonlinearities like ReLUs. Including even a small-scale comparative experiment in the appendix would significantly strengthen this point and help isolate the contribution of this specific architectural choice from the overall framework.
*   **Ablation Studies:** The network architecture is described as somewhat "ad-hoc." An ablation study on the number of stages, channels, or filter sizes could provide valuable insight into how architectural choices affect the rate-distortion performance.

**Clarifying Questions:**
*   The current framework requires training and storing a separate model for each desired point on the rate-distortion curve (i.e., for each λ). This could be impractical for deployment. Have you considered methods for training a single model that can operate across a range of bit rates, for example by conditioning the network on the λ parameter?
*   The entropy model used for both the rate loss and the final arithmetic coder assumes the quantized coefficients are marginally independent. While the learned transforms are designed to produce such a representation, some residual dependencies likely remain. Do you believe that incorporating a more sophisticated, context-adaptive entropy model (e.g., one that models spatial dependencies between coefficients) could lead to significant further gains in compression rate?
