- End-to-end trained image compression system that achieves better bit-rate vs. quality trade-offs than established methods (e.g., JPEG-2000).
- Key technical contribution: a differentiable approximation of the "rate" function enabling effective training across different rate–distortion trade-offs.
- The differentiable rate approach is likely to have impact beyond compression for tasks needing differentiable approximations of similar functions.
- Authors provided a thoughtful response to the reviewer’s pre-review question.
- Reviewer notes that, in principle, a sufficiently complex network could learn to produce codes within a fixed range that maximize entropy (meeting the upper bound).
- Reviewer finds the second argument convincing: enforcing a specific form on compressor outputs would force a more complex network or require a separate variable-rate encoder to match the current system’s effective compression.
