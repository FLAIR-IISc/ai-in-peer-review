This paper extends a rate-distortion optimization framework to deep encoders and decoders, and enhances a simple entropy encoding scheme with adaptive entropy coding. The authors also discuss the relationship between their approach and variational autoencoders.

The paper is very well-written, and the empirical results are a key strength. The model demonstrates a visible improvement over JPEG 2000, and to my knowledge, this is the highest level of performance achieved by a learned image compression model to date.

However, the novelty of the submission is somewhat limited, as the core rate-distortion optimization approach has been previously published. In some respects, this work could be seen as a step backward from prior efforts, as it optimizes for Mean Squared Error (MSE) rather than a perceptual metric.

I have a few specific points and questions for the authors:

1.  Equation 10 appears to be incorrect. I believe the partition function should depend on `g_s(y; theta)`. If this is the case, it would mean that the claimed equivalence to a VAE does not hold for non-Euclidean metrics, which would be an important clarification.

2.  What was the rationale for optimizing MSE rather than a perceptual metric, as was done in previous work? This choice is surprising, especially since the evaluation was also performed solely in terms of PSNR.

3.  The paper's improvements stem from both deeper architectures and the introduction of adaptive entropy coding. What is the individual contribution of each component? An ablation study showing the performance with deeper models but without adaptation (as in the previous paper) would be very informative. The paper would be significantly strengthened by providing more detail on the adaptive coder and its specific effects on performance. I would be willing to raise my score if the authors can provide this analysis.
