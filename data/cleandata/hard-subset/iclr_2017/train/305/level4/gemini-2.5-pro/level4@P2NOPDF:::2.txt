This paper presents a method for learned image compression by extending a rate-distortion optimization framework. The primary contributions are the application of this framework to deep encoders and decoders, the introduction of an adaptive entropy coding scheme, and an analysis of the method's connection to variational autoencoders (VAEs).

The quantitative and qualitative results are impressive, demonstrating a clear improvement over established codecs like JPEG 2000. To my knowledge, this work sets a new state-of-the-art for learned image compression systems. The paper is also well-structured and clearly written, making the proposed method easy to follow.

Despite these strengths, there are several areas that require clarification and improvement:

1.  **Novelty:** The novelty of the work is somewhat limited, as the core rate-distortion optimization framework has been published previously. The main contributions appear to be the scaling of the architecture to deeper networks and the addition of adaptive entropy coding. It would be helpful if the authors could further clarify any other subtle but crucial novel contributions that I may have overlooked.

2.  **Choice of Distortion Metric:** The paper optimizes for Mean Squared Error (MSE), a departure from prior work in this area which focused on perceptual metrics. This choice is surprising, and in some respects, represents a regression. Consequently, the evaluation is limited to PSNR, which is known to correlate poorly with human perception of image quality. The authors should provide a strong rationale for reverting to MSE and consider including evaluations with perceptual metrics (e.g., SSIM, MS-SSIM).

3.  **Potential Error in Theoretical Formulation:** There appears to be a potential error in Equation 10. I believe the partition function should depend on `g_s(y; theta)`, not just `y`. If this is correct, the claimed equivalence between the proposed method and a VAE would only hold for specific cases (like the Euclidean metric), and not in the general case. A clarification or correction on this point is necessary.

4.  **Missing Ablation Study and Lack of Detail:** The paper does not adequately disentangle the contributions of the two main extensions: deeper architectures and adaptive entropy coding. An ablation study is crucial to understand the source of the performance gains. Specifically, it would be highly informative to see the performance of the deep architecture *without* adaptive entropy coding, to benchmark it against the previous, non-adaptive version. Following this, the paper provides insufficient detail about the adaptive entropy coder itself. Given its importance as a key contribution, the authors should elaborate on its implementation and analyze its impact on the final compression rate more thoroughly.

In summary, this paper achieves impressive empirical results in learned image compression. However, the work is weakened by concerns about limited novelty, the choice of a non-perceptual distortion metric, a potential error in the theoretical analysis, and a lack of experimental ablation. I would be willing to increase my score if the authors can convincingly address the points above, particularly by providing an ablation study for the adaptive entropy coder and clarifying the theoretical formulation in Equation 10.
