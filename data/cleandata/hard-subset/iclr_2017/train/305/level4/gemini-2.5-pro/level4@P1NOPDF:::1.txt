This is the most convincing paper on image compression with deep neural networks that I have read to date. The paper is exceptionally well-written, and its use of rate-distortion theory in the objective function is an elegant and natural fit for the proposed framework. A key strength is the comparison against a strong baseline (JPEG2000), which is a welcome improvement over previous papers that often considered only the weaker JPEG standard. I expect this paper to have a significant impact on the field.

To further strengthen the manuscript, I highly recommend including results on standard benchmark images such as Lena, Barbara, and Baboon. Additionally, it is crucial to compare the proposed method against state-of-the-art classical compression algorithms, like the one I referenced in my questions. Providing these comparisons is important because the submitted version does not yet make it clear how neural network-based approaches are positioned relative to the best traditional methods. Clarifying the performance gap between these two categories of methods would substantially improve the paper's contribution.
