- Proposes a novel end-to-end deep neural network framework for lossy image compression with strong execution.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, with accessible and rigorous presentation.  
- Elegant, principled integration of rate-distortion theory into the learning objective (rate = latent entropy, distortion = reconstruction error).  
- Experimental comparison includes a strong and relevant baseline (JPEG2000), improving over prior work that used only JPEG.  
- Experimental evaluation needs expansion to more standard benchmark images (e.g., Lena, Barbara, Baboon) for thorough comparison.  
- Lacks comparisons to top-performing classical codecs (e.g., BPG/HEVC-based methods) to assess practical viability.  
- Current results do not clearly position the method relative to the absolute state of the art in image compression.  
- Overall: significant, well-executed contribution with strong promise; recommend acceptance but urge inclusion of the suggested additional comparisons.
