This paper presents an extension of a known rate-distortion optimization framework, applying it to deep encoder-decoder architectures and incorporating an adaptive entropy coding model in place of a simpler one. The authors also explore the connection between their method and variational autoencoders.

Given that the core rate-distortion optimization technique has been previously published, the submission's novelty is somewhat limited unless a key technical innovation was overlooked. In some respects, the work takes a step back from prior art by optimizing for Mean Squared Error (MSE) instead of a perceptual metric. However, the empirical results are impressive, demonstrating a clear visual improvement over JPEG 2000. To my knowledge, this level of performance has not been achieved by other learned encoding methods. The manuscript is very well-written.

I believe there may be an error in Equation 10; the partition function should likely depend on g_s(y; theta). If so, the claimed equivalence to a VAE would not hold for non-Euclidean distortion metrics.

Could the authors elaborate on the rationale for optimizing MSE rather than a perceptual metric, especially since the latter was used in the foundational work? Considering the authors' backgrounds, the exclusive reliance on PSNR for evaluation is also surprising.

To better isolate the contributions, what is the performance gain from the adaptive entropy coding versus that from the deeper network architectures? This is a crucial detail, and an ablation study showing performance without adaptation (as in the previous paper) would be insightful. The paper would be significantly strengthened by providing more detail on the adaptive coder and its impact. I would be willing to increase my score if the authors provide these clarifications.
