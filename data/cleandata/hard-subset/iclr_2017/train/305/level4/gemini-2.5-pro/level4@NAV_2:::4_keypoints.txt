- Introduces an end-to-end optimized lossy image compression framework with nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms and a uniform quantizer jointly optimized for rate–distortion.
- Builds flexible transforms via cascades of convolutions and a biologically-inspired Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN) nonlinearity.
- Addresses non-differentiable quantization by replacing it with additive uniform noise as a continuous, differentiable training proxy.
- Provides a compelling connection between the rate–distortion loss and the variational autoencoder objective.
- Trains on a large image database and evaluates against standard codecs for thorough benchmarking.
- Strong recommendation for acceptance by the reviewer.
- Empirical results are competitive with or superior to JPEG and JPEG 2000 in rate–distortion performance and show dramatic perceptual quality improvements across bit rates.
- Methodologically sound and well-executed; the noise relaxation effectively enables stochastic gradient descent through quantization.
- Implements full entropy coding (CABAC) to report actual compressed bit rates for fair, direct comparisons rather than relying solely on entropy estimates.
- Quantitative gains on MS-SSIM and qualitative improvements: reduced blocking/ringing and preservation of salient image structures.
- Suggestion: discuss sensitivity to architectural choices (number of stages, filters) since design was chosen “somewhat ad-hoc.”
- Suggestion: quantify efficiency trade-offs between GDN and simpler nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU) in terms of parameters or computation for similar PSNR.
- Practicality concern: separate model trained per λ; ask about feasibility of a single rate-adaptive model or conditioning the network on λ.
- Entropy model assumes spatial independence within channels; recommend exploring spatial context models in the entropy coder and estimate potential gains.
