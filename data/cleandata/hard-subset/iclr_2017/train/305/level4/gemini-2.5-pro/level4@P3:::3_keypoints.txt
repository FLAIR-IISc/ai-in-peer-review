- Presents a novel end-to-end deep framework for lossy image compression with analysis transform, quantizer, and synthesis transform.  
- Architecture uses cascaded convolutions and biologically-inspired GDN nonlinearity.  
- Overcomes non-differentiable quantization by replacing it with additive uniform noise during training to obtain a differentiable proxy.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results, outperforming JPEG and JPEG 2000 on PSNR and especially MS-SSIM.  
- Produces qualitatively more natural images that avoid blocking and ringing artifacts.  
- Implements full entropy coding (CABAC) and reports real bit rates, showing practical viability.  
- Connects the method to VAEs while clearly explaining critical differences relevant to compression.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough (Kodak dataset, rate-distortion curves, striking visual examples).  
- Uses a non-parametric model for latent marginals, improving flexibility and reducing model error.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation of architecture, optimization, and prior work.  
- Suggests adding an ablation study comparing GDN to standard activations (e.g., ReLU).  
- Recommends discussing or developing a single model that supports multiple bit rates instead of separate models per λ.  
- Requests quantitative inference-time/complexity measurements versus optimized JPEG/JPEG 2000 implementations.  
- Asks authors to clarify the trade-off of explicitly modeling p(y) (−log p(y)) vs. relying on encoder capacity to produce high-entropy latents and whether modeling p(y) offloads complexity to the density model and entropy coder.  
- Decision: Accept.
