This paper presents a compelling end-to-end trainable system for image compression and decompression, which achieves superior rate-distortion performance compared to established algorithms like JPEG-2000. In addition to demonstrating the efficacy of deep learning for a novel application, a key contribution of the paper is the introduction of a differentiable approximation for the rate function. The authors show that this enables effective end-to-end training for various rate-distortion trade-offs. This contribution is likely to have an impact beyond image compression, potentially benefiting other tasks that require differentiable proxies for similar non-differentiable objectives.

The authors provided a thoughtful response to my pre-review question. I would still argue that to minimize distortion under a fixed range and quantization level, a sufficiently complex network should automatically learn to produce codes that maximize entropy within that range (i.e., meet the upper bound). However, their second argument is convincing: forcing the network to do so imposes a specific structure on how the compressor's output is used. To match the compression efficiency of the current system—which relies on a separate variable-rate encoder to store the quantized representation `q`—the network would need to become significantly more complex to carry out the computations currently handled by that separate encoder.
