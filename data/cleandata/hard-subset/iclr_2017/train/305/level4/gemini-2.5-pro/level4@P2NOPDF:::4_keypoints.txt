- Proposes an end-to-end trainable non-linear autoencoder for lossy image compression that directly optimizes rate–distortion.
- Introduces an elegant relaxation for the non-differentiable quantization step, enabling gradient-based optimization through the whole model.
- Trains the system on a large natural image database to learn an efficient compression scheme.
- Demonstrates strong empirical results, often matching or outperforming JPEG 2000, especially on perceptual metrics.
- Qualitative examples show the learned method avoids common artifacts of traditional codecs.
- Provides thorough analysis of how the network allocates bits and represents image information.
- Paper is very clear, well-organized, and well-motivated.
- Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance due to novelty, results, and potential impact.
