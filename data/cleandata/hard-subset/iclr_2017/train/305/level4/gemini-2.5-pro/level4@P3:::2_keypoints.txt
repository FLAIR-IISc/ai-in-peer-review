- End-to-end DNN framework for lossy image compression using convolutional layers and GDN nonlinearities (encoder → quantizer → decoder) trained on a rate–distortion objective.
- Main technical contribution: a continuous differentiable proxy for quantization (additive uniform noise) enabling gradient-based optimization.
- Thoughtful discussion connecting the rate–distortion objective to variational autoencoders and clarifying similarities and differences.
- Empirical result: consistently superior rate–distortion performance and visual quality versus JPEG and JPEG 2000, measured by MS-SSIM.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: state-of-the-art perceptual performance producing more natural-looking artifacts across bit rates.
- Key reason: rigorous, complete framework including a full CABAC-based entropy coder and reporting of actual bit rates.
- Supporting: MSE optimization still yields high visual quality, avoiding blocking/ringing and preserving contours/edges.
- Supporting: Deeper architecture plus practical entropy coding is a meaningful extension of prior work; proxy approximates discrete behavior well.
- Contribution: Demonstrates scalability to deeper networks and integration into a full coding pipeline that outperforms established standards.
- Suggestion: Discuss potential benefits and challenges of optimizing the deeper model directly for perceptual metrics like MS-SSIM.
