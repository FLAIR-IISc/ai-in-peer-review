- End-to-end optimized image compression framework with nonlinear analysis transform, quantizer, and synthesis transform jointly optimized for rate–distortion.
- Key contribution: make the objective differentiable by replacing quantization with additive noise, enabling SGD training.
- Demonstrates superior performance to JPEG and JPEG 2000 in rate–distortion metrics and perceptual quality across bit rates.
- Strong recommendation to accept; work is exceptionally well-executed and convincing.
- Principled integration of classical rate–distortion theory into a deep learning framework.
- Comprehensive evaluation that establishes a new benchmark over a strong baseline (JPEG 2000).
- Paper is well-written and the technical approach is sound.
- Use of generalized divisive normalization (GDN) is well-motivated by neuroscience and density modeling.
- Clear and insightful discussion of similarities and differences with variational autoencoders.
- Experiments validate the continuous relaxation and report actual bit rates from a practical entropy coder rather than just entropy estimates.
- Visual examples strongly support the measured MS-SSIM improvements.
- Suggestion: include comparisons with modern classical codecs (e.g., BPG/HEVC intra) for fuller context.
- Suggestion: add results on traditional test images (Lena, Barbara, Mandrill) for easier cross-paper comparison.
- Reviewer questions: provide quantitative comparisons to state-of-the-art classical algorithms and results on classic images to better position the approach.
