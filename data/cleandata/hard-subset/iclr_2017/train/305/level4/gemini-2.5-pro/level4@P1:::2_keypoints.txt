- Presents an end-to-end deep nonlinear encoder-decoder framework for image compression, trained via a continuous proxy for the rate-distortion loss.  
- Core contributions: scaling prior rate-distortion optimization to deeper models, integrating an adaptive entropy coder (CABAC), and analyzing connections to VAEs.  
- Empirical results strongly outperform standard codecs (JPEG, JPEG 2000) in PSNR, MS-SSIM and especially visual quality.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: a complete, well-engineered learned compression system that convincingly outperforms JPEG 2000 and reduces blocking/ringing artifacts.  
- Key reason: successful scaling to deeper architectures plus a practical entropy coder yields a state-of-the-art model with thorough evaluation and valuable VAE connections.  
- Strength: impressive empirical gains, particularly at low bit rates, supported by quantitative metrics and qualitative figures.  
- Strength: clear writing and a helpful derivation of the continuous loss and its relation to the VAE framework.  
- Limitation: core optimization (continuous proxy for rate-distortion) is not novel, building on the authors' prior work.  
- Limitation: switching to MSE optimization from prior perceptual metrics may be seen as a step back despite good visual results.  
- Request: provide an ablation study to quantify performance gains from deeper architecture versus CABAC (e.g., new architecture with a non-adaptive coder).  
- Request: clarify motivation and technical reasons for optimizing MSE rather than a perceptual metric for this deeper, color-image model.  
- Request: clarify the formal VAE equivalence conditions, specifically the role of normalization constant Z(λ) and when the decomposition in Equation 16 holds.  
- Request: provide more entropy coder details for reproducibility and quantify how much online probability adaptation (CABAC) reduces bit rate versus a non-adaptive coder using learned marginals.
