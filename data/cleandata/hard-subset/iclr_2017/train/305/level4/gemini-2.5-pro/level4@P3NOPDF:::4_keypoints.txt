- Novel end-to-end learning framework for image compression using a rate–distortion formulation and a continuous relaxation to make quantization differentiable for gradient descent.

- Integrates transformation, quantization, and entropy coding into a single trainable neural network, replacing hand-engineered pipelines.

- Strong empirical results: competitive or superior rate–distortion curves versus JPEG/JPEG 2000, especially at low bitrates.

- Qualitative reconstructions are more natural and exhibit fewer blocking/ringing artifacts than traditional codecs.

- Clear, well-organized presentation with transparent experimental setup and motivation.

- Thorough analysis including quantitative RD curves and insightful qualitative visualizations.

- Minor suggestion: include computational complexity details (encoding/decoding time, model size) relative to traditional codecs.

- Minor suggestion: discuss potential for a single variable-bitrate model instead of training separate models per λ.

- Minor suggestion: comment on generalization to out-of-distribution image types (medical, line art, screen content).

- Strong recommendation for acceptance; paper is significant, well-executed, and likely to inspire follow-up work.
