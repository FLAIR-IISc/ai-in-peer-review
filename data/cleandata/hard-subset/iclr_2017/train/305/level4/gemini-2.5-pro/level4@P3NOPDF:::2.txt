This paper presents a framework for end-to-end learned image compression, extending a previously published rate-distortion optimization approach to deeper encoder/decoder architectures and incorporating an adaptive entropy coding scheme. The authors also draw a connection between their framework and Variational Autoencoders (VAEs).

The primary strength of this work lies in its empirical results. The presented method demonstrates a clear and significant improvement over the JPEG 2000 standard in terms of PSNR, which is a notable achievement for a learned compression algorithm. To my knowledge, this level of performance is state-of-the-art for such methods. The paper is also well-structured and clearly written, making the proposed method easy to follow.

Despite the strong results, I have several concerns and questions for the authors:

**1. Novelty and Contributions:**
While the results are impressive, the conceptual novelty appears somewhat limited, as the core rate-distortion optimization framework builds directly upon previously published work. The main contributions seem to be the application of this framework to deeper architectures and the integration of adaptive entropy coding. Clarification on any other key novel components would be appreciated.

**2. Choice of Distortion Metric (MSE/PSNR):**
A significant concern is the choice to optimize for Mean Squared Error (MSE) and evaluate using PSNR. This feels like a step backward from prior work, which explored perceptual metrics. Given the community's increasing focus on perceptual quality for image compression, and potentially the authors' own backgrounds in this area, the reliance solely on PSNR is surprising. Could the authors comment on the rationale for this choice and whether experiments with perceptual losses were attempted?

**3. VAE Equivalence and Equation 10:**
There appears to be an error in Equation 10. The partition function in the denominator should likely depend on `g_s(y; theta)`, not just `y`. If this correction is accurate, the claimed equivalence between the proposed method and a VAE would only hold for the specific case of a Euclidean metric (where the partition function is constant) and not for the general case. This is a crucial point that could undermine the generality of the connection drawn to VAEs. I would ask the authors to please verify this equation and clarify its implications.

**4. Ablation Study for Adaptive Entropy Coding:**
The paper does not clearly disentangle the contributions of the two main architectural improvements: the use of deeper encoders/decoders and the introduction of adaptive entropy coding. An ablation study is necessary to understand the impact of each component. Specifically, it would be highly informative to see the performance of the deep architecture *without* the adaptive context model, similar to the setup in the previous paper. This would quantify the gains from the adaptive coder alone.

Furthermore, the paper would be strengthened by providing more details on the implementation of the adaptive coder and a more thorough analysis of its effects. Addressing these points, particularly by providing an ablation study for the adaptive coding, would significantly improve the paper, and I would be inclined to raise my score accordingly.
