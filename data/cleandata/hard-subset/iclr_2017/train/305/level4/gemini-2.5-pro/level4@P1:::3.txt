### **Summary of the Paper**

This paper presents a novel, end-to-end trained image compression and decompression system that achieves better rate-distortion trade-offs than established algorithms like JPEG and JPEG-2000. The architecture consists of a nonlinear analysis transform, a quantizer, and a nonlinear synthesis transform, which are jointly optimized to minimize a weighted sum of rate and distortion. A key contribution is the introduction of a continuous and differentiable proxy for the rate-distortion loss function, which cleverly circumvents the problem of non-differentiability introduced by the quantization step. This allows the entire system to be trained effectively with stochastic gradient descent for different points along the rate-distortion curve. The authors demonstrate that their method not only outperforms standard codecs on objective metrics like PSNR but also yields a dramatic improvement in perceptual quality, an observation supported by MS-SSIM scores. The paper also provides a thoughtful connection to variational autoencoders, clarifying the conceptual differences between generative modeling and rate-distortion optimization.

### **Decision and Key Reasons**

**Decision:** Accept

This paper is a strong contribution to both the image compression and machine learning communities. My decision is based on two key reasons:

1.  **State-of-the-Art Performance:** The paper presents a complete, end-to-end optimized system that demonstrates compelling performance. It surpasses widely-used standards like JPEG-2000, especially in terms of visual quality at low bit rates. The reduction of common artifacts (blocking, ringing) is a significant practical achievement.
2.  **Novel Methodological Contribution:** The introduction of a differentiable relaxation of the rate objective is an elegant solution to a difficult optimization problem. This technique for handling discrete variables in a deep learning framework is highly significant and likely to have an impact on other applications beyond image compression.

### **Supporting Arguments**

The claims made in the paper are thoroughly supported by empirical results. The authors provide extensive comparisons against JPEG and JPEG-2000 on the standard Kodak dataset. The rate-distortion curves clearly show that their method is highly competitive on PSNR and consistently superior when measured by MS-SSIM, which aligns better with human perception. The visual examples provided are particularly convincing; they showcase a more natural degradation of image quality at low bit rates, where contours are preserved and textures are simplified rather than being replaced by the visually jarring artifacts characteristic of linear transform codes.

The core technical innovation—the continuous proxy for the rate-distortion objective—is well-motivated and validated. By replacing the hard quantization function with additive uniform noise during training, the authors enable gradient-based optimization while maintaining a close approximation of the true rate and distortion in the discrete domain. The discussion in Section 3.1, which frames this approach in the context of variational autoencoders, is particularly insightful. It clarifies the fundamental goal of a compression system—to operate at a specific trade-off point `λ`—in contrast to a generative model, which implicitly seeks to maximize likelihood. This situates the work well within the broader literature and highlights its unique contributions.

### **Additional Feedback for Improvement**

*The following points are intended as constructive suggestions to further strengthen the paper and are not critical to my decision to accept.*

I had previously engaged with the authors regarding the choice to optimize an entropy model for the quantized codes `q` rather than forcing the codes themselves to have a simple, high-entropy distribution (e.g., uniform). While one could argue that a sufficiently complex network might learn to produce uniformly distributed codes automatically to minimize distortion, I am now convinced by the authors' reasoning. Forcing a specific distributional form on the latent codes would implicitly require the network to perform the complex task of entropy coding. The current modular approach, which separates the transformation from the subsequent variable-rate encoding, is a pragmatic and effective design choice. A brief mention of this design rationale in the paper could be beneficial for readers.

### **Questions for the Authors**
