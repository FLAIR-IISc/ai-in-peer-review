This is a strong paper that demonstrates an end-to-end trained system for image compression and decompression, achieving superior rate-distortion trade-offs compared to established algorithms like JPEG-2000. A key contribution, beyond demonstrating the efficacy of deep learning in a new application area, is the introduction of a differentiable proxy for the bit-rate. The authors show this enables effective end-to-end training of a single model that can operate at various points along the rate-distortion curve. I expect this novel technique will have an impact beyond image compression, as it could be valuable for other tasks that require differentiable approximations of similar discrete or non-differentiable functions.

The authors provided a thoughtful response to my pre-review question. I would still argue that, to minimize distortion under a fixed output range and quantization scheme, a sufficiently powerful network should learn to automatically produce codes that maximize entropy (i.e., meet the upper bound on rate). However, the authors make a convincing counter-argument: doing so would impose a specific structure on the compressor's output. To match the compression efficiency of the proposed system, the network would then need to become more complex, as it would have to implicitly learn the computations currently performed by the separate, variable-rate entropy coder that is used to store the quantized representation, q.
