- Extends prior rate-distortion optimization to deep encoder–decoder architectures and an adaptive entropy model, with connections drawn to VAEs.  
- Core rate-distortion approach is established in prior work, so novelty is limited.  
- Potential step backward by optimizing MSE (PSNR) instead of a perceptual metric used in prior art.  
- Strong practical results: visibly improves over JPEG 2000 and appears to be the best-performing learned image compression model to date.  
- Paper is well written and easy to follow.  
- Possible error in Equation 10: partition function may need to depend on g_s(y; theta), which would break the claimed VAE equivalence for non-Euclidean distortion metrics.  
- Question: Why optimize MSE/PSNR rather than a perceptual metric given prior work and authors’ background?  
- Question: Need an ablation to quantify the relative contributions of the deeper architecture versus the adaptive entropy coding.  
- Recommendation: Provide more details and analysis on the adaptive coder and its specific impact; reviewer may raise score if added.
