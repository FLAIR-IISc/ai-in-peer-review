This paper presents a highly convincing and well-executed approach to image compression using deep neural networks. It stands out as a significant contribution and is arguably the most compelling work on this topic to date.

The manuscript is exceptionally well-written and clear. A key strength lies in its principled use of rate-distortion theory, which is elegantly integrated into the network's objective function. This provides a solid theoretical foundation that has been missing in some prior work. Furthermore, the empirical evaluation is strengthened by the choice of a competitive baseline, JPEG2000, which offers a more realistic performance benchmark than the JPEG standard used in many previous papers. Given its strong methodology and promising results, this work is poised to have a substantial impact on the field.

To further strengthen the paper and clarify its position within the broader literature, the following additions are recommended. The experimental evaluation would be more comprehensive if it included results on standard benchmark images (e.g., Lena, Barbara, Baboon). More importantly, the paper should include comparisons with state-of-the-art classical compression methods that may outperform JPEG2000. At present, the manuscript does not provide a clear picture of how this neural network-based approach compares to the best-performing traditional codecs. A direct comparison and discussion are essential to accurately contextualize the method's performance and understand the relative standing of learned versus classical compression techniques.
