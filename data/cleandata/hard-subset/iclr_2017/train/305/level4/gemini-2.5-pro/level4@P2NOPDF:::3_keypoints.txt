- Proposes a novel end-to-end trained image compression system that outperforms JPEG 2000 on rate–distortion.
- Main technical contribution is a continuous, differentiable proxy for the rate term in the loss enabling gradient-based optimization.
- The differentiable rate approximation allows effective end-to-end training across different rate–distortion trade-offs.
- This rate proxy is likely broadly useful beyond image compression for tasks involving discrete or entropy-related objectives.
- Reviewer found the authors’ responses to initial questions thoughtful and persuasive.
- Forcing the network to learn an entropy-maximized latent (e.g., uniform) would impose a rigid representation and demand a much more complex model.
- Decoupling representation learning from the final entropy coder is a pragmatic, effective design choice.
