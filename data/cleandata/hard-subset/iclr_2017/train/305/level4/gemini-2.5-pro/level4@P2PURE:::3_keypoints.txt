- Presents an end-to-end trainable image compression/decompression system that outperforms established codecs (e.g., JPEG-2000) on rate-distortion trade-offs.
- Introduces a differentiable proxy for the rate function, enabling effective end-to-end training for different rate-distortion trade-offs.
- This differentiable-rate contribution likely generalizes beyond image compression to tasks needing differentiable approximations of non-differentiable functions.
- Reviewer notes authors provided a thoughtful response to prior questions.
- Reviewer argues a sufficiently complex network with a fixed-range quantizer could implicitly learn to maximize entropy and approach the rate's upper bound.
- Authors’ counterargument is convincing: forcing that behavior would impose a specific output structure and would demand a much more complex network to replicate the separate variable-rate entropy coder’s computations.
