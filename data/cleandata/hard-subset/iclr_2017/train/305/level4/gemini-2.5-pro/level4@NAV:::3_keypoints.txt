- Introduces a well-executed, end-to-end trainable image compression/decompression system.  
- Method outperforms established codecs (including JPEG-2000) on rate-distortion trade-offs.  
- Central contribution is a differentiable proxy for the "rate" function.  
- The differentiable rate proxy is critical for effectively training across a spectrum of rate-distortion objectives.  
- Anticipated broader impact: useful beyond compression for tasks needing differentiable approximations of similar functions.  
- Authors provided an insightful response to the reviewer's preliminary query.  
- Reviewer’s contention: a sufficiently large network with fixed-range quantization could implicitly learn to maximize entropy.  
- Authors’ rebuttal (persuasive): forcing that structure would rigidly constrain outputs and would require a much more complex network to match the efficiency achieved by using a separate variable-rate encoder to store quantized codes (q).
