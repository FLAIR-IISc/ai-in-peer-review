This paper presents a novel, end-to-end trained system for image compression and decompression that achieves superior rate-distortion trade-offs compared to established codecs like JPEG-2000. Beyond demonstrating the successful application of deep learning to a new domain, a key contribution is the introduction of a continuous and differentiable proxy for the rate function. This innovation is critical for enabling effective end-to-end optimization and allows for direct control over the rate-distortion balance. I anticipate this technique will have a considerable impact beyond image compression, benefiting other tasks that require differentiable approximations of similar discrete or information-theoretic functions.

The authors provided a thoughtful and clarifying response to my pre-review questions. My main point of discussion centered on the necessity of the explicit rate term in the loss function. I had posited that a sufficiently powerful network, when trained to minimize distortion under a fixed-range quantization scheme, might implicitly learn to produce codes with the highest possible entropy to make optimal use of the representational capacity.

However, the authors' counter-argument is convincing. While a network could theoretically learn to maximize entropy, their proposed approach of directly optimizing an explicit rate term is more pragmatic and modular. It forces a specific structure on the compressor's output, which can then be efficiently handled by a standard variable-rate entropy coder (e.g., an arithmetic coder). To achieve the same level of compression without this explicit rate term, the network itself would need to become significantly more complex to implicitly learn and perform the computations currently handled by this separate, well-established coding step. This design choice is well-justified, leading to a more efficient and effective system.

In summary, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of image compression. The novel differentiable rate approximation is a key technical achievement, and the empirical results are compelling. The work is well-executed and clearly presented, and I recommend its acceptance.
