- Paper extends known rate-distortion optimization to deep encoder-decoder networks and replaces a simple entropy model with an adaptive entropy coder; also discusses links to VAEs.  
- Novelty is limited because the core rate-distortion technique was previously published unless an overlooked technical innovation exists.  
- The method opts to optimize MSE instead of a perceptual metric used in prior work, which may be a step back.  
- Empirical results are strong, showing clear visual improvement over JPEG 2000 and reportedly unmatched performance among learned methods.  
- Manuscript is very well written.  
- Possible error in Equation 10: the partition function likely should depend on g_s(y; θ); if true, claimed VAE equivalence fails for non-Euclidean distortions.  
- Authors should justify optimizing MSE and exclusive use of PSNR for evaluation given prior use of perceptual metrics and the authors’ backgrounds.  
- Need ablation separating gains from the adaptive entropy coder versus deeper network architectures to isolate contributions.  
- Paper requires more detail on the adaptive coder and quantitative impact of adaptation; clarifications could raise the reviewer’s score.
