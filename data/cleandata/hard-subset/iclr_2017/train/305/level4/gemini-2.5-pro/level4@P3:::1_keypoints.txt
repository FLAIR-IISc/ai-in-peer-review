- Novel end-to-end trainable framework for lossy image compression using convolutional analysis/synthesis transforms and GDN nonlinearities.  
- Core technical contribution: continuous relaxation of quantization by replacing it with additive uniform noise to enable SGD optimization of a rate–distortion (R + λD) objective.  
- Frames the approach in information-theoretic rate-distortion terms and relates (but distinguishes) it from VAEs.  
- Architecture leverages GDN nonlinearities, motivated by neuroscience and density modeling, improving efficiency and visual quality.  
- Implements a full entropy coder and evaluates actual bit rates rather than relying on entropy estimates.  
- Empirical results show clear and substantial improvement over JPEG 2000, especially on perceptual MS-SSIM.  
- Qualitative results demonstrate much more natural compressed images that avoid blocky and ringing artifacts common in traditional codecs.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and validates the noise-quantization approximation empirically.  
- Suggestion: compare against stronger classical codecs (e.g., BPG/HEVC-based) to position against state-of-the-art non-learning methods.  
- Suggestion: report results on standard benchmark images (e.g., Lena, Barbara, Baboon) for easier comparison by others.  
- Suggestion: provide quantitative complexity analysis (operations per pixel or encode/decode times on hardware) compared to JPEG 2000.  
- Question: quantify how much deeper/wider a ReLU-based network would need to match GDN-based model performance (GDN parameter-efficiency).  
- Question: consider training a single rate-adaptive model conditioned on λ to avoid training separate models per rate (practical deployment).
