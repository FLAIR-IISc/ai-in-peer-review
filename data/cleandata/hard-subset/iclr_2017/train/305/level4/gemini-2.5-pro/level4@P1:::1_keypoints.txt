- Presents an end-to-end learned image compression framework with nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms and a uniform scalar quantizer.  
- Introduces a novel training methodology using a continuous differentiable proxy (additive uniform noise) to directly optimize rate–distortion via SGD.  
- Network architecture uses cascaded convolutions and GDN nonlinearities inspired by human vision.  
- Demonstrates superior rate–distortion (PSNR) and substantially better perceptual quality (MS-SSIM and visual inspection) versus JPEG and JPEG 2000.  
- Connects the approach to variational autoencoders, explaining similarities and key differences.  
- Implements a full entropy coder to report actual compressed bitrates rather than relying on entropy estimates.  
- Qualitative results show graceful degradation at low bitrates, avoiding blocking and ringing artifacts of linear transform codes.  
- Decision: Accept — paper is principled, well-executed, and likely to have significant impact.  
- Suggests comparing against more modern classical codecs (e.g., BPG/HEVC-based) to position the method relative to current state-of-the-art.  
- Recommends including classic benchmark images (Lena, Barbara, Mandrill) in evaluations for comparability with prior literature.  
- Requests clarification on comparisons to recent non-learning-based codecs beyond JPEG 2000.  
- Asks for quantitative details on GDN vs. ReLU: how much larger a ReLU-based model must be for comparable rate–distortion and whether artifacts differ qualitatively.
