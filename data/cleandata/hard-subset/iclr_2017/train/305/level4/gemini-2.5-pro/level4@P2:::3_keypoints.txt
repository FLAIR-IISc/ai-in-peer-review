- Presents an end-to-end optimized lossy image compression framework using three-stage convolutional analysis/synthesis transforms with GDN nonlinearities.  
- Key technical contribution: replace hard quantization with additive uniform noise to create a continuous, differentiable proxy for the rate–distortion loss, enabling SGD training.  
- Relates formulation to VAEs while distinguishing compression goals (optimize rate–distortion curve via λ) from generative modeling (likelihood maximization).  
- Empirical results: outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 on PSNR and notably on perceptual quality (MS-SSIM).  
- Visual reconstructions avoid blocking/ringing artifacts and preserve smooth contours and sharp edges at low bitrates.  
- The additive-noise relaxation makes distortion differentiable and approximates discrete entropy by the differential entropy of the noisy representation, enabling joint optimization of encoder, decoder, and entropy model.  
- Demonstrates a complete codec by implementing CABAC and reporting actual bitrates rather than only entropy estimates.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Suggestion: compare current non-parametric marginal modeling of quantized coefficients to training the encoder to match a fixed prior (ablation or discussion).  
- Suggestion: provide quantitative comparisons to ReLU-based networks to substantiate claims that GDN is more parameter-efficient.  
- Positive: appendix gives detailed architecture and optimization information, aiding reproducibility.  
- Reviewer questions: please elaborate why non-parametric marginals were chosen over a fixed high-entropy prior, and whether a simpler scheme (maximize entropy within a fixed range + fixed coder) was tested.
