To date, this represents the most compelling work I have encountered on image compression using deep neural networks. The manuscript is exceptionally well-written, and the application of rate-distortion theory to the objective function is elegant and integrates seamlessly into the proposed framework. Furthermore, the evaluation is strengthened by the choice of a suitable baseline (JPEG2000), a notable improvement over previous papers that often limit their comparisons to JPEG. I anticipate this paper will have a significant impact on the field.

To further strengthen the evaluation, I recommend including results on standard benchmark images (e.g., Lena, Barbara, Baboon) and incorporating references to state-of-the-art classical methods, such as those mentioned in my detailed questions. It is important to explicitly articulate how neural network-based techniques compare to the leading traditional algorithms. The submitted version does not yet offer a clear understanding of the relative positioning of these two categories of methods.
