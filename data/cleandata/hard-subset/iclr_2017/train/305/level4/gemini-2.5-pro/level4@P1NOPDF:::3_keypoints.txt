- Strong paper showing end-to-end trained image compression/decompression that yields superior rate–distortion performance compared to established methods like JPEG-2000.
- Key technical contribution: a differentiable proxy for bit-rate that enables effective end-to-end training and lets a single model operate at multiple points on the rate–distortion curve.
- The differentiable bit-rate technique is likely to have impact beyond image compression for tasks needing differentiable approximations of discrete/non-differentiable functions.
- Reviewer raised (and authors responded to) a theoretical point: while a sufficiently powerful network could try to produce maximally entropic codes under fixed quantization, the authors argue this would impose a specific output structure and require extra complexity to replicate the separate entropy coder’s computations.
