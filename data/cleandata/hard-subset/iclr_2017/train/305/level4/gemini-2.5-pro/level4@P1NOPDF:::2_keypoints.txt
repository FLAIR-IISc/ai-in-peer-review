- Paper extends a rate-distortion optimization framework to deep autoencoders and adds an adaptive entropy coding scheme, and examines links to VAEs.  
- Core rate-distortion framework was introduced previously, so novelty is limited.  
- Compared to prior work, this paper optimizes for MSE instead of a perceptual metric, which may be a step backward.  
- Results show significant, visible improvement over JPEG 2000; possibly the first learning-based method to reach this performance.  
- Paper is very well written.  
- Potential error in Equation 10: the partition function likely depends on g_s(y; θ); needs correction/clarification.  
- If Equation 10 is incorrect, the claimed equivalence to VAEs may break for non-Euclidean distortion metrics—this distinction must be clarified.  
- Authors should justify the choice to optimize for MSE rather than a perceptual metric and expand evaluation beyond PSNR.  
- Need an ablation study to separate contributions of deeper network architectures versus the adaptive entropy coder.  
- Provide detailed description and clear analysis of the adaptive coder’s impact; show model performance without the adaptive coder for direct comparison.  
- Reviewer willing to raise score if the above concerns—especially the ablation study—are satisfactorily addressed.
