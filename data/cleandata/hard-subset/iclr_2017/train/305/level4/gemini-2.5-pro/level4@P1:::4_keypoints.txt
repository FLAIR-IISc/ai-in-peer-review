- Proposes a novel end-to-end image compression framework with joint optimization of analysis transform, quantizer, and synthesis transform to minimize a rate-distortion objective.
- Replaces non-differentiable quantization with additive uniform noise during training, creating a differentiable proxy for the true loss.
- Enables gradient-based optimization over large image databases, with empirical verification that the noise-based proxy matches true rate/distortion (Fig. 4).
- Connects the approach to VAEs conceptually but emphasizes the different objective (rate-distortion vs. likelihood).
- Implements a complete entropy coder and reports actual bit rates for fair comparisons with standards.
- Achieves state-of-the-art quantitative and qualitative results, outperforming JPEG and JPEG 2000 on RD curves and perceptual metrics (e.g., MS-SSIM).
- Visual reconstructions exhibit more natural-looking artifacts, preserving contours and edges while gracefully simplifying textures at low bit rates.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing novel methodology, strong motivation, and compelling results.
- Suggests more systematic architectural exploration and ablation studies (depth, channels, filter sizes) to justify ad-hoc choices.
- Requests quantification of the benefit of GDN versus simpler nonlinearities (e.g., how much larger a ReLU model would need to be).
- Notes the computational burden of training separate models for each λ and suggests exploring a single model that can operate across the rate-distortion curve.
- Asks authors to comment on training computational cost per RD point and whether more sophisticated context models for entropy coding were tried and their impact.
