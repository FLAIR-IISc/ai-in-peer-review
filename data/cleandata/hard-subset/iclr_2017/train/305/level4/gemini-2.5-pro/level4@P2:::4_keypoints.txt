- Proposes an end-to-end learned nonlinear-transform compression framework (analysis transform → quantizer → synthesis transform) optimized for rate–distortion.
- Introduces a continuous relaxation of quantization by adding uniform noise to enable gradient-based joint training and to proxy rate/distortion.
- Provides a clear discussion linking the approach to VAEs while emphasizing the differences that make it suitable for compression.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art empirical performance, outperforming JPEG and JPEG 2000 on PSNR and especially MS-SSIM, with much better perceptual quality.
- Novel, principled optimization framework that allows joint training of encoder, decoder, and entropy model parameters via SGD.
- Elegant justification that additive uniform noise models quantization error and differential entropy gives a tight upper bound on discrete entropy.
- Thorough empirical validation on the Kodak dataset with superior RD curves, qualitative examples, and preserved contours/edges at low bitrates.
- Includes a functional CABAC-based entropy coder so reported bitrates are actual achievable file sizes, not just estimates.
- Suggests formalizing comparisons between GDN/IGDN and simpler nonlinearities to isolate architectural benefits.
- Requests a brief computational-complexity/runtime comparison (e.g., MACs or CPU/GPU time) versus JPEG 2000 to support practical relevance.
- Notes the need to train a separate model per rate-point and asks whether a single bitrate-conditioned model was considered.
- Asks for intuition why the relaxed differential entropy is positively biased relative to discrete entropy in coarse quantization and whether symmetric encoder/decoder design was a choice or experimentally justified.
