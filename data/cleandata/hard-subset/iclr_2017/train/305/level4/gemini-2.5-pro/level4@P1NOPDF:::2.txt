This paper extends a rate-distortion optimization framework to deep autoencoders and incorporates an adaptive entropy coding scheme. Furthermore, the paper explores the relationship between this framework and variational autoencoders (VAEs).

Since the core rate-distortion optimization framework was introduced in a previous publication, the novelty of this submission is somewhat limited. In one respect, this work could be seen as a step backward from the prior art, which optimized for a perceptual metric, whereas this paper optimizes for Mean Squared Error (MSE). However, the results demonstrate a significant and visible improvement over JPEG 2000. To my knowledge, this is the first learning-based compression method to achieve this level of performance. The paper is also very well written.

I believe there may be an error in Equation 10. Specifically, the partition function should likely depend on g_s(y; θ). If this is correct, it would imply that the proposed framework is not equivalent to a VAE when using non-Euclidean distortion metrics, a distinction that would be important to clarify.

The submission would be strengthened if the authors could address the following points:

1.  Could the authors elaborate on the decision to optimize for MSE rather than a perceptual metric, as was done in previous work? The evaluation is also limited to PSNR, which is surprising given that perceptual quality is a primary motivation for learned image compression.

2.  The paper introduces two main changes over prior work: deeper network architectures and an adaptive entropy coder. An ablation study is needed to disentangle their respective contributions. It would be highly informative to see the model's performance without the adaptive coder, which would enable a more direct comparison to the previous system.

The paper would be significantly strengthened by providing more detail on the adaptive coder and a clear analysis of its impact. I would be willing to raise my score if the authors can satisfactorily address these points, particularly the request for an ablation study.
