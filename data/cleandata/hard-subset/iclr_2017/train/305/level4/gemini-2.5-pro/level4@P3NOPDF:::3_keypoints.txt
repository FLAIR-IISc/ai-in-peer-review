- Presents a novel end-to-end trained image compression/decompression system that outperforms established codecs (e.g., JPEG-2000) on rate-distortion trade-offs.
- Introduces a continuous, differentiable proxy for the rate function, enabling effective end-to-end optimization and direct control of rate-distortion balance.
- Predicts broad impact of the differentiable rate approximation beyond image compression for tasks needing differentiable approximations of discrete/information-theoretic functions.
- Authors gave a thoughtful and clarifying response to pre-review questions.
- Main discussion questioned whether an explicit rate term is necessary, suggesting a powerful network might implicitly maximize entropy under fixed quantization.
- Authors convincingly argue that an explicit rate term is more pragmatic and modular, enforcing structure that standard entropy coders can exploit.
- Without the explicit rate term, the network would need much greater complexity to replicate standard coding operations, making the proposed design choice well-justified.
- Empirical results are compelling, the work is well-executed and clearly presented.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.
