- Novel end-to-end lossy image compression framework with encoder, uniform quantizer, and decoder built using cascaded convolutions and GDN nonlinearity.  
- Training handles non-differentiable quantization by replacing it with additive uniform noise, forming a continuous relaxation.  
- Relaxed objective is connected to the variational autoencoder (VAE) framework.  
- Model trained for MSE outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 in rate-distortion and perceptual quality (MS-SSIM) on benchmarks.  
- Visual results show fewer blocking/ringing artifacts, better contour preservation, and smoother gradients than traditional codecs.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Novel and effective optimization enabling joint optimization of transform and entropy model, grounded by VAE connection.  
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical results and qualitative improvements, with real bit rates reported using a CABAC-like entropy coder.  
- Paper well-motivated vs. traditional separate-design transform coding; noise relaxation closely approximates discrete rate/distortion (validated in Figure 4).  
- The learned degradation resembles semantic simplification rather than basis-driven artifacts, a desirable property for lossy codecs.  
- Suggestion: Add a formal ablation study comparing GDN to common nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU) with matched parameter counts.  
- Suggestion: Discuss or explore a rate-adaptive model to avoid training one model per λ (practical flexibility concern).  
- Suggestion: Report concrete computational complexity and runtime (e.g., MACs/pixel) versus a JPEG 2000 software implementation.  
- Question 1: Provide training time and hardware used for a single model at a given λ.  
- Question 2: Would incorporating more spatial context in the entropy model improve reported bit rates beyond the current raster-scan context?  
- Question 3: Have you tried a fully stochastic encoder instead of deterministic encoder + uniform noise, and how does that affect compression performance?
