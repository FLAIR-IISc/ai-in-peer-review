- End-to-end learned image compression jointly optimizing nonlinear analysis transform, quantizer, and nonlinear synthesis transform.  
- Transforms implemented as deep convolutional networks using biologically-inspired GDN nonlinearity.  
- Quantization non-differentiability handled via continuous differentiable proxy (additive uniform noise) enabling SGD training.  
- Paper connects the optimization to Variational Autoencoders and discusses theoretical relationships.  
- Empirical results achieve state-of-the-art, outperforming JPEG and JPEG 2000 in PSNR and notably in visual quality (MS-SSIM).  
- Recommendation: Accept — decision driven by compelling empirical advances.  
- Key strength: substantial perceptual improvements (reduced blocking/ringing, natural simplifications) validated quantitatively and qualitatively.  
- Inclusion of a practical entropy coder (CABAC) and reporting of true bit rates makes comparisons rigorous.  
- Novelty is limited: core continuous relaxation idea builds on prior work; this paper mainly scales and integrates components.  
- Choice to optimize MSE (vs. perceptual losses) is conservative for comparability but may be a missed chance to push perceptual gains further.  
- Suggestion: disentangle contributions of deeper architecture vs. context-adaptive entropy coding via ablation studies.  
- Suggestion: clarify conditions under which the VAE-equivalence holds, specifically independence of partition function Z from synthesis output.  
- Suggestion: discuss why MSE optimization yields strong perceptual results—possible role of GDN/IGDN nonlinearities.  
- Questions for authors: explain Z(λ) dependence on gs(ỹ;θ) in the VAE formulation, quantify relative gains from architecture vs. entropy coder, and provide intuition on GDN’s role in perceptual performance.
