- End-to-end trainable image compression/decompression system that outperforms JPEG-2000 on rate–distortion.  
- Key contribution: a differentiable approximation of the rate function enabling end-to-end training for various rate–distortion trade-offs.  
- The differentiable-rate technique could benefit other tasks that need differentiable proxies for non-differentiable objectives.  
- Authors gave a thoughtful response to the pre-review question.  
- Reviewer argues a sufficiently complex network could learn codes that maximize entropy within a fixed range/quantization to minimize distortion.  
- Reviewer agrees more with the second argument: forcing entropy-maximization imposes a specific output structure, and matching the current system’s compression efficiency (which uses a separate variable-rate encoder for q) would require a much more complex network to replicate that encoder’s computations.
