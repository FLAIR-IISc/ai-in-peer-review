- Proposes a novel end-to-end image compression framework using a non-linear transform autoencoder optimized for rate–distortion.  
- Designs a complete system (analysis transform, uniform quantizer, synthesis transform) that outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000.  
- Introduces a differentiable training trick: replace quantization with additive uniform noise to create a continuous proxy for the rate–distortion loss.  
- Achieves higher PSNR at lower bitrates and substantially improved perceptual quality, supported by subjective examples and MS-SSIM gains.  
- Recommends acceptance because the method delivers state-of-the-art empirical results and a broadly useful differentiable approximation for discrete/quantized latent variables.  
- Experimental validation is rigorous: evaluations on the Kodak dataset show clear rate–distortion advantages, especially at low bitrates.  
- Qualitative results demonstrate avoidance of blocking and ringing artifacts typical of linear transform codes, yielding more pleasant reconstructions.  
- The continuous relaxation is shown to be a sound, low-variance proxy for true discrete rate and distortion.  
- Paper carefully relates the approach to variational autoencoders, clarifying similarities and key objective differences.  
- Suggests explicitly framing the design choice of separating transform and entropy coder and discussing the increased network complexity required to match a dedicated coder like CABAC.  
- Recommends expanding quantitative comparisons of GDN/IGDN nonlinearities versus standard activations (e.g., ReLU) in terms of performance, model size, and computational cost.  
- Requests clarification on trade-offs and challenges if the network were forced to produce directly compressible, high-entropy-bounded codes to obviate an external coder.
