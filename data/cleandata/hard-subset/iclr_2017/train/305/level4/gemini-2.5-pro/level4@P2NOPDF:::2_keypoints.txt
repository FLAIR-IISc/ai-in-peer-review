1. Paper extends rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders/decoders, introduces adaptive entropy coding, and links the method to VAEs.  
2. Quantitative and qualitative results are strong, outperforming codecs like JPEG 2000 and likely setting a new state-of-the-art.  
3. Paper is well-structured and clearly written, making the method easy to follow.  
4. Novelty is limited since the core rate-distortion framework is prior work; main gains appear from scaling depth and adding adaptive entropy coding.  
5. Choice of MSE as distortion metric is surprising and regressive versus perceptual metrics; evaluation limited to PSNR and should include perceptual measures (SSIM, MS-SSIM) with justification.  
6. Potential error in Equation 10: partition function may depend on g_s(y; theta), which could invalidate the claimed general VAE equivalence except in special cases—needs clarification/correction.  
7. Missing ablation study disentangling deeper architecture vs. adaptive entropy coding; need performance of deep model without adaptive coding.  
8. Insufficient detail on the adaptive entropy coder’s implementation and its quantitative impact on compression rate; authors should elaborate and analyze this component.  
9. Reviewer would raise the score if theoretical clarification and ablation (especially for adaptive entropy coder) are convincingly provided.
