This is the most convincing paper on image compression using deep neural networks that I have encountered. The paper is exceptionally well-written, and its use of rate-distortion theory within the objective function is elegantly integrated into the proposed framework. The comparison against a strong baseline like JPEG2000—a notable improvement over prior work that often only considers JPEG—strengthens the empirical evaluation. I expect this paper will have a significant impact on the field.

To further improve the paper, I strongly recommend expanding the experimental evaluation. The authors should include results on standard benchmark images (e.g., Lena, Barbara, Baboon) and provide comparisons against state-of-the-art classical compression methods. As it stands, the submission does not clearly establish how this neural network-based approach performs relative to the best traditional methods. Including these comparisons is crucial for readers to understand the positioning of these two categories of methods and to fully appreciate the contributions of this work.
