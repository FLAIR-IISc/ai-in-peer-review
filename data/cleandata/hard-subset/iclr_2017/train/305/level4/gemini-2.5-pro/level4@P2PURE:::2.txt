This paper extends a previously published rate-distortion optimization framework by applying it to deep encoder-decoder architectures and upgrading the simple entropy model to an adaptive one. The authors also draw connections between their framework and variational autoencoders.

The primary contribution lies in these extensions, as the core rate-distortion optimization approach has been established in prior work. Consequently, the novelty is somewhat limited, though I may have overlooked a more subtle contribution. In one aspect, the work could be seen as a step backward from prior art, as it optimizes for Mean Squared Error (MSE) rather than a more advanced perceptual metric. Despite these concerns, the practical results are a significant strength. The method achieves a visible improvement over JPEG 2000, and to my knowledge, represents the best-performing learned image compression model to date. The paper is also very well written and easy to follow.

I believe there may be an error in Equation 10. The partition function should likely depend on g_s(y; theta). If this is correct, the claimed equivalence between this approach and a Variational Autoencoder (VAE) would not hold for non-Euclidean distortion metrics.

I have a few questions for the authors:
1.  What was the motivation for optimizing MSE rather than a perceptual metric, as was done in previous work? Given the authors' background, the exclusive reliance on PSNR for evaluation is surprising.
2.  What are the relative contributions of the deeper architecture versus the adaptive entropy coding? An ablation study showing the performance without adaptation, similar to the setup in the previous paper, would be an important addition to disentangle these effects.

The paper would be significantly strengthened by providing more detail on the adaptive coder and its specific impact on performance. I would be willing to increase my score if the authors can provide this analysis.
