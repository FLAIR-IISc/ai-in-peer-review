- Presents an end-to-end trained image compression system that outperforms JPEG and JPEG-2000 on rate–distortion trade-offs.  
- Uses a jointly optimized nonlinear analysis transform, quantizer, and nonlinear synthesis transform.  
- Introduces a continuous, differentiable proxy for the rate–distortion loss to handle quantization non-differentiability.  
- Replaces hard quantization with additive uniform noise during training to enable gradient-based optimization across the RD curve.  
- Demonstrates superior objective metrics (PSNR, especially MS-SSIM) and markedly improved perceptual quality with fewer blocking/ringing artifacts.  
- Provides extensive empirical validation on the Kodak dataset, with clear rate–distortion curves and convincing visual examples.  
- Frames the approach relative to variational autoencoders, clarifying differences between compression (λ-driven RD optimization) and generative modeling (likelihood).  
- Decision: Accept — justified by state-of-the-art performance and a novel, broadly applicable methodological contribution.  
- Suggests briefly explaining the design choice to model entropy of quantized codes rather than forcing a simple latent distribution.  
- Asks authors to clarify the trade-offs of using a separate entropy coder (CABAC) versus training the network to output maximally entropic bitstreams or more integrated coding approaches.
