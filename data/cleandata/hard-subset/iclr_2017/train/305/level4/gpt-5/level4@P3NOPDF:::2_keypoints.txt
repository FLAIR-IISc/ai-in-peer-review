- Paper extends prior R–D optimization by using deeper analysis/synthesis transforms and adaptive entropy coding, and links the method to VAEs.
- Empirical results show clear, consistent improvements over JPEG 2000 and state-of-the-art learned compressors at the time.
- Paper is clearly written and easy to follow.
- Practical contribution: integrating adaptive entropy coding with learned transforms is valuable for real-world compression.
- Novelty concern: core R–D approach is prior work; deeper transforms and adaptation appear incremental unless a new technical contribution is highlighted.
- Surprising choice to optimize MSE and evaluate with PSNR instead of perceptual metrics given community priorities and authors’ backgrounds.
- Request to clarify rationale for MSE over perceptual metrics and to report MS-SSIM/other perceptual metrics and visual comparisons or a user study.
- Hard to attribute gains: need ablations isolating the effect of adaptive entropy coding versus increased transform depth.
- Request ablations that toggle adaptation with fixed networks, vary transform depth with fixed entropy model, and report BD-rate deltas per component.
- Equation (10) appears incorrect: the partition function likely depends on g_s(y; θ), invalidating the VAE equivalence under non-Euclidean distortions.
- Request to verify derivation, state assumptions for exact VAE connection (e.g., Euclidean distortion, noise model), and discuss implications for perceptual metrics.
- Adaptive entropy model needs more detail: form of conditional priors, context type (causal/spatial/hierarchical), parameter prediction and transmission.
- Quantify side-information rate for adaptation and its impact on net bpp, and provide computational complexity and decoding speed measurements.
- Discuss training stability for the adaptive model and any regularization or temperature/scale handling.
- Training details missing: whether additive uniform noise approximates quantization, and full hyperparameters/architectures for reproducibility (depths, receptive fields, activations, entropy parameterization).
- Evaluation requests: provide R–D curves and BD-rate numbers vs baselines on standard datasets, and compare against prior non-adaptive method under identical protocols.
- Suggest including perceptual metrics (MS-SSIM), qualitative examples at matched bitrates, and discussion of extending to non-Euclidean/perceptual distortions.
- Overall: well-written with strong empirical performance but moderate novelty; reviewer is borderline and would raise score if derivation is fixed, ablations added, and evaluation expanded.
