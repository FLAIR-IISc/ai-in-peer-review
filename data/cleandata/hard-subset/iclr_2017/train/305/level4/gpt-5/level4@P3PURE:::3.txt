This is a strong paper that presents an end-to-end trained image compression–decompression system achieving better bit-rate–quality trade-offs than established image compression algorithms (e.g., JPEG 2000). Beyond demonstrating the efficacy of deep learning for compression, a key contribution is the introduction of a differentiable “rate” function, which enables effective training across different rate–distortion trade-offs. I expect this idea to have impact beyond compression for tasks that can benefit from differentiable approximations to similar objectives.

The authors provided a thoughtful response to my pre-review question. I still contend that, to minimize distortion under a fixed range and quantization, a sufficiently expressive network would learn to automatically produce codes confined to the fixed range with maximal entropy (i.e., meeting the upper bound). However, their second argument is convincing: enforcing this constraint effectively imposes a particular structure on how the compressor output is used. To match the effective compression of the current system, the network would then need to internalize the computations currently carried out by a separate variable-rate encoder used to store q, which would require a more complex model.
