This is a strong paper that presents an end-to-end trained image compression/decompression system that achieves superior bitrate–quality trade-offs compared to established codecs (e.g., JPEG-2000). Beyond demonstrating the effectiveness of deep learning for this application, a major contribution is the introduction of a differentiable surrogate for the “rate” term, which the authors show enables effective training across different rate–distortion operating points. I expect this idea to influence areas beyond compression—wherever differentiable approximations to similar quantities could be beneficial.

The authors provided a thoughtful response to my pre-review. I still contend that, under a fixed range and quantization, a sufficiently expressive network optimizing for distortion would learn to produce codes within that range with maximal entropy (i.e., attaining the upper bound). However, their second argument is compelling—imposing this constraint forces a particular structure on how the compressor’s output is utilized, and matching the effective compression of the current system would then require a more complex network capable of performing the computations that are currently handled by a separate variable-rate encoder used to store q.
