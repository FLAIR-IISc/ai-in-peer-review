- End-to-end trained image compression/reconstruction system achieving superior rate–distortion trade-offs vs. established codecs (e.g., JPEG 2000).

- Introduces a differentiable surrogate for the rate term, enabling effective training across rate–distortion operating points and likely impactful beyond compression.

- Authors provided a thoughtful and thorough response to the reviewer’s pre-review question.

- Reviewer maintains that with fixed dynamic range and quantization, a sufficiently expressive network could learn codes confined to that range with maximum entropy to minimize distortion (i.e., reach the upper bound).

- Reviewer finds the authors’ counterargument compelling: enforcing such constraints imposes structure on how compressor output is consumed, and matching the effective compression would require a more complex network to subsume the variable-rate encoder that stores q.
