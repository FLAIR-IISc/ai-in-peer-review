This is a strong paper that presents an end-to-end trained image compression and decompression system that achieves better bitrate-quality trade-offs than established codecs (e.g., JPEG2000). Beyond demonstrating the effectiveness of deep learning for this task, a key contribution is the introduction of a differentiable approximation to the rate function, which enables effective training across different rate-distortion operating points. I expect this idea to have impact beyond compression, in other settings that could benefit from differentiable surrogates for similar objectives.

The authors provided a thoughtful response to my pre-review question. I still contend that, under a fixed range and quantization, a sufficiently expressive network trained to minimize distortion would automatically learn to produce codes within the fixed range with maximal entropy (i.e., it would meet the upper bound). However, the authors' second argument is convincing: enforcing this structure imposes a particular form on how the compressor output is used. To match the effective compression of the current system without the separate variable-rate encoder used to store q, one would need a more complex network capable of performing the computations that encoder currently handles.
