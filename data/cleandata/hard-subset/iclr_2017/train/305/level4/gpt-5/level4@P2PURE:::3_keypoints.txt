- Strong paper: end-to-end trained image compression–decompression system that outperforms established codecs (e.g., JPEG-2000) on bitrate–quality trade-offs.
- Key contribution: a differentiable approximation to the rate function that enables effective training across different rate–distortion trade-offs.
- The differentiable rate surrogate is likely to impact other tasks that could benefit from differentiable surrogates of similar functions.
- Authors provided a thoughtful response to the reviewer’s pre-review question.
- Reviewer’s contention: under fixed range and quantization, a sufficiently expressive network could learn codes within the fixed range with maximal entropy (reaching the upper bound).
- Counterpoint accepted: forcing this would impose a specific compressor-output form, and matching the current system’s effective compression would require a more complex network to replicate the separate variable-rate encoder used to store q.
