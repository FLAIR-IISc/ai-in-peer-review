- Extends prior rate–distortion optimization to deep encoders/decoders, adopts adaptive entropy coding, and links the method to VAEs.  
- Well written but novelty seems limited since the core rate–distortion approach was previously introduced.  
- Method may be a step backward conceptually because prior work optimized perceptual metrics while this paper uses MSE.  
- Empirical results show clear, visible improvement over JPEG 2000 and strong learned-compression performance.  
- Concern about Eq. 10: the partition function appears to depend on g_s(y; θ), so the claimed VAE equivalence for non-Euclidean metrics may be incorrect.  
- Questions: why optimize MSE instead of a perceptual metric, and why report evaluation only in PSNR?  
- Request for ablation: quantify the relative contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoders/decoders, include non-adaptive results, and provide more detail on the adaptive coder’s design and impact.  
- Reviewer indicates willingness to raise the score if the paper adds a clearer ablation isolating adaptive coding effects and expands on the adaptive coder’s design and influence.
