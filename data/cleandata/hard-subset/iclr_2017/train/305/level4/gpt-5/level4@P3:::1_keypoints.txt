- Proposes end-to-end lossy image compression jointly optimizing analysis/synthesis transforms and an entropy model under a rate–distortion objective.  
- Core contribution: convolutional transform coder using GDN/IGDN layers implementing local gain control.  
- Core contribution: differentiable quantization surrogate via additive uniform noise enabling SGD on the R–D objective.  
- Core contribution: connects the relaxed objective to variational autoencoders while evaluating discrete-domain bitstreams with an actual entropy coder.  
- Core contribution: strong empirical results showing improved PSNR (especially at low rates) and markedly better MS-SSIM/perceptual quality than JPEG and JPEG2000, including real bitrates and side information.  
- Paper is well written, information-theoretically grounded, and empirically convincing.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Primary reason: principled R–D formulation with a sound quantization relaxation tied to generative modeling and evaluated with real bitstreams.  
- Primary reason: strong empirical evidence of perceptual gains and competitive or superior PSNR to classical codecs, with careful analysis of relaxed vs. true R–D.  
- Problem/motivation: addresses limitations of linear transforms and scalar quantization using nonlinear learned transforms motivated by natural image statistics.  
- Method: additive-uniform-noise relaxation is justified as a differentiable approximation to quantization.  
- Literature placement: clearly explains links and differences to VAEs and prior learned-compression/generative work, arguing for optimizing R–D with an actual entropy coder for fair codec comparisons.  
- Evidence: demonstrates the relaxed objective tracks true distortion and rate, reports real bitrates with a CABAC-style coder and includes side information.  
- Evidence: evaluates on Kodak plus additional images and shows perceptual improvements (fewer blocking/ringing, better edges) consistent with higher MS-SSIM.  
- Implementation: provides detailed architectures, optimization, and latent density modeling.  
- Suggestion: add comparisons to stronger conventional codecs such as BPG (HEVC intra) or JPEG XR for better positioning.  
- Suggestion: include standard test images (Lena, Barbara, Mandrill, Peppers) in appendix with matched bitrates.  
- Suggestion: clarify rate-control practicalities—whether each λ needs a separate model or multi-rate operation is feasible.  
- Suggestion: report average encoding/decoding times and model size for practicality context.  
- Suggestion: quantify side-information overhead as a fraction of the total bitstream at low rates.  
- Entropy-model critique: quantify rate loss from assuming independent latent marginals and evaluate simple dependency models or adaptive vs. non-adaptive coders.  
- Entropy-model detail request: specify how often the coder’s probability model is updated and whether adaptation is per-image or reusable.  
- GDN/architecture critique: provide an ablation comparing GDN/IGDN to standard nonlinearities (ReLU/PReLU) with matched capacity to isolate GDN’s contribution.  
- Architecture suggestion: discuss impact of number of stages and channel counts and provide a sensitivity analysis.  
- Distortion/perception suggestion: try training with MS-SSIM to show potential gains and corroborate perceptual claims.  
- Evaluation suggestion: summarize aggregate results with BD-rate/BD-PSNR tables in addition to per-image plots.  
- Reproducibility request: release trained models, entropy coder, and scripts to reproduce bitstreams and figures.  
- Question 1: how stable is training across initializations and dataset subsets—do runs converge to similar R–D and transforms?  
- Question 2: what is the bitrate penalty from independent latent marginals versus simple scale or lightweight context models?  
- Question 3: at very low bitrates, how large is the bias between differential (relaxed) entropy and discrete entropy, and can you provide a calibration curve from relaxed rate to actual bits?  
- Question 4: can analysis/synthesis transforms be shared across rates with only entropy-model adjustments, or is re-training necessary to reach the R–D convex hull?  
- Question 5: for color images, is there benefit from using a perceptual color space (YCbCr) versus RGB within the learned transforms?  
- Question 6: are there image classes or failure modes where the method underperforms JPEG2000 in PSNR at higher rates, and what diagnostics explain those cases?
