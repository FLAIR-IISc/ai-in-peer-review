This paper extends a previously published rate–distortion optimization approach to deep encoders and decoders, moves from a simple entropy coding scheme to adaptive entropy coding, and discusses the connection to variational autoencoders.

While the paper is very well written, the novelty appears limited, since the core rate–distortion approach has already been introduced (unless I overlooked a new trick). In some respects, it even feels like a step backward: earlier work optimized a perceptual metric, whereas this submission uses MSE. That said, the empirical results show a clear, visible improvement over JPEG 2000, and I am not aware of any other learned encoder that has demonstrated this level of performance.

I have a concern about Equation 10. It appears incorrect: the partition function should depend on g_s(y; theta). If so, the approach is not equivalent to a VAE for non-Euclidean metrics.

Questions and requests:
- What motivated optimizing MSE rather than a perceptual metric, as in prior work? Given the authors’ backgrounds, it is also surprising that the evaluation is reported only in terms of PSNR.
- What is the relative contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus the deeper encoders and decoders? This seems crucial. It would be informative to include results without adaptation, as in the previous paper, and to provide more detail about the adaptive coder and its impact.

I would be happy to raise my score if the paper includes a clearer ablation isolating the effect of adaptive entropy coding and expands on the adaptive coder’s design and influence on performance.
