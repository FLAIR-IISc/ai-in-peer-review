- Proposes an end-to-end image compression system using cascaded convolutions with GDN/IGDN nonlinearities optimized for rate–distortion.
- Replaces quantization during training with additive uniform noise to enable SGD while approximating the discrete objective.
- Implements a CABAC-style adaptive entropy coder and reports true bit rates rather than just entropy estimates.
- Analyzes the connection between the relaxed training objective and variational autoencoders.
- Shows strong empirical rate–distortion performance vs. JPEG and JPEG 2000, with notably improved perceptual quality and competitive PSNR/MS-SSIM on Kodak and other images.
- Decision: Accept (weak).
- Reason 1 for accept: Credible, practically complete system with strong empirical gains and better visual quality than JPEG 2000 on many images.
- Reason 2 for accept: Paper is clear and demonstrates feasibility with real bitstreams; contributions are valuable though incremental.
- Technical soundness: Continuous relaxation is well motivated, validated empirically, and described with enough detail for reproduction.
- Practical significance: Reporting real bit rates with a concrete entropy coder strengthens the work relative to papers that only use entropy estimates.
- Comparison baseline: JPEG and JPEG 2000 are appropriate and visual/MS-SSIM comparisons support perceptual quality claims.
- Novelty: Main idea was introduced earlier by the authors; here the additions are deeper transforms, full GDN/IGDN stack, and adaptive entropy coding—useful but incremental.
- Request to clarify VAE equivalence: Partition function in the non-Euclidean case likely depends on θ via gs/gp and should be corrected.
- Request motivation for MSE: Authors should justify MSE choice and, if possible, provide a small comparison using a perceptual objective with the same architecture.
- Ablation request on coding: Separate effects of deeper transforms vs. adaptive entropy coding, e.g., compare to a non-adaptive entropy code or fixed marginal model.
- Architectural ablation request: Replace GDN with standard pointwise nonlinearities (matched parameter count) to quantify its contribution to RD and perceptual quality.
- Generalization and efficiency: Discuss computational cost (encode/decode time), memory, model size, scaling with image size/channels, and robustness across datasets beyond Kodak.
- Reporting suggestion: Add systematic MS-SSIM summaries alongside per-image plots to complement PSNR summaries.
- Question: Why use MSE rather than a perceptual objective, and can the authors share results for a perceptual-loss-trained variant?
- Question: How much gain comes from the adaptive entropy coder vs. learned transforms—provide RD curves for transforms + non-adaptive coder and for adaptive coder with simpler transforms.
- Question: Does the partition function in the non-Euclidean VAE derivation depend on θ via gs, and how does that affect the claimed equivalence and optimization?
- Question: How sensitive is performance to λ at test time; do models generalize to nearby rates or is per-λ training required?
- Question: Could a single model operate across multiple rates (e.g., scaling latents or learned rate controllers), and how would performance compare to per-λ models?
- Question: Can you quantify the contribution of GDN/IGDN vs. pointwise nonlinearities at matched depth/parameters for RD and perceptual artifacts?
- Question: What are typical encode/decode times on CPU/GPU for Kodak-sized images, model size, and are there deployment bottlenecks?
