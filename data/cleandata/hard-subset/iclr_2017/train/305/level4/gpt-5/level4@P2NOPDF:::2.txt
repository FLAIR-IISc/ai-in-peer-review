This paper extends a prior rate–distortion optimization framework for learned compression to deep encoders and decoders, and replaces a simple entropy coder with an adaptive one. It also discusses connections to variational autoencoders.

On novelty, since the core rate–distortion approach has already been published, the incremental contribution seems limited unless I have overlooked a new element. In some respects, the work steps back from earlier efforts that optimized a perceptual objective; here the loss is MSE. That said, the empirical results show clear improvements over JPEG 2000, and I am not aware of another learned codec that reaches this level of performance. The paper is also very well written.

Technical concern: Equation (10) appears incorrect. The partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ); otherwise the normalization is inconsistent. If this is correct, the claimed equivalence to a VAE would fail for non-Euclidean distortion measures.

Questions and requests:
- Why was MSE chosen over a perceptual metric as in previous work? Given the authors’ backgrounds, it is surprising that evaluation is limited to PSNR.
- Please clarify the contribution of adaptive entropy coding relative to deeper encoder/decoder architectures. An ablation without adaptation (as in the prior paper) would be very helpful to isolate the gains due to the adaptive coder. More detail on the adaptive coder’s design and its quantitative impact would substantially strengthen the paper; I would be inclined to increase my score with this additional analysis.
