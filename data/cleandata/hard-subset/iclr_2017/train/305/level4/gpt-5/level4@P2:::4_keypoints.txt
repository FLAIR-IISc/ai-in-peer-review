- Proposes an end-to-end learned image compression system using three-stage convolutional analysis/synthesis transforms with GDN/IGDN, uniform scalar quantization, and entropy coding.  
- Introduces a continuous relaxation of quantization by replacing rounding with additive uniform noise to enable gradient-based optimization of R + λD.  
- Connects the relaxed training objective to variational autoencoders while highlighting differences important for lossy compression.  
- Implements an actual CABAC-like entropy coder and reports real bitstreams for evaluation.  
- Demonstrates improved rate–distortion performance over JPEG/JPEG2000, especially at lower bit rates, and consistent perceptual gains (MS-SSIM).  
- Visual results show fewer blocking and ringing artifacts compared to conventional codecs.  
- Decision: Accept — justified by principled formulation and strong empirical evidence.  
- Method is well-motivated and grounded in known statistical structure of natural images; GDN/IGDN choice is appropriate.  
- The additive-noise relaxation is standard, validated empirically, and effectively approximates discrete rate/distortion behavior.  
- Insightful VAE connection clarifies why compression optimization differs from generative modeling.  
- Evaluation is rigorous: includes grayscale and RGB results on Kodak, PSNR/MS-SSIM comparisons, and detailed appendix for reproducibility.  
- Scope covers multiple λ values with separate models per rate and examines bias/variance and visual quality impacts.  
- Suggest adding comparisons to strong modern baselines like BPG (HEVC intra) for further context.  
- Recommend expanded ablation studies on GDN vs. pointwise activations, depth/capacity, and overcompleteness contributions.  
- Ask to explore single-model multi-rate mechanisms (conditional parameters or learned rate knobs) instead of per-λ training.  
- Entropy modeling could be strengthened beyond raster-scan per-channel marginals; analyze gains from spatial/context models.  
- Propose validating generalization on additional datasets (e.g., BSDS) beyond Kodak.  
- Request reporting of model size, encoding/decoding speed (CPU/GPU), and memory for deployment assessment.  
- Advise discussing failure modes where PSNR lags JPEG2000 and whether training with perceptual metrics could address them.  
- Recommend tightening the VAE section with a concise derivation and minor notation cleanup for clarity.  
- Questions for authors: ability to adapt a single model for multiple λs; sensitivity to noise width and i.i.d. assumption; expected gains from stronger entropy models; GDN vs. architecture contributions; encoder/decoder runtimes and model sizes; color handling and chroma subsampling choices; plans to release code/models for reproducibility.
