- End-to-end trained image codec that improves rate–distortion trade-off over JPEG 2000.
- Key technical contribution: a differentiable surrogate for the rate term enabling direct gradient-based optimization.
- Differentiable rate modeling avoids brittle approximations and REINFORCE-style estimators.
- Empirical results show consistent improvements across bitrates, not just a single operating point.
- Methodology is general and likely applicable to other problems needing differentiable proxies for discrete objectives.
- Reviewer initially raised a conceptual alternative (implicit high-entropy codes), but found the authors’ rebuttal persuasive.
- Explicit rate modeling and side-information (q) improve modularity, controllability, and reduce reconstruction network burden versus implicit approaches.
- Suggestion: provide calibration/fidelity analysis comparing the surrogate to true entropy or actual bit counts across operating points.
- Suggestion: analyze sensitivity to rate–distortion trade-off parameters and whether one model can be adapted post hoc to multiple bitrates.
- Suggestion: report computational costs (training time, inference latency) and memory footprint relative to classical and neural baselines.
- Suggestion: detail the role, dimensionality, encoding, and bitrate scaling of side-information q and include an ablation isolating its contribution.
- Suggestion: include qualitative failure cases or domains where the method underperforms to clarify limitations.
- Overall assessment: strong, timely paper with elegant, practical idea; likely influential and recommended for acceptance.
