This paper generalizes a prior rate–distortion optimization framework to deeper encoder and decoder architectures, and replaces a simple entropy coding scheme with adaptive entropy coding. It also clarifies how the approach relates to variational autoencoders.

Given that the underlying rate–distortion method has already been published, the incremental novelty here appears limited (unless I’ve overlooked a new component). In some respects the work even moves backward, as earlier efforts optimized a perceptual metric whereas this paper uses MSE. That said, the results clearly outperform JPEG 2000, and I am not aware of another learned codec demonstrated to reach this level of performance. The paper is very well written.

Equation 10 seems incorrect; the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ). If so, the method would not be equivalent to a VAE under non-Euclidean distortion metrics.

What motivated optimizing MSE rather than a perceptual metric as in prior work? Given the authors’ backgrounds, it is also unexpected that the evaluation is limited to PSNR.

What is the relative contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoders and decoders? This is important; an ablation without adaptation, as in the previous paper, would be useful. Please provide more detail on the adaptive coder and its effects; I would be happy to raise my score once this is addressed.
