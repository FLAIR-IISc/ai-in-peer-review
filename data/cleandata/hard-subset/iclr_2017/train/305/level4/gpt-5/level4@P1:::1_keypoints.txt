- End-to-end optimized image codec using cascaded convolutional transforms with GDN/IGDN.
- Rate–distortion optimized via continuous relaxation of quantization (additive uniform noise) enabling SGD.
- Links the relaxation to VAEs while emphasizing compression-specific differences.
- Trained per operating point (λ) and uses an implemented CABAC-like entropy coder reporting real bitrates.
- Empirically outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 in PSNR at low–medium rates and shows consistently superior MS-SSIM/perceptual quality.
- Decision: Accept — principled R–D formulation with a differentiable proxy that yields a practical, high-performing codec.
- Strong, consistent perceptual gains supported by MS-SSIM and actual bitstreams rather than entropy estimates.
- Addresses joint end-to-end learning of analysis/synthesis transforms and entropy models under scalar quantization.
- GDN/IGDN choice is well-motivated by prior Gaussianization and coding-efficiency results.
- Continuous relaxation is standard and carefully connected to VAEs; comparisons to JPEG/JPEG2000 are reasonable baselines.
- Results largely support claims: relaxed loss tracks discrete rate/distortion; extensive qualitative evidence and MS-SSIM gains.
- Some ablations provided, but more systematic comparisons would better substantiate GDN’s efficiency claim.
- Limitation: baselines do not include strongest non-neural classical codecs beyond JPEG 2000.
- Limitation: separate models per λ raise practical deployment questions about maintaining multiple models.
- Limitation: entropy model assumes independent marginals with limited spatial context, possibly underutilizing dependencies.
- Request: include results on Lena/Barbara/Baboon and stronger classical state-of-the-art comparisons.
- Request: clarify mapping from λ to achieved bpp and stability under distributional shift.
- Request: discuss feasibility of a single model covering multiple rates (e.g., via scaling or conditional methods).
- Request: detail the entropy coder’s contribution and how close bitrates are to discrete entropy and an oracle.
- Request: expand ablation comparing GDN/IGDN vs ReLU/sigmoid with similar parameter counts and RD curves.
- Request: report cross-dataset generalization beyond Kodak and a few images.
- Request: include encoding/decoding runtime and memory metrics and compare to JPEG 2000.
- Request: specify color-space handling (RGB vs luma–chroma) and its impact on RD and visuals.
- Request: quantify residual bias between relaxed differential entropy and discrete entropy across rates/channels.
- Reproducibility: consider releasing code/models and exact entropy coding configuration.
- Questions: how is λ chosen for target bitrate and how robust is this mapping across images?
- Questions: can the framework be extended to a single model supporting multiple rates without RD degradation?
- Questions: do dependencies remain after GDN and would modest context modeling in the entropy coder help?
- Questions: how sensitive are results to the independence assumption in the latent space and spline density parameterization?
- Questions: are there systematic failure cases where PSNR is worse than JPEG 2000 at higher rates?
- Questions: can the method be directly optimized for MS-SSIM (or other perceptual metrics) while keeping MSE benefits?
- Overall: compelling, well-executed work that would be a clear benchmark with stronger classical baselines and clarifications.
