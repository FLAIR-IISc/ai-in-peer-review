- Paper proposes an end-to-end trainable image compression system jointly optimizing analysis/synthesis transforms, quantizer, and learned entropy model.
- Key technical idea: relax quantization by replacing rounding with additive uniform noise to enable SGD on a differentiable rate–distortion objective.
- Transforms use three-stage convolutional cascades with GDN/IGDN nonlinearities motivated by biological gain control and Gaussianization.
- Authors connect the relaxed objective to variational autoencoders and clarify differences between compression and generative modeling.
- Importantly, they implement a CABAC-style entropy coder and evaluate actual bitrates, not just entropy estimates.
- Experiments on Kodak and other datasets show consistent MS-SSIM gains and generally improved PSNR vs JPEG and JPEG 2000, with more natural low-rate artifacts.
- Decision: Accept.
- Main reasons: strong end-to-end formulation with a practical differentiable rate proxy and an implemented entropy coder; convincing empirical and ablation results.
- Problem framing: clear, information-theoretic formulation targeting joint learning of transforms and entropy model for rate–distortion.
- Motivation/placement: GDN/IGDN choice is well-justified and comparisons to prior learned-compression work are fair and insightful.
- Claims/evidence: actual bitstreams evaluated, side information included, relaxed objective shown to approximate discrete R–D behavior with empirical validation.
- Visual results substantiate qualitative advantages like fewer blocking/ringing artifacts and better contour preservation.
- Author rebuttal convincing: explicit entropy model is preferable to forcing networks to saturate uniform quantization ranges, clarifying design trade-offs.
- Suggestion: add comparisons to more modern intra-image baselines (e.g., WebP, BPG/HEVC-intra).
- Suggestion: include ablations isolating GDN/IGDN vs pointwise nonlinearities at matched capacity and depth.
- Suggestion: explore or discuss single models that cover multiple rates (conditioning on λ or learned scale) to avoid per-λ training.
- Suggestion: acknowledge the limitation of a factorized prior and discuss spatial/context models, autoregressive or hyperprior approaches and CABAC’s role.
- Suggestion: report runtime, memory, and parameter counts for encode/decode compared to JPEG 2000 to assess deployability.
- Suggestion: include color fidelity metrics or discuss chroma artifacts since MS-SSIM was computed on luma only.
- Suggestion: provide λ-to-bpp calibration curves or mapping to help practitioners.
- Suggestion: include per-image aggregate statistics (e.g., medians, confidence intervals) to complement set-level R–D summaries.
- Question: quantify the performance loss from assuming independent latents and any gains from simple context models over y.
- Question: can transforms be conditioned on λ (or a learned parameter) to handle multiple rates without retraining?
- Question: provide formal bounds/conditions for how additive-uniform-noise relaxation bounds discrete entropy and distortion across λ and quantization levels.
- Question: report sensitivity to initialization and training hyperparameters; what fraction of runs reach comparable R–D performance?
- Question: provide encode/decode times and peak memory for 512×512 and 1080p on CPU/GPU; any entropy coder bottlenecks?
- Question: what is the average overhead for signaling λ and other headers, and how does this amortize with image size?
- Question: detail CABAC implementation: number of contexts, adaptation speed, and any alternative scan orders/tiling tried.
- Question: empirical comparison of GDN vs ReLU when scaling depth/width and qualitative artifact differences.
- Question: for RGB models, do you operate in RGB or a decorrelated space (YCbCr), and does a learned color transform help?
- Question: characterize failure cases where JPEG 2000 outperforms your method in PSNR at high rates and the content patterns driving that.
