- Strong paper presenting an end-to-end trained image compression/decompression system that outperforms established codecs (e.g., JPEG2000) on bitrate–quality trade-offs.
- Key contribution: a differentiable approximation to the rate function that enables effective training across different rate–distortion operating points.
- The differentiable rate surrogate is likely to impact domains beyond compression that need differentiable proxies for similar objectives.
- Authors provided a thoughtful response to the reviewer’s pre-review question.
- Reviewer’s contention: with fixed range and quantization, a sufficiently expressive network optimized for distortion would naturally produce codes with maximal entropy (meeting the upper bound).
- Authors’ counterargument (convincing): enforcing the proposed structure constrains how compressor output is used, and matching the current system without the separate variable-rate encoder for q would require a more complex network to replicate the encoder’s computations.
