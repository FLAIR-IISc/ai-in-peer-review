- Presents an end-to-end learned image compression system optimizing analysis/synthesis transforms, a uniform quantizer (relaxed via additive uniform noise), and an adaptive entropy coder, using three-stage convnets with GDN/IGDN and reporting actual bitrates via a CABAC-style coder.  
- Empirical results: consistent rate–distortion gains and visibly better perceptual quality versus JPEG and often JPEG 2000 at matched bitrates.  
- Decision: Weak Accept — strong practical/empirical results but limited conceptual novelty and missing key ablations.  
- Approach is well-motivated and grounded; VAE connection via noise relaxation is clearly articulated.  
- Novelty is moderate: deeper architecture and upgraded adaptive coder vs. prior GDN-based work are solid engineering but not a major conceptual advance.  
- Evaluation strength: reporting actual bitrates (CABAC) and careful training/evaluation methodology strengthen the claims.  
- Missing ablations make it hard to attribute gains to deeper transforms, GDN, or adaptive entropy coding.  
- Technical concern: Equation 10 likely incorrect — the partition function should depend on gs(y; θ), undermining VAE equivalence for non-Euclidean/perceptual metrics.  
- Ask authors to justify optimizing MSE/PSNR instead of perceptual metrics (e.g., MS-SSIM) and to provide perceptual-trained/evaluated results or rationale.  
- Request ablation quantifying the contribution of the adaptive entropy coder vs. transform improvements (report RD curves with a non-adaptive coder).  
- Ask for more detail on adaptive coder design: contexts, adaptation schedule, and bit-rate gap to measured discrete entropy across rates.  
- Request ablations on architectural choices: depth/width, GDN/IGDN vs. pointwise nonlinearities, down/up-sampling, and marginal model parameterization granularity.  
- Suggest evaluating single conditional model vs. separate models per λ to report trade-offs and practicality.  
- Practicality requests: report encoding/decoding runtime and memory vs. JPEG/JPEG 2000 on common hardware; quantify side-information overhead at different rates.  
- Ask about generalization: sensitivity to training dataset and cross-distribution performance (e.g., ImageNet-trained models on other datasets).  
- Specific technical questions to authors: confirm whether the partition function depends on θ via gs and implications for VAE equivalence; motivation for MSE optimization and availability of MS-SSIM results; quantify bitrate savings from adaptive coder; quantify GDN importance vs. ReLU at similar model sizes; report computational cost scaling with image size and channels.
