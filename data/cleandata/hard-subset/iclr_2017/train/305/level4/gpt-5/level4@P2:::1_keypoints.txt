- Proposes an end-to-end image compression codec using cascaded convolutions + GDN, uniform quantization relaxation, learned inverse, and an implemented entropy coder.
- Optimizes all components jointly for rate–distortion via additive uniform noise as a continuous quantization proxy.
- Clarifies connections to variational autoencoders while emphasizing compression-specific differences (RD curve operation, discrete coding).
- Shows consistent perceptual improvements over JPEG/JPEG2000, strong MS-SSIM gains across rates, and frequent PSNR gains especially at low rates.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Principled, well-motivated end-to-end RD formulation that produces real bitstreams.
- Key reason 2: Convincing empirical and visual improvements versus JPEG/JPEG2000 backed by MS-SSIM and often PSNR.
- Addresses learning joint analysis/synthesis and entropy model to avoid suboptimal modular transform coding.
- Positions work within transform coding and generative models, highlighting substantive differences and novel practical choices (GDN/IGDN, implemented coder).
- Continuous relaxation is theoretically justified and empirically validated; CABAC is used to report actual bits.
- Training on ImageNet and evaluation on Kodak/other images is appropriate; visuals show reduced blocking/ringing.
- Architecture and optimization details are documented; VAE relationship and proxy assumptions are clarified.
- Ablations are limited but preliminary GDN vs. simpler nonlinearity comparisons are discussed.
- Suggest adding stronger classical baselines (HEVC intra/BPG, WebP) and standard test images (Lena/Barbara/Baboon) for better positioning.
- Request clarification on CABAC vs. JPEG2000 context modeling and potential impact of stronger context models.
- Ask for complexity metrics: model size, encode/decode time, memory on CPU/GPU, and scaling with image size/rate.
- Recommend ablations quantifying contributions of GDN/IGDN, number of stages, and channel capacity.
- Discuss trade-offs between separate per-λ models and a single conditional model for rate sweep; possible extensions for rate control.
- Suggest more benchmarks (Tecnick, BSDS500) to demonstrate robustness, especially for RGB.
- Encourage releasing code, trained models, and bitstream specs for reproducibility.
- Questions: sensitivity to latent independence and possible gains from lightweight spatial/context models over ŷ.
- Questions: report aggregate statistics for proxy bias (mean/variance of rate/distortion) and RD-curve shifts (e.g., BD-rate).
- Questions: detail side information transmitted, overhead per image, and gains from adaptive modeling vs fixed models.
- Questions: reliability of λ selection for target bit rates and methods (per-image λ or post-quantization) to hit targets.
- Questions: whether RGB is processed directly or via learned color space and comparison to chroma subsampling strategies.
- Questions: typical encode/decode times for 512×512 on CPU/GPU and feasibility for real-time/embedded deployment.
- Questions: add comparisons to HEVC intra/BPG (and optionally WebP) to better position against top classical codecs.
