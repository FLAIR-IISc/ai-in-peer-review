- Presents an end-to-end trainable image compression system using cascaded conv transforms with GDN/IGDN, uniform scalar quantization relaxed by additive uniform noise, and a learned entropy model.
- Key technical contribution: a continuous, differentiable relaxation of the discrete rate–distortion objective enabling SGD and connecting to VAEs while addressing operational RD differences.
- Implements an operational arithmetic entropy coder (CABAC-style) and reports actual bitrates.
- Empirical results: outperforms JPEG/JPEG 2000 in PSNR at low rates and shows large, consistent gains in MS-SSIM and visual quality.
- Decision: Accept.
- Primary reason 1: principled and effective end-to-end formulation that tackles differentiating through quantization.
- Primary reason 2: strong empirical evidence with improved RD, perceptual quality, and real-bitrate evaluation.
- Addresses classical modular transform coding by jointly optimizing nonlinear transform and entropy model for RD trade-offs.
- Well positioned relative to transform coding, VAEs, and learned compression; distinctions from VAEs are clearly articulated.
- Continuous relaxation validated empirically (small distortion bias; diminishing rate bias at finer quantization).
- Inclusion of implemented entropy coder and real bitrate reporting increases rigor and comparability.
- Visual examples corroborate MS-SSIM gains and reduced ringing/blocking; architectural/training details provided in appendix.
- Suggest adding stronger baselines (e.g., BPG/HEVC intra) for better contextualization.
- Suggest exploring a single conditional model for multiple λ values to improve practicality.
- Note that factorized marginals and raster-scan CABAC limit spatial/context exploitation; stronger context/hyperprior models could reduce rate further.
- Recommend compact ablation comparing GDN/IGDN vs pointwise activations at matched capacity.
- Recommend broader perceptual evaluation (additional metrics or user study) and releasing models/code for reproducibility.
- Reviewer found authors’ reply convincing that decoupling transform learning from entropy coding is cleaner than forcing the network to subsume variable-rate coding.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to stages/channel counts/filter sizes and heuristics for capacity vs λ?
- Question: can a single model be conditioned on λ (or learned scale) to cover RD curve, and how did it perform if tried?
- Question: how robust is the factorized marginal model; measured rate loss vs simple spatial context model?
- Question: have you evaluated against more recent baselines (e.g., BPG) beyond JPEG/JPEG 2000?
- Question: encoding/decoding runtimes and memory usage relative to JPEG 2000 on CPU/GPU?
- Question: do you operate in RGB or a color transform (YCbCr); any color artifacts at low bitrates?
- Question: CABAC setup — how many contexts per channel and how much adaptation contributes to final bitrates?
- Question: does the perceptual advantage persist when evaluating on full-color perceptual metrics (not just luma MS-SSIM)?
