- Proposes an end-to-end image compression codec using cascaded conv analysis/synthesis transforms with GDN/IGDN, uniform scalar quantization, and an entropy coder.
- Replaces non-differentiable quantization with additive uniform noise to produce a continuous proxy loss optimized for rate–distortion via SGD.
- Relates the relaxed objective to variational autoencoders but emphasizes the need to operate across the full RD curve via a trade-off parameter λ.
- Trains models per rate point (per λ) on a large dataset and models marginal latents non-parametrically.
- Implements a CABAC-style entropy coder and reports actual bitrates rather than differential-entropy estimates.
- Demonstrates improved RD performance over JPEG and JPEG 2000 on standard tests (including Kodak), especially at low rates.
- Shows consistent perceptual quality gains measured by MS-SSIM and by visual inspection despite MSE training.
- Decision: Accept, citing a principled continuous relaxation and strong, practically relevant results from a complete codec.
- Tackles classic lossy compression by jointly optimizing transform, quantization surrogate, and entropy model for RD performance.
- The additive-uniform-noise relaxation is well motivated and tied to a nonparametric marginal model for accurate entropy estimation.
- Clearly positions the work relative to VAEs, JPEG/JPEG2000, and prior learned codecs, noting important distinctions for compression.
- Methodological completeness (implemented entropy coder, per-λ optimization) makes comparisons credible and maps the achievable RD convex hull.
- Empirical evidence shows the relaxed loss tracks true RD and yields PSNR/MS-SSIM improvements and better artifact profiles.
- Paper is clear and rigorous about model architecture (three-stage conv + GDN/IGDN), objectives, and optimization details with reasonable ablations.
- Suggestion: Develop a single conditional multi-rate model (e.g., via conditioning or learned scaling) to avoid per-λ training and enable fine-grained rate control.
- Suggestion: Provide systematic ablations comparing GDN/IGDN vs pointwise nonlinearities, number of stages/receptive field, marginal parameterizations, and noise widths.
- Suggestion: Improve entropy modeling by exploring spatial/context models or channel-wise autoregressive priors to reduce independence assumption limits.
- Suggestion: Report encoder/decoder runtime, model size, and memory footprints, with breakdowns for analysis/synthesis vs entropy coding.
- Suggestion: Clarify JPEG/JPEG2000 configuration choices and evaluate on additional datasets/resolutions to assess generalization and robustness.
- Suggestion: Try perceptual or mixed losses given strong MS-SSIM gains to assess perceptual training effects.
- Suggestion: Quantify overhead for transmitting λ and any model indices and report their impact at very low rates.
- Question: How many contexts and what adaptation schedule are used in the CABAC-style coder, and are marginal models shared per channel across spatial positions?
- Question: How sensitive is RD performance to uniform-noise width/centering, and were non-uniform or learned dequantization relaxations tried?
- Question: Can the architecture be conditioned on λ (e.g., FiLM or latent variance scaling) to enable a single multi-rate model?
- Question: Were residual dependencies among latents measured post-GDN, and would a simple hyperprior/context model capture enough structure to improve rates?
- Question: What are average encode/decode times and GPU/CPU requirements for Kodak-resolution images, and how does runtime scale with channels/stages?
- Question: What color space is used internally and is chroma subsampling applied to ensure fair JPEG/JPEG2000 comparisons?
- Question: How does the model generalize to high-resolution photos or out-of-training domains, and are there failure cases where JPEG2000 wins at higher rates?
- Question: Beyond λ, is any per-image parameter adaptation transmitted (e.g., marginal tables), what is the typical overhead, and how robust is decoding to model mismatch?
