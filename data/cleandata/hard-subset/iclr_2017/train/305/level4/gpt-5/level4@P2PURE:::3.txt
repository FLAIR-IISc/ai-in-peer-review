This is a strong paper that presents an end-to-end trained image compression–decompression system that achieves better bitrate–quality trade-offs than established image compression algorithms (e.g., JPEG-2000). Beyond demonstrating the effectiveness of deep learning for this application, a key contribution is the introduction of a differentiable approximation to the rate function, which the authors show enables effective training across different rate–distortion trade-offs. I expect this to have impact beyond compression itself, for other tasks that might benefit from differentiable surrogates of similar functions.

The authors provided a thoughtful response to my pre-review question. I still contend that, under a fixed range and quantization, a sufficiently expressive network would learn to automatically produce codes within a fixed range with maximal entropy (i.e., achieving the upper bound). However, the second argument is convincing: doing so imposes a specific form on how the compressor output is used, and matching the current system’s effective compression would require a more complex network capable of performing the computations currently handled by a separate variable-rate encoder used to store q.
