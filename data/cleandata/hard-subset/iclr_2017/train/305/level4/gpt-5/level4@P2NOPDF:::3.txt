This paper presents an end-to-end learned image compression–decompression system that achieves a better rate–distortion trade-off than established codecs such as JPEG 2000. Beyond demonstrating the value of deep learning for this application, a key contribution is the introduction of a differentiable proxy for the rate term, which enables effective training across different rate–distortion operating points. I anticipate this idea will have impact beyond image compression, benefiting other tasks that require differentiable approximations to otherwise non-differentiable objectives.

The authors provided a thoughtful response to my pre-review question. I still contend that, under a fixed range and quantization, a sufficiently expressive network trained to minimize distortion would, in principle, learn to produce codes within the allowed range that achieve maximal entropy, effectively reaching the theoretical upper bound. However, their second argument is convincing: enforcing this behavior implicitly constrains the form of the compressor output. To match the compression effectiveness of the current system, one would then need a substantially more complex network capable of performing the computations that are presently handled by a separate variable-rate encoder used to store q.
