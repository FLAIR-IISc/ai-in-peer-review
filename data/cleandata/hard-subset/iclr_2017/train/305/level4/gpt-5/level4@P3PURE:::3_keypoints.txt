- Strong paper presenting an end-to-end trained image compression system that outperforms established algorithms (e.g., JPEG 2000) on bit-rate–quality trade-offs.
- Introduces a differentiable "rate" function that enables effective training across different rate–distortion trade-offs.
- The differentiable rate idea is expected to have impact beyond compression for tasks needing differentiable approximations to similar objectives.
- Authors provided a thoughtful response to the reviewer’s pre-review question.
- Reviewer contends a sufficiently expressive network could, in principle, learn to confine codes to a fixed range with maximal entropy under fixed quantization to minimize distortion.
- Reviewer is convinced by the authors’ second argument that enforcing the constraint imposes structural use of compressor output, so matching the current system would force the network to internalize the separate variable-rate encoder’s computations, requiring a more complex model.
