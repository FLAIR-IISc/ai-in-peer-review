1. Summary of contributions
- The paper presents a highly convincing deep-learning-based image compression method. It is clearly written, and the incorporation of rate–distortion theory into the training objective is natural and well executed.
- The empirical evaluation uses an appropriate baseline (JPEG2000, not only JPEG), making the comparisons meaningful.
- Overall, the work appears poised to have strong impact in learned image compression.

2. Decision
Accept.
Key reasons: (i) the rigorous and well-motivated rate–distortion formulation, and (ii) solid empirical comparisons against a reasonable baseline (JPEG2000), together with a clear, well-written presentation.

3. Supporting arguments
- The rate–distortion perspective fits seamlessly with the model and training setup, enhancing both rigor and clarity.
- Prior neural compression papers often compare only to JPEG; using JPEG2000 as a baseline is a more appropriate standard and makes the results more compelling.
- The writing quality and organization make the technical contributions easy to follow, increasing the likelihood of impact in the community.

4. Additional feedback (to help improve the paper; not prerequisites for acceptance)
- Please include results on the standard images Lena/Barbara/Baboon (not “Gibbons”), as these remain common points of reference and facilitate comparison to prior literature.
- Add state-of-the-art references and comparisons with strong classical methods (including the classical approach I mentioned in my earlier questions). It is important to make explicit how neural approaches compare to the best pre-neural codecs.
- Clarify, in the text and figures, how your method is positioned relative to both categories (learned vs. classical) so that readers can readily understand where it stands.

Questions for the authors
- Could you add experiments on Lena/Barbara/Baboon and report their rate–distortion results alongside your current benchmarks?
- Which state-of-the-art classical codecs/methods (including the one I referenced in my questions) can you compare against directly, and how do your results stack up on common datasets and these canonical images?
- Can you provide a brief positioning statement summarizing how your approach compares to the best classical methods in terms of both PSNR and perceptual quality, to make the relative standing unambiguous?
