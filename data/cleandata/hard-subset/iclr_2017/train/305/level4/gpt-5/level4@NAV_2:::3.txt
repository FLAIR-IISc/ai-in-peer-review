This paper presents an end-to-end trained image compression and reconstruction system that achieves superior rate–distortion trade-offs compared to established codecs (e.g., JPEG 2000). Beyond demonstrating the value of deep learning for this application, a key contribution is the introduction of a differentiable surrogate for the rate term, which enables effective training across different rate–distortion operating points. I anticipate this idea will have impact beyond compression for other problems that can benefit from differentiable approximations to discrete objectives.

The authors provided a thoughtful and thorough response to my pre-review question. I still maintain that, under a fixed dynamic range and quantization scheme, a sufficiently expressive network could learn to produce codes confined to that range while achieving the maximum possible entropy (i.e., reaching the upper bound) to minimize distortion. That said, their second argument is compelling: enforcing this effectively imposes a particular structure on how the compressor’s output is consumed, and matching the effective compression of the current system would then require a more complex network capable of subsuming the functionality now handled by the separate variable-rate encoder used to store q.
