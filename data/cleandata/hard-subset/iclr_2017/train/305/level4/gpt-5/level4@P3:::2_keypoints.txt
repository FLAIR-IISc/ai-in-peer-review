1) Presents an end-to-end learned image compression codec using cascaded convolutional analysis/synthesis transforms with GDN/IGDN, uniform scalar quantization (relaxed with additive uniform noise), and a CABAC-style entropy coder.  
2) Trains with a relaxed rate–distortion objective and links this relaxation to a VAE-style log-likelihood under certain conditions.  
3) Empirically outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 in rate–distortion (especially at low bitrates) and yields notably better perceptual quality (supported by MS-SSIM and visual comparisons).  
4) Evaluations use actual bitstreams (practical entropy coder in loop) and test images not used for training, increasing practical relevance.  
5) Decision: Accept.  
6) Key reason 1: Strong empirical significance — consistent bitrate improvements and clear perceptual gains versus widely used baselines.  
7) Key reason 2: Technical completeness and clarity — coherent end-to-end formulation, working entropy coder, and insightful VAE connection.  
8) Problem/motivation: Addresses joint learning of nonlinear transforms and marginal models to optimize rate–distortion, bridging transform coding and generative modeling.  
9) Methodological soundness: The additive-uniform-noise relaxation is theoretically justified and empirically verified as a good approximation to discrete entropy and MSE.  
10) GDN/IGDN choice: Supported by prior work for Gaussianizing image statistics and shown here to reduce depth/parameter needs for strong compression.  
11) Empirical support detail: Reports include side information in bitstream, adaptive coding, and convincing MS-SSIM/visual gains while avoiding reliance on raw entropy estimates.  
12) Novelty: Incremental conceptually but substantial in delivering a fully operational codec (deeper conv–GDN stacks, RGB support, practical entropy coder) with state-of-the-art performance.  
13) Feedback — VAE equivalence: Clarify whether partition function Z(λ) depends on θ via gs and when px|ỹ is properly normalizable for non-Euclidean metrics or non-affine gp.  
14) Feedback — MSE vs perceptual training: Add an ablation comparing MSE-trained vs perceptual-metric–trained models (e.g., MS-SSIM) at matched capacity to quantify PSNR/MS-SSIM/appearance trade-offs.  
15) Feedback — Adaptive entropy coding: Report rate gaps between relaxed differential entropy, discrete entropy estimate, and realized bit rate, and ablate adaptive vs non-adaptive arithmetic coding.  
16) Feedback — Architectural ablations: Quantify GDN vs ReLU/Leaky ReLU at fixed depth/width (small table/figure) to support claims about GDN efficiency.  
17) Feedback — Fairness in color handling: Clarify comparisons versus JPEG’s default 4:2:0 (report 4:2:0 and 4:4:4 or luminance-only results) to address chroma-treatment concerns.  
18) Feedback — Complexity and throughput: Provide encode/decode runtimes and model size relative to JPEG 2000 to inform deployment feasibility.  
19) Question 1: Should Z(λ) be Z(λ,θ) in Eq. (16) given gs dependence, and when is the VAE equivalence valid for non-Euclidean metrics/non-affine gp?  
20) Question 2: Provide quantitative summaries (mean bias/variance) of the relaxed entropy/distortion approximations versus actual bitrate and MSE across test images and rates.  
21) Question 3: What is the average bitrate gain of the adaptive coder versus a non-adaptive arithmetic coder with the same transforms and marginals?  
22) Question 4: At fixed depth/width, how much does performance degrade replacing GDN/IGDN with ReLU or tanh, and how much extra capacity would ReLU need to match GDN?  
23) Question 5: Did you try MS-SSIM or hybrid training, and how did rate–distortion and perceived quality change at low bitrates?  
24) Question 6: Why train separate models per λ rather than a single model with learned rate-control (conditioning/feature scaling/λ-annealing), and what trade-offs were observed?  
25) Question 7: Did you explore YCbCr transforms and chroma subsampling, and could that reduce rate without harming luma MS-SSIM?  
26) Question 8: How sensitive is performance to training set distribution/preprocessing, and have you tested on larger/diverse benchmarks beyond Kodak and standard images?
