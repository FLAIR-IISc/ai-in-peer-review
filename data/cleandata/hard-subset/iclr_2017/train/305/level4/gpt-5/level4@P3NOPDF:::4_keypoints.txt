- Introduces an end-to-end learned image compression framework that directly optimizes rate–distortion using a differentiable relaxation for backprop through quantization and rate estimation.  
- Principled problem formulation casting compression as a rate–distortion loss with a trainable relaxation.  
- Technical contribution: a simple, effective relaxation of quantization and rate terms enabling stable joint training of encoder, decoder, and entropy model.  
- Comprehensive experiments show strong rate–distortion performance on natural images with gains over standard codecs and supporting qualitative reconstructions.  
- Provides insightful analysis and ablations on learned representations, rate–distortion weighting, failure modes, and trade-offs.  
- Clear writing, informative figures, and easy-to-follow presentation.  
- Needs more details on training data (size, diversity, preprocessing) and evaluation on out-of-distribution or very high-resolution images.  
- Lacks runtime and complexity characterization (encoding/decoding speed, memory, computational cost) critical for practical deployment.  
- Unclear how bitrate control is handled (single model for multiple rates vs. separate models per λ) and whether rate scaling is smooth.  
- Perceptual quality evaluation is limited; recommend MS-SSIM or a user study, especially at low bitrates.  
- Would benefit from clearer positioning and comparison with contemporaneous learned compression methods.  
- Suggested additions: dataset statistics and cross-dataset generalization tests.  
- Suggested additions: runtime benchmarks on common hardware and complexity analyses for encoder/decoder/entropy model.  
- Suggested additions: demonstrate variable-rate capability within a single model or clarify trade-offs of multiple models.  
- Suggested additions: add perceptual metrics and a small qualitative user study.  
- Suggested additions: expand discussion and ablations on the relaxation’s sensitivity and training vs. inference behavior; release code and pretrained models.  
- Overall assessment: a well-written, timely, and solid contribution with strong empirical results; recommendation to accept with high confidence.
