- Presents an end-to-end image compression system jointly optimizing nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms and quantizer via SGD using GDN/IGDN layers and additive uniform-noise relaxation.  
- Extends prior work by using deeper encoder/decoder architectures and an adaptive entropy coding scheme, and relates the relaxed objective to VAEs.  
- Empirically produces visibly superior reconstructions and outperforms JPEG 2000, with clear writing and careful presentation.  
- Decision: weak accept, mainly because it sets a new bar for learned image compression and provides a complete, practical system.  
- Tackles joint rate–distortion optimization for lossy compression rather than separate optimization of components.  
- Well-motivated use of GDN/IGDN and continuous relaxation; novelty is incremental (deeper nets + adaptive coding) rather than conceptual.  
- Experimental evidence is compelling (visual gains over JPEG 2000) but incremental novelty and some theoretical/ablation gaps remain.  
- Concern that the paper returns to MSE/PSNR training/evaluation rather than a perceptual objective used in earlier work.  
- Request to clarify the VAE connection and a likely error around Eq. 10 regarding partition-function dependence on gs(y; θ).  
- Ask authors to justify optimizing MSE instead of a perceptual metric and why evaluation emphasizes PSNR.  
- Recommend ablation studies isolating the contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper transforms (e.g., results without adaptation).  
- Ask for more details on the adaptive entropy coder design and its impact on bit rates for reproducibility.  
- Specific questions for authors: clarify Eq. 10/partition-function dependence, motivation for MSE/PSNR choice, and quantify gains from adaptive coding versus architecture depth.
