- Strong paper: end-to-end trained image compression/decompression achieves superior bitrate–quality trade-offs versus established codecs (e.g., JPEG-2000).  
- Major contribution: introduces a differentiable surrogate for the rate term, enabling effective training across different rate–distortion operating points.  
- The differentiable-rate idea is expected to influence areas beyond compression where differentiable approximations to similar quantities help.  
- Reviewer’s contention: under fixed range and quantization, a sufficiently expressive network optimizing distortion would produce codes with maximal entropy (attaining the upper bound).  
- Authors’ counterargument: imposing that constraint enforces a specific structure on compressor outputs, so matching the current system’s effective compression would require a more complex network.  
- The authors provided a thoughtful response to the reviewer’s pre-review.
