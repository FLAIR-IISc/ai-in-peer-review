- Paper extends a prior rate–distortion framework to deeper encoder/decoder architectures and replaces simple entropy coding with adaptive entropy coding, and clarifies links to VAEs.  
- Incremental novelty appears limited because the underlying rate–distortion method was already published; no clearly new component identified.  
- In some ways the work regresses by optimizing MSE rather than the perceptual metric used in earlier work.  
- Results outperform JPEG 2000 and demonstrate a high-performing learned codec; the paper is well written.  
- Equation 10 seems incorrect: the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ), which would break equivalence to a VAE under non-Euclidean distortion metrics.  
- Reviewer asks for motivation for optimizing MSE instead of a perceptual metric and notes surprise that evaluation is limited to PSNR given the authors’ backgrounds.  
- Requests clarity on the relative contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoders/decoders.  
- Suggests an ablation study without adaptive coding (as in the previous paper) to isolate effects.  
- Requests more detail on the adaptive coder and its empirical effects; reviewer would consider raising their score if these issues are addressed.
