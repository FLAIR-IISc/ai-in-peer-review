Summary
The paper extends a previously published rate–distortion (R–D) optimization framework for learned image compression by (i) replacing shallow transforms with deep analysis/synthesis transforms and (ii) moving from a simple entropy model to an adaptive entropy coding scheme. It also discusses connections to variational autoencoders (VAEs). Empirically, the method shows clear gains over JPEG 2000, and, to my knowledge, achieves state-of-the-art performance among learned compressors at the time of writing. The paper is clearly written and easy to follow.

Strengths
- Strong empirical performance: consistent, visible improvements over JPEG 2000 across rates.
- Clear exposition that ties R–D optimization with probabilistic modeling and VAEs.
- Practical advance: integration of adaptive entropy coding with learned transforms is valuable for real-world compression.

Concerns and weaknesses
- Novelty: The core R–D optimization approach has appeared before; the main additions (deeper transforms and adaptive entropy coding) read as incremental engineering improvements rather than a fundamentally new framework. If there is a substantive new technical contribution beyond scaling depth and adding adaptation, please make it explicit.
- Objective and evaluation: The paper reverts from a perceptually motivated distortion (used in prior work) to mean squared error (MSE), and evaluates primarily with PSNR. This is surprising given the authors’ backgrounds and the community’s emphasis on perceptual quality.
  - Please clarify the rationale for optimizing MSE rather than a perceptual metric (e.g., MS-SSIM or a feature-based loss).
  - Consider reporting MS-SSIM and/or other perceptual metrics, and include visual comparisons or a small user study if feasible.
- Ablations and attribution of gains: It is difficult to disentangle the contributions of deeper transforms from those of adaptive entropy coding.
  - Please provide an ablation that holds the networks fixed while toggling adaptation on/off (e.g., replicate the previous non-adaptive coder) to quantify the gain from adaptation alone.
  - Conversely, show the effect of increasing transform depth with a fixed entropy model.
  - Reporting BD-rate deltas for each component would make the contribution breakdown clear.
- Equation and VAE equivalence: Equation (10) appears to be incorrect: the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ). If so, the equivalence to a VAE does not hold under non-Euclidean distortions because the normalization term is no longer constant with respect to the parameters. Please verify the derivation, clarify the assumptions under which the VAE connection is exact (e.g., Euclidean distortion, specific noise model), and discuss implications for optimizing perceptual metrics.

Requests for clarification and additional detail
- Adaptive entropy model:
  - Describe the adaptation mechanism in more detail: the form of the conditional priors, context (causal? spatial? hierarchical?), and how parameters are predicted and transmitted.
  - Quantify the side-information rate for adaptation and its impact on net bpp.
  - Provide computational complexity and decoding speed measurements; adaptation often adds overhead.
  - Discuss training stability (e.g., any regularization of the prior or temperature/scale parameters).
- Training details:
  - Specify whether additive uniform noise is used during training to approximate quantization, and how this interacts with the adaptive entropy model.
  - Include hyperparameters and architectures sufficient for reproducibility (depth, receptive fields, activation functions, entropy model parameterization).
- Evaluation:
  - Provide rate–distortion curves and BD-rate numbers versus baselines (JPEG 2000 at multiple settings) on standard datasets.
  - If possible, compare against the prior non-adaptive method under identical training/data protocols to isolate improvements due to this paper.
  - Consider including perceptual metrics (MS-SSIM) and qualitative examples at matched bitrates.

Minor comments
- Please correct or annotate Equation (10) as noted above, and clarify the dependence of the partition function on g_s(y; θ).
- If VAE equivalence requires Euclidean distortion and particular likelihoods, state this explicitly in the main text.
- A brief discussion on how the method might extend to non-Euclidean or perceptual distortions (and what breaks) would be valuable.

Overall assessment
This is a well-written paper that demonstrates strong empirical compression performance and makes a practical step forward by combining deep transforms with adaptive entropy coding. However, the novelty relative to prior published work appears moderate, and key evidence is missing to attribute the gains to specific components and to address perceptual quality.

I am currently borderline. With (i) a corrected and clarified derivation around Equation (10) and the VAE connection, (ii) ablations quantifying the contributions of adaptive coding versus deeper transforms, and (iii) expanded evaluation that includes perceptual metrics and/or qualitative results, I would be inclined to raise my score. If I have overlooked a substantial technical novelty, please highlight it during rebuttal.
