- Presents an end-to-end learned image compression system that outperforms JPEG 2000 on rate–distortion trade-off.
- Introduces a differentiable proxy for the rate term enabling effective training across different rate–distortion operating points.
- The differentiable-rate idea is likely broadly useful for other tasks needing differentiable approximations to non-differentiable objectives.
- Authors provided a thoughtful response to the reviewer’s pre-review question.
- Reviewer’s counterargument: a sufficiently expressive network under fixed range/quantization could, in principle, learn maximal-entropy codes and reach the theoretical upper bound.
- Authors’ second argument is convincing: enforcing maximal-entropy behavior constrains compressor output form, and matching current system’s effectiveness would require a much more complex network to replicate the separate variable-rate encoder that stores q.
