- End-to-end optimization of lossy image compression over analysis/synthesis transforms, uniform scalar quantization, and entropy coding.  
- Transforms implemented as three-stage convolutional cascades using GDN/IGDN inspired by biological gain control.  
- Non-differentiable quantization handled via a continuous relaxation: replace rounding with additive uniform noise for SGD training.  
- Learn nonparametric, factorized marginal models for latents and train separate models per Lagrange multiplier λ to trace the R–D curve.  
- Evaluation uses an actual CABAC-like entropy coder and reports true bitrates rather than differential entropies.  
- Empirical results: generally outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 in PSNR at many bitrates and shows substantial gains in MS-SSIM.  
- Visual quality improvements: fewer blocking/ringing artifacts and compelling subjective reconstructions.  
- Paper clarifies connections and differences to variational autoencoders.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main reason: principled, unified end-to-end compression framework with a sound relaxation and real bitrate reporting.  
- Main reason: strong empirical evidence of improved rate–distortion and perceptual quality across diverse images and bitrates.  
- Significance: addresses an important, timely problem by directly optimizing the classical R + λD objective and tackling non-differentiable quantization.  
- Positioning: builds on transform coding and deep architectures; GDN/IGDN motivated by prior work on Gaussianization and gain control.  
- Rigor: clear methodology (architecture, training, entropy model), validated relaxation, actual entropy coding, multiple metrics and datasets, and acknowledged limitations.  
- Limitation: single model per λ is less convenient than a variable-rate model — suggest discussing rate-control strategies within one model.  
- Limitation: factorized latent independence likely leaves coding gains on the table; recommend experiments with spatial/context models or a hyperprior.  
- Suggestion: provide ablations comparing GDN/IGDN to pointwise nonlinearities at matched capacity and analyze sensitivity to network depth/capacity.  
- Suggestion: report encode/decode runtimes, model sizes, and memory to contextualize deployment practicality.  
- Suggestion: clarify color-space handling and chroma subsampling for baselines and ensure consistent MS-SSIM computation.  
- Suggestion: analyze the relaxation–discretization gap’s effect on λ selection and final operating points.  
- Reproducibility: consider releasing code, models, and bitstream format.  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to the factorized latent assumption and what bitrate savings would autoregressive/hyperprior models yield?  
- Question: can one trained transform serve multiple λ by modulating a few parameters, or do transforms qualitatively change across rates?  
- Question: how does GDN/IGDN compare to ReLU/PReLU when depth/parameters are matched, especially at low bitrates?  
- Question: what are average encoding/decoding times and model sizes, and how do they scale with resolution?  
- Question: has more structured entropy coding context or scan orders been tested, and what extra gain is feasible?  
- Question: is the uniform-noise relaxation robust to training instabilities at very low rates, and were special tricks required?  
- Question: for RGB, were alternative color spaces or learned color transforms explored and how do they affect R–D?
