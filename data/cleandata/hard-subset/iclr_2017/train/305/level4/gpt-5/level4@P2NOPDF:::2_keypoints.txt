- Paper extends prior rate–distortion framework to deep encoders/decoders and replaces a simple entropy coder with an adaptive one; also discusses VAE connections.
- Novelty appears limited because the core rate–distortion approach was previously published; contribution may be incremental.
- The paper reverts to MSE loss (PSNR) instead of the perceptual objectives used in earlier work, which is surprising given authors’ background.
- Empirical results show clear improvements over JPEG 2000 and competitive learned-codec performance despite the above concerns.
- Manuscript is well written.
- Technical concern: Equation (10) seems incorrect — the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ); otherwise normalization is inconsistent and VAE equivalence may fail for non-Euclidean distortions.
- Request for justification of choosing MSE/PSNR over perceptual metrics and broader evaluation.
- Request for ablation isolating the impact of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoder/decoder architectures, plus more detail on the adaptive coder’s design and quantitative contribution.
