- Proposal: End-to-end image compression jointly optimizing analysis/synthesis transforms, scalar quantizer, and entropy model using CNNs with GDN/IGDN.  
- Key technical contribution: Continuous relaxation of quantization via additive uniform noise enabling backpropagation through rate and distortion.  
- Implementation: Non-parametric latent marginals and a CABAC-style entropy coder reporting true bit rates.  
- Theory connection: Relates the relaxed objective to variational autoencoders, clarifying similarities and differences.  
- Empirical results: Consistent MS-SSIM gains and competitive or superior PSNR vs. JPEG/JPEG 2000 on Kodak and held-out images, with fewer low-rate artifacts.  
- Validation: Shows the relaxed objective is a reliable proxy for the discrete objective across rates.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main reason 1: Strong methodological contribution—differentiable proxy for rate enabling end-to-end codec optimization.  
- Main reason 2: Convincing empirical evidence—better rate–distortion (especially perceptual) with real bitstream evaluation.  
- Problem significance: Addresses full end-to-end rate–distortion optimization rather than isolated component tuning.  
- Motivation/placement: Well-grounded in transform coding and quantization theory and properly positioned relative to VAEs.  
- Technical soundness: Additive uniform noise relaxation validated empirically; non-parametric marginals reduce model mismatch; GDN choices justified.  
- Empirical rigor: Large training set, held-out tests, strong baselines, both PSNR and MS-SSIM reported, and actual entropy-coded bitstreams used.  
- Practical relevance: Efficient encode/decode at test time and produces natural degradations valuable for real-world use.  
- Feedback—comparisons: Include modern strong baselines (e.g., BPG/HEVC intra) for context.  
- Feedback—single model: Discuss or attempt a single scalable model for multiple rates and trade-offs vs per-λ models.  
- Feedback—entropy modeling: Evaluate more advanced context models to isolate transform vs coder contributions.  
- Feedback—ablations: Provide systematic ablations comparing GDN to pointwise nonlinearities at fixed model size/depth.  
- Feedback—complexity/deployment: Report throughput, memory, and parameter counts per λ for standard hardware and embedded feasibility.  
- Feedback—evaluation protocol: Provide standardized summaries (e.g., Bjøntegaard delta rates) to synthesize results across images.  
- Feedback—color handling: Clarify color space processing and any chroma subsampling to ensure fair comparisons.  
- Feedback—reproducibility: Release code/models and entropy coder to facilitate adoption.  
- Question 1: Quantify how differential-entropy estimates deviate from realized bit rates across λ (average absolute/relative error) and whether corrections help training.  
- Question 2: Measure residual dependencies among latents y after training and whether lightweight context models would reduce rate.  
- Question 3: For fixed parameter budgets, how does GDN compare to ReLU/PReLU in RD performance and where does GDN help most?  
- Question 4: Did you try conditioning mechanisms (on λ or scale) for single-model multi-rate operation and how do they compare RD-wise to per-λ models?  
- Question 5: Did you try simple spatially adaptive contexts for CABAC and what incremental gains were observed?  
- Question 6: Can perceptual losses or texture priors be integrated to preserve textures at low rates without harming rates?
