This paper extends a previously published rate–distortion optimization approach to deep encoders and decoders, and replaces a simple entropy coding scheme with adaptive entropy coding. It also discusses connections to variational autoencoders.

Because the underlying rate–distortion approach has already appeared, the novelty here seems limited (unless I missed a new idea). In some respects it is even a step backward, as prior work optimized a perceptual metric whereas this paper uses MSE. That said, the results show a clear improvement over JPEG 2000, and I am not aware of any other learned codec that has demonstrated this level of performance. The paper is very well written.

Equation 10 appears to be incorrect; the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ). If so, the approach would not be equivalent to a VAE for non-Euclidean distortion measures.

Why optimize MSE rather than a perceptual metric as in earlier work? Given the authors’ backgrounds, it is also surprising that the evaluation is limited to PSNR.

What is the contribution of adaptive entropy coding relative to using deeper encoders and decoders? This seems important; it would be helpful to report performance without adaptation, as in the previous paper. More details about the adaptive coder and its impact should be provided, and I would be inclined to raise my score if these points are addressed.
