This paper extends a previously published rate–distortion optimization framework to deep encoders and decoders, replaces a simple entropy coding scheme with adaptive entropy coding, and discusses the relationship to variational autoencoders.

Because the underlying rate–distortion approach has already appeared, the incremental novelty here seems limited (unless I missed a key innovation). In one respect the work even feels like a step backward: prior work optimized a perceptual metric, whereas this paper uses MSE. That said, the empirical results are strong—the method visibly outperforms JPEG 2000—and I am not aware of any other learned codec demonstrated to reach this level of performance. The paper is also very well written.

I have a technical concern: Equation (10) appears incorrect. The partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ). If so, the approach would not be equivalent to a VAE for non-Euclidean distortion metrics.

Why was MSE chosen as the optimization target rather than a perceptual metric, as in earlier work? Given the authors’ backgrounds, it is also surprising that the evaluation is reported only in terms of PSNR.

What is the relative contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoders and decoders? This seems crucial. An ablation without adaptation, as in the previous paper, would clarify the source of the gains. More detail on the adaptive coder and its effects should be provided. I would be happy to raise my score if these analyses are added.
