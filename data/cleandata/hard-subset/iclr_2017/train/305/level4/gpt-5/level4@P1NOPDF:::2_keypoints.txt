- Paper extends a prior rate–distortion optimization framework to deep encoders and decoders and replaces simple entropy coding with adaptive entropy coding.  
- Authors discuss the relationship between their method and variational autoencoders (VAEs).  
- Incremental novelty appears limited because the underlying rate–distortion approach was previously published.  
- The change from optimizing a perceptual metric (in prior work) to MSE here feels like a step backward.  
- Empirical results are strong: the method visibly outperforms JPEG 2000 and achieves high performance among learned codecs.  
- The paper is very well written.  
- Technical concern: Equation (10) seems incorrect — the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ), affecting VAE equivalence for non-Euclidean distortion metrics.  
- Why was MSE chosen as the optimization target instead of a perceptual metric used in earlier work?  
- Surprising that evaluation is reported only in PSNR given authors’ backgrounds.  
- Unclear relative contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoders/decoders; an ablation without adaptation is needed.  
- More detail required on the adaptive coder and its specific effects; reviewer may raise score if these analyses are added.
