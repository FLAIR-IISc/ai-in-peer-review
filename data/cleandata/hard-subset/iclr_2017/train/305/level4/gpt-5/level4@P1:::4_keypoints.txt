- End-to-end image compression using cascaded convolutional analysis/synthesis transforms with GDN/IGDN, uniform scalar quantization, and joint marginal entropy modeling.
- Continuous relaxation of quantization via additive uniform noise to enable SGD-based joint rate–distortion optimization; under certain conditions equivalent to a VAE ELBO.
- Implements arithmetic entropy coding and reports actual bitrates; shows improved rate–distortion vs. JPEG/JPEG2000, especially at low rates, with higher MS-SSIM and better visual quality.
- Decision: Accept — principled, well-motivated method with compelling empirical results and perceptual quality.
- Tackles classical lossy compression by jointly optimizing transforms and entropy model for chosen rate–distortion trade-offs; relaxation is simple and effective, matching true metrics at finer quantization.
- Technical novelty: use of GDN/IGDN cascades is neuroscience-inspired and rooted in prior Gaussianizing work; distinguishes from batch norm and situates relative to VAEs.
- Empirical validation: models trained per λ, tested on Kodak and other held-out sets, report CABAC-style actual bitrates including side information, and outperform baselines in PSNR (notably at low rates) and MS-SSIM.
- Scientific rigor: detailed architecture, training, marginal modeling, entropy coding, and analysis of proxy objective bias/variance; careful comparison methodology with caveats.
- Clarify operating points: emphasize that separate models are trained for each λ and discuss deployment implications of this constraint.
- Entropy modeling limitation: current independent marginal and raster-scan coding is simple; estimate potential gains from stronger context models (spatial or hyperpriors).
- Complexity/runtime: add concise encoding/decoding runtime and memory comparisons on CPU/GPU relative to JPEG/JPEG2000 for practical relevance.
- Ablations: include compact ablation (e.g., GDN vs ReLU with matched parameters) to quantify GDN/IGDN benefits for compression.
- Evaluation breadth: add summarized results on a larger, more diverse test set beyond standard/qualitative galleries to support generalization.
- VAE connection: clarify in the main text when the relaxed objective no longer corresponds to a normalized likelihood (e.g., with non-Euclidean/perceptual metrics).
- Question — rate control/model reuse: how sensitive is performance to λ mismatches at test time and can a single model with learned scale/conditional transforms achieve similar convex-hull performance?
- Question — entropy coding gap: measurements of gap between reported bitrates and (i) discrete entropy of codes and (ii) performance with stronger context models; expected gains from simple spatial contexts?
- Question — relaxation accuracy: does the positive bias of the relaxed differential entropy at coarse quantization harm discrete performance at very low rates?
- Question — architecture sensitivity: robustness to changes in depth/width/filter sizes; is depth critical when using GDN or can shallower/wider nets match performance?
- Question — computational cost: typical training and inference times per 256×256 image and observations on energy/compute for mobile/embedded deployment?
- Question — color handling: effects of learned color space vs RGB or Y’CbCr on rate–distortion and artifacts for RGB models?
- Question — generalization to high resolution: behavior on >1 MP images without retraining and how performance scales with image size?
- Overall conclusion: strong, timely contribution advancing neural image compression with a principled, practical end-to-end formulation.
