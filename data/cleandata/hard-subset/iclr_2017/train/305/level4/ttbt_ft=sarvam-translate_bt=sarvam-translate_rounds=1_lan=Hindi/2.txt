This paper expands on the rate-distortion optimization approach for deep encoders and decoders, ranging from a simple entropy encoding scheme to adaptive entropy coding. Additionally, the paper also discusses the relationship of the approach with variable autoencoders.

Given that the rate-distortion optimization approach has already been published, the novelty of this submission is probably not significant (correct me if I've missed any new trick). In some ways, this paper also takes a step back, as the previous work optimized for a perceptual metric using MSE. However, the results are a clear improvement over JPEG 2000, and I am not aware of any other learned encoding that has been shown to achieve this level of performance. This paper is very well written.

Equation 10 seems incorrect, and I believe the partition function should depend on g_s(y; theta). This would mean that this approach is not equivalent to VAE for non-Euclidean metrics.

What was the reason for optimizing MSE instead of a perceptual metric, as in the previous work? Given the author's background, it's surprising that the evaluation was also done only in terms of PSNR.

What is the contribution of adaptive entropy coding compared to the impact of deep encoders and decoders? This seems like an important piece of information, so it would be interesting to see the performance without optimization, as in the previous paper. More information should be provided on the adaptive coder and its effects, and I would be happy to give a high score when the author does so.
