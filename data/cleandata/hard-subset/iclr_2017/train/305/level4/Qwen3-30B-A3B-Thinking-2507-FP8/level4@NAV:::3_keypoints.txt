- Well-written paper presenting an end-to-end trained image compression/decompression system that outperforms established algorithms (e.g., JPEG-2000) on bit-rate vs. quality.
- Introduces a differentiable rate function and effectively uses it to train across diverse rate–distortion trade-offs.
- Likely to influence applications beyond compression where differentiable approximations of similar functions are beneficial.
- Authors provided a thoughtful response to the reviewer’s pre-review query.
- Reviewer notes a sufficiently complex network could potentially generate codes within a fixed quantization range achieving maximum entropy (meeting the upper bound for minimal distortion).
- Counterpoint by authors is compelling: enforcing that code format would require a significantly more complex network versus using a separate variable-rate encoder to store q.
