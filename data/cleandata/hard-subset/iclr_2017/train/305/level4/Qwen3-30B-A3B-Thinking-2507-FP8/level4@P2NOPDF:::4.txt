

This paper presents a compelling end-to-end learning framework for image compression, achieving state-of-the-art rate-distortion performance through a novel relaxation technique that enables effective optimization over natural image datasets. The method elegantly addresses the challenges of joint optimization for compression efficiency and reconstruction quality, eliminating the need for hand-crafted components. The core innovation lies in the relaxation strategy, which facilitates tractable training while maintaining strong theoretical grounding for rate-distortion trade-offs.  

The experimental results are robust and significant, consistently outperforming prior learned and traditional compression methods across multiple benchmarks. The authors provide thorough ablation studies that rigorously validate the design choices, including the impact of the relaxation mechanism and network architecture. Additionally, the analysis offers valuable insights into the learned representations and their relationship to perceptual quality, strengthening the paper's technical contribution.  

The writing is clear and well-structured, with the methodology, results, and analysis presented cohesively. The work represents a meaningful advancement in the field, demonstrating both practical utility and theoretical soundness. Given the novelty of the approach, the strength of the empirical evidence, and the paper's overall quality, I strongly recommend acceptance.
