- Paper extends rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders/decoders and moves from basic entropy encoding to adaptive entropy coding, relating this to VAEs.  
- Novelty is limited since the core rate-distortion framework has been previously published; clarify any overlooked technical contributions.  
- The work regresses from prior studies by optimizing MSE rather than perceptual metrics, losing a key advancement of earlier work.  
- Results show substantial improvement over JPEG 2000 and claim unprecedented performance for a learned codec.  
- Paper is well-written.  
- Equation 10 appears incorrect: the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ), so the approach is not equivalent to a VAE under non-Euclidean metrics.  
- Why optimize MSE and report only PSNR instead of using perceptual metrics, given the authors’ expertise and prior work?  
- Clarify the separate contributions of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper network architectures; provide a comparison without adaptation.  
- Provide detailed analysis of the adaptive coder’s effects and its marginal gains; address these points to warrant a score revision.
