- Decision: Recommend Reject — promising but insufficient novelty and inconsistent metric choice (MSE vs perceptual).

- Builds directly on Ballé et al. (2016); novelty over prior work is unclear.

- Choosing MSE over a perceptual metric is surprising given authors' prior perceptual-focused work and limits contribution.

- Key claimed contribution is adaptive entropy coding for a learned model, but its specific benefit is not isolated.

- Lacks ablation studies to separate the gains from adaptive entropy coding versus deeper nonlinear transforms.

- Claim of “end-to-end optimized” is misleading because entropy coding uses standard CABAC rather than a fully learned coder.

- VAE relationship is interesting but not equivalent due to different optimization objectives; Equation 10 may be incorrect as written.

- Recommendation: provide ablation study isolating adaptive entropy coding effects.

- Recommendation: justify the choice of MSE over perceptual metrics with supporting evidence.

- Recommendation: explicitly state novelties and advances over Ballé et al. (2016).

- Recommendation: include detailed analysis of computational complexity and practical implementation, especially for entropy coding.

- Question: What would be the performance impact if entropy coding were fully learned end-to-end instead of using CABAC?
