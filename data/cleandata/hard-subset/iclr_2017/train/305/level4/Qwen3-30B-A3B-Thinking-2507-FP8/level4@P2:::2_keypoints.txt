- Decision: Accept.  
- Major contribution: end-to-end optimized image compression using biologically inspired nonlinear transforms with clear empirical gains.  
- Method: cascaded generalized divisive normalization (GDN) for analysis/synthesis, uniform quantizer, and adaptive entropy coding.  
- Optimization: joint rate–distortion training using a continuous relaxation of quantization.  
- Results: superior rate–distortion performance and substantially better visual quality across all bit rates.  
- Metrics: improvements supported by both PSNR and MS-SSIM.  
- Relation to prior work: connection to variational autoencoders is well-explained, with important distinctions (discrete implementation vs continuous VAEs).  
- Experimental evaluation: thorough comparisons to JPEG and JPEG 2000 across Kodak, standard test images, and personal photos with consistent results.  
- Practicality: provides a working implementation with adaptive entropy coding.  
- Writing and presentation: well-written, clearly explained methodology and experiments.  
- Requested clarification 1: justify optimizing for MSE rather than a perceptual metric and discuss expected effects of perceptual optimization.  
- Requested clarification 2: potential issue with Equation 10 — partition function dependence on the synthesis transform and implications for VAE equivalence with non-Euclidean metrics.  
- Requested clarification 3: more details on preliminary results with other activation functions, including which functions and parameter efficiency comparisons.  
- Requested clarification 4: nuanced analysis of where visual improvements are most pronounced (image types and bit-rate ranges).  
- Suggested improvement: clarify how the continuous relaxation used for training maps to the discrete deployment.  
- Suggested analysis: separate the contribution of adaptive entropy coding from that of the deeper network architecture to quantify their respective impacts.
