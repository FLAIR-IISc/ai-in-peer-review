Introduces an end-to-end optimized image compression framework using nonlinear transforms that outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 in rate–distortion.  
Jointly optimizes analysis transform, quantizer, and synthesis transform via a continuous relaxation of the rate, enabling gradient-descent training.  
Key innovation is a differentiable approximation of the rate function that lets the model be trained for any point on the rate–distortion curve by adjusting one trade-off parameter.  
Shows superior RD performance and substantially better visual quality (subjective and MS-SSIM) across bit rates for grayscale and RGB images.  
Draws a theoretical connection to variational autoencoders while acknowledging important differences.  
Experimental validation is thorough: multiple datasets and both PSNR and MS-SSIM metrics consistently favor the proposed method.  
Decision: Accept — the approach is well-motivated and addresses a longstanding challenge with a principled end-to-end framework.  
Needs a clearer, more structured comparison to VAEs (Section 3.1) to clarify novelty and differences.  
Authors increased network capacity for the largest λ values and RGB models but do not justify why those specific λ values required more capacity.  
Notes potential benefits of using perceptual metrics but does not explore this direction or discuss limitations and future work in depth.  
Raises concern that the relaxed (differential) entropy may be positively biased for coarse quantization; asks for quantification of this bias and its practical impact.  
Requests a sketch of the proof or reference for the claim about affine/invertible perceptual transforms and translation-invariant metrics relating to the density in equation (16).  
Asks for runtime/compression-time details (training vs encoding/decoding) and comparisons to standard methods, since encoding/decoding efficiency is emphasized.
