- End-to-end trained image compression system achieving superior rate–distortion performance versus JPEG-2000 and other baselines.
- Core contribution: a differentiable approximation of the rate function enabling effective optimization across rate–distortion trade-offs.
- Enables joint optimization of encoder, quantizer, and decoder by differentiating the rate term through a quantization approximation.
- Eliminates the need for separate, non-differentiable rate estimation steps, streamlining training and maintaining flexible rate–distortion control.
- Deliberately separates variable-rate entropy encoding from the core network to avoid forcing the network to replicate entropy coding computations.
- This separation is theoretically justified and practically efficient, letting the network focus on feature extraction and quantization.
- Strong empirical validation on standard benchmarks, with consistent improvements especially at low bitrates and without perceptual quality loss.
- Methodological novelty and differentiable rate modeling have broader applicability beyond image compression (e.g., neural communication, video coding, RL).
- Paper is clear and reproducible, with public code and ablation studies that isolate the impact of the differentiable rate function.
- Framework bridges deep learning and information theory, enabling new optimization strategies not feasible with conventional pipelines.
- Technical arguments are rigorous, results compelling, and reviewer recommends acceptance.
