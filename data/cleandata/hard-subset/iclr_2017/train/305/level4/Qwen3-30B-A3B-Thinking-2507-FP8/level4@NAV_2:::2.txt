

This paper presents an end-to-end optimized image compression method using nonlinear transforms, uniform quantization, and adaptive entropy coding. It extends previous work on rate-distortion optimization for deep networks and explores connections to variational autoencoders. The authors demonstrate significant improvements in both rate-distortion performance and visual quality compared to standard methods like JPEG and JPEG 2000 across multiple test images.

Accept. The paper makes a strong contribution with visible improvements over established compression methods, and the results are well-supported by both objective metrics and visual examples.

The approach delivers a noticeable improvement in visual quality across all bit rates, which is supported by objective quality measures like MS-SSIM. The method outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 in rate-distortion performance for most test images, particularly at lower bit rates. The paper is well-structured and provides sufficient technical details for reproducibility.

To further strengthen the paper, please clarify the relationship between the approach and variational autoencoders, particularly regarding the assumptions about the distortion metric. The discussion about optimizing for MSE rather than a perceptual metric would benefit from more context, as this choice seems to contrast with the authors' previous work.

Equation 10 appears to be incorrect as written; the partition function should depend on g_s(y; theta). Could you clarify this?
Why was MSE optimized instead of a perceptual metric, as in your previous work? This choice seems to contradict the approach taken in your prior publications.
How much of the performance gain is attributable to adaptive entropy coding versus the deeper network architecture? It would be valuable to see results without adaptive coding to isolate these effects.
