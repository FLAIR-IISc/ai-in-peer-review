

This paper presents a compelling end-to-end learning approach for image compression, effectively optimizing rate-distortion performance through a novel relaxation technique. The method learns efficient compression representations directly from large datasets of natural images, avoiding traditional hand-crafted components. The authors demonstrate strong empirical results across standard benchmarks, with clear ablation studies that validate the contribution of each design choice. The analysis thoroughly examines trade-offs between compression efficiency and reconstruction quality, providing valuable insights into the learned representations. The technical execution is sound, and the paper is well-structured with precise experimental details. Given the significance of the problem, the methodological innovation, and the thorough evaluation, I strongly recommend acceptance.
