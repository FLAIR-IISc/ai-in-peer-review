

This paper extends rate-distortion optimization (RDO) to deep encoders/decoders and advances from basic entropy encoding to adaptive entropy coding, while briefly connecting the method to variational autoencoders (VAEs). However, the core RDO framework has been previously established, limiting the novelty claim. The work regresses from prior efforts that optimized for perceptual quality by adopting MSE as the distortion metric—a choice that appears inconsistent with the authors' own prior work on perceptual metrics. The evaluation remains restricted to PSNR, which is surprising given this background and the field's growing emphasis on perceptual quality.  

The results demonstrate a significant improvement over JPEG 2000, and to our knowledge, no prior learned codec achieves comparable performance. The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly structured.  

Key concerns require clarification:  
1. **Equation 10** appears incorrect. The partition function should depend on $g_s(y; \theta)$ (the entropy model's output), not solely on the latent $y$. This error undermines the claimed equivalence to VAEs for non-Euclidean distortion metrics (e.g., MSE), as the derivation assumes a fixed entropy model. The authors should correct this and clarify the conditions under which VAE equivalence holds.  
2. **MSE vs. perceptual optimization**: The rationale for abandoning perceptual metrics (used in the authors' prior work) is unexplained. Given the field's shift toward perceptual quality, this choice merits justification.  
3. **Contribution of adaptive entropy coding**: The paper does not isolate the impact of adaptive coding from deeper network architectures. An ablation study comparing adaptive vs. non-adaptive entropy coding (as in the authors' earlier work) is essential to attribute performance gains accurately. Without this, it remains unclear whether the improvement stems from the coding scheme, network depth, or both.  

The authors should address these points to strengthen the contribution. Specifically:  
- Correct Equation 10 and revise the VAE discussion.  
- Provide a clear justification for MSE and include perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS, MS-SSIM) in evaluation.  
- Add ablation results demonstrating the specific gain from adaptive entropy coding.  

These revisions would clarify the technical contribution and align the work with broader trends in learned image compression. As currently presented, the novelty is incremental, and the methodological choices lack sufficient justification. However, the empirical results are compelling, and the writing is excellent. Addressing the above points would warrant a substantially higher score.
