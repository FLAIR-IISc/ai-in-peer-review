

This paper presents a compelling end-to-end trained image compression and decompression system that achieves superior bit-rate vs. quality trade-offs compared to established algorithms like JPEG-2000. Beyond demonstrating deep learning's efficacy for this new application, a key contribution is the introduction of a differentiable rate function, which the authors show enables effective training with customizable rate-distortion trade-offs. I anticipate this work will have broader impact beyond image compression, benefiting other tasks requiring differentiable approximations to similar functions.

The authors provided a thoughtful response to my pre-review query. I maintain that, to minimize distortion under a fixed quantization range, a sufficiently complex network would automatically produce codes within that range with maximum entropy (i.e., achieving the upper bound). However, the authors' second argument is convincing: enforcing this approach forces a specific "form" on how the compressor output is used, and to match the effective compression of the current system—which relies on a separate variable rate encoder for storing q—would require a more complex network capable of performing computations currently handled by that external encoder.
