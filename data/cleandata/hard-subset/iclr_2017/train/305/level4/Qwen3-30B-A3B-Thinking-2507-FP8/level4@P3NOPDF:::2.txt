

This paper extends rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders/decoders and advances from simple to adaptive entropy coding, while discussing connections to variational autoencoders (VAEs). However, the novelty is limited as the core optimization approach has been previously published. The work regresses to MSE-based optimization (rather than perceptual metrics used in prior work), which weakens its alignment with recent progress in learned image compression. While the results represent a significant improvement over JPEG 2000—among the first learned encoders to achieve this level of performance—the evaluation solely on PSNR is unexpected given the authors' background and the field's shift toward perceptual metrics.  

The technical contribution requires clarification. Equation (10) appears incorrect: the partition function should explicitly depend on the synthesis transform $g_s(y; \theta)$, meaning the approach does not reduce to a VAE under non-Euclidean metrics (e.g., perceptual losses). This undermines the VAE relationship claim.  

Crucially, the paper fails to isolate the contribution of adaptive entropy coding from architectural improvements. The authors should provide ablation studies comparing the full pipeline (deep encoders + adaptive coding) against a baseline using the same deep architecture but without adaptive entropy coding (as in prior work). Without this, it remains unclear whether the performance gains stem from deeper networks, adaptive coding, or both. The description of the adaptive coder’s mechanism and its specific impact on rate-distortion trade-offs is also insufficient.  

The paper is well-written and addresses a meaningful problem, but these gaps prevent a strong recommendation. To strengthen the submission, the authors must:  
1. Correct Equation (10) and clarify the VAE relationship under non-Euclidean metrics.  
2. Justify the choice of MSE over perceptual metrics (or include perceptual evaluations).  
3. Provide ablation results demonstrating adaptive entropy coding’s specific contribution.  

Addressing these points would substantially improve the paper’s technical rigor and contribution clarity. As currently written, the novelty is modest, and the focus on MSE limits the work’s relevance to state-of-the-art compression research. The results are promising but lack the depth required for a high-impact publication at ICLR.
