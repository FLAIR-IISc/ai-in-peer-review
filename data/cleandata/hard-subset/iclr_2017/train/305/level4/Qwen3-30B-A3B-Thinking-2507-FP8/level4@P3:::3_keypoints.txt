- End-to-end optimized image compression jointly trains analysis/synthesis transforms and a quantizer to minimize rate–distortion.
- Introduces a continuous relaxation of quantization that enables gradient-based optimization of the full compression pipeline.
- Employs cascaded nonlinear transforms with generalized divisive normalization (GDN) followed by uniform scalar quantization.
- Shows superior rate–distortion performance and markedly better visual quality than JPEG and JPEG 2000 across multiple images and bitrates.
- Clarifies the relationship to variational autoencoders, highlighting differences in optimization objectives and implementation.
- Decision: Accept — significant contribution with a novel, effective end-to-end trainable framework.
- Supporting argument: The continuous relaxation is theoretically sound, practically effective, and broadly applicable to optimizing discrete representations.
- Supporting argument: Empirical results demonstrate significant PSNR and MS-SSIM gains and use a full entropy-coded pipeline for fair comparison.
- Supporting argument: Provides valuable conceptual clarification of compression vs. generative modeling (VAEs).
- Feedback: Include detailed computational complexity and encoding/decoding time benchmarks versus standard codecs.
- Feedback: More systematic evaluation of GDN versus other nonlinearities to link GDN to observed visual improvements.
- Feedback: Discuss practical limitations such as training data requirements and scalability to higher resolutions.
- Question: Report how many images showed consistent improvements across all bit rates and identify the exceptions.
- Question: How would optimizing directly for a perceptual metric like MS-SSIM (instead of MSE) change performance?
- Question: Detail the specific impact of the additive-noise continuous relaxation on final compression performance and differences between relaxed loss and actual rate–distortion.
