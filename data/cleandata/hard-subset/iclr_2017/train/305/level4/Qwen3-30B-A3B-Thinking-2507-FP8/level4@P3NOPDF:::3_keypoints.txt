- End-to-end trainable image compression system achieving superior rate-distortion performance versus JPEG-2000.  
- Core contribution: a differentiable approximation of the rate function that enables effective, customizable rate–distortion training.  
- Technique is broadly applicable beyond image compression to other rate-constrained tasks (e.g., neural network compression, communication systems).  
- Experimental results are strong: clear comparisons to baselines, consistent RD improvements across datasets and metrics, especially at low bitrates.  
- Ablation studies effectively isolate the impact of the rate approximation and quantization strategy.  
- Visualizations of reconstructions support the quantitative perceptual-quality gains.  
- Authors’ response justifies the fixed-range quantization constraint: it aligns model outputs with a separate entropy coder and avoids requiring the model to learn the entire compression pipeline.  
- Technical execution is robust with clear description of how the rate approximation is integrated into training.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: briefly state in the main text how the differentiable rate handles discrete quantization (e.g., straight-through estimators).  
- Minor practical suggestion: report computational overhead of the proposed method versus baselines.  
- Overall judgment: novel, well-implemented, convincing results; recommend acceptance.
