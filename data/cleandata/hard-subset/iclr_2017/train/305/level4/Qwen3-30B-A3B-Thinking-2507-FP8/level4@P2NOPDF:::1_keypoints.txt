- Presents a compelling DNN-based image compression method with an elegant integration of rate-distortion theory into the loss.
- Uses JPEG2000 as the primary baseline, a stronger and more relevant benchmark than prior JPEG-only comparisons.
- Writing is clear and well-structured, and technical contributions are presented precisely.
- Work has substantial potential for significant impact in the field.
- Lacks quantitative results on standard test images (Lena, Barbara, Baboon); these should be included.
- Needs explicit positioning and comparison against the strongest classical methods beyond JPEG2000 (e.g., J. Chen, M. K. Mihcak).
- Unclear whether the neural approach consistently outperforms or only matches top non-NN techniques across key metrics (rate-distortion curves, PSNR, MS-SSIM at specific bitrates).
- Recommend adding a concise table or figure directly comparing the proposed method to top classical methods.
- Including those comparisons and standard test images would decisively clarify the method’s standing and make the paper definitive.
