- Strong, well-executed advancement in neural image compression and the most convincing work in the field to date.  
- Successfully integrates rate-distortion theory into the objective, yielding a coherent, theoretically grounded framework.  
- Writing is clear, logical, and effectively communicates technical contributions.  
- Using JPEG2000 as a baseline is a meaningful and rigorous experimental choice (better than comparing only to JPEG).  
- Recommend adding quantitative results on standard test images (Lena, Barbara, Baboon) for community comparability.  
- Recommend explicitly positioning the neural method against the absolute best classical methods (with a dedicated table/discussion of PSNR/MS-SSIM at matched bitrates).  
- Core contributions are robust and well-supported by experiments.  
- Ablation studies convincingly show the impact of design choices (entropy model, architecture).  
- Demonstrates consistent improvements over prior neural methods (e.g., Ballé et al., 2016).  
- Outperforms JPEG2000 across metrics and bitrates, especially at low bitrates important for visual quality.  
- Technical novelty: elegant fusion of information theory with deep learning, avoiding pixel-reconstruction-only framing.  
- Framework is generalizable and provides a strong foundation for future work.  
- Overall assessment: outstanding paper that merits acceptance; requested clarifications would elevate it to a definitive reference.
