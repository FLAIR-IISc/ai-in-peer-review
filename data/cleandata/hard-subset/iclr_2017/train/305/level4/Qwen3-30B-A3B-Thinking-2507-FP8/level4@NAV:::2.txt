

This paper extends rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders and decoders, transitioning from basic entropy encoding to adaptive entropy coding. It also explores the relationship between this approach and variational autoencoders.  
While the core rate-distortion optimization method has been previously published, the novelty of this submission is arguably limited (please correct me if I have overlooked a novel element). In certain aspects, the work appears regressive, as earlier studies optimized for perceptual metrics whereas this paper employs MSE. Nevertheless, the results demonstrate a clear improvement over JPEG 2000, and to my knowledge, no other learned compression method has achieved comparable performance. The paper is exceptionally well written.  
Equation 10 appears incorrect; the partition function should depend on g_s(y; theta), implying the approach is not equivalent to a VAE for non-Euclidean metrics.  
Why was MSE optimized instead of a perceptual metric, as in prior work? Given the authors' expertise, it is surprising that evaluation was restricted to PSNR.  
What is the specific contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus the impact of deeper encoders/decoders? This is critical; the authors should provide performance results without adaptation (as in their prior work) and elaborate on the adaptive coder's effects.
