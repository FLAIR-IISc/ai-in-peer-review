- Paper presents a well-motivated end-to-end image compression system that achieves superior bitrate-vs-quality trade-offs compared to JPEG-2000.  
- Key contribution: a differentiable approximation to the rate function enabling effective training across diverse rate–distortion trade-offs.  
- This differentiable-rate innovation is likely to have impact beyond image compression.  
- Authors provided a thoughtful response to the reviewer’s pre-review query.  
- Reviewer notes theoretically a sufficiently complex network under a fixed quantization range could learn codes with maximum entropy (reaching the upper bound).  
- Authors’ rebuttal is compelling: enforcing a fixed-range constraint would require a specific structural form and far greater network complexity to match the current system’s efficiency, which relies on a separate variable-rate encoder for quantized values.
