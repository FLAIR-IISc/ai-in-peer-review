- Extends rate–distortion optimization to deep encoders/decoders and introduces adaptive entropy coding, relating the approach to VAEs.  
- Novelty is limited because the core optimization approach has been published previously.  
- Regresses to MSE-based optimization instead of perceptual metrics, weakening alignment with recent learned-compression work.  
- Shows significant improvement over JPEG 2000 but evaluation is limited to PSNR only.  
- Equation (10) appears incorrect: the partition function should depend on the synthesis transform g_s(y; θ).  
- The incorrect equation undermines the claimed reduction to a VAE under non-Euclidean (perceptual) metrics.  
- Fails to isolate adaptive entropy coding’s contribution from architectural/depth improvements.  
- Lacks ablation studies comparing the full pipeline to the same deep architecture without adaptive entropy coding.  
- Description of the adaptive coder’s mechanism and its specific impact on rate–distortion trade-offs is insufficient.  
- Paper is well-written and addresses a meaningful problem, but gaps prevent a strong recommendation.  
- Required fixes: correct Eq. (10) and clarify the VAE relationship under non-Euclidean metrics.  
- Required fixes: justify MSE choice or include perceptual evaluations.  
- Required fixes: provide ablation results demonstrating adaptive entropy coding’s specific contribution.  
- Conclusion: novelty is modest and focus on MSE limits relevance; results are promising but lack depth for a high-impact ICLR paper.
