- Proposes an end-to-end image compression framework with joint optimization of analysis/synthesis transforms, quantization, and synthesis via SGD.  
- Introduces a continuous relaxation of quantization enabling gradient-based training of the entire compression pipeline.  
- Uses generalized divisive normalization (GDN/IGDN), inspired by biology, to produce more effective nonlinear representations than linear transforms.  
- Demonstrates superior rate–distortion performance compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000 across bit rates, with notable MS-SSIM visual quality gains.  
- Implements a full compression system with actual entropy coding rather than relying solely on entropy estimates.  
- Provides a clear theoretical connection to variational autoencoders while highlighting differences relevant to compression.  
- Decision: Accept — primary reasons are the differentiable rate approximation enabling end-to-end training and significant PSNR/MS-SSIM improvements.  
- Main technical contribution: a technically sophisticated continuous quantization relaxation with thorough theoretical justification.  
- Empirical contribution: extensive experiments and visuals (e.g., Fig. 5) showing contour/edge preservation and reduced artifacts.  
- Suggestion: include more detailed analysis of network architecture choices and why GDN/IGDN outperforms simpler nonlinearities.  
- Suggestion: explore using perceptual metrics during training to further improve visual quality.  
- Suggestion: discuss computational complexity and inference-time cost relative to standard compression algorithms.  
- Clarifying question: provide ablation studies comparing network depth/width with different nonlinearities to support the GDN efficiency claim.  
- Clarifying question: elaborate on "preliminary results" that other activations can match quality only with larger models.  
- Clarifying question: discuss how the framework could be extended to video or 3D point-cloud compression.  
- Overall verdict: substantial, well-written, technically sound contribution with potential impact beyond image compression.
