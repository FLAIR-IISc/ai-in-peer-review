- Extends rate-distortion optimization to deep encoder/decoder architectures and advances from basic to adaptive entropy coding, with a brief VAE connection.  
- Novelty is limited because the core RDO framework is already established.  
- Authors revert to MSE distortion, abandoning prior perceptual optimization work without explanation.  
- Evaluation is restricted to PSNR and omits perceptual metrics despite field trends.  
- Results show significant improvement over JPEG 2000 and appear unmatched by prior learned codecs.  
- The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly structured.  
- Equation 10 seems incorrect: the partition function should depend on the entropy model output g_s(y; θ), not only on y; this undermines the claimed VAE equivalence and needs correction and clarification.  
- The rationale for choosing MSE over perceptual metrics must be justified, and perceptual evaluations (e.g., LPIPS, MS-SSIM) should be included.  
- The specific contribution of adaptive entropy coding is unclear without an ablation separating its effect from deeper network architectures.  
- Required revisions: correct Eq.10 and revise the VAE discussion; justify MSE and add perceptual metrics; provide ablation comparing adaptive vs non-adaptive entropy coding.  
- Overall verdict: methodological choices and novelty are incremental and need stronger justification, but empirical results are compelling and could merit a higher score if concerns are addressed.
