- End-to-end nonlinear-transform image compression optimized for rate-distortion using SGD with a continuous relaxation of quantization.
- Joint optimization of analysis/synthesis transforms and quantizer enables gradient-based training for compression.
- Key innovation: generalized divisive normalization (GDN) inspired by biological vision yields better image-statistics representation than linear transforms.
- Demonstrates significant improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000 on PSNR and MS-SSIM, with visually superior results at all bit rates.
- Decision: Accept.
- Substantial contribution: shows end-to-end optimized nonlinear transforms can achieve superior rate-distortion and visual quality.
- Strong empirical evidence through comprehensive comparisons against established baselines and visual examples.
- Theoretical link between rate-distortion optimization and variational inference is well-motivated and clearly presented.
- Addresses core review questions: clear problem definition, well-motivated approach via literature and biology, and thorough theoretical/empirical support.
- Practical implementation includes a working entropy coder and evaluation using actual bit rates, not just entropy estimates.
- Visual quality improvements notably avoid blocking and ringing artifacts common in linear transform coding.
- Recommendation to compare against more recent state-of-the-art methods (e.g., Toderici, Gregor) for better context.
- Suggest clarifying distinctions from variational autoencoders and prior neural compression methods to better position the contribution.
- Question: how will the computational cost of training scale for large-scale deployment with limited resources?
- Question: does the GDN/IGDN efficiency advantage hold across the full end-to-end pipeline, including entropy coding?
- Question: can a unified color model be developed instead of training separate models for color and grayscale to improve performance and reduce complexity?
