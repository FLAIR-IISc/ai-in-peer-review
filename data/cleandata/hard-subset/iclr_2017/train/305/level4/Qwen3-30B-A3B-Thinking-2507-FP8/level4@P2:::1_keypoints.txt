- Presents an end-to-end optimized image compression method using nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms, uniform quantization, and a continuous relaxation of quantization for SGD-based joint optimization.  
- Model uses three stages of convolutional linear filters with nonlinear activations, inspired by biological neuron models (GDN/IGDN).  
- Continuous relaxation of quantization is a key technical contribution enabling gradient-based optimization of rate-distortion.  
- Demonstrates superior rate-distortion performance and improved visual quality versus JPEG and JPEG 2000, supported by MS-SSIM.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions due to strong theoretical grounding and compelling empirical results.  
- Technical significance: successfully optimizes the full compression pipeline end-to-end, addressing a longstanding challenge.  
- Use of GDN/IGDN is well-justified by prior work on visual-system modeling and Gaussianization of image statistics.  
- Paper should better position itself against the absolute state-of-the-art in classical compression methods (e.g., BPG, WebP).  
- Authors should include results on standard test images (Lena, Barbara, Baboon) and compare to best classical methods, not just JPEG/JPEG 2000.  
- Need clearer discussion and concrete comparisons of computational cost trade-offs: training vs inference and scaling with image resolution.  
- Clarify distinctions between this compression approach and variational autoencoders/generative modeling, emphasizing discrete-domain implications for implementation.  
- Suggested revisions: add comparisons to top classical methods, include standard test images, discuss computational trade-offs, and clarify relation to best classical approaches in intro/discussion.  
- Reviewer questions to address: (1) performance vs best classical methods in rate-distortion and visual quality, (2) detailed computational costs of training and inference vs traditional methods, (3) main limitations preventing real-world deployment.
