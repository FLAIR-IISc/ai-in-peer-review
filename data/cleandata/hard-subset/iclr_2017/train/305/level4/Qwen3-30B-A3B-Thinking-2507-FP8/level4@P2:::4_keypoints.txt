1. Presents an end-to-end optimized image compression framework that jointly trains nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms and quantization.  
2. Uses generalized divisive normalization (GDN) transforms inspired by biological visual systems.  
3. Introduces a continuous relaxation of quantization via additive uniform noise to enable gradient-based optimization.  
4. Provides theoretical and empirical justification that the relaxation approximates rate (differential entropy) and distortion with minimal bias.  
5. Achieves state-of-the-art rate–distortion performance versus JPEG and JPEG 2000 across test sets.  
6. Delivers dramatic visual quality improvements at all bit rates, supported by PSNR and perceptual MS-SSIM metrics.  
7. Implements a complete compression pipeline (including entropy coding) and reports actual bit rates for fair comparison.  
8. Decision: Accept — novelty in relaxation and strong empirical validation are the main merits.  
9. Avoids common pitfalls of generative-model-based compression by addressing discrete-domain optimization properly.  
10. GDN is argued to be more efficient than ReLU, Gaussianizing statistics and requiring fewer parameters.  
11. Results are consistent across diverse datasets (Kodak, standard images, personal photos) with statistically significant gains.  
12. Suggests clarifying computational cost/training burden and discussing deployment/inference feasibility.  
13. Recommends expanding the explanation linking GDN’s biological motivation (local gain control) to observed compression gains.  
14. Advises addressing RGB model limitations and comparing separate-channel transforms to joint color-space approaches (e.g., YCbCr).  
15. Asks for a parameter-count comparison between GDN and ReLU variants for equivalent bit-rate performance.  
16. Requests clarification on why gains are modest at high bit rates — fixed quantization bin size vs transform architecture limits.  
17. Queries how much of the rate–distortion gap versus JPEG 2000 is due to the simpler CABAC entropy coder lacking spatial adaptation.  
18. Overall: significant contribution demonstrating that end-to-end optimization with continuous relaxation can outperform engineered transform coding.
