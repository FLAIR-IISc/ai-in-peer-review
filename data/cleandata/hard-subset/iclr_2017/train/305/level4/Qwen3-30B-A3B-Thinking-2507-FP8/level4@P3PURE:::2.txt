

This paper extends rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders and decoders, transitioning from a basic entropy encoding scheme to adaptive entropy coding, and discusses the relationship to variational autoencoders (VAEs). While the core rate-distortion optimization approach has been previously published, the novelty of this submission appears limited (please clarify if I have overlooked a key innovation). Notably, the work regresses from prior efforts that optimized for perceptual metrics, instead employing MSE as the distortion measure. Despite this, the results demonstrate a significant improvement over JPEG 2000, and to our knowledge, no other learned image codec has achieved comparable performance. The paper is well-written.  

Equation 10 appears incorrect; the partition function should depend on $g_s(y; \theta)$, which would invalidate the claim of equivalence to a VAE under non-Euclidean metrics. The rationale for optimizing MSE rather than a perceptual metric (as in prior work) is unclear, particularly given the authors' background. It is notable that evaluation was restricted to PSNR without perceptual metrics.  

Crucially, the contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper network architectures requires clarification. The authors should provide performance comparisons without adaptive coding (as in prior work) to isolate its impact. A more detailed analysis of the adaptive coder's contribution is essential; a higher score will be considered upon such clarification.
