- End-to-end image compression approach that directly optimizes rate-distortion via a novel differentiable relaxation of discrete compression operations.  
- Reformulates compression as a continuous optimization problem, enabling backpropagation through approximated quantization and entropy coding.  
- Learns a joint representation that balances perceptual quality and bitrate without hand-engineered components.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on benchmarks (CLIC, KODAK) while maintaining competitive computational efficiency at inference.  
- Relaxation mechanism is well-motivated and elegantly bridges discrete operations with differentiable optimization.  
- Thorough ablation studies demonstrate the relaxation’s necessity and superiority over naive approximations or discrete optimization.  
- Robust experimental comparisons against classical (JPEG, JPEG2000) and recent neural methods show superior rate-distortion curves.  
- Analysis of learned representations and quantization behavior gives valuable insights into adaptation to perceptual constraints.  
- Practical relevance: designed for end-to-end training/deployment, avoids complex post-processing, with clear and reproducible presentation.  
- Minor limitation: lacks explicit discussion of training computational overhead compared to simpler baselines.  
- Overall recommendation: significant, well-executed contribution with strong acceptance endorsement.
