

This paper extends rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders/decoders and advances from simple to adaptive entropy coding, while discussing connections to variational autoencoders. However, the novelty appears limited since the core rate-distortion approach is already established in prior work (please clarify if a key innovation was overlooked). The work regresses to using MSE as the distortion metric—a step back from earlier efforts that optimized for perceptual quality. Despite this, the results represent a clear improvement over JPEG 2000, and to the best of my knowledge, no prior learned codec achieves this performance level. The paper is well-written, but several critical gaps require addressing.

Equation 10 appears incorrect: the partition function should depend on $g_s(y; \theta)$, not $g_s(y)$, as $g_s$ is parameterized by $\theta$. This implies the approach does not reduce to a VAE under non-Euclidean metrics, contradicting the paper’s claim. Please clarify or correct this.

The choice to optimize MSE instead of a perceptual metric—despite the authors’ background in perceptual compression—is surprising. Given the field’s focus on perceptual quality, it’s unclear why evaluation is limited to PSNR. Please justify this design decision and provide perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS, VMAF) to validate real-world utility.

Crucially, the contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper networks remains ambiguous. The paper does not isolate the effect of adaptive coding (e.g., by comparing against the non-adaptive baseline in prior work). Without this ablation, it’s impossible to assess whether adaptive coding drives the performance gains or if deeper architectures alone suffice. Detailed experiments comparing adaptive vs. non-adaptive entropy coding, alongside ablations of network depth, are essential to quantify each component’s impact. This omission is a critical weakness.

To strengthen the submission, the authors should: (1) correct Equation 10 and clarify the VAE relationship; (2) justify the MSE choice and include perceptual metrics; (3) provide ablation studies isolating adaptive coding’s contribution. Addressing these points would significantly elevate the work’s technical rigor and contribution. I will reconsider my score upon receiving these revisions.
