- Introduces an end-to-end optimized image compression framework using cascaded convolutional transforms with generalized divisive normalization (GDN).  
- Replaces discontinuous quantization with a continuous relaxation (additive uniform noise) to enable gradient-based training.  
- Continuous relaxation is justified as a valid proxy for the discrete rate-distortion problem and empirically approximates actual bit rates (Fig. 4).  
- Achieves superior rate-distortion performance over JPEG and JPEG 2000 on standard benchmarks.  
- Demonstrates dramatically improved visual quality at all bit rates, validated by subjective assessment and MS-SSIM metrics.  
- Bridges biological visual modeling (GDN) with practical compression, showing nonlinear learned transforms outperform traditional linear transform coding.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: The continuous relaxation is elegantly engineered, scientifically rigorous, and solves a core training obstacle.  
- Key reason 2: Visual quality improvements are substantial, consistent across images and bit rates, and exceed typical R-D gains.  
- Positions work clearly against prior transform coding and generative models, emphasizing actual bit-rate reporting for fair comparison.  
- Shows GDN efficiently Gaussianizes image statistics, enabling better compression (extends Ballé et al. 2015).  
- Training/testing protocol is thorough (ImageNet training, Kodak testing, PSNR and MS-SSIM comparisons).  
- Empirical results: preserves contours/edges at low rates while JPEG/JPEG 2000 show blocking/ringing artifacts (Fig. 5–7).  
- Notes that MSE-optimized model aligns with perceptual quality as evidenced by universal MS-SSIM improvements.  
- Suggests clarifying computational cost of training vs inference (training time, GPUs, encoding/decoding complexity).  
- Recommends discussing limitations of the entropy coder (basic CABAC implementation) and potential gains from more advanced context-adaptive coding.  
- Suggests adding commentary on scalability to video or real-time applications given inference efficiency.  
- Requests from authors: quantify bit-rate/quality gains of GDN vs ReLU for fixed model size.  
- Requests: clarify behavior at boundary λ→0 (extremely low bit rate) and whether artifacts remain coherent.  
- Requests: estimate impact of spatially adaptive coding on final bit rate and how much gap it could close.
