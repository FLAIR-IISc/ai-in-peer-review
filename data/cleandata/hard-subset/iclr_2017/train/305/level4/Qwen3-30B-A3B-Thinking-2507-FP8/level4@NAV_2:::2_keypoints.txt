- Presents an end-to-end optimized image compression method using nonlinear transforms, uniform quantization, and adaptive entropy coding, extending prior rate–distortion deep network work and linking to variational autoencoders.
- Demonstrates significant improvements in rate–distortion performance and visual quality over JPEG and JPEG 2000 across multiple test images and bitrates.
- Strong contribution with results well-supported by objective metrics (e.g., MS-SSIM) and visual examples; paper is well-structured and reproducible.
- Delivers noticeable visual-quality improvements across all bit rates, particularly at low bit rates.
- Needs clarification on the relationship to variational autoencoders, especially the assumptions about the distortion metric.
- The choice to optimize MSE instead of a perceptual metric (contrary to the authors’ prior work) requires more justification and context.
- Equation 10 appears incorrect: the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ); please clarify and correct.
- Request ablation to isolate contributions: quantify how much gain comes from adaptive entropy coding versus deeper network architecture.
- Suggest providing results without adaptive coding (and other ablations) to better attribute performance improvements.
