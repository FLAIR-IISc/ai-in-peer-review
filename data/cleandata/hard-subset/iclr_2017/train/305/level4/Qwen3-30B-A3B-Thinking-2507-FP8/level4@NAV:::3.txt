

This is a well-written paper presenting an end-to-end trained image compression and decompression system that achieves superior bit-rate vs. quality trade-offs compared to established algorithms (e.g., JPEG-2000). Beyond demonstrating deep learning's efficacy for image compression, a key contribution is the introduction of a differentiable rate function, which the authors effectively leverage for training across diverse rate-distortion trade-offs. This work is likely to influence applications beyond compression, particularly where differentiable approximations of similar functions would be beneficial.  

The authors provided a thoughtful response to my pre-review query. While I maintain that a sufficiently complex network could inherently generate codes within a fixed quantization range achieving maximum entropy (i.e., meeting the upper bound for minimal distortion), the second point they raise is compelling: enforcing this specific code format would necessitate a significantly more complex network to replicate the functionality currently handled by the separate variable rate encoder used to store q.
