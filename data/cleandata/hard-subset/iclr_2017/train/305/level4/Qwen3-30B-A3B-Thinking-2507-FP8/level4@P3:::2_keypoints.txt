- Proposes an end-to-end image compression framework jointly optimizing analysis/synthesis transforms, quantizer, and entropy code.  
- Uses a biologically-inspired nonlinear transform (Generalized Divisive Normalization) and a continuous relaxation of quantization to enable gradient-based training.  
- Demonstrates superior rate-distortion performance and substantially improved visual quality versus JPEG and JPEG 2000, with actual bit-rate measurements.  
- Decision: Accept — compelling contribution with strong empirical results and rigorous evaluation.  
- Key technical contribution: effective continuous relaxation of quantization that enables end-to-end neural compression training.  
- Empirical strengths: better PSNR and MS-SSIM across bit rates, thorough comparisons on standard datasets, and clear positioning in prior literature.  
- Clarifies distinction from VAEs and builds on prior nonlinear transform work (Ballé et al., 2015).  
- Notable that MSE-optimized models still yield strong perceptual quality, suggesting the transform captures useful visual features.  
- Recommendation: clarify the relationship between the continuous relaxation and the actual discrete compression objective, with quantitative comparison.  
- Recommendation: provide more detail on the adaptive CABAC entropy coder and isolate its contribution (e.g., compare with a fixed coder).  
- Recommendation: expand discussion of optimizing MSE vs perceptual metrics and analyze which visual artifacts the nonlinear transform addresses.  
- Question: clarify the claim that the relaxed loss is a generative log-likelihood — equation 10’s partition function appears to depend on the synthesis transform; does VAE equivalence hold for non-Euclidean metrics?  
- Question: why optimize for MSE rather than a perceptual metric here, and would perceptual optimization improve visual quality further?  
- Question: provide qualitative analysis of specific image types or features where the method outperforms JPEG 2000.  
- Question: elaborate on the broader implication that additive-noise relaxation might improve robustness of supervised classification networks.  
- Overall: significant and well-executed contribution to learned image compression that would benefit from minor clarifications.
