- Paper presents an end-to-end trained image compression/decompression system that achieves superior bit-rate vs. quality trade-offs compared to JPEG-2000.
- Main contribution is a differentiable rate function that enables effective training and customizable rate–distortion trade-offs.
- Work likely has broader impact beyond image compression for tasks needing differentiable approximations of similar functions.
- Authors provided a thoughtful response to the reviewer’s pre-review query.
- Reviewer notes that a sufficiently complex network could theoretically produce max-entropy codes within a fixed quantization range, but accepts the authors’ argument that forcing that form would demand a more complex network to replicate the external variable-rate encoder’s computations.
