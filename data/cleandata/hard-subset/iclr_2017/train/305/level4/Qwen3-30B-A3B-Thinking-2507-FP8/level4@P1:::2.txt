

# Review for "END-TO-END OPTIMIZED IMAGE COMPRESSION"

## 1. Summary of Contributions

This paper presents a novel image compression framework that jointly optimizes analysis and synthesis transforms, quantization, and entropy coding for rate-distortion performance. The authors introduce a nonlinear transform architecture inspired by biological visual systems, using cascaded convolutional layers with generalized divisive normalization (GDN) as the key nonlinearity. Unlike prior work, they optimize the entire model end-to-end for rate-distortion performance using a continuous relaxation of the quantization step, enabling stochastic gradient descent optimization. The method demonstrates superior rate-distortion performance compared to standard JPEG and JPEG 2000, with particularly notable improvements in visual quality across all bit rates as confirmed by both subjective evaluation and objective MS-SSIM metrics. The paper also provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the relationship between their approach and variational autoencoders, highlighting key differences in their optimization objectives and constraints.

## 2. Decision

I recommend **Reject** this paper. While the results are promising and the technical approach is sound, the paper lacks sufficient novelty compared to prior work, and the choice to optimize for MSE rather than a perceptual metric appears inconsistent with the authors' own previous work.

## 3. Supporting Arguments

The paper's primary contribution is an end-to-end optimization framework for image compression using nonlinear transforms. However, this framework builds directly on the authors' prior work (Ballé et al., 2016), where they demonstrated superior visual quality by optimizing for a perceptual distortion metric rather than MSE. The current paper's decision to optimize for MSE rather than a perceptual metric is surprising given their demonstrated expertise in perceptual quality metrics, and this choice limits the paper's contribution.

The paper's most significant technical contribution is the application of adaptive entropy coding to a learned compression model. However, the paper does not provide sufficient ablation studies to determine the specific contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus the benefits of the deeper nonlinear transforms. Without comparisons showing performance differences when using non-adaptive entropy coding, it's impossible to determine how much of the observed improvement stems from the entropy coding versus the transform architecture itself.

Furthermore, the paper's claim of being "end-to-end optimized" is somewhat misleading. The authors optimize the transforms and quantization parameters, but they still rely on a standard entropy coding approach (CABAC) rather than fully end-to-end learning of the coding process. The paper's relationship to variational autoencoders is interesting, but the authors themselves note key differences in the optimization objectives that prevent the approach from being equivalent to VAEs.

## 4. Additional Feedback

To strengthen the paper, I recommend the following improvements:

1. Provide a clear ablation study showing the contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus the nonlinear transforms. This is essential to properly attribute the observed performance improvements.

2. Justify the choice of MSE over a perceptual metric more thoroughly, especially given the authors' previous work on perceptual image quality. If they believe MSE optimization is sufficient for their purposes, they should provide evidence supporting this claim.

3. Clarify the relationship between the authors' approach and prior work (Ballé et al., 2016) by explicitly stating what is new and how this work advances the previous research.

4. Include a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity and practical implementation considerations, particularly regarding the entropy coding scheme.

## 5. Questions for the Authors

To better understand the paper's contribution and ensure confidence in my assessment, I would like to ask the following questions:

1. The paper states that "the paper is very well written," but the authors previously optimized for a perceptual metric in Ballé et al. (2016) and demonstrated superior visual quality. What was the specific rationale for choosing MSE as the distortion metric in this paper rather than a perceptual metric, and how does this decision align with the authors' previous work?

2. Equation 10 appears to be incorrect as written. The partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ), meaning the approach cannot be equivalent to a VAE for non-Euclidean metrics. Could the authors clarify this point and correct the equation if necessary?

3. The paper claims the approach is "end-to-end optimized," but the entropy coding is still based on a standard CABAC implementation. What would be the impact on performance if the entropy coding were fully learned end-to-end rather than using a standard implementation?

4. Could the authors provide a comparison of their method with the Toderici et al. (2016) approach, which also aims for end-to-end image compression with multiple rate-distortion trade-offs? How do the two approaches differ in terms of optimization and performance?

These questions would help clarify the novelty and contribution of this work relative to prior publications in the field.
