

This is the most compelling paper on image compression using deep neural networks I have encountered to date. The work is exceptionally well-written, and the integration of rate-distortion theory into the objective function is both elegant and seamless within the proposed framework. The authors appropriately benchmark against a robust baseline (JPEG2000), a significant improvement over prior work that often only compared to JPEG. I anticipate this paper will have a substantial impact on the field.

To further strengthen the contribution, the authors should include results on standard test images (Lena, Barbara, Baboon) alongside state-of-the-art classical methods, as referenced in my queries. The current submission does not clearly establish how neural network approaches compare to the absolute best prior classical techniques. Specifically, it remains ambiguous how the proposed method positions itself relative to the most advanced non-NN compression algorithms. Including these results and explicitly stating the relative performance against the strongest classical baselines would provide critical context for the community and solidify the paper's significance.
