- Proposes a well-motivated end-to-end trainable image compression system that achieves superior rate–distortion performance compared to methods like JPEG-2000.  
- Key contribution is a differentiable approximation of the rate function, enabling effective end-to-end training with customizable rate–distortion trade-offs.  
- The differentiable-rate idea is likely to influence other domains that require differentiable approximations of similar functions.  
- Authors provided a thoughtful and substantive response to the reviewer’s pre-review query.  
- Reviewer acknowledges a theoretical possibility that a sufficiently complex network could learn codes within a fixed quantization range to achieve maximum entropy and meet the distortion bound under a fixed bitrate.  
- However, enforcing a fixed code range imposes structural constraints; to match the presented system’s performance the network would have to replicate the external variable-rate encoder, implying a much more complex architecture, so the authors’ approach is more efficient and practical.
