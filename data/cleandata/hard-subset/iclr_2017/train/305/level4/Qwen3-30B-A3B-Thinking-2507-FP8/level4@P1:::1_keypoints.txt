- Presents an end-to-end optimized image compression method using nonlinear transforms and continuous relaxation of quantization for gradient-based training.  
- Uses biologically inspired generalized divisive normalization (GDN) and jointly optimizes analysis/synthesis transforms and quantization to improve rate–distortion performance.  
- Demonstrates significant gains in objective (PSNR) and perceptual (MS-SSIM) metrics across a wide range of bit rates.  
- Provides a complete solution that can operate at any point on the rate–distortion curve.  
- Recommendation: Accept — the contribution is compelling, well-grounded in theory, and supported by strong empirical evidence.  
- Strength: Rigorous grounding in rate–distortion theory with clear, quantifiable improvements over established baselines.  
- Strength: Thorough empirical evaluation, comparing against JPEG 2000 across multiple metrics and datasets.  
- Strength: GDN nonlinearities are well-motivated and effectively capture natural image statistics.  
- Strength: Continuous relaxation of quantization is theoretically sound and practically effective.  
- Strength: Careful distinction between continuous optimization and discrete compression implementation avoids common evaluation pitfalls.  
- Positioning: Builds on and advances prior neural-network compression work with fair, insightful comparisons to related methods.  
- Suggestion: Report results explicitly on standard test images (Lena, Barbara, Peppers, Mandrill) to aid comparison with prior work.  
- Suggestion: Include analysis of which image types or features benefit most from the method versus traditional codecs.  
- Suggestion/Question: Provide per-image and per-bit-rate MS-SSIM tables/figures, clarify which images underperform and why, and compare to more recent codecs (e.g., BPG).
