

This paper presents one of the most compelling cases for image compression using deep neural networks to date. The writing is clear and well-structured, and the integration of rate-distortion theory into the objective function is both natural and effective within the proposed framework. The comparison to a meaningful baseline—JPEG2000, rather than the commonly used JPEG in prior work—significantly strengthens the evaluation. I anticipate this paper will have a substantial impact on the field.

To further solidify the contribution, the authors should include results on standard test images (Lena, Barbara, and Baboon), alongside state-of-the-art classical methods. Specifically, the comparison to the best previous methods—such as those referenced in the discussion of classical approaches—needs to be explicitly stated. As presented in the current submission, the relative positioning of neural network methods versus these established classical techniques remains unclear. Including these results and clarifying this comparison is essential for a complete assessment of the paper's novelty and performance.
