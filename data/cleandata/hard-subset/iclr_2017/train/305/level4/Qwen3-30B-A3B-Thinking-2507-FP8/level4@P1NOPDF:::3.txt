

This paper presents a well-motivated end-to-end trainable image compression system that achieves superior rate-distortion performance compared to established methods like JPEG-2000. Beyond demonstrating deep learning's applicability to image compression, the key contribution lies in introducing a differentiable approximation of the rate function. This enables effective end-to-end training with customizable rate-distortion trade-offs, and the approach is likely to influence other domains requiring differentiable approximations of similar functions.

The authors provided a thoughtful response to my pre-review query. While I maintain that a sufficiently complex network could, in principle, automatically learn to produce codes within a fixed quantization range that achieve maximum entropy (thereby meeting the theoretical upper bound for distortion minimization under a fixed bitrate constraint), the authors' second point is compelling. Specifically, enforcing a fixed code range imposes a structural constraint on how compressor outputs are processed. To match the effective compression performance of their current system—which relies on a separate variable-rate encoder for storing quantized values—the network would need to replicate the functionality of this external encoder. This would necessitate a significantly more complex architecture, making the authors' approach more efficient and practical.
