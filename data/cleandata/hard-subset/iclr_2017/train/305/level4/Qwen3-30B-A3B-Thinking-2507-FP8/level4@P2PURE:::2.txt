

This paper extends rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders and decoders, transitioning from a basic entropy encoding scheme to adaptive entropy coding, and examines the relationship between this approach and variational autoencoders.  

While the core rate-distortion optimization framework has been previously published, the novelty of this work appears limited (please clarify if a significant technical contribution was overlooked). Moreover, the paper regresses from prior work by optimizing for MSE instead of perceptual metrics, which undermines a key advancement in earlier studies. Nevertheless, the results demonstrate a substantial improvement over JPEG 2000, and no prior learned codec has achieved comparable performance. The paper is well-written.  

Equation 10 appears incorrect; the partition function should depend on $g_s(y; \theta)$, implying the approach is not equivalent to a VAE under non-Euclidean metrics.  

Why was MSE optimized instead of a perceptual metric, as in prior work? Given the authors' expertise, the exclusive use of PSNR for evaluation is unexpected.  

The contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper network architectures requires clarification. A comparison with performance without adaptation (as in prior work) is essential to isolate the impact of the coding scheme. Detailed analysis of the adaptive coder’s effects and its marginal gains should be provided. I will revise my score upward upon addressing these points.
