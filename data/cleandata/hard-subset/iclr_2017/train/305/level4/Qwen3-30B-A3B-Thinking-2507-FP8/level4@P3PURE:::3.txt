

This paper presents a well-motivated end-to-end trained image compression and decompression system that achieves superior bit-rate versus quality trade-offs compared to established algorithms such as JPEG-2000. Beyond demonstrating deep learning's efficacy for image compression, a key contribution is the introduction of a differentiable approximation to the "rate" function, enabling effective training across diverse rate-distortion trade-offs. This innovation is likely to impact fields beyond compression where differentiable approximations of similar functions are beneficial.

The authors provided a thoughtful response to my pre-review query. I maintain that, under a fixed quantization range, a sufficiently complex network could theoretically learn to produce codes within that range achieving maximum entropy (i.e., meeting the theoretical upper bound for entropy within the range). However, the authors' second point is compelling: enforcing such a fixed-range constraint would necessitate a specific structural form for how the compressor output is utilized. To achieve compression efficiency equivalent to the current system—which relies on a separate variable rate encoder to store the quantized values (q)—the network would require significantly greater complexity to replicate the computations currently handled by this external component.
