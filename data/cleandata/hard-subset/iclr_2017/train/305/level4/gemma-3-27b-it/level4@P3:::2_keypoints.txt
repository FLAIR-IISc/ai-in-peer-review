- Extends prior rate-distortion optimization to deeper convolutional encoder-decoder models and adds adaptive entropy coding; discusses link to VAEs.  
- Strong empirical results showing clear improvement over JPEG and JPEG 2000 in both PSNR and perceptual quality (MS-SSIM) with illustrative examples.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented, with detailed architecture and optimization that aid reproducibility.  
- Inclusion of an adaptive entropy coder is a crucial step toward practicality, but its isolated impact is not demonstrated.  
- Work is incremental over prior techniques, but the empirical gains in compression performance and visual fidelity are substantial.  
- Needs clarification on why MSE was chosen as the optimization criterion instead of previously used perceptual metrics.  
- Requests an ablation study comparing performance with and without adaptive entropy coding and more implementation details (beyond mentioning CABAC).  
- Asks for further clarification and quantification of the VAE equivalence issue under non‑Euclidean distortion (Equation 10) and its effect on the loss.  
- Requests information on computational cost: training resources and encoding/decoding times for typical image sizes.  
- Recommendation/decision: Accept — strong empirical results outweigh incremental novelty.
