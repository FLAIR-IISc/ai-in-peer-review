This paper extends rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders and decoders, and advances the entropy coding scheme from a simple approach to adaptive entropy coding. Furthermore, the paper discusses the relationship between this method and variational autoencoders.

While the core approach to rate-distortion optimization has been previously published, the novelty of this submission is arguably limited (though I may have missed a key innovation). In some respects, this work represents a step back from previous iterations, which optimized for perceptual metrics; here, mean squared error (MSE) is used instead. However, the results demonstrate a clear improvement over JPEG 2000, and I am unaware of any other learned encoding scheme achieving comparable performance. The paper is exceptionally well written.

Equation 10 appears to be incorrect; the partition function should depend on g_s(y; theta). This implies that the approach is not equivalent to a variational autoencoder when non-Euclidean metrics are employed.

I am curious about the rationale for optimizing MSE rather than a perceptual metric, as was done in prior work. Given the authors’ expertise, the exclusive reliance on PSNR for evaluation is also surprising.

The contribution of adaptive entropy coding, as opposed to the impact of deeper encoders and decoders, remains unclear. This appears to be a critical aspect of the work, and it would be valuable to see performance results without adaptation, mirroring the setup of the earlier paper. More detailed explanation of the adaptive coder and its effects would be beneficial; I would be happy to consider a higher score upon inclusion of this information.
