This paper presents a compelling approach to image compression using deep neural networks, and is the most convincing work on this topic I have encountered to date. The writing is excellent, and the integration of rate-distortion theory into the objective function is particularly well-executed and natural within the proposed framework. A key strength of this work is its comparison to JPEG2000, a strong and relevant baseline, which is a significant improvement over prior studies that primarily focused on JPEG. I anticipate this paper will have a substantial impact on the field.

To further strengthen the results, I recommend including performance metrics on the standard test images Lena, Barbara, and Baboon. Additionally, a more thorough comparison to state-of-the-art classical image compression methods—such as the one mentioned in my questions to the authors—is crucial. Currently, it remains unclear how the performance of this neural network-based approach compares to the very best existing methods overall. Establishing this positioning is important for fully evaluating the contribution of this work. While the paper demonstrates clear advantages, a more direct and comprehensive comparison will definitively illustrate the benefits of the proposed method relative to the current state-of-the-art in image compression.
