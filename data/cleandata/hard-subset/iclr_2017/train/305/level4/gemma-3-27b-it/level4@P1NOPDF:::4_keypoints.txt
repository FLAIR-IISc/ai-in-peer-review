- Presents an end-to-end learning method for image compression optimizing rate-distortion with a clever relaxation technique.  
- Learns an efficient compression scheme directly from a database of natural images.  
- Core idea: formulate compression as a differentiable optimization to allow gradient-based learning of encoder and decoder.  
- Significant departure from hand-crafted transforms and rate-distortion heuristics, leveraging autoencoder architectures and optimization advances.  
- Results are compelling and competitive with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Detailed ablation studies clearly demonstrate the contribution of components (relaxation choice, loss weighting).  
- Comparisons to JPEG and JPEG 2000 are well-executed, showing notable PSNR and MS-SSIM improvements.  
- Theoretical justification for the relaxation is sound and discussion of implications is insightful.  
- Limitations: high computational cost for training and potential artifacts at high compression ratios.  
- Minor concerns: potential sensitivity to hyperparameters and limited evaluation across diverse image datasets.  
- Overall assessment: novel, effective, well-written work representing a significant advance; strong recommendation for acceptance.
