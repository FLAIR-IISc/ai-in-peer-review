This is a strong paper that demonstrates an end-to-end trained image compression and decompression system achieving improved bit-rate versus quality trade-offs compared to established image compression algorithms such as JPEG-2000. Beyond showcasing the efficacy of deep learning for a novel application, a key contribution of this work is the introduction of a differentiable approximation to the rate function. The authors demonstrate its effectiveness for training with various rate-distortion trade-offs, and I anticipate this technique will have impact extending beyond image compression—specifically, for other tasks that could benefit from differentiable approximations to similar functions.

The authors provided a thoughtful response to my pre-review question. I still contend that, to minimize distortion under a fixed rate and quantization, a sufficiently complex network would learn to automatically produce codes within a given range with the highest possible entropy (i.e., it would approach the theoretical upper bound). However, the authors’ second argument is convincing: enforcing such behavior would impose a specific structure on the compressor’s output, requiring a more complex network architecture to match the effective compression performance of the current system, which relies on a separate variable rate encoder for storing the quantized values.
