This paper extends an approach to rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders and decoders, and from a simple entropy encoding scheme to adaptive entropy coding. In addition, the paper discusses the approach's relationship to variational autoencoders.

Given that the approach to rate-distortion optimization has been previously published, the novelty of this submission is arguably limited. While the paper builds upon existing work, it even represents a step backward in some regards, as earlier work optimized for a perceptual metric, whereas this paper utilizes MSE. However, the results demonstrate a visible improvement over JPEG 2000, and I am unaware of any other learned encoding method achieving this level of performance. The paper is very well written. 

Equation 10 appears incorrect; I believe the partition function should depend on g<sub>s</sub>(y; θ). This would imply that the approach is not strictly equivalent to a VAE for non-Euclidean metrics.

What was the rationale for optimizing for MSE rather than a perceptual metric, as done in previous work? Given the authors’ expertise, it is surprising that the evaluation was solely performed using PSNR.

What is the contribution of adaptive entropy coding compared to the impact of deeper encoders and decoders? This appears to be a crucial aspect, and it would be insightful to see performance results without adaptation, mirroring the prior work. More detail on the adaptive coder and its effects would be appreciated. I would be happy to reconsider a higher score with this additional information.

**Decision:** Accept

**Key Reasons:** The paper presents a significant improvement over standard compression methods (JPEG and JPEG 2000) in terms of visual quality and, in many cases, rate-distortion performance. Despite the incremental nature of the work, the demonstrated results are compelling.

**Supporting Arguments:**

The paper tackles a well-defined and important problem – image compression – and builds upon a solid foundation of previous work in rate-distortion optimization and neural networks. The approach is well-motivated, drawing inspiration from both engineering principles and neuroscience (specifically, local gain control in biological neurons). The experimental results, particularly the visual comparisons, convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method. The inclusion of an adaptive entropy coder and its integration within the framework are crucial contributions. While the theoretical connection to variational autoencoders is interesting, the practical improvements in compression performance are the primary justification for acceptance.

**Additional Feedback:**

*   **Equation 10:** Please address the potential error in Equation 10 regarding the partition function. Clarifying this point is important for understanding the theoretical underpinnings of the method.
*   **MSE vs. Perceptual Metric:** A more detailed explanation of the decision to use MSE instead of a perceptual metric would be helpful. Were there practical considerations, or is the intention to explore the limitations of optimizing for MSE?
*   **Adaptive Entropy Coding:** A more thorough ablation study to isolate the effect of the adaptive entropy coding would strengthen the paper. Presenting results with a simpler, non-adaptive entropy coder would allow for a clearer understanding of its contribution. Further elaboration on the specific implementation of the adaptive coder is also desirable.
*   **Generalization:** While the results are promising, it would be useful to discuss the potential limitations of the method and areas for future research, such as generalization to different image types or video compression.
*   **Computational Cost:** A brief discussion of the computational cost of training and inference would be valuable.
