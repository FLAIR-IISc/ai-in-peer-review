- End-to-end trainable image compression system that outperforms JPEG-2000 on bitrate vs. quality trade-offs.  
- Key contribution: a differentiable approximation to the rate function enabling effective training across rate-distortion trade-offs.  
- Differentiable rate function has potential impact beyond compression for tasks needing differentiable approximations of rate-limiting functions.  
- Reviewer appreciated authors’ response to whether a standard network would naturally learn entropy-maximizing codes under fixed rate/quantization.  
- Authors argue enforcing entropy-maximizing outputs would require a specific output structure and a much more complex network.  
- Replicating the variable-rate encoder’s functionality for storing quantized values would add substantial computational burden.  
- Innovation lies in the method (learnable, differentiable rate) that unlocks direct rate-distortion optimization within deep learning, with results demonstrating its advantages.
