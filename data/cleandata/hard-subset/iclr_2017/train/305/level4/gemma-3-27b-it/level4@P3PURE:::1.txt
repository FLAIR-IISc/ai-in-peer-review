This is the most convincing paper on image compression with deep neural networks that I have read to date. The paper is very well written, and the application of rate-distortion theory within the objective function integrates seamlessly into the proposed framework. Importantly, the paper’s comparison to a reasonable baseline—JPEG2000, rather than solely JPEG as seen in previous work—provides a more robust evaluation. I anticipate this paper will have a significant impact on the field.

However, inclusion of results on standard benchmark images such as Lena, Barbara, and Baboon (rather than Gibbons) would be beneficial. Furthermore, it is important to clearly position the performance of this neural network approach relative to state-of-the-art classical methods, including those mentioned in my questions. Currently, the submitted version does not fully clarify how these two categories of methods compare; understanding their respective strengths and weaknesses is crucial for a comprehensive assessment.
