- Demonstrates end-to-end trained image compression with improved rate–distortion vs. JPEG/JPEG 2000 and introduces a differentiable rate approximation with broader applicability.  
- Decision: Reject, request major revisions due to insufficient architectural justification and inadequate comparison to existing entropy coding.  
- Core claim (end-to-end optimization yields better performance) is compelling but lacks explanation of why it succeeds.  
- Benefits of GDN nonlinearity over simpler alternatives (e.g., ReLU) are not rigorously demonstrated; ablation studies are insufficient.  
- Comparisons emphasize visual quality and PSNR but lack detailed bitrate/entropy estimates tied to the use of CABAC.  
- Unclear how the custom CABAC implementation compares to entropy coding in standard JPEG/JPEG 2000 implementations.  
- Request: comprehensive ablation studies to quantify each component’s contribution (GDN, stages, filter sizes) and comparisons to simpler architectures.  
- Request: detailed entropy coding analysis comparing achieved entropy of custom CABAC vs. standard schemes under comparable conditions.  
- Request: provide architectural justification for chosen filter sizes, channel counts, and downsampling factors.  
- Request: strengthen theoretical connection between the relaxed loss and variational autoencoders, clarifying differing constraints and implications.  
- Request: discuss computational cost of training and inference and scaling behavior with image size and compression ratio.  
- Clarifying Q: How was the relaxed loss’s approximation to true rate–distortion empirically verified—provide quantitative bias/variance analysis of relaxed entropy/distortion estimates.  
- Clarifying Q: Why use a fixed uniform scalar quantizer—have vector or learned quantization schemes been explored within this framework?  
- Clarifying Q: Elaborate on the claim that the rate–distortion objective is an instantiation of unsupervised learning and potential applications to other unsupervised problems.  
- Clarifying Q: How sensitive is model performance to preprocessing choices (amount of noise added, downsampling factor) described in the appendix?
