- Presents an end-to-end learning method for image compression that jointly optimizes rate–distortion and uses a relaxation to handle quantization non-differentiability.
- Recommends accepting the paper; approach is well-motivated and shows clear improvements in objective and perceptual quality over JPEG and JPEG 2000.
- Departs from traditional transform coding by end-to-end optimization of analysis and synthesis transforms to better address joint rate–distortion optimization.
- Motivation ties to Shannon information theory and the challenges of optimal quantization in high-dimensional spaces; literature positioning is strong.
- Uses generalized divisive normalization (GDN) nonlinearity, justified by prior work showing its effectiveness in Gaussianizing image densities.
- Provides comprehensive experimental results on Kodak and other datasets demonstrating consistent MS-SSIM and often PSNR improvements.
- Ablation studies and analysis of the relaxed loss function are convincing and supported by visual comparisons.
- Authors acknowledge limitations of MSE as an optimization criterion and suggest exploring perceptual metrics in future work.
- Implements entropy coding and reports actual bit rates, demonstrating practical applicability.
- Suggestion: clarify training data details—exact ImageNet subset selection and preprocessing (resizing/downsampling) procedures.
- Suggestion: report computational cost and training resources (training time, hardware) to assess practicality.
- Suggestion: evaluate generalization to other image types (medical, satellite) beyond natural images.
- Suggestion: include more detailed comparisons to recent learned compression methods (e.g., VAE- or GAN-based approaches).
- Suggestion: provide deeper intuition for why GDN is particularly well-suited for this task beyond prior citations.
- Question: provide quantitative bias and variance estimates for the relaxed entropy/distortion estimates.
- Question: elaborate on the specific constraints that differentiate the approach from standard VAEs and how they help approximate the discrete problem.
- Question: clarify motivation for architectural choices (number of filters, downsampling factors)—empirical selection or prior knowledge?
