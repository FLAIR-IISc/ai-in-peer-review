- Strong paper: end-to-end trained image compression/decompression achieves favorable bitrate–quality trade-offs vs. algorithms like JPEG-2000.
- Main contribution: introduction of a differentiable approximation to the rate function.
- This differentiable rate enables effective training across different rate–distortion trade-offs.
- The technique is likely to impact other tasks that could use differentiable approximations to similar functions.
- Authors responded thoughtfully to the reviewer’s pre-review question.
- Reviewer’s point: theoretically, a sufficiently complex network could learn codes within a fixed quantization range that maximize entropy and meet the upper bound.
- Authors’ rebuttal convincing: rigidly enforcing the constraint shapes compressor outputs, and replicating the current system’s performance would require a much more complex network to replace the separate variable-rate encoder for storing q.
