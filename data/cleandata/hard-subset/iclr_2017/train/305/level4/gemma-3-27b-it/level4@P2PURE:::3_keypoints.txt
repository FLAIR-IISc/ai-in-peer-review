- End-to-end trained image compression system that outperforms JPEG-2000 on bit-rate vs. quality trade-offs.  
- Key contribution: a differentiable approximation to the rate function enabling effective training under varying rate–distortion constraints.  
- The differentiable rate approximation could benefit other tasks that need differentiable approximations of similar functions.  
- Reviewer conjecture: with fixed rate and quantization, a sufficiently complex network could learn maximal-entropy codes and reach the upper bound.  
- Authors' rebuttal: forcing that structure would constrain compressor outputs and would likely require a far more complex network to replicate the separate variable-rate encoder used for quantizing q.
