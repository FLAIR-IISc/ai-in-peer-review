This is a well-written paper that demonstrates an end-to-end trained image compression and decompression system, achieving improved rate-distortion performance compared to established algorithms like JPEG and JPEG 2000. Beyond demonstrating the efficacy of deep learning for this application, a key contribution lies in the introduction of a differentiable approximation to the rate function, enabling effective training across varying rate-distortion trade-offs. This advancement holds potential impact beyond image compression, benefiting other tasks requiring differentiable approximations of similar functions.

**Reject** with a request for major revisions. While the results are promising, the paper lacks sufficient justification for the architectural choices and a more thorough comparison to existing entropy coding methods.

The core argument – that end-to-end optimization yields superior performance – is compelling. However, the paper doesn’t fully elucidate *why* this approach is successful. The authors highlight the use of GDN inspired by biological neurons, but the specific benefits of this nonlinearity over simpler alternatives (e.g., ReLU) aren’t rigorously demonstrated. The ablation studies presented are insufficient to justify the complexity of the chosen architecture. Additionally, the comparison to JPEG and JPEG 2000 primarily focuses on visual quality and PSNR; a more detailed analysis of the achieved bitrates and their corresponding entropy estimates is crucial, especially given the use of a CABAC entropy code. It remains unclear how the performance of this custom entropy code compares to those already integrated within JPEG and JPEG 2000 implementations.

To improve the paper, I suggest the following:

*   **Ablation Studies:** Conduct more comprehensive ablation studies to evaluate the contribution of each component of the architecture (e.g., GDN, number of stages, filter sizes). Compare performance against simpler architectures with fewer parameters.
*   **Entropy Coding Analysis:** Provide a detailed comparison of the entropy achieved by the custom CABAC implementation versus standard entropy coding schemes used in JPEG and JPEG 2000, under comparable conditions. 
*   **Architectural Justification:** Elaborate on the rationale behind the specific architectural choices. Why were these particular filter sizes, numbers of channels, and downsampling factors selected?
*   **Theoretical Connection:** Strengthen the theoretical connection between the relaxed loss function and variational autoencoders. A more in-depth discussion of the differences in constraints and their implications would be beneficial.
*   **Computational Cost:** Discuss the computational cost of training and inference. How does this method scale with image size and desired compression ratio?

I have a few clarifying questions:

*   The authors mention that the relaxed loss function provides a “good approximation” to actual rate-distortion values (Section 4). How was this approximation empirically verified? Could you provide a more quantitative analysis of the bias and variance of the relaxed entropy and distortion estimates?
*   The choice of a fixed uniform scalar quantizer seems somewhat arbitrary. Have the authors explored other quantization strategies, such as vector quantization or learned quantization schemes, within the end-to-end optimization framework?
*   In Section 3.1, you state that the rate-distortion objective is a particular instantiation of unsupervised learning. Can you elaborate on this connection and discuss potential applications of this framework to other unsupervised learning problems?
*   The appendix details the preprocessing steps taken before training. How sensitive is the performance of the trained model to these preprocessing choices (e.g., the amount of noise added, downsampling factor)?
