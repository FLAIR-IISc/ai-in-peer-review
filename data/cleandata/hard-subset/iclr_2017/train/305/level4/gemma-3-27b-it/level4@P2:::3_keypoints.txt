- Presents an end-to-end trained image compression system that outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 on rate-distortion metrics.  
- Introduces a differentiable approximation to the rate function, enabling training across rate-distortion trade-offs.  
- Technique potentially generalizes to other tasks requiring differentiable approximations of similar functions.  
- Reviewer recommends accept: novel core idea, well-executed, and significant empirical improvements.  
- Formulates compression as joint end-to-end optimization rather than separate transform/quantizer/entropy-code design.  
- Uses a tailored architecture with cascaded convolutions, GDN nonlinearities, and a continuous proxy for quantization.  
- Connects the relaxed loss function to variational autoencoders, offering theoretical grounding.  
- Strong empirical evaluation (Kodak and other standard images) showing rate-distortion and perceptual (MS-SSIM) gains; test images and curves provided online.
