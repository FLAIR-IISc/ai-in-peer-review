- Presents a compelling neural-network-based approach to image compression, described as the most convincing work in this area the reviewer has seen.
- Writing is exceptionally clear and well-structured.
- Integrates rate-distortion theory elegantly into the training objective.
- Compares against a strong baseline (JPEG2000) rather than only JPEG.
- Core contribution: a deep-learning-based image compression method grounded in rate-distortion theory that achieves promising results.
- Experimental results are promising but currently limited in scope.
- Reviewer requests inclusion of standard benchmark datasets (Lena, Barbara, Baboon) for comparison with existing literature.
- Reviewer asks for contextualization versus additional state-of-the-art classical compression methods (beyond JPEG2000) with proper references.
- It is unclear whether observed gains justify the increased computational complexity of neural-network methods.
- Reviewer requests a detailed comparison table showing PSNR, SSIM, MS-SSIM, and bitrate across compression ratios for neural and classical methods on standard datasets.
- Strengths summarized: clear writing, strong theoretical foundation, relevant baseline, and promising results.
- Weaknesses summarized: limited benchmarks, lack of comprehensive comparison to classical methods, and missing competitive analysis.
- Overall assessment: highly promising paper with significant potential; recommended for acceptance contingent on adding requested results and in-depth comparative analysis.
