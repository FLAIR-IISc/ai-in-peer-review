This is the most convincing paper on image compression with deep neural networks I have read to date. The paper is very well written, and the application of rate-distortion theory within the objective function integrates seamlessly into the proposed framework. Importantly, the paper includes a comparison to a reasonable baseline (JPEG2000), a welcome change from previous work that often focused solely on JPEG. I anticipate this paper will have a significant impact on the field.

I would appreciate the inclusion of results on standard test images like Lena, Barbara, and Baboon (my apologies, not Gibbons!), alongside comparisons to state-of-the-art methods employing more classical techniques – such as the one I mentioned in my questions. It’s crucial to clearly demonstrate how neural network-based approaches perform relative to these established methods. As it stands, the submitted version doesn’t fully clarify the positioning of both categories of techniques; I’m still unsure of their relative strengths and weaknesses.
