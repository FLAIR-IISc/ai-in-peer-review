This work presents a highly compelling approach to image compression using deep neural networks – arguably the most convincing I’ve encountered in the literature to date. The manuscript is exceptionally well-written, and the integration of rate-distortion theory into the objective function is seamless and effective. A key strength of this submission is the comparison to a relevant and strong baseline (JPEG2000), a notable improvement over previous studies that primarily focused on JPEG. I anticipate this paper will have a significant impact on the field. 

To further strengthen the evaluation, I recommend including results on standard benchmark images such as Lena, Barbara, and Baboon. Furthermore, a more comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art classical methods – including those like JPEG2000, as previously discussed – is crucial. Currently, it remains unclear how the performance of this neural network-based approach positions relative to the best existing methods across both categories; explicitly clarifying this comparison will be invaluable to readers.
