- Paper presents an end-to-end optimized image compression framework using nonlinear transforms with strong motivation and clear methodology.  
- Decision: Accept — significant advance showing superior performance and visual quality over established methods.  
- Jointly optimizes analysis/synthesis transforms and quantization, moving beyond separate component optimization in transform coding.  
- Uses Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN) inspired by biology, effectively capturing image statistics.  
- Introduces continuous relaxation of quantization to enable gradient-based optimization — a key innovation.  
- Evaluates using actual bit rates (not just entropy estimates), increasing practical relevance.  
- Reports improvements in objective (MS-SSIM) and subjective visual quality.  
- Provides a useful connection to variational autoencoders while noting important differences.  
- Suggestion: expand benchmark comparisons to standard test images (e.g., Lena, Barbara, Mandrill/Baboon).  
- Suggestion: compare against broader state-of-the-art classical (BPG/HEVC) and recent learned compression schemes.  
- Suggestion: expand discussion on alternative simpler nonlinearities and potential links to adversarial robustness.  
- Q1: Request to quantify bias between relaxed MSE and actual post-quantization MSE (e.g., table averaged over validation set).  
- Q2: Ask how sensitive performance is to downsampling factors (sk) and whether different configurations were tested.  
- Q3: Ask whether allowing entropy model parameters to adapt during encoding affects performance significantly.  
- Q4: Request specific training cost details (number of GPUs, training time in days) to assess reproducibility.
