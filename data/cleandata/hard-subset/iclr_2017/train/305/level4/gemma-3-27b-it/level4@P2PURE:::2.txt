This paper applies rate-distortion optimization to deep neural encoders and decoders, extending previous work by incorporating adaptive entropy coding in place of a simpler entropy scheme. The authors also discuss connections between their approach and variational autoencoders. 

While the underlying rate-distortion optimization technique has been previously published, the novelty of this submission is incremental. Indeed, the shift to minimizing mean squared error (MSE) represents a departure from earlier work that optimized for perceptual metrics, and could be seen as a step backward in that regard. Nevertheless, the results demonstrate a clear improvement over JPEG 2000, and to the best of my knowledge, no other learned compression method has achieved comparable performance. The paper is exceptionally well-written.

I identified a potential error in Equation 10; the partition function appears to depend on g_s(y; theta), which would invalidate the equivalence to a VAE when using non-Euclidean metrics. 

I am curious about the rationale for optimizing MSE instead of a perceptual metric, as was done in the authors’ prior work. Given their expertise, the exclusive use of PSNR for evaluation is also somewhat surprising. 

Furthermore, the contribution of adaptive entropy coding relative to the impact of deeper encoders and decoders remains unclear. This is a critical distinction, and it would be valuable to see performance results without adaptation, mirroring the setup of the earlier publication. More detailed explanation of the adaptive coder and its effects would be beneficial; I would be inclined to increase my score upon receiving this clarification.
