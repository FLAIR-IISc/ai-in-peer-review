- Paper applies rate-distortion optimization to deep neural encoders/decoders and extends prior work by incorporating adaptive entropy coding and discussing links to VAEs.  
- Novelty is incremental because the underlying rate-distortion technique was published before; switching to MSE from perceptual metrics may be a step backward.  
- Empirical results show clear improvement over JPEG 2000 and potentially the best performance among learned compression methods to date.  
- The manuscript is exceptionally well-written.  
- Potential error in Equation 10: the partition function seems to depend on g_s(y; θ), which would break the claimed VAE equivalence for non-Euclidean metrics.  
- Reviewer questions the rationale for optimizing MSE and the exclusive use of PSNR rather than perceptual metrics, given the authors’ prior work.  
- Unclear how much improvement comes from adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoder/decoder architecture; requests ablation (results without adaptation) and a more detailed explanation of the adaptive coder.
