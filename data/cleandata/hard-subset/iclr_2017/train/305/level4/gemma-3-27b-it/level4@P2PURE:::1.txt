This is the most compelling work on image compression using deep neural networks that I have encountered to date. The paper is exceptionally well-written, and the integration of rate-distortion theory into the objective function is seamless and natural. The authors demonstrate a strong comparison to a relevant baseline (JPEG2000), a significant improvement over prior work that often limited comparisons to JPEG alone. I anticipate this paper will have a substantial impact on the field.

To further strengthen the evaluation, I request the inclusion of results on standard benchmark images such as Lena, Barbara, and Baboon (rather than Gibbons). Furthermore, a clear comparison to state-of-the-art classical methods, including the one mentioned in my questions, is crucial. It remains unclear from the submitted version how these neural network-based methods position themselves relative to the best performing traditional approaches; clarifying this comparison is important for a complete assessment.
