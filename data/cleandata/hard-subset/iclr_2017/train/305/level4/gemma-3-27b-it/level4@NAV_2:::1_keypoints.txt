- Highly compelling paper on image compression using deep neural networks with excellent presentation.
- Seamless, well-motivated integration of rate–distortion theory into the objective.
- Strong comparison to JPEG 2000 (not just JPEG), increasing the paper’s significance and impact.
- Recommendation: accept, based on novel end-to-end optimization and demonstrable visual quality improvements.
- Tackles joint optimization of rate and distortion via end-to-end learning, building on transform coding principles.
- Positions work within probabilistic source modeling and generative modeling, drawing insightful parallels to VAEs while noting compression constraints.
- Use of biologically-inspired GDN nonlinearity is well-justified by visual processing models.
- Thorough experiments on the Kodak dataset showing consistent PSNR and MS-SSIM improvements over baselines.
- Ablation study and discussion of the relaxed loss/VAEs are well-reasoned and add rigor.
- Authors acknowledge limitations and provide comprehensive related-work discussion.
- Suggest adding results on standard test images (Lena, Barbara, Mandrill/Baboon) for broader comparability.
- Recommend direct empirical comparisons to other classical compression methods (e.g., curvelets, wavelet families).
- Ask for clarification of computational cost of training (approximate times and hardware).
- Clarifying question: sensitivity of results to choice of downsampling factors (sk) in the analysis transform.
- Clarifying question: details and computational cost/complexity of the entropy coding stage versus analysis/synthesis transforms.
- Clarifying question: intuition for why a perceptual (non-Euclidean) distortion metric might break the VAE equivalence and its implications.
- Clarifying question: quantify the bias of the relaxed distortion term (provide confidence intervals or statistical tests).
