- Paper proposes an end-to-end trained image compression system that outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 in rate-distortion.  
- Key contribution: a differentiable approximation to the rate function enabling gradient-based optimization across rate-distortion trade-offs.  
- Continuous relaxation of quantization is crucial, allowing effective training and connection to variational autoencoders.  
- Method shows consistent improvements in visual quality, supported by quantitative (PSNR, MS-SSIM) and qualitative comparisons on Kodak and additional images.  
- Includes an actual entropy coding step and makes test images/results available, aiding reproducibility.  
- Decision: Accept — well-motivated, novel, and convincingly validated experimentally.  
- Suggestion: Provide deeper discussion of complexity vs. entropy and trade-offs of achieving entropy-maximizing codes.  
- Suggestion: Better justify why GDN is especially suitable for compression beyond its Gaussianizing effect.  
- Suggestion: Report computational resources and training time to assess practicality.  
- Suggestion: Include an ablation study to quantify contributions of components (GDN vs ReLU, number of stages).  
- Question: What challenges prevented optimizing with a perceptual metric instead of MSE, and why was MSE chosen?  
- Question: Why use a piecewise linear function for marginal densities; were alternatives (e.g., Gaussian mixtures) considered?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to the positive bias of the relaxed entropy for coarse quantization, and were mitigation methods explored?  
- Question: Please provide a rough estimate of training time in GPU hours for a typical model.
