- Overall assessment: Very strong paper on deep-learning image compression with clear writing and seamless integration of rate–distortion theory.  
- Decision: Accept — novel, well-supported approach with promising results and thorough experiments.  
- Novelty: End-to-end optimization of nonlinear transforms inspired by biology offers a compelling alternative to traditional, hand-designed pipelines.  
- Performance: Demonstrates superior rate–distortion performance vs. JPEG and JPEG 2000 on the Kodak dataset.  
- Visual quality: Noticeable improvement in perceptual quality, corroborated by objective metrics like MS-SSIM.  
- Theory: Insightful theoretical connection to variational autoencoders, with clear discussion of necessary differences for practical compression.  
- Optimization trick: Uses a continuous relaxation of quantization, justified for SGD-based optimization.  
- Practicality: Reports performance using actual bit rates rather than estimated entropy, showing feasibility of a complete compression system.  
- Baseline choice: Strength in comparing to JPEG 2000 rather than only JPEG, improving credibility of results.  
- Suggested experiments: Include standard benchmark images (Lena, Barbara, Mandrill) to strengthen comparisons.  
- Comparative positioning: Recommend explicitly comparing to more advanced classical compression methods beyond JPEG/JPEG 2000.  
- Entropy coding: Explanation is detailed; add a brief discussion of its limitations and potential improvements.  
- Q1 (architecture): Clarify rationale/selection process for GDN architecture (stages, filter sizes, downsampling).  
- Q2 (activations): Provide details and parameter/compute trade-offs from experiments with ReLUs versus GDN.  
- Q3 (per-image variability): Address whether performance on new images can be predicted or characterized without retraining.  
- Q4 (state-of-the-art comparison): Report comparisons to recent classical methods using context modeling/advanced entropy coding, including RD performance and computational complexity.
