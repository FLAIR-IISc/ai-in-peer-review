This paper extends a previous approach to rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders and decoders, incorporating adaptive entropy coding. Furthermore, the paper elucidates the connection between this approach and variational autoencoders.

I recommend accepting this paper, despite the incremental nature of the contribution, due to the compelling empirical results demonstrating improved compression performance compared to standard codecs like JPEG and JPEG 2000. The authors clearly present a technically sound method and provide extensive experimental validation.

The work builds upon previously published research on rate-distortion optimization. However, the successful application of this optimization to a deep, end-to-end trainable compression model, coupled with the integration of adaptive entropy coding, represents a significant advance. While the shift from perceptual optimization to MSE is a point of consideration, the achieved visual quality improvements—supported by objective metrics like MS-SSIM—justify this design choice. The method consistently outperforms established codecs, indicating a tangible benefit to the field. The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains the technical details.

Equation 10 appears to be incorrect; the partition function should depend on g_s(y; theta). This would imply the equivalence to a VAE does not hold for non-Euclidean metrics.  Could the authors clarify the derivation of this equation and the implications for the relationship to variational autoencoders when using perceptual metrics?  

What motivated the decision to optimize for MSE, as opposed to a perceptual metric employed in prior work? Given the authors’ expertise, it is surprising that evaluation relied solely on PSNR; a more comprehensive perceptual evaluation would strengthen the findings. 

What is the specific contribution of the adaptive entropy coding compared to the improvements gained from the deeper encoders and decoders? Providing a performance comparison *without* adaptive coding (similar to the previous paper) would help isolate the benefits of this component. More detailed information regarding the adaptive coder's implementation and its performance impact would be beneficial. Finally, could the authors elaborate on any computational constraints that precluded a more extensive perceptual evaluation?
