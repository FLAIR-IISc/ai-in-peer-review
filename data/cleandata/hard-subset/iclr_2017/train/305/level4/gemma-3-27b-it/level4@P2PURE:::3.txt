This paper presents a compelling end-to-end trained image compression and decompression system that outperforms established algorithms like JPEG-2000 in terms of bit-rate versus quality trade-offs. Beyond demonstrating the effectiveness of deep learning in a new application domain, a key contribution is the introduction of a differentiable approximation to the rate function, enabling effective training with varying rate-distortion constraints. This differentiable rate function has the potential to impact areas beyond image compression, specifically tasks that could benefit from differentiable approximations of similar functions. 

The authors’ response to my pre-review question was insightful. I still contend that, given a fixed rate and quantization level, a sufficiently complex network should, in theory, learn to generate codes with maximal entropy within the allowed range – effectively reaching the upper bound. However, the authors’ counterargument is persuasive: enforcing such behavior would impose a specific structure on the compressor’s output, and to match the current system’s compression performance while adhering to this structure would likely require a significantly more complex network capable of replicating the computations currently handled by the separate variable rate encoder used for quantizing *q*.
