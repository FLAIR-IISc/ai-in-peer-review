This paper presents a compelling end-to-end trained image compression and decompression system that outperforms existing algorithms, such as JPEG-2000, in terms of bit-rate versus quality. Beyond demonstrating the successful application of deep learning to image compression, a significant contribution lies in the development of a differentiable approximation to the rate function. This innovation enables effective training across various rate-distortion trade-offs and has the potential to impact fields beyond compression, particularly those leveraging differentiable approximations of related functions.

While a sufficiently complex network *could* theoretically learn to maximize entropy under fixed range and quantization constraints, the authors convincingly argue that enforcing this directly would necessitate a significantly more complex network architecture to achieve comparable compression performance to the current system – specifically, one capable of replicating the computations currently handled by the separate variable rate encoder used for 'q'.
