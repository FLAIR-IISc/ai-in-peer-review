* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The use of rate-distortion theory provides a solid theoretical basis for the proposed objective function.
* **Relevant Baseline:** Comparing against JPEG2000 is a more rigorous and informative benchmark than comparisons to JPEG alone.
* **Promising Results:** The initial experimental results demonstrate the potential of deep learning for image compression.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Benchmark Datasets:** The current evaluation is limited; inclusion of standard datasets (Lena, Barbara, Baboon) is essential.
* **Lack of Comprehensive Comparison:** Performance is not clearly positioned relative to state-of-the-art classical image compression methods.
* **Missing Competitive Analysis:** A detailed comparison table demonstrating performance against relevant methods is needed.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the minor weaknesses outlined above, this is a highly promising paper with the potential to significantly advance the field of image compression. Addressing the requests for additional results and a more thorough competitive analysis would substantially strengthen the submission and solidify its impact.
