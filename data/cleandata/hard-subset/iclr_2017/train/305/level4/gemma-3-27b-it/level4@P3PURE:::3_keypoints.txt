- Strong paper demonstrating an end-to-end trained image compression system that improves rate–quality trade-offs compared to algorithms like JPEG-2000.
- Main contribution: a differentiable approximation to the rate function for use in training.
- Empirically effective across various rate–distortion trade-offs and likely useful beyond image compression for tasks needing differentiable approximations.
- Authors gave a thoughtful response to the reviewer’s pre-review question.
- Reviewer contends that a sufficiently complex network could implicitly learn to produce codes within a range with maximal entropy, approaching the theoretical upper bound under fixed rate and quantization.
- Reviewer accepts authors’ counterargument that enforcing that behavior would require a specific output structure and a more complex architecture, whereas the current system uses a separate variable-rate encoder to store quantized values.
