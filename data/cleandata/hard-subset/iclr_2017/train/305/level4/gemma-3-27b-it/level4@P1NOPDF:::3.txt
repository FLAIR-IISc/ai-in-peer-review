This is a strong paper demonstrating an end-to-end trained image compression and decompression system that achieves favorable bit-rate versus quality trade-offs compared to established algorithms like JPEG-2000. Beyond showcasing the efficacy of deep learning in a new application area, a key contribution is the introduction of a differentiable approximation to the rate function. The authors demonstrate this enables effective training across various rate-distortion trade-offs, and I anticipate this technique will have impact beyond image compression – potentially benefiting other tasks that could leverage differentiable approximations to similar functions.

The authors provided a thoughtful response to my pre-review question. I still maintain that, to minimize distortion under a fixed rate and quantization, a sufficiently complex network would learn to automatically produce codes within the given range exhibiting the highest possible entropy (and thus meeting the theoretical upper bound). However, the authors’ subsequent argument is convincing: enforcing such a constraint rigidly shapes the compressor’s output, and to match the performance of the current system, the network would require greater complexity to replicate the computations currently handled by the separate variable-rate encoder used to store *q*.
