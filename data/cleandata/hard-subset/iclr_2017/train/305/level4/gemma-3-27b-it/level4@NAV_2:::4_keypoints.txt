- Proposes a novel end-to-end learning method that jointly optimizes the full compression pipeline (analysis transform, uniform quantizer, synthesis transform) for rate-distortion.
- Introduces a continuous relaxation of quantization to enable gradient-based optimization.
- Establishes a compelling connection to variational autoencoders as theoretical grounding.
- Shows improved rate-distortion performance and notably better perceptual quality compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000.
- Recommends acceptance: approach is theoretically sound, empirically strong, and a significant advance.
- Clearly motivates the problem and situates the work relative to transform coding and VAE approaches.
- Justifies use of nonlinear transforms (GDN) via biological inspiration and Gaussianizing image densities.
- Experimental evaluation uses PSNR, MS-SSIM, and visual comparisons, demonstrating consistent perceptual gains.
- Practical relevance enhanced by implementing an entropy coder and reporting actual bit rates.
- Suggestion: provide a more explicit comparison of the optimization objectives between this method and VAEs.
- Suggestion: evaluate robustness on other image types (e.g., medical, satellite) to broaden applicability.
- Suggestion: include discussion of computational cost for training and encoding/decoding.
- Question: Why were GDN exponents fixed for stability, and would learning them affect performance or stability?
- Question: How sensitive are results to using 10 sampling points for the piecewise-linear approximation of marginals?
- Question: How does performance change when varying the number of stages (network depth) in the ablation study?
