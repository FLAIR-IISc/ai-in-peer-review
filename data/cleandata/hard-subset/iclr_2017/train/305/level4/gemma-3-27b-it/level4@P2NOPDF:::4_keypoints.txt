- Proposes an end-to-end learning approach to image compression using rate-distortion optimization and a novel relaxation technique.  
- Core idea is well-motivated and technically sound.  
- Results are promising and competitive with existing methods.  
- Analysis is thorough and includes insightful ablation studies that isolate component contributions.  
- Visualizations help explain how learned representations capture important image features.  
- Strength lies in elegant integration of optimization and learning, replacing hand-engineered transforms with learned representations.  
- Method has potential for improved compression efficiency as datasets and compute scale.  
- Minor concern: lacks detailed discussion of computational cost for training and inference relative to JPEG/WebP.  
- Minor concern: limited evaluation on diverse datasets—generalization should be tested further.  
- Minor concern: needs more detailed analysis of failure cases where the method performs poorly.  
- Overall assessment: clearly written, innovative, experimentally convincing; recommended for acceptance.
