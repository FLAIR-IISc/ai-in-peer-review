- Decision: Accept — important problem, well-motivated approach, strong empirical evidence.  
- Main contribution: End-to-end trained image compression system outperforming JPEG and JPEG 2000 on rate-distortion trade-offs.  
- Technical novelty: Introduces a differentiable approximation to the rate function enabling effective training across different rate-distortion trade-offs.  
- Broader impact: Differentiable rate approximation may benefit other tasks beyond compression.  
- Theoretical insight: Connects the relaxed loss function to variational autoencoders, providing useful context.  
- Empirical evidence: Consistent improvements in rate-distortion and perceptual quality (MS-SSIM), supported by compelling visual examples.  
- Evaluation strength: Extensive tests across multiple datasets and bit rates enhance result credibility.  
- Writing/clarity: Section 3.1 (VAE relationship) is dense and could be made more accessible.  
- Architecture explanation: Need clearer rationale for specific architectural choices (filter counts, downsampling factors).  
- Reproducibility: Authors note high training cost; more details on training infrastructure and time would help reproducibility.  
- Metric choice: Using MSE is pragmatic but exploring perceptual metrics could yield further improvements.  
- Question 1: Request quantitative bias/variance analysis for the relaxed distortion and entropy estimates.  
- Question 2: Ask whether renormalization to fix scaling ambiguity causes instability or alters learned filters.  
- Question 3: Ask about sensitivity to optimization choices (e.g., Adam vs. SGD) and impact on performance.  
- Question 4: Request details on ReLU experiments — how much larger the network must be and what architectural changes are needed.
