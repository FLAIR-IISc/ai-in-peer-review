This paper extends rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders and decoders, and generalizes the entropy encoding scheme from a simple fixed-rate approach to adaptive entropy coding. Furthermore, the paper discusses the connection between this approach and variational autoencoders.

While the underlying rate-distortion optimization framework has been previously published, the novelty of this submission is somewhat limited (please correct me if I have overlooked a key innovation). In some respects, this work represents a step back from previous efforts, which optimized for perceptual metrics; here, the authors utilize mean squared error (MSE). However, the results demonstrate a clear improvement over JPEG 2000, and to the best of my knowledge, no other learned encoding scheme has achieved comparable performance. The paper is exceptionally well written and clear.

I believe there is an error in Equation 10. The partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ). This would imply that the approach is not equivalent to a variational autoencoder when using non-Euclidean metrics.

I am curious about the rationale for optimizing MSE instead of a perceptual metric, as was done in prior work. Given the authors’ expertise, I find it surprising that the evaluation was limited to PSNR.

It would be valuable to understand the contribution of adaptive entropy coding relative to the impact of deeper encoders and decoders. This seems crucial, and it would be insightful to see performance results without adaptation, mirroring the setup of the previous paper. More details regarding the adaptive coder and its effects are needed. I would be happy to consider a higher score if the authors could provide this information.
