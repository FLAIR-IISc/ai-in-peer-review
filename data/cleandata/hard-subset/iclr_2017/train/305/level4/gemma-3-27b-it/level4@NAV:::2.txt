This work builds upon existing rate-distortion optimization techniques by applying them to deep neural networks, specifically extending the framework to deep encoders and decoders and incorporating adaptive entropy coding instead of a simpler entropy scheme. The authors also explore connections between their approach and variational autoencoders.

While the underlying rate-distortion optimization methodology has been previously published, the novelty of this submission is incremental. Furthermore, the shift from optimization based on perceptual metrics in prior work to the use of MSE here could be seen as a regression. However, the demonstrated performance gains over JPEG 2000 are substantial, and to the best of my knowledge, no other learned compression method has achieved comparable results. The manuscript is exceptionally well-written and clear.

I identified a potential error in Equation 10; the partition function appears to require dependence on g_s(y; theta), which would invalidate the equivalence to a VAE when using non-Euclidean metrics.

The rationale for prioritizing MSE optimization over the perceptual metrics used in the authors’ earlier work is unclear. Considering their expertise, the exclusive reliance on PSNR for evaluation is also somewhat surprising. 

Finally, disentangling the contribution of the adaptive entropy coding from the benefits derived from the deeper network architectures would strengthen the paper considerably. It would be valuable to see results without adaptive entropy coding, mirroring the prior work. More detailed analysis of the adaptive coder and its impact on performance is needed; addressing these points will warrant a more favorable assessment.
