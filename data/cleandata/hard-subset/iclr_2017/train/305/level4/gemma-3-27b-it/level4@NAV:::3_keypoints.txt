- End-to-end trained image compression system that outperforms existing algorithms (e.g., JPEG-2000) on bit-rate vs. quality.
- Demonstrates successful application of deep learning to image compression.
- Main technical contribution: a differentiable approximation to the rate function enabling effective training across rate–distortion trade-offs.
- This differentiable-rate idea could impact other fields that rely on differentiable approximations of related functions.
- Authors’ rebuttal: although a sufficiently complex network might learn to maximize entropy under fixed range/quantization, enforcing that directly would require a much more complex architecture.
- Specifically, matching current performance would need a network capable of reproducing the separate variable-rate encoder computations used for the quantization parameter q.
