- Presents an end-to-end optimized nonlinear-transform image compression method using cascaded conv layers and GDN nonlinearities.  
- Key contribution: joint optimization of analysis, synthesis, and an adaptive entropy code for rate–distortion performance.  
- Draws connections between the proposed model and variational autoencoders (VAEs).  
- Demonstrates superior rate–distortion performance and visual quality (MS-SSIM) compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000.  
- Thorough experiments, clear presentation, and online availability of test images and RD curves.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Novelty caveat: RD optimization not entirely new, but notable advance in fully-optimized deep compression networks.  
- Well-motivated move beyond separate optimization of transform, quantizer, and entropy coding.  
- Potential error in Equation 10: partition function likely depends on the synthesis transform, which may break VAE equivalence for non‑Euclidean metrics.  
- Unclear rationale for optimizing MSE instead of a perceptual metric given authors’ prior work.  
- Request to disentangle contributions of adaptive entropy coder versus deeper network—provide results without adaptive coding.  
- Ask for more details on adaptive coder implementation (context modeling, arithmetic coding) and comparison to entropy coding used in JPEG 2000.
