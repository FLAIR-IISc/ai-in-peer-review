- Presents an end-to-end trainable image compression system that outperforms JPEG-2000 on rate-distortion trade-offs.
- Introduces a differentiable approximation to the rate function enabling effective training under varying rate-distortion constraints.
- Demonstrates potential broader impact of the differentiable rate function for other tasks needing similar differentiable approximations.
- Authors answered pre-review concerns, arguing that enforcing entropy-maximizing behavior a priori would require a more complex architecture than using a separate variable-rate encoder for the quantization parameter q.
- Core autoencoder-based approach with a differentiable rate term is well-executed and conceptually sound.
- Experimental results on Lena and Kodak datasets are compelling, with visualizations showing superior reconstructed image quality across bitrates.
- Ablation studies, especially on different entropy models, provide useful design insights.
- Limited comparison to more recent learned compression methods (e.g., vector quantization or attention-based approaches).
- Insufficient detailed analysis of computational cost, including encoding and decoding times relative to JPEG-2000.
- Discussion of the entropy model is brief; more detail on its limitations and possible improvements is needed.
- Notational inconsistencies (notably the definition and role of q/quantization step size) reduce clarity.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with a valuable contribution; recommended as Strong Accept.
