- Proposes a compelling deep neural-network image compression method grounded in rate–distortion theory.
- Clear writing and a well-motivated framework that distinguishes it from prior work.
- Uses JPEG 2000 as a baseline for more rigorous comparison than prior studies that used only JPEG.
- Decision: Accept — novel, well-executed approach with superior objective and subjective performance.
- End-to-end optimized model combining rate–distortion concepts with cascaded convolutional layers and biologically inspired nonlinearities.
- Moves beyond MSE optimization and demonstrates visually superior results versus JPEG and JPEG 2000.
- Employs a continuous relaxation of quantization enabling effective gradient-based optimization; comparison to variational autoencoders adds context.
- Empirical results are promising but currently evaluated primarily on the Kodak dataset.
- Recommend expanding evaluation to standard benchmark images (e.g., Lena, Barbara, Baboon).
- Suggest broader comparisons to state-of-the-art classical codecs (e.g., BPG, WebP) that use prediction and entropy coding.
