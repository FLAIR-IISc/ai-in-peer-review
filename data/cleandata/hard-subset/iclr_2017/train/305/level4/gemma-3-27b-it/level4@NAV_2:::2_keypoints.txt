- Extends prior rate-distortion optimization to deep end-to-end encoders/decoders, adds adaptive entropy coding, and connects the approach to variational autoencoders.  
- Recommendation to accept despite incremental novelty due to strong empirical gains over JPEG and JPEG 2000.  
- Method is technically sound, clearly presented, and supported by extensive experiments.  
- Applying rate-distortion optimization to a deep trainable model with adaptive entropy coding is a meaningful advance.  
- Authors shifted from perceptual optimization to MSE; visual quality gains are supported by MS-SSIM but this choice warrants discussion.  
- Method consistently outperforms established codecs, indicating a tangible benefit to the field.  
- Paper is exceptionally well written and explains technical details clearly.  
- Equation 10 seems incorrect: the partition function should depend on g_s(y; theta), calling into question the VAE equivalence for non-Euclidean metrics.  
- Clarify derivation of Equation 10 and implications for the VAE relationship when using perceptual metrics.  
- Why optimize for MSE rather than a perceptual metric used in prior work, and why rely largely on PSNR for evaluation?  
- Need ablation comparing performance without adaptive entropy coding to isolate its contribution versus deeper encoder/decoder architecture.  
- Provide more details on the adaptive coder implementation, its performance impact, and any computational constraints that limited perceptual evaluation.
