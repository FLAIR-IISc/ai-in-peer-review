- Novel end-to-end learning method for image compression optimizing analysis, synthesis, and quantizer jointly for rate-distortion.
- Continuous relaxation of quantization enables gradient-based optimization.
- Achieves improved rate-distortion performance and notably better visual quality than JPEG and JPEG 2000.
- Use of Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN) as a biologically inspired nonlinearity.
- Evaluation on Kodak and standard test images shows consistent improvements, especially in MS-SSIM.
- Reports actual bitrates (not just entropy estimates), demonstrating practical viability.
- Theoretical connection to variational autoencoders offers useful context.
- Recommendation: accept — approach is well-motivated, results compelling, and paper is well-written.
- Suggested ablation study to quantify contributions of components (e.g., GDN vs. other nonlinearities, architectures).
- Request for more detailed discussion and timings on computational cost for training and compression/decompression.
- Suggestion to analyze sensitivity to the rate-distortion trade-off parameter λ.
- Recommend comparison to more recent learned image codecs to position the work within current literature.
- Question: details and compression efficiency comparison of the CABAC implementation versus JPEG 2000 entropy coding?
- Question: sensitivity of performance to the width of the uniform noise used in the quantization relaxation?
- Question: any preliminary results optimizing directly for perceptual metrics and associated tradeoffs?
- Question: rough estimate of training time for a typical dataset and network configuration?
- Question: were other network architectures tried and discarded, and why?
