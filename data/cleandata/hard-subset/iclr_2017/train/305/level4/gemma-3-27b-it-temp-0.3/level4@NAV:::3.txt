This paper presents a compelling end-to-end trained image compression and decompression system that outperforms existing algorithms, such as JPEG-2000, in terms of bit-rate versus quality. Beyond demonstrating the successful application of deep learning to image compression, a significant contribution lies in the development of a differentiable approximation to the rate function. This innovation enables effective training across a range of rate-distortion trade-offs and has the potential to impact fields beyond compression, specifically those benefiting from differentiable approximations of related functions. 

The authors’ response to my initial question was well-considered. While a sufficiently complex network *could* theoretically learn to maximize entropy within a fixed range and quantization, achieving comparable compression efficiency to the current system without imposing a specific output form would likely necessitate a significantly more complex network architecture – one capable of replicating the computations currently handled by the separate variable rate encoder used for storing 'q'.
