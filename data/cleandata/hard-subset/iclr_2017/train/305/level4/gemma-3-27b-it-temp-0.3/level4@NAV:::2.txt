This work builds upon existing rate-distortion optimization techniques by applying them to deep neural network architectures and incorporating adaptive entropy coding, moving beyond simpler entropy schemes. The authors also connect this approach to the framework of variational autoencoders.

While the underlying rate-distortion optimization methodology has been previously reported, the novelty of this submission is incremental. Notably, a previous iteration of this work leveraged perceptual metrics for optimization, whereas this paper utilizes MSE, which could be viewed as a regression in some respects. Nevertheless, the presented results demonstrate a clear performance gain compared to JPEG 2000, and to the best of my knowledge, no other learned compression scheme has achieved comparable results. The manuscript is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented.

I identified a potential error in Equation 10; the partition function appears to require dependence on g_s(y; theta). If this is the case, the method would not be equivalent to a VAE when employing non-Euclidean metrics. 

The rationale for shifting from a perceptual metric to MSE is unclear. Considering the authors’ expertise, the exclusive reliance on PSNR for evaluation is also somewhat surprising.

The individual contribution of the adaptive entropy coding component, relative to the impact of employing deeper encoders and decoders, remains unclear. Understanding this distinction is important, and a performance comparison without adaptive coding – mirroring the setup of the prior work – would be valuable. Further elaboration on the adaptive coder’s implementation and its specific effects on performance is needed; I would be pleased to reconsider my assessment with the inclusion of this information.
