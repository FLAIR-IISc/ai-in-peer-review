- Presents an end-to-end trained image compression/decompression system with superior bit-rate vs. quality trade-offs compared to algorithms like JPEG-2000.
- Key technical contribution: a differentiable approximation of the "rate" function enabling gradient-based training.
- Shows the differentiable rate can be used to train models with different rate–distortion trade-offs effectively.
- The differentiable rate idea is expected to have broader impact beyond image compression.
- Authors provided a thoughtful response to pre-review questions.
- Reviewer contends a sufficiently complex network trained to minimize distortion under fixed range/quantization could learn high-entropy codes that meet the upper bound.
- Reviewer accepts the authors' counterargument that forcing such a constraint would impose a specific compressor output structure, necessitating a more complex network to replicate the separate variable-rate encoder used to store q.
