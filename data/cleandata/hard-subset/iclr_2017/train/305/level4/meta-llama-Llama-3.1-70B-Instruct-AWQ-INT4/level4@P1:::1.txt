This is the most convincing paper on image compression with deep neural networks that I have read so far. The paper is very well written, and the use of rate-distortion theory in the objective fits smoothly into the framework. The paper is compared to a reasonable baseline (JPEG2000, as opposed to previous papers only considering JPEG). I would expect this paper to have a very good impact.

**Decision:** Accept

**Key reasons for this choice:** (1) The paper presents a well-motivated approach to image compression using deep neural networks, grounded in rate-distortion theory. (2) The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their method, including comparisons to a reasonable baseline (JPEG2000) and a demonstration of the visual quality of the compressed images.

**Supporting arguments for the reasons:**

* The paper provides a clear and well-motivated explanation of the rate-distortion theory and how it is used to optimize the neural network for image compression.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through a thorough evaluation, including comparisons to JPEG2000 and a demonstration of the visual quality of the compressed images.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Additional feedback:**

* A comparison to state-of-the-art methods in image compression, including both classical and deep learning-based approaches, would provide a clearer understanding of the positioning of the proposed method.
* Consider providing more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method, including training and testing times, to provide a more complete understanding of its practicality.

**Questions for the authors:**
