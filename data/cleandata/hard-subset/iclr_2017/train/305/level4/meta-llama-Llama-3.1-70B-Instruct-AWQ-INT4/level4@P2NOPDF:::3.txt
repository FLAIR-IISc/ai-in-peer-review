This paper presents a well-designed end-to-end trained image compression and decompression system, achieving better trade-offs between bit-rate and image quality compared to established algorithms like JPEG-2000. A key contribution of the paper is the introduction of a differentiable "rate" function, enabling effective training with various rate-distortion trade-offs. This innovation is expected to have a broader impact, extending beyond image compression to other tasks that can benefit from differentiable approximations to similar functions.

The authors provided a thoughtful response to my pre-review question. However, I still argue that a sufficiently complex network, when optimized to minimize distortion under a fixed range and quantization, would automatically learn to produce codes within that range with the highest possible entropy, effectively meeting the upper bound. Nevertheless, the authors' second argument is convincing: by doing so, it would impose a specific form on the compressor output, requiring a more complex network to replicate the effective compression currently achieved with the aid of a separate variable rate encoder for storing q.
