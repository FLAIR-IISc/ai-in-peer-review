This paper extends an existing approach to rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders and decoders, and upgrades from a simple entropy encoding scheme to adaptive entropy coding. Additionally, the paper explores the relationship between this approach and variational autoencoders.

Given that the underlying approach to rate-distortion optimization has already been published, the novelty of this submission is arguably limited. In some respects, this paper can be seen as a step backward, as the earlier work optimized for a perceptual metric, whereas this paper uses mean squared error (MSE). Nevertheless, the results demonstrate a visible improvement over JPEG 2000, and the reviewer is not aware of any other learned encoding method that has achieved this level of performance. The paper is well-written and clear in its presentation.

However, there appears to be an error in Equation 10, as the partition function seems to be missing its dependence on g_s(y; theta). This omission suggests that the approach may not be equivalent to a variational autoencoder (VAE) for non-Euclidean metrics.

A question arises regarding the choice of optimizing MSE rather than a perceptual metric, as was done in previous work. Given the authors' backgrounds, it is surprising that the evaluation was only performed in terms of peak signal-to-noise ratio (PSNR). The authors may want to provide additional insight into this design decision.

It would also be beneficial to understand the contribution of adaptive entropy coding compared to the effect of deeper encoders and decoders. This seems like a crucial piece of information, and it would be interesting to see the performance without adaptation, as in the previous paper. Providing more details on the adaptive coder and its effects would strengthen the paper, and the reviewer would be willing to consider a higher score if the authors address this point.
