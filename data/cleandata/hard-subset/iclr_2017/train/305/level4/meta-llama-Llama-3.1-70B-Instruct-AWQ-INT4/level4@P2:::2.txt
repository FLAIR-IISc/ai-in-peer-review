This paper extends an approach to rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders and decoders, and from a simple entropy encoding scheme to adaptive entropy coding. In addition, the paper discusses the approach's relationship to variational autoencoders.

**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**
The paper presents an end-to-end optimized image compression method based on nonlinear transform coding. The authors develop a framework for jointly optimizing the analysis and synthesis transforms, as well as the entropy code, for rate-distortion performance. They also discuss the relationship between their approach and variational autoencoders.

**Decision and Key Reasons**
I am inclined to accept this paper, but with some reservations. The key reasons for my decision are:

1. The paper presents a visible improvement over JPEG 2000 in terms of rate-distortion performance.
2. The approach is well-motivated and well-placed in the literature, building upon previous work on rate-distortion optimization.

**Supporting Arguments**
The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the approach and its relationship to variational autoencoders. The experimental results demonstrate a significant improvement over JPEG 2000, which is a widely used image compression standard. However, I do have some concerns regarding the novelty of the approach, as it builds upon previous work.

**Additional Feedback**
To improve the paper, I would suggest addressing the following points:

1. Clarify the novelty of the approach, as it seems to build upon previous work.
2. Provide more detail on the adaptive entropy coding scheme and its effects on the performance.
3. Consider optimizing for a perceptual metric, as was done in previous work, and evaluate the performance using metrics other than PSNR.
4. Equation 10 appears to be incorrect, and the partition function should depend on g_s(y; theta). This would mean that the approach is not equivalent to a VAE for non-Euclidean metrics.

**Questions for the Authors**
1. What was the reason for optimizing MSE rather than a perceptual metric as in previous work?
2. Can you provide more detail on the adaptive entropy coding scheme and its effects on the performance?
3. How does the performance of the approach change when optimizing for a perceptual metric rather than MSE?

Overall, the paper presents a significant improvement over JPEG 2000, but could benefit from further clarification and detail on the approach and its relationship to previous work.
