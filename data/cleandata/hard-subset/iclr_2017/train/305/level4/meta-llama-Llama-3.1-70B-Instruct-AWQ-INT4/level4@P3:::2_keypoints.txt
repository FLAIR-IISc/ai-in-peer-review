- Presents an end-to-end optimized image compression method using nonlinear transforms, deep encoders/decoders, and adaptive entropy coding, related to VAEs.
- Claims better rate-distortion performance than JPEG and JPEG 2000 across most images and bit rates.
- Shows substantial perceptual quality improvements (MS-SSIM) and more natural-looking compressed images versus JPEG/JPEG 2000.
- Paper is well-written and results are visually and quantitatively impressive.
- Novelty is limited because the core rate-distortion optimization approach has been published before.
- Surprising choice to optimize MSE rather than a perceptual metric given the authors' prior work.
- Equation 10 appears incorrect; partition function should depend on g_s(y; theta), affecting VAE equivalence for non-Euclidean metrics.
- Contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoders/decoders is unclear and needs isolation.
- Authors should provide more detail on the adaptive entropy coding scheme and its impact on results.
- Include experiments comparing performance with and without adaptation to separate contributions.
- Clarify and correct Equation 10 and the relationship to VAEs for non-Euclidean metrics.
- Explain the rationale for optimizing MSE and consider comparisons using a perceptual metric.
