This paper extends a rate-distortion optimization approach to deep encoders and decoders and upgrades a simple entropy encoding scheme to an adaptive entropy coding scheme. Additionally, it explores the relationship between this approach and variational autoencoders.

While the rate-distortion optimization approach itself is not new, having been previously published, the submission's novelty is somewhat limited. Furthermore, the current work can be seen as a step backward, as it optimizes for mean squared error (MSE) rather than a perceptual metric, as was done in earlier work. Nevertheless, the results show a noticeable improvement over JPEG 2000, and, to my knowledge, no other learned encoding has achieved this level of performance. The paper is well-written and clear in its presentation.

However, there appears to be an error in Equation 10, as the partition function should likely depend on g_s(y; theta). This discrepancy suggests that the approach may not be equivalent to a variational autoencoder for non-Euclidean metrics.

It would be beneficial for the authors to explain their decision to optimize for MSE rather than a perceptual metric, as was done in their previous work. Given the authors' backgrounds, it is surprising that the evaluation was only performed in terms of peak signal-to-noise ratio (PSNR).

To better understand the contributions of this work, it would be helpful to know the impact of adaptive entropy coding compared to the effects of deeper encoders and decoders. A comparison of the performance without adaptation, similar to the previous paper, would provide valuable insight. Additional details on the adaptive coder and its effects would also be appreciated, and providing this information could potentially lead to a higher score.
