**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

This paper proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression method based on nonlinear transforms. The method consists of a nonlinear analysis transformation, a uniform quantizer, and a nonlinear synthesis transformation. The transforms are constructed in three successive stages of convolutional linear filters and nonlinear activation functions, inspired by models of biological neurons. The authors jointly optimize the entire model for rate-distortion performance over a database of training images using stochastic gradient descent. The paper claims to achieve better rate-distortion performance than standard JPEG and JPEG 2000 compression methods, as well as improved visual quality.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

I recommend accepting this paper, with two key reasons:

1. The paper presents a well-motivated and well-placed approach in the literature, leveraging rate-distortion theory to optimize image compression with deep neural networks.
2. The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, comparing it to reasonable baselines (JPEG 2000) and demonstrating its effectiveness in achieving better rate-distortion performance and improved visual quality.

**Supporting Arguments for the Decision**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The use of rate-distortion theory in the objective function is a natural fit for the problem of image compression, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness in achieving better performance. The evaluation is thorough, and the authors provide a fair comparison to JPEG 2000, which is a more challenging baseline than JPEG.
