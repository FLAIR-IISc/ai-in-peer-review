This paper presents a compelling end-to-end trained image compression and decompression system, outperforming established algorithms such as JPEG-2000 in terms of bit-rate versus quality trade-offs. A significant contribution of this work is not only demonstrating the efficacy of deep learning in a novel application but also introducing a differentiable "rate" function, which enables effective training under various rate-distortion trade-offs. This innovation is expected to have far-reaching implications, extending beyond image compression to other tasks that could benefit from differentiable approximations of similar functions.
