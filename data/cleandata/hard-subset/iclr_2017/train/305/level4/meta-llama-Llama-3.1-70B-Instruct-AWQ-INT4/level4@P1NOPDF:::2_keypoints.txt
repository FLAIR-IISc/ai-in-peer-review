- Paper extends existing rate–distortion optimization to deep encoders/decoders and replaces simple entropy coding with adaptive entropy coding; also explores links to VAEs.
- Novelty is limited because the underlying rate–distortion approach was previously published.
- In some respects the work is a step backward by optimizing MSE instead of a perceptual metric used in earlier work.
- Results show visible improvement over JPEG 2000 and appear to be the best-known learned method at this performance level.
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.
- Equation 10 appears incorrect: the partition function is missing dependence on g_s(y; θ), casting doubt on the claimed VAE equivalence for non‑Euclidean metrics.
- Reviewer questions the choice to optimize MSE/PSNR rather than a perceptual metric and asks for authors’ rationale given their backgrounds.
- Reviewer requests clarification of the contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoder/decoder depth, and performance results without adaptation.
- More details on the adaptive coder and its effects are needed; addressing this could lead the reviewer to raise the score.
