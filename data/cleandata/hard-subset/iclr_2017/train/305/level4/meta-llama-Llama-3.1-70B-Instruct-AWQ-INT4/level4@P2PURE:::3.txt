This paper presents a well-designed end-to-end trained image compression and decompression system, achieving superior bit-rate versus quality trade-offs compared to established image compression algorithms, such as JPEG-2000. A key contribution of this work is not only demonstrating the efficacy of deep learning in a new application, but also introducing a differentiable version of the "rate" function. The authors successfully show that this differentiable rate function can be used for effective training with various rate-distortion trade-offs. This innovation is expected to have a broader impact, extending beyond image compression to other tasks that may benefit from differentiable approximations to similar functions.

The authors provided a thoughtful response to my pre-review question. However, I still argue that a sufficiently complex network, when trained to minimize distortion under a fixed range and quantization, would automatically learn to produce codes within a fixed range with the highest possible entropy, thereby meeting the upper bound. Nevertheless, the authors' second argument is convincing: imposing such a constraint would force a specific structure on the compressor output, which would require a more complex network to replicate the computations currently handled by a separate variable rate encoder used to store q.
