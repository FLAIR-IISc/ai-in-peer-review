This paper presents an end-to-end learning method for image compression that optimizes for rate-distortion performance. By cleverly relaxing the quantization step, the authors are able to learn an efficient image compression method by optimizing over a database of natural images. The results are impressive, and the analysis is thorough.

I recommend acceptance of this paper. The method is interesting, and the results are compelling. The authors have done a good job of evaluating their method against standard image compression algorithms, such as JPEG and JPEG 2000, and have demonstrated its superiority in terms of both rate-distortion performance and visual quality.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the method and its underlying principles. The authors have also done a good job of providing additional details and explanations in the appendix, which should be helpful for readers who want to delve deeper into the method.

One potential criticism of the paper is that it does not provide a clear explanation of how the method can be extended to other types of data, such as video or audio. However, this is a minor quibble, and the authors have already demonstrated the effectiveness of their method on a challenging task.

Overall, I think this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of image compression. I recommend acceptance.

Additional feedback:

* The authors may want to consider providing more details on how the method can be implemented in practice, such as the computational resources required and the time complexity of the algorithm.
* The authors may also want to consider providing more analysis on the robustness of the method to different types of noise or distortions.
* The paper could benefit from more visualizations of the compressed images, to help illustrate the quality of the method.

Questions for the authors:

* Can you provide more details on how the method can be extended to other types of data, such as video or audio?
* How does the method perform on images with different types of noise or distortions?
* Can you provide more information on the computational resources required to implement the method in practice?
