This paper extends an existing approach to rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders and decoders, and from a simple entropy encoding scheme to adaptive entropy coding. Additionally, the paper discusses the approach's relationship to variational autoencoders.

Given that the approach to rate-distortion optimization has already been published, the novelty of this submission is arguably not very high. In some ways, this paper even represents a step backward, since earlier work optimized for a perceptual metric, whereas here Mean Squared Error (MSE) is used. However, the results show a visible improvement over JPEG 2000, and the reviewer is not aware of any other learned encoding that has achieved this level of performance. The paper is well-written.

There appears to be an error in Equation 10; the partition function should likely depend on g_s(y; theta). This would imply that the approach is not equivalent to a Variational Autoencoder (VAE) for non-Euclidean metrics.

It would be beneficial to understand the reason for optimizing MSE rather than a perceptual metric, as was done in previous work. Given the authors' backgrounds, it is surprising that the evaluation was only performed in terms of Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio (PSNR).

The contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus the effect of deeper encoders and decoders is unclear. This information seems crucial, and it would be interesting to see the performance without adaptation, as in the previous paper. More details on the adaptive coder and its effects should be provided. If the authors can provide this information, the reviewer would be happy to reconsider the score.
