This paper presents the most convincing work on image compression using deep neural networks that I have come across to date. The writing is clear and concise, and the incorporation of rate-distortion theory into the objective function is seamlessly integrated into the framework. A notable strength of the paper is its comparison to a reasonable baseline, namely JPEG2000, which is a more comprehensive evaluation than the JPEG-only comparisons found in previous works.

I anticipate that this paper will have a significant impact in the field. To further strengthen the manuscript, I suggest including results on the Lena, Barbara, and Baboon images, in addition to comparisons with state-of-the-art methods that employ more classical approaches. It is essential to clearly position the neural network-based methods in relation to the best previous techniques, as this information is not readily apparent from the submitted version.

Providing a more comprehensive evaluation will enable readers to better understand the relative merits of both categories of methods. Specifically, I would like to see a comparison with classical methods, such as those mentioned in my previous questions. By including these additional results and comparisons, the authors can more effectively demonstrate the advantages and limitations of their approach, ultimately leading to a more impactful and influential paper.
