This paper presents an end-to-end optimized image compression method based on nonlinear transforms. The approach extends the authors' previous work on rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders and decoders, and from a simple entropy encoding scheme to adaptive entropy coding. The paper also discusses the approach's relationship to variational autoencoders.

The authors claim to have developed a complete image compression method that offers improvements in rate-distortion performance over JPEG and JPEG 2000 for most images and bit rates. The compressed images are also shown to be more natural in appearance than those compressed with JPEG or JPEG 2000, with substantial improvements in perceptual quality as estimated with the MS-SSIM index.

The paper is well-written, and the results are impressive, with visible improvements over JPEG 2000. However, the novelty of this submission is arguably not very high, given that the approach to rate-distortion optimization has already been published. The use of mean squared error (MSE) as a distortion metric, rather than a perceptual metric as in previous work, is also surprising, especially given the authors' backgrounds.

I recommend accepting this paper, but with some revisions to address the following concerns:

1. Equation 10 appears to be incorrect, and the partition function should depend on g_s(y; theta). This would mean that the approach is not equivalent to a VAE for non-Euclidean metrics.
2. The authors should provide a clear explanation for why they chose to optimize MSE rather than a perceptual metric, as in their previous work.
3. The contribution of adaptive entropy coding versus the effect of deeper encoders and decoders is not clear. The authors should provide more detail on the adaptive coder and its effects, and consider including a comparison with the performance without adaptation.

To improve the paper, I suggest the authors:

* Provide more detail on the adaptive entropy coding scheme and its effects on the results.
* Include a comparison with the performance without adaptation to separate the contributions of the deeper encoders and decoders from the adaptive entropy coding.
* Address the potential error in Equation 10 and clarify the relationship between the approach and VAEs for non-Euclidean metrics.
* Provide a clear explanation for the choice of MSE as a distortion metric, and consider including a comparison with a perceptual metric.

Questions for the authors:

* Can you provide more detail on the adaptive entropy coding scheme and its effects on the results?
* How does the performance of the method change when using a perceptual metric instead of MSE?
* Can you clarify the relationship between the approach and VAEs for non-Euclidean metrics, and address the potential error in Equation 10?
* What is the reason for optimizing MSE rather than a perceptual metric, as in previous work?
