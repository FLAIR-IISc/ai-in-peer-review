- Proposes a novel end-to-end optimized image compression method using nonlinear transforms and a continuous proxy for quantizer-induced discontinuity.  
- Jointly optimizes the model for rate-distortion performance over a training image database.  
- Shows dramatic visual-quality improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000 at all bit rates, supported by MS-SSIM objective estimates.  
- Recommends acceptance based on novelty/effectiveness and thorough evaluation/comparisons.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and well-situated in related work (including links to variational autoencoders).  
- Evaluation is comprehensive and demonstrates better rate-distortion performance for most test images.  
- Suggests adding optimization details: hyperparameter choices and convergence behavior.  
- Requests discussion of computational complexity: training/testing time and memory requirements.  
- Recommends additional comparisons to other codecs (e.g., JPEG‑XR, WebP).  
- Asks for more details on the perceptual metric implementation and parameters used.  
- Questions asking for clarification on GDN transform choice and optimization.  
- Questions about robustness across image types with varying texture and detail.  
- Requests details on the entropy coding implementation and its parameters.
