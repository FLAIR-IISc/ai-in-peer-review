This paper extends a rate-distortion optimization approach to deep encoders and decoders, and upgrades a simple entropy encoding scheme to an adaptive entropy coding scheme. Additionally, it explores the relationship between this approach and variational autoencoders.

While the rate-distortion optimization approach itself has been previously published, the novelty of this submission may be limited (please correct me if I have overlooked any new insights). In some respects, this paper appears to take a step back by optimizing for mean squared error (MSE), whereas earlier work targeted a perceptual metric. Nevertheless, the results demonstrate a noticeable improvement over JPEG 2000, and the authors have achieved a level of performance that is unmatched by other learned encoding methods. The paper is well-written.

However, there appears to be an error in Equation 10, as the partition function should seemingly depend on g_s(y; theta). This discrepancy suggests that the proposed approach may not be equivalent to a variational autoencoder (VAE) for non-Euclidean metrics.

It would be beneficial to understand the motivation behind optimizing for MSE rather than a perceptual metric, as was done in previous work. Given the authors' backgrounds, it is surprising that the evaluation was only performed in terms of peak signal-to-noise ratio (PSNR).

The contribution of adaptive entropy coding, as opposed to the impact of deeper encoders and decoders, is unclear. It would be valuable to see the performance without adaptive entropy coding, similar to the previous paper. Providing more details about the adaptive coder and its effects would be beneficial, and addressing this point could lead to a higher score.
