- Proposes a novel image compression method using nonlinear transforms jointly optimized for rate-distortion.
- Method uses a cascade of convolutional linear filters and nonlinear activations inspired by biological neurons.
- Demonstrates better rate-distortion performance and visually superior results compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000.
- Well-motivated and grounded in rate-distortion theory.
- Clear, concise explanation of the method with a range of supporting experiments.
- Significant contribution showing nonlinear transforms can outperform traditional linear methods.
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and provides a thorough literature review situating the work.
- Experiments are well-presented with multiple examples and method comparisons.
- Reviewer recommends accepting the paper.
- Suggestion to include more comparisons to state-of-the-art and classical compression methods.
- Suggestion to report computational resources required for training and testing.
- Request for more optimization details (hyperparameters, number of iterations).
- Request for more visualizations of compressed images at different bit rates.
- Suggestion to evaluate performance on other image types (e.g., medical, scientific).
- Suggestion to discuss potential applications (image storage, transmission).
