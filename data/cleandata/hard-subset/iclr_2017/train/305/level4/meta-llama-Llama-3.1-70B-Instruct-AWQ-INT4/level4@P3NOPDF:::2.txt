This paper proposes an extension of a rate-distortion optimization approach to deep encoders and decoders, as well as an upgrade from a simple entropy encoding scheme to adaptive entropy coding. Additionally, the authors discuss the relationship between this approach and variational autoencoders.

While the rate-distortion optimization method has been previously published, the novelty of this submission may be considered limited, as it primarily builds upon existing work. In some respects, this paper can be seen as a step backward, as it optimizes for Mean Squared Error (MSE) instead of a perceptual metric, which was used in earlier work. Nevertheless, the results demonstrate a notable improvement over JPEG 2000, and the reviewer is not aware of any other learned encoding method that has achieved this level of performance. The paper is well-written and clear in its presentation.

However, there appears to be an error in Equation 10, as the partition function seems to be missing its dependence on g_s(y; theta). This discrepancy suggests that the proposed approach may not be equivalent to a Variational Autoencoder (VAE) for non-Euclidean metrics.

The reviewer would like to inquire about the motivation behind optimizing for MSE instead of a perceptual metric, as was done in previous work. Given the authors' backgrounds, it is surprising that the evaluation was only performed in terms of Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio (PSNR). A more in-depth explanation for this choice would be beneficial.

Furthermore, it would be valuable to understand the contribution of adaptive entropy coding compared to the impact of deeper encoders and decoders. A comparison of the performance without adaptation, as in the previous paper, would provide insight into the effectiveness of the adaptive coder. Additional details on the adaptive coder and its effects would be appreciated, and the reviewer would be willing to consider a higher score if the authors provide this information.

Overall, while the paper presents some interesting results, it would benefit from addressing the mentioned concerns and providing more clarity on the contributions of the proposed approach.
