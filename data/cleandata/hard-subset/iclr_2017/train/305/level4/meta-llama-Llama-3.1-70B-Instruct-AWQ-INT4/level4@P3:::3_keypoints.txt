- Proposes end-to-end optimized image compression using nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms and jointly optimized entropy coding for improved rate-distortion trade-offs.
- Trains the model with a stochastic gradient descent variant using a continuous proxy to handle the quantizer’s discontinuous loss.
- Approach is well-motivated by biological neuron models and prior Gaussianizing image-density work.
- Provides a thorough comparison and discussion of how the nonlinear transform coding framework relates to, and differs from, variational autoencoders.
- Introduces a differentiable approximation of the rate function, enabling effective gradient-based training across different rate-distortion points.
- Presents extensive experiments against JPEG and JPEG 2000 showing generally better rate-distortion performance and markedly improved visual quality at low bit rates.
- Paper is judged a strong contribution with potential broader impact for applications needing differentiable approximations.
- Requests a more detailed analysis of computational complexity and comparison to established compression algorithms.
- Requests more insight into optimization details: hyperparameter selection and convergence behavior.
- Suggests evaluating robustness across diverse image types and considering extensions to other data modalities (e.g., video or audio).
