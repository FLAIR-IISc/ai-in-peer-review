- Presents an end-to-end trained image compression/decompression system that achieves better bit-rate vs. image-quality trade-offs than JPEG-2000.  
- Introduces a differentiable "rate" function that enables effective training across different rate–distortion trade-offs.  
- The differentiable rate function is expected to have broader impact beyond image compression for tasks needing differentiable approximations.  
- Authors gave a thoughtful response to the reviewer’s pre-review question.  
- Reviewer argues a sufficiently complex network optimized for distortion under fixed range/quantization could learn high-entropy codes that meet the upper bound.  
- Reviewer concedes the authors’ counterargument: forcing that code form would require a more complex network, while using a separate variable-rate encoder for storing q achieves effective compression more feasibly.
