- End-to-end trained image compression/decompression system achieves superior bitrate-vs-quality trade-offs versus established algorithms (e.g., JPEG-2000).
- Demonstrates the efficacy of deep learning in the image compression domain.
- Introduces a differentiable approximation of the "rate" function enabling training across variable rate-distortion trade-offs.
- Differentiable rate function is a key innovation with potential impact beyond image compression for tasks needing differentiable approximations.
- Reviewer posed a pre-review concern about whether a sufficiently complex network could meet entropy/range constraints; authors provided a thoughtful response.
- Authors’ counterargument: not enforcing a fixed range avoids restricting how compressor outputs are used and sidesteps needing a more complex network to replicate separate variable-rate encoding.
- Matching the proposed system’s effective compression would otherwise require a more complex network to perform computations handled by a separate encoder storing q.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution demonstrating deep learning’s potential for superior compression performance and broader methodological implications.
