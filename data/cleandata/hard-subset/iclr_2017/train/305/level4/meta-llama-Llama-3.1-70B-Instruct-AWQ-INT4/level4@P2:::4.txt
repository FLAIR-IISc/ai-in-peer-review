This paper presents a well-written and thorough approach to end-to-end learning for image compression. The authors propose an optimization method for rate-distortion performance, utilizing a clever relaxation technique to learn an efficient image compression model from a database of natural images.

The specific question tackled by the paper is the optimization of image compression methods using a nonlinear transform coding framework. The approach is well-motivated, building upon existing literature in the field, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their method.

The paper supports its claims through a comprehensive analysis of the proposed method, including experimental results and comparisons to existing image compression methods, such as JPEG and JPEG 2000. The authors demonstrate that their method generally exhibits better rate-distortion performance and a significant improvement in visual quality, as measured by the MS-SSIM index.

I recommend acceptance of this paper. The method is interesting, the results are thorough, and the analysis is well-done.

Some minor points for the authors to consider:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the relationship between the proposed method and existing generative models, such as variational autoencoders.
* The authors may want to provide more insight into the choice of the generalized divisive normalization (GDN) transform and its parameters.
* It would be helpful to include more examples of compressed images to further illustrate the visual quality improvements achieved by the proposed method.

Questions for the authors:

* Can you provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly in terms of training and inference time?
* How do you plan to extend the proposed method to other types of data, such as video or audio?
* Have you considered using other distortion metrics, such as SSIM or VIF, to evaluate the performance of the proposed method?
