This paper builds upon an existing rate-distortion optimization method by applying it to deep encoders and decoders and enhancing the entropy encoding scheme to adaptive entropy coding. Additionally, the authors examine the connection between their approach and variational autoencoders.

While the underlying rate-distortion optimization technique has been previously published, the novelty of this submission may be considered limited (please clarify if I have overlooked any new developments). In some respects, this work appears to be a step back, as it utilizes MSE, whereas earlier research optimized for a perceptual metric. Nevertheless, the results demonstrate a noticeable improvement over JPEG 2000, and I am unaware of any other learned encoding method that has achieved comparable performance. The paper is exceptionally well-written.

However, Equation 10 seems to be incorrect, as I believe the partition function should be dependent on g_s(y; theta), indicating that the approach is not equivalent to a VAE for non-Euclidean metrics.

I would appreciate it if the authors could provide insight into their decision to optimize MSE instead of a perceptual metric, as was done in previous work. Given the authors' backgrounds, it is surprising that the evaluation was only conducted in terms of PSNR.

It would be beneficial to understand the contribution of adaptive entropy coding compared to the impact of deeper encoders and decoders. A comparison of the performance without adaptation, as in the previous paper, would be valuable. More details on the adaptive coder and its effects should be provided. If the authors can address this, I would be willing to consider a higher score.
