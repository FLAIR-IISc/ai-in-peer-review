- Paper presents an end-to-end trained image compression system that outperforms established codecs (e.g., JPEG-2000) on rate–quality trade-offs.  
- Main technical contribution is a differentiable approximation of the "rate" function enabling effective training for different rate–distortion trade-offs.  
- The differentiable rate formulation could have broader impact for tasks needing differentiable approximations of similar functions.  
- Authors gave a thoughtful response to the reviewer’s pre-review question.  
- Reviewer notes a counterargument: a sufficiently complex network might automatically produce fixed-range, maximum-entropy codes and thus minimize distortion under fixed quantization.  
- Reviewer is convinced by the authors’ second argument that forcing that form would require a far more complex network to replicate the separate variable-rate encoder’s computations, so the proposed approach is preferable.
