- Well-written paper demonstrating an end-to-end trained image compression/decompression system that outperforms established algorithms (e.g., JPEG-2000) on bit-rate vs. quality trade-offs.
- Major contribution: a differentiable approximation of the "rate" function enabling training for different rate–distortion trade-offs.
- The differentiable rate formulation is likely to impact other tasks that can use differentiable approximations of similar functions.
- Authors provided a thoughtful response to a pre-review question.
- Critique: under fixed range and quantization, a sufficiently complex network could learn high-entropy codes and meet the upper bound on distortion minimization.
- Concession: authors’ second argument is convincing—forcing a specific code form would require a more complex network to replicate the variable-rate encoder’s computations used in the current system.
