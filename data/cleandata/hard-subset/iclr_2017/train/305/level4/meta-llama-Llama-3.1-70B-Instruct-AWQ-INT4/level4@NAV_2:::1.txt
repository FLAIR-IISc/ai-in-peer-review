This paper presents a novel approach to image compression using nonlinear transforms, which are jointly optimized for rate-distortion performance. The method is based on a cascade of convolutional linear filters and nonlinear activation functions, inspired by models of biological neurons. The authors demonstrate that their approach outperforms standard image compression methods such as JPEG and JPEG 2000 in terms of rate-distortion performance, and also produces visually more pleasing results.
