This paper presents a well-written and innovative end-to-end learning method for image compression. By optimizing for rate-distortion performance and utilizing a clever relaxation technique, the proposed method is able to learn an efficient image compression approach by optimizing over a database of natural images.

The method itself is interesting, and the results are compelling. The authors provide a thorough analysis of their approach, which further strengthens their claims. Overall, the quality of the submission is high, making it easy to recommend acceptance.

However, to provide a more comprehensive review, I would like to see a few aspects expanded upon or clarified. Firstly, it would be beneficial to include a more detailed comparison with existing image compression methods, highlighting the advantages and limitations of the proposed approach. Additionally, the authors may want to consider providing more insights into the relaxation technique used, as it appears to be a crucial component of their method.

Minor suggestions for improvement include providing more visualizations of the compressed images, to help illustrate the quality of the results. Furthermore, some of the mathematical notation could be clarified or expanded upon, to ensure that the reader can easily follow the derivations.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel approach to image compression, which is a significant contribution to the field. The authors have demonstrated the effectiveness of their method on a database of natural images, and the results are promising.

The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise. The authors have done an excellent job of explaining the technical details of their approach, making the paper accessible to a wide range of readers.

Overall, I believe that this paper has the potential to make a significant impact in the field of image compression, and I strongly recommend its acceptance for publication at ICLR 2017.
