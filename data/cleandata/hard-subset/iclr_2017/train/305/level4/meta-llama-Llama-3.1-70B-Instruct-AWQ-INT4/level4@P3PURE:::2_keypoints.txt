- Extends prior rate–distortion optimization work to deep encoders/decoders and adaptive entropy coding; relates approach to VAEs.
- Novelty is limited since the core rate–distortion approach was previously published.
- Using MSE (PSNR) instead of a perceptual metric is a regression relative to earlier work that optimized perceptual quality.
- Results visibly outperform JPEG 2000 and may be the best learned encoding performance the reviewer knows.
- Paper is well-written.
- Equation 10 likely contains an error: the partition function should depend on g_s(y; θ).
- That error implies the method may not be equivalent to a VAE for non‑Euclidean metrics.
- The choice to optimize MSE and evaluate only with PSNR needs justification given the authors’ backgrounds.
- The separate contributions of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoders/decoders are unclear and require ablation (performance without adaptation).
- More details on the adaptive coder and its specific effects should be provided; reviewer would reconsider the score if these are supplied.
