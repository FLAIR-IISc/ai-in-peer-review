- End-to-end trained image compression/decompression system that outperforms JPEG-2000 on bit-rate vs. quality.
- Introduces a differentiable "rate" function enabling effective training across rate–distortion trade-offs.
- Innovation likely extends beyond image compression to other tasks needing differentiable approximations.
- Authors’ response to the reviewer’s initial inquiry was thorough.
- Reviewer contends a sufficiently complex network minimizing distortion with fixed range and quantization would yield maximum-entropy codes, reaching the upper bound.
- Authors’ persuasive counter: that strategy would impose a specific compressor output structure and require a much more complex network, whereas a separate variable-rate encoder for storing q enables more efficient compression.
