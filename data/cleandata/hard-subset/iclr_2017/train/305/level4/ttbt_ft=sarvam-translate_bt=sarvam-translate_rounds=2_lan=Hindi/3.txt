This is a good research paper that demonstrates an end-to-end trained image compression and decompression system that achieves a better bit-rate versus quality trade-off compared to established image compression algorithms (such as JPEG-2000). In addition to showing the effectiveness of 'deep learning' for a new application, a significant contribution of this paper is introducing a differential version of the "rate" function, which the authors have shown can be used for effective training with various rate-distortion trade-offs. I hope that its impact will extend beyond compression applications to other tasks that can benefit from differential estimates of similar functions.

The authors thoughtfully answered my pre-review question. I would still argue that, under a certain limit and quantization, a sufficiently complex network will automatically learn to generate a code within a certain limit with the highest possible entropy (i.e., it will meet the upper limit). But the second argument is reassuring – doing so imposes a specific "shape" on the use of the compressor output, which, to match the effective compression of current systems, would require a more complex network capable of performing the calculations being done by a different variable rate encoder currently being used to store the Q.
