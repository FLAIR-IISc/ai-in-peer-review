This paper elaborates on the rate-distortion optimization approach for deep encoders and decoders, ranging from a simple entropy encoding scheme to adaptive entropy coding. Additionally, the paper discusses the relationship of the approach with variable auto-encoders.

Given that the rate-distortion optimization approach has already been published, the novelty of this submission is perhaps not significant (correct me if I've missed any new trick). In some ways, this paper also takes a step back, as previous work had optimized for a perceptual metric using MSE. However, the results are clear improvements over JPEG 2000, and I am unaware of any other learned encoding that has been shown to achieve this level of performance. This paper is very well written.

Equation 10 seems incorrect, and I believe the partition function should depend on g_s (y; theta). This would mean that this approach is not equivalent to VAE for non-Euclidean metrics.

Like in the previous work, what was the reason for optimizing MSE instead of a perceptual metric? Given the author's background, it's surprising that the evaluation was also done only in terms of PSNR.

What is the contribution of adaptive entropy coding compared to the impact of deep encoders and decoders? This seems like an important piece of information, so it would be interesting to see the performance without optimization, as was the case in the previous paper. More information should be provided about the adaptive coder and its effects, and I would be happy to give high marks when the author does so.
