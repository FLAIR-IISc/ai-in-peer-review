This paper expands on the rate-distortion optimization method for deep encoders and decoders, extending it from a simple entropy encoding scheme to adaptive entropy coding. Additionally, the paper discusses the relationship of the method with variational autoenactors.

Since the rate-distortion optimization method has already been published, the novelty of this submission is not very high (correct me if I'm missing any new techniques). In some cases, this paper even represents a step backward, as previous work was optimized for a perceptual metric where MSE was used here. However, the results are a visible improvement over JPEG 2000, and I don't know of any other learned encoding that has been able to achieve this level of performance. The paper is very well written.

Equation 10 seems wrong, and I think the partition function should depend on g_s(y; theta). This means that this method is not equivalent to VAE for non-Euclidean matrices.

What was the reason for optimizing MSE instead of a perceptual metric in the previous work? Considering the author's background, it's surprising that even the evaluation was only done in terms of PSNR.

What is the contribution of adaptive entropy coding to the effect of deep encoders and decoders? This seems like an important piece of information, so it would be interesting to see performance without adaptation, as in the previous paper. More detailed information should be provided about the adaptive coder and its effects, and I would be happy to give a high score when the authors provide it.
