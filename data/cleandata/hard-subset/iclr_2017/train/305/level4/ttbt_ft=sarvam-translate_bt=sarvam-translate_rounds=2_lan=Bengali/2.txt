This paper expands on the rate-distortion optimization method for deep encoders and decoders, ranging from a simple entropy encoding scheme to adaptive entropy coding. Furthermore, the paper discusses the relationship of the method with variational autoenactors.

Since the rate-distortion optimization method has already been published, the novelty of this submission is not significant (correct me if I miss any new techniques). In some cases, this paper even represents a step back, as the previous work was optimized for a perception metric where MSE was used here. However, the results are a visible improvement over JPEG 2000, and I am not aware of any other learned encoding that has been able to achieve this level of performance. The paper is very well written.

Equation 10 seems incorrect, and I think the partition function should depend on g_s (y; theta). This means that this method is not equivalent to VAE for non-Euclidean matrices.

What was the reason for optimizing MSE instead of a perception metric in the previous work? Considering the author's background, even the evaluation was only done in terms of PSNR, which is surprising.

What is the contribution of adaptive entropy coding to the impact of deep encoders and decoders? This seems like an important piece of information, so it would be interesting to see the performance without adaptation, as was the case in the previous paper. More detailed information should be provided about the adaptive coder and its impact, and I would be happy to give a high score when the authors provide it.
