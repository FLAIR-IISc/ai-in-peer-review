- Presents an end-to-end trained image compression/decompression system that outperforms established algorithms (e.g., JPEG-2000) on bitrate–quality trade-offs.
- Main technical contribution is a differentiable "rate" function that enables effective training across various rate–distortion trade-offs.
- The differentiable rate approximation may have implications beyond compression for tasks needing similar differentiable approximations.
- Authors provided a well-considered response to the reviewer’s pre-review question.
- Reviewer contends that under fixed range and quantization, a sufficiently complex network could learn codes approaching the theoretical maximum entropy.
- Reviewer finds persuasive the authors’ counterargument that enforcing a specific output form would force the network to become more complex, effectively replicating the separate variable-rate encoder used to store q.
