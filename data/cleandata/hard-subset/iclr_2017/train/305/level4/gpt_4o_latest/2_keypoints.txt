- Paper extends prior rate-distortion optimization to deep encoders/decoders and to adaptive entropy coding, and explores links to variational autoencoders.
- Novelty is limited since the rate-distortion approach was published before; authors should clarify new contributions.
- Focusing on MSE (PSNR) is a step back from prior perceptual-metric optimization and is surprising given authors’ prior work.
- Results clearly improve over JPEG 2000 and appear to be the best among learned methods to date.
- Paper is exceptionally well written.
- Equation 10 seems incorrect: the partition function likely depends on g_s(y; theta), which would break equivalence to a VAE for non-Euclidean metrics.
- Why was MSE chosen instead of a perceptual metric, and why are evaluations limited to PSNR?
- The relative impact of adaptive entropy coding versus deeper encoder/decoder architectures is unclear; ablation without adaptation is needed.
- More details on the adaptive coder and its empirical influence are required; addressing these would likely improve the review score.
