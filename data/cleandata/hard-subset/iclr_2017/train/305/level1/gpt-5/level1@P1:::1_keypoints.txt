- Problem: End-to-end optimization of lossy image compression under a rate–distortion objective using learned nonlinear transforms and scalar quantization.
- Method: Three-stage cascaded convolutional transforms with GDN/IGDN nonlinearities, uniform scalar quantization, and additive uniform-noise relaxation for gradient-based training.
- Theoretical link: The continuous relaxation corresponds to a VAE ELBO under specific choices, with clear distinction between generative modeling and R–D optimization goals.
- Entropy coding: Implemented a practical CABAC-like entropy coder and report true bitrates including side information, not just differential entropy estimates.
- Empirical results: Outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 in MS-SSIM and generally in PSNR on Kodak and standard images, with visually cleaner reconstructions (reduced blockiness/ringing).
- Rigor and motivation: Well-motivated use of GDN (neuroscience grounding) to reduce dependencies; solid literature placement and clear contrasts to prior work.
- Surrogate justification: Additive uniform noise is a principled surrogate for quantization; empirical validation shows small bias/variance and expected behavior across rates.
- Training details: Architecture, parameterization, optimizer, and stabilization measures are thoroughly reported.
- Baseline limitation: Missing comparisons to more modern codecs (e.g., BPG/HEVC-intra, WebP) for fuller context.
- Single-rate models: Training one model per λ is less practical; a variable-rate or conditional model would be preferable.
- Ablations needed: Quantitative ablation studies (GDN vs. ReLU, number of stages/filters) are lacking and would strengthen claims.
- Entropy model limitation: Independence assumption in latent space is a limitation; modest spatial/contextual modeling could improve rates.
- Complexity metrics missing: No encode/decode runtime, memory, or training compute/time reported to assess deployability.
- Evaluation breadth: Limited datasets and resolutions; higher-resolution experiments and a subjective user study would strengthen perceptual claims.
- Quantization sensitivity: Question about annealing/mismatched uniform-noise widths and sensitivity of results to this choice.
- Entropy model training: Questions on initialization and stability of piecewise-linear marginals and what prevents degenerate densities when learning jointly.
- Residual dependencies: Request for measured residual dependencies after GDN and whether simple context models could close the gap to the relaxed entropy.
- Variable-rate test-time adaptation: Can a single trained model be adapted at test time by scaling latents or quantization without retraining?
- Generalization to high-res pipelines: How performance scales to high-resolution images and different camera pipelines given 256×256 training crops.
- Color processing and fairness: Clarify color space used (RGB vs. luminance-chrominance) and whether chroma subsampling was considered for fair comparisons with JPEG.
- Practicality and reproducibility: Request for encode/decode times for typical image sizes, model sizes per λ, and willingness to release code/models.
