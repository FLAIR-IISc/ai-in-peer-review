Summary and contributions
The paper proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression system built from cascaded convolutional transforms with generalized divisive normalization (GDN) in analysis and its inverse (IGDN) in synthesis, a uniform scalar quantizer, and a nonparametric, factorized entropy model. To train through quantization, the authors introduce a continuous relaxation by replacing rounding with additive uniform noise and show that, under MSE distortion, the relaxed objective aligns with a VAE-style ELBO. The entire pipeline (transforms and entropy model) is optimized for a chosen rate–distortion trade-off λ via stochastic gradient descent, and evaluation is done with an actual arithmetic coder (CABAC), reporting real bitrates. Experiments on Kodak and additional images demonstrate consistent visual improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000 across bitrates, with MS-SSIM gains and often better PSNR, and show that the relaxed loss tracks the discrete RD objective well.

Decision: Accept
Key reasons: (1) Strong, well-motivated end-to-end formulation that bridges transform coding and VAEs, with a practical training relaxation for quantization; (2) Compelling empirical results with actual bitstreams, showing clear perceptual and often quantitative gains over established standards.

Supporting arguments
- Problem: Learn transforms and entropy model jointly to optimize RD in the presence of quantization. The question is precise and important.
- Motivation and placement: The work is well grounded in transform coding, gain control literature, and variational methods. The link to VAEs clarifies the relaxation; the distinction from generative modeling is thoughtfully argued.
- Evidence and rigor: The proxy loss is validated against discrete RD; the method is compared on standard datasets with real arithmetic coding. The architectural and training details are thorough. Visual quality and MS-SSIM improvements are substantial and consistent; PSNR gains are frequent, especially at low rates.

Constructive feedback (to help improve the paper)
- Provide a head-to-head comparison with contemporary learned codecs (e.g., Toderici et al., Gregor et al.) where feasible, even if bitstreams differ, to contextualize gains.
- Report runtime, memory, and parameter counts for encode/decode, and compare to JPEG 2000 to characterize practicality.
- Analyze the contribution of GDN vs. common activations (ablation) more quantitatively, including parameter efficiency.
- Discuss fairness details: RGB vs. Y’CbCr, 4:2:0 subsampling, and strength of the CABAC contexts relative to JPEG 2000’s entropy coding.
- Explore a single model operating across λ via conditioning to avoid per-λ training.

Questions for the authors
- How large is the average gap between differential entropy (relaxed) and actual coded rate across bitrates and images? Any systematic residual bias?
- What are the encoding/decoding times and FLOPs per megapixel, and how do they scale with the number of channels?
- How sensitive is performance to the independence assumption in the latent entropy model? Would simple spatial/context models close remaining RD gaps?
- Can the same network be trained to accept λ as input at test time to produce a continuum of RD points?
- Do results generalize to other datasets (e.g., DIV2K) or camera RAW, and how robust is the method to domain shift?
