- Summary: Proposes an end-to-end nonlinear transform coding framework using learned convolutional analysis/synthesis transforms with GDN/IGDN and a differentiable rate–distortion objective via additive uniform noise and learned per-channel nonparametric marginals.

- Key technical contribution: Introduces a practical continuous relaxation of quantization (noise replacement) enabling SGD training, connects the relaxation to VAEs, and evaluates with an actual entropy coder to report real bitrates.

- Empirical result: Outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 on Kodak and other images in PSNR at low–mid rates and shows consistent perceptual quality gains (MS-SSIM and visuals).

- Decision: Accept.

- Main reason 1: Strong, principled end-to-end methodology that enables direct optimization of a full compression pipeline including entropy coding.

- Main reason 2: Convincing empirical evidence for both perceptual quality and rate–distortion with careful evaluation using actual bitrates.

- Problem placement: Well-motivated focus on classical rate–distortion for practical codecs, bridging transform coding and generative models.

- Method novelty/soundness: Systematizes additive uniform noise relaxation with nonparametric marginals and uses GDN nonlinearities motivated by Gaussianization.

- Independence assumption: Code-space independence is explicit and nonparametric marginals reduce model mismatch.

- Training approach: Trains separate models per λ to map the convex rate–distortion frontier.

- Correctness/rigor: Validates the relaxation by comparing relaxed vs. quantized entropy/distortion and documents architecture/optimization details.

- Use of entropy coder: Employs a real entropy coder (CABAC) and reports actual bitrates, avoiding differential-entropy pitfalls.

- Empirical evidence detail: Presents RD curves, qualitative examples, and consistent MS-SSIM improvements with expected artifact reduction.

- Broader impact: Demonstrates learned compression can surpass classic transforms and the VAE/relaxation link will likely spur further research.

- Limitation — entropy model: Assumes independence and uses simple CABAC with raster-scan contexts, leaving coding gains on the table.

- Limitation — single-rate models: Separate per-λ models complicate deployment and rate control; a unified or progressive model would be preferable.

- Limitation — baselines: Missing comparisons to modern intra codecs (HEVC/BPG) and recurrent VAE-like approaches with actual bitstreams.

- Limitation — fairness/evaluation nuances: Differences in color sampling (JPEG 4:2:0 vs. RGB) and PSNR on luma only may affect comparisons; common color-space ablations needed.

- Limitation — ablations: Lacks systematic ablations isolating GDN vs. ReLU, stage count, and parametric vs nonparametric marginals.

- Limitation — complexity: High training cost and unspecified encoding/decoding runtime and memory footprints.

- Suggested ablations: Replace GDN with BN+ReLU or PReLU, vary stages/channels, and compare parametric vs. nonparametric marginals with RD and bits reported.

- Suggested entropy improvements: Add simple spatial context or a shared hyperprior and report gap between sum of marginal entropies and achieved bits.

- Suggested rate control: Discuss λ-to-bitrate mapping, variance across images, and provide a simple procedure to hit target rates.

- Suggested runtime reporting: Include encoding/decoding speed on CPU/GPU and model size.

- Suggested color handling/baselines: Evaluate in a common color space (e.g., YCbCr 4:4:4) and compare to BPG and a VAE baseline with the same architecture.

- Questions for authors — bitrate predictability: How predictable is achieved bitrate vs. λ across images; provide calibration curve or rate-control scheme.

- Questions for authors — entropy gap: What is the gap between (i) sum of learned marginal differential entropies, (ii) discrete entropies after quantization, and (iii) actual CABAC bits, and how much does adaptation reduce it?

- Questions for authors — GDN importance: At matched capacity, what RD gap arises when substituting ReLU/LeakyReLU (with/without BN) for GDN?

- Questions for authors — multi-rate operation: Can a single trained model operate across rates (e.g., latent scaling, learned step sizes, λ conditioning), and what breaks when attempting this?

- Questions for authors — side information & deployment: How much side information is included per image, is the model shared out-of-band, and what is the deployment scenario?

- Questions for authors — generalization: Tested beyond Kodak or on higher-resolution images — do gains persist?

- Questions for authors — perceptual training: Does training with MS-SSIM (or a differentiable proxy) further improve perceptual quality without overly harming PSNR?

- Overall verdict: Timely, well-executed paper that advances learned image compression with a practical, principled end-to-end formulation; recommend acceptance.
