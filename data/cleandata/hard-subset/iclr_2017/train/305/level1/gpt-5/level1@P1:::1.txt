Summary and contributions
The paper tackles end-to-end optimization of lossy image compression under a rate–distortion (R–D) objective. It proposes a nonlinear transform coding model composed of three cascaded convolutional stages with generalized divisive normalization (GDN) nonlinearities in the analysis transform and an approximate inverse IGDN in the synthesis transform, followed by uniform scalar quantization. To enable gradient-based training, the authors introduce a continuous relaxation that replaces quantization with additive uniform noise and learn nonparametric marginal densities to estimate the rate term. They show the relaxed loss coincides with the variational autoencoder (VAE) ELBO under specific choices, while emphasizing key differences between generative modeling and R–D optimization. Importantly, they implement an actual entropy coder (CABAC-like) and report true bitrates, not just entropy estimates. Experiments on Kodak and other standard images demonstrate generally better PSNR R–D performance than JPEG and JPEG 2000 at most bitrates and a large, consistent gain in perceptual quality as measured by MS-SSIM, with visually cleaner reconstructions that avoid blockiness and ringing. The paper is carefully motivated, technically sound, and makes a compelling empirical case.

Decision: Accept
Key reasons:
- Strong, well-motivated end-to-end R–D formulation with a principled quantization relaxation, tied to VAEs, and evaluated using actual bitstreams; the approach is novel and rigorous relative to prior compression and representation-learning work.
- Clear and consistent empirical gains over JPEG and JPEG 2000 in perceptual quality (MS-SSIM) and generally in PSNR, along with a practical decoding pipeline.

Supporting arguments
1) Problem addressed: The paper targets joint optimization of the analysis transform, quantizer, and synthesis transform for lossy image compression under an R–D trade-off. It seeks to learn nonlinear transforms that make independent scalar quantization effective, thereby approximating high-dimensional vector quantization.

2) Motivation and placement in literature: The motivation is strong. Classical transform coders use linear transforms with independently quantized coefficients; higher-order dependencies remain and manifest as artifacts. GDN, rooted in visual neuroscience and known to Gaussianize image statistics, is an apt choice to reduce dependencies beyond linear decorrelation. The paper contrasts its discrete R–D objective with continuous generative modeling objectives, clarifies the ELBO correspondence, and distinguishes its goals from prior generative compression and recurrent binary representations (Gregor et al., Toderici et al.). The literature discussion is fair and knowledgeable.

3) Evidence and rigor: The relaxed loss is well justified: additive uniform noise to approximate quantization yields a continuous surrogate where the differential entropy of y+U approximates discrete entropy. The paper empirically validates the surrogate by comparing relaxed versus actual R–D, showing small bias/variance and the expected behavior at fine/coarse quantization. Training details are substantial (architecture, parameterization, optimizer, stabilization measures). Reporting actual bits via a CABAC-like coder (with side information included) eliminates common pitfalls of comparing differential entropies to bitrates. Visual results are convincing and match the MS-SSIM improvements. While PSNR gains are not universal, they are frequent, especially at lower rates. The scope of baselines (JPEG, JPEG 2000) is reasonable for the time, though missing more modern codecs. Overall, the claims are substantiated.

Areas to improve that do not change my decision
- Baselines: Consider adding BPG/HEVC-intra and/or WebP comparisons to contextualize performance against more modern codecs.
- Single model across rates: Currently, a separate model is trained per λ. A conditional or scale-parameterized model supporting variable rate would be more practical; even a simple global gain or learned per-channel step size at test time would help.
- Ablations: Quantify the contribution of GDN vs. ReLU/sigmoid activations and of each architectural choice (number of stages/filters). Preliminary statements are encouraging; numbers would strengthen the case.
- Entropy model: The independence assumption in the latent space is a limitation. Even modest spatial/context modeling or channel coupling in the entropy coder could improve rates; an analysis of residual dependencies after GDN would be informative.
- Complexity: Provide encode/decode runtime and memory on typical hardware, and training compute/time. This will help assess deployability.
- Evaluation breadth: Include more diverse datasets and higher-resolution images. A small subjective study (pairwise preference) would strengthen the perceptual claims beyond MS-SSIM.

Questions for the authors
- Quantization relaxation: Did you try annealing the uniform noise width during training or mismatched widths (e.g., >1 early, →1 late)? How sensitive are results to this choice?
- Entropy model learning: How are the piecewise-linear marginals initialized? Do you observe instability or overfitting when learning ψ jointly with θ, φ? What prevents degenerate densities?
- Residual dependencies: After GDN, how large are measured pairwise/conditional dependencies across channels and space? Could a simple context (e.g., neighboring latents) in entropy coding close part of the gap to the relaxed differential entropy?
- Variable-rate operation: Can a single trained model be adapted at test time to different rates via scaling the latents or adjusting quantization step sizes without retraining (e.g., by calibrating the entropy model online)?
- Generalization: The training images were downsampled/cropped to 256×256 and filtered to avoid artifacts. How does performance scale to high-resolution images and natural camera pipelines? Any domain shift observed?
- Color handling and fairness: JPEG is evaluated with 4:2:0 subsampling. For your RGB models, do you process in RGB or a luminance-chrominance space? Could chroma subsampling in your pipeline improve rates further?
- Practicality: What are typical encode/decode times for a 512×512 image, and model sizes for the λ models? Would you consider releasing code/models to facilitate reproducibility?

Overall, this is a timely, well-executed paper that advances learned compression with a principled and practical end-to-end approach.
