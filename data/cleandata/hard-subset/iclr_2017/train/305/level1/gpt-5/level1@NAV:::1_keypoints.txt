- Proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression system using cascaded conv transforms with GDN/IGDN, uniform scalar quantization, and a factorized entropy model.  
- Introduces a continuous relaxation of quantization (replace rounding with additive uniform noise) and shows the relaxed MSE objective aligns with a VAE-style ELBO.  
- Optimizes transforms and entropy model jointly for a chosen rate–distortion trade-off λ via SGD and evaluates with an actual arithmetic coder (CABAC) reporting real bitrates.  
- Demonstrates consistent visual improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000 on Kodak and other images, with MS-SSIM gains and often better PSNR.  
- Shows the relaxed loss tracks the discrete RD objective well in experiments.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Strong, well-motivated end-to-end formulation bridging transform coding and VAEs with a practical quantization relaxation.  
- Key reason 2: Compelling empirical results with real bitstreams, showing clear perceptual and often quantitative gains over standards.  
- Problem statement is precise and important: joint learning of transforms and entropy model under quantization.  
- Motivation and placement are solid, linking transform coding, gain control, and variational methods while distinguishing from generative modeling.  
- Evidence and rigor: proxy loss validated against discrete RD; comparisons on standard datasets with real arithmetic coding; thorough architecture and training details.  
- Visual quality and MS-SSIM improvements are substantial and consistent; PSNR gains common, especially at low rates.  
- Suggestion: Provide head-to-head comparisons with contemporary learned codecs (e.g., Toderici, Gregor) for context.  
- Suggestion: Report runtime, memory, and parameter counts for encode/decode and compare to JPEG 2000 to assess practicality.  
- Suggestion: Quantitatively ablate GDN vs. common activations including parameter efficiency.  
- Suggestion: Clarify fairness details (RGB vs Y’CbCr, 4:2:0 subsampling) and CABAC context strengths relative to JPEG 2000’s coding.  
- Suggestion: Explore training a single model conditioned on λ to cover multiple RD points without per-λ training.  
- Question: What is the average gap between differential entropy (relaxed) and actual coded rate across bitrates and images—any systematic bias?  
- Question: What are encoding/decoding times, FLOPs per megapixel, and how do they scale with channel count?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the independence assumption in the latent entropy model; could simple spatial/context models close RD gaps?  
- Question: Can the network accept λ as input at test time to produce a continuum of RD points?  
- Question: Do results generalize to other datasets (e.g., DIV2K) or camera RAW, and how robust is the method to domain shift?
