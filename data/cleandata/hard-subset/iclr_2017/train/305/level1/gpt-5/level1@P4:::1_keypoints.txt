- Presents an end-to-end optimized image compression system using cascaded convolutions, GDN/IGDN nonlinearities, uniform scalar quantization, and a learned inverse transform.  
- Main technical contribution: a differentiable surrogate for joint rate–distortion optimization using additive i.i.d. uniform noise, nonparametric marginal models, and SGD on estimated rate (differential entropy) + MSE distortion.  
- Relates the approach to VAEs under specific assumptions while explicitly targeting arbitrary points on the rate–distortion curve via a λ trade-off.  
- Implements a practical CABAC-style entropy coder to report actual bitrates rather than just entropy estimates.  
- Demonstrates improved rate–distortion performance over JPEG and JPEG 2000 on Kodak and other held-out images, with consistent visual quality gains especially at low rates and substantial MS-SSIM improvement.  
- Paper is clearly written, technically careful, and empirically validated (including validation of the noise relaxation).  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: principled, differentiable end-to-end RD optimization with a practical entropy coder and evaluation on true bits.  
- Key reason: convincing empirical support with qualitative improvements, MS-SSIM gains, and PSNR advantages for most images.  
- Addresses the non-differentiability of quantization with a standard but carefully implemented additive-uniform-noise relaxation, empirically shown to track discrete RD well.  
- Situates contributions in literature (transform coding, GDN, neuroscience, VAEs) and clearly distinguishes from batch norm and pointwise nonlinearities.  
- Comparison to related work (Gregor et al., Toderici et al.) is fair and highlights novelty in end-to-end RD training with true bitrates.  
- Technical details (convs, sampling, GDN/IGDN, Adam training, parameterizations) are specified and justified; entropy model uses learnable independent marginals.  
- Architecture choices are somewhat ad-hoc but supported by ablations indicating GDN/IGDN efficiency over ReLU-like nonlinearities.  
- Empirical rigor: trained on large ImageNet subset, tested on Kodak and other held-out images, reports PSNR and MS-SSIM, and provides per-image results online.  
- Limitation: requires training a separate model per λ; a conditional/scale-latent variable-rate model would improve practicality.  
- Limitation: independent-marginal entropy model likely leaves spatial/channel dependencies unexploited; simple context models could reduce bitrate further.  
- Suggestion: perform controlled ablations to quantify GDN/IGDN contribution vs. pointwise nonlinearities at matched parameter budgets.  
- Suggestion: improve reproducibility by specifying JPEG 2000 settings, reporting side-information overhead, and possibly adding a small human study.  
- Suggestion: report encoder/decoder runtime and memory (CPU/GPU) compared to JPEG/JPEG 2000 for deployability assessment.  
- Questions: How sensitive are RD results to the latent independence assumption and what are residual dependencies or potential gains from context models?  
- Questions: Did you explore annealing noise width or alternative straight-through estimators and their effects on convergence/bias at very low rates?  
- Questions: How large is the side-information overhead relative to total bits at low rates — please provide numbers.  
- Questions: How do RD results vary with stages, filter sizes, and channel counts at fixed parameter budgets; are there diminishing returns beyond three stages?  
- Questions: What is the impact of relaxing GDN/IGDN exponent and symmetry constraints on performance and stability?  
- Questions: How well do models trained on 256×256 crops generalize to much larger images (e.g., 4K) and are there tiling/boundary artifacts?  
- Questions: Were JPEG 2000 baselines tuned beyond defaults (context-adaptive options, rate allocation) to ensure fair comparisons?  
- Questions: Could a hypernetwork or learned scale parameters enable a single model to support continuous λ at test time?  
- Questions: Do results generalize to other datasets (DIV2K, BSD500) or exhibit domain-shift issues?
