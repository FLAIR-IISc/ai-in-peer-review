- Problem: Learn a nonlinear analysis/synthesis transform with uniform scalar quantization and an entropy model to beat hand-designed codecs (JPEG, JPEG 2000) in rate–distortion tradeoff.  
- Method: End-to-end optimizable pipeline using three-stage convolutional transforms with GDN/inverse GDN and uniform quantization; replace rounding with additive uniform noise for differentiability.  
- Entropy model & coding: Factorized per-channel nonparametric (piecewise-linear) marginals plus a practical CABAC-like arithmetic coder reporting actual bitrates.  
- Theory link: The noise-relaxed objective maps to a VAE-like ELBO under MSE, clarifying conceptual differences between compression and generative modeling.  
- Empirical results: Consistently better perceptual quality and MS-SSIM at all rates, and competitive or superior PSNR versus JPEG and often JPEG 2000, especially at low bitrates.  
- Decision: Accept — significant, novel, and practical demonstration that learned transforms can surpass legacy codecs in perceptual quality.  
- Rigor and clarity: Strong motivation, well-justified relaxation, empirical validation against actual bits/distortion, and clear training/architecture details for reproducibility.  
- Validation of relaxation: Distortion estimates are largely unbiased; relaxed entropy overestimates discrete entropy mainly at coarse quantization, with bias diminishing at finer steps.  
- Reporting advantage: Providing actual coded bitrates avoids pitfalls of comparing differential entropies to bits and is a major strength.  
- Visual quality: Qualitative results show reduced blocking/ringing and better edge/contour rendering.  
- Limitation — baselines: Lacks comparison to stronger contemporary codecs (e.g., BPG/HEVC intra or WebP), limiting claims about state-of-the-art performance.  
- Limitation — rate flexibility: Requires a separate network per λ (no single variable-rate or scalable model presented).  
- Limitation — entropy model: Factorized marginal model ignores spatial and cross-channel dependencies; CABAC contexts are relatively simple.  
- Limitation — ablations: Limited quantitative ablations isolating GDN vs. pointwise nonlinearities or architecture depth/width at matched parameter budgets.  
- Suggestion: Include stronger baselines (BPG/HEVC/WebP) to better calibrate competitiveness.  
- Suggestion: Explore variable-rate mechanisms (learned quantization scales, conditional entropy models, latent importance maps) to cover multiple R–D points per model.  
- Suggestion: Ablate GDN contribution versus pointwise nonlinearities and vary entropy model choices (nonparametric vs Gaussian, with/without online adaptation).  
- Suggestion: Report encoding/decoding time, memory usage, and hardware considerations (CPU vs GPU) compared to JPEG 2000.  
- Suggestion: Clarify color handling and chroma subsampling (YCbCr vs RGB) for fairer comparisons with JPEG.  
- Suggestion: Provide per-image R–D scatter plots, confidence intervals, or paired comparisons for stronger statistical rigor.  
- Question: How sensitive are results to the latent independence assumption and what gains would a simple hyperprior/context model yield?  
- Question: Can you share per-image PSNR/MS-SSIM vs bitrate and the actual bitstreams or code to reproduce results?  
- Question: What happens if you vary λ or quantization step at test time without retraining — can a single model approximate multiple rates?  
- Question: What are typical encoding/decoding runtimes for 512×512 images on CPU and GPU compared to JPEG 2000?  
- Question: Would training directly on MS-SSIM or other perceptual losses further improve perceptual quality at a fixed bitrate with the same entropy coder?  
- Question: How robust is the additive-noise relaxation compared to straight-through estimators or annealed noise widths in terms of bias/variance and final R–D?  
- Question: How large is side-information overhead (λ index, model parameters) per image, and is it included in the reported bitrates?
