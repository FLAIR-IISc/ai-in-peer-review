- Novel method using cascaded convolutional layers + biologically-inspired GDN nonlinearities with end-to-end SGD and a continuous relaxation for quantization.

- Outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 on rate–distortion and MS-SSIM, yielding visually superior results across bit rates.

- Establishes links to variational autoencoders while emphasizing discrete-domain compression differences.

- Decision: Accept — paper is well-motivated, scientifically rigorous, and makes a significant contribution.

- Novelty/Impact: GDN + end-to-end optimization is innovative and drives notable improvements over traditional codecs.

- Empirical Rigor: Extensive experiments across datasets, metrics (MSE, MS-SSIM, PSNR), bit rates, and qualitative examples.

- Practical Significance: Achieves better rate–distortion trade-offs and demonstrates practical feasibility with an implemented entropy code.

- Suggestion: Provide a detailed analysis of computational complexity (encoding/decoding and training) versus JPEG/JPEG 2000.

- Suggestion: Perform ablation studies to clarify the necessity and generality of GDN compared to other nonlinearities.

- Suggestion: Include comparisons to more recent neural compression methods (e.g., Toderici et al., 2016) for broader context.

- Question: How does computational efficiency (training and inference) compare to JPEG/JPEG 2000, especially for real-time applications?

- Question: Can the approach be extended to video compression, and what challenges would that raise?

- Question: How sensitive is performance to the training dataset, and would domain-specific retraining (e.g., medical images) substantially change results?
