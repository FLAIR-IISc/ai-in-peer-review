- Proposes a novel end-to-end optimized image compression method using cascaded nonlinear transforms inspired by biological vision (GDN), jointly optimizing analysis/synthesis transforms and a uniform quantizer via a continuous relaxation for gradient-based training.
- Shows competitive or superior rate–distortion performance vs JPEG and JPEG 2000, with notably better perceptual quality measured by MS-SSIM.
- Decision: Accept — primary reason is strong empirical improvements in visual quality; comparable RD plus perceptual gains justify acceptance.
- Theoretical grounding linking the method to variational autoencoders provides valuable conceptual framework and strengthens the paper’s foundations.
- Motivation and framing are clear: linear transforms are limited for natural images and GDN provides a principled nonlinear alternative.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough: comparisons on Kodak and authors’ images, metrics include MSE and MS-SSIM, plus detailed ablation studies and parameter tuning in the appendix.
- Reproducibility is supported by a working implementation and publicly available compressed images.
- Explanation of the continuous relaxation (additive noise replacing quantization) is generally clear but would benefit from a more intuitive explanation or visual comparison of distributions.
- Training is computationally expensive; the paper should report concrete training time and resource numbers and discuss possible cost-reduction strategies.
- The paper omits discussion of extending the approach to video; a brief discussion of challenges and opportunities for video compression would be useful.
- The choice of GDN is well motivated, but a direct controlled comparison with standard activations (ReLU, sigmoid) would strengthen the argument.
- CABAC implementation description is brief; more details on context modeling and entropy coding strategies are needed for replication.
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the width of the additive noise (quantization bin size) and have other noise distributions been tried?
- Question: What specific rule determines the number of transform channels as a function of target bit rate?
- Question: Are there particular image types or features where the method performs especially well or poorly compared to JPEG/JPEG 2000?
- Question: What is the approximate training time on a typical GPU for a given lambda, and how does training time scale with dataset size?
- Question: Please elaborate on the heuristic for adapting the spline approximation range for marginals and how frequently this adaptation occurs during training.
