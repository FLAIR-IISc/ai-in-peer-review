- Recommendation: Reject due to lack of clear, compelling advantage over existing methods and ambiguity in experiments/evaluation.
- Main contribution: End-to-end optimized image compression using cascaded convolutional linear filters with GDN nonlinearities and continuous relaxation of quantization.
- Optimization: Jointly optimizes analysis and synthesis transforms for rate-distortion via gradient-based training using a continuous proxy for quantization.
- Results: Competitive with JPEG and JPEG 2000 and noticeably better visual quality at low bitrates, but improvements in MS-SSIM are modest.
- Motivation: Strong—addresses limitations of traditional transform coding and benefits from GDN’s Gaussianizing properties.
- Theoretical grounding: Connection to variational autoencoders provides insight for the continuous relaxation approach.
- Technical novelty: Continuous proxy for discontinuous loss and end-to-end SGD are logical and clever solutions.
- Evidence concerns: “Dramatic improvement in visual quality” is mainly supported by subjective examples, lacking formal perceptual validation.
- Metric issues: Choice and combination of MSE and entropy as evaluation metrics are not thoroughly justified.
- Comparative fairness: Comparison to JPEG/JPEG 2000 may be unfair; entropy coding (e.g., CABAC) differences are acknowledged but not resolved.
- Quantitative gains: MS-SSIM improvements are statistically significant but not consistently large across images.
- Architecture justification: Network design choices (filters, downsampling) appear ad-hoc and insufficiently motivated.
- Reproducibility and complexity: Implementation complexity and lack of detailed reproducibility information raise deployment concerns.
- Performance variability: Method’s rate-distortion benefits vary significantly across images; claims need quantification for most images.
- Suggested ablation: Include ablation studies to quantify contributions of GDN, cascaded structure, and pointwise nonlinearities.
- Computational cost: Provide training, encoding, and decoding time comparisons to JPEG/JPEG 2000.
- Perceptual study: Conduct formal human-subject evaluations to substantiate visual-quality claims.
- Entropy coding details: Explain entropy coding scheme, context modeling, and adaptation strategies in depth.
- Hyperparameter analysis: Report sensitivity to λ, learning rate, and other hyperparameters; describe selection procedure.
- Generalization: Assess performance on out-of-training-domain images and discuss degradation behavior.
- Reproducibility request: Release code and trained models to facilitate validation and follow-up work.
- Questions for authors: Provide training time on specific hardware, encode/decode complexity relative to standards, statistical analysis (p-values/effect sizes) for MS-SSIM, how λ was chosen, and potential for per-image or per-class adaptation.
