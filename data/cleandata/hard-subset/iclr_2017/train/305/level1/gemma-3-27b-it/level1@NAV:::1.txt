## Review of "END-TO-END OPTIMIZED IMAGE COMPRESSION"

This paper presents a novel image compression method based on end-to-end optimized nonlinear transforms. The authors construct an analysis and synthesis transformation using cascaded convolutional layers and nonlinear activations (specifically, Generalized Divisive Normalization – GDN), and jointly optimize the entire model for rate-distortion performance using stochastic gradient descent. A key contribution lies in using a continuous relaxation of the quantization step to enable optimization despite the discontinuity, and framing this as a variational autoencoder. The method demonstrates improved rate-distortion performance and, importantly, superior visual quality compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000 on a test image dataset.

**Reject** – While the approach is intriguing and the results promising, the paper lacks sufficient detail regarding the experimental setup and analysis to confidently assess the validity of the claims. Specifically, the experimental section feels somewhat hand-wavy and lacks rigorous statistical analysis.

The paper successfully identifies a significant problem – the suboptimal nature of traditionally designed image compression pipelines – and proposes a well-motivated approach. The justification for using GDN, drawing on previous work in biological vision and density estimation, is strong. The connection to variational autoencoders provides a useful theoretical framing. The authors clearly articulate the challenges of joint optimization with quantization and offer a sensible solution via continuous relaxation. The reported visual improvements are compelling.

However, the experimental section is a significant weakness. The description of the training process lacks specificity (e.g., hyperparameter details, stopping criteria). The claim of improvement over JPEG and JPEG 2000 is presented without sufficient statistical rigor.  Averaging rate-distortion curves across images, as acknowledged by the authors, is a complex issue, and the chosen approach isn't thoroughly justified.  The appendix contains a wealth of information, but presents it in a fragmented manner, making it hard to grasp the full experimental design. Finally, while the code is stated to be available, access would be vital to confirm the reproducibility of these results.

**Additional Feedback:**

*   **Quantify Visual Quality:** While visual examples are provided, a more thorough quantitative assessment of perceptual quality beyond MS-SSIM would be beneficial.
*   **Ablation Studies:** Investigating the contribution of different components (e.g., GDN vs. ReLU) through ablation studies would strengthen the analysis.
*   **Computational Cost:** A discussion of the computational cost of training and compression/decompression would be valuable.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Briefly address the sensitivity of the results to hyperparameter choices.
*   **Training Data Details**:  Was any data augmentation performed? More specifics about the ImageNet subset used for training would be helpful.
*   **Clarify Entropy Coding:** The description of the entropy coding scheme feels cursory. More detail is needed to understand its efficiency relative to state-of-the-art methods.
