- Paper proposes end-to-end image compression using cascaded nonlinear transforms (GDN) with continuous quantization relaxation and shows improved RD and visual quality vs JPEG/JPEG2000.
- Recommendation: Reject.
- Primary reason: Novelty is incremental and the paper fails to show consistent, significant improvement over established methods.
- Empirical evaluation lacks statistical rigor and shows variability across images; "better for most (but not all) images" is insufficient.
- Authors need systematic quantitative analysis (e.g., average gains with confidence intervals) rather than per-image examples.
- MS-SSIM improvements are reported but the link to perceived visual quality is not firmly established.
- Discussion of summarizing per-image performance is valid but does not excuse lack of statistically supported conclusions.
- Secondary criticism: Presentation is verbose with extensive background (VAEs, biology) that could be streamlined.
- The VAE connection appears tangential to the main compression contribution.
- Additional feedback: perform statistical significance tests (p-values, CIs) for RD and MS-SSIM results.
- Additional feedback: include ablation studies to quantify contributions of GDN, cascaded structure, and alternatives (e.g., ReLU).
- Additional feedback: report computational complexity and training/encoding/decoding costs in detail.
- Additional feedback: compare against more recent codecs (e.g., BPG, WebP) beyond JPEG/JPEG2000.
- Additional feedback: study sensitivity to the width of the uniform noise used in quantization relaxation.
- Additional feedback: clarify selection and role of λ and provide a principled selection method.
- Questions for authors: provide statistical analyses (p-values, confidence intervals) supporting RD and MS-SSIM improvements.
- Questions for authors: how does performance change using alternative nonlinearities (ReLU, sigmoid) instead of GDN?
- Questions for authors: what are training and inference time/costs compared to JPEG and JPEG2000?
- Questions for authors: how sensitive are results to the noise distribution width used in the relaxation?
- Questions for authors: can you provide a principled method for choosing λ for specific applications?
