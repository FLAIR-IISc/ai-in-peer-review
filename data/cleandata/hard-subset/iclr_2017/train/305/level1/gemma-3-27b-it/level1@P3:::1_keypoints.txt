- Proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression method using cascaded convolutional linear filters and nonlinear activations inspired by biology.  
- Jointly optimizes analysis and synthesis transforms for rate-distortion using SGD and a continuous relaxation of quantization.  
- Demonstrates improved rate-distortion performance and visually better results than JPEG and JPEG 2000.  
- Significant contribution bridging learned representations and practical image compression.  
- Recommendation: Reject due to insufficient experimental rigor and clarity around entropy coding and comparisons.  
- Concern that relying on a single CABAC implementation questions fairness and reproducibility of results.  
- Well-motivated core idea: end-to-end optimization addresses limitations of separately optimized transform-coding components.  
- Insightful theoretical connection to variational autoencoders and justification for the relaxed loss function.  
- Strong architectural choice: use of generalized divisive normalization (GDN) to Gaussianize image densities.  
- Performance claims over JPEG/JPEG 2000 feel incomplete due to superficial entropy coding description.  
- Unclear whether improvements stem from learned transforms or a more efficient entropy coder.  
- Multiple caveats in comparisons (JPEG 2000 settings, chroma subsampling) weaken conclusions.  
- Lack of statistical significance testing for rate-distortion curves undermines the claims.  
- Qualitative visual comparisons are subjective and not sufficiently backed by quantitative perceptual metrics beyond MS-SSIM.  
- Appendix is extensive but forces readers to sift through too much detail to assess claim validity.  
- Suggestion: provide detailed CABAC implementation description, context modeling, and justify the implementation choice.  
- Suggestion: compare with other entropy coding libraries (e.g., JPEG 2000 implementations) to strengthen results.  
- Suggestion: perform statistical significance testing on rate-distortion curves.  
- Suggestion: run ablation studies to isolate contributions of GDN, cascaded structure, and other components.  
- Suggestion: report computational cost of training and encoding/decoding compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000.  
- Suggestion: investigate robustness to hyperparameters and provide guidelines for choosing them.  
- Suggestion: analyze the continuous relaxation of quantization more deeply, including its limitations and optimization effects.  
- Suggestion: clarify the role and interpretation of λ in the optimization and trade-offs.  
- Question: were any CABAC parameters tuned or optimized specifically for the learned transform outputs?  
- Question: how sensitive are results to hyperparameters like learning rate and filter counts?  
- Question: provide a breakdown of computational cost for training and encoding/decoding relative to baselines.  
- Question: has robustness to small input perturbations (adversarial examples) been evaluated for the learned transforms?  
- Question: how was the claim that the relaxed distortion term is “mostly unbiased” verified and was it tested independently of the evaluation dataset?
