- Proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression method using cascaded convolutional transforms and GDN nonlinearities.  
- Frames the model as a variational autoencoder and uses a continuous relaxation of quantization to enable SGD-based joint optimization.  
- Reports improved rate-distortion performance and superior visual quality compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000 on the test set.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection due to insufficient experimental detail and lack of rigorous statistical analysis.  
- Training procedure description is incomplete (missing hyperparameters, stopping criteria, etc.).  
- The claim of improvement over JPEG/JPEG 2000 lacks sufficient statistical rigor and justification.  
- Averaging rate-distortion curves across images is a complex issue and the chosen averaging approach isn't well justified.  
- The appendix contains useful information but is fragmented and makes the experimental design hard to follow.  
- Code availability is asserted but access is necessary to confirm reproducibility.  
- Suggests adding quantitative perceptual quality metrics beyond MS-SSIM.  
- Recommends ablation studies to isolate contributions of components (e.g., GDN vs. ReLU).  
- Requests discussion of computational cost for training and encoding/decoding.  
- Asks for analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and robustness of results.  
- Seeks more details on training data and any data augmentation, including specifics of the ImageNet subset used.  
- Finds the entropy coding description cursory and requests more detail to assess its efficiency relative to state-of-the-art.
