## Review of "END-TO-END OPTIMIZED IMAGE COMPRESSION"

This paper presents a novel image compression method based on end-to-end optimization of nonlinear transforms. The core idea is to learn analysis and synthesis transforms, constructed from cascaded convolutional layers and nonlinear activations (specifically, Generalized Divisive Normalization – GDN), jointly optimized for rate-distortion performance. The authors cleverly address the non-differentiability of the quantization step by using a continuous relaxation, drawing parallels to variational autoencoders while maintaining the ability to operate at any point along the rate-distortion curve. The results demonstrate improved rate-distortion performance and, crucially, significantly enhanced visual quality compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000.

I recommend **accepting** this paper. The approach is both innovative and practically impactful, addressing a fundamental problem in image processing with a well-motivated and rigorously evaluated solution. The improvement in visual quality, particularly, is a significant contribution.

The paper’s strength lies in its comprehensive approach. The authors clearly articulate the problem, position their work within the existing literature on transform coding and generative models, and provide a detailed explanation of their method. The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating consistent improvements over established standards across a variety of images and bitrates. The connection to variational autoencoders provides a valuable theoretical grounding, while the focus on operating at any point on the rate-distortion curve distinguishes it from purely generative approaches. The inclusion of actual bitrates in the evaluation, rather than relying solely on entropy estimates, strengthens the practical relevance of the work.

However, the paper could benefit from further clarification in a few areas. While the benefits of GDN are mentioned, a more intuitive explanation of *why* it leads to improved performance compared to other nonlinearities would be helpful. Additionally, the optimization process is described, but details regarding hyperparameter tuning and computational resources would be valuable for reproducibility. Finally, the authors mention potential improvements using perceptual metrics during training; elaborating on the challenges and potential benefits of this approach would be insightful. 

Specifically, I would like to ask:

*   Could the authors elaborate on the choice of the specific GDN parameters and how sensitive the results are to variations in these parameters?
*   What were the computational resources (e.g., GPUs, training time) required to train the models?
*   What are the primary challenges in replacing the MSE loss with a perceptual metric like MS-SSIM during training, and how might these challenges be addressed?
