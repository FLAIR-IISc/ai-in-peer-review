- Novel end-to-end image compression framework jointly optimizing analysis transform, quantizer, and synthesis transform using cascaded conv filters + GDN nonlinearities.  
- Introduces continuous relaxation of quantization via additive uniform noise to enable gradient-based optimization.  
- Demonstrates superior rate-distortion and notably better MS-SSIM visual quality than JPEG and JPEG 2000 across multiple test images.  
- Decision: Accept — strong theoretical justification and compelling empirical validation support the contribution.  
- The paper situates itself well in literature and clearly distinguishes compression objectives from generative modeling.  
- The continuous relaxation technique is theoretically well-motivated and addresses optimization challenges from quantization.  
- Empirical results show consistent MS-SSIM improvements and visual examples that reduce blocking/ringing artifacts and preserve contours/edges.  
- Practical implementation strength: uses a proper entropy coder (CABAC) for fair comparison to standard codecs.  
- Suggestion: provide detailed analysis of computational complexity, training vs. inference time, and scaling with image size.  
- Suggestion: include a comprehensive comparison to recent generative compression methods with a metric table (PSNR, MS-SSIM, bitrates, compute).  
- Suggestion: compare GDN/IGDN to simpler nonlinearities (ReLU, sigmoid) to justify efficiency claims.  
- Suggestion: add aggregate statistics (tables) showing average PSNR and MS-SSIM gains across the full test set.  
- Question: identify specific images where the method underperforms and the image characteristics that cause difficulty.  
- Question: provide empirical comparisons of ReLU/sigmoid-based variants vs. GDN/IGDN in rate-distortion and model complexity.  
- Question: clarify how entropy approximation bias for finer quantization affects practical implementation and final compression performance.  
- Question: explain how the method optimizes the convex hull of distortions and rates and whether there are theoretical guarantees of finding the optimal hull.
