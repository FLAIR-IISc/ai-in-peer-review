- Presents an end-to-end optimized image compression method using nonlinear transforms inspired by biology, with GDN as the key nonlinearity.  
- Jointly optimizes analysis transform, uniform quantizer, and synthesis transform for rate-distortion performance.  
- Demonstrates superior rate-distortion performance vs. JPEG and JPEG 2000, with notable MS-SSIM improvements and better visual quality.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Tackles a fundamental image-compression problem with a well-motivated approach bridging classical compression theory and deep learning.  
- Key reason 2: Empirical results are robust and consistent across multiple metrics and test images, supported by clear visual evidence.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical justification for the continuous relaxation, relating to VAEs while clarifying differences in optimization goals.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, using standard datasets, metrics, and extensive visual comparisons.  
- Demonstrates practical feasibility via a simple entropy coder that yields actual bit rates for fair comparisons.  
- Additional feedback: include computational-complexity comparisons to traditional codecs (esp. for real-time) and discuss potential architectural improvements beyond the ad-hoc design.  
- Questions for authors: (1) Provide a detailed parameter-efficiency comparison between GDN and rectified linear units; (2) How sensitive are results to the continuous relaxation (uniform noise approximation) and could alternative relaxations improve performance?
