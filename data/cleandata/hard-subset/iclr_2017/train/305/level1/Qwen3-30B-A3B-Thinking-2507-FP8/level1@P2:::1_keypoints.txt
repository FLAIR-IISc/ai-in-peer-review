- Proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression framework with nonlinear transform coding and GDN, using a continuous relaxation of quantization for SGD training.

- Demonstrates superior rate-distortion performance versus JPEG and JPEG 2000, with particularly strong MS-SSIM visual quality improvements.

- Paper is well-written, thoroughly motivated, and provides comprehensive experimental validation across standard test images.

- Recommendation: accept — significant contribution showing joint optimization of nonlinear transform coding outperforms traditional methods.

- Continuous relaxation of quantization is a technically sound and clever solution enabling tractable end-to-end optimization.

- Empirical results show consistent PSNR and MS-SSIM gains across datasets and strong qualitative improvements even when optimized for MSE.

- Positions the work well in literature and clearly distinguishes the approach from generative models and related nonlinear transforms.

- Request for quantitative computational complexity/timing comparisons with JPEG and JPEG 2000 to support efficiency claims.

- Suggest more comprehensive comparisons of activation functions (GDN/IGDN vs ReLU/sigmoid) to clarify GDN’s specific contribution.

- Note that training separate models per λ is computationally expensive; recommend exploring single-model multi-rate approaches.

- Architecture choices (filter counts/sizes) appear ad-hoc; a sensitivity analysis would help assess robustness.

- Question: Provide quantitative timing or FLOPs comparisons for encoding/decoding versus JPEG and JPEG 2000.

- Question: Elaborate specific rate-distortion and visual-quality differences between GDN/IGDN and simpler nonlinearities.

- Question: Clarify how JPEG 2000 rate points were selected for comparisons and whether selection affected conclusions.

- Question: Would optimizing directly for a perceptual metric (e.g., MS-SSIM) further improve visual quality and how would it affect rate-distortion trade-offs?
