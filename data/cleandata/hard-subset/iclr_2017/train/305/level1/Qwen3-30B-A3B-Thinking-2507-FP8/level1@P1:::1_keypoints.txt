- Summary: Proposes end-to-end optimized nonlinear transform coding using cascaded convolutional filters and GDN nonlinearities, with a continuous relaxation of quantization to enable SGD; shows better rate–distortion and MS-SSIM than JPEG/JPEG 2000 despite MSE training.

- Decision: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions.

- Contribution significance: Addresses an important problem and demonstrates that joint end-to-end optimization of nonlinear transform coding substantially outperforms traditional methods.

- Theoretical foundation: Provides a strong theoretical treatment, relating the approach to variational autoencoders while highlighting key differences that favor compression.

- Empirical evidence: Convincing, well-documented comparisons across metrics and images, with particularly compelling visual/perceptual improvements.

- Writing and clarity: Paper is well-written and technically sound.

- Revision point — quantization relaxation: Need clearer explanation of how the continuous relaxation during training maps to effective discrete compression and any potential train–test mismatch.

- Revision point — complexity and speed: Require detailed computational complexity and inference speed numbers to assess practicality relative to standard codecs.

- Revision point — nonlinearities ablation: Include results comparing GDN to simpler activation functions to isolate the contribution of biologically inspired nonlinearities versus end-to-end optimization.

- Revision point — video extension: Add discussion or preliminary thoughts on extending the approach to video compression.

- Question — quantizer mismatch: How do the authors ensure models trained with continuous relaxation perform well with actual uniform quantization, and what empirical evidence supports no mismatch?

- Question — image-specific PSNR behavior: What image characteristics cause only marginal PSNR gains at high bit rates for some images, and do these correspond to images where traditional methods already excel?

- Question — multi-λ training cost: How does computational cost scale with training separate models per λ, and is it feasible to train a single model that operates across multiple rate–distortion points without retraining?
