- Proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression framework jointly training analysis transform, uniform quantizer (relaxed), and synthesis transform.  
- Uses cascades of linear convolutions and biologically-inspired GDN/IGDN nonlinearities in the transforms.  
- Introduces a continuous relaxation of quantization to enable gradient-based optimization of rate-distortion.  
- Demonstrates superior rate-distortion and visual quality (notably MS-SSIM) versus JPEG and JPEG 2000 across bit rates.  
- Clarifies relation to variational autoencoders while highlighting critical differences for compression tasks.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical justification and analysis of the relaxation’s accuracy in approximating discrete rate-distortion.  
- Presents comprehensive experiments on standard test images using PSNR and MS-SSIM and reports actual bit rates (not just entropy estimates).  
- Shows practical implementability with details on entropy coding and efficient encode/decode despite higher training cost.  
- Decision: Accept — significant, novel, and technically sound contribution with clear empirical gains.  
- Recommend adding detailed computational complexity/training vs encode-decode trade-off (preferably a comparison table).  
- Suggest including results for simpler nonlinearities (ReLU/sigmoid) to substantiate GDN/IGDN advantages.  
- Advise plotting rate-distortion curves across different λ values and expanding discussion comparing their CABAC to JPEG 2000’s entropy coding.  
- Question: Which specific images did the method underperform on versus JPEG/JPEG 2000, and why?  
- Question: What quantitative results (rate-distortion, visual quality, model size, compute) were observed for simpler nonlinearities?
