- Proposes a novel nonlinear transform coding method achieving state-of-the-art rate–distortion and improved visual quality vs. JPEG/JPEG2000.  
- Introduces an end-to-end optimization framework that jointly trains analysis/synthesis transforms and the entropy code using a SGD variant.  
- Employs generalized divisive normalization (GDN) nonlinearity, inspired by neural models, to model natural image statistics.  
- Explores and contrasts the relationship between the proposed method and variational autoencoders.  
- Decision: Accept — paper makes significant contributions with potential high impact.  
- Novelty/significance: Distinct approach with potential for substantial rate–distortion and visual-quality gains.  
- Technical soundness: Well-motivated and technically solid, grounded in image statistics.  
- Experimental results: Comprehensive evaluations showing advantages over traditional codecs.  
- Suggestion: Compare to other state-of-the-art learned methods (e.g., Toderici et al. 2016, Gregor et al. 2016).  
- Suggestion: Include ablation studies to quantify each component’s contribution (e.g., effect of GDN).  
- Suggestion: Report additional visual-quality metrics (PSNR, SSIM) alongside MS-SSIM.  
- Question: How will the method be extended to other data types such as video or audio?  
- Question: Can authors provide deeper insights into the relationship and potential combination with variational autoencoders?  
- Question: How will computational complexity be addressed for practical deployment?
