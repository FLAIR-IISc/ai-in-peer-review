- Presents a novel image compression method using cascaded linear convolutions and a GDN-based nonlinear transform, jointly optimized for rate-distortion with SGD.  
- Model pipeline: nonlinear analysis transform → uniform quantizer → nonlinear synthesis transform.  
- Motivated by limitations of linear transform coding and biological inspiration for GDN joint nonlinearity.  
- Compared against JPEG and JPEG 2000, showing significant improvements in rate-distortion and visual quality.  
- Thorough experiments with multiple metrics (PSNR, MS-SSIM) and clear presentation of results.  
- Paper is well-written, methods and optimization procedure are clearly described.  
- Decision: accept based on novelty, strong motivation, and superior empirical performance.  
- Request more optimization details: hyperparameter choices and convergence criteria.  
- Suggest more visualizations of compressed (especially RGB) images to demonstrate visual quality.  
- Recommend discussing potential applications (storage, transmission) of the method.  
- Questions for authors: provide hyperparameter details for optimization.  
- Questions for authors: plans to extend the approach to other data types (video, audio).  
- Questions for authors: consideration of alternative nonlinearities (ReLU, sigmoid, etc.).
