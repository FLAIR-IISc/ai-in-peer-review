**Summary**

This paper presents a novel image compression method based on nonlinear transform coding. The method consists of a nonlinear analysis transformation, a uniform quantizer, and a nonlinear synthesis transformation. The transforms are constructed using cascades of linear convolutions and nonlinear activation functions, specifically a generalized divisive normalization (GDN) joint nonlinearity inspired by biological neurons. The entire model is jointly optimized for rate-distortion performance over a database of training images using stochastic gradient descent. The method is compared to standard JPEG and JPEG 2000 compression methods, and the results show improved rate-distortion performance and visual quality.

**Decision**

I decide to accept this paper, with the following key reasons:

1. The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to image compression, which is a fundamental problem in engineering.
2. The method is thoroughly evaluated and compared to standard compression methods, and the results show significant improvements in rate-distortion performance and visual quality.

**Supporting arguments**

The paper provides a clear and concise introduction to the problem of image compression and the challenges of joint optimization of rate and distortion. The authors motivate their approach by highlighting the limitations of traditional linear transform coding methods and the potential benefits of using nonlinear transformations. The method is well-explained, and the optimization procedure is clearly described.

The experimental results are thorough and well-presented, with comparisons to standard compression methods and evaluations using multiple metrics (PSNR, MS-SSIM). The results show significant improvements in rate-distortion performance and visual quality, which is consistent with the authors' claims.

**Additional feedback**

While the paper is well-written and clear, there are a few areas that could be improved:

* The authors could provide more details on the optimization procedure, such as the choice of hyperparameters and the convergence criteria.
* The paper could benefit from more visualizations of the compressed images, particularly for the RGB images.
* The authors could discuss the potential applications of their method, such as image compression for storage or transmission.

**Questions for the authors**

* Can you provide more details on the choice of hyperparameters for the optimization procedure?
* How do you plan to extend this method to other types of data, such as video or audio?
* Have you considered using other types of nonlinear transformations, such as rectified linear units or sigmoidal functions?
