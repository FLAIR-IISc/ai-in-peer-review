- Novel end-to-end learned lossy image compression using a non-linear autoencoder with biologically-inspired GDN nonlinearities.  
- Continuous relaxation of quantization by replacing it with additive uniform noise enables end-to-end SGD training.  
- Insightful connection drawn between the relaxed rate-distortion loss and VAEs, with clear differences between compression and generative modeling.  
- Full entropy coding implemented; method outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 on Kodak with significant perceptual quality gains (MS-SSIM and visuals).  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main reason 1: Novel and effective solution to end-to-end optimization in presence of quantization via the continuous relaxation.  
- Main reason 2: State-of-the-art rate-distortion performance and substantially better visual quality across bit rates.  
- Strong motivation and placement, bridging transform coding, computational neuroscience (GDN), and deep generative models; Section 3.1 is particularly insightful.  
- Technical soundness: additive-noise proxy for quantization is well-justified and empirically validated; CABAC-based entropy coder used for actual bit-rate reporting.  
- Empirical results: clear RD advantage (especially under MS-SSIM) and qualitatively more natural outputs that preserve edges while simplifying texture.  
- Suggestion: report quantitative computational complexity (e.g., MACs/pixel) versus JPEG 2000 to assess practical viability.  
- Suggestion: include architecture ablation studies to justify choices (number of stages, subsampling, etc.).  
- Suggestion: acknowledge limitation that a separate model is trained per rate (λ) and discuss potential multi-rate approaches.  
- Question: provide quantitative data on how much larger/deeper a ReLU-based network must be to match GDN performance.  
- Question: report any training instabilities or low-rate performance degradation due to the additive-noise quantization approximation.  
- Question: quantify how much performance gain comes from learned transforms versus the entropy coder and whether a stronger spatial-context coder could yield further gains.
