- Proposes an end-to-end learned image compression framework using deep convolutional transforms and GDN nonlinearity.  
- Introduces additive uniform noise as a differentiable proxy for quantization, enabling joint SGD optimization of a rate-distortion objective.  
- Demonstrates performance gains over JPEG and JPEG 2000 on PSNR and perceptual quality, producing fewer common compression artifacts.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Strength: novel and elegant solution to quantization non-differentiability enabling end-to-end training.  
- Strength: strong empirical results showing convincing perceptual improvement over engineered codecs.  
- Methodology: principled continuous relaxation of the loss to enable gradient-based learning for rate-distortion.  
- Experiments: thorough validation with a full entropy coder and reported actual bit rates on a standard dataset.  
- Visual and metric evidence: preserves contours and avoids blocky/ringing artifacts; MS-SSIM shows consistent advantage across bit rates.  
- Clarity: clearly distinguishes the approach from variational autoencoders.  
- Suggestion: provide quantitative comparison of computational complexity (e.g., operations-per-pixel) versus JPEG 2000.  
- Suggestion: quantify GDN’s parameter-efficiency relative to standard activations like ReLU in the main paper.  
- Question: can a single rate-adaptive model be trained to target multiple bit rates instead of one model per λ?  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to the non-parametric piecewise-linear model of latent coefficient distributions, and could a simpler parametric model suffice?
