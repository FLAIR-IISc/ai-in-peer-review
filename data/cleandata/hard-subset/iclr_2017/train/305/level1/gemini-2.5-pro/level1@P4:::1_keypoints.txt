- Proposes an end-to-end learned lossy image compression framework with nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms, uniform scalar quantization, and GDN nonlinearities.  
- Enables gradient-based training despite non-differentiable quantization by replacing quantizer with additive uniform noise as a continuous relaxation.  
- Draws a connection between the relaxed rate-distortion objective and Variational Autoencoders, while distinguishing compression goals from generative modeling.  
- Implements a full entropy coder (CABAC) to report actual bit rates rather than relying on entropy estimates.  
- Empirical results show the method outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 in PSNR across most bit rates and yields substantial perceptual quality gains (MS-SSIM and visual inspection).  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Justification: High novelty and technical soundness, particularly the continuous relaxation technique enabling end-to-end optimization.  
- Justification: Compelling empirical evidence and rigorous comparisons against strong baselines with actual bit-rate reporting.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, with insightful discussion (Section 3.1) and effective figures.  
- Continuous relaxation is a principled approximation and is empirically validated (Figure 4).  
- Architecture choice (GDN) is justified by prior work on natural image statistics; visual results reduce blocking and ringing artifacts.  
- Work is novel and significant, representing a landmark application of deep learning to image compression and enabling nonlinear, data-driven transforms to surpass traditional linear transforms.  
- Suggestion: Discuss trade-offs of training separate models per λ versus a single scalable model covering multiple rates.  
- Suggestion: Quantify the performance/capacity difference between GDN and ReLU-based networks to strengthen activation choice.  
- Suggestion: Acknowledge remaining latent-space correlations and discuss potential gains from a more sophisticated context model in the entropy coder.  
- Question: How would one compress at intermediate rates not corresponding to a pre-trained λ—retrain or interpolate model parameters?  
- Question: Clarify the apparent contradiction about subsampling factors and the final dimensionality of latent representation y relative to input x.  
- Question: Explain why the relaxed entropy estimate is positively biased at low rates and why that bias diminishes with increasing rate.
