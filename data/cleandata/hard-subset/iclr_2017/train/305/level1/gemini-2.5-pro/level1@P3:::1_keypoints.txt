- Novel end-to-end optimized lossy image compression framework that learns nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms via SGD.  
- Multi-stage convolutional autoencoder using biologically-inspired Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN) nonlinearity.  
- Continuous relaxation of non-differentiable quantization by replacing it with additive uniform noise to enable gradient-based optimization of rate-distortion.  
- Insightful connection between the relaxed loss and Variational Autoencoders, with clear distinction between compression and generative modeling objectives.  
- Full practical evaluation including implementation of CABAC entropy coding to produce actual bitstreams, not just entropy estimates.  
- Empirical results outperform JPEG and JPEG 2000 in PSNR and show large, consistent perceptual quality improvements (visuals and MS-SSIM).  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Strength: principled joint optimization replaces separately designed components of traditional codecs, adapting transforms to data, quantizer, and rate-distortion trade-off.  
- Strength: the additive-noise quantizer proxy is an effective, trainable approximation enabling deep nonlinear transform training (supported by Figure 4).  
- Architectural claim: GDN is motivated by natural image statistics and appears more efficient than standard nonlinearities like ReLU for this task.  
- Rigor: reporting actual compressed sizes and CABAC use demonstrates practical feasibility beyond theoretical models.  
- Suggestion: report quantitative computational complexity (encoding/decoding times, FLOPs) compared to baselines to assess practicality.  
- Suggestion: provide ablation comparing GDN to simpler nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU) with similar parameter counts to isolate GDN’s contribution.  
- Suggestion: address impracticality of training separate models per rate (λ); discuss or develop rate-adaptive single-model approaches.  
- Suggestion: improve entropy model beyond marginal independence to exploit spatial correlations (context-adaptive models) for additional gains.  
- Questions for authors: provide encoder/decoder timing and platform comparisons, detail whether ReLUs require larger networks to match GDN, propose strategies for rate-adaptive inference, and estimate gains/approaches for spatially-aware entropy models.
