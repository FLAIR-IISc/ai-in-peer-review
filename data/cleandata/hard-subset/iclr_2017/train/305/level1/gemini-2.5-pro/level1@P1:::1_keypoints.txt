- Presents an end-to-end deep neural image compression framework with encoder, uniform-quantizer (relaxed), and decoder using GDN nonlinearities.
- Introduces continuous relaxation of quantization by adding uniform noise to enable end-to-end SGD optimization.
- Provides a theoretical connection to variational autoencoders while emphasizing different objectives.
- Implements a complete codec (arithmetic entropy coder) and reports actual bit rates.
- Demonstrates improved PSNR and substantially better MS-SSIM and perceptual quality vs. JPEG and JPEG 2000 on Kodak.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Novel, principled optimization framework—additive-noise relaxation is an elegant, effective solution for differentiable quantization.
- Key reason 2: State-of-the-art empirical results with both quantitative gains and superior visual quality (replaces blocking/ringing with natural smoothing).
- Empirical validation shows the relaxation is a reliable proxy for discrete rate-distortion and evaluations use a fair, full-codec comparison.
- Architectural choice: GDN is a strong, parameter-efficient inductive bias that helps achieve high performance with a compact architecture.
- Suggested improvement: support a single model across multiple bitrates (e.g., conditioning on λ) to avoid training separate models per rate.
- Suggested improvement: include an ablation directly comparing GDN to ReLU-based networks with comparable parameter counts.
- Suggested improvement: enhance the entropy model to exploit spatial dependencies (e.g., autoregressive priors) for further rate gains.
- Questions for authors: confirm piecewise-linear latent density parameters are fixed for a given λ and not sent as side information; provide encoder/decoder computational complexity or runtime vs. JPEG 2000; explain how λ values were chosen and whether sparse training yields a smooth R–D curve.
