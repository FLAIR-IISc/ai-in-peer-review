- Novel end-to-end image compression optimizing analysis, synthesis, and entropy code jointly using a GDN nonlinear transform.
- Uses additive uniform noise as a continuous relaxation to enable gradient-based optimization despite non-differentiable quantization.
- Implements an actual entropy coder (CABAC) and reports real bit rates; shows better rate-distortion and visual quality than JPEG and JPEG 2000 for most images.
- Decision: Accept — significant advance with rigorous, well-motivated approach and strong empirical evidence.
- Addresses limitation of traditional methods that optimize transform, quantizer, and entropy code separately.
- GDN is claimed to Gaussianize image densities and improve compression efficiency over linear transforms.
- Continuous relaxation approach is clever and empirically validated (Figure 4).
- Implementation details and entropy coding make this a complete, not just partial, solution.
- Quantitative metrics (PSNR, MS-SSIM) and qualitative visuals (Figures 5–7) show substantial improvements across bit rates.
- Paper positions itself clearly relative to VAEs and generative-model work.
- Suggest clarifying why GDN specifically improves compression and how its parameters relate to rate-distortion.
- Recommend including comparisons with newer codecs (WebP, BPG, Toderici et al.) to strengthen relevance.
- Request more training details: dataset specifics, training time, compute resources, and convergence iterations.
- Ask to clarify whether a single representation supports all rate points or if models are optimized per λ.
- Request quantitative comparisons of GDN vs. ReLU/sigmoid on same dataset for RD and visual quality.
- Ask authors to include results from models with simpler nonlinearities in supplementary material.
- Request details on which images underperform relative to JPEG 2000 and explanations.
- Ask how much a more advanced, spatially adaptive entropy coder would reduce the gap with JPEG 2000.
