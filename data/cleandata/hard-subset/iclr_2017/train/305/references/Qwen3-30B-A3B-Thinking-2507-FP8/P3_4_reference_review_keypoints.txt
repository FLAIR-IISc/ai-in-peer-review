- Proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression framework using cascaded nonlinear transforms with GDN, jointly optimizing transforms, quantizer (via continuous relaxation), and entropy code.  
- Introduces a continuous relaxation technique to handle quantization during gradient-based optimization.  
- Implements a full compression system reporting actual bit rates (not just entropy estimates).  
- Demonstrates superior rate-distortion and perceptual performance compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000, despite training with MSE.  
- Situates the work within rate-distortion theory and argues against separate optimization of transform, quantizer, and entropy code.  
- Distinguishes the compression approach from generative modeling/VAEs while drawing useful theoretical connections.  
- Validates the relaxed loss approximation to actual rate-distortion behavior (fig. 4).  
- Provides comprehensive experiments across PSNR, MS-SSIM, multiple datasets, bit rates, and strong visual comparisons showing reduced blocking artifacts.  
- Notes the surprising result that MSE-trained models produce improved perceptual quality (MS-SSIM).  
- Recommends accept with minor revisions, citing theoretical soundness and strong empirical evidence.  
- Requests a brief discussion in the main text on challenges of comparing rate-distortion curves across images and their online per-image plots.  
- Asks for quantified encoding/decoding (inference) speed comparisons to JPEG/JPEG 2000 for practical deployment concerns.  
- Suggests a more systematic ablation study comparing activation functions and architectures to support the claimed efficiency of GDN/IGDN.  
- Requests a short theoretical discussion of why MSE training yields perceptual improvements.  
- Seeks clarification on whether perceptual advantages truly hold for all images/bitrates given a counterexample in figure 15.  
- Asks for elaboration on how GDN’s specific functional form aids rate-distortion optimization beyond density estimation.  
- Questions how much performance loss is due to the CABAC implementation’s crude spatial adaptation versus the compression model itself.
