- Proposes end-to-end optimized image compression using cascaded convolutions + GDN nonlinearities, uniform quantization relaxed with additive uniform noise, and joint SGD optimization of analysis, synthesis, and entropy coding.

- Claims superior rate-distortion (PSNR) and perceptual quality (MS-SSIM) versus JPEG and JPEG 2000, with fewer blocking/ringing artifacts.

- Decision: Accept — judged a significant technical contribution with strong empirical evidence.

- Key novelty: continuous relaxation of quantization via additive uniform noise to enable backpropagation through the quantizer.

- Theoretical support: the relaxed density approximates the discrete PMF and the noise models quantization error in marginal moments.

- Experimental rigor: comparisons to JPEG/JPEG2000, PSNR and MS-SSIM metrics, visual examples, ablation studies, related-work comparisons, and actual bitrate reporting with an entropy coder.

- Results summary: generally better rate-distortion performance across most images/bitrates and consistently improved perceptual quality.

- Clarity: paper is well-written and figures effectively illustrate concepts and results.

- Suggestion: report concrete computational complexity, encoding/decoding times, and memory usage compared to JPEG/JPEG2000.

- Suggestion: discuss or demonstrate scalability to higher resolutions (e.g., 1080p, 4K).

- Suggestion: include more detailed ablation comparing GDN/IGDN to simpler nonlinearities (ReLU, sigmoid) for performance and model size.

- Suggestion: investigate training with perceptual metrics (train with MS-SSIM instead of MSE) to assess visual-quality gains.

- Question: provide encoding/decoding runtimes on standard hardware for various image sizes.

- Question: how would results change if the model were trained with MS-SSIM rather than MSE — would visual improvements increase?

- Question: provide detailed analysis quantifying how much performance gain is due to GDN/IGDN versus other architectural choices.

- Question: which image types (natural scenes vs. textures) benefit most from the method?

- Question: share any preliminary results on models with simpler nonlinearities to show the contribution of GDN/IGDN.
