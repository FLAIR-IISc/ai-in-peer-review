1. Proposes end-to-end optimized image compression using cascaded nonlinear transforms (GDN) jointly trained with quantization and entropy coding via SGD.  
2. Introduces a biologically-inspired cascaded GDN/IGDN transform architecture for better image representation.  
3. Uses a continuous relaxation of quantization (additive uniform noise) to enable gradient-based training, with theoretical and empirical justification.  
4. Shows superior rate-distortion performance and perceptual quality compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000 across tested images and bit rates (PSNR and MS-SSIM).  
5. Provides visual results demonstrating reduced compression artifacts consistent with quantitative metrics.  
6. Argues and analyzes why nonlinear cascaded transforms outperform linear transforms for natural image statistics.  
7. Clarifies the relationship to variational autoencoders and distinguishes the method from generative modeling approaches.  
8. Decision: Accept — strong contribution, well-motivated and rigorously evaluated.  
9. Suggestion: Quantify “most images” by reporting the percentage or count where the method outperforms baselines.  
10. Suggestion: Include comparisons with more recent deep learning-based compression methods to better situate the work.  
11. Suggestion: Clarify how using a more advanced entropy coder (vs. simple CABAC) might change comparisons to JPEG 2000.  
12. Suggestion: Explain why optimizing for MSE yields improved perceptual quality, possibly via analysis of learned representations.  
13. Question: Explain the single image where the method underperforms JPEG 2000 at high bit rates and whether this generalizes.  
14. Question: Share preliminary results comparing GDN to simpler nonlinearities (ReLU, sigmoid) and parameter-efficiency tradeoffs.  
15. Question: Would a more sophisticated symbol ordering for entropy coding (like JPEG 2000) substantially improve bit rates and affect comparisons?  
16. Question: Provide analysis of the statistical properties of learned representations to explain the MSE–perceptual quality disconnect.  
17. Question: Discuss the RGB-first-stage filters versus channel-separate transforms (e.g., Y'CbCr) and whether different color transforms could improve performance.
