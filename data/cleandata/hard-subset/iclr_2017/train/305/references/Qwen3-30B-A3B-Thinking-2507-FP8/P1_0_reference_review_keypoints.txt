- Novel end-to-end image compression framework using cascaded nonlinear transforms (GDN) and a continuous relaxation of quantization to enable gradient-based joint optimization.
- Demonstrated superior rate-distortion (PSNR) and perceptual quality (MS-SSIM) versus JPEG and JPEG 2000, with fewer blocking/ringing artifacts.
- Thorough theoretical formulation connecting relaxed rate-distortion optimization to but distinguishing from variational autoencoders and generative models.
- Strong empirical validation with visual comparisons, multiple metrics, and extensive figures; appendix provides reproducibility details (architecture, optimization, entropy coding).
- Decision: Accept.
- Main acceptance reasons: significant methodological advance in end-to-end nonlinear transform coding and rigorous, multi-metric experimental results.
- Well-written and well-organized presentation with effective figures and sufficient implementation detail.
- Suggestion: provide quantitative estimates of computational complexity and runtime for training/encoding/decoding versus JPEG/JPEG 2000 for practical deployment.
- Suggestion: expand comparisons to newer baselines like BPG and other deep-learning-based compression methods.
- Suggestion: more explicit discussion of limitations and which image characteristics lead to worse performance on some test images.
- Suggestion: include ablation studies to clarify how GDN specifically contributes to compression performance versus other nonlinearities.
- Question: provide details on images and characteristics where the method underperforms in PSNR and why.
- Question: recommend specific metrics or evaluation approaches to fairly compare methods across high/low bit rates and image sets.
- Question: provide quantitative comparisons of parameter counts and computational complexity between GDN-based models and comparable ReLU models.
- Question: clarify whether the performance advantages hold consistently across grayscale and RGB images and how separate RGB models perform.
