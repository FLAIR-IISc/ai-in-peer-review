- Introduces end-to-end optimized image compression with joint optimization of transform, quantizer, and entropy coding using nonlinear transforms (cascaded convolutions + GDN).  
- Uses continuous relaxation via additive uniform noise to enable gradient-based optimization through quantization.  
- Pipeline includes analysis/synthesis transforms, uniform scalar quantization, approximate inverse transforms, and entropy coding.  
- Reports improved rate-distortion (PSNR) and perceptual quality (MS-SSIM) versus JPEG and JPEG 2000.  
- Links nonlinear transforms and visual quality improvements to biological visual processing mechanisms.  
- Distinguishes the approach from variational autoencoders, emphasizing different optimization objectives relevant to compression.  
- Provides comprehensive theoretical foundations, implementation details (including CABAC-based entropy coding), and thorough empirical evaluation across datasets.  
- Decision: Accept — contribution is significant, well-motivated, and rigorously evaluated.  
- Continuous relaxation is clever and shown to approximate actual rate-distortion well; actual bit rates are reported rather than entropy estimates.  
- Empirical results are compelling with notable visual quality gains and better rate-distortion for most images and bit rates.  
- Paper clearly explains why focusing on achievable rate-distortion points differs from minimizing differential entropy.  
- Suggest clarifying the gap between the continuous relaxation and actual bit rates with explicit analysis.  
- Suggest expanding explanation of why MSE-optimized models yield better visual quality than JPEG/JPEG 2000.  
- Recommend analyzing and presenting failure cases where the method underperforms in PSNR.  
- Advise comparing to more recent deep learning compression methods beyond Toderici et al. (2016).  
- Request discussion of computational trade-offs: training vs. encoding/decoding complexity compared to JPEG/JPEG 2000.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to λ and whether certain λ values suit specific image types.  
- Questions about GDN: how it yields efficiency advantages over pointwise nonlinearities and generalizes to other nonlinearities.  
- Ask how raster-scan entropy coding ordering affects final bit rate vs. more sophisticated schemes and potential gains.  
- Request preliminary results comparing GDN/IGDN to simpler nonlinearities (ReLU/sigmoid) to quantify contribution of nonlinearity choice.  
- Ask how performance depends on training data size/diversity and whether larger or curated datasets would improve results.
