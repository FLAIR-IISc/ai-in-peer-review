- Proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression framework using cascaded convolutions with GDN and a continuous relaxation of quantization for gradient-based training.  
- Jointly optimizes analysis/synthesis transforms and entropy coding, yielding fewer artifacts and more natural images despite MSE-based training.  
- Reports consistent improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000 in PSNR and MS-SSIM, with visually superior results across tested images and bit rates.  
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions due to significant contribution and strong results.  
- Provides a clear theoretical justification, distinguishing the method from VAEs and motivating continuous quantization relaxation.  
- Uses biologically inspired GDN nonlinearities and shows they outperform simpler nonlinearities in rate–distortion experiments.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: multiple image sets, metrics, actual bit rates, and ablation studies.  
- Demonstrates novelty and impact by showing end-to-end optimization of the whole pipeline improves perceptual quality and reduces common artifacts.  
- Requests more detailed comparisons to recent neural-network compression methods (e.g., Toderici et al., Gregor et al.).  
- Asks for a computational complexity analysis (training time, inference time, memory) to assess practical deployability.  
- Seeks clarification on the link between MS-SSIM improvements and subjective perceptual quality with concrete example analyses.  
- Asks why one image (figure 15) underperforms JPEG 2000 at high rates and whether this is a general limitation.  
- Questions how to reconcile universal MS-SSIM gains with non-universal PSNR gains across images/bit rates.  
- Requests more details on ablation results comparing GDN to ReLU (model size vs. performance trade-offs).  
- Asks how much of the performance gap is due to the relatively simple CABAC-based entropy coder versus what could be gained with a more sophisticated coder.
