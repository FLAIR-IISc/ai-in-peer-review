

# Review of "END-TO-END OPTIMIZED IMAGE COMPRESSION"

1. Summary:
The paper introduces a novel end-to-end framework for optimizing image compression using nonlinear transforms, addressing the longstanding challenge of joint rate-distortion optimization. The authors propose a model consisting of cascaded linear convolutions and Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN) nonlinearities for the analysis transform, followed by uniform scalar quantization and an approximate inverse transform for reconstruction. The key innovation is the use of a continuous relaxation (additive uniform noise) to handle the non-differentiable quantization step, enabling end-to-end training via stochastic gradient descent. The approach demonstrates superior rate-distortion performance compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000 across multiple metrics and image sets, with particularly impressive improvements in perceptual quality as measured by MS-SSIM.

2. Decision:
Accept. The paper makes a significant contribution to the image compression field with a technically sound approach that effectively addresses a well-known challenge in the field.

3. Supporting arguments:
The paper successfully tackles the core problem of joint optimization in lossy compression through a well-motivated approach. The continuous relaxation of quantization using additive uniform noise is theoretically well-justified and empirically validated (Figure 4), providing a practical solution to the gradient discontinuity problem. The empirical results are comprehensive and convincing: the method demonstrates consistent improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000 across multiple metrics (PSNR and MS-SSIM) and image sets, with perceptual quality improvements holding across all tested bit rates. The paper provides a clear theoretical analysis of the relationship to variational autoencoders while highlighting key differences that make their approach more appropriate for compression, strengthening the paper's theoretical foundation.

4. Additional feedback:
- The paper would benefit from more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of encoding and decoding with the proposed method compared to standard compression methods, particularly for resource-constrained applications.
- While the paper demonstrates visual improvements, a more systematic analysis of the specific types of compression artifacts eliminated by the approach would strengthen the perceptual quality claims.
- The authors mention exploring simpler nonlinearities but only provide preliminary results. A more complete comparison of different nonlinearity types with respect to performance, model size, and computational requirements would be valuable for understanding the full scope of the contribution.

5. Questions for authors:
- Could you provide more specific metrics on the computational complexity (in terms of FLOPs or inference time) of your method compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000 for both encoding and decoding?
- The paper states that GDN/IGDN transforms are more efficient than pointwise nonlinearities, but the comparison is limited to qualitative observations. Could you provide quantitative metrics on the model size (number of parameters) and computational requirements needed to achieve equivalent rate-distortion performance?
- The empirical validation of the continuous relaxation (Figure 4) is limited to the training set. Could you provide additional validation on a separate test set to further confirm the quality of this approximation?
