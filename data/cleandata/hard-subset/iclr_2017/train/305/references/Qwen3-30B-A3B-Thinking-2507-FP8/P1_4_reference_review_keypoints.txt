- Proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression framework using cascaded convolutions and GDN, with continuous relaxation of quantization to enable gradient-based joint optimization.

- Uses uniform scalar quantization, an approximate inverse transform for reconstruction, and trains the entire pipeline with stochastic gradient descent.

- Demonstrates superior rate–distortion performance and visually fewer artifacts than JPEG and JPEG 2000 across most images and bit rates.

- Provides a detailed analysis of the relationship to variational autoencoders and highlights fundamental differences between compression and generative modeling.

- Recommendation: accept.

- Key reasons for acceptance: addresses a fundamental problem with a well-motivated, novel approach and rigorous validation.

- Key reasons for acceptance: impressive quantitative (PSNR, MS-SSIM) and qualitative results evident in visual examples.

- Paper is well-written, thoroughly motivated, and places work in context with classical and recent deep-learning compression methods.

- Continuous relaxation of quantization is clearly explained and empirically shown to approximate the discrete problem well.

- Experimental evaluation is robust: comparisons to JPEG/JPEG 2000 on multiple datasets (Kodak, standard test images, authors’ photos) with compelling visual comparisons.

- Shows that end-to-end optimization of the full compression pipeline yields substantial improvements over separately optimized traditional components.

- Claim that GDN/IGDN transforms are more efficient than pointwise nonlinearities; fewer parameters/stages required (claim needs more concrete evidence).

- Suggests comparison to more recent learned compression methods (e.g., Toderici et al., Gregor et al.) to better contextualize results.

- Notes one image (figure 15) where the method slightly outperforms JPEG 2000 at low rates but underperforms at high rates; requests analysis of this case.

- Recommends statistical analysis across the full test set (e.g., paired t-tests) to strengthen perceptual-advantage claims.

- Requests specific parameter counts to substantiate the efficiency claim for GDN/IGDN versus pointwise nonlinearities.

- Asks why separate RGB models were trained instead of a joint first-stage color-channel optimization and why that architecture was chosen.

- Asks for analysis of which image features or attributes are better captured by the proposed nonlinear cascade compared to traditional methods.

- Requests elaboration on how GDN’s mathematical relation to sigmoidal functions connects to observed compression efficiency.

- Asks whether additive-noise relaxation could improve robustness in supervised classification and whether this hypothesis has been tested.

- Asks how interpreting the rate–distortion objective as an unsupervised learning/density estimation instantiation could be leveraged to improve other unsupervised tasks.

- Concludes the paper is a strong, significant contribution likely of interest to the ICLR community.
