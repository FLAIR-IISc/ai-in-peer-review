- Summary: Proposes end-to-end optimized image compression using cascaded convolutions + GDN, continuous quantization relaxation, and shows superior rate-distortion and visual quality vs. JPEG/JPEG2000.

- Decision: Recommend accept with minor revisions.

- Key reason 1: Demonstrates significant gains from optimizing the entire compression pipeline end-to-end.

- Key reason 2: Approach is well-motivated, technically sound, and clearly situated within related work.

- Theoretical strength: Provides strong justification for nonlinear transforms (GDN) to remove higher-order dependencies left by linear transforms.

- Theoretical strength: Clearly distinguishes the method from variational autoencoders and addresses related confusions.

- Technical strength: Uses a continuous relaxation of quantization to enable gradient-based optimization, validated empirically.

- Empirical strength: Comprehensive comparisons against JPEG and JPEG2000 on multiple datasets with both PSNR and MS-SSIM metrics.

- Empirical strength: Visual results convincingly show reduced blocking, ringing, and aliasing artifacts.

- Empirical caveat: Entropy coding uses CABAC and authors acknowledge its limitations affecting results.

- Rigor and reproducibility: Well-written with sufficient implementation and training details for replication.

- Suggested improvement 1: Compare more directly to recent deep-learning compression works (e.g., Toderici et al., Gregor et al.).

- Suggested improvement 2: Provide detailed computational complexity analysis for training and inference versus JPEG/JPEG2000.

- Suggested improvement 3: Strengthen evidence for GDN efficiency with comparisons of model size, parameters, and performance across activations.

- Suggested improvement 4: Quantify perceptual (MS-SSIM) improvements across image categories and bit rates.

- Question 1: Request concrete statistics on MS-SSIM improvement variability across image types and bit-rate ranges.

- Question 2: Ask for comparative rate-distortion and complexity discussion versus Toderici et al. (2016) and explanation why this method is more rate-distortion optimal.

- Question 3: Request detailed results comparing GDN to other activation functions with specific numbers.

- Question 4: Ask how results would change using a more sophisticated entropy coder than CABAC and how that affects comparisons.

- Overall assessment: Strong, well-executed contribution showing clear benefits of end-to-end optimization and worthy of publication, with minor suggested enhancements.
