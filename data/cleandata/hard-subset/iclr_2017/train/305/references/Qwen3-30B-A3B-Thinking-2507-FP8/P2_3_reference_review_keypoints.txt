- Novel end-to-end image compression jointly optimizes transform, quantizer, synthesis, and entropy code using cascaded GDN, uniform quantization relaxation, and gradient-based training, yielding better PSNR and perceptual quality than JPEG/JPEG2000.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant contribution with strong theoretical motivation and compelling empirical evidence.  
- Well-motivated critique of traditional separately-optimized transform coding and clear positioning within related work, including links to biological vision.  
- Strong theoretical foundation: continuous relaxation of quantization via additive uniform noise is well-justified and enables practical rate–distortion optimization.  
- Empirical results are thorough: consistent PSNR and visual improvements on Kodak and other datasets, with qualitative avoidance of blocking/ringing artifacts.  
- Performance varies by image but generally provides visual improvements; some images remain failure cases.  
- Suggestion: include comparative analysis of nonlinearities (ReLU, sigmoid vs. GDN) on parameters, rate–distortion, and visual quality.  
- Suggestion: analyze and explain failure cases (e.g., fig.15) to identify image characteristics that hurt performance.  
- Suggestion: discuss scalability and applicability to video or other multimedia formats.  
- Question: specify which images underperform and what image characteristics explain poorer performance.  
- Question: explain why MS-SSIM/perceptual gains (e.g., “Lena”) are more pronounced at lower bit rates.  
- Question: provide concrete training time and computational resource details and comparisons to other methods.  
- Question: how does performance scale with larger training sets and where do diminishing returns occur?  
- Question: how sensitive is final performance to the choice of relaxation/noise distribution used during training?  
- Overall conclusion: a strong, rigorously supported end-to-end optimization framework that meaningfully advances image compression and merits publication.
