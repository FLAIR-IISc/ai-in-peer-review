- Proposes end-to-end optimized image compression using cascaded convolutions with GDN/IGDN nonlinearities and a continuous relaxation of quantization for gradient-based training.
- Key contribution: continuous relaxation of quantization enabling joint optimization of transform, quantizer, and entropy coding, improving rate–distortion.
- Shows superior empirical performance vs. JPEG and JPEG 2000 using PSNR and MS-SSIM, with visibly better perceptual quality across images and bit rates.
- Provides theoretical analysis relating the method to variational autoencoders and explains why the approach is better suited for compression.
- Validates that the relaxed training loss approximates true rate–distortion and supplies detailed implementation info for reproducibility.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing significant contribution, clear positioning in literature, and strong empirical evidence.
- Strength: addresses a fundamental limitation of separately optimized compression components and demonstrates substantial gains from joint optimization.
- Strength: experimental rigor with multiple images/bit rates and both quantitative and qualitative evaluations.
- Limitation noted: relatively small gains from entropy coding improvements; authors acknowledge this and discuss it.
- Suggestion: include quantitative comparisons to show why GDN/IGDN yield better compression efficiency than other nonlinearities.
- Suggestion: add analysis of computational complexity and memory requirements for practical deployment.
- Suggestion: provide more details on the entropy coding scheme and potential gains from more sophisticated, spatially-aware coders.
- Question: supply specific examples/data on alternative activations (e.g., ReLU) and how much larger networks would need to be for comparable performance.
- Question: how sensitive is performance to the choice and width of additive-noise relaxation, and would other relaxation methods perform similarly?
- Question: identify which test images the method underperforms on and explain the reasons to clarify limitations.
- Question: report insights on trade-offs (model size, training time, performance) when using simpler nonlinearities like rectified-linear or sigmoidal functions.
