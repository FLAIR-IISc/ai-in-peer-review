1. Proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression framework using cascaded GDN nonlinear transforms and uniform scalar quantization.  
2. Jointly optimizes analysis/synthesis transforms and entropy coding for rate-distortion via stochastic gradient descent.  
3. Introduces a continuous relaxation of quantization using additive uniform noise to enable gradient-based training.  
4. Demonstrates superior rate-distortion performance and markedly better visual quality than JPEG and JPEG 2000, despite optimizing for MSE.  
5. Distinguishes the method from variational autoencoders and clarifies differences between compression and generative modeling.  
6. Provides strong empirical evidence: rate-distortion curves, PSNR/MS-SSIM metrics, and visual comparisons across diverse images.  
7. Successfully addresses the optimization challenge posed by quantization; the noise relaxation approximation is empirically effective.  
8. Visual results show reduced blocking and ringing artifacts common to linear transform coding.  
9. Theoretical contribution: clarifies relationship between rate-distortion optimization and VAEs to avoid misleading comparisons.  
10. Suggests clarifying computational complexity with concrete inference-speed comparisons to JPEG/JPEG 2000 for practical adoption.  
11. Recommends expanding visual-quality analysis with qualitative explanations of why GDN preserves edges and reduces artifacts.  
12. Advises discussing limitations and how the method could be adapted to optimize perceptual metrics (e.g., MS-SSIM) instead of MSE.  
13. Asks for insight into why GDN-based transforms give superior visual results under MSE beyond natural image statistics.  
14. Requests quantitative comparisons showing how much more parameter- or layer-efficient GDN/IGDN is versus pointwise nonlinearities.  
15. Requests reproducibility details: which JPEG/JPEG 2000 implementations and settings were used (e.g., libjpeg/OpenJPEG, chroma subsampling, quant tables).  
16. Asks about sensitivity of final results to the choice of noise distribution in the continuous relaxation (e.g., uniform vs Laplacian).
