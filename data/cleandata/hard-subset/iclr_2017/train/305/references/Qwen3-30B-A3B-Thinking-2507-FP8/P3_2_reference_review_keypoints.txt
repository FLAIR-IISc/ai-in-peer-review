- Proposes an end-to-end image compression framework that jointly optimizes analysis transform, quantizer, synthesis transform, and entropy coding.  
- Uses cascaded nonlinear transforms (GDN/IGDN), continuous relaxation of quantization, uniform scalar quantization, and approximate inverse transforms.  
- Directly optimizes the rate–distortion trade-off and demonstrates improvements in PSNR and MS-SSIM over JPEG and JPEG 2000.  
- Establishes theoretical connections to variational autoencoders while clearly distinguishing compression-specific objectives.  
- Provides thorough experimental validation with strong visual results and compares against standard baselines and datasets.  
- Paper is recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Key reasons for acceptance: significant methodological advance enabling end-to-end optimization and compelling empirical/perceptual improvements.  
- Strength: methodological innovation—GDN cascades and continuous quantization relaxation tailored to rate–distortion optimization.  
- Strength: solid theoretical foundation that differentiates the approach from generative modeling (VAEs).  
- Strength: extensive empirical validation using objective and perceptual metrics and compelling visual comparisons.  
- Strength: technical rigor with detailed implementation and acknowledgement of entropy coding limitations.  
- Minor concern: lacks statistical significance testing to confirm rate–distortion improvements across datasets.  
- Minor concern: JPEG 2000 comparison details (OpenJPEG settings and fairness across bit rates) are insufficiently specified.  
- Minor concern: insufficient analysis of why MSE-optimized models improve perceptual quality (MS-SSIM).  
- Suggestion: better clarify relationship and advantages relative to prior works (Gregor et al., Toderici et al.).  
- Suggestion: report computational costs (training time, resources) to assess practical feasibility.  
- Suggestion: provide more evidence for GDN efficiency (e.g., parameter-count vs performance comparisons).  
- Suggestion: discuss additional limitations (e.g., video compression, non-natural images).  
- Question: which specific test images does the method underperform on, and why?  
- Question: what image characteristics caused underperformance in figure 15 at high rates?  
- Question: quantitative and visual comparisons of simpler nonlinearities (ReLU, sigmoid) versus GDN/IGDN?  
- Question: can the additive-noise relaxation improve supervised classification robustness—any preliminary evidence?  
- Question: how sensitive are results to the choice of quantization relaxation (uniform noise vs other distributions)?
