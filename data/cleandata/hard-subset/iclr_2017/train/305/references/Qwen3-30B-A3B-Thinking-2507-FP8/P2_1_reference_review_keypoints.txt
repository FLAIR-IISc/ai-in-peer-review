- Proposes end-to-end optimized image compression using nonlinear transform coding with cascaded GDN, jointly optimizing analysis/synthesis transforms, quantization, and entropy coding.
- Key contributions: continuous relaxation of quantization for gradient-based training, a full compression framework reporting actual bit rates, improved PSNR/MS-SSIM vs. JPEG/JPEG 2000, and fewer visible artifacts.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
- Strong theoretical foundation: clear, well-justified continuous relaxation and informative comparison to variational autoencoders.
- Rigorous empirical evaluation: uses Kodak benchmark, visual comparisons, reports PSNR and MS-SSIM, releases code/images, and shows consistent improvements across test images.
- Clear distinction from prior work: directly optimizes rate-distortion rather than adapting a generative model for compression.
- Suggestion—Computational complexity: paper claims efficiency but lacks concrete timing and memory metrics versus JPEG/JPEG 2000.
- Suggestion—Comparison to other deep methods: should provide more detailed comparisons to Toderici et al. and recent DL-based compression approaches.
- Suggestion—Scalability: paper focuses on 256×256 images and needs discussion of scaling to higher resolutions (1080p, 4K).
- Suggestion—MSE vs perceptual quality: request deeper analysis of why MSE optimization yields more natural/perceptually better images, and the role of GDN.
- Question 1: Provide encoding/decoding time and memory requirements compared to JPEG/JPEG 2000 and discuss deployment impact.
- Question 2: Explain how varying λ to get different rate-distortion points compares to Toderici’s single-representation multi-rate approach.
- Question 3: Clarify the diversity and categories of test images beyond the Kodak set (how many categories/images).
- Question 4: Supply quantitative evidence that ReLU networks need substantially more parameters/stages than GDN (param counts, stages, or PSNR differences).
