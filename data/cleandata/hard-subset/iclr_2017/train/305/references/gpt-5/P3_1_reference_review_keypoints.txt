- End-to-end optimization of lossy image compression jointly learns analysis/synthesis transforms, quantization proxy, and an entropy model to optimize Lagrangian rate–distortion.
- Cascaded convolutional architecture with GDN/IGDN acts as a learned nonlinear transform plus scalar quantization behaving like a parametric vector quantizer.
- Continuous relaxation of quantization via additive unit-width uniform noise enables SGD and uses relaxed differential entropy as a surrogate for discrete entropy.
- Nonparametric per-channel marginal models (piecewise linear densities) are learned to reduce model mismatch when estimating rates.
- The approach is connected to VAEs through a specific generative model and approximate posterior, with emphasis on differences important for practical compression (discrete coding, finite λ).
- An actual entropy coder (CABAC-like) is implemented and real bitrates are reported rather than only entropy estimates.
- Empirical results show better MS-SSIM and typically better PSNR than JPEG/JPEG2000, with qualitatively more natural artifacts (edge/contour preservation, reduced ringing/blocking).
- Overall assessment: clean, well-motivated, and practical demonstration that learned nonlinear transforms can outperform classical transform coding.
- Decision: Accept, based on methodological soundness and clear technical contribution.
- Strength: Principled additive-uniform relaxation and careful discussion of its role in RD optimization and VAE connections.
- Strength: GDN/IGDN are principled nonlinearities that Gaussianize image statistics and yield efficient transforms with few stages.
- Strength: Reporting actual bitrates and analyzing bias/variance between relaxed objective and true RD is a notable empirical contribution.
- Reproducibility: Architecture, training, and entropy coding are described in sufficient detail with practical optimization tips in the appendix.
- Suggestion: Add stronger modern baselines (e.g., BPG/HEVC-intra, WebP) to strengthen empirical claims.
- Suggestion: Address practicality of training separate models per λ and consider a single variable-rate model or discuss λ selection for target rates.
- Suggestion: Independence assumption across latents is limiting; discuss spatial/contextual or hierarchical priors and expected gains.
- Suggestion: Expand evaluation beyond Kodak to larger datasets (Tecnick, CLIC) and report aggregate metrics like BD-rate; include runtime and complexity comparisons.
- Suggestion: Provide ablation studies for GDN vs ReLU/sigmoid, number of stages, and density model capacity to justify architectural choices.
- Suggestion: Clarify color handling (RGB vs YCbCr), MS-SSIM computation details, and report chroma distortion or perceptual color metrics.
- Suggestion: Specify side-information overhead and how per-channel marginal models are conveyed and their impact on small images.
- Question: How is a target bitrate mapped to a choice of λ at test time and can targets be hit without iterative search?
- Question: Are per-channel marginal PMFs fixed by λ or is per-image adaptation transmitted; how much adaptation occurs in CABAC and how close is coding to learned continuous densities?
- Question: Were simple context models or hyperpriors tried and what RD gains were observed relative to independent marginals?
- Question: Can the authors quantify parameter/compute savings of GDN/IGDN versus ReLU-based networks for matched RD?
- Question: How does performance generalize beyond Kodak and are there failure cases with objectionable artifacts?
- Question: What are encode/decode times and memory requirements for typical images (e.g., 512x512) on CPU/GPU for embedded deployment?
- Question: What happens if training uses MS-SSIM or a learned perceptual metric instead of MSE?
