- Tackles end-to-end optimization of lossy image compression by jointly learning analysis/synthesis transforms and an entropy model for R+λD, using additive i.i.d. uniform noise to relax quantization for SGD.  
- Uses cascaded convolutions with learned GDN/IGDN nonlinearities and a nonparametric factorized prior over latents; implements a real entropy coder and reports actual bitrates.  
- Frames the approach relative to VAEs but explicitly optimizes rate–distortion rather than generative likelihood.  
- Empirically improves MS-SSIM and often PSNR vs JPEG/JPEG2000, producing more natural low-rate artifacts; results reported on Kodak and additional images.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: principled formulation bridging classical transform coding and deep learning with a simple, effective quantization relaxation and implemented entropy coding.  
- Key reason: strong empirical and perceptual gains over standard codecs, with sound analysis of the relaxation and clear positioning vs prior work.  
- Methodology strength: additive-uniform relaxation preserves discrete entropy connection, makes rate and distortion differentiable, and shows small/diminishing bias; nonparametric marginals and GDN/IGDN are well-motivated.  
- Literature positioning: clearly relates to transform coding, VQ, VAEs, and recent neural compression work, clarifying differences in objectives and evaluation.  
- Empirical evaluation strength: reports real bitstreams including side information, compares to standard codecs, and shows qualitative improvements in artifacts.  
- Feasibility: high training cost but efficient inference (feed-forward convs + scalar quant + entropy coding) and end-to-end implementable.  
- Limitation: training a separate model per λ (single-rate models) limits practicality for deployment/standardization.  
- Limitation: entropy model is simple (factorized, per-channel marginals, simple coding order) and likely leaves bits on the table versus context/adaptive models.  
- Limitation: insufficient ablations to quantify GDN/IGDN benefits, variations in depth/width, and alternative priors.  
- Limitation: evaluation breadth is limited (Kodak small); more datasets, high-res images, and details on baseline codec settings needed for fairness.  
- Limitation: missing practical metrics—model size, FLOPs, encode/decode time, and memory—important for deployment comparisons.  
- Suggested improvements: add systematic ablations (nonlinearities, depth/width, priors), report compute/runtime/memory, explore multi-rate conditioning or scalable coding, evaluate stronger entropy models, broaden dataset evaluation, and release code/models/bitstreams.  
- Questions for authors: how many parameters per λ and decoder footprint for multiple λ models; can parameters be amortized across λ (conditional norms/scales)?  
- Question: how close are coded bitrates to estimated entropies (average bits/pixel gap)?  
- Question: were alternative priors (mixtures of logistics) or simple spatial context models tried, and how did they affect R–D and stability?  
- Question: can you provide systematic ablations quantifying GDN/IGDN vs ReLU/sigmoid at equal capacity?  
- Question: typical encode/decode times and FLOPs for 512×512 RGB and runtime scaling with depth/channels?  
- Question: sensitivity to training set size and whether performance improves significantly with more data?  
- Question: do color images use RGB or a decorrelated color space, and were any gains observed from color transforms?  
- Overall verdict: strong, well-motivated advance in neural image compression with principled training and convincing real-bit empirical results—recommend accept.
