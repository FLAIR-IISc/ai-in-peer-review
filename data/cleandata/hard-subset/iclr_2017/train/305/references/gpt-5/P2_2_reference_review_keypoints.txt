- Proposes end-to-end optimization of analysis/synthesis transforms and entropy model for lossy image compression using a continuous relaxation of quantization (additive uniform noise) for SGD training.  
- Uses a convolutional cascade with down/up-sampling and GDN/IGDN nonlinearities, learning parameters per rate.  
- Models latent entropy with independent nonparametric (piecewise-linear) marginals and implements a CABAC-style arithmetic coder to report real bitrates including side information.  
- Shows the noise-relaxed objective is a good proxy for discrete rate–distortion (analyzing bias/variance).  
- Demonstrates consistent perceptual (MS-SSIM) gains over JPEG/JPEG2000 and PSNR gains at low rates on test images.  
- Clarifies theoretical and practical connections to VAEs, noting equality of objectives under specific choices but different end goals.  
- Decision: Accept, based on principled end-to-end formulation, faithful evaluation with real bitrates, and convincing empirical improvements.  
- Motivation: Strong case for moving from separate hand-designed coder components to learned nonlinear transforms adapted to image statistics.  
- Literature placement: Careful comparisons to prior neural compression work and precise positioning relative to VAEs.  
- Technical soundness: Additive noise relaxation is appropriate; empirical checks show differential entropy and distortion surrogates are reliable; architecture and optimization are well-detailed.  
- Empirical rigor: Use of a real arithmetic coder, disjoint train/test splits, multiple objective metrics (PSNR, MS-SSIM), qualitative examples, and disclosure of coding details.  
- Significance: Establishes a practical template for neural transform coding (GDN, noise relaxation, learned priors, real bitstream evaluation) with notable perceptual gains despite MSE training.  
- Limitation: Entropy model assumes independent marginals, leaving spatial/contextual coding gains unexplored.  
- Limitation: Comparisons are limited to JPEG/JPEG2000; missing stronger modern codecs (e.g., HEVC/BPG).  
- Limitation: Trains separate models per λ (no single multi-rate model), reducing convenience.  
- Limitation: Lacks quantitative runtime and model footprint comparisons to classical codecs.  
- Limitation: Ablations are limited—role of GDN, transform vs entropy model, and relaxation not fully isolated.  
- Feedback: Add quantitative runtime/memory (encode/decode time, model size) for deployment context.  
- Feedback: Include ablations (GDN vs pointwise nonlinearities, linear baseline, parametric vs nonparametric marginals).  
- Feedback: Report aggregate RD summaries (e.g., BD-rate) alongside per-image plots for PSNR and MS-SSIM.  
- Feedback: Compare to a stronger modern codec (or discuss expected gaps due to the entropy coder).  
- Feedback: Clarify how side information scales with image size and λ and report its share of total bits.  
- Feedback: Discuss prospects for single multi-rate models and stronger context/hyperprior models as future directions.  
- Questions: Provide encoding/decoding times and model sizes vs JPEG2000 (CPU/GPU).  
- Questions: How sensitive are gains to the independence assumption; any results with spatial/contextual or autoregressive entropy models?  
- Questions: Quantify the bit gap between learned differential entropy and realized arithmetic-coding bitrate across rates.  
- Questions: Quantitative ablation: replace GDN/IGDN with pointwise nonlinearities—what changes?  
- Questions: Can a single model cover multiple λ (latent scaling or conditioning) and retain RD optimality?  
- Questions: Do results generalize to other datasets (e.g., Tecnick) and higher resolutions?  
- Questions: Have you trained directly with perceptual losses (e.g., MS-SSIM) and how does that affect RD/perceptual performance?
