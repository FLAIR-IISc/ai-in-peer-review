- Paper proposes end-to-end learned image compression optimizing nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms, the quantized latent distribution, and an entropy code for a rate–distortion objective.  
- Architecture: cascaded convolutional nets with GDN/IGDN trained per rate to minimize R + λD (D = MSE).  
- Quantization relaxation: replace rounding with additive unit-uniform noise during training to enable unbiased gradients.  
- Rate estimation: use differential entropy of relaxed latents with nonparametric per-channel marginal models.  
- Practical bitstream: implement a CABAC-like entropy coder and report actual bitrates rather than entropy surrogates.  
- The paper connects the method to VAEs while explaining key differences (discrete codes, rate–distortion focus).  
- Empirical claim: better perceptual quality (MS-SSIM) and often better PSNR than JPEG/JPEG2000, especially at low rates, with more natural artifacts.  
- Decision: Accept — formulation is strong and well-motivated, bridging classical transform coding and deep learning with a practical coder.  
- Empirical evidence is convincing due to use of actual bitrates and consistent perceptual gains.  
- Novelty: explicit application of uniform-noise relaxation and VAE ideas to end-to-end transform coding with a full entropy coder is novel.  
- GDN/IGDN use is well motivated by Gaussianization benefits and validated in the coding context.  
- Objective and training are technically sound; biases in rate estimation are documented and argued to be manageable.  
- Training details are carefully engineered and described to support reproducibility (stabilization, normalization, optimizer schedule, DCT param).  
- Entropy coding includes side information and spatial adaptation, lending credibility to reported rates.  
- Qualitative results show reduced blocking/ringing and improved contour fidelity versus baselines.  
- Quantitative evaluation covers PSNR and MS-SSIM on Kodak with per-image plots and reconstructions.  
- Suggestion: compare to more modern codecs (HEVC intra/BPG, WebP) to contextualize state-of-the-art performance.  
- Suggestion: improve entropy model (contextual or hyperprior) or include ablation showing gains from dependency modeling.  
- Suggestion: discuss or demonstrate a multi-rate single-model approach rather than training per λ for deployability.  
- Suggestion: include systematic ablation quantifying GDN’s advantage (RD curves vs parameter count/depth).  
- Suggestion: report encoding/decoding runtime and memory on CPU/GPU and compare to JPEG/JPEG2000.  
- Suggestion: test generalization on additional datasets (e.g., Tecnick) and report mean/std across images.  
- Suggestion: clarify color handling (RGB vs YCbCr) and report MS-SSIM on RGB as well as luma or justify choice.  
- Question: quantify the gap and variance between relaxed rate estimates and realized arithmetic-coded bitrates; does straight-through fine-tuning close it?  
- Question: report measured residual spatial cross-correlation in latents and joint vs marginal entropy to estimate dependencies.  
- Question: sensitivity of results to CABAC decision-tree choices and gains from simple raster neighborhood contexts.  
- Question: how does RD performance scale with model capacity at fixed λ; is there underfitting at high rates with smaller models?  
- Question: have you tried optimizing MS-SSIM (or using the exponential family mapping) and how does that affect artifacts and rates?  
- Question: any failure cases or class-specific weaknesses (e.g., periodic textures like “Barbara”) and how to address them?  
- Question: will trained models and the entropy coder be released to facilitate reproducibility?
