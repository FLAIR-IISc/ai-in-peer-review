- End-to-end optimization of lossy image compression learning analysis/synthesis transforms and an entropy model for an R+λD objective.
- Quantization non-differentiability addressed by replacing rounding with additive uniform noise during training to enable SGD while relating to the discrete problem.
- Transforms are cascades of convolutions with GDN nonlinearities and IGDN inverse; code space assumed factorized and modeled with nonparametric univariate densities.
- Connects the relaxed training objective to the ELBO in VAEs and clarifies differences between RD-optimized compression and generative modeling.
- Evaluates actual bitrates using an implemented CABAC entropy coder and demonstrates improved perceptual quality and state-of-the-art results vs JPEG/JPEG2000 on Kodak despite MSE training.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Main strengths: principled, practical end-to-end RD optimization that bridges classical transform coding and deep learning and reports real bitstreams.
- Empirical evidence: compelling visual improvements, competitive-to-better RD curves, and careful analysis of the relaxation and implemented bitstream.
- Novelty/positioning: additive uniform noise is known but is integrated here into a deployable RD-optimized pipeline tied to a concrete entropy coder and discrete evaluation.
- GDN/IGDN choice is well motivated by Gaussianization and biological plausibility and shows empirical efficiency for compression.
- Relaxation justification: uniform noise yields a continuous density matching discrete pmf at integer points; empirical bias/variance of relaxed distortion and entropy is small.
- The factorized latent code model is a clear simplification and acknowledged limitation; CABAC’s weak adaptation only modestly helps.
- Training separate models per λ is pragmatic and aligns with RD theory though less convenient than variable-rate single-model approaches.
- Qualitative gains: reduced blocking/ringing, more natural edges/contours, consistently higher MS-SSIM, and competitive PSNR at low–mid rates.
- Reporting actual bitrates is a meaningful advance over contemporaneous learned methods that used only entropy estimates.
- Evaluation dataset (Kodak, classic images, additional photos) is conventional and relevant; per-image RD curves provided online to address aggregation concerns.
- Limitation: latent independence assumption likely leaves significant compression gains on the table compared to context/hyperprior models.
- Limitation: mismatch between per-element nonparametric marginals used in training and per-channel shared distributions used by the entropy coder may cause suboptimality and potential overfitting.
- Limitation: comparisons focus on JPEG/JPEG2000; absence of HEVC-intra/BPG baselines weakens claims about state-of-the-art.
- Practicality concern: per-λ models require both encoder/decoder to share parameter sets and raise deployment issues (model storage, standardization); bitstream only signals λ.
- Aggregation suggestion: include standard summaries (e.g., Bjøntegaard delta-rate over Kodak) to make comparisons crisper.
- Requested additional experiments: report encode/decode runtimes, compute complexity, and memory footprint (CPU/GPU) since GDN/IGDN can be expensive.
- Ablation requests: replace GDN with pointwise nonlinearities, vary number of stages, test tied-per-channel marginals vs per-element.
- Clarify and report RGB results, internal colorspace, and chroma handling to ensure fair comparison to JPEG/J2K.
- Consider adding a simple variable-rate mechanism (per-channel scaling, learned quantization step, importance maps) to approach a single-model solution across rates.
- Clarify regularization of nonparametric densities and sensitivity to spline resolution and range adaptation to avoid degenerate solutions.
- Questions for authors: quantify train–test gap from per-element training marginals vs per-channel CABAC; did you try training with per-channel shared marginals?
- Questions: does adding simple spatial context (causal neighborhoods) to the entropy model improve performance; any preliminary results?
- Questions: provide Bjøntegaard delta-rate numbers vs JPEG2000 on Kodak for PSNR and MS-SSIM.
- Questions: report encode/decode runtimes and model sizes for the evaluated λ models and scaling with channels/stages.
- Questions: which colorspace is used for RGB and how are chroma subsampling differences handled in comparisons?
- Questions: did you evaluate against HEVC-intra/BPG; if not, what gap do you anticipate given the factorized model?
