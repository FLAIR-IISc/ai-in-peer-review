- Problem: Learn an image compression system by directly optimizing the full pipeline (analysis transform, quantization, synthesis, entropy model) for rate–distortion.
- Approach: Nonlinear transform coder with cascaded convolutions and GDN/IGDN; replace quantization with additive uniform noise surrogate; learn nonparametric marginals and implement a CABAC-like entropy coder to report real bitrates.
- Claims: End-to-end optimization outperforms JPEG/JPEG2000 (especially at low rates and in MS-SSIM), yields perceptually more natural reconstructions despite MSE training, and the continuous relaxation closely matches discrete metrics enabling practical training.
- Contributions: Principled rate–distortion objective linked to VAEs while retaining discrete coding, a practical GDN-based architecture, and thorough qualitative/quantitative evaluation with actual coded bits.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: Strong, well-motivated methodological advance that addresses optimization through quantization with a principled surrogate and produces a deployable compressor with compelling empirical gains.
- Novelty/significance: Closes gap between differential surrogates and real entropy coding; additive uniform noise is well-justified and validated; GDN provides a meaningful learned joint nonlinearity over linear+pointwise baselines.
- Correctness/rigor: Objective and VAE connection are correctly derived; entropy model and coder are explicit and implemented; training details and hyperparameters are documented and surrogate is empirically validated against discrete metrics.
- Empirical evaluation: Uses Kodak plus extra images, reports PSNR and MS-SSIM, provides per-image curves and qualitative comparisons; improvements are consistent though comparisons are limited to JPEG/JPEG2000.
- Clarity/reproducibility: Paper is well written with clear model/optimization description and an appendix with sufficient implementation details.
- Baselines suggestion: Include stronger modern baselines (HEVC-intra/BPG) or at least discuss/partially compare to better contextualize performance.
- Entropy modeling suggestion: Independence and simple contexts likely leave bits on the table—provide ablation vs stronger context models or offline spatial-context entropy estimates.
- Ablation suggestion: Systematically compare GDN vs ReLU/sigmoid networks at matched parameter counts/depths to quantify GDN’s contribution.
- Multi-rate suggestion: Discuss memory/maintenance trade-offs of one model per λ and explore conditional/weight-sharing approaches for multiple rates.
- Runtime/complexity suggestion: Report encoding/decoding speed, parameter counts, and memory versus JPEG2000 to assess practicality.
- Generalization suggestion: Evaluate on a second held-out dataset (e.g., Tecnick, CLIC val) to increase confidence in generalization.
- Fairness/detail suggestion: Clarify JPEG’s 4:2:0 chroma subsampling vs your RGB compression and report consistent Y-channel metrics or subsample RGB for fair comparison.
- Presentation fixes: Tighten references, fix typos, clarify upsampling equation formatting, and justify kernel sizes and sampling factors.
- Question — bitrate gap: How close are achieved bitrates to modeled discrete entropy; quantify coding overhead (bits/symbol) and estimated gains from stronger context models.
- Question — codec comparison: Can you provide BPG/HEVC-intra comparisons at matched rates (e.g., on Kodak)?
- Question — model scaling: How sensitive are results to channel counts and depth; is there a scaling law or saturation in bpp vs parameters?
- Question — GDN impact: At matched budgets, how much gain is attributable to GDN specifically and are there visual artifact differences?
- Question — training stability: Were curriculum or λ-schedules needed; is additive-noise relaxation robust at very low rates and was noise annealing tried?
- Question — single multi-rate model: Can transforms be conditioned on λ to produce a single model covering multiple rates without loss?
- Question — RGB/color handling: Did you try learned color transforms or internal chroma subsampling and how do these compare to naive RGB compression?
- Question — domain generalization: How does the model perform on non-natural-image domains (graphics, text, medical images)?
