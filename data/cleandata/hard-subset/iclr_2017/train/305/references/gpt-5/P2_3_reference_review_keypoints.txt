- Replace non-differentiable quantization with additive i.i.d. uniform noise to obtain a differentiable surrogate whose rate term is the relaxed latents’ differential entropy and whose distortion approximates post-quantization MSE.
- Use cascaded conv layers with GDN/IGDN for analysis/synthesis and learn nonparametric spline marginal priors for relaxed latents.
- Train separate models per Lagrange multiplier λ via SGD; at test time perform scalar quantization and CABAC-like entropy coding and report true bit rates.
- Empirically show consistent perceptual improvements and competitive or better PSNR vs JPEG/JPEG 2000 on Kodak and other images, with large MS-SSIM gains.
- Clarify and derive the connection to VAEs, noting similarities and key differences for compression (discrete codes, RD trade-off, practical entropy coding).
- Recommendation: Accept — principled formulation, effective surrogate, biologically inspired transform, and clear empirical gains measured in actual bit rates.
- Novelty: While uniform-noise surrogates are known, the integration with GDN transforms, spline marginals, per-λ training, and implemented entropy coder yields a compelling, complete learned codec.
- Technical soundness: The relaxation is well motivated — relaxed entropy matches discrete mass at integers, noise mimics quantization moments, and empirical checks show small bias diminishing with finer quantization.
- VAE equivalence: The VAE view is clearly derived for Gaussian likelihood and the practical distinctions for compression are convincingly explained.
- Empirical rigor: Training, architectures, and optimization are detailed; test sets are standard; actual bit rates via CABAC are reported; results include PSNR, MS-SSIM, and qualitative comparisons.
- Clarity and placement: The paper is well written, positioned relative to transform coding, VAEs, and learned compression prior work; appendices support reproducibility.
- Suggest training a single model that can traverse the RD curve (e.g., learned global scale or conditional gain) instead of one model per λ.
- Suggest improving the entropy model by capturing dependencies (context models, hyperpriors, autoregressive latents) beyond independent marginals.
- Request ablations comparing GDN/IGDN vs ReLU/batch-norm at matched capacity, varying stages/channels, and parametric vs nonparametric marginals to isolate contributions.
- Recommend reporting encoding/decoding runtime, memory, and model size vs JPEG 2000 on CPU/GPU to assess deployability.
- Recommend broader dataset evaluation and reporting variance/statistical significance to address dataset bias beyond Kodak.
- Ask to clarify color pipeline and chroma subsampling used relative to baselines (provide 4:4:4 comparisons if possible).
- Encourage releasing code, models, and bitstreams to improve reproducibility and adoption.
- Question: How are λ values chosen per image at test time to meet target bit rates and what overhead arises from storing multiple decoders?
- Question: How close is the CABAC implementation to empirical entropy of quantized latents, and are gaps due to independence assumptions or coder/context limitations?
- Question: Can you quantify residual dependencies among latents (mutual information/spatial/channel) and estimate rate recoverable with simple context models?
- Question: Provide controlled experiments demonstrating parameter efficiency of GDN vs common activations for equal RD performance.
- Question: What are typical encode/decode times for 512×512 RGB on CPU and GPU, and are there numerical bottlenecks from GDN/IGDN?
- Question: Clarify the claim of overcompleteness when net output dimensionality is half the input — in what sense are first-stage filters overcomplete?
- Question: How sensitive are results to number of spline bins and marginal prior support; does relaxed training produce degenerate priors or instability at extreme λ?
