1. Summary and contributions
The paper tackles lossy image compression by optimizing a nonlinear transform coding pipeline end-to-end for rate–distortion. The core ideas are: (i) analysis/synthesis transforms built as cascades of convolutions with generalized divisive normalization (GDN) and its inverse (IGDN); (ii) uniform scalar quantization in the latent; (iii) a continuous relaxation of quantization using additive uniform noise to enable SGD; (iv) nonparametric, factorized marginal models for the relaxed latents; (v) training separate models per λ to trace the R–D curve; and (vi) reporting actual bitrates via an arithmetic coder (CABAC-style), not just entropy estimates. Empirically, on Kodak and several classic images, the method shows better MS-SSIM and frequently better PSNR at low-to-mid rates than JPEG/JPEG2000, with qualitatively fewer ringing/blocking artifacts.

2. Decision: Reject
Key reasons:
- Presentation and rigor issues (numerous equation/notation errors, incomplete/garbled references, underspecified decoder operations) that impede reproducibility and technical clarity.
- Evaluation is not sufficiently comprehensive or fair for a compression paper: missing strong baselines (e.g., HEVC/BPG, WebP), limited datasets, no runtime/complexity analysis, and per-rate models without a practical discussion of side information and deployment.

3. Supporting arguments
Technical correctness and clarity
- Several equations appear malformed or ambiguous:
  • Eq. (3) and (4) show the exponent as “12” instead of 1/2; please fix the notation.
  • Eq. (5) defines upsampling v̂(k) with a case but leaves the “otherwise” branch empty; the interpolation/insertion rule is not specified. This is critical for reproducibility and for understanding invertibility/aliasing.
  • Eq. (10) states pỹ(n) = Pq(n) “for all n ∈ ZM”. This equality only makes sense as equality of bin masses (integrals) or values at integers for the convolved density; please formalize precisely (density vs PMF).
  • The uniform noise is alternately denoted U(0,1) and “centered on yi”; ensure consistency (U(yi−1/2, yi+1/2)).
- Important implementation details are missing or vague:
  • Exact upsampling/downsampling filters when fusing with convolution (“implemented jointly”) should be specified.
  • Entropy coder: the context design is only roughly described; the decision tree encoding per coefficient needs full details to reproduce actual bitrates.
  • Side information: only a λ index is transmitted, but the decoder must have access to the model parameters (filters, GDN/IGDN, and marginal models). How are these distributed and versioned? What is the memory footprint?
  • Parameter counts, model sizes, and exact architectures for RGB and high-rate variants are not fully enumerated.
- The reference list is in very poor shape: numerous entries are incomplete, duplicated, or malformed (e.g., “?, \Q2016\E”, missing titles/authors/venues, broken diacritics). This fails the basic standards of scholarly presentation.

Empirical evaluation
- Baselines: JPEG2000 is not the state of the art at the time; comparisons to HEVC intra/BPG and WebP are expected. Reporting PSNR/MS-SSIM vs those is critical to establish competitiveness beyond classic codecs.
- Datasets: Kodak and a few classic images are small and dated. A larger modern testbed (e.g., Tecnick, CLIC, subset of ImageNet-val, DIV2K, or a withheld portion of the same distribution used for training) should be used, with per-image and averaged R–D curves.
- Runtimes/complexity: No encoding/decoding latency, FLOPs, or memory measurements. As a learned codec with GDNs and 3-stage conv stacks, complexity is central; practical viability cannot be judged.
- Per-λ models: Training a separate model for each rate raises deployment concerns (decoder storage, model selection latency). While reasonable for research, practical implications and comparisons with single-model variable-rate approaches (e.g., Toderici et al.) should be quantified.
- Entropy coding fairness: The proposed coder is simplified and uses raster scan with limited adaptation. JPEG2000 employs sophisticated context modeling; conversely, modern learned codecs use hyperpriors/autoregressive models. The gap between the factorized latent assumption and the applied entropy coding should be analyzed (bits-back from dependence; impact on R–D).
- Ablations: The paper asserts GDN efficiency; however, no ablation against ReLU/sigmoid or per-channel normalizations is included in the main results. Similarly, the impact of the independence assumption, the spline density resolution, and the architecture depth/width on R–D is not quantified.

Positioning and claims
- The VAE connection is well articulated, but the distinctions could be tightened with experiments (e.g., show how varying λ maps to NLL in the Gaussian slack; compare to a pure VAE trained for likelihood and then quantized).
- Claims of “substantially improved perceptual quality” are supported with MS-SSIM and qualitative exemplars, but human studies would strengthen the claim, especially as training used MSE.

4. Additional feedback (to help improve the paper)
- Fix all equation typos, specify up/downsampling precisely, and provide a complete, reproducible architecture table for each model variant (kernel sizes, channels, strides, parameter counts).
- Release code, trained weights, and the arithmetic coder to enable verification.
- Add strong baselines (BPG/HEVC intra, WebP) and evaluate on larger modern datasets. Include BD-rate summaries and per-image plots.
- Report complexity: wall-clock times on CPU/GPU, memory footprint, model sizes, and decoder load time for multiple λ models. Discuss practical deployment options (e.g., variable-rate single model, conditional transforms).
- Provide ablations: with/without GDN, different nonlinearities, depth/width variations, factorized vs weakly context-modeled latents, and effect of the uniform-noise relaxation on the true discretized R–D.
- Clarify entropy coding: detail context design, adaptation rules, and side info overhead. Consider a slightly stronger context (e.g., simple spatial neighbors) to narrow the gap to practical codecs without overengineering the comparison.
- Clean up and standardize the references thoroughly.

5. Questions for the authors
- What are the exact upsampling/downsampling operators used in the synthesis and analysis (filters, stride, any anti-aliasing), and how do they affect invertibility and aliasing?
- How large are the trained models per λ (parameters, bytes), and what is the total decoder footprint if multiple λ models are supported? What is the encoding/decoding speed on standard hardware?
- How sensitive is performance to the factorized latent assumption? Do you observe spatial/channel dependencies in residuals; could a simple context (e.g., neighbors) recover significant bits?
- Can you provide ablations comparing GDN/IGDN to ReLU/sigmoid/BN-based networks at matched parameter counts and depths?
- How does the method compare to BPG/HEVC intra and WebP in PSNR/MS-SSIM on Kodak and a larger dataset?
- Does training a single conditional model that modulates λ at test time (e.g., via scale hyperparameters) approach the performance of per-λ models?
- How robust is the continuous relaxation: do you observe bias between differential entropy estimates and actual bitrates across rates and images beyond the small sample shown?
