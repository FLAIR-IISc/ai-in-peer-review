1. Summary and contributions
This paper tackles end-to-end optimization of image compression by learning nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms, the quantized latent distribution, and an entropy code jointly for a target rate–distortion trade-off. The key ideas are:
- A cascaded convolutional architecture with generalized divisive normalization (GDN) and its learned approximate inverse (IGDN), trained per rate point to minimize R + λD with D = MSE.
- A principled continuous relaxation of quantization by replacing rounding with additive unit-uniform noise during training, enabling unbiased gradient-based optimization; the rate term is estimated via the differential entropy of the relaxed latents using nonparametric per-channel marginal models.
- A practical bitstream via an implemented entropy coder (CABAC-like), reporting actual bits rather than entropy surrogates.
- A clear connection to variational autoencoders (VAEs), with differences carefully delineated (discrete codes, rate–distortion vs generative modeling).
Empirically, on Kodak and additional images (grayscale and RGB), the method yields better perceptual quality (MS-SSIM) and often better PSNR vs JPEG/JPEG2000, especially at low rates, with artifacts that are visually more natural (edge preservation, fewer ringing/blocking artifacts).

2. Decision
Accept. Reasons:
- Strong, well-motivated formulation that bridges classical transform coding and modern deep learning with a clean, differentiable rate–distortion objective and a practical entropy coder.
- Convincing empirical evidence with actual bitrates and competitive PSNR and substantially improved perceptual quality over standard baselines.

3. Supporting arguments
Novelty and positioning:
- While uniform-noise relaxation and VAEs are known, applying this relaxation explicitly to end-to-end transform coding with learned nonlinearities and evaluating with a full entropy coder is novel and well grounded. The paper articulates the VAE connection and key divergences (discrete coding, rate–distortion tradeoff).
- The use of GDN/IGDN as learnable joint nonlinearities is well motivated by prior work showing Gaussianization benefits, and here is validated in a coding setting.

Technical soundness:
- The objective is sound: per-latent independent marginal models with continuous relaxation give a tractable, differentiable estimate of rate and distortion. The paper evaluates the approximation quality for both terms and documents biases (rate overestimation at coarse quantization), arguing (reasonably) that constant biases mainly shift the effective λ without harming performance.
- The training setup is carefully engineered (stabilization of GDN/IGDN parameters, filter normalization, Adam schedule, DCT parameterization) and described in sufficient detail to reproduce.
- The entropy coding is implemented and integrated; side information is included; adaptation across spatial positions within channels is allowed, making reported rates credible.

Empirical evaluation:
- Qualitative results compellingly demonstrate the benefit of nonlinear transforms (reduced blocking/ringing, better contour fidelity).
- Quantitative results cover PSNR and MS-SSIM across a widely used dataset (Kodak), with consistent perceptual gains and frequent PSNR gains at low rates. The authors are appropriately cautious about summarizing RD across images and provide per-image plots and reconstructions.

4. Suggestions to improve (helpful, not decisive for acceptance)
- Baselines: Consider adding comparisons to more modern codecs (e.g., HEVC intra/BPG, WebP), which were available at submission time, to contextualize performance versus state-of-the-art beyond JPEG/JPEG2000.
- Entropy model: The independence assumption in the latent space leaves coding gains on the table. Even a lightweight context (e.g., autoregressive neighbors) or a learned hyperprior could reduce bits significantly. An ablation comparing independent vs simple context models would be informative.
- Multi-rate practicality: Training a separate model per λ reduces deployability. Please discuss and, if possible, demonstrate a single model supporting multiple rates (e.g., conditioning, gain control, or bit allocation via learned scalars).
- Ablations: You note preliminary experiments with ReLU/sigmoid. Please include a systematic ablation quantifying GDN’s advantage (RD curves vs parameter count/depth), as this is a central modeling choice.
- Runtime: Report encoding/decoding speed and memory footprint on CPU/GPU, and compare to JPEG/JPEG2000, to substantiate claims of efficiency at inference.
- Generalization: Training uses 6.5k ImageNet images; testing mainly on Kodak. Consider a secondary test set (e.g., Tecnick) and report statistics (mean/std) across images to strengthen generalization claims.
- Color handling: Clarify color space used during training for RGB (raw RGB vs YCbCr), and report MS-SSIM on RGB as well as luma, or justify the choice.

5. Questions for the authors
- Quantization-relaxation gap: Can you quantify, across rate points, the average difference between the relaxed rate estimate and the realized arithmetic-coded bitrates, and its variance? Does fine-tuning with straight-through estimators for rounding further close any gap?
- Latent dependencies: What is the measured residual spatial cross-correlation in the latent channels post-GDN? Could you report entropy of joint pairs vs sum of marginals to estimate dependence?
- CABAC details: How sensitive are results to your decision tree for coding qi (mode-first, bifurcation)? Have you tried simple raster neighborhood contexts, and what gains did they yield?
- Model capacity: You increase channels at high rates. How does RD performance scale with capacity at fixed λ? Is there evidence of underfitting at high rates with smaller models?
- Perceptual metrics: You optimize MSE but report strong MS-SSIM gains. Have you tried training with an MS-SSIM-like distortion within your framework (via the exponential family mapping in Eq. 16), and how does that affect artifacts and rates?
- Robustness and artifacts: Do failure cases exist where your method introduces structured artifacts (e.g., texture removal leading to plastic appearance)? Any class-specific weaknesses (e.g., periodic textures like “Barbara”) and how might the model address them?
- Reproducibility: Will you release trained models and the entropy coder to facilitate comparison?

Overall, this paper makes a substantive, well-executed contribution to learned image compression, with a principled objective, careful engineering, and strong empirical evidence.
