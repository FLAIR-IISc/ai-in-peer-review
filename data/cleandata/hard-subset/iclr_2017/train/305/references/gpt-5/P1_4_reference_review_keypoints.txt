- Proposes end-to-end lossy image compression with jointly learned analysis/synthesis transforms and rate term via SGD.  
- Key technical idea: continuous relaxation of quantization by replacing rounding with additive uniform noise for backpropagation while approximating discrete entropy and distortion.  
- Transforms use cascades of convolutions and GDN/IGDN (with approximate inverse); latent distribution modeled by independent nonparametric (piecewise-linear) marginals.  
- Models trained separately per Lagrange multiplier λ to trace the rate–distortion curve.  
- Implements a CABAC-style entropy coder and reports actual bitrates (including side information).  
- Empirical results: consistent MS-SSIM improvements over JPEG/JPEG2000 and frequent PSNR gains, especially at low bitrates; qualitatively more natural artifacts.  
- Decision: Accept — principled differentiable surrogate for quantization plus practical entropy coding makes the work scientifically sound and relevant.  
- Strong empirical evidence and qualitative results support the claims.  
- Positions work between transform coding and probabilistic modeling, clarifying links to VAEs and common evaluation pitfalls.  
- Validates the relaxation empirically, showing small bias/variance between relaxed and actual quantized entropy/distortion.  
- Provides training/architecture details and parameterizations that stabilize learning.  
- Limitation: entropy model assumes independent latents; lacks context modeling compared to advanced codecs/hyperpriors.  
- Limitation: evaluation concentrated on Kodak (24 images) with no user study and no comparison to stronger baselines like BPG/HEVC-intra.  
- Limitation: uses separate models per λ instead of a single variable-rate model.  
- Limitation: limited ablations on GDN vs. standard nonlinearities and sparse runtime/complexity reporting.  
- Suggestion: include comparisons to BPG (HEVC intra) or WebP to better calibrate performance.  
- Suggestion: report encoding/decoding runtime, FLOPs, memory footprint, and model size for fair codec comparisons.  
- Suggestion: provide ablations on GDN/IGDN vs. ReLU/sigmoid, nonparametric vs. parametric latents, and the impact of CABAC adaptation.  
- Suggestion: discuss or explore single variable-rate models (e.g., learned scaling/conditioning) versus per-λ training.  
- Suggestion: analyze spatial statistics of residual mismatch to quantify how much context modeling could help.  
- Suggestion: expand test sets (e.g., Tecnick, CLIC) and report cross-dataset generalization.  
- Suggestion: include a small user study to corroborate MS-SSIM perceptual trends.  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to the latent independence assumption and would simple context models reduce bitrate for fixed distortion?  
- Question: what are encoding/decoding times and FLOPs for grayscale/RGB models at different rates, and how do they compare to JPEG2000?  
- Question: model parameter count and transmitted side information size — is λ index the only extra info beyond image size/color?  
- Question: robustness of additive-uniform-noise relaxation at very low rates and any systematic biases affecting training or performance?  
- Question: GDN/IGDN stability and invertibility — constraints to prevent IGDN explosion and any numerical issues at test time?  
- Question: feasibility and R–D trade-off of training a single conditional model covering multiple λ values?  
- Question: how does performance scale with network capacity/depth and is there evidence of diminishing returns beyond the 3-stage design?  
- Question: would training with a perceptual loss (e.g., MS-SSIM) further improve subjective quality without sacrificing rate?
