- Paper proposes end-to-end learned lossy image compression using convolutional GDN/IGDN transforms, additive-uniform relaxation of quantization, learned marginal priors, and CABAC entropy coding.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reasons: principled, practical relaxation enabling rate–distortion optimization and convincing empirical gains with real bitstreams.
- Empirical claims: better PSNR than JPEG/JPEG2000 on most images, substantially better MS-SSIM, and visibly improved artifacts at low rates.
- Methodological contribution: continuous relaxation of quantization via additive uniform noise enables stochastic-gradient training of a discrete coding problem.
- Architecture choice: nonlinear analysis/synthesis built from convolutions with GDN/IGDN motivated by Gaussianizing image statistics and biological plausibility.
- Theoretical placement: clear connection to transform coding and VAEs under the relaxation, with careful discussion of differences for compression objectives.
- Implementation strength: actual entropy coder (CABAC-based) used to report true bit rates rather than relying on differential entropy alone.
- Rigor: relaxed rate/distortion empirically track discrete entropy/MSE; optimization and training details are extensive.
- Acknowledged limitations: independence assumptions in latent space and a relatively simple entropy coder that likely leave compression performance on the table.
- Baseline fairness concern: should compare to stronger modern baselines (e.g., BPG/H.265 intra) and clarify JPEG/JPEG2000 settings and chroma handling.
- PSNR reporting: currently Y-channel only—request to report RGB PSNR or justify Y-only evaluation.
- Entropy modeling question: quantify gap between CABAC implementation and oracle entropy on learned marginals; consider simple context or channel conditioning experiments.
- Model ablations requested: sensitivity to number of stages/channels, GDN vs ReLU/sigmoid, GDN symmetry/exponent choices, and parameter-matched comparisons.
- Multi-rate limitation: separate model per λ—suggest experiments for single-model multi-rate operation (e.g., learned gain/scale).
- Generalization/resolution concern: training on 256×256 crops—report performance on higher-resolution images, tiling/padding strategy, and overfitting/distribution-shift signs.
- Complexity/efficiency: request FLOPs, runtime, and model size comparisons vs JPEG/JPEG2000 for deployment context.
- Perceptual claims: MS-SSIM supports claims but recommend additional perceptual metrics or a small user study for stronger validation.
- Requested measurements: per-rate breakdown of differential entropy vs discrete entropy vs realized CABAC bit rate.
- Dependency analysis: ask whether shuffling latents changes bit rate to measure remaining spatial correlations and independence assumption impact.
- High-res and tiling question: how does the method perform on full-resolution images and what boundary handling is used?
- Training details requested: exact training time, number of updates, convergence behavior across λ, and any mode-collapse evidence in marginal estimators.
- GDN effectiveness: ask if GDN/IGDN still outperform ReLU when networks are matched for depth/width and compute.
- Perceptual-loss experiments: ask whether training directly with MS-SSIM or other perceptual losses yields consistent RD improvements without hurting PSNR.
