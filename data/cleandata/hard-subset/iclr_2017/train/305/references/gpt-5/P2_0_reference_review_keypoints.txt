- End-to-end lossy image compression optimizing nonlinear analysis/synthesis transforms, quantizer relaxation, and an entropy model for R+λD.
- Uses cascaded convolutional transforms with GDN/IGDN to reduce higher-order image dependencies.
- Replaces discrete quantization with additive uniform noise relaxation whose differential entropy and MSE approximate discrete entropy and quantization distortion.
- Learns nonparametric, per-latent marginal models for the relaxed code distribution.
- Evaluates with an actual arithmetic entropy coder (CABAC) and reports true bit rates including side information.
- Trained per λ on ImageNet crops and tested on Kodak and other datasets, showing consistent MS-SSIM improvements over JPEG/JPEG 2000 and often better PSNR, especially at low rates.
- Decision: Accept.
- Primary reason: principled, differentiable R–D optimization with an implemented entropy coder and strong empirical perceptual/PSNR gains.
- The paper addresses a central transform-coding problem and justifies moving beyond linear transforms due to higher-order dependencies in images.
- Literature placement is strong, contrasting transform coding with generative/recurrent learned-compression approaches and motivating discrete-code comparisons.
- The additive uniform-noise relaxation is appropriate, empirically shows near-unbiased MSE and diminishing entropy bias at finer quantization.
- Learning nonparametric per-latent marginals fits the smoothing effect of noise; independence assumption is standard and acknowledged as a limitation.
- GDN/IGDN is well motivated by prior Gaussianization work; architecture and optimization stabilization are well described.
- Reporting true bit rates with a working entropy coder increases experimental credibility.
- Visual results convincingly show fewer basis-artifacts; MS-SSIM gains are broad and PSNR gains are common at low rates.
- The relationship to VAEs is carefully derived, clarifying why compression objectives differ from standard generative modeling.
- Limitation: models are trained per λ (no single variable-rate model), reducing deployment flexibility.
- Limitation: entropy model assumes independent latents and uses simple raster-scan coding with limited context adaptation.
- Limitation: baselines are limited to JPEG/JPEG 2000; stronger codecs (e.g., HEVC/BPG) are not compared and runtime/complexity is not reported.
- Limitation: main-text ablations on GDN vs simpler nonlinearities are minimal.
- Suggested ablations: GDN/IGDN vs ReLU/sigmoid under equal parameter budgets; nonparametric vs parametric marginals; effect of latent independence (autoregressive/context models).
- Suggest reporting encoding/decoding runtime and memory on CPU/GPU and comparisons vs JPEG/J2K for practicality.
- Suggest including or justifying omission of stronger baselines like BPG (HEVC-intra) and reporting aggregate R–D summaries (e.g., BD-rate).
- Suggest discussion/experiments on variable-rate extensions (shared model with scale parameter or conditioning on λ) and joint training tradeoffs.
- Questions: sensitivity to latent independence and any experiments with simple contexts or hyperpriors and measured bit-rate gains?
- Questions: feasibility of a single model across multiple rates (scaling latents or conditioning on λ) without large R–D loss?
- Questions: robustness of the uniform-noise relaxation at very coarse quantization; observed optimization pathologies and any formal entropy-bias bounds?
- Questions: encoding/decoding times and model sizes for different λ values and architectural speed/accuracy tradeoffs?
- Questions: generalization to higher-resolution images and diverse datasets; observed distribution-shift effects?
- Questions: color handling—compress in RGB or apply a color transform; does joint color-space learning improve R–D?
- Questions: choice of spline resolution for marginal models, frequency of dynamic-range re-fit, and sensitivity of rates to this discretization?
- Questions: feasibility of integrating more advanced entropy models (context-adaptive or hierarchical) into end-to-end training without losing differentiability?
