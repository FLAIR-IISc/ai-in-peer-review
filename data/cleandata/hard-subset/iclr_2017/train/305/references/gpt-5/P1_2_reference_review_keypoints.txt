- Jointly optimizes analysis/synthesis transforms, quantization (via uniform-noise relaxation), and entropy model to minimize a rate–distortion objective for lossy image compression.
- Replaces hard quantization in training with additive uniform noise to enable end-to-end SGD and provides analysis relating this relaxation to discrete entropy.
- Uses cascaded convolutions with GDN/IGDN transforms inspired by vision models, trained separately for different rate–distortion trade-offs (per-λ models).
- Employs nonparametric marginal models and a CABAC-style entropy coder to report actual bitrates rather than proxy metrics.
- Clarifies relation to variational autoencoders while emphasizing differences due to discrete coding and rate–distortion goals.
- Empirically achieves improved MS-SSIM across images and bitrates and often better PSNR than JPEG/JPEG2000, with more natural artifacts at low rates.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Strong, principled end-to-end formulation connecting classical rate–distortion theory to deep models with a well-justified continuous relaxation and implemented entropy coder.
- Key reason 2: Convincing empirical improvements in perceptual quality and PSNR over common baselines, with validation of the relaxation’s accuracy.
- Key reason 3: Clear exposition, solid positioning relative to VAEs and prior work, and demonstrated practical feasibility.
- Importance: Lossy image compression is significant and a learned nonlinear transform optimized end-to-end for rate–distortion is impactful and timely.
- Related work: Good background and distinction from generative modeling; missing comparisons to stronger codecs like BPG/HEVC-intra.
- Quantization relaxation: Additive uniform noise is principled and empirically validated to approximate discrete rate and distortion.
- Entropy modeling: Jointly trained nonparametric marginals reduce mismatch; independence assumption is standard but limiting, and raster-scan coding underuses spatial dependencies.
- Training strategy: Per-λ training aligns with rate–distortion convex hull interpretation but is less flexible in practice.
- Implementation: Filter/GDN parameterization, optimization details, and CABAC-like coding are adequate and reproducible.
- Empirical evaluation: Kodak and standard images show consistent MS-SSIM gains, frequent PSNR gains, and qualitative improvements; actual bitrate reporting strengthens claims.
- Limitations: Missing comparisons to modern codecs, limited statistical aggregation, and some evaluation breadth concerns.
- Suggested baseline additions: Compare to BPG/HEVC-intra and WebP to better situate performance.
- Practicality suggestions: Report model size, encoding/decoding runtime (CPU/GPU), and memory footprint.
- Single-model flexibility: Recommend exploring a single model supporting multiple rates via conditioning or adaptive quantization.
- Entropy model improvements: Try spatial context models or channel-wise hyperpriors to capture dependencies and reduce bitrate.
- Ablations requested: Quantify GDN vs ReLU/other nonlinearities, number of stages/channels, and nonparametric vs parametric priors.
- Fairness: Clarify color space conversions, chroma subsampling, and consistent PSNR/MS-SSIM computation across methods.
- Generalization: Test on more datasets (Tecnick, CLIC), higher resolutions, and report sensitivity to training set size/preprocessing.
- Reproducibility: Release code/models or provide detailed hyperparameters.
- Questions for authors include sensitivity to GDN choice, gap to oracle entropy coding and potential gains from spatial context, computational complexity and real-time feasibility, single-model rate support, training stability of the noise relaxation, RGB vs grayscale/chroma handling, and experiments with richer priors (mixtures/flows) while maintaining discrete coding.
