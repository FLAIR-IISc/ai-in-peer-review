Summary and contributions
The paper tackles lossy image compression as end-to-end optimization of a nonlinear transform coding pipeline. The analysis transform is a convolutional cascade with generalized divisive normalization (GDN), followed by uniform scalar quantization; the synthesis transform uses an approximate inverse (IGDN). Because quantization is non-differentiable, training replaces rounding with additive uniform noise and uses a nonparametric per-coefficient marginal model to estimate rate via differential entropy. The authors optimize R+λD with MSE distortion, train separate models per λ, implement an arithmetic entropy coder (CABAC-style), and report actual bitrates. Empirically, on Kodak (and some classic images), they show improved MS-SSIM at all rates versus JPEG/JPEG2000, and PSNR gains for most images, with qualitatively more pleasing artifacts. They also draw connections to VAEs, clarifying similarities and differences.

Decision: Reject
Key reasons: (1) The evaluation is not rigorous enough to substantiate the claimed performance: limited baselines (no modern codecs), incomplete and potentially unfair comparisons, and a lack of quantitative aggregate metrics (e.g., BD-rate) over a standard test suite. (2) Several methodological and reporting ambiguities (e.g., fixed unit quantization with changing filter normalizations, ad hoc architectures per λ, limited entropy modeling) undermine reproducibility and the strength of the empirical claims.

Supporting arguments
- Baselines and fairness. The comparisons exclude stronger modern baselines (e.g., BPG/HEVC-intra, WebP, recent learned codecs), which were available and widely used for benchmarking. Comparing only to JPEG/JPEG2000 makes it hard to assess state of the art performance. For color, JPEG is evaluated with 4:2:0 while the proposed method operates in full resolution color spaces; a 4:4:4 JPEG comparison should be included to avoid penalizing JPEG. The entropy coder used is acknowledged to be relatively weak and to exploit limited context, which complicates fairness relative to JPEG2000’s mature coder; paradoxically, this could both understate your method or, because architecture capacity varies with λ, overstate gains at specific points. A rigorous aggregate R–D summary (e.g., BD-rate on Kodak for PSNR and MS-SSIM) is missing; per-image qualitative galleries are helpful but not a substitute for quantitative summaries.

- Methodological clarity. The “unit bin” quantization assumption while simultaneously renormalizing filters after every step creates an implicit, learned per-channel/space scaling that effectively changes quantization step sizes. This deserves explicit treatment (or removal of renormalization during final training) to align the relaxed objective with the discrete system. The upsampling operator (eq. 5) is underspecified (“otherwise,” with no value) and appears to be zero-insertion; please clarify boundary handling and anti-aliasing. The independence assumption in the latent space plus per-channel spatially shared marginals is a strong modeling choice; the resulting coder is known to leave substantial dependency unmodeled. Without context models or hyperpriors, it is unclear whether reported gains arise from the transform or from suboptimal entropy coding in baselines.

- Evaluation of the relaxation. The paper states the relaxed distortion is “mostly unbiased,” and the relaxed entropy overestimates at coarse quantization, but provides only qualitative plots. Please report quantitative bias/variance statistics (e.g., mean absolute bias and correlation across images/rates) to justify the approximation.

- Ad hoc, per-λ architectures. Capacity changes with λ (e.g., 256/192 channels at high rates and RGB), and distinct models are trained per λ. This complicates claims about the method’s intrinsic R–D efficiency and practical deployment (storage and side information for many models). A single variable-rate model (even if not optimal) or a controlled ablation where architecture is fixed across λ would strengthen the claims.

- Limited ablations. Although the text mentions preliminary tests with ReLU/sigmoid, there is no systematic study. Key ablations missing: GDN vs pointwise nonlinearity; effect of number of stages; effect of entropy model resolution (10 samples per unit); with/without filter DCT parameterization; with/without filter renormalization; effect of context adaptation in the entropy coder.

- Perceptual claims. The paper asserts consistent perceptual superiority and cites MS-SSIM improvements. MS-SSIM is computed on luma only and is known to have biases. A small-scale user study or additional perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS) would substantiate the claim. Also clarify the color processing pipeline alignment across methods for metric computation.

- Reproducibility and practical aspects. Important implementation details are scattered and some are ambiguous (e.g., IGDN exact form, upsampling, padding). Training/compute cost, encoding/decoding runtime on CPU/GPU, and model sizes are not reported. Side information overhead (model index λ, model parameters assumed shared) should be quantified. Code availability is not stated.

- Presentation/formatting. There are numerous citation and typographical issues (e.g., malformed references with question marks/backslashes; “continous”; broken equation (10) domain; eq. (5) incomplete). Figures are essential to the argument but not all are clearly described; variable naming could be streamlined.

Feedback to improve the paper
- Expand baselines to include BPG/HEVC-intra and a recent learned codec; report BD-rate on Kodak for PSNR and MS-SSIM; include both 4:2:0 and 4:4:4 JPEG where appropriate.
- Provide a single variable-rate model or a fixed-architecture sweep across λ; quantify storage/side-info implications for per-λ models.
- Clarify the quantization/renormalization interplay; consider training with fixed scaling in the final fine-tuning to align with unit step size.
- Add ablations on GDN vs pointwise nonlinearities, number of stages, entropy model resolution, and entropy coder strength (e.g., with/without simple spatial context).
- Quantify the relaxation accuracy with bias/variance metrics; include scatter plots with regression.
- Report runtime, memory, and model size; specify padding and upsampling precisely; release code/models.
- Improve references and fix formatting.

Questions for the authors
1. How does your method compare to BPG/HEVC-intra in PSNR and MS-SSIM on Kodak (BD-rate)?  
2. What is the quantitative bias/variance of the relaxed objective for both rate and distortion across the tested operating points?  
3. How much do filter renormalization and unit-bin quantization interact? If renormalization is removed in final training, do results change?  
4. Can you provide a variable-rate model (e.g., learned rate control) and compare its R–D to per-λ models?  
5. What are encode/decode runtimes on CPU/GPU and model sizes?  
6. How sensitive are results to the independence assumption in the latent space? Do simple spatial/context priors or hyperpriors further improve bitrates?  
7. Please provide ablations replacing GDN/IGDN with ReLU/sigmoid and varying depth; how much of the gain is due to GDN specifically?  
8. For color images, can you include 4:4:4 JPEG comparisons and clarify the color transform used during coding and metric computation?
