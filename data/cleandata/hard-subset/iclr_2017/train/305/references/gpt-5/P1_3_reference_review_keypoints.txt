- Proposed end-to-end learned nonlinear transform coding using convolutional analysis/synthesis with GDN/IGDN, additive uniform-noise relaxation for quantization, a learned factorized prior, and a context-adaptive arithmetic coder reporting real bit rates.  
- Demonstrates improved perceptual quality and generally better PSNR/MS-SSIM than JPEG and JPEG 2000 on Kodak, with strong MS-SSIM gains.  
- Clarifies the connection to VAEs and why compression requires different design choices (discrete coding, λ selection, bitstream evaluation).  
- Decision: Accept — reasons: principled end-to-end formulation, convincing empirical gains, and clear literature positioning.  
- Validates the additive-noise relaxation for quantization (bias/variance checks) as a principled, empirically supported approach.  
- Model design: GDN cascade is biologically motivated and empirically effective, enabling efficient models with few stages.  
- Rate modeling/coding: Non-parametric factorized marginals plus CABAC-like coder produce true bitrates, enabling fair comparisons to deployed codecs.  
- Empirics: Marked reduction in blocking/ringing, better contour preservation at low rates, and compelling qualitative results.  
- VAE relation: Shows relaxed objective ≈ VAE with uniform posterior and explains necessary divergences for practical compression.  
- Suggest adding stronger modern baselines (e.g., BPG/HEVC) or matched-rate comparisons to contextualize gains beyond JPEG/J2K.  
- Recommend ablation studies isolating GDN vs ReLU, stage/channel counts, prior choices, and entropy coder adaptivity.  
- Ask for encoding/decoding runtime and memory comparisons (GPU/CPU) versus JPEG/J2K to assess deployability.  
- Note inconvenience of training separate models per λ; suggest exploring single-model variable-rate mechanisms (quantization scaling, pruning, λ-conditioning).  
- Request broader testing (Tecnick/CLIC/high-res) and reporting of aggregate metrics with CIs; clarify color-space and chroma subsampling handling.  
- Ask for bitstream breakdown (headers, side info, latents) and effects of CABAC adaptivity vs static arithmetic coding.  
- Suggest a small user study to strengthen perceptual quality claims, especially at low rates.  
- Question sensitivity of RD results to noise width/distribution and whether noise annealing or mismatched noise was tried.  
- Ask how much improvement remains if spatial/channel dependencies are modeled (context models/hyperpriors) and gap between differential entropy and achieved bits.  
- Ask if transforms can be reused for variable-rate coding via per-channel scaling without retraining.  
- Request encoding/decoding runtimes for 512×512 RGB on CPU/GPU and comparison to JPEG 2000.  
- Inquire about training stability of GDN/IGDN and importance of parameterizations/normalizations.  
- Ask how performance generalizes to high-resolution, non-natural images (medical, graphics, text overlays).  
- Clarify internal color space, whether metrics are computed on luma or RGB, and channel-symmetric coding.  
- Ask about reproducibility: will code/models and entropy coder be released or full bitstream reproduction details provided?
