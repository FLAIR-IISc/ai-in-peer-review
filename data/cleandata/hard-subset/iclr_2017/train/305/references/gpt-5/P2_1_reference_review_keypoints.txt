- Proposes end-to-end lossy image compression with jointly learned analysis/synthesis transforms using cascaded convolutions and GDN/IGDN.  
- Handles quantization by replacing rounding with additive uniform noise, enabling stochastic gradient descent.  
- Models rate with nonparametric marginal densities on relaxed latents and uses an implemented arithmetic coder to report actual bitrates.  
- Establishes and clarifies the relationship to variational autoencoders, noting key differences for compression.  
- Demonstrates improved perceptual quality (MS-SSIM) and often better PSNR than JPEG/JPEG2000, especially at low bitrates, with more natural artifacts.  
- Empirically validates that the continuous relaxation is a good surrogate for discrete rate and distortion during training.  
- Decision: Accept — contribution is substantive and timely due to sound relaxation and real entropy coding plus strong empirical evidence.  
- Problem motivation: well-framed joint learning of transforms, grounded in transform coding and vision literature.  
- Methodological soundness: additive uniform noise relaxation is standard and carefully applied and validated.  
- Entropy model: independent nonparametric piecewise-linear marginals reduce model mismatch and are realized with an actual arithmetic coder.  
- Architecture and training: detailed descriptions (staged convs, GDN/IGDN, stability tricks) improve reproducibility.  
- Empirical support: per-image RD curves and visual examples convincingly show perceptual gains and reduced blocking/ringing.  
- Scope/novelty: fills a gap in learned compression by combining RD optimization, realistic rate accounting, and practical comparisons.  
- Recommendation to include modern baseline (e.g., HEVC intra/BPG) or discuss expected gaps for stronger practical comparison.  
- Suggest reporting BD-rate summaries (per-image and average) to quantify RD gains alongside qualitative cautions.  
- Request ablations: GDN/IGDN vs ReLU/other nonlinearities, nonparametric vs parametric density, and independence vs simple context models.  
- Practical deployment concern: training a separate model per λ; discuss strategies for a single conditional/multi-rate model.  
- Ask to report encoder/decoder runtime, parameter count, memory, and throughput on common hardware for deployability assessment.  
- Recommend broader evaluation on larger, more diverse datasets and higher resolutions for generalization.  
- Clarify color-space choices and sensitivity to chroma subsampling (JPEG uses 4:2:0) in comparisons.  
- Question sensitivity to independence assumption in latent entropy model and potential gains from lightweight spatial context.  
- Request BD-rate numbers vs JPEG/JPEG2000 for both PSNR and MS-SSIM (per-image and average).  
- Request encoder/decoder runtimes and memory footprints for 512×512 and 1080p on CPU and GPU, and comparisons to JPEG2000.  
- Ask about training stability across random seeds/dataset splits and evidence of overfitting to ImageNet crops.  
- Ask for quantification of GDN/IGDN contribution when matching depth/width to pointwise nonlinearities.  
- Ask whether the relaxation remains accurate at very low rates (<0.1 bpp) and if bias affects optimization there.  
- Ask about feasible approaches to cover a continuum of rates in a single model (e.g., learned scale hyperprior or λ-conditioning).
