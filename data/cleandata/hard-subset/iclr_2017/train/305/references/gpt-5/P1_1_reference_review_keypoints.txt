- Paper proposes end-to-end optimization of lossy image compression learning transforms, quantized latent distribution, and entropy code to optimize rate–distortion.
- Key technical ingredients: cascaded conv transforms with GDN/IGDN to Gaussianize latents.
- Key technical ingredients: differentiable quantization surrogate using additive uniform noise for SGD on a relaxed objective.
- Key technical ingredients: nonparametric, factorized marginal models for latents.
- Key technical ingredients: implemented CABAC-like entropy coder to report actual bitrates.
- Paper relates the relaxed objective to VAEs and explains conceptual/practical differences with rate–distortion optimization.
- Empirically shows consistent perceptual improvements (MS-SSIM) over JPEG/JPEG2000 and better PSNR for most images, especially at low rates.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: Strong, well-motivated end-to-end framework bridging transform coding and deep learning with a differentiable rate–distortion objective and real bitrates.
- Key reason: Convincing empirical evidence and ablations validating the quantization/noise relaxation.
- Problem/motivation: Replaces piecemeal codec design with a single end-to-end objective; uniform noise surrogate and marginal likelihood make SGD tractable.
- Relation to literature: Precisely connects to VAEs and situates against contemporary neural compression and classical transform coding.
- Technical soundness: Design choices (GDN/IGDN, factorized priors, constraints) motivated by Gaussianization and stability; evaluates bias/variance of the surrogate.
- Empirical rigor: Large-scale training, standard test sets, and reporting of actual bitstreams are major strengths; visuals and MS-SSIM favor the method.
- Practicality: Inference is efficient (feed-forward transforms + scalar quantization + entropy coding); training is costly.
- Concern: Missing comparisons to stronger modern baselines (e.g., BPG/HEVC-intra), limiting placement against state-of-the-art.
- Concern: Fully factorized latent prior and modest CABAC context underuse spatial dependencies, leaving potential rate savings untapped.
- Concern: Separate model per λ raises storage/maintenance concerns versus single-codec or progressive solutions.
- Concern: Limited evaluation breadth (Kodak small); no user study provided.
- Concern: Encoding/decoding time, memory, and energy are not reported.
- Suggestion: Add comparison to BPG/HEVC-intra (at least on Kodak) and discuss visual/artifact differences.
- Suggestion: Provide runtime/complexity metrics, model size per λ, and discuss single conditional model feasibility.
- Suggestion: Extend evaluation to larger test sets (e.g., Tecnick, CLIC) and align aggregate R–D curves.
- Suggestion: Include ablations (GDN vs pointwise nonlinearity, stages, priors, CABAC adaptation).
- Suggestion: Clarify fairness details for baselines (JPEG/JPEG2000 params, color handling, chroma subsampling).
- Suggestion: Release code/models and entropy coder for reproducibility.
- Questions: How does the method compare to BPG/HEVC-intra on Kodak in PSNR/MS-SSIM and visually?
- Questions: What are encoding/decoding times and memory for 512×512 on CPU/GPU; is it real-time?
- Questions: Can a single model with continuous rate-control cover multiple rates near the convex hull?
- Questions: How sensitive are gains to GDN/IGDN vs ReLU/ELU at matched capacity?
- Questions: How much bitrate is lost by the factorized prior and raster-scan CABAC; have simple spatial contexts been tried?
- Questions: Do results hold on larger test sets and are there failure modes where JPEG2000 dominates?
- Questions: Details on side-information overhead for λ and model selection and scaling if many λ values are supported?
