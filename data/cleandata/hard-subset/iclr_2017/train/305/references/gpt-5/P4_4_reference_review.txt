1. Summary of contributions
- The paper presents an end-to-end optimized image compression framework based on cascaded convolutional transforms with generalized divisive normalization (GDN) and its inverse (IGDN), followed by uniform scalar quantization and entropy coding.
- To enable gradient-based training despite quantization, the authors replace quantization with additive uniform noise during optimization and jointly learn analysis/synthesis transforms and nonparametric marginal entropy models. They then evaluate with actual entropy coding (CABAC), reporting true bit rates rather than entropy estimates.
- The formulation is connected to variational autoencoders (VAEs) by showing that the relaxed rate–distortion objective corresponds to a VAE with a specific Gaussian likelihood and a uniform approximate posterior over quantization bins.
- Empirically, on the Kodak dataset and additional images, the method outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 at most rates in PSNR and improves MS-SSIM consistently, while yielding more visually natural artifacts. The paper also analyzes the fidelity of the relaxed objective to the actual rate/distortion after quantization.

2. Decision
Accept. Key reasons:
- Technical soundness and clarity of an end-to-end, differentiable rate–distortion formulation with a principled quantization relaxation, and a complete system reporting actual bitrates.
- Novelty and significance of learning nonlinear transforms with GDN/IGDN for compression, yielding strong empirical performance and perceptual quality over widely used baselines.

3. Supporting arguments
Clarity:
- The paper is clearly written and well-structured. The transforms, training objective, and relationship to VAEs are carefully derived. Architectural and training details are provided in the appendix, including entropy coding implementation and evaluation protocol.

Technical soundness:
- Replacing rounding with additive uniform noise is a standard and appropriate relaxation; the paper validates that the relaxed objective closely tracks true rates and distortions, with predictable bias at coarse quantization diminishing at higher rates.
- The equivalence to a VAE objective (under MSE) is correctly derived. The argument for unit quantization step size without loss of generality is sound.
- The entropy model (factorized, nonparametric marginals) and CABAC implementation are coherent; bitstreams include side information. The separation between training relaxation and evaluation with real quantization/entropy coding makes the claims robust.

Novelty and placement:
- Compared to contemporaneous learned compression works (e.g., recurrent/auto-regressive frameworks or purely generative models), this paper (i) optimizes an explicit rate–distortion objective end-to-end, (ii) employs learned joint nonlinearities (GDN/IGDN) rather than only pointwise activations, and (iii) reports actual bitrates with an implemented coder. The VAE connection is insightful and helps position the work within representation learning.
- While additive-noise relaxations and entropy modeling are known, their integration with GDN-based transforms and full-system evaluation represents a meaningful advancement for learned compression.

Empirical support:
- The method shows consistent MS-SSIM gains and frequent PSNR gains over JPEG and JPEG 2000 across rates on common evaluation images. Visual comparisons substantiate claims about artifact characteristics.
- The authors discuss the challenges of aggregating rate–distortion across images and provide per-image curves and artifacts, which is appropriate.

Limitations (do not outweigh strengths but temper claims):
- Baselines exclude stronger modern standards (e.g., BPG/HEVC intra) that were available; this makes it difficult to situate absolute performance versus state of the art in classical codecs.
- The entropy model is factorized across latents with limited spatial context; stronger context/hyperprior models may further improve rates.
- A separate model is trained per λ; a single model covering multiple bitrates would be more practical.
- No user study; perceptual claims rest on MS-SSIM and qualitative examples.
- No ablation quantifying the contribution of GDN vs. simpler nonlinearities in this setup (though preliminary notes are provided).

4. Additional feedback (to help improve the paper; not all are part of the acceptance decision)
- Include comparisons to a stronger modern codec (e.g., BPG/HEVC intra) to better contextualize performance. Reporting BD-rate (PSNR and MS-SSIM) would facilitate standardized comparisons.
- Provide an ablation study: GDN/IGDN vs. ReLU/tanh with similar parameter budgets; number of stages; factorized vs. lightly context-aware entropy models; impact of nonparametric marginal modeling resolution.
- Report computational complexity and throughput (encode/decode time on CPU/GPU), and model size. This will help assess deployability.
- Clarify generalization: performance on a disjoint, larger, and more diverse test set beyond Kodak (e.g., Tecnick, CLIC validation), and sensitivity to training set size and preprocessing.
- Analyze the relaxed-vs-actual rate/distortion calibration across the full operating range and different architectures; quantify any systematic bias.
- Consider a single model across rates (e.g., via learned scale parameters or conditional transforms) and/or per-image λ control at inference time.
- Explore richer entropy models (spatial context, hierarchical priors) given the observed dependence structure after GDN.

Questions for the authors
- How does the method compare to BPG (HEVC intra) in PSNR and MS-SSIM on Kodak and other standard datasets? If not available, what prevents inclusion?
- What are the encode/decode runtimes and memory footprints for typical image sizes? How do they scale with the number of channels/stages?
- How sensitive is performance to the independence assumption in the code space? Did you test simple spatial/context models or channel-wise autoregressive coding?
- Can a single trained model achieve a range of rates (e.g., via changing quantization step or learned scaling) without retraining for each λ? If attempted, how does the rate–distortion envelope compare?
- Could you provide quantitative ablations isolating the effect of GDN/IGDN versus pointwise nonlinearities, holding parameter count and depth constant?
- How much of the reported bitrate is side information (e.g., λ index, model identifiers)? Please provide a breakdown.
- For RGB, which color space was used for training and evaluation? Do conclusions change if training is done in YCbCr with chroma subsampling?
- Did you observe any failure cases (images or structures) where the method underperforms JPEG 2000 at high rates? What characterizes those cases?
