1) Summary of the paper’s claims and contributions
The paper proposes an end-to-end image compression framework that jointly optimizes analysis and synthesis transforms, quantization, and an entropy model for a given rate–distortion operating point. The key ideas are:
- Replace non-differentiable quantization with additive uniform noise to obtain a continuous, differentiable relaxation of both the rate (via differential entropy) and distortion terms, enabling stochastic gradient descent.
- Use cascaded convolutional transforms with generalized divisive normalization (GDN/IGDN) to better Gaussianize and decorrelate latent coefficients than typical linear transforms.
- Learn nonparametric, factorized marginal models for latent variables and implement a practical entropy coder (CABAC), reporting actual bitrates rather than differential entropy proxies.
- Demonstrate strong qualitative improvements and competitive or superior RD performance compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000 on Kodak and additional images; despite training on MSE, the method yields substantial gains in MS-SSIM and perceptual appearance across rates.

Overall, the paper aims to convincingly show that a learned nonlinear transform coding pipeline can be trained end-to-end for RD performance and realized as a complete codec with real bitstreams.

2) Decision: Accept
Primary reasons:
- Clear, well-motivated end-to-end RD formulation with a simple and effective quantization relaxation; strong and timely conceptual contribution.
- Solid empirical evidence with actual entropy coding, improved perceptual quality, and competitive PSNR/MS-SSIM versus widely used baselines.

3) Supporting arguments
What problem is tackled?
- Learning a compression model that optimizes the classical rate–distortion objective directly, for continuous-valued images that must be quantized, overcoming the optimization difficulties due to non-differentiable quantization.

Is the approach well-motivated and placed in the literature?
- Yes. The paper connects classical transform coding, vector quantization, and modern deep learning. The use of GDN is motivated by prior work on Gaussianizing image statistics. The relationship to VAEs is explicitly analyzed, carefully distinguishing compression from generative modeling. The practical choice to implement an entropy coder and report bitrates is important and often missing in related work (e.g., conceptual compression). Prior neural compression efforts (e.g., Toderici et al.) are discussed with a fair comparison of goals and limitations.

Does the paper support its claims rigorously?
- Methodology: The quantization relaxation via additive uniform noise is well justified; the nonparametric marginal modeling reduces model mismatch; and separate training per λ explores the convex RD tradeoff. Architectural and training details are described.
- Empirical support: The authors validate the relaxation by comparing relaxed RD to discrete RD; they show multiple examples and RD curves on Kodak; they provide MS-SSIM and PSNR comparisons and use an actual entropy coder. The perceptual results are compelling and consistent with MS-SSIM.
- Limitations: The primary baselines are JPEG and JPEG2000; more modern baselines (e.g., BPG/HEVC-intra) are not included. The latent independence assumption may limit ultimate efficiency; the entropy coder is relatively simple in spatial adaptation. The evaluation set (Kodak, 24 images) is small, though common, and broader testing would strengthen claims of generality. An ablation on the role of GDN vs pointwise nonlinearities is only briefly discussed.

4) Additional feedback and questions to help improve the paper (not all required for acceptance)
Suggestions for strengthening the work:
- Baselines: Include stronger modern baselines (e.g., BPG/HEVC intra, WebP) to calibrate performance against the current state of the art, especially at low bitrates.
- Fairness in color handling: JPEG is evaluated with 4:2:0 subsampling while your method appears to code three color channels without subsampling. Please add JPEG 4:4:4 results and clarify color space and chroma handling for all methods. Report whether PSNR is on RGB or Y’CbCr components, and ensure consistent handling across codecs.
- Ablations: Quantify the contribution of GDN/IGDN versus pointwise nonlinearities for the same model capacity, as well as the effect of the nonparametric marginals vs parametric alternatives.
- Entropy modeling: The independence assumption is a known bottleneck. Including a simple autoregressive/context model or a channel-wise hyperprior would likely help; even a partial experiment (or analysis of residual correlations in latents) would be informative.
- Generalization and datasets: Add results on larger and more diverse test sets (e.g., DIV2K, Tecnick, BSDS500) to increase confidence in generality. Consider reporting rate savings aggregated via Bjøntegaard metrics or carefully controlled matched-rate points.
- Complexity: Provide encoding/decoding runtime, memory, and model size; discuss practical deployment and latency vs JPEG/JP2K.
- Single model for multiple rates: While training per-λ is principled, practitioners often need variable rate from one model. Consider adding gains/quantization scaling or conditional modules to sweep across rates without retraining.
- Reproducibility: If possible, release code, trained models, and bitstreams to facilitate verification.

Questions to the authors:
1) Relaxation fidelity: Beyond figure 4, can you quantify the gap between differential entropy and achieved bitrates across rates and images (mean and variance), and how it scales with λ?
2) Entropy coder overhead: What is the average overhead (bits/pixel or percentage) of your CABAC stream relative to the discrete entropy of the learned marginals?
3) Latent dependencies: Have you measured residual spatial/channel correlations in ŷ? How much bitrate could be saved by a simple 2D context model?
4) Architecture per λ: You increase channels for larger λ. How sensitive are the RD gains to capacity? Would a single fixed-capacity architecture across λ significantly change conclusions?
5) JPEG configuration: Please report JPEG 4:4:4 results and clarify whether MS-SSIM and PSNR are computed on luma only or on full color, and ensure consistent color transforms across methods.
6) Training details: How robust is training to the noise width (fixed to 1)? Did you try learned quantization step sizes or per-channel scalings, and how did that affect RD?
7) Computational cost: What are typical encode/decode times on CPU/GPU for Kodak-sized images? Any insights on model efficiency vs GDN’s parameterization?

Overall, this is a strong, well-argued paper that advances learned compression by bringing end-to-end RD optimization together with practical entropy coding and delivers convincing perceptual and quantitative results.
