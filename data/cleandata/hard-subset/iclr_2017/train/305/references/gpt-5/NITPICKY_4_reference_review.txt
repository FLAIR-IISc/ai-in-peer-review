Summary and contributions
- The paper proposes an end-to-end learned image compression system based on cascades of linear convolutions and generalized divisive normalization (GDN) in the analysis transform and an approximate inverse (IGDN) in the synthesis transform. Quantization is handled during training via a continuous relaxation (additive i.i.d. uniform noise) to enable stochastic gradient descent on a rate–distortion objective.
- The rate term uses a factorized, nonparametric marginal model of latents (piecewise-linear PDFs), trained jointly with the transforms. At evaluation time, true scalar quantization and an implemented entropy coder (CABAC-style) are used, and actual bitrates are reported.
- The paper draws a connection to VAEs by interpreting the relaxed rate–distortion objective as a KL minimization with a specific Gaussian “slack” term.
- Experiments on Kodak and a few classic images show perceptual improvements vs. JPEG/JPEG2000; MS-SSIM improves consistently, and PSNR is better for most images, especially at low rates. The authors also provide training and architectural details (e.g., channel counts, down/up-sampling factors) and an analysis of the relaxation gap between differential entropy and true entropy.

Decision: Reject
Key reasons:
1) Serious violations of ICLR double-blind policy and uneven experimental evidence. The paper contains author-identifying information and URLs to non-anonymized supplemental material. The core empirical case relies heavily on external qualitative figures and per-image plots hosted on a non-anonymized website, and the test set is small (mostly Kodak).
2) Methodological and reporting gaps that undermine confidence: missing modern baselines, limited ablations, several technical ambiguities/typos (including malformed equations), and insufficient analysis of practical aspects (model size, compute, decoder memory).

Supporting arguments
- Anonymization and reproducibility:
  - The manuscript contains author names/affiliations (footnotes, citations) and links to a clearly identifying website. This breaks double-blind reviewing and is grounds for rejection.
  - Heavy reliance on external qualitative material “available online” complicates reproducibility and objective assessment during review. A self-contained, anonymized appendix is needed.
- Experimental scope and baselines:
  - Comparisons are limited to JPEG and JPEG2000 (OpenJPEG defaults). By 2016/2017, BPG (HEVC intra), WebP, and modern J2K settings are standard baselines. Missing these makes conclusions about state-of-the-art performance incomplete.
  - Evaluation primarily on Kodak (24 images) plus a few classics. No larger, diverse test sets (e.g., Tecnick, CLIC, DIV2K) or cross-dataset generalization. No statistical aggregation (e.g., BD-Rate/BD-PSNR) across images; the paper explicitly discourages summaries.
  - Each rate point is a separate model indexed by λ; this inflates deployment/storage and complicates fair real-world comparison (codecs typically provide a single model with rate control). The bitstream includes a λ index, but the paper omits the decoder’s model storage footprint.
- Methodological/clarity issues:
  - Eq. (5) for upsampling is malformed: it has an “otherwise,” with no branch, leaving boundary handling undefined. Several typographical issues (notation inconsistencies, missing hats/subscripts, nonstandard exponent placement in GDN/IGDN) impede unambiguous reimplementation.
  - The entropy model is factorized across latents, yet the arithmetic coder introduces limited adaptation. The mismatch between training (factorized PDFs) and coding (context-adaptive decisions) isn’t quantified; a stronger context model is likely to change rates materially.
  - The relaxation analysis (differential vs. discrete entropy) is qualitative. Bias/variance of the surrogate is discussed but not statistically summarized across rates/images; no confidence intervals.
  - No ablations isolating the contributions of GDN vs. simpler nonlinearities, number of stages, or the nonparametric marginal model. Claims about GDN efficiency are stated as “preliminary results” without figures/tables.
- Practicality:
  - Compute complexity and latency (FLOPs, runtime on CPU/GPU) and memory footprints (model size per λ; total storage if multiple λ are deployed) are not reported.
  - The entropy coder description is high-level; implementation choices (context design, adaptation schedule) appear simplistic compared to JPEG2000/HEVC; the impact on bitrates is only briefly acknowledged.
- Formatting/references:
  - References contain numerous inconsistencies and placeholders (e.g., “?, \Q2016\E”), missing venues, incomplete author names, broken titles. This detracts from polish and makes verification difficult.

Additional feedback to improve the paper (not all part of the decision)
- Provide anonymized, self-contained supplementary material with all images, code, and pretrained models. Include scripts to reproduce figures and bitrates.
- Add strong baselines: BPG/HEVC-intra, WebP, and tuned JPEG2000; report BD-Rate/BD-PSNR and BD-MS-SSIM over multiple datasets (Kodak, Tecnick, DIV2K validation, CLIC).
- Include ablations: GDN vs. ReLU/LeakyReLU/Swish; number of stages; factorized vs. context models; piecewise-linear density resolution; training with and without per-channel sharing of marginals.
- Quantify compute and memory: FLOPs/frame, runtime on typical hardware, model size per λ and total if multiple models are shipped. Discuss how to support a continuum of rates (e.g., scale hyperprior, learned rate control) within a single model.
- Clarify and fix equations (especially Eq. 5) and notation throughout; provide precise boundary/upsampling rules.
- Expand the analysis of the relaxation: report mean/variance of the rate and distortion gaps across datasets and rates; show scatter plots and confidence intervals.
- Discuss color processing choices (YCbCr vs RGB) and their effect; evaluate both PSNR-Y and PSNR-RGB consistently.

Questions for the authors
1) How many λ models are trained and stored? What is the total model size and decoder memory if multiple rates are supported? Can a single model cover a continuum of rates without retraining?
2) What are the exact computational costs (FLOPs, ms per megapixel) for encode/decode on CPU and GPU? How do they compare to JPEG2000 and BPG?
3) Please correct Eq. (5) and specify the exact upsampling operation and boundary handling used in synthesis. Are there any learned upsampling filters or zero-insertion + convolution?
4) How sensitive are results to the factorized latent assumption? What is the bitrate gain from your (limited) context adaptation, and how much headroom remains vs. an oracle with perfect context?
5) Can you provide quantitative estimates (mean±std) of the relaxation bias for rate and distortion across datasets and rates?
6) Why were modern baselines (BPG/HEVC-intra) omitted? If added, how does your method compare in PSNR and MS-SSIM?
7) Please provide ablations demonstrating the necessity of GDN/IGDN vs. simpler nonlinearities at matched parameter counts and depths.
