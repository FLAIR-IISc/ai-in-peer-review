- Paper proposes end-to-end learned lossy image compression optimizing transforms, quantization relaxation, and entropy models for rate–distortion trade-offs.
- Core methods: cascaded conv layers with GDN/IGDN transforms, additive uniform-noise relaxation of quantization, and nonparametric factorized marginal models (piecewise-linear splines).
- Practical evaluation: per-λ trained models encoded with a CABAC-style entropy coder and reported actual bitrates.
- Empirical results: strong MS-SSIM improvements and competitive-to-better PSNR versus JPEG/JPEG2000, especially at low bitrates.
- Clarifies relation to VAEs and provides principled probabilistic interpretation of the relaxation.
- Decision: Accept.
- Primary reasons: novel, well-motivated methodology bridging classic rate–distortion and deep learning, with solid empirical evidence using real bitstreams.
- Strength: elegant and justified additive-noise quantization relaxation enabling differentiable rate and distortion estimates.
- Strength: end-to-end per-λ optimization exposes the convex hull of achievable RD points and aligns with transform coding intuition.
- Strength: GDN/IGDN transform design is simple, effective, and motivated by Gaussianization/vision literature.
- Strength: rigorous evaluation reporting actual bitrates and thorough qualitative artifact analysis.
- Strength: accurate contextualization of prior work and differences from VAEs.
- Concern: factorized latent entropy model limits compression — lacks spatial/context/hyperprior modeling used by stronger codecs.
- Concern: training separate model per λ reduces practicality compared to multi-rate models.
- Concern: missing comparisons to stronger baselines (e.g., BPG/HEVC intra, contemporary learned codecs).
- Concern: small test set (Kodak) and limited aggregated metrics; BD-rate or larger suites would help interpretability.
- Concern: limited quantitative ablations on architecture, entropy model resolution, and CABAC adaptation effects.
- Concern: no reported encoding/decoding runtime, memory footprint, or model size analysis.
- Suggested improvements: include BPG/learned-codec comparisons and a compact ablation table for key design choices.
- Suggested improvements: explore multi-rate single-model strategies (conditioning, learned quantizer scaling).
- Suggested improvements: add minimal spatial context or hyperprior to entropy model to close performance gaps.
- Suggested improvements: improve reproducibility (detailed baselines, release code/models/entropy coder).
- Suggested improvements: report FLOPs, latency, and memory for encoder/decoder variants.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to factorized latent assumption and potential gains from simple autoregressive/hyperprior models?
- Questions: robustness of additive-noise relaxation across rates and risk of suboptimal discretized behavior?
- Questions: can a single conditional model support multiple λs with small losses (λ sampling or learned scale)?
- Questions: encoding/decoding runtimes and model sizes for 128/192/256-channel variants vs. JPEG/JP2K?
- Questions: internal color space for RGB and potential benefits of learned color transforms?
- Questions: how much MS-SSIM gain stems from transform design vs. entropy coding details (e.g., non-adaptive coder RD delta)?
- Overall summary: strong, principled, and practical contribution to learned image compression; addressing context modeling and multi-rate usability would further increase impact.
