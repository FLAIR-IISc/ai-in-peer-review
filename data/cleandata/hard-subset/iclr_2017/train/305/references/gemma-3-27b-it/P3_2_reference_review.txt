## ICLR 2024 Review: END-TO-END OPTIMIZED IMAGE COMPRESSION

**1. Summary:**

This paper presents a novel end-to-end optimized image compression framework based on cascaded nonlinear transformations, specifically utilizing Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN). The authors build upon their previous work, demonstrating that jointly optimizing the analysis and synthesis transforms, combined with uniform scalar quantization and a continuous relaxation for optimization, leads to superior performance compared to traditional transform coding methods like JPEG and JPEG 2000 – particularly in terms of perceptual quality.  The framework is rigorously evaluated, showing improved rate-distortion performance and visually more pleasing reconstructions. The work offers a compelling connection between rate-distortion optimization and variational autoencoders, highlighting shared underlying principles.

**2. Decision:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. 

The paper tackles a fundamental problem in a creative and technically sound manner. The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, and the detailed analysis and clear writing make the paper a significant contribution to the field. The connection to VAEs provides a valuable theoretical grounding and positions the work within a broader research context.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

* **Strong Motivation & Problem Formulation:** The authors clearly articulate the limitations of traditional transform coding, highlighting the difficulty of joint rate-distortion optimization. The proposed framework directly addresses these challenges through end-to-end learning.
* **Novelty & Technical Soundness:** The use of cascaded GDN transforms, optimized jointly with the synthesis network and entropy coding, is a novel approach. The continuous relaxation trick for handling the quantization step is clever and allows for gradient-based optimization. The mathematical derivation linking the approach to variational autoencoders is insightful.
* **Compelling Experimental Validation:** The extensive experimental evaluation on the Kodak dataset and other standard image sets demonstrates consistent improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000, particularly in visual quality as assessed by perceptual metrics (MS-SSIM).  The inclusion of rate-distortion curves and qualitative comparisons enhances the credibility of the results. The availability of compressed images online is a commendable practice.
* **Thorough Analysis & Discussion:** The paper provides a detailed analysis of the method, including a discussion of its relationship to existing approaches and potential limitations. The acknowledgment of differences between the relaxed optimization and the discrete problem is important.  The discussion of the GDN parameters and their effect on performance adds depth to the work.



**4. Additional Feedback (for improvement):**

* **Computational Cost:** While the paper mentions the high computational cost of training, providing more specific details on the resources required (e.g., GPUs, training time) would be helpful.
* **Ablation Studies:** Conducting ablation studies to assess the contribution of different components of the framework (e.g., GDN vs. other nonlinearities, specific network architecture choices) would strengthen the analysis.
* **Comparison with State-of-the-Art Learned Compression:**  The comparison is mainly with JPEG and JPEG2000. Including a comparison with other recent learned image compression methods (e.g. those employing VQ-VAE, or similar architectures) would provide a more comprehensive benchmark.
* **Entropy Code Details:** While the CABAC implementation is mentioned, providing more details about the context modeling used within the CABAC engine, and an analysis of its efficiency compared to other entropy coding methods, could be insightful.  It would also be useful to state explicitly the bit depth of the images used in evaluation.




**Questions for the Authors:**

1. **Regarding computational cost:** What was the total training time for the models presented in the paper, and what hardware was used (e.g. number of GPUs, memory)?
2. **Regarding parameter choices:** Were any specific criteria used to determine the number of filters and stages in the analysis and synthesis transforms?  Could you elaborate on the justification for these architectural choices?
3. **Regarding the continuous relaxation:**  How sensitive is the performance of the method to the width of the uniform noise used in the relaxation step?  Have you explored different noise distributions?
4. **Regarding generalization:** Do you observe any systematic differences in performance across different image datasets (e.g., natural images vs. synthetic images)?
5. **Regarding perceptual metrics:** While MS-SSIM shows impressive improvements, could you provide a more in-depth analysis of the types of artifacts reduced by your method compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000? Are there specific visual features that your method excels at preserving?



In conclusion, this is a well-executed and impactful piece of research that pushes the boundaries of image compression. The combination of theoretical grounding, innovative methodology, and strong experimental validation makes it a valuable contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points raised above.
