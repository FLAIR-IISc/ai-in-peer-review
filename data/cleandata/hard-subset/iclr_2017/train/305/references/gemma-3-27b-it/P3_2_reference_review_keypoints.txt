- Proposes an end-to-end image compression framework using cascaded GDN transforms with joint analysis/synthesis optimization and continuous relaxation for quantization.
- Shows improved rate-distortion and perceptual quality (MS-SSIM) over JPEG and JPEG 2000, with visual examples and online compressed images.
- Connects the proposed method theoretically to variational autoencoders, providing a principled grounding.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions due to solid technical contribution and convincing results.
- Strong motivation and clear problem formulation addressing limitations of traditional transform coding and joint rate-distortion optimization.
- Novel technical contributions: cascaded GDN, joint optimization with entropy coding, and a continuous relaxation for quantization enabling gradient-based training.
- Compelling experimental validation with rate-distortion curves, qualitative comparisons, and evaluations on Kodak and other datasets.
- Thorough analysis including discussion of limitations, relationship to prior work, and effects of GDN parameters.
- Request for more specific details on computational cost (training time, GPU resources, memory).
- Suggestion to include ablation studies to isolate contributions of GDN, nonlinearities, and architectural choices.
- Recommend comparisons with recent learned compression methods (e.g., VQ-VAE and similar learned models) for a more comprehensive benchmark.
- Ask for more details on entropy coding: CABAC context modeling, efficiency versus other entropy coders, and the image bit depth used.
- Questions for authors: total training time and hardware used.
- Questions for authors: rationale for number of filters and stages in analysis/synthesis transforms.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to the width/distribution of uniform noise in the continuous relaxation.
- Questions for authors: generalization across different image datasets (natural vs. synthetic).
- Questions for authors: deeper analysis of perceptual artifact reduction and specific visual features preserved compared to JPEG/JPEG2000.
