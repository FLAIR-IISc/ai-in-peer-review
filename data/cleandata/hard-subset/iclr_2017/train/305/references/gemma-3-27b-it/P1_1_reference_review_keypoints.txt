- Proposes end-to-end optimized image compression using cascaded nonlinear transforms (GDN) with continuous relaxation through quantization and a practical entropy coder, yielding visually appealing results competitive with JPEG/JPEG 2000.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; novel, well-motivated approach with strong experimental support.

- Novelty & motivation: End-to-end paradigm and neuroscience-inspired GDN are compelling and well-connected to prior work.

- Technical soundness: Rigorous formulation; continuous relaxation for quantization is clever and relation to VAEs is well-explained.

- Empirical validation: Extensive experiments on Kodak and ImageNet, use of MS-SSIM and PSNR, and actual bitrate reporting with CABAC strengthen claims.

- Presentation: Generally clear writing with helpful figures and diagrams.

- Computational cost: Needs more detail on training and encoding/decoding computational requirements and comparison to JPEG/JPEG 2000.

- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Request ablation studies or discussion on sensitivity to channels, filter sizes, learning rate, etc.

- Generalization: Ask for discussion of limits when applied to very different image types (medical, satellite).

- Entropy coding details: Request more specifics on CABAC context modeling (e.g., context depth) to better interpret bitrates.

- Figure 8 clarification: Grayscale transform architecture in appendix needs a clearer visual representation.

- Questions for authors: Have you explored simpler nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU) and their trade-offs in performance vs. complexity?

- Questions for authors: Please explain why GDN/IGDN are more efficient beyond requiring fewer stages—any theoretical insight into representational power?

- Questions for authors: How sensitive is optimization to the sampling interval in the piecewise linear approximation of marginals?

- Questions for authors: Provide more detail on ImageNet preprocessing (random downsampling/cropping) for reproducibility.

- Questions for authors: What avenues would you pursue to improve the currently rudimentary entropy coding relative to JPEG 2000?
