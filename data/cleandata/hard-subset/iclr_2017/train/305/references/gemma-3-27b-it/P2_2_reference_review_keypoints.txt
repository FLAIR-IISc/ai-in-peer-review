- Presents an end-to-end optimized image compression framework using cascaded nonlinear transforms (GDN) that jointly optimizes analysis, synthesis, and entropy coding for rate–distortion.
- Uses a continuous relaxation via additive noise to enable gradient-based optimization through quantization.
- Demonstrates competitive or superior rate–distortion performance and visibly improved image quality versus JPEG and JPEG 2000 on standard datasets.
- Establishes a theoretical connection to variational autoencoders, grounding the approach in generative-model theory.
- Decision: accept with minor revisions.
- Novelty/motivation: clear departure from traditional pipelines with well-justified use of GDN (Gaussianization and biological plausibility).
- Technical soundness: continuous relaxation is a key, well-considered contribution; network architecture, optimization, and entropy coding are thoroughly described.
- Empirical validation: convincing quantitative and qualitative results, with online examples and empirical verification of the relaxation’s accuracy.
- Theoretical contribution: linking to VAEs situates the work within broader research and suggests future directions.
- Request to report concrete computational cost: provide training time/resources and ways to reduce cost.
- Suggests an ablation study comparing GDN to alternative nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU) to strengthen claims.
- Requests expanded details on CABAC implementation, specifically context modeling and operating range.
- Asks for discussion of generalization limits to other image types (e.g., medical or satellite imagery).
- Question on sensitivity to the persistent positive bias in the relaxed entropy estimate and any mitigation during optimization.
- Question on robustness of the entropy code to out-of-distribution data and safeguards against invalid/negative entropy.
- Request for breakdown of training time by stage (forward, backward, entropy optimization) and note on essential hardware acceleration (GPUs).
- Request for a more granular analysis of when the method outperforms or underperforms JPEG/JPEG 2000 across image types and bitrates.
- Overall conclusion: well-written, technically sound, promising work deserving acceptance with minor revisions.
