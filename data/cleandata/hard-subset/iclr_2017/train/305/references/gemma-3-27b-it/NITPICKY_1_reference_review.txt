This paper presents a novel end-to-end optimized image compression framework based on cascaded nonlinear transformations, specifically utilizing Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN). The authors aim to overcome limitations of traditional transform coding by jointly optimizing the analysis and synthesis transforms directly for rate-distortion performance, moving beyond separate optimization of each component. They leverage a continuous relaxation of the quantization step to enable gradient-based optimization and report results using actual bit rates, demonstrating a complete lossy compression method. The work builds upon previous research by the authors exploring perceptual image compression and extends it to MSE optimization, enabling comparison with standard methods.

**Reject.** While the paper presents an interesting approach and demonstrates improved visual quality compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000, the sheer volume of minor flaws and inconsistencies, coupled with a lack of rigorous comparative analysis, leads me to recommend rejection. The paper feels prematurely submitted, lacking the polish expected for ICLR.

The primary concerns stem from inconsistencies in mathematical notation and presentation, a lack of clarity in experimental details, and a tendency towards hand-wavy explanations rather than precise definitions. For instance, the transition between equations (9) and (10) is glossed over; while conceptually understandable, a more explicit derivation or justification is needed. Similarly, the claim regarding the equivalence to variational autoencoders, while interesting, requires significantly more detailed justification given the differences outlined in the text.  The reliance on phrases like "relatively small" or "substantially improved" without quantifiable evidence weakens the claims. The repeated citation of arXiv preprints as definitive sources is also problematic.

Specifically:

* **Notation:** The use of multiple definitions for 'i' and 'k' within the same equation (e.g., indexing channels and stages) is confusing. The subscript notation for down/upsampling factors (sk, ŝk) is unclear and could benefit from more descriptive naming.
* **Experimental Details:** The description of the ImageNet dataset preprocessing is vague. The specifics of the randomized downsampling and cropping need to be clarified—what range of downsampling factors was used? What is the distribution of cropping positions? The justification for increasing network capacity for higher lambda values is weak; a more systematic exploration of architecture scaling would be beneficial. The precise parameters for the CABAC implementation, beyond stating it's based on Marpe et al. (2003), are missing. How were the initial probability models derived?
* **Loss Function:** The claim of using a "non-parametric" representation of the marginals pỹi is misleading. Piecewise linear functions are *parametric*, albeit with a potentially large number of parameters. This needs to be stated accurately. The impact of the sampling interval on model error needs to be quantified, not just asserted.
* **Comparison to VAEs:** The discussion of the relationship to variational autoencoders is superficial. The stated correspondence relies on specific choices (Gaussian distortion, etc.). A more rigorous derivation and discussion of the limitations would be necessary.
* **Figures:** Several figures lack adequate captions or labeling, making them difficult to interpret (e.g., Figure 9). Figure 4 lacks units on the axes.
* **Typos/Grammatical Errors:** Numerous minor typos and grammatical errors throughout the paper detract from its overall quality.

**Additional Feedback:**

*   **Ablation Studies:** Conduct ablation studies to assess the contribution of each component of the proposed framework (GDN, cascaded structure, continuous relaxation).
*   **Perceptual Metric:** While MSE optimization is used for comparability, explore optimizing directly with a perceptual metric (as in previous work) and compare the results.
*   **Computational Cost:** Provide a more detailed analysis of the computational cost of training and encoding/decoding.
*   **Generalization:** Investigate the generalization performance of the model on datasets beyond Kodak and ImageNet.
*   **Statistical Significance:** Report statistical significance tests for all quantitative comparisons.

**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the randomized downsampling and cropping procedures used during ImageNet preprocessing?
2.  What is the justification for using a piecewise linear approximation of the marginal densities pỹi, and how was the number of sampling points determined?
3.  Can you provide a more rigorous derivation of the equivalence between your proposed framework and variational autoencoders, clearly outlining the assumptions and limitations?
4.  What is the computational cost (training time, encoding/decoding time) of your method compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000?
5.  What specific implementation details of the CABAC entropy code were used? Specifically, how were the initial probability models derived and updated?
