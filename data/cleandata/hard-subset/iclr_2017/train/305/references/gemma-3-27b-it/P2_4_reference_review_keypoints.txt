- Presents an end-to-end optimized image compression framework using cascaded nonlinear transforms (GDN) with continuous relaxation of quantization for gradient-based optimization.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Clearly motivates joint optimization of analysis/synthesis transforms to improve rate-distortion over traditional separate optimization.  
- Technically sound: biologically inspired GDN, continuous relaxation of quantization, and useful connection to variational autoencoders.  
- Empirically strong: outperforms JPEG and JPEG 2000 in rate-distortion and perceptual quality, with actual bitrates reported and ablation insights.  
- Good transparency: provides links to per-image results and an appendix with architectural/optimization details.  
- Writing and presentation are generally clear.  
- Requested more detail on computational cost (training time, GPU memory) due to model complexity.  
- Asked to discuss generalization/robustness, especially to images outside the training distribution.  
- Suggests comparing to other learned compression methods (e.g., VQ-VAE) beyond JPEG/JPEG 2000.  
- Recommends improving figure clarity and resolution, especially in the appendix.  
- Questions: whether perceptual metrics were used instead of MSE and related challenges.  
- Questions: rationale or tuning for using 128 filters in the first stage.  
- Questions: potential for hardware acceleration of GDN/IGDN for practical deployment.  
- Questions: timeline for open-sourcing the code to aid reproducibility.
