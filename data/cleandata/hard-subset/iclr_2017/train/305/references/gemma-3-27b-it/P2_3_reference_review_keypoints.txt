- Summary: Proposes end-to-end optimized image compression using cascaded nonlinear transforms (GDN), continuous relaxation of quantization, joint rate–distortion optimization, and links to variational autoencoders; reports improved visual quality vs. JPEG/JPEG2000.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Novelty & motivation: Fresh, well-motivated approach addressing limits of hand-engineered transforms via biologically-inspired nonlinearities and joint optimization.
- Technical soundness: Solid methodology—continuous relaxation for quantization, clear VAE connection, and detailed implementation choices.
- Empirical validation: Strong results—better visual quality and MS-SSIM, competitive rate–distortion; code and test images provided for reproducibility.
- Clarity of presentation: Well-written with helpful diagrams and equations.
- Computational cost: Needs more detailed reporting of training resources, time, and GPU/memory requirements.
- Ablation studies: Recommend experiments to isolate contributions (e.g., GDN vs other nonlinearities, cascade depth).
- Perceptual metric justification: Suggest exploring optimization with perceptual metrics rather than only MSE.
- Entropy coding details: Request deeper discussion of CABAC implementation and comparison to state-of-the-art entropy coders.
- Q1 (GDN parameters): Did you try other parameterizations/constraints for GDN and what were the effects?
- Q2 (cascade sensitivity): How sensitive are results to number of stages and what's the performance vs. complexity trade-off?
- Q3 (continuous relaxation for supervision): How would the relaxation be applied to tasks like object recognition and what challenges are expected?
- Q4 (entropy coding impact): Would more advanced entropy coding notably change results, or is the main gain from optimized nonlinear transforms?
