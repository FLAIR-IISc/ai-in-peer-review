## ICLR 2024 Review: END-TO-END OPTIMIZED IMAGE COMPRESSION

**1. Summary:**

This paper presents a novel end-to-end optimized image compression framework utilizing cascaded nonlinear transforms, specifically employing Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN). The authors move away from the traditional, modular approach of transform coding (transform, quantizer, entropy code) and jointly optimize these components directly for rate-distortion trade-offs. They cleverly address the non-differentiability introduced by quantization via a continuous relaxation using additive uniform noise, enabling stochastic gradient descent. Importantly, they evaluate performance using actual bit rates and demonstrate improved visual quality compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000, despite optimizing for MSE. The work builds upon their previous successes with perceptual metrics and extends the approach to color images and complete bit-rate evaluations.

**2. Decision:**

**Accept (with minor revisions)**. This paper presents a significant contribution to the field of image compression. The end-to-end optimization approach is innovative and yields promising results, demonstrating a clear advantage in visual quality over established methods. The theoretical grounding and experimental validation are robust, and the connection to variational autoencoders is insightful. 

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

* **Novelty & Significance:** The end-to-end optimization framework is a noteworthy departure from traditional approaches. The ability to learn the transform, quantization, and entropy coding jointly, guided by a rate-distortion objective, is a significant advance.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The reported results consistently demonstrate superior performance in terms of perceptual quality (MS-SSIM) and competitive rate-distortion performance compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000.  The extensive image set and the availability of results online are commendable. The qualitative examples provided are compelling.
* **Sound Theoretical Foundation:** The authors provide a clear and well-motivated theoretical basis for their approach. The continuous relaxation of the quantization step is a clever solution to the gradient problem, and the connection to variational autoencoders offers valuable insight.
* **Thorough Evaluation:** The authors are careful to compare against established methods using a standard benchmark dataset (Kodak) and a diverse set of other images. Their use of actual bit rates instead of entropy estimates is a crucial step towards practical relevance.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and logically organized.  The appendix provides valuable details about the network architecture and optimization process.



**4. Additional Feedback (for improvement):**

* **Computational Cost:** While the paper acknowledges the high computational cost of training, a more detailed discussion of training time and resources would be beneficial. It would be helpful to indicate the scale of computational resources required to reproduce these results.
* **Scalability:** The current work focuses on relatively small image sizes (256x256).  Investigating the scalability of this approach to higher resolutions would be valuable. Do the benefits persist as image size increases?
* **Ablation Studies:** A more detailed ablation study, exploring the impact of different GDN parameters, network architectures, and optimization strategies, could provide further insights into the effectiveness of the proposed approach. For instance, comparing results with ReLU or Sigmoid activations more systematically.
* **Entropy Coding Details:** The entropy coding scheme described in the appendix is relatively simple. While sufficient for demonstrating the core concept, exploring more sophisticated entropy coding techniques might yield further improvements in compression performance.
* **Perceptual Metric Selection:** While MS-SSIM is used effectively, briefly discussing the limitations of this metric, and the potential benefits of incorporating other perceptual metrics, could add nuance.

**5. Questions for the Authors:**

* **Regarding the choice of GDN:** You state GDN is more efficient than ReLU in terms of parameter efficiency. Can you quantify this efficiency (e.g., number of parameters needed to achieve comparable performance)? Were other normalization layers (e.g., batch normalization) considered and why were they rejected?
* **Regarding Scalability:** Have you explored the performance of this method on higher resolution images (e.g., 1024x1024)? If so, what were the results? If not, are there any anticipated challenges in scaling this approach to higher resolutions?
* **Regarding the continuous relaxation:** The use of uniform noise for the continuous relaxation is a pragmatic choice.  Have you explored other noise distributions (e.g., Gaussian) and what were the impacts on training and final performance?
* **Regarding Entropy Coding:** The current entropy coding scheme leverages spatial context to some degree, but is less sophisticated than those used in modern codecs.  What is the potential gain in compression ratio by replacing your entropy coder with a state-of-the-art one (e.g., a CABAC implementation from JPEG2000)?  How much would this add to the complexity of the system?




Overall, this is a strong paper that presents a compelling and innovative approach to image compression. The minor revisions suggested above would further strengthen the work and enhance its impact. I strongly recommend acceptance.
