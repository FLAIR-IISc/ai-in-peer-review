- Proposes end-to-end optimized image compression using cascaded nonlinear transforms with GDN.  
- Uses additive-noise continuous relaxation to enable gradient-based training through quantization.  
- Jointly optimizes analysis, synthesis, and entropy coding for actual rate-distortion performance.  
- Evaluates using real bitrates and shows visually superior results and improved MS-SSIM vs JPEG/JPEG 2000.  
- Draws connections to variational autoencoders, providing theoretical grounding.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Novelty: addresses suboptimal separate optimization in traditional codecs; GDN motivated by higher-order dependencies and biological vision.  
- Technical soundness: continuous relaxation is effective; transforms and entropy coding are well explained.  
- Empirical validation: convincing results on a large test set, images available online, thorough comparisons to established codecs.  
- Clarity: generally well-written with helpful diagrams (notably Figure 1).  
- Request more detail on computational cost (training time, hardware) for reproducibility.  
- Recommend an ablation study to quantify contributions of components (e.g., GDN vs ReLU, cascaded structure).  
- Suggest comparing/discussing recent learned codecs (e.g., VQ‑VAE, generative-model-based methods) and methodological differences.  
- Ask for deeper analysis of rate–distortion curves and how the method reshapes them versus JPEG/JPEG 2000.  
- Question: rationale and sensitivity for using 10 sampling points in the piecewise-linear marginal approximation.  
- Question: effect of the slight positive bias in the relaxed entropy estimate on lambda selection and possible compensation.  
- Question: could adaptive parameter selection or online learning during encoding yield benefits?  
- Question: any experiments or insights on using perceptual loss instead of MSE during training?
