- Proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression framework using cascaded nonlinear transforms (GDN).
- Jointly optimizes analysis and synthesis transforms for rate–distortion performance.
- Handles quantization non-differentiability via continuous relaxation with additive noise.
- Demonstrates competitive results versus JPEG and JPEG 2000 with superior perceptual quality.
- Draws a connection between the method and variational autoencoders for theoretical grounding.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Novelty: significant departure from traditional separately optimized compression pipelines.
- Technical soundness: GDN and noise-relaxation approach are well-justified.
- Empirical evidence: quantitative (MSE, MS-SSIM, PSNR) and visual examples support improved perceptual quality.
- Reproducibility: test images and code are made available online.
- Presentation: generally well-written and logically organized despite complexity.
- Computational cost: training cost acknowledged but lacks detailed training time and hardware requirements.
- Encoding/decoding speed: comparison with existing codecs is missing and would be valuable.
- Generalization: training limited to ImageNet; evaluation on other datasets is needed to assess robustness.
- Ablation studies: more thorough exploration of nonlinearities, stages, filter sizes, and GDN parameters is needed.
- Entropy coding: description is brief and needs more implementation and performance details versus state-of-the-art.
- Question: request for specific training time and memory requirements on given hardware.
- Question: ask how the method scales to higher-resolution images and possible mitigation (e.g., patch-based processing).
- Question: inquire about sensitivity to the λ parameter and ways to select it automatically.
- Question: ask whether training with other perceptual loss functions beyond MSE/MS-SSIM was explored and their effects.
