- Presents an end-to-end learned image compression framework using cascaded convolutions and GDN with joint optimization of analysis and synthesis transforms.  
- Uses a continuous relaxation of quantization during training and reports actual bitrates for evaluation.  
- Demonstrates superior perceptual quality and rate-distortion performance compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Novelty: Tackles joint rate–distortion optimization with a fresh, biologically inspired, deep-learning approach.  
- Technical soundness: GDN and the quantization relaxation are well-motivated; links to VAEs clarify the method.  
- Empirical validation: Rigorous comparisons, PSNR/MS-SSIM gains, subjective examples, actual bitrate reporting, and ablation studies support claims.  
- Presentation: Well-written, detailed architecture and optimization description allowing reproducibility.  
- Computational cost: Paper notes high training cost but lacks detailed training time, hardware, and optimization strategies.  
- Generalizability: Evaluation focuses on one architecture; sensitivity to depth, filter sizes, and non-natural image domains is not explored.  
- Entropy coding: Appendix covers entropy coding but needs more detail on context modeling and adaptive arithmetic coding.  
- Relation to learned compression: Should compare/contrast more explicitly with other learned autoencoder-based compression methods.  
- Question on quantization relaxation: Request quantification of bias across bitrates/images and comparison to alternatives (e.g., Gumbel-Softmax).  
- Question on GDN: Ask which properties of GDN (Gaussianization, nonlinearity, etc.) drive its efficiency for compression.  
- Question on perceptual metrics: Asks about challenges and any preliminary results when optimizing directly for perceptual metrics.  
- Future work: Suggests exploring combinations with vector quantization or transform coding to further improve performance.
