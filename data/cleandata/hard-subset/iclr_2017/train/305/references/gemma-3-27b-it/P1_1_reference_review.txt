## Review of "END-TO-END OPTIMIZED IMAGE COMPRESSION"

**1. Summary:**

This paper presents a novel end-to-end optimized image compression framework based on cascaded nonlinear transformations, specifically using Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN). The authors move beyond traditional transform coding's separate optimization of transform, quantizer, and entropy code. They optimize directly for rate-distortion, employing a continuous relaxation to enable gradient-based optimization through the quantization step. Importantly, they demonstrate practical performance with an actual entropy code and report bitrates, showcasing a viable lossy compression method. The work builds upon previous research demonstrating the benefits of perceptual optimization and introduces a method competitive with JPEG and JPEG 2000, while demonstrably producing more visually appealing results.

**2. Decision:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. The paper tackles a significant problem in a novel and compelling way. The approach is well-motivated, and the experimental results clearly support the claims of improved visual quality compared to established methods. The thoroughness of the experimental validation, including comparisons with standard codecs and visual examples, is impressive.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

* **Novelty and Motivation:** The end-to-end optimization paradigm is a clear advancement over traditional image compression pipelines.  The motivation, rooted in neuroscience and perceptual modeling, is well-articulated and provides a strong rationale for using GDN. The authors effectively connect their work to existing literature in compression, generative models (VAEs), and perceptual coding.
* **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical formulation is rigorous, and the continuous relaxation approach to address the non-differentiability of quantization is clever and well-justified.  The explanation of the relationship to variational autoencoders provides valuable context and clarifies the underlying principles.
* **Empirical Validation:** The extensive experimental evaluation is a major strength. Comparing against JPEG and JPEG 2000 on standard datasets (Kodak, ImageNet) and a diverse set of images provides convincing evidence of the method's effectiveness. The inclusion of MS-SSIM as a perceptual metric alongside PSNR is crucial and reinforces the subjective improvements observed in the visual examples. The use of a practical entropy code (CABAC) and reporting actual bitrates significantly strengthens the claim of a complete compression method.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the complex concepts involved. The figures and diagrams are helpful in understanding the architecture and optimization process.



**4. Additional Feedback (for improvement):**

* **Computational Cost:** While the paper acknowledges high training costs, a more detailed discussion of the computational requirements for *both* training and compression/decompression would be beneficial. A comparison to the complexity of JPEG/JPEG 2000 encoding/decoding would be particularly insightful.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** A brief discussion of the sensitivity of performance to hyperparameters (e.g., number of channels, filter sizes, learning rate) would be helpful.  Were ablation studies performed to understand the contribution of different components of the architecture?
* **Generalization:** The method appears to perform well on the tested datasets. However, discussing potential limitations regarding generalization to significantly different types of images (e.g., medical images, satellite imagery) would strengthen the paper.
* **Entropy Coding Details:** While the use of CABAC is appreciated, providing more specific details on the context modeling used within the entropy coder (e.g. context depth) would allow for a better understanding of the bit rate achieved.
* **Figure 8 Clarification:** In the appendix, figure 8 showing the grayscale transform architecture is a bit difficult to parse. A clearer visual representation would be appreciated.

**5. Questions for the Authors:**

*  The paper mentions the potential for simplifying the nonlinearities (e.g., using ReLU). Have you explored this more extensively? If so, what was the trade-off between performance and computational complexity?
* You state that GDN/IGDN transforms are more efficient for compression. Could you elaborate on *why* this is the case, beyond simply stating that they require fewer stages? Are there theoretical reasons or insights into their representational power?
* The continuous relaxation approach introduces approximations. How sensitive is the optimization to the sampling interval used in the piecewise linear approximation of the marginal distributions? Have you investigated the effect of varying this parameter?
* Could you briefly describe the preprocessing steps applied to the ImageNet training set in more detail, especially the randomization of downsampling and cropping? This would aid reproducibility.
* In the entropy coding section, you mention that the coding scheme is rudimentary compared to JPEG 2000.  What avenues would you explore to improve the entropy coding aspect of your framework for future work?



Overall, this is a strong and innovative paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of image compression.  The clear presentation, thorough experimental evaluation, and compelling results justify its acceptance. Addressing the suggestions above would further enhance the quality and impact of the work.
