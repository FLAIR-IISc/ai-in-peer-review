- Proposes an end-to-end learned image compression framework optimizing nonlinear transform, quantization, and entropy coding jointly.  
- Uses cascaded convolutions with GDN and a continuous relaxation to enable gradient-based training through quantization.  
- Formulates compression as rate–distortion optimization and implements a practical entropy coder to report real bitrates.  
- Draws a theoretical connection between the method and variational autoencoders (VAEs).  
- Recommendation: Accept — judged a significant, well-motivated, and empirically strong contribution.  
- Novelty: Moves beyond traditional transform coding and is inspired by biological visual processing (GDN).  
- Technical soundness: Continuous relaxation and GDN are well-explained; limitations are acknowledged and addressed.  
- Empirical validation: Strong visual results and bitrate comparisons versus JPEG and JPEG2000 on standard datasets.  
- Practicality: Reporting actual bitrates and providing test images strengthens the work’s applicability.  
- Thoroughness: Paper includes discussions on biases, relation to generative models, and detailed implementation notes.  
- Request more detail on inference computational complexity and how transforms scale with image size.  
- Suggests ablation studies to isolate contributions of components like GDN versus ReLU.  
- Recommends discussing challenges and choices for perceptual metrics beyond MSE for color images.  
- Asks about applicability and extensions to other data types (e.g., video, audio).  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to network initialization, choice and sensitivity of sampling points in relaxed density, encoding/decoding time comparisons on CPU, and evidence on efficiency of GDN versus other activations.
