## Review of "END-TO-END OPTIMIZED IMAGE COMPRESSION"

**1. Summary:**

This paper presents a novel end-to-end optimized image compression framework based on cascaded nonlinear transformations, specifically utilizing Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN). The authors move beyond traditional transform coding by jointly optimizing the analysis and synthesis transforms directly for rate-distortion performance, using stochastic gradient descent and a continuous relaxation of the quantization process. The approach is motivated by the limitations of hand-engineered transforms and the benefits of biologically-inspired nonlinearities in representing image data. They demonstrate improved visual quality and competitive rate-distortion performance compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000, despite optimizing for MSE rather than perceptual metrics. They also establish a compelling connection between their approach and variational autoencoders.

**2. Decision:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. The paper presents a significant contribution to the field of image compression, offering a well-motivated and empirically validated alternative to established methods. The results are compelling, particularly the improved visual quality, and the theoretical connection to variational autoencoders provides valuable insight.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

* **Novelty and Motivation:** The paper tackles a long-standing problem – efficient image compression – with a fresh perspective. The motivation for end-to-end optimization and the use of nonlinear transformations, inspired by biological vision, are clearly articulated and grounded in prior work. The limitations of traditional, separate optimization of transform, quantization, and entropy coding are convincingly addressed.
* **Technical Soundness:** The proposed framework is technically sound and carefully constructed. The use of a continuous relaxation for optimization is a clever way to circumvent the non-differentiability of the quantization step. The explanation of the relationship to variational autoencoders is insightful and highlights the broader relevance of the work.  The implementation details, particularly regarding the entropy coding and network architecture, are sufficiently detailed.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results are strong.  The consistent improvement in visual quality, as demonstrated by the example images and MS-SSIM metrics, is a key strength. While PSNR results are not uniformly better, the overall rate-distortion performance is competitive, and the qualitative gains are substantial.  The availability of the test images and code online facilitates reproducibility.  The careful comparison to JPEG and JPEG 2000, and the consideration of potential biases in evaluation metrics, add to the credibility of the results.
* **Clarity of Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the key concepts and technical details. The inclusion of diagrams and equations helps to clarify the method.

**4. Additional Feedback (For Improvement):**

* **Computational Cost:** While the paper mentions the high computational cost of training, a more detailed discussion of the resources required (e.g., training time, GPU memory) would be beneficial. This will help potential adopters assess the practicality of the approach.
* **Ablation Studies:** Including ablation studies to assess the contribution of different components of the framework (e.g., GDN vs. other nonlinearities, cascade depth) would strengthen the analysis and provide further insights.
* **Perceptual Metric Justification:** While the authors rightly point out the limitations of MSE, exploring optimization *with* a perceptual metric, even briefly, would be a worthwhile extension and address a comment in the discussion.
* **Entropy Coding Details:** A more in-depth discussion of the CABAC implementation, especially regarding its limitations compared to state-of-the-art entropy coders used in JPEG 2000 and modern codecs, would be helpful.

**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  Regarding the GDN parameters, you mention fixing exponents and enforcing symmetry.  Did you explore other parameterizations or constraints, and if so, what was the impact on performance?
2.  The paper highlights the benefits of the cascaded GDN architecture. Have you investigated the sensitivity of the results to the number of stages, and what is the trade-off between performance and computational complexity as the number of stages increases?
3.  You mention the potential for using the continuous relaxation as a form of regularization for supervised learning tasks. Could you elaborate on how you envision applying this concept to object recognition, and what specific challenges you anticipate?
4.  The entropy coding seems to be a key component. Would a more advanced entropy coding scheme significantly alter the results, or is the core advantage primarily derived from the optimized nonlinear transforms?
