- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — significant advance in image compression.  
- Core contribution: End-to-end optimized image compression framework using cascaded GDN nonlinear transforms.  
- Key technical idea: Continuous relaxation of quantization enables gradient-based joint optimization of transform, quantizer, and entropy code.  
- Practicality: Demonstrates feasibility with an actual entropy coder and reports achievable bitrates.  
- Results: Improved visual/perceptual quality over JPEG and JPEG 2000, especially under MS-SSIM, even when trained with MSE.  
- Novelty/significance: Holistic optimization departs from conventional separate-component design, yielding performance gains.  
- Theoretical soundness: Well-reasoned formulation with insightful connection to variational autoencoders.  
- Empirical validation: Extensive experiments on Kodak and other datasets convincingly support claims.  
- Reproducibility: Clear presentation and detailed appendix with architecture and optimization details.  
- Computational cost concern: More detail needed on training time, hardware, and strategies to reduce computational burden.  
- Generalization: Recommend testing on more diverse domains (medical, satellite) to demonstrate robustness.  
- Complexity comparison: Provide computational complexity and runtime comparison of encoding/decoding vs JPEG/JPEG 2000.  
- Ablation study: Request systematic evaluation of contributions from GDN, cascade structure, and end-to-end training.  
- Loss choice discussion: Need deeper discussion on limitations of MSE and challenges/opportunities for perceptual losses.  
- Question — training stability: Did training encounter stability issues (vanishing/exploding gradients) and how were they mitigated?  
- Question — entropy coding: Provide details on CABAC implementation, context modeling, and comparison to JPEG2000’s contexts.  
- Question — λ sensitivity: How sensitive is performance to the rate–distortion tradeoff parameter λ and is there a robust operating range?  
- Question — scalability: Feasibility of scaling to 4K/8K and potential optimizations (distributed training, model parallelism)?  
- Question — future work: Plans for alternative loss functions or regularizers beyond perceptual metrics to improve performance.  
- Overall conclusion: Well-executed, impactful work with minor clarifications and analyses recommended to strengthen practical applicability.
