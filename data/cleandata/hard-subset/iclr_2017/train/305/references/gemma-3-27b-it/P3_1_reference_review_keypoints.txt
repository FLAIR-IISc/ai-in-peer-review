- End-to-end optimized image compression framework using cascaded nonlinear transforms (GDN) with joint optimization of transform, quantizer, and entropy code.  
- Uses additive uniform noise as a continuous relaxation to make quantization differentiable for SGD.  
- Evaluates using actual bit rates and shows improved visual quality vs JPEG and JPEG 2000 despite MSE optimization.  
- Extends prior work to color images and full bit-rate evaluations; connects method to variational autoencoders.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — significant contribution with robust theory and experiments.  
- Novelty: Joint end-to-end optimization departs from traditional modular transform coding and is a notable advance.  
- Strong empirical results: Superior perceptual quality (MS-SSIM) and competitive rate-distortion on Kodak and diverse images; qualitative examples compelling.  
- Sound theoretical foundation: Continuous relaxation for quantization is clever; VAE connection is insightful.  
- Thorough evaluation: Uses standard benchmarks and actual bitrates; results available online.  
- Clear presentation: Well-written paper with useful appendix details on architecture and optimization.  
- Request for more details on computational cost and resources needed to reproduce training.  
- Scalability concern: Experiments limited to 256×256; need evaluation and discussion for higher resolutions.  
- Suggest stronger ablation studies on GDN parameters, network architectures, and comparisons with ReLU/Sigmoid.  
- Entropy coding is relatively simple; exploring more advanced coders could further improve compression.  
- Perceptual metric: MS-SSIM is fine but discuss its limitations and potential alternatives.  
- Questions for authors: quantify GDN parameter efficiency and rationale versus other normalizations; scalability to 1024×1024 and expected challenges; effects of other noise distributions for relaxation; potential gains and complexity from using state-of-the-art entropy coders.
