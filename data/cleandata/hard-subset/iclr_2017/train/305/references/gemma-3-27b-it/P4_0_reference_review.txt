This paper presents a novel end-to-end optimized image compression framework based on cascaded nonlinear transformations, specifically Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN). The core contribution is the joint optimization of analysis and synthesis transforms, coupled with an entropy code, directly for rate-distortion performance, utilizing a continuous relaxation to overcome the non-differentiability of the quantization step. The authors demonstrate improved visual quality and rate-distortion performance compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000 on standard image datasets.

**Reject** with a strong encouragement to revise and resubmit. While the ideas presented are compelling and the experimental results are promising, the paper suffers from a lack of clarity in crucial aspects of the methodology and insufficient detail to enable reproducibility. The sheer volume of technical details makes it difficult to discern the key innovations and assess their impact thoroughly.

The primary reason for rejection is the insufficient clarity and detail regarding the experimental setup and evaluation. While the authors mention using ImageNet for training and Kodak for testing, specific details about data preprocessing (beyond basic downsampling and cropping) and hyperparameter selection are missing. The “heuristic” for adapting the piecewise linear approximation of the marginals (Section 6.1) is vaguely described. Crucially, the implementation details of the entropy coding scheme (Section 6.2) are insufficient to understand how it compares to established methods like those in JPEG 2000; a claim is made that it’s a relatively small gain, but without more detail this is unconvincing. Finally, the assertion that all test images are available online doesn’t address the lack of code availability, which is essential for verification.

Secondly, the connection to variational autoencoders, although presented extensively, feels somewhat forced. While the mathematical formalism allows for an equivalence, the authors acknowledge the differences in application and objectives, rendering the detailed discussion less impactful. The emphasis on MSE as a distortion metric, despite acknowledging the potential benefits of perceptual metrics, also weakens the overall argument, as the primary claim is improved *visual* quality. The justification for fixing the GDN exponents is reasonable, but could be more explicit.

**Additional Feedback:**

*   **Clarity:** The paper is densely written and requires significant effort to follow. Restructuring the presentation, breaking down complex equations, and using more descriptive variable names would improve readability.
*   **Reproducibility:** Providing code or detailed pseudocode is critical for enabling independent verification of the results. The lack of this significantly limits the impact of the work.
*   **Ablation Studies:** Performing ablation studies to evaluate the contribution of individual components (e.g., GDN vs. other nonlinearities, the continuous relaxation) would strengthen the analysis.
*   **Statistical Significance:** Provide statistical significance tests to support claims of improvement over JPEG and JPEG 2000, especially regarding the MS-SSIM metric.
*   **Entropy Coding Details:** Elaborate on the choices made in the entropy coding scheme (CABAC-based) and provide a more detailed comparison with standard methods, including complexity analysis.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** Discuss the sensitivity of the results to key hyperparameters, such as the learning rate and the number of sampling points for the piecewise linear functions.
*   **Computational Cost:** Provide a more detailed analysis of the computational cost of training and compression/decompression.

**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  Could you provide more details on the specific data preprocessing steps applied to the ImageNet and Kodak datasets?
2.  Can you elaborate on the heuristic used to adapt the range of the piecewise linear approximation of the marginals?
3.  What is the computational complexity of your entropy coding scheme compared to the entropy coding used in JPEG 2000?
4.  Will you release the source code for this work to facilitate reproducibility?
5.  How sensitive are the results to the choice of hyperparameters, particularly the learning rate and the number of sampling points for the piecewise linear functions?
6.  What is the approximate training time for a single λ value, and the compression/decompression time for a typical image?
