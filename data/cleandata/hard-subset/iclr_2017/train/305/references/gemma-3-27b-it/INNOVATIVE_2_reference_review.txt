This paper introduces a novel end-to-end optimized image compression framework, moving beyond traditional transform coding’s separate optimization of transform, quantizer, and entropy code. The core idea – leveraging cascaded convolutions and generalized divisive normalization (GDN) jointly optimized for rate-distortion – is compelling and represents a significant departure from established techniques. The authors demonstrate promising results, achieving improved perceptual quality compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000, despite optimizing for MSE. The connection drawn to variational autoencoders provides a valuable theoretical perspective.

**Reject.** While the approach is highly innovative and the results promising, the paper feels incomplete and lacks sufficient rigor to warrant acceptance at this stage. The reliance on empirical evaluation without a deeper theoretical analysis of the advantages offered by the specific architectural choices and GDN is a significant weakness. 

The primary concern lies in the somewhat hand-wavy justification for architectural decisions. While the authors allude to the benefits of GDN and overcomplete representations inspired by visual neuroscience, the paper lacks a systematic exploration of alternative architectures or a detailed ablation study to isolate the contribution of each component. The claim of visual superiority is supported by example images, but lacks comprehensive quantitative perceptual metrics beyond MS-SSIM applied to a limited dataset. The comparison to JPEG and JPEG 2000, while visually striking, lacks statistical significance and is not robust to varying image content. The fact that performance varies substantially across images, as highlighted by the authors themselves in Section 4, is concerning and suggests the method isn’t universally superior.

Furthermore, the discussion of the relationship to variational autoencoders feels somewhat superficial. While the analogy is interesting, the authors acknowledge limitations when using perceptual metrics, and don’t fully explore the implications of these differences. The emphasis on relaxing the quantization step for optimization, while a pragmatic solution, requires more justification – why does this relaxation accurately reflect the discrete problem, and what are the potential biases introduced? The claim that the continuous relaxation provides a good approximation to the actual rate-distortion values feels somewhat under-substantiated beyond the provided figure 4, and requires further analysis. 

**Additional Feedback:**

*   **Ablation Studies:** Conduct comprehensive ablation studies to evaluate the impact of different components (number of stages, filter sizes, GDN vs. ReLU, etc.) on performance.
*   **Architecture Search:** Briefly discuss the rationale behind the specific network architecture (number of filters, downsampling factors) and explore the potential for automated architecture search.
*   **Perceptual Metrics:** Employ a wider range of perceptual metrics (e.g., NIQE, BRISQUE) and conduct statistical tests to demonstrate the significance of the improvements. Consider a user study to validate the subjective visual quality.
*   **Theoretical Analysis:** Provide a more rigorous theoretical justification for the choice of GDN and the relaxation of the quantization step.
*   **Generalization:** Test the method on a more diverse dataset of images and analyze its performance across different image types (e.g., natural images, medical images, synthetic images).
*   **Computational Cost:** Discuss the computational cost of training and encoding/decoding compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000.

**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  Beyond the visual examples, can you provide a more robust statistical analysis demonstrating the perceptual advantage of your method across a wider range of images?
2.  What specific architectural choices were critical to the performance of the model, and how were these choices made? Could you perform an ablation study to demonstrate the impact of each component?
3.  What is the theoretical justification for the choice of GDN over other non-linearities, specifically regarding its impact on the rate-distortion tradeoff?
4.  The relaxation of the quantization step using additive noise is crucial for optimization. How sensitive is the performance to the width of the noise distribution? Does this relaxation introduce any significant biases in the optimization process?
5.  What is the computational cost (training and inference) of the proposed method compared to standard compression algorithms like JPEG and JPEG 2000? How does this affect the practicality of the approach?
