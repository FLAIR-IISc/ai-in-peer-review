- Paper proposes end-to-end optimized image compression using cascaded nonlinear transforms with GDN, jointly optimizing transform, quantizer, and entropy code via SGD.
- Introduces a continuous relaxation of quantization with additive noise and demonstrates a practical entropy coding implementation.
- Shows visually superior results versus JPEG/JPEG2000 and competitive or better rate-distortion performance, especially in artifact reduction.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Novelty: End-to-end optimization and the continuous relaxation for quantization are significant departures from traditional pipelines.
- Empirical strength: Convincing quantitative and visual comparisons, MS-SSIM improvements, and evaluation across diverse images.
- Practicality: Demonstrated a feasible entropy coding scheme, strengthening applicability.
- Theory: Connection to variational autoencoders provides useful theoretical grounding and context.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with clear explanations of architecture, optimization, and entropy coding; appendix is informative.
- Request more discussion of computational cost (GPU hours, memory) for reproducibility and scalability.
- Ask for ablation studies comparing GDN to simpler nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU) and analyses of model complexity vs. performance.
- Suggest exploring adaptive entropy coding improvements, such as advanced context modeling within CABAC.
- Recommend more comprehensive statistical analysis of rate-distortion curves (average curves, confidence intervals).
- Suggest briefly discussing challenges and potential for extending the framework to video compression.
- Questions: quantify bias from the continuous relaxation at different bit rates and possible correction strategies during training.
- Questions: justify network architecture choices and report any hyperparameter optimization performed.
- Questions: whether perceptual metrics (MS-SSIM, LPIPS) were used in training and the impact on model capacity and results.
- Questions: provide detailed hardware and training time (per λ) to aid reproducibility.
- Questions: report sensitivity of performance to IGDN/IGDN inverse parameter choices and potential for further optimization.
