This paper presents a novel end-to-end optimized image compression framework based on cascaded nonlinear transformations, specifically employing Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN). The authors aim to jointly optimize the analysis and synthesis transforms for a given rate-distortion trade-off, addressing the limitations of traditional transform coding methods that optimize components separately. They leverage a continuous relaxation of the quantization step for gradient-based optimization and demonstrate improved visual quality compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000, despite using Mean Squared Error (MSE) as the training objective. The work builds upon previous research demonstrating the benefits of nonlinear transformations and perceptual optimization in image compression.

**Reject.** While the core idea of end-to-end optimized nonlinear compression is compelling and the presented results show promise, the paper suffers from a multitude of minor but significant flaws – in presentation, methodological rigor, and experimental detail – that collectively undermine confidence in the validity and reproducibility of the findings. The sheer number of these issues suggests a lack of careful attention to detail throughout the development and presentation of this work.

The primary issue lies in the lack of clarity and precision in several critical aspects. Firstly, the connection drawn to variational autoencoders (VAEs), while presented as insightful, feels contrived and ultimately doesn’t add substantial value. The authors acknowledge limitations of this analogy, yet dedicate considerable space to it. Secondly, the empirical evaluation, despite demonstrating visual improvements, lacks sufficient quantitative rigor. While perceptual metrics are reported, the comparison with JPEG and JPEG 2000 feels somewhat superficial, lacking a standardized testing protocol and detailed parameter settings for the baselines. The claim of outperforming JPEG and JPEG 2000 "for most images" is not adequately substantiated with a comprehensive analysis across the tested dataset. Furthermore, the entropy code implementation is described briefly and lacks sufficient detail regarding its implementation and potential impact on the overall results. The assertion that it is "simple" and its performance is "relatively small" begs for quantitative justification.

Beyond these major concerns, numerous minor issues contribute to a sense of incompleteness and imprecision. The explanation of the GDN and IGDN operations, while present, could benefit from more intuitive explanations and illustrative examples. The discussion of the optimization process lacks detail regarding learning rates, batch sizes, and convergence criteria. The network architecture, though described, would benefit from a clearer diagram. The appendix, while extensive, feels disorganized and contains a lot of supplemental information which is necessary to reproduce the results, but should be more tightly integrated into the main body of the text. The numerous references to figures throughout the text that were not present in the version of the paper I reviewed were distracting and frustrating. The citation style is inconsistent (e.g., sometimes year only, sometimes full author list).

**Additional Feedback (for potential revision, should the authors choose to resubmit):**

*   **Clarify the VAE connection:** Either strengthen the analogy with concrete examples and implications or remove it altogether.
*   **Provide a standardized evaluation protocol:** Detail the specific settings used for JPEG and JPEG 2000, and perform a statistically significant comparison across a diverse set of images. Include standard metrics like PSNR and SSIM for both methods in a tabular format.
*   **Provide Implementation Details for the Entropy Code:** Provide a detailed description of the CABAC implementation, including parameters used and any optimizations applied. Quantify its contribution to the overall compression performance.
*   **Improve clarity of GDN/IGDN explanation:** Add a visual illustration or a simpler explanation to aid understanding.
*   **Detail the Optimization Process:** Provide specific details on the optimization algorithm, learning rate schedule, batch size, and convergence criteria.
*   **Refine Network Architecture Description:** Include a clear diagram of the network architecture.
*   **Restructure the Appendix:** Integrate essential details into the main text and organize the appendix for improved readability.
*   **Ensure Consistency and Completeness:** Proofread carefully for inconsistencies in citation style and ensure all referenced figures are present.

**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  What specific modifications were made to the OpenJPEG implementation used for comparison? Were default settings used, or were parameters tuned for optimal performance?
2.  Can you provide a more detailed explanation of how the piecewise linear function used for approximating the marginal densities is updated during training? What is the sampling interval used, and how does it affect the accuracy of the approximation?
3.  What is the computational cost (in terms of FLOPs or runtime) of the encoding and decoding process compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000?
4.   Regarding the claims of perceptual improvements - did you conduct any subjective testing (e.g., user studies) to validate these findings, beyond the MS-SSIM metric?
5.   The figures referenced in the text were unavailable to me. Can you provide a complete version of the paper with all figures included?
