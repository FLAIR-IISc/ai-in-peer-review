## Review of "END-TO-END OPTIMIZED IMAGE COMPRESSION"

**1. Summary:**

This paper presents a novel end-to-end optimized image compression framework based on cascaded nonlinear transformations, specifically utilizing Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN). The authors move beyond traditional transform coding by jointly optimizing the analysis and synthesis transforms directly for rate-distortion trade-offs, using stochastic gradient descent. A key innovation is the use of a continuous relaxation of the quantization step to enable gradient-based optimization. They demonstrate superior performance compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000, particularly in terms of perceptual quality, while achieving competitive or better rate-distortion performance as measured by MSE and PSNR.  The authors also establish a connection between their framework and variational autoencoders, highlighting theoretical similarities.

**2. Decision:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. The paper presents a significant advance in image compression, offering a compelling alternative to traditional methods. The results are convincing, demonstrating improved perceptual quality and competitive rate-distortion performance. The theoretical connection to variational autoencoders provides valuable insight into the underlying principles.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

* **Novelty and Significance:** The end-to-end optimization approach, combined with the GDN transformation and continuous relaxation of quantization, is a novel contribution.  Moving beyond hand-engineered transforms to learned ones, optimized directly for compression, is a significant step forward.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper provides a thorough empirical evaluation on the Kodak dataset and several standard images. The visual comparisons clearly demonstrate the superior perceptual quality of the proposed method compared to JPEG and JPEG 2000, a claim supported by MS-SSIM results. The quantitative rate-distortion curves further corroborate the improved performance, particularly at lower bitrates.
* **Theoretical Grounding:** The authors thoughtfully connect their work to the established field of variational autoencoders, providing a theoretical framework and justification for their approach. This helps to position the work within the broader machine learning landscape.
* **Practical Considerations:** The inclusion of an actual entropy coding stage (CABAC) and reporting of actual bitrates, rather than just entropy estimates, demonstrates the feasibility of the method as a complete compression solution. 

**4. Additional Feedback (for improvement):**

* **Computational Cost:** While the paper acknowledges the high training cost, it would be beneficial to provide more details about the computational resources required (e.g., number of GPUs, training time) and potential avenues for reducing this cost.  Could techniques like knowledge distillation or transfer learning be explored?
* **Ablation Study:** A more detailed ablation study analyzing the contribution of different components (e.g., GDN, number of stages, architecture choices) would strengthen the paper.  What performance degradation is observed when using ReLU activations instead of GDN?
* **Comparison to State-of-the-Art Learned Compressors:** The paper primarily compares against JPEG and JPEG 2000.  A comparison to other recent learned image compression methods (e.g., those based on VQ-VAE, or other autoregressive models) would provide a more comprehensive benchmark.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** Discuss the sensitivity of the method to hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate, λ). A brief discussion on how these parameters were tuned and their impact on performance would be valuable.
* **Appendix Clarity:** While the appendix is thorough, some sections (particularly those describing the optimization details) could benefit from clearer explanations and more concise phrasing.

**5. Questions for the Authors:**

*  You mention the possibility of further improvements by optimizing with a perceptual metric. Have you explored this further, and if so, what challenges did you encounter?
*  The paper emphasizes the efficiency of GDN over pointwise nonlinearities. Is this efficiency maintained across different image datasets or network architectures?
*  Regarding the continuous relaxation of quantization, how sensitive is the performance to the width of the uniform noise distribution used during training?
*  In the context of the connection to variational autoencoders, the distortion metric used in your formulation directly influences the properties of the generative model. Could different distortion metrics (e.g., perceptual losses) lead to qualitatively different generative capabilities?
* Can you provide more detail on the limitations of your current entropy coding implementation (CABAC), and the potential gains achievable with more sophisticated entropy coding schemes?



Overall, this is a strong and well-written paper with significant potential to advance the field of image compression. The authors have successfully demonstrated a novel and effective approach to end-to-end optimization, resulting in compelling results.  Addressing the minor points above will further strengthen the paper and enhance its impact.
