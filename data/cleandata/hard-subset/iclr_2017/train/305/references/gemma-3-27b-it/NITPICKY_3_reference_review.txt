This paper presents a novel end-to-end optimized image compression framework based on cascaded nonlinear transforms, specifically employing Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN). The authors aim to overcome limitations of traditional transform coding by jointly optimizing the analysis and synthesis transforms directly for rate-distortion performance, using a continuous relaxation of the quantization step to enable gradient-based optimization. The work builds on prior research demonstrating improved perceptual quality with optimized nonlinear transforms and introduces a practical entropy coding scheme for evaluating actual bitrates.

**Reject.** While the core idea of end-to-end optimized nonlinear transform coding is compelling and the authors demonstrate improvements over JPEG and JPEG 2000, the paper suffers from a concerning number of methodological oversights, inconsistencies, and a lack of rigorous analysis that undermine confidence in the reported results. The numerous minor flaws, when taken together, suggest a lack of meticulousness that is unacceptable for a top-tier conference like ICLR.

The primary issues are as follows:

1. **Insufficient Ablation Studies:** The paper claims GDN is crucial, but lacks a comprehensive ablation study comparing performance with simpler nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU, sigmoid) *with comparable model capacity*. The authors mention preliminary tests hinting at GDN's efficiency but provide no quantitative data. This makes it impossible to assess whether the observed gains are genuinely attributable to GDN's specific properties or simply to the increased model complexity it introduces.  The statement that ReLU "generally requires a substantially larger number of model parameters/stages" is asserted without supporting evidence.

2. **Questionable Entropy Coding Implementation:** The description of the entropy coding scheme is vague and relies on an outdated CABAC framework. While the authors acknowledge it's likely sub-optimal, they don't quantify the potential performance loss compared to state-of-the-art entropy coders. The claim that actual bit rates are only “slightly larger than the entropy” feels unsubstantiated, given the described implementation. Furthermore, the inclusion of image size, color mode, and lambda in the bitstream *before* arithmetic coding bypasses the efficiency of the coder and should be clearly justified.

3. **Ambiguous Rate-Distortion Curve Evaluation:** The comparison strategy across images is poorly defined. The authors admit difficulties in combining rate-distortion curves and then proceed to offer a qualitative assessment. The reliance on visually selecting images with comparable bitrates is subjective and potentially misleading. The use of PSNR as a metric, alongside MS-SSIM, feels discordant given the focus on perceptual quality and the use of MSE during training; a better justification is needed.

4. **Inconsistent Optimization Details:** The optimization process, while described, lacks crucial details. The criteria for reducing the learning rate are vague (“stagnated improvement”), and the rationale behind specific parameter choices (e.g., the DCT parameterization) is not fully explained.  The handling of symmetry constraints on the GDN parameters appears ad-hoc.

5. **Minor Technical Inaccuracies and Formatting:** The equation numbering is inconsistent. Several statements lack sufficient mathematical rigor or justification. The appendix contains a significant amount of detail, suggesting a lack of conciseness in the main body of the paper.



**Additional Feedback (for potential revision, should the authors choose to resubmit):**

* **Rigorous Ablation Studies:** Conduct a thorough ablation study, systematically comparing GDN to other nonlinearities (ReLU, sigmoid, tanh) while carefully controlling for model capacity.
* **Entropy Coding Analysis:** Provide a more detailed analysis of the entropy coding scheme, including a comparison to more modern techniques and a quantification of its efficiency.  Justify the choice of CABAC, or explore alternative entropy coding methods.
* **Standardized Rate-Distortion Evaluation:** Define a clear and objective method for comparing rate-distortion performance across images. Consider using a Pareto front analysis to visualize the trade-offs.
* **Detailed Optimization Protocol:** Provide a more detailed and reproducible description of the optimization process, including learning rate schedules, batch sizes, and regularization techniques.
* **Mathematical Rigor:** Review the mathematical derivations and statements for accuracy and clarity.
* **Conciseness:** Streamline the paper, removing unnecessary details and focusing on the core contributions.
* **Address the connection to VAEs more precisely**: the explanation is a bit hand-wavy and misses the crucial KL divergence term, and what it represents in the context of the relaxed quantization.


**Questions for the authors:**

1.  What is the computational cost difference (training time, inference time, memory footprint) between the GDN-based model and a comparable model using ReLU activations?
2.  How sensitive are the results to the choice of downsampling/upsampling factors? Were other factors explored?
3.  Can you provide a more detailed justification for the choice of the CABAC entropy coding scheme? Have you explored more modern alternatives?
4.  What are the values of the hyperparameters used during optimization (e.g. batch size, momentum)?
5.  How was the range of the piecewise linear functions for approximating pỹi determined dynamically during training? Can you describe this process in more detail?
