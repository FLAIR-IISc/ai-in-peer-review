- Proposed method: end-to-end optimized image compression using cascaded nonlinear transforms with GDN, jointly optimizing transform, quantizer, and entropy code via SGD with a continuous relaxation of quantization.  
- Implementation: includes an actual entropy coder and reports real bitrates, demonstrating visually improved results over JPEG and JPEG 2000.  
- Theoretical grounding: draws a careful connection to variational autoencoders and discusses optimization challenges.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — significant contribution with clear visual and quantitative advantages.  
- Novelty/significance: departs from traditional pipelines; GDN is a neuroscience-motivated and effective nonlinearity.  
- Empirical strength: strong results on Kodak and additional images, improved MS-SSIM and competitive rate-distortion.  
- Sound methodology: sensible continuous relaxation for quantization and thoughtful theoretical discussion.  
- Presentation quality: well-written with a useful appendix of implementation details.  
- Ablation request: include studies to isolate contributions of components (e.g., GDN vs. other nonlinearities).  
- Computational cost: provide quantitative training time and resource requirements.  
- Entropy code details: give more detail on entropy coder performance and comparisons to other state-of-the-art coders beyond CABAC.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: discuss sensitivity to λ and network architecture to assess robustness.  
- Noise choice: clarify trade-offs in using uniform noise versus other noise distributions (e.g., Gaussian).  
- Question — λ→∞ / lossless: does performance saturate as λ approaches infinity and can lossless compression be achieved?  
- Question — perceptual training: would training with a perceptual metric improve visual quality and what challenges would that pose?  
- Question — overfitting: were any techniques used to prevent overfitting beyond the rate-distortion objective?  
- Question — marginal approximation: rationale for piecewise linear marginals and impact of sampling point count on performance and cost?  
- Question — user study: any plans to conduct a user study to objectively measure perceived image quality?  
- Overall conclusion: well-motivated, technically sound, experimentally validated paper; minor revisions suggested to strengthen impact.
