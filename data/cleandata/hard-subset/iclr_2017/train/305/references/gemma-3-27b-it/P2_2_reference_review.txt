## Review of "END-TO-END OPTIMIZED IMAGE COMPRESSION"

**1. Summary:**

This paper presents a novel end-to-end optimized image compression framework based on cascaded nonlinear transformations, specifically utilizing Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN). The authors move beyond traditional transform coding by jointly optimizing the analysis, synthesis transforms, and entropy coding directly for rate-distortion performance. They tackle the challenge of optimizing through quantization by employing a continuous relaxation based on additive noise. They demonstrate, through experiments on standard datasets, that their method achieves comparable or superior rate-distortion performance to JPEG and JPEG 2000, and importantly, produces visually more pleasing results, avoiding common artifacts associated with linear transform coding.  The work establishes a connection to variational autoencoders, providing a theoretical justification for the approach.

**2. Decision:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. The paper addresses a fundamental problem in image compression with a novel and promising approach. The results are compelling, particularly the improved visual quality compared to standard methods.  The connection to VAEs is insightful and provides a solid theoretical foundation.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

* **Novelty & Motivation:** The work presents a clear departure from traditional image compression pipelines, offering a truly end-to-end optimized solution. The motivation for using GDN – its ability to Gaussianize image densities and its biological plausibility – is well-articulated and builds upon prior work by the authors.
* **Technical Soundness:** The use of a continuous relaxation to enable gradient-based optimization through the quantization step is a clever and essential contribution.  The authors carefully consider the implications of this relaxation and empirically demonstrate its validity.  The detailed description of the network architecture, optimization process, and entropy coding scheme is thorough.
* **Empirical Results:** The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. While the quantitative rate-distortion performance is competitive, the qualitative improvements in image quality are particularly striking.  The comparison to JPEG and JPEG 2000, and the availability of compressed images online, significantly strengthens the claims. The empirical verification of the relaxed loss function's accuracy is reassuring.
* **Theoretical Connection:** Establishing a link to variational autoencoders provides a valuable theoretical underpinning, placing the work within a broader context of generative modeling and potentially opening avenues for further research.

**4. Additional Feedback (For Improvement):**

* **Computational Cost:** The paper mentions the high computational cost of training. Providing a more concrete estimate of the training time and resources required would be valuable. It would be helpful to discuss potential avenues for reducing this cost.
* **Ablation Study:**  While the authors justify their choice of GDN, a brief ablation study exploring the performance of alternative nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU) would further strengthen the argument for GDN's effectiveness.
* **Entropy Coding Details:** The description of the CABAC implementation could be expanded.  Specifically, explaining how context modeling is performed and detailing the range on which it operates would be beneficial.
* **Generalization:** The paper primarily focuses on standard image datasets. Discussing the potential limitations of the method when applied to other types of images (e.g., medical images, satellite imagery) would provide a more complete picture.

**5. Questions for the Authors:**

*  You mention that the continuous relaxation provides a good approximation to the discrete entropy, particularly at finer quantization.  However, the plot in Figure 4 shows a persistent positive bias.  How sensitive is the overall performance to this bias, and are there any adjustments made during optimization to mitigate its effects?
*  The appendix details the handling of edge cases during the arithmetic coding.  How robust is the entropy code to data outside of the training distribution, and are there any safeguards in place to prevent negative entropy (which would indicate an invalid code)?
*  Regarding the computational cost of training, can you provide a breakdown of the time spent on each stage (e.g., forward pass, backward pass, entropy coding optimization)?  Are there any hardware acceleration techniques (e.g., GPUs) that are essential for practical training?
*  You state that the method exhibits comparable or superior performance to JPEG and JPEG 2000. Are there any specific image types or conditions (e.g., image complexity, bit rate) where the proposed method consistently outperforms these standards, and vice-versa?  A more granular analysis beyond the summary curves would be appreciated.



In conclusion, this is a well-written, technically sound, and highly promising paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of image compression.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above.
