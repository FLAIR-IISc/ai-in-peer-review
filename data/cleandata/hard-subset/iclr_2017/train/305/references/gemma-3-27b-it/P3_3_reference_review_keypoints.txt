- Presents an end-to-end optimized image compression framework using cascaded nonlinear GDN transforms and uniform scalar quantization with a noise-based continuous relaxation for gradient optimization.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions due to strong results and sound approach.  
- Novelty: Jointly optimizes transform, quantizer, and entropy code, departing from traditional separated pipelines and motivated by biological vision.  
- Technical soundness: Continuous relaxation of quantization and clear description of GDN/IGDN transforms enable replication and link to variational autoencoders.  
- Empirical results: Visually superior to JPEG/JPEG 2000 and competitive or better in rate-distortion, especially at low bitrates; uses actual bitrate measurements.  
- Comparison: Thorough comparisons to JPEG and JPEG 2000, with acknowledgment of fairness issues like chroma subsampling and implementation differences.  
- Requested improvement: Include statistical significance analysis (p-values/confidence intervals) to strengthen performance claims.  
- Requested improvement: Provide more details on entropy coding implementation and CABAC context modeling and how it was tailored or compared to JPEG 2000.  
- Requested improvement: Discuss computational cost—training resources, time, and comparison to other deep compression methods.  
- Requested improvement: Add ablation studies comparing GDN to simpler nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU) or no nonlinearity.  
- Requested improvement: Report sensitivity of results to the regularization parameter λ and whether λ was tuned per image or fixed.
