This paper proposes an end-to-end optimized image compression framework based on cascaded nonlinear transforms, specifically utilizing Generalized Divisive Normalization (GDN). The core idea is to learn the analysis and synthesis transforms jointly to minimize a rate-distortion cost, offering a potential alternative to traditional transform coding methods like JPEG and JPEG 2000. The authors demonstrate empirically that their approach achieves visually superior results, especially in avoiding common artifacts associated with linear transform coding. The work builds upon their previous research showing perceptual benefits of nonlinear transforms and provides a comprehensive exploration of the method, including details on optimization and entropy coding.

**Reject.** While the paper presents a compelling approach and demonstrates promising results, the experimental validation and analysis, particularly regarding the comparison with established compression methods, lack sufficient rigor and clarity to justify acceptance at ICLR. Specifically, the extensive reliance on visual comparison, coupled with complexities in the reported rate-distortion curves, make it difficult to confidently assess the actual gains achieved by the proposed method.

The primary justification for rejection stems from the limitations in the comparative analysis. While the authors acknowledge the difficulties in fair comparison (discussed in Section 6.3), the provided summaries of rate-distortion performance feel incomplete and potentially misleading. The claim of “substantial improvements” relies heavily on visual inspection and aggregated curves, without a clear, statistically sound demonstration of superiority across a diverse set of images. The choice of JPEG 2000 parameters (particularly the method of targeting bitrates/quality) significantly impacts the comparison, and the potential sensitivity of results to these choices isn’t thoroughly addressed. Furthermore, the practical implications of the significant computational cost of training are not fully explored, diminishing the impact of the contribution.

A secondary concern revolves around the relationship to variational autoencoders (VAEs). While the authors draw parallels, the nuances of how the MSE distortion metric influences the optimization and how it deviates from a typical VAE setup aren’t fully elucidated. The discussion feels somewhat cursory, and a more in-depth analysis of this connection would strengthen the theoretical underpinnings of the work.

**Additional Feedback:**

*   **Statistical Significance:** It's crucial to provide statistically significant evidence of improvement over JPEG and JPEG 2000. This could involve reporting confidence intervals or conducting statistical tests (e.g., paired t-tests) on PSNR, MS-SSIM, or other relevant metrics across the test image dataset.
*   **Ablation Studies:** Conducting ablation studies to assess the contribution of different components of the architecture (e.g., GDN, the cascade of layers) would provide valuable insights into the effectiveness of the proposed design.
*   **Computational Cost:** The training cost is a major concern. The paper should explicitly quantify the training time and resources required, and discuss potential strategies for reducing this cost (e.g., transfer learning, model compression).
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** Investigate the sensitivity of the performance to different hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate, regularization strength, network capacity). A sensitivity analysis would reveal the robustness of the results and provide guidance for practical implementation.
*   **Clarify Entropy Coding:** Provide a more detailed explanation of the entropy coding scheme, including how it adapts to the data and how it compares to state-of-the-art entropy coders commonly used in image compression.
*   **VAE Connection:** Expand the discussion of the relationship to VAEs. Specifically, discuss the implications of using MSE as the distortion metric and how it affects the learned representation compared to VAEs trained with perceptual losses.
*   **Reproducibility:** Release code and trained models to facilitate reproducibility and enable other researchers to build upon this work.

**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  Could you provide a table summarizing the average PSNR and MS-SSIM scores for your method, JPEG, and JPEG 2000 across the Kodak dataset at several target bit rates, along with standard deviations or confidence intervals?
2.  How sensitive is the performance of your method to the choice of the GDN parameters (e.g., beta and gamma)? Did you perform any hyperparameter tuning, and if so, how?
3.  What is the approximate training time for a single model (e.g., for a specific lambda value) on a typical GPU setup?
4.  Could you elaborate on the limitations of using MSE as a distortion metric and how it might affect the learned representation compared to using a perceptual metric?
5.  How does the performance of your method compare to more modern entropy coding schemes like Range Coding or Arithmetic Coding used in advanced image compression standards like AVIF?
