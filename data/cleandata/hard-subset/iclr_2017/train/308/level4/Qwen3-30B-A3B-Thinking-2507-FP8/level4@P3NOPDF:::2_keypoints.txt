- Provides a valuable theoretical analysis explaining GAN training issues (gradient vanishing in standard GANs and instability in variants) and proposes a perturbation-based mechanism to mitigate them.

- Core insight: perturbation regularizes the discriminator’s gradient flow; the connection to dropout (Bernoulli noise) should be explicitly explored (analogy, convergence, generalization).

- Empirical validation is insufficient; paper needs comparative experiments (e.g., FID, training stability, mode coverage) on benchmarks like CIFAR-10/STL-10 and plots/samples/gradient analyses showing perturbation’s effect.

- Must address limitations and sensitivity: hyperparameters (noise magnitude/distribution), generalization to complex architectures (progressive, StyleGAN) and other losses beyond WGAN; include ablation studies.

- Writing requires tightening and minor grammatical fixes; title and abstract should explicitly highlight the perturbation mechanism as the key innovation.

- Overall assessment: theoretically strong and promising but hinges on empirical evidence and deeper dropout discussion; revisions could make it a compelling ICLR submission.
