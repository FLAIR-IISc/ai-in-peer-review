

This work makes a significant contribution toward clarifying the training dynamics of generative adversarial networks (GANs) and their variants. The authors identify the root cause of two critical challenges: gradient vanishing in standard GANs and gradient instability in their variants. Crucially, they propose a perturbation-based technique to mitigate these issues, offering a principled approach to stabilize training. This insight has the potential to catalyze more rigorous research in GAN optimization.  

I find the perturbation technique particularly compelling, as it conceptually parallels dropout regularization, where noise is introduced via a Bernoulli distribution. The authors should explicitly discuss this connection in the paper, as it would strengthen the theoretical foundation and contextualize their method within established deep learning practices.  

Beyond the theoretical analysis, I request empirical validation of the perturbation approach. Specifically, it would be valuable to include comparative experiments demonstrating its effectiveness—such as visualizations of generated samples, loss curves, or convergence metrics—alongside baseline GANs and variants. Figures analogous to Figures 2 and 3 in the paper would provide concrete evidence of the technique’s impact on stability and performance. Without such empirical support, the practical utility of the perturbation method remains speculative.  

Overall, the paper presents a novel and timely analysis with strong theoretical motivation. Addressing the connection to dropout and providing empirical results would substantially enhance its contribution and impact.
