

This paper makes a valuable contribution toward clarifying the training dynamics of generative adversarial networks (GANs) and their variants. The authors provide novel insights into why gradients vanish in the original GAN formulation or become unstable in its variants, and they propose a perturbation-based method to mitigate these issues. I believe this work will likely inspire more principled research in GAN training.  

I am particularly interested in the perturbation trick for avoiding gradient instability and vanishing. This approach bears a conceptual resemblance to the dropout technique, where perturbations can be interpreted as Bernoulli-distributed noise. It would be beneficial to explicitly discuss this connection in the paper. Additionally, while the theoretical analysis is compelling, I request empirical validation of the perturbation method. Specifically, could the authors include comparative experiments (e.g., analogous to Figures 2 and 3) demonstrating the performance of the perturbed GAN against baseline variants? Such results would strengthen the practical relevance of the proposed approach.
