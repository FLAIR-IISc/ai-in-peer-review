

SUMMARY  
This paper addresses critical challenges in training generative adversarial networks (GANs), analyzing the consequences of asymmetric divergence functions and identifying sources of training instability. It proposes an alternative approach based on smoothing techniques to mitigate these issues.  

STRENGTHS  
The paper presents a strong theoretical foundation with well-motivated questions and insightful solutions. Its novel application of concepts from analysis and differential topology enriches the discussion. The proposed methods offer concrete pathways to reduce instability in GAN training, making a valuable contribution to the field.  

WEAKNESSES  
The exposition is overly technical in places, and certain arguments require deeper development (which is acceptable for future work). The length could be reduced without compromising core insights.
