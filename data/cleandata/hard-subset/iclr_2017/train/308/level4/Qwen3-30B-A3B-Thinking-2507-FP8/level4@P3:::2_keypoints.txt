- Decision: Accept.
- Major contribution: Provides significant theoretical insights into fundamental challenges in GAN training.
- Core analysis: Shows instability and saturation arise because real and generator distributions lie on low-dimensional manifolds.
- Consequence: Low-dimensional supports yield perfect discriminators with zero gradients on generator support, causing vanishing gradients in original GANs.
- Modified GANs: Demonstrates that discriminator optimality leads to unstable updates in modified formulations.
- Solution: Proposes principled distributional perturbation to smooth training dynamics with a clear theoretical foundation.
- Key achievement: Rigorous proofs answering why gradients vanish, why updates become unstable, and how to fix these issues.
- Literature placement: Well-motivated extension of prior theoretical work, filling a significant gap in understanding GAN behavior.
- Notable analysis: Compelling study of optimal discriminator behavior when distributions lie on low-dimensional manifolds.
- Connection to practice: Strong theoretical link between analysis and the perturbation technique offering a path to more stable GAN training.
- Requested improvements: Needs more detailed guidance on practical implementation of perturbation, especially selecting the perturbation parameter (σ) and its trade-offs.
- Suggested expansion: Elaborate the connection between the perturbation technique and dropout/other regularization methods.
- Questions for authors: Provide architecture/training details and sensitivity analysis for Figure 3, theoretical relation between σ and sample quality/convergence rates, and direct comparisons showing perturbation removes need for careful discriminator scheduling in terms of sample quality and compute.
