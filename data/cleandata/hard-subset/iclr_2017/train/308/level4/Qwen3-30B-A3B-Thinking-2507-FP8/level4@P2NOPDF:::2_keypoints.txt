- Provides rigorous analysis of gradient behavior in GANs, explaining causes of vanishing gradients and instability.  
- Proposes a principled perturbation-based technique to mitigate these gradient pathologies.  
- Perturbation mechanism is compelling and conceptually resembles dropout (continuous perturbations vs Bernoulli); paper should discuss this relationship.  
- Paper lacks empirical validation demonstrating the perturbation’s practical efficacy.  
- Reviewer requests experiments comparable to Figures 2–3: quantitative comparisons of training stability, convergence speed, and quality metrics (e.g., FID, Inception Score) between standard and perturbed GANs.  
- Recommend testing the method across diverse datasets and architectures to establish generality.  
- Absence of experiments weakens the practical impact; adding empirical results would make the method actionable.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance pending empirical analysis and refinement of the dropout analogy.
