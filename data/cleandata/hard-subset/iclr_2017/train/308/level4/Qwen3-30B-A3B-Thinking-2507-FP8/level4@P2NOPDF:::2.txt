

This paper makes a valuable contribution toward clarifying the training dynamics of generative adversarial networks (GANs) and their variants. The authors provide a rigorous analysis of gradient behavior during GAN training, revealing the underlying causes for vanishing gradients in standard GANs and instability in common variants. Crucially, they propose a perturbation-based technique to mitigate these issues, offering a principled solution to a well-known challenge in GAN training. This work is likely to stimulate further theoretical and practical advancements in the field.  

The proposed perturbation mechanism is particularly compelling. It conceptually resembles the dropout technique, though the distributions differ (Bernoulli in dropout versus continuous perturbations here). A brief discussion of this relationship would strengthen the paper by contextualizing the method within established practices.  

Regarding empirical validation, the theoretical insights would benefit from supporting experiments. While the paper establishes a strong analytical foundation, additional empirical results are needed to demonstrate the perturbation’s practical efficacy. Specifically, including new experiments comparable to Figures 2–3 (e.g., quantitative comparisons of training stability, convergence speed, or quality metrics like FID or inception scores between standard GANs and the perturbed variant) would substantially bolster the claims. Such results would clarify whether the theoretical advantages translate to tangible improvements across diverse datasets and architectures.  

The paper’s core contribution—linking gradient pathology to training dynamics and proposing a solution—is significant and well-executed. However, the absence of empirical validation for the perturbation technique weakens the practical impact of the findings. Addressing this gap would elevate the work from a theoretical insight to a fully actionable method.  

Overall, this is a strong submission with clear theoretical value. With minor revisions to include the requested empirical analysis and refine the dropout analogy, it would be highly suitable for publication at ICLR. The perturbation approach is novel, well-motivated, and addresses a persistent challenge in GAN research. I recommend acceptance pending these clarifications.
