

This is an excellent paper that makes a significant contribution to one of the most important and recently developed areas in neural networks: generative adversarial networks. The authors provide a rigorous theoretical analysis of GAN convergence and stability—both critical aspects of the field. To my knowledge, this represents one of the first substantial theoretical investigations into GANs, offering deep mathematical insights that contrast with the predominantly empirical nature of most prior work. The paper directly addresses the well-documented instability issues plaguing early GAN architectures, which severely limited their practical utility. Theorems 2.4–2.6 are novel and introduce compelling mathematical techniques. I have minor technical questions regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5, but these do not detract from the paper's overall strength. I maintain my positive assessment and strongly recommend acceptance.
