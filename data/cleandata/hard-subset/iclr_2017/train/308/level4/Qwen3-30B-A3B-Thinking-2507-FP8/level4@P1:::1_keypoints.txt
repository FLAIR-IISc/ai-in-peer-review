- Significant theoretical contribution explaining GAN training dynamics, instability, and saturation through rigorous analysis.
- Proven that generator distributions typically lie on low-dimensional manifolds, enabling perfect discriminators and causing vanishing gradients.
- Novel theoretical framework including a softened metric / noise-regularized distributions approach to address instability.
- Decision: Accept.
- Theorems 2.4–2.6 provide the first rigorous explanation for vanishing gradients and instability in GAN training.
- Lemma 1 and Theorems 2.1–2.2 show that data and generator manifolds admit perfect discriminators that are constant almost everywhere, explaining gradient collapse.
- Analysis shows the original GAN cost leads to vanishing gradients as the discriminator improves, while the −log D cost yields unstable updates with infinite variance under noisy discriminator estimates.
- Proposed fix: add noise to discriminator inputs to produce continuous distributions and meaningful gradients; Theorem 3.3 links Wasserstein distance of originals to JSD of noisy distributions.
- Suggest integrating the “continuous” vs “absolutely continuous” discussion into the main text for clarity.
- Recommend stronger connections to practical methods (e.g., label smoothing, spectral normalization) to bridge theory and practice.
- Advise adding brief empirical validation to demonstrate practical impact of the theoretical framework.
- Question: How does the KL(Pg‖Pr) − 2JSD(Pg‖Pr) objective relate to mode collapse and suggest training modifications?
- Question: What practical guidance can be given for selecting noise variance σ² across datasets and architectures?
- Question: How does backpropagating through noisy samples (∇θJSD(Pr+‖Pg+)) relate to WGAN—are the methods complementary or competing?
