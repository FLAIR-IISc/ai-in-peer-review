- Strong contribution tackling two critical GAN challenges: convergence and stability.
- Provides the first rigorous theoretical analysis of GAN convergence and stability.
- Focus on stability is timely, addressing early GAN instability that hindered practical adoption.
- Theoretical framework is well-motivated and advances understanding of GAN dynamics.
- Theorems 2.4–2.6 offer novel mathematical techniques characterizing convergence under non-trivial conditions.
- Results are foundational and likely to guide future work on GAN robustness.
- Minor technical concern: treatment of the Lipschitz constant in the proof of Theorem 2.5.
- Recommendation to clarify assumptions (e.g., Lipschitz treatment) in the supplementary material.
- Overall theoretical rigor is high and the work departs from empirical trends by grounding GAN analysis in formal mathematics.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance for substantial theoretical advancement and clear field relevance.
