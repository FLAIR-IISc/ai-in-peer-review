- Paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of GAN training instability and saturation, identifying root causes and proposing a smoothing fix.  
- Key insight: optimal discriminator is constant on the generator support, causing vanishing gradients.  
- Proposed practical solution: add input noise to create continuous distributions and stabilize training.  
- Theoretical contributions are strong and rigorous, with compelling proofs for Theorems 2.1, 2.2, 2.4, and 2.6.  
- Showing that smoothing makes Jensen-Shannon divergence meaningful between distributions is a major contribution.  
- Connection to the Wasserstein metric (Theorem 3.3) further strengthens the theoretical foundation.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions due to technical strength and practical relevance.  
- Recommend moving technical proofs (Theorems 2.1, 2.2, 2.4) to the appendix to improve readability.  
- Suggest shortening the manifold discussion (Section 2.1) to focus on core insights.  
- Authors should clarify domains of random variables, especially in Theorems 2.4 and 2.6, and fix the Corollary 2.1 typo referencing "Theorem 1.3."  
- Request clearer, explicit linkage between theoretical results and practical implementation in Section 3.  
- Advise explicitly distinguishing "noiseless" vs "noisy" distributions throughout the paper.  
- Clarifying Q1: Specify whether "closed manifolds" include boundaries and how boundaries affect the analysis.  
- Clarifying Q2: Explain implications of generator support g(Z) having lower dimension than X for required noise variance in practice.  
- Clarifying Q3: Provide guidance on choosing noise level σ to trade off stability and fidelity in practice.  
- Clarifying Q4: Explain how the gradient proportional to 2∇θJSD(Pr+‖Pg+) differs from directly minimizing JSD(Pr+‖Pg+) and the practical training implications.
