

This is a strong contribution addressing two critical challenges in generative adversarial networks (GANs), a cornerstone of modern neural network research. The authors provide the first rigorous theoretical analysis of GAN convergence and stability, offering deep insights that are notably absent from most prior work in the field. Their focus on stability is particularly timely, as early GAN implementations suffered from severe instability, hindering practical adoption—a problem this paper directly addresses.  

The theoretical framework is well-motivated and significantly advances our understanding of GAN dynamics. Theorems 2.4–2.6 present novel contributions, introducing elegant mathematical techniques that effectively characterize convergence behavior under non-trivial conditions. These results are not merely incremental; they establish foundational principles for future work on GAN robustness.  

I have minor technical concerns regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5, specifically about the treatment of the Lipschitz constant in the gradient step analysis. While these points do not undermine the paper’s core contributions, I recommend the authors clarify the assumptions in the supplementary material. That said, this is a very minor point, and the overall theoretical rigor remains high.  

The paper’s significance is amplified by its departure from the prevalent trend of empirical GAN improvements. By grounding the analysis in formal mathematics, the authors deliver a rare and valuable perspective that will inform both theoretical and applied research. This work deserves acceptance for its substantial theoretical advancement and clear relevance to a field where practical success has long been hampered by instability.
