- Significant theoretical contribution analyzing GAN training dynamics and instability.  
- Shows instability and saturation occur when data and generator distributions lie on low-dimensional, disjoint manifolds.  
- Proves the optimal discriminator becomes perfect and its gradient vanishes almost everywhere, explaining vanishing gradients in standard GANs.  
- Demonstrates the modified GAN cost avoids vanishing gradients but yields unstable updates (Cauchy-distributed gradients).  
- Proposes a principled perturbation-based solution (adding noise to inputs) that smooths distributions and enables stable gradient updates.  
- Establishes a connection between Wasserstein distance of original distributions and Jensen–Shannon divergence of perturbed distributions.  
- Recommends acceptance, citing major theoretical advance and practical implications for GAN research.  
- Requests exploration of relationship between the perturbation approach and dropout (continuous analog) and effects of different noise distributions.  
- Requests additional empirical validation comparing standard GAN training with the perturbed approach.  
- Notes the paper is well-written, with clear theorems and rigorous proofs, and will likely influence future GAN research.
