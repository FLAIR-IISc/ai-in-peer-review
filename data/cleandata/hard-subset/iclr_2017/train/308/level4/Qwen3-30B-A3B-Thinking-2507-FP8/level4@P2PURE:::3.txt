

SUMMARY  
This paper addresses critical challenges in training generative adversarial networks (GANs), examining the consequences of employing asymmetric divergence functions and identifying sources of training instability. It subsequently proposes an alternative approach based on smoothing techniques.  

PROS  
The work presents strong theoretical foundations, poses insightful questions, and provides compelling answers. It effectively leverages concepts from analysis and differential topology. The proposed methodology offers practical avenues for mitigating instability in GAN training.  

CONS  
The paper is lengthy and technically dense; some arguments and implications require further development (which is acceptable as future work).
