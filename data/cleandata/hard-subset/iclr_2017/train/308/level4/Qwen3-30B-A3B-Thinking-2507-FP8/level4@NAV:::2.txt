

This paper makes a valuable contribution toward clarifying the training dynamics of generative adversarial networks (GANs) and their variants. The authors identify the root cause of gradient vanishing in standard GANs and gradient instability in their variants, and propose a perturbation-based method to mitigate these issues. This work will likely inspire more principled research in GAN training.  

The perturbation technique is particularly intriguing, as it bears a notable analogy to dropout—where the perturbation can be interpreted as sampling from a Bernoulli distribution. The authors should elaborate on this connection. Additionally, while the theoretical analysis is compelling, empirical validation of the perturbation’s effectiveness is needed. We recommend including comparative experiments (e.g., analogous to Figures 2–3) demonstrating the performance of the perturbed GAN against baselines.
