

This is a strong submission addressing two critical theoretical challenges in generative adversarial networks (GANs), a foundational method in deep learning. The authors provide the first rigorous theoretical analysis of GAN convergence and stability, a significant contribution given that practical implementations of early GANs suffered from severe instability. The paper stands out from the majority of empirical work in the field by delivering deep theoretical insights into this architecture. Theorems 2.4–2.6 are novel and introduce interesting mathematical techniques. I have minor concerns regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5, but these do not undermine the paper's substantial contributions. Overall, this is an excellent theoretical work that merits acceptance.
