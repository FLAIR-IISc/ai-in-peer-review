- Paper addresses GAN instability from asymmetric divergence functions and proposes a smoothing-based stabilization with theoretical analysis.  
- Strong theoretical rigor and conceptual novelty; smoothing the discriminator is a fresh, mathematically grounded approach.  
- Demonstrates how asymmetric divergences (e.g., Jensen–Shannon) induce mode collapse and vanishing gradients linked to data/generator geometry.  
- Proposed method: convolve the discriminator output with a kernel to stabilize training without altering generator architecture or loss.  
- Differential topology (manifolds and boundaries) is used substantively to strengthen the theoretical argument.  
- Opens future work directions, e.g., applying smoothing to other generative models and studying convergence rates.  
- Weakness: Paper is dense and long; technical sections (Sections 2.1–2.2) contain detailed proofs that distract from the main narrative.  
- Recommend moving routine/standard proofs (Lemmas 1–2, consequences like Theorem 2.1) to the appendix or citing standard references.  
- Presentation issues: inconsistent theorem numbering (e.g., Corollary 2.1 cites non-existent Theorem 1.3) causes confusion.  
- Clarity gaps: unspecified domains for random variables (e.g., z in p(z) in Theorem 2.4) and ambiguous statement about boundaries in Theorem 2.2.  
- Minor technical suggestions: note Lemma 1 generalizes to L^p spaces; correct references (Corollary 2.1 → Theorem 2.1).  
- Copy edits: fix wording/grammar (e.g., "for generating not plausibly looking pictures"), typos ("relly"→"rely"), and small theorem phrasing/capitalization issues.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, conceptually important, and practically promising; needs minor revisions for clarity and presentation.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
