

This paper provides a valuable contribution toward a clearer understanding of generative adversarial network (GAN) training procedures. By analyzing the training dynamics of GANs and their variants, the authors explain why gradients either vanish in the original GAN or become unstable in its variants. Crucially, they propose a perturbation-based method to mitigate these issues. I believe this work will inspire more principled research in GAN training.  

I am particularly interested in the perturbation technique for addressing gradient instability and vanishing gradients. This approach bears a strong resemblance to the dropout technique, where perturbations can be interpreted as Bernoulli-distributed noise. It would be insightful to explicitly discuss this connection. Additionally, while the theoretical analysis is compelling, is there empirical evidence supporting the effectiveness of this perturbation? Could the authors include supplementary experiments—such as comparative visualizations similar to Figures 2 and 3—to demonstrate performance improvements in the perturbed GAN framework?
