- Rigorous theoretical analysis explaining GAN instability and saturation during training.  
- Shows instability stems from low-dimensional manifold structure of data and generator distributions.  
- Demonstrates that manifold overlap leads to perfect discriminators and vanishing gradients.  
- Proposes adding noise (smoothing) to create smoother distributions and restore meaningful gradients.  
- Provides new theoretical tools for analyzing and improving generative models.  
- Decision: Accept — substantial theoretical contribution and principled smoothing solution.  
- Addresses three key questions: identifies problem, grounds approach in literature/mathematics, and supports claims.  
- Claims are supported by formal proofs (Theorems 2.1–2.6) and experiments (Figures 1–3).  
- Corollary 3.2 shows smoothing enables stable gradient updates in theory.  
- Suggests moving detailed proofs to an appendix to improve paper flow.  
- Recommends stronger, more explicit guidance on translating smoothing to specific GAN architectures and clarifying the Wasserstein vs. JSD discussion.  
- Asks how optimal noise variance scales with manifold dimensionality and affects implementation.  
- Requests guidance on when smoothing outperforms other stabilization methods (e.g., spectral normalization, gradient penalty).  
- Seeks clarification on how smoothing defends against measure-0 adversarial examples and how that differs from other adversarial defenses.
