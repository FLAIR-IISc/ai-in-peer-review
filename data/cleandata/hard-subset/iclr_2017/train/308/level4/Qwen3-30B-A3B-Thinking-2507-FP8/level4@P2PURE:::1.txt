

This is a strong submission addressing two critical aspects of generative adversarial networks (GANs), a major recent development in neural networks. The authors provide the first rigorous theoretical analysis of GAN convergence and stability, offering deep insights into this architecture—unlike most prior work in the field. The stability analysis is particularly significant, as early GAN implementations were notoriously unstable and performed poorly in practice. Theorems 2.4–2.6 are novel and introduce interesting mathematical techniques. I have minor concerns regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5, but these do not undermine the paper's substantial contributions. The theoretical depth and relevance of this work make it a valuable addition to the literature. I recommend acceptance.
