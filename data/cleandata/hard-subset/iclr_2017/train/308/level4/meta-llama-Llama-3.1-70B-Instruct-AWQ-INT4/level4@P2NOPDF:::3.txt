SUMMARY 
This paper explores crucial issues related to training generative adversarial networks (GANs), discussing the implications of using an asymmetric divergence function and the sources of instability in GAN training. The authors propose an alternative approach using a smoothening method to address these challenges.

PROS 
The paper presents a strong theoretical foundation, raises important questions, and provides insightful answers. It effectively leverages concepts from analysis and differential topology, offering a novel perspective on the topic. Furthermore, the authors propose potential avenues for avoiding instability in GANs, contributing to the ongoing discussion in the field.

CONS 
While the paper is well-written, it could benefit from condensation, as some sections appear overly technical. Additionally, certain parts and consequences require further development, which is acceptable as a direction for future work.

MINOR COMMENTS

- Section 3 provides a clear, intuitive, and simple solution to the problem at hand.
