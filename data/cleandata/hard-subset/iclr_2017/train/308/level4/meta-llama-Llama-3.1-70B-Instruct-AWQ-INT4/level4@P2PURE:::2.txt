This paper makes a valuable contribution to providing a clearer understanding of the generative adversarial network (GAN) training procedure. 

The authors offer new insights into the training dynamics of GANs and their variants, shedding light on the reasons behind the vanishing gradients in the original GAN and the unstable gradients in its variants. Notably, they also propose a solution to mitigate these issues by introducing perturbations. I believe this paper will inspire more principled research in this direction.

I am particularly interested in the perturbation trick for avoiding gradient instability and vanishment. Interestingly, this technique bears some resemblance to the dropout trick, where the perturbation can be viewed as a Bernoulli distribution. It would be beneficial to discuss this connection in more detail. In addition to the theoretical analysis, I would like to see empirical evidence to justify the effectiveness of this trick. Specifically, I suggest adding experiments similar to those presented in Figures 2 and 3, but for the perturbed GAN, to facilitate comparison and demonstrate its impact.
