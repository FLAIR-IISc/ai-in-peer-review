- Paper provides clear theoretical analysis of GAN training dynamics, explaining gradient vanishing in original GANs and instability in variants.
- Authors propose a perturbation trick to avoid gradient vanishing and instability.
- Perturbation idea is promising and potentially inspires further principled research.
- Reviewer is particularly interested in connection between the perturbation trick and dropout (perturbation as Bernoulli).
- Reviewer requests the authors discuss the relation to dropout.
- Reviewer recommends adding empirical experiments (e.g., perturbed GAN versions comparable to Figures 2 and 3) to validate the trick.
- Decision: Accept.
- Main reasons: clear, thorough analysis of GAN training and a novel, theoretically supported solution to gradient issues.
- Supporting arguments: paper is well-structured, easy to follow, and provides strong theoretical backing for the proposed method.
- Additional feedback: include more empirical studies to strengthen the paper’s claims.
- Additional feedback: further explore and clarify the connection to existing techniques like dropout.
- Questions for authors: provide more empirical details and comparisons to existing methods.
- Questions for authors: clarify practical applicability, potential limitations, and challenges of the proposed method.
