SUMMARY 
This paper addresses important questions about the difficulties in training generative adversarial networks. It discusses consequences of using an asymmetric divergence function and sources of instability in training GANs. Then it proposes an alternative using a smoothening approach.

PROS 
The paper provides a theoretical foundation for understanding the challenges in training GANs, offering insightful explanations and novel solutions. The authors' use of concepts from analysis and differential topology is a notable strength, as it brings a fresh perspective to the field. The proposed approach to avoiding instability in GANs is also a significant contribution.

CONS 
The paper is quite technical and lengthy, which may make it challenging for some readers to follow. Some sections, such as 2.1, could be shortened by moving proofs to the appendix. Additionally, some consequences and ideas are still in the early stages of development, which is acceptable for future work but may leave some readers seeking more comprehensive conclusions.

DECISION 
Based on the paper's strengths and weaknesses, I lean towards accepting it. However, I would like to see some revisions to address the concerns mentioned above.

SUPPORTING ARGUMENTS 
The paper's theoretical contributions are its most significant strength. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the sources of instability in GAN training and propose a novel solution using a smoothening approach. The use of concepts from analysis and differential topology is a notable aspect of the paper, as it brings a new perspective to the field.

The paper's weaknesses are primarily related to its length and technicality. Some sections, such as 2.1, could be shortened by moving proofs to the appendix. Additionally, some consequences and ideas are still in the early stages of development, which may leave some readers seeking more comprehensive conclusions.
