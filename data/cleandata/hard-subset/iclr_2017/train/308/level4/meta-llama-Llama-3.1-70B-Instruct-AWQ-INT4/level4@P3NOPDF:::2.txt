This paper makes a valuable contribution to providing a clearer understanding of the generative adversarial network (GAN) training procedure. By offering new insights into the training dynamics of GANs and their variants, the authors reveal the reasons behind the vanishing or unstable gradients in the original GAN and its variants, respectively. More importantly, they propose a solution to overcome these difficulties by introducing perturbation, which is a significant contribution to the field.

I am particularly interested in the perturbation trick for avoiding gradient instability and vanishment. Notably, this technique bears some resemblance to the dropout trick, where the perturbation can be viewed as a Bernoulli distribution. A discussion on this connection would be beneficial, as it could provide additional context and deepen the understanding of the proposed method. Furthermore, while the theoretical analysis is thorough, it would be helpful to include empirical studies to justify the effectiveness of this trick. To this end, I suggest adding experiments similar to Figures 2 and 3, which compare the performance of the perturbed GAN to its unperturbed counterpart.

Overall, I believe this paper has the potential to inspire more principled research in this direction, and with some additional empirical validation, it could make an even more significant impact on the field of GANs.
