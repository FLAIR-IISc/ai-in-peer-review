SUMMARY 
This paper explores crucial issues in training generative adversarial networks (GANs) and examines the implications of employing an asymmetric divergence function, as well as the sources of instability in GAN training. The authors propose an alternative approach using a smoothening method to address these challenges.

PROS 
The paper presents well-grounded theory, raises interesting questions, and provides insightful answers. It also makes innovative use of concepts from analysis and differential topology. Furthermore, the authors suggest potential avenues for mitigating instability in GANs.

CONS 
While the paper is well-written, it could benefit from some editing to reduce its length and technical complexity. Certain sections and consequences require further development, which is acceptable as a direction for future work.

MINOR COMMENTS
- Section 2.1 could be condensed by moving the proofs to the appendix, making the main text more concise.
- Section 3 offers a simple and intuitive solution to the problem at hand.
- On page 2, the second bullet point implies that P_g is smaller than the data distribution in some other x, resulting in a non-zero KL divergence.
- On page 2, the phrase "for not generating plausibly looking pictures" should be corrected to "for generating not plausibly looking pictures."
- Lemma 1 appears to hold in greater generality.
- Theorem 2.1 seems to be a fundamental result from basic analysis; a reference could potentially replace the proof.
- In Theorem 2.4, it would be helpful to remind the reader of the definition of p(z).
- Lemma 2 also appears to be a basic result from analysis; a reference could potentially replace the proof.
- The domain of the random variables should be specified.
- A minor typo: "relly" should be corrected to "rely."
- Theorem 2.2 assumes that the closed manifolds have a boundary, but this is not explicitly stated.
- Corollary 2.1 references "assumptions of Theorem 1.3," but there is no Theorem 1.3 in the paper.
- In Theorem 2.5, "Therefore" could be replaced with "Then" for better clarity.
- In Theorem 2.6, "Is a" should be corrected to "is a."
- The numbering of the theorems can be confusing and may require reorganization.
