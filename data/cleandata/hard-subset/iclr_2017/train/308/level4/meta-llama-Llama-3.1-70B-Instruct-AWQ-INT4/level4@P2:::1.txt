This is a strong submission regarding one of the most important and recently introduced methods in neural networks - generative adversarial networks. The authors analyze theoretically the convergence of GANs and discuss the stability of GANs. Both are very important. To the best of my knowledge, this is one of the first theoretical papers about GANs and the paper, contrary to most of the submissions in the field, actually provides deep theoretical insight into this architecture.

The paper claims to make theoretical steps towards fully understanding the training dynamics of generative adversarial networks. It provides a new direction designed to avoid the instability issues in GANs, and examines in depth the theory behind it. The authors also state a series of open questions and problems, that determine several new directions of research that begin with their methods.

I decide to accept this paper. My main reason for this choice is that the paper provides novel and interesting mathematical techniques to analyze the convergence and stability of GANs. Theorems 2.4-2.6 are particularly noteworthy in this regard. 

My supporting arguments for the decision are as follows. The paper tackles the specific question of understanding the training dynamics of GANs, which is a crucial problem in the field of deep learning. The approach is well-motivated, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the existing literature on GANs. The paper also supports its claims with rigorous theoretical results, including several theorems that provide new insights into the behavior of GANs.

One potential criticism of the paper is that it may be too theoretical, and that the results may not have immediate practical implications. However, I believe that the paper's contributions to the theoretical understanding of GANs are significant enough to warrant acceptance, even if the practical implications are not immediately clear.

I have some technical questions regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5, but these are minor and do not affect my overall assessment of the paper.

Additional feedback for the authors: One potential area for improvement is to provide more discussion of the practical implications of the paper's results. While the theoretical contributions are significant, it would be helpful to provide more guidance on how the results can be used in practice to improve the training of GANs. Additionally, the authors may want to consider providing more comparisons to existing methods for training GANs, in order to help readers understand the relative advantages and disadvantages of the approach proposed in the paper.

Questions for the authors: Can you provide more intuition for why the noise terms in Theorem 3.2 are necessary to avoid the vanishing gradients problem? Are there any potential drawbacks to using the noisy discriminator approach proposed in the paper? How do the authors plan to extend the results of the paper to more complex GAN architectures?
