- Addresses GAN training difficulties, analyzing effects of asymmetric divergence and sources of instability; proposes a smoothing method to avoid these issues.
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis of GAN problems using concepts from analysis and differential topology.
- Proposes promising avenues to mitigate instability in GAN training.
- Paper is long and highly technical, potentially hard for non-experts to follow.
- Some parts and consequences require further development, suitable for future work.
- Recommendation: accept.
- Strength: deep theoretical treatment and novel approach with clear proofs for experts.
- Suggestion: shorten Section 2.1 and move some proofs to the appendix for conciseness.
- Suggestion: add more context and intuitive explanations of analysis and topology concepts for non-experts.
- Question: how can the proposed approach be applied to other generative models (e.g., VAEs)?
- Question: how will the approach scale to more complex scenarios and large-scale datasets?
- Question: how does the proposed approach relate to other instability remedies (different divergences or regularization)?
