This paper makes a valuable contribution to our understanding of the generative adversarial network (GAN) training procedure, providing new insights into its dynamics and those of its variants. The authors shed light on the reasons behind the vanishing or unstable gradients in original GANs and their variants, respectively. Moreover, they propose a solution to mitigate these issues by introducing perturbations. I believe this work will inspire more principled research in this direction.

I am particularly interested in the perturbation technique for avoiding gradient instability and vanishing. Interestingly, this approach bears some resemblance to the dropout technique, where the perturbation can be viewed as a Bernoulli distribution. It would be beneficial to discuss this connection in more detail. In addition to the theoretical analysis, I would like to see some empirical evidence to justify the effectiveness of this technique. Could the authors include additional experiments, similar to Figures 2 and 3, to compare the performance of the perturbed GAN?
