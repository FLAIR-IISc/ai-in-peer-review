This paper is a strong submission that provides valuable insights into one of the most important and recently introduced methods in neural networks - generative adversarial networks (GANs). The authors' theoretical analysis of the convergence and stability of GANs is particularly noteworthy, as these are crucial aspects of GANs that have significant implications for their practical applications. To the best of my knowledge, this is one of the first theoretical papers to delve into the intricacies of GANs, offering a level of depth and understanding that is rare in submissions in this field.

The discussion on stability issues in GANs is especially important, given that the initial versions of GANs were plagued by instability and poor performance in practice. The authors' theorems, specifically Theorems 2.4-2.6, are novel and introduce interesting mathematical techniques that contribute to the paper's overall value. While I do have some minor technical questions regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5, these do not detract from the paper's significance.

Overall, I believe this paper makes a substantial contribution to the field and demonstrates a high level of technical sophistication. I hope it will be accepted.
