- Paper provides clearer understanding of GAN training dynamics and variants.  
- Identifies why gradients vanish in original GANs and become unstable in variants.  
- Proposes introducing perturbation to avoid vanishing/unstable gradient issues.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough, rigorous, and well-motivated.  
- The perturbation-based solution is novel and theoretically supported.  
- Reviewer accepts the paper and finds it likely to inspire principled research.  
- Main weakness: empirical validation is limited and needs more experiments.  
- Suggestion: add experiments across more datasets and architectures to justify the method.  
- Suggestion: discuss links between the perturbation trick and other regularization techniques (e.g., dropout).  
- Suggestion: provide implementation details, hyperparameter choices, and training procedure.  
- Questions for authors: provide implementation/hyperparameter details, relate perturbation to other regularizers, and discuss potential limitations or drawbacks.
