This is a strong submission regarding one of the most important and recently introduced methods in neural networks - generative adversarial networks. The authors analyze theoretically the convergence of GANs and discuss the stability of GANs. Both are very important. To the best of my knowledge, this is one of the first theoretical papers about GANs and the paper, contrary to most of the submissions in the field, actually provides deep theoretical insight into this architecture. The stability issues regarding GANs are extremely important since the first proposed versions of GANs architecture were very unstable and did not work well in practice. Theorems 2.4-2.6 are novel and introduces mathematical techniques are interesting. I have some technical questions regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5 but these are pretty minor.

The paper claims to make theoretical steps towards fully understanding the training dynamics of generative adversarial networks. The authors perform targeted experiments to verify their assumptions, illustrate their claims, and quantify the phenomena. The paper is divided into three sections. The first section introduces the problem at hand. The second section is dedicated to studying and proving rigorously the problems including instability and saturation that arise when training generative adversarial networks. The third section examines a practical and theoretically grounded direction towards solving these problems, while introducing new tools to study them.

I decide to accept this paper with one key reason for this choice: the paper provides deep theoretical insight into the architecture of generative adversarial networks. The authors' analysis of the convergence of GANs and discussion of the stability of GANs are very important and novel.

The authors provide supporting arguments for the reasons of their decision by presenting several theorems and lemmas that prove the instability and saturation of GANs. They also provide experimental results that verify their assumptions and illustrate their claims. The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their methods and results.

To improve the paper, I would suggest that the authors provide more intuitive explanations of their mathematical techniques and theorems. While the paper is well-written, some of the proofs and lemmas may be difficult for non-experts to follow. Additionally, the authors could provide more discussion on the implications of their results and how they can be applied in practice.

I have some technical questions regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5. Specifically, I would like the authors to clarify the following points:

* How do the authors derive equation (3) from the previous equations?
* Can the authors provide more explanation on how they arrive at the conclusion that the JSDs are in the opposite sign, which means they are pushing for the distributions to be different?

Overall, this is a strong submission that provides important theoretical insights into the architecture of generative adversarial networks. With some minor revisions to address the technical questions and provide more intuitive explanations, this paper has the potential to make a significant impact in the field.
