This paper makes a valuable contribution to provide a more clear understanding of generative adversarial network (GAN) training procedure. With the new insight of the training dynamics of GAN, as well as its variant, the authors reveal the reason that why the gradient is either vanishing in original GAN or unstable in its variant. More importantly, they also provide a way to avoid such difficulties by introducing perturbation. I believe this paper will inspire more principled research in this direction.

I accept this paper.

The main reason for my decision is that the authors provide a thorough analysis of the theoretical foundations of GANs and their variants, and they propose a novel solution to address the instability and vanishing gradient issues in GAN training.

The authors' analysis of the training dynamics of GANs and their variants is rigorous and well-motivated, and their solution of introducing perturbation to avoid the instability and vanishing gradient issues is innovative and well-supported by theoretical analysis.

One potential weakness of the paper is that it could benefit from more empirical studies to justify the proposed solution. While the authors provide some empirical results, such as Fig 2 and 3, more experiments would be helpful to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed solution in different settings.

* Consider providing more details on the implementation of the proposed solution, such as the choice of hyperparameters and the training procedure.
