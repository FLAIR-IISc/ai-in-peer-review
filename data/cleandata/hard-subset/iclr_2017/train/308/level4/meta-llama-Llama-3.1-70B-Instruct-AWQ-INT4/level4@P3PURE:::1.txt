This paper is a strong submission that provides valuable insights into one of the most important and recently introduced methods in neural networks - generative adversarial networks (GANs). The authors' theoretical analysis of the convergence of GANs and discussion of their stability are both crucial contributions. To the best of my knowledge, this is one of the first theoretical papers on GANs, and it stands out from most submissions in the field by providing deep theoretical insights into this architecture. The stability issues surrounding GANs are extremely important, as the initial proposed versions of the GAN architecture were highly unstable and did not perform well in practice. Theorems 2.4-2.6 are novel and introduce interesting mathematical techniques. I do have some minor technical questions regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5, but these do not detract from the overall quality of the paper. Overall, I believe this paper is a valuable contribution to the field and I hope it will be accepted.
