SUMMARY

This paper tackles important questions surrounding the challenges of training generative adversarial networks (GANs). It delves into the consequences of employing an asymmetric divergence function and identifies sources of instability in GAN training. The authors then propose an alternative approach using a smoothing technique.

PROS

The paper presents a strong theoretical foundation, raises intriguing questions, and provides insightful answers. It also makes innovative use of concepts from analysis and differential topology. Furthermore, the authors propose potential avenues to mitigate instability in GANs.

CONS

While the paper is well-written, it is somewhat lengthy and technically demanding. Certain sections and their consequences require further development, which is acceptable as a direction for future work.

MINOR COMMENTS

- Section 2.1 could be condensed, with proofs potentially relocated to the appendix.
- Section 3 offers a simple, intuitive solution to the problem at hand.
- On page 2, the second bullet point implies that P_g is smaller than the data distribution in some other x, resulting in a non-zero KL divergence.
- On page 2, the phrase "for not generating plausibly looking pictures" should be revised to "for generating not plausibly looking pictures."
- Lemma 1 appears to hold in greater generality.
- Theorem 2.1 seems to be a fundamental result in analysis, and referencing an existing proof could be more efficient.
- In Theorem 2.4, it would be helpful to remind the reader of the definition of p(z).
- Lemma 2 also appears to be a basic result in analysis, and a reference could replace the proof.
- It would be beneficial to specify the domain of the random variables.
- "relly" should be corrected to "rely."
- Theorem 2.2 assumes closed manifolds; it would be useful to clarify whether these manifolds have a boundary.
- Corollary 2.1 refers to "assumptions of Theorem 1.3," which is not present in the paper. This should be corrected.
- In Theorem 2.5, "Therefore" could be replaced with "Then."
- In Theorem 2.6, "Is a..." should be revised to "is a."
- The numbering of theorems is somewhat confusing and could be improved for clarity.
