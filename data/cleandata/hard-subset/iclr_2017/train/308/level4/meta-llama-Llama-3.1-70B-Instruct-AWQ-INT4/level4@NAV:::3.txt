REVIEW SUMMARY 
This paper tackles crucial challenges in training generative adversarial networks, examining the implications of utilizing an asymmetric divergence function and identifying sources of instability in GAN training. The authors then propose a novel approach using a smoothening method to address these issues.

PROS 
The paper excels in its theoretical foundation, posing pertinent questions and providing insightful answers. It also demonstrates an innovative application of concepts from analysis and differential topology, offering potential avenues for mitigating instability in GANs.

CONS 
The paper's length and technical complexity may deter some readers. While the authors have made significant progress, certain sections and consequences still require further development, which is a suitable direction for future research.

MINOR COMMENTS
- Consider condensing Section 2.1 by relocating proofs to the appendix.
- Section 3 presents a clear, intuitive, and straightforward solution.
- On page 2, the second bullet point implies that P_g is smaller than the data distribution in some other x, resulting in a non-zero KL divergence.
- On page 2, the phrase "for not generating plausibly looking pictures" should be revised to "for generating not plausibly looking pictures."
- Lemma 1 can be generalized further.
- Theorem 2.1 appears to be a fundamental concept in analysis, and referencing it might eliminate the need for a proof.
- In Theorem 2.4, it would be helpful to remind the reader of the definition of p(z).
- Lemma 2 also seems to be a basic concept in analysis, and a reference could replace the proof.
- Specify the domain of the random variables.
- Correct the typo "relly" to "rely."
- In Theorem 2.2, clarify whether the closed manifolds have boundaries or not.
- In Corollary 2.1, the reference to "assumptions of Theorem 1.3" is unclear, as Theorem 1.3 is not present.
- In Theorem 2.5, consider replacing "Therefore" with "Then."
- In Theorem 2.6, change "Is a..." to "is a."
- The numbering of theorems is confusing and may require reorganization.
