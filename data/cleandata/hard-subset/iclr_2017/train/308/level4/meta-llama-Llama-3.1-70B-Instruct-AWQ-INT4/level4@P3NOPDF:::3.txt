SUMMARY

This paper tackles important questions about the challenges of training generative adversarial networks (GANs). It discusses the consequences of using an asymmetric divergence function and identifies sources of instability in training GANs, ultimately proposing an alternative approach using a smoothing technique.

STRENGTHS

The paper excels in its theoretical foundations, posing interesting questions and providing insightful answers. It makes innovative use of concepts from analysis and differential topology, shedding new light on the subject. Furthermore, it proposes potential avenues to avoid instability in GANs, a significant contribution to the field.

WEAKNESSES

While the paper is well-written, some sections feel overly technical and lengthy. Certain parts and consequences require further development, which is acceptable as future work.

MINOR COMMENTS

- Section 2.1 could be shortened by moving proofs to the appendix, improving the overall flow of the paper.
- Section 3 presents a simple and intuitive solution to the problem at hand.
- On page 2, the second bullet point should be revised to: "This also means that P_g is smaller than the data distribution in some other x, which in turn will make the KL divergence non-zero."
- The phrase "for not generating plausibly looking pictures" on page 2 should be corrected to "for generating not plausibly looking pictures."
- Lemma 1 appears to hold in greater generality, which could be explored further.
- Theorem 2.1 seems to be a basic result from analysis, and a reference could potentially replace the proof.
- In Theorem 2.4, it would be helpful to remind the reader about the definition of p(z).
- Lemma 2 also appears to be a basic result from analysis, and a reference could potentially replace the proof.
- The domain of the random variables should be specified.
- A minor typo: "relly" should be corrected to "rely."
- Theorem 2.2 assumes the existence of closed manifolds; it would be beneficial to clarify whether these manifolds have boundaries.
- Corollary 2.1 refers to "assumptions of Theorem 1.3," but Theorem 1.3 is not present in the paper.
- In Theorem 2.5, "Therefore" could be replaced with "Then" for improved clarity.
- In Theorem 2.6, "Is a..." should be corrected to "is a."
- The numbering of theorems could be improved for better organization and clarity.
