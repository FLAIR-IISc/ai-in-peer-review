This paper is of high quality and warrants acceptance. I reaffirm my initial assessment.

The submission presents a rigorous examination of one of the most significant and recently developed methods in neural networks - generative adversarial networks (GANs). The authors provide a theoretical analysis of GAN convergence and discuss the stability of GANs, both of which are crucial aspects. To my knowledge, this is one of the pioneering theoretical papers on GANs, offering profound theoretical insights into this architecture, in contrast to many other submissions in the field. The stability issues surrounding GANs are of paramount importance, given that the initial versions of the GAN architecture were plagued by instability and poor practical performance. Theorems 2.4-2.6 introduce novel concepts and employ interesting mathematical techniques. I do have some minor technical questions regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5, but these do not detract from the overall quality of the paper.
