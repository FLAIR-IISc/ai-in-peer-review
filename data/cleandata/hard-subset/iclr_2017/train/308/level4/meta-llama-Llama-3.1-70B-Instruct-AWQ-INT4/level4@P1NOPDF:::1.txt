This is a strong submission regarding one of the most important and recently introduced methods in neural networks - generative adversarial networks. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the convergence of GANs and discuss their stability, both of which are crucial aspects of this architecture. To the best of my knowledge, this is one of the first theoretical papers on GANs, and it offers deep theoretical insights that set it apart from most submissions in the field.

The stability issues surrounding GANs are particularly significant, as the initial versions of the GAN architecture were notoriously unstable and did not perform well in practice. The authors' work in addressing these issues is, therefore, highly relevant. Theorems 2.4-2.6 are novel and introduce interesting mathematical techniques. I do have some technical questions regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5, but these are relatively minor and do not detract from the overall quality of the paper.

I believe this paper has the potential to make a valuable contribution to the field and I hope it will be accepted.
