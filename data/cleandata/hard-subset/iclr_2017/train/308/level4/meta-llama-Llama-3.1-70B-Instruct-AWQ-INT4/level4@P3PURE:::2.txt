This paper makes a valuable contribution to providing a clearer understanding of the generative adversarial network (GAN) training procedure. 

With the new insights into the training dynamics of GANs and their variants, the authors reveal the reasons behind the vanishing gradients in the original GAN and the unstable gradients in its variants. More importantly, they also provide a way to avoid such difficulties by introducing perturbations. I believe this paper will inspire more principled research in this direction.

I am particularly interested in the perturbation trick for avoiding gradient instability and vanishment. Notably, this trick bears some resemblance to the dropout technique, where the perturbation can be viewed as a Bernoulli distribution. A discussion on this connection would be beneficial. 

In addition to the theoretical analysis, I would like to see empirical studies that justify the effectiveness of this trick. It would be great if the authors could include additional experiments, similar to Figures 2 and 3, to compare the performance of the perturbated GAN with the original GAN and its variants.
