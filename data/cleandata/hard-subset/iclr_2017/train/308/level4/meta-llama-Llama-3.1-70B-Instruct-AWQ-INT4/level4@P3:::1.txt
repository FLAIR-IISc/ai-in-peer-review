This is a strong submission regarding one of the most important and recently introduced methods in neural networks - generative adversarial networks. The authors analyze theoretically the convergence of GANs and discuss the stability of GANs. Both are very important. To the best of my knowledge, this is one of the first theoretical papers about GANs and the paper, contrary to most of the submissions in the field, actually provides deep theoretical insight into this architecture. The stability issues regarding GANs are extremely important since the first proposed versions of GANs architecture were very unstable and did not work well in practice.

**Accept**

My decision is based on the following key reasons: 
1. The paper addresses a fundamental problem in the field of neural networks, specifically the theoretical understanding of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). 
2. The authors provide a rigorous analysis of the convergence and stability of GANs, which is a crucial aspect of this architecture.

**Supporting arguments:**

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors have done an excellent job of placing their work in the context of existing literature. The theoretical analysis is thorough, and the authors have provided several novel theorems (Theorems 2.4-2.6) that shed light on the convergence and stability of GANs. The mathematical techniques used in the proofs are interesting and demonstrate a deep understanding of the subject matter.

**Additional feedback:**

While the paper is well-written, there are a few minor issues that the authors may want to address. Specifically, I had some technical questions regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5. However, these are minor and do not detract from the overall quality of the paper.

**Questions for the authors:**

1. Can the authors provide more intuition behind the proof of Theorem 2.5? Specifically, how do they arrive at equation (3)?
2. How do the authors plan to extend their analysis to more complex GAN architectures, such as those used in practice?

Overall, this is a strong submission that provides significant theoretical insight into the convergence and stability of GANs. I believe it will be a valuable contribution to the field and look forward to seeing the authors' future work.
