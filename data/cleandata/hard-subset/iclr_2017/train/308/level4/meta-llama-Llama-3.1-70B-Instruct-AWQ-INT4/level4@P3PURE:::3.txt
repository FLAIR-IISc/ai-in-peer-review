SUMMARY 
This paper addresses important questions about the difficulties in training generative adversarial networks (GANs). It discusses the consequences of using an asymmetric divergence function and identifies sources of instability in training GANs, ultimately proposing an alternative approach using a smoothening method.

PROS 
The paper presents a solid theoretical foundation, explores relevant questions, and provides insightful answers. It also makes an interesting use of concepts from analysis and differential topology. Furthermore, the paper proposes avenues to avoid instability in GANs, contributing to the ongoing efforts to improve GAN training.

CONS 
While the paper is well-written, it is a bit too long and technical. Some parts and consequences still need to be further developed, which is a natural direction for future work.

MINOR COMMENTS

- Section 3 provides a nice, intuitive, and simple solution to the problem at hand.
- On page 2, in the second bullet point, it should be noted that this also implies that P_g is smaller than the data distribution in some other x, which in turn will make the KL divergence non-zero.
- On page 2, the sentence "for not generating plausibly looking pictures" should be corrected to "for generating not plausibly looking pictures."

- The proof of Theorem 2.1 appears to be basic analysis, and a reference could potentially replace the proof.

- In Theorem 2.5, "Therefore" could be replaced with "Then."
- In Theorem 2.6, "Is a" should be "is a."
