SUMMARY 
This paper addresses important questions about the difficulties in training generative adversarial networks (GANs). It discusses the consequences of using an asymmetric divergence function and sources of instability in training GANs, providing a solid understanding of these issues. The paper also proposes an alternative approach using a smoothening method to avoid these instability issues.

PROS 
The paper presents a thorough theoretical analysis of the problems in GAN training, answering interesting questions and providing nice answers. It makes an interesting use of concepts from analysis and differential topology. Additionally, the paper proposes avenues to avoid instability in GANs, which is a significant contribution to the field.

CONS 
The paper is a bit too long and technical, which may make it challenging for non-experts to follow. Some parts and consequences still need to be further developed, which is perfectly fine for future work.

DECISION 
Based on the paper's contributions and the importance of the questions it addresses, I decide to accept this paper.

SUPPORTING ARGUMENTS 
The paper's main strengths lie in its thorough theoretical analysis and the proposal of a novel approach to address the instability issues in GAN training. The authors provide a solid understanding of the problems in GAN training, which is essential for making progress in this field.

The paper's use of concepts from analysis and differential topology is also noteworthy. The authors demonstrate a deep understanding of these concepts and apply them effectively to the problem at hand.

While the paper is technical and may be challenging to follow at times, the authors provide clear explanations and proofs, making it possible for experts in the field to understand and appreciate the contributions.

ADDITIONAL FEEDBACK 
To improve the paper, the authors could consider shortening Section 2.1 and moving some of the proofs to the appendix. This would make the paper more concise and easier to follow.

Additionally, the authors could provide more context and explanations for the non-expert readers. For example, they could elaborate on the concepts from analysis and differential topology and provide more intuitive explanations for the proposed approach.
