This paper makes a valuable contribution to our understanding of the generative adversarial network (GAN) training procedure, providing new insights into the training dynamics of GANs and their variants. The authors successfully identify the reasons behind the vanishing or unstable gradients in original GANs and their variants, respectively. Moreover, they propose a solution to overcome these difficulties by introducing perturbations. I believe this work will inspire more principled research in this direction.

I found the perturbation trick for avoiding gradient instability and vanishment particularly interesting. Notably, this trick bears some resemblance to the dropout technique, where the perturbation can be viewed as a Bernoulli distribution. A discussion on this connection would be beneficial. In addition to the theoretical analysis, it would be helpful to include empirical studies to justify the effectiveness of this trick. I suggest adding experiments similar to Figures 2 and 3, but for the perturbed GAN, to facilitate comparison.
