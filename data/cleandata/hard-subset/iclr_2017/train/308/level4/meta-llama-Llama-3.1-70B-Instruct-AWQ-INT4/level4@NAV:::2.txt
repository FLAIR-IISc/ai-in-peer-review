This paper offers a significant contribution to elucidating the generative adversarial network (GAN) training procedure, providing novel insights into the training dynamics of GAN and its variants. The authors successfully identify the underlying causes of vanishing gradients in the original GAN and unstable gradients in its variant, and propose a perturbation-based approach to mitigate these issues. This work is poised to stimulate further research in this area, guided by a more profound understanding of GAN training. 

The introduction of the perturbation technique to stabilize gradients is a compelling aspect of this paper, bearing resemblance to the dropout technique, where the perturbation can be interpreted as a Bernoulli distribution. A more in-depth discussion of this connection would be beneficial. To complement the theoretical analysis, it would be valuable to include empirical studies validating the effectiveness of this approach. The addition of experimental results, similar to those presented in Figures 2 and 3, but for the perturbated GAN, would provide a comprehensive comparison and further substantiate the authors' claims.
