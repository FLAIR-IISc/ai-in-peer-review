This paper makes valuable contributions to providing a clearer understanding of the Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) training method.

With new insights into the training dynamics of GANs and their variants, the authors reveal why the gradients of the original GAN disappear or become unstable in their variants. More importantly, they also provide a way to avoid such difficulties through perturbation induction. I believe this paper will inspire more policy-oriented research in this area.

I am very interested in the perturbation technique to avoid gradient instability and vanishing. In fact, it is quite related to the dropout technique, where perturbation can be viewed as a Bernoulli distribution. It would be great if this connection could be discussed. With theoretical analysis, is there any empirical research to support this technique? Add some experiments like Figure 2 and 3 for perturbed GANs for comparison?
