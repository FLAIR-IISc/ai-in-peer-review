This paper makes valuable contributions to providing a clearer understanding of the Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) training method.

With new insights into the training dynamics of GANs and their variants, the authors reveal the reason why gradients in the original GAN become vanishing or unstable in their variants. More importantly, they also provide a way to avoid such difficulties through perturbation induction. I believe this paper will inspire further policy-oriented research in this area.

I am very interested in the perturbation technique to avoid gradient instability and vanishing. In fact, it is quite related to the dropout technique, where perturbation can be seen as a Bernoulli distribution. It would be great if this connection could be discussed. Along with theoretical analysis, is there any empirical research to support this technique? Please add some experiments like Figure 2 and 3 for perturbed GANs for comparison?
