- The paper offers a rigorous theoretical framework attributing GAN instability to the geometry of real and generated distributions lying on low-dimensional manifolds.  
- It shows such manifold supports permit a "perfect" discriminator, causing the Jensen–Shannon divergence to saturate.  
- The authors prove a near-optimal discriminator yields vanishing gradients for the original minimax GAN objective.  
- They prove the commonly used −log D objective induces generator gradients with infinite variance (Cauchy), explaining extreme instability.  
- The proposed fix is adding continuous noise to discriminator inputs to "smear" distributions and prevent perfect separation.  
- Analysis shows input noise produces smooth, well-behaved gradients that pull generated samples toward the data manifold (contrastive-like effect).  
- They connect the noisy-training procedure to minimizing a bound on the Wasserstein distance, offering a more appropriate metric for manifold-supported distributions.  
- Decision: Accept, based on a novel diagnosis and a theoretically grounded solution.  
- Strength: Clear logical flow from the manifold assumption (Lemma 1) to results about discriminator gradients (Theorems 2.1/2.2).  
- Key formal results: Theorem 2.4 (vanishing gradients), Theorem 2.6 (infinite-variance gradients), and Theorem 2.5 (clarifying what −log D optimizes).  
- Supporting results for the solution: Theorems 3.1–3.3 justify instance noise and its Wasserstein interpretation.  
- Suggested improvement: discuss relations to other regularizers (e.g., dropout, augmentation) and clarify unique benefits of continuous noise.  
- Suggested improvement: provide practical guidance on annealing the noise variance for tightening the Wasserstein bound.  
- Suggested improvement: add higher-level intuition in the main text for technical proofs (e.g., Lemma 2 on transversal intersection).  
- Questions for authors: provide empirical experiments showing gradient-norm stability with noisy inputs and evidence that the method improves sample quality or reduces mode collapse on benchmarks.
