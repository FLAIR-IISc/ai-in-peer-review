- Provides a rigorous theoretical analysis showing GAN instability when data and generator lie on disjoint low-dimensional manifolds.
- Proves an optimal discriminator can perfectly separate supports, causing either vanishing or highly unstable gradients depending on the cost function.
- Shows the original log(1−D) generator cost yields vanishing gradients as the discriminator improves (Theorem 2.4).
- Analyzes the −log(D) alternative, relating it to reverse KL and JSD, explaining mode dropping and showing infinite-variance updates near an optimal/noisy discriminator (Theorems 2.5, 2.6).
- Proposes adding continuous noise to discriminator inputs to make distributions absolutely continuous and produce smooth, informative gradients (Section 3, Theorem 3.1).
- Proves a bound relating the Wasserstein distance between true distributions to noise variance and the JSD of smoothed distributions, motivating training with annealed noise (Theorem 3.3, Corollary 3.2).
- Recommendation: accept—paper combines a clear theoretical explanation of GAN failure modes with a practical, principled fix tied to better distance metrics.
- Suggests improving readability by moving standard proofs to the appendix to focus on core results.
- Recommends clarifying the KL divergence discussion to note that P_g must integrate to 1 and thus high mass in low-data regions implies low mass elsewhere.
- Notes minor corrections needed (theorem numbering, typos, wording) to improve presentation.
- Asks authors to clarify whether “closed” manifolds in Theorem 2.2 exclude boundaries or are intended to include manifolds with boundaries.
