This paper makes a valuable contribution by providing a clearer understanding of the training procedure for Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). Building on new insights into the training dynamics of GANs and their variants, the authors reveal why the generator's gradient either vanishes in the original GAN formulation or becomes unstable in many of its variants. More importantly, they propose a way to avoid these difficulties by introducing a perturbation to the generator's samples. I believe this paper will inspire more principled research in this direction.

I am particularly interested in the proposed perturbation technique for avoiding gradient instability and vanishing. This approach seems conceptually related to dropout, where the injected noise can be viewed as being drawn from a Bernoulli distribution. It would strengthen the paper to include a discussion of this potential connection.

Beyond the theoretical analysis, the paper would benefit from a more thorough empirical study to validate this technique. The theoretical arguments are compelling, but they would be significantly strengthened by experimental results. For instance, could the authors add experiments for the perturbed GAN that are analogous to those presented in Figures 2 and 3? This would allow for a direct comparison and provide strong empirical evidence for the method's effectiveness.
