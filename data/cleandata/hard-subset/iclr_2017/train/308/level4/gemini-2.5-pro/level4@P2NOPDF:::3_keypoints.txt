- Paper analyzes GAN training instabilities from asymmetric divergence functions and proposes a smoothing technique to mitigate them.
- Strong theoretical depth with rigorous analysis addressing stability and convergence of GANs.
- Novel application of mathematical analysis and differential topology offers a fresh perspective.
- Provides a constructive, well-motivated method for improving training stability.
- Presentation is dense and technical; readability could be improved and overall length reduced.
- Practical implications and consequences of the theoretical findings are underexplored and warrant further work.
- Recommend adopting a standard, sequential numbering scheme for theorems, lemmas, and corollaries.
- Suggest moving lengthy proofs in Section 2.1 to an appendix to improve main-text flow.
- Recommend citing standard references for routine proofs (e.g., Theorem 2.1 and Lemma 2) to reduce redundancy.
- Note: the generator assigning high probability to samples the discriminator finds unlikely implies P_g must have lower density elsewhere, contributing to non-zero KL divergence.
- Clarify whether “closed manifolds” in Theorem 2.2 are assumed to have no boundary.
- Remind readers of the definition of p(z) (the prior over latent space) in Theorem 2.4.
- Lemma 1 appears to hold under more general conditions—consider presenting a more general form.
- Explicitly specify domains and supports of random variables and probability distributions throughout the theoretical sections.
- Typo/wording: rephrase “for not generating plausibly looking pictures” to “for generating implausible pictures.”
- Fix reference in Corollary 2.1 to “assumptions of Theorem 1.3” (Theorem 1.3 not found).
- Wording fixes: in Theorem 2.5 consider replacing “Therefore” with “Then”; in Theorem 2.6 start the sentence with lowercase “is a”; correct “relly” to “rely” on page 7.
