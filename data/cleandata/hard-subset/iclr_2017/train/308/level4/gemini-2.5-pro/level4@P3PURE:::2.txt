This paper makes a valuable contribution by providing a clearer understanding of the Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) training procedure. Drawing on new insights into the training dynamics of GANs and their variants, the authors reveal why the original GAN formulation suffers from vanishing gradients, while its variants can exhibit unstable gradients. More importantly, they propose a method to mitigate these issues by introducing perturbation. I believe this paper will inspire more principled research in this direction.

The proposed perturbation technique for stabilizing training is particularly interesting. This method seems closely related to dropout, where the perturbation can be viewed as sampling from a Bernoulli distribution. It would be beneficial if the authors could discuss this connection.

Beyond the theoretical analysis, the paper would be strengthened by an empirical study to validate this technique. Could the authors add experiments for the perturbed GAN, similar to those presented in Figures 2 and 3, to allow for a direct comparison?
