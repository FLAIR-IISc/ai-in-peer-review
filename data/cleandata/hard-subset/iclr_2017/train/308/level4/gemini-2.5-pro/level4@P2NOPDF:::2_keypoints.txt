- Paper analyzes GAN training dynamics, identifies root causes of vanishing gradients in original GANs and unstable gradients in variants, and proposes a perturbation technique as a remedy.

- Provides a clear and insightful theoretical explanation for widely observed GAN training instabilities.

- Analysis directly motivates a practical perturbation-based solution to stabilize GAN training.

- Claims a significant potential impact by encouraging more rigorous, theory-driven GAN research.

- Suggest adding a discussion connecting the perturbation method to existing regularization techniques (e.g., dropout/Bernoulli perturbations) and highlighting similarities/differences.

- Major weakness: lack of empirical validation—authors should add experiments (e.g., analogues to Figures 2 and 3) on perturbed GANs to demonstrate practical effectiveness and stability.
