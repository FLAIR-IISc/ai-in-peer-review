### **Summary**

This paper addresses important questions regarding the difficulties of training Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). The authors discuss the consequences of using asymmetric divergence functions and identify sources of training instability. They then propose an alternative training procedure based on a smoothing approach to mitigate these issues.

### **Strengths**

*   The paper is theoretically well-grounded, posing pertinent questions and providing insightful answers regarding GAN training dynamics.
*   The authors make interesting and effective use of concepts from analysis and differential topology to support their arguments.
*   The work proposes promising avenues for avoiding the instability commonly encountered when training GANs.

### **Weaknesses**

*   The paper is somewhat lengthy and technically dense, which may hinder its accessibility.
*   Certain sections and the implications of the theoretical results could benefit from further development, though this is acknowledged as a direction for future work.

### **Detailed Comments and Questions**

*   **Section 2.1:** This section could be shortened to improve readability. Consider moving all proofs to the appendix to streamline the main text.
*   **Section 3:** The solution presented in this section is commendably intuitive and simple.
*   **Page 2, Second Bullet Point:** It is noted that P_g can be larger than the data distribution. It is worth also stating that this implies P_g must be smaller than the data distribution for some other x, which is another factor that ensures the KL divergence remains non-zero.
*   **Lemma 1:** This lemma appears to hold under more general conditions than those stated.
*   **Theorem 2.1 & Lemma 2:** These results and their proofs seem to cover standard material from basic analysis. To improve conciseness, the authors could consider citing a relevant textbook or reference in place of providing full proofs.
*   **Theorem 2.2:** It should be clarified whether the closed manifolds under consideration have a boundary.
*   **Theorem 2.4:** In the statement of the theorem, it would be helpful to briefly remind the reader of the definition of p(z).
*   **General Clarity:** Throughout the paper, it would be beneficial to explicitly specify the domain of all random variables.
*   **Theorem Numbering:** The numbering scheme for theorems, lemmas, and corollaries is confusing and should be revised for clarity. For example, in Corollary 2.1, the text refers to the "assumptions of Theorem 1.3," but I was unable to locate a Theorem 1.3.

### **Typos and Formatting Suggestions**

*   **Page 2:** The phrase "for not generating plausibly looking pictures" should be corrected to "for generating not-plausible-looking pictures."
*   **Theorem 2.5:** "Therefore" could be replaced with "Then" for a more standard theorem-statement style.
*   **Theorem 2.6:** The capitalization should be corrected: "Is a..." should be "is a...".
*   A typo was found: "relly" should be "rely".
