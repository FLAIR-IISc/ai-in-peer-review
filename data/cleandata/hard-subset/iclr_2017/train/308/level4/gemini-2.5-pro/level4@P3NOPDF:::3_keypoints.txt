- Paper analyzes GAN instability from asymmetric divergences and vanishing gradients when data and generator supports don't overlap; proposes adding input noise to smooth distributions and stabilize training.

- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis addressing core GAN training problems.

- Strength: novel application of mathematical analysis and differential topology to formalize instability intuitions.

- Strength: practical implication — a simple noise-based method to encourage support overlap and more stable training.

- Weakness: paper is dense and highly technical; Section 2 needs streamlining for broader accessibility.

- Weakness: empirical validation and practical exploration of theoretical claims are limited compared to the heavy theoretical focus.

- Suggestion: move dense proofs (Lemma 1, Theorem 2.1, Lemma 2) to the appendix and cite standard references in the main text.

- Issue: theorem and corollary numbering is confusing/incorrect (e.g., Corollary 2.1 references a non-existent Theorem 1.3); renumber for consistency.

- Clarification: explicitly state the converse that if P_g is high where P_data is low then P_g must be lower elsewhere, affecting KL divergence.

- Clarification: state whether closed manifolds in Theorem 2.2 are assumed to have a boundary.

- Clarification: briefly remind the reader of the definition of p(z) in Theorem 2.4.

- Suggestion: consider whether Lemma 1 holds under more general conditions and comment on its full generality.

- Suggestion: specify the domains of the random variables used throughout the analysis for clarity.

- Minor wording/typos to fix: rephrase "for not generating plausibly looking pictures" to "for generating not plausibly looking pictures"; correct "relly" → "rely".

- Minor wording: in Theorem 2.5 replace "Therefore" with "Then"; in Theorem 2.6 change "Is a..." to lowercase "is a...".
