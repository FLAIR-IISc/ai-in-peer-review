- Strong recommendation for acceptance; reviewer maintains original positive evaluation.
- Presents a robust theoretical analysis of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs).
- Focuses on two critical issues: convergence and stability of GANs.
- Claims novelty as one of the first papers providing deep theoretical insight, contrasting with mainly empirical prior work.
- Stability investigation is particularly valuable given early GANs' notorious instability in training.
- Primary theoretical contributions are Theorems 2.4–2.6, which are novel and introduce compelling mathematical techniques.
- Reviewer has a minor technical question about the proof of Theorem 2.5, but it does not diminish the paper’s overall strength.
