- Presents theoretical analysis of GAN training dynamics, explaining vanishing and unstable gradients.  
- Proposes a perturbation term during training to mitigate gradient pathologies.  
- Claims the work offers important insights that could lead to more principled, stable GAN training.  
- Main strength: clear theoretical dissection of common GAN gradient issues, grounding observations previously empirical.  
- Novelty: the perturbation-based stabilization is well-motivated and a concrete method addressing root causes.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of connections to existing regularization/noise-injection methods (e.g., dropout) and whether it generalizes or differs.  
- Weakness: no empirical validation—needs experiments to demonstrate practical effectiveness.  
- Weakness: should include direct experimental comparisons (e.g., analogs of Figures 2 and 3) for GANs trained with the proposed perturbation to support theoretical claims.
