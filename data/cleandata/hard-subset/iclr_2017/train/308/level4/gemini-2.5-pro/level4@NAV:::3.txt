**SUMMARY**

This work investigates significant challenges associated with the training of generative adversarial networks. The submission delves into the ramifications of using an asymmetric divergence function and explores sources of training instability. To address these issues, the authors propose an alternative method that incorporates a smoothing strategy.

**PROS**

*   The paper is theoretically well-grounded, posing pertinent questions and offering insightful answers.
*   The work creatively applies principles from analysis and differential topology.
*   It presents potential strategies for mitigating training instability in GANs.

**CONS**

*   The presentation is quite dense and the manuscript's length could be reduced for clarity.
*   Certain sections and the implications of the findings could be more thoroughly explored, though this is acknowledged as a suitable direction for future research.

**MINOR COMMENTS**

*   In Section 2.1, consider shortening the content, perhaps by relocating the proofs to an appendix to improve the main text's flow.
*   The solution presented in Section 3 is notable for its simplicity and intuitive nature.
*   Regarding the second bullet point on page 2: It follows that P_g would be smaller than the data distribution for other values of x, which would result in a non-zero KL divergence.
*   On page 2, the phrase "for not generating plausibly looking pictures" should be corrected to "for generating not plausibly looking pictures."
*   The statement in Lemma 1 could likely be generalized further.
*   The proof for Theorem 2.1 could potentially be omitted and replaced with a citation, as it appears to be a standard result from analysis.
*   For Theorem 2.4, it would be helpful to briefly redefine or remind the reader of the distribution p(z).
*   Similarly, Lemma 2 covers a fundamental concept in analysis; its proof might be replaced by a suitable reference.
*   Please specify the domain for all random variables.
*   Typo: "relly" should be "rely".
*   Regarding Theorem 2.2, please clarify whether the closed manifolds under consideration are with or without a boundary.
*   In Corollary 2.1, the text refers to "assumptions of Theorem 1.3," but I was unable to locate a Theorem 1.3 in the manuscript.
*   In Theorem 2.5, consider replacing "Therefore" with "Then" for better logical flow.
*   In Theorem 2.6, the sentence beginning "Is a..." should likely start with a lowercase "is a".
*   The numbering scheme for theorems is confusing and could be revised for clarity.
