SUMMARY
This paper investigates key challenges in training Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), discussing the consequences of using asymmetric divergence functions and identifying sources of training instability. The authors then propose a novel training approach based on instance noise to address these issues.

PROS
- The paper is theoretically well-grounded, asks insightful questions about the difficulties in GAN training, and provides compelling answers.
- It makes effective use of concepts from analysis and differential topology to build its arguments.
- It proposes concrete methods to mitigate instability in GAN training.

CONS
- The manuscript is somewhat lengthy and technically dense, which may hinder its accessibility.
- While the paper lays a strong foundation, some of the presented ideas and their consequences require further development, though this is acknowledged as a direction for future work.

MINOR COMMENTS
- Section 2.1 could be shortened for improved readability. Consider moving the proofs to the appendix.
- The solution presented in Section 3 is commendably simple and intuitive.
- On page 2, regarding the second bullet point: if P_g is larger than the data distribution for some x, it must be smaller for some other x. This imbalance is what ensures the KL divergence remains non-zero.
- On page 2, the phrase "for not generating plausibly looking pictures" should be corrected to "for generating pictures that are not plausible."
- Lemma 1 appears to hold under more general conditions than those stated.
- Theorem 2.1 is a standard result from analysis. The proof could be replaced by a citation to a standard text.
- In the statement of Theorem 2.4, it would be helpful to briefly remind the reader of the definition of p(z).
- Similar to Theorem 2.1, Lemma 2 is a basic result from analysis, and its proof could be replaced by a citation.
- The domains of the random variables should be explicitly specified.
- Typo: "relly" should be "rely".
- Regarding Theorem 2.2, please clarify whether the closed manifolds are assumed to be with or without a boundary.
- In Corollary 2.1, the text refers to the "assumptions of Theorem 1.3," but there is no Theorem 1.3 in the manuscript. Please correct the reference.
- In Theorem 2.5, consider replacing "Therefore" with "Then" for better logical flow.
- In Theorem 2.6, the sentence begins with "Is a...", which appears to be a capitalization error.
- The numbering of theorems, lemmas, and corollaries is inconsistent and confusing. Please revise for clarity.
