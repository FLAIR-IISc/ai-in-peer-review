This paper makes a valuable contribution by providing a clearer understanding of the training dynamics of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). Leveraging new insights into the training process for GANs and their variants, the authors explain why the original GAN formulation is prone to vanishing gradients, while its variants can suffer from gradient instability. More importantly, they propose a method to overcome these difficulties by introducing perturbations. I believe this work will inspire more principled research in this area.

The proposed perturbation method for avoiding gradient instability and vanishing gradients is particularly interesting. This approach is reminiscent of the dropout technique, where the perturbation can be viewed as noise drawn from a Bernoulli distribution. It would strengthen the paper to discuss this connection. Furthermore, while the theoretical analysis is valuable, it would be more compelling if supported by an empirical study. I suggest adding experiments for the perturbed GAN, analogous to those presented in Figures 2 and 3, to facilitate a direct comparison.
