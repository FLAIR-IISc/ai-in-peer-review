- Paper studies GAN training difficulties from asymmetric divergence functions and proposes a smoothing-based alternative training procedure.
- The work is theoretically well-grounded and asks pertinent questions about GAN dynamics.
- Authors effectively use analysis and differential topology to support arguments.
- Proposed approach offers promising ways to reduce GAN training instability.
- Paper is long and technically dense, reducing accessibility.
- Some sections and implications need more development (acknowledged as future work).
- Section 2.1 should be shortened; move proofs to the appendix to improve readability.
- Section 3 presents an intuitive and simple solution.
- Clarify that if P_g is larger than the data distribution at some x, it must be smaller for some other x, ensuring KL divergence remains non-zero.
- Lemma 1 likely holds under more general conditions than stated.
- Theorem 2.1 and Lemma 2 cover standard analysis material; consider citing a textbook instead of full proofs.
- Theorem 2.2 needs clarification on whether the closed manifolds have a boundary.
- Theorem 2.4 should briefly remind the reader of the definition of p(z).
- Explicitly specify the domain of all random variables throughout the paper.
- Theorem/lemma/corollary numbering and references are confusing and should be revised (e.g., Corollary 2.1 references an unlocated Theorem 1.3).
