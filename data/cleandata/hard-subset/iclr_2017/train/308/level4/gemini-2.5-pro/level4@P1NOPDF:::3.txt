**SUMMARY**

This paper investigates key challenges in training Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). The authors discuss the consequences of using asymmetric divergence functions and identify sources of training instability. To address these issues, they propose an alternative training objective based on a smoothing approach, providing a strong theoretical justification for their method.

**STRENGTHS**

*   The paper is theoretically well-grounded, asking insightful questions about the challenges of training GANs and providing compelling answers.
*   The authors make novel and interesting use of concepts from analysis and differential topology to formalize their arguments.
*   The work proposes a promising avenue for mitigating the training instability commonly observed in GANs, with the solution in Section 3 being particularly elegant and intuitive.

**WEAKNESSES**

*   The paper is quite dense and lengthy, which may make the technical arguments challenging for some readers to follow.
*   While the theoretical contributions are strong, certain aspects and their practical consequences could be developed further. The authors acknowledge this, and it presents a clear path for future research.

**DETAILED COMMENTS AND SUGGESTIONS**

**Clarity and Presentation:**
*   The theorem and lemma numbering (e.g., 2.1, 2.4, 2.2, 2.5, 2.6) appears non-sequential, which can be confusing. Adopting a standard sequential numbering scheme would improve readability.
*   Section 2.1 could be shortened for improved flow. For instance, the proofs could be moved to the appendix to allow the main text to focus on the core insights.
*   For greater clarity, please specify the domains of the random variables used throughout the paper.
*   In the statement of Theorem 2.4, it would be helpful to briefly remind the reader of the definition of p(z).
*   Regarding Theorem 2.2, please clarify whether the closed manifolds are assumed to be without boundary.

**Technical Suggestions:**
*   The proofs for Theorem 2.1 and Lemma 2 cover standard results from analysis. Citing a relevant textbook or paper could suffice in place of full proofs, which would help condense the paper.
*   Lemma 1 appears to hold under more general conditions than those stated. It might be beneficial to comment on this potential for generalization.
*   On page 2, in the second bullet point, it is worth noting that if P_g is larger than the data distribution for some x, it must also be smaller for some other x (assuming both are valid probability distributions). This imbalance would also contribute to a non-zero KL divergence.

**Minor Corrections:**
*   In Corollary 2.1, there is a reference to "the assumptions of Theorem 1.3," but I could not locate a Theorem 1.3 in the paper. This appears to be a typo.
*   On page 2, the phrase "for not generating plausibly looking pictures" is grammatically awkward. It could be rephrased to "for generating implausible pictures."
*   In the statement of Theorem 2.5, using "Then" instead of "Therefore" might read more naturally.
*   In Theorem 2.6, the capitalization of "Is a..." should be corrected to "is a...".
*   There is a typo: "relly" should be "rely".
