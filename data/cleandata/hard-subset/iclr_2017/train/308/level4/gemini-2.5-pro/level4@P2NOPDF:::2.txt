This paper presents a valuable analysis of the training dynamics of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). By providing novel insights into the GAN training procedure and its variants, the authors identify the root causes of two major issues: vanishing gradients in the original GAN formulation and unstable gradients in subsequent variants. More importantly, the paper proposes a solution to mitigate these training difficulties by introducing a perturbation technique. I believe this work provides an important theoretical contribution that will inspire more principled research in this area.

**Strengths:**

*   The paper offers a clear and insightful theoretical explanation for the widely observed but poorly understood training instabilities in GANs.
*   The analysis directly motivates a practical solution—the introduction of perturbations—to stabilize the training process.
*   The work has the potential to have a significant impact on the field by encouraging more rigorous and theoretically-grounded approaches to developing and training GANs.

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

1.  **Connection to Existing Regularization Techniques:** I am particularly interested in the proposed perturbation method for preventing gradient vanishing and instability. This technique bears a conceptual resemblance to dropout, where the perturbation can be viewed as being drawn from a Bernoulli distribution. A discussion of this connection, exploring potential similarities or differences, would be a valuable addition and would help place the proposed method within the context of existing regularization techniques.

2.  **Empirical Validation:** The primary weakness is the lack of empirical validation for the proposed perturbation method. While the theoretical analysis is compelling, the claims would be significantly strengthened by experimental results that demonstrate the practical effectiveness of the technique. I strongly suggest the authors add experiments to support their theoretical findings. For instance, including experiments analogous to Figures 2 and 3, but applied to the proposed perturbed GAN, would allow for a direct comparison and provide clear evidence of the method's ability to stabilize training dynamics.
