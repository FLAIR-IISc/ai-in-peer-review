- Paper makes a significant contribution by elucidating GAN training dynamics and offering a novel perspective on both GANs and variants.  
- Authors diagnose underlying causes of vanishing and unstable gradients.  
- Proposes a practical perturbation technique to stabilize gradients and circumvent those problems.  
- Work could stimulate more theoretically grounded research in GAN training.  
- Perturbation technique conceptually parallels dropout-like noise (e.g., Bernoulli sampling) and this connection should be discussed.  
- Theoretical analysis is insightful but lacks empirical validation of the proposed perturbation.  
- Recommend additional experiments on perturbed GANs analogous to Figures 2 and 3 to provide direct comparative assessment and stronger justification.
