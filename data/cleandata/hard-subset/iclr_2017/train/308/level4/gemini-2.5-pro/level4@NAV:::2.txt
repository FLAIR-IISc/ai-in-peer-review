This paper presents a significant contribution by elucidating the training dynamics of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). By offering a novel perspective on the training process for both GANs and their variants, the authors successfully diagnose the underlying causes of vanishing and unstable gradients. Furthermore, they propose a practical solution to circumvent these problems through the introduction of a perturbation. This work has the potential to stimulate more theoretically-grounded research in this domain.

The proposed perturbation technique for stabilizing gradients is particularly intriguing. There appears to be a conceptual parallel to the dropout method, where the added noise could be interpreted as a sample from a Bernoulli distribution. The paper would be strengthened by a discussion exploring this potential connection. Additionally, while the theoretical analysis is insightful, the paper would benefit from empirical validation of the proposed technique. I recommend including additional experiments for the perturbed GAN that parallel the analyses presented in Figures 2 and 3. This would provide a direct comparative assessment and more robustly justify the method's effectiveness.
