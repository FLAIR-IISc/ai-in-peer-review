This is a strong submission that makes a significant contribution to our understanding of generative adversarial networks (GANs), one of the most important and influential methods recently introduced in machine learning. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the convergence and stability of GANs—two issues of critical importance for this class of models. The investigation into stability is particularly welcome, given that early GAN architectures were notoriously unstable and difficult to train in practice.

To the best of my knowledge, this is one of the first papers to offer such a rigorous theoretical treatment of GANs. In contrast to the majority of submissions in this area, this work provides deep theoretical insight into the architecture. The contributions in Theorems 2.4-2.6 appear novel, and the mathematical techniques introduced are interesting. While I have some minor technical questions regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5, these do not detract from the overall strength and quality of this submission. I maintain my positive evaluation of this paper.
