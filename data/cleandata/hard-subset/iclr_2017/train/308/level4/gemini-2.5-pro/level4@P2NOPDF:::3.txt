### **Summary**

This paper investigates fundamental difficulties encountered in the training of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the consequences of using asymmetric divergence functions and identify key sources of training instability. Building on this analysis, the paper proposes a novel smoothing technique as a practical solution to mitigate these issues.

### **Strengths**

*   **Theoretical Depth:** The paper is theoretically well-grounded, addressing important and challenging questions about the stability and convergence of GANs. The authors provide insightful answers supported by rigorous arguments.
*   **Novel Application of Mathematical Concepts:** The work makes excellent use of concepts from mathematical analysis and differential topology to formalize the problems in GAN training. This approach provides a fresh and valuable perspective on the topic.
*   **Constructive Solutions:** Beyond diagnosing the problems, the paper proposes a concrete method for improving training stability. The proposed avenues for avoiding instability are well-motivated by the theoretical discussion.

### **Weaknesses**

*   **Clarity and Conciseness:** The paper is quite dense and technical in some sections, which may make it less accessible to a broader audience. The overall length could be reduced to improve readability.
*   **Scope of Consequences:** While the paper lays a strong theoretical foundation, some of the practical implications and consequences of the findings could be explored more deeply. However, this is a reasonable direction for future work.

### **Detailed Comments and Suggestions**

The solution proposed in Section 3 is notable for its simplicity and intuitive appeal. The following are more specific suggestions for improving the manuscript:

**Suggestions for Restructuring and Clarity:**

*   **Theorem Numbering:** The current numbering scheme for theorems, lemmas, and corollaries is confusing. Adopting a more standard, sequential numbering system (e.g., Theorem 1, Lemma 1, Corollary 1, Theorem 2, etc.) would significantly improve readability and ease of reference.
*   **Section 2.1:** This section is quite long. To improve the flow of the main text, consider moving the detailed proofs to an appendix.
*   **Standard Proofs:** The proofs for Theorem 2.1 and Lemma 2 appear to be based on standard results from mathematical analysis. The paper could be made more concise by citing a standard reference or textbook in place of providing these full proofs.

**Technical and Clarifying Questions:**

*   **Page 2, Second Bullet Point:** The text notes that the generator may assign high probability to samples the discriminator considers unlikely. It is also worth mentioning the inverse: this implies that the generated distribution P_g must have lower density than the data distribution elsewhere to integrate to one, which also contributes to a non-zero KL divergence.
*   **Theorem 2.2:** For clarity, please specify whether the "closed manifolds" are assumed to be without boundary.
*   **Theorem 2.4:** In the statement of the theorem, it would be helpful to briefly remind the reader of the definition of p(z) (e.g., the prior distribution over the latent space).
*   **Generality of Lemma 1:** Lemma 1 appears to hold under more general conditions than those stated. The authors could consider presenting it in a more general form.
*   **Variable Domains:** Throughout the theoretical sections, please explicitly specify the domains and supports of the random variables and probability distributions being discussed.

**Minor Typos and Wording Suggestions:**

*   **Page 2:** "for not generating plausibly looking pictures" should be rephrased for clarity, for instance, to "for generating implausible pictures."
*   **Corollary 2.1:** The text refers to the "assumptions of Theorem 1.3," but I was unable to locate a Theorem 1.3. Please verify the reference number.
*   **Theorem 2.5:** Consider replacing "Therefore" with "Then" for a more standard phrasing.
*   **Theorem 2.6:** The sentence beginning "Is a..." should likely start with a lowercase "is a."
*   On page 7, "relly" should be corrected to "rely."
