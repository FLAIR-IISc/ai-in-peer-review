- Paper studies GAN training instabilities from asymmetric divergence functions and proposes a smoothing-based alternative objective with theoretical justification.  
- Strength: Theoretical grounding is strong, asking insightful questions and providing compelling answers.  
- Strength: Novel use of analysis and differential topology to formalize arguments.  
- Strength: Proposed smoothing solution (Section 3) is elegant, intuitive, and promising for mitigating GAN instability.  
- Weakness: Paper is dense and lengthy, making the technical arguments hard to follow for some readers.  
- Weakness: Practical consequences and extensions of the theory are underdeveloped and need further work.  
- Clarity: Theorem and lemma numbering is non-sequential and confusing; use sequential numbering.  
- Clarity: Section 2.1 is long; move proofs to the appendix to improve flow.  
- Clarity: Specify domains of all random variables for greater clarity.  
- Clarity: Remind readers of the definition of p(z) in Theorem 2.4.  
- Clarity: Clarify whether closed manifolds in Theorem 2.2 are assumed to have no boundary.  
- Technical: Replace lengthy proofs of standard analysis results (Theorem 2.1, Lemma 2) with citations to textbooks/papers.  
- Technical: Note that Lemma 1 may hold under more general conditions and comment on possible generalizations.  
- Technical: Add a remark that if P_g exceeds data distribution at some x it must be lower elsewhere, contributing to non-zero KL divergence.  
- Minor: Fix reference typo to "Theorem 1.3" in Corollary 2.1.  
- Minor: Rephrase "for not generating plausibly looking pictures" to "for generating implausible pictures."  
- Minor: In Theorem 2.5, use "Then" instead of "Therefore" for smoother wording.  
- Minor: Correct capitalization in Theorem 2.6 from "Is a..." to "is a...".  
- Minor: Correct typo "relly" to "rely".
