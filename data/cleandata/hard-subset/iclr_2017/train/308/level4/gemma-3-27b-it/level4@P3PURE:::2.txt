This paper makes a valuable contribution to our understanding of generative adversarial network (GAN) training dynamics. Through novel insights into the training process of GANs and their variants, the authors reveal the root causes of both vanishing gradients in the original GAN formulation and instability in its modifications. Importantly, they propose a method to mitigate these difficulties by introducing a perturbation technique. I believe this work will inspire more principled research in this area.

I am particularly interested in the proposed perturbation trick to avoid gradient instability and vanishing. This approach shares similarities with the dropout technique, where the perturbation can be viewed as sampling from a Bernoulli distribution. A discussion of this connection would be beneficial. Beyond the theoretical analysis, it would be valuable to see empirical validation of this trick. Specifically, could the authors include experiments—perhaps mirroring the style of Figures 2 and 3—to demonstrate the performance of the perturbed GAN in a comparative setting?
