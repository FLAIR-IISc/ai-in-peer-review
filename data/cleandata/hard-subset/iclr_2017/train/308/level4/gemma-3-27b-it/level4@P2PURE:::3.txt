SUMMARY
This paper investigates key challenges in training generative adversarial networks (GANs), focusing on the implications of using an asymmetric divergence function and identifying sources of training instability. It then presents an alternative approach based on a smoothing technique.

PROS
The work offers strong theoretical insights, raises important questions, and provides compelling answers. It effectively leverages concepts from analysis and differential topology. The proposed method offers promising directions for mitigating instability in GAN training.

CONS
The paper is somewhat lengthy and technically dense. While not a major concern at this stage, certain aspects and their consequences would benefit from further development in future work.
