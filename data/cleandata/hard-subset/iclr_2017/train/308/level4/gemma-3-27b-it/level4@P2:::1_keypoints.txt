- Overall verdict: strong submission — Accept with enthusiasm for tackling GAN convergence and stability.  
- Provides novel theoretical analysis of GAN convergence and stability, distinguishing it from most empirical papers.  
- Addresses instability in early GAN architectures and explains why training is difficult.  
- Theorems 2.4–2.6 are novel and use interesting mathematical techniques.  
- Theorems 2.1–2.2 on perfect discrimination give insightful explanations for failure of standard training.  
- Section 2.2 gives a thorough analysis of original and alternative cost functions, showing deep mathematical understanding.  
- Theorems on vanishing gradients (Thm 2.4) and instability (Thm 2.6) rigorously explain observed training difficulties.  
- Proposes a promising improvement direction (Section 3): softer metrics, distributions, noise injection, and links to Wasserstein distance.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations and outline open questions and avenues for future work.  
- Minor technical concern: clarification needed on the proof of Theorem 2.5 (assumptions about optimal discriminator).  
- Suggestion: improve clarity of notation by defining key symbols when introduced.  
- Suggestion: add more intuitive explanations alongside formal proofs for key theorems to broaden accessibility.  
- Suggestion: include some experimental validation (e.g., preliminary noise-injection results) to support theoretical claims.  
- Suggestion: discuss relationship to recent GAN stabilization techniques (spectral normalization, gradient penalties) for context.  
- Specific question: clarify assumptions about properties of optimal discriminator D* and its impact on derivation of equation (3).  
- Specific question: guidance on choosing noise distribution for discriminator inputs and practical limits for high-dimensional data.  
- Specific question: how does the proposed framework relate to/augment techniques like gradient clipping or weight normalization?  
- Specific question: anticipated challenges in translating theoretical findings into practical improvements for real-world applications?
