This is a strong paper with a high likelihood of acceptance. I maintain my original positive evaluation.
This submission offers a valuable analysis of generative adversarial networks (GANs), a recently introduced and highly influential class of neural network models. The authors provide a theoretical investigation of GAN convergence and stability – both critical areas for advancing the field. To the best of my knowledge, this work represents one of the first theoretical treatments of GANs, and, unlike many submissions in this area, delivers substantial theoretical insight into their behavior. Addressing the stability issues inherent in GANs is particularly important, given the initial difficulties in achieving reliable performance with early GAN architectures. Theorems 2.4–2.6 are novel and employ interesting mathematical techniques. I have a few minor technical questions concerning the proof of Theorem 2.5, but these do not detract significantly from the paper’s overall contribution.
