SUMMARY
This paper addresses important questions regarding the difficulties encountered when training generative adversarial networks. It discusses the consequences of utilizing an asymmetric divergence function and identifies sources of instability during GAN training. The authors then propose an alternative approach based on a smoothing technique.

PROS
The paper presents strong theoretical contributions, posing insightful questions and providing compelling answers. It makes an interesting and effective use of concepts from analysis and differential topology. Furthermore, the work proposes promising avenues for mitigating instability in GANs.

CONS
The paper is somewhat lengthy and technically dense. While some parts and their implications could benefit from further development – which is perfectly acceptable for future work – the core ideas are well-presented.

MINOR COMMENTS
- Section 2.1 could be concise; consider moving the proofs to the appendix.
- Section 3 provides a nice, intuitive, and simple solution.
- On page 2, the second bullet point states that P_g is smaller than the data distribution for some x, which in turn leads to a non-zero KL divergence.
- On page 2, change “for not generating plausibly looking pictures” to “for generating not plausibly looking pictures.”
- Lemma 1 could potentially hold in a more general setting.
- Theorem 2.1 appears to rely on basic analytical results; a relevant reference could obviate the need for the proof.
- In Theorem 2.4, briefly remind the reader about the distribution p(z).
- Lemma 2 also seems to rely on basic analytical results; a reference might be sufficient.
- Please explicitly state the domain of the random variables used throughout the paper.
- Correct “relly” to “rely.”
- In Theorem 2.2, clarify whether the closed manifolds are assumed to have a boundary. This question was raised in the discussion.
- In Corollary 2.1, the reference to “assumptions of Theorem 1.3” is unclear, as Theorem 1.3 could not be located.
- In Theorem 2.5, replace “Therefore” with “Then.”
- In Theorem 2.6, change “Is a…” to “is a…”
- The numbering of the theorems is somewhat confusing.
