- Paper analyzes fundamental GAN training challenges caused by asymmetric divergence measures and proposes a smoothing technique to mitigate instabilities.  
- Strong theoretical foundation using analysis and differential topology to explain GAN dynamics and equilibrium difficulties.  
- Proposed smoothing approach is conceptually simple and promising for improving GAN stability.  
- Mathematical tools and rigorous analysis are a notable strength of the paper.  
- Paper’s length and high technical depth may hinder accessibility for some readers.  
- Some parts of the analysis need further development to clarify consequences and practical implications.  
- Extensive mathematical formalism sometimes obscures core intuition; stronger connections to practice are needed.  
- Section 2.1 should be streamlined and detailed proofs moved to the appendix for readability.  
- Section 3’s proposed solution is intuitive and a clear strength.

- Fix phrasing on page 2: “for not generating plausibly looking pictures” → “for generating not plausibly looking pictures.”

- Theorem 2.1 appears to rely on standard results—cite relevant references to shorten the paper.  
- Theorem 2.4 should briefly remind the reader of the distribution p(z).
