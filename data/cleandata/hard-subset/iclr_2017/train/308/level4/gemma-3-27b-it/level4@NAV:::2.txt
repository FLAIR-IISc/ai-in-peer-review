This paper offers a significant contribution to our understanding of the GAN training process. 

The authors provide novel insights into GAN training dynamics, and those of its variants, successfully explaining the origins of both vanishing gradients in the original GAN formulation and instability in modified approaches. Critically, they propose a perturbation-based method to mitigate these issues. I anticipate this work will stimulate further, more theoretically grounded research in this area.

I am particularly intrigued by the proposed perturbation technique for addressing gradient instability. The approach shares similarities with the dropout method, specifically resembling a Bernoulli distribution in its implementation. A discussion of this connection would be valuable. Furthermore, while the theoretical analysis is compelling, supporting empirical evidence would strengthen the findings. I recommend including additional experiments—similar in scope to Figures 2 and 3—to demonstrate the efficacy of the perturbated GAN and provide a comparative analysis.
