- Strength: Theorems 2.1–2.6 are novel and present an interesting mathematical framework for analyzing GAN behavior.

- Strength: Section 2.2 analysis of original and alternative cost functions clarifies why intuitive modifications can still cause mode dropping.
- Strength: Section 3 introduces a novel metric with supporting theorems pointing to a promising direction for stabilizing GAN training.
- Strength: Notable connection established between the Wasserstein distance and the Jensen–Shannon divergence of noisy distributions.
- Strength: Authors provide sufficient justification for assumptions and generally rigorous proofs.
- Minor issue: Theorem 2.5’s link to reversed KL (KL(Pg||Pr)) needs expanded intuition and discussion of implications for mode dropping.
- Minor issue: Some proofs (notably Gaussian-process steps in Theorem 2.6) require clearer, more detailed explanations.

- Minor issue: Related work discussion could be more comprehensive regarding theoretical GAN literature.

- Question: How sensitive is performance to the choice and level of noise added to the discriminator input (e.g., Gaussian vs. other distributions)?
