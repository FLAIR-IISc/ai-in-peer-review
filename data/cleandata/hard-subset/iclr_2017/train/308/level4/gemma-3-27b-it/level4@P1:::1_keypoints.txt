- Strong submission addressing GAN convergence and stability, important and timely topics.  
- Provides one of the first deep theoretical analyses of GANs with novel insights.  
- Theorems 2.4–2.6 are novel and use interesting mathematical techniques.  
- Minor technical questions exist regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Tackles why GANs are difficult to train and moves beyond empirical observations.  
- Identifies instability and saturation problems for original and alternative cost functions (Theorems 2.5, 2.6).  
- Theorems on perfect discrimination (Theorems 2.1, 2.2) clarify conditions under which training fails.  
- Introduction of softer metrics (Section 3) offers a promising direction to address instability.  
- Connection to Wasserstein distance (Theorem 3.3) is compelling.  
- Rigorous mathematical treatment is a major strength compared to much of the GAN literature.  
- Request for a more detailed proof of Theorem 2.5.  
- Ask for clearer practical implications and implementation details for the proposed noise injection strategy (noise type and level σ).  
- Recommend additional experimental validation to demonstrate improved stability and convergence.  
- Notation can be dense; suggest clearer variable names or reminders of definitions.  
- Questions for authors: details on noise injection implementation and σ selection.  
- Questions for authors: any preliminary experimental results showing effectiveness?  
- Questions for authors: practical consequences of Cauchy-distributed gradient updates (Theorem 2.6) and implications for optimization algorithms.  
- Questions for authors: comparison of the proposed approach to WGANs, including pros and cons.
