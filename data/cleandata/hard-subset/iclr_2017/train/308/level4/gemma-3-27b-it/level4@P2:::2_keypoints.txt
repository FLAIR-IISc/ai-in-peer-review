- Novel theoretical analysis explaining gradient vanishing in the original GAN and instability in a common variant.  
- Proposal of a perturbation-based approach to mitigate vanishing and instability, offering a principled research direction.  
- Strong recommendation for acceptance based on the significance of the theoretical insights and potential impact of the solution.  
- Rigorous linkage of training degradation to discriminator improvement, properties of modeled distributions, and limitations of common cost functions.  
- Perturbation technique is theoretically compelling but lacks sufficient empirical validation.  
- The connection between the proposed perturbation and dropout/stochastic regularization is noted but not fully explored.  
- Reviewer requests experimental results (e.g., analogous to Figures 2 and 3) showing effects on training stability, gradient behavior, and sample quality with and without perturbation.  
- Reviewer asks for sensitivity analysis to perturbation distribution and magnitude, and guidelines for selecting perturbation parameters.  
- Three specific questions: (1) elaborate on relation to dropout and pros/cons of viewing it as stochastic regularization, (2) provide empirical evidence/experiments supporting effectiveness, (3) characterize sensitivity to perturbation type and scale and give parameter-selection guidance.
