This is a strong submission addressing a critical and recently introduced topic in neural networks – generative adversarial networks. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of GAN convergence and stability, both of which are highly important areas of research. To the best of my knowledge, this is among the first papers to offer a rigorous theoretical treatment of GANs, and it stands out from many submissions in this field by providing genuinely deep theoretical insight into the architecture. The stability issues inherent in GANs are particularly significant, given the practical difficulties encountered with early GAN implementations. Theorems 2.4–2.6 are novel and employ interesting mathematical techniques. I have a few technical questions regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5, but these are relatively minor. I maintain my original positive evaluation and hope this paper will be accepted.
