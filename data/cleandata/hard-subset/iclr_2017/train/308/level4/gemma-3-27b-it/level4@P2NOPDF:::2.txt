This paper offers a valuable contribution to our understanding of Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) training dynamics. Through novel analysis of both the original GAN formulation and its variants, the authors illuminate the root causes of gradient vanishing and instability – common challenges in GAN training. Crucially, they propose a perturbation-based approach to mitigate these difficulties, representing a potentially significant advance in the field. I anticipate this work will stimulate further research into more principled GAN training methodologies.

I am particularly intrigued by the proposed perturbation technique. Its effectiveness in addressing gradient issues is compelling, and a connection to the well-established dropout technique is readily apparent; the perturbation can be conceptualized as a sampling from a Bernoulli distribution. Discussing this relationship more explicitly would strengthen the paper. Beyond the theoretical analysis, empirical validation of this technique is important. Including experimental results – similar in presentation to Figures 2 and 3 – demonstrating the performance of the perturbed GAN would provide further justification for the approach and allow for a more direct comparison with existing methods. Specifically, showing how the perturbation affects training stability and sample quality would be highly beneficial.
