## REVIEW

**SUMMARY**

This paper investigates critical challenges in training generative adversarial networks (GANs), focusing on the implications of asymmetric divergence functions and the resulting training instabilities. It then presents a novel approach based on a smoothing technique to address these issues.

**PROS**

The work is theoretically sound, posing important questions and providing insightful answers. It demonstrates a creative application of concepts from analysis and differential topology. Furthermore, the paper suggests promising directions for improving the stability of GAN training.

**CONS**

The manuscript is somewhat lengthy and technically dense. While not a major concern, certain aspects and their implications would benefit from further elaboration in future work.

**MINOR COMMENTS**

- Section 2.1 could be streamlined, potentially by relocating the proofs to an appendix.
- Section 3 offers a clear, intuitive, and straightforward solution.
- Page 2, second bullet point: Clarification – this implies that the generator’s distribution, P_g, has support smaller than the data distribution for certain values of x, resulting in a non-zero KL divergence.
- Page 2: Correct “for not generating plausibly looking pictures” to “for generating not plausibly looking pictures”.
- Lemma 1 appears to hold under more general conditions.
- The proof of Theorem 2.1 may benefit from a citation to relevant work in basic analysis.
- In Theorem 2.4, briefly reminding the reader of the distribution p(z) would improve clarity.
- Lemma 2 could potentially be supported by a reference to established results in analysis. 
- Please explicitly define the domain of the random variables used throughout the paper.
- Correct the typographical error: “relly” should be “rely”.
- Theorem 2.2: Clarify whether the closed manifolds are assumed to have a boundary. (This point is already raised as a question for the authors.)
- Corollary 2.1: The reference to “assumptions of Theorem 1.3” is unclear; Theorem 1.3 could not be located.
- Theorem 2.5: Replace “Therefore” with “Then”.
- Theorem 2.6: Correct “Is a…” to “is a”.
- The numbering of theorems throughout the paper may be confusing to the reader.
