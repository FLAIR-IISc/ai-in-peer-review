This paper makes a valuable contribution to our understanding of generative adversarial network (GAN) training. 
By offering new insights into the training dynamics of GANs and their variants, the authors reveal the underlying reasons for both vanishing gradients in the original GAN formulation and instability in its variants. Importantly, they also propose a method to mitigate these difficulties through the introduction of perturbations. I believe this work will inspire more principled research in this direction.

I am particularly interested in the perturbation trick used to avoid gradient instability and vanishing. In fact, this is quite reminiscent of the dropout technique, where the perturbation can be viewed as sampling from a Bernoulli distribution. It would be beneficial if the authors discussed this connection in more detail. Beyond the theoretical analysis, is there any empirical evidence to support this trick? It would strengthen the paper to include experiments, similar to Figures 2 and 3, demonstrating the performance of the perturbed GAN for comparison.
