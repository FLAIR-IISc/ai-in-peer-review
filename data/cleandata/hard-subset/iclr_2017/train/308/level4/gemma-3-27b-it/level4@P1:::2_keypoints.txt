- Paper provides valuable insights into GAN training dynamics, explaining vanishing gradients and instability in variants.  
- Introduces perturbation (noise injection) to avoid gradient vanishing and instability.  
- Reviewer accepts the paper; finds core theoretical arguments convincing and addressing a fundamental open question.  
- Proposed noise-based smoothing of the optimization landscape is well-motivated and promising.  
- Rigorous analysis shows optimal discriminator can be “too perfect,” yielding uninformative gradients for the generator.  
- Shows commonly used alternative cost function can suffer from instability.  
- Perturbation trick is theoretically grounded and related to dropout; reviewer requests discussion of this connection.  
- Reviewer asks for empirical validation—add experiments (analogous to Figures 2 and 3) demonstrating perturbed GAN behavior and sample quality.  
- Suggest adding discussion of Wasserstein distance practical estimation/optimization challenges.  
- Recommend clarifying notation (e.g., different norms) for readability.  
- Suggest discussing hyperparameter sensitivity, especially noise variance, and providing guidance for choosing it.
