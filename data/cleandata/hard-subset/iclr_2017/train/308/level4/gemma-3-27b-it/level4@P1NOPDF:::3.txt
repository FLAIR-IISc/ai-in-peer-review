SUMMARY
This paper addresses important questions regarding the difficulties in training generative adversarial networks. It discusses the consequences of using an asymmetric divergence function and identifies sources of instability during GAN training. The authors then propose an alternative approach based on smoothing.

PROS
The paper presents strong theoretical contributions, raises insightful questions, and provides compelling answers. 
It makes an interesting and effective use of concepts from analysis and differential topology.
The work proposes promising avenues for avoiding instability in GANs.

CONS
The paper is somewhat lengthy and technical. Some parts and their consequences could benefit from further development—which is perfectly reasonable for future work.

MINOR COMMENTS
- Section 2.1 could be shortened. For example, moving the proofs to the appendix would improve readability.
- Section 3 presents a nice, intuitive, and simple solution.
- On page 2, the second bullet point states that P_g is smaller than the data distribution for some x, which in turn leads to a non-zero KL divergence. 
- On page 2, the phrase “for not generating plausibly looking pictures” should be “for generating pictures that do not look plausible.”
- Lemma 1 likely holds in a more general setting.
- Theorem 2.1 appears to rely on basic analysis; a citation could obviate the need for the proof.
- In Theorem 2.4, it would be helpful to remind the reader about the distribution p(z).
- Lemma 2 also seems to follow from basic analysis; a citation might suffice.
- Please specify the domain of the random variables used throughout the paper.
- “relly” should be “rely.”
- Theorem 2.2: Do the closed manifolds have a boundary? (This question was already raised in the paper.)
- Corollary 2.1 refers to “assumptions of Theorem 1.3,” but I could not locate Theorem 1.3.
- Theorem 2.5: Change “Therefore” to “Then.”
- Theorem 2.6: Change “Is a…” to “is a…”
- The numbering of the theorems is somewhat confusing. Consider a more consistent or streamlined numbering scheme.
