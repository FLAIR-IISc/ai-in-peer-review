- Paper provides valuable theoretical analysis of GAN training dynamics, explaining root causes of gradient vanishing and instability.  
- Authors propose a perturbation-based technique to mitigate gradient problems and improve training stability.  
- Central insight on how generator-discriminator interplay leads to gradient issues is well-articulated and convincing.  
- Perturbation approach is intuitive, practically appealing, and likened to dropout via a Bernoulli formulation.  
- Recommend elaborating the connection to dropout with theoretical analysis and literature grounding.  
- Empirical validation is currently limited; visualizations help but are insufficient to prove effectiveness.  
- Request quantitative experiments (e.g., Inception Score, FID, convergence speed) comparing perturbated GAN to baselines (original GAN, DCGAN) and stabilized variants (WGAN, spectral norm).  
- Suggest an ablation study on perturbation magnitude and strategies to find sensitivity and optimal settings.  
- Ask for clearer articulation and discussion of theoretical assumptions and their limitations.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution with a promising practical method; recommend acceptance conditional on expanded empirical validation and clarifications.
