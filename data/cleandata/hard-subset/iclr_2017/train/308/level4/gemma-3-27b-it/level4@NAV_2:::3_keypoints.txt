- Paper studies GAN training instability, linking it to divergence choice and distribution geometry, and proposes smoothing-based mitigation tools.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form due to dense presentation and lack of clarity despite potentially valuable theory.  
- Identifies instability sources: Jensen-Shannon divergence and perfect discrimination when generator/data live on low-dimensional supports.  
- Proposes smoothing via Wasserstein metric and noise injection as a promising stabilization direction.  
- Main weakness: excessive mathematical formalism (many theorems/lemmas) that obscures core ideas and practical relevance.  
- Some results feel over-generalized or insufficiently motivated for practical GAN training.  
- Concerns about verification: long proofs, possible reliance on established results without proper citations (e.g., Theorem 2.1, Lemma 2).  
- Connections between theory and practical improvements are not made clear; final section on softer metrics is promising but underdeveloped.  
- Suggestion: streamline presentation by moving proofs to appendix and emphasize intuition in main text.  
- Suggestion: clearly articulate practical implications and how results translate to improved GAN training procedures/architectures.  
- Suggestion: better connect to existing literature on GAN training and optimal transport.  
- Suggestion: ensure consistent notation and clear definitions for terms.  
- Suggestion: expand the smoothing approach with implementation details and empirical evaluation on standard benchmarks.  
- Suggestion: fix typographical errors and verify theorem numbering/consistency.  
- Questions for authors: justify choice of noise distribution used in smoothing and its advantages over alternatives.  
- Question: how does the computational cost of smoothing compare to existing GAN methods?  
- Question: have preliminary experiments been done on standard GAN benchmarks; if so, provide results.  
- Question: what are the limitations and possibly unrealistic assumptions of the theoretical analysis?  
- Question: provide an intuitive explanation of why manifold dimensionality is crucial for GAN training instability.
