## Review of Generative Adversarial Networks: Theoretical Challenges and a Smoothening Approach

**SUMMARY**

This paper investigates fundamental challenges in training Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), focusing on the implications of employing asymmetric divergence measures and the resulting instabilities. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of these issues and propose a novel approach based on a smoothing technique to mitigate them. This work offers valuable insights into the behavior of GANs and suggests potential pathways towards more stable and reliable training procedures.

**PROS**

The paper presents a strong theoretical foundation for understanding GAN training dynamics. The authors identify important questions regarding the inherent difficulties in achieving equilibrium and provide insightful answers rooted in analysis and differential topology. The proposed smoothing approach offers a promising avenue for improving GAN stability, and the paper’s exploration of these concepts is commendable. The use of mathematical tools to analyze GAN behavior is particularly noteworthy.

**CONS**

While theoretically sound, the paper’s length and technical depth might hinder accessibility for some readers. Certain aspects of the analysis and their consequences could benefit from further development, though this is reasonable for a work of this nature and suggests clear directions for future research. The extensive mathematical formalism, while rigorous, sometimes obscures the core intuition, and a greater emphasis on connecting the theory to practical implications would be beneficial.

**DETAILED COMMENTS**

* **Section 2.1:** This section could be streamlined for better readability. Consider moving detailed proofs to the appendix to maintain focus on the key theoretical results.
* **Section 3:** The proposed solution is intuitive, conceptually simple, and a clear strength of the paper.
* **Page 2, Second Bullet:** The statement regarding *P<sub>g</sub>* being smaller than the data distribution in certain regions is accurate and important. Clarifying that this leads to a non-zero KL divergence would further strengthen the explanation.
* **Page 2:** Correct the phrasing: change “for not generating plausibly looking pictures” to “for generating not plausibly looking pictures.”
* **Lemma 1:** The authors may consider discussing the generality of this lemma. Could it hold under broader conditions?
* **Theorem 2.1:** The proof appears to rely on standard analytical results. Citing a relevant reference could shorten the paper and acknowledge existing work.
* **Theorem 2.4:** A brief reminder of the distribution *p(z)* would improve clarity for readers unfamiliar with the specific notation used.
* **Lemma 2:** Similar to Theorem 2.1, this lemma may benefit from a citation to existing analytical literature. Explicitly stating the domain of the random variables involved would also enhance clarity.
* **Typo:** Correct “relly” to “rely.”
* **Theorem 2.2:** Clarify whether the closed manifolds being discussed possess boundaries. This point was raised in the questions submitted by the authors, and addressing it within the theorem would be beneficial.
* **Corollary 2.1:** The reference to “Theorem 1.3” is unclear, as this theorem does not appear to exist within the paper. Please verify the theorem number.
* **Theorem 2.5:** Replace “Therefore” with “Then” for improved grammatical flow.
* **Theorem 2.6:** Change “Is a…” to “is a…” for correct capitalization.
* **Theorem Numbering:** The numbering of theorems appears inconsistent. Ensure a clear and logical numbering scheme throughout the paper.

**OVERALL ASSESSMENT**

This paper offers a valuable theoretical contribution to the understanding of GAN training dynamics. The authors have identified a key issue – the asymmetry of divergence measures – and developed a promising approach to address it. Despite some areas where clarity and conciseness could be improved, the paper’s strengths – a rigorous theoretical foundation, novel insights, and a clear proposed solution – outweigh its weaknesses. This work has the potential to influence future research on GANs and contribute to the development of more stable and effective generative models. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised in the detailed comments.
