This paper offers a valuable contribution to understanding the training dynamics of generative adversarial networks (GANs). Through new insights into these dynamics, and those of GAN variants, the authors illuminate the causes of both vanishing gradients in the original GAN formulation and instability in its variants. Importantly, they propose a method for mitigating these difficulties through the introduction of perturbation. I believe this work will stimulate further principled research in this area. 

I am particularly interested in the perturbation technique used to address gradient instability and vanishing gradients. This approach bears a resemblance to the dropout technique, where the perturbation can be interpreted as sampling from a Bernoulli distribution; discussing this connection would be beneficial. Beyond the theoretical analysis, is there empirical evidence to support the effectiveness of this perturbation trick? Including experimental results, similar to those presented in Figures 2 and 3, demonstrating the performance of the perturbed GAN would strengthen the paper.
