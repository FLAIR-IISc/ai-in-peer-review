## Review

This paper investigates critical challenges in the training of generative adversarial networks, specifically addressing the implications of asymmetric divergence functions and the resulting instability. It offers a novel approach to mitigate these issues through a smoothing technique.

**Strengths:**

The work presents a strong theoretical foundation, posing important questions and providing insightful answers. It demonstrates a creative application of concepts from analysis and differential topology to the problem of GAN training. Furthermore, the paper suggests promising directions for improving the stability of GANs.

**Weaknesses:**

The manuscript is somewhat lengthy and technically dense. While not a major concern, certain aspects and their implications would benefit from further elaboration in future work.

**Minor Comments:**

- Section 2.1 could be streamlined; consider relocating the proofs to an appendix.
- Section 3 offers a clear, intuitive, and straightforward solution.
- Regarding the second bullet point on page 2: it’s worth noting that if P_g is smaller than the data distribution for some x, the Kullback-Leibler divergence will be non-zero.
- On page 2, revise “for not generating plausibly looking pictures” to “for generating not plausibly looking pictures.”
- Lemma 1’s validity likely extends to a more general setting.
- The proof of Theorem 2.1 appears to rely on standard analytical results and could potentially be simplified with a relevant citation.
- In Theorem 2.4, briefly reminding the reader of the distribution p(z) would enhance clarity.
- Lemma 2 also appears to be a standard analytical result that could be supported by a reference.
- Please explicitly define the domain of the random variables used throughout the paper.
- Correct the typographical error: “relly” should be “rely.”
- In Theorem 2.2, clarify whether the closed manifolds are assumed to have a boundary. (This point is already raised as a question.)
- In Corollary 2.1, the reference to “assumptions of Theorem 1.3” is unclear, as Theorem 1.3 was not found.
- In Theorem 2.5, consider replacing “Therefore” with “Then.”
- In Theorem 2.6, correct “Is a…” to “is a.”
- The numbering of the theorems appears inconsistent and could be clarified.
