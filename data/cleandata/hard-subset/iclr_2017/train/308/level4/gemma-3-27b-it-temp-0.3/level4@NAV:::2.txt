This paper offers a significant contribution to our understanding of the GAN training process. Through novel insights into the training dynamics of GANs and their variants, the authors effectively explain the origins of both vanishing gradients in the original GAN formulation and instability issues in related architectures. Crucially, they propose a perturbation-based approach to mitigate these challenges. I anticipate this work will stimulate further, more theoretically grounded research in this area.

I am particularly intrigued by the proposed perturbation technique for addressing gradient instability and vanishing gradients. The approach bears a notable resemblance to the dropout technique, with the perturbation potentially viewed as a Bernoulli distribution; a discussion of this connection would be valuable. Beyond the theoretical analysis, it would be beneficial to see empirical validation of this technique. Specifically, including experimental results – perhaps similar in scope to Figures 2 and 3 – demonstrating the performance of the perturbed GAN would strengthen the paper’s impact.
