- Reviewer views the paper as very strong and hopes it is accepted, maintaining their original positive evaluation.
- Paper addresses generative adversarial networks (GANs), a highly important recent method in neural networks.
- Provides theoretical analysis of GAN convergence and examines GAN stability.
- Both convergence and stability topics are highly important and relevant.
- To the reviewer’s knowledge, this is among the first theoretical works on GANs.
- Unlike many submissions, the paper offers deep theoretical insight into GAN architectures.
- Stability issues are especially important due to the historical instability and poor performance of early GANs.
- Theorems 2.4–2.6 are novel and introduce interesting mathematical techniques.
- Reviewer has a few minor technical questions about the proof of Theorem 2.5.
