Very good paper; I hope it will be accepted. I keep my original evaluation.

This is a strong submission on one of the most important and recently introduced methods in neural networks: generative adversarial networks (GANs). The authors provide a theoretical analysis of GAN convergence and discuss the stability of GANs—both are very important. To the best of my knowledge, this is among the first theoretical papers on GANs and, unlike most submissions in the field, it offers deep theoretical insight into this architecture. The stability issues in GANs are extremely important, as the earliest versions of the architecture were highly unstable and performed poorly in practice. Theorems 2.4–2.6 are novel, and the mathematical techniques they introduce are interesting. I have some technical questions regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5, but these are fairly minor.
