This paper makes a valuable contribution by providing a clearer understanding of the training procedure for generative adversarial networks (GANs). By analyzing the training dynamics of GANs and a variant, the authors explain why gradients either vanish in the original GAN or become unstable in the variant. More importantly, they propose introducing perturbations as a way to avoid these issues. I believe this work will inspire more principled research in this direction.

I am particularly interested in the perturbation technique for mitigating gradient vanishing and instability. This seems closely related to the dropout technique, where the perturbation can be modeled as a Bernoulli distribution. It would be helpful if the paper discussed this connection explicitly. Beyond the theoretical analysis, is there any empirical evaluation to support this approach? Could you include experiments analogous to Figs. 2 and 3 for the perturbed GAN to facilitate comparison?
