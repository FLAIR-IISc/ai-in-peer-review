SUMMARY
This paper tackles important questions about the difficulties of training generative adversarial networks (GANs). It analyzes the consequences of using asymmetric divergences and identifies sources of instability in GAN training, then proposes a smoothing-based alternative.

PROS
- Strong theoretical framing; raises good questions and provides thoughtful answers.
- Makes interesting use of concepts from analysis and differential topology.
- Proposes practical avenues to mitigate instability in GANs.

CONS
- Somewhat long and highly technical. Some parts and implications still need further development (which is reasonable for future work).
