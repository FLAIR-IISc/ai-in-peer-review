This paper offers a meaningful contribution by clarifying the training procedure of generative adversarial networks (GANs).

Drawing on new insights into the training dynamics of GANs and their variants, the authors explain why gradients tend to vanish in the original GAN and become unstable in certain variants. More importantly, they propose a perturbation-based method to mitigate these issues. I expect this work to motivate more principled research in this area.

I am particularly interested in the perturbation technique for preventing gradient vanishing and instability. This appears closely related to the dropout mechanism, where the perturbation can be modeled as a Bernoulli distribution; it would be helpful to discuss this connection. Beyond the theoretical analysis, is there empirical evidence supporting this technique? Could you include experiments analogous to Figures 2 and 3 for the perturbed GAN for comparison?
