SUMMARY
This paper tackles important challenges in training generative adversarial networks. It examines the consequences of using an asymmetric divergence and identifies sources of instability in GAN training. It then proposes an alternative based on smoothing.

PROS
- Strong theory; well-posed questions with solid answers.
- Interesting application of concepts from analysis and differential topology.
- Suggests practical ways to avoid instability in GANs.

CONS
- Somewhat long and technical. Certain parts and implications need further development (appropriate for future work).
