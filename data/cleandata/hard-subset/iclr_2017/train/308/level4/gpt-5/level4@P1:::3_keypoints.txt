- Paper analyzes why GAN training is unstable and generator updates degrade as discriminator improves, focusing on low-dimensional manifold supports.
- Shows discriminators can be perfect and locally constant on supports, causing vanishing gradients for the original generator loss and instability for the −log D alternative.
- Proves existence of smooth perfect discriminators with zero gradients almost everywhere on supports.
- Demonstrates vanishing generator gradients as D→D* for the original loss.
- Identifies −log D generator updates as optimizing KL(Pg‖Pr) − 2·JSD, explaining mode dropping behavior.
- Provides a formal instability result: noisy discriminator approximations yield Cauchy-distributed (heavy-tailed) generator updates.
- Proposes adding continuous noise to real and generated samples to make distributions absolutely continuous and obtain smooth, interpretable gradients.
- Derives that optimizing JSD between the smoothed distributions gives an upper bound on the Wasserstein distance between the original distributions.
- Claims the smoothing fix yields a stable training direction and reduces need for delicate discriminator/generator scheduling.
- Decision: Accept — strong theoretical insights and a principled, simple remedy with rigorous analysis and practical implications.
- Problem relevance: Addresses central GAN questions (why updates worsen, instability roots, practical divergences) that are timely and important.
- Motivation/placement: Builds on prior GAN and divergence work and conceptually links manifold geometry to Wasserstein reasoning via noise smoothing.
- Support for claims: Theorems and targeted experiments substantiate theoretical claims qualitatively and align with empirical observations.
- Caveat: Some proofs are standard and could be shortened or referenced; a few statements need clarification (e.g., manifold boundaries, variable domains).
- Exposition suggestion: Shorten Section 2.1 proofs and move details to appendix; improve theorem/lemma numbering and cross-references.
- Clarify assumptions: Remind reader of priors and Jacobian assumptions in Theorem 2.4 and specify domains of random variables and noise operationalization.
- Fix references/typos: Correct cross-reference (Corollary 2.1 → Theorem 1.3) and multiple typos throughout.
- Technical suggestion: State Lemma 1 in greater generality or cite references; cite standard analysis/topology for Theorem 2.1 and Lemma 2.
- Practical emphasis: Stress that adding noise to both discriminator inputs and generator outputs is crucial; backpropagating through noiseless g(Z) is risky.
- Request guidance: Provide practical advice on noise distribution choice, annealing schedules, covariance selection, and estimating constants in Wasserstein bound.
- Questions for authors: Quantify gradient variance/bias and heavy tails as D→optimality; sensitivity to noise choices and σ-annealing; training recommendations for noise/backprop interaction with BN; comparisons to WGAN/Lipschitz enforcement; applicability when supports are not exact manifolds; how to estimate bound constants; and whether JSD of smoothed distributions is a practical evaluation metric.
