SUMMARY
This paper addresses important questions about the difficulties in training generative adversarial networks (GANs). It analyzes the consequences of using an asymmetric divergence and identifies sources of instability in GAN training. It then proposes an alternative based on a smoothing approach.

PROS
- Strong theoretical grounding; the paper raises good questions and provides thoughtful answers.
- Interesting use of concepts from analysis and differential topology.
- Proposes practical avenues to avoid instability in GANs.

CONS
- A bit too long and quite technical. Some parts and their consequences still need to be further developed (which is perfectly fine for future work).
