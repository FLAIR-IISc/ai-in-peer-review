Very good paper; I hope it will be accepted. I maintain my original evaluation.

This is a strong submission on generative adversarial networks (GANs), one of the most important recent methods in neural networks. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of GAN convergence and discuss GAN stability—both are crucial topics. To the best of my knowledge, this is among the first theoretical papers on GANs, and, unlike most submissions in this area, it offers genuine theoretical insight into the architecture.

Stability issues in GANs are extremely important, as the initial versions of the architecture were highly unstable and performed poorly in practice. Theorems 2.4–2.6 are novel and introduce interesting mathematical techniques. I have some technical questions regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5, but these are minor.
