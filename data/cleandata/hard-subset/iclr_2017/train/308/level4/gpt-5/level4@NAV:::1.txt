Review- This is a very strong paper; I hope it is accepted. I maintain my original evaluation.
This submission addresses one of the most important and recently introduced methods in neural networks—generative adversarial networks. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of GAN convergence and examine the stability of GANs. Both topics are highly important. To the best of my knowledge, this is among the first theoretical works on GANs and, unlike many submissions in the area, it offers deep theoretical insight into this architecture. Stability issues in GANs are particularly important because the earliest GAN architectures were very unstable and performed poorly in practice. Theorems 2.4–2.6 are novel, and the mathematical techniques they introduce are interesting. I have a few technical questions regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5, but these are fairly minor.
