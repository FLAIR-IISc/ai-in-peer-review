This is a very strong submission, and I maintain my original evaluation. I hope it will be accepted.

The paper addresses one of the most important recent developments in neural networks—generative adversarial networks (GANs)—and offers a rigorous theoretical analysis of their convergence and stability. Both topics are crucial, particularly given the well-documented instability of early GAN formulations.

To the best of my knowledge, this is among the first works to provide substantive theoretical insight into GANs, moving beyond predominantly empirical studies. The results offer a clear and meaningful understanding of the dynamics of GAN training.

Theorems 2.4–2.6 appear novel, and the mathematical techniques introduced are interesting and potentially useful beyond this specific setting. I do have a few technical questions regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5, but these are minor and do not affect my overall assessment.
