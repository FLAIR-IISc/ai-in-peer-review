- GAN instability arises because data and generator lie on low-dimensional manifolds with typically disjoint supports, yielding an (almost everywhere) perfect discriminator and vanishing generator gradients.
- At discriminator optimum, the common −log D generator update optimizes KL(Pg‖Pr) − 2 JSD(Pg‖Pr), which biases toward sharp samples and encourages mode dropping.
- With noisy discriminator approximation, generator gradient distributions become heavy-tailed (centered Cauchy), producing highly unstable updates.
- Remedy proposed: smooth both real and generated samples with injected noise to produce overlapping continuous densities Pr+ and Pg+.
- Smoothing yields well-behaved, interpretable generator gradients weighted by distances to data and model manifolds.
- The paper derives an explicit bound relating JSD(Pr+, Pg+) to the Wasserstein distance between Pr and Pg (Theorem 3.3).
- Contribution combines rigorous theorems (Theorems 2.1–2.6, etc.) with targeted experiments illustrating vanishing/exploding gradients and smoothing effects.
- Decision: Accept.
