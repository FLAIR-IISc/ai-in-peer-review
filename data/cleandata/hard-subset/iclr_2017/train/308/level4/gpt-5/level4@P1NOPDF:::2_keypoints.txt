- Paper clarifies GAN training dynamics, explaining why gradients vanish in the original GAN and become unstable in certain variants, and proposes perturbations to mitigate these issues.

- Reviewer highlights the proposed perturbation technique and urges an explicit discussion of its relationship to dropout: is it equivalent to, a generalization of, or fundamentally different from dropout?

- Reviewer asks authors to specify where the perturbation acts (inputs, features, parameters, or gradients) and how that choice affects training dynamics.

- Reviewer requests empirical evidence: experiments analogous to Figs. 2 and 3 directly comparing the perturbed GAN to original methods under the same setups.

- Suggested evaluations should demonstrate practical impact and trade-offs (convergence speed, stability across architectures/datasets, sensitivity to perturbation magnitude/distribution).

- Overall: positive assessment — theoretical insight and principled remedy are valuable, but clarifying dropout relation and adding empirical comparisons would substantially strengthen the paper.
