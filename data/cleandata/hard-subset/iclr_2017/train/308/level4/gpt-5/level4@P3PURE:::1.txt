Very good paper; I hope it will be accepted. I maintain my original evaluation. This is a strong submission on one of the most important and recently introduced methods in neural networks—generative adversarial networks (GANs). The authors provide a theoretical analysis of GAN convergence and discuss the stability of GANs; both topics are very important. To the best of my knowledge, this is among the first theoretical papers on GANs and, unlike many submissions in the field, it offers deep theoretical insight into this architecture. Stability issues are crucial, since the earliest GAN architectures were very unstable and often did not work well in practice. Theorems 2.4–2.6 are novel, and the introduced mathematical techniques are interesting. I have some technical questions regarding the proof of Theorem 2.5, but these are minor.
