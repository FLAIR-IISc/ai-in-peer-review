This paper makes a valuable contribution by providing a clearer understanding of the generative adversarial network (GAN) training procedure. With new insights into the training dynamics of GANs and their variants, the authors reveal why the gradient either vanishes in the original GAN or becomes unstable in certain variants. More importantly, they propose introducing perturbations to avoid these difficulties. I believe this paper will inspire more principled research in this direction.

I am particularly interested in the perturbation trick for avoiding gradient instability and vanishing. This appears closely related to the dropout trick, where the perturbation can be viewed as a Bernoulli distribution. It would be great if the paper could discuss this connection explicitly. Beyond the theoretical analysis, is there any empirical study to justify this trick? Could you please add experiments analogous to Figs. 2 and 3 for the perturbed GAN for comparison?
