- Paper explains GAN training instability: disjoint or low-dimensional supports make the optimal discriminator perfect and flat, causing vanishing or highly unstable generator gradients.  
- Uses analysis and differential topology (transversality, Sard’s lemma, Urysohn’s lemma) to formalize “perfect discrimination” theorems and divergence blow-up on manifolds.  
- Shows how discriminator approximation quality affects generator gradients and formalizes why asymmetric divergences fail on manifold supports.  
- Proposes smoothing both distributions with additive noise (instance noise) and annealing as a practical fix that yields informative gradients.  
- Proves the generator gradient becomes a weighted average of attractive/repulsive forces to data/model manifolds under smoothing.  
- Derives a bound relating W(Pr,Pg) to noise variance and JSD(Pr+ || Pg+), connecting smoothed JSD to a meaningful distance.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: well-posed questions, insightful and technically sound answers with practical implications.  
- Decision: Accept — main reasons are rigorous diagnosis of instability via analysis/topology and a simple theoretically justified smoothing fix linked to Wasserstein distance.  
- Supporting argument (soundness): Theorems formalize empirical observations; they explain vanishing gradients for original loss and heavy-tailed gradients for −log D.  
- Supporting argument (insight): Identification of KL(Pg||Pr) − 2 JSD in the −log D update explains mode dropping and instability; smoothing yields manifold-aligned gradients.  
- Supporting argument (practicality): Instance-noise + annealing is easy to implement, matches heuristics, and the W–JSD bound is useful for training and evaluation.  
- Positioning: Well motivated in GAN literature, complements f-divergence analyses, and opens paths toward Wasserstein-based metrics.  
- Presentation suggestions: shorten or relocate technical proofs (e.g., Section 2.1), fix theorem numbering and forward/back references, and streamline long technical parts.  
- Clarity suggestions: state domains and definitions early (e.g., p(z), Z, X), clarify manifold/closure/boundary assumptions, and explicitly note where derivatives are evaluated and noise assumptions.  
- Theorem/lemma suggestions: note Lemma 1’s greater generality for common architectures, cite standard topology results with sketches, and specify Jacobian norms/integrability in Theorem 2.4.  
- Wording/typos: fix several minor wording issues, typos (“relly”), and incorrect theorem references (e.g., Corollary 2.1 referencing a missing Theorem 1.3).  
- Exposition suggestions: draw clearer connections to prior instance-noise work and WGAN, provide annealing schedule guidance, and report sensitivity analyses.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity of stabilization to noise distribution/covariance and annealing schedules; impact of correlated discriminator errors; generality of Lemma 1 to modern architectures; empirical tightness of the W–JSD bound; quantification of mode-collapse reduction; use of smoothed JSD for training/evaluation; confirm KL positivity when Pg underestimates Pr on sets of positive Pr-measure.
