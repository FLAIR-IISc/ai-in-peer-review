- Proves GAN data/model lie on low-dimensional, (almost surely) disjoint or non-transversally intersecting manifolds, implying smooth perfect discriminators with zero gradients on supports and saturated KL/JSD even for nearby manifolds.
- Shows original GAN generator gradients vanish as D→D*, while the -log D variant yields heavy-tailed, high-variance (Cauchy-like) updates under noisy discriminator approximation, explaining observed instability.
- Proposes instance noise (additive continuous noise to real/gen samples and to generator outputs during backprop) to make distributions absolutely continuous, restoring smooth, informative gradients and eliminating measure-zero adversarial directions.
- Proves the generator gradient under instance noise corresponds to minimizing JSD between the noisy distributions and motivates annealing the noise.
- Derives a bound relating true Wasserstein distance to a term that decays with noise variance plus the JSD between noisy distributions, connecting noisy-JSD minimization to Wasserstein optimization.
- Decision: Accept (weak accept) — strong theoretical analysis and a simple principled mitigation; empirical validation needs strengthening.
- Problem significance: Addresses a central GAN bottleneck—training instability and sensitivity to discriminator strength—with precise statements and proofs.
- Placement/motivation: Builds on classical GAN theory and transport theory, clarifies -log D practice, and links local KL/Pg behavior to mode dropping.
- Rigor/correctness: Manifold lemmas, perfect discrimination theorems, vanishing-gradient and Cauchy-instability results are plausible and technically sound under stated assumptions, supported by plots.
- Instance-noise rationale: Noise makes distributions absolutely continuous; gradients attract to data manifold and repel overconfident model regions; adding noise during generator backprop fixes measure-zero adversarial directions.
- Wasserstein–noisy-JSD bound: Provides a clean optimization target and justifies annealing noise while minimizing noisy JSD.
- Limitation: Instability theorem assumes independent white-noise in D and ∇D, which may not hold for real discriminators with structured, correlated errors.
- Limitation: Experiments validating instance-noise efficacy are limited; broader benchmarks and ablations are needed.
- Feedback — dropout/Bernoulli: Request discussion whether multiplicative Bernoulli/dropout confers absolute continuity (likely not) and clarification which noise types satisfy assumptions (multiplicative Gaussian, additive Laplace/Cauchy).
- Feedback — dropout timing: Ask whether applying Bernoulli perturbations early (standard dropout) yields absolute continuity at discriminator input under common activations.
- Empirical requests: Compare baseline GAN vs instance-noise GAN on CIFAR-10/CelebA/LSUN with stability metrics (gradient norms, discriminator accuracy, loss curves), sample quality (IS, FID), and mode coverage.
- Ablation requests: Test noise type (Gaussian/Laplace/Bernoulli), magnitude and annealing schedules, noise to D only vs both D and generator backprop, and sensitivity to architectures (DCGAN, ResNet).
- Empirical claim: Show instance noise allows training discriminator closer to optimality without harming generator updates.
- Related work: Situate approach relative to WGAN, gradient penalty variants, f‑GAN, MMD‑GAN, feature matching; clarify differences/synergies between noisy-JSD minimization and direct EMD optimization.
- Assumptions/general: Discuss robustness of vanishing/instability results when D is not optimal and discriminator error is structured/correlated; consider relaxing white-noise assumption.
- Assumptions detail: Clarify “perfect alignment” probability-one statements and interactions with normalization layers and skip connections.
- Practical guidance: Provide concrete training recipe/pseudocode for instance noise with annealing (initial σ, decay schedule, stopping criteria, dataset heuristics).
- Constants/interpretation: Interpret constants in the Wasserstein–noisy-JSD bound (role and magnitude of C, relation to Lipschitz constants).
- Question 1: Does multiplicative Bernoulli noise make the input distribution absolutely continuous; if not, formalize why additive continuous noise is necessary and whether multiplicative Gaussian suffices?
- Question 2: How do instability conclusions change if D−D* and ∇D−∇D* are correlated Gaussian processes or bounded but biased; can milder conditions still yield high-variance -log D updates?
- Question 3: Which annealing schedules σ(t) work best across datasets, how sensitive are results to σ0 and decay rate, and can σ be adapted online from gradient norms or JSD estimates?
- Question 4: Can you add experiments directly comparing GAN with and without instance noise across architectures with quantitative metrics and gradient norm plots?
- Question 5: Have you tried anisotropic Gaussians or learned covariance for noise to reflect perceptual metrics, and are there pitfalls?
- Question 6: Is the bound W(Pr,Pg) ≤ 2√V + 2C√JSD tight in any regimes; can C be replaced by a data-dependent Lipschitz diameter to reduce looseness?
- Question 7: How does instance noise interact with label smoothing, batch norm, spectral normalization, or gradient penalties — additive, redundant, or conflicting effects?
