- Proves when data and model supports are low-dimensional, misaligned manifolds, the optimal discriminator is almost-everywhere flat on each support, explaining vanishing gradients (Thm. 2.4).
- Shows the non-saturating GAN update can produce heavy-tailed (Cauchy) gradient noise, causing massive instability (Thm. 2.6).
- Proposes instance noise (input perturbation) so convolved distributions become absolutely continuous, yielding smooth, distance-weighted gradients (Thm. 3.2/Cor. 3.2).
- Provides a training objective with an explicit bound that controls the true Wasserstein distance under perturbation (Thm. 3.3).
- Theory is careful, insightful, and actionable.
- Decision: Accept — because of strong, well-placed theory unifying vanishing-gradients and instability and a simple principled perturbation with guarantees and a Wasserstein connection.
- Support: Important, timely problem; mathematically sound results with precise assumptions and useful corollaries (e.g., annealing); targeted experiments qualitatively corroborate theoretical claims.
- Request: Expand relation between the perturbation trick and dropout/Bernoulli perturbations—do multiplicative Bernoulli/dropout preserve absolute-continuity guarantees, or is added Gaussian needed?
- Request: Add ablations — perturb D only vs G only vs both; Gaussian vs Bernoulli vs Laplace; isotropic vs anisotropic covariances; and annealing schedules — report gradient norm/variance and sample quality (IS/FID).
- Request: Clarify where to inject noise (raw inputs, intermediate features, or both) and practical ranges for σ and annealing.
- Request: Discuss robustness to finite-precision/approximation errors when the discriminator is near-optimal and when noise processes are correlated rather than white.
- Question: Can the authors formalize when Bernoulli/dropout-like perturbations suffice — is absolute continuity strictly necessary or can mixtures with continuous components deliver similar bounds?
- Question: How sensitive are results to the latent dimension relative to data dimension?
- Question: Do the JSD-of-noisy distributions and the Wasserstein bound track each other empirically during training as noise is annealed?
