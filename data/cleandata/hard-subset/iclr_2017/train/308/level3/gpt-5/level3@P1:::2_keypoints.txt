- Main claim: Explains why improved discriminators cause vanishing/unstable gradients in GANs and provides a principled mitigation (instance noise) with theoretical justification.
- Theorem results: With data and generator on low-dimensional manifolds, the optimal discriminator is perfect almost everywhere with zero gradient on supports (Thms 2.1–2.3).
- Vanishing gradients: For the original GAN objective, generator gradients vanish as the discriminator approaches optimality (Thm 2.4, Cor. 2.1).
- Non-saturating objective: The −log D update equals ∇θ[KL(Pg||Pr) − 2 JSD(Pg||Pr)] at θ0, explaining bias toward sharp, mode-dropping samples (Thm 2.5).
- Noisy D instability: Noisy approximations of D produce heavy-tailed, high-variance generator updates as D nears optimality (Thm 2.6).
- Mitigation: Adding continuous additive noise (“instance noise”) to discriminator inputs and backpropagated generator outputs makes distributions absolutely continuous and yields smooth, informative gradients (Thm 3.2, Cor. 3.2).
- Gradient interpretation: Smoothed gradients move samples toward the data manifold, repel overrepresented model regions, and are proportional to ∇θ JSD of smoothed distributions.
- Wasserstein/JSD relation: A bound connects Wasserstein distance between true distributions and the JSD of their smoothed variants with explicit dependence on noise variance, motivating annealed noise (Thm 3.3, Lemma 4).
- Literature placement: Analysis is well situated among prior work (Goodfellow et al., Theis et al., Narayanan & Mitter, Villani, etc.) and connects empirical heuristics to theory.
- Empirical support: Targeted experiments convincingly show gradient vanishing and increasing gradient variance as D improves (Figs. 2–3).
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Provides a strong, coherent theoretical account of GAN pathologies grounded in manifold support and discriminator optimality.
- Key reason 2: Offers a practical, theoretically justified mitigation (instance noise with annealing) and tools for analyzing training.
- Suggestion — dropout relation: Clarify whether multiplicative Bernoulli/dropout-like perturbations produce similar smoothing effects or if additive noise is crucial for convolution/density results.
- Suggestion — experiments: Add empirical comparisons of baseline vs perturbed GAN with annealed noise using gradient norms, discriminator accuracy/JSD on smoothed data, sample quality, mode coverage, and ablations (noise on D-only vs D+G).
- Suggestion — assumptions: Discuss realism of independence/white-noise assumption in Thm 2.6 and implications of compactness/smoothness assumptions for practical networks (e.g., ReLU, batchnorm).
- Suggestion — practical guidance: Provide heuristics for initial noise scale, annealing schedule, noise family choices, and monitoring JSD(Pr+||Pg+) to adapt σ.
- Suggestion — adversarial examples: Include a small empirical illustration comparing backprop with/without generator-side noise to show measure-zero adversarial directions.
- Suggestion — exposition: Consolidate assumptions near each theorem and clean minor editorial/reference issues.
- Question — noise tuning: How should initial noise variance and annealing schedules be chosen or adapted in practice (e.g., criteria based on smoothed JSD or gradient stats)?
- Question — additive vs multiplicative noise: Empirically, does additive Gaussian instance noise outperform Bernoulli/dropout-style perturbations on stability and sample quality?
- Question — Thm 2.6 sensitivity: How sensitive are heavy-tailed update conclusions to the independence/white-noise assumption; are Cauchy-like tails observed with realistic discriminators?
- Question — empirical validation: Can you show improved mode coverage and stability on synthetic mixtures and image datasets with ablations (D-only vs D+G noise; different noise families)?
- Question — generator/stochasticity: Do conclusions hold when Z has equal/higher dimension than X or when gθ includes stochastic layers?
- Question — test-time noise: Should noise be removed at test time, and what are the sample quality/coverage trade-offs as σ→0 during training?
- Overall: Coherent theoretical framework and principled stabilization technique with clear implications; adding suggested empirical comparisons and clarifications would further strengthen the paper.
