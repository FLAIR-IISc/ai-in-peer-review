**Paper Review**

**Summary**

This paper makes significant theoretical contributions to understanding the training dynamics of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). The authors analyze the effects of asymmetric divergences, identify sources of instability, and propose a smoothing-based alternative to improve GAN training. The paper provides a solid understanding of the issues in GAN training and introduces principled research directions to address them.

**Decision**

I recommend accepting this paper, with some minor revisions.

**Reasons for the decision**

1. The paper tackles a specific and important problem in the field of GANs, providing a thorough analysis of the training dynamics and identifying the sources of instability.
2. The approach is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the theoretical framework.
3. The paper supports its claims with rigorous mathematical proofs and intuitive explanations, demonstrating a high level of scientific rigor.

**Supporting arguments**

The paper's main contributions are:

1. A thorough analysis of the effects of asymmetric divergences on GAN training, which provides new insights into the behavior of GANs.
2. The identification of sources of instability in GAN training, including the perfect discriminator theorem and the consequences of using different cost functions.
3. The proposal of a smoothing-based alternative to improve GAN training, which is supported by theoretical results and intuitive explanations.

**Additional feedback**

To improve the paper, I suggest:

1. Shortening Section 2.1 and moving proofs to the appendix, as suggested in the guidelines.
2. Clarifying the definition of the Wasserstein metric and its relation to the transportation metric and the earth mover's distance.
3. Providing more intuition behind the proof of Lemma 1 and the application of the Morse Lemma.
4. Considering the addition of more experimental results to support the theoretical findings.

**Questions for the authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the experimental setup used to verify the theoretical results?
2. How do you plan to extend the smoothing-based alternative to more complex GAN architectures?
3. Can you discuss the potential applications of the Wasserstein metric in evaluating generative models?
