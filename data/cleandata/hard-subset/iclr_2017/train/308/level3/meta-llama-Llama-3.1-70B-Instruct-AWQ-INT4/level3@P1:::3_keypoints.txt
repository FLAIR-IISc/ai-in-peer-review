- Problem: The paper studies theoretical understanding of unstable GAN training dynamics and proposes principled fixes.
- Motivation: GANs generate realistic images but lack theory explaining their unstable behavior.
- Main identified instability #1: Use of asymmetric divergences can cause vanishing gradients.
- Main identified instability #2: Generator distributions being non-continuous can make the discriminator perfect and cause unstable updates.
- Proposed solution: A smoothing-based alternative that adds continuous noise to discriminator inputs to produce smoother gradients and more stable updates.
- Theoretical contribution: Provides analysis and bounds (including on Wasserstein distance) supporting the smoothing approach.
- Writing and placement: Well-motivated, well-placed in the literature, and generally well-written with clear explanations.
- Novel perspective: Uses analysis and differential topology concepts to give a new viewpoint on GAN training.
- Criticism: The paper is long, technical, and some consequences need further development.
- Suggested structural edits: Shorten Section 2.1 and move proofs to the appendix.
- Notation/terminology issues: Clarify notation and terms (e.g., definition of "closed manifolds") and specify domains of random variables.
- Presentation fixes: Improve theorem numbering to avoid confusion and fix typos (e.g., "relly" → "rely").
- Generality note: Mention that Lemma 1 holds in greater generality.
- Decision: Accept — significant contribution and practical solution to GAN instability.
- Questions for authors: Provide more intuition for why smoothing yields smoother gradients, compare to other stabilization methods (batch/spectral normalization), detail experimental setup/results, and discuss how noise distribution choice affects performance.
