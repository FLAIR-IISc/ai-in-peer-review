**Summary of the paper's contributions**

The paper provides a theoretical analysis of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), a recent and popular method in deep learning. The authors tackle the problem of understanding the training dynamics of GANs, which have been observed to be unstable in practice. They provide a rigorous study of the sources of instability in GANs, including the problem of mode dropping and the saturation of the discriminator. The paper also introduces new tools to study these problems and provides a new direction for solving them.

**Decision**

I recommend accepting this paper. The submission is strong, and the authors have made a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The paper provides deep theoretical insights into the behavior of GANs, which is essential for understanding their limitations and improving their performance.

**Supporting arguments**

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors have clearly placed their work in the context of the existing literature on GANs. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, and the authors have provided novel and interesting mathematical techniques to study the convergence of GANs. The paper also addresses a critical issue in the field, namely the stability of GANs, which has been a major concern for practitioners.

The theorems presented in the paper, particularly Theorems 2.4-2.6, are novel and provide new insights into the behavior of GANs. While I have some minor technical questions about the proof of Theorem 2.5, overall, the paper is well-written, and the arguments are clear and well-supported.

**Additional feedback**

To further improve the paper, I suggest that the authors provide more intuition about the mathematical techniques used in the proofs.
