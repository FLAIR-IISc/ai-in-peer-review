**Summary**

This paper provides a theoretical analysis of the training dynamics of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). The authors clarify why gradients vanish in original GANs and become unstable in variants. They introduce a perturbation trick to avoid these issues and provide a theoretical analysis to support their claims. The paper also explores the connection between the proposed perturbation trick and dropout/Bernoulli perturbation.

**Decision**

Based on the review, I decide to **Accept** the paper. The paper provides a valuable contribution to the understanding of GAN training dynamics, and the authors' approach is well-motivated and well-placed in the literature. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, and the results are supported by empirical evaluations.

**Supporting Arguments**

1. The paper tackles a specific and important problem in the field of GANs, which is the instability of training dynamics.
2. The authors provide a clear and well-motivated approach to addressing this problem, which is the introduction of a perturbation trick.
3. The theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides a deep understanding of the underlying mechanisms of GAN training dynamics.
4. The empirical evaluations support the theoretical claims and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Additional Feedback**

1. The authors are encouraged to discuss the connection between the proposed perturbation trick and dropout/Bernoulli perturbation in more detail.
2. The authors should consider adding more empirical evaluations to compare the performance of the proposed approach with other existing methods.
3. The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the technical terms and concepts, especially for readers who are not familiar with the field of GANs.

**Questions for the Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how the perturbation trick relates to dropout/Bernoulli perturbation?
2. How do you plan to extend the proposed approach to other types of GANs, such as conditional GANs or GANs with different architectures?
3. Can you provide more insights on how the proposed approach can be used to improve the stability of GAN training dynamics in practice?
