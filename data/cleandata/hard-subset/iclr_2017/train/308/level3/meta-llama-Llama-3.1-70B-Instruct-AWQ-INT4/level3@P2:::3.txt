**Summary of the paper**

This paper provides a theoretical analysis of the training dynamics of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). The authors identify the sources of instability in GAN training and propose a smoothing-based alternative to alleviate these issues. The paper makes a strong theoretical contribution by asking good questions and providing insightful answers. The authors also propose practical avenues to reduce instability in GAN training.

**Decision**

I decide to accept this paper with minor revisions. The paper provides a thorough analysis of the training dynamics of GANs and identifies the sources of instability. The proposed smoothing-based alternative is a nice, intuitive, and simple solution to alleviate these issues.

**Supporting arguments**

The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the training dynamics of GANs, identifying the sources of instability and proposing a smoothing-based alternative. The authors use a combination of theoretical and empirical methods to support their claims. The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the theoretical concepts.

The authors' use of analysis and differential topology concepts is interesting and provides a new perspective on the problem. The paper also proposes practical avenues to reduce instability in GAN training, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Additional feedback**

While the paper is well-written, there are some minor issues that need to be addressed. The paper is somewhat long and technical, and some parts and consequences need further development. Section 2.1 could be shortened, and the proofs could be moved to the appendix.

Additionally, there are some minor errors and typos that need to be corrected. For example, the phrasing in some places could be improved, and there are some references that are not clearly defined.

**Questions for the authors**

1. Can you clarify the relationship between the proposed smoothing-based alternative and other methods that have been proposed to alleviate instability in GAN training?
2. How do you plan to extend this work to other types of generative models, such as Variational Autoencoders (VAEs)?
3. Can you provide more details on the experimental setup and the datasets used to evaluate the proposed method?

**Minor revisions**

1. Shorten Section 2.1 and move proofs to the appendix.
2. Clarify the relationship between the proposed smoothing-based alternative and other methods that have been proposed to alleviate instability in GAN training.
3. Correct minor errors and typos, such as "relly" → "rely" and "Therefore" → "Then" in Theorem 2.5.
4. Specify the domains of the random variables used.
5. Clarify in Theorem 2.2 whether closed manifolds have boundary.
