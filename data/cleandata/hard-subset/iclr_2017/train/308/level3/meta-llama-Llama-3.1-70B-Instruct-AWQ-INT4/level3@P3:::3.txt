This paper provides a significant theoretical contribution to understanding the training dynamics of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). The authors thoroughly analyze the effects of asymmetric divergences, identify sources of instability, and propose a smoothing-based alternative to address these issues.

The paper's primary contribution is its strong theoretical foundation, which provides insightful answers to fundamental questions about GAN training. The authors' use of analysis and differential topology concepts is interesting and effectively employed to study the problems inherent in GAN training.

One of the paper's key strengths is its ability to propose practical avenues for reducing instability in GAN training. The authors' suggestion to add continuous noise to the inputs of the discriminator is a valuable contribution, as it provides a potential solution to the vanishing gradients and instability issues that plague GAN training.
