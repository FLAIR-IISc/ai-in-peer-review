- Paper provides a theoretical analysis of GAN training dynamics and identifies sources of instability.  
- Key instability sources: the optimal discriminator is perfect with near-zero gradients (vanishing gradients) and some GAN variants produce unstable generator updates.  
- Authors propose a perturbation trick—adding noise to discriminator inputs—to introduce distance on manifolds and mitigate vanishing gradients and instability.  
- The paper includes a thorough theoretical analysis and proof that the perturbation trick yields smooth gradients.  
- Experiments show the perturbation trick improves training stability and sample quality compared to original GANs and variants.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on the paper’s theoretical contribution and analysis.  
- Suggestion: include more empirical evaluations comparing perturbed GANs to other regularization methods (e.g., dropout, Bernoulli perturbations).  
- Suggestion: provide a more detailed discussion of the connection and differences between the perturbation trick and dropout/Bernoulli perturbations.  
- Questions for authors: clarify the perturbation vs. dropout/Bernoulli relationship and trade-offs.  
- Questions for authors: explain plans to extend the approach to other models (VAEs, Normalizing Flows).  
- Questions for authors: supply additional empirical comparisons to other regularization techniques.
