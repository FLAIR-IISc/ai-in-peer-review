- Tackles GAN training instability with strong theoretical analysis of asymmetric divergences and proposes a smoothing-based mitigation tied to generator output dimensionality.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Theoretical contributions are substantial and use differential topology to provide insightful explanations for GAN behavior.  
- Strength: rigorous theorems showing how support and dimensionality cause vanishing gradients or unstable updates, and a theoretically grounded smoothing technique with preliminary experiments.  
- Weakness: paper is long and technically dense; Section 2.1 should be streamlined and detailed proofs moved to the appendix.  
- Clarification needed: explicitly state that KL divergence remains nonzero if Pg is smaller than Pr at some x, and fix phrasing to “for generating not plausibly looking pictures.”  
- Lemma 1 likely holds in greater generality—acknowledge this to broaden impact.  
- Theorems 2.1 and 2.2 use basic analysis results—cite existing literature instead of reproducing full proofs.  
- Theorem 2.4 should briefly remind the reader of the domain of p(z).  
- Specify domains of all random variables throughout the paper for clarity.  
- Fix typos and inconsistencies (theorem numbering, wording in Sections 2.5 & 2.6, erroneous reference to Theorem 1.3 in Corollary 2.1).  
- Question: what is the computational cost of the proposed smoothing technique and how does it scale with data dimensionality?  
- Question: have you empirically evaluated different noise distributions (as suggested in Section 3), and what were the findings?  
- Question: how does the smoothing technique interact with other stabilization methods (e.g., spectral normalization, gradient penalty), and would combining them improve results?
