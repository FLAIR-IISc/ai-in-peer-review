- Paper makes a significant theoretical contribution to understanding GANs, addressing instability and saturation.  
- Strong recommendation to accept based on theoretical depth and novelty.  
- Theorems 2.4–2.6 are highlighted as innovative and important.  
- Analysis of generator gradient behavior via the lens of perfect discrimination is compelling.  
- Rigorous proofs link vanishing gradients and unstable updates to cost function properties and low-dimensional supports.  
- Introduces novel theoretical tools and explores softer metrics like the Wasserstein distance.  
- Finds that adding noise to the discriminator can stabilize training and is an insightful remedy.  
- The paper’s narrative progression—from identifying instability to analyzing causes to proposing noise injection—is well-structured.  
- Request for clarification on Theorem 2.5: conditions for the claimed Cauchy distribution and intuition for its appearance.  
- Suggests fleshing out connections to practice, e.g., how to choose optimal noise levels.  
- Recommends a deeper discussion of how the approach affects adversarial example vulnerabilities.
