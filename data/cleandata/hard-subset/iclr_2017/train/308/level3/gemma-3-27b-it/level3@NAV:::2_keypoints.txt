- Paper addresses theoretical understanding of GAN training instability and why gradients vanish or become unstable.
- Authors propose a perturbation trick to mitigate gradient vanishing/instability, supported by rigorous theoretical analysis.
- Core insight: interaction between discriminator accuracy and generator gradients drives instability.
- Identification of perfect discrimination as a root cause of gradient issues is a key contribution.
- Theoretical framework leveraging manifold dimensionality and optimal transport is robust and convincing.
- Connection to existing techniques (e.g., contrastive divergence) is well-established.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
- Need for more empirical validation: add experiments showing impact of perturbation on stability and sample quality (with/without perturbation).
- Suggest direct comparisons across settings and architectures to provide concrete evidence of effectiveness.
- Connection to dropout/Bernoulli perturbation is noted but not fully explored; clarify similarities/differences.
- Choice of Gaussian perturbation appears arbitrary; discuss influence of alternative noise distributions and justify selection.
