- Strength: Insightful analysis of Jensen-Shannon divergence and identification of perfect discriminators (Theorems 2.1 & 2.2).
- Strength: Detailed examination of gradient behavior and vanishing gradients (Theorems 2.4 & 2.6).

- Strength: Proposed smoothing technique toward stabilization, grounded in Wasserstein-related concepts.

- Weakness: Empirical validation is limited, largely graphical, and lacks thorough analysis.
- Weakness: Figures (e.g., Figure 2) lack clear labels and explanations.
- Weakness: No quantitative comparisons or metrics (e.g., Inception Score, FID) to demonstrate practical improvements.
- Weakness: Arguments rely heavily on asymptotic/limit cases, raising questions about relevance to practical GAN training.

- Recommendation: Strengthen empirical evaluation with baseline comparisons on standard datasets and quantitative metrics.

- Recommendation: Proofread and fix notation/theorem-numbering inconsistencies.
- Recommendation: Expand practical discussion of Theorem 3.3 and the role of Wasserstein distance in GANs.
