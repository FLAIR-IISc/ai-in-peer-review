- Recommendation: Accept — significant theoretical contributions on GAN convergence and stability.  
- Main contribution: Rigorous analysis explaining why GAN training is unstable due to distributional properties and perfect discriminators.  
- Theorems 2.1 & 2.2: Disjoint supports or low-dimensional manifolds permit perfect discriminators, causing vanishing gradients and unstable updates.  
- Theorems 2.4–2.6: Analyze consequences of different GAN cost functions (original vs. -logD) on training dynamics.  
- Theorem 2.5 / JSD connection: Links JSD, GAN cost functions, and mode dropping via implicit minimization of KL(Pg||Pr).  
- Mode dropping explanation: Generators prioritize realistic-looking samples over covering the full data distribution.  
- Proposed remedy: Introduces a softened metric based on Wasserstein distance combined with noise injection to smooth distributions.  
- Theorems 3.2 & 3.3: Theoretically justify noise injection and Wasserstein-based metrics improving gradient behavior.  
- Proof detail concern: Some proof steps (notably Thm 2.6’s Cauchy gradient claim) need more explicit derivation or references.  
- Experimental concern: Empirical validation is brief; more detailed experiments (esp. demonstrating noise injection benefits) are needed.  
- Relation to recent work: Paper should discuss connections to newer GAN-theory approaches (e.g., spectral normalization, gradient penalties).  
- Assumptions clarity: Need discussion on sensitivity of results to assumptions about smoothness and dimensionality of distributions.  
- Q1 for authors: Please justify/reference the claim that gradient components become Cauchy distributed in Thm 2.5.  
- Q2 for authors: How sensitive is Thm 3.3’s bound to the choice of ball diameter C and to data dimensionality?  
- Q3 for authors: What noise distribution and variance do you recommend for injection, and how do they affect GAN performance?  
- Q4 for authors: Provide a concrete example illustrating how the proposed Wasserstein-based metric improves sample quality over JSD.
