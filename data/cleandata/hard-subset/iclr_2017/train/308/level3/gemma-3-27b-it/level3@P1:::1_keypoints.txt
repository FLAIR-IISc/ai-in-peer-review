- Decision: Accept — valuable, novel theoretical analysis of GAN training dynamics.  
- Main focus: rigorously addresses instability and saturation in GAN training.  
- Key issue identified: when the discriminator is too good, generator updates vanish or become unstable.  
- Theorems 2.1 & 2.2: perfect discrimination is often achievable due to the generator’s low-dimensional output manifold and disjoint supports.  
- Theorems 2.4–2.6: original GAN cost causes vanishing gradients; alternative cost leads to instability.  
- Methodology: employs measure theory and differential geometry for rigorous, novel analysis.  
- Proposed remedy: introduces a novel metric and advocates using noisy samples to mitigate instability.  
- Request: expand Theorem 2.5 proof, clarifying the gradient connection to KL and JSD divergences.  
- Request: provide more intuition for Theorem 3.3 on the Wasserstein–JSD relationship for noisy distributions.  
- Suggestion: include minimal experimental validation of the proposed noise-based approach.  
- Presentation issue: notation inconsistency (e.g., absolute continuity); define terms consistently early.  
- Question: how sensitive are results to assuming discriminator noise is a centered Gaussian process?  
- Question: how should one select/tune the noise variance for regularization in practice?  
- Question: challenges foreseen in applying insights to complex/high-resolution GAN architectures?  
- Question: provide experimental details for Figure 2 (specific settings and architectures).
