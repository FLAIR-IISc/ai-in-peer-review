- Paper analyzes why GAN training is unstable, attributing issues to vanishing gradients and discriminator–generator interplay, and proposes adding noise to discriminator inputs as a theoretical remedy.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical treatment of GAN dynamics explaining failures of original and modified cost functions.  
- Strength: Theorems 2.1 and 2.2 show existence of “perfect discriminators” and clarify why updates become problematic as the discriminator improves.  
- Strength: Introducing a “softer” metric via input noise is well-motivated and shown theoretically to improve gradient behavior.  
- Strength: Connections to contrastive divergence and Wasserstein distance provide useful context.  
- Weakness: Empirical validation is limited; Figures 1–3 are illustrative but not comprehensive.  
- Weakness: Lacks direct experimental comparison of GANs trained with and without the proposed noise perturbation on sample quality and stability metrics (e.g., Inception Score, FID).  
- Weakness: No direct comparison to other stabilization methods such as Wasserstein GAN or Spectral Normalization.  
- Suggestion: Discuss relationship to dropout/Bernoulli perturbation and clarify theoretical/practical differences.  
- Suggestion: Expand empirical evaluation, replicate key figures with noise addition, and test different noise distributions and variances.  
- Suggestion: Provide practical implementation details on choosing and possibly adjusting noise variance during training.  
- Suggestion: Discuss computational cost and whether the noise step adds significant overhead.  
- Question: How does the proposed noise perturbation relate to dropout and what are the key theoretical distinctions?  
- Question: What GAN architecture and hyperparameters were used for Figures 2 and 3?  
- Question: What guidance can you give for selecting the noise variance (σ^2) and does it depend on dataset or architecture?  
- Question: Is there a recommended noise distribution (Gaussian, uniform, etc.) and what are the tradeoffs?
