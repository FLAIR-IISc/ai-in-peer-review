- Tackles the fundamental problem of understanding GAN training dynamics and provides a significant theoretical contribution.
- Recommendation: accept.
- Strength: offers deep theoretical insight rather than incremental empirical improvements.
- Well-motivated and well-positioned within literature; connects to maximum likelihood and Jensen-Shannon divergence.
- Clearly states research questions: worsening updates with better discriminator, instability sources, and mitigation strategies.
- Strong theoretical support via Theorems 2.1, 2.2, 2.4, 2.5, 2.6; proofs appear sound (with a noted question on Theorem 2.5).
- Demonstrates link between perfect discriminator scenarios, vanishing gradients (Thm 2.4), and unstable updates (Thm 2.6).
- Proposes a noise-based approach (Section 3) with Theorems 3.1–3.3 linking Wasserstein distance and JSD of noisy distributions.
- Carefully considers discriminator approximation quality and the role of adversarial examples.
- Writing is dense; would benefit from more intuitive explanations alongside formal derivations.
- Theorem 2.5 assumes independent noise processes r and η; request discussion of sensitivity to violations of this assumption.
- Question about robustness of instability result to small correlations introduced by iterative training.
- Request brief discussion of the computational overhead of the proposed noise-based approach.
- Suggests limited empirical validation (e.g., training curves or sample quality comparisons) to strengthen claims.
- Practical concern about computing Wasserstein distance; asks for discussion of approximations or efficient estimation methods.
