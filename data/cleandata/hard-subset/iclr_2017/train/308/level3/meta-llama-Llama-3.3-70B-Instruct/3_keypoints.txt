- Proposes using answer information to improve question classification by enhancing question representations.  
- Introduces Group Sparse Autoencoders (GSA) and Group Sparse Convolutional Neural Networks (GSCNNs) to encode group structure from answer sets.  
- Well-motivated approach grounded in dictionary learning and sparse coding.  
- Clear and well-structured presentation of methodology (GSA and GSCNNs).  
- Strong theoretical foundations, including connections to analysis and differential topology.  
- Proposes techniques to avoid instability in GANs (noted as an interesting contribution).  
- Experimental results on four datasets show significant improvements over strong baselines with rigorous setup and metrics.  
- Paper is long and technically dense, which may hinder accessibility for some readers.  
- Relationship between GSA and traditional sparse coding needs further development.  
- Suggested revisions: shorten basic autoencoder introduction and move proofs to the appendix.  
- Need for corrections/clarifications in theorem numbering and references to improve readability.  
- Reviewer questions to authors: how group-sparse constraint is enforced in GSCNNs and its effect; how hyperparameters (number of groups, sparsity) are selected; and potential applications beyond question classification.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the points above.
