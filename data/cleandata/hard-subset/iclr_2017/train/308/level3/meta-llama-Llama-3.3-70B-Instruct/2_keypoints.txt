- Proposal: Introduces Group Sparse Autoencoders (GSA) with inter- and intra-group sparse constraints for dictionary learning and sparse coding.
- Architecture: Embeds GSA into CNNs (GSCNN) so CNNs learn more meaningful, dictionary-based representations.
- Results: Demonstrates significant improvements over strong baselines on four datasets.
- Decision: Recommend accept due to novelty and effectiveness of GSA and its CNN application.
- Motivation: Well-motivated approach, clear problem statement, and inspiration from dictionary learning/sparse coding.
- Clarity: Provides a clear explanation of the framework and its integration into CNNs.
- Evidence: Empirical results support the claims of improved performance.
- Suggestion: Discuss relationship to other sparse coding methods such as Sparse Group Lasso (SGL).
- Suggestion: Provide sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (sparsity levels, number of groups).
- Suggestion: Explore and report applicability to other tasks (e.g., text classification, information retrieval).
- Question: How were hyperparameters (sparsity parameters, number of groups) chosen and how do they affect performance?
- Question: How does GSA compare to other sparse coding approaches like SGL in computational complexity and optimization?
- Question: Have the authors tried the model on other tasks and are there any preliminary results?
