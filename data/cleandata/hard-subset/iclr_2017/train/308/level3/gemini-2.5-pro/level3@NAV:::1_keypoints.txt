- Presents a rigorous theoretical analysis of GAN training dynamics, focusing on saturation and instability rather than heuristics.  
- Shows that when data lie on low-dimensional manifolds, the optimal discriminator can produce vanishing or unreliable gradients for the generator.  
- Formally analyzes this vanishing-gradient phenomenon across different GAN cost functions.  
- Demonstrates that the original GAN cost yields vanishing gradients while the "-log D" formulation leads to high-variance, unstable updates.  
- Proposes a theoretically-grounded direction for achieving more stable GAN training.  
- Theorems 2.4–2.6 are novel and introduce techniques to formalize instability and gradient issues.  
- Provides foundational theoretical justification for empirical phenomena observed by practitioners.  
- Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance, calling the paper an outstanding, foundational contribution.  
- Minor request: clarify the step in Theorem 2.5 where results for JSD and KL gradients are subtracted and ensure aligned assumptions.
