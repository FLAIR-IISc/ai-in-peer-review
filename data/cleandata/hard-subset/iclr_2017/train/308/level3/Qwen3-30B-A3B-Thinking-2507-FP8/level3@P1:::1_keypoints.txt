- Significant theoretical contribution analyzing GAN training dynamics, instability, and saturation.
- Main contributions: (1) proof of perfect discrimination on low-dimensional manifolds, (2) demonstration of vanishing gradients and unstable updates, (3) solution using noisy distributions.
- Strong recommendation for acceptance due to groundbreaking theoretical analysis.
- Framework moves beyond heuristics and enables principled, practical improvements to GAN training.
- Theorem 2.3: common divergences (JSD, KL) are maximized even when distributions are close, explaining optimization failure.
- Theorem 2.4: rigorous proof that gradients vanish as the discriminator approaches optimality.
- Theorem 2.6: shows instability of the alternative cost via a novel connection to Cauchy distributions.
- Analysis of approximations to the optimal discriminator is a clever, well-motivated approach aligned with practice.
- Proposed solution (Section 3): adding noise to inputs creates continuous/noisy distributions that avoid perfect discrimination.
- Theorem 3.3: relates Wasserstein distance of original distributions to JSD of noisy distributions, enabling controlled, stable training.
- Minor request: expand derivation in Theorem 2.5 to clarify emergence of the KL(Pg‖Pr) term.
- Minor concern: assumption of independent Gaussian noise in Theorem 2.6 may be overly restrictive—discuss practical impact.
- Two clarification requests: intuition for the KL−2·JSD gradient form in Theorem 2.5, and a brief note on why only gθ(z) depends on θ in Theorem 3.2’s gradient.
