- Paper makes a significant theoretical contribution explaining GAN training instability.
- Shows instability arises because data and generator distributions lie on low-dimensional manifolds.
- Proves the optimal discriminator becomes perfect with zero gradients almost everywhere, causing vanishing/unstable gradients for the generator.
- Theorems 2.4–2.6 provide strong mathematical justification for these claims.
- Analyzes original GAN cost and the -log D alternative, explaining why -log D can still be unstable.
- Proposes adding noise to inputs to create “softer” metrics that yield meaningful gradients and are theoretically motivated.
- Recommends acceptance due to novelty, rigor, and practical importance.
- Suggests more practical guidance on choosing noise parameters (variance, distribution) and trade-offs between stability and sample quality.
- Recommends strengthening connections to practical techniques like spectral normalization and gradient penalty.
- Asks for clarification in Theorem 2.5 proof: details on the step deriving equation (3) and the gradient–KL divergence relationship.
- Requests concrete implementation examples showing how to tune noise parameters for specific datasets or architectures.
