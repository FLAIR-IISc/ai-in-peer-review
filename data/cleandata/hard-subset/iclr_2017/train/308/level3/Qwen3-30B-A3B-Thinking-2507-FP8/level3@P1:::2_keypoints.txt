- Strong theoretical contribution explaining GAN instability: optimal discriminator becomes perfect on low-dimensional manifolds, causing vanishing generator gradients.  
- First rigorous proof that common alternative generator update has unstable updates with infinite variance, explaining empirical instability.  
- Proposes noise-regularization (adding continuous noise to discriminator inputs) as a principled fix that creates continuous distributions and smooth gradients.  
- Theoretical link to Wasserstein distance: noise-regularized training provably minimizes Wasserstein distance between original distributions, offering an alternative to Jensen–Shannon.  
- Rigorous mathematical results highlighted: Lemma 1 (generator distributions lie on low-dimensional manifolds) and Theorems 2.4–2.6 (vanishing gradients and instability).  
- Theorem 3.3 connects the proposed noise approach to practical training by showing Wasserstein minimization.
