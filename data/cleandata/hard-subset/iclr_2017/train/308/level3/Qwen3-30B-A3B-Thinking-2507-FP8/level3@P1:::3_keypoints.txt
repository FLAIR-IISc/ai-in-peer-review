- Significant theoretical contribution identifying low-dimensional data support as core cause of GAN instability and vanishing gradients.  
- Rigorous differential-topology analysis showing optimal discriminator is constant almost everywhere on data and generator manifolds.  
- Shows vanishing gradients explain why GAN training becomes difficult as discriminator improves.  
- Analyzes both original GAN loss and -logD loss, revealing the latter leads to unstable updates (Cauchy-distributed gradients).  
- Proposes adding noise to discriminator inputs to smooth distributions and produce stable, non-vanishing gradients.  
- Provides rigorous justification for noise injection, including Wasserstein-distance bounds.  
- Connects results to Wasserstein metric as a robust distance measure not affected by low-dimensional supports.
