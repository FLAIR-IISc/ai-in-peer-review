- The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis showing GAN instability arises when data and generator supports are disjoint or on low-dimensional manifolds, making the optimal discriminator constant a.e., causing vanishing gradients and unstable updates with infinite variance.

- The authors propose a perturbation trick (adding noise to inputs) that makes distributions continuous, creating “softer” metrics and avoiding the identified theoretical problems.

- Carefully designed experiments (Figures 2 and 3) support and illustrate the theoretical claims.

- The paper is well-structured, clearly written, and offers both theoretical insights and practical guidance for GAN training.

- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; the perturbation trick is theoretically grounded and potentially impactful in practice.

- The paper fills a critical gap by rigorously explaining fundamental causes of GAN instability and vanishing gradients.

- The approach is well-motivated, builds on foundational GAN work, and connects to optimal transport and manifold learning.

- Theoretical claims are supported by rigorous proofs (notably Theorems 2.4, 2.6, and 3.3) and corroborating experiments.
