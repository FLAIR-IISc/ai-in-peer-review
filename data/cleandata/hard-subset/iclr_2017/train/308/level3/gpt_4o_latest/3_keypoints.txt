- Decision: Accept.
- Main contribution: Rigorous theoretical analysis of GAN instability and saturation with a novel smoothening framework.
- Method: Uses differential topology and divergence functions to identify instability sources and propose mitigation.
- Practical proposal: Introduces noise injection to smooth distributions for stable training dynamics and interpretable gradients.
- Evidence: Detailed mathematical proofs and targeted experiments that support theoretical claims.
- Strength: Strong theoretical contributions that advance understanding of GAN training dynamics.
- Strength: Proposed smoothening and noise-based approach is intuitive, implementable, and addresses vanishing gradients.
- Strength: Experimental results validate assumptions and quantify discussed phenomena.
- Weakness: Paper is overly technical and lengthy, limiting accessibility for broader audiences.
- Weakness: Section 2.1 should be shortened and proofs moved to the appendix per guidelines.
- Weakness: Some theorems and assumptions need clearer statements (domains of variables, practical implications) and better numbering.
- Minor issues: Typos and phrasing errors (e.g., "relly" → "rely", misuse of "Therefore"), and inconsistent theorem references.
- Question: Request more intuition/examples for Theorem 3.3 and its relation to the Wasserstein metric.
- Question: How sensitive is the smoothening approach to noise distribution and variance, and are there selection guidelines?
- Question: What is the computational overhead of noise injection and smoothened metrics compared to standard GAN training?
