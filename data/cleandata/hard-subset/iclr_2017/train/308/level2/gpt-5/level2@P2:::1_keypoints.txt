- Paper formalizes that when data and model live on differing low-dim manifolds, the optimal discriminator is perfect and locally constant, explaining vanishing generator gradients.  
- Shows the non-saturating generator update (−log D) is locally equivalent to optimizing KL(Pg||Pr) − 2 JSD(Pg||Pr), biasing toward plausible samples and tolerating mode dropping.  
- Proves a stylized result that noisy discriminator errors can produce heavy-tailed (Cauchy) gradient noise, offering a theoretical mechanism for empirical instability.  
- Proposes instance noise (adding continuous input noise and annealing) to force overlapping supports, derive smooth generator gradients, and stabilize training.  
- Derives a bound linking the Wasserstein distance between true distributions to the JSD of noisy distributions plus a noise-variance term, motivating noise annealing.  
- Decision: Weak Accept — insightful and mostly sound analysis addressing important GAN pathologies, with a practical regularization proposal.  
- Main strength: rigorous identification of manifold-support mismatch as a core source of perfect discrimination and vanishing gradients.  
- Main strength: clear theoretical explanation for mode dropping via the −log D local equivalence result.  
- Main strength: instance-noise proposal is well-motivated and yields intuitive gradient dynamics (attract data, repel model).  
- Concern: Wasserstein section is inconsistent — mixes W1 and W2 definitions/inequalities and needs correction.  
- Concern: Pinsker/JSD coefficient typos and imprecise constants undermine precision (though not qualitative claims).  
- Concern: Theorem 2.6 assumes unrealistic independence of discriminator noise/gradients; result is illustrative but needs qualification or empirical support.  
- Concern: Several technical assumptions (compactness, smoothness) are strong and should be stated and discussed more carefully.  
- Concern: Empirical validation is limited and lacks comparisons to common stabilization techniques and comprehensive benchmarks.  
- Suggested fix: Choose W1 or W2 consistently and adjust lemmas/inequalities and constants accordingly.  
- Suggested fix: Correct JSD/Pinsker constants and isolate constants for clarity.  
- Suggested fix: Better qualify Theorem 2.6’s scope and empirically test gradient-tail behavior as D approaches optimality.  
- Suggested fix: Expand experiments to show instance-noise + annealing improves metrics across datasets and versus baselines, and provide practical noise schedules/ablations.  
- Questions for authors: resolve metric inconsistency and restate Theorem 3.3 with correct Wasserstein definition and constants.  
- Questions for authors: provide sensitivity analysis and practical recipe for instance-noise (initial σ, decay schedule) across datasets/architectures.  
- Questions for authors: report empirical evidence of heavy-tailed generator gradients as D nears optimality (tail indices, variance/quantiles).  
- Questions for authors: compare instance noise empirically to feature matching, minibatch discrimination, gradient penalties, etc.  
- Questions for authors: can the analysis extend to f-GANs or Wasserstein GANs with Lipschitz critics, and how do conclusions change under those constraints?
