- Formalization: shows generators with piecewise-linear or smooth monotone activations produce distributions supported on a countable union of manifolds with dimension ≤ latent dim (Lemma 1).
- Perfect discrimination: disjoint or misaligned low-dimensional supports admit an optimal discriminator with accuracy 1 and zero gradient a.e., causing JSD saturation and divergent KLs (Theorems 2.1–2.3).
- Generator gradient analysis: original GAN objective gradients vanish as D→optimal; the −log D alternative yields centered Cauchy updates with infinite variance under noisy D, explaining instability (Theorems 2.4, 2.6).
- Instance-noise remedy: adding continuous input noise smooths supports, yields explicit gradients toward the data manifold, and backpropagating generator noise makes updates equal the gradient of JSD between noisy distributions (Theorem 3.2, Corollary 3.2).
- Wasserstein link: provides a bound relating the noiseless Wasserstein distance to the JSD between noisy distributions plus noise variance, justifying annealing noise while optimizing a tractable divergence (Theorem 3.3).
- Experiments: targeted measurements demonstrate vanishing gradients for the original objective and high-variance/exploding gradients for −log D as D improves, supporting theoretical claims.
- Decision: Accept — due to rigorous geometric/measure-theoretic analysis and a practical, theoretically grounded stabilization strategy.
- Significance/clarity: addresses core GAN pathologies (saturation, vanishing/unstable gradients, mode dropping) clearly and significantly.
- Technical correctness/novelty: careful assumptions, clean vanishing-gradient results, novel explanation of −log D instability, and insightful instance-noise/JSD derivations; Wasserstein bound is useful.
- Literature placement: complements f-divergence and IPM discussions and empirical stabilization work, though related-work coverage could be expanded.
- Empirical support caveat: experiments are appropriate but limited to targeted statistics rather than full training outcomes.
- Essential feedback — clarify assumptions: explicitly state architectural conditions (biases, batchnorm, intermediate noise) and robustness when supports have small absolutely continuous parts.
- Essential feedback — discriminator error model: relax the independent white-noise assumption for D and ∇D and provide arguments or empirical evidence for more realistic, correlated/bounded errors.
- Essential feedback — validate instance noise empirically: add image-dataset experiments showing stabilization/diversity improvements, report gradient stats, and give practical guidance on noise choice and annealing.
- Nice-to-have: relate to f-divergence GANs and IPMs, quantify adversarial-example mitigation from generator noise, and discuss tightness/constants in Theorem 3.3.
- Rebuttal questions: how to choose/anneal noise in practice; effects if generator noise makes Pg absolutely continuous; extending Theorem 2.6 to bounded/correlated errors; sensitivity to noise distribution/covariance; empirical correlation between noisy-JSD minimization and reduced noiseless Wasserstein.
