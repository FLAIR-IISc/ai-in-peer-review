- Paper analyzes GAN instability by characterizing generator gradients under common objectives for manifold-supported distributions.  
- Claim 1: With real and model distributions on non-overlapping low-dimensional manifolds, optimal discriminators are perfect and locally flat, causing vanishing gradients for the original GAN loss.  
- Claim 2: The −log D generator update avoids vanishing gradients but is intrinsically unstable under realistic discriminator approximation noise.  
- Claim 3: Adding continuous instance noise smooths distributions, yields well-behaved gradients tied to ∇ JSD of the smoothed distributions, and connects JSD of noisy distributions to true Wasserstein distance with a noise-controlled term.  
- Decision: Accept — strong theoretical advances and a principled, practical remedy (instance noise with annealing) supported by analysis and preliminary experiments.  
- Supporting: Theorems 2.1–2.3 rigorously show perfect discrimination and measure-theoretic issues on manifolds.  
- Supporting: Theorem 2.4 formalizes vanishing gradients as D→D* for the original loss.  
- Supporting: Theorem 2.6 explains −log D instability via heavy-tailed updates from discriminator noise.  
- Supporting: Section 3 and Corollary 3.2 show smoothing restores absolute continuity and stable gradients; Theorem 3.3 gives a bound linking smoothed JSD to Wasserstein distance.  
- Literature/tools: Work appropriately situates itself using Sard’s lemma, Pinsker, and Kantorovich–Rubinstein duality.  
- Empirical feedback: Expand validation—ablate noise types/variance schedules, compare to strong baselines, report quantitative metrics and stability diagnostics.  
- Modeling assumptions: Discuss realism of white, independent noise across x and explore correlated or gradient-structured noise models.  
- Practicality: Clarify applicability to finite datasets and discrete pixelization, and address nonsmooth activations (e.g., ReLU corners) in proofs.  
- Reproducibility: Provide implementation details—noise annealing schedules, architectures, and optimization settings.  
- Questions: How sensitive are results to the noise distribution/covariance and how should practitioners choose/anneal it?  
- Questions: Do instability results hold under more realistic, correlated discriminator error models?  
- Questions: Can you quantify practical bias–variance trade-offs introduced by noise?  
- Questions: How does instance-noise GAN empirically compare to WGAN variants and gradient-penalty methods on identical setups?
