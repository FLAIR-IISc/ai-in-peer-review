- Tackles instability in GAN training and aims to provide a theoretical foundation beyond heuristics.  
- Identifies saturation and mode dropping as key issues arising from generator–discriminator interplay on low-dimensional manifolds.  
- Rigorously proves why perfect discrimination causes vanishing gradients or instability.  
- Proposes a noise-based stabilization direction and introduces new analytical tools.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection with encouragement to revise and resubmit.  
- Presentation is dense and lacks clarity about practical implications of the proposed solution.  
- The noise-based solution feels underdeveloped and the leap from theory to method is insufficiently justified.  
- Theoretical proofs and analysis of cost functions are mathematically sound and insightful.  
- Experimental validation is weak: limited details and unclear effectiveness of the proposed method in practice.  
- Discussion of Wasserstein distance vs. JSD under noise is promising but needs concrete examples and comparative results.  
- Lacks thorough comparison to existing stabilization techniques (e.g., WGAN, spectral normalization).  
- Suggested improvements: clearer theorem explanations with examples, stronger quantitative experiments vs. baselines, analysis of computational cost and scalability, discussion of limitations and future work, and principled guidance on selecting noise variance.
