- Paper analyzes GAN training dynamics, identifies instability and saturation, and proposes softened metrics/distributions (e.g., Wasserstein) to mitigate vanishing gradients and unstable updates.
- Recommendation: Reject, with strong encouragement to resubmit after significant revision.
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical analysis that correctly identifies problems from perfect discrimination and their effects on gradient flow.
- Strength: Insightful examination of original and alternative GAN cost functions explaining instability and mode dropping.
- Strength: Proposal of softer metrics/distributions (notably Wasserstein) as a promising direction for stability.
- Weakness: Presentation is overly dense with heavy notation and insufficient intuitive explanation or illustrative examples.
- Weakness: Empirical validation is limited; figures are poorly integrated and theoretical claims lack strong experimental support.
- Weakness: Unclear how “softened metrics” are implemented in practice—how noise is added and its impact on sample quality and convergence is not demonstrated.
- Weakness: Related work discussion is cursory and does not fully contextualize contributions within the broader GAN literature.
- Weakness: Connection between theoretical results and practical training guidance for stable GANs is not clearly established.
- Fix: Improve clarity—simplify notation, add intuition, break complex arguments into smaller steps and include examples.
- Fix: Strengthen experiments—compare to WGAN/LSGAN on benchmarks, show sample quality, convergence speed, stability, and gradient/discriminator behavior plots.
- Fix: Provide practical guidance—specify noise distributions/variance, learning rate/hyperparameter recommendations, and implementation details.
- Fix: Expand related work and explicitly compare the proposed approach to existing stability methods.
- Questions to address in revision: sensitivity to noise choice; computational cost of adding noise; performance across datasets/architectures; limitations/failure conditions; provide concrete implementation examples (TensorFlow/PyTorch).
