**Summary**

This paper makes theoretical steps towards fully understanding the training dynamics of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). The authors identify the sources of instability in GAN training, including the fact that the optimal discriminator will be perfect and its gradient will be zero almost everywhere when the supports of the real and generated distributions lie on low-dimensional manifolds. They also show that the commonly used alternative cost function leads to unstable updates. To address these issues, the authors propose adding continuous noise to the inputs of the discriminator, which smoothes the distribution of the probability mass and leads to a softer metric. They also introduce a new metric, the Wasserstein distance, which incorporates the notion of distance between points in the manifolds.
