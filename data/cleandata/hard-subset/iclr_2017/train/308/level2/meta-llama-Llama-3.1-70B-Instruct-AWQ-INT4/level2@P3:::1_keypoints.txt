- The paper provides theoretical advances in understanding GAN training dynamics and sources of instability.  
- Identifies that an optimal discriminator becomes perfect with zero gradients almost everywhere when real and generated supports lie on low-dimensional manifolds.  
- Shows commonly used alternative generator cost leads to unstable updates.  
- Proposes adding continuous noise to discriminator inputs to smooth distributions and produce a softer metric.  
- Introduces the Wasserstein distance as a new, meaningful metric incorporating distances between manifold points.
