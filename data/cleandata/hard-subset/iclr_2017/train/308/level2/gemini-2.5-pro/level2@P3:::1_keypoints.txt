- The paper rigorously explains GAN instability by showing generators lie on low-dimensional manifolds and optimal discriminators perfectly separate non-overlapping supports, causing vanishing gradients.  
- It analyzes the −log(D) generator objective, deriving that it minimizes a combination of reverse KL and negative JSD and can produce gradients with infinite variance (Cauchy), explaining mode dropping and extreme instability.  
- Proposes adding instance noise to discriminator inputs to make distributions absolutely continuous, yielding smooth gradients and meaningful learning signals.  
- Introduces the Wasserstein distance as a better metric for manifold-supported distributions and provides a bound (Theorem 3.3) linking the proposed noisy-GAN training to minimizing W(Pr, Pg).  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: First rigorous, convincing theoretical account of common GAN failure modes (vanishing gradients, instability with −log(D)).  
- Key reason 2: Well-motivated, theoretically grounded solution (instance noise) tied to minimizing Wasserstein distance.  
- Supporting argument: Formalization of the low-dimensional manifold pathology and proofs that JSD/KL are uninformative in this setting.  
- Supporting argument: Novel formal analysis of −log(D), identification of its implicit objective, and proof of infinite-variance gradients under realistic noise.  
- Supporting argument: Principled solution with proofs that noise fixes non-overlapping supports and that gradients move generator manifold toward data manifold.  
- Suggestion: Add stronger empirical validation (e.g., CIFAR-10) to demonstrate practical benefits of the noise-based method.  
- Suggestion: Discuss limitations of the white-noise assumption and effects of structured/correlated discriminator errors.  
- Suggestion: Provide practical guidance on the noise variance hyperparameter and potential annealing schedules.  
- Question: How robust is the Cauchy/infinite-variance conclusion in Theorem 2.6 to different noise models (heavy-tailed or correlated noise)?  
- Question: Does minimizing the upper bound in Theorem 3.3 guarantee monotonic reduction of the true Wasserstein distance during training?  
- Question: Was adding noise to only real or only generated samples considered, and is adding noise to both necessary/theoretically required?
