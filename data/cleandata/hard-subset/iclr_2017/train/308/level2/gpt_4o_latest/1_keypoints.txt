- Paper provides theoretical analysis of GAN training instability and saturation, introduces new analytical tools, and proposes practical mitigation strategies.
- Proposes smoothing distributions via noise injection and advocates the Wasserstein metric as a more robust alternative to Jensen–Shannon divergence.
- Authors back theoretical claims with targeted experiments and present open questions for future work.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Novelty: fills a critical theoretical gap in GAN literature and offers innovative ideas (noise smoothing, Wasserstein view).
- Theoretical rigor: solid proofs (e.g., "Perfect Discrimination Theorems") and analysis of gradient vanishing/instability.
- Practical relevance: proposed noise injection and softer metrics have clear implications for improving GAN stability and performance.
- Experimental validation: experiments effectively illustrate the theoretical phenomena, though are targeted rather than extensive.
- Suggestion — clarity: improve explanations and add more intuitive descriptions for readers less versed in advanced math.
- Suggestion — experimental scope: expand empirical evaluation across more GAN architectures and datasets.
- Suggestion — related work: provide more detailed comparisons with existing stabilization methods (e.g., Wasserstein GANs).
- Suggestion — open questions: offer more concrete directions or frameworks to address the posed open questions.
- Question for authors: how does noise injection empirically compare to stabilization techniques like gradient penalty in WGANs?
- Question for authors: can the theoretical results be extended to other generative models (e.g., VAEs)?
- Question for authors: how sensitive are methods to noise choice and magnitude, and are there practical tuning guidelines?
- Conclusion: significant theoretical contribution with practical value; strengths outweigh weaknesses and warrants acceptance.
