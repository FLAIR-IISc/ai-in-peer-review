- Paper analyzes GAN instability from data and generator concentrating on low-dimensional, disjoint manifolds, causing perfect discriminators, JSD saturation, infinite KLs, and vanishing or unstable generator gradients.
- Formalizes claims with transversality-based “perfect discrimination” theorems, proves vanishing gradients as D→D*, and shows -log D gradients can be heavy-tailed under a simple noise model.
- Proposes instance noise (added to real, fake, and generator backprop inputs) to smooth distributions, ensure overlapping support, and produce meaningful distance-weighted attraction/repulsion gradients.
- Provides an upper bound linking Wasserstein distance between original distributions to noise variance and JSD of convolved distributions, motivating annealing noise while minimizing smoothed JSD.
- Decision: Weak accept.
- Key reason: Principled, largely rigorous analysis that explains observed GAN pathologies and offers a simple, actionable remedy (instance noise + annealing).
- Key reason: Connects JSD-based training to optimal transport intuition via a bound, bridging theory and practice.
- Supporting: Manifold-support and transversality results are compelling and align with empirical observations; JSD saturation and diverging KLs are important.
- Supporting: Vanishing-gradient result is well motivated and using a Sobolev-type norm for D’s approximation is reasonable.
- Supporting: Characterization of -log D update as gradient of KL(Pg||Pr) − 2 JSD(Pg||Pr) clarifies its realism-seeking and destabilizing tendencies.
- Supporting: Instance-noise gradient form matches intuition of attracting samples to data-density regions and repelling overconfident model regions.
- Supporting: Adding noise to the generator during backprop closes the adversarial “measure-zero” loophole.
- Supporting: Bounding W(Pr,Pg) by noise variance and smoothed JSD gives a principled rationale to anneal noise and train D near-optimality on the smoothed problem.
- Criticism: Assumption of white-noise independence between D*−D and its gradient in Theorem 2.6 is unrealistic; heavy-tailed conclusion may overstate typical variance blow-up.
- Criticism: Ambiguity/inconsistency in Wasserstein metric usage (W1 vs W2); Lemma 4 and W≤Cδ bound need disambiguation and corrected constants/inequalities.
- Criticism: Paper lacks quantitative empirical validation of gradient norms, stability, and comparisons to existing heuristics.
- Criticism: Related work coverage is incomplete—missing connections to prior instance-noise/noise-based stabilization, f-GANs, energy/feature matching methods, and WGAN literature.
- Suggestion: Clarify W1 vs W2 choice and fix inequalities/constants in Lemma 4 and Theorem 3.3; state proper bounds (e.g., W1(P,P+ε) ≤ E||ε||).
- Suggestion: In Lemma 1, specify domain assumptions on latent Z and network architecture to justify finite-union-of-manifolds claim for ReLU nets, or relax to countable union.
- Suggestion: Temper Theorem 2.6 by adopting a more realistic noise/error model (correlated, bounded, Lipschitz) or label the result as heuristic and provide empirical corroboration.
- Suggestion: Expand related work to include f-GAN, noise/label smoothing, feature matching, and optimal-transport/WGAN methods.
- Suggestion: Provide empirical validation: ablations on noise level/annealing schedule, metrics like FID/precision-recall, and comparisons to baseline stabilization tricks; show D trained near-optimality on smoothed problem.
- Suggestion: Improve exposition—fix typos, standardize notation (e.g., JSD), and clarify when D is fixed vs re-optimized.
- Question: Are you using W1 or W2? Reconcile definition in 3.1 with Lemma 4 and the TV inequality and provide correct constants.
- Question: How sensitive is performance to initial noise variance and annealing rate, and can you provide guidance or convergence rates as variance→0?
- Question: Can you derive instability results for Theorem 2.6 under a more realistic model where D’s approximation error is bounded, input-correlated, and Lipschitz?
- Question: Can you show quantitative improvements (FID, precision/recall) and training stability curves on CIFAR-10/LSUN/ImageNet versus common baselines?
- Question: How does optimizing JSD on smoothed distributions compare theoretically and empirically to directly optimizing a Wasserstein surrogate (e.g., WGAN)?
- Question: Do you add noise at inference? If not, how do you ensure the generator trained with noisy backprop produces high-quality, noiseless samples at test time?
