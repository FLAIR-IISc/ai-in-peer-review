- Paper analyzes GAN instability using measure theory and differential geometry for distributions on low-dimensional manifolds.  
- Main contributions: perfect discrimination result showing optimal discriminator is locally constant with zero gradient on supports.  
- Characterizes −log D generator update as gradient of KL(Pg||Pr) − 2 JSD(Pg||Pr), explaining mode dropping and JSD sign.  
- Instability result: under white, independent discriminator approximation noise, −log D updates have Cauchy-distributed coordinates with infinite variance.  
- Proposed remedy: add continuous instance noise to make distributions absolutely continuous, preventing discriminator saturation and yielding attraction/repulsion generator gradients.  
- Links JSD(Pr*||Pg*) to Wasserstein distance and suggests annealing noise while minimizing JSD to bound W(Pr,Pg).  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Key reason 1: critical mathematical inconsistencies in the Wasserstein section undermine the central bound.  
- Key reason 2: instance noise remedy lacks convincing empirical validation and comparison to related work.  
- Key reason 3: instability theorem relies on unrealistic independence/whiteness assumptions and lacks stress-testing; referenced figures are missing.  
- Wasserstein inconsistency detail: paper mixes squared-cost W (W2^2) with W1 inequalities, producing dimensionally inconsistent bounds and incorrect constants.  
- Scope/realism concern: manifold/transversality results assume infinite-capacity/optimal discriminators and may not reflect finite neural networks.  
- Empirical support missing: no experiments, ablations, or metrics (FID, precision/recall) to validate stability, mode coverage, or noise schedules.  
- Prior work relation: instance noise has been previously used (e.g., Sønderby et al.); paper should cite and contrast with IPM-based approaches like WGAN/MMD-GAN and gradient-penalty methods.  
- Clarity/rigor issues: typos, notational inconsistencies, unspecified compactness/smoothness assumptions, and imprecise statements about manifolds with boundary.  
- Constructive fix 1: choose W1 or W2 consistently (prefer W1 via Kantorovich–Rubinstein) and correct Lemma 4/Theorem 3.3 with proper constants.  
- Constructive fix 2: add experiments on standard datasets with ablations (noise on D vs D+G, schedules, σ selection) and comparisons to stabilizing baselines.  
- Constructive fix 3: temper Theorem 2.6 by relaxing independence/whiteness assumptions or present it as an extreme-case heuristic and provide more realistic noise analyses.  
- Constructive fix 4: clarify backprop noise injection, reparameterization costs, and variance implications for stochastic gradients.  
- Constructive fix 5: tighten proofs, state assumptions explicitly, correct Lemma 1’s manifold discussion, and fix typos/notation.  
- Suggest positioning: discuss pros/cons of instance noise versus changing the divergence (IPMs) and whether they can be combined.  
- Questions for authors: which Wasserstein distance is intended, corrected bounds/constants, and empirical evidence for instance noise improving stability and mode coverage?  
- Additional questions: sensitivity to noise distribution/annealing, robustness of Theorem 2.6 under correlated/spatially structured errors, kernel interpretation of attraction/repulsion, and behavior under finite-capacity models.  
- Overall: paper gives valuable theoretical insights but has a key theoretical inconsistency and insufficient empirical validation for acceptance.
