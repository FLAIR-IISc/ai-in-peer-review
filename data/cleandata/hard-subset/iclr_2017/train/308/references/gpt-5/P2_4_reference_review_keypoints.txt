- Paper studies GAN instability as discriminator improves and contrasts generator losses (-log D vs log(1−D)) using a geometric manifold view.  
- Claims: data and model concentrate on low-dim manifolds with almost surely measure-zero intersections, yielding smooth perfect discriminators that are locally constant.  
- Derives vanishing gradients for the original generator loss as D→D*.  
- Shows -log D generator loss can produce heavy-tailed, infinite-variance updates under noisy discriminator approximations.  
- Proposes “instance noise” (adding continuous noise to real and fake samples) to make distributions absolutely continuous, stabilize discriminator gradients, and yield kernel-weighted attraction to data manifold with repulsion from model manifold.  
- Connects noisy JSD to noiseless Wasserstein distance via an upper bound depending on noise variance and JSD(Pr+||Pg+), recommending annealed-noise training to indirectly minimize W(Pr,Pg).  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main rejection reasons: serious technical issues/inconsistencies in the Wasserstein section and overly strong/unrealistic assumptions in proofs.  
- Wasserstein correctness issue: paper defines W using squared cost but applies inequalities valid for W1 (cost ||·||), breaking Lemma 4 and downstream results.  
- Theorem 3.3 omits constants (e.g., Pinsker) and mishandles inequalities turning KL sums into JSD, so constants are incorrect.  
- Theorem 2.6’s “white-noise” independence assumption across x and between D and ∇D is unrealistic, non-measurable, and makes the infinite-variance (Cauchy) conclusion not practically compelling.  
- Several results assume compact, boundary-free smooth manifolds and smoothness (e.g., Urysohn’s smooth lemma), which are too strong or unqualified for typical image/ReLU settings.  
- Novelty: geometric argument about manifold singularity and vanishing gradients is insightful and aligns with prior analyses, but not sufficiently positioned relative to prior work (instance noise, label smoothing, WGAN).  
- Empirics are insufficiently described: figures referenced lack experimental details, baselines, quantitative evaluations, and no clear demonstration that noise-annealing improves sample quality/stability.  
- Recommendations: fix optimal transport section by choosing W1 vs W2 consistently and carrying constants correctly; clarify manifold/domain assumptions and ReLU-specific issues; weaken or reframe Theorem 2.6 with realistic noise models; and provide systematic experiments on gradient behavior and instance-noise schedules.  
- Exposition fixes: define discriminator approximation norm precisely, state constants/inequalities clearly, correct typos, and reference standard lemmas properly.  
- Questions for authors: which Wasserstein metric is intended (W1 vs W2)? Can bounds be corrected with tight constants?  
- Questions: how sensitive are results to manifold and alignment assumptions, and do they extend to piecewise-linear manifolds with boundaries (ReLU)?  
- Questions: can Theorem 2.6 be replaced with a realistic noise model yielding finite-variance instability or variance bounds?  
- Questions: what concrete annealing schedule for instance noise is recommended, and how does performance vary with initial variance/decay and adding noise to real and fake?  
- Questions: can authors provide quantitative empirical validation of instance noise vs other stabilizers (feature matching, gradient penalties, WGAN) across datasets and architectures?
