- Paper studies why GAN optimization fails as discriminator improves and how to get stable generator signals.
- Argues GAN data/model live on low-dimensional manifolds in high-dimensional space, causing disjoint or nearly disjoint supports.
- Proves “perfect discrimination”: smooth discriminators can be constant with zero gradient on supports, explaining vanishing generator gradients.
- Shows original log(1−D) generator objective yields vanishing gradients as D→D*.
- Shows non-saturating −log D update locally optimizes KL(Pg||Pr) − 2·JSD(Pg||Pr), explaining mode dropping and instability.
- Demonstrates that noisy discriminator approximations can make −log D gradients heavy-tailed (Cauchy), causing unstable updates.
- Proposes instance noise (add continuous noise to real and generated samples) to make Pr and Pg absolutely continuous and provide informative gradients.
- Derives Gaussian-noise generator gradient as attraction to data manifold and repulsion from model manifold; backpropagating through noise aligns generator with ∇ JSD(Pr+ || Pg+).
- Connects instance noise to Wasserstein metric by bounding W(Pr,Pg) via noise variance and JSD between smoothed distributions, motivating annealed noise schedules.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reasons for accept: strong theoretical analysis explaining core GAN pathologies and a clear principled remedy (instance noise) tied to Wasserstein.
- Situates work among likelihood divergences, JSD, and prior heuristics; manifold support argument is plausible and standard.
- Theoretical results use standard math tools (Urysohn, Sard, transversality) and are presented with clear assumptions.
- Heavy-tail Cauchy result is idealized but captures practical instability; authors note measurability caveats.
- Smoothing/convolution results are clean and yield interpretable gradients.
- Wasserstein upper bound proof uses standard inequalities (TV, Pinsker); bounded-support assumption is explicit.
- Empirical support is referenced (figures on discriminator collapse, vanishing gradients, gradient variance) but details are sparse.
- Suggestion: add systematic empirical evaluation (datasets, schedules, baselines, fidelity/coverage metrics) and test annealing sensitivity.
- Suggestion: clarify assumptions and scope—generator architectures covered by Lemma 1, case of Pr with small off-manifold density, and extension to discrete data.
- Suggestion: weaken assumptions in Theorem 2.6 (white-noise and independence) or provide simulation showing heavy-tailed updates under more realistic errors.
- Suggestion: give derivation/intuition and a synthetic example for Theorem 2.5’s KL−2JSD decomposition.
- Suggestion: discuss tightness and bounded-support issues in Theorem 3.3 and possible extensions to sub-Gaussian tails.
- Suggestion: provide practical guidance/pseudocode for instance-noise training (where to add noise, schedule, batch-norm handling) and default recommendations.
- Questions for authors include: how to choose/anneal noise variance in practice; evidence that adding noise to both D and G avoids adversarial exploitation; robustness when Pr has small off-manifold density; effect of correlated/colored discriminator errors on Cauchy result; guidance on discriminator optimization under noise; and empirical quantification of mode coverage improvements.
