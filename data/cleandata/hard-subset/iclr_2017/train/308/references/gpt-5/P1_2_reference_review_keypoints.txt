- Core claim: GAN instability arises because Pr and Pg lie on low-dimensional manifolds, allowing a smooth perfect discriminator that yields vanishing generator gradients (Theorem 2.4).
- −log D update characterization: at θ0 it equals gradient of KL(Pg||Pr) − 2·JSD(Pg||Pr), encouraging sharp samples but promoting mode collapse (Theorem 2.5).
- Noise instability result: adding noise to discriminator outputs makes the generator update Cauchy-distributed with infinite variance, explaining practical instability (Theorem 2.6).
- Proposed fix: smooth Pr and Pg by adding noise to discriminator inputs and generator outputs, making distributions absolutely continuous and stabilizing gradients.
- Geometric effect: gradient of log(1−D*) becomes a kernel-weighted attraction to the data manifold and repulsion from the model manifold (Theorem 3.2).
- With generator noise, the update is asserted to be proportional to ∇θ JSD(Pr+ || Pg+) (Corollary 3.2).
- Wasserstein link: bounds relating JSD(Pr+ , Pg+) to W(Pr,Pg) suggest annealing noise while minimizing JSD can reduce Wasserstein distance (Theorem 3.3).
- Decision: Reject.
- Main reason 1: No empirical validation, algorithmic instantiation, experiments, or ablations to support theoretical proposals.
- Main reason 2: Several strong or under-specified theoretical assumptions and idealized conditions undermine practical relevance.
- Positive: Problem is important and the manifold-support perspective is well motivated and aligned with prior work.
- Rigor concern: Many results rely on compactness and ideal separations that do not address finite-capacity neural discriminators or SGD training.
- Theorem 2.4 issue: vanishing-gradient bound uses a strong norm closeness assumption (including gradients) unrealistic for finite-data networks.
- Theorem 2.6 issue: assumes independent white noise across x and numerator/denominator independence, which is unrealistic and yields qualitative conclusions.
- Corollary concern: proportionality to ∇θ JSD lacks full derivation and missing constants/conditions that affect optimization behavior.
- Wasserstein bound concern: constants (e.g., Pinsker factor) and bounded-diameter support assumptions are restrictive for common image domains.
- Contribution assessment: offers valuable conceptual direction (noise + annealing) but lacks concrete method and empirical evidence to be a complete contribution.
- Recommendation: provide a concrete algorithm specifying noise distributions, annealing schedule, how to update D and G, and defenses against adversarial effects.
- Experimental recommendation: run extensive experiments on CIFAR-10/CelebA/LSUN with FID/IS/precision-recall, qualitative samples, and comparisons to strong baselines and stabilization techniques.
- Ablation recommendation: compare no noise vs D-only vs D+G noise, different σ schedules, kernel shapes (Gaussian vs heavy-tailed), and sensitivity analyses.
- Theory improvements: relax white-noise independence, provide conditions (e.g., correlated Gaussian, Lipschitz) for instability, formalize proportionality constants, and address ReLU boundary measure-zero issues.
- Presentation fixes: include referenced figures, clarify norms and expectations, move key proofs into main text or add intuition for heavy tools, and discuss finite-sample/discriminator-capacity effects.
- Key questions for authors: specify the exact training procedure and noise schedule; report sensitivity to discriminator capacity; rigorously derive generator-noise → ∇θ JSD result with constants; show robustness of Theorem 2.6 under correlated noise; empirically validate the Wasserstein bound and guide σ(t); and quantify mode-dropping improvements.
