- Paper tackles why GAN training is unstable and why generator gradients degrade, using manifold geometry and divergence analysis.
- Claim 1: Disjoint or misaligned low-dimensional supports admit smooth perfect discriminators with zero gradients on supports, causing vanishing/uninformative generator gradients.
- Claim 2: For saturating loss gradients vanish as D→D*; for non-saturating loss generator updates correspond to ∇KL − 2∇JSD (promoting sharp samples/mode dropping) and, under a white-noise discriminator error model, updates can be Cauchy-distributed with infinite variance.
- Claim 3: Adding noise to inputs and generator outputs smooths Pr and Pg, restores meaningful gradients (attraction to data manifold, repulsion from model mass), makes generator gradient ∝ ∇θ JSD(Pr+||Pg+), and suggests annealing.
- Claim 4: Motivates Wasserstein distance and proves W(Pr,Pg) ≤ 2√V + 2C√JSD(Pr+||Pg+), relating true distance to noise variance and smoothed JSD.
- Decision: Reject.
- Main reason 1: Insufficient novelty—core insights and prescriptions overlap substantially with prior/parallel work (instance noise, WGAN, Huszár) and contributions not clearly delineated.
- Main reason 2: Practical stabilization claims (instance noise/annealing, usefulness of bounds) lack empirical support and referenced figures are missing.
- Main reason 3: Some theoretical assumptions are unrealistic and parts of the analysis are hand-wavy or incomplete, reducing scientific rigor.
- Supporting—novelty/placement: Manifold/JSD saturation and recommendations (Wasserstein, instance noise) have been previously articulated; formalization appears incremental.
- Supporting—correctness/rigor: Perfect-discriminator theorems are plausible but their practical relevance with finite-capacity discriminators and training dynamics is not resolved.
- Supporting—instability theorem critique: Relies on unrealistic independent white-noise Gaussian error model for D and acknowledges measurability issues, making conclusions illustrative rather than practical.
- Supporting—proof issues: Several proofs defer steps, rely on strong assumptions (compactness, smoothness, boundary conditions), and fail to quantify norms/constants (e.g., a(z), b(z)).
- Supporting—presentation issues: Referenced figures missing and some claims left to the reader, undermining evaluation of correctness.
- Empirical critique: No quantitative experiments or ablations showing that noise+annealing improves stability, mode coverage, or sample quality, nor comparisons to WGAN-GP, spectral normalization, or other stabilizers.
- Feedback 1: Clearly delineate novelty vs. instance noise, WGAN/Wasserstein bounds, f-GANs, and Huszár; highlight any genuinely new technical advances.
- Feedback 2: Replace white-noise discriminator error model with more realistic approximation models (e.g., Lipschitz-bounded errors or generalization bounds) and derive variance/conditioning results.
- Feedback 3: Provide a concrete algorithmic recipe for adding/annealing noise (type, covariance, where to add) and validate with experiments against strong baselines.
- Feedback 4: Strengthen the Wasserstein–JSD-with-noise connection (tighten bounds, make scale-aware) and provide empirical diagnostics on sensitivity to constants like C.
- Feedback 5: Improve clarity and rigor by including figures, fixing notation/typos, moving essential proofs to the main text, and defining assumptions upfront.
- Questions for authors (condensed): What is precise novelty vs prior work; can you provide empirical evidence for noise+annealing versus WGAN-GP/spectral norm; how sensitive are conclusions to the discriminator error model; how should practitioners choose noise/annealing and diagnostics; how do manifold/support assumptions interact with convnets/data augmentation; can constants (a(z), b(z), C) be estimated or bounded in practice?
