Summary and claimed contributions
- The paper analyzes instability in GAN training when Pr and Pg lie on low-dimensional manifolds. It proves that (i) with disjoint or non-transversally intersecting supports, there exist perfect discriminators that are locally constant on the supports, implying vanishing generator gradients under the original loss; (ii) using the alternative −log D generator update implicitly optimizes KL(Pg||Pr)−2 JSD and can be unstable. 
- To mitigate issues, the paper proposes adding continuous noise (instance noise) to both data and generated samples. It derives gradients under optimal discriminators for the smoothed distributions and argues this avoids vanishing/unstable gradients. 
- It connects the JSD between smoothed distributions to a bound on the Wasserstein distance between Pr and Pg, suggesting annealing noise while training yields convergence in a meaningful metric.

Decision: Reject
Key reasons: (1) Multiple substantial technical flaws/gaps in the mathematical arguments (incorrect assumptions, nonstandard/incorrect definitions, unjustified steps) that undermine the main claims; (2) Poor scholarly presentation (numerous typos, broken references, inconsistent notation, missing figures/results referenced in text), and insufficient empirical evidence to support the proposed remedy.

Supporting arguments
- Lemma 1 (image of Z under NN is contained in a countable union of manifolds of dim ≤ dim Z): The proof for smooth strictly increasing nonlinearities relies on “a consequence of the Morse Lemma” to claim coordinate projections have isolated critical points. This is not generally true without strong genericity/nondegeneracy assumptions (e.g., Morse functions on compact manifolds). Critical sets can be manifolds, not isolated, and the projection of a manifold need not be a manifold nor a countable union thereof under the given assumptions. The argument needs precise conditions (e.g., properness, generic perturbations, stratifications) or should cite results from geometric measure theory/Whitney stratification. As written, the lemma is not correct.
- Theorem 2.2: The constructed “optimal” D* is an indicator of disjoint open sets M̂, P̂. While it achieves zero loss, it is not smooth globally (contradicting the claim “D* is smooth in a neighbourhood of almost any x” follows, but “optimal discriminator” in the logistic loss sense is Pr/(Pr+Pg), not an indicator). The optimality claim requires care: any measurable D with values in [0,1] that equals 1 and 0 on the supports attains the maximum; however, invoking smooth optimality and local gradients requires regularization assumptions and approximation arguments, which are absent.
- Theorem 2.4 (vanishing gradients): Uses an ad hoc norm ‖D‖ = sup |D| + ‖∇x D‖2 and inequalities with missing constants/typos. The final bound is garbled (“< M −”), and the transition steps (Jensen, chain rule under expectation, bounds with D* and ε) are not rigorous as stated. This part needs a clean, fully specified inequality with all constants and assumptions (bounded Jacobian w.r.t. z, measurability, dominated convergence).
- Theorem 2.5: The identity relating −∇θ log D*(gθ) at θ0 to ∇θ[KL(Pg||Pr) − 2 JSD] relies on interchanging gradients and expectations and treating D* fixed at θ0. The algebra contains sign/notation errors (e.g., “− log D*/−D*”) and should be carefully rederived. A precise statement with conditions (differentiability of densities, dominated convergence, support conditions) is necessary.
- Theorem 2.6 (Cauchy-distributed updates): Assumes D*−D is white noise, independent across x, and independent from ∇x errors; notes non-measurability; then concludes each coordinate of the update is Cauchy with infinite variance. This model is unrealistic for neural networks (errors are highly correlated in x), and the non-measurability caveat makes the derivation formally invalid. As a result, the theorem does not support the claimed practical instability.
- Smoothing section: Theorem 3.1 (convolution on manifolds) is fine conceptually, but needs careful measure-theoretic setup (densities w.r.t. Hausdorff measure on manifolds). Corollary 3.1 includes a heavy-tailed kernel “P(x) ∝ 1/‖x‖^{d+1}” without conditions for normalization/support.
- Theorem 3.2/Corollary 3.2: The gradient formulas under optimal D* are plausible, but the equality “= 2 ∇θ JSD(Pr+‖Pg+)” requires the optimal discriminator for the smoothed distributions and justified interchange of gradient and integral. This should be shown explicitly with assumptions; currently, it is asserted without proof.
- Wasserstein distance: The definition of W(P,Q) uses ∫‖x−y‖^2, but subsequent inequalities (Kantorovich–Rubinstein duality, W ≤ C δ(P,Q)) and bounds to √V clearly refer to the 1-Wasserstein distance (with ‖x−y‖, not squared). Lemma 4 and Theorem 3.3 mix squared/unsquared costs and misapply Jensen’s inequality to produce √V. Constants in Pinsker’s inequality and the relation to JSD are also off. This section needs a consistent choice of W1 or W2 and corrected constants.
- Empirical support: The paper references Figures 1–3 and DCGAN experiments, but no figures or quantitative results are provided here. Claims about gradient norms increasing, stability improvements with noise, and sample quality are not substantiated within the submission.

Additional feedback (to help improve the paper)
- Fix all notation and typographical errors (numerous misspellings: “adresssing,” “symetrical,” “aboslutely,” “transversallity,” “arize,” “Corolary,” etc.), equation numbering, and missing terms in inequalities. Ensure consistent use of symbols (‖·‖, gradients, Jacobians).
- Repair the bibliography (broken entries like “?, \Q2016\E”), add missing citations (e.g., Huszár blog post, prior “instance noise”/input noise work, f-GAN, WGAN/WGAN-GP, gradient penalty/spectral normalization).
- Choose and clearly define the Wasserstein metric (W1 or W2), then revise Lemma 4 and Theorem 3.3 with correct constants and assumptions (compact support, bounded diameter, Pinsker constants, JSD vs TV).
- Rework Lemma 1 rigorously or replace with a weaker, correct statement (e.g., Pg is singular w.r.t. Lebesgue in X when dim Z < dim X for standard NN classes; cite e.g., results on piecewise-linear nets and rectifiability).
- Remove Theorem 2.6 or replace with a realistic stochastic approximation analysis (e.g., biased/noisy gradients with bounded variance, correlation structure), or provide empirical evidence matching theoretical predictions.
- Provide a proper experimental section: controlled ablations comparing baseline GAN vs. instance-noise on standard datasets; noise schedules; effects on mode dropping; stability metrics; visuals and FID/KID/precision–recall; sensitivity to σ; comparisons to WGAN-GP/spectral norm/consistency regularization.
- Clarify when gradients equal JSD gradients under optimal D* for smoothed distributions, with precise assumptions.

Questions for the authors
1. Lemma 1: Under what precise conditions (compactness, genericity) do your projection arguments hold? Can you provide a correct, fully rigorous proof or adjust the statement?
2. Theorem 2.6: Why is the white-noise model appropriate for discriminator approximation errors? Can you propose a measurable, correlated Gaussian process model and re-derive a meaningful instability result?
3. Wasserstein: Are you using W1 or W2? Please correct the definition and all subsequent bounds, and provide the correct constants linking TV, KL, and JSD.
4. Corollary 3.2: Can you provide a formal proof that the gradient equals 2∇θ JSD(Pr+‖Pg+), including conditions for interchanging gradients and integrals?
5. Experiments: Please include the referenced figures and quantitative results. How do your instance-noise results compare to WGAN-GP and spectral normalization across datasets? How is the noise annealing schedule chosen, and how sensitive are results?
6. Optimal discriminator: In Theorem 2.2 the constructed D* is an indicator. How do you reconcile this with the form of the Bayes-optimal logistic discriminator Pr/(Pr+Pg)? Under what regularization is your D* optimal?
7. Pinsker/JSD constants: Please fix constants and clarify whether you use natural logs or base-2 logs throughout, as this affects the JSD bound.
