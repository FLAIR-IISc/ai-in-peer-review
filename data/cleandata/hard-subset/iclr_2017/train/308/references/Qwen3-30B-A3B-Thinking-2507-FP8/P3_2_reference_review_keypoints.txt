- Core claim: GAN instability arises because Pr and Pg lie on low-dimensional manifolds, yielding perfect discriminators with zero gradients (vanishing) or infinite-variance updates for -log D.  
- Main theoretical contributions: proof generators produce low-dim support (Lemma 1); existence of perfect discriminators for non-transversal manifold intersections (Thms 2.1, 2.2); vanishing gradients (Thm 2.4) and unstable updates (Thm 2.6); noise-adding solution; link between Wasserstein and JS of noisy distributions (Thm 3.3).  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Primary rejection reason 1: Lack of novelty—manifold-based explanation and Wasserstein perspective echo prior work (e.g., Theis et al., Arjovsky/WGAN).  
- Primary rejection reason 2: No empirical validation or comparisons to state-of-the-art stabilization methods.  
- Novelty concern detail: Core insight already discussed in literature; formalization does not add substantial new theoretical insight beyond WGAN-related work.  
- Empirical validation concern: Proposed noise-based fix is purely theoretical; no experiments showing improved stability or sample quality versus WGAN, WGAN-GP, spectral norm, gradient penalties, etc.  
- Practical impact concern: Claims about annealing noise lack guidance on schedules, practical implementation, or how to estimate/minimize Wasserstein in practice.  
- Literature/context omission: Fails to adequately relate to WGAN literature and prior geometric analyses of data manifolds (e.g., Narayanan & Mitter).  
- Theoretical feedback: Need more precise mathematical statement and empirical support for the low-dimensional manifold assumption and discussion of non-optimal discriminators in practice.  
- Empirical feedback: Demonstrate empirically that generator gradients vanish and -log D yields unstable updates; compare noise approach against SOTA on standard benchmarks (CIFAR-10, LSUN, etc.).  
- Relation to existing work: Clarify differences/improvements over WGAN and connect analysis to mode collapse/mode dropping.  
- Question 1: How does the analysis handle overlapping supports when Pr and Pg lie on the same low-dimensional manifold?  
- Question 2: What is the optimal noise magnitude/distribution to add, and how does it depend on data and architecture?  
- Question 3: How does this noise-based approach differ from or improve upon WGAN?  
- Question 4: How does the gradient in Theorem 3.2 compare to WGAN-GP gradients used in practice?  
- Question 5: What is the theoretical basis and practical schedule for annealing the noise, and how does it compare to typical learning-rate schedules?
