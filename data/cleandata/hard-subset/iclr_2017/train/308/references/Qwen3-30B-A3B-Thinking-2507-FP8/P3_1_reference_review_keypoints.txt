- The paper proves data and generator distributions typically lie on low-dimensional manifolds, producing disjoint supports.  
- It presents "perfect discrimination" theorems showing optimal discriminators become perfect and constant on these manifolds, causing generator gradient issues.  
- It analyzes both the original GAN loss (vanishing gradients) and the -logD alternative (unstable gradients with infinite variance/Cauchy behavior).  
- It proposes adding continuous noise to inputs to smooth distributions, yielding continuous Pr+ and Pg+ and enabling stable gradients.  
- It connects the noisy-GAN approach to the Wasserstein distance, bounding the original distance by the noisy distance plus a noise-variance term.  
- The paper is well-written with clear mathematical statements, rigorous proofs, and insightful connections to established concepts.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Main acceptance reasons: first rigorous geometric explanation for GAN instability and thorough analysis of gradient behaviors.  
- Minor concern: clearer linkage between theoretical findings and existing empirical observations is needed.  
- Minor concern: practical implementation details for the noisy GAN (e.g., choosing noise level) are underdeveloped.  
- Revise to explicitly relate theory to the heuristic of training the discriminator more often to avoid vanishing gradients.  
- Suggest adding a simple empirical validation (e.g., MNIST ablation) showing noise improves training stability.  
- Recommend comparing the noisy-GAN approach to recent stabilization techniques (spectral norm, gradient penalty, Salimans et al.).  
- Request a practical discussion of noise-annealing schedules and how to choose σ in practice.  
- Clarify "manifold" terminology to mean low-dimensional submanifolds of the ambient space, not necessarily smooth manifolds.  
- Question: provide a concrete example where dim(Z) < dim(X) does not yield a low-dimensional support for Pg.  
- Question: how does finite numerical precision affect the practical Cauchy/infinite-variance gradient behavior?  
- Question: what is the specific benefit of adding noise to generator outputs in addition to discriminator inputs?  
- Question: how tight/practical is the bound between Wasserstein(Pr,Pg) and JSD(Pr+,Pg+); how much noise is needed to achieve a target Wasserstein distance?  
- Question: when noise is annealed out, will final generated samples still lie on the data manifold or be adversely affected?
