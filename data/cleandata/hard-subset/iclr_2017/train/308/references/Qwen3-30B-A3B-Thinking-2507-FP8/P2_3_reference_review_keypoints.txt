- Paper identifies GAN instability as arising from real and generator distributions lying on low-dimensional, disjoint manifolds.  
- Optimal discriminators become perfect with zero gradients on these manifolds, causing vanishing generator gradients and instability.  
- Lemma 1: generator outputs (neural nets) lie on low-dimensional manifolds when latent dim < data dim.  
- Theorems 2.1–2.3: rigorous proof that disjoint low-dim supports yield perfect discriminators with zero gradients almost everywhere.  
- Theorem 2.4 & Corollary 2.1: original GAN cost leads to vanishing gradients for the generator.  
- Theorem 2.6: alternative cost (−log D) produces gradient updates with infinite variance, explaining instability.  
- Empirical results (Figures 2–3) support the theoretical claims.  
- Proposed solution: add noise to discriminator/generator inputs to create continuous distributions and meaningful gradients.  
- Theorem 3.2 & Corollary 3.2: noise makes distributions continuous and enables stable gradient updates.  
- Theorem 3.3: connects the noise-based approach to the Wasserstein distance as a suitable metric for manifold-supported distributions.  
- Paper is rigorous, well-motivated, fills a significant theoretical gap, and is recommended for acceptance.  
- Requested improvements: provide practical guidance on noise magnitude, effects on sample quality, and computational costs.  
- Requested experiments: empirical comparisons of the noise-based method on benchmarks (e.g., CIFAR-10, ImageNet).  
- Suggest discussing relations and potential combinations with other stabilization techniques (spectral normalization, gradient penalties).  
- Questions for authors: details on noise annealing schedules and how noise magnitude affects sample quality.  
- Questions for authors: theoretical analysis/conditions for noisy distributions converging to original distributions under annealing.  
- Questions for authors: empirical evidence (e.g., gradient distribution plots) demonstrating infinite variance of alternative-cost gradients.  
- Request a comparison of the noise-based approach versus WGAN-GP and Spectral Normalization in sample quality, stability, and efficiency.
