- Paper identifies a fundamental cause of GAN instability: generator and data supports lie on low-dimensional manifolds, causing the optimal discriminator to be perfect and its gradient to vanish on the generator support.

- Lemma 1: neural-network generators with latent dim < data dim inevitably produce distributions supported on low-dimensional manifolds.

- Theorems 2.1–2.2: under natural conditions the optimal discriminator attains accuracy 1 and has zero gradient on the generator support.

- Theorems 2.4–2.6: the vanishing discriminator gradient implies original GAN cost yields vanishing gradients for the generator, while common alternative costs produce unstable updates.

- Proposed solution: add continuous noise to inputs to smooth distributions, inducing a Wasserstein-like metric that enables stable training without delicate update balancing.

- Theorem 3.3: provides a bound showing the Wasserstein distance between true distributions can be controlled by noise level and the JSD of the noisy distributions, giving theoretical justification for the noise approach.

- Paper is well placed in literature, clarifying and extending prior work (Goodfellow et al., Theis et al.) and relating to VAEs and likelihood methods.

- Decision: Accept — strong theoretical contribution with a principled and promising solution.

- Major weakness: lacks concrete empirical validation of the noise-based solution; referenced figures are not discussed in-text.

- Practical implementation details missing: how to choose and anneal the noise level in practice.

- Computational cost unclear: need explicit statement whether noise addition imposes significant overhead during training.

- Key question 1: please provide an empirical comparison of the noise-based approach vs standard GAN training on simple datasets (e.g., MNIST/CIFAR-10).

- Key question 2: what annealing schedule for noise do you recommend and how does it compare to typical GAN training schedules?

- Key question 3: how does backpropagating through noisy samples affect the quality of generated samples relative to standard GAN training?
