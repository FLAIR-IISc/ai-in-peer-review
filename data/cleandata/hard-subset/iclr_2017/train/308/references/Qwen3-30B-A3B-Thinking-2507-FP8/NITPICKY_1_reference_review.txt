

# Review of "Understanding GAN Training Instability"

## 1. Summary of Contributions
The paper presents a comprehensive theoretical analysis of GAN training instability, identifying that the core issue stems from the fact that data and generated distributions typically lie on low-dimensional manifolds with disjoint supports. The authors prove that this leads to perfect discriminators with vanishing gradients for the generator when using the original cost function, and unstable updates with the alternative cost function. They propose adding noise to the inputs to create "softer" distributions that can be meaningfully compared using standard divergences, and establish a connection between the Wasserstein distance of the original distributions and the JSD of the noisy distributions. The paper makes a strong theoretical contribution that could guide future GAN research.

## 2. Decision
Reject. The paper contains numerous minor but pervasive technical and formatting inconsistencies that undermine its credibility as a rigorous theoretical contribution.

## 3. Supporting Arguments

The paper's theoretical contributions are significant, but the following minor issues collectively prevent me from recommending acceptance:

**Consistent Notation Errors:**
- Inconsistent use of "Jensen-shannon" vs. "Jensen-Shannon" throughout the text and equations.
- Inconsistent spacing in mathematical expressions (e.g., "log Pr(x) Pg(x)" instead of "log Pr(x)/Pg(x)").
- Theorem 2.4 contains a typo: "‖∇θEz∼p(z)[log(1−D(gθ(z)))]‖2 < M" should have "M" instead of "M" (missing the subscript 2).
- Theorem 2.6 has a typo: "Ez∼p(z) [−∇θ logD(gθ(z))]" should be "Ez∼p(z)[−∇θ logD(gθ(z))]".

**Technical Inaccuracies:**
- The paper states "the distributions are not continuous" (Section 2.1), but should say "not absolutely continuous" (as clarified in Appendix B).
- Theorem 2.3 claims "JSD(Pr‖Pg) = log 2" without qualification, but the maximum JSD is log 2, not that it's always log 2.
- The paper states "the architecture of the generator used by GANs doesn't differ significantly from other approaches like variational autoencoders" (Introduction), which is misleading as the training objectives differ fundamentally.

**Formatting and Consistency Issues:**
- Inconsistent use of "manifolds" vs. "manifold" (e.g., "manifolds" used when referring to a single manifold).
- Inconsistent citation format in references (e.g., "Goodfellow et al.,? \Q2014\E" vs. proper citation style).
- Inconsistent equation numbering and formatting.
- The paper states "Proof. See Appendix A" for Lemma 1, but the proof is actually in Appendix A (the text is correct, but the reference is confusingly phrased).

**Minor Technical Errors in Proofs:**
- Theorem 2.5 uses inconsistent notation: "Ez∼p(z) [−∇θ logD∗(gθ(z))|θ=θ0 ]" should be "Ez∼p(z)[−∇θ logD∗(gθ(z))]|θ=θ0".
- In Theorem 3.2, the statement "b > a if and only if Pr+ > Pg+ " is not precisely defined (it should be pointwise at gθ(z)).

These issues are relatively minor individually, but collectively they create a perception of carelessness that undermines the paper's theoretical rigor. As a perfectionist reviewer, I believe these inconsistencies are significant enough to recommend rejection, especially given the paper's strong theoretical claims that would benefit from precise presentation.

## 4. Additional Feedback

To improve the paper, I recommend:

1. Carefully proofreading the entire manuscript for consistent notation, especially regarding JSD, manifolds, and probability distributions.

2. Clarify the distinction between "continuous" and "absolutely continuous" random variables throughout, as this is critical to the paper's arguments (this is partially addressed in Appendix B, but should be consistent in the main text).

3. Fix all typographical errors in equations and text (e.g., "imposible" → "impossible," inconsistent spacing in mathematical expressions).

4. Ensure consistent citation format throughout (e.g., remove question marks and backslashes from references).

5. Clarify the statement about generator architecture in the introduction to avoid misleading readers about the fundamental differences between GANs and VAEs.

6. Provide a more precise statement about the relationship between Pr+ and Pg+ in Theorem 3.2 (the pointwise nature of the inequality).

I believe addressing these issues would strengthen the paper significantly, and I would be happy to reconsider if these errors were corrected.
