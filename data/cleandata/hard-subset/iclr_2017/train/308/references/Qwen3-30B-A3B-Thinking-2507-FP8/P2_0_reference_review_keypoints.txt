- The paper proves GAN generators' supports lie in a countable union of low-dimensional manifolds.  
- It shows typical mismatch of data and generator manifolds yields a perfect discriminator with zero gradient almost everywhere.  
- The original GAN objective suffers vanishing gradients as the discriminator improves.  
- The alternative objective (-log D) produces unstable gradients with infinite variance.  
- Adding noise to discriminator and generator inputs creates continuous distributions and meaningful gradients.  
- The authors derive a theoretical link between Wasserstein distance and the Jensen–Shannon divergence of noisy distributions.  
- They propose a practical training procedure that injects noise and anneals it to stabilize GAN training without heavy hyperparameter tuning.  
- Recommendation: Accept — the paper makes a significant, rigorous theoretical contribution to GAN stability.  
- Strength: provides a rigorous theoretical foundation explaining long-observed GAN training failures.  
- Strength: strong mathematical framework using manifold, measure, and probability theory with clear proofs.  
- Strength: theory leads to a simple, principled, and implementable noise-based solution.  
- Strength: situates and contrasts the work well within existing GAN literature.  
- Suggestion: include empirical validation figures and details to support theoretical claims.  
- Suggestion: add a brief empirical comparison with methods like WGAN, LSGAN, and spectral normalization.  
- Suggestion: discuss computational overhead introduced by noise injection.  
- Suggestion: provide clearer guidance and sensitivity analysis for the noise annealing schedule.  
- Question: provide details on empirical validation (datasets, architectures, and noise effects).  
- Question: how does the proposed method compare to WGAN in sample quality and stability?  
- Question: how does the Wasserstein–JSD bound behave as noise variance decreases; does it become tight as noise→0?  
- Question: what noise schedules are recommended and how sensitive is final sample quality to schedule choice?  
- Question: provide intuition or examples showing why backprop through noisy samples prevents adversarial examples.
