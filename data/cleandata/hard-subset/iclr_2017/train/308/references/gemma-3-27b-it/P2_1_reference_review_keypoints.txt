- Paper investigates GAN training instability, attributing it to cost functions and low-dimensional generator/discriminator manifolds, and proposes adding noise plus using Wasserstein distance to smooth optimization.  
- Recommendation: Reject due to incomplete work and insufficient empirical support for the proposed solution.  
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical analysis (Theorems 2.1, 2.2, 2.5, 2.6) that sheds light on perfect discrimination and gradient issues.  
- Weak empirical link: Theoretical results are not convincingly translated into practical GAN improvements or strong experimental gains.  
- Limited experiments: Figures are sparse and do not demonstrate clear advantages over established methods.  
- Redundancy: Paper contains excessive background material and verbose derivations that reduce focus.  
- Questionable assumptions: Analysis depends on unrealistic/simplifying assumptions (near-optimal discriminator, Gaussian noise) that may not hold in practice.  
- Abstract noise model: The Gaussian process noise argument (Theorem 2.6) feels theoretical and detached from neural-network discriminators.  
- Missing comparisons: No empirical or theoretical comparison to stabilization methods like WGAN-GP or spectral normalization.  
- Main fix suggested: Perform robust empirical validation on standard benchmarks with FID/IS and ablations on noise levels and Wasserstein weighting.  
- Practicality improvements: Provide concrete implementation guidance (noise magnitude, how to integrate Wasserstein in training) and report computational overhead.  
- Presentation fix: Streamline background and focus on core contributions to improve clarity.  
- Limitations: Authors should explicitly acknowledge and discuss the limits of their assumptions and analysis.  
- Questions for authors: Clarify implementation details of noise and Wasserstein weighting, computational cost, sensitivity to noise choice, applicability to other generative models, and potential downsides of added noise.  
- Conclusion: Valuable theoretical insights but currently lacking empirical validation and practical guidance, so reject with encouragement to strengthen experiments and applicability.
