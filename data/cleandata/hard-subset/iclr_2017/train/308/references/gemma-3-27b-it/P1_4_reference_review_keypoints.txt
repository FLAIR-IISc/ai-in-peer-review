- Paper analyzes GAN instability, blaming standard divergences (KL, JSD) when generator outputs lie on low-dimensional/disjoint supports, causing perfect discriminators and problematic gradients.  
- Proposes smoothing distributions via added noise and advocates using the Wasserstein metric to stabilize GAN training.  
- Recommendation: Reject, with encouragement to revise and resubmit due to incomplete theory and weak empirical support.  
- Theoretical contributions are strong and mathematically sound but rely on heavy assumptions about low-dimensional manifolds and (nearly) disjoint support that need stronger justification.  
- Major weakness: insufficient empirical validation and lack of compelling experimental results demonstrating practical benefits on standard benchmarks (e.g., CIFAR-10, CelebA).  
- Presentation is dense and notation-heavy; the paper needs clearer intuition, simpler explanations, and better visual aids to improve accessibility.  
- Proposed smoothing technique is under-specified: implementation details, noise tuning, and the relationship between noise level, Wasserstein distance, and sample quality are unclear.  
- Authors should explicitly discuss limitations of their assumptions and how the method performs when assumptions fail.  
- Need deeper analysis of noise robustness: effects of noise type (Gaussian, uniform) and selection of optimal noise parameters.  
- Compare and position the approach against existing GAN stabilization techniques (spectral normalization, gradient penalty) in terms of performance and compute cost.  
- Provide a detailed experimental setup: architectures, hyperparameters, training procedures, and thorough discussion of Figures 1–3.  
- Consider adding visualizations illustrating low-dimensional generator manifolds and how smoothing impacts them.  
- Explore how the framework addresses mode collapse and whether smoothing/Wasserstein mitigates it.  
- Questions for authors: give an intuitive explanation of why noise alleviates perfect discrimination.  
- Questions for authors: present detailed experiments and quantitative results validating the smoothing technique.  
- Questions for authors: report sensitivity of performance to noise distribution and parameters.  
- Questions for authors: compare computational cost of Wasserstein-based training with other GAN methods.  
- Questions for authors: discuss extending the framework to higher-dimensional generator outputs and practical challenges in estimating Wasserstein distance.
