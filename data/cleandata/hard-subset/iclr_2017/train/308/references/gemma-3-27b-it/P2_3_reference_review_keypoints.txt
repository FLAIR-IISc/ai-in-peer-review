- Decision: Reject, with encouragement to revise and resubmit.

- Key reason 2: Paper is overly dense and mathematically difficult to follow, needing clearer exposition.
- Supporting: Identifies and analyzes the "perfect discriminator" problem and divergence-related gradient issues.
- Supporting: Limited empirical validation — figures feel preliminary and there’s no comparison to standard stabilization methods (WGAN, Spectral Norm).
- Supporting: Claims about gradient norm growth lack quantitative analysis and clear linkage to the proposed fix.
- Supporting: Complexity and accessibility issues — heavy notation, scarce intuitive explanations or visual aids.
- Supporting: Novelty concerns — noise injection is not new, and the practical novelty/impact of the theoretical metric is unclear.
- Supporting: Unclear how the proposed noise + Wasserstein approach translates into a concrete training algorithm or noise choices.
- Requested revision: Provide comprehensive empirical comparisons on standard datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, CelebA) versus state-of-the-art methods.
- Requested revision: Simplify presentation, reduce dense derivations, add intuitive explanations and visuals.
- Requested revision: Expand implementation details — specify noise distributions, parameter choices, and operational use of the Wasserstein metric.
- Requested revision: Better situate work in related literature on GAN stabilization and clarify differences/benefits.
- Requested analysis: Explain how noise magnitude affects learning dynamics and whether principled noise-variance choices or bounds exist.
- Requested analysis: Study whether performance depends on the intrinsic dimensionality of the data manifold.
- Questions for authors: Provide concrete noise-injection implementation and parameter choices.
- Questions for authors: Quantitatively compare to existing stabilization methods on standard benchmarks.
- Questions for authors: Report computational cost, especially for Wasserstein calculations.
- Questions for authors: Theorize or bound how noise interacts with generator/discriminator learning dynamics.
- Questions for authors: Explain practical estimation of the Wasserstein distance for GAN evaluation.
