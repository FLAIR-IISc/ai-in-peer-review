- Paper analyzes GAN instability arising from generator and data lying on low-dimensional manifolds, causing perfect discrimination and vanishing/unstable gradients.  
- Authors propose smoothing distributions with added noise and using the Wasserstein metric to stabilize GAN training.  
- Decision: Reject (encouraged to revise and resubmit).  
- Strength: Strong, rigorous theoretical foundation linking manifold geometry to GAN training failure.  
- Strength: Novel approach of noise injection plus Wasserstein guidance with thoughtful gradient analysis.  
- Strength: Comprehensive analysis of original GAN and -logD cost functions highlighting practical pitfalls.  
- Weakness: Insufficient empirical validation; figures are limited and no quantitative comparisons on standard datasets.  
- Weakness: Dense, mathematically challenging presentation that hampers accessibility and practical uptake.  
- Weakness: Missing concrete implementation details (how to add noise, compute Wasserstein efficiently, hyperparameters).  
- Weakness: Some claims are overly assertive without adequate empirical or theoretical justification (e.g., adversarial example claims).  
- Requested: Provide comprehensive empirical comparisons to methods like WGAN-GP and spectral normalization.  
- Requested: Supply implementation guidelines: noise type/variance, scheduling, and efficient Wasserstein estimation.  
- Requested: Clarify relation to recent GAN-stability methods and architectural regularizations.  
- Requested: Empirically test adversarial robustness of the proposed approach.  
- Requested: Offer a clearer, more intuitive explanation of Theorem 3.3 connecting JSD closeness to small Wasserstein distance.
