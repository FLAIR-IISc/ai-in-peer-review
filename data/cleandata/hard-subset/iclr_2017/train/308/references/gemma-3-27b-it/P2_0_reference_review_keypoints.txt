- Paper summary: analyzes GAN instability theoretically, links failures to disjoint/low-dimensional supports, and proposes noise injection + Wasserstein metric to stabilize training.
- Decision: Reject (encouraged to revise and resubmit).
- Main critique 1: Theoretical rigor and novelty are limited; results restate known properties of distributions/divergences rather than provide novel GAN-specific insight.
- Main critique 2: Presentation is overly complex and dense, with heavy notation and insufficient intuition for broad audiences.
- Limited empirical validation: Experiments are sparse, lack baselines, and do not convincingly support the proposed method.
- Redundancy: The paper repeats arguments and theorems (e.g., KL symmetry) unnecessarily, inflating length without new content.
- Missing implementation details: Noise-injection approach is described conceptually but lacks specifics (noise type, scale, integration into training) for reproducibility.
- Questionable assumptions: Claim that discriminator error from noise is a centered Cauchy distribution (Theorem 2.6) is strong and needs further justification.
- Recommendation: Simplify exposition, add intuition, visual aids, and concrete examples to make core ideas clearer.
- Recommendation: Expand empirical evaluation with comparisons to state-of-the-art stabilizers (WGAN-GP, spectral normalization) on standard datasets and metrics (FID, IS).
- Recommendation: Focus the paper on its single most novel contribution (theory vs. noise technique vs. Wasserstein connection) and streamline accordingly.
- Recommendation: Explicitly situate the work relative to recent GAN advances (spectral norm, gradient penalties, consistency regularization).
- Question for authors: Provide full implementation details for noise injection (distribution, variance, training integration).
- Question for authors: How does the proposed approach compare empirically to WGAN-GP and spectral normalization in quality and stability?
- Question for authors: Justify the Cauchy-distribution claim and its underlying assumptions.
- Question for authors: What practical challenges exist when applying this theoretical framework to real-world GAN training?
