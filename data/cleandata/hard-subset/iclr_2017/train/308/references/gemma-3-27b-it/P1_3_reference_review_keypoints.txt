- Tackles GAN instability with a theoretical analysis identifying perfect discrimination on low-dimensional manifolds and problematic properties of common losses, proposing noise injection within a Wasserstein framework for more robust gradients.

- Decision: Reject — strong theory but incomplete contributions and weak empirical validation.

- Strong theoretical foundation but lacks comprehensive experiments demonstrating effectiveness against standard GAN stabilization methods.

- Figures are illustrative but do not provide rigorous quantitative comparisons on benchmarks.

- Mathematical notation and derivations are dense and hard to follow for non-experts.

- Connection and novelty relative to WGAN and related work are not clearly delineated.

- Paper gives no concrete guidance on how much noise to add, which noise distribution to use, or how to schedule noise during training.

- Authors must include rigorous empirical evaluation against strong baselines (e.g., SN-GAN, WGAN-GP) on standard datasets with metrics like FID and Inception Score.

- Clearly articulate specific advantages of the proposed approach over existing WGAN and stabilization techniques.

- Improve accessibility with intuitive explanations, geometric interpretations, and a plain-language summary of core takeaways.

- Provide practical implementation recommendations (noise type, level, scheduling) for practitioners.

- Discuss potential limitations and computational costs associated with the noise-based approach.

- Question: Provide experimental comparison vs SN-GAN and WGAN-GP on benchmark datasets.

- Question: Why choose Gaussian noise theoretically, and have other distributions (uniform, Laplacian) been tested?

- Question: How sensitive is performance to noise magnitude and is there an optimal noise level or tuning strategy?

- Question: Precisely how does this approach differ from original WGAN and which problems does WGAN not address?

- Question: What is the computational overhead of adding noise and computing the Wasserstein distance compared to standard GAN training?
