- Paper addresses GAN training instability, attributing it to JSD properties and low-dimensional support of data/generator distributions.
- Authors theoretically show perfect discrimination and resulting vanishing gradients/instability when supports lie on manifolds.
- Proposed solution: inject noise into distributions to smooth divergences and stabilize training, with theoretical justification.
- Decision: Reject.
- Main reason: presentation is overly dense and hard to follow due to heavy theorem/proof focus.
- Main reason: insufficient empirical validation—only a single figure; no demonstration of improved image quality, stability, or mode coverage.
- Main reason: noise injection is incremental and resembles existing techniques (label smoothing, alternative losses) without clear novelty or superiority.
- Main reason: unclear connection between theoretical conditions and real-world architectures/training dynamics.
- Revision suggestion: make the theory more accessible with intuitive explanations and visual aids.
- Revision suggestion: run thorough experiments comparing noise injection to state-of-the-art GAN methods, reporting FID/IS and sample images.
- Revision suggestion: clarify novelty vs. existing stabilization methods and provide practical hyperparameter guidance (e.g., noise variance).
- Revision suggestion: expand related-work discussion, especially links to non-convex optimization and alternative GAN objectives.
- Questions for authors: which architectures were considered and do they meet the low-dimensional support assumptions?
- Questions for authors: how does noise injection concretely differ from label smoothing or Wasserstein approaches?
- Questions for authors: what is the computational cost and sensitivity to noise distribution/type across datasets?
