- Paper analyzes GAN training instability arising from cost functions (KL, JSD, adversarial) on low-dimensional manifold-supported distributions, showing perfect discriminators cause vanishing gradients or unstable updates.  
- Proposes a smoothed discriminator with noise injection, provides theoretical justification, and links the approach to the Wasserstein distance for analyzing/optimizing GAN training.  
- Decision: Reject — strong theory but insufficient empirical validation to support practical claims.  
- Strength: Rigorous mathematical analysis illuminating geometric causes of GAN instability.  
- Weakness: No experimental results to substantiate the proposed stabilization method or claimed improvements.  
- Weakness: Mathematically dense and hard to follow for readers without deep background in information theory, differential geometry, and optimization.  
- Weakness: Contribution feels incremental relative to existing GAN theory and stabilization methods without compelling empirical gains.  
- Suggestion: Include empirical validation (even small-scale benchmarks like CIFAR-10 or CelebA) to demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Suggestion: Clarify relation to existing methods (e.g., WGAN) and explicitly state differences/improvements.  
- Suggestion: Add intuitive explanations, visuals, or analogies to improve accessibility.  
- Suggestion: Discuss computational cost of the noise injection technique and possible mitigation strategies.  
- Suggestion: Refine and make notation consistent (e.g., clear symbols for distributions vs. densities).  
- Question: What is the anticipated computational cost versus standard GAN training?  
- Question: Have different noise distributions/variances or noise types been explored and how do they affect performance?  
- Question: What are the practical training impacts of the identified instabilities (mode collapse, slow convergence, etc.)?  
- Question: How does minimizing distance between noisy distributions intuitively translate to improvements in the original noiseless distributions?  
- Question: Are there challenges applying this approach to more complex data or GAN architectures (conditional GANs, StyleGAN)?
