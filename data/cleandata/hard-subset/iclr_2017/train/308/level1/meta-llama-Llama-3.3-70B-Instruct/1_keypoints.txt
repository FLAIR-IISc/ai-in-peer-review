- The paper provides a theoretical analysis of GAN training dynamics to explain instability and mode dropping.
- Authors introduce a new analytical direction and tools for studying GAN instability.
- Paper is organized into three parts: introduction, sources of instability, and softer metrics/distributions.
- Theoretical results include theorems showing existence of perfect discriminators and consequences of different cost functions.
- The authors propose using the Wasserstein distance as a metric to compare generator and data distributions.
- Decision: Accept — the paper is thorough, well-motivated, and offers new insights and tools on a significant problem.
- Paper is well-structured, easy to follow, with rigorous analysis and well-explained proofs.
- Clear conclusions and future research directions are provided.
- The use of the Wasserstein distance is highlighted as a significant contribution.
- Suggestion: add more experimental results to validate the theoretical analysis.
- Suggestion: discuss implications for other deep learning models (e.g., VAEs).
