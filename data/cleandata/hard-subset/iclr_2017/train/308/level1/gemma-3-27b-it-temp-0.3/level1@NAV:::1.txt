## Review of "Towards Understanding the Training Dynamics of Generative Adversarial Networks"

This paper tackles the fundamental problem of understanding the notoriously unstable training dynamics of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). The authors aim to move beyond heuristic approaches and provide a theoretical foundation for these instabilities, ultimately proposing a new direction for more stable training. The paper is structured logically, beginning with problem definition, followed by a rigorous theoretical analysis of instability and saturation, and concluding with a theoretically grounded approach and new tools for study. This is a valuable contribution as a deeper understanding of GAN training is crucial for wider applicability and improved performance.

I recommend **rejecting** the paper in its current form. While the theoretical analysis is ambitious and potentially insightful, the presentation is dense and lacks sufficient clarity to fully assess the validity of the claims. Furthermore, the connection between the theoretical findings and the proposed practical direction, while promising, feels underdeveloped.

The core issue is the level of mathematical rigor and the lack of intuitive explanation. Many theorems and lemmas are presented without sufficient motivation or discussion of their implications. For example, the perfect discrimination theorems are stated and proven, but the practical consequences for training are not thoroughly explored beyond the observation of vanishing gradients. The jump to introducing a new metric and its relation to the Wasserstein distance feels abrupt and requires more justification. The authors claim to provide a “new direction,” but the practical implementation details and expected benefits are not clearly articulated. The reliance on numerous, often unmotivated, mathematical results makes it difficult to follow the central argument.

Despite this, the paper shows potential. To improve it, I suggest the following: 

*   **Provide more intuition:** Explain the core concepts and theorems in a more accessible way, perhaps with simplified examples or visualizations.
*   **Strengthen the connection to practice:** Elaborate on how the theoretical findings translate into concrete improvements in GAN training. Provide more details on the proposed approach and expected performance gains.
*   **Clarify notation and definitions:** Ensure all mathematical notation is clearly defined and consistent throughout the paper.
*   **Address the limitations:** Acknowledge the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis and discuss their potential impact on the results.

I have several clarifying questions for the authors:

*   The paper frequently refers to "manifolds." Can you provide a more intuitive explanation of why the generator's output is constrained to a low-dimensional manifold, and how this impacts training?
*   Theorem 3.3 relates the Wasserstein distance to the JSD of noisy distributions. How does the choice of noise distribution (e.g., Gaussian with different variances) affect the performance of the proposed training method?
*   The paper mentions instability issues even with the alternative cost function. Can you provide a more detailed explanation of why these issues persist, and how the proposed approach addresses them specifically?
*   What computational overhead is associated with the proposed method, particularly the calculation of the Wasserstein-related terms?
