- Significant theoretical contribution explaining fundamental GAN training instability.
- Identifies the role of low-dimensional manifolds in data and generator distributions as a root cause.
- Proves why gradients vanish and why parameter updates become unstable in GANs.
- Proposes noise injection as a theoretically grounded solution to create a softer/continuous metric between distributions.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reasons for accept: well-motivated, rigorous theoretical contributions, and experimental validation.
- Theorems 2.1–2.2: establish conditions under which perfect discrimination occurs.
- Theorems 2.4–2.6: rigorously explain vanishing gradients and observed instability.
- Theorem 3.3 and Corollary 3.2: show noise injection yields a continuous metric that enables stable training.
- Proofs are detailed and appear correct, with Appendix A providing sufficient mathematical rigor.
- Experiments (Figures 1–3) effectively support claims about gradient behavior and instability.
- Suggested improvement: add a detailed comparison of computational cost of noise injection vs. standard GAN training.
- Suggested improvement: discuss practical implementation challenges of the proposed approach.
- Questions for authors: how does noise injection scale to high-dimensional data (e.g., 256×256 images)? 
- Questions for authors: provide intuition for why the Cauchy distribution appears in Theorem 2.6 and how it relates to practical gradient explosion.
