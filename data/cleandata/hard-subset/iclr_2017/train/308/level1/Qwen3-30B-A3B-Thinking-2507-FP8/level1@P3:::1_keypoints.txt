- Key theoretical claim: GAN instability stems from real and generated data lying on low-dimensional, disjoint (or nearly disjoint) manifolds, causing the optimal discriminator to be perfect and generator gradients to vanish.

- Formal results: Proves that Jensen-Shannon divergence is maximized under these manifold conditions and that discriminator gradients vanish as it approaches optimality (Theorems 2.3, 2.4).

- Objective pathology: Shows the commonly used -log D generator objective is unstable because approximating the discriminator with noise leads to Cauchy-distributed gradients.

- Proposed fix: Recommends adding noise to inputs to smooth distributions and proves this yields well-behaved gradients amenable to standard GAN training.

- Decision: Reject — strong theoretical insight but insufficient practical relevance and empirical validation.

- Strength: Novel, rigorous theoretical analysis linking low-dimensional manifolds to GAN training failure modes.

- Major weakness: No empirical comparison to contemporary methods (notably WGAN), undermining claims of practical novelty or superiority.

- Gap in argument: Showing JSD is maximized is technically correct but the paper doesn't fully explain why that specifically impedes practical gradient-based optimization.

- Experimental weakness: Figures/experiments lack error bars, statistical tests, and comparisons to baselines, so they don't demonstrate practical improvement from the noise-based solution.

- Recommendation: Add a thorough comparison to existing stabilization methods (especially WGAN) and clarify relations/advantages.

- Recommendation: Discuss how results change when the discriminator is not trained to optimality, reflecting practical training setups.

- Recommendation: Provide intuitive explanation for the gradient form in Equation 4 and its practical benefits.

- Recommendation: Expand experiments to include quantitative sample-quality metrics (e.g., FID) and baseline comparisons.

- Question for authors: How does the noise-based approach compare to WGAN in stability, sample quality, and computational cost?

- Question for authors: How does the theoretical maximized JSD manifest in practice and specifically cause instability for gradient descent?

- Question for authors: What are practical guidelines for noise magnitude, annealing, and sensitivity when applying the proposed method?

- Question for authors: Why is WGAN not discussed and what are the key theoretical/practical differences between your method and WGAN?
