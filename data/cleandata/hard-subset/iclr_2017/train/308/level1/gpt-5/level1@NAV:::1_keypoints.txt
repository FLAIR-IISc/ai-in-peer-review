- Paper offers a principled theoretical account of GAN training instability and gradient vanishing/exploding.  
- Proves "perfect discrimination" theorems: if real and model distributions lie on low-dim, non-aligned manifolds, the optimal discriminator is constant almost everywhere, yielding zero generator gradients.  
- Proves a vanishing-gradient result for the original GAN loss as D → D*.  
- Proves an instability result for the −log D generator update under small discriminator noise, producing Cauchy-distributed updates with infinite variance.  
- Proposes instance noise (adding noise to real and generated samples) as a remedy, with annealing.  
- Shows smoothing via noise restores continuity and yields well-behaved gradients proportional to the JSD between smoothed distributions.  
- Gives a bound linking the true Wasserstein distance between Pr and Pg to the JSD of their noisy counterparts plus a term controlled by noise variance (Theorem 3.3).  
- Analysis is rigorous and accompanied by targeted empirical checks.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main reasons: strong, well-motivated theoretical explanations with novel proofs; a principled, practical remedy supported by theory and tied to Wasserstein geometry.  
- Manifold-support and transversality arguments convincingly explain divergence saturation despite visually good samples; Urysohn construction and gradient analyses are technically sound.  
- The instability result, despite relying on white-noise assumptions, captures heavy-tailed gradient behavior observed in practice.  
- Smoothing approach is theoretically grounded, yields tractable JSD optimization, and connects smoothed objectives to true distances as noise is annealed.  
- Suggested improvement: relax independence/white-noise assumptions in Theorem 2.6 (consider correlated noise).  
- Suggested empirical work: compare instance noise to popular stabilization methods, report precision/recall and FID, and ablate noise schedules/variances.  
- Suggested scope expansion: discuss applicability to discrete domains (text) and cases where dim Z ≈ dim X or generator image is full-dim.  
- Minor fixes: tighten exposition, define absolute continuity early, streamline measure-theoretic caveats, fix typos, include referenced figures.  
- Key question: can Theorem 2.6 be extended to correlated, heteroskedastic, or bounded discriminator errors and still show heavy tails/instability?  
- Key question: how sensitive is performance to the noise distribution and annealing schedule; is there a principled schedule from theory?  
- Key question: does instance noise reduce mode dropping empirically relative to strong baselines?  
- Key question: can the bounds be adapted to alternative metrics (e.g., IPMs with Lipschitz constraints) without input noise?  
- Key question: what occurs when the generator’s image is not strictly lower-dimensional or when latent and data dimensions match?
