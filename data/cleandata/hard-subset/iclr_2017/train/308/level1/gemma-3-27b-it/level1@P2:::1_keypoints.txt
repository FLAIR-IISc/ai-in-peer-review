- Recommendation: reject due to significant shortcomings in presentation, rigor, and justification despite commendable problem ambition.

- Main critique: theoretical results feel disconnected from practical GAN training and the proposed noise-based fix is weakly motivated.

- Clarity/motivation: paper dives into dense math without building sufficient intuition or connecting theorems to concrete GAN training challenges.

- Theorems presentation: numerous theorems (2.1, 2.2, 2.5, 2.6, 3.2, 3.3) are given with minimal explanation of practical significance; “perfect discriminators” and “perfect alignment” relevance is unclear.

- Rigor/correctness: proofs are informally presented and rely on hand-waving; Appendix A proof sketches are essential but not assessable in review timeframe.

- Noise proposal justification: the leap from theory to adding isotropic Gaussian noise feels ad-hoc and insufficiently justified.

- Unrealistic assumptions: Theorem 3.3 depends on assumptions (e.g., support on a ball of diameter C) unlikely to hold in high-dimensional GAN outputs.

- Experimental support: figures show known phenomena (discriminator convergence, vanishing gradients, gradient norm growth) but don’t establish causality or validate the proposed solution.

- Need for stronger evaluation: requires quantitative comparisons (e.g., FID, Inception Score), ablations, and direct comparisons of GANs trained with/without the proposed noise.

- Suggested focus: simplify scope to a targeted contribution, develop more intuition, and better connect theory to practical training guidance (hyperparameters, architectures).

- Address limitations: expand discussion on limitations of theoretical setup and noise-distribution assumptions.

- Questions for authors: provide details on experimental setup and noise variance impact; justify choice of isotropic Gaussian vs. other noises.

- Questions for authors: clarify sensitivity to low-dimensional manifold assumptions and applicability to high-dimensional, complex data.

- Questions for authors: detail realistic assumptions for Theorem 3.3 and highlight crucial proof steps and mathematical tools used in Appendix A.
