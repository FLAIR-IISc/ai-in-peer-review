- Paper tackles theoretical understanding of GAN training dynamics and proposes noise + Wasserstein-based direction for more stable training.
- Decision: Reject.
- Main concern: Lack of empirical rigor — experiments are minimal, lack architecture/dataset/hyperparameter details, and provide no quantitative evaluation or baselines.
- Main concern: Mathematical complexity and clarity — dense notation, poorly motivated theorems/definitions (e.g., transversality, perfect alignment), and abrupt leap to solutions.
- Main concern: Unclear practical implications — no guidance on noise variance, Wasserstein estimation, implementation, or comparisons to existing methods.
- Recommendation: Strengthen empirical validation with diverse datasets, architectures, metrics (Inception/FID), and comparisons to SOTA (e.g., WGAN, spectral norm).
- Recommendation: Improve clarity and motivation — simplify notation, provide intuitive explanations and examples linking theorems to GAN training.
- Recommendation: Provide implementation details — hyperparameter choices, practical Wasserstein estimation, computational cost and scalability.
- Recommendation: Acknowledge and discuss limitations and assumptions of the theoretical analysis.
- Recommendation: Clarify connections to related work on alternative divergences and regularization techniques.
- Question: How should the variance of added noise be chosen and how does it affect performance?
- Question: What are the computational challenges of estimating the Wasserstein distance in high dimensions and how can they be addressed?
- Question: How does the proposed approach compare to WGAN in stability, sample quality, and computational cost?
- Question: How sensitive are the theoretical results to violations of smoothness/continuity assumptions in real-world scenarios?
- Question: If noise is non-Gaussian, how do the theoretical results and empirical performance change?
