- Rigorous theoretical analysis showing GAN training instability arises because Pr and Pg lie on low-dimensional manifolds.  
- Proof that when supports are disjoint/non-aligned, an optimal discriminator can perfectly separate Pr and Pg.  
- Result that the optimal discriminator’s input gradients are zero almost everywhere on these manifolds, causing vanishing generator gradients with the original log(1-D) cost.  
- Formal derivation that the -log(D) generator cost minimizes a combination of KL(Pg‖Pr) and the JSD, explaining mode-dropping.  
- Under a noisy discriminator model, gradients from the -log(D) cost follow a Cauchy distribution with infinite variance, providing a theory for extreme instability.  
- Proposal to add continuous noise to discriminator inputs so Pr+ and Pg+ become absolutely continuous with overlapping support, yielding smooth, informative gradients.  
- Theorem 3.3 linking the noise-based procedure to the Wasserstein distance: W(Pr,Pg) is bounded by a function of the noise variance and JSD(Pr+,Pg+).  
- Decision: Accept, based on the paper’s groundbreaking, rigorous analysis and principled stabilization direction.  
- Core strength: meticulous, technically sound proofs that turn empirical GAN heuristics into formal theory.  
- Novelty: first comprehensive theoretical treatment of GAN training pathologies.  
- Requested improvement: include the referenced experimental figures and detailed experimental setups to empirically validate theoretical claims.  
- Concern: Theorem 2.6’s assumption that discriminator error is white Gaussian noise is strong; discuss robustness under more realistic/correlated noise models.  
- Concern: Theorem 3.3’s bound depends on C (diameter of noisy support); discuss practical implications when C is large or unbounded.  
- Question for authors: How sensitive is the Cauchy-gradient conclusion to the specific white Gaussian noise assumption—do other noise models produce similarly extreme variance?  
- Question for authors: Practical advice for scheduling/annealing noise variance—fixed schedule versus adaptive strategies based on training state or JSD?  
- Question for authors: For finite training budgets, might an optimal non-zero noise level V exist that best approximates the true Wasserstein distance?
