- Provides a rigorous theoretical analysis explaining GAN training instabilities.  
- Core contribution: data and generator distributions lie on low-dimensional manifolds, permitting a perfect discriminator.  
- Proves a perfect discriminator causes vanishing gradients for the generator under the original GAN loss.  
- Shows the commonly used -logD objective yields unstable, high-variance (potentially infinite-variance) generator updates.  
- Derives that the -logD objective relates to KL(Pg || Pr), offering a theoretical explanation for mode collapse.  
- Proposes adding noise to discriminator inputs and proves this yields smooth, meaningful gradients for the generator.  
- Connects the noise-regularized approach to optimizing a bound on the Wasserstein distance (Theorem 3.3).  
- Novelty concentrated in Section 3: reframes GAN training away from divergence maximization toward a more stable metric.  
- Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance based on the theoretical explanation and the practical, principled solution.  
- Suggested improvement: make the connection between theoretical noise models (e.g., white noise) and practical Gaussian noise explicit.  
- Suggested improvement: discuss practical implications and possible schedules for annealing the noise variance V.  
- Question: how sensitive is Theorem 2.6’s instability result to other forms of discriminator approximation error?  
- Question: how does the (potentially large/unknown) support diameter C affect the usefulness of the Theorem 3.3 bound and minimizing JSD as a surrogate?
