- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimization algorithm for training few-shot classifiers.  
- Learns both a task-common initialization and an LSTM-driven update mechanism tailored to few-shot tasks.  
- Evaluated on Mini-ImageNet and shows competitive performance, particularly strong in the 5-shot setting.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novelty: Uses LSTMs to learn optimization strategies, departing from traditional gradient-based meta-learning.  
- Motivation: Well-grounded in prior meta-learning and metric learning literature.  
- Theoretical intuition: Draws parallels between LSTM cell updates and gradient descent, supporting the approach.  
- Empirical validation: Outperforms baselines and is competitive with Matching Networks.  
- Experimental rigor: Includes appropriate baselines, ablation studies, and visualization of learned updates.  
- Visualization: Offers insights into the adaptability of the learned optimization strategy across tasks.  
- Request for clarity: Need more detailed comparison with Matching Networks in the 1-shot setting due to overlapping confidence intervals.  
- Scalability concern: Paper should discuss challenges in scaling to larger datasets or many-class tasks.  
- Reproducibility suggestion: Provide more details on hyperparameter tuning, training time, and computational requirements.  
- Questions for authors: Sensitivity to meta-learner hyperparameters; extension to imbalanced/noisy tasks; effect of varying number of updates during meta-training and meta-testing.
