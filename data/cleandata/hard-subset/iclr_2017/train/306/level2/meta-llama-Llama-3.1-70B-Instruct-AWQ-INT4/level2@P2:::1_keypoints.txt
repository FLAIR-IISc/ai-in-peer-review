- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimization algorithm for few-shot classification tasks.  
- Trained to quickly generalize to new classification tasks with only a few examples.  
- Demonstrates competitiveness with state-of-the-art deep metric-learning methods for few-shot learning.  
- Accepted because experiments and analysis thoroughly substantiate the claimed contributions.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and well-motivated.  
- Related work section is thorough and properly positions the contribution within meta-learning and few-shot literature.  
- Strength: provides detailed analysis of the learned optimization strategy, including visualization of gate values (Equation 2).  
- Minor issue: need more detail on how the fixed number of updates for baseline-finetune was chosen and whether it was optimized per task.  
- Minor issue: should discuss limitations, e.g., how performance degrades as number of classes increases.  
- Minor issue: should report any specific training challenges or limitations encountered.  
- Suggestion: provide details on computational resources required to train the meta-learner for reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: justify the choice of a 2-layer LSTM and report whether other architectures were explored.  
- Suggestion: discuss potential real-world applications of the approach where data is limited.  
- Questions for authors requested: clarify update-count selection for baseline, degradation with more classes, training challenges, compute requirements, and rationale/alternatives for 2-layer LSTM.
