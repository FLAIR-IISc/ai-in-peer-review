**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to few-shot learning using a meta-learner model based on Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks. The meta-learner is trained to learn the optimization algorithm used to train another learner neural network classifier in the few-shot regime. The model is designed to learn both a good initialization for the learner's parameters and a successful mechanism for updating the learner's parameters to a given small training set for a new classification task. The authors demonstrate that their approach outperforms natural baselines and is competitive with state-of-the-art methods in metric learning for few-shot learning.

**Decision**

Based on the review, I recommend **accepting** this paper for publication.

**Reasons for the Decision**

1. **Originality and Novelty**: The paper presents a novel approach to few-shot learning using a meta-learner model based on LSTM networks. This approach is distinct from existing methods in the field and offers a fresh perspective on the problem.
2. **Technical Soundness**: The paper demonstrates a good understanding of the technical aspects of few-shot learning and meta-learning. The authors provide a clear and concise description of their model, including the parametrization of the meta-learner and the training objective.
3. **Experimental Evaluation**: The paper presents a thorough experimental evaluation of the proposed method, including comparisons with state-of-the-art methods in metric learning. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach in few-shot learning tasks.

**Additional Feedback**

1. **Discussion of Limitations**: While the paper presents a novel approach to few-shot learning, it would be beneficial to discuss the limitations of the method. For example, how does the approach perform in scenarios with a large number of classes or a small number of training examples?
2. **Comparison with Other Meta-Learning Methods**: While the paper compares the proposed method with state-of-the-art methods in metric learning, it would be beneficial to compare the approach with other meta-learning methods. This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed method.
3. **Visualization of Meta-Learner**: The paper includes a visualization of the optimization strategy learned by the meta-learner. However, it would be beneficial to provide more details on how the meta-learner updates the learner during training. This could include visualizations of the input gate values and the forget gate values over time.
