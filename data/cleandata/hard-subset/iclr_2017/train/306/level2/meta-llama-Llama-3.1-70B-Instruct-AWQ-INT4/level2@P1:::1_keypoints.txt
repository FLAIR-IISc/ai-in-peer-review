- Recommendation: accept — tackles a well-defined few-shot problem and is well-motivated in the literature.  
- Main contribution: proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns the optimization algorithm and a beneficial common initialization for few-shot classifiers.  
- Claim: the meta-learner can outperform traditional gradient-based optimization (which needs many iterative steps) and is competitive with deep metric-learning methods.  
- Experiments: thorough, well-presented comparisons to baselines and SOTA, including visualization of the learned optimization strategy.  
- Weakness: no detailed analysis of computational cost, especially versus traditional gradient-based methods.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of potential limitations, notably the need for a large meta-training set.  
- Suggested improvement: include a detailed computational cost analysis and explicit discussion of limitations.  
- Suggested improvement: add further experiments on other datasets and more comparisons to other SOTA methods.  
- Questions for authors: provide computational cost details, plans to address large meta-training requirements, and results on additional datasets/SOTA comparisons.
