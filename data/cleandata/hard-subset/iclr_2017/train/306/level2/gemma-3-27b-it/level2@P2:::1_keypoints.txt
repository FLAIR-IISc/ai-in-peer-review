- Proposal: LSTM-based meta-learner that learns both initialization and a parameter update rule for few-shot learning, evaluated on Mini-ImageNet and competitive with Matching Networks.  
- Decision: Reject.

- Core weakness: No compelling evidence that the LSTM meta-learner truly learns a superior optimization algorithm beyond comparable accuracy.  
- Visualization (Figure 3) is suggestive but inconclusive about what the meta-learner has learned.  
- Missing ablation studies to isolate contributions of learned initialization, LSTM gates (forget/input), and parameter sharing.  
- Results rely on a single dataset (Mini-ImageNet), limiting claims of generalizability.  
- Experimental comparison issues: self-implemented Matching Networks may introduce variation; discrepancies with Vinyals et al. (2016) are unexplained.  
- Gradient Independence Assumption is glossed over; its impact on validity is not discussed.  
- Reported claim of outperforming baselines is weakened because the fine-tuned baseline underperforms nearest-neighbor due to apparent overfitting, suggesting experimental or regularization issues.  

- Suggested ablations: disable learned initialization, simplify/fix gate mechanisms, remove parameter sharing to quantify each component’s effect.  
- Suggested analysis: deeper inspection of learned update rules and gate patterns; visualize learned learning rates over training for different tasks.  
- Suggested baselines: compare the learned optimizer directly to standard optimizers (SGD, Adam) with carefully tuned hyperparameters.  
- Suggested evaluation: test on additional few-shot benchmarks to assess generalization.  
- Suggested theory/discussion: elaborate on the Gradient Independence Assumption and possible mitigations or justifications.  
- Suggested sensitivity analysis: report results’ sensitivity to meta-learner hyperparameters (LSTM size, meta learning rate, gradient clipping).  
- Implementation detail request: precisely describe and justify differences in the Matching Networks implementation.  

- Questions for authors: what patterns emerge in learned gate values?  
- How sensitive are results to the Gradient Independence Assumption and can it be mitigated?  
- How does performance change without parameter sharing in the LSTM?  
- What implementation differences explain the discrepancy with the original Matching Networks results?  
- What is the computational cost of meta-training compared to training a standard classifier on a large dataset?
