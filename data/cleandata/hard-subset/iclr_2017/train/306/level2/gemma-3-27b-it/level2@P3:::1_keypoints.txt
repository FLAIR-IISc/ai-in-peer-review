- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning optimizer that predicts parameter updates for few-shot learners, framing the task as learning to optimize rather than directly learning a classifier.  
- Decision: reject with request for major revisions due to insufficient detail and clarity preventing full evaluation.  
- Experimental results on Mini-ImageNet are competitive but validation is limited and analysis is shallow.  
- Major missing ablations and detailed analysis of the learned optimization strategy (e.g., which inputs drive updates).  
- Visualization of LSTM gates (Figure 3) is superficial and needs deeper interpretation.  
- Need to analyze whether the learned strategy resembles known optimizers or is novel.  
- Claim of competitiveness with Matching Networks requires more rigorous comparison and hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.  
- Initialization strategy is claimed beneficial but not quantified; compare to a carefully tuned pre-trained baseline.  
- Experimental setup lacks crucial reproducibility details: nonstandard Mini-ImageNet split and missing Matching Networks implementation specifics (conv architecture, dropout).  
- Impact of the gradient independence assumption (section 3.3.1) is not explored and should be investigated.  
- Batch Normalization strategy and choice of running averages need justification and empirical evaluation versus alternatives.  
- Request clearer definition of the problem setting and a more precise meta-learning objective formulation.  
- Ask for discussion of computational cost and overhead of the LSTM-based meta-learner compared to other meta-learning methods.  
- Suggest exploring applicability to other learner architectures (e.g., ResNets) and datasets beyond Mini-ImageNet.  
- Recommend deeper discussion of analogies and differences between the LSTM cell-state update and gradient descent.  
- Suggest including comparisons to prominent methods like Model-Agnostic Meta-Learning (MAML).  
- Questions for authors: provide detailed examples of LSTM-generated updates, report hyperparameter sensitivity, quantify computational overhead, show effect of removing gradient independence, and evaluate different Batch Normalization strategies.
