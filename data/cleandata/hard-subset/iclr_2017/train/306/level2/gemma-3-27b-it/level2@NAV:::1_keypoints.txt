- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that predicts parameter updates to learn initialization and an optimization strategy for few-shot learning.  
- Motivation is clear and the LSTM-as-optimizer formulation is novel and interesting.  
- Recommend rejecting in current form due to insufficient experimental validation and comparative analysis.  
- Reported competitiveness with Matching Networks is weak because comparisons use a custom Matching Networks implementation and a self-created Mini-ImageNet split.  
- Lacks ablation study to separate contributions of learned initialization versus learned update rule.  
- Evaluations rely on a single dataset (Mini-ImageNet); additional benchmarks like Omniglot and tieredImageNet are needed.  
- Gate-value visualizations are insightful but limited; deeper analysis of what the meta-learner is learning is required.  
- Parameter-sharing strategy for the LSTM needs clearer explanation of effects on model size and compute cost.  
- The gradient independence assumption requires justification and discussion of ignoring second-order effects.  
- Paper should report computational resources and overhead for training/inference given meta-learning’s potential cost.  
- Encourage resubmission after addressing validation and analysis gaps; core idea has merit but is not yet solidified.  
- Requests for clarification: sensitivity to meta-learner hyperparameters, computational overhead versus fine-tuning, and quantitative evidence that the learned initialization speeds few-shot convergence.
