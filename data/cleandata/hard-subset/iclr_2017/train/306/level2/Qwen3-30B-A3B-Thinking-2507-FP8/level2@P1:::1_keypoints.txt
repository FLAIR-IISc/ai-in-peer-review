- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimization algorithm and a good initialization for few-shot classification tasks.  
- Key insight: gradient descent updates resemble LSTM cell state updates, enabling the LSTM to learn the optimizer.  
- Motivation: standard gradient-based optimization and initializations are suboptimal in few-shot settings; the approach addresses this.  
- Empirical claim: competitive performance against baselines and Matching Networks on Mini-ImageNet.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Strength: well-motivated, novel application of meta-learning to few-shot learning with a clear conceptual connection to LSTM updates.  
- Strength: experimental evaluation is thorough with appropriate baselines and insightful visualization of learned optimization (Figure 3).  
- Strength: effectively distinguishes the work from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) by focusing on few-shot rather than large-scale optimization.  
- Revision 1: add comparisons to more recent/further few-shot state-of-the-art methods (e.g., Prototypical Networks, Relation Networks, MAML).  
- Revision 2: clarify exact differences from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) and why this method suits few-shot learning specifically.  
- Revision 3: provide detailed description of the Mini-ImageNet construction and how it differs from Vinyals et al. (2016).  
- Questions for authors: compare to Prototypical/Relation Networks status-wise; explain interpretation of input gate behavior in Figure 3 for 1-shot vs 5-shot; and justify why meta-training must match the number of updates used at meta-test and whether the meta-learner generalizes across different update counts.
