- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimization algorithm and initialization for few-shot learning.  
- Claims the model learns appropriate parameter updates for a fixed number of steps and a beneficial learner initialization.  
- Empirically outperforms two baselines and is competitive with Matching Networks, especially in 5-shot settings on Mini-ImageNet.  
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Addresses the challenge that gradient-based optimization struggles with limited data and is well-motivated by limitations of existing optimizers and transfer learning.  
- LSTM meta-learner formulation is clever, borrowing from LSTM cell state update mechanics.  
- Experimental design and metrics are appropriate and generally provide strong support for the claims.  
- Paper includes sufficient technical details (architecture, training, hyperparameters) for reproducibility.  
- Direct comparison to Matching Networks is incomplete due to a different dataset split, potentially affecting fairness.  
- Recommend more analysis of why the method outperforms baselines, particularly regarding overfitting in the fine-tuning baseline.  
- Recommend including broader comparisons to recent few-shot learning methods beyond Matching Networks.  
- Recommend clarifying how the learned optimization strategy relates to performance across different tasks.  
- Recommend discussing potential limitations in scaling to larger datasets or more complex tasks.  
- Requests clarification on tests beyond Mini-ImageNet and whether the learned strategy generalizes across architectures.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution to meta-learning that would be strong with the suggested minor improvements.
