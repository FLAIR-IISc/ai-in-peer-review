- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimization algorithm for few-shot classifier training.  
- Shows the LSTM update rule closely resembles gradient descent, enabling learning of both initialization and task-specific updates.  
- Trains end-to-end to directly optimize few-shot task performance, with the meta-learner adapting learning rate and updates based on gradient, loss, and parameters.  
- Positions the work clearly within meta-learning literature and distinguishes it from prior LSTM optimizer work (Andrychowicz et al.).  
- Paper accepted for publication.  
- Significant contribution: demonstrates a meta-learner can learn a beneficial initialization plus an adaptive optimizer tailored to few-shot learning.  
- Experiments on Mini-ImageNet outperform natural baselines and are competitive with Matching Networks.  
- Provides clear ablation studies and visualizations that illuminate how the meta-optimizer adapts across tasks.  
- Strong technical execution: clear model description and handling of implementation details (parameter sharing, gradient preprocessing, batch normalization).  
- Training procedure matches test-time conditions, which is crucial for meta-learning performance.  
- Lacks comparison with recent meta-learning methods like MAML—adding this would improve context.  
- Needs ablation separating the contributions of learned initialization versus adaptive update strategy.  
- Could benefit from theoretical discussion on why training for a fixed number of updates improves performance and its relation to the meta-learning objective.  
- Should include a detailed comparison of computational efficiency (training/inference time) versus other few-shot methods.  
- Requires clarification on whether meta-training must use the same number of updates as meta-testing for practical application.
