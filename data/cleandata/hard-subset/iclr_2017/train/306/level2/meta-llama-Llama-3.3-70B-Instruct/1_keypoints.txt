- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns the optimization algorithm used to train a learner neural network in few-shot settings.  
- Meta-learner is designed to learn a common initialization that enables rapid fine-tuning and quick convergence.  
- Demonstrates competitiveness with deep metric-learning techniques for few-shot learning.  
- Approach is well-motivated and well-situated within the literature.  
- Paper provides a clear and thorough explanation of the meta-learning formulation, model architecture, and training procedure.  
- Experiments on Mini-ImageNet show the model outperforms natural baselines and is competitive with state-of-the-art metric learning methods.  
- Visualization of the learned optimization strategy offers insight into how the meta-learner updates the learner.  
- Suggest providing more details on hyperparameter selection and sensitivity to hyperparameters.  
- Recommend deeper analysis of the learned optimization strategy and comparisons with other optimization methods.  
- Questions for authors: how the meta-learner handles tasks with different numbers of classes/examples; whether it can be fine-tuned during meta-testing; how it compares to methods like MAML.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — well-executed, significant contribution that could be strengthened with additional analysis and clarification.
