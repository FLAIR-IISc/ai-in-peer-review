- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimizer and initialization for few-shot CNN classification.  
- Frames learner parameter updates as LSTM cell updates, taking gradients and loss as input to produce updated parameters.  
- Meta-learner is trained end-to-end to minimize learner performance on a held-out test set per task.  
- Demonstrates strong results on Mini-ImageNet, outperforming fine-tuning baselines and competitive with Matching Networks.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Novel and insightful formulation replacing hand-designed optimizers with a learned LSTM optimizer and learned initialization.  
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical validation with careful experimental setup (including batch-norm handling).  
- Strength: Paper is clear and well-motivated, with an intuitive analogy between LSTM updates and gradient descent.  
- Strength: Methodologically sound training procedure, parameter sharing for high-dimensional parameters, and gradient preprocessing.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation with appropriate baselines and fair comparisons.  
- Weakness: Assumes gradient independence (ignores meta-learner gradient dependence), needing discussion of limitations.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility concern due to custom Mini-ImageNet split; provide exact splits/lists.  
- Suggestion: Clarify role of the first standard LSTM layer versus the modified meta-learner layer.  
- Suggestion: Strengthen gate-activation visualizations with quantitative statistics (e.g., mean/variance of learned learning rates).  
- Suggestion: Report computational cost and compare training/inference time against baselines and Matching Networks.  
- Question: Explain why 1-shot uses T=12 updates while 5-shot uses T=5—was this chosen by hyperparameter search?  
- Question: Clarify batch-normalization during meta-training—does using batch statistics on Dtest leak information between test examples?
