- Novel LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimizer and initialization to update a CNN learner for few-shot classification.  
- Meta-training across episodes minimizes learner generalization loss after a small fixed number of updates, enabling rapid convergence.  
- Demonstrated competitive performance on Mini-ImageNet, outperforming strong fine-tuning baselines and matching state-of-the-art metric methods.  
- Core insight: analogy between LSTM cell updates and gradient descent; learning initial state (c0) and update rules is crucial for few-shot adaptation.  
- Experiments are thorough and carefully designed, including handling BatchNorm to avoid information leakage between episodes.  
- Recommendation: Accept — due to conceptual novelty and strong empirical support.  
- Ablation study suggested to disentangle contributions of learned initialization vs learned update rule.  
- Clarify the 2-layer LSTM meta-learner architecture with more detail or a diagram showing layer interactions and state flow.  
- Provide discussion of computational cost (meta-training unrolls) and compare runtime/complexity to baselines and Matching Networks.  
- Question: Are BatchNorm running statistics stable when computed on tiny episode training sets; were alternatives like Layer/Group Norm considered?  
- Question: How was the number of updates T chosen for each task and how sensitive is performance to this hyperparameter?  
- Question: More clearly distinguish this approach from Andrychowicz et al. (2016), highlighting architectural and objective differences critical for few-shot success.
