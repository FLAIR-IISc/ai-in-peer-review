- Summary: Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns both optimizer updates and a task-agnostic initial parameter state for few-shot CNN classification, trained end-to-end to minimize final loss on new tasks.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Novel, well-motivated approach framing learning-to-learn via an LSTM-as-optimizer and jointly learned initialization is a significant conceptual contribution.  
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical results on Mini-ImageNet, outperforming fine-tuning baselines and competitive with Matching Networks, supported by effective ablations.  
- Insight: The analogy between LSTM cell updates and gradient descent provides a principled parameterization of a dynamic optimizer that uses history of gradients/losses.  
- Insight: Learning the initial learner parameters (c0) gives a powerful inductive bias that enables rapid adaptation from few examples and explains poor Baseline-finetune performance.  
- Experimental rigor: Transparent Mini-ImageNet split, appropriate baselines, and careful BatchNorm handling to avoid cross-episode leakage are strengths.  
- Relationship to prior work: Heavy reliance on Andrychowicz et al. (2016) machinery; paper should more explicitly delineate its novel contributions (application to few-shot and learnable c0).  
- BatchNorm ambiguity: Description of using "batch statistics for both training and testing set" is unclear and could be misinterpreted as potential information leakage.  
- Computational cost: Unrolling and backpropagating through optimization is expensive; a discussion of training time and resources is warranted.  
- Analysis gap: Figure 3 is qualitative; quantitative analysis of learned optimizer behavior (e.g., learned rates vs. loss/gradient norms) would strengthen interpretation.  
- Question 1: Clarify exact BatchNorm procedure—are BN statistics for meta-loss computed from the episode's D_test mini-batch or from running averages on D_train?  
- Question 2: Explain choice of update steps T (12 for 1-shot, 5 for 5-shot) and sensitivity of performance to this hyperparameter.  
- Question 3: Did stronger regularization (L2, dropout) for the fine-tuning baseline reduce its gap to nearest-neighbor or the meta-learned method?
