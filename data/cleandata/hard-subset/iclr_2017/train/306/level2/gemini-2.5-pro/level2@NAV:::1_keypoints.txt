- Presents an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimization algorithm and optimal initial weights for a separate learner to enable rapid few-shot classification.
- Strong recommendation for acceptance based on novelty and empirical validation.
- Novel framing: treats few-shot learning as "learning an optimizer," addressing poor initialization and the mismatch of generic optimizers for few update steps.
- Elegant analogy between gradient descent updates and LSTM cell-state updates enabling task-specific dynamic updates.
- Empirical results on Mini-ImageNet are strong, outperforming fine-tuning baselines and competitive with Matching Networks.
- Paper is well-written, clearly positioned in related work, and explains model details like parameter sharing and input preprocessing thoroughly.
- Visualization of learned gate values offers preliminary insight into the learned optimization strategy.
- Suggested improvement: provide an ablation or deeper discussion on the "gradient independence assumption" and its potential impact.
- Suggested improvement: include an ablation study showing the importance of the batch normalization strategy in the meta-learning setting.
- Suggested improvement: discuss anticipated challenges and scalability when extending to more classes/examples.
- Clarifying question: how sensitive is performance to the number of update steps (T) and how were T values chosen for 1-shot vs 5-shot?
- Clarifying question: what is the computational overhead of training the meta-learner compared to metric-learning models like Matching Networks?
