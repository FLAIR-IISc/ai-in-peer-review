- Proposes learning an optimizer as an LSTM that stores learner parameters in cell state to perform a fixed small number of adaptation steps for few-shot classification.  
- Meta-learner computes coordinate-wise input and forget gates from loss, gradient, parameter value, and previous gate to learn both initialization and update rule.  
- Meta-training unrolls T optimization steps per episode and backpropagates through them to minimize episode test loss, matching train/test conditions.  
- Parameter sharing across coordinates with log-scaling keeps the meta-learner compact and scalable.  
- Practical techniques include a stable initialization scheme, a “gradient independence” approximation to avoid second derivatives, and episode-safe BatchNorm handling.  
- Experiments on Mini-ImageNet (5-way 1-shot/5-shot) show strong gains over nearest-neighbor and fine-tuning, and competitive or better performance than Matching Networks.  
- Gate visualizations indicate interpretable strategies (e.g., weight-decay-like forgetting) and adaptive behavior across tasks.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reasons for acceptance: novel, well-motivated formulation unifying initialization and update rules; competitive empirical results and careful practical design with released code.  
- Problem/motivation: addresses mismatch of standard optimizers to fixed, few-step adaptation regimes—matching meta-objective to fixed-step test loss is principled.  
- Methodology: elegant mapping of parameter updates to LSTM cell updates and compact coordinate-wise parameter sharing using informative gating signals.  
- Soundness: training/backprop through unrolled optimization is reasonable; BatchNorm handling avoids episode leakage and maintains consistency.  
- Empirics: clear gains on Mini-ImageNet; 5-shot surpasses Matching Nets and 1-shot is competitive; fine-tuning baseline overfits in 1-shot highlighting meta-training benefits.  
- Suggested essential improvements: report full means and 95% CIs and number of meta-test episodes for statistical significance.  
- Suggestion: ensure architectural parity (32 vs 64 filters) across methods or include control with 64 filters to ensure fair comparison.  
- Suggestion: provide ablations isolating learned initialization, gating, preprocessing, and second LSTM layer to identify key contributors.  
- Suggestion: strengthen fine-tuning baseline with regularization, augmentation, and modest hyperparameter search under the same T-step constraint.  
- Suggestion: analyze sensitivity to number of updates T and generalization to T′ ≠ T.  
- Suggestion: report computational cost (unroll length, memory, wall-clock per episode, total training time).  
- Suggestion: discuss limits of coordinate-wise sharing and test per-tensor/per-layer context features to see if modest context helps.  
- Suggestion: include results on an additional benchmark (e.g., Omniglot) or justify focus on Mini-ImageNet.  
- Clarification sought: exact LSTM hidden sizes, number of meta-learner parameters relative to learner, and signs of meta-learner overfitting.  
- Clarification sought: number of episodes used for meta-training/meta-testing and variance across random class splits.  
- Clarification sought: sensitivity to the “gradient independence” approximation and whether higher-order gradient experiments were tried.  
- Clarification sought: how well meta-learner trained for T steps generalizes to fewer/more test steps.  
- Clarification sought: how Matching Networks hyperparameters and augmentation/preprocessing were tuned and matched across methods.  
- Clarification sought: effect of removing BatchNorm or replacing with group/layer norm.  
- Clarification sought: any failure modes where forget gate causes excessive parameter shrinkage within an episode and safeguards used.  
- Clarification sought: scalability to deeper learners (more filters or residual blocks) and any stability or speed issues.  
- Overall: timely, well-argued contribution advancing meta-learning with an optimizer-as-LSTM approach; with requested clarifications and ablations it would be a strong conference paper.
