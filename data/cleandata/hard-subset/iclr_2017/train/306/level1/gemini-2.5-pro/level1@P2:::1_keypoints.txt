- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimizer for few-shot image classification, producing both a learned initialization and update sequence for a classifier.  
- Frames few-shot learning as a "learning to optimize" problem via an analogy between gradient descent and LSTM cell updates.  
- Strong empirical results on Mini-ImageNet: outperforms fine-tuning baselines and is competitive with or superior to Matching Networks.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novel and elegant formulation that opens a new meta-learning direction beyond metric learning.  
- Practical viability demonstrated by solid experimental performance.  
- LSTM framework jointly learns a universal initialization (c0) and adaptive, task-specific update rules (input/forget gates).  
- Improves over standard transfer learning with fixed optimizers, which tend to overfit in low-data regimes.  
- Rigorous experimental setup with transparent Mini-ImageNet splits and re-implemented baselines for fair comparison.  
- Paper is well-written and addresses important implementation details (parameter sharing, batch-norm handling) aiding reproducibility.  
- Recommend deeper analysis of the learned optimizer (e.g., correlate gate behavior with episode properties).  
- Note counter-intuitive choice of update counts (1-shot: 12 updates vs 5-shot: 5); suggest ablation/discussion.  
- Request more meta-learner architectural details (e.g., hidden state sizes) to improve clarity and reproducibility.  
- Questions for authors: rationale for differing update counts, possibility of task-dependent c0, computational cost compared to Matching Networks, and sensitivity to learner architecture depth (e.g., ResNets).
