- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimizer for a CNN learner by mapping LSTM cell updates to gradient-descent updates, and learns a shared initial parameterization.  
- Demonstrates strong results on Mini-ImageNet for 1-shot and 5-shot classification, outperforming baselines and beating Matching Networks in 5-shot.  
- Decision: Accept — judged a significant contribution to meta-learning and few-shot learning.  
- Novelty/conceptual elegance: frames few-shot as “learning to optimize,” with initial learner weights represented as the LSTM’s initial cell state.  
- Strong empirical validation: convincingly outperforms standard fine-tuning and is competitive with state-of-the-art metric learners.  
- Motivation: standard optimizers fail in few-shot settings; the method directly addresses poor update dynamics and initialization.  
- Technical execution: uses coordinate-wise parameter sharing, careful input preprocessing, gate initialization to mimic stable SGD, and special handling of BatchNorm to avoid episode information leakage.  
- Experimental design: appropriate baselines (including Baseline-finetune) that highlight the value of a learned optimizer; comparisons to Matching Networks strengthen the claim.  
- Improvement request: provide more quantitative analysis of the learned optimizer (e.g., correlate forget-gate activations with parameter magnitudes and input-gate values with gradient variance).  
- Improvement request: ablation on number of updates T — report sensitivity and whether a meta-learner trained for one T generalizes to other T at test time.  
- Improvement request: discuss scalability to deeper architectures (e.g., ResNets) beyond the relatively shallow CNN used.  
- Question: motivation and role of the 2-layer LSTM design; have you tried a single-layer variant?  
- Question: clarify BatchNorm during meta-training — are BN statistics for D_test computed from D_test batches or from running averages from D_train updates?  
- Question: rationale for choosing T=12 for 1-shot and T=5 for 5-shot, and how these values were determined.
