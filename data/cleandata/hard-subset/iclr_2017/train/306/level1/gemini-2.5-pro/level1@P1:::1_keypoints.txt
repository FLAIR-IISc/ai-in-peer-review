- Novel approach: frames few-shot learning as meta-learning by using an LSTM "meta-learner" to learn an optimizer for a standard neural network "learner".
- Key mechanism: treats gradient descent like an LSTM cell update, feeding learner gradients and losses into the meta-learner to output parameter updates.
- Joint learning: meta-learner learns both an effective update rule (LSTM gates) and a good initial learner parameterization (learned c_0).
- Training objective: end-to-end optimization for learner generalization after a fixed number of training steps.
- Empirical results: strong performance on Mini-ImageNet, outperforming fine-tuning baselines and competitive with Matching Networks (superior in 5-shot).
- Decision: Accept.
- Main reasons for acceptance: high novelty/conceptual elegance and strong empirical validation.
- Supporting strengths: well-motivated, clearly written, technically sound, with rigorous meta-training/validation/testing splits and appropriate baselines.
- Additional insight: visualization of meta-learner gates suggests adaptive, nontrivial update strategies.
- Implementation care: thoughtful handling of batch normalization to avoid episode information leakage.
- Suggested ablations: evaluate effect of not learning c_0 and impact of fixing the forget gate (f_t=1).
- Clarity request: simplify/explicitly tabulate batch-norm statistics used across the four training/testing scenarios.
- Complexity concern: discuss computational/memory overhead from unrolling learner optimization and compare training time to baselines like Matching Networks.
- Architecture generality: clarify whether the meta-learner's strategy generalizes beyond the used 4-layer CNN to deeper learners.
- Questions for authors: effect of assuming gradient independence w.r.t. meta-parameters, training a meta-learner robust to variable update horizons, and scalability/generalization to larger N-way classification settings.
