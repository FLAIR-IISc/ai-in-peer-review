- Presents an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimization algorithm for a separate CNN few-shot classifier.  
- Frames the learner’s parameter update as an LSTM cell-state update, enabling end-to-end training to minimize final test loss after fixed optimization steps.  
- Learns both an optimal parameter initialization and a custom update rule for rapid convergence from few examples.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance with state-of-the-art metric-learning methods on Mini-ImageNet, especially strong in 5-shot and competitive in 1-shot.  
- Recommends the paper for Acceptance based on novel methodology and strong empirical results.  
- Key contribution: combining learned initialization (LSTM initial cell state) with learned update rule addresses few-shot optimization failures.  
- Evaluation is thorough with well-chosen baselines, including fine-tuning comparisons that highlight meta-learning benefits.  
- Shows scientific rigor in handling Batch Normalization to avoid information leakage between episodes.  
- Suggests clarifying the 2-layer LSTM meta-learner and how the “normal” and “modified” layers interact.  
- Recommends an ablation study to isolate contributions of learned initialization, input gate, and forget gate.  
- Suggests more quantitative analysis linking gate-value visualizations to training-episode properties.  
- Asks about sensitivity to the number of inner-loop updates (T) and how T was chosen for 1-shot vs 5-shot.  
- Asks about trade-offs if the gradient independence assumption were relaxed (e.g., computing full gradients).
