- Proposes framing the classifier optimizer as a learnable LSTM-based meta-learner that updates learner parameters via loss and gradient inputs.  
- Represents learner parameters as LSTM cell state and draws an analogy between gradient descent and LSTM cell updates.  
- Meta-learner jointly learns a task-agnostic initialization and a fast update rule from a distribution of few-shot tasks.  
- Trained end-to-end to minimize the learner’s final loss on each few-shot task’s test set.  
- Empirically evaluated on Mini-ImageNet, outperforming fine-tuning baselines and competitive with Matching Networks (better in 5-shot).  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Novel and elegant reformulation of optimization as a learnable recurrent model, shifting paradigm from static embeddings to dynamic optimization.  
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical results with careful experimental details (parameter sharing, batch-norm handling) that support claims.  
- Novelty: First to apply learned optimizers effectively to few-shot learning and to jointly learn initialization (`c0`) and update rule.  
- Technical soundness: Compact/scalable via parameter sharing; training objective aligns with few-shot generalization; justifies gradient-independence assumption and batch-norm treatment.  
- Clarity: Well-written, intuitive motivation, clear algorithm/figures, and transparent experimental documentation.  
- Suggested ablations: quantify importance of learning `c0` versus fixed/init pre-trained; test impact of fixing forget gate `ft` to 1.  
- Suggested evaluation: test generalization of meta-learners across different k-shot settings (e.g., train on 5-shot, test on 1- or 10-shot).  
- Suggested analysis: report computational costs (time/memory) compared to Matching Networks and baselines due to backprop through unrolled updates.  
- Questions for authors: clarify batch-norm strategy during meta-training, justify choice and sensitivity of update counts T (12 for 1-shot, 5 for 5-shot), and whether learned strategies differ across layer types.
