- Paper casts few-shot learner parameter updates as an LSTM cell-state update, with gates learned from gradients, loss, previous gates, and parameters.
- Meta-learner shares parameters across coordinates for scalability and uses gradient/loss preprocessing for stability.
- Training optimizes the test loss after a fixed T inner-loop updates, matching training and testing conditions.
- The method meta-learns the learner initialization c0, providing a prior suited for fast adaptation.
- Practical issues (per-episode batch-norm stats, gate bias initialization) are addressed thoughtfully.
- Empirically outperforms nearest-neighbor and fine-tuning baselines on Mini-ImageNet and is competitive with Matching Networks (stronger on 5-shot).
- Visualizations indicate the learned optimizer adopts meaningful strategies (e.g., weight-decay-like forgetting).
- Decision: Weak accept.
- Key reason 1: Novel, well-motivated adaptation of learned optimizers to few-shot learning that jointly learns initialization and update rule for few steps.
- Key reason 2: Competitive benchmark results, released code, and careful experimental practices that increase credibility.
- Problem framed clearly: rapid generalization in k-shot N-way classification via learned updates and initialization.
- Positioning: Appropriate extension of learned optimizer work to meta-learning, contrasted with metric and memory-augmented approaches.
- Results largely support claims but with caveats about comparability and ablation depth.
- Concern: Uses a non-standard Mini-ImageNet split and reimplements Matching Nets while omitting an optional fine-tuning variant, limiting direct comparison.
- Concern: Limited ablations—unclear contributions of learned initialization vs learned optimizer, preprocessing, gate architectures, and gradient-independence approximation.
- Concern: Missing reporting details such as confidence intervals, number of test episodes, and computational overhead (time/memory).
- Recommendation: Provide ablations for learned-init only vs learned-gates only vs full model, gate variants, preprocessing, and robustness to different inner-loop lengths.
- Recommendation: Strengthen baselines and comparability by evaluating canonical Mini-ImageNet split, Omniglot, FCE Matching Nets with fine-tuning, and other baselines like MANN or Prototypical Networks.
- Recommendation: Quantify variance (confidence intervals), training/inference cost (time, memory, per-parameter LSTM state), and sensitivity to learner architecture and mixed N/k training.
- Method detail request: Discuss and, if possible, experimentally assess the gradient-independence assumption and BN handling (per-episode vs running stats).
- Clarification needed: Specify whether c0 is learned per-parameter and how this scales in meta-parameters.
- Questions for authors: quantify contribution of learned c0 vs learned gates with corresponding numbers.
- Questions for authors: report sensitivity to test-time adaptation steps when deviating from trained T.
- Questions for authors: provide computational overhead (time/memory) per episode and meta-parameter counts for optimizer and c0.
- Questions for authors: test robustness to matching learner capacity (e.g., 64-filter CNN) and generalization across N and k.
- Questions for authors: confirm whether Matching Nets were re-tuned on the split and whether FCE/fine-tuning variants were included.
- Request: Provide results on a standard Mini-ImageNet split for fair comparison with prior work.
