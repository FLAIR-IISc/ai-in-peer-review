- Proposes an LSTM-based learned optimizer that treats learner parameters as LSTM cell state for rapid few-shot N-way classification adaptation.  
- Parameterizes per-parameter updates with coordinate-shared LSTM gates informed by gradient, loss, parameter value, and previous gate values.  
- Jointly learns a task-common parameter initialization and an update rule tuned for a fixed small number of adaptation steps via episodic meta-training.  
- Training includes gradient/loss preprocessing, careful per-episode batch-norm handling to avoid inter-episode leakage, and a gradient-independence approximation (no second derivatives).  
- Empirically outperforms nearest-neighbor over pre-trained embeddings and fine-tuning baselines on Mini-ImageNet, and is competitive with Matching Networks (better on 5-shot in authors’ setup).  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: Novel, well-motivated meta-learning formulation that explicitly learns both initialization and optimizer for few-shot settings.  
- Key reason: Clear empirical gains over strong baselines and competitive performance with state-of-the-art metric learning on a standard benchmark.  
- Positioning: Distinct from general learned optimizers and metric-based methods by optimizing generalization after a fixed number of updates with an episodic objective.  
- Technical soundness: Coordinate-wise LSTM, preprocessing, and BN strategy are reasonable; gradient-independence is a known approximation and empirically effective.  
- Minor clarity issue: Ambiguity in the sign/convention of the candidate update in the update rule.  
- Evidence: Uses established episodic protocols, reports confidence intervals, provides architecture/training details and gate visualizations indicating task-dependent adaptation.  
- Limitation: Uses an in-house Mini-ImageNet split and reimplementation of Matching Networks, possibly reducing comparability to prior work.  
- Limitation: Limited ablations to separate contributions of learned initialization vs learned update rule and to probe gate roles.  
- Limitation: Only evaluates 5-way classification and does not assess generalization across different N or mismatched numbers of adaptation steps.  
- Suggested improvement: Release code and standard splits; add Omniglot to improve comparability and reproducibility.  
- Suggested ablations: learn-only-initialization vs learn-only-optimizer, remove/modify forget gate, remove preprocessing, and test second-order gradients on small models.  
- Fairness/controls: Match model capacity across methods and enable Matching Networks’ optional test-time fine-tuning for tighter comparison.  
- Generalization tests: Evaluate cross-N and cross-k generalization and sensitivity to different numbers of adaptation steps T at meta-test.  
- Efficiency request: Report compute cost, training time, memory footprint, and adaptation latency versus standard optimizers/baselines.  
- BN ablation: Provide experiments comparing per-episode BN statistics to alternatives and quantify impact.  
- Questions for authors: clarify c̃t sign and transformation; report results with 64-filter learner for capacity parity; explain Matching Networks setup and optional fine-tuning omission.  
- Questions for authors: provide ablation results for learning only initialization or only optimizer, and experiments retaining/truncating second-order terms.  
- Questions for authors: quantify sensitivity to different adaptation step counts at meta-test and whether 1-shot-trained meta-learner generalizes to 5-shot.  
- Questions for authors: confirm BN running averages are computed only from the episode’s Dtrain and report differences versus other BN strategies.  
- Questions for authors: give meta-training episode count, total training time, memory footprint, and compare adaptation latency to fine-tuning with standard optimizers.
