- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that encodes learner parameters in cell state and uses learned per-parameter input/forget gates as update rules for few-shot learning.  
- Jointly learns a task-common initialization and a data-driven update schedule; uses parameter sharing and gradient/loss preprocessing for scalability.  
- Aligns training with evaluation via episodic training that optimizes test loss over episodes.  
- Provides practical implementation details (batch norm handling, gate initializations) and gate visualizations.  
- Empirical results on 5-way Mini-ImageNet (1- and 5-shot) outperform simple baselines and are competitive with Matching Networks, especially strong in 5-shot.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main reason: conceptual novelty and clear framing of per-parameter optimizer dynamics as an LSTM that learns both initialization and update policy.  
- Main reason: empirical competitiveness and evidence the learned optimizer adapts across tasks.  
- Strength: problem formulation and motivation are well defined and positioned against meta-learning, learned optimizers, and metric learning.  
- Strength: episodic training optimizing test loss, reported CIs, and visualizations lend experimental rigor.  
- Weakness: relies on the “gradient independence” approximation (ignoring second derivatives) without sufficient analysis.  
- Weakness: comparisons omit other meta-learners and may be unfair due to architecture mismatch (learner uses 32 filters vs. baselines’ 64).  
- Weakness: key ablations are missing (e.g., learned initialization vs learned updates, fixed vs learned update steps).  
- Weakness: computational overhead (compute/memory/wall-clock) is not quantified.  
- Suggestion: provide full numerical results with CIs, number of episodes, seeds, and release Mini-ImageNet split/code for reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: add ablations—(a) ft=1 vs learned forget gate, (b) fixed vs variable number of steps, (c) learned init + vanilla SGD vs learned updates, (d) effect of preprocessing/BN strategy.  
- Suggestion: report compute/memory and wall-clock per episode versus SGD/ADAM.  
- Suggestion: evaluate robustness to N-way/k-shot mismatch and transfer to different learner architectures/deeper CNNs.  
- Suggestion: discuss fairness of architectural choices across baselines.  
- Question: how much of the gain comes from learned initialization versus the learned update rule?  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to training the meta-learner for T steps when evaluated at different T?  
- Question: what is the computational overhead per episode relative to SGD/ADAM?  
- Question: can the learned optimizer transfer to different learner architectures without re-meta-training?  
- Question: did you test ft fixed at 1, and what was the effect?  
- Question: can you release exact Mini-ImageNet splits and report generalization beyond 5-way/5-shot?
