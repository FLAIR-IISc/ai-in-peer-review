1) Summary of claims and contributions
The paper addresses few-shot learning by framing it as meta-learning of an optimizer and initialization. It proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner whose cell state encodes the learner’s parameters, with learned input/forget gates implementing per-parameter update rules tailored to a fixed, small number of updates. The method jointly learns a task-common initialization and a data-driven update schedule, uses parameter-sharing across coordinates and gradient/loss preprocessing for scalability, and aligns training with evaluation by optimizing test loss over episodes. Practical details (batch norm handling, gate initializations) and gate visualizations are provided. On 5-way Mini-ImageNet (1- and 5-shot), the approach outperforms simple baselines and is competitive with Matching Networks, especially strong in 5-shot.

2) Decision: Accept
Key reasons: (i) Conceptual novelty and clarity—casting per-parameter optimizer dynamics as an LSTM cell that simultaneously learns initialization and update policy for few-shot regimes; (ii) Empirical competitiveness with thoughtful engineering to match meta-train/test conditions and evidence that the learned optimizer adapts across tasks.

3) Supporting arguments
- Problem: Clearly defined k-shot, N-way episodic classification; importance well motivated.
- Motivation and placement: Solid linkage to meta-learning, learned optimizers, and metric learning; distinguishes from Andrychowicz et al. (general optimization) and Matching Networks (metric learning).
- Support for claims: Episodic training optimizing test loss is appropriate; results on Mini-ImageNet with confidence intervals and reasonable baselines. Visualizations and BN protocol lend rigor. However, some concerns remain: reliance on the “gradient independence” approximation (ignoring second derivatives) is under-examined; comparisons omit other meta-learners; architecture mismatches (learner uses 32 filters vs. 64 in baselines) may affect fairness; ablations (e.g., learned init vs. learned updates) are missing; computational overhead is not quantified.

4) Suggestions to improve (helpful, not central to decision)
- Provide full numerical results with CIs, report number of episodes, seeds, and release the Mini-ImageNet split/code for reproducibility.
- Add ablations: (a) ft=1 vs learned forget gate; (b) fixed vs variable number of steps; (c) learned init + vanilla SGD vs learned updates; (d) effect of preprocessing/BN strategy.
- Report compute/memory and wall-clock per episode versus SGD/ADAM.
- Evaluate robustness to N-way/k-shot mismatch and to different learner architectures/deeper CNNs.
- Discuss fairness of architectural choices across baselines.

Questions for authors
- How much of the gain comes from the learned initialization versus the learned update rule?
- How sensitive is performance to training the meta-learner for T steps when evaluated at different T?
- What is the computational overhead per episode relative to SGD/ADAM?
- Can the learned optimizer transfer to different learner architectures without re-meta-training?
- Did you test ft fixed at 1, and what was the effect?
- Could you release the exact Mini-ImageNet splits and report per-way/per-shot generalization beyond 5-way?
