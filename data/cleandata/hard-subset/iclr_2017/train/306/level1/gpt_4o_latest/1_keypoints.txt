- Decision: Accept.  
- Core contribution: Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimization algorithm to train a neural network classifier for few-shot learning.  
- Learned initialization: Meta-learner also learns a task-common initialization that aids rapid generalization with minimal data.  
- Motivation: Clearly motivated by limitations of standard gradient-based optimization in the few-shot regime.  
- Positioning: Well-situated within meta-learning literature, relating to Matching Networks and gradient-based meta-learning.  
- Empirical performance: Demonstrates competitive results on Mini-ImageNet, particularly strong in 5-shot classification, outperforming strong baselines.  
- Rigor: Provides detailed model architecture, training procedure, and evaluation protocol ensuring reproducibility and comparability.  
- Interpretability: Offers visualizations that help interpret the learned optimization strategy.  
- Suggestion — statistical reporting: Need clearer reporting of confidence intervals and statistical significance for the metrics.  
- Suggestion — ablations: Include ablation studies to separate contributions of learned initialization versus learned update rules.  
- Suggestion — scalability: Address scalability to larger datasets and more complex learners for practical applicability.  
- Suggestion — broader baselines: Compare against other recent meta-learning methods (e.g., MAML) for a more comprehensive evaluation.  
- Question — hyperparameter sensitivity: How sensitive is performance to hyperparameters like number of updates and LSTM architecture?  
- Question — extension to larger tasks: Can the method handle tasks with many more classes/examples, and what modifications are needed?  
- Question — computational cost: How does training time and memory usage compare to other meta-learning methods?
