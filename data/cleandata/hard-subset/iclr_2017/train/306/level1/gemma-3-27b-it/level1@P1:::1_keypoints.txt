- Proposes a meta-learning approach that learns an LSTM-based optimization algorithm for few-shot learning rather than directly learning classifiers.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novelty: Learning the optimization process with a meta-learner is a fresh and valuable contribution.  
- Motivation: Clearly motivates problems of fixed update budgets and poor initialization in few-shot settings.  
- Technical soundness: LSTM formulation for modeling optimization updates is reasonable, with suitable parameter sharing and preprocessing.  
- Empirical validation: Competitive results on Mini-ImageNet and useful ablations/visualizations support the approach.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written with clear notation and explanations.  
- Dataset details missing: Need precise Mini-ImageNet split and class selection details for reproducibility.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Lacks thorough search-space description and sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters.  
- Computational cost: No discussion of training time or resource requirements compared to other methods.  
- Error analysis: Missing detailed analysis of failure cases or class-specific weaknesses.  
- Comparison to other meta-learning: Should more explicitly compare and relate to methods like MAML and other recent approaches.  
- Q1: Request empirical evidence about ignoring dependencies of losses on meta-learner parameters and whether incorporating them helps.  
- Q2: Ask to quantify variability in meta-learner gate values (e.g., standard deviations) and implications for generalization.  
- Q3: Question sensitivity to the number of updates used in meta-training/meta-testing and whether an optimal update count exists.  
- Q4: Ask whether regularization (dropout, weight decay) could reduce overfitting in fine-tuning baselines and yield fairer comparisons.
