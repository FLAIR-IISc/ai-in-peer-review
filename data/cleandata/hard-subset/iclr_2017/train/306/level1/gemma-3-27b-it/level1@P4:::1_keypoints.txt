- Paper proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner to learn optimization and initialization for few-shot classification, evaluated on Mini-ImageNet.  
- Recommendation: Reject, with strong encouragement to revise and resubmit.  
- Main rejection reason: unclear comparison with strong meta-learning baselines, hindering assessment of novelty/effectiveness.  
- Baselines used (nearest neighbor, fine-tuning, Matching Networks) are weak or insufficiently detailed, so improvements are not convincing.  
- Experimental setup lacks implementation and hyperparameter details for fair evaluation and reproducibility.  
- Key design choices (2-layer LSTM, LSTM size, preprocessing, initialization biases) lack justification and systematic analysis.  
- No ablation studies to quantify the contribution of individual components (initialization, architecture, preprocessing).  
- Visualization is limited: gate visualizations are interesting but deeper analysis (gradient norms, parameter updates) is needed to explain learned optimization.  
- Dataset details (exact Mini-ImageNet class splits for meta-train/val/test) are not fully specified.  
- The simplifying gradient independence assumption is noted but not discussed for its potential impact on results.  
- Requested comparisons: include MAML, Reptile, and other state-of-the-art meta-learning baselines.  
- Requested analyses: sensitivity to LSTM architecture, rationale for preprocessing/initialization choices, impact of second-order gradients, and full hyperparameter listings (learning rates, optimizers, batch sizes, iterations).
