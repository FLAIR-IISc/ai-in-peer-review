- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimizer tailored for few-shot learning, jointly learning initialization and update rules.
- Motivates method by arguing standard gradient-based optimization requires many updates and suffers from poor random initialization in few-shot settings.
- Key contribution: simultaneously learns an optimal classifier initialization and a task-specific parameter update rule that works within a fixed number of updates, with a theoretical link to LSTM cell-state updates.
- Recommends acceptance.
- Main reasons for acceptance: well-defined, significant problem and competitive empirical performance against strong baselines and matching networks, especially for 5-shot learning.
- Experimental validation: thorough experiments on Mini-ImageNet with careful meta-learning protocol and correct handling of batch normalization during meta-training and meta-testing.
- Notes proper matching of training and testing conditions, addressing a common oversight in related work.
- Visualization of learned input and forget gates offers insight into how the meta-learner adapts to different tasks.
- Suggested improvements: add discussion of limitations, generalization to different network architectures beyond the simple CNN used, and scalability to larger datasets or more complex tasks.
- Questions for authors: how the meta-learner performs on datasets with many more classes (e.g., 100+ classes) and what the computational complexity is compared to standard meta-learning methods.
