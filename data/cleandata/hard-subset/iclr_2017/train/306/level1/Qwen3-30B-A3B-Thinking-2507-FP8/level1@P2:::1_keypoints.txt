- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns initialization and update rules to optimize classifiers for few-shot learning.
- Decision: Reject due to insufficient differentiation from prior work and weak empirical validation.
- Main novelty unclear relative to Andrychowicz et al. (2016); paper acknowledges but doesn’t clearly explain differences or advantages.
- Empirical evaluation is limited: only two baselines plus Matching Networks on Mini-ImageNet, missing other meta-learning methods (e.g., MAML).
- Reported improvements are marginal (overlapping confidence intervals on 1-shot) and not convincingly better on 5-shot.
- No ablation studies to attribute gains to learned initialization versus learned update rules.
- Gradient independence assumption (sec 3.3.1) lacks justification and may affect validity for few-shot learning.
- Dataset description is insufficient: custom Mini-ImageNet version lacks details on class selection and differences from the original.
- Architecture choice (two-layer LSTM with a modified second layer) is not justified versus a single-layer LSTM.
- Sensitivity to the number of updates (different models trained for 1-shot vs 5-shot) is not analyzed or justified.
- Recommend clearly articulating novelty vs. prior LSTM-optimizer work and adding a comparative table of related methods.
- Recommend expanding experiments to include more meta-learning baselines (e.g., MAML) and performing ablation studies.
- Recommend strengthening theoretical justification for key assumptions and clarifying dataset preparation details.
- Questions for authors: clarify differences from Andrychowicz et al., justify two-layer LSTM design, explain sensitivity and choice of update counts, and compare/relate to MAML.
