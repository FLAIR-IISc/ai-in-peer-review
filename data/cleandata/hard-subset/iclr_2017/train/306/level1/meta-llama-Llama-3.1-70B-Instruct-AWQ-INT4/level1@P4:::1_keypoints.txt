- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns the optimization algorithm for a learner network in few-shot settings, capturing short-term and long-term knowledge.  
- Demonstrates competitiveness with deep metric-learning techniques for few-shot learning.  
- Well-defined problem and clear motivation.  
- Model is well-motivated and grounded in meta-learning and optimization literature.  
- Provides a clear, concise introduction on few-shot learning and limitations of traditional gradient methods.  
- Thorough evaluation with comparisons to state-of-the-art; results show competitive performance.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of the learned optimization strategy (e.g., input and forget gate behaviors).  
- Needs discussion of limitations, such as effects of using a fixed number of meta-learner updates for tasks with varying example counts.  
- Requires more detailed comparisons to other meta-learning methods with different architectures or optimization techniques.  
- Question: request insights into how input and forget gates adapt across tasks.  
- Question: ask how the model could be extended to handle tasks with varying numbers of examples.  
- Question: request more detailed comparisons to other meta-learning architectures and optimizers.  
- Additional: paper is well-written but contains minor typos and formatting issues.  
- Additional: figures and tables are clear, though some captions need improvement.  
- Additional: references are comprehensive but some citations could be clarified.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
