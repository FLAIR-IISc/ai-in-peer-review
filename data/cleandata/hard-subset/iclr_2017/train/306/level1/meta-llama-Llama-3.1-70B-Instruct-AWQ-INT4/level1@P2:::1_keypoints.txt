- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimization algorithm for few-shot classification.  
- Meta-learner captures short-term within-task and long-term across-task knowledge.  
- Claims competitiveness with deep metric-learning methods and outperformance of natural baselines.  
- Decision: accept.  
- Reason 1: Novel, well-motivated LSTM meta-learner addressing limits of traditional gradient-based optimizers.  
- Reason 2: Strong empirical evidence, evaluated thoroughly on Mini-ImageNet with SOTA comparisons.  
- Paper is well-written with a thorough related-work section and clear exposition.  
- Experimental evaluation is rigorous; using a simple CNN learner isolates meta-learner performance.  
- Results are impressive: outperform baselines and competitive with state-of-the-art; optimization visualizations are insightful.  
- Suggest analysis of how meta-learner parameters are learned and interpretation of gate values (input/forget).  
- Recommend discussing computational complexity and scalability to larger datasets.  
- Suggest exploring application to other few-shot tasks (regression, reinforcement learning).  
- Ask for details on meta-learner parameter initialization and update procedure during training.  
- Ask how the method handles poorly-initialized learner parameters requiring more complex optimization.  
- Ask whether other meta-learning approaches (memory-augmented networks, gradient-based hyperparameter optimization) were explored and how they compare.
