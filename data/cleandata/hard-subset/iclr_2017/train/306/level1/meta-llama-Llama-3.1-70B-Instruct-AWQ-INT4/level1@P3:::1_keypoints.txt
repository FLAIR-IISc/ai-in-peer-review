- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimizer to train a learner classifier for few-shot tasks, capturing both short-term task knowledge and long-term cross-task knowledge.  
- Meta-learner also learns a task-common initialization and is trained to quickly converge learners to good solutions.  
- Demonstrates competitiveness with deep metric-learning methods (e.g., Matching Networks) on few-shot benchmarks.  
- Decision: accept — problem is well-defined, solution is novel and well-motivated.  
- Decision justification: thorough evaluation and comparisons with state-of-the-art; results are competitive or better than baselines.  
- Paper strengths: clear writing, well-explained method, and novel use of LSTM to learn optimization dynamics.
