**Summary of the Paper's Contribution**

This paper proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner model to address the challenge of few-shot learning, where a classifier needs to generalize quickly from a small number of examples. The meta-learner learns to optimize another learner neural network classifier by capturing both short-term knowledge within a task and long-term knowledge common among all tasks. The model is trained to converge a learner classifier to a good solution quickly on each task and also learns a task-common initialization for the learner classifier. The authors demonstrate that their approach is competitive with state-of-the-art deep metric-learning techniques for few-shot learning.

**Decision: Accept**

**Reasons for Acceptance**

2. The approach is well-motivated and grounded in the literature, building upon previous work on meta-learning and few-shot learning.
