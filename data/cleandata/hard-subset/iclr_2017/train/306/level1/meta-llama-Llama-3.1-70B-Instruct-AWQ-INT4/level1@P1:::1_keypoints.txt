- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimization update rule to train few-shot classifiers.  
- Claims the meta-learner is competitive with deep metric-learning techniques.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection due to major clarity and analysis issues.  
- Explanation of the meta-learner’s architecture and operation is unclear and hard to understand.  
- Unclear how the meta-learner ensures rapid convergence of the learner classifier on each task.  
- Experimental results are presented but lack thorough analysis and task-wise breakdown.  
- Insufficient comparison with state-of-the-art few-shot methods.  
- Missing experimental details (hyperparameters, training procedure, number of iterations, learning rates).  
- Reviewer asks for detailed answers on model workings, convergence mechanism, comparative analysis, and experimental settings.  
- Paper could be strong if revised to improve clarity, provide deeper analysis, and include full experimental details.
