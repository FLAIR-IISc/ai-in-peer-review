- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns optimization updates and a general initialization for few-shot neural network classification.
- Meta-learner updates classifier parameters using gradient and loss information to enable rapid convergence.
- Demonstrates competitiveness with state-of-the-art deep metric-learning approaches on few-shot tasks.
- Decision: accept — approach is well-motivated and experimentally supported.
- Strength: clear, well-structured introduction and thorough related-work review.
- Strength: the LSTM-based meta-learner model is well-described.
- Strength: experimental results are convincing and include a visualization of the learned optimization strategy.
- Suggestion: provide more details on hyperparameter selection for the meta-learner (e.g., layers, learning rate).
- Suggestion: explore other optimization algorithms (e.g., Adam, RMSProp) within the meta-learner.
- Suggestion: analyze the learned initialization and update rules to better understand meta-learner behavior.
- Questions for authors: clarify hyperparameter selection, report insights into gate values and their temporal behavior, and evaluate performance on tasks with more classes/examples or plans for scaling.
