- Reviewer raised score to 8 after authors clarified the meta-learning task.
- Paper formulates few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem where each example is a sequence of training batches followed by a test batch.
- An LSTM meta-learner is used, with second-layer memory cells modeling updated parameters of a base learner; update rules are related to gradient descent.
- Simplifying assumptions include sharing weights across second-layer cells (analogous to a shared learning rate).
- Method is competitive with—and outperforms—Matching Networks on 1‑ and 5‑shot Mini‑ImageNet.
- Base learner (ConvNet classifier) differs from nearest‑neighbor/embedding approaches, showing a distinct route to state-of-the-art performance.
- Insight about the relationship between LSTM memory cells and gradient descent updates appears novel and interesting.
- Paper provides practical training/design insights for LSTM meta-learners (initialization, weight sharing, normalization/rescaling).
- Writing is often opaque; task description is abstract and would benefit from a concrete example and diagram to improve accessibility.
- Details of the N‑class few‑shot setup (which classes are seen during meta‑training and softmax output size) are unclear and need clarification.
- Figure 2 plots are uninformative and the discussion around them lacks insight.
- Overall recommendation: interesting, convincing results and likely accept, but presentation must be improved for clarity.
