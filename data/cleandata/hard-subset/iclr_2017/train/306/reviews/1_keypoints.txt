- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework to learn an optimizer for another neural network.  
- Paper is well written and presentation is clear.  
- Core idea: draw a parallel between Robbins–Monro updates and LSTM updates to satisfy quick adaptation vs. slower transferable learning.  
- Reuses useful tricks from prior work (Andrychowicz et al. 2016) like parameter sharing and normalization, and introduces well-motivated novel design choices (specific batch-norm implementation).  
- Experiments are convincing; overall a strong paper.  
- Concern 1: Potential redundancy of feeding loss, gradient, and parameters to the meta-learner—ablation studies are needed to test simpler inputs.  
- Concern 2: Could other architectural components (neurons, unit types, etc.) be learned similarly—authors’ opinion/insight requested.  
- Concern 3: Related work is shallow; should cite earlier meta-learning efforts (e.g., Samy Bengio PhD thesis 1989, Bengio et al. 1994 on genetic programming, Schmidhuber’s work).  
- Overall recommendation: relevant to a wide ICLR audience.
