- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that treats optimization as a learnable process, with cell state encoding classifier parameters and gates producing parameter updates from gradients, losses, and past gate values.
- Jointly learns a task-agnostic network initialization and a task-adaptive update rule tuned for a fixed small number of steps to suit few-shot regimes.
- Scales to deep nets via parameter sharing across coordinates, gradient/loss preprocessing, and careful batch-normalization handling to avoid cross-episode leakage.
- Shows competitive results on Mini-ImageNet 5-way 1-shot and 5-shot, outperforming sensible baselines and matching/reaching comparable performance to Matching Networks.
- Provides practical training insights (gate initialization, per-episode BN stats, fixed-update training) that are broadly useful.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: Original, well-motivated meta-learning formulation that learns both initialization and update rule with a clear mapping from SGD to gated updates.
- Key reason: Competitive empirical performance on a challenging benchmark plus actionable training recommendations.
- Addresses an important problem: standard optimizers and random init need many steps; learned optimization is a compelling direction for few-shot deep learning.
- Well-situated in literature: builds on learning-to-optimize work and complements metric-based few-shot methods, noting relations to GRUs and prior “learning to learn” work.
- Empirical rigor: sensible episode construction, fixed-update training, and per-episode BN increase ecological validity, though more ablations are needed to isolate contributions.
- Notes limitation: gradient-independence assumption follows prior work but should be discussed regarding potential biases or limitations.
- Suggestion: Clarify recurrent analogy and experimentally justify LSTM vs GRU (e.g., include a GRU baseline and expand the qualitative discussion).
- Suggestion: Improve clarity of meta-training/validation/test class splits and episode construction, e.g., with a compact schematic.
- Ablation suggestions: quantify gate roles by fixing ft=1, making it a scalar per-layer, removing learned initialization c0, and varying unrolled steps T at test vs training.
- Figure suggestion: Replace/augment Figure 2 with an episode/BN pipeline and gate-behavior visualizations tied to optimization progress.
- BN suggestion: Report performance using running stats from the support set vs batch stats on the query set, confirm no cross-episode leakage, and include a short ablation.
- Robustness suggestion: Add results on another few-shot benchmark or show cross-architecture generalization to support broader applicability.
- Reproducibility request: Release exact Mini-ImageNet class splits, episode generation scripts, and all hyperparameters.
- Minor fix: Typo in Section 3.2 (“it” → “its”).
- Question: Did you try a GRU-based meta-learner and how do performance, stability, and parameter count compare?
- Question: What are the relative gains from learned initialization c0, learned it, and learned ft—please provide ablation numbers.
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the number of unrolled updates at test time, and does training over a distribution of T help robustness?
- Question: Did you try second-derivative-aware training on smaller models to assess effects of the gradient-independence assumption?
- Question: Have you explored coarser parameter sharing for gates (e.g., per-layer) and its impact on variance and sample efficiency?
- Question: Confirm BN protocol: are query/test stats computed without mixing episodes, and what happens if BN is frozen at meta-test time?
- Question: What are training and inference costs relative to standard fine-tuning and Matching Networks, and are there memory-saving tricks for longer unrolls?
- Question: How well does a meta-learner trained on 5-way transfer to 20-way tasks, and does a 1-shot-trained meta-learner degrade gracefully on 5-shot?
