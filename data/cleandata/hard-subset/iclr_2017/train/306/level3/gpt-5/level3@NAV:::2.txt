1. Summary of contributions
The paper proposes a novel meta-learning approach that interprets SGD parameter updates as a gated recurrent update with trainable gates, learning both an optimizer (via per-coordinate gated updates it and ft) and an initialization for the base learner. The method is conceptually original and important for transfer learning/few-shot learning: it explicitly optimizes for rapid adaptation under a fixed budget of updates and handles batch norm carefully in the episodic setting. The paper is clearly structured overall, and the model is feasible to implement with parameter sharing. Empirically, it attains competitive results on mini-ImageNet with proper comparisons to strong baselines and Matching Networks, and it includes practical guidance (e.g., gate bias initialization, BN protocol).

2. Decision
Weak accept. Key reasons: (i) the idea is original, timely, and significant for transfer/meta-learning; (ii) the empirical results are competitive and the approach is practical. Some analysis and clarity gaps are fixable.

3. Supporting arguments
- What problem is tackled? Learning an optimizer and initialization that enable few-shot generalization with a small, fixed number of updates.
- Is the approach well motivated and placed in the literature? Yes. It builds on meta-learning and learned optimizers (e.g., Andrychowicz et al.) and is well contrasted with metric-learning. One caveat: the proposed update is closer in spirit to a GRU-like gated update than a canonical LSTM cell (the paper partially acknowledges this).
- Do results support the claims? Largely yes: competitive accuracy with appropriate baselines supports the central claims. However, the experimental evaluation would be stronger with ablations isolating the roles of it and ft, the learned initialization, and the number of updates T.

4. Additional feedback (to help improve the paper)
- Clarify and visualize the meta-train/val/test class splits and per-episode sampling; the current text is hard to follow and risks confusion about leakage. A small schematic would help more than the current Figure 2, whose added value is limited.
- Provide ablations: (a) ft fixed to 1 (pure additive update), (b) fixed learning rate vs learned it, (c) learned init only vs learned gates only, (d) tie gates to sum to 1 (GRU-like) vs untied, (e) sensitivity to T and gate bias initialization.
- Report parameter counts for meta-learner vs learner and time/memory overhead.
- Detail the BN protocol precisely to rule out cross-episode leakage.
- Fix minor typo in Section 3.2 (“it” → “its”).
- Releasing the evaluation code would be a major plus for reproducibility.

Questions for the authors
- How much of the gain comes from the learned initialization versus the learned gates? Please quantify with ablations.
- How sensitive is performance to T and to the gate bias initialization heuristics?
- Could you report results when enforcing a GRU-like constraint (gates sum to 1)?
- Can you provide a precise, visual description of the class splits and episode construction to verify no leakage?
- What is the meta-learner’s parameter count and per-episode compute overhead?
- Will you release the evaluation code and data splits?
