- Proposes meta-learning that models SGD updates as gated recurrent computation: learner params = LSTM cell state; meta-learner learns input/forget gates and initialization.  
- Uses gradient and loss as gate inputs, parameter sharing across coordinates, preprocessing and BN handling to make the method practical.  
- Trains by optimizing learner test loss after a fixed number of updates to match test-time conditions.  
- Achieves competitive results on Mini-ImageNet 5-way 1-shot/5-shot, outperforming strong baselines and matching/surpassing Matching Networks on the authors’ split.  
- Decision: Accept — novel, influential framing of meta-learning as a learned gated optimizer with useful practical insights.  
- Problem addressed: few-shot learning via jointly learned initialization and update rule for rapid adaptation in few steps.  
- Motivation/positioning: well-placed in literature, builds on learned optimizers and meta-learning and aligns objective with task (optimize after T updates).  
- Empirical support: results largely back claims with proper baselines and CIs, but some analyses and experiment descriptions are incomplete.  
- Pros: original SGD-as-gated-RNN idea, potential impact on optimizer/transfer design, competitive empirical performance, and practical recommendations (gate biasing, BN, preprocessing, matching update counts).  
- Cons: update is conceptually more GRU-like than full LSTM (no output gate), class-split/episode generation is confusing, and key ablations (gate roles, T, hyperparams) are lacking.  
- Figure/text issue: Fig.2 offers limited added insight and could be replaced by clearer per-episode dataflow schematic.  
- Clarify gated-update analogy and justify separate it and ft versus tied or ft=1 variants.  
- Provide a compact visual schematic of meta-train/val/test class disjointness and episode sampling for reproducibility.  
- Recommended ablations: ft fixed to 1, tied gates (ft=1−it), loss/gradient-only inputs, varying T at train/test, preprocessing parameter p, and inclusion/exclusion of loss input.  
- BN ablation: compare per-episode running stats vs global stats and quantify leakage/performance impact.  
- Visualization request: show distributions of it and ft per layer and over time alongside loss trajectories to link dynamics to outcomes.  
- Broaden evaluation: add Omniglot or tieredImageNet, other N-way settings, and stronger fine-tuning baselines (e.g., weight decay, cosine classifier).  
- Complexity analysis: report memory/time overhead vs standard SGD and discuss scalability to deeper learners.  
- Minor fix: correct typo in Sec.3.2 (“it” → “its”).  
- Questions for authors: did you test ft=1 or tied gates; which gate inputs matter; how does performance change when T′≠T at test; confirm BN running stats come only from support sets; are meta splits strictly disjoint and will you release split lists; will you release code, splits, and seeds for reproducibility?
