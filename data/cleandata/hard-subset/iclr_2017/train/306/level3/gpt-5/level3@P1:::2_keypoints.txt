- Proposes an LSTM-style meta-learner that stores learner parameters in cell state and uses per-parameter input/forget gates conditioned on gradients, losses, parameters, and previous gates to form a learned optimizer for few-shot with few fixed updates.  
- Trains the meta-learner end-to-end to minimize test loss after T inner-loop updates, jointly learning a task-common initialization c0 and a task-adaptive update rule.  
- Uses practical engineering choices (per-coordinate sharing, loss/gradient preprocessing, careful BN to avoid inter-episode leakage, small-gate init, gradient-independence) to make the approach feasible.  
- Empirical results on mini-ImageNet 5-way 1-shot and 5-shot are competitive with Matching Networks and substantially better than nearest-neighbor and simple fine-tuning baselines.  
- Provides qualitative analyses of learned gates and actionable guidance for stable training.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reasons for acceptance: original and well-motivated formulation that learns both initialization and update rule matched to few-shot evaluation; solid empirical results and practical insights.  
- Problem/motivation: Few-shot regimes stress SGD due to limited steps and random init; optimizing for a fixed small number of updates is well-motivated.  
- Novelty/significance: Casting parameter updates as gated recurrent transitions with per-parameter gates conditioned on gradients/losses is elegant and important for transfer/few-shot learning.  
- Methodological soundness: Objective matches test-time episode construction and fixed update count; BN handling is thoughtful; gradient-independence is a reasonable engineering simplification.  
- Empirical support: Results meaningfully compare to strong baselines despite dataset/split differences; meta-learned initialization and updates reduce fine-tuning overfitting.  
- Clarify gating analogy: The update is closer to GRU-style untied gates; discuss explicitly and ablate GRU-tying or ft fixed to 1 to isolate forgetting’s role.  
- Episode construction clarity: Meta-train/val/test splits and episode sampling are hard to follow—add a schematic and publish exact class splits for reproducibility.  
- Ablation (gate roles): Quantify contributions of ft and it (e.g., fix ft=1, constrain/tie gates, remove dependence on Lt) and report correlations between gate values, gradient norms, and loss.  
- Ablation (init vs update): Disentangle learned c0 vs learned optimizer by comparing learned c0 with standard optimizer, random c0 with learned optimizer, and both learned.  
- Ablation (steps): Test robustness when training for T but evaluating at different T′ (fewer/more steps) and report performance vs steps.  
- Ablation (preprocessing): Show sensitivity to preprocessing parameter p and effect of disabling preprocessing.  
- Ablation (BN): Demonstrate that the proposed BN protocol is necessary and does not leak information across episodes.  
- Figure 2: Replace with more informative plots (accuracy vs inner-loop step, gate value distributions, t-SNE of parameter trajectories).  
- Fairness of baselines: The fine-tune baseline may be under-regularized—consider adding L2-SP or layer-wise regularization to strengthen comparisons.  
- Minor: Typo in Section 3.2 (“it” → “its”); public release of code and mini-ImageNet splits would improve reproducibility.
