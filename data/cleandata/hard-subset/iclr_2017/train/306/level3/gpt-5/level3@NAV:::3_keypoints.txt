- Formulates few-shot learning as sequential meta-learning: episodes are sequences of training batches followed by a held-out test batch.  
- Proposes an LSTM meta-learner that learns to update a ConvNet classifier.  
- Second-layer LSTM cell state explicitly represents the base-learner’s parameters.  
- LSTM input/forget gates implement learned update rules closely related to gradient descent.  
- Introduces practical design choices: per-coordinate parameter sharing, gradient-independence (no second derivatives), gate bias initialization, input normalization/rescaling.  
- Proposes a batch-norm protocol that avoids leakage across episodes.  
- Empirically competitive on Mini-ImageNet 1-shot and 5-shot; clearly outperforms Matching Networks in 5-shot.  
- The LSTM–gradient-descent connection is novel and thought-provoking.  
- Reviewer decision: Accept. Key reasons: novel formulation linking LSTM dynamics to optimization and strong Mini-ImageNet results (esp. 5-shot).  
- Problem addressed: rapid adaptation from very few labels by learning initialization and update rules in an episodic framework.  
- Well situated relative to Matching Networks, memory-augmented models, and learned optimizers, differing by using LSTM cell state as learner parameters.  
- Experiments and ablations (gate visualizations, training heuristics) are sound and provide insight despite simplifying assumptions.  
- Suggestion: Clarify the N-way episodic setup (which classes in meta-train vs meta-test, softmax head instantiation, last-layer reinitialization).  
- Suggestion: Provide a concrete end-to-end episode example or schematic showing train batches and final test evaluation.
