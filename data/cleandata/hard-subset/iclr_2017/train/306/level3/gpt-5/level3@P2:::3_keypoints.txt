- Paper frames few-shot learning as sequential meta-learning where an LSTM meta-learner parameterizes an optimizer that updates a ConvNet base learner across a small number of within-episode steps.  
- LSTM cell state encodes base-learner parameters and learned gates mimic/extend gradient descent to learn both a good initialization and a data-driven update rule for few-step adaptation.  
- Gradient/loss preprocessing and parameter sharing across coordinates keep the meta-learner compact; practical guidance on gate init, normalization, and batch-norm handling is provided.  
- Empirical results on Mini-ImageNet (5-way, 1- and 5-shot) are competitive with or better than Matching Networks and outperform nearest-neighbor and fine-tuning baselines.  
- Novel conceptual contribution: mapping LSTM updates to gradient descent and using LSTM-as-optimizer for few-shot adaptation.  
- Decision: Accept — motivated by a novel, well-placed formulation and strong empirical performance with a distinct base-learner paradigm.  
- Problem tackled: rapid adaptation in N-way k-shot classification optimized across episodes for performance after a fixed small number of updates.  
- Positioning: clearly situated between learned optimizers and metric-learning approaches, explicitly optimizing for rapid few-shot adaptation and learned initialization.  
- Results largely support claims but comparisons use an internal Mini-ImageNet split complicating direct numerical comparison to prior work.  
- Missing ablations: no study of the “gradient independence” simplification, impact of parameter sharing, or sensitivity to number of unrolled steps T.  
- Clarity/presentation needs improvement; the episodic setup and Figure 2 are sometimes opaque and would benefit from a concrete walkthrough and clearer diagrams.  
- Suggestion: add a concrete episode illustration showing final-layer sizing, c0 initialization, T unrolled updates on support set, and query-set meta-loss.  
- Clarify whether the final linear softmax head is re-initialized per episode, whether c0 is class-agnostic, and how varying N is handled.  
- Experimental improvements requested: report 95% CIs, number of test episodes, backbone details, and ablations for T, forget gate, preprocessing, parameter sharing, and gradient-independence.  
- Measure computational cost: memory for unrolling, wall-clock time vs Matching Nets, and scaling with T/model size.  
- Sensitivity analyses requested: gate bias init, preprocessing parameter p, batch-norm statistics strategy, and generalization across N, k, and T at test time.  
- Visualization/analysis requests: replace Figure 2 with learning curves, gate statistics per layer, histograms of learned learning rates, and qualitative episodes showing optimizer behavior.  
- Strengthen baselines: stronger fine-tuning baselines with regularization, linear probes on frozen features, and better disentanglement of learned initialization benefits.  
- Specific questions for authors: details on final-layer inclusion in c0 and handling of variable N; parameter-sharing granularity and alternatives; experiments with second-order gradients; robustness to T mismatch; batch-norm per-episode stats; memory/time costs; cross-shot/dataset transfer; and common failure modes.  
- Overall: interesting, timely contribution with convincing results and a clear conceptual advance that would benefit from improved exposition and targeted ablations.
