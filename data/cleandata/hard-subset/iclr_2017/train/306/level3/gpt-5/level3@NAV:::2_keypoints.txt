- Proposes a novel meta-learning method that models SGD updates as gated recurrent updates with trainable per-coordinate gates, learning both an optimizer and a base-learner initialization.  
- Conceptually original and important for transfer/few-shot learning; explicitly optimizes for rapid adaptation under a fixed update budget and handles batch-norm in episodic setting.  
- Paper is clearly structured and the model is implementable with parameter sharing; includes practical guidance (gate bias init, BN protocol).  
- Empirically achieves competitive results on mini-ImageNet with comparisons to strong baselines and Matching Networks.  
- Decision: Weak accept — idea is original/timely and empirically competitive, though analysis/clarity gaps remain.  
- Core problem tackled: learning an optimizer plus initialization to enable few-shot generalization with few updates.  
- Well motivated and situated in literature (builds on learned optimizers, contrasted with metric-learning); update is GRU-like more than an LSTM.  
- Results largely support claims but would be stronger with ablations isolating roles of gates, initialization, and update count T.  
- Clarify and better visualize meta-train/val/test class splits and per-episode sampling to avoid confusion/leakage.  
- Provide ablations: (a) fix ft=1, (b) fixed learning rate vs learned it, (c) learned init only vs learned gates only, (d) tie gates to sum to 1 vs untied, (e) sensitivity to T and gate-bias initialization.  
- Report parameter counts for meta-learner vs learner and time/memory overhead per episode.  
- Precisely detail BN protocol to rule out cross-episode leakage.  
- Fix minor typo in Section 3.2 (“it” → “its”).  
- Releasing evaluation code and data splits would greatly aid reproducibility.  
- Questions for authors: quantify how much gain comes from learned initialization vs learned gates with ablations.  
- Questions: how sensitive is performance to T and gate-bias initialization heuristics?  
- Questions: report results under a GRU-like constraint (gates summing to 1).  
- Questions: provide a precise visual description of class splits and episode construction to verify no leakage.  
- Questions: what is the meta-learner’s parameter count and per-episode compute overhead, and will you release the evaluation code and data splits?
