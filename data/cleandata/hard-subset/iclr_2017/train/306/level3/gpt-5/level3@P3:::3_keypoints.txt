- Frames few-shot learning as sequential meta-learning optimizing test performance after a fixed, small number of inner-loop updates.
- Uses a 2-layer LSTM as meta-learner where the second-layer cell state directly models base-learner parameters and updates mirror gradient descent (c_t = f_t ⊙ c_{t-1} + i_t ⊙ (−∇_θ L_t)).
- Practical design choices enable scaling to convnets: per-parameter shared LSTM weights, gradient/loss preprocessing, careful gate initialization, and a BN protocol to avoid leakage.
- Empirical results on Mini-ImageNet 5-way: outperforms Matching Networks on 5-shot and is comparable on 1-shot; demonstrates a ConvNet base learner updated by a learned optimizer.
- Novel and interesting insight relating LSTM memory updates to gradient-based updates, with concrete training guidance for meta-optimizers.
- Reviewer raised score to 8 after rebuttal clarified meta-learning task and episode construction.
- Recommendation: Accept — strong, convincing results and a conceptually distinct approach from metric-learning.
- Problem is timely: learns initialization and update rule to achieve high accuracy after few inner-loop updates.
- Method is well positioned relative to Matching Nets and prior learning-to-learn-by-gradient-descent work; thoughtfully adapts to few-shot with an episode-level objective.
- Claims are supported by Mini-ImageNet gains, gate visualizations, and ablations; authors disclose their split and reimplementation.
- Suggest adding a concrete end-to-end episode example and schematic for clarity (e.g., 5-way 1-shot).
- Request clarification of N-way setup: which classes are meta-train vs meta-test, softmax head parametrization, label remapping, and whether final layer is part of learned initialization/updates.
- Figure 2 should be replaced with a cleaner block diagram and per-parameter update zoom; gate visualizations should show distributions and step evolution with interpretive commentary.
- Quantify impact of the gradient-independence assumption by comparing inclusion of second-order terms on a small scale.
- Discuss scalability and memory: per-parameter LSTM state cost, runtime overhead, wall-clock/memory vs baselines, and possible per-layer/per-channel parameterizations.
- Address generality: training a single meta-learner for variable k, N, and step budgets and performance when test-step counts differ from training.
- Note baseline fairness: Matching Nets used a larger backbone; include apples-to-apples backbone comparisons if feasible.
- Question: Is the classifier’s final layer reinitialized per episode, included in c0, and updated by the LSTM; how are label permutations handled?
- Request release of Mini-ImageNet class split and episode sampling code for reproducibility.
- BN question: how sensitive is performance to collecting BN stats on the support set vs alternatives like per-episode BN or group/layer norm?
- Ask whether ignoring second derivatives causes degradation and if including them improves stability or accuracy in any experiments.
- Ablation requests: effects of removing parameter sharing, disabling loss preprocessing, or changing gate bias initialization with quantitative deltas.
- Ask how accuracy changes if a meta-learner trained for T steps is run for T±k steps and whether learned gates show interpretable schedules.
- Request evaluations beyond Mini-ImageNet (e.g., Omniglot) to test robustness across domains.
- Overall assessment: interesting, well-motivated contribution with strong empirical results; accept conditional on added clarity, ablations, and requested clarifications.
