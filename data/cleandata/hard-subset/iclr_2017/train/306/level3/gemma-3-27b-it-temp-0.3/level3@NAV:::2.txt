## Review of "LSTM-based Meta-Learner for Few-Shot Learning"

This paper proposes a novel meta-learning approach that frames SGD updates as a gated recurrent model, specifically an LSTM, with trainable parameters. The core idea is to learn an optimization algorithm tailored for the few-shot learning regime, including a beneficial initialization for the learner network. This is a promising and original contribution to the field of transfer learning, addressing a critical challenge in scenarios with limited data. The paper is generally well-structured and clearly presents the motivation and proposed methodology. The competitive results achieved compared to state-of-the-art methods, along with the practical recommendations offered, further strengthen the work. 

I recommend **Accept** with minor revisions. The novelty of the approach and the solid experimental validation make this a valuable contribution to the conference. However, the analogy to LSTMs feels somewhat forced, as the authors acknowledge, and is closer to a GRU implementation. This doesn’t invalidate the approach, but a more nuanced discussion of this point would be beneficial. 

The description of how the meta-sets were created for training, validation, and testing is difficult to follow. A visualization illustrating this data separation would significantly improve clarity. Furthermore, while the results are competitive, the experimental evaluation feels incomplete. A more thorough analysis of the impact of key parameters, particularly *it* and *ft*, is needed. How do different values affect convergence speed and final performance? The effect of the preprocessing step also warrants further investigation. Finally, Figure 2, depicting the computational graph, provides limited insight and could be removed or significantly revised to highlight the key information flow.

**Additional Feedback (for improvement, not decision-making):**

*   The minor typo in Section 3.2 ("it" → "its") should be corrected.
*   Releasing the evaluation code would be a major asset to the community and greatly enhance the reproducibility of the results.
*   Could the meta-learner be applied to different learner architectures beyond the simple CNN used in the experiments?
*   How sensitive is the performance to the choice of ADAM hyperparameters for the meta-learner itself?
*   What is the computational cost of the meta-learning phase compared to traditional pre-training or fine-tuning approaches?
