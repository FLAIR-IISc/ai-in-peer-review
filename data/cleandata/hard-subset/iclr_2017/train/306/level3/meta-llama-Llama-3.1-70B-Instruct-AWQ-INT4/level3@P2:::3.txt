**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

The paper proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner model to tackle the few-shot learning problem, where a classifier must quickly generalize after seeing very few examples from each class. The meta-learner learns the exact optimization algorithm used to train another learner neural network classifier in the few-shot regime. The model is competitive with deep metric-learning techniques for few-shot learning and provides a novel approach to this problem.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

Based on the paper's contributions and the reviewer's feedback, I decide to **accept** this paper. The key reasons for this decision are:

1. The paper tackles a well-defined problem in few-shot learning and provides a novel approach using an LSTM-based meta-learner model.
2. The results are competitive with state-of-the-art methods, including Matching Networks, and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper provides a clear and well-motivated approach to few-shot learning, which is a challenging problem in machine learning. The use of an LSTM-based meta-learner model is novel and interesting, and the results demonstrate its effectiveness. The paper also provides practical training and design insights for LSTM meta-learners, which is valuable for the research community.

However, there are some areas that need improvement. The writing is often opaque, and the task description is abstract and would benefit from a concrete example and diagram to improve accessibility. Additionally, the details of the N-class few-shot setup are unclear and need clarification.

**Additional Feedback**

To improve the paper, I suggest the following:

1. Provide a concrete example and diagram to illustrate the meta-learning task and the LSTM-based meta-learner model.
2. Clarify the details of the N-class few-shot setup, including which classes are seen during meta-training and the softmax output size.
3. Improve the writing to make it more accessible to a broader audience.
4. Provide more insight into the relationship between LSTM memory cells and gradient descent updates, which appears to be a novel contribution of the paper.

**Questions for the Authors**

To clarify my understanding of the paper and provide additional evidence for my assessment, I would like the authors to answer the following questions:

1. Can you provide a concrete example of the meta-learning task and how the LSTM-based meta-learner model is applied to it?
2. How do you select the classes for the N-class few-shot setup during meta-training, and what is the softmax output size?
3. Can you provide more insight into the relationship between LSTM memory cells and gradient descent updates, and how this contributes to the effectiveness of the proposed approach?
