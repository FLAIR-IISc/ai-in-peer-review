- Proposes sequential meta-learning for few-shot classification using an LSTM-based meta-learner that optimizes a base learner’s parameters.  
- Meta-learner is trained to learn both a good parameter initialization and an update mechanism for adapting to new tasks from small training sets.  
- Approach is well-motivated and situated within existing meta-learning and few-shot literature.  
- Authors explain limitations of traditional gradient-based optimization in few-shot settings and how their method addresses them.  
- Experiments show the method is competitive and outperforms state-of-the-art (including Matching Networks) on Mini-ImageNet.  
- Paper provides insights suggesting the meta-learner learns distinct optimization strategies that adapt to different training sets.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance conditional on revisions to improve presentation and clarity.  
- Writing is often opaque; the task description should include a concrete example and a diagram for accessibility.  
- Details of the N-class few-shot setup are unclear (which classes are seen during meta-training and what is the softmax output size) and need clarification.  
- Figure 2 plots are uninformative and the surrounding discussion lacks insight; replace with more informative visualizations.  
- Requested revisions: add a concrete example and diagram, clarify N-class setup, simplify and clarify writing, and improve Figure 2.  
- Reviewer questions for authors: how the meta-learner is initialized and how weights are shared across second-layer cells; how variable numbers of classes are handled; more insight into the learned optimization strategy; and plans to scale to many classes or limited labeled data.
