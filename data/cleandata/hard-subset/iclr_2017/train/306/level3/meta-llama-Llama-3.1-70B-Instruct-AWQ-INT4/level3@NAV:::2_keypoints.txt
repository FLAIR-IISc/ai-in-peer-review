- Proposes a novel meta-learning approach interpreting SGD updates as a gated recurrent model and introduces an LSTM-based meta-learner optimizer.  
- Meta-learner captures short-term task-specific knowledge and long-term knowledge across tasks, and learns a task-common initialization for the learner.  
- Demonstrates competitiveness with deep metric-learning methods for few-shot learning.  
- Decision: Accept — idea is original, important, well-motivated, and well-placed in literature.  
- Decision: Accept — approach is feasible and shows competitive results with state-of-the-art few-shot methods.  
- Paper is clearly and well-structured, with detailed model, training, and experimental descriptions.  
- Related work is thoroughly analyzed and the approach is well positioned within meta-learning/few-shot literature.  
- Experiments show improvement over natural baselines and competitiveness with SOTA; includes visualization of the learned optimization strategy.  
- Clarify the LSTM analogy: model behavior is closer to GRUs than LSTMs.  
- Description of data separation in meta-sets is hard to follow and would benefit from a visualization.  
- Experimental evaluation is partial; needs more analysis of parameter effects, especially it and ft.  
- Figure 2 offers limited value and could be removed or replaced with a more informative figure.  
- Minor typo in Section 3.2 ("it" → "its").  
- Question: Provide details on meta-learner LSTM initialization and its impact on performance.  
- Question: How will the approach scale to more challenging scenarios (many classes, very limited data)?  
- Question: Provide deeper analysis of the learned optimization strategy, including input and forget gate behaviors.  
- Question: How will you address overfitting in the meta-learner when the number of updates is small?
