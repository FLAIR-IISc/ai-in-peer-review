- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimization algorithm for fast adaptation in few-shot learning.  
- Frames few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem where the meta-learner updates a ConvNet base learner via sequences of training batches and a test batch.  
- Meta-learner learns a task-common initialization for the base learner to capture shared knowledge across tasks.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions, citing novelty and competitive results.  
- Paper is clear, well-motivated, and provides practical training/design insights for LSTM meta-learners.  
- Task description is abstract and needs a concrete example or diagram for clarity.  
- Details of the N-class few-shot setup are unclear (which classes seen during meta-training and softmax output size).  
- Figure plots are uninformative and the accompanying discussion lacks sufficient insight.  
- Reviewer requests more insight into the optimization strategy learned by the meta-learner and how it adapts across tasks/datasets.
