- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimizer for another neural network, drawing a parallel between Robbins–Monro updates and LSTM updates.  
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation.  
- Core idea: meta-learner LSTM captures short-term task-specific adaptation and long-term transferable knowledge across tasks.  
- Formulation enables learning a task-common initialization for the learner classifier to encode shared fundamentals.  
- Reuses existing techniques (parameter sharing, normalization) and introduces novel design choices, including a specific batch-norm implementation.  
- Experiments are convincing: outperforms natural baselines and is competitive with state-of-the-art metric learning methods for few-shot learning.  
- Reviewer accepts paper because it is well-motivated, well-situated in related work, and supported by strong experimental results.  
- Concern: potential redundancy of feeding loss, gradient, and parameters to the meta-learner — ablation studies on simpler inputs are needed.  
- Concern: unclear whether other architectural components (neurons, unit types) could be learned similarly; authors’ insight would be valuable.  
- Related-work gap: should cite earlier meta-learning efforts (e.g., Samy Bengio PhD 1989, Bengio et al. 1994, Schmidhuber’s meta-learning work).  
- Suggestion: provide more implementation details of the meta-learner LSTM (architecture and hyperparameters).  
- Suggestion: include visualizations of the learned optimization strategy (gate values, learned learning rates).  
- Suggestion: discuss potential applications beyond few-shot learning (e.g., other ML areas or robotics).  
- Suggestion: consider adding more baselines or comparison methods to strengthen empirical evaluation.  
- Questions for authors: provide LSTM implementation/hyperparameters, applicability to other domains, more optimization visualizations, and consideration of additional baselines.
