**Summary of the Paper's Contribution**

The authors of this paper propose a novel approach to few-shot learning using an LSTM-based meta-learner model. The model is designed to learn an optimization algorithm that can quickly adapt to new tasks with few examples. The authors formulate few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem, where the meta-learner learns to update the parameters of a base learner (a ConvNet classifier) using a sequence of training batches followed by a test batch. The LSTM meta-learner is trained to learn a task-common initialization for the base learner, which captures fundamental knowledge shared among all tasks.
