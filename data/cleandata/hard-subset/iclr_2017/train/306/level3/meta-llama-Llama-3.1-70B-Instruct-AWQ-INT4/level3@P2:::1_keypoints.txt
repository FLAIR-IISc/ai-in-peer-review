- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning optimizer drawing a parallel between Robbins–Monro updates and LSTM updates to balance quick adaptation and slower transferable learning.
- Paper is well-written and clear, reusing tricks from prior work (parameter sharing, normalization) and introducing a well-motivated batch-norm design.
- Tackles few-shot learning by training a meta-learner to learn the optimizer used to train a classifier in the few-shot regime.
- Method is well-motivated, well-placed in the literature, and clearly explained.
- Experiments are convincing: approach outperforms natural baselines and is competitive with metric-learning state-of-the-art; results are clearly presented.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Concern: potential redundancy in feeding loss, gradient, and parameters to the meta-learner — requests ablation studies with simpler inputs.
- Concern: needs discussion on whether other architectural components (neurons, unit types, etc.) could be learned similarly and the authors’ opinion on this.
- Concern: related work omits earlier meta-learning efforts (e.g., Bengio PhD 1989, Bengio et al. 1994, Schmidhuber); related work should be expanded.
- Requested revisions: provide ablation studies on inputs, discuss learnability of other architectural components, and expand the related work to include earlier meta-learning literature.
