- Proposes a novel meta-learning approach interpreting SGD updates as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters — original and important for transfer learning.
- Paper is well-structured with a clear explanation and relation to previous meta-learning and few-shot work.
- Writing is generally clear but some sections require more detail and clarity.
- Method advantage: feasible meta-learner that can optimize neural nets for few-shot tasks.
- Experimental evaluation is competitive with state-of-the-art and includes useful practical recommendations.
- Analogy to LSTM is inaccurate; model is closer to GRU than LSTM.
- Description of data separation in meta-sets is confusing and would benefit from visualization.
- Experimental analysis is incomplete: effects of hyperparameters (especially it and ft) need deeper study.
- Figure 2 offers limited value and should be improved or removed.
- Minor typo in Section 3.2 ("it" → "its").
- Releasing evaluation code would be a significant positive for reproducibility.
- Decision: Accept.
- Primary reasons: methodological novelty/importance and competitive experimental results.
- Questions for authors: clarify relation to prior work; justify hyperparameter choices and report sensitivity; analyze effects of it and ft; state plans/timeline for releasing code.
