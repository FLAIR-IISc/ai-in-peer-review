**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

This paper proposes a novel meta-learning approach that interprets stochastic gradient descent (SGD) updates as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters. The authors introduce an LSTM-based meta-learner model that learns to optimize a learner neural network classifier in the few-shot learning regime. The model is designed to capture both short-term knowledge within a task and long-term knowledge common among all tasks. The paper presents a clear and well-structured approach, with competitive results compared to state-of-the-art methods in few-shot learning.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

Based on the paper's contributions and limitations, I decide to **accept** this paper. The two key reasons for this choice are:

1. **Originality and importance of the idea**: The paper proposes a novel and interesting meta-learning approach that has the potential to advance the field of transfer learning.
2. **Competitive results and proper comparison to state-of-the-art**: The paper presents competitive results compared to state-of-the-art methods in few-shot learning, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Supporting Arguments for the Reasons**

The paper's idea of interpreting SGD updates as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters is original and important for transfer learning research. The authors provide a clear and well-structured approach, with a well-defined problem statement, a novel solution, and a thorough evaluation. The paper's results are competitive with state-of-the-art methods in few-shot learning, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Additional Feedback for Improvement**

While the paper is well-structured, some parts lack clarity, such as the description of data separation in meta sets. Adding visualizations to illustrate this process would be helpful. Additionally, the experimental evaluation is only partially satisfying, and the effects of parameters (especially it and ft) need more analysis. Figure 2 has limited value and could be improved or removed. A minor typo in Section 3.2 ("it" → "its") should be corrected.

**Questions for the Authors**

To clarify my understanding of the paper and provide additional evidence for my assessment, I would like the authors to answer the following questions:

1. Can you provide more details on the intuition behind the design of the input gate (it) and the forget gate (ft) in the LSTM-based meta-learner model?
2. How did you select the hyperparameters for the meta-learner model, and what is the sensitivity of the results to these hyperparameters?
3. Can you provide more analysis on the effects of the number of updates (T) on the performance of the meta-learner model?
4. How do you plan to extend this work to more challenging scenarios, such as few-shot learning with many classes or few-shot learning with limited labeled data?

**Releasing the Evaluation Code**

Releasing the evaluation code would be a major plus, allowing other researchers to reproduce and build upon the results presented in this paper.
