- Proposal: Novel meta-learning approach treating SGD updates as a gated recurrent model with an LSTM-based meta-learner for few-shot classification, capturing short- and long-term task knowledge with competitive results.  
- Decision: Accept — due to originality/importance and competitive results with proper state-of-the-art comparisons.  
- Supporting argument: Clear problem statement, novel solution, and thorough evaluation showing effectiveness.  
- Clarify data handling: Description of data separation in meta-sets lacks clarity and would benefit from visualizations.  
- Parameter analysis: Experimental evaluation needs more analysis of parameter effects, especially the input gate (it) and forget gate (ft).  
- Figure and typo: Figure 2 has limited value and could be improved or removed; correct typo in Section 3.2 ("it" → "its").  
- Q1: Explain intuition behind design of the input gate (it) and forget gate (ft).  
- Q2: Describe hyperparameter selection for the meta-learner and sensitivity of results to those choices.  
- Q3: Provide analysis of how the number of updates (T) affects meta-learner performance.  
- Q4: How will the approach be extended to harder scenarios (many classes or very limited labeled data)?  
- Reproducibility: Releasing evaluation code would be a major plus for reproducibility and follow-up work.
