- Proposes a novel meta-learning method that models SGD updates as a gated recurrent model and uses an LSTM-based meta-learner to learn optimization for few-shot classifiers.  
- Key idea: exploit similarity between standard optimizers and LSTM update rules and train the meta-learner to produce fast-converging learners via an objective tied to generalization after limited updates.  
- Paper is well-motivated and situates itself clearly in the meta-learning and few-shot literature.  
- Experimental results show the meta-learner is competitive with deep metric-learning baselines.  
- Provides practical recommendations (e.g., use batch normalization, initialize meta-learner LSTM with small random weights).  
- Limitation: the analogy presented is closer to GRUs than LSTMs, which may constrain the approach’s applicability.  
- Description of data separation in meta-sets is unclear and would benefit from visualization.  
- Experimental evaluation is incomplete—effects of hyperparameters (especially it and ft) require deeper analysis.  
- Figure 2 adds little value and should be removed or replaced with a more informative figure.  
- Requests clarification on scaling to harder scenarios (many classes, large-scale datasets).  
- Requests insight into the optimization strategy learned by the meta-learner and how it adapts across tasks/datasets.  
- Asks authors to address the GRU-vs-LSTM analogy limitation and explain mitigation strategies.  
- Recommendation: accept, but authors should address limitations and provide more insight into the learned optimizer.  
- Minor typo: Section 3.2 "it" → "its".  
- Releasing evaluation code would be a major plus for reproducibility.
