- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns the optimization algorithm to train a neural classifier for few-shot learning.  
- Meta-learner approach is novel and competitive with deep metric-learning methods (e.g., Matching Networks).  
- Decision: accept the paper.  
- Key reasons for acceptance: addresses a well-defined few-shot learning problem with a novel LSTM meta-learner.  
- Key reasons for acceptance: experimental results are competitive with state-of-the-art and demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Paper offers practical training and design insights for LSTM meta-learners useful to the community.  
- Writing is often opaque and the task description is abstract, reducing accessibility.  
- Details of the N-class few-shot experimental setup are unclear and need clarification.  
- Suggest adding a concrete example and a diagram to illustrate the meta-learning task and model.  
- Suggest clarifying which classes are seen during meta-training and the softmax output size for N-way tasks.  
- Suggest improving exposition, especially on the relationship between LSTM memory cells and gradient-descent-like updates.  
- Reviewer questions: provide a concrete meta-learning example and application of the LSTM meta-learner.  
- Reviewer questions: explain class selection during N-class meta-training and specify softmax output dimensionality.  
- Reviewer questions: provide more insight/evidence on how LSTM memory cells relate to gradient descent and why that aids performance.
