- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimizer to tackle few-shot learning tasks.  
- Draws an analogy between Robbins-Monro updates and LSTM updates to enable fast adaptation and slower transferable learning.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear presentation.  
- Core idea is innovative and builds on prior work (Andrychowicz et al. 2016) with novel design choices like a specific batch normalization implementation.  
- Experiments on Mini-ImageNet are convincing, outperforming several baselines and competing with state-of-the-art metric-learning methods.  
- Decision to accept is based on novelty/relevance to ICLR and strong experimental evidence.  
- Supporting argument: the approach effectively leverages LSTM dynamics for meta-learning and introduces meaningful architectural choices.  
- Concern 1: Potential redundancy in feeding loss, gradient, and parameters to the meta-learner—ablation studies needed to test simpler inputs.  
- Concern 2: Unclear if other architectural components (neurons, unit types) could be learned similarly—authors should provide insights.  
- Concern 3: Related work is shallow; earlier meta-learning efforts (e.g., Bengio 1989/1994, Schmidhuber) should be cited and discussed.  
- Question 1: Please clarify the importance of each input component (loss, gradient, parameters) to the meta-learner and how inputs were chosen.  
- Question 2: Have you considered applying this method to other architectural components, and what are the expected benefits or challenges?  
- Question 3: Provide details on the specific batch normalization implementation and how its design was determined for this framework.
