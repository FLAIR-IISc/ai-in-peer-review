This paper proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework to learn an optimizer for another neural network, which is a novel and well-motivated approach to address the few-shot learning problem. The authors draw a parallel between Robbins-Monro updates and LSTM updates to satisfy quick adaptation vs. slower transferable learning, and reuse useful tricks from prior work, such as parameter sharing and normalization, while introducing well-motivated novel design choices.

The paper is well-written, and the presentation is clear. The authors provide convincing experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach, which is competitive with deep metric-learning techniques for few-shot learning.
