- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimizer for another network to address few-shot learning.
- Draws a parallel between Robbins–Monro updates and LSTM updates to balance quick adaptation and slower transferable learning.
- Reuses established tricks (parameter sharing, normalization) while introducing well-motivated novel design choices.
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation.
- Provides convincing experiments showing competitive performance with deep metric-learning methods for few-shot learning.
- Tackles the few-shot learning problem where classifiers must generalize from very few examples per class.
- Approach is well-motivated and well-placed within meta-learning and few-shot literature.
- Experimental results support the paper’s claims.
- Recommendation: accept.
- Suggestion: add ablation studies testing simpler inputs to the meta-learner (e.g., only loss or gradient).
- Suggestion: explore whether other architectural components (neurons/unit types) can be learned similarly.
- Suggestion: improve related work by citing earlier meta-learning efforts (e.g., Samy Bengio’s PhD thesis, Schmidhuber).
- Suggestion: provide more insight into the learned optimization strategy by visualizing LSTM gate values and weights.
- Overall: strong paper with significant contribution; minor revisions would make it excellent.
