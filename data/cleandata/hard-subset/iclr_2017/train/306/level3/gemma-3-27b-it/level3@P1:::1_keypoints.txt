- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning optimizer using the analogy between gradient updates and LSTM cell state updates.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation, methodology, and results.  
- Builds on prior techniques (parameter sharing, normalization) and introduces a specific batch-normalization implementation.  
- Experiments on Mini-ImageNet are convincing and competitive with strong baselines; visualizations give qualitative insight.  
- Recommendation: reject but strongly encouraged to resubmit after addressing concerns.  
- Primary concern: possible redundancy of inputs to the meta-learner (loss, gradient, parameters) — unclear if all are necessary.  
- Requires ablation studies removing each input component to quantify their contributions and potentially simplify the model.  
- Scope limitation: paper only targets learning an optimizer for a classifier; generalization to learning architectures or other learner components is unexplored.  
- Authors should discuss feasibility, limitations, and challenges of extending the framework to discover network architectures or neuron types.  
- Related work section is superficial and should cite foundational meta-learning literature (e.g., Bengio’s early work, Schmidhuber).  
- Missing analyses: detailed computational cost comparison and hyperparameter sensitivity studies.  
- Reviewer questions requested: ablation results for inputs, views on extending to architectures, computational cost details, and specifics/choices in the batch-normalization strategy.
