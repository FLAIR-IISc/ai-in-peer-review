- Decision: Accept — novel, feasible meta-learning approach with competitive results and practical recommendations.
- Core idea: Interpret SGD updates as a gated recurrent model (LSTM-like) with trainable parameters to learn optimization rules for few-shot learning.
- Novelty/impact: Original contribution to transfer learning/meta-learning by learning the optimizer rather than hand-designing updates.
- Results: Achieves performance comparable to state-of-the-art metric learning (Matching Networks) on Mini-ImageNet.
- Insights: Gate-value visualizations give useful behavioral insights and the paper recommends training for a specific number of updates during meta-training.
- Reproducibility: Authors’ stated intention to release evaluation code would enhance reproducibility and impact.
- GRU vs LSTM analogy: Clarify why the approach is functionally closer to GRUs than LSTMs, emphasizing gate similarities.
- Data separation: Provide a simple diagram to illustrate meta-set creation and train/test splits for clarity.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Include sensitivity analysis for key gates ('it' and 'ft') to show their impact on performance.
- Figure 2: Simplify the computational graph or improve the caption, as the current figure is overly complex and adds limited insight.
- Typo: Fix minor typo in Section 3.2 ("it" → "its").
- Ablation studies: Add ablations (e.g., remove parameter sharing or preprocessing) to quantify each component’s contribution.
- Computational cost: Discuss the computational overhead of the meta-learning approach relative to other few-shot methods.
- Q1 for authors: How was the number of hidden units for parameter sharing chosen, and were alternative sharing strategies tested?
- Q2 for authors: Can you elaborate on the different strategies learned for 1-shot vs 5-shot settings based on gate visualizations?
- Q3 for authors: Is the fine-tuned baseline’s overfitting (worse than nearest-neighbor) consistent across datasets/initializations, and which regularization methods were tried?
