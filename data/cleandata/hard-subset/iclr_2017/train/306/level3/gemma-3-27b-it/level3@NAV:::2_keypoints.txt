- Proposes learning SGD-like updates via an LSTM-style meta-learner for few-shot learning, an original and impactful idea.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly motivates limitations of standard gradient-based methods in low-data regimes.  
- Empirical results are competitive with state-of-the-art (e.g., Matching Networks) and provide useful training/parameter recommendations.  
- Releasing evaluation code would be a valuable contribution.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; novelty and results outweigh limitations.  
- Primary strength: interpreting optimization as a learnable recurrent process opens new research avenues.  
- Clarify LSTM vs. GRU similarity and explain why the difference doesn’t undermine the approach.  
- Data separation in meta-sets is hard to follow; add a diagram illustrating class splits across train/val/test.  
- Experimental analysis feels incomplete—need deeper study of key parameters (it and ft) on convergence and performance, including optimal ranges.  
- Figure 2 offers little novel insight and should be revised or removed.  
- Minor typo: Section 3.2 "it" should be "its".  
- Questions for authors: justify choice of LSTM architecture and design decisions; detail strategies to prevent meta-learner overfitting; provide an intuitive explanation of the it and ft gate behaviors shown in Figure 3.
