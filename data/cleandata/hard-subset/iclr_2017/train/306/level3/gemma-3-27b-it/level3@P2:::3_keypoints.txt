- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner framing few-shot learning as sequential episodes of training batches followed by a test batch.  
- Uses LSTM memory cells to model base-learner (CNN) parameter updates inspired by gradient descent.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance and outperforms Matching Networks on Mini-ImageNet, especially in 5-shot.  
- Employs a convolutional base-learner rather than embedding-based approaches, showing an alternative viable route.  
- Connects LSTM cell dynamics to optimization, offering a novel perspective on learned update rules.  
- Provides practical training tips (initialization, weight sharing, normalization/rescaling) useful for reproducibility.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Presentation is unclear/abstract; needs a concrete episode example and clearer diagrams illustrating data flow through the meta-learner.  
- Details of the N-class few-shot setup are ambiguous (which classes are used in meta-train/meta-val/meta-test and how softmax output size is handled).  
- Figure 2/gate visualizations are uninformative and lack concrete interpretation or insight.  
- More analysis needed on whether gate variability implies task-adaptive learning rates or other behaviours.  
- Writing is often opaque and needs clearer, more intuitive explanations.  
- Ask authors for a concrete episode example and accompanying diagram of meta-training.  
- Ask authors to clarify class partitioning and how the base learner handles novel-class softmax sizes during testing.  
- Ask authors to elaborate on insights from LSTM gate visualizations and why the fine-tuned baseline underperforms nearest-neighbor, and whether learned initialization addresses overfitting.
