- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning optimizer for few-shot learning, motivated by an analogy between Robbins–Monro updates and LSTM updates.  
- Leverages parameter sharing and introduces a specific batch-norm implementation, reflecting thoughtful design choices.  
- Experimental results are convincing and competitive with deep metric-learning methods; overall recommendation is acceptance.  
- Requests strong ablation studies to test potential redundancy among meta-learner inputs (loss, gradient, parameters), e.g., loss+gradient or gradient-only.  
- Suggests expanding discussion on applying the meta-learning approach to other architectural components (neuron types, layer connectivity), including feasibility and challenges.  
- Criticizes the related work as shallow and missing seminal meta-learning references (e.g., Bengio 1989 PhD, Bengio et al. 1994, Schmidhuber’s 1990s work).  
- Asks authors to elaborate on batch-norm design choices and alternatives considered.  
- Requests information on computational costs of training the meta-learner versus training the learner directly.  
- Asks whether different input combinations to the meta-learner might be beneficial for different tasks or architectures.
