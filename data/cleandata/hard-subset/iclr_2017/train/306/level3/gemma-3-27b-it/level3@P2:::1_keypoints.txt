- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Main strengths: novel and effective LSTM-based meta-learning framing that treats optimizer as a recurrent process.  
- Core idea: draw analogy between gradient descent and LSTM updates to learn initializations and update rules for few-shot learners.  
- Builds on prior work (e.g., Andrychowicz et al. 2016) while adding novel implementation choices.  
- Important design choices: parameter sharing and a specific batch normalization strategy crucial for performance.  
- Presentation: well-written, clearly motivated, and theoretically grounded via the LSTM–gradient connection.  
- Experiments: convincing results on Mini-ImageNet, competitive with strong baselines and Matching Networks.  
- Ablations/analysis: some ablation studies and visualizations provided, offering insight into meta-learner behavior but not extensive.  
- Concern: potential redundancy of feeding loss, gradient, and parameters to the meta-learner—need systematic ablation to justify all three inputs.  
- Concern: paper focuses only on learning an optimizer; unclear whether meta-learning could extend to neuron types or connectivity and what challenges that would entail.  
- Concern: related work section is shallow and omits seminal/meta-learning classics (e.g., Bengio 1989/1994, Schmidhuber early 90s) that should be cited and discussed.  
- Question 1: Provide ablation results showing impact of removing loss, gradient, and parameters individually and in combination.  
- Question 2: Can the approach be extended to learn other architectural components (neuron types, connectivity)? What challenges and solutions do the authors foresee?  
- Question 3: Elaborate on computational cost of training the meta-learner versus training the learner directly and how complexity scales with learner size.  
- Question 4: Comment on the gradient independence assumption’s impact on accuracy/stability and possible mitigation strategies.  
- Question 5: Clarify batch normalization specifics—sensitive hyperparameters and how optimal values were determined.
