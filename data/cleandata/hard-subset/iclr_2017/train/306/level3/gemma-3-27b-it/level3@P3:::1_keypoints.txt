- Proposes a novel LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimizer for few-shot learning.
- Insightful parallel drawn between Robbins–Monro updates and LSTM updates.
- Reuses effective techniques from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) such as parameter sharing and normalization.
- Introduces a well-justified batch-normalization implementation tailored for meta-learning.
- Presentation is exceptionally clear and well-written.
- Experimental results show competitive performance with deep metric-learning methods on Mini-ImageNet.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Primary reasons for acceptance: novelty, potential impact, and convincing empirical efficacy in the few-shot regime.
- Comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., Matching Networks) are thorough; ablation studies are present but limited; qualitative visualizations add insight.
- Concern about input redundancy: feeding loss, gradient, and parameters may be redundant and costly—request ablation to test removing inputs.
- Question whether architectural components (e.g., number of layers) could also be learned via meta-learning.
- Related work is incomplete—missing Bengio (1989 PhD), Bengio et al. (1990,1994), and Schmidhuber’s 1992/1993 meta-learning work; recommend expanding citations.
- Request clarification on the batch-norm running-average update weighting scheme used during meta-testing.
- Request a detailed breakdown of computational cost for training the meta-learner versus standard network training.
