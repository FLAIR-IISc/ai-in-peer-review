- Proposes a novel LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimization algorithm for few-shot base learners, framing learning as a sequential meta-learning problem.  
- Insightful connection between LSTM memory cells and gradient descent updates, offering a fresh perspective on meta-learning.  
- Empirical results are competitive, outperforming Matching Networks on 1- and 5-shot Mini-ImageNet, demonstrating practical potential.  
- Practical training insights (initialization, weight sharing, normalization) are valuable contributions.  
- Reviewer recommends accepting the paper based on idea strength, novelty, and promising results.  
- Writing and presentation need substantial improvement for clarity.  
- Task description is too abstract; a concrete example of training/test batch sequences is necessary.  
- N-class setup details (class selection per task and softmax size) are unclear and must be elaborated.  
- Figure 2 is uninformative and should be revised or removed.  
- Discussion of visualized gate values in Figure 3 lacks sufficient insight and needs deeper analysis.  
- Reviewer notes improvement in score after clarification of the meta-learning task, strengthening confidence in the work.  
- Requests from reviewer: clarify relation of weight sharing to a shared learning rate; specify class selection strategy across episodes; report performance sensitivity to LSTM layer/hidden sizes; provide detailed analysis of whether the learned update resembles known optimizers (e.g., Adam, SGD with momentum).
