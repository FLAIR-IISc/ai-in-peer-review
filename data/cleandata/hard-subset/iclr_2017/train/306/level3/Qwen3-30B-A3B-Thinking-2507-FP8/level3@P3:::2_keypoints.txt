- Novel meta-learning approach: interprets SGD updates as a gated recurrent model and proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner for few-shot optimization.  
- Meta-learner jointly learns an optimal initialization and task-specific optimization strategy to converge rapidly with few examples.  
- Well-motivated framing that addresses limitations of standard gradient-based methods in few-shot settings.  
- Empirical results are competitive with state-of-the-art metric learning methods (Matching Networks) on Mini-ImageNet.  
- Significant contribution by treating optimization as a learnable, adaptable process for meta-learning.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Core idea is original and important for transfer/meta-learning research.  
- Experimental results outperform natural baselines and are competitive with SOTA; model structure and implementation recommendations are clearly explained.  
- Authors thoughtfully address practical issues like parameter sharing and batch normalization in the meta-learning context.  
- Clarify the LSTM analogy: model structure appears closer to GRUs (gates don’t sum to one), so state this distinction early.  
- Add a clear visual illustrating meta-training, meta-validation, and meta-testing splits (Section 2) for clarity.  
- Provide deeper parameter-sensitivity analysis, especially for the it and ft gates, to strengthen empirical claims.  
- Replace or improve Figure 2 with a visualization that better illustrates the meta-learning process rather than a standard LSTM diagram.  
- Fix minor typo in Section 3.2 ("it" → "its").  
- Releasing evaluation code would greatly aid reproducibility and community use.  
- Questions for authors: clarify LSTM vs GRU differences; explain training to a fixed number of updates and its necessity; interpret input-gate variability in Figure 3; explain why fine-tuned baseline underperforms nearest-neighbor (possible overfitting); discuss challenges for meta-learners that generalize across varying numbers of examples and classes.
