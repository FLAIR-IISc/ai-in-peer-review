- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns to optimize a classifier for few-shot tasks by framing optimization as a meta-learning problem.
- Key innovation: draws a parallel between Robbins-Monro stochastic approximation updates and LSTM cell state updates.
- Meta-learner jointly learns a task-common initialization and task-specific optimization strategy for the learner.
- Well-motivated by limitations of standard gradient-based methods in few-shot learning.
- Strong empirical results on Mini-ImageNet, outperforming simple baselines and competitive with metric-learning methods.
- Recommendation: accept.
- Contribution: formalizes connection between gradient updates and LSTM cell states, bridging optimization theory and meta-learning.
- Practical contributions: parameter sharing across gradient coordinates and a novel batch normalization strategy for meta-learning.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, with appropriate baselines and convincing improvements.
- Writing is clear and the approach is well-motivated and accessible.
- Suggestion: add ablation studies to test necessity of feeding loss, gradient, and parameters to the meta-learner.
- Suggestion: discuss whether other components (e.g., architecture or activation functions) could be learned via similar meta-learning.
- Suggestion: strengthen related work by citing early meta-learning (Bengio 1989, Bengio et al. 1994, Schmidhuber).
- Suggestion: more explicitly contrast this work with Andrychowicz et al. (2016), who also used LSTMs to learn optimizers.
- Question: how does the learned task-common initialization differ from standard transfer learning/fine-tuning, and what specific advantages does it provide?
- Question: why choose an LSTM-based meta-learner over a GRU given GRU’s fewer parameters, and how does this choice affect performance and complexity?
