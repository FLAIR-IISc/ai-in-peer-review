- Proposal: LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimizer and task-common initialization for few-shot classifiers by relating Robbins–Monro updates to LSTM cell updates.  
- Contribution: Addresses rapid adaptation with limited examples and provides an optimal starting point for training.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance based on motivation, technical soundness, and results.  
- Empirical evidence: Competitive performance vs. strong baselines and SOTA metric learning on Mini-ImageNet.  
- Strength: Clear presentation, effective reuse of prior techniques (parameter sharing, normalization), and a well-motivated novel batch-norm design.  
- Experiments: Thorough, with ablation studies on update counts and visualizations of the meta-learner’s learned behavior.  
- Suggestion 1: Add ablations to test if simpler input representations (omitting loss, gradient, parameters) suffice and whether current inputs are redundant.  
- Suggestion 2: Discuss whether other architectural components (neurons, unit types, etc.) could be learned similarly.  
- Suggestion 3: Expand related work to cite earlier meta-learning efforts (e.g., Bengio 1989 PhD thesis, Bengio et al. 1994, Schmidhuber).  
- Questions for authors: Clarify potential redundancy of input representation, generalizability to learning other architecture components, and why earlier meta-learning works were omitted from related work.
