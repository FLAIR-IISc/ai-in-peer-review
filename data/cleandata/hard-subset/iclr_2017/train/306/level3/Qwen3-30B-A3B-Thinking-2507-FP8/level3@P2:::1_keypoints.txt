- Proposes a novel LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimizer and initialization for few-shot classification, drawing a parallel between gradient updates and LSTM state updates.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on Mini-ImageNet, outperforming simple baselines and competing with metric-learning methods; writing and presentation are clear.  
- Recommendation: accept — idea is novel, well-executed, and empirically validated.  
- Key technical strength: learns task-specific optimization and beneficial initialization, addressing overfitting and few-shot optimization challenges.  
- Solid implementation details (e.g., specialized batch normalization) and thoughtful extension of Andrychowicz et al. (2016).  
- Suggests ablation studies to test whether feeding loss, gradient, and parameters to the meta-learner are all necessary.  
- Recommends expanding related work to cite earlier meta-learning efforts (Bengio 1989, Bengio et al. 1994, Schmidhuber).  
- Encourages a deeper discussion of extending the framework to learn other architectural components (neuron/layer types).  
- Asks how performance compares when meta-learner receives only gradients versus full inputs (loss and parameters).  
- Asks for elaboration on how learned strategies differ between 1-shot and 5-shot settings and why.  
- Asks how Matching Networks could be fine-tuned within this meta-learning framework and the expected impact.
