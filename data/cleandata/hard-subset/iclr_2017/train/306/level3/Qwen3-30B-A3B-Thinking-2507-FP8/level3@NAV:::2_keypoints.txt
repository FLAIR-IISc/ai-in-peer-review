- Proposes a meta-learning method that models SGD updates as a gated recurrent (LSTM-based) model to learn initialization and update rules for few-shot classification.  
- Central idea: use LSTM-like gates to dynamically control learning rates and parameter forgetting, addressing optimizer limitations in small-data regimes.  
- Empirically competitive with state-of-the-art metric-learning methods (e.g., Matching Networks) on Mini-ImageNet, with significant gains in 5-shot settings.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly motivated, and gives practical deployment recommendations.  
- Recommendation: Accept with Major Revisions.  
- Critical issue 1: Model resembles a GRU rather than an LSTM (gates do not sum to one), so the LSTM analogy/misnaming is misleading.  
- Critical issue 2: Insufficient analysis of key hyperparameters (e.g., it and ft); no sensitivity study showing their impact on performance.  
- Supporting concerns: GRU/LSTM confusion undermines technical precision despite strong empirical results.  
- Experimental deficiencies: ambiguous data-split description (needs visualization), Figure 2 of limited value, and missing parameter sensitivity analyses.  
- The gradient independence assumption (Section 3.3.1) is stated but not adequately justified.  
- Requested fixes: clarify GRU-like nature or justify LSTM naming, add ablations/sensitivity studies for it/ft, visualize data splits (e.g., Figure 1), fix typo ("it" → "its"), and release evaluation code for reproducibility.  
- Author questions: Why does the model align more with GRUs than LSTMs and what are the theoretical implications?; provide sensitivity analysis of it/ft across tasks (1-shot vs 5-shot); can you visualize meta-set class distributions to clarify methodology?
