

**Review**

1. The paper proposes an innovative LSTM-based meta-learner for few-shot learning, framing the problem as a sequential optimization task where each example consists of training batches followed by a test batch. The authors model the meta-learner's memory cells to represent the parameters of a base learner, with update rules directly inspired by gradient descent. The approach demonstrates competitive performance against Matching Networks on Mini-ImageNet, with notable improvements in the 5-shot setting, while offering a distinct alternative to metric-learning approaches by using a ConvNet classifier rather than nearest-neighbor methods. The insight connecting LSTM memory cells to gradient descent updates represents a novel theoretical contribution.

2. Accept. The paper makes a significant contribution to few-shot learning with convincing empirical results, and the core technical insight is compelling and well-supported.

3. The problem tackled is clearly defined: learning an optimization algorithm specifically tailored for few-shot learning scenarios. The approach is well-motivated by the limitations of standard gradient-based methods in few-shot settings and properly situated within the meta-learning literature, with thoughtful connections to prior work on learning optimization algorithms. The empirical results are scientifically rigorous, showing consistent improvements over baselines on Mini-ImageNet and demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. The authors provide thorough ablation studies and practical design insights for LSTM meta-learners.

4. While the technical contribution is strong, the paper's presentation requires substantial improvement. The task description is abstract and would benefit from a concrete example and diagram to clarify the sequential meta-learning formulation. The details of the N-class few-shot setup (which classes are seen during meta-training and softmax output size) need clarification. Figure 2 is uninformative, and the discussion around it lacks insight. Additionally, the writing is often opaque and would be more accessible with better organization and explanatory examples. The authors should provide a clear explanation of how the meta-learner handles class distinctions during training and testing, and include a diagram illustrating the sequential meta-learning process. The relationship between the LSTM meta-learner's parameters and the base learner's architecture also requires further elaboration.
