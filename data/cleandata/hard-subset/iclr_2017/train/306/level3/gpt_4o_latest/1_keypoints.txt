- Paper proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns both initialization and parameter update rules for few-shot learning.  
- Key insight: parallels between Robbins-Monro update rule and LSTM updates to justify the meta-learner design.  
- Framework addresses fast task-specific learning and slower extraction of cross-task generalizable knowledge.  
- Uses parameter sharing, normalization techniques, and a novel batch-normalization strategy to improve robustness and efficiency.  
- Empirical results on Mini-ImageNet are competitive with state-of-the-art metric-learning methods, notably strong in 5-shot classification.  
- Decision: Accept — paper is strong, well-motivated, and relevant to the ICLR audience.  
- Novelty: introduces a unique combination of LSTM-based optimization and few-shot meta-learning.  
- Empirical strength: rigorous experiments and clear comparisons demonstrate performance gains over baselines.  
- Supporting argument: theoretical grounding connecting LSTM updates to optimization rules is compelling.  
- Design choices (parameter sharing and BN) are well-explained and justified.  
- Suggestion: perform ablation studies to test redundancy among inputs (loss, gradient, parameters) to the meta-learner.  
- Suggestion: explore whether other network components (neuron counts, unit types) can be learned via meta-learning.  
- Suggestion: expand Related Work to include foundational meta-learning references (e.g., Bengio’s thesis, Schmidhuber, genetic programming).  
- Questions: assess computational trade-offs of using all three inputs vs simpler combinations and impacts on scalability.  
- Questions: can the framework learn other architectural components (activations, layer configs)?  
- Questions: how sensitive are results to the LSTM choice and have alternatives like GRUs been evaluated?
