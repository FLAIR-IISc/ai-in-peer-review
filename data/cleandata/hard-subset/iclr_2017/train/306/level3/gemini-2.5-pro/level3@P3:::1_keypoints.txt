- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimization algorithm for a CNN learner by mapping gradient updates to LSTM cell-state updates.
- Meta-learner learns both a shared initialization (LSTM initial cell state) and an efficient, task-dependent update rule for rapid few-shot adaptation.
- Method is clearly presented and achieves competitive results on the Mini-ImageNet few-shot benchmark.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Novel formulation framing optimization as sequence modeling; principled link to Robbins–Monro updates and data-driven optimizer discovery.
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical performance and careful design (e.g., parameter sharing and batch-norm handling) competitive with metric-learning baselines.
- Strength: Conceptually clean model and clear writing, facilitating understanding of the approach.
- Strength: Convincing experimental comparisons to fine-tuning, pre-trained nearest-neighbor baselines, and Matching Networks.
- Insight: Visualization of learned gate values indicates dynamic, task-dependent update rules rather than fixed schedules.
- Weakness: Related work section is shallow and omits foundational meta-learning/learning-to-learn literature.
- Weakness: Lacks ablation studies justifying the necessity of the meta-learner’s inputs (loss, gradient, previous parameters).
- Suggestion: Expand related work to cite early learning-to-learn efforts (e.g., Bengio, Schmidhuber).
- Suggestion: Run ablations on meta-learner inputs ([∇L, L, θ, i_{t-1}]) to clarify which inputs drive performance.
- Suggestion: Discuss broader applicability—whether framework could learn other network aspects (activations, connectivity, neuron counts).
- Question: Have you run experiments with simpler meta-learner inputs (e.g., only gradient or gradient+loss)?
- Question: Can this meta-learning framework be extended to learn architectural components (activation functions, connectivity, neuron counts)?
- Question: For batch normalization at meta-test time, are running averages computed per episode from the k-shot training set or carried over from meta-training?
