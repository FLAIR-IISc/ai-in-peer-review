- Novel idea: frame optimization as a learning problem using an LSTM meta-learner to learn an optimizer for a CNN learner.  
- Mechanism: parameterize LSTM gates to control per-parameter learning rates and decay, and learn initial cell state c0 as weight initialization.  
- Training: end-to-end meta-training to minimize learner loss after a fixed number of inner updates.  
- Empirical result: competitive on Mini-ImageNet, outperforming fine-tuning and nearest neighbors and matching/exceeding Matching Networks in 5-shot.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason — novelty/significance: introduces a new, compelling direction for meta-learning by redesigning the learning process itself.  
- Key reason — empirical support: strong experiments on a relevant benchmark with appropriate baselines and reproducibility details (code provided).
