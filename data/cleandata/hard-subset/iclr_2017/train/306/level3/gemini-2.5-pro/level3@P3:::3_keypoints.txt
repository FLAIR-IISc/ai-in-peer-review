- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimization algorithm to update a separate CNN learner for few-shot classification.  
- Frames LSTM cell-state updates as analogous to gradient descent, allowing the meta-learner to learn update rules and parameter initialization.  
- Employs a compact weight-sharing scheme and trains end-to-end on episodic few-shot tasks.  
- Evaluated on Mini-ImageNet, showing competitive and sometimes state-of-the-art performance, notably in 5-shot settings.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novel formulation: an elegant, distinct alternative to metric/embedding-based few-shot approaches.  
- Strong empirical results: outperforms pretrained nearest-neighbor and standard fine-tuning baselines and beats Matching Networks in 5-shot.  
- Demonstrates the value of a learned optimizer given standard fine-tuning struggles in the few-shot regime.  
- Provides practical implementation insights (LSTM initialization, parameter sharing, input preprocessing) useful for practitioners.  
- Presentation needs clarity: meta-learning setup is abstract and would benefit from a concrete episode example and diagram.  
- Visualizations (Figure 3) are uninformative and require deeper analysis connecting gate behaviors to optimization intuition.  
- Writing is sometimes opaque and jargon-heavy; simplify language and add intuition for design choices.  
- Question 1 for authors: clarify the learner’s final-layer architecture during meta-training and how final-layer weights are handled across episodes with different N-way classes.  
- Question 2 for authors: clarify Batch Normalization procedure—are batch statistics computed separately for an episode’s D_test (and how does that affect the meta-training signal)?
