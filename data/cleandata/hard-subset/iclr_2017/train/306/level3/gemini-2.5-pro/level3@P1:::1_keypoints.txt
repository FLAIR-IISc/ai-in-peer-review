- Proposes a meta-learning framework that treats optimization as a learnable model: an LSTM "meta-learner" outputs parameter updates for a separate CNN "learner" in few-shot classification.  
- Draws an analogy between SGD updates and LSTM cell updates, letting the LSTM learn per-parameter update rules (learning rates, forget gates) and the initial cell state as a learned initialization.  
- Demonstrates strong performance on Mini-ImageNet, outperforming sensible baselines and matching or exceeding Matching Networks (notably in 5-shot).  
- Paper is well-written with a clear core idea and a sound experimental setup.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Primary reason 1: Novel, intuitive idea of modeling the optimizer as an RNN offering a fresh meta-learning perspective beyond metric learning.  
- Primary reason 2: Convincing empirical results that validate learning an optimizer as an effective alternative.  
- Strength: Conceptual elegance—replacing fixed gradient updates with a parameterized LSTM yields richer, history-conditioned optimization strategies.  
- Experimental rigor: Thorough comparisons (nearest-neighbor, fine-tuning) and careful implementation details (e.g., BatchNorm handling) that strengthen claims.  
- Suggested improvement: Add an ablation study on meta-learner inputs (gradient, loss, parameters, previous gate) to identify necessary signals and reduce redundancy.  
- Suggested improvement: Broaden related work discussion to more explicitly contrast with earlier meta-learning and learned-update-rule work (e.g., Schmidhuber, Bengio) and clarify local vs global update distinctions.  
- Questions for authors: (1) Have you tested simpler input formulations for the meta-learner (e.g., gradient only), and how did that affect performance and training stability? (2) Could the meta-learning paradigm be extended to learn other learner components (activation functions, unit types, small architectural motifs) for rapid adaptation?
