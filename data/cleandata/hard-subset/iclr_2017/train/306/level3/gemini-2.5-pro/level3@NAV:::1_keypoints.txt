- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimizer for a separate learner network, targeting few-shot learning.
- Core contribution: draws a motivated parallel between gradient-based optimizer updates and LSTM cell-state updates.
- Framing enables learning both a good parameter initialization and a bespoke update rule for rapid adaptation from few examples.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presents the methodology.
- Recommendation: Accept, based on novelty/elegance and strong empirical results.
- Strength: Novel formulation that treats optimization as a learnable sequential process.
- Empirical support: Thorough Mini-ImageNet evaluation showing the meta-learner outperforms baselines and is competitive with metric-learning methods.
- Builds on prior work (Andrychowicz et al., 2016) using parameter sharing and introduces a tailored batch normalization strategy for meta-learning.
- Weakness: Related work discussion is shallow; should acknowledge earlier foundational meta-learning work (Bengio, Schmidhuber).
- Weakness: Needs an ablation study on meta-learner inputs (loss, gradient, parameters) to assess individual contributions; gradient-only input might suffice.
- Questions for authors: (1) Please provide ablation on meta-learner inputs; (2) Can other learner architecture components (e.g., neuron counts or unit types) be meta-learned within this framework?
