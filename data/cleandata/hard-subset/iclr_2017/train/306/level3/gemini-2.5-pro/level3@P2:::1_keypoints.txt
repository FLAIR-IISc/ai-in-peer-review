- Introduces an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimization algorithm for a separate learner network in few-shot classification.  
- Frames learner parameters as the LSTM cell state and gradients as inputs, learning gates as a learned update rule.  
- Meta-learner jointly learns a shared initialization (c0) and a task-adaptive optimizer in an end-to-end differentiable way.  
- Model presentation is clear and the formulation is elegant.  
- Strong empirical validation on Mini-ImageNet: outperforms standard fine-tuning and is competitive with (and in 5-shot superior to) Matching Networks.  
- Employs sensible design choices: parameter sharing across learner parameters and gradient preprocessing (Andrychowicz et al., 2016).  
- Proposes a careful strategy for handling batch-normalization statistics to avoid information leakage between episodes.  
- Gate activation visualizations indicate the meta-learner has learned a non-trivial, adaptive optimization strategy.  
- Suggests an ablation study to quantify the contribution of rich inputs (gradient, loss, previous params, previous gates) to the meta-learner.  
- Recommends deeper discussion of early meta-learning work (1990s) to better contextualize the contribution.  
- Asks why previous parameters are fed explicitly to the meta-learner when they may already be encoded in the LSTM cell state and whether simpler input configurations were tried.  
- Asks whether the meta-learning approach could be extended to learn architectures or other hyperparameters end-to-end and what challenges that would pose.
