- Novel idea: reframes learner optimization as a learned process via an LSTM-like meta-learner that outputs parameter updates for a classifier.  
- Meta-learner objectives: learns a transferable common initialization and a dynamic per-parameter update rule (learning-rate/decay-like) for rapid few-step convergence.  
- Empirical result: outperforms naive fine-tuning and is competitive with state-of-the-art metric-learning (Matching Networks) on Mini-ImageNet.  
- Practical contribution: provides a careful strategy for handling Batch Normalization in meta-learning and will release code.  
- Decision: Accept — main reasons are high novelty (learning the optimizer itself) and strong empirical validation.  
- Strength: modeling updates with a recurrent/gated network captures optimization dynamics; learning c0 and gates yields transferable init and data-dependent update rules.  
- Experimental rigor: authors reimplemented baselines for fair comparison; finding that meta-learners specialized for a specific number of updates (T) work best is noteworthy.  
- Clarify model analogy: refer to the update as a "gated recurrent model" or justify LSTM terminology (update resembles GRU-like behavior).  
- Improve clarity: add a diagram showing class partitioning and episode sampling to make the meta-learning setup easier to follow.  
- More analysis requested: include ablations on the forget gate (e.g., fix f_t=1), sensitivity to T, and deeper analysis of learned i_t across layers/time.  
- Figure suggestion: replace or improve Figure 2 (unrolled computation graph) with a more informative visualization.  
- Minor fix: typo in Sec 3.2 — use "its" instead of "it's".  
- Questions for authors: clarify the forget gate's role (ablation), how T was chosen and sensitivity to it, and whether the approach scales to deeper/residual architectures and the parameter-sharing scheme.
