- Novel meta-learning method frames learner optimization as a trainable LSTM meta-learner, mapping SGD updates to gated recurrent updates.  
- Meta-learner parameterizes a learning-rate gate (it) and forgetting gate (ft) based on gradient, loss, and previous parameters, and learns initial weights c0 for fast adaptation.  
- End-to-end training optimizes learner performance after a fixed number of updates; experiments on Mini-ImageNet are competitive with state-of-the-art.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Key justification: original and significant idea—learning an optimizer and initialization jointly is a principled advance for few-shot learning.  
- Key justification: strong empirical validation with proper baselines and competitive (often superior) results to Matching Networks.  
- Strength: clear mapping from SGD to LSTM makes the concept intuitive and the approach feasible to implement and train.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation on Mini-ImageNet showing significant improvements over simple baselines and competitiveness with contemporary methods.  
- Strength: practical contributions include handling BatchNorm in meta-learning to avoid leakage and useful LSTM gate initialization strategies.  
- Suggestion: clarify analogy to GRUs vs. LSTMs since model uses cell state directly and lacks an output gate.  
- Suggestion: add a diagram to clarify hierarchical data splitting and episode sampling for meta-train/val/test.  
- Suggestion: deepen experimental analysis with quantitative study of learned it/ft behaviors and comparison to standard learning-rate schedules.  
- Suggestion: replace or augment Figure 2 with more useful visuals (e.g., data-splitting diagram or detailed architecture).  
- Minor: typo in Section 3.2 ("its" vs "it"); positive: code release praised for reproducibility.  
- Questions: why choose T=12 for 1-shot and T=5 for 5-shot—was this tuned and how sensitive is performance to T?  
- Questions: have you analyzed dataset properties (e.g., inter-class similarity) that drive different learned update schedules across tasks?
