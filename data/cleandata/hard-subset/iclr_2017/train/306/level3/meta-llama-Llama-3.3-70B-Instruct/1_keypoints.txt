- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimization algorithm for few-shot learning.
- Draws a parallel between the Robbins–Monroe update rule and the LSTM update rule to enable learned update rules.
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation and useful ideas like parameter sharing and normalization.
- Experiments demonstrate effectiveness and competitive results versus state-of-the-art deep metric-learning methods.
- Concern about redundancy: unclear why loss, gradient, and parameters are all required as inputs to the meta-learner.
- Related work section is incomplete and should include older meta-learning work (e.g., Samy Bengio, Schmidhuber).
- Requests more analysis of the learned update rules and the meta-learner’s behavior.
- Recommendation: accept—novel, well-motivated approach with promising impact and convincing experiments.
- Requested revisions: add analysis of update rules, expand related work, and consider alternative/leaner inputs to the meta-learner.
- Question: why are loss, gradient, and parameters all necessary, and could fewer inputs suffice?
- Question: how do the learned update rules compare to traditional optimizers like gradient descent or Adam?
- Question: provide details on hyperparameter selection and how meta-learner hyperparameters were chosen.
