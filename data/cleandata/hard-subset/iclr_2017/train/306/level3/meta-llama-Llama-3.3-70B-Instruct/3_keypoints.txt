- Paper proposes vocabulary selection techniques to improve neural machine translation efficiency.  
- Investigates selection methods: word co-occurrences, bilingual embeddings, word alignments, phrase pairs, and SVMs.  
- Analyzes impact on both decoding and training speed.  
- Reports decoding speed improvements up to 90% without accuracy loss.  
- Reports training speed improvements up to 25% with a bi-directional LSTM encoder.  
- Strength: comprehensive analysis of vocabulary selection techniques.  
- Strength: thorough experiments on two language pairs.  
- Strength: insightful discussion of speed–accuracy trade-offs.  
- Strength: clear and well-structured presentation.  
- Weakness: writing could be clearer.  
- Weakness: plots are not informative enough and need improvement.  
- Weakness: insufficient detailed analysis comparing strengths/weaknesses of each selection method.  
- Weakness: limited context about related work and how this approach builds on prior research.  
- Suggested additions: discuss limitations and future directions.  
- Reviewer decision: accept — approach is well-motivated and claims are supported by experiments.  
- Questions for authors: provide more details on the word-alignment selection method and comparisons, address data requirements/limitations, and give deeper insights on optimizing the speed–accuracy trade-off for different use cases.
