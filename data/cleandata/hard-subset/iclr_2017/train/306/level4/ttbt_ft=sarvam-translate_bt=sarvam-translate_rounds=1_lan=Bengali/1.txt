This work presents an LSTM-based meta-learning framework for learning optimization algorithms of another learning algorithm (here a neural network).

The research paper is well-written globally, and the presentation of the main content is clear. The main point of this paper is: creating parallelism between the Robbins-Monroe update rule and the LSTM update rule, and utilizing it to fulfill two main requirements of few-shot learning (1-rapid acquisition of new knowledge, 2-gradual acquisition of general transferable knowledge) is quite intriguing.

Several techniques, such as parameter sharing and normalization, have been reused from (Andrychowicz et al. 2016), and the new design choices (specific implementations of batch normalization) are well-inspired.

The experiments are convincing. It is a strong paper. My only concerns/questions are as follows:

1.  Could using loss, gradient, and parameters as input to the meta-learner be excessive? Have you done an ablative study to ensure that a simpler combination is not sufficient?

2.  It would be great if other architectural elements of the network (number of neurons, type of units, etc.) could also be learned in a similar way. Do you have any opinions on this?

3.  The related work section (mainly focusing on meta-learning) is somewhat superficial. Meta-learning is a fairly old topic, and similar methods have been tried to solve the same problem without using LSTM:

    - Sammy Bengio PhD thesis (1989) is entirely on this topic;-)
    - Using genetic programming to find a new learning rule for neural networks (S. Bengio, Y. Bengio, and J. Cloutier. 1994)
    - I am sure Schmidt Huber has done something, you find it and update the related work section.

Overall, I like the paper. I believe the discussed content is relevant to a wide audience of ICLR.
