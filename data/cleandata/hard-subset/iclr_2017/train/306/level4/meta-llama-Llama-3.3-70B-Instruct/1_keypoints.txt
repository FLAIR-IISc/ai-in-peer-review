- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimizer for another neural network.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presents its contributions.  
- Key idea: link Robbins–Monro update rule with LSTM updates to enable fast task-specific learning and slower extraction of generalizable knowledge (few-shot learning).  
- Uses parameter sharing and normalization techniques inspired by prior work plus a novel batch-normalization design—these choices are well-justified.  
- Experimental results are convincing, making the paper strong.  
- Question: Is it necessary to feed loss, gradient, and parameters into the meta-learner, or could simpler input combinations suffice; were ablation studies done?  
- Suggestion: Explore learning other architectural components (e.g., neuron counts, unit types) via the same meta-learning approach.  
- Critique: Related work is incomplete—should cite earlier meta-learning efforts (e.g., Samy Bengio 1989, Bengio et al. 1994, Schmidhuber) and expand that section.  
- Overall assessment: Engaging work relevant to a broad ICLR audience.
