- Reviewer revised score to 8 after authors' clarifications.
- Paper proposes framing few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem with sequences of training batches followed by a test batch.
- Inputs to the meta-learner include base-learner outputs (loss, gradients) and base-learner state (parameters).
- Uses an LSTM meta-learner whose second-layer memory cells directly model base-learner parameter updates.
- Motivates LSTM updates by analogy to gradient descent and trains updates using final-test-batch loss.
- Makes simplifying assumptions such as weight sharing across second-layer cells (like a single learning rate).
- Recreates Mini-ImageNet and shows competitive performance with Matching Networks on 1-shot and 5-shot tasks.
- Strength: novel and natural sequential meta-learning formulation that advances the area.
- Strength: competitive empirical results on Mini-ImageNet 1-shot and 5-shot experiments.
- Strength: uses a ConvNet base learner rather than nearest-neighbor embedding, showing method versatility.
- Strength: provides insight into LSTM vs. gradient-descent relationship as an interesting contribution.
- Strength: offers practical training/design tips (initialization, weight sharing, normalization) aiding reproducibility.
- Weakness: writing is opaque and would benefit from clearer, more concise presentation and a concrete example/diagram.
- Weakness: unclear definition of N-way few-shot setup and class split usage in Mini-ImageNet (training/validation/testing classes and softmax outputs).
- Weakness: Figure 2 plots are not informative and are insufficiently discussed.
- Overall: interesting paper with compelling results and a clear accept recommendation pending improved clarity and presentation.
