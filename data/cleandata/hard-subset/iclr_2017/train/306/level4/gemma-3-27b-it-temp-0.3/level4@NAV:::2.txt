This paper presents a novel meta-learning framework that reframes the stochastic gradient descent (SGD) update as a gated recurrent model with learnable parameters. This is a creative and potentially impactful contribution to the field of transfer learning. The paper is well-organized, though some sections could benefit from increased clarity.

**Strengths:**

- The proposed approach to meta-learning is both intriguing and practically viable.
- The results achieved are competitive with current state-of-the-art methods, and the comparison is appropriately conducted.
- The paper offers valuable insights and recommendations for implementing this approach in real-world systems.

**Weaknesses:**

- The proposed recurrent model architecture bears a stronger resemblance to Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs) than Long Short-Term Memory (LSTMs).
- The explanation of the meta-dataset split is difficult to understand and would be significantly improved with a visual representation.
- The experimental evaluation, while promising, is somewhat limited. Further investigation into the sensitivity of the model to its parameters (particularly 'it' and 'ft') would be beneficial.
- Figure 2 provides limited additional information and its inclusion is questionable.

**Comments:**

- A minor typographical error exists in section 3.2: "This means each coordinate has it" should read "its".
> The authors intend to release the code used for their experiments. 
This is a highly positive step and would greatly enhance the reproducibility and impact of this work.
