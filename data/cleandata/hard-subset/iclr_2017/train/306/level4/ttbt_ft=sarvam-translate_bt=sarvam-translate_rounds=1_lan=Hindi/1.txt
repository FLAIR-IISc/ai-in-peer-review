This work presents an LSTM-based meta-learning framework for learning the optimization algorithm of another learning algorithm (here an NN).

The paper is well-written globally, and the presentation of the main content is clear. The paper's summary: It is interesting to establish the similarity between the Robbins-Munro update rule and the LSTM update rule and to use it to fulfill two main desired outcomes of some shot learning (1- rapid acquisition of new knowledge, 2- slow extraction of general transferable knowledge).

Several tricks reused from (Andychovitch et al. 2016) such as parameter sharing and normalization, and novel design options (specific implementation of batch normalization) are well-motivated.

The experiments are convincing. This is a strong paper. My only concerns/questions are as follows:

1. Could it be unnecessary for the meta-learner to use loss, gradient, and parameters as input? Have you conducted ablative studies to ensure that simple combinations are not sufficient?
2. It would be great if other architectural components of the network could also be learned in a similar way (number of neurons, type of units, etc.). Do you have any opinion about this?
3. The related work section (mainly focused on meta-learning) is a bit shallow. Meta-learning is an old topic, and similar approaches have been tried to solve the same problem, even if they were not using LSTM:

    - Sammy Bengio PhD thesis (1989) is about this;-)
    - Using genetic programming for a new learning rule for neural networks (S. Bengio, Y. Bengio, and J. Cloutier. 1994)
    - I am sure Schmidt Huber has done something, make sure you find it and update the related work section.

Overall, I liked this paper. I believe that the material discussed is relevant to a wide audience in ICLR.
