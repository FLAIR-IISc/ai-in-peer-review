- Reviewer raised score to 8 after authors’ prompt revisions and clearer meta-learning description.
- Paper reframes few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning task where each example is a sequence of training batches followed by a test batch.
- An LSTM meta-learner ingests base-learner outputs and parameters; second-layer LSTM cells directly represent evolving base-learner parameters.
- Authors emphasize a correspondence between LSTM cell updates and gradient descent and train the meta-learner using final-test-batch loss.
- Several simplifications are used, notably coordinate-wise parameter sharing across the second LSTM layer.
- Reconstructed Mini-ImageNet benchmark; method is competitive with Matching Networks and outperforms on 5-shot.
- Using a simple ConvNet base learner contrasts with embedding+nearest-neighbor approaches and yields strong results.
- The LSTM-to-gradient-descent observation appears novel and intellectually interesting.
- Paper gives practical training guidance (initialization, coordinate sharing, normalization/rescaling of losses/gradients/parameters).
- Exposition is dense and hard to read; task description uses niche notation and needs a concrete walk-through and figure.
- The N-way few-shot protocol and Mini-ImageNet class-split/softmax-dimension details are ambiguous and unclear.
- Figure 2 plots are difficult to interpret and lack sufficient explanatory analysis.
- Overall assessment: strong empirical paper and clear accept, but presentation and clarity need substantial improvement.
- Question: Are Mini-ImageNet 64/16/20 splits strict during meta-training, and what softmax dimensionality is used then?
- Question: During meta-testing, are output heads reinitialized per episode and how are parameters shared/initialized across episodes?
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the coordinate-wise parameter-sharing assumption and were grouped sharing schemes tried?
- Question: What is the effect of incorporating second-order (gradient-dependence) terms on stability and performance?
- Question: Can you provide ablations quantifying contributions of gradient/loss preprocessing and BN-statistics management?
- Suggestion: Add a concrete episode example and schematic showing train-sequence → meta-learner updates → final test loss flow.
- Suggestion: Clarify class-split protocol, softmax dimensionality across splits, and handling of output layers for disjoint classes.
- Suggestion: Enrich Figure 2 analysis with quantitative summaries (e.g., gate-value distributions, correlations with loss reduction).
- Suggestion: Include ablations for number of meta-steps, gate-bias initializations, and training for fixed T versus variable step counts.
