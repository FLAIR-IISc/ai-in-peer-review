- Reframes few-shot learning by meta-learning a coordinate-wise LSTM-like optimizer and a shared task-common initialization for the base classifier.
- Maps gradient-based parameter updates to gated RNN (LSTM-style) cell-state dynamics, using gradients and loss as inputs with parameter sharing and preprocessing for compactness and stability.
- Trains end-to-end to minimize test loss after a fixed number of inner updates, aligning training and evaluation and giving practical guidance (initialization, episodic batch-norm, clipping, small initial rates).
- Empirically competitive on Mini-ImageNet 5-way 1-shot and 5-shot, outperforming reasonable baselines and matching some metric-learning methods.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reasons for acceptance: novel, well-motivated formulation linking gradient descent to gated dynamics and a practical end-to-end few-shot learner; strong, targeted empirical results.
- Problem framed: rapid adaptation of high-capacity classifiers from few labels by meta-learning both update rule and initialization.
- Motivation: well-grounded in meta-learning/optimizer-learning literature; gating interpretation gives an intuitive per-parameter learning/forgetting control.
- Evidence: competitive Mini-ImageNet results and an episodic training procedure matching few-update test constraints, though more ablations would strengthen claims.
- Suggest clarifying the gated-update analogy and justify LSTM-style vs GRU-style design (and provide empirical comparison if possible).
- Make meta-train/val/test splits and episode sampling easier to follow (diagram or table suggested).
- Strengthen empirical analysis with gate/function ablations (e.g., fix ft=1, remove loss input, scalar vs per-parameter it), sensitivity to inner-update count T and preprocessing parameter p, and report memory/time overhead of the coordinate-wise meta-learner.
- Include consistent confidence intervals and ensure comparable backbone capacities across baselines (or explain differences).
- Consider moving or enriching Figure 2 if it adds little; improve visualization of gradient/loss flow into gates.
- Minor fixes: correct typo in 3.2 (“it” -> “its”).
- Public code release recommended for reproducibility and adoption.
- Ask authors to justify LSTM-style separate gates vs GRU-style tied gates and report any empirical trade-offs.
- Request gate-importance ablations and statistics (effects of fixing ft, shared vs per-parameter it, gate-value distributions).
- Ask about sensitivity to number of inner-loop updates T, especially when test T differs from training T.
- Query sensitivity to gradient/loss preprocessing parameter p and the assumption of gradient independence; whether limited second-order variants were tried.
- Request detailed batch-normalization procedure per episode (collection/reset/decay schedule).
- Clarify exact class splits and sampling protocol to rule out leakage, and confirm matching model capacities/data augmentations across baselines.
- Ask how well the learned optimizer+initialization generalizes when N and/or k differ from training (e.g., 5→10-way or 1→5-shot).
