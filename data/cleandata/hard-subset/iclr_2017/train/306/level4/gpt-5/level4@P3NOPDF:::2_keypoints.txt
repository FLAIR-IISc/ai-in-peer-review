- Proposes meta-learning approach interpreting SGD updates as a gated recurrent model to learn transferable optimization dynamics.
- Novelty/relevance: Casting SGD as gated recurrence is an interesting, principled connection between optimization and sequence modeling.
- Empirical competitiveness: Results are competitive with strong baselines.
- Practical value: Provides useful implementation recommendations for practitioners.
- Potential impact: Formulation could influence future learned optimizers and cross-task transfer work.
- Framing concern: The update rule resembles GRU-style gating more than LSTM mechanisms and needs clarification or justification.
- Meta-setup clarity: Data splits (meta-train/val/test and within-task support/query) and the meta-training flow are hard to follow and need schematic/pseudocode.
- Limited ablations: Needs deeper analysis of learned gates (e.g., it and ft) and ablations disabling/tying gates to show contributions.
- Sensitivity analysis: Report robustness to initialization, learning-rate schedules, unroll horizon, random seeds, and tasks.
- Figure 2 is uninformative and should be replaced with gate ablations or learning/generalization plots or moved to appendix.
- Evaluation scope: Broaden tasks/datasets, compare to related learned optimizers (including GRU variants), and report computational overhead (time, memory, meta-parameters).
- Clarify gating equations and relationship to known RNN cells, and discuss choice implications for optimization dynamics.
- Notation: Define all symbols at first use and consider a compact notation box for consistency.
- Algorithmic clarity: Include a clear algorithm box showing task sampling, inner/outer loops, and meta-update details (regularization, clipping).
- Data visualization: Add diagrams showing task formation and allocation to meta splits and within-task adaptation/evaluation.
- Inspect learned behavior: Visualize gate values over time/layers to explain when the optimizer opens/closes updates.
- Fairness of comparisons: Ensure comparable hyperparameter tuning budgets, include wall-clock and step-based comparisons, and describe early stopping/validation protocols.
- Generalization checks: Test cross-domain transfer and out-of-distribution robustness to loss curvature, noise, and model size changes.
- Minor edits: Fix typo (“it” → “its”), release reproducible code and scripts, and move lengthy derivations to the appendix.
- Overall: Well-motivated and promising work but needs clearer meta-learning setup and more thorough experimental/ablation analysis; recommendation: weak accept contingent on improvements.
