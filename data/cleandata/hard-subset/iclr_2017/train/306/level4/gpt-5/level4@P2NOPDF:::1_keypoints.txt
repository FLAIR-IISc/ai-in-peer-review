- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimizer for another neural network learner.  
- Writing is clear and core ideas are well presented.  
- Central insight: parallels between Robbins–Monro updates and LSTM updates, enabling fast task-specific learning and slower extraction of transferable knowledge.  
- Uses techniques from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) (parameter sharing, normalization) plus motivated new design choices (e.g., batch-normalization implementation).  
- Experiments are convincing; overall assessment: strong paper of broad interest.  
- Concern 1: Potential input redundancy—feeding loss, gradients, and parameters to the meta-learner may be redundant; request ablation studies to test simpler input sets.  
- Concern 2: Interest in extending learnable components—ask about feasibility/roadmap for learning structural aspects (number of neurons, unit types).  
- Concern 3: Related work is shallow; request expansion to include earlier work (Samy Bengio’s 1989 PhD thesis, Bengio/Bengio/Cloutier 1994 on genetic programming for learning rules, and Schmidhuber et al. contributions).
