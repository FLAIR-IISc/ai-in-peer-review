1. Paper is well written with a clear presentation of the main material.  
2. Core idea is intriguing: draw a parallel between Robbins–Monro updates and LSTM updates to enable fast acquisition of new knowledge and slower extraction of transferable knowledge.  
3. Reuses techniques from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) (parameter sharing, normalization) and introduces well-motivated novel design choices (specific batch-normalization implementation).  
4. Experiments are convincing.  
5. Overall assessment: strong paper, relevant to a wide ICLR audience.  
6. Concern: potential redundancy in feeding loss, gradient, and parameters to the meta-learner—request ablation studies to test simpler input combinations.  
7. Question/suggestion: could other architectural components (e.g., neuron counts, unit types) be learned similarly—author opinion requested.  
8. Related work is somewhat shallow; reviewer suggests adding older/meta-learning work (Samy Bengio’s 1989 PhD, Bengio/Bengio/Cloutier 1994 on genetic programming, Schmidhuber’s relevant work).
