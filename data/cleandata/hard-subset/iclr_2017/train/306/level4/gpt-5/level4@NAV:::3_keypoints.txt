- Reviewer raised score to 8 after authors’ revisions and clarification of the meta-learning task.  
- Paper casts few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning task: sequences of training batches followed by a test batch.  
- Method uses an LSTM meta-learner whose second-layer memory cells directly represent base-learner parameters, drawing parallels to gradient descent.  
- LSTM is trained by optimizing the base learner’s loss on the final test batch, with simplifying assumptions like shared weights across second-layer cells (uniform learning rate).  
- Authors reconstructed Mini-ImageNet and show the LSTM meta-learner is competitive with or outperforms Matching Networks on 1- and 5-shot tasks.  
- Strength: Framing few-shot as sequential meta-learning is compelling and appears to advance the state of the art (novelty not fully certain).  
- Strength: Using a ConvNet base learner (rather than nearest-neighbor on embedding) demonstrates a qualitatively different, effective strategy.  
- Strength: The connection between LSTM memory updates and gradient descent is novel and interesting.  
- Strength: Paper provides practical guidance for designing/training LSTM meta-learners (initialization, coordinate-wise weight sharing, normalization/rescaling), aiding reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Writing is sometimes opaque and hard to follow; Section 2 is abstract and would benefit from a concrete example or diagram.  
- Weakness: Key experimental details (exact N-way/K-shot formulation, how Mini-ImageNet class splits are handled, softmax outputs during meta-training) are unclear.  
- Weakness: Figure 2 plots are largely uninformative and the discussion provides little additional insight.  
- Overall: Interesting paper with convincing results and a clear accept recommendation, but presentation should be strengthened.
