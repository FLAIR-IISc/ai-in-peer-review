- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns the optimization algorithm for another neural network learner.  
- Paper is well written and clearly presents the core material.  
- Central idea is compelling: connect Robbins–Monro updates to LSTM updates to enable fast task-specific learning and slower extraction of transferable knowledge.  
- Reuses techniques from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) (parameter sharing, normalization) and introduces well-motivated novel design choices, notably a specific batch-normalization implementation.  
- Experimental results are convincing; overall a strong contribution.  
- Concern: redundancy of feeding loss, gradient, and parameters to the meta-learner—ablation studies on simpler input combinations are missing.  
- Concern: unclear extensibility to architecture search (learning neuron counts, unit types); requests thoughts or preliminary results.  
- Concern: related work section is shallow; should expand to older meta-learning and non-LSTM prior work (e.g., Samy Bengio’s 1989 PhD thesis).  
- Suggestion: cite genetic programming work on learning rules (S. Bengio, Y. Bengio, J. Cloutier, 1994) and locate/incorporate relevant J. Schmidhuber prior work.
