- Proposal: LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimizer to train a neural-network classifier in the few-shot regime.
- Writing and clarity: Manuscript is well written and key ideas are clearly conveyed.
- Central insight: Maps Robbins–Monro/gradient-descent style updates to LSTM cell updates to enable rapid within-task adaptation and slower cross-task knowledge accumulation.
- Reused techniques: Adopts coordinate-wise parameter sharing and input normalization from Andrychowicz et al. (2016).
- Novel design choices: Introduces careful handling of batch normalization across episodes and other implementation details.
- Empirical evaluation: Results are convincing and support the claims.
- Overall judgment: Strong submission, likely of broad interest to the ICLR community.
- Concern 1 (inputs): Question whether feeding loss, gradient, and parameters jointly is redundant and requests ablation studies to test simpler input combinations.
- Concern 2 (extension): Asks if the framework can be extended to meta-learn other architectural choices (units, activations, layer configs) and requests the authors' perspective.
- Concern 3 (related work): Requests broader historical coverage—cite Samy Bengio’s thesis on learning rules, mid-1990s genetic-programming work (S. Bengio, Y. Bengio, J. Cloutier), and Schmidhuber’s fast weights/self-modifying networks—clarify similarities/differences.
