- Presents an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimizer for another neural network.  
- Paper is well written with a clear presentation of core ideas.  
- Key insight: parallels between Robbins–Monro updates and LSTM updates to support (1) rapid acquisition of new knowledge and (2) slower extraction of transferable knowledge for few-shot learning.  
- Reuses techniques from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) like parameter sharing and normalization, and introduces a well-motivated batch-normalization implementation.  
- Experiments are convincing and support the claims.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper of broad interest to the ICLR audience.  
- Concern 1: potential redundancy of feeding loss, gradient, and parameters to the meta-learner—request for ablation studies to test simpler input combinations.  
- Concern 2: interest in whether architectural components (e.g., number of neurons, unit types) could be learned similarly and what challenges that entails.  
- Concern 3: related work section is shallow—should cite older meta-learning work such as Samy Bengio’s 1989 thesis, Bengio et al. (1994) on genetic programming for learning rules, and relevant Schmidhuber work.
