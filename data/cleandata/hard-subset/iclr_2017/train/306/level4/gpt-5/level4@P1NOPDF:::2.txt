This paper proposes a novel meta-learning approach that interprets the SGD update rule as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters. The idea is original and relevant to transfer learning, and the paper is generally well structured, though certain explanations could be clearer.

Pros:
- An interesting and practical approach to meta-learning.
- Competitive results with appropriate comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines.
- Useful recommendations for real-world systems.

Cons:
- The analogy appears closer to GRUs than LSTMs.
- The description of how the data are partitioned into meta-sets is hard to follow; a visual illustration would help.
- The experimental evaluation is only partially satisfying. In particular, studying the effect of the parameters of i_t and f_t would be valuable.
- Figure 2 adds little value.

Remarks:
- Minor typo in Section 3.2: “This means each coordinate has it” should be “its.”
- “We plan on releasing the code used in our evaluation experiments.” This would be a significant plus.
