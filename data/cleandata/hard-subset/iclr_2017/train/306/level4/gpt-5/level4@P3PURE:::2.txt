This paper presents a meta-learning approach that interprets the SGD update rule as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters. The idea is original and important for transfer learning, and the paper is generally well organized, though some explanations could be clearer.

Pros:
- An interesting and feasible approach to meta-learning.
- Competitive results with proper comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.
- Useful recommendations for practical systems.

Cons:
- The proposed analogy appears closer to GRUs than to LSTMs; the paper should clarify and justify this framing.
- The description of the data separation into meta sets is hard to follow and would benefit from a clear visualization.
- The experimental evaluation is only partially satisfying; in particular, analyzing the effect of the gating parameters i_t and f_t would be valuable.
- Figure 2 adds little value in its current form.

Remarks:
- Minor typo in Section 3.2: “This means each coordinate has it” should be “its.”
- “We plan on releasing the code used in our evaluation experiments.” This would certainly be a major plus.
