This paper presents a meta-learning approach that interprets the SGD update rule as a gated recurrent model with learnable parameters. The idea is novel and relevant to transfer learning, and the paper is generally well organized, though some explanations would benefit from greater clarity.

Pros:
- Proposes an interesting, practical, and feasible method for meta-learning.
- Reports competitive results with appropriate comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.
- Offers useful recommendations for practical deployment.

Cons:
- The recurrent analogy appears more aligned with GRUs than with LSTMs; clarifying this distinction and the design choices would strengthen the narrative.
- The description of how data are split into meta sets is difficult to follow; a clear schematic or diagram would help.
- The empirical evaluation feels incomplete; an analysis of sensitivity to key parameters (e.g., i_t and f_t) would be especially informative.
- Figure 2 adds limited value in its current form.

Remarks:
- Minor typo in Section 3.2: “This means each coordinate has it” should be “its.”
- The stated intention to release the code used in the experiments would be a major plus and would significantly enhance the paper’s reproducibility and impact.
