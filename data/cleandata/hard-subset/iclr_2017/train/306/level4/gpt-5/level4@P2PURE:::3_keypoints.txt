- Reviewer raised score to 8 after authors’ responsiveness and clarification of the meta-learning task.
- Paper frames few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem with sequences of training batches and a final test batch.
- Meta-learner inputs include base-learner outputs (loss, gradients) and base-learner parameters; an LSTM is used to update parameters.
- Second-layer LSTM memory cells are used to represent updated base-learner parameters; parallels to gradient descent are highlighted.
- LSTM meta-learner is trained using the base-learner’s prediction loss on the final test batch.
- Method includes simplifying assumptions such as weight sharing across second-layer cells (analogous to a single learning rate).
- Authors recreated Mini-ImageNet and show the approach is competitive with Matching Networks on 1- and 5-shot tasks.
- Strength: framing few-shot as sequential meta-learning is natural and advances the state of the art.
- Strength: using a ConvNet base learner (not nearest-neighbor on embeddings) yet achieving strong results is encouraging.
- Strength: the observation linking LSTM memory updates and gradient descent appears novel and interesting.
- Strength: paper gives practical guidance for designing/training LSTM meta-learners (initialization, weight sharing, normalization/rescaling).
- Weakness: writing is frequently opaque; task description is highly abstract and needs a concrete example/diagram for accessibility.
- Weakness: unclear definition/details of the N-class few-shot setup and Mini-ImageNet class/split and softmax output conventions.
- Weakness: Figure 2 plots are uninformative and the discussion around them adds little insight.
- Overall: interesting paper with convincing results and a clear accept, but presentation needs significant clarification to improve readability.
