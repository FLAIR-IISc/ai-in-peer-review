- Reviewer raised score to 8 after authors’ clarifications, especially about the meta-learning task.
- Paper frames few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem: sequences of training batches followed by a test batch.
- An LSTM meta-learner uses second-layer memory cells to model base-learner parameter updates; parallels drawn between LSTM updates and gradient descent.
- Meta-learner is trained using the base learner’s prediction loss on the final test batch.
- Simplifying assumptions include sharing weights across all second-layer cells (analogous to a shared learning rate).
- Recreated Mini-ImageNet and achieved results competitive with or better than Matching Networks on 1- and 5-shot tasks.
- Strength: approach is intriguing and appears to advance state of the art, though novelty relative to all recent work is uncertain.
- Strength: uses a different base learner (ConvNet classifier) rather than nearest-neighbor-on-embedding, showing an alternative successful methodology.
- Strength: observation about LSTM memory updates vs gradient descent is novel and interesting.
- Strength: paper gives practical, reproducible insights (initialization, coordinate-wise weight sharing, normalizing/rescaling inputs).
- Weakness: writing is often opaque and abstract; needs a concrete example and diagram to improve accessibility.
- Weakness: unclear N-way, K-shot setup and Mini-ImageNet training details (which classes are exposed during meta-training and softmax size).
- Weakness: Figure 2 is not informative on its own and lacks interpretative discussion.
- Overall: interesting and convincing results with a clear accept inclination, but presentation must be substantially improved.
