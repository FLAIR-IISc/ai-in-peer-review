- Approach: Meta-learns both a transferable classifier initialization and a per-parameter update rule by treating updates as LSTM/GRU-style state transitions.  
- Meta-learner inputs/outputs: Receives preprocessed loss and gradients (with parameter-sharing across coordinates) and produces a fixed small sequence of updates optimized for validation loss after T steps.  
- Practical engineering: Addresses batch-normalization leakage and stabilizes training via gate-bias initialization.  
- Empirical results: Beats nearest-neighbor and fine-tuning baselines and is competitive with Matching Networks on Mini-ImageNet, particularly strong on 5-shot.  
- Qualitative analysis: Visualizations of learned gate behaviors provide insight into learned update dynamics.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reasons for accept: Conceptual novelty (learned optimizer + initialization targeting few-update generalization) and empirical competitiveness supported by careful engineering.  
- Motivation and objective: Clearly motivates meta-learning for few-shot and aligns training objective with deployment (optimize test loss after T inner updates).  
- Related work placement: Properly situates itself among optimizer-learning, GRU/LSTM-style updates, learned initialization, and hyperparameter optimization literature.  
- Methodological soundness: Compact, numerically stable meta-optimizer via coordinate sharing and preprocessing; batch-norm protocol avoids leakage; gradient-independence (ignoring second derivatives) is a reasonable trade-off but needs discussion.  
- Support from results: Shows naive fine-tuning overfits in 1-shot, supporting need for learned initialization and update rules.  
- Clarify GRU/LSTM analogy and sign convention for the candidate cell update (c̃t) to avoid confusion.  
- Data-splits/reproducibility: Provide a concise schematic/table and exact class lists for Dmeta-train/val/test to aid reproducibility.  
- Fairness/architecture parity: Baseline learners use 64 filters vs meta-learner’s 32 filters—either match capacities or report matched-architecture results; report meta-learner LSTM sizes, total params, and compute/memory costs.  
- Ablations/sensitivity requested: Isolate contributions of learned initialization vs learned updates, ablate gate designs (ft/it fixed/tied), vary number of inner updates at test time, and test the gradient-independence assumption with higher-order differentiation experiments.  
- Broader evaluation: Add other benchmarks (Omniglot, CIFAR-FS, Tiered-ImageNet) and report variance across seeds to strengthen generality claims.  
- Visualizations improvement: Replace/augment Figure 2 with gate trajectories for representative layers and aggregate statistics (distributions of it and ft over time).  
- Minor edits: Fix typos (e.g., “each coordinate has its”), standardize notation and vector bolding, and ensure equation refs are consistent.  
- Questions for authors: (1) Did you run baselines with 32-filter backbones to match capacity? (2) Any experiments with second-order gradients? (3) How does performance change if evaluated with different numbers of inner updates? (4) Can ft’s weight-decay behavior be quantified? (5) Clarify BN stats handling at meta-test time to ensure no leakage. (6) How well does a meta-learner trained on 1-shot transfer to 5-shot (and vice versa)? (7) Confirm code availability, commit hashes, splits, and reproduction scripts.
