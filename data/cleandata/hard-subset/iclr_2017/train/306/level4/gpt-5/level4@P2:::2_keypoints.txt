- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that outputs per-parameter gated update rules and a learned classifier initialization for few-shot learning.  
- Trains the meta-learner episodically so the training objective matches test-time conditions by optimizing on episode test losses given gradients/losses from the episode training set.  
- Keeps the meta-learner compact via parameter sharing across coordinates, gradient/loss preprocessing, and a BN protocol that avoids cross-episode leakage while aligning train/test conditions.  
- Achieves strong results on Mini-ImageNet 1-shot and 5-shot, outperforming baselines and matching or exceeding Matching Networks, with practical advice on initialization and BN.  
- Decision: Accept — original, well-motivated approach with competitive empirical results and useful practical insights, though clarity and ablations could be improved.  
- Significance/novelty: Learning an optimizer tailored to few update steps plus a task-common initialization is compelling and elegantly maps gradient descent to gated updates.  
- Positioning: Well-situated relative to metric-learning and learned-optimizer prior work, emphasizing episode-level train/test matching.  
- Methodological soundness: Parameter sharing, numerical preprocessing, and BN handling are sensible and practically relevant; gradient-independence approximation is reasonable.  
- Empirical support: Beats nearest-neighbor and fine-tuning baselines on Mini-ImageNet and is notably stronger in 5-shot; naive fine-tuning overfits in 1-shot, motivating the method.  
- Clarify GRU/LSTM analogy: Make the GRU-like nature of the updates explicit earlier and explain why the LSTM framing was chosen.  
- Meta-set/data split clarity: Add a concise diagram and a concrete example for meta-training/validation/test splits and episode construction.  
- Ablations on gates/components: Include experiments isolating input/forget gate contributions and the effect of the learned initialization.  
- Sensitivity studies: Report sensitivity to update steps T, preprocessing hyperparameter p, and BN strategy variations.  
- Replace Figure 2: Consider a schematic showing per-parameter gated updates and episode information flow instead of the current computational graph.  
- Reproducibility: Public code release and more training details (batch sizes, episode counts, runtimes, memory, meta-learner parameter counts) are needed.  
- Q: GRU vs LSTM — did you try a GRU formulation and how did it compare in performance and stability?  
- Q: Role of gates — what happens empirically if ft or it are disabled/simplified, tied across layers, or if Lt is removed from gate inputs?  
- Q: Learned initialization — how much gain comes from learned init alone vs. learned optimizer alone vs. the full model?  
- Q: Number of steps — how sensitive is performance to mismatched train/test update counts; does the meta-learner generalize to different numbers of steps?  
- Q: Preprocessing and BN — how critical are log-scaling preprocessing and your BN protocol; provide results without preprocessing or with alternative BN choices.  
- Q: Gradient-independence — did you try variants accounting for higher-order terms (truncated backprop, limited higher-order gradients) and what are the trade-offs?  
- Q: Compute/memory — report training/inference overheads, parameter counts, runtime, and memory compared to standard fine-tuning.  
- Q: Generality — have you tested deeper learners or other backbones, and do learned updates transfer across architectures/datasets?  
- Minor issue: Typo in Sec. 3.2 (“each coordinate has it” → “its”) and add confidence intervals plus clarify exact Mini-ImageNet split in main table.  
- Overall: Well-motivated, practically useful contribution to few-shot learning with competitive results and clear paths for stronger ablations and clarity.
