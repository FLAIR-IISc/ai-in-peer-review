- Paper frames few-shot learning as sequential meta-learning with an LSTM meta-learner that uses its cell state as the base learner’s parameters and learns update rules over T steps.  
- LSTM gates are parameterized using loss, gradient, parameters, and prior gate values, exploiting an analogy between gradient descent and LSTM updates.  
- Meta-learner is trained end-to-end to minimize the base learner’s test loss after T updates, encouraging useful initialization and fast adaptation.  
- Scalability achieved via coordinate-wise parameter sharing and gradient/loss normalization; practical fixes (initialization, batch-norm handling, gradient-independence) enable stable training.  
- Empirical result: competitive with Matching Networks on Mini-ImageNet 1-shot and superior in 5-shot using a simple ConvNet classifier.  
- Decision: Accept (score 8) — strong empirical results, clear meta-learning formulation distinct from metric-learning, and useful practical guidance.  
- Strength: Natural and powerful problem framing that directly models learned parameter updates inside an LSTM.  
- Strength: Technical novelty in linking LSTM updates to gradient descent and focusing the objective on test loss after T steps with learned initialization.  
- Strength: Strong empirical baselines and results; fine-tuning baseline underperforms, supporting the approach.  
- Strength: Practical contributions with concrete training details that aid reproducibility.  
- Improvement: Improve clarity and accessibility with a concrete walkthrough/diagram of a single episode (e.g., 5-way, 1-shot).  
- Improvement: Precisely specify dataset class splits and how the per-episode final classifier layer is configured.  
- Improvement: Make gate-value figures more interpretable (summarize distributions, highlight consistent patterns, link to performance).  
- Improvement: Add ablations/sensitivity studies (no learned init, no preprocessing, no gate variants, no parameter sharing) and sensitivity to number of update steps and T mismatch.  
- Question: Is the final linear layer re-instantiated per episode as an episodic 5-way softmax with labels remapped, and are those weights part of the learned initialization?  
- Question: During meta-training, are episodes sampled only from the 64 training classes (with val/test disjoint), and was any pretraining on all 100 classes used?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the number of updates T, and what happens if test-time T differs from training-time T?  
- Question: What is the granularity of parameter sharing for gates (per-coordinate identical, per-layer, or other), and have alternatives been tried?  
- Question: Exactly how is batch normalization handled within episodes for meta-train and meta-test, and are running averages reset to avoid leakage?  
- Question: What are the memory/time costs of backpropagating through T updates, any truncation used, and how many steps/episodes were needed to converge?  
- Question: How many test episodes and random seeds were used to compute reported means/confidence intervals — please provide counts for reproducibility.  
- Question: Have comparisons been made to other learned-optimizer baselines adapted to few-shot, and can the method extend to deeper CNNs or semi-supervised episodes?  
- Final: After clarifications and revisions on task setup, reviewer raised score to 8; paper is compelling with solid results and practical impact.
