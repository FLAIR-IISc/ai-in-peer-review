- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns the optimization algorithm for another neural-network learner.
- Paper is well written and core ideas are clearly explained.
- Central insight: parallels between the Robbins–Monro update and LSTM update enable rapid acquisition of new knowledge and slower extraction of transferable knowledge.
- Reuses techniques from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) (parameter sharing, normalization) and introduces justified novel design choices (notably a specific batch normalization).
- Experiments are persuasive and the submission is strong.
- Concern 1: Supplying loss, gradients, and parameters as inputs to the meta-learner may be redundant—ablation studies are requested to test simpler input combinations.
- Concern 2: Recommend exploring whether other architectural components (e.g., neuron counts, unit types) could be learned similarly and request authors’ thoughts.
- Concern 3: Related work is too shallow; should cite earlier meta-learning and learning-rule search literature (Samy Bengio 1989, Bengio/Bengio/Cloutier 1994, and Schmidhuber’s relevant work).
- Overall: Positive recommendation—likely of broad interest to the ICLR community.
