- Reviewer raised score to 8 after authors’ revisions and clearer meta-learning task description.  
- Paper frames few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem with episodes of training batches followed by a test batch.  
- Meta-learner is an LSTM that receives base learner parameters, losses, and gradients; second-layer cells directly represent base learner parameters.  
- LSTM updates are leveraged as analogs of gradient descent; meta-learner trained on base learner loss on the final test batch.  
- Simplifying assumptions include coordinate-wise weight sharing (like a shared learning rate across parameters).  
- On Mini-ImageNet, the method is competitive with or outperforms Matching Networks on 1-shot and 5-shot tasks.  
- Strength: novel and natural sequential meta-learning formulation that advances the state of the art.  
- Strength: uses a simple ConvNet base learner (not nearest-neighbor embeddings) and shows strong results.  
- Strength: novel connection between LSTM cell updates and gradient descent.  
- Strength: provides practical, reproducible guidance for training LSTM meta-learners (initialization, rescaling, etc.).  
- Weakness: writing is often opaque; Section 2 is abstract and would benefit from a concrete example and episode diagram.  
- Weakness: experimental details for Mini-ImageNet (which classes seen during meta-training, softmax outputs) are unclear.  
- Weakness: Figure 2 plots and discussion are uninformative.  
- Overall: interesting paper with convincing results and a clear accept recommendation if exposition is improved.
