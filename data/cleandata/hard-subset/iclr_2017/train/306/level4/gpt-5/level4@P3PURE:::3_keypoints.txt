- Paper frames few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem where each example is a sequence of training batches followed by a test batch.  
- LSTM meta-learner uses second-layer memory cells to represent and update base-learner parameters, drawing parallels to gradient descent.  
- Meta-learner is trained by optimizing the base learner’s loss on the final test batch of each sequence.  
- Simplifying assumptions include sharing weights across second-layer cells (analogous to a shared learning rate).  
- On Mini-ImageNet, the LSTM meta-learner is competitive with or better than Matching Networks for 1- and 5-shot tasks.  
- The approach achieves state-of-the-art results using a ConvNet base learner rather than nearest-neighbor-on-learned-embedding.  
- The connection between LSTM memory updates and gradient descent is novel and interesting.  
- Paper provides practical, reproducible guidance (initialization, weight sharing, normalization/rescaling of inputs).  
- Writing is often opaque and the task description is abstract; a concrete example/diagram would improve accessibility.  
- Precise experimental setup for N-class few-shot (class splits, softmax outputs during meta-training) is ambiguous and needs clarification.  
- Plots (Figure 2) are uninformative and the discussion around them adds little value.  
- Overall assessment: convincing results and compelling framing; clear accept but presentation and experimental detail clarifications would strengthen the paper.
