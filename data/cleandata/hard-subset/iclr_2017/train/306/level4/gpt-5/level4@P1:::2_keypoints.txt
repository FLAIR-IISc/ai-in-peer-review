- Proposal: Meta-learning approach that interprets gradient descent updates as gated recurrent updates and trains an LSTM-based meta-learner to output per-parameter updates for a CNN classifier.  
- Mechanism: LSTM cell state holds learner parameters; input/forget gates act as learned, data-dependent learning-rate and forgetting terms computed from gradients, losses, and parameter history.  
- Design choices: Shares meta-learner parameters across coordinates, preprocesses gradients/losses to stabilize scale, and trains end-to-end by minimizing test loss over episodes to align training and evaluation.  
- Practical guidance: Provides recommendations on initialization, using gradient-independence to avoid second derivatives, and careful batch-normalization handling in episodic settings.  
- Empirical results: On Mini-ImageNet 1-shot and 5-shot, outperforms nearest-neighbor and fine-tuning baselines and is competitive with Matching Networks, with clear gains in 5-shot.  
- Interpretability: Visualizations of gate values give preliminary insight into the learned optimization strategy.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Original, well-motivated framing of optimizer-as-LSTM tailored to few-shot learning with learned initialization and update rule matching episodic evaluation.  
- Key reason 2: Competitive empirical performance against strong baselines and useful practical recommendations and literature placement.  
- Supporting point: Addresses rapid generalization by learning both initialization and updates rather than relying on generic optimizers.  
- Supporting point: Related work is well-covered and the method is clearly positioned relative to learned-optimizer, metric-learning, and memory-augmented methods.  
- Supporting point: The mapping between gradient updates and gated recurrent dynamics is conceptually sound (noting resemblance to GRU).  
- Supporting point: Training protocol and stability techniques (parameter sharing, preprocessing, gate bias initialization, gradient clipping) are practical and effective.  
- Supporting point: BN handling avoids information leakage across episodes while aligning train/test conditions.  
- Area for improvement: Clarify analogy/nomenclature since the learned update resembles a GRU more than a classic LSTM.  
- Area for improvement: Clarify meta-dataset splits and episode construction (suggest a compact illustrative figure).  
- Area for improvement: Add ablations to isolate roles of it and ft (e.g., ft=1, fixed learning rate) and quantify contribution of learned initialization c0.  
- Area for improvement: Improve or relocate Figure 2 to better show computation graph, preprocessing, parameter sharing, and gradient flow.  
- Additional feedback: Report sensitivity to number of updates, runtime/memory overhead vs SGD/Adam and Matching Networks, and sensitivity to preprocessing scale parameters.  
- Additional feedback: Expand gate visualizations (aggregate per layer/step and correlate with loss/gradient norms), fix minor typos, and release code for reproducibility.  
- Questions for authors: Did you try GRU-based parameterization and how does it compare?  
- Questions for authors: What are the effects of ft=1 or fixed it, and how much gains come from gating vs learned initialization?  
- Questions for authors: How much improvement comes from learning c0 alone vs learning both c0 and the update rule?  
- Questions for authors: How does accuracy degrade if evaluation uses T±k steps versus trained T?  
- Questions for authors: Quantify performance differences of BN strategies, the empirical gap when including second-order terms, granularity trade-offs for parameter sharing, and meta-test-time latency vs fine-tuning.
