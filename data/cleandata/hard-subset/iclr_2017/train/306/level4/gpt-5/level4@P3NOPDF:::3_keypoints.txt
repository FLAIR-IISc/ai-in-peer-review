- Reviewer raised score to 8 after authors’ responsiveness and clearer meta-learning task description.  
- Paper frames N-way K-shot classification as a sequential meta-learning problem with training batches followed by a test batch.  
- Meta-learner ingests signals from a base learner (loss, gradients, parameters) and updates an internal state across steps.  
- Second-layer LSTM cell states are used as surrogates for base-learner parameters, leveraging an LSTM–gradient-descent analogy.  
- Meta-learner is trained end-to-end to minimize loss on the final test batch of each episode.  
- Practical design choices (coordinate-wise weight sharing, input normalization/rescaling, initialization, simulating test conditions) improve performance and reproducibility.  
- Strong empirical results: competitive with or better than Matching Networks on Mini-ImageNet 1-shot and 5-shot using a ConvNet base learner.  
- Architectural diversity: achieves state-of-the-art with a classifier base rather than nearest-neighbor embeddings.  
- Insightful and novel explicit use of LSTM memory cells as parameter surrogates.  
- Revised manuscript improved clarity on meta-learning setup and training objective.  
- Exposition remains dense and uses abstract notation; a concrete episode walk-through and diagram are recommended.  
- Experimental protocol details are unclear: task sampling from training classes, softmax output dimensionality during meta-training, construction of support/query batches, and per-task parameter reinitialization need explicit answers.  
- Figures (e.g., Fig. 2) lack informative labels/takeaways; request ablations for weight sharing, normalization, and auxiliary signals, plus learning curves across and within episodes.  
- Request discussion of computational cost: training stability/variance, wall-clock time, memory usage, and sensitivity to episode length and N/K.  
- Suggest comparison with vanilla gradient descent or learned optimizers that don’t embed parameters in LSTM cells to isolate the unique benefit.  
- Clarification questions: whether LSTM second-layer cells map one-to-one to base parameters and how differing parameter counts are handled.  
- Clarification questions: whether meta-learner parameters are universally shared or allow per-layer/module specialization.  
- Clarification questions: how gradients through long sequences are computed (truncated BPTT?) and how truncation affects performance.  
- Overall: interesting, timely, and empirically convincing paper; recommend acceptance pending improved exposition, clearer protocol details, and more informative figures/ablations.
