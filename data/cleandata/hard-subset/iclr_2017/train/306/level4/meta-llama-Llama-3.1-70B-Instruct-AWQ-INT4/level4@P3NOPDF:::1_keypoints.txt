- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Core contribution: intriguing parallel between Robbins–Monro update and LSTM update to enable fast few-shot learning and slower transferable knowledge extraction.  
- Good use of techniques from prior work (parameter sharing, normalization) and well-motivated novel design choices (batch-norm implementation).  
- Experiments are convincing and demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Concern 1: Are loss, gradient, and parameters all necessary as inputs to the meta-learner? Ablation studies are needed to assess the importance of each input.  
- Concern 2: Could other architectural components (number of neurons, unit types) be learned similarly? Authors should discuss or explore this.  
- Concern 3: Related work is incomplete—should cite earlier/meta-learning work (e.g., Samy Bengio 1989 thesis; Bengio et al. 1994; and Jürgen Schmidhuber’s contributions).  
- Overall: Strong, relevant paper for ICLR that could make a significant contribution after addressing the above concerns.
