- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework to learn an optimizer for another neural network.  
- Draws an intriguing parallel between Robbins–Monro update rules and LSTM updates to meet few-shot learning desiderata (fast task learning, slow transfer of general knowledge).  
- Paper is globally well-written with clear presentation of main material.  
- Reuses effective tricks from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) such as parameter sharing and normalization.  
- Introduces well-motivated novel design choices, including a specific implementation of batch normalization.  
- Experiments are convincing and demonstrate the proposed approach’s effectiveness compared to other methods.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reasons: novel, well-motivated approach and strong experimental evidence.  
- Supporting argument: clear exposition makes contributions easy to understand.  
- Supporting argument: the LSTM meta-learner motivation and RM analogy are compelling.  
- Supporting argument: experiments are well-designed and supportive of claims.  
- Suggestion: test whether simpler input combinations (e.g., only gradient and parameters) to the meta-learner suffice.  
- Suggestion: expand the related work to include earlier meta-learning history (Samy Bengio, Yoshua Bengio, Jürgen Schmidhuber).  
- Future work: consider learning other architectural components (number/type of units) via the meta-learner.  
- Questions for authors: results with simpler input combinations? details on the specific batch normalization implementation and differences? plans to apply the method beyond few-shot learning?
