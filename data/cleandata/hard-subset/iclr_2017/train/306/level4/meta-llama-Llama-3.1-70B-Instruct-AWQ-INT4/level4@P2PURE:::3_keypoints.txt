- Reviewer updated score to 8 after authors’ responsiveness and manuscript clarifications (esp. meta-learning task).
- Paper frames few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem where each example is a sequence of train batches followed by a test batch.
- Meta-learner uses an LSTM that receives base-learner outputs (loss, gradients) and base parameters, directly modeling parameter updates in its memory cells.
- Authors draw an insightful analogy between LSTM memory-cell updates and gradient-descent update rules.
- Simplifying assumptions include sharing weights across second-layer cells (analogous to a shared learning rate).
- The authors recreate Mini-ImageNet and show the LSTM meta-learner is competitive with or outperforms Matching Networks on 1- and 5-shot tasks.
- Base learner is a simple ConvNet classifier, differing from Vinyals et al.’s nearest-neighbor-on-learned-embedding approach.
- Practical training/design insights are provided (initialization, coordinate weight sharing, normalization/rescaling of loss/gradients/parameters).
- Reviewer unsure about the novelty relative to recent meta-learning work but finds the execution strong.
- Writing is sometimes opaque; task description is abstract and would benefit from a concrete example and diagram for accessibility.
- Key experimental details are unclear (precise N-class few-shot setup, whether softmax outputs use 64 vs. 100 classes during meta-training).
- Figure 2 plots are uninformative and the discussion provides limited insight.
- Overall assessment: interesting, convincing results and a clear accept, contingent on improved clarity; reviewer may raise score with better writing.
