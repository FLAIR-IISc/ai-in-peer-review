- Reviewer raised score to 8 after authors' responsiveness and clearer meta-learning task description.
- Paper casts few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem: sequences of training batches followed by a test batch.
- Uses an LSTM meta-learner whose second-layer memory cells model base-learner parameter updates, noting similarities to gradient descent.
- Makes simplifying assumptions such as sharing weights across all second-layer cells (analogous to a single learning rate).
- Recreated Mini-ImageNet and achieves competitive/outperforming results versus Matching Networks on 1- and 5-shot tasks.
- Base learner is a ConvNet classifier, a notably different and promising approach compared to embedding + nearest-neighbor methods.
- The connection between LSTM memory-cell updates and gradient descent is a novel and interesting insight.
- Paper offers practical, actionable insights for designing/training LSTM meta-learners (initialization, weight sharing, normalizing/rescaling inputs).
- Writing is opaque and the task description is abstract; a concrete example and diagram would improve accessibility.
- Key dataset/experimental details are unclear (e.g., whether only 64 of 100 classes are seen during meta-training and what the softmax outputs are).
- Figure 2 plots are not very informative and the accompanying discussion provides little insight.
- Overall verdict: convincing and interesting work deserving acceptance, but presentation and clarity need improvement.
