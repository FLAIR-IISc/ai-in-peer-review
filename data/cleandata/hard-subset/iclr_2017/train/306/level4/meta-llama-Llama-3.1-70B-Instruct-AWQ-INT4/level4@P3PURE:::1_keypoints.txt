- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework to learn an optimizer for neural networks; paper is well-written and clear.
- Core idea: link Robbins–Monro update rule to LSTM updates to achieve fast few-shot learning and slower extraction of transferable knowledge.
- Leverages techniques from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) (parameter sharing, normalization) and introduces well-motivated novel choices, including a specific batch-normalization implementation.
- Experiments are convincing, making this a strong paper overall.
- Concern: using loss, gradient, and parameters as inputs to the meta-learner may be redundant—ablative studies are requested to test simpler input combinations.
- Suggestion: consider whether other architectural components (e.g., neuron counts, unit types) could be learned similarly and provide the authors' opinion.
- Related work is shallow—meta-learning has older precedents (Bengio PhD 1989, Bengio et al. 1994 on genetic programming, Schmidhuber’s work); recommend updating citations and discussion.
- Overall recommendation: paper is relevant and of broad interest to the ICLR community.
