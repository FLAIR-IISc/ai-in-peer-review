- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework to learn the optimization algorithm for another neural network.  
- Paper is well-written and the main ideas are clearly presented.  
- Core contribution: parallels Robbins–Monroe update rule with LSTM updates to support fast few-shot learning and slower extraction of transferable knowledge.  
- Reuses techniques from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) (parameter sharing, normalization) and introduces well-motivated novel design choices, including a specific batch-normalization implementation.  
- Experiments are convincing and the paper is overall strong.  
- Question: Is feeding loss, gradient, and parameters to the meta-learner necessary? Request ablation studies to test simpler input combinations.  
- Suggestion: Explore whether other architectural components (number of neurons, unit types) can be learned similarly and request authors’ thoughts.  
- Related work is too narrow; recommend adding earlier/meta-related literature such as Samy Bengio’s 1989 thesis, Bengio/Bengio/Cloutier (1994) on genetic programming for learning rules, and Schmidhuber’s relevant contributions.  
- Overall positive: paper is relevant to a wide ICLR audience.
