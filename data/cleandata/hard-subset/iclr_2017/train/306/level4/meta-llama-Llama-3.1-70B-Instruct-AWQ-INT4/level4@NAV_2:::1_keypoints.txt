- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework to learn the optimization algorithm for another model (a neural network).  
- Paper is well-written and presentation is clear.  
- Main idea: relate Robbins–Monro update rule to LSTM updates to capture few-shot desiderata (fast acquisition of new info, slower extraction of generalizable knowledge).  
- Reuses effective tricks from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) such as parameter sharing and normalization.  
- Introduces novel, well-motivated design choices, including a specific implementation of batch normalization.  
- Experiments are convincing and support the claims; overall a strong paper.  
- Concern 1: Potential redundancy in using loss, gradient, and parameters as inputs to the meta-learner — requests ablation studies to test simpler input combinations.  
- Concern 2: Asks whether other architectural components (number of neurons, unit types, etc.) could be learned similarly and requests the authors’ opinion.  
- Concern 3: Related work section is shallow; reviewer recommends citing older meta-learning and learning-rule-search work (Bengio PhD 1993; Bengio et al. 1995; Schmidhuber 1992/1993).  
- Overall recommendation: paper is relevant to a wide ICLR audience and the reviewer likes it.
