- Proposes a novel meta-learning approach interpreting SGD updates as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters — original and important for transfer learning.
- Targets few-shot learning by using an LSTM-based meta-learner to learn the optimization algorithm for training a classifier in low-data regimes.
- Approach is well-motivated with a thorough discussion of limitations of traditional gradient-based methods for few-shot learning.
- Paper structure is clear overall, but clarity could be improved in some explanations.
- Recommendation to accept based on novelty/clarity of approach and competitive empirical results with proper SOTA comparisons.
- Main strength: learns a beneficial common initialization for the learner network, enabling rapid convergence.
- Provides useful analysis of the optimization strategy learned by the meta-learner, aiding interpretability.
- Analogy between SGD and LSTM is questionable — closer to GRUs than LSTMs.
- Description of meta-set data separation is hard to follow and would benefit from visualization.
- Experimental evaluation is only partly satisfying; needs deeper analysis of the effects of parameters it and ft.
- Minor issues: typo in Section 3.2 ("it" → "its") and Figure 2 adds little value and could be removed.
- Major plus: authors plan to release the evaluation code.
- Suggested improvements: add more details on the learned optimization strategy and the effects of it and ft.
- Suggested improvements: include more visualizations for meta-set separation and the optimization process.
- Suggested improvements: provide more detailed comparisons to other meta-learning methods (e.g., memory-augmented networks, gradient-based hyperparameter optimization).
- Questions for authors: clarify effects of parameters it and ft on the learned optimizer; how to extend to many-class few-shot settings; provide visualizations of meta-set separation/optimization; compare directly to other meta-learning approaches.
