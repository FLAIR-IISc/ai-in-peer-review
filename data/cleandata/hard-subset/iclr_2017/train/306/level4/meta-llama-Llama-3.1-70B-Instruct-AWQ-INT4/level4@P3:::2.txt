This paper proposes a novel approach to meta-learning by interpreting the stochastic gradient descent (SGD) update rule as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters. The idea is original and important for research related to transfer learning. The paper has a clear structure, but clarity could be improved at some points.

I decide to accept this paper with one major reason being the competitive results and proper comparison to state-of-the-art methods. The authors have demonstrated that their approach is effective in few-shot learning tasks and have provided a thorough evaluation of their method.

The supporting arguments for my decision include the following:

* The paper presents a well-motivated approach to meta-learning, which is a key area of research in machine learning. The authors have identified a specific problem in few-shot learning and proposed a novel solution.
* The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the authors have compared their method to state-of-the-art approaches in few-shot learning. The results are competitive, and the authors have provided a detailed analysis of their method's performance.

However, there are some areas that need improvement:

* The analogy between the SGD update rule and the gated recurrent model is closer to Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs) than Long Short-Term Memory (LSTMs). The authors should clarify this point and provide a more accurate comparison.
* The description of the data separation in meta-sets is hard to follow and could be visualized. The authors should consider adding a diagram or figure to illustrate this process.
* The experimental evaluation is only partly satisfying, especially the effect of the parameters of it and ft would be of interest. The authors should consider providing more detailed analysis of these parameters and their impact on the method's performance.
* Figure 2 does not add much value to the paper and could be removed or replaced with a more informative figure.

Additional feedback to improve the paper:

* The authors should consider providing more details about the implementation of their method, including the architecture of the neural network and the hyperparameters used in the experiments.
* The authors should discuss the limitations of their approach and potential avenues for future research.
* The authors should consider providing more comparisons to other meta-learning approaches, including those that use different architectures or optimization methods.
