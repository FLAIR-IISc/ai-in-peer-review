- Proposed method: cast few-shot learning as sequential meta-learning where each example is a sequence of training batches followed by a test batch; inputs include base-learner outputs and parameters, and an LSTM models parameter updates via its inner memory cells.  
- Strength: novel and natural sequential meta-learning formulation.  
- Strength: Outperforms Vinyals 2016 on 1-shot and 5-shot Mini-ImageNet.  
- Strength: Uses a ConvNet base learner instead of nearest-neighbor-on-learned-embedding, offering a different paradigm.  
- Strength: Novel insight connecting LSTM memory cells to gradient-descent-like updates.  
- Strength: Provides practical advice for designing and training LSTM meta-learners, aiding reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Writing is sometimes opaque and would benefit from a concrete illustrative example.  
- Weakness: The exact N-class few-shot problem formulation is unclear and needs clarification.  
- Weakness: Figure 2 plots are uninformative and the accompanying discussion lacks insight.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Supporting argument: Strong experimental performance and practical meta-learner design insights, tempered by clarity issues.  
- Suggested improvements: add a brief concrete example, clarify N-class softmax/output setup during meta-training, and provide more informative plots with better discussion.  
- Questions for authors: provide a concrete example of the learning problem; clarify how many softmax outputs the ConvNet has during meta-training; improve plots and related discussion.
