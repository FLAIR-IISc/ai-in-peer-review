- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns the optimizer for another neural network.  
- Paper is well written and clearly presented.  
- Core idea: draw a parallel between Robbins–Monro updates and LSTM updates to achieve fast task-specific learning and slower extraction of transferable knowledge.  
- Reuses useful techniques from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) (parameter sharing, normalization) and introduces well-motivated design choices (specific batch-norm implementation).  
- Experiments are convincing and results are competitive with state-of-the-art.  
- Concern: potential redundancy in using loss, gradient, and parameters as inputs to the meta-learner—ask for ablation studies.  
- Question: can other architectural components (number of neurons, unit types, etc.) be learned similarly?  
- Related work is shallow; should cite earlier/meta-learning work (e.g., Bengio PhD 1993, Bengio et al. 1995, Schmidhuber) and expand the section.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Reasons for acceptance: novel and well-motivated approach, convincing experiments, clear writing and organization.  
- Supporting points: tackles a specific few-shot learning problem with clear motivation; builds on prior techniques; experimental design is strong.  
- Suggestion: include more analysis of the learned optimizer compared to traditional optimizers.  
- Suggestion: provide details on computational cost and comparisons to other methods.  
- Suggestion: expand related work to cover more recent meta-learning and few-shot literature.  
- Questions for authors: share ablation details justifying inputs to the meta-learner.  
- Questions for authors: elaborate on extending the approach to learn architectural components.  
- Questions for authors: provide detailed computational cost comparisons.
