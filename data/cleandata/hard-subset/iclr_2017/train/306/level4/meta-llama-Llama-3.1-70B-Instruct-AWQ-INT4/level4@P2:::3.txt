**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper proposes a novel approach to tackle few-shot learning with neural networks by leveraging meta-learning. The authors formulate few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem, where each example includes a sequence of batches of training pairs, followed by a final test batch. They apply an LSTM to this meta-learning problem, using the inner memory cells in the second layer to directly model the updated parameters of the base learner. The paper shows that the meta-learner LSTM is competitive with the current state-of-the-art (Matching Networks) on 1- and 5-shot learning.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

I recommend accepting this paper with a score of 8. The key reasons for this decision are:

1. The paper proposes a novel and intriguing approach to tackle few-shot learning with neural networks by leveraging meta-learning.
2. The proposed approach is competitive with and outperforms the current state-of-the-art (Matching Networks) on 1- and 5-shot learning.

**Supporting Arguments for the Decision**

The paper has several strengths that support its acceptance. Firstly, the idea of casting the few-shot learning problem as a sequential (meta-)learning problem is natural and intriguing. The authors have advanced the state of the art in this area, and their approach is competitive with the current state-of-the-art. Additionally, the paper offers several practical insights about how to design and train an LSTM meta-learner, which should make it easier for others to replicate this work and apply these ideas to new problems.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement**

The paper has some weaknesses that need to be addressed. The writing is at times quite opaque, and the task description in Section 2 is very abstract and uses notation and language that is not common outside of this sub-area. To improve the paper's clarity and accessibility, I suggest adding a brief concrete example (based on MNIST is fine), perhaps paired with a diagram illustrating a sequence of few-shot learning tasks. Additionally, the precise nature of the N-class, few-shot learning problem is unclear, and the authors should provide more details about the Mini-ImageNet data set and the softmax layer of the ConvNet base learner.

**Additional Feedback and Questions**

To further improve the paper, I suggest providing more insight around the plots in Figure 2 and discussing the implications of the results in more detail. Additionally, I have several questions that need to be clarified:

* Does the Mini-ImageNet data set have 100 labels, of which 64/16/20 are used during meta-training/validation/testing?
* How many outputs does the softmax layer of the ConvNet base learner have during meta-training?
* Can the authors provide more details about the weight sharing across all second layer cells and the importance of normalizing/rescaling the loss, gradient, and parameter inputs?
