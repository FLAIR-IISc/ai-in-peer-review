- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework to learn an optimizer for neural networks; paper is well-written and clear.  
- Key idea: link Robbins–Monro update rule to LSTM updates to enable fast task-specific learning and slower, transferable knowledge acquisition.  
- Builds on techniques from Andrychowicz et al. (parameter sharing, normalization) and introduces motivated novel choices, e.g., a specific batch-normalization implementation.  
- Experiments are convincing and strengthen the paper.  
- Concern: potential redundancy of using loss, gradient, and parameters as inputs to the meta-learner; requests ablative studies to test simpler input combinations.  
- Suggestion: investigate learning other architectural components (e.g., neuron counts, unit types) and comment on feasibility.  
- Related work is shallow; recommend citing older/meta-learning work (Samy Bengio’s 1989 thesis, Bengio et al. 1994, Schmidhuber) and expanding the related work section.  
- Overall recommendation: positive — relevant to a wide ICLR audience.
