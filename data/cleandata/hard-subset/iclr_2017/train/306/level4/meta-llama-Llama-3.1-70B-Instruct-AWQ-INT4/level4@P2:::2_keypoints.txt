- Proposes a novel meta-learning approach interpreting SGD updates as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters.  
- Original and important idea for transfer learning and few-shot learning research.  
- Paper is well-structured with thorough method formulation, LSTM-based meta-learner design, and training procedure.  
- Experimental setup is organized, describing datasets, baselines (including Matching Networks), and results.  
- Method achieves competitive few-shot learning results and proper comparison to state-of-the-art.  
- Clarity could be improved in places; some descriptions are hard to follow.  
- The SGD-to-gated-model analogy aligns more closely with GRUs than LSTMs.  
- Data separation in meta-sets is poorly explained and would benefit from a visualization.  
- Experimental evaluation is partially unsatisfying; effects of parameters it and ft should be investigated.  
- Figure 2 adds little value and could be removed.  
- Minor typo: "has it" in 3.2 should be "its".  
- Authors plan to release code used in experiments, which would be a significant plus.  
- Decision: Accept, based on originality and competitive results.  
- Questions for authors: provide details on LSTM meta-learner initialization and plans to extend to more challenging/many-class few-shot scenarios.
