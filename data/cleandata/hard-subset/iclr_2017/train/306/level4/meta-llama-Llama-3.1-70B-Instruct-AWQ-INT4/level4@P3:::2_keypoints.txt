- Proposes a novel meta-learning approach that interprets the SGD update rule as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters.  
- Idea is original and important for transfer learning and few-shot learning research.  
- Paper has a clear structure but certain explanations lack clarity.  
- Recommendation: Accept, justified by competitive results and proper comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Empirical support: Method is effective on few-shot tasks with thorough evaluation and detailed performance analysis.  
- Strength: Well-motivated approach that targets a specific few-shot learning problem.  
- Strength: Experimental evaluation compares favorably to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Issue: The SGD–recurrent analogy is closer to GRUs than LSTMs and needs clarification and a more accurate comparison.  
- Issue: Description of data separation in meta-sets is hard to follow and should be visualized (diagram/figure).  
- Issue: Experimental evaluation is partly unsatisfying — need more analysis of the parameters (it and ft) and their effects.  
- Issue: Figure 2 adds little value and could be removed or replaced with a more informative figure.  
- Suggested additions: More implementation details (network architecture, hyperparameters).  
- Suggested additions: Discussion of limitations and potential future research directions.  
- Suggested additions: More comparisons to other meta-learning approaches using different architectures/optimizers.  
- Questions for authors: Provide details on meta-learner LSTM initialization and its effect on performance.  
- Questions for authors: How will this approach scale to more complex tasks or larger datasets?  
- Questions for authors: Provide more analysis of the learned optimization strategy and comparisons to other optimization methods.
