This paper presents a novel approach to meta-learning by interpreting the stochastic gradient descent (SGD) update rule as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters. The idea is both original and significant, with important implications for research related to transfer learning. The paper has a clear structure, although there are some points where clarity could be improved.

The paper has several strengths:

- It proposes an interesting and feasible approach to meta-learning, which is a valuable contribution to the field.
- The results are competitive, and the authors provide a proper comparison to state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper offers good recommendations for practical systems, which is essential for real-world applications.

However, there are also some weaknesses and areas for improvement:

- The analogy between the SGD update rule and recurrent neural networks would be more accurately described as being closer to Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs) than Long Short-Term Memory (LSTMs) networks.
- The description of the data separation in meta sets is somewhat hard to follow and could be greatly improved with the addition of visualizations.
- The experimental evaluation is only partially satisfying, particularly with regards to the effect of the parameters of the inner loop (it) and the outer loop (ft). A more in-depth analysis of these parameters would be of significant interest.
- Figure 2 does not provide much value and could potentially be removed or replaced with more informative content.

Minor corrections:

- There is a small typo in Section 3.2: "This means each coordinate has it" should be "This means each coordinate has its".

It is worth noting that the authors plan on releasing the code used in their evaluation experiments, which would be a major plus.
