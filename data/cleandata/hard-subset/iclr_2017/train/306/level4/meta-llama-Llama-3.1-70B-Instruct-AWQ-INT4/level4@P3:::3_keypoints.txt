- Proposes a novel sequential meta-learning formulation for few-shot learning using an LSTM to model base-learner parameter updates.  
- Uses inner memory cells of a second-layer LSTM to represent updated parameters of a ConvNet base learner.  
- Reports competitive (and better) performance vs. Matching Networks on 1- and 5-shot Mini-ImageNet.  
- Recommendation: accept (score 8) due to novelty and strong empirical results.  
- Key reason: novel and intriguing framing of few-shot as sequential meta-learning.  
- Key reason: empirically outperforms current state-of-the-art on standard benchmarks.  
- Strength: outperforms SOTA on Mini-ImageNet 1- and 5-shot experiments.  
- Strength: uses a ConvNet base learner rather than nearest-neighbor-on-embedding, showing architectural difference.  
- Strength: provides practical insights for designing and training LSTM meta-learners, aiding reproducibility.  
- Weakness: writing is sometimes opaque, hindering understanding of the learning problem.  
- Weakness: unclear description of the N-class few-shot setup and missing implementation details.  
- Weakness: Figure 2 plots are uninformative and discussion around them lacks insight.  
- Suggestion: add a concrete example (e.g., MNIST) and a diagram illustrating a sequence of few-shot tasks.  
- Suggestion: clarify N-class few-shot details, including softmax outputs of the ConvNet during meta-training.  
- Suggestion: improve Figure 2 and add more interpretive discussion.  
- Question: clarify the precise N-class few-shot setup and softmax output configuration during meta-training.  
- Question: how will this approach scale to many-class few-shot scenarios or to settings with very limited labeled data?  
- Question: can authors provide more insight into the optimization strategy learned by the meta-learner and its task adaptation?
