This paper proposes a novel approach to meta-learning by interpreting the stochastic gradient descent (SGD) update rule as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters. The idea is original and holds significant importance for research related to transfer learning. The paper has a clear structure, but there are areas where clarity could be improved.

The paper has several strengths. Firstly, the approach to meta-learning presented is both interesting and feasible. The authors demonstrate competitive results and provide a proper comparison to state-of-the-art methods, which helps to contextualize the effectiveness of their approach. Additionally, the paper offers good recommendations for practical systems, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

However, there are also some areas for improvement. One potential issue is that the analogy presented in the paper would be closer to Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs) than Long Short-Term Memory (LSTMs). Furthermore, the description of the data separation in meta sets is somewhat difficult to follow and could benefit from visualization to enhance understanding. The experimental evaluation, while satisfactory in some respects, leaves some questions unanswered, particularly with regards to the effect of the parameters of the inner loop (it) and the outer loop (ft). Finally, Figure 2 does not add significant value to the paper and could potentially be removed or replaced.

On a minor note, there is a small typo in Section 3.2, where "This means each coordinate has it" should be corrected to "This means each coordinate has its".

A major positive aspect of the paper is the authors' plan to release the code used in their evaluation experiments. This would be a significant plus, as it would allow other researchers to build upon and extend their work.

In conclusion, while the paper has some areas for improvement, it presents a novel and important approach to meta-learning, and the authors demonstrate competitive results and provide valuable insights for practical systems. With some revisions to address the issues mentioned above, this paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
