This paper introduces a novel meta-learning approach by reinterpreting the stochastic gradient descent (SGD) update rule as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters. The concept is innovative and holds significant relevance to transfer learning research. The overall structure of the paper is well-organized, although certain sections could benefit from enhanced clarity.

Strengths:
- The proposed approach to meta-learning is both intriguing and viable.
- The results are competitive, and the comparison to state-of-the-art methods is adequate.
- The paper provides useful recommendations for practical systems.

Weaknesses:
- The analogy presented is more akin to Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs) than Long Short-Term Memory (LSTMs).
- The explanation of data separation in meta sets is unclear and would be improved with visual aids.
- The experimental evaluation is partially satisfying, with a notable omission being the impact of the parameters of the proposed approach.
- Figure 2 does not contribute significantly to the paper.

Comments:
- A minor typo is present in section 3.2: "This means each coordinate has it" should be corrected to "its."
- The authors' plan to release the code used in their evaluation experiments is a significant positive aspect that will enhance the paper's value.
