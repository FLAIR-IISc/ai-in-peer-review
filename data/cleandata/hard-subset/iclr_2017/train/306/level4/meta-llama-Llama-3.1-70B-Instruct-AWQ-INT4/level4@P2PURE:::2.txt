This paper proposes a novel approach to meta-learning by reinterpreting the stochastic gradient descent (SGD) update rule as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters. This idea is original and holds significant importance for research in transfer learning. While the paper has a clear structure, there are areas where clarity could be improved.

The paper has several strengths:
- It presents an interesting and feasible approach to meta-learning.
- The results are competitive and properly compared to the state-of-the-art.
- The authors provide good recommendations for practical systems.

However, there are some limitations and areas for improvement:
- The proposed analogy is more closely related to Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs) than Long Short-Term Memory (LSTMs).
- The description of data separation in meta sets is difficult to follow and could be improved with a visual representation.
- The experimental evaluation is only partially satisfying, particularly with regards to the impact of parameters 'it' and 'ft', which would be of interest.
- Figure 2 does not provide significant value.

Minor corrections:
- In Section 3.2, there is a small typo: "This means each coordinate has it" should be "This means each coordinate has its".

A major positive aspect is the authors' plan to release the code used in their evaluation experiments, which would be a significant contribution.
