This paper presents a novel approach to meta-learning by reinterpreting the stochastic gradient descent (SGD) update rule as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters. The idea is original and holds significant importance for research related to transfer learning. The paper has a clear structure, but there are areas where clarity could be improved.

The paper has several strengths:

* The approach to meta-learning is both interesting and feasible.
* The results are competitive, and the comparison to state-of-the-art methods is proper.
* The authors provide good recommendations for practical systems.

However, there are some weaknesses and areas for improvement:

* The analogy presented would be more accurately described as being similar to Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs) rather than Long Short-Term Memory (LSTMs).
* The description of data separation in meta-sets is convoluted and could benefit from visualization to enhance understanding.
* The experimental evaluation is only partially satisfying. Specifically, it would be of interest to investigate the effect of the parameters of the inner loop (it) and the outer loop (ft) on the results.
* Figure 2 does not provide significant value and could potentially be removed or replaced.

Minor corrections and suggestions:

* A small typo is present in Section 3.2: "This means each coordinate has it" should be corrected to "its".

The authors' plan to release the code used in their evaluation experiments would be a significant plus, allowing other researchers to build upon and verify their results.
