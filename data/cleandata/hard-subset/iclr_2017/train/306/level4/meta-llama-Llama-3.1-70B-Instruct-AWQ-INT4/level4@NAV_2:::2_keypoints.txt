- Proposes a novel meta-learning approach that interprets SGD updates as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters, significant for transfer learning.  
- Uses an LSTM-based meta-learner to learn the optimization algorithm and a quick-converging initialization for few-shot classifiers, with parametrization for a fixed number of updates.  
- Paper structure is clear overall but certain explanations lack clarity.  
- Shows competitive results and proper comparisons to state-of-the-art deep metric-learning few-shot methods.  
- The SGD-to-LSTM analogy is questionable; the mapping is closer to GRUs and should be clarified and compared accurately.  
- Description of data separation into meta-sets is hard to follow and would benefit from a visual diagram.  
- Experimental evaluation is incomplete—need analysis of how input gate (it) and forget gate (ft) parameters affect meta-learner performance.  
- Figure 2 adds little value and should be removed or replaced with a more informative figure.  
- Recommend releasing the experiment code to improve reproducibility and community impact.  
- Suggest a more detailed analysis of the meta-learner’s behavior, learned optimization strategy, and limitations.  
- Encourage exploring applications of the meta-learning model to other domains such as NLP or reinforcement learning.  
- Key questions for authors: effects of it/ft on performance, plans for extending to other domains/tasks, and computational resources required to train the meta-learner.
