This paper introduces a novel approach to meta-learning by reinterpreting the stochastic gradient descent (SGD) update rule as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters. The idea is original and has significant implications for research in transfer learning. The paper's structure is clear, but there are areas where clarity could be improved.

The paper has several strengths:

* The approach to meta-learning is both interesting and feasible.
* The results are competitive with state-of-the-art methods, and the authors provide a proper comparison.
* The paper offers good recommendations for practical systems.

However, there are also some weaknesses:

* The analogy between the SGD update rule and recurrent neural networks would be more accurately drawn with Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs) rather than Long Short-Term Memory (LSTMs).
* The description of data separation in meta-sets is unclear and could benefit from visualization to facilitate understanding.
* The experimental evaluation is only partially satisfying. In particular, it would be interesting to see an analysis of the effect of the parameters of the meta-learning algorithm.
* Figure 2 does not provide significant insights and could be removed or replaced.

Minor errors were found, including a typo in Section 3.2: "This means each coordinate has it" should be "This means each coordinate has its".

A significant strength of the paper is the authors' plan to release the code used in their evaluation experiments, which would greatly facilitate reproducibility and further research in this area.
