- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimizer for another neural network.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presents core ideas.  
- Key contribution: draws a compelling parallel between Robbins–Monro updates and LSTM updates to balance fast adaptation and slow generalization for few-shot learning.  
- Incorporates established techniques (parameter sharing, normalization) and introduces well-justified novel design choices (specific batch normalization implementation).  
- Experimental results are convincing and support the method’s effectiveness.  
- Question: Are loss, gradient, and parameters all necessary as inputs to the meta-learner, and were ablation studies performed to test simpler input combinations?  
- Suggestion: Explore learning other architectural components (e.g., neuron counts, unit types) with the same approach.  
- Related-work critique: the meta-learning literature review is incomplete and should include earlier, non-LSTM approaches.  
- Specific citation suggestions: Samy Bengio’s 1989 PhD thesis, Bengio/Bengio/Cloutier (1994) on genetic programming for learning rules, and relevant work by Schmidhuber.  
- Overall impression: engaging paper with broad relevance to the ICLR audience.
