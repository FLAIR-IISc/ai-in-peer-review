- Reviewer raised score to 8 after authors' responsiveness and clarifications, especially on the meta-learning task.  
- Paper formulates few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem with sequences of train batches followed by a test batch.  
- Inputs at each step include base-learner outputs (loss/gradients) and base-learner parameters; an LSTM meta-learner is used.  
- The LSTM's second-layer memory cells are used to model updated base-learner parameters, drawing a comparison to gradient-descent updates.  
- Meta-learner updates are computed from the base learner’s loss on the final test batch.  
- Authors make simplifying assumptions such as sharing weights across second-layer cells (analogous to a single learning rate).  
- The paper recreates Mini-ImageNet and reports results competitive with or better than Matching Networks on 1- and 5-shot tasks.  
- Using a ConvNet base learner (rather than nearest-neighbor-on-embedding) and achieving state-of-the-art is a notable contribution.  
- The memory-cell vs. gradient-descent insight appears novel and interesting.  
- Paper provides practical training/design insights (initialization, weight sharing, normalizing/rescaling inputs) aiding replication.  
- Writing is sometimes opaque; a concrete example and diagram would improve accessibility.  
- The description of the N-class few-shot setup (which classes are seen during meta-training and softmax output size) is unclear and needs clarification.  
- Figure 2 plots are not very informative and their discussion is limited.  
- Overall, results are convincing and the paper is a clear accept pending improved presentation.
