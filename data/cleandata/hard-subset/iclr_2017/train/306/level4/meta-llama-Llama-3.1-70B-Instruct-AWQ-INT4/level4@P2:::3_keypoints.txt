- Paper proposes framing few-shot learning as sequential meta-learning and uses an LSTM whose second-layer cells model updated base-learner parameters.  
- Authors apply this meta-learner to few-shot tasks and report competitiveness with or improvement over Matching Networks on 1- and 5-shot learning.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance (score 8) based on novelty and empirical performance.  
- Casting few-shot learning as a sequential/meta problem is natural and intriguing.  
- Paper provides practical insights for designing and training an LSTM meta-learner, aiding reproducibility.  
- Writing is sometimes opaque; the task description is abstract and uses uncommon notation.  
- Suggest adding a concrete example (e.g., MNIST) and a diagram to clarify the sequential task formulation.  
- Precise definition of the N-class few-shot problem and details about the Mini-ImageNet dataset and ConvNet softmax are unclear.  
- Request more detail on weight sharing across all second-layer cells and on normalizing/rescaling loss, gradient, and parameter inputs.  
- Ask for more interpretation of Figure 2 and deeper discussion of result implications.  
- Specific clarification questions: Mini-ImageNet label splits (64/16/20?), number of softmax outputs during meta-training, and implementation details for shared second-layer cells.
