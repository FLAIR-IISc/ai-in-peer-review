This paper proposes a novel approach to meta-learning by interpreting the stochastic gradient descent (SGD) update rule as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters. The idea is original and important for research related to transfer learning.

The paper has a clear structure, but clarity could be improved at some points. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the proposed method, including the formulation of the meta-learning problem, the design of the LSTM-based meta-learner, and the training procedure. The experimental evaluation is also well-organized, with a clear description of the datasets, the baseline methods, and the results.

One of the strengths of the paper is its competitive results, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in few-shot learning tasks. The authors also provide a proper comparison to state-of-the-art methods, including Matching Networks, which is a well-established baseline in the field.

However, there are some areas that could be improved. The analogy between the SGD update rule and the gated recurrent model would be closer to Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs) than LSTMs. The description of the data separation in meta-sets is hard to follow and could be visualized to improve clarity. Additionally, the experimental evaluation is only partly satisfying, as the effect of the parameters of it and ft would be of interest. Figure 2 does not add much value to the paper and could be removed.

Minor errors were found in the text, such as a small typo in 3.2: "This means each coordinate has it" should be "its".

In terms of future work, the authors plan to release the code used in their evaluation experiments, which would be a major plus.

Decision: Accept

Reasons:

1. Originality and importance of the proposed approach to meta-learning.
2. Competitive results and proper comparison to state-of-the-art methods.

Supporting arguments:

* The proposed method is a novel interpretation of the SGD update rule as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters.
* The experimental evaluation demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method in few-shot learning tasks.
* The authors provide a thorough explanation of the proposed method and the experimental evaluation.

Additional feedback:

* Consider adding a visualization of the data separation in meta-sets to improve clarity.
* Investigate the effect of the parameters of it and ft on the performance of the proposed method.
* Remove Figure 2 as it does not add much value to the paper.

Questions for the authors:

* Can you provide more details on the initialization of the meta-learner LSTM and how it affects the performance of the proposed method?
* How do you plan to extend the proposed method to more challenging scenarios, such as few-shot learning with many classes?
