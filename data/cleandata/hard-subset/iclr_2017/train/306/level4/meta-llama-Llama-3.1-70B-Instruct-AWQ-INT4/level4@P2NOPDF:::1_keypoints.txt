Introduces an LSTM-based meta-learning framework to learn optimizers for neural networks; paper is well-written and clear.

Draws an intriguing parallel between Robbins–Monro updates and LSTM updates to balance fast task-specific learning and slower transferable learning.

Effectively reuses tricks from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) (parameter sharing, normalization) and proposes a well-motivated batch-normalization variant.

Experiments are convincing and demonstrate the framework’s effectiveness; overall a strong paper.

Concern: Using loss, gradient, and parameters as inputs to the meta-learner may be redundant—ablation studies are needed to analyze input components.

Suggestion: Explore whether other architectural components (e.g., neuron counts, unit types) can be meta-learned and discuss future research directions.

Related-work critique: Section is too narrow—should cite earlier meta-learning and learning-rule search work (Samy Bengio 1989; Bengio et al. 1994) and acknowledge relevant contributions by Schmidhuber.

Overall endorsement: Reviewer likes the paper and finds it relevant to a broad ICLR audience.
