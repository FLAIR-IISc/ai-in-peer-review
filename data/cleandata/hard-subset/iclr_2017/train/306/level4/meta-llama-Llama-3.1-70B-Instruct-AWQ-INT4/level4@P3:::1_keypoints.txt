- Overall: Well-written paper presenting an LSTM-based meta-learning framework to learn optimizers for neural network classifiers.  
- Problem: Tackles few-shot learning where classifiers must generalize from very few examples per class.  
- Method: Proposes an LSTM meta-learner that learns the optimization algorithm for training a learner network in the few-shot regime.  
- Motivation: Well-motivated and positioned in literature; addresses weaknesses of gradient-based optimization in few-shot settings.  
- Clarity: Clear explanation and detailed description of the model, including parameter sharing and preprocessing.  
- Implementation: Introduces a novel implementation of batch normalization for the approach.  
- Experiments: Convincing results on Mini-ImageNet showing competitiveness with deep metric-learning methods.  
- Related work: Provides a thorough related-work discussion and comparison with other meta-learning approaches.  
- Acceptance decision: Recommend accept based on strong contributions to meta-learning and few-shot learning.  
- Suggested ablation: Include ablative studies to evaluate the importance of meta-learner inputs (loss, gradient, parameters).  
- Architecture learning: Discuss potential for learning other architectural components (e.g., neuron counts, unit types).  
- Broader related work: Provide a broader survey of meta-learning approaches and deeper comparisons with other methods.  
- Questions: Clarify meta-learner LSTM initialization and its effect on training.  
- Questions: Explain plans to scale to harder scenarios (many classes or fewer examples per class).  
- Questions: Consider applying the approach to other domains such as reinforcement learning or NLP.
