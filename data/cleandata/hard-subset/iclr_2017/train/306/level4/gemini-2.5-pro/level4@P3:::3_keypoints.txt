- Problem addressed: few-shot learning via meta-learning that treats finding a good optimizer as a learning problem.  
- Core method: LSTM-based meta-learner whose cell state represents learner network parameters, motivated by a connection between LSTM updates and gradient descent.  
- Meta-training approach: train the meta-learner over many episodes to learn both an update rule and a task-agnostic parameter initialization for rapid adaptation.  
- Evaluation: tested on a self-recreated Mini-ImageNet benchmark, showing competitive 1-shot and superior 5-shot performance versus state-of-the-art (e.g., Matching Networks).  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: novel, well-motivated framework offering a fundamentally different alternative to metric-learning approaches.  
- Key reason 2: strong and convincing empirical results validating the approach.  
- Strength: elegant theoretical insight linking LSTM cell updates to gradient descent that motivates the architecture.  
- Strength: yields a different successful model family (ConvNet trained by a learned optimizer), increasing methodological diversity.  
- Strength: thorough experimental comparisons, including fine-tuning and a reproduction of Matching Networks.  
- Practical contributions: useful implementation details (parameter sharing, gradient/loss preprocessing, careful LSTM initialization) aiding reproducibility.  
- Main weakness: presentation is often dense and opaque, particularly the task description, reducing accessibility.  
- Suggested improvement: add a simple, concrete example (e.g., MNIST episode diagram) to clarify the meta-learning task and notation.  
- Suggested improvement: provide clearer experimental details, especially on Mini-ImageNet class splits and setup.  
- Suggested improvement: expand visualization/analysis (e.g., interpret gate-value plots) to better connect plots to learned optimization strategies.  
- Question 1 for authors: clarify how the learner's final softmax layer is structured—trained over all 64 classes or sized per episode (N classes sampled from 64)?  
- Question 2 for authors: clarify the BatchNorm handling choice (using batch stats for test sets during meta-training but running averages during meta-testing) and whether performance is sensitive to this choice.
