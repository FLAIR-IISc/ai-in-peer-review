- Introduces an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimization algorithm for a CNN learner for few-shot classification.  
- Draws an insightful parallel between LSTM cell-state updates and gradient descent to motivate the model design.  
- Meta-learner also learns a shared, optimal parameter initialization to enable rapid adaptation.  
- Evaluated on a recreated Mini-ImageNet and achieves performance competitive with or superior to metric-learning methods (e.g., Matching Networks).  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Major strength: novel and well-motivated reframing of few-shot learning by learning the optimizer rather than a metric.  
- Empirical results support the effectiveness and suggest a promising new research direction.  
- Paper offers practical training insights (parameter sharing, input preprocessing, initialization strategies) useful for follow-up work.  
- Main weakness: presentation is sometimes opaque, hindering accessibility for a broader audience.  
- Experimental description lacks detail (episode construction, class partitioning) making replication ambiguous.  
- Suggestion: add a simple, concrete example episode (e.g., 2-shot, 3-class MNIST) and a diagram to clarify Section 2.  
- Suggestion: expand interpretation of Figure 3 visualizations to explain what gate patterns indicate about the learned strategy.  
- Question: clarify Mini-ImageNet class splits—are episodes during meta-training drawn only from the 64 training classes, and how does the learner’s softmax size vary between meta-training and meta-testing?  
- Question: clarify Batch Normalization handling—are BN statistics for D_test computed from D_test batch during evaluation, and what is the motivation/implementation to avoid leaking test information?
