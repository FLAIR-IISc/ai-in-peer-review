- Presents a novel meta-learning approach: an LSTM-based meta-learner learns an optimization algorithm for a separate learner network in few-shot learning.  
- Core insight: analogy between LSTM cell-state updates and gradient descent enables learned initializations and bespoke update rules for rapid adaptation.  
- Evaluated on a recreated Mini-ImageNet and achieves competitive, state-of-the-art results, especially superior in the 5-shot setting.  
- Recommendation: accept, based on conceptual novelty and convincing empirical results.  
- Strength: Casting few-shot learning as a sequential/meta-learning problem is insightful and effective.  
- Strength: Strong empirical evaluation showing competitiveness with leading metric-learning methods (e.g., Matching Networks).  
- Strength: Uses a standard convolutional base learner rather than embedding/metric-based architectures, opening a distinct research direction.  
- Strength: Novel conceptual connection between LSTM updates and gradient descent motivates the model design.  
- Strength: Paper provides useful practical training details (initialization, parameter sharing, preprocessing) aiding reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Presentation is dense and difficult to parse, hindering accessibility.  
- Weakness: Section 2’s task description is overly abstract and would benefit from a simple concrete example and diagram.  
- Weakness: Several experimental protocol details—particularly the Mini-ImageNet setup—are unclear.  
- Weakness: Visualizations of LSTM gate values (Figure 3) are not informative and lack interpretation of discovered optimization strategies.  
- Suggestion: Revise for clarity and add a concrete toy example in Section 2 to improve accessibility.  
- Question for authors: During meta-training on Mini-ImageNet, does the learner’s final softmax have 64 outputs (meta-training classes) or 100 outputs, and how is the architecture adapted for unseen classes at meta-test time?
