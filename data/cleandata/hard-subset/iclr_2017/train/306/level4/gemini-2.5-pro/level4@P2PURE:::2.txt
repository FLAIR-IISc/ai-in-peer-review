This paper proposes a novel approach to meta-learning by interpreting the SGD update rule as a gated recurrent model with learnable parameters. The core idea is original and significant for research in transfer learning. While the paper is well-structured, its clarity could be enhanced in several key sections.

**Strengths:**
*   The paper presents a compelling and feasible approach to meta-learning.
*   The experimental results are competitive and are properly benchmarked against relevant state-of-the-art methods.
*   The work offers valuable recommendations for developing practical systems.

**Weaknesses:**
*   The analogy drawn to LSTMs is noted, but the proposed model architecture more closely resembles a Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU).
*   The description of how the data is partitioned into meta-sets is difficult to follow. A visualization or diagram would significantly improve the reader's understanding of this process.
*   The experimental evaluation is not fully comprehensive. An analysis of the effect of the input gate ($i_t$) and forget gate ($f_t$) parameters, for instance through an ablation study, would be of particular interest.
*   Figure 2 provides limited insight and could be removed or replaced with a more informative visualization.

**Additional Remarks:**
*   There is a minor typo in Section 3.2: "This means each coordinate has it" should be "...has its".
*   The authors' intention to release the code from their experiments is commendable and would be a significant contribution to the community.
