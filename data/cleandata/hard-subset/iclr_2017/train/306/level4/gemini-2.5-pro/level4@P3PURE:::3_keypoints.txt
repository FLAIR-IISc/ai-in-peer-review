- Reviewer raised score to 8 after authors' responsiveness and clarification of the meta-learning task.
- Paper formulates few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem: each meta-example is a sequence of training batches followed by a test batch.
- Meta-learner inputs include base-learner outputs (loss, gradients) and parameters; an LSTM is used to model parameter updates.
- Authors highlight an interesting similarity between LSTM memory-cell updates and gradient descent.
- LSTM meta-learner is trained to minimize the base learner's loss on the final test batch, with simplifying assumptions like coordinate-wise weight sharing.
- Recreated Mini-ImageNet and reports competitive or better results than Matching Networks on 1- and 5-shot tasks.
- Using a simple ConvNet base learner instead of nearest-neighbor is a notable methodological contribution.
- Paper offers practical design/training insights for LSTM meta-learners (initialization, weight sharing, normalizing loss/gradients).
- Weakness: writing is opaque and highly abstract; paper needs a simple concrete example or diagram for clarity.
- Weakness: experimental setup details are unclear (e.g., whether meta-training sees only 64 classes and how the softmax is structured).
- Weakness: Figure 2 is hard to interpret and its discussion lacks sufficient insight.
- Overall judgment: interesting and convincing work, strong candidate for acceptance if presentation and clarity are improved; score may be raised after revision.
