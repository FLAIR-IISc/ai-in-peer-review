- Presents an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimizer for another neural network learner.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents its contributions.  
- Core idea: connect Robbins–Monro and LSTM update rules to achieve fast adaptation and slow, transferable knowledge extraction.  
- Design choices (parameter sharing, normalization from Andrychowicz et al.) and a novel batch-normalization implementation are well-motivated.  
- Experimental results are convincing and support the paper’s claims.  
- Question: Is feeding loss, gradient, and parameters simultaneously to the meta-learner redundant—are ablation studies available?  
- Suggestion: Could the method be extended to learn architectural components (e.g., neuron counts, activation types)?  
- Related work is incomplete; should cite earlier learning-rule searches (Bengio 1989 PhD thesis, Bengio et al. 1994) and relevant work by Jürgen Schmidhuber.  
- Overall assessment: excellent paper with contributions of broad interest to ICLR.
