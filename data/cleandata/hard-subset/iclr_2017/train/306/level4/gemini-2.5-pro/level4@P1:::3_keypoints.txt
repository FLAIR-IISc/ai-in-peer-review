- Frames few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem where an LSTM meta-learner updates a base classifier’s parameters via its cell state, analogous to gradient descent.  
- Meta-learner receives base-learner losses and gradients at each inner step and proposes parameter updates; it is trained to minimize the base learner’s final test loss per task.  
- Uses design choices like parameter sharing across coordinates (a single learned update rule) and preprocessing of inputs (loss, gradients).  
- Achieves competitive or state-of-the-art results on Mini-ImageNet, notably outperforming Matching Networks in the 5-shot setting.  
- Novelty: learning an optimizer for a standard classifier is a fundamentally different and promising direction compared to prior metric-learning approaches.  
- Practical value: paper gives useful implementation/training details (meta-learner initialization, parameter sharing, input preprocessing) for replication.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reasons for acceptance: elegant and effective formulation, strong empirical results, and a new research direction.  
- Weakness — clarity and presentation: writing and abstract notation are opaque; recommend a simple concrete example (e.g., MNIST) and a diagram of a single episode.  
- Weakness — visualization: Figure 3’s gate-activation plots are hard to interpret and don’t clearly support claims; suggest more detailed analysis or better visualizations.  
- Question: Are the Mini-ImageNet class splits (64/16/20) entirely disjoint between meta-training, meta-validation, and meta-testing?  
- Question: If splits are disjoint, what is the size of the base learner’s final softmax during meta-training — 64 outputs for all training classes or N outputs per task (e.g., N=5)?
