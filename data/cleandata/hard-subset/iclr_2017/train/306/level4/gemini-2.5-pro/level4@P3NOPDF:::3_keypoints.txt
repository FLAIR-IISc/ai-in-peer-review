- Reviewer updated score to 8 (Accept) after authors' response and manuscript revisions clarifying the meta-learning setup.
- Paper frames few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning task: sequences of training batches followed by a test batch.
- Core model: LSTM meta-learner that processes inputs derived from a base learner (losses and gradients).
- Key idea: use LSTM cell state to represent and update base learner parameters, analogous to gradient descent.
- Simplifying assumptions include sharing meta-learner weights across all base-learner parameters (like a single learned learning rate).
- Meta-learner is trained by minimizing the base learner’s prediction loss on the final test batch of each sequence.
- Empirical results on recreated Mini-ImageNet show the LSTM meta-learner is competitive with or surpasses Matching Networks on 1-shot and 5-shot tasks.
- Strength: elegant, sequential formulation offering a significant conceptual advance in meta-learning for few-shot tasks.
- Strength: achieves strong empirical performance using a parametric ConvNet base learner, showing alternative viable approaches to non-parametric methods.
- Strength: novel theoretical insight connecting LSTM updates to gradient descent, motivating the architecture.
- Strength: useful practical guidance on implementation (parameter initialization, weight sharing, input normalization).
- Weakness: paper clarity and presentation are weak—Section 2 is abstract and uses dense notation; needs a simple concrete example and diagram.
- Weakness: experimental setup on Mini-ImageNet is ambiguous (64/16/20 split, class disjointness, final classifier outputs) and needs clarification for reproducibility.
- Weakness: figures (notably Figure 2) are hard to interpret and the discussion provides limited insight.
- Conclusion: technically strong and recommend acceptance pending improvements in presentation and clearer experimental details.
