- Reviewer increased score to 8 after authors' responsive clarifications, especially about the meta-learning task.
- Paper frames few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning task: sequences of training batches followed by a final test batch.
- Meta-learner input includes training loss, gradients, and current base-learner parameters at each step.
- Meta-learner is an LSTM that uses second-layer memory cells to represent and update base-learner parameters.
- Authors draw a novel connection between LSTM memory-cell updates and standard gradient-descent updates.
- LSTM meta-learner is trained to minimize the base-learner’s loss on the final test batch.
- Practical simplifications introduced (e.g., coordinate-wise weight sharing in second LSTM layer) analogous to a single learning rate.
- Experiments on Mini-ImageNet show competitive or superior results to Matching Networks on 1-shot and 5-shot tasks.
- Method achieves strong results using a simple ConvNet base learner, differing from embedding+nearest-neighbor approaches.
- Paper provides useful practical design choices and heuristics (initialization, weight sharing, input normalization) aiding reproducibility.
- Major weakness: manuscript presentation is opaque and often hard to follow.
- Section 2 is overly abstract and uses unfamiliar notation; a simple concrete example and diagram are needed for clarity.
- Experimental details for Mini-ImageNet are unclear (e.g., whether meta-training sees only 64 classes, and size of final softmax during meta-training).
- Figure 2 is difficult to interpret and the text discussion is limited.
- Conclusion: convincing and interesting work with strong empirical results, but acceptance contingent on improving manuscript clarity.
