- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimization algorithm for a separate "learner" network.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents core concepts.  
- Strong, compelling analogy between Robbins–Monro update rule and LSTM update dynamics motivates the approach.  
- Leverages this analogy to address few-shot learning trade-off: fast task-specific learning vs. slower extraction of general knowledge.  
- Makes well-motivated design choices (parameter sharing, input normalization) and introduces a tailored batch-normalization variant.  
- Empirical results are convincing and demonstrate method viability.  
- Question about input feature redundancy: need ablation studies to justify using loss, gradient, and previous parameters as inputs.  
- Suggests extending meta-learning scope to include architectural aspects (neurons, activations, connectivity) and requests discussion of feasibility/challenges.  
- Related-work section should better acknowledge foundational meta-learning literature (e.g., Bengio 1989 thesis; Bengio et al. 1994; works by Jürgen Schmidhuber).
