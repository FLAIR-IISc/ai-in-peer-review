This paper proposes a novel approach to meta-learning by framing the SGD update rule as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters. The idea is original and holds significant potential for research in transfer learning and related fields. While the paper is well-structured, certain sections could benefit from improved clarity.

**Strengths:**
*   The paper presents a novel and practical approach to meta-learning.
*   The experimental results are competitive and are appropriately benchmarked against state-of-the-art methods.
*   The authors provide valuable recommendations for implementing the proposed method in practical systems.

**Weaknesses:**
*   The analogy drawn to LSTMs seems slightly inaccurate; the proposed model more closely resembles a Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU).
*   The description of how the data is partitioned into meta-sets is difficult to follow. A diagram or visualization would greatly enhance clarity in this section.
*   The experimental evaluation is somewhat limited. A more thorough analysis, particularly an ablation study on the effects of the input gate (i_t) and forget gate (f_t) parameters, would be of significant interest.
*   Figure 2 provides limited insight and could potentially be omitted or replaced with more informative content.

**Additional Comments:**
*   A minor typo was found in Section 3.2: "This means each coordinate has it" should be "This means each coordinate has its".
*   The authors' plan to release the code used in their evaluation experiments is highly commendable and would be a valuable contribution to the community.
