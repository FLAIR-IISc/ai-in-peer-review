- Proposes a meta-learning framework where an LSTM is trained as an optimizer for a target network and connects LSTM updates to the Robbins–Monro stochastic approximation to enable fast task adaptation and slow transfer.

- Paper is clearly written with exceptionally clear presentation of core ideas.

- Novel conceptual framing: interpreting LSTM updates as Robbins–Monro updates provides a strong theoretical motivation for meta-learning.

- Well-motivated design choices, including parameter sharing, adapted input normalization, and a novel batch-normalization implementation.

- Empirical evaluation is thorough and provides convincing results on challenging few-shot benchmarks.

- Potential input redundancy concern: the meta-learner uses loss, gradient, and parameters as inputs—ablation studies are needed to determine each input's contribution.

- Suggests possible extension to learning network architecture aspects (hidden units, activations, connectivity) and asks authors to comment on adaptability to structural search.

- Related-work section is shallow on historical context; should cite seminal meta-learning works (e.g., Bengio 1989, Bengio et al. 1994, Schmidhuber’s body of work).

- Overall conclusion: strong, timely paper with a valuable theoretical connection and compelling experiments; addressing ablations and historical related work would improve it but do not undermine its merits.
