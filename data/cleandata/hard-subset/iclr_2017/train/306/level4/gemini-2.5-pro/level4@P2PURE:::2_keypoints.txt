- Proposes modeling SGD updates as a gated recurrent model with learnable parameters — an original and significant idea for meta-learning/transfer learning.
- Paper is generally well-structured but several sections lack clarity.
- Strength: Presents a compelling and feasible meta-learning approach.
- Strength: Experimental results are competitive and properly benchmarked against state-of-the-art.
- Strength: Offers valuable recommendations for building practical systems.
- Weakness: The analogy to LSTMs is misleading; the architecture more closely resembles a GRU.
- Weakness: Description of meta-set data partitioning is hard to follow; a diagram/visualization would help.
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is incomplete — e.g., missing ablation/analysis of input gate (i_t) and forget gate (f_t).
- Weakness: Figure 2 provides limited insight and should be removed or replaced with a more informative visualization.
- Additional remark: Minor typo in Sec. 3.2 ("it" should be "its").
- Additional remark: Authors’ intention to release code is commendable and would benefit the community.
