- Introduces an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimizer for few-shot classification.
- Paper is well-written and concepts are presented clearly.
- Central idea: equating gradient-descent updates with LSTM cell-state updates is well-motivated for few-shot learning.
- Effectively uses prior techniques (parameter sharing, gradient preprocessing) and introduces a tailored Batch Normalization for meta-learning.
- Experiments are convincing and show competitive performance, outperforming Matching Networks on 5-shot Mini-ImageNet.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Two main reasons for accept: novel/elegant learnable-optimization formulation and strong empirical validation.
- Strength: learns a task-agnostic parameter initialization (c0) and a rapid-update rule addressing few-shot challenges.
- Technical execution and implementation details are solid and comparisons use appropriate baselines.
- Suggestion: run an ablation study on meta-learner inputs (loss, gradient, parameters) to assess necessity.
- Suggestion: discuss potential to meta-learn other learner components (neurons, activations) and limitations.
- Suggestion: strengthen related work by adding historical context and connections to early work on learned synaptic update rules.
