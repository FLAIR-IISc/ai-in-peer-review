- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimization algorithm for a separate learner network in few-shot learning.  
- Draws an analogy between LSTM cell-state updates and gradient-based parameter updates to frame optimization as sequential learning.  
- Meta-learner learns a task-agnostic initialization for learner parameters and a fast, task-specific update rule for rapid convergence.  
- Evaluated on Mini-ImageNet, outperforming nearest-neighbor and fine-tuning baselines and competitive with Matching Networks.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novelty/significance: Learning the optimizer itself with an RNN is an innovative way to address low-data generalization.  
- Strong empirical validation: Results convincingly show practical viability and competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Conceptual framing: Separates slow across-task learning (meta-parameters/initialization) from fast within-task adaptation (unrolled updates).  
- Implementation quality: Builds on prior work (parameter sharing, input preprocessing) and thoughtfully handles BatchNorm in episodic training.  
- Strength: Offers a powerful alternative to metric-learning by targeting optimization rather than feature or distance design.  
- Suggested improvement: Expand related work to reference early meta-learning/update-rule research (e.g., Schmidhuber, Bengio et al.) and clarify distinctions.  
- Suggested improvement: Include an ablation study on meta-learner inputs (loss, gradient, parameters, previous learning rate) to justify input complexity.  
- Suggested improvement: Briefly discuss broader applications, e.g., whether the framework could learn architectural hyperparameters.  
- Question: Did the authors experiment with simpler meta-learner input variants (e.g., only gradient and loss)?  
- Question: Can the approach feasibly be extended to learn architectural aspects of the learner (hidden units, non-linearities)?
