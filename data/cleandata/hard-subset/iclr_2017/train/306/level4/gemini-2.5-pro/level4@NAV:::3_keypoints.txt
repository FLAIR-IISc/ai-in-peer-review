- Reviewer increased score to 8 after authors clarified the meta-learning task and revised the manuscript.
- Frames few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem: sequences of training batches followed by a test batch.
- Meta-learner inputs include base-learner outputs (training loss, gradients) and the base-learner’s current parameters.
- Uses an LSTM meta-learner, with second-layer memory cells directly modeling base-learner parameter updates.
- Notes a novel/interesting connection between LSTM memory updates and gradient descent update rules.
- Trains the LSTM by optimizing the base-learner’s prediction loss on the final test batch; uses simplifications like weight-sharing across second-layer cells (coordinate-wise).
- Reproduced Mini-ImageNet and achieved competitive or better results than Matching Networks on 1-shot and 5-shot benchmarks.
- Employs a ConvNet base learner (distinct from embedding + nearest-neighbor approaches), showing state-of-the-art performance via a different methodology.
- Provides practical, reproducible insights for designing/training LSTM meta-learners (initialization, coordinate-wise sharing, input normalization, simulating test conditions).
- Weakness: writing is occasionally opaque and the abstract task description needs a concrete example/diagram for clarity.
- Weakness: unclear experimental details about the N-class few-shot setup (which classes are seen during meta-training and the ConvNet softmax output dimensionality).
- Weakness: Figure 2 plots are not very informative and the discussion around them lacks insight.
