This paper proposes a novel approach to meta-learning by framing the SGD update rule as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters. The core idea is both original and significant for research in transfer learning. While the paper is clearly structured, the clarity of the presentation could be improved in several places.

**Strengths:**
*   The paper presents an interesting and practical approach to meta-learning.
*   The method achieves competitive results, and the authors provide a thorough comparison to state-of-the-art methods.
*   The paper offers valuable insights and recommendations for implementing the proposed system in practice.

**Weaknesses:**
*   The analogy drawn to LSTMs is somewhat imprecise; the described architecture more closely resembles a Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU).
*   The description of how the data is partitioned into meta-sets is difficult to follow. A diagram or visualization would significantly improve clarity on this point.
*   The experimental evaluation is not entirely comprehensive. In particular, an ablation study or analysis of the influence of the parameters *i*t and *f*t would be a valuable addition.
*   Figure 2 provides limited insight and could be replaced with a more informative visualization, such as the one suggested for the data partitioning.

**Minor Remarks:**
*   There is a small typo in Section 3.2: "This means each coordinate has it" should be "This means each coordinate has its".
*   The authors' stated intention to release the code used in the evaluation is highly commendable and would be a major contribution to the community.
