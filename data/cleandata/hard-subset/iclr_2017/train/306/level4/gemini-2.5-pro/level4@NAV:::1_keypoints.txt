- Introduces a meta-learning framework using an LSTM to learn an optimization algorithm for a target neural network.  
- Manuscript is well-articulated and concepts are presented clearly.  
- Draws a compelling parallel between the Robbins–Monroe update rule and LSTM updates.  
- Leverages that connection to separate fast task-specific learning from slower consolidation of transferable knowledge.  
- Provides strong motivation for design choices, adapting prior techniques (parameter sharing, normalization) and proposing a novel batch-normalization implementation.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and provides convincing support for the proposed framework.  
- Question: potential redundancy among meta-learner inputs (loss, gradient, parameters); requests ablation studies to test simpler input subsets.  
- Question: could the framework be extended to learn architectural aspects (e.g., hidden unit counts, activation types)?  
- Suggestion: literature review should acknowledge earlier foundational work on learning-to-learn (e.g., Samy Bengio 1989 thesis, Bengio et al. 1994, likely Schmidhuber contributions).  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality paper, supported for acceptance.
