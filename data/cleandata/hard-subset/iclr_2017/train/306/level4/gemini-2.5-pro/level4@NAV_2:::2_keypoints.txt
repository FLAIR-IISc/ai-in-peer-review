- Proposes a novel meta-learning method where an LSTM-based meta-learner outputs both an initialization and a sequence of parameter updates for a learner network.  
- Meta-learner is trained to optimize generalization after a fixed number of updates, achieving competitive results on Mini-ImageNet.  
- Recommendation: Accept, based on two main strengths: high originality/conceptual elegance and strong empirical results.  
- Conceptual insight: parallels between gradient descent and LSTM cell-state updates enable learning both initialization and update rules for few-shot learning.  
- Learned initialization (LSTM cell state) and learned input/forget gates provide data-dependent, sophisticated update dynamics tailored to low-data settings.  
- Empirical evidence: rigorous comparisons show significant gains over standard fine-tuning and outperform Matching Networks in 5-shot (competitive in 1-shot); authors plan to release code.  
- Clarify data splits: description of meta-training/meta-validation/meta-testing and episode construction is confusing—suggest adding a split/episode diagram.  
- Model analogy caveat: the proposed update rule more closely resembles a GRU than a standard LSTM; acknowledge this connection.  
- Ablation studies needed: analyze contributions of components (e.g., fix forget gate to 1, or use scalar learned learning rate) to quantify importance of learned dynamics.  
- Figure critique: Figure 2 (computational graph) is abstract/redundant; space could be better used for a data-split diagram.  
- Minor typo: Section 3.2 "it own" should be "its own."  
- Question 1: Clarify the role of the first standard LSTM layer in the described 2-layer LSTM and how it preprocesses inputs for the second modified layer.  
- Question 2: Explain potential mismatch from using batch statistics for both training and test sets during meta-training but running averages for the test set during meta-testing, and its impact on performance.
