- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimization algorithm for a separate learner in few-shot classification.  
- Cleverly parallels gradient descent updates with LSTM cell state updates.  
- Meta-learner jointly learns a good parameter initialization (c_0) and a task-specific update rule (input/forget gates) for rapid adaptation.  
- Provides detailed model architecture and practical implementation choices (parameter sharing, Batch Normalization).  
- Empirical results on Mini-ImageNet outperform strong baselines and are competitive with state-of-the-art metric-learning methods, especially in 5-shot.  
- Recommends acceptance based on novelty, elegance, and strong empirical evidence.  
- Key strength: reframes few-shot learning as learning an optimization algorithm, offering a principled, end-to-end approach to priors and adaptation.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing to fine-tuning, nearest-neighbors, and Matching Networks.  
- Constructive feedback: related work should cite foundational "learning to learn" research (e.g., Schmidhuber 1992/93; Bengio et al. 1990/95).  
- Constructive feedback: expand analysis of gate activation visualizations and correlate them with learner gradient properties.  
- Question for authors: Were ablation studies performed on meta-learner inputs (loss, gradient, previous parameters, previous gates)?  
- Question for authors: Can the meta-learning framework be extended to learn other aspects of the learner (architecture, regularization) for few-shot settings?
