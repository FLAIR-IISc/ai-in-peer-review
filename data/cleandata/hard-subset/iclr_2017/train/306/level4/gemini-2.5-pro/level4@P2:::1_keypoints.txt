- Introduces an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns optimizer dynamics and initial weights for few-shot classification.
- Frames gradient descent updates as analogous to LSTM cell state updates to enable rapid task adaptation.
- Evaluated on Mini-ImageNet, showing competitive or superior performance to state-of-the-art metric-learning methods.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Strength: novel and elegant formulation that addresses poor initialization and inefficient updates in few-shot learning.
- Strength: convincing empirical results with well-designed comparisons and strong 5-shot performance.
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and explains the LSTM-optimizer analogy and parameter sharing choices thoroughly.
- Experimental rigor includes appropriate baselines, careful BatchNorm handling, and gate-activation visualizations suggesting non-trivial learned behavior.
- Suggestion: add an ablation study to assess the contribution of meta-learner inputs (parameters, loss, gradient).
- Suggestion: clarify computational and memory costs of backpropagating through the unrolled optimization path.
- Question: Have you performed ablations to determine the relative importance or redundancy of the meta-learner inputs?
- Question: Any qualitative evidence that the learned forget gate helps escape poor local optima (e.g., ft << 1 correlated with high loss/small gradients)?
- Question: Could this meta-learning framework be extended to learn other learner aspects (architecture, activations) end-to-end?
