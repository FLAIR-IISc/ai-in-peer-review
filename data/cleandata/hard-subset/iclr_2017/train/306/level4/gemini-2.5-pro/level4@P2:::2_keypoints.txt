- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimizer by treating learner parameters as the LSTM cell state.  
- Learns both a shared initialization and task-specific update rule, trained end-to-end to minimize final test loss in few-shot settings.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on Mini-ImageNet, outperforming baselines and competitive with Matching Networks (notably in 5-shot).  
- Novel conceptual contribution: replaces hand-designed optimizers (SGD/Adam) with a learned, parameterized update rule tailored to few-shot learning.  
- Interprets LSTM gates as optimization components: input gate as dynamic learning rate, forget gate for decay/preservation, and c0 as learned initialization.  
- Evaluation compares against nearest-neighbor, fine-tuning, and Matching Networks, supporting the central claim that learning the optimizer is viable.  
- Analysis of why the method works is limited; gate-activation visualizations are qualitative and insufficient.  
- Lacks ablation studies isolating contributions of components (e.g., effect of fixing forget gate ft to 1).  
- Unclear how sensitive the meta-learner is to the fixed number of updates T at meta-test time; may be learning a trajectory for a specific horizon.  
- Batch normalization procedure differs between meta-training and meta-testing (batch stats vs running averages); motivation and impact need clarification.  
- Suggest acknowledging that the update rule resembles a GRU more than a standard LSTM for precision.  
- Meta-set partitioning description is text-heavy and would benefit from a diagram illustrating episode sampling.  
- Figure 2 is generic; recommend showing gradient flow in a meta-training step or removing it to save space.  
- Minor typo: "it's" should be "its" in Section 3.2.  
- Positive note: authors plan to release code, which will aid reproducibility.
