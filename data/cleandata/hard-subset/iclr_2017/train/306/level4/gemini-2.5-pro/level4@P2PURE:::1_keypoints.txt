- Paper proposes a meta-learning framework that uses an LSTM to learn an optimizer for another neural network.  
- Writing and presentation are clear.  
- Central idea: link between Robbins–Monroe update rule and LSTM updates to achieve fast task-specific learning and slower extraction of general knowledge.  
- Authors effectively motivate design choices, combining prior techniques (Andrychowicz et al., 2016) like parameter sharing and normalization with a novel batch-normalization implementation.  
- Experimental results are convincing and make the paper strong.  
- Concern 1: Possible redundancy in feeding loss, gradient, and parameters simultaneously to the meta-learner; no reported ablation studies.  
- Concern 2: Interest in whether architectural components (number of neurons, unit types) could also be learned—authors’ perspective requested.  
- Concern 3: Related-work section is shallow; should cite older/meta-learning work (e.g., Bengio 1989 thesis, Bengio/Bengio/Cloutier 1994 on genetic programming for learning rules, and relevant Schmidhuber contributions).  
- Overall recommendation: favorable—relevant and of broad interest to ICLR.
