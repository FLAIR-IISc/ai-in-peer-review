- Reviewer raised score to 8 after authors clarified the meta-learning task and revised the manuscript.

- Paper frames few-shot learning as meta-learning: an LSTM meta-learner learns an optimization algorithm for a separate base learner.

- Episodes are sequential: multiple training batches (support) followed by a query batch; meta-learner receives base learner losses and gradients.

- LSTM internal state is used to represent and update base learner parameters directly.

- Authors draw an analogy between LSTM updates and stochastic gradient descent, suggesting learned task-specific optimization.

- Practical simplifications include weight-sharing across parameters (like a single learning rate) and input normalization to make the model tractable.

- Achieves competitive or superior results to Matching Networks on 1-shot and 5-shot Mini-ImageNet.

- Novel formulation: elegant, natural sequential meta-learning perspective and training an LSTM as an optimizer.

- Strong empirical performance that advances few-shot learning benchmarks.

- Divergent approach: learns an optimization algorithm for a parametric classifier rather than relying on embedding + non-parametric classification, expanding research directions.

- Insightful connection between LSTM memory updates and gradient-based optimization.

- Practical contributions include useful implementation details (initialization, weight sharing, normalization) for replication.

- Main weakness: manuscript is dense, abstract, and often opaque, limiting accessibility.

- Lacks a concrete illustrative example and diagram (e.g., MNIST) to clarify the full meta-learning data flow.

- Ambiguity in experimental setup (unclear whether softmax during meta-training covers 64 training classes or all 100), hindering reproducibility.

- Insufficient analysis of results: Figure 2 is under-explained and its significance is unclear.

- Conclusion: strong candidate for acceptance; authors should improve clarity and provide more explicit experimental details to increase impact.
