- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimization algorithm for another neural network (the optimizee).
- Paper is well-written and ideas are presented clearly.
- Central insight: parallels Robbins-Monro updates with LSTM update mechanism to enable fast acquisition of new knowledge and slower extraction of transferable knowledge.
- Adapts prior techniques (parameter sharing, normalization) from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) and introduces novel design choices including a specific batch normalization implementation.
- Experimental results are convincing and support the paper’s claims.
- Concern 1: Inputs to the meta-learner (loss, gradient, parameters) may be redundant—ablation studies are needed to evaluate simpler input combinations.
- Concern 2: Could the meta-learning approach be extended to learn optimizee architectural components (e.g., neuron counts, activation types)? A discussion of such extensions is requested.
- Concern 3: Related work should include older seminal meta-learning literature (e.g., Samy Bengio’s 1989 thesis, Bengio et al. 1994 on genetic programming) and a more thorough review of Schmidhuber’s contributions.
- Overall: High-quality paper, relevant and of significant interest to the ICLR community.
