This paper proposes a novel approach to meta-learning by interpreting the Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) update rule as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters. The core idea is original and presents a significant contribution to research in optimization and transfer learning. While the paper is generally well-structured, its clarity could be enhanced in several key areas.

**Strengths:**

*   The central concept of framing an optimizer as a learnable recurrent model is an innovative and compelling direction for meta-learning research.
*   The empirical results are competitive. The authors provide a thorough comparison against relevant state-of-the-art methods, effectively positioning their work within the field.
*   The paper offers valuable insights and practical recommendations that could guide the implementation of similar meta-learning systems.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement:**

*   The analogy to LSTMs is helpful, but the proposed update mechanism appears to more closely resemble a Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU), particularly in how the input and forget gates are combined. Clarifying this relationship or further justifying the choice of the LSTM analogy would strengthen the paper's theoretical framing.
*   The description of how the data is partitioned into meta-sets for training and evaluation is difficult to follow. A diagram or figure illustrating this process—showing the splits across tasks and within each task—would greatly improve reader comprehension.
*   The experimental evaluation could be strengthened with a more detailed analysis of the learned optimizer. An ablation study exploring the specific impact of the model's parameters, particularly the input gate (`i_t`) and forget gate (`f_t`), would provide deeper insight into the meta-learner's behavior.
*   Figure 2, in its current form, offers limited insight. The authors could consider replacing it with a more informative visualization, such as the suggested diagram for the data separation, or removing it entirely.

**Minor Remarks:**

*   There is a small typo in Section 3.2: "This means each coordinate has it" should be corrected to "This means each coordinate has its".
*   The authors' plan to release the code from their experiments is commendable. This would be a significant contribution to the community, promoting reproducibility and enabling future work to build upon these findings.
