- Reviewer raised score to 8 after authors clarified the meta-learning task and responded to concerns.  
- Paper frames few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem: sequences of training batches followed by a test batch.  
- Inputs at each sequence step include base-learner outputs (loss, gradients) and the base-learner parameters.  
- Uses an LSTM meta-learner, with second-layer memory cells directly modeling updated base-learner parameters.  
- Key insight: similarity between LSTM memory updates and gradient-descent updates.  
- Meta-learner updates are driven by base-learner loss on the final test batch.  
- Simplifying assumptions include weight sharing across second-layer cells (analogous to a shared learning rate).  
- Recreated Mini-ImageNet and demonstrated competitive or better performance than Matching Networks on 1-shot and 5-shot tasks.  
- Base learner is a simple convolutional classifier, distinct from nearest-neighbor embedding approaches, yet achieves strong results.  
- Paper offers practical design/training tips for LSTM meta-learners (initialization, coordinate weight sharing, input normalization).  
- Main weakness was initial lack of clarity and opaque writing, requiring multiple readings and related-work consultation.  
- Task description and notation were overly abstract; a concrete example/diagram (e.g., MNIST) would aid accessibility.  
- Ambiguities remained about N-class few-shot setup details (e.g., whether only 64 classes used during meta-training and softmax architecture).  
- Figure 2 plots are hard to interpret and the discussion provides little explanation.  
- Conclusion: compelling and novel approach with convincing Mini-ImageNet results; authors addressed key issues and reviewer recommends acceptance.
