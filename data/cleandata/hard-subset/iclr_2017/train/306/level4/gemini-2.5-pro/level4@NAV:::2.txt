This work introduces a novel meta-learning method by re-casting the SGD update rule as a gated recurrent model with learnable parameters. The central idea is both original and a significant contribution to the field of transfer learning. While the paper is well-organized, certain sections would benefit from improved clarity.

Pros:
- The paper presents a compelling and viable new direction for meta-learning.
- The method demonstrates competitive performance and is thoroughly benchmarked against current state-of-the-art methods.
- The authors provide valuable insights for practical implementation.

Cons:
- The proposed model's analogy seems closer to a Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU) than the referenced LSTM.
- The explanation of how data is partitioned into meta-sets is difficult to follow and would be significantly clarified by a diagram.
- The experimental evaluation could be more thorough; an analysis of the specific impact of the `it` and `ft` parameters would be particularly insightful.
- Figure 2 offers limited informational value to the reader.

Remarks:
- A minor typo was found in Section 3.2: "This means each coordinate has it" should be "…has its".
- The authors’ intention to release the code for their experiments is highly commendable, as it would be a substantial asset for the community and enhance the work's impact.
