- Introduces an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimizer for a neural network; writing is clear and well-structured.  
- Core insight: parallels between Robbins–Monro updates and LSTM updates to enable fast acquisition and slower consolidation for few-shot learning.  
- Leverages prior techniques (parameter sharing, normalization) and introduces a novel batch-normalization variant; design choices are well-motivated.  
- Experimental results are convincing and strongly support the paper’s claims.  
- Primary concern 1 — Input redundancy: meta-learner uses loss, gradient, and parameters as inputs; an ablation is needed to justify all three.  
- Primary concern 2 — Scope extension: interesting to consider meta-optimizing architectural aspects (neurons, activations, depth); authors should discuss feasibility and challenges.  
- Primary concern 3 — Related work is too shallow given history; earlier meta-learning-for-optimization work should be cited and contextualized.  
- Specific missing references: Samy Bengio (1989 PhD “Learning to Learn”), Bengio/Bengio/Cloutier (1994) on genetic programming for learning rules, and relevant Jürgen Schmidhuber work.  
- Overall assessment: novel, technically sound, thorough experiments, minor refinements suggested; recommendation for acceptance.
