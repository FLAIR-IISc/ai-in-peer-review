

This paper introduces a novel meta-learning approach by framing the SGD update rule as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters. The core idea is original and represents a significant contribution to transfer learning research. The paper is well-structured, though some sections would benefit from improved clarity.

**Strengths:**
- The proposed method offers an interesting and practical approach to meta-learning.
- Results are competitive with state-of-the-art methods, supported by comprehensive comparisons.
- The paper provides valuable, actionable recommendations for real-world system implementation.

**Concerns:**
- The analogy to recurrent networks is more accurately aligned with GRUs than LSTMs; the paper should clarify this distinction.
- The description of meta-set data partitioning is unclear and would be greatly enhanced by a visual illustration.
- The experimental evaluation lacks sufficient depth, particularly regarding the impact of key hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate η and fine-tuning steps f_t).
- Figure 2 provides limited insight and could be omitted or significantly improved.

**Minor Notes:**
- Section 3.2 contains a typo: "each coordinate has it" should read "each coordinate has its."
- The authors mention plans to release code for evaluation experiments. This would be a significant advantage for reproducibility and community adoption.
