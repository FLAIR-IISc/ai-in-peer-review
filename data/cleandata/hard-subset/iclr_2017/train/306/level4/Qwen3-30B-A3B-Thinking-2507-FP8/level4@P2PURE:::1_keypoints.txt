- Introduces an LSTM-based meta-learning framework for learning optimization algorithms for neural networks.  
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation of main contributions.  
- Core insight: parallels between Robbins–Monro updates and LSTM updates to meet two few-shot desiderata—rapid acquisition of new knowledge and slower extraction of generalizable knowledge.  
- Uses parameter sharing and normalization techniques (reusing ideas from Andrychowicz et al., 2016) and a novel batch-normalization design; these choices are well-motivated.  
- Experimental results are compelling and support the approach.  
- Concern: possible redundancy in feeding loss, gradient, and parameters into the meta-learner; requests ablative studies to test simpler input combinations.  
- Suggestion: investigate whether other architectural components (e.g., number of neurons, unit types) can be learned in the same way.  
- Related work is somewhat superficial; reviewer asks to cite prior relevant work (Samy Bengio 1989 PhD thesis, Bengio et al. 1994 on genetic programming for update rules, and Schmidhuber’s prior work).  
- Overall assessment: compelling and relevant to a broad ICLR audience.
