- Introduces a novel meta-learning framework reinterpreting SGD updates as a GRU-like mechanism with learnable parameters—original and significant.  
- Conceptually innovative and technically sound, providing a principled unification of optimization and meta-learning.  
- Experimental results are competitive and well-compared to state-of-the-art across standard benchmarks.  
- Practical implementation recommendations are well-justified and useful for real-world deployment.  
- Planned code release will substantially improve reproducibility and community adoption.  
- Must correct the analogy: the mechanism resembles a GRU rather than an LSTM, since it lacks memory gates.  
- Meta-set data partitioning (task splits, support/query separation) is unclear and needs a visual schematic for clarity.  
- Lacks ablation studies on key hyperparameters (e.g., learning rates η and λ); include these analyses to assess impact.  
- Figure 2 provides little insight and should be omitted or significantly revised to visualize the core mechanism (e.g., parameter dynamics).  
- Minor typo in Section 3.2: change "each coordinate has it" to "each coordinate has its."  
- Overall promising work with strong empirical support; addressing clarity and experimental depth would elevate it to a high standard.
