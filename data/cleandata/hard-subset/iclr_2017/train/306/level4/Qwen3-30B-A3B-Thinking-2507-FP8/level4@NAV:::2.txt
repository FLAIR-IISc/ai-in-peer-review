

This paper proposes a novel meta-learning approach by reinterpreting the SGD update rule as a gated recurrent architecture with trainable parameters. The core idea is original and holds significant potential for transfer learning research. While the paper maintains a clear overall structure, certain sections would benefit from enhanced clarity.

Pros:
- Presents an innovative and implementable framework for meta-learning
- Achieves competitive performance with rigorous comparisons against state-of-the-art methods
- Provides actionable recommendations for practical system deployment

Cons:
- The analogy more accurately aligns with GRUs rather than LSTMs
- The description of meta-set data partitioning is difficult to follow and would be significantly improved with visual aids
- The experimental evaluation lacks completeness, particularly regarding parameter sensitivity analysis for θ and ϕ
- Figure 2 offers minimal substantive contribution

Remarks:
- Minor typo in Section 3.2: "This means each coordinate has it" should read "its"
> The planned release of evaluation code would constitute a major advantage for reproducibility and community adoption.
