- Clear, well-structured LSTM-based meta-learning framework.  
- Novel and compelling parallel drawn between Robbins–Monro stochastic approximation and LSTM updates.  
- Addresses few-shot requirements: rapid task adaptation and gradual extraction of transferable knowledge.  
- Integrates prior techniques (parameter sharing, normalization) and introduces a well-motivated batch normalization approach.  
- Robust experiments convincingly demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Overall high-quality contribution; recommendation for acceptance.  
- Concern: input redundancy—using loss, gradient, and parameters may be unnecessary; ablation studies (e.g., gradients alone or gradients+parameters) should be shown.  
- Concern: explore whether other architectural components (layer sizes, activations, depth) could be learned and discuss feasibility/benefits.  
- Concern: related work lacks historical depth—must cite foundational works (Bengio 1989 thesis; Bengio, Bengio & Cloutier 1994; early Schmidhuber meta-learning papers).
