

This paper introduces a novel meta-learning approach by modeling the SGD update rule as a gated recurrent network with trainable parameters. The core idea is original and holds significant potential for transfer learning research. While the paper maintains a clear structure, certain sections would benefit from improved exposition.

Pros:
- Presents an original and practically viable approach to meta-learning.
- Achieves competitive results with thorough comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.
- Offers well-founded recommendations for real-world system implementation.

Cons:
- The analogy is more analogous to GRUs than LSTMs due to the absence of a separate memory cell.
- The description of meta-set data partitioning is unclear and would be substantially improved with a visual aid.
- The experimental evaluation is incomplete, particularly regarding parameter sensitivity analysis for the learning rate (it) and fine-tuning (ft) procedures.
- Figure 2 provides limited insight and could be omitted without loss of clarity.

Remarks:
- Minor typo in Section 3.2: "This means each coordinate has it" → "its".
> The authors' plan to release code for evaluation experiments would constitute a significant advantage for reproducibility and community adoption.
