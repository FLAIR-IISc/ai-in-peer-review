- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that models parameter updates as a gated recurrent process to improve few-shot learning by learning both optimization strategy and initialization.  
- Core contribution: simultaneous meta-learning of task-specific update rules (adaptive gates) and a beneficial initialization for the learner network.  
- Well-motivated and clearly connected to prior meta-learning and optimization work; implementation is rigorous and empirical results are competitive, especially for 5-shot tasks.  
- Decision: Reject due to insufficient theoretical and empirical justification for core claims.  
- Main criticism: inadequate analysis of why the LSTM-based meta-learner outperforms baselines.  
- Architectural concern: model resembles a GRU more than an LSTM; choice of LSTM vs GRU appears arbitrary and needs ablation.  
- Gate-behavior issue: lack of quantitative analysis linking it/ft gate values to performance, loss regions, and differences between 1-shot and 5-shot tasks.  
- Visualization shortcoming: Figure 3 is descriptive but not diagnostic — doesn’t substantiate claims about learned update mechanisms.  
- Experimental omissions: no study on sensitivity to number of training updates vs testing updates.  
- Reproducibility concern: uses a custom Mini-ImageNet split without clarifying alignment with standard benchmarks (Vinyals et al.).  
- Suggested experiments: compare LSTM, GRU, and simpler alternatives; quantify gate correlations with task difficulty and performance.  
- Suggested clarifications: show meta-set partitioning figure and ablate the importance of learned initialization c0 vs random/pretrained weights.  
- Key author questions: how do c0 values compare to standard pretraining (ImageNet)? why initialize only input gate bias small instead of standard SGD-like gate biases? would task-specific fine-tuning improve 1-shot performance?  
- Final assessment: promising idea and solid implementation, but lacking analysis of learned behavior (gate dynamics and architectural choice); code release is positive but doesn’t replace needed analytical evidence.
