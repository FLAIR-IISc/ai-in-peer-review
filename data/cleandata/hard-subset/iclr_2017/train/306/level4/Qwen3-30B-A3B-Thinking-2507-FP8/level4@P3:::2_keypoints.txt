- Paper proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that generates adaptive parameter updates for few-shot classifiers, showing competitive results on Mini-ImageNet.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Fundamental flaw: model is mischaracterized as LSTM when it more closely resembles a GRU variant.  
- Mischaracterization undermines theoretical framing and literature placement (confuses with prior LSTM-based optimizers).  
- Specific architectural issues: gates are not constrained to sum to 1 and the structure resembles GRUs rather than standard LSTMs.  
- Lack of evidence that the claimed task-common initialization (c₀) is meaningfully better than random or pre-trained weights.  
- Insufficient analysis of gate behavior: the paper shows iₜ and fₜ variability but does not correlate gate dynamics with performance or task difficulty (e.g., 1-shot vs 5-shot).  
- No hyperparameter sensitivity or ablation studies (e.g., LSTM/GRU depth, preprocessing p), so robustness is unclear.  
- Constructive fix: explicitly state and justify the GRU-like design and reposition the contribution in related work.  
- Constructive fix: add ablations on c₀, correlate iₜ/fₜ with task difficulty and performance, and analyze hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Constructive fix: clarify meta-set partitioning with a visual diagram and improve Figure 2 to highlight parameter sharing.  
- Constructive fix: explain batch normalization handling during meta-testing and why running averages don't induce distribution shift.  
- Questions for authors: how does c₀ compare to ImageNet pretraining for convergence speed?  
- Questions for authors: why does fₜ show task-specific behavior and can you provide examples supporting the claimed forgetting dynamics?  
- Questions for authors: why doesn’t training for a fixed number of updates generalize to different update counts at test time?  
- Overall: promising idea but technical misrepresentation and incomplete empirical validation prevent acceptance without substantial revision.
