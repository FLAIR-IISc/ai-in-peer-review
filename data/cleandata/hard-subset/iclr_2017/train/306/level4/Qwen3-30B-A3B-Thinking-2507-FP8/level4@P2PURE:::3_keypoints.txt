- Reviewer raised score to 8 after authors clarified the meta-learning task and revised the manuscript.  
- Paper frames few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem: sequences of training batches followed by a test batch, with base-learner outputs and parameters fed to a meta-learner LSTM.  
- LSTM second-layer memory cells are used to directly model/update base-learner parameters, with an analogy drawn to gradient descent updates.  
- Meta-learner training optimizes the base learner’s prediction loss on the final test batch; assumes weight sharing across second-layer cells (uniform learning rate).  
- Authors recreated Mini-ImageNet and report competitive state-of-the-art results on 1-shot and 5-shot tasks, outperforming Vinyals et al. (2016).  
- Using a simple ConvNet base learner (vs. matching networks’ embedding + nearest neighbor) makes the strong results more compelling and methodologically novel.  
- The LSTM–gradient-descent insight is novel and of independent interest.  
- Paper gives useful practical guidance for training LSTM meta-learners (initialization, weight sharing, input normalization, simulating test conditions).  
- Writing and presentation are often opaque; section 2 uses non-standard notation, making the paper hard to follow without multiple readings.  
- The exact N-class setup for Mini-ImageNet (which classes are observed during meta-training and softmax size) is unclear and missing critical experimental details.  
- Figure 2’s plots are uninformative and the accompanying discussion lacks insight.  
- Overall: interesting and convincing results, but presentation needs substantial improvement for clarity and accessibility.
