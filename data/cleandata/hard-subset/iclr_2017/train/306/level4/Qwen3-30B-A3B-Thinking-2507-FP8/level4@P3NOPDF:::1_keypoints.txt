- Introduces an LSTM-based meta-learning framework linking Robbins–Monro updates to LSTM dynamics to enable fast task adaptation and gradual extraction of transferable knowledge.  
- Core insight is well-motivated and elegantly executed; presentation and writing are clear.  
- Experimental results convincingly demonstrate effectiveness; reuse of parameter sharing/normalization is justified and the batch-normalization implementation adds value.  
- Concern 1 — Input redundancy: unclear if loss, gradient, and parameters are all necessary; ablation studies (e.g., loss+gradient or gradient-only) are needed.  
- Concern 2 — Architectural learning: authors should discuss feasibility and challenges of extending the framework to learn network architecture components (neurons, layers, activations).  
- Concern 3 — Related work depth: must include and discuss seminal prior work on “learning to learn” (Bengio 1989 PhD, Bengio et al. 1994, Schmidhuber early 1990s) and substantially expand the related-work section.  
- Overall: strong, relevant contribution with recommendation to accept pending these minor revisions.
