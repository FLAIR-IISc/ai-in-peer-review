- Novel LSTM-based meta-learner that parallels Robbins–Monro updates to LSTM updates.  
- Addresses few-shot needs: fast task adaptation and extraction of transferable knowledge.  
- Learns both optimal parameter update rules for a fixed number of steps and a beneficial parameter initialization.  
- Well-motivated critique of standard gradient-based optimization in few-shot settings.  
- Thoughtful implementation details, notably batch normalization adapted for meta-learning.  
- Recommendation to accept: technically sound and novel approach.  
- Strong experimental results: outperforms natural baselines and is competitive with state-of-the-art metric learning (notably Matching Networks in 5-shot).  
- Clear theoretical foundation, well-executed implementation, logical structure, and rigorous presentation.  
- Suggestion: include ablation studies to confirm necessity of inputs to the meta-learner (loss, gradient, parameters).  
- Suggestion: explore meta-learning of other architectural components (e.g., neuron counts or unit types).  
- Suggestion: expand related work to acknowledge earlier meta-learning/learning-to-learn work (Bengio 1989 PhD, Bengio et al. 1994 genetic programming, Schmidhuber’s prior contributions).
