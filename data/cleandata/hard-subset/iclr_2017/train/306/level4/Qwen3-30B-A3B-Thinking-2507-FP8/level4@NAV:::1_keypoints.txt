- Introduces an LSTM-based meta-learning framework to learn an optimizer for neural networks.  
- Paper is well-written and contributions are clearly presented.  
- Main contribution: draws a parallel between Robbins–Monro updates and LSTM updates to address rapid adaptation and gradual transfer in few-shot learning.  
- Reuses techniques from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) (parameter sharing, normalization) and introduces justified novel design choices (e.g., specific batch normalization).  
- Experimental results are convincing and support the claims.  
- Concern: inputting loss, gradient, and parameters to the meta-learner may be redundant; ablation studies needed to justify inputs.  
- Concern: could other architectural components (number of neurons, unit types) be learned similarly—authors’ viewpoint on this extension is missing.  
- Concern: related work section is superficial and omits earlier relevant work (Samy Bengio 1989 PhD thesis; Bengio, Bengio & Cloutier 1994 on genetic programming for optimizers; Schmidhuber’s related work).  
- Overall assessment: relevant and impactful for a broad ICLR audience.
