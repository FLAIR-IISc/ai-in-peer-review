- Reviewer raises score to 8 after clarified meta-learning task.  
- Paper frames few-shot meta-learning as a sequential optimization problem: sequences of training batches followed by a test batch.  
- Meta-learner is an LSTM that ingests base-learner outputs and state; second-layer memory cells model parameter updates analogous to gradient descent.  
- Meta-learner weights are trained using base-learner test loss; weight sharing across memory cells enforces a uniform learning-rate-like behavior.  
- Evaluated on a recreated Mini-ImageNet, achieving competitive results with Matching Networks on 1-shot and 5-shot tasks using a simple ConvNet base learner.  
- Strength: sequential/meta formulation is insightful and extends existing paradigms beyond prior persistent-learning work.  
- Strength: demonstrates state-of-the-art-level performance can come from fundamentally different architectures (ConvNet classifier vs. embedding+nearest-neighbor).  
- Strength: novel, theoretically compelling connection between LSTM memory updates and gradient-descent-style updates.  
- Strength: provides practical, detailed implementation guidance (initialization, weight sharing, input normalization, meta-training simulation) valuable for replication.  
- Weakness: exposition is often opaque; abstract notation in Section 2 needs intuitive grounding and concrete examples/diagrams.  
- Weakness: Mini-ImageNet experimental setup is unclear—ambiguous whether meta-training sees 64 classes total or 64 classes per batch, affecting interpretation.  
- Weakness: Figure 2 plots are minimally informative and lack contextual discussion.  
- Overall: strong paper with sound methodology and compelling results; will accept if clarity issues (task definition/exposition) are addressed in final version.
