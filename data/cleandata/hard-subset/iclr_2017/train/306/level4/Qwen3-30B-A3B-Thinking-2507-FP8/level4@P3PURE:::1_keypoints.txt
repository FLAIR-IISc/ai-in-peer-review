- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimizer for neural networks.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear presentation of the core material.  
- Main contribution: draws a parallel between Robbins–Monro updates and LSTM updates to achieve fast task-specific learning and slower extraction of transferable knowledge.  
- Reuses Andrychowicz et al. (2016) techniques (parameter sharing, normalization) and introduces a well-motivated batch-normalization design.  
- Experimental results are convincing, supporting the method's effectiveness.  
- Concern — Input redundancy: unclear if providing loss, gradient, and parameters to the meta-learner is redundant; ablation studies on input combinations are needed.  
- Concern — Architectural flexibility: authors should discuss or explore whether meta-learning can also discover architectural components (number of neurons, unit types, etc.).  
- Concern — Related work: section is shallow and should cite earlier meta-learning literature, specifically Bengio (1989 PhD thesis), Bengio et al. (1994) on evolving learning rules, and Schmidhuber’s learning-to-learn work.
