- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that directly learns optimization algorithms for classifier networks in few-shot learning.  
- Frames few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning task where the meta-learner processes gradients and losses from the base learner to produce parameter updates.  
- Main contribution: modeling learner parameter updates via LSTM cell states, enabling learning of task-specific update rules and a favorable classifier initialization.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance on Mini-ImageNet for 1-shot and 5-shot classification, matching Matching Networks and outperforming baselines.  
- Recommendation: Accept — novel perspective, technically sound link between LSTM updates and gradient descent, and validated experimentally.  
- Establishes that LSTM gates (it and ft) can learn adaptive learning rates and forgetting behavior, explicitly capturing gradient-based optimization.  
- Shows meta-learned optimization can handle few-shot classification without metric learning or embedding spaces.  
- Ablation studies indicate training the meta-learner for the target number of updates (12 for 1-shot, 5 for 5-shot) is crucial for performance.  
- Visualization (Fig. 3) provides evidence that the learned update rules vary meaningfully across tasks.  
- Suggested improvement: add a concrete illustrative example (e.g., MNIST) in Section 2 to improve accessibility.  
- Suggested improvement: clarify Mini-ImageNet setup — whether 64/16/20 classes are disjoint splits and the softmax dimensionality used (64 vs. 100).  
- Suggested improvement: enhance Figure 2 with annotations explaining the computational graph’s relevance to meta-optimization.  
- Clarification requested: state the fixed number of updates used for the "Baseline-finetune" baseline for 1-shot and 5-shot and how that compares to the meta-learner’s update counts.  
- Clarification requested: confirm whether the meta-learner’s internal update count is fixed per task (e.g., 12 for all 1-shot tasks) and discuss implications for generalization to tasks with different update requirements.
