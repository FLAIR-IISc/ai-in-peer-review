- Reviewer raised score to 8 after authors clarified the meta-learning task in revisions.  
- Paper frames few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem with sequences of training batches followed by a test batch.  
- Inputs to the meta-learner include the base learner’s outputs (loss, gradients) and current parameters; an LSTM models the meta-learning process.  
- Second-layer LSTM memory cells are used to represent base-learner parameter updates, drawing a parallel to gradient descent.  
- Meta-learner updates are driven by the base learner’s loss on the test batch.  
- Key simplifications include weight sharing across second-layer cells (akin to a uniform learning rate).  
- Authors recreated the Mini-ImageNet setup for experiments.  
- Results: LSTM meta-learner is competitive with, and outperforms, Matching Networks on 1- and 5-shot Mini-ImageNet.  
- Contribution: achieves strong results using a simple ConvNet base learner rather than nearest-neighbor embeddings.  
- Novel insight: linking LSTM memory-cell updates to gradient-descent-like behavior.  
- Paper offers practical replication guidance (initialization, weight sharing, normalization, simulating test conditions).  
- Weakness: writing is sometimes opaque and uses abstract notation; a concrete example and diagram would aid accessibility.  
- Weakness: ambiguity in Mini-ImageNet setup (whether meta-training uses only 64 classes or all 100), affecting base-learner architecture interpretation.  
- Weakness: Figure 2’s plots lack clear explanations and discussion of significance.  
- Overall: technically sound and well-motivated work, but presentation must be clarified for the reviewer to retain the updated score.
