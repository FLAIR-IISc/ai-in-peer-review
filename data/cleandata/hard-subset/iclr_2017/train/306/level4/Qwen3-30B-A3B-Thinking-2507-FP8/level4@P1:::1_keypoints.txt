- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns both an optimization algorithm and a task-agnostic initialization for few-shot classification.  
- Core insight: LSTM update structure parallels gradient-based optimization, enabling joint learning of initialization and task-specific update trajectories.  
- Claims to address two few-shot challenges: rapid adaptation from few examples and a generalizable initialization across tasks.  
- Introduces novel technical elements: task-specific gate dynamics and a batch-normalization strategy tailored for meta-testing.  
- Strong empirical validation on Mini-ImageNet (1-shot and 5-shot) with rigorous ablations and multiple experimental configurations.  
- Ablation studies highlight the necessity of key design choices, notably explicit update count matching.  
- Demonstrates the meta-learner learns adaptive update rules (via gate values) rather than a fixed optimizer.  
- Experimental protocol matches meta-learning conventions and training mirrors test-time conditions (Algorithm 1).  
- Results: outperforms coarse baselines (e.g., fine-tuning) and is competitive with state-of-the-art metric-learning methods; less overfitting in 1-shot settings.  
- Practical implementation strengths: justified parameter sharing (Andrychowicz et al.), clear gradient preprocessing, and thoughtful batch-norm adaptation to prevent dataset leakage.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant contribution bridging optimization theory and few-shot practice.  
- Suggested revision 1: clarify meta-learner inputs with ablations comparing gradients-only vs. full inputs.  
- Suggested revision 2: discuss how framework could extend to learning architectural choices (depth, activations).  
- Suggested revision 3: deepen related work by citing Bengio (1993) and Schmidhuber (1992) on learned update rules.  
- Two clarifying questions: (1) is the current loss L_t used to guide update direction or mainly for adaptive learning-rate control? (2) how does the learned task-common initialization c0 interact with task-specific updates—is it reset per task or used as a starting point?
