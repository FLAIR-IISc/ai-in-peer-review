

This paper introduces a novel meta-learning approach by interpreting the SGD update rule as a gated recurrent network with trainable parameters. The core idea is original and holds significant potential for transfer learning research. The paper is well-structured, though some sections would benefit from enhanced clarity.

**Strengths:**
- The proposed approach is both conceptually interesting and practically feasible.
- The results are competitive, with adequate comparison to state-of-the-art methods.
- The paper provides valuable practical recommendations for system implementation.

**Weaknesses:**
- The analogy more closely aligns with Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs) than Long Short-Term Memory (LSTMs), as the mechanism lacks the gating complexity of LSTMs.
- The description of data separation within meta-training and meta-testing sets is difficult to follow and would be significantly improved with a visual illustration.
- The experimental evaluation is partially unsatisfying; the sensitivity analysis of key parameters (θ and φ) is insufficiently explored.
- Figure 2 provides limited insight and does not substantially contribute to the paper's core arguments.

**Additional Notes:**
- Typographical error in Section 3.2: "This means each coordinate has it" should read "its".
- The authors' plan to release the code used in the evaluation experiments would constitute a significant benefit to the community.
