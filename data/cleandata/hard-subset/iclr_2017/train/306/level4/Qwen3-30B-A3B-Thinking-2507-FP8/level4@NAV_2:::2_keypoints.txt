- Novel LSTM-based meta-learner framing gradient-based optimization as a gated recurrent process that learns task-specific update rules and an optimal initialization, showing competitive 1-shot and 5-shot results on Mini-ImageNet.  
- Recommendation: Accept — well-motivated problem, technically sound meta-learning framework, competitive empirical results and solid theoretical grounding.  
- Significant contribution: systematically tackles task-specific optimization under a fixed update budget and optimal initialization; LSTM captures short-term task dynamics and long-term cross-task knowledge with rigorous implementation choices.  
- Implementation/training strengths: parameter sharing, gradient preprocessing, batch-norm handling are well-justified; training mirrors test-time; gradient independence assumption noted as a pragmatic simplification.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify the LSTM vs GRU analogy, visualize gate (it/ft) dynamics across datasets, add sensitivity analysis for gate parameters, replace Figure 2 with a clearer computational graph, and fix the typo in Section 3.2.  
- Questions for authors: how performance changes with fixed vs flexible update counts; whether the learned initialization generalizes to other architectures; computational overhead vs standard fine-tuning; applicability to non-image few-shot tasks (text, tabular).
