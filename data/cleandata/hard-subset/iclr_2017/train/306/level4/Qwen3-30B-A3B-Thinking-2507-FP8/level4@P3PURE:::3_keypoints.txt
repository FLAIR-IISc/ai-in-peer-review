- Reviewer raised score to 8 after authors' responsiveness and manuscript updates.  
- Frames few-shot learning as sequential meta-learning: each example = sequence of training batches then a test batch; meta-learner (LSTM) receives base-learner outputs (loss, gradients) and parameters.  
- Uses second-layer LSTM memory cells to represent updated base-learner parameters and notes parallels between LSTM updates and gradient descent.  
- Meta-learner is trained using the base-learner’s prediction loss on the final test batch.  
- Key simplifications: weight sharing across second-layer cells (akin to a uniform learning rate) and normalization of loss/gradient/parameter inputs.  
- Strong empirical results on Mini-ImageNet, outperforming Matching Networks in 1- and 5-shot settings.  
- Strength: sequential formulation is natural and advances the state of the art, offering a complementary approach to embedding-based methods.  
- Strength: novel, conceptually interesting insight linking LSTM memory updates to gradient descent.  
- Strength: provides practical, actionable implementation guidance (initialization, weight sharing, input normalization) that aids replication.  
- Weakness: writing and notation are often opaque (Section 2 especially); a concrete example/diagram would improve clarity.  
- Weakness: important methodological details are ambiguous (N-class setup, whether meta-training sees 64 vs 100 classes, softmax output size, loss function, data splits).  
- Weakness: Figure 2’s plots lack context and interpretation, reducing informativeness.  
- Recommendation: accept contingent on clarifying methodological details and substantially improving exposition.
