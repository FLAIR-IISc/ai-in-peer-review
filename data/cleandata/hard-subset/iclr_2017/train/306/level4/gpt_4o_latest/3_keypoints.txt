- Reviewer raised score to 8 after authors clarified the meta-learning task.  
- Paper frames few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem with sequences of training batches and a final test batch.  
- Each meta-step inputs base-learner outputs (loss, gradients) and base-learner parameters; an LSTM meta-learner updates the base learner.  
- LSTM second-layer memory cells are used to explicitly model updated base-learner parameters, drawing parallels to gradient descent.  
- The meta-learner is trained using the base learner’s prediction loss on the final test batch.  
- Simplifying assumptions include weight-sharing across second-layer cells (analogous to a uniform learning rate).  
- Experiments replicate Mini-ImageNet and show competitive or superior performance to Matching Networks on 1-shot and 5-shot tasks.  
- The base learner is a simple ConvNet classifier, differing fundamentally from nearest-neighbor-on-embedding approaches.  
- Novel and compelling insight: relation between LSTM memory cells and gradient-descent-style updates.  
- Paper provides practical, reproducible insights for designing/training LSTM meta-learners (initialization, weight sharing, input normalization/rescaling).  
- Reviewer unsure about definitive novelty due to not being fully versed in all recent meta-learning work.  
- Writing is dense and difficult to follow; task description and notation are abstract and need clearer exposition and examples/diagrams.  
- Key experimental/task formulation details (e.g., how N-class few-shot and Mini-ImageNet class splits and softmax outputs are handled) are ambiguous.  
- Plots in Figure 2 are not informative and lack adequate discussion.  
- Conclusion: strong paper with compelling results and a clear accept, but presentation needs significant improvement for broader accessibility.
