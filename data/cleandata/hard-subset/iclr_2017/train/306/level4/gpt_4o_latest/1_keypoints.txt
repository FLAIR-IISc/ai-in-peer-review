- Introduces an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns the optimization algorithm for another learner (a neural network).  
- Manuscript is well-written with a clear presentation of core material.  
- Central idea is compelling: analogy between Robbins–Monro updates and LSTM updates to address few-shot learning’s needs (fast adaptation + gradual generalization).  
- Incorporates prior techniques (Andrychowicz et al. 2016) like parameter sharing and normalization, plus novel design choices (specific batch-norm implementation).  
- Design choices are well-justified and thoughtfully integrated.  
- Experimental results are persuasive and support the paper’s claims.  
- Question: Using loss, gradient, and parameters as inputs to the meta-learner may be redundant—request ablation studies to test simpler input combinations.  
- Question: Could other architectural components (e.g., neuron counts, unit types) be learned similarly—asks for thoughts or preliminary insight.  
- Concern: Related work section is superficial and should better cite earlier meta-learning efforts (e.g., Samy Bengio 1989 PhD, Bengio et al. 1994 genetic programming, and relevant work by Jürgen Schmidhuber).  
- Overall conclusion: strong, valuable contribution likely to resonate with the ICLR audience.
