- Presents an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimization algorithm for another neural network.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear presentation of core material.  
- Novel idea: parallels Robbins–Monro update rule with LSTM updates to address few-shot needs (rapid task-specific learning and slower generalizable learning).  
- Reuses techniques from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) (parameter sharing, normalization) and introduces a motivated batch normalization design.  
- Experiments are convincing; method achieves competitive performance on Mini-ImageNet, particularly in the 5-shot setting.  
- Visualization of the learned optimization strategy provides preliminary insight into meta-learner behavior.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main acceptance reasons: novelty of framing optimization as an LSTM-learned problem and demonstrated competitiveness addressing data inefficiency.  
- Argues standard gradient-based optimization fails in few-shot due to poor initialization and suboptimal updates; meta-learner learns task-common initialization and adaptive updates.  
- Practical implementation choices (batch normalization, parameter sharing) are thoughtful and well-considered.  
- Suggestion: Ablation studies to test whether loss, gradient, and parameters are all necessary inputs to the meta-learner.  
- Suggestion: Explore or discuss extending meta-learning to architectural components (e.g., neuron counts, unit types).  
- Suggestion: Expand related work to include historical meta-learning literature (e.g., Samy Bengio 1989, Bengio et al. 1994 genetic programming, Schmidhuber’s work).  
- Question: Clarify impact and potential failure modes of the gradient independence assumption from Andrychowicz et al. (2016).  
- Question: Provide quantitative analysis of LSTM gate values (e.g., average forget gate across datasets and updates).  
- Question: Justify the preprocessing choice p = 10 and analyze sensitivity/theoretical basis for this parameter.
