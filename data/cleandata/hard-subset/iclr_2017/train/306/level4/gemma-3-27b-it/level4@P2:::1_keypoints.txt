- Introduces an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimizer for another network by paralleling Robbins–Monroe and LSTM updates.  
- Targets two few-shot learning challenges: rapid acquisition of new knowledge and slower extraction of transferable knowledge.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents the core ideas.  
- Combines techniques from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) (parameter sharing, normalization) with a novel, well-motivated batch normalization implementation.  
- Experimental results are convincing, showing strong performance on Mini-ImageNet and competitive comparisons to Matching Networks.  
- Practical considerations like gradient clipping and the specific batch normalization strategy improve robustness and applicability.  
- Decision: Accept — novel and effective approach with solid empirical support and relevance to ICLR.  
- Suggestion: Perform ablation studies to assess the relative importance of loss, gradient, and parameter inputs to the meta-learner.  
- Suggestion: Explore whether architectural components (e.g., neuron counts, activation types) could also be learned within this meta-learning framework.  
- Suggestion: Broaden related work to include earlier meta-learning literature (e.g., Bengio 1989 thesis, Bengio et al. 1994, Schmidhuber’s work) for historical context.  
- Question: Did you investigate including second-order gradient contributions (rather than assuming gradient independence) and their effect on performance?  
- Question: What dataset characteristics appear to drive the observed variability in the meta-learner’s input gate values?  
- Question: For the fine-tuned baseline that underperformed nearest-neighbor, did you try varying the number of fine-tuning updates and what were the results?
