- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimizer for another neural network.  
- Paper is well-written and presents the core material clearly.  
- Central insight: parallels between Robbins–Monroe updates and LSTM updates to tackle few-shot learning (fast adaptation + slower generalization).  
- Effectively integrates parameter sharing and normalization, with a novel specific batch normalization design.  
- Experimental results are convincing and demonstrate the method’s promise.  
- Overall a strong submission with potential to stimulate further work; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Concern: possible input redundancy—the meta-learner uses loss, gradient, and parameters; ablation studies are needed to assess each input’s contribution.  
- Question: potential to extend approach to architectural meta-learning (e.g., neuron counts, activation types) and discussion of feasibility/benefits.  
- Related-work coverage is limited; reviewer suggests adding earlier foundational work (Bengio’s 1989 thesis, Bengio & Cloutier 1994 on genetic programming) and citing Jürgen Schmidhuber’s learning-to-learn contributions.
