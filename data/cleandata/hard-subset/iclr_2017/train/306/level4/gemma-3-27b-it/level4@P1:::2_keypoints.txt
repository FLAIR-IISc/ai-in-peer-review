- Novel idea: frame SGD updates as a gated recurrent model (LSTM) with trainable parameters — original and valuable for transfer learning.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Tackles few-shot learning with a well-motivated, technically sound LSTM-based meta-learner.  
- Experimental results on Mini-ImageNet outperform strong baselines and are competitive with Matching Networks.  
- Meta-learner jointly learns a task-common initialization and an adaptive optimization strategy for few updates.  
- Ablation study is limited but gate-value visualizations provide some insight into learned behavior.  
- Paper is well-structured overall but clarity could be improved in specific areas.  
- Suggest expanding on the GRU analogy and explicitly justify choosing LSTM over GRU.  
- Request clearer visualization/diagram of meta-training, meta-validation, and meta-testing set construction.  
- Recommend deeper analysis of learned it and ft gate parameters to identify consistent weight patterns across tasks.  
- Suggest removing Figure 2 as it adds little beyond the text explanation.  
- Typo: Section 3.2 “it” should be “its.”  
- Question: clarify the simplifying assumption about meta-learner gradients and its potential impact; any validating experiments?  
- Question: differences in learned optimization strategies between separately trained 1-shot and 5-shot meta-learners?  
- Question: why does the fine-tuned baseline perform worse than nearest neighbor; could this indicate sensitivity to overfitting?  
- Question: have you explored fine-tuning the meta-learner at test time; results or challenges?
