- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns the optimization algorithm for another learner (a neural network).
- Paper is globally well-written with clear presentation of core material.
- Central idea: draw a parallel between Robbins–Monroue update and LSTM updates to satisfy two few-shot desiderata—rapid acquisition of new knowledge and slower extraction of transferable knowledge.
- Reuses techniques from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) (parameter sharing, normalization) and introduces well-motivated novel choices, notably batch normalization.
- Experiments are convincing and overall the paper is strong.
- Concern: Is providing loss, gradient, and parameters as inputs to the meta-learner necessary; were ablation studies run to test simpler input combinations?
- Question: Could other architectural components (number of neurons, unit types) be learned similarly; any experiments or thoughts?
- Issue: Related work is shallow—should cite earlier meta-learning/learning-rule search work (e.g., Samy Bengio 1989 PhD thesis; Bengio et al. 1994 genetic programming; Schmidhuber’s contributions).
- Recommendation: Accept; relevant to a broad ICLR audience.
