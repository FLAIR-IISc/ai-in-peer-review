This paper presents a novel meta-learning framework that reframes the stochastic gradient descent (SGD) update as a gated recurrent model with learned parameters. This is a significant and original contribution with potential impact on transfer learning research. The paper is well-organized, though improvements to clarity in certain sections would be beneficial.

**Strengths:**

- The proposed approach to meta-learning is both compelling and practically viable.
- The results achieved are competitive, and the paper provides a thorough comparison against existing state-of-the-art methods.
- The authors offer insightful recommendations for implementation in real-world systems.

**Weaknesses:**

- The architectural analogy more closely resembles Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs) than Long Short-Term Memory (LSTMs) networks.
- The explanation of data partitioning into meta-sets is difficult to understand and would benefit from a visual representation.
- The experimental evaluation, while strong in some areas, is incomplete. Further investigation into the influence of the 'it' and 'ft' parameters would be valuable.
- Figure 2 provides limited additional insight.

**Comments:**

- A minor typographical error exists in section 3.2: "This means each coordinate has it" should read "its".
> The authors intend to release the code used for their experiments, which would be a substantial asset to the research community.
