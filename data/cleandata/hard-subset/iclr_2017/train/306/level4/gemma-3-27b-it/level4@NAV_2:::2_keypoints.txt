- Decision: Accept with minor revisions; promising new direction in meta-learning.
- Novelty: Frames SGD update as a gated recurrent model (LSTM) with trainable parameters — original and valuable for transfer learning.
- Clarity: Paper is well-structured but several areas could be clearer.
- Performance: Competitive with state-of-the-art metric-learning (Matching Networks), especially in the 5-shot setting.
- Related work: Thorough discussion situating the contribution within meta-learning and few-shot learning.
- Implementation: Inclusion of batch normalization adaptation for the meta-learning setting is commendable.
- LSTM vs GRU: The LSTM analogy feels forced; the model’s structure is arguably closer to a GRU and should be acknowledged.
- Data presentation: Description of meta-training/meta-validation/meta-testing would benefit from a visual diagram of the hierarchical dataset structure.
- Gate analysis: Recommend investigating learned input (it) and forget (ft) gate parameters to understand the meta-learner’s optimization strategy.
- Initial weights: Suggest analyzing learned initial weights and their effect on convergence speed.
- Figure 2: Provides limited insight and could be omitted or replaced with a more informative visualization (e.g., gradient/parameter flow).
- Typo: Section 3.2 — "This means each coordinate has it" should be "its."
- Question: Motivation for choosing LSTM over GRU given structural similarities?
- Question: Will authors provide a visualization of the hierarchical meta-sets and datasets?
- Question: Have the learned input/forget gate parameters been analyzed; any observations?
- Question: What is the computational overhead relative to standard fine-tuning or metric-learning, and are there scalability concerns for larger models/datasets?
