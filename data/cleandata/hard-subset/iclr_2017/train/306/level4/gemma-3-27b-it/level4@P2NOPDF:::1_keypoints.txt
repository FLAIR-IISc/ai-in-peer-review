- Introduces an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimization algorithm for a neural network.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents core ideas.  
- Main contribution: draws a parallel between Robbins–Monro updates and LSTM equations to address rapid learning and slower transferable knowledge in few-shot learning.  
- Effectively reuses prior techniques (parameter sharing, normalization) and proposes a specific batch normalization design.  
- Experimental results are compelling and support the proposed approach’s efficacy.  
- Overall assessment: strong submission with significant relevance to ICLR.  
- Suggestion: perform ablation studies to evaluate redundancy of meta-learner inputs (loss, gradient, network parameters).  
- Suggestion: consider extending the meta-learning framework to learn architectural choices (layer widths, activation types).  
- Related work critique: section is limited; recommend citing Bengio (1989), Bengio et al. (1994), and Jürgen Schmidhuber’s meta-learning publications.  
- Conclusion: paper is valuable and likely to stimulate further LSTM-based meta-learning research.
