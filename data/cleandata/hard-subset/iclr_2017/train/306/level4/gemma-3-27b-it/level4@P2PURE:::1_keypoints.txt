- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimization algorithm for a neural network.
- Paper is well-written with a clear presentation of core ideas.
- Key contribution: draws a parallel between Robbins–Monro updates and LSTM updates to address few-shot learning needs (rapid acquisition and gradual generalization).
- Effectively adapts techniques from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) such as parameter sharing and normalization.
- Introduces novel design choices, including a specific batch normalization implementation.
- Experimental results are compelling and support the claims; overall a strong paper.
- Concern: Is feeding loss, gradient, and parameters to the meta-learner necessary? Ablation studies on input combinations are needed.
- Question: Can the approach be extended to learn architectural aspects (e.g., neuron counts, unit types)?
- Related work is too limited; should include earlier meta-learning and learning-rule discovery work (e.g., Samy Bengio 1989, Bengio et al. 1994) and investigate Schmidhuber’s contributions.
- Recommendation: Accept—highly relevant and likely of broad interest to ICLR.
