- Reviewer raised score to 8 after authors’ clarifications, especially on the meta-learning task.  
- Paper formulates few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem with episodes of training batches followed by a test batch.  
- An LSTM meta-learner is used, with second-layer memory cells modeling updated base-learner parameters; LSTM updates resemble gradient descent.  
- Meta-learner updates are computed from the base learner’s loss on the final test batch.  
- Authors make simplifying assumptions (e.g., sharing weights across second-layer cells, akin to a shared learning rate).  
- Experiments replicate Mini-ImageNet and show the LSTM meta-learner matches or outperforms Matching Networks on 1- and 5-shot tasks.  
- Using a simple ConvNet base learner (vs. nearest-neighbor embedding in prior work) and still achieving state-of-the-art is encouraging.  
- The insight linking LSTM memory updates to gradient descent is novel and interesting.  
- Paper offers practical training/design insights for LSTM meta-learners (initialization, weight sharing, normalization/rescaling).  
- Writing is sometimes opaque and hard to follow; the paper needs clearer exposition and a concrete example/diagram.  
- The precise N-class few-shot formulation (how classes are sampled and softmax output size during meta-training) is unclear and needs clarification.  
- Figure 2 plots are not very informative and the discussion around them is limited.  
- Overall: convincing results and clear acceptance recommendation, contingent on improving presentation; reviewer may raise score with better writing.
