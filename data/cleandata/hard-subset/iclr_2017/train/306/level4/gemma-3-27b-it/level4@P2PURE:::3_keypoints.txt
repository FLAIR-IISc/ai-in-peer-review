- Formulates few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning task: sequences of training batches followed by a final test batch.
- Meta-learner LSTM receives base-learner outputs (loss, gradients) and current parameters as inputs and is trained on the base learner’s final-test loss.
- Uses LSTM second-layer memory cells to model base-learner parameter updates, leveraging similarities between LSTM updates and gradient descent.
- Simplifying assumption: weight sharing across all second-layer cells (analogous to a uniform learning rate).
- Base learner is a simple ConvNet classifier, contrasting with learned-embedding/nearest-neighbor approaches.
- Reproduces Mini-ImageNet experiments and reports competitive/outperforming results versus Matching Networks on 1- and 5-shot tasks.
- Novel observation: relationship between LSTM memory cell updates and gradient descent is a valuable contribution.
- Provides practical guidance for designing/training LSTM meta-learners (initialization, weight sharing, normalization/rescaling).
- Strength: insightful framing and promising empirical performance, despite uncertain novelty relative to recent meta-learning work.
- Weakness: writing is sometimes opaque and task description is overly abstract; needs a concrete example/diagram for clarity.
- Weakness: unclear definition/handling of N-class few-shot setup (which classes are seen during meta-training and softmax size on the base ConvNet).
- Weakness: Figure 2 plots lack detail and sufficient discussion.
- Recommendation: paper is compelling and accepted; reviewer updated score to 8 after manuscript clarifications.
