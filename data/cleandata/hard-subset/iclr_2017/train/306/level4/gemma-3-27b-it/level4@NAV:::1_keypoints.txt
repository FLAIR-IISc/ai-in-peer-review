- Introduces an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns the optimization algorithm used by another learner (a neural network).  
- Paper is well-written with a clear and concise presentation.  
- Central contribution: links Robbins–Monro update rule to LSTM updates and uses this to address few-shot learning (fast adaptation and slow extraction of general knowledge).  
- Provides strong justification for re-implementing prior techniques (parameter sharing, normalization per Andrychowicz et al. 2016) and for novel design choices, including batch-normalization implementation.  
- Experimental results are persuasive and support the paper’s claims; overall a strong submission.  
- Question: Is feeding loss, gradient, and parameters to the meta-learner all strictly necessary? Request ablation studies on simpler input combinations.  
- Question/suggestion: Could the framework be extended to meta-learn other architectural aspects (number of neurons, activation types)? Authors’ thoughts requested.  
- Suggest expanding the related work on meta-learning, citing earlier work (e.g., Samy Bengio PhD 1989; Bengio, Bengio & Cloutier 1994 on genetic programming) and surveying Jürgen Schmidhuber’s contributions.  
- Overall conclusion: valuable, relevant to ICLR, and recommended for the conference program.
