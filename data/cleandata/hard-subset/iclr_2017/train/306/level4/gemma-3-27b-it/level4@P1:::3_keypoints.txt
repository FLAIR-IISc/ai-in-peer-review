- Proposes framing few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem using an LSTM meta-learner that models base-learner parameter updates in its memory cells.
- Inputs to the meta-learner include base-learner outputs (loss, gradients) and current parameters; meta-updates are driven by final-test-batch loss.
- Notes a novel insight linking LSTM memory cell updates to gradient-descent-like updates.
- Simplifying assumptions include weight sharing across second-layer cells (uniform learning rate analogue) and gradient independence.
- Recreates Mini-ImageNet and shows the LSTM meta-learner is competitive with or outperforms Matching Networks on 1- and 5-shot tasks.
- Strength: casting few-shot as sequential/meta-learning is intriguing and advances the state of the art.
- Strength: achieves competitive results with a different base-learner (ConvNet classifier) compared to embedding+nearest-neighbor approaches.
- Strength: provides practical, reproducible insights on LSTM meta-learner design (initialization, weight sharing, normalization/rescaling).
- Weakness: writing and notation are opaque; the task description is hard to follow and needs concrete examples/diagrams.
- Weakness: unclear details about the N-class few-shot setup, particularly how Mini-ImageNet class splits map to base-learner softmax outputs.
- Weakness: Figure 3 plots are not informative and the discussion adds little insight.
- Decision: reviewer recommends accepting the paper despite clarity issues.
- Supporting argument: addresses an important problem with a clever method and convincing experimental evidence.
- Additional feedback: add a simplified example (e.g., MNIST) and a diagram to improve accessibility.
- Additional feedback: improve Figure 3 visualizations with annotations to highlight key points.
- Additional feedback: expand discussion/justification of hyperparameters and specific LSTM architecture choices.
- Additional feedback: include ablation studies to quantify contributions of components like weight sharing and preprocessing.
- Question: does the base learner’s softmax have 64 outputs during meta-training or 100, given the 64/16/20 class split in Mini-ImageNet?
- Question: what is the impact of the gradient independence assumption, and were dependency terms ever included in gradient computations?
- Question: quantify the improvement from matching the number of meta-training and meta-testing updates and explain why matching matters.
- Question: how sensitive are results to the preprocessing parameter p in Equation 6, and was a range of p values tested?
