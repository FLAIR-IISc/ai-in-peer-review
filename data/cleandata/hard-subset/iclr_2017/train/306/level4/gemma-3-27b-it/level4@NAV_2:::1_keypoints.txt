- Presents an LSTM-based meta-learning framework to learn an optimizer for another neural network.  
- Paper is well written and clearly presented.  
- Core idea (linking Robbins–Monro updates to LSTM updates to tackle rapid few-shot learning vs slower generalization) is intriguing.  
- Reuses techniques from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) (parameter sharing, preprocessing) and introduces well-motivated novelties (notably batch normalization).  
- Experiments are convincing and support the claims.  
- Question: Is providing loss, gradient, and parameters to the meta-learner necessary — were ablations run to test simpler input sets?  
- Suggestion: Consider extending meta-learning to other learner architecture components (e.g., neuron counts, unit types) and discuss feasibility/benefits.  
- Related work section is incomplete; add older/meta-learning references (e.g., Samy Bengio’s 1989 thesis, Bengio/Bengio/Cloutier 1994 on genetic programming for learning rules, Jürgen Schmidhuber’s related work).  
- Overall recommendation: accept — relevant and of broad interest to ICLR.
