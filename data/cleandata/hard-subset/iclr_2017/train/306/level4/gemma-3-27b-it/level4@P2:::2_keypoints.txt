- Decision: Accept — novel idea, tackles important problem, competitive experimental results.  
- Contribution: Frames SGD updates as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters (LSTM-based meta-learner).  
- Motivation: Correctly targets gradient-based optimization weaknesses in low-data / few-shot regimes and advocates learning an optimizer.  
- Method: LSTM meta-learner learns both initialization and task-specific update strategy for the learner network.  
- Empirical results: Competitive performance on Mini-ImageNet, outperforming baselines and comparable to Matching Networks, especially in 5-shot.  
- Ablations/visualizations: Provided and useful but not exhaustive; give some insight into meta-learner behavior.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-structured but could improve clarity in certain areas (dataset splits, figures, explanations).  
- GRU vs LSTM: Reviewer suggests discussing the closer analogy to GRU and implications of using full LSTM.  
- Meta-set visualization: Recommend clearer description and a schematic of meta-training/validation/testing splits.  
- Gate parameter analysis: Ask for deeper analysis of learned input/forget gates across datasets and tasks, with ablations.  
- Figure 2: Current figure not informative—suggest removal or replacement with a diagram showing gradient/loss flow between meta-learner and learner.  
- Minor typo: In Section 3.2, "it" should be "its".  
- Q1 for authors: Why choose LSTM over GRU — theoretical or empirical justification?  
- Q2 for authors: What is the impact and computational cost of relaxing the gradient independence assumption?  
- Q3 for authors: Can you provide more examples/visualizations of input/forget gate behavior and common patterns across datasets?  
- Q4 for authors: How sensitive is performance to the number of meta-learned updates and how to choose it for new tasks?
