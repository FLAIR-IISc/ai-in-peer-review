Decision: Accept — novel LSTM-based meta-learner with competitive results and clear motivation.

Core idea: Reframe SGD updates as a trainable gated recurrent model (LSTM) to enable task-specific optimization and fast adaptation.

Empirical result: Competitive performance on Mini-ImageNet compared to strong baselines (e.g., Matching Networks).

Analysis: Ablation study and gate-value visualizations (Figure 3) provide insight into meta-learner behavior.

Practical concerns addressed: Discussion of batch normalization and other implementation challenges in meta-learning.

Suggestion — GRU vs LSTM: Need clearer rationale for choosing LSTM and empirical comparison to GRU variants.

Suggestion — Meta-set visualization: Clarify and visually illustrate meta-training, meta-validation, and meta-testing splits and data flow.

Suggestion — Gate parameter analysis: Provide deeper analysis/ablations of it and ft gates, their effect on learning rates and convergence.

Suggestion — Figure 2 redundancy: Consider simplifying or removing the computational-graph figure to improve readability.

Suggestion — Gradient independence: Discuss potential impacts of the gradient independence assumption and scenarios where it may fail.
