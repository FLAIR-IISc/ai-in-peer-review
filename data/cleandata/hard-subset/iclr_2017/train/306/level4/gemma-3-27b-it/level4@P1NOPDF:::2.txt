This paper presents a novel approach to meta-learning by interpreting the stochastic gradient descent (SGD) update rule as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters. The idea is original and potentially impactful for research in transfer learning. The paper is well-structured, though clarity could be improved in certain areas.

**Pros:**

*   An interesting and feasible approach to meta-learning.
*   Competitive results with a thorough comparison to state-of-the-art methods.
*   Useful recommendations for practical system implementation.

**Cons:**

*   The analogy to gated recurrent units (GRUs) feels closer than to long short-term memory (LSTMs).
*   The description of data separation within the meta-sets is difficult to follow and would benefit from visualization.
*   The experimental evaluation is only partially convincing. Specifically, investigating the effect of the key parameters (it and ft) would be of interest.
*   Figure 2 provides limited insight. 

**Remarks:**

*   There is a minor typo in section 3.2: “This means each coordinate has it” should read “its.”
*   The authors state: “We plan on releasing the code used in our evaluation experiments.” This would be a significant benefit to the research community.
