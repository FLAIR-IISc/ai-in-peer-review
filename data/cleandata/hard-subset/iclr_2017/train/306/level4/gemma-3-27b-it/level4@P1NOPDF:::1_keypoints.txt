- Presents an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimizer for another neural network.  
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation of core material.  
- Central idea: parallel between Robbins–Monro update and LSTM update to meet few-shot learning desiderata (fast acquisition, slower generalization).  
- Reuses techniques from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) like parameter sharing and normalization, and introduces well-motivated novel choices (batch normalization).  
- Experiments are convincing and demonstrate a strong contribution.  
- Question: Is it necessary to provide loss, gradient, and parameters as inputs to the meta-learner; were ablation studies done to test simpler input combinations?  
- Suggestion: Explore learning other architectural components (e.g., neuron counts, unit types) in the same meta-learning framework.  
- Criticism: Related work section is limited; should include earlier/meta-learning efforts (Samy Bengio PhD 1989, Bengio et al. 1994 genetic programming, and work by Jürgen Schmidhuber).  
- Overall judgment: Strong paper, highly relevant to the ICLR audience.
