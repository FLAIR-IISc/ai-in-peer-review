- Presents an LSTM-based meta-learning framework that learns an optimizer for another neural network.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear presentation of core material.  
- Central idea: draw a parallel between Robbins–Monro update and LSTM updates to address few-shot learning’s needs (fast acquisition, slower extraction of transferable knowledge).  
- Reuses techniques from Andrychowicz et al. (2016) (parameter sharing, normalization); batch normalization design choices are well-motivated.  
- Experiments are convincing and support the claims; overall a strong paper.  
- Concern: possible redundancy in providing loss, gradient, and parameters as inputs to the meta-learner—ablative studies are needed.  
- Suggestion: consider extending the meta-learning framework to learn architectural components (e.g., neuron counts, unit types).  
- Related work is somewhat shallow; should cite early meta-learning/optimization work (Samy Bengio 1989 thesis, Bengio et al. 1994 on genetic programming, Schmidhuber’s contributions).  
- Decision: Accept — approach is novel, well-motivated, and experimentally promising.  
- Supporting argument: effective solution to few-shot learning with insightful LSTM-optimizer connection and competitive performance.  
- Additional feedback: expand related work, run ablative analyses on input features, and explore learning other architectural parameters.
