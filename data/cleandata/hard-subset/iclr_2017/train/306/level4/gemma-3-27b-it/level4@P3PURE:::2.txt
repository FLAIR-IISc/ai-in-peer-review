This paper presents a novel approach to meta-learning by framing the Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) update rule as a gated recurrent model with trainable parameters. This interpretation is original and represents a significant contribution to research in transfer learning. The paper is well-structured, though clarity could be enhanced in certain areas.

**Pros:**

*   An interesting and feasible approach to meta-learning.
*   Competitive results with a proper comparison to state-of-the-art methods.
*   Good recommendations for practical system implementations.

**Cons:**

*   The analogy to recurrent neural networks would be more accurate if framed as a GRU rather than an LSTM.
*   The description of data separation within the meta-sets is difficult to follow and would benefit from visualization.
*   The experimental evaluation is only partially convincing; investigating the impact of the parameters *it* and *ft* would be particularly valuable.
*   Fig. 2 provides limited insight.

**Remarks:**

*   A minor typographical error exists in Section 3.2: “This means each coordinate has it” should read “its”.
*   The authors state: “We plan on releasing the code used in our evaluation experiments.” This would be a substantial benefit and is highly encouraged.
