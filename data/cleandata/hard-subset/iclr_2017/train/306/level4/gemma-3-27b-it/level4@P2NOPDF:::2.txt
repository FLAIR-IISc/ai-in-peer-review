This paper presents a novel approach to meta-learning by framing the Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) update rule as a gated recurrent model with learnable parameters. This is a compelling and original idea with potential significance for research in transfer learning and related areas. The paper is well-structured, though some sections could benefit from increased clarity.

**Strengths:**

*   The proposed approach to meta-learning is both interesting and practically feasible. It offers a fresh perspective on how optimization algorithms can be learned.
*   The paper demonstrates competitive performance compared to existing state-of-the-art meta-learning methods, supported by a thorough comparison.
*   The authors provide useful recommendations for implementing the proposed method in practical systems, adding to its immediate applicability.

**Weaknesses:**

*   While the recurrent model analogy is insightful, the architecture more closely resembles a Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU) than a Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) network. This distinction should be acknowledged and perhaps clarified.
*   The description of how data is partitioned into meta-training and meta-testing sets is difficult to follow. A visual representation of this data separation would substantially improve understanding.
*   The experimental evaluation, while demonstrating promising results, feels somewhat incomplete. Specifically, a more detailed analysis of the impact of key hyperparameters (particularly 'it' and 'ft') would be highly valuable. Understanding their influence on performance is crucial.
*   Figure 2 does not contribute significantly to understanding the method or results and could be removed without loss of information. 

**Further Comments:**

*   A minor typographical error exists in Section 3.2: "This means each coordinate has it" should read "its".

The authors’ statement regarding code release is encouraging. Publicly releasing the evaluation code would greatly enhance reproducibility and facilitate further research in this direction. This would be a significant addition to the paper.
