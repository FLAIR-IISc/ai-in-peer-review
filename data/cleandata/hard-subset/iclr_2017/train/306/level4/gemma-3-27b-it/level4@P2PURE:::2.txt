This paper presents a novel meta-learning approach by framing the stochastic gradient descent (SGD) update rule as a gated recurrent model with learnable parameters. This is an original and potentially impactful contribution to research on transfer learning. The paper is well-structured, though clarity could be enhanced in certain sections.

**Pros:**

*   The proposed approach to meta-learning is both interesting and practically feasible.
*   The paper achieves competitive results and provides a thorough comparison to state-of-the-art methods.
*   The authors offer useful recommendations for implementing the method in practical systems.

**Cons:**

*   The analogy to recurrent neural networks is more closely aligned with GRUs than LSTMs.
*   The description of how data is separated into meta-sets is difficult to understand and would benefit from a visual representation.
*   The experimental evaluation is only partially convincing; further investigation into the impact of the parameters *it* and *ft* would be valuable.
*   Figure 2 provides limited insight.

**Remarks:**

*   A minor typographical error exists in Section 3.2: "This means each coordinate has it" should read "its."
*   The authors’ plan to release the evaluation code is a significant positive and would greatly facilitate reproducibility and further research.
