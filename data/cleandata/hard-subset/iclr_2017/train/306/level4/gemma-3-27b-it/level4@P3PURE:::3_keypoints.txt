- Reviewer updated score to 8 after authors clarified the meta-learning task.
- Paper frames few-shot learning as a sequential meta-learning problem with episodes of training batches followed by a final test batch.
- An LSTM meta-learner is used, taking base-learner outputs (loss, gradients, parameters) as inputs.
- The second-layer LSTM memory cells are used to directly model the base-learner parameter updates.
- The authors note a compelling similarity between LSTM memory updates and gradient descent.
- Meta-learner updates are computed from the base learner’s prediction loss on the final test batch.
- Simplifying assumptions include weight sharing across second-layer cells (analogous to a uniform learning rate).
- The authors recreated Mini-ImageNet and report competitive results, outperforming Matching Networks on 1- and 5-shot tasks.
- The base learner is a simple ConvNet rather than a nearest-neighbor-on-embedding, making the results notable.
- Paper provides practical insights for training an LSTM meta-learner (initialization, coordinate weight sharing, normalizing/rescaling inputs).
- Reviewer unsure about the work’s novelty due to limited familiarity with recent meta-learning literature.
- Main weakness: writing is occasionally opaque and requires multiple readings; Section 2 is particularly abstract.
- Suggestion to add a concrete example (e.g., MNIST) and a diagram illustrating a sequence of few-shot tasks for clarity.
- Clarification needed on the N-class few-shot setup (which classes are seen during meta-training and softmax output size).
- Figure 2’s plots are uninformative and the accompanying discussion provides limited insight.
- Overall judgment: interesting, compelling results and a clear accept if presentation is improved; reviewer may raise score with clearer writing.
