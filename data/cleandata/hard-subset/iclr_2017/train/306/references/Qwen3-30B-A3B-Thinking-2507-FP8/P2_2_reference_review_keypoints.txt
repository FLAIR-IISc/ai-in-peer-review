- Paper proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns initialization and update rules for few-shot (1–5 shot) learning, showing competitive 5-shot and comparable 1-shot results on Mini-ImageNet.

- Decision: Reject.

- Primary reason for rejection: omission of comparison to MAML, a contemporaneous and now-standard baseline for few-shot/meta-learning.

- Using a custom Mini-ImageNet split (not Vinyals et al. 2016) prevents direct comparison to prior work and undermines empirical claims.

- No ablation study is provided to isolate the contribution of components (e.g., forget vs. input gate) in the LSTM meta-learner.

- Strengths: solid motivation, well-explained technical approach, and promising empirical results.

- Claim of being “competitive with state-of-the-art” is unsubstantiated without MAML comparison.

- The authors’ dataset/version differences lead to results that don’t match Vinyals et al., casting doubt on comparative performance.

- Methodological clarity lacking for some details (e.g., how batch normalization is implemented in the meta-learning setup).

- Recommendation 1: Add comparison to MAML to validate novelty and performance relative to the main competitor.

- Recommendation 2: Use the standard Mini-ImageNet splits from Vinyals et al. (2016) for fair benchmarking.

- Recommendation 3: Include an ablation study to quantify the importance of specific meta-learner components and preprocessing.

- Recommendation 4: Expand discussion and experimental evidence on scalability to more complex tasks and larger datasets.

- Questions for authors: Why was MAML not included? How would results change using Vinyals’ splits? Can you ablate forget vs. input gate? How does the approach scale to larger, more complex tasks?
