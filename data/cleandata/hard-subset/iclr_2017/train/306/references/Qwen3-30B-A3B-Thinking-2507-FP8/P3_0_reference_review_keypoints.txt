- LSTM-based meta-learner represents learner parameter updates as LSTM cell state with gates learning adaptive learning rates and forgetting.  
- Simultaneously learns a task-common initialization for the learner and an effective optimization strategy for few-shot learning.  
- Empirical results show improvements over simple baselines and competitive performance with Matching Networks on Mini-ImageNet (1-shot and 5-shot).  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and positions itself well within meta-learning literature.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Main reasons for acceptance: addresses an important problem with a novel approach and presents compelling experimental evidence.  
- Methodological innovation: elegant use of LSTM updates to learn both initialization and optimization strategy, extending prior learned-optimizer work to few-shot tasks.  
- Empirical rigor: experiments use a standard benchmark, compare relevant baselines, and report statistical confidence intervals.  
- Clarity: strong presentation and helpful figures (e.g., computational graph) aiding comprehension.  
- Theoretical foundation: intuitive LSTM–gradient analogy but lacks strong theoretical guarantees; gradient independence assumption discussed.  
- Key contribution: overcomes few-shot limitation of gradient methods by learning initialization and fast optimization.  
- Request for reproducibility: provide detailed Mini-ImageNet construction (class selection and splits) to clarify differences from prior work.  
- Suggestion for ablation studies: test effects of removing input/forget gates, using random vs learned initialization, and fixing update counts across shot settings.  
- Need clearer differentiation from Andrychowicz et al. (2016), emphasizing focus on few-shot learning versus general optimization.  
- Ask for discussion of computational complexity: training time and memory requirements of the meta-learner versus standard training.  
- Recommend adding a limitations section addressing scalability to larger architectures/datasets and mismatched update counts between meta-training and meta-testing.  
- Questions for authors: provide details on dataset construction and generalizability to original Mini-ImageNet.  
- Questions for authors: explain why fine-tuned baseline underperforms nearest-neighbor in 1-shot—overfitting or other causes?  
- Questions for authors: how sensitive is performance to number of meta-training updates (e.g., 12 vs 5 updates)?  
- Questions for authors: how does the approach scale to complex learners like ResNets and larger datasets—any experiments?  
- Questions for authors: request more theoretical justification or any convergence guarantees for the learned optimization strategy.
