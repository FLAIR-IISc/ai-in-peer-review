- Paper proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner optimizer for few-shot learning that jointly learns initialization and update rules to converge with few examples.
- Reviewer recommends Reject due to insufficient experimental validation and comparisons.
- Authors used their own Mini-ImageNet split, making results incomparable to prior work and undermining claims of competitiveness.
- Lack of reported confidence intervals and statistical tests prevents assessment of whether reported performance differences are significant.
- Paper does not clearly distinguish its method from prior LSTM-optimizer work (Andrychowicz et al., 2016).
- Recommendation: use exact dataset splits from Vinyals et al. (2016) or explicitly acknowledge and avoid claiming competitiveness without standard benchmarking.
- Recommendation: provide statistical significance testing (confidence intervals, p-values) for all performance comparisons.
- Recommendation: clarify methodological differences and advantages over Andrychowicz et al. (2016) for few-shot learning.
- Recommendation: include full details on how the 100 ImageNet classes were selected and how train/val/test splits were created.
- Recommendation: justify or analyze the gradient-independence assumption (Section 3.3.1) and its impact on performance.
- Question: exactly how was the Mini-ImageNet dataset created and split for reproducibility?
- Question: how can the authors claim state-of-the-art competitiveness given the nonstandard dataset?
- Question: can the authors provide statistical significance for performance differences versus baselines?
- Question: how does this approach specifically differ from and improve upon Andrychowicz et al. (2016)?
