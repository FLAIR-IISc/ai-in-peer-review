- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that treats the LSTM cell state as learner parameters to learn initialization and update rules for few-shot classification.  
- Simultaneously learns a task-common initialization and an effective optimization strategy for few-shot tasks.  
- Empirical results show the method outperforms nearest-neighbor and fine-tuning baselines and is competitive with Matching Networks on Mini-ImageNet, especially in 5-shot.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and makes a significant contribution to meta-learning.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Strength: Insightful connection between LSTM updates and gradient descent, with clear mathematical formulation and considered implementation details.  
- Strength: Ablation study demonstrates the benefit of meta-training the learner initialization.  
- Concern: Claim of superiority over Matching Networks in 5-shot is somewhat overstated given overlapping confidence intervals and dataset differences.  
- Concern: Comparison omits the fully-conditional embedding (FCE) version of Matching Networks, which was state-of-the-art.  
- Concern: Insufficient analysis of why the meta-learner learns its specific update rules beyond visualizations.  
- Feedback: Clarify how their Mini-ImageNet differs from Vinyals et al. (class selection, splits, preprocessing) to aid result interpretation.  
- Feedback: Include results for Matching Networks with FCE to strengthen comparative claims.  
- Feedback: Provide deeper analysis of learned meta-learner behavior (differences between 1-shot and 5-shot, interpretation of gate values, comparison to standard optimizers).  
- Feedback: Discuss computational efficiency compared to fine-tuning and other meta-learning methods.  
- Feedback: Briefly discuss potential extensions beyond few-shot learning to show broader applicability.  
- Question: Provide statistical significance details for 5-shot vs. Matching Networks and clarify 1-shot confidence interval overlap.  
- Question: Describe construction of their Mini-ImageNet (100 classes, train/val/test splits, selection procedure).  
- Question: Explain why different numbers of updates were used for 1-shot vs. 5-shot and whether this generalizes.  
- Question: Explain why fine-tuning baseline performs worse than nearest-neighbor, especially in 1-shot (overfitting concerns).  
- Question: Have they tested on other few-shot benchmarks (Omniglot, tiered-ImageNet) and do they expect similar gains?
