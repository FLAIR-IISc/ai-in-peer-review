- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner optimizer for few-shot learning that treats LSTM updates as learned gradient-descent-like parameter updates and learns a task-common initialization plus fast optimization strategy.  
- Shows strong performance on Mini-ImageNet 1-shot and 5-shot, outperforming baselines and providing insights into the learned optimization strategy.  
- Recommendation: Accept — novel, well-motivated contribution with rigorous experiments and careful meta-learning setup.  
- Motivation: Clearly articulates limitations of standard gradient-based optimization in few-shot settings and positions work within meta-learning literature, distinct from general learned optimizers.  
- Methodology: Sound approach leveraging the connection between LSTM updates and gradient descent; addresses batch-normalization pitfalls by collecting per-dataset stats and erasing running stats.  
- Experiments: Thorough comparisons to nearest-neighbor and fine-tuned baselines; identifies fine-tuned baseline overfitting in 1-shot and visualizes meta-learner gate values for interpretability.  
- Suggestion: Provide theoretical justification for the gradient independence assumption (Section 3.3.1).  
- Suggestion: Compare against more recent SOTA methods (e.g., Prototypical Networks, Relation Networks) to better situate results.  
- Suggestion: Include computational analysis (training/inference time, parameter counts) to assess efficiency.  
- Suggestion: Analyze sensitivity to the number of adaptation updates (e.g., why 12 for 1-shot, 5 for 5-shot).  
- Question: How does the meta-learner compare to or integrate with Prototypical Networks?  
- Question: Why choose LSTM over GRU — what are the specific differences in update rules and their implications?  
- Question: How was preprocessing parameter p=10 chosen and how sensitive are results to it?  
- Question: Can the authors elaborate on patterns in input gate variations across datasets and correlations with dataset characteristics?
