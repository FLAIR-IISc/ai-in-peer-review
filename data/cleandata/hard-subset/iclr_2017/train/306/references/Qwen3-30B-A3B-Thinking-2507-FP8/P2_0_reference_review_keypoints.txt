- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that treats optimization updates as LSTM cell updates to improve few-shot learning.  
- Claims the meta-learner learns both a good classifier initialization (initial cell state) and an effective update strategy (input/forget gates).  
- Shows the method outperforms simple baselines and is competitive with Matching Networks on Mini-ImageNet, with useful visualizations of learned behavior.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Key reason: Novel, well-motivated framing of optimization as LSTM updates that contributes to meta-learning literature.  
- Key reason: Empirical results outperform baselines and achieve competitive Mini-ImageNet performance.  
- Strength: Clever, well-explained conceptual connection between LSTM updates and optimization steps.  
- Strength: Clear motivation explaining why standard optimizers fail in few-shot settings.  
- Strength: Strong baseline comparisons (nearest-neighbor, fine-tuning).  
- Strength: Informative visualizations (Figure 3) that illustrate meta-learner adaptation.  
- Strength: Sufficient implementation and training details provided.  
- Minor weakness: Limited comparison to more recent state-of-the-art methods (e.g., Prototypical Networks, Relation Networks, MAML).  
- Minor weakness: Uses a custom Mini-ImageNet construction, complicating direct comparisons to prior work.  
- Minor weakness: No ablation study demonstrating the importance of specific components (input gate, forget gate, initial cell state).  
- Recommendation: Expand experiments to compare with recent SOTA few-shot methods to better establish competitiveness.  
- Recommendation: Clarify and/or run experiments on the standard Mini-ImageNet to address dataset construction differences.  
- Recommendation: Add an ablation study to quantify the contribution of each component of the meta-learner.  
- Recommendation: Discuss computational cost of training and inference relative to other methods.  
- Recommendation: Provide explicit evidence that the meta-learned initialization outperforms standard/random initializations.  
- Question: Provide confidence intervals or standard errors to assess the statistical significance of 1-shot vs. 5-shot comparisons with Matching Networks.  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to training for a specific number of updates vs. testing with a different number?  
- Question: Clarify how parameter sharing across gradient coordinates is implemented in practice.  
- Question: How robust is performance to different values of the hyperparameter p (e.g., p ≠ 10)?  
- Question: How does the meta-learned initialization compare to standard initializations (Xavier, He) given the same number of updates?
