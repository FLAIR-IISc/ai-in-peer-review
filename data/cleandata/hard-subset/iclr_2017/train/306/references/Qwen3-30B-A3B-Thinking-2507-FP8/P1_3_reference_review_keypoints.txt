- Presents an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns both initialization and update rules to improve few-shot learning.  
- Frames optimization as LSTM cell-state updates, addressing few-updates and random-initialization issues.  
- Claims superior performance to baselines (nearest-neighbor, fine-tuning) and competitive results with Matching Networks on Mini-ImageNet.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Novelty: principled, elegant connection between LSTM updates and gradient-based optimization, advancing meta-learning by modeling the optimization process.  
- Empirical validation: strong Mini-ImageNet results (e.g., 5-shot 83.5% vs. fine-tune 73.1%) and informative visualizations of meta-learner behavior.  
- Well-positioned in related work with clear motivation and writing.  
- Suggestion: analyze differences between their Mini-ImageNet split and Vinyals et al. (2016) to clarify comparisons.  
- Suggestion: include an ablation on sensitivity to number of meta-updates (e.g., 12 vs. other values).  
- Suggestion: provide more training/hyperparameter details (e.g., rationale for gradient clipping and ADAM lr) for reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: add a brief discussion of broader real-world applications and impact.  
- Question: clarify specific dataset differences from Vinyals et al. and how they affect comparisons.  
- Question: explain how the meta-update counts (12 for 1-shot, 5 for 5-shot) were chosen and report sensitivity tests.  
- Question: detail how the nearest-neighbor baseline avoided overfitting and whether any regularization was used.
