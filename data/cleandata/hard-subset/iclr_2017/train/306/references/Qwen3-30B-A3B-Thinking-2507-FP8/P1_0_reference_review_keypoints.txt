- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that frames optimization as LSTM cell-state updates for few-shot learning.  
- Claims the meta-learner learns both a task-common initialization and effective few-shot update rules.  
- Reports empirical results outperforming simple baselines and competitive with metric-learning methods.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main rejection reason: experimental results lack sufficient statistical rigor to support performance claims.  
- Main rejection reason: dataset creation methodology (custom Mini-ImageNet) is unjustified, preventing fair comparison to prior work.  
- No statistical significance testing, confidence intervals, or clear evidence backing the claim of “much better” performance.  
- Creating a custom Mini-ImageNet without justification is a major red flag for reproducibility and comparison.  
- Paper omits discussion of computational costs (training time, inference time, memory) for the meta-learner.  
- Visualization in Figure 3 is insufficient to prove a task-common initialization across tasks.  
- Learner architecture (4 conv layers × 32 filters) is too simple compared to state-of-the-art few-shot models.  
- Memory requirements of the LSTM-based meta-learner are not addressed and may be substantial.  
- Insufficient differentiation from related work (Andrychowicz et al., 2016); unclear novelty relative to prior learned-optimizer work.  
- Recommendation: use standard Mini-ImageNet or clearly compare datasets to enable benchmarking.  
- Recommendation: provide statistical significance testing (confidence intervals, p-values) for all results.  
- Recommendation: perform thorough ablation studies to quantify contributions of model components.  
- Recommendation: include computational cost analysis (training/inference time, memory) against baselines.  
- Recommendation: present stronger quantitative/visual evidence for the task-common initialization claim.  
- Recommendation: clarify how the approach specifically targets few-shot learning vs. general learned optimizers.  
- Recommendation: evaluate with more complex learner architectures to better compare with SOTA.  
- Questions for authors: Why create a custom Mini-ImageNet and how does it differ from the standard version?  
- Questions for authors: Can you provide p-values or confidence intervals to support performance claims?  
- Questions for authors: What happens if the number of updates during meta-testing differs from meta-training?  
- Questions for authors: How does the method scale to more complex datasets or larger numbers of classes?  
- Questions for authors: What are the computational requirements (training time, memory) compared to standard few-shot methods?
