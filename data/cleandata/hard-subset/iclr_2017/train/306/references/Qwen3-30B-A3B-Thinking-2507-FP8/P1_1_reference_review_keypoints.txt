- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns update rules and task-common initialization for few-shot classification.  
- Claims the meta-learner captures short-term task knowledge and long-term cross-task knowledge to optimize generalization with few updates.  
- Reports outperforming simple baselines and being competitive with Matching Networks on Mini-ImageNet.

- Decision: Weak Accept (major revisions needed).

- Strength: Clear motivation and problem formulation addressing limitations of gradient-based optimization in few-shot regimes.  
- Strength: Novel technical contribution by adapting LSTM-based optimizers to learn both initialization and update rules.  
- Strength: Experimental setup follows standard few-shot practices (Mini-ImageNet and reasonable baselines).

- Concern: Limited comparisons—missing evaluation against recent meta-learning baselines like MAML and Prototypical Networks.  
- Concern: No statistical significance analysis; overlapping confidence intervals make performance claims uncertain.  
- Concern: Insufficient transparency on how the 100 Mini-ImageNet classes were selected, hindering reproducibility.  
- Concern: Lacks discussion of computational cost of meta-learner training and inference.

- Revision: Add comparisons to recent meta-learning methods (MAML, Prototypical Networks, Relation Networks).  
- Revision: Include statistical significance tests (p-values/confidence intervals) to support performance claims.  
- Revision: Clarify dataset creation process and provide selection details or random seed for Mini-ImageNet classes.  
- Revision: Report computational cost (training and inference time) compared to baselines.  
- Revision: Conduct ablation studies to separate effects of learned initialization vs. learned update rule.  
- Revision: Provide deeper interpretation of Figure 3’s gate-value visualizations and their optimization implications.

- Q1: How were the 100 Mini-ImageNet classes selected; was selection random and can the seed be provided?  
- Q2: Why compare only to Matching Networks and not to more recent standard benchmarks like MAML or Prototypical Networks?  
- Q3: Can you provide more detailed statistical analyses (e.g., p-values) to substantiate the claimed 5-shot improvement?  
- Q4: How does training and inference time of the meta-learner compare to baselines and Matching Networks?  
- Q5: What do the dataset-dependent variations in input gate values (Figure 3) imply about the learned optimization strategy?  
- Q6: Was the number of meta-testing updates treated as a tuned hyperparameter, and how was the optimal number determined?
