- Tackles few-shot learning by using an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimization algorithm and a common initialization for faster task adaptation.  
- Frames parameter updates as LSTM cell updates to capture short- and long-term learning dynamics.  
- Demonstrates competitive improvements on Mini-ImageNet versus baselines and some metric-learning methods.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Clear problem statement and motivation showing standard optimizers struggle in few-shot settings.  
- Novel framing of optimization as an LSTM update is insightful and well-motivated.  
- Empirical validation includes ablations and gate visualizations that suggest benefits of meta-learned initialization.  
- Paper is well written, organized, and includes helpful algorithmic details (Algorithm 1).  
- Needs deeper analysis of what the meta-learner actually learns (gate patterns, learned learning-rate schedules, component ablations).  
- Missing discussion and quantitative comparison of computational cost (training time and memory).  
- Lacks analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity for the meta-learner (LSTM size, number of updates T, etc.).  
- Experiments are limited to Mini-ImageNet; evaluation on other few-shot datasets would strengthen generalization claims.  
- Should clarify implications of the gradient independence assumption on meta-learned gradient accuracy.  
- Requests authors explain architectural choices for the LSTM meta-learner (layers, hidden size).  
- Asks for typical training time of the meta-learner vs. training/fine-tuning standard classifiers.  
- Wants to know whether learned optimization strategies differ across shot settings (k=1 vs k=5).  
- Inquires about sensitivity to preprocessing/normalization of gradients and losses.  
- Seeks clarification on gate-visualization methodology (averaging, variance, and per-dataset examples).
