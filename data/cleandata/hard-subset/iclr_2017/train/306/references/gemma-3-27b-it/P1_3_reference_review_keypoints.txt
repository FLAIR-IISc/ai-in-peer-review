- Tackles few-shot learning by using an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns to optimize a learner network for better performance with limited data.  
- Frames optimization as LSTM cell updates to capture both short-term task knowledge and long-term shared knowledge.  
- Demonstrates competitive empirical results on the Mini-ImageNet benchmark.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Novelty & motivation: Well-motivated problem and a novel idea of modeling optimization with an LSTM meta-learner.  
- Technical soundness: Meta-learner formulation, parameter sharing, and preprocessing are technically sound and connected to prior work.  
- Acknowledges and justifies the common gradient independence assumption.  
- Empirical validation: Strong comparisons to baselines and Matching Networks; visualizations provide insight into learned optimization behavior.  
- Recommendation: Add an ablation study to quantify the contribution of components like parameter sharing and preprocessing.  
- Recommendation: Analyze hyperparameter sensitivity (learning rate, LSTM size, etc.) to show robustness.  
- Recommendation: Discuss scalability to larger datasets and more complex tasks.  
- Recommendation: Quantify computational cost relative to other few-shot methods.  
- Recommendation: Justify or analyze the choice of the number of update steps (e.g., 10/5) and its impact.  
- Question: Clarify the meaning of variability in input gate visualizations and differences between 1-shot and 5-shot strategies.  
- Question: Have experiments been attempted that incorporate full gradient information to assess the impact of the gradient independence assumption?  
- Question: Report robustness to initialization by measuring performance variance across random seeds.  
- Question: Explain why Mini-ImageNet was chosen and whether similar results are expected on other benchmarks (tieredImageNet, Omniglot).
