- Summary: Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns parameter update rules to enable fast few-shot adaptation, evaluated on Mini-ImageNet.  
- Decision: Accept (Weak Accept) — interesting and well-motivated but empirical validation is not fully convincing.  
- Novelty & Motivation: Learning an optimizer within a meta-learning framework for few-shot learning is a significant and well-argued contribution.  
- Technical Soundness: LSTM formulation, parameter sharing, and gradient/loss preprocessing are reasonable and well-justified; batch-norm considerations are noted.  
- Empirical Results: Competitive performance on Mini-ImageNet and insightful gate-value visualizations suggest learned, nontrivial update rules.  
- Clarity & Presentation: Paper is generally well-written, with clear formulation and a helpful Algorithm 1.  
- Limited Ablation: Missing comprehensive ablations to disentangle effects of learned initialization vs. learned update rule and other components.  
- Dataset Specificity: Evaluation restricted to Mini-ImageNet; additional datasets are needed to demonstrate generality.  
- Implementation Details: Insufficient description of experimental setup and hyperparameter tuning, hindering reproducibility.  
- Baseline Comparisons: Lacks comparisons to more recent/stronger meta-learning baselines (e.g., Prototypical Networks, Relation Networks).  
- Ablation Study Suggestion: Examine impacts of parameter sharing, LSTM architecture, initialization, and gradient/loss preprocessing.  
- Hyperparameter Sensitivity Suggestion: Analyze sensitivity to learning rates, number of updates, batch size, and other tuning choices.  
- Generalization Suggestion: Test on diverse datasets with varying resolution, class distribution, and intra-class variation.  
- Computational Cost Suggestion: Quantify training cost and trade-offs between performance and compute for the meta-learner.  
- Theoretical Analysis Suggestion: Explore theoretical properties or convergence behavior of the learned optimizer.  
- Q1 (hyperparams): Request detailed hyperparameter tuning description and rationale, including choice of number of updates T per k-shot.  
- Q2 (Gradient Independence): Ask for discussion/evidence on the impact and validity of the Gradient Independence Assumption.  
- Q3 (Gate Visualizations): Ask for quantitative comparisons of gate values across datasets and for recurring patterns.  
- Q4 (Meta-learner Gradients): Inquire about observed gradients, training instabilities, or challenges during meta-learner training.  
- Q5 (LSTM Initialization): Ask how different LSTM weight initializations (e.g., orthogonal) affect performance.  
- Q6 (Limitations): Request explicit limitations and scenarios where the approach might perform poorly.  
- Conclusion: Promising and technically solid approach, but needs deeper analysis and broader empirical validation to strengthen claims.
