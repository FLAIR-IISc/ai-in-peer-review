- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns initialization and update rules to rapidly adapt a learner network for few-shot tasks.  
- Reports competitive results on Mini-ImageNet but uses a custom train/test split.  
- Decision: Reject due to insufficient experimental validation and unclear advantage over baselines.  
- Results comparable to Matching Networks with wider confidence intervals; the claim of much better 5-shot performance is unsupported.  
- Custom Mini-ImageNet split introduces variability and complicates direct comparison to prior work.  
- Meta-learner architecture is substantially more complex than many baselines, but complexity is not justified by commensurate performance gains.  
- The use of the gradient independence assumption weakens theoretical grounding and its impact is not analyzed.  
- No ablation studies to quantify the contribution of components like the forget gate or parameter sharing.  
- Needs evaluation on broader, more diverse datasets to demonstrate generality.  
- Requires a more extensive hyperparameter search and sensitivity analysis.  
- Visualizations (e.g., gate values) are interesting but inconclusive; recommend more informative visual analyses, possibly of gradients.  
- Clarify the role and effects of learning the initial cell state (c0) versus random or pre-trained initialization.  
- Theoretical analysis of convergence or generalization properties would strengthen the work.  
- Provide a detailed discussion of computational cost compared to other few-shot methods.  
- Question: Provide details and rationale for the custom Mini-ImageNet split and how it compares to the original in difficulty and variance.  
- Question: What architectural choices and hyperparameters were tried to close the gap with Matching Networks?  
- Question: How does the gradient independence assumption affect learning, and were mitigation strategies explored?  
- Question: Did you try alternative fine-tuning schedules or regularization when fine-tuning Matching Networks?  
- Question: Explain what information the LSTM hidden state encodes and how it represents the learned optimization strategy.
