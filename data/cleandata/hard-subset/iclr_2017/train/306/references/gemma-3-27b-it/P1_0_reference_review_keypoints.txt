- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns optimizer updates and a task-common initialization for few-shot classification, evaluated on Mini-ImageNet.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Novelty: Using an LSTM to learn an optimization algorithm and linking LSTM updates to gradient descent is a fresh idea.  
- Motivation: Clearly argues standard gradient-based optimization is limited in few-shot settings and motivates a meta-learned optimizer.  
- Experimental validation: Competitive performance on Mini-ImageNet; shows finetuning can overfit compared to nearest-neighbor.  
- Ablation & analysis: Gate-value visualizations offer initial interpretability of the learned optimization strategy.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written; Algorithm 1 and Figure 2 clarify the method.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Needs discussion of robustness to choices like ADAM lr=0.001 and gradient clipping=0.25.  
- Computational cost: Should report meta-training resources and compute requirements.  
- Scalability: Should discuss applicability to larger datasets and more complex tasks.  
- Comparison to other meta-learning methods: Request more comprehensive comparisons (e.g., MAML, Reptile) and performance tables across datasets.  
- Initialization strategy: Ask for rationale and impact of initializing forget/input gate biases and effects on training stability.  
- Question on approximations: Request quantification of the impact of ignoring dependency of gradients on meta-learner parameters.  
- Gate visualizations: Ask for more examples across tasks/datasets to identify consistent meta-learner behaviors.  
- Finetuning baseline: Ask whether finetuning’s underperformance vs nearest-neighbor holds across learning rates and update counts.  
- Architectural exploration: Suggest investigating alternative LSTM variants or other recurrent architectures for the meta-learner.
