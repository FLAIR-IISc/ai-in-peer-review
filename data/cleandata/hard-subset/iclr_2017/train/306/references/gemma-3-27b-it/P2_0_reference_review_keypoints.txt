- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns update rules for few-shot learning.  
- Evaluated on Mini-ImageNet and shows competitive performance.  
- Decision: Reject due to limited novelty and insufficient supporting evidence.  
- Limited novelty: LSTM optimizer concept is not new; application to few-shot feels incremental.  
- Insufficient empirical evidence: performance gains are not clearly superior and lack statistical testing.  
- Limited experimental scope: only Mini-ImageNet and a single learner architecture, risking implementation bias.  
- Missing ablation studies to quantify contributions of components (e.g., forget gate, parameter sharing).  
- Visualization is qualitative (Figure 3) and does not replace quantitative analysis.  
- Uses gradient independence assumption without adequate discussion of its impact.  
- Suggestion: clarify how this work differs from prior LSTM-based optimizers and meta-learning.  
- Suggestion: expand experiments to more datasets (tieredImageNet, Omniglot) and architectures (ResNet) with statistical tests.  
- Suggestion: perform ablations on gates, parameter sharing, LSTM variants, and hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Suggestion: justify and possibly mitigate the gradient independence assumption.  
- Suggestion: provide detailed hyperparameter search methodology.  
- Suggestion: improve code documentation and running instructions for reproducibility.  
- Q: How is overfitting of the meta-learner to Mini-ImageNet addressed?  
- Q: What is the computational cost compared to methods like Matching Networks?  
- Q: How does performance vary with similarity between meta-training and meta-testing tasks?  
- Q: Were alternative RNNs (e.g., GRU) or architectures explored, and with what results?  
- Q: How sensitive is the method to preprocessing and are alternatives considered?
