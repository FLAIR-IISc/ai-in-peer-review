- Paper proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner to learn optimizers for few-shot learning, aiming for better initialization and faster adaptation.
- Decision: Reject.
- Main concern: Limited empirical advantage—results are marginal and not clearly better than Matching Networks.
- 1-shot results comparable to Matching Networks with overlapping confidence intervals; 5-shot is worse.
- Dataset dependency: authors used a custom Mini-ImageNet split, hindering direct comparison to prior work.
- Reproducibility concerns: Matching Networks were re-implemented by authors, raising fairness questions despite code availability.
- Lack of ablation studies: sensitivity to meta-learner hyperparameters and preprocessing is not explored.
- Suggest stronger baseline comparisons using standard Mini-ImageNet splits and established libraries.
- Recommend reporting confidence intervals and statistical tests for all results.
- Request hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (learning rate, LSTM size, gate biases).
- Ask for better visualization of the learned update rule (e.g., learned learning rates across tasks).
- Recommend stronger theoretical justification for using LSTMs as optimizers beyond analogy to gradient descent.
- Questions for authors: provide dataset/code recreation details, performance across random seeds, evaluation on original Mini-ImageNet split.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to LSTM depth/hidden size, computational training cost compared to standard training, and why ADAM was chosen for meta-learner training.
