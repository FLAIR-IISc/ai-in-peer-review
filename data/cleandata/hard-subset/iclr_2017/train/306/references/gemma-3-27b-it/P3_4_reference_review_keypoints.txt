- Proposed method: an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns an optimizer to train a learner network for few-shot learning, with parameter sharing and preprocessing to handle high-dimensional parameters, showing competitive results on Mini-ImageNet.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Novelty: Learning an optimizer via LSTM is a compelling idea, with an insightful connection between LSTM updates and gradient descent.  
- Technical soundness: Model is well-defined; parameter sharing, preprocessing, and training procedure are reasonable; gradient-independence simplification is acknowledged.  
- Empirical results: Encouraging performance on Mini-ImageNet, outperforming strong baselines and comparable to state-of-the-art metric learning; gate-value analysis provides qualitative insight.  
- Clarity and presentation: Paper is well-written, structured, and includes helpful algorithm pseudocode and visualizations.  
- Ablation studies needed: assess effects of parameter sharing, preprocessing (sensitivity to p=10), and different LSTM architectures (layers/hidden units).  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: provide more thorough discussion and analysis of hyperparameter choices and their impact.  
- Comparison to other meta-learning methods: include comparisons to MAML, Reptile, etc., and discuss differences/ complementarities.  
- Generalization: evaluate on additional few-shot benchmarks (e.g., tieredImageNet, Omniglot) to demonstrate broader applicability.  
- Visualization enhancement: visualize learned learning rates (input-gate outputs) over time and across datasets for deeper insight.  
- Question — gradient independence: how sensitive are results to this assumption and can higher-order approximations mitigate issues?  
- Question — LSTM size choice: rationale for 2-layer LSTM and chosen hidden size; was architecture tuned?  
- Question — meta-testing stability: does collecting then discarding batch statistics per dataset cause instability; would running averages help?  
- Question — scalability: how does computational cost scale with learner network size and are there challenges for larger models?  
- Question — transferability: can a meta-learner trained on one set of tasks transfer effectively to different classes or modalities?
