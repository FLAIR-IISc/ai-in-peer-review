- Paper tackles few-shot learning by meta-learning an LSTM-based optimizer that learns a shared initialization and update rule for rapid adaptation.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Novelty & motivation: Framing optimization as LSTM updates is a novel, well-motivated connection between optimization and recurrent models.  
- Technical soundness: Model and training details are clearly described; builds on prior learned-optimizer work with justified design choices (parameter sharing, preprocessing, gradient independence).  
- Empirical validation: Competitive results on Mini-ImageNet, outperforming simple baselines and comparable to state-of-the-art; gate visualizations give qualitative insight.  
- Clear writing: Paper is well-written, organized, and easy to follow.  
- Ablation studies: Needs ablations to quantify contributions of components (parameter sharing, preprocessing, learned initial cell state).  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Should report sensitivity analyses to show which hyperparameters matter and how performance varies.  
- Computational cost: Needs discussion/comparison of training time and computational resources versus other few-shot methods.  
- Theoretical justification: Would benefit from more formal/theoretical explanation for why the LSTM optimizer is effective.  
- Error analysis: Should include analysis of failure cases to reveal limitations and guide future work.  
- Question — gradient independence: Were gradient dependencies ever modeled, and how would the independence assumption affect learned updates?  
- Question — choice of LSTM: Why use LSTM over alternatives (e.g., GRU); were other recurrent architectures tried?  
- Question — visualization differences: Can authors provide dataset-specific examples where gate behavior differs and explain the causes?  
- Question — scalability: Are there challenges scaling to larger datasets/models and what modifications would be required?  
- Conclusion: Strong, compelling paper whose impact would be increased by the suggested minor revisions.
