- Summary: Proposes an LSTM-based meta-learner that learns optimizer-like updates for few-shot classification, achieving competitive results on Mini-ImageNet.  
- Decision: Recommend accept (with minor revisions).  
- Novelty & motivation: Novel framing of optimization as LSTM updates, motivated by limitations of gradient-based methods in few-shot learning.  
- Technical soundness: Model and analogy to gradient descent are well-justified; sensible parameter sharing and preprocessing choices.  
- Experimental validation: Convincing Mini-ImageNet results against baselines and Matching Networks; visualization of gate behavior included.  
- Writing & organization: Clear, well-structured, and easy to follow with good related-work coverage.  
- Ablation studies needed: Recommend analyses on LSTM depth/hidden size, parameter sharing, and preprocessing contributions.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Request discussion/experiments on sensitivity to hyperparameter choices.  
- Computational cost: Ask for comparison of training time and resource requirements versus other few-shot methods.  
- Error analysis: Suggest qualitative error analysis showing success/failure cases to reveal model weaknesses.  
- Generalization: Recommend evaluations on datasets beyond Mini-ImageNet to show broader applicability.  
- Gradient independence assumption: Ask for deeper discussion of its potential impact and problematic scenarios.  
- LSTM architecture question: Why a 2-layer LSTM and were alternatives (deeper LSTMs, GRUs) tried?  
- Initialization strategy question: Rationale and alternatives tested for forget/input gate bias initializations.  
- Empirical effect of gradient independence: Did preliminary experiments compare results with/without this assumption and quantify any discrepancies?  
- Preprocessing choice question: Why use p = 10 in the preprocessing function and were other values explored?  
- Computational resources question: Request training time and hardware details for Mini-ImageNet experiments.
