- Proposes a meta-learned LSTM optimizer that updates a base classifier’s parameters by treating the LSTM cell state as the learner’s parameters.  
- Learns per-parameter input/forget gates (acting like learned learning rates and weight decay) conditioned on loss, gradient, parameter value, and previous gates.  
- Learns a task-common initialization c0 for rapid adaptation with very few inner-loop updates.  
- Shares meta-learner parameters across coordinates and uses log-preprocessing of loss/gradients for scalability and stability.  
- Trains the meta-learner to directly maximize post-adaptation test-set performance after a fixed number of updates, aligning train and test conditions.  
- Provides practical batch-normalization handling for episodic meta-learning to avoid leakage while matching train/test statistics.  
- Empirically outperforms simple baselines and is competitive with Matching Networks on Mini-ImageNet, notably surpassing them in 5-shot.  
- Visualizations indicate the meta-learner discovers meaningful gating strategies across updates.  
- Decision: Weak accept.  
- Key reason 1: Novel, elegant learned-optimizer instantiation specialized for few-shot generalization, including learned initialization.  
- Key reason 2: Competitive empirical results on Mini-ImageNet with clear protocol and useful implementation insights.  
- Clear articulation of failure modes of standard gradient-based training in few-shot settings and justification for meta-learning approach.  
- Well-positioned relative to prior learned optimizers and few-shot methods, highlighting differences like episodic objective and explicit initialization.  
- LSTM-as-parameters formulation is methodologically sound; parameter sharing and preprocessing choices are appropriate.  
- Sensible training choices (gate bias init, gradient clipping) and using test-set loss as meta-objective are strengths.  
- Empirical comparisons show improvements over nearest-neighbor and fine-tuning baselines, supporting claims about initialization and update-rule benefits.  
- Limited evaluation scope: only Mini-ImageNet with a small CNN; no results on Omniglot, tieredImageNet, deeper backbones, or non-vision tasks.  
- Baseline comparability issues: architectural mismatches (Matching Nets reimplemented with larger convs) and omission of the fine-tuned Matching Nets variant.  
- Missing ablations on key design choices: learned vs fixed initialization, forget-gate necessity, second-order effects, preprocessing, number of inner updates, removal of first LSTM layer, and BN handling.  
- Scalability concerns: per-parameter LSTM state is memory-intensive; paper lacks compute/memory/time quantification and trains separate meta-learners for 1-shot and 5-shot.  
- Reproducibility issues: different Mini-ImageNet split from prior work and insufficient hyperparameter/split release.  
- Suggestion: add ablations for learned initialization, forget gate, gradient-independence, preprocessing/BN strategies, and sensitivity to inner steps and horizon.  
- Suggestion: expand evaluation to additional benchmarks (e.g., Omniglot, tieredImageNet) and test cross-architecture transfer for generality.  
- Suggestion: harmonize model capacities across methods and include the Matching Nets fine-tuning variant.  
- Suggestion: report compute and memory overhead (per-parameter state, wall-clock) and discuss scaling strategies like block-wise sharing.  
- Suggestion: release Mini-ImageNet splits and code for fair comparison.  
- Question: How much gain comes from learned initialization c0 versus learned update gates—ablation with random/pre-trained c0?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to inner-loop steps T, and does the meta-learner generalize to different T than trained on?  
- Question: What is the computational and memory footprint of the per-parameter LSTM (per-weight state size, total memory for CNN)?  
- Question: Was the gradient-independence assumption evaluated (ignoring second-order terms), and how does full higher-order training compare?  
- Question: Can you provide results with matched model capacities across baselines and your learner, and include the fine-tuned Matching Nets variant?  
- Question: Do learned gate policies and initialization transfer across N-way settings or to unseen architectures?  
- Question: Will you release the exact Mini-ImageNet class splits and episode generation code?
