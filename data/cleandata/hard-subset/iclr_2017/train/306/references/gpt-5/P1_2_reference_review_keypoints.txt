- Proposes an LSTM-based meta-optimizer that parameterizes learner parameter updates as LSTM cell-state updates for few-shot N-way classification.
- Learns both a task-common initialization c0 and a learned update rule to rapidly adapt a small CNN learner in a fixed, small number of inner steps.
- LSTM gates depend on loss, gradients, parameters, and previous gate states; meta-parameters are shared across coordinates to scale.
- Uses episodic meta-training that directly optimizes the post-adaptation test loss after T inner updates, aligning training and evaluation.
- Introduces careful gradient/loss preprocessing and an episodic batch-normalization treatment to avoid cross-episode leakage while matching train/test conditions.
- Empirically evaluated on Mini-ImageNet (custom split) and outperforms nearest-neighbor and fine-tuning baselines, and is competitive with Matching Networks—notably better in 5-shot.
- Visualizes gate values to provide qualitative insight suggesting dataset-dependent, nontrivial optimization behaviors.
- Decision: Accept — motivated by a principled formulation and competitive empirical results with a reproducible protocol.
- Technical soundness: clear formulation, justified gating functions, and argued need for parameter sharing and preprocessing.
- Training detail emphasized: meta-learner trained for the exact number of inner steps used at test time is an important design choice.
- Suggestion: Ablate learned initialization vs learned optimizer by fixing inner updates or using random c0 to isolate sources of gain.
- Suggestion: Ablate gate/input features (remove loss/parameter inputs, disable forget gate) to assess component contributions.
- Suggestion: Compare episodic BN to alternatives (group/instance norm) to quantify BN’s role.
- Fairness concern: Ensure architectural parity across methods (e.g., learner uses 32 filters vs matching nets 64) and clarify its effect on comparisons.
- Experimental clarity: Report whether Matching Networks had optional fine-tuning, include confidence intervals, number of evaluation episodes, and absolute accuracies in main text.
- Robustness suggestion: Report sensitivity to number of inner steps T and behavior when test-time T differs from training T.
- Generalization suggestion: Test across different N-way settings, larger k (beyond 1 and 5), and cross-dataset validation (e.g., Omniglot).
- Computational concern: Quantify memory/time overhead of backpropagating through T steps and impact of ignoring second-order terms; note any stability issues.
- Clarity suggestion: Provide more training details (meta-episode counts, meta-batch size, LSTM hidden sizes, c0 initialization).
- Clarify the choice and effect of gradient/loss preprocessing (p=10) and whether alternatives were tried.
- Questions: How much gain is from c0 vs learned update rule (request ablations)?
- Questions: Sensitivity to inner update count T and effect of mismatched test-time T?
- Questions: Impact of the gradient-independence assumption and whether second-order or truncated variants were tried?
- Questions: Runtime/memory overhead vs SGD/Adam fine-tuning and scaling with learner size?
- Questions: Were BN statistics and episodic BN protocol applied fairly across baselines, and does matching capacity (32 vs 64 filters) change rankings?
- Questions: How does method transfer to different N-way tasks or larger k (e.g., 10-shot), and does combining with metric-based inference or fine-tuning improve results?
