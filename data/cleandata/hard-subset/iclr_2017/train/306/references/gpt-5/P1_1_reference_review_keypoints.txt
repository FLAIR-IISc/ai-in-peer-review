- Framing few-shot classification as meta-learning where an LSTM meta-learner parameterizes base-learner updates.  
- LSTM cell state stores learner parameters; input/forget gates act as learned per-coordinate learning-rate and weight-decay controls.  
- Meta-learner jointly learns initialization and a task-adapted update rule, trained end-to-end to minimize episode test loss after T updates.  
- Shared LSTM parameters across coordinates plus gradient/loss preprocessing for training stability.  
- Practical details addressed: batch-normalization in episodic settings and a gradient-independence (ignore second derivatives) approximation.  
- Experiments on Mini-ImageNet (5-way, 1-shot/5-shot) show competitive 1-shot performance with Matching Networks and stronger 5-shot performance.  
- Baselines include nearest-neighbor and fine-tuning; fine-tuning underperforms due to overfitting, highlighting the method’s benefits.  
- Visualizations of gate dynamics provide qualitative insight into learned optimization behavior.  
- Code is released, improving reproducibility.  
- Decision: weak accept.  
- Key reasons for acceptance: well-motivated and conceptually clean approach; competitive empirical results; practical, end-to-end algorithm with open-source code.  
- Novelty: specializes learned-LSTM optimizer to few-shot classification with an episodic objective optimizing post-update test performance.  
- Method is clearly described (gate parametrizations, preprocessing, parameter sharing, BN handling, gradient-independence stated).  
- Empirical support is solid on Mini-ImageNet with competitive comparisons and informative visual diagnostics.  
- Concern: fairness/comparability of baselines — reproduced Matching Networks variant omitted optional fine-tuning; hyperparameter/architecture parity unclear.  
- Concern: network capacity mismatch — author’s learner uses 32 filters but baselines reported with 64 filters; needs reconciliation or matched comparisons.  
- Request to release exact Mini-ImageNet split used for consistent future comparisons.  
- Ablation requests: quantify contribution of learned initialization c0 vs learned optimizer, and controls using fixed SGD/Adam.  
- Ablation requests: test gate/input variants (remove loss Lt, previous gate state) and compare to simpler learned per-parameter LR schedules.  
- Ablation request: quantify effect of ignoring second derivatives or show experiments without the gradient-independence approximation.  
- Sensitivity analyses requested: effect of mismatch in number of update steps T between training and testing, and whether one meta-learner can generalize across T.  
- Generalization tests requested: cross-way (e.g., train 5-way, test 10-way) and cross-shot evaluations, plus results on other datasets (Omniglot or other ImageNet subsets).  
- Practicality concerns: provide wall-clock training/inference time, memory footprint, parameter counts, and discuss scalability to larger learners.  
- BN details: formalize episodic BN strategy, clarify running-average decay and effect of test-time batch size variations.  
- Questions for authors: confirm base CNN filter count (32 vs 64) and effect of capacity matching on results.  
- Questions: how close are reproduced Matching Networks numbers to original and was optional fine-tuning tried?  
- Questions: performance when only learning c0 vs only learned optimizer with randomized c0.  
- Questions: rationale and sensitivity for chosen T values (12 for 1-shot, 5 for 5-shot) and behavior when allowing different numbers of steps.  
- Questions: any small-scale experiments with exact meta-gradients (no gradient-independence) and comparison results.  
- Questions: sensitivity of training to preprocessing parameter p and gate bias initializations; any failure modes.  
- Questions: can the approach handle variable N in final layer without retraining (dynamic classifier head)?  
- Overall verdict: well-motivated, carefully executed, competitive meta-learning contribution; recommend acceptance.
