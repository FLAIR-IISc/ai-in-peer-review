- Paper casts few-shot adaptation as learned optimizer + initialization by storing learner parameters in LSTM cell state and using LSTM gates as update rules.  
- Meta-learner inputs per-parameter gradients, current loss, and prior gate values with coordinate-wise parameter sharing and preprocessing; trained episodically to optimize post-update test performance.  
- Method also learns a global initial parameter vector for the base CNN as a task-common prior.  
- Practical engineering: gate-bias initialization for stability, per-episode batch-norm handling, and ignores second derivatives (gradient independence assumption).  
- Empirical result: on authors’ mini-ImageNet split the approach outperforms fine-tuning and nearest-neighbor baselines and is competitive with Matching Networks, especially in 5-shot.  
- Decision: Accept — motivated formulation and solid empirical gains with careful engineering and an episodic objective matching test conditions.  
- Novelty: adapts learned-optimizer ideas to few-shot episodic setting, framing “optimization-as-model” as an alternative to metric- and memory-based approaches.  
- Conceptual novelty: jointly learning initialization and update rule via LSTM cell-state view is elegant and practically useful for rapid adaptation.  
- Technical soundness: coordinate-wise sharing and gradient/loss preprocessing keep meta-learner tractable and stabilize training across parameter scales.  
- Training loop and per-episode BN handling are thoughtfully designed to match meta-train and meta-test conditions and avoid information leakage.  
- Empirical support includes episodic mini-ImageNet experiments with confidence intervals, comparisons to baselines and Matching Nets, and qualitative gate-value analysis showing task-dependent behavior.  
- Major limitation: comparisons use the authors’ Matching Nets reimplementation and a custom mini-ImageNet split, limiting comparability to prior work.  
- Limitation: ignores second-order terms (gradient independence) without ablation to quantify impact in this setting.  
- Limitation: contribution of learned initialization vs. learned optimizer is not disentangled — an ablation is missing.  
- Limitation: coordinate-wise optimizer ignores parameter structure (channels/kernels), which may limit scalability and performance on larger backbones.  
- Limitation: robustness/generalization across different N, k, and numbers of inner updates T is not evaluated; method trained for specific T and k.  
- Suggested ablations: disable forgetting or replace gating with scalar learning rates; train SGD/Adam from learned initialization; compare to cosine classifier and stronger fine-tuning baselines.  
- Clarify definition/sign of candidate cell state c̃t and whether it’s set directly to ±∇Lt or computed parametrically.  
- Report meta-training stability details: gradient clipping, sensitivity to gate bias init, and failure modes (e.g., exploding updates).  
- BN recommendation: use per-episode statistics consistently in meta-train/meta-test or evaluate LayerNorm/GroupNorm alternatives and quantify impact.  
- Provide compute/memory costs: wall-clock per episode, total meta-training time, and scaling with learner parameter count.  
- Extend evaluation: include Omniglot for comparability and test cross-condition generalization (train at 5-shot test at 1-shot; vary T at test time).  
- Questions: is c̃t = −∇θLt or +∇θLt, and how are descent directions implemented given sigmoid gates?  
- Questions: how well does vanilla SGD/Adam perform from learned c0, and how does the learned optimizer perform from random init?  
- Questions: how robust is performance to T mismatch between train and test (fewer/more inner steps)?  
- Questions: did you try including second-order terms (even approximate) on small models to evaluate benefit vs cost?  
- Questions: during meta-training do you use batch stats or running averages on Dtest, and can you quantify performance differences?  
- Questions: can the same meta-learner handle variable N at meta-test and changes in base learner backbone size?
