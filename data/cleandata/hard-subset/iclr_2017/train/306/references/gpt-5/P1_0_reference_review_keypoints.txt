- Problem: Few-shot learning where standard gradient-based inner-loop training from random initialization is too slow and overfits with only k examples per class.
- Idea: Model the inner-loop optimizer as an LSTM with per-parameter cell states and learned input/forget gates conditioned on gradients, loss, parameters, and previous gates.
- Initialization: Meta-learner also learns a task-common initial cell state c0 to provide a good starting point across episodes.
- Scalability: Use parameter sharing across coordinates and gradient/loss preprocessing for stability; train by backprop through unrolled T updates with a "gradient independence" approximation.
- BatchNorm: Careful handling to avoid cross-episode leakage by reinitializing running stats per episode.
- Empirics: On Mini-ImageNet 5-way 1-shot/5-shot, method outperforms nearest-neighbor and fine-tuning baselines and is competitive with or better than Matching Networks, with gate visualizations offering qualitative insight.
- Decision: Recommend accept.
- Key reasons for acceptance: Novel, well-motivated formulation unifying learned initialization and learned per-parameter update rules; competitive and sometimes superior empirical results; informative ablations/visualizations.
- Motivation & placement: Strong literature positioning relative to learned optimizers, memory-based meta-learning, and metric-learning approaches; argues learning optimizer + initialization is critical for few-shot.
- Technical novelty: Elegant mapping of gradient descent to LSTM updates, with gates conditioned on rich inputs and justified parameter sharing for scalability.
- BN handling: Thoughtful strategy to avoid leaking test statistics across episodes.
- Empirical rigor: Appropriate benchmark and episodic evaluation, reporting confidence intervals and demonstrating clear improvements, especially in 5-shot settings.
- Reproducibility: Algorithm and relevant hyperparameters described and code promised.
- Baseline comparisons requested: Add Omniglot results or justify omission; consider Memory-Augmented Neural Networks and other meta-learners as baselines or discuss expected behavior.
- Matching Networks parity: Validate your Matching Nets implementation against the original split and clarify whether fine-tuning was allowed at meta-test.
- Capacity fairness: CNN learner uses 32 filters vs baselines' 64—report matched-capacity results or justify choice to avoid confounded comparisons.
- Ablations needed: Separate effects of learned initialization c0 vs learned update rule (e.g., fixed gates, only c0 with SGD).
- Higher-order gradients: Analyze the impact of the gradient-independence assumption by comparing to exact meta-gradients on smaller models.
- Inner-loop steps T: Study robustness to mismatch between training T and test T and show accuracy vs T curves.
- Sensitivity analyses: Report sensitivity to preprocessing parameter p and gate initializations.
- Sign clarity: Clarify sign convention—whether negative gradient for descent is absorbed into learned gating or elsewhere.
- Memory/scalability details: Provide per-parameter LSTM hidden dimensionality and total memory footprint, and discuss scaling to larger backbones.
- Training specifics: Report number of meta-training episodes, episode batch size, unroll length T, truncation strategy, and wall-clock/runtime vs baselines.
- BN confirmation: Confirm no test-set statistics are used at meta-test and consider results without BN or with GroupNorm/LayerNorm for robustness.
- Additional evaluations: Provide per-way (e.g., 20-way) results and cross-shot generalization (train on 1-shot, test on 5-shot).
- Transferability: Test whether the learned optimizer transfers to new architectures or datasets without retraining.
- Specific questions for authors: (1) What is the per-parameter LSTM state size and overhead vs SGD/MAML? (2) Can learned initialization alone recover most gains, or learned update rule alone? (3) How sensitive is performance to inner-loop update count T—can you share curves? (4) Did you try exact higher-order meta-gradients and how did they compare? (5) What absolute accuracy does Matching Networks reach on your split with original hyperparameters? (6) Do learned gates show systematic differences by layer or parameter type and suggest implicit regularization?
- Overall judgment: Strong, well-motivated paper with clear contributions and solid results that would benefit from a few more comparisons and ablations.
