Summary
- The paper tackles few-shot N-way classification via meta-learning. It proposes to train an LSTM-based meta-optimizer whose cell state corresponds to the learner’s parameters. The meta-learner outputs per-parameter “input” and “forget” gates that update the learner using gradients and losses, with parameter sharing across coordinates. It also learns a task-common initialization. The training objective is the test loss on episodes, aligning training and evaluation for few-shot learning. The method includes practicalities (gradient/loss preprocessing, batch norm handling across episodes) and reports competitive/better results than baselines and Matching Networks on a custom Mini-ImageNet split, plus some gate visualizations.

Decision: Reject
Key reasons:
1) Critical methodological inconsistencies and ambiguities (notably the sign of the update, gating ranges, and batch norm protocol) that undermine correctness/clarity.
2) Empirical evidence is not sufficiently convincing: key numbers (Table 1) are not shown; baselines are not matched in backbone capacity; dataset split is custom and not released; important ablations are missing.

Supporting arguments
- Core update inconsistency: In Sec. 3.1, to match θt = θt−1 − αt∇Lt, Eq. (2) implies c̃t = −∇Lt. However, you later state “We set … c̃t = ∇θt−1Lt” (no minus). With it = σ(·) ∈ (0,1), the update ct = ft ct−1 + it c̃t would ascend the gradient. This is either a sign error in the text or a serious flaw; it must be clarified and experimentally verified.
- Gating ranges: Both it and ft are sigmoids in (0,1), so the per-parameter step size cannot be negative and cannot exceed 1. Combined with per-parameter normalization, this may still work, but it is a strong design constraint that is neither motivated nor ablated. Consider justifying or relaxing this (e.g., signed scaling, softplus/tanh parameterization, separate direction terms).
- BatchNorm protocol ambiguity: 3.4 asserts the necessity of matching meta-train and meta-test conditions, yet the described protocol differs: meta-train uses batch stats for both train and test, while meta-test uses running averages for testing. Please justify the mismatch, quantify sensitivity, and ensure no leakage across episodes. Also clarify whether running stats at test are computed solely from Dtrain within the episode (they appear to be).
- Inconsistent update counts: Sec. 5.1 says the 1-shot model makes 12 updates; Sec. 5.2 says 10. Which is used for the reported results?
- Empirical reporting: Table 1 is referenced but not included; exact accuracies with 95% CIs are missing. This prevents evaluating the claims. Also, the learner backbone differs across methods: your learner uses 32 filters per conv layer, while baselines/Matching Nets use 64 filters (as per Vinyals et al.). This mismatch undermines fairness; please standardize backbones and re-run.
- Reproducibility: You use a custom Mini-ImageNet split; it must be released. Otherwise, claims are not comparable. Also specify the number of episodes used for meta-train/val/test and how CIs are computed.
- Missing ablations: No studies on (i) effect of the gradient independence assumption (ignoring second-order terms), (ii) preprocessing parameter p, (iii) the role of learned initialization c0, (iv) the first-layer LSTM vs. one-layer meta-learner, (v) number of updates T at meta-train vs. meta-test.
- Scalability/compute: The method maintains per-parameter LSTM state (at least ft, it histories and cell state) across tens of thousands of parameters. There is no discussion of memory/computation, meta-batch size, truncation/BPTT length, or wall-clock training cost. Practicality is a core concern.
- Literature placement: The connection to Andrychowicz et al. (learning to learn optimizers) is acknowledged, but novelty is incremental: tailoring to few-shot episodes and learning an initialization. Comparison to more recent and standard few-shot/meta-learning baselines is missing: ProtoNets, Relation Networks, MAML/Meta-SGD/Reptile, LEO, R2D2, etc. Even if contemporaneous with early work, the current paper should situate itself and, ideally, include at least ProtoNets and MAML-style baselines on Mini-ImageNet.
- Overclaim: The Introduction suggests “guarantee that the initialization is an optimal starting point”; empirically you optimize expected episode test loss on meta-train tasks; there is no guarantee of optimality. Please tone down.
- Notational/format issues: Algorithm 1 indexing Θd−1/Θd is unclear; multiple references are malformed (escaping artifacts, incomplete titles), and several figures/tables are missing. There are typos (“neutral networks,” “Imagenet,” etc.). These detract from readability.

Additional feedback (to improve the paper; not all are part of the decision)
- Provide complete, fair, and reproducible results: include Table 1 with means/CIs; align backbones across all methods; release the dataset split and code sufficient to reproduce results; report number of episodes and seeds; include per-class variance if possible.
- Clarify the meta-learner architecture: hidden size(s), exact parameter sharing scheme, inputs to each layer (what goes to the first vs. second LSTM), whether ft and it are per-parameter scalars or vectors, and how losses (scalars) are broadcast. Resolve Eq. (1)/(2) references and the sign.
- Add ablations: learned init on/off; BN strategies; with/without gradient independence; different gating parameterizations; effect of T and mismatch between meta-train and meta-test T; sensitivity to p in preprocessing.
- Discuss compute/memory and scaling to larger learners; consider block-wise parameter sharing or layer-wise learned optimizers to reduce overhead.
- Broaden baselines and discuss strengths/limitations compared to metric-based methods (ProtoNets) and gradient-based adaptation (MAML).
- Fix references and formatting to ICLR standards.

Questions for the authors
1) What is the exact sign used for c̃t? Is it c̃t = −∇θLt or ∇θLt? If the latter, how can the method descend the loss with it ∈ (0,1)?
2) Are it and ft emitted per-parameter (coordinate-wise) or per-tensor/layer? What are the hidden sizes? Please specify memory/compute overhead and meta-batch size.
3) Do you backpropagate through T steps fully (no truncation)? What is the typical T, number of episodes per update, and total meta-training steps?
4) Can you provide the missing Table 1 with means and 95% CIs, and ensure all methods use the same backbone (filters, BN, dropout)? If not, justify differences.
5) How sensitive are results to the BN protocol? Can you train using the same test-time BN behavior (running averages) to fully match conditions?
6) What is the quantitative effect of ignoring second-order terms (the gradient independence assumption)?
7) Will you release the Mini-ImageNet split and code to ensure comparability?
