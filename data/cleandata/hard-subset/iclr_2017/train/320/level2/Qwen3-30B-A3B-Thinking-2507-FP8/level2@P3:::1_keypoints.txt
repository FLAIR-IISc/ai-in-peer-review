- Method combines pre-trained VGG features, a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration to learn feature weights for visual servoing.
- Uses pre-trained ImageNet VGG features (with architectural modifications) as the visual representation and justifies their suitability for servoing.
- Bilinear dynamics model is well-motivated and enables efficient prediction of feature changes under actions.
- Fitted Q-iteration provides a simple, sample-efficient way to learn optimal feature weights with only ~20 training trajectories.
- Shows robustness to visual variation, viewpoint changes, and occlusions in the presented experiments.
- Demonstrates substantial improvements over pixel-based and hand-designed keypoint visual servoing methods.
- Achieves more than two orders of magnitude improvement in sample efficiency compared to standard model-free deep RL.
- Strong experimental evaluation includes multiple baselines (classical IBVS, end-to-end CNNs, model-free RL) and ablation studies.
- Synthetic car-following benchmark is well-designed to test occlusions, viewpoint shifts, and appearance changes.
- Paper is clear, well-situated in literature, and empirically rigorous — reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Lacks discussion/experiments showing generalization to real-world, more complex, or dynamic environments.
- Needs clarification of computational and deployment requirements on real hardware versus simulation.
- Requests more detail on the VGG modifications (removal of max-pooling, use of dilated convolutions) and their effect on feature representations.
- Wants deeper analysis of feature depth trade-offs (e.g., why conv5_3 is too spatially invariant) and their relation to servoing performance.
- Calls for more specific experiments/analysis demonstrating claimed robustness to occlusion and a comparison of computational efficiency vs classical methods.
