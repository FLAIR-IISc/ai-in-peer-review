- Proposes visual servoing method combining pre-trained VGG features, bilinear predictive dynamics, and sample-efficient RL.  
- Claims effective target following using only 20 training trajectories and >100x sample-efficiency vs standard model-free RL.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Addresses a meaningful robotics problem with a well-motivated combination of vision and RL techniques.  
- Novelty: leveraging pre-trained VGG features for visual servoing, grounded in prior feature usefulness.  
- Main technical contribution: sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration (FQI) algorithm for learning feature weights.  
- Empirical evidence: thorough comparisons and ablations showing FQI outperforms CEM and TRPO, and beats end-to-end CNN and classical IBVS baselines.  
- Experiments: conducted in challenging CitySim3D synthetic environment with metrics showing robustness to visual variation, viewpoint changes, and occlusions.  
- Baseline comparisons include ORB features and C-COT tracker for context.  
- Concern: “> two orders of magnitude” sample-efficiency claim lacks precise numerical comparison to methods like DQN/TRPO.  
- Concern: No adequate explanation for why deep conv5_3 features underperform compared to shallower VGG layers.  
- Concern: Simulation-to-real gap not discussed; real-world deployment challenges need acknowledgment.  
- Concern: 16Hz computational efficiency claim lacks comparisons of runtime/complexity with other methods.  
- Concern: Paper states servoing is “extremely simple” but omits explicit time-complexity analysis of the execution-time policy.  
- Suggested improvements: add a table quantifying sample-efficiency, discuss conv5_3 underperformance, address sim-to-real limitations, and compare computational requirements.  
- Questions for authors: provide exact sample-efficiency numbers; explain conv5_3’s shortcomings; how the method handles complete occlusion.
