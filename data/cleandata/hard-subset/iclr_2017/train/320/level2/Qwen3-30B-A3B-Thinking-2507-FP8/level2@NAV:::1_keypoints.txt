- Presents a visual servoing method combining pre-trained visual features, bilinear predictive dynamics, and sample-efficient RL.
- Claims effective learning from only 20 training trajectories, vastly outperforming pixel-based and hand-designed keypoint methods.
- Reports more than two orders of magnitude better sample efficiency than standard model-free deep RL.
- Recommends acceptance; contribution is clear and well-supported.
- Experimental design is thorough, with comprehensive comparisons to pixel, ORB/SURF, end-to-end CNN, and classical visual servoing baselines.
- Results consistently demonstrate superiority of the proposed method, especially in sample efficiency (FQI-based approach needing only 20 trajectories).
- Use of pre-trained VGG features is well-justified and provides practical advantages.
- Paper is well-written, method is clearly explained, and experimental details are sufficient for reproducibility.
- Authors acknowledge the limitation of using a synthetic environment.
- Suggestion: add discussion on handling cases where pre-trained features may not generalize to novel object appearances.
- Suggestion: provide a more detailed comparison of inference speed between the proposed method and the tracker-based baseline (16Hz vs 1Hz).
- Suggestion: include a more systematic exploration of how different feature resolutions/scales affect performance.
- Key strength: practical significance—learning visual servoing with minimal data addresses a major robotics limitation.
