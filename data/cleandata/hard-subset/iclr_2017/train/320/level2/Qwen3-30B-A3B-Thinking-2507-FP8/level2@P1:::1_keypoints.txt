- Presents a novel visual-servoing approach combining pre-trained deep visual features, learned predictive dynamics, and fitted Q-iteration to automatically weight features.  
- Key innovation: sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration to select visual-feature weights for robust servoing under visual variation, viewpoint changes, and occlusion.  
- Demonstrated on a complex synthetic car-following benchmark with strong empirical results.  
- Claims remarkable sample efficiency: effective servo learned from just 20 training trajectories versus >1000 for standard RL.  
- Method substantially outperforms pixel-based and hand-designed keypoint baselines.  
- Technical foundation builds on bilinear dynamics models and fitted Q-iteration and is well-motivated.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation including multiple ablation studies on features, weight optimization, and baselines.  
- Finds pre-trained VGG features (notably conv4_3) particularly effective; provides intuition for layer performance differences.  
- Shows generalization to novel targets in the benchmark.  
- Recommends acceptance due to significance, soundness, and practical impact of sample efficiency.  
- Suggests adding a clearer discussion of limitations for more complex scenes (multiple objects, extreme occlusion).  
- Recommends systematic analysis of how feature-layer depth affects servoing performance.  
- Requests computational complexity details (inference time, memory) for real-world applicability.  
- Questions for authors: real-world robot performance differences from simulation; effects of other pretrained backbones (ResNet, Inception); feasibility of extending to multiple simultaneous targets.
