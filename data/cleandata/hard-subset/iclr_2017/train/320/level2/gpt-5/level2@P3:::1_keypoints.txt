- Paper proposes visual servoing in learned CNN feature space with locally connected bilinear dynamics and a one-step lookahead controller.
- Uses a linear Q-function and fitted Q-iteration to learn nonnegative feature weights whose greedy policy recovers the servo objective.
- Evaluated in a 3D city simulator for quadcopter car-following; code, environment, and videos provided.
- Decision: Accept.

Key reasons
- Clear, well-motivated integration of representation learning, predictive modeling, and RL into a simple, efficient servoing method.
- Strong empirical performance and sample efficiency with thorough ablations and reproducible artifacts.

Supporting arguments — problem and motivation
- Addresses limitations of hand-crafted features by using pre-trained CNN features for invariance with little task data.
- Learning action-conditional feature dynamics provides necessary predictive structure for control.

Supporting arguments — methodological novelty
- Servoing in multi-scale CNN feature space with a locally connected bilinear dynamics model linear in controls.
- Linear Q-function whose greedy minimizer matches the weighted servo objective enables stable, sample-efficient FQI with minimal constraints.

Supporting arguments — empirical support
- Dynamics learned from ~10k triplets; RL weights learned from ≈20 trajectories (2 iterations of 10 trajectories).
- Mid-level VGG features (notably conv4_3) outperform pixels, conv5_3, and hand-crafted keypoints, and generalize to novel cars.
- FQI-based weighting outperforms no-weighting, CEM, and TRPO, achieving better performance with far fewer trajectories.
- Beats classical IBVS with ORB and tracker-based baselines; runtime ~16 Hz vs ~1 Hz for tracker-based control.

Supporting arguments — rigor and clarity
- MDP, cost, dynamics objective, servo objective, and FQI updates are clearly defined; appendices provide implementation details and ablations.

Areas for improvement
- All experiments are in simulation; a small real-robot study would increase impact and credibility.
- RL cost uses privileged ground-truth pose; exploring image-only cost surrogates would improve deployability.
- Report total data requirements and sensitivity to dynamics dataset size to clarify sample-efficiency claims.
- Provide quantitative dynamics prediction errors by layer and study correlation between dynamics accuracy and control success.
- Test robustness to backbone choice (e.g., ResNet) or light fine-tuning to assess generality and speed tradeoffs.
- Evaluate failure modes quantitatively: heavy occlusion, abrupt maneuvers, distractors, target leaving FOV.

Questions for clarification
- How sensitive is weight learning to the privileged cost and how does performance change with an image-only proxy cost?
- How is the goal observation x* obtained for new targets, and how does the method handle markedly different initial views?
- How does performance change if dynamics data are collected from broader distributions or with fewer than 10k samples?
- Are there theoretical or empirical stability guarantees for the FQI variant under different hyperparameters (ν, γ)?
- What hardware produced the 16 Hz runtime, is feature extraction the bottleneck, and would a lighter backbone preserve performance?
- In multi-object scenes, do learned weights reliably focus on the true target and can channel-wise visualizations explain weight behavior?
