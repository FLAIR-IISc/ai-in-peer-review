- Paper proposes learned visual servoing combining pre-trained VGG features, a multiscale bilinear feature-dynamics model, and a fitted Q-iteration that learns non-negative per-channel/scale weights for a one-step lookahead servo.  
- Q-function is linear in weights so greedy minimization matches the weighted servo objective; FQI variant jointly rescales parameters and learns a bias to stabilize Bellman error minimization.  
- Evaluated in CitySim3D for quadrotor-car following: learns effective servo with only 20 RL trajectories (dynamics trained on 100), runs ~16 Hz, robust qualitatively to pose changes and occlusion.  
- Empirically outperforms pixel- and keypoint-based IBVS and a strong tracker baseline, generalizes better to novel cars, and is >100× more sample efficient than end-to-end TRPO.  
- Decision: Accept (weak accept) based on strong sample efficiency and effective integration of components.  
- Key reason 1: Simple, well-motivated integration of pre-trained features, bilinear dynamics, and tailored FQI yields strong performance and sample efficiency.  
- Key reason 2: Clear empirical evidence that learned feature weights and dynamics give robustness and generalization superior to pixel/keypoint IBVS and much more data-efficient than model-free RL.  
- Motivation: Addresses brittleness of classical visual servoing (hand-designed features/Jacobians) and sample inefficiency of model-free deep RL by leveraging transfer from pre-trained CNNs.  
- Method soundness: Bilinear dynamics are linear in control for cheap Jacobian computation; Q-approximation exactly matches the servo objective, making the induced policy the proposed controller.  
- FQI variant: Joint global scale/bias optimization before weight updates is sensible and empirically improves stability.  
- Ablations: Mid-level VGG features (up to conv4_3) perform best; conv5_3 is too spatially invariant—matches intuition about feature spatial detail.  
- Generalization: VGG-feature policies generalize to novel cars better than pixels and outperform IBVS with ORB and C-COT tracker on cost and runtime; IBVS with ground-truth depth was used as a fair comparator.  
- Comparison to TRPO: FQI-weighted servo surpasses TRPO after 2 iterations and remains better after 50 iterations, using far fewer trajectories; end-to-end pixel CNNs require much more data and underperform.  
- Positioning: Situates work relative to photometric servoing, model-based video prediction, and batch RL (regularized/neural FQI); choices are timely for ICLR.  
- Weakness 1: Evaluation confined to a synthetic simulator; no real-world transfer or quantitative robustness studies to back occlusion/lighting claims.  
- Weakness 2: Method relies on large-scale pretraining (VGG on ImageNet) while end-to-end baselines do not, which affects fairness of sample-efficiency claims.  
- Weakness 3: RL cost uses ground-truth car pose in camera frame—acceptable in sim but limits real-world applicability without a proxy.  
- Weakness 4: Generality beyond target following not empirically explored; broader task evaluation would strengthen impact.  
- Weakness 5: Some algorithmic details are terse (non-negativity constraints, regularization sensitivity); no theoretical closed-loop stability or convergence guarantees.  
- Suggested improvement: Provide quantitative robustness experiments stratified by occlusion, viewpoint change, and lighting.  
- Suggested improvement: Analyze learned weights—sparsity, per-scale/channel contributions, sensitivity to regularizer ν; visualize salient feature maps.  
- Suggested improvement: Expand dynamics ablations (fully vs locally connected, neighborhood size, dynamics dataset size like 25/50/100 trajectories).  
- Suggested improvement: Compare against a short-horizon MPC that rolls out bilinear dynamics for 2–5 steps to test value of Q-weighted one-step lookahead.  
- Suggested improvement: Clarify sample-efficiency comparisons by separating RL trajectories, dynamics data, and reliance on pretraining; consider fine-tuning VGG on servo data.  
- Suggested improvement: Discuss how RL cost could be instantiated on real robots (detectors/pose estimators, human annotation) and effects of noisy cost.  
- Suggested improvement: Report compute details (GPU/CPU), per-step latency breakdown (VGG vs optimization), and any batching tricks.  
- Questions for authors: How sensitive is performance to amount/coverage of dynamics training data and does generalization degrade with fewer dynamics trajectories?  
- Question: Which solver was used for non-negative least squares (θ ≥ 0), how does ν affect stability/performance, and any local minima issues?  
- Question: Can you quantify robustness to occlusion (occlusion fraction vs cost) and to unseen background distractors?  
- Question: Have you tried other pre-trained backbones (ResNet) or fine-tuning the feature extractor to preserve spatial detail?  
- Question: How does performance change if the goal image differs from the initial frame or if target appearance changes mid-episode?  
- Question: Can you provide results with a short-horizon MPC using the bilinear model to verify benefits of learned weights versus planning depth?  
- Question: What failure modes were observed (depth errors, feature aliasing), how often does the servo lose the target, and under what conditions?
