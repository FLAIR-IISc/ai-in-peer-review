- Proposes a visual servoing method combining pretrained convolutional features, a multiscale bilinear predictive model, and fitted Q-iteration with nonnegative feature/channel/scale weights.
- Claims that weighted deep features yield more robust target-directed servoing than pixels or hand-crafted keypoints.
- Claims sample-efficiency (~20 trajectories) compared to model-free RL and generalization to novel targets, occlusions, and viewpoint/appearance changes.
- Provides code, simulator, and videos for reproducibility.
- Decision: Accept.
- Primary reason 1: Strong empirical evidence of substantial sample-efficiency and performance gains over well-chosen baselines.
- Primary reason 2: Clean, well-motivated integration of pretrained features, simple predictive dynamics, and an analytically tractable Q-approximation producing a practical controller.
- Problem motivation and positioning in visual servoing, video prediction, and RL are clear and sensible.
- Technical soundness: bilinear locally connected dynamics with exact linearization and linear-in-parameters Q approximator are coherent and efficient.
- Experimental rigor: comprehensive ablations (VGG layers), comparisons to pixels, CEM/TRPO, end-to-end TRPO, and classical IBVS; fixed test sets and open resources; reported runtime advantages.
- Limitation: Experiments limited to a synthetic car-following benchmark, leaving sim-to-real and broader task generalization unproven.
- Limitation: Statistical significance and robustness analyses are limited; cost relies on simulator-derived target pose.
- Suggestion: Add statistical tests, variance across seeds, success rates, and time-to-capture metrics.
- Suggestion: Quantify robustness to occlusions, viewpoint changes, distractors, and model mis-specification via controlled ablations.
- Suggestion: Include failure mode analysis, stability guarantees, or empirical convergence regions.
- Suggestion: Explore other backbones (lighter encoders) and modest feature finetuning to trade off spatial fidelity and sample efficiency.
- Suggestion: Provide preliminary real-world experiments or domain randomization to evaluate sim-to-real transfer.
- Questions: Sensitivity to VGG layer choice and multiscale weight dimensionality; any overfitting in θ?
- Questions: Do learned weights concentrate on car-activating channels; any interpretability analysis?
- Questions: How does performance degrade with dynamics model errors or unmodeled target motion?
- Questions: Can the approach handle multiple candidate targets or online target switches?
- Questions: What is wall-clock training time versus TRPO baselines, including feature computation?
- Questions: Have you tried modest finetuning of the feature extractor on servoing data?
- Questions: How does the method perform on other servoing tasks (manipulation, homing) without redesign?
