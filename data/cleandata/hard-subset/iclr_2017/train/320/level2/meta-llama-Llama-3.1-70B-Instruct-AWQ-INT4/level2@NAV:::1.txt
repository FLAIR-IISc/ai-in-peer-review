**Summary of the Paper's Claims**

The paper proposes a novel approach to visual servoing that combines learned visual features, learned predictive dynamics models, and reinforcement learning. The authors claim that their method can learn an effective visual servo using low amounts of data, enabling quick adaptation to new targets. They demonstrate their approach on a complex synthetic car following benchmark and show significant improvement over conventional methods.

I recommend accepting this paper based on the following key reasons:

1. **Novelty**: The paper presents a novel approach to visual servoing that combines learned visual features, predictive dynamics models, and reinforcement learning. This approach addresses the limitations of traditional visual servoing methods, which rely on manually designed features and analytical dynamics models.
2. **Strong Experimental Results**: The authors provide extensive experimental results on a complex synthetic car following benchmark, demonstrating significant improvement over conventional methods. The results show that their approach can learn an effective visual servo using low amounts of data and generalize well to new targets.
