**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to visual servoing, which combines learned visual features, learned predictive dynamics models, and reinforcement learning to learn visual servoing mechanisms. The approach uses pre-trained features from a convolutional neural network (CNN) and a bilinear predictive model to predict the dynamics of the visual features. The method also uses a sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration algorithm to learn the weights of the features and the control policy. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on a synthetic car following benchmark, showing substantial improvement over conventional approaches and state-of-the-art model-free deep reinforcement learning algorithms.

**Decision**

Accept
