- Presents a novel, highly sample-efficient visual servoing method combining VGG features, a learned local bilinear dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration to weight feature channels; policy is a one-step lookahead minimizing weighted feature distance.
- Demonstrated on simulated car-following, learning effective policy from ~20 trajectories and achieving over two orders of magnitude better sample efficiency than model-free deep RL, while outperforming classical visual servoing.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Elegant synthesis of deep learning, model-based control, and RL; linear-in-weights Q-function enables very efficient FQI.
- Key reason 2: Strong experimental results—major contribution is dramatic sample efficiency improvement with thorough baseline comparisons.
- Main strength: Intelligent problem decomposition—use pre-trained ImageNet features for perception, a simple data-efficient dynamics model, and targeted RL for control.
- Evaluation is comprehensive: includes strong baselines (TRPO, IBVS+ORB, tracker), ablations over VGG layers, generalization tests to novel cars, and qualitative robustness checks (occlusion/distractors).
- Real-world applicability concern: Entirely in simulation; paper should discuss transfer challenges like camera latency, motion blur, and actuator inaccuracies.
- Target representation concern: Target defined by a single static image; discuss extensions to deformable or appearance-changing targets beyond VGG invariances.
- Cost function concern: RL training uses privileged ground-truth 3D target position; discuss learning feature weights with sparser or image-only rewards.
- Question for authors 1: Confirm ground-truth car position is used only during FQI training (not at test); comment on feasibility of replacing it with image-based costs.
- Question for authors 2: How sensitive is final policy performance to the quality and coverage of data collected by the hand-coded policy used to train the dynamics model?
- Question for authors 3: conv5_3 underperforms conv4_3—can you elaborate on the spatial invariance hypothesis and whether learned weights for conv5_3 were noisier or less sparse to support it?
