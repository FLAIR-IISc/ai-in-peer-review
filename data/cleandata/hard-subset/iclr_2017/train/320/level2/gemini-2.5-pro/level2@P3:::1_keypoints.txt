- Proposes learning visual-servo policies by combining ImageNet VGG features, a learned bilinear feature-dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration (FQI) to weight task-relevant features; policy minimizes weighted one-step predicted feature error to a target.
- Evaluated on a simulated quadcopter car-following task, showing large gains in performance and sample efficiency (over 100x) versus model-free deep RL and classical visual-servo methods.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: Novel linear Q-function formulation where FQI learns feature weights, directly addressing deep RL sample inefficiency.
- Key reason: Strong, comprehensive experimental comparisons across end-to-end RL, classical methods, and visual trackers that demonstrate clear improvements.
- Using pre-trained VGG features gives a strong perceptual prior; bilinear dynamics are simple to learn from little data and support efficient control optimization.
- Main contribution: Reduces policy learning to learning linear weights via FQI, enabling extreme sample efficiency (e.g., good performance with only 20 trajectories vs TRPO’s much higher data needs).
- Empirical rigor: realistic simulation with distractors/occlusions, ablations over features and optimizers, generalization tests on unseen cars, and public release of code/environment.
- Suggestion: Discuss limitations and failure modes of the bilinear dynamics model in highly nonlinear or out-of-distribution scenarios.
- Suggestion: Expand analysis on why intermediate VGG features (conv4_3) outperform shallower/deeper layers, clarifying the spatial-precision vs invariance trade-off.
- Suggestion: Add a short discussion on sim-to-real transfer challenges and real-time constraints of running VGG and control on real robots.
- Question: Confirm whether 3D ground-truth target position is used only for computing training rewards and not required by the test-time policy.
- Question: How sensitive is final performance to the data-collection policy used to train dynamics—would random exploration suffice?
- Question: Provide intuition for why the two-step FQI update (solving for scaling α and bias b before weights) yields more stable learning than solving all parameters jointly.
