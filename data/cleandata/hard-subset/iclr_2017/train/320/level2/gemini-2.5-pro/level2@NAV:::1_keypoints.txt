- Proposes a novel, sample-efficient visual servoing method combining pre-trained deep features, a learned predictive dynamics model, and reinforcement learning.
- Core idea: servo a camera by minimizing a weighted distance in the feature space of a pre-trained VGG network.
- Key technical component: fitted Q-iteration to learn feature weights that select task-relevant features and enable a simple greedy servoing policy.
- Achieves effective target-following from only 20 training trajectories, demonstrating strong sample efficiency.
- Recommendation: Accept, based on methodological novelty and empirical results.
- Strength: Clever synthesis of deep representations, dynamics modeling, and RL that directly addresses sample-efficiency in robotic learning.
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation against TRPO, classical IBVS, and ablations showing substantial improvements.
- Result: Reports over two orders of magnitude improvement in sample efficiency versus TRPO.
- Suggested improvement: Discuss limitations of using ImageNet-pretrained features for out-of-distribution targets.
- Suggested improvement: Discuss limits of the simple bilinear dynamics model for more complex, non-linear dynamics.
- Question: Does the RL cost rely on ground-truth 3D target position, and how feasible is training without such privileged information?
- Question: Clarify failure modes, e.g., policy behavior under prolonged full occlusion of the target.
