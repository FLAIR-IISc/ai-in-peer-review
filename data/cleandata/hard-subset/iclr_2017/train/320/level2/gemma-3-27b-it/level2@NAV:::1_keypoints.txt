- Proposes combining pre-trained visual features, a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and reinforcement learning for visual servoing/target following.  
- Claims this combination enables rapid adaptation to new targets with minimal training data, outperforming manual-feature and analytical-model approaches.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness using pre-trained deep features and a sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration (FQI) algorithm.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Well-motivated work addressing an important robotics problem with strong experimental validation.  
- Highlights impressive sample efficiency (20 training trajectories) and substantial improvement over conventional and standard deep RL baselines.  
- Holistic framework intelligently integrates learned features, bilinear dynamics, and RL.  
- Use of FQI yields computational efficiency and superior empirical performance.  
- Synthetic car-following benchmark is challenging and appropriate; ablation studies comparing features and optimizers add rigor.  
- Lacks real-world experiments — demonstrating on real data would increase impact.  
- Needs expanded discussion on limitations of the bilinear dynamics model and conditions under which it may fail and how to address that.  
- Requires a clearer, more intuitive explanation for why deeper VGG layers beyond a point degrade performance.  
- Clarification requested on sensitivity to choice of pre-trained features (e.g., were ResNet or other models evaluated?).  
- Clarification requested on selection and sensitivity analysis of the FQI regularization parameter (ν).  
- Clarification requested on computational/inference cost of the bilinear dynamics model versus an end-to-end learned policy for real-time control.
