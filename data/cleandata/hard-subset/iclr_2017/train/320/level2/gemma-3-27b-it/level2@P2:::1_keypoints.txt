- Central claim: combining pre-trained visual features, a bilinear dynamics model, and sample-efficient RL (Fitted Q-iteration) enables effective visual servoing with minimal data.  
- Recommendation: reject — promising idea and improvements, but paper lacks clarity and rigor to assess the contribution.  
- Experimental setup under-described: environment details, training data generation, and noise parameters for the hand-coded policy are not reported.  
- Unclear train/test distribution: need to know if training data represents test conditions (car motions, initial conditions).  
- Baseline comparisons are unconvincing: chosen baselines differ in complexity/data efficiency and may not be directly comparable.  
- Improvement over TRPO is modest given added complexity and use of pre-trained features.  
- Ablation studies are incomplete; contributions of learned dynamics, FQI, and feature weighting are not isolated.  
- Choice of bilinear dynamics model is insufficiently justified; alternatives (e.g., neural nets) and trade-offs with accuracy are not explored.  
- Analysis of feature scale (VGG depth) is superficial; the claim that deeper features are “too spatially invariant” needs empirical support.  
- Paper would benefit from a deeper analysis of the learned dynamics (parameter visualizations, limitations).  
- Writing is verbose and repetitive; paper should be more concise.  
- Suggestion: provide detailed experimental setup and hand-coded policy parameters.  
- Suggestion: strengthen baselines by including state-of-the-art continuous-control RL methods.  
- Suggestion: perform thorough ablations isolating each component’s contribution.  
- Suggestion: analyze and visualize the bilinear model’s learned parameters and limitations.  
- Suggestion: investigate why deeper VGG features degrade performance and test other feature types.  
- Suggestion: clarify practical significance of reported performance gains.  
- Question: what are the distributions of car motions and initial conditions in training vs test sets?  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to the hand-coded policy’s parameters used for training data?  
- Question: what is the computational cost of the bilinear model compared to alternatives (e.g., neural networks)?  
- Question: why do deeper VGG features degrade performance — spatial invariance or other factors?  
- Question: provide a detailed computational time breakdown for feature extraction, dynamics prediction, and RL.
