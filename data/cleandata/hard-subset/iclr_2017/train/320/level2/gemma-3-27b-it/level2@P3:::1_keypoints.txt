- Problem and approach: Addresses visual servoing (target following) by combining learned visual features, learned predictive dynamics, and reinforcement learning.  
- Core idea: Perform servoing in the space of learned visual features from a pre-trained VGG rather than pixels or hand-designed keypoints.  
- Dynamics model: Uses a bilinear predictive model to estimate feature dynamics.  
- Control / learning: Employs a sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration algorithm to learn feature weights for the servoing task.  
- Results: Shows strong performance on a synthetic car-following benchmark, outperforming conventional approaches and standard deep RL in sample efficiency.  
- Decision: Recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Strengths: Tackles a relevant robotics problem, proposes a novel and well-motivated method, clear writing, well-defined experiments, thorough baselines, and includes a helpful video demo.  
- Key contribution: Significant improvement in sample efficiency over existing methods.  
- Concern 1: Choice of bilinear dynamics feels somewhat arbitrary; needs broader exploration or justification of alternative models.  
- Concern 2: Explanation of fitted Q-iteration could be more intuitive, particularly how it selects relevant features.  
- Concern 3: Hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., regularization ν, learning rates) is not fully investigated; ablation would help.  
- Concern 4: Evaluation is limited to a synthetic environment; demonstrating or discussing real-world transfer would increase impact.  
- Questions for authors: Were other dynamics models tried and why were they rejected? What are bilinear model limitations and fixes? Can learned feature weights be interpreted or visualized? How sensitive is performance to ν? Are there plans/challenges for real-robot evaluation?
