- Paper addresses visual servoing (target following) by combining learned visual features, predictive dynamics, and reinforcement learning for fast adaptation to new targets.
- Method operates in the space of pre-trained VGG visual features and learns feature weights via a sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration algorithm.
- Core idea of leveraging pre-trained features plus learned dynamics and RL is compelling and well-motivated.
- Demonstrates strong sample efficiency, learning effective visual servo controllers with only 20 trajectory samples.
- Experimental results on a synthetic car-following benchmark convincingly show superior performance to pixel-based, hand-designed keypoint, and standard deep RL baselines.
- Ablation studies (feature representations, optimization methods) strengthen the validity of claims.
- Qualitative sample trajectories and provided code/videos aid reproducibility and understanding.
- Recommendation: accept.
- Suggestion to clarify why a bilinear dynamics model is appropriate and its connection to the Jacobian.
- Request for discussion on sensitivity to regularization hyperparameter (ν) and its effect on performance.
- Recommend addressing generalization challenges to real-world, more complex visual/dynamics scenarios.
- Ask for more detail on fitted Q-iteration implementation, specifically how the linear system in Equation 5 is solved.
- Note that appendix references are not all accessible in the submitted version and should be made available.
- Question: why did the deepest VGG layer (conv5_3) perform worse than shallower layers—loss of spatial information or other causes?
- Question: how sensitive is learned feature dynamics to the quality of the hand-coded policy used for training data?
- Question: how does the approach scale to higher-dimensional states or more complex control tasks, and what are potential bottlenecks?
- Question: why are locally connected sparse bilinear connections helpful—computational efficiency, regularization, or both?
