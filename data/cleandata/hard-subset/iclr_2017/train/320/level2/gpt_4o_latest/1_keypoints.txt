- Paper proposes a novel visual-servoing method combining pre-trained deep (VGG) features, a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration for target following.  
- Method yields strong robustness to visual variations and occlusions and is highly sample-efficient (effective with ~20 training trajectories).  
- Empirical results show significant improvements over classical image-/position-based visual servoing, end-to-end RL, and alternative feature choices on a synthetic car-following benchmark.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reasons: method is novel and practically useful, achieves state-of-the-art sample efficiency and robustness, and claims are well-supported by extensive experiments.  
- Supporting argument 1 (Novelty): Integrating pre-trained deep features with bilinear dynamics and fitted Q-iteration addresses distractors and fast adaptation to new targets.  
- Supporting argument 2 (Experimental rigor): Thorough comparisons, ablations, and generalization tests convincingly demonstrate superiority.  
- Supporting argument 3 (Practical utility): Computationally simple and data-efficient, suitable for real-world applications.  
- Suggestion 1: More explicit discussion of limitations, especially domain gaps between synthetic and real environments.  
- Suggestion 2: Ablation/discussion on why particular VGG layers (e.g., conv4_3) work best and how this generalizes to other networks.  
- Suggestion 3: Validate on a real-world dataset or physical robot to strengthen impact.  
- Suggestion 4: Provide deeper analysis comparing to standard RL algorithms (e.g., why TRPO fails and why fitted Q-iteration succeeds).  
- Question 1: How does the method handle domain shift to real-world environments, and have you considered fine-tuning pre-trained features?  
- Question 2: Can the bilinear dynamics model be extended to more complex non-linear dynamics without losing computational efficiency?  
- Question 3: How sensitive is performance to hyperparameters (e.g., regularization in Q-iteration)?
