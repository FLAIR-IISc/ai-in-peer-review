- Proposes a novel visual servoing approach combining learned visual features, learned predictive dynamics models, and reinforcement learning.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a complex synthetic car-following benchmark with substantial improvement over conventional methods and model-free RL.  
- Claims the method learns with low data and enables quick adaptation to new targets.  
- Supports claims with extensive experiments comparing to classical image-based and position-based visual servoing and TRPO-based end-to-end policies.  
- Approach is well-motivated and the paper provides a clear overview of related work and limitations of traditional methods.  
- Paper is well-organized with detailed descriptions of the visual feature extractor, predictive dynamics model, and RL algorithm.  
- Results are impressive, showing effective policies learned from limited data.  
- Provides thorough analysis including baseline comparisons and ablation studies to evaluate component importance.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty, strong experimental support, and clear presentation.  
- Suggested improvement: include more implementation details (specific architectures for the feature extractor and predictive dynamics model).  
- Suggested improvement: analyze robustness to visual variations such as lighting changes and occlusions.  
- Question: How are visual features selected and how do different features affect performance?  
- Question: Provide more details on the RL algorithm, including the reward function and exploration strategy.  
- Question: How will the approach extend to more complex tasks (cluttered environments or multiple targets)?
