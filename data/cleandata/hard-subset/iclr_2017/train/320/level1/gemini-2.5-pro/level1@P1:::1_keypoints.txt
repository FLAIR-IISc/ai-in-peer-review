- Presents a sample-efficient method for visual servoing combining pre-trained VGG features, a learned bilinear feature dynamics model, and FQI to learn feature weights for target following.  
- Decision: Accept — considered a strong paper with a clear, significant contribution.  
- Key reason: Novel synthesis of deep representations, simple model-based control, and RL to learn a task-specific metric over features for sample-efficient visuomotor learning.  
- Key reason: Thorough experiments show much higher sample efficiency and better final performance than end-to-end RL and classical visual-servo baselines.  
- Strength: Intelligent problem decomposition — VGG features provide invariances, bilinear dynamics are tractable in feature space, and FQI adapts features to task.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation, including comparisons to TRPO, IBVS, tracker baselines, ablations over VGG layers, and tests on seen/novel cars.  
- Strength: Paper is clear, well-written, and authors release code and simulation environment, aiding reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Experiments are simulation-only, leaving real-world applicability and issues like sensor noise and unmodeled dynamics untested.  
- Weakness: RL cost relies on simulator ground-truth 3D target positions, which are unavailable in most real-world setups.  
- Improvement: Add discussion on sim-to-real gaps and robustness of the bilinear dynamics to real-world effects and camera variations.  
- Improvement: Provide details on the hand-coded policy used to collect dynamics data and assess sensitivity to its quality/coverage.  
- Improvement: Justify the FQI modification (intermediate α and b fit) with intuition or an ablation versus standard FQI.  
- Question: Why is a bilinear dynamics model particularly suitable for CNN feature dynamics under ego-motion, and were other parametric models (e.g., small MLP) considered?  
- Question: How could the framework be adapted to real-world tasks without ground-truth cost — e.g., using binary classifiers, learned rewards, or few demonstrations?  
- Question: Given the sample efficiency, was fine-tuning VGG layers considered, and would that risk overfitting with only ~20 trajectories?
