- Proposes a sample-efficient visual servoing method using pre-trained VGG features, a learned bilinear dynamics model, and Fitted Q-Iteration (FQI) to learn feature weights for control.  
- Demonstrates effective quadcopter-car tracking policy learned from only 20 trajectories and claims >100× sample-efficiency over TRPO.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Novel synthesis: combines pre-trained deep features, a simple learned dynamics model, and a custom RL algorithm for feature selection.  
- Key technical insight: formulate the Q-function linear in feature weights, enabling efficient Bellman error minimization as linear regression.  
- Two-step update in each FQI iteration stabilizes and speeds up learning by fitting scale/bias before weights.  
- Bilinear dynamics in feature space chosen for simplicity and sample efficiency versus complex video-prediction models.  
- Final policy: one-step planner that minimizes weighted predicted feature error.  
- Ablations show pre-trained semantic features (VGG conv4_3) outperform pixels and other layers.  
- Direct TRPO comparison on same policy structure isolates and validates FQI’s sample-efficiency benefits.  
- End-to-end CNN trained with TRPO performs poorly, supporting the hybrid feature+dynamics approach.  
- Outperforms classical IBVS with a C-COT tracker, indicating learned features + dynamics better support control than generic tracking+analytical models.  
- Shows generalization to novel cars and commits to releasing code and environment for reproducibility.  
- Suggested improvement: discuss real-world transfer challenges (camera calibration, motion blur, latency).  
- Suggested improvement: acknowledge limitations of bilinear dynamics for deformable or articulated targets and possible extensions.  
- Suggested improvement: clarify the connection and motivation between the servoing policy and the Q-function earlier in the paper.  
- Question: Have you tried iteratively re-training the dynamics model with data from the learned policy to reduce distributional shift?  
- Question: How crucial are the VGG dilated convolutions versus using standard VGG with upsampled feature maps?  
- Question: Have you analyzed or visualized learned feature weights (which conv4_3 channels get high weight and whether they correspond to car-specific features)?
