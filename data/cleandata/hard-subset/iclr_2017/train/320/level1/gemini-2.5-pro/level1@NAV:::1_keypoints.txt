- Novel, sample-efficient visual servoing method combining pre-trained visual features, a learned bilinear dynamics model in feature space, and a tailored FQI-based RL to weight feature channels.  
- Uses VGG features, learns a bilinear model to predict feature changes from actions, and fits a Q-function to learn per-channel weights focusing on task-relevant information.  
- Validated on a challenging simulated car-following task, demonstrating robust performance with far less training data than model-free deep RL.  
- Reports ~two-orders-of-magnitude improvement in sample efficiency over TRPO.  
- Main strength: principled decomposition (pre-trained extractor + simple dynamics + efficient RL) that avoids data-hungry end-to-end learning.  
- Bilinear dynamics choice is clever: enables an analytic policy and a structured Q-function approximator that makes FQI highly sample-efficient.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, including comparisons to classical IBVS and modern TRPO baselines.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Suggested improvement: include a simple real-world demonstration to strengthen robustness claims.  
- Suggested improvement: explain the intuition behind the two-step FQI update (solve for scaling α before full weights θ).  
- Open question: how sensitive is learned dynamics performance to the data collection policy used to train the model?  
- Open question: limitations of the Q-function being linear in feature-prediction errors for tasks with complex, non-quadratic cost structures.
