- Proposes learning visual servoing policies using pre-trained VGG features, a learned multiscale bilinear dynamics model, and a reinforcement learning algorithm to select task-relevant features.
- Frames the control policy as weighted minimization of predicted feature error, yielding a Q-function linear in feature weights and enabling sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration (FQI).
- Demonstrates the method on a simulated quadcopter car-following task with claims of large performance and sample-efficiency gains over classical visual servoing and model-free deep RL.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Novel, well-motivated synthesis of pre-trained features, learned dynamics, and RL with a sound, elegant linear-FQI technical contribution addressing sample efficiency.
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical validation showing superior performance and over two orders of magnitude better sample efficiency versus standard deep RL.
- Leverages pre-trained VGG to avoid large-scale task-specific representation learning and improve data efficiency.
- Uses a bilinear dynamics model that is expressive enough for first-order motion effects yet learnable from ~100 trajectories.
- Designs the servoing policy so the Q-function is linear in weights, turning Q-learning into a regularized least-squares problem and enabling learning from ~20 trajectories.
- Thorough experiments include ablations over feature layers, comparisons to TRPO, classical IBVS, and tracker-augmented baselines, and tests on seen and novel cars to show generalization.
- Suggest clarifying the intuition for the two-step FQI update (α and b separate from θ) to improve readability and stability explanation.
- Recommend discussing simulation-to-reality transfer challenges (camera blur, latencies, unmodeled dynamics) since evaluation is purely simulated.
- Ask for discussion of sensitivity to dynamics model training data collected from a hand-coded policy and its generality.
- Request a detailed inference-time computational cost breakdown (feature extraction vs. dynamics prediction vs. control) to assess real-time feasibility.
- Questions for authors: limitations of linear Q-function approximation and possibilities for extending FQI to more expressive Q-functions.
- Questions for authors: robustness to larger domain shifts (lighting, weather, different backgrounds).
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to choice of pre-trained network (e.g., VGG vs. ResNet) and dependence on ImageNet-style pretraining.
