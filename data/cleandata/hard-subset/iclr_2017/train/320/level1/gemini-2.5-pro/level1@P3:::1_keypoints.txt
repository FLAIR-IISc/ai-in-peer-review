- Novel, sample-efficient visual servoing method combining pre-trained VGG features, a learned locally connected bilinear dynamics model, and Fitted Q-Iteration (FQI) to weight features.  
- Uses VGG feature maps (rather than pixels or hand-crafted keypoints) as the visual representation.  
- Learns a simple predictive model of how those features change under robot actions via a locally connected bilinear model.  
- Introduces a linear Q-function parameterized by feature weights θ and uses FQI to learn these weights so the policy minimizes weighted one-step prediction error.  
- Claims robustness to visual variation, generalization to novel targets, and high sample-efficiency.  
- Empirical result: effective policy learned from only 20 trajectories on a simulated car-following task.  
- Outperforms classical visual servoing, end-to-end policies, and is >100× more sample-efficient than TRPO in experiments.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: Elegant synthesis of pre-trained features, learned dynamics, and sample-efficient RL with a principled servoing objective.  
- Key reason: Strong empirical evidence and comprehensive baselines demonstrating exceptional data efficiency and performance.  
- Pre-trained VGG features avoid learning visuals from scratch, while feature dynamics are task-specific and learnable efficiently.  
- The bilinear locally connected model balances expressiveness and learnability; action-linearity enables an analytic servoing solution.  
- The structured Q-function makes the learned weights directly correspond to the servoing objective, enabling efficient regression-based learning.  
- Experimental setup is rigorous with clear train/validation/test splits and novel-car generalization tests; appendices aid reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: Discuss sim-to-real transfer challenges (motion blur, sensor noise, latency) and potential mitigation.  
- Suggestion: Discuss extending goal representation beyond a single static image to more abstract or dynamic goals.  
- Suggestion: Clarify the locally connected bilinear model (e.g., illustrative figure or intuitive explanation) to improve readability.  
- Question: Why do VGG feature dynamics generalize so well across different car models and environments—are they mainly capturing geometric warping?  
- Question: How sensitive is dynamics learning to the data collection policy—would random exploration suffice versus on-task noisy hand-coded trajectories?  
- Question: Please clarify FQI stability, the role of regularization, and importance of the two-step update (global α,b then θ) for convergence.  
- Question: What are expected failure modes (e.g., highly non-rigid targets, dynamic distractors) and limitations of the bilinear dynamics model?
