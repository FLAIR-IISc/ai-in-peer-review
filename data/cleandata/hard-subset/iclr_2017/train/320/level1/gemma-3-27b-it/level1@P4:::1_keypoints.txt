- Proposes visual servoing policy learning in learned feature space using predictive dynamics and RL.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on a synthetic car-following benchmark with ~100x sample efficiency gains.  
- Recommendation: Reject due to insufficient clarity in method and weak connection between learned dynamics and RL objective.  
- Method description is dense; numerous equations and components need a clearer, cohesive explanation.  
- The link between the learned feature dynamics and the improvement in RL sample efficiency is not fully explained.  
- Experimental setup lacks a comprehensive ablation isolating contributions of components (e.g., bilinear dynamics, feature weighting).  
- Strength: Convincing empirical superiority over pixel-based, hand-designed, and standard deep RL baselines.  
- Strength: Use of a realistic synthetic benchmark and availability of code and videos improves reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Fitted Q-iteration description is hard to follow with abrupt section transitions and unclear updates for α and b.  
- Weakness: Rationale for design choices (specific bilinear form, Q-function regularization) is weak or missing.  
- Weakness: Justification for bilinear dynamics is limited; lacks discussion of limitations and alternatives.  
- Suggestion: Add step-by-step fitted Q-iteration explanation or pseudocode and clarify α and b updates.  
- Suggestion: Provide deeper justification of bilinear model, its assumptions, and why locally connected variant is used.  
- Suggestion: Run a more thorough ablation (random feature weights; no learned dynamics; varying Q regularization; alternative dynamics).  
- Suggestion: Report hyperparameter sensitivity (learning rate, regularization, exploration noise) and discuss real-world deployment challenges.  
- Clarify Equation (7) meaning of ū and detail cost-function constants in the appendix.  
- Questions for authors: Explain intuitively how learned dynamics drive RL sample efficiency, why bilinear was chosen, ν sensitivity, role/choice of ū, and plans for real-world evaluation.
