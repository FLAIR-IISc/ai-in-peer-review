- Tackles visual servoing (target following) by learning policies in a learned feature space rather than raw pixels.  
- Core idea is compelling and could improve robustness and adaptability of visual servoing.  
- Emphasizes sample efficiency for quick adaptation to new targets with minimal data.  
- Recommendation: Reject due to insufficient experimental rigor and clarity.  
- Experimental validation lacks comprehensive baseline comparisons, limiting claims of superiority.  
- Reliance on a synthetic, highly-controlled environment (CitySim3D) raises concerns about real-world transferability.  
- Presentation of experimental results lacks sufficient detail and comparative analysis.  
- Unclear whether improvements stem from the proposed method or from weak baselines (pixel servoing, ORB, basic tracker).  
- Missing comparisons to stronger baselines like model-free deep RL (DDPG, PPO) and visual imitation learning.  
- Approach (pre-trained features + bilinear dynamics + fitted Q-iteration) is well-motivated and sensible.  
- Pre-trained features and bilinear dynamics provide computationally tractable, sample-efficient modeling.  
- Detailed explanation of fitted Q-iteration is a strength.  
- Choice of hyperparameters (e.g., regularization ν) appears arbitrary and underexplored.  
- Ablation studies are limited; need deeper analysis of each component’s impact (bilinear model, weighting scheme).  
- Visualizations of learned feature weights and dynamics predictions are lacking.  
- Claim of “two orders of magnitude” sample-efficiency improvement over model-free RL is unsubstantiated without direct comparisons to standard deep RL methods.  
- Additional feedback: include stronger baselines such as DDPG/PPO and visual imitation methods.  
- Additional feedback: perform at least limited real-world evaluation to assess generalizability.  
- Additional feedback: conduct more comprehensive ablation studies and hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.  
- Additional feedback: provide visualizations and detailed error analysis to reveal failure modes.  
- Additional feedback: clarify and intuitively explain the RL cost function and its relation to servoing behavior.  
- Additional feedback: justify the bilinear dynamics model assumption (motivation and limitations).  
- Questions: Why were stronger baselines (DDPG, PPO, visual imitation) omitted?  
- Questions: How sensitive are results to the regularization coefficient ν in fitted Q-iteration?  
- Questions: Can you provide visualizations of learned feature weights and dynamics model predictions?  
- Questions: What are the main failure modes and how could they be addressed?  
- Questions: What assumptions justify using a bilinear dynamics model, and how do violations affect performance?  
- Questions: Why did deeper VGG features (conv5_3) perform worse—can you explain this behavior?
