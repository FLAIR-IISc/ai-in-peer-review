- Tackles visual servoing/target following by combining learned visual features, predictive dynamics, and RL for fast adaptation with minimal data.
- Core contribution: servoing in pre-trained VGG feature space using a bilinear predictive model and sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration to select relevant features.
- Recommendation: accept — method is novel, well-motivated, and addresses key limitations of hand-engineered features and analytic dynamics.
- Demonstrates large sample-efficiency gains (over two orders of magnitude vs. standard model-free deep RL).
- Experiments on a synthetic car-following benchmark are well-designed with thorough baseline comparisons (pixels, keypoints, other RL).
- Supporting videos strengthen empirical claims.
- Paper is exceptionally well-structured and clearly presents motivation, related work, method, and results.
- Mathematical derivations (dynamics linearization, Q-function approximation) are clearly presented in the appendix.
- Ablation studies on feature choices and optimization methods provide insightful analysis.
- Limitation: lacks evaluation on real-world data or more realistic visual variations (lighting, clutter).
- Limitation: insufficient discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity (learning rates, regularization, architectures).
- Limitation: needs a more detailed computational cost breakdown for dynamics training and RL phases.
- Limitation: choice among bilinear model variants needs more comprehensive comparison and justification.
- Limitation/question: rationale for using only 2 sampling iterations in FQI (given 10 inner iterations) is unclear—convergence vs. compute tradeoff.
- Question: Was dynamics-model hyperparameter tuning done per feature level or kept consistent across levels?
- Question: What value was used for the FQI regularization term ν, and how sensitive are results to ν?
- Question: Can authors provide an intuitive explanation of why FQI yields strong sample efficiency and how the analytic Q-function approximation contributes?
- Question: Any estimate of performance/speedup from using a less computationally demanding network than VGG for feature computation?
- Question: How were car trajectories sampled to ensure diversity and prevent bias toward specific road types or layouts?
