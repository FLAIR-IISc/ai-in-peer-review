- Paper proposes combining pre-trained CNN features (VGG), a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration for sample-efficient visual servoing.
- Decision: Accept.

- Main reason for acceptance: method achieves orders-of-magnitude improvement in sample efficiency over prior deep RL approaches.
- Secondary reason: robust performance in a complex synthetic environment, outperforming pixel-based and hand-keypoint methods.

- Strong empirical results: convincing comparisons to baselines (pixel servoing, ORB/tracker IBVS, TRPO) with validation/test sets showing generalization.
- Well-motivated approach: pre-trained features for invariance, bilinear model for tractable prediction, and FQI for efficient policy learning.
- Technical soundness: clear description of bilinear dynamics, FQI, linearization and cost details; implementation details and released code/videos increase credibility.
- Clear problem statement: limitations of existing visual servoing methods are well-articulated.

- Generalization & dataset size concern: training used relatively few car models—evaluation on larger, more diverse target sets is needed.
- Ablation studies needed: quantify contributions of bilinear dynamics, use of pre-trained features, and each pyramid scale.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: further analysis of regularization, learning rates, and exploration noise is desirable.
- Real-world applicability: experiments are synthetic; discussion/experiments on lighting, sensor noise, and depth inaccuracies would strengthen claims.
- Comparison to other RL methods: broader comparison to other sample-efficient algorithms (e.g., SAC, PPO) would be helpful.

- Question: How sensitive is performance to the choice of pre-trained CNN architecture (e.g., ResNet, Inception)?
- Question: What is the impact of the bilinear model neighborhood size (nf) on performance and computational cost?
- Question: How critical is the specific reward/cost function choice to the learned policy’s success?
- Question: How robust is the method to inaccurate or unavailable depth information in real-world settings?
