- Paper combines pretrained CNN features, a multiscale bilinear feature-space dynamics model, and sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration (FQI) to do visual servoing for target following.
- Control policy: one-step lookahead that minimizes predicted next-step feature error to a goal image with per-channel/scale weights and quadratic control regularization.
- Key idea 1: servo in robust CNN feature space rather than raw pixels or hand-crafted keypoints for invariance to appearance/viewpoint.
- Key idea 2: use a structured bilinear dynamics model to predict feature evolution under camera motion for tractable, differentiable one-step optimization.
- Key idea 3: use a linear Q-function approximator whose greedy action matches the servo policy, enabling efficient FQI to select task-relevant channels and scales.
- Empirical result: on a realistic quadrotor–car following simulator, the method achieves strong performance and sample efficiency (~20 trajectories for FQI), outperforming pixel-based, classical IBVS, and end-to-end RL baselines.
- Code, simulator, and videos are released, and runtime is ~16 Hz dominated by VGG feature extraction.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: well-motivated integration of representation learning, model learning, and RL with a clear link between Q-function and execution-time policy yielding sample efficiency.
- Key reason: comprehensive empirical comparisons on a challenging simulator substantiate robustness and efficiency claims.
- Strength: Ablations show mid-level VGG features (up to conv4_3) work best; conv5_3 is too spatially invariant; pixel methods fail under occlusion/distractors.
- Strength: FQI learns effective weights in as few as two sampling iterations (20 trajectories) and outperforms CEM-tuned weights and TRPO trained with much more data.
- Strength: Comparisons to classical IBVS (ORB/tracker) are fair; tracker-based IBVS is competitive but slower and still behind the proposed method.
- Strength: Reproducibility aided by released code/simulator and clear computational profiling.
- Limitation: All experiments are in simulation; no real-world validation or sim-to-real analysis to support robustness and quick adaptation claims.
- Limitation: RL cost uses ground-truth target pose in camera frame (Appendix B), raising concerns about obtaining training signals in real deployment.
- Limitation: Dynamics model trained on ~10k triplets from a hand-coded policy — the “20 trajectory” data claim applies only to RL weight learning, not dynamics learning.
- Limitation: Missing comparisons to contemporary off-policy continuous-control methods (e.g., DDPG/NAF); TRPO baselines may be under-optimized.
- Limitation: Memory constraints force different dynamics modalities for pixels vs. semantic features; more complexity/impact analysis is needed.
- Limitation: No analysis of learned weights (which channels/scales are selected), stability properties, or sensitivity to dynamics/model mismatch.
- Suggested clarification: report separate data counts for dynamics training vs. RL weight learning and sensitivity to dynamics data coverage/mismatch.
- Suggested ablation: servo with same features but no learned dynamics (identity or finite differencing) to isolate bilinear predictor value.
- Suggested baselines: include DDPG/NAF for end-to-end and weight-learning comparisons given their sample-efficiency claims.
- Suggested interpretability: visualize channels/scales with large learned weights and test generalization to novel cars.
- Suggested practical discussion: how to obtain training costs without ground-truth poses (self-supervision/weak supervision) and plans for sim-to-real transfer.
- Suggested robustness tests: larger viewpoint shifts, stronger occlusions, dynamic distractors, and domain shift (lighting/weather).
- Suggested analysis: effect of α,b fitting step on stability/convergence, multiple seeds, and confidence intervals.
- Suggested ablation: evaluate lighter backbones (AlexNet/MobileNet) for speed/accuracy trade-offs to show approach is not VGG-dependent.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to bilinear dynamics inaccuracies and reduced dynamics data; can FQI succeed with fewer dynamics triplets or dynamics from different objects?
- Question: how to obtain comparable training signals in the real world without ground-truth pose and can FQI work with image-only costs without collapsing to distractors?
- Question: report total sample budget including dynamics learning and whether FQI can work with <10k dynamics triplets.
- Question: distribution of learned weights across channels/scales — do they consistently emphasize certain layers or spatial locations?
- Question: runtime/performance with lighter feature extractors and speed/accuracy trade-offs?
- Question: were off-policy actor-critic baselines (DDPG/NAF) tried and how do their sample requirements/asymptotic performance compare?
- Question: any convergence guarantees or empirical stability diagnostics for the two-step α,b and θ updates, and sensitivity to non-negativity constraint and regularization ν?
