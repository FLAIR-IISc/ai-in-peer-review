- Tackles visual servoing for target following by servoing in a learned pre-trained feature space (dilated VGG feature maps) instead of pixels or hand-crafted keypoints.
- Trains a locally-connected bilinear predictive model to predict next-step feature maps given current features and action, enabling exact linearization for control.
- Uses a weighted multi-scale servoing objective with per-channel weights and control regularization learned via a fitted Q-iteration (FQI) whose greedy policy recovers the servoing rule.
- Demonstrates robust tracking, generalization to novel cars, and strong sample-efficiency (>100x vs model-free baselines) on a synthetic quadrotor car-following task using ~20 RL trajectories for weights and 100 trajectories for dynamics.
- Decision: Weak accept — novel, well-motivated integration with strong empirical and ablation support.
- Key reason 1: Novel and well-motivated integration of pre-trained features, bilinear dynamics, and tailored FQI yielding substantial sample-efficiency gains.
- Key reason 2: Careful empirical comparisons and ablations showing generalization and robustness to distractors/viewpoint changes.
- Problem/motivation: Addresses gap between engineered IBVS and data-hungry model-free RL by leveraging transfer from pre-trained features.
- Methodological soundness: Bilinear locally-connected dynamics justified by image-motion bilinearities and enables closed-form quadratic policy steps.
- Methodological soundness: Q-approximator designed so its greedy action matches the servoing policy; FQI variant stabilizes training via rescaling and non-negativity constraints on weights.
- Methodological soundness: Multi-scale features and per-channel weighting provide practical relevance selection without end-to-end training.
- Empirical: Mid-level VGG features (up to conv4_3) work best; conv5_3 is too spatially invariant.
- Empirical: FQI weight-learning outperforms alternatives (CEM, TRPO) with orders-of-magnitude fewer trajectories.
- Empirical: Baselines include IBVS with ORB and strong tracker (C-COT), end-to-end TRPO on pixels and VGG; proposed method outperforms and runs faster at test time.
- Rigor and clarity: Paper is well written, provides algorithmic/training details, ablations, and code/simulator with results averaged over 100 held-out trajectories.
- Limitation: Evaluation only in a synthetic simulator; no real-robot or sim-to-real evidence limits external validity.
- Limitation: RL cost uses privileged 3D car pose from simulator, complicating real-world deployment without that supervision.
- Limitation: Linear-in-weights Q-function may limit expressivity for more complex trade-offs.
- Limitation: Missing some continuous-control baselines (e.g., DDPG), weakening sample-efficiency claims against broader model-free methods.
- Limitation: Dynamics predictive model trained from 100 trajectories (10k transitions); sensitivity to this dataset size is not explored.
- Suggested improvement: Add small-scale real-robot or hardware-in-the-loop experiments to demonstrate real-world feasibility.
- Suggested improvement: Show sensitivity plots for number of dynamics and FQI trajectories (performance vs data).
- Suggested improvement: Report confidence intervals/standard errors and variability across random seeds.
- Suggested improvement: Visualize which feature channels receive high weights and the spatial support used by the bilinear model for interpretability.
- Suggested improvement: Evaluate alternative backbones (ResNet, shallower nets) and effect of fine-tuning features with dynamics data.
- Suggested improvement: Explore image-only proxy rewards (tracker-derived or learned estimators) to remove dependence on privileged state.
- Suggested improvement: Discuss and profile computational footprint of the locally-connected bilinear model and consider compression/pruning.
- Suggested improvement: Ablate bias terms in bilinear dynamics and vary local neighborhood size to show robustness to moving objects and model choices.
- Questions for authors: How sensitive is FQI to non-negativity constraints on w and λ, and would negative weights help discount distractors?
- Question: How would you train without privileged 3D pose? Have image-only proxy costs been tried?
- Question: How robust is the method to mismatches between goal image x* and current viewpoint; does re-setting x* help?
- Question: Can you report variance over seeds and confidence intervals for main plots; how stable is FQI optimization?
- Question: Why were off-policy baselines like DDPG not included; would you expect similar sample-efficiency gaps?
- Question: How does performance change with amount/diversity of dynamics training data (fewer than 10k transitions)?
- Question: Can the bilinear dynamics be extended to multi-step planning (short-horizon MPC) without losing sample efficiency, and would that help harder tasks?
