- Addresses visual servoing by servoing in pretrained deep feature space with a locally connected bilinear feature dynamics and a greedy controller trained via sample‑efficient fitted Q‑iteration (FQI).
- Servo policy minimizes weighted Euclidean distance between predicted next‑step features and a goal feature map, with channel/scale weights learned via a linear Q approximator for efficient FQI.
- Demonstrates strong performance on a synthetic quadcopter–car following task using ~20 RL trajectories, outperforming pixel-space and keypoint methods and >100× more sample‑efficient than model‑free RL baselines.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Well-motivated integration of pretrained features, lightweight predictive models, and FQI yielding sample efficiency and robustness.
- Key reason 2: Thorough empirical comparisons and ablations showing advantages over classical IBVS and deep RL.
- Problem/motivation: Targets quick adaptation to new targets with minimal per‑target data, sitting between engineered IBVS and data‑hungry end‑to‑end RL.
- Approach soundness: Locally connected bilinear dynamics and multiscale features are sensible approximations; linear Q formulation with closed‑form inner minimization is elegant and sample‑efficient.
- Empirical: Best performance from layers up to conv4_3; conv5_3 too invariant; method generalizes to novel cars while pixels/conv5_3 degrade.
- Empirical comparisons: ORB‑based IBVS and tracker IBVS underperform; TRPO and end‑to‑end CNNs need many more samples and still underperform.
- Sample efficiency: FQI converges with only 2 iterations × 10 trajectories, with most gains after the first iteration.
- Rigor/clarity: Training procedures, losses, and costs are well described and code/simulator released; linearization for control is exact for the bilinear model.
- Limitation: Evaluation is purely simulated; sim‑to‑real domain shift may challenge dynamics and feature statistics.
- Limitation: RL cost uses privileged simulator state (GT 3D pose), limiting realism and potentially inflating sample‑efficiency claims.
- Limitation: Baseline fairness concerns (tracker speed, possible better modern trackers, architecture tuning for end‑to‑end baselines).
- Limitation: Limited theoretical guarantees about convergence with the proposed α,b re‑scaling; claims are empirical.
- Suggested improvement: Validate on real robot or photorealistic simulator and discuss sim‑to‑real strategies (feature choice, fine‑tuning).
- Suggested improvement: Demonstrate vision‑only reward alternatives (e.g., learned detector/pose estimator) and their effect on sample efficiency.
- Suggested ablations: Measure impact of α,b re‑scaling; compare fully vs locally connected bilinear models; report parameter counts and runtime.
- Suggested analysis: Report sparsity/selection patterns in learned weights across channels/scales.
- Suggested baselines: Add model‑based MPC using learned dynamics and test lighter backbones for speed–performance tradeoffs.
- Suggested robustness tests: Stronger occlusions, lighting changes, camera perturbations, and failure mode reporting.
- Clarify computational profile: Provide per‑frame latency breakdown (feature extraction vs control solve) for on‑board deployment discussion.
- Questions for authors: Sensitivity to VGG layer/pyramid choice and whether feature fine‑tuning was tried.
- Questions for authors: Can FQI learn with vision‑only costs (e.g., bounding box) or noisy rewards?
- Questions for authors: Parameter count and memory footprint of the locally connected bilinear model and scaling with resolution/action dims?
- Questions for authors: Do learned weights become sparse or concentrate on semantically meaningful channels?
- Questions for authors: Does multi‑step planning with learned dynamics improve over greedy one‑step control?
- Questions for authors: Robustness to camera calibration errors, latency, or dynamics mismatch?
- Questions for authors: Can FQI be extended to jointly learn feature weights and dynamics (or fine‑tune backbone) without losing sample efficiency?
