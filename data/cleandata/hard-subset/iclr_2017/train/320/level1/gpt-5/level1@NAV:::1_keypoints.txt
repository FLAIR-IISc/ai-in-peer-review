- Replaces hand-engineered features and Jacobians with learned components: pretrained multiscale VGG features (spatial-preserving), a bilinear predictive feature dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration (FQI) that learns per-channel/scale feature weights and control penalties.  
- Derives an analytic Q-approximator linear in weights, enabling a sample-efficient FQI procedure.  
- Achieves robust quadcopter car-following in simulation, outperforming pixel/keypoint IBVS, end-to-end TRPO, and unweighted variants, using as few as ~20 RL trajectories; code and videos released.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Strong, well-motivated integration of pretrained features, learned dynamics, and a tailored RL objective producing a practical, efficient controller.  
- Key reason 2: Convincing empirical evidence and ablations showing large sample-efficiency gains and generalization to novel targets.  
- Motivation/support: Principled use of pretrained features plus a lightweight bilinear model leverages invariances without large data.  
- Technical novelty: Q-parameterization makes the greedy action equivalent to a weighted one-step objective; the α,b re-scaling is a useful practical detail.  
- Empirical breadth: Evaluations across VGG layers, with/without weights, multiple baselines (TRPO, CEM, CNN end-to-end, ORB/IBVS, tracker-based IBVS), unseen cars, and runtime comparisons (~16 Hz vs ~1 Hz).  
- Claim scope: Experiments are in simulation using ground-truth pose; claims are limited to sample efficiency and robustness within that setting.  
- Suggestion: Report confidence intervals, success metrics, and failure-mode analysis (e.g., long occlusions, fast maneuvers).  
- Suggestion: Analyze sensitivity to VGG layer choice and number of RL trajectories; include a small hyperparameter sweep.  
- Suggestion: Clarify baseline fairness (data/feature access) and report TRPO variance across seeds.  
- Suggestion: Explore replacing ground-truth cost with self-supervised or learned proxies and consider a small hardware demo for real-world impact.  
- Suggestion: Provide memory/computation details of the locally connected bilinear model and discuss scalability to higher resolutions/backbones.  
- Question: How can the ground-truth cost be replaced in real settings without target pose; can detection/tracking suffice and how would that affect FQI?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to specific VGG layers or learned mixing of layers?  
- Question: What occurs if learned weights are unconstrained (allow negatives); does non-negativity aid stability?  
- Question: Can the dynamics model transfer across object categories or must it be retrained for new targets?  
- Question: How robust is the method to prolonged occlusions or target loss and does the policy reliably recover?  
- Question: Would lighter backbones (e.g., MobileNet) keep performance while improving throughput beyond ~16 Hz?  
- Question: Are there failure cases where higher-level invariances (e.g., conv5_3) help under severe appearance shifts?
