- Decision: Accept.

- Contribution: Proposes visual servoing in learned deep-feature space (modified VGG-16) using one-step lookahead controls that minimize predicted feature error to a goal image.

- Contribution: Learns a multiscale bilinear predictive model of feature dynamics with a locally connected variant for parameter efficiency and fast Jacobian computation.

- Contribution: Introduces a sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration (FQI) with a linear Q-function to learn per-channel/scale feature weights and control regularizers, matching the servo objective.

- Empirical result: Strong performance on a synthetic quadcopter car-following benchmark with robustness to viewpoint changes, occlusions, and generalization to novel cars.

- Empirical result: Large sample-efficiency gains over model-free deep RL (TRPO) and favorable runtime vs. tracker-based IBVS; ablations validate feature-layer choices and weighting schemes.

- Key reason for accept: Method is well-motivated and technically sound, combining pre-trained features, bilinear dynamics, and an FQI tailored to the servo objective.

- Key reason for accept: Clear empirical gains and ablations that support the paper’s claims.

- Problem/motivation: Hand-engineered features and interaction matrices are brittle; pre-trained conv features plus short-horizon predictive dynamics is a well-placed solution.

- Method clarity: Servo objective and relation to the linear Q-function are clearly specified and yield efficient closed-form or efficiently solvable policies.

- Method clarity: Bilinear locally connected dynamics and multiscale features are described in reproducible detail; training uses ADAM and efficient Jacobian computation.

- FQI details: FQI exploits scale invariance, uses L2 regularization and non-negativity constraints for stability; no formal convergence proof but construction is standard and well-matched to control structure.

- Experimental rigor: Fixed splits, >100 test rollouts, multiple baselines, and released code/environment improve reproducibility.

- Ablations: Mid-level VGG features (conv1_2–conv4_3) outperform pixels and conv5_3; learned weighting via FQI outperforms TRPO/CEM with same dynamics.

- Baseline comparisons: Classical IBVS and PBVS comparisons are informative; PBVS with ground-truth poses serves as a useful upper bound.

- Privileged-information caveat: RL cost and termination use ground-truth 3D car positions—needs discussion as a simulation limitation and alternatives for real settings.

- Sample-efficiency caveat: Dynamics training uses 10k transitions (100 trajectories) and RL uses 20 trajectories—these budgets should be separated and reported when comparing to model-free RL.

- Statistical reporting: Include standard deviations/confidence intervals and significance testing for reported averages.

- Generality limitation: Results are on a single synthetic domain; adding real-world validation or a second task would strengthen claims.

- Feature analysis request: Explain why conv5_3 underperforms, quantify spatial invariance loss, and analyze effects of dilated-conv modifications and fine-tuning.

- Overfitting/control complexity: Discuss regularization beyond L2, sparsity or grouping for many w and λ parameters, and show weight distributions/stability across runs.

- Failure modes: Provide qualitative failure examples (heavy occlusion, rapid viewpoint changes, similar distractors) and quantify robustness thresholds.

- Fairness in IBVS comparisons: Make explicit which IBVS variants (with ground-truth depths/dynamics) are reported and ensure consistent resource assumptions.

- Question — cost dependence: Does learning require ground-truth 3D positions for cost and termination, and how do purely image-based costs perform?

- Question — data efficiency: How does performance change with fewer/more dynamics samples and different FQI/iteration budgets; any overfitting when increasing θ dimensionality?

- Question — dynamics robustness: How sensitive is the controller to model mismatch or large controls that violate the bilinear approximation; is online re-linearization or model adaptation used?

- Question — feature weighting: What is θ’s dimensionality for the best setting (conv4_3), and are learned weights sparse or broadly distributed across layers/channels/scales?

- Question — target specification: Can the method follow a class exemplar unseen in the scene, and how does it disambiguate among multiple similar objects?

- Question — generalization: How well does the policy transfer across camera intrinsics, height ranges, or lighting conditions not seen in training?

- Question — end-to-end fine-tuning: Were joint fine-tuning experiments of the feature extractor with dynamics/weights attempted?

- Question — runtime: Report wall-clock training time for dynamics and FQI, and inference FPS on CPU/GPU; can lighter backbones (e.g., MobileNet) preserve performance with higher FPS?
