- Decision: Accept.  
- Main contribution: Novel visual servoing approach combining pre-trained deep features, bilinear predictive dynamics, and reinforcement learning.  
- Goal: Fast adaptation to new targets with minimal training data using learned features and predictive models.  
- Key methods: Uses multiscale pre-trained features (e.g., VGG), a bilinear dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration to learn feature weights.  
- Empirical claim: Substantial improvements in sample efficiency (over two orders of magnitude) vs. model-free RL and classical methods.  
- Robustness: Demonstrated resilience to occlusions, visual variations, and distractors.  
- Benchmark results: Strong performance on a synthetic car-following benchmark, outperforming IBVS, PBVS, and end-to-end deep RL in performance and compute.  
- Generalization: Shows generalization to novel targets in simulation.  
- Runtime: System runs at ~16 Hz, with most time spent on feature extraction.  
- Limitation: Experiments are simulated; real-world noise, sensor inaccuracies, and complex environments are not evaluated.  
- Suggested experiment: Test on real-world datasets or physical robots to strengthen claims.  
- Suggested ablations: More studies on the bilinear dynamics choices (locally connected vs. fully connected) and feature-weighting effects.  
- Computational improvement: Consider evaluating lightweight networks or optimizations to reduce feature extraction cost for real-time use.  
- Questions for authors: Performance on noisy/incomplete real-world observations and physical robots; applicability beyond target following (e.g., manipulation, multi-agent); sensitivity to choice of pre-trained features (VGG vs. others).
