- Proposes a novel visual servoing approach combining learned visual features, bilinear predictive dynamics, and reinforcement learning.  
- Focuses on target-following as the primary visual servoing task.  
- Uses standard deep features with a bilinear predictive model to learn an effective servo controller.  
- Claims robustness to visual variation, changes in viewing angle/appearance, and occlusions.  
- Employs a sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration algorithm to learn weights for visual features.  
- Provides clear, detailed method description and motivation rooted in prior work.  
- Validates approach with extensive experiments on a complex synthetic car-following benchmark.  
- Shows substantial improvement over pixel-based and hand-designed keypoint methods.  
- Reports more than two orders of magnitude better sample-efficiency than standard model-free deep RL.  
- Includes detailed comparisons with classical image-based and position-based visual servoing, demonstrating superiority.  
- Reviewer requests more discussion of limitations and future work, especially real-world challenges (lighting, occlusions).  
- Reviewer asks for details on computational efficiency and comparisons to other methods.  
- Reviewer requests clarifications: extension to multi-target/cluttered scenarios, handling partial occlusion/varying appearance, and real-world robotics applications/demonstrations.
