- Proposed method: combine pre-trained VGG features, a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and Fitted Q-Iteration for visual servoing.
- Key innovation: learn optimal weights for pre-trained features to focus on target objects and ignore distractors.
- Performance: achieves effective target following with only 20 training trajectories.
- Sample efficiency: shows >100× improvement over standard model-free deep RL and large gains versus pixel/keypoint methods.
- Robustness claim: robust to visual variations, viewpoint changes, appearance changes, and occlusions.
- Decision: Accept — clear contribution with strong empirical support.
- Motivation/literature: well-motivated problem and thorough literature review positioning the work against prior methods.
- Baselines: rigorous comparisons to pixel-based servoing, ORB keypoints, TRPO, and IBVS with ORB/C-COT tracker.
- Experimental setup: experiments on a well-defined synthetic car-following benchmark with consistent evaluation protocols.
- Ablation studies: feature representation and optimization ablations support the method’s effectiveness.
- Evidence for sample-efficiency: empirically supported claim that 20 trajectories suffice while others need hundreds/thousands.
- Requested analysis: deeper investigation into why VGG conv5_3 features underperform and their spatial invariance properties.
- Occlusion analysis: request quantitative evaluation of performance degradation under increasing/severe occlusion.
- Computational analysis: request detailed breakdown of time for feature extraction versus servoing across architectures.
- Feature generality: paper claims compatibility with “any pre-trained features” but lacks examples beyond VGG; suggest discussing alternatives.
- Question for authors: what properties of conv5_3 cause worse performance and how to adapt the method for such features?
- Question for authors: provide quantitative results showing performance at different occlusion levels.
- Question for authors: how does the required number of training trajectories scale with task/environment complexity and across different target objects?
