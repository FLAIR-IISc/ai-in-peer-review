- Proposes a sample-efficient visual servoing method combining pretrained VGG features, a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration to learn feature weights.  
- Uses pre-trained deep features (VGG) and a bilinear model to avoid manual feature engineering and extensive modeling.  
- Learns optimal feature weights with a sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration enabling rapid adaptation to new targets with only ~20 training trajectories.  
- Demonstrates robustness to visual variation, viewpoint changes, appearance differences, and some occlusions.  
- Empirically outperforms baselines (pixel-based, ORB keypoints, C-COT tracker, TRPO, IBVS), especially in sample efficiency.  
- Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and situates itself properly in related work.  
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation with multiple strong baselines and ablation studies supporting the method’s components.  
- Theoretical justification and mathematical detail for the bilinear dynamics model and Q-function approximation are provided.  
- Implementation details, environment setup, training procedures, and evaluation metrics are thoroughly described.  
- Contribution is both practically significant (fast adaptation) and theoretically interesting (sample-efficient RL for servoing).  
- Accept recommendation based on addressing a significant problem and large empirical gains in sample efficiency (>100× vs model-free RL).  
- Request to explain why VGG conv5_3 features perform worse (possible excessive spatial invariance or effects of removed pooling).  
- Suggest stronger experiments on severe occlusions to substantiate robustness claims.  
- Ask for hardware details for the reported runtime comparisons (16Hz vs 1Hz) since speed depends on platform.  
- Request clarification on how and why the modified VGG with dilated convolutions aids visual servoing.  
- Questions for authors: why conv5_3 is unsuitable, what information VGG features lose and how the bilinear model compensates, ablation separating learned weights vs bilinear model contributions, and sensitivity to the quality of the hand-coded policy used to train the dynamics.
