- Proposes a visual-servoing method combining learned visual features, a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and reinforcement learning for sample-efficient target adaptation.  
- Uses standard pre-trained deep features (VGG) with a bilinear predictive model to learn robust servoing from only ~20 training trajectories.  
- Demonstrates large improvements over pixel-based methods and hand-designed keypoints.  
- Achieves >100x better sample-efficiency compared to standard model-free deep RL.  
- Recommends acceptance, arguing the problem is important and the approach is well-motivated and builds on prior work.  
- Experimental evaluation is rigorous, with careful comparisons across seen and novel targets supporting the sample-efficiency claim.  
- Key strength: clear combination of pre-trained features with a simple, interpretable servo mechanism that learns feature weighting via fitted Q-iteration.  
- Method is computationally efficient (16 Hz) and robust to visual variations, occlusions, and viewpoint changes.  
- Suggests clarifying limitations of the synthetic environment and discussing generalization to real-world scenarios.  
- Recommends more detailed analysis of failure modes for the feature-weighting mechanism and improvements for challenging visual variations.  
- Questions for authors: how the method handles sustained, complex occlusions that fully hide the target, and which specific VGG layers were used and why.
