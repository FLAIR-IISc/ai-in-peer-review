- Proposes a visual servoing method combining pre-trained VGG features, a learned bilinear predictive dynamics model, and reinforcement learning for fast adaptation with minimal data.  
- Core contribution: weighted multiscale feature representation with a one-step lookahead policy optimized via sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration.  
- Achieves effective visual servoing on a synthetic car-following benchmark using just 20 training trajectories.  
- Reports over two orders of magnitude (≈100x) improvement in sample efficiency versus standard model-free deep RL.  
- Method is robust to visual variation, viewpoint changes, and occlusions in the evaluated setting.  
- Decision: Accept — well-motivated, scientifically rigorous, and empirically strong.  
- Well-situated in prior work; justifies using pre-trained features and learning feature weights rather than hand-designing them.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive, comparing to IBVS, end-to-end deep RL, and other baselines, with informative ablation studies.  
- Suggestion: discuss limitations under severe occlusion and performance in more complex real-world environments beyond the synthetic benchmark.  
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis of why mid-level VGG features (conv1_2–conv4_3) work best and why conv5_3 is less suitable.  
- Suggestion: clarify computational trade-offs and potential for speed improvements (e.g., replacing VGG).  
- Suggestion: discuss extensions to multiple target objects or more complex manipulation tasks beyond car following.  
- Question: request specific analysis of what aspects of deep features make conv5_3 unsuitable for Q-value approximation and whether processing can mitigate this.  
- Question: how would performance compare to classical IBVS when depth must be estimated rather than provided as ground truth?  
- Question: provide concrete occlusion scenarios/examples (e.g., from supplementary videos) that show the limits of robustness.  
- Question: compare sample efficiency to other model-based RL methods that use dynamics models (e.g., MBPO, PETS).
