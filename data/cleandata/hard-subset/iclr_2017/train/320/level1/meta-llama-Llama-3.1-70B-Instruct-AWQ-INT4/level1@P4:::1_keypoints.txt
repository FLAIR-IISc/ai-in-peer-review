- Proposes a novel visual servoing method combining learned visual features, a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and reinforcement learning, robust to visual variation, viewpoint changes, appearance, and occlusions.  
- Evaluated on a complex synthetic car-following benchmark, outperforming conventional methods and achieving >100× sample-efficiency over model-free deep RL.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novelty & impact: Novel combination with potential substantial impact for robotics and autonomous systems.  
- Technical soundness: Well-motivated approach with convincing technical treatment and results.  
- Clear problem statement: Clearly defines visual servoing challenges and limitations of existing approaches; solid related-work coverage.  
- Well-motivated approach: Justifies use of learned features + predictive model + RL and analyzes strengths/limitations.  
- Convincing experiments: Strong empirical comparisons to baselines on a challenging benchmark.  
- Clarity & organization: Well-written and organized, though related work could be more concise.  
- Experimental evaluation: Needs more detail on RL algorithm parameters and bilinear model settings.  
- Broader impact: Would benefit from a more detailed discussion of applications and limitations.  
- Questions for authors: Provide RL/bilinear model parameter details; plans for dynamic/multi-target extensions; and computational complexity/requirements.
