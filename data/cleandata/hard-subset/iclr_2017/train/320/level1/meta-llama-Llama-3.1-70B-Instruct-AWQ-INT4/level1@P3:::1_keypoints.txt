- Decision: Accept.

- Contribution: Combines learned visual features, a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and reinforcement learning for visual servoing.

- Key technical point: Shows standard deep features from classification models plus a bilinear predictive model can enable effective, learned visual servoing.

- Robustness claim: Demonstrated robustness to visual variation, viewpoint changes, appearance shifts, and occlusions.

- Motivation and framing: Tackles the well-defined, relevant robotics problem of visual servoing and clearly states limitations of standard approaches.

- Related work: Provides a thorough literature review and situates the method within visual servoing, RL, and deep learning.

- Experimental strength: Extensive, well-designed experiments on a complex synthetic car-following benchmark with substantial improvement over classical methods and model-free RL.

- Supporting argument 1: Addresses a concrete research question and articulates benefits of learned features and predictive models.

- Supporting argument 2: Approach is well-motivated and clearly builds upon and improves existing methods.

- Supporting argument 3: Strong empirical comparisons against model-free RL and classical image-based visual servoing.

- Suggested improvement 1: Include detailed comparisons with other deep-learning-based visual servoing methods (end-to-end and imitation learning).

- Suggested improvement 2: Report hyperparameter choices and sensitivity analyses (e.g., regularization coefficient, number of sampling iterations).

- Suggested improvement 3: Add visualizations of learned features and the servoing process to clarify how the method works.

- Question 1: How will the approach extend to multi-object tracking or dynamic, more complex environments?

- Question 2: What are the computational efficiency details (time complexity and memory requirements)?

- Question 3: How do the authors plan to mitigate overfitting common to deep learning approaches?
