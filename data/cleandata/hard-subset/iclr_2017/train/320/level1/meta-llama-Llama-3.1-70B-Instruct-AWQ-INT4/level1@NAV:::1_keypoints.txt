- Proposes combining learned visual features, learned predictive dynamics models, and reinforcement learning for visual servoing.  
- Shows standard deep features (from classification) with a bilinear predictive model can yield effective, robust visual servoing.  
- Robust to visual variation, viewpoint/appearance changes, and occlusions.  
- Evaluated on a complex synthetic car-following benchmark with substantial performance gains.  
- Achieves >100× improvement in sample efficiency over standard model-free deep RL.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Well-motivated: addresses the need to avoid manual design of visual features and dynamics models.  
- Strong experimental results supporting effectiveness and sample efficiency.  
- Technically sound: clear method and component explanations.  
- Comprehensive comparative evaluation vs. classical IBVS, PBVS, and model-free deep RL showing superiority.  
- Suggest clarifying relationship to related work on learned features and predictive dynamics in other applications.  
- Recommend providing more implementation details (specific architectures for features and predictive dynamics).  
- Recommend discussion of limitations (large data needs, risk of overfitting).  
- Questions for authors: provide implementation details, plans to address data/overfitting issues, and clarify relations to related work.
