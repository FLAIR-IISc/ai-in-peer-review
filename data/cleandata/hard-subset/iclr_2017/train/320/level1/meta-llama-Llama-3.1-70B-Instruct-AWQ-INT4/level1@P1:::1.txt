**Summary**

The paper proposes a novel approach to visual servoing by combining learned visual features, learned predictive dynamics models, and reinforcement learning. The authors aim to design algorithms that can learn a visual servo using low amounts of data, enabling quick adaptation to new targets. They demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on a complex synthetic car following benchmark, achieving substantial improvement over conventional methods and showing an improvement in sample-efficiency of more than two orders of magnitude over standard model-free deep reinforcement learning algorithms.
