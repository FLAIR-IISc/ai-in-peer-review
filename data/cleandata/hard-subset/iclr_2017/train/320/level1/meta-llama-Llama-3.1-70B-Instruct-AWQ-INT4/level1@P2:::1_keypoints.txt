- Proposes a novel visual servoing approach combining learned visual features, a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and reinforcement learning.  
- Focuses on target following and claims robustness to visual variation, viewpoint changes, appearance shifts, and occlusions.  
- Evaluated on a complex synthetic car-following benchmark with substantial improvement over conventional methods.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Acceptance reasons: tackles a relevant robotics problem and proposes a novel combination of methods.  
- Acceptance reasons: approach is well-motivated, grounded in literature, and clearly evaluated.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and provides a thorough literature review.  
- Evaluation is comprehensive and includes detailed comparisons to classical image-based visual servoing and deep RL baselines.  
- Key technical contribution: use of a bilinear predictive model to learn dynamics of visual features.  
- Novel methodological element: fitted Q-iteration to learn feature weights and optimize the servoing policy.  
- Requested improvement: provide more implementation details and hyperparameters for the bilinear model and fitted Q-iteration.  
- Requested improvement: include more visualizations (videos/images) of the servoing policy in action.  
- Requested improvement: discuss potential limitations such as large training data needs and overfitting risks.  
- Requested improvement: add comparisons with model-based RL and other deep learning-based visual servoing approaches.  
- Questions for authors: provide implementation/hyperparameter details; plans for multi-target servoing and occlusion handling; use of 3D or other sensor features; strategies to mitigate overfitting with limited samples.
