- Combines learned visual features, learned predictive dynamics models, and reinforcement learning to enable low-data, fast-adapting visual servoing.  
- Evaluated on a complex synthetic car-following benchmark, showing substantial improvement over conventional methods and >100× sample-efficiency vs. model-free deep RL.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Tackles a well-motivated, classic robotics problem with a clear introduction to its significance.  
- Method is novel and well-placed in the literature, clearly differentiating from prior work.  
- Paper is clearly and well-structured, detailing the visual feature dynamics model, RL algorithm, and experimental setup.  
- Provides thorough result analysis, comparisons to baselines, and discussion of limitations and future work.  
- Request for more implementation details: specific architecture and hyperparameters of the visual feature dynamics model.  
- Suggestion to add more visualizations (servoing policy plots, examples of feature dynamics in action).  
- Ask for discussion of potential applications beyond the synthetic benchmark and how to adapt to other visual servoing tasks.  
- Request for a detailed analysis of computational efficiency and time complexity of the RL algorithm and dynamics model.  
- Question about overfitting mitigation in the RL algorithm and which regularization techniques will be used.
