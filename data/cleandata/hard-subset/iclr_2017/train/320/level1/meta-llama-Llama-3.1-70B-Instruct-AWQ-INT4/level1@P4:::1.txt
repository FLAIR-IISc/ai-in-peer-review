**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

The paper proposes a novel approach to visual servoing that combines learned visual features, learned predictive dynamics models, and reinforcement learning. The authors demonstrate that standard deep features, obtained from a model trained for object classification, can be used together with a bilinear predictive model to learn an effective visual servo that is robust to visual variation, changes in viewing angle and appearance, and occlusions. The approach is evaluated on a complex synthetic car following benchmark, where it achieves substantial improvement over conventional approaches and shows an improvement in sample-efficiency of more than two orders of magnitude over standard model-free deep reinforcement learning algorithms.
