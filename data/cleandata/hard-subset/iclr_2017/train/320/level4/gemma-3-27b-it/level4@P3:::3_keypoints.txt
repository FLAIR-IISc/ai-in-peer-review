- Decision: Accept — novel and effective approach with convincing results and improved sample efficiency.  
- Proposal: Use pre-trained convolutional spatial feature maps, bilinear multi-scale dynamics, and reinforcement learning for visual servoing.  
- Learning objective: Policy learned by minimizing a regularized weighted average of distances to features predicted by the dynamics model.  
- Major strength: Extremely sample-efficient learning — successful with only 20 trajectories versus orders of magnitude more for model-free RL.  
- Use of pre-trained features: Leverages existing visual representations to reduce task-specific data needs.  
- Dynamics model: Bilinear one-step model provides computationally efficient prediction of action effects on features.  
- RL method: Fitted Q-iteration effectively learns optimal feature weightings.  
- Experiments: Synthetic but well-designed; show robustness to visual variation, viewpoint change, and occlusion.  
- Baselines and comparisons: Thorough comparisons to hand-designed feature methods and ground truth demonstrate advantages.  
- Reproducibility: CitySim3D environment and code are released.  
- Recommendation to improve realism: Increase diversity of 3D car models and background clutter to better test generalization.  
- Suggested extension: Explore end-to-end training by fine-tuning the convolutional network with dynamics and RL components.  
- Presentation feedback: Streamline implementation detail presentation in main text and move full details to supplementary material.  
- Minor issues: A few typographical errors noted (examples provided).  
- Question 1: How does performance degrade with more complex, nonlinear dynamics and have more sophisticated dynamics models been tried?  
- Question 2: Clarify motivation and effect of the trajectory-max-length penalty term on learning and exploration.  
- Question 3: Compare computational cost of TRPO versus FQI and whether TRPO’s performance justifies extra compute.  
- Question 4: What challenges (stability, architecture) do you anticipate for end-to-end fine-tuning of the convolutional network?
