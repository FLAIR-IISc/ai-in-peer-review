- Introduces a bilinear dynamics model to predict next-frame features y_{t+1} from current features y_t and action u_t.
- Formulates servoing with a Q-function that learns channel-specific weights to reflect feature importance.
- Proposes a refined Bellman-error optimization that leverages policy invariance to Q-function scaling.
- Thorough empirical investigation of methods linking actions and frame representations to predict future features.
- Justifies use of a locally connected bilinear model as a good trade-off between computational cost and representational power.
- Channel-weighting approach demonstrably outperforms unweighted methods in experiments.
- Novel Bellman-error optimization offers an elegant alternative to fixed-point iteration schemes in Q-learning.
- Lacks direct experimental comparisons between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models.
- Needs clearer distinction from standard Fitted Q-Iteration, especially concerning the fixed second term in Eq. 5.
- Experimental validation is limited in scope and relies on relatively simple simulations with no established benchmarks.
- Proposed VGG modification requires further empirical evidence to show performance gains over the original architecture.
- Overall technically sound and novel, but would be significantly strengthened by broader and deeper experimental analysis.
