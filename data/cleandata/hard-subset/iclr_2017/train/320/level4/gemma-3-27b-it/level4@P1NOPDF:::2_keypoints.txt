- Investigates visual servoing using a learned visual representation.  
- Proposes an action-conditional bilinear model of visual features extracted from a pre-trained VGG.  
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.  
- Addresses single-step prediction limitation by using a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values.  
- Evaluates approach using a fitted-value (fitted-Q) methodology.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically sound, and conceptually thorough.  
- Experiments convincingly show the advantages of the value-weighted objective.  
- Claims to be the first to outline a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm.  
- Empirical results indicate that leveraging pre-trained visual features improves generalization.  
- Reviewer recommends the paper as a valuable resource for robotics researchers.  
- Contribution to the representation-learning problem is limited; it does not explore end-to-end representation learning.  
- Policy representation is fixed and values are approximated linearly with problem-specific features, which may reduce alignment with ICLR’s emphasis on generalizable learning approaches.
