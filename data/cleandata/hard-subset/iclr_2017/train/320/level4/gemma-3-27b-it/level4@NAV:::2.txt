This paper explores the advantages of visual servoing through the utilization of a learned visual representation. The authors introduce a method for learning an action-conditional bilinear model of visual features – extracted from a pre-trained VGG network – and subsequently deriving a control policy via dynamics linearization. A novel multi-scale, multi-channel, and locally-connected bilinear model is presented. To address the single-step prediction horizon of the bilinear model, a weighted objective function is proposed, integrating long-term policy values. Evaluation is performed using a fitted-value iteration approach. 

The paper is exceptionally well-written, mathematically rigorous, and presents a comprehensive conceptual framework. The experimental results clearly demonstrate the effectiveness of the value-weighted objective, representing a significant contribution. Furthermore, the paper appears to be the first to detail a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm. The empirical validation of using pre-trained visual features for improved generalization is also noteworthy. 

Overall, I recommend this paper for acceptance as it holds considerable value for the robotics research community. However, considering the focus of this conference, I believe the contribution regarding the “representation” aspect is somewhat limited. While the paper effectively demonstrates the utility of a pre-trained VGG representation, it does not explore end-to-end learning of this representation – though this is not necessarily a requirement. The work dedicates a greater proportion of its effort to the control problem than to the representation learning problem itself. Additionally, the policy representation remains fixed, and values are approximated linearly using features specific to the problem domain. This does not diminish the paper’s merit, but it may be less directly aligned with the typical scope of ICLR.
