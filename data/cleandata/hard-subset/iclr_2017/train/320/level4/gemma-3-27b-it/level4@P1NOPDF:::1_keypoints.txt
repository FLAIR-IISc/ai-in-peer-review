- Proposes a bilinear dynamics model to predict next-frame features from current frame and action.
- Formulates visual servoing with a Q-function that learns per-feature-channel weights.
- Introduces an optimization for the Bellman error exploiting Q-parameter scale invariance to enable an elegant Fitted Q-Iteration.
- Argues convincingly for a locally connected bilinear model as a computationally efficient yet expressive choice.
- Shows weighted channel errors outperform unweighted treatment, validated in experiments.
- Presents a robust Bellman-error optimization scheme that improves over typical minimization approaches.
- Lacks experimental comparison between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models.
- Missing direct comparison between the proposed FQI and an iterative scheme that holds the second term fixed.
- Experimental results are small-scale and toy-like, making claims somewhat unconvincing despite sparse domain benchmarks.
- The proposed modified VGG requires experimental validation versus the original VGG.
- Overall: technically solid contributions but requires stronger, broader experiments to substantiate claims.
