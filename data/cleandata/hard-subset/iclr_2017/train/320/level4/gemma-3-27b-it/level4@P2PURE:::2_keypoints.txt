- Paper studies visual servoing using learned visual representations and action-conditional models.
- Proposes an action-conditional bilinear model of visual features extracted from a pre-trained VGG.
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.
- Derives control policies by linearizing the learned feature dynamics.
- Addresses single-step prediction horizon with a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values via a fitted-value approach.
- Paper is well-written, mathematically sound, and conceptually thorough.
- Experiments convincingly demonstrate the benefits of the value-weighted objective—significant contribution.
- Introduces what appears to be the first trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm.
- Empirical results show that using pre-trained visual features improves generalization.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance and believes the work is valuable for the robotics community.
- Contribution to visual representation learning is limited: relies on pre-trained VGG and does not explore end-to-end representation learning.
- Focus is more on control than representation learning; policy representation is fixed and values are approximated linearly with task-specific features, making it less central to ICLR's core themes.
