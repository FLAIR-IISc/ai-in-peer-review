This paper introduces a novel approach to learning visual servoing by combining learned visual features, predictive dynamics models, and reinforcement learning, specifically utilizing Q-iteration. The core contributions lie in a bilinear dynamics model for predicting future feature states given an action, a Q-function formulation that learns channel-specific feature weights for improved robustness, and an efficient optimization scheme for the Bellman error within the Q-learning framework.

I recommend **rejecting** this paper. While the technical ideas presented are interesting and demonstrate potential, the experimental validation lacks the robustness and scale necessary to convincingly support the claims made. Specifically, the experiments are conducted within a simplified synthetic environment, and the improvements demonstrated over baseline methods, while statistically significant, appear modest given the complexity of the proposed approach.

The paper effectively explores different methods for modeling the relationship between actions and future feature states, advocating for a locally connected bilinear model that balances computational efficiency and representational capacity. The rationale for this choice is sound, and the work builds logically upon previous research in dynamics modeling. However, the limited experimental comparison between the fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models weakens this argument. A more thorough ablation study quantifying the trade-offs between these approaches would strengthen the paper.

The concept of weighting feature channels to emphasize relevant objects is a valuable contribution. The experimental results clearly demonstrate that uniformly weighting all channels degrades performance, validating the effectiveness of the learned weighting scheme. Furthermore, the proposed optimization strategy for the Bellman error, leveraging the invariance of the optimal policy to Q-function scaling, offers a clever and elegant solution to a common challenge in Q-learning. A comparison to standard iterative approaches for solving the Bellman error, explicitly demonstrating the benefits of the proposed method, would further enhance this section.

However, the experimental evaluation remains the primary weakness. The scale and simplicity of the simulated environment raise concerns about the generalizability of the results to more complex, real-world scenarios. A more extensive evaluation on diverse, challenging benchmarks is crucial. The modification to the VGG network architecture, intended to improve spatial invariance, lacks a clear demonstration of its benefits; it is unclear whether the altered network outperforms the original. A direct comparison would be necessary. 

Furthermore, the experimental section focuses heavily on comparisons with baseline methods (image-based visual servoing, TRPO, etc.), but these comparisons feel somewhat incomplete. While the paper shows improvements, the scale of these improvements, relative to the added complexity, does not provide compelling evidence of a breakthrough. 

**Additional feedback to improve the paper:**

*   **Expand the experimental evaluation:** Consider testing on more realistic simulations or even real-world robotic platforms. Investigate scenarios with more complex lighting conditions, occlusions, and object interactions.
*   **Ablation studies:** Conduct thorough ablation studies to assess the contribution of each component of the proposed system, including the bilinear dynamics model, the feature weighting scheme, and the optimized Q-iteration algorithm.
*   **VGG Modification Validation:** Directly compare the performance of the modified VGG network with the original VGG network to validate the effectiveness of the architectural changes.
*   **Discussion of Limitations:** Acknowledge the limitations of the current study, such as the reliance on synthetic data and the relatively simple task environment. Discuss potential directions for future research to address these limitations.
*   **Clarify Notation:** Ensure consistent and clear notation throughout the paper. For example, explicitly define the dimensions and meanings of key variables.
*   **Expand Related Work:** While the related work section provides a decent overview, it could be expanded to include more recent advances in visual servoing, reinforcement learning, and deep learning for robotics.

**Questions for the authors:**

*   What is the computational cost associated with the locally connected bilinear model compared to the fully connected model, and how does this impact real-time performance on a robotic platform?
*   How sensitive is the performance of the proposed method to the choice of hyperparameters, such as the learning rate, regularization coefficient, and discount factor?
*   Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the reasoning behind the specific cost function used in the reinforcement learning experiments?
*   Do you anticipate that the proposed method would be more or less effective in scenarios with significantly more complex dynamics or a larger state space?
*   What steps would be required to adapt the proposed method to a real-world robotic platform, and what are the anticipated challenges?
