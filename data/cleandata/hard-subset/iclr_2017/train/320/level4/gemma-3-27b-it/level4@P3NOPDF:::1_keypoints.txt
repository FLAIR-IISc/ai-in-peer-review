- Novel approach: Q-iteration for visual servoing using a learned dynamics model and channel-wise weighting of feature errors.  
- Main contributions: bilinear (locally connected) dynamics model, channel-specific Q-function weights, and an optimization scheme exploiting Q-function scaling invariance.  
- Strength: locally connected bilinear model offers a good trade-off between computational efficiency and representational power.  
- Strength: channel-wise weighting addresses differing importance of feature channels; experiments show worse performance when unweighted.  
- Strength: optimization leverages scaling invariance to provide an elegant FQI-like scheme improving over direct Bellman-residual minimization.  
- Weakness: lacks quantitative comparison between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models.  
- Weakness: needs clearer differentiation from standard Fitted Q-Iteration and discussion of trade-offs from holding the second term fixed.  
- Experimental limitation: experiments are small-scale/toy simulations, weakening the strength of claims despite lack of benchmarks.  
- Experimental limitation: modified VGG feature extractor is not yet experimentally shown to outperform the original.  
- Overall: technically novel and promising but requires stronger experimental validation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing comparative dynamics evaluation, FQI connection clarity, and scalability of experiments.
