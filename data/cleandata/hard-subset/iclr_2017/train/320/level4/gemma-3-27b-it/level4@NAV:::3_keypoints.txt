- Introduces a novel visual servoing method that uses spatial feature maps from pre-trained CNNs combined with bilinear one-step visual dynamics and reinforcement learning for target following.
- Trains the servoing policy by minimizing a regularized, weighted average of distances to features predicted by the learned visual dynamics model.
- Provides controlled simulation experiments that rigorously quantify the benefits of using pre-trained deep features for visual servoing.
- Demonstrates substantial performance gains over multiple baselines, including methods using ground-truth bounding boxes.
- Proposes a principled method to learn multi-scale feature weights via an efficient trust-region fitted Q-iteration to reduce sensitivity to distractors.
- Shows strong sample efficiency, attributed to the chosen Q-function approximator and the model-based one-step feature dynamics.
- Publicly releases a virtual city environment as a benchmark for visual servoing research.
- Experiments would be strengthened by increased visual complexity and diversity (more clutter, distractors, varied 3D models and object appearances/motions).
- Recommends exploring end-to-end training (fine-tuning the convolutional network) to potentially improve generalization and directly assess deep representation benefits.
- Reproducibility is aided by clear formalism, but practical implementation details are numerous and scattered—suggest consolidating key choices and providing a public implementation.
- Notes minor typographical errors on page 2 that should be fixed.
- Overall assessment: a compelling, well-founded paper with authors responsive to feedback and capable of addressing the suggested improvements.
