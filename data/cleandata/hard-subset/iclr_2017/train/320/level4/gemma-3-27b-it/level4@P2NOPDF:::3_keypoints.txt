- Proposal: Use spatial feature maps from pre-trained convnets + multi-scale bilinear one-step visual dynamics integrated with reinforcement learning for target-following visual servoing.
- Contribution: Quantitative evaluation showing benefits of pre-trained deep features for visual servoing.
- Contribution: Method outperforms multiple baselines, including those using ground-truth bounding boxes.
- Contribution: Principled multi-scale feature weighting learned via an efficient trust-region fitted Q-iteration to handle visual distractors.
- Contribution: High sample efficiency from the chosen Q-function approximator combined with model-based one-step visual dynamics.
- Contribution: Release of a virtual city benchmark environment for visual servoing research.
- Suggestion: Increase environmental complexity (more clutter, diverse distractors, varied 3D car models, higher traffic) to better test robustness.
