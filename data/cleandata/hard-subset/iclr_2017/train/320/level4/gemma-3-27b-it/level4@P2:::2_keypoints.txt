- Paper proposes an action-conditional bilinear model to predict dynamics of VGG-extracted visual features and derives a control policy via linearization, with a multi-scale/multi-channel locally-connected variant.  
- Introduces a weighted objective incorporating long-term policy values estimated by a fitted Q-iteration approach (trust-region fitted-Q iteration).  
- Experiments show benefits of the value-weighted objective and the proposed trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm.  
- Demonstrates that pre-trained VGG features are useful for visual servoing and generalize well.  
- Recommendation: reject — work is well-written and sound but its main contribution is in control, not representation learning, making it less aligned with ICLR.  
- Major weakness: heavy reliance on fixed pre-trained VGG features without end-to-end representation learning or analysis of why those features work.  
- Major weakness: fixed policy representation and linear Q-function approximation limit exploration of more powerful, non-linear solutions.  
- Suggestion: discuss limitations of using pre-trained features and justify not pursuing end-to-end learning.  
- Suggestion: provide ablations on feature choice (VGG layers, alternative pre-trained networks) to assess impact.  
- Suggestion: investigate non-linear Q-function approximators to evaluate trade-offs.  
- Suggestion: clarify the novelty and differences of the proposed trust-region fitted-Q iteration compared to existing methods.  
- Reviewer questions: why not pursue end-to-end learning; how sensitive is performance to VGG layer choice; computational cost of non-linear Q approximators; detailed comparison and specific modifications in trust-region fitted Q-iteration?
