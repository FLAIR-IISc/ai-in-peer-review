- Proposes visual servoing method that uses pre-trained convolutional feature maps, multi-scale bilinear one-step dynamics, and reinforcement learning to learn a servoing policy minimizing a regularized weighted distance to predicted features.
- Recommendation: Reject due to reliance on a relatively simple synthetic environment limiting generalizability and conference impact.
- Strength: Controlled experiments quantify benefits of pre-trained deep features for visual servoing.
- Strength: Demonstrates clear performance gains over several baselines, including those using ground-truth bounding boxes.
- Strength: Principled learning of multi-scale visual feature weights via an efficient fitted Q-iteration algorithm.
- Strength: Good sample efficiency enabled by the Q-function approximator and model-based one-step dynamics.
- Strength: Provides an open-source virtual city environment for benchmarking.
- Weakness: Benchmark environment lacks sufficient visual complexity (clutter, diverse distractors, varied appearance and motion).
- Weakness: Limited diversity of 3D car models and insufficient distractor vehicles reduce robustness assessment.
- Suggestion: Evaluate end-to-end training and fine-tuning of the convolutional network to test representation learning benefits and generalization.
- Weakness: Implementation details are scattered across the paper and appendix, hurting clarity.
- Suggestion: Consolidate implementation details or provide a comprehensive open-source implementation.
- Minor issue: Typographical errors present (examples given on p.2).
- Suggestion (summary): Use more complex benchmarks, investigate representation learning/end-to-end training, improve documentation, and fix typos.
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the choice of pre-trained convolutional architecture (e.g., VGG vs ResNet)?
- Question: How were bilinear dynamics hyperparameters (e.g., regularization) selected and how sensitive is performance to them?
- Question: What alternatives to the chosen cost function were considered and what motivated the final choice?
- Question: What is the computational cost of feature extraction, dynamics prediction, and reinforcement learning, and what are the bottlenecks?
- Question: What concrete plans exist to evaluate the method in more realistic environments or on real robotic platforms/datasets?
