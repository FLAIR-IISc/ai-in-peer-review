- Contributions: proposes bilinear dynamics for next-frame features, a Q-function weighting feature channels, and an optimized Bellman-error FQI method.  
- Decision: Reject — technically sound but experiments are limited in scale/complexity and lack strong baselines.  
- Strength: Thorough exploration of action–frame connections and clear justification for locally connected bilinear model.  
- Strength: Feature-channel weighting is valuable and empirically improves performance over unweighted approaches.  
- Strength: Elegant optimization of Bellman error exploiting scale invariance of Q-function parameters.  
- Weakness: Experimental validation is underdeveloped and does not comprehensively compare the three dynamics models (fully connected, conv, locally connected).  
- Weakness: No direct comparison between the proposed FQI and a standard iterative FQI that holds the target Q fixed.  
- Weakness: Experiments are limited to a simple synthetic environment, limiting claims of real-world applicability.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to strong, image-based continuous-control RL baselines (e.g., DDPG, TD3, SAC).  
- Weakness: Modified VGG network is introduced without conclusive empirical evidence or quantitative ablation.  
- Weakness: Insufficient evidence of generalization to more complex scenarios or higher-dimensional inputs.  
- Suggestion: Expand evaluation to more realistic simulators or real robotic platforms.  
- Suggestion: Compare against established deep RL algorithms for continuous, image-based control.  
- Suggestion: Provide an ablation study showing the impact of the VGG modifications versus the original.  
- Suggestion: Report sensitivity to key hyperparameters (learning rate, regularization, etc.) and guidance for selection.  
- Suggestion: Include a detailed analysis of failure cases and potential remedies.  
- Suggestion: Present quantitative results with error bars or confidence intervals for statistical significance.  
- Question: Provide a detailed computational-cost and parameter-count comparison of the locally connected bilinear model versus the fully connected model.  
- Question: Why was γ=0.9 chosen and was any sensitivity analysis performed to justify it?  
- Question: How was exploration (Gaussian noise) ensured to be sufficiently diverse to avoid premature convergence?  
- Question: How does random initialization of the quadcopter relative to the car affect results and why was that distribution chosen?
