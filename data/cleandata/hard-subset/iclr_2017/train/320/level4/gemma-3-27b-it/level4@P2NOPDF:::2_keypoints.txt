- Introduces an action-conditional bilinear model of visual features extracted from a pre-trained VGG for visual servoing.  
- Bilinear model employs a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected architecture.  
- Addresses the single-step prediction limitation by using a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values for credit assignment.  
- Policy evaluation is performed via fitted-value iteration.  
- Presents what appears to be the first trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm in this context.  
- Empirical results strongly support the benefit of the value-weighted objective and the utility of pre-trained visual features for generalization.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically rigorous, and provides a thorough exploration of the problem.  
- Recommends acceptance for the robotics community due to solid and impactful contributions.  
- Concern: Limited contribution to representation learning for ICLR because it relies on pre-trained VGG and does not learn representations end-to-end.  
- Concern: Policy representation is fixed and value functions are linearly approximated with task-specific features, reducing generalizability and alignment with ICLR’s emphasis on learned representations.
