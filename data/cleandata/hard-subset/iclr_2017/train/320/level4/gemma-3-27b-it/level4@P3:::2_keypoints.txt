- Paper studies visual servoing by combining pre-trained visual features, learned bilinear action-conditional dynamics, and reinforcement learning for target following and rapid adaptation.  
- Uses multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected bilinear dynamics and VGG features with a weighted objective optimized via fitted Q-iteration.  
- Recommendation: reject.  
- Main reason: contribution does not align with ICLR’s core focus on novel representation learning.  
- Primary concern: relies on a fixed pre-trained feature extractor and does not investigate end-to-end feature learning or adaptation.  
- Imbalance: paper emphasizes control and fitted Q-iteration over representation learning contributions.  
- Additional critique: policy representation is fixed and value function uses a linear, problem-specific approximation, limiting representation learning novelty.  
- Strength: well-written and mathematically rigorous.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments that show benefits of the weighted objective and trust-region fitted Q-iteration.  
- Strength: demonstrates significant sample-efficiency gains over standard deep RL baselines.  
- Suggested improvement: explore fine-tuning VGG features or adding a learned adaptation layer to strengthen representation learning claims.  
- Suggested improvement: discuss limitations of fixed feature extractors and potential failure modes in more complex scenarios.  
- Suggested improvement: clarify and compare the novelty of the fitted Q-iteration method against existing approaches.  
- Suggested improvement: provide ablation studies isolating contributions of the bilinear model, multi-scale features, and weighted objective.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to choice of pre-trained features (layers or different networks).  
- Questions for authors: computational cost of learning/evaluating the bilinear dynamics model versus alternatives.  
- Questions for authors: how the regularization parameter ν was tuned and its impact on the learned policy.  
- Questions for authors: how the method handles significant non-rigid deformations or appearance changes of the target.
