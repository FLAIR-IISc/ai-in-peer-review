- Paper proposes combining pre-trained VGG features, a learned action-conditional bilinear dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration for visual servoing.  
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected bilinear model and a weighted objective that mixes one-step predictions with long-term value estimates.  
- Empirically shows benefits of the weighted objective and that pre-trained visual features aid generalization.  
- Proposes a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm (appears novel in this context).  
- Reviewer recommends rejection despite solid writing and math, viewing the contribution as incremental for ICLR.  
- Main concern: limited focus on representation learning—reliance on fixed pre-trained VGG without end-to-end adaptation.  
- Second concern: fixed policy representation and linear Q-function approximation reduce expressiveness and depart from ICLR’s emphasis on learned representations.  
- Supporting argument: combination of existing techniques (pre-trained features, bilinear models, fitted Q) lacks substantial novelty beyond application-specific integration.  
- Supporting argument: paper emphasizes control aspects more than advancing representation learning.  
- Supporting argument: linear Q approximation simplifies but limits policy expressiveness.  
- Suggested ablation studies to isolate contributions of the bilinear model, fitted Q-iteration, and VGG features.  
- Suggested comparisons to end-to-end learning methods to contextualize and justify design choices.  
- Suggested expanded discussion of limitations, especially fixed representation and linear Q approximation.  
- Suggested analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., learning rates, regularization).  
- Praised code availability but recommended better documentation for reproducibility.  
- Questions: Have you tried fine-tuning VGG during RL, and if not, why?  
- Question: What is the computational cost of the bilinear dynamics model versus simpler models, and is the complexity justified?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the regularization coefficient (ν) in fitted Q-iteration?  
- Question: Can you elaborate on limitations of the linear Q-function approximation and potential alternatives?
