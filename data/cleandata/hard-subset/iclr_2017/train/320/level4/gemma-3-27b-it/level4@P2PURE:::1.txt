This paper introduces a novel approach to visual servoing based on Q-iteration. The primary contributions are: 
1. A bilinear dynamics model for predicting next-frame features given an action and the current frame.
2. A formulation of servoing using a Q-function that learns weights for individual feature channels.
3. An effective method for optimizing the Bellman error to learn the Q-function.

Strengths:
+ The paper thoroughly investigates various methods for relating actions and frame representations to predict subsequent frame features. The authors convincingly argue for a locally connected bilinear model, which offers a good compromise between computational cost and representational power.
+ The concept of weighting feature channels to reflect object importance appears promising, and is shown to be more effective than treating all channels equally – experimental results consistently demonstrate performance degradation when using unweighted channels.
+ Solving the Bellman equation is a significant challenge in Q-learning. This work presents a robust optimization scheme grounded in the key insight that scaling the Q-function parameters does not alter the optimal policy. This enables a more streamlined Fitted Q-Iteration (FQI) approach compared to conventional schemes that fix the (ct + γ min Q_{t+1}) term. 

Weaknesses:
- While Section 4 provides rationale for the design choices, I would appreciate more experimental comparisons between the three dynamics model architectures: fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected.
- I am curious to see a more detailed comparison between the proposed FQI approach and an iterative approach where the second term in Eq. 5 is held constant.

Experimental Results:
- The experimental results are somewhat unconvincing due to the limited scale and reliance on toy simulations. However, it is important to acknowledge the current lack of established benchmarks in this research area.
- As noted in the pre-review discussion, the proposed modification to the VGG network requires experimental validation. It is currently unclear whether this modified VGG architecture outperforms the original version. 

Overall:
The paper presents a technically novel and well-formulated contribution. However, strengthening the experimental evaluation, as suggested above, would significantly reinforce the claims made.
