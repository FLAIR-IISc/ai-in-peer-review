- Decision: Accept — compelling approach combining learned representations with reinforcement learning and strong empirical results.
- Core idea: Use spatial feature maps from pre-trained CNNs within a model-based RL framework for visual servoing/target following.
- Method: Combine multiscale bilinear models predicting one-step feature-map dynamics with an RL policy that minimizes a weighted, regularized distance to predicted features.
- Representation choice: Leverages pre-trained convolutional features instead of hand-designed or pixel-based features for better generalization.
- Empirical gains: Substantial performance improvements over pixel-based and hand-designed feature baselines.
- Sample efficiency: Achieves strong performance with only ~20 training trajectories, outperforming state-of-the-art model-free deep RL.
- Benchmarking: Introduces a virtual city environment to aid reproducibility and future comparisons.
- Algorithmic detail: Fitted Q-iteration implementation is a critical component for learning effective feature weights.
- Suggestion — environment: Increase benchmark complexity (visual clutter, distractors, more diverse 3D models) to better test robustness.
- Suggestion — training: Explore end-to-end fine-tuning of the convolutional network to potentially improve performance and insights for joint learning.
- Suggestion — clarity/reproducibility: Consolidate dispersed implementation details or provide a comprehensive open-source implementation.
- Minor edits: Correct a few typographical errors (e.g., "relatively recent addition", "to directly learn").
- Question 1: How sensitive is performance to the accuracy of the bilinear dynamics model, and what happens with simpler/less accurate models?
- Question 2: Were other pre-trained networks (ResNet, Inception) evaluated, and why was VGG chosen?
- Question 3: How was the Q-function regularization parameter (ν) selected, and what effect does it have on the policy?
