- Paper studies visual servoing using learned visual features, combining pre-trained VGG features, action-conditional bilinear dynamics, and reinforcement learning for target-following and fast adaptation.
- Method derives a policy by linearizing predicted feature dynamics and introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected bilinear model variant.
- To overcome the bilinear model’s one-step horizon, the authors propose a weighted objective incorporating long-term policy values and optimize it with fitted Q-iteration.
- Empirically, the weighted objective and fitted Q-iteration are effective; experiments and comparisons are convincing.
- The proposed trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm appears to be a novel contribution.
- Reviewer recommends rejection because the main novelty is in the control framework, not in representation learning.
- The paper demonstrates utility of existing pre-trained VGG features but does not learn or tailor representations specifically for visual servoing.
- Policy representation is fixed and Q-values are approximated linearly using problem-specific features, limiting broader impact on representation learning.
- Suggested improvement: explore fine-tuning or modifying pre-trained features to better support visual servoing.
- Suggested improvement: provide ablation study on different VGG feature levels and explain why deeper features can perform worse.
- Suggested improvement: include computational complexity analysis of the bilinear model and fitted Q-iteration for real-time control relevance.
- Suggested improvement: test generalization in more complex and visually diverse environments.
- Clarifying question: explain the mathematical properties that make the linear Q-function approximator tractable (Section 5.2).
- Clarifying question: were TRPO hyperparameters optimized for sample efficiency or were longer TRPO training runs due to computational constraints (Section 6.3)?
- Clarifying question: why was the bilinear model chosen over alternatives, what alternatives were considered, and what are its limitations?
