- Introduces a bilinear dynamics model to predict future frame features from current features and actions.  
- Proposes a Q-function that learns channel-specific weights to prioritize informative visual features during servoing.  
- Presents an optimization method for the Q-function leveraging Bellman-error properties and scaling invariance for more stable Fitted Q-Iteration.  
- Justifies choosing a locally connected bilinear model as a trade-off between computation and representational power.  
- Channel-wise weighting is effective: experiments show it outperforms uniform channel weighting.  
- The Bellman-error-based optimization is a significant contribution, enabling more stable and efficient policy learning than direct Bellman residual minimization.  
- Lacks direct empirical comparison between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models.  
- Needs clearer differentiation from standard Fitted Q-Iteration, especially regarding the fixed second term in Eq. 5.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope and scale, relying on toy simulations rather than established benchmarks.  
- The modified VGG feature extractor requires more rigorous validation to show consistent improvement over the original.  
- Overall: technically sound and novel approach, but impact depends on more extensive experimental validation of dynamics models and VGG modification.
