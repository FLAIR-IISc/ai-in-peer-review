- Paper studies visual servoing using a learned visual representation and control via dynamics linearization.
- Proposes an action-conditional bilinear model of visual features extracted from a pre-trained VGG network.
- Introduces a novel multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected bilinear model architecture.
- Identifies the single-step prediction horizon issue and proposes a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values.
- Evaluates the approach using fitted-value iteration.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, mathematically rigorous, and presents a comprehensive conceptual framework.
- Experimental results clearly show the effectiveness of the value-weighted objective.
- Claims to be the first to detail a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm.
- Provides empirical validation that pre-trained visual features improve generalization.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing considerable value for the robotics community.
- Notes limited contribution on the “representation” side—does not explore end-to-end representation learning.
- Points out that the policy representation is fixed and values are approximated linearly with problem-specific features, making alignment with ICLR’s typical scope less direct.
