- Proposes a novel visual servoing approach using Q-iteration with three main contributions: a bilinear dynamics model, a Q-function that weights feature channels, and an optimized Bellman-error learning method.
- Advocates a locally connected bilinear model as a good trade-off between computational cost and expressive power for predicting next-frame features.
- Shows weighted feature-channel Q-function improves performance over unweighted error aggregation.
- Introduces a robust Bellman-error optimization exploiting scale invariance of Q-function parameters to enable a cleaner FQI-style approach.
- Lacks sufficient experimental comparison between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics architectures.
- Needs clearer distinction and discussion of differences from standard FQI, especially about holding the second term in Eq. 5 fixed during optimization.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to small-scale/toy simulations, which is somewhat unsatisfying given the claims.
- The proposed modification to VGG requires empirical validation; it’s unclear if it outperforms the original. 
- Overall: technically solid and novel, but stronger experiments are needed to fully substantiate the claims.
