- Introduces a novel visual servoing approach combining learned visual features, predictive bilinear dynamics, and Q-iteration reinforcement learning.  
- Core contributions: locally connected bilinear dynamics model, channel-specific Q-function feature weighting, and an efficient Bellman error optimization scheme.  
- Reviewer recommendation: reject due to insufficient experimental validation and limited scale.  
- Experiments run in a simplified synthetic environment, raising generalizability concerns to real-world scenarios.  
- Improvements over baselines are statistically significant but modest relative to the approach's complexity.  
- Rationale for using a locally connected bilinear model is sound, balancing efficiency and representational capacity.  
- Comparison between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected models is incomplete; a stronger ablation is needed.  
- Learned channel weighting is a valuable idea and experiments show uniform weighting degrades performance.  
- The Bellman error optimization leveraging policy invariance to Q-scaling is clever and elegant.  
- Missing empirical comparison to standard iterative Bellman solvers to demonstrate concrete benefits of the proposed optimization.  
- VGG architecture modification lacks direct evidence of improvement versus the original network.  
- Baseline comparisons (IBVS, TRPO, etc.) are present but feel incomplete and do not justify added complexity.  
- Suggested improvements: expand experiments to realistic/real-world setups and harder conditions (lighting, occlusions, interactions).  
- Suggested experiments: thorough ablation studies for dynamics model, feature weighting, and Q-iteration algorithm.  
- Paper should explicitly compare modified vs. original VGG and acknowledge current limitations and future directions.  
- Additional fixes: clarify notation (dimensions/meanings of variables) and expand related work to include recent advances.  
- Questions for authors: runtime and computational cost of locally connected bilinear model vs fully connected; real-time feasibility.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to hyperparameters (learning rate, regularization, discount factor).  
- Questions for authors: justification for the chosen RL cost function and expected performance in higher-complexity dynamics/state spaces.  
- Questions for authors: steps and anticipated challenges to adapt the method to real-world robotic platforms.
