- Proposes a novel visual servoing method using Q-iteration with three main contributions: bilinear dynamics model, Q-function weighting of feature channels, and an improved Bellman error optimization.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection despite promising technical ideas due to weak experimental validation and limited practical impact.  
- Positive: locally connected bilinear dynamics convincingly balances compute and expressiveness compared to FC and conv alternatives.  
- Positive: channel-weighted Q-function helps focus on relevant objects and outperforms an unweighted baseline.  
- Positive: FQI optimization exploiting Q-function scaling invariance is an elegant improvement over standard FQI.  
- Major concern: experiments are limited and rely on a synthetic environment, raising generalizability concerns.  
- Major concern: insufficient comparison to established reinforcement learning baselines (e.g., PPO, SAC) and contemporary methods.  
- Missing: direct empirical comparison between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models.  
- Missing: ablation/analysis showing benefits of the proposed FQI optimization vs. standard iterative target-Q approaches.  
- Missing: no evidence that the modified VGG architecture provides advantage over the original.  
- Missing: unclear how performance scales with increased visual complexity (distractors, lighting) or transfers to real-world data.  
- Recommendation: expand experimental evaluation to include established RL baselines and more realistic or real-world environments.  
- Recommendation: run direct comparisons of FC, conv, and locally connected bilinear models.  
- Recommendation: provide comparative analysis of the proposed FQI scheme vs standard iterative FQI.  
- Recommendation: experimentally validate the VGG modification against the unmodified network.  
- Recommendation: discuss limitations of synthetic benchmarks and challenges for real-world deployment.  
- Question: what is the computational cost and inference time compared to other visual servoing methods?  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to hyperparameters (learning rate, regularization, exploration noise) and to proposed design choices?  
- Question: why choose a bilinear model for feature dynamics and what alternative models were considered?  
- Question: how does performance degrade with increasing visual complexity (distractors, lighting changes)?  
- Question: what steps and challenges are expected to deploy this approach on a real robot platform?
