- Proposes an action-conditional bilinear model of visual features extracted from a pre-trained VGG network to derive control policies via linearized dynamics.  
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.  
- Recognizes one-step prediction limitation and adds a weighted objective that incorporates long-term values of the current policy.  
- Evaluates the method using a fitted-value approach.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically sound, and conceptually thorough.  
- Experiments clearly show the benefits of the value-weighted objective—major contribution.  
- Presents what appears to be the first outline of a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm.  
- Empirical results indicate using pre-trained visual features improves generalization.  
- Recommends the paper as valuable for robotics researchers.  
- Criticism: contribution to the “representation” problem is limited—does not explore end-to-end learned representations.  
- Paper focuses more on control than representation learning, with a fixed policy representation.  
- Values are approximated linearly using problem-specific features, which may limit relevance to ICLR’s representation-learning focus.
