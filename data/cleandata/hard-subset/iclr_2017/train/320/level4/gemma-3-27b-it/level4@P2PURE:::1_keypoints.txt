- Introduces a bilinear dynamics model to predict next-frame features from current frame and action.
- Formulates servoing with a Q-function that learns per-feature-channel weights reflecting object importance.
- Proposes an optimization scheme for the Bellman error that leverages Q-parameter scaling invariance to simplify Fitted Q-Iteration.
- Thorough evaluation of architectures motivates a locally connected bilinear model as a cost–representational-power compromise.
- Empirical evidence shows learned channel weighting outperforms unweighted features.
- The Bellman optimization method is robust and more streamlined than conventional fixed-term schemes.
- Lacks sufficient experimental comparison between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models.
- Needs a detailed comparison between the proposed FQI scheme and an iterative approach that holds the second term in Eq.5 constant.
- Experimental validation is limited in scale and relies on toy simulations, reducing conviction of results.
- The proposed modification to the VGG network is not experimentally validated against the original VGG.
- Overall: technically novel and well-formulated but would benefit from stronger, broader experimental evaluation.
