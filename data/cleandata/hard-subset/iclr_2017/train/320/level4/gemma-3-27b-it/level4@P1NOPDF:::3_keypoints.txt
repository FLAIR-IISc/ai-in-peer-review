- Paper proposes visual servoing by combining pre-trained CNN spatial feature maps with bilinear one-step visual-dynamics models and RL to learn a feature-distance minimization policy.
- Controlled simulation experiments show benefits of using pre-trained deep features for target following.
- Method outperforms several sensible baselines, including those using ground-truth bounding boxes.
- Principled multi-scale feature weighting and an efficient trust-region fitted Q-iteration address distractors.
- Good sample efficiency achieved via the chosen Q-function approximator and model-based one-step visual feature dynamics.
- Authors provide an open-source virtual city environment for benchmarking visual servoing.
- Experiments need greater visual complexity (more clutter, distractors, diverse and realistic 3D car models) to better test robustness.
- Evaluate end-to-end fine-tuning to assess representation learning benefits and generalization in harder visual conditions.
- Improve reproducibility by consolidating practical implementation details and linking to an open-source implementation.
- Minor typos noted on p.2 ("learning is a relatively recent addition", "be applied to directly learn").
- Overall: solid, interesting contribution with promising revisions and room for stronger experiments and clearer reproducibility.
