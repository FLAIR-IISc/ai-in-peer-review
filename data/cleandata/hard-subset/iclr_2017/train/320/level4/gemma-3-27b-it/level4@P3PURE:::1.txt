The paper proposes a novel approach for learning visual servoing based on Q-iteration. The main contributions of the paper are:
1. A bilinear dynamics model for predicting the next frame (features) based on the action and current frame.
2. A formulation of servoing with a Q-function that learns weights for different feature channels.
3. An elegant method for optimizing the Bellman error to learn the Q-function.

Pros:
+ The paper effectively explores different methods for connecting the action (u<sub>t</sub>) and frame representation (y<sub>t</sub>) to predict next-frame features (y<sub>t+1</sub>). The authors advocate for a locally connected bilinear model, arguing that it strikes a balance between computational cost and expressive power.
+ The idea of weighting different feature channels to capture the importance of objects within those channels appears more effective than treating errors across all channels equally. This is experimentally validated, with unweighted performance consistently suffering.
+ Solving the Bellman error is a challenging problem in Q-learning approaches. This paper presents a robust optimization scheme based on the key observation that scaling the Q-function parameters does not affect the optimal policy chosen. This enables a more elegant FQI approach compared to typical optimization schemes that directly minimize (c<sub>t</sub> + γ min<sub>u</sub> Q<sub>t+1</sub>).

Cons:
- While Section 4 provides strong arguments for different architectural choices, I would have appreciated more experimental results comparing the three approaches – fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics.
- I would have liked to see a clearer distinction between the proposed approach and standard FQI, particularly regarding the implications of holding the second term in Eq. 5 fixed during iterative optimization.

Experimental results:
- Overall, I find the experimental results somewhat unsatisfying, given the small scale and use of toy simulations. However, the current lack of established benchmarks in this domain should be acknowledged.
- As noted in the pre-review discussion, the proposed modification to the VGG network requires experimental validation. In its current form, it is unclear whether the modified VGG would outperform the original version.

Overall, the paper presents a solid contribution in terms of technical novelty and problem formulation. However, stronger experiments, as suggested above, would bolster the paper’s claims.
