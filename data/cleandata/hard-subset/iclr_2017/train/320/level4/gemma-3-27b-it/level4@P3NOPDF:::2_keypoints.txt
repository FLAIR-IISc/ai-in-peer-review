- Proposes an action-conditional bilinear model of visual features from a pre-trained VGG for visual servoing control.
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.
- Derives a control policy via dynamics linearization based on the learned model.
- Develops a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values to mitigate short-sighted single-step prediction.
- Evaluates the approach using a fitted-value method and demonstrates empirical improvements.
- Provides rigorous analysis, clear writing, and strong experimental validation of the value-weighted objective.
- Appears to be the first to outline a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm in this context.
- Shows that leveraging pre-trained visual features improves generalization in visual servoing.
- Recommends acceptance based on significance and execution of the contribution.
- Critiques limited novelty on the representation-learning front since representations are pre-trained rather than learned end-to-end.
- Notes the policy representation is fixed and the value function is approximated linearly with problem-specific features, limiting generalization.
- Suggests the paper’s emphasis on control over representation learning may place it slightly outside ICLR’s core focus on representation/function-approximation innovations.
