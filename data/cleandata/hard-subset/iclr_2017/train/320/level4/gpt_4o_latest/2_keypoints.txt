- Studies visual servoing using a learned action-conditional bilinear model over visual features.  
- Extracts features from a pre-trained VGG and derives a policy by linearizing the learned dynamics.  
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.  
- Bilinear model predicts only one-step dynamics, so authors add a value-weighted objective to account for long-term policy returns.  
- Uses a fitted-value approach for evaluation.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically rigorous, and conceptually thorough.  
- Experiments effectively demonstrate the advantage of the value-weighted objective—highlighted as a key contribution.  
- Proposes what appears to be the first trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm.  
- Empirical results show pre-trained visual features improve generalization.  
- Recommends the paper as valuable for robotics researchers.  
- Contribution to representation learning is limited: relies on pre-trained VGG and does not explore end-to-end representation learning.  
- Policy representation is fixed and values are approximated linearly with problem-specific features, making the work more control-focused and potentially less aligned with ICLR’s representation emphasis.
