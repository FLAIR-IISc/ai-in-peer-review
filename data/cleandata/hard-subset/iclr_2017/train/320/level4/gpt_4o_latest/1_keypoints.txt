- Introduces a bilinear dynamics model to predict next-frame features from current frame and action.
- Proposes a Q-function formulation that assigns weights to different feature channels for servoing.
- Develops a refined optimization approach for the Bellman error to learn the Q-function effectively.
- Thorough exploration of methods relating action and frame representation, advocating locally connected bilinear model for efficiency and expressiveness.
- Locally connected bilinear model balances computational cost and representational power compared to alternatives.
- Weighting feature channels is compelling and empirically outperforms unweighted approaches.
- Robust optimization insight: scaling Q-function parameters doesn’t change the optimal policy, enabling a more elegant FQI.
- Lacks experimental comparisons between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models.
- No direct empirical comparison between the proposed FQI approach and iterative methods that fix the second term in Eq. 5.
- Experimental results are limited and rely on toy simulations, reducing impact and generality.
- Modified VGG architecture changes are unvalidated; unclear if they outperform the original.
- Overall: solid technical contributions and formulation, but stronger experimental validation is needed to boost credibility.
