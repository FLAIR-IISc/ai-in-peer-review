- Paper tackles visual servoing for target following by modeling one-step multi-scale CNN feature dynamics with bilinear transforms and learning a policy via RL minimizing distance to predicted features.  
- Contribution: thorough simulation studies quantifying the value of pre-trained deep features for visual servoing.  
- Contribution: clear performance gains over strong baselines, including methods using ground-truth bounding boxes.  
- Contribution: principled method to learn multi-scale feature weights using an efficient trust-region fitted Q-iteration to handle distractors.  
- Contribution: strong sample efficiency attributed to the chosen Q-function approximator and the one-step model-based visual feature dynamics.  
- Contribution: release of an open-source virtual city environment for benchmarking visual servoing.  
- Suggestion: evaluate on more challenging, visually complex benchmarks (more clutter, distractors, varied appearance/motion, diverse 3D car models).  
- Suggestion: assess end-to-end training / fine-tuning of convolutional networks to test generalization and the role of representation learning.  
- Suggestion: improve reproducibility and presentation by consolidating implementation details, summarizing critical aspects in one section, and providing a complete open-source implementation link.  
- Minor typographical corrections: “relative[ly]” → “relatively” and “be applied [to] directly learn” → “be applied to directly learn”.  
- Conclusion: solid and interesting paper with revisions addressing pre-review feedback; authors can likely address suggested improvements to increase impact.
