- Proposes visual servoing using an action-conditional bilinear dynamics model over visual features extracted from a pre-trained VGG network.  
- Derives a policy by linearizing the learned bilinear dynamics.  
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally connected variant of the bilinear model.  
- Notes the bilinear model predicts only one step ahead, so proposes a value-weighted objective to incorporate long-term returns.  
- Uses a fitted-value approach for policy evaluation.  
- Presents a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm, claimed as a first.  
- Experiments show the value-weighted objective improves performance.  
- Empirical results indicate pre-trained visual features aid generalization.  
- Paper is well written, mathematically rigorous, and conceptually thorough.  
- Recommends the paper for robotics researchers but criticizes limited novel contribution to representation learning.  
- Notes the work does not explore end-to-end learning of visual features and focuses more on control than representation.  
- Points out the policy representation is fixed and value approximation is linear with problem-specific features, limiting alignment with ICLR’s emphasis.
