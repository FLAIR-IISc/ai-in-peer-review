- Paper tackles visual servoing by combining multiscale modified VGG features, a learned short-horizon bilinear dynamics model, and a sample-efficient RL scheme.  
- Main method: one-step model-based servo policy minimizing predicted next-step feature error with per-channel/scale weights learned via a linear-in-weights FQI.  
- Empirical claim: more sample-efficient than model-free RL, more robust than pixels/keypoints, and generalizes to unseen cars in a synthetic quadcopter–car-following benchmark.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Primary reason 1: Empirical validation is limited and missing critical ablations supporting core design choices (locally connected vs FC vs conv bilinear dynamics; modified vs original VGG; proposed FQI vs standard target-network).  
- Primary reason 2: Evaluation restricted to a single synthetic benchmark and robustness claims (occlusion, distractors, viewpoint) are qualitative, so generality is unclear.  
- Strength: Problem is timely and motivation to use pre-trained features plus learned predictive dynamics for data efficiency is well justified.  
- Strength: The one-step optimal control formulation yields an analytically tractable Q-function and a clever scaling trick to stabilize/speed FQI.  
- Criticism: Locally connected bilinear dynamics are argued but not experimentally compared to FC or convolutional variants (even on smaller layers).  
- Criticism: No quantitative comparison between the modified VGG (no pooling + dilation) and the original VGG to show improved spatial detail/servoing.  
- Criticism: No direct comparison of the proposed FQI optimization with standard fitted Q-iteration (target networks) or supervised weight tuning.  
- Experimental scope issue: Results use only one synthetic domain (five training cars + held-out set) and lack quantified robustness metrics (success rates vs occlusion/distractors/viewpoint).  
- Baseline concerns: Need clarity on hyperparameter tuning parity (e.g., TRPO), fairness in compute/time budgets, and stronger geometric baselines (e.g., IBVS with depth or dense photometric methods).  
- Recommended ablations: compare bilinear dynamics variants with matched parameter counts, modified vs original VGG (and lighter backbones), and FQI vs standard target-network FQI / supervised tuning.  
- Recommended evaluations: quantify robustness under controlled occlusion/distractor/viewpoint/noise, test generalization to more target categories and training-data scaling, and report std/CI and normalized metrics.  
- Realism: Add a different simulated benchmark or real-world experiments (or offline real-image failure analysis) to test sim-to-real viability.  
- Implementation detail request: Discuss FQI stability/convergence, sensitivity to regularization ν, inner iterations K, exploration variance, and validation/selection procedures.  
- Questions for authors (summary): provide quantitative comparisons for dynamics model variants, VGG modification effects, FQI vs target-network FQI, robustness quantification, data-efficiency vs number of trajectories, TRPO tuning details and IBVS performance reasons, and training/inference compute budgets and real-time feasibility.
