- Learns an action-conditional bilinear model over VGG-extracted visual features and derives a control policy by linearizing the dynamics.  
- Proposes a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally connected variant of the bilinear model.  
- Addresses the one-step prediction limitation by introducing a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy value; uses a fitted-value approach for policy evaluation.  
- Paper is well written, mathematically sound, and conceptually thorough.  
- Experiments clearly demonstrate the advantage of the value-weighted objective—viewed as a key contribution.  
- Presents what appears to be the first description of a trust-region fitted Q-iteration algorithm.  
- Empirically shows that leveraging pre-trained visual features improves generalization.  
- Recommends acceptance and expects the work to be useful to robotics researchers.  
- Limited contribution to representation learning: relies on pre-trained VGG features and does not explore end-to-end representation learning.  
- Policy representation is fixed and the value function is linearly approximated with problem-specific features, reducing alignment with ICLR’s primary focus on representation learning.
