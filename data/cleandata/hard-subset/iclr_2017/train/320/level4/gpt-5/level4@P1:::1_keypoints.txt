- Proposes a visual servoing framework using pre-trained convolutional features, a learned locally-connected bilinear dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration to learn feature/control weights for one-step lookahead control.  
- Learns short-horizon visual feature dynamics with a bilinear model instantiated as locally connected bilinear dynamics to balance expressiveness and tractability.  
- Defines the servoing policy as the minimizer of a weighted next-feature prediction error, with feature-channel weights learned via a Q-function linear in those weights.  
- Introduces an FQI scheme that leverages invariance to non-negative scaling of Q-parameters for more stable and efficient Bellman error minimization.  
- Empirically demonstrates on a synthetic car-following benchmark robustness to distractors/occlusions and improved sample efficiency vs model-free deep RL.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Primary rejection reason: insufficient empirical validation—only a single synthetic setup with small-scale experiments and no real-world or diverse benchmarks.  
- Secondary rejection reason: missing crucial ablations to support central design choices (locally connected bilinear dynamics and VGG modifications).  
- Paper targets a timely problem: sample-efficient visual servoing that generalizes to new targets with little task-specific data.  
- Motivation and relation to prior work are well-articulated; using pre-trained CNN features and short-horizon predictive models is reasonable.  
- Positive empirical points: learning feature-channel weights improves performance over unweighted baselines.  
- Positive empirical points: FQI scheme exploiting scale invariance is principled and appears useful.  
- Limitation: evaluation confined to one simulated task, limiting evidence of robustness and generalization.  
- Limitation: no head-to-head comparisons among fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected bilinear dynamics.  
- Limitation: VGG modifications (removed pooling, dilations) are not empirically validated against original pretrained features.  
- Recommended ablations: compare dynamics variants (fully connected vs conv vs locally connected) on prediction, runtime, memory, and servoing performance.  
- Recommended ablations: test sensitivity to local neighborhood size and channel-wise independence assumptions.  
- Recommended feature tests: compare modified VGG vs original VGG across layers/resolutions for prediction quality and servoing.  
- Recommended RL/optimization analysis: compare proposed FQI vs iterative approach that keeps the target Q fixed, and report convergence/stability metrics and sensitivity to regularization/exploration.  
- Recommended generalization tests: evaluate on more target types/environments, including real-robot or higher-fidelity sims, and controlled occlusion/viewpoint stress tests.  
- Recommended practical analysis: provide runtime/memory profiling, discuss real-time trade-offs, and analyze scaling with number of channels and feature scales.  
- Broader comparisons: include stronger model-based baselines and tuned model-free baselines with equalized sample budgets.  
- Data ablations: study the number of trajectories used for FQI and the impact of dynamics training data size.  
- Questions for authors: provide head-to-head comparisons among dynamics variants and sensitivity to neighborhood size/channel independence.  
- Questions for authors: do modified VGG features outperform original VGG, and how do different VGG layers trade off invariance vs spatial precision?  
- Questions for authors: empirically compare your FQI scheme to a fixed-target Bellman-error minimization and report sensitivity to ν, γ, and exploration variance.  
- Questions for authors: how does the policy generalize to drastically different target classes, real images, or hardware, and how robust is it to mis-specified costs or long occlusions?  
- Questions for authors: what are training/test runtime and memory budgets, and could lighter backbones (ResNet-18, MobileNet) maintain performance?  
- Overall: technically solid and clean formulation but requires stronger, more comprehensive empirical evidence to justify central design choices and claims.
