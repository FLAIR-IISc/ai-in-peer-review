- Studies visual servoing using a learned action-conditional bilinear dynamics model over visual features from a pre-trained VGG network and derives control via local linearization.  
- Proposes a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally connected variant of the bilinear model to better capture spatial structure.  
- Introduces a value-weighted objective to account for long-term returns despite one-step-ahead dynamics and uses a fitted-value approach for policy evaluation.  
- Claims the first formulation of a trust-region fitted Q-iteration algorithm.  
- Experiments show the value-weighted objective improves performance and pre-trained visual features enhance generalization across tasks.  
- Paper is clearly written, mathematically rigorous, and conceptually thorough; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Contribution to representation learning is limited: relies on fixed pre-trained VGG features and does not pursue end-to-end representation learning.  
- Policy class is fixed and value functions are linearly approximated with problem-specific features, reducing the representation-learning impact and alignment with ICLR’s core focus.
