- Tackles target-following visual servoing using bilinear one-step dynamics of multi-scale pre-trained convnet feature maps combined with reinforcement learning.
- Provides controlled simulation experiments quantifying the utility of pre-trained deep features for visual servoing.
- Shows clear performance gains over several strong baselines, including methods using ground-truth bounding boxes.
- Learns principled multi-scale visual feature weights via an efficient trust-region fitted Q-iteration algorithm to mitigate distractors.
- Demonstrates good sample efficiency enabled by the chosen Q-function approximator and model-based one-step visual feature dynamics.
- Releases an open-source virtual city environment for benchmarking visual servoing.
- Experiments should use richer, more realistic and diverse visual conditions (more clutter, distractors, varied car models and environments) to test robustness.
- Evaluate end-to-end training/fine-tuning of the convnet to assess representation learning benefits and improve generalization.
- Improve reproducibility by consolidating and simplifying implementation details and providing a complete open-source implementation.
- Fix minor typos in the manuscript (e.g., “learning is a relative[ly] recent addition”, “be applied [to] directly learn”).
- Overall assessment: interesting and solid work with strong results and likely to be further strengthened by addressing the above suggestions.
