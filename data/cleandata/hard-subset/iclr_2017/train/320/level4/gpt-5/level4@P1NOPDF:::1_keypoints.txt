- Proposal: learn visual servoing via Q-iteration using a bilinear dynamics model that predicts y_{t+1} from y_t and u_t.  
- Q-function: weight feature channels so different channels contribute unequally to the control objective.  
- Bellman optimization: exploit scaling invariance of Q parameters to derive a cleaner fitted Q-iteration (FQI) for minimizing Bellman error.

- Pro: thorough exploration of action–feature coupling; locally connected bilinear model is a persuasive trade-off between efficiency and expressiveness.  
- Pro: channel-weighting is sensible and experimentally superior to uniform penalization of features.  
- Pro: optimization insight (scaling invariance) yields a more principled FQI than typical fixed-target schemes.

- Con: missing direct experimental comparison among fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics variants.  
- Con: unclear, empirically or analytically, how the proposed FQI differs from an iterative scheme that holds the target term fixed.

- Experiments: evaluation is small-scale and toy-based, limiting convincingness (but domain lacks standard benchmarks).  
- Experiments: modified VGG architecture needs validation—unclear if it matches or improves on original VGG.

- Overall: technically solid and novel in dynamics modeling, channel-weighted Q, and Bellman optimization, but empirical evidence is limited and stronger experiments are required.
