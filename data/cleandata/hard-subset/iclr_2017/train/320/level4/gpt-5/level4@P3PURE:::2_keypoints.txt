- Investigates visual servoing with a learned visual representation by learning an action-conditional bilinear model over VGG features and deriving control via local linearization.  
- Proposes a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally connected variant of the bilinear model.  
- Because the bilinear model predicts only one step ahead, introduces a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values.  
- Evaluation uses a fitted-value approach and the paper appears to be the first to outline a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm.  
- Paper is well written, mathematically solid, and conceptually thorough.  
- Experiments demonstrate benefits of the value-weighted objective.  
- Empirical results show pre-trained visual features improve generalization.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, noting the work will benefit robotics researchers.  
- Concern: limited contribution to representation learning for ICLR since representation is pre-trained (VGG) rather than learned end-to-end.  
- Concern: paper focuses more on control than representation; policy representation is fixed.  
- Concern: value is approximated linearly using problem-specific features, reducing alignment with ICLR’s emphasis on representation learning.
