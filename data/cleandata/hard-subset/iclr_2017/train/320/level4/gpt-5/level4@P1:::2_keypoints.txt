- Contribution: Proposes multiscale, multi-channel VGG features, an action-conditional bilinear predictive model (with a locally connected variant), and a one-step servoing policy via linearization.  
- Key addition: Introduces a value-weighted objective over feature channels/scales with weights learned to reflect long-term value.  
- Algorithmic novelty: Formulates a fitted Q-iteration (FQI) procedure with an analytic linear Q-function whose greedy action recovers the weighted servoing policy; outlines a trust-region FQI variant.  
- Empirical results: Demonstrates robust target following on CitySim3D, strong sample efficiency (effective policies from ~20 trajectories), and better performance than pixel/keypoint baselines and model-free RL.  
- Generalization: Leveraging pre-trained features improves transfer to novel cars versus learning from scratch.  
- Writing and rigor: Paper is well written, mathematically solid, and conceptually thorough.  
- Main reservation: Representation learning contribution is limited because features are not learned end-to-end, making alignment with ICLR’s focus on representation learning weaker.  
- Problem relevance: Tackles sample-efficient visual servoing robust to viewpoint, appearance variation, occlusion, and rapid adaptation to new targets.  
- Motivation: Using pre-trained deep features and bilinear dynamics is sensible and computationally attractive; connecting one-step control to long-horizon objectives via learned weights is well motivated.  
- Experimental rigor: Compares multiple representations (pixels, VGG layers), dynamics variants (full vs local), weighting strategies (none, CEM, TRPO), and strong policy baselines; code and videos provided.  
- Results summary: VGG conv4_3 outperforms pixels; learned FQI weights improve performance and sample efficiency over TRPO/CEM; approach is robust under noise/occlusion.  
- Suggestion: Clarify choice to use off-the-shelf features and discuss why end-to-end feature learning was not pursued (sample limits/overfitting).  
- Suggestion: Run small-scale fine-tuning or joint training of VGG blocks with the bilinear model to test task-specific adaptation.  
- Suggestion: Analyze which channels/scales receive high weights and whether they correspond to car semantics.  
- Suggestion: Add ablations on bilinear model variants (fully vs locally connected, kernel size, action-independent bias) and on the linearization step’s effect on control.  
- Suggestion: Report results across other backbones (e.g., ResNet) and explain conv5_3’s poorer performance (likely excessive invariance).  
- Suggestion: Clarify the “trust-region” terminology in the FQI variant and whether an explicit constraint is enforced.  
- Suggestion: Discuss real-world transfer, domain shift, robustness to calibration errors, and runtime considerations (VGG cost; consider lightweight extractors).  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to VGG layer and backbone choice; were ResNet or lighter models tried and do weights down-weight deeper invariant layers?  
- Question: Do high-weight channels correspond to semantically meaningful car features; any visualizations or sparsity patterns?  
- Question: How critical are action-independent biases in the bilinear model for handling moving distractors or car dynamics?  
- Question: Is the “trust-region” aspect an explicit constraint or mainly normalization via α and b?  
- Question: How does performance degrade when the bilinear model is imperfect or trained with fewer trajectories; can FQI compensate via reweighting?  
- Question: Were limited end-to-end fine-tuning experiments attempted under the same data budget and what were the results?  
- Question: How robust is the method to real-world noise (rolling shutter, illumination), camera miscalibration, and dynamics mismatch?  
- Question: What is the benefit of multi-scale features versus single-scale when the target is far from the goal pose?  
- Question: For the TRPO baseline, were instabilities observed due to the matrix inverse layer and were any training tricks used to improve stability?  
- Question: Could the Q-approximation be expanded beyond linear weights without losing greedy-policy equivalence, or is linearity essential for tractability?
