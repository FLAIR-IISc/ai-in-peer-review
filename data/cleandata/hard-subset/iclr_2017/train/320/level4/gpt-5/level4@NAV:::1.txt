The paper introduces a novel method for learning visual servoing grounded in Q-iteration. The main contributions of the paper are:
1. A bilinear dynamics model that predicts next-frame features based on the current frame and action
2. A servoing formulation with a Q-function that learns per-channel feature weights
3. An elegant procedure for optimizing the Bellman error to train the Q-function

Pros:
+ The paper carefully examines multiple ways to couple the action (u_t) and frame representation (y_t) to predict next-frame features (y_{t+1}), ultimately arguing for a locally connected bilinear model that balances computational cost and expressivity.
Cons:
- While Sec. 4 provides good justification for the choices, I would have liked to see more empirical comparisons among the three variants: fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics.

Pros:
+ Learning channel-wise weights to reflect the varying importance of objects across channels appears more effective than treating errors uniformly; the experiments consistently show that unweighted losses underperform.
+ Minimizing the Bellman error is challenging in Q-learning. The paper presents a robust optimization scheme based on the key observation that scaling the Q-function parameters does not change the resulting optimal policy. This enables a more elegant FQI procedure compared to typical approaches that keep (c_t + gamma min_u Q_{t+1}) fixed.
Cons:
- I would still like to see a direct comparison between FQI and an iterative method that holds the second term in Eq. 5 fixed.

Experimental results:
- Overall, the experiments feel limited in scale and rely on toy simulations. That said, the scarcity of benchmarks in this domain is acknowledged.
- As noted in the pre-review, the proposed modification to VGG should be validated experimentally; it is not clear whether the modified VGG outperforms the original.

Overall, the paper’s contributions are solid in technical novelty and problem formulation, but stronger experimental evidence, as suggested above, would better substantiate its claims.
