- Core idea: Servo in deep-feature space using a one-step action-conditional predictive model and a greedy policy that minimizes predicted feature error to a target.
- Feature representation: Multiscale spatially-detailed features from pre-trained VGG-16 with dilated convolutions to preserve spatial info.
- Predictive model: Bilinear, locally-connected dynamics over feature maps, linear in control, trained from image-action-image triplets.
- Policy: Analytic one-step lookahead with exact linearization (from bilinearity) and a weighted objective across channels and scales.
- FQI procedure: Linear Q-approximator matched to policy structure, learns feature/control weights with α/b calibration for stability.
- Empirical result: Learns effective visual servos on a synthetic quadrotor car-following benchmark with ~20 RL trajectories.
- Baselines: Outperforms classical IBVS (ORB and tracker-based) and model-free deep RL (TRPO) in sample efficiency and performance.
- Ablations: Best feature layer around conv4_3; conv1_2–conv4_3 outperform pixels and conv5_3; shows reasonable generalization to novel cars.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reasons for accept: Strong integration of pre-trained features, learned predictive dynamics, and a sample-efficient RL procedure; clear empirical gains and robustness.
- Problem motivation: Addresses limitation of hand-crafted features/Jacobians in visual servoing by leveraging CNN invariances while keeping data needs low.
- Methodological soundness: Bilinear locally-connected model is principled for short-horizon camera-induced appearance changes and enables efficient Jacobians and a matching linear Q.
- Empirical rigor: Thorough comparisons across layers, weight learning ablations, robust baselines, and separate train/val/test protocols including novel-object generalization.
- Claims support: Provides qualitative and quantitative evidence for robustness to distractors/occlusions, feature benefits, and sample efficiency; analytic mappings are correct.
- Scope/limitations: Fixed pre-trained visual representation (no end-to-end rep learning); experiments in simulation with privileged state-based reward.
- Clarify novelty: Emphasize FQI tailored to the analytic servo objective (with α/b calibration) rather than a generic “trust-region” FQI.
- Quantify sample efficiency: Report explicit steps/trajectories per method to substantiate the >100× claim.
- Representation analysis: Provide diagnostics on spatial information retention vs. invariance across VGG layers to explain performance differences.
- Additional ablations requested: Compare fully vs. locally connected bilinear dynamics; vary feature channels/scales; report performance vs. number of RL trajectories.
- Transfer to real data: Discuss implementing rewards without ground-truth pose (vision-based proxies, self-supervision, sparse human feedback).
- Interpretability: Visualize learned feature weights across channels/scales and relate them to semantic cues driving control.
- Baselines: Consider adding model-based baselines using optical flow or learned dense correspondence to reduce advantage from privileged representations.
- Question — reward design: How to implement comparable rewards on real robots and sensitivity to reward noise or proxy rewards?
- Question — dynamics model: How does performance degrade with model/prediction error and have multi-step/stochastic variants been tried?
- Question — feature choice: Why is conv4_3 the best trade-off and could lightweight backbones match performance with lower latency?
- Question — FQI stability: How sensitive is performance to ν, non-negativity constraints, and α/b calibration; do divergences occur without regularization?
- Question — exploration: How critical is Gaussian exploration scale with few trajectories; were adaptive exploration or prioritized failure sampling tried?
- Question — generalization: How does performance scale with target diversity and would fine-tuning dynamics or features on new targets help?
- Question — real-time performance: Have feature-extraction compute costs been profiled on embedded hardware or mitigated via pruning/quantization?
