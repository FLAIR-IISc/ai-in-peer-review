- Tackles visual servoing by acting in pre-trained CNN feature space using a multiscale bilinear predictive model and fitted Q-iteration.  
- Policy is an analytic weighted one-step lookahead from exact linearization of bilinear dynamics, yielding a linear Q-function approximator.  
- Experiments on simulated quadcopter–car-following show large gains over pixel-based servoing, hand-engineered keypoints, and model-free RL, with strong sample efficiency (~20 trajectories) and some generalization.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Primary reason: strong empirical performance and sample efficiency plus a simple, computationally efficient formulation combining pre-trained features, learned dynamics, and fitted value learning.  
- Motivation: addresses limits of hand-engineered features and analytic models; pre-trained CNN features give useful invariances without large task data.  
- Approach: bilinear dynamics over feature maps (with multiscale locally connected variant) balances expressivity and parameter efficiency; one-step controller is simple and effective.  
- RL component: fitted Q-iteration with scaling/bias calibration is practical and aligns one-step surrogate objectives with long-horizon returns.  
- Experiments: thorough ablations (feature layers, weighting strategies) and strong baselines; deeper layers (up to conv4_3) perform best while overly invariant layers degrade localization.  
- Runtime and generalization (to unseen cars) are discussed and favorable.  
- Limitation: representation is fixed (not learned end-to-end); linear Q-approx is hand-crafted and problem-specific, reducing alignment with representation-learning emphasis.  
- Suggestion: clarify novelty relative to existing FQI/NFQ methods and emphasize specific new elements (linear Q-structure, calibration).  
- Suggestion: analyze sensitivity to backbone/layer choice (VGG vs ResNet, conv4_3 vs conv3_x) and test light fine-tuning.  
- Suggestion: discuss replacing privileged ground-truth cost with image-based or weak supervision and the train/deploy gap.  
- Suggestion: comment on real-world transfer, robustness to camera calibration errors, occlusions, and extreme viewpoint shifts.  
- Suggestion: report parameter counts and training times for locally connected dynamics and discuss memory/runtime trade-offs and faster backbone options.  
- Suggestion: indicate applicability to other servoing tasks (grasping, multi-object following) and non-stationary/deformable targets.  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to backbone and feature layer choice; any ResNet or fine-tuning experiments?  
- Question: do learned feature weights transfer across targets of the same class without re-learning?  
- Question: how much improvement comes from modeling camera motion vs. target motion; any quantitative ablation?  
- Question: can the method be trained with image-based cost signals rather than privileged ground-truth positions?  
- Question: are there convergence guarantees for the FQI variant under the linear Q-structure and non-negativity constraints; how does regularization ν affect stability?  
- Question: sensitivity to exploration variance and number of trajectories; can predictive model uncertainty guide exploration?  
- Question: what are model sizes and training times for the locally connected dynamics at each scale; any memory bottlenecks?  
- Question: how does performance degrade with occlusion or lighting shifts; do different scales contribute differently under occlusion?  
- Question: is action minimization fully analytical at run time for all scales/channels; any numerical ill-conditioning and remedies?  
- Question: what changes are needed to apply the method to other servoing domains (e.g., manipulation) with larger depth/viewpoint variation?  
- Overall assessment: clear, well-executed contribution with strong empirical evidence and practical impact; merits acceptance despite not being end-to-end.
