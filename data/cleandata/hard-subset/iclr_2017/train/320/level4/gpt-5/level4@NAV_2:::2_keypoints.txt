- Contribution summary: Proposes visual servoing using action-conditional bilinear dynamics on pre-trained VGG features, with a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected bilinear variant and a fitted-value weighted objective to encourage long-horizon performance.

- Method detail: Uses one-step predictive model linearization to derive a servoing policy and learns long-horizon weights via a trust-region style fitted Q-iteration.

- Empirical claim: Value-weighted objective and pre-trained features are key drivers of improved performance, generalization, and sample efficiency.

- Decision: Accept.

- Key reasons for acceptance: Strong integration of learned predictive feature dynamics with a fitted-value objective yielding substantial empirical gains and sample efficiency; clear evidence that pre-trained features and weighting improve robustness and generalization.

- Problem significance: Tackles data-efficient visual servoing for robust target following under appearance/viewpoint changes and occlusions without manual feature engineering.

- Positioning and motivation: Leverages pre-trained CNN features, bilinear predictive models for camera-induced feature changes, and fitted Q-iteration to align one-step control with long-term returns; well placed relative to IBVS/PBVS and RL/video prediction literature.

- Experimental support: Outperforms pixel/keypoint baselines on a synthetic car-following benchmark, shows large sample-efficiency gains vs model-free RL, generalizes to novel targets with mid-level VGG features, and ablations support claimed trends (e.g., deeper features can hurt spatial precision).

- Novelty: First to describe a trust-region style fitted-Q iteration approach (per reviewer), and methodologically sound comparisons.

- Scope limitation: Paper emphasizes control and fitted-value learning over representation learning and does not perform joint end-to-end feature adaptation.

- Policy/value expressivity: Fixed policy form and linear Q-approximation improve tractability and data efficiency but expressivity limits and extensions (nonlinear metrics, adaptive feature selection) should be clarified.

- Analysis suggestion: Add sensitivity studies on VGG layer choice, numbers of scales, and locally connected vs fully connected bilinear dynamics to contextualize robustness.

- Clarity/computation request: Clarify the “trust-region fitted-Q” terminology/constraints versus the α-scaling/bias step and provide a computational profile (feature extraction vs optimization).

- Question — trust-region FQI: Is there an explicit trust-region constraint in the Bellman error optimization or is this referring to the α-scaling/bias stabilization step, and how does it relate to standard FQI?

- Question — representation adaptation: Did you try fine-tuning (e.g., last conv block) under tight data budgets, and what trade-offs in sample efficiency vs robustness are expected?

- Question — feature choice sensitivity: How sensitive is performance to selected VGG layers and multi-scale weighting, and do locally connected bilinear dynamics consistently beat fully connected variants when capacity allows?

- Question — failure modes and robustness: What are dominant failures (heavy occlusion, lighting, fast maneuvers) and how does feature weighting handle distractors with similar appearance?

- Question — comparisons and budgets: Provide exact trajectory/sample counts and wall-clock times for your method vs TRPO/CNN baselines, and confirm matched exploration noise and horizon lengths across methods.
