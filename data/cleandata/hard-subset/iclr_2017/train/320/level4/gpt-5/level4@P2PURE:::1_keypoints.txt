- Proposes a Q-iteration-based visual servoing method with a bilinear dynamics model predicting next-frame features from current features and action.  
- Introduces a Q-function that learns channel-specific weights over features for servoing.  
- Presents an optimization scheme for the Bellman error that leverages scale-invariance of the Q-function to improve Fitted Q-Iteration.  
- Empirically argues a locally connected bilinear model balances computational cost and expressiveness versus other couplings.  
- Shows channel weighting outperforms unweighted feature treatments, validating varying object importance across channels.  
- Provides a principled scheme for stabilizing Bellman error optimization by re-scaling Q-function parameters.  
- Lacks experimental comparisons among fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics architectures.  
- Does not directly compare the proposed FQI scheme to an iterative baseline that holds the target term fixed.  
- Experiments are small-scale and toy-based, limiting empirical convincibility; modified VGG requires validation against the original.  
- Overall: technically solid and novel, but needs stronger, broader experiments to fully substantiate claims.
