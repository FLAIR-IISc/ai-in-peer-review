- Method: Uses ImageNet-pretrained convolutional feature maps and a one-step multiscale bilinear predictive model for visual servoing in feature space.
- Control: Employs a simple greedy servo that selects actions minimizing weighted Euclidean distance between predicted next-step features and goal features.
- Learning: Learns per-channel, per-scale feature weights and control regularization via sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration with a linear Q-function whose greedy action matches the servo update.
- Empirical claim: Standard deep features (e.g., VGG conv layers) can support effective visual servoing when combined with learned dynamics and weighting.
- Robustness: Method shows robustness to distractors, viewpoint/appearance changes, and occlusions using the learned dynamics and weights.
- Performance: Substantially outperforms classical IBVS with hand-designed features and model-free deep RL baselines.
- Sample efficiency: More than two orders of magnitude more sample-efficient than standard model-free deep RL, learning from ~20 trajectories.
- Reproducibility: Code and the CitySim3D environment are released for reproducibility.
- Decision: Reviewer recommends acceptance; method is technically sound, well-motivated, and empirically convincing.
- Key reason: Principled, computationally efficient integration of pretrained features, bilinear dynamics, and fitted Q-iteration yields a simple and effective servo.
- Baseline strength: Clear performance gains over strong baselines (TRPO-trained CNN policies, classical IBVS variants) with superior sample efficiency.
- Problem motivation: Addresses target following under realistic visual variation and occlusion where hand-engineered features fail.
- Literature placement: Sits at the intersection of IBVS/PBVS, video prediction, and RL, improving both classical IBVS and model-free RL by learning representation and dynamics.
- Experimental rigor: Thorough simulator, tasks, and baselines with multiple feature choices and ablations; conv4_3 performed best.
- Ablations: FQI outperforms TRPO and no-weighting/CEM; classical IBVS (ORB/tracker) is less accurate and slower.
- Qualitative support: Trajectories and ablations support claims about occlusion and distractor robustness.
- Method clarity: Fitted Q-iteration formulation and analytic structure are clearly specified.
- Suggested benchmark improvements: Expand dataset with more diverse 3D models, lighting/weather, and clutter to stress-test generalization.
- Representation learning suggestion: Evaluate fine-tuning feature extractor jointly with dynamics/weighting to measure task-specific gains.
- Reproducibility suggestion: Consolidate implementation details into a single section or appendix and emphasize minimal reproduction steps.
- Minor typos: Two small wording errors noted on p.2 to correct.
- Question on features: Ask sensitivity to backbone/layer choice (e.g., ResNet vs VGG, conv3 vs conv4) and use of lighter models for runtime.
- Question on fine-tuning: Ask whether partial fine-tuning was tried and its effect on sample efficiency and robustness.
- Dynamics/planning questions: Ask about multi-step rollout error, potential receding-horizon planning benefits, and locally vs fully connected bilinear variants.
- FQI details questions: Probe importance of non-negativity constraints, occurrence of degenerate solutions, and sensitivity to regularization ν and α,b scaling across seeds.
- Robustness/generalization questions: Request quantitative occlusion/distractor evaluations, weight transferability across targets, and any real-camera zero-shot tests.
- Cost/termination questions: Ask sensitivity to cost design and τ threshold and effects of alternative costs on learned weights.
- Runtime/deployment question: Request detailed runtime breakdown (feature extraction vs dynamics vs policy) and acceleration opportunities (pruning, lighter backbones, caching).
