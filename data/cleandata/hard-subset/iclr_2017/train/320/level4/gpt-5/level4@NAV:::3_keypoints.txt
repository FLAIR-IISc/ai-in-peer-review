- Summary: Uses spatial feature maps from pre-trained CNNs plus bilinear one-step visual dynamics and RL to learn a target-following visual-servoing policy by minimizing weighted distances to predicted features.

- Contribution: Controlled simulation studies quantifying the utility of pre-trained deep features for visual servoing.

- Contribution: Demonstrates clear performance improvements over multiple baselines, including those using ground-truth bounding boxes.

- Contribution: Principled learning of multi-scale visual feature weights with an efficient trust-region fitted Q-iteration to handle distractors.

- Contribution: Good sample efficiency due to the chosen Q-function approximator and model-based one-step visual feature dynamics.

- Contribution: Provides an open-source virtual city environment for benchmarking visual servoing.

- Suggestion: Use a more complex, visually richer benchmark (more clutter, distractors, varied 3D car models and appearance/motion diversity) to test robustness.

- Suggestion: Evaluate end-to-end training/fine-tuning of the convnet to assess representation learning benefits and improve generalization.

- Suggestion: Improve reproducibility by consolidating and summarizing practical implementation details in the paper and linking full open-source code.

- Typos: Minor textual errors noted ("relative[ly]" and "be applied [to] directly learn").

- Conclusion: Paper is solid and interesting; authors responded well to review and are likely able to address suggested improvements.
