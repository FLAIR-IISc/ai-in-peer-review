- Paper proposes visual servoing in learned feature space using multiscale VGG features, a bilinear one-step feature dynamics model, and FQI to learn nonnegative channel/scale weights for one-step lookahead control.  
- Method is sample-efficient, robust to viewpoint/appearance changes and occlusions, outperforms baselines on a simulated car-following benchmark, and CitySim3D + code are released.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong empirical results, substantial sample efficiency, and clear gains over classical IBVS and end-to-end RL baselines.  
- Motivation: Classical servoing relies on hand-crafted features and calibrated dynamics; pre-trained CNN features offer transferable, spatially informative representations for servoing.  
- Method soundness: Bilinear dynamics linear in controls produce a tractable quadratic objective; Q-function matches servo objective enabling analytic action minimization and efficient FQI.  
- Empirical evidence: Multiscale VGG up to conv4_3 beats pixels and deeper, more invariant features; FQI policies succeed with ~20 trajectories while TRPO needs far more and still underperforms.  
- Baselines: IBVS with ORB or tracker boxes underperform and are much slower; performance gap remains even with strong side information like ground-truth depth.  
- Clarity/positioning: Paper clearly states problem, relates bilinear dynamics to prior image-dynamics work, and justifies one-step predictive control with feature weighting for long-horizon robustness.  
- Suggestion — benchmarks: Increase visual complexity and target diversity (clutter, traffic, varied lighting/weather, more 3D car models) to better stress-test robustness.  
- Suggestion — representation learning: Explore fine-tuning/backbone adaptation or joint training of features and dynamics on limited task data to evaluate benefits under harder conditions.  
- Suggestion — reproducibility: Consolidate implementation details (feature normalization, constraints, regularization, optimization) into a single section/appendix and cross-reference code.  
- Minor fixes: Correct typos on p.2: “relative[ly]” and “be applied [to] directly learn.”  
- Question — feature choice: How sensitive is performance to chosen VGG layers; did you try ResNet or other backbones; guidance on layer selection for spatial specificity vs invariance?  
- Question — weight learning: Are learned feature weights shared across targets or target-specific, and how well do they generalize to novel cars or adapt with limited data?  
- Question — regularization: How sensitive are results to the L2 regularization coefficient; did you try sparsity-inducing penalties to prune channels?  
- Question — dynamics capacity: Trade-offs observed between fully vs locally connected bilinear models (accuracy vs memory/time); evidence for cross-channel interaction benefits?  
- Question — cost shaping/termination: How robust are results to alternative image-space costs, termination rules, and discount factors; any ablation studies?  
- Question — FQI stability: How critical is the α,b rescaling step for stability; effects of removing it; how do sampling (S) and inner iterations (K) affect variance and convergence?  
- Question — sim-to-real: Anticipated challenges for real-world deployment (sensor noise, latency, dynamics mismatch) and whether the bilinear model can be adapted online for sim-to-real gaps?  
- Question — occlusions/distractors: Do learned weights systematically downweight background channels or does robustness mainly come from dynamics; any diagnostics on which channels/scales get large weights?  
- Overall assessment: A well-motivated, carefully executed study that advances data-efficient visual servoing by leveraging pre-trained features and simple predictive dynamics within an RL framework.
