- Paper studies feature-space visual servoing combining pre-trained deep features, learned multiscale bilinear dynamics, and an FQI-based policy for sample-efficient target following.
- Proposes a locally connected multiscale bilinear dynamics model for one-step lookahead feature prediction.
- Policy minimizes predicted next-step distance to a goal feature map with learned per-channel, per-scale weights learned via a linear Q-approximation and FQI.
- Exploits an observed invariance to non-negative scaling and bias in Q-parameters to optimize the Bellman error efficiently.
- Shows large sample-efficiency gains on a synthetic car-following benchmark and improved robustness to distractors/occlusions vs. pixel/keypoint baselines.
- Decision: reject (borderline).
- Primary concern 1: experiments are limited to a single synthetic setup and miss critical ablations (dynamics variants, feature choices, FQI optimization).
- Primary concern 2: key design choices (VGG modifications, benefit of new FQI) are not empirically substantiated, hindering assessment of generality and robustness.
- Positive: problem/motivation are timely and well-placed; advocating feature-space servoing for rapid adaptation is sensible.
- Positive: locally connected bilinear dynamics and multiscale features are reasonable design choices; linear Q-approximation aligns with servoing objective.
- Missing: no empirical comparison of fully connected vs. convolutional vs. locally connected dynamics, nor trade-off analysis for accuracy/params/runtime.
- Missing: lack of ablations showing impact of VGG modifications, alternative backbones, or systematic layer selection analysis.
- Missing: no comparison of the proposed FQI two-step optimization against standard fitted Q-iteration or target-network schemes to isolate benefits.
- Scope limitation: results confined to one synthetic benchmark; lack of diverse simulated or real-world tests reduces confidence in generalization.
- Baseline limitations: comparisons miss stronger baselines like model-based MPC over learned dynamics and alternative feature predictors.
- Unclear attribution: sample efficiency gains are not disentangled across pre-trained features, bilinear dynamics, and FQI contributions.
- Suggested ablations: compare dynamics variants, quantify VGG changes and layer choices, contrast FQI with standard FQI/target networks, and test sensitivity to training data and regularization.
- Suggested realism tests: add another domain or small real-world experiment, evaluate robustness to unseen distractors, calibration errors, and dynamics mismatch.
- Suggested clarity/reproducibility additions: detail Jacobian computation, runtime/profile breakdown, wall-clock latency, failure modes, and goal-image selection sensitivity.
- Key questions for authors: request head-to-head dynamics comparisons and locality effects.
- Key questions: ask how results change with original VGG or other backbones and which layers balance invariance vs. precision.
- Key questions: compare the two-step FQI to standard fitted Q-iteration/target-network for stability and performance gains.
- Key questions: ask about generalization to disparate targets, lighting/weather changes, and evidence beyond synthetic cars.
- Key questions: inquire about obstacles to real-world deployment and sensitivity to calibration/actuation delays.
- Key questions: ask behavior when targets leave FOV or are heavily occluded and whether weighting reliably prioritizes correct channels.
- Key questions: request ablations quantifying which component drives sample efficiency.
- Key questions: ask for per-step latency on typical hardware and scaling with feature resolution, scales, and action dimensionality.
