- Tackles visual servoing for target following using spatial feature maps from pre-trained convnets, bilinear one-step visual dynamics at multiple scales, and an RL policy minimizing weighted distances to predicted features.
- Provides controlled simulation experiments quantifying the usefulness of pre-trained deep features for visual servoing.
- Shows clear performance gains over strong baselines, including methods using ground-truth bounding boxes.
- Introduces principled learning of multi-scale visual feature weights via an efficient trust-region fitted Q-iteration algorithm to handle distractors.
- Achieves good sample efficiency due to the chosen Q-function approximator and model-based one-step visual feature dynamics.
- Releases an open-source virtual city environment to benchmark visual servoing.
- Suggests expanding benchmarks to more complex visual conditions (clutter, distractors, diverse appearances/motion, more realistic 3D car models).
- Recommends evaluating end-to-end training/fine-tuning of the convnet to measure representation-learning benefits and generalization.
- Advises improving reproducibility by reorganizing/summarizing implementation details in the main text and linking to complete open-source code.
- Notes minor typos on p.2 (“relative[ly]” and “be applied [to] directly learn”).
- Overall conclusion: paper is interesting and solid; authors can address suggested improvements to further strengthen it.
