- Introduces a novel visual servoing method grounded in Q-iteration with three main contributions: a bilinear dynamics model, a Q-function learning per-channel feature weights, and an optimized Bellman-error training procedure.  
- Proposes a bilinear dynamics model that predicts next-frame features from the current frame and action.  
- Formulates servoing with a Q-function that learns channel-wise feature weights to reflect varying object importance.  
- Presents an elegant Bellman-error optimization procedure leveraging the observation that scaling Q-function parameters doesn’t change the optimal policy.  
- Carefully compares ways to couple action and frame representation and argues for a locally connected bilinear model as a cost–expressivity tradeoff.  
- Lacks sufficient empirical comparisons among fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics.  
- Shows that learning channel-wise weights outperforms unweighted losses in experiments.  
- Provides a robust FQI procedure, but does not directly compare it to iterative methods that hold the second term in Eq. 5 fixed.  
- Experiments are limited in scale and rely on toy simulations, limiting empirical support.  
- The proposed modification to VGG is not experimentally validated against the original.  
- Overall: technically solid contributions and problem formulation, but would benefit from stronger and broader experimental validation.
