The paper proposes a novel approach for learning visual servoing based on Q-iteration. The main contributions are:
1. A bilinear dynamics model for predicting next-frame features based on the current frame and action.
2. A formulation of servoing with a Q-function that learns weights for different feature channels.
3. An elegant method for optimizing the Bellman error to learn the Q-function.

Pros:
- The paper carefully explores different ways to connect the action (u_t) and the frame representation (y_t) to predict next-frame features (y_{t+1}). It argues convincingly for a locally connected bilinear model as a good balance between computational efficiency and expressive capacity.
- The idea of weighting different channels to capture the varying importance of objects across channels is more effective than treating errors uniformly across all channels. The experiments consistently show that the unweighted variant underperforms.
- Solving the Bellman error is challenging in Q-learning approaches. The paper presents a solid optimization scheme based on the key observation that scaling the Q-function parameters does not affect the resulting policy. This enables a more elegant fitted Q-iteration (FQI) approach, as opposed to typical optimization schemes that keep (c_t + gamma min_u Q_{t+1}) fixed.

Cons:
- While Section 4 provides good arguments for the different architectural choices, I would have liked to see more experimental comparisons among the three dynamics models: fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected.
- I would also like to see a direct comparison between the proposed FQI method and an iterative approach that holds the second term in Eq. 5 fixed.

Experimental results:
- Overall, the experimental results are not fully satisfying given the small scale and toy simulations. However, the lack of established benchmarks in this domain should be acknowledged.
- As noted in the pre-review section, the proposed modification to VGG needs experimental validation. In its current form, it is unclear whether the modified VGG would outperform the original model.

Overall assessment:
The paper offers solid technical novelty and clear problem formulations. Strengthening the experimental evaluation—particularly with comparisons among the proposed dynamics models, an ablation contrasting FQI with an approach that fixes the second term in Eq. 5, and empirical validation of the VGG modification—would substantially bolster the paper’s claims.
