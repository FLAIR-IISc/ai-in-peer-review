- Proposes a bilinear dynamics model to predict next-frame features from current frame and action.
- Introduces a Q-function servoing formulation that learns per-channel feature weights.
- Presents an optimization method for the Bellman error exploiting scaling invariance to enable a cleaner fitted Q-iteration.
- Carefully compares ways to connect action and frame representations and advocates a locally connected bilinear model as a good efficiency–expressivity tradeoff.
- Shows that channel-weighting outperforms unweighted error treatment across experiments.
- Provides a solid optimization scheme for Q-learning by leveraging the fact that scaling Q-parameters does not change the policy.
- Lacks experimental comparisons among the three dynamics architectures (fully connected, convolutional, locally connected).
- Does not compare the proposed FQI method directly to an iterative approach that holds the second term in Eq. 5 fixed.
- Experimental evaluation is small-scale and toy-like, limiting empirical support despite domain benchmark scarcity.
- The proposed VGG modification is not experimentally validated against the original VGG.
- Overall: technically novel with clear formulations, but requires stronger experimental ablations and validations to bolster claims.
