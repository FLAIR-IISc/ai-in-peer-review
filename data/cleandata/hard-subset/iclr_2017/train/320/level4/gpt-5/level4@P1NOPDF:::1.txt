This paper proposes a novel approach for learning visual servoing via Q-iteration. The main contributions are:
1. A bilinear dynamics model that predicts next-frame features y_{t+1} from the current features y_t and action u_t.
2. A Q-function formulation of servoing that learns weights over feature channels, allowing different channels to contribute unequally.
3. An elegant method for optimizing the Bellman error to learn the Q-function.

Pros:
- The paper thoughtfully explores alternative ways to couple action u_t with the frame representation y_t to predict y_{t+1}. The argument for a locally connected bilinear model is persuasive, striking a good balance between computational efficiency and representational power.
- Weighting feature channels to capture the varying importance of objects across channels is a sensible idea and appears more effective than penalizing all channels equally. This is supported by experiments showing consistently worse performance when the errors are unweighted.
- Solving the Bellman error is often challenging in Q-learning. The paper presents a solid optimization scheme based on the key observation that scaling the Q-function parameters does not change the resulting policy. This insight enables a cleaner fitted Q-iteration (FQI) approach compared to typical schemes that hold the target term (c_t + gamma min_u Q_{t+1}) fixed.

Cons:
- While Section 4 offers good arguments for the different dynamics choices, I would have liked to see direct experimental comparisons among the three approaches: fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics.
- It would also help to clarify, empirically or analytically, how the proposed FQI method differs from an iterative approach that holds the second term in Eq. 5 fixed.

Experimental results:
- Overall, the experimental evaluation is not fully convincing due to its small scale and reliance on toy simulations. That said, the relative lack of established benchmarks in this domain is a fair constraint and should be acknowledged.
- As noted in the pre-review section, the proposed modification to VGG requires experimental validation. In its current form, it is unclear whether the modified VGG outperforms or even matches the original architecture.

Overall assessment:
The paper offers a solid technical contribution with clear novelty in its dynamics modeling, channel-weighted Q-function formulation, and Bellman error optimization strategy. However, the empirical evidence is limited. Stronger experiments—particularly direct comparisons of the proposed dynamics variants and a validation of the VGG modification—would significantly bolster the paper’s claims.
