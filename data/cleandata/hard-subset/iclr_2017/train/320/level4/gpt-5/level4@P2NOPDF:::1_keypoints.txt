- Contribution: Proposes a locally connected bilinear dynamics model to predict next-frame features from current frame and action.
- Contribution: Introduces a Q-function that learns channel-wise feature weights to capture varying object/feature importance.
- Contribution: Presents an optimization scheme for minimizing Bellman error that leverages scale invariance of Q to enable a cleaner fitted Q-iteration.

- Strength: Clearly motivates and balances computational cost vs. expressiveness for the locally connected bilinear model.
- Strength: Channel-specific weighting is compelling and empirically outperforms unweighted losses.
- Strength: Bellman-error treatment is technically strong; scaling insight yields a cleaner FQI than methods that fix the second term.

- Weakness: Empirical evaluation lacks direct comparisons among fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected bilinear architectures.
- Weakness: No quantified head-to-head comparison between the proposed FQI variant and alternative iterative baselines (convergence, sample efficiency, final performance).
- Weakness: Experiments are small-scale and toy-based, leaving questions about robustness, generalization, and sensitivity to design choices.
- Weakness: The modified VGG feature extractor is not sufficiently ablated or validated against the standard backbone.

- Overall: Technically solid and methodologically interesting work, but the conceptual contributions need stronger empirical validation (architecture comparisons, FQI ablations, and VGG validation) to demonstrate robustness and practical impact.
