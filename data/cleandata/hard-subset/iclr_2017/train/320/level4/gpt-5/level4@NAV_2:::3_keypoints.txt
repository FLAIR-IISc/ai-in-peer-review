- Summary: Uses spatial CNN feature maps from pretrained classifiers, multi-scale bilinear one-step feature dynamics, and RL to learn a servoing policy that minimizes weighted distances between predicted next-step and goal feature maps.
- Contribution: Carefully controlled simulation studies quantifying the usefulness of off-the-shelf deep features for visual servoing.
- Contribution: Demonstrates clear performance gains versus reasonable baselines, including ones using ground-truth bounding boxes.
- Contribution: Proposes a principled method to learn multi-scale feature weights via an efficient trust-region fitted Q-iteration to mitigate distractors.
- Contribution: Achieves strong sample efficiency through the chosen Q-function parametrization and the model-based one-step feature-dynamics predictor.
- Contribution: Releases an open-source virtual city environment for benchmarking visual servoing.
- Suggestion: Broaden the benchmark with heavier clutter, more varied distractors, more diverse 3D car assets, and richer scenes to better test robustness.
- Suggestion: Evaluate end-to-end fine-tuning/representation learning of the backbone to measure benefits under tougher visual conditions.
- Suggestion: Improve reproducibility by consolidating implementation heuristics and releasing a complete open-source implementation (beyond the environment).
- Suggestion: Fix typos noted on p.2 (“relative[ly]” and “be applied [to] directly learn”).
- Conclusion: Overall solid and compelling work with a strong revision and response; authors can feasibly address many suggestions to further strengthen the paper.
