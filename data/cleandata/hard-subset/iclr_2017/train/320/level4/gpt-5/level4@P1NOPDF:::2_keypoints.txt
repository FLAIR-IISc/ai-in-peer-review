- Studies visual servoing using a learned visual representation.
- Learns an action-conditional bilinear model over features from a pre-trained VGG network.
- Derives a control policy by linearizing the learned dynamics.
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally connected variant of the bilinear model.
- Proposes a value-weighted objective to incorporate long-term returns because the model predicts only one step ahead.
- Uses a fitted-value approach for evaluation.
- Paper is well written, mathematically solid, and conceptually thorough.
- Experiments clearly demonstrate the advantages of the value-weighted objective and improved generalization from pre-trained features.
- Claims novelty in outlining a trust-region fitted Q-iteration algorithm.
- Recommends acceptance and believes the work will be useful to robotics researchers.
- Limitation: limited contribution to representation learning (no end-to-end learning), fixed policy representation, and linear, problem-specific value approximation—less aligned with ICLR’s core focus.
