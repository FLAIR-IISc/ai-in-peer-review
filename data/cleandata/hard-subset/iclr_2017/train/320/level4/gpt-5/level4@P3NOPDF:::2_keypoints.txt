- Paper studies visual servoing by learning action-conditional bilinear dynamics over pre-trained VGG visual features and deriving control via local linearization.  
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally connected bilinear dynamics model tailored to visual features.  
- Proposes a value-weighted training objective that incorporates long-horizon returns despite one-step-ahead predictions.  
- Presents a trust-region adaptation of fitted Q-iteration for policy evaluation.  
- Experiments show the value-weighted objective improves performance.  
- Empirical results indicate pre-trained VGG features enhance generalization.  
- Paper is clear, well written, and mathematically solid with careful derivations.  
- Conceptually ties learned visual dynamics to control through local linearization effectively.  
- The value-weighted objective is a notable and convincingly demonstrated contribution.  
- Trust-region FQI is an interesting algorithmic contribution with potential follow-up impact.  
- Limited contribution to representation learning; no end-to-end training or fine-tuning of visual features.  
- Emphasis on control and optimization reduces alignment with ICLR’s focus on representation learning.  
- Policy class is fixed and value function uses linear, problem-specific features, limiting flexibility.  
- Suggested improvements: add end-to-end or fine-tuning experiments to isolate benefits of fixed VGG features.  
- Suggested analyses: sensitivity to feature extractor and bilinear model structure, and limits of local linearization.  
- Suggested clarifications: details and stability of the fitted-value procedure and practical enforcement of the trust-region constraint.  
- Suggested comparisons: include stronger model-free and recent model-based baselines or ablations (standard FQI vs trust-region FQI).  
- Overall: technically strong and recommended for acceptance on merit, but its ICLR fit would improve with more representation-learning-focused experiments.
