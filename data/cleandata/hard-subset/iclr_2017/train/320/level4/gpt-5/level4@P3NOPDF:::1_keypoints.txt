- Summary: Proposes a Q-iteration visual servoing method using a locally connected bilinear dynamics model and a per-channel weighted Q-function, exploiting Q-parameter scale-invariance to stabilize FQI updates.

- Contribution: Introduces a locally connected bilinear dynamics model predicting next-frame features as a trade-off between expressiveness and tractability.

- Contribution: Proposes a Q-function that learns per-channel importance weights to prioritize task-relevant visual signals.

- Contribution: Develops a scale-invariant FQI optimization scheme that normalizes Q-parameters to improve Bellman error minimization stability.

- Strength: Provides a well-argued design choice for locally connected bilinear dynamics as a middle ground between fully connected and convolutional models.

- Strength: Empirically supports the value of learned per-channel weights, showing performance drops when omitted.

- Strength: Presents an elegant, well-motivated Bellman error optimization leveraging greedy-policy scale-invariance to avoid ad hoc target-freezing.

- Weakness: Lacks a direct, quantitative ablation comparing fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics under matched conditions.

- Weakness: Does not empirically or theoretically compare the proposed scale-invariant FQI update against iterative target-freezing approaches.

- Weakness: Experimental scope is limited to small-scale/toy tasks, leaving generality and robustness unclear.

- Weakness: Presents a modified VGG backbone without convincing ablation showing it improves downstream servoing over standard VGG.

- Suggestion: Add direct comparisons of the three dynamics parameterizations reporting feature prediction accuracy, control performance, and computational cost.

- Suggestion: Provide head-to-head experiments between scale-invariant FQI and target-freezing schemes analyzing convergence, stability, and data efficiency.

- Suggestion: Ablate learned channel weights versus uniform weighting across more tasks and analyze correlations with task-relevant visual regions.

- Suggestion: Validate the modified VGG by comparing original VGG, the modified variant, and lighter backbones on perception and control metrics.

- Suggestion: Test on more challenging/diverse environments or include transfer experiments to assess generalization.

- Suggestion: Clarify computational complexity and data requirements, and include sensitivity analysis to dataset size and noise.

- Suggestion: Release code and models to facilitate benchmarking and comparisons.

- Writing/Clarity: Paper is generally clear with strong exposition of the scaling argument; fix minor typos and explicitly define symbols in Section 4/Equation 5.

- Overall Assessment: Technically novel and promising contributions but needs substantially stronger empirical validation to substantiate central design choices for publication.
