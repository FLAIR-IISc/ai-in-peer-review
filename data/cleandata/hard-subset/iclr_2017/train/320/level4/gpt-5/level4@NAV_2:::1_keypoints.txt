- Central claim: Servo in learned multi-scale CNN feature space (modified VGG-16) using a learned bilinear predictive dynamics model and RL for target following.  
- Feature design: Proposes multiscale features via reduced-pooling/dilated VGG-16 to preserve spatial detail for servoing.  
- Dynamics model: Learns short-horizon feature dynamics with a bilinear model and advocates a locally connected bilinear variant as a good expressiveness/compute trade-off.  
- Servo objective and weighting: Servo by minimizing predicted next-step feature error to goal with per-channel/scale weights learned via a linear Q-parameterization.  
- FQI optimization: Uses fitted Q-iteration with an efficient optimization exploiting scale/bias invariance of Q-parameters for the greedy policy.  
- Empirical claim: On a synthetic quadcopter car-following benchmark, method reportedly learns effective controllers with ≈20 trajectories, outperforming pixel/keypoint baselines and standard model-free RL in sample efficiency.  
- Decision: Reviewer recommends rejection.  
- Main reasons for rejection: Evaluation is limited to a small synthetic setting and lacks crucial ablations validating core modeling choices.  
- Positive: Problem motivation is clear and well-situated in literature.  
- Positive: Technical approach is sound and novel; locally connected bilinear dynamics and linear Q-weight learning are reasonable and elegant.  
- Insight: Noted neat practical observation that scaling Q-parameters and bias does not change the greedy policy.  
- Missing ablation — dynamics: No sufficient empirical comparison of fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected bilinear dynamics to justify inductive bias.  
- Missing ablation — backbone: No direct comparison of modified VGG-16 to standard pretrained VGG-16 to validate reduced-pooling/dilation choices.  
- Missing ablation — FQI variant: No head-to-head comparison with conventional target-fixed fitted Q-iteration to show benefits in stability, convergence, or sample efficiency.  
- Empirical scope concern: Results confined to one synthetic environment; robustness claims (distractors, occlusion, viewpoint changes) are insufficiently tested and no real-world validation.  
- Additional requested experiments: Dynamics, feature, and RL ablations; robustness tests under occlusion/lighting/distractors; sample-efficiency curves across seeds.  
- Reporting and analysis requests: Provide means, standard deviations, seed counts, significance tests, and runtime breakdowns per pipeline component.  
- Clarification request: Explain alignment between myopic one-step feature objective and long-horizon task cost and when learned weights compensate for myopia.  
- Key author questions: Quantitative comparisons of dynamics parameterizations, VGG modification ablations, FQI variant comparisons, generalization to viewpoint/occlusion/clutter, parameter dimensionality vs data, sensitivity to γ/λ/exploration, and sim-to-real or real-robot plans.
