- Proposes learning an action-conditional bilinear model on pre-trained VGG visual features to derive a linearized policy for visual servoing.  
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.  
- Uses a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values to compensate for one-step-ahead dynamics predictions.  
- Evaluates the approach with a fitted-value/fitted-Q methodology and outlines a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm.  
- Empirically shows that value-weighted objectives improve performance and that pre-trained visual features aid generalization.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically solid, and conceptually thorough; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Contribution to representation learning is limited: relies on pre-trained VGG and does not explore end-to-end feature learning.  
- Paper emphasizes control/optimization over representation learning, which may reduce alignment with ICLR’s focus.  
- Policy representation is fixed and values are linearly approximated with problem-specific features, potentially limiting applicability.  
- Suggests more discussion on choice and implications of using pre-trained VGG features.  
- Recommends more insight on trade-offs between different bilinear model variants.  
- Requests details on computational cost and real-time implementation feasibility.  
- Asks how the approach would extend to more complex tasks (robotic manipulation, cluttered navigation).  
- Seeks deeper discussion of benefits and limitations of using pre-trained visual features in visual servoing.
