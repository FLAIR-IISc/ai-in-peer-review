- Proposes visual servoing method using spatial feature maps from pre-trained convnets, bilinear one-step feature dynamics, and RL to minimize weighted feature prediction errors.
- Decision: reviewer recommends accepting the paper.
- Approach is well-motivated, combining learned features, predictive dynamics, and RL.
- Demonstrates significant performance gains over sensible baselines, including ones using ground-truth bounding boxes.
- Method is sample-efficient due to the chosen Q-function approximator and model-based one-step visual dynamics.
- Provides an open-source virtual city environment useful for benchmarking visual servoing.
- Contributions include controlled simulation experiments, principled multi-scale feature weight learning, and clear exposition.
- Notable novelty: use of pre-trained deep features for visual servoing rather than traditional methods.
- Suggest adding more complex visual conditions (clutter, distractors, varied appearance/motion) to the benchmark.
- Recommend investigating end-to-end training effects on performance and generalization.
- Advise grouping/simplifying implementation details for readability and providing an open-source implementation of the method.
- Questions for authors: provide computational-efficiency details versus other RL algorithms; how to handle distractors in complex environments; applicability to other tasks like manipulation or navigation.
- Typos to fix: p.2 "learning is a relative[ly] recent addition" and "be applied [to] directly learn".
