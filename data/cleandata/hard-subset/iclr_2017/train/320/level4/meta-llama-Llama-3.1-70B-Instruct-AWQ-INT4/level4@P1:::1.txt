The paper proposes a novel approach for learning visual servoing based on Q-iteration. The main contributions of the paper are: 
1. A bilinear dynamics model for predicting next frame features based on action and current frame, 
2. Formulation of servoing with a Q-function that learns weights for different feature channels, and 
3. An elegant method for optimizing the Bellman error to learn the Q-function.

The paper does a good job of exploring different ways to connect the action (ut) and frame representation (yt) to predict next frame features (y_{t+1}). They argue in favour of a locally connected bilinear model which strikes the balance between computation and expressive ability. However, more experimental results comparing the three approaches - fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics - would be beneficial.

One of the strengths of the paper is the idea of weighting different channels to capture the importance of objects in different channels, which seems more effective than treating errors across all channels equally. This is also validated experimentally, where unweighted performance suffers consistently.

The paper presents a solid optimization scheme based on the key observation that scaling Q-function parameters does not affect the best policy chosen. This enables a more elegant FQI approach as opposed to typical optimization schemes. However, a comparison between FQI and an iterative approach that holds the second term in Eq. 5 fixed would be useful.

The experimental results are somewhat unsatisfying due to the small scale and toy simulations. However, the lack of benchmarks in this domain needs to be recognized. Additionally, the idea of modifying the VGG network needs to be experimentally validated. In its current form, it is not clear whether the modified VGG would perform better than the original version.

Overall, the contribution of the paper is solid in terms of technical novelty and problem formulations. However, the paper could use stronger experiments to bolster its claims.

Decision: Reject with resubmission.

Reasons for rejection: 
- Lack of comprehensive experimental results comparing different approaches
- Limited scale and toy simulations
- Need for experimental validation of the modified VGG network

Supporting arguments for the decision:

The paper presents a novel approach for learning visual servoing based on Q-iteration. However, the experimental results are limited, and more comprehensive comparisons between different approaches are needed to fully evaluate the proposed method.

Additional feedback:

To improve the paper, the authors could consider the following:

- Provide more experimental results comparing the three approaches: fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics.
- Validate the modified VGG network experimentally to demonstrate its effectiveness.
- Increase the scale and complexity of the simulations to better demonstrate the proposed method's capabilities.
- Consider adding more baseline comparisons to evaluate the proposed method's performance.

Questions for the authors:

- Can the authors provide more details on the choice of hyperparameters for the FQI algorithm?
- How do the authors plan to extend the proposed method to more complex and real-world scenarios?
- Can the authors provide more insights into the modified VGG network and its design choices?
