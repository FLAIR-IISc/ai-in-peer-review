**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel approach to visual servoing, specifically target following, by leveraging spatial feature maps from convolutional networks pre-trained on general image classification tasks. The authors combine bilinear models of one-step dynamics of visual feature maps at multiple scales with a reinforcement learning algorithm to learn a servoing policy. This policy is learned by minimizing a regularized weighted average of distances to features predicted by the aforementioned model of visual dynamics.

**Decision**

Based on the paper's contributions, clarity, and potential impact, I recommend accepting this paper.

**Reasons for Decision**

The paper presents a well-motivated approach to visual servoing, combining learned visual features, predictive dynamics models, and reinforcement learning. The authors demonstrate significant performance benefits over sensible baselines, including those using ground truth bounding boxes. The proposed method is also sample-efficient, thanks to the choice of Q-function approximator and the model-based one-step visual feature dynamics. Additionally, the open-source virtual city environment provided by the authors will be a valuable resource for benchmarking visual servoing.

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper's contributions are substantial, including controlled experiments in simulation, principled learning of multi-scale visual feature weights, and good sample efficiency. The authors also provide a clear and detailed explanation of their method, making it easy to follow and understand. The use of pre-trained deep features for visual servoing is a significant departure from traditional approaches, and the results demonstrate the potential of this method.

**Additional Feedback**

To further improve the paper, I suggest the following:

* Consider adding more complex visual conditions to the benchmark, such as clutter, distractors, and appearance and motion variety.
* Investigate the impact of end-to-end training on the method's performance and generalization.
* Group, simplify, or reorganize the exposition of implementation details to improve readability.
* Provide an open-source implementation of the method for completeness.

**Questions for Authors**

* Can you provide more details on the computational efficiency of the proposed method, particularly in comparison to other reinforcement learning algorithms?
* How do you plan to address the issue of distractors in more complex visual environments?
* Have you considered applying the proposed method to other visual servoing tasks, such as manipulation or navigation?
