- Proposes a novel visual servoing approach using Q-iteration.
- Introduces a bilinear dynamics model to predict next-frame features from action and current frame.
- Formulates servoing with a Q-function that learns per-channel weights for features.
- Presents an elegant Bellman-error optimization scheme leveraging scale-invariance to enable Fitted Q-Iteration (FQI).
- Explores fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected ways to connect action and frame representations, arguing for a locally connected bilinear model as a good tradeoff.
- Lacks experimental comparison between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics to validate that modeling choice.
- Channel-weighting idea is a key strength, shown experimentally to outperform unweighted error treatment.
- Should compare the proposed FQI optimization to an iterative scheme that holds the second term fixed.
- Experiments are small-scale and limited to toy simulations, which weakens empirical claims (though benchmarks are scarce).
- Modification of VGG is proposed but not experimentally validated against the original network.
- Overall: solid technical novelty and formulation but needs stronger, broader experiments to substantiate claims.
