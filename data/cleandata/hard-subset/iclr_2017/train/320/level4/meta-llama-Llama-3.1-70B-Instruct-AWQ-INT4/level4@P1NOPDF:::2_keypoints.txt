- Proposes an action-conditional bilinear model of visual features (from pre-trained VGG) for visual servoing.
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.
- Bilinear model only predicts one-step-ahead dynamics, so authors propose a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values.
- Uses a fitted-value approach for evaluation.
- Paper is well-written, mathematically sound, and conceptually thorough.
- Experiments demonstrate benefits of the value-weighted objective.
- Claims to be the first to outline a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm.
- Shows empirically that pre-trained visual features improve generalization.
- Reviewer recommends the paper as potentially beneficial to robotics researchers.
- Concern: contribution to representation learning is limited—does not explore end-to-end learned visual representations.
- Policy representation is fixed and values are approximated linearly with problem-specific features.
- Overall, paper focuses more on control than on representation learning, making it less aligned with ICLR’s core interests.
