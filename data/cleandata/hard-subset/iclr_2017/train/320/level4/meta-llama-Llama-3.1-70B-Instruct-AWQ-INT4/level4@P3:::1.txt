**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper proposes a novel approach for learning visual servoing based on Q-iteration. The main contributions of the paper are: (1) a bilinear dynamics model for predicting next frame features based on action and current frame, (2) formulation of servoing with a Q-function that learns weights for different feature channels, and (3) an elegant method for optimizing the Bellman error to learn the Q-function.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

Based on the review, I recommend accepting the paper with minor revisions. The key reasons for this decision are: (1) the paper presents a solid technical contribution in terms of problem formulation and novelty, (2) the proposed approach shows promise in addressing the challenges of visual servoing, and (3) the experimental results, although limited, demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper does a good job of exploring different ways to connect the action (ut) and frame representation (yt) to predict next frame features (y_{t+1}). The authors argue in favor of a locally connected bilinear model, which strikes a balance between computation and expressive ability. The idea of weighting different channels to capture the importance of objects in different channels seems more effective than treating errors across all channels equally, and this is validated experimentally.

The paper also presents a solid optimization scheme for solving the Bellman error, based on the key observation that scaling Q-function parameters does not affect the best policy chosen. This enables a more elegant FQI approach as opposed to typical optimization schemes.
