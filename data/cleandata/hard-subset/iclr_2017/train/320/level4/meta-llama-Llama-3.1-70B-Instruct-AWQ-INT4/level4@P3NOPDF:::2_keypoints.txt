- Investigates visual servoing using a learned visual representation, combining vision and control.  
- Proposes a novel method: action-conditional bilinear model paired with a linearized dynamics policy.  
- Uses pre-trained VGG features as the visual representation for policy derivation.  
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.  
- Addresses the bilinear model’s one-step prediction limit by proposing a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values.  
- Evaluates using a fitted-value approach to account for long-horizon effects.  
- Presents a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm, claimed as a novel contribution.  
- Experiments show the value-weighted objective improves performance and generalization.  
- Empirical results indicate pre-trained visual features aid generalization.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically solid, and conceptually thorough, making a valuable robotics contribution.  
- Reviewer recommends the paper for its robotics/control contributions.  
- Critique: limited contribution to representation learning for ICLR—no end-to-end representation learning explored.  
- Critique: policy representation is fixed and values are linearly approximated with problem-specific features, reducing alignment with ICLR’s emphasis on representation learning.
