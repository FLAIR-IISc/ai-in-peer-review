- Proposes a learned action-conditional bilinear model combined with a linearized dynamics-based policy derivation for visual servoing.  
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model that predicts one-step dynamics.  
- Addresses limitation of single-step prediction via a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values using a fitted-value approach.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically sound, and conceptually thorough.  
- Experiments demonstrate clear benefits of the value-weighted objective.  
- Introduces (appears to be first) a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm.  
- Shows empirically that using pre-trained visual features improves generalization.  
- Reviewer recommends the paper as valuable to robotics researchers.  
- Limitation: contribution to representation learning is limited in scope.  
- Does not explore end-to-end representation learning; focuses more on control than on learned visual representations.  
- Policy representation is fixed and value functions are approximated linearly with problem-specific features, reducing alignment with ICLR’s primary focus.
