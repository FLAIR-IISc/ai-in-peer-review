- Proposes an action-conditional bilinear model of VGG visual features and derives a policy via linearized dynamics.  
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.  
- Bilinear model predicts one-step ahead; authors propose a value-weighted objective to incorporate long-term policy values.  
- Evaluation uses a fitted-value approach and outlines a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm (claimed as novel).  
- Empirical results show benefits of the value-weighted objective and that pre-trained visual features aid generalization.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically solid, and conceptually thorough; recommended for robotics researchers.  
- Limited contribution to representation learning: relies on pre-trained VGG and does not learn representations end-to-end.  
- More focus on control than representation: fixed policy representation and linear value approximation with problem-specific features.  
- Consequently, the work is less aligned with ICLR’s typical emphasis on representation learning and end-to-end approaches.
