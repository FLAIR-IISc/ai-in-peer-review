- Studies visual servoing using a learned visual representation.
- Proposes an action-conditional bilinear model of visual features (from a pre-trained VGG) and derives a policy via linearization of the dynamics.
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.
- Addresses one-step prediction limitation by proposing a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values.
- Uses a fitted-value evaluation approach and outlines a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm (claimed novel).
- Empirical results show benefits of the value-weighted objective and pre-trained features, with strong performance on a synthetic car-following benchmark versus conventional methods and model-free DRL.
- Paper is well written, mathematically solid, and conceptually exhaustive.
- Limitation: greater focus on control than representation learning; policy representation is fixed and values are approximated linearly with problem-specific features.
- Suggested improvements: discuss choice of pre-trained features and task relevance; consider end-to-end representation learning; explore more flexible policy representations (e.g., neural nets).
- Suggested improvement: provide details on computational efficiency, time complexity, and required computational resources.
- Overall recommendation: accept; significant contribution to robotics and visual servoing.
