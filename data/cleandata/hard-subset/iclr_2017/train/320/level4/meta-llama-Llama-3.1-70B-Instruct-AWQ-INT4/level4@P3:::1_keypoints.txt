- Paper proposes a novel Q-iteration-based approach for visual servoing.  
- Main contributions: a bilinear dynamics model to predict next-frame features from action and current frame.  
- Main contributions: a Q-function formulation that learns per-channel feature weights for servoing.  
- Main contributions: an elegant Bellman-error optimization scheme for learning the Q-function.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Key reasons: solid technical novelty in problem formulation and method.  
- Key reasons: approach shows promise for addressing visual servoing challenges.  
- Key reasons: experimental results indicate effectiveness, though limited in scope.  
- Strength: thorough exploration of ways to connect action and frame representations.  
- Strength: locally connected bilinear model balances computational cost and expressiveness.  
- Strength: channel-weighting of errors is more effective than uniform-channel treatment.  
- Strength: scaling invariance of Q-parameters enables a cleaner FQI optimization.  
- Suggested improvements: add experiments comparing fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics.  
- Suggested improvements: experimentally validate the modified VGG network.  
- Suggested improvements: compare FQI to an iterative approach that holds the second term in Eq. 5 fixed.  
- Suggested improvements: expand experiments to more realistic, larger-scale scenarios and report computational efficiency.  
- Questions for authors: justify choice and advantages of the locally connected bilinear model.  
- Questions for authors: how will the method extend to multi-object tracking or dynamic environments?  
- Questions for authors: provide more details on computational efficiency and real-world application potential.
