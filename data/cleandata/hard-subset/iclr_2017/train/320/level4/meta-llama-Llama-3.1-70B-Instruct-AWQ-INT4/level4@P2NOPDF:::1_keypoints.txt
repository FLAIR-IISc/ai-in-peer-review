- Proposes a Q-iteration based visual servoing method with three contributions: bilinear dynamics, Q-function that weights feature channels, and a Bellman-error minimization scheme.
- Introduces a locally connected bilinear dynamics model for predicting next-frame features from actions and current frames.
- Recommends but lacks experimental comparison between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics (Section 4).
- Shows that learning weights for different feature channels improves performance versus treating channel errors equally; unweighted performance consistently suffers.
- Develops an optimization scheme for Bellman error that leverages scaling invariance of Q-parameters, leading to a fitted Q-iteration (FQI) approach.
- Suggests comparing FQI to an iterative approach that holds the second term in Equation 5 fixed.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to small-scale/toy simulations, which weakens empirical claims despite domain benchmark scarcity.
- The proposed VGG network modification should be experimentally validated against the original VGG to confirm benefits.
- Overall, the paper has solid technical novelty and formulation but needs stronger, broader experiments to more convincingly support its contributions.
