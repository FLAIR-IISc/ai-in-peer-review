The paper presents a novel Q-iteration-based approach for learning visual servoing. The key contributions of this work include:
1. A bilinear dynamics model for predicting next frame features based on the current frame and action
2. A Q-function formulation for servoing that learns weights for different feature channels
3. An efficient method for optimizing the Bellman error to learn the Q-function

Strengths:
+ The authors explore various methods for connecting the action (ut) and frame representation (yt) to predict next frame features (y_{t+1}). They justify the choice of a locally connected bilinear model, which balances computational efficiency and expressiveness.
+ The proposed approach of weighting different channels to capture object importance in various channels appears more effective than treating errors across all channels equally. This is supported by experimental results, which show consistent performance degradation when using unweighted methods.
+ The paper introduces a solid optimization scheme for solving the Bellman error, leveraging the observation that scaling Q-function parameters does not affect the optimal policy. This enables a more elegant Fitted Q-Iteration (FQI) approach compared to typical optimization schemes.

Weaknesses:
- Section 4 would benefit from additional experimental results comparing the three approaches: fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics.
- A comparison between FQI and an iterative approach that holds the second term in Eq. 5 fixed would be valuable.

Experimental Results:
- Although the experimental results are somewhat limited in scale and scope, it is essential to acknowledge the lack of established benchmarks in this domain.
- As noted in the pre-review section, the modification of the VGG architecture requires experimental validation to demonstrate its effectiveness compared to the original version.

Overall, the paper contributes to the field through its technical novelty and problem formulation. However, more comprehensive experiments would strengthen the paper's claims and provide a more convincing demonstration of the proposed approach.
