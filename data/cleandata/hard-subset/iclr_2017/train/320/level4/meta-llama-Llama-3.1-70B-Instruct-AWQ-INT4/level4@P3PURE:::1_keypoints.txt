- Proposes a novel Q-iteration based visual servoing method with three main contributions: bilinear dynamics, feature-channel-weighted Q-function, and a Bellman-error optimization scheme.
- Introduces a bilinear dynamics model to predict next-frame features from current features and action.
- Formulates servoing with a Q-function that learns per-feature-channel weights to reflect channel importance.
- Presents an optimization trick for the Bellman error exploiting scale invariance of Q-parameters to enable a cleaner fitted Q-iteration (FQI).
- Strength: thoroughly explores ways to connect actions and frame representations for next-frame prediction.
- Strength: argues for a locally connected bilinear model as a good trade-off between computation and expressiveness.
- Strength: shows that weighting feature channels outperforms unweighted error aggregation in experiments.
- Strength: provides a solid, elegant FQI-style optimization scheme instead of fixing terms as in typical approaches.
- Limitation: needs more experimental comparisons between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics.
- Limitation: should compare FQI to an iterative approach that holds the second term in Eq.5 fixed.
- Limitation: experiments are small-scale/toy and benchmarks are lacking, reducing empirical impact.
- Limitation: modified VGG network proposal requires experimental validation against the original VGG.
- Overall: technically novel and well-formulated, but stronger and broader experiments are required to substantiate claims.
