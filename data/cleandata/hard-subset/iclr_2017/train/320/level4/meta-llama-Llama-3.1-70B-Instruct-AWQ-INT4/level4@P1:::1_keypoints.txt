- Proposes a Q-iteration based visual servoing method with three contributions: bilinear dynamics model, Q-function that weights feature channels, and an efficient Bellman-error optimization.

- Advocates a locally connected bilinear dynamics model as a balance between computation and expressiveness; requests more experiments comparing fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected variants.

- Weighting feature channels is a key strength and is experimentally shown to outperform an unweighted error across channels.

- Presents an elegant Fitted Q-Iteration (FQI) optimization leveraging Q-function scale invariance; asks for a comparison to an iterative scheme that holds the second term in Eq. 5 fixed.

- Experimental evaluation is limited to small-scale, toy simulations and is insufficient to fully support claims.

- The proposed modifications to the VGG network are not experimentally validated against the original VGG.

- Decision: Reject with resubmission due to insufficient experimental comparisons, limited scale, and lack of VGG validation.

- Suggested improvements: add thorough comparisons between dynamics models, validate modified VGG, scale up simulations, and include more baseline methods.

- Questions for authors: provide FQI hyperparameter details, describe plans for extension to complex/real-world scenarios, and clarify design choices for the modified VGG.
