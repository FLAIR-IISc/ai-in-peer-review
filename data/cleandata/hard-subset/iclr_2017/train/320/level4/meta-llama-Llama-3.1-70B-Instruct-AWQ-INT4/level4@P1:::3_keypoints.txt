- Proposes using spatial feature maps from pre-trained convnets, bilinear one-step visual dynamics at multiple scales, and RL to learn target-following visual servoing.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Well-motivated placement in literature addressing visual servoing with learned features, predictive models, and RL.  
- Strong empirical support: substantial results on a complex synthetic car-following benchmark.  
- Addresses limits of traditional methods relying on hand-designed features and analytic dynamics, improving robustness and generalization.  
- Controlled simulation experiments quantify the usefulness of pre-trained deep features and show clear gains over sensible baselines (including ground-truth boxes).  
- Method contribution: principled learning of multi-scale visual feature weights via trust-region fitted Q-iteration and a sample-efficient Q-approximator combined with model-based one-step dynamics.  
- Limitation: experiments lack challenging visual variations (clutter, distractors, appearance/motion diversity, richer environments).  
- Suggestion: investigate end-to-end training / fine-tuning of the convnet to potentially improve performance and generalization.  
- Exposition could be improved by better organizing and simplifying practical implementation details and tricks.  
- Question: provide more details on how pre-trained features were selected and whether they were fine-tuned for the task.  
- Question: how will the approach be extended to more complex visual conditions (clutter, distractors, varied appearance/motion)?  
- Question: have you explored the impact of end-to-end training on performance?  
- Question: provide details on computational efficiency, time complexity, and required computational resources.
