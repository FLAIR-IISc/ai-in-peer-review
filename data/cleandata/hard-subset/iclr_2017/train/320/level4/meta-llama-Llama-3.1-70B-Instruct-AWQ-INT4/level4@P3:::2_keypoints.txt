- Proposes an action-conditional bilinear model on pre-trained VGG features with a multi-scale, multi-channel locally-connected variant and policy derived via linearized dynamics.  
- Introduces a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values to address one-step-ahead prediction limits, evaluated with a fitted-value approach.  
- Paper is well written, mathematically solid, and conceptually exhaustive.  
- Experiments show benefits of the value-weighted objective and empirical generalization from pre-trained visual features.  
- Novel contribution includes outlining a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm.  
- Uses pre-trained features rather than end-to-end learning, raising alignment concerns with representation-learning focus.  
- Policy representation is fixed and values are approximated linearly with problem-specific features, reducing generality.  
- Requests clarification on how choice of pre-trained features affects performance and whether end-to-end learning would help.  
- Requests more insight into bilinear model and weighted-objective design choices and their performance impact.  
- Requests comparison to state-of-the-art visual servoing methods and scenarios where the proposed method outperforms others.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance pending the requested clarifications.
