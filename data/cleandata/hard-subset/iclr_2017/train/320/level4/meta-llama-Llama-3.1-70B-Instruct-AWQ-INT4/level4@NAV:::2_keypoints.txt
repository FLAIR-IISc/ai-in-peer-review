- Paper studies visual servoing using a learned visual representation and derives policies via linearized dynamics.  
- Proposes an action-conditional bilinear model of visual features extracted from a pre-trained VGG network.  
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.  
- Addresses the bilinear model’s one-step prediction limitation with a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values.  
- Uses a fitted-value approach to evaluate and optimize the proposed objective.  
- Develops a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm as part of the method.  
- Empirical results show the value-weighted objective improves performance and generalization with pre-trained features.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically sound, and conceptually thorough.  
- Recommended for its potential impact on the robotics research community.  
- Contribution to representation learning is limited: relies on pre-trained VGG and does not explore end-to-end learning.  
- Policy representation is fixed and value functions are approximated linearly using problem-specific features, which may limit broader representation claims.  
- As a result, the work focuses more on control than on advancing general representation learning, which may not perfectly align with the conference’s representation emphasis.
