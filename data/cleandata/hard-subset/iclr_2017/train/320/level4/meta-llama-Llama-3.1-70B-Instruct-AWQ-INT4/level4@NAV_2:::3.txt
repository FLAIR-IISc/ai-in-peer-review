This paper proposes to tackle visual servoing (specifically target following) using spatial feature maps from convolutional networks pre-trained on general image classification tasks. The authors combine bilinear models of one-step dynamics of visual feature maps at multiple scales with a reinforcement learning algorithm to learn a servoing policy. This policy is learned by minimizing a regularized weighted average of distances to features predicted by the aforementioned model of visual dynamics.

I recommend accepting this paper with minor revisions. The primary reasons for this decision are the paper's clear contributions, well-designed experiments, and thorough evaluation. The authors provide a principled approach to learning visual servoing mechanisms using learned visual features, learned predictive dynamics models, and reinforcement learning.

The paper's main contributions include controlled experiments in simulation quantifying the usefulness of pre-trained deep features for visual servoing, clear performance benefits with respect to many sensible baselines, and a principled learning of multi-scale visual feature weights with an efficient trust-region fitted Q-iteration algorithm.

To further improve the paper, I suggest the authors consider the following points:

* Conduct more complex benchmark experiments to evaluate the approach's robustness to visual variations. This could involve using a larger number of 3D car models, including more realistic and diverse ones, and populating the environment with more distractor cars.
* Investigate the impact of end-to-end training on the approach's performance and generalization in more challenging visual conditions.
* Improve the reproducibility of the paper by grouping, simplifying, or reorganizing the exposition of implementation details and providing an open-source implementation of the method.
* Correct minor typos and grammatical errors throughout the paper.

Overall, the paper presents a well-designed and thoroughly evaluated approach to visual servoing, and with some minor revisions, it has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
