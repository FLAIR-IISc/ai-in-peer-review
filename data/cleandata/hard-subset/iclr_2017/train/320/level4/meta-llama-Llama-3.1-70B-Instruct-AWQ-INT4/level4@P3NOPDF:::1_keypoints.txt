- Introduces a novel Q-iteration approach for learning visual servoing with three contributions.
- Contribution: bilinear dynamics model to predict next-frame features from action and current frame.
- Contribution: Q-function formulation that learns weights for different feature channels.
- Contribution: an elegant Bellman error optimization using the observation that Q-parameter scaling doesn't change the optimal policy.
- Strength: thoroughly explores methods to link action (ut) and frame representation (yt) for predicting y_{t+1}.
- Strength: favors a locally connected bilinear model as a good trade-off between computation and expressiveness.
- Strength: channel-weighting in the Q-function outperforms treating channel errors equally; unweighted baselines perform worse.
- Strength: solid optimization scheme enabling a more elegant Fitted Q-Iteration (FQI).
- Weakness: lacks experimental comparison between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics.
- Weakness: should compare FQI to an iterative approach that holds the second term in Eq.5 fixed.
- Weakness: experiments are small-scale/toy and need more complex scenarios to demonstrate robustness.
- Weakness: modified VGG idea is not experimentally validated against the original VGG.
- Conclusion: technically novel and well-formulated, but requires stronger, more comprehensive experiments to convincingly support the claims.
