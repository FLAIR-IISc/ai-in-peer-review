- Proposes a Q-iteration-based visual servoing method with a bilinear dynamics model, Q-function that weights feature channels, and an efficient Bellman-error optimization.  
- Introduces a locally connected bilinear model balancing computational efficiency and expressiveness for predicting next-frame features.  
- Formulates a Q-function that learns per-channel weights to capture object importance rather than treating channel errors equally.  
- Provides experimental evidence that weighted-channel Q-functions outperform unweighted methods.  
- Develops an optimization scheme for the Bellman error leveraging scale-invariance of Q-parameters to enable a cleaner Fitted Q-Iteration.  
- Strength: well-justified exploration of action-frame connection choices and rationale for locally connected model.  
- Strength: channel-weighting idea is empirically supported and appears more effective than uniform error treatment.  
- Strength: elegant and efficient Bellman-error optimization improving FQI implementation.  
- Weakness: lacks comparative experiments in Section 4 between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics.  
- Weakness: missing comparison between FQI and an iterative approach that holds the second term in Eq. 5 fixed.  
- Experimental results are limited in scale/scope, partly due to lack of established benchmarks; modified VGG needs validation against the original.  
- Overall: technical novelty and solid formulation, but claims would be strengthened by more comprehensive experiments.
