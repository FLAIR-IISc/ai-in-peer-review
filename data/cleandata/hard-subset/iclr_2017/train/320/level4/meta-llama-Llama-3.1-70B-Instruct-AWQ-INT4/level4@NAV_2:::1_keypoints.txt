- Proposes a novel Q-iteration approach for learning visual servoing.
- Main contributions: bilinear dynamics model, Q-function that weights feature channels, and an elegant Bellman-error optimization method.
- Compares ways to connect action and frame representation and favors a locally connected bilinear model for compute vs. expressivity.
- Lacks sufficient experimental comparison between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics.
- Channel-weighted Q-function is more effective than treating all channel errors equally; unweighted performance degrades.
- Presents a solid optimization scheme exploiting scale invariance of Q-function parameters to enable an FQI-style solution.
- Missing comparison between the proposed FQI approach and an iterative scheme that holds the second Bellman term fixed.
- Experimental evaluation is small-scale and toyish, limiting empirical strength of claims.
- A modified VGG is proposed but not experimentally validated against the original VGG.
- Overall technical novelty and formulation are solid but would benefit from stronger experiments.
- Conference decision: Accept.
- Acceptance reasons: novel visual-servoing Q-iteration, innovative channel-weighted Q-function, and effective Bellman-error optimization.
- Requested improvements: more dynamics comparisons, validate modified VGG, and test on more realistic or real-world scenarios.
- Additional suggestions: provide dataset details, discuss potential real-world applications, and outline limitations and future work.
