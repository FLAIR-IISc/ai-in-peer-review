- Proposes visual servoing using an action-conditional bilinear model on pre-trained VGG features, with linearized dynamics to derive policies.  
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.  
- Proposes a value-weighted objective to incorporate long-term policy values despite one-step prediction dynamics.  
- Presents a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm (claimed as first outline) and uses a fitted-value approach for evaluation.  
- Experiments on a synthetic car-following benchmark show substantial gains over pixel-based and hand-designed keypoint methods.  
- Uses pre-trained VGG features and shows empirical generalization benefits from these visual features.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically solid, and conceptually thorough; recommended for acceptance.  
- Main limitation: limited contribution to representation learning—relies on fixed pre-trained features rather than end-to-end learning.  
- Policy representation is fixed and value function is approximated linearly with problem-specific features, which may limit generality.  
- Requests clarification on why end-to-end representation learning was not pursued.  
- Requests discussion of limitations of fixed policy/linear value approximation and directions for improvement.  
- Requests more analysis of trade-offs between components of the value-weighted objective.  
- Requests more details on the fitted-value evaluation: value function approximator choice and optimization procedure.  
- Requests discussion of applicability and challenges for real-world deployment beyond the synthetic benchmark.  
- Requests information on computational efficiency and potential ways to improve runtime/resource usage.
