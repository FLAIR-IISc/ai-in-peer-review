- Summary: Uses pre-trained CNN spatial feature maps and multi-scale bilinear one-step dynamics plus RL to learn a visual-servoing policy that minimizes a regularized weighted distance to predicted features.
- Contribution: Provides controlled simulation experiments quantifying the effectiveness of pre-trained deep features for target following.
- Contribution: Demonstrates clear performance gains over sensible baselines, including methods using ground-truth bounding boxes.
- Contribution: Proposes a principled method to learn multi-scale visual feature weights via a trust-region fitted Q-iteration algorithm that handles distractors.
- Contribution: Achieves good sample efficiency through the Q-function approximator and model-based one-step visual dynamics.
- Contribution: Releases an open-source virtual city environment as a useful benchmark for visual servoing.
- Suggestion: Evaluate on more complex, realistic environments with clutter, distractors, and more diverse 3D car models to test robustness to visual variation.
- Suggestion: Investigate end-to-end fine-tuning of the convolutional network to assess benefits of representation learning for servoing.
- Suggestion: Improve reproducibility by consolidating and clarifying implementation details and providing an open-source implementation.
- Typos: Two minor typos noted on p.2 ("relative[ly]" and "be applied [to] directly learn").
- Conclusion: Paper is interesting and solid, authors responded well to feedback, and addressing suggestions would further strengthen the work.
