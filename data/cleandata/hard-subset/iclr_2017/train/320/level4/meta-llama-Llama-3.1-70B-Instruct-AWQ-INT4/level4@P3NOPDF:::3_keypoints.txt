- Proposes using pre-trained CNN spatial feature maps plus bilinear one-step dynamics and RL to learn visual servoing (target following).  
- Controlled simulation experiments quantitatively evaluate the method.  
- Method outperforms sensible baselines, including ones using ground-truth bounding boxes.  
- Learns principled multi-scale visual feature weights via a trust-region fitted Q-iteration to handle distractors.  
- Demonstrates good sample efficiency through the Q-function choice and model-based one-step dynamics.  
- Provides an open-source virtual city benchmark environment for visual servoing.  
- Suggest expanding the benchmark to more complex visual conditions (clutter, distractors, varied 3D models).  
- Suggest investigating end-to-end training / fine-tuning to evaluate representation learning benefits.  
- Recommend improving reproducibility with clearer implementation details and an open-source implementation.  
- Notes some typos that should be corrected.  
- Overall well-written and solid; with revisions addressing limitations it could make a significant contribution.
