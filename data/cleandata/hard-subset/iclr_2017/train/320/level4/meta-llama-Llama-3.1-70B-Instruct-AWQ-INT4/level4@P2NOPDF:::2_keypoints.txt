- Proposes an action-conditional bilinear model of visual features extracted from a pre-trained VGG, with a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant.  
- Derives a policy by linearizing the learned feature dynamics.  
- Introduces a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values to overcome one-step prediction limits of the bilinear model.  
- Uses a fitted-value evaluation approach and outlines a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm.  
- Experiments show the value-weighted objective improves performance and pre-trained visual features aid generalization.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically solid, and conceptually comprehensive; strongly recommended for robotics researchers.  
- Contribution to representation learning is limited — does not explore end-to-end learned representations, relying on pre-trained features.  
- Policy representation is fixed and values are approximated linearly with problem-specific features, making alignment with ICLR’s emphasis on representation learning weaker.
