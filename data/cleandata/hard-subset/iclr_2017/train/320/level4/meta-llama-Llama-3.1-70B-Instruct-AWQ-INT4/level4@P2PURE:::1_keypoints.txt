- Proposes a novel visual servoing approach using Q-iteration with three contributions: a bilinear dynamics model, a Q-function that weights feature channels, and an optimization method for the Bellman error.  
- Introduces a bilinear dynamics model to predict next-frame features from actions and current frames.  
- Formulates servoing with a Q-function that learns per-channel weights to reflect object importance.  
- Presents an elegant optimization scheme for the Bellman error leveraging scale-invariance of the Q-function parameters.  
- Argues convincingly that a locally connected bilinear model balances computational efficiency and expressive power.  
- Shows that per-channel weighting improves performance versus unweighted error aggregation.  
- Section 4 needs more experiments comparing fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics.  
- A comparison between the proposed FQI and an iterative scheme that holds the second term fixed is missing.  
- Experimental evaluation is small-scale and toy-like, limited by the lack of domain benchmarks.  
- The claimed benefit of modifying VGG must be experimentally validated to show improvement over the original.  
- Overall technically solid and well-formulated, but requires stronger experimental support to back claims.
