- Proposes Q-iteration-based visual servoing with three main contributions: bilinear dynamics model, Q-function that weights feature channels, and an optimization method for the Bellman error.  
- Explores methods to connect action and frame representations, arguing locally connected bilinear model balances computation and expressiveness.  
- Lacks sufficient experimental comparison among fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics.  
- Channel-weighting in the Q-function is more effective than treating all channels equally; unweighted baseline performs worse.  
- Presents an elegant Bellman-error optimization exploiting scale invariance of Q-parameters to enable a fitted Q-iteration (FQI) approach.  
- Requests a direct comparison between the proposed FQI variant and iterative optimization schemes that hold the second term fixed.  
- Experiments are small-scale/toy and therefore somewhat unsatisfying; stronger, larger-scale experiments are needed to bolster claims.  
- Modification of VGG network is proposed but not experimentally validated against the original VGG.  
- Overall assessment: technically novel and clearly presented; recommendation is accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: add experiments comparing dynamics models, validate modified VGG, provide more FQI/optimization details, and discuss limitations and future work.  
- Questions for authors: provide FQI hyperparameter choices, rationale for number of FQI iterations, and discuss limitations and possible extensions of the bilinear dynamics model.
