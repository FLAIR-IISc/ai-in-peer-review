- Proposes a sample-efficient visual servoing method combining pre-trained convolutional features, a learned bilinear feature dynamics model, and Fitted Q-Iteration to learn feature-channel weighting.  
- Demonstrated on a simulated quadcopter car-following task, learning a robust policy from only 20 trajectories.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novel technical formulation: uses RL to learn feature-channel weights for an analytically solvable one-step lookahead controller rather than an end-to-end policy.  
- Exceptional sample efficiency: outperforms model-free baselines (e.g., TRPO) by over two orders of magnitude in data efficiency.  
- Uses modified VGG features to reduce spatial invariance and improve visual generalization without task-specific data.  
- Bilinear dynamics model: balances expressiveness and learnability; locally connected variant reduces parameter count.  
- FQI variant: formulates Q as weighted feature prediction errors and optimizes Bellman error with a two-step update exploiting scale invariance.  
- Experiments support main claims but lack thorough ablation studies isolating design choices (modified VGG, locally connected bilinear, FQI scheme).  
- Recommend adding ablation studies, a discussion of sim-to-real transfer challenges, and empirical comparisons of bilinear model variants.  
- Questions for authors: provide comparative results using unmodified VGG, compare the proposed FQI update to standard FQI stability/convergence, and report computational training cost vs. TRPO.
