- Proposes a visual-servoing method inspired by fitted Q-iteration with three contributions: a bilinear dynamics model, a channel-weighted Q-function, and an optimization for the Bellman error.
- Thorough exploration of action–frame interaction models and a convincing argument for a locally connected bilinear dynamics model balancing cost and expressiveness.
- Learning per-channel weights is well-motivated and experimentally shown to outperform an unweighted baseline.
- Elegant Bellman-error optimization that uses invariance to Q-function scaling to enable an FQI-style update without fixing the target term.
- Lacks empirical comparison among the three dynamics architectures (fully connected, convolutional, locally connected); needs an ablation study.
- Missing experimental comparison between the proposed optimization and a traditional fixed-target iterative Q-update.
- Experiments are small-scale and simulated; claims need support from larger or more realistic benchmarks.
- Proposed modification to VGG is not experimentally validated; requires an ablation to justify the architectural change.
- Overall: solid technical ideas but limited empirical validation; stronger experiments and ablations would significantly improve the paper.
