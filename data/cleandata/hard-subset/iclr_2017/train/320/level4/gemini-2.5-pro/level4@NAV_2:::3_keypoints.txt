- Novel approach combining pre-trained deep visual features, a learned predictive model of feature dynamics, and a sample-efficient RL algorithm for target following.  
- Uses spatial VGG feature maps and models one-step dynamics with a multi-scale bilinear model; policy operates in feature space.  
- Introduces a fitted Q-iteration (FQI) that learns weights over feature channels and scales to focus on the target and ignore distractors.  
- Q-function approximator is linear in learnable weights and aligns with the servoing policy structure, enabling very efficient FQI and feature selection.  
- Method is highly sample-efficient, outperforming model-free RL (e.g., TRPO) by orders of magnitude.  
- Empirical validation in a simulated quadcopter car-following task shows superior performance vs. pixel-based, ORB, deep-RL, and state-of-the-art tracker-based visual servoing baselines, including generalization to novel targets.  
- Provides an open-source simulation environment to facilitate future research.  
- Recommendation: accept — due to a principled, data-efficient method and thorough, well-controlled empirical evaluation against strong baselines.  
- Suggestion: evaluate in visually richer, more cluttered environments with more distractors and diverse 3D models to better test robustness.  
- Suggestion: investigate end-to-end fine-tuning of convolutional features to explore potential performance gains and representation learning.  
- Suggestion: consolidate key implementation details and hyperparameters in the main paper and point to code for exhaustive settings to improve reproducibility.
