- Novel, sample-efficient visual-servoing method combining pre-trained VGG features, a multiscale bilinear dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration to learn task-specific feature weights.
- Key technical contribution: Q-function formulation whose greedy policy matches the servoing objective and an optimization scheme that stabilizes FQI by fitting scaling and bias during Bellman error minimization.
- Claims strong sample efficiency: effective policy learned from ~20 training trajectories, outperforming model-free deep RL baselines.
- Decision: Weak Accept.
- Strength: Elegant approach that bypasses hand-designed features by learning weightings over general-purpose deep features.
- Strength: The proposed FQI variant is a noteworthy stabilization technique (joint α and b optimization) compared to standard target-network methods.
- Weakness: Empirical validation is limited to a single simulated car-following environment.
- Weakness: Missing ablation studies to justify the locally connected bilinear model choice versus fully connected alternatives.
- Weakness: Missing ablation comparing modified VGG (dilated convolutions, no max-pooling) to original VGG to quantify impact on spatial information.
- Weakness: Baseline comparisons incomplete—TRPO baseline may be under-tuned and classical IBVS has unfair advantage (ground-truth depth).
- Recommendation: Include ablation comparing fully-connected vs locally-connected bilinear dynamics (even on lower-dim features).
- Recommendation: Include ablation comparing modified VGG to original VGG features to quantify the benefit of dilated convolutions.
- Request: Provide empirical comparison showing how the joint scaling/bias FQI step improves stability and performance versus holding target Q fixed.
- Question: How sensitive is the learned dynamics model to the quality of the data-collection policy—would random actions suffice?
- Question: Clarify the cost function choice—were simpler costs (e.g., L2 target-center error) tested and how did they perform?
