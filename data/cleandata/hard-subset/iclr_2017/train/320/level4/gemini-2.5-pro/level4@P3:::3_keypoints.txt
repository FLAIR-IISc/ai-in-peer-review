- Paper proposes servoing in learned VGG feature space using a bilinear feature dynamics model and a sample-efficient FQI to weight features for target following.  
- Method is robust to distractors, occlusions, and viewpoint changes and succeeds in a simulated car-following task with only 20 training trajectories.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Principled and effective integration of pre-trained deep features, a learned dynamics model, and tailored FQI improves sample efficiency.  
- Key reason 2: Thorough empirical evaluation showing substantial gains over strong baselines (TRPO, IBVS with ORB, C-COT).  
- Pre-trained VGG features provide a powerful visual prior that avoids learning representations from scratch.  
- Bilinear feature dynamics are simple to learn from small data yet expressive enough for accurate one-step prediction needed by the servo.  
- The FQI-based learned feature weighting addresses distractors by prioritizing features that contribute to long-term task success; the Q-approximator’s linearity in weights yields stable, efficient updates.  
- The method achieves impressive sample efficiency, outperforming a TRPO baseline that uses 100× more data.  
- Empirical validation includes strong baselines, ablations over VGG layers, and generalization tests to unseen car models.  
- Suggested improvement: increase visual complexity of the benchmark (dynamic distractors, more 3D car models, varied lighting/weather).  
- Suggested improvement: explore fine-tuning the VGG end-to-end to study trade-offs between performance and sample efficiency.  
- Suggested improvement: consolidate experimental hyperparameters/implementation details for clarity and reproducibility; code release is appreciated.  
- Minor typos noted (two examples on page 2).  
- Questions for authors: any insights or results on fine-tuning the feature extractor and its effect on sample efficiency?  
- Questions for authors: how sensitive is performance to the accuracy/amount of data for the bilinear dynamics model, and when does performance degrade?  
- Questions for authors: how does the FQI-learned weighting handle temporarily more salient distractors (e.g., another car cutting in), and does the policy recover gracefully?
