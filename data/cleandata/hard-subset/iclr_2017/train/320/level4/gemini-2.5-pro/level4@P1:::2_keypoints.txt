- Proposes a sample-efficient visual servoing method that performs control in VGG feature space using a learned action-conditional, multi-scale bilinear dynamics model.  
- Uses a weighted combination of multi-scale feature errors as the servoing objective and learns those weights with a fitted Q-iteration (FQI) algorithm to capture long-term value.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on a simulated car-following task, learning effective policies from only 20 trajectories and generalizing to novel targets.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: Novel integration of deep features, model-based control, and value-function learning yields a complete, highly sample-efficient system.  
- Key reason: Thorough experiments show substantial improvements over conventional visual servoing and model-free RL baselines.  
- Strength: Well-written, mathematically sound, and conceptually coherent system that links one-step predictive control to long-horizon RL via a linear Q-function approximator.  
- Strength: The FQI-based weight learning is elegant and central to achieving sample efficiency.  
- Strength: Using pre-trained visual features enables generalization in control tasks without heavy data requirements.  
- Concern: Primary contribution is in control/usage of features rather than representation learning, which may reduce fit for ICLR.  
- Limitation: Fixed pre-trained VGG features and lack of end-to-end fine-tuning limit claims about representation learning and asymptotic performance.  
- Limitation: Linear Q-function approximation and fixed policy class may be less general than nonlinear deep-RL approaches and could fail in more complex servoing tasks.  
- Suggestion: Paper should better position trade-offs between fixed pre-trained representations and end-to-end learning for the ICLR audience.  
- Suggestion/Question: Clarify limitations and possible extensions of the linear Q approximation and whether the framework can support nonlinear Q-functions.  
- Suggestion/Question: Provide more intuition on the two-step FQI update (solving scale/bias then weights) and how it affects stability or convergence versus standard FQI.
