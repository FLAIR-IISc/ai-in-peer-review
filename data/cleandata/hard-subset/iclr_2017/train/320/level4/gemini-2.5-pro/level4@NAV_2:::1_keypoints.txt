- Method combines pre-trained visual features, a learned bilinear feature-dynamics model, and sample-efficient RL for visual servoing; evaluated on a simulated car-following task.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, conditional on addressing experimental validation gaps.

Strengths:
- Learns predictive feature dynamics in a pre-trained feature space (locally connected bilinear model) to trade expressivity and parameter count.
- Frames weighted servoing as Q-learning to learn channel/scale weights, enabling selection of task-relevant visual info and outperforming an unweighted baseline.
- Proposes a novel FQI optimization that exploits Q-function scale invariance via a two-step solve for scaling/bias, improving sample efficiency.

Weaknesses / experimental gaps:
- Lacks ablation studies comparing locally connected bilinear dynamics to fully connected or convolutional variants.
- No empirical comparison of the proposed FQI against a standard FQI baseline (e.g., using a fixed target network).
- Experiments are exclusively simulated, with limited discussion or validation of sim-to-real transfer.

Suggested improvements:
- Add experiments comparing dynamics model variants (locally connected vs fully connected vs convolutional).
- Compare the proposed FQI to a standard FQI implementation (e.g., fixed target network) to demonstrate practical benefits.
- Provide an ablation study comparing the modified VGG to the original off-the-shelf VGG features.

Questions for authors:
- Can you provide empirical results comparing the locally connected bilinear model to fully connected or convolutional dynamics models?
- How does your FQI’s convergence and final performance compare to a more standard FQI with a fixed target network?
- Have you evaluated using features from the original VGG, and can you ablate the impact of your VGG modifications on performance?
