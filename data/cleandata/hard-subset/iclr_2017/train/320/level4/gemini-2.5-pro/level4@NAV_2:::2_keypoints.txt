1. Integrates pre-trained VGG features, an action-conditional bilinear dynamics model, and RL to perform visual servoing in feature space.  
2. Performs servoing on deep features rather than raw pixels for better invariance and generalization.  
3. Proposes a simple bilinear one-step dynamics model to predict feature evolution under actions.  
4. Introduces a sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration (FQI) that learns weights over feature channels and scales to connect short-horizon models to long-term rewards.  
5. Uses the learned weighting to adapt a greedy servoing policy for expected long-term performance.  
6. Validated on a complex simulated car-following task, achieving effective policies from very few trajectories.  
7. Recommendation: accept, citing the novel and effective combination and strong empirical results.  
8. Strength: pre-trained features provide invariance, the bilinear model yields a tractable control objective, and the FQI weighting is a significant contribution.  
9. Empirical claim: learns robust policy from ~20 trajectories and outperforms TRPO by over two orders of magnitude in sample efficiency.  
10. Strong baselines and comparisons (pixel models, ORB keypoints, C-COT tracker) demonstrate the method’s advantages.  
11. Suggestion: clarify framing — emphasize model-based/sample-efficient RL contribution vs representation learning since features are fixed.  
12. Suggestion: discuss limitations of quadratic-policy/linear-Q assumptions and how to extend to more complex non-linear policies.  
13. Question: provide intuition for the two-step FQI update (solve scalar α and bias b before full weight θ) and its effect on stability/performance.  
14. Question: explain how the learned dynamics implicitly capture geometric/depth information and robustness to large target depth variations.
