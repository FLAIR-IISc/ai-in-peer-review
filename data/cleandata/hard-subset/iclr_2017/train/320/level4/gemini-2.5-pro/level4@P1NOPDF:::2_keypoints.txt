- Paper studies visual servoing using a learned visual representation and shows benefits of this approach.  
- Proposes an action-conditional bilinear model to predict one-step dynamics of visual features extracted from a pre-trained VGG.  
- Derives a control policy by linearizing the learned bilinear dynamics.  
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.  
- Recognizes the bilinear model is myopic and introduces a weighted objective that incorporates the long-term value of the current policy.  
- Uses a fitted-value approach to address policy evaluation.  
- Presents a trust-region fitted Q-iteration algorithm (claimed to be the first such presentation).  
- Experiments empirically demonstrate the advantage of the value-weighted objective.  
- Empirical results show pre-trained visual features improve generalization.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically solid, and conceptually thorough.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance and believes the contributions are valuable to robotics researchers.  
- Reservation: limited contribution to core "representation learning"—does not learn visual features end-to-end within the control loop.  
- Reservation: policy representation is fixed and the value function is approximated linearly with problem-specific features, making the work feel more control-focused than aligned with ICLR’s representation-learning emphasis.
