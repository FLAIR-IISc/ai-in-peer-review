- Proposes an action-conditional bilinear model that learns dynamics of visual features from a pre-trained VGG and derives a policy by linearizing these learned dynamics.  
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model to boost performance.  
- Identifies the one-step bilinear model's short-horizon limitation and addresses it with a value-weighted objective incorporating long-term value estimates.  
- Performs policy evaluation using a fitted-value iteration approach.  
- Formally introduces a trust-region fitted Q-iteration algorithm (claimed as a first).  
- Empirically shows the value-weighted objective improves control policy learning.  
- Demonstrates that using pre-trained visual features improves generalization in visual servoing tasks.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically rigorous, and presents a conceptually complete framework.  
- Recommends acceptance, citing strong contributions relevant to robotics and reinforcement learning.  
- Main reservation: the work aligns more with control/RL than representation learning, making its fit to ICLR uncertain.  
- Notes the method does not learn visual features end-to-end and uses a fixed policy representation and linear value-function approximation, emphasizing control-centric design.
