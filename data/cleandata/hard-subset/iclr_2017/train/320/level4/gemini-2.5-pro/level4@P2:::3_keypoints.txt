- Proposes a novel, sample-efficient visual servoing framework combining pre-trained VGG features, a multiscale bilinear one-step feature dynamics model, and a specialized FQI that weights feature channels.  
- Recommendation: Accept (strongly recommended).  
- Method is well-motivated: avoids costly end-to-end learning by leveraging pre-trained features for data efficiency.  
- Bilinear dynamics model is simple yet sufficient, and its linearity with controls enables an analytic, efficient servoing policy.  
- Main novelty: FQI with a Q-function linear in feature weights enables efficient learning of task-relevant visual cues from few interactions.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, using a 3D quadcopter-following-car simulation and strong, relevant baselines.  
- Significantly outperforms classical IBVS, even when IBVS uses C-COT tracking or ORB features.  
- Achieves much lower trajectory costs and >100× fewer training episodes than model-free TRPO on raw pixels (20 vs. 2000+ episodes).  
- Ablation studies show FQI-based feature weighting is critical, outperforming uniform weighting and TRPO-learned weights.  
- Authors open-sourced the simulation and code, aiding reproducibility.  
- Suggested improvements: test in more visually complex scenarios (dynamic distractors, clutter, varied weather/textures).  
- Suggested future work: explore fine-tuning or end-to-end representation learning to adapt features to the task.  
- Presentation suggestion: streamline main text by moving fine-grained implementation details to the appendix and rely on code for full reproducibility.
