- Proposes an action-conditional bilinear model to predict one-step evolution of VGG visual features.  
- Enhances the model with multi-scale, multi-channel, and locally-connected architecture.  
- Derives a control policy by linearizing the learned dynamics.  
- Introduces a value-weighted objective to incorporate long-term rewards despite one-step predictions, with strong empirical benefits.  
- Presents a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm, a novel contribution to the RL community.  
- Demonstrates that using pre-trained VGG features yields strong generalization across tasks and environments.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically rigorous, and conceptually thorough.  
- Main reservation: limited contribution to representation learning since dynamics are learned over fixed, pre-trained features rather than end-to-end.  
- Fixed policy representation and linear value-function approximation further shift focus away from deep representation learning.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, impactful work for model-based visual servoing/control; recommend acceptance though thematic fit for ICLR (representation learning focus) is debatable.
