- Proposes a visual-servoing method combining pre-trained VGG features, an action-conditional bilinear dynamics model, and FQI-learned feature weighting.  
- Performs servoing in a weighted learned feature space rather than pixel space for robustness to occlusions and distractors.  
- Demonstrates exceptional sample efficiency, learning a competent controller from only 20 trajectories.  
- Outperforms classical IBVS, a state-of-the-art visual tracker, and TRPO-based deep RL by a large margin.  
- Ablation studies validate the necessity of the learned feature-weighting scheme via FQI.  
- Using pre-trained VGG features sidesteps large representation-learning data needs and enables rapid adaptation to new targets.  
- The bilinear dynamics model is simple enough to learn from little data yet expressive for one-step feature evolution.  
- The Q-function linear in feature weights yields computationally efficient and stable FQI updates.  
- Empirical results show much lower cost than TRPO trained on over 100× more data and good generalization to novel targets.  
- Paper is well-written, method clearly explained, and code/environment are released for reproducibility.  
- Suggest discussing trade-offs between using fixed pre-trained representations versus fully end-to-end learned systems and potential asymptotic benefits of the latter.  
- Ask about limitations of the linear Q-function approximation for more complex rewards or dynamics and scalability concerns.  
- Recommend evaluating sensitivity to the choice of pre-trained feature extractor (e.g., ResNet, MobileNet vs VGG-16).  
- Request more intuition on the two-step FQI update (solving for α and b, then θ) and its relation to methods like LSTD-Q or other policy-evaluation techniques.
