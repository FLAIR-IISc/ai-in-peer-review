- Proposes a Q-iteration-based visual servoing approach with a locally connected bilinear dynamics model, a feature-channel-weighting Q-function, and an improved Bellman-error optimization.  
- Contribution: bilinear dynamics model predicting next-frame features from current frame and action.  
- Contribution: Q-function formulation that learns per-feature-channel importance weights.  
- Contribution: optimization scheme for Bellman error leveraging policy invariance to Q-function scaling for cleaner Fitted Q-Iteration updates.  
- Strength: Thorough exploration and justification of interaction models, favoring locally connected bilinear as a cost/expressivity tradeoff.  
- Strength: Channel-weighting idea effectively captures object importance and outperforms unweighted baselines.  
- Strength: Elegant optimization insight allowing avoidance of holding target Q-values fixed during FQI.  
- Weakness: Lacks direct experimental comparison between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models.  
- Weakness: Missing empirical comparison between the proposed optimization and standard iterative methods that fix the target Q-term.  
- Experimental: Results are limited in scale and rely on simulated/toy environments, reducing external validity.  
- Experimental: Proposed VGG modifications are suggested but not experimentally validated against standard VGG.  
- Overall: Technically novel and well-formulated contributions but would be much stronger with broader and deeper experimental validation.
