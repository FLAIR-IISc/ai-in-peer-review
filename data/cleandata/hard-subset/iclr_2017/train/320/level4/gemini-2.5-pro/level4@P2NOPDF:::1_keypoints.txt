- Introduces a Q-learning approach for visual servoing with three contributions: a bilinear dynamics model, a Q-function that weights feature channels, and a scale-invariant Bellman optimization.

- Strength: Thorough architectural exploration and argument that a locally connected bilinear model balances efficiency and expressiveness.

- Strength: Q-function-based channel weighting identifies task-relevant visual cues and outperforms an unweighted baseline.

- Strength: Exploits scale-invariance of the optimal policy to derive an elegant FQI optimization that avoids fixing target Q-values.

- Weakness: No empirical ablation comparing fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models to support the architectural claim.

- Weakness: Lacks a direct experimental comparison between the proposed Bellman optimization and standard iterative (target-fixed) approaches.

- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is limited to small simulated environments and needs validation on more complex or realistic tasks.

- Weakness: Proposed VGG modifications are speculative without experimental evidence of improvement.

- Overall: Technically novel and well-motivated contributions, but empirical validation is insufficient; paper would be stronger with the suggested ablation studies and harder benchmarks.
