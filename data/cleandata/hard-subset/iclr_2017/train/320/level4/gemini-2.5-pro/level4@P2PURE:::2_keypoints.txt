- Proposes an action-conditional bilinear dynamics model over visual features extracted from a pre-trained VGG for visual servoing.  
- Presents a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.  
- Derives a policy by linearizing the learned dynamics model.  
- Addresses the bilinear model’s single-step nature with a value-weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy value.  
- Uses fitted-value iteration for policy evaluation and introduces a trust-region fitted Q-iteration algorithm (claimed novel).  
- Experiments empirically demonstrate the benefit of the value-weighted objective.  
- Experiments show using pre-trained visual features improves generalization.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically solid, and conceptually comprehensive.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, noting strong relevance to the robotics community.  
- Main reservation: limited contribution to core "representation learning"—relies on pre-trained VGG instead of end-to-end feature learning.  
- Additional concern: policy representation is fixed and the value function is a linear approximation over problem-specific features, reducing alignment with ICLR’s central themes.
