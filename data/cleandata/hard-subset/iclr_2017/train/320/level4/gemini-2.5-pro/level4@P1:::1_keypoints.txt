- Novel, sample-efficient visual servoing method combining pre-trained VGG features, a learned bilinear feature dynamics model, and reinforcement learning.
- Proposes learning a bilinear model to predict how visual feature representations change in response to robot actions.
- Formulates servoing as minimizing a weighted distance in feature space, with an RL agent learning per-channel weights to focus on task-relevant features.
- Introduces a fitted Q-iteration (FQI) procedure with a specialized Bellman-error optimization that leverages Q-function positive-scaling invariance.
- Demonstrates strong sample efficiency, learning effective policies from only 20 trajectories.
- Reviewer leans toward acceptance based on technical novelty and practical sample efficiency.
- Strength: Locally connected bilinear dynamics model is well-motivated as a trade-off between expressiveness and computational tractability.
- Strength: Weighted feature servoing is intuitive and empirically outperforms an unweighted baseline by focusing on target-relevant features.
- Strength: The FQI optimization is an elegant algorithmic contribution addressing common Q-learning instability.
- Weakness: Experimental validation is limited to a single, relatively simple simulated car-following task, limiting claims of generality.
- Weakness: Paper lacks ablation studies to isolate and justify key design choices.
- Requested experiment: Empirical comparison of locally connected bilinear dynamics vs fully connected and convolutional variants.
- Requested experiment: Quantify benefits of the proposed FQI vs a conventional target-fixed iterative Bellman-error minimization (convergence/performance).
- Requested experiment: Ablation comparing the modified VGG (dilated convs replacing max-pooling) against standard VGG to validate necessity.
