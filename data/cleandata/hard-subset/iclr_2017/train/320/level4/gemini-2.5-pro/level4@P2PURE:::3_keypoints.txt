- Paper proposes visual servoing for target following using pre-trained convolutional spatial feature maps combined with a bilinear one-step dynamics model and RL-based policy optimization.  
- Controlled simulation experiments quantify benefits of pre-trained deep features for visual servoing.  
- Shows clear performance gains over several baselines, including methods using ground-truth bounding boxes.  
- Introduces a principled method to learn weights of multi-scale visual features via a trust-region fitted Q-iteration that handles distractors.  
- Demonstrates good sample efficiency enabled by the chosen Q-function approximator and model-based one-step visual dynamics.  
- Releases an open-source virtual city environment for benchmarking visual servoing algorithms.  
- Suggests evaluating under more complex visual conditions (more clutter, distractors, varied object appearance/motion) to test robustness.  
- Recommends exploring end-to-end training/fine-tuning of the convolutional network to assess representation learning benefits and generalization.  
- Calls for better reproducibility: summarize critical implementation details in the paper and provide an open-source implementation.  
- Notes minor typos to correct (examples: p.2 wording errors).  
- Overall assessment: solid and interesting paper with good revisions; authors appear capable of addressing suggested improvements.
