- Proposes an action-conditional bilinear model of visual dynamics using VGG-extracted features and derives a policy via linearized dynamics.
- Introduces multi-scale, multi-channel, and locally-connected variants of the bilinear model.
- Presents a weighted objective that incorporates long-term values of the incumbent policy to compensate for the single-step dynamics model.
- Evaluates long-term effects using a fitted-value approach.
- Demonstrates strong mathematical rigor and a comprehensive conceptual framework.
- Empirically validates the effectiveness of the value-weighted objective.
- Formalizes a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm (claimed first).
- Shows that pre-trained visual features improve generalization.
- Recommends the paper for acceptance due to its likely benefit to robotics research.
- Critique: contribution to the "representation" problem is limited—uses pre-trained VGG rather than learning representations end-to-end.
- Critique: policy representation is fixed and values are approximated linearly with problem-specific features.
- Concern: these representation and value-approximation choices reduce alignment with ICLR’s primary focus.
