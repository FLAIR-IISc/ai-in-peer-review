- Proposes Fitted Q-Iteration for visual servoing with a bilinear dynamics model, a channel-weighted Q-function, and a direct Bellman error optimization.  
- Strength: Systematic exploration of dynamics architectures (FC, conv, locally connected) and arguments favoring locally connected bilinear as a good trade-off.  
- Strength: Q-function that learns per-channel feature weights to attend to relevant visual information, outperforming an unweighted baseline.  
- Strength: Elegant Bellman error optimization exploiting scale invariance of the optimal policy to derive a simplified, potentially more stable update rule.  
- Weakness: Lacks empirical ablation comparing the three dynamics architectures to validate the claim that locally connected bilinear is best in practice.  
- Weakness: No comparison of the proposed Bellman optimization against traditional iterative FQI that fixes target Q-values, so benefits are unquantified.  
- Weakness: Experiments are small-scale and simulated, limiting assessment of robustness, scalability, and real-world applicability.  
- Weakness: Proposes a VGG modification but provides no experimental validation against the original architecture.  
- Overall: Technically solid and novel contributions, but impact is limited by insufficient empirical validation; more comprehensive experiments and ablations are needed.
