- Investigates visual servoing with a learned visual representation using action-conditional bilinear dynamics on pre-trained VGG features.
- Derives a policy by linearizing the learned one-step dynamics model.
- Proposes a multi-scale, multi-channel, and locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.
- Introduces a weighted objective that incorporates long-term values to mitigate short-sightedness of the one-step model.
- Handles policy evaluation with a fitted-value approach.
- Paper is well-written, mathematically solid, and conceptually thorough.
- Key experimental contribution: demonstrates benefits of the value-weighted objective.
- Appears to be the first to outline a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm.
- Empirically shows that pre-trained visual features improve generalization.
- Recommends acceptance based on overall contribution and usefulness to robotics researchers.
- Limitation: limited contribution to representation learning—relies on pre-trained VGG and does not learn features end-to-end.
- Focus is disproportionately on control rather than representation, reducing alignment with ICLR’s central themes.
- Policy representation is fixed and the value function is approximated linearly with problem-specific features, further limiting representation novelty.
