- Paper proposes a Q-iteration-based visual servoing method with three contributions: a bilinear dynamics model, a Q-function that weights visual feature channels, and a refined Bellman-error optimization.  
- Authors justify a locally connected bilinear model as a good trade-off between expressiveness and computational efficiency.  
- Weighting feature channels by learned importance is a meaningful improvement over uniform channel error and shows consistent experimental gains.  
- The proposed optimization leverages scale-invariance of the optimal policy to produce a more robust Fitted Q-Iteration procedure than standard target-fixing approaches.  
- The paper lacks an empirical comparison between the three candidate dynamics models (fully connected, convolutional, locally connected).  
- An ablation comparing the proposed FQI-style optimization to a standard iterative method that holds the target term constant is missing.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scale and uses simplistic simulations, which weakens empirical claims despite the lack of established benchmarks.  
- The modified VGG architecture is proposed but not experimentally validated against the standard VGG, leaving its benefits unproven.  
- Overall, the methods are technically sound and novel, but the paper needs more rigorous and comprehensive experimental validation.
