- Proposes a novel visual servoing method using Q-iteration with three main contributions: bilinear dynamics, a Q-function that weights feature channels, and an optimization for the Bellman error.
- Introduces a locally connected bilinear dynamics model argued to balance computational efficiency and expressiveness for predicting next-frame features.
- Presents a Q-function formulation that learns per-feature-channel weights, improving over unweighted baselines.
- Develops an Fitted Q-Iteration optimization exploiting Q-function scaling invariance to simplify Bellman error minimization.
- Lacks experimental comparison among the three dynamics architectures (fully connected, convolutional, locally connected) to justify the locally connected choice.
- Missing empirical comparison between the proposed FQI method and standard iterative schemes that hold the target Q-value fixed.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to small-scale, simulated toy problems, reducing the persuasiveness of results.
- Proposed modifications to the VGG network are not directly validated against the original VGG, leaving their benefit unclear.
- Overall: solid technical contributions and formulation, but claims need stronger empirical validation through broader and comparative experiments.
