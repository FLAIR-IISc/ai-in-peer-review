- Novel method: uses pre-trained convolutional spatial feature maps, a multi-scale bilinear one-step dynamics model, and trust-region fitted Q-iteration to learn a visual servoing policy that minimizes regularized weighted distance to predicted target features.
- Effective use of pre-trained features: clearly demonstrated and quantified in controlled simulation experiments.
- Strong empirical performance: significantly outperforms sensible baselines, including those using ground-truth bounding boxes.
- Principled learning framework: learns multi-scale feature weights and employs an efficient trust-region fitted Q-iteration, improving robustness to distractors.
- Sample efficiency: achieves notable sample efficiency attributed to the model-based one-step dynamics and an effective Q-function approximator.
- Community contribution: release of an open-source virtual city benchmark for visual servoing tasks.
- Suggestion — harder benchmarks: evaluate in more complex visual conditions (more clutter, higher density of distractors, varied target appearance/motion, larger/diverse 3D car models).
- Suggestion — end-to-end learning: explore fine-tuning the convolutional network or end-to-end representation learning to improve generalization in challenging visuals.
- Suggestion — clearer implementation details: consolidate practical tricks/parameters into a dedicated section and provide an open-source implementation for reproducibility.
- Minor typos: two corrections on p.2 ("relative[ly]" and "be applied [to] directly learn").
- Overall conclusion: solid, interesting paper with responsive authors and confidence that suggested improvements can strengthen the final manuscript.
