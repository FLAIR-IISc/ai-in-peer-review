- Novel method combining pre-trained VGG features, a learned bilinear one-step dynamics model, and an FQI-based policy to solve visual servoing for target-following.  
- Learns channel- and scale-wise feature weights via a sample-efficient linear Q-function formulation to prioritize task-relevant information.  
- Servoing policy is implemented by greedily minimizing distance to a target feature representation.  
- Achieves extreme sample efficiency: learns a robust policy from only 20 trajectories, >100× more data-efficient than TRPO.  
- Methodologically sound and well-written; Q-function structure that matches the servoing policy is an elegant design choice.  
- Using fixed pre-trained features enables the reported sample efficiency but limits contribution to representation learning (not end-to-end).  
- Bilinear dynamics model is simple but expressive enough to capture local feature-space dynamics from small datasets.  
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation in a simulated quadcopter domain, including ablations across VGG layers.  
- Strong baselines: outperforms TRPO, classical IBVS with ORB, and tracker-based C-COT; robust to occlusions and distractors.  
- Main limitation/suggestion: discuss fine-tuning VGG features vs. fixed features and implications for bilinear dynamics stability.  
- Algorithmic novelty: two-step FQI update (solve scaling α and bias b before feature weights θ) appears crucial and needs more intuition/comparison to standard FQI/NFQ.  
- Real-world transfer concern: question whether the simple bilinear model and assumptions hold under noisy, more complex physical dynamics.  
- Policy representation question: comparison to a generic CNN taking VGG features performed poorly (Appendix); this result should be highlighted in the main text.
