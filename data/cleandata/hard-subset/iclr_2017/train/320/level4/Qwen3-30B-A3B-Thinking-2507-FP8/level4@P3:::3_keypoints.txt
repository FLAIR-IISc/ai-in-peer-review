- Problem: visual servoing overly relies on manually designed features and models that limit generalization.
- Proposed method: combines pre-trained deep features (VGG), predictive visual dynamics, and fitted Q-iteration to learn feature weights for servoing.
- Key innovation: a sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration algorithm that learns optimal feature weights for robust control.
- Empirical claim: effective visual servoing learned with ~20 training trajectories.
- Sample efficiency: >100× improvement over standard model-free deep RL methods.
- Performance: outperforms pixel-based methods, hand-designed keypoints, and standard RL baselines; supported by ablation studies and comparisons to TRPO and CEM.
- Decision: Accept.
- Writing and contribution: well-written, scientifically rigorous, and a strong contribution to visual servoing.
