- Proposes a visual servoing method combining pre-trained VGG features, a locally connected bilinear dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration for policy learning.
- Key claim: only 20 training trajectories suffice to learn effective visual servoing for complex car-following tasks.
- Shows substantial improvement over pixel- and hand-keypoint-based approaches and >100× better sample efficiency than standard model-free deep RL.
- Decision: Accept — significant, well-motivated, and rigorously evaluated contribution to visual servoing.
- Addresses the core review questions: problem relevance, literature grounding, and comprehensive experimental validation.
- Most compelling contribution is the dramatic sample efficiency demonstrated on a challenging task.
- Provides thorough empirical comparisons and robustness checks supporting the method’s advantages.
- Limitation: relies on pre-trained features rather than end-to-end learning, which may constrain generality.
- Suggestion: clarify the relationship between the bilinear dynamics model and the Q-value function approximation.
- Suggestion: analyze why VGG conv4_3 outperforms conv5_3 to give practical implementation guidance.
- Suggestion: include qualitative analysis of learned feature weights to reveal which visual elements drive servoing.
- Questions for authors: why conv5_3 lacks necessary spatial information, how the method generalizes beyond the synthetic benchmark, and sensitivity to neighborhood size in the bilinear model.
