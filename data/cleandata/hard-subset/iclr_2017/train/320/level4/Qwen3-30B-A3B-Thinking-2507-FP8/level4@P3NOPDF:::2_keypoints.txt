- Novel visual servoing approach using an action-conditional bilinear model on pre-trained VGG features.  
- Policy derived via linearization of dynamics with a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected bilinear variant.  
- Introduces a value-weighted objective that injects long-term value estimates into one-step dynamics prediction.  
- Uses fitted-Q iteration formalized as a trust-region algorithm—claimed as a first in literature.  
- Experiments show the value-weighted objective substantially improves performance over baselines.  
- Pre-trained visual features empirically improve generalization across robotic manipulation tasks.  
- Technical contributions are substantial: bilinear model extension, value-weighting, and TR-FQI.  
- Empirical results convincingly outperform alternatives, especially for tasks needing precise visual alignment.  
- Paper is well-structured, mathematically rigorous, and thoroughly evaluated.  
- Major critique: limited contribution to representation learning—visual features are fixed, not learned end-to-end.  
- Value approximation relies on problem-specific linear features instead of jointly learning visual-control representations.  
- Misalignment with ICLR: representation treated as an external component rather than advanced or analyzed deeply.  
- Recommendation: accept for strong control/robotics contributions, with caveat that representation advances are modest; better fit for robotics/control venues.
