- Integrates pre-trained VGG features, a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and RL for visual servoing (target following).
- Introduces a sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration algorithm that automatically selects task-relevant visual features.
- Demonstrates learning effective visual servoing with minimal data (just 20 trajectory samples in the car-following benchmark).
- Shows more than two orders of magnitude better sample efficiency than standard model-free deep RL.
- Outperforms baselines including end-to-end CNN policies, image-pixel methods, hand-crafted keypoints, and tracker-based methods.
- The bilinear dynamics formulation and fitted Q-iteration offer a sound theoretical contribution to the field.
- Comprehensive experiments and ablation studies validate the necessity of feature weighting and the use of pre-trained features.
- Evidence of generalization to novel cars and particularly compelling sample-efficiency results (Figures 5 and 6).
- Recommendation: accept, based on scientific rigor and meaningful practical advance in data-efficient robot learning.
- Limitation: paper should discuss failure cases and scenarios where pre-trained features may fail.
- Limitation: unclear how the method scales to more complex visual servoing tasks beyond the car-following benchmark.
- Suggestion: include computational/runtime comparisons against tracker-based baselines on real hardware.
- Noteworthy that the trust-region fitted Q-iteration is a significant RL contribution despite not pursuing end-to-end representation learning.
- Question: clarify how spatial locality is enforced in the bilinear model and its computational cost versus fully connected models.
- Question: explain why deeper VGG conv5_3 features perform worse for servoing—possible loss of spatial information?
- Question: provide more quantitative comparisons of improvement in both sample efficiency and final performance metrics versus pixels/keypoints.
