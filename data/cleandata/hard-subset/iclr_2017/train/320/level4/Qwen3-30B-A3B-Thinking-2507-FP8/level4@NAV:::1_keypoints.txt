- Introduces a bilinear dynamics model that predicts next-frame features from action and current frame.  
- Proposes a Q-function that weights feature channels to capture object importance.  
- Presents an elegant optimization for Bellman error leveraging scaling invariance of Q-function parameters.  
- Thorough evaluation of action-to-frame representations; locally connected bilinear model balances efficiency and expressiveness.  
- Channel-weighting significantly outperforms uniform error treatment across channels, supported by experiments.  
- Bellman-error solution is robust and enables a more elegant FQI implementation than conventional fixes.  
- Theory would be stronger with experimental comparisons of fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models.  
- The distinction between FQI and an iterative approach fixing the second term in Eq. 5 needs empirical validation.  
- Results are limited to small-scale toy simulations; stronger validation on larger or domain-specific benchmarks is needed.  
- The claimed superiority of the modified VGG architecture requires experimental verification.  
- Overall: technically sound and novel formulation/methods, but enhanced experimental validation is essential.
