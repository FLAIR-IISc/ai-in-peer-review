- Studies visual servoing using a learned visual representation.
- Proposes an action-conditional bilinear model of visual features (from pre-trained VGG) with policy derived by linearizing the dynamics.
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.
- Bilinear model predicts only one-step dynamics; addresses this by weighting objectives with long-term values under the current policy.
- Evaluates using fitted-value iteration and introduces a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm (claimed first outline).
- Experiments show the value-weighted objective improves performance.
- Empirically demonstrates pre-trained visual features aid generalization.
- Paper is well-written, mathematically rigorous, and conceptually comprehensive; recommended for robotics researchers.
- Criticism: limited contribution to representation learning—does not learn representations end-to-end.
- Criticism: disproportionate focus on control over representation learning.
- Criticism: policy representation is fixed and values are approximated linearly with problem-specific features, reducing alignment with ICLR’s typical focus.
