- Paper proposes a novel Q-iteration approach for visual servoing with three contributions: a bilinear dynamics model, a channel-weighted Q-function, and a Bellman-error optimization using parameter-scaling invariance.

- Bilinear dynamics model is a strong, well-motivated choice favoring locally connected bilinear layers for expressiveness and efficiency, but lacks comparative/ablation experiments versus FC or convolutional variants.

- Channel-weighted Q-function is a compelling idea that adaptively emphasizes salient feature channels and empirically outperforms unweighted baselines.

- The Bellman-error optimization leveraging parameter scaling invariance yields a cleaner FQI formulation and potentially more stable learning, but is not compared to standard iterative FQI methods.

- Experimental evaluation is weak: confined to small-scale simulated environments and missing real-world validation or comparisons to established visual-servoing benchmarks.

- The paper modifies VGG for feature extraction but provides no direct experimental comparison to standard VGG to justify the change.

- Overall assessment: strong technical novelty and well-motivated contributions, but insufficient empirical validation undermines the claimed effectiveness and practical impact.

- Recommended fixes: include dynamics-model variant comparisons/ablations, add real-world or more complex simulation results, and directly compare modified VGG to standard VGG features.
