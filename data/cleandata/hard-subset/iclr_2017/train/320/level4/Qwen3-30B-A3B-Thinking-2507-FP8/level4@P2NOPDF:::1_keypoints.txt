- Introduces three contributions: bilinear dynamics model for next-frame feature prediction, channel-weighted Q-function to prioritize features, and a Bellman-error optimization using parameter-scaling invariance.  
- Bilinear (locally connected) model is well-motivated as a trade-off between efficiency and expressiveness for feature transitions.  
- Channel-weighting mechanism significantly outperforms uniform error weighting, improving servoing accuracy.  
- Recognizes Q-function parameter scaling invariance, enabling a streamlined Fitted Q-Iteration that avoids iterative fixed-point updates.  
- Lacks quantitative comparisons between dynamics model variants (fully connected, convolutional, locally connected), weakening justification for the bilinear choice.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to small synthetic tasks and lacks benchmarks or real-world visual-servoing comparisons.  
- Modified VGG backbone is used without ablation or comparison to the standard architecture, so its benefits are unvalidated.  
- Technical ideas are novel and address meaningful gaps in visual servoing learning.  
- Experimental evidence is insufficient to conclusively demonstrate superiority over existing methods.  
- Recommendation: strengthen empirical validation by comparing all dynamics models, evaluating on standard/real-world benchmarks, and providing ablations for the modified VGG before acceptance.
