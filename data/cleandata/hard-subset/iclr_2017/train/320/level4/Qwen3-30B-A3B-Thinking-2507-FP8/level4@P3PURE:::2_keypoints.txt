- Paper studies visual servoing using a learned action-conditional bilinear model of visual features from a pre-trained VGG network.
- Authors derive a policy by linearizing the learned feature dynamics.
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.
- Bilinear model is one-step predictive, so paper proposes a weighted objective that incorporates long-term values under the current policy.
- Addresses evaluation via a fitted-value approach and introduces a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm.
- Experiments convincingly show the benefit of the value-weighted objective.
- Empirical use of pre-trained VGG features improves generalization in experiments.
- Paper is well-written, mathematically sound, and conceptually thorough.
- Recommended for its potential impact on robotics research.
- Contribution to representation learning is limited: it does not learn features end-to-end.
- Paper focuses more on the control problem than on representation learning, with a fixed policy representation.
- Value function is approximated linearly using problem-specific features, reducing alignment with ICLR’s representation focus.
