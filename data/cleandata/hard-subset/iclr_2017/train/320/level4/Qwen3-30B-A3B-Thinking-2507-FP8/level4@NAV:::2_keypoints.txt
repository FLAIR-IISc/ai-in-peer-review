- Paper studies visual servoing using a learned visual representation and derives policies via linearized dynamics.
- Introduces an action-conditional bilinear model of visual features extracted from a pre-trained VGG network.
- Proposes a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.
- Addresses the bilinear model’s one-step prediction limit by adding a weighted objective that incorporates long-term values under the current policy.
- Evaluates long-term objectives using a fitted-value approach.
- Manuscript is well-written, mathematically rigorous, and conceptually thorough.
- Experimental results substantiate the benefits of the value-weighted objective—identified as a key contribution.
- Appears to be the first formalization of a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm.
- Empirically, using pre-trained visual features improves generalization.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance for its value to the robotics research community.
- Contribution to the “representation” problem is limited: relies on pre-trained VGG features rather than end-to-end representation learning.
- Policy representation is fixed and value approximation uses linear, problem-specific features, making the work less aligned with ICLR’s typical focus.
