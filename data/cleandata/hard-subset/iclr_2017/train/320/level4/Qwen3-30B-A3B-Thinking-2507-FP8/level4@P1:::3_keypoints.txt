- Paper proposes combining ImageNet-pretrained VGG features, a bilinear feature dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration for sample-efficient visual servoing (target following).  
- Claims robustness to visual variation, viewpoint change, and occlusion, and requires only ~20 training trajectories.  
- Demonstrates improvements over pixel- and keypoint-based baselines and orders-of-magnitude better sample efficiency than model-free deep RL on a synthetic benchmark.  
- Recommendation: reject.  
- Main criticism: experimental validation is limited to a synthetic car-following environment with low visual complexity.  
- Environment lacks distractor objects, diverse lighting, complex occlusions, and varied car appearances, undermining robustness claims.  
- Test set uses only a few car models, so generalization to real-world variety is not demonstrated.  
- Paper does not explore end-to-end fine-tuning of the pretrained convnet, missing representation-learning insights relevant to ICLR.  
- Sample-efficiency claim is under-supported: evaluated only on a single synthetic benchmark without multi-environment or multi-task validation.  
- Presentation issues: implementation details are scattered and overwhelming; reproducibility would benefit from clearer organization and open-source code.  
- Need clearer discussion of limitations and explicit justification for choosing a bilinear dynamics model over alternatives when used with pretrained features.
