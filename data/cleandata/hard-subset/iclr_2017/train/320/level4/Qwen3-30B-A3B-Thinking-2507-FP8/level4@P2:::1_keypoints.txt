- Novel method for visual servoing combining learned visual features, a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and reinforcement learning.  
- Uses modified VGG (removed pooling + dilated convolutions) to preserve spatial information alongside a bilinear feature-prediction model.  
- Introduces a sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration that automatically learns optimal feature weights to focus on relevant objects and ignore distractors.  
- Achieves strong sample efficiency (only 20 training trajectories), claiming >100x improvement over standard model-free deep RL.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Addresses an important robotics problem by reducing reliance on manual feature engineering via an elegant deep learning + RL integration.  
- Technical approach is well-motivated with theoretical grounding for the bilinear dynamics and Q-function optimization.  
- Empirical results show clear improvements over classical image-based servoing and end-to-end deep RL baselines.  
- Demonstrates robustness to viewpoint changes and occlusions, generalization to unseen target cars, and real-time execution (16 Hz vs tracker baselines at 1 Hz).  
- Bilinear model offers a good trade-off between expressiveness and computational efficiency; scaling invariance of Q-function parameters enables a cleaner optimization.  
- Request more detailed ablation comparing fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models to quantify performance differences.  
- Ask for validation of the modified VGG against the original VGG to justify architecture changes.  
- Recommend strengthening claims of generalization to other visual servoing tasks (e.g., manipulation, navigation) with experiments or discussion.  
- Questions for authors: quantify performance contributions of dynamics model variants; evaluate using original VGG with max-pooling; elaborate on generalization challenges to other visual servoing tasks.
