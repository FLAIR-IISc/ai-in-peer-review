- Proposes visual servoing combining pre-trained (modified) VGG features, a bilinear dynamics model, and a sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration that learns feature weights.  
- Demonstrated on a synthetic car-following benchmark using only 20 training trajectories, claiming large improvements over pixel/keypoint baselines and model-free RL.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Major concern: No experimental evidence that the modified (dilated, no max-pool) VGG is necessary or better than standard VGG for this task.  
- Major concern: Evaluation is limited to a single synthetic car-following environment, so generality and real-world applicability are unclear.  
- Major concern: Paper doesn’t test whether classical visual servoing methods could match performance if combined with learned features.  
- Major concern: Sample-efficiency claims lack comparison to other sample-efficient RL approaches (e.g., model-based RL).  
- Recommendation: Add an ablation comparing modified VGG to standard VGG to justify architectural changes.  
- Recommendation: Expand experiments to additional tasks/environments to demonstrate generality.  
- Recommendation: Compare against other sample-efficient RL methods that could solve the problem without the proposed feature-weighting approach.  
- Recommendation: Clarify how the bilinear dynamics model and feature weighting interact and whether either component is redundant.  
- Question: Provide performance metrics of standard VGG (with max-pooling) vs. modified VGG on the task.  
- Question: How would a simpler baseline—standard VGG plus a weighted sum of feature errors without bilinear dynamics/FQI—perform?  
- Question: What specific limitations of the synthetic benchmark impede direct real-world application?  
- Question: How does the described downsampling across scales affect spatial information and why are multiple scales necessary?  
- Overall: Technically interesting approach with promise, but experimental validation is insufficient to support the paper’s claims.
