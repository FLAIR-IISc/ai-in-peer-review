- Proposes visual servoing using spatial feature maps from pre-trained CNNs, combining multi-scale bilinear one-step feature dynamics with an RL policy minimizing predicted feature distances.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance in simulation and clear gains over multiple strong baselines, including ones using ground-truth bounding boxes.  
- Principled learning of multi-scale feature weights via trust-region fitted Q-iteration effectively mitigates distractors.  
- Method shows notable sample efficiency due to model-based dynamics and Q-function approximator.  
- Open-source virtual city environment provided as a useful benchmarking asset.  
- Experimental realism is limited: current scenes lack diverse high-fidelity 3D models, richer distractors, and varied lighting/weather to convincingly test robustness.  
- Recommends evaluating end-to-end training (fine-tuning the CNN with the servoing policy) to assess representation learning benefits.  
- Reproducibility hampered by scattered and inconsistently detailed implementation notes; consolidate essential details into a concise "Implementation Details" subsection and link to well-documented code.  
- Suggests simplifying or omitting non-critical technical minutiae in the paper and relying on linked code for full implementation.  
- Minor typos need correction (examples given on p.2).  
- Overall: strong, well-executed contribution that would benefit from more complex visual tests, end-to-end ablations, and clearer reproducibility materials.
