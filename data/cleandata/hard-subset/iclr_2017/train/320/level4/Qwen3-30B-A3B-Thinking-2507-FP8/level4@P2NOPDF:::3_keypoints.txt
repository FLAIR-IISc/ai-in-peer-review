- Problem & approach: Uses pre-trained CNN spatial feature maps plus multi-scale bilinear one-step dynamics and RL to learn a visual-servoing (target-following) policy.  
- Objective: Policy minimizes a regularized weighted average of distances between observed and predicted feature maps under the dynamics model.  
- Empirical validation: Quantitative simulation results show efficacy of pre-trained deep features for visual servoing.  
- Performance: Strong gains over diverse baselines, including methods that use ground-truth bounding boxes.  
- Feature-weight learning: Proposes a principled method to learn multi-scale visual feature weights via trust-region fitted Q-iteration to reduce distractor interference.  
- Sample efficiency: High sample efficiency attributed to model-based one-step dynamics and Q-function approximation.  
- Benchmark contribution: Provides an open-source virtual city environment for visual-servoing evaluation.  
- Suggestion — benchmark realism: Experiments need greater visual complexity (clutter, varied distractors, more realistic 3D car models and traffic) to better test robustness.  
- Suggestion — representation learning: Evaluate end-to-end fine-tuning of the CNN to determine if learned representations improve generalization in harder visual conditions.  
- Suggestion — reproducibility: Consolidate implementation details (e.g., in a dedicated appendix) and provide a clear link to code for full reproducibility.  
- Minor issues: A couple of typos/phrasing errors noted on page 2 that should be fixed.  
- Overall assessment: Well-executed and interesting work with strong revisions; addressing the suggested improvements would further strengthen the contribution.
