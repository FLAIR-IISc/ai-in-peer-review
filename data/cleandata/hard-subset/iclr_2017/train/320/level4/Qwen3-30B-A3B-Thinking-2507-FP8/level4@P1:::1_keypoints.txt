- Proposes a framework combining pre-trained VGG features, a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration to learn feature weights for visual servoing.
- Claims effective policies learned from only 20 training trajectories, outperforming pixel- and hand-keypoint-based approaches and far more sample-efficient than standard model-free DRL.
- Reviewer recommends rejection: technical idea is interesting but experimental validation is insufficient.
- Primary weakness: experiments are limited to a single synthetic car-following benchmark with low diversity and no real-world validation.
- Lacks comparisons to relevant and more recent deep RL baselines for visual servoing.
- Ambiguity around the “20 training trajectories” claim—unclear whether this is total or per-target and no analysis of variability across samples.
- Inadequate validation of the modified VGG: no ablation comparing the modified network to original VGG or other feature extractors.
- Unable to determine whether improvements stem from the feature representation, the VGG modifications, or other factors.
- Claim that standard classification-trained deep features + bilinear model suffice is not well supported due to limited environment/task diversity.
- Suggested improvement: perform thorough ablation studies comparing original vs modified VGG and alternative feature methods.
- Suggested improvement: expand experiments across diverse synthetic and real-world environments (lighting, occlusion, viewing angles, object types).
- Suggested improvement: clarify the 20-trajectory setup and report performance statistics over multiple random seeds/training samples.
- Suggested improvement: compare against recent deep RL visual-servoing methods beyond generic model-free baselines.
- Questions for authors: what exactly are the 20 trajectories (same vs different cars) and how does performance scale with trajectory count?
- Questions for authors: how does the modified VGG compare to the original in feature quality for servoing, and could alternative extractors match results?
- Questions for authors: how are robustness claims (visual variation, angle changes, occlusions) validated in the synthetic setup and how would the method generalize to real scenarios?
