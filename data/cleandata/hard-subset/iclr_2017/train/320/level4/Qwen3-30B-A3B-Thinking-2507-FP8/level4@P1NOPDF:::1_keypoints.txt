- Introduces a Q-iteration framework for visual servoing with three contributions: a bilinear dynamics model, a channel-weighted Q-function, and a Bellman error optimization using parameter scaling.

- Bilinear dynamics model is well-motivated and offers a good trade-off between efficiency and expressiveness; authors favor locally connected models but do not provide empirical comparisons to fully connected or convolutional alternatives.

- Channel-weighting mechanism is a strong contribution: adaptive per-channel weights prioritize salient features and consistently outperform unweighted baselines.

- Bellman error optimization is elegant—parameter scaling preserves optimal policy and yields a robust FQI scheme—but the paper lacks a comparison to iterative methods that hold the second term constant.

- Experimental validation is weak: results are limited to small toy simulations without domain-specific benchmarks, weakening claims of practical impact.

- No rigorous comparisons to established visual servoing baselines are provided, leaving real-world efficacy uncertain.

- The modified VGG used for feature extraction is not experimentally validated; it’s unclear if it improves over standard VGG.

- Recommended improvements: ablation studies for dynamics model variants, a clear FQI vs. iterative optimization comparison, and controlled validation of the modified VGG.

- Overall: technically sound and conceptually novel with a well-justified channel-weighting strategy, but the experimental section needs strengthening to support broader claims.
