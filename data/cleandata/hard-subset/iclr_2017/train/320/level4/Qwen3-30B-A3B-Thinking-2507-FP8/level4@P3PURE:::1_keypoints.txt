- Introduces a bilinear dynamics model for predicting next-frame features from the current frame and action.  
- Proposes a Q-function formulation that learns channel-specific weights to prioritize important feature channels.  
- Presents an optimization method for the Bellman error that leverages the invariance of optimal policy to scaling Q-parameters for cleaner FQI.  
- Thorough exploration of action-frame representation interactions; locally connected bilinear model claimed to balance efficiency and expressiveness.  
- Channel-weighting strategy empirically outperforms uniform weighting; unweighted error degrades performance.  
- Optimization approach is well-motivated: scaling Q-parameters preserves the optimal policy, enabling a cleaner Fixed-Point Q-Iteration.  
- Missing experimental comparisons between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models to substantiate claimed trade-offs.  
- No direct comparison between the proposed FQI method and an iterative approach that fixes the second term in Eq.5, leaving practical advantages unclear.  
- Experiments are limited to small toy simulations and lack validation on real-world or domain-specific benchmarks.  
- Modified VGG architecture is not experimentally validated, so its benefits over the original VGG are unclear.  
- Overall: technically novel and well-formulated, but empirical evaluation is weak; recommend adding dynamics-model comparisons, validating the modified VGG, and expanding experiments beyond toy settings.
