- Proposes a novel visual servoing approach combining pre-trained visual features, a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and reinforcement learning for fast adaptation to new targets.  
- Shows standard deep features from VGG plus a bilinear predictive model can learn servoing robust to viewing angle, appearance changes, occlusions, and other visual variations.  
- Introduces a sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration algorithm that automatically selects the most relevant visual features.  
- Demonstrates effective visual servoing with only ~20 training trajectory samples.  
- Claims over two orders of magnitude improvement in sample efficiency versus standard model-free deep RL.  
- Uses pre-trained visual features to achieve good generalization across different targets.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically sound, and conceptually comprehensive.  
- Experiments provide compelling evidence of effectiveness and robustness under varied visual conditions.  
- Recommendation: accept, based on significant contribution to sample efficiency and strong technical motivation and evaluation.  
- Suggestion: expand discussion on limitations of using pre-trained features versus end-to-end learning, especially for targets with very different visual characteristics.  
- Suggestion: include more detailed analysis of computational complexity compared to alternative methods.  
- Questions for authors: expected performance in real-world environments different from the simulated city, and adaptability/challenges for extending the method to more complex manipulation tasks.
