

The paper introduces a novel visual servoing learning method using Q-iteration. Key contributions include:  
1. A bilinear dynamics model predicting next-frame features from action and current frame  
2. A Q-function formulation weighting feature channels to capture object importance  
3. An elegant optimization approach for the Bellman error to learn the Q-function  

Pros:  
+ The paper thoroughly evaluates connections between action (ut) and frame representation (yt) for predicting next-frame features (y_{t+1}). The advocacy for a locally connected bilinear model effectively balances computational efficiency and expressive capacity.  
+ Channel-weighting strategy significantly outperforms uniform error treatment across channels, validated experimentally by consistent unweighted performance degradation.  
+ The solution to the Bellman error problem is robust, leveraging the key insight that scaling Q-function parameters preserves the optimal policy. This enables a more elegant FQI implementation compared to conventional methods fixing (c_t + \gamma min_u Q_{t+1}).  

Cons:  
- Section 4's theoretical arguments would be strengthened by experimental comparisons of the three dynamics models (fully connected, convolutional, locally connected).  
- The distinction between FQI and an iterative approach fixing the second term in Eq. 5 requires empirical validation.  

Experimental results:  
- The results are limited by small-scale toy simulations; while domain-specific benchmark absence is acknowledged, stronger validation is needed.  
- The modified VGG architecture requires experimental verification to confirm superiority over the original version, as currently unsupported.  

Overall, the paper offers technically sound novelty in problem formulation and methodology. However, enhanced experimental validation addressing the above points is essential to substantiate the claims.
