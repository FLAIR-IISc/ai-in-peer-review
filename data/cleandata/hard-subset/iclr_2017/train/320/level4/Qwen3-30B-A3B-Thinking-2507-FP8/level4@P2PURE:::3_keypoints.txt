- Method: Combines pre-trained convnet spatial feature maps, bilinear one-step visual dynamics across scales, and RL to derive a servoing policy by minimizing a regularized weighted average distance to predicted features.  
- Validation: Quantitative simulation results demonstrate the utility of pre-trained deep features for visual servoing.  
- Performance: Shows significant gains over multiple strong baselines, including methods using ground-truth bounding boxes.  
- Feature weighting: Learns multi-scale visual feature weights via a trust-region fitted Q-iteration algorithm, effectively mitigating distractors.  
- Sample efficiency: Achieves notable sample efficiency attributed to the Q-function approximator and model-based one-step feature dynamics.  
- Release: Provides an open-source virtual city benchmark environment for visual servoing.  
- Benchmark improvement: Experiments would benefit from greater visual complexity (more clutter, distractors, diverse realistic 3D car models, varied appearance/motion).  
- Representation learning: Should evaluate end-to-end training / convnet fine-tuning to assess impact on generalization and representation learning benefits.  
- Reproducibility: Consolidate and reorganize implementation details in the paper and link to open-source code for full reproducibility.  
- Typos: Fix wording on p.2—remove "(ly)" in "learning is a relatively recent addition" and change "be applied to directly learn" → "be applied directly to learn".  
- Overall: Interesting and robust contribution with limitations in experimental scope, but authors appear capable of addressing suggested improvements.
