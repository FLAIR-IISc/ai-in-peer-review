- Proposes combining learned visual features, a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and reinforcement learning for fast adaptation to new visual servoing targets with minimal data.
- Shows that standard pre-trained deep features, used with a bilinear predictive model and sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration, yield robust servoing under visual variation, viewpoint changes, and occlusions.
- Key contribution: a weighted multiscale feature representation for servoing where weights are learned via a modified fitted Q-iteration to focus on relevant features and ignore distractors.
- Empirical claim: learns effective servoing with only 20 training trajectories, achieving over two orders of magnitude better sample efficiency than standard model-free deep RL.
- Theoretical justification for the bilinear dynamics model and the Q-function optimization is presented and considered compelling.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
- Strength: leverages pre-trained features to outperform pixel-based, hand-designed keypoint, and standard deep RL approaches.
- Requested improvement 1: provide a thorough experimental comparison of fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected bilinear dynamics models.
- Requested improvement 2: experimentally validate the VGG modifications (removed max-pooling, dilated convolutions) against the original VGG architecture.
- Requested improvement 3: add ablation studies demonstrating the individual contributions of feature weights, bilinear dynamics, and Q-iteration.
- Clarification question 1: justify choosing the locally connected bilinear model over the fully connected variant, especially regarding computational vs. performance trade-offs.
- Clarification question 2: report sensitivity to VGG modifications and whether the method works with standard VGG-16 without those changes.
