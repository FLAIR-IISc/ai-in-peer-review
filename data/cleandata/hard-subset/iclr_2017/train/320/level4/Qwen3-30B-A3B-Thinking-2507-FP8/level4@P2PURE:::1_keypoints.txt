- Proposes learning visual servoing via Q-iteration with three main contributions: a bilinear dynamics model, channel-specific Q-function weights, and an optimized Bellman-error learning method.  
- Argues for a locally connected bilinear dynamics model as a good tradeoff between computational efficiency and expressiveness.  
- Channel-weighting in the Q-function effectively captures feature importance and outperforms uniform error weighting in experiments.  
- Key optimization insight: scaling Q-function parameters does not change the optimal policy, enabling a cleaner FQI-style Bellman-error optimization.  
- Needs experimental comparisons among fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models to justify model choice.  
- The difference between the proposed FQI and an iterative approach that fixes the second term in Eq. 5 is insufficiently clarified and lacks empirical validation.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to small-scale toy simulations and does not convincingly support the claimed advantages.  
- The modified VGG network requires experimental validation; it is unclear if it outperforms the original VGG.  
- Overall: technically novel and well-motivated, but requires stronger experimental validation (broader benchmarks, ablations, and VGG comparisons).
