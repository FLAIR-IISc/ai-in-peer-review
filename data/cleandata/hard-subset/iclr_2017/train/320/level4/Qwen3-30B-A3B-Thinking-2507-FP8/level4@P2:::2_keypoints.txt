- Proposes a visual servoing approach that combines pre-trained visual features, a learned bilinear predictive dynamics model, and reinforcement learning.
- Leverages VGG features with a bilinear (and locally-connected) dynamics model to predict feature evolution and fitted Q-iteration to learn feature weights.
- Shows robustness to visual variations, changes in viewing angle, and occlusions.
- Extremely sample-efficient: requires only 20 trajectories for RL and reports >100× improvement over model-free deep RL.
- Paper is well-structured, mathematically rigorous, and conceptually thorough.
- Bilinear dynamics and its locally-connected variant provide an elegant solution for high-dimensional feature spaces.
- Fitted Q-iteration effectively solves the long-term optimization without complex multi-step planning.
- Experiments are well-designed and outperform pixel-based and hand-designed keypoint baselines.
- Main limitation: relies on pre-trained VGG features and does not explore end-to-end representation learning.
- Suggested improvement: add detailed discussion on limitations of pre-trained features versus end-to-end learning, especially for novel object appearances.
- Suggested improvement: provide deeper analysis of why certain VGG feature layers work better for visual servoing.
- Question 1: How would the method perform if the target object’s appearance (color, texture, shape) differed significantly from training examples, and can it be extended without extensive retraining?
- Question 2: How does the method scale to more complex environments with higher-dimensional state spaces and what are the computational requirements?
- Practical contribution: method enables quick adaptation to new targets, is valuable for real-world robotics, and the authors provide code and videos.
- Overall recommendation: accept, based on strong empirical results and significant practical sample-efficiency improvements.
