- Proposes a novel visual servoing method using an action-conditional bilinear model over VGG-extracted visual features to derive control via linearized dynamics.  
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model to increase representational capacity.  
- Develops a weighted objective that integrates long-term value estimates via fitted-value iteration to overcome one-step-ahead dynamics limitations.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically rigorous, and conceptually thorough.  
- Experiments convincingly demonstrate the advantage of the value-weighted objective, marking it as a key contribution.  
- Introduces the first trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm for this visual servoing context.  
- Empirically shows that pre-trained visual features improve generalization performance.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, highlighting strong methodological and practical contributions to robotics.  
- Major critique: contribution to representation learning is modest—representations are fixed and not learned end-to-end.  
- Disproportionate effort on control mechanics (bilinear dynamics, value weighting, policy derivation) versus representation learning.  
- Value approximation depends on problem-specific feature engineering rather than a unified, learnable representation.  
- Conclusion: solid robotics paper with rigorous experiments, but limited impact on core representation-learning questions—borderline fit for ICLR.
