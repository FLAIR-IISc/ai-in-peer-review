This paper introduces a new Q-iteration based method for visual servoing. The key contributions include:
1. A bilinear dynamics model used to predict subsequent frame (feature) states based on the chosen action and the current frame.
2. A Q-function formulation for servoing that learns channel-specific weights for feature importance.
3. A refined optimization strategy for minimizing Bellman error to facilitate Q-function learning.

Strengths:
+ The work thoroughly investigates various methods for relating action inputs (ut) and frame representations (yt) to predict future frame features (y_{t+1}). The authors convincingly justify their choice of a locally connected bilinear model, highlighting its balance between computational efficiency and representational power.
+ The concept of weighting individual feature channels based on their relevance to the task appears promising, and demonstrably outperforms a uniform weighting scheme in experiments.
+ Addressing the challenging problem of Bellman error optimization in Q-learning, this paper proposes a robust optimization method. The core insight – that scaling the Q-function parameters does not alter the optimal policy – allows for a more streamlined FQI implementation compared to conventional approaches that directly minimize (ct + \gamma minu Q_{t+1}).

Weaknesses:
- While Section 4 provides a reasonable justification for the design choices, additional experimental comparison of the fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models would be beneficial.
- Further clarification regarding the distinction between the proposed iterative approach and standard FQI, particularly concerning the fixed second term in Eq. 5, would strengthen the analysis.

Experimental Evaluation:
- The experimental results, while presented, are somewhat limited in scope and scale, relying on relatively simple simulations. However, it is important to acknowledge the current lack of established benchmarks in this research area.
- The claim regarding modifications to the VGG network requires further empirical validation. Currently, it remains unclear whether the proposed modifications yield performance improvements over the original VGG architecture.

Overall:
The paper presents a technically sound and novel contribution to the field, with a well-defined problem formulation. However, strengthening the experimental evaluation, as previously suggested, would significantly reinforce the paper’s conclusions and impact.
