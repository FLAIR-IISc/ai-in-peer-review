- Novel visual servoing method using pretrained CNN spatial feature maps, multi-scale bilinear one-step visual dynamics, and reinforcement learning to learn a servoing policy.
- Controlled simulation experiments show pretrained deep features outperform various baselines, including ground-truth bounding boxes.
- Introduces a principled method to learn multi-scale visual feature weights using trust-region fitted Q-iteration to mitigate distractors.
- Demonstrates strong sample efficiency attributed to the Q-function approximator and model-based one-step visual dynamics.
- Provides an open-source virtual city environment as a benchmark for visual servoing.
- Suggests enhancing benchmarking with more complex/diverse visual conditions: clutter, distractors, varied appearances, motions, and more realistic 3D car models.
- Recommends investigating end-to-end training and fine-tuning of the convolutional network to improve generalization and representation learning.
- Notes reproducibility concerns due to many practical implementation tricks; requests a summary of critical details and an open-source implementation link.
- Points out typographical corrections needed on page 2 for two quoted phrases.
- Concludes the paper is well-founded and promising, and the authors appear capable of addressing the suggested improvements.
