- Novel Q-iteration-based visual servoing using a bilinear dynamics model to predict next-frame features from current frame and action.
- Servoing formulation uses a Q-function to learn weights for different feature channels.
- Efficient and effective Bellman-error optimization method for learning the Q-function.
- Argues locally connected bilinear model balances computational efficiency and expressiveness better than fully connected or convolutional alternatives.
- Introduces weighted channel importance to capture object relevance, shown to outperform uniform error treatment.
- Optimization leverages scaling invariance of Q-function parameters to enable a more refined FQI approach.
- Missing comprehensive experimental comparisons among fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models.
- Lacks comparison between proposed FQI and an iterative method that fixes the second term in Equation 5.
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scale and relies on toy simulations, making results somewhat underwhelming.
- Modified VGG architecture requires experimental validation against the original VGG.
- Technical contributions and formulations are substantial but experimental support needs strengthening to convincingly demonstrate efficacy.
