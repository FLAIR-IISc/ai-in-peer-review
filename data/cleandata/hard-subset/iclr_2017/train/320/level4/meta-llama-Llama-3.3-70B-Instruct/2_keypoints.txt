- Explores visual servoing with an action-conditional bilinear model on pre-trained VGG features, deriving policy by linearizing dynamics.  
- Introduces a bilinear model variant with multi-scale, multi-channel, and local connectivity.  
- Proposes a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values to overcome one-step-ahead prediction limits, evaluated with a fitted-value approach.  
- Paper is well-structured, mathematically rigorous, and conceptually thorough.  
- Experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the value-weighted objective—a significant contribution.  
- Appears to be the first to outline a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm.  
- Shows empirically that using pre-trained visual features improves generalization.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance; work is valuable to robotics researchers.  
- Contribution to representation learning is limited, mainly demonstrating utility of pre-trained VGG rather than end-to-end learning.  
- Policy representation is fixed and values are linearly approximated with problem-specific features, which may not fully align with ICLR’s representation focus.
