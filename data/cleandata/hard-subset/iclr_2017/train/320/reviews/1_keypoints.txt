- Proposes a bilinear dynamics model to predict next-frame features from current frame and action.
- Formulates visual servoing with a Q-function that learns per-feature-channel weights.
- Introduces an optimization scheme for the Bellman error exploiting scale-invariance of Q-parameters to enable an elegant FQI approach.
- Thorough exploration of ways to connect action and frame representation, favoring a locally connected bilinear model for balance of compute and expressiveness.
- Locally connected bilinear model argued preferable to fully connected or convolutional alternatives.
- Per-channel weighting of features is more effective than treating channel errors equally; experiments show unweighted performance is worse.
- Presents a solid optimization idea by noting scaling Q-parameters doesn’t change the best policy, simplifying FQI.
- Reviewer requests more experimental comparison between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics.
- Reviewer asks for clarification/experiments comparing FQI to an iterative approach that holds the second term in Eq.5 fixed.
- Experimental evaluation is unsatisfying due to small-scale, toy simulations and lack of domain benchmarks.
- Modification of VGG needs experimental validation; unclear if modified VGG outperforms the original.
- Overall: technically novel and solid formulation, but requires stronger experiments to bolster claims.
