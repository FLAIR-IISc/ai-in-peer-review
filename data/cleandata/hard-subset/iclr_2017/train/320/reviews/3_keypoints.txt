- Proposal: use pre-trained convolutional spatial feature maps + bilinear one-step visual dynamics and RL to learn target-following visual servoing.
- Optimization: policy learned by minimizing a regularized weighted average of distances to features predicted by the visual dynamics model.
- Contribution: controlled simulation experiments quantifying the usefulness of pre-trained deep features for visual servoing.
- Contribution: clear performance gains over multiple sensible baselines, including methods using ground-truth bounding boxes.
- Contribution: principled learning of multi-scale visual feature weights via an efficient trust-region fitted Q-iteration to handle distractors.
- Contribution: good sample efficiency from the chosen Q-function approximator and model-based one-step feature dynamics.
- Contribution: release of an open-source virtual city environment for benchmarking visual servoing.
- Suggestion: evaluate on more complex, diverse, and realistic visual conditions (clutter, distractors, varied appearances/motion, more 3D car models).
- Suggestion: investigate end-to-end training/fine-tuning of the convnet to assess representation learning benefits and improve generalization.
- Suggestion: improve reproducibility by regrouping/simplifying implementation details and providing open-source code with a concise summary in the paper.
- Minor: fix typos quoted on p.2.
- Conclusion: paper is interesting and solid despite experimental limits, and authors can likely address suggested improvements.
