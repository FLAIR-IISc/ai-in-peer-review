- Investigates visual servoing using a learned visual representation.  
- Proposes an action-conditional bilinear model of visual features (from pre-trained VGG) with policy derived via linearized dynamics.  
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.  
- Bilinear model is one-step predictive; paper proposes a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values.  
- Evaluation uses a fitted-value approach.  
- Paper is well written, mathematically solid, and conceptually exhaustive.  
- Experiments demonstrate benefits of the value-weighted objective.  
- Introduces a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm (claimed to be first).  
- Shows that pre-trained visual features empirically aid generalization.  
- Recommends the paper as valuable to robotics researchers.  
- Critique: contribution to representation learning is limited—relies on pre-trained VGG and does not learn end-to-end.  
- Critique: focus is more on control than on representation learning; policy representation is fixed and values are approximated linearly with problem-specific features, making it less aligned with ICLR.
