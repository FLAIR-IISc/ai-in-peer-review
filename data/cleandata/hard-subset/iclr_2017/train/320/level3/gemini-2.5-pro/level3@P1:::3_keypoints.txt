- Method: perform visual servoing in VGG feature space using a learned bilinear one-step predictive model and FQI to weight feature channels/scales.  
- Contribution: combines pre-trained deep features, a learned visual-dynamics model, and sample-efficient RL; authors release an open-source simulation environment.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main reason 1: principled combination of representations, model-based control, and FQI-enabled Q-function formulation that makes learning feature weights efficient.  
- Main reason 2: strong empirical gains, including ~two-orders-of-magnitude improvement in sample efficiency over standard deep RL.  
- Strength: leverages pre-trained VGG to avoid needing massive visual training data.  
- Strength: bilinear dynamics model is simple, data-efficient, and linear in controls for efficient policy optimization.  
- Strength: FQI for feature weighting effectively isolates target from distractors and makes Bellman error minimization tractable.  
- Empirical: thorough comparisons to TRPO and IBVS baselines (ORB, C-COT); outperforms baselines even with depth/G.T. boxes.  
- Empirical: learns effective policies from ~20 trajectories and generalizes to novel car models.  
- Improvement (robustness): evaluate in more diverse/challenging visual conditions (clutter, lighting, weather, varied 3D models).  
- Improvement (representation): consider fine-tuning convolutional features end-to-end to explore trade-offs between sample efficiency and task-specific representation.  
- Improvement (reproducibility): consolidate experimental details and hyperparameters (single table) to aid replication.  
- Question 1: have you explored more expressive, nonlinear feature-dynamics models and their trade-offs with sample efficiency?  
- Question 2: how sensitive is FQI to hyperparameters (regularization ν and inner-loop iterations K)?  
- Question 3: does training the dynamics model on hand-coded policy data bias it; would broader/random data improve generality?
