- Proposes a visual servoing method combining pre-trained VGG features, a learned action-conditional bilinear dynamics model, and sample-efficient RL to learn target-following policies with little task-specific data.
- Formulates the servoing policy as minimizing a weighted objective over feature prediction errors and learns those feature weights via fitted Q-iteration (FQI).
- Introduces a multi-scale, locally-connected bilinear model to predict one-step feature evolution across feature levels.
- Demonstrates on a simulated quadcopter car-following task that the method substantially outperforms pixel- and keypoint-based baselines.
- Shows over two orders of magnitude better sample efficiency than model-free deep RL (e.g., TRPO), achieving strong performance with ~20 training trajectories.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Elegant synthesis of pre-trained visual representations, model-based control, and value-function learning; using FQI to learn weights is clever and pragmatic.
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical results convincingly support claims of improved performance and sample efficiency for robotics-relevant tasks.
- Strength: Using off-the-shelf VGG features provides useful invariances and generalization without end-to-end training.
- Strength: The locally-linear bilinear dynamics model is tractable in feature space and enables analytic control steps.
- Main methodological novelty: framing weight learning as an RL problem with a linear Q-function over prediction errors enables very sample-efficient FQI.
- Suggestion: Better position the work vis-à-vis representation learning and justify the decision not to fine-tune VGG.
- Suggestion: Add ablations (importance of multi-scale features, locally-connected vs fully-connected bilinear model) to clarify design choices.
- Suggestion: Discuss real-world applicability issues (sim-to-real gap, VGG latency, robustness to unmodeled dynamics).
- Question: How sensitive is overall performance to errors in the bilinear dynamics model and where would that modeling assumption fail?
- Question: Please clarify the intuition and benefits of the two-step theta update (scaling alpha then refit) within each FQI iteration.
- Question: Have you considered features from dense-prediction pretraining (e.g., segmentation) that may preserve spatial detail better than classification-trained VGG?
