- Novel, sample-efficient visual servoing method combining pre-trained VGG features, a learned dynamics model, and reinforcement learning.  
- Performs servoing in VGG feature space instead of raw pixels.  
- Proposes a multi-scale, locally-connected action-conditional bilinear model to predict one-step feature evolution.  
- Introduces a value-weighted objective with feature weights learned via a novel, efficient trust-region fitted Q-iteration (FQI) algorithm.  
- Embeds long-term value into a simple, greedy servoing policy by learning feature weightings.  
- Experiments on a simulated car-following task show large sample-efficiency improvements over model-free deep RL.  
- Demonstrates greater robustness to visual distractors than classical visual-servoing methods.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically sound, and conceptually thorough.  
- Main limitation: reliance on fixed, ImageNet-pretrained VGG features rather than end-to-end representation learning.  
- Hypothesis that deeper features underperform due to spatial invariance is plausible; a visualization/analysis would strengthen the claim.  
- Requests clarification on novelty of the proposed FQI relative to prior regularized fitted value-iteration work.  
- Asks about sensitivity to choice of pre-trained network and whether results generalize to architectures like ResNet.
