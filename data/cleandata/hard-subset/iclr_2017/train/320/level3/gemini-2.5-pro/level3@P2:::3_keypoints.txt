This paper proposes sample-efficient visual servoing by combining pre-trained VGG features, a learned multiscale bilinear dynamics model, and reinforcement learning.  
Policy operates as servoing in feature space, minimizing weighted Euclidean distance between predicted next-step and target features.  
Main technical contribution: a fitted Q-iteration (FQI) algorithm to learn feature- and scale-wise weights, automatically selecting relevant features and ignoring distractors.  
Strong empirical results in a simulated car-following task, showing large gains in final performance and over two orders of magnitude improvement in sample efficiency versus baselines.  
Authors provide an open-source simulation environment for benchmarking.  
Decision: Accept — solid, well-executed contribution to learning-based robotics and control.  
Novel and effective synthesis of pre-trained features, learned dynamics, and sample-efficient RL; Q-function formulation enabling efficient FQI is a noteworthy technical advance.  
Comprehensive experimental validation against IBVS with ORB and C-COT, method ablations, and model-free RL (TRPO); the proposed method outperforms all baselines.  
Particularly impressive sample efficiency: high-performing policy learned from roughly 20 trajectories.  
Approach leverages pre-trained perception to avoid learning from pixels and uses a tractable local predictive model in feature space.  
FQI effectively learns a task-specific cost metric (feature weights) that yields robust long-term behavior.  
Suggestion: evaluate generalization under more diverse visual conditions or on a physical robot to strengthen robustness claims.  
Suggestion: discuss or experiment with fine-tuning the feature extractor and the trade-offs with sample efficiency and generalization.  
Suggestion: consolidate experimental details and hyperparameters in the main text and perform a final proofreading pass.  
Question: How sensitive is the learned dynamics model and final policy to the data collection strategy; is the initial hand-coded policy sufficient or is iterative data collection needed?  
Question: Provide more intuition on the two-step FQI update and the role/necessity of the global scaling factor α for stability and convergence.  
Question: Report test-time computational breakdown (VGG forward pass, dynamics prediction, action selection) to identify bottlenecks and compare to the C-COT tracker throughput.
