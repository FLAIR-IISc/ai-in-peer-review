- Proposes a cohesive framework combining pre-trained visual features, a learned predictive dynamics model, and sample-efficient RL for visual servoing.  
- Uses a bilinear model to predict next-frame features and evaluates connectivity patterns, favoring a locally connected variant for expressiveness and efficiency.  
- Introduces a Q-function with per-feature-channel weights to identify task-relevant visual cues and a scale-invariant optimization enabling efficient Fitted Q-Iteration (FQI).  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Primary weakness: experimental validation is limited to a single, small-scale synthetic car-following simulation.  
- Claims of robustness and sample efficiency are undermined by lack of tests on diverse or standard robotics benchmarks.  
- Reported sample efficiency (learning from 20 trajectories) is notable but inconclusive given the task simplicity.  
- Key design choices lack empirical justification, e.g., no direct comparison of locally connected vs fully connected or convolutional bilinear models.  
- No comparison of the proposed FQI optimization to a standard iterative fixed-target Q-network approach.  
- No ablation study showing whether the modified VGG provides measurable benefit over the original VGG features.  
- Recommendation: expand experiments to diverse/standard benchmarks and perform rigorous ablation studies to substantiate claims.  
- Reviewer questions: provide empirical comparisons for (1) dynamics model variants, (2) FQI vs fixed-target approach, and (3) justification for the VGG modification.
