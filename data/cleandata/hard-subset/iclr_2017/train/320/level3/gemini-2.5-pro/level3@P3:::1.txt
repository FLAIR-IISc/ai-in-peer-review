### **Review**

#### **1. Summary of Contributions**

This paper presents a novel and sample-efficient method for learning visual servoing policies. The core idea is to combine the strengths of pre-trained deep visual features, a learned dynamics model, and reinforcement learning. The authors propose learning a bilinear dynamics model to predict the evolution of feature maps from a modified VGG network, conditioned on the robot's action. This predictive model is then used within an RL framework where a Q-function is formulated to learn per-channel weights for the feature-space error. This weighting allows the agent to automatically identify and focus on task-relevant visual features. A key technical contribution is an elegant Fitted Q-Iteration (FQI) algorithm that exploits the scale-invariance of the optimal policy with respect to the Q-function's parameters, enabling a more efficient optimization of the Bellman error. The authors demonstrate on a synthetic car-following task that their method is substantially more sample-efficient than standard model-free deep RL approaches and outperforms classical visual servoing methods.

#### **2. Decision and Key Reasons**

**Decision: Reject**

My recommendation is to reject this paper in its current form, though I believe the core ideas are promising. The decision is based on two primary reasons:

1.  **Insufficient Experimental Validation:** The paper's empirical evaluation is limited to a single, custom-built synthetic environment. While the results are positive, the lack of experiments on standard benchmarks or in a real-world setting makes it difficult to assess the true effectiveness and generality of the proposed method.
2.  **Unsupported Claims for Key Design Choices:** The paper makes several specific claims about the superiority of its architectural and algorithmic choices—such as the locally connected dynamics model and a modified VGG network—without providing the necessary ablation studies or direct experimental comparisons to justify them.

#### **3. Supporting Arguments for Decision**

The paper's technical formulation is its main strength. The idea of learning a dynamics model in the feature space of a pre-trained network is well-motivated for sample efficiency. The bilinear model, particularly the locally connected variant, is a reasonable choice for balancing expressiveness and computational cost. Furthermore, the Q-function formulation, which learns weights for different feature channels, is an intelligent way to perform automatic feature selection for the control task. The proposed FQI optimization, which cleverly solves for a scale and bias factor before the main update, is a novel and elegant contribution.

Despite these strengths, the experimental support for the claims is not yet at the level required for publication at ICLR.

*   **Limited Experimental Scope:** The evaluation is conducted exclusively on a synthetic quadcopter car-following task. While visually complex, this single domain is insufficient to demonstrate the general applicability of the framework. Without results on established robotics or RL benchmarks, or a real-robot demonstration, the claims of robustness and effectiveness remain limited to this specific setting.
*   **Lack of Comparative Analysis for Dynamics Model:** The paper argues that a locally connected bilinear model is preferable to fully connected or convolutional alternatives. This is a central claim about the dynamics model, but it is not substantiated with a direct experimental comparison. A study showing the performance and computational trade-offs between these variants is necessary to validate this design choice.
*   **Unvalidated Feature Extractor Modification:** The authors modify the VGG-16 network by removing pooling layers and adding dilated convolutions to preserve spatial information. While the motivation is clear, there is no experiment to show that this modified network outperforms the standard, off-the-shelf VGG network as a feature extractor for this task. It is unclear if this modification provides a significant benefit or is a necessary component of the system's success.
*   **FQI Optimization Needs Stronger Justification:** The proposed two-step FQI update (Eq. 5 and Eq. 6) is a key algorithmic contribution. However, its practical benefit over a more standard FQI implementation (e.g., using a fixed target network from the previous iteration) is not demonstrated. An ablation study comparing these two optimization schemes would be required to quantify the improvement offered by this novel step.

In summary, while the paper introduces several promising technical ideas, its conclusions are weakened by an evaluation that is too narrow and lacks the rigorous ablations needed to support its central claims.

#### **4. Additional Feedback for Improvement**

The paper has a strong technical core and could be a valuable contribution with a more thorough experimental section. The following suggestions are offered to help strengthen a future submission:

*   **Broaden the evaluation:** To demonstrate the generality of your approach, please consider evaluating it on additional tasks, preferably from standard robotic simulation benchmarks (e.g., PyBullet, MuJoCo) or through real-world robot experiments. This would significantly strengthen the paper's impact.
*   **Include crucial ablation studies:** To bolster your claims, please provide the following comparisons:
    1.  An empirical comparison of the **locally connected, fully connected, and convolutional bilinear dynamics models** to justify the choice of the locally connected version.
    2.  A performance comparison between your **modified VGG feature extractor and the original VGG network** to validate the architectural changes.
    3.  A comparison of your proposed **FQI optimization scheme against a standard FQI implementation** with a fixed target Q-function to highlight the benefits of your novel update rule.
*   **Strengthen baselines:** The comparison against TRPO is a good demonstration of sample efficiency. However, ensuring that the model-free baseline is highly optimized (e.g., through hyperparameter tuning) would make the claimed two-orders-of-magnitude improvement more convincing.

#### **5. Questions for Authors**

1.  Could you provide more intuition or, ideally, experimental results comparing the locally connected bilinear dynamics model to fully connected and convolutional alternatives? This would help solidify the claim about its superior balance of performance and efficiency.
2.  The proposed FQI update scheme first solves for a scaling factor `α` and bias `b` (Eq. 5). Could you clarify the performance difference between this method and a more standard approach where the target Q-function parameters from the previous iteration are held fixed without this initial scaling step?
3.  Was the original, unmodified VGG network tested as a feature extractor in your framework? A direct comparison would be valuable for understanding the impact of removing max-pooling and adding dilated convolutions.
