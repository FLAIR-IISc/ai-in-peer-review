- Novel method: visual servoing in pre-trained VGG feature space using a learned bilinear dynamics model and sample-efficient RL to minimize weighted feature distance to a target.  
- Key technical contribution: a two-step Fitted Q-Iteration (FQI) update that exploits scaling invariance of θ for a computationally efficient and stable Q-function update.  
- Architectural choice: a locally connected bilinear dynamics model proposed as a compromise between expressiveness and parameter efficiency.  
- Empirical claim: significant improvements in sample efficiency and final performance on a simulated car-following task compared to TRPO and classical visual servoing.  
- Decision: Reject, primarily due to insufficient experimental validation and concerns about generalizability.  
- Major weakness: Evaluation confined to a single, custom synthetic environment (CitySim3D), making results hard to contextualize and generalize.  
- Major weakness: Lack of ablation validating the VGG modification (removing max-pooling and adding dilated convolutions) versus standard VGG-16 features.  
- Major weakness: No empirical comparison of the chosen bilinear dynamics architecture against fully connected per-channel or convolutional alternatives.  
- Major weakness: Baseline comparisons are limited—TRPO is a sample-inefficient baseline and classical IBVS baselines were given ground-truth depth, creating uneven comparisons.  
- Strength: The FQI formulation and two-step update are technically elegant and likely contribute to stability and rapid convergence.  
- Suggestion: Make the connection between the policy in Eq.(3) and the Q-function explicit (policy as argmin_u Q(s,u)).  
- Suggestion: Further explore why deepest VGG features (conv5_3) perform worse, e.g., visualize learned feature weights to show down-weighting.  
- Positive: Authors released code and environment, improving reproducibility.  
- Questions for authors: provide empirical comparisons for bilinear vs alternative dynamics models; quantify impact of VGG modification; ablate the two-step FQI vs standard FQI update.
