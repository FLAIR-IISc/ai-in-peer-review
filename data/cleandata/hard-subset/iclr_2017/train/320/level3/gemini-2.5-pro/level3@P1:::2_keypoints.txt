- Proposes a sample-efficient visual servoing method combining pre-trained VGG features, an action-conditional bilinear dynamics model, and FQI-based RL.  
- Formulates a greedy servoing policy that minimizes weighted Euclidean distance in predicted feature space, with channel/scale weights learned via FQI.  
- Key insight: learning the servoing objective (weights) via FQI shapes a one-step cost to approximate long-term Q-values.  
- Bilinear dynamics model is simple, learnable from few samples, and linear in control, enabling an analytic one-step servo solution.  
- Empirical result: substantially better performance and over two orders-of-magnitude sample-efficiency vs. model-free TRPO on a quadcopter car-following task.  
- Outperforms classical IBVS and trackers by implicitly capturing target motion in the learned dynamics.  
- Shows generalization to unseen cars, supporting the use of general-purpose pre-trained features for transfer.  
- Decision: Accept — novel, effective synthesis of pre-trained representations, model-based control, and value learning.  
- Limitation: Limited contribution to representation learning since VGG is fixed; paper could explore fine-tuning or end-to-end features.  
- Limitation: Bilinear model’s scope may be limited for highly non-linear or non-rigid object dynamics; merits discussion.  
- Suggestion: Provide qualitative/visual analysis of learned weights to show which feature channels/scales are emphasized.  
- Question: Have you considered fine-tuning the VGG extractor to yield task-specific features despite higher data needs?  
- Question: How would the method generalize to entirely different object classes (e.g., pedestrians) without retraining the dynamics model?  
- Question: Please clarify the motivation or theoretical justification for the two-step FQI update (Eq. 5 and Eq. 6) versus standard FQI.
