- Proposes a sample-efficient visual servoing method combining pre-trained VGG features, a learned action-conditional bilinear dynamics model, and model-based RL.  
- Uses multi-scale VGG feature space and a locally-connected bilinear model to predict one-step feature dynamics.  
- Derives a greedy control policy by analytically minimizing predicted distance to a target feature representation.  
- Introduces a weighted objective over feature channels/scales, with weights learned via RL to prioritize task-relevant features.  
- Develops a fitted Q-iteration (FQI) variant that approximates the Q-function as a linear function of weighted predicted feature errors, making weight learning tractable.  
- Experiments on a simulated quadcopter car-following task show strong performance and high sample efficiency (≈20 trajectories).  
- Demonstrates superior final performance and sample efficiency compared to classical visual servoing and model-free deep RL (large improvement over TRPO).  
- Shows that using pre-trained features aids generalization to novel target objects.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Strength: intelligent decomposition avoids data-hungry end-to-end learning by leveraging pre-trained features and focusing learning on task-specific components.  
- Strength: bilinear dynamics model is well-motivated and enables an efficient analytical policy solution.  
- Key contribution: integrating RL to learn feature-channel weights addresses irrelevant/distracting features in high-dimensional spaces.  
- The linear Q-formulation over feature errors allows direct, sample-efficient FQI optimization of feature importance.  
- Paper introduces a novel FQI variant that improves stability and efficiency.  
- Caveat: limited contribution to representation learning since VGG features are fixed; fine-tuning could be explored but may harm sample efficiency.  
- Caveat: framing is control/MBRL-heavy and might fit robotics venues better; suggest analyzing learned weights to appeal to ICLR readers.  
- Question: why does the two-step FQI update (solve α,b then θ) offer more stability/effectiveness than a single-step optimization?  
- Question: does the observed superiority of conv4_3 over conv5_3 hold for modern architectures (e.g., ResNets) with different invariance properties?  
- Question: how sensitive is final performance to the quality/diversity of data collected by the hand-coded policy used to train the bilinear model?
