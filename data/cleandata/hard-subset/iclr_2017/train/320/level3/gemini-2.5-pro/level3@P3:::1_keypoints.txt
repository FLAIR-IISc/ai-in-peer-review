- Proposes a sample-efficient visual servoing method combining pre-trained visual features, a learned bilinear dynamics model, and reinforcement learning.  
- Learns a locally connected bilinear dynamics model to predict feature-map evolution from a modified VGG network conditioned on actions.  
- Uses a Q-function that learns per-channel weights to focus on task-relevant feature-space errors.  
- Introduces an Fitted Q-Iteration (FQI) algorithm exploiting scale-invariance for more efficient Bellman error optimization.  
- Demonstrates improved sample efficiency and performance over model-free RL and classical visual servoing on a synthetic car-following task.  
- Recommendation: Reject in current form despite promising ideas.  
- Primary rejection reason 1: Experimental validation is limited to a single custom synthetic environment, limiting claims of generality and robustness.  
- Primary rejection reason 2: Key design choices (locally connected dynamics, modified VGG, FQI update) lack ablation studies and direct empirical justification.  
- Strength: Technically well-motivated idea of learning dynamics in pre-trained feature space and automatic feature selection via Q-weighting.  
- Strength: Bilinear locally connected model balances expressiveness and computational cost; FQI two-step update is an elegant contribution.  
- Weakness: No empirical comparison between locally connected, fully connected, and convolutional bilinear dynamics variants.  
- Weakness: No experiment comparing the modified VGG feature extractor to the standard VGG to justify architectural changes.  
- Weakness: No ablation showing practical benefit of the proposed FQI scaling/bias step versus a standard fixed-target FQI.  
- Suggested improvements: Broaden evaluation to standard benchmarks or real robots, add the outlined ablations, and strengthen/tune model-free baselines for fair comparison.  
- Questions for authors: provide comparisons of dynamics model variants, clarify performance gain from the FQI scaling/bias step versus fixed-target FQI, and report results using the unmodified VGG as a feature extractor.
