- Paper combines pre-trained VGG features, a one-step predictive bilinear dynamics model in feature space, and fitted Q-iteration to learn feature weights for visual servoing.  
- Core contributions: servoing in VGG feature space with preserved spatial detail, a multiscale locally-connected bilinear predictive model, a linear-in-feature/control Q-function enabling analytic greedy policy, and a sample-efficient FQI procedure.  
- Empirical evaluation on a synthetic quadcopter–car-following benchmark shows robustness to viewpoint and occlusion and ~100x sample-efficiency gains over model-free RL.  
- Paper is well written, mathematically clear, and empirically convincing.  
- Actionable insight: deeper VGG features up to conv4_3 generalize best; conv5_3 is too spatially invariant for this task.  
- Decision: Reject due to limited novelty in representation learning and lack of end-to-end learning; main contributions are in control/systems integration.  
- Problem motivation and literature placement are strong, contrasting classical visual servoing and model-free RL and justifying use of pre-trained features.  
- The multiscale locally bilinear predictive model is technically appropriate and computationally efficient.  
- The linear Q-parameterization yielding a tractable FQI scheme is elegant and contributes to sample efficiency; intermediate scaling/bias fitting stabilizes learning.  
- Experiments use proper splits and thorough ablations across layers, baselines (pixels, keypoints, trackers, TRPO), and weight-learning methods.  
- Main results are limited to simulation; no real-robot validation or domain-randomization experiments to support sim-to-real transfer.  
- Learning does not adapt the visual representation, so contribution to representation learning (ICLR focus) is modest.  
- Suggest clarifying novelty of the FQI variant and positioning relative to existing stabilization techniques (target networks, double Q, regularization).  
- Recommend exploring end-to-end fine-tuning of visual layers or bilinear dynamics to strengthen representation-learning claims.  
- Recommend adding a real-robot experiment or domain randomization study to increase transfer credibility.  
- Suggested ablations/diagnostics: alternative backbones (ResNet), layer selection sensitivity, locally-connected vs fully-connected dynamics, camera/noise robustness, per-feature weight distributions, learning curves and seed variance.  
- Suggested comparisons: model-based RL baselines (Dyna-style planning, PILCO-like methods) in feature space.  
- Questions for authors include sensitivity to pre-trained features, importance and trade-offs of locally-connected structure, role of non-negativity constraints on weights, robustness to domain shift, extensibility to multi-step predictive models, scaling with data/trajectories, and stability details of FQI hyperparameters and tricks.  
- Overall: strong robotics/control paper with clever integration, but limited fit for ICLR due to fixed representation and simulation-only validation.
