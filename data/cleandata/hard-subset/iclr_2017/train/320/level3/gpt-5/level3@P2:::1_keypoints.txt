- Proposes learned visual servoing in pre-trained deep feature space (modified VGG-16) using multiscale features, a learned bilinear dynamics model, and a one-step greedy servoing policy.
- Learns per-channel, per-scale feature weights and control regularization via a sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration (FQI) scheme with a scaling/bias invariance to stabilize optimization.
- Motivates a locally connected bilinear dynamics model as a tractable trade-off capturing local spatial structure and action effects.
- Experiments on a synthetic quadcopter–car-following benchmark show conv4_3 features outperform pixels/conv5_3, FQI-learned weighting beats unweighted/TRPO, and the method is more sample-efficient than pixel-based model-free RL and classical IBVS baselines.
- Decision: Weak Reject.
- Primary concern 1: Empirical evaluation limited to one synthetic domain, lacking standard benchmarks or real-world validation.
- Primary concern 2: Key modeling/algorithmic choices (locally connected dynamics, modified VGG) are insufficiently ablated or compared.
- Scope/external validity: Robustness claims (viewpoint, appearance, occlusion) are demonstrated only in one simulator and one task, limiting generality.
- Dynamics model choice: Comparison between locally connected, convolutional (tied-weight), and fully connected bilinear models is incomplete and under-quantified.
- Feature backbone modification: No controlled ablation comparing modified VGG to original VGG or alternative backbones within the same pipeline.
- FQI design and optimization: The scaling/bias trick’s empirical benefit over standard target-network/fixed-target FQI is not quantified; sensitivity to hyperparameters and seed variance not reported.
- Sample-efficiency comparisons: Reported gains may be confounded by representation pretraining and separate dynamics training; comparisons do not control for representation differences.
- Cost function and baselines: RL cost uses privileged state shaping; baseline parity and additional modern baselines (learned Jacobians, model-based MPC) are missing for context.
- Ablation suggestion: Compare locally connected vs convolutional vs fully connected bilinear dynamics matched for parameter count; report prediction and closed-loop performance and compute/memory.
- Ablation suggestion: Compare modified VGG vs original VGG vs smaller backbones (AlexNet, ResNet) within the same downstream pipeline.
- Ablation suggestion: Compare per-channel weights vs per-layer weights vs unweighted.
- Ablation suggestion: Compare one-step greedy servoing vs short-horizon planning (2–5 steps) using the same model.
- Evaluation improvement: Add at least one real-world dataset or hardware demo or a standard visual control benchmark.
- Robustness testing: Quantify sensitivity to occlusions, distractors, viewpoint and lighting changes through controlled stress tests.
- Reporting: Provide learning curves, variance across seeds, and sensitivity analyses for S, K, ν, exploration noise, and number of RL trajectories.
- FQI ablation: Include a baseline using fixed-target FQI to isolate the effect of the scaling/bias optimization and discuss convergence/stability.
- Sample-efficiency fairness: Separate dynamics pretraining cost from RL trajectories and include model-based baselines that reuse the same dynamics model.
- Qualitative analyses: Visualize learned per-channel/scale weights and analyze failure cases (distractors, severe occlusions).
- Request runtime/practicality: Report end-to-end latency on CPU/GPU and scalability with channel/layer count; test lighter backbones like MobileNet.
- Question 1: Provide quantitative comparisons of locally connected, convolutional (tied weights), and fully connected bilinear dynamics for prediction and closed-loop performance under similar parameter budgets.
- Question 2: What is the empirical benefit of the α,b “policy-independent” parameter fit versus standard target-network or fixed-target fitted Q-iteration, including variance across seeds?
- Question 3: Does the modified VGG (dilations, reduced pooling) outperform original VGG in the same pipeline, and which layers/scales matter most after weight learning?
- Question 4: How does performance change under controlled occlusions, lighting changes, and backgrounds with lookalike distractors—can robustness be quantified?
- Question 5: How do results vary with (a) number of RL trajectories and (b) size of dynamics pretraining data—where do diminishing returns occur?
- Question 6: Have you tried multi-step planning or MPC with the bilinear model, and does it outperform the one-step greedy policy?
- Question 7: What is the runtime latency on CPU/GPU and how does it scale with channels/layers; can a lighter backbone preserve performance?
- Question 8: How sensitive are results to the pose-based cost; can training with image-only costs (photometric or feature distances) yield similar policies?
- Question 9: Can you add photometric IBVS with learned Jacobians or a model-based controller using your learned dynamics (e.g., iLQR in feature space) as baselines?
- Overall note: The formulation and FQI idea are elegant and the code/environment release is appreciated, but stronger ablations and broader evaluation are needed for impact.
