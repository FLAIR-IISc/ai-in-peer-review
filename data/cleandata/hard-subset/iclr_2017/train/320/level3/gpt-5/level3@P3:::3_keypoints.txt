- Problem & proposal: Servo target-following by optimizing in pre-trained multi-scale convolutional spatial feature maps using a bilinear one-step visual dynamics model and a weighted squared-distance control objective.
- Policy & learning: Instantiate policy as a weighted one-step objective and learn feature/channel/scale weights plus control regularization via a sample-efficient, trust-region–style fitted Q-iteration with a linear Q approximator.
- Key empirical contributions: New CitySim3D benchmark and code; controlled synthetic car-following experiments showing utility of pre-trained deep features and generalization to novel cars.
- Baselines & performance: Substantial gains over pixel-space servoing, hand-engineered keypoints, tracker-based IBVS (even with ground-truth depth), and end-to-end CNN/TRPO policies.
- Sample efficiency: Very few RL trajectories (~20) needed to learn weights, achieving >100× fewer samples than model-free deep RL baselines.
- Runtime: Model-based dynamics + linear Q approximator yields fast runtime (~16 Hz) versus ~1 Hz for tracker-based baseline.
- Representation findings: Mid-level VGG features (conv1_2–conv4_3) outperform pixels and deeper conv5_3 for servoing, retaining spatial detail and invariances.
- Policy coherence: Greedy action under the linear Q equals the weighted one-step objective, enabling efficient learning of feature importance and handling distractors.
- Generalization: Demonstrated transfer to novel car models with retained spatial specificity from mid-level features.
- Limitations: Evaluations are simulation-only with limited car models, controlled visual conditions, and a cost that uses privileged ground-truth car pose.
- Scope caveat: Claims about real-world robustness are limited and acknowledged by authors; simulator and code released to foster further work.
- Suggested broader evaluations: Test more diverse visual conditions (clutter, occlusion, weather), more 3D models, and report robustness breakdowns per feature scale.
- Planning ablation: Recommend exploring multi-step planning/short-horizon MPC with learned dynamics versus one-step greedy control.
- Model ablations: Report effects of locally vs fully connected bilinear models, neighborhood size, pyramid scales, regularization, and non-negativity constraints.
- Representation improvements: Try end-to-end fine-tuning or self-supervised auxiliary losses; compare learned weights to spatial attention masks and visualize emphasis patterns.
- RL/reproducibility suggestions: Clarify the “trust-region-like” FQI step, sensitivity to hyperparameters (S, K, dataset size), identifiability concerns, and consolidate implementation details in the main text.
- Reproducibility checklist: Ensure code/simulator links are active, versioned, and include scripts to reproduce figures.
- Minor fixes: Correct typos and bibliography formatting inconsistencies.
- Key questions for authors: Can training costs be vision-only (no privileged pose)? How sensitive is performance to neighborhood size, channels/scales, and dynamics capacity?
- Data-efficiency questions: How does performance change with fewer dynamics training examples or fewer than 20 RL trajectories, and what are relative contributions of one-step dynamics vs Q-weight learning?
- Feature generalization questions: Have other backbones (e.g., ResNet) or self-supervised features been tried, and do learned weights transfer across targets?
- Planning & real-world questions: Any experiments with multi-step rollouts/MPC, photo-realistic sims, or real UAVs; main practical obstacles (calibration, latency, photometric gaps)?
- Stability question: Does FQI reliably converge across random seeds, and do non-negativity constraints ever impair learning?
- Overall assessment: Novel, well-motivated approach with strong empirical gains and sample efficiency; recommended for acceptance.
