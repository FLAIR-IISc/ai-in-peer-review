- Tackles sample-efficient visual servoing for target following, aiming to maintain a desired camera-target configuration robust to distractors, viewpoint, lighting, and occlusion.
- Servos in a learned feature space using pre-trained VGG features (dilated to preserve spatial detail), an action-conditional one-step bilinear predictive model (including a locally-connected variant), and a greedy one-step policy minimizing predicted feature-to-goal distance with multi-scale per-channel weights.
- Learns weights with a sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration (linear Q-approximation) whose greedy action matches the servo objective.
- On a synthetic quadcopter–car benchmark, achieves effective policies with ~20 RL trajectories, outperforming pixel IBVS and hand-engineered keypoints and being >100× more sample-efficient than model-free deep RL baselines.
- Pre-trained deep features (conv1_2–conv4_3) generalize better to novel cars than pixels or conv5_3; FQI value-weighted objective improves over unweighted or TRPO-learned weights.
- Code, simulator, and videos are released.
- Decision: Reject.
- Key reason 1: Limited representation learning contribution because the method relies on fixed pre-trained features rather than end-to-end representation learning.
- Key reason 2: Evaluation confined to a synthetic domain and algorithmic novelty is incremental (tailored linear FQI) without theoretical guarantees or broad applicability shown.
- Paper clearly positions itself against IBVS/PBVS, model-free deep RL, and video prediction; approach is well-motivated.
- Empirical comparisons and ablations (feature layers, weighting methods, baselines) support claims within the synthetic benchmark.
- Training uses privileged ground-truth pose for reward/cost, reducing realism and clouding claims about sample efficiency in real settings.
- No real-robot experiments; generalization tested only across novel car meshes and initial states, not across environments or real-world domain shift.
- The proposed FQI variant is practical but lacks theoretical analysis (stability, convergence, approximation error) and its “trust-region FQI” novelty/positioning is unclear.
- Suggestion: Fine-tune feature extractor with auxiliary/self-supervised losses or RL signals to strengthen representation claims and test whether conv5_3 spatial specificity improves.
- Suggestion: Clarify or revise the “trust-region FQI” claim and position it relative to regularized FQI and NFQ.
- Suggestion: Replace privileged pose-based cost with image-derived surrogates or learned reward models and provide an experiment showing feasibility.
- Suggestion: Provide theoretical/local stability analysis of the greedy one-step controller under bilinear dynamics and discuss failure modes with biased predictions.
- Suggestion: Broaden baselines to include model-based RL in latent space (e.g., E2C-style) and Dyna-style methods for fair sample-efficiency comparisons.
- Suggestion: Report robustness to dynamics dataset size/quality, backbone choice, and domain shift; show if learned weights are sparse/interpretable across scales/channels.
- Suggestion: Detail hyperparameter search budgets, wall-clock compute for baselines, and parameter counts for fully vs locally connected models.
- Key questions for authors: clarify the novelty and formal relation of the FQI variant to prior methods; sensitivity to dynamics training data; transferability and sparsity of learned feature weights; feasibility of image-derived costs; performance with other backbones or fine-tuning; failure rates under heavy occlusion/distractors; and benefits of modeling uncertainty in the bilinear model.
- Overall: Well-written and empirically strong within its domain, but core novelty is control/engineering-focused rather than representation learning, and reliance on privileged costs plus synthetic-only validation limit suitability for ICLR.
