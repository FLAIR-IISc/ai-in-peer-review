- Paper proposes learning visual servoing in CNN feature-map space using pretrained spatial features, bilinear one-step predictive dynamics, and a weighted feature-matching servo policy learned via fitted Q-iteration (FQI).
- Uses dilated VGG feature maps, locally-connected or per-channel bilinear dynamics, and a linear Q-approximator whose greedy action equals the one-step weighted servo policy.
- On a synthetic quadcopter–car-following benchmark, method outperforms pixel-based servoing, ORB/tracker IBVS, and end-to-end TRPO in performance and sample efficiency.
- Mid-level VGG features (up to conv4_3) work best; conv5_3 is too invariant for the task.
- Demonstrates competitive computational speed relative to tracker-based IBVS and releases code plus simulator for reproducibility.
- Decision: Accept — motivated by a strong combination of pretrained features, lightweight dynamics, and tailored FQI yielding major sample-efficiency and performance gains.
- Strong empirical evidence with ablations: compares pixels vs. VGG layers, dynamics variants, weighted vs. unweighted controls, and multiple policy learning approaches.
- Sample-efficiency claim supported: FQI needs ~20 trajectories vs. >100× more for TRPO.
- Generalization: shows transfer to novel car appearances with mid-level features transferring best.
- Baselines: compares to IBVS (ORB, C-COT tracker), PBVS (with ground-truth pose), and end-to-end CNN policies, contextualizing performance gaps.
- Reproducibility: code, simulator, and many training/eval details provided.
- Suggested broader evaluation: test more diverse scenes, lighting/weather changes, occlusions, dynamic distractors, other target types, and photorealistic or real-world sim-to-real experiments.
- Representation suggestions: try light fine-tuning of the feature backbone with FQI or auxiliary dynamics losses and compare other backbones (e.g., ResNet) or lighter networks for speed.
- Analysis requests: analyze learned weight patterns (sparsity/interpretability), sensitivity to ν and γ, robustness to dynamics/action noise, and multi-step rollout degradation of the bilinear model.
- Planning comparison: include multi-step planning (e.g., MPC) over learned dynamics to compare with one-step lookahead policy.
- Clarity/reproducibility edits: consolidate implementation details in a concise “recipe” and fix minor typos/notation inconsistencies.
- Question on data efficiency: clarify numbers of unique sequences used to train dynamics vs. FQI, sensitivity to fewer dynamics samples, and failure modes.
- Question on goal specification: how method handles goal views from different instances, template crops, mid-trajectory re-identification, or disambiguating distractors.
- Dynamics questions: compare locally-connected vs. fully-connected bilinear dynamics for different feature layers and test constant-velocity or optical-flow baselines.
- FQI questions: sensitivity to regularization ν, non-negativity constraint on θ, whether negative weights help, and variance across random seeds with learning curves.
- Cost design question: cost uses ground-truth 3D pose—how to approximate in real settings, and sensitivity to bias in the cost signal.
- Runtime question: provide detailed runtime breakdown (feature extraction vs. dynamics vs. optimization), hardware used, and whether lighter backbones can reach real-time (>30 Hz).
- Generalization question: test training on one vehicle set and testing on out-of-distribution vehicles/environments and report how VGG layer choice affects OOD robustness.
