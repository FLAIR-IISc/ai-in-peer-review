- Proposes a target-following visual servoing framework operating on pre-trained convolutional spatial feature maps with a bilinear one-step visual dynamics model.  
- Learns a policy by minimizing a regularized, weighted distance between predicted features and a goal, with weights over multi-scale feature channels learned via fitted Q-iteration (FQI).  
- FQI-based weighting yields a principled way to emphasize task-relevant features and downweight distractors.  
- Controlled simulation experiments in a released virtual city benchmark show clear performance gains and much better sample efficiency versus strong baselines, including tracker-based IBVS and model-free deep RL.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Reason: Well-motivated approach, well-situated in literature, and empirical results convincingly support claims of sample efficiency and consistent improvements.  
- Problem tackled: Data-efficient, target-following visual servo policies that generalize across visual variation by combining pretrained features, predictive dynamics, and RL.  
- Motivation: Builds on strengths of pretrained CNN features and bilinear motion models and addresses feature/scale selection under distractors via a Q-function aligned with the servoing objective.  
- Results: Substantial cost reductions versus pixels/hand-crafted features, two orders of magnitude better sample efficiency than model-free RL, and supportive ablations across VGG layers and weighting.  
- Suggested improvements: Evaluate in more diverse/realistic conditions (clutter, stronger distractors, varied assets, domain shifts).  
- Suggested improvements: Explore end-to-end fine-tuning or lightweight adapters to measure representation learning and generalization benefits.  
- Suggested improvements: Improve reproducibility by regrouping/simplifying implementation details, clarifying hyperparameters, and providing open-source code with a reproducibility checklist.  
- Minor: Fix typos noted on p.2.  
- Questions: How sensitive is performance to backbone/layer choice and feature downsampling?  
- Questions: Can FQI weight-learning be interpreted as a trust-region/regularized update with stability guarantees or convergence diagnostics?  
- Questions: How robust is the one-step bilinear model to larger motions or dynamic backgrounds, and would limited multi-step rollout (MPC) help?  
- Questions: What is the failure rate under heavy occlusion/similar distractors and how do learned weights distribute across scales/channels in those cases?  
- Questions: Any preliminary results in real or photo-real domains (e.g., CARLA) to assess sim-to-real sensitivity?
