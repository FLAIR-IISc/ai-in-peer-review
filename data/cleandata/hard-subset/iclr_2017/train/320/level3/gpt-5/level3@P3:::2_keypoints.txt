- Tackles visual servoing by servoing in learned deep feature space using pre-trained VGG features and an action-conditional bilinear model for feature dynamics.
- Uses multiscale intermediate VGG-16 conv features (dilated to preserve resolution); mid-level layers (e.g., conv4_3) generalize better than pixels or deepest features.
- Proposes a parameter-efficient, locally-connected action-conditional bilinear dynamics model that is linear in controls, enabling closed-form action selection.
- Introduces a value-weighted servoing objective that learns feature and control weights by aligning one-step quadratic objectives with long-horizon returns.
- Defines an analytic Q-function linear in feature and action-weight parameters so the greedy policy equals the servo policy.
- Presents a trust-region–style fitted-Q iteration (rescale α and fit bias b before FQI) to improve stability and sample efficiency.
- Empirically evaluated on a synthetic quadrotor car-following task, achieving strong performance with ≈20 trajectories and outperforming IBVS with ORB, tracker baselines, and end-to-end RL in sample efficiency.
- Paper is well written, mathematically grounded, and well contextualized within visual servoing and RL literature.
- Decision: Weak Accept.
- Key reasons for acceptance: strong integration of representation, dynamics modeling, and fitted-value RL with compelling sample efficiency and robust behavior.
- Clear empirical evidence supports benefits of value-weighted objective and pre-trained features, with careful ablations and strong baselines.
- Problem addressed: data-efficient, robust visual servoing that adapts quickly to new targets by selecting task-relevant features and ignoring distractors.
- Approach placement: well-motivated relative to IBVS/PBVS, end-to-end RL, and video-prediction models; bilinear model and pre-trained features are justified.
- Results largely support claims: FQI outperforms unweighted/TRPO weights, mid-level features generalize better, method outperforms classical baselines and is far more sample-efficient than end-to-end RL.
- Caveat: evaluation is only in simulation; no real hardware transfer shown.
- Caveat: representation contribution limited because VGG encoder is fixed; no end-to-end learning.
- Caveat: linear Q-function trades stability and interpretability for narrower representational capacity, possibly limiting novelty.
- Suggest clarifying and formalizing the “trust-region FQI” connection to TRPO/CPI and providing stability analysis or bounds.
- Suggest exploring partial end-to-end fine-tuning (e.g., last VGG block or adapters) to improve performance while retaining sample efficiency.
- Recommend broader generalization tests (lighting, weather, stronger occlusions, moving distractors) and visualization of learned per-scale/channel weights.
- Recommend comparing to a short-horizon MPC baseline using the same one-step predictive model to justify the one-step policy choice.
- Ask for sensitivity analysis: performance vs. number of trajectories, discount γ, regularization ν, and dynamics locality (locally-connected vs fully connected).
- Request discussion of sim-to-real prospects, bilinear model assumptions under real camera motion, and embedded-hardware timing.
- Questions: is trust-region FQI theoretically connected to TRPO/CPI or heuristic; are α,b steps critical for stability?
- Questions: how critical are non-negativity constraints θ ≥ 0; what if negative weights are allowed?
- Questions: did authors try multi-step prediction or Dyna-style rollouts; sensitivity to model error and compounding error?
- Questions: why conv5 features degrade Q approximation; relate receptive field/stride to servo suitability.
- Questions: generalization to out-of-distribution targets (e.g., trucks), appearance/lighting shifts, and minimal training data to beat classical baselines.
- Questions: any real-world or photorealistic sim experiments, observed failure modes, and runtime trade-offs with lighter backbones?
- Questions: provide ablations for fully connected vs locally connected bilinear dynamics on semantic features.
- Questions: sensitivity to geometric cost design and termination thresholds; would learned cost shaping change learned weights?
