- Paper studies visual servoing using an action-conditional bilinear model on pre-trained VGG feature maps, a linearized servoing policy, and a multi-scale locally-connected variant to reduce parameters.  
- Authors optimize a weighted objective aligned with long-horizon values via a fitted-value approach and propose a trust-region fitted-Q iteration to stabilize value learning.  
- Experiments on a synthetic car-following benchmark show pre-trained features and value-weighted objectives improve robustness, sample efficiency, and generalization versus pixels/hand-crafted features.  
- Paper is well written, mathematically clean, and conceptually thorough.  
- Decision: Reject — main novelty is control/value learning with fixed features, offering limited contribution to representation learning and weak fit for a representation-learning venue.  
- Problem and motivation are clear and the approach is well grounded in related literature.  
- Claims are largely supported: bilinear model plus linearized control is sound and ablations support the value-weighted objective.  
- Empirical gains over IBVS/trackers and model-free TRPO are substantial in the simulator used.  
- Representation learning contribution is limited because features are fixed (VGG) and the value approximator is a linear, problem-specific φ.  
- The “trust-region FQI” is under-positioned relative to NFQ/LSPI and other regularized value methods and lacks established theoretical properties.  
- Evaluation is strong for robotics but narrow for representation learning: single synthetic domain, no end-to-end modern visual RL baselines under equal sample budgets, and limited representation analysis.  
- Suggested improvement: jointly fine-tune features and dynamics (e.g., auxiliary video-prediction + value loss) to strengthen representation-learning claims.  
- Suggested improvement: broaden baselines to contemporary model-based RL and end-to-end Q/value methods with comparable sample budgets.  
- Suggested improvement: add real-world tests or sim-to-real transfer and analyze robustness to occlusion, lighting, and target identity shifts.  
- Suggested improvement: clarify computational/memory costs and runtime trade-offs of the locally connected multi-scale model.  
- Suggested improvement: provide more ablations on multi-scale weighting and channel selection sensitivity.  
- Question: what exactly makes the fitted-Q variant a “trust-region” method and how does it relate to NFQ/LSPI and regularized Bellman residual minimization?  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to γ, weight regularization ν, and non-negativity constraints on w and λ?  
- Question: can VGG layers be fine-tuned without sacrificing sample efficiency; are there preliminary results?  
- Question: how do different VGG layers and the locally connected neighborhood size affect generalization and stability?  
- Question: does the value-weighted objective remain beneficial when planning beyond one step or under stochastic dynamics?
