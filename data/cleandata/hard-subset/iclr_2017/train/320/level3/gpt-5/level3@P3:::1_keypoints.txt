- Tackles visual servoing by learning dynamics and acting in CNN feature space rather than pixels or handcrafted keypoints.  
- Introduces an action-conditional bilinear dynamics model that predicts next-frame feature maps from current features and controls.  
- Proposes a locally connected bilinear dynamics architecture to balance expressiveness and compute while allowing exact linearization in control.  
- Formulates the Q-function as linear in per-channel, per-scale feature weights so the greedy action matches a weighted one-step lookahead servoing objective.  
- Presents an FQI optimization trick exploiting scale/bias policy-invariance to jointly optimize target and current Q scales/biases for more stable/efficient fitting.  
- Empirically shows sample-efficient, robust servoing on a synthetic car-following task, with benefits from per-channel weighting and certain VGG layers, and gains over TRPO and IBVS.  
- Decision: Reject—main concern is that experiments are too limited to support key architectural and algorithmic claims.  
- Key experimental issue: results rely on a single toy simulated task without standard benchmarks or real-robot validation.  
- Missing/incomplete ablations: no head-to-head comparisons of locally connected vs fully connected vs convolutional dynamics on semantic features.  
- Missing comparison: no empirical validation that the modified VGG outperforms unmodified VGG at matched resolutions.  
- Missing comparison: no ablation comparing the proposed FQI scale/bias step versus standard fixed-target FQI.  
- Positive supporting arguments: formulation is coherent, bilinear linearization enables efficient control, and per-channel weighting ablation convincingly helps.  
- Insufficient supporting evidence: claimed preference for locally connected dynamics is not demonstrated on semantic features due to omitted comparisons.  
- Insufficient supporting evidence: the benefits of the modified VGG architecture are asserted but not quantitatively validated.  
- Insufficient supporting evidence: the FQI variant's concrete advantages (convergence, stability, sample efficiency) are not shown against standard approaches.  
- Suggestion: include systematic dynamics architecture comparisons (FC, conv, locally connected) with parameter counts, training time, Jacobian costs, and servo FPS.  
- Suggestion: ablate FQI variants—hold target fixed, fit only bias, fit only scale, full α,b step—and compare Bellman error, policy performance, and seed variability.  
- Suggestion: provide a proof sketch and failure conditions for the scale-invariance of min_u Qθ,b(s,u) under α≥0 and b.  
- Suggestion: validate modified VGG choice by comparing prediction error and servo performance to original VGG at matched spatial resolution.  
- Suggestion: broaden evaluation with at least one standard simulated benchmark and ideally a small real-robot study, testing generalization, occlusions, and lighting shifts.  
- Suggestion: discuss how costs/rewards would be obtained on real systems and whether pose or image-based surrogate rewards suffice.  
- Suggestion: visualize learned per-channel weights across layers/scales and analyze correlations with semantics or objectness.  
- Suggestion: provide conditions/safeguards ensuring the Q-induced quadratic in u remains convex and control outputs are bounded.  
- Questions for authors: empirical comparison of FQI with α,b optimization vs standard fixed-target FQI (convergence, final performance, variance)?  
- Questions for authors: can scalable approximations (low-rank, separable kernels) enable fair comparisons to FC/conv bilinear dynamics on semantic features?  
- Questions for authors: does modified VGG consistently reduce prediction error or improve control relative to original VGG at the same downsampled resolution?  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to receptive field size nf, number of scales, discount γ, L2 penalty ν, and the non-negativity constraint on θ?  
- Questions for authors: would self-supervised temporal/tracking features work in this framework to reduce reliance on ImageNet pretraining?
