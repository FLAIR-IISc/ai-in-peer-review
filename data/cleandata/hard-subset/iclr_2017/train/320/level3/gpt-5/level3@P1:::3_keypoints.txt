- Paper proposes visual servoing in multi-scale pre-trained VGG feature space using a bilinear one-step dynamics model and FQI with linear per-channel/scale weights for sample-efficient target following.
- Method is analytic at execution, simple, and data-efficient, demonstrated in a CitySim3D car-following task with code and environment released.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: principled, simple integration of pre-trained features, lightweight predictive model, and tailored Q-approximator yields practical, sample-efficient visual servoing.
- Key reason: clear empirical gains and sample-efficiency over sensible baselines with careful ablations.
- Motivation: addresses limitations of hand-engineered features in IBVS and sample inefficiency of model-free deep RL by leveraging pre-trained invariances.
- Method novelty: weighted multi-scale feature prediction error with quadratic control cost and analytic greedy policy due to bilinear dynamics linearity in control.
- Q-function design: linearity in feature/control weights aligns FQI with servoing objective; non-negativity maintains interpretability and greedy consistency.
- Dynamics learning: multiscale locally connected bilinear predictor balances expressivity and data/compute efficiency and yields exact control Jacobians.
- Empirical—layer selection: conv1_2–conv4_3 strike best balance; conv5_3 loses spatial detail and underperforms.
- Empirical—sample efficiency: FQI with ~20 rollouts outperforms TRPO even with >100× data; CEM and unweighted variants underperform.
- Empirical—baselines: outperforms pixel and ORB IBVS, competitive with tracker-based IBVS while being faster; surpasses end-to-end model-free CNN policies given similar budgets.
- Empirical—generalization: performs well on seen and novel car models, benefitting from pre-trained features and learned weights.
- Clarity/reproducibility: algorithm, architectures, and hyperparameters are clearly specified and code/environment are released.
- Suggestion—broaden evaluation: test on more diverse visuals, lighting/weather, occlusions, and moving distractors; report robustness curves.
- Suggestion—real-world validation: add a small hardware demo to address sim-to-real concerns.
- Suggestion—representation learning: explore fine-tuning features and joint training of dynamics/weights to improve generalization.
- Suggestion—multi-step planning: compare greedy one-step servo to short-horizon MPC to quantify trade-offs.
- Suggestion—analyze weights: visualize per-channel/scale weights, sparsity, and how they suppress distractors.
- Suggestion—method details: consolidate hyperparameters, termination criteria, computational costs, and include confidence intervals/multiple seeds.
- Suggestion—clarify baselines: explicitly compare to IBVS with ground-truth boxes vs. tracker boxes and list assumptions.
- Questions—sensitivity: how sensitive is performance to discount factor, regularization ν, and non-negativity of weights; could negative weights help?
- Questions—model error: does compounding model error degrade behavior and would multi-step planning/MPC help?
- Questions—ablations: locally connected vs. fully connected bilinear dynamics trade-offs?
- Questions—data/exploration: how are exploration noise and collection schedules chosen and what if dynamics data is reduced (e.g., 10 trajectories)?
- Questions—failure modes: observed failures (occlusion, rapid acceleration, out-of-FOV) and their frequency?
- Questions—multiple targets: can the approach handle multiple candidate targets and ambiguous goal images?
- Questions—runtime and backbones: exact per-step timings on GPU/CPU and whether lightweight backbones (e.g., MobileNet) preserve performance?
- Questions—transfer: do learned weights transfer across targets/tasks or require per-target relearning; any evidence of few-shot adaptation?
- Questions—stability: comment on closed-loop stability under the learned one-step controller and any Lyapunov-like empirical indicators?
- Minor: fix typos and streamline implementation description; ensure code release includes exact model snapshots, layer choices, and reproduction scripts.
