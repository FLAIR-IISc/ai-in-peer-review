Summary and contributions
This paper studies visual servoing with learned visual representations and predictive dynamics. It proposes an action-conditional bilinear model over pre-trained VGG feature maps, derives a simple servoing policy via linearized (exact, given bilinearity) dynamics, and introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally connected variant to reduce parameters while preserving spatial structure. Although the model is one-step predictive, the authors optimize a weighted objective that aligns with long-horizon values, learned via a fitted-value approach. They further present a trust-region fitted-Q iteration variant to stabilize value learning. Experiments on a synthetic car-following benchmark show that value-weighted objectives and pre-trained features substantially improve robustness and sample efficiency, generalizing better than pixels or hand-crafted features. The paper is well written, mathematically clean, and conceptually thorough.

Decision: Reject
Reason: The core novelty lies in control and value learning with fixed pre-trained features, yielding limited contribution to representation learning; thus the fit to a representation-learning venue is weak.

Supporting arguments
- Problem and motivation are clear: reduce feature/dynamics engineering in visual servoing and enable data-efficient adaptation. The approach is well grounded in literature on visual servoing, video prediction, and fitted Q-iteration.
- Claims are largely supported: the bilinear model plus linearized control is sound; ablations convincingly show the benefit of the value-weighted objective; empirical gains over IBVS/trackers and model-free TRPO are substantial in this simulator.
- However, the representation is not learned end-to-end; it relies on fixed VGG features and a linear value approximator with problem-specific feature function φ. This diminishes contributions to representation learning.
- The “trust-region FQI” novelty is under-positioned versus prior fitted Q/NFQ/LSPI and regularized/constraint-based value methods; theoretical properties are not established.
- Evaluation is strong for robotics but narrow for representation learning: one domain, synthetic only, no end-to-end comparisons with modern visual RL under equal sample budgets, and limited analysis of learned representations.

Feedback to improve (helpful, not decisive)
- Jointly fine-tune features and dynamics (e.g., auxiliary video-prediction loss + value loss) to strengthen representation-learning contributions.
- Broaden baselines: compare to contemporary model-based RL with learned latent dynamics and to end-to-end Q/value methods with similar sample budgets.
- Add real-world tests or sim-to-real transfer; analyze robustness to occlusion, lighting, and target identity shifts.
- Clarify computational/memory costs of the locally connected model and runtime trade-offs across scales.
- Provide more ablations on multi-scale weights and channel selection sensitivity.

Questions for authors
- What exactly makes your fitted-Q variant a “trust-region” method? How does it relate to NFQ/LSPI and regularized Bellman residual minimization?
- How sensitive is performance to γ, weight regularization ν, and the non-negativity constraints on w, λ?
- Can you fine-tune VGG layers without sacrificing sample efficiency? Any preliminary results?
- How do different VGG layers and the locally connected neighborhood size affect generalization and stability?
- Does the value-weighted objective remain beneficial when planning beyond one step, or with stochastic dynamics?
