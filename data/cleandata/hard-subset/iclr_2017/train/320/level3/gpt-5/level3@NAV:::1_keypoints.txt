- Proposal: learned visual servoing in pretrained feature space with a bilinear dynamics model, per-feature-channel Q weights, and a scale-invariant FQI scheme.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main criticism 1: Empirical validation is too limited — toy simulation, few targets, no standard benchmarks or real-robot results.  
- Main criticism 2: Missing critical ablations/comparisons, especially between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics and against alternative Q updates.  
- Strength: Core ideas are technically solid and novel (feature-space bilinear model, per-channel Q-weighting, exploiting scale-invariance for FQI).  
- Limitation: Results on a bespoke environment with few trajectories limit external validity and robustness claims (noise, calibration, dynamics mismatch).  
- Claim that locally connected bilinear dynamics outperform alternatives is unsupported without controlled, matched-parameter comparisons.  
- The modified VGG backbone appears central to performance but lacks ablations against the original backbone and other architectures (e.g., ResNet).  
- The practical advantage of the Eq. 5 optimization insight over standard frozen-target approaches is not empirically demonstrated.  
- Requested ablation: compare FC vs conv vs locally connected bilinear dynamics with matched capacity, FLOPs, training stability, and wall-clock.  
- Requested FQI comparisons: classical FQI with frozen target and an iterative scheme holding the second term fixed; report convergence, ν sensitivity, final performance.  
- Ask to quantify per-channel weighting benefits with statistical tests and analyze which channels receive weight (interpretability).  
- Validate modified VGG by end-to-end comparisons to original/backbone alternatives and report effects on prediction accuracy and control.  
- Add stronger benchmarks and robustness tests (standard simulators, photorealistic or real robots, occlusion, lighting, dynamics shift) and include sample-complexity curves.  
- Clarify identifiability under scale-invariance: how degenerate θ is prevented (regularization) and whether b affects policy selection in practice.
