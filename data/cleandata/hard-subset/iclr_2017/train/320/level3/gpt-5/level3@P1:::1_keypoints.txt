- Paper combines pre-trained visual features, a learned bilinear action-conditioned predictive model, and sample-efficient RL to learn which feature channels/scales matter for visual servoing.  
- Core idea: learn a bilinear, action-conditioned multi-scale predictive model to enable one-step lookahead servoing in feature space.  
- Core idea: use an analytic servo policy that minimizes a weighted quadratic distance between predicted and target features with per-channel and per-scale weights and control penalties.  
- Core idea: learn those weights via a fitted Q-iteration with a Q-function linear in the weights, exploiting scaling/shift invariance to alternate fitting α, b and θ for faster optimization.  
- Architectural argument: advocate a locally connected bilinear dynamics model as a compromise between expressiveness and tractability, and use a modified multiscale VGG (reduced pooling + dilations) to preserve spatial detail.  
- Empirical claim: method achieves effective target following on a synthetic CitySim3D quadrotor-car benchmark, robust to distractors/occlusions and far more sample-efficient than model-free deep RL (20 vs hundreds/thousands of trajectories).  
- Baselines: method reportedly outperforms classical IBVS variants using ORB/tracker and a TRPO-trained CNN policy on the benchmark.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main rejection reason 1: experimental validation is limited to a single synthetic setup and lacks required ablations to support central claims (e.g., superiority of locally connected dynamics and benefit of modified VGG).  
- Main rejection reason 2: the FQI contribution is promising but lacks comparisons to closely related alternatives and sensitivity analyses, leaving unclear how much gain comes from dynamics, features, or the new FQI optimization.  
- Strength: problem is well-motivated and literature positioning is solid.  
- Strength: technical formulation is elegant—bilinear model gives exact Jacobian, convex quadratic action selection, and a linear Q-approximator; scale-invariance trick is well-justified for this approximator.  
- Evidence gaps: no systematic comparison of locally connected vs fully connected vs convolutional dynamics with matched semantic feature levels, parameter counts, and memory/runtime analyses.  
- Evidence gaps: no empirical comparison of modified VGG vs original VGG under identical training and weighting to justify architectural changes.  
- Evidence gaps: missing comparisons of the FQI variant to standard fitted Q-iteration (hold target Q fixed), LSPI/LSTD-Q, or pure policy-evaluation/regression baselines.  
- Generality concern: results confined to one synthetic domain with privileged termination criteria; no other simulated domains or real-robot validation to support broader claims.  
- Reproducibility: paper is clearly written with explicit equations/algorithms and code/simulator availability is a plus.  
- Recommended ablations: compare dynamics architectures (fully connected, convolutional, locally connected), vary neighborhood size and report params/training time/servo performance.  
- Recommended feature studies: compare conv1_2–conv5_3 levels and modified vs original VGG for same dynamics/weights; analyze which channels/scales receive large weights.  
- Recommended FQI analyses: compare to conventional target-network FQI, LSPI/LSTD-Q, Monte Carlo regression baselines, and sensitivity to ν, γ, and positivity constraints.  
- Broaden evaluation suggestions: add at least one more simulated domain (e.g., manipulation), a small real-robot test if feasible, and stronger classical baselines (photometric IBVS, optical-flow control, learned keypoints, learned-dynamics MPC).  
- Practical suggestions: report runtime/memory trade-offs across dynamics variants and feature extraction costs; consider alternatives to VGG for speed.  
- Key questions for authors: provide direct comparisons between dynamics variants, FQI variant vs standard FQI, modified vs original VGG benefits, sensitivity to regularization/positivity constraints and feature selection, dependence on privileged termination, and per-iteration sample/wall-clock efficiency with variance across seeds.
