- Proposes a novel visual-servoing approach combining learned visual features, learned predictive dynamics, and reinforcement learning.  
- Shows standard deep features (VGG) with a bilinear predictive model can yield robust visual servoing tolerant to visual variation, viewpoint changes, appearance shifts, and occlusions.  
- Paper is well written and mathematically solid with clear explanations of the method and components.  
- Experiments demonstrate the benefit of a value-weighted objective and significant gains over pixel-based and hand-designed keypoint baselines.  
- Use of pre-trained VGG features improves generalization.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection due to limited contribution to representation learning and misalignment with the conference’s focus on representation work.  
- Fixed policy representation is noted as a limitation that may restrict applicability to more complex tasks.  
- Suggests the paper should discuss limitations and future directions more thoroughly.  
- Recommends exploring advanced RL algorithms and extending to more complex tasks (robotic manipulation, navigation).  
- Requests clarification on extending the approach to complex tasks, computational efficiency/time complexity, and required computational resources.  
- Asks for more details on robustness evaluations across visual variations like lighting changes and occlusions.
