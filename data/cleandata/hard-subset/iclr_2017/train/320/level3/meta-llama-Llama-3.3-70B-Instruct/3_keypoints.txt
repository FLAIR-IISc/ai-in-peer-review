- Proposes a novel visual servoing approach combining learned visual features, learned predictive dynamics models, and reinforcement learning.  
- Demonstrates method on a complex synthetic car-following benchmark with substantial improvement over conventional methods and model-free deep RL.  
- Contributions include controlled experiments and clear empirical performance benefits.  
- Introduces principled learning of multi-scale visual feature weights.  
- Shows good sample efficiency compared to baselines.  
- Releases an open-source virtual city environment for benchmarking visual servoing.  
- Provides a detailed analysis including comparisons to other methods and an ablation study.  
- Paper clearly frames the problem of visual servoing and situates work in related literature.  
- Experimental results and ablations support the authors’ claims.  
- Reviewer recommendation: Accept.  
- Suggested improvements: evaluate on more complex/varied visual conditions to test robustness.  
- Suggested improvements: explore end-to-end training and representation learning to potentially boost performance.  
- Suggested improvements: improve reproducibility by simplifying implementation details and releasing code.  
- Clarification questions for authors: rationale for choosing a bilinear model for visual feature dynamics.  
- Clarification questions for authors: how the method handles partial occlusion or varying object appearance.  
- Clarification questions for authors: details on computational efficiency, time complexity, and required resources.
