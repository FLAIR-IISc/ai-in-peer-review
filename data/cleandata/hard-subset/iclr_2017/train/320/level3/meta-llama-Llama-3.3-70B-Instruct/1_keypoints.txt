- Proposes a novel visual servoing approach using Q-iteration that combines learned visual features, learned predictive dynamics models, and reinforcement learning.  
- Main contributions: bilinear dynamics model, Q-function formulation, and Bellman-error optimization.  
- Advocates a locally connected bilinear model to link actions and frame representations for next-frame feature prediction.  
- Technical novelty and problem formulation are solid and well-motivated.  
- Channel-weighting idea to capture object importance is effective.  
- Optimization scheme for solving the Bellman error is sound.  
- Major limitation: lacks experimental comparisons between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics.  
- Experimental results are small-scale and toy simulations, making validation unsatisfying despite domain benchmark scarcity.  
- Reviewer recommends stronger experiments, including comparisons between FQI and iterative approaches.  
- Reviewer requests validation of the modified VGG architecture.  
- Reviewer asks for more training details (number of iterations, batch size, learning rate) to aid reproducibility.  
- Overall recommendation: reject in current form due to insufficient experiments, but paper has potential with additional empirical work.  
- Specific author questions requested: more training details; plan to address limited experiments/scale; comparisons across dynamics architectures; validation strategy for modified VGG and Q-function.
