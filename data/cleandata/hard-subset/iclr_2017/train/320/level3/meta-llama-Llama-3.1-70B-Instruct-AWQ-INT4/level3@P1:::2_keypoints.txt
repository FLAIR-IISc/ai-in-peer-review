- Proposes a novel visual-servoing approach that learns visual representations and a predictive dynamics model to control actions from camera observations.  
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected bilinear model to predict visual features and a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values.  
- Proposes a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm for policy learning.  
- Experiments show the value-weighted objective improves performance and pre-trained visual features aid generalization.  
- Demonstrates effective learning with low data and outperforms baseline methods in the presented tasks.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically solid, and conceptually thorough.  
- Limitation: contribution to representation learning is limited because authors rely on pre-trained VGG features rather than end-to-end learning.  
- Limitation: emphasis is on control; the policy representation is fixed and value functions are linearly approximated with problem-specific features.  
- Concern: approach is less aligned with ICLR’s focus on representation learning and deep learning.  
- Recommendation: accept—valuable to robotics researchers and offers a novel visual-servoing method.  
- Suggested improvements: learn visual representation end-to-end and explore more flexible policy representations (e.g., deep neural networks).  
- Suggested extensions: apply the method to other tasks (robotic manipulation, navigation) to demonstrate generality.  
- Questions for authors: how will you extend to end-to-end representation learning, improve policy expressiveness, and apply the approach beyond visual servoing?
