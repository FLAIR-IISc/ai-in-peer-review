- Proposes a novel visual-servoing approach combining learned visual features, predictive dynamics models, and reinforcement learning.
- Uses pre-trained VGG deep features to improve robustness to visual variation, viewpoint changes, appearance changes, and occlusions.
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected bilinear model to predict visual feature dynamics.
- Proposes a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values for better control.
- Evaluates the method with a fitted-value/fitted-Q approach and demonstrates empirical benefits.
- Recommendation: accept, based on novelty and experimental validation.
- Paper is well-written, mathematically sound, and conceptually thorough.
- The bilinear predictive model is a significant technical contribution enabling efficient feature dynamics prediction.
- Introduces a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm claimed to be novel.
- Experiments show the value-weighted objective helps and that pre-trained features aid generalization vs. classical and end-to-end baselines.
- Concern: reliance on pre-trained VGG features and lack of end-to-end learning limits contribution to representation learning.
- Requests clarification on plans for end-to-end learning, expected benefits/challenges, and extensions to more complex tasks (manipulation, navigation).
- Asks for more insight into bilinear model design choices and comparisons to other predictive models.
- Minor suggestions: add visualizations of learned features/predicted dynamics, provide more implementation details for trust-region fitted-Q, and discuss limitations and future work.
