- Proposes a novel visual servoing approach combining learned visual features, learned predictive dynamics, and reinforcement learning.  
- Uses pre-trained VGG features modified to remove spatial invariance.  
- Introduces a bilinear model to predict feature dynamics.  
- Evaluated on a synthetic car-following benchmark.  
- Demonstrates substantial improvement over classical image-based visual servoing and model-free deep RL.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance due to well-motivated approach, thorough evaluation, and significant empirical gains.  
- Paper clearly addresses limitations of manual feature design and analytical dynamics models.  
- Method is well-placed in literature and design choices (pre-trained features, bilinear model) are justified.  
- Results are scientifically rigorous with detailed experimental setup, metrics, and comparisons.  
- Main limitation: limited contribution to representation learning since features are pre-trained and not learned end-to-end.  
- Secondary concern: emphasis is more on control than on representation learning; policy uses fixed representation and linear value approximation.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations and justify design choices while demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Suggested improvement: provide more implementation details of the bilinear model and the RL algorithm.  
- Suggested improvement: analyze robustness to visual variations (lighting changes, occlusions).  
- Questions for authors: bilinear model architecture/training details; handling unsuitable pre-trained features; robustness analyses; consideration of other features (optical flow, depth).
