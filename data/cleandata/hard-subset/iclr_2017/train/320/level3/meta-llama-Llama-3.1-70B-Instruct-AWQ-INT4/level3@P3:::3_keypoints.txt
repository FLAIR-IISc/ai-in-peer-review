- Proposes a novel visual servoing approach combining pre-trained convolutional spatial feature maps, a bilinear one-step visual dynamics model, and reinforcement learning.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a complex synthetic car-following benchmark with substantial improvements over multiple sensible baselines.  
- Outperforms methods using ground-truth bounding boxes, classical image-based visual servoing, and model-free deep RL.  
- Well-motivated method that builds on recent deep learning and RL advances.  
- Provides a clear, thorough explanation of components: pre-trained features, bilinear dynamics, and fitted Q-iteration policy learning.  
- Main contribution: shows usefulness of pre-trained deep features for robust visual servoing across visual variation, viewpoint changes, appearance shifts, and occlusions.  
- Secondary contribution: principled learning of multi-scale visual feature weights via an efficient trust-region fitted Q-iteration algorithm.  
- Achieves good sample efficiency using a Q-function approximator and model-based one-step feature dynamics (learns with ~20 training trajectories).  
- Experiments support claims with multiple comparisons and ablations demonstrating improved performance and sample efficiency.  
- Limitation: experiments are only in simulation and may not capture real-world visual servoing complexities.  
- Suggested extensions: evaluate in more complex, diverse, and realistic visual conditions (clutter, distractors, varied appearance, motion).  
- Suggested investigation: end-to-end training or fine-tuning of the convolutional network to assess representation learning and generalization.  
- Reproducibility recommendation: simplify/regroup implementation details and release concise open-source code.  
- Minor issues: fix typos (e.g., quoted on page 2).  
- Recommendation/decision: Accept.  
- Questions for authors: provide more simulation/task details, plan for more realistic conditions, and comment on end-to-end/fine-tuning possibilities.
