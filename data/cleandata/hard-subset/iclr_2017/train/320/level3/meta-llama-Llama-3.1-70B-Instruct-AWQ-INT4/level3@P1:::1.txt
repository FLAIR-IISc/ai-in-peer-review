**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

The paper proposes a novel approach to visual servoing by combining learned visual features, learned predictive dynamics models, and reinforcement learning. The authors introduce a bilinear dynamics model to predict next-frame features from the current frame and action, and formulate visual servoing with a Q-function that learns per-feature-channel weights. They also propose an optimization scheme for the Bellman error that exploits the scale-invariance of Q-parameters, enabling an elegant fitted Q-iteration (FQI) approach. The paper presents a thorough exploration of ways to connect action and frame representation, favoring a locally connected bilinear model for balance of compute and expressiveness.
