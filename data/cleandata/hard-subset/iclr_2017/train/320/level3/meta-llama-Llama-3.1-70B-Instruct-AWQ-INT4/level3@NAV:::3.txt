**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

This paper proposes a novel approach to visual servoing that combines learned visual features, learned predictive dynamics models, and reinforcement learning. The authors claim that their method can learn an effective visual servo using low amounts of data, enabling quick adaptation to new targets. They demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on a complex synthetic car following benchmark, achieving substantial improvement over conventional methods and state-of-the-art deep reinforcement learning algorithms.

The paper's contributions include:

1. A proposal to use pre-trained convolutional spatial feature maps and bilinear one-step visual dynamics with reinforcement learning to learn target-following visual servoing.
2. A method for learning the weights of visual features using fitted Q-iteration, which leads to a sample-efficient algorithm for learning the visual servo.
3. A demonstration of the effectiveness of the proposed approach on a complex synthetic car following benchmark, with significant improvements over multiple baselines.
4. The release of an open-source virtual city environment for benchmarking visual servoing.
