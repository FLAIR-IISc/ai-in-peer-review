**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

This paper proposes a novel approach to visual servoing, which involves learning predictive dynamics models and reinforcement learning to learn visual servoing mechanisms. The authors introduce a bilinear dynamics model to predict next-frame features from the current frame and action, and formulate visual servoing with a Q-function that learns per-feature-channel weights. They also present an optimization scheme for the Bellman error exploiting scale-invariance of Q-parameters, enabling an elegant fitted Q-iteration (FQI) approach. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on a complex synthetic car following benchmark, achieving substantial improvement over conventional approaches and model-free deep reinforcement learning algorithms.

**Supporting Arguments**

While the paper presents some novel ideas, there are several concerns that need to be addressed:

* The authors claim that their approach can learn an effective visual servo using low amounts of data, but the experimental evaluation does not provide sufficient evidence to support this claim.

* The optimization scheme for the Bellman error is not clearly explained, and the authors do not provide sufficient details on how the scale-invariance of Q-parameters is exploited.
