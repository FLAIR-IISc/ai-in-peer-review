- Proposes a novel visual servoing method combining pre-trained convolutional spatial feature maps, bilinear one-step visual dynamics, and RL to do target-following by predicting next-frame features given current image and action.  
- Uses the predicted features to minimize error between target and predicted features and demonstrates effectiveness in controlled simulations.  
- Recommends acceptance based on novelty/significance and rigorous evaluation.  
- Novelty: combines pre-trained spatial features, bilinear dynamics, and RL to overcome limits of hand-designed features and models.  
- Rigorous evaluation: thorough comparisons to multiple sensible baselines, including methods using ground-truth bounding boxes.  
- Demonstrates clear performance gains over baselines across experiments.  
- Shows the usefulness of pre-trained deep convolutional feature maps for visual servoing.  
- Introduces principled learning of multi-scale visual feature weights via an efficient trust-region fitted Q-iteration.  
- Claims good sample efficiency, important for real-world applicability.  
- Experimental limitation: evaluations are restricted to controlled simulation environments.  
- Reproducibility could be improved by regrouping/simplifying implementation details and adding a concise summary in the paper.  
- Suggests future work on end-to-end training or fine-tuning the convnet to test representation learning benefits and generalization.  
- Requests more details on computational efficiency and time complexity of the bilinear dynamics and trust-region fitted Q-iteration.  
- Asks how the authors will address evaluation in more complex, realistic visual conditions.  
- Asks for clarification of potential real-world visual servoing applications.
