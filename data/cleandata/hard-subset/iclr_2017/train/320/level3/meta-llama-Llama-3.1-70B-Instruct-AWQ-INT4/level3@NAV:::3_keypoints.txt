- Proposes a visual servoing method combining pre-trained convolutional spatial feature maps, bilinear one-step visual dynamics, and reinforcement learning.
- Claims sample-efficient learning of visual servoing via learned visual features, predictive dynamics, and RL, enabling quick adaptation to new targets.
- Demonstrates substantial improvement on a complex synthetic car-following benchmark over conventional methods and state-of-the-art deep RL.
- Releases an open-source virtual city environment for benchmarking visual servoing.

- Recommendation: accept.

- Tackles a well-defined, relevant robotics/computer vision problem (visual servoing).
- Approach is well-motivated and grounded in literature, integrating deep learning, RL, and visual servoing ideas.
- Evaluation is thorough and convincing, with comparisons to multiple baselines showing significant gains.

- Technical contributions are significant: novel use of pre-trained spatial features with bilinear dynamics and RL.
- Learning visual feature weights via fitted Q-iteration yields a sample-efficient algorithm for visual servoing.
- Presentation is clear and well-organized, with detailed analysis of strengths and limitations.

- Suggest evaluating under more complex, diverse, and realistic visual conditions (clutter, distractors, varied appearance/motion).
- Recommend investigating end-to-end training or fine-tuning the CNN to assess representation learning and generalization.
- Improve reproducibility by simplifying implementation details and providing concise open-source code/documentation.
- Fix typos and minor errors throughout the paper.

- How will the approach extend to more complex/dynamic environments (multiple targets, changing lighting)?
- Provide more details on computational efficiency: training/testing times and comparisons to state-of-the-art.
- How will the authors address potential overfitting when using pre-trained convolutional spatial feature maps?
