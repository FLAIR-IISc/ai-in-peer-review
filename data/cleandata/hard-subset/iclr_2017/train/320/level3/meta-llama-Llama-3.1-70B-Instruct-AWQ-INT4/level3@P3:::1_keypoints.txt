- Proposal: Novel visual servoing method combining learned visual features, bilinear predictive dynamics, and reinforcement learning.  
- Technical contribution: Use of a bilinear dynamics model to predict next-frame features from current frame and action.  
- Technical contribution: Q-function formulation that learns per-feature-channel weights for visual servoing.  
- Empirical claim: Method is robust to visual variation, view changes, appearance shifts, and occlusions.  
- Benchmarking: Evaluated on a synthetic car-following task and shows substantial improvement over conventional and model-free RL baselines.  
- Recommendation: Accept with reservations — approach is novel and well-formulated.  
- Major concern: Experimental evaluation is limited to small-scale/toy simulations, hindering assessment of generalizability.  
- Major concern: Modified VGG network lacks experimental validation and it’s unclear if it outperforms the original.  
- Major concern: Need comparisons between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models.  
- Clarification requested: The FQI approach and its relation to an iterative method that holds the second term in Eq. 5 fixed.  
- Reporting issue: More details needed on experimental setup and hyperparameter choices.  
- Suggested experiments: Validate VGG modification, compare dynamics/feature models, and evaluate on more realistic/challenging benchmarks.  
- Questions for authors: Provide hyperparameter details for FQI and the bilinear model, and rationale for chosen FQI iterations.  
- Questions for authors: Report computational efficiency and time complexity of the bilinear model and FQI algorithm.  
- Questions for authors: Describe plans to extend the approach to real-world robotic systems and more complex visual servoing tasks.
