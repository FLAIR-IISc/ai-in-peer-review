1. Proposes a novel visual servoing approach combining learned visual features, learned predictive dynamics, and reinforcement learning.  
2. Introduces a bilinear dynamics model to predict next-frame features from current frame and action.  
3. Formulates visual servoing with a Q-function that learns per-feature-channel weights.  
4. Presents a Bellman-error optimization scheme exploiting Q-parameter scale-invariance to enable fitted Q-iteration (FQI).  
5. Reviewer recommends rejection due to limited scope and unsatisfying experimental evaluation.  
6. Experiments are limited to a small-scale toy simulation and lack real-world validation.  
7. Paper lacks comparisons to state-of-the-art visual servoing methods and established benchmarks.  
8. Experimental comparison between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics is incomplete.  
9. Technical contributions (bilinear model and Bellman optimization) are novel and solid.  
10. Experiments indicate per-channel feature weighting outperforms equal-channel treatment.  
11. Modified VGG network requires experimental validation against the original VGG.  
12. Reviewer requests comparison of FQI to an iterative approach that holds the second term in Eq. 5 fixed.  
13. Suggests evaluating on domain benchmarks (e.g., KITTI) to demonstrate real-world effectiveness.  
14. Questions for authors: provide more comparisons of dynamics architectures, VGG performance/efficiency, and detailed explanation of the Bellman-error optimization exploiting scale-invariance.
