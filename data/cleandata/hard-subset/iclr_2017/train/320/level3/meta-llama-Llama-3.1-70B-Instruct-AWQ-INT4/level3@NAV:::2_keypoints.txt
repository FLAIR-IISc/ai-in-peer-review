- Proposes a novel visual servoing method using learned visual features and predictive dynamics with a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected bilinear model and a weighted objective incorporating long-term policy values.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Addresses a specific, well-defined visual servoing problem in robotics.  
- Approach is well-motivated and grounded in recent deep learning and reinforcement learning work.  
- Empirical results are thorough, showing significant improvement over pixel- or hand-designed keypoint–based baselines using a fitted-value evaluation.  
- Provides a clear introduction, comprehensive related work, and detailed analysis of results, noting strengths and weaknesses.  
- Suggests adding more implementation details on the bilinear model and the weighted objective.  
- Recommends analyzing computational complexity of the proposed method.  
- Requests more insight into hyperparameter choices and their effects on performance.  
- Asks for clarification on why VGG was chosen and its impact on performance.  
- Inquires about extending the method to more complex tasks such as object manipulation.  
- Seeks discussion of potential real-world robotics applications.
