- Proposed contributions: bilinear dynamics model predicting next-frame features from current frame and action, Q-function with per-feature-channel weights, and a scale-invariant Fitted Q-Iteration optimization scheme.
- Empirical claim: method significantly improves visual servoing on a synthetic car-following benchmark versus conventional and model-free deep RL baselines.
- Recommendation: reject the paper at this stage.
- Primary reason 1: lacks thorough experimental comparison to state-of-the-art alternatives (e.g., fully connected, convolutional, locally connected dynamics).
- Primary reason 2: experimental evaluation is insufficient—limited to small-scale/toy simulations without domain benchmarks or broader validation.
- Concern: claim of data efficiency is unsupported by the presented experiments.
- Concern: choice of a bilinear dynamics model is not well justified compared to alternatives.
- Concern: the optimization scheme for the Bellman error and exploitation of Q-parameter scale-invariance is not clearly explained.
- Concern: experimental results are not deeply analyzed and lack explanations for performance gains.
- Requested improvement: provide comprehensive comparisons with FC, CNN, and locally connected dynamics baselines.
- Requested improvement: expand experiments to domain benchmarks and larger-scale, more challenging simulations.
- Requested improvement: clearly motivate and explain the bilinear dynamics model and its advantages.
- Requested improvement: detail the Bellman-error optimization and precisely how scale-invariance is used.
- Requested improvement: include deeper analysis of results to explain why the method outperforms others.
- Questions for authors: explain bilinear model advantages, justify locally connected bilinear choice versus FC/CNN, provide optimization details for Bellman error and scale-invariance, describe plans for scaling experiments, and offer a more thorough result analysis.
