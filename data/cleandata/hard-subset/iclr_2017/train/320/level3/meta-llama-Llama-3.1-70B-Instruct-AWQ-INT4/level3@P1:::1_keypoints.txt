- Proposes a novel visual-servoing approach combining learned visual features, learned predictive dynamics, and reinforcement learning.  
- Introduces a bilinear dynamics model to predict next-frame features from current features and actions.  
- Formulates visual servoing with a Q-function that learns per-feature-channel weights.  
- Proposes an optimization scheme for the Bellman error exploiting Q-parameter scale-invariance, enabling an elegant FQI.  
- Explores action-frame representation connections and favors a locally connected bilinear model for compute–expressiveness tradeoff.
