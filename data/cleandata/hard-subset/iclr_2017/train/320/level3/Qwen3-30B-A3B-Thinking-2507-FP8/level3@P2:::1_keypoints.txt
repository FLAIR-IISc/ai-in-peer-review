- Proposed method: combines pre-trained VGG features, a learned bilinear dynamics model, and sample-efficient RL to learn weighted visual servoing; claims ~20 training trajectories and >100× sample-efficiency vs. model-free RL.  
- Decision: recommend rejection.  
- Strength: technically sound, well-motivated, and novel combination of learned features, predictive dynamics, and RL.  
- Strength: elegant Q-function approximation and FQI scheme exploiting scale-invariance of Q-parameters.  
- Strength: thorough ablations showing per-channel feature weighting improves performance over equal weighting.  
- Empirical weakness: experiments confined to a simple synthetic car-following benchmark with limited complexity.  
- Main claim weakness: “substantial improvement” and “> two orders of magnitude” sample-efficiency not convincingly demonstrated in realistic settings.  
- Missing comparisons to state-of-the-art visual servoing methods and real-robot evaluations to establish broader impact and generalization.  
- Need more ablations comparing fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected bilinear dynamics to justify preferring locally connected models.  
- Need experiments verifying whether the modified VGG (removed max-pooling, dilated convolutions) is necessary versus standard VGG features.  
- Request more analysis of computational efficiency versus alternatives (e.g., tracker-based methods).  
- Clarification requested: provide precise sample counts and whether sample-efficiency claims are based on absolute or relative performance.  
- Clarification requested: show experiments demonstrating modified VGG features are superior for servoing compared to standard VGG.  
- Clarification requested: explain preference for locally connected bilinear model and provide direct performance comparison with fully connected variant.  
- Clarification requested: provide learning curves showing FQI performance evolution across iterations to understand convergence.
