- Presents a method for visual servoing combining pre-trained visual features (VGG), a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and sample-efficient reinforcement learning to adapt quickly to new targets.  
- Core claim: effective visual servo learned with only 20 training trajectories, outperforming conventional approaches and >100x more sample-efficient than standard model-free deep RL.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Strong literature placement and motivation bridging visual servoing and RL communities.  
- Solid theoretical formulation; bilinear dynamics model and trust-region fitted Q-iteration are well-justified and novel in this context.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough with appropriate baselines (pixel-based, keypoint, RL) and tests on seen and novel targets.  
- Results consistently favor the proposed approach across feature representations and tasks.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically rigorous, and conceptually exhaustive.  
- Limitation: relies on pre-trained VGG features rather than learning representations end-to-end, limiting contribution to representation learning.  
- Suggestion: clarify boundary between control contribution and borrowed representation learning.  
- Suggestion: discuss failure modes, e.g., targets with appearance very different from ImageNet.  
- Suggestion: provide a more comprehensive computational-efficiency comparison (beyond reported 1Hz vs 16Hz execution speeds).  
- Suggestion: analyze why deeper VGG features (conv5_3) perform worse despite deeper layers generally helping.  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to choice of pre-trained network or layer, and would fine-tuning help?  
- Question: How would the sample-efficiency comparison change if baseline algorithms were optimized for this task or used domain-specific features?  
- Question: What is the expected performance if the feature representation were learned end-to-end with the dynamics model and policy — would sample-efficiency gains persist?
