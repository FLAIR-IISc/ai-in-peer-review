- Tackles visual servoing by combining pre-trained VGG features, a bilinear predictive visual dynamics model, and reinforcement learning.
- Introduces a multi-scale, locally-connected bilinear dynamics model for predicting visual feature changes under actions.
- Uses a value-weighted objective on pre-trained features to improve policy learning and feature weighting.
- Proposes a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm to learn feature weights that optimize long-term policy value.
- Demonstrates strong empirical results: effective learning with only ~20 training trajectories on a car-following benchmark.
- Claims robustness to visual variation, viewpoint changes, and occlusions, and good generalization to new targets with minimal retraining.
- Reports more than two orders of magnitude improvement in sample efficiency over standard model-free RL (e.g., TRPO).
- Strengths: clear problem formulation, solid mathematical foundation, and thorough experimental validation.
- Limitation: relies entirely on pre-trained VGG features; no end-to-end representation learning or contribution to representation learning.
- Suggests the paper should better justify not pursuing end-to-end learning and discuss integration with recent visual representation techniques.
- Recommends adding more detailed comparisons to recent work on visual representation learning to strengthen positioning.
- Overall recommendation: accept — practical, sample-efficient solution relevant to robotics communities.
