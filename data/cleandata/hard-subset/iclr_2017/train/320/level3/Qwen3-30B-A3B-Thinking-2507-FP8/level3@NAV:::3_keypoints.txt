- Presents a visual servoing method combining pre-trained deep features, bilinear visual dynamics, and RL for rapid adaptation to new targets with minimal data.  
- Operates in learned feature space rather than pixels or hand-designed keypoints, improving robustness and performance.  
- Key contribution: principled learning of multi-scale visual feature weights using a trust-region fitted Q-iteration algorithm.  
- Demonstrates robust handling of distractors and remarkable sample efficiency—effective learning from just 20 training trajectories.  
- Shows substantial improvements over multiple baselines, including methods using ground-truth bounding boxes.  
- Reports more than two orders of magnitude better sample efficiency compared to model-free deep RL.  
- Method is simple and computationally efficient: minimizes Euclidean distance between predicted and target features.  
- Paper is well-motivated, well-placed in literature, and experimentally thorough within the simulation environment.  
- Open-source release of the CitySim3D environment is a valuable community resource.  
- Recommendation: expand evaluation to more diverse visual conditions (clutter, varied object appearances, more 3D models).  
- Suggest investigating end-to-end fine-tuning of the convolutional network to assess representation learning benefits.  
- Suggest improving reproducibility by simplifying implementation details and providing a concise codebase summary; fix a minor typo on page 2.  
- Overall decision: Accept—significant, well-executed contribution that is already strong but could be improved with the above suggestions.
