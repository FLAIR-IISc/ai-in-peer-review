- Proposes a visual servoing method combining pre-trained VGG features, a bilinear dynamics model, and reinforcement learning for fast adaptation with minimal data.  
- Uses pre-trained VGG for representation and a locally connected bilinear model to predict feature changes from robot actions.  
- Main innovation: sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration that learns per-channel feature weights to identify task-relevant features.  
- Demonstrates effective servoing on a synthetic car-following benchmark using only 20 training trajectories.  
- Reports substantial improvement over pixel-based and hand-designed keypoint methods and >100x sample efficiency vs. model-free deep RL.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection despite promising technical ideas.  
- Strength: technically well-motivated formulation and elegant weighted visual servoing with Q-learning.  
- Theoretical insight: scaling Q-parameters does not change the optimal policy, enabling efficient FQI implementation.  
- Major weakness: experimental evaluation is limited and does not sufficiently support the paper’s broad claims.  
- Benchmark is synthetic and narrow in scope, lacking real-world validation for practical quick-adaptation claims.  
- Insufficient comparison of the locally connected bilinear model against fully connected and convolutional alternatives.  
- No experimental validation that the modified VGG (removed pooling, added dilations) outperforms the original VGG on the task.  
- “Substantial improvement” claims are inadequately substantiated due to limited baselines and environments.  
- Sample-efficiency claim is based only on the synthetic benchmark, with unclear scaling to complex real-world visual variation.  
- Recommended improvements: more thorough comparisons and ablations of model architectures and VGG modifications.  
- Recommended improvements: extend experiments to real-world robotics or more visually diverse synthetic environments.  
- Recommended improvements: compare against recent model-based RL methods for sample efficiency.  
- Questions for authors: performance on diverse real-world tasks (manipulation, cluttered navigation)?  
- Questions for authors: empirical comparison of locally connected bilinear vs fully connected models in compute and task performance?  
- Questions for authors: how does the method handle partial occlusion and how much performance degrades?
