- Proposes a visual-servoing method combining pre-trained deep features, a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and reinforcement learning operating in feature space rather than pixels or hand-designed keypoints.  
- Key contributions: showing ImageNet-trained features work for visual servoing; introducing a bilinear dynamics model; a sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration (FQI) to learn feature weights; large performance gains with only 20 training trajectories.  
- Main empirical claim: substantial improvement over baselines (pixel-based, ORB/SIFT, end-to-end CNN, classical box-based servoing) and two-orders-of-magnitude sample efficiency vs. standard model-free RL.  
- Strong experimental validation on a synthetic car-following benchmark (CitySim3D) with comparisons to multiple sensible baselines.  
- Method demonstrates robustness to visual variation, viewpoint changes, occlusions, and generalization to novel cars from the same dataset.  
- Theoretical foundation is clear: bilinear dynamics + FQI provide a principled, sample-efficient learning framework.  
- Paper is well-written, well-structured, and situates itself appropriately in related work.  
- Decision: Accept — considered a strong, scientifically rigorous, and empirically validated contribution.  
- Suggest expanding experiments to more complex, diverse visual conditions and real-world data to assess applicability beyond the synthetic environment.  
- Recommend investigating representation learning / fine-tuning of convolutional features to potentially improve generalization and performance.  
- Ask for clearer, more concise implementation details and a step-by-step reproduction guide to improve reproducibility.  
- Minor fixes: correct typos (example on page 2).  
- Question: provide more details on how the bilinear dynamics model specifically handles occlusions.  
- Question: explain why TRPO requires ~100x more trajectories than FQI to reach comparable performance.  
- Question: clarify how well the method generalizes to entirely different car datasets (unseen makes/models) beyond the same dataset.
