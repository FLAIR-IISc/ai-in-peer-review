- Combines pre-trained VGG features, a bilinear predictive visual dynamics model, and a trust-region fitted-Q algorithm for sample-efficient visual servoing.
- Uses a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values instead of relying on one-step prediction.
- Introduces a multi-scale, multi-channel, locally-connected bilinear model for efficient, robust computation under visual variations.
- Learns effective visual servoing on a synthetic car-following benchmark with only 20 trajectories.
- Significantly outperforms pixel-based and hand-designed keypoint methods and is over two orders of magnitude more sample-efficient than model-free deep RL.
- Decision: Accept — important, practical contribution to robotics with strong sample efficiency and robustness.
- Well-motivated problem statement and compelling rationale for using pre-trained classification features.
- Methodology is mathematically sound; locally-connected bilinear model and trust-region fitted-Q are carefully derived.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough with multiple baselines, ablation studies, and tests on seen and novel cars demonstrating generalization.
- Empirical evidence that pre-trained visual features aid generalization in vision-based control.
- Suggestion: clarify the paper’s contribution to control vs its limited contribution to representation learning for the ICLR audience.
- Suggestion: provide more detailed computational-efficiency analysis (training time, resource requirements) beyond runtime comparisons.
- Suggestion: discuss limitations where ImageNet features may not be relevant (e.g., non-photorealistic or domain-shifted visuals).
- Question: clarify the specific novelty of the “trust-region fitted-Q iteration” relative to existing fitted-Q methods.
- Question: explain why VGG conv4_3 outperforms deeper conv5_3 for this task and what properties make conv4_3 suitable.
- Question: justify the choice of a bilinear model for visual dynamics versus other predictive models and whether theoretical guarantees exist.
