- Paper proposes visual servoing using pre-trained visual features, a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and reinforcement learning with per-feature-channel Q-weighting.  
- Authors introduce a scale-invariance insight for Q-parameters enabling an efficient FQI optimization.  
- Claims substantial sample-efficiency gains (over two orders of magnitude) on a synthetic car-following benchmark.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Technical contributions (bilinear/locally connected dynamics, scale-invariance trick, per-channel weighting) are novel and well-motivated.  
- Locally connected bilinear model balances efficiency and expressiveness.  
- Per-channel weighting demonstrably outperforms uniform weighting in experiments.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a small synthetic benchmark with only 5 training cars, lacking real-world complexity and diversity.  
- Baselines are insufficient and not comprehensive—missing standard RL baselines like DDPG or SAC.  
- Modified VGG network is not adequately validated; no evidence the changes help over original VGG.  
- Sample-efficiency claims are not convincingly supported by experiments or comparisons to stronger baselines.  
- Recommended experiments: compare fully connected vs. convolutional vs. locally connected dynamics models.  
- Recommended analysis: compare FQI optimization to an iterative scheme holding the second term fixed.  
- Recommended expansion: test on more challenging and real-world visual servoing tasks and clarify real-data sample requirements.  
- Overall: technically sound and innovative approach, but empirical validation is too limited to support the paper’s strong claims.
