- Leverages pre-trained deep features, a learned bilinear visual dynamics model, and reinforcement learning for visual servoing.  
- Learned policy effectively follows a target in a complex simulated environment and outperforms existing methods, including those using ground-truth bounding boxes.  
- Releases CitySim3D environment as a valuable benchmark for the community.  
- Learns feature weights via fitted Q-iteration, demonstrating good sample efficiency.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Core idea is well-defined, experiments are well-designed, and results clearly show effectiveness and sample-efficiency advantages.  
- Ablation studies and baseline comparisons are convincing and highlight benefits of pre-trained features and learned visual dynamics.  
- Limitation: evaluations are restricted to a synthetic environment; need tests under more realistic/varied visual conditions (clutter, distractors, diverse 3D models).  
- Suggest investigating end-to-end training or fine-tuning the convolutional network to improve representation learning and generalization.  
- Suggest improving reproducibility by streamlining the implementation and adding a concise code-structure summary in the paper; fix minor typos.  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the bilinear model’s approximation accuracy and to complex motions or lighting changes?  
- Question: How was the regularization coefficient (ν) for fitted Q-iteration chosen and how does it affect the learned policy?  
- Question: Are there any challenging dependencies or setup instructions in the open-source code for new users?
