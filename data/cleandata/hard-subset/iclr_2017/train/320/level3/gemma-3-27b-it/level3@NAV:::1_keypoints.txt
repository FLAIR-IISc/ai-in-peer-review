- Proposes a novel visual-servoing method combining VGG-based learned features, a bilinear dynamics model, and Fitted Q-Iteration (FQI).
- Introduces per-channel feature weighting learned via FQI as a significant contribution.
- Demonstrates substantially improved sample efficiency over standard deep RL on a synthetic car-following benchmark.
- Presents a scaling-invariance argument that simplifies FQI optimization, argued to be mathematically sound.
- Recommendation: Reject due to limited impact from evaluation only on a small-scale synthetic environment.
- Main experimental weakness: lack of real-world or more complex simulation evaluations, limiting claims of generalizability and robustness.
- Modifications to the VGG network are not thoroughly justified experimentally; unclear if they outperform standard VGG.
- Comparison to existing methods is incomplete; baseline choices need clearer justification and broader coverage.
- Requested comparison between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models to assess model design choices.
- Requested empirical validation of the scaling argument by comparing FQI with an iterative approach that fixes the second term in Eq.5.
- Ask to clarify the reward/cost function used in the RL framework with more detailed explanation.
- Recommend ablation studies to quantify the impact of bilinear dynamics, feature weighting, and FQI individually.
- Suggest investigating robustness to noise in visual features and action execution.
- Ask for a detailed analysis of computational complexity relative to other approaches.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity of performance to the specific VGG architecture modifications and whether changes were systematic or heuristic.
- Question: impact of the discount factor (γ) on learning dynamics and final servo performance.
- Question: why deeper VGG features (conv5_3) performed worse than shallower ones—lack of spatial information or other factors?
