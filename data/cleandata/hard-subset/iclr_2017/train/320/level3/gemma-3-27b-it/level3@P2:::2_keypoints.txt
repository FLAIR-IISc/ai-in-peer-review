- Proposed method: visual servoing in learned feature space using fixed pre-trained VGG features, a bilinear feature-dynamics model, and sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration to learn feature weights.  
- Empirical result: substantial improvements on a synthetic car-following benchmark over pixel-based, hand-designed features, and standard deep RL, with strong sample efficiency.  
- Ablations and comparisons of weighting schemes plus demonstration videos provide useful insight and support claims.  
- Decision: Reject — primary contribution is control-focused rather than advancing representation learning, making it less suitable for ICLR.  
- Main critique: heavy reliance on fixed pre-trained VGG features (no significant modification or end-to-end learning) limits novelty in representation learning.  
- Fitted Q-iteration is a noteworthy algorithmic contribution but its value is tied to the pre-defined feature space.  
- Using a linear Q-function approximation improves efficiency but likely limits capacity for complex policies.  
- Need stronger justification for why VGG features work well for visual servoing (semantic content vs other properties).  
- Suggest investigating end-to-end learning or fine-tuning VGG layers to strengthen representation contributions.  
- Recommend discussing generalization and transfer to real-world scenarios, including domain adaptation and sensor-noise robustness.  
- Ask for a detailed computational complexity analysis of the fitted Q-iteration to assess scalability.  
- Recommend comparisons to model-based RL methods to provide a more complete evaluation.  
- Question: Why did deeper VGG layer (conv5_3) perform worse — spatial invariance or information loss?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to bilinear dynamics hyperparameters (depth, regularization)?  
- Question: What is the computational cost of training the dynamics model and Q-function versus end-to-end deep RL?  
- Question: What are the limitations of the linear Q-function approximation in more complex scenarios?  
- Question: Have you explored techniques to mitigate the sim-to-real domain gap?
