- Decision: Accept.

- Core idea: Combines pre-trained CNN features, a bilinear feature-dynamics model, and reinforcement learning for rapid adaptation in visual servoing.

- Problem focus: Tackles target-following with limited data and aims to reduce reliance on manual features and precise dynamics.

- Empirical strength: Strong results with substantial sample-efficiency gains over pixel-based, hand-crafted, and standard deep RL baselines.

- Reproducibility: Authors released environment and code, facilitating follow-up work.

- Technical soundness: Bilinear dynamics and fitted Q-iteration are reasonable and computationally tractable; linearization aids optimization.

- Ablations and comparisons: Thorough experiments and ablation studies demonstrate contributions of components and some generalization to novel cars.

- Generalization & complexity concern: Needs evaluation under varied lighting, clutter, diverse car models, dynamic backgrounds, and more realistic/real-world settings.

- End-to-end learning suggestion: Explore fine-tuning or end-to-end training of pre-trained CNN features to potentially improve performance.

- Implementation details suggestion: Include a concise summary of implementation and a hyperparameter table; fix minor typos for clarity.

- Cost-function clarification: Provide stronger justification for the chosen reinforcement-learning cost function and how it captures desired servo behavior.

- Questions for authors: Propose solutions for conv5_3 spatial-invariance issues; explain which components drive sample-efficiency gains; discuss handling the reality gap and deployment strategies; clarify code dependencies and provide a Dockerfile for easy reproduction.
