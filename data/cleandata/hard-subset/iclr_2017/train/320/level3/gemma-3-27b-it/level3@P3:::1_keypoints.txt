- Decision: Reject; encourage resubmission after substantial revisions.
- Contribution: Proposes combining learned visual features, a bilinear dynamics model, and Fitted Q-Iteration (FQI) for visual servoing.
- Core claim: Learns action policies from image features, predicts future features, and optimizes feature weights via a scaling-invariant Q-function, showing improvements in a simulated car-following task.
- Main critique: Empirical evaluation is insufficient—limited to a small synthetic environment without benchmarks or real-world tests.
- Missing analyses: Reviewer requests about comparing fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models remain unaddressed.
- Overstated results: Claims of “substantial improvement” and “>100x sample-efficiency” are not justified by the narrow experimental scope.
- VGG modification: The benefit of the modified VGG network is not convincingly validated and may add unnecessary complexity.
- FQI concerns: The proposed scaling-invariant FQI is clever but its practical advantages are not fully explored or clearly compared to alternatives.
- Ablation needed: Lack of detailed ablation studies showing the contributions of feature weighting, dynamics model choice, and other components.
- Experimental expansion: Recommend evaluation on more challenging/realistic environments and standard robotics benchmarks.
- Baseline comparisons: Suggest comparisons to state-of-the-art RL algorithms (e.g., PPO, SAC) to support sample-efficiency claims.
- Dynamics model comparison: Request thorough quantitative comparison of model variants on cost, expressiveness, and accuracy.
- FQI analysis: Ask for experiments comparing the proposed FQI to iterative/alternative optimizers and visualization of Q-function evolution.
- VGG validation: Recommend direct comparison of modified VGG vs original across layers and tasks to justify design choices.
- Robustness testing: Recommend testing robustness to lighting, camera calibration, and target appearance variations.
- Question—generalization: What visual servoing tasks and environment complexities is the approach suited for or limited by?
- Question—scalability: How does computational cost scale with feature dimensionality and dynamics model complexity?
- Question—FQI intuition: Provide a simple explanation/example why scaling Q-parameters does not change the optimal policy.
- Question—VGG layer choice: Why was conv4_3 chosen—was a systematic layer search performed?
- Question—real-world deployment: What challenges must be addressed to deploy this method on real robotic platforms?
