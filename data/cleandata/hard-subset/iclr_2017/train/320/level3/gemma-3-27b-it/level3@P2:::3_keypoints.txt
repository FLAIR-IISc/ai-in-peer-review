- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Contribution: Proposes visual servoing that moves the camera in learned feature space using learned predictive dynamics and RL for fast adaptation to new targets.  
- Method: Uses sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration to learn which visual features are most suitable for the task.  
- Empirical result: Demonstrates strong performance on a synthetic car-following benchmark with large gains over classical and deep RL baselines.  
- Sample efficiency: Can learn effective visual servoing from only ~20 training trajectories.  
- Ablations: Ablation studies show the importance of different feature representations and system components.  
- Baseline comparisons: Thorough comparison with TRPO and classical IBVS validates the approach’s superiority.  
- Reproducibility: Code and environment are open-sourced, but experimental setup and hyperparameters need fuller documentation.  
- Generalization concern: Experiments are limited to a synthetic environment; discussion or evaluation on real-world/complex visuals would strengthen claims.  
- Representation learning: Suggests exploring end-to-end training or fine-tuning the feature extractor for potential gains.  
- Minor issues: Contains a typo ("CitySim3D1" → "CitySim3D") and other small clarifications could improve clarity.  
- Question — dynamics model: How sensitive is performance to the bilinear model accuracy and were alternatives explored?  
- Question — reward design: How was the cost/reward function chosen and what effect do different rewards have?  
- Question — feature depth: Why did deepest VGG features (conv5_3) underperform—loss of spatial info or other reasons?  
- Question — compute: Please provide details on computational resources required for training and deployment.  
- Question — robustness: How robust is the method to lighting, camera parameter changes, and real-world variability, and how might robustness be improved?
