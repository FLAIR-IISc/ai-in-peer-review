- Decision: Accept.
- Problem: Tackles visual servoing/target following by combining learned visual features, a predictive dynamics model, and reinforcement learning for quick adaptation.
- Approach: Servo in learned feature space using pre-trained VGG features, a bilinear predictive model, and a sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration to weight features.
- Key contribution: A fitted Q-iteration algorithm that learns feature weightings enabling sample-efficient visual servoing.
- Model component: Bilinear predictive model provides a computationally efficient way to predict effects of robot motion on visual features.
- Sample efficiency: Achieves effective servoing with only ~20 training trajectories.
- Empirical results: Strong performance on a synthetic car-following benchmark, outperforming pixel-based, hand-designed keypoint, and standard deep RL baselines.
- Ablations and validation: Thorough comparisons across feature representations and optimization methods; videos included to demonstrate performance.
- Representation limitation: Heavy reliance on pre-trained VGG features; limited novel contribution to representation learning itself.
- Linearity concern: Use of a linear Q-function aids efficiency but may limit expressiveness in complex scenarios; potential trade-offs with non-linear approximators need discussion.
- Real-world generalization: Experiments are synthetic; transfer challenges (sensor noise, lighting, complex dynamics) should be addressed.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Results could benefit from discussion of robustness to learning rates and regularization choices.
- Question — complexity: Clarify computational cost and scaling trade-offs of the bilinear dynamics model as feature dimensionality grows.
- Question — deep features: Explain why deeper VGG layers (conv5_3) performed worse beyond loss of spatial information.
- Question — algorithm choice: Justify choice of fitted Q-iteration over alternative Q-learning methods and its specific benefits here.
- Question — fine-tuning: Indicate whether VGG fine-tuning would be needed for real deployment and how much domain-specific data would be required.
