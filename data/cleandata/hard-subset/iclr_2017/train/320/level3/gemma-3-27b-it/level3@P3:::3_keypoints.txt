- Proposed method: combines pre-trained convolutional features, a bilinear one-step visual dynamics model, and reinforcement learning for visual servoing/target-following.  
- Key contribution: learns a policy in feature space, leveraging robustness of pre-trained features and fast adaptation with limited data.  
- Empirical result: strong simulated performance, outperforming several baselines (including methods using ground-truth bounding boxes).  
- Sample efficiency: demonstrates impressive sample efficiency compared to standard deep RL algorithms.  
- Community contribution: release of the CitySim3D environment as a benchmark.  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance based on strong empirical results and clear methodology.  
- Clarity and structure: paper is well-structured, clearly written, and motivation is well-articulated.  
- Experimental rigor: thorough experimental setup with convincing baseline comparisons, ablation studies, and feature analyses.  
- Technical insight: shows benefits of pre-trained features and learning feature weights via fitted Q-iteration.  
- Limitation — generalization: needs evaluation in more complex, diverse, and realistic visual conditions (clutter, distractors, varied appearances, wider 3D model range).  
- Limitation — end-to-end learning: should investigate end-to-end training or fine-tuning of the convolutional network for task-specific representation gains.  
- Reproducibility: code released but recommend simplifying and summarizing key implementation choices/dependencies to improve reproducibility.  
- Minor edits: fix typographical errors noted on p.2.  
- Q1 for authors: Why choose a bilinear dynamics model over alternatives (e.g., RNNs), and were alternatives evaluated?  
- Q2 for authors: How sensitive is fitted Q-iteration to the regularization coefficient (ν), and was a systematic hyperparameter search performed?  
- Q3 for authors: What failure cases or limitations were observed in simulation, and which scenarios/visual conditions are most challenging?  
- Q4 for authors: Are there plans to expand CitySim3D with more varied scenarios, objects, and sensor modalities?
