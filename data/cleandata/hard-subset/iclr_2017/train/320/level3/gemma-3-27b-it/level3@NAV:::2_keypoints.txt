- Tackles visual servoing using learned visual features instead of hand-engineered features and analytical models.  
- Proposes using pre-trained VGG features within an action-conditional bilinear dynamics model and fitted Q-iteration for policy learning.  
- Introduces a multi-scale, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model.  
- Presents a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm as part of the contribution.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a synthetic car-following benchmark, showing benefits of a value-weighted objective.  
- Shows strong empirical results and generalization when using pre-trained visual features.  
- Achieves high sample efficiency (notably good results with only 20 training samples).  
- Provides rigorous mathematical formulation and convincing experimental validation.  
- Compares favorably against classical IBVS and deep RL baselines (TRPO, CEM) and includes ablation studies varying feature representations.  
- Limitation: contribution to representation learning is limited due to reliance on pre-trained VGG features rather than end-to-end learning.  
- Limitation: fixed policy representation and linear value approximation shift focus toward control rather than new representation learning.  
- Suggested additions: explicitly discuss limits of pre-trained features and paths to end-to-end/task-specific feature learning.  
- Suggested additions: deeper analysis of which VGG layers/features drive performance.  
- Suggested additions: discuss sensitivity to key hyperparameters (e.g., regularization ν).  
- Suggested additions: provide a detailed computational cost comparison to alternatives.  
- Clarifying question: what is the role of the parameter W(l)c,0 in the locally connected bilinear model and how does it differ from other weights?  
- Clarifying question: why do deeper VGG features (conv5_3) perform worse — loss of spatial info or other factors, and could attention mitigate this?
