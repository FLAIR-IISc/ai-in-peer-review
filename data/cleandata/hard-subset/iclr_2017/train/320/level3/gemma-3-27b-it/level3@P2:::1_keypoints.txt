- Decision: Reject.

- Contribution: Proposes visual servoing using learned visual features, a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and Fitted Q-Iteration to enable rapid adaptation to new targets.

- Strength: Technically interesting approach with a solid theoretical foundation.

- Main concern: Experimental validation is limited to a synthetic CitySim3D benchmark, undermining claims of real-world applicability.

- Generalization worry: Synthetic setup may not capture real-world challenges like lighting, sensor noise, and imperfect dynamics.

- Insufficient baselines: Comparisons to deep RL methods are vague—specific algorithms, hyperparameters, and tuning procedures are not provided.

- Missing SOTA comparisons: Needs explicit experiments against continuous-control RL algorithms (e.g., DDPG, TD3, SAC) and clearer details on classical IBVS/PBVS implementations.

- Ablation gap: No experiments isolating the contributions of major components (modified VGG, bilinear model variants, feature weighting).

- Unvalidated VGG modification: Dilated VGG is proposed but no evidence shows it improves performance over standard VGG.

- Weak justification for locally connected dynamics: Claim that fully connected models are too large is asserted without systematic comparison to FC and conv alternatives or cost/performance tradeoff analysis.

- FQI clarity: The optimization/iterative strategy exploiting Q-scale invariance is opaque and needs clearer explanation and comparisons to straightforward iterative updates.

- Recommended experiments: Evaluate on real robotic platforms or established benchmarks to demonstrate robustness and practical impact.

- Suggested ablations: Test pre-trained vs. random VGG features, compare fully connected vs. locally connected bilinear models, and measure the effect of learned feature weighting.

- Methodological improvements: Provide detailed baseline hyperparameters, report training times and statistical significance, and release well-documented code for reproducibility.

- Questions for authors: Specify baseline RL hyperparameters, show results with original VGG, report compute resources, quantify computational benefits of locally connected bilinear model, and state plans for real-world evaluations.
