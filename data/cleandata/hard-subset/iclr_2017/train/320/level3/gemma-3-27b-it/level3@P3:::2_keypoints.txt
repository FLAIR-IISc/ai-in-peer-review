- Paper studies visual servoing for target following using learned visual representations.  
- Proposes an action-conditional bilinear model on VGG-extracted features and derives a policy via linearized dynamics.  
- Introduces a multi-scale, locally-connected variant of the bilinear model to reduce parameters and capture local structure.  
- Proposes a weighted objective that incorporates long-term policy values to mitigate the one-step predictive limitation of the bilinear model.  
- Introduces a trust-region fitted-Q iteration algorithm to learn the weighting in the objective.  
- Experiments are conducted on a synthetic car-following benchmark demonstrating the method’s effectiveness.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, mathematically rigorous, and conceptually thorough.  
- Experiments convincingly show the value of the value-weighted objective and that pre-trained visual features aid generalization.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main reason for acceptance: significant contribution to sample-efficient reinforcement learning for robotic control, achieving strong performance with only 20 training samples.  
- Trust-region fitted-Q iteration is highlighted as a valuable algorithmic contribution.  
- Supporting argument: combining pre-trained features, a bilinear dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration is well-motivated and effective for generalization and sample efficiency.  
- Empirical comparisons and ablations clearly isolate component contributions and show superiority over conventional IBVS and standard deep RL.  
- Authors provide rigorous derivations, thorough experiments, and thoughtful discussion of limitations.  
- Weakness: focus is more on control than representation learning, which may be less aligned with ICLR’s emphasis.  
- Concern: reliance on pre-trained VGG limits the paper’s contribution to representation learning; end-to-end or task-specific feature learning could strengthen the work.  
- Suggestion: provide deeper justification for the locally-connected bilinear model beyond parameter sparsity, explaining why its structure suits visual servoing.  
- Questions for authors: Did you try end-to-end learning or fine-tuning VGG and what challenges arose?  
- Questions for authors: What specific visual-servoing properties motivated the locally-connected architecture beyond sparsity?  
- Questions for authors: How would you adapt this approach to real-world environments with clutter, lighting changes, or different cameras/robots?  
- Questions for authors: What are the limitations of approximating the Q-function linearly in more complex, nonlinear scenarios?
