## Review of "Visual Servoing with Learned Dynamics and Fitted Q-Iteration"

This paper tackles the problem of visual servoing, specifically target following, by combining learned visual features, a bilinear dynamics model, and reinforcement learning. The core contribution lies in formulating visual servoing as a Q-learning problem with per-feature-channel weights, and introducing an optimization scheme leveraging the scale-invariance of Q-parameters to enable an efficient Fitted Q-Iteration (FQI) approach. The authors argue for a locally connected bilinear model as a good balance between computational cost and expressiveness, and demonstrate its effectiveness in a synthetic car-following benchmark.

I recommend **rejecting** this paper in its current form. While the technical formulation is novel and the proposed FQI optimization is clever, the experimental validation is insufficient to support the claims of significant improvement and generalization. 

The paper’s strength lies in its elegant theoretical formulation. The insight that scaling Q-parameters doesn’t affect the optimal policy is a valuable simplification for FQI. The locally connected bilinear model appears to be a reasonable compromise, and the per-channel weighting of features is a sensible approach. However, the experimental evaluation is a major weakness. The car-following benchmark, while complex, is limited in scope. The lack of comparison to standard deep reinforcement learning algorithms on more established domain benchmarks (e.g., robotic manipulation tasks) makes it difficult to assess the true value of the proposed approach. The claim of “more than two orders of magnitude” improvement in sample efficiency needs stronger evidence. Furthermore, the modification of the VGG network requires validation – it’s unclear if the modified version actually *outperforms* the original. The experiments comparing different weighting schemes (FQI vs. TRPO vs. no weighting) are a step in the right direction, but more comprehensive comparisons are needed.

**Additional Feedback (for improvement):**

*   **Expand Experimental Evaluation:** Include comparisons on more diverse and challenging benchmarks, ideally involving real-world robotic platforms.
*   **Ablation Studies:** Conduct more thorough ablation studies to isolate the impact of each component (bilinear dynamics, FQI, per-channel weighting).
*   **Clarify FQI vs. Iterative Approach:** Provide experiments comparing FQI to a simpler iterative approach where the second term in Eq. 5 is held fixed. This would help demonstrate the benefits of the proposed optimization scheme.
*   **VGG Modification Validation:** Quantify the performance difference between the modified and original VGG networks.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Analyze the sensitivity of the results to key hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate, regularization coefficient).

**Questions for the Authors:**

*   What is the computational cost overhead of the locally connected bilinear model compared to a fully connected model of similar capacity?
*   How does the performance of the proposed approach degrade when the training and testing environments have significant domain shifts (e.g., different lighting conditions, textures)?
*   Could you elaborate on the choice of the cost function in Eq. 9? What alternatives were considered, and why was this particular function chosen?
*   What is the impact of the discount factor (γ) on the performance of the algorithm?
