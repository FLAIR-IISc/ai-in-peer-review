- Introduces a novel visual servoing approach combining pre-trained visual features, a bilinear dynamics model, and reinforcement learning in feature space.
- Uses a bilinear model to predict next-frame features and fitted Q-iteration (FQI) to learn a Q-function that weights feature channels for task relevance.
- Shows sample efficiency and robustness to visual variations on a synthetic car-following benchmark with improvements over conventional methods.
- Decision: Reject due to insufficient experimental validation and limited scope of experiments.
- Strength: Bilinear dynamics model balances computational efficiency and expressiveness for frame prediction.
- Strength: Channel-weighting effectively prioritizes object-relevant features, validated experimentally.
- Strength: Novel, computationally efficient Bellman-error optimization leveraging parameter scaling for FQI.
- Weakness: Experiments confined to small-scale synthetic simulations with no real-world benchmarks, limiting generalizability.
- Weakness: Modified VGG is not compared to the original or other feature backbones, leaving its necessity unclear.
- Weakness: No comparison between FQI and iterative Q-learning variants that fix the second Bellman term.
- Weakness: Paper does not explore trade-offs between fully connected, convolutional, and locally connected dynamics models.
- Feedback: Expand experiments to real-world tasks (e.g., robotic manipulation or navigation) and compare to state-of-the-art methods.
- Feedback: Provide ablation studies and comparisons with other backbones (ResNet, EfficientNet) to justify architecture choices.
- Feedback: Include comparisons with iterative Q-learning methods to demonstrate FQI advantages.
- Feedback: Address scalability to higher-dimensional control spaces and more complex environments via larger sims or hardware experiments.
- Feedback: Improve clarity of key equations and algorithms for readers less familiar with reinforcement learning.
- Question: How does the method perform on real-world tasks or physical robots; are there plans for such validation?
- Question: Why was VGG chosen and how do the modifications improve performance versus original or other architectures?
- Question: Can the bilinear dynamics model handle more complex, highly non-linear dynamics or is extra modeling needed?
- Question: How does the proposed FQI algorithm compare to iterative Q-learning in convergence speed and performance?
