- Novel visual servoing method combining pre-trained CNN feature maps, multi-scale bilinear dynamics, and reinforcement learning.  
- Uses trust-region fitted Q-iteration to learn multi-scale visual feature weights for servoing policies.  
- Demonstrates significant empirical improvements over baselines, including methods using ground-truth bounding boxes.  
- Achieves high sample efficiency (≈ two orders of magnitude better than standard model-free RL) due to the Q-function approximator and one-step feature dynamics.  
- Shows robustness to distractors, occlusions, and visual variations in controlled simulations.  
- Contributes an open-source virtual city environment for visual servoing benchmarking.  
- Paper judged methodologically novel and empirically strong, leading to an accept decision.  
- Suggestion: evaluate in more complex visual conditions with richer object appearance, lighting, and distractors.  
- Suggestion: explore end-to-end training of features to potentially improve generalization in challenging visuals.  
- Suggestion: improve reproducibility by clarifying implementation details and providing a direct open-source link.  
- Minor issues: fix typographical errors for clarity.

- Question: provide more details on computational efficiency compared to end-to-end RL approaches.
