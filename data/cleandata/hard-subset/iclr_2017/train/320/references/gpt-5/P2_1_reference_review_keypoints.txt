- Proposed method: combine modified spatial-preserving VGG features, a multiscale bilinear feature-dynamics model, and an FQI that learns nonnegative per-channel/per-scale feature weights and control penalties.
- Key design: linear-in-weights Q-function enables efficient fitted-Q updates and an analytic quadratic action for execution.
- Empirical claim: outperforms IBVS (ORB/tracker), CEM tuning, TRPO (end-to-end and same parameterization) and is faster at runtime.
- Sample-efficiency: RL stage uses only ~20 trajectories; dynamics pre-trained from ~10k (xt,ut,xt+1) triplets.
- Representation finding: mid-level VGG features (conv1_2–conv4_3) generalize best; conv5_3 is too invariant and pixels/generalize poorly.
- Reproducibility: code, simulator, and videos provided; clear ablations and implementation details.
- Recommendation: Accept — clean, well-motivated synthesis with strong empirical gains and reproducible artifacts.
- Motivation/support: timely problem, good positioning versus IBVS/PBVS, model-free RL, and video prediction literature; bilinear locally-connected dynamics justified.
- Ablations/supporting experiments: comparisons across feature depth, baselines (TRPO, CEM, IBVS, PBVS), and generalization to novel cars.
- Limitation: evaluation only in simulation; real-world validation and robustness claims need strengthening.
- Limitation: RL cost uses privileged ground-truth target pose — limited discussion of practical perceptual cost alternatives.
- Limitation: no theoretical guarantees for the modified FQI (per-iteration scaling α and bias b), though empirically stable.
- Limitation/question: clarify semantic-feature bilinear model choice and parameter/counts for high-dimensional features.
- Requested clarifications: specify which bilinear variant was used, parameter counts, memory and training times per VGG level.
- Suggested experiments: small-scale adaptation to unseen targets (few-shot RL fine-tuning) to demonstrate quick adaptation.
- Suggested sensitivity analyses: vary RL trajectory count (e.g., 5/10/20/40), study regularization ν and nonnegativity constraints, compare to no-dynamics baseline, and report variance/confidence intervals.
- Planning question: discuss why one-step lookahead suffices and whether multi-step planning or receding horizon helps or harms.
- Deployment concerns: comment on camera calibration dependence and robustness to changes in intrinsics or height.
- Additional questions for authors: sensitivity to VGG modification choices, use of lighter backbones, robustness to noisy costs/partial observability, interpretability/sparsity of learned weights, and reporting of success rates/tracking error with SEs.
