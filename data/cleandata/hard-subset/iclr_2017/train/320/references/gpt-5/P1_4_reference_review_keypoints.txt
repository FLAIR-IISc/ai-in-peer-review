- Problem: Learn visual servoing policies that quickly adapt to new targets with minimal task-specific data, robust to viewpoint, lighting, and occlusion.
- Approach: Use fixed pre-trained VGG-16 features (dilated to preserve spatial detail), multiscale bilinear predictive feature dynamics, and a one-step greedy controller minimizing weighted feature prediction error, with weights learned via sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration (FQI).
- Technical tweak: Pre-fitting scaling and bias (α, b) to exploit greedy action invariance stabilizes and accelerates FQI.
- Evaluation: Synthetic quadcopter car-following benchmark (CitySim3D) with ablations over VGG layers, dynamics variants, and weight-learning methods; baselines include end-to-end TRPO, IBVS (ORB and C-COT tracker), and PBVS.
- Main results: conv4_3 features + bilinear dynamics + FQI perform best, generalize to unseen cars, outperform pixels and conv5_3, and are far more sample-efficient than model-free TRPO; code and environment released.
- Decision: Weak accept.
- Reason 1: Simple, well-motivated combination yields strong empirical gains and impressive sample efficiency on a challenging task with clear ablations.
- Reason 2: Paper is carefully engineered, reproducible, and contributes useful benchmark/code to the community.
- Motivation & positioning: Sensible reuse of ImageNet features to balance invariance and spatial specificity, addressing sample-efficiency limits of end-to-end deep RL.
- Methodological clarity: Multiscale feature pyramid, bilinear dynamics, one-step controller, and linear Q-approximation are clearly explained; α,b pre-fit is a neat, practical insight.
- Empirical rigor — ablations: Layer-wise ablations show tradeoff between invariance and spatial specificity, with conv4_3 as sweet spot.
- Empirical rigor — weight-learning: Comparisons show FQI outperforms TRPO and CEM in performance and sample efficiency.
- Empirical rigor — baselines: Strong, fair baselines used (end-to-end TRPO, IBVS with ORB and C-COT, PBVS upper bound).
- Empirical rigor — generalization: conv1_2–conv4_3 maintain performance on unseen cars; pixels and conv5_3 degrade more.
- Practicality: Method runs at ~16 Hz (feature extraction limited) and is faster than tracker baseline; open-sourcing increases impact.
- Clarify sample accounting: Reported “20 trajectories” for FQI omits 10k (xt, u, xt+1) tuples used to train dynamics; discuss total data budget, collection ease, and fairness to model-free baselines.
- Reward design without privileged state: Current cost uses ground-truth 3D pose; discuss real-world alternatives (self-supervised image surrogates or learned rewards).
- Scope & generality: All results are in one synthetic domain; add a second domain or sim-to-real pilot to demonstrate broader applicability.
- Dynamics model analysis: Provide prediction error vs horizon and correlate model accuracy with servo performance; compare to optical-flow or ConvLSTM predictors.
- Weights & policy analysis: Report channel/scale sparsity after FQI, consider spatially varying weights/attention, and analyze sensitivity to regularization ν and exploration variance.
- Statistical reporting: Add confidence intervals/standard errors and significance testing for average trajectory cost.
- Fairness of IBVS baseline: Ensure equivalent priors/access across methods or discuss implications since IBVS uses ground-truth depth and tracker initialization.
- Question — total sample usage: When including 10k dynamics tuples, how do total environment steps compare to end-to-end TRPO baselines?
- Question — training weights without GT: How would you train feature weights with only images (no 3D pose), and have image-based costs been tried?
- Question — dynamics generalization: How sensitive is the dynamics model to target appearance/domain shift (e.g., trucks, motorcycles) and was a single dynamics model reused across targets?
- Question — end-to-end finetuning: Did you try finetuning VGG layers jointly with bilinear dynamics to improve conv5_3 spatial specificity?
- Question — alternative predictors: How does the bilinear model compare to optical-flow warping or lightweight convolutional action-conditioned predictors?
- Question — robustness: How does performance vary with occlusion severity, lighting changes, and camera calibration perturbations; any failure cases?
- Question — optimization details: Did relaxing θ ≥ 0 help, and how critical is the α,b step to FQI stability and convergence?
