- Paper combines modified pre-trained VGG features, per-scale bilinear feature dynamics, and fitted Q-iteration over non-negative channel/scale weights with an analytic one-step controller for visual servoing.
- Reports strong performance and ~100x better RL sample efficiency vs model-free TRPO in a CitySim3D quadrotor–car-following simulator; code and simulator released.
- Recommendation: Reject.
- Main critique: Limited novelty — components (pretrained features, bilinear dynamics, linear FQI) are established; contribution is a pragmatic, incremental combination.
- Main critique: Evaluation uses privileged information (3D ground-truth rewards, FOV/termination flags) and simulation-only tests, weakening real-world claims.
- Main critique: Dynamics model trained on 100 hand-coded trajectories (10k triplets), so the “sample-efficiency” claim should account for supervised dynamics data, not just ~20 RL trajectories.
- Positive: Well-written paper with reasonable design choices; conv4_3 layer choice and analytically solvable controller are sensible.
- Nuance on novelty: Embedding the servo objective as a Q-function with non-negative weights and the α,b calibration is a modest, domain-specific contribution.
- Generalization concern: Tests limited to novel cars in the same simulator; no cross-domain transfer (textures, lighting, camera intrinsics) or robustness checks to dynamics mismatch.
- Limitation: Learned weights are global (state-independent), limiting adaptability to occlusion, clutter, and context-specific relevance of features.
- Missing diagnostics: Little analysis of bilinear model prediction quality (per-scale errors, action dependence) or planning depth vs one-step trade-offs.
- Missing ablations: No clear attribution of gains between dynamics model, pretrained features, and learned weights.
- Reframe recommendation: Explicitly separate supervised dynamics data budget from RL data and report total sample/compute costs.
- Reward recommendation: Replace privileged 3D costs with image-derived rewards or demonstrate self-supervised image-cost driven RL to improve realism.
- Real-world validation: Add real-robot experiments or hardware-in-the-loop tests to demonstrate sim-to-real feasibility and latency handling.
- Suggested ablations: dynamics dataset size; bilinear vs simpler/more expressive predictors; state-dependent weighting (e.g., small gating network); multi-step MPC vs one-step lookahead.
- Evaluation metrics: Report predictive-model metrics (per-layer MAE/PSNR/flow proxies) and relate them to control performance to justify the bilinear model.
- Baselines: Add model-based RL with learned dynamics+MPC, modern off-policy actor-critic with representation learning, and photometric IBVS with robust optimization.
- Representation robustness: Test alternative pretrained features (ResNet, self-supervised) and sensitivity to VGG modifications (pooling/dilation).
- Failure analysis: Provide quantitative analysis of failure modes (heavy occlusion, sudden target loss) and recovery behavior.
- Q for authors: How would FQI weighting be learned without 3D ground-truth and FOV flags — can a purely image-based cost achieve similar performance?
- Q for authors: Total number of frames/actions for dynamics and weight training, and performance if dynamics data reduced to 10 trajectories?
- Q for authors: Did you try state-dependent weights (e.g., small MLP over feature stats) and does that help with occlusion/multi-object scenes?
- Q for authors: How accurate are one-step predictions as action magnitude or scene motion increases; do multi-step rollouts degrade rapidly and is partial unrolling beneficial?
- Q for authors: Can the method follow non-car targets or transfer across different cities/textures/camera intrinsics without retraining dynamics?
- Q for authors: For photometric IBVS and TRPO baselines, were robust losses, multi-scale alignment, and comparable observation normalization/network capacity applied?
