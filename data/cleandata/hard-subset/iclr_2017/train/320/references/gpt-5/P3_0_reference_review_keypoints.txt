- Paper proposes sample-efficient visual servoing using frozen VGG features, a bilinear one-step predictive feature dynamics model linear in control, and a tailored fitted Q-iteration to learn per-channel/scale feature weights and control regularization.
- Policy minimizes a weighted Euclidean distance between predicted next-step features and a goal feature, with a linear-in-parameters Q enabling stable, efficient FQI and a normalization trick for scaling invariance.
- Experiments in a 3D quadrotor–car simulator show VGG conv4_3 features outperform pixels and conv5_3, and FQI is >100× more sample-efficient than TRPO for learning weights.
- The method outperforms classical IBVS with ORB or a strong tracker, and beats end-to-end CNN RL baselines; code, simulator, and videos are released.
- Conference decision: Reject (borderline).
- Primary concern 1: Evaluation is simulation-only and relies on privileged cost signals (ground-truth target pose), weakening real-world robustness and practicality claims.
- Primary concern 2: Methodological novelty is incremental—combines known components (frozen features, one-step servo, linear FQI) and feels more like strong engineering than a major algorithmic advance.
- Motivation/positioning is sensible, well-situated between IBVS/PBVS and model-free RL, but discussion of dense alignment on deep feature pyramids could be expanded.
- Bilinear feature-dynamics model is appropriate for small motions and gives exact Jacobians, but the bilinear assumption for deep features is heuristic and needs analysis versus motion magnitude.
- Q-function design (linear in φ with bias) couples tightly to the servo objective and the α/b normalization refit is a useful stabilization trick.
- Key limitation: learned weights θ are global and state-independent, preventing spatial attention or context-dependent feature selection important under clutter/occlusion.
- Empirical positives: clear VGG layer ablations, strong sample-efficiency gains for learning θ (20 trajs), and comparison to TRPO and classical baselines.
- Empirical concerns: reward uses privileged 3D pose and FOV termination unavailable in real settings, with no discussion of practical alternatives.
- Empirical concerns: all results are from a single synthetic environment with fixed intrinsics and motion, leaving robustness to noise, lighting, and dynamics mismatch unclear.
- Empirical concerns: dynamics model training used 10k triplets from a hand-coded controller (100 trajs), which may bias results toward the task distribution.
- Empirical concerns: inconsistency in whether tracker-based IBVS Jacobians include car dynamics—clarification needed on which variant is plotted.
- Generalization claims are limited: tests show novel cars in-domain only, no out-of-distribution or domain-shift evaluations.
- Novelty/significance: the combo is practical and well-executed but each component is known; main conceptual contribution is demonstrating that re-weighted ImageNet features plus a learned predictive model yield low-data servoing.
- Suggested improvements: add real-world robot validation to address privileged rewards and sim bias.
- Suggested improvements: propose or demonstrate reward designs without privileged pose (self-supervised detectors, feature-based distances).
- Suggested improvements: extend to state-dependent spatial attention or per-layer gating to enable context-dependent weighting while keeping tractability.
- Suggested improvements: analyze dynamics prediction error vs. action magnitude and compare bilinear dynamics to flow-based warping in feature space.
- Suggested improvements: add baselines for dense photometric/feature-space alignment and modern learned keypoint/descriptors.
- Suggested improvements: provide detailed architecture, layer/channel choices, parameter counts, and runtime breakdown for reproducibility.
- Questions for authors include sensitivity to dynamics mis-specification; whether θ are shared or task-specific; clarification on IBVS variant used; size of local neighborhood nf and flow-based experiments; feasibility of vision-only rewards; and whether fine-tuning features was tried and its effect on sample efficiency.
