- Paper proposes visual servoing by combining pre-trained VGG features, a learned bilinear locally-connected feature dynamics model, and a fitted Q-iteration that learns per-channel multi-scale feature weights and control penalties for a one-step lookahead controller.  
- Key technical idea: derive a Q-function linear in the feature weights so FQI is efficient and yields a policy that minimizes predicted feature error to a goal map.  
- Empirical claims: learned mid-level VGG features with bilinear dynamics outperform raw-pixel servoing and classical IBVS (ORB/tracker), are sample-efficient vs TRPO, and generalize to novel cars on a CitySim3D benchmark.  
- Decision: reviewer recommends accept.  
- Strength: well-motivated, simple, and novel integration of pre-trained features, bilinear dynamics, and analytically structured Q-function enabling very sample-efficient policy improvement.  
- Strength: thorough empirical study with ablations across VGG layers and comparisons to classical IBVS and modern RL baselines supporting robustness and sample efficiency claims.  
- Motivation: visual servoing needs robust discriminative features and accurate feature Jacobians; pre-trained deep features provide invariances and short-horizon learned dynamics suffice for greedy servoing.  
- Motivation: learning per-channel weights via RL focuses control on task-relevant features/distractors and ties learning to the servo objective.  
- Dynamics design: bilinear locally-connected dynamics fit convolutional feature structure, are linear in controls, and can model local spatial interactions plus action-independent biases.  
- Q-function design: linearity in weights and constrained non-negativity stabilizes FQI, makes argmin policy invariant to positive scaling/bias, and reduces optimization difficulty.  
- Training protocol: dynamics trained supervised on ~10k pairs; RL weight learning performed in 2 sampling iterations of ~10 trajectories each with validation-based model selection.  
- Empirical finding: mid-level features (conv4_3) perform best—deeper layers are too spatially invariant for servoing.  
- Empirical comparisons: method outperforms raw-pixel servoing, ORB-based IBVS, tracker-based IBVS (C-COT) in robustness and speed, and achieves lower cost than TRPO with far fewer RL samples.  
- Empirical generalization: mid-level VGG features and learned dynamics generalize to unseen cars.  
- Limitation: all experiments are in simulation—no real-robot validation, limiting real-world robustness claims.  
- Limitation: sample-efficiency claim applies only to RL weighting stage; supervised dynamics training used ~100 trajectories/10k samples collected with a hand-crafted policy.  
- Limitation: RL cost uses privileged ground-truth pose for per-step costs/terminations, which may not be available in real deployments.  
- Limitation: no baseline using model-based short-horizon MPC with the learned dynamics to compare against the one-step greedy controller.  
- Analysis gap: more analysis needed of learned weights (sparsity, channel/scale selection) and robustness under heavy occlusions/distractors.  
- Suggestions: clarify data budget breakdown and sensitivity curves for dynamics vs RL data; add short-horizon MPC baseline using bilinear dynamics; measure/model dynamics uncertainty; analyze feature-weight patterns and transfer; test harder distractors/occlusions and lighting/viewpoint shifts; provide timing and alternative backbones; include small real-world validation if possible.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to using pose-based privileged costs vs proxy/noisy costs; scaling of performance with fewer dynamics/RL samples; transfer to new target classes and whether only weights must be relearned; sparsity/distribution of learned weights across channels/scales and seen vs novel targets; results of short-horizon MPC with bilinear dynamics; whether TRPO was given the same VGG features and why it underperformed; and steps required to remove privileged pose info and close sim-to-real gap.
