- Problem: Learn sample-efficient visual servoing for target following without hand-designed features or calibrated models.
- Approach: Combine pre-trained CNN features, a learned bilinear feature-dynamics model linear in control, and fitted Q-iteration (FQI) to learn nonnegative multi-scale feature weights and control penalties.
- Policy: One-step lookahead that minimizes weighted L2 distance between predicted features and a goal image.
- Technical: Locally connected bilinear dynamics per feature channel enabling exact linearization in control.
- Q-function: Analytic Q-approximator linear in feature weights so the greedy action equals the servo objective.
- Stabilization: Jointly fit scaling α and bias b for current and target Q-functions to accelerate and stabilize FQI.
- Empirics: In CitySim3D quadcopter-following-car benchmark, learns effective policies with 20 RL trajectories and 10k dynamics transitions.
- Baselines: Outperforms unweighted policies, CEM-/TRPO-tuned weightings, classical IBVS with ORB points, and end-to-end CNN+TRPO.
- Generalization: Shows transfer to novel car instances; code, simulator, and videos provided.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Strong, well-motivated idea tying model-based prediction, pre-trained features, and FQI into a coherent, efficient servoing framework.
- Key reason 2: Convincing empirical evidence of large sample-efficiency gains and performance improvements with careful ablations.
- Motivation: Uses pre-trained features for invariances and bilinear dynamics for short-horizon camera-induced motion; FQI for learning feature importance is well justified.
- Experimental design: Thorough ablations across VGG layers, weighting methods, classical and learned baselines, with clear train/val/test splits and metrics aligned to task.
- Claims vs results: Supports claims of sample efficiency, superior performance (notably conv4_3), and limitations of very deep, spatially invariant features.
- Limitation: Simulation-only evaluation; sim-to-real transfer, sensing noise, and latency untested.
- Limitation: Privileged cost uses ground-truth target pose during RL training, which may be impractical in real deployments.
- Limitation: Baseline tuning may be limited; only VGG explored and broader architecture/hyperparameter sensitivity is not fully addressed.
- Limitation: Method focuses on one-step servo objective and may struggle with non-myopic, long-horizon, or complex occlusion scenarios.
- Suggestions: Add experiments with non-privileged image-derived rewards or sparse human feedback.
- Suggestions: Test robustness to occlusions, lighting, camera errors, and dynamics mismatch; report failure rates.
- Suggestions: Evaluate alternative backbones (e.g., ResNet) and provide per-component runtime profiling.
- Suggestions: Consider uncertainty-aware dynamics (ensembles) or brief multi-step MPC and clarify VGG layer selection or learned projectors.
- Suggestions: Report more interpretable success metrics (time in FOV, distance thresholds) alongside cumulative cost.
- Questions: How to design rewards without ground-truth pose and can FQI be learned from image-only or sparse feedback?
- Questions: Sensitivity to layer choice, number of scales, and possibility of automatic selection/regularization?
- Questions: How do bilinear dynamics handle moving distractors, abrupt target maneuvers, or multiple similar targets?
- Questions: Impact of dynamics model error and comparisons to identity or optical-flow predictors?
- Questions: Generalization to other tasks (e.g., object reaching) and data needed to adapt dynamics?
- Questions: Stability of FQI across seeds and regularization; any failure modes like weight collapse?
