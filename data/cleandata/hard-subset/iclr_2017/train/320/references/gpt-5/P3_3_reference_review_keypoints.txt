- Combines pre-trained VGG features, a learned multiscale bilinear feature dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration to learn nonnegative per-channel/scale weights so a one-step greedy servo approximates long-horizon returns.  
- Keeps servoing controller simple (minimize next-step feature error) while learning a bilinear model for tractable control and a matched Q-function for sample-efficient weight learning.  
- Empirically outperforms IBVS (ORB/tracker), pixel-intensity servoing, and TRPO on a CitySim3D quadrotor-following-car benchmark, showing strong sample efficiency and ~16 Hz runtime.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main reason 1: Limited novelty—approach is mainly a synthesis of known components; weight-learning trick is incremental.  
- Main reason 2: Empirical validation restricted to a single synthetic domain with privileged training signals and limited ablations; real-world viability not demonstrated.  
- Methodology: Bilinear dynamics are sensible and yield exact control linearization but are established in prior work; this is a specific instantiation and multiscale stacking.  
- Methodology: Q-function approximator matches the servo objective enabling analytic minimization, but the α/b scaling trick is heuristic with no theoretical guarantees or sensitivity analysis.  
- Learned weights are global per-channel/scale, which may be too restrictive in cluttered scenes where state-dependent masks/attention could be needed.  
- Experiments strength: Good baseline comparisons, seen vs novel car analysis, clear sample-efficiency gains, and the insight that deep VGG layers may be too invariant for servoing.  
- Experiments limitation: Single synthetic task only—no real-robot or real-video tests and no other task domains (e.g., stationary objects, manipulation).  
- Experiments limitation: RL cost uses privileged ground-truth 3D car positions for shaping, termination, and FOV checks, which is unrealistic without detectors and not discussed.  
- Experiments limitation: Baseline fairness concerns—TRPO may be under-tuned, C-COT tracker speed/accuracy tradeoffs not fully addressed, and IBVS is given privileged depth/dynamics in variants.  
- Experiments limitation: Insufficient ablations—unclear roles of multiscale vs single-scale, local vs fully connected bilinear, semantics vs pixels with learned weights, and the α/b fitting step.  
- Experiments limitation: Generalization tests use the same renderer/background/lighting; out-of-domain robustness and stronger stress tests are missing.  
- Overall: Coherent and practical approach but moderate novelty and insufficient experimental evidence for ICLR.  
- Suggested improvement: Add thorough ablations for scale, bilinear variants, θ≥0 constraint, α/b step, and dynamics training data size.  
- Suggested improvement: Visualize and analyze learned weights by layer/channel and across target classes to interpret what the model emphasizes.  
- Suggested improvement: Clarify which bilinear variant is used per VGG layer, report parameter counts, and runtime per variant.  
- Suggested improvement: Discuss removing privileged cost signals—train with image-space surrogates, weak labels, or self-supervision and test FQI convergence.  
- Suggested improvement: Compare to a simple learned attention/mask trained with the same data but without RL to isolate the value of Q-weight fitting.  
- Suggested improvement: Strengthen baselines (better-tuned TRPO/DDPG/SAC, model-based video-prediction+MPC, photometric servoing with robust losses and pyramids).  
- Suggested improvement: Add at least one real-video or hardware-in-the-loop experiment to validate practical viability.  
- Question: Which dynamics variant (fully vs locally connected) is used for each VGG layer, and what parameterization is used when locally connected is infeasible?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the choice of pre-trained backbone (e.g., ResNet, smaller nets) and to pooling/dilation removals?  
- Question: How much does the α/b fitting step contribute to sample efficiency and stability; what happens if α=1,b=0?  
- Question: Are the learned weights state- or goal-dependent in any way, and would conditioning weights on (xt, x*) improve robustness?  
- Question: How would training proceed without access to ground-truth 3D positions for costs/termination; have image-based surrogates been tried?  
- Question: Can you report generalization to different backgrounds/lighting, different renderers, and other target classes (pedestrians, trucks)?  
- Question: What failure modes were observed (severe occlusions, large viewpoint changes), and how does performance degrade with compounding prediction error in the dynamics model?
