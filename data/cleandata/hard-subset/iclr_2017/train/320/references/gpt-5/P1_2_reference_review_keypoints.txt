- Paper proposes visual servoing using fixed pre-trained VGG features, a locally connected bilinear short-horizon feature-dynamics model, and a one-step analytic servo policy.  
- Feature/channel/scale weights and control regularization are learned via a linear Q-approximator and fitted Q-iteration, yielding a closed-form greedy servo action.  
- Method is sample-efficient: dynamics trained from ~100 trajectories, RL fine-tuned with ~20 trajectories, and generalizes to novel car appearances.  
- Empirically outperforms pixel-based servoing, IBVS with ORB/tracker, CEM/TRPO-tuned policy variants, and end-to-end CNN policies on a synthetic quadrotor car-following benchmark.  
- Ablations across VGG layers show mid-level conv4_3 best; deepest conv5_3 hurts due to excessive spatial invariance.  
- TRPO and CEM require far more samples and still underperform; tracker-based IBVS is competitive but slower and less robust on average.  
- Runtime is ~16 Hz, mainly limited by VGG feature extraction; code, simulator, and videos are released.  
- Decision: weak accept — simple, well-motivated combination gives strong gains in sample efficiency and robustness; empirical study is careful.  
- Strength: aligns pre-trained invariances, learned short-horizon dynamics, and RL selection of task-relevant features into a coherent, efficient pipeline.  
- Strength: analytic control via bilinear model Jacobian and linear Q-parameterization is elegant and computationally efficient.  
- Limitation: evaluation is entirely in simulation and uses privileged pose-based costs; real-world transfer and image-only rewards are not demonstrated.  
- Limitation: scope limited to car-following with fixed intrinsics/platform; broader task diversity would strengthen claims.  
- Limitation: lacks quantitative dynamics-model accuracy (one/multi-step errors) and deeper ablation on locally vs fully connected bilinear models and neighborhood sizes.  
- Limitation: stability/convergence far from goal and behavior under large motions or occlusions need analysis; multi-step MPC effects unexplored.  
- Baseline concerns: request for fairness checks — TRPO sensitivity and matched-sample comparisons, IBVS robustness to depth noise, and other ablation details.  
- Efficiency suggestion: consider lighter backbones (e.g., MobileNet), feature reuse across scales, and profiling to reduce VGG inference bottleneck.  
- Clarity edits requested: define symbols, tighten notation, and report standard deviations/confidence intervals on test costs.  
- Key questions for authors: how to obtain learning signal without ground-truth pose (image-based rewards), sensitivity to dynamics mismatch, visualization/interpretability of learned weights, generalization to other backbones and domain shifts, matched-sample TRPO/IBVS results, and detailed per-step runtime breakdown.
