1. Summary and contributions
The paper tackles visual servoing without hand-engineered features or calibrated feature Jacobians by combining (i) pretrained CNN features (VGG with modified pooling/dilation), (ii) a simple, multiscale bilinear predictive model for feature dynamics, and (iii) a fitted Q-iteration scheme that learns nonnegative feature weights so that a one-step lookahead, quadratic servo objective aligns with long-horizon returns. The controller is extremely simple: at each step, choose the control that minimizes the predicted weighted feature distance to the goal plus control regularization; thanks to the bilinear dynamics, this can be solved analytically. The Q-function class is chosen to be linear in the same weights, making fitted Q-iteration sample-efficient and stable. On a CitySim3D car-following benchmark, the method achieves robust tracking and generalizes to novel cars with only 20 RL trajectories to learn the weights (dynamics trained from 100 trajectories), outperforming pixel-based servoing, classic IBVS with ORB/tracking features (even with ground-truth depth), and model-free deep RL baselines, while being much more sample-efficient. Code and environment are released.

2. Decision: Accept
Key reasons:
- Novel and well-motivated integration: using pretrained features + bilinear feature dynamics + a Q-function tailored to the analytic servo objective is an elegant, bold idea that opens a promising direction for sample-efficient visuomotor control.
- Strong empirical signal for sample efficiency and robustness versus reasonable baselines, with careful ablations across feature layers and weighting methods.

3. Supporting arguments
- Problem significance: Visual servoing that is robust to distractors, viewpoint, and occlusion with minimal data is important. This work directly targets that regime.
- Originality: While pretrained features and video prediction are known, (a) treating deep feature maps as “images” with locally connected bilinear dynamics for action-conditional prediction and (b) designing a Q-approximator whose greedy action recovers the analytic servo controller are conceptually fresh. The α,b initialization trick for FQI within this structured class is neat and pragmatic.
- Sound motivation and positioning: The paper situates itself against IBVS/PBVS, photometric servoing, model-free deep RL, and recent prediction models. The choice of pretrained ImageNet features to induce invariances is well argued, and the multiscale design is justified and validated (conv4_3 emerges as best trade-off).
- Empirical support: 
  - Sample efficiency: Only 20 trajectories for RL (weights), orders of magnitude fewer than TRPO-based alternatives.
  - Generalization: Trained on a handful of cars, tested on novel cars; deeper features up to conv4_3 generalize better than pixels or conv5_3.
  - Ablations: FQI vs TRPO vs CEM; unweighted vs weighted; end-to-end policies vs dynamics-based; pixels vs multiple VGG layers. Results consistently support claims.
  - Baselines: Includes classical IBVS (with ground-truth depth) and tracking-based IBVS; proposed method still wins and runs faster than tracker-based approach.
- Clarity and reproducibility: Clear problem statement, algorithm, and open-source release.

4. Feedback for improvement (to help the authors; not blockers)
- Real-world validation: A small hardware demonstration (even quasi-static) would greatly strengthen the impact and address sim-to-real questions about feature dynamics, lighting, and calibration drift.
- Fairness/diagnostics for baselines: 
  - For TRPO-on-servo-params, report wall-clock compute and include learning curves beyond 50 iterations; try including the same α,b trick or other policy optimization algorithms (e.g., PPO) for completeness.
  - For tracker and IBVS, discuss sensitivity to depth noise and imperfect initialization. Including a depth-free IBVS variant and a better-tuned ORB/SIFT pipeline could clarify robustness.
- Cost/reward design: The RL cost uses ground-truth car pose in the simulator. Please discuss how this could be replaced with vision-only surrogates (e.g., learned car/keypoint detectors) to enable real deployment.
- Dynamics modeling: Provide error metrics for the bilinear model on held-out sequences and a study of planning horizon sensitivity (e.g., does two-step lookahead help?). A comparison to a small learned conv net dynamics (beyond bilinear) would contextualize the trade-off.
- Feature choices: You fix VGG weights after modifying pooling/dilation. A brief justification or small experiment comparing “freeze vs light fine-tuning” would be useful, especially given spatial invariance concerns for conv5_3.
- Regularization and sparsity: Since the learned weights act as feature selectors, consider L1 regularization or group-sparsity across channels/scales to improve interpretability and possibly performance.
- Stability/convergence: Expand on the theoretical intuition for the FQI procedure with the α,b step and nonnegativity constraints. Any guarantees or observed failure modes?

Questions for the authors
- How sensitive is performance to errors in the bilinear model? If you perturb dynamics or reduce the dynamics training set, how does servo performance degrade?
- Can the method handle targets with significant independent motion (e.g., higher-speed, lateral motion)? How much of that is absorbed by the action-independent bias term vs the weights?
- How are goal images chosen in practice for new targets? Can the system recover if the first frame is occluded or contains distractors? Any mechanism for online reweighting?
- Did you try L1 or other sparsity-inducing penalties on the weights to encourage automatic feature selection? If so, how did it affect sample efficiency and generalization?
- Could you comment on performance with different pretrained backbones (e.g., ResNet) or self-supervised features? Any indications that more modern, less invariant mid-level features improve generalization to novel targets?
- How brittle is the method to camera uncalibration or small intrinsics changes, given the locally connected dynamics assumption?

Overall, this is a compelling, novel direction that trades end-to-end training for structure and sample efficiency. Despite being sim-only, it is an impactful contribution for ICLR.
