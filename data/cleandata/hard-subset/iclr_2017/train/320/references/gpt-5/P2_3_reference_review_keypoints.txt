- Paper proposes visual servoing that combines modified pre-trained VGG features, a learned multiscale bilinear locally-connected short-horizon dynamics model, and sample-efficient RL (linear-in-weights Q-function via fitted Q-iteration) to learn per-channel/scale servoing weights.

- Empirical claim: on the CitySim3D quadcopter car-following benchmark the method outperforms pixel-based servoing, classical IBVS/tracker baselines, and TRPO, requires ≈20 RL rollouts, generalizes to novel cars, and code/simulator are released.

- Decision: Reject (borderline).

- Key reason 1: Evaluation is confined to a synthetic simulator and relies on ground-truth 3D car pose for the RL cost, raising questions about training/deployment with realistic signals or real hardware.

- Key reason 2: Sample-efficiency claims are misleading because the method uses substantial supervised data to train the dynamics model (100 trajectories, ~10k transitions) that the model-free baselines must learn end-to-end.

- Supporting: Problem and motivation are clear and well positioned in literature (IBVS/PBVS, deep RL, video prediction).

- Supporting: Technical approach is sensible—one-step feature-space lookahead with multiscale bilinear predictor, channel/scale weighting, Q-design aligning greedy action with servo objective, and α,b scaling to stabilize FQI.

- Supporting: Empirical ablations show conv1_2–conv4_3 features work best; comparisons to unweighted variants, CEM, TRPO, and classical baselines show gains; runtime ≈16 Hz versus slower baselines.

- Limitation: RL cost uses GT pose (not available in real settings) making reproducibility without simulator privileges unclear.

- Limitation: Single synthetic domain and task with no real-robot tests limits confidence under real-world photometric changes, calibration errors, or unmodeled dynamics.

- Limitation: Fairness of sample-efficiency comparisons—dynamics training data not counted; missing comparison to model-based baselines using the same dynamics.

- Limitation: No theoretical analysis of FQI convergence/stability or sensitivity analyses to bilinear predictor errors and multi-step model bias.

- Feedback: Report total environment interactions (dynamics + RL), and plot performance vs total samples; include wall-clock/compute costs.

- Feedback: Replace or complement GT-cost with realistic proxy rewards (tracker/detector/segmentation) or show robustness to noisy proxy rewards.

- Feedback: Add model-based baselines (e.g., MPC/iLQR/CEM using the same learned dynamics) to isolate benefits of dynamics vs Q-weight learning vs pre-trained features.

- Feedback: Provide variance/error bars, statistical tests, and failure-mode analyses (occlusion, distractors, lighting).

- Feedback: Expand ablations—locally connected vs fully connected bilinear, multiscale vs single-scale, conditioning weights on state vs fixed, sensitivity to action discretization and control penalties.

- Feedback: Consider a small real-world validation (e.g., table-top visual servo) to show feasibility without GT pose.

- Feedback: Discuss how rewards would be defined in practice and robustness when feature dynamics is misspecified or trained with limited data.

- Question 1: What is the total number of environment interactions including dynamics training, and how does the “two orders of magnitude” claim change when counting all data?

- Question 2: Can the RL stage learn effective weights when the reward is derived from tracker/detector signals instead of ground-truth pose; any preliminary results?

- Question 3: Are learned weights fixed across states or conditioned on observation/goal, and how sensitive is performance to domain shift?

- Question 4: How sensitive is performance to errors in the bilinear dynamics model; do multi-step rollout errors materially degrade servoing and can FQI compensate for model bias?

- Question 5: Did you evaluate MPC using the same learned dynamics and feature-space distance (e.g., CEM over actions), and how does it compare?

- Question 6: Please provide more details on stability/regularization of the α,b estimation during FQI and any failure modes encountered.
