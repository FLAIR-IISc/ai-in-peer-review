- Paper tackles sample-efficient visual servoing by combining pretrained VGG features, a bilinear predictive feature dynamics model, and RL to learn feature-channel-and-scale weights for control.  
- Central idea: servo in multiscale VGG feature space using bilinear dynamics and one-step lookahead to minimize weighted feature distance to a goal image.  
- Q-function is parameterized analytically (linear-in-parameters) so its greedy action matches the servoing action, enabling efficient fitted Q-iteration (FQI).  
- Technical elements: multiscale dilated VGG features, bilinear feature dynamics with exact linearization, linear Q-approximation, and a stabilized FQI routine enforcing nonnegativity and scaling.  
- Empirically, method learns effective policies with very few RL trajectories (~20) on a simulated quadrotor-car following benchmark (CitySim3D).  
- Outperforms pixel-based servoing, ORB-based IBVS, and model-free TRPO (pixel and feature) in both performance and sample efficiency.  
- Generalizes better than pixel methods to novel cars and runs in real time (~16 Hz); code and environment released.  
- Decision: Accept — novel, well-motivated combination of representation transfer, simple predictive dynamics, and tailored sample-efficient RL.  
- Paper positions itself well between classical IBVS/PBVS and modern deep RL, building on bilinear models and FQI.  
- Thorough empirical comparisons and ablations across feature layers, pixel vs VGG, connectivity variants, and several baselines.  
- Conv4_3 identified as the best tradeoff layer: spatial specificity and invariance balance.  
- Strength: elegant exploitation of problem structure leading to strong sample and compute efficiency.  
- Strength: practical pipeline with supervised dynamics training plus small-batch RL weights learning and open-source release.  
- Weakness: simulation-only evaluation and use of privileged ground-truth pose for RL cost limits real-world deployability claims.  
- Weakness: narrow task scope (single family: quadrotor car following) and unclear scalability to other tasks/targets without retuning.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of bilinear dynamics fidelity versus more expressive learned-dynamics/MPC or short-horizon planning.  
- Weakness: implementation clarity gaps (which bilinear variant per VGG layer, parameter counts, memory, local connectivity effects).  
- Key question: how would rewards be specified without ground-truth pose in real-world deployment; can cost be approximated in feature space?  
- Key question: which bilinear variant was used per VGG layer, and what are parameter counts/training times and stability differences?  
- Key question: did the authors try short-horizon planning (e.g., CEM) using learned dynamics in weighted feature space, and how does it compare?  
- Key question: sensitivity and transferability of learned weights θ across unseen targets and scenes?  
- Key question: dynamics data requirements — how does performance change if dynamics training data is reduced and is there model-bias compounding?  
- Suggestion: add a real-robot pilot or sim-to-real study and reward proxies that don’t rely on privileged pose.  
- Suggestion: include a short-horizon MPC baseline over learned dynamics for comparison and provide more architecture/parameter/training details and channel-weight visualizations.
