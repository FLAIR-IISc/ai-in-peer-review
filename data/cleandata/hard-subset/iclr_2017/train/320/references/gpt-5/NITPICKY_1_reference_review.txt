Summary and contributions
The paper tackles visual servoing by combining (i) fixed, pre-trained deep visual features (VGG-16 with modified pooling/dilation), (ii) a learned short-horizon bilinear feature-dynamics model, and (iii) a fitted Q-iteration (FQI) scheme that learns nonnegative weights over multiscale feature channels and control regularization. The resulting policy is a one-step lookahead that minimizes a weighted squared feature-distance to a goal image plus control costs; the Q-function is chosen to be linear in these weights, enabling efficient FQI with an analytic argmin over actions (quadratic in u due to linear feature dynamics). Experiments in a synthetic quadrotor–car-following environment (CitySim3D) show that: (a) mid-level VGG features (up to conv4_3) beat pixels and deeper conv5_3; (b) the proposed FQI weighting substantially outperforms unweighted policies and TRPO-tuned weights for the same dynamics; (c) end-to-end CNN policies require far more samples; (d) classical IBVS with ORB or with a tracker is worse (and slower) than the proposed method. The code and simulator are released.

Decision: Reject
Key reasons: (1) Numerous technical and presentation issues (inconsistent model specification, equation error, incomplete optimization details, bibliographic/formatting problems) that undermine clarity and reproducibility; (2) Experimental claims on sample efficiency and robustness are insufficiently controlled and rely on privileged signals and a substantial supervised dynamics dataset not accounted for in comparisons.

Supporting arguments
- Model inconsistency and missing details:
  - Section 4.2 proposes two bilinear variants (fully connected per-channel and locally connected), but Appendix C.2 states “We do not use the latter for the semantic features since they are too high-dimensional,” contradicting the earlier motivation that locality reduces parameters. It is unclear which variant is actually used for VGG features in the main results.
  - Critical hyperparameters are unspecified: neighborhood size nf, parameter counts, regularization choices for dynamics (weight decay is given, but no architecture-specific sizes), and which exact VGG layers are used at each pyramid level.
  - The action argmin assumes unconstrained quadratic minimization, yet actions are normalized to [-1, 1]. Is the solution clipped or box-constrained? This matters for stability and fairness in comparisons.
  - The nonnegativity constraints on θ (w, λ) are not operationalized (projected LS? NNLS? solver used?).

- FQI derivation and optimization issues:
  - Equation (5) includes an L2 penalty ν‖θ‖2 while optimizing over α and b. Since θ(k−1) is fixed in (5), the regularizer is constant and has no effect. Likely the intended regularizer was on αθ (i.e., να2‖θ‖2) or on α and b. As written, (5) is incorrect.
  - No discussion of the contraction/stability of the modified FQI with rescaling, nor of the effect of nonnegativity constraints on convergence.
  - The paper claims computational efficiency due to precomputable features φ, but does not report the complexity of the per-iteration inner solves (e.g., matrix sizes, time per LS), nor memory cost for locally connected bilinear models.

- Experimental design and claims:
  - Sample efficiency claims are framed around “20 trajectories” for RL, but the method leverages a substantial supervised dataset to train the dynamics (10,000 triplets, ~100 trajectories). The cost to collect this data, and its impact on performance, is not accounted for relative to model-free baselines. This makes the “two orders of magnitude” claim misleading.
  - The cost function used for RL requires privileged access to the car’s 3D position in the camera frame and FOV checks. This is not available in typical real-world settings and is not matched to baselines (e.g., the TRPO CNN also optimizes this privileged cost). The paper does not discuss how the approach would be trained without such privileged signals.
  - Statistical rigor: results are reported as average trajectory cost over 100 test cases with fixed sampled initializations shared across methods, but there are no standard deviations, confidence intervals, or multiple seeds. The validation procedure selects best parameters on 10 validation trajectories, raising concerns about overfitting to a small validation set.
  - Baselines: TRPO is tested, but not DDPG/SAC or modern model-based RL (e.g., PETS, MBPO-style) that could be more sample-efficient. The end-to-end CNN architecture is relatively small and unstable; no hyperparameter sensitivity or stronger vision backbones are explored. IBVS baselines receive ground-truth depth (generous) and a strong tracker, yet the proposed method benefits from privileged RL cost; fairness is not fully established.
  - Robustness claims (occlusions, lighting, viewpoint changes) are not quantitatively evaluated with controlled perturbations; evidence is anecdotal.

- Presentation/formatting issues:
  - Numerous bibliography artifacts (“?, \Q2015\E”, duplicated author-year formats, stray punctuation), typos (“standarized”), and footnote handling inconsistencies. Figures and tables are referenced but not clearly described in the text provided (e.g., Table 1/3 not visible).
  - The modified VGG with dilations reuses pretrained weights; the mapping from pooled to dilated layers should be explicitly detailed to ensure correctness.

Additional feedback (to improve the paper)
- Fix Eq. (5) (regularize αθ or explicitly state that the regularizer is constant and remove it), and fully specify the solvers for (5)/(6) under nonnegativity constraints.
- Clarify which bilinear model variant is used for each feature type, report nf, parameter counts, and memory/computation trade-offs. Provide ablations vs. fully connected vs. locally connected for semantic features if feasible.
- State clearly the action optimization (box constraints vs. clipping). Analyze how control constraints affect stability.
- Provide complete feature details: exact VGG layers used at each pyramid level, downsampling method to 32×32, standardization statistics, and which layers are concatenated. Include an ablation across layers and scales.
- Report results with multiple seeds and standard deviation; avoid using the same fixed initializations across all tests only.
- Account for all data when comparing sample efficiency (supervised dynamics data + RL trajectories). Add baselines with learned dynamics (e.g., simple linear/locally linear models on pixels) and stronger model-free methods (DDPG/SAC). Consider a model-based baseline that uses the same dynamics but optimizes directly long-horizon via shooting/MPC.
- Evaluate robustness to controlled occlusions, lighting, and distractors; visualize learned weights w across channels/scales to interpret what the policy focuses on.
- Discuss how to train without privileged costs; propose practical surrogates (e.g., image-based proxy rewards) or inverse RL.

Questions for the authors
1) Which bilinear dynamics variant is used for VGG features in the main results? What are nf, parameter counts, and training times per model?
2) How are nonnegativity constraints enforced in (5)/(6)? Which optimizer is used? Please correct Eq. (5) or justify the current regularization.
3) Is the action argmin box-constrained to [-1,1]? If so, how is it solved?
4) Precisely which VGG layers are included at each pyramid level? How are features downsampled and standardized?
5) How many training cars are used for dynamics learning (5 vs. more)? What is performance as a function of the size of the supervised dynamics dataset?
6) Can you provide results with multiple random seeds and confidence intervals, and controlled occlusion/light perturbations?
7) How would you train the weighting without access to ground-truth 3D costs in the real world?
