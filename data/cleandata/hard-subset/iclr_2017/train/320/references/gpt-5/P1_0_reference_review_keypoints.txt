- Paper combines pre-trained VGG multiscale features, bilinear predictive dynamics in feature space, and fitted Q-iteration to learn feature weights for one-step lookahead visual servoing.  
- Visual backbone: intermediate VGG-16 conv feature maps with reduced pooling and dilations to preserve spatial detail across scales.  
- Dynamics model: short-term bilinear (and locally connected) dynamics linear in control, enabling analytic Jacobians and exact linearization for fast action selection.  
- Control/RL: weighted multiscale quadratic servoing objective whose minimizer yields control; weights learned via sample-efficient FQI using a linear Q approximator aligned with the servo objective.  
- Empirical claim: effective quadcopter–car-following servo on CitySim3D using only 20 RL trajectories for weight learning, outperforming pixel-based, hand-crafted IBVS, and TRPO baselines.  
- Code and simulator are released, making experiments reproducible.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Elegant, well-motivated integration of pre-trained features, predictive dynamics, and structured Q-function yields strong sample efficiency.  
- Key reason 2: Thorough, fair empirical comparisons demonstrate superiority and computational efficiency.  
- Clear problem formulation: one-step predicted features minimizing distance to goal map is simple and effective; multiscale features provide robustness.  
- Novelty: novel combination of pre-trained CNN features as servo images, learned bilinear dynamics, and FQI-learned channel/scale weights with a policy-consistent linear Q-function.  
- Algorithmic soundness: bilinear model gives exact linearization; Q-function matches servo objective so greedy policy equals servo minimizer; two-stage FQI with α,b fitting improves stability.  
- Practical constraints: non-negativity on weights and invariance exploitation (scale/bias) are sensible design choices.  
- Empirical rigor: dynamics trained on 10k transitions with generic policy; RL uses only 20 trajectories — separating dynamics and RL data highlights small extra data needed for task adaptation.  
- Strong baselines: compared against multiple VGG layers, pixels, TRPO, CEM, end-to-end TRPO, classical IBVS/PBVS with favorable assumptions, and ablations.  
- Ablations: layer-wise performance trends and locally connected bilinear dynamics are analyzed and motivated.  
- Limitation: evaluation only in simulator; no real-robot experiments.  
- Limitation: RL cost uses privileged ground-truth car pose for training, which is unavailable at deployment.  
- Limitation: dynamics training requires 10k transitions — the “20 trajectories” claim applies only to RL phase, not total data.  
- Limitation: no formal convergence or stability guarantees; results are empirical.  
- Suggested improvements: explicitly report separate data budgets for dynamics learning, RL, and total compute/time to avoid confusion over the “20 trajectories” claim.  
- Suggested improvements: add real-world or sim-to-real evidence (hardware demo or domain randomization).  
- Suggested robustness ablations: sensitivity to VGG layer choice, multiscale composition, regularization ν, occlusions, lighting shifts, and calibration errors.  
- Suggested model comparisons: contrast bilinear dynamics with simpler affine or flow-based models and analyze the benefit of local connectivity.  
- Suggested reward work: discuss training without ground-truth pose (self-supervision, trackers, photometric or learned rewards).  
- Suggested analysis: inspect learned weights (channels/scales emphasized), qualitative failures, and reasons for conv5_3 underperformance.  
- Suggested efficiency reporting: provide per-step computational profile (feature extraction, dynamics, control solve) and options with lighter backbones.  
- Questions for authors: feasibility without ground-truth pose and potential proxy/self-supervised rewards?  
- Questions for authors: dynamics generalization across domain shifts, cities, weather, and camera intrinsics?  
- Questions for authors: interpretability of learned weights and correlation with semantics or motion sensitivity?  
- Questions for authors: importance of locally connected bilinear vs fully connected or linear models, and effect of removing action-independent term?  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to FQI iterations, exploration noise, regularization ν, and necessity of α,b pre-fit?  
- Questions for authors: attempts at end-to-end fine-tuning of VGG with dynamics or weights?  
- Questions for authors: main obstacles to real-world deployment (latency, calibration, depth/scale ambiguity) and mitigation strategies?
