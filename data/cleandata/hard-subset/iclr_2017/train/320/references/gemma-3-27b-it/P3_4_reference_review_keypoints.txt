- Paper proposes combining pre-trained VGG features, a learned bilinear dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration to learn visual servoing policies, achieving strong sample efficiency (≈20 trajectories).  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Novelty & motivation: The method is a novel, well-justified alternative to hand-engineered visual servoing and dynamics models.  
- Strong empirical results: Significant performance and sample-efficiency gains over pixel-based, hand-designed keypoint, and standard deep RL baselines on a synthetic car-following benchmark.  
- Theoretical justification: Bilinear dynamics and an analytical Q-function provide a tractable framework for fitted Q-iteration.  
- Reproducibility: Code and CitySim3D environment are publicly released.  
- Thorough comparison: Extensive baseline comparisons demonstrate the method’s advantages.  
- Request to discuss bilinear model limits: Need clarification on dynamics types the bilinear model cannot capture and the impact in complex scenarios.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Recommend discussing sensitivity to key hyperparameters (e.g., regularization ν, learning rate) and tuning strategy.  
- Real-world generalization: Experiments are synthetic; discuss challenges and necessary modifications for transfer to real-world robots (lighting, occlusion, complex dynamics).  
- Ablation on feature levels: Deeper VGG features usually help but conv5_3 underperforms—require deeper ablation to explain layer-wise trade-offs and role of spatial resolution/receptive field.  
- Clarify cost function: Provide more detail on term definitions and how relative weighting between position and terminal costs is chosen or learned.  
- Questions for authors: (1) Elaborate bilinear model capacity limits, (2) describe hyperparameter tuning and ν sensitivity, (3) outline obstacles for real-world transfer and needed extensions, (4) explain why conv5_3 performs worse and relation to spatial resolution/receptive field, (5) clarify how cost function weights were determined or learned.
