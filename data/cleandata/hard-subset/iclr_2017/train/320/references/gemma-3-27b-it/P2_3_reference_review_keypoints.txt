- Proposes a sample-efficient visual servoing method combining pre-trained VGG features, a locally connected bilinear dynamics model, and Fitted Q-Iteration.
- Demonstrates strong performance on a synthetic car-following benchmark, outperforming pixel-based, keypoint-based, and standard deep RL baselines.
- Code and environment are publicly available, supporting reproducibility.
- Novelty: tackles reliance on hand-designed features by leveraging pre-trained features and learned dynamics for sample efficiency.
- Technical soundness: bilinear dynamics with Q-function approximation and Fitted Q-Iteration is well-motivated and clearly explained.
- Empirical validation: comprehensive experiments and ablations show large gains in sample efficiency (≈ two orders of magnitude) and robust comparisons.
- Suggests exploring further model simplification (pruning, dimensionality reduction) and discussing trade-offs between accuracy and computational cost.
- Notes limitation of synthetic-only experiments and requests discussion of real-world generalization challenges (domain adaptation, sensor noise).
- Recommends a more detailed account of hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity to ensure broader applicability.
- Requests additional visualizations of the learned dynamics (e.g., predicted vs. actual feature maps) to build intuition.
- Questions choice of VGG features and whether other pre-trained networks (e.g., ResNet) were evaluated.
- Asks why deeper VGG features (conv5_3) performed worse—possible limits of the bilinear model capturing long-range dependencies.
- Queries whether the bilinear model’s local filter size was tuned and how sensitive performance is to that parameter.
- Requests discussion of anticipated real-world deployment challenges and mitigation strategies for robotic systems.
- Asks about the role and tuning of regularization ν in Fitted Q-Iteration and the impact of setting ν to zero.
