- Paper proposes sample-efficient visual servoing by combining pre-trained visual features, a learned bilinear dynamics model, and Fitted Q-Iteration for fast target following.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Problem significance: Addresses an important, practical visual servoing challenge requiring sample-efficient adaptation.  
- Novelty: Novel combination of transfer-learned features, simplified bilinear dynamics, and FQI to reduce sample complexity.  
- Experimental validation: Strong results showing large gains in sample efficiency (≈100×) over standard deep RL and image-based baselines.  
- Ablations: Feature representation ablations provide useful insight into component contributions.  
- Reproducibility: Code and supplementary materials provided, supporting reproducibility.  
- Dynamics model limitation: Need clearer discussion and analysis of when the bilinear dynamics assumption fails and its impact.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: More detail needed on sensitivity to regularization (ν) and whether systematic hyperparameter sweeps were performed.  
- Generalization: Request for more rigorous quantification of generalization to unseen cars and discussion of factors affecting generalization.  
- Computational cost: Ask for formal comparison of training and inference time and resource usage versus baselines.  
- Related work: Suggest expanding related work to include recent neural dynamics-modeling approaches for control.  
- Q1 for authors: Provide examples/quantitative analysis of errors by the bilinear dynamics model and failure modes.  
- Q2 for authors: Describe hyperparameter tuning for FQI, sensitivity to ν, and results of any searches.  
- Q3 for authors: Plans to more systematically evaluate generalization across textures, lighting, or different object types?  
- Q4 for authors: Provide detailed computational-resource information (GPU type, training time, inference speed) compared to baselines.
