- Tackles visual servoing (target following) using pretrained visual features, a bilinear dynamics model, and Fitted Q-Iteration for sample-efficient learning.  
- Key contribution: a sample-efficient visual servo that adapts quickly to new targets with far less data than pixel-based or standard deep RL methods.  
- Empirical result: strong performance on a synthetic car-following benchmark, outperforming pixel- and keypoint-based methods and standard model-free RL (e.g., TRPO).  
- Uses pretrained VGG features and a locally connected bilinear dynamics model to predict visual dynamics.  
- Ablation studies compare different feature representations and show generalization to novel cars.  
- Code and environment are publicly available, supporting reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Strength: Clear motivation emphasizing sample efficiency and limitations of hand-designed features in traditional visual servoing.  
- Strength: Novel and technically sound combination of pretrained features, bilinear dynamics, and Fitted Q-Iteration.  
- Strength: Convincing empirical validation and insightful ablations demonstrating sample-efficiency gains.  
- Strength: Well-written and logically structured manuscript.  
- Suggestion: Provide a detailed discussion of bilinear model limitations and scenarios where it may fail.  
- Suggestion: Include a sensitivity analysis for key hyperparameters (learning rate, regularization, exploration noise).  
- Suggestion: Add qualitative visualizations (trajectories, feature activations) and examples of successes/failures.  
- Suggestion: Provide a detailed computational cost breakdown for feature extraction, dynamics prediction, and Q-iteration; discuss scaling with image resolution and VGG size.  
- Suggestion: Discuss generalization to other target types (pedestrians, drones) and required modifications.  
- Questions: Why choose VGG specifically and how do other pretrained networks (e.g., ResNet) affect performance?  
- Questions: How sensitive is performance to local neighborhood size (nf) in the locally connected bilinear model?  
- Questions: How was the discount factor (γ) chosen and how does it affect learning?  
- Questions: Why did deeper VGG features (conv5_3) perform worse—too invariant or other causes; could attention help?  
- Questions: How was the regularization coefficient (ν) in Fitted Q-Iteration selected and what is its practical impact?
