- Paper tackles visual servoing (target following) using pre-trained VGG features, a bilinear feature-dynamics model, and Fitted Q-Iteration for sample-efficient policy learning; code/env released.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions due to relevance, novelty, and strong empirical evidence.
- Strongly motivated: clearly frames limitations of hand-engineered methods and the need for sample-efficient learning for rapid target adaptation.
- Novel combination: merging pre-trained features, bilinear dynamics, and Fitted Q-Iteration is novel and technically well-justified.
- Simplified Q-function approximation and bilinear dynamics rationale are reasonable and grounded in prior work.
- Compelling empirical results: substantial improvements over pixel-based, keypoint, and standard RL baselines, with two orders of magnitude better sample efficiency.
- Extensive comparisons and ablation studies strengthen the validity of results.
- Reproducibility: public code and CitySim3D environment facilitate verification and follow-up work.
- Suggestion: provide a clearer explanation or visual illustration of why the bilinear model approximates feature dynamics well compared to simpler linear models.
- Suggestion: include hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (e.g., regularization ν, dynamics learning rate) to assess robustness.
- Suggestion: discuss sim-to-real transfer challenges and strategies for generalization to real-world data.
- Suggestion: visualize learned feature weights (w) to improve interpretability of what the controller prioritizes.
- Suggestion: report and analyze failure cases or limitations to demonstrate critical understanding.
- Question: why did the deepest VGG feature (conv5_3) perform worse—due to spatial invariance, pooling-induced information loss, or other factors?
- Question: how was the regularization coefficient (ν) chosen, and how do different values affect learning and final performance?
- Question: how sensitive is performance to the initial state distribution used during RL training?
- Question: have alternative, less expensive backbones (e.g., MobileNet) been explored to trade off performance and computation?
- Question: how would the proposed method perform without ground-truth depth (e.g., with monocular depth estimates) compared to IBVS methods?
