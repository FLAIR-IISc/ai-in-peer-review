- Proposes a sample-efficient visual servoing method combining pre-trained VGG features, a bilinear feature dynamics model, and Fitted Q-Iteration, adapting to new targets with ~20 trajectories.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on a synthetic car-following benchmark, outperforming pixel- and keypoint-based baselines and standard deep RL by ~two orders of magnitude in sample efficiency.  
- Ablation studies show the importance of feature selection and validate design choices.  
- Methodology is well-justified: bilinear model for short-term visual dynamics and linear Q-function approximation for sample-efficient learning.  
- Clear motivation and comprehensive placement within related work, highlighting limitations of prior hand-designed features.  
- Code and CitySim3D environment are publicly released, enabling reproducibility.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions due to strong results and practical impact.  
- Request more discussion on bilinear model limitations, especially in complex or highly non-linear scenes and moving-object scenarios.  
- Ask for a detailed hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (learning rates, regularization ν, exploration noise) and summary table.  
- Suggest discussing generalization to other visual servoing tasks (e.g., grasping, manipulation) beyond car following.  
- Recommend visualizations of learned VGG feature weights/activation maps to clarify which features drive performance.  
- Advise briefly comparing or discussing applicability of recent sample-efficient RL approaches (offline RL, model-based RL).  
- Question: Provide examples and limits of how the bilinear model handles different object motions.  
- Question: Was the poorer performance of deeper VGG features (conv5_3) consistent across hyperparameters and seeds, and is it due to loss of spatial information?  
- Question: Provide a computational cost breakdown for feature extraction, dynamics prediction, and FQI.  
- Question: How sensitive is FQI performance to weight initialization and random seeds; was variability observed?
