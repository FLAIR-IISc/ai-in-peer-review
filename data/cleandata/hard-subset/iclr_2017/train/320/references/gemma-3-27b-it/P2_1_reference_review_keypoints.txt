- Method: combines pre-trained VGG features, a learned bilinear dynamics model, and Fitted Q-Iteration for visual servoing (target following).  
- Claim: approach achieves strong sample efficiency on a synthetic car-following benchmark and outperforms model-free deep RL and traditional methods.  
- Reproducibility: authors release code and data.  
- Decision: reviewer recommends accept — novel, effective approach addressing sample efficiency.  
- Motivation: clearly argues limitations of hand-designed features and need for quick adaptation to new targets.  
- Novelty: bilinear dynamics to predict feature changes and adapted FQI enable efficient policy learning.  
- Empirical strength: substantial improvements over baselines (pixels, hand-designed keypoints, standard deep RL) and insightful comparison to tracker-based IBVS.  
- Presentation: paper is well-structured and easy to follow.  
- Ablation gap: needs a more detailed ablation study to quantify contributions of pre-trained features, bilinear dynamics, and FQI, and impact of different VGG layers.  
- Real-world generalization: experiments limited to synthetic data; discussion or evaluation on real-world datasets is needed.  
- Computational analysis: missing detailed training/inference time and computational cost breakdown.  
- FQI explanation: adaptations (linear Q approximator, weighting) require more intuition and detail.  
- Dynamics limitation question: bilinear model may be insufficient for complex dynamics; consider RNNs or more sophisticated models.  
- VGG depth question: deepest features (conv5_3) lose spatial info—consider combining deep features with spatial attention.  
- Cost sensitivity question: performance may depend on cost function design; need experiments with alternative costs.  
- Real-world robustness question: ask how method would handle lighting changes, occlusions, and viewpoint variation.  
- Regularization question: request explanation and sensitivity analysis for FQI regularization coefficient ν.
