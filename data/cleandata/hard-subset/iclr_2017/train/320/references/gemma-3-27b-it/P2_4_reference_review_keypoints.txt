- Approach: Combines pre-trained VGG features, a learned bilinear dynamics model, and Fitted Q-Iteration for sample-efficient visual servoing.  
- Experimental setup: Evaluated on a synthetic car-following benchmark with public code and CitySim3D environment.  
- Decision: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Motivation: Addresses reliance on hand-engineered features and emphasizes practical sample efficiency for adapting to new targets.  
- Technical soundness: Bilinear dynamics and FQI are well-justified; linearization used for efficient optimization.  
- Empirical results: Strong improvements over pixel-, keypoint-based methods and standard deep RL; good generalization to unseen cars.  
- Ablations: Analysis comparing different VGG layer features shows which representations work better.  
- Comparison to TRPO: FQI demonstrates substantially better sample efficiency.  
- Reproducibility: Public code and environment enhance credibility and reproducibility.  
- Limitation — simulation: Method only evaluated in simulation; real-world issues (sensor noise, lighting, occlusions) need discussion.  
- Limitation — camera assumptions: Assumes known camera parameters; robustness to calibration errors should be addressed.  
- Visualization request: More visualizations suggested (e.g., learned weights w and λ, weighted feature activations) to improve interpretability.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Recommend reporting sensitivity to key hyperparameters (regularization ν, dynamics learning rate).  
- Related work: Suggest expanding discussion of recent model-based RL and meta-learning literature for context.  
- Questions for authors: Explain why deeper VGG (conv5_3) performed worse and provide quantitative analysis; compare bilinear dynamics to alternatives (e.g., RNNs); report robustness to imperfect camera calibration; provide end-to-end runtime for the learned servoing policy.
