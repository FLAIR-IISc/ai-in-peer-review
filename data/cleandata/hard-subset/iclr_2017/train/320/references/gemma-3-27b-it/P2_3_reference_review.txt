## ICLR 2024 Review

**Paper Title:** Learning Visual Servoing with Reinforcement Learning and Predictive Dynamics

**1. Summary:**

This paper tackles the problem of visual servoing, specifically target following, by combining learned visual features from pre-trained networks (VGG), a learned bilinear dynamics model, and reinforcement learning (Fitted Q-Iteration). The authors aim to develop a sample-efficient visual servoing approach that can quickly adapt to new targets with limited data. They demonstrate strong performance on a synthetic car-following benchmark, significantly outperforming baseline methods including pixel-based servoing, hand-designed keypoints, and standard deep RL algorithms. The code and environment are publicly available, facilitating reproducibility.

**2. Decision:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. The paper presents a novel and effective approach to visual servoing that addresses a key challenge – sample efficiency – while achieving strong results. The combination of pre-trained features, learned dynamics, and a carefully chosen RL algorithm is compelling, and the experimental results convincingly demonstrate the benefits of the proposed method.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

*   **Novelty and Motivation:** The paper addresses a clear limitation of traditional visual servoing methods – reliance on hand-designed features and domain knowledge.  The motivation for using pre-trained features and learning dynamics is well-articulated, and the goal of sample efficiency is highly relevant.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed method – a bilinear dynamics model coupled with Fitted Q-Iteration – appears technically sound. The explanation of the Q-function approximation and the optimization process is clear. The use of a locally connected bilinear model seems like a sensible approach to manage the complexity of the dynamics model.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results are comprehensive and convincing. The comparisons against a diverse set of baselines (pixel-based, keypoint-based, and standard deep RL) clearly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed approach. The ablation studies comparing different feature representations and learning algorithms further strengthen the findings. The improvement in sample efficiency – two orders of magnitude over standard deep RL – is particularly noteworthy.
*   **Reproducibility:**  The public availability of the code and environment is a significant strength, enabling easy verification and extension of the work.

**4. Additional Feedback:**

*   **Dynamics Model Complexity:** While the locally connected bilinear model is a good step toward reducing complexity, further exploration of model simplification techniques (e.g., pruning, dimensionality reduction) could be considered.  A discussion of the trade-offs between model accuracy and computational cost would be valuable.
*   **Generalization to Real-World Data:** The experiments are conducted in a synthetic environment. While this allows for controlled evaluation, it's important to acknowledge the limitations. A discussion of potential challenges and strategies for generalizing to real-world scenarios (e.g., domain adaptation, robustness to sensor noise) would be beneficial.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:**  The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the hyperparameter tuning process.  Were any specific hyperparameters particularly sensitive to performance?  Could these sensitivities limit the applicability of the method?
*   **Visualizations:** While sample trajectories are shown, more visualizations of the learned dynamics model (e.g., showing predicted vs. actual feature maps) could help to build intuition and understand the model's behavior.

**5. Questions for the Authors:**

*   Regarding the choice of VGG features: Were other pre-trained networks (e.g., ResNet) considered? If so, why was VGG ultimately chosen?
*   The paper mentions that deeper VGG features (conv5\_3) performed worse. Can you elaborate on why you believe this is the case? Is it a limitation of the bilinear dynamics model’s ability to capture long-range dependencies, or something else?
*   The local connectivity of the bilinear model is defined by a filter size. Was this parameter tuned, and how sensitive is the performance to its value?
*   Regarding real-world deployment: What specific challenges do you anticipate when transferring this method to a real-world robotic system, and what steps would you take to address them?
*   You mention regularization (ν) in the FQI algorithm. What is the practical impact of setting ν to zero, and how did you determine the optimal value of ν?



Overall, this is a strong paper with significant potential. The combination of techniques presented is innovative, the results are compelling, and the public availability of resources promotes reproducibility. Addressing the suggested feedback would further enhance the quality and impact of the work.
