- Proposes combining pre-trained VGG features, a learned bilinear dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration to enable sample-efficient visual servoing (target following).  
- Evaluated on a synthetic car-following benchmark and shows significant improvements, notably an impressive 20-sample learning curve.  
- Decision: Accept — novel, effective approach with clear presentation and available code/videos.  
- Novelty: Uses transfer learning to avoid hand-crafted features and reduce data requirements for visual servoing.  
- Technical soundness: Bilinear dynamics, linearization for optimization, and fitted Q-iteration for feature weighting are reasonable and well-justified.  
- Empirical validation: Strong comparisons to pixel/keypoint baselines and SOTA deep RL, plus ablation studies supporting claims.  
- Reproducibility: Code, simulation environment, and supplementary videos provided.  
- Limitation: Bilinear dynamics may fail with non-rigid object motion or large viewpoint changes; failure cases need analysis.  
- Generalization concern: All experiments are synthetic — discuss challenges for real-world transfer (lighting, sensor noise, imperfect dynamics).  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Results lack discussion on sensitivity to learning rate, regularization, and exploration noise.  
- Computational cost: Needs detailed breakdown of runtime/compute for feature extraction, dynamics prediction, and FQI.  
- Questions for authors: Why does conv5_3 underperform across targets? Were more complex dynamics (e.g., RNNs) explored? What real-world adaptations are needed? How was FQI regularization ν chosen and how sensitive are results?
