- Problem and approach: Learn visual servoing policies from images by combining pre-trained VGG features, a learned bilinear dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration for sample-efficient target following; evaluated in a synthetic car-following benchmark with released code.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — novel, well-motivated, and empirically strong.  
- Novelty & motivation: The fusion of pre-trained features, learned dynamics, and FQI addresses limits of hand-engineered methods and enables rapid adaptation to new targets.  
- Technical soundness: Bilinear dynamics and FQI are well-justified; linearization for policy optimization is practical and clearly explained.  
- Empirical validation: Strong improvements over baselines (pixels, keypoints, standard deep RL) and demonstrated generalization to unseen cars.  
- Reproducibility: Code and environment are publicly available, supporting reproducibility.  
- Dynamics model limitation: Request more discussion on bilinear model limitations, alternatives (e.g., deeper neural dynamics), and scaling with environment complexity.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Ask for detailed sensitivity analysis of FQI hyperparameters (regularization, learning rate, etc.).  
- Real-world transfer: Experiments are synthetic—need discussion of challenges and future work for real-world transfer (sensor noise, lighting, imperfect dynamics).  
- Ablation study: Recommend expanding ablations to isolate the contributions of pre-trained features, dynamics model, and FQI.  
- Question on prediction horizon: How long is the bilinear model accurate for, and have techniques like MPC been explored to extend horizon?  
- Question on VGG modifications: Why remove some max-pooling layers and add dilated convolutions, and what was the empirical impact?  
- Question on FQI initialization and cost: How sensitive is FQI to weight initialization, were alternatives tried, and what is the computational cost vs end-to-end deep RL?  
- Question on real-world deployment: What are the key challenges and required modifications to transfer this approach to real robotic systems?
