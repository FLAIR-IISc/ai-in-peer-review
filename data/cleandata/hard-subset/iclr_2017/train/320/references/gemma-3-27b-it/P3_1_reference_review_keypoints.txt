- Paper addresses visual servoing (target following) by combining pre-trained VGG features, a bilinear feature-dynamics model, and Fitted Q-Iteration for sample-efficient policy learning.  
- Key contribution: fast, sample-efficient visual servo policy that adapts to new targets with little data.  
- Strong empirical results on a simulated car-following benchmark, outperforming conventional IBVS and model-free deep RL (e.g., TRPO).  
- Code and simulation environment are publicly released, aiding reproducibility.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Clear motivation: replaces hand-designed features and domain knowledge with learned features for practical robotic systems.  
- Novel and technically sound combination of pre-trained features, bilinear dynamics, and fitted Q-iteration.  
- Bilinear dynamics model is simple, efficient, and well-motivated, but its limits need discussion.  
- Compelling experimental comparisons and ablations that support sample-efficiency claims.  
- Suggest adding a deeper discussion of real-world transfer challenges (lighting, sensor noise, imperfect dynamics).  
- Recommend justifying or empirically probing why the bilinear assumption holds (and where it fails).  
- Ask for hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (e.g., regularization ν, dynamics learning rate) to show robustness.  
- Suggest visualizing learned feature weights (heatmaps) to show which features are prioritized and robustness to distractors.  
- Request an ablation specifically removing the dynamics model to quantify its contribution versus using only pre-trained features + RL.  
- Questions for authors: steps for real-world deployment, bilinear-model failure modes, reasons deeper VGG features perform worse, scalability of Fitted Q-Iteration with feature dimensionality, and exploration of alternative cost functions (velocity/acceleration terms).
