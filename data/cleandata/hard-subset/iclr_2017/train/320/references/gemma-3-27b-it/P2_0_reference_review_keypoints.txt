- Paper tackles visual servoing (target following) using pre-trained VGG features, a bilinear feature-dynamics model, and Fitted Q-Iteration, evaluated on a synthetic car-following benchmark with code released.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Novelty: Compelling combination of pre-trained features, learned dynamics, and RL to replace hand-engineered features.  
- Empirical results: Strong quantitative gains, including ~100× sample efficiency improvement over standard deep RL and outperforming traditional IBVS.  
- Reproducibility: Code and CitySim3D environment publicly available.  
- Technical soundness: Bilinear dynamics and linearization are justified and enable computational efficiency; Fitted Q-Iteration explanation is clear.  
- Evaluation: Comprehensive comparisons and ablation studies across baselines.  
- Limitations: Need more discussion on behavior in complex, cluttered, or real-world scenarios and generalization to varied targets/environments.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Request analysis of sensitivity to key hyperparameters (e.g., regularization ν, learning rate).  
- Visualization: Recommend visualizing learned bilinear dynamics (how features change with actions) for interpretability.  
- Action space clarification: Request more detail on action-space dimensions and ranges.  
- Generalization question: Need details on how the “novel car” set was constructed and whether those cars were completely unseen during training.  
- Real-world applicability question: Ask what challenges and modifications are required to deploy on real robotic platforms.  
- Dynamics model choice question: Why choose locally connected bilinear over fully connected—what trade-offs in expressiveness vs performance?  
- Reward function question: Were alternative reward formulations tested and how sensitive is performance to the choice of cost/reward?
