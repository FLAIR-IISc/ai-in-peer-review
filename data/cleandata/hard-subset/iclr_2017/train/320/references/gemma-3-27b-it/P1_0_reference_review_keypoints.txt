- Problem & approach: Combines pre-trained VGG features, a learned bilinear dynamics model, and Fitted Q-Iteration for sample-efficient visual servoing (target following).
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Empirical result: Demonstrates strong performance and ~two orders of magnitude improvement in sample efficiency over standard deep RL.
- Comparison: Outperforms pixel-based and keypoint-based visual servoing, highlighting benefits of pre-trained features.
- Motivation: Well-motivated use of pre-trained features, bilinear dynamics, and fitted Q-iteration for efficient weight learning.
- Novelty: Novel combination of components focused on quick adaptation to new targets.
- Reproducibility: Code and the CitySim3D synthetic environment are provided.
- Real-world limitations: Needs discussion of transfer challenges (lighting, sensor noise, imperfect dynamics).
- Ablation detail: Request more granular ablation (e.g., bilinear model vs. simpler dynamics).
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Ask for analysis of sensitivity to hyperparameters (learning rate, regularization, etc.).
- Visualizations: Recommend visualizations of learned feature weights (e.g., heatmaps).
- Dynamics model alternatives: Suggest brief comparison to other dynamics models (e.g., neural networks).
- Real-world transfer question: What modifications are needed to deploy in real robotics settings?
- Feature tuning question: Have the authors tried fine-tuning VGG during RL and what are the trade-offs?
- Computational cost question: Request breakdown of computational cost for dynamics learning and Q-iteration.
- Scaling & generalization questions: How does the method scale to higher-dimensional features and generalize to different target types?
