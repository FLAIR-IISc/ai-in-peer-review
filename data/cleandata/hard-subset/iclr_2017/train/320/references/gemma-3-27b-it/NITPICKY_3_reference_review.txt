This paper proposes a visual servoing method combining pre-trained features (VGG), a bilinear dynamics model, and reinforcement learning (Fitted Q-Iteration) to achieve sample-efficient target following. The core idea is to learn weights for the VGG features to focus on relevant visual information, allowing for robust servoing with minimal data. The authors demonstrate promising results on a synthetic car following benchmark, showcasing improved performance and sample efficiency compared to baseline methods.

**Reject.** While the presented approach demonstrates potential and achieves promising results, the paper suffers from a concerning number of minor flaws in presentation, technical detail, and experimental justification, ultimately hindering a confident assessment of its contribution. The level of polish expected for ICLR is not met.

The primary issues stem from a lack of clarity in several methodological choices, insufficient justification for design decisions, and a somewhat superficial comparison to existing work. While the authors demonstrate performance gains, the reasons behind *why* their method succeeds are not convincingly articulated. 

Specifically:

1. **Bilinear Dynamics Model:** The motivation for the locally connected bilinear model isn't sufficiently explained. The authors state it reduces parameters, but don't quantify this reduction or present any ablation studies comparing it to the fully connected version on this task. The choice of filter size (nf x nf) for the local connections is arbitrary and lacks justification. Why was this particular size chosen? 

2. **Fitted Q-Iteration Implementation:** The explanation of the FQI algorithm, especially the parameter initialization step (5), is convoluted and difficult to follow. The regularization term (ν) is mentioned but its impact isn’t analyzed. Ablation studies exploring different regularization strengths would be valuable. What is the sensitivity of the method to this hyperparameter? The claim of computational efficiency is made without concrete timing comparisons against TRPO.

3. **Experimental Details & Baselines:** The description of the experimental setup lacks crucial details. For example, the noise level added during the exploration policy isn't consistent across all experiments. The comparison to TRPO feels incomplete. The TRPO policies were trained for different iterations (2 vs 50) – a more fair comparison would involve tuning both methods for optimal performance. The baseline methods (IBVS, CNNs) lack sufficient depth of analysis. The statement that VGG features aren't optimized for servoing is a generalization that could be further substantiated with more detailed experiments. The reported performance gaps need to be scrutinized—are they statistically significant?

4. **Feature Selection & Multiscale Representation:** The rationale for using features up to VGG conv4_3 but not conv5_3 is weak.  The hypothesis that conv5_3 lacks spatial information is plausible, but requires empirical verification. What is the spatial resolution of the features being used at each layer? How does this affect the dynamics model and the subsequent control?

5. **Formatting and Clarity:** Throughout the paper, the writing is sometimes imprecise. For example, there are numerous instances where mathematical notation could be clarified (e.g. Equation 3).  The reference list is long but lacks consistent formatting. There are several references to online code/videos that are essential for reproducibility. A more robust method for versioning and ensuring accessibility of these resources should be considered.



**Additional Feedback (for Improvement):**

* **Ablation Studies:** Conduct more comprehensive ablation studies. Analyze the impact of different feature levels, dynamics model variations, regularization strengths, and FQI parameters.
* **Statistical Significance:** Provide statistical significance testing for all experimental results.
* **Computational Cost:** Include a detailed breakdown of the computational cost of each method (training and inference time).
* **Error Analysis:**  Provide a qualitative error analysis. Show examples of failure cases and discuss the limitations of the proposed approach.
* **Theoretical Justification:** Strengthen the theoretical justification for the bilinear dynamics model and the Q-function approximation.
* **Reproducibility:** Ensure all code and data are readily available and well-documented to facilitate reproducibility.




**Questions for the Authors:**

1. Could you provide a more detailed justification for the choice of the locally connected bilinear model, including a parameter count comparison to the fully connected version and any empirical results comparing their performance on this task?
2.  What is the sensitivity of the algorithm to the regularization coefficient (ν) in the FQI implementation?
3. Why was the exploration noise set to a different level in different experiments? 
4.  What metrics were used to determine the optimal number of training iterations for the TRPO baselines, and how would the results change if both TRPO and FQI were tuned for the same level of performance?
5.  Can you provide examples of failure cases and discuss the limitations of the proposed approach in handling these scenarios?
6. What is the spatial resolution of the features being used at each level (l) of the multiscale pyramid? How does this affect the performance of the dynamics model and the subsequent control?
