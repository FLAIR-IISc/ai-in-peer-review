- Tackles visual servoing (target following) by combining pre-trained VGG features, learned bilinear feature dynamics, and Fitted Q-Iteration for sample-efficient policy learning.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Key claim: the combination yields much greater sample efficiency than image-based, hand-designed, or model-free RL baselines on a synthetic car-following benchmark.  
- Empirical strength: experiments show clear performance gains and up to two orders of magnitude improvement in sample efficiency.  
- Technical approach: bilinear dynamics model + fitted Q-iteration to learn feature weights is well-motivated and tractable.  
- Reproducibility: authors provide code and the CitySim3D dataset.  
- Request clearer justification of why a bilinear model suitably captures camera/visual feature dynamics in this context.  
- Recommend additional ablation studies (e.g., remove bilinear model or simplify Q-weighting) to isolate contributions.  
- Ask for expanded discussion on generalization limits—sensitivity to lighting, occlusion, viewpoint, and other visual variations.  
- Request analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (learning rate, regularization, etc.).  
- Request clearer reporting of computational cost: training time and inference speed versus baselines.  
- Question why a locally connected bilinear model was chosen over a fully connected one and its performance impact.  
- Ask how performance scales with environment complexity (distractors, clutter).  
- Request elaboration on the design of the image-coordinate distance cost term and how it promotes effective following.  
- Question potential bias from using identical initial states across test car sets and whether randomized initial states would alter conclusions.  
- Ask about extending the method to higher-DOF robotic platforms and necessary modifications.
