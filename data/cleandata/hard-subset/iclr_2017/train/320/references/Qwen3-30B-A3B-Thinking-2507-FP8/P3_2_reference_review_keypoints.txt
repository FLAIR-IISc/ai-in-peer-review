- Paper proposes combining ImageNet-pretrained VGG features, a bilinear visual-dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration to enable sample-efficient visual servoing.
- Key claim: the method learns effective servoing with ~20 policy training trajectories and outperforms pixel- or hand-crafted-feature approaches.
- Evaluated on a synthetic car-following benchmark showing robustness to visual variation, viewpoint changes, and occlusions.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Main reasons for acceptance: meaningful problem, novel and well-motivated combination of techniques, and rigorous experimental evaluation against multiple baselines.
- Technical strength: insight that pre-trained features + simple bilinear dynamics + sample-efficient RL suffice for visual servoing.
- Empirical strength: thorough comparisons (IBVS with ORB, C-COT, TRPO, position-based methods) showing superior performance and sample efficiency.
- Most compelling evidence: FQI with conv4_3 reaches low cost with 20 trajectories, while TRPO needs ~500 trajectories to reach worse performance.
- Issue: the "more than two orders of magnitude" sample-efficiency claim is misleading; observed gap is closer to ~25x, not 100x.
- Suggestion: better explain VGG modifications (removing pooling, using dilated convs) and how they preserve spatial info for servoing.
- Suggestion: more explicit discussion of real-world limitations (need for pretrained VGG, domain shift from simulation).
- Clarify total data needs: dynamics model used 100 trajectories plus 20 for policy — clarify the combined requirement.
- Questions for authors: why conv4_3 outperforms conv5_3 (loss of spatial detail vs. semantic invariance); quantitative generalization to novel cars vs. training cars.
- Question: how method compares to depth-free classical IBVS or pixel-only approaches in real-world settings where depth is unavailable.
