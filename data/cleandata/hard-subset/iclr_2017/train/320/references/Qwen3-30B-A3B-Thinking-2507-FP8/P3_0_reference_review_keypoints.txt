- Uses pre-trained VGG features, learned bilinear dynamics, and sample-efficient RL for visual servoing.  
- Shows VGG features provide useful invariances (viewpoint, lighting, occlusion) for servoing.  
- Proposes a computationally efficient bilinear model for short-term visual feature dynamics prediction.  
- Introduces fitted Q-iteration to learn feature weights, focusing on target-relevant features and ignoring distractors.  
- Demonstrates extreme sample efficiency (effective car-following with ~20 trajectories), outperforming baselines by orders of magnitude.  
- Thorough experiments and ablation studies compare against pixel-based, hand-crafted, and end-to-end deep RL baselines.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions due to strong empirical evidence and clear contribution.  
- Main concern: Evaluation in synthetic CitySim3D may not capture real-world visual servoing challenges (reality gap).  
- Suggest clarifying the exact “two orders of magnitude” sample-efficiency claim with concrete numbers.  
- Recommend discussing transfer to real-world settings and how to address the reality gap.  
- Ask for a detailed computational efficiency breakdown (feature extraction, dynamics prediction, weight learning).  
- Request deeper analysis of why some VGG layers (e.g., conv4_3) work better and why deeper features lose useful servoing information.  
- Ask for quantitative comparison of the bilinear dynamics model versus more complex video-prediction models.  
- Request discussion of key factors and challenges for extending the approach to other visual servoing tasks.  
- Overall: paper is strong, sample efficiency is impressive, and with the minor revisions it is well suited for acceptance.
