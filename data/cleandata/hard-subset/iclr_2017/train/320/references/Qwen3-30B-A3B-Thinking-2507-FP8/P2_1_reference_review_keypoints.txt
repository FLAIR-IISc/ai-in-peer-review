- Contribution: Proposes a visual servoing method combining pre-trained VGG features, a bilinear dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration to achieve sample-efficient RL-based servoing.  
- Main result: Learns effective servoing with only ~20 training trajectories and shows robustness to viewpoint, lighting, and occlusions.  
- Sample-efficiency claim: Achieves over two orders of magnitude improvement in sample efficiency compared to standard model-free deep RL (e.g., TRPO).  
- Decision: Reviewer recommends accepting the paper.  
- Motivation strength: Clearly motivates replacing hand-designed features and dynamics with pre-trained features plus learned weights to exploit statistical regularities.  
- Solution rationale: Uses RL to learn feature weights to distinguish targets from distractors—addressing why naive pre-trained features alone are insufficient.  
- Experimental rigor: Extensive comparisons to many baselines (pixel-based, ORB/SIFT/SURF, IBVS, end-to-end CNN TRPO, CEM) with consistent outperformance and supportive ablations across VGG layers.  
- Theoretical grounding: Provides solid analysis (bilinear dynamics linearization, Q-function approximation) and principled handling of multi-scale/multi-channel features.  
- Practical implications missing: Recommend quantifying real-world training time savings (minutes/hours) to contextualize sample efficiency.  
- Failure modes: Suggest adding discussion of limitations and failure cases (extreme occlusion/lighting, theoretical limits).  
- Computational details lacking: Ask for inference/runtime and memory requirements beyond the reported 16Hz vs 1Hz comparison.  
- Sim-to-real concerns: Recommend discussion of challenges and considerations for transferring from the synthetic benchmark to real robots.  
- Question on training data: Clarify whether the 20 trajectories cover single or multiple targets and how diverse they are in viewpoint/lighting.  
- Question on features: Ask why deeper VGG layers (conv5_3) performed worse—spatial invariance or other causes?  
- Question on IBVS baseline: How would IBVS perform if depth were estimated from monocular images rather than using ground-truth depth?  
- Question on generalization: How would the approach extend to other servoing tasks (e.g., manipulation/grasping) and what additional challenges arise?
