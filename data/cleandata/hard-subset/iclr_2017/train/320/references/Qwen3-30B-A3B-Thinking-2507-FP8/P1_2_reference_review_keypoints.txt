- Proposes combining pre-trained VGG features, a bilinear dynamics model, and reinforcement learning for sample-efficient visual servoing adaptable to new targets.  
- Addresses limitations of hand-designed features/models by leveraging learned visual features and statistical regularities.  
- Uses pre-trained VGG features (ImageNet) as the visual representation for servoing.  
- Introduces a bilinear dynamics model to predict how visual features change with robot motion.  
- Proposes a sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration algorithm to learn feature weights that maximize long-horizon reward.  
- Claims effective learning with only 20 training trajectory samples for reinforcement learning.  
- Demonstrates substantial improvements over pixel-based, hand-designed features, end-to-end CNNs, and classical visual servoing.  
- Reports more than two orders of magnitude improvement in sample-efficiency over standard deep RL methods.  
- Evaluated on a complex synthetic car-following benchmark with comprehensive baseline comparisons.  
- Recommends acceptance: significant problem, novel/justified approach, and strong experimental evidence.  
- Theoretical foundations are sound, with appropriate linearization of bilinear dynamics and Q-function approximation.  
- Experimental results are compelling and reproducible; ablations show component importance and generalization to novel cars.  
- Code and supplementary materials (including videos) are provided to enhance reproducibility.  
- Request to clarify total data requirements: whether the “20” RL samples exclude the 10,000 dynamics-model samples.  
- Ask for more details on the VGG modifications (which pooling layers removed, dilation rates, etc.) for reproducibility.  
- Suggest discussing limitations such as prolonged occlusions or distractors with similar appearance.  
- Recommend addressing sim2real transfer challenges and how to adapt the approach for real-world deployment.  
- Question whether the 20 samples refer only to RL fine-tuning or include dynamics-model training data.  
- Ask why deepest VGG features (conv5_3) perform poorly, possibly due to spatial invariance.  
- Ask how the method handles target vs. distractor objects with very similar appearance and whether feature weighting can distinguish them.  
- Question whether using a more efficient backbone (e.g., MobileNet) would improve runtime and how that affects performance.  
- Ask how the method is expected to perform under real-world domain shift compared to the synthetic results.
