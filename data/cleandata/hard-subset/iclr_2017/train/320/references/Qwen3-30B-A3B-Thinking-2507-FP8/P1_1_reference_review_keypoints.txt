- Paper proposes sample-efficient visual servoing using pre-trained VGG features, a learned bilinear dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration.  
- Claims robust servoing to viewpoint, lighting, and occlusions with only ~20 training trajectories.  
- Shows large improvements over pixel-based, hand-keypoint (ORB/SIFT/SURF), end-to-end CNN, and classical IBVS baselines.  
- Reports >100× sample-efficiency improvement over standard model-free deep RL.  
- Well-motivated positioning in literature, advocating pre-trained features for servoing.  
- Rigorous experiments on a synthetic car-following benchmark with ablations and generalization to unseen cars.  
- Technical soundness: clear bilinear dynamics formulation and fitted Q-iteration description.  
- Implementation/efficiency claim: method runs at ~16 Hz vs 1 Hz for C-COT.  
- Decision: Recommend accept.  
- Suggest adding a limitations discussion (real-world hardware, sensor noise, more complex dynamics).  
- Request more hardware and component-level detail to support computational efficiency claims.  
- Recommend deeper analysis of why mid-level VGG features (e.g., conv4_3) outperform conv5_3.  
- Suggest explicit comparison to recent end-to-end deep-learning visual servoing methods.  
- Q1: Clarify whether 20 training trajectories is total or per target.  
- Q2: Explain why conv5_3 features poorly approximate Q-values and whether this generalizes.  
- Q3: Provide exact baseline sample counts backing the “two orders of magnitude” claim.  
- Q4: Justify non-negative constraint on feature weights and describe effects of allowing negative weights.
