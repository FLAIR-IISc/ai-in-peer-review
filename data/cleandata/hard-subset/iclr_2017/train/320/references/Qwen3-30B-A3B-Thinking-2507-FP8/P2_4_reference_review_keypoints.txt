- Problem: sample-efficient visual servoing that adapts quickly to new targets.  
- Method: combine pre-trained VGG features, a bilinear model for feature dynamics, and fitted Q-iteration to learn feature weights.  
- Main claim: learn visual servoing with just 20 training trajectories.  
- Sample-efficiency claim: over two orders of magnitude improvement versus model-free RL (TRPO).  
- Experimental setup: synthetic car-following benchmark comparing to pixel-based and hand-designed keypoints.  
- Evidence: Fig. 5 — substantial performance gains across feature representations.  
- Evidence: Fig. 6 — dramatic sample-efficiency advantage (20 samples vs. thousands for TRPO).  
- Evidence: Table 1 — robustness to occlusions.  
- Evidence: Ablations (Sec. 6.3) — isolate contribution of fitted Q-iteration.  
- Decision: Reviewer recommends Accept based on motivation and empirical strength.  
- Improvement: Clarify what is truly novel relative to other work using pre-trained features with RL.  
- Improvement: Discuss limitations and failure modes (e.g., why deeper VGG conv5_3 underperforms).  
- Improvement: Discuss real-world relevance and sim-to-real generalization.  
- Question: Were other pre-trained backbones (ResNet, EfficientNet) evaluated?  
- Question: Provide concrete examples and quantitative limits of robustness to viewpoint, lighting, and occlusions.
