- Proposes a sample-efficient visual servoing method combining pre-trained VGG features, learned bilinear dynamics, and a fitted Q-iteration (FQI) procedure.  
- Achieves effective car-following policies using only 20 training trajectories, far more sample-efficient than standard deep RL (20 vs. 2000+).  
- Uses a weighted multiscale feature representation with a bilinear dynamics model and learns feature weights to maximize long-term reward.  
- Empirical results show substantial improvements over pixel-based, hand-designed keypoints, and model-free RL baselines on seen and novel cars.  
- Ablation studies demonstrate that the bilinear dynamics, weighted features, and FQI are all necessary for performance gains.  
- Comparison to classical IBVS/PBVS methods shows the learned approach can outperform engineered methods and run faster (16Hz vs. 1Hz for tracker-based).  
- Theoretical grounding provided via linearization of bilinear dynamics and motivation for learning feature weights rather than using raw pre-trained features.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions due to strong contribution and empirical support.  
- Major strength: convincingly addresses sample inefficiency in visual servoing with a novel and well-executed combination of techniques.  
- Area for improvement: evaluation limited to synthetic CitySim3D—need discussion of transfer and failure modes in real-world scenarios (lighting, occlusion, dynamics).  
- Area for improvement: computational efficiency—VGG feature extraction is a bottleneck (16Hz); discuss faster backbones or model compression.  
- Area for improvement: limited theoretical justification for why specific VGG layers (conv4_3) work best; discuss spatial invariance and servoing relevance.  
- Question: How well does the method generalize to targets that differ significantly in appearance from training (new models, colors, textures)?  
- Question: How would performance change without ground-truth depth for classical baselines and in real-world deployment—need depth estimation integration?  
- Question: Could the poorer performance of deeper VGG features (conv5_3) be due to spatial invariance, and how should that inform feature selection for other tasks?
