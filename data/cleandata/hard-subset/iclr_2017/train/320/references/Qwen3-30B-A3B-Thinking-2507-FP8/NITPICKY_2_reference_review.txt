

# Review of "Learning Visual Servoing with Pre-trained Features and Reinforcement Learning"

## 1. Summary of Claims and Contributions

The paper proposes a novel approach to visual servoing that combines pre-trained visual features (from VGG network), learned predictive dynamics models, and reinforcement learning to create a sample-efficient visual servoing mechanism. The key contributions are:
- A method using pre-trained VGG features for visual servoing that can be quickly adapted to new targets with minimal data (20 training trajectory samples)
- A bilinear dynamics model to predict feature changes in response to robot actions
- A fitted Q-iteration algorithm to learn weights for relevant visual features, addressing the challenge of distinguishing target objects from distractors
- Demonstrated improvements over conventional approaches based on image pixels or hand-designed keypoints, and over standard model-free deep reinforcement learning algorithms

The paper claims a sample-efficiency improvement of more than two orders of magnitude over standard model-free deep reinforcement learning algorithms, with promising results on a complex synthetic car-following benchmark.

## 2. Decision

Reject. The paper contains significant methodological inconsistencies and misleading claims that undermine its scientific rigor and clarity.

## 3. Supporting Arguments

The paper suffers from multiple critical issues that prevent confident assessment of its claims:

**First, there is a fundamental disconnect between the theoretical formulation and practical implementation.** The paper defines the servoing policy in Equation (1) as minimizing the error between the target and predicted features without weights or normalization: π(xt,x*) = argmin_u ||y* - f(yt,u)||^2. However, the actual implementation in Equation (3) includes weights, normalization by spatial resolution (1/|y(l)_·,c|), and control regularization (sum_j λ_j u^2_j). This disconnect is never properly addressed, making it unclear how the theoretical foundation supports the practical implementation.

**Second, the paper contains inconsistent and confusing notation throughout.** The * operator in Equation (2) is defined as a "locally connected operator" but the definition provided in Appendix A is unclear and inconsistent with standard convolution notation. The cardinality notation |y(l)_·,c| is used to represent spatial resolution, but cardinality is a set operation, not a spatial dimension. These notational inconsistencies make it difficult to understand the core algorithm.

**Third, the sample-efficiency claims are misleading.** The abstract states the method uses "just 20 training trajectory samples for reinforcement learning," but Section 6.1 reveals that the 20 trajectories are for the FQI learning phase only, while the dynamics model was trained on 10,000 samples (100 trajectories) from a hand-coded policy. The paper conflates these two distinct components (dynamics learning and policy learning), making the sample-efficiency claim inaccurate. The claim of "more than two orders of magnitude" improvement is also unsupported, as the comparison to TRPO uses different metrics (TRPO uses 50 sampling iterations with 4,000 samples per iteration, while FQI uses 2 sampling iterations with 10 trajectories each).

**Fourth, the paper has significant formatting inconsistencies.** References show inconsistent citation styles (e.g., "DeSouza & Kak, 2002" vs. "DeSouza and Kak, 2002"), equation formatting errors (e.g., "∥∥∥y(l)∗,c − f (l)c (y(l)t,c,u)∥∥∥2 2" should be "||y(l)*,c - f(l)c(y(l)t,c,u)||^2_2"), and inconsistent figure references (Figures 3-7 are mentioned but not included in the text).

## 4. Additional Feedback for Improvement

The paper has promising ideas but requires significant clarification and correction to be scientifically rigorous. To improve the paper:

1. **Clarify the theoretical-practical disconnect**: Explicitly show how Equation (1) leads to Equation (3), explaining the role of weights, normalization, and regularization. Provide a unified mathematical formulation.

2. **Fix notation inconsistencies**: Define all symbols consistently (especially the * operator), and use standard mathematical notation for spatial resolution (e.g., "H×W" instead of |y(l)_·,c|).

3. **Refine sample-efficiency claims**: Clearly separate the dynamics learning phase from the policy learning phase, and provide a proper comparison to standard baselines. Avoid conflating trajectory count with sample count.

4. **Improve reference formatting**: Ensure consistent citation style throughout the paper and in the references section.

5. **Provide architectural details**: Include a clear diagram of the modified VGG network, detailing exactly which max-pooling layers were removed and where dilated convolutions were added.

6. **Clarify the comparison to TRPO**: Explain why TRPO was run for 50 iterations while FQI used only 2, and provide a more precise comparison of sample efficiency (e.g., total number of environment interactions).

**Questions for Authors**:

1. How exactly does the policy in Equation (1) relate to the implementation in Equation (3)? What is the theoretical justification for adding the weights and normalization terms?

2. The paper claims "improvement in sample-efficiency of more than two orders of magnitude over standard model-free deep reinforcement learning algorithms." What specific standard baselines were compared to, and how was the sample count calculated (e.g., number of environment interactions vs. number of trajectories)?

3. In Section 6.1, the paper states "We ran the overall algorithm for only S = 2 sampling iterations and chose the parameters that achieved the best performance on 10 validation trajectories." How does this relate to the "20 training trajectory samples" claimed in the abstract? Are the 20 trajectories used for both dynamics learning and policy learning?

4. The paper states "VGG features conv1 2 through conv4 3 achieve some degree of generalization on the novel cars" (Section 6.2), but Figure 5 is not included in the text. Can the authors provide the figure or at least describe the key findings from it?
