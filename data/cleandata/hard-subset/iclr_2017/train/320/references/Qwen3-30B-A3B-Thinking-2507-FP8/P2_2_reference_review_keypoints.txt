- Uses pre-trained VGG features to avoid large task-specific datasets for visual servoing.  
- Proposes a bilinear model that predicts how robot motion affects visual features.  
- Introduces a sample-efficient fitted Q-iteration to learn feature weights for long-horizon performance.  
- Demonstrates effective visual servoing on a synthetic car-following benchmark with only 20 training trajectories (>100× sample-efficiency vs. model-free RL).  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Addresses a significant robotics problem with a well-motivated, impactful approach.  
- Experimental results are compelling, showing dramatic improvements and reduced sample requirements.  
- Strong literature grounding and clear motivation highlighting limits of hand-designed features/models.  
- Bilinear dynamics model is justified by prior work and leverages spatial structure of features.  
- Fitted Q-iteration is well-designed with a clear theoretical foundation for Q-function approximation.  
- Rigorous experiments with comprehensive baselines (pixels, keypoints, RL, classical methods).  
- Ablation studies demonstrate importance of components (bilinear model, feature weighting).  
- Shows meaningful generalization to novel cars, indicating practical robustness.  
- Suggest real-world validation or discussion of sim-to-real transfer and possible robot experiments.  
- Suggest including failure-case analysis to present limitations and edge cases.  
- Request more detailed computational efficiency analysis (scaling with feature resolution and robot configurations).  
- Recommend discussing feature selection and comparing other pretrained models (e.g., ResNet) vs. VGG.  
- Ask for precise numerical comparisons supporting claims like “more than two orders of magnitude” improvement.  
- Ask how performance changes if VGG features are fine-tuned for visual servoing.  
- Request details on parameter reduction and effects on training time/memory from the locally connected bilinear model.  
- Ask about generalization to other visual servoing tasks (manipulation, navigation) and domain-specific challenges.  
- Overall assessment: outstanding paper, significant contribution, and major step toward practical learning-based visual servoing.
