- Proposes combining pre-trained VGG features, a learned bilinear dynamics model, and sample-efficient RL (FQI) for visual servoing.
- Claims strong sample efficiency, learning effective car-following visual servo with only 20 RL trajectories.
- Demonstrates large improvements over pixel-based, hand-designed features, and standard model-free deep RL (over two orders of magnitude sample efficiency).
- Empirical evaluation includes rigorous ablation studies, multiple baselines, and tests on novel targets with viewpoint, lighting, and occlusion variations.
- The approach is theoretically grounded (linear approximation of bilinear dynamics and Q-function) and implemented reproducibly (code and details provided).
- Runs at ~16Hz and shows good generalization and robustness to moderate visual variations.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested clarification on the specific baseline RL algorithms and exact sample counts to substantiate the “two orders of magnitude” sample-efficiency claim.
- Suggests explicitly discussing limitations such as extreme occlusions and severe lighting changes, since evaluation covers only moderate variations.
- Recommends a detailed computational complexity breakdown (feature extraction, dynamics prediction, weight learning) beyond the reported 16Hz runtime.
- Encourages discussion or preliminary results on generalizability to other visual servoing tasks (e.g., manipulation, navigation).
- Asks for rationale and possible ablation for chosen VGG feature levels (conv1_2 through conv4_3) and how to select levels per task.
- Requests learning curves (cost vs. trajectories) comparing FQI and TRPO to show convergence speed differences.
- Asks for additional evidence (e.g., feature visualizations) supporting the claim that conv5_3 is too spatially invariant for Q-value approximation.
- Asks why features were downsampled to 32×32 and whether other resolutions were explored.
- Seeks clarification whether the reported 20 RL samples exclude the 10,000 dynamics-model training samples to understand the true sample-efficiency claim.
