- Uses pre-trained VGG features as visual representation to avoid hand-designed features.  
- Proposes a bilinear dynamics model for efficient prediction of visual feature dynamics.  
- Introduces fitted Q-iteration (FQI) to learn feature weights for long-horizon performance instead of hand-tuning.  
- Demonstrates extreme sample efficiency—effective policies learned from ~20 trajectories, far fewer than standard deep RL.  
- Shows robustness to viewpoint, lighting, and occlusions and generalization to novel targets.  
- Avoids task-specific feature engineering by leveraging statistical regularities in pre-trained networks.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Main acceptance reasons: well-motivated, technically sound approach with clear empirical advantages and strong sample-efficiency evidence.  
- Empirical support: comprehensive experiments on a synthetic car-following benchmark and strong comparisons to TRPO and other baselines.  
- Theoretical/algorithmic foundation: linear Q-function approximation enables efficient FQI; bilinear model is well-motivated by prior work.  
- Minor weakness: lacks ablation comparing the bilinear model to alternative dynamics models.  
- Suggestion: clarify why specific VGG layers (e.g., conv4_3) work better than others (e.g., conv5_3) and discuss limitations of pre-trained features.  
- Suggestion: add more qualitative visualizations (e.g., weighted features across scenes, occlusion cases) to justify feature weighting.  
- Suggestion: explicitly position the bilinear model relative to other visual-dynamics/video-prediction models for context.  
- Suggestion: discuss scalability and challenges for more complex or real-world environments.  
- Questions for authors: provide ablations comparing VGG vs random/other pre-trained features; explain conv5_3’s poorer performance; show qualitative occlusion-handling examples; clarify advantages over traditional IBVS and why weighted features improve upon standard feature-error minimization.
