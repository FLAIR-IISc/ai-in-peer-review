- Proposes a novel visual servoing method combining pre-trained VGG features, a bilinear dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration for sample-efficient control.
- Addresses the key limitation of prior methods: high data requirements to learn robust controllers for new targets.
- Uses pre-trained VGG conv features plus learned feature weights to distinguish targets from distractors.
- Employs a bilinear dynamics model extended to feature maps and a sample-efficient FQI procedure to learn feature weights.
- Demonstrates effective car-following visual servoing with only 20 training trajectories.
- Shows substantial improvements over pixel-based, hand-designed keypoint (ORB/SIFT/SURF), end-to-end TRPO, and classical IBVS baselines.
- Claims more than two orders of magnitude better sample efficiency than standard model-free deep RL (TRPO).
- Paper is well-motivated, technically sound, and makes a clear contribution to vision-based robot control.
- Decision: Accept.
- Acceptance reasons: clearly identified problem, novel and well-justified approach, substantial empirical improvements.
- Leverages pre-trained features to exploit statistical regularities while learning weights to focus on the target.
- Bilinear dynamics and FQI choices are well-motivated and theoretically grounded (linearization and Q-function approximation).
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: multiple baselines, ablation studies, and generalization tests on novel cars.
- Supplementary materials (code, videos, environment) support reproducibility.
- Suggest clarifying why pre-trained classification features are suitable for servoing (e.g., spatial invariance, discriminative properties).
- Recommend discussing method limitations: absence of target in training, sensitivity to VGG layer choice, and large unseen appearance changes.
- Suggest comparisons to more recent deep visual servoing/dynamics works (e.g., Visual Dynamics, Embed to Control).
- Ask for clearer computational requirements (hardware, memory, comparison to baselines) beyond the reported ~16Hz runtime.
- Question why conv5_3 features perform worse—possibility of excessive spatial invariance or other factors.
- Ask how much FQI vs TRPO performance gap is due to inherent sample efficiency vs TRPO hyperparameter/iteration choices.
- Question rationale for restricting feature weights to be non-negative and whether negative weights might help.
- Ask how the method handles prolonged occlusions of the target and how it compares to other methods in such scenarios.
