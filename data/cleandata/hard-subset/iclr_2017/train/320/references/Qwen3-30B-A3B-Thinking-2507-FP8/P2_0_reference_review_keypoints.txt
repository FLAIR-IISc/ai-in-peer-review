- Novel method combining pre-trained VGG features, a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration to learn visual servoing with only ~20 trajectories.  
- Decision: Accept — strong contribution and large empirical improvements in sample efficiency.  
- Motivation: Addresses limitations of hand-designed features and domain-specific dynamics in traditional visual servoing.  
- Methodological novelty: Synergistic use of pre-trained CNN features, bilinear dynamics (Censi & Murray justification), and fitted Q-iteration to learn feature weights.  
- Empirical evaluation: Thorough tests on a synthetic car-following benchmark, outperforming pixel-based, ORB, TRPO, and classical IBVS baselines.  
- Sample-efficiency result: Achieves substantial improvements (over two orders of magnitude) versus baselines (20 vs hundreds/thousands of trajectories).  
- Ablations and analyses: Layer-wise comparisons, feature representations, and optimization variants support the design choices.  
- Reproducibility: Provides implementation details, code availability, and supplementary videos.  
- Limitation: Lacks formal theoretical analysis explaining why ImageNet-trained VGG features generalize to visual servoing.  
- Limitation: Experiments are synthetic; real-robot or more realistic noisy simulations are needed for stronger validation.  
- Limitation: Computational complexity and runtime comparisons with baselines are not analyzed in detail (method reported at ~16Hz).  
- Questions for authors: Provide specific evidence why ImageNet features outperform task-specific features?  
- Questions for authors: Why does VGG conv5_3 perform worse than earlier layers, and is this limitation general?  
- Questions for authors: How does the simple one-step Euclidean feature-minimization servo compare to multi-step planning approaches?
