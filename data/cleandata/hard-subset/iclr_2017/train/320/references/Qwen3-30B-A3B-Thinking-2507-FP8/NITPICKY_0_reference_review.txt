

# Review for ICLR

## Summary of Contributions
The paper proposes a novel approach to visual servoing that combines pre-trained visual features (from VGG-16), learned bilinear dynamics models, and reinforcement learning to achieve sample-efficient visual servoing. The key contribution is a method that can learn effective visual servoing policies using only 20 training trajectory samples, with substantial improvements over conventional approaches based on pixel intensities or hand-designed keypoints. The authors demonstrate their method's effectiveness on a synthetic car-following benchmark, showing significant gains in sample efficiency compared to standard model-free deep reinforcement learning algorithms.

## Decision
Reject. The paper contains multiple significant methodological inconsistencies and formatting errors that undermine its scientific rigor and reproducibility, despite the potentially interesting contribution.

## Supporting Arguments

1. **Inconsistent and Unclear Sample Efficiency Claim**: The paper claims "improvement in sample-efficiency of more than two orders of magnitude over standard model-free deep reinforcement learning algorithms" (Section 7). However, in Section 6.3, they compare against their specific TRPO implementation (not standard model-free RL algorithms), stating "the cost incurred by this policy is still about one and a half times the cost of our policy, despite using more than 100 times as many trajectories." This comparison is misleading because they don't compare against standard implementations of algorithms like TRPO or DQN. The paper should have directly compared against standard implementations of model-free RL algorithms, not their own modified versions.

2. **Formatting Inconsistencies and Typos**: The paper contains multiple formatting inconsistencies that undermine reproducibility:
   - Inconsistent notation: "VGG conv4 3" vs "VGG-16" vs "conv4_3" (Section 4.1, 6.2)
   - Mathematical errors: Equation (3) contains a syntax error with "∥∥∥y(l)∗,c − f (l)c (y(l)t,c,u)∥∥∥2 2" (extra closing brace)
   - Inconsistent reference formatting: Some references use "et al." while others don't (e.g., "DeSouza & Kak, 2002" vs "DeSouza et al., 2002")
   - Inconsistent terminology: "pixel intensities" vs "pixel values" vs "RGB values"
   - The paper mentions "CitySim3D1" in the introduction but refers to "CitySim3D" in the references

3. **Lack of Technical Clarity**: Several key technical details are unclear:
   - The paper states "the features at each level are obtained from the features below through a downsampling operator d(y(l−1)) = y(l) that cuts the resolution in half" but doesn't specify the downsampling method (max-pooling, average-pooling, etc.).
   - The paper claims "VGG conv5 3 is not as suitable for approximating Q-values" but doesn't provide evidence or detailed analysis for this claim.
   - The paper states "our method implicitly incorporates that in the learned feature dynamics" (referring to car dynamics), but doesn't explain how the bilinear model captures car dynamics information.

4. **Experimental Setup Ambiguity**: The paper claims to use "just 20 training trajectory samples for reinforcement learning" (Section 7), but Section 6.1 states they used "S = 2 sampling iterations" with "10 trajectories" per iteration, for a total of 20 trajectories. However, it's unclear if these 20 trajectories are the only samples used for RL training, or if there's additional data for training the dynamics model (which was trained on 10,000 samples). The paper doesn't clarify this distinction.

## Additional Feedback

1. **Notation Consistency**: Please standardize all feature layer notations (e.g., "conv4_3" instead of "conv4 3" or "conv4-3") and ensure consistent capitalization (e.g., "VGG" vs "vgg").

2. **Clarify Sample Efficiency Comparison**: Provide direct comparisons with standard implementations of model-free RL algorithms (e.g., TRPO, DQN) rather than comparing against your own modified versions. Include the exact number of samples required by standard algorithms for fair comparison.

3. **Explain Multiscale Feature Combination**: Clarify how the multiscale features are combined in the objective function (Equation 3), particularly regarding the spatial resolution normalization term "1/|y(l)·,c|".

4. **Justify VGG Feature Choice**: Provide more detailed analysis for why VGG conv4_3 is optimal but conv5_3 is not suitable, beyond the hypothesis about spatial invariance.

5. **Clarify Data Usage**: Explicitly state how many samples were used for training the dynamics model versus the RL policy, and whether these datasets overlap.

## Questions for Authors

1. Can you provide a direct comparison between your method and standard implementations of model-free RL algorithms (e.g., TRPO, PPO) using the exact same hyperparameters and experimental setup?

2. What specific downsampling method (max-pooling, average-pooling, etc.) was used to obtain features at different scales, and how did this choice impact performance?

3. Can you provide more detailed analysis for why VGG conv5_3 features are unsuitable for Q-value approximation, including visualizations or quantitative metrics?

4. How exactly does your bilinear dynamics model incorporate car dynamics information, given that it's trained on general visual features without car-specific information?

5. Please clarify the exact sample count for your "20 training trajectory samples" claim - were these samples used solely for RL training, or did they include samples used for dynamics model training?
