- Proposes combining pre-trained VGG features, a bilinear predictive dynamics model, and fitted Q-iteration to achieve sample-efficient visual servoing.
- Demonstrates robust car-following in simulation using only 20 training trajectories with large sample-efficiency gains over pixel-based and model-free RL methods.
- Shows substantial improvement over hand-designed features and pixel methods; claims >100x sample-efficiency advantage vs TRPO.
- Uses VGG conv4_3 features, a locally-connected bilinear dynamics model, and a weighted FQI procedure to learn feature weights.
- Ablation studies indicate the combination of pre-trained features, bilinear dynamics, and weighted FQI is essential for performance.
- Well-motivated and well-placed in the literature with fair comparisons to IBVS/PBVS classical methods and modern RL baselines.
- Recommendation: Accept — strong contribution with compelling empirical results and thorough evaluation.
- Suggestion: Discuss sim-to-real transfer and domain-shift challenges to address real-world applicability.
- Suggestion: Provide a clearer, intuitive explanation and a simple example of the locally-connected bilinear dynamics model.
- Suggestion: Include analysis of failure modes (e.g., extreme occlusions, unusual lighting, out-of-distribution appearances).
- Suggestion: Compare computational efficiency and runtime to baselines (noting method runs at 16Hz vs C-COT at 1Hz).
- Question: How much performance gain comes from pre-trained features versus the bilinear dynamics model — request isolating ablation.
- Question: What specific visual variations were tested and how did the method perform on the most challenging cases (complete occlusion, extreme lighting)?
- Question: Why does conv4_3 outperform deeper conv5_3 — due to spatial invariance or other factors?
- Question: Clarify whether the 20 training trajectories are from 20 unique car instances or repeated trajectories of a single car.
