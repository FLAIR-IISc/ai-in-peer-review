### 1. Summary of Contributions

The paper proposes a novel and sample-efficient method for learning visual servoing controllers. The approach cleverly combines three key components: (1) powerful, pre-trained visual features extracted from a VGG network to provide robustness to visual variations; (2) a learned, multiscale bilinear dynamics model that predicts how these features change in response to robot actions; and (3) a reinforcement learning algorithm based on fitted Q-iteration (FQI) that learns to weight the feature channels and scales appropriately for the task. The core idea is that by learning to prioritize features relevant to the target object, the simple greedy servoing policy can achieve effective long-horizon behavior. The method is evaluated on a challenging synthetic car-following task, where it demonstrates the ability to learn an effective policy from only 20 training trajectories, a significant improvement in sample efficiency of over two orders of magnitude compared to standard deep reinforcement learning baselines.

### 2. Decision and Key Reasons

**Decision:** Reject

**Key Reasons:**
1.  The description of the core methodology contains critical ambiguities and potential flaws, particularly concerning the reinforcement learning update rule and data collection process (Algorithm 1). This prevents a confident assessment of the method's correctness and reproducibility.
2.  The paper is beset by a significant number of presentation and formatting issues, including a completely corrupted reference list and numerous inconsistencies, which fall substantially below the expected quality for publication at ICLR.

### 3. Supporting Arguments for the Decision

While the paper's high-level approach is compelling, my recommendation for rejection is based on a meticulous review that revealed numerous methodological and presentational deficiencies.

**Methodological Clarity and Rigor:**

*   **Flawed Algorithm Description:** Algorithm 1, which details the main learning loop, appears to be described incorrectly. In line 3, the data collection for each sampling iteration `s` uses an exploration policy based on `π_{θ(0)}`. The parameter `θ(0)` is the initial set of weights, and it is not updated within the `s` loop. This implies that the agent collects data using the same, non-improving policy in every sampling iteration. This would be a highly inefficient learning strategy and contradicts the spirit of iterative methods like FQI. The update `θ(0) ← θ(K)` at the end of the loop is confusingly placed and suggests the authors intended to use the updated policy, but the algorithm as written does not reflect this. This is a major flaw that calls the entire learning process into question.
*   **Unmotivated FQI Variant:** The paper proposes a two-step FQI update (Eq. 5 and 6), where a global scaling factor `α` is first found before updating the individual feature weights `θ`. The justification provided is "to speed up the optimization," but this claim is not supported by any theoretical argument or empirical ablation. It is unclear why this decomposition is superior to a standard FQI update that solves for all parameters simultaneously. Without proper motivation, this novel component seems arbitrary.
*   **Ambiguous Model Specification:** There is a confusing contradiction between the main text and the appendix regarding the type of dynamics model used for VGG features. Section 4.2 introduces a "fully connected bilinear model" and then a "locally connected bilinear model." The text suggests the fully connected model is used for semantic features. However, Appendix C.2 states, "We do not use the latter [fully connected dynamics] for the semantic features since they are too high-dimensional... to fit in memory." This must be clarified. Is it a per-channel fully connected model, or the locally connected one?
*   **Inscrutable Cost Function:** The cost function in Equation (9) (Appendix B) contains a terminal cost defined as `(T − t+ 1) c(·, ·, st)`. The term `c(·, ·, st)` is not defined, making the penalty term for task failure impossible to interpret.
*   **Missing Hyperparameters:** Key details required for reproducibility are missing. For instance, the kernel size `nf` for the locally connected model is never specified. The "standardization" of VGG features mentioned in Section 4.1 is not detailed (e.g., per-channel, per-image, mean/std values).

**Presentation and Formatting:**

*   **Corrupted Reference List:** The entire list of references is malformed, containing BibTeX export errors such as `Bay et al\.,? \Q2006\E, Bay et al\.,`. This level of carelessness is unacceptable for a top-tier conference submission.
*   **Inconsistent Formatting:** There are numerous formatting inconsistencies. For example, citations sometimes use "et al." and other times use "&" for the same number of authors (e.g., `(De Boer et al., 2005)` vs. `(Kingma & Ba, 2014)`).
*   **Misuse of Footnotes:** Critical methodological details are relegated to footnotes. The definition of the locally connected operator (Footnote 4) is essential to understanding the dynamics model and should be in the main body of the paper. Timing results (Footnote 7) and URLs should also be integrated more appropriately.
*   **Minor Errors:** There are several minor but distracting typos, such as the period at the end of Equation (2).

While the core ideas are promising, the sheer volume of these minor and major issues undermines the paper's credibility and makes it impossible to rigorously evaluate its claims.

### 4. Additional Feedback for Improvement

I believe the research direction is valuable. The combination of pre-trained features, learned local dynamics, and sample-efficient RL for weighting is a powerful paradigm for robotics. Should the authors choose to revise this work, I would suggest the following:

*   **Rewrite the Methodology:** The entire methods section, particularly the FQI algorithm, needs to be rewritten with exacting clarity and precision. Provide a clear, step-by-step walkthrough of the learning process, ensuring that Algorithm 1 is correct.
*   **Justify Novelty:** Provide a clear motivation or ablation study for the two-step FQI update to demonstrate its benefit over a standard implementation.
*   **Improve Experimental Analysis:** The evaluation would be strengthened by including more interpretable metrics alongside average cost, such as task success rate or final distance from the target. Error bars on all plots are essential for assessing the significance of results. Furthermore, when comparing to baselines like IBVS that are given oracle information (ground truth depth), the text should more explicitly frame this as an upper-bound comparison.
*   **Thorough Proofreading:** The manuscript requires a complete and thorough proofreading pass to fix the references, citation styles, typos, and other formatting errors. All critical information should be moved from footnotes into the main text.

### 5. Questions for the Authors

To clarify my understanding, I would appreciate answers to the following:
1.  Could you please clarify the data collection process in Algorithm 1? Is the exploration policy indeed fixed across all `S` sampling iterations, or is it updated with the most recently learned parameters `θ(K)` from the previous iteration?
2.  What is the precise definition of the terminal cost term `c(·, ·, st)` in Equation (9) of the appendix?
3.  Regarding the VGG feature dynamics, was the model a per-channel fully connected one, or the locally connected variant described in Equation (2)?
4.  What was the spatial kernel size (`nf` x `nf`) used for the locally connected dynamics model?
