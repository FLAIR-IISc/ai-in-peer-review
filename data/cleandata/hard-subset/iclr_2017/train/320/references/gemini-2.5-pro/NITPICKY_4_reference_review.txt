### Review Summary

This paper presents a method for learning sample-efficient visual servoing policies by combining pre-trained visual features (from VGG), a learned bilinear dynamics model, and a reinforcement learning algorithm based on fitted Q-iteration (FQI). The core idea is to learn a set of weights over the channels and spatial scales of the visual features, such that a simple one-step greedy policy that minimizes the weighted feature error corresponds to a near-optimal long-horizon policy. The method is evaluated on a simulated car-following task, where it demonstrates remarkable sample efficiency, learning an effective policy from only 20 trajectories. It shows substantial improvements over policies based on pixels or hand-designed keypoints, as well as model-free deep RL methods like TRPO, which require orders of magnitude more data.

### Decision: Reject

While the paper presents a promising direction with strong empirical results, the manuscript in its current form is not ready for publication. My recommendation is based on two primary reasons: 1) The paper suffers from severe formatting and presentation issues, most notably a corrupted reference list, that fall far below the standards for a top-tier conference. 2) There are critical ambiguities, inconsistencies, and potential errors in the description of the core methodology that preclude a complete understanding and reproduction of the work.

### Supporting Arguments

My decision is based on a meticulous examination of the paper, which revealed a substantial number of flaws that, in aggregate, undermine the submission's quality.

**1. Severe Presentation and Formatting Issues:**

*   **Corrupted References:** The bibliography is unusable. Almost every entry is malformed with extraneous characters (e.g., "Bay et al\.,? \Q2006\E, Bay et al\.,"). This is a critical failure of manuscript preparation that should have been caught before submission.
*   **Misuse of Footnotes:** The paper relies heavily on footnotes for crucial information that belongs in the main text. Footnote 4 defines the "locally connected operator," a key component of the dynamics model. Footnote 5 defines the "interaction matrix," a standard term in the field. URLs (Footnotes 1, 2, 3, 6) should also be handled differently. This makes the paper difficult to read and suggests poor organization.

**2. Critical Methodological Ambiguities and Inconsistencies:**

*   **Contradictory Model Description:** There is a major contradiction regarding the dynamics model used for the main results. Section 4.2 motivates the "locally connected bilinear model" as a way to handle high-dimensional semantic features by enforcing sparsity. However, Appendix C.2 states, "We do not use the latter [locally connected dynamics] for the semantic features since they are too high-dimensional for the dynamics model to fit in memory." This leaves the reader confused as to which model—the fully connected channel-independent model or the locally connected one—was actually used to generate the impressive VGG-based results. This ambiguity is critical, as the model architecture is central to the paper's contribution.
*   **Potential Flaw in Algorithm 1:** The pseudocode for the FQI algorithm appears to contain a logical error. Line 8 states `θ(0) ← θ(K)`. This updates the initial parameter set. However, the exploration policy in line 3 is defined as `N(πθ(0), σ^2exploration)`. This implies that for every new sampling iteration `s`, the exploration is performed around the *original* initial policy, not the improved policy from the previous iteration. If this is not a typo, it is a highly unusual and inefficient exploration strategy that requires justification.
*   **Unclear Operator Definition:** The definition of the locally connected operator `*` in Footnote 4 is confusing. The formula `(W ∗ y)kh,kw = ... ∑ Wkh,kw,ih−kh,iw−kw yih,iw` appears to define a standard convolution, where the kernel weights `W` depend only on the relative displacement (`ih-kh`, `iw-kw`), not the absolute output location (`kh`, `kw`). A true locally-connected layer would have weights indexed by the output location, making the kernel untied. This lack of clarity on a core mathematical component is a significant flaw.
*   **Vague Cost Function:** The cost function in Equation (9) is poorly defined. The "otherwise" case for early termination specifies a penalty of `(T - t+ 1) c(·, ·, st)`. The term `c(·, ·, st)` is recursive and its arguments are unclear. This makes the reinforcement learning objective ambiguous.

**3. Minor Technical and Notational Issues:**

*   **Motivation for FQI Variant:** The two-step update in Section 5.3 (solving for a scalar `α` and bias `b` in Eq. 5 before updating the full weights `θ` in Eq. 6) is presented without justification. While this may be an effective heuristic, the paper does not explain the reasoning behind this specific design choice over a more standard FQI update.
*   **Inconsistent Notation:** The notation for the Q-function in Section 5.2 is clumsy. The expression `φ(st,u)>θ` suggests `θ` is a column vector, but the definition `θ> = [ w> λ> ]` is not constructed consistently with this. Non-standard notation, such as using the cardinality operator `|·|` to denote spatial normalization in Equation (3), adds to the confusion.

### Additional Feedback

Despite my recommendation, I believe the core contribution is valuable. The idea of using FQI to learn a feature weighting for a simple, dynamics-based servoing policy is elegant and the results are compelling. Should the authors choose to revise and resubmit, I offer the following suggestions for improvement:

1.  **Thoroughly Revise and Proofread:** The manuscript requires a complete overhaul. Fix the bibliography, integrate footnotes into the main text, and perform a comprehensive review for clarity, consistency, and correctness.
2.  **Clarify the Methodology:** Provide an unambiguous description of the exact dynamics model used for the VGG features. Justify the specific choices made in the FQI algorithm (e.g., the two-step update). Provide a clear, complete definition of the cost function.
3.  **Improve Experimental Flow:** The experiments section would be more readable if the setup (Figures 3, 4) were presented before the main results (Figure 5).

### Questions for the Authors

To better assess the work, I would need the following clarifications:

1.  Which specific bilinear dynamics model architecture (fully connected channel-independent, or locally connected) was used for the VGG features in your main experiments? Please resolve the contradiction between Section 4.2 and Appendix C.2.
2.  Is the description of the exploration policy update in Algorithm 1 correct? Does the agent explore around the initial policy `πθ(0)` in every sampling iteration, or is it updated with the newly learned `πθ(K)`?
3.  Could you provide a precise, non-recursive definition for the termination penalty in the cost function (Equation 9)?
4.  Can you clarify the mathematical definition of the locally connected operator `*` in Footnote 4 and explain how it differs from a standard convolution?
