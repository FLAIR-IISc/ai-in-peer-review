- Proposal: Direct Future Prediction (DFP) predicts multistep future measurement vectors conditioned on observation, candidate action, and goal to convert control into supervised learning.
- Acting: Choose the action whose predicted future measurements best match a goal via a linear utility over measurement differences.
- Key advantages: Removes Bellman bootstrapping, uses dense multivariate supervision, and supports goal-conditioning at train and test time.
- Architecture: Processes image, measurement, and goal streams with a dueling-style decomposition into expectation and action-advantage streams plus zero-mean normalization.
- Empirical claim: On ViZDoom tasks DFP matches or outperforms strong deep RL baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR on simple tasks) and won the Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Generalization: DFP shows robustness across texture/environment variations and can adapt to unseen test-time goals after goal-agnostic training.
- Ablations: Vectorial measurements and multi-offset predictions both improve performance, supporting the dense multivariate supervision thesis.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Simple, well-motivated alternative to standard RL leveraging dense measurements and a clear goal-conditioned formulation.
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical evidence on challenging 3D tasks, including generalization, ablations, and an external competition win.
- Key reason 3: Clear positioning relative to UVFA, generalized value functions, Monte Carlo vs TD, and multi-objective RL.
- Problem/motivation: Dense telemetry-like measurements provide richer supervision than sparse rewards and many goals can be linear utilities over those measurements.
- Relation to prior work: Related to UVFA and generalized value functions but differs by predicting future measurement vectors enabling post-hoc goal selection.
- Methodology: Simple supervised regression to multi-step measurement deltas at multiple offsets, with straightforward action and goal conditioning.
- Empirical comparisons: DFP is competitive on simple tasks and substantially better on complex Battle scenarios, with proper seeding and large test evaluations.
- Goal-agnostic training: Randomized-goal training nearly matches task-specific training and generalizes well to novel goals.
- External validation: Competition win corroborates effectiveness against more complex baselines using extra supervision.
- Limitations: Reactive (no memory), myopic beyond chosen offsets, relies on informative measurement streams, and lacks theoretical analysis of optimality or error compounding.
- Baselines/details requested: Clarify action-space cardinality in D3/D4, pruning/frame-skip, and full A3C hyperparameters including workers, frame stacking, learning rates, entropy, and recurrence choices.
- DSR comparison: DSR appears under-trained; consider reclassification or separate discussion.
- Targets/horizons: Provide sensitivity studies to offset choices/weights and discuss behavior for longer horizons or delayed-consequence tasks.
- Training signals/counterfactuals: Explore supervision for unexecuted actions (importance weighting, model-based rollouts) and effect of exploration schedules on coverage.
- Measurements: Clarify whether measurements are privileged vs human-observable HUDs, possibility of learning them from pixels, and robustness to noisy/partial/sparse measurements.
- Architecture ablations: Quantify benefit of expectation/action decomposition and zero-mean normalization; test recurrence/memory for partial observability.
- Theory connections: Discuss links to successor representations/features and potential transfer/error bounds in multi-objective settings.
- Reproducibility request: Release code/configs and provide full hyperparameters (ε schedule, replay size, update lag, optimizer) and training details in an appendix.
