1. Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): supervised prediction of action-conditional future measurement vectors, selecting actions by maximizing goal-weighted predictions.  
2. Introduces an architecture with separate perception, measurement, and goal modules and a dueling-style expectation/action decomposition.  
3. Demonstrates strong empirical gains over DQN and A3C in ViZDoom, especially on tasks with richer measurements, and won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch.  
4. Shows generalization across environment variations (domain randomization) and across unseen goals via goal-agnostic training.  
5. Ablations indicate multi-measurement and multi-horizon predictions materially improve performance versus scalar reward proxies.  
6. Decision: Accept — based on strong, consistent empirical results and a simple, well-motivated reframing of control as supervised learning.  
7. Key motivation: dense, multidimensional measurements provide richer supervisory signals than sparse scalar rewards, making direct prediction attractive.  
8. Paper is well situated in literature, connecting to forward models, Monte Carlo vs TD, GVFs/UVFAs, and deep RL in 3D.  
9. Results largely support claims, with larger margins when more measurements are available and validated generalization experiments.  
10. Caveat: Missing comparisons to strong auxiliary-task baselines (e.g., A3C/UNREAL) that also use dense measurements.  
11. Caveat: Limited statistical reporting — only 3 seeds and no confidence intervals or error bands.  
12. Caveat: Evaluation domain limited to Doom with shaped measurement streams; sparse-reward/delayed-objective settings not tested.  
13. Positive: Method is sample/time efficient due to fast simulation throughput and simple supervised updates.  
14. Positive: Goal-agnostic training performs well on unseen goals, addressing practical needs.  
15. Positive: Architectural choices (dueling split, multi-horizon) are empirically justified.  
16. Suggestion: Include A3C with auxiliary prediction baselines (e.g., UNREAL) to test whether benefits arise from richer supervision or SL framing.  
17. Suggestion: Report variance/CI over more seeds and add learning curves with shaded error bands.  
18. Suggestion: Add at least one sparse/delayed reward task to delineate where DFP may underperform TD methods.  
19. Suggestion: Ablate performance vs number/choice of measurements and test learned low-dimensional cumulants.  
20. Suggestion: Study sensitivity to horizon choices and loss weights; justify schedules or explore learned discounting.  
21. Suggestion: Quantify effect of action/expectation stream decomposition via ablations.  
22. Suggestion: Discuss exploration (ε-greedy interaction with goal randomization) and consider intrinsic bonuses or curiosity.  
23. Suggestion: Make theoretical links to GVFs/UVFAs more explicit and consolidate hyperparameters for reproducibility; release code/checkpoints.  
24. Questions: How would DFP compare to A3C with equivalent auxiliary losses or pixel control?  
25. Questions: How sensitive is performance to measurement selection/scaling and to noisy/irrelevant measurements?  
26. Questions: How were horizons and loss weights chosen, and could learned discounting match fixed schedules?  
27. Questions: How does goal distribution in training affect downstream performance and potential negative transfer?  
28. Questions: How do replay size and update cadence affect stability and performance?  
29. Questions: How does DFP perform on truly sparse/delayed rewards and can it incorporate bootstrapping/eligibility traces?  
30. Questions: Can DFP combine with recurrence or hierarchical options for POMDPs, and how to handle continuous actions?
