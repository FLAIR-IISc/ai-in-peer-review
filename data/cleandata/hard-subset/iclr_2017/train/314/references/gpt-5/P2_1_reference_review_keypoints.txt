- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): supervised prediction of future measurement changes conditioned on sensory input, measurements, goal vector, and action; selects actions that maximize goal-weighted predicted measurements.
- Contribution: goal-conditioned predictive control reducing sensorimotor control to supervised learning with dense multivariate feedback.
- Contribution: dueling-style architecture that predicts action-conditional future measurements at multiple temporal offsets.
- Contribution: strong empirical results in ViZDoom—outperforming DQN/A3C on hard tasks, better generalization, and winning the Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Contribution: ablations showing benefits of vector-valued measurements, multi-horizon prediction, and goal-agnostic training enabling test-time goal changes.
- Decision: Accept — novel, well-motivated, timely, and impactful for RL in high-dimensional sensory domains.
- Reason: Strong empirical support with large, consistent gains over strong baselines and convincing generalization across environments and goals.
- Problem/motivation: Critiques sparse scalar TD methods and motivates supervised prediction using dense temporal structure and measurements.
- Technical approach: Multi-horizon prediction conditioned on goals supports test-time goal changes; expectation/action-stream decomposition adapts dueling networks sensibly.
- Benchmarks: Four ViZDoom tasks of increasing difficulty, using raw images and measurements for evaluation.
- Baselines: Compared to DQN, A3C, and DSR with reward scalarization to match DFP goals; A3C tuning and DSR training speed reported.
- Results: DFP competitive on easy tasks, substantially superior on complex tasks (e.g., 6x frags in D3, 2.5x in D4 vs A3C); largest gains with multiple measurements.
- Training/reporting: Training speed (steps/day), evaluation over 1M steps, and aggregated curves across runs are provided.
- Generalization: Texture randomization and larger model capacity improve transfer to unseen textures and robustness.
- Goal generalization: Models trained with randomized goals perform nearly as well as task-specific models on main goals and much better on novel goals.
- Ablations: Predicting multiple measurements and multiple horizons both contribute meaningfully to performance gains.
- External validation: Competition win against sophisticated baselines adds credibility to empirical claims.
- Clarity & reproducibility: Paper is clearly written with appendices on architecture/baselines and uses the public ViZDoom platform.
- Suggestion — baselines: Add UVFA-style goal-conditioned A3C/DQN and successor-representation/factorization baselines for fairer comparisons.
- Suggestion — reporting: Provide standard deviations/confidence intervals across seeds and ensure identical training budgets (frames, wall-clock, compute) for baselines.
- Suggestion — analysis: Test sensitivity to which measurements are available/noisy/omitted, ablate horizon choices/weights and the expectation/action decomposition, and discuss failure modes when dense measurements are absent.
- Suggestion — broader evaluation: Include memory-based variants (e.g., conv-LSTM) for partial observability and an additional domain beyond Doom to test generality.
- Suggestion — theory: Provide analysis linking accurate future-measurement prediction to policy improvement under linear goal weights and discuss off-policy stability with small replay buffers.
- Questions for authors: Did you try UVFA-style goal-conditioned baselines? How does DFP compare to successor features? How sensitive is performance to measurement choice? Why weight only the last three horizons? Any recurrent/frame-stack experiments? Please report variance across seeds and compute/wall-clock metrics. How far outside training goal distributions can you generalize?
