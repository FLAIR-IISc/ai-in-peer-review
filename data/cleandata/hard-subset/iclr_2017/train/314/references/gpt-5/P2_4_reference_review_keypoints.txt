- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict vector-valued future measurements at multiple horizons and score actions by goal-weighted predictions.  
- Architecture uses a dueling-style decomposition (expectation + action-specific deltas) to produce action-conditional future measurement vectors.  
- Claims dense multivariate measurements give richer supervision than sparse scalar rewards, improving stability and performance.  
- Claims goal conditioning enables training with randomized goals and test-time adaptation to unseen goals.  
- Empirically, DFP outperforms DQN and A3C on challenging 3D Doom tasks, especially harder scenarios, and generalizes across textures and goals.  
- Ablations show multi-measurement and multi-horizon prediction improve performance even when evaluating a single metric.  
- DFP-trained agent won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch track, providing external validation.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-motivated, and situated within related work (UVFA, predictive representations, Monte Carlo, successor-like methods).  
- Decision: Accept — strong empirical evidence and clear methodological contribution (goal-parameterized future measurement prediction).  
- Experiments use fair baselines and sensible design, but some statistical reporting (error bars/significance) is missing.  
- Suggest clarifying connections to successor features and UVFA to sharpen conceptual contributions.  
- Recommend sensitivity analysis of temporal offsets and weighting coefficients, with guidance for new domains.  
- Advise including variance/error bars across seeds for main results to improve rigor.  
- Suggest including memory-augmented baselines (A3C-LSTM) to isolate benefits from learning signal vs memory.  
- Request clarification on compute/parity (wall-clock/sample budgets) and O(|A|) inference cost scalability to large/continuous action spaces.  
- Suggest discussing robustness when measurement streams are unavailable/noisy and consider releasing code for reproducibility.  
- Questions: how many composite actions are output vs combinatorial, and how does training scale with |A|?  
- Questions: how were temporal offsets and weights chosen and how sensitive is performance to them or to discounting schemes?  
- Questions: how critical is the exact set of measurements and could learned predictors substitute for hand-chosen stats?  
- Questions: what distributions/normalizations were used for randomized-goal training and how do negative weights affect stability?  
- Questions: which A3C variant was used (feedforward vs recurrent) and were n-step returns/entropy tuning explored to match DFP horizons?  
- Questions: did off-policy training with replay buffer cause instability and how does buffer size affect results?
