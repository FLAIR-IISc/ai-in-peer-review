- Proposal: Direct Future Prediction (DFP) predicts action-conditional future measurement vectors at multiple temporal offsets and selects actions by maximizing goal utility g^T f instead of using Bellman bootstrapping.  
- Architecture: Simple deep network with convolutional perception, measurement/goal encoders, and a dueling-style prediction head trained via Monte Carlo regression.  
- Empirical results: Extensive ViZDoom experiments show DFP matches/exceeds DQN, A3C, and DSR, with especially large gains on complex tasks.  
- Generalization: Models generalize to novel environment textures and to test-time goals not seen during training.  
- Ablations: Multivariate measurements and multi-horizon prediction outperform scalar reward prediction; design ablations provided.  
- External validation: A DFP-based agent won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch track.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Clear, well-motivated supervised formulation replacing sparse scalar rewards with dense vector measurements, enabling strong empirical performance.  
- Key reason 2: Thorough experimental evidence with sizable margins over state-of-the-art deep RL baselines on challenging 3D tasks.  
- Motivation/placement: Well-situated against Monte Carlo, predictive representations, UVFA, and successor representations; novelty is explicit action-conditional multi-horizon vector prediction.  
- Empirical support credibility: Training/testing protocols, multiple seeds, train/test texture splits, and ablations are described and convincing for the domain.  
- Caveat — baseline fairness: Unclear whether A3C baselines included recurrence (LSTM) and frame stacking; this could understate A3C performance.  
- Caveat — off-policy targets: Targets are collected under an evolving ε-greedy policy with no off-policy correction, potentially inducing bias.  
- Caveat — horizon/credit assignment: Short prediction horizons (up to 32 steps) and use of only last three offsets may limit long-horizon performance.  
- Caveat — limited goal class: Experiments only use linear utility functions, limiting generalization claims to linear goals over measurements.  
- Suggestion — clarify baselines: Explicitly report whether A3C used recurrence/frame stacking and detail entropy regularization and learning-rate choices.  
- Suggestion — report variability: Add standard deviations or confidence intervals across seeds for main metrics.  
- Suggestion — sensitivity analyses: Vary temporal offsets τ and loss weights, replay buffer size and update frequency, goal-randomization regimes, and ablate expectation/action streams.  
- Suggestion — policy interpretation: Discuss the implicit continuation policy assumed when predicting f and the effect of changing goals at test time.  
- Suggestion — action scalability: Discuss scaling to large or continuous action spaces and possible solutions (policy head, gradients w.r.t. actions).  
- Suggestion — long-horizon benchmarks: Include tasks with more delayed outcomes to probe limits vs TD methods.  
- Suggestion — writing fixes: Correct citation formatting, minor typos, and inconsistent action-count reporting.  
- Questions for authors (condensed): Which behavior policy generated future targets and were any off-policy corrections used?  
- Question: How sensitive is DFP to choice of temporal offsets and loss weights, and were longer or learned horizons tried?  
- Question: Did A3C baselines include LSTM/recurrence and frame stacking; how does DFP compare to A3C-LSTM on D3/D4?  
- Question: Provide result variability across random seeds and main hyperparameter details (replay size M, update freq k, optimizer, input normalization).  
- Question: In goal-agnostic training, how often and how are goals switched/sampled; can the agent handle mid-episode goal switches?  
- Question: What is the computational overhead of predicting all actions and any tricks for larger action sets?  
- Question: Have nonlinear utilities or learned goal embeddings been tested beyond linear combinations?
