- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict vectors of future intrinsic measurements at multiple time offsets and choose actions by maximizing goal-weighted utility.
- Uses a simple architecture with perception, measurement, and goal modules and a dueling-style prediction head (expectation + action-specific deviation).
- Claims supervised multistep measurement prediction reframes action selection, avoids bootstrapping, and supports test-time goal parameterization.
- Strong empirical gains over DQN, A3C, and DSR, especially on harder Doom tasks with richer measurement streams; won Visual Doom Full Deathmatch track.
- Shows generalization across textures/environments and to unseen goals when trained with randomized goals.
- Ablations indicate benefits from vector-valued measurements and multi-horizon prediction.
- Decision: Accept — idea is clear, simple, distinct from mainstream TD/policy-gradient RL, and addresses sparse/bootstrapping issues.
- Novelty/significance: distinct practical instantiation of goal-conditioned prediction vs. UVFA/predictive representations via direct multi-horizon measurement prediction.
- Technical soundness: training via supervised regression with multistep Monte Carlo targets is stable; dueling head sensibly separates expectation and action effects.
- Approach is well suited to settings with dense, meaningful measurements and linear utility-formulated goals.
- Empirical evaluation: four scenarios of increasing difficulty, 50M steps for baselines, averages over three seeds, long test runs, and large quantitative gains (e.g., 6x frags in D3).
- Generalization experiments are thoughtfully designed (train/test texture splits, multiple regimes) showing benefit of diversity and capacity.
- Goal-agnostic randomized-goal training approaches goal-specific performance and improves generalization to new goals.
- Related work: paper situates itself well relative to TD/Monte Carlo debates, multi-objective RL, UVFA, predictive representations, and recent 3D visuomotor RL.
- Suggestion: clarify whether A3C/DQN receive the same observation stream (images + measurements) to avoid input confound.
- Suggestion: report standard deviations/confidence intervals and provide sensitivity analyses/hyperparameter search details for baselines.
- Ablation suggestion: train DFP to predict only scalar reward (keeping everything else equal) while giving baselines full measurement inputs to isolate benefits.
- Suggestion: include recurrence (e.g., LSTM) experiments to address partial observability and provide training curves across seeds for stability assessment.
- Practical suggestion: report computational cost per step and wall-clock efficiency vs. A3C, and clarify handling of action combinatorics (total action count, pruning).
- Broader-scope suggestion: discuss limitations where meaningful measurement vectors are unavailable and how to learn suitable measurement features.
- Questions for authors: do DQN/A3C baselines receive measurement vectors or only images and scalar reward; can inputs be equalized?
- Questions: how sensitive is DFP to choice of temporal offsets and aggregation weights; any learned weighting/discount experiments?
- Questions: how does performance change for non-linear goals (learned utility networks) versus linear utility?
- Questions: what is the total number of discrete actions in D3/D4 and how does scaling to larger action spaces affect stability and sample efficiency?
- Questions: have recurrent variants of DFP been evaluated under significant partial observability, and do gains persist?
- Question/request: provide variance across seeds and statistical significance for main tables/curves.
