1. Summary of contributions
- The paper proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): instead of optimizing a scalar reward via TD methods, a network predicts vectors of future measurement changes at multiple temporal offsets, conditioned on current observation, action, and a goal vector. Actions are chosen by maximizing the inner product between the goal vector and predicted future measurements.
- The approach reduces control to supervised regression on experience, leveraging dense, multivariate measurement streams as supervision. An architecture with separate expectation and action streams (akin to dueling networks) is used.
- Empirical evaluation on four ViZDoom scenarios shows strong performance, including generalization across textures and goals, and a win in the Visual Doom AI Competition. Ablations suggest benefits from vector-valued measurements and multi-horizon prediction.

2. Decision: Reject
Key reasons:
- Insufficiently rigorous and controlled comparisons to established RL baselines; several confounds remain (training budgets, capacity, auxiliary structure, tuning), and statistical reporting is thin.
- The methodological novelty is incremental relative to established frameworks (GVFs, UVFA, successor features, Monte Carlo returns); the contribution feels like a specific instantiation rather than a substantial advance in principle.
- Scope is narrow (single domain), with limited analysis of when/why the approach succeeds or fails, and minimal theoretical grounding.

3. Supporting arguments
On problem and motivation
- The problem of exploiting dense, multivariate feedback and goal-parameterized control is important. Positioning with respect to predictive representations (GVFs), UVFA, and successor representations is acknowledged, but the paper stops short of clarifying what fundamentally distinguishes DFP beyond training via supervised regression on multi-step targets and using goal linearity.

On methodological soundness and baselines
- Fairness of comparisons is questionable:
  - A3C is from an independent implementation; details on architecture match, frame stacking, frame-skip, optimizer, and entropy schedules are not fully standardized. Dueling architecture and other architectural advantages appear in DFP but not mirrored in baselines.
  - DSR is severely undertrained relative to DFP/A3C/DQN and thus not informative.
  - Hyperparameter search is uneven across methods; only three seeds are used and no confidence intervals are reported. Given high-variance FPS environments, this is a concern.
  - Reward/measurement shaping favors dense supervision; a stronger RL baseline would include n-step/Monte Carlo returns, multi-horizon targets, and possibly auxiliary prediction tasks to match the supervision density.
- The replay/experience protocol is off-policy with a small buffer and periodically updated policy; potential biases or instabilities are not analyzed.
- The multi-horizon objective uses only the last three time steps of six offsets with ad hoc weights; sensitivity analysis is limited.

On claims and evidence
- Performance gains on Battle scenarios are compelling, but could plausibly be explained by denser supervised targets and architectural choices rather than the core idea per se. Without matched-capacity baselines that also exploit multi-horizon targets and dense measurement signals, it is difficult to attribute improvements.
- Generalization over textures is promising but still within a single simulator; the competition result is notable, yet not a controlled scientific evaluation.
- Goal-agnostic training results are interesting; however, no comparison is made to UVFA/goal-conditioned RL trained with TD or to successor features, which are natural baselines for parameterized goals.
- No statistical significance, calibration of predictions, or robustness to environment stochasticity is reported.

On novelty
- Predicting future measurement vectors as cumulants and selecting actions via a linear scalarization is very close to GVFs/UVFA/successor features. The main distinction is the supervision signal (regression to empirical future differences) and architectural packaging. The paper does not convincingly argue a conceptual advance beyond these frameworks.

4. Suggestions for improvement (constructive)
- Strengthen baselines and controls:
  - Include dueling-DQN, n-step Q-learning/Monte Carlo, and A2C/A3C with comparable architecture and capacity, matched preprocessing, and thorough hyperparameter tuning. Consider adding auxiliary prediction losses to RL baselines to equalize supervision density.
  - Add UVFA and successor-feature baselines trained with TD, using the same goal parameterization.
  - Report results over more seeds with confidence intervals and standardized training budgets (environment steps and wall-clock).
- Ablations and analysis:
  - Isolate the effect of the expectation/action stream by removing it or adding it to baselines.
  - Test sensitivity to the set of temporal offsets and weighting; analyze performance vs horizon.
  - Compare using cumulative discounted sums vs finite-difference targets; analyze bias/variance.
  - Evaluate sample efficiency, not just final performance.
- Scope and robustness:
  - Include additional domains (e.g., a subset of Atari or a different 3D environment) to show generality.
  - Analyze behavior under environment stochasticity and partial observability; consider memory.
- Clarify theory/positioning:
  - Provide a precise connection to GVFs/UVFA/successor features, and articulate what DFP adds beyond a practical recipe.
  - Discuss off-policy aspects and any convergence guarantees or known failure modes.
- Reproducibility:
  - Release code, exact network specs, preprocessing, training schedules, and environment configurations.

Questions for the authors
- Target definition: f comprises differences mt+τ − mt at multiple τ. Why choose differences rather than cumulative sums or discounted returns? How sensitive are results to this choice and to the temporal offsets and their weights?
- Normalization: How are measurements scaled/normalized during training and testing? Do different scalings change relative performance or stability?
- Architecture fairness: Did you evaluate A3C/DQN with the same perception backbone, dueling streams, and action-conditioning to control for capacity and inductive bias?
- Baseline strength: How do RL baselines perform with n-step returns or Monte Carlo targets matched to your horizons? What about UVFA or successor features trained with TD?
- Variance/robustness: What is the variance across seeds (report mean/std)? How deterministic are the environments? How does DFP handle stochastic transitions?
- Goal conditioning: What distribution over goals was used during randomized-goal training, and how does test-time performance vary with unseen goal vectors, including negative weights?
- Replay/off-policy: With a small experience memory and changing goals across episodes, is training effectively off-policy? Did you observe any instability or bias as ε decays?
- Prediction calibration: Are the predicted futures calibrated (e.g., via reliability diagrams)? Does miscalibration harm action selection?
