- Proposal: Introduces Direct Future Prediction (DFP), a goal-conditioned supervised formulation that predicts short-term changes in a vector of intrinsic measurements to select actions.  
- Acting rule: Choose the action whose predicted measurement changes maximize a linear goal utility.  
- Architecture: Perception CNN for RGB, MLPs for measurements/goals, and a dueling-style head that outputs measurement-change predictions for all actions.  
- Training: Monte-Carlo regression on experience tuples with an experience buffer; goals are included because future outcomes depend on policy conditioned on goals.  
- Experiments: Evaluated on four first-person 3D Doom tasks, showing strong gains over DQN and A3C, especially on harder combat tasks with multiple measurements.  
- Ablations: Vectorial measurements and multi-horizon prediction both improve performance; their combination yields the best results.  
- Generalization: Randomized-goal training generalizes to new goals; training on texture-randomized maps yields cross-environment transfer; higher-capacity models help.  
- Competitions: Method won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch track, supporting robustness and practicality.  
- Baselines: Compared to DQN, A3C, and DSR where feasible; baselines used dense shaped rewards aligned with measurement changes.  
- Empirical gains: Matches A3C on easiest task, superior on Navigation, and dominates combat tasks (up to 6× frags) with wider gaps when multiple measurements exist.  
- Practicality: Trains fast (millions of frames/day), simple to implement, and shows strong empirical performance.  
- Positioning: Well situated relative to Monte Carlo methods, multi-objective RL, UVFA, and predictive representations; goal-conditioning relates to UVFA.  
- Rigor and clarity: Precise formulation, clear loss and architecture descriptions, averaged learning curves, coherent main narrative with some details in appendices.  
- Decision: Accept — clear idea, strong empirical evidence, compelling generalization, and external competition win.
