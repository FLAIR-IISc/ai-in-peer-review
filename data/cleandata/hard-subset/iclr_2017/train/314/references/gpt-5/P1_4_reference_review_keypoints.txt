- Contribution: Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP) that predicts multivariate future measurements conditioned on observation, candidate action, and a goal vector; actions chosen by maximizing g^T F(o,a,g); architecture shares perception and uses a dueling-style expectation/action-difference split.

- Key claims: Dense multivariate measurement prediction stabilizes and speeds learning; goal-parameterization enables training without fixed goals and test-time goal adaptation; DFP outperforms strong deep RL baselines on ViZDoom and generalizes across environments/goals (competition win).

- Decision: Accept — motivated by an original, well-placed formulation distinct from standard deep RL and strong empirical results with meaningful ablations.

- Motivation: Well-argued departure from sparse scalar rewards toward dense, multivariate sensory/measurement feedback in embodied settings.

- Novelty: Related to UVFA/GVFs but novel in predicting intrinsic measurements directly with goal-conditioned action selection and a dueling-style action-conditional prediction architecture.

- Empirical setup: Evaluated on four Doom scenarios (increasing difficulty) plus domain-randomized variants, all from pixels requiring navigation and combat.

- Baselines: Compared to DQN, A3C, and DSR with measurement-based objectives and reasonable hyperparameter tuning for fairness.

- Outcomes: DFP matches/exceeds A3C on simple tasks and dramatically outperforms on complex Battle tasks (e.g., 6x in D3, 2.5x in D4); performance improves with richer measurement vectors and generalizes under texture randomization and goal randomization.

- Ablations: Vectorial measurement prediction and multi-horizon prediction both outperform scalar and single-horizon alternatives.

- External validation: Won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch track against a strong LSTM-based DQN that used extra supervision.

- Clarity: Exposition, formulation, architecture, and training are clearly explained with implementation details in appendices.

- Baselines feedback: Clarify whether A3C used frame stacking/LSTM and consider adding stronger or alternative baselines (e.g., UVFA-style or successor-feature variants) for fairness.

- Policy-consistency concern: Long-horizon predictions condition only on the first action and current observation, raising off-policy/policy-mismatch issues that should be discussed (and how replay/evolving policy mitigates them).

- Sensitivity analyses requested: Report sensitivity to choice of temporal offsets, loss weights, and out-of-distribution or nonlinear goal functions.

- Architecture/inputs feedback: Specify frame stacking vs. recurrence usage, architectural/optimizer details, measurement normalization, and consider releasing code and seeds.

- Scope and limitations: Discuss handling sparse/noisy/incomplete measurements and the extension to continuous action spaces (e.g., parameterizing F over continuous actions).

- Questions for authors: How are long-horizon predictions reconciled with only conditioning on the first action; did A3C include LSTM/frame stacking; sensitivity to offsets and weights; observation stack used; measurement/goal normalization; ability to handle nonlinear goals; provide training curves for goal-agnostic models; robustness to partial/delayed/corrupted measurements.

- Overall verdict: A clean, well-motivated supervised alternative to deep RL with strong empirical support and practical implications; recommend acceptance.
