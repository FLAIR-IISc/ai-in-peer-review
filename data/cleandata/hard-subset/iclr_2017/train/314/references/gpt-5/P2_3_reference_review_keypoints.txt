- Problem: Learn sensorimotor control from raw visual input in complex 3D environments with dynamically specified goals.
- Key idea: Replace scalar reward with a vector of intrinsic measurements and train a goal-conditioned predictor to forecast future measurement changes per action.
- Action selection: Choose actions greedily based on goal-weighted predicted outcomes over multiple horizons.
- Formulation: Direct Future Prediction (DFP) reduces control to supervised learning by regressing future measurement differences; goals are linear weights over predicted measurements.
- Architecture: Simple perception backbone with measurement/goal encoders and a dueling-style prediction head split into expectation and action streams.
- Empirical claim (performance): DFP outperforms DQN/A3C/DSR, especially on harder multi-measurement ViZDoom tasks.
- Empirical claim (generalization): DFP generalizes to unseen textures and to novel, unseen goals.
- Empirical claim (ablations): Vectorial feedback and multi-horizon prediction both improve performance.
- Empirical claim (competition): DFP won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch track.
- Decision: Accept — conceptually novel, clear, and empirically strong.
- Conceptual novelty: Offers a well-motivated, dense, vectorial self-supervision alternative to reward-centric deep RL.
- Empirical evidence: Substantial improvements on challenging tasks, convincing generalization, and informative ablations.
- Research question addressed: Can dense multidimensional measurement streams via supervised prediction yield effective, sample-efficient, goal-flexible control?
- Positioning: Well-placed relative to sparse-reward RL, GVFs, UVFA, multi-objective RL, and MC vs TD tradeoffs.
- Baseline competitiveness: Uses strong baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR) and shows large gains on complex “Battle” settings.
- Goal-conditioning result: Training with randomized goals generalizes well to unseen goals and nearly matches dedicated training.
- Ablation result: Predicting multiple measurements and multiple horizons aids performance even for single-metric evaluation.
- Practicality: Simple ε-greedy exploration and small replay suffice; training is fast in ViZDoom.
- Fairness caveat: DFP predicts multiple measurements while baselines optimize scalarized rewards; auxiliary multi-task/value heads for baselines were not tried.
- Scope limitation: Only discrete actions, short prediction horizons (≤32 steps), and reactive policies — may struggle with long-term credit assignment or partial observability.
- Reporting limitation: Results report means; more variance reporting and hyperparameter sensitivity analysis needed; references poorly formatted.
- Suggestion — baselines: Add UVFA-style and A3C with auxiliary measurement predictions to isolate vectorial supervision effects.
- Suggestion — analysis: Study sensitivity to horizons and specific measurements, evaluate recurrent variants, and test longer horizons/temporal abstraction.
- Suggestion — scaling/practicality: Discuss choosing measurements in new domains, noisy/sparse measurements, learned measurement discovery, and continuous/action scaling.
- Suggestion — reproducibility: Release code/configs and full architecture/hyperparameter details; fix references.
- Questions for authors: Sensitivity to measurement choice/quality and missing/corrupted measurements?
- Questions for authors: Can you provide a UVFA baseline or A3C with auxiliary measurement prediction losses?
- Questions for authors: What is the impact of horizon selection/weighting and does >32-step horizon help on Battle 2?
- Questions for authors: Have you evaluated recurrent DFP variants for memory-requiring tasks?
- Questions for authors: How sensitive is performance to replay buffer size and ε schedule; any off-policy instability?
- Questions for authors: How would DFP scale to continuous or larger action spaces (e.g., as a critic for an actor)?
