- Problem: Learn sensorimotor control from raw sensory input in complex 3D environments without sparse rewards or fixed goals.  
- Approach: Supervised learning to predict future low-dimensional measurements (health, ammo, frags) conditioned on observation, candidate action, and goal vector; choose actions that maximize predicted goal utility.  
- Model: Deep network with image, measurement, and goal modules plus a dueling-style head that predicts per-action future measurement vectors at multiple horizons; trained by Monte Carlo regression with a small replay buffer and ε-greedy exploration.  
- Claim 1: Dense, vectorial measurement prediction yields rich supervision that accelerates and stabilizes learning.  
- Claim 2: The method generalizes across environments and to dynamically specified goals.  
- Claim 3: It outperforms strong deep RL baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR) on ViZDoom tasks.  
- Claim 4: Using multiple measurements and multi-horizon prediction improves performance.  
- Claim 5: The approach won the Visual Doom AI Competition (Full Deathmatch) without extra supervision.  
- Decision: Accept — justified by strong empirical performance and methodological simplicity enabling goal-conditioned control.  
- Supporting: The paper clearly poses the predictive-supervision formulation and its practical importance for sensorimotor control.  
- Literature: Well-motivated positioning relative to Monte Carlo vs TD, UVFA/predictive representations, successor features, and multi-objective RL; distinctions are clearly explained.  
- Benchmarks: DFP matches or exceeds DQN/A3C across four ViZDoom scenarios, with notable gains in harder tasks and proper training/tuning practices reported.  
- Generalization: Training across texture variants and goal-agnostic training show strong transfer to unseen textures and new goals, including antagonistic objectives.  
- Ablations: Multi-measurement and multi-horizon predictions both improve frag performance even when evaluated on a single metric.  
- Real-world corroboration: Competition win supports robustness and generalization claims.  
- Caveat 1: No explicit UVFA-like goal-conditioned RL baseline included, making it harder to isolate benefits of vectorial prediction vs goal-conditioning.  
- Caveat 2: Method assumes availability of informative dense measurements (task-dependent requirement).  
- Caveat 3: Feedforward, memory-free model limits conclusions about partial observability and long-horizon planning; recurrence could help.  
- Caveat 4: No analysis of prediction accuracy/calibration or error distribution is provided.  
- Feedback 1: Add a UVFA-like baseline predicting goal-conditioned scalar values to disentangle benefits of vector prediction.  
- Feedback 2: Report variance/error bars, number of seeds, and statistical tests for final metrics.  
- Feedback 3: Clarify label construction and confirm loss application only to executed action’s slot.  
- Feedback 4: Analyze prediction quality (calibration/correlation) to see if improvements stem from ranking vs absolute accuracy.  
- Feedback 5: Ablate horizons, loss weights, and replay buffer size; show sample-efficiency curves.  
- Feedback 6: Include recurrent variants (and recurrent A3C) to evaluate partial observability effects.  
- Feedback 7: Discuss measurement selection principles and robustness to noisy/partial or learned measurements.  
- Questions 1: Are future measurement targets computed under the behavior policy after executing a_t, and how sensitive is DFP to off-policy data when goals change?  
- Questions 2: Is loss applied only to the executed action’s slot, and did you try inferring counterfactual labels for non-executed actions?  
- Questions 3: How were horizons and loss weights chosen, and what is performance sensitivity to different horizon sets or learned weighting?  
- Questions 4: How well does the method extrapolate to goal vectors outside training distribution and what are failure modes?  
- Questions 5: What principles guide measurement selection and have you tested noisy/partial measurements or auto-learned latent measurements?  
- Questions 6: Are predicted futures calibrated across actions/time, and does calibration correlate with control performance?  
- Questions 7: Can you provide empirical comparisons to UVFA/successor features using the same inputs and architecture?
