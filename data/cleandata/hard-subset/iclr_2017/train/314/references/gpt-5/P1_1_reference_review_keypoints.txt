- Proposal: Direct Future Prediction (DFP) reframes control as supervised, goal-conditioned prediction of multivariate future measurements per action, selecting actions greedily by predicted goal-scored utility.
- Key idea 1: Use dense, multivariate measurement streams instead of sparse scalar rewards to stabilize and enrich supervision.
- Key idea 2: Parameterize goals so one model can pursue novel goal combinations at test time.
- Key idea 3: Use a dueling-style head (expectation + action-specific advantage) to sharpen action distinctions.
- Empirical result: Strong performance in ViZDoom, outperforming DQN and A3C, especially in complex Battle scenarios and generalization tests.
- Ablations: Vector-valued and multi-horizon predictions are important for performance gains.
- External validation: Won Visual Doom AI competition Full Deathmatch track without extra supervision.
- Decision: Accept.
- Main reasons for accept: Conceptual novelty and clarity; strong empirical evidence across visual 3D tasks and generalization.
- Motivation/support: Targets raw-pixel control and dynamic goals, leveraging natural measurement streams to improve stability and sample efficiency.
- Literature positioning: Reasonable relation to UVFA, successor representations, predictive/state-representation work; connection to successor features could be deeper.
- Methodology: Straightforward multi-horizon regression objective, off-policy updates with replay, and sensible architecture separating expectation and action deltas.
- Empirical rigor: Multiple scenarios, baselines, ablations, generalization tests, and goal-agnostic training; notable large gains in difficult scenarios.
- Limitation 1: Limited theoretical grounding—no optimality guarantees and potential bias from limited horizons and weighting choices.
- Limitation 2: Sensitivity to design choices (time offsets, weighting, exploration, replay size) is underexplored and somewhat ad hoc.
- Limitation 3: Baselines and fairness—A3C tuning reported but DSR undertrained, no comparisons to recent goal-conditioned or model-based methods, only three seeds and no statistical tests.
- Limitation 4: Scope restricted to Doom; need tests on sparser-measurement domains to delineate method limits.
- Suggested improvement: Clarify relationship to successor features/representations, GPI, and transfer properties.
- Suggested improvement: Analyze horizon sensitivity—vary τ sets, weighting schedules, and longer horizons.
- Suggested improvement: Report calibration/accuracy of predicted futures vs horizon and relation to control performance.
- Suggested improvement: Expand ablations (action-stream normalization, goal-conditioning, measurement scaling, memory vs reactive inputs).
- Suggested improvement: Improve reproducibility—detail compute, update ratios, replay size, release code/configs, add more seeds/confidence intervals.
- Suggested improvement: Stress tests in sparser/partial measurement and sparse-reward settings to show where DFP is preferable.
- Key questions: Sensitivity to temporal offsets and using only last three offsets; would longer horizons help?
- Key questions: How are measurement scales normalized and how robust is performance to mis-specified or noisy measurements?
- Key questions: Can you quantify predictive accuracy across horizons/actions and its correlation with returns?
- Key questions: How critical is the action-stream normalization layer and what happens if removed or replaced?
- Key questions: Any off-policy stability issues with small replay and ε-greedy behavior; would larger/prioritized replay help?
- Key questions: How does DFP perform with sparser measurements or delayed effects?
- Key questions: Can you compare to an SF/GPI baseline to clarify similarities/differences?
- Key questions: Does adding memory (LSTM) or stacking frames change performance in Navigation/Battle?
- Key questions: How does goal-agnostic training handle test-time goals that emphasize rarely seen measurements?
