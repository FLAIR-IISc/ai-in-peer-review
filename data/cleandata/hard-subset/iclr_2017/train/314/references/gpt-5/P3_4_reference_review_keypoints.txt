- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): goal-conditioned supervised prediction of future measurement vectors to choose actions by matching predictions to a goal vector.

- Converts control from scalar TD reward optimization to regression on dense, multivariate short- to mid-horizon measurement changes.

- Network architecture: separate perception, measurement, and goal modules plus a dueling-style prediction head decomposing action-averaged expectation from action-specific deltas.

- Evaluated on multiple ViZDoom scenarios; shows strong gains over DQN and A3C in complex tasks and won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch.

- Shows generalization to unseen textures and goal transfer when trained with randomized goals.

- Ablations show vectorial (multiple measurements) and multi-horizon prediction outperform scalar reward prediction.

- Decision: Accept — cited for conceptual novelty, clarity, and strong empirical/practical impact.

- Motivation and connections: well-motivated shift to dense measurement streams; relates to Monte Carlo prediction, multi-objective RL, UVFAs, and implicitly to successor representations.

- Methodology: straightforward supervised regression on future measurement differences with greedy action selection; dueling decomposition is appropriate.

- Empirical highlights: matches A3C on simple tasks, substantially outperforms on harder navigation/combat tasks (up to 6× frags improvement).

- Training tricks: texture randomization and larger capacity improve transfer; randomized-goal training yields flexible goal-conditioned behavior.

- Practicality: trains quickly in ViZDoom and does not require privileged labels used by some competitors.

- Scientific rigor: multiple seeds and clear protocols; strong empirical evidence though lacking formal theoretical grounding.

- Baseline concerns: lacking comparisons to A3C with LSTM/auxiliary tasks, UVFA, and successor-feature baselines—these could alter conclusions.

- Measurement design concerns: needs discussion of applicability when intrinsic measurements aren’t available and sensitivity to measurement choice, normalization, and scaling.

- Requested ablations/analyses: test dueling head and action-normalization effects, report sample efficiency, more seeds/standard deviations, and study horizon weights/offsets or learned weighting.

- Scalability concerns: discuss scaling to larger discrete action spaces and continuous control, and combining DFP with memory (e.g., conv-LSTM) for partial observability.

- Evaluation metrics request: include cumulative return, survival time, per-episode distributions, not just final-episode means.

- Questions about measurements: how were they normalized, sensitivity to choices, and can learned measurement features replace hand-selected ones?

- Baseline questions: were A3C-LSTM or auxiliary-loss variants tested, and can DQN/A3C be augmented with measurement-target auxiliaries?

- Goal-conditioning questions: robustness to non-linear utilities and out-of-distribution goal vectors at test time.

- Training-detail questions: replay buffer size, update lag, ε schedules, handling of early terminations for long-horizon targets, and any off-policy instability.

- Theory/connection questions: formal relation to successor features and conditions under which DFP recovers optimal policies when rewards are linear in measurements.

- Credit-assignment question: is there evidence multi-horizon targets improve credit assignment beyond providing extra supervision?

- Overall judgment: compelling and well-executed paper; addressing baseline and analysis concerns would further strengthen the work.
