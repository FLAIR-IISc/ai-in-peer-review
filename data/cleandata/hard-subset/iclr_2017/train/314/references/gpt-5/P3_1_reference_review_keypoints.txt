- Proposal: Introduces Direct Future Prediction (DFP), predicting short-horizon intrinsic measurement changes under candidate actions conditioned on a goal vector, choosing actions by inner product with the goal.
- Key claims: Dense vector-valued measurement supervision speeds and stabilizes learning vs. sparse rewards.
- Key claims: Conditioning on parameterized goals allows training without a fixed objective and generalization to new goals.
- Key claims: A simple reactive architecture with expectation/action-stream decomposition outperforms DQN, A3C, and DSR on challenging Doom tasks and won the Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Decision: Accept.
- Primary reason 1: Conceptual clarity and practicality—reframes control as supervised prediction linking predictive representations and goal-conditioned control.
- Primary reason 2: Strong empirical evidence—consistent, substantial gains over solid baselines on rich 3D tasks and demonstration of generalization; competition win adds credibility.
- Supporting: Clear literature placement and novelty—distinct from UVFA/GVFs via multihorizon vector prediction and goal-parameterized selection in high-dimensional perception.
- Supporting: Methodologically sound—uses Monte Carlo short-horizon regression (avoids bootstrapping) and a sensible expectation/action (dueling-style) architecture.
- Supporting: Empirical rigor—four task scenarios, comparisons to DQN/A3C/DSR, ablations on vector/multihorizon benefits, texture-randomization generalization, multiple seeds and tuned baselines.
- Supporting: Significance—offers a simpler, competitive alternative to RL with appealing goal flexibility and measurement-driven supervision.
- Caveat: Baselines scope—missing stronger baselines with auxiliary prediction tasks (UNREAL-style) or recurrence; DSR limited by training speed.
- Caveat: Assumptions/generalizability—requires useful low-dimensional measurements and uses a linear goal parameterization, which may be limiting.
- Caveat: Horizon and credit assignment—short horizons (≤32 steps) and reactive policy may struggle on long-horizon, partial-observability, or exploration-heavy tasks.
- Caveat: Off-policy issues—small replay buffer and ε-greedy exploration could introduce bias; discussion of off-policy Monte Carlo targets with changing policies is needed.
- Suggestion: Add A3C variants with auxiliary measurement-prediction and/or recurrence (UNREAL-like) and clarify A3C partial-observability settings.
- Suggestion: Broaden horizon analysis—sensitivity to temporal offsets, weighting, and inclusion of longer horizons.
- Suggestion: Elaborate measurement choice—guidance for domains without obvious stats and exploration of learned (unsupervised/self-supervised) measurements.
- Suggestion: Clarify theory/connections—relation to successor representations, UVFA, when DFP approximates value functions, and placement relative to model-based RL.
- Suggestion: Improve reproducibility—move key architectural/training details into main text, release code/models, and fix reference formatting.
- Q1: Sensitivity to measurement choice/scaling and effect of irrelevant/noisy measurements on learning/action selection?
- Q2: Results with longer prediction horizons or learned horizon weighting—what breaks first as horizons increase?
- Q3: Impact of replay buffer size and ε schedule—evidence of off-policy bias or benefit from larger buffers?
- Q4: Can you include an A3C baseline augmented with (multi-horizon) measurement predictions to isolate vector supervision benefits?
- Q5: How does DFP perform under stronger partial observability (e.g., mazes) and with added recurrence?
- Q6: Can goal parameterization extend beyond linear utilities (constraints, non-linear trade-offs), and does it generalize at test time?
- Q7: In domains without explicit measurements, have you tried learning the measurement vector jointly (representation learning)?
