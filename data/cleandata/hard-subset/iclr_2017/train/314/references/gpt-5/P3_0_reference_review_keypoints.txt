- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): goal-conditioned supervised learning that predicts future intrinsic measurements and selects actions by greedy utility maximization g^T F(o,a,g).
- Claims dense, vector-valued measurement streams stabilize training compared to sparse scalar rewards.
- Claims goal-conditioning enables training without a fixed target and test-time adaptation to new goals.
- Claims a Monte-Carlo-style predictor with an expectation/action-stream decomposition learns effective pixel-based policies without bootstrapping.
- Claims DFP outperforms strong deep RL baselines on ViZDoom, generalizes across textures and goals, and won the Visual Doom AI competition.
- Contribution: architectural expectation/action decomposition inspired by dueling networks for measurement prediction.
- Contribution: empirical study across four 3D tasks with generalization experiments and ablations.
- Decision: Accept — simple, well-motivated reframing with strong empirical evidence and practical alternative to bootstrapped value learning.
- Positioned clearly within SL vs RL trade-offs and multi-objective/goal-conditioned RL; distinct from TD and successor representations.
- Formulation is clean: L2 regression on multi-horizon measurement deltas that is easy to optimize and avoids bootstrap instability.
- Expectation/action-stream split acts as a sensible inductive bias mirroring dueling architectures.
- Empirical: DFP matches or outperforms DQN and A3C on ViZDoom, with large gains on harder Battle scenarios.
- Empirical: Generalizes across unseen textures when trained on varied textures; larger capacity improves invariance.
- Empirical: Training with random goals yields policies that adapt to held-out primary goals at test time.
- Ablations show predicting multiple measurements and multiple horizons improves downstream performance, supporting dense-vector supervision.
- Broader implication: Attractive for domains with intrinsic measurements (robotics/embodied agents) and for changing goals due to practical simplicity.
- Caveat — baseline fairness/tuning: A3C hyperparameters and stronger baselines (n-step, auxiliary-task A3C/UNREAL) need fuller disclosure and comparison.
- Caveat — DSR baseline was under-trained due to speed constraints, limiting that comparison.
- Caveat — statistical rigor: results are means over three runs; need stddevs/confidence intervals and statistical tests.
- Caveat — scope/assumptions: requires low-dimensional intrinsic measurements and linear utility; may not suit tasks needing long-horizon credit assignment or memory.
- Caveat — learning signal coverage: only the executed action’s target is supervised, raising concerns about predictions for rarely executed actions.
- Caveat — exploration: uses epsilon-greedy with no intrinsic exploration or uncertainty estimation, which may limit sparse-reward performance.
- Question — baselines: provide full A3C config (workers, recurrence, frame stacking, reward clipping, entropy) and whether auxiliary dense-supervision tasks were tried.
- Question — non-executed actions: measured calibration/accuracy for non-executed action predictions, and any methods to reduce hallucinated advantages?
- Question — horizons/discounting: sensitivity to temporal offsets and weights, and whether geometric discounting can be emulated via g and offsets.
- Question — goal parametrization: have nonlinear goal parametrizations (e.g., goal MLP) been explored for complex preferences?
- Question — uncertainty/risk: any experiments predicting distributions (quantiles) or using ensembles to capture uncertainty and enable risk-sensitive control?
- Question — memory/PO: any experiments with recurrence or on tasks requiring memory (long corridors, occlusions)?
- Question — no explicit measurements: can learned latent measurements replace hand-defined vectors, and how does that affect performance?
- Feedback — clarify training details: experience memory size, update lag, ε schedule, optimizer, input preprocessing, and network capacity vs baselines.
- Feedback — report sample efficiency and wall-clock time, and include variance across seeds.
- Feedback — provide calibration/error plots for predicted vs realized measurement deltas by horizon and by action.
- Feedback — analyze failure modes needing long-term planning and discuss extensions with recurrence or hierarchical options.
- Feedback — explicitly discuss connections and trade-offs with successor representations and goal-conditioned value functions.
