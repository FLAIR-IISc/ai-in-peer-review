- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future measurements from sensory inputs/actions as a supervised alternative to sparse-reward RL.  
- Strong empirical performance in VizDoom: outperforms DQN/A3C/DSR and wins the Full Deathmatch track.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — significant contribution, solid motivation, clear writing, thorough experiments.  
- Novelty: Insightful reframing of sensorimotor control as supervised prediction; ties to SL vs RL debate.  
- Empirical strength: Convincing results and ablation studies showing benefits of multi-dimensional feedback and multi-step prediction.  
- Generalization: Demonstrates transfer to unseen environments and goal adaptation without retraining.  
- Presentation: Clear exposition, helpful architecture figure, and baseline details aiding reproducibility.  
- Theoretical gap: Reviewer requests deeper theoretical analysis (e.g., relation between prediction error and the Bellman equation).  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Calls for more analysis of temporal offsets (τs) and loss coefficient choices.  
- Computational cost: Wants detailed comparison of training time and memory requirements versus baselines.  
- Error analysis: Recommends qualitative analysis of failure cases to reveal limitations.  
- Questions for authors: Explore non-linear goal functions u(f;g), alternative normalization methods in action stream, sensitivity to chosen measurements, and scalability to more complex real-world environments.
