- Proposes supervised sensorimotor control by predicting future measurements from interleaved sensory and measurement streams instead of reward-based RL.  
- Trains agents to predict effects of actions on future measurements, enabling dense supervision and flexible goal specification.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance in Doom, outperforming DQN, A3C, DSR and winning the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch.  
- Conceptually simple method with strong empirical validation and useful analysis of vectorial feedback benefits.  
- Recommendation: Accept enthusiastically due to significant contribution and impressive results.  
- Addresses RL challenge of learning control from raw sensory input and motivates departure from sparse reward signals.  
- Positions work well within RL literature and highlights advantages of multi-dimensional sensory feedback.  
- Ablation studies show importance of vectorial feedback and multi-step prediction; generalization analysis across textures shows robustness.  
- Paper is clearly written, with well-explained architecture and reproducible experimental setup.  
- Suggests adding formal theoretical analysis of convergence and relation to temporal-difference methods.  
- Requests more thorough discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and tuning to improve reproducibility.  
- Asks for a detailed comparison of computational cost (training time, memory) versus baselines.  
- Recommends an explicit discussion of limitations and environments/tasks where the method may fail.  
- Question on goal vector g: sensitivity to its choice and whether automated/adaptive goal learning was explored.  
- Question on architecture: rationale for expectation/action stream separation and results of alternative architectures.  
- Question on scalability: how performance scales with higher-resolution inputs and larger action spaces.  
- Question on real-world deployment: anticipated challenges from sensor noise and uncertainty for robotics applications.
