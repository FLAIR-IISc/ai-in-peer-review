- Proposes reframing sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future measurements of sensory streams rather than using scalar rewards.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance in 3D Doom environments, outperforming DQN/A3C/DSR and winning the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Argues the method leverages rich temporal structure and multi-dimensional feedback to select goal-directed actions without engineered rewards.  
- Positions the work within prior prediction-based literature (Jordan & Rumelhart, Sutton) and claims novel application to control.  
- Clear model and experimental presentation, supported by ablations and a supplementary video.  
- Practical significance emphasized by competition win as evidence of real-world viability.  
- Recommends adding a fuller discussion of limitations and environments where the approach may fail.  
- Requests analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and robustness of the proposed method.  
- Suggests reporting computational cost and comparisons to training resources for RL baselines.  
- Asks for clearer intuition and examples on goal parameterization (how vector g maps to behaviors).  
- Questions how measurement selection affects performance and whether measurements can be learned automatically.  
- Raises scalability concerns for high-dimensional state spaces and identifying concise measurement sets.  
- Inquires about exploration strategies beyond epsilon-greedy (e.g., intrinsic motivation) and their effects.  
- Seeks clarification on differences from imitation learning and how this method avoids relying on expert demonstrations.
