- Proposes framing sensorimotor control as supervised prediction of future sensory measurements instead of reward-based RL, enabling dense supervision and dynamic goal specification.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance in Doom, outperforming deep RL baselines and winning the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch track.
- Recommendation: Accept (minor revisions) due to significant challenge tackled, conceptual simplicity, and strong experimental validation.
- Novelty: Recasting control as measurement prediction is a compelling, biologically motivated alternative to traditional RL.
- Empirical validation: Results are impressive, with superior performance on complex Doom tasks and informative ablations showing the importance of vectorial feedback and multi-step prediction.
- Generalization: Shows good transfer across environments and adaptability to dynamically specified goals, addressing a common RL limitation.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, and provides sufficient experimental and architectural details for reproducibility.
- Suggest adding theoretical analysis on convergence and comparisons with traditional RL to strengthen the paper.
- Recommend a more detailed hyperparameter sensitivity analysis to demonstrate robustness.
- Ask for clearer reporting of computational costs (training time, GPU memory) given intensive experiments.
- Request guidance or heuristics for selecting effective measurements and whether a principled selection method exists.
- Suggest a more explicit comparison to imitation learning, clarifying similarities, differences, and trade-offs.
- Question: In D3-tx and D4-tx experiments, what fraction of textures were completely novel versus variations of seen textures?
- Question: How expressive is the goal vector g for encoding complex or abstract goals, and what are limitations or strategies for such goals?
- Question: Why was the chosen action-stream normalization used, were alternatives tested, and how critical is this design choice?
- Question: How would hierarchical RL integrate with this prediction-based approach—would prediction targets differ across hierarchy levels?
