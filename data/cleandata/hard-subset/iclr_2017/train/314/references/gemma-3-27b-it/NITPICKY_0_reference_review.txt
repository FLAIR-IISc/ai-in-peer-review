This paper proposes a novel approach to sensorimotor control, framing it as a supervised learning problem by predicting future measurements from sensory input and actions, rather than relying on sparse scalar rewards typical of reinforcement learning. The core idea – leveraging the rich temporal structure of sensory and measurement streams – is compelling and potentially impactful. The authors demonstrate strong empirical results in Doom, even winning a competition, outperforming more complex approaches.

**Reject.** While the empirical results are impressive, the paper suffers from a multitude of minor flaws, inconsistencies, and a lack of rigor in presentation that, cumulatively, overshadow the potentially significant contribution. The review borders on obsessive detail, but aims to highlight areas requiring significant improvement before publication.

The issues begin with the introduction, which, while conceptually sound, is excessively verbose and contains unnecessary historical context. The writing style is also inconsistent; for instance, shifting between formal mathematical notation and colloquial phrasing ("a multidimensional stream of sensations is a more appropriate model for an organism"). The background section contains a litany of cited works without sufficient synthesis – it reads like a laundry list rather than a coherent discussion of related research.  Specific citations are also questionable; for example, citing Adolph & Berger (2006) as justification for a dense reward signal seems tenuous. 

The model description is plagued by inconsistencies. Equation (2) defines *p<sub>at</sub>* as the prediction, yet the subsequent text consistently refers to *pat*. The architecture description, particularly the expectation/action stream split (Section 3.2), lacks clarity and justification. Why is a normalization layer (Equation 6) necessary? What are the implications of its application? The claim that "introducing additional structure…enhances its ability to learn the fine differences between actions" requires empirical support, not assertion.  The description of training (Section 3.1) is vague; what optimizer is used? What are the specific hyperparameters? How is the learning rate schedule defined?  The term “experience memory” feels imprecise; it’s a replay buffer, and should be called as such.

The experimental section is particularly problematic. The setup section uses imprecise language (“more complicated maze”). What *specifically* makes D4 more complex? The selection of temporal offsets (τ1,…τn) seems arbitrary – is there a justification for these values? The ablation study (Table 4) only compares against an extremely limited set of baselines. Why not compare against a model trained with only the *most* important measurements, as determined by some criterion? The discussion of the Visual Doom AI Competition is self-congratulatory and provides insufficient details about the competing methods – specifically, what *additional* supervision was used by the second-place submission?

Throughout the paper, tables and figures lack sufficient captions and clear labeling. Figure 3 needs a legend; the lines are indistinguishable. Table 1 reports “average frags” but doesn't define the episode length – is this a rate or a total?  The paper makes several sweeping statements ("our approach significantly outperforms…") that are not adequately supported by statistical analysis.  There's a consistent lack of error bars or confidence intervals. The use of abbreviations like “DFP” is introduced without prior definition. Finally, the formatting is inconsistent (e.g., inconsistent spacing after periods).

**Additional Feedback (for potential revision):**

*   **Clarify Notation:** Ensure consistency in variable names and mathematical notation.
*   **Justify Architectural Choices:** Provide a rationale for all architectural decisions, especially the normalization layer.
*   **Provide Hyperparameter Details:** Report all hyperparameters used in training and experimentation.
*   **Statistical Significance:** Include error bars or confidence intervals to support claims of performance improvement.
*   **Detailed Baseline Comparison:** Provide a more comprehensive comparison against relevant baselines, including a thorough description of their implementation and hyperparameter settings.
*   **Improve Figure and Table Clarity:** Ensure all figures and tables are clearly labeled, have informative captions, and are easy to interpret.
*   **Strengthen the Connection to Existing Literature:** Instead of simply listing related work, synthesize it and explain how this work builds upon or differs from existing approaches.
*   **Address the Arbitrariness of Temporal Offsets:** Provide a justification for the choice of temporal offsets.
*   **Clarify Experimental Setup:**  Provide precise descriptions of the experimental environments and tasks.

**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  What is the theoretical justification for the normalization layer in the action stream?
2.  How sensitive are the results to the choice of temporal offsets (τ1,…τn)? Have you performed an ablation study to evaluate the impact of different offset values?
3.  Can you provide more details about the second-place submission in the Visual Doom AI Competition, specifically regarding the type and amount of additional supervision used?
4.  What specific steps were taken to ensure the reproducibility of the experimental results?
5.  Was any attempt made to perform a statistical significance test (e.g., t-test, ANOVA) to determine if the observed performance differences are statistically significant? If not, why?
