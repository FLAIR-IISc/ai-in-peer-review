- Reframes sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future measurements from current sensory input and actions.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance in Doom, outperforming deep RL baselines and winning the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Argues that dense, multi-dimensional measurement signals are more informative than sparse scalar rewards.  
- Novel approach with solid motivation and clear connections to prior work (Jordan & Rumelhart, Sutton).  
- Empirical validation is strong across multiple Doom scenarios and against established algorithms (DQN, A3C, DSR).  
- Ablation studies show the importance of vectorial measurements and multi-step prediction; model generalizes across textures and goals.  
- Paper is well-written with helpful figures and supplementary video.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Request for deeper theoretical analysis, including convergence properties and discussion of limitations.  
- Ask for analysis of sensitivity to hyperparameters (architecture, temporal offsets) for the proposed method.  
- Concern about scalability to more complex real-world, higher-dimensional sensory inputs and dynamics.  
- Suggest expanding on limitations of linear goal representation and possible alternatives for goal functions.  
- Ask for more details on experience replay sampling strategy (uniform vs. prioritized).  
- Questions for authors: examples of generalization failures; detailed architectural differences from DQN; feasibility of predicting full sensory streams long-term; specifics of goal vector randomization in goal-agnostic training.
