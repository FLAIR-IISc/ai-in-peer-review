- Reframes reinforcement learning as supervised learning by training agents to predict effects of actions on future vectorial measurements from raw sensory input, showing strong empirical performance.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Strong empirical results that outperform state-of-the-art methods and win the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Novel, well-motivated approach emphasizing dense, multi-dimensional feedback over sparse scalar rewards, with convincing connections to prior work.  
- Clear and comprehensive experiments: comparisons to baselines, generalization tests, importance of vectorial feedback, goal-agnostic training, and ablation studies.  
- Well-written and organized with sufficient implementation and experimental details for reproducibility.  
- Suggestion to include a more thorough discussion of limitations, e.g., reliance on well-defined measurements and scalability to more complex environments.  
- Recommend providing theoretical analysis on convergence conditions and sample-efficiency guarantees to bolster empirical claims.  
- Request for a more detailed comparison of computational cost and training speed versus baselines.  
- Question on sensitivity to measurement selection and whether relevant measurements can be automated or learned.  
- Question about extending the approach to continuous action spaces and required methodological changes.  
- Question on the effects and trade-offs of longer prediction horizons beyond the current ~32 steps.  
- Question asking for a direct comparison with reward shaping using engineered dense rewards to assess relative benefits of measurement prediction.
