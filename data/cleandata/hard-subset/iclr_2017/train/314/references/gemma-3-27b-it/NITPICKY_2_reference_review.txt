This paper proposes a novel approach to sensorimotor control, framing it as a supervised learning problem by predicting future measurements from sensory input and actions, rather than relying on traditional reward-based reinforcement learning. The core idea – leveraging the interlocked temporal structure of sensory and measurement streams – is interesting and potentially impactful, particularly in complex, immersive environments where defining a sparse reward can be challenging. The authors demonstrate strong performance in the Doom environment, even winning the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition, surpassing a significantly more complex baseline.

**Reject.** While the experimental results are impressive, particularly the competition win, the paper suffers from a multitude of minor flaws and lacks sufficient rigor in its presentation and analysis. The sheer number of these issues, even if individually small, collectively undermine confidence in the reproducibility and generalizability of the findings.

The most significant issue is the lack of detailed explanation regarding hyperparameter choices and architectural specifics. While Appendix A is mentioned, it's not readily accessible in this review process and even then, lacks crucial detail. For example, the specific convolutional network architecture used for the perception module is not fully described, making replication difficult.  The choice of temporal offsets (1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32) feels arbitrary and lacks justification. Why these specific offsets? Were other configurations explored? Similarly, the coefficients (0.5, 0.5, 1) for weighting future measurements are not adequately motivated. The ablation study, while present, feels incomplete. The reasoning behind choosing *only* those ablations isn't clear. Why not ablate the action stream, for example?

The writing quality is uneven. Several sentences are convoluted and could benefit from clearer phrasing. The connection between the theoretical motivation (Jordan & Rumelhart, Sutton’s work) and the practical implementation isn't always well-articulated. The discussion section feels somewhat superficial, glossing over potential limitations and future directions. The comparison to prior work is thorough but lacks critical assessment. Simply stating that the approach outperforms others isn't enough; a deeper analysis of *why* it outperforms them is needed.  The claim of being “simpler” than the winning competition submission requires more justification, considering the described network architecture.  

Furthermore, the experiments, while impressive, lack sufficient statistical rigor.  Reporting average performance is good, but including standard deviations or confidence intervals would strengthen the claims.  The ablation study lacks a clear indication of statistical significance. How much do the reported performance differences truly matter? The presentation of results in tables is sometimes unclear, and the link between tables and the accompanying text isn't always seamless.

**Additional Feedback (for potential revision):**

*   **Detailed Architecture:** Provide a complete description of the network architecture, including layer sizes, activation functions, and any regularization techniques used. Include a diagram in the paper itself, not just in an appendix.
*   **Hyperparameter Tuning:**  Explain the process used for selecting hyperparameters. Were they chosen via grid search, random search, or another method? Report the range of values explored for each hyperparameter.
*   **Justification of Choices:** Provide clear justifications for all key design choices, such as the temporal offsets, weighting coefficients, and network architecture.
*   **Statistical Analysis:**  Report statistical significance for all performance comparisons. Include standard deviations or confidence intervals.
*   **Ablation Study Expansion:**  Expand the ablation study to include more comprehensive analyses of different components of the model.
*   **Error Analysis:** Perform an error analysis to identify common failure modes of the model.
*   **Code Release:** Releasing the code would significantly increase the reproducibility and impact of the work.
*   **Clarify the Novelty:** Precisely delineate what aspects of this approach are truly novel compared to existing work in supervised learning and reinforcement learning.

**Questions for the Authors:**

*   Can you provide the complete architecture details, including all layer sizes and activation functions, directly within the paper?
*   What methods were used for hyperparameter tuning, and what range of values were explored for the key hyperparameters?
*   Why were the specific temporal offsets (1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32) chosen? Were other configurations considered, and if so, what were their results?
*   Could you provide a more detailed explanation of why your approach is considered "simpler" than the winning submission in the Visual Doom AI Competition, given the described network architecture?
*   What are the computational costs associated with training this model compared to the baseline RL methods?
