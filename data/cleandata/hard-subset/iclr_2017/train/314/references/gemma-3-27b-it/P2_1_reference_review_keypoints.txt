- Reframes sensorimotor control as supervised prediction of future measurements instead of reinforcement learning, leveraging temporal structure and vectorial measurements.  
- Empirical claim: outperforms state-of-the-art deep RL on Doom and wins the Full Deathmatch Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Novelty/motivation: insightful reframing addressing sparse rewards and learning from raw sensory input.  
- Strong empirical results: competitive SOTA performance, competition win, and ablations showing importance of vectorial measurements and multi-step prediction.  
- Generalization/goal flexibility: demonstrates transfer across environments and adaptability to dynamically specified goals.  
- Presentation: generally well-written with helpful figures and tables.  
- Theoretical justification needed: request for convergence/sample-complexity analysis and relation to SL theory.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: ask for sensitivity analysis on architecture, learning rate, and other hyperparameters.  
- Comparison to imitation learning: request clearer comparison/contrast with imitation learning approaches.  
- Network details: request more detailed architecture (layers, filter sizes) in main paper for reproducibility.  
- Q1: What prevents predicting full sensory streams and what developments could enable it?  
- Q2: Is there an optimal prediction horizon or does performance plateau with more steps?  
- Q3: What specific challenges did the unseen Full Deathmatch environments pose and which method components drove success?  
- Q4: Have you tried more complex goal functions beyond u(f;g)=g>f, and how did they affect performance/generalization?
