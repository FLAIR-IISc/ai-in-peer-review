- Reframes sensorimotor control as supervised prediction of future measurements instead of reward-based RL.  
- Claims predicting future measurements provides denser, more informative supervision and better performance.  
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art on Visual Doom tasks and won the Full Deathmatch competition.  
- Demonstrates generalization across environments and goals and shows benefits of vectorial measurements.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Novelty: Significant, well-motivated shift from RL to prediction-based learning with contextual connections to TD learning.  
- Strong empirical validation: consistent wins, ablations showing importance of vector feedback and multi-step prediction.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: well-structured paper with detailed model, training, and experimental setup.  
- Generalization highlighted as a key practical strength over environment-specific methods.  
- Request for more detailed computational cost analysis (e.g., GPU hours) to assess scalability.  
- Ask for discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and which settings critically affect performance.  
- Recommend qualitative error analysis to identify failure modes and situations where the model struggles.  
- Suggest explicitly relating and distinguishing the method from prior "world models" work.  
- Question whether improved generalization arises from robust representations or simply more varied training data.  
- Question whether the goal mapping u(f; g) can be more complex or learned rather than linear.  
- Question scalability to higher-dimensional, real-world robotics and key adaptation challenges.  
- Question whether stochastic action selection was tried and how it impacts performance.
