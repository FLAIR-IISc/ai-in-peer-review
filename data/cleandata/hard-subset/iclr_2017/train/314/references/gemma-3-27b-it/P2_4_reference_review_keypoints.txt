- Proposes reframing sensorimotor control as supervised prediction of future measurements from raw sensory inputs instead of using scalar rewards.  
- Uses dense, multivariate measurements (e.g., health, ammo) to overcome sparse reward problems.  
- Shows strong empirical performance in the Doom environment, including winning the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — approach is compelling and results convincing.  
- Novelty: Insightful prediction-based framing; denser feedback argued as superior to sparse rewards; links to SL vs RL literature.  
- Empirical validation: Consistently outperforms baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR) across four Doom scenarios.  
- Ablation studies: Demonstrate the importance of vectorial feedback and multi-step prediction.  
- Generalization: Good performance on randomly textured maps, especially when trained with diverse goals.  
- Presentation: Well-written with a clear explanation and helpful architectural diagram.  
- Theoretical gap: Paper needs deeper theoretical justification or links to policy/value-based RL frameworks.  
- Hyperparameter reporting: Limited details on DFP hyperparameters and sensitivity; more analysis requested.  
- Computational cost: No comparison of training time or memory requirements versus baselines.  
- Goal representation: Assumes linear goal-param measurement relationship; suggest exploring non-linear representations.  
- Error analysis: Lacks analysis of failure modes and specific situations where measurement predictions fail.  
- Questions for authors: Ask about theoretical motivations, hyperparameter ranges/sensitivity, computational cost comparison, experiments with complex goal representations, and examples/causes of prediction failures.
