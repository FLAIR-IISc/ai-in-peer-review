- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Core idea: Frame sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future measurements (e.g., health, ammo) from sensory input and actions.
- Motivation: Predicting multi-dimensional, dense feedback is argued to be more natural and efficient than sparse scalar rewards in complex environments.
- Empirical strength: Strong results in Doom, outperforming state-of-the-art deep RL and winning the Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Vectorial feedback: Demonstrates benefits of vector-valued measurements over scalar reward signals.
- Ablation studies: Thorough experiments isolating components, showing importance of vectorial feedback and multi-step prediction.
- Generalization: Shows transfer to unseen environments and the ability to adapt to new goals without retraining.
- Presentation: Paper is clear, well-written, and logically presented.
- Theoretical needs: Lacks rigorous theoretical analysis (e.g., convergence properties versus TD learning); a brief discussion would help.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Needs more detail on robustness to learning rate, batch size, and architecture choices.
- Computational cost: More detailed breakdown of training resources and runtime comparisons to baselines is requested.
- Failure cases: Paper should discuss limitations and scenarios where the approach struggles.
- Relation to MPC: Should explicitly compare and contrast with model-predictive control approaches.
- Questions for authors: Sensitivity to goal vector design and whether the model learns internal goal representations.
- Questions for authors: Rationale or search process for chosen temporal offsets (1,2,4,8,16,32).
- Questions for authors: Request for detailed GPU hours/training resource figures.
- Questions for authors: Concerns about scalability to more complex, higher-dimensional real-world environments.
