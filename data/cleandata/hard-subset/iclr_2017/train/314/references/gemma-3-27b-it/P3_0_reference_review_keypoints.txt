- Main claim: Reframe RL as supervised learning by predicting future measurements (e.g., health, ammo) from sensory input, actions, and goals (Direct Future Prediction, DFP).
- Empirical performance: DFP achieves state-of-the-art results in Doom, outperforming DQN/A3C/DSR and winning the Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Key advantage: Dense, multi-dimensional feedback enables better learning and generalization than sparse scalar rewards.
- Generalization and flexibility: Method generalizes across textured mazes and adapts to dynamically specified goals.
- Ablations: Multi-dimensional measurements and predicting multiple future time steps are crucial to performance.
- Presentation: Paper is clearly written with helpful architecture diagrams and thorough comparison to prior work.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Theoretical gap: Lacks formal justification for why DFP works and why multiple future steps help.
- Hyperparameter concerns: Needs discussion of sensitivity and robustness to learning rate, architecture, and prediction horizon.
- Computational cost: More detailed comparison of training time and resource trade-offs versus baselines is needed.
- Limitations: Should explicitly discuss scenarios where DFP may fail (e.g., very sparse/delayed feedback).
- Diagnostics: Recommend adding visualizations of predicted future measurements to aid interpretation.
- Questions for authors: Clarify challenges and potential fixes for predicting full sensory streams and whether visual-prediction methods could help.
- Questions for authors: Explain intuition for importance of multiple-step predictions and provide competition training/hyperparameter details.
- Questions for authors: Discuss anticipated challenges and solutions for extending DFP to continuous action spaces.
