- Proposes framing sensorimotor control as supervised prediction of future measurements instead of reward-based RL.  
- Leverages temporal structure and vectorial measurements to choose actions aligned with goals.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance in Doom and wins Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch.  
- Shows generalization to new environments and goals; multi-step prediction and vectorial measurements are important.  
- Novel, well-motivated shift from sparse rewards to rich, dense supervisory signals.  
- Strong experimental results and ablation studies supporting core claims.  
- Paper is well-written with clear technical and experimental descriptions.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Limitation: Reliance on well-defined measurements; unclear performance with noisy or incomplete measurements.  
- Request more detailed comparison of computational cost (training time, memory) versus baseline RL methods.  
- Suggest reporting sensitivity to hyperparameters.  
- Recommend clearer distinction from model-based and other prediction-based RL in related work.  
- Suggest deeper ablation on measurement window sizes (number of future steps predicted).  
- Question: Challenges and future directions for predicting full sensory streams.  
- Question: Whether alternative goal formulations to u(f;g)=g>f were explored and their effects.  
- Question: Is there a point of diminishing returns when increasing the number of predicted measurements?  
- Question: How does the diversity/range of training goals affect generalization to new goals?
