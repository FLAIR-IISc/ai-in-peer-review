- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP), reframing RL as supervised learning by predicting future sensory/measurement streams instead of scalar rewards.  
- Claims DFP enables learning generalizable sensorimotor skills by leveraging dense, multi-dimensional feedback.  
- Empirical results are strong: DFP outperforms DQN/A3C/DSR on challenging Doom tasks and won the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Ablation studies show benefits of vectorial measurements and multi-horizon predictions; representations generalize and support goal-agnostic training.  
- Paper is well-written, with a clear architecture and thorough experimental setup.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions based on empirical strength and clarity.  
- Suggestion: Provide a stronger theoretical justification (e.g., links to information theory or optimal control).  
- Suggestion: Discuss sensitivity to key hyperparameters like temporal offsets and network details.  
- Suggestion: Report computational cost comparisons (training time, memory) versus baselines.  
- Suggestion: Describe failure cases or scenarios where DFP underperforms.  
- Suggestion: Outline future work, e.g., combining DFP with memory-based models or hierarchical RL.
