- Proposes framing sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting effects of actions on future measurement streams instead of RL with sparse rewards.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results in Doom, outperforming deep RL baselines and winning the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions due to clear contribution and convincing experiments.  
- Novelty: Well-motivated departure from RL that leverages temporally dense sensory and measurement streams.  
- Empirical strength: Broad evaluation, ablations, and competitive win substantiate effectiveness.  
- Presentation: Paper is clear, well-written, and reproducible with detailed architecture and experimental setup.  
- Generalization and flexibility: Shows transfer across environments (D3-tx, D4-tx) and adaptation to dynamically specified goals.  
- Suggestion: Add deeper theoretical grounding (connections to supervised learning theory, convergence/generalization analysis).  
- Suggestion: Provide a systematic analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (learning rates, architectures).  
- Suggestion: Expand comparison to imitation learning, especially regarding distribution shift.  
- Suggestion: Report computational cost details (GPUs, training time) for reproducibility and practicality.  
- Question: Is D4→D4-tx improvement due mainly to texture diversity or other factors like network capacity?  
- Question: Does the distribution of randomized training goals affect adaptation to novel goals?  
- Question: Any theoretical investigations on sample complexity or convergence rates?
