- Framing sensorimotor control as supervised direct future prediction instead of reward-based RL.
- Train agent to predict effects of actions on future measurements, bypassing a traditional reward function.
- Strong empirical results in Doom: outperforms state-of-the-art RL and won the Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Claims improved sample efficiency and generalization; ablation studies show benefits of vectorial feedback and multi-step prediction.
- Paper is well-written with a clear architecture and experimental setup.
- Experiments demonstrate generalization across different Doom environments.
- Lacks detailed theoretical analysis (e.g., convergence properties compared to TD learning).
- Needs more analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (learning rate, network architecture, etc.).
- Missing discussion of computational cost versus other RL methods (training time, resources).
- Should discuss broader applicability and limitations for other domains (e.g., robotics).
- Needs more guidance on practical selection of the goal vector representation.
- Question: Have you explored non-linear forms for u(f; g) (e.g., neural networks) instead of linear combinations?
- Question: Any insights or heuristic arguments about theoretical convergence of the method?
- Question: How does training time compare to A3C and DSR given the same number of simulation steps?
- Question: Have you tried different normalization schemes for the action stream and tested sensitivity?
