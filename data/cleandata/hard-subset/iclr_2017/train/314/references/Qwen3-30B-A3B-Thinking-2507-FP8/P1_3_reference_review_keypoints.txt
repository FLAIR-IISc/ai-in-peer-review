- Novel approach reframes reinforcement learning as supervised learning by predicting future measurement streams (health, ammo, frags) instead of using sparse scalar rewards.  
- Direct Future Prediction (DFP) trains agents to predict action effects on future measurements and enables flexible, test-time goal specification.  
- Validated in Doom with extensive experiments showing DFP outperforms DQN, A3C, and DSR across tasks.  
- Real-world validation: DFP achieved ~50% higher performance than the next-best entry in the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch.  
- Ablation studies indicate vectorial measurements and multi-temporal prediction are critical to DFP’s success.  
- The paper is well-motivated and well-positioned in literature, arguing supervised learning is appropriate with dense feedback.  
- Contributions include clarifying the role of cotemporal sensory and measurement streams as effective supervision for embodied agents.  
- Recommendation: Accept, based on strong empirical results and clear contribution.  
- Request stronger theoretical connections to frameworks like predictive state representations and successor representations.  
- Ask for clearer justification of the two-stream prediction module and comparison to simpler architectures.  
- Suggest adding concrete examples of extensions (e.g., memory incorporation, continuous actions) to strengthen impact.  
- Recommend discussing limitations, especially environments lacking meaningful measurements.  
- Question: How does measurement-stream supervision differ from intrinsic rewards/curiosity, and why might it be superior?  
- Question: Why is predicting a single measurement (e.g., frags) insufficient compared to multiple measurements?  
- Question: How were measurements selected and is there a principled way to choose them for new environments?  
- Question: How does DFP compare to goal-conditioned RL (e.g., Schaul et al. 2015) and what specific advantages does it offer?
