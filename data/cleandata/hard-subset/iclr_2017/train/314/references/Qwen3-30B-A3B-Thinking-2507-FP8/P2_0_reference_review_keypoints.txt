- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP), reframing RL as supervised learning by predicting future measurement vectors (e.g., health, ammo) to select actions.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance, generalization across environments/goals, and a win in the Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Decision: Accept — compelling contribution with strong empirical support.
- Identifies the problem that RL struggles with sparse rewards and acquiring general, flexible skills in complex 3D sensorimotor tasks.
- Motivates approach well and situates it in prior work, claiming novelty in using vectorial measurement prediction for goal-directed behavior.
- Provides robust experimental validation: compares to DQN/A3C/DSR across four Doom scenarios, includes ablations and generalization tests, and reports statistically significant gains.
- Acknowledges limitations such as discrete action spaces and reliance on an available measurement stream.
- Suggestion: clarify how measurement streams are defined and whether they are hand-crafted or can be discovered automatically.
- Suggestion: discuss computational complexity and compare training time/resource trade-offs with baselines.
- Suggestion: expand discussion on adapting the approach to continuous control (important for robotics).
- Question: how would DFP handle environments where meaningful measurements are not readily available or must be learned from sensory input?
- Question: how did the measurement stream enable winning the competition without extra supervision used by other teams?
- Question: how would the method handle completely novel goal types requiring predictions of measurements not seen during training?
