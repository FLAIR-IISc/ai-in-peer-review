- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP), reframing reinforcement learning as supervised learning by predicting future low-dimensional measurements from sensory streams.  
- Claims DFP outperforms deep RL on complex 3D tasks (Doom), generalizes across environments, handles test-time goal specification, and won the Visual Doom AI Competition by >50%.

- Decision: Accept.

- Main reason for acceptance: conceptually clear, well-motivated alternative to RL that addresses sparse rewards and raw sensory input challenges.  
- Empirical reason: robust, thorough experiments showing consistent superiority over strong baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR) across progressively harder scenarios.

- Core insight: temporal structure of measurement streams supplies dense supervision, enabling supervised learning to replace sparse-reward RL in these settings.  
- Experimental support: systematic comparisons, ablation study showing benefits of multi-measurement and multi-temporal-offset prediction, and strong competition results even versus methods using extra supervision.

- Well-situated in literature with links to predictive representations, multi-objective decision-making, and parameterized goals, filling the gap of leveraging multidimensional feedback.

- Suggestion: add a clearer theoretical justification for why supervised prediction of measurements outperforms RL in the studied settings.  
- Suggestion: discuss limitations where measurements aren’t available and how to learn or infer measurements (e.g., via self-supervision).  
- Suggestion: more explicitly position the work relative to recent predictive state and successor representation literature.

- Question: How would DFP perform in the Doom competition if enemy-presence ground truth were unavailable during training?  
- Question: Is there a principled sampling distribution for training goal vectors beyond uniform random, and could that improve generalization?  
- Question: How exactly does dense measurement supervision improve learning dynamics (convergence speed, stability, exploration) over sparse rewards?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the normalization layer ensuring action-stream predictions average to zero, and would alternatives or omission change results?
