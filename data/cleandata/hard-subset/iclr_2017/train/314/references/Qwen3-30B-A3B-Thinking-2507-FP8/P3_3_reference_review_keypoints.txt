- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): reframe RL as supervised learning by predicting future measurements (health, ammo, frag) from sensory input and goal.  
- Claims DFP outperforms deep RL baselines on Visual Doom tasks, especially when using rich measurement vectors.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — strong contribution, well-motivated and empirically validated.  
- Strong theoretical motivation linking supervised prediction ideas to prior work (Jordan & Rumelhart, Sutton).  
- Compelling empirical results: large gains in complex Doom scenarios, ~50% improvement over second-best in competition.  
- Thorough evaluation: ablations on vector measurements and temporal offsets, generalization and goal-agnostic training experiments.  
- Simple, easily implementable architecture comparable in complexity to standard RL baselines.  
- Clarify “extraneous data” claim since game-provided measurements are part of environment, not external data.  
- Expand theoretical justification for why predictive measurement supervision yields better training dynamics than sparse rewards.  
- Provide a more detailed comparison to the second-best baseline (Lample & Chaplot, 2016) to contextualize the 50% improvement.  
- Clarify how goal vectors are specified at test time and give examples converting tasks into goal vectors.  
- Question: Why does predictive measurement supervision stabilize/accelerate training versus traditional RL—request for theoretical explanation.  
- Question: How does DFP relate to model-based RL that predicts states/rewards, and what makes DFP particularly effective?  
- Question: Why do goal-agnostic models generalize to new goals—does the measurement stream encode goal info or does training enable generalization?  
- Question: How will DFP evolve as predictive sensory models improve, and would predicting full sensory streams change the framework?
