- Proposes reframing sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future measurement vectors instead of relying on sparse scalar rewards.  
- Leverages temporally dense sensory and measurement streams (e.g., health, ammo, frags) as rich supervisory signals.  
- Demonstrates significant empirical gains over state-of-the-art deep RL methods (DQN, A3C, DSR) in challenging 3D ViZDoom scenarios.  
- Validated across four progressively harder Doom tasks, with particularly large advantages in the most complex settings (D3, D4).  
- Won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch track, reportedly outperforming the second-place entry by over 50%.  
- Ablation studies show predicting multiple vectorial measurements and multiple temporal offsets substantially improves performance versus single scalar prediction.  
- Shows generalization across environments and the ability to adapt to new goals without retraining.  
- Decision: accept — judged a compelling, well-supported contribution with robust empirical evidence.  
- Recommendation: clarify the distinction between measurements and raw sensory input and justify why certain signals are treated as measurements.  
- Recommendation: provide deeper analysis of why prediction-based supervision outperforms traditional RL, beyond the claim of richer supervision.  
- Recommendation: expand discussion of limitations, particularly the purely reactive/no-explicit-memory nature and implications for long-term planning.  
- Recommendation: report exact competition metrics to substantiate the stated 50%+ win margin.  
- Question: how would the approach perform if given additional engine supervision (e.g., ground-truth enemy presence) like the second-place entry?  
- Question: why does predicting multiple measurements improve performance for a single evaluation metric — better representations or specific measurement relationships?  
- Question: how straightforward would it be to integrate memory-based models or hierarchical skill structures into the direct future prediction framework?
