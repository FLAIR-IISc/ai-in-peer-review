- Paper reframes sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future meaningful measurements (DFP) instead of using scalar rewards in RL.
- DFP trains a model to predict how actions affect future measurements and selects actions to achieve specified goals at test time.
- Empirical results show DFP outperforms strong deep RL baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR) in complex 3D Doom scenarios and won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch track.
- Recommendation: Accept — the approach is well-motivated and shows robust, significant empirical gains.
- Novelty: Well-positioned against prior supervised vs. RL discussions and connects to goal-conditioned and vectorial-feedback literature.
- Experimental validation: Thorough, with four progressively harder scenarios, fair baseline comparisons, ablations, generalization tests, and competition results.
- Technical contribution: Clear architecture with expectation and action streams and a well-described training procedure using a small experience memory.
- Clarify goal generalization: Need details and evidence on handling completely novel goals or unseen measurements at test time.
- Expand theoretical discussion: Add analysis on why vectorial feedback should outperform scalar rewards (e.g., information-theoretic or convergence arguments).
- Address computational efficiency: Report and compare training times/resources across methods (DSR noted as slow but not quantified).
- Discuss limitations: Explicitly state task types where DFP may struggle, such as unobservable measurements or highly nonlinear measurement-goal relationships.
- Question 1: Explain why random goal-vector training nearly matches goal-specific training and whether certain measurements get prioritized.
- Question 2: Clarify why predicting multiple measurements improves performance even when evaluating a single metric (e.g., frags).
- Question 3: Discuss how to integrate memory (e.g., recurrent networks) into DFP and expected benefits for long-term planning tasks.
- Question 4: Provide exact competition metrics (e.g., average frags per game) for the winning model and runner-up to quantify the >50% claim.
- Question 5: Report sensitivity to fixed temporal offsets (1,2,4,8,16,32) and whether results generalize across different prediction horizons.
