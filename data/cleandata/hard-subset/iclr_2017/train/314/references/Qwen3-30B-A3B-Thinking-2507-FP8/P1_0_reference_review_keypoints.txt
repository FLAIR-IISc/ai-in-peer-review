- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP), reframing sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future measurement vectors rather than using scalar rewards.  
- DFP trains a model to predict effects of actions on future measurements and selects actions that optimize predicted measurements and goals.  
- Strong empirical results in Doom: DFP outperforms RL baselines (e.g., 6× A3C in D3, 2.5× in D4) and won the Visual Doom AI Competition by >50%.  
- Ablations show vectorial feedback and multi-horizon prediction are key; method generalizes across environments and supports dynamic test-time goals.  
- Well-motivated and positioned within literature, arguing for dense measurement feedback in real-world sensorimotor tasks.  
- Decision: Accept — compelling contribution with strong empirical support.  
- Limitation: Model is purely reactive (no explicit memory); paper lacks discussion on integrating memory-based approaches.  
- Paper insufficiently differentiates from predictive state representations (PSRs); needs clearer comparison to that line of work.  
- Missing detail about the second-best competition submission; a brief comparison would contextualize the performance gap.  
- Needs discussion of failure modes — e.g., how DFP handles environments where measurements are uninformative about desired behavior.  
- Question: How do authors ensure agents use all measurements rather than focusing on the most salient ones; how robust is DFP to irrelevant measurements?  
- Question: How would DFP scale to continuous action spaces; would the action-stream architecture require changes?  
- Question: Is random-goal training optimal, or would training on goal distributions matching test-time perform better?
