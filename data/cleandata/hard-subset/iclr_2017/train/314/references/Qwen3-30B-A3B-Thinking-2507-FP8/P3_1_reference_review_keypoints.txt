- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): reframe RL as supervised learning by predicting future measurement streams to choose actions.  
- Key contributions: simple effective framework, strong empirical gains over RL baselines, demonstrated generalization, and a Visual Doom AI Competition win.  
- Decision: Accept — strong empirical contribution with practical validation.  
- Addresses challenges of sensorimotor control from raw input and learning skills for dynamically specified goals using dense measurement supervision.  
- Empirical results: DFP significantly outperforms DQN/A3C/DSR across Doom scenarios, with large gains in the hardest tasks; ablations show vectorial measurements and multi-horizon predictions matter.  
- Generalization: models transfer across environments and adapt to novel goals; competition win provides real-world evidence.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical analysis explaining why DFP outperforms traditional RL.  
- Missing comparison to recent model-based RL approaches that predict future states (e.g., Oh et al., 2015; Finn et al., 2016).  
- Measurement selection concern: assumes available measurements but does not discuss how to choose or learn them in domains without natural measurements.  
- Computational efficiency: mentions training speed but lacks quantitative comparison of computational cost versus DQN/A3C.  
- Goal specification limitation: assumes goals are linear combinations of measurements and does not address more complex goal representations.  
- Question: How would measurement streams be defined or learned in domains without natural measurements?  
- Question: How does DFP relate to model-based prediction approaches, and why does it outperform them?  
- Question: How were competition constraints handled, and would the method need adaptation for other formats?  
- Question: How would the approach be extended to handle goals that aren’t linear combinations of measurements?  
- Overall: A simple supervised-learning approach with strong empirical results and practical impact, deserving acceptance subject to clarifications.
