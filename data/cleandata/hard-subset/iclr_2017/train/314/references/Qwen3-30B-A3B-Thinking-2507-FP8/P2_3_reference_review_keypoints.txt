- Reframes sensorimotor control as supervised learning via Direct Future Prediction (DFP), predicting future measurements instead of scalar rewards.  
- Uses temporally dense measurement streams (health, ammo, frags) as supervision from raw experience.  
- DFP significantly outperforms deep RL baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR) in complex 3D Doom tasks.  
- Won the Visual Doom AI Competition by >50% over the second-best submission.  
- Method generalizes across environments and supports dynamically specified goals at test time.  
- Vectorial measurements and multiple temporal offsets are important for performance.  
- Recommendation: accept — simple, powerful alternative to RL with compelling experiments.  
- Strength: well-defined, important problem and solid literature positioning highlighting reward sparsity issues.  
- Strength: rigorous empirical evaluation with four scenarios, strong baselines, and ablation studies.  
- Strength: theoretical insight — goal-agnostic training enables handling new goals without retraining.  
- Suggestion: discuss implications of the model being purely reactive and how to integrate memory or hierarchical skills.  
- Suggestion: clarify how the goal vector g is constructed and used in the objective for different test-time goals.  
- Suggestion: address computational complexity, inference time, and memory requirements compared to RL methods.  
- Question: how were goal vectors sampled during goal-agnostic training and do training/test distributions match?  
- Question: request details on the metric used for the >50% competition margin (e.g., average frags, win rate).  
- Question: ask why predicting measurements at multiple future times helps — general temporal information vs. specific temporal structure.  
- Question: what is the size of the "small experience memory" (M), how was it chosen, and how sensitive is performance to it?  
- Question: could DSR catch up with substantially more training steps, and would that close the gap with DFP?
