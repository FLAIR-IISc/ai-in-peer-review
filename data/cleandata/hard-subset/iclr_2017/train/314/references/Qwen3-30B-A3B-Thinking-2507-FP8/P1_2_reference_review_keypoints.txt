- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): reframe RL as supervised learning by predicting future vectorial measurements to choose actions.  
- Claims advantages: richer temporally-dense supervision, training without fixed goals, and superior performance on complex 3D tasks; won Visual Doom AI Competition by >50%.  
- Recommendation: accept — addresses a key RL limitation and shows robust empirical gains.  
- Theoretical grounding: well-connected to supervised control, Monte Carlo methods, and predictive state representations.  
- Strong empirical evidence: outperforms DQN/A3C/DSR, especially on multi-measurement tasks (D3/D4), with convincing ablation studies.  
- Comparisons and literature placement are thorough and fair; computational differences between methods are acknowledged.  
- Request to clarify goal representation with a concrete example of a goal vector in practice.  
- Raise concern about measurement acquisition: clarify whether game-provided measurements count as extraneous external supervision.  
- Note limitation that the method is purely reactive (no explicit memory); discuss effects on long-term planning and possible extensions.  
- Ask to specify which metric underlies the reported "50% improvement" in the competition.  
- Question how the approach would perform if engine measurements were unavailable and had to be inferred from vision.  
- Ask how performance changes when goals are complex/non-linear functions of measurements versus simple linear combinations.  
- Request concrete examples of dynamically specified goals the model can handle that traditional RL would struggle with.
