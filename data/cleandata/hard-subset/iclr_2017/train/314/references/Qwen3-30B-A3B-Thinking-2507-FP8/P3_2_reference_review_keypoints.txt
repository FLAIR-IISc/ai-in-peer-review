- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future measurement vectors instead of scalar rewards to cast sensorimotor control as supervised learning.
- Claims DFP gives richer temporal supervision, supports goal-agnostic training, and achieves state-of-the-art performance in 3D (Doom), winning the Visual Doom AI Competition by >50%.
- Decision: recommend accept.
- Strong theoretical motivation: identifies limitations of sparse scalar rewards and situates approach within supervised motor-control literature.
- Very strong empirical results: outperforms DQN, A3C, DSR across scenarios, especially in harder environments (D3/D4); ablations show vectorial targets and multi-horizon prediction matter.
- Competition win provides compelling real-world validation, beating the next best entry by a large margin.
- Strong generalization and flexibility: models generalize across environments and support dynamically specified goals at test time; goal-agnostic training nearly matches goal-specific training.
- Clarify claim of training on "raw experience" since internal game measurements (health, ammo, frags) were used.
- Request deeper theoretical insight: explain why predicting future measurements works vs other supervised approaches and when it might fail.
- Strengthen comparisons to related frameworks like Universal Value Function Approximators and predictive state representations.
- Clarify the reported "50% performance difference" (percent improvement vs absolute frag difference).
- Ask for quantification of how measurement streams stabilize/accelerate training (e.g., learning curves, training dynamics).
- Request details on how goal vectors are generated during randomized goal (goal-agnostic) training.
- Question scalability to environments without provided measurements and implications for generalization beyond Doom.
- Ask how DFP might integrate with future sensory-frame prediction methods and whether predicting sensory features could blur measurements vs sensory streams.
