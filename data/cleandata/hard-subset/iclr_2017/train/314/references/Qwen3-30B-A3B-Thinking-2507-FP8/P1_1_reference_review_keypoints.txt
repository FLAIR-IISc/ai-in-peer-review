- Proposal: Frame sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future measurement vectors (e.g., health, ammo) instead of using scalar RL rewards (Direct Future Prediction, DFP).  
- Key insight: Temporally dense measurement streams provide rich supervision for predicting action effects and selecting actions to meet goals.  
- Claims: DFP handles dynamic goals at test time without goal-specific training and mitigates sparse-reward limitations of RL.  
- Empirical result: Strong performance across multiple Doom scenarios, outperforming DQN, A3C, DSR, and winning the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch by a large margin.  
- Generalization: Demonstrated robustness across environments and goals (Table 2) and effectiveness of vectorial measurements over scalar rewards (Table 4).  
- Theoretical positioning: Builds on supervised-learning advantages noted by Jordan & Rumelhart and connects to Monte Carlo methods, multi-objective decision-making, and parameterized goals.  
- Strength of experiments: Comprehensive evaluation, ablation studies, and real-world competition success lend convincing empirical support.  
- Decision: Reviewer recommends accept due to novelty, motivation, and strong empirical evidence.  
- Suggestion 1: Clarify relationship to predictive state representations (PSRs) and how this work advances that literature.  
- Suggestion 2: Expand discussion of limitations—model is reactive with no explicit memory—and potential impacts on long-horizon planning.  
- Suggestion 3: Move implementation-heavy architecture details to an appendix to improve main-text readability.  
- Suggestion 4: Provide guidance on selecting appropriate measurements in new environments and practical measurement identification.  
- Question 1: How well does DFP generalize when sensory–measurement relationships differ substantially from training environments?  
- Question 2: How does the approach handle cases where measurements are unavailable or must be estimated from raw inputs, and would estimated measurements still yield gains?  
- Question 3: How much of the competition win is due to the measurement-based framing versus specific DFP implementation details?  
- Question 4: How could DFP be extended with memory mechanisms (e.g., LSTM) for long-term planning, and what performance impact is expected?
