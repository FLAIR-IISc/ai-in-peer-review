- Paper proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): reframes RL as supervised learning by predicting future measurement vectors conditioned on current sensory input and a goal.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Main claim: Dense, multidimensional measurement streams provide richer supervision than scalar rewards, enabling flexible goal specification at test time.  
- Empirical result: DFP outperforms DQN, A3C, and DSR on increasingly complex Doom scenarios, with large gains in Battle tasks.  
- Ablation finding: Vectorial measurements and multi-temporal predictions are key contributors to performance.  
- Generalization: Model can be trained without a fixed goal and generalize to new goals at test time.  
- Independent validation: Won Full Deathmatch track of Visual Doom AI Competition by a large margin.  
- Reproducibility request: Clarify training details—experience memory size, value of k for updates, and motivation for not using standard replay; include full implementation details in Appendix.  
- Baseline comparison concern: DSR was trained far fewer steps (1M vs 50M); authors should acknowledge this and discuss whether more training would change results.  
- Quantification request: Specify what “50% better” means (e.g., frags, win rate) for scientific precision.  
- Limitations: Paper should more fully discuss being purely reactive with no explicit memory and implications for tasks needing long-term planning.  
- Question 1: Explain why regime c (goal sampled from [-1,1]) generalizes better but performs worse on the main task than regime b (uniform [0,1]).  
- Question 2: Characterize the relative importance of different measurements (health, ammo, frags) and whether the approach works with fewer measurements.  
- Question 3: Provide intuition for the action-stream normalization that forces average predictions to zero and why it aids learning.  
- Question 4: How would the method handle training when the agent has no prior knowledge of any goal (no fixed goal regime at all)?
