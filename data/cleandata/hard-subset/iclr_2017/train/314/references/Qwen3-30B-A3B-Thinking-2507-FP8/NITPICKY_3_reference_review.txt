

# Review for ICLR

## 1. Summary of Contributions

The paper proposes a novel approach to sensorimotor control that frames reinforcement learning as supervised learning using a stream of measurements instead of scalar rewards. The authors argue that the temporal structure of sensory and measurement streams provides rich supervision for predicting future measurements, which can then guide action selection. The approach is evaluated on immersive 3D environments using the ViZDoom platform, with claims of superior performance over state-of-the-art deep RL methods, particularly in complex tasks involving multiple measurements.

## 2. Decision

Reject. The paper contains critical technical inconsistencies that undermine the validity of the proposed method, and the manuscript suffers from significant formatting and detail issues that suggest insufficient preparation for publication.

## 3. Supporting Arguments

The most serious issue is a fundamental mathematical inconsistency in the model architecture (Section 3.2). The paper claims that "the output of the network has the same dimensionality as the output of the action stream" (w · dim(f)), but equation (7) states that the output is "p = <pa1, ..., paw> = <A1(j)+E(j), ..., Aw(j)+E(j)>". The expectation stream E(j) has dimensionality dim(f), while the action stream outputs have dimensionality w · dim(f). Adding E(j) (dim(f)) to each action stream output (dim(f)) would produce a vector of dimensionality dim(f), not w · dim(f), unless dim(f) = 1. This inconsistency renders the described architecture mathematically incoherent and makes it impossible to understand how the network would function in the general case (where dim(f) > 1).

Second, the paper contains critical omissions and inconsistencies in experimental reporting:
- The paper states "the agent has access to eight sub-actions" but fails to specify the total number of possible actions (2^8 = 256), creating confusion about the action space.
- The paper claims to outperform the second-best submission by "more than 50%" in the Visual Doom AI Competition but provides no numerical values to support this claim.
- The paper reports "average health" for D1 and D2 (which only have health as a measurement) but "average frags" for D3 and D4 (which have three measurements), despite the goal in D3 and D4 being a linear combination of all three measurements. This inconsistent reporting of metrics makes it difficult to properly evaluate the results.

Additionally, the manuscript contains multiple formatting issues that suggest insufficient attention to detail:
- References contain strange artifacts like ".,? \Q2006\E" instead of standard citation formats.
- The paper states "resulting in possible actions" without specifying the number.
- The paper mentions "the last column of Table 2 additionally reports the performance of a higher-capacity model trained in D4-tx. This combination is referred to as D4-tx-L" but fails to define what "L" means.

## 4. Additional Feedback

To improve the paper, the authors should:
1. Correct the fundamental mathematical inconsistency in the network architecture description. The authors should provide a clear derivation of the dimensionality of each component and ensure consistency throughout the model description.
2. Specify all missing numerical details, including the number of possible actions, exact performance metrics for the Visual Doom competition, and precise values for hyperparameters like M and N.
3. Standardize the reporting of metrics across all scenarios to ensure consistency (e.g., report all metrics as the weighted combination used in training).
4. Correct all reference formatting issues to conform to standard academic conventions.
5. Clarify the relationship between the goal vector g in equation (1) and the coefficients used in the experiments (0.5, 0.5, 1).

## 5. Questions for Authors

1. Could you clarify the mathematical relationship between the dimensionality of the expectation stream (dim(f)) and the action stream (w · dim(f)) in equation (7)? How does the addition of these two components produce a vector of dimensionality w · dim(f) as claimed?
2. What specific metric was used to calculate the "more than 50%" performance improvement over the second-best submission in the Visual Doom AI Competition? Could you provide the exact numerical values?
3. Why does the paper report "average health" for D1 and D2 but "average frags" for D3 and D4, despite the fact that the goal in D3 and D4 was a linear combination of all three measurements (ammo, health, and frags)?
4. What does the "L" in "D4-tx-L" refer to, and why was this model architecture specifically used for the competition submission?
