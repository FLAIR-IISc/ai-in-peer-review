This paper presents an approach to sensorimotor control using supervised learning techniques, framing the problem as predicting the effects of actions on future measurements within an immersive environment (Doom). The core idea – leveraging the rich supervisory signal provided by the co-temporal structure of sensory input and measurements – is compelling, and the demonstrated performance, including winning the Visual Doom AI Competition, is impressive. The work is well-motivated, addresses key challenges in reinforcement learning, and offers a potentially valuable alternative to traditional reward-based approaches.

The decision is to **accept** the paper. The work presents a novel and effective approach to sensorimotor control, backed by strong experimental results and a clear, well-written presentation. The demonstrated success in a challenging environment like Doom, and specifically winning a competitive track, provides significant evidence for the efficacy of the proposed method. 

The supporting arguments for this decision are as follows:

*   **Novelty:** The approach of framing sensorimotor control as a supervised learning problem, predicting future measurements instead of relying on a sparse reward signal, represents a departure from standard reinforcement learning paradigms and is a valuable contribution.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The extensive experiments in Doom, consistently outperforming state-of-the-art deep RL methods, strongly support the claims made in the paper. The win in the Visual Doom AI Competition provides particularly compelling external validation.
*   **Generalization:** The demonstrated ability of the learned models to generalize across environments and goals further strengthens the significance of the work.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and clearly articulates the motivation, approach, and results.

Additional feedback to improve the paper:

*   **Clarify the relationship to imitation learning:** While the paper briefly mentions imitation learning, a more detailed discussion of the differences and similarities could be beneficial. How does this approach differ from directly learning from human demonstrations, and what are the trade-offs?
*   **Ablation study details:** While an ablation study is included, expanding on the rationale behind choosing specific ablations and providing more in-depth analysis of the results would strengthen the findings. For example, why were only those specific ablations chosen?
*   **Computational Cost:**  A more thorough discussion of the computational cost (training time, resources) associated with the proposed approach compared to the baselines would be valuable. 
*   **Future Work Discussion:** The section on future work is good, but could be expanded to include potential applications beyond game playing.  Are there specific real-world robotics or control problems where this approach could be particularly effective?
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the baselines were tuned, providing some insight into the sensitivity of the proposed method to hyperparameter choices would be useful for reproducibility and wider adoption.
*   **Goal Vector Initialization:** How is the goal vector g initialized? Is this crucial for performance, and is any particular initialization strategy more effective?



Questions for the authors:

*   The paper states that predicting full sensory streams is beyond current capabilities. Could the authors elaborate on the limitations preventing this, and what advancements would be needed to address them?
*   The choice of temporal offsets (1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32) seems arbitrary. Was any analysis performed to determine the optimal set of offsets, or to understand the impact of different offset configurations?
*   In the ablation study, what is the rationale behind only predicting the *last* temporal offset? Does this have a specific theoretical justification?
*   The paper mentions "randomized goals" during training. How does the range of the randomly sampled goal vector impact the learning process and generalization performance?
