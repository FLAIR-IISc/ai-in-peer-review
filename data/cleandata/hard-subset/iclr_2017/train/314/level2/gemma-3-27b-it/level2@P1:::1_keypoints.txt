- Decision: Accept.  
- Core contribution: Reframes sensorimotor control as supervised learning via future prediction of measurements instead of sparse-reward RL.  
- Empirical strength: Strong results in Doom, including winning the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch.  
- Novelty/insight: Using co-temporal structure of high-dimensional sensory and low-dimensional measurement streams is a significant departure from standard RL.  
- Generalization evidence: Victory and experiments show generalization to unseen, randomly textured Doom environments.  
- Ablations: Well-designed ablation studies support the importance of key components (e.g., predicting multiple measurements).  
- Motivation/justification: Clear argument that the approach suits environments with dense, multi-dimensional feedback versus sparse scalar rewards.  
- Presentation: Well-written, well-structured, with sufficient architectural and experimental detail.  
- Limitation discussion needed: More in-depth analysis of environments where the approach may not be suitable and challenges in defining measurements.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Provide insight into sensitivity of the proposed method to learning rate, architecture, and other key hyperparameters.  
- Computational cost: Include discussion of training resources and compute requirements.  
- Broader generalization: Test or discuss generalization beyond Doom to other simulated environments.  
- Mathematical formalism: A more rigorous formulation of the training objective and prediction module would improve clarity.  
- Q1 (temporal offsets): Rationale for choosing τ = {1,2,4,8,16,32} and whether other combinations were explored.  
- Q2 (diminishing returns): Is there a point of diminishing returns as the number of predicted measurements increases?  
- Q3 (hard-to-express goals): Which goal types are difficult to express as future measurements and how might the method be adapted?  
- Q4 (OOD goals): How far out-of-distribution can new goals be before performance degrades?  
- Q5 (supervision comparison): Detailed comparison of the supervision used by the winning model versus the baseline and how that impacted results.
