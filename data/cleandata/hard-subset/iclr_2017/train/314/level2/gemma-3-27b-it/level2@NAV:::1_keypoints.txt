- Proposes a novel supervised-learning approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments, leveraging cotemporal structure of high-dimensional sensory and low-dimensional measurement streams.  
- Learns to predict the effect of actions on future measurements, enabling control without explicit rewards and adaptation to changing goals.  
- Empirically validated in Doom, including winning the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Recommendation: accept — approach is innovative and a compelling alternative to traditional RL.  
- Strong empirical results and competition win provide substantial support for the claims.  
- Claims to overcome RL limitations (sparse rewards, poor generalization) with clear rationale and contextualization.  
- Experimental setup appears rigorous with comparisons to state-of-the-art methods and ablation studies.  
- Generalization results are encouraging, suggesting robust and transferable skill learning.  
- Weakness: predictor network architecture (Figure 1) needs more detailed explanation, especially the expectation and action streams.  
- Weakness: ablation studies should include more in-depth analysis of effects of different temporal prediction offsets.  
- Weakness: reliance on Doom as the sole test environment may limit the generalizability claim; testing on diverse environments is desirable.  
- Questions: request details on hyperparameter tuning for baselines and sensitivity to learning rate, model complexity comparisons (# parameters) with the competition winner, and required computational resources for training.
