- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): treat sensorimotor control as supervised prediction of future low-dimensional measurements from high-dimensional sensory input.  
- Trains the agent to predict measurement changes for each action across multiple future horizons and selects actions by maximizing a goal-weighted linear combination of predicted changes.  
- Claims learned from raw experience without external supervision and can be trained goal-agnostically then deployed for new goals at test time.  
- Decision: Accept — strong empirical evidence and compelling conceptual contribution.  
- Empirical strength: DFP significantly outperforms strong baselines (A3C, DQN, DSR), especially on complex Battle scenarios (e.g., ~6x frags on D3).  
- Competition result: DFP variant won the Visual Doom AI Competition, supporting robustness and generalization.  
- Generalization: training on diverse environments enables transfer to held-out textured maps; goal-agnostic training yields a single model that handles novel test goals nearly as well as specialized models.  
- Ablations: vectorial (multi-measurement) feedback and multi-horizon prediction are crucial; both outperform scalar rewards and single-horizon prediction.  
- Clarity/positioning: paper is well-written and connects to supervised control, Monte Carlo methods, and universal value functions.  
- Measurement selection concern: distinction between sensory inputs and measurements is somewhat arbitrary; sensitivity to which measurements are included needs discussion.  
- Model-based RL relation: approach has a model-based flavor by predicting outcomes; should be contrasted with full-state or latent-state prediction to highlight tractability via low-dimensional measurements.  
- Temporal offsets issue: choice and weighting of prediction horizons (τ) are important hyperparameters; their selection and sensitivity should be discussed for reproducibility.  
- Randomized-goal training behavior: request qualitative description of learned behaviors (reusable primitives vs. random behavior) under constantly shifting training objectives.  
- Questions for authors: how to express non-linear or threshold-based goals (e.g., keep health above X or conditional frags); does explicit memory (e.g., LSTM) add benefit beyond multi-horizon prediction for long-term planning; and what exact variant/modifications of DFP were used to win the competition?
