- Paper introduces Direct Future Prediction (DFP), framing sensorimotor control as supervised learning instead of reinforcement learning.  
- Agent predicts future changes in low-dimensional "measurements" (e.g., health, ammo) from pixels, an action, and a goal vector.  
- Actions are chosen by selecting the action with the best predicted future according to the current goal, enabling adaptable objectives at test time.  
- Claims substantial performance gains over state-of-the-art deep RL baselines and strong generalization to new environments and goals.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Primary reason 1: DFP shows significant, well-supported performance improvements over strong baselines in challenging, dynamic tasks.  
- Primary reason 2: The method addresses goal-agnostic learning, enabling learning of general skills and adaptation to novel goals.  
- Empirical support: DFP outperforms A3C by 2.5–6× in frags in Doom "Battle" scenarios.  
- Real-world validation: winning the Visual Doom AI Competition in unseen environments demonstrates robustness and generalization.  
- Goal-agnostic training results: models trained with randomized goals perform nearly as well on specific tasks as specialized models while generalizing better.  
- Ablation study: multi-dimensional measurements outperform single scalar reward, supporting the core hypothesis.  
- Suggested improvement: clarify the conceptual distinction and guidelines for splitting "sensory input" vs. "measurements" in new domains.  
- Suggested improvement: discuss limitations of a purely reactive model for tasks requiring long-term planning or memory.  
- Clarification question 1: How sensitive is performance to experience memory size and policy update frequency?  
- Clarification question 2: Have you considered non-linear goal functions (beyond linear g>f), and what new behaviors or objectives might they enable?
