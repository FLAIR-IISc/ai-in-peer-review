- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): cast control as supervised prediction of future measurement vectors to choose actions per goal.
- Replaces sparse scalar rewards with dense, multi-dimensional measurement streams for richer supervision.
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on ViZDoom, significantly outperforming DQN and A3C, especially on complex Battle tasks.
- Independent validation: method won the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Can be trained without a fixed goal and generalizes to dynamically specified goals at test time.
- Ablation studies show vectorial feedback and multi-horizon (multiple temporal offsets) prediction improve performance.
- Generalization experiments show robustness to new textures and new objectives (e.g., pacifist, ammo-drainer behaviors).
- Architecture builds on dueling-network ideas, is well-described, and well-situated in related literature (supervised control, universal value functions).
- Decision: Accept.
- Feedback: clarify principles and sensitivity for choosing measurement vector m_t versus raw sensory inputs.
- Feedback: more explicit comparison/contrast to model-based RL, noting DFP predicts low-dimensional task-relevant outcomes rather than full states.
- Feedback: discuss effects of stochastic environments given L2 regression (unimodal prediction) assumption.
- Question: during randomized-goal training, is ε-greedy data collection conditioned on the sampled goal (i.e., actions aim to maximize g_i^T F and stored with that g_i)?
- Question: clarify goal vector dimensionality—does g match f (e.g., 3 measurements × 6 offsets = 18D for Battle)?
- Question: how were temporal offsets (1,2,4,8,16,32) chosen and how sensitive is performance to this hyperparameter?
