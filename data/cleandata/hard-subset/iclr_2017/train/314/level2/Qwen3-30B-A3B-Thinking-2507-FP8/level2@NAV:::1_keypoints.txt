1. Frames sensorimotor control as supervised learning using cotemporal high-dimensional sensory and lower-dimensional measurement streams.  
2. Key contributions: (a) use cotemporal structure as a rich supervisory signal without extraneous supervision, and (b) train without fixed goals while supporting dynamically changing test-time goals.  
3. Method outperforms state-of-the-art deep RL in complex 3D environments, especially on multi-measurement tasks.  
4. Won the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition, beating the runner-up by over 50% without extra supervision.  
5. Ablation studies demonstrate clear benefits of vectorial measurements compared to scalar rewards.  
6. Shows impressive generalization across different environments.  
7. Experimental design is rigorous with multiple complexity scenarios, comparisons to DQN/A3C/DSR, and comprehensive ablations.  
8. Theoretical grounding ties the approach to supervised learning, temporal-difference learning, and multi-objective decision-making.  
9. Reviewer recommends acceptance, viewing the work as a significant paradigm shift with convincing empirical support.  
10. Suggested clarification: explain how the measurement stream captures all relevant environmental state/dynamics.  
11. Suggested extension: discuss how memory mechanisms could integrate with the primarily reactive framework.  
12. Suggested clarification: provide concrete, practical examples of how the goal vector g is defined beyond linear combinations.
