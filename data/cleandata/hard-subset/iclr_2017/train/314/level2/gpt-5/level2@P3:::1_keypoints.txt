- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict multi-horizon, goal-conditioned future measurement vectors and greedily select actions maximizing goal-weighted predictions.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Presents a clear, well-motivated alternative to value-based RL using dense vectorial feedback yielding strong empirical gains.  
- Key reason 2: Supports dynamic goal specification and cross-goal generalization with extensive experiments and a competition win.  
- Novelty: Separates sensory and measurement streams, uses vector-valued goal conditioning g^T f, and adapts a dueling-style architecture for multi-measurement prediction.  
- Relation to prior work: Related to UVFA and successor representations but differs by directly predicting multi-horizon measurement changes without bootstrapping.  
- Empirical results: Matches or exceeds DQN, A3C, DSR across four ViZDoom scenarios, with large gains in multi-measurement tasks (e.g., Battle).  
- Generalization: Shows transfer across textures/environments and goal-agnostic training nearly matches task-specific training while generalizing to new goals.  
- Ablations: Vector-valued supervision and multi-horizon targets significantly contribute to performance.  
- Competition validation: Won Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch without extra engine supervision, corroborating robustness.  
- Rigor: Provides training details, baselines from published implementations, hyperparameter search, multiple seeds, and long evaluations.  
- Clarification needed: Explicitly compare to UVFA/successor features—when do they approximate the same object and what are bias/variance or compounding-error trade-offs?  
- Clarification needed: Discuss dependence on availability, informativeness, scaling, and noise/partiality of measurement streams.  
- Clarification needed: Justify horizon choices and loss weighting (last three offsets with 0.5,0.5,1) and include sensitivity analysis over horizons/weights.  
- Nice-to-have: Report wall-clock and sample efficiency vs A3C/DQN under matched hardware and environment steps.  
- Nice-to-have: Analyze multi-step model-based rollout with the learned predictor vs one-step greedy action selection.  
- Nice-to-have: Evaluate recurrent or frame-stacking extensions to address partial observability and memory.  
- Nice-to-have: Discuss applicability to continuous action spaces and possible gradient-based action refinement.  
- Nice-to-have: Provide calibration plots and error decomposition for predictions across horizons and actions, plus failure-case analysis.  
- Open question: How robust is performance to adding irrelevant measurements or injecting noise into measurements?  
- Open question: How does the goal distribution during goal-agnostic training affect learned representations and goal generalization?  
- Open question: Are multi-horizon targets redundant or complementary—do short horizons aid stability while long horizons drive task reward?  
- Open question: How sensitive is DFP to action-space granularity and would factorized/branched action heads help?  
- Open question: Can DFP recover conventional discounted-return behavior via goal vector choices, and how does it compare to Q-learning on synthetic discounted tasks?
