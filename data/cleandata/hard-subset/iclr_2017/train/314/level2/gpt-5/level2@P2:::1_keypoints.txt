- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future vector measurements conditioned on observation, action, and goal; choose actions by maximizing goal·predicted-change.

- Replaces sparse scalar rewards with dense, multi-dimensional measurement supervision.

- Supports goal-parameterized control: train without fixed goals and pursue arbitrary test-time goals over measurements.

- Uses a simple reactive architecture: perception module plus dueling-style “expectation + action” prediction head.

- Demonstrates strong empirical performance in 3D Doom tasks, outperforming DQN, A3C, and DSR, with competition win.

- Shows generalization across environment variations (textures/mazes) and across goals.

- Ablations indicate vector-valued supervision and multi-horizon predictions materially improve performance.

- Decision: Accept — motivated reframing and compelling empirical results on challenging domains.

- Strength: Novel, clear idea bridging supervised learning and control without hand-crafted rewards or imitation.

- Strength: Strong empirical support with convincing training curves and large test-time margins.

- Strength: Goal-parameterization generalizes well; goal-agnostic training matches/surpasses goal-specific on main task.

- Strength: Ablations substantiate components’ contributions (vector measurements, multi-horizon).

- Concern: Baselines fairness — A3C/DQN lack auxiliary-task supervision; comparison to UNREAL-style auxiliary actor-critic or UVFA-like goal-conditioned RL needed.

- Concern: Short finite horizons (1–32 steps) may limit handling of sparse or long-delayed rewards; need analysis of horizon scaling and failure modes.

- Concern: Exploration limited to ε-greedy; sensitivity to ε schedules and benefit of advanced exploration not evaluated.

- Concern: Partial observability — methods use single frames; lack of recurrent baselines (A3C-LSTM or recurrent DFP) leaves open whether advantage holds with memory.

- Concern: Off-policy stability — small replay buffer and evolving policy require analysis of stability vs buffer size, batch size, update frequency.

- Concern: Reproducibility — many implementation details in appendices; public code and full hyperparameters would help.

- Suggestion: Add baseline where actor-critic predicts the same measurement vector (auxiliary loss) and pixel/feature-control to isolate effect of dense supervision.

- Suggestion: Provide architectural ablations (no dueling split, no action-stream normalization) and match compute for single-step vs multi-step predictions.

- Suggestion: Study sensitivity to goal parametrization (scaling, sparsity), calibrate prediction heads, and plot prediction error vs returns.

- Suggestion: Vary horizon offsets and loss weights, test longer horizons or learned discounting, and report computation vs performance tradeoffs.

- Suggestion: Show sample-efficiency learning curves vs environment steps, not wall-clock time.

- Suggestion: Discuss whether randomized training goals implicitly aid exploration and whether goal curricula improve learning.

- Suggestion: Briefly discuss extension to continuous action spaces (e.g., conditioned predictions + CEM/gradient action selection).

- Suggestion: Clean up reference formatting and missing bibliographic details.

- Questions: How does A3C augmented with auxiliary measurement prediction perform compared to DFP?

- Questions: How sensitive is DFP to choice and scaling of measurement channels and to noisy/irrelevant measurements?

- Questions: What is the effect of much longer prediction horizons (e.g., 128/256 steps) and different weighting schemes?

- Questions: How large is the experience memory M, and how do performance and stability change with M, batch size, and update frequency k?

- Questions: Were recurrent DFP variants or A3C-LSTM baselines tried, and what were the results on D3/D4?

- Questions: In goal-agnostic training, what distribution over g was used and how far can test-time g extrapolate without loss of performance?

- Questions: Will the authors release code and environment configs to facilitate replication?
