- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future intrinsic measurement changes from pixels and a goal vector for sensorimotor control.
- Claim: dense, vector-valued measurement streams stabilize and accelerate training versus scalar reward RL.
- Claim: goal-parameterization allows training without a fixed goal and adaptation to new goals at test time.
- Claim: DFP outperforms strong deep RL baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR) on challenging 3D Doom tasks and generalizes across environments and goals; also won Visual Doom AI competition.
- Decision: Accept.
- Reason: simple, well-motivated reformulation with strong empirical gains on realistic 3D tasks.
- Reason: convincing demonstrations of goal-conditioned control and generalization, supported by ablation studies.
- Supporting: addresses an important visuomotor control problem and is well situated relative to UVFA, predictive representations, and Monte Carlo methods.
- Supporting: substantial experiments—four scenarios of increasing difficulty, tuned DQN/A3C comparisons, generalization tests, goal-agnostic training, and multi-horizon ablations.
- Supporting: training is efficient due to fast simulation and results are averaged over multiple runs.
- Limitation: incomplete statistical rigor—no error bars or significance testing reported.
- Limitation: uneven baseline coverage (limited DSR comparison due to speed).
- Limitation: depends on accessible, informative measurements and linear goal composition assumption.
- Limitation: purely reactive control without memory, which may fail under partial observability.
- Limitation: evaluation confined to discrete actions and a single domain (Doom).
- Suggested improvements: report variance/confidence intervals, significance tests, and learning curves with uncertainty.
- Suggested improvements: clarify and justify hyperparameter choices (temporal offsets, loss weights, replay size, k-update) and provide sensitivity analyses.
- Suggested improvements: analyze sample efficiency in environment-steps vs wall-clock, robustness to noisy/partial/sparser measurements, non-linear goal parameterizations, memory (e.g., LSTM), continuous actions, and add domains beyond Doom.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to choice/scaling of measurements and to training goal distribution?
- Questions for authors: what are M and k in the experience memory regime and how do they affect stability?
- Questions for authors: do results degrade if goal g changes mid-episode and how is action selection handled then?
- Questions for authors: can you report variance across seeds and per-task sample-efficiency curves?
- Questions for authors: how does DFP perform when only a sparse scalar reward is available?
