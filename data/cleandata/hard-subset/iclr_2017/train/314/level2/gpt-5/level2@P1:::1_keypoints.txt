- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict action-conditional future measurement vectors at multiple horizons and select actions by maximizing goal-weighted predictions.
- Uses a structured network with separate streams for expectation and action-advantage (dueling-style) to capture action differences.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance in ViZDoom, outperforming DQN/A3C especially on complex tasks and winning a competition.
- Shows generalization across textures and goals, and ablations highlighting benefits of vectorial feedback and multi-horizon prediction.
- Claims a practical, scalable supervised-learning alternative to reward-based RL when dense, multidimensional measurements exist.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Strong empirical results and generalization from a simple, scalable method.
- Key reason 2: Clear, well-motivated reframing of control as supervised prediction with goal-conditioning enabling flexible training and deployment.
- Problem & motivation: Targets pixel-based 3D control and flexible goals; argues dense measurements give richer supervision than sparse rewards.
- Literature placement: Connects to UVFA, predictive representations, Monte Carlo, dueling networks, and successor representations while offering a distinct practical instantiation.
- Method & correctness: Uses Monte Carlo targets for multi-horizon measurement deltas, predicts all actions but applies loss to executed action, and validates goal-conditioning.
- Empirical rigor — baselines: Compares to DQN and A3C with tuning; DSR comparison limited by speed but core evidence is solid.
- Empirical rigor — scenarios: Uses increasing difficulty scenarios, multiple runs, 1M-step tests, and randomized-goal training showing strong generalization.
- Empirical rigor — ablations: Shows multiple measurements and horizons matter; cross-texture tests and larger models strengthen claims.
- Practicality: Trains quickly with simple infrastructure, offering a speed advantage over many RL methods.
- Clarify supervision: Ask whether loss is applied only to executed-action heads or includes auxiliary losses for non-executed actions.
- Clarify termination handling: Ask how targets are computed across episode termination and how measurement deltas are defined upon death.
- Clarify normalization: Ask how measurements are normalized and sensitivity to horizon weights and horizon set {1,2,4,8,16,32}.
- Baselines and fairness: Request A3C-LSTM results, UVFA-style goal-conditioned baselines, and reporting of variance/significance across seeds.
- Goal-conditioning robustness: Ask sensitivity to choice/number/noisy measurements, possibility of nonlinear goal maps or learned embeddings, and goal sampling distribution effects.
- Architecture ablations: Request ablation for expectation/action split and exploration of recurrence or multi-step sequence models for delayed effects.
- Exploration and data efficiency: Ask about alternative exploration methods, larger replay buffers, off-policy issues, and wall-clock training time comparisons.
- Generalization scope: Request transfer tests to different layouts/topologies and whether randomized-goal training improves environment generalization as well as goal generalization.
