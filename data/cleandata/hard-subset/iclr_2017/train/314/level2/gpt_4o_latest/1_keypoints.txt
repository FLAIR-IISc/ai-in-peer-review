- Proposes framing sensorimotor control as supervised learning that predicts future measurements from high‑dim sensory streams and low‑dim measurement streams.  
- Key contribution: replace sparse scalar RL rewards with dense, multidimensional feedback from cotemporal sensory/measurement structure.  
- Enables learning directly from raw sensory input and dynamic goal adaptation at test time.  
- Validated on ViZDoom with extensive experiments, outperforming state‑of‑the‑art deep RL methods.  
- Demonstrated generalization by winning the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novelty/impact: significant departure from traditional RL, addressing sparse rewards and goal flexibility.  
- Strong empirical results: better performance and generalization than DQN, A3C, DSR across tasks.  
- Methodology is clearly explained (architecture, training, experimental setup) and vectorial feedback is well justified.  
- Robust results and ablation studies highlight importance of multivariate feedback and multi‑temporal predictions.  
- Practical utility: generalizes across environments and adapts to new goals.  
- Suggestion: incorporate memory or temporal abstraction (recurrent/hierarchical components) to enrich behavior.  
- Suggestion: extend approach to continuous action spaces for broader applicability.  
- Suggestion: provide more clarity on hyperparameter tuning and computational efficiency of baselines.  
- Suggestion: evaluate beyond Doom‑based environments to test wider generalizability.  
- Question: how robust is the method to noisy or incomplete sensory/measurement streams?  
- Question: what modifications are needed to adapt the method to continuous action spaces?  
- Question: how does the approach scale in computational resources and training time versus RL methods?
