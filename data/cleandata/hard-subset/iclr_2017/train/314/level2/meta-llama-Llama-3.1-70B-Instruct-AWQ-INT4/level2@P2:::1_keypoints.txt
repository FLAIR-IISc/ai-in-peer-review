- Proposes a novel sensorimotor control method using high-dimensional sensory + low-dimensional measurement streams with cotemporal supervision.  
- Trains a sensorimotor control model via supervised learning from raw sensory input without extraneous supervision.  
- Recommendation: accept, based on novelty and strong experimental validation.  
- Evidence includes extensive experiments and comparison to state-of-the-art deep RL methods.  
- Demonstrated empirical success, including winning the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation of assumptions, model architecture, and related work.  
- Experimental results show superior performance on challenging tasks and generalization across environments and goals.  
- Main weakness: evaluation limited to a narrow set of scenarios; broader generalization is unclear.  
- Suggestion: provide fuller model architecture details (layers, units, activations) and exact training hyperparameters (batch size, iterations, learning rate).  
- Suggestion: include more detailed result analysis and per-scenario comparisons (average return, std dev, steps to reach performance).  
- Suggestion: expand discussion of potential real-world applications (robotics, autonomous vehicles).  
- Questions for authors: provide architecture/training details, deeper comparison to deep RL, applications discussion, plans for scaling to more complex environments, and required computational resources (GPUs, training time).
