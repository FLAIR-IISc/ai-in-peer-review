- Proposes sensorimotor control using high-dimensional sensory and low-dimensional measurement streams with cotemporal structure as supervision.
- Uses cotemporal alignment to train a control model via supervised learning without extraneous rewards.
- Evaluated in immersive 3D Doom simulations.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance and strong generalization across environments and goals.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Well-motivated departure from reward-based RL, offering a fresh perspective on sensorimotor control.
- Key reason 2: Experimental results convincingly outperform deep RL baselines and generalize well.
- Paper is clearly written with thorough background, related work, and detailed model/training description.
- Experimental evaluation is extensive, well-designed, and includes comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.
- Limitation: Method depends on cotemporal sensory–measurement structure, which may not hold in all environments.
- Suggested improvement: Provide more details on hyperparameter tuning for both the proposed method and baselines.
- Suggested improvement: Add ablation studies to assess component importance (e.g., vectorial feedback vs scalar rewards).
- Suggested improvement: Discuss applications beyond Doom and limitations in more complex/dynamic settings.
- Questions for authors: Clarify the experience memory and how it updates the predictor.
- Questions for authors: Explain selection and intuition for six temporal offsets used to predict future measurements.
- Questions for authors: Discuss benefits and limitations of using a Monte-Carlo–type method in this setting.
