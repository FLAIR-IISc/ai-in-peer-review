**Summary of the Paper**

The paper presents an approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments using a high-dimensional sensory stream and a lower-dimensional measurement stream. The approach utilizes supervised learning techniques to train a sensorimotor control model by interacting with the environment. The model learns to act based on raw sensory input from a complex three-dimensional environment and can pursue dynamically changing goals at test time. The authors evaluate their approach in immersive three-dimensional simulations based on the classical first-person game Doom and demonstrate that it outperforms sophisticated prior formulations, particularly on challenging tasks.
