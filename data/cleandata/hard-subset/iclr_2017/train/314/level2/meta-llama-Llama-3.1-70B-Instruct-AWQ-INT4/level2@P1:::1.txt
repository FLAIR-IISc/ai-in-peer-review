The paper proposes an approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments by utilizing a high-dimensional sensory stream and a lower-dimensional measurement stream. The cotemporal structure of these streams provides a rich supervisory signal, enabling training a sensorimotor control model through supervised learning techniques without extraneous supervision. The approach is evaluated in immersive three-dimensional simulations based on the classical first-person game Doom, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance and generalization across environments and goals.

I decide to **Accept** this paper, with the two key reasons being:

1. The approach is well-motivated and departs from the traditional reward-based formalization commonly used in reinforcement learning, providing a fresh perspective on sensorimotor control.
2. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach, outperforming state-of-the-art deep RL methods in challenging tasks and showing impressive generalization across environments and goals.

The paper provides a clear and well-structured presentation of the approach, including a thorough discussion of the background and related work. The authors also provide a detailed description of the model architecture and training procedure, making it easy to follow and understand.

The experimental evaluation is extensive and well-designed, covering various scenarios and comparing the proposed approach to state-of-the-art deep RL methods. The results are impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach in challenging tasks and its ability to generalize across environments and goals.

One potential limitation of the approach is its reliance on the cotemporal structure of the sensory and measurement streams, which may not be present in all environments. However, the authors provide a clear discussion of this limitation and its potential implications.

To further improve the paper, I would suggest the following:

* Provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process for the proposed approach and the baseline methods.
* Consider adding more ablation studies to evaluate the importance of different components of the approach, such as the use of vectorial feedback versus scalar rewards.
* Provide more discussion on the potential applications of the approach beyond the Doom environment, and its potential limitations in more complex and dynamic environments.

Questions to the authors:

* Can you provide more details on the experience memory and how it is used to update the predictor?
* How did you select the temporal offsets for predicting future measurements, and what is the intuition behind using six temporal offsets?
* Can you provide more discussion on the potential benefits and limitations of using a Monte-Carlo-type method in this setting?
