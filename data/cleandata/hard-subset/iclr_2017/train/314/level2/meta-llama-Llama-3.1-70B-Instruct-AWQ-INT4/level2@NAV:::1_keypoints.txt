- Presents an approach using a high-dimensional sensory stream and a lower-dimensional measurement stream for sensorimotor control in immersive environments.  
- Claims the model can be trained by interaction alone (no extra supervision), learns from raw sensory input, and can pursue dynamically changing goals at test time.  
- Evaluated in immersive 3D Doom simulations and reported to outperform sophisticated prior formulations, especially on challenging tasks.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novelty: Method is distinct from traditional RL; measurement stream provides a rich supervisory signal.  
- Empirical evaluation: Extensive experiments showing improvements over state-of-the-art deep RL methods.  
- Theoretical soundness: Clear, well-motivated framework grounded in RL and sensorimotor control literature.  
- Experimental rigor: Multiple scenarios and evaluation metrics; results are well-presented and understandable.  
- Comparison to prior work: Thorough comparisons demonstrating superior performance on difficult tasks.  
- Suggestion: Clarify the relationship between sensory and measurement streams and how they generate supervisory signals.  
- Suggestion: Discuss limitations and potential areas for improvement.  
- Suggestion: Provide more experimental setup details (specific scenarios, metrics, hyperparameters).  
- Question: Provide more detail on the predictor network architecture (layers, activations).  
- Question: Explain how temporal offsets for future measurement prediction and objective coefficients were selected.  
- Question: Discuss potential real-world applications (e.g., robotics, autonomous vehicles).
