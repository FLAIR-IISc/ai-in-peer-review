- Decision: Accept — paper should be accepted for its innovative approach and strong empirical results.  
- Novel approach: Uses supervised learning to predict future low-dimensional measurements from raw high-dimensional sensory streams and adapt to changing goals at test time.  
- Dense feedback: Replaces scalar RL rewards with dense, multidimensional measurement targets for faster, more stable training.  
- Dynamic goals: Training paradigm does not require fixed goals and allows goal specification at test time.  
- Empirical domain: Evaluated in first-person Doom; outperforms state-of-the-art RL methods on complex tasks.  
- Practical validation: Won the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition, demonstrating real-world effectiveness.  
- Generalization: Shows strong generalization across environments and goals.  
- Well-motivated: Grounded in literature and addresses sparse rewards and goal generalization challenges with theoretical and empirical support.  
- Rigorous evaluation: Extensive experiments and comparisons with strong baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR).  
- Significant contribution: Introduces a new training paradigm and highlights benefits of vectorial feedback over scalar rewards.  
- Suggestion — memory & hierarchy: Lacks memory and temporal abstraction; future work should explore memory-based or hierarchical architectures.  
- Suggestion — continuous actions: Extend approach to continuous action spaces to increase applicability to robotics and other domains.  
- Suggestion — ablation detail: Provide deeper analysis of specific architectural components (e.g., normalization layer in action stream).  
- Suggestion — training clarity: Clarify randomized goal training details, especially how goal distributions affect learned policies.  
- Question — temporal offsets: How sensitive is performance to choice of temporal offsets for future measurement prediction and can this be learned?  
- Question — architectures & representations: Were alternative perception architectures (e.g., transformers) or goal representations explored, and how might they affect performance?  
- Question — robustness: How does the model handle noisy or partially observable sensory streams, and would mechanisms like attention improve robustness?
