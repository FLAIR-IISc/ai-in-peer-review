- Decision: Accept with enthusiasm — supervised prediction of future measurements is an effective alternative to reward-based RL, demonstrated strongly.  
- Core idea: Frame sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future measurements from current sensory input and actions.  
- Motivation/novelty: Shifts from sparse reward signals to dense multi-dimensional measurement supervision, grounded in prior motor-control work.  
- Empirical validation: DFP consistently outperforms strong baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR) across increasing Doom scenarios with supportive ablations.  
- Competition result: Won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch track, outperforming others without extra supervision.  
- Generalization: Method generalizes across textured environments and adapts to dynamically changing goals, including goal-agnostic training.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written and includes a helpful architecture diagram.  
- Theoretical critique: Lacks deeper theoretical analysis explaining why measurement prediction serves as an effective proxy for optimal control.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: No sensitivity analysis provided for learning rate, network details, or temporal offset weighting.  
- Computational cost: Missing detailed discussion of training time, memory usage, and comparison to baselines.  
- Qualitative results: Would benefit from qualitative examples (e.g., gameplay videos) to illustrate agent behavior.  
- Measurement selection: Criteria for choosing which sensations become measurements and automated selection strategies are not addressed.  
- Q1 (temporal offsets): How sensitive is performance to chosen temporal offsets and were strategies like learning offsets explored?  
- Q2 (goal vector): How were random goal vectors generated in goal-agnostic experiments and were regularization/normalization applied?  
- Q3 (training resources): What was the training time and hardware used for the winning Visual Doom model?  
- Q4 (action normalization): Was the action stream normalization crucial, and did omitting it cause instability or divergence?  
- Q5 (future work): Could this approach be combined with model-based RL (e.g., using the predictor as a world model)?
