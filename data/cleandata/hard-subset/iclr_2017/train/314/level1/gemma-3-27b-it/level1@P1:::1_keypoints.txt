- Novel approach: frames sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future measurements from sensory input and actions instead of using reward-based RL.  
- Motivation and impact: leveraging rich supervisory signals from sensory-measurement interplay is well-motivated and potentially impactful for complex immersive environments.  
- Strong empirical results: achieves state-of-the-art performance in Doom and wins the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch track.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Primary reason for acceptance: compelling empirical superiority over existing methods on challenging tasks, including beating strong baselines with simpler supervision.  
- Methodological novelty: departs from conventional RL formulations and offers a plausible solution to sensorimotor control challenges.  
- Ablation results: experiments show the importance of vectorial measurements and multi-step prediction.  
- Generalization: experiments indicate learned policies do not merely overfit to specific environments.  
- Baseline comparisons: evaluation against strong baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR) provides solid benchmarking.  
- Reproducibility: architectural details are sufficiently described to allow potential replication.  
- Competition win: victory in Visual Doom AI Competition is a significant, persuasive result.  
- Theoretical gap: lacks formal analysis of conditions (e.g., observability, dynamics) under which the approach succeeds.  
- Goal representation concern: linear combination of future measurements for goals seems arbitrary and underexplored; learning richer goal representations could help.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: more thorough analysis of sensitivity to hyperparameters is needed.  
- Computational cost: clearer comparison of training time and memory requirements versus baselines is missing.  
- Qualitative analysis: paper would benefit from qualitative visualizations of agent behavior and attention/planning.  
- Question — temporal offsets: was any systematic exploration of different temporal offset combinations performed, and what were the results?  
- Question — architecture: what are the specific advantages of the expectation and action streams compared to simpler architectures?  
- Question — competition details: please provide specifics on the environment configuration and competing agents’ characteristics.  
- Question — randomized goals: how does the range of random goal vectors affect generalization across environments?  
- Question — loss weighting: how sensitive are results to the (0.5, 0.5, 1) measurement-weight coefficients, and can these weights be learned?
