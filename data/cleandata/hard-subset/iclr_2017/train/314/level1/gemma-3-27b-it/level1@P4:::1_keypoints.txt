- Decision: Accept — significant problem, novel supervised framing, and strong experimental validation including a competition win.  
- Significance: Tackles sensorimotor control from raw sensory input in complex environments.  
- Novelty: Frames control as supervised learning using cotemporal sensory and measurement streams, avoiding explicit reward shaping.  
- Avoids sparse-reward issues: Uses naturally occurring supervisory signals from paired streams.  
- Technical soundness: Clear network architecture and training procedure with informative ablation studies.  
- Ablations: Ablation studies show contributions of multi-dimensional measurements and multiple time offsets.  
- Baseline comparisons: Compared against strong baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR) and shown to outperform them.  
- Empirical results: State-of-the-art performance in Doom scenarios and win in Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Generalization: Experiments indicate ability to adapt to new environments and goals.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and well-situated within existing literature.  
- Goal representation concern: Uses linear combinations of future measurements; question sensitivity to coefficient choice and potential for non-linear or hierarchical goals.  
- Temporal abstraction limitation: Model is reactive and lacks hierarchical temporal abstraction or memory mechanisms.  
- Computational cost: More detail requested on training speed and GPU/time costs relative to baselines.  
- DSR reproducibility: Need specific DSR configuration details to clarify replication challenges.  
- Normalization layer question: Request for intuition/justification and alternatives for the action-stream normalization (Eq. 6).  
- Q1 (goals): How are goal vectors g determined or learned in practice; can meaningful goals be discovered automatically?  
- Q2 (measurements): Are there diminishing returns with more predicted measurements; is there an optimal subset per task?  
- Q3 (memory/planning): How could the DFP approach be extended to incorporate memory or long-term planning?  
- Q4 (baselines): Details requested on hyperparameter searches for DQN, A3C, DSR (learning rates and key parameter ranges).
