- Novel approach: frames sensorimotor control in 3D as supervised learning instead of reinforcement learning, leveraging co-temporal sensory and measurement streams.  
- Core idea is compelling and well-motivated, claiming dense supervision from temporal structure avoids sparse RL rewards.  
- Enables learning from raw sensory input without pre-defined goals during training (goal-agnostic learning).  
- Strong empirical performance, including winning the Visual Doom AI Competition and outperforming a more complex, extra-supervised model.  
- Recommendation: accept — approach is significant, presentation clear, and results strong.  
- Paper grounding: solid justification comparing RL limitations to supervised learning principles.  
- Experiments: extensive Doom evaluations with SOTA RL baselines, thorough ablation studies, and demonstrated generalization under dynamic goals.  
- Requested clarifications: provide more rationale for specific design choices within the prediction module (expectation vs action streams).  
- Requested clarifications: detail training regime effects, especially mini-batch size and experience replay buffer size.  
- Specific questions for authors: why choose temporal offsets (1,2,4,8,16,32) and sensitivity to them?  
- Specific question: what distribution was used for sampling randomized goals and how does it affect learning?  
- Specific question: intuition and advantages of splitting prediction into expectation and action streams versus a single stream?  
- Specific question: what computational resources (GPUs, training time) were used to assess reproducibility?
