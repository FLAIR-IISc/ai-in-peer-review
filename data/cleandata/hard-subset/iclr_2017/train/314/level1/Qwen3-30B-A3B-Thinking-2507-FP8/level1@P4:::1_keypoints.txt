- Frames sensorimotor control as supervised learning predicting future measurements instead of reinforcement learning.  
- Leverages high-dimensional sensory input plus lower-dimensional measurements (health, ammo) as rich supervisory signals.  
- Predicting future measurements from current sensory input, measurements, and goals enables control without fixed training-time goals.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance in complex 3D Doom environments, outperforming deep RL baselines.  
- Won the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition, beating the runner-up by >50%.  
- Decision: Accept — method is well-motivated, technically sound, and empirically strong.  
- Effectively addresses learning from raw sensory input and situates work in existing supervised/RL literature.  
- Formulation using vectorial measurements is well-justified and argued to match immersive environment structure.  
- Experiments are comprehensive: four difficulty scenarios, ablation studies, and generalization tests.  
- Consistently outperforms strong baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR), especially in multi-measurement tasks.  
- Paper provides sufficient model and training detail for reproducibility.  
- Request clearer explanation of the normalization layer in the action stream (Eq. 6) and its effect on learning dynamics.  
- Suggest discussing limitations of the purely reactive model for long-term planning and possible extensions.  
- Recommend stronger theoretical insight into why vectorial measurements outperform scalar rewards beyond empirical results.  
- Questions for authors: clarify intuition/necessity of normalization layer; specify competition D4-tx-L variant modifications; justify “no extraneous supervision” given provided measurements and whether method works without them; elaborate practical limits of goal flexibility.
