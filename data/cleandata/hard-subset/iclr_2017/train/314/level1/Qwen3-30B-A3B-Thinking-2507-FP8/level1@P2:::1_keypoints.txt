- Proposes a supervised-learning framework (predict future measurements from current sensory input, measurements, and a goal) leveraging cotemporal structure for sensorimotor control.  
- Claims this approach (DFP) outperforms sophisticated RL on complex 3D tasks, generalizes across environments/goals, and won the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Argues vectorial, temporally dense measurement streams provide richer supervision than scalar rewards, enabling more effective learning.  
- Decision: Accept — novel, well-motivated alternative to RL with strong empirical validation.  
- Identifies two core challenges: learning from raw sensory input in complex 3D environments and acquiring generalizable, goal-flexible skills.  
- Positions work relative to prior supervised and goal-parameterized learning, citing foundational literature supporting supervised approaches when dense feedback exists.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: four Doom scenarios, comparisons to DQN/A3C/DSR, and consistent DFP outperformance, especially large gains in hardest scenarios (D3, D4).  
- Ablation study shows importance of vectorial measurements; generalization experiments demonstrate robustness across environments.  
- Competition win versus a strong baseline (using extra game-engine supervision) provides compelling real-world validation.  
- Paper is well-situated in literature and connects to Schaul et al. (2015) while highlighting its novel contributions.  
- Suggestion: add a detailed discussion of limitations (scaling to more complex sensory inputs, constraints on usable measurements).  
- Suggestion: include sensitivity analysis for training hyperparameters (experience memory size, off-policy training).  
- Suggestion: provide a more explicit comparison of computational efficiency and training speed versus traditional RL.  
- Question: how would adding memory (LSTMs/recurrent models) affect performance, complexity, and training efficiency?  
- Question: why do models trained with random goal vectors generalize better — request theoretical or mechanistic explanation.  
- Question: provide specific evidence on training dynamics differences versus RL (learning curves, performance variance).
