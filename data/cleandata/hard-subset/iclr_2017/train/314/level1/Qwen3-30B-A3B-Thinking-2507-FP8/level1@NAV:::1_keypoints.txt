- Paper introduces supervised prediction of future low-dimensional measurements from high-dimensional sensory input as an alternative to sparse-reward RL for sensorimotor control in 3D environments.
- Key contributions: enables learning from raw pixels without extraneous supervision, supports training without fixed goals, and allows flexible goal specification at test time.
- Strong empirical validation in VizDoom: extensive experiments, outperforms state-of-the-art deep RL on complex tasks, and won the Full Deathmatch track in unseen environments.
- Recommendation: accept — approach is well-motivated and well-situated within related work, addressing RL limitations in complex environments.
- Experimental rigor: thorough comparisons to strong baselines, ablation studies, and demonstration of generalization across environments and goals.
- Ablations show vectorial, multi-time-offset measurement prediction yields significantly richer training signal than scalar rewards.
- Particularly impressive: goal-agnostic training and successful generalization to new, previously unseen goals.
- Suggested improvements: clarify relationship to successor representations (Kulkarni et al., 2016b).
- Suggested improvements: provide a detailed discussion of computational complexity versus standard RL methods.
- Questions for authors: how does the method work when measurement streams are not provided and must be inferred from sensory input? Why does randomized goal training outperform fixed-goal training in goal-agnostic experiments?
