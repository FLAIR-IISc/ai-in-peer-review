1. Paper reframes sensorimotor control as supervised learning via Direct Future Prediction (DFP) using cotemporal sensory and measurement streams.  
2. DFP predicts future lower-dimensional measurements from raw sensory input and goal signals.  
3. Key contribution: avoids sparse scalar rewards by using dense, vectorial supervision.  
4. Empirical claim: consistently outperforms deep RL baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR) across Doom scenarios.  
5. Demonstrates generalization across environments and goals without retraining.  
6. External validation: won Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch, >50% better than a more complex baseline.  
7. Strong motivation: addresses RL’s reliance on sparse rewards and is grounded in supervised control literature.  
8. Thorough experimental design with ablations showing importance of vectorial feedback and multiple temporal offsets.  
9. Goal-agnostic training experiments show models can adapt to new goals with minimal performance loss.  
10. Suggestion: strengthen the theoretical connection to predictive state representations (PSR).  
11. Suggestion: expand limitations section to discuss when being purely reactive fails and how memory could help.  
12. Suggestion: clarify architecture details, especially the normalization layer in the prediction module.  
13. Suggestion: clarify whether training used only agent-collected “raw experience” or included human demonstrations.  
14. Question: request an information-theoretic characterization of the measurement stream’s advantage over scalar rewards.  
15. Question: ask why training with goal range [-1,1] outperformed [0,1] on the pacifist test and implications for generalization.  
16. Question: request specific dimensionalities (number of measurements vs image pixels) used in experiments.  
17. Question: ask for practical implications of goal-flexible training for real-world, dynamically changing goals.
