- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict vector-valued future measurements conditioned on sensory input and a goal, selecting actions by maximizing predicted goal utility.  
- Trains via dense, endogenous supervision enabling goal-agnostic training and dynamic goal specification at test time.  
- Architecture integrates perception, measurement, and goal modules with a dueling-style split into expectation and action streams.  
- Strong empirical results in ViZDoom: outperforms DQN/A3C/DSR, generalizes to unseen textures, benefits from vectorial feedback and multi-horizon prediction, and wins Visual Doom AI Full Deathmatch.  
- Decision: Accept — motivated by a simple, impactful reformulation and compelling empirical superiority/generalization.  
- Problem addressed: visuomotor control from raw input in complex 3D environments with changing goals.  
- Good placement relative to RL, UVFA, predictive representations, and multi-objective RL; argument for dense vectorial measurements is timely.  
- Solid empirical rigor: strong baselines, multiple seeds, long rollouts, ablations, cross-environment tests, and public competition result.  
- Limitations: discrete actions only, short prediction horizons, no memory/partial-observability handling, limited theoretical analysis, and constrained baseline tuning for DSR.  
- Clarify sensitivity to choice and scaling of measurements and strategies when measurements are unavailable or noisy.  
- Analyze horizon length effects: performance and stability for larger τ or different offset weightings.  
- Report statistical significance/confidence intervals and variance across seeds for key metrics.  
- Discuss computational cost of predicting all actions per step and scalability to large action spaces or continuous control.  
- Consider adding memory (e.g., recurrent modules) and evaluating partial observability.  
- How does performance change if training goal distribution differs from test-time goals (e.g., sparse/adversarial goals)?  
- What is the minimal/necessary set of measurements for DFP to match A3C, and how robust is DFP to corrupted measurement channels?  
- Were comparisons made to dueling DQN and UVFA baselines with similar architectural capacity?  
- How large is the experience memory M and update lag k, and how sensitive are results to these choices?  
- Can DFP handle stochastic dynamics or delayed effects beyond 32 steps without degrading training stability?
