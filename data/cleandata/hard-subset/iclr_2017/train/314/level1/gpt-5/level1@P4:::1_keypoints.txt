- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future measurement changes conditioned on sensory input, action, and goal, then select actions by maximizing goal · predicted measurements.  
- Uses dense, vectorial measurement supervision instead of sparse scalar rewards, enabling training without fixed goals and goal-conditioned control at test time.  
- Introduces a neural architecture with separate expectation and action-difference streams, similar to dueling networks, to predict action-conditioned measurement futures.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on four ViZDoom scenarios, often outperforming DQN and A3C in learning speed and final performance; DSR compared on a simpler task.  
- Shows generalization across textures and goals and won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch track without extra supervision.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reasons for acceptance: substantial empirical gains on challenging 3D visuomotor tasks and a simple, well-motivated shift to dense multivariate prediction for goal-directed control.  
- Problem framed: learning sensorimotor control from high-dimensional inputs with ability to pursue varying goals at test time.  
- Motivates approach by relating to Monte Carlo, multi-objective RL, UVFA, and successor representations while emphasizing direct action-conditioned measurement prediction and explicit goal parameterization.  
- Comparisons: careful empirical comparisons to DQN and A3C across scenarios, with some tuning for A3C; DSR evaluated where feasible.  
- Generalization experiments: cross-texture transfer improves with diverse training and larger models aid transfer.  
- Goal-agnostic training: randomized-goal training nearly matches single-goal training and better generalizes to new goals.  
- Ablations: multiple measurements and temporal offsets improve performance, supporting the dense multivariate supervision hypothesis.  
- External validation: competition win provides competitive, unseen-environment validation.  
- Technical soundness: supervised regression objective and architecture are plausible; training protocol is standard though no formal guarantees are provided.  
- Suggest reporting compute budgets, wall-clock times, hyperparameter sweeps, error bars, and per-seed variance for baseline parity.  
- Recommend discussing how to design/select measurement vectors in domains lacking obvious measurements and robustness to partial/noisy measurements.  
- Ask for analysis of temporal-offset choice and loss-weight sensitivity, including longer horizons and learning offset weights end-to-end.  
- Request clarification on action-space scaling: number of composite actions in scenarios and strategies for large or continuous action spaces.  
- Suggest experiments with memory/partial observability (frame stacking or recurrent modules) for tasks needing longer-term memory.  
- Ask whether larger or prioritized replay buffers affect stability and performance.  
- Reproducibility: include precise architecture hyperparameters, optimizer settings, preprocessing, training schedules, and ideally release code.  
- Question measurement sensitivity and dimensionality: tests with incomplete/noisy or learned measurements, and categorical/mixed targets?  
- Question temporal horizons: impact of horizons beyond 32 steps, degradation with shorter/longer horizons, and learned offset weighting?  
- Question action-space details: exact action counts per scenario, inference cost scaling, and sub-action factorization or action sampling experiments?  
- Question continuous actions: any preliminary results for continuous domains or action sampling at decision time?  
- Baseline fairness questions: A3C network capacity, preprocessing, use of multi-step returns or auxiliary tasks to match dense supervision, and per-seed/stddev results?  
- Generalization questions: do randomized-goal-trained policies improve cross-environment generalization beyond texture transfer, and tests on layout or enemy behavior variations?  
- Competition model question: differences between the competition-winning model and the D4-tx-L setup (architecture, schedule, offsets, goal distribution)?
