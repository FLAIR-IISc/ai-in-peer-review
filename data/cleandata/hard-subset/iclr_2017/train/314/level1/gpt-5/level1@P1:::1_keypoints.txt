- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future intrinsic measurements at multiple horizons conditioned on observation, measurements, action, and goal, and select actions whose predictions best match the goal.
- Key idea 1: Use dense, multidimensional measurement streams as supervision instead of sparse scalar rewards.
- Key idea 2: Parameterize goals as linear combinations over predicted future measurement differences to enable goal-agnostic training and dynamic test-time goals.
- Key idea 3: Practical deep architecture with perception/measurement/goal encoders and a dueling-style expectation/action stream for action-conditional prediction.
- Empirical claim: DFP outperforms strong deep RL baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR where feasible) on Doom-based 3D visuomotor tasks, with large gains in complex combat scenarios.
- Generalization: Shows transfer to unseen textures/environments and to novel goal vectors; won the Visual Doom AI competition on unseen maps.
- Ablation result: Vectorial measurements and multi-horizon predictions both materially improve performance.
- Decision: Accept.
- Acceptance reason 1: Strong, convincing empirical results in challenging 3D embodied control domains with robust generalization and competition win.
- Acceptance reason 2: Conceptually clear, well-motivated alternative to reward-centric TD/policy-gradient RL leveraging dense measurements and goal-parameterization.
- Acceptance reason 3: Careful ablations and comparisons indicate the core design choices are impactful.
- Problem/motivation support: Tackles visuomotor control from raw sensory input using natural dense feedback and positions well relative to related prediction/representation work.
- Method soundness: Simple, transparent formulation predicting action-conditional future measurement deltas with stable supervised regression and no bootstrapped TD targets.
- Empirical rigor: Extensive experiments across four Doom scenarios, multiple baselines and seeds, ablations, training speed and evaluation details.
- Empirical details: Reports performance over 50M steps, trains efficiently in wall-clock time, and shows goal-agnostic training nearly matches dedicated models.
- External validation: Competition win outperforms a stronger baseline that used extra supervision.
- Rigor caveat: Limited theoretical analysis but strong empirical evidence.
- Feedback—baselines: Explicitly state whether baselines received the same measurement vector mt and consider reporting A3C-LSTM and additional SR/UVFA baselines on harder tasks.
- Feedback—analysis: Report prediction accuracy/calibration and its correlation with control, ablate the dueling split, and test sensitivity to horizon choices and replay settings.
- Feedback—generalization claims: Texture transfers are limited; include transfer to unseen layouts and denser goal-space sweeps to substantiate broader generalization.
- Feedback—scope/limitations: Discuss cases with unavailable/partial measurements and whether auxiliary signals can substitute; clarify off-policy target issues and potential bias.
- Questions for authors (1): Do all baselines receive the same measurement vector mt as input?
- Question (2): How are measurements normalized/scaled and how sensitive is DFP to these choices?
- Question (3): What is the exact target policy for predicted futures and how does policy evolution affect target stationarity?
- Question (4): How critical is the dueling expectation/action decomposition versus single-head predictors?
- Question (5): Were A3C-LSTM or frame-stacked variants evaluated for partial observability, and how do they compare?
- Question (6): Can DFP handle continuous actions via parameterized action-conditional predictors and has it been tried in continuous-control domains?
- Question (7): How far does goal generalization extend (e.g., extrapolation beyond training goal ranges or emphasizing long-horizon effects)?
- Question (8): Can you demonstrate transfer to unseen map topologies (new mazes) in addition to texture transfer?
