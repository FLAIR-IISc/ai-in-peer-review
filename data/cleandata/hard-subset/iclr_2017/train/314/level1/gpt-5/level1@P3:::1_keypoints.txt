- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future measurement-change vectors conditioned on observation, candidate action, and goal, then select actions by maximizing predicted measurement-goal inner product.  
- Model ingests raw pixels and low-dimensional measurements, fuses with a goal embedding, and uses a dueling-style expectation + action-advantage decomposition to output multi-horizon action-conditional predictions.  
- Trains by regressing to Monte Carlo multi-horizon targets from a short replay buffer with ε-greedy data collection, avoiding bootstrapping.  
- Extensive ViZDoom experiments show competitive performance on simple tasks and strong wins over DQN/A3C on harder combat tasks, plus generalization across textures/maps and test-time goal adaptation; won Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Strong empirical performance and compelling demonstrations of goal-parameterized generalization in challenging 3D visuomotor tasks.  
- Key reason 2: Simple, well-motivated formulation bridging predictive representations and control, advantageous when dense multivariate feedback is available.  
- Targets an important problem: learning to act from raw sensory input in 3D environments with dynamically specified goals and exploiting dense vector-valued measurements.  
- Situates method at intersection of predictive representations (GVFs), UVFAs, and successor representations, and correctly differentiates DFP from DSR by regressing finite-horizon action-conditioned outcomes.  
- Methodologically sound: Monte Carlo multi-horizon targets remove bootstrapping instability, dueling split plausible for action differences, and goal-conditioning is end-to-end.  
- Empirically rigorous: suite of four tasks, relevant ablations, generalization tests, randomized-goal zero-shot adaptation, multiple seeds, and external competition validation.  
- Impact: Shows direct prediction of intrinsic measurements is a robust alternative to scalar-reward RL and enables flexible goal-parameterized behavior without retraining.  
- Concern: Fairness of baselines — DQN/A3C implementations, hyperparameters, and lack of recurrence/frame stacking may bias comparisons; details needed.  
- Concern: Policy dependence — Monte Carlo targets collected under current behavior policy entangle "take a then follow evolving policy," raising stability/off-policy concerns with replay.  
- Concern: Horizon design is heuristic (offsets/weights); sensitivity analysis and relation to discounted returns are lacking.  
- Concern: Architecture ablations missing — benefits of expectation/action split, goal/measurement embeddings, and normalization are not quantified.  
- Suggestion: Formalize connections to successor features and UVFAs, and clarify conditions for generalized policy improvement and role of policy dependence.  
- Suggestion: Strengthen baselines by including recurrent A3C/LSTM, frame stacking, and matched preprocessing; report hyperparameter sensitivities.  
- Suggestion: Report data efficiency and wall-clock compute (samples, hours), and include training curves comparing goal-agnostic vs fixed-goal training.  
- Suggestion: Add robustness/sensitivity ablations on horizon spacing, measurement subsets, replay size/ε-schedule, and dueling vs monolithic head.  
- Suggestion: Clarify evaluation details: normalization of measurements, frame rates, action repeat, terminal penalties, and include confidence intervals.  
- Q1: Did DQN/A3C use frame stacks or recurrence (LSTM), and were observations/preprocessing identical across methods?  
- Q2: Do targets F(o_t,a,g) correspond to “take action a at t, then follow current behavior policy,” and how sensitive is learning to policy drift with replay?  
- Q3: How sensitive are results to chosen temporal offsets and last-three weighting; any comparison to exponential discounting?  
- Q4: What is the marginal benefit of the expectation/action split and action-normalization layer versus a single fully connected head?  
- Q5: For randomized-goal training, what distribution over goal weights was used and how far can test-time goals extrapolate beyond training?  
- Q6: How critical are specific measurements (ammo, frags); can DFP work with only sparse scalar rewards or noisy/partial measurements?  
- Q7: Can you report training variance across seeds and describe failure modes (e.g., overfitting textures or degenerate behaviors)?
