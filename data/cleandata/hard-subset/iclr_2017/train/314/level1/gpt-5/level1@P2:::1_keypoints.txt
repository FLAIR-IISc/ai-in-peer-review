- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): supervised prediction of action-conditional future measurements from raw sensory input and a goal vector, selecting actions by maximizing goal-weighted predicted measurements.
- Key claims: dense multivariate measurement prediction stabilizes/accelerates training vs scalar rewards.
- Key claims: goal-conditioned parameterization enables training without fixed goals and flexible test-time goal changes.
- Key claims: DFP outperforms deep RL baselines (DQN, A3C) on challenging 3D ViZDoom tasks and won the Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Paper is clear, well-motivated, and provides ablations showing benefits of multiple measurements and multi-horizon prediction.
- Decision: Accept.
- Main reason: Strong empirical evidence that a simple supervised objective can outperform state-of-the-art deep RL on hard visuomotor tasks, with generalization and competition results.
- Main reason: Conceptual novelty—reframes control as supervised prediction over intrinsic measurements and connects to UVFA/PSR with a practical architecture.
- Problem significance: Tackles learning sensorimotor control from raw sensory streams with flexible test-time goals, a timely and important challenge.
- Literature placement: Related to UVFA, generalized value functions, and predictive representations but differs by predicting action-conditional, multi-horizon measurements and using goal-weighted selection.
- Empirical support: Extensive comparisons to DQN/A3C (and DSR where feasible) across four scenarios showing large performance gains on harder tasks.
- Generalization evidence: Training on varied textures and increasing model capacity improve cross-environment transfer.
- Goal flexibility evidence: Randomized-goal training nearly matches task-specific models and generalizes better to new goals.
- Ablations: Vector-valued measurements and multi-horizon predictions substantially improve performance even for single-metric evaluations.
- Practicality: High training throughput on ViZDoom and external competition success indicate relevance and scalability.
- Limitation: Off-policy/statistical consistency concerns—DFP predicts under the data-collection policy which can introduce bias; sensitivity to replay size/refresh rate should be discussed.
- Limitation: Baseline completeness—DQN variant lacks dueling/double/prioritized variants and A3C lacks LSTM; architectural/memory differences may explain some gains.
- Limitation: Unclear performance on long-horizon/sparse reward tasks since DFP relies on dense short-to-mid horizons.
- Limitation: Statistical reporting is limited—more seeds/confidence intervals needed to quantify variability.
- Suggested improvements: Clarify exact learning targets (fi computation and weighting) and why certain horizon weightings work best.
- Suggested analyses: Sensitivity tests for measurement set, horizon set, coefficients, replay buffer size, update frequency, and ε schedule.
- Suggested baselines: Add dueling/double/prioritized DQN and A3C-LSTM (or explain infeasibility and expected gaps) and report their throughput.
- Suggested reporting: Provide learning curves with shaded variance, compute budgets, hardware, and frames-per-second for each method.
- Broader applicability: Discuss scenarios with scarce/costly measurements, learning measurements from representations, and extension to continuous actions.
- Questions for authors: Sensitivity of DFP to temporal offsets and horizon weighting; attempts with learned/exponential weights?
- Questions for authors: Magnitude of off-policy bias from small replay and changing policy; performance vs buffer size or on-policy variants?
- Questions for authors: Can you report dueling DQN/double/prioritized and A3C-LSTM results to isolate architecture/memory effects?
- Questions for authors: How are measurement vectors chosen practically; can DFP predict learned latent measurements useful across goals?
- Questions for authors: How does DFP perform with non-linear goal utilities or constraints—any preliminary results?
- Questions for authors: Effect of much longer horizons or sparser measurement feedback; could eligibility traces or n-step returns help?
- Questions for authors: Will code, trained models, and environment configs be released for reproducibility?
