**Summary**

This paper presents an approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments, utilizing a high-dimensional sensory stream and a lower-dimensional measurement stream. The cotemporal structure of these streams provides a rich supervisory signal, enabling training a sensorimotor control model through supervised learning techniques without extraneous supervision. The model learns to act based on raw sensory input from a complex three-dimensional environment, and experiments demonstrate that it outperforms sophisticated prior formulations, particularly on challenging tasks.

**Decision**

Based on the reviewing criteria, I decide to **Accept** this paper with two key reasons:

1. The approach is well-motivated and well-placed in the literature, building upon the idea of supervised learning for sensorimotor control and addressing the limitations of traditional reinforcement learning methods.
2. The paper supports its claims with extensive experiments in three-dimensional simulations based on the classical first-person game Doom, demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach in various scenarios and its ability to generalize across environments and goals.

**Supporting arguments**

The paper provides a clear and concise introduction to the problem of sensorimotor control in immersive environments, highlighting the limitations of traditional reinforcement learning methods and the potential benefits of using supervised learning techniques. The authors also provide a thorough review of related work, positioning their approach within the context of existing literature.

The experimental evaluation is comprehensive and well-designed, covering various scenarios of increasing difficulty and comparing the proposed approach to state-of-the-art deep RL methods. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach in achieving high performance in complex tasks and its ability to generalize across environments and goals.
