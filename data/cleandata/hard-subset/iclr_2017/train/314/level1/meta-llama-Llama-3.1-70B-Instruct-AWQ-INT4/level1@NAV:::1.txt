**Summary**

The paper presents an approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments, utilizing a high-dimensional sensory stream and a lower-dimensional measurement stream. The approach leverages the cotemporal structure of these streams to provide a rich supervisory signal, enabling training of a sensorimotor control model through supervised learning techniques. The model is trained without extraneous supervision, learning to act based on raw sensory input from a complex three-dimensional environment. The approach is evaluated in immersive three-dimensional simulations based on the classical first-person game Doom, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance and generalization across environments and goals.

**Decision**

I recommend accepting this paper, with two key reasons:

1. **Well-motivated approach**: The paper presents a well-motivated approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments, leveraging the cotemporal structure of sensory and measurement streams to provide a rich supervisory signal. This approach is grounded in the literature and addresses key challenges in sensorimotor control.
2. **Strong empirical results**: The paper presents extensive experiments demonstrating state-of-the-art performance and generalization across environments and goals. The results are well-presented and provide a thorough evaluation of the approach.
