- Paper proposes supervised sensorimotor control using cotemporal high-dimensional sensory and lower-dimensional measurement streams as a rich supervisory signal.  
- Training uses supervised learning without external rewards, learning from raw sensory input in complex 3D environments.  
- Evaluated in immersive 3D Doom simulations and achieves state-of-the-art performance on challenging tasks.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main reason 1: Well-motivated departure from traditional reward-based RL with clear explanation of RL limitations and benefits of the proposed method.  
- Main reason 2: Strong empirical results showing the method outperforms deep RL, especially with richer measurements.  
- Supporting point: Thorough analysis of RL limitations and clear description of how cotemporal structure provides supervision.  
- Supporting point: Extensive experiments substantiate effectiveness in challenging scenarios.  
- Requested improvement: Provide more details on the predictor network architecture (layers, activations).  
- Requested improvement: Include analysis of convergence compared to traditional RL.  
- Requested improvement: Discuss broader potential applications beyond immersive 3D simulations.  
- Questions for authors: Specify predictor network implementation (layers, activations).  
- Questions for authors: Explain selection and intuition behind temporal offsets used.  
- Questions for authors: Discuss potential limitations in scenarios with very high-dimensional sensory input or complex measurement streams.
