- Proposes reducing sensorimotor control in immersive environments to supervised learning using high-dimensional sensory and lower-dimensional measurement streams.  
- Formulation enables training without a fixed goal and pursuing dynamically changing goals at test time.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance in 3D Doom simulations, outperforming state-of-the-art deep reinforcement learning methods.  
- Reviewer decision: Accept.  
- Approach is well-motivated by environment characteristics and the cotemporal structure of sensory and measurement streams.  
- Experimental evaluation is extensive and shows particular strength on challenging tasks.  
- Paper makes a significant contribution with potential applications in robotics and video games.  
- Suggest clarifying the relationship and interaction between sensory and measurement streams and their role in the supervisory signal.  
- Recommend providing more architectural details about components and their interactions.  
- Advise discussing potential limitations and directions for future work, including scalability to more complex environments.  
- Questions for authors: how do the streams interact to produce supervision, how were architecture choices and hyperparameters selected, and have you considered real-world or robotics applications?
