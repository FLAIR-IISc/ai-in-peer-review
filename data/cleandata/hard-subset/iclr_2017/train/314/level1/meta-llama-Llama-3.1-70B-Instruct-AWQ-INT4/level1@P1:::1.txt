**Summary of the paper**

This paper presents an approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments, utilizing a high-dimensional sensory stream and a lower-dimensional measurement stream. The cotemporal structure of these streams provides a rich supervisory signal, enabling training a sensorimotor control model by interacting with the environment. The model is trained using supervised learning techniques, without extraneous supervision, and learns to act based on raw sensory input from a complex three-dimensional environment. The approach is evaluated in immersive three-dimensional simulations based on the classical first-person game Doom, and demonstrates state-of-the-art performance in challenging tasks.

**Decision: Accept**

I recommend accepting this paper for publication. The main reasons for this decision are:

1. **Well-motivated approach**: The paper presents a well-motivated approach to sensorimotor control, departing from the traditional reward-based formalization of reinforcement learning. The authors provide a clear explanation of the limitations of traditional approaches and demonstrate the benefits of their proposed method.
2. **Strong empirical results**: The paper presents extensive experimental results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach in challenging tasks. The results show that the approach outperforms state-of-the-art deep reinforcement learning methods, particularly in scenarios with richer measurements.
