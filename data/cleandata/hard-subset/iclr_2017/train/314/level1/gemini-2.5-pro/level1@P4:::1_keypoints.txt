- Presents Direct Future Prediction (DFP), reframing sensorimotor control as supervised prediction of future low-dimensional measurements from sensory input and goals.  
- Allows dynamic test-time goals by selecting actions predicted to best satisfy the current goal; trained without a fixed objective.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance in 3D Doom environments, substantially outperforming DQN and A3C.  
- Shows effective generalization across environments and goals, with thorough ablation studies supporting design choices.  
- Victory in the Visual Doom AI Competition provides strong external validation.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Novel, well-motivated formulation leveraging dense, multidimensional feedback instead of sparse scalar rewards.  
- Key reason 2: Strong, comprehensive experimental validation across scenarios and baselines, plus ablations and generalization tests.  
- Situates work within prior supervised control and goal-conditioned RL, with the specific measurement-prediction formulation being novel.  
- Baselines and scenarios: fair comparisons to DQN/A3C on increasingly complex tasks, with DFP especially superior on "Battle" tasks.  
- Ablation study: vectorial measurements and multi-horizon predictions are beneficial.  
- Generalization: goal-agnostic training and randomized-texture experiments show policy flexibility to new goals and visuals.  
- Architecture: sensible design using a dueling-like structure; paper is clear and well-presented.  
- Suggest clarifying how goals are encoded in the goal vector g in practice (e.g., mapping scalar objectives to full g).  
- Suggest discussing relation to model-based RL since DFP predicts action consequences (albeit only measurements).  
- Recommend addressing limitations when meaningful low-dimensional measurements are unavailable, noisy, or hard to define.  
- Question: How does the goal vector g influence learning if g is an input but not in the regression loss—what mechanism teaches the network to use g?  
- Question: How does a model trained with a fixed goal still generalize to new goals at test time—does the goal module learn a trivial mapping or is another factor responsible?
