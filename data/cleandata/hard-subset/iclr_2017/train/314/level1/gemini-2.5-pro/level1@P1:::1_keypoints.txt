- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): frame control as supervised prediction of future measurement vectors conditioned on sensory input, measurements, goal, and action.  
- Uses dense, multi-dimensional measurement streams (health, ammo) as supervision, avoiding scalar rewards.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains in complex 3D tasks, outperforming deep RL baselines (DQN, A3C).  
- Shows flexibility to pursue dynamically specified goals at test time, even unseen during training.  
- Exhibits robust generalization to novel environments and textures, validated by winning the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: four Doom scenarios of increasing difficulty showing large performance gaps (2.5–6x frags advantage in hardest settings).  
- Generalization across environments supported by randomized-texture maze tests and competition results.  
- Generalization across goals shown: models trained on randomized goals perform well on specific tasks and novel goals (e.g., pacifist).  
- Ablation study confirms predicting vectorial measurements and multi-step futures is superior to scalar rewards and single-step prediction.  
- Decision: Accept — motivated by an elegant formulation and rigorous, state-of-the-art empirical validation.  
- Suggest discussing DFP’s place on the model-free vs. model-based spectrum since it predicts outcomes but not full sensory states.  
- Recommend analyzing sensitivity to what is included in the measurement vector (noisy/irrelevant measurements) and possibly learning important measurements.  
- Propose an ablation on the dueling-style architecture to justify the expectation/action split.  
- Asks about sensitivity to chosen temporal offsets (τ) and whether other schemes were tried.  
- Raises concern about long-term planning limits when consequences occur many hundreds of steps later compared to value-based methods.  
- Questions rationale for concatenating goal vector early versus alternative goal-conditioning methods (multiplicative gating or UVF-style combinations).
