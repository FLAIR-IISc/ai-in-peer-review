- Introduces Direct Future Prediction (DFP): frame control as supervised prediction of future low-dimensional measurements from pixels and candidate actions.  
- Leverages dense, multivariate measurement stream (e.g., health, ammo) to avoid sparse RL rewards.  
- Trains without a fixed objective so agents can pursue dynamically specified goals at test time.  
- Demonstrated on ViZDoom, significantly outperforming state-of-the-art deep RL baselines.  
- Reviewer strongly recommends Accept.  
- Reason 1: DFP is a simple, elegant, and powerful alternative to standard RL, well-suited for complex environments with rich feedback.  
- Reason 2: Claims are substantiated by extensive, rigorous experiments, strong baselines, ablations, and a competition victory.  
- Main strength: Reformulating control as prediction exploits temporally dense multivariate signals often collapsed into a scalar reward in RL.  
- Empirical support: DFP surpasses A3C by a large margin; ablations show vectorial feedback and multi-step predictions are critical.  
- Generalization: Goal-agnostic training and performance in unseen environments validated by winning the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Suggestion: Discuss principles for selecting which variables belong to the sensory vs. measurement streams and what to predict vs. condition on.  
- Suggestion: Provide discussion on sensitivity and intuition for choosing temporal prediction offsets (τ).  
- Clarification request 1: In goal-agnostic training, is the random goal vector g provided during action selection or only used when training predictor F?  
- Clarification request 2: How does DFP perform when the measurement stream is sparse or effectively scalar, and when would advantages over methods like A3C diminish?
