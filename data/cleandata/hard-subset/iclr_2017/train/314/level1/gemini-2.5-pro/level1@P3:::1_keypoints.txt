- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future measurement changes per action from pixels, measurements, and a goal vector, then pick the action maximizing goal-weighted prediction.
- Contribution: uses dense, multi-dimensional measurement signals as supervision instead of sparse scalar rewards.
- Contribution: decouples world knowledge (predictor F) from objectives, allowing training without fixed goals and dynamic goal specification at test time.
- Contribution: strong empirical results in Doom, significantly outperforming A3C/DQN on complex tasks.
- Contribution: demonstrates generalization to unseen visuals and novel goals, validated by winning the Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: novel, well-motivated formulation that sidesteps credit-assignment and sample-efficiency issues of sparse rewards.
- Key reason: exceptional empirical validation — large performance gains, extensive ablations, and zero-shot goal generalization.
- Supporting point: elegant separation of F and g (argmax_a g^T F(...)) enables flexible, goal-directed behaviors.
- Supporting point: ablations show vectorial feedback and multi-horizon prediction are critical to performance.
- Supporting point: competition win provides strong external validation of robustness and generalization.
- Feedback: clarify connection to model-based RL/dynamics modeling in measurement space.
- Feedback: discuss training stability and why off-policy experience replay did not harm learning.
- Feedback: address the linear goal representation assumption and potential extensions to non-linear objectives.
- Question: how does the network learn to interpret the goal vector g during training given the regression loss does not depend explicitly on g?
- Question: is the specialist vs. generalist performance gap fundamental or could it be closed with larger capacity or more diverse goal sampling?
