- Reformulates sensorimotor control as supervised learning: predict future low-dimensional measurements from pixels, action, and goal.  
- Represents goal as a desired weighting over future measurements, enabling dynamic objectives at test time without retraining.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results in ViZDoom: DFP significantly outperforms state-of-the-art deep RL (e.g., A3C), especially on complex tasks.  
- Shows substantial gains in frag count (DFP > A3C by factor 2.5–6) in Battle scenarios, indicating improved credit assignment via dense supervision.  
- Goal-conditioned model trained with randomized goals performs nearly as well as specialized models and generalizes to novel behaviors (e.g., “pacifist”).  
- Ablation study confirms benefits of vectorial (multi-dimensional) feedback and multi-horizon prediction.  
- Strong generalization to new goals and unseen environments, validated by winning the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Primary strength: simple, stable, and efficient learning by leveraging continuous multi-faceted supervisory signals instead of sparse scalar rewards.  
- Suggestion: provide a principled guideline for distinguishing measurements vs. sensory input in new domains (dimensionality, semantics, or goal-definability).  
- Suggestion: explore more sophisticated exploration strategies (e.g., uncertainty or disagreement among predicted futures) beyond ε-greedy.  
- Suggestion: discuss scope and limitations for real-world deployment where measurements may be noisy, partially observable, or must be learned.  
- Question: clarify intuition for feeding goal into the network during training vs. training a goal-agnostic predictor and applying goals at test time.  
- Question: report the training update frequency k (policy update every k experiences) and sensitivity to this parameter.  
- Question: clarify sensitivity to chosen temporal offsets τ for predictions and impact of using only longer-term horizons.
