## Review of "Sensorimotor Control with High-Dimensional Sensory Streams"

This paper presents a novel approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments, framing the problem as supervised learning through prediction of future measurements given current sensory input and actions. The core idea – leveraging the rich supervisory signal inherent in the co-temporal structure of sensory and measurement streams – is compelling and well-motivated, particularly in complex environments where sparse reward signals hinder traditional reinforcement learning. The authors demonstrate strong empirical results, including winning the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition, showcasing the practical effectiveness of their method.

**Reject.** While the paper presents a promising approach and impressive results, the presentation lacks sufficient detail regarding implementation and experimental setup to fully assess the validity of the claims. Specifically, the ablation studies, while present, don't offer enough granularity to understand *why* certain components contribute to performance.

The paper convincingly argues that dense, multi-dimensional feedback is superior to sparse rewards. The results in Doom, particularly outperforming methods with more complex architectures and additional supervision, are significant. The generalization across environments and goals is also a strong point. However, the description of the baselines is somewhat cursory. While implementations are linked, details regarding hyperparameter tuning and specific configurations are limited, making direct comparison difficult. The architecture description, while provided, could benefit from more clarity regarding layer sizes and specific choices. The ablation study, while showing the benefit of multi-dimensional feedback and future prediction, doesn't explore the impact of different measurement sets or prediction horizons in sufficient detail.

To strengthen the paper, I recommend the following:

*   **Detailed Baseline Configuration:** Provide a more thorough description of the hyperparameters used for each baseline method, including the search space explored.
*   **Ablation Study Expansion:** Conduct a more granular ablation study, exploring the impact of different combinations of measurements, prediction horizons, and network architectures.
*   **Error Analysis:** Include a qualitative analysis of failure cases to understand the limitations of the approach.
*   **Computational Cost:** Discuss the computational resources required for training and inference.
*   **Clarify Goal Representation:** How sensitive is the performance to the choice of goal representation (linear combination of measurements)? Are there alternative representations that might be more effective?



I would be interested in understanding: What specific architectural choices were made to optimize performance in the Doom environment, and how might these choices generalize to other environments? What is the sensitivity of the approach to the choice of temporal offsets (τ1, …, τn) used for future prediction? Finally, could the authors elaborate on the specific advantages of their approach over existing imitation learning techniques, given that both rely on supervised learning from experience?
