This paper introduces a novel on-policy deep reinforcement learning method incorporating auxiliary intrinsic variables. 
The approach builds upon and can be understood as a specific instance of universal value function-based methods, with appropriate related work cited. While the paper effectively consolidates existing techniques for 3D navigation, I recommend a more explicit articulation of the contributions in the abstract and introduction.
A key area for improvement would be an analysis of the method’s limitations. Investigating scenarios where generalization to new goals breaks down would strengthen the work. Furthermore, given the on-policy nature of the algorithm, the potential for catastrophic forgetting when dealing with infrequently revisited goals should be addressed.
As the primary contribution lies in the integration of established concepts and demonstration of empirical gains, I suggest including results on additional benchmark domains, such as Atari, potentially leveraging ROM data as intrinsic variables. 
Despite these points, the paper demonstrates a clear empirical benefit from the proposed formulation, and the experimental insights gained could prove valuable for the development of future reinforcement learning agents.
