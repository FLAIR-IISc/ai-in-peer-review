The paper introduces an on-policy method for predicting future intrinsic state variables. The work focuses on the Doom environment (specifically vizDoom), and moves beyond typical reward prediction by training a model to forecast sequences of (health, ammunition, frags), weighted by corresponding “goal” triplets provided as input. This weighting scheme serves as a mechanism for exploration, with the ability to optimize solely for long-term goal achievement during evaluation.

The reported results are strong; the model achieved first place in the 2016 vizDoom competition. The experimental evaluation appears rigorous, including comparisons against A3C, DQN, and an attempt to benchmark against the related DSR approach (Kulkarni et al., 2016). DFP consistently outperforms these baselines, or achieves comparable performance when they reach optimal levels, as observed in scenario D1 with A3C. Furthermore, an ablation study provides evidence supporting the contribution of each component of the proposed model.

The paper builds upon established research areas in reinforcement learning, including intrinsic motivation (Singh et al. 2004), auxiliary task learning, and forward modeling. The December 4th revision provides adequate, though not exhaustive, citations to relevant prior work.

I have a few minor comments regarding presentation:
- The environment is more accurately described as 2D, despite being rendered with a 3D perspective where height is not a relevant dimension for agent action or perception.
- The notation “P” in equation (2) and subsequent uses could be misinterpreted as representing probability, rather than prediction.
- The repeated use of the index “j” (with differing font styles) in equation (6) may cause confusion.
- Tables presenting results would benefit from explicitly including units for each measured variable, particularly given the heterogeneous units in Table 1.

Overall, I recommend accepting this paper. While further experiments in diverse environments or a discussion of incremental novelty could be considered, the paper is technically sound, experimentally validated within the Doom environment, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility in the appendix. Its success as the vizDoom competition winner further strengthens its contribution as a significant and reliable work.
