This paper presents an on-policy deep RL method with additional auxiliary internal variables.

- This method is a specific case of a universal value function-based approach, and the authors have mentioned the correct references. Perhaps the biggest claimed technical contribution of this paper is simplifying several existing ideas for solving 3D navigation problems. I think the contribution should be more clearly stated in the abstract/introduction.
- I wanted to see the failure modes of this approach. Under what circumstances does the model have problems generalizing to changing goals? There are other conceptual problems as well - since this is an on-policy method, catastrophic error will occur if the agent does not repeatedly train on distant past goals.
- Since the main contribution of this paper is to integrate several key ideas and show empirical benefits, I wanted to see results on other domains like Atari (perhaps using ROM as the internal variable).

Overall, I think this paper clearly shows the empirical benefit of using the proposed underlying formulations, and the practical insights gained from this paper could be valuable for future agents.
