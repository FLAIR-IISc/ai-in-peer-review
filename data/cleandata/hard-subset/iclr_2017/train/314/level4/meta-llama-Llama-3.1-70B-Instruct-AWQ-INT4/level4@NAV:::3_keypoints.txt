- Proposes an on-policy Deep Future Prediction (DFP) method that forecasts sequences of intrinsic-measurement triplets (health, ammo, frags) weighted by input goal triplets to guide exploration and select actions by maximizing long-term goals.

- Demonstrates strong results, including winning the 2016 vizDoom competition.

- Experimental evaluation is robust, comparing DFP against A3C, DQN, and DSR, where DFP matches or outperforms alternatives across scenarios.

- Ablation study indicates the added model complexity contributes positively to performance.

- Literature references relevant prior work on intrinsic motivation, auxiliary variables, and forward modeling, though not exhaustive.

- Minor suggested revisions: clarify that Doom is 2D despite 3D presentation.

- Notation suggestions: avoid using "P" that might imply probability and disambiguate the double use of "j" in equation (6).

- Presentation improvement: include units for heterogeneous measurements in result tables (e.g., Table 1).

- Overall recommendation: strong accept candidate—possible concerns about novelty or limited environments are outweighed by solid experiments, reproducibility, and the competition win.
