- Proposes an on-policy method (DFP) that predicts future intrinsic measurements to guide action selection.
- Model predicts sequences of triplets (health, ammunition, frags) weighted by an input sequence of goal triplets to enable guided exploration.
- At test time, actions are chosen by maximizing the long-term goal specified by the goal triplets.
- All experiments are conducted in vizDoom (Doom); method trained and evaluated solely in that environment.
- Results are strong: DFP won the 2016 vizDoom competition and outperforms or matches A3C, DQN, and a comparison attempt to DSR.
- Ablation study shows the added model complexity is beneficial.
- Paper adequately references prior work on intrinsic motivation, auxiliary variables, and forward modeling, though references may not be exhaustive.
- Minor issues: Doom is described as 3D but is effectively a 2D environment presented in 3D.
- Minor notation issues: use of "P" in Eq.(2) misleading (suggests probability), and reuse of "j" in Eq.(6) may confuse.
- Presentation suggestion: results tables should repeat measurement units, especially for heterogeneous metrics.
- Recommendation: clear accept—experimentally sound, reproducible appendix, and considered a milestone due to vizDoom win despite limited novelty or single-environment evaluation.
