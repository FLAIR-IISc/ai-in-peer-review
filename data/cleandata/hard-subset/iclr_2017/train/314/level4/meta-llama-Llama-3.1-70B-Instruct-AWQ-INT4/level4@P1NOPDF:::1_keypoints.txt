- Paper introduces a novel deep RL algorithm that predicts low-dimensional game measurements from raw sensory input, improving performance and generalization across goals/environments.
- Algorithm won the Visual Doom AI competition, demonstrating strong empirical performance in new 3D environments.
- Core idea: supervised prediction of low-dimensional measurements conditioned on a given goal vector and the current action; choose actions that maximize predicted outcomes for the goal.
- Learning is supervised (Monte Carlo regression) rather than using temporal-difference (no TD relationship or successor features).
- Key contributions: emphasis on Monte Carlo estimation, use of low-dimensional measurements, parameterized goals, and empirical comparison to prior work.
- Authors provide relevant literature review (Section 2) on future-state prediction and goal-driven function approximators.
- Empirical results show the algorithm can produce generalizable policies that adapt to limited goal changes without further training.
- Paper is well-written and the empirical results are compelling and of clear interest to the community.
- Caveat 1: training assumes on-policy expectations while learning from a replay buffer—this on-policy approximation should be discussed.
- Caveat 2: the method relies on additional metadata (measurements) not available to compared algorithms; limitations when such measurements are absent should be more clearly stated.
