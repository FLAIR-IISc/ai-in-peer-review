- Empirical paper extending deep RL to large state spaces, achieving superior performance in novel 3D environments from raw sensory data and winning the Visual Doom AI competition.  
- Core idea: predict low-dimensional measurements (e.g., ammo/health) via supervised learning conditioned on a goal vector and current action, then pick the action that maximizes the predicted goal-conditioned outcome.  
- Learning is Monte Carlo supervised regression rather than successor features or TD methods; predictions do not enforce temporal (TD) consistency between states.  
- Authors provide a thorough literature review on future-state prediction and goal-driven function approximators.  
- Key contributions: emphasis on Monte Carlo estimation, use of low-dimensional measurements, parametrized goals, and empirical comparisons to prior work.  
- Empirical results show better generalization across goals and environments and the ability to handle limited goal changes without retraining.  
- Paper is well-communicated and presents compelling empirical evidence, making a significant contribution.  
- Minor issue: supervised Monte Carlo training assumes on-policy expectations but is trained from a replay buffer (off-policy), a discrepancy that should be discussed.  
- Minor issue: algorithm uses additional metadata (which sensory parts to predict) that baselines lack; authors should explicitly acknowledge and discuss limitations when such measurements are unavailable.
