- Proposes a novel sensorimotor control method using high-dimensional sensory streams and low-dimensional measurement streams with a supervised-learning training algorithm.  
- Trains a model to act from raw 3D sensory input and pursue dynamically changing goals at test time.  
- Evaluated in immersive 3D Doom simulations, claiming state-of-the-art performance and generalization across environments and goals.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Reason 1: Well-motivated approach addressing a challenging problem with a clear and concise problem formulation and solution.  
- Reason 2: Extensive experiments and evaluations that convincingly demonstrate effectiveness and generalization.  
- Supports claims with thorough analysis, comprehensive prior-work review, and compelling empirical results.  
- Key technical contributions include use of supervised learning (without extraneous supervision), Monte Carlo estimation, low-dimensional measurement prediction, and parametrized goals.  
- Suggestion: Discuss the training approximation that assumes on-policy data while learning from a replay buffer (add to limitations/discussion).  
- Suggestion: Clarify limitations regarding use of additional metadata that indicates which sensory inputs are worth predicting.  
- Question: Provide more details on the predictor network architecture, hyperparameter choices, and number of layers.  
- Question: Explain how training handles dynamically changing goal vectors and detail the randomized goal-vector training regime.
