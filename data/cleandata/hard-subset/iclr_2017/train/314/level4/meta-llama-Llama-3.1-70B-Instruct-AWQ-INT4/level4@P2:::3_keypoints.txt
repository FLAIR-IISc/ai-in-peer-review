- Presents an innovative sensorimotor control method using cotemporal high-dimensional sensory and lower-dimensional measurement streams.  
- Learns from raw experience without extra supervision and can adapt to dynamically changing goals.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Method is well-motivated with a clear, well-organized presentation and comprehensive related-work discussion.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and convincing.  
- Outperforms state-of-the-art methods (DQN, A3C, DSR), especially on challenging tasks like Battle and Battle 2.  
- Ablation study supports the usefulness of the model’s added complexity.  
- Minor clarity issue: relationship between sensory stream and measurement stream needs clearer explanation.  
- Needs more discussion of potential limitations and how the approach scales to more complex environments.  
- Could include more discussion of potential real-world applications.  
- Could compare to a broader set of state-of-the-art methods.  
- Questions for authors: clarify sensory vs. measurement stream relationship; discuss scalability to complex tasks; describe potential real-world applications.
