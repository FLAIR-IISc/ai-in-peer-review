The paper proposes an on-policy approach for predicting future intrinsic measurements. The authors conducted experiments in the vizDoom environment, training their model to forecast sequences of triplets comprising health, ammunition, and frags, weighted by input "goal" triplets. By adjusting the weights of these goal triplets, exploration can be guided. During testing, actions can be taken by maximizing long-term goals.

The results are noteworthy, as the model secured a win in the 2016 vizDoom competition. The experimental section appears robust, featuring comparisons between the proposed Deep Future Prediction (DFP) method and other approaches such as A3C, DQN, and DSR. The DFP method outperforms or equals the performance of other approaches in various scenarios.

An ablation study supports the claim that the added complexity of the proposed model contributes to its effectiveness. The paper adequately references prior work in predicting intrinsic motivation, auxiliary variables, and forward modeling, despite not being exhaustive.

Some minor suggestions for improvement include:

- Clarifying that the Doom environment is 2D, despite its 3D presentation.
- Avoiding potential confusion by using a distinct notation for prediction, as the use of "P" may imply probability.
- Using a different font or notation for the double use of "j" in equation (6) to avoid ambiguity.
- Including units of measurement in the results tables for clarity, particularly in Table 1 where measurements are heterogeneous.

Overall, this paper is a strong candidate for acceptance. While some may argue that experiments in diverse environments or limited novelty could be concerns, the paper's sound experimental design, reproducibility, and achievement as a vizDoom winner outweigh these considerations.
