This paper presents an on-policy deep reinforcement learning (RL) method that incorporates additional auxiliary intrinsic variables. The approach is a special case of the universal value function-based method, and the authors appropriately cite relevant references. However, the paper's primary technical contribution could be more clearly stated in the abstract and introduction. It appears that the main contribution lies in distilling existing ideas to effectively solve 3D navigation problems.

One notable omission is the discussion of failure modes for this approach. It would be beneficial to explore the circumstances under which the model struggles to generalize to changing goals. Furthermore, as an on-policy method, the model is susceptible to catastrophic forgetting if the agent does not repeatedly train on goals from the distant past. These conceptual limitations warrant further examination.

Given that the paper's main contribution is the integration of key ideas and the demonstration of empirical advantages, it would be valuable to see results in other domains, such as Atari games, potentially utilizing the ROM as intrinsic variables. This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the method's applicability and robustness.

Despite these limitations, the paper does demonstrate a clear empirical advantage of using the proposed underlying formulations. The experimental insights presented in this work may be valuable for the development of future agents, and the authors' efforts to integrate and apply existing ideas to a challenging problem are commendable.
