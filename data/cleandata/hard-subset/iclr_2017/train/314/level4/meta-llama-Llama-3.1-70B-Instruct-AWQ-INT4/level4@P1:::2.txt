This paper presents an approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments, leveraging a high-dimensional sensory stream and a lower-dimensional measurement stream. The cotemporal structure of these streams provides a rich supervisory signal, enabling the training of a sensorimotor control model through interaction with the environment. The model is trained using supervised learning techniques, but without extraneous supervision, and learns to act based on raw sensory input from a complex three-dimensional environment.

I decide to accept this paper, with two key reasons for this choice: (1) the approach is well-motivated and effectively integrates existing ideas to solve 3D navigation problems, and (2) the paper demonstrates a clear empirical advantage over state-of-the-art deep RL methods in challenging tasks.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem, discussing the limitations of traditional reinforcement learning approaches and the benefits of their proposed method. The paper is well-structured, and the writing is clear and concise. The experimental results are extensive and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach in various scenarios.

One potential weakness of the paper is the lack of discussion on failure modes and limitations of the approach. It would be valuable to explore under what circumstances the model may struggle to generalize to changing goals or exhibit catastrophic forgetting. Additionally, the authors could provide more insights into the choice of hyperparameters and the sensitivity of the model to these parameters.

To further improve the paper, I suggest the authors consider the following:

* Provide more detailed analysis of the model's performance in different scenarios, including failure modes and limitations.
* Explore the application of the approach to other domains, such as Atari games, to demonstrate its broader applicability.
* Discuss potential avenues for future work, including the integration of memory-based models and the application of the approach to continuous action spaces.
* Consider providing more details on the experimental setup, including the choice of hyperparameters and the sensitivity of the model to these parameters.

Questions for the authors:

* Can you provide more insights into the choice of hyperparameters and the sensitivity of the model to these parameters?
* How do you plan to address the potential issue of catastrophic forgetting in the on-policy learning setting?
* Have you considered exploring the application of the approach to other domains, such as Atari games or robotics?
