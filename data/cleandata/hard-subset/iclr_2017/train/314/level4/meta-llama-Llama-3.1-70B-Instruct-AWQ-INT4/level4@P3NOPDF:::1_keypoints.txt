- Presents a novel deep RL algorithm that uses low-dimensional observations as supervised prediction targets.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance in novel 3D environments from raw sensory data and won the Visual Doom AI competition.  
- Core idea: predict low-dimensional measurements (e.g., ammo, health) conditioned on a given goal vector and the current action.  
- At decision time, select the action that maximizes the predicted outcome according to the goal.  
- Learning is supervised Monte Carlo regression rather than TD learning or successor feature propagation.  
- The goal vector is provided/parameterized (not learned), enabling goal-conditioned predictions and policies.  
- Key contributions: Monte Carlo estimation focus, use of low-dimensional measurements, parameterized goals, and empirical comparisons to prior work.  
- Builds on prior work predicting future states and goal-driven function approximators (reviewed in Section 2).  
- Empirically shows generalization across goals and environments and can adapt to limited goal changes without retraining.  
- Paper is well communicated and provides convincing empirical results, making a significant contribution to deep RL.  
- Minor concern: supervised Monte Carlo learning assumes on-policy returns but training uses a replay buffer, potentially introducing bias—needs discussion.  
- Another concern: algorithm uses extra metadata (which sensory parts to predict) that baselines do not; limitations when such measurements are unavailable should be stated.
