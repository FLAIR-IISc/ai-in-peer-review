The paper presents a novel approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments. The authors utilize a high-dimensional sensory stream and a lower-dimensional measurement stream, leveraging the cotemporal structure of these streams to provide a rich supervisory signal for training a sensorimotor control model. This approach enables the model to learn from raw sensory input in complex three-dimensional environments without extraneous supervision.

I decide to accept this paper with one key reason being the impressive results demonstrated in the experiments, particularly in the Visual Doom AI Competition, where the authors' model won the Full Deathmatch track. The second reason is the sound experimental design, which includes comparisons with state-of-the-art deep reinforcement learning methods and an ablation study that supports the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

The paper's approach to sensorimotor control is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of their method. The use of a high-dimensional sensory stream and a lower-dimensional measurement stream is a novel aspect of this work, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness in learning to act in complex environments.

The experimental results are impressive, with the authors' model outperforming state-of-the-art deep reinforcement learning methods in several scenarios. The ablation study provides additional evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach, demonstrating that the added complexity of the model is useful.

One potential criticism of this work is that the experiments are limited to a single environment (Doom). However, I believe that the results demonstrated in this environment are sufficient to warrant publication, particularly given the novelty and effectiveness of the proposed approach.

To further improve this paper, I suggest that the authors consider conducting experiments in additional environments to demonstrate the generalizability of their approach. Additionally, the authors may want to explore the application of their method to other domains, such as robotics or autonomous vehicles.

Minor comments on the form:

* Doom is described as a 3D environment, whereas it is actually a 2D environment presented in (fake) 3D.
* The use of "P" in (2) may be misleading, as it stands for prediction but not probability.
* The double use of "j" in (6) may be misleading.
* Results tables could repeat the units of the measurements, particularly as they are heterogeneous in Table 1.

Overall, I believe that this paper is a clear accept, and I recommend publication.
