- Novel approach: leverages cotemporal high-dimensional sensory and low-dimensional measurement streams to train sensorimotor control via supervised learning without extraneous supervision.
- Capable of acting from raw sensory input in complex 3D environments and pursuing dynamically changing goals at test time.
- Empirical results show improved performance over state-of-the-art deep RL in challenging tasks.
- Demonstrates generalization across different environments and goals.
- Recommendation: accept based on contributions and strong empirical evidence.
- Key technical contributions include parametrized goals and Monte Carlo estimation for training.
- Thorough empirical comparisons to prior work, showing significant improvements and limited goal-change generalization without retraining.
- Well-motivated framing: using low-dimensional observations as supervised prediction targets.
- Suggestion: discuss the supervised-training approximation that assumes on-policy data while learning from a replay buffer.
- Suggestion: explicitly state what additional metadata the algorithm uses (which parts of sensory input are predicted) and note limitations.
- Questions for authors: clarify details of the supervised-training approximation and its effect on performance.
- Questions for authors: how will the approach handle sensory environments that do not provide measurement streams?
