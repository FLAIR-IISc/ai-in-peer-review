- Proposes an on-policy method (DFP) that predicts sequences of intrinsic measurement triplets (health, ammo, frags) weighted by goal triplets.  
- Goal-weighting mechanism enables guided exploration and action selection by maximizing long-term goals at test time.  
- All experiments are conducted in the vizDoom environment.  
- Method produced impressive results and won the 2016 vizDoom competition.  
- DFP outperforms or matches A3C, DQN, and a comparison attempt with DSR; ablation study supports the added model complexity.  
- Experimental section appears sound and reproducible (appendix provides implementation details).  
- Paper cites intrinsic motivation, auxiliary variables, and forward modeling literature adequately, though not exhaustively.  
- Minor factual error: Doom is described as 3D but is effectively a 2D/pseudo-3D environment.  
- Notational issues: using "P" suggests probability though it denotes prediction; double use of "j" in Eq.(6) is confusing.  
- Presentation issue: results tables should repeat units of measurement, especially Table 1 with heterogeneous metrics.  
- Reviewer recommendation: clear accept—experimentally solid and a milestone paper.
