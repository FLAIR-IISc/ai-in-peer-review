This paper presents an on-policy deep reinforcement learning (RL) method that incorporates auxiliary intrinsic variables. The approach is a special case of the universal value function-based method, and the authors provide accurate citations to relevant references. A significant technical contribution of this paper is the successful distillation of existing ideas to tackle 3D navigation problems. However, the contributions of the paper could be more clearly articulated in the abstract and introduction to provide readers with a better understanding of the work.

To further strengthen the paper, it would be beneficial to discuss the failure modes of the proposed approach. Specifically, it would be valuable to explore the circumstances under which the model struggles to generalize to changing goals. Additionally, there are conceptual concerns associated with the on-policy nature of the method, such as the potential for catastrophic forgetting if the agent does not repeatedly train on goals from the distant past.

Given that the primary contribution of the paper lies in integrating key ideas and demonstrating empirical advantages, it would be interesting to see results in other domains, such as Atari games, potentially utilizing the ROM as intrinsic variables. This would help to further establish the generalizability of the proposed approach.

Overall, the paper demonstrates a clear empirical advantage of using the proposed formulation, and the experimental insights gained from this work may be valuable for the development of future agents.
