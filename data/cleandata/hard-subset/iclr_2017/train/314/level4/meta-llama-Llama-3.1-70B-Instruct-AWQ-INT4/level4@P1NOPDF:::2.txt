This paper presents an on-policy deep reinforcement learning (RL) method that incorporates additional auxiliary intrinsic variables. 

- The method is a special case of a universal value function-based approach, and the authors correctly cite relevant references. Perhaps the paper's most significant technical contribution is distilling existing ideas to solve 3D navigation problems. However, the contributions could be more clearly stated in the abstract and introduction to provide a clearer understanding of the paper's scope and impact.
- I would have liked to see an analysis of the approach's failure modes. Under what circumstances does the model struggle to generalize to changing goals? There are also conceptual concerns – as an on-policy method, catastrophic forgetting will occur if the agent does not repeatedly train on goals from the distant past.
- Since the paper's main contribution lies in integrating key ideas and demonstrating empirical advantages, it would be beneficial to include results from other domains, such as Atari, to further validate the approach. Utilizing the ROM as intrinsic variables could be a promising direction for future research.
- Overall, the paper demonstrates a clear empirical advantage of using the proposed underlying formulations, and the experimental insights gained from this research may be valuable for the development of future agents.
