This paper presents a novel approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments, utilizing a high-dimensional sensory stream and a lower-dimensional measurement stream. The authors propose a method that leverages the cotemporal structure of these streams to provide a rich supervisory signal, enabling the training of a sensorimotor control model through supervised learning techniques. The model learns to act based on raw sensory input from a complex three-dimensional environment without extraneous supervision.

The paper's main contributions are its ability to learn without a fixed goal at training time and to pursue dynamically changing goals at test time. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through extensive experiments in three-dimensional simulations based on the classical first-person game Doom. The results show that their approach outperforms sophisticated prior formulations, particularly on challenging tasks, and that the trained models successfully generalize across environments and goals.
