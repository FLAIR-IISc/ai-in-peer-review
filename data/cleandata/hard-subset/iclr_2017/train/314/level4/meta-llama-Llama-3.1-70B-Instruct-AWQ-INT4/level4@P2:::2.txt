This paper proposes a novel approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments, utilizing a high-dimensional sensory stream and a lower-dimensional measurement stream. The authors demonstrate that the cotemporal structure of these streams provides a rich supervisory signal, enabling the training of a sensorimotor control model through supervised learning techniques without extraneous supervision.

I recommend accepting this paper, with the primary reason being its empirical success in outperforming state-of-the-art deep RL methods in complex 3D environments. The paper's contribution lies in its ability to distill existing ideas and demonstrate their effectiveness in solving challenging tasks.

However, I have a few concerns that need to be addressed. Firstly, the paper could benefit from a clearer statement of its contributions in the abstract and introduction. The authors should explicitly highlight what they believe is the main technical contribution of their work.

Additionally, I would like to see a discussion on the failure modes of this approach. Under what circumstances does the model struggle to generalize to changing goals? The authors should also address the conceptual problem of catastrophic forgetting, which can occur when the agent doesn't repeatedly train on goals from the distant past.

To further strengthen the paper, I suggest including results on other domains, such as Atari, to demonstrate the broader applicability of the proposed approach. This could involve using the ROM as intrinsic variables.

Overall, I believe that this paper demonstrates a clear empirical advantage of using the proposed underlying formulations, and the experimental insights presented here could be valuable for future agents.

Additional feedback:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the relationship between the proposed approach and existing methods, such as universal value function-based approaches.
* The authors should consider including more visualizations or illustrations to help explain the architecture and training process of the model.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the ablation study results, particularly in terms of understanding the importance of predicting measurements at multiple temporal offsets.
* The authors should consider including more details on the hyperparameter tuning process and the sensitivity of the model to different hyperparameters.

Questions for the authors:

* Can you provide more insight into how the model handles situations where the goal is not clearly defined or is changing rapidly?
* How do you plan to address the issue of catastrophic forgetting in future work?
* Are there any plans to extend the proposed approach to continuous action spaces or more complex environments?
