This paper presents a novel approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments. The authors propose a method that utilizes a high-dimensional sensory stream and a lower-dimensional measurement stream, leveraging their cotemporal structure to provide a rich supervisory signal for training a sensorimotor control model. The model is trained using supervised learning techniques, without extraneous supervision, and learns to act based on raw sensory input from complex three-dimensional environments.

I recommend accepting this paper. The key reasons for this choice are the paper's clear and well-motivated approach, which effectively addresses the challenges of sensorimotor control in immersive environments, and the strong empirical results that demonstrate the method's advantages over state-of-the-art deep RL methods.

The paper's approach is well-placed in the literature, drawing on relevant work in supervised learning, reinforcement learning, and sensorimotor control. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the strengths and limitations of their method, including its ability to learn without a fixed goal at training time and to pursue dynamically changing goals at test time.

The empirical results are impressive, demonstrating the method's ability to outperform sophisticated prior formulations, particularly on challenging tasks. The results also show that the trained models successfully generalize across environments and goals. The paper's evaluation is thorough, including comparisons to state-of-the-art deep RL methods, ablation studies, and an analysis of the method's performance in the Visual Doom AI Competition.

To further improve the paper, I suggest that the authors provide more discussion on the limitations of their approach, including potential failure modes and the challenges of catastrophic forgetting. Additionally, it would be valuable to see results on other domains, such as Atari, to further demonstrate the method's generality.

Some specific questions I would like the authors to address are:

* Can the authors provide more insight into how the method handles catastrophic forgetting, and whether there are any strategies for mitigating this issue?
* How do the authors plan to extend their approach to more complex tasks, such as those requiring temporal abstraction and hierarchical organization of learned skills?
* Can the authors provide more details on the architecture of the predictor network, including the specific design choices and hyperparameters used in the experiments?

Overall, I believe that this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of sensorimotor control in immersive environments, and that its empirical insights and experimental results will be valuable for future research in this area.
