- Proposes a novel sensorimotor control method using high-dimensional sensory input plus lower-dimensional measurements as supervisory targets.  
- Trains the model with supervised learning on raw sensory data from complex 3D environments, without extraneous supervision.  
- Demonstrates improved performance and better generalization across goals and environments, evidenced by winning the Visual Doom AI competition.  
- Provides a thorough empirical comparison to relevant prior work, including state-of-the-art deep RL methods, and outperforms them on challenging tasks.  
- Core idea: predict low-dimensional measurements conditioned on a goal vector and current action, using these as supervised targets.  
- Approach contrasts with traditional RL that relies on scalar rewards or TD relationships between state predictions.  
- Highlights contributions: focus on Monte Carlo estimation, use of low-dimensional "measurements" for prediction, and parametrized goals.  
- Paper is well-written and the empirical results are compelling.  
- Learned policies can adapt to limited changes in goals without additional training.  
- Potential issue: supervised training assumes on-policy data but uses a replay buffer sampled from prior policies (possible mismatch).  
- Algorithm uses additional metadata not available to compared methods, which may restrict applicability in some sensory environments.  
- Reviewer requests a clearer discussion of limitations, particularly the impact of additional metadata.  
- Reviewer asks for more experimental details: architectures used for compared algorithms, layer sizes, activations.  
- Reviewer requests hyperparameter tuning details and ranges explored, and specifics of the Visual Doom AI competition tasks, environments, and evaluation metrics.  
- Reviewer asks for clarification on potential applications and how the authors plan to address limitations for real-world use (e.g., robotics).
