This paper presents a novel approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments, utilizing a high-dimensional sensory stream and a lower-dimensional measurement stream. The cotemporal structure of these streams provides a rich supervisory signal, enabling the training of a sensorimotor control model through supervised learning techniques. The model learns to act based on raw sensory input from a complex three-dimensional environment, without requiring extraneous supervision.

I decide to accept this paper, with two key reasons for this choice. Firstly, the approach presented in the paper demonstrates a significant improvement over state-of-the-art deep RL methods in immersive three-dimensional simulations. The results show that the model outperforms sophisticated prior formulations, particularly on challenging tasks, and generalizes across environments and goals. Secondly, the paper provides a clear and well-motivated approach to sensorimotor control, building upon existing ideas and providing a novel formulation that supports learning from raw experience and without extraneous data.

The supporting arguments for these reasons include the extensive experiments conducted in three-dimensional simulations based on the classical first-person game Doom. The results demonstrate that the presented approach outperforms state-of-the-art deep RL methods, particularly on complex tasks. Additionally, the paper provides a thorough analysis of the approach, including its advantages and limitations, and discusses potential extensions and future work.

To further improve the paper, I suggest that the authors provide more insight into the failure modes of their approach. Specifically, it would be valuable to discuss under what circumstances the model has problems generalizing to changing goals. Additionally, the authors could explore the limitations of their on-policy method, including the potential for catastrophic forgetting if the agent does not repeatedly train on goals from the distant past.

Finally, I would like to see the authors explore the application of their approach to other domains, such as Atari games, using the ROM as intrinsic variables. This would provide further evidence of the generalizability and effectiveness of their approach.
