- Proposes training sensorimotor control from cotemporal high-dimensional sensory and low-dimensional measurement streams using supervised learning without extra supervision.
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art deep reinforcement learning in complex 3D environments.
- Main contribution framed as distilling existing ideas and demonstrating their practical effectiveness.
- Recommendation: accept based on strong empirical results.
- Needs clearer, explicit statement of main technical contributions in abstract and introduction.
- Request a discussion of failure modes and circumstances where the model struggles to generalize to changing goals.
- Must address catastrophic forgetting when goals from the distant past are not repeatedly trained on.
- Suggest including experiments on other domains (e.g., Atari) to show broader applicability, possibly using ROM as intrinsic variables.
- Ask for a more detailed comparison and discussion of relationships to existing methods (e.g., universal value function approaches).
- Recommend adding more visualizations or illustrations to clarify architecture and training.
- Call for deeper analysis of ablation results, especially the role of predicting measurements at multiple temporal offsets.
- Request more details on hyperparameter tuning and model sensitivity to different hyperparameters.
- Question: how does the model handle ill-defined or rapidly changing goals?
- Question: what are plans to address catastrophic forgetting in future work?
- Question: are there plans to extend the approach to continuous action spaces or more complex environments?
