This paper presents an on-policy deep reinforcement learning (RL) method that incorporates additional auxiliary intrinsic variables.

The method can be viewed as a special case of a universal value function-based approach, and the authors correctly cite relevant references. However, the paper's main technical contribution appears to be the distillation of existing ideas to tackle 3D navigation problems. To improve clarity, the contributions of this work should be more explicitly stated in the abstract and introduction.

A more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach would be beneficial, including an analysis of its failure modes. Specifically, it would be valuable to investigate under what circumstances the model struggles to generalize to changing goals. Furthermore, there are conceptual issues to consider, such as catastrophic forgetting, which is inherent to on-policy methods. If the agent does not repeatedly train on goals from the distant past, its performance may degrade significantly.

Given that the primary contribution of this paper lies in integrating key ideas and demonstrating empirical advantages, it would be desirable to see results in other domains, such as Atari, potentially utilizing ROM as intrinsic variables. This would provide a more thorough understanding of the method's applicability and robustness.

Overall, this paper demonstrates a clear empirical advantage of using the proposed underlying formulations, and the experimental insights gained from this work may be valuable for the development of future agents.
