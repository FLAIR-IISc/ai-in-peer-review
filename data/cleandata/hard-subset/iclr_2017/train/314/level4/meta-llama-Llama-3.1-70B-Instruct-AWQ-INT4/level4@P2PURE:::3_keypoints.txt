- Proposes an on-policy method (DFP) for predicting future intrinsic measurements, evaluated exclusively in vizDoom.
- Trains model to predict sequences of triplets (health, ammunition, frags) weighted by input “goal” triplets, enabling goal-guided exploration and test-time action selection by maximizing long-term goal.
- Empirical results are strong—DFP won the 2016 vizDoom competition.
- Experimental evaluation is sound, comparing DFP to A3C, DQN, and an attempted comparison to DSR.
- DFP outperforms other methods or matches their optimal performance (e.g., A3C ceiling in scenario D1).
- Ablation study indicates the model’s added complexity provides measurable benefits.
- Paper situates itself in existing RL literature (intrinsic motivation, auxiliary tasks, forward modeling) and references prior work adequately though not exhaustively.
- Minor issues: Doom described as 3D though it’s pseudo-3D/2D; notation concerns—“P” may imply probability and “j” is reused confusingly; tables should repeat heterogeneous units (Table 1).
- Reviewer recommendation: clear accept based on experimental soundness, reproducibility, and milestone result.
