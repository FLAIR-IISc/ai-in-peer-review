This paper presents a novel approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments, utilizing a high-dimensional sensory stream and a lower-dimensional measurement stream. The authors propose a method that leverages the cotemporal structure of these streams to provide a rich supervisory signal, enabling the training of a sensorimotor control model through supervised learning techniques. The model learns to act based on raw sensory input from a complex three-dimensional environment, without requiring extraneous supervision.

I recommend accepting this paper. The approach is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their method. The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach in various scenarios, including the Visual Doom AI Competition.

The paper's contributions are significant, as it presents a new formulation for sensorimotor control that departs from traditional reinforcement learning approaches. The use of a measurement stream and a goal vector allows for more flexible and dynamic goal specification, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, comparing it to state-of-the-art deep reinforcement learning methods and demonstrating its superiority in several scenarios. The ablation study and additional experiments further support the effectiveness of the proposed method.
