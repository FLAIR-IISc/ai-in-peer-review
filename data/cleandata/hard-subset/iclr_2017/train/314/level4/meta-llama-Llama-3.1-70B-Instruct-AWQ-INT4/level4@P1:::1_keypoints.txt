- Proposes training sensorimotor control by exploiting cotemporal structure between a high-dimensional sensory stream and a low-dimensional measurement stream, using supervised learning from raw sensory input.  
- Recommends acceptance based on two main reasons.  
- Reason 1: Well-motivated approach addressing learning from raw sensory input in complex, dynamic 3D environments.  
- Reason 2: Strong empirical support, including extensive 3D experiments and a winning entry in the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Positions the work clearly within supervised learning, reinforcement learning, and sensorimotor control literature; highlights contributions: Monte Carlo estimation, low-dimensional measurements for prediction, and parametrized goals.  
- Empirical claims: learns generalizable policies that adapt to changing goals without retraining; outperforms prior work in certain scenarios.  
- Concern: paper assumes on-policy training but uses a replay buffer—this approximation should be discussed as it may affect algorithm performance.  
- Concern: algorithm uses extra metadata (which parts of sensory input to predict) not used by compared methods; limitation should be made explicit and discussed for environments lacking such measurements.  
- Suggested addition: provide more details on network architecture and training hyperparameters.  
- Suggested addition: include analysis of robustness to different types of sensory noise and perturbations.  
- Questions for authors: how will they address the dependency on additional metadata indicating what to predict?  
- Questions for authors: can they provide full neural network architecture and hyperparameter details?  
- Questions for authors: how do they plan to extend the approach to more complex settings (multiple agents, highly dynamic environments)?
