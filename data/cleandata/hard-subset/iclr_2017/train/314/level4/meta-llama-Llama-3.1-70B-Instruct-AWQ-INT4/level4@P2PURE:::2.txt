This paper presents an on-policy deep reinforcement learning (RL) method that incorporates additional auxiliary intrinsic variables. The approach is a special case of a universal value function-based method, and the authors provide accurate citations to relevant references. However, the primary claimed technical contribution of this paper lies in distilling existing ideas to address 3D navigation problems, which could be more clearly articulated in the abstract and introduction.

To strengthen the paper, it would be beneficial to include an analysis of the method's failure modes, particularly in scenarios where the model struggles to generalize to changing goals. Furthermore, there are conceptual concerns, such as the potential for catastrophic forgetting, which is inherent in on-policy methods when the agent is not repeatedly trained on goals from the distant past.

Given that the paper's main contribution is the integration of key ideas and the demonstration of empirical advantages, it would be valuable to see results from other domains, such as Atari, potentially utilizing the ROM as intrinsic variables. This would help to further establish the generalizability of the proposed approach.

Overall, the paper demonstrates a clear empirical advantage of using the proposed underlying formulations, and the experimental insights gained from this work may be valuable for the development of future agents.
