- Presents a novel deep RL algorithm achieving state-of-the-art performance in novel 3D environments from raw sensory data; winner of the Visual Doom AI competition.  
- Core idea: supervise prediction of low-dimensional measurements (e.g., ammo, health) conditioned on a goal vector and the current action.  
- Action selection: choose the action that maximizes the predicted outcome for the given goal.  
- Learning is supervised Monte Carlo regression (no TD), unlike successor feature representations that rely on TD relationships.  
- Key contributions: focus on Monte Carlo estimation, use of low-dimensional measurements, parameterized goals, and thorough empirical comparisons to prior work.  
- Builds on prior work in future-state prediction and goal-driven function approximators (reviewed in Section 2).  
- Demonstrates generalizable policies that can adapt to limited goal changes without further training.  
- Paper is well-communicated with compelling empirical results, making a significant contribution.  
- Concern: supervised Monte Carlo training assumes on-policy data, but the method uses a replay buffer—discrepancy between assumed and actual data distribution should be discussed.  
- Concern: method leverages additional metadata (which sensory parts to predict) that baselines do not; authors should acknowledge limitations and discuss failure modes when such measurements are unavailable.
