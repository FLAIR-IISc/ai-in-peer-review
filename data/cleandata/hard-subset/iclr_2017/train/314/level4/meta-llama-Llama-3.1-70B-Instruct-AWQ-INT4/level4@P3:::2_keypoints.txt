- Proposes a novel sensorimotor control method that leverages cotemporal structure between high-dimensional sensory streams and lower-dimensional measurement streams for supervision.  
- Trains a control model via supervised learning on raw sensory input from complex 3D environments without extraneous supervision.  
- Method is clearly motivated and effectively addresses challenges of sensorimotor control in immersive environments.  
- Strong empirical results showing advantages over state-of-the-art deep RL methods.  
- Approach is well-situated in literature, drawing on supervised learning, RL, and sensorimotor control prior work.  
- Authors analyze strengths and limitations, including training without fixed goals and pursuing dynamic goals at test time.  
- Demonstrates impressive performance on challenging tasks and superior generalization across environments and goals.  
- Thorough evaluation includes comparisons to SOTA deep RL, ablation studies, and analysis in the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on clarity, motivation, and empirical strength.  
- Suggests more discussion of limitations, potential failure modes, and catastrophic forgetting.  
- Requests results on additional domains (e.g., Atari) to demonstrate generality.  
- Asks for clarification on catastrophic forgetting mitigation, extensions to temporal abstraction/hierarchical skills, and detailed predictor network architecture and hyperparameters.
