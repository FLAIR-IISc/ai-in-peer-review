- Proposes an on-policy method (DFP) that predicts future intrinsic measurements to guide behavior.  
- Predicts sequences of triplets (health, ammunition, frags) rather than binary outcomes like win/loss.  
- Uses input "goal" triplet weights to bias predictions for guided exploration and test-time behavior.  
- At test time, the agent acts by maximizing the long-term weighted goal.  
- Experiments are entirely conducted in the vizDoom environment.  
- Results are strong — the method won the 2016 vizDoom competition.  
- Empirical comparisons to A3C, DQN, and DSR show DFP matches or outperforms alternatives in many scenarios.  
- Ablation study indicates the added model complexity provides tangible benefits.  
- Contributions are positioned within existing RL work on intrinsic motivation, auxiliary tasks, and forward models; references are adequate but not exhaustive.  
- Minor issues: Doom is described as 3D but is pseudo-3D; notation "P" suggests probability but denotes prediction.  
- Minor issues: equation (6) reuses "j" with different fonts causing possible confusion.  
- Minor issues: results tables should repeat heterogeneous units (e.g., Table 1).  
- Overall recommendation: clear accept — solid experiments and milestone achievement, though broader environments or stronger novelty could be raised as caveats.
