- Paper presents an empirical deep RL study using deep neural nets as function approximators.  
- Proposed algorithm improves performance in novel 3D environments from raw sensory data and generalizes better across goals and environments.  
- Algorithm was the winner of the Visual Doom AI competition.  
- Core idea: use additional low-dimensional observations (e.g., ammo, health) as supervised prediction targets conditioned on a given goal vector and current action.  
- At test time, choose the action that maximizes the predicted outcome according to the goal.  
- Learning is supervised/Monte Carlo (no TD or successor-feature temporal prediction).  
- Key contributions: Monte Carlo estimation focus, use of low-dimensional measurements, parametrized goals, and empirical comparisons to prior work.  
- Demonstrates policies that generalize and can adapt to limited goal changes without further training.  
- Paper is well-written and presents compelling empirical results, making a significant contribution.  
- Concern: supervised Monte Carlo training assumes on-policy returns but is trained from a replay buffer of prior-policy episodes; this discrepancy needs discussion.  
- Concern: method relies on extra metadata about which sensory measurements to predict; limitations and failure modes when such measurements are unavailable should be acknowledged.
