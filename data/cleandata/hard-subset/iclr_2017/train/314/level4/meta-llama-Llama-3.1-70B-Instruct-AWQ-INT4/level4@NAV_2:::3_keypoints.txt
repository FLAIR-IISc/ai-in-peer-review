- Paper introduces a novel sensorimotor control method using high-dimensional sensory streams and lower-dimensional measurement streams with cotemporal supervision.  
- Method trains a control model via supervised learning on raw sensory input from complex 3D environments without extraneous supervision.  
- Reviewer recommends accepting the paper.  
- Approach is well-motivated and clearly explained.  
- Experimental results are strong, including success in the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Contribution: a new formulation for sensorimotor control that departs from traditional reinforcement learning.  
- Measurement stream and goal vector enable flexible and dynamic goal specification.  
- Thorough evaluation shows superiority over state-of-the-art deep RL methods in several scenarios.  
- Ablation study and additional experiments support the method’s effectiveness.  
- Minor suggestion: clarify that the Doom environment is not truly 3D.  
- Minor suggestion: avoid misleading notation such as reuse of "j" in equation (6).  
- Minor suggestion: repeat units of measurement in result tables for clarity.  
- Suggested improvements: provide more details on the predictor network architecture and components.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss limitations, applicability to other environments/tasks, and add more comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Questions for authors: provide training details and hyperparameters for the predictor network.  
- Questions for authors: plans for extending the approach to more complex environments and tasks.  
- Questions for authors: consideration of applying the approach to other domains (robotics, autonomous vehicles).  
- Questions for authors: more insights into the ablation study and the importance of each component.
