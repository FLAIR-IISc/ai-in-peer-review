- Novel deep RL algorithm achieving state-of-the-art performance in complex 3D environments with raw sensory data.  
- Notable generalization across goals and environments, validated by winning the Visual Doom AI competition.  
- Core idea: predict low-dimensional measurements (e.g., ammo, health) via supervised learning conditioned on a goal vector and current action.  
- Action selection by maximizing predicted outcomes w.r.t. the goal, rather than using value/T D updates.  
- Differs from successor features by using supervised Monte Carlo regression and avoiding TD relationships between current and next predictions.  
- Paper includes a comprehensive review of prior work on future-state prediction and goal-driven function approximators.  
- Key contributions: focus on Monte Carlo estimation, use of low-dimensional measurements for prediction, parametrized goals, and thorough empirical comparisons.  
- Empirical results show policies generalize to limited goal changes without additional training; overall writing and results are strong.  
- Minor concern: supervised training assumes on-policy data but learns from a replay buffer, creating a mismatch that should be discussed.  
- Minor concern: method relies on extra metadata identifying measurable sensory components, which should be acknowledged and its limitations in metadata-scarce environments discussed.
