This paper introduces an on-policy deep reinforcement learning (RL) approach that incorporates auxiliary intrinsic variables. 

- The proposed method is a specific instantiation of a universal value function-based framework, and the authors provide accurate citations to relevant literature. A key technical contribution of this paper is the distillation of existing concepts to address 3D navigation challenges. However, the contributions could be more explicitly stated in the abstract and introduction to provide clarity.
- It would be beneficial to discuss the failure modes of this approach, including scenarios where the model struggles to generalize to changing goals. Furthermore, as an on-policy method, it is susceptible to catastrophic forgetting if the agent does not repeatedly train on goals from the distant past, highlighting a conceptual limitation.
- Given the primary contribution of this paper lies in integrating key ideas and demonstrating empirical benefits, it would be valuable to see results in other domains, such as Atari, potentially utilizing the ROM as intrinsic variables.
- Overall, the paper demonstrates the empirical advantages of the proposed formulations, and the experimental insights gained from this work may be beneficial for the development of future agents.
