- Proposes a method for sensorimotor control that leverages cotemporal structure between a high-dimensional sensory stream and a low-dimensional measurement stream to provide supervision for supervised learning.  
- Trains a model to act from raw sensory input without extraneous supervision.  
- Key contributions: learns without a fixed goal during training and can pursue dynamically changing goals at test time.  
- Extensive experiments in Doom-based 3D simulations show the approach outperforms prior methods, especially on challenging tasks, and generalizes across environments and goals.  
- Recommendation: accept, based on a well-motivated approach, solid grounding in literature, clear explanation, and comprehensive experimental evaluation.  
- Experimental comparisons: DFP outperforms DQN, A3C, and DSR in most scenarios; ablation study supports the value of added model complexity.  
- Potential weakness: novelty may be limited, though results and presentation are compelling.  
- Minor form issues: Doom is described as 3D though it is 2D with fake 3D; notation concerns — "P" in Eq.(2) (prediction vs probability) and repeated use of "j" in Eq.(6) may confuse readers.  
- Suggested additions: provide more details on network architecture and hyperparameters, deeper analysis of ablation results, and discussion of real-world applications.  
- Questions for authors: specify neural network architecture (layers/units), provide detailed ablation-effect analysis, and discuss potential real-world uses.
