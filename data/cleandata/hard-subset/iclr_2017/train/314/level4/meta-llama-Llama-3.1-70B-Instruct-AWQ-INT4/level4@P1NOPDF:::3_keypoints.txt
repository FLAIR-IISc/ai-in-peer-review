- Proposes an on-policy method that predicts future intrinsic measurements, evaluated in vizDoom.
- Model predicts sequences of triplets (health, ammo, frags) weighted by input "goal" triplets to enable exploration.
- At test time the agent acts by maximizing long-term goal via the predicted measurements.
- Results are impressive and the method won the 2016 vizDoom competition.
- Experimental evaluation is sound, comparing DFP to A3C, DQN, and DSR.
- DFP outperforms or matches other methods (e.g., A3C hits a ceiling in scenario D1).
- Ablation study shows the model’s added complexity is beneficial.
- Prior work on intrinsic motivation, auxiliary variables, and forward modeling is acknowledged, though references may not be exhaustive.
- Minor issue: Doom is described as 3D but is actually a 2D pseudo-3D environment.
- Minor issue: Using "P" in Eq.(2) is misleading since it suggests probability rather than prediction.
- Minor issue: The double use of "j" in Eq.(6) may cause confusion.
- Minor issue: Results tables should repeat units because measurements are heterogeneous (Table 1).
- Recommendation: Clear accept—paper is correct, experimentally sound, and a milestone deserving publication.
