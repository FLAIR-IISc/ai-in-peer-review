- Proposes an on-policy method (DFP) that forecasts future intrinsic metrics instead of just rewards/outcomes.
- Model predicts sequences of triplets (health, ammunition, frags) weighted by input "goal" triplets to guide exploration and decision-making.
- At test time actions are chosen to maximize long-term goals via the weighted forecasts.
- Experiments are all in the vizDoom environment.
- Results are strong: DFP won the 2016 vizDoom competition.
- Experimental evaluation is robust, comparing DFP to A3C, DQN, and an attempted comparison to DSR.
- DFP outperforms or matches baselines (e.g., matches A3C at its optimum in scenario D1).
- Ablation study supports the usefulness of the model’s added complexity.
- Situates work within established areas (intrinsic motivation, auxiliary predictions, forward models); references are adequate though bibliography may not be exhaustive.
- Minor manuscript issues: Doom described as 3D is misleading (pseudo-3D), use of "P" in eq.(2) suggests probability, dual use of "j" in eq.(6) is confusing, and result tables should repeat units (especially Table 1).
- Overall recommendation: clear accept — despite possible calls for more diverse environments or novelty questions, strong experiments, reproducibility focus, and competition win justify publication.
