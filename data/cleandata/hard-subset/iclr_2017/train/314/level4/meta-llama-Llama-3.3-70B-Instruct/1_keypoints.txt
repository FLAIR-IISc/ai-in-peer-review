- Introduces a novel deep RL algorithm that excels in large 3D state spaces using raw sensory input and wins the Visual Doom AI competition.  
- Core idea: predict supplementary low-dimensional measurements (e.g., ammo, health) conditioned on a goal vector and current action via supervised learning.  
- Action selection: choose the action that maximizes the predicted outcome for the specified goal.  
- Learning is supervised (Monte Carlo estimation) with no temporal-difference relation between current and next-state predictions, unlike successor features.  
- Builds on prior predictive-future and goal-conditioned function approximation work and emphasizes Monte Carlo training.  
- Key contributions: Monte Carlo focus, use of low-dimensional measurements, parametrized goals, and thorough empirical comparison to prior methods.  
- Demonstrates strong generalization: policies adapt to limited goal changes without additional training.  
- Empirical results are compelling and the paper is well-written.  
- Minor critique: supervised training assumes on-policy data but learning uses a replay buffer—this approximation should be discussed.  
- Limitation: relies on extra metadata/predictable measurements; effectiveness may drop in environments lacking such measurements.
