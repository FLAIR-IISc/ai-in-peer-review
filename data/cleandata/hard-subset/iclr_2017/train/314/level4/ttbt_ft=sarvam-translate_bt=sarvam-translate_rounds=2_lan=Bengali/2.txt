This paper presents an on-policy deep RL approach with additional helpful underlying variables.

- This approach is a specific example of a universal value function-based approach, and the authors have mentioned the correct references. Perhaps the biggest claimed technical contribution of this paper is the integration of many existing concepts for solving 3D navigation problems. I think the contributions in the abstract/introduction section should be more clearly stated.
- I would have liked to see the failure modes of this approach. In what situations is it problematic to generalize with the variable goals of the model? There are other conceptual problems as well - since this is an on-policy approach, catastrophic forgetting will occur if the agent does not repeatedly train on goals from the distant past.
- Since the main contribution of this paper is the integration of several key concepts and the demonstration of empirical benefits, I would have liked to see results in other domains, such as Atari (possibly using ROM as the underlying variable).
Overall, I believe this paper shows the clear empirical benefits of using the proposed underlying variables and the experimental insights of this paper, and may be valuable for future agents.
