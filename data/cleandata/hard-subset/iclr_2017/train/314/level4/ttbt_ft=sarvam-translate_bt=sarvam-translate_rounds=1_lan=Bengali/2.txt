This paper presents an on-policy deep RL method with additional helpful underlying variables.

- This method is a specific example of a universal value function-based method, and the authors have mentioned the correct references. Perhaps the biggest claimed technical contribution of this paper is the integration of many existing concepts for solving 3D navigation problems. I think the contributions in the abstract/introduction section should be more clearly stated.
- I would have liked to see the failure modes of this method. Under what circumstances is it problematic to generalize with the variable goals of the model? There are other conceptual problems as well - since this is an on-policy method, catastrophic forgetting will occur if the agent does not repeatedly train on distant past goals.
- Since the main contribution of this paper is the integration of several key concepts and the demonstration of empirical benefits, I would have liked to see results in other domains, such as Atari (perhaps using ROM as the underlying variable).
Overall, I believe this paper shows the clear empirical benefits of using the proposed underlying variables and the experimental insights of this paper, and may be valuable for future agents.
