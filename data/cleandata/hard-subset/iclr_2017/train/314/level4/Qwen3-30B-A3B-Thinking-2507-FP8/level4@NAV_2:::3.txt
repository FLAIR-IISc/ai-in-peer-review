

This paper presents an innovative approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments by leveraging the cotemporal structure of high-dimensional sensory and lower-dimensional measurement streams. The authors propose a supervised learning framework that learns from raw sensory input without extraneous supervision, enabling training without fixed goals and flexible deployment to dynamically specified goals at test time. The approach demonstrates remarkable effectiveness in complex three-dimensional environments, as evidenced by its victory in the Visual Doom AI Competition and superior performance over state-of-the-art deep reinforcement learning methods across multiple challenging scenarios.

I recommend acceptance with the following key reasons: (1) The approach demonstrates significant empirical superiority over existing methods, particularly on complex tasks, and (2) The paper provides compelling experimental evidence supporting the claims, including thorough ablation studies and robust generalization across environments.

The supporting arguments for this decision are substantial. The authors conducted extensive experiments in Doom-based environments that clearly demonstrate the advantages of their method over DQN, A3C, and DSR. The results show consistent and significant performance improvements, especially when multiple measurements are used instead of a single scalar reward. The approach's ability to generalize across different environments and adapt to new goals at test time is particularly impressive. The paper's victory in the Visual Doom AI Competition, where the model outperformed a substantially more complex submission by over 50%, provides strong real-world validation of the approach's effectiveness.

For improvement, the authors should clarify the distinction between the 2D nature of Doom (which is presented as 3D) and the claimed 3D environment in the introduction. While the paper references prior work adequately, it could better acknowledge the connection to predictive state representations and forward modeling literature. The notation in Equation (2) uses "p" for prediction, which may cause confusion with probability notation, and the use of "j" in Equation (6) with different fonts could be clarified for better readability.

To further strengthen the paper, the authors might consider:
1. Providing more explicit discussion of the limitations of the approach in truly 3D environments beyond Doom
2. Including a more detailed comparison of the computational efficiency of their method versus the baselines
3. Adding a brief explanation of why the temporal offsets (1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32) were chosen

I would like to ask the authors to clarify: How does the approach handle situations where the goal vector contains negative weights (e.g., minimizing a particular measurement)? Additionally, what is the theoretical basis for the specific normalization procedure used in Equation (6)?
