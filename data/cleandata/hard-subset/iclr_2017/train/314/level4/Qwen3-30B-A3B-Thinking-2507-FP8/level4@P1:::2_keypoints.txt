- Proposes supervised-learning framework that predicts future measurements from sensory input and a goal to solve sensorimotor control in 3D.  
- Claims training directly from raw experience without extraneous supervision and flexible goal specification at test time.  
- Strong empirical results in Doom-based 3D simulations, outperforming state-of-the-art deep RL and winning the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch by >50%.  
- Recommendation: reject because the paper fails to clearly establish novelty relative to prior work.  
- Core approach appears to be a specific instantiation of the universal value function (Schaul et al., 2015), not a fundamentally new method.  
- The claim of departing from reward-based RL is misleading; goals are intrinsic measurements, making the method similar to UVF.  
- Insufficient distinction and comparison with the universal value function framework; lack of clear articulation of advances beyond it.  
- Lacks critical analysis of limitations and failure modes, especially conditions under which the model fails to generalize to new goals.  
- The claim of “training without a fixed goal” is weakly supported—randomized-goal training yields only modest improvements.  
- Practical concerns about relying on an experience memory instead of a fully off-policy approach (data efficiency, catastrophic forgetting) are unaddressed.  
- Limited evidence of generality: experiments are confined to Doom, with no results on other domains (e.g., Atari) to support broader applicability.  
- Needs clearer explanation of why multiple measurements provide richer supervision than scalar rewards and the mechanisms behind observed benefits.  
- Requests for authors: (1) explicitly explain advances beyond UVF, (2) provide detailed analysis of goal-agnostic training limitations with examples, (3) discuss extension to continuous action spaces and associated challenges.
