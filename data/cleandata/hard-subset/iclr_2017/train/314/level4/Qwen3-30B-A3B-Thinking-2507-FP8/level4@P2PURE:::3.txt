

The paper introduces an on-policy approach for predicting future intrinsic measurements. All experiments were conducted in the vizDoom environment, where the authors trained a model to predict (a sequence of) triplets representing (health, ammunition, frags), weighted by (a sequence of) input "goal" triplets. Adjusting the weights of these goal triplets guides exploration. At test time, agents act by maximizing long-term goal achievement.  

The results are compelling, as this model secured victory in the 2016 vizDoom competition. The experimental evaluation is robust:  
- Comparisons with A3C, DQN, and DSR (Kulkarni et al., 2016) demonstrate that DFP outperforms these methods (or matches their ceiling/optimum, as with A3C in scenario D1).  
- An ablation study confirms that all proposed model components contribute significantly to performance.  

The work engages with well-established domains in reinforcement learning, including intrinsic motivation (Singh et al., 2004), auxiliary variables, and forward modeling. The December 4th revision adequately cites prior work, though the coverage is not exhaustive.  

Minor editorial suggestions:  
- Doom is described as a 3D environment; it is a 2D environment (with height non-discriminative/actionable) rendered in (fake) 3D.  
- The use of "P" in Equation (2) and thereafter is potentially misleading, as it denotes prediction rather than probability.  
- Equation (6) uses "j" twice with different fonts, which could cause confusion.  
- Table 1 should explicitly state units for all measurements, given their heterogeneity.  

This paper merits a clear acceptance. While experiments are confined to Doom and novelty may be incremental, the work is experimentally rigorous (sound methodology, reproducible appendix), achieves a notable milestone (vizDoom competition win), and delivers a "correct" contribution without unnecessary complexity.
