

This paper introduces an on-policy deep reinforcement learning method incorporating auxiliary intrinsic variables.  
- While the method constitutes a special case of universal value function approaches (with appropriate citations), the primary technical contribution appears to be the distillation of multiple existing techniques for 3D navigation. This contribution should be more clearly articulated in the abstract and introduction.  
- The paper lacks analysis of failure modes, particularly regarding generalization to novel goals. Additionally, as an on-policy method, it risks catastrophic forgetting if the agent fails to repeatedly train on goals from the distant past.  
- Given the paper's focus on integrating key ideas to demonstrate empirical advantages, results on additional domains (e.g., Atari, where ROMs could serve as intrinsic variables) would strengthen the claims.  
Overall, the paper demonstrates clear empirical advantages of the proposed formulation, and its experimental insights could inform future agent design.
