- Proposes a novel deep RL algorithm that attains strong performance in complex 3D environments from raw sensory input.  
- Achieves first place in the Visual Doom AI competition, demonstrating empirical strength.  
- Core innovation: supervise predictions of low-dimensional game-engine metrics (e.g., health, ammo) rather than value functions.  
- Predictions are conditioned on an explicit goal vector and the current action; actions are chosen to maximize predicted outcomes relative to the goal.  
- Uses supervised learning / Monte Carlo estimation instead of temporal-difference (TD) updates, differing from successor feature approaches.  
- Contextualizes work within goal-conditioned function approximation and future-state prediction literature.  
- Key contributions: Monte Carlo estimation, use of low-dimensional metrics for supervision, goal-parameterized predictions, and rigorous empirical baselines.  
- Demonstrates robust zero-shot generalization to novel goals and environments without additional training.  
- Paper is well-written, methodology is sound, and results are convincingly presented.  
- Minor concern: supervised training assumes on-policy trajectories but learns from off-policy replay buffer, introducing potential approximation error.  
- Minor concern: reliance on game-engine metrics (health, ammo) is not present in baselines and may limit applicability where such structured metrics are unavailable.
