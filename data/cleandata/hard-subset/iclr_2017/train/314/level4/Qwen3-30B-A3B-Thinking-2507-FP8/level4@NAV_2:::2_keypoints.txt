- Proposes a novel supervised sensorimotor control method leveraging cotemporal structure to predict future measurements from current sensory input and goals without extraneous supervision.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance in complex 3D simulations and won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch track, outperforming prior methods by >50%.
- Recommends acceptance.
- Empirical advantage over existing deep RL methods, especially for tasks requiring dynamic goal pursuit.
- Argues rich measurement streams provide better supervisory signals than traditional scalar rewards.
- Experiments are thorough across four progressively complex scenarios, showing consistent outperformance of baselines.
- Shows impressive generalization: a model trained in one environment performs well in unseen variants.
- The approach is simple yet achieves superior results, constituting a significant contribution.
- Suggest clarifying the technical novelty versus universal value function approaches in the abstract/introduction.
- Suggest adding analysis of failure modes, especially when generalizing to goals substantially different from training.
- Suggest addressing potential on-policy training limitation regarding catastrophic forgetting over diverse/extended training.
- Asks under what specific conditions the model struggles to generalize to new goals and how to address them.
- Asks how the method mitigates catastrophic forgetting given on-policy training with many goals.
- Asks whether experiments could be extended to other domains (e.g., Atari) to demonstrate broader applicability beyond Doom.
