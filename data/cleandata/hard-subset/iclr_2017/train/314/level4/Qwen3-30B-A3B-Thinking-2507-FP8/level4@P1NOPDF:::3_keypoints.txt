- Introduces an on-policy method (DFP) that predicts intrinsic state sequences (health, ammunition, frags) rather than binary outcomes or raw scores.  
- Predictions are weighted by user-specified goal triplets provided as input, allowing flexible objective specification.  
- Dynamically adjusting goal weights guides exploration during training.  
- At test time, agents act to maximize long-term achievement of the specified goals.  
- Method won the 2016 vizDoom competition.  
- Empirically outperforms or matches established baselines (A3C, DQN) and a recent comparable approach (DSR), notably where others plateau (e.g., A3C in scenario D1).  
- Ablation study shows each proposed component meaningfully contributes to performance.  
- Situates work within prior RL themes (intrinsic motivation, auxiliary variables, forward modeling) with adequate citations in the revision.  
- Reproducibility is strong, supported by detailed appendix and thorough experimental validation.  
- Generalization to additional environments would strengthen broader applicability.  
- Minor technical/clarification issues: Doom described as "3D" (should note pseudo-3D/2D with height non-actionable).  
- Minor notation/format issues: Equation (2) uses "P" (may be confused with probability), Equation (6) has inconsistent font for "j", and Table 1 should specify units for all metrics.
