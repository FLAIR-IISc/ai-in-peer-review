- Strong advancement in deep RL for complex 3D environments, validated by a Visual Doom AI competition win.  
- Algorithm uses low-dimensional game-engine metadata as supervised prediction targets to learn goal-conditioned policies from raw sensory inputs.  
- Policies generalize across diverse goals/environments and adapt to novel goal configurations without retraining.  
- Core innovation: condition supervised prediction on a parametrized goal vector and the current action to predict outcomes.  
- Actions are selected by maximizing the model’s predicted outcome toward the specified goal.  
- Learning is fully supervised using Monte Carlo trajectory estimates, avoiding TD bootstrapping and TD error propagation.  
- Clear contrast with prior goal-driven RL (e.g., successor features), emphasizing Monte Carlo estimation, engineered measurements, and parametrized goals.  
- Empirical evaluation is robust and well-designed, showing superior sample efficiency and final performance over baselines.  
- Writing and experimental presentation are clear; results are compelling and impactful for the RL community.  
- Minor concern: replay buffer contains off-policy episodes, creating a mismatch with the on-policy supervised objective—needs discussion of convergence/performance implications.  
- Minor concern: reliance on engineered low-dimensional metadata limits applicability to real-world settings without internal state access; authors should acknowledge and suggest mitigations.  
- Overall: well-executed, impactful paper with strong empirical validation; recommendation to accept.
