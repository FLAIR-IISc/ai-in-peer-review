- Introduces an on-policy method (DFP) that predicts future intrinsic measurements to guide behavior.  
- Trained in vizDoom to predict sequences of triplets (health, ammunition, frags) weighted by goal triplets; adjusting weights guides exploration and agents act to maximize long-term goal achievement.  
- Strong empirical result: won the 2016 vizDoom competition.  
- Robust evaluation: compares to A3C, DQN, and DSR, with DFP outperforming or matching baselines (A3C matches ceiling in D1).  
- Ablation study shows all proposed model components significantly contribute to performance.  
- Places work in RL contexts of intrinsic motivation, auxiliary variables, and forward modeling; Dec 4 revision adds adequate citations though coverage isn’t exhaustive.  
- Minor editorial: Doom is described as 3D but is effectively 2D rendered in fake 3D.  
- Notation issues: "P" in Eq.(2) misleading (denotes prediction, not probability); Eq.(6) uses 'j' twice with different fonts.  
- Table 1 should explicitly state units for heterogeneous measurements.  
- Recommendation: clear acceptance—limited to Doom and somewhat incremental, but experimentally rigorous, reproducible, and achieves a noteworthy milestone.
