

The paper introduces an on-policy approach for predicting future intrinsic states, specifically targeting sequences of (health, ammunition, frags) triplets. The model leverages user-specified goal triplets (weighted by the user) to guide exploration, with test-time agents acting to maximize long-term goal alignment. All experiments are conducted in the vizDoom environment (a 2D environment with pseudo-3D rendering, not a true 3D space as described), where the method achieved first place in the 2016 vizDoom AI Competition. The experimental evaluation is robust, featuring comparisons against A3C, DQN, and DSR (Kulkarni et al., 2016). DFP consistently outperforms these baselines across vizDoom scenarios (e.g., surpassing A3C in D1, where A3C plateaus), and an ablation study confirms that all proposed components contribute meaningfully to performance.  

The work situates itself within well-established RL domains: intrinsic motivation (Singh et al., 2004), auxiliary learning, and forward modeling. The paper adequately references key prior work, though completeness could be slightly improved.  

**Minor concerns:**  
1.  **Environment description:** Doom is inaccurately labeled as "3D" (it is a 2D environment with 3D-like presentation; height is non-discriminative and non-actionable).  
2.  **Notation:** The symbol "P" in Equation (2) (and later) is misleading, as it denotes prediction (not probability), conflicting with standard usage.  
3.  **Equation (6):** The double use of "j" (with differing fonts) risks confusion.  
4.  **Tables:** Units should be explicitly repeated in Table 1 (e.g., "frags" vs. "ammo count") due to heterogeneous metrics.  

**Overall assessment:** This is a strong, experimentally rigorous contribution. The focus on vizDoom is justified by the competition’s prominence and the method’s clear victory. The paper delivers a technically sound, reproducible solution to exploration via intrinsic prediction, validated through comprehensive comparisons and ablation. While extending experiments to additional environments would be valuable, the current work’s depth, clarity, and real-world validation (vizDoom win) outweigh this limitation. The novelty lies in the effective integration of weighted goal-driven prediction for exploration, not in isolated components.  

The paper meets ICLR’s standards for technical quality, clarity, and impact. It is a clear accept.
