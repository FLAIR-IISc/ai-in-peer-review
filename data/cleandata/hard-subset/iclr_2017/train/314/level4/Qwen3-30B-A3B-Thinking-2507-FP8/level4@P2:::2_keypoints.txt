- Introduces a supervised learning framework that predicts future measurements from current sensory input, measurements, and goal specifications for sensorimotor control in 3D environments.  
- Enables training without fixed goals and effective pursuit of dynamically changing goals at test time.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance, winning the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch and outperforming state-of-the-art deep RL, especially on complex tasks.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on a well-motivated approach and robust, scientifically rigorous experimental results.  
- Argues for the value of vectorial measurements over scalar rewards and situates the work relative to universal value function approximators.  
- Experimental design is thorough: multiple complexity levels, generalization tests across environments, and ablation studies showing benefits of vectorial measurements and multi-temporal prediction.  
- Claims a principled conceptual shift enabling learning from raw experience without reward engineering, not merely an incremental improvement.  
- Suggests clarifying the relationship to prior UVFA work (Schaul et al., 2015) and explicitly stating how the formulation differs and builds on it.  
- Recommends the abstract/introduction more precisely articulate the specific technical contribution and its practical distillation for 3D navigation.  
- Requests discussion of limitations, including circumstances that hinder goal generalization and strategies to mitigate catastrophic forgetting from on-policy training.  
- Notes experiments are limited to Doom and recommends testing other domains (e.g., Atari) to strengthen generalizability claims.  
- Asks for more detailed analysis of training dynamics and learning curves, particularly for complex Battle scenarios.  
- Requests clarification on the distinction between measurement stream and sensory input and criteria for selecting measurements.  
- Concludes the paper is a strong contribution that advances sensorimotor learning using supervised techniques on raw sensory input.
