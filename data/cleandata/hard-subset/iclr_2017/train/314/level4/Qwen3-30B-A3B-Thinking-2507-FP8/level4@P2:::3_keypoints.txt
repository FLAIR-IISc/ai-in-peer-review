- Proposes a supervised-learning formulation for sensorimotor control in immersive 3D environments as an alternative to RL.
- Uses a high-dimensional sensory stream plus a low-dimensional measurement stream to provide dense, temporally rich supervision.
- Model predicts future measurements from current sensory input, measurements, and a goal vector, then selects actions to optimize predicted outcomes.
- Enables training without fixed goals and flexible goal specification at test time.
- Evaluated in the Visual Doom AI Competition and won the Full Deathmatch track, achieving state-of-the-art performance.
- Strong experimental validation: outperforms DQN, A3C, and DSR across four progressively harder Doom scenarios.
- Particularly large performance gains on complex tasks (up to 6x in Battle scenarios D3/D4).
- Ablation studies indicate vectorial measurements and multi-step prediction are major contributors to success.
- Theoretically connects to prior supervised control work and shows RL as a special case where reward is a measurement.
- Overall writing is clear and the experimental methodology is rigorous.
- Minor clarity issues: Doom described as “3D” though technically pseudo-3D.
- Notational concerns: use of "p" in Eq.(2) is misleading (prediction vs probability) and double use of "j" in Eq.(6) could confuse readers.
- Results tables should state units for heterogeneous measurements (e.g., Table 1).
- Suggest adding discussion on scaling to more complex or continuous action spaces.
- Recommend including computational-efficiency analysis, especially training-speed comparisons to baselines.
- Ask whether the measurement stream could be automatically derived from sensory input rather than predefined.
- Specific question: did training rely on privileged game-engine internals available in the competition, and would that generalize to real-world settings?
- Specific question: in goal-agnostic training, how does the model learn to prefer desirable vs undesirable measurements without explicit reward signals?
- Overall recommendation: accept — strong contribution with compelling empirical and practical validation, broadly applicable despite being demonstrated in Doom only.
