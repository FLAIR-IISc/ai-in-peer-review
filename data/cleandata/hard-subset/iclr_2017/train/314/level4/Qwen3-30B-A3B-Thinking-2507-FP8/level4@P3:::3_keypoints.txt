- Novel approach: formulate sensorimotor control as supervised prediction of future vectorial measurements (health, ammo, frags) from sensory input and a goal specification instead of reward-based RL.  
- Leverages cotemporal structure to provide richer, temporally dense supervision compared to sparse scalar rewards.  
- Demonstrated state-of-the-art performance and won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch track.  
- Empirical results: consistent superiority over DQN, A3C, and DSR across four Doom scenarios.  
- Quantitative highlights: DFP outperforms A3C by factors of ~6 and ~2.5 in Battle and Battle 2 scenarios respectively.  
- Ablation studies show both vectorial measurements and multi-temporal prediction are critical to the method’s success.  
- Experimental validation is thorough and scientifically rigorous, with progressively challenging scenarios.  
- Well-situated in related work with clear explanation of advantages over prior supervised, RL, and prediction-based methods.  
- Decision: accept — significant contribution with a simple, effective method that advances reinforcement learning.
