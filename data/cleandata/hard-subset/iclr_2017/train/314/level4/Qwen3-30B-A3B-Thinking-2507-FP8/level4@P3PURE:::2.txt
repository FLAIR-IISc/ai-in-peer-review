

This paper introduces an on-policy deep reinforcement learning method incorporating auxiliary intrinsic variables. While the approach is correctly positioned as a special case of universal value function-based methods with appropriate citations, the most significant claimed technical contribution—synthesizing existing ideas to address 3D navigation challenges—requires clearer articulation in the abstract and introduction.  

The review would benefit from a detailed analysis of failure modes: under what conditions does the model fail to generalize to novel goals? Additionally, as an on-policy method, the approach faces inherent limitations due to catastrophic forgetting if the agent does not repeatedly sample goals from the distant past during training.  

Given that the primary contribution centers on integrating key concepts and demonstrating empirical advantages, results on broader domains such as Atari (e.g., utilizing ROMs as intrinsic variables) would strengthen the evaluation.  

Overall, the paper establishes clear empirical advantages for the proposed formulation, and its experimental insights could inform future agent design. However, the contributions and limitations require more precise exposition.
