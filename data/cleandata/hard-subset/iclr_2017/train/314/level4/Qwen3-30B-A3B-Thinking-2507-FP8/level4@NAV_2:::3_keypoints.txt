- Presents a novel supervised learning framework that leverages cotemporal structure between high-dimensional sensory streams and lower-dimensional measurements for sensorimotor control.  
- Trains from raw sensory input without extraneous supervision, allowing learning without fixed goals and flexible goal specification at test time.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical effectiveness in complex 3D-like environments, including victory in the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Shows superior performance over state-of-the-art deep RL methods (DQN, A3C, DSR) across multiple challenging scenarios.  
- Recommendation: accept, based on significant empirical superiority and compelling experimental evidence.  
- Provides thorough experimental validation, including extensive Doom-based experiments, ablation studies, and robust generalization across environments.  
- Performance gains are especially pronounced when using multiple measurements instead of a single scalar reward.  
- Real-world validation: outperformed a much more complex competition submission by over 50%.  
- Suggest clarifying the paper’s claim about 3D environments given Doom’s essentially 2D nature as presented.  
- Recommend better acknowledging connections to predictive state representations and forward-modeling literature.  
- Notation issues: “p” in Equation (2) may be confused with probability notation; font/usage of “j” in Equation (6) should be clarified.  
- Suggest discussing limitations in truly 3D environments beyond Doom.  
- Recommend reporting computational efficiency comparisons versus baseline methods.  
- Ask authors to justify the choice of temporal offsets (1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32).  
- Request clarification on handling goal vectors with negative weights (e.g., measurement minimization).  
- Ask for theoretical justification of the normalization procedure used in Equation (6).
