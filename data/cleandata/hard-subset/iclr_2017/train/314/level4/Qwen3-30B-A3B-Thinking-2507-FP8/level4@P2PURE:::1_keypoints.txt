- Introduces a novel Deep RL algorithm that supervises prediction of low-dimensional measurements (e.g., ammo/health) conditioned on a goal vector and the current action.  
- Actions are chosen by selecting the action that maximizes the predicted outcome relative to the specified goal.  
- Uses supervised learning without temporal-difference (TD) relationships, differing from successor features and emphasizing Monte Carlo estimation over TD learning.  
- Main contributions: Monte Carlo focus, use of low-dimensional measurements as prediction targets, parameterized goals, and rigorous empirical comparisons to prior work.  
- Demonstrates superior performance in novel 3D environments from raw sensory data and won the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Shows policies generalize to limited goal modifications without further training.  
- Paper is well-written with compelling empirical results and high relevance to the community.  
- Minor concern: Monte Carlo regression assumes on-policy rollouts while training uses a replay buffer of past policies, creating an on-policy/off-policy mismatch that should be addressed.  
- Minor concern: Method relies on provided metadata specifying which sensory components are predictive targets; the paper should acknowledge this dependency and discuss limits when such measurements are unavailable.
