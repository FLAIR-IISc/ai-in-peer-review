- Introduces an on-policy method that predicts future intrinsic measurement triplets (health, ammo, frags) weighted by goal triplets to guide exploration and acts by maximizing long-term goal-directed reward at test time.  
- All experiments are conducted in vizDoom and the method won the 2016 vizDoom competition.  
- Empirical comparison shows DFP consistently outperforms A3C, DQN, and DSR (with parity to A3C at performance ceilings in scenario D1).  
- Ablation study demonstrates that each model component contributes meaningfully to performance.  
- Positions the work within intrinsic motivation, auxiliary variables, and forward modeling; cites prior work but literature review is not exhaustive.  
- Minor technical/presentation issues: Doom characterized as “3D” though effectively 2D; symbol “P” in Eq.(2) misleading (used for prediction, not probability); Eq.(6) uses “j” in two font styles; result tables lack units for heterogeneous measurements.  
- Recommendation: clear accept—limited to Doom and incremental novelty, but rigorously executed, experimentally sound, reproducible (appendix), and a milestone contribution.
