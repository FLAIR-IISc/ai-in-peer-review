- Introduces an on-policy deep RL method that uses auxiliary intrinsic variables.  
- Method is correctly positioned as a special case of universal value function (UVF) approaches with appropriate citations.  
- The main technical contribution—synthesizing existing ideas to address 3D navigation—needs clearer articulation in the abstract and introduction.  
- Paper lacks a detailed analysis of failure modes, specifically when the model fails to generalize to novel goals.  
- As an on-policy method, it faces inherent limitations like catastrophic forgetting if past goals are not repeatedly sampled.  
- Evaluation is narrow; including broader domains (e.g., Atari using ROMs as intrinsic variables) would strengthen empirical claims.  
- Empirical results demonstrate clear advantages and provide useful experimental insights for agent design.  
- Contributions and limitations require more precise and explicit exposition.
