

The paper introduces an on-policy approach for predicting future intrinsic measurements, specifically trained to forecast sequences of triplets representing health, ammunition, and frags (score), weighted by input sequences of "goal" triplets. This weighting mechanism guides exploration, and at test time, agents act by maximizing the long-term goal-directed reward. All experiments are conducted in the vizDoom environment. The results are compelling, as the method secured victory in the 2016 vizDoom competition. The experimental section is rigorous and well-justified:  
- The paper compares its method (DFP) against A3C, DQN, and DSR (Kulkarni et al., 2016), demonstrating consistent outperformance (or equivalence at performance ceilings, as with A3C in scenario D1).  
- An ablation study confirms that all components of the proposed model contribute meaningfully to performance.  

The work situates itself within established domains of reinforcement learning, including intrinsic motivation (Singh et al., 2004), auxiliary variables, and forward modeling. The current revision sufficiently cites prior work, though the literature review is not exhaustive.  

Minor technical and presentation notes:  
- Doom is described as a "3D environment," but it is a 2D environment (with height as non-discriminative and non-actionable) rendered in (fake) 3D.  
- The use of "P" in Equation (2) and subsequent derivations is potentially misleading, as it denotes prediction rather than probability (the conventional meaning of "P").  
- Equation (6) uses "j" in two different font styles, which may cause confusion.  
- Results tables (e.g., Table 1) should specify units for all measurements, as the reported values are heterogeneous.  

This paper is a clear accept. While experiments are limited to Doom and the novelty may be incremental, the work is rigorously executed, experimentally sound, and represents a milestone achievement (vizDoom competition winner). The appendix provides sufficient detail for reproducibility, and the contribution aligns with the conference’s emphasis on robust, impactful research.
