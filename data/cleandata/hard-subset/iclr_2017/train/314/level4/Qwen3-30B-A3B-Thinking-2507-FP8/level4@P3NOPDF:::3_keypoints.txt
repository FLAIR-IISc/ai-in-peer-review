- Introduces an on-policy method (DFP) for predicting intrinsic states in RL, applied to vizDoom.  
- Core innovation: predict sequences of triplets (health, ammo, frags) weighted by user-specified goal triplets.  
- Weighting mechanism guides exploration, enabling agents to optimize long-term goal achievement at inference time.  
- Practical significance: approach won the 2016 vizDoom competition.  
- Experimental evaluation is robust, comparing DFP to strong baselines (A3C, DQN, DSR).  
- DFP consistently outperforms or matches baselines across Doom scenarios, including cases where baselines plateau.  
- Ablation study confirms each architectural component is necessary and contributes to performance gains.  
- Experimental design is thorough, with detailed results and supplementary material supporting reproducibility.  
- Positions work within literature on intrinsic motivation, auxiliary prediction, and forward modeling; cites key prior work but could expand the survey.  
- Minor issue: describes Doom as "3D" though it is pseudo-3D (2D with 3D rendering); clarify this.  
- Notation issue: "P" in Eq.(2) denotes prediction weight, not probability—define explicitly.  
- Eq.(6) notation: reuse of "j" in different fonts is confusing—standardize index notation.  
- Table presentation: consistently repeat units for heterogeneous metrics (frags, health) in Table 1.  
- Overall assessment: methodology is sound, experiments compelling, real-world validation strong; recommends strong acceptance as a milestone contribution.
