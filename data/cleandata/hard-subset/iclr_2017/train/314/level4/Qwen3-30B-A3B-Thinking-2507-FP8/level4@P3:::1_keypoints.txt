- Proposes supervised learning for sensorimotor control by predicting future measurement vectors (e.g., health, ammo) instead of using sparse scalar rewards.
- Shows the method outperforms state-of-the-art deep RL (DQN, A3C, DSR) on challenging 3D Doom tasks.
- Model won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch, beating the next-best by >50% in unseen environments.
- Enables goal-agnostic training and runtime specification of goals, demonstrating generalization to new goals.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: four progressively hard scenarios, multiple independent runs, and environment/goal generalization tests.
- Reviewer recommends clarifying earlier the distinction and motivation for treating certain signals as measurements versus sensory inputs.
- Reviewer asks the authors to more explicitly discuss limitations when measurement vectors are not naturally available.
- Reviewer flags a training-regime concern: replay buffer/off-policy sampling vs. on-policy assumption of the supervised approach.
- Question: Why predict designated measurements (health, ammo) instead of the full sensory stream, and how to adapt if the environment lacks such measurements?
- Question: Is improved performance on D4-tx due to model architecture or increased training-environment diversity?
- Question: How does the method perform with more realistic, non-uniform goal distributions instead of the uniform goal sampling used in training?
