

The paper introduces an on-policy framework for predicting future intrinsic states. All experiments are conducted in the vizDoom environment, where the model forecasts (a sequence of) triplets (health, ammunition, frags), weighted by (a sequence of) input goal triplets provided by the user. Adjusting the weights of these goal triplets enables guided exploration. At inference time, agents act by optimizing long-term goal achievement.  

The results are compelling, as this approach secured victory in the 2016 vizDoom competition. The experimental evaluation appears robust:  
- Comparative analysis demonstrates DFP's superiority over A3C, DQN, and DSR (Kulkarni et al., 2016), with DFP matching or exceeding baselines (e.g., A3C in scenario D1 when reaching performance ceilings).  
- An ablation study validates that each component of the proposed model contributes meaningfully to performance.  

The work addresses well-established domains in reinforcement learning (intrinsic motivation, auxiliary variables, forward modeling). The current revision (December 4th) sufficiently cites relevant prior literature, though completeness could be enhanced.  

Minor formatting observations:  
- Doom is mischaracterized as a 3D environment; it is fundamentally 2D (height is non-discriminative and non-actionable, rendered in pseudo-3D).  
- The notation "P" in Equation (2) (and subsequent uses) is potentially confusing, as it denotes prediction rather than probability.  
- The dual use of "j" (with distinct font styles) in Equation (6) risks ambiguity.  
- Table 1 and other results tables should explicitly state measurement units for clarity, given heterogeneous metrics.  

This paper merits strong acceptance. While broader environmental validation or incremental novelty could be noted, the work is experimentally rigorous (Doom-focused, reproducible via appendix), milestone-driven (vizDoom champion), and free of substantive flaws.
