- Novel formulation: cast sensorimotor control as supervised prediction of future low-dimensional measurements conditioned on current observations, actions, and goal vectors, removing need for scalar rewards.  
- Empirical strength: achieves state-of-the-art in complex 3D simulations and outperforms prior deep RL methods by large margins (including 50%+ win in Visual Doom AI Competition).  
- Generalization: method generalizes across environments and supports dynamically specified goals without retraining.  
- Motivation and positioning: paper provides a thorough analysis of RL limitations (sparse rewards, fixed goals) and clearly distinguishes from imitation learning, TD methods, and prior goal-conditioned work.  
- Core technical contribution: using Monte Carlo estimation to directly predict future measurements from raw sensory input addresses the stated learning problem.  
- Results validity: experimental results (Tables/metrics) convincingly support claims—better than DQN, A3C, DSR across scenarios, 6x advantage in Battle, plus Tables 2–3 for generalization and goal-agnostic training.  
- Ablation: study (Table 4) reportedly shows necessity of vectorial measurements and multi-temporal prediction.  
- Presentation: paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Clarification needed #1: explicitly state that measurement streams (e.g., health, ammo) are pre-defined environment interfaces and discuss dependence on domain-specific structured feedback.  
- Clarification needed #2: address the on-policy assumption vs. experience-replay sampling from prior policies and implications for convergence guarantees.  
- Additional evaluation requests: (a) compare performance of model trained with randomized goals to one trained with goal vectors optimized for the test goal; (b) report exact performance when predicting only frag count versus all three measurements and compare to scalar-reward baselines (DQN/A3C).
