

This paper introduces an on-policy deep reinforcement learning method incorporating auxiliary intrinsic variables. While the approach builds upon universal value function frameworks (properly citing foundational work), the primary technical contribution—synthesizing established techniques for 3D navigation—requires clearer articulation in the abstract and introduction. The current presentation risks understating how this integration advances the field beyond prior work.

The analysis lacks critical examination of failure modes. Specifically, the paper does not address how the method generalizes to novel goals outside its training distribution. Given the on-policy nature of the algorithm, catastrophic forgetting is an inherent risk when the agent encounters goals from earlier training phases it no longer revisits frequently. A discussion of this limitation and potential mitigation strategies would significantly strengthen the work.

The empirical validation is limited to 3D navigation tasks. To better substantiate the broader claim of the method’s utility, results on additional domains—such as Atari environments—would be valuable. Leveraging ROMs as intrinsic goal representations (e.g., treating specific game states as goals) could demonstrate the framework’s versatility and provide a more compelling case for its general applicability.

Despite these gaps, the paper demonstrates consistent empirical gains over baselines in its primary domain, and the experimental insights regarding the role of auxiliary variables are noteworthy. The method effectively validates the utility of its core formulation for navigation tasks. However, the contribution’s significance would be substantially enhanced by: (1) a sharper statement of novelty in the introduction, (2) analysis of generalization limits and catastrophic forgetting risks, and (3) expansion to at least one additional benchmark domain like Atari. These additions would transform the work from a competent integration of existing ideas into a more impactful contribution to the field.
