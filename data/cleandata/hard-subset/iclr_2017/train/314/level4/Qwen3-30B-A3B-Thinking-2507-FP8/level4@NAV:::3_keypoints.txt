- Introduces an on-policy framework (DFP) that predicts future intrinsic-state triplets (health, ammunition, frags) weighted by user-provided goal triplets to enable guided exploration.  
- Agents act by optimizing long-term goal achievement at inference using those predicted, weighted futures.  
- Empirical results are strong: approach won the 2016 vizDoom competition.  
- Comparative evaluation shows DFP matches or outperforms A3C, DQN, and DSR (e.g., matches/exceeds A3C in scenario D1).  
- Ablation study indicates each model component meaningfully contributes to performance.  
- Paper engages established topics (intrinsic motivation, auxiliary variables, forward modeling) and the revision cites relevant prior work, though citation completeness could improve.  
- Minor issues: Doom is mischaracterized as 3D; notation "P" in Eq. (2) is confusing (prediction vs probability); dual use of "j" with different fonts is ambiguous; tables should report measurement units.  
- Recommendation: strong acceptance—experimentally rigorous, reproducible appendix, milestone result—though broader environment validation and novelty incrementalities could be noted.
