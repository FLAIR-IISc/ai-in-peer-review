

This paper introduces an on-policy deep reinforcement learning method incorporating auxiliary intrinsic variables.  
- The approach constitutes a specific instance of a universal value function framework, with appropriate reference citations. While the primary technical claim centers on distilling established techniques for 3D navigation, the contributions require clearer articulation in the abstract and introduction.  
- The analysis would benefit from examining failure modes, particularly the model's generalization limitations when faced with novel goal distributions. Additionally, as an on-policy method, the approach risks catastrophic forgetting due to insufficient retraining on historically encountered goals.  
- Given the paper's core contribution of integrating key ideas to demonstrate empirical efficacy, results on supplementary domains (e.g., Atari using ROM as intrinsic variables) would strengthen the evaluation.  
Overall, the work establishes clear empirical advantages for the proposed formulation, and its experimental insights hold significant potential for advancing future agent design.
