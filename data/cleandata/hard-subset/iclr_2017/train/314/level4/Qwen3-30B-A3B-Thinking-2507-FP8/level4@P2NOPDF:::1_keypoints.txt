- Introduces a novel deep RL algorithm that achieves state-of-the-art performance in 3D visual environments and wins the Visual Doom AI competition.  
- Leverages low-dimensional, game-provided observations (e.g., health, ammo) as supervised prediction targets.  
- Conditions predictions on an externally defined goal vector and the current action.  
- Selects actions by maximizing predicted outcomes relative to the specified goal.  
- Contrasts with successor features by using supervised learning (Monte Carlo) instead of TD updates and avoiding state-to-state predictions.  
- Key contributions: shift from TD to Monte Carlo estimation; strategic use of low-dimensional measurements; parametrized goal conditioning; thorough empirical validation.  
- Demonstrates strong generalization to novel goals and environmental perturbations without additional training.  
- Paper is clearly written with compelling empirical results and an adequate literature review.  
- Minor concern: supervised training assumes on-policy Monte Carlo returns but uses a replay buffer (off-policy), introducing approximation errors and potential convergence issues that should be discussed.  
- Minor concern: reliance on game-provided metadata is a critical dependency absent in many real-world settings; authors should clarify if features can be inferred from raw sensory input or if this is a fundamental limitation.  
- Overall: strong, impactful contribution with minor, addressable issues and high relevance to goal-conditioned RL.
