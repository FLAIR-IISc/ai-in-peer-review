- Introduces an on-policy deep RL method using auxiliary intrinsic state variables.  
- Approach is a specific instantiation of the universal value function (UVF) framework and is already appropriately cited.  
- Primary technical contribution is under-specified in the abstract and introduction.  
- Paper mainly consolidates established techniques for 3D navigation; this integration should be explicitly framed as the core contribution.  
- Missing failure mode analysis—no discussion of scenarios where the agent fails to generalize to novel goals or shifted goal distributions.  
- On-policy design incurs inherent limitations (e.g., catastrophic forgetting) when goals are not revisited in dynamic environments.  
- Lack of addressing these limitations weakens the claim of practical viability.  
- Empirical evaluation is narrow; broader domains are needed to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Suggests evaluating on Atari (e.g., using ROMs as intrinsic rewards) to contextualize against established baselines.  
- Empirical results do show advantages—auxiliary intrinsic variables effectively guide exploration.  
- Overall useful foundation, but requires clearer articulation of novelty and more rigorous evaluation of limitations.
