

The paper introduces an on-policy approach for predicting intrinsic state sequences, specifically targeting health, ammunition, and frags in the vizDoom environment. Instead of predicting binary outcomes like win/loss or raw scores, the authors train a model to forecast sequences of these state triplets, weighted by user-specified goal triplets provided as input. Adjusting these goal weights dynamically guides exploration, and at test time, agents act to maximize long-term goal achievement. The method achieved the 2016 vizDoom competition victory, with results demonstrating strong performance against established baselines (A3C, DQN) and a recent comparable approach (DSR by Kulkarni et al., 2016). DFP consistently outperforms or matches these methods, particularly where others plateau (e.g., A3C in scenario D1). An ablation study further validates that all proposed components contribute meaningfully to the model’s effectiveness.

The work situates itself within well-established RL domains—predicting intrinsic motivation (Singh et al., 2004), auxiliary variables, and forward modeling—with adequate (though not exhaustive) citation of prior work in the December 4 revision.  

Minor technical points for clarification:  
- Doom is described as "3D," but it is a 2D environment (with height as non-discriminative/actionable) rendered in pseudo-3D.  
- Equation (2) uses "P" for "prediction," which may confuse readers expecting probability notation.  
- Equation (6) uses two different fonts for "j," risking ambiguity.  
- Table 1 should specify units for all metrics, given their heterogeneity.  

This paper presents a rigorously validated, experimentally sound contribution to RL, achieving a notable milestone as the vizDoom competition winner. While expanding to additional environments could strengthen the case for broader applicability, the current work’s technical soundness, thorough ablation, and reproducibility (via appendix details) make it a clear accept. The novelty is well-grounded within the literature, and the results are compelling without overclaiming. The minor editorial points do not detract from the paper’s substantial contribution.
