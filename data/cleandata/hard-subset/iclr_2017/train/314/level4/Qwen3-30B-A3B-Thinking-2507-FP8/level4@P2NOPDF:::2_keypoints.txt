- Introduces an on-policy deep RL method with auxiliary intrinsic variables built on universal value function frameworks, but the main technical contribution (synthesizing known techniques for 3D navigation) is not clearly stated in the abstract/introduction.  
- Presentation risks understating how the integration advances the field beyond prior work.  
- Lacks critical analysis of failure modes, particularly generalization to novel goals outside the training distribution.  
- On-policy design poses a risk of catastrophic forgetting for earlier-seen goals; the paper omits discussion of this limitation and mitigation strategies.  
- Empirical validation is confined to 3D navigation; testing on additional domains (e.g., Atari) would better demonstrate generality—suggests using ROMs as intrinsic goal representations.  
- Despite shortcomings, shows consistent empirical gains over baselines in navigation and provides useful insights into the role of auxiliary variables.  
- Suggested improvements: sharpen novelty claim in the introduction, analyze generalization and forgetting risks, and evaluate on at least one additional benchmark like Atari.
