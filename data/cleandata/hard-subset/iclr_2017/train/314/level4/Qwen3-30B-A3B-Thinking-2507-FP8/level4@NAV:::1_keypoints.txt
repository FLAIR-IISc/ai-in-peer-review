- Novel deep RL algorithm that uses raw sensory inputs in 3D environments and won the Visual Doom AI competition.  
- Core innovation: supervised prediction of low-dimensional auxiliary observations (e.g., ammo, health) conditioned on an explicit goal vector and the current action.  
- Actions chosen by maximizing the predicted outcome relative to the goal, rather than via value-function TD updates.  
- Uses Monte Carlo estimation (not TD learning) for training.  
- Leverages low-dimensional auxiliary measurements as prediction targets to guide learning.  
- Employs explicit goal parametrization (the goal vector is provided, not learned).  
- Provides rigorous empirical comparisons against relevant prior methods.  
- Demonstrates policies that generalize and adapt to limited goal changes without additional training.  
- Paper is well-structured with compelling empirical results likely to attract attention.  
- Minor concern: supervised training assumes on-policy returns but learns from a replay buffer—this approximation should be explicitly discussed.  
- Minor concern: method requires metadata specifying which sensory components to predict; clarify limitations for sensory-rich environments lacking such measurements.
