- Novel deep RL algorithm achieves strong performance in 3D environments from raw sensory input and shows superior generalization across diverse goals and environments.
- Method won the Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Leverages low-dimensional in-game measurements (e.g., ammo, health) as supervised prediction targets.
- Predictions are conditioned on a provided (not learned) goal vector and the current action.
- Actions are chosen by selecting the action that maximizes the predicted outcome relative to the goal.
- Differs from successor features: learning is fully supervised with no TD relationship between current and next-state predictions.
- Paper situates the approach well within prior work on goal-driven function approximation and future state prediction.
- Key contributions: Monte Carlo estimation instead of TD learning; use of supervised low-dimensional measurements; parametrized goals; comprehensive empirical comparisons.
- Demonstrates learned policies that generalize and adapt to limited goal changes without further training.
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition and convincing empirical results.
- Minor concern: supervised training uses an on-policy approximation while learning from a replay buffer of past-policy trajectories; this should be explicitly discussed.
- Minor concern: algorithm relies on metadata identifying which sensory components to predict; limitations in environments lacking such measurements should be acknowledged.
