- Paper proposes supervised prediction of future low-dimensional measurements from high-dimensional sensory input, goal-conditioned at test time, achieving SOTA in Visual Doom AI Competition and strong generalization.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Core contribution deemed insufficiently novel for ICLR — mainly an empirical application of known supervised-learning ideas to sensorimotor control.  
- Claimed novelty over prior work (e.g., Schaul et al., 2015) is overstated; vectorial measurements and goal-parameterization are not new.  
- The approach is primarily empirical; theoretical or conceptual advances are negligible.  
- The characterization as a "Monte Carlo" method vs. TD learning is misleading; training is standard regression on replay, not novel Monte Carlo or TD updates.  
- Mischaracterizes relationship to successor features and other TD-based methods.  
- Recommend clarifying reliance on environment-provided measurements and discussing cases where such measurements are unavailable (e.g., raw pixels only).  
- Raise concern about on-policy vs. off-policy mismatch: using experience replay with a regression loss assumes on-policy futures but samples from older policies.  
- Ask for stronger, practical comparisons/ablations against Schaul et al. (2015) and goal-conditioned RL baselines to substantiate claimed differences.  
- Request clarification on what "trained without a fixed goal" means in practice and how training differs from standard goal-conditioned RL (e.g., goal sampling).  
- Questions for authors: (1) How does sampling randomized goals during training differ from standard goal-conditioned RL? (2) How would the method work if environment measurements are unavailable — can measurements be learned from pixels? (3) How sensitive is performance to replay memory size M and does larger M worsen the on-policy mismatch?
