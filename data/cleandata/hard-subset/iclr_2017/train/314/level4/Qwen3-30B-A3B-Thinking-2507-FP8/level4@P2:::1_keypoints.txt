- Novel approach: reframes sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future measurements from current sensory input, action, and goal, enabling training from raw inputs and flexible goals.  
- Decision: Accept — strong contribution with compelling empirical and theoretical support.  
- Empirical success: DFP outperforms SOTA deep RL in Doom scenarios and won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch by >50%.  
- Rich supervision: Vectorial, temporally dense measurements provide much richer training signal than sparse scalar rewards; ablations (Table 4) confirm this.  
- Generalization: Models generalize across environments and to new goals without retraining (Table 3).  
- Theoretical grounding: Clear links to supervised learning, Monte Carlo methods, and parametrized goals; contrasts with TD learning.  
- Limitation: Relies on availability of meaningful measurements (e.g., health, ammo, frags), which may not be present in many environments.  
- Methodological concern: Uses replay buffer with past-policy experiences despite on-policy framing — potential bias/training-dynamics issue needing clarification.  
- Strength: Simplicity relative to competitors (no reward shaping or extra supervision) is a notable advantage.  
- Question: How would the approach perform when environments do not provide measurements — can measurements be learned from sensory input?  
- Question: How are the predicted measurements selected and could they be automatically discovered rather than manually specified?  
- Question: How is the goal vector specified at test time — must a user provide it or can the system infer it?
