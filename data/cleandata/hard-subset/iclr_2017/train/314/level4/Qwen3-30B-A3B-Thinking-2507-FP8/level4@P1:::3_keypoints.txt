- Proposes supervised learning for sensorimotor control by predicting future measurements from sensory input, current measurements, and a goal vector.  
- Enables learning from raw sensory input without extraneous supervision and supports flexible goal specification at test time.  
- Evaluated extensively in ViZDoom, outperforming state-of-the-art deep RL methods, especially on complex tasks.  
- Model won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch, beating the second place by over 50%.  
- Decision: Accept — simple, effective approach with rigorous experiments and strong contribution.  
- Paper addresses sensorimotor control in dynamic 3D-like environments and aims for goal-adaptive generalizable skills.  
- Motivation: cotemporal structure of sensory and measurement streams provides dense supervision alternative to sparse RL rewards.  
- Thorough literature contextualization linking supervised learning, Monte Carlo methods, and vector-valued feedback.  
- Strong empirical support: comparisons to DQN/A3C/DSR, ablation studies, generalization experiments, and competition results.  
- Clarify claim about Doom being a 3D environment since height is not discriminative/actionable in Doom.  
- Notation issues: using "P" for predictions (confusing with probability) and reusing "j" for two different concepts; revise for clarity.  
- Include units in result tables because measurements (health, frags, etc.) are heterogeneous.  
- Better explain how "vectorial measurements" differ from standard RL rewards, particularly regarding temporal density of supervision.  
- Questions: clarify generalization to true 3D environments where height matters; explain why predicting multiple measurements helps a single-measurement task (are measurements dependent?); report robustness to different visual styles/object distributions not seen during training.
