- Paper introduces a supervised prediction approach for sensorimotor control that uses cotemporal high-dimensional sensory streams and low-dimensional measurement streams.  
- Frames learning as predicting how actions affect future vectorial measurements given current sensory input, measurements, and a goal vector.  
- Eliminates need for extrinsic rewards or fixed goals during training, enabling goal-agnostic learning and test-time goal specification.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance in Visual Doom AI Competition, outperforming strong baselines by >50% in Full Deathmatch using only raw visuals and intrinsic measurements.  
- Decision: Accept — strong empirical contribution demonstrated by competition win and large gains over baselines.  
- Core insight: dense vectorial measurements provide superior supervision to sparse scalar rewards, well-motivated and rigorously validated.  
- Novelty: applies supervised future prediction with vectorial measurements to raw sensory input in complex 3D environments.  
- Empirical strengths: large gains in Battle scenarios (6x vs DQN, 2.5x vs DSR/A3C), generalization across 100 textured environments, and ablation confirming vectorial feedback and multi-step prediction importance.  
- The paper should clarify the relationship between measurement streams and environment dynamics (e.g., sufficiency of measurements to specify state).  
- Needs deeper explanation for why vectorial feedback outperforms scalar rewards beyond empirical results (e.g., credit assignment reasoning).  
- Method relies on experience replay (contrary to an on-policy claim); should detail how buffer composition, size, and sampling prevent catastrophic forgetting.  
- Suggested improvements: explicitly state core contribution in abstract/introduction, expand failure analysis on extreme test-time goals, broaden empirical validation beyond Doom (e.g., Atari), and better justify the normalization layer in Section 3.2.  
- Questions for authors: why do negative goal weights ([-1,1]) improve generalization; sensitivity to experience buffer size M and update freq k and chosen defaults; how much of the performance gap is due to model capacity vs. method.  
- Overall: compelling, empirically grounded alternative to reward-based RL with recommended minor presentation and analysis refinements.
