- Proposes an on-policy method that predicts future intrinsic state triplets (health, ammunition, frags) to guide exploration.  
- Uses user-specified weighted goal triplets to steer behavior, with agents acting to maximize long-term goal alignment at test time.  
- Evaluated exclusively in vizDoom (a 2D environment with pseudo-3D rendering), and the method won the 2016 vizDoom AI Competition.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, including comparisons to A3C, DQN, and DSR, where DFP consistently outperforms baselines.  
- Ablation study shows each proposed component contributes meaningfully to overall performance.  
- Situates the work within intrinsic motivation, auxiliary learning, and forward modeling literature and cites key prior work.  
- References are adequate but could be made more complete.  
- Minor concern: environment is inaccurately described as "3D" when vizDoom is essentially 2D with 3D-like rendering.  
- Minor concern: notation “P” in Equation (2) is misleading since it denotes prediction, conflicting with standard probability notation.  
- Minor concern: Equation (6) reuses the index "j" in different fonts, which may confuse readers.  
- Minor concern: Table 1 should repeat units explicitly due to heterogeneous metrics (e.g., frags vs. ammo count).  
- Overall assessment: technically solid, reproducible, and impactful work; recommended for acceptance to ICLR.
