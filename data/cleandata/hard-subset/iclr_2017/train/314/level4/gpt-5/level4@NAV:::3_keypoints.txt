- Introduces an on-policy method to forecast future intrinsic measurements.  
- Experiments are exclusively conducted in Doom (vizDoom).  
- Model predicts sequences of triplets (health, ammunition, frags) weighted by input “goal” triplets.  
- Modifying goal weights provides a mechanism to guide exploration.  
- At test time the agent acts by maximizing the long-term goal alone.  
- Strong empirical results: won the 2016 vizDoom competition.  
- Comparisons to A3C, DQN, and attempted DSR show DFP outperforms or matches baselines.  
- Ablation study indicates each added component meaningfully contributes.  
- Builds on well-established themes (intrinsic motivation, auxiliary variables, forward modeling) and cites prior work adequately.  
- Minor critique: Doom is described as 3D but functions effectively as 2D for these tasks.  
- Minor notation issues: using “P” for prediction may confuse with probability; reuse of “j” is misleading.  
- Suggests results tables should reiterate measurement units due to heterogeneous metrics.  
- Overall recommendation: clear accept—empirically solid and reproducible, though broader environments or greater novelty could be desired.
