- Proposes supervised-learning approach (DFP) that predicts future vector-valued measurements (health, ammo, frags) conditioned on current observation and goal vector for sensorimotor control.
- Predicts measurement sequences at multiple temporal offsets weighted by goal triplets, enabling shaping of behavior via goal weights and dynamic goals at test time.
- Uses dense multivariate feedback instead of sparse scalar reward and supports goal-agnostic training with dynamic test-time goals.
- Extensive experiments in ViZDoom show DFP outperforms strong deep RL baselines (A3C, DQN) on challenging scenarios and matches them where they saturate.
- Demonstrates compelling generalization across textures/environments and goals; ablations show vectorial feedback and multi-horizon prediction are important.
- Method won the 2016 Visual Doom AI Competition (Full Deathmatch), beating a strong LSTM DQN with extra supervision.
- Decision: Accept.
- Primary reason 1: Strong, reproducible empirical results that significantly outperform standard deep RL on challenging tasks, including a competition win.
- Primary reason 2: Simple, well-motivated formulation reframing control as supervised prediction over rich measurement streams enabling flexible goals.
- Problem significance: Tackles acting from raw sensory input in complex 3D-style environments and pursuing multiple goals—central to scalable RL.
- Placement and motivation: Situates work relative to supervised vs TD learning, Monte Carlo, multi-objective RL, UVFAs, and predictive representations; revision adds some intrinsic motivation/auxiliary prediction citations.
- Method clarity: Architecture cleanly separates perception, measurements, and goals and uses an expectation/action-stream decomposition; training via straightforward regression on gathered experience.
- Empirical support: Across four scenarios DFP equals or surpasses baselines, with largest gains in complex multi-measurement settings; generalization and ablation studies strengthen claims.
- Reproducibility: Paper provides implementation details and fair baselines; experimental setup appears careful.
- Environment clarification: Doom is pseudo-3D (height not actionable); a brief note would avoid confusion.
- Notation issues: Recommend avoiding P for prediction (may imply probability) and disambiguating double use of j in equations.
- Results tables: Recommend repeating units/ranges for heterogeneous measurements to aid interpretation (especially Table 1).
- Related work: Suggest a short paragraph acknowledging adjacent lines on intrinsic motivation, auxiliary losses, and forward modeling beyond current citations.
- Limitations: Paper should note reactive (no memory) design, testing in a single game, and focus on discrete actions to set expectations.
- Questions on training: Request quantification of impact of memory size M and update frequency k on stability/performance and sensitivity to these choices.
- Questions on goal sampling: Ask sensitivity to goal vector distributions (e.g., [0,1] vs [-1,1]) and whether curricula/structured goals improve generalization.
- Questions on temporal offsets/weights: Ask robustness to chosen offsets {1,2,4,8,16,32} and weighting scheme; whether other schedules were tested.
- Questions on architecture: Request ablation isolating contribution of action-stream normalization beyond dueling-style separation.
- Questions on variance: Ask how many independent runs per condition and standard deviations for final performance metrics.
- Observations and memory: Ask whether inputs are single or stacked frames and whether adding recurrence could help, especially in D3/D4.
- Competition details: Request training compute, total steps, and domain randomization used for Full Deathmatch win.
- Environment realism question: Ask if similar gains are expected in fully 3D environments where verticality and physics are semantically important.
