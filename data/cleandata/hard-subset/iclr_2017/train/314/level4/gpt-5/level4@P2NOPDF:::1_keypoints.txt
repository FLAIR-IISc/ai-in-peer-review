- Empirically driven algorithm achieving strong performance from raw sensory input in novel 3D environments; won the Visual Doom AI competition.
- Central idea: predict future low-dimensional measurements (e.g., health, ammo) conditioned on action and a supplied goal vector, and select actions that maximize predicted outcomes.
- Learning is supervised via Monte Carlo regression with no temporal-difference coupling between current and next-state predictions.
- Paper positions itself within predictive modeling in RL and goal-conditioned function approximators.
- Key contributions: focus on Monte Carlo estimation rather than TD learning.
- Key contributions: use of low-dimensional measurements as prediction targets.
- Key contributions: parameterized goals enabling flexible test-time behavior.
- Key contributions: thorough empirical comparisons against relevant baselines.
- Demonstrated test-time adaptation to limited goal changes without additional training, indicating generalization.
- Paper is clearly written, well motivated, and empirically compelling; of significant interest to the community.
- Minor concern: off-policy training with Monte Carlo targets—replay-buffered off-policy data may bias or hurt sample efficiency; discuss impacts and mitigations (e.g., buffer refresh, policy update schedules).
- Minor concern: reliance on environment-provided measurements may give the method an advantage over baselines; acknowledge limitations when such measurements are unavailable and suggest workarounds (e.g., learning proxy measurements from pixels).
