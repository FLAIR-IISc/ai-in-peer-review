This paper presents an on-policy deep RL method augmented with auxiliary intrinsic variables.

- Positioning and contributions: The method appears to be a special case of a universal value function–based approach, and the authors cite the appropriate prior work. The primary technical contribution seems to be distilling and integrating existing ideas to solve 3D navigation tasks. Please state the contributions more explicitly and concretely in the abstract and introduction so that the novelty and scope are clear.

- Failure modes and generalization: I would like to see an analysis of failure cases. Under what conditions does the model struggle to generalize to changing goals? A related conceptual concern is that, as an on-policy method, the approach is susceptible to catastrophic forgetting unless the agent repeatedly trains on goals from the distant past.

- Breadth of evaluation: Since the main contribution is integrating several key ideas and demonstrating empirical gains, it would strengthen the paper to show results on other domains, such as Atari (for example, possibly using the ROM as intrinsic variables).

Overall, the paper demonstrates a clear empirical advantage for the proposed formulations, and the experimental insights are likely to be valuable for future agents.
