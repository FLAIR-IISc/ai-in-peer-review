- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future changes in a vector of intrinsic measurements conditioned on observation and a goal, then pick actions whose predicted measurements best match the goal.
- Key idea: turn control into supervised learning by using dense multivariate measurement streams as supervision, enabling dynamic goals at test time.
- Shows DFP matches or surpasses DQN and A3C in ViZDoom, especially on challenging combat tasks, and won the 2016 Visual Doom AI Competition (Full Deathmatch).
- Decision: Accept.
- Reason 1 for acceptance: Strong, reproducible empirical evidence that a simple predictive formulation outperforms state-of-the-art deep RL baselines and generalizes across environments and goals.
- Reason 2 for acceptance: Clear, technically sound paper well-situated in related work, with a practical architecture and thorough ablations.
- Problem addressed: Learning sensorimotor control from raw visual input while supporting dynamically specified goals.
- Motivation/placement: Bridges supervised learning and control; relates to predictive representations, Monte Carlo methods, UVFA, and multi-objective RL.
- Empirical evidence: Head-to-head comparisons with DQN/A3C/DSR showing DFP’s superiority on harder tasks and when multiple measurements are present.
- Generalization evidence: Experiments across textures, environments, and higher-capacity variants show robustness.
- Goal-randomized training: Enables adaptation to new goals at test time with minimal main-task loss and improved robustness.
- Ablations: Demonstrate benefits of vector-valued targets and multi-horizon prediction.
- External validation: Victory in Visual Doom AI Competition over a more supervised baseline.
- Suggest clarifying Doom’s “2.5D” nature while emphasizing first-person visual complexity.
- Notation suggestions: Avoid using P for predictions and reusing j; consider clearer symbols like F(·) or ŷ and distinct subscripts.
- Results clarity: Reiterate units and normalization choices in result tables when combining heterogeneous measurements.
- On-policy vs off-policy: Discuss stability/efficiency trade-offs of ε-greedy behavior with a short replay buffer and why a small buffer works.
- Broader evaluation: Recommend testing or discussing other 3D environments to strengthen generality claims.
- Limitations/extensions: Expand discussion on memory, temporal abstraction, and continuous action spaces and how DFP could integrate with them.
- Reproducibility: Centralize key hyperparameters and report variance across seeds for baselines.
- Questions for authors — measurement design: How sensitive is performance to choice, scaling, and noise of measurements?
- Questions — goal sampling: How were goal vectors sampled/normalized in goal-randomized training; any curriculum or structured sampling tried?
- Questions — multi-horizon targets: How sensitive are results to chosen offsets and weights; were learned weights or discount-like schedules tried?
- Questions — experience memory: What buffer size worked best and did larger buffers harm performance due to non-stationarity?
- Questions — baseline tuning: Were stabilizers like reward clipping, frame stacking, or different entropy schedules considered for A3C/DQN?
- Questions — generalization tests: Any robustness checks to lighting changes, object variants, or partial observability (e.g., fog)?
- Questions — computational cost: Report wall-clock training time and hardware for DFP vs baselines.
- Questions — continuous actions: Any anticipated obstacles to adapting DFP to continuous action spaces (e.g., action sampling or policy gradients)?
