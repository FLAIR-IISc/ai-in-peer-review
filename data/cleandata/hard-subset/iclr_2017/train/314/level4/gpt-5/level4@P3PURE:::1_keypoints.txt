- Introduces an empirically driven algorithm that learns from raw sensory input and achieves superior performance in novel 3D environments, including winning the Visual Doom AI competition.  
- Core idea: supervised prediction of low-dimensional environment measurements (e.g., ammo, health) conditioned on a provided, non-learned goal vector and the current action.  
- Action selection is performed by choosing the action that maximizes the predicted outcome under the specified goal.  
- Differs from successor feature approaches by using fully supervised Monte Carlo targets with no TD linkage between current and next state predictions.  
- Main contributions: (i) emphasis on Monte Carlo estimation rather than TD, (ii) use of low-dimensional measurements as prediction targets, (iii) parameterized goals, and (iv) careful empirical comparison to baselines.  
- Demonstrates generalization: learned policies transfer to modest changes in goals without additional training.  
- Paper is clearly written and presents compelling empirical results of likely significant interest.  
- Minor concern: supervised Monte Carlo training assumes on-policy returns while using a replay buffer—this off-policy approximation should be more explicitly discussed.  
- Minor concern: method relies on privileged metadata (known measurements); the advantage and limitations when such measurements are unavailable should be stated.
