- Proposes an on-policy deep RL method with auxiliary intrinsic variables for goal-conditioned control, framed as a specific UVFA instance and showing improved performance on 3D navigation tasks.
- Clear empirical gains on 3D navigation: consistently outperforms baselines on reported tasks.
- Sensible synthesis of prior ideas: combines goal conditioning/UVFA framing with intrinsic signals in a coherent approach.
- Practical relevance: design choices are useful for agents in visually rich, goal-driven environments.
- Limited novelty: primarily an instantiation of known ideas (goal conditioning, UVFAs, auxiliary signals) within an on-policy framework.
- Lacks failure mode analysis, especially regarding generalization to unseen or shifted goals and goal changes mid-episode.
- Catastrophic forgetting risk due to on-policy training is not quantified or mitigated.
- Narrow empirical scope: evaluation confined to 3D navigation—generalization to other domains (e.g., Atari) is untested.
- Missing ablations: unclear contributions of auxiliary variables, goal-conditioning mechanism, and on-policy updates.
- Request to clearly state contributions, separating algorithmic novelty, architectural/training insights, and empirical findings.
- Request failure-mode experiments: distribution-shifted goals, dynamic goal switches, and forgetting metrics for old goals.
- Request expanded experiments: at least one non-navigation domain, plus sample efficiency and stability (seed variance) reporting.
- Request ablations/diagnostics: remove/alter auxiliary variables, compare on-policy vs replay/off-policy fixes, and test sensitivity to goal encoding and auxiliary loss weights.
- Request detailed implementation and reproducibility info: architectures, optimization, compute budget, and training curves with confidence intervals.
- Minor suggestions: clarify terminology for “universal value functions” and “intrinsic variables,” and explain how intrinsic variables are instantiated and learned.
- Minor suggestions: report computational overhead of auxiliary components and include qualitative visualizations of learned goal representations or trajectories.
- Overall assessment: well-executed empirical work with convincing 3D navigation gains but limited novelty and narrow evaluation; stronger claims would require clearer contributions, thorough failure analysis, and broader experiments.
