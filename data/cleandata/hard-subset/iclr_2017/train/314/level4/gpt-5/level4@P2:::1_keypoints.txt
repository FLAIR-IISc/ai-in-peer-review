- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): supervised learning that predicts future intrinsic measurement changes from (observation, action, goal) and picks actions maximizing goal·predictions.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Well-motivated alternative to TD-based RL that reframes control as supervised, goal-conditioned prediction on dense measurements.  
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical performance, generalization, and ablations, plus a competition win.  
- Problem/motivation: Tackles raw-sensor control in 3D environments and adapting to changing goals by exploiting dense multidimensional measurement streams.  
- Approach: Learn F(o,a,g) predicting multi-horizon measurement differences and act by maximizing g^T F without TD targets.  
- Empirical support: Outperforms DQN, A3C, DSR on ViZDoom scenarios, especially hard combat tasks; shows robustness across environments and goals.  
- Architecture and fairness: Uses a simple DQN-like architecture with separate expectation/action streams to enable fair baseline comparisons.  
- Ablations: Vector-valued measurements and multi-horizon prediction substantially improve performance.  
- Generalization tests: Texture randomization, cross-environment testing, and goal-agnostic training show robustness and adaptability.  
- Training/competition: Reasonable training throughput and a variant won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch.  
- Presentation: Well written with sufficient implementation detail and literature grounding.  
- Feedback — replay buffer bias: Need discussion on on-policy target assumption vs. replay buffer use and sensitivity to buffer size/update lag.  
- Feedback — measurement reliance: Clarify advantage from extra game-provided measurements, their availability in real settings, and sensitivity to fewer/noisier measurements.  
- Feedback — goal vector design: Provide guidance on choosing/normalizing goals, handling conflicting objectives, and scale sensitivity.  
- Feedback — temporal offsets/weights: Justify chosen time offsets and loss weights and include sensitivity analyses.  
- Feedback — architecture ablations: Include ablation isolating expectation/action streams and report prediction calibration/error metrics.  
- Feedback — limitations: Emphasize memoryless, discrete-action scope; discuss extensions to recurrence and continuous control; add statistical variability (error bars).  
- Q1: Sensitivity to replay memory size, update frequency, and ε-greedy schedule; any off-policy bias observed?  
- Q2: How were goal vectors sampled during goal-agnostic training and how crucial is goal-conditioning for generalization?  
- Q3: How were measurements selected; how does performance change if measurements are excluded or noisy?  
- Q4: Sensitivity to choice/scaling of temporal offsets and loss weights; would learned horizon weighting help?  
- Q5: Ablation without the expectation/action split and effect of the action-normalization layer on stability?  
- Q6: How to adapt DFP to sparse scalar-reward settings; effective proxy measurements?  
- Q7: For continuous actions, plan to sample actions to evaluate F or optimize a differentiable policy maximizing g^T F?  
- Q8: Were predicted measurements calibrated (e.g., reliability plots); would directly learning u(f;g) hurt goal generalization?  
- Q9: Beyond textures, tested generalization to new enemy types/dynamics/weapons and observed failure modes?
