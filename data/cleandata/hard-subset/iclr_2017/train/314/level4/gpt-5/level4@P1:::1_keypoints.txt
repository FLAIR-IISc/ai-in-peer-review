- Proposes reframing action selection as supervised prediction of future low-dimensional measurements conditioned on observation, candidate action, and goal, using Monte Carlo regression instead of TD learning.  
- Trains to predict measurement changes at multiple future horizons and selects actions whose predicted outcomes best match a goal vector.  
- Enables goal-conditioned policies that can adapt to new, dynamically specified goals at test time without retraining.  
- Demonstrates strong performance on ViZDoom tasks, generalization across textures and goals, and won the Visual Doom AI Competition (Full Deathmatch).  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Primary reason: Strong empirical performance and generalization outperforming DQN, A3C, and DSR, especially with multiple measurements.  
- Primary reason: Clear, well-motivated supervised, goal-conditioned formulation as a practical Monte Carlo alternative to TD-based RL.  
- Novelty: Clean use of dense, vectorial measurement prediction conditioned on action and goal to exploit multidimensional feedback, without bootstrapping.  
- Relation to prior work: Positions itself in Monte Carlo vs TD debate and connects to UVFA, multi-objective RL, and predictive representations while departing from scalar rewards.  
- Empirical rigor: Careful comparisons, compelling generalization and ablation studies showing benefits of multiple measurements and multi-horizon prediction.  
- Communication: Paper is clear, well-structured, and baselines were run with reasonable hyperparameter searches.  
- Feedback: Clarify off-policy training details—replay buffer size, parameter update speed, observed bias/instability, and any importance weighting or on-policy ablations.  
- Feedback: Explicitly acknowledge use of environment-provided low-dimensional measurements and consider augmenting baselines with the same auxiliary targets for fairer comparison.  
- Feedback: Discuss limitations when intrinsic measurements are unavailable or noisy and possible extensions (learning measurement proxies or predicting learned features).  
- Feedback: Clarify goal vector normalization, training vs test goal distributions, sensitivity to misspecified/adversarial goals, and performance vs goal magnitude/sparsity.  
- Feedback: Address partial observability by discussing or testing recurrence (e.g., LSTM) since the policy is reactive and Doom is partially observable.  
- Questions: How sensitive is performance to replay buffer size/staleness and did you try purely on-policy training or importance sampling?  
- Questions: How robust is the method to measurement choice, dimensionality, noise, dominance of certain measurements, and how are measurements normalized across horizons?  
- Questions: Have you evaluated A3C/DQN augmented with the same measurement prediction auxiliary tasks or reward shaping to isolate the supervised signal benefit?  
- Questions: How were randomized goals sampled and normalized during training, and how does performance vary on unseen goal distributions?  
- Questions: Any anticipated difficulties or preliminary results for continuous actions or adding recurrence?  
- Questions: Please report training wall-clock time and sample efficiency relative to A3C under identical hardware and clarify the importance of the multi-horizon setup to efficiency.
