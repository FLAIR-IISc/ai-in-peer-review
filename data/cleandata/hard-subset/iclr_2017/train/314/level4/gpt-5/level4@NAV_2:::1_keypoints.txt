- Proposes supervised, Monte Carlo-style prediction of future low-dimensional measurements (health, ammo) conditioned on observation, action, and a provided goal vector, selecting actions that maximize predicted goal-weighted outcomes.  
- Avoids TD bootstrapping and TD relationships between consecutive state predictions.  
- Learns from raw visual input and generalizes across goals and environments.  
- Shows strong empirical results on Doom-based tasks, including winning the Visual Doom AI competition.  
- Decision: Accept — due to compelling empirical performance and a clear, simple alternative to TD-based deep RL.  
- Problem addressed: robust sensorimotor control from raw sensory streams in complex 3D environments with dynamic goals.  
- Motivation/placement: clearly contrasts with TD-based RL and connects to predictive representations and goal-conditioned value function literature while highlighting differences.  
- Technical approach: supervised prediction of future measurement changes conditioned on action and goal, leveraging dense low-dimensional feedback.  
- Evidence: strong comparisons to baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR), improved performance on harder tasks, and generalization to new goals without retraining.  
- Communication: paper is clearly written with a thorough empirical evaluation.  
- Training concern: uses a replay buffer that mixes policies despite Monte Carlo targets assuming on-policy trajectories — potential bias needs discussion.  
- Comparative assumptions: method benefits from explicit measurement metadata; clarify limitations when such measurements are unavailable or incomplete.  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to off-policyness in the replay buffer; any on-policy or controlled-staleness experiments?  
- Question: How much performance gain is due to using low-dimensional measurements versus scalar reward?  
- Question: In what settings are measurements unavailable or noisy, and how would the method degrade or adapt (e.g., learned proxies)?  
- Question: How are goal vectors specified at test time across scenarios, and how sensitive is behavior to misspecified goals?
