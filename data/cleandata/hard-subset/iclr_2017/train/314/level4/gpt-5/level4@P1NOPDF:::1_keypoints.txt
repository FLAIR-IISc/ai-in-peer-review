- Empirical paper proposing a new deep RL algorithm that operates on raw sensory inputs and excels in novel 3D environments.
- Method won the Visual Doom AI competition, demonstrating strong empirical performance.
- Core idea: predict low-dimensional game measurements (e.g., ammo, health) as supervised targets conditioned on a provided goal vector and the current action.
- Action selection is done by choosing the action that maximizes the predicted outcome under the current goal.
- Learning uses fully supervised Monte Carlo targets rather than temporal-difference updates, i.e., no TD coupling across time.
- Key contributions: emphasis on Monte Carlo estimation, prediction of low-dimensional measurements, parameterized (provided) goals, and thorough empirical baselines.
- Learned policies generalize to modest test-time goal changes without further training.
- Paper is clearly written and presents compelling empirical results of likely interest to the community.
- Minor concern: training uses supervised regression from a replay buffer while assuming on-policy trajectories—off-policy Monte Carlo bias and practical impact should be discussed.
- Minor concern: method leverages additional metadata (measurements) not used by compared algorithms; limitations when such measurements are unavailable should be explicitly acknowledged.
