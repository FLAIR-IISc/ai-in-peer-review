Summary
This paper proposes an on-policy deep reinforcement learning method augmented with auxiliary intrinsic variables for goal-conditioned control. The approach can be interpreted as a specific instance of universal value function approximators (UVFAs), and the paper appropriately cites relevant prior work. The central contribution appears to be a careful integration of existing ideas to solve 3D navigation tasks, with empirical results showing improved performance over baselines.

Strengths
- Clear empirical gains on 3D navigation: The method consistently outperforms baselines on the reported tasks, suggesting that the combination of auxiliary variables and the chosen training setup is effective in practice.
- Sensible synthesis of prior ideas: Framing the method within UVFAs and combining goal conditioning with additional intrinsic signals provides a coherent approach to handling variable goals and complex observations.
- Practical relevance: The insights and design choices presented could be useful to practitioners building agents that operate in visually rich, goal-driven environments.

Weaknesses and Concerns
- Limited novelty: Conceptually, the method largely instantiates known ideas (goal conditioning/UVFAs, auxiliary signals) within an on-policy framework. The paper would benefit from a sharper articulation of what is technically new versus what is a principled synthesis or engineering contribution.
- Lack of failure mode analysis: The paper does not sufficiently examine where the approach breaks down. In particular:
  - Generalization to changing or unseen goals is not characterized. How does performance degrade as goals move further from the training distribution, or when goals change mid-episode?
  - As an on-policy method, the agent is vulnerable to catastrophic forgetting: goals that are not revisited will be forgotten. The paper does not quantify this effect or discuss mitigation strategies.
- Narrow empirical scope: Given that the main contribution is an integration yielding empirical gains, it would be valuable to test beyond 3D navigation. For example, applying the method to Atari (e.g., with ROM or other task descriptors as intrinsic variables) would help assess generality.
- Missing ablations: It is unclear how much each component (auxiliary variables, goal conditioning mechanism, on-policy updates) contributes to the overall performance. Strong ablations would significantly strengthen the claims.

Requests for Clarification and Additional Experiments
- Clearly state the contributions in the abstract and introduction. Distinguish between:
  - Novel algorithmic elements (if any),
  - New training or architectural insights,
  - Empirical findings that may generalize to other settings.
- Provide failure mode analyses:
  - Evaluate under distribution shift in goals (e.g., hold-out goal sets, perturbed goal representations).
  - Test dynamic-goal scenarios (goal switches within episodes).
  - Quantify forgetting by measuring performance on “old” goals as training proceeds without revisiting them.
- Expand experimental coverage:
  - Include at least one additional domain distinct from 3D navigation. Atari or other benchmark suites with goal-like conditioning would be informative.
  - Report sample efficiency and stability (variance across seeds), especially important for on-policy methods.
- Add ablations and diagnostics:
  - Remove or vary auxiliary intrinsic variables to measure their contribution.
  - Compare on-policy versus simple off-policy corrections or replay for mitigating forgetting (even if only as a diagnostic).
  - Analyze sensitivity to the goal-encoding scheme and to the weighting of auxiliary losses.
- Provide implementation details sufficient for reproduction: network architecture, optimization settings, compute budget, and training curves with confidence intervals.

Minor Comments
- Improve terminology consistency around “universal value functions” and “intrinsic variables” and clarify how the latter are instantiated and learned.
- Discuss computational overhead introduced by the auxiliary components.
- Include qualitative visualizations of learned goal representations or trajectories to illustrate behavior differences.

Overall Assessment
This is a well-executed empirical paper that demonstrates clear gains on 3D navigation by thoughtfully integrating goal-conditioned value learning with auxiliary intrinsic variables. The work is positioned appropriately within the UVFA literature and makes a practical contribution. However, the novelty is limited, and the scope of evaluation is narrow. The paper would be significantly strengthened by (1) a clearer statement of contributions, (2) a thorough analysis of failure modes—especially generalization to new goals and catastrophic forgetting in an on-policy setting, and (3) broader, more diverse experiments with strong ablations.

With these additions, the paper could provide both a solid empirical benchmark and actionable insights for building robust goal-conditioned agents. As it stands, the empirical advantage is convincing for 3D navigation, and the insights are likely useful, but the claims of general utility would benefit from broader validation.
