- Proposes an on-policy method that predicts future measurement vectors (e.g., health, ammo, frags) from raw input and conditions predictions on a goal vector to guide behavior.
- Reformulates sensorimotor control as supervised prediction of future measurements, enabling learning without external labels and goal-parameterized control at test time.
- Demonstrates strong empirical results in ViZDoom, outperforming or matching A3C/DQN, with ablations and a first-place finish in the 2016 Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: strong, clear empirical evidence including state-of-the-art performance and a competition win.
- Key reason: simple, well-motivated formulation leveraging dense measurement streams with solid ablations and comparisons.
- Comparative performance: DFP outperforms baselines across four scenarios, substantially exceeding A3C/DQN in harder settings and documenting attempted DSR comparisons.
- Ablations: predicting multiple measurements and multiple temporal offsets improves performance even for single-metric evaluation.
- Generalization: training with varied textures/environments and higher-capacity models improves transfer; goal-agnostic training generalizes well to novel goals.
- Methodological soundness: architecture aligned with DQN for fair comparison; experimental protocols and reporting are careful and detailed; literature positioning improved in revision.
- Clarify environment description: Doom is effectively 2.5D, not full 3D—state this to avoid confusion.
- Notation issues: avoid using "P" for non-probabilistic prediction and reuse of “j” in different fonts to prevent reader confusion.
- Tables/units: reiterate measurement units in result tables (especially Table 1) since metrics are heterogeneous.
- Literature scope: prior work coverage is adequate but could briefly cite related strands (intrinsic motivation, auxiliary tasks, forward models like Singh et al., 2004).
- Question (goal conditioning): How sensitive is test-time performance to the training distribution over goal vectors (e.g., [0,1] vs. [-1,1]) and to test goals outside that distribution?
- Question (goal conditioning): Did you try curriculum strategies over goal vectors to facilitate learning?
- Question (training dynamics): How do experience memory size and update lag (k) affect stability and sample efficiency, and are there trade-offs with off-policy data?
- Question (training dynamics): How sensitive are results to the choice of temporal offsets and weighting coefficients; did alternative schedules (e.g., exponential) change outcomes?
- Question (architecture): Can you quantify the contribution of the expectation/action-stream split beyond existing ablations (e.g., controls without normalization)?
- Question (architecture): Did you try lightweight memory (frame stacking or simple recurrence) and how did it interact with DFP?
- Question (scope/transfer): How robust is performance to stronger domain shifts (lighting, enemy types, physics tweaks), and what failure cases were observed?
- Question (scope/transfer): Are extensions to continuous action spaces straightforward, and what changes to the action-stream would be required?
