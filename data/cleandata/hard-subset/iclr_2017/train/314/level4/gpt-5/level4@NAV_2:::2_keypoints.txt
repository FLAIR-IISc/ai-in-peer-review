- Proposes an on-policy deep RL method augmented with auxiliary intrinsic measurements to drive sensorimotor control in immersive 3D environments.  
- Conceptually akin to universal value function approaches, with goals parameterized over future measurements.  
- Main contribution is a practical distillation and integration of existing ideas into a working system that learns from raw sensory streams.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on challenging Doom-based navigation and combat tasks.  
- Recommendation: Accept — motivated by compelling empirical gains and a well-executed synthesis of known concepts.  
- Problem/motivation: Addresses learning from high-dimensional observations with dynamically changing goals using dense intrinsic measurements as supervision.  
- Relation to prior work: Properly situates the method as operationalizing UVFA-style ideas rather than introducing a new theoretical construct.  
- Empirical support: Clear performance improvements over strong baselines substantiate the paper’s practical claims.  
- Scope/limitations: Needs clearer articulation of contributions and discussion of potential failure modes and robustness limits.  
- Concern: Uncertainty about robustness to distributional shifts in goals and on-policy training raising catastrophic forgetting risks for infrequent goals.  
- Suggestion: Make methodological novelties vs. adaptations explicit in abstract/introduction to highlight what drives gains.  
- Suggestion: Analyze and report failure modes—e.g., measurement trade-offs, long-horizon dependencies, sparse/delayed measurement effects.  
- Suggestion: Provide experiments or analysis on catastrophic forgetting and mitigation strategies (e.g., goal-balanced sampling or rehearsal).  
- Suggestion: Demonstrate applicability beyond Doom (e.g., Atari) using accessible intrinsic variables to test generality.  
- Questions for authors: When do changing or OOD goals cause failures and which measurement/horizon combinations are brittle?  
- Questions for authors: Did you observe catastrophic forgetting during on-policy training and which mitigation strategies help?  
- Questions for authors: How are goals sampled during training, how sensitive are results to this distribution, and would curriculum strategies help?  
- Questions for authors: How does performance vary with different temporal offsets/weightings for future measurements and short- vs long-horizon trade-offs?  
- Questions for authors: Have you tried other domains (e.g., Atari) with proxy intrinsic variables, and what obstacles do you anticipate?
