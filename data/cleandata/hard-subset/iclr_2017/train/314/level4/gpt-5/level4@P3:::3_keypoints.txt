- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict vector-valued future measurement changes and choose actions that align predictions with a goal vector.  
- Decouples training from a fixed reward, enabling goal randomization and dynamic goal specification at test time.  
- Operates on raw RGB input plus low-dimensional measurements with an architecture separating expectation and action-specific deviation streams.  
- Empirically outperforms strong RL baselines (DQN, A3C) on ViZDoom, especially on complex multi-measurement battle tasks.  
- Shows generalization across textures and environments, and goal-agnostic training transfers to unseen test-time goals.  
- Ablations indicate multi-measurement and multi-horizon prediction materially improve performance.  
- Demonstrated robustness and competitiveness via a Visual Doom AI challenge win without extra supervision.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reasons: strong empirical results with ablations supporting design choices, and conceptual clarity/practical impact of supervised prediction framing.  
- Motivation: dense vector measurements provide more stable and informative supervision than sparse scalar rewards.  
- Related work: positioned relative to TD/Monte Carlo, predictive representations, UVFA, multi-objective RL, and successor representations; extends these via goal-parameterized prediction from rich sensory input.  
- Evidence: experiments span four scenarios of increasing difficulty; DFP matches A3C on basic tasks and substantially outperforms on battle tasks.  
- Generalization evidence is convincing when training includes diverse environments/textures.  
- Ablation evidence: multivariate and multi-horizon predictions help even when evaluating a single metric.  
- Implementation: method is straightforward to implement; training details and reasonable baseline hyperparameter searches are provided.  
- Architecture: expectation vs. action-stream decomposition is sensible and empirically beneficial.  
- Clarify “on-policy”: training uses ε-greedy plus a small replay buffer and periodic updates; need clarity on off-policy sample usage and sensitivity to replay size/update lag.  
- Measurements and scaling: request more detail on normalization/standardization, handling sparse/volatile measurements, and how scaling affects training/goal weighting.  
- Prediction horizons: ask for sensitivity analysis on horizon choices, weighting coefficients, and whether longer horizons or learned weights help.  
- Partial observability and memory: model is reactive (single frame); comment on effects of frame stacks or recurrent memory for complex scenarios.  
- Fairness of comparisons: note DSR was only evaluated on basic task due to speed—request discussion of sample efficiency and wall-clock cost across methods.  
- Relation to UVFA/DSR: ask for deeper comparison clarifying differences in training targets, bias/variance, and policy dependence.  
- Competition setup: request summary of differences between the competition agent and the paper’s main D4-tx-L setup for reproducibility.  
- Minor style/notation: suggest calling Doom “2.5D,” avoid using P for predictions, don’t reuse j, and repeat units in result tables.  
- Question — goal distribution: request exact sampling distribution and normalization of g for randomized-goal training and sensitivity of performance to this choice.  
- Question — measurement choice: ask how critical the chosen measurements are and whether adding/removing or learned pseudo-measurements was tried.  
- Question — horizon and discounting: ask how DFP compares to optimizing discounted scalarized objectives and whether learned temporal weighting or larger τ help.  
- Question — off-policy tolerance: ask robustness to larger replay buffers, off-policy data, and mixing in expert/scripted trajectories.  
- Question — exploration: ask whether goal-driven exploration or intrinsic curiosity/count-based methods were tried beyond ε-greedy.  
- Question — architecture: ask importance of expectation/action-stream vs single head, especially as action set grows, and effects of dueling-style variants.  
- Question — generalization failure modes: ask what fails when training on a single texture and whether data augmentation helps.  
- Question — computational cost: request wall-clock training times and sample budgets per scenario for all methods to quantify efficiency advantages.  
- Overall verdict: strong, well-executed paper with clear contributions and substantial empirical impact; recommend acceptance.
