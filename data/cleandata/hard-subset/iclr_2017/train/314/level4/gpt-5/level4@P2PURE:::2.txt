This paper proposes an on-policy deep RL method augmented with auxiliary intrinsic variables.
- The method is a specific instance of a universal value function–based approach, and the authors cite the appropriate prior work. The main claimed technical contribution appears to be distilling existing ideas to address 3D navigation problems. The contributions should be stated more clearly in the abstract and introduction.
- I would like to see an analysis of failure modes. Under what conditions does the model struggle to generalize to changing goals? There are also conceptual concerns: because this is an on-policy method, catastrophic forgetting is likely if the agent does not repeatedly train on goals from the distant past.
- Since the primary contribution is integrating several key ideas and demonstrating empirical gains, I would have liked to see results on other domains such as Atari (potentially using the ROM as intrinsic variables).

Overall, the paper demonstrates clear empirical advantages for the proposed formulations, and the experimental insights may be valuable for future agents.
