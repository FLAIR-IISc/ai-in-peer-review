- Proposes supervised learning for sensorimotor control by predicting future low-dimensional measurement vectors conditioned on observation, action, and goal, selecting actions that align predictions with the goal.
- Uses Monte Carlo–style regression (no bootstrapping) and dense vector-valued feedback to stabilize and accelerate learning from pixels.
- Trains without fixed goals and supports goal-conditioned behavior at test time, including generalization to unseen goals.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance in ViZDoom, winning the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch and outperforming DQN, A3C, and DSR on harder tasks.
- Paper is clearly written, well-situated relative to RL, UVFA, and successor-representation literature, and includes solid ablations.
- Ablations show multi-measurement and multi-horizon prediction improve performance versus scalar rewards and single-horizon prediction.
- Architecture uses a goal-conditional predictor with action- and expectation-stream decomposition, a transparent and reasonable design choice.
- Generalization: models trained with texture variation generalize to new environments; goal-agnostic training adapts to new goals nearly as well as goal-specific training.
- Caveat: Approach depends on access to meaningful, low-dimensional measurements, which may not be available in all domains.
- Caveat: Training uses a small replay buffer and ε-greedy data while optimizing a policy-dependent Monte Carlo objective, introducing a mild off-policy approximation needing discussion.
- Request to clarify off-policy effects, potential bias from replay data, and whether larger buffers cause instability.
- Suggest explicitly highlighting that baselines use scalarized rewards while DFP leverages vector-valued supervision, and recommend a baseline with auxiliary measurement prediction.
- Recommend discussing measurement selection in new domains, robustness to noisy/partial measurements, and limitations with only sparse rewards.
- Ask for sensitivity analysis to goal vector normalization/constraint, choice of temporal offsets, and weights for horizons.
- Recommend discussing/reactive policy limits and whether recurrence or memory improves performance on harder tasks.
- Request compute and sample efficiency reporting (wall-clock training time, steps/sec) and hyperparameter tuning effort.
- Encourage releasing code and training configs to improve reproducibility.
- Questions: size of replay buffer vs episode length, parameter update frequency, effects of larger buffers, and signs of bias when ε decays.
- Questions: whether goal vectors are normalized/constrained and how goal distribution affects goal-agnostic training.
- Questions: sensitivity to temporal offset choices {1,2,4,8,16,32}, weighting choices, longer horizons, or learned weights.
- Questions: how to design or learn measurements in domains without explicit stats, and whether learned auxiliary features were tried.
- Questions: whether baselines augmented with auxiliary measurement prediction were evaluated to isolate signal vs objective benefits.
- Questions: degree of overfitting to layout under texture randomization and performance with procedurally generated layouts.
- Questions: how DFP performs with very sparse/delayed/noisy measurements and whether benefits persist with fewer measurements.
