This paper proposes an on-policy deep RL approach augmented with auxiliary intrinsic variables.
- The method can be seen as a specific instance of a universal value function framework, and the authors cite the pertinent prior work. Arguably the main technical contribution is consolidating existing ideas to address 3D navigation. The contributions should be articulated more explicitly in the abstract and introduction.
- I would like to see an analysis of failure cases. Under what conditions does the model struggle to generalize to changing goals? There are additional conceptual concerns—because the method is on-policy, it risks catastrophic forgetting unless the agent continually trains on goals from the distant past.
- Since the core contribution is integrating key ideas and demonstrating empirical gains, I would be interested in results on other domains such as Atari (potentially using the ROM as intrinsic variables).
Overall, the paper presents clear empirical advantages for the proposed formulations, and the experimental insights could be valuable for future agents.
