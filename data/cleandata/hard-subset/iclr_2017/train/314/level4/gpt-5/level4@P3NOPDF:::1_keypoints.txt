- Summary: Supervised Monte Carlo-trained predictor forecasts future low-dimensional measurements conditioned on action and goal; agent selects actions maximizing predicted outcomes and achieves strong 3D first-person performance (won Visual Doom competition).

- Contribution: Introduces a goal-parameterized predictive model trained with supervised Monte Carlo regression instead of TD bootstrapping.

- Contribution: Leverages environment-provided low-dimensional measurements as prediction targets, decoupling them from policy learning signals.

- Contribution: Uses a simple action-selection rule that optimizes arbitrary test-time goal vectors via the predictive model.

- Contribution: Provides empirical comparisons to deep RL baselines and demonstrates limited zero-shot adaptation to new goals.

- Relation to prior work: Positions among model-free deep RL and successor-feature approaches, differing by avoiding TD bootstrapping and focusing on measurement prediction rather than full-state or reward-only approaches.

- Empirical evaluation: Strong results on raw-pixel 3D tasks, outperforming baselines, demonstrating test-time goal adjustment, and validated by competition performance.

- Strength: Clear, simple training objective and decision rule with strong empirical performance from vision input.

- Strength: Demonstrated generalization to modified goal vectors and careful literature comparison.

- Concern: Off-policy bias—Monte Carlo regression assumes on-policy rollouts but training uses a replay buffer of older policies; needs discussion and mitigation strategies.

- Concern: Reliance on privileged environment measurements may unfairly advantage the method and limit applicability; paper should state the assumption, justify comparisons, and suggest workarounds.

- Concern: External goal specification offloads task design to the experimenter; discuss mechanisms for acquiring or adapting goals (e.g., meta-learning, preference learning).

- Concern: Limited zero-shot generalization—require analysis of how performance degrades as goals deviate and as measurement dimensionality/diversity changes.

- Question: How sensitive is performance to the choice, number, or corruption/removal of specific measurements?

- Question: Is there measurable bias from off-policy Monte Carlo training via the replay buffer, and how is it mitigated?

- Question: Can the method be extended to environments lacking explicit measurements by learning a latent measurement space jointly?

- Question: How does sample efficiency compare to TD-based methods in these environments?

- Overall assessment: Strong, practical approach with clear benefits and competition-validated performance; acceptance recommended pending clearer discussion of privileged measurements and replay-based off-policy issues.
