This paper introduces an on-policy method for predicting future intrinsic measurements. All experiments are conducted in Doom (specifically vizDoom). Rather than predicting only win/loss or the number of frags (score), the model is trained to predict sequences of triplets (health, ammunition, frags), each weighted by corresponding sequences of goal triplets provided as input. Adjusting the goal-triplet weights offers a mechanism to guide exploration. At test time, actions are selected by maximizing the long-term goal alone.

The results are impressive: this model won the 2016 vizDoom competition. The experimental section appears sound:
- The paper compares DFP with A3C, DQN, and attempts a comparison with DSR (a recent related approach by Kulkarni et al., 2016). DFP outperforms the other methods, or matches them when they reach a ceiling/optimum (as with A3C in scenario D1).
- An ablation study supports the claim that the paper’s “added complexity” is indeed useful.

Predicting intrinsic motivation (Singh et al., 2004), auxiliary variables, and forward modeling are well-studied areas in reinforcement learning. The December 4th revision adequately cites prior work, even if it is not entirely exhaustive.

A few minor comments on presentation:
- Doom is described as a 3D environment, whereas it is actually a 2D environment (height is not a discriminative/actionable dimension) rendered in “fake” 3D.
- The notation “P” in (2) (and thereafter) may be misleading, as it denotes prediction rather than probability (its usual interpretation).
- The double use of “j” in (6), even with different fonts, may cause confusion.
- The results tables should repeat the units of measurement, especially given their heterogeneity in Table 1.

Overall, this is a clear accept. While one could argue for experiments in additional environments or note that the novelty is limited, I believe papers that are both correct (no-nonsense, experimentally sound on Doom, with an appendix that enables reproducibility) and milestone (vizDoom winner) should be published.
