This paper proposes an on-policy deep RL method that augments learning with auxiliary intrinsic variables.

- Positioning and contributions: The approach can be interpreted as a special case of universal value function approximators (UVFA), and the paper appropriately cites the relevant prior work. The main technical novelty appears to be the careful integration and distillation of existing ideas to address 3D navigation tasks. The contributions should be articulated more explicitly and prominently in the abstract and introduction to clarify what is new versus what is adopted from prior literature.

- Failure modes and generalization: I would like to see an analysis of failure cases. Under what conditions does the method fail to generalize to new or changing goals? Given that the algorithm is on-policy, it seems vulnerable to catastrophic forgetting unless the training procedure continues to revisit goals encountered in the distant past. A discussion or empirical study of this limitation would be valuable.

- Breadth of evaluation: Since the primary contribution is the integration of several ideas yielding empirical improvements, it would strengthen the paper to demonstrate results beyond 3D navigation. For example, evaluations on Atari (potentially using ROM identifiers or other metadata as intrinsic variables) would better establish generality.

Overall, the paper provides clear empirical evidence in favor of the proposed formulation, and the experimental insights are likely to be useful for designing future agents.
