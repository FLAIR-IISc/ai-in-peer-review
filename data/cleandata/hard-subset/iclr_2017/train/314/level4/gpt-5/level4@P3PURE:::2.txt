This paper presents an on-policy deep reinforcement learning method augmented with auxiliary intrinsic variables.

Summary and positioning:
- The method appears to be a special case of a universal value function–based approach, and the authors cite the appropriate prior work. Perhaps the most substantive claimed contribution is the distillation and integration of several existing ideas to address 3D navigation problems. The contributions should be stated more explicitly and crisply in the abstract and introduction.

Concerns and questions:
- I would like to see analysis of failure modes. Under what circumstances does the model struggle to generalize to changing goals?
- There are conceptual concerns stemming from the on-policy nature of the method: catastrophic forgetting is likely if the agent does not repeatedly train on goals encountered in the distant past.
- Since the main contribution is the integration of key ideas with demonstrated empirical benefits, it would strengthen the paper to include results beyond 3D navigation, e.g., on Atari (perhaps using the ROM as intrinsic variables).

Overall assessment:
- The paper demonstrates clear empirical advantages of the proposed formulation, and the experimental insights are likely to be valuable for future agents. Clarifying the contributions upfront, presenting failure analyses, and expanding evaluation to additional domains would further solidify the paper.
