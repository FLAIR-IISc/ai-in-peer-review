- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): select actions by predicting future intrinsic measurement changes conditioned on sensory input, candidate action, and a goal vector, then maximize goal-weighted predicted changes.
- Uses dense, vector-valued feedback instead of sparse scalar rewards, enabling training without a fixed goal and dynamic goal specification at test time.
- Architecture: perception, measurement, and goal encoders with a prediction head split into expectation and action streams, predicting multiple future horizons.
- Strong empirical results in ViZDoom: outperforms DQN/A3C on hard tasks, generalizes across textures and goals, ablation shows multivariate and multi-horizon benefits, and won the Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Decision: Accept.
- Primary reason 1: Strong empirical evidence DFP outperforms competitive deep RL baselines with clear generalization.
- Primary reason 2: Simple, well-motivated, easy-to-implement formulation with practical impact.
- Problem/approach fit: Well-motivated use of dense intrinsic measurements to replace sparse-reward RL with supervised multi-horizon prediction.
- Empirical strength: Substantial gains on complex tasks, convincing ablations on measurement multiplicity and horizons, solid generalization and external validation.
- Position in literature: Related to UVFA, GVFs, and Monte Carlo prediction but advances practical, scalable pixel-based goal conditioning with useful architectural choices.
- Clarify novelty/scope: More explicitly contrast DFP with UVFA, DSR, and GVFs and state what is new versus reused.
- Discuss failure modes/limitations: Address sparse or uninformative measurements, severe partial observability, and very long-horizon delayed effects.
- Catastrophic forgetting and goal coverage: Analyze forgetting when goal distributions shift and consider curricula, balanced replay, or relabeling to improve coverage.
- Broader evaluation: Test on another domain (e.g., Atari), use engineered indicators or score-only settings, and evaluate recurrent variants for partial observability.
- Sensitivity and ablations: Report sensitivity to choice/number of measurements, horizon settings and weights, ε schedule, replay size, and expectation/action split.
- Baselines: Add UVFA-style baseline with identical perception/compute; compare to A3C/DQN with auxiliary tasks; match compute/training budgets and report variance.
- Practicality: Discuss measurement selection when only sparse rewards exist and extensions to continuous action spaces.
- Presentation: Fix citation formatting/typos, define “frags”, and release code/models if possible.
- Measurement design question: How sensitive is performance to which measurements are predicted and to their noise/scale; can proxies be learned when only sparse rewards exist?
- Horizons question: Why chosen offsets/weights (1,2,4,8,16,32) and have you tried learning weights or longer horizons?
- Goal generalization question: How far can the model extrapolate in goal space and are there pathologies for conflicting or extreme goals?
- Replay and policy question: How sensitive are results to replay size/staleness and would prioritized or goal-balanced replay help?
- Architecture question: Measured gain from expectation/action split versus a single head and interactions with action count?
- Partial observability question: Effects of frame stacking or recurrence (e.g., LSTM) on performance and goal generalization?
- Robustness question: How does DFP handle measurement drift, missing measurements at test time, or online adaptation to measurement changes?
- Competition details request: Provide training details for the winning entry (data budget, randomization, hyperparameters) and note differences from the paper setup.
