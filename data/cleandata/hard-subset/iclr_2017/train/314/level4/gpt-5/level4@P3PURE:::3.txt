The paper presents an on-policy approach for predicting future intrinsic measurements. All experiments are conducted in the Doom environment (specifically vizDoom). Instead of predicting only win/loss or the number of frags (score), the model predicts sequences of triplets (health, ammunition, frags), weighted by corresponding sequences of “goal” triplets provided as input. Adjusting the weights of the goal triplet serves to guide exploration; at test time, one can act by maximizing the long-term goal alone.

The results are impressive: this model won the 2016 vizDoom competition. The experimental section appears sound:
- There are comparisons of DFP with A3C, DQN, and an attempt to compare with DSR (a recent similar approach from Kulkarni et al., 2016). DFP outperforms the other approaches, or matches them when they reach a ceiling/optimum (as with A3C in scenario D1).
- An ablation study supports the claim that the model’s added components are useful.

Predicting intrinsic motivation (Singh et al., 2004), using auxiliary variables, and forward modeling are well-studied areas in reinforcement learning. The December 4th revision adequately references prior work, even if it is not completely exhaustive.

A few comments (nitpicks) on presentation:
- Doom is described as a 3D environment, whereas it is actually a 2D environment (height is not a discriminative/actionable dimension) presented in fake 3D.
- The use of “P” in equation (2) (and subsequently) may be misleading, as it denotes prediction rather than probability (its usual meaning).
- The double use of “j” (albeit in different fonts) in equation (6) may cause confusion.
- Results tables could repeat the units of measurement, particularly given the heterogeneity in Table 1.

Overall, I recommend a clear accept. One could argue that experiments should span additional environments or that the novelty is limited, but I believe that papers that are correct, experimentally sound on Doom, and include sufficient details for reproducibility—and that represent a milestone (vizDoom winner)—should be published.
