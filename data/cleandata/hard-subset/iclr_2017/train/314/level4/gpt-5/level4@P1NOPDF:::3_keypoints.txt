- Proposes an on-policy method that predicts sequences of intrinsic measurement triplets (health, ammunition, frags) weighted by input “goal” triplets to guide exploration and action selection.
- Experiments are conducted exclusively in VizDoom/Doom.
- Model can act by maximizing long-term goal vectors at test time.
- Achieves strong results—won the 2016 VizDoom competition.
- Empirically outperforms DQN and A3C (matches A3C ceiling in scenario D1) and attempts comparison with DSR; ablation study shows each component contributes meaningfully.
- Prior work on intrinsic motivation, auxiliary tasks, and forward modeling is cited adequately in the revision, though not exhaustively.
- Paper is experimentally solid and reproducible (appendix supports reproducibility).
- Minor form issues: Doom described as 3D though effectively 2D, misleading use of “P” in eq. (2) (prediction vs. probability), confusing reuse of “j” in eq. (6), and tables should repeat measurement units.
- Overall recommendation: clear accept—correct, experimentally sound, somewhat incremental but a milestone due to VizDoom win; additional environments could strengthen generality.
