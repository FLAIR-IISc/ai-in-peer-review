- Introduces a new Deep RL algorithm that predicts low-dimensional measurements (e.g., ammo, health) from raw sensory inputs and conditions predictions on a provided goal vector and the current action.
- After training, selects actions by maximizing predicted outcomes under the given goal, enabling goal-directed policy selection without retraining.
- Uses fully supervised Monte Carlo regression for future-measurement prediction rather than TD bootstrapping; learning is not temporally linked across states.
- Contrasts with successor features: learning is supervised and lacks TD-based bootstrapping or temporal consistency between predictions.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance in novel 3D environments from raw inputs and generalization across goals and environments.
- Won the Visual Doom AI competition, providing a notable empirical validation.
- Main contributions: emphasis on Monte Carlo estimation, use of low-dimensional measurement targets, parameterized goals, and extensive empirical comparison to strong baselines.
- Shows that learned policies can adapt to limited goal changes without additional training.
- Paper is clearly written and presents compelling empirical results of significant interest.
- Minor critique: supervised Monte Carlo training assumes on-policy returns, yet training uses replay buffer data from earlier policies—this approximation should be discussed.
- Minor critique: method leverages extra metadata (which parts of sensory input to predict); limitations when such measurements are unavailable and the advantage over competing methods should be stated explicitly.
