This paper proposes an on-policy method that predicts sequences of future intrinsic measurements, rather than directly estimating value functions. All experiments are conducted in VizDoom. Instead of predicting only win/loss or frags, the model predicts sequences of triplets (health, ammunition, frags) weighted by input “goal” vectors. Varying the goal weights provides a straightforward mechanism for guided exploration; at test time, the agent can act by maximizing the long-term weighted goal.

The results are strong: the approach won the 2016 VizDoom competition. The experimental methodology appears solid:
- The paper compares DFP with A3C, DQN, and attempts a comparison with DSR (Kulkarni et al., 2016), a related approach. DFP generally outperforms the baselines, or matches them when they saturate (e.g., A3C in scenario D1).
- An ablation study indicates that each component of the proposed architecture contributes meaningfully to performance.

The ideas of predicting intrinsic signals (e.g., intrinsic motivation; Singh et al., 2004), using auxiliary tasks, and forward modeling are well established in reinforcement learning. The December 4th revision appropriately cites prior work, even if the coverage is not exhaustive.

Minor comments on presentation:
- Doom is described as a 3D environment; more precisely, it is a 2D environment rendered to appear 3D, with height not serving as a meaningful control dimension.
- The symbol “P” in equation (2) and later denotes prediction rather than probability, which could be confusing given standard notation.
- Equation (6) uses “j” in two roles (albeit in different fonts); this may mislead readers.
- Results tables should repeat measurement units, especially when heterogeneous metrics appear together (e.g., Table 1).

Overall assessment: clear accept. While one might argue that experiments are confined to a single domain and that the incremental novelty is modest, the work is careful, empirically convincing on VizDoom, and accompanied by sufficient detail for reproducibility. Combined with the competitive milestone (VizDoom 2016 winner), these factors warrant publication.
