- Paper introduces a new deep RL algorithm that outperforms prior methods in novel 3D environments from raw sensory inputs and won the Visual Doom AI competition.
- Core idea: supervise predictions of low-dimensional measurements (e.g., ammo, health) conditioned on a goal vector and current action.
- Decision rule: choose the action that maximizes the predicted outcome under the given goal after supervised training.
- Learning is purely supervised Monte Carlo regression, with no temporal-difference constraints linking successive states.
- Key novelties: emphasis on Monte Carlo estimation, use of low-dimensional measurement targets, parameterized (supplied) goals, and empirical comparisons to prior methods.
- Empirical claim: the algorithm generalizes across goals and environments and can adapt to limited goal changes without additional training.
- Presentation: paper is clearly written and empirically compelling, likely of significant interest.
- Concern: supervised training assumes on-policy returns but uses replay buffers—this on-policy approximation should be discussed.
- Concern: method relies on extra metadata (measurements) not available to compared methods, and this dependency/limitation should be made explicit.
