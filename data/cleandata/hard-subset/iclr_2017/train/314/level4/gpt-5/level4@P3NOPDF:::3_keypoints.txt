- Proposes an on-policy method that predicts sequences of future intrinsic measurements conditioned on a goal vector; applied to vizDoom and won the 2016 competition.
- Strong empirical performance: outperforms DQN and A3C across scenarios and matches A3C when it saturates; comparison to DSR is useful.
- Careful experimental design with ablations showing each proposed component materially improves performance.
- Clear problem formulation: goal-conditioned prediction of task-relevant measurements is a sensible alternative to value estimation in high-dimensional, partially observable environments.
- Adequate positioning relative to intrinsic motivation, auxiliary tasks, and forward modeling in the literature.
- Reproducibility and impact: appendix contains implementation details and competition success underscores practical significance.
- Limited evaluation scope: all experiments are in vizDoom, so broader validation is needed to claim generality.
- Perceived novelty concern: approach ties to existing auxiliary prediction and goal-conditioning ideas and may be viewed more as an effective integration than a fundamentally new concept.
- Environment description correction: Doom is effectively a 2D environment rendered to appear 3D; height is not an actionable dimension.
- Notation concern: using P for prediction is potentially misleading (commonly used for probability); suggest a different symbol or clarification.
- Indexing clarity: double use of j in Equation (6) is confusing; rename one index for readability.
- Table clarity: reiterate units for all reported measurements (especially heterogeneous metrics) to avoid ambiguity.
- Overall assessment: clear accept—method is simple, coherent, well-executed with rigorous evaluation and practical impact; recommend acceptance.
