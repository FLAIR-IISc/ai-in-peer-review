- Proposes an on-policy method that predicts sequences of future intrinsic measurements (health, ammunition, frags) rather than estimating value functions.  
- Predictions are weighted by input “goal” vectors, enabling simple, goal-conditioned behavior and guided exploration.  
- Varying goal weights at test time allows the agent to act by maximizing long-term weighted goals.  
- All experiments are conducted in VizDoom; approach won the 2016 VizDoom competition.  
- Empirical results are strong: DFP generally outperforms or matches baselines (A3C, DQN) and compares favorably to related DSR.  
- Experimental methodology is solid, including comparisons to baselines and an ablation study showing each component matters.  
- Builds on established ideas (intrinsic signals, auxiliary tasks, forward modeling) and cites prior work sufficiently in the revision.  
- Concern: experiments are limited to a single domain (VizDoom), and the methodological novelty is modest.  
- Minor presentation issues: Doom is 2D rendered as 3D (height not a control dimension).  
- Notational confusions: “P” denotes prediction not probability, and symbol “j” is used inconsistently in Eq. (6).  
- Suggestion: results tables should repeat measurement units when heterogeneous metrics are combined.  
- Overall recommendation: clear accept — careful, reproducible, empirically convincing work justified by the VizDoom competition win.
