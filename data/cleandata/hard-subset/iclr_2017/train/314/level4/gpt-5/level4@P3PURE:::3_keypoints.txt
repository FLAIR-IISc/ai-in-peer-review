- Paper proposes an on-policy method that predicts future intrinsic measurements (triplets: health, ammunition, frags) weighted by input “goal” triplets to guide exploration and action selection.
- All experiments are conducted in the vizDoom environment; at test time actions can maximize long-term goal alone.
- Method achieved strong empirical results, winning the 2016 vizDoom competition.
- Experimental evaluation compares DFP to A3C, DQN, and a DSR attempt; DFP outperforms or matches baselines where they reach optimal performance.
- Ablation study shows the proposed components meaningfully contribute to performance.
- Prior work on intrinsic motivation, auxiliary variables, and forward modeling is acknowledged; the December 4th revision provides adequate referencing.
- Presentation nit: Doom is inaccurately described as a 3D environment when it is essentially 2D presented in faux 3D.
- Presentation nit: Using “P” to denote prediction (rather than probability) in equation (2) may be misleading.
- Presentation nit: Reusing the symbol “j” (in different fonts) in equation (6) can cause confusion.
- Presentation nit: Results tables should repeat measurement units due to heterogeneous metrics in Table 1.
- Overall recommendation: clear accept—despite limited novelty or single-environment evaluation, the work is correct, experimentally sound, reproducible, and a notable milestone.
