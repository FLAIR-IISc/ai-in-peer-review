- Introduces an on-policy method for predicting future intrinsic measurements, evaluated in vizDoom.  
- Model predicts sequences of triplets (health, ammunition, frags) weighted by input goal-triplet sequences.  
- Goal-triplet weighting guides exploration; actions at test time are chosen by maximizing the long-term goal.  
- Empirical results are strong: the model won the 2016 vizDoom competition.  
- Experiments compare DFP to A3C, DQN, and DSR; DFP outperforms or matches baselines (e.g., matches A3C in scenario D1).  
- Ablation study shows the added model complexity provides measurable benefit.  
- Prior work on intrinsic motivation, auxiliary variables, and forward modeling is cited adequately in the December 4th revision, though not exhaustively.  
- Presentation issue: Doom is described as a 3D environment but is actually 2D rendered in fake 3D.  
- Notational issue: using “P” to denote prediction may mislead readers who expect probability.  
- Notational issue: the double use of “j” in equation (6) could cause confusion.  
- Results tables should repeat units of measurement, especially given heterogeneous units in Table 1.  
- Overall verdict: clear accept — experimentally sound, reproducible appendix, and a milestone result despite limited novelty and single-environment testing.
