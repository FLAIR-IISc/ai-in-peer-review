The paper presents an approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments, framing the problem as supervised learning through prediction of future intrinsic measurements. The method utilizes a high-dimensional sensory stream (visual input from Doom) and a lower-dimensional measurement stream (health, ammunition, frags) to establish a rich supervisory signal for training a control model. The model is trained to predict the effect of actions on future measurements, enabling learning without a fixed goal and facilitating dynamic goal pursuit at test time. The results demonstrate strong performance, culminating in winning the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition.

**Accept.** The paper addresses a challenging problem in reinforcement learning – sensorimotor control from raw sensory input – with a novel and effective approach. The experimental results are compelling, particularly the demonstrated success in the Visual Doom AI Competition, and convincingly support the claims made by the authors.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly positioning itself within the broader context of reinforcement learning and highlighting the limitations of traditional reward-based formulations. The authors effectively argue for the benefits of their approach, emphasizing the rich supervision provided by the temporal structure of sensory and measurement streams. The experimental setup is thorough, including comparisons to state-of-the-art deep reinforcement learning methods (DQN, A3C, DSR) across multiple scenarios of increasing difficulty. The ablation study provides valuable insights into the importance of different components of the proposed architecture, and the generalization experiments demonstrate the robustness of the learned policies. The results consistently demonstrate superior performance compared to existing methods, offering strong support for the paper’s claims.

To further improve the paper, I suggest the following:
*   Clarify that Doom, while presented in a pseudo-3D manner, is fundamentally a 2D environment regarding agent action and perception.
*   Reconsider the use of "p" to denote prediction, as it could be misinterpreted as probability.
*   Address the potential for confusion arising from the dual use of "j" in equation (6).
*   Enhance the readability of the results tables by explicitly stating the units of the measurements.
*   While the related work is generally well-covered, a discussion of the connections to intrinsic motivation and curiosity-driven learning might be beneficial.

I have a few clarifying questions for the authors:
*   In the ablation study, how sensitive is the performance to the specific temporal offsets (1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32) chosen for predicting future measurements? Was any investigation done into the optimal selection of these offsets?
*   The paper mentions the possibility of using a classification loss for categorical measurements. Were any such measurements considered during the experiments, and if so, why was the regression loss ultimately preferred?
*   Could the authors elaborate on the computational resources required for training the models, particularly the D4-tx-L model? This information would be helpful for reproducibility and benchmarking.
*   Regarding the goal-agnostic training regime, what is the rationale behind sampling goal vectors uniformly from [-1, 1] versus [0, 1]? Does this choice significantly impact performance or generalization?
