- Paper frames sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future measurements from raw sensory input.
- Leverages co-temporal structure of sensory streams and agent measurements to provide rich supervisory signal without reward-based RL.
- Demonstrates strong empirical results in the Doom environment, achieving state-of-the-art performance and winning the Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Review recommends rejection despite strong results due to insufficient clarity on core contributions and lack of discussion on limitations.
- Contribution seen as effective integration of existing techniques rather than a fundamentally novel theoretical advance.
- Abstract/introduction need clearer articulation of what is specifically novel (formulation, architecture, or combination).
- Lacks critical discussion of potential failure modes, e.g., catastrophic forgetting from on-policy learning.
- Evaluation is limited to Doom; absence of other benchmarks (e.g., Atari) reduces confidence in generalizability.
- Ablation studies are useful but would benefit from more detailed hyperparameter reporting for each variant.
- Suggest evaluating goal generalization more thoroughly and providing visualizations/quantitative performance across varied goals.
- Recommend discussing mitigation strategies for on-policy drawbacks and other limitations.
- Ask authors how on-policy learning affects generalization and whether they explored techniques to prevent catastrophic forgetting.
- Ask about applicability where meaningful measurements are hard to define, comparative computational cost versus deep RL, and sensitivity to temporal offset hyperparameters (τ1,...,τn).
