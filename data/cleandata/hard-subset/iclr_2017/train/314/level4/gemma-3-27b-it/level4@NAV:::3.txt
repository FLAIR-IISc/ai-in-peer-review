The paper introduces an on-policy method for predicting future intrinsic state variables. Evaluations are conducted within the vizDoom environment, where the model is trained to forecast sequences of (health, ammunition, frags), weighted by input "goal" triplets. This weighting scheme serves as a mechanism for exploration control; at test time, action selection is driven by maximizing long-term goal achievement.

The reported results are strong, highlighted by the model's winning performance in the 2016 vizDoom competition. The experimental validation is thorough, including comparisons against A3C, DQN, and DSR (Kulkarni et al., 2016), demonstrating superior performance or reaching comparable optimal levels (as seen with A3C in scenario D1). An ablation study provides evidence supporting the contribution of each component of the proposed model. 

The work builds upon established research in intrinsic motivation (Singh et al. 2004), auxiliary task learning, and forward modeling within reinforcement learning. The December 4th revision provides adequate, though not necessarily comprehensive, citation of relevant prior work.

I have a few minor suggestions regarding presentation:
- The environment is more accurately described as 2D, despite its 3D rendering; the vertical dimension does not represent a meaningful degree of freedom.
- The notation “P” in equation (2) and elsewhere might be misinterpreted as a probability, given its conventional usage.
- The dual use of the index “j” in equation (6), though visually distinct, could cause confusion.
- Tables presenting results could benefit from explicitly including units for each measured quantity, particularly given the heterogeneous units in Table 1.

Overall, I recommend accepting this paper. While expansions to other environments or considerations of incremental novelty could be suggested, the paper is technically sound, experimentally validated on a challenging benchmark (Doom), and includes an appendix supporting reproducibility – characteristics of both a valuable and impactful contribution, as underscored by its success in the vizDoom competition.
