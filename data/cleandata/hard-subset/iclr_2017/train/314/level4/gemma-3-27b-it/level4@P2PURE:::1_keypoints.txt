- Introduces a novel deep RL algorithm that predicts low-dimensional game measurements conditioned on a fixed goal vector and the current action, choosing actions that maximize predicted outcomes.  
- Operates directly from raw sensory data and demonstrates improved generalization across unseen 3D environments and goals.  
- Was the winning entry in the Visual Doom AI competition, showing strong empirical performance.  
- Uses supervised Monte Carlo regression (not TD learning) to train predictions, differing from successor feature and TD-based approaches.  
- Key contributions: emphasis on Monte Carlo estimation, exploitation of low-dimensional measurements, use of parametrized goals, and thorough empirical comparisons.  
- Shows learned policies can adapt to limited goal changes without additional training.  
- Paper is clearly written and presents compelling empirical results of likely interest to the community.  
- Minor concern: training is described as on-policy but uses a replay buffer, creating a distribution mismatch that should be discussed.  
- Minor concern: algorithm relies on supplementary metadata about which measurements are useful; this advantage over baselines and limitations when such signals are unavailable should be explicitly acknowledged.
