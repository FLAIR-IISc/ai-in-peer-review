- Introduces an on-policy method (DFP) that predicts future intrinsic measurement sequences (health, ammunition, frags) weighted by input “goal” triplets to drive exploration and long-term goal maximization.  
- Experiments conducted in vizDoom; agent acts by maximizing predicted long-term goal achievement at test time.  
- Method achieved first place in the 2016 vizDoom competition, demonstrating strong empirical performance.  
- Experimental evaluation includes comparisons to A3C, DQN, and an attempted comparison to DSR; DFP consistently outperforms or matches baselines.  
- Includes an ablation study demonstrating the contribution of each model component.  
- Builds on established RL areas: intrinsic motivation, auxiliary prediction, and forward modeling; December 4th revision cites relevant prior work though not exhaustively.  
- Minor presentation issue: describing Doom as a 3D environment is inaccurate—it's effectively 2D with a pseudo-3D perspective.  
- Notation concern: using "P" in equations to denote predictions is misleading since "P" commonly denotes probability.  
- Potential confusion from reusing the index "j" (in different fonts) within equation (6).  
- Results tables should explicitly state units of measurement, especially Table 1 with heterogeneous units.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — technically sound, experimentally validated on a challenging platform, despite limited novelty or additional-environment experiments.
