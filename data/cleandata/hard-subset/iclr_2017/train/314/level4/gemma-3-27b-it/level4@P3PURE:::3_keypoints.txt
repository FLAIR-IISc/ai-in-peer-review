- Proposes an on-policy method that predicts future intrinsic measurements.  
- Experiments run exclusively in vizDoom, predicting sequences of (health, ammunition, frags) triplets.  
- Predictions are weighted by input “goal” triplets, and changing weights provides exploration/guidance.  
- At test time the agent acts to maximize long-term goal achievement.  
- Strong empirical results: won the 2016 vizDoom competition.  
- Provides comparisons to A3C, DQN, and an attempted comparison to DSR; DFP outperforms or matches baselines.  
- Ablation study shows the model’s added complexity yields measurable benefits.  
- Prior work on intrinsic motivation, auxiliary variables, and forward modeling is cited adequately though coverage isn’t exhaustive.  
- Presentation issues: Doom is more accurately pseudo-3D (2D with 3D projection), not true 3D.  
- Notation problems: “P” suggests probability but denotes prediction; repeated use of index “j” (in different fonts) is confusing.  
- Results tables should explicitly repeat units given heterogeneous measures.  
- Overall recommendation: accept—rigorous, reproducible, and impactful despite limited environment diversity and modest novelty concerns.
