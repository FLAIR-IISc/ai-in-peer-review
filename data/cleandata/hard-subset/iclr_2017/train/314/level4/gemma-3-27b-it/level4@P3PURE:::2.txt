This paper presents an on-policy deep reinforcement learning method incorporating additional auxiliary intrinsic variables. The method represents a specific instance of a universal value function-based approach, and the authors appropriately cite relevant prior work. A key claimed technical contribution of this paper is the distillation of existing ideas to effectively address 3D navigation problems. However, I believe the contributions would benefit from more explicit articulation in the abstract and introduction.

I would have appreciated an analysis of the method’s failure modes. Under what conditions does the model struggle to generalize to novel goals? Furthermore, a conceptual concern arises from the on-policy nature of the algorithm: catastrophic forgetting is a potential issue if the agent does not continually retrain on goals from the distant past. 

Given that the primary contribution of this work lies in integrating several key ideas and demonstrating empirical improvements, I would have found results on additional domains, such as Atari (potentially utilizing ROM states as intrinsic variables), to be insightful. 

Overall, this paper demonstrates a clear empirical advantage to the proposed formulation, and the experimental insights derived from this work may prove valuable for the development of future agents.
