- Presents a supervised-learning approach that predicts future measurements from raw sensory input and achieves state-of-the-art performance, winning the Full Deathmatch in the Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Reviewer recommends rejection because the work lacks sufficient novelty and reads as an incremental integration of existing techniques rather than a paradigm shift.
- Approach builds heavily on established methods (supervised learning, temporal-difference ideas, function approximation) and mainly demonstrates successful application to a specific domain.
- The core idea of using future measurements as supervisory signal is interesting and well-motivated, with clear connections to prior prediction and temporal-difference literature.
- Experimental setup is rigorous and results are impressive; ablation studies highlight the importance of vectorial feedback and multi-step prediction.
- Paper lacks deeper theoretical analysis (e.g., convergence properties) and formal comparisons to existing reinforcement-learning algorithms.
- On-policy formulation raises concerns about catastrophic forgetting that are not adequately addressed.
- Evaluation is limited to the Doom environment, reducing evidence for generalizability; omitting other benchmarks (e.g., Atari) weakens broad applicability claims.
- Discussion of failure modes and scenarios where the model fails to generalize is minimal and needs expansion.
- Contributions should be reframed to emphasize empirical integration and demonstrated benefits rather than overstated novelty.
- Recommended improvements: address catastrophic forgetting and mitigation strategies, test generalization across more environments, analyze failure modes, expand theoretical discussion, and report hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Reviewer questions for authors: what catastrophic-forgetting mitigations were considered; how performance changes with temporal distance to goals; computational cost compared to SOTA RL; which goal types/environments cause consistent failures; and details/rationale of the hyperparameter search.
