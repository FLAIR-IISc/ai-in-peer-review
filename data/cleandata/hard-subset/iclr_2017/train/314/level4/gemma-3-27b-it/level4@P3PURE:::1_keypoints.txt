- Introduces a new deep RL algorithm that improves performance in novel 3D environments from raw sensory data and won the Visual Doom AI competition.  
- Core idea: supervise prediction of low-dimensional measurements (e.g., ammo, health) conditioned on a provided goal vector and the current action.  
- Action selection is done by choosing the action that maximizes the predicted outcome with respect to the specified goal (argmax over predictions).  
- Learning is supervised via Monte Carlo regression rather than temporal-difference updates or successor feature formulations.  
- Key contributions: emphasis on Monte Carlo estimation, use of low-dimensional measurements, explicit goal parametrization, and comprehensive empirical comparisons.  
- Empirically demonstrates learned policies generalize to limited changes in goals and environments without additional training.  
- Paper is well-written and presents compelling empirical results of significant community interest.  
- Minor concern: Monte Carlo regression assumes on-policy returns but training uses a replay buffer of past policies, creating a sampling/assumption mismatch that should be discussed.  
- Minor concern: the method leverages metadata (which sensory measurements to predict) not available to compared baselines; limitations in environments lacking such measurements should be explicitly addressed.
