- Introduces an algorithm that uses low-dimensional measurements as supervised prediction targets conditioned on a goal vector and the current action.  
- Action selection is done by choosing the action that maximizes the predicted outcome for a specified goal; learning is supervised Monte Carlo regression, not TD learning.  
- Shows strong empirical performance in novel 3D environments and won the Visual Doom AI competition.  
- Demonstrates improved generalization across goals and environments without further training.  
- Main contributions: focus on Monte Carlo estimation, use of low-dimensional measurements, parameterized goals, and comprehensive empirical comparisons.  
- Paper is well-written and the empirical results are compelling and of high interest.  
- Concern: Supervised training assumes on-policy data but uses a replay buffer, creating a mismatch that should be discussed.  
- Concern: Method benefits from metadata specifying which measurements to predict—an advantage not available to compared algorithms and a potential limitation.  
- Suggest expanding discussion/analysis of bias introduced by using a replay buffer with Monte Carlo regression and its impact.  
- Suggest explicitly acknowledging limitations when meaningful measurements are unavailable and exploring adaptations for such environments.  
- Recommend clarifying the relationship to imitation learning and potential synergies or differences.  
- Question: Please provide a detailed analysis of the impact and bias from using a replay buffer with Monte Carlo regression.  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the choice of measurements, and are there strategies to identify or learn them when not obvious?  
- Question: How could the goal vector be learned rather than provided, e.g., via exploration or interaction with the environment?
