This paper introduces a novel approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments, framing the problem as supervised learning through prediction of future measurements derived from raw sensory input. The core idea leverages the rich supervisory signal present in the co-temporal structure of sensory streams and agent measurements to train a control model without relying on traditional reward-based reinforcement learning. The authors demonstrate compelling results in the challenging Doom environment, achieving state-of-the-art performance and winning the Visual Doom AI Competition.

**Reject.** While the empirical results are strong and demonstrate significant progress, the paper lacks sufficient clarity in articulating its core contributions beyond a successful integration of existing techniques, and fails to address potential limitations or failure modes. The lack of broader evaluation beyond the Doom environment also limits the generalizability of the findings.

The paper effectively motivates the challenges of sensorimotor control and the limitations of traditional reinforcement learning in complex, dynamic environments. The proposed approach, which reduces the problem to supervised learning by predicting future measurements, is a logical and interesting direction. The authors correctly position their work within the existing literature, referencing relevant advancements in supervised learning, reinforcement learning, and related areas like temporal difference learning and prediction-based control. The experimental setup is well-defined, utilizing the challenging Doom environment and demonstrating superior performance compared to existing deep reinforcement learning methods, including a win in a competitive AI challenge. The ablation studies provide valuable insight into the importance of vectorial feedback and multi-step prediction.

However, the paper's contributions feel incremental rather than fundamentally novel. The framing of the problem as supervised learning through future prediction, while effective, builds upon existing ideas rather than presenting a groundbreaking theoretical advance. The abstract and introduction could be strengthened to more clearly articulate the specific contributions of this work beyond this integration. Furthermore, the paper lacks a critical discussion of potential limitations. For example, the on-policy nature of the method raises concerns about catastrophic forgetting when adapting to dynamically changing goals. Exploring this limitation, or discussing mitigation strategies, would strengthen the work. The absence of evaluation on other domains, such as Atari, limits the generalizability of the conclusions. While Doom is a compelling environment, demonstrating success in other benchmarks would increase confidence in the robustness of the approach.

**Additional Feedback (for potential improvement):**

*   **Clarify Contributions:** Explicitly state the key novel aspects of the approach in the abstract and introduction. Is it the specific formulation of prediction, the architecture, or the combination of factors?
*   **Address Limitations:** Discuss potential failure modes and limitations of the approach, such as catastrophic forgetting due to on-policy learning.
*   **Broader Evaluation:** Consider evaluating the method on other benchmark environments, such as Atari games, to assess its generalizability. Perhaps framing intrinsic variables using the game's ROM data could allow for such an evaluation.
*   **Goal Generalization Analysis:** Provide a more detailed analysis of how well the agent generalizes to unseen goals. Visualize or quantify the performance across a wider range of goal variations.
*   **Ablation Study Details:** Provide more details on the specific hyperparameters used for each ablation study variant.

**Questions for the Authors:**

*   How does the on-policy nature of the algorithm affect its ability to generalize to new goals? Have you explored any techniques to mitigate potential catastrophic forgetting?
*   What are the limitations of this approach in environments where it is difficult to define meaningful measurements?
*   Could you elaborate on the computational cost of this approach compared to standard deep reinforcement learning algorithms?
*   How sensitive is the performance to the choice of temporal offsets (τ1, ..., τn) used for future prediction? Did you perform a more extensive hyperparameter search on these values?
