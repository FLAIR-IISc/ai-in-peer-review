This paper presents a novel on-policy deep reinforcement learning method incorporating auxiliary intrinsic variables to enhance performance in 3D navigation tasks. The approach builds upon universal value function frameworks, appropriately citing relevant prior work. While the authors effectively distill several existing ideas into a cohesive solution for challenging 3D environments, the core contributions would benefit from more explicit articulation in the abstract and introduction. 

The primary strength of this work lies in its empirical demonstration of performance gains through the integration of these key concepts. The experimental results are compelling and provide valuable insights into the potential of this formulation for future agent design. However, a more thorough analysis of the method’s limitations would strengthen the paper significantly. 

Specifically, I would have liked to see a discussion of failure modes. Under what conditions does the model struggle to generalize to novel goal locations or significantly different environmental configurations? The on-policy nature of the algorithm also raises concerns about catastrophic forgetting. Without continual training on previously encountered goals, performance degradation is likely, and exploring mitigation strategies or characterizing this effect would be beneficial. The paper does not address this potential issue.

Furthermore, given the authors' claim of integrating existing ideas to achieve empirical advantages, it would be valuable to assess the method’s broader applicability. Evaluating performance on established benchmark domains like Atari, potentially utilizing the game’s internal ROM states as intrinsic variables, would offer a more comprehensive understanding of its strengths and weaknesses. This broader evaluation could reveal whether the observed benefits are specific to 3D navigation or generalize to other complex reinforcement learning problems.

The writing is generally clear, but some minor improvements would enhance readability. For example, the phrase “underlying formulations” could be made more specific, perhaps referring to the specific combination of intrinsic reward signals and value function structure. 

In summary, this paper demonstrates a clear empirical advantage to the proposed approach and offers potentially valuable experimental insights for the reinforcement learning community. Addressing the concerns regarding failure modes, catastrophic forgetting, and broader domain applicability would elevate the work and further solidify its contribution. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing these points.
