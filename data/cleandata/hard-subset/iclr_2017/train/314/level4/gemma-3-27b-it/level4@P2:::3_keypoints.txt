- Main contribution: Frames sensorimotor control as supervised prediction of future intrinsic measurements (health, ammo, frags) weighted by input goal vectors.  
- Practical result: Method (DFP) won the 2016 Visual Doom AI Competition, demonstrating strong empirical performance.  
- Comparative evaluation: DFP outperforms or matches A3C, DQN, and DSR across tasks.  
- Ablation findings: Ablation study supports multi-dimensional measurements and multi-step prediction as beneficial design choices.  
- Experimental rigor: Well-defined experimental setup with clear environment and hyperparameter descriptions and strong generalization across Doom maps.  
- Related work: Connections to intrinsic motivation, auxiliary tasks, and forward modeling are adequately discussed (though not exhaustive).
