- Proposes a novel on-policy deep RL method using auxiliary intrinsic variables to improve 3D navigation performance.
- Builds on universal value function frameworks and appropriately cites relevant prior work.
- Effectively combines several existing ideas into a cohesive solution for challenging 3D environments.
- Core contributions are not explicitly articulated enough in the abstract and introduction.
- Primary strength is strong empirical demonstration of performance gains from the integrated approach.
- Experimental results are compelling and provide useful insights for future agent design.
- Lacks a thorough analysis of limitations; more discussion of failure modes is needed.
- Needs evaluation of when the model fails to generalize to novel goal locations or significantly different environments.
- On-policy nature raises concerns about catastrophic forgetting without continual training on prior goals; mitigation strategies should be discussed.
- Broader applicability is unclear; recommend evaluating on established benchmarks (e.g., Atari using ROM states as intrinsic variables).
- Minor writing improvements would enhance clarity (e.g., specify “underlying formulations” as the combination of intrinsic rewards and value structure).
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing failure modes, catastrophic forgetting, and broader evaluation.
