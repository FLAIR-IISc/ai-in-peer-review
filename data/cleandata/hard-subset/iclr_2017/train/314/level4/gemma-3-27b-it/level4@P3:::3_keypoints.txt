- Decision: Accept — paper makes a significant contribution and should be accepted.  
- Core idea: Frame sensorimotor control as supervised prediction of future intrinsic measurements, avoiding explicit rewards.  
- Technical contribution: Novel prediction-based approach (DFP) leveraging cotemporal structure of sensory inputs.  
- Empirical results: Extensive evaluation in ViZDoom showing state-of-the-art performance and winning the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Baseline comparisons: Consistently outperforms strong baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR), especially on complex tasks.  
- Ablation studies: Ablations demonstrate the importance of multiple-measurement prediction and other components.  
- Generalization and robustness: Generalization experiments and competition success show practical robustness.  
- Presentation and reproducibility: Well-written, clear network architecture, thorough experimental setup, and appendix with implementation details.  
- Environment wording: Clarify Doom is a 2D engine with a pseudo-3D perspective rather than a true 3D environment.  
- Notation issues: Change ambiguous symbol “P” for prediction and avoid reusing “j” in different fonts to reduce confusion.  
- Table clarity: State units for measured quantities (health, ammunition, frags) in tables (e.g., Table 1).  
- Future work: Expand discussion on combining the approach with hierarchical RL and continuous action methods.  
- Goal-agnostic training: Provide a more detailed analysis of performance variance across different goal vectors (Table 3).  
- Question — cost of predictions: What is the computational cost and practical limit of predicting more measurements?  
- Question — full-sensory prediction: What prevents predicting full sensory streams today, and what developments are needed?  
- Question — competition details: Provide specifics on model modifications or parameter tuning used for the Visual Doom AI Competition entry.
