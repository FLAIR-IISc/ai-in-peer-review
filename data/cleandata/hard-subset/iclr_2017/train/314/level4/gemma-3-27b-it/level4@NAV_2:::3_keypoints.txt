- Paper frames sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future intrinsic measurements from high-dimensional visual input and low-dimensional measurements.  
- Model predicts effects of actions on future measurements, enabling goal-agnostic training and dynamic goal pursuit at test time.  
- Empirical results are strong, including winning the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Recommendation: Accept — novel, effective approach addressing a challenging RL problem with compelling experimental support.  
- Well-motivated positioning versus traditional reward-based RL, highlighting benefits of temporal structure and rich supervision.  
- Thorough experimental setup with comparisons to DQN, A3C, DSR, ablation studies, and generalization experiments showing superior performance.  
- Ablation study and generalization tests provide valuable insights into component importance and policy robustness.  
- Suggested clarifications: note Doom’s pseudo-3D but fundamentally 2D nature; avoid using "p" for prediction; resolve dual use of "j" in Eq.(6); state units in result tables.  
- Suggested addition: discuss connections to intrinsic motivation and curiosity-driven learning.  
- Clarifying questions: sensitivity to chosen temporal offsets (1,2,4,8,16,32); use of regression vs classification loss for categorical measurements; computational resources required (esp. D4-tx-L); rationale and impact of sampling goal vectors from [-1,1] vs [0,1].
