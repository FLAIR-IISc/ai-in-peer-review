- Proposes an on-policy method (DFP) that predicts sequences of intrinsic measurement triplets (health, ammo, frags) weighted by input goal triplets to guide behavior and exploration.  
- Exploration is controlled by modulating the weights of goal triplets; actions maximize long-term goal achievement at test time.  
- All experiments are conducted in the vizDoom environment, and the model won the 2016 vizDoom competition.  
- Experimental evaluation is rigorous, with comparisons against A3C, DQN, and attempted comparison to DSR, where DFP outperforms or matches baselines.  
- An ablation study shows each model component contributes to overall performance.  
- The paper provides sufficient implementation detail (appendix) for reproducibility.  
- Prior work on intrinsic motivation, auxiliary variables, and forward modeling is cited adequately, though coverage is not exhaustive.  
- Minor presentation issues: Doom is described as 3D but is really 2D with pseudo-3D rendering.  
- Notational issues: using “P” for predictions (misleading as probability) and double use of “j” in equation (6) may confuse readers.  
- Results tables should repeat units of measurement due to heterogeneous data (Table 1).  
- Overall recommendation: accept — technically sound, experimentally validated, significant contribution despite questions about broader novelty or additional environments.
