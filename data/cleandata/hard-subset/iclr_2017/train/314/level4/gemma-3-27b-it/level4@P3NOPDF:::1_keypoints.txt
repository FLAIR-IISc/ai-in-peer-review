- Novel algorithm uses low-dimensional, game-provided measurements as supervised prediction targets conditioned on a goal vector and the agent’s action.  
- Employs Monte Carlo estimation (fully supervised regression) rather than temporal-difference learning or successor features.  
- Selects actions by maximizing predicted outcomes aligned with parameterized, a priori goal vectors.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance and generalization in unseen 3D environments; won the Visual Doom AI competition.  
- Shows policy adaptability to limited goal changes without additional training.  
- Presents a thorough related-work review and rigorous empirical comparisons to prior methods.  
- Training assumes an implicit on-policy expectation while learning from an off-policy replay buffer; potential discrepancy is not discussed.  
- Relies on metadata specifying which sensory aspects to predict, providing prior-knowledge not available to compared algorithms.  
- Lacks explicit discussion of limitations when such measurements are unavailable and of sensitivity to measurement quality/availability.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and communicates concepts clearly.
