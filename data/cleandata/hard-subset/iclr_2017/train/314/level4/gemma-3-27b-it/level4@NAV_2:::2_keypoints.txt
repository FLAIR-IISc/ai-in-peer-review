- Framing: casts sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future measurements from raw sensory input.  
- Empirical strength: strong results, outperforming state-of-the-art deep RL and winning the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Ablations: studies convincingly show benefits of vectorial feedback and multi-step prediction.  
- Novelty concern: paper reads as incremental—builds on existing supervised/future-prediction and multi-dimensional feedback ideas without clearly stating what is new.  
- Related work gap: connection to universal value function approximators and similar work is acknowledged but not deeply analyzed.  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends rejecting the paper in its current form.  
- Clarify contribution: need explicit statement of what is novel (prediction task formulation, network architecture, training regime, or their combination).  
- Failure-mode analysis: should investigate scenarios where the model fails to generalize to changing goals.  
- Catastrophic forgetting: paper should discuss potential forgetting in on-policy learning and any mitigation used.  
- Broader evaluation: recommend testing on additional domains (e.g., Atari) to assess generality and comparability.  
- Reproducibility: provide more details on hyperparameter search and sensitivity analysis.  
- Question—design choices: which specific design elements most drive performance gains over standard deep RL baselines?  
- Question—horizon: how does performance degrade as the prediction time horizon increases and is there a point of diminishing returns?  
- Question—replay: what experience replay mechanism was used (memory size, sampling strategy)?  
- Question—compute cost: what is the computational cost relative to baselines and is the performance improvement worth the extra resources?
