- Proposes a novel supervised learning approach combining raw sensory input and low-dimensional measurements to predict action effects on future measurements conditioned on state and goal.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance, notably winning the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Outperforms state-of-the-art RL methods (DQN, A3C, DSR) across increasingly complex Doom environments.  
- Framing sensorimotor control as supervised prediction leverages temporal correlations and circumvents sparse-reward RL challenges.  
- Shows good generalization to unseen environments and varying goals.  
- Ablation studies indicate the importance of vectorial feedback (multiple measurements) and multi-step prediction horizons.  
- Verdict: Accept — paper is well-written, well-motivated, and presents a compelling solution.  
- Raises concern that using an experience replay buffer introduces an off-policy element; requests discussion of effects on convergence and stability.  
- Notes limitation when explicit measurements (health, ammo, etc.) are unavailable; suggests exploring learned latent measurements from raw input.  
- Points out the simplification of using linear goal combinations and asks for discussion of more complex (e.g., non-linear) goal representations.  
- Question: Please elaborate on the impact of the replay buffer on training convergence/stability and mitigation techniques used.  
- Question: How would the framework be adapted when explicit measurements are not provided; is learning a latent measurement space feasible and what are the challenges?  
- Question: What are the benefits/drawbacks of non-linear goal representations and challenges to adapt the algorithm?  
- Question: In the ablation, were measurement weightings during training or prediction experimented with, and what were the results?
