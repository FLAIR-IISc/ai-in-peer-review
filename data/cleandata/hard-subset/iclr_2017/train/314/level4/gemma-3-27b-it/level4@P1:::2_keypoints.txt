- Presents a supervised-learning approach for sensorimotor control using high-dimensional sensory streams and low-dimensional measurement streams to provide rich supervision.  
- Trains a control model from raw sensory input to pursue dynamically changing goals and demonstrates this in 3D immersive environments.  
- Strong empirical results: outperforms existing methods across multiple Doom scenarios and won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch track.  
- Decision: Reject — promising results but insufficient analysis of limitations and generalizability.  
- Well-motivated and well-positioned in literature, with clear connection to Universal Value Function ideas.  
- Ablation studies show vectorial feedback and multi-step prediction are important for performance.  
- Major concern: heavy focus on Doom raises questions about generalizability to other domains.  
- Lacks thorough analysis of failure modes and conditions where the model struggles or fails to generalize.  
- Needs clearer articulation of core novelty (formulation vs. architecture vs. combination) in abstract and introduction.  
- Should address catastrophic forgetting and long-term stability given on-policy training and continued training setup.  
- Recommend testing broader applicability (e.g., Atari or other domains using game state measurements) to strengthen claims.  
- Request analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity to assess robustness and usability.  
- Suggest architectural ablations (expectation and action streams) in addition to measurement/prediction studies.  
- Questions for authors: why predicting full sensory streams is infeasible and how to tackle it in future work?  
- Questions for authors: specifics on training vs testing environment differences and how generalization was evaluated.  
- Questions for authors: computational cost of training/running the model compared to baselines.  
- Questions for authors: clarification on preference for TD versus Monte Carlo methods in practice.  
- Questions for authors: limitations of linear goal vector g and whether nonlinear goal representations were explored.
