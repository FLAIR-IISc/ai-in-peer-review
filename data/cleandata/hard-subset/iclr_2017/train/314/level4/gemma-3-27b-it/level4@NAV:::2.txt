This paper introduces a novel on-policy deep reinforcement learning method that incorporates auxiliary intrinsic variables. 

The approach builds upon and can be framed as a specific instance of universal value function methods, with appropriate related work cited. While the paper effectively consolidates existing techniques for tackling 3D navigation challenges, arguably its primary contribution – this distillation of ideas – would benefit from more explicit articulation in the abstract and introduction. 

A key area for improvement lies in the analysis of potential limitations. Investigating failure modes, particularly concerning generalization to novel goals, would strengthen the work. Furthermore, given the on-policy nature of the method, the potential for catastrophic forgetting when dealing with infrequently revisited goals should be addressed.

As the core contribution centers around integrating established concepts and demonstrating empirical gains, extending the evaluation to additional domains – such as Atari environments, potentially leveraging ROM data as intrinsic variables – would further validate the approach's broader applicability.

Despite these points, the paper demonstrates clear empirical benefits from the proposed formulation, and the experimental insights gained are likely to be valuable for the development of future reinforcement learning agents.
