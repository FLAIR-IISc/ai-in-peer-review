- Introduces a novel RL algorithm that augments standard RL with auxiliary supervised learning using low-dimensional measurements (e.g., ammo, health).  
- Predictions are conditioned on a provided goal vector and the agent’s current action.  
- At test time, the agent selects the action that maximizes the predicted outcome relative to the goal.  
- Uses supervised Monte Carlo regression instead of temporal-difference (TD) successor-feature approaches.  
- Empirically strong: demonstrates superior performance and won the Visual Doom AI competition.  
- Shows policies that generalize across different goals and environments and can adapt to goal changes without retraining.  
- Paper situates the work well in related literature and provides thorough empirical comparisons with baselines.  
- Well-written and of significant interest to the deep RL community.  
- Concern: supervised training assumes on-policy data, but learning uses a replay buffer containing data from prior policies—this potential mismatch needs discussion.  
- Concern: method uses extra metadata identifying relevant measurements, giving it an advantage over baselines; paper should acknowledge limitations in environments without such measurements and discuss feature self-discovery as future work.
