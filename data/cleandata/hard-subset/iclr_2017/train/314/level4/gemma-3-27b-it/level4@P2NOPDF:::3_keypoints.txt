- Introduces a novel on-policy RL method that predicts future intrinsic state variables.  
- Experiments in the challenging vizDoom environment, predicting sequences of (health, ammunition, frags) rather than raw reward.  
- Predictions are weighted by input "goal" triplets to guide exploration; agent optimizes for long-term goal achievement at test time.  
- Reported results are compelling, including winning the 2016 vizDoom competition.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and well-structured.  
- Strong comparisons to baselines (A3C, DQN, attempted DSR replication); DFP outperforms or matches these methods.  
- Ablation study effectively shows the value of the model’s architectural choices.  
- Builds on literature around intrinsic motivation, auxiliary variable prediction, and forward modeling; Dec 4 revision shows reasonable awareness of prior work though not exhaustive.  
- Minor presentation issue: describing Doom as 3D is technically inaccurate (fundamentally 2D).  
- Notation concern: using "P" in eq. (2) may mislead readers into thinking it denotes a probability.  
- Ambiguity risk: dual use of index "j" with differing font styles in eq. (6).  
- Results tables should explicitly repeat units for heterogeneous variables (e.g., Table 1).  
- Overall recommendation: accept — technically sound, experimentally validated, and a valuable contribution despite potential to broaden environments or emphasize novelty.
