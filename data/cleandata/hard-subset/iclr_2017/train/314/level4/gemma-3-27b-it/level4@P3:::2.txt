This paper presents a novel approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments, framing the problem as supervised learning through prediction of future measurements given raw sensory input. The authors leverage the rich supervisory signal provided by the cotemporal structure of sensory and measurement streams, achieving state-of-the-art results in the challenging Doom environment and winning the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition.

**Reject.** While the paper presents compelling empirical results and demonstrates a significant achievement in the Visual Doom AI Competition, the contributions, as presented, lack sufficient novelty to warrant acceptance. The approach builds heavily on established techniques – supervised learning, temporal difference learning, and function approximation – and primarily demonstrates a successful integration and application of these concepts to a specific domain. The framing as a novel method feels incremental rather than a paradigm shift.

The core idea of using future measurements as a supervisory signal is interesting and well-motivated. The authors correctly position their work within the existing literature on reinforcement learning and supervised learning, acknowledging the trade-offs and benefits of each approach. The connection to prior work on temporal differences and prediction is clearly articulated. The experimental setup is rigorous, and the results are impressive, particularly the performance in the Visual Doom AI Competition, exceeding the second-best submission by a substantial margin. The ablation studies provide valuable insights into the importance of vectorial feedback and multi-step prediction.

However, the paper lacks a deeper exploration of the theoretical underpinnings of the approach. While the authors acknowledge the on-policy nature of the method, they do not adequately address potential issues like catastrophic forgetting or the limitations of generalizing to drastically different goal distributions. Further, the reliance on the Doom environment as the primary evaluation platform limits the generalizability of the findings. The absence of results on other benchmark tasks, such as Atari, weakens the claim of broad applicability. The discussion of failure modes is minimal. A more thorough analysis of the method's weaknesses and limitations would strengthen the paper. The contributions, while significant in the context of the Doom environment, are more accurately described as a successful application and refinement of existing techniques rather than a fundamentally novel algorithm.

Here are some points to consider for improvement:

*   **Clarify the Contributions:** Reframe the contributions to emphasize the successful integration of existing ideas and the empirical benefits demonstrated in a challenging environment. Avoid overly strong claims of novelty.
*   **Address Catastrophic Forgetting:** Discuss the potential for catastrophic forgetting in this on-policy method and explore potential mitigation strategies.
*   **Explore Generalization:** Investigate the model's performance on a wider range of environments and tasks, such as Atari, to assess its generalizability.
*   **Analyze Failure Modes:** Provide a more detailed analysis of the scenarios where the model fails to generalize or performs poorly.
*   **Theoretical Discussion:** Expand the theoretical discussion, potentially exploring the convergence properties of the proposed approach or comparing it more formally to existing reinforcement learning algorithms.
*   **Consider Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Report the results of a sensitivity analysis of the key hyperparameters.

I have the following questions for the authors:

*   What strategies were considered to mitigate catastrophic forgetting, and why were they not implemented in the final version of the model?
*   How does the performance of the model degrade as the temporal distance between current measurements and future goal states increases?
*   What is the computational cost of the proposed approach compared to other state-of-the-art reinforcement learning algorithms?
*   Are there any specific types of goals or environments where the model consistently struggles? If so, what factors contribute to these difficulties?
*   Could you provide more detail on the hyperparameter search process and the rationale behind the chosen hyperparameters?
