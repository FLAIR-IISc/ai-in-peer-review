This paper introduces an on-policy deep reinforcement learning method that incorporates auxiliary intrinsic variables. 
The approach can be framed as a specific instance of a universal value function-based method, and the authors appropriately cite relevant prior work. While a significant claimed technical contribution is the consolidation of existing ideas to effectively address 3D navigation tasks, this contribution could be more explicitly highlighted in the abstract and introduction. 

I would have appreciated a discussion of the method's limitations and failure modes. Specifically, the paper does not address how well the model generalizes when faced with changing goals, or the potential for catastrophic forgetting inherent in on-policy learning if the agent is not continually retrained on previously encountered goals. 

Given that the primary contribution appears to be the integration of several key ideas and demonstration of empirical improvements, I believe results on additional domains – such as Atari, potentially leveraging ROM data as intrinsic variables – would strengthen the paper. 

Overall, this work demonstrates a clear empirical benefit from the proposed formulation, and the experimental insights provided may be valuable for the development of future reinforcement learning agents.
