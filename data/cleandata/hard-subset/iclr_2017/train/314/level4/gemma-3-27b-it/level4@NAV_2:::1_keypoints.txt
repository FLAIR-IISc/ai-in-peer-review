- Novel algorithm builds on deep RL to solve large-state-space problems from raw sensory input, showing superior performance and generalization in unseen 3D environments and winning the Visual Doom AI competition.
- Core idea: predict low-dimensional game-provided measurements (e.g., ammo, health) conditioned on a goal vector and current action, then choose actions that maximize predicted outcomes.
- Learning is supervised Monte Carlo regression (no TD temporal-difference links), unlike successor features or TD-based methods.
- Thorough related-work review covering future-state prediction and goal-driven function approximation.
- Key contributions: emphasis on Monte Carlo estimation, use of low-dimensional measurements, parameterized goals, and strong empirical comparisons to baselines.
- Empirical strengths: convincing competition result, ablation studies, and demonstrated ability to generalize to limited goal changes without retraining.
- Paper quality: well-written, clear algorithm explanation, and significant, well-executed contribution — recommendation: Accept.
- Concern: Monte Carlo regression with a replay buffer implicitly assumes on-policy returns; sampling from past policies may introduce approximation error that should be discussed.
- Concern: Method relies on access to metadata identifying measurable sensory quantities; limitations and applicability where such measurements are unavailable should be acknowledged.
- Question 1: Please elaborate on the on-policy assumption with replay buffers and strategies to mitigate resulting issues.
- Question 2: How would the approach perform when informative measurements are hard to define, and can the method be extended to learn useful measurements from raw input?
- Question 3: What experiments justify the chosen network architecture (e.g., expectation/action stream split) and were alternative architectures explored?
