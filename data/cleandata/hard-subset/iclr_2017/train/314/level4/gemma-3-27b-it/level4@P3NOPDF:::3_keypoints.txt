- Proposes an on-policy RL method (DFP) that predicts sequences of intrinsic measurement triplets (health, ammo, frags) weighted by input goal triplets to guide exploration and action selection, achieving state-of-the-art performance and winning the 2016 vizDoom competition.

- Experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing against strong baselines (A3C, DQN, DSR) where DFP generally outperforms or matches baselines; ablation studies demonstrate each component's contribution.

- Builds on established RL concepts (intrinsic motivation, auxiliary prediction, forward modeling); December 4th revision cites relevant prior work but could broaden coverage.

- Minor presentation issue: describing Doom as a 3D environment is technically inaccurate; it is pseudo-3D with no discriminative/actionable height dimension.

- Notation concern: using "P" in equation (2) is potentially misleading since it suggests probability but represents a prediction.

- Notation concern: the index “j” is used in two different fonts in equation (6), which could cause confusion.

- Results tables (especially Table 1) should explicitly repeat units for each metric due to heterogeneous measurements.

- Recommendation: accept—the paper is technically sound, experimentally validated on a challenging benchmark, and represents an important contribution despite potential incremental novelty or limited environment scope.
