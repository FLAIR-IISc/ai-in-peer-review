- Paper introduces a new deep RL algorithm using supervised prediction of low-dimensional measurements conditioned on a goal vector and action.
- Algorithm selects actions by maximizing predicted measurements with respect to a specified goal at test time.
- Learning is fully supervised via Monte Carlo regression rather than temporal-difference (TD) methods or successor features.
- Key contributions: Monte Carlo estimation focus, use of low-dimensional measurements, goal parametrization, and thorough empirical comparisons.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance in novel 3D environments using raw sensory input.
- Won the Visual Doom AI competition, showing practical competitiveness.
- Shows better generalization across goals and environments, allowing goal changes without retraining for limited perturbations.
- Paper is well-written and presents compelling empirical evidence of its claims.
- Minor concern: Monte Carlo regression assumes on-policy returns, but training uses a replay buffer with off-policy episodes—this discrepancy should be discussed.
- Minor concern: The method relies on privileged low-dimensional metadata (which inputs to predict); limitations when such measurements are unavailable should be highlighted.
