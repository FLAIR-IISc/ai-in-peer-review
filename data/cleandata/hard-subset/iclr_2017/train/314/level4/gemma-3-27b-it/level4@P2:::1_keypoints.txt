- Proposes reframing sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future low-dimensional measurements from raw sensory input conditioned on action and goal.  
- Removes reliance on a traditional reward function and enables flexible goal specification at test time.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance, including winning the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch.  
- Outperforms state-of-the-art deep RL methods and shows impressive generalization.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Addresses an important problem: control from high-dimensional sensory input with dynamic goals; well-motivated approach.  
- Mitigates sparse-reward issues by using temporal correlations between sensory and measurement streams as supervisory signal.  
- Situates work clearly relative to prior supervised-control perspectives and successor-representation methods.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough across challenging Doom environments and includes strong baselines.  
- Generalization experiments are particularly noteworthy and support practical impact.  
- Ablation studies highlight the importance of vectorial feedback and multi-step prediction.  
- Concern: replay buffer creates an on-policy approximation within the Monte Carlo regression framework and needs discussion.  
- Limitation: approach depends on availability of informative, low-dimensional measurements and thus leverages prior knowledge.  
- Suggestion: explore alternative (nonlinear) goal representations beyond linear combinations of future measurements.  
- Suggestion: include discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Question: impact of the on-policy approximation from using a replay buffer and any experiments with different buffer sizes or sampling.  
- Question: how to adapt the method to environments where informative low-dimensional measurements are hard or impossible to define.  
- Question: were attempts made to learn the goal vector instead of supplying it, and what are the challenges?  
- Question: sensitivity of performance to temporal offsets (τ1,…,τn) and whether alternative selection strategies were explored.
