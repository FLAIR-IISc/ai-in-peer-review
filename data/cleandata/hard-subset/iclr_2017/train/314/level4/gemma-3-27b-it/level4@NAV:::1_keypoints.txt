- Introduces an empirically grounded algorithm extending deep RL to unseen 3D environments using raw sensory input and demonstrating strong generalization across goals and environments.  
- Core idea: supervise predictions of low-dimensional game-provided measurements (e.g., ammo, health) conditioned on a goal vector and action; select actions by maximizing predicted goal-relevant outcomes.  
- Method differs from successor-feature approaches by using fully supervised learning (Monte Carlo regression) rather than temporal-difference learning or explicit next-state prediction.  
- Main contributions: emphasis on Monte Carlo estimation over TD, strategic use of low-dimensional measurements, parametrized goals, and a thorough empirical comparison to prior methods.  
- Strong empirical result: won the Visual Doom AI competition and learned policies that generalize to modest changes in goal specification without retraining.  
- Paper quality: clearly written with persuasive empirical evidence.  
- Limitation/concern 1: training assumes on-policy Monte Carlo expectations but uses a replay buffer (off-policy samples), creating a mismatch that needs discussion.  
- Limitation/concern 2: relies on supplemental metadata identifying useful measurements (not available to baselines); implications and limits when such measurements are absent should be explicitly addressed.
