This paper presents an on-policy deep reinforcement learning method that incorporates additional auxiliary intrinsic variables. 

The method represents a specific case of a universal value function approach, and the authors appropriately cite relevant prior work. Perhaps the most significant claimed technical contribution of this paper is the distillation of many existing ideas to effectively address 3D navigation problems. However, I believe the contributions would benefit from a more explicit and focused statement in the abstract and introduction.

I would have appreciated a discussion of the approach’s failure modes. Under what conditions does the model struggle to generalize to novel goals? Furthermore, there are conceptual concerns: as this is an on-policy method, catastrophic forgetting is a potential issue if the agent does not repeatedly train on goals from earlier experiences.

Given that the primary contribution of this work lies in integrating several key ideas and demonstrating empirical improvements, I would have liked to see results on additional domains, such as Atari. Exploring the use of ROM states as intrinsic variables could be particularly insightful. 

Overall, this paper demonstrates a clear empirical advantage to the proposed formulation, and the experimental insights gained from this work may be valuable for the development of future agents.
