- Paper proposes an on-policy method (DFP) that predicts future intrinsic measurements to guide behavior in vizDoom.  
- Model predicts sequences of triplets (health, ammunition, frags) weighted by input "goal" triplets to direct exploration.  
- Adjusting goal-triplet weights provides a mechanism to influence exploration; at test time the agent optimizes for long-term goals.  
- Experimental validation is entirely within the Doom/vizDoom environment.  
- Method achieved strong results, winning the 2016 vizDoom competition.  
- Empirical comparisons against A3C, DQN, and a benchmark attempt at DSR show DFP outperforms or matches baselines.  
- Ablation study demonstrates the model’s additional complexities meaningfully improve performance.  
- Paper appropriately cites prior work on intrinsic motivation, auxiliary variables, and forward modeling, though citations may not be exhaustive.  
- Minor presentation issues: Doom should be described as pseudo-3D (2D with rendered height), not full 3D.  
- Notational concerns: "P" in Eq.(2) suggests probability but denotes prediction; dual use of "j" in Eq.(6) could mislead.  
- Results tables need repeated units (metrics are heterogeneous, e.g., Table 1).  
- Overall recommendation: clear accept — methodologically sound, experimentally rigorous on Doom, and of milestone significance despite limited environment scope and possible novelty questions.
