- Introduces a novel deep RL algorithm that uses raw sensory data to achieve superior performance and generalization in unseen 3D environments; won the Visual Doom AI competition.  
- Core innovation: supervise predictions of low-dimensional measurements (e.g., ammo, health) conditioned on a provided goal vector and current action.  
- Action selection is done by choosing the action that maximizes the predicted outcome for the goal.  
- Method relies on supervised Monte Carlo estimation rather than successor features or temporal-difference learning; predictions have no TD relationship across states.  
- Paper provides a thorough literature review on future-state prediction and goal-driven function approximators.  
- Main contributions: Monte Carlo emphasis, use of low-dimensional measurements, parameterized goals, and empirical comparisons to prior methods.  
- Empirically shows learned policies generalize and can adapt to limited goal changes without further training.  
- Paper is well-written and presents compelling empirical results, constituting a valuable contribution.  
- Suggestion: explicitly discuss the approximation from using Monte Carlo regression on replay-buffer data generated by earlier policies (assumed on-policy).  
- Suggestion: acknowledge dependency on extra metadata (knowing which measurements to predict) and limitations when such measurements are unavailable.
