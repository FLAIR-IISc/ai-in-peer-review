- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): frame control as supervised learning by predicting future low‑dimensional measurements from pixels and using a weighted goal vector at test time.  
- Shows strong empirical performance: significantly outperforms DQN/A3C on Doom tasks and won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch.  
- Decision: Accept — simple, powerful idea backed by thorough experiments.  
- Excellent empirical results: large gains in complex scenarios (e.g., 6× and 2.5× frag improvements over A3C in Battle and Battle 2).  
- Ablation studies: predicting multiple measurements and multiple future time steps is critical to performance.  
- Generalization and flexibility: training on varied textures and goal‑agnostic training produce models that generalize and can pursue novel or contradictory goals.  
- Reproducibility: detailed appendices provide sufficient information for replication.  
- Nomenclature/clarity suggestions: note Doom is technically 2.5D, avoid using p_at (might be confused with probability), and add measurement units to table headers.  
- Literature positioning: strengthen discussion by relating DFP to auxiliary prediction tasks and intrinsic motivation approaches for sparse rewards.  
- Question — temporal offsets: how sensitive is performance to the chosen τ = {1,2,4,8,16,32}, and were other spacings or longer horizons explored?  
- Question — goal conditioning: have you analyzed the joint representation j to see how it is modulated by the goal vector g or whether distinct goal‑conditioned features emerge?  
- Question — measurement dependence: have you considered predicting learned sensory features (e.g., via an autoencoder) instead of relying on engine‑provided measurements?
