- Introduces Direct Future Prediction (DFP), an on-policy method that predicts future intrinsic measurements to guide agent behavior.
- Uses a goal vector input to specify relative importance of measurements, enabling behavior control at test time by changing weights.
- Evaluated extensively in the VizDoom first-person shooter domain and achieved state-of-the-art performance, winning the 2016 VizDoom competition.
- Exceptional empirical performance is the paper's strongest point, demonstrating practical success on a challenging benchmark.
- Thorough experimental validation with rigorous comparisons to strong baselines (A3C, DQN) and a thoughtful comparison to DSR.
- Includes a valuable ablation study showing the contribution of different model components and justifying architectural complexity.
- Paper is clearly written and reproducible, with an appendix providing sufficient implementation details.
- Situates the work well within related literature (intrinsic motivation, auxiliary prediction, forward modeling) with adequate citations.
- Limited generalizability: evaluation confined to VizDoom; testing on Atari or continuous control would strengthen claims.
- Minor presentation nitpick: describe Doom as 2.5D rather than 3D for precision.
- Notation issues: use of "P" for prediction may be misleading and dual use of index "j" could confuse readers.
- Table clarity: heterogeneous measurements in tables should repeat units in headers or captions to avoid ambiguity.
- Recommendation: clear accept — strengths and empirical results outweigh concerns about incremental novelty or limited domain range.
