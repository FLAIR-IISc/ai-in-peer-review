This paper proposes an on-policy deep reinforcement learning method augmented with auxiliary intrinsic variables.

- The authors correctly identify their method as a special case of an approach based on universal value functions and cite the relevant literature. The primary technical contribution appears to be the synthesis of several existing ideas to solve 3D navigation problems. However, this contribution should be stated more explicitly in the abstract and introduction to better frame the paper's impact.

- The analysis would be strengthened by an investigation of the approach's failure modes. For example, under what conditions does the model fail to generalize to changing goals? A further conceptual concern is catastrophic forgetting; as an on-policy method, the agent may struggle if it does not continually train on goals from its distant past.

- Given that the main contribution is empirical—demonstrating the advantage of integrating several key ideas—the evaluation would be more compelling with results on other domains. For instance, the authors could test their method in the Atari environment, potentially using the game's ROM state as the intrinsic variables.

Overall, the paper provides a clear empirical demonstration of the proposed formulation's benefits. The experimental insights are valuable and are likely to be useful for the development of future agents.
