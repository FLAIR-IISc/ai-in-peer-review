- Proposes an on-policy method (Direct Future Prediction, DFP) to directly predict future intrinsic measurements from the environment.  
- Predicts sequences of measurement triplets (health, ammo, frags) conditioned on a goal triplet that weights measurement importance.  
- Goal-weighting provides a mechanism to guide agent exploration and policy behavior.  
- At test time the agent maximizes predicted long-term measurements according to a fixed goal.  
- Experiments are conducted exclusively in VizDoom and the model won the 2016 VizDoom competition.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and compares against strong baselines (A3C, DQN, DSR).  
- DFP outperforms or matches baselines, especially where A3C hits a performance ceiling.  
- Ablation studies justify the model components and design choices.  
- Paper is well-situated in RL literature (intrinsic motivation, auxiliary tasks, forward modeling), though literature review is not exhaustive.  
- Presentation issues: Doom described as 3D though it’s a 2D engine with pseudo-3D rendering.  
- Notational concerns: use of "P" for prediction may be confusing, and reuse of "j" with different fonts in Eq.(6) is misleading.  
- Results tables need clearer units for heterogeneous measurements (repeat units in headers/captions).  
- Overall recommendation: clear accept — method is sound, experimentally robust, reproducible, and makes a significant contribution despite limited novelty and narrow environment coverage.
