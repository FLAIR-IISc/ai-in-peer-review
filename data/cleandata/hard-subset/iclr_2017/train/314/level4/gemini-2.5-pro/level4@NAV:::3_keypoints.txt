- Introduces an on-policy method that predicts future intrinsic measurement sequences rather than single outcomes.  
- Training predicts measurement triplets (health, ammunition, frags) weighted by a user-provided "goal" triplet.  
- Goal weighting enables guided exploration and test-time behavior that maximizes a long-term objective.  
- Experimental validation is entirely within the ViZDoom environment.  
- Empirical results are strong and include a first-place finish in the 2016 ViZDoom competition.  
- Benchmarked against A3C, DQN, and DSR, showing superior or comparable performance, especially where A3C plateaus.  
- Ablation study demonstrates that the model’s added complexity is justified and contributes to performance.  
- Paper is well-grounded in RL paradigms (intrinsic motivation, auxiliary tasks, forward modeling) and credits prior work, though related-work coverage is not exhaustive.  
- Minor presentation issues: Doom described as 3D though it is more accurately 2.5D.  
- Notational confusions: "P" in Eq.(2) denotes prediction (not probability), and font reuse of "j" in Eq.(6) is ambiguous.  
- Results tables need clearer unit annotations, especially Table 1 with heterogeneous measurements.  
- Recommendation: clear acceptance justified despite limited domain scope or incremental novelty due to robust, reproducible experiments and notable empirical achievement.
