- Presents an on-policy deep RL method augmented with auxiliary intrinsic variables for goal-conditioned tasks, implementing a form of universal value function and citing related work.

- Main technical contribution is effectively distilling and integrating several existing ideas to solve complex 3D navigation problems.

- Contribution framing is weak; the distilled/integrated contributions should be stated more explicitly in the abstract and introduction.

- Evaluation lacks analysis of failure modes, e.g., when the model fails to generalize to novel or dynamically changing goals.

- On-policy design raises a risk of catastrophic forgetting if the agent is not continually exposed to diverse or past goals; authors should discuss mitigation mechanisms.

- Claims of generality need broader validation—e.g., experiments in other domains like Atari (using RAM as intrinsic variables) would strengthen the case.

- Empirically, the method shows clear advantages on the chosen tasks and provides valuable experimental insights for future goal-conditioned agents.
