- Introduces a novel deep RL algorithm that achieves superior performance and generalization in complex 3D environments and won the Visual Doom AI competition.

- Core technical idea: predict auxiliary low-dimensional observations (e.g., health, ammo) as supervised targets conditioned on a user-specified goal vector and the agent's current action.

- Action selection is done by choosing the action predicted to maximize the goal-conditioned outcome.

- Learning is fully supervised via Monte Carlo estimation rather than temporal-difference learning, distinguishing it from successor representations.

- Paper is well-situated relative to prior work on future-state prediction and goal-driven function approximation.

- Key contributions: Monte Carlo estimation focus, use of low-dimensional measurements as targets, parametrized goals, and strong empirical comparisons to prior methods.

- Experimental results are strong and demonstrate policies that generalize to moderate goal changes without additional training.

- Paper is well-communicated and presents convincing empirical evidence.

- Concern 1: Training uses Monte Carlo regression that assumes on-policy data but samples from a replay buffer (off-policy), creating a policy mismatch that needs explicit discussion.

- Concern 2: Method relies on privileged low-dimensional metadata unavailable to baseline algorithms, and the authors should more prominently acknowledge this limitation and its implications for real-world applicability.
