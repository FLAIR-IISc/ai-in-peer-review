- Decision: Accept — straightforward, effective method that achieves state-of-the-art results on a difficult benchmark.  
- Core idea: Frame sensorimotor control as supervised Direct Future Prediction (DFP), predicting future changes in a vector of intrinsic measurements conditioned on sensory input and an action.  
- Supervision signal: Uses a temporally dense, multi-dimensional measurement stream (vs. a scalar reward) and accepts a goal vector to enable dynamic objectives at test time.  
- Empirical domain: Demonstrated in the first-person visual Doom environment.  
- Results: Significantly outperforms deep RL baselines (DQN, A3C) and won the 2016 Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch track.  
- Experimental rigor: Strong baselines, comprehensive ablation studies, and generalization experiments support core claims.  
- Ablations: Show vectorial measurements and multi-horizon predictions are crucial for performance.  
- Clarity & reproducibility: Paper is well-written with sufficient architectural and training details.  
- Positioning: Appropriately situated relative to forward models, intrinsic motivation, and supervised control literature.  
- Limitations: Novelty could be seen as incremental and evaluation is mainly limited to Doom; broader domain tests would strengthen claims.  
- Minor suggestions: Clarify Doom as 2.5D vs 3D, avoid confusing notation like "pat" in Eq.(2), and repeat metric names in result table headers for readability.
