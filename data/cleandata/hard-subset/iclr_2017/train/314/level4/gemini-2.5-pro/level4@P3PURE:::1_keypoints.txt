- Empirical paper introduces a novel Deep RL algorithm that performs strongly and generalizes across goals/environments using raw sensory data from complex 3D worlds.
- Algorithm won the Visual Doom AI competition.
- Key idea: use additional low-dimensional environment observations (e.g., health, ammo) as supervised prediction targets.
- Predictions are conditioned on a goal vector and the current action.
- Action selection is done by choosing the action that maximizes the predicted outcome for the desired goal.
- Training uses Monte Carlo regression (fully supervised) rather than temporal-difference (TD) learning or successor representations.
- Main contributions: focus on Monte Carlo estimation over TD, use of low-dimensional measurements, parameterized goals, and strong empirical comparisons to prior work.
- Empirical results show generalizable policies that can adapt to limited goal changes without further training.
- Paper is well-written and the empirical evidence is convincing and of significant interest.
- Minor concern: Monte Carlo regression assumes on-policy expectations but training uses an off-policy replay buffer—implications should be discussed.
- Minor concern: algorithm benefits from privileged low-dimensional metadata that competing methods lack; limitations and applicability where such measurements are unavailable should be acknowledged.
