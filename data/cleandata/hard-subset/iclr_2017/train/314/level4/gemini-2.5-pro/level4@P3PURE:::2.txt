This paper presents an on-policy deep reinforcement learning method that incorporates auxiliary intrinsic variables. The method is a specific instance of a universal value function-based approach, and the authors correctly cite the relevant prior work. The primary technical contribution appears to be the successful distillation and integration of several existing ideas to solve complex 3D navigation problems. This contribution, however, should be stated more explicitly in the abstract and introduction.

The paper would be strengthened by an analysis of the approach's failure modes. For example, under what circumstances does the model have difficulty generalizing to changing goals? There are also potential conceptual problems to address. Since the method is on-policy, the agent is likely susceptible to catastrophic forgetting if it does not repeatedly train on goals from the distant past.

Given that the main contribution of the paper is to integrate several key ideas and demonstrate an empirical advantage, the results would be more compelling if they were validated on other domains. For instance, showing results on Atari games (perhaps using the RAM state as the intrinsic variable) would significantly bolster the paper's claims.

Overall, this paper shows a clear empirical advantage for the proposed formulation. The experimental insights it provides could be valuable for the development of future agents.
