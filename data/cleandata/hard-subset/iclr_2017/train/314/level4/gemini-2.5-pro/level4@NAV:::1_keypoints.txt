- Empirical paper introducing a novel DRL algorithm that wins the Visual Doom AI competition and handles raw sensory 3D inputs.
- Algorithm trains supervised predictors of low-dimensional measurements (e.g., health, ammo) conditioned on action and a fixed goal vector.
- At deployment, the agent selects actions by maximizing predicted outcomes for the desired goal.
- Method differs from successor features by using purely supervised learning (Monte Carlo targets) rather than TD-based temporal relationships.
- Main contributions: Monte Carlo estimation instead of TD, strategic use of low-dimensional measurements, parametrized goals, and thorough empirical comparisons.
- Demonstrates superior performance and policies that generalize to moderate goal changes without retraining.
- Paper is well-written and presents persuasive empirical results of high community interest.
- Minor concern: Monte Carlo regression assumes on-policy returns but training uses off-policy replay-buffer data—this approximation should be discussed.
- Minor concern: Algorithm benefits from metadata identifying useful measurements, an advantage not shared by baselines; limitations in environments without such measurements should be stated.
