- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future changes in low-dimensional measurements from pixels, conditioned on action and a goal vector, and pick actions that best satisfy the goal.  
- Reframes sensorimotor control as supervised, Monte‑Carlo-style future prediction rather than scalar reward maximization.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results in ViZDoom, outperforming deep RL baselines (A3C, DQN) and winning the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Key contributions: simple goal-directed supervised formulation, state-of-the-art performance vs RL baselines, and generalization to unseen environments and dynamic goals.  
- Decision: Accept — paper is strong, convincing, and of significant community interest.  
- Main reason 1: exceptional empirical performance combined with method simplicity.  
- Main reason 2: strong generalization and flexibility — single trained model handles varied environments and novel, changing goals without retraining.  
- Supporting evidence: comparisons across four difficulty levels show DFP’s superiority, especially in complex “Battle” scenarios with rich measurement streams.  
- Generalization results: training with varied textures yields robust transfer to new settings; goal-agnostic training enables flexible, conflicting-goal behavior without fine-tuning.  
- Ablations: vectorial measurement streams and multi-horizon prediction are critical design choices for success.  
- Feedback 1: potential off-policy learning mismatch — replay buffer with Monte Carlo targets assumes current policy; recommend brief discussion of why this doesn’t harm performance.  
- Feedback 2: reliance on pre-defined low-dimensional measurements is a structural assumption; discuss applicability limits where such measurements aren’t available.  
- Questions: how sensitive are training stability and performance to replay memory size and policy update frequency?  
- Questions: how were measurements chosen, and how sensitive is performance to this choice (e.g., predicting only frag count vs full measurement vector)?
