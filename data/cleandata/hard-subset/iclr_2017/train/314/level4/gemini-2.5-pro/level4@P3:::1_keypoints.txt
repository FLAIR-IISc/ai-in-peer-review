- Novel formulation: cast sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future low-dimensional measurements instead of standard RL value functions.  
- Method (DFP): predicts future measurement changes conditioned on current pixels, measurements, a candidate action, and a goal vector.  
- Dense, multi-dimensional supervision provides a richer training signal than scalar reward, improving stability and learning.  
- Goal-conditioning allows a single, goal-agnostic policy to pursue changing goals at test time without retraining.  
- Strong empirical performance in Visual Doom: DFP outperforms A3C, DQN, and DSR, especially on complex Battle tasks (up to 6× A3C).  
- Extensive experiments, ablation studies, and generalization tests back the claims (see Tables 1–4 and Figure 3).  
- Ablation results indicate vectorial feedback and multi-horizon prediction are important contributors to success.  
- Victory in the Visual Doom AI Competition provides unbiased validation of generalization to unseen environments.  
- Experimental methodology is sound and transparent, with fair baseline tuning and comparisons.  
- Concern: training uses experience replay (off-policy data), creating a potential mismatch with the Monte Carlo targets assumed from the current policy.  
- Concern: relies on a pre-defined, low-dimensional “measurement” stream (e.g., health, ammo), which may not be available in all environments.  
- Question: Can the method identify or learn such measurement streams from raw sensory input when they are not provided?  
- Question: How does using older-policy rollouts in replay affect stability or create feedback-loop risks, and how is this mitigated?
