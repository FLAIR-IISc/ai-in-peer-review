- Introduces Direct Future Prediction (DFP), an on-policy method that predicts future intrinsic game measurements instead of a single reward scalar.
- Model predicts a future measurement vector (health, ammunition, frags) and uses a user-specified goal vector to weight these predictions.
- At test time the agent selects actions that maximize the expected goal-weighted sum of predicted future measurements.
- All experiments are conducted in the VizDoom environment; method won the 2016 VizDoom competition.
- Empirical results show DFP generally outperforms or matches strong baselines (A3C, DQN, DSR), with A3C reaching optimality in one scenario.
- A thorough ablation study demonstrates each architectural component contributes positively to performance.
- Paper is well-situated in prior work on auxiliary prediction, forward modeling, and intrinsic motivation, with adequate referencing.
- Minor: Doom should be described as 2.5D/pseudo-3D for technical precision.
- Minor: Notation issues — using 'P' for prediction may be confused with probability.
- Minor: Reuse of index 'j' in different fonts in Eq. (6) could confuse readers.
- Minor: Results tables should explicitly state units for heterogeneous measurements (e.g., Table 1).
- Recommendation: Accept — significant, well-validated contribution with strong results and good reproducibility, though broader-environment experiments could strengthen claims.
