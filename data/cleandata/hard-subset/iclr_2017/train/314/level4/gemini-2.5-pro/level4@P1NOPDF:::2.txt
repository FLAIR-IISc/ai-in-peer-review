This paper presents an on-policy deep reinforcement learning method augmented with auxiliary intrinsic variables.

- The method is a special case of an approach based on universal value functions, and the authors correctly cite the relevant literature. The primary technical contribution of this paper appears to be the synthesis of several existing ideas to solve complex 3D navigation problems. This contribution should be stated more clearly in the abstract and introduction to better frame the work.

- The paper would be significantly strengthened by an analysis of the approach's failure modes. For instance, under what circumstances does the model have difficulty generalizing to changing goals? There is also an unaddressed conceptual problem: as an on-policy method, the agent is susceptible to catastrophic forgetting if it does not repeatedly train on goals from the distant past.

- Since the main contribution of this paper is the successful integration of several key ideas to show an empirical advantage, the evaluation would be more compelling with results from other domains. Applying the method to Atari games, for example (perhaps using the RAM state as the intrinsic variable), would provide a stronger test of the approach's generality.

Overall, this paper demonstrates a clear empirical advantage for the proposed formulation. The experimental insights it provides are valuable and will likely be beneficial for the design of future agents.
