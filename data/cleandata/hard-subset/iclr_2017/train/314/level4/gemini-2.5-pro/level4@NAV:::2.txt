This work introduces an on-policy deep reinforcement learning algorithm augmented with auxiliary intrinsic variables. The approach can be framed as a particular instance of a universal value function, and the authors are commendable for appropriately referencing prior work in this area. The primary contribution appears to be the synthesis and effective application of several existing concepts to solve 3D navigation tasks. To strengthen the paper, this contribution should be more explicitly articulated in the abstract and introduction.

The evaluation would be substantially strengthened by an analysis of the method's failure modes. For instance, an investigation into the conditions under which the model fails to generalize to changing goals would be insightful. There are also conceptual concerns; the on-policy nature of the algorithm makes it susceptible to catastrophic forgetting unless the agent systematically revisits goals from earlier in its training history.

Given that the paper's central claim is the empirical benefit derived from integrating existing techniques, its conclusions would be more robust if validated across a broader range of domains. Demonstrating efficacy on standard benchmarks like Atari, perhaps by utilizing game ROM states as the intrinsic variables, would significantly bolster the paper's claims of generality.

Overall, the paper successfully demonstrates a clear empirical advantage for the proposed formulation. The experimental insights presented are valuable and are likely to inform the development of future goal-conditioned agents.
