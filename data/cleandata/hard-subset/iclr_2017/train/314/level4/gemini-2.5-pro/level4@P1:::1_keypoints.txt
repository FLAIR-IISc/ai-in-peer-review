- Introduces Direct Future Prediction (DFP): train a network to predict future low-dimensional measurements from high-dimensional sensory input, conditioned on actions and a goal vector.  
- Frames sensorimotor control as supervised learning using rich, temporally dense, multi-dimensional supervision instead of sparse scalar rewards.  
- Uses Monte Carlo-style prediction targets rather than bootstrapped targets common in RL.  
- At test time, selects actions by choosing the action predicted to best achieve the current goal.  
- Evaluated in challenging 3D first-person environments (Doom) with strong empirical performance.  
- DFP significantly outperforms state-of-the-art deep RL baselines (DQN, A3C), especially as task complexity increases.  
- Demonstrates strong generalization to visually distinct, unseen environments (randomly-textured mazes).  
- Supports goal-agnostic training: agents trained on randomized goals can pursue new goals at test time with little performance loss.  
- Ablation study shows multi-dimensional measurement vectors and multi-horizon prediction are crucial for success.  
- Real-world validation: a model trained with DFP won the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Suggests discussing implications of using experience replay with Monte Carlo targets, since replay introduces off-policy data.  
- Recommends explicitly addressing reliance on privileged low-dimensional measurements and limitations in domains lacking such measurements.
