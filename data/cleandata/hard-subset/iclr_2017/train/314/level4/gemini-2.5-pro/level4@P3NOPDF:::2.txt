This paper presents an on-policy deep reinforcement learning method that incorporates auxiliary intrinsic variables to guide the agent. The approach can be framed as a specific instance of the Universal Value Function (UVF) framework, and the authors correctly situate their work within this context by citing the relevant literature. The primary contribution appears to be the successful distillation and integration of several existing ideas to create a system that demonstrates strong performance on complex 3D navigation tasks. While this is a valuable engineering and empirical contribution, the paper would benefit from a more explicit statement of its novel contributions in the abstract and introduction.

The main strength of the paper lies in its clear empirical results. The authors show a significant performance advantage for their proposed formulation on a challenging domain. The experimental insights and the specific combination of techniques presented could be valuable for other researchers working on similar goal-oriented navigation problems.

However, the paper has a few weaknesses that should be addressed:

1.  **Analysis of Failure Modes:** The paper effectively demonstrates the success of the approach but would be strengthened by an analysis of its limitations and failure modes. Under what circumstances does the model fail to generalize to new or changing goals? An investigation into cases where the agent's performance degrades would provide a more complete picture of the method's capabilities and robustness.

2.  **Catastrophic Forgetting:** A potential conceptual issue arises from the use of an on-policy learning algorithm. On-policy methods are susceptible to catastrophic forgetting, where the agent forgets previously learned skills. In this context, if the goal-sampling strategy does not ensure that goals from the distant past are periodically revisited, the agent's ability to reach those goals could degrade over time. The authors should discuss this potential limitation and clarify whether any mechanisms are in place to mitigate it.

3.  **Generality of the Approach:** Given that the paper's main contribution is the empirical validation of an integrated approach rather than a fundamentally new algorithm, its claims would be significantly bolstered by demonstrating effectiveness in other domains. The current evaluation is confined to 3D navigation. Testing the method on a different class of problems, such as the Atari suite (perhaps using the RAM state as the intrinsic variable), would provide a more convincing test of its generality and broader applicability.

In summary, this paper presents a well-executed empirical study that combines several powerful ideas to achieve impressive results in 3D navigation. The experimental insights are likely to be valuable to the community. While the novelty of the underlying components is limited, the successful synthesis is a noteworthy contribution. The work could be improved by providing a deeper analysis of the method's limitations, addressing the potential for catastrophic forgetting, and broadening the experimental evaluation to demonstrate the generality of the approach.
