- Introduces a DRL algorithm that predicts future low-dimensional measurements from raw sensory data, conditioned on a goal vector and current action.
- Training uses supervised Monte Carlo estimation rather than temporal-difference learning; at test time actions are chosen to maximize predicted goal-specific outcomes.
- Algorithm is purely supervised (unlike successor representations) and does not enforce temporal-difference relationships between successive predictions.
- Major contributions: Monte Carlo estimation over TD, focusing on low-dimensional measurements instead of full states, parameterized goals, and strong empirical comparisons.
- Achieved top performance in the Visual Doom AI competition, demonstrating superior empirical performance.
- Learned policies generalize across different goals and novel 3D environments and can adapt to limited goal changes without additional training.
- Paper provides a good overview of related work on future-state prediction and goal-conditioned function approximators.
- Potential weakness: Monte Carlo training assumes on-policy rollouts, but data come from a replay buffer of past policies—on-policy/off-policy mismatch should be discussed.
- Limitation: method relies on privileged metadata specifying which measurements to predict, giving an advantage over baselines and restricting applicability where such measurements are unavailable.
- Paper is well-written, results are compelling, and the work is of clear interest to the community.
