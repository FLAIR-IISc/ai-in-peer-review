- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): supervised learning to predict future low-dimensional measurements from pixels, action, and goal, selecting actions that maximize predicted outcomes.
- Frames sensorimotor control as supervised Monte Carlo-style prediction rather than traditional reinforcement learning.
- Leverages dense, multivariate measurement streams (e.g., health, ammo) as a rich supervisory signal to stabilize and accelerate training.
- Demonstrates DFP outperforms state-of-the-art deep RL baselines (A3C, DQN) on complex 3D first-person tasks.
- Shows particularly large gains in "Battle" scenarios (e.g., ~6x improvement over A3C in D3).
- Key advantage: learns general, goal-conditioned skills without a fixed training goal, enabling dynamic goals at test time without retraining.
- Ablation study: predicting multiple measurements and multiple future timesteps is significantly more effective than predicting a single scalar (frags).
- Trained with randomized goals generalizes nearly as well to specific tasks and far better to novel objectives (e.g., pacifist strategy).
- Generalizes across visually distinct, unseen environments; validated by winning the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Paper is well-written, experiments are thorough, and empirical results are compelling and of interest to the community.
- Improvement point: training uses replay buffer, introducing off-policy Monte Carlo targets—paper should acknowledge and discuss this approximation.
- Improvement point: reliance on curated, environment-provided measurements is a stronger assumption than standard RL and limits applicability; discuss limitations and alternatives.
- Question: clarify why off-policy training data does not harm stability or performance and whether buffer size or on-policy training was tested.
- Question: how would the method apply to real-world domains (e.g., robotics) where clean, pre-defined measurements are unavailable or must be learned?
- Question: provide intuition why multi-timescale prediction helps—implicit regularization vs. better modeling of environment dynamics?
