- Introduces Direct Future Prediction (DFP): reframe control as supervised prediction of future intrinsic measurements for sensorimotor control.  
- Trains agent from raw pixels, current measurements, and a goal; uses observed outcomes as dense supervision enabling goal-agnostic training.  
- Evaluated in Visual Doom and significantly outperforms deep RL baselines (A3C, DQN); won 2016 Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch on unseen maps.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong empirical results and a clear, well-executed central idea.  
- Exceptional empirical performance: DFP beats A3C by factors of ~6 and ~2.5 in the most challenging scenarios.  
- Conceptual strength: simple, effective supervised predictive framework leveraging multivariate feedback and multi-step predictions.  
- Ablation study: predicting multiple measurements and multiple future time steps substantially improves performance versus single scalar predictions.  
- Generalization: robust to random textures and generalizes to unseen map layouts in competition settings.  
- Goal-conditioned flexibility: a goal-agnostic agent performs nearly as well as task-specific training and generalizes better to novel goals (e.g., aggressive vs. pacifist).  
- Minor terminology suggestion: describe Doom as 2.5D rather than 3D for precision.  
- Notation clarity: consider refining symbols (e.g., F, p, P) and avoid reusing j in conflicting roles to reduce ambiguity.  
- Table readability: repeat measurement units in column headers (e.g., "Avg. Health," "Avg. Frags") for clarity.  
- Generality suggestion: discuss applicability to other domains (e.g., robotic manipulation or other game genres) where intrinsic measurements differ.  
- Question: have you tried non-linear or more complex goal parameterizations (e.g., thresholds or multi-objective trade-offs)?  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to the chosen temporal offsets τ = {1,2,4,8,16,32}, and would denser/ sparser sets change behavior?  
- Question: how do ε-greedy exploration and randomized-goal training interact; does randomized goal pursuit provide more structured exploration than ε-greedy?
