### Summary

This paper introduces a novel on-policy method, Direct Future Prediction (DFP), for learning agent behavior by predicting future intrinsic measurements. The agent is trained to predict a sequence of future values for a set of measurements (e.g., health, ammunition, frags) conditioned on its current state and a "goal" vector. This goal vector, provided as input, specifies the relative importance of each measurement, allowing the agent's behavior to be directed and controlled at test time by simply altering these weights. The method is evaluated extensively in the VizDoom environment, a challenging first-person shooter domain. The results are highly impressive, as the DFP agent won the 2016 VizDoom competition, demonstrating its state-of-the-art performance.

### Strengths

1.  **Exceptional Empirical Performance:** The most compelling strength of this work is its outstanding performance. Achieving first place in the 2016 VizDoom competition is a significant accomplishment and provides strong evidence for the method's effectiveness in a complex and competitive environment.

2.  **Thorough Experimental Validation:** The experimental section is sound and comprehensive.
    *   **Comparative Analysis:** The authors provide rigorous comparisons against strong deep reinforcement learning baselines, including A3C and DQN. DFP is shown to outperform these methods, or match them in scenarios where performance reaches a ceiling. The paper also includes a thoughtful comparison with DSR, a recent and conceptually similar approach, which helps to position the work within the current literature.
    *   **Ablation Study:** The paper includes a valuable ablation study that systematically investigates the contribution of the model's different components. This analysis supports the authors' thesis that the "added complexity" of the DFP architecture is justified and that each element is beneficial to its overall performance.

3.  **Well-Presented and Reproducible:** The paper is clearly written, and the appendix provides sufficient detail to facilitate the reproduction of the results. This commitment to clarity and reproducibility is a commendable aspect of the work. The conceptual link to related areas like intrinsic motivation, auxiliary variable prediction, and forward modeling is well-established, with adequate citation of prior work.

### Areas for Improvement

While the paper is strong, a few minor points could be addressed to further improve its clarity and precision.

1.  **Generalizability:** While the results on VizDoom are compelling, the evaluation is confined to a single (albeit complex) environment. Demonstrating the method's applicability to other domains, such as the Atari suite or continuous control tasks, would further strengthen the paper's claims of general utility.

2.  **Minor Comments on Presentation (Nitpicks):**
    *   **Environment Description:** The paper describes Doom as a 3D environment. For the sake of precision, it would be more accurate to describe it as a 2.5D environment presented with a 3D perspective, as the vertical dimension is not a fully actionable or discriminative aspect of the game mechanics.
    *   **Notation:** The notation could be slightly refined for clarity. The use of "P" in Equation (2) to denote a prediction, while defined, could be momentarily misleading for readers accustomed to "P" representing a probability distribution. Similarly, the dual use of the index "j" (albeit in different fonts) in Equation (6) could cause a brief moment of confusion.
    *   **Table Clarity:** The results tables, particularly Table 1, present heterogeneous measurements. It would be helpful to repeat the units of measurement in the table headers or captions to improve readability and prevent any potential ambiguity for the reader.

### Recommendation

This is a **clear accept**.

One could argue that the novelty is incremental or that the experiments could have been conducted on a wider range of environments. However, I believe the paper's strengths far outweigh these potential criticisms. The work presents a well-executed, no-nonsense approach that achieves state-of-the-art results on a challenging benchmark. Publishing "milestone" papers that are experimentally sound and demonstrate clear, practical success is of great value to the research community. The DFP method is an important contribution, and this paper effectively communicates its design, implementation, and superior performance.
