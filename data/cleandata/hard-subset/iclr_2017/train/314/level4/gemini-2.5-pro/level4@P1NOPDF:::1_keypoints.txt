- Introduces a new algorithm that predicts low-dimensional, game-provided measurements (e.g., health, ammo) conditioned on a user-specified goal vector and the current action.
- At test time, actions are chosen by selecting the action that maximizes the predicted outcome with respect to the specified goal.
- Learning is fully supervised Monte Carlo regression rather than TD-based; predictions for current and next states are not temporally linked.
- Distinct from successor representations: the approach uses supervised learning with no TD relationships.
- Key contributions: emphasis on Monte Carlo estimation, use of low-dimensional measurements, a parameterized-goal framework, and extensive empirical comparisons.
- Empirically strong results in novel 3D environments and winner of the Visual Doom AI competition.
- Demonstrates improved generalization—policies can adapt to moderate changes in goal vectors without additional training.
- Paper is well-written and the empirical results are compelling and relevant to the deep RL community.
- Minor concern: Monte Carlo regression assumes on-policy trajectories but training uses off-policy replay buffer data; this approximation needs explicit discussion.
- Minor concern: Method relies on access to structured low-dimensional measurements (metadata), limiting applicability in environments without such measurements and differing from raw-sensor-only baselines.
