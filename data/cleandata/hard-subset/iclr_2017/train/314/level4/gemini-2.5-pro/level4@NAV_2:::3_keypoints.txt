- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future intrinsic measurements from current raw sensory input, current measurements, and a goal as a supervised learning task.  
- Provides dense, rich self-supervision allowing training without a fixed objective and enabling dynamic goal pursuit at test time.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance and won the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Significantly outperforms deep RL baselines (DQN, A3C), especially on complex combat and navigation tasks.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and methodologically sound.  
- Ablation study shows vectorial measurement streams outperform scalar rewards and multi-step prediction is beneficial.  
- Goal-agnostic training demonstrates learning general skills and adapting to novel objectives nearly as well as task-specific training.  
- Positions work well within related literature and presents a simple, effective formulation.  
- Appendices include architecture and baseline details, aiding reproducibility.  
- Minor suggested fixes: note Doom is often described as "2.5D"; avoid using `p` (may be confused with probability); avoid reusing `j` for different meanings; add units to result table columns (e.g., Health (%), Frags (count)).
