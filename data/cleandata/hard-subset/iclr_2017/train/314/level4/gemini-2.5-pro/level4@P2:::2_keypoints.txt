- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future measurement vectors conditioned on state, action, and goal, then choose actions that best satisfy the goal.  
- Demonstrates DFP in Doom, showing agents outperform deep RL baselines and winning the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Strong empirical validation: significantly outperforms A3C and DQN across increasingly difficult scenarios.  
- Elegant, goal-conditioned supervised formulation that uses dense, multi-dimensional measurements to avoid sparse-reward issues.  
- Ablations show vectorial feedback and multi-horizon prediction are critical to high performance.  
- Goal-agnostic training yields flexible policies that generalize to new, unseen goals (e.g., pacifist strategy).  
- Good literature positioning, synthesizing prior ideas (universal value functions, supervised control) into an effective framework.  
- Suggest sharpening abstract/introduction to emphasize the contribution as a powerful synthesis and large-scale validation.  
- Recommend discussing failure modes and limits of goal generalization, especially for out-of-distribution test goals.  
- Ask authors to clarify experience replay’s interaction with training stability and potential catastrophic forgetting, and to report sensitivity to measurement selection/noisy or reduced measurement vectors.
