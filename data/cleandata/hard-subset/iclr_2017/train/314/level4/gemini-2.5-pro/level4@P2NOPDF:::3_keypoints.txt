- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP), an on-policy RL method that predicts future intrinsic measurement sequences instead of a single value function.  
- Experiments are conducted exclusively in the VizDoom environment.  
- Predicts sequences of measurement triplets (health, ammunition, frags) conditioned on a goal triplet that dynamically weights measurements.  
- Goal conditioning encourages diverse exploration during training and lets the agent optimize different long-term objectives at test time.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance, including winning the 2016 VizDoom competition.  
- Provides rigorous experiments with comparisons to strong baselines (A3C, DQN, DSR) where DFP matches or outperforms them across scenarios.  
- Includes a comprehensive ablation study that justifies the model’s design choices.  
- Situates the work among forward modeling, auxiliary task prediction, and intrinsic motivation literature, with an adequate prior-work review.  
- Minor presentation suggestions: clarify Doom as 2.5D rather than 3D.  
- Minor notation issues: avoid using "P" for predictions (confusion with probability) and avoid overloading the index "j."  
- Recommend adding units to heterogeneous result table columns (e.g., Table 1) for clarity.  
- Overall recommendation: clear accept—significant empirical achievement, experimentally sound, reproducible, though broader environment evaluation could strengthen generality.
