- Reframes sensorimotor control as goal-conditioned supervised learning predicting future low‑dimensional measurements (Direct Future Prediction, DFP).
- DFP uses a deep network that integrates pixels, measurements, and goals to predict outcomes for candidate actions.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance in Visual Doom, outperforming A3C and DQN, especially on complex tasks.
- Shows impressive generalization to unseen environments and dynamically specified goals; won Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Decision: Accept.
- Primary reason 1: exceptional empirical performance with large margins over state‑of‑the‑art baselines.
- Primary reason 2: valuable conceptual insight that supervised future prediction with multivariate feedback is a viable alternative to value‑based RL.
- Experiments show stark superiority over baselines (e.g., ~6x frags vs A3C in scenario D3) with fair architectural/hyperparameter comparisons.
- Generalization evidence: training on varied textures and competition success validate performance in unseen settings.
- Goal‑agnostic training (randomized goals) yields nearly as good task performance and much greater flexibility at test time.
- Ablation study shows predicting multiple measurements outperforms predicting a single scalar reward, supporting the multivariate‑feedback hypothesis.
- Main novelty is the effective synthesis and large‑scale validation of existing ideas (direct prediction, vectorial feedback, goal conditioning).
- Suggestions: explicitly frame contribution as empirical synthesis, analyze failure modes (long‑term planning limits), discuss catastrophic forgetting with on‑policy data, and broader applicability to other domains.
- Questions for authors: how well does goal generalization hold for anti‑correlated or adversarial goals; how was experience memory size M chosen and does forgetting occur; how does DFP perform in domains with only sparse scalar rewards?
