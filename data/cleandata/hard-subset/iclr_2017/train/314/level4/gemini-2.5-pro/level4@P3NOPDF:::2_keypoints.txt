- Proposes an on-policy deep RL method using auxiliary intrinsic variables to guide agents, framed as a specific instance of the Universal Value Function (UVF) framework.
- Correctly cites and situates the work within relevant UVF and related literature.
- Main contribution is the distillation and integration of several existing ideas into a coherent system rather than a fundamentally new algorithm.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on challenging 3D navigation tasks, with clear and convincing experimental results.
- Experimental insights and the particular combination of techniques are likely useful to researchers in goal-oriented navigation.
- Paper should state its novel contributions more explicitly in the abstract and introduction.
- Lacks analysis of failure modes; needs investigation into when and how the method fails to generalize to new or changing goals.
- Raises concern about catastrophic forgetting due to on-policy learning; authors should discuss whether goal-sampling revisits past goals or other mitigations exist.
- Evaluation is limited to 3D navigation; testing on other domains (e.g., Atari/RAM-based tasks) would strengthen claims of generality and broader applicability.
