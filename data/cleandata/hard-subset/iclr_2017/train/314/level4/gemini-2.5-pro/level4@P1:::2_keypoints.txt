- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): supervise an agent to predict future changes in multi-dimensional measurements conditioned on sensory input, current measurements, goal, and action to select actions that best satisfy the goal.  
- DFP can be trained without a fixed goal and generalizes to dynamically specified goals at test time.  
- Strong empirical results: DFP significantly outperforms state-of-the-art deep RL baselines across multiple challenging 3D Doom scenarios.  
- Demonstrated robustness and generalization, including success in the Visual Doom AI Competition and across environments with different textures.  
- In complex "Battle" scenarios, DFP outperforms A3C by a factor of ~2.5–6 in frag count.  
- Effective use of dense, multi-dimensional measurement streams as supervision; ablation shows vectorial feedback is critical to performance.  
- Architecture choices—splitting prediction into expectation and action streams and predicting multiple future time steps—are well-motivated and validated by ablations.  
- Conceptually a practical synthesis of existing ideas (universal value functions, Monte Carlo), with the main contribution being empirical and engineering success rather than a wholly novel theory.  
- Suggests clearer positioning of the contribution as a novel synthesis and strong empirical application.  
- Calls for analysis of limitations and failure modes, especially when test goals differ substantially from the training distribution.  
- Recommends testing on other domains (e.g., Atari) to support claims of broad applicability where measurement vectors are less obvious.  
- Raises question about catastrophic forgetting: whether randomized-goal training retains competence when goal distributions shift over time without continual diverse retraining.
