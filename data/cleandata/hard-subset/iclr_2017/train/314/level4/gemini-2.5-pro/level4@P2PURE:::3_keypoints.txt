- Proposes an on-policy method that predicts future intrinsic measurements (health, ammo, frags) conditioned on a goal triplet to guide exploration.
- Experiments are limited to VizDoom, training the model to predict sequences of measurement triplets and acting to maximize predicted long-term outcomes for a fixed goal.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance, including winning the 2016 VizDoom competition.
- Shows DFP generally outperforms strong baselines (A3C, DQN, DSR), and performs comparably where baselines reach optimal policy (e.g., A3C in D1).
- Provides a thorough ablation study that supports the necessity of the model’s components.
- Situates the work within reinforcement learning themes (intrinsic motivation, auxiliary predictions, forward modeling) and references prior work adequately.
- Minor clarity issues: Doom should be described as 2.5D rather than fully 3D.
- Notational concerns: using "P" for prediction may be misleading (conventionally a probability), and variable "j" appears with inconsistent fonts in Eq. (6).
- Presentation suggestion: explicitly state units in heterogeneous result tables (e.g., Table 1) for readability.
- Possible limitations: experiments confined to a single environment and conceptual novelty could be seen as incremental.
- Recommendation: strong accept — methodologically sound, reproducible, and a milestone result deserving publication.
