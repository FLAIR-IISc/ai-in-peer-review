- Empirical work introducing a novel RL algorithm that learns from raw sensory data and excels in complex 3D environments.  
- Algorithm frames decision-making as a supervised prediction task rather than TD learning.  
- Uses Monte Carlo estimates as supervised regression targets, avoiding bootstrapped TD methods.  
- Predicts low-dimensional, human-interpretable measurements (e.g., health, ammo) instead of full future states.  
- Conditions predictions on both the agent's action and a parameterized goal vector provided as input.  
- Goal vector enables generalization across different goals and environmental configurations at test time.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance and outperforms relevant baselines.  
- Winning entry in the Visual Doom AI competition, highlighting practical effectiveness.  
- Paper is well-written, methodology clearly explained, and experiments are compelling.  
- Minor concern: replay buffer introduces off-policy data, so Monte Carlo targets may not match the current policy.  
- Minor concern: reliance on privileged, low-dimensional measurements may give an advantage and limits applicability where such signals are unavailable.
