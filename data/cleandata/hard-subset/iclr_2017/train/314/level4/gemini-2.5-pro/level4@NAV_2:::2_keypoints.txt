- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): supervise action selection by predicting future low-dimensional measurements from pixels, action, and a goal.  
- Trained on self-generated experience with parameterized goals, enabling dynamic goal pursuit at test time.  
- Strong empirical results: significantly outperforms deep RL baselines (DQN, A3C) on challenging Doom tasks and won the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Demonstrates large gains (2.5–6x) over A3C in complex "Battle" scenarios, showing practical value in immersive environments.  
- Valuable formulation: synthesizes existing ideas into a simple, scalable supervised-learning architecture for control using vectorial, multi-step feedback.  
- Ablation studies confirm vectorial feedback and multi-step prediction are critical to performance.  
- Goal-agnostic training generalizes to novel goals not seen during training.  
- Robustness/generalization shown across different textures and environments.  
- Not a fundamentally new learning paradigm, but contribution lies in the elegant formulation and extensive empirical validation.  
- Suggest clarifying in the introduction that the main contribution is the synthesis and empirical demonstration, not a novel theory.  
- Recommend adding a discussion of limitations and failure modes (e.g., environments with extremely sparse measurements where TD methods may be preferable).  
- Suggest expanding empirical scope beyond Doom (e.g., Arcade Learning Environment using RAM measurements) to delineate where DFP is most effective.  
- Question about catastrophic forgetting risk from using a small experience memory if goal distributions shift over long training—does the agent need continual revisiting of goals?  
- Question about specific failure modes in goal generalization, such as handling non-linear or conflicting composite goals (e.g., maximize frags while minimizing ammo use).
