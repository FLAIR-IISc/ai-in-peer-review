- Proposes neural equivalence networks (EQNETs) for continuous semantic representations of mathematical and logical expressions.  
- Aims to represent and infer procedural knowledge by composing equivalence-class representations to capture compositional structure.  
- Claims EQNETs outperform baseline models (TREENNs, RNNs) for learning semantic representations of symbolic expressions.  
- Validates approach with an exhaustive evaluation across diverse algebraic and boolean expression types.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on correctness, experimental soundness, and significant contribution.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly explained, and well-situated in prior literature.  
- Experimental section includes comparisons and an ablation study that support the model’s added complexity.  
- Suggests providing more implementation details (specific hyperparameters and optimization algorithm).  
- Recommends additional visualizations (e.g., t-SNE) of learned representations to illustrate the semantic space.  
- Requests clarification on extending EQNETs to more complex expressions with multiple variables and operators.  
- Asks for more insights into optimization: convergence rates and effects of different hyperparameters.  
- Seeks discussion of potential real-world applications such as automated theorem proving or program synthesis.
