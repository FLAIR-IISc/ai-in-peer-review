- Proposes Neural Equivalence Networks (EQNETs) to learn continuous semantic representations of mathematical and logical expressions.  
- Claims EQNETs capture semantic equivalence even for syntactically different expressions.  
- Presents exhaustive evaluation across diverse symbolic algebraic and boolean expression types.  
- Reports EQNETs significantly outperform existing architectures.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Reason 1: Well-motivated, technically sound approach.  
- Reason 2: Experimental evaluation shows strong effectiveness in representing semantic equivalence.  
- Clear, well-structured presentation of model architecture and training objective.  
- Thorough evaluation demonstrating generalization to unseen equivalence classes and state-of-the-art performance with significant margins.  
- Suggestion: Discuss limitations and future work.  
- Suggestion: Evaluate on more complex expressions (multiple variables, nested expressions).  
- Suggestion: Provide insights/visualizations of learned semantic representations.  
- Question: Provide details on optimization procedure, hyperparameters, and algorithm used.  
- Question: How are exploding/diminishing gradients handled, especially for deep expression trees?  
- Question: Clarify the role and impact of the subexpression forcing mechanism on performance.
