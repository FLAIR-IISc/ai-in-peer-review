- Reframes sensorimotor control as supervised prediction of low-dimensional future measurements conditioned on observation, action, and a goal vector, selecting actions that maximize g^T predicted measurements.
- Introduces Direct Future Prediction (DFP): a goal-conditioned, multihorizon Monte Carlo regressor from raw RGB and measurements with a dueling-style decomposition.
- Empirically, DFP outperforms DQN, A3C, and DSR in four ViZDoom scenarios, generalizes across textures and new goals, and won the Visual Doom Full Deathmatch competition.
- Key contributions: Monte Carlo training focus, using low-dimensional measurements as targets, explicit goal conditioning, and careful empirical ablations and generalization studies.
- Decision: Accept — simple, well-motivated formulation with strong empirical performance and clear communication.
- Tackles complex 3D sensorimotor control and flexible goal pursuit, building on UVFA, predictive representations, and Monte Carlo vs TD distinctions.
- Methodologically coherent: multihorizon prediction of measurement differences, linear goal utility, argmax action selection, and sensible encoder + dueling architecture.
- Extensive empirical support: multiple scenarios, ablations, goal-agnostic training to goal-conditioned testing, and external competition validation.
- Scientifically interesting: challenges TD dominance by showing supervised Monte Carlo prediction over dense measurements can be competitive.
- Replay buffer concern: training uses Monte Carlo targets with replay sampling — quantify buffer size, analyze sensitivity to buffer size and policy shift, and discuss bias.
- Measurement metadata concern: DFP relies on privileged measurements — make this explicit, discuss settings without measurements, and expand ablation on reward-only signals.
- Baseline fairness concern: provide more hyperparameter/search details, compute budgets, seeds, mean±std, and significance tests for DQN/A3C/DSR comparisons.
- Goal-distribution concern: discuss how training-goal distributions affect generalization, including negative weights and curricula over goal magnitudes.
- Scalability/applicability concern: discuss applicability to sparse scalar rewards, continuous actions, noisy/expensive measurements, and comparison to successor features/UVFAs.
- Question — replay specifics: how large is the replay buffer relative to episodes, how often are targets recomputed, and effects of purely on-policy updates?
- Question — horizon sensitivity: how sensitive is performance to chosen horizons and loss weights; would learned weighting or longer horizons help?
- Question — measurement selection: impact of removing a measurement at training on evaluating that metric, and can DFP learn latent measurements via auxiliary learning?
- Question — goal extrapolation: how well does the model extrapolate to goal vectors outside training range and are there calibration plots of predicted vs realized g^T outcomes?
- Question — successor features comparison: any direct comparisons and observed computational/sample-efficiency trade-offs?
- Question — memory/partial observability: does adding recurrence (e.g., LSTM) improve performance in maze and battle tasks?
- Question — continuous actions: any preliminary adaptations of DFP to continuous control (e.g., sampling candidate actions or learning a policy head)?
- Overall: clear, thoughtful, and empirically strong paper with minor, addressable concerns that do not detract from main contributions.
