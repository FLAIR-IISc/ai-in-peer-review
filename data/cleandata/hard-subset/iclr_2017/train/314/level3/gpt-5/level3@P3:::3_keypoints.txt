- Introduces Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future intrinsic measurement changes instead of scalar rewards and select actions by maximizing goal-weighted predicted measurements.
- DFP frames control as supervised learning of multivariate, multi-horizon predictions F(o,a,g) and greedy action selection via g^T F.
- Architecture combines perception, measurement, and goal modules with a dueling-style split (expectation vs action) for multi-action, multi-horizon prediction.
- Empirical setup: ViZDoom experiments predicting (health, ammo, frags) at multiple time offsets; goal vectors steer behavior and exploration.
- Results: DFP matches/surpasses DQN and A3C across scenarios and won the 2016 Visual Doom AI Competition (Full Deathmatch).
- Ablations show multi-measurement and multi-horizon prediction materially improve performance.
- Training is fast and stable from raw pixels, enables goal-agnostic training and test-time goal switching, and yields simple greedy action selection.
- Strong empirical rigor: four ViZDoom benchmarks, clear protocols, multi-horizon targets, and consistent evaluation metrics.
- Comparisons: DFP equals A3C on Basic, outperforms on Navigation, and strongly dominates in Battle scenarios; DSR is slower and worse.
- Generalization: training on multi-texture variants and higher-capacity models improves transfer to new textures and goals.
- Goal-randomized training generalizes to new goals (e.g., pacifist, ammo-drain) nearly as well as main goal training.
- External validation via competition win highlights robustness and practical impact.
- Reproducibility: architecture, horizons, losses, and baselines are described; training speed and episode specs are provided.
- Minor departure from pure on-policy: a small replay buffer is used—authors should discuss off-policy bias and sensitivity to buffer size.
- Motivation/placement: method is well motivated as a supervised Monte-Carlo-style alternative, related to UVFAs, predictive representations, and auxiliary tasks.
- Notation suggestions: avoid using p for predictions and clarify repeated index j in Eq. (6) to prevent confusion.
- Clarify Doom rendering as pseudo-3D (not true 3D) to avoid nitpicks.
- Reporting suggestion: state units/scales or normalization for heterogeneous measurements in every table.
- Baselines caveat: acknowledge that A3C can benefit from auxiliary tasks and ensure apples-to-apples comparisons (e.g., UVFAs on same goals).
- Limitations: limited environment diversity beyond Doom, no recurrence/memory, and discrete-action restriction—discuss extensions to recurrence and continuous actions.
- On-policy claim: briefly justify why a small experience memory does not induce significant off-policy bias.
- Question — goal sampling: how sensitive is performance to the training goal distribution (e.g., [0,1] vs [-1,1]) and does curriculum help?
- Question — measurement design: how were measurements normalized/scaled; were learned embeddings or extra intrinsic signals tried?
- Question — multi-horizon choices: why the specific offsets and terminal weighting (0.5, 0.5, 1) and how sensitive are results to these choices?
- Question — exploration: beyond ε-greedy, did goal variation aid exploration and does changing goal weights during training improve coverage?
- Question — architecture ablation: quantify gains from the expectation/action split and action-normalization versus a single-head predictor.
- Question — off-policy buffer: what is the maximum buffer size before off-policy degradation occurs?
- Question — goal switching: can the agent switch goals mid-episode and respond immediately; any experiments on rapid goal changes?
- Question — statistical robustness: are improvements significant across seeds for hardest scenarios; include variance/error bars for final evaluations.
