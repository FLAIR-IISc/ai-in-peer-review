- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future low-dimensional measurements from pixels and choose actions that maximize utility under a goal vector.
- Introduces architecture with perception/measurement/goal encoders and a dueling action/expectation split to predict multi-horizon measurements for all actions in one pass.
- Supports goal-agnostic training and dynamic test-time goals; reports strong ViZDoom performance including winning the Visual Doom AI Full Deathmatch track.
- Ablations show vectorial measurements and multi-step horizons improve performance.
- Positions DFP relative to UVFA/GVFs and Monte Carlo prediction; novelty is mainly in formulation and engineering integration rather than a new algorithmic primitive.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reasons for acceptance: clear empirical advantages on challenging 3D tasks (including generalization and competition win) and a simple, well-motivated, fast-to-train formulation with useful experimental insights.
- Problem addressed: sensorimotor control from pixels in 3D with changing test-time goals; addresses sparse rewards via dense measurement targets and goal-conditioning.
- Motivation and placement: well-motivated, cites relevant work, but should more explicitly state what is new versus adopted.
- Empirical support: strong across four ViZDoom scenarios, generalization to unseen maps/goals, validated ablations, efficient and stable training with multiple seeds.
- Clarify contributions: explicitly state DFP as a UVFA/GVF special case with linear utilities over measurement features and specify assumptions for when DFP works best.
- Failure-mode analysis requested: evaluate generalization limits to OOD goals, conflicting objectives, non-linear utilities, and missing/noisy measurements.
- Partial observability/long-horizon concerns: reactive model may fail on tasks needing memory; test recurrence or memory-augmented variants.
- Catastrophic forgetting and non-stationarity: quantify forgetting with changing goals, test buffer-size effects, and consider rehearsal or regularization mitigations.
- Baselines and generality: add UVFA/successor-feature and strong auxiliary-task baselines; demonstrate portability beyond Doom (Atari, Minecraft, continuous control).
- Robustness ablations: test dueling split, multi-horizon head, linear vs non-linear utilities, goal sampling distribution sensitivity, and measurement scaling.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to measurement choices/scaling; training goal distribution and OOD goal performance; contribution of dense supervision vs architecture; evidence of catastrophic forgetting and buffer/replay effects; performance on long-term memory tasks and impact of recurrence; request more seeds/CIs and code/hyperparameters.
- Overall assessment: limited algorithmic novelty but a clear, practical contribution with compelling empirical evidence and insights valuable to the community.
