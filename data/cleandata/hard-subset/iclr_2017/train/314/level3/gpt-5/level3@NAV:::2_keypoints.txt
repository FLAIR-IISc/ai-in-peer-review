- Proposes an on-policy–flavored deep sensorimotor method that predicts auxiliary intrinsic measurements at multiple horizons and selects actions by maximizing a goal-weighted linear utility.  
- Conceptually a special case of UVFAs with Monte Carlo training targets and goal-conditional inputs, applied to 3D first-person navigation/combat.  
- Integrates known ideas (UVFA, dueling heads, dense auxiliary targets) into a practical recipe and shows strong empirical performance, including a Doom competition win.  
- Ablations are informative (vectorial vs scalar feedback; multiple temporal offsets) and yield clear experimental insights for future agents.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key accept reasons: strong, consistent empirical advantage on challenging 3D tasks with careful baselines and ablations.  
- Key accept reasons: clear, well-motivated formulation that distills prior concepts into a deployable method with demonstrated goal generalization.  
- Problem & motivation: well placed relative to UVFA, successor representations, and dense auxiliary prediction; citations and positioning are appropriate.  
- Evidence: results vs DQN/A3C/DSR, multi-goal generalization experiments, and competition benchmark support claims; ablations substantiate component importance.  
- Novelty: algorithmic novelty is modest but empirical contribution and design insights are significant.  
- Feedback: Clarify contributions in abstract/intro—frame advance as distilled integration rather than a novel algorithm.  
- Feedback: Analyze failure modes and generalization limits under goal shifts, domain shifts, or adversarial measurement noise.  
- Feedback: Measure catastrophic forgetting risks under predominantly on-policy training; include retention tests and replay buffer size study.  
- Feedback: Provide a direct UVFA baseline with the same architecture and MC/TD targets to isolate sources of gains.  
- Feedback: Broaden evaluation domains (e.g., Atari with RAM stats, DM Lab, continuous control) to test generality beyond Doom.  
- Questions: Is the method functionally equivalent to a UVFA trained with MC returns over vector-valued rewards, and how does it differ from successor features empirically?  
- Questions: How sensitive is performance to the choice and noise of measurements and to the training goal distribution?  
- Questions: How does generalization behave for goals outside the convex hull of training goals?  
- Questions: What is the impact of replay size and off-policy sampling on stability and forgetting?  
- Questions: Can authors report statistical significance over more seeds and provide learning curves for failure cases?
