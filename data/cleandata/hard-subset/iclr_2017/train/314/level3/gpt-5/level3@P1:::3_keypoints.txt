- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict multi-horizon changes in a vector of intrinsic measurements and choose actions that maximize goal·predicted_change.  
- Reframes goal-conditioned control as supervised learning from pixels and measurements rather than TD/value-based RL.  
- Architecture: perception trunk with an action-conditional prediction head split into expectation and action-differentiating streams.  
- Trains with ε-greedy data and a small replay buffer; learning is supervised regression to multi-step measurement deltas.  
- Empirical results: DFP matches or outperforms DQN, A3C, and DSR on multiple ViZDoom tasks and won the 2016 Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch.  
- Ablations: vector-valued prediction outperforms scalar reward prediction; multi-horizon prediction outperforms single-horizon; architectural split is beneficial.  
- Generalization: good transfer across textures/environments with sufficient appearance diversity and strong adaptation to new test-time goals after goal-agnostic training.  
- Sample-efficiency and wall-clock efficiency are claimed to be favorable relative to baselines.  
- Positioning: situates DFP relative to Monte Carlo methods, UVFA, predictive representations, and recent deep RL in 3D environments.  
- Baselines and comparisons are reasonably strong and matched for inputs/rewards, with hyperparameter tuning for A3C.  
- Limitations: experiments confined to Doom-like tasks, discrete actions, reactive (no memory) agents, and no theoretical guarantees.  
- Clarity/notation issues: suggest renaming prediction symbols (avoid p), clarify subscripts and indices, and improve table captions for measurement units/normalization.  
- Wording: suggest phrasing Doom as “immersive first-person 3D visuals” to avoid pedantry about 2.5D.  
- Training details: recommend reporting replay buffer size, update cadence, ε schedules, τ weighting, and sensitivity analyses for reproducibility.  
- Exploration: randomized-goal training likely aids exploration; recommend analysis of visitation statistics and ablation without goal randomization.  
- Compute/efficiency: suggest reporting wall-clock time, frames/sec, and hardware usage versus baselines.  
- Broader scope: recommend discussing extensions to continuous control and adding memory (recurrent modules), possibly with pilot results.  
- Question: sensitivity to replay buffer size, update lag, and on-policy vs off-policy training?  
- Question: robustness to choice of horizons, temporal offsets, and loss weighting; any learned/goal-dependent weighting tried?  
- Question: importance of measurement vector design and robustness to noisy/irrelevant measurements or omitted measurements.  
- Question: how are goal distributions chosen in training; any curricula or adversarial goal sampling tested?  
- Question: can randomized goals be quantified for their effect on state-action coverage versus fixed-goal baselines?  
- Question: any experiments with recurrent DFP for partial observability or with continuous action parameterizations?  
- Question: theoretical relation of DFP’s supervised Monte Carlo targets to UVFA/Monte Carlo value estimation regarding bias/variance and convergence under function approximation?
