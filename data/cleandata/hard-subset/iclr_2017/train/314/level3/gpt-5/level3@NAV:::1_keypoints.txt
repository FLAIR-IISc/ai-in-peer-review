- Tackles goal-directed sensorimotor control from raw high-dimensional input in 3D and proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP) that predicts future measurement changes per action to maximize goal utility.
- Frames learning as supervised Monte Carlo regression (not TD or successor features), using dense multivariate feedback and low-dimensional measurements as prediction targets.
- Introduces parametrized goal conditioning for test-time goal changes and shows DFP outperforms DQN, A3C, and DSR on novel 3D Doom tasks; won the Visual Doom AI competition.
- Decision: Accept — strong empirical gains, simple well-motivated idea, and clear evidence of generalization across environments and goals.
- Situates work relative to UVFA, multi-objective RL, and Monte Carlo vs TD; justifies moving from scalar rewards to vector measurements for dense supervision.
- Empirical rigor: four scenarios of increasing difficulty, comparisons to tuned baselines, ablations (vector vs scalar targets, multi-horizon), texture randomization, and goal-agnostic training.
- Practical significance: simple architecture, competitive training throughput, strong zero-shot adaptation to new goal weights; competition win supports external validity.
- Request to clarify on-policy assumption: training uses supervised returns but replay buffer adds off-policy data; report sensitivity to buffer size and a pure on-policy variant.
- Ask to discuss reliance on extra metadata (choice of measurements) and suggest viable proxies or learned latent measurements when metadata are unavailable.
- Recommend reporting confidence intervals across seeds and per-scenario compute budgets to strengthen robustness and fairness claims.
- Suggest a clearer conceptual and empirical contrast to successor features/UVFA with generalized policy improvement.
- Advise analysis of sensitivity to training goal distributions and to noisy or delayed measurements.
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the choice and dimensionality of measurement vectors, and to missing/noisy/delayed measurements?
- Question: Can you ablate replay buffer size and include an on-policy-only variant to quantify bias?
- Question: How does DFP compare to successor features with GPI under matched architectures?
- Question: Does training with randomized goals reduce sample efficiency for a specific downstream goal?
- Question: How does the method scale to larger or continuous action spaces?
- Question: Are the predicted measurements calibrated, and does calibration affect action-selection robustness?
