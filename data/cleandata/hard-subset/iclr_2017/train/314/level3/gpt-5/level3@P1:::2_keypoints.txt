- Proposes goal-conditioned supervised prediction of future intrinsic measurement deltas from pixels as an alternative to reward-centric RL for sensorimotor control.
- Predicts action-conditioned measurement changes at multiple horizons and selects actions maximizing goal-weighted predicted outcomes.
- Architecture couples a perception stack with a dueling-like prediction head trained with Monte Carlo targets and ε-greedy data collection.
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains over DQN and A3C in ViZDoom, especially on multi-measurement combat tasks and generalization to new textures/layouts.
- Shows effective goal-agnostic training (randomized goals) with good transfer to novel goals and competitive training speed from dense supervision.
- Ablations confirm multi-measurement and multi-horizon prediction materially improve performance.
- External validation: method won the Visual Doom AI Competition, outperforming LSTM-DQN variants with extra supervision.
- Paper is well written, clearly positioned relative to SL vs TD, UVFA, predictive representations, and dueling architectures.
- Decision: Accept, based on strong empirical evidence and a clean, well-motivated conceptual framework linking goal-conditioned control and dense supervision.
- Tackles timely problems: visuomotor control from pixels and dynamic, goal-conditional behavior with better credit assignment than sparse RL.
- Distinctive choice: predict measurement vectors directly rather than learning value functions, optimizing goal-weighted predictions at test time.
- Comparisons vs. DQN/A3C are favorable and consistent across seeds, with up to 6x margins in some combat tasks.
- Generalization experiments show transfer to unseen textures and mazes, improved by higher-capacity models.
- Goal-agnostic training nearly matches fixed-goal training and generalizes to diverse objectives (e.g., pacifist, ammo-drain).
- Ablations support the central hypothesis that dense, vectorial, multi-horizon supervision helps learning.
- Experimental setup and hyperparameters are described in detail with reasonable baseline implementations and evaluation protocols.
- Suggest clarifying novelty and explicitly relating the method to UVFA, framing it as supervised UVFA over future measurement deltas.
- Recommend analyzing failure modes (long-horizon credit assignment, partial observability, sparse or misaligned measurements) and reporting negative results.
- Raise concern about catastrophic forgetting under changing goals; suggest evaluating goal retention and techniques like goal-aware replay.
- Encourage broader applicability tests beyond Doom (Atari, robotics simulators) to strengthen generality claims.
- Suggest adding recurrent extensions (e.g., LSTM) to address partial observability and report their effect on mazes/adversarial tasks.
- Ask for more analysis details: variance across seeds, sensitivity to horizon set/weights, and robustness to irrelevant/noisy measurements.
- Request formalization of the UVFA connection and conditions under which maximizing g^T F equals optimizing a goal-conditioned Q.
- Ask for details on goal sampling, normalization, and sensitivity to goal distribution and scaling.
- Question choices around horizon weights and set {1,2,4,8,16,32}, and whether longer horizons help in some tasks.
- Inquire about replay size effects, prioritized replay across goals, and evidence of forgetting for infrequent goals.
- Request clarification on measurement design: impact of adding irrelevant/delayed measurements and need for feature selection.
- Ask whether recurrent encoders or frame stacking were tried and whether they aid Battle2 or goal generalization.
- Seek baseline fairness details: compute budgets, wall-clock parity, and results under equalized training steps and hyperparameter search.
- Ask about exploration strategy sensitivity (ε-schedule) and whether goal-directed exploration could improve generalization or safety.
