- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future intrinsic measurements for candidate actions and pick action maximizing goal-weighted predictions; goals are parameterized and changeable at test time.  
- Operates on raw visual input plus low-dimensional measurements, trains from interaction only, and uses a perception/measurement/goal architecture with a dueling-style prediction head for multi-horizon, action-conditional measurement vectors.  
- Extensive ViZDoom experiments show DFP matches/outperforms DQN and A3C, generalizes across textures and novel goals, benefits from vectorial multi-horizon prediction, and won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: Strong empirical evidence that supervised, goal-parameterized future prediction can outperform state-of-the-art deep RL on challenging 3D visuomotor tasks.  
- Key reason: Clear, well-motivated formulation with practical advantages (dense supervision, goal flexibility), supported by ablations and generalization studies.  
- Tackled problem: Learning sensorimotor control from high-dimensional sensory input while supporting dynamic test-time goals via predicting future intrinsic measurements conditioned on actions and goals.  
- Positioning: Well-connected to supervised forward models, Monte Carlo methods, multi-objective RL, universal value function approximators, and predictive representation literature.  
- Performance support: DFP equals A3C on easiest task and substantially outperforms A3C/DQN on Navigation and Battle scenarios, with competition win corroborating strength.  
- Goal flexibility support: Randomized-goal training yields near-parity on main goal and better generalization to new goals than single-goal training.  
- Generalization support: Training across textures transfers to novel textures; increasing capacity further improves transfer.  
- Ablations: Vectorial measurements and multi-horizon predictions both meaningfully improve performance, even when evaluating a single metric.  
- Scientific rigor: Experiments report protocols, offsets, weighting, ε-greedy schedule, memory, and baseline tuning; fair observation/action setups and comparable architectures used.  
- Environment wording: Recommend clarifying Doom description (e.g., “immersive first-person 3D rendering”) to avoid pedantry.  
- Notation/presentation: Suggest replacing p in Eq.(2), avoid overloaded indices in Eq.(6), state w = |A|, and repeat/normalize units in mixed-measurement tables and axes.  
- Measurement normalization: Define measurement scaling (ammo/health) in main text, not just appendices.  
- Baselines/fairness: Add wall-clock training time and sample efficiency comparisons (steps to reach X performance); discuss variance across seeds and include error bars.  
- Deeper analysis: Study sensitivity to goal vector scaling, temporal offset choices/weights, experience memory size, ε schedule, and robustness to noisy/partial measurements.  
- Scope/limitations: Discuss behavior with sparse/delayed measurements, potential for learned auxiliary measurements, and partial observability (recommend recurrent variant experiment).  
- Questions — measurement design: How sensitive is DFP to measurement choice/scaling, and do you normalize per-dimension? Have you tried learned auxiliary measurements?  
- Questions — temporal offsets: Why choose τ = {1,2,4,8,16,32} and the given loss weights; how does performance vary with different horizons or a single long horizon?  
- Questions — policy/data: How sensitive are results to memory size, update frequency, and ε schedule; any instability or overfitting?  
- Questions — baselines/metrics: Can you report sample efficiency and wall-clock time for DFP vs A3C, and did you evaluate an LSTM A3C for partial observability?  
- Questions — goal generalization: How far can test-time goals extrapolate beyond training distribution; any failure modes for extreme or long-horizon goals?  
- Questions — robustness: How does DFP handle noisy, delayed, or missing measurements and can it downweight unreliable signals?  
- Questions — action space: Have you explored factorized action heads or continuous-action extensions to avoid scaling with combinatorial sub-actions?
