- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict short sequences of future intrinsic measurement deltas and choose actions by maximizing a goal-weighted linear utility.  
- Reduces control to supervised learning from dense measurement feedback, allows training without fixed goals and test-time goal changes via goal weights.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance across multiple ViZDoom scenarios, generalization to unseen textures/goals, and won the 2016 Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Ablations show multivariate, multi-horizon prediction and the expectation/action stream decomposition are valuable.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Strong, carefully executed empirical evidence with clear gains over strong baselines (A3C, DQN) and a competition win.  
- Key reason 2: Simple, well-motivated, reproducible formulation with insightful ablations and practical utility.  
- Problem addressed: learning sensorimotor control from raw vision and enabling flexible goal-parameterized behavior without reward engineering.  
- Positioning: Well situated relative to intrinsic motivation, auxiliary tasks, UVFA, successor representations, and forward models; shift to dense measurements is compelling.  
- Evidence: Multiple scenarios, rigorous comparisons, generalization experiments, ablations, and described training details/seeds.  
- Writing/form fixes: Correct Doom description (2D engine rendered pseudo-3D) and polish phrasing.  
- Notation fixes: Rename P in Eq. (2) to avoid probability confusion, clarify index usage in Eq. (6), and make normalization notation unambiguous.  
- Tables/units: Repeat units for heterogeneous measurements and state episode summaries are at termination.  
- Terminology: Clarify “on-policy” given use of a small replay buffer (slightly off-policy).  
- Measurement details: Clarify measurement normalization, goal-weight scaling, report sensitivity analyses and offset-weight schedule.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to goal-weight magnitudes/normalization; absolute measurements vs deltas impact; sample efficiency vs A3C when matched for wall-clock/parallelism; handling sparser measurement streams and potential auxiliary learned measurements; transfer to continuous action spaces; plan to release code and environment configs.
