Summary of claims and contributions
- The paper proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP), a goal-conditioned, largely on-policy deep RL approach that reframes control as supervised prediction of future changes in a low-dimensional measurement stream (e.g., health, ammo, frags) given raw sensory inputs. Actions are selected by maximizing the predicted utility under a goal vector.
- It introduces a practical architecture with separate perception, measurement, and goal encoders and a dueling-style action/expectation split to predict multi-horizon future measurements for all actions in one forward pass.
- The method supports training without a fixed goal and can pursue dynamically changing test-time goals. It reports strong empirical results in ViZDoom, including winning the Visual Doom AI Full Deathmatch track, and ablations showing benefits of vectorial measurements and multi-step horizons.
- The work is well placed in the literature on UVFA/GVFs, Monte Carlo prediction, and deep RL baselines, positioning DFP as a specific, scalable instantiation that integrates these ideas for 3D navigation/combat from pixels.

Decision: Accept
Key reasons: (1) Clear and consistent empirical advantages on challenging 3D tasks, including meaningful generalization and a competition win. (2) A simple, well-motivated formulation that distills and integrates existing concepts into a practical, fast-to-train agent, with useful experimental insights likely to inform future agents.

Supporting arguments
1) What problem is tackled? Learning sensorimotor control from pixels in complex 3D environments while enabling test-time goal changes. The paper addresses sparse reward/credit assignment by using dense intrinsic measurements as targets and goal-conditioning, effectively a universal value function over measurement features.

2) Is the approach well motivated and well placed? Yes. The paper draws clear connections to Monte Carlo prediction, multi-objective RL, GVFs, and universal value function approximators (UVFA). It cites relevant contemporaneous 3D agents (A3C, DQN, SR, memory-based methods). That said, the novelty is primarily in the formulation and engineering integration rather than a new algorithmic primitive; this should be stated more explicitly in the abstract/intro.

3) Do results support the claims? Largely yes.
- Strong performance across four ViZDoom scenarios, with large margins on the harder tasks where the measurement stream is richer.
- Demonstrations of generalization to unseen textures/maps and to unseen goals, including goal-agnostic training.
- Ablations validate that (i) vectorial measurements outperform scalar reward prediction, and (ii) multi-horizon prediction helps.
- Training is efficient and stable. Multiple seeds and careful baseline tuning are reported. The claims are empirical and appropriately cautious.

Additional feedback to improve the paper (helpful suggestions; not all impact the accept decision)
- Clarify contribution and positioning:
  - Make explicit in the abstract/introduction that DFP is a special case of UVFA/GVF with linear utilities over a learned measurement feature space, combined with a dueling-style head and Monte Carlo training. State clearly what is new (goal-conditioned future-measurement prediction at scale, design/engineering choices, empirical findings) versus what is adopted from prior work.
  - Include a concise statement of assumptions under which DFP is expected to work best (dense, informative measurement streams; goals expressible as linear combinations of future measurements; short-to-medium planning horizons).

- Analyze failure modes and generalization limits:
  - Where does DFP struggle to generalize to changing goals? E.g., goals outside the training distribution, conflicting objectives, or non-linear utilities. Provide calibration plots of predicted vs realized measurements and breakdown by goal vector angle/magnitude.
  - Show cases where measurements are misspecified, incomplete, or noisy. What happens if an important latent factor (e.g., enemy presence) is not in the measurement stream?
  - Evaluate partial observability and longer temporal credit assignment; the model is reactive and may fail on tasks requiring memory.

- Catastrophic forgetting and policy non-stationarity:
  - As the method is largely on-policy with a small buffer, discuss and quantify forgetting when goals from the distant past are not revisited. Simple diagnostics: performance on a held-out set of fixed goals as training progresses with changing goal distributions; buffer size sweeps; periodic rehearsal or prioritized replay by goal.
  - Consider mitigations: rehearsal buffers stratified by goal, regularization (EWC-style), or auxiliary goal-conditioned value heads.

- Baselines and broader applicability:
  - Add UVFA-style baselines that directly condition Q on goals with the same measurement features, and successor-feature baselines, to isolate whether the gains are from dense supervision versus architecture/training.
  - Compare to strong auxiliary-task agents (e.g., pixel control, reward prediction) when feasible.
  - Demonstrate portability beyond Doom. For example, Atari with RAM variables (or other game-state measurements) as the measurement stream, Minecraft/DM-Lab goals, or simple continuous-control domains using proprioceptive measurements. This would substantiate the “general sensorimotor” claim.

- Ablations and robustness:
  - Ablate the dueling split, the multi-horizon head, and linear vs non-linear utilities u(f; g).
  - Report sensitivity to the goal sampling distribution during training and to scaling/normalization of measurements.
  - Explore memory-augmented versions (e.g., LSTM), and report whether they reduce DFP’s limitations on long-horizon tasks.

Questions for the authors
- How sensitive is DFP to the choice and scaling of the measurement set? If critical measurements are omitted or corrupted, does performance degrade gracefully?
- What is the distribution of goals used during randomized-goal training, and how does performance vary as test-time goals move outside this distribution?
- How much of the gain comes from dense measurement supervision versus the action/expectation architecture? Can a UVFA or SR baseline with the same backbone close the gap?
- Do you observe catastrophic forgetting with drifting goals? How do buffer size, replay strategy, or periodic goal rehearsal affect stability?
- How does DFP perform on tasks requiring long-term memory or non-Markovian dependencies? Would adding recurrence materially change results?
- Could you report more seeds and confidence intervals in the main tables, and provide code or exact hyperparameters for reproducibility?

Overall, despite limited algorithmic novelty, the paper offers a clear, practical contribution with compelling empirical evidence and insightful analysis of multivariate measurements and goal-conditioning that should be valuable to the community.
