Summary and contributions
The paper proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP), an on-policy sensorimotor control method that replaces reward prediction with goal-conditioned prediction of future intrinsic measurements. Concretely, in ViZDoom the model predicts short sequences of measurement deltas (health, ammunition, frags), then selects actions maximizing a goal-weighted linear utility. This reduces control to supervised learning from dense measurement feedback, enables training without fixed goals, and supports test-time goal changes by altering goal weights. The approach achieves strong empirical performance across multiple Doom scenarios, generalizes to unseen textures and goals, and won the 2016 Visual Doom AI Competition. Ablations demonstrate the value of multivariate, multi-horizon prediction and the architectural decomposition into expectation/action streams.

Decision: Accept
Key reasons: (1) Strong, carefully executed empirical evidence with clear gains over strong baselines (A3C, DQN) and a compelling milestone result (competition win). (2) A simple, well-motivated formulation that is reproducible and practically useful, with insightful ablations.

Supporting arguments
1. Problem: Learn sensorimotor control from raw vision in complex environments and enable flexible, goal-parameterized behavior without reward engineering.
2. Motivation and placement: Well situated among intrinsic motivation, auxiliary tasks, UVFA, successor representations, and forward modeling; the shift from sparse rewards to dense intrinsic measurements is clear and compelling. Citations are appropriate if not exhaustive.
3. Evidence: Multiple scenarios, comparisons to A3C/DQN/DSR, generalization experiments, and ablations support claims. The reported A3C ceiling in one scenario and the magnitude of gains in Battle tasks are convincing. Training details and seeds are described; evaluation appears rigorous.

Additional feedback to improve the paper (helpful, not central to the decision)
- Correct form issues: Doom is a 2D engine rendered as pseudo-3D; adjust phrasing.
- Notation: In Eq. (2), P is used for “prediction” and may be confused with probability; rename. In Eq. (6), avoid the double use of j and clarify indices; ensure normalization notation is unambiguous.
- Tables: Repeat units for heterogeneous measurements and clearly state that episode summaries are at termination.
- Terminology: The use of a small replay buffer makes training slightly off-policy; clarify “on-policy” characterization.
- Clarify measurement normalization and goal-weight scaling; report sensitivity analyses and the offset-weight schedule.

Questions for the authors
- How sensitive is performance to goal-weight magnitudes and normalization across tasks?
- Does predicting absolute future measurements versus deltas change stability or performance?
- What is the sample efficiency relative to A3C when matched on wall-clock and parallelism?
- How does DFP handle sparser measurement streams; can auxiliary learned measurements help?
- Any evidence on transfer to continuous action spaces?
- Can you release code and environment configs to aid reproducibility?
