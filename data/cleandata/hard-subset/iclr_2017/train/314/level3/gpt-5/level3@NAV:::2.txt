Summary and contributions. The paper proposes a simple, on-policy–flavored deep sensorimotor learning method that predicts auxiliary intrinsic measurements (health, ammo, frags) at multiple horizons and selects actions by maximizing a goal-weighted linear utility over these predictions. Conceptually, this is a special case of universal value function approximators (UVFAs) with Monte Carlo training targets and goal-conditional inputs, instantiated for 3D first-person navigation/combat. The work integrates known ideas (UVFA, dueling heads, dense auxiliary targets) into a practical recipe and demonstrates strong empirical performance and goal generalization in Doom, including a competition win. The ablations are informative (vectorial vs scalar feedback; multiple temporal offsets), and the paper offers clear experimental insights for future agents.

Decision. Accept. Key reasons: (1) Strong and consistent empirical advantage on challenging 3D tasks with careful baselines and ablations; (2) A clear, well-motivated formulation that distills prior concepts into a deployable method with demonstrated utility and goal generalization.

Supporting arguments. Problem: learning visuomotor control that generalizes to changing goals. Motivation/literature: well placed relative to UVFA, successor representations, and dense auxiliary prediction; the paper cites appropriate prior work and positions its formulation clearly. Evidence: results vs DQN/A3C/DSR across scenarios, multi-goal generalization, and a competition benchmark support the main claims; the ablations substantiate the importance of vectorial measurements and multi-horizon prediction. While the algorithmic novelty is modest, the empirical contribution is significant and the design insights are broadly useful.

Feedback to improve the paper (non-blocking). 
- Clarify contributions in the abstract/intro: emphasize that the advance is a distilled integration of UVFA-style goal conditioning with direct future prediction, rather than a novel algorithm. 
- Analyze failure modes and generalization limits: when do predictions/behaviors degrade under goal shifts, domain shifts, or adversarial measurement noise? 
- Discuss/measure catastrophic forgetting risks under predominantly on-policy training; include retention tests for goals not revisited and a study of replay buffer size. 
- Provide a direct UVFA baseline with the same architecture and MC/TD targets to isolate what drives the gains. 
- Broaden domains: e.g., Atari with RAM statistics as intrinsic measurements, DM Lab, or continuous control, to test applicability beyond Doom.

Questions for authors.
- Is the method functionally equivalent to a UVFA trained with MC returns over vector-valued rewards? What differs from successor features empirically? 
- How sensitive is performance to the choice and noise of measurements, and to the goal distribution at train time? 
- How does generalization behave for goals outside the convex hull of training goals? 
- What is the impact of replay size and off-policy sampling on stability and forgetting? 
- Can you report statistical significance over more seeds and learning curves for failure cases?
