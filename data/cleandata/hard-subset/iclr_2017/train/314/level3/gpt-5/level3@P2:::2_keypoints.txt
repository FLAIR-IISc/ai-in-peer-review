- Proposes goal-conditioned sensorimotor control that predicts short-horizon intrinsic measurement vectors from pixels and greedily selects actions aligning predicted measurements with a goal vector.
- Uses a perception module plus “expectation + action” streams (dueling-style) and trains by supervised regression on near on-policy ε-greedy data with a small replay buffer.
- Demonstrates strong performance on multiple ViZDoom tasks, outperforming DQN/A3C on hard scenarios, generalizing to new textures and dynamic test-time goals, and winning the Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Ablations show vectorial multi-offset measurement prediction outperforms scalar reward prediction and single-horizon targets.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: Strong, convincing empirical gains and cross-goal generalization on challenging 3D environments.
- Key reason: A clean, practical integration of UVFA, multi-objective RL, and Monte Carlo ideas yielding an easy-to-implement, impactful approach.
- Problem tackled: Robust sensorimotor control from pixels with dynamic test-time goals when dense low-dimensional intrinsic measurements are available.
- Motivation/placement: Well-motivated as an alternative to sparse rewards; clearly positioned relative to UVFA, successor representations, and TD vs Monte Carlo debates.
- Novelty: Mainly an empirical/practical distillation and reparameterization of existing ideas rather than a deep theoretical advance.
- Experimental rigor: Sound design with multiple difficulty levels, strong baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR), ablations, texture-randomized training/test split, and competition results.
- Claims substantiation: Results support superior performance on hard scenarios, texture generalization, goal adaptation, and benefit of vector/multi-offset predictions.
- Caveats: Limited analysis of failure modes, robustness to diverse goal distributions, broader domains, and potential catastrophic forgetting with a small buffer.
- Clarify contribution: Explicitly position as a goal-conditioned supervised alternative to TD-based RL and specify what is novel vs prior UVFA-like work.
- Failure modes & limits: Add qualitative/quantitative analysis on occlusions, partial observability, long horizons, rapid goal switching, rare events, adversarial textures, and OOD goals.
- Goal distribution & forgetting: Test how fixed vs randomized goals affect catastrophic forgetting and consider replay diversification or regularization methods.
- Baselines & fairness: Provide steps-to-threshold/sample-efficiency curves, report seed variance, and include UVFA-style or successor-feature baselines to isolate benefits.
- Broader applicability: Add at least one non-Doom domain (e.g., Atari proxy or continuous-control discretized tasks) to demonstrate generality.
- Uncertainty & loss shaping: Explore robust losses (Huber), per-dimension normalization/weighting, heteroscedastic regression, and calibration of predicted futures.
- Memory & hierarchy: Try recurrent variants for partial observability and discuss compatibility with hierarchical skills and temporal abstraction.
- Continuous actions: Discuss extensions to continuous actions (actor maximizing g^T F, CEM, or gradient-based refinement).
- Novelty question: How precisely does predicting intrinsic measurements differ from UVFA or successor features beyond target representation—formalize advantages/trade-offs.
- On-/off-policy & forgetting question: With a small replay memory and randomized goals, did you observe catastrophic forgetting or policy oscillations; any metrics on goal coverage/retention?
- Goal generalization question: How far can the model extrapolate to goal vectors outside training range (negative weights, larger magnitudes); any systematic sweeps?
- Sample efficiency question: How do DFP’s steps-to-threshold compare to A3C on hard tasks, normalized by wall-clock and environment steps?
- Measurement choice question: How sensitive is performance to which measurements are included or if measurements are noisy/missing?
- Temporal offsets question: How were offsets/weights chosen; did you try learned weights or curriculum over horizons?
- Uncertainty question: Are predictions calibrated and would modeling uncertainty improve action selection under noisy dynamics?
- Recurrent models question: Did adding memory (e.g., LSTM) help with partial observability and what were the effects?
- Broader domains question: Anticipated obstacles applying DFP to domains without explicit intrinsic counters and what proxies would you use?
