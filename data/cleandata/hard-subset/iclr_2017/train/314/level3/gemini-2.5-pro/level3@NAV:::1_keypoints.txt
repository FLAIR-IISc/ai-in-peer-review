- Core contribution: reframes sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future low-dimensional game measurements conditioned on sensory input, action, and a goal vector.  
- Action selection: agent chooses actions that maximize predicted future measurements for the current goal instead of optimizing a scalar reward.  
- Empirical result: DFP achieves strong, compelling performance in challenging Visual Doom scenarios, significantly outperforming A3C and DQN.  
- Generalization and real-world validation: learned policies generalize to new goals and unseen environments and won the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Recommendation: Accept — justified by the elegant reframing and exceptionally strong empirical evidence.  
- Positioning: clearly distinguishes its Monte Carlo-style supervised prediction approach from dominant temporal-difference/value-function RL methods.  
- Experimental rigor: thorough comparisons to strong baselines, ablation studies validating vector feedback and multi-step prediction, and comprehensive generalization experiments.  
- Impact: demonstrates that simpler supervised methods can outperform complex RL algorithms in measurement-rich tasks, making it of significant community interest.  
- Improvement suggestion 1: discuss potential bias and implications of using an experience replay buffer instead of a pure on-policy setup.  
- Improvement suggestion 2: clarify the limitation that the method relies on pre-defined, meaningful measurements not available to baseline algorithms.  
- Questions for authors: (1) Does sampling from older experiences introduce observable bias in future predictions or affect training stability? (2) How sensitive is performance to the choice of measurements (e.g., omitting "frag count" in Battle)?
