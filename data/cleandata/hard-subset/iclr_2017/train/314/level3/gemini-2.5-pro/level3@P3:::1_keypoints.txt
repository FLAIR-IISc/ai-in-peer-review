- DFP reframes sensorimotor control as supervised Monte Carlo regression predicting future low-dimensional measurement vectors from raw visual input, proposed action, and a goal vector.  
- Main contributions: supervised prediction formulation, goal-conditioning for dynamic goals, and extensive empirical validation claiming state-of-the-art results.  
- Empirical performance: significantly outperforms strong baselines (A3C, DQN, DSR) on ViZDoom tasks, including a 6x improvement in "Battle" and victory in the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Uses dense, vectorial, temporally-rich measurements as supervision instead of sparse scalar rewards, yielding more stable and effective learning in complex scenarios.  
- Goal-conditioning enables a single model to pursue changing goals at test time without retraining, achieving near-specialist performance.  
- Thorough ablation studies isolate the benefits of vectorial feedback and multi-step prediction; paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Training procedure mixes on-policy Monte Carlo targets with experience-replay (off-policy) data, creating a mismatch that is noted but not fully analyzed.  
- Reliance on a curated, semantically meaningful measurement vector is a key assumption and potential limitation compared to reward-only baselines.  
- Suggested improvement: discuss why off-policy replay does not destabilize supervised on-policy training (e.g., slow policy drift or regression robustness).  
- Question for authors: can the measurement vector be learned end-to-end when intrinsic measurements are unavailable, for example via auxiliary unsupervised objectives?
