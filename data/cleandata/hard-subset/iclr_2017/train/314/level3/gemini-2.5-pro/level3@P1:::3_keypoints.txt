- Introduces Direct Future Prediction (DFP): train agent to predict future intrinsic measurements from current sensory input, action, and a goal (supervised formulation of control).
- Represents goals as weightings over desired changes in future measurements, giving a dense, multi-dimensional supervisory signal.
- Action selection at test time chooses the action predicted to best satisfy the specified goal.
- Evaluated in VizDoom; comprehensive experiments show DFP significantly outperforms deep RL baselines (A3C, DQN), especially on harder tasks.
- Demonstrated strong real-world validation by winning the Full Deathmatch track of the 2016 Visual Doom AI Competition in unseen environments.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Primary reasons for accept: outstanding empirical results (state-of-the-art performance, thorough baselines, ablations, competition win) and an elegant, flexible goal-conditioned formulation enabling varied goals without retraining.
- Quantitative support: DFP outperforms A3C by factors of ~6 and ~2.5 in the “Battle” and “Battle 2” scenarios; ablations show vectorial feedback and multi-horizon predictions are critical.
- Goal-agnostic training with randomized goals achieves near-specialist performance and generalizes better to novel goals (e.g., “pacifist” strategy).
- Paper is well-motivated and situated in literature (connections to supervised control, Monte Carlo methods, UVFAs) with a concrete, high-performing implementation.
- Notational clarity suggestions: rename p_at to p_a and distinguish pre- and post-normalized A_i(j) (e.g., A'_i(j)).
- Presentation suggestion: add units/context for Ammo, Health, Frags in Table 3 to improve readability.
- Minor factual nitpick: Doom is technically 2.5D rather than fully 3D; mention for precision.
- Scope suggestion: validate DFP in a different domain (e.g., robotics simulator) to strengthen claims of generality.
- Question — Sensitivity to measurements: how does performance change if key measurements (e.g., health) are removed from the measurement stream but kept in the goal?
- Question — Temporal offsets: why choose {1,2,4,8,16,32}, how sensitive is performance to these values, and is there a principled way to choose them?
- Question — Relationship to reward shaping: how does DFP compare to RL agents trained with an engineered dense reward composed from the same measurements?
- Question — Complex goal functions: can DFP handle non-linear or multi-constraint goals (e.g., maintain health above threshold while maximizing frags), and what are the implications for architecture/training?
