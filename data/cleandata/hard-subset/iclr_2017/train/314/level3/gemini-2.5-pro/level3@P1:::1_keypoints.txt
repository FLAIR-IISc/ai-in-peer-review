- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): supervise learning to predict future low-dimensional measurements conditioned on current sensory input, measurements, and a goal vector.  
- Selects actions at test time by choosing the action predicted to best satisfy the specified goal.  
- Trains via Monte Carlo-style regression on raw experience and substantially outperforms A3C and DQN in ViZDoom tasks.  
- Key contributions: goal-conditioned future-prediction formulation, strong generalization across unseen environments and goals, and thorough empirical validation.  
- Empirical significance highlighted by winning the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novelty & simplicity: offers a clear, well-motivated alternative to TD learning; goal-conditioning yields a single flexible agent for diverse objectives.  
- Strong empirical results: large performance gains (up to ~6x vs A3C in "Battle"), robust generalization, and competition validation.  
- Dense vectorial measurements provide richer supervision than scalar rewards; ablations show vector feedback and multi-horizon prediction are beneficial.  
- Goal-conditioning with randomized goals produces near task-specific performance and much better adaptation to novel goals (e.g., “pacifist”) without extra training.  
- Results are rigorously presented and compared to strong baselines; victory in competition reinforces practical utility.  
- Raises concern about on-policy/off-policy mismatch from using an experience replay buffer—request discussion of potential instabilities.  
- Notes limitation: reliance on meaningful, low-dimensional hand-picked measurements restricts applicability; ask for discussion of domains without such signals.  
- Asks authors: did you try purely on-policy training vs replay buffer, and how did performance compare?  
- Asks authors: how would the approach apply when measurements are not available or must be learned—would predicting aggregations of raw sensory input work?  
- Asks authors: sensitivity of performance to the chosen temporal offsets {1,2,4,8,16,32} and impact of denser or sparser horizons?
