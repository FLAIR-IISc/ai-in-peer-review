- Introduces Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future measurement vectors conditioned on state, measurements, goal, and action, then pick the action maximizing the goal-weighted prediction.
- Trained as supervised learning rather than scalar-reward RL, enabling dynamic goal specification at test time.
- Strong empirical results: DFP significantly outperforms A3C and DQN in Visual Doom and won the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Leverages dense, multi-dimensional measurements and relates to UVFA; uses a dueling-style architecture separating action-independent and action-dependent components.
- Experiments across four scenarios show the performance gap widens in complex "Battle" settings, supporting measurement vectors over scalar rewards.
- Generalization strengths: goal-agnostic training handles unseen goals at test time and generalizes across randomized textures/environments.
- Clarify contribution: mainly a strong distillation and integration of existing ideas (goal-conditioning, future prediction, dueling nets) rather than a fundamentally new algorithm.
- Request analysis of failure modes, especially for long-horizon goals that may be poorly served by greedy one-step selection.
- Raise concern about catastrophic forgetting due to on-policy training and shifting goal distributions; suggest discussing retention over time.
- Recommend demonstrating broader applicability beyond Doom (e.g., Atari with RAM measurements) to strengthen generality claims.
- Ask for intuition/sensitivity analysis on chosen temporal offsets (1,2,4,...,32) and whether predicting a single distant future was tried.
- Ask whether goal-agnostic training implies learning reusable primitive skills with g acting as a selector at test time.
- Ask whether goal vectors were stored in replay memory alongside state-action tuples and how that interacts with the on-policy learning setup.
