- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP), framing sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future intrinsic measurement vectors conditioned on state, action, and goal vector.  
- Treating goals as inputs allows training without a fixed objective and dynamic goal pursuit at deployment.  
- Strong recommendation: clear accept based on novelty and outstanding empirical validation.  
- Extensive experiments in VizDoom demonstrate DFP matches or outperforms baselines (A3C, DQN), especially in complex "Battle" scenarios.  
- Victory in the 2016 VizDoom AI Competition highlights robust generalization to unseen environments.  
- Ablation study shows vectorial measurements and multi-horizon predictions are critical to performance.  
- Good literature positioning, relating to intrinsic motivation, forward models, and universal value functions.  
- Presentation suggestion: report units for heterogeneous measurements in result tables for clarity.  
- Notational fixes recommended: avoid confusing "P" for prediction (may imply probability) and clarify repeated index "j" in equation (6).  
- Minor suggestion: state that Doom is a pseudo-3D environment for technical precision.  
- Questions for authors: how DFP applies when intrinsic measurements are sparse or less obvious (e.g., puzzles), and how sensitive performance is to the training distribution of randomized goal vectors.
