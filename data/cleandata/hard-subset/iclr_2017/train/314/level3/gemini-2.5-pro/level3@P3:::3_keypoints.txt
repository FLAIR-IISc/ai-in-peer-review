1. Introduces Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future intrinsic measurement vectors and select actions by maximizing goal-weighted predicted outcomes.  
2. Enables dynamic, test-time goal specification via a goal vector without retraining.  
3. Validated in VizDoom, outperforming A3C/DQN and winning the 2016 Visual Doom AI Competition.  
4. Recommendation: Clear accept based on strong empirical results and demonstrated generalization.  
5. Tackles flexible sensorimotor control from high-dimensional visual input and learning reusable skills for changing goals.  
6. Well motivated: dense multi-dimensional measurements offer richer supervision than scalar rewards and ties well to prior literature.  
7. Claims are well supported by rigorous experiments showing DFP’s superiority in complex "Battle" scenarios.  
8. Ablation study confirms both vectorial measurements and multi-horizon predictions are important.  
9. Demonstrates generalization and flexibility: goal-agnostic training produces diverse behaviors (e.g., pacifist) and robust transfer to unseen maps.  
10. Minor factual correction suggested: Doom is technically pseudo-3D (2.5D), not true 3D.  
11. Notational clarity issues: avoid using "p" for prediction in Eq.(2) and reuse of index "j" in Eq.(6).  
12. Presentation tweak: repeat measurement names in Table 3 headers to improve readability.  
13. Question: How sensitive is performance to the choice of temporal offsets (τ) and were they tuned or heuristically chosen?  
14. Question: How to extend DFP to environments lacking human-defined intrinsic measurements—can such measurements be learned unsupervised?
