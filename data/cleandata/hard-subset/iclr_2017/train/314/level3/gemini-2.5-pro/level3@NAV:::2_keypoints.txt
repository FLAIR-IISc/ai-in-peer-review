- Presents Direct Future Prediction (DFP): an on-policy supervised agent predicting future intrinsic measurement changes from sensory input, current measurements, and a goal.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance in challenging Doom 3D scenarios, outperforming deep RL baselines and generalizing to unseen environments and changing goals.  
- Decision: Accept — justified by exceptionally strong empirical results and useful experimental insights from integrating existing ideas.  
- Empirical evidence: DFP significantly outperforms A3C and DQN; ablation studies validate multi-step, vectorial measurement predictions over single scalar rewards.  
- Properly positioned in literature (connects to UVFA); main contribution is elegant application and empirical validation rather than a wholly new algorithm.  
- Suggestion: Clarify in abstract/introduction that the contribution is a powerful integration of existing ideas, not a fundamentally new algorithm.  
- Suggestion: Add analysis of failure modes and investigate conditions where the agent fails to generalize to new goals.  
- Suggestion: Demonstrate broader applicability by testing in other domains (e.g., Atari using RAM as intrinsic measurements).  
- Concern: On-policy training with randomized goals may cause catastrophic forgetting; discuss whether skills are forgotten and how the experience buffer helps.  
- Question for authors: Which types or conditions of goals does the model struggle to generalize to?  
- Question for authors: Have you investigated catastrophic forgetting and how crucial the experience buffer is in mitigating it?
