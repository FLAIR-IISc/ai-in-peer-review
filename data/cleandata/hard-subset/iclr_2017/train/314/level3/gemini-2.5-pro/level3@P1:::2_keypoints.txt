- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): frame sensorimotor control as supervised prediction of future low-dimensional measurements from pixels, action, and a goal vector.  
- Uses a goal vector to allow dynamic, test-time objectives rather than a fixed reward function.  
- Acts greedily on predicted future measurements to produce complex behaviors in 3D environments.  
- Demonstrates large empirical gains over state-of-the-art RL (A3C, DQN) on ViZDoom, notably 6x and 2.5x frag improvements in hard scenarios.  
- Validated by winning the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch, showing strong generalization to unseen environments.  
- Recommendation: accept, primarily because the simple formulation yields compelling empirical advantages on challenging visuomotor tasks.  
- Second primary reason: solid experimental evidence (ablation and goal-generalization) that vectorial, multi-step future prediction is a stronger learning signal than scalar rewards.  
- Situates work relative to prior methods (universal value functions, Monte Carlo); contribution is an effective synthesis and large-scale validation rather than a radically new algorithm.  
- Ablation studies show both vectorial measurements and multi-horizon prediction materially improve performance.  
- Goal-agnostic training experiments indicate agents can learn general skills and adapt to novel or counterintuitive objectives at test time.  
- Suggest clarifying the contribution in the abstract/introduction to emphasize synthesis and large-scale validation.  
- Recommend analyzing failure modes: limits of generalization (e.g., goals requiring planning beyond prediction horizon or anticorrelated goals).  
- Raise potential catastrophic forgetting in randomized, largely on-policy goal training and ask for discussion of mitigation (experience memory use).  
- Suggest discussing broader applicability beyond Doom (e.g., using game RAM or other domains without explicit measurements).  
- Questions for authors: (1) did you observe catastrophic forgetting across shifting goal distributions and is experience memory sufficient? (2) what kinds of test goals (e.g., outside training convex hull) cause poor generalization? (3) how sensitive is performance to temporal offsets {τ_n} and the chosen exponential spacing?
