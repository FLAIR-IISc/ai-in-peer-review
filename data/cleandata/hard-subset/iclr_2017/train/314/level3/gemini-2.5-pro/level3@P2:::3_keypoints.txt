- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future low-dimensional measurements from pixels + action + goal, framing control as supervised learning.  
- Trains a single, goal-agnostic policy that can be steered at test time by changing the goal vector.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains: DFP substantially outperforms A3C and DQN on VizDoom and won the 2016 Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch.  
- Decision: Clear Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Exceptional empirical results and robustness, with large performance gaps in complex combat scenarios.  
- Key reason 2: Elegant, well-motivated formulation using dense, multi-dimensional measurement prediction, validated by ablation studies.  
- Supporting: Systematic comparisons across four scenarios show 2.5–6× improvement in frags on hardest tasks versus A3C.  
- Supporting: Generalization experiments show training on varied textures yields robust policies to unseen environments.  
- Supporting: Goal-agnostic training performs nearly as well as specialists while generalizing to novel goals (e.g., pacifist behavior).  
- Supporting: Ablations show multi-measurement and multi-horizon predictions outperform single scalar or single-horizon targets.  
- Supporting: Paper appropriately connects to prior supervised-control, universal value functions, and predictive state representation work.  
- Suggestion: Mention that Doom is technically a 2.5D engine for precision.  
- Suggestion: Repeat measurement units (frags, ammo, health) in heterogeneous table headers for clarity.  
- Suggestion: Improve notation—avoid ambiguous use of p for prediction and clarify Ai(j) vs. normalized A'i(j).  
- Question: How would DFP transfer to environments with different dynamics (e.g., MuJoCo, other game engines) and what adaptations are needed?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to scaling/normalization between goal vector components and measurements (e.g., health 0–100 vs frags 0–10)?  
- Question: Have you tried a fully off-policy DFP with larger replay; if not, what stability issues do you expect when behavior and target policies diverge?
