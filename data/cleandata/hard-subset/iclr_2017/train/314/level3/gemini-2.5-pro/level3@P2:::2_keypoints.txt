- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future multi-dimensional measurements from pixels, current measurements, and a goal vector to reframe control as supervised learning.  
- Enables training without external supervision, supports no fixed goal, and allows dynamic goals at test time.  
- Strong empirical results: DFP significantly outperforms DQN and A3C on Visual Doom tasks and won the Full Deathmatch competition.  
- Ablation shows predicting a vector of future measurements is substantially better than predicting a single scalar reward.  
- Goal-agnostic training: agents trained on randomized goals generalize well to unseen specific goals, nearly matching goal-specific training.  
- Builds on prior ideas (e.g., UVFA) but contributes a practical architecture, training regime, and demonstration in raw-pixel 3D environments.  
- Experiments are comprehensive across four Doom scenarios; advantage grows in complex Battle scenarios using health/ammo/frags.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Suggest clarifying contribution framing as a specific instantiation of goal-conditioned value-function methods and emphasize empirical synthesis.  
- Recommend adding analysis of failure modes and limitations (which goals fail, effects of inaccurate predictions).  
- Recommend demonstrating broader applicability (e.g., apply DFP to Atari using RAM as measurements).  
- Q1 for authors: Have you investigated catastrophic forgetting with on-policy/randomized-goal training and how to mitigate it?  
- Q2 for authors: Can you provide qualitative analysis of failure modes (e.g., why Pacifist and Drain Ammo perform worse)?  
- Q3 for authors: Clarify the conceptual advantage of DFP over goal-conditioned Q-learning beyond using a vectorial measurement stream.
