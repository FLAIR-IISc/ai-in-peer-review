- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): frame control as supervised prediction of future low-dimensional measurements from pixels, action, and goal, selecting actions that maximize predicted goal outcomes.
- Contribution: a simple Monte Carlo-style supervised regression algorithm for learning from raw experience.
- Contribution: empirical evidence that DFP substantially outperforms deep RL baselines (DQN, A3C) on VizDoom first-person shooter tasks.
- Contribution: demonstrates strong generalization—single model adapts to changing goals at test time and generalizes across unseen environments.
- Contribution: validated externally by winning the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Paper is well-written, method clearly explained, with extensive and persuasive experimental results.
- Decision: Accept.
- Primary reason: outstanding empirical results, including state-of-the-art performance and independent competition validation.
- Primary reason: novel and elegant formulation that handles multi-dimensional, temporally dense feedback and enables flexible, zero-shot goal adaptation.
- Supporting evidence: in Battle scenarios (D3/D4), DFP outperforms A3C by a factor of 2.5–6 in frag count.
- Ablation: predicting vectorial measurements is critical—predicting only scalar reward (frags) performs significantly worse.
- Goal-conditioning: training with randomized goals yields near-specialist performance and enables strong generalization to novel goals (e.g., "pacifist" behavior).
- Competition result underscores robustness and data-efficiency, outperforming more complex recurrent models that used extra supervision.
- Concern: training uses experience replay (off-policy) while objective is conceptually on-policy, introducing a potential dataset-policy mismatch that should be discussed.
- Concern: method depends on availability of meaningful low-dimensional measurement streams (privileged information); paper should clarify applicability when such measurements are not available and limitations compared to standard RL.
