- Presents a novel supervised-learning approach to sensorimotor control using cotemporal structure of high-dimensional sensory and low-dimensional measurement streams.  
- Reframes sensorimotor learning away from scalar-reward RL to vectorial feedback and multivariate measurements, claiming richer supervisory signals that stabilize and accelerate training.  
- Empirical results show superiority over state-of-the-art RL on complex Doom-based tasks and good generalization across environments and goals.  
- Practical utility evidenced by success in the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main weakness: contributions insufficiently distinguished from prior universal value function approaches (e.g., Schaul et al., 2015), with abstract/introduction lacking clarity.  
- Lacks analysis of failure modes, including generalization to changing goals and catastrophic forgetting in on-policy training.  
- Limited experimental scope: evaluation confined to Doom-based tasks, no standard benchmarks (e.g., Atari) to establish generality.  
- Suggestion: explicitly clarify novel theoretical/methodological contributions relative to UVFAs and supervised formulations.  
- Suggestion: include experiments or discussion on failure modes (catastrophic forgetting, robustness to goal changes, scalability).  
- Suggestion: expand experiments to additional domains (Atari or other benchmarks) to validate generality.  
- Suggestion: perform further ablation studies on key architectural choices (e.g., action-stream normalization) and training regimes (single vs. randomized goals).  
- Questions for authors: how does this compare to UVFAs in theoretical novelty and computational efficiency?  
- Questions for authors: were instances of catastrophic forgetting observed and how were they mitigated?  
- Questions for authors: can the method extend to continuous action spaces and what changes are required?  
- Questions for authors: why were Atari/other standard benchmarks omitted and are there plans to evaluate them?  
- Overall: promising empirical approach but needs clearer contribution framing, deeper limitations analysis, and broader experimental validation to be acceptable.
