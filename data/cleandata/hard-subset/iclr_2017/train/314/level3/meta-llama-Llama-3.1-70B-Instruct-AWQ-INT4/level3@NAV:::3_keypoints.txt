- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future intrinsic measurements from high-dimensional sensory and low-dimensional measurement streams instead of rewards.  
- Uses a parameterized function approximator to learn directly from raw sensory input and measurements.  
- Claims DFP outperforms state-of-the-art deep RL methods and won the 2016 Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Reason 1: Extensive, well-documented experiments in immersive 3D simulations with reproducible setup and SOTA comparisons.  
- Reason 2: Presents a milestone contribution to sensorimotor control with potential impact on RL and robotics.  
- Approach is well-motivated and properly situated among intrinsic motivation, auxiliary tasks, and forward modeling literature.  
- Empirical evidence is strong: ablation studies, method comparisons, and demonstrated generalization across environments and goals.  
- Minor form issue: incorrectly describes Doom as a 3D game instead of a 2D game rendered in fake 3D.  
- Notation issues: ambiguous use of "P" for prediction vs probability and double use of "j" in equation (6).  
- Suggest repeating units for heterogeneous measurements in result tables for clarity.  
- Ask for more architecture/training details (number of layers, learning-rate schedule).  
- Questions for authors: explain experience memory usage for predictor updates, selection of temporal offsets τ1…τn, details of goal-agnostic training and test-time goal adaptation, and plans to extend to continuous action spaces and more complex environments.
