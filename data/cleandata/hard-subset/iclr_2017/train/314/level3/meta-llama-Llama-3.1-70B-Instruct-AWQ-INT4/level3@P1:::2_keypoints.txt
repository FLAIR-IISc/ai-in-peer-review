- Presents a sensorimotor control approach using high-dimensional sensory streams and low-dimensional measurement streams, exploiting their cotemporal structure as supervision.  
- Trains a control model via supervised learning from raw sensory input in complex 3D environments without external supervision.  
- Enables learning without fixed training-time goals and supports pursuing dynamically changing goals at test time.  
- Accepted by reviewer primarily because it empirically outperforms state-of-the-art deep RL on challenging tasks.  
- Provides useful experimental insights that can inform future agent design.  
- Main technical novelty is largely a distilled integration of existing ideas rather than a new algorithm.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results in immersive Doom-based 3D simulations, outperforming prior methods.  
- Shows models generalize across different environments and goals.  
- Potential issue: on-policy training may cause catastrophic forgetting if past goals are not revisited.  
- Reviewer requests further analysis of failure modes, conditions limiting generalization to changing goals, and expansion to other domains (e.g., Atari).  
- Suggests clarifying novel contributions in the abstract/introduction and adding more limitations/future-work analysis.  
- Asks specific clarifying questions: how to address catastrophic forgetting; detailed predictor network architecture; how temporal offsets τ are chosen and their effect; more analysis of generalization across environments/goals.
