- Paper proposes sensorimotor control using high-dimensional sensory streams and lower-dimensional measurement streams, exploiting their cotemporal structure as supervision.  
- Claims supervised-learning training of a control model without extraneous supervision, acting from raw 3D inputs and pursuing dynamic test-time goals.  
- Decision: accept; reason 1 — clear, well-motivated approach grounded in literature and integrating existing ideas effectively.  
- Decision: accept; reason 2 — extensive Doom-based 3D experiments show clear empirical advantage over prior methods.  
- Method positioned as a special case of universal value function approaches, with proper acknowledgment of prior work.  
- Experiments: four increasingly difficult scenarios, comparisons to state-of-the-art deep RL, consistent outperformance especially on complex tasks.  
- Finding: vectorial measurements give significant benefit over conventional scalar rewards.  
- Reviewer requests more analysis of failure modes and conditions limiting generalization to changing goals.  
- Raises a conceptual concern that on-policy training can cause catastrophic forgetting if distant past goals aren’t revisited.  
- Suggests expanding experiments to other domains (e.g., Atari with ROM as intrinsic variables) to show broader applicability.  
- Improvement request: clarify main technical contribution versus being a distillation/integration of existing ideas.  
- Improvement request: provide additional analysis of failure modes and generalization failures.  
- Improvement request: address on-policy training issue and consider alternatives to mitigate catastrophic forgetting.  
- Questions for authors: details of future predictor architecture and how it matches DQN; rationale and selection of temporal offsets τ and use of the latest three timesteps; description of experience memory and its use in predictor updates; and evaluation details comparing vectorial feedback to scalar rewards.
