Paper proposes a supervised method that predicts effects of actions on future low-dimensional measurements from high-dimensional sensory inputs for sensorimotor control in immersive environments.
Decision: Accept — approach is well-motivated and achieves state-of-the-art performance compared to deep RL baselines.
Clear, well-structured presentation with thorough methodology, motivation, and experimental reporting.
Novel supervised formulation for action-effect prediction addresses a significant RL challenge.
Strong experimental results in navigation and battle scenarios demonstrating SOTA performance.
Ablation studies and comparisons are provided to analyze strengths and limitations.
Request more network architecture details (number of layers, units per layer, activation functions).
Request more hyperparameter tuning details (value ranges and selection method).
Recommend additional experiments on other scenarios/environments to further validate effectiveness.
Recommend deeper analysis of limitations, potential failure modes, and future improvements.
Questions for authors: provide architecture specifics; detail hyperparameter selection and ranges; supply more experimental results on other environments; discuss limitations and mitigation plans.
