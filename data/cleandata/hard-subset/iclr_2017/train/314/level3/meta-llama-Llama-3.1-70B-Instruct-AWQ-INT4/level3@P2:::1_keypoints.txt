- Presents a novel supervised-learning approach for sensorimotor control using cotemporal high-dimensional sensory streams and low-dimensional measurement streams.  
- Trains a model to predict low-dimensional measurements conditioned on goal vectors and actions, enabling action selection from raw sensory input.  
- Demonstrates strong generalization across environments and goals in 3D Doom simulations.  
- Outperforms prior methods and won the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Recommendation: accept, motivated by novelty and compelling empirical results.  
- Key technical contributions: Monte Carlo estimation, low-dimensional measurement supervision, and parameterized goal conditioning.  
- Shows policies can adapt to limited goal changes at inference without additional training, an advantage over traditional RL.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, with comparisons to prior work and ablation studies.  
- Minor concern: supervised training assumes on-policy data but uses a replay buffer—needs deeper discussion of this approximation.  
- Minor concern: method depends on extra metadata (which measurements to predict) not available to baselines; limitations should be clarified.  
- Suggestion: include more architecture/implementation details and hyperparameter choices and rationale.  
- Suggestion: discuss potential applications/extensions (continuous actions, complex goals).  
- Suggestion: add visualizations (videos/screenshots) of agent behavior.  
- Questions for authors: explain how low-dimensional measurements were chosen for experiments.  
- Question for authors: how will they address the requirement for extra measurement metadata in future work?  
- Question for authors: clarify implications of using a replay buffer on the on-policy training assumption.
