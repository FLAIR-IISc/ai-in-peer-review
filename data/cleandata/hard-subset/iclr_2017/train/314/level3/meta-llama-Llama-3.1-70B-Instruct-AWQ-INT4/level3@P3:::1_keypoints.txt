- Novel approach to sensorimotor control using high-dimensional sensory input plus low-dimensional measurement stream; new algorithm outperforms prior methods and won the Visual Doom AI competition.

- Core idea: supervise prediction of low-dimensional measurements conditioned on a goal vector and action, then select actions that maximize predicted outcomes for the goal.

- Learning framed as supervised Monte Carlo regression rather than temporal-difference learning or successor features.

- Main contributions: focus on Monte Carlo estimation, use of low-dimensional measurements, parameterized goal conditioning, and thorough empirical comparisons.

- Learned policies can adapt to limited goal changes without additional training.

- Paper is well-communicated with clear, concise explanation and compelling empirical results.

- Decision: Accept.

- Reasons: novel, well-motivated approach and thorough experimental validation demonstrating effectiveness.

- Supporting arguments: clear exposition, well-presented experiments across scenarios, and thorough comparison to prior work establishing novelty.

- Minor concern: supervised training assumes on-policy data but uses a replay buffer—this approximation needs discussion.

- Minor concern: method relies on extra metadata (which measurements to predict) unavailable to compared algorithms; limitations when such measurements are absent should be noted.

- Recommendation: provide more details on architecture and training procedure to facilitate reproducibility.

- Recommendation: discuss potential applications and future work directions.

- Questions for authors: provide architecture/training details; explain plans to address reliance on extra metadata and replay-buffer on-policy mismatch; describe potential applications and future directions.
