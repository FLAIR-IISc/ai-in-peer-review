This paper presents a novel approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments, leveraging a high-dimensional sensory stream and a lower-dimensional measurement stream. The authors introduce a new algorithm that outperforms prior methods in novel 3D environments from raw sensory data and generalizes across goals/environments. The algorithm was the winner of the Visual Doom AI competition.

The core idea is to supervise prediction of low-dimensional measurements conditioned on a given goal vector and the current action. Action selection is done by choosing the action that maximizes the predicted outcome for the specified goal. Learning is framed as supervised Monte Carlo regression rather than temporal-difference learning or successor features.

The paper's key contributions include focusing on Monte Carlo estimation, using low-dimensional measurements, parametrized goal conditioning, and empirical comparisons to prior work. The authors demonstrate that learned policies can respond to limited goal changes without additional training.

The paper is well-communicated and presents compelling empirical results of significant interest. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach, and the experimental results are thorough and well-presented.

**Decision:** Accept

**Reasons:**

1. The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments.
2. The experimental results are thorough and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Supporting arguments:**

* The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach, making it easy to understand and follow.
* The experimental results are well-presented and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in various scenarios.
* The authors provide a thorough comparison to prior work, which helps to establish the novelty and significance of their approach.

**Additional feedback:**

* Minor concern: supervised training makes an on-policy assumption but uses a replay buffer, an approximation that should be discussed.
* Minor concern: method relies on extra metadata (which measurements to predict) unavailable to compared algorithms; limitations when such measurements are absent should be more clearly noted.
* Consider providing more details on the architecture and training procedure to facilitate reproducibility.
* Consider discussing potential applications and future work directions.

**Questions for the authors:**

* Can you provide more details on the architecture and training procedure to facilitate reproducibility?
* How do you plan to address the limitations of the proposed approach, such as the reliance on extra metadata and the use of a replay buffer?
* What are the potential applications of the proposed approach, and what are the future work directions?

Overall, the paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness. With some minor revisions to address the concerns mentioned above, the paper is ready for publication.
