**Summary of the paper's claims and contributions**

The paper presents a novel approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments, leveraging deep learning techniques to learn from raw sensory data. The algorithm, called Direct Future Prediction (DFP), outperforms prior methods in novel 3D environments and generalizes across goals and environments. The core idea is to supervise the prediction of low-dimensional measurements (e.g., ammo, health) conditioned on a given goal vector and the current action. The algorithm was the winner of the Visual Doom AI competition, demonstrating its effectiveness in complex and dynamic environments. The paper's key contributions include the focus on Monte Carlo estimation, the use of low-dimensional measurements, parametrized goal conditioning, and empirical comparisons to prior work.

**Additional feedback and suggestions**

While the paper is well-written and presents compelling results, there are a few minor concerns that should be addressed:

1. **On-policy assumption**: The paper's use of a replay buffer in supervised training makes an on-policy assumption. The authors should discuss this approximation and its potential limitations.
2. **Extra metadata**: The method relies on extra metadata (which measurements to predict) unavailable to compared algorithms. The authors should more clearly note the limitations of their approach when such measurements are absent.
3. **Broader implications**: The paper's results have significant implications for sensorimotor control in immersive environments. The authors could further discuss the broader implications of their work and potential applications in other domains.
