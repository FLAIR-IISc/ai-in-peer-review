- Presents an on-policy deep RL method using auxiliary intrinsic variables for 3D navigation; is a special case of universal value functions and mainly distills/integrates prior ideas—authors should clarify contributions in abstract/introduction.
- Main technical novelty is limited: more distillation/integration than a new algorithmic advance.
- Empirical results show clear advantage over state-of-the-art in complex 3D environments, especially with richer measurements.
- Trained models demonstrate generalization across environments and goals.
- Paper lacks in-depth analysis of failure modes and conditions where the model struggles to generalize to changing goals.
- On-policy training raises a conceptual concern of catastrophic forgetting if past goals are not revisited; authors should discuss this limitation and mitigations.
- Recommend expanding experiments to other domains (e.g., Atari using ROM as intrinsic variables) to show broader applicability.
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to address clarity, analysis, and broader evaluation.
- Revision request 1: Clarify the paper’s contributions in the abstract and introduction.
- Revision request 2: Provide a more in-depth analysis of failure modes and generalization limits to changing goals.
- Revision request 3: Discuss catastrophic forgetting from on-policy training and potential solutions.
- Revision request 4: Expand experiments to other domains (e.g., Atari) to demonstrate generality.
