- Paper proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP), a deep-learning method for sensorimotor control that predicts low-dimensional future measurements conditioned on goals and actions.  
- DFP outperforms prior methods in novel 3D environments and won the Visual Doom AI competition, showing strong generalization across goals and environments.  
- Core contributions: Monte Carlo estimation focus, prediction of low-dimensional measurements, parametrized goal conditioning, and empirical comparisons to prior work.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Primary reason 1: significant empirical results demonstrating effectiveness in complex, dynamic environments.  
- Primary reason 2: novel approach and contributions that advance sensorimotor control with deep function approximators.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly explained, and presents thorough, well-presented empirical evaluations.  
- Concern: supervised training with a replay buffer implies an on-policy assumption that needs discussion.  
- Concern: method relies on extra metadata (which measurements to predict) not available to some baselines; limitations should be noted.  
- Suggestion: discuss broader implications and potential applications of the approach in other domains.  
- Question: provide details on how low-dimensional measurements were chosen for the algorithm.  
- Question: how will the on-policy assumption and replay buffer use be addressed in future work?  
- Question: discuss limitations when extra metadata/measurements are not available.  
- Question: how could this algorithm be applied to other domains like robotics or autonomous vehicles?
