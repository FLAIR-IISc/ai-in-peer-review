**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper presents a novel approach to sensorimotor control in immersive environments, leveraging a high-dimensional sensory stream and a lower-dimensional measurement stream. The authors propose a new algorithm that outperforms prior methods in novel 3D environments from raw sensory data and generalizes across goals and environments. The core idea is to supervise the prediction of low-dimensional measurements conditioned on a given goal vector and the current action. The algorithm was the winner of the Visual Doom AI competition.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

I recommend accepting this paper, with two key reasons:

1. **Strong empirical results**: The paper presents compelling empirical results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach in various scenarios, including the Visual Doom AI competition. The results show that the approach outperforms state-of-the-art deep RL methods, particularly in complex tasks.
2. **Well-motivated and well-placed in the literature**: The paper provides a clear and well-motivated introduction to the problem of sensorimotor control in immersive environments. The authors thoroughly discuss the limitations of traditional RL approaches and provide a convincing argument for their proposed solution.
