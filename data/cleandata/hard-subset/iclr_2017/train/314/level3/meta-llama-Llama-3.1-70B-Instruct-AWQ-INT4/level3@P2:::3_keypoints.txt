- Proposes a novel sensorimotor control approach using a high-dimensional sensory stream and lower-dimensional measurement stream.  
- Leverages cotemporal structure of sensory and measurement streams as a supervisory signal.  
- Trains a sensorimotor control model via supervised learning from raw sensory input without extraneous supervision.  
- Enables learning without fixed training-time goals and pursuit of dynamically changing goals at test time.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing strong motivation and grounding in related literature.  
- Paper is clearly explained with thorough, well-executed empirical evaluations.  
- Experiments in 3D Doom simulations show the method outperforms prior formulations, especially on hard tasks.  
- Demonstrates generalization to new goals and across environments within the evaluated domain.  
- Limitation: experiments conducted in a single environment (Doom), which may limit generality.  
- Situates method relative to intrinsic motivation, auxiliary tasks, and forward modeling literature.  
- Presentation is clear but has minor notation/terminology issues (misleading use of "P" in Eq. 2; double use of "j" in Eq. 6).  
- Suggested improvements: evaluate on more diverse and complex scenarios (multiple agents, dynamic environments).  
- Requests for authors: clarify how the measurement stream guides exploration and the role of goal weights.  
- Requests for authors: provide more details on predictor network architecture and design choices for handling high-dimensional inputs.
