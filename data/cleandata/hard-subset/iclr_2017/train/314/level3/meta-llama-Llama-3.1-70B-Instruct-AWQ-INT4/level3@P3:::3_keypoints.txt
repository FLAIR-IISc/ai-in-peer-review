- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): predict future measurements from high-dimensional sensory input, actions, and goals using supervised learning.
- Trained without extraneous supervision and evaluated in immersive 3D-like simulations, including the 2016 Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Recommendation: accept based on contributions, experiments, and milestone status.
- Well-motivated departure from traditional RL by leveraging cotemporal sensory/measurement structure instead of sparse scalar rewards.
- Strong empirical results: outperforms DQN, A3C, DSR and generalizes across environments and goals.
- Sound experimental design with prior-work comparisons, ablation studies, and component analyses.
- Notational issues: misuse of "P" in Eq.(2) and duplicate use of index "j" in Eq.(6) may confuse readers.
- Minor form issue: Doom described as 3D though it is a faux 3D environment.
- Presentation suggestion: repeat units in result tables for heterogeneous measurements.
- Future work suggestion: extend to continuous action spaces and incorporate memory-based models to reduce reactivity.
- Question: provide architecture details of the predictor network and configurations used in experiments.
- Question: how are varying goals during training/test handled when the goal vector is not fixed?
- Question: clarify the role and impact of predicting measurements at multiple temporal offsets on performance.
