This paper presents an on-policy deep reinforcement learning (RL) method that utilizes auxiliary intrinsic variables for 3D navigation. The approach is a special case of universal value function approaches, and the authors acknowledge and cite prior work in this area. The method's main technical contribution appears to be the distillation and integration of existing ideas rather than a novel algorithmic advance. The authors should clarify the contributions in the abstract and introduction to provide a clearer understanding of the paper's significance.

The paper demonstrates a clear empirical advantage over state-of-the-art deep RL methods in complex 3D environments, particularly in scenarios with richer measurements. The experiments provide useful insights for future agents, and the results show that the trained models generalize across environments and goals. However, the paper could benefit from a more in-depth analysis of failure modes and conditions where the model struggles to generalize to changing goals.

One conceptual issue with the on-policy training approach is that it can cause catastrophic forgetting if goals from the distant past are not revisited. The authors should discuss this limitation and potential solutions to mitigate it.

To further demonstrate the broader applicability of the approach, the authors could expand their experiments to other domains, such as Atari games using ROM as intrinsic variables. This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the method's capabilities and limitations.

Overall, I recommend accepting the paper, but with revisions to address the above points. The paper's contributions, while not entirely novel, provide a valuable addition to the field of deep RL, and the empirical results are impressive.

Specifically, I would like the authors to:

1. Clarify the contributions in the abstract and introduction to provide a clearer understanding of the paper's significance.
2. Provide a more in-depth analysis of failure modes and conditions where the model struggles to generalize to changing goals.
3. Discuss the limitation of on-policy training causing catastrophic forgetting and potential solutions to mitigate it.
4. Expand experiments to other domains, such as Atari games using ROM as intrinsic variables, to demonstrate broader applicability.

Questions for the authors:

1. How do the authors plan to address the limitation of on-policy training causing catastrophic forgetting?
2. Can the authors provide more details on the architecture and training procedure used in the experiments?
3. How do the authors plan to extend the approach to continuous action spaces?
4. Can the authors provide more insights into the ablation study, particularly on the importance of predicting measurements at multiple temporal offsets?
