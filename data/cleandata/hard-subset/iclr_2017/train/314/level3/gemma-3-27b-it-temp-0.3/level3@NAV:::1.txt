## Review of "Sensorimotor Control via Direct Future Prediction"

This paper presents a compelling approach to sensorimotor control in complex 3D environments, framing the problem as supervised learning rather than traditional reinforcement learning. The core idea – predicting low-dimensional measurements conditioned on goals and actions – is well-motivated and demonstrably effective, as evidenced by its winning performance in the Visual Doom AI Competition. The authors successfully demonstrate generalization across environments and goals, a significant advancement in the field. The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly communicates a complex idea with compelling empirical results.

**Accept** – This paper represents a significant contribution to deep reinforcement learning, offering a novel and successful approach to sensorimotor control. The strong empirical results, particularly the competition win, and the clear presentation make it a valuable addition to the conference.

The strength of this work lies in its departure from reward-based RL, leveraging the rich supervisory signal present in the interplay between sensory input and measurable states. The Monte Carlo regression framing, combined with the focus on low-dimensional measurements and goal conditioning, provides a surprisingly effective and efficient learning mechanism. The experiments are thorough, demonstrating performance gains over established methods and showcasing the model’s ability to adapt to new goals without retraining. The ablation studies further solidify the importance of the design choices.

However, two minor points warrant consideration. The use of a replay buffer introduces an on-policy assumption violation, which should be discussed in more detail. While the authors acknowledge this, elaborating on the potential impact and justification for this approximation would be beneficial. Secondly, the method’s reliance on pre-defined measurements (health, ammo, etc.) is a limitation. The paper should more explicitly acknowledge that this approach is contingent on the availability of such metadata and may not be applicable in scenarios where these measurements are absent or difficult to obtain.

**Additional Feedback & Questions:**

*   Could the authors elaborate on the choice of temporal offsets (1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32)? Was this determined empirically, and how sensitive is performance to these values?
*   The paper mentions the potential for extending the approach to continuous action spaces. Could the authors briefly discuss potential challenges and strategies for achieving this?
*   How does the performance of the model degrade when the goal vector is significantly different from those seen during training? A more detailed analysis of generalization capabilities beyond the tested scenarios would be valuable.
*   Regarding the on-policy assumption and replay buffer, what is the size of the replay buffer and how does this impact the stability and performance of the learning process?
