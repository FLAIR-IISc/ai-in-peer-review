## Review of "Sensorimotor Control with Intrinsic Measurements"

This paper presents an on-policy deep reinforcement learning approach for 3D navigation, leveraging a stream of sensory input alongside lower-dimensional measurements for richer supervision. The core idea – framing sensorimotor control as a supervised learning problem by predicting future measurements – is well-motivated and builds upon existing concepts like universal value function approaches, as appropriately cited. The authors demonstrate strong empirical results, notably winning the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition, outperforming more complex methods. 

I recommend **Accept** with minor revisions. The primary strength of this work lies in its compelling empirical performance and the insightful experimental results demonstrating generalization and the benefits of vectorial feedback. However, the contribution feels more like a skillful integration and distillation of existing ideas rather than a fundamentally novel algorithmic breakthrough.

The paper convincingly shows *that* the approach works well, but less clearly articulates *why* it works so well compared to other methods. While the authors highlight the benefits of dense, multi-dimensional supervision, a more detailed analysis of the learned representations and the impact of different measurement types would strengthen the argument. The discussion section touches on potential extensions, but further elaboration on the limitations of the current approach would be valuable. 

A key concern is the potential for catastrophic forgetting inherent in on-policy training. The paper doesn't address how the model retains knowledge of previously encountered goals, especially when revisiting them is infrequent. Analyzing failure modes, particularly scenarios where the model struggles to generalize to significantly different goals or environments, would be beneficial. 

**Additional Feedback:**

*   **Clarify Contributions:** The abstract and introduction should more explicitly state the specific contributions of this work beyond simply combining existing ideas. Is it the specific network architecture, the choice of measurements, or the training regime?
*   **Ablation Study Analysis:** Expand the discussion of the ablation study results. What does the performance drop when predicting only a scalar reward tell us about the value of the multi-dimensional measurement stream?
*   **Catastrophic Forgetting:** Discuss the implications of on-policy learning and potential mitigation strategies (e.g., experience replay with prioritized sampling).
*   **Broader Applicability:** While Doom is a compelling environment, exploring the method's performance in other domains (e.g., Atari with ROM state as intrinsic measurements) would further demonstrate its generalizability.

**Questions for the Authors:**

*   Can you elaborate on the choice of temporal offsets (1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32)? Was this determined empirically, and how sensitive is performance to these values?
*   How does the model handle situations where the predicted measurements are noisy or inaccurate?
*   What specific architectural choices (e.g., the expectation/action stream split) were most critical to the observed performance gains?
*   Could you provide a qualitative analysis of the learned representations? What features are being extracted from the sensory input and measurements?
