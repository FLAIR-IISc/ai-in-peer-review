- Introduces DFP: frame sensorimotor control as supervised learning of future measurements conditioned on current state, action, and goal.  
- Key innovation: use low-dimensional measurements (e.g., health, ammo) as supervisory signals instead of scalar rewards to enable flexible goal-directed behavior.  
- Empirical result: DFP outperforms state-of-the-art deep RL methods in complex Doom environments.  
- Generalization: demonstrates strong generalization across goals and environments.  
- Real-world validation: won the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Presentation: paper is well-communicated with clear motivation, thorough experiments, and convincing ablation studies (vectorial measurements, multi-step prediction).  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance, arguing supervised Monte Carlo regression can outperform traditional RL with significant empirical support.  
- Minor concern: need to explicitly discuss tension between on-policy assumption and use of a replay buffer.  
- Minor concern: should more clearly acknowledge reliance on environment-provided measurements (metadata) that compared algorithms may not have.  
- Revision suggestion: add detailed discussion of measurement selection and generalization to environments lacking natural measurements.  
- Revision suggestion: provide clearer comparison of DFP’s computational efficiency versus baselines.  
- Revision suggestion: include brief analysis of robustness to noisy measurements.  
- Question to authors: clarify how the method handles environments without explicit measurements and whether measurements can be learned from raw sensory input via self-supervision.
