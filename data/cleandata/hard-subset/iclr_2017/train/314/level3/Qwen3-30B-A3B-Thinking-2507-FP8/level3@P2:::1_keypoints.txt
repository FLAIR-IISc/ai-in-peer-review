- Proposes supervised learning of future measurements conditioned on current state, goal vector, and action for sensorimotor control in 3D environments.  
- Leverages temporal structure of sensory and measurement streams as a rich supervisory signal to learn from raw sensory input without extra supervision.  
- Uses vectorial measurements instead of scalar rewards and supports dynamic goal changes at test time without retraining.  
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art deep RL baselines in Doom scenarios and won the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Decision: Accept — significant contribution with strong empirical validation and clear motivation.  
- Rigorous experiments include comparisons to DQN, A3C, and DSR across four increasingly difficult Doom tasks, with large gains in complex Battle scenarios (e.g., 6× and 2.5× improvements).  
- Generalization tests show the model adapts across environments and goals without retraining; competition win offers external validation.  
- Suggest deeper discussion of the on-policy assumption vs. using a replay buffer and its effects on training dynamics.  
- Recommend elaborating limitations when structured measurements (health, ammo, etc.) are unavailable or hard to obtain.  
- Ask for more details on computational efficiency and training speed comparisons to baselines.  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the choice or availability of measurements, and can the model learn these measurements from raw input?  
- Question: How does the small replay buffer affect training stability and convergence, and would the method work without replay?  
- Question: Request details of the competition training regime and the specific architecture used for the winning entry (e.g., D4-tx-L).
