- Presents Direct Future Prediction (DFP), reframing sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future measurements from sensory input and goal vectors.  
- Trains agents to predict measurements (health, ammo, frags) and choose actions that maximize predicted future outcomes, avoiding sparse scalar rewards.  
- Enables flexible goal specification at test time via goal vectors.  
- Empirical results are strong: won Full Deathmatch track of Visual Doom AI Competition and outperformed state-of-the-art deep RL methods by >50% in that contest.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Main contribution: shows simple supervised methods with rich, temporally dense measurement streams can outperform sophisticated RL in complex 3D environments.  
- Experimental rigor: thorough comparisons against DQN, A3C, DSR and convincing ablation studies supporting vectorial measurements and multi-temporal prediction.  
- Strengths: clear problem formulation, substantial empirical gains, demonstrated generalization across environments/goals, practical utility via competition success, careful experimental design.  
- Literature positioning is appropriate, citing related supervised-control and goal-specification work.  
- Minor clarification: Doom uses 2D sprites in pseudo-3D—mention as a technical note about "3D" characterization.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify notation in equations (2) and (6), include measurement units in result tables, and detail randomized-goal regime implementation.  
- Questions for authors: scalability to more complex (nonlinear) goal specifications, dependence on choice/number of measurements, and extension to continuous action spaces (required modifications).  
- Overall: reproducible, well-designed experiments that provide a solid foundation despite limited environment diversity (all Doom-based).
