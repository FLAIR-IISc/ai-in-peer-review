- Reframes sensorimotor control as supervised learning using sensory and measurement streams rather than reinforcement learning.
- Uses high-dimensional sensory input plus low-dimensional measurements to create dense supervisory signals.
- Key insight: temporal interlock between sensory and measurement streams provides sufficient supervision without extra labels.
- Model predicts future measurements from current observations, measurements, and a goal, then chooses actions to maximize predicted goal achievement.
- Evaluated extensively in 3D Doom environments with strong empirical advantages over deep RL baselines.
- Demonstrated substantial performance gains (e.g., ~6x in Battle) and won the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch track.
- Shows strong generalization across environments and dynamically specified goals at test time.
- Ablation studies show vectorial measurements and multiple temporal offsets are critical to performance.
- Experimental design is thorough, comparing to relevant baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR) and showing robust results.
- Method is simple and effective in complex immersive domains where traditional RL struggles.
- Weakness: presentation should more clearly articulate novelty relative to universal value function approaches (Schaul et al., 2015).
- Weakness: potential for catastrophic forgetting during on-policy training with limited experience memory is not fully addressed.
- Weakness: lacks detailed failure-mode analysis for goal generalization and conditions where it fails.
- Weakness: limited discussion of applicability beyond Doom and to domains with less readily available measurements.
- Questions for authors: specify goal configurations where generalization fails and boundary conditions for generalization.
- Questions for authors: explain how catastrophic forgetting of earlier goals is mitigated with on-policy training and small replay.
- Questions for authors: discuss adaptation to environments with ambiguous or unavailable measurements and whether the method can learn measurements from raw input.
