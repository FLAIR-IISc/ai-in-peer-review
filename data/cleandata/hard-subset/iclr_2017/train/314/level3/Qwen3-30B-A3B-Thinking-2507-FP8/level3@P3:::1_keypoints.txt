- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP): supervise prediction of future low-dim measurements conditioned on goal and action instead of scalar reward optimization.  
- Leverages cotemporal structure of sensory and measurement streams to provide dense, temporally rich supervision.  
- Learns from raw sensory input, is goal-conditioned, and can generalize to new goals/environments without retraining.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance in complex 3D Doom environments and won the Visual Doom AI Competition by a large margin.  
- Framing learning as supervised Monte Carlo regression and using low-dimensional measurements are key methodological contributions.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Addresses limitations of traditional RL that rely on sparse scalar rewards; approach is well-motivated by literature and environment structure.  
- Strong empirical support: thorough evaluations across four Doom scenarios showing DFP outperforms DQN, A3C, and DSR, especially with multiple measurements.  
- Ablation studies confirm the importance of vectorial feedback and multi-temporal prediction.  
- Generalization experiments and dynamic-goal tests show robustness and flexibility of the method.  
- Competition victory provides compelling practical validation and shows simplicity can outperform approaches using extra supervision.  
- Suggest clarifying the on-policy assumption when training with a replay buffer and why the approximation works in practice.  
- Recommend discussing applicability when measurement metadata (e.g., health, ammo) is unavailable and clarifying boundaries of the approach.  
- Ask for more detail on computational efficiency and training speed comparisons with other methods.  
- Suggest discussing extensions to continuous action spaces as a future direction.  
- Question: How does the model distinguish desirable vs. undesirable measurements under randomized goals and generalize to conflicting goal distributions?  
- Question: Provide concrete dimensionality ratios (sensory input vs. measurement dimensions) used in experiments.  
- Question: Which specific aspects of the hardest tasks (D3, D4) make them difficult for standard RL, and how does measurement-based learning address these challenges?  
- Question: Explain how the model’s generalization (Table 2) enabled success on the competition’s previously unseen environments.
