- Proposes supervised prediction of future measurements for sensorimotor control in immersive 3D environments instead of traditional RL.  
- Uses an on-policy method combining intrinsic measurements (health, ammo, frag count) with visual input to train without fixed goals and allow flexible goal changes at test time.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains over state-of-the-art deep RL on complex Doom navigation tasks.  
- Model won the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Decision: Accept (with minor revisions).  
- Paper is well-motivated, properly situated in related work, and supported by rigorous experiments showing superior performance and generalization.  
- Technical novelty is unclear—method appears to be an application of universal value function concepts rather than a novel algorithmic advance.  
- Authors should explicitly clarify how their formulation differs from prior work in the abstract and introduction.  
- Paper lacks analysis of failure modes; needs experiments/analysis on conditions where the model fails to generalize to changing goals.  
- On-policy training raises catastrophic forgetting concerns; authors should address how goals from the distant past are retained or revisited.  
- Recommend broader applicability experiments (e.g., ROM as intrinsic variables in Atari) and more detailed failure-case analysis to strengthen the paper.
