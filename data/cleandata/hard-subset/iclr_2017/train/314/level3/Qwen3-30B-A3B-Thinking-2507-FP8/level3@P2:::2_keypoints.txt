- Presents a sensorimotor control method that predicts future measurements from high-dim sensory input, current measurements, and a goal, framing control as supervised learning.  
- Trains from raw experience without extraneous supervision and generalizes to dynamic goals at test time.  
- Strong empirical results: outperforms DQN/A3C/DSR and wins Visual Doom Full Deathmatch by >50%.  
- Extensive experiments across four increasing-difficulty Doom scenarios showing superior performance and generalization.  
- Key strength: vectorial measurements provide richer supervisory signal than scalar rewards, enabling better performance on complex tasks.  
- Technical novelty is limited—mainly a distillation/integration of existing ideas rather than a new algorithm.  
- Needs clearer, explicit statement of specific contributions relative to prior work (e.g., universal value functions) in abstract/introduction.  
- Should include analysis of failure modes and where the model struggles to generalize to changing goals.  
- On-policy training may risk catastrophic forgetting; discuss how experience replay/memory mitigates this.  
- Recommend broader-domain experiments (e.g., Atari with ROM states) to demonstrate generality.  
- Questions for authors: clarify differences from Schaul et al.'s universal value function framework; explain why predicting multiple measurements improves single-measurement evaluation; provide examples of goal vectors that cause poor performance and why.
