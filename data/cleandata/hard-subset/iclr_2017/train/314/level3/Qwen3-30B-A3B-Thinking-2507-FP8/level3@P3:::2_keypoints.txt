- Proposes predicting future vectorial measurements from current sensory input, measurements, and goals to select actions for sensorimotor control.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical advantages in 3D navigation (Visual Doom AI Competition win, >50% lead).  
- Argues vectorial measurements offer benefits over scalar rewards and shows strong generalization across environments/goals.  
- Decision: Accept — compelling empirical case with significant, well-documented results.  
- Strength: Strong empirical validation, real-world competition win, proper literature placement, and controlled ablation studies.  
- Limitation: Technical contribution is largely an integration/distillation of existing ideas rather than a novel algorithmic advance.  
- Recommendation: Clarify in abstract/introduction that the main contribution is application/distillation, not a fundamentally new algorithm.  
- Recommend deeper analysis of failure modes, especially failures to generalize under changing goals, with example cases.  
- Raise concern about catastrophic forgetting in on-policy training when past goals aren’t revisited; request discussion of mitigation strategies.  
- Suggest expanding experiments to other domains (e.g., Atari with ROM as intrinsic variables) to demonstrate broader applicability.  
- Recommend explicitly relating the method to Schaul et al. (2015) and universal value function approaches, noting it is a special case.  
- Ask authors to characterize novelty relative to Schaul et al. (2015) and which parts of that framework they leverage.  
- Ask for more details on conditions where the model struggles to generalize with changing goals.  
- Ask how catastrophic forgetting is mitigated in practice and for evidence it did not significantly affect experiments.  
- Ask whether the approach has been considered for other domains and what the main challenges would be.
