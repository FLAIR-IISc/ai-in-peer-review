- Introduces Direct Future Prediction (DFP), an on-policy RL method that predicts future intrinsic measurements instead of relying on scalar rewards.  
- Leverages cotemporal structure of sensory and measurement streams to provide dense supervision from raw experience.  
- Trains a predictor conditioned on current observations, goals, and actions to anticipate future measurements, enabling flexible goal pursuit.  
- Demonstrates DFP outperforms state-of-the-art (A3C, DQN, DSR) in challenging vizDoom scenarios.  
- Achieved victory in the 2016 Visual Doom AI Competition with no extra supervision beyond competitors.  
- Recommends acceptance; paper makes a clear, substantial contribution to the field.  
- Key claim: vectorial future prediction provides superior supervision to scalar rewards in complex 3D-like environments.  
- Empirical gains are compelling (up to 6× over A3C in hardest scenarios) while using a less complex architecture.  
- Ablation study effectively validates the importance of key components.  
- Shows good generalization to unseen environments and ability to adapt to new goals at test time.  
- Minor issues: describes Doom as “3D” though it’s 2D rendered in fake 3D; notational inconsistencies in equations (2) and (6); results tables should repeat measurement units.  
- Suggestions: clarify how the goal-weighting mechanism enables exploration during training and detail how the normalization layer in eq. (6) affects learning dynamics.  
- Overall: method’s simplicity combined with strong empirical performance represents a valuable milestone.
