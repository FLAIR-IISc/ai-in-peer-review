## Review of "Sensorimotor Control via Direct Future Prediction"

This paper presents an on-policy deep reinforcement learning method for 3D navigation, leveraging auxiliary intrinsic variables (sensory and measurement streams) to facilitate learning. The core idea is to frame sensorimotor control as a supervised learning problem: predicting the effect of actions on future measurements, rather than relying on sparse reward signals. The approach demonstrates impressive results in the Doom environment, even winning the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition. The authors appropriately position their work within the reinforcement learning landscape, citing relevant prior work on universal value function approaches and the benefits of dense feedback.

I recommend **Accept** this paper. While the contribution isn’t a groundbreaking algorithmic innovation, it’s a compelling demonstration of how distilling and effectively integrating existing ideas can yield state-of-the-art performance in a challenging domain. The empirical validation is strong and provides valuable insights into the benefits of rich, multivariate feedback for sensorimotor control.

My reasoning rests on the clear empirical advantage demonstrated by the proposed method. The results in Doom, and particularly the victory in a competitive setting against more complex models, are substantial. The ablation studies provide supporting evidence for the efficacy of the multi-dimensional measurement stream and multi-step prediction. The demonstration of generalization across environments, though potentially limited (as discussed below), is also a positive aspect.

However, the paper could be strengthened with a more nuanced discussion of its limitations. The current framing downplays the on-policy nature of the method and the potential for catastrophic forgetting. While the authors mention a small experience replay buffer, a more thorough analysis of how this mitigates (or doesn’t) the issue of revisiting past goals would be valuable.  Furthermore, the generalization analysis feels slightly superficial. It would be helpful to see failure cases, or a more quantitative assessment of how performance degrades when the environment changes drastically. Finally, exploring the applicability of this approach beyond 3D navigation, as suggested by the reviewers, would solidify the broader relevance of the work.

Specifically, I have these questions for the authors:

*   Could you elaborate on the effect of the experience replay buffer size on performance and stability, particularly concerning catastrophic forgetting?
*   Can you provide specific examples of scenarios where the model *fails* to generalize, and analyze the underlying reasons? Are there specific types of environmental changes that are particularly problematic?
*   The paper mentions that goal vectors can be defined in terms of future measurements. Are there any limitations to what can be expressed as a goal in this framework? Could you explore learning the goal representation itself, rather than hand-defining it?
*   Regarding the success in the Doom competition, could you provide more details about the specific environment variations encountered, and how the model’s performance compared on those variations?
