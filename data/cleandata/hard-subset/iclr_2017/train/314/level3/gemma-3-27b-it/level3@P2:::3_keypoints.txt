- Decision: Accept — sound approach, thoroughly evaluated, achieves state-of-the-art and wins Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Core idea: Frame sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future intrinsic measurements rather than using reward-based RL.
- Motivation: Leverages dense cotemporal sensory-measurement signals to overcome sparse rewards and enable goal-free training and dynamic goal adaptation.
- Empirical strength: Robust comparisons to DQN, A3C, and DSR across Doom scenarios show DFP consistently outperforms baselines.
- Ablations: Demonstrate importance of vectorial (multi-measurement) feedback and multi-step prediction for performance gains.
- Generalization: Shows robustness in randomly textured environments and strong adaptability demonstrated by competition win.
- Competition result: Outperforms a more complex, extra-supervision second-place entry, highlighting simplicity and effectiveness.
- Positioning: Contributions are clearly articulated and well-situated within RL, supervised learning, forward modeling, and intrinsic motivation literatures.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with a clear model architecture and training procedure.
- Environment detail: Correct to note Doom is effectively 2.5D, not fully 3D.
- Notation issues: Using "p" for predicted values and reusing index "j" can be misleading; suggest clearer variable names.
- Table presentation: Units for measurements (health, ammo, frags) are inconsistent or missing; include explicit units for readability.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Request brief discussion of sensitivity to key hyperparameters (learning rate, network architecture).
- Future work: Recommend expanding discussion on memory, hierarchical organization, and continuous actions, including potential challenges.
- Q1 (prediction horizon): Ask whether predicting more future steps exhibits diminishing returns or continuous improvement.
- Q2 (goal generalization): Ask how DFP compares to models trained per-goal and whether generalization trades off with per-goal performance.
- Q3 (competition comparison): Ask for elaboration on advantages of the simpler approach versus additional supervision used by the second-place submission and contexts where extra supervision helps.
- Q4 (goal distribution): Ask what distribution was used for randomized goals during training and how different distributions affect performance.
