- Presents an on-policy deep RL method for 3D navigation that predicts future measurements of sensory and measurement streams instead of relying on sparse rewards.  
- Frames sensorimotor control as a supervised learning problem of action-conditioned future-measurement prediction.  
- Leverages dense, multivariate feedback (measurement stream) as an auxiliary signal to facilitate learning.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance in Doom, including winning the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Authors situate the work among universal value function and dense-feedback prior work appropriately.  
- Contribution is more an effective integration and distillation of existing ideas than a novel algorithmic breakthrough.  
- Empirical validation is strong, with ablations supporting the importance of multi-dimensional measurements and multi-step prediction.  
- Shows some generalization across environments, but the analysis of generalization is limited.  
- Paper downplays on-policy limitations and risks of catastrophic forgetting despite using a small experience replay buffer.  
- Recommends deeper analysis of how replay buffer size affects performance and stability.  
- Requests examples and analysis of failure cases and quantitative measures of performance degradation under drastic environment changes.  
- Suggests exploring limits of goal-vector representations and the possibility of learning goal representations rather than hand-defining them.  
- Asks for more details on the Doom competition setups and how performance varied across specific environment variations.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — compelling empirical demonstration with valuable insights, but needs a more nuanced discussion of limitations and broader applicability.
