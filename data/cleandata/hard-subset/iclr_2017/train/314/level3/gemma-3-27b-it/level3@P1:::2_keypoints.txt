- Decision: Accept — solid, well-executed study with significant practical contribution and strong empirical results.
- Core idea: Reframe sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting action effects on future measurements using cotemporal sensory/measurement streams.
- Empirical strength: Outperforms strong deep RL baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR) across multiple Doom scenarios and wins Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch.
- Generalization: Shows transfer to new environments via texture randomization and to dynamically changing goals.
- Ablations: Demonstrates importance of multivariate measurements and multi-horizon prediction.
- Architecture & training: Predictor network inspired by DQN is well-justified; training procedure and hyperparameters are described for reproducibility.
- Novelty framing: Paper integrates existing ideas but achieves notable empirical success — novelty should be emphasized more clearly in abstract/introduction.
- Limitations: Missing discussion of failure modes and conditions where the method struggles to generalize to new goals.
- On-policy concern: Training appears on-policy; catastrophic forgetting of past goals is a potential issue despite use of experience replay.
- Broader applicability: Experiments limited to Doom; testing on other domains (e.g., Atari) would strengthen claims of generality.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Choice of goal vector temporal offsets and coefficients needs further analysis and sensitivity study.
- Comparison request: Authors should clarify differences from universal value function approaches and related prior work.
- Empirical questions: Request details on observed failure modes, on-policy forgetting mitigation, alternative temporal offset/coefficient choices, and why multivariate measurements are crucial.
