- Decision: Accept with enthusiasm.
- Proposal: Recast sensorimotor control as supervised prediction of low-dimensional future measurements conditioned on actions and goals.
- Empirical result: Outperforms state-of-the-art deep RL baselines (DQN, A3C, DSR) in the Doom domain.
- Generalization: Shows strong generalization across environments and goal adaptation.
- Real-world validation: Model won the Full Deathmatch track of the Visual Doom AI Competition.
- Motivation: Leverages temporal structure of sensory and measurement streams to avoid sparse, hand-engineered rewards.
- Learning paradigm: Trains from raw experience without imitation or hand-crafted features.
- Ablations: Ablation studies demonstrate importance of vectorial measurements and multi-step future prediction.
- Comparative advantage: Outperformed a more complex model with additional supervision in the competition, indicating efficiency and elegance.
- Reproducibility: Method, network architecture, and training procedure are clearly described for replication.
- Related work: Related work section is comprehensive and appropriately contextualized.
- On-policy vs. Off-policy concern: Use of a replay buffer introduces off-policy elements—discussion of potential bias and regularization needed.
- Measurement dependency: Method depends on access to informative, low-dimensional measurements; limitations if such measurements are unavailable need discussion.
- Goal representation: Current linear combination of future measurements is simple; more sophisticated goal representations could improve performance.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: A brief analysis of sensitivity to hyperparameters (learning rate, batch size, architecture) would be valuable.
- Computational cost: Comparison of training time and memory usage versus baselines is missing and would complete the evaluation.
- Q1 for authors: How sensitive is performance to temporal offsets (τ1,...,τn) and was an ablation done?
- Q2 for authors: What directions do the authors envision for more sophisticated goal representations?
- Q3 for authors: How could useful measurements be learned from raw sensory input when explicit measurements are unavailable?
- Q4 for authors: Please provide detailed computational resource breakdown (training time, memory) compared to baselines.
