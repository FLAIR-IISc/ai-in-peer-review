- Recommendation: Accept — significant advancement with strong empirical results warranting acceptance.
- Novelty: Frames sensorimotor control as supervised learning (predicting low-dimensional measurements) rather than traditional RL.
- Core idea: Predict measurements conditioned on goals and actions is compelling and clearly communicated.
- Empirical validation: Strong results including winning the Visual Doom AI Competition demonstrate practical effectiveness.
- Writing and positioning: Paper is well-written and situates its contributions relative to prior work (Monte Carlo estimation, low-dim measurements, goal conditioning).
- Strength: Effectively leverages readily available supervisory signals to avoid sparse-reward issues and enable stable learning from raw sensory data.
- Generalization: Demonstrates notable generalization across environments and goals, suggesting adaptable policies.
- Strength: Comprehensive empirical comparisons support the method’s advantages.
- Concern: Use of a replay buffer introduces an on-/off-policy mismatch; paper needs deeper discussion and possible ablations on its impact to convergence/stability.
- Concern: Method depends on access to “extra metadata” (measurements); implications when such signals are unavailable should be emphasized.
- Feedback — hyperparameters: Need discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and whether extensive sweeps were performed.
- Feedback — ablations: Expand ablation studies to test different measurement combinations and their relative importance.
- Feedback — goal representation: Explore richer goal representations (non-linear, hierarchical) beyond linear combinations of future measurements.
- Feedback — computational cost: Provide a more detailed analysis of training resources and scaling with environment/action complexity.
- Feedback — visualizations: Add visualizations (e.g., activation maps) to illustrate learned internal representations.
- Question 1: Did the replay buffer cause instability or convergence issues, and were techniques like importance sampling considered?
- Question 2: How does performance degrade if measurements are limited/unavailable, and can informative measurements be identified automatically?
- Question 3: What was the hyperparameter tuning process and how sensitive is performance to parameter changes?
- Question 4: What is the computational cost of training and how does it scale with environment/action complexity?
- Question 5: What specific failure cases or limitations were observed in experiments?
