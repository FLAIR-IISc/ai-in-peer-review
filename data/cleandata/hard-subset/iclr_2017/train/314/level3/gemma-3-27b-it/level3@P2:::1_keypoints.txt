- Novel formulation: cast sensorimotor control as supervised prediction of future measurements conditioned on actions and goals, leveraging cotemporal sensory-measurement structure.  
- Empirical performance: convincingly outperforms state-of-the-art deep RL in Doom and wins the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — clear, effective solution with strong experimental support.  
- Motivation: addresses sparse rewards and exploration challenges in RL by using dense, multidimensional measurement feedback.  
- Method benefit: supervised learning reformulation uses readily available measurements (health, ammo) as supervision, offering an alternative to traditional RL.  
- Experimental strength: consistent gains across multiple Doom scenarios, including complex Battle tasks.  
- Ablation results: demonstrate the importance of vectorial feedback (multiple measurements) and multi-temporal prediction horizons.  
- Generalization: learned policies show robustness and adaptability to new environments (e.g., texture generalization).  
- Comparative evaluation: thorough comparisons to DQN, A3C, DSR show a clear advantage for the proposed method.  
- Presentation: well-written paper with clear methodology; architecture and implementation details sufficiently described (appendix).  
- Replay buffer issue: uses an off-policy replay buffer despite Monte Carlo/on-policy framing — needs discussion of implications for convergence and sample efficiency.  
- Measurement dependence: method relies on identifying informative measurements; discuss limitations when measurements are unavailable or noisy and possible approaches to learn measurements from raw input.  
- Goal representation: uses a linear goal function u(f; g); consider exploring richer goal representations (e.g., learned embeddings).  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: paper should discuss sensitivity to key hyperparameters (learning rate, replay buffer size) and tuning strategies.  
- Further generalization: suggest evaluating transfer to different environment types (other game genres, simulated robotic tasks).  
- Questions for authors: clarify replay buffer impact vs pure on-policy, strategies for scenarios lacking measurements, sensitivity/tuning of learning rate and buffer size, plans for broader generalization tests, and rationale for linear goal representation versus learned alternatives.
