- Proposal: Frame sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting future intrinsic measurements (health, ammo) given sensory input, action, and goal (Direct Future Prediction, DFP).  
- Claim: DFP enables learning control policies without explicit rewards and can adapt to dynamically changing goals.  
- Empirical result: DFP outperforms A3C and DQN in the Doom environment and wins the Visual Doom AI Competition Full Deathmatch track.  
- Generalization: Method shows transfer to unseen Doom environments and goal flexibility at test time.  
- Decision: Accept — strong, compelling contribution with convincing experimental validation.  
- Evidence strength: Extensive experiments and statistically persuasive performance gains across complex scenarios.  
- Ablation: Ablation study justifies model components and design choices.  
- Practical validation: Competition win demonstrates real-world applicability and robustness against a more complex, supervised competitor.  
- Motivation & positioning: Clear articulation of limitations of traditional RL and good coverage of related work.  
- Terminology issue: Doom should be described as 2.5D rather than “three-dimensional” for precision.  
- Notation issue: Symbol “p” in Eq.(2) and repeated “j” in Eq.(6) are potentially confusing and should be revised.  
- Results presentation: Tables should include units for measurements (e.g., Health %, Ammo units, Frags count).  
- Environment scope: Generalization claims rely on Doom-only variations; testing on fundamentally different environments would strengthen applicability claims.  
- Computational cost: Paper should discuss training and computational cost of DFP versus baselines.  
- Question—temporal offsets: Why were offsets (1,2,4,8,16,32) chosen and was sensitivity analysis performed?  
- Question—measurement interactions: Were there interactions between predicted measurements (e.g., predicting health aiding frags prediction)?  
- Question—goal vector usage: Request a concrete example of exponential decay goal vector usage in practice (e.g., a Doom scenario).  
- Question—limitations: Ask authors to discuss environments or task types where DFP may be less effective.  
- Question—competition baseline details: Request details on the additional supervision used by the competitor to contextualize the comparison.
