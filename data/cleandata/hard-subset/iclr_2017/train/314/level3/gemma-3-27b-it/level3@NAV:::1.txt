## Review of "Sensorimotor Control via Direct Future Prediction"

This paper presents a compelling approach to sensorimotor control by framing it as a supervised learning problem focused on predicting low-dimensional measurements conditioned on goals and actions. The core idea – utilizing the rich supervisory signal from the interplay between sensory input and measurements – is well-motivated, particularly in complex 3D environments like Doom where sparse rewards are problematic. The method demonstrates impressive performance, culminating in winning the Visual Doom AI Competition, a strong indicator of its effectiveness. The paper clearly articulates the benefits of this approach, including its ability to generalize across goals without retraining, a significant step towards more flexible and adaptable agents. The contribution is substantial, lying in the shift towards Monte Carlo estimation, the strategic use of low-dimensional measurements, and the effective parametrization of goal conditioning.

**Accept.** I recommend acceptance of this paper. The demonstrated performance, particularly winning the Visual Doom AI competition, and the novel framing of reinforcement learning as a supervised prediction problem are significant contributions. The paper is well-written and the results are clearly presented, supporting the claims made.

The strength of this work resides in its practical success and insightful approach. The authors convincingly demonstrate that by focusing on predicting readily available measurements (health, ammo), they can achieve state-of-the-art performance in a challenging environment. The ability to adapt to new goals *without* further training is particularly impressive and points towards a more generalizable form of intelligence.  The empirical validation is strong, going beyond standard benchmark comparisons to include a competitive win against other sophisticated agents. 

However, there are a few points that could enhance the paper. The reliance on a replay buffer, while common, introduces an on-policy approximation that warrants further discussion regarding its potential impact on convergence and stability.  Furthermore, the method’s dependence on “extra metadata” – the specific measurements to predict – is a limitation that deserves more explicit acknowledgement. While these measurements are readily available in Doom, their absence in other domains could severely hinder the approach’s applicability.

**Additional Feedback/Questions:**

*   Could the authors elaborate on the specific hyperparameter tuning process for each baseline method? More detail here would strengthen the comparison.
*   The paper mentions the potential benefits of integrating memory-based models. Could the authors briefly discuss *how* such an integration might be implemented and what challenges it might introduce?
*   Regarding the replay buffer, how sensitive is the performance to the size and sampling strategy of the buffer? Does a larger buffer consistently improve performance, or is there a diminishing return?
*   How might the method be adapted to situations where relevant measurements are *not* directly provided, but must be inferred from raw sensory input?
