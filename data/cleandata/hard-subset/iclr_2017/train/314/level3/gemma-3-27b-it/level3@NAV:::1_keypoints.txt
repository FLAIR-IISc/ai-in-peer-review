- Frames sensorimotor control as supervised learning by predicting low-dimensional measurements conditioned on goals and actions.  
- Well-motivated approach for complex 3D environments with sparse rewards (e.g., Doom).  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance, including winning the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Key contributions: Monte Carlo estimation shift, use of low-dimensional measurements, and effective goal-conditioning parametrization.  
- Method generalizes across goals without retraining, enabling flexible, adaptable agents.  
- Practical success supported by clear presentation and solid empirical validation beyond standard benchmarks.  
- Strength: leverages readily available measurements (health, ammo) as rich supervisory signals.  
- Limitation: reliance on a replay buffer introduces an on-policy approximation that may affect convergence and stability.  
- Limitation: dependence on extra metadata (specific measurements) reduces applicability to domains lacking such signals.  
- Request for more detail on hyperparameter tuning procedures for each baseline to strengthen comparisons.  
- Suggestion to discuss how memory-based models could be integrated and the challenges that would entail.  
- Request for analysis of replay buffer sensitivity (size and sampling strategy) and adaptation when measurements must be inferred from raw sensory input.
