- Proposes Direct Future Prediction (DFP), an on-policy method that predicts future intrinsic measurements instead of learning from scalar rewards.  
- Leverages cotemporal structure of sensory input and measurement streams as rich supervisory signal.  
- Applied to VizDoom, achieving state-of-the-art performance and winning the 2016 VizDoom AI Competition.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing compelling experimental results and clarity of approach.  
- Core contribution: reframes sensorimotor control as supervised learning to predict future measurements rather than reward optimization.  
- Effectively positioned within literature, connecting to intrinsic motivation, auxiliary tasks, and forward modeling.  
- Robust experimental setup with comparisons to A3C, DQN, and a DSR attempt.  
- Ablation study supports the importance of proposed model components.  
- Suggest clarifying that Doom is fundamentally 2D with pseudo-3D rendering for accuracy.  
- Notation issues: “P” in Eq.(2) is misleading (prediction vs probability) and the double use of “j” in Eq.(6) may confuse readers.  
- Results tables should repeat units for heterogeneous measurements (e.g., health points, rounds, frags count) in column headers.  
- Question: How are goal vectors initialized in randomized goals training and is performance sensitive to their initial distribution?  
- Question: Provide more detailed hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (learning rate, mini-batch size, network architecture).  
- Question: Provide detailed computational cost breakdown for DFP training (GPU type, training time) for reproducibility.  
- Question: How well would DFP generalize to substantially different domains (e.g., robotic manipulation) and what adaptations would be needed?  
- Question: How were temporal offsets (1,2,4,8,16,32) chosen and were alternative offset schedules explored?
