- Frames sensorimotor control as supervised prediction of future measurements from sensory input and actions, avoiding reward-based RL complexities.  
- Validated in Visual Doom AI Competition; method wins Full Deathmatch track.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Well-motivated discussion of RL limitations in complex visual 3D environments.  
- Positions work within prior literature and honestly characterizes contribution as integration/distillation of existing ideas.  
- Strong empirical evaluation comparing DFP to DQN, A3C, DSR across increasingly difficult Doom scenarios.  
- DFP consistently outperforms baselines, particularly in harder scenarios.  
- Ablation studies show multivariate measurements and multi-temporal predictions are crucial.  
- Generalization experiments demonstrate robustness to changes in environment textures.  
- Competition win and reported training speeds provide strong independent validation.  
- Model architecture (perception, measurement, goal modules; expectation and action streams) is clearly described and justified.  
- Connections to prior relevant work (e.g., Wang et al.) are acknowledged.  
- Suggest clarifying the paper’s specific contribution in the abstract/introduction to highlight the unique combination and benefits.  
- Recommend investigating catastrophic forgetting from on-policy training, including experiments on revisiting older goals and failure-mode analysis.  
- Suggest expanding experiments beyond Doom (e.g., Atari) to show broader applicability.  
- Questions for authors: rationale for chosen temporal offsets and whether alternatives were tested.  
- Questions for authors: was there analysis of which measurements are most important (feature/measurement selection)?  
- Questions for authors: how sensitive are results to experience replay memory size (M)?  
- Questions for authors: details on differences and benefits of the competition-winning D4-tx-L variant versus the standard model.  
- Questions for authors: have mitigation techniques for catastrophic forgetting (e.g., periodic replay of earlier goals) been considered?
