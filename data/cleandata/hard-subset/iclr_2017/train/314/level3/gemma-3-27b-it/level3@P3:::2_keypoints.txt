- Presents an on-policy deep RL approach for 3D sensorimotor control (ViZDoom) by reframing RL as supervised prediction of future measurements (health, ammo) from sensory input, actions, and goals.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance, outperforming DQN, A3C, and DSR, and winning the Visual Doom AI Competition.  
- Leverages intrinsic, temporally dense environmental signals to learn without sparse external rewards.  
- Strong experimental validation with consistent results across increasingly complex scenarios.  
- Ablation studies show vectorial feedback and multi-step prediction are important for performance.  
- Provides useful analysis of generalization across environments and effective use of the ViZDoom platform.  
- Novelty is not clearly articulated—unclear if the main contribution is the technique combination, scale, intrinsic measurements emphasis, or architectural element.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of failure modes and scenarios where the model struggles or degrades under drastic environment changes.  
- Does not sufficiently address catastrophic forgetting inherent to on-policy learning or practical mitigation strategies beyond revisiting goals.  
- Would benefit from broader evaluation beyond Doom (e.g., Atari with ROM-state intrinsic variables) to demonstrate generality.  
- Requests clarification on the core novelty and contribution framing.  
- Asks for specific mechanisms used to mitigate catastrophic forgetting with randomized goals.  
- Requests examples and characterization of consistent failure cases and contributing factors.  
- Queries about experiments with more complex goal representations (non-linear or hierarchical) and their effects.  
- Asks about sensitivity to hyperparameters, especially learning rate and temporal-offset loss coefficients.  
- Requests concrete computational cost metrics (GPU hours) for training the best DFP models.  
- Asks how well the method generalizes to novel action spaces or environments with different action sets.
