DFP proposes directly predicting future intrinsic measurements instead of using reward-based RL.  
The idea is well-motivated by the temporal structure of sensory and measurement streams.  
Strong empirical validation in VizDoom, including winning the 2016 competition.  
DFP outperforms baselines such as A3C, DQN, and a DSR baseline.  
Ablation study shows each model component meaningfully contributes to performance.  
Paper is accepted for publication due to strong experiments and milestone achievement.  
Work is well-situated within literature on intrinsic motivation, auxiliary tasks, and forward modeling.  
Experiments are well-designed and results are clearly presented.  
Minor corrections: Doom should not be described as “three-dimensional” (it’s 2D with fake 3D).  
Notation issues: “P” in Eq.(2) is misleading (prediction, not probability) and “j” is used twice in Eq.(6).  
Results tables should repeat units for heterogeneous measurements (e.g., “health %”, “ammo count”, “frags”).  
Questions for authors: how to integrate memory (e.g., LSTM) into DFP; how goal-space exploration was ensured with randomized goals; and why DFP outperforms baselines beyond dense multivariate feedback.
