- Positions work within deep RL and builds on empirical successes using deep function approximators.  
- Introduces a new algorithm that outperforms prior methods in novel 3D environments from raw sensory data and generalizes across goals/environments.  
- Algorithm was the winner of the Visual Doom AI competition.  
- Core idea: supervise prediction of low-dimensional measurements (e.g., ammo, health) conditioned on a given goal vector and the current action.  
- Action selection is done by choosing the action that maximizes the predicted outcome for the specified goal.  
- Learning is framed as supervised Monte Carlo regression rather than temporal-difference learning or successor features.  
- Key contributions: focus on Monte Carlo estimation, use of low-dimensional measurements, parametrized goal conditioning, and empirical comparisons to prior work.  
- Demonstrates learned policies can respond to limited goal changes without additional training.  
- Paper is well-communicated and presents compelling empirical results of significant interest.  
- Minor concern: supervised training makes an on-policy assumption but uses a replay buffer, an approximation that should be discussed.  
- Minor concern: method relies on extra metadata (which measurements to predict) unavailable to compared algorithms; limitations when such measurements are absent should be more clearly noted.
