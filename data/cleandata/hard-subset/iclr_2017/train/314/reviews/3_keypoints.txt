- Proposes an on-policy method (DFP) that predicts future intrinsic measurements rather than rewards.
- Experiments run in vizDoom predicting sequences of triplets (health, ammunition, frags) weighted by input goal triplets.
- Goal-weighted predictions enable guiding exploration by changing goal weights; actions at test time maximize long-term goals.
- Method achieved strong empirical results, winning the 2016 vizDoom competition.
- Empirical comparisons show DFP outperforms or matches A3C, DQN, and a DSR attempt (with A3C reaching a ceiling in one scenario).
- Ablation study indicates the proposed model components and added complexity are useful.
- Paper situates itself among prior work on intrinsic motivation, auxiliary tasks, and forward modelling and cites relevant literature adequately though not exhaustively.
- Minor form issues: Doom described as 3D though it is 2D presented in fake 3D.
- Notational issues: "P" in equation (2) is misleading (used for prediction, not probability), and the double use of "j" in (6) may confuse.
- Presentation suggestion: results tables should repeat units for heterogeneous measurements.
- Overall recommendation: clear accept — experimental soundness, reproducibility, and milestone status justify publication despite limited environment diversity or perceived novelty.
