- Proposal: Introduce recursion into neural architectures to improve generalizability of program-learning networks.
- Implementation: Incorporated recursion into the Neural Programmer-Interpreter framework.
- Evaluation: Tested on four tasks (grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, quicksort).
- Results: Learned recursive programs achieved perfect generalization with small amounts of training data.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends accepting the paper.
- Problem focus: Addresses poor generalizability of neural program learners in a specific, well-defined way.
- Motivation: Approach is well-motivated and grounded in programming languages and CS theory; recursion is a natural solution.
- Evidence: Strong experimental support and thorough analysis back the authors’ claims.
- Writing: Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.
- Novelty: Use of recursion in neural architectures is novel and discussed thoroughly (benefits and challenges).
- Limitation noted: More results on additional tasks/datasets would strengthen claims of generalizability.
- Suggestion: Paper could better discuss limitations and future research directions.
- Question: Request for details on construction and coverage of verification sets (base cases and reduction rules).
- Question: Ask how the approach will extend to more complex tasks/datasets and what challenges/limitations may arise.
- Question: Request information on computational resources and how training/evaluation cost compares to other approaches.
