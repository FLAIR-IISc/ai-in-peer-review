- Proposes using recursion as an abstraction to make neural program learners generalize and enable provable guarantees.  
- Demonstrates the approach on four tasks: grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.  
- Recommends accept: paper tackles a well-defined generalization problem in neural programming with a clear motivation.  
- Recommends accept: using recursion is novel, well-grounded in programming-language theory, and well-integrated into prior work.  
- Experimental results reportedly achieve perfect generalization across the tested tasks.  
- Provides a verification procedure to prove correctness of the learned programs.  
- Minor issue: paper lacks sufficient detail about the neural programming architecture (e.g., LSTM controller, domain-specific encoders).  
- Minor issue: paper lacks details on the training procedure and hyperparameters (optimizer, learning rate, batch size).  
- Minor issue: verification procedure needs more explanation (how verification set is constructed and how verification is performed).  
- Suggestion: discuss limitations of the proposed approach and potential future research directions, including improving verification.  
- Question for authors: please provide architecture specifics for the LSTM controller and domain encoders.  
- Question for authors: please provide training details and hyperparameters (optimization algorithm, learning rate, batch size).  
- Question for authors: please clarify the construction of the verification set and the verification methodology.  
- Question for authors: what are the method’s limitations and how might the verification procedure be improved?
