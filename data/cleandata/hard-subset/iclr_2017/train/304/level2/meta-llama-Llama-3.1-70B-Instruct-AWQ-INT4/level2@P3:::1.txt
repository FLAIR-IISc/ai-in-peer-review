**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach to improve the generalizability of neural networks that learn programs from data. The authors introduce the concept of recursion, a fundamental concept in programming languages, to neural architectures. They demonstrate that by incorporating recursion, neural networks can learn more robust and interpretable programs that generalize well to unseen inputs. The authors apply this approach to the Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) framework and show that it achieves state-of-the-art results on four tasks: grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.
