The authors of this paper propose a novel approach to improve the generalizability of neural networks that learn programs from data. They introduce the concept of recursion into neural architectures, which enables the network to learn the right program and generalize to unknown situations. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach by incorporating recursion into the Neural Programmer-Interpreter framework and evaluating it on four tasks: grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort. They show that the learned recursive programs achieve perfect generalization with small amounts of training data.

I recommend accepting this paper. The key reasons for this choice are:

1. The paper tackles a specific and well-defined problem in the field of neural programming, which is the poor generalizability of neural networks that learn programs from data.
2. The approach proposed by the authors is well-motivated and grounded in the literature on programming languages and computer science. The use of recursion is a natural and elegant solution to the problem of generalizability.

The paper provides strong evidence to support the claims made by the authors. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in achieving perfect generalization on four tasks. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the results and discuss the implications of their findings.
