- Claim: Introduces recursion in neural architectures to improve generalizability and interpretability, applied to NPI on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort, claiming perfect generalization and superior results.
- Decision: Reviewer recommends accepting the paper.
- Reason (novelty): Recursion as an abstraction for neural programs is a novel and significant contribution to generalization.
- Reason (empirics): Strong empirical evidence across four tasks showing perfect generalization and outperforming prior work.
- Support: Clear and well-motivated explanation of the generalization problem and how recursion helps.
- Support: Detailed description of the approach and modifications to the NPI framework to incorporate recursion.
- Support: Thorough and well-presented experimental results across multiple tasks.
- Support: Clear explanation of the verification procedure used to prove perfect generalization.
- Feedback: Paper needs a more detailed discussion of limitations and potential future work, especially verification scalability to large input domains.
- Feedback: Paper should include a more detailed comparison with other neural programming approaches (e.g., curriculum learning, other abstractions).
- Feedback: More implementation details are requested (specific NPI modifications and training procedure).
- Question: Please provide more details on the implementation and training of the recursive neural programs.
- Question: How will the authors address verification infeasibility for tasks with very large input domains, and can they compare to other approaches in more depth?
