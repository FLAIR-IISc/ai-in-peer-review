- Decision: Accept.

- Core idea: Introduce recursion into neural program synthesis to address poor generalization.

- Empirical results: Strong performance on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort with consistent perfect generalization vs non-recursive baselines.

- Provable generalization: Presents a verification procedure enabling formal proofs of generalization — a major novelty.

- Tasks selection: Chosen tasks cover a useful range of algorithmic complexity.

- Implementation: Reimplemented NPI and successfully integrated recursion, demonstrating solid engineering and understanding.

- Limitation — scalability: Needs deeper discussion on how the verification procedure scales to tasks with very large state spaces.

- Limitation — hyperparameters: Missing analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and whether tuning was biased toward recursive models.

- Limitation — ablation: Would benefit from a systematic ablation study varying levels of recursion (e.g., partial recursion) across tasks.

- Reproducibility — trace generation: Recursive trace generation needs clearer description (algorithm/pseudocode) and explicit connection to Section 2.2.

- Presentation: Some formatting inconsistencies (equation numbering and figure referencing) should be fixed.

- Question — verification cost: What is the computational cost of verification as task complexity and size of V increase?

- Question — hyperparameter sweeps: How sensitive are results to hyperparameter choices; were sweeps performed?

- Question — training methods: What alternative training procedures beyond explicit execution traces are envisioned for learning recursive neural programs?

- Question — quicksort encoder: Why was the quicksort domain-specific encoder modified for verification and how did that affect learning?
