- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Central claim: Incorporating recursion into neural program synthesis reduces learning complexity and enables provable generalization.
- Contributions: Augments NPI with recursion and demonstrates improved generalization on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.
- Empirical results: Recursive models show superior generalization versus non-recursive baselines.
- Verification: Presents a procedure to prove perfect generalization under certain conditions.
- Strength: Strong motivation and connection to programming-language concepts (recursion, base cases, reduction rules).
- Reproducibility: Implementation details are sufficiently clear and include effective ablation studies.
- Weakness — verification clarity: Explanation of the verification procedure—especially construction of verification set V—needs more concrete examples.
- Weakness — limitations: Lack of thorough discussion on scalability and applicability to complex or continuous state spaces.
- Weakness — analysis: Paper does not fully analyze why recursion improves generalization; learned representations are not compared in detail.
- Weakness — partial recursion: The “partially recursive” bubble sort result needs clearer explanation of what it lacks versus full recursion.
- Suggestion: Clarify assumptions behind “provable generalization” (e.g., input constraints) and expand on limitations and representation analyses.
