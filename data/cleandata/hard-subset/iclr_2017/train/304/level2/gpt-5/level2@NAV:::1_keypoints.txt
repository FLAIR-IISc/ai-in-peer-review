- Paper claims adding explicit recursion to neural program architectures improves generalization, interpretability, and enables provable perfect generalization.  
- Method: modify NPI training traces to induce recursion and evaluate on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.  
- Introduces a verification procedure that enumerates base cases and reduction rules to build a finite verification set V; matching V implies perfect generalization to reference program P.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Novelty—explicit recursion is a principled architectural/training abstraction to enforce program structure and reduce length-based overfitting.  
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical support—compelling results across diverse tasks and a reproducible verification methodology.  
- Situates well in neural programming literature, identifying spurious length dependencies and opaque hidden-state issues.  
- Recursive reformulation (including tail recursion) reduces each subprogram’s input domain and resets hidden state across calls, improving learning.  
- Experiments (small-data) show full recursion achieves 100% accuracy on long inputs where non-recursive/partial-recursive models fail (e.g., bubble sort, quicksort).  
- Verification framework is a meaningful advance: analytically derived V for addition and carefully constructed V for other tasks that substantiate claims.  
- Paper is useful for researchers of neural program learners and verifiable ML.  
- Suggest clarifying formal status of the “proof”: state assumptions, soundness/completeness of V construction, and possible failure modes.  
- Request code and exact verification sets plus quantification of verification coverage (e.g., counts of observation transitions).  
- Recommend ablations on hidden-state reset, tail-recursion optimization, encoder design, and sensitivity to stack depth limits.  
- Suggest comparisons to stronger non-NPI baselines (Neural GPU, DNC with curricula) and more systematic sample-efficiency reporting.  
- Ask about learning recursion with weaker supervision and applicability beyond toy algorithmic domains.  
- Questions: how to guarantee completeness of V for topological sort/quicksort and can V be bounded or automated; practical recursion-depth and stack limits; sensitivity to environment observation choices; transferability to other NPAs without full trace supervision.
