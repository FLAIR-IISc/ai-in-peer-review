- Paper claim: Explicit recursion is a key abstraction for neural program induction to generalize beyond training distributions and admit provable guarantees.  
- Method: Instantiate recursion in NPI by using recursive execution traces and resetting LSTM hidden state at each call.  
- Verification: Introduce a procedure that, given finite base cases and reduction rules V, verifies model step-outputs match a reference P on all reachable states, yielding provable generalization.  
- Decision: Weak Accept.  
- Main reasons for acceptance: (1) Clear empirical evidence recursion improves generalization on nontrivial tasks with tiny training sets; (2) Novel, generally applicable verification methodology enabled by recursion.  
- Motivation: Positions recursion as a principled programming-language abstraction that reduces state-space explosion and aids modular reasoning; prior NPI work didn’t exploit recursion or verification.  
- Technical soundness — implementation: Resetting controller state at calls prevents spurious long-range dependencies and enables modular composition.  
- Technical soundness — verification: Construct V of reachable step-input observations per subprogram and check M(i)=P(i) for all i in V to establish correctness, assuming V completeness and closed-world observations.  
- Empirical — addition: Recursive NPI trained on five 1-digit sums generalizes to 5000-digit sums; non-recursive fails on longer inputs.  
- Empirical — bubble sort: Fully recursive model achieves 100% across tested lengths; non-recursive and partial recursion degrade quickly.  
- Empirical — topological sort: Trained on 1–6 traces and generalizes to graphs up to 120 nodes; non-recursive performs poorly.  
- Empirical — quicksort: Recursive succeeds across lengths for a divide-and-conquer, non-tail-recursive algorithm; non-recursive fails at moderate sizes.  
- Significance: Verification after training improves reliability and interpretability and appears portable to other NPA-like architectures when subcall observation spaces are finite.  
- Caveat — supervision: Requires strong supervision via full recursive execution traces and domain-specific encoders exposing high-level observations, limiting applicability.  
- Caveat — scope of guarantees: Provable generalization depends on finiteness and correct construction of V; not applicable to large/continuous observation spaces.  
- Caveat — fairness of comparisons: Encoder was modified (boolean comparison) for bubble sort verification; need explicit mapping of results to encoder versions and fair baselines.  
- Caveat — reproducibility: No code or verification artifacts released; releasing V/SV and code would strengthen the contribution.  
- Suggested improvements — reporting: Clarify supervision signals and training hyperparameters; report sample complexity curves and stack depths/call counts at test time.  
- Suggested improvements — ablations: Test NPI without state reset, partial/incorrect recursion traces, and non-recursive models with identical observation interfaces.  
- Suggested improvements — tooling: Automate/formalize V construction; provide pseudo-code or tool to emit V and SV with completeness checks.  
- Suggested improvements — extensions: Apply to more algorithms (merge sort, DFS/BFS, parsing), explore mutual recursion, and analyze numerical robustness (thresholds, parameter perturbations).  
- Questions for authors: How is tail-recursion optimization handled in NPI; any explicit transformations?  
- Questions for authors: What were maximum call depths for quicksort and topological sort; any failures from deep recursion or stack overflow?  
- Questions for authors: For bubble sort, can you report comparisons where both recursive and non-recursive models use the same boolean-comparison encoder?  
- Questions for authors: How do you ensure completeness when constructing V for quicksort/topological sort — formal proof or empirical coverage?  
- Questions for authors: Did you try training from I/O pairs only with a recursion-capable architecture, and what were the results?  
- Questions for authors: How sensitive is verification to numerical deviations around decision thresholds; do you use margin-based checks?  
- Questions for authors: Will you release code and the verification sets V/SV to facilitate replication and extension?
