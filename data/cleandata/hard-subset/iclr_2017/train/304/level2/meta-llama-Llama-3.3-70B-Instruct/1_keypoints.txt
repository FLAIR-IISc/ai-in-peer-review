- Proposes incorporating recursion into neural architectures to improve program-learning generalizability.
- Argues recursion is essential for neural programs to generalize and provides provable guarantees.
- Modifies the Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) framework to support recursion.
- Evaluates the approach on four tasks: grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.
- Reports that recursive neural programs achieve perfect generalization on all tasks.
- Provides detailed analysis of base cases and reduction rules used to build verification sets.
- Constructs verification sets to prove correctness of learned programs, though construction details are limited.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explains the approach and analysis.
- Reviewer accepts the paper as a novel, well-motivated solution addressing generalization limitations.
- Suggests adding more details on verification-set construction and the proof procedure.
- Recommends discussing limitations of the approach and directions for future research.
- Requests clarifications: how verification sets were constructed, how coverage of base cases/reduction rules is ensured, limitations and remedies, and broader potential applications.
