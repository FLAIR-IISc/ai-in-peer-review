- Problem & contribution: Proposes adding explicit recursion to neural program learners to improve generalization by decomposing tasks into self-similar subproblems.  
- Implementation: Demonstrates the idea within the Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) by training on explicitly recursive execution traces.  
- Tasks & results: Evaluated on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort; recursive models achieve perfect generalization from very small training sets while non-recursive models fail as complexity grows.  
- Verification contribution: Introduces a verification procedure using base cases and reduction rules to provably guarantee perfect generalization for learned recursive programs.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Novel, well-motivated use of recursion that elegantly addresses spurious correlations and poor generalization.  
- Key reason 2: Provides a method for provable generalization, a significant step toward trustworthy neural program synthesis.  
- Motivation & approach support: Recursion plausibly prevents learning length-dependent spurious dependencies; NPI’s stack makes recursion natural; enabling recursion via trace modification is simple and effective.  
- Empirical support: Experiments are well-designed across diverse algorithmic patterns and clearly show superiority of recursion.  
- Verification significance: Recursion reduces infinite input spaces to finite verification sets, enabling formal guarantees not seen in prior work.  
- Supervision concern: Method depends on strongly supervised, explicitly recursive traces; needs discussion on effort to generate traces and whether recursion can be learned from non-recursive data.  
- Generalizability concern: Shown only on NPI; should clarify required architectural primitives (e.g., stack) and applicability to other models like DNCs or NTMs.  
- Verification set construction: Need more detail on practical complexity and manual effort for building the verification set V.  
- Scope limitation: Verification is not suitable for very large or continuous state spaces and this restriction should be emphasized.  
- Overall assessment: An excellent paper with strong theoretical and empirical backing that opens a promising research direction.
