- Proposes teaching recursion explicitly to neural program learners to improve generalization and enable formal verification.  
- Adapts the Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) to learn recursive programs for addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort using recursive execution traces.  
- Empirical results show recursive models achieve perfect generalization from very small training sets and outperform non-recursive models.  
- Key contribution: a method to provide provable guarantees of perfect generalization by verifying base cases and reduction rules with a finite verification set.  
- Decision: Accept — paper is strong, with two main contributions: recursion for generalization and tractable formal verification.  
- Strength: Insightful connection between recursion and eliminating spurious input dependencies, clearly motivated and presented.  
- Strength: Ablation-like experiments (e.g., partially recursive bubble sort) support that learning correct recursive structure is essential.  
- Novelty: Verification procedure reduces an infinite verification problem to a finite set, enabling formal proof for learned neural programs.  
- Weakness: Requires strong supervision in the form of explicit recursive execution traces, so the model is taught recursion rather than discovering it.  
- Weakness: Constructing the verification set V is manual and task-specific, raising concerns about scalability and generality.  
- Suggestion: Frame the method explicitly as imposing a strong inductive bias via recursive traces to explain data efficiency and generalization.  
- Clarification requested: whether the bubble sort results used the original encoder or the modified encoder introduced for verification.  
- Clarification requested: whether the verification guards against the model learning a different-but-correct recursive algorithm than the traced reference.  
- Questions: How difficult is constructing SV for quicksort in practice, and can this be automated from a formal specification?  
- Questions: Any experiments on learning recursion from weaker supervision (e.g., non-recursive traces with allowed self-calls) or details on the failure mode for non-recursive addition (carry handling vs. length confusion)?
