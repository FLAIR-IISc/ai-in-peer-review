- Proposes incorporating recursion into neural program synthesis to learn true program semantics and generalize to arbitrary input complexity.  
- Modifies the Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) training to learn recursive solutions.  
- Evaluated on four tasks: addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.  
- Key contributions: achieving perfect generalization from very few training examples using recursion.  
- Key contributions: introducing a verification method that provides provable guarantees of generalization.  
- Strong accept recommendation based on (1) the elegant, impactful idea of recursion for provable generalization.  
- Strong accept recommendation based on (2) robust empirical and theoretical evidence supporting the claims.  
- Well-motivated: addresses spurious correlations (e.g., input length) that hinder existing models.  
- Recursion decomposes problems into finite base cases and reduction rules, naturally fitting the solution.  
- Experiments: recursive models attain 100% accuracy on complex unseen inputs while non-recursive baselines fail.  
- Verification procedure is a standout contribution, raising rigor beyond standard test-set evaluation.  
- Suggested improvement: discuss supervision requirements and explore learning recursive structures from less supervision (e.g., I/O pairs or non-recursive traces).  
- Question 1: How complex is constructing the verification set V for a new task, and can it be automated?  
- Question 2: How robust is learning to variations, inefficiencies, or noise in the training traces?
