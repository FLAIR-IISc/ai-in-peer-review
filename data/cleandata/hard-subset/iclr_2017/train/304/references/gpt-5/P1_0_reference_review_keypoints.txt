- Paper proposes using explicit recursion in NPI traces to improve generalization of neural program executors on algorithmic tasks.
- Authors introduce a verification procedure that reduces generalization for recursive programs to checking a finite set V of step-input sequences against a reference program P.
- Empirically, recursive variants achieve 100% generalization on long inputs for addition, bubble sort (full recursion), topological sort, and quicksort with few training traces; non-/partially-recursive variants fail on larger sizes.
- Decision: Reject.
- Main reason 1: Contribution is mainly a trace/supervision reformulation of NPI (which already supports recursion), not a new model or method that induces recursion.
- Main reason 2: “Provably perfect generalization” depends on hand-crafted, task-specific V sets and encodings; completeness of V is not generally guaranteed.
- Motivation/placement: Recursion is well-motivated and natural for NPI, but the paper overstates novelty by presenting recursion as an architectural contribution rather than a supervision/curriculum design.
- Empirical support: Results are compelling within NPI but limited to toy benchmarks with full trace supervision and lack robustness tests (no noisy/partial-trace or IO-only settings).
- Empirical support: Baselines are limited to non-/partially-recursive NPI variants; no comparisons to other algorithmic models or stronger NPI curricula.
- Empirical support: Bubble sort verification uses a modified encoder exposing pairwise comparisons, simplifying verification and potentially inflating claims.
- Empirical support: Test sizes are modest (30 instances per length); broader stress/adversarial testing is missing.
- Verification/proofs: The theorem reducing correctness to matching P on V is insightful but hinges on accurately constructing V.
- Verification/proofs: Construction of V is non-trivial—analytic for addition but heuristic/random coverage for other tasks—so guarantees are contingent on completeness of V.
- Verification/proofs: Verification checks agreement with a specific reference program P, not necessarily satisfaction of a task-level spec (e.g., topological sort’s non-uniqueness).
- Verification/proofs: Method relies on hand-designed program sets, argument spaces, and encoders to keep V finite, limiting generality.
- Constructive feedback: Temper claims—frame guarantees as task- and encoding-dependent and contingent on a complete V; distinguish agreement-with-P from spec-level correctness.
- Constructive feedback: Provide a formal, general procedure or conditions to construct a complete V and prove finiteness/compute-ability, or automate V construction.
- Constructive feedback: Broaden evaluation—compare to other neural algorithm learners, stronger NPI curricula; report seed stability, success rates, sensitivity to noisy/partial traces.
- Constructive feedback: Quantify V sizes, verification-set sizes, verification runtime, and maximum recursion depth handled.
- Constructive feedback: Explore reducing supervision—IO-only learning or discovering recursion from partial/non-recursive traces; ablate necessary supervision signals.
- Constructive feedback: Avoid environment/encoder tweaks for verification unless also evaluated in the original setting and clearly separate those results.
- Constructive feedback: Release code and the verification harness.
- Question 1: How do you formally establish completeness of V for topological sort and quicksort—can you provide constructions or proofs?
- Question 2: What are the exact sizes of V and the verification sets for each task, and how long does verification take?
- Question 3: How robust is training to random initialization and small perturbations in traces—what are success rates across seeds?
- Question 4: For topological sort, does verification ensure agreement with a fixed DFS variant or correctness w.r.t. any valid order; can you verify relational specs instead of a fixed P?
- Question 5: What are results and V size for bubble sort verification without the modified encoder?
- Question 6: To what extent can the model discover recursive calls from non-recursive traces or IO-only supervision?
- Question 7: What practical limits exist on recursion depth/stack size, and how does tail-recursion optimization manifest in your NPI implementation?
