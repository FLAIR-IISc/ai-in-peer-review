- Paper claim: Explicit recursion in neural program induction enables robust generalization and finite verification.
- Method: Insert recursive calls into NPI training traces so subproblems run with fresh context and bounded step horizons.
- Verification: Proposes reducing correctness on arbitrary inputs to checking a finite set of base cases and reduction rules for recursive programs.
- Empirical result: Recursive NPI shows large out-of-distribution gains on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort with very small training sets.
- Verification evidence: Provides verification sets (e.g., addition: 20,181; topological sort: 73 graphs; quicksort: one 10-element array) showing learned programs match reference step outputs.
- Decision: Weak Accept — idea is simple, well-motivated, and yields strong empirical and conceptual contributions despite heavy supervision.
- Motivation point: Identifies length/scale-dependent generalization failures and argues recursion fits NPI due to call stack and hidden-state reset.
- Novelty: Explicit recursion plus a verification methodology is novel relative to prior algorithm-learning models.
- Technical contribution: Supervision-trace modification to create recursive calls yields bounded per-call horizons and makes full verification plausible.
- Empirical support detail: Recursive variants often achieve perfect or substantially improved generalization where iterative baselines fail, sometimes from only 4–6 traces.
- Limitation: Verification depends on constructing a complete set V of reachable step inputs; completeness is analytic for addition but heuristic for other tasks.
- Scope restriction: Approach requires small finite observation sets and heavy supervised traces/program sets, limiting applicability.
- Clarify verification pipeline: Need a precise, reproducible algorithm for building V and proofs of completeness for tasks beyond addition.
- Strengthen experiments: Request full accuracy-vs-size plots, per-call step counts, ablations (partial recursion, LSTM size, hidden-state reset), and failure cases.
- Baselines: Recommend comparisons to non-NPI baselines (Neural GPU, DNC) or discussion of adding recursion to them.
- Supervision engineering: Ask for explicit accounting of domain engineering and experiments on learning recursion from partial/non-recursive traces.
- Code request: Urges release of Keras NPI reimplementation and verification tooling to increase impact and credibility.
- Question on V completeness: How is V guaranteed complete for topological sort and quicksort; what stopping criterion or invariants are used?
- Question on encoder sensitivity: How dependent are verification/generalization on encoder choices (e.g., bubble sort predicate exposure)?
- Question on robustness: How do learned controllers handle small distribution shifts or expanded observation vocabularies?
- Question on trace supervision: Can method work with partial or iterative traces, or discover recursion during/after training?
- Question on recursion depth: Are there stack depth issues at inference and was tail-call optimization implemented in NPI?
- Question on generality: Can the verification approach extend to perceptual/continuous inputs where base cases/reductions are large or continuous?
