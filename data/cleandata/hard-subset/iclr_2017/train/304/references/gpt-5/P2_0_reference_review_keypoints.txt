- Paper adds explicit recursion via recursive execution traces to NPI and claims improved out-of-distribution generalization and provable perfect generalization via finite verification sets.
- Decision: Reject.
- Main critique: The “provably perfect generalization” claim is not rigorously supported due to reliance on hand-crafted observation abstractions, reference semantics, and ad hoc coverage procedures.
- Technical novelty is limited: NPI already supports recursion; contribution mainly consists of providing recursive traces and a verification workflow under heavy supervision.
- Verification theorem reduces generalization to covering base cases and reductions, but completeness of the verification set V is not formally established for most tasks.
- For addition the verification is reasonable; for bubble sort the encoder was altered to expose comparisons, changing the task to make verification tractable.
- For topological sort and quicksort V is built via “careful reasoning” plus random generation, lacking a formal completeness argument and therefore resembling conformance testing more than formal proof.
- Empirics show strong generalization for recursive variants, but evaluations use limited baselines, small test samples per length, and some domain-specific encoder changes that confound comparisons.
- The “perfect generalization” claim is not empirically exhaustive and rests on the unproven soundness/completeness of designer-chosen abstractions.
- Scope limited: verification does not scale to large or continuous observation spaces and requires heavy supervision (full execution traces).
- Suggested improvement: formalize the verification framework, precisely define observation abstractions, and provide a proof or mechanized procedure for constructing complete V for at least one nontrivial task.
- Suggested improvement: separate and strengthen the empirical demonstration (recursion helps) and the verification methodology with cleaner controls and stronger baselines.
- Suggested improvement: report ablations on hidden-state reset, recursion depth, tail vs non-tail recursion, training trace size, and robustness to noisy traces.
- Suggested improvement: explore reducing supervision by learning recursion from partial traces or I/O only, and release code/traces and verification tooling for reproducibility.
- Key questions for authors: how is completeness of V guaranteed for topological sort and quicksort; can an exact algorithm construct V from P and the abstraction?
- Key question: how sensitive are verification guarantees to the choice of observation abstraction, and can different abstractions yield different V and conclusions?
- Key question: in bubble sort, can the original encoder be verified without exposing comparisons?
- Key question: were stronger non-recursive baselines (curriculum, more traces, other architectures) tried, and how do results change?
- Key question: can the model learn recursion without explicit recursive traces (from non-recursive traces or I/O)?
- Key question: are verification sets and code available to substantiate claims?
