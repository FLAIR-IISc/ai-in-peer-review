- Tackles neural program synthesis generalization by explicitly adding recursion to NPI and verifies provable generalization on four tasks (addition, bubble sort, topological sort, quicksort) with 100% accuracy.  
- Decision: Accept — compelling argument, strong empirical results, and a novel verification procedure enabling provable generalization.  
- Clear problem motivation: recursion addresses spurious correlations and lack of formal guarantees in prior work.  
- Novel contribution: explicit incorporation of recursion and formal verification in neural program synthesis.  
- Strong empirical evidence: perfect accuracy on diverse tasks after limited training, and comparison highlights original NPI limitations.  
- Provable generalization: verification via checking base cases and reductions is a major advance.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written and explanations of NPI and recursive modifications are clear.  
- Connect to broader program induction: paper should more explicitly relate to inductive program synthesis, version space algebra, and related concepts.  
- Scalability concern: verification does not scale to very large state spaces; explore abstraction or compositional verification.  
- Training data size: results are based on relatively small training sets — test sensitivity to dataset size and diversity.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: discuss how performance depends on hyperparameter choices.  
- Verification set completeness: clarify how randomly generated verification candidates ensure coverage and completeness.  
- Alternative architectures: have recursive mechanisms been tested in architectures other than NPI, and what challenges arise?  
- Future work and verification alternatives: elaborate on training without execution traces and consider other verification techniques (e.g., model checking, abstract interpretation).
