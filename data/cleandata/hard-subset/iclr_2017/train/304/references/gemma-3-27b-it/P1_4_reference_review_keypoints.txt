- Tackles generalization in neural program learning by introducing recursion to overcome spurious correlations and enable provable generalization.
- Integrates recursion into the NPI architecture and demonstrates empirical perfect generalization on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort from small training sets.
- Provides a formal verification procedure and verification set construction to prove generalization guarantees.
- Decision: Accept — significant and compelling contribution with unique formal verification aspect.
- Clear problem statement and motivation, with insightful analysis of why existing methods fail.
- Novelty: explicit use of recursion within NPI plus provable generalization is a notable advance despite NPI not being new.
- Strong empirical results showing consistent perfect generalization compared to non-recursive baselines.
- Rigorous and well-explained verification procedure, with acknowledgement of scalability limitations.
- Paper is well-written and logically organized.
- Suggests adding discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity to assess robustness.
- Recommends more detailed ablation studies, including partial recursion effects, to isolate contributors to improvement.
- Ask for discussion of computational cost of training and verification.
- Recommend discussing limitations and challenges when extending to more complex tasks or data structures.
- Request code release to improve reproducibility and impact.
- Questions: how to scale or approximate verification for large state spaces (e.g., abstractions or focused verification)?
- Questions: dependency on quality of constructed recursive training traces and potential for the model to learn recursion autonomously.
- Questions: whether recursion benefits generalize beyond NPI to other neural programming architectures with different memory mechanisms.
- Questions: impact of LSTM size (e.g., variations from the used 2-layer LSTM) on performance and provable generalization.
