- Paper proposes incorporating recursion into neural program architectures to improve generalization and enable provable guarantees.
- Implemented recursion in the Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) and evaluated on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.
- Empirical results show significantly improved generalization, achieving 100% accuracy on test inputs.
- Formal verification framework provided, allowing proofs of perfect generalization given appropriate verification sets.
- Reviewer decision: Accept with enthusiasm.
- Novelty: Using recursion to enforce semantic understanding and enable provable generalization is original and significant.
- Strong empirical evidence across multiple tasks, including scalable tasks like topological sort and quicksort.
- Formal verification is a major strength and a landmark contribution for trustworthy neural program synthesis.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains integration of recursion into NPI.
- Suggest discussing applicability of the recursive approach to other architectures (e.g., DNCs, NTMs) and potential adaptation challenges.
- Recommend additional ablation studies to isolate benefits of specific recursive components and training trace modifications.
- Ask for more detail on the complexity and construction of verification sets, especially for large input spaces.
- Recommend expanding discussion of limitations, future work (e.g., learning recursion structures), and verification scalability.
- Request evaluation of computational cost trade-offs introduced by recursion during training and inference.
- Questions for authors: why NPI was chosen, how verification set completeness was ensured, bottlenecks and strategies for scaling verification, direct comparison to non-recursive NPI, and sensitivity to training-data/traces generation methods.
