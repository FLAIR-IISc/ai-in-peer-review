- Paper tackles generalization in neural program learning by integrating recursion into neural architectures to reduce spurious correlations and complexity.  
- Authors modify Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) to support recursion and empirically achieve perfect generalization on sorting, addition, and graph traversal.  
- Work includes provable correctness via a well-defined verification procedure, a rare contribution in neural program learning.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions due to strong results and formal guarantees.  
- Strong motivation: addresses a well-known generalization problem and logically motivates recursion based on program design principles.  
- Novelty & significance: explicitly applying recursion within NPI and providing verification is novel and important.  
- Empirical results: recursive models reach 100% test accuracy while non-recursive models fail on the same tasks.  
- Clear explanation: implementation of recursion in NPI and the verification process are described thoroughly.  
- Verification procedure: well-defined and bridges empirical performance with formal guarantees, with acknowledgement of limitations for large state spaces.  
- Related work: related work section should be expanded to include program synthesis, inductive programming, neural-symbolic methods, and formal methods.  
- Scalability & cost: discuss computational cost and scalability of training recursive programs versus non-recursive counterparts.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: report on hyperparameter sweeps or sensitivity (learning rate, LSTM size) to strengthen empirical claims.  
- Ablation studies: include targeted ablations (e.g., partial vs. full recursion) to isolate the impact of recursion.  
- Generalization beyond verification set: discuss limits of verification and risks of failing outside the verified space.  
- Verification scalability question: consider techniques like abstract interpretation or compositional reasoning to scale verification.  
- Role of training traces: clarify sensitivity to recursive training trace construction and whether curricula can discover recursion from non-recursive traces.  
- Alternative architectures: comment on suitability of other neural program architectures for recursion beyond NPI.  
- Connection to formal languages: explore whether learned recursive programs map to traditional programming languages and can be translated automatically.  
- Generalization to other tasks: test or discuss applicability of recursive approach to tasks beyond sorting, addition, and graph traversal and identify problem types that benefit most.
