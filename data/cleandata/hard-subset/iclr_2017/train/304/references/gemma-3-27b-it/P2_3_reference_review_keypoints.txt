- Summary: Proposes adding recursion to neural programming (NPI) to improve generalization and provides formal proofs on four tasks (addition, bubble sort, topological sort, quicksort).  
- Decision: Recommend accept with minor revisions for its novelty, strong results, and provable guarantees.  
- Novelty & motivation: Leveraging recursion to avoid spurious dependencies and enable provable generalization is novel and well-argued.  
- Strong empirical results: Achieves 100% accuracy on evaluated tasks and shows clear improvement over previous NPI baselines.  
- Provable generalization: Introduces a verification procedure that convincingly proves correctness across valid inputs, with recursion simplifying verification.  
- Clear presentation: Paper is well-written with helpful task and program set descriptions.  
- Scalability of verification: Verification method may not scale to very large or infinite domains and needs discussion of alternatives.  
- Generalization to other architectures: Should discuss how the recursive approach could transfer beyond NPI to other neural programming frameworks.  
- Ablation study: Recommend finer-grained ablations on levels of recursion (e.g., partial vs. full recursion) to clarify when full recursion is necessary.  
- Discussion of limitations: Paper should more thoroughly address tasks or program types where recursion might not help or could be hard to learn.  
- Question — verification scalability: How can the method extend to extremely large/infinite state spaces (e.g., via abstraction or symbolic methods)?  
- Question — hyperparameter sensitivity: How sensitive are results to hyperparameters and what hyperparameter tuning was done?  
- Question — generalization to novel program structures: How well would the approach discover new program structures, and would curriculum learning help?  
- Question — tail recursion optimization: Was tail recursion optimization implemented and did it affect learning or performance?  
- Overall: High-quality contribution combining strong empirical performance with formal verification; suggested minor improvements to broaden impact and address scalability/limitations.
