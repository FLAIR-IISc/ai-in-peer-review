- Problem: Addresses poor generalization in neural program learning due to spurious dependencies and proposes recursion as a solution.
- Proposal: Integrates recursion into Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) to enable better generalization and provable guarantees.
- Results: Recursive NPI achieves perfect generalization on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort with formal verification.
- Decision: Recommend accept with minor revisions.
- Motivation: Clearly articulates the generalization challenge and justifies recursion as a core abstraction.
- Novelty & significance: Introducing recursion for provable generalization is novel and moves the field toward more reliable AI.
- Empirical strength: Strong, consistent empirical results across multiple tasks using small training sets.
- Verification strength: Provides a rigorous verification procedure and shows how recursion simplifies verification.
- Writing: Paper is well-written and logically structured.
- Related work: Needs expanded discussion of program synthesis, inductive programming, formal methods, and interaction with curriculum learning.
- Ablations: More focused ablation studies needed to isolate contributions of different recursive components (e.g., partial vs. full recursion, tail-call effects).
- Scalability: Discuss strategies for scaling verification to large domains (abstraction, sampling) since current verification has limits.
- Training details: Clarify modifications to the training procedure, including trace generation and any loss/regularization specifics.
- Reproducibility: Recommend releasing code to improve reproducibility and impact.
- Questions for authors: Clarify analytic vs. empirical verification set construction, trace generation process and sensitivity, verification runtime and scaling behavior, applicability to non-stack architectures, and robustness to noisy inputs.
