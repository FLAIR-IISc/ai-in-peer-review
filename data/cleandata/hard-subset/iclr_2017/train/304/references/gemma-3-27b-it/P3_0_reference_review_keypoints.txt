- Tackles generalization failures in neural program learning and attributes them to spurious dependencies in training data.  
- Proposes incorporating recursion into neural architectures to improve generalization and enable provable correctness.  
- Implements recursion within the Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) on four tasks: addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.  
- Achieves perfect generalization (100% accuracy) on all valid inputs across those tasks.  
- Introduces a novel verification procedure based on covering base cases and reduction rules to prove correctness.  
- Decision: Accept — recursion plus verification is a strong, convincing contribution to neural program learning.  
- Clear problem motivation and well-argued rationale linking recursion to program semantics and generalization.  
- Novelty: applying recursion specifically to obtain provable generalization in neural program learners.  
- Strong empirical evidence showing recursive models outperform non-recursive baselines in generalization.  
- Rigorous verification is a major strength — the authors don’t just claim but prove generalization within the covered domain.  
- Methodology is well explained, including how recursion was induced in NPI and how training traces were constructed.  
- Concern about scalability: the verification procedure may not scale to tasks with large or complex state spaces.  
- Suggest exploring approximate verification techniques and discussing practical limitations for complex programs.  
- Recommend investigating alternative training methods (regularization or architectural constraints) to induce recursion beyond trace modification.  
- Request more ablation studies to isolate which recursive components drive performance gains.  
- Suggest explicit comparisons to curriculum learning approaches that use structural/task information.  
- Encourage releasing code to improve reproducibility and accelerate follow-up research.  
- Ask for clarification on verification computational cost and practical limits as problem size grows.  
- Ask for training details: effective techniques, hyperparameter sensitivity, and stability for recursive NPI models.  
- Question the choice of NPI and whether other neural programming architectures might be better suited for recursion.  
- Ask about robustness beyond the verification set: handling unexpected edge cases or inputs dissimilar to training/verification data.
