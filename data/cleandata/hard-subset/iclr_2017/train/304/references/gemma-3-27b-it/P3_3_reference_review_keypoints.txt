- Paper addresses neural program generalization by incorporating recursion to avoid spurious dependencies.  
- Implements recursion in Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) across addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.  
- Achieves perfect generalization on unseen inputs for the chosen tasks.  
- Provides formal verification procedures that give provable guarantees of generalization.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Strong motivation: clearly articulates limits of current neural program learning and role of spurious correlations.  
- Novelty & significance: framing recursion as a core abstraction for generalization and coupling it with verification is novel and important.  
- Empirical results: consistent superior performance of recursive NPI models (100% accuracy on unseen data).  
- Formal verification: the ability to prove generalization is the paper’s most compelling contribution.  
- Clear presentation: well-written with helpful examples and algorithms.  
- Related work: needs deeper discussion connecting to program synthesis, automated theorem proving, and formal verification literature.  
- Scalability of verification: current procedure limited for very large state spaces; alternatives (model checking, abstract interpretation) should be discussed.  
- Reproducibility: more detail needed on training data generation and hyperparameters.  
- Ablation study: examine partial recursion effects more thoroughly across tasks.  
- Generalization to other architectures: discuss applicability and challenges beyond NPI (e.g., differentiable neural computers).  
- Key questions: practical scaling/automation of verification set construction, alternative training methods (RL, meta-learning), identifying specific spurious correlations, and robustness to noisy/malformed inputs.
