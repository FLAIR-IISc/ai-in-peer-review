- Paper addresses poor generalization in neural programming by incorporating recursion into architectures to capture true program semantics rather than spurious training correlations.
- Main contribution: show recursion enables provably perfect generalization for certain tasks and implement this in NPI with empirical success on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Reason for acceptance: novel, well-motivated solution to an important problem.
- Reason for acceptance: strong empirical results showing recursive models generalize where non-recursive models fail.
- Reason for acceptance: proposes a sound verification procedure enabling formal guarantees of generalization.
- Supporting argument: clear motivation and literature positioning linking recursion to improved generalization.
- Supporting argument: compelling empirical demonstrations (100% accuracy on test inputs after small training).
- Supporting argument: theoretical justification that recursion reduces verification from infinite to finite by using base cases and reduction rules.
- Supporting argument: novelty in explicitly using recursion in neural programming to obtain provable guarantees.
- Feedback: clarify the exact scope and constraints of “provably perfect generalization” and what counts as a valid input per task.
- Feedback: more clearly explain why and how NPI “naturally supports recursion.”
- Feedback: discuss practical limitations and conditions where verification is or isn’t feasible.
- Feedback: provide concrete analysis showing why recursion prevents learning spurious dependencies (e.g., behavior across input lengths).
- Feedback: detail the verification set construction, especially how base cases and reductions are comprehensively covered for complex tasks.
- Question: how do you ensure the verification set covers all reduction cases, particularly for complex reductions like quicksort’s?
- Question: how general is the bubble sort encoder modification and would the approach work with the original NPI encoder?
- Question: provide precise comparisons to prior work’s generalization performance on the same tasks.
