- Paper proposes adding explicit recursion to NPI-style models to achieve “provably perfect generalization” and shows 100% accuracy on four recursive tasks (addition, bubble sort, topological sort, quicksort).  
- Authors modify training traces to include recursive structure and provide a verification procedure over base cases and reduction rules to claim formal generalization.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Major rejection reason: “Provably perfect generalization” is overstated and only applies to problems with small, finite sets of base cases and reduction rules.  
- Major rejection reason: Approach needs explicit recursive traces during training, i.e., strong supervision, so the model is not discovering recursion autonomously.  
- Limitation: Demonstrations are limited to four tasks with natural recursive structure and do not show applicability to non-recursive or real-world tasks (images, speech, etc.).  
- Limitation: Verification procedure is essentially manual and task-specific, not a general automated verification method.  
- Comparison gap: Paper fails to compare against other generalization-focused neural programming methods (e.g., Neural GPU, Neural Programmer).  
- Requested clarification: Explicitly state and qualify the scope of the “provably perfect generalization” claim.  
- Requested clarification: Add a dedicated discussion of limitations, especially for tasks without natural recursive decomposition.  
- Suggested experiments: Study learning recursion with less supervision or from non-recursive/partial traces to strengthen claims.  
- Suggested comparisons: Evaluate against prior work that targets generalization (Neural GPU, Neural Programmer, etc.).  
- Clarify relation to formal verification literature and how this differs from existing neural-network verification approaches.  
- Key questions for authors: How to apply the method to non-recursive tasks or force recursive formulations for them?  
- Key questions for authors: Precisely characterize the class of problems amenable to finite verification sets.  
- Key questions for authors: How does this approach differ from formal verification in safety-critical systems?  
- Key questions for authors: How to handle tasks with infinite or very large base-case spaces (e.g., sorting arbitrary-length arrays)?  
- Key questions for authors: Would non-recursive models trained on much larger datasets learn recursion and generalize without recursive traces?
