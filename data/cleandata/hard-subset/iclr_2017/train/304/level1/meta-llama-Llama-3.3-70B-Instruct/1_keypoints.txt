- Proposes incorporating recursion into neural architectures to improve generalizability of program-learning networks.  
- Implements recursion within the Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) framework.  
- Evaluates on four tasks: grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.  
- Learned recursive programs show superior generalizability and interpretability with small training sets.  
- Provides a verification procedure that can prove learned recursive programs generalize perfectly.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Key reason: tackles the important problem of poor generalizability in neural program learning.  
- Key reason: approach is well-motivated, grounded in literature, and clearly explains how recursion helps.  
- Key reason: strong empirical evidence across the four tasks supports the claims.  
- Supporting point: recursion reduces program complexity and enhances interpretability.  
- Supporting point: the verification procedure is a significant contribution.  
- Suggested improvement: add more implementation details of the recursion mechanism in NPI.  
- Suggested improvement: provide analysis of computational complexity and scalability to larger programs.  
- Suggested improvement: include more experiments on additional tasks and datasets.  
- Questions for authors: detail the recursion implementation in NPI.  
- Questions for authors: how will the approach extend to more complex programs and larger datasets?  
- Questions for authors: provide more analysis on computational complexity and scalability.
