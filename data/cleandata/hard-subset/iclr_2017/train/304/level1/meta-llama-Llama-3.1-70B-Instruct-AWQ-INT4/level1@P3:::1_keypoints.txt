- Paper proposes adding recursion to neural architectures (via NPI) to improve generalization and interpretability, demonstrated on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.
- Decision: Accept.
- Well-motivated: recursion naturally decomposes problems, aiding reasoning about learned programs.
- Experiments: learned recursive programs achieve perfect generalization from few examples across four tasks, with a verification procedure to prove correctness.
- Significant contribution: introduces a new approach for learning recursive neural programs that improves interpretability and generalization.
- Clarity/organization: paper is well-written but the verification procedure section needs more detail.
- Related work: literature review should be broadened to better position the contribution among existing neural programming and recursion work.
- Future work: authors suggest exploring new training methods and architectures with built-in recursion; more detail on these directions would be helpful.
- Question: request for more detail on how the verification procedure constructs the set V of valid step-input sequences and performs verification.
- Question: how will the verification set be constructed to cover large input domains (e.g., MNIST or speech) where exhaustive coverage is infeasible?
- Question: provide details on practical use, deployment, and real-world performance of the learned recursive programs.
