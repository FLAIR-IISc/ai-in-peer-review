**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

This paper proposes a novel approach to improve the generalization of neural programming architectures by incorporating recursion. The authors argue that recursion is an essential element for neural programs to generalize and provides a way to prove guarantees about the behavior of these programs. They demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach by applying it to the Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) framework on four tasks: grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort. The authors claim that their recursive neural programs achieve perfect generalization from a small number of training examples and provide a verification procedure to prove the correctness of their approach.

**Decision: Accept**

I recommend accepting this paper because it presents a novel and well-motivated approach to improving the generalization of neural programming architectures. The authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of their method, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.

**Supporting Arguments**

1. **Well-motivated approach**: The authors provide a clear explanation of the limitations of existing neural programming architectures and how recursion can help address these issues. They also provide a thorough review of related work and position their approach within the context of existing research.
2. **Strong experimental results**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on four tasks, showing that their recursive neural programs achieve perfect generalization from a small number of training examples. The results are also compared to non-recursive versions of the programs, which helps to highlight the benefits of recursion.
3. **Verification procedure**: The authors provide a verification procedure to prove the correctness of their approach, which adds an extra layer of rigor to their method. This procedure can be used to verify the correctness of the learned neural programs on a set of problem inputs.

**Additional Feedback**

1. **Clarity and organization**: The paper is well-written and easy to follow. However, some sections could be reorganized to improve the flow of the paper. For example, the section on the verification procedure could be moved to an appendix, as it is a technical detail that may not be of interest to all readers.
2. **Related work**: While the authors provide a good review of related work, there are some notable omissions. For example, the work on neural Turing machines and differentiable neural computers could be discussed in more detail.
3. **Future work**: The authors mention several directions for future work, including exploring new ways to train recursive neural programs and developing novel neural programming architectures integrated with recursion. It would be helpful to provide more details on these directions and how they plan to pursue them.
