**Summary**

The paper proposes incorporating recursion into neural architectures to improve generalization and interpretability. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach by implementing recursion in the Neural Programmer-Interpreter framework on four tasks: grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort. They show that the recursive neural programs achieve superior generalization and interpretability with small amounts of training data. The paper also provides a verification procedure to prove that the learned recursive programs generalize perfectly.
