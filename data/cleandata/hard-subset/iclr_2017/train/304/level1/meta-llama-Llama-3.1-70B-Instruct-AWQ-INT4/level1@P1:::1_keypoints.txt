- Proposes incorporating recursion into neural programming architectures to improve generalization and enable provable guarantees.  
- Applies recursion to the NPI framework on four tasks (grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, quicksort).  
- Claims recursive neural programs achieve perfect generalization from few training examples.  
- Provides a verification procedure to prove correctness of learned programs on sets of inputs.  
- Decision: Accept — novel, well-motivated approach with strong empirical support.  
- Well-motivated: clearly explains limitations of prior neural program models and positions contribution within related work.  
- Strong experimental results: recursive models outperform non-recursive baselines and generalize from small data.  
- Verification procedure adds rigorous, formal validation of learned program behavior.  
- Clarity/organization: paper is readable but some technical details (e.g., verification) could be moved to an appendix for flow.  
- Related work: omits deeper discussion of neural Turing machines and differentiable neural computers.  
- Future work: explore training without explicit execution traces and design architectures that natively integrate recursion, but more detail is needed.  
- Question: how to train recursive programs with partial, non-recursive, or no execution traces?  
- Question: how will new recursive neural architectures be developed and what advantages will they offer?  
- Question: what are the approach’s limitations and applicability to more complex domains like NLP or vision?
