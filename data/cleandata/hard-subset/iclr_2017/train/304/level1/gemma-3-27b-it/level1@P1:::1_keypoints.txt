- Decision: Accept.

- Main contribution: Introduces recursion into neural program synthesis (recursive NPI) to improve generalization and enable formal verification.

- Empirical result: Recursive NPI outperforms non-recursive approaches on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort, with strong generalization particularly on sorting tasks.

- Theoretical result: Enables provable guarantees of correctness via a formal verification process using a verification set.

- Novelty & motivation: Well-motivated claim that recursion reduces problem complexity and improves interpretability/generalization.

- Presentation: Paper is clear, well-written, with detailed NPI modifications, experimental setup, and extensive appendix.

- Limitations acknowledged: Applicability issues for tasks with very large input domains.

- Verification concern: Verification relies on constructing a specific verification set; scalability and practicality of set construction need discussion and automation.

- Architectural scope: Paper focuses on one recursion implementation; exploring other architectures would strengthen generality claims.

- Curriculum learning comparison: More detailed analysis needed on why recursion outperforms curriculum learning and whether benefits are more than regularization.

- Task generality: Demonstrating effectiveness on a wider range of programming tasks would solidify claims.

- Computational cost: Need discussion of training and verification computational costs versus non-recursive models.

- Question: How sensitive is verification to the chosen input instances and can a verification set be insufficient even if the program is correct?

- Question: What criteria and strategies were used to generate the recursive training traces, and were alternatives explored?

- Question: For tasks where verification set construction is expensive, what alternative assurance methods are available?

- Question: Can the claimed complexity reduction from recursion be quantified (e.g., in states or transitions)?

- Question: How does recursive NPI compare to other state-of-the-art neural program synthesis methods beyond the paper’s baselines?
