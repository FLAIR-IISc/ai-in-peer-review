- Tackles poor generalization in neural program synthesis by incorporating recursion into neural architectures (NPI).
- Claims recursion improves generalization and enables provable guarantees about program behavior.
- Implements recursive augmentation of NPI on four tasks: addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.
- Reports superior generalization from small training datasets and a method for formal verification of learned programs.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance; core idea is insightful and well-motivated.
- Strong point: clear connection between recursion and tractability for formal verification.
- Paper is well-structured with clear explanation of approach and training modifications to induce recursion.
- Inclusion of verification details and a detailed appendix improves reproducibility and understanding.
- Limitation: unclear how well approach generalizes to more complex, real-world problems.
- Limitation: lacks deeper analysis of the learned recursive programs and their internal representations.
- Concern about reliance on modified training traces and whether recursion can be learned without explicit trace changes.
- Reviewer questions: how recursion specifically simplifies verification beyond fewer cases; scaling verification to large input domains; critical architectural/hyperparameter choices for learning recursion; results when adding recursion but keeping original traces or using curriculum learning.
