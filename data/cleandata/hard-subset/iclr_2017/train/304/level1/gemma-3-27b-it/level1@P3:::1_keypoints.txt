- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Contribution: Proposes augmenting neural architectures with recursion to improve generalization in neural program synthesis.
- Framework & tasks: Demonstrated using the NPI framework on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.
- Main claim: Recursion yields superior empirical generalization and enables provable guarantees about learned programs’ behavior.
- Motivation: Clearly articulates poor generalization and lack of formal guarantees in prior neural program synthesis; recursion is well-motivated as a solution.
- Methodology: Sound application of recursion within NPI and a novel verification procedure to enable formal guarantees.
- Results: Recursive models achieve perfect generalization on tested tasks with small training sets and outperform non-recursive baselines.
- Novelty: Novel combination of recursion in neural program synthesis with formal verification for provable generalization.
- Limitation — scope: Empirical evaluation is limited in scope (small training sets, relatively simple tasks).
- Suggestion — scalability: Paper should discuss strategies for scaling verification to very large input domains (e.g., sampling, abstraction).
- Suggestion — ablations: Need ablation studies isolating effects of individual recursive calls/components.
- Suggestion — generality: Discuss applicability of the approach to other architectures (e.g., DNCs, transformers).
- Suggestion — training traces: Clarify how recursive training traces are generated and what strategies ensured adequate coverage.
- Suggestion — verification set V: Provide concrete examples or pseudo-code for constructing the verification set V for each task.
- Question 1: How computationally expensive is verification and how does it scale with input size (e.g., graph vertices)?
- Question 2: Will recursion provide similar benefits for more complex algorithms (graph algorithms, symbolic manipulation) and what challenges arise?
- Question 3: Were heuristics used to select training examples, and was performance sensitive to the specific training examples?
- Question 4: For quicksort, what difficulties in earlier versions prevented verification and what changes enabled it?
- Question 5: Any considerations or safeguards for recursion depth and avoiding stack overflow for very deep recursions?
