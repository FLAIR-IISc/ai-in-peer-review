This paper represents a significant improvement over the original Neural Program Induction (NPI) work, demonstrating substantially better generalization performance when the model is trained on data in recursive form. The authors showcase improved sample complexity and generalization results on addition and bubble sort programs, and importantly, introduce two more challenging and compelling tasks – topological sort and quicksort – motivated by feedback during the review process. Furthermore, they provide a formal proof of perfect generalization for the algorithms learned by their model, which, to the best of my knowledge, constitutes the first such result in the field of neural program induction.
