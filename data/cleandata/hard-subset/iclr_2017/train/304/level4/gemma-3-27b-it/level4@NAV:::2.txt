This paper presents a compelling and accessible contribution to the field of Neural Programming Interpreters. The authors demonstrate a clever and effective method for enhancing NPI capabilities through the introduction of recursion, leading to improved generalization performance from limited execution data. 

This work serves as a strong illustration of how a focused, yet substantial, extension can significantly broaden the practical applicability of a machine learning technique. The authors’ consistent use of notation with the foundational DeepMind paper is particularly commendable, facilitating comprehension for readers, including those not deeply familiar with the area – I found it greatly aided my understanding when referencing the original work.

My primary concern lies with the generality of the provided proofs. While the arguments hold for the discrete examples presented, extending this to continuous input spaces – such as the 3D car example from the original paper – raises questions. Specifically, it remains unclear how the approach avoids intractable computation when considering all possible execution paths in such scenarios. A more detailed discussion of generalization in continuous domains would strengthen the work.

Finally, it would be highly beneficial for the community if the source code were to be made publicly available.
