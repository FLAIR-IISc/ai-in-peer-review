- Significant improvement over original NPI: model generalizes far better when trained on recursive traces.  
- Core idea: recasting execution traces into a recursive structure is insightful and well-motivated.  
- Empirical gains: improved sample complexity and generalization on addition and bubblesort tasks.  
- Added more challenging tasks (topological sort and quicksort) following reviewer feedback, strengthening the evaluation.  
- Theoretical contribution: formal proof showing the learned algorithms generalize perfectly—likely a first in neural program induction.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and covers both simple and more complex algorithmic tasks.  
- Concern: practical implications of the perfect generalization guarantee are not fully explored.  
- Concern: scalability to programs with much larger state spaces or more complex control flow is unclear; limitations should be discussed.  
- Missing comparisons: lacks baselines beyond original NPI (e.g., sequence-to-sequence models, reinforcement learning methods).  
- Hyperparameter choices (notably LSTM size) seem arbitrary; an ablation or sensitivity analysis is needed.  
- Presentation issues: recursive trace representation and its link to the generalization guarantee need clearer, expanded examples and explanation.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper—recommend acceptance contingent on addressing the above concerns.
