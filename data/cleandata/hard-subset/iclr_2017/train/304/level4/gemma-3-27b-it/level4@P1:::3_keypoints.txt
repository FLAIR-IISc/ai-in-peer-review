- Paper significantly improves NPI by training on recursively structured traces, yielding much better generalization.  
- Demonstrates improved sample complexity and generalization for addition and bubble sort.  
- Extends approach to more complex tasks: topological sort and quicksort (added after reviewer discussion).  
- Provides a formal verification procedure and demonstrates perfect generalization of learned algorithms—claimed first such guarantee in neural program induction.  
- Major strength: strong technical contribution of incorporating recursion into neural program learning.  
- Addresses a key limitation of neural program synthesis: lack of guarantees beyond the training distribution.  
- Well-motivated within prior work and builds directly on the NPI framework.  
- Empirical results on four diverse tasks consistently favor recursive training.  
- Detailed verification procedure and analytic derivation of base cases and reduction rules bolster credibility.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations of the verification process and outline avenues for future work.  
- Inclusion of topological sort and quicksort shows method handles more complex control flow and reasoning.  
- Requested clarification: provide pseudocode or more detailed explanations of how recursive training traces are constructed for each task.  
- Suggested explicit comparison between recursion and more sophisticated curriculum learning strategies.  
- Concern about scalability: verification procedure is computationally expensive and needs discussion of scaling strategies.  
- Request rationale: explain why NPI was chosen and how it compares to other neural programming architectures for supporting recursion.
