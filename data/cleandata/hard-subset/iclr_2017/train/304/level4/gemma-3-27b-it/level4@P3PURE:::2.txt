This is a very interesting and readily accessible paper. The authors present a concise yet effective approach to significantly enhance the capabilities of Neural Programming Interpreters (NPIs). By incorporating recursion, NPI generalizes more effectively from a limited number of execution traces. 

This work serves as a compelling illustration of how a small, though non-trivial, extension can substantially improve the practical applicability of a machine learning method. I particularly appreciate the consistent notation used throughout, mirroring that of the original DeepMind paper. This consistency facilitated understanding, especially for a reader less familiar with the field, allowing for seamless reference to the original work.

My primary critique concerns the generality of the provided proofs. While the arguments hold for the numerical examples presented – where all possible execution paths up to the next recursive call can be iterated over – the applicability to continuous input spaces remains unclear. Specifically, it is unclear how this approach would generalize in scenarios like the 3D car example from the original paper. It appears that proving generalization will remain computationally intractable in such continuous cases.

Finally, will the authors be releasing the source code for this work?
