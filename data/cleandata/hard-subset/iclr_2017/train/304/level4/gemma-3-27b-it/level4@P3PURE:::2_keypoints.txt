- Paper is interesting, accessible, and easy to read.
- Authors present a concise, effective method to enhance Neural Programming Interpreters (NPIs).
- Incorporating recursion enables NPIs to generalize better from few execution traces.
- The small, non-trivial extension substantially improves practical applicability.
- Consistent notation (matching the original DeepMind paper) aids understanding and reference.
- Main critique: the provided generalization proofs lack broad applicability.
- Proofs hold for the discrete/numerical examples where execution paths can be enumerated.
- Unclear how the approach or proofs extend to continuous input spaces (e.g., 3D car example).
- Concern that proving generalization in continuous cases may be computationally intractable.
- Asks whether the authors will release the source code.
