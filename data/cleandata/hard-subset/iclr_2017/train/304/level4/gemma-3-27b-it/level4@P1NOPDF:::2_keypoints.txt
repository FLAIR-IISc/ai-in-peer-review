- Positive evaluation: paper is interesting and readily accessible.  
- Method: introduces a small, effective extension—incorporating recursion—to significantly enhance Neural Programming Interpreters (NPI).  
- Benefit: recursion enables better generalization from a limited number of execution traces.  
- Contribution: demonstrates that a modest, non-trivial extension can substantially improve practical applicability of a machine learning method.  
- Presentation: consistent notation with the original DeepMind paper makes the work easier to follow for readers unfamiliar with the field.  
- Concern: the provided generalization proofs appear limited—valid for discrete/numerical examples where execution paths can be enumerated, but unclear for continuous input spaces (e.g., the 3D car example).  
- Open question/request: a proof of generalization may be intractable for continuous cases; would the authors consider releasing source code?
