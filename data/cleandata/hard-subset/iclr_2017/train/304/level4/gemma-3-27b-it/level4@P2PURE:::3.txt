This paper represents a significant advancement over the original Neural Program Induction (NPI) framework, demonstrating substantially improved generalization performance when training on data presented in recursive form. The authors achieve stronger results in terms of sample complexity and generalization for both addition and bubblesort programs, and notably expand the experimental evaluation to include two more challenging tasks – topological sort and quicksort – incorporated in response to feedback from reviewers. Crucially, they provide a formal proof of perfect generalization for the learned algorithms, a result that, to the best of my knowledge, constitutes the first of its kind in the field of neural program induction.
