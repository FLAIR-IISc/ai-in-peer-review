- Substantial improvement over prior Neural Program Induction (NPI) with significantly enhanced generalization when training on recursive data representations.  
- Demonstrated strong empirical results on addition and bubblesort with improved sample complexity and generalization.  
- Extended experiments (after reviewer feedback) to include more challenging tasks: topological sort and quicksort.  
- Recursive data representation is crucial, helping the model learn algorithmic principles rather than memorizing I/O pairs.  
- Results on topological sort and quicksort validate broader applicability and greater real-world relevance.  
- Provides a formal proof of perfect generalization for the learned algorithms, a novel theoretical guarantee in NPI.  
- The formal proof is a major strength but its clarity and correctness should be carefully examined.  
- Paper lacks a detailed discussion of limitations—e.g., which program classes are hard to learn and when recursion may be unhelpful.  
- Need analysis of computational cost and trade-offs of generating recursive traces, including training time and resource comparisons to prior NPI methods.  
- Would benefit from experiments on variations of learned algorithms (e.g., different quicksort pivot strategies) to test adaptability.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with recommendation for acceptance, contingent on addressing minor clarifications.
