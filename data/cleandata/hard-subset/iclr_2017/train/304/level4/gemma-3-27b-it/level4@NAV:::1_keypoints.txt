- Paper studies recursion in neural programming, arguing it yields better generalization and sample efficiency.
- Extends the Neural Programmer Interpreter (NPI) to learn from execution traces that include recursive calls.
- Proposes a verification method based on checking base cases and reduction rules, and perfectly infers Bubblesort and Tower of Hanoi.
- Main strength is simplicity: the core change is using recursive traces in training data.
- Unclear whether this is a major advance or if results stem from simplifying the problem via available execution traces.
- Questions about scalability to richer input domains (e.g., sorting MNIST digits) and the potential decoupling of perception from program logic.
- Paper does not discuss limitations or which problems remain unsolvable without access to execution traces.
