- Paper proposes adding recursion to Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) via recursive execution traces to improve generalization from limited data.
- Authors modify training data to include recursive calls and show NPI trained on these traces attains perfect performance on tasks like bubble sort and topological sort.
- They present a verification method that checks only base cases and reduction rules to certify correctness.
- Reviewer recommends rejection despite promising results, calling the paper incomplete and its significance unclear.
- Major concern: heavy dependence on complete execution traces, which shifts the problem from program synthesis to trace learning.
- The paper downplays practical limitations where obtaining execution traces is difficult or impossible.
- Example critique: trace-based decomposition (e.g., MNIST digit sorting) can trivialize problems by decoupling subproblems.
- The verification procedure’s success is tied to the recursive traces, so it may not reflect a general advance in neural program verification.
- Paper fails to clearly state whether its goal is learning given traces or general program synthesis.
- Lacks thorough comparison to non-recursive approaches using advanced training methods (curriculum learning, data augmentation).
- Suggestions: expand discussion of limitations and alternative training paradigms without full traces.
- Suggestions: clarify the paper’s precise contribution and scope (trace-based vs. general synthesis).
- Suggestions: strengthen empirical comparisons to state-of-the-art non-recursive methods.
- Suggestions: provide a detailed explanation of how recursion enables verification and why it’s hard for non-recursive models.
- Suggestions: evaluate on more complex tasks and discuss scalability/intractability of the verification method.
- Reviewer questions: how does recursive NPI compare to non-recursive NPI with curriculum learning?
- Reviewer questions: what are challenges when execution traces are unavailable?
- Reviewer questions: intuitive explanation for why recursion simplifies verification?
- Reviewer questions: how does verification set size scale with program complexity?
- Reviewer questions: are there theoretical guarantees on generalization beyond empirical verification?
