This work represents a substantial advancement over prior Neural Program Induction (NPI) research, demonstrating markedly improved generalization performance through training on recursively structured traces. The authors present compelling results in terms of sample complexity and generalization capability for both addition and bubblesort, and significantly enhance the scope of the investigation with the inclusion of two novel, more challenging tasks – topological sort and quicksort – incorporated in response to previous feedback. Critically, this paper provides a formal proof of perfect generalization for the learned algorithms, a result, to the best of my knowledge, not previously achieved within the field of neural program induction.
