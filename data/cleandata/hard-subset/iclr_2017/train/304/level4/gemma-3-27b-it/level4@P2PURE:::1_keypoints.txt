- Shows recursion in neural programming (via NPI) improves generalization to unseen cases and learning from limited data.
- Trains NPI models using execution traces that include recursive calls.
- Introduces a verification method using a small set of base cases and reduction rules.
- Reports perfect inference on Bubblesort and Tower of Hanoi.
- Strength: simplicity — only the training traces are modified to introduce recursion.
- Raises doubt whether access to execution traces effectively solves neural programming or if tasks chosen were too simple.
- Notes potential decoupling of perception and program logic (e.g., MNIST sorting splits digit recognition from sorting), reducing problem complexity.
- Asks which problems would remain intractable even with execution traces.
