This is a compelling and readily accessible paper. The authors introduce a concise yet effective extension to Neural Programming Interpreters, significantly enhancing their capabilities through the addition of recursion. This allows NPIs to generalize more effectively from limited execution traces. 
It serves as a strong illustration of how a small, though substantial, modification can greatly improve the practical utility of a machine learning technique.
I particularly appreciate the consistency in notation between this work and the original DeepMind paper. This facilitated understanding for a reader, like myself, who is not deeply familiar with the field, allowing for seamless reference to the prior work.
My primary concern lies with the generality of the provided generalization proofs. While the current proofs hold for the discrete examples presented – where all possible execution paths up to the next recursive call can be enumerated – it is unclear how this approach would scale to continuous input spaces (e.g., the 3D car example from the original paper). It appears a proof of generalization would remain computationally intractable in such continuous cases.
Will the source code be made publicly available?
