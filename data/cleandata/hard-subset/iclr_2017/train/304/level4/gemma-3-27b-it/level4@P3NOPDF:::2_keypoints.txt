- Introduces recursion to Neural Program Interpreters (NPIs), a simple extension that notably improves generalization with limited training data.  
- Small, non-trivial modification that significantly enhances practical applicability of NPIs.  
- Uses notation and terminology consistent with the original DeepMind NPI paper, aiding readability and cross-referencing.  
- Convincing experimental results across tasks, with well-chosen examples demonstrating improved generalization.  
- Ablation studies effectively show the impact of the recursive extension versus non-recursive baselines.  
- Theoretical generalization proofs feel incomplete, especially regarding applicability to continuous input spaces.  
- Unclear how recursion bounds recursive calls or keeps the execution-path search tractable in continuous domains.  
- Requests more detailed discussion of theoretical limitations and possible analyses/alternatives for continuous-space generalization.  
- Lack of statement about source code availability; releasing code would aid reproducibility and further research.  
- Overall assessment: strong, clearly presented paper with a compelling contribution, pending clarification on theoretical proofs and code release plans.  
- Questions for authors: elaborate on theoretical limits in continuous spaces and confirm plans/timeline for releasing source code.
