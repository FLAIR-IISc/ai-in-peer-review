- Claims recursion is crucial for neural programming architectures, enabling strong generalization and learning from smaller datasets.
- Builds on Reed & de Freitas’ Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) that learns from program traces.
- Extends NPI by training on execution traces that include recursive calls.
- Introduces a verification method that evaluates learned programs on a limited set of base cases and reduction rules.
- Empirically achieves perfect solutions on Bubblesort and the Tower of Hanoi.
- Strength: very simple idea — the only change is in the execution traces used during training.
- Reviewer questions the overall significance: does this effectively solve neural programming when execution traces are available?
- Notes that with complex inputs (e.g., sorting MNIST digits), execution traces can decouple perception from program logic, simplifying the task.
- Asks which problems would remain intractable for the proposed method even with access to execution traces.
