- Paper claims explicit recursion in neural program induction enables strong OOD generalization and learning from limited data, using NPI trained on recursive execution traces and a correctness check based on base cases and reduction rules; demonstrated on Bubble Sort and Tower of Hanoi.
- Positive: idea is simple and clear (only training traces changed), results are compelling, and the verification approach is elegant.
- Concern: if full execution traces are available, the problem may be essentially solved for these domains—supervision may make the task too easy.
- Example concern: extending to perceptual inputs (e.g., sorting MNIST images) could decompose into perception + symbolic sorting, reducing the novelty/challenge.
- Key missing analysis: need tasks or examples where execution traces are available but the method fails to learn or generalize to clarify limits.
- Suggested limitations to explore: perceptual grounding, noisy/partial traces, longer-range dependencies, or recursion with complex state.
- Clarification requested: disentangle whether gains come from recursion itself, trace structure, or the verification strategy, and discuss scalability to richer domains.
- Overall verdict: neat, well-executed contribution with strong demos, but needs a sharper statement of applicable problem settings and examples stressing its limitations.
