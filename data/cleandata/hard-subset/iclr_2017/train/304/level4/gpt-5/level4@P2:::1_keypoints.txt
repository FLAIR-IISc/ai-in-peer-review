- Proposal: add recursion as an explicit abstraction in neural programming (modify NPI traces to include recursive calls) to improve out-of-distribution generalization and interpretability.  
- Implementation: leverage NPI’s call stack and hidden-state reset across subprograms to train with recursive execution traces.  
- Empirical results: strong generalization on grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort from very small training sets to much larger inputs.  
- Verification: propose a procedure testing finite base cases and reduction rules to claim provable perfect generalization for learned programs.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main reasons for acceptance: simple, well-motivated idea that integrates cleanly into existing architecture and yields large generalization gains; novel combination of empirical results and provable verification.  
- Problem significance: directly addresses prior neural program learners’ failures to generalize and opaque hidden-state dependencies.  
- Relation to prior work: builds naturally on NPI (resetting hidden state at subprogram boundaries); argument that recursion can be applied to other architectures is plausible.  
- Evidence and ablations: recursive traces produce robust/generalization; bubble sort ablation shows full recursion is necessary; verification supports provable claims including non-tail recursion.  
- Verification caveat: constructing the valid observation sets is task-dependent and requires careful design; feasibility shown but limitations exist.  
- Scope/limits feedback: clarify when verification is tractable, especially for perception-heavy or large/infinite observation spaces.  
- Supervision feedback: discuss reliance on full recursive execution traces and whether supervision can be relaxed (partial/noisy traces or I/O-only training) or learned autonomously.  
- Generality feedback: add a short section on adapting the approach to other architectures (DNC/NTM/HAM) to broaden impact.  
- Verification detail feedback: provide algorithmic detail/pseudocode for constructing verification set V, observation mappings, complexity and completeness guarantees, and failure modes if V is incomplete.  
- Empirical breadth feedback: include more diagnostics/ablations (hidden-state reset effect, stack depth limits, return threshold α) and sensitivity to encoder choices for graph/quick-sort tasks.  
- Consistency feedback: ensure task list (addition, bubble sort, topological sort, quicksort) is named consistently throughout.  
- Key questions for authors: can recursion be learned from I/O-only or partial traces; sensitivity to noisy/partial traces; sizes/completeness and automation of verification sets V and SV; computational cost of verification.  
- Additional questions: examples where traces exist but method fails (e.g., large/continuous observation spaces); how to decouple perception and program logic for verifiability; stack discipline, termination, and failure modes for deep/non-tail recursion.  
- Overall judgment: well-motivated, clear, and impactful work; strengthen discussion of limits, supervision needs, and verification automation to improve the paper.
