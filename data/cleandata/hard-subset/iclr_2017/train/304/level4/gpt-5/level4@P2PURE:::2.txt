This is a very interesting and generally easy-to-read paper. The authors present a small but effective extension that makes Neural Programming Interpreters significantly more powerful: enabling recursion, which allows NPI to generalize better from fewer execution traces. It nicely illustrates how a modest yet nontrivial modification can make a machine learning method substantially more practical. I also appreciate the consistent notation with the original DeepMind paper; as a non-expert, this made it straightforward to read the original paper in tandem.

My main critique is that the generalization proofs are somewhat vague. For the numerical examples in the paper, one can enumerate all possible execution paths up to the next recursive call. However, how would this approach extend to a continuous input space (e.g., the 3D car example in the original paper)? It seems that a proof of generalization would still be intractable in the continuous case.
