This paper substantially improves upon the original Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) by training on recursive execution traces, which yields markedly stronger generalization. The authors demonstrate improved sample complexity and generalization on addition and bubblesort, and they introduce two more challenging tasks—topological sort and quicksort (the latter added following reviewer discussion). Moreover, they provide formal proofs that the learned algorithms generalize perfectly, which, to my knowledge, is the first such result in neural program induction.
