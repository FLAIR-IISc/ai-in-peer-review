- Claim: Modeling recursion in execution traces enables neural programming architectures to generalize strongly to out-of-domain tests and learn from less training data.  
- Method: Builds on Reed & de Freitas (NPI) by training NPI models on traces that include recursive calls.  
- Verification: Proposes verifying learned programs by testing on a small set of base cases and reduction rules.  
- Results: Demonstrates perfect inference of Bubblesort and Tower of Hanoi by the NPI with recursive traces.  
- Simplicity: The primary change is only to the execution traces used during training.  
- Skepticism about impact: Reviewer questions whether availability of execution traces essentially solves neural programming, making the problem possibly too easy.  
- Example critique: In complex input domains (e.g., sorting MNIST digits), traces reduce the task to recognition plus symbolic algorithm learning, which may not be harder than separate subproblems.  
- Request for clarity: Asks for a counterexample— a task where execution traces are available but the proposed method fails— to delineate the approach’s limits and when additional innovations are required.
