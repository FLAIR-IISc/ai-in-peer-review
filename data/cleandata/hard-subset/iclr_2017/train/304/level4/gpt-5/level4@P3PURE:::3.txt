This paper presents a substantial improvement over the original NPI framework by demonstrating that training on recursive-form execution traces leads to markedly better generalization. The authors provide empirical evidence of improved sample complexity and stronger generalization on addition and bubblesort, and they broaden the evaluation to two more challenging and meaningful tasks: topological sort and quicksort (the latter added following reviewer discussion).

Beyond the empirical results, the paper offers formal proofs that the algorithms learned by the model generalize perfectly—an achievement that, to the best of my knowledge, is the first of its kind in neural program induction. Overall, the combination of improved sample efficiency, stronger generalization across multiple algorithmic tasks, and rigorous theoretical guarantees represents a significant advance over prior NPI work.
