- Paper is engaging and relatively easy to follow.
- Authors introduce a compact modification that enables recursion in NPI, substantially increasing its power.
- Enabling recursion leads to better generalization from a small number of execution traces.
- Compelling demonstration that a modest extension can significantly enhance practical utility.
- Consistent notation with the original DeepMind paper simplifies parallel reading for non-specialists.
- Main critique: the generalization proofs feel somewhat vague.
- For numerical examples, one can enumerate all execution paths up to the next recursive call.
- Unclear how the generalization proof extends to continuous input spaces (e.g., the 3D car example); may be intractable.
- Asks whether the authors plan to release the source code.
