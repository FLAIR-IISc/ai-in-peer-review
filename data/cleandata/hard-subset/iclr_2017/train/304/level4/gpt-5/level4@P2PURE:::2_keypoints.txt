- Paper is interesting and easy to read.
- Introduces a small but effective extension—adding recursion to Neural Programming Interpreters—that improves model power and generalization from fewer execution traces.
- Shows how a modest, nontrivial modification can make the method substantially more practical.
- Uses notation consistent with the original DeepMind NPI paper, making it easier for non-experts to follow both works.
- Main critique: the generalization proofs are vague—enumerating execution paths works for the discrete numerical examples but likely fails or is intractable for continuous input spaces (e.g., the 3D car example).
- Asks whether the authors plan to release the source code.
