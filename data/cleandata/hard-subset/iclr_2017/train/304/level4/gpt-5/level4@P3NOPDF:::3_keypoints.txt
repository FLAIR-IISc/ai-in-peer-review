- Significant improvement over Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) by training on recursive execution traces.
- Recursive-trace supervision yields markedly better generalization across input lengths and structures.
- Demonstrates lower sample complexity to reach perfect generalization.
- Maintains zero-error performance when extrapolating far beyond the training distribution.
- Empirical evaluation expanded beyond addition and bubble sort to include topological sort and quicksort, raising task difficulty.
- Provides a formal proof that the learned algorithms generalize perfectly — likely the first such result in neural program induction.
- The theoretical result addresses a core criticism of neural program induction by giving conditions for guaranteed generalization.
- Overall, exposing recursion in supervision is presented as a key ingredient enabling algorithmic behavior that truly generalizes.
