- Claim: Explicit recursion in training traces is crucial for neural programming architectures to achieve strong out-of-distribution generalization and data efficiency.
- Method: Train Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) models on execution traces that include recursive calls.
- Verification: Propose checking correctness on a small set of base cases and reduction rules to yield provable generalization.
- Empirical result: With recursive traces, NPI reportedly generalizes perfectly on tasks like Bubble Sort and Tower of Hanoi from small training sets and gains interpretability.
- Decision: Reject.
- Main reason for rejection: The central contribution is primarily a change in supervision (making traces recursive), leaving unclear whether this truly advances neural program induction beyond replaying provided executions.
- Scope concern: The approach appears limited to symbolic settings where traces decouple perception from algorithmic structure.
- Missing boundaries: The paper does not characterize tasks or problem classes where the approach fails or cannot be applied.
- Mimicry concern: With high-quality recursive traces, NPI may be learning straightforward imitation of execution traces rather than discovering algorithms under weaker supervision.
- Perception gap: In richer perceptual domains (e.g., sorting MNIST), execution traces would separate perception from logic, reducing the challenge to perception plus symbolic processing.
- Verification details lacking: Need quantification of verification set sizes relative to training and discussion of failure modes if verification is incomplete.
- Supervision robustness: The paper should evaluate performance when traces are partial, noisy, non-recursive, or absent to show degradation and limits.
- Suggestion: Clarify the primary takeaway and precisely characterize the class of tasks for which recursive traces yield provable generalization.
- Suggestion: Provide at least one concrete counterexample task where recursive traces exist but the method fails, or explain why none exist.
- Suggestion: Consider a perceptual variant (e.g., sorting MNIST) to test scalability beyond purely symbolic inputs.
- Questions for authors: Do recursive traces effectively "solve" neural programming for the considered family of tasks, and if not, what are the remaining hard cases?
- Questions for authors: How sensitive is verification to coverage of base cases/reductions and can undercoverage be detected automatically?
- Questions for authors: Can the model discover recursion from non-recursive/partial/noisy traces, or does performance collapse to non-generalizing behavior?
