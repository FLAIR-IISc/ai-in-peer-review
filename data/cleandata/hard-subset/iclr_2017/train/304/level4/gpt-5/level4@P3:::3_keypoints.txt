- Paper tackles generalization in neural program induction by adding recursion to NPI via hidden-state reset and training on recursive execution traces across four tasks (addition, bubble sort, toposort, quicksort).
- Conceptual contribution: advocates recursion as a core abstraction to remove spurious dependencies and enable compositional generalization.
- Practical contribution: modifies NPI training to use recursive traces, including full (non-tail) recursion for algorithms like quicksort.
- Empirical contribution: shows strong generalization and sample efficiency from tiny training sets (e.g., 200 addition traces; 4 quicksort) with perfect accuracy on much larger inputs.
- Verification contribution: presents a procedure that constructs a finite set of step-input sequences (V) and proves learned step outputs match a reference program, giving provable perfect generalization for the tasks.
- Decision: Accept — recursion is simple, well-motivated, empirically impactful, and paired with a novel verification method.
- Problem/motivation: correctly targets length/generalization failures from spurious dependencies; recursion isolates context via a call stack.
- Placement in literature: builds on NPI and contrasts prior iterative/curriculum approaches lacking provable generalization.
- Approach: simple, elegant implementation leveraging LSTM state reset on calls; explores partial vs full recursion and non-tail recursion.
- Results: generalization to very large instances (e.g., 5000-digit addition, 120-vertex toposort) often from extremely small training sets; partial recursion ablations support claims.
- Verification rigor: verification framework is notable and, to the reviewer’s knowledge, the first of its kind for neural program induction.
- Caveat — supervision: relies on fully supervised recursive execution traces; weaker supervision remains future work.
- Caveat — scope of verification: constructing V can be hard or infeasible for large or perception-heavy tasks; acknowledged by authors.
- Caveat — baselines/variance: comparisons focus on non-recursive NPI; broader baselines (Neural GPU, etc.) and seed variance reporting would strengthen claims.
- Caveat — implementation details: hyperparameters, seeds, training stability, runtime/stack limits need more detail for reproducibility.
- Feedback — verification: formalize and provide an explicit algorithm to construct V, report sizes of V and SV, and release scripts/code.
- Feedback — evaluation: report multiple seeds, confidence intervals, runtime/compute and memory/stack statistics.
- Feedback — causal ablations: isolate effect of hidden-state reset vs recursion (e.g., non-recursive with reset control).
- Feedback — broaden baselines: compare to other architectures where feasible to contextualize performance.
- Feedback — nuance claims: qualify “first provable guarantees” against classical program synthesis/verification literature.
- Feedback — scalability: discuss automating V (symbolic execution/model checking) and extension to high-dimensional inputs.
- Feedback — presentation: fix reference formatting and summarize subprogram sets in main text for readability.
- Questions — sensitivity: how sensitive are results to LSTM size, depth, and call-state reset; what if hidden state isn’t reset?
- Questions — discovering recursion: any results on learning recursion from non-recursive traces or IO-only data?
- Questions — verification completeness: exact sizes of V and SV per task and algorithmic guarantees for constructing V?
- Questions — stack depth: worst-case call stack depth for quicksort at test time and how stack depth is bounded in implementation?
- Questions — robustness: does verification require exact trace equivalence or can output-only correctness suffice; robustness to trace noise?
- Questions — training parity: were recursive and non-recursive models trained on identical input instances, and could supervision density explain gains?
- Questions — generality: tested other recursive algorithms (mergesort, BST ops, DFS/BFS) and any failure modes observed?
- Questions — scaling to rich inputs: how to approximate/bound V and verify tasks with large or continuous observation spaces (images, speech)?
