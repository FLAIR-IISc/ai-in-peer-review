- Extends Neural Programmer-Interpreters (NPI) to support recursion, claiming substantially better generalization from fewer execution traces.  
- Retains original NPI formalism and notation, yielding clear and readable exposition.  
- Conceptual contribution: a small, well-motivated change (recursion) yields significant practical generalization gains.  
- Empirical evidence: discrete algorithmic tasks show improved generalization to longer inputs and fewer traces.  
- Theoretical/generalization proofs are underspecified; key assumptions (bounded depth, determinism, finite state/action spaces, exact execution) are not explicit.  
- Proof techniques appear to rely on enumerability of call-free segments and may not extend to continuous or uncountable state spaces.  
- Unclear how guarantees would transfer to continuous domains (e.g., 3D navigation) without extra regularity/discretization assumptions.  
- Evaluation scope is narrow—limited to symbolic/algorithmic tasks and missing perception or continuous-control settings.  
- Missing practical implementation details: stack-frame representation, max recursion depth, termination handling, training regimen, teacher forcing, and ablations.  
- Important open questions: exact assumptions/theorems for generalization, extension to continuous domains, stack encoding, mutual recursion/tail-call support, non-termination safeguards, sensitivity to trace coverage, and code release plans.  
- Suggestions: make theoretical assumptions explicit, delimit discrete vs. continuous scope, provide formal statements or mark conjectures, and discuss possible continuous-case approaches (Lipschitz/discretization).  
- Additional suggestions: include implementation details and ablations (remove recursion/increase data/alternative curricula), add experiments with learned perception or discretized control, and release code.  
- Overall: elegant, convincing empirical result within discrete domains; main limitation is theoretical treatment confined to enumerable, discrete settings.  
- Minor editorial fixes: change “generalization proves” → “generalization proofs” and “a prove of generalization” → “a proof of generalization.”
