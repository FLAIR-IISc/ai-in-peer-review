This paper is engaging and relatively easy to follow.

The authors introduce a compact yet clever modification that makes Neural Programming Interpreters substantially more powerful. By enabling recursion, NPI achieves better generalization from a small number of execution traces.

It’s a compelling demonstration of how a modest but nontrivial extension can significantly enhance the practical utility of a machine learning approach.

I also appreciate the consistent notation with the original DeepMind paper. As a non-specialist, this made reading the two in parallel straightforward.

My main critique is that the generalization proofs feel somewhat vague. For the numerical examples, one can enumerate all possible execution paths up to the next recursive call. However, how does this extend to a continuous input space (e.g., the 3D car example from the original paper)? It seems that a generalization proof would remain intractable in the continuous case.
