This paper is interesting and easy to read.  
Authors propose a small, effective extension—adding recursion—to NPI, yielding significantly better generalization from fewer execution traces.  
The work is a compelling example of a small nontrivial change making an ML method more practical.  
Consistent notation with the original DeepMind NPI paper is appreciated and aids readability.  
Main critique: the generalization proofs are vague and may not extend to continuous input spaces.  
For discrete/numerical examples, one can iterate execution paths until the next recursive call, but this approach seems intractable for continuous cases (e.g., 3D car).  
Reviewer asks whether the authors will release the source code.
