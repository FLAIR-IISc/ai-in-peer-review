- Training on recursive-form execution traces substantially improves over the original NPI framework, producing markedly better generalization.
- Empirical results show improved sample complexity and stronger generalization on addition and bubblesort.
- Evaluation is broadened to two more challenging tasks: topological sort and quicksort (quicksort added after reviewer discussion).
- The paper provides formal proofs that the learned algorithms generalize perfectly.
- This is claimed to be the first perfect-generalization proof in neural program induction.
- The combination of empirical gains and rigorous theoretical guarantees constitutes a significant advance over prior NPI work.
