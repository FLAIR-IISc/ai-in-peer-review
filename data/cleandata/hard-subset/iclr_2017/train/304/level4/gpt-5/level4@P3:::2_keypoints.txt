- Proposal: incorporate recursion into neural program architectures (NPI) by modifying execution traces to include recursive calls.  
- Technical insight: NPI’s call/return abstraction and hidden-state reset naturally support learning recursive programs.  
- Implementation: train recursive NPIs (including tail recursion) on modified execution traces.  
- Tasks: evaluated on grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.  
- Empirical result: recursive variants generalize from small training sets to much larger inputs (e.g., thousands of digits, long arrays, large DAGs).  
- Comparison: partial recursion helps, but full recursion is required for perfect generalization in some tasks (e.g., bubble sort).  
- Conceptual contribution: recursion decomposes problems into base cases and reduction rules enabling finite verification.  
- Verification method: define a verification set V of step input sequences; matching P on V guarantees correctness under stated assumptions.  
- Rigor: verification provides a rare, meaningful form of provable generalization for discrete structured tasks.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Motivation and positioning: well-motivated, grounded in program semantics, and situated relative to NTM/DNC, Neural GPU, Pointer Networks, and NPI.  
- Scope caveat: verification claims should be clarified—assumptions about valid inputs, finiteness of cases, and dependence on encoders must be explicit.  
- Construction of V: paper should provide a formal algorithm/pseudocode, complexity discussion, and methods to detect coverage gaps.  
- Generality: discuss portability beyond NPI and required architectural properties (stack, context reset, argument passing); consider experiments on other controllers.  
- Ablations: include experiments isolating contributions of recursive traces, hidden-state reset, and tail recursion.  
- Practicals: analyze stack-depth requirements, tail-call optimizations, runtime/memory overhead, and failure modes due to stack limits.  
- Robustness: report variance across random seeds, training stability, and sensitivity to hyperparameters.  
- Learning signal: discuss prospects for inducing recursion from I/O pairs or partial traces and using search/induction rather than full supervision.  
- Presentation and reproducibility: improve figures/tables, complete references, and release code and trace generators.  
- Questions for authors: formalize V construction and termination; specify minimal architectural abstractions; report max recursion depths and tail-call handling; ablate hidden-state reset; show robustness across seeds; address verification for continuous domains; discuss learning from I/O; and confirm code release.
