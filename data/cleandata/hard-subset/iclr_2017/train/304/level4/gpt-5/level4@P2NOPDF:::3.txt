This paper substantially advances the original NPI framework, demonstrating markedly improved generalization when the model is trained on recursive execution traces. The authors report better sample efficiency and stronger generalization on addition and bubble sort, and they extend the evaluation to two more challenging tasks—topological sort and quicksort (added following reviewer discussion). Most notably, they provide a formal proof that the learned algorithms generalize perfectly, which, to the best of my knowledge, is the first such result in neural program induction.
