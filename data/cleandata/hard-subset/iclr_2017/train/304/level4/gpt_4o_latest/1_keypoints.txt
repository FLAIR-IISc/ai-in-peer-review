- Paper argues recursion in neural program architectures is key for strong out-of-domain generalization and learning from less data.
- Builds on Reed & de Freitas (NPI) framework that learns from program traces.
- Main extension: train NPI models on traces that include recursive calls.
- Shows correctness can be verified by testing a learned program on a small set of base cases and reduction rules.
- Empirically demonstrates NPI can perfectly infer solutions for Bubblesort and Tower of Hanoi when trained with recursive traces.
- The idea is simple: the primary change is the recursive execution traces provided to training.
- Reviewer questions broader implications: does availability of execution traces effectively solve neural programming?
- Concern that the problem may be too simple when traces decouple perception and program logic (e.g., MNIST-sorting would reduce to digit recognition + symbolic sorting).
- Asks for examples of tasks where execution traces are available but the proposed method would fail.
