This is a very interesting and reasonably readable research paper.

The authors present a small but excellent method for making neural programming interpreters (NPI) more powerful. By using recursion, NPI can generalize more effectively with fewer execution traces.

This is an interesting example of how a small but important extension can make a machine learning method more practical.

I also realize that the same notation is used in this paper and in the original DeepMind paper. Being a non-expert in the field, it was easy to read the original paper together.

One of my criticisms is that the generalization proofs are somewhat vague. For the paper's numerical examples, you can iterate over all possible execution paths until the next recursive call. However, how will this method generalize to a continuous input space (such as the original paper's 3D car example)? It seems the generalization proof will still be difficult in the continuous case?
