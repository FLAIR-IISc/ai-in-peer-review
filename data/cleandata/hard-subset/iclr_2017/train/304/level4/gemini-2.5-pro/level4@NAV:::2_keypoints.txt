- Well-written, accessible paper with a compelling contribution.  
- Introduces recursion into Neural Programmer-Interpreters (NPIs) as an elegant architectural extension.  
- Recursion substantially improves generalization from a small number of execution traces.  
- Serves as a clear case study showing a targeted architectural change can yield practical improvements.  
- Maintains notational consistency with the original DeepMind NPI paper, aiding comprehension and direct comparison.  
- Primary reservation: generalization proofs are underspecified and may not extend cleanly to continuous input spaces.  
- Unclear how the approach would apply to tasks like the 3D car example; formal generalization proofs in continuous cases may be intractable.  
- Requests clarification on plans to release source code to support reproducibility.
