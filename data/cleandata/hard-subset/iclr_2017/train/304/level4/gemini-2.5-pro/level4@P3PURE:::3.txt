This paper presents a significant improvement over the original Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) framework. The authors demonstrate that training the model on recursively structured execution traces leads to substantially better generalization. They report superior sample complexity and generalization results for the original addition and bubblesort tasks, and also introduce two new, more challenging tasks: topological sort and quicksort (which were added in response to reviewer feedback).

A key contribution of this work is a formal proof that the algorithms learned by the model generalize perfectly. To my knowledge, this is the first time that perfect generalization has been formally proven for an algorithm learned in the neural program induction setting.
