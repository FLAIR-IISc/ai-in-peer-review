- Claims recursion is critical for neural programming architectures, enabling robust out-of-distribution generalization and learning from smaller datasets.  
- Method is an extension of Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) that trains on program execution traces.  
- Central contribution: training NPI models on traces that explicitly include recursive calls.  
- Correctness of learned programs can be verified via a minimal set of base cases and reduction rules.  
- Demonstrated ability to perfectly infer algorithms like Bubblesort and Tower of Hanoi.  
- Strength: simplicity—the only change is to the execution traces provided during training.  
- Concern: providing execution traces may overly simplify the problem, potentially making neural program inference trivial.  
- Key open question: what realistic tasks have available execution traces but would still defeat this method?
