This paper is well-written, interesting, and presents an elegant extension to Neural Programmer-Interpreters (NPIs). By incorporating recursion, the authors demonstrate that the NPI model can be made significantly more powerful, enabling it to generalize more effectively from a smaller number of execution traces. The work serves as a compelling example of how a modest, yet non-trivial, architectural modification can substantially enhance the practical utility of a machine learning model.

I also commend the authors for adhering to the notation of the original NPI paper. This consistency greatly aids readability and allows for straightforward comparison, which is particularly helpful for those less familiar with the topic.

My primary criticism concerns the arguments for generalization, which I found to be somewhat vague. For the numerical examples provided, one can enumerate all possible execution paths until the next recursive call. However, it is not clear how this proof technique would apply to a continuous input space (e.g., the 3D car navigation task from the original paper). It appears that a formal proof of generalization would remain intractable in the continuous case. Could the authors clarify their position on this?

Finally, do the authors plan to release the source code for this work?
