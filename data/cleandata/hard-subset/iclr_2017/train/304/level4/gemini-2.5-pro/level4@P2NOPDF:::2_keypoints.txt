- Introduces recursion into the Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI), significantly improving generalization from fewer execution traces.  
- Recursion is a small architectural change that yields large gains in sample efficiency and practical applicability.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and maintains notational consistency with the original NPI work, aiding accessibility.  
- Main concern: generalization proofs rely on enumerating all execution paths up to recursive calls, which may not extend to continuous input spaces.  
- Authors should clarify the scope of their generalization claims or discuss challenges in proving generalization for continuous domains.  
- Request for release of source code to aid reproducibility and community use.
