- Introduces recursion to the Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI), allowing the model to call itself and learn recursive algorithms from few execution traces.
- Enhances NPI’s expressive power and data efficiency on tasks with inherent recursive structure.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and easy to follow.
- Strong adherence to the original DeepMind NPI notation, aiding comprehension and comparison.
- The extension is conceptually elegant and a non-trivial, impactful architectural improvement.
- Addresses a key limitation (lack of recursion), moving NPI closer to a general programming interpreter.
- Main critique: generalization arguments are convincing for discrete numerical tasks but unclear for continuous or high-dimensional state spaces.
- Current proof technique relies on enumerating execution paths between recursive calls, which seems intractable for continuous state spaces (e.g., 3D car navigation).
- Requests clarification on how generalization claims extend to continuous/high-dimensional inputs or alternative analysis methods.
- Asks whether the authors plan to release source code to facilitate reproducibility and further research.
