This is a well-written and accessible paper presenting a compelling contribution. The authors introduce an elegant extension to Neural Programmer-Interpreters (NPIs) by incorporating recursion. This modification significantly enhances the model's capacity for generalization from a limited number of execution traces. The work serves as an excellent case study of how a targeted, non-trivial architectural change can lead to substantial practical improvements in a machine learning method. I also commend the authors for maintaining notational consistency with the original DeepMind paper, which greatly facilitated comprehension for a non-expert and enabled a direct comparison with the foundational work.

My primary reservation concerns the generalization proofs, which I found to be somewhat underspecified. While the logic holds for the paper's numerical examples—where one can enumerate all possible execution paths up to the next recursive step—it is unclear how this reasoning extends to continuous input spaces. For instance, how would the proposed approach apply to a task like the 3D car example from the original NPI paper? It seems that a formal proof of generalization may remain intractable in the continuous case.

To aid in reproducibility, are there plans to release the source code?
