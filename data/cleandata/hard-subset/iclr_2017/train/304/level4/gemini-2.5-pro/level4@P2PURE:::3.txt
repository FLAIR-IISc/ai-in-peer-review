This paper presents a significant improvement over the original Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI), demonstrating that training the model on program traces in a recursive form leads to substantially better generalization. The authors provide evidence of improved sample complexity and generalization on addition and bubblesort programs. They also introduce two new, more challenging tasks—topological sort and quicksort (added in response to reviewer feedback)—further strengthening their results. A key contribution of this work is a formal proof that the learned algorithms generalize perfectly. To my knowledge, this is the first time that perfect generalization has been formally proven for a model in the neural program induction literature.
