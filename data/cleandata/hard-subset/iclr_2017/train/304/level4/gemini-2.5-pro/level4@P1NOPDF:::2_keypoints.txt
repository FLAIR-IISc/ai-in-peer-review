- Well-written, accessible paper presenting an interesting contribution to Neural Programming Interpreters (NPIs).  
- Proposes a simple but effective extension: incorporating recursion into the NPI framework.  
- Recursion enables significantly better generalization from fewer execution traces.  
- Demonstrates that a conceptually small modification can yield substantial practical improvements.  
- Uses notation consistent with the original DeepMind NPI paper, improving readability and contextual understanding.  
- Main critique: the generalization proofs are vague and their extension to continuous input spaces (e.g., 3D car manipulation) is unclear and may be intractable.  
- Asks whether the authors plan to release their source code.
