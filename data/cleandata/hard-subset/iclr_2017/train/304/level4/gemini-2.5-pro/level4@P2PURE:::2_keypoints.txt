- Paper is well-written, interesting, and proposes an elegant extension to Neural Programmer-Interpreters (NPIs).  
- Incorporating recursion makes the NPI model significantly more powerful and enables better generalization from fewer execution traces.  
- The modest architectural modification substantially enhances the model’s practical utility.  
- Adhering to the original NPI notation improves readability and facilitates direct comparison with prior work.  
- Primary criticism: arguments for generalization are vague—proof technique used for discrete numerical examples may not extend to continuous input spaces (e.g., 3D car navigation).  
- It’s unclear whether a formal proof of generalization is feasible in continuous domains; authors should clarify their position.  
- Reviewer asks whether the authors will release the source code.
