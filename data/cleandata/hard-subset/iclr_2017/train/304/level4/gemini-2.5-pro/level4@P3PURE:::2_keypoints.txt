- Paper is well-written and engaging.  
- Introduces a small but effective extension to Neural Programmer-Interpreters (NPIs) by enabling recursion.  
- Recursion improves generalization from fewer execution traces.  
- The focused, non-trivial modification makes the method more practical.  
- Retains original NPI notation, aiding comparison and reader comprehension.  
- Main critique: generalization proofs are vague about applicability to different input spaces.  
- For discrete numerical examples, generalization can be shown by iterating execution paths until the next recursive call; unclear how this extends to continuous spaces (e.g., 3D car), where proofs may be intractable.  
- Asks whether the authors plan to release the source code.
