- Claims recursion in neural programming enables strong out-of-domain generalization and learning from smaller training sets.
- Builds on the Neural Programmer Interpreters (NPI) framework that learns from program execution traces.
- Main contribution: training NPI models on execution traces that include recursive calls.
- Proposes a verification method evaluating a small set of base cases and reduction rules to check learned program correctness.
- Empirically achieves perfect inference for algorithms like Bubblesort and Tower of Hanoi.
- Strength: core idea is simple—only modify training execution traces to include recursion.
- Raises a fundamental question whether access to execution traces effectively solves neural programming for tasks where traces exist.
- Notes that providing traces can decouple perception from algorithmic reasoning (e.g., sorting MNIST digits), simplifying the problem.
- Asks for examples of tasks where execution traces are available but the proposed method would fail to learn the correct program.
