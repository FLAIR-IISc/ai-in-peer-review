This is a well-written and engaging paper that presents a small yet effective extension to Neural Programmer-Interpreters (NPIs). By enabling recursion, the authors demonstrate that the NPI model can generalize more effectively from a smaller number of execution traces. This work serves as a compelling example of how a focused, non-trivial modification can significantly enhance the practicality of a machine learning method. I also appreciate that the authors used the same notation as the original NPI paper, which facilitates comparison and aids readers less familiar with the topic.

My primary critique concerns the generalization proofs, which I found to be somewhat vague regarding their applicability to different input spaces. For the numerical examples presented in the paper, generalization can be shown by iterating over all possible execution paths until the next recursive call. However, it is not clear how this proof technique would extend to a continuous input space (e.g., the 3D car example from the original paper). Could the authors clarify this point? It seems that a proof of generalization would remain intractable in the continuous case.

Finally, are there any plans to release the source code?
