- Improves on the Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) framework by training on recursively structured execution traces.  
- Training on recursive traces substantially enhances the model’s generalization capabilities.  
- Strong empirical evidence: better sample complexity and generalization on addition and bubblesort tasks.  
- Evaluation extended to more complex tasks (topological sort and quicksort) in response to reviewer feedback.  
- Provides a formal proof that the learned algorithms generalize perfectly.  
- Claims to be the first formal proof of perfect generalization for a model in the neural program induction literature.
