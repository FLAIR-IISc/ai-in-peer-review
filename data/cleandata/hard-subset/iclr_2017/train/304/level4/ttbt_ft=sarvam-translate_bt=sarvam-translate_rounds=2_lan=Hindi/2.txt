This is a very interesting and quite readable paper.

The author presents a small but excellent approach to making Neural Programming Interpreters (NPIs) more powerful. By allowing recursion, the NPI performs better generalization than smaller execution traces.

This is an interesting example of how a small but non-trivial extension can make a machine learning method quite practical.

I also appreciate that the same notation was used in this paper and the original DeepMind paper. Being a non-expert on the subject, it was easy to read the original paper along with this one.

One point of criticism is that the proof of generalization is a bit vague. For numerical examples in the paper, you can iterate over all possible execution paths until the next recursive call. However, how will this approach generalize to a continuous input space (like the 3D car example in the original paper)? It seems that the proof of generalization will still be difficult in the continuous case?
