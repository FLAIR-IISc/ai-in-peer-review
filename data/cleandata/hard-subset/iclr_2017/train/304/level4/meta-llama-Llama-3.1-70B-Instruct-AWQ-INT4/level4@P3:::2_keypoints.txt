- Paper is interesting, readable, and presents a small, effective approach: adding recursion to Neural Programming Interpreters (NPI) to improve power and generalization from fewer traces.
- Recommendation: accept, because recursion in NPI demonstrably improves generalization across tasks.
- Well-motivated and well-situated in the literature, clearly identifying limitations of prior neural programming architectures and proposing a natural recursive extension.
- Thorough experimental evidence showing improved generalization on grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.
- Provides a detailed analysis of the verification procedure for ensuring correctness of learned neural programs.
- Limitation: generalization proofs appear limited to discrete input spaces and may not extend directly to continuous inputs (acknowledged by authors).
- Suggested improvement: include more details on training procedures and hyperparameter settings.
- Suggested improvement: add more comparisons with other neural programming architectures to strengthen empirical claims.
- Request: consider releasing source code to benefit the research community.
- Minor query: clarify what is meant by “complex and interdependent” memory updates in Section 2.2.
- Minor query: provide more detail on how the verification set was constructed for each task (Section 3.3).
- Minor query: clarify how training and verification sets were selected for each task, especially given training sets can be much smaller than verification sets (Section 4.2).
