This is a very interesting and fairly easy to read paper. The authors present a small yet impactful approach to significantly enhance the capabilities of Neural Programming Interpreters (NPIs). By incorporating recursion, NPI generalizes better from fewer execution traces.

It's an interesting example of how a small but non-trivial extension can make a machine learning method significantly more practical. The approach is well-motivated, and the authors do a good job of explaining the benefits of their proposed method.

I also appreciate that the authors have maintained consistency in notation with the original DeepMind paper. As a non-expert on this topic, I found it helpful to read the original paper in tandem, which facilitated a deeper understanding of the subject matter.

My one point of critique is that the generalization proofs are somewhat vague. For the numerical examples presented in the paper, it is possible to iterate over all possible execution paths until the next recursive call. However, it is unclear how this approach would generalize to a continuous input space (e.g., the 3D car example in the original paper). It seems that a proof of generalization would still be intractable in the continuous case.

Are the authors planning on releasing the source code?
