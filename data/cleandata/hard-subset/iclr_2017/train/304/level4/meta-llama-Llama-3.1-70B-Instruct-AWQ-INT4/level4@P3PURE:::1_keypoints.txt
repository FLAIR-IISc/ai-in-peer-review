- Claim: training with recursive execution traces enables strong out-of-domain generalization and learning from less data.  
- Method: extends Reed & de Freitas’ NPI by including recursive calls in training traces.  
- Verification: correctness checked by evaluating learned programs on small sets of base cases and reduction rules.  
- Results: demonstrates perfect inference for Bubblesort and Tower of Hanoi.  
- Strength: simple intervention—only modification is to the execution traces.  
- Question: unclear whether access to execution traces effectively solves neural program induction in general.  
- Critique: problem may be too easy—traces can decouple perception (e.g., MNIST) from program logic, simplifying the task.  
- Open issue: need examples where traces are available but the method fails to infer the correct solution.
