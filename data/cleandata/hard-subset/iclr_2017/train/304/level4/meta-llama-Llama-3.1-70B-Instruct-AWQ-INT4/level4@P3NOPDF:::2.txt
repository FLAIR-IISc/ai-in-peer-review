This paper presents a fascinating and easily digestible approach to enhancing the capabilities of Neural Programming Interpreters (NPIs). By incorporating recursion, the authors demonstrate that NPIs can generalize more effectively from fewer execution traces, thereby making them significantly more powerful. This work serves as an intriguing example of how a relatively minor yet non-trivial extension can substantially improve the practicality of a machine learning method.

The authors' decision to maintain consistency in notation with the original DeepMind paper is also noteworthy. As a non-expert in this field, I found it helpful to be able to read the original paper in conjunction with this one, facilitating a deeper understanding of the subject matter.

One point of critique is that the generalization proofs presented in the paper could be more comprehensive. While the numerical examples provided can be iterated over all possible execution paths until the next recursive call, it remains unclear how this approach would generalize to continuous input spaces, such as the 3D car example presented in the original paper. It appears that proving generalization in the continuous case may still be intractable.

I would appreciate it if the authors could provide further clarification on this matter. Additionally, I would like to inquire about the possibility of releasing the source code accompanying this work, as it would be beneficial for further research and experimentation.

Overall, this paper offers a compelling contribution to the field of Neural Programming Interpreters, and with some additional clarification on the generalization proofs, it has the potential to make a significant impact.
