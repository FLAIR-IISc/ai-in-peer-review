- Proposes adding recursion to Neural Programming Interpreters (NPIs), significantly improving generalization from fewer execution traces and making NPIs more practical.  
- Consistent notation with the original DeepMind paper aids readability and helps non-experts follow the work.  
- Generalization proofs are incomplete: numerical examples cover discrete execution paths but it's unclear how this extends to continuous input spaces (e.g., the 3D car example).  
- Continuous-case generalization may remain intractable and should be more thoroughly addressed.  
- Releasing the source code would help the community reproduce results and enable further experimentation.
