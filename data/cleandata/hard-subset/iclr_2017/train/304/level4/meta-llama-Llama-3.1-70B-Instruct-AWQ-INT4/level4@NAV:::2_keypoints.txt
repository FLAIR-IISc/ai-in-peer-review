- Manuscript is engaging and accessible.  
- Proposes a compact, effective enhancement to Neural Programming Interpreters by incorporating recursion.  
- Recursion significantly amplifies NPI capabilities and improves generalization from fewer execution traces.  
- Demonstrates how a modest extension can substantially increase the practicality of a machine learning approach.  
- Consistent notation aligned with the original DeepMind paper aids readability for non-experts.  
- Main critique: generalization proofs are ambiguous—approach of iterating execution paths may work for discrete numerical examples but is unclear/intractable for continuous input spaces (e.g., 3D car).  
- Asks whether the authors will release the source code.
