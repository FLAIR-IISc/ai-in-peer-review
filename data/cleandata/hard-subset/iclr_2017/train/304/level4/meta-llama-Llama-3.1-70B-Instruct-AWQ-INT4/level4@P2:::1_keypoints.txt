- Main claim: Handling recursion is crucial for neural programming architectures, enabling strong generalization and data efficiency.
- Method: Builds on Reed & de Freitas (NPI) by training NPI models on execution traces that include recursive calls.
- Verification: Correctness is verified by testing learned programs on a small set of base cases and reduction rules.
- Results: NPI architecture perfectly learns Bubblesort and Tower of Hanoi using the recursive training approach.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: Simple, effective approach that yields strong out-of-domain generalization and learning from less data.
- Supporting argument: The approach is clearly motivated and demonstrated on multiple algorithmic tasks.
- Supporting argument: Empirical analysis highlights improved generalization and sample efficiency from recursion.
- Limitation suggested: Paper needs a more detailed discussion of the method’s limitations and applicable problem types.
- Extension suggested: Authors should explore applying the approach to more complex, large-domain problems (e.g., MNIST).
- Comparative analysis suggested: Paper would benefit from deeper analysis of relations to other recursion-handling methods.
- Questions for authors: Clarify problem-class limitations, plans for scaling to complex domains, and relation to alternative methods.
