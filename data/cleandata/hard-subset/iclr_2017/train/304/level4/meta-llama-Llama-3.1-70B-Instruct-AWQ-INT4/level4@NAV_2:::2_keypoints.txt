- Paper is interesting, easy to read, and presents a small but effective extension to Neural Programming Interpreters (NPI).  
- Main idea: incorporate recursion into NPI, which yields significantly better generalization from fewer execution traces.  
- The extension is non-trivial yet practical, demonstrating how a small change can have large impact.  
- Novelty and potential impact justify acceptance; idea is well-motivated and well-situated in the literature.  
- Empirical results are convincing and support the authors' claims.  
- Presentation is clear and detailed, with good organization and helpful reuse of DeepMind paper notation.  
- Major critique: the generalization proofs are vague — they work for discrete/numerical examples but seem intractable for continuous input spaces (e.g., 3D car example).  
- Request clearer discussion of limitations and how/when the generalization argument applies, especially for continuous domains.  
- Ask whether the authors will release source code, which would benefit the community.  
- Suggest adding more details on the training procedure and hyperparameters used in experiments.  
- Suggest including additional visualizations (plots/diagrams) to better illustrate recursive NPI performance.
