- Shows significant improvement over original NPI: training on recursive traces yields far better generalization.
- Demonstrates better sample complexity and generalization for addition and bubblesort; adds topological sort and quicksort tasks.
- Provides a rigorous verification procedure proving perfect generalization of the learned algorithms (claimed first in neural program induction).
- Acceptance rationale: (1) recursion in traces improves NPI generalization, (2) verification proves perfect generalization.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides a thorough explanation of approach and results.
- Verification procedure may not be feasible for tasks with very large input domains; needs more discussion of this limitation and mitigation.
- Recommend adding more implementation and training-procedure details for reproducibility.
- Recommend more discussion on how learned algorithms relate to traditional programming concepts (recursion vs. loops).
- Recommend expanding discussion of future work on training recursive neural programs without explicit or complete execution traces.
- Question: How will the authors address verification scalability for very large input domains?
- Question: How do the learned algorithms map to traditional notions of recursion and loops?
- Question: Can the authors provide more details on plans to train recursive models from partial or non-recursive traces?
