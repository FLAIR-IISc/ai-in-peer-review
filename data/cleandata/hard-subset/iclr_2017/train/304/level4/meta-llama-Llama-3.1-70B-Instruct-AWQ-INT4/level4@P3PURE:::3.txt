This paper significantly improves upon the original Neural Program Induction (NPI) work by demonstrating that the model exhibits far superior generalization capabilities when trained on traces in recursive form. The authors present enhanced sample complexity and generalization results for addition and bubblesort programs, and introduce two new, more challenging tasks: topological sort and quicksort, which were incorporated based on reviewer discussion. Furthermore, they provide a rigorous proof that the algorithms learned by the model generalize perfectly, a feat that, to my knowledge, has not been achieved previously in neural program induction.
