This manuscript presents an engaging and accessible read. 
The authors propose a compact yet effective enhancement to Neural Programming Interpreters, significantly amplifying their capabilities. By incorporating recursion, NPI exhibits improved generalization from a reduced number of execution traces.
This work serves as a compelling example of how a modest yet meaningful extension can substantially elevate the practicality of a machine learning approach.
I appreciate the consistent notation used throughout this paper and its alignment with the original Deepmind paper, facilitating a seamless reading experience, particularly for non-experts in this domain.
However, my primary critique lies in the generalization proofs, which appear somewhat ambiguous. While iterating over all possible execution paths until the next recursive call is feasible for the numerical examples presented, it remains unclear how this approach would generalize to continuous input spaces (e.g., the 3D car example in the original paper). It seems that proving generalization may still be intractable in the continuous case.
Do the authors plan to release the source code accompanying this work?
