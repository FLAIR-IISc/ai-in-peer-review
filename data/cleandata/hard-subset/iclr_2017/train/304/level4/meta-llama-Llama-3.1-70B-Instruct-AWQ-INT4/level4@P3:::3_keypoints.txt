- Significant improvement over original NPI: model generalizes far better when trained on recursive traces.
- Demonstrated better sample complexity and generalization for addition and bubblesort.
- Introduced two new tasks (topological sort and quicksort) as more interesting benchmarks.
- Proven that the learned algorithms generalize perfectly — claimed first proof of this in neural program induction.
- Acceptance decision based on (1) novel recursion-based approach and (2) rigorous verification procedure.
- Paper is well-motivated, well-placed in literature, and clearly explains the problem and approach.
- Experimental results are impressive and demonstrate effectiveness across tasks.
- Verification procedure is a significant contribution and shown applicable to addition, topological sort, and quicksort.
- Concern: verification procedure may not scale to more complex tasks or larger input domains (acknowledged by authors).
- Request for more detailed explanations of the recursive formulations, especially for topological sort and quicksort.
- Request for deeper insight into why recursion improves generalization and its relation to task structure.
- Suggestion to discuss potential applications where provable generalization is crucial.
- Question: provide more details on constructing verification sets for each task, especially topological sort and quicksort.
- Question: how will the scalability issue of the verification procedure be addressed for more complex tasks or larger domains?
- Question: discuss potential limitations of the approach, particularly regarding types of tasks it can be applied to.
