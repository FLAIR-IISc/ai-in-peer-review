This paper proposes incorporating recursion into neural programming architectures to improve generalization and interpretability. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach by modifying the Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) framework to learn recursive programs for tasks such as grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort. They show that the learned recursive programs achieve perfect generalization from a small number of training examples and provide a verification procedure to prove the correctness of the learned programs.

I accept this paper because it presents a novel and well-motivated approach to improving the generalization of neural programming architectures. The authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of their method, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach. The paper also raises interesting questions about the potential of neural programming architectures to learn complex algorithms from small amounts of data.

One of the key strengths of the paper is its simplicity and elegance. The authors show that incorporating recursion into the NPI framework requires only a small modification to the training procedure, which makes their approach easy to understand and implement. The paper also provides a thorough analysis of the benefits of recursion in neural programming architectures, including improved generalization and interpretability.

The experimental results are also impressive, demonstrating perfect generalization on a range of tasks. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the results, including a comparison with non-recursive versions of the tasks, which helps to highlight the benefits of their approach.

One potential limitation of the paper is that it relies on the availability of execution traces for training the NPI model. While the authors show that their approach can be effective in this setting, it is unclear how well it would generalize to more complex tasks or domains where execution traces are not available. However, this is a minor quibble, and the paper overall presents a compelling case for the importance of recursion in neural programming architectures.
