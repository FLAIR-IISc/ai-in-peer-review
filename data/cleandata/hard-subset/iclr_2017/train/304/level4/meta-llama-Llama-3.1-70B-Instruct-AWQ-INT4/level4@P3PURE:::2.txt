This paper presents a very interesting and fairly easy to read approach to enhancing the capabilities of Neural Programming Interpreters (NPIs). The authors propose a small yet nifty extension that allows recursion, enabling NPIs to generalize better from fewer execution traces.

It is an interesting example of how a small but non-trivial extension can make a machine learning method significantly more practical. I also appreciate that the authors maintained consistency in notation with the original DeepMind paper, making it easy to read the two papers in tandem, even for a non-expert in this topic.

My primary critique is that the generalization proofs could be more rigorously presented. While the numerical examples in the paper can be iterated over all possible execution paths until the next recursive call, it remains unclear how this approach would generalize to continuous input spaces (e.g., the 3D car example in the original paper). It seems that proving generalization in the continuous case would still be intractable. The authors may want to consider addressing this limitation in future work.

Additionally, I would like to inquire about the authors' plans for releasing the source code accompanying this paper.
