This is a very interesting and fairly easy to read paper. The authors present a small, yet nifty approach to make Neural Programming Interpreters significantly more powerful. By allowing recursion, NPI generalizes better from fewer execution traces.

I recommend accepting this paper, with the main reason being that the authors have successfully demonstrated the effectiveness of incorporating recursion into Neural Programming Interpreters, achieving improved generalization performance on various tasks.

The paper is well-motivated and well-placed in the literature. The authors have clearly identified the limitations of existing neural programming architectures and proposed a novel solution to address these limitations. The use of recursion is a natural extension of the Neural Programmer-Interpreter framework, and the authors have provided a thorough analysis of its benefits and implications.

The paper supports its claims through extensive experimental results, demonstrating the improved generalization performance of the recursive Neural Programmer-Interpreter on various tasks, including grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort. The authors have also provided a detailed analysis of the verification procedure, which is essential for ensuring the correctness of the learned neural programs.

One potential criticism of the paper is that the generalization proofs may be limited to discrete input spaces and may not be directly applicable to continuous input spaces. However, this is a minor point, and the authors have acknowledged the potential limitations of their approach in the conclusion section.

To further improve the paper, I would suggest that the authors consider providing more details on the training procedure and the hyperparameter settings used in their experiments. Additionally, it would be helpful to include more comparisons with other neural programming architectures to demonstrate the superiority of their approach.

Finally, I would like to ask the authors if they plan to release the source code for their implementation, as this would be a valuable resource for the research community.

Minor comments:

* In Section 2.2, the authors mention that "the memory updates of these neural programs are so complex and interdependent that it is difficult to reason about the behaviors of the learned neural program under previously unseen situations." Could the authors provide more details on what they mean by "complex and interdependent" memory updates?
* In Section 3.3, the authors describe the verification procedure for ensuring the correctness of the learned neural programs. Could the authors provide more details on how they constructed the verification set for each task?
* In Section 4.2, the authors mention that the training set can often be considerably smaller than the verification set. Could the authors provide more details on how they selected the training set and the verification set for each task?
