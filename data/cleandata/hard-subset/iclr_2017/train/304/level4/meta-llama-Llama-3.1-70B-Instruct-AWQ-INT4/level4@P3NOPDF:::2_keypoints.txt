- Introduces a clear, accessible method that augments Neural Programming Interpreters (NPIs) with recursion.  
- Shows that adding recursion enables better generalization from fewer execution traces, increasing NPI power.  
- Demonstrates that a small but non-trivial extension can substantially improve practical utility.  
- Maintains notation consistency with the original DeepMind NPI paper, aiding comprehension.  
- As a non-expert, the reviewer found the paper readable and helpful alongside the original work.  
- Critique: the generalization proofs are incomplete and could be more comprehensive.  
- Unclear how the presented generalization approach extends to continuous input spaces (e.g., 3D car example), which may remain intractable.  
- Requests clarification on generalization in continuous cases and asks whether the authors will release source code.  
- Overall positive assessment: compelling contribution with potential for significant impact pending clarification.
