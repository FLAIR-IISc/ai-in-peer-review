This paper significantly improves upon the original Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) work, demonstrating that the model exhibits substantially better generalization capabilities when trained on recursive program traces. The authors report improved sample complexity and generalization results for addition and bubblesort programs, and introduce two new, more challenging tasks - topological sort and quicksort - which were incorporated in response to reviewer discussion. Notably, they provide a formal proof that the algorithms learned by the model generalize perfectly, a milestone achievement that, to my knowledge, marks the first time such a guarantee has been established in the realm of neural program induction.
