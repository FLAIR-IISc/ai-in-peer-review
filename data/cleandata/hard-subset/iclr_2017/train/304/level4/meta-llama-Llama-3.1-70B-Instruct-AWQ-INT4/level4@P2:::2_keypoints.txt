- Paper introduces recursion into Neural Programming Interpreters (NPI) to improve generalization from fewer execution traces.  
- Decision: accept — approach is well-motivated and effectively addresses poor generalizability in neural programming architectures.  
- Authors identify lack of recursion as a key limitation in prior neural programming models and build upon existing literature.  
- Empirical and theoretical support provided, including recursive NPI formulations and verification sets to argue for perfect generalization.  
- Recursive NPI achieves impressive results, including perfect generalization on addition, topological sort, and quicksort.  
- Critique: generalization proofs are somewhat vague for continuous input spaces.  
- Suggestion: provide more details on construction of verification sets, especially for large input domains.  
- Suggestion: discuss limitations such as need for careful recursive program design and potential computational complexity increases.  
- Suggestion: provide more context comparing the approach to other neural programming architectures (e.g., NTM, DNC).  
- Questions: request more details on verification set construction for large domains.  
- Questions: ask how authors will address design and computational complexity limitations, and whether they will release source code.
