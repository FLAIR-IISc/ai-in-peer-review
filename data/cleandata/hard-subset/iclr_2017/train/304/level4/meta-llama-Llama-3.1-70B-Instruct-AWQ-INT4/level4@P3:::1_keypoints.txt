- Paper proposes adding recursion to neural programming (NPI) to improve generalization and interpretability.
- Authors modify the Neural Programmer-Interpreter to learn recursive programs for addition, bubble sort, topo sort, and quicksort.
- Learned recursive programs achieve perfect generalization from few training examples.
- The paper includes a verification procedure to prove correctness of learned programs.
- Novel, well-motivated approach with clear and thorough method explanation.
- Simplicity: recursion requires only a small modification to the NPI training procedure.
- Strong empirical results with detailed analysis and comparisons to non-recursive baselines.
- Main limitation: approach depends on availability of execution traces for training.
- Unclear how well the method scales to more complex tasks or domains lacking execution traces.
- Suggestion: provide a more formal analysis of why recursion improves generalization.
- Suggestion: include comparisons with other neural programming approaches (e.g., RL, imitation learning).
- Suggestion: show preliminary results on more complex tasks (e.g., sorting MNIST digits) to demonstrate scalability.
- Suggestion: expand discussion of potential applications and associated challenges.
- Questions for authors: detail construction of verification sets and coverage of base cases/reduction rules.
- Questions for authors: how to apply and scale the approach to tasks like sorting MNIST and planned future extensions.
