- Introduces recursion into Neural Programming Interpreters (NPIs), enabling better generalization from fewer execution traces.  
- A small but non-trivial extension that substantially improves practical applicability.  
- Paper is clear and accessible, readable even for non-experts.  
- Consistent notation with the original DeepMind NPI paper helps readers follow and cross-reference.  
- Proofs of generalization are vague and not fully convincing.  
- Unclear how the method generalizes to continuous input spaces (e.g., the 3D car example); proving generalization there may be intractable.  
- Suggestion: address the continuous-case limitation in future work.  
- Question about whether the authors will release their implementation/source code.
