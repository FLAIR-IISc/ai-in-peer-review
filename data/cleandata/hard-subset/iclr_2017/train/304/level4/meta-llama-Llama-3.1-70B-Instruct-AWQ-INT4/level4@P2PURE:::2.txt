This paper presents an intriguing and accessible approach to enhancing the capabilities of Neural Programming Interpreters (NPIs). The authors' introduction of recursion to NPIs allows for better generalization from fewer execution traces, making it a noteworthy example of how a minor yet non-trivial extension can significantly improve the practicality of a machine learning method.

I appreciate the clarity of the paper, which makes it easy to follow, even for a non-expert in this field. The consistent notation used in this paper and the original DeepMind paper is also commendable, as it facilitates a deeper understanding of the topic by enabling readers to easily refer to the original paper.

My primary criticism concerns the proofs of generalization, which appear somewhat vague. While the numerical examples in the paper can be iterated over all possible execution paths until the next recursive call, it is unclear how this approach would generalize to continuous input spaces, such as the 3D car example in the original paper. It seems that providing a proof of generalization in the continuous case may still be intractable. The authors may want to consider addressing this limitation in future work.

Additionally, I would like to inquire about the authors' plans regarding the release of the source code for their implementation.
