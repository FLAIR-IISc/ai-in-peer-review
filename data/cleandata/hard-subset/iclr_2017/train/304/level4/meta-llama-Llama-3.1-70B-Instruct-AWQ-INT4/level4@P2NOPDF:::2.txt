This paper presents a fascinating and accessible approach to significantly enhance the capabilities of Neural Programming Interpreters (NPIs). By incorporating recursion, the authors demonstrate improved generalization of NPIs from fewer execution traces, showcasing the potential of a small yet substantial extension to make a machine learning method more practical.

The paper's use of consistent notation with the original Deepmind paper is appreciated, as it facilitates a seamless reading experience, particularly for non-experts in the field. Reading the original paper in tandem with this submission was effortless, thanks to the shared notation.

One point of critique is that the generalization proofs could be more comprehensive. While the numerical examples in the paper can be iterated over all possible execution paths until the next recursive call, it is unclear how this approach would generalize to continuous input spaces, such as the 3D car example in the original paper. It appears that proving generalization in the continuous case may still be intractable.

The authors may consider addressing this limitation in future work. Additionally, the release of the source code would be beneficial for the research community, allowing for further experimentation and verification of the results.
