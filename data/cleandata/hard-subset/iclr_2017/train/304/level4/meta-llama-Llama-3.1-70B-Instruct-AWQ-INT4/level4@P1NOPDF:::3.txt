This paper significantly improves upon the original Neural Program Induction (NPI) work, demonstrating that the model generalizes substantially better when trained on recursive program traces. The authors achieve better sample complexity and generalization results for addition and bubblesort programs, and introduce two new, more complex tasks - topological sort and quicksort, added in response to reviewer discussions. Notably, they provide a rigorous proof that the algorithms learned by the model generalize perfectly, a feat that, to my knowledge, has not been accomplished previously in neural program induction.

The paper's key insight - that recursive program traces facilitate better generalization - is both intuitive and well-supported by the experimental results. The addition of topological sort and quicksort tasks serves to further strengthen the paper's contributions, as these tasks are more nuanced and challenging than the original addition and bubblesort programs.

The authors' proof of perfect generalization is a significant achievement, providing a theoretical foundation for the empirical results. This proof not only demonstrates the model's capabilities but also sheds light on the underlying mechanisms that enable effective neural program induction.

One potential area for improvement is the presentation of the experimental results. While the paper provides a clear overview of the methodology and findings, some of the figures and tables could be improved for clarity and readability. For example, the use of consistent axis labels and clearer legends would facilitate easier interpretation of the results.

In terms of broader impact, this work has the potential to significantly advance the field of neural program induction. By providing a more effective approach to training models and a theoretical foundation for understanding their behavior, this paper could enable further innovations in program induction and related areas.

Overall, this paper is a strong contribution to the field, offering significant improvements to the state-of-the-art in neural program induction. The authors' rigorous approach, combined with their ability to generalize to more complex tasks, makes this work a valuable addition to the ICLR 2017 program.
