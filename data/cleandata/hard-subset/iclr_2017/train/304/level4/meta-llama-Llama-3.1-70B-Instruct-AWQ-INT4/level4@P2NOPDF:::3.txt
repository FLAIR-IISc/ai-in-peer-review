This paper significantly improves upon the original Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) work by demonstrating that the model achieves better generalization when trained on recursive program traces. The authors present superior sample complexity and generalization results for addition and bubblesort programs, and introduce two novel and more challenging tasks: topological sort and quicksort. Notably, these new tasks were added in response to reviewer discussion, showcasing the authors' willingness to engage with feedback and strengthen their work. Furthermore, the authors provide a rigorous proof that the algorithms learned by the model generalize perfectly, a feat that, to my knowledge, has not been achieved previously in the field of neural program induction.
