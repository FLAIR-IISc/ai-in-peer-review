This paper presents a compelling and accessible contribution to the field of Neural Programming Interpreters. The authors demonstrate a clever and effective method for enhancing NPI capabilities through the introduction of recursion, leading to improved generalization performance with reduced reliance on extensive execution traces. 

This work serves as a valuable illustration of how a focused, yet substantial, extension can markedly improve the practical applicability of a machine learning technique. The consistent use of notation with the foundational DeepMind paper is particularly commendable; as someone not deeply familiar with this specific area, it greatly facilitated understanding and connection to prior work.

My primary concern lies with the presented generalization proofs. While the analysis appears sound for the discrete examples provided – where exhaustive iteration over execution paths is feasible – its applicability to continuous input spaces remains unclear. Specifically, it’s not obvious how this approach would scale to scenarios like the 3D car example from the original NPI paper, suggesting that proving generalization in continuous domains may still present significant challenges.

Finally, could the authors please indicate whether they intend to publicly release the source code for this work?
