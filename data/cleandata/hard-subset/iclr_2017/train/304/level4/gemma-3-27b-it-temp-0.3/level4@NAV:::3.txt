This work represents a substantial advancement over prior Neural Program Induction (NPI) research, demonstrating markedly improved generalization performance through training on recursively structured traces. The authors present compelling results – achieving superior sample complexity and generalization – on both addition and bubblesort, and importantly, expand the experimental evaluation to include two more challenging tasks, topological sort and quicksort (incorporated following feedback from review). Crucially, this paper provides a formal proof of perfect generalization for the learned algorithms, a result – to the best of my knowledge – unprecedented in the field of neural program induction.
