- Paper claims recursion is crucial for neural programming architectures, enabling strong generalization to out-of-domain tests and learning from smaller training sets.  
- Builds on Reed & de Freitas (2016) NPI by training NPIs on execution traces that include recursive calls.  
- Demonstrates correctness verification by evaluating learned programs on a small set of base cases and reduction rules.  
- Shows NPI can perfectly infer solutions for Bubblesort and the Tower of Hanoi when trained with recursive traces.  
- Praises conceptual simplicity: the only modification is providing recursive execution traces during training.  
- Questions significance and novelty: unclear if the result means neural programming is solved when execution traces are available or if the tasks are trivial under that assumption.  
- Raises concern that execution traces can decouple perception from logic (e.g., MNIST sorting), making the problem easier, and asks what tasks require traces but cannot be solved by this method.
