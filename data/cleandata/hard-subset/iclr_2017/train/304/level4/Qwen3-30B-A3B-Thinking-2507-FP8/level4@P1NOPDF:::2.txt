

This paper presents an elegant and well-written approach that significantly enhances Neural Programming Interpreters (NPIs) by incorporating recursion. The authors demonstrate that this small yet non-trivial extension substantially improves generalization performance, enabling the model to learn effectively from fewer execution traces. This is a compelling example of how a focused architectural modification can substantially increase the practical utility of a machine learning method.

The consistent use of notation with the original DeepMind NPI paper is particularly appreciated. As a reader new to the field, this allowed me to readily cross-reference the two works without confusion, greatly facilitating comprehension.

My primary concern pertains to the generalization guarantees. While the proofs for the discrete numerical examples are plausible—since all execution paths can be enumerated until the next recursive call—the paper does not address how the approach would generalize to continuous input spaces. Specifically, the 3D car example from the original paper involves continuous state and action spaces. It remains unclear whether the current framework can provide tractable generalization proofs in such settings, or if the approach would face similar intractability challenges as in the continuous case. A more rigorous discussion of these theoretical limitations would strengthen the paper.

Finally, the authors are encouraged to release the source code upon publication, as this would greatly facilitate reproducibility and further research in the community.
