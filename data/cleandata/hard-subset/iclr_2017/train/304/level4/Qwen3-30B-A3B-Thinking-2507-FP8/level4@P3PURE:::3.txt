

This paper significantly improves upon the original NPI work by demonstrating that training on recursively structured traces yields substantially better generalization. The authors achieve superior sample complexity and generalization performance on addition and bubblesort programs, and incorporate two new, more challenging tasks—topological sort and quicksort—following reviewer suggestions. Crucially, they provide a formal proof that the algorithms learned by the model generalize perfectly, representing the first such proof in neural program induction.
