

This paper presents an engaging and accessible contribution. The authors introduce a modest yet effective extension to Neural Programming Interpreters (NPIs) through the incorporation of recursion, substantially enhancing their capability to generalize from limited execution traces. This exemplifies how a non-trivial yet minimal modification can significantly improve the practicality of a machine learning approach. The consistent use of notation with the original DeepMind paper is particularly commendable, greatly facilitating comprehension for non-specialists like myself who could readily cross-reference both works.  

My sole substantive concern pertains to the generality of the theoretical claims. While the paper demonstrates generalization via exhaustive path enumeration for discrete numerical examples, the approach's applicability to continuous input domains (e.g., the 3D car scenario from the original work) remains unclear. A rigorous proof of generalization for such cases appears potentially intractable—could the authors elaborate on this limitation or propose a pathway forward?  

Additionally, will the authors release the source code to support reproducibility?
