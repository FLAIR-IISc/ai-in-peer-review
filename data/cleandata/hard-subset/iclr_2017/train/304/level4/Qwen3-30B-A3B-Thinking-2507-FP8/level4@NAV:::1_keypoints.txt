- Claim: Recursion handling is critical for neural programming architectures, enabling strong out-of-domain generalization and efficient learning from limited data.  
- Method: Builds on Reed & de Freitas' NPI by training NPI models on execution traces that include recursive calls.  
- Contribution: Proposes a verification approach that evaluates learned programs solely on base cases and reduction rules.  
- Results: Demonstrates the NPI architecture can perfectly infer solutions for Bubblesort and the Tower of Hanoi.  
- Simplicity: The only modification is providing the training pipeline with recursive traces.  
- Doubt: Questions whether this implies the neural programming problem is effectively solved when execution traces are available, or if the task is overly constrained.  
- Critique/example: For perceptual tasks (e.g., sorting MNIST digits), execution traces decouple perception from program logic, reducing the problem to known subtasks rather than a harder joint task.  
- Open challenge: Asks for examples of problems requiring execution traces where the proposed method would fail to generalize.
