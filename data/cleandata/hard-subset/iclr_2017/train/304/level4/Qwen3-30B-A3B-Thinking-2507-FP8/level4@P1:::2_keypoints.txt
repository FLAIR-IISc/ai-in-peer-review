- Main contribution: Integrating recursion into Neural Programmer-Interpreter yields significantly improved generalization for neural programs.  
- Empirical evidence: Recursive neural programs achieve 100% accuracy on unseen inputs across four tasks (addition, bubble sort, topological sort, quicksort) after training on very small datasets.  
- Theoretical advance: Introduces a verification procedure that enables formal proofs of perfect generalization for discrete tasks.  
- Practical impact: Shows recursion as a key abstraction that addresses poor generalization in neural programming architectures.  
- Writing and placement: Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and consistent with prior NPI literature.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance based on convincing empirical and theoretical contributions.  
- Scope concern: Verification is demonstrated only for discrete, structured inputs; unclear applicability to continuous input spaces (e.g., the 3D car example from NPI).  
- Encoder clarification: Needs explanation of how the modified domain-specific encoder for topological sort affects generalization to arbitrary comparable elements.  
- Reproducibility: Reviewer requests that authors consider releasing source code to facilitate replication and further research.  
- Question 1: How would the verification procedure be adapted for continuous input spaces like the 3D car example?  
- Question 2: How does the modified domain-specific encoder for topological sort affect generalization when applied to arrays of arbitrary comparable elements?
