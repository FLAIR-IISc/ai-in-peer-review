- Methodological advance: training on recursively structured program traces substantially improves generalization over original NPI.  
- Empirical gains: shows superior sample efficiency and near-perfect accuracy on addition and bubblesort.  
- New benchmarks: introduces topological sort and quicksort to test more complex algorithmic patterns.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides the first formal proof that the learned algorithms generalize perfectly to unseen inputs.  
- Proof strength: proof demonstrates correctness across all inputs for the new tasks, not just the training distribution.  
- Experimental-theory alignment: experiments substantiate the theoretical claims, including perfect generalization on complex tasks.  
- Robustness analysis: authors analyze failure modes (e.g., quicksort with repeated elements) and identify minor limitations.  
- Ablation studies: show recursive traces are necessary and superior to flat representations.  
- Comparison to prior work: thorough comparison to original NPI and prior empirical-only studies.  
- Presentation: paper is well-structured and the theoretical section is accessible yet precise.  
- Minor concerns: need brief discussion of computational cost of recursive trace generation.  
- Clarification requested: specify whether the proof applies to all recursive algorithms or only certain classes.
