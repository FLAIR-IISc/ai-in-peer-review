- Proposal: incorporate recursion into neural programming to improve generalization and enable provable guarantees.  
- Implementation: applied recursion within the NPI framework across addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.  
- Empirical claim: recursive neural programs achieve perfect generalization from minimal training data.  
- Verification: develop a procedure that reduces generalization to verifying finite base cases and reduction rules, enabling formal proofs.  
- Addition result: recursive model generalizes to arbitrary-length inputs after training on 200 examples; non-recursive fails.  
- Bubble sort result: fully recursive version sorts arrays of arbitrary length; non- and partially-recursive versions fail beyond length 8.  
- Topological sort and quicksort: recursive models similarly achieve perfect generalization where non-recursive fail.  
- Motivation: recursion is justified by CS principles as it prevents spurious input dependencies via decomposition.  
- NPI suitability: recursion is supported naturally in NPI through its call structure without architectural changes.  
- Decision: Accept — paper advances both empirical and theoretical understanding with novel provable guarantees.  
- Limitation 1: claim that recursion is "necessary" should be tempered and compared to alternative architectures/training procedures.  
- Limitation 2: verification procedure needs clearer discussion of limitations and tractability for large input domains.  
- Limitation 3: more systematic explanation required for how recursive training traces were constructed for each task.  
- Question 1: why did bubble sort require three tail-recursive calls for perfect generalization, and is there a systematic way to determine needed recursion?  
- Question 2: what determines verification set size (e.g., 20,181 for addition vs small for quicksort) and can it be estimated principledly?  
- Question 3: how general is the observation that NPI supports recursion—do other neural programming frameworks support recursion without modification?
