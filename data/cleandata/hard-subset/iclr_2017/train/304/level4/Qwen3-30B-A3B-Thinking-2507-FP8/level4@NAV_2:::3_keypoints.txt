Proposes recursion as a necessary abstraction for neural programming; adds recursion to the Neural Programmer-Interpreter to achieve perfect generalization on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort, and provides the first provable guarantees via verification of base cases and reduction rules.

2. Recommendation: accept—paper provides a major theoretical contribution by formally proving perfect generalization for neural programs.

3. Method and evidence: well-motivated decomposition into base cases and reduction rules, strong empirical results showing 100% generalization from minimal data across tasks, and a novel verification procedure that avoids exhaustive testing; outperforms non-recursive models.

4. Limitation: needs expanded discussion of verification scalability and feasibility for large input domains and more comparisons with recent hierarchical neural programming approaches.

5. Clarification requests: how the verification procedure scales to complex domains (e.g., images, audio) and how recursion can be adapted when base cases or reduction rules are not analytically defined.
