

Review:  
This paper presents an interesting and well-written contribution that significantly enhances Neural Programming Interpreters (NPIs) through a simple yet effective extension: enabling recursion. The approach demonstrably improves generalization from limited execution traces, making NPIs more practical for real-world applications. The authors’ choice to maintain consistent notation with the original DeepMind paper is particularly appreciated; as a non-expert, this facilitated seamless comparison between the two works.  

The primary critique concerns the generalization guarantees. While the discrete numerical examples allow exhaustive iteration over execution paths until recursive calls, the paper does not address how this approach would generalize to continuous input spaces (e.g., the 3D car example from the original paper). It remains unclear whether the proposed method can provide tractable generalization guarantees in such settings, as the continuous case likely renders a formal proof intractable.  

The authors should clarify this limitation in the revised manuscript. Additionally, will the source code be released?
