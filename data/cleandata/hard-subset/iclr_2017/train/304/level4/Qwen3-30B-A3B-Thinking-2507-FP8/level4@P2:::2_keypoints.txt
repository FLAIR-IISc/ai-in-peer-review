- Introduces recursion in the NPI framework to enable neural programs to generalize perfectly from very small training sets.  
- Shows recursion yields provable generalization by decomposing problems into finite base cases and reduction rules.  
- Empirically achieves 100% test accuracy on four tasks (addition, bubble sort, topological sort, quicksort) after training on few examples.  
- Recursive models outperform non-recursive variants, which fail at moderate input complexities.  
- Verification procedure tests a finite set of base cases and reduction rules instead of exhaustive inputs, enabling rigorous guarantees.  
- Clear connection to prior NPI work and consistent notation enhances clarity and comparability.  
- Well-structured empirical evaluation across multiple discrete, combinatorial tasks.  
- Demonstrates recursion helps models learn true program semantics rather than spurious input-length correlations.  
- Verification method is a significant methodological contribution that makes generalization tractable for discrete programs.
