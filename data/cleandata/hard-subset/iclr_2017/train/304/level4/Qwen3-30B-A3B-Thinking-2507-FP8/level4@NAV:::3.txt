

This paper represents a substantial improvement over the original NPI work, demonstrating markedly enhanced generalization when models are trained on recursively structured traces. The authors establish superior sample complexity and generalization performance for addition and bubblesort programs, and introduce two novel, more challenging tasks—topological sort and quicksort—incorporated following reviewer suggestions. Critically, they provide a formal proof that the algorithms induced by the model generalize perfectly, a result that, to my knowledge, constitutes the first such demonstration in neural program induction.
