

This paper presents an interesting and accessible contribution that significantly enhances Neural Programming Interpreters (NPIs) through a simple yet effective extension enabling recursion. The approach demonstrates improved generalization from fewer execution traces, making the method more practical. The consistent notation with the original DeepMind paper is particularly appreciated, facilitating seamless comparison for non-experts.  

A primary concern is the vagueness in the generalization proofs. While the numerical examples allow exhaustive iteration over execution paths prior to recursive calls, the extension to continuous input spaces (e.g., the 3D car example from the original paper) remains unclear. It is uncertain whether a tractable generalization proof exists for such cases.  

The authors are encouraged to address this point in the final version. Additionally, will the source code be released?
