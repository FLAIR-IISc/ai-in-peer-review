

This paper significantly advances the original NPI work by demonstrating that training on recursive trace representations leads to substantially improved generalization. The authors present improved sample complexity and generalization results for addition and bubblesort programs, and introduce two novel and more challenging tasks—topological sort and quicksort—incorporated in response to reviewer feedback. Furthermore, they provide the first formal proof that the algorithms learned by the model generalize perfectly, a milestone not previously achieved in neural program induction.
