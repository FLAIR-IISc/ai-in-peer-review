- Presents a clear, well-written extension to NPIs by adding recursion, improving practicality.
- Recursion enables significantly better generalization from limited training examples.
- The contribution is elegant and simple, yielding meaningful capability gains without added complexity.
- Uses consistent notation with the original DeepMind NPI paper, improving accessibility and comprehension.
- Core technical idea is clearly articulated and empirically validated across multiple tasks.
- Empirical results show recursive NPIs outperform non-recursive versions, especially with scarce data.
- Main theoretical concern: proofs rely on discrete path enumeration and do not address generalization in continuous input domains (e.g., 3D car navigation).
- Paper should discuss whether recursion supports generalization in continuous spaces or requires extra assumptions/approximations.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough but would benefit from comparisons to recent neural program synthesis work with recursion and explicit continuous-task demonstrations.
- Recommend releasing source code for reproducibility.
- Overall assessment: strong, well-structured contribution suitable for acceptance, with minor theoretical/disclosure gaps to address.
