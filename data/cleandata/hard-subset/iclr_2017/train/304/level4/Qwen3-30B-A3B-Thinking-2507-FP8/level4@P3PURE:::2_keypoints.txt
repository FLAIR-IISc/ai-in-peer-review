- Proposes a simple, effective extension to Neural Programming Interpreters (NPIs) by enabling recursion, which improves generalization from limited execution traces.  
- Paper is well-written and keeps notation consistent with the original DeepMind NPI paper, aiding comparison for non-experts.  
- Main critique: generalization guarantees are limited to discrete numerical examples where exhaustive iteration over execution paths until recursion is possible.  
- Unclear whether the method extends tractably to continuous input spaces (e.g., the 3D car example); formal guarantees may be intractable there.  
- Authors should clarify this limitation in the revised manuscript.  
- Asks whether the source code will be released.
