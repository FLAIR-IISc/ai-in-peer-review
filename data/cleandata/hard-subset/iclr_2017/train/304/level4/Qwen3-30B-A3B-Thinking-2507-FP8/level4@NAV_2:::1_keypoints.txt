- Proposal: Incorporating recursion into Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) yields provably perfect generalization across tasks with minimal training data.  
- Method: Modify training traces to include recursive elements so NPI learns recursive programs for addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.  
- Verification: Introduce a verification procedure that proves perfect generalization by testing only base cases and reduction rules instead of exhaustive inputs.  
- Recommendation: Accept — novel, theoretically grounded approach addressing a core limitation in neural program generalization.  
- Theoretical contribution: First provable guarantees of perfect generalization for neural programs via the verification method.  
- Empirical support: Clear motivation and empirical results showing perfect generalization across multiple tasks with minimal data, backed by verification.  
- Suggested improvement: Add discussion of how the verification procedure scales to more complex tasks and applicability/limitations for non-recursive tasks.  
- Suggested improvement: Explicitly compare verification set size to training set size for each task to clarify efficiency gains.  
- Clarification requested: How does the verification procedure handle base cases or reduction rules in continuous domains (e.g., sorting images by brightness)?  
- Clarification requested: Why did the non-recursive NPI implementation from Reed & de Freitas (2016) fail to learn recursive programs despite the architecture’s apparent capability for recursion?
