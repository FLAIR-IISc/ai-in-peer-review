- Summary: Introduces recursion into neural program induction (via training on recursive NPI traces) to constrain behavior to base cases/reductions, enable finite verification, and improve generalization.  
- Decision: Weak Accept — simple idea with strong empirical/generalization and verification results across multiple algorithmic tasks.  
- Motivation: Addresses length-based OOD generalization failures in neural program induction and situates work relative to NPI and differentiable machines.  
- Approach: Minimal principled change—recursive traces plus stack-based context isolation—reduces state space and enables reasoning about correctness.  
- Claims: Recursion yields provable guarantees via finite verification sets and dramatically improves generalization from limited data.  
- Evidence: Near-perfect generalization demonstrated on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort; verification constructions V and SV well described.  
- Rigor: Convincing distinction between provability in the recursive setting versus intractability without recursion.  
- Key limitation: Heavy reliance on full execution traces and task-specific encoders may decouple perception from control and trivialize the problem.  
- Verification limitation: Verification depends on crafting V; in high-dimensional perceptual domains V may be huge or infeasible.  
- Scope clarification requested: Specify realistic sources of explicit execution traces and provide domains where traces exist but the method fails to infer correct programs.  
- Harder evaluations requested: Test settings where perception is entangled with control (e.g., sorting MNIST digits or raw-symbol arrays without privileged comparisons).  
- Ablations requested: (i) partial/noisy/non-recursive traces, (ii) non-recursive NPI with matched data, (iii) recursive traces without hidden-state reset, (iv) sensitivity to incomplete V.  
- Verification questions: Can V be generated automatically from reference semantics, and what guarantees hold if SV undercovers V; report failure cases when V is perturbed/incomplete.  
- Generality questions: Does the method extend to mutual recursion, unbounded branching, or tasks with dynamic data-dependent subroutine sets?  
- Data-efficiency request: Quantify sample complexity improvements vs. prior NPI and other baselines (NTM/DNC/Neural GPU) under matched supervision.
