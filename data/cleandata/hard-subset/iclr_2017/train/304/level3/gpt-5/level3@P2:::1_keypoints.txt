- Introduces explicit recursion in Neural Programming Architectures (NPI) by supervising recursive execution traces so subprograms call themselves and hidden state is reset across calls.
- Key technical intervention: modify supervision to use recursive traces, constraining each call to a fixed local subproblem and enabling tractable verification.
- Proposes a verification methodology: construct the set V of feasible step-input sequences from base cases and reductions, and check the learned model against a verification set covering V.
- Empirical claim: recursive NPIs trained on very few traces generalize to much larger inputs on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort, while non-recursive variants fail.
- Demonstrated strong generalization with tiny training sets (e.g., 200 addition traces, 6 topological sort traces, 4 quicksort traces).
- Reported verification set sizes and successful matching to the reference program on V (e.g., addition ~20K inputs; topological sort 73 graphs; quicksort one 10-element array).
- Decision: weak accept, motivated by a clear, well-placed insight and a novel pathway toward conditional, testable guarantees of perfect generalization.
- Strength: minimal yet impactful intervention leveraging NPI’s call/return mechanism to improve out-of-distribution generalization and interpretability.
- Caveat: guarantees are conditional on full recursive supervision, hand-designed domain encoders/primitives, and a complete V.
- Limitation: V may be prohibitively large for high-dimensional perceptual inputs; applicability to perceptual-front-end tasks is unclear.
- Suggestion: emphasize conditional nature of “provably perfect” claims and clarify when V is tractable or not.
- Suggestion: discuss practicality of obtaining recursive traces, robustness to noisy/partial traces, and whether recursion can be learned from non-recursive traces.
- Suggestion: provide more details and possible automation for constructing V (pseudo-code or tooling) to strengthen the verification contribution.
- Suggestion: add ablations on primitives/encoders, tail vs non-tail recursion, and sensitivity to trace deviations.
- Suggestion: include stronger baselines (e.g., Neural GPU/DNC, NPI with curricula/regularization) to show margins under comparable supervision.
- Suggestion: show a small end-to-end experiment coupling perception (e.g., CNN digit reader) with recursive NPI to demonstrate verification feasibility with perceptual inputs.
- Suggestion: consolidate verification set sizes/coverage in the main text and release code and artifacts for replication.
- Questions for authors: how to ensure V is complete and when the iterative observation-to-observation construction converges to the reachable set?
- Question: how sensitive is verification to changes in primitives/encoders (e.g., exposing values vs comparators)?
- Question: can the model learn recursion from non-recursive or partial traces, and how robust is training to noisy/incorrect traces?
- Question: are there tasks where traces exist but learning recursion fails (e.g., mutual recursion, non-tail recursion, complex argument passing, non-local dependencies)?
- Question: how deep can recursion go before stack/hidden-state issues impair learning or inference?
- Question: for perceptual-front-end tasks, can V be bounded or approximated to still yield useful guarantees and any empirical attempts?
- Request: report variance across seeds, training stability, learning curves, and quantify quicksort generalization across array lengths and distribution shifts (duplicates, adversarial patterns).
