- Contribution: Introduces training neural program models on recursive execution traces and using call-stack reset semantics to bound per-call reasoning.
- Implementation/results: Instantiated on NPI, yielding large gains in sample complexity and OOD generalization on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.
- Theoretical advance: Proposes a verification procedure that constructs a finite set V (base cases + reductions) and proves agreement on V implies perfect generalization — claimed first formal generalization guarantee for learned neural programs.
- Decision: Accept.
- Main reasons for acceptance: Novel idea (recursion as a first-class training signal) that decisively improves generalization and sample efficiency, plus a rigorous verification framework upgrading empirical claims to provable guarantees.
- Problem context: Prior neural program models fail to generalize to larger inputs and lack provable guarantees.
- Motivation/positioning: Recursion matches algorithmic specification, and call-state resets reduce effective state space; paper engages prior work and explains limits of length-based curricula.
- Empirical evidence: Strong results with very small training trace counts (e.g., addition: 5 one-digit traces; quicksort: 4 traces) and a clear ablation on bubblesort (non-recursive vs partial vs full recursion).
- Verification credibility: Verification method is principled and appears complete for the evaluated tasks.
- Clarify proof assumptions: Need precise statement of assumptions for constructing V, its completeness, and termination/stack-depth guarantees (esp. for non–tail recursion like quicksort).
- Ablations requested: Separate the effect of call-state reset from recursion and test sensitivity to encoder design (e.g., exposing comparisons vs raw values).
- Scalability concerns: Discuss limits when V is large/continuous and propose heuristics or approximations with guarantees.
- Baselines: Compare to stronger recent baselines (Neural GPU variants, differentiable stacks) on the same tasks.
- Robustness/stability: Report training stability, variance over seeds, failure modes with noisy/partial traces, and whether partial traces can induce recursion.
- Reproducibility: Release code and verification scripts to encourage adoption.
- Specific open questions: How is V proven complete/formalized? What are bounds/guardrails on recursion depth and stack growth for quicksort? How robust are results to removing hand-engineered observations? Can the approach extend to other architectures (e.g., DNC) without trace supervision and what changes are needed?
