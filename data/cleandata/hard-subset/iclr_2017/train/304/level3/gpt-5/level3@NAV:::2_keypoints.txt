- Paper introduces recursion into the NPI framework, showing that training on recursive traces greatly improves sample efficiency and generalization on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.
- Authors propose a verification procedure reducing generalization proofs to checking base cases and reduction rules.
- Writing is clear, well organized, and reuses NPI notation to aid readability.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key acceptance reasons: simple, well-motivated extension that substantially improves generalization across tasks; provides a credible path toward verifiable neural program generalization.
- Problem significance: addresses generalization and verifiability—major bottlenecks in neural program learning.
- Placement and motivation: positioned relative to NPI, DNC/NTM, Neural GPU; argues recursion reduces spurious length dependencies and hidden-state entanglement.
- Empirical evidence: strong results with perfect/near-perfect generalization on longer inputs using very small training sets; informative ablations comparing non-recursive, partially, and fully recursive traces.
- Rigor caveat: “provably perfect generalization” depends on constructing a complete verification set V, which is currently manual and problem-specific.
- Scalability caveat: approach likely does not scale to continuous or high-dimensional perceptual domains.
- Request to clarify verification: define assumptions for V completeness, quantify effort to construct V, report coverage and failure modes when V is incomplete.
- Suggest automating V construction: can V be derived mechanically from a specification or interpreter; any guarantees on minimality/complexity?
- Request resource metrics: report stack depth distributions, inference latency/memory vs non-recursive NPI, and sensitivity to α and LSTM size.
- Supervision questions: robustness to partially recursive or noisy traces and progress toward discovering recursion without explicit traces.
- External validity: discuss adapting to continuous/perceptual domains, why intractable, and alternatives (symbolic interfaces, discretization).
- Reproducibility: will code and verification scripts be released?
- Specific technical questions: how to detect/prevent spurious recursive calls that pass V but fail off-distribution; quantify sizes of V and SV per task relative to primitives and observation dimensionality; sensitivity to domain encoders; training instabilities and mitigations for quicksort (non-tail recursion).
