- Proposes adding recursion to Neural Programmer-Interpreters (NPI) by modifying training traces, enabling learning of recursive programs that generalize perfectly from very small training sets (e.g., 6 traces for topological sort).
- Introduces a novel verification procedure that proves perfect generalization by checking base cases and reduction rules.
- Decision: Accept — recursion yields provable generalization and strong empirical performance where non-recursive methods fail.
- Recursion addresses poor generalization, makes verification tractable, and is empirically validated on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort; paper is well-positioned in prior work.
- Additional feedback: needs more rigorous generalization proofs, discussion of continuous input spaces (e.g., 3D navigation), code release, and investigation of verification scalability.
- Question: How can the generalization proofs be made more rigorous for tasks with complex reduction rules?
- Question: How might this approach be extended to handle continuous input spaces, and what theoretical/practical challenges arise?
- Question: Will the authors release the source code for the recursive NPI implementation?
