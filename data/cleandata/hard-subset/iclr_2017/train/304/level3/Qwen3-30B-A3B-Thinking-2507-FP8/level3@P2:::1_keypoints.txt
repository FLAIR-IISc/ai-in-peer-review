- Incorporating recursion into the Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) enables provable perfect generalization for neural programs.  
- Authors modify training traces to include recursive elements so the model can learn recursive programs.  
- Demonstrated on four tasks (grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, quicksort) with perfect generalization from very small training sets (4–200 examples).  
- They provide a verification procedure that proves generalization by testing a finite set of base cases and reduction rules.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear theoretical motivation and convincing empirical evidence for the tasks considered.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; contribution is novel and important.  
- Theoretical insight is significant: recursion reduces verification burden and yields provable correctness (e.g., 20,181 base cases for addition proving all lengths).  
- Empirical highlight: recursive bubble sort achieves 100% accuracy up to length 5000 from 100 examples, whereas non-recursive fails beyond length 8.  
- Major concern: approach depends on availability of execution traces with recursive structure, which may not exist for raw-input domains (images, speech).  
- Risk that decoupling perception from program logic (e.g., sorting MNIST) could trivialize real-world problems.  
- Suggestion: clearly articulate limitations regarding need for recursive execution traces and their impact on applicability.  
- Suggestion: provide a detailed analysis of trade-offs between the simplicity of trace modification and imposed problem-setup constraints.  
- Suggestion: discuss extensions for domains without available traces, such as learning to generate traces from raw inputs.  
- Suggestion: include comparisons to other program-generalization methods (curriculum learning, alternative architectures).  
- Clarification requested: how would the approach handle sorting MNIST where traces must be generated from images and whether that reduces to separate perception and program logic tasks?  
- Clarification requested: are there tasks with available traces where the recursive approach still fails, and what characterizes such tasks?  
- Clarification requested: how does the verification procedure scale when base cases or reduction rules are numerous or infinite, and what are its practical limits?
