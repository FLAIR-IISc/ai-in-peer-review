- Introduces recursion into the NPI framework to substantially improve neural program generalization.
- Small modification enables perfect generalization from very small training sets (4–200 examples across tasks).
- Provides first provable guarantees of perfect generalization for neural programs via verification of base cases and reduction rules.
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort with recursive models outperforming non-recursive ones.
- Addition: recursive model generalizes to arbitrary length after 5 examples; non-recursive fails.
- Bubble sort: fully recursive model achieves 100% accuracy on arrays up to length 10; non-recursive models fail on longer arrays.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear explanations and consistent notation.
- Recommendation: accept, due to conceptual simplicity and major theoretical advance.
- Limitation: verification becomes intractable for continuous input spaces — more discussion needed.
- The construction of the verification set (covering base cases and reduction rules) is described vaguely and needs concrete detail or examples.
- Strong claim that non-recursive models cannot be verified due to hidden-state propagation needs further justification and explanation.
- Request for source code release to facilitate reproducibility and further research.
