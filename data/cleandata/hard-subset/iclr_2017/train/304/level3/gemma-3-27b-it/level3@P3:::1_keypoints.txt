- Proposal: Augment NPI training traces with recursion to improve generalization and enable provable correctness.
- Core idea: Simple augmentation of traces, not complex architectural changes, to capture recursive program structure.
- Empirical result: Achieves perfect generalization on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort with limited training data.
- Verification: Introduces a procedure to provide provable generalization for learned programs.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions due to strong results and novel contribution.
- Strength: Clear motivation linking recursion to reduced problem complexity and formal reasoning.
- Strength: Straightforward, well-explained adaptation of NPI emphasizing that traces drive the improvement.
- Weakness: Questions remain about scalability to larger, higher-dimensional, or more complex state spaces.
- Concern: Potential “perception decoupling” where traces separate perception from program logic may limit real-world applicability.
- Limitation: Verification procedure may be infeasible for very large input domains; alternatives need exploration.
- Architectural question: Unclear whether benefits are specific to NPI or generalize to other frameworks (e.g., transformers).
- Suggested experiments: Test on tasks with higher dimensionality or complex state to assess scalability.
- Future work: Explore training recursive programs without explicit traces and alternative verification/validation methods.
- Request for clarification: Authors should discuss concrete failure cases, mitigation strategies, and potential alternative verification techniques.
