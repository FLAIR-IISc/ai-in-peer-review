- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Contribution: Introduces recursion into Neural Programmer-Interpreters (NPIs) to improve generalization.
- Claim: Adding recursion significantly boosts generalization from limited training data.
- Readability: Uses existing NPI notation (Reed & Freitas 2016), making the work accessible.
- Empirical result: Experiments show improved generalization versus non-recursive baselines (sorting, topological sort).
- Theoretical result: Presents a formal verification procedure and claims of perfect generalization.
- Strength: Verification procedure is a significant advancement for neural program synthesis confidence.
- Weakness: Proofs of generalization feel vague and rely on covering base cases and reduction rules without enough rigor.
- Limitation: Scalability to continuous or more complex input spaces (e.g., 3D car environments) is unclear.
- Suggestion: Elaborate on how the verification set is constructed with concrete examples per task.
- Suggestion: Discuss limitations and scalability challenges of the verification procedure and future directions.
- Suggestion: Consider releasing source code to benefit the research community.
- Suggestion: Clarify the relationship between recursion depth and generalization (limits, diminishing returns).
- Questions for authors: How does recursion affect computational cost (training/inference)?
- Questions for authors: How does the size of the finite verification test set scale with problem complexity?
