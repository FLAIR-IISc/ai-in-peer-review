- Proposal: augment neural architectures with recursion in the NPI framework to improve generalization in program synthesis.  
- Experiments: evaluated on grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort showing superior generalization.  
- Major contribution: formal verification of perfect generalization enabled by recursion.  
- Motivation: recursion reduces complexity and facilitates reasoning about program behavior for provable guarantees.  
- Empirical strength: strong experimental results, especially for bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.  
- Novelty: formal verification of neural programs is a significant and rare contribution in the field.  
- Verification detail: clear procedure with base cases and reduction rules that makes the claim credible.  
- Writing and clarity: paper is well-written and explains concepts and experiments clearly.  
- Task selection: inclusion of topological sort and quicksort strengthens the generality of findings.  
- Analysis insight: non-recursive NPIs learn spurious dependencies; recursion mitigates this issue.  
- Clarify training traces: need more detail and concrete examples/pseudocode showing how recursive traces are generated.  
- Scalability concern: verification may not scale to complex tasks or large/infinite state spaces; authors should discuss potential solutions.  
- Ablation suggestion: perform systematic ablation over recursion depth/levels to understand optimal recursion use.  
- Comparative context: compare recursion to other generalization techniques (curriculum learning, augmentation, regularization).  
- Environment encoding: topological sort environment encoding is dense and should be better justified and explained.  
- Q1 for authors: provide pseudocode for generating recursive training traces for topo sort and quicksort.  
- Q2 for authors: thoughts on using abstract interpretation or model checking to scale verification.  
- Q3 for authors: were hyperparameters changed when moving from non-recursive to fully recursive training?  
- Q4 for authors: specify the spurious dependencies learned by non-recursive NPI and how recursion prevents them.  
- Q5 for authors: describe criteria for the size and composition of the verification set, especially for randomly generated tasks.
