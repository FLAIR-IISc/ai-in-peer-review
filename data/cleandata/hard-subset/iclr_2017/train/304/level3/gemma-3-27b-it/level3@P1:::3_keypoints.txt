- Claim: Incorporating recursion into neural architectures improves generalization and interpretability for learned programs.
- Method: Implement recursion within the Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) and evaluate on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.
- Result: Recursive models show substantially better generalization with far less training data than prior approaches.
- Theoretical contribution: Provide a formal verification procedure proving perfect generalization given a complete verification set—first provable generalization guarantees for neural programs.
- Decision: Accept — paper offers strong empirical results and a novel, rigorous verification method.
- Strength: Insightful connection between recursion as a programming primitive and benefits for neural program synthesis.
- Empirics: Convincing cross-task improvement, including generalization to much longer inputs.
- Task diversity: Inclusion of topological sort and quicksort extends evaluation beyond original NPI tasks.
- Verification clarity: Clear explanation of how recursion reduces verification to base cases and reductions; detailed construction of verification set.
- Ablation request: More ablation studies (e.g., partial vs. full recursion) needed to isolate recursion’s contributions.
- Verification scalability: Concerns about applying the verification procedure to tasks with huge input domains; alternative techniques should be explored.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Missing analysis on robustness to learning rate, LSTM size, and other hyperparameters.
- Reproducibility: Recommend releasing code to facilitate replication and further research.
- Comparative context: Paper should briefly compare to other generalization techniques (curriculum learning, data augmentation).
- Question: How are training traces generated to explicitly contain recursive elements, and can these be discovered automatically or must they be hand-designed?
- Question: How is completeness of the verification set V ensured for complex tasks where base cases and reductions are hard to enumerate?
- Question: What are the tradeoffs of different domain-specific encoders and their impact on the ease and correctness of verification?
- Question: Does the added complexity of the quicksort implementation affect training speed or require special verification considerations?
