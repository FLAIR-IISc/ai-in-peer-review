- Decision: Accept with enthusiasm.

- Core contribution: Integrates recursion into neural program learning (NPI) to substantially improve generalization and enable formal verification of correctness.

- Tasks evaluated: Experiments on addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort (the latter two added after feedback).

- Main empirical result: Training on recursive traces consistently improves generalization across all four tasks.

- Theoretical result: Provides a formal proof of perfect generalization for the recursive approach.

- Verification: Presents a detailed verification procedure with thorough base case/reduction analysis that supports the correctness claims.

- Ablations: Comparison of non-recursive, partially recursive, and fully recursive models shows full recursion is crucial for best performance.

- Positioning: Clearly motivated problem and well-situated within prior NPI literature, addressing known limitations.

- Reproducibility concern: Implementation details need clarification and the NPI code used should be released to enhance reproducibility.

- Verification scaling: Acknowledges difficulty verifying very large input domains and suggests exploring strategies (e.g., sampling, abstraction) for scaling.

- Computational cost: Recommends discussing the training cost tradeoffs between recursive and non-recursive models.

- Question 1 (hyperparams): Request for detailed hyperparameters and description of any hyperparameter tuning strategies per task.

- Question 2 (training without traces): Asks for elaboration on approaches to train recursive neural programs without explicit execution traces.

- Question 3 (verification techniques): Suggests considering symbolic execution or abstract interpretation to reduce the input search space for verification.

- Question 4 (encoder modification): Raises concern that the simplified domain-specific encoder for bubble sort may limit the generality of the findings to more complex tasks.
