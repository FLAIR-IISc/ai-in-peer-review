- Extends Neural Programmer-Interpreters (NPIs) with recursion to improve generalization and enable provable guarantees.
- Argues convincingly that recursion is a key abstraction for better generalization and verification in neural program learning.
- Experiments (grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, quicksort) show recursive NPIs achieve perfect generalization where non-recursive models struggle.
- Reuse of Reed & de Freitas (2016) notation improves readability for familiar readers.
- Recommendation: Accept — simple, effective modification with strong results and formal verification merits publication.
- Strength: Recursion decomposes problems into smaller subproblems, reducing learning complexity and aiding discovery of robust semantics.
- Strength: Demonstration of provable generalization is a significant step toward more reliable AI systems.
- Empirical results consistently show recursive NPIs outperform non-recursive counterparts as input complexity increases.
- Improvement: Discussion of continuous input spaces (e.g., robotics) feels dismissive; should explore approximate or sampling-based verification approaches.
- Improvement: Generalization proofs are described as "vague" and need more detail on verification set construction and guarantees.
- Improvement: Provide concrete examples of base cases and reduction rules for each task and better integrate Appendix A.6 into the main text.
- Improvement: Authors repeatedly promise code release; a firm commitment and timeline are needed for reproducibility.
- Question: Any ideas or ongoing work to adapt verification to continuous input spaces using approximations or sampling?
- Question: Please provide a concrete base case and reduction rule example for topological sort and explain how verifying them ensures perfect generalization.
- Question: What is the anticipated timeline for releasing the source code?
- Question: Were hyperparameter searches or ablation studies performed to ensure robustness to training-data variations?
- Question: Is the non-recursive bubble sort performance drop around length 8 consistent across random seeds or tied to specific training data?
