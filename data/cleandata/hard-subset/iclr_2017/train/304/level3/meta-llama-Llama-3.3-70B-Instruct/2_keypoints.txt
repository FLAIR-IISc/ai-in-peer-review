- Proposes Tartan (TRT), a hardware accelerator that exploits per-layer variable precision to make inference time proportional to used precision.
- TRT uses bit-serial processing of activations and weights and cascaded adder trees to slice output computation.
- Demonstrates improved execution time and energy efficiency versus DaDianNao (DaDN), with evaluated area overhead.
- Enables trading off accuracy for further gains in performance and energy without network retraining.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly explained, and uses consistent notation throughout.
- Evaluation is thorough, comparing performance, energy, and area to state-of-the-art.
- Limitations noted: assumes each layer fits on-chip and needs further study for other network architectures and ML algorithms.
- Suggests providing TRT source code to enable reproducibility and community adoption.
- Recommends exploring TRT’s application to other algorithms and network architectures.
- Recommends investigating interactions and potential synergies between TRT and techniques like quantization, pruning, and compression.
- Asks authors to clarify plans for releasing TRT source code to the research community.
- Asks authors to detail potential applications beyond image classification and plans to explore them, plus how TRT interacts with quantization/pruning/compression.
