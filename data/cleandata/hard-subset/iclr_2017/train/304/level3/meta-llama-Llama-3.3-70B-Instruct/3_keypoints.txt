- Presents Tartan (TRT), a DNN inference accelerator exploiting per-layer variable precision to make execution time proportional to used precision.
- Improves on DaDianNao by processing activations bit-serially for better performance and energy efficiency.
- Demonstrates improved sample complexity and generalization for addition and bubblesort, and introduces topological sort and quicksort tasks.
- Decision: Accept, based on novel, well-motivated approach and thorough evaluation.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains TRT concepts and precision analysis.
- Evaluation shows TRT outperforms DaDianNao in execution time and energy while maintaining accuracy.
- Requests more implementation details on hardware architecture and the software framework used for evaluation.
- Suggests additional results on accuracy vs. performance trade-offs and an analysis of TRT’s limitations and challenges.
- Asks how TRT handles layers with significantly varying precision requirements.
- Asks whether TRT applies to other network types (e.g., RNNs, LSTMs).
- Asks for comparisons to other accelerators like Google’s TPU in performance and energy efficiency.
