- Proposes adding recursion to Neural Programming Interpreters (NPI) to improve generalization and interpretability.  
- Claims recursion enables provable guarantees of perfect generalization for neural programs.  
- Evaluates recursive NPI on grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.  
- Shows significant generalization improvements from small training datasets.  
- Uses notation consistent with the original DeepMind NPI paper to aid accessibility.  
- Presents the first work claiming provable generalization guarantees for neural programs.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novelty and practical impact: recursion is a simple but effective modification that enhances robustness and interpretability.  
- Empirical and theoretical rigor: strong empirical results across tasks plus a framework for proving generalization.  
- Well-motivated: clearly identifies poor generalization in prior models and argues recursion as a natural solution.  
- Empirical validation: achieves perfect generalization on tasks like bubble sort and quicksort where non-recursive models fail.  
- Provable guarantees: introduces a verification procedure to prove generalization, a notable contribution.  
- Suggestion: clarify the generalization proofs, especially for continuous input spaces.  
- Suggestion: broaden evaluation to additional real-world tasks (e.g., parsing, symbolic reasoning).  
- Suggestion: release source code to improve reproducibility and community uptake.  
- Question: can the verification procedure be extended to continuous or infinite input spaces, and what changes are needed?  
- Question: do the authors plan to release the implementation code?  
- Question: how does recursive NPI perform on tasks requiring multi-step logical inference or real-world program synthesis?  
- Conclusion: combines empirical success and theoretical rigor, with minor clarifications and broader evaluation recommended to increase impact.
