- Introduces recursion into neural program architectures to improve generalization by training on recursive execution traces.
- Argues models trained on recursive traces learn true algorithm semantics rather than spurious input-length correlations.
- Implements the approach in the NPI framework and evaluates on grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.
- Shows dramatic improvements: recursive models trained on very few examples achieve perfect accuracy on much larger, more complex inputs.
- Presents a novel formal verification procedure that uses recursion to create a finite verification set proving perfect generalization.
- Claims this is the first provable-generalization result for a neurally-induced program, enhancing reliability of learned programs.
- Recommendation: strongly accept the paper.
- Key reason 1: simple, intuitive, and highly effective solution to the generalization problem.
- Key reason 2: pioneering work on provable generalization moves the field beyond empirical testing.
- Supporting evidence: direct improvement over original NPI, with non-recursive models failing where recursive models succeed.
- Supporting evidence: exceptional sample efficiency and applicability to more complex tasks involving graphs and divide-and-conquer.
- Supporting evidence: rigorous and novel verification framework applied successfully to all four tasks.
- Suggestion: clarify the construction of the verification set with a concrete, walk-through example.
- Suggestion: discuss limitations of requiring full recursive execution traces and consider whether recursion could be learned from non-recursive traces or input-output pairs.
- Suggestion: contrast why NPI's LSTM reset on subprogram calls makes it particularly suitable for recursion versus persistent-state RNNs.
- Question: how was completeness of the verification set for complex tasks like quicksort ensured beyond manual inspection?
- Question: have the authors considered architectural changes to encourage or learn recursion, reducing reliance on perfect traces?
- Question: does the performance gap imply non-recursive NPI memorizes sequence properties while recursion enforces context-independent modular rules?
