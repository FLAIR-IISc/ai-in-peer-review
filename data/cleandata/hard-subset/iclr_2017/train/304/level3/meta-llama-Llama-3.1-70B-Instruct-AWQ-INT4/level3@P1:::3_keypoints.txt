- Paper proposes adding recursion to neural programming architectures, adapting NPI to learn recursive programs for addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.
- Experiments show significant generalization improvements, especially with limited training data, and include a formal proof of perfect generalization.
- Decision: Accept.
- Clear problem statement and strong motivation addressing poor generalizability in neural program induction.
- Experiments are well-designed and demonstrate effectiveness across multiple tasks.
- Shows significant improvement over original NPI baseline, particularly with small datasets.
- Formal proof of perfect generalization is a novel and important contribution.
- Suggest clarifying why recursion specifically improves generalization.
- Recommend discussing limitations for very large input domains and strategies to mitigate them.
- Request more implementation details (network architectures and hyperparameters).
- Questions for authors: why recursion aids generalization, how to handle large input domains, and exact architecture/hyperparameter details.
