- Paper proposes adding recursion to neural programming architectures to improve generalization, implemented by modifying the Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI).  
- Claims show perfect generalization on four tasks (grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, quicksort).  
- Demonstrates that recursion reduces program complexity, making learning and generalization easier.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Well-motivated approach with clear argumentation about the necessity of recursion for generalization.  
- Strong empirical results: perfect generalization on tasks with few training examples and comparison to non-recursive baselines.  
- Novel contribution: introducing recursion to neural programming is a significant departure from prior methods.  
- Thorough evaluation, including non-recursive comparisons, supporting the effectiveness of the approach.  
- Clear and concise writing that aids understanding.  
- Suggested improvement: provide more detailed discussion of limitations when incorporating recursion into neural programs.  
- Suggested improvement: expand analysis of the proposed verification procedure, including its limits and applications.  
- Suggested improvement: give more detailed plans for future work, especially applying recursion to more complex tasks.  
- Questions for authors: how will the approach extend to complex domains like NLP or vision; how to handle more complex recursive structures; and more detail on the verification procedure’s limitations and uses.
