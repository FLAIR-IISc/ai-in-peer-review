**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

This paper introduces a novel extension to Neural Programming Interpreters (NPI), a type of neural network architecture designed to learn programs from data. The key contribution is the incorporation of recursion, a fundamental concept in programming languages, into the NPI framework. The authors demonstrate that this extension enables the learning of recursive neural programs that generalize perfectly to unseen inputs, a significant improvement over previous approaches. The paper also provides a verification procedure to prove the correctness of the learned programs, which is a crucial aspect of program learning. The authors evaluate their approach on four tasks: grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort, and show that their recursive NPI model outperforms non-recursive baselines in terms of generalization.
