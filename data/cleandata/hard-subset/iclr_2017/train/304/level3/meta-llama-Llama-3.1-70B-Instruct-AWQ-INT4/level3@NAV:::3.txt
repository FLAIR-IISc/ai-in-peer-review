**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper "Making Neural Programming Architectures Generalize via Recursion" proposes a novel approach to improve the generalizability of neural programming architectures by incorporating recursion. The authors demonstrate that by training on recursive traces, neural networks can learn to generalize perfectly on various tasks, including addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort. They also provide a formal proof that the learned algorithms generalize perfectly, which is a significant contribution to the field of neural program induction.

**Decision: Accept**

I decide to accept this paper for several reasons:

1. **Well-motivated approach**: The authors provide a clear motivation for their approach, highlighting the limitations of existing neural programming architectures and the benefits of incorporating recursion.
2. **Significant improvement over original NPI**: The paper demonstrates a significant improvement in generalization performance over the original Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) architecture, which is a well-established baseline in the field.
3. **Strong empirical results**: The authors provide strong empirical results on various tasks, including addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach.
