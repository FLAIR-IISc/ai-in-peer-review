- Proposes adding recursion to neural programming architectures to improve generalizability.
- Implements recursion by modifying the Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) to learn recursive programs.
- Claims recursive neural programs achieve perfect generalization on tasks: grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort with few training examples.
- Introduces a verification procedure to prove correctness of learned recursive programs, claimed to be broadly applicable.
- Recommendation: accept.
- Novelty/Impact: recursion in neural programs is a novel idea with potential to significantly improve generalization; supported by empirical results.
- Soundness/Completeness: provides thorough analysis, clear method description, and a verification method for correctness.
- Supporting argument: method is simple, well-motivated, and empirically effective across multiple tasks.
- Feedback: verification procedure needs clearer explanation and more detail on constructing verification sets.
- Feedback: paper should more explicitly discuss limitations, e.g., scalability to larger input domains (sorting MNIST digits).
- Feedback: suggest more concrete future work directions—applying to complex tasks, improving verification efficiency, integrating with other architectures.
- Question: how to extend the approach to larger input domains such as sorting MNIST digits?
- Question: request more details on constructing the verification set and applying the verification procedure to different tasks.
- Question: how would the approach perform on more complex tasks like program synthesis or natural language processing?
- Question: please discuss limitations and potential failure cases of the approach.
