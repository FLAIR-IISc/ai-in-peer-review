- Paper proposes using recursion in neural programming architectures to improve generalization by training on recursive traces.
- Authors claim recursive training yields perfect generalization across tasks like addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.
- Provides a formal proof that the learned recursive algorithms generalize perfectly.
- Decision: Accept.
- Strong motivation: clearly argues limitations of existing neural program architectures and benefits of recursion.
- Shows significant improvement in generalization over the original NPI baseline.
- Presents strong empirical results across multiple tasks.
- Recursion enables provable generalization guarantees.
- Demonstrates improved sample complexity and generalization empirically.
- Introduces new tasks (topological sort and quicksort) to showcase applicability.
- Suggestion: clarify the mechanisms linking recursion to improved generalization.
- Suggestion: evaluate the approach on other neural programming architectures (e.g., NTM, DNC).
- Suggestion: provide more detailed description of the verification procedure and verification set construction.
- Questions for authors: describe implementation challenges for recursive NPI, plans to extend to more complex tasks, and deeper insights into recursion–generalization relationship.
