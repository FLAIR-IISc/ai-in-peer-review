**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper "Making Neural Programming Architectures Generalize via Recursion" proposes incorporating recursion into neural programming architectures to improve their generalization capabilities. The authors argue that recursion is a crucial concept in programming languages and can be used to reduce the complexity of programs, making it easier for neural networks to learn and generalize. They demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach by modifying the Neural Programmer-Interpreter (NPI) framework to learn recursive programs and achieving perfect generalization on four tasks: grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.

**Decision: Accept**

I recommend accepting this paper for several reasons:

1. **Well-motivated approach**: The authors provide a clear and compelling argument for why recursion is necessary for neural programs to generalize. They also demonstrate a good understanding of the limitations of current neural programming architectures.
2. **Strong empirical results**: The authors present impressive empirical results on four tasks, showing that their approach can achieve perfect generalization with a small number of training examples. They also provide a thorough analysis of the results, including a comparison with non-recursive versions of the tasks.

**Supporting Arguments for the Decision**

1. **Novelty of the approach**: The authors propose a new approach to neural programming that incorporates recursion, which is a significant departure from current methods. This novelty is a major strength of the paper.
2. **Thorough evaluation**: The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, including a comparison with non-recursive versions of the tasks. This evaluation helps to establish the effectiveness of their approach.
3. **Clarity of the writing**: The authors' writing is clear and concise, making it easy to follow their arguments and understand their approach.

**Additional Feedback to Improve the Paper**

1. **More discussion of limitations**: While the authors do discuss some limitations of their approach, they could provide more detail on the challenges of incorporating recursion into neural programming architectures.
2. **More analysis of the verification procedure**: The authors propose a verification procedure to prove the correctness of their learned recursive programs. They could provide more analysis of this procedure, including its limitations and potential applications.
3. **More discussion of future work**: The authors mention several potential directions for future work, including applying their approach to more complex tasks and exploring new ways to train recursive neural programs. They could provide more detail on these directions and how they plan to pursue them.
