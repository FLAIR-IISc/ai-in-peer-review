**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

This paper introduces a novel approach to improving the generalization of Neural Programming Interpreters (NPI) by incorporating recursion. The authors demonstrate that recursion enables NPI to learn more robust and interpretable programs from a small number of training examples. They show that recursion helps to reduce the complexity of problems, making it easier to reason about the behavior of the learned program. The paper also presents a verification procedure to prove the perfect generalization of the learned recursive programs.
