- Proposes incorporating recursion into neural programming (NPI) to improve generalization and interpretability.  
- Modifies NPI training to include recursive calls and adds a verification procedure to prove correctness.  
- Evaluated on four tasks: grade-school addition, bubble sort, topological sort, and quicksort.  
- Empirical results: recursive NPI models generalize perfectly to unseen inputs while non-recursive models fail.  
- Novel and significant idea: recursion as a fresh abstraction for neural programs.  
- Paper is well-motivated with clear explanation of limitations of current neural programming architectures.  
- Provides thorough analysis including a verification procedure for learned recursive programs.  
- Concern: approach may not scale to more complex tasks or larger input domains.  
- Concern: verification procedure may be infeasible or unscalable for complex tasks; proposed alternative’s scalability is unclear.  
- Question: how will authors address scalability for more complex tasks or larger input domains?  
- Question: provide more details and scalability analysis of the alternative verification-set construction.  
- Question: how to extend the approach to other neural programming architectures (e.g., NTM, DNC)?  
- Minor: paper needs a more detailed explanation of the NPI framework and its recursion modifications.  
- Minor: provide more training details (number of iterations, learning rate).  
- Minor: include a more detailed analysis of time and space complexity.
