- Main result: Empirical study showing SGD with smaller batch sizes converges to flatter minima, and flatter minima yield better generalization.  
- Novelty: Limited methodological novelty, but the study is valuable for illuminating generalization in deep networks.  
- Theoretical impact: Results suggest which assumptions are legitimate for developing new theories about generalization.  
- Practical impact: Findings can be used heuristically to design algorithms that improve generalization by manipulating mini-batch sizes.  
- Earlier concern: Reviewer questioned correctness of a claimed property (scale invariance) of the proposed sharpness criterion.  
- Resolution: Authors removed the scale-invariance claim in the revision, resolving the reviewer’s concern.
