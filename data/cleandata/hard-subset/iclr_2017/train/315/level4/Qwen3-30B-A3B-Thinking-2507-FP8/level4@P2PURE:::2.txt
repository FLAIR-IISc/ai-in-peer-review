

The paper presents an empirical investigation supporting two key claims: (1) SGD with smaller batch sizes converges to flatter minima, and (2) flatter minima exhibit superior generalization ability.  

Pros and Cons:  
While the paper offers limited novelty, its empirical insights provide significant value in addressing fundamental questions regarding the generalization properties of deep neural networks.  

Significance:  
These findings could impact both theory and practice: theoretically, by suggesting which assumptions are valid for real-world scenarios to inform new theoretical frameworks; practically, by enabling heuristic algorithmic improvements through strategic adjustment of mini-batch sizes to enhance generalization.  

Comments:  
I initially questioned the authors' claim regarding the scale invariance of their proposed sharpness criterion; this concern has been resolved as the claim was removed from the revised manuscript.
