- Paper analyzes transition between Large-Batch (LB) and Small-Batch (SB) regimes, showing LB often yields worse generalization.  
- Presents a clear theoretical and empirical framework attributing the gap to sharp minima and optimization trajectories.  
- Experimental results are thorough, well-designed, and convincingly support the core thesis.  
- Writing is clear and accessible, with effective illustrative figures.  
- Primary strength: rigorous characterization of the LB–SB performance gap and its connection to loss landscape geometry.  
- Successfully bridges theoretical insights with practical validation, likely serving as a foundational reference.  
- Reviewer suggests exploring a mitigation: inject controlled noise (e.g., rescaled Gaussian) into model parameters during LB training to emulate SB regularization.  
- Recommended evaluations: (1) quantify noise scale needed to match SB performance, (2) analyze how noise interacts with the optimization dynamics in Section 3, (3) extend experiments to additional architectures/datasets.  
- This extension is feasible, would reconcile LB computational efficiency with SB generalization, and make the work more prescriptive.  
- Overall verdict: strong paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to incorporate the suggested noise-based analysis.
