- Paper empirically tests two claims: (1) smaller SGD batch sizes lead to flatter minima, and (2) flatter minima generalize better.  
- Novelty is modest, but the empirical rigor provides valuable insights into generalization in deep learning.  
- The study empirically validates the link between batch size, solution flatness, and generalization, with implications for theory and practice.  
- Practical takeaway: adjusting mini-batch size is a simple heuristic to potentially improve generalization.  
- Reviewer’s prior concern about the sharpness criterion being scale-invariant was addressed: the authors withdrew that claim.  
- Writing sometimes lacks precision; terminology (e.g., “flatter minima”) should be used consistently and tied to the specific sharpness metric.  
- Experimental design is sound, but the paper should better discuss why smaller batches yield flatter minima and address confounders (learning rate schedules, weight decay).  
- Overall assessment: well-executed empirical analysis, incremental but useful; suitable for publication after minor revisions to sharpen technical exposition.
