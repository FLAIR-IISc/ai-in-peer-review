- Empirical study of how SGD batch size relates to minima flatness and generalization in deep networks.  
- Finds smaller batch sizes converge to flatter minima.  
- Shows flatter minima yield better generalization performance.  
- Limited theoretical novelty but a strong and valuable empirical contribution.  
- Results matter both theoretically (inform reasonable assumptions) and practically (suggest mini-batch heuristics to improve generalization).  
- Prior concern about scale invariance addressed: the scale-invariance claim in the sharpness criterion was removed.
