- Clearly explains why large-batch SGD often fails to achieve optimal convergence and generalization.  
- Shows that increasing batch size reduces gradient-noise, which is empirically linked to poorer generalization.  
- Provides strong experimental evidence across architectures and datasets, notably validation accuracy decay with batch size.  
- Presents well-supported analysis that large batches bias optimization toward sharp minima, while small batches favor flatter minima.  
- Lacks a nuanced mechanism discussion of how batch-size-induced noise reduction interacts with loss-landscape geometry.  
- Recommends explicit curvature/Hessian or sharpness metrics and discussion of escape dynamics from minima/saddles; cite related work (e.g., Zhang et al.).  
- Advises quantifying practical trade-offs (accuracy vs training speed/hardware), e.g., impact of reducing batch size from 1024 to 128 and associated cost.  
- Writing is generally clear with minor phrasing issues corrected in revision.  
- Figures effectively support claims, especially Figure 3 (validation accuracy vs batch size).  
- Overall a strong, timely paper with robust evidence; recommend acceptance pending minor theoretical and practical refinements.
