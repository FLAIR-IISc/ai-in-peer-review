

The paper presents an empirical investigation demonstrating that: 1) SGD with smaller batch sizes converges to flatter minima, and 2) flatter minima exhibit superior generalization capability.  

Pros and Cons:  
While the paper offers limited novelty, its value lies in significantly illuminating key questions regarding the generalization behavior of deep networks.  

Significance:  
These findings could substantially influence both theoretical development—by identifying plausible assumptions for real-world scenarios in future theory construction—and practical applications—by enabling heuristic algorithm design that leverages mini-batch size manipulation to enhance generalization.  

Comments:  
Initial concerns regarding the accuracy of the authors' claim about scale invariance in their sharpness criterion have been resolved; the claim was omitted in the revised manuscript.
