

The paper presents a well-motivated contribution addressing batch size effects in deep learning optimization. While the core methodology is sound and the empirical results are compelling, the paper would be strengthened by incorporating additional investigations. Specifically, introducing rescaled Gaussian noise to gradients during the large-batch (LB) regime could enable the model to achieve benefits typically associated with the small-batch (SB) regime, potentially bridging the performance gap between these settings. This modification warrants exploration as it may offer a practical path to leveraging SB advantages without incurring the computational costs of small batches.
