

The paper presents an interesting and potentially useful contribution. To further strengthen the work, the authors could investigate adding rescaled Gaussian noise to gradients during the LB regime; this approach may enable the model to achieve benefits typically associated with the SB regime. The current analysis would be significantly enhanced by experimental validation of this hypothesis, as it directly addresses a key limitation in the current methodology. Specifically, demonstrating that such noise injection bridges the performance gap between the two regimes would provide compelling evidence for the proposed mechanism. The authors should consider including these experiments in a revision, as they would substantially clarify the relationship between batch size and gradient dynamics. Without this validation, the claim that the SB regime offers superior benefits remains partially unproven within the LB context. The suggested modification is both theoretically sound and practically feasible, requiring only minor adjustments to the training procedure. This would offer a valuable extension to the paper's core findings and provide a more complete understanding of the underlying optimization dynamics. The authors' current results are promising but would gain considerably more impact with this additional empirical support.
