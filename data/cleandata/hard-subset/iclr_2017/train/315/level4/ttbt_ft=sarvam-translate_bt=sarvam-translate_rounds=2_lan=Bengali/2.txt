This paper presents an empirical study demonstrating: 1. that SDG converges with small batch sizes in Flatter Minima, and 2. that Flatter Minima has good generalization capabilities.

Advantages and Disadvantages:
Although this paper contains very little novelty, I believe this work is extremely valuable for shedding light on some interesting questions related to the generalization of deep networks.

Importance:
I believe results like this can have implications in both theory and practice. It can be useful for proposing what assumptions are valid in real-world situations for creating new theories, or for using itheuristically for developing new algorithms with generalizations, such as smart manipulation of mini-batch sizes.

Comments:
I had some concerns about a claim made by the authors that had been resolved. They claimed that their proposed sharpness criterion was scale invariance. They resolved this by removing this claim in the revised version.
