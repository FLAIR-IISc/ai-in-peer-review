This paper is an empirical study to justify the following: 1. SDG converges to a flat minimum with a small batch size, 2. The flat minimum has better generalization capacity.

Pros and cons:
Although this paper lacks novelty, I think it is very valuable in shedding light on some interesting questions surrounding deep network generalization.

Importance:
I think results like this can have an impact on both theory and practice, respectively, by suggesting which assumptions are valid for real scenarios to create new theories, or can be empirically used to develop new algorithms with generalization through smart manipulation of mini-batch sizes.

Notes:
Initially, I had some concerns about the accuracy of the claim made by the authors, which have now been resolved. They claimed that their proposed sharpness criterion is scale invariance. They addressed this by removing the claim in the revised version.
