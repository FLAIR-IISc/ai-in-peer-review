This paper presents an empirical investigation supporting the connection between Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) with small batch sizes, convergence to flatter minima, and improved generalization performance in deep networks.

While the work doesn’t introduce substantial novelty, it provides valuable insights into the ongoing discussion surrounding generalization in deep learning. 

The findings have the potential to impact both theoretical development and practical applications. Theoretically, these results can inform the assumptions underlying new generalization theories. Practically, they offer a heuristic for algorithm design, suggesting that manipulating mini-batch sizes could be a useful strategy for improving generalization.

I previously raised a concern regarding the authors’ claim of scale invariance for their proposed sharpness criterion. This has been appropriately addressed in the revised manuscript through the removal of that claim.
