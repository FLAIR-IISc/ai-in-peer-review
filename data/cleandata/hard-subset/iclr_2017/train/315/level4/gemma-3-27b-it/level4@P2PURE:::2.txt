This paper presents an empirical investigation supporting the hypothesis that smaller batch sizes in stochastic gradient descent (SGD) lead to convergence at flatter minima, and that flatter minima correlate with improved generalization performance. 

Regarding strengths and weaknesses, while the paper’s approach lacks substantial novelty, I believe the work provides valuable insights into the ongoing discussion surrounding generalization in deep networks.

The findings have the potential to impact both theoretical understanding and practical applications. Theoretically, these results may inform the assumptions underlying new generalization theories by providing evidence relevant to real-world scenarios. Practically, they suggest a heuristic approach to developing new algorithms—improving generalization through careful manipulation of mini-batch sizes.

I previously raised a concern regarding the authors’ claim of scale invariance for their proposed sharpness criterion. This concern has been addressed in the revised version through the removal of this claim.
