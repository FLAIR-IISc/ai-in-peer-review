- Paper studies LB vs SB training, identifying sharp minima for LB and flatter minima for SB, and proposes adding gradient noise to LB to bridge this gap.  
- Core idea: gradient noise injection in LB induces SB-like behavior and improves generalization.  
- Experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and ImageNet with MLPs and CNNs show consistent generalization improvements, often matching or exceeding SB.  
- Ablation studies exist but are limited; they show sensitivity to the noise scaling factor.  
- Theoretical justification for why noise improves generalization is weak and underspecified.  
- Claim of finding flatter minima needs stronger, more direct analysis (e.g., loss landscape visualizations following Keskar et al.).  
- Noise scaling is chosen empirically; a principled or adaptive selection method is needed.  
- Suggest exploring other noise distributions (e.g., uniform, gradient-correlated) and adaptive noise schedules.  
- Lacks detailed comparison to existing LB optimization methods (e.g., LARS, layer-wise adaptive rates); include performance, cost, and implementation comparisons.  
- Notation clarity issues: define all symbols before first use.  
- Provide more comprehensive theoretical intuition (even preliminary) to strengthen the contribution.  
- Overall assessment: promising and solid contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen theory and related-work comparisons.  
- Reviewer confidence: 7/10.
