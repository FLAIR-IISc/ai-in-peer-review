This paper presents an empirical investigation supporting the connection between Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) with small batch sizes, the convergence to flatter minima, and improved generalization performance in deep networks. 

While the work does not introduce substantial novelty, it provides valuable insights into the ongoing discussion surrounding generalization in deep learning.

The results have the potential to impact both theoretical understanding and practical applications. Theoretically, these findings can inform the validity of assumptions used in developing new generalization theories. Practically, they offer a heuristic approach for algorithm design, suggesting that manipulating mini-batch sizes could improve generalization capabilities.

I previously raised a concern regarding the authors’ claim of scale invariance for their proposed sharpness criterion. This has been appropriately addressed in the revised version through the removal of this claim.
