- Paper studies how large-batch training yields sharper minima and worse generalization than small-batch training, attributing this to gradient-noise differences.  
- Decision: Reject due to limited novelty and insufficiently strong evidence.  
- Main weakness: largely observational study establishing correlation but not causation between batch size, sharpness, and generalization.  
- The proposed sharpness metric is imperfect and its direct link to generalization is unproven.  
- Experiments do not convincingly explain why large-batch methods converge to sharper minima (no mechanistic account).  
- Remedies tested (data augmentation, conservative training, robust optimization) show limited success and lack deep analysis of failure modes.  
- Appendix C’s idealized performance model feels tangential and does not substantively support the core claims.  
- Suggestion: strengthen theoretical grounding and relate empirical findings to established theory on sharp/flat minima.  
- Suggestion: provide rigorous justification and comparisons for the sharpness metric, or alternative measures.  
- Suggestion: attempt to explain mechanistically why large batches favor sharper minima (loss landscape vs optimization dynamics).  
- Suggestion: analyze why remedies fail, including parameter tuning and ablation studies across architectures/datasets.  
- Suggestion: expand related-work discussion to include more recent studies on batch size and generalization.  
- Suggestion: add statistical significance testing beyond reporting means and standard deviations.  
- Question: rationale and sensitivity analysis for choosing subspace dimensionality p=100 in the sharpness/Hessian approximation.  
- Question: can authors propose mechanisms beyond noise explaining the sharpness–generalization link?  
- Question: what parameter-tuning and ablation procedures were used for the attempted remedies?  
- Question: how do findings relate to loss-landscape concepts like mode connectivity and barrier heights?  
- Question: were experiments run with other optimizers (e.g., SGD with momentum) and did results hold across optimizers?
