- Paper provides valuable insight into why large-batch gradient descent underperforms.
- Main finding: large batches converge to sharp minima while small batches find flat minima, and flatness correlates with better generalization.
- Well-motivated, clearly written, and experimentally convincing.
- Strong ablation studies that systematically explore learning rate scaling and optimizer choice.
- Effective loss-landscape visualizations that clearly illustrate sharp vs. flat minima.
- Limitation: experiments are limited to CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 and may not generalize to other datasets.
- Limitation: evaluation focuses on fully connected and convolutional networks; transformers and recurrent models are not studied.
- Findings have practical implications for training deep neural networks.
- Overall assessment: strong submission; recommend acceptance.
