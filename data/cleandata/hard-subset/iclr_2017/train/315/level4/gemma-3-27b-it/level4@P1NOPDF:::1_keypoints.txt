- Investigates why large-batch gradient descent often performs poorly, attributing it to convergence to sharp minima that generalize poorly.
- Provides empirical evidence across datasets and architectures linking batch size, sharpness of minima, and generalization performance.
- Proposes Sharpness-Aware Minimization (SAM) to explicitly minimize local loss sharpness and mitigate large-batch issues.
- Insightful analysis of the optimization landscape with helpful loss-landscape visualizations.
- SAM shows consistent generalization improvements across different datasets and architectures.
- Related work discussion is insufficient; lacks comprehensive review of second-order or landscape-flattening techniques.
- Computational cost of SAM is a concern; paper lacks detailed timing or scaling analysis for large models.
- Missing ablation study on SAM hyperparameter sensitivity, especially the sharpness parameter rho.
- Experiments rely mainly on Adam; should explore other optimizers combined with SAM.
- Variability in improvement magnitude across datasets needs discussion to explain when SAM helps most.
- Minor: notation inconsistencies (rho defined differently in places).
- Minor: some figures need clearer labels and larger font sizes.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with practical method; recommend acceptance.
