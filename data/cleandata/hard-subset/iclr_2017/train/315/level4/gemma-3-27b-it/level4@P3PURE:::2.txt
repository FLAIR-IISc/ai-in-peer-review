This paper presents an empirical investigation supporting the hypothesis that stochastic gradient descent (SGD) with smaller batch sizes converges to flatter minima, and that flatter minima exhibit better generalization performance.

**Pros and Cons:** While the paper introduces limited novelty, I believe the work provides valuable insights into important questions surrounding generalization in deep neural networks. 

**Significance:** The results have the potential to impact both theoretical and practical research. Theoretically, they can inform the assumptions underlying new generalization theories by grounding them in observations from realistic training scenarios. Practically, the findings could serve as a heuristic for developing new algorithms that promote generalization through informed manipulation of mini-batch sizes.

**Comments:** I previously raised a concern regarding the authors’ claim of scale invariance for their proposed sharpness criterion. This concern has been addressed in the revised version through the removal of that claim, which I appreciate.
