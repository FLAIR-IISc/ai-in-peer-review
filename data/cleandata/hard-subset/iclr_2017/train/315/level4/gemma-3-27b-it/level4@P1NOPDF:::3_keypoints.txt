- Paper is interesting, potentially useful, and addresses an important question about SGD behavior in non-convex optimization.
- Study investigates interplay between local and global optimization in SGD, focusing on benefits of sharp vs. broad minima.
- Theoretical analysis explains why large-batch (LB) and small-batch (SB) SGD exhibit contrasting behaviors.
- Experiments generally support the theoretical findings.
- Key empirical finding: LB SGD tends to converge to sharp minima, while SB SGD favors broader minima.
- Broader minima are hypothesized to generalize better; analysis of loss landscape and curvature around stationary points is a strong point.
- Main concern: limited exploration of hybrid strategies to combine LB and SB strengths.
- Suggestion: add rescaled Gaussian gradient noise during LB regime to mimic SB advantages and improve generalization/escape bad minima.
- Other suggested avenues: adaptive batch size strategies and integrating sharpness-aware minimization (SAM) into LB.
- Experimental evaluation should be more comprehensive (more datasets and architectures, e.g., ImageNet).
- Recommend quantifying sharpness (e.g., Hessian largest eigenvalue) to validate theoretical claims directly.
- Writing is generally clear but some mathematical derivations are dense and could use clearer, more accessible explanations.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions if suggested extensions and clarifications are addressed.
