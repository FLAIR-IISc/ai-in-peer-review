- Empirical study of how small-batch SGD relates to flatness of minima and generalization in deep nets.
- Tests the hypothesis that smaller batch sizes lead to flatter minima and improved generalization.
- Little algorithmic novelty, but provides valuable empirical insights into generalization.
- Experiments add evidence linking minima flatness to better generalization and offer practical perspective.
- Potentially high impact: informs theoretical assumptions and suggests batch-size manipulation as a practical heuristic.
- Authors removed an earlier (incorrect) claim of scale invariance for their sharpness criterion in response to reviewer feedback.
- Overall a solid, clearly presented empirical paper with thorough experiments.
