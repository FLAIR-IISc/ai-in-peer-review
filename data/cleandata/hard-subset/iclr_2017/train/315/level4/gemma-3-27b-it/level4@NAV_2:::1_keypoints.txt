- Paper attributes the large-batch generalization gap to convergence to sharper minima and provides numerical evidence linking batch size, sharpness, and generalization.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection due to lack of deeper theoretical grounding and remedies that are not demonstrably effective; causality is not firmly established.  
- Strong empirical demonstration that large-batch methods yield sharper minima associated with worse generalization, using parametric plots and a sharpness metric across multiple architectures/datasets.  
- Heavy reliance on empirical results; a preliminary theoretical analysis would strengthen the claims.  
- Proposed remedies (data augmentation, conservative training, robust optimization) showed limited success and were not thoroughly investigated; the claim they “do not correct the problem” is too strong.  
- Open question: sensitivity of results to optimizer choice (ADAM used exclusively).  
- Open question: how the proposed sharpness metric correlates with the Hessian spectrum; a detailed eigenvalue analysis is needed.  
- Open question: quantify the dimensionality of the subspace along which minima are sharp.  
- Open question: further experimental investigation of dynamic batch size adjustment is warranted.
