- Paper empirically shows SGD with smaller batch sizes converges to flatter minima and that flatter minima correlate with better generalization.
- Limited novelty but valuable empirical insight into generalization of deep networks.
- Results could inform theoretical assumptions and practical heuristics for algorithm design (batch-size manipulation).
- Authors removed an earlier claim of scale invariance for their sharpness criterion in the revision.
- Experiments are well-designed across multiple datasets (e.g., MNIST, CIFAR-10) and architectures (CNNs, MLPs), strengthening conclusions.
- Proposed sharpness metric is well-motivated, clearly explained, and supported by compelling visualizations.
- Consistent correlation observed between smaller batches, flatter loss landscapes, and improved generalization; robustness would benefit from error bars/statistical tests.
- More thorough comparison to prior work (e.g., PAC-Bayes, information bottleneck) is needed to position the sharpness criterion relative to other generalization measures.
- Limitations should be acknowledged: correlation versus causation, potential confounding factors, and increased computational cost of very small batch training.
- Writing is generally clear but could be more concise; recommend streamlining the introduction and adding a summary table of key results.
- Overall assessment: solid empirical study with recommendation for acceptance subject to addressing the minor comments.
