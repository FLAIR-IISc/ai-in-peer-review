- Study links smaller SGD batch sizes to convergence to flatter minima and improved generalization.
- Central hypothesis has limited novelty but provides valuable empirical evidence.
- Experiments are reasonably thorough across datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10) and architectures (MLP, CNN).
- Uses a quantitative sharpness metric to evaluate flatness and reinforces the geometry–generalization connection.
- The findings can inform theoretical assumptions about deep network training dynamics.
- Practical implication: tuning mini-batch size could be a heuristic to improve generalization; sharpness is a practical assessment tool.
- Authors addressed a reviewer concern by removing an unsupported claim of scale invariance, improving credibility.
- Paper needs more analysis of sensitivity to other hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate, optimizer).
- Causality is not established; potential confounders like noise-induced regularization from small batches should be discussed.
- Recommend comparing the proposed sharpness metric to other flatness measures (Hessian eigenvalues, participation ratio).
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address hyperparameter sensitivity, confounding factors, and metric comparisons.
