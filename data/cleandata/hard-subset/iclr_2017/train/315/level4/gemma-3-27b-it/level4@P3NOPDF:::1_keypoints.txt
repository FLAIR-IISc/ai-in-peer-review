- Paper shows large-batch training tends to converge to sharp minima, which correlates with degraded generalization performance.  
- Strong empirical evidence across datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10, ImageNet) and architectures (MLP, CNN, ResNet).  
- Visualizations of the loss landscape and perturbation analysis effectively quantify and illustrate sharpness differences.  
- Theoretical analysis links sharpness and loss-curvature to generalization, connecting theory to empirical findings.  
- Experiments include different optimizers (SGD, Adam) and ablations on learning-rate scaling, showing LR tweaks don’t fully fix the gap.  
- Comparison to related work (batch normalization, learning-rate warm-up) provides useful context and differentiation.  
- Weakness: magnitude of the generalization gap varies by dataset/architecture; paper should discuss factors affecting when the gap is most problematic.  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis depends on loss-landscape assumptions that may not always hold; more discussion of limitations and alternative frameworks is needed.  
- Open question: precise mechanisms linking sharpness to generalization remain unresolved; future theoretical work should be outlined.  
- Presentation could be clearer—some sections are dense and need more intuitive explanations (e.g., perturbation analysis and Hessian connection).  
- Overall: strong, well-validated contribution; recommended for acceptance (confidence 8/10).
