- Paper studies how batch size, gradient noise, and generalization interact, distinguishing small-batch (SB) and large-batch (LB) regimes.  
- SB regime: high gradient noise helps find wide, generalizable minima.  
- LB regime: reduced noise can lead to sharper minima and poorer generalization.  
- Timely and valuable contribution for scaling training without losing model quality.  
- Exposition is clear and provides a useful conceptual framework (SB vs LB).  
- Empirical results across standard benchmarks support the central claims.  
- Theoretical grounding links gradient noise, loss landscape geometry, and generalization.  
- Main weakness: paper diagnoses the problem but does not test the implied solution of replicating SB noise in LB training.  
- Missing experiment: inject rescaled zero-mean Gaussian noise into LB gradients to match SB variance and test if generalization recovers.  
- Missing analysis: study how injected noise scale affects performance, how it should scale with batch size, and whether annealing is beneficial.  
- Missing comparison: contrast explicit gradient noise with other regularizers (weight decay, dropout) in LB settings.  
- Conducting these experiments would validate the causal role of gradient noise and provide a practical solution, increasing the paper’s impact.
