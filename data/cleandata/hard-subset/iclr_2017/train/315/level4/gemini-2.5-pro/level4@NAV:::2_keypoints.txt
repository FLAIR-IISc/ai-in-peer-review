- Empirical study tests two hypotheses: smaller SGD batch sizes lead to flatter minima, and flatter minima yield better generalization.
- Novelty is limited, but the study adds significant value by illuminating important questions about deep network generalization.
- Findings are potentially significant for theory by suggesting realistic assumptions and for practice as a heuristic for algorithm design via batch-size manipulation.
- Practical impact: results could guide development of algorithms that improve generalization through intelligent mini-batch sizing.
- Initial concern about the claimed scale invariance of the sharpness criterion was resolved; authors removed that claim in the revision.
