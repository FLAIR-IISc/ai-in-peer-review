- Paper offers a valuable, thought-provoking analysis of optimization dynamics with interesting, useful core ideas.  
- Draws an insightful distinction between large-batch (LB) and small-batch (SB) training regimes.  
- Conclusions would be strengthened by deeper investigation into the relationship between LB and SB regimes.  
- Authors argue SB advantages are often attributed to inherent gradient noise.  
- Suggests an experiment: add appropriately rescaled Gaussian noise to gradients during LB training.  
- Key question: can injected noise make LB recover the beneficial dynamics and performance of SB?  
- Successfully showing this would provide causal evidence that gradient noise differentiates the regimes and make the contribution more robust.
