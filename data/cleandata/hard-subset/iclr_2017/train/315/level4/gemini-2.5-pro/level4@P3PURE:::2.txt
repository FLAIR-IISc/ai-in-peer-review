This paper presents an empirical study that provides evidence for two related hypotheses: first, that SGD with small batch sizes tends to converge to flatter minima, and second, that these flatter minima correspond to solutions with better generalization performance.

While the paper's contributions are primarily empirical with limited theoretical novelty, the work is valuable for the insights it provides into important questions surrounding the generalization of deep networks. The results have the potential to impact both theory and practice. Theoretically, these findings can inform the development of more realistic assumptions for future analyses of deep learning models. Practically, they could heuristically guide the development of new algorithms that improve generalization by, for example, adaptively manipulating mini-batch sizes.

My initial concerns regarding a claim made by the authors have been satisfactorily addressed. The authors had previously claimed their proposed sharpness criterion was scale-invariant; this claim has been removed in the revised version, resolving the issue.
