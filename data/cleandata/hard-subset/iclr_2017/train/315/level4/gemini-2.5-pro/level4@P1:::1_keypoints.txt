- Decision: Accept — reviewer recommends clear acceptance based on the paper’s contributions and evidence.
- Central claim: Large-batch (LB) training finds sharp minima while small-batch (SB) training finds flatter minima, explaining LB’s generalization gap.
- Empirical strength: Robust experiments across six network configurations, multiple datasets, and architectures (fully-connected and convolutional) support the claim.
- Visualization & metric: 1D parametric plots effectively illustrate basin differences; the proposed sharpness metric is a practical heuristic showing LB solutions are 1–2 orders of magnitude sharper.
- Warm-starting result: Initializing LB from SB iterates shows LB can reach flat minima, implying SB noise aids exploration and escape from sharp basins.
- Impact: Addresses a high-impact bottleneck for scaling and parallelizing deep learning training.
- Sharpness metric caveat: Need clarification on the metric’s definition and sensitivity to solving the maximization inexactly (only 10 L-BFGS-B steps).
- Alternative curvature validation: Suggest using Hessian-free methods (e.g., Lanczos on Hessian-vector products) or finite differences to estimate largest eigenvalues as cross-validation.
- Optimizer generality: Experiments rely mainly on ADAM; explicit results for non-adaptive optimizers (e.g., SGD with momentum) would strengthen conclusions.
- Remedies preliminary: Appendix E’s attempts to improve LB methods (e.g., adversarial training) feel preliminary and may depend on implementation/hyperparameters.
- Question on constraint C: Request motivation for the unusual term (|(A+x)i|+1) versus standard l_inf or l_2 norm balls.
- Question on high-curvature subspace: Have the authors characterized the small-dimensional directions of high curvature (e.g., layer-wise attribution)?
- Question on batch schedule remedy: Have experiments been run with a curriculum of gradually increasing batch size, and how do those compare to pure SB and LB in accuracy and training time?
