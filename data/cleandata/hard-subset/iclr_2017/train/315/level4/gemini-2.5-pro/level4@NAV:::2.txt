This paper conducts an empirical study to validate two hypotheses: 1) Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) with smaller batch sizes converges to flatter minima, and 2) such flatter minima exhibit better generalization capabilities.

**Pros and Cons:**
While the novelty of the paper is limited, the work provides significant value by illuminating interesting questions related to the generalization of deep networks.

**Significance:**
These findings have the potential to impact both theory and practice. Theoretically, they can inform the development of new frameworks by suggesting realistic assumptions for practical scenarios. Practically, they could serve as a heuristic for developing new algorithms that improve generalization through the intelligent manipulation of mini-batch sizes.

**Comments:**
My initial concern about the correctness of the authors' claim regarding the scale invariance of their proposed sharpness criterion has been resolved. The authors have addressed this issue by removing the claim in the revised manuscript.
