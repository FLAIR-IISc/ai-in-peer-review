- Paper presents a comprehensive empirical study linking mini-batch size, solution sharpness, and generalization in deep networks.
- Provides strong evidence for hypothesis (1): smaller SGD batch sizes tend to converge to flatter, wider minima.
- Provides strong evidence for hypothesis (2): flatter minima are strongly correlated with better generalization performance.
- Work is empirical rather than theoretical but offers large-scale, systematic validation of commonly observed phenomena.
- Results offer empirical grounding for theorists to develop more realistic generalization bounds and theories focused on loss-surface geometry.
- Findings give practitioners a clear rationale for using small batch sizes and motivate optimizers that seek wider valleys (e.g., adaptive batch-size schemes).
- The authors addressed a previous concern by removing an incorrect claim about the scale-invariance of their sharpness metric, improving correctness.
- Overall conclusion: a valuable contribution that bridges empirical observation and theory, providing robust evidence and a foundation for future work.
