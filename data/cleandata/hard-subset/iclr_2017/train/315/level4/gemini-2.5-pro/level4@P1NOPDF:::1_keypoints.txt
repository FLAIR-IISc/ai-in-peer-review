- Investigates why large-batch training generalizes worse, arguing large batches converge to sharp minima while small-batch SGD noise favors flat minima with better generalization.  
- Supports the claim with comprehensive experiments and loss-landscape visualizations across CIFAR-10/100, ImageNet, and modern architectures.  
- Clear, focused hypothesis that provides an intuitive explanation for a common empirical observation.  
- Empirical rigor: thorough and convincing validation across datasets and models.  
- Timely and relevant for understanding trade-offs in large-scale/parallel training.  
- Concern: the paper’s central notion of “sharpness” is scale-dependent and can be changed by reparameterization; needs discussion of this limitation or scale-invariant alternatives.  
- Concern: strong correlation shown but causation is unclear—sharpness may be a symptom rather than the root cause (e.g., lack of exploratory noise in large-batch optimizers).  
- Practical gap: diagnosis is strong but prescriptive solutions are limited; the paper could propose algorithms or regularizers to enable large-batch training that finds flat minima.  
- Overall: well-written and well-executed study; addressing sharpness definition and pathways to methods would increase impact.
