This paper presents an empirical study investigating two related hypotheses: first, that SGD with smaller batch sizes converges to flatter minima, and second, that these flatter minima exhibit better generalization.

While the paper's core ideas may not be entirely novel, its value lies in providing strong empirical evidence that illuminates key questions about the generalization of deep networks. The results could have a significant impact on both theory and practice. Theoretically, they can help inform which assumptions are legitimate when building new frameworks for realistic scenarios. Practically, these findings could inspire new algorithms that improve generalization by strategically manipulating mini-batch sizes.

My initial concern regarding the authors' claim that their proposed sharpness criterion is scale-invariant has been addressed. The authors have removed this claim in the revised manuscript, resolving the issue.
