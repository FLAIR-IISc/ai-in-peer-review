- Empirical study of the generalization gap between small-batch (SB) and large-batch (LB) training in deep learning.  
- Central hypothesis: LB training converges to "sharp" minima while SB training (via gradient noise) finds "flat" minima that generalize better.  
- Strong empirical evidence through loss-landscape visualizations and a proposed sharpness metric quantifying minima sharpness.  
- Experiments are thorough, covering multiple architectures (variants of AlexNet and VGG) and datasets.  
- 1D parametric plots effectively illustrate SB vs. LB differences; sharpness metric shows LB solutions have much higher sharpness.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Reasons for acceptance: clear, rigorous empirical analysis of an important practical problem; significant implications for practitioners and theorists despite no new algorithm.  
- Potential impact: informs theory of loss-surface geometry and motivates new optimization heuristics (e.g., adaptive batch sizing, regularization to promote flatness).  
- Improvement noted: authors removed an earlier, problematic claim about scale-invariance of the sharpness metric, strengthening the manuscript.
