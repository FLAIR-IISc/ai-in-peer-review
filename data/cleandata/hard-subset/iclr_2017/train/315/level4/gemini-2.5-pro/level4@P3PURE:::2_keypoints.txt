- Empirical evidence that SGD with small batch sizes tends to converge to flatter minima.
- Demonstrates that flatter minima correspond to solutions with better generalization performance.
- Contributions are primarily empirical with limited theoretical novelty.
- The work is valuable for insights into deep network generalization and can influence both theory and practice.
- Theoretically, results can inform more realistic assumptions for future analyses of deep learning models.
- Practically, findings could guide algorithmic choices (e.g., adaptive mini-batch sizes) to improve generalization.
- Reviewer’s concern about a claimed scale-invariance of the sharpness criterion was addressed by the authors removing that claim.
