- Empirical support for two main claims: smaller SGD batch sizes converge to flatter minima, and flatter minima correspond to better generalization.
- Core ideas have limited novelty but the paper’s value lies in extensive empirical evidence that solidifies long-held intuitions.
- Results can influence theory by suggesting which loss-landscape assumptions hold in practice.
- Practical implications: insights could guide new optimization heuristics that manipulate mini-batch sizes to improve generalization.
