This paper presents an empirical investigation into two key assertions: first, that Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) with smaller batch sizes converges to flatter minima, and second, that these flatter minima exhibit superior generalization capabilities.

Pros and Cons:
While the paper offers limited novelty, its contribution to understanding the generalization properties of deep networks is substantial and noteworthy.

Significance:
The findings of this study have the potential to influence both theoretical and practical advancements. They can inform the development of new theories by identifying legitimate assumptions for real-world scenarios, and they can also be leveraged to create novel algorithms that enhance generalization through strategic manipulation of mini-batch sizes.

Comments:
Initially, I had reservations regarding the accuracy of the authors' claim that their proposed sharpness criterion was scale-invariant. However, this concern has been addressed in the revised version, as the authors have removed this claim, thereby resolving the issue.
