- Empirical study testing two claims: (1) smaller SGD batch sizes converge to flatter minima, and (2) flatter minima generalize better.
- Paper offers limited novelty but provides valuable empirical insight into deep network generalization.
- Results could substantially impact theory and practice by suggesting realistic assumptions and guiding algorithm design.
- Findings may be used heuristically to improve generalization via manipulation of mini-batch sizes.
- Reviewer initially raised a correctness concern about the authors' sharpness criterion being scale-invariant.
- Authors resolved the issue in the revision by removing the scale-invariance claim.
