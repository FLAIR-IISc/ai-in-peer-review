- Thorough analysis of large-batch gradient descent generalization, identifying sharp minima as a key reason for poorer performance.
- Proposes injecting noise into gradients to mimic small-batch behavior and mitigate sharp minima.
- Provides comprehensive theoretical analysis supported by empirical evidence showing the approach’s effectiveness.
- Paper is well-structured with clear and concise writing.
- Major concern: heavy reliance on the Gaussian noise assumption; robustness to other noise distributions is not explored.
- Major concern: lacks analysis of how noise injection affects algorithm convergence rate.
- Major concern: needs a more detailed comparison with existing methods aimed at improving large-batch generalization.
- Minor: introduction could be more engaging and emphasize practical implications.
- Minor: notation consistency (e.g., boldface for vectors/matrices) should be improved for readability.
- Minor: paper would benefit from additional visualizations to illustrate key concepts and results.
- Minor: conclusion should be more concise and summarize main findings and implications.
- Overall assessment: promising paper with valuable insights; rating 7/10 and would improve with the suggested revisions.
