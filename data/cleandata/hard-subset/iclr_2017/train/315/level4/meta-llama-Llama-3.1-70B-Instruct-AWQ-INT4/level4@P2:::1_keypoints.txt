- Paper studies why large-batch SGD degrades deep model generalization, attributing it to convergence behavior.
- Main finding: large-batch methods converge to sharp minimizers of training/test functions, harming generalization.
- Small-batch methods converge to flat minimizers, attributed to gradient estimation noise.
- Authors provide numerical evidence: 1D parametric plots and sharpness measures supporting the claim.
- Paper proposes remediation strategies: data augmentation, conservative training, and robust optimization.
- Manuscript is well-written, well-motivated, and appropriately situated in the literature.
- Experiments are thorough, well-designed, and clearly explained.
- Recommendation: accept — contribution is significant and insightful.
- Improvement needed: stronger theoretical analysis of large-batch convergence properties to support empirical results.
- Improvement needed: more experiments across additional architectures and datasets, with details on those extra experiments.
- Questions for authors: why do large batches favor sharp minima; how results vary with different optimizers/architectures; what is the computational cost and scaling of the sharpness measure?
