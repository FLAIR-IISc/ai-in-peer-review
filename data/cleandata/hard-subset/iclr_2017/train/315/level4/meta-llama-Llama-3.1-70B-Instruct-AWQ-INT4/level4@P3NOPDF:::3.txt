This paper presents an interesting and useful contribution to the field. The authors' exploration of the trade-offs between the Linear Bottleneck (LB) and Stochastic Bottleneck (SB) regimes is well-motivated and effectively analyzed. 

One potential avenue for further investigation could be to introduce rescaled Gaussian noise to gradients during the LB regime. This might allow the authors to leverage the advantages of the SB regime, such as improved generalization performance, while still maintaining the efficiency benefits of the LB regime. By exploring this hybrid approach, the authors may uncover additional insights into the relationship between these two regimes and further enhance the utility of their proposed method.

Overall, the paper is well-written and effectively conveys the authors' key findings. The experiments are thorough, and the results are clearly presented. However, incorporating additional investigations, such as the one suggested above, could strengthen the paper and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the topic.
