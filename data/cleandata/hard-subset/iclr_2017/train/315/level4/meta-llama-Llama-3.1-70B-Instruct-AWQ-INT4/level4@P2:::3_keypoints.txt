- Paper shows large-batch SGD tends to converge to sharp minimizers causing poorer generalization, while small-batch SGD converges to flat minimizers that generalize better.  
- Authors provide numerical evidence (parametric plots, sharpness metrics) supporting sharpness as the cause of the large-batch generalization gap.  
- Paper explores remedies for the gap including data augmentation, conservative training, and robust optimization.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance but with reservations due to missing analyses and evaluations.  
- Suggests exploring adding rescaled Gaussian noise to gradients in the large-batch regime to potentially combine benefits of both regimes.  
- Requests deeper analysis of the relationship between minimizer sharpness and actual generalization performance.  
- Calls for more thorough evaluation of proposed remedies, including hyperparameter tuning details and comparisons to existing methods.  
- Asks the authors to discuss implications of findings for design of models and optimization algorithms.  
- Overall: paper is promising and could be significant with additional experiments and analyses.
