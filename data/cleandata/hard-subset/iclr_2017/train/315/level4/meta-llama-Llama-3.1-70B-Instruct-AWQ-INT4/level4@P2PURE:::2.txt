The paper presents an empirical study that aims to justify two key claims: first, that stochastic gradient descent (SGD) with smaller batch sizes converges to flatter minima, and second, that these flatter minima exhibit better generalization ability.

Pros and Cons:
Although the paper does not introduce significant novelty, I believe it holds considerable value in providing insight into intriguing questions surrounding the generalization of deep networks.

Significance:
I think these results may have substantial implications for both theoretical and practical applications. On the theoretical front, they can inform the development of new theories by suggesting legitimate assumptions for real-world scenarios. In practice, they can be leveraged heuristically to design novel algorithms that enhance generalization through the strategic manipulation of mini-batch sizes.

Comments:
Initially, I had concerns regarding the accuracy of a claim made by the authors, which has since been resolved. Specifically, they had asserted that their proposed sharpness criterion was scale-invariant, but this claim has been removed in the revised version, addressing my concerns.
