**Summary of the Paper**

The paper investigates the cause of the generalization gap in deep learning models trained with large-batch stochastic gradient descent (SGD) methods. The authors argue that large-batch methods tend to converge to sharp minimizers of the training function, which leads to poor generalization performance. In contrast, small-batch methods converge to flat minimizers, which are more robust to noise and generalize better. The authors provide numerical evidence to support their claims and discuss several strategies to improve the generalization performance of large-batch methods.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

I recommend accepting the paper, with the following key reasons:

1. The paper tackles an important problem in deep learning, namely the generalization gap in large-batch SGD methods.
2. The authors provide a clear and well-motivated approach to understanding the cause of the generalization gap and propose several strategies to improve the performance of large-batch methods.
