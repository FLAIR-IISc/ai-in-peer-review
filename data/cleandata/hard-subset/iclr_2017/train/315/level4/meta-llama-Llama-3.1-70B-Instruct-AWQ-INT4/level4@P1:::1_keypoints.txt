- Paper studies why large-batch SGD degrades model quality, attributing it to convergence to sharp minimizers.  
- Numerical evidence shows large-batch methods find sharp minima while small-batch methods find flat minima due to gradient noise.  
- Well-motivated work with thorough literature discussion and convincing experiments.  
- Clear explanation of findings and implications for generalization.  
- Discusses mitigation strategies for large-batch generalization gap: data augmentation, conservative training, robust optimization.  
- Reviewer accepts the paper as it provides new insights and a thorough investigation of an important problem.  
- Key reason for acceptance: clear, convincing evidence linking large-batch training to sharp minima and poorer generalization.  
- Secondary reason: useful discussion of strategies to address the generalization gap, offering directions for future work.  
- Minor suggestion: provide more experimental setup details and hyperparameter settings for reproducibility.  
- Minor suggestion: expand discussion of implications for practical deep learning applications.  
- Question for authors: clarify the relationship between minimizer sharpness and generalization performance.
