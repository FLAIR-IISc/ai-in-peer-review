The paper presents an empirical study that aims to justify two key claims: (1) Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) with smaller batch sizes converges to flatter minima, and (2) flatter minima have better generalization ability.

Pros and Cons:
Although the paper offers little in terms of novelty, I believe the work holds significant value in providing insight into some intriguing questions surrounding the generalization of deep networks.

Significance:
I think these results may have a substantial impact on both theoretical and practical aspects of deep learning. The findings could inform the development of new theories by suggesting legitimate assumptions for real-world scenarios, or be used heuristically to develop novel algorithms that enhance generalization through clever manipulation of mini-batch sizes.

Comments:
Initially, I had concerns regarding the correctness of a claim made by the authors, which has since been resolved. Specifically, the authors had previously asserted that their proposed sharpness criterion is scale-invariant. However, they have addressed this issue by removing the claim in the revised version.
