- Paper studies how batch size affects generalization, arguing large-batch methods converge to sharp minimizers causing poor generalization.
- Authors provide numerical evidence across multiple deep learning architectures using 1-D parametric plots and perturbation-based sharpness measures.
- Main decision: accept, because the work offers valuable insights into deep learning generalization with potential theoretical and practical impact.
- Strength: thorough empirical investigation linking batch size to generalization behavior.
- Strength: uses multiple architectures and evaluation metrics to substantiate claims.
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation that makes results easy to follow.
- Weakness: discussion of related work is limited and could better situate contributions within broader literature.
- Weakness: paper lacks explicit discussion of practical implications for practitioners and researchers.
- Suggested extensions/questions: relate findings to regularization and early stopping, discuss implications for architecture and training algorithm design, and propose how results could inspire new algorithms to improve generalization.
