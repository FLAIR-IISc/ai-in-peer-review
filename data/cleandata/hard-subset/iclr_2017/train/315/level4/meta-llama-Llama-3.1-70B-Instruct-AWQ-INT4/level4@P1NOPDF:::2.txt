The paper presents an empirical study that investigates two key claims: first, that stochastic gradient descent (SGD) with smaller batch sizes converges to flatter minima, and second, that these flatter minima have better generalization ability.

Pros and Cons:
While the paper does not introduce significant novelty, I believe its value lies in shedding light on interesting questions surrounding the generalization of deep networks.

Significance:
I think these findings have the potential to impact both theoretical and practical aspects of deep learning. On the theoretical side, they can inform the development of new theories by suggesting legitimate assumptions for real-world scenarios. On the practical side, they can be used heuristically to develop new algorithms that improve generalization by strategically manipulating mini-batch sizes.

Comments:
I initially had concerns about the correctness of a claim made by the authors regarding the scale invariance of their proposed sharpness criterion. However, this issue has been resolved in the revised version, as the authors have removed this claim.
