Interesting paper that undoubtedly provides value to the community by discussing the limitations of large batch gradient descent.

The paper presents a thorough analysis of the generalization performance of large batch gradient descent, providing insights into why it often fails to match the performance of its small batch counterpart. The authors identify the issue of sharp minima, which are prevalent in large batch gradient descent, as a key factor contributing to this disparity. They propose a novel method to mitigate this issue by injecting noise into the gradient, effectively simulating the behavior of small batch gradient descent.

The authors provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis, which is supported by empirical evidence, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach. The paper is well-structured, and the writing is clear and concise. However, there are a few areas that could be improved for better clarity and flow.

One major concern is that the paper relies heavily on the assumption that the noise injected into the gradient is Gaussian. While the authors provide some justification for this assumption, it would be beneficial to explore the robustness of their method to different noise distributions. Furthermore, the authors could provide more insight into how the noise injection mechanism affects the convergence rate of the algorithm.

Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods that also aim to improve the generalization performance of large batch gradient descent. While the authors provide some discussion on this topic, a more thorough analysis would strengthen their claims.

Overall, this is a well-written paper that provides valuable insights into the limitations of large batch gradient descent. With some revisions to address the concerns mentioned above, this paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.

Rating: 7/10

Minor comments:

* The introduction could be more engaging, perhaps by highlighting the practical implications of the research.
* The notation could be improved for better readability. For example, the use of boldface font for vectors and matrices could be more consistent throughout the paper.
* The paper could benefit from more visualizations to illustrate the key concepts and results.
* The conclusion could be more concise, summarizing the main findings and implications of the research.
