- Paper shows large-batch SGD converges to sharp minimizers (poorer generalization) while small-batch SGD converges to flat minimizers, attributing this to gradient noise.  
- Authors present numerical evidence and propose strategies to reduce the large-batch generalization gap.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection.  
- Primary reason 1: heavy reliance on numerical experiments without a comprehensive analysis of underlying causes.  
- Primary reason 2: lack of a clear theoretical framework connecting observed convergence behavior to existing theory.  
- Suggested future direction: study adding rescaled Gaussian noise to gradients in large-batch regimes.  
- Suggested future direction: explore robust optimization techniques to improve large-batch generalization.  
- Request 1 for authors: provide a detailed theoretical analysis of large-batch convergence and causes of the generalization gap.  
- Request 2 for authors: clarify how results relate to recent theoretical work on deep network loss surfaces.  
- Request 3 for authors: supply more evidence for the effectiveness of proposed mitigation strategies.  
- Additional feedback: paper is well-written but contains minor errors (missing citations, formatting) and should include more experimental details (architectures, hyperparameters).
