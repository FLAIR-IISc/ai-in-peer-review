This paper is an empirical study that demonstrates: 1. SGD converges with small batch sizes in Flatter Minima, and 2. Flatter Minima has good generalization ability.

Advantages and Disadvantages:
Although there is very little novelty in this paper, I believe this work is extremely valuable for shedding light on some interesting questions related to the generalization of deep networks.

Importance:
I believe that results like this can have implications in both theory and practice. It can be useful by proposing what assumptions are valid in real-world situations for creating new theories, or by using itheuristically for developing new algorithms with generalization, such as smart manipulation of mini-batch sizes.

Comments:
I had some concerns about a claim made by the authors earlier, which has now been resolved. They claimed that their proposed sharpness criterion is scale invariance. They have resolved this by removing this claim in the revised version.
