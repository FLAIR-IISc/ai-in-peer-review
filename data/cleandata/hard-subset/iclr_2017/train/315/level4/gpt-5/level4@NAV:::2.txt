Review- This paper is an empirical study supporting that: 1. SGD with smaller batch sizes tends to converge to flatter minima, 2. flatter minima exhibit superior generalization.

Pros and Cons:
Although the work has limited novelty, it is valuable for shedding light on important questions about the generalization of deep networks.

Significance:
These findings may influence both theory and practice—by indicating which assumptions are reasonable in real-world settings for developing new theory, and by serving as heuristics to design algorithms that promote generalization through careful manipulation of mini-batch sizes.

Comments:
I previously had concerns about the correctness of a claim by the authors, which have now been resolved. They had asserted that their proposed sharpness criterion is scale-invariant; they addressed this by removing the claim in the revised version.
