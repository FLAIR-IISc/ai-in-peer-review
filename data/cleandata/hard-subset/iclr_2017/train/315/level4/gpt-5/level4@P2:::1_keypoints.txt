- Central claim: LB training tends to converge to sharp minima while SB finds flat minima, and sharpness correlates with poorer test performance.
- Empirical evidence: demonstrated across CNNs and FCNs via 1D/curvilinear interpolations showing sharper LB landscapes.
- Sharpness metric: practical local max-perturbation measure (full space and random subspaces) shows LB solutions 1–2 orders of magnitude sharper.
- Warm-start experiments: LB can generalize well if initialized near SB-discovered flat basins, suggesting initialization/sampling dynamics matter.
- Not classical overfitting: gap is not due to late-epoch degradation; preliminary remedies narrow but do not close the gap.
- Decision: weak accept.
- Key reason for accept: timely, practically important question with clear implications for scalable deep learning.
- Key reason for accept: diverse, carefully presented empirical evidence and an insightful practical sharpness metric.
- Strength — relevance: addresses crucial scalability vs generalization trade-off in deep learning.
- Strength — clear hypothesis & evidence: multiple experimental probes (parametric plots, metrics, warm-starts, batch thresholds) support the sharp-vs-flat narrative.
- Strength — methodology: random-subspace sharpness and warm-starts mitigate single-measure biases and suggest dynamic sampling fixes.
- Strength — applicability: results hold across architectures, datasets, and several optimizers (ADAM, checks for SGD/ADAGRAD/adaQN).
- Limitation — heuristic metric: maximization solved inexactly (10 L-BFGS-B steps) and depends on epsilon, subspace dim, and iterations.
- Limitation — hyperparameter controls: unclear if learning rates, schedules, weight decay, dropout, BN were tuned per batch size; could confound results.
- Limitation — fairness of comparison: stopping “until loss ceased to improve” may bias against LB; matched compute/updates/epochs or wall-clock should be considered.
- Limitation — scope: more modern architectures or deeper Hessian analyses (e.g., Lanczos) would strengthen validation.
- Limitation — causality: correlation between sharpness and generalization is persuasive but not proven causally; theory is lacking.
- Suggestion — hyperparameter fairness: report per-batch-size tuning and consider LR scaling rules and longer schedules to isolate batch effects.
- Suggestion — training budget controls: add experiments matched by gradient evaluations, epochs, examples processed, or wall time.
- Suggestion — sharpness robustness: report sensitivity to epsilon, ascent steps, subspace dimension p, and seeds; include approximate top Hessian eigenvalues.
- Suggestion — BatchNorm: clarify BN handling with large batches (per-replica vs global) and per-GPU batch sizes to rule out BN confounds.
- Suggestion — broader baselines: include noise-injection, stochastic weight averaging, and other regularizers to test if noise alone explains escapes from sharp basins.
- Suggestion — dynamic sampling: evaluate small-to-large batch schedules to see if they reproduce SB generalization with LB efficiency.
- Suggestion — additional analyses: plot test error vs sharpness across runs, examine distance from initialization, and analyze curvature anisotropy (number of sharp directions).
- Question — hyperparameters: how were learning rates, schedules, and regularization tuned per batch size; were LR scaling rules tried?
- Question — budget matching: are comparisons matched by updates, examples processed, or wall time; how sensitive are results to matching scheme?
- Question — BatchNorm: how were BN statistics computed for LB (per-replica vs global) and could BN explain part of the gap?
- Question — sharpness sensitivity: how do conclusions change with epsilon, ascent iterations, and subspace dimension p?
- Question — Hessian evidence: do you have top-eigenvalue/eigenvector estimates (power/Lanczos) for SB vs LB solutions on smaller models?
- Question — dynamic batch sizing: can gradual batch increases close the gap without SB pretraining?
- Question — optimizer/regularizer dependence: were momentum/Nesterov, label smoothing, or weight averaging tried and do they affect sharpness or the gap?
- Question — augmentation/adversarial effects: when augmentation or adversarial training narrows the gap, does robustness to corruptions improve or is gain limited to test accuracy?
