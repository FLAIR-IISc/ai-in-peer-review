The paper presents an empirical study supporting two claims: 1) SGD with smaller batch sizes tends to converge to flatter minima, and 2) flatter minima exhibit better generalization.

Pros and Cons:
Although the paper offers limited novelty, it provides valuable insights into important questions regarding generalization in deep networks.

Significance:
These results may influence both theory and practice: theoretically, by indicating which assumptions are reasonable in real-world settings for developing new theories; and practically, by inspiring heuristic algorithms that improve generalization through careful manipulation of mini-batch sizes.

Comments:
I previously raised a concern about the correctness of a claim that the proposed sharpness criterion is scale-invariant. The authors addressed this by removing that claim in the revised version.
