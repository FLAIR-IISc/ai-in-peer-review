- Empirical finding: Large-batch (LB) training tends to converge to sharp minima while small-batch (SB) training converges to flatter minima, correlating with poorer generalization for LB.  
- Methods: Visualized loss landscapes along parametric curves and proposed a practical sharpness metric to quantify basin sharpness.  
- Experimental breadth: Results are consistent across fully connected and convolutional (AlexNet-/VGG-like) architectures and multiple datasets.  
- Diagnostics: One-dimensional and curvilinear trajectory plots convincingly show sharper basins near LB solutions than SB ones.  
- Sharpness metric: Heuristic but scalable metric distinguishes SB and LB minima by 1–2 orders of magnitude in experiments.  
- Mechanistic insight: Warm-start experiments suggest SGD gradient noise helps escape sharp basins early, enabling later LB convergence to flatter regions.  
- Mitigation attempts: Data augmentation, conservative training, and adversarial training reduce but do not eliminate sharpness differences.  
- Practical implication: Findings inform batch-size choices and training protocols (e.g., warm-start/dynamic batching) for scalable training.
