- Hypothesis: Large-batch (LB) training converges to sharper minima that generalize worse, while small-batch (SB) noise favors flatter, better-generalizing minima.
- Contributions: Extensive empirical evidence across architectures/datasets using parametric/curvilinear plots, a tractable sharpness metric, batch-size threshold experiments, and warm-start studies.
- Remedies tested: Data augmentation, conservative/adversarial/stability training partially close the gap but LB still finds relatively sharp basins.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Well-motivated, practically important question with a coherent empirical story linking batch size, noise, and sharp vs. flat minima.
- Key reason 2: Careful, multi-faceted experiments including diagnostics and ablations support the conclusions.
- Problem framing: Addresses a widely observed phenomenon limiting parallelism and situates work appropriately in related literature.
- Evidence quality: Consistent 1–2 order-of-magnitude sharpness gap between LB and SB with matched training loss but diverging test accuracy, despite heuristic metric use.
- Diagnostic clarity: Parametric and curvilinear plots compellingly show LB minima are much narrower than SB minima.
- Mechanistic insight: Warm-starts show SB can guide LB into flatter regions, supporting the “noise helps exploration” mechanism.
- Practical implication: Sets expectations about limits of naive batch-size scaling and suggests design principles like dynamic sampling, noise injection, or better initialization.
- Improvement — sharpness metric: Need discussion of invariance to parameter rescaling/BN, sensitivity to epsilon and subspace dimensionality, and calibration vs. top Hessian eigenvalues.
- Improvement — hyperparameters: More systematic decoupling of batch size from learning-rate schedules, momentum, weight decay, and total updates (epochs vs steps), including LB-tailored scaling/warmup.
- Improvement — optimizer diversity: Extend experiments beyond ADAM to SGD+momentum to confirm optimizer-agnostic conclusions.
- Improvement — architecture/normalization: Test effects without batch normalization and under different parameterizations due to scale invariances affecting curvature.
- Improvement — additional remedies: Explore explicit gradient-noise injection, dynamic batch-size schedules, or flatness-seeking objectives under LB constraints.
- Question 1: How sensitive are sharpness measurements to epsilon and p; can you provide a small study or confidence bounds?
- Question 2: Were total parameter updates matched and learning-rate schedules (including warmup) tuned separately per batch size?
- Question 3: Do results hold with SGD+momentum and alternative regularizations/data pipelines?
- Question 4: How does batch normalization influence sharpness conclusions; any experiments without BN or with frozen BN?
- Question 5: In warm-starts, what is the parameter distance between SB iterates and LB optima when test accuracy aligns; implications for dynamic-batch strategies?
- Question 6: Did you try explicit gradient-noise injection in LB to match SB variance; what were effects on test accuracy and sharpness?
- Question 7: Can you report approximate top Hessian eigenvalues or spectral norms at SB and LB optima to corroborate the sharpness metric?
- Question 8: Which remedies most reduced sharpness without sacrificing LB speedups, and under what hyperparameters?
