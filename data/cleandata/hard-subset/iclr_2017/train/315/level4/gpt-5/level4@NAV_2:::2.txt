The paper presents an experimental investigation supporting two claims: 1) SGD with smaller mini-batches tends to converge to flatter minima, and 2) flatter minima exhibit superior generalization performance.

Pros and Cons:
While the contribution is not particularly novel, the study is nonetheless valuable for illuminating key issues surrounding generalization in deep neural networks.

Significance:
These findings can influence both theory and practice: theoretically, by indicating which assumptions are realistic in practical settings for future analyses; and practically, by motivating heuristic algorithmic designs that encourage generalization via judicious control of mini-batch sizes.

Comments:
I previously raised a concern about the correctness of a claim regarding the proposed sharpness metric being scale-invariant. This has been resolved in the revised version, where the authors have removed that assertion.
