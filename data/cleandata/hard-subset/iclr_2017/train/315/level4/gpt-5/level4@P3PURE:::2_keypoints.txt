- Paper presents empirical evidence for two main claims: (1) smaller SGD batch sizes converge to flatter minima, and (2) flatter minima generalize better.  
- Limited novelty but offers valuable empirical data clarifying how deep networks generalize.  
- Findings could influence theory by indicating which assumptions are appropriate for real-world settings.  
- Results may be used heuristically to design algorithms that improve generalization via mini-batch size manipulation.  
- Reviewer’s prior concern about the sharpness criterion’s scale-invariance was addressed; the authors removed that claim in the revision.
