- Paper empirically supports two claims: (1) smaller SGD mini-batches tend to converge to flatter minima, and (2) flatter minima lead to better generalization.  
- Limited novelty but provides a valuable clarification of how batch size affects optimization dynamics and generalization in deep networks.  
- Findings have theoretical and practical significance: suggest realistic modeling assumptions and motivate heuristics/algorithms that control mini-batch size to improve generalization.  
- Reviewer previously flagged an incorrect claim about the proposed sharpness criterion being scale-invariant; the authors removed that claim in the revision.
