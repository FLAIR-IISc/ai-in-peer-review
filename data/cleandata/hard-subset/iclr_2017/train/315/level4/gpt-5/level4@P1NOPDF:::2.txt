The paper presents an empirical study supporting two main claims: (1) SGD with smaller batch sizes tends to converge to flatter minima, and (2) flatter minima exhibit better generalization.

Pros and Cons:
While the paper offers limited novelty, it provides valuable insights into important questions about generalization in deep networks. The empirical evidence is useful for understanding the relationship between batch size, sharpness/flatness of minima, and generalization performance.

Significance:
These results have potential impact on both theory and practice. Theoretically, they help identify which assumptions are realistic for modeling generalization in real-world settings. Practically, they may inspire heuristic methods that improve generalization by judiciously choosing or adapting mini-batch sizes during training.

Comments:
I previously had concerns about the correctness of one of the authors’ claims: they had asserted that their proposed sharpness criterion is scale-invariant. This issue has been addressed in the revised version, where the authors have removed that claim.
