The paper presents an empirical study supporting two main claims: (1) SGD with smaller batch sizes converges to flatter minima, and (2) flatter minima exhibit better generalization.

Pros and Cons:
Although the paper has limited novelty, it provides valuable empirical evidence that sheds light on important questions regarding the generalization of deep networks.

Significance:
These results could influence both theory and practice: they suggest which assumptions are appropriate for real-world settings when developing new theories, and they may be used heuristically to design new algorithms that improve generalization by intelligently manipulating mini-batch sizes.

Comments:
I previously had concerns about the correctness of a claim made by the authors regarding their sharpness criterion being scale-invariant. This has been addressed in the revised version, where the authors have removed that claim.
