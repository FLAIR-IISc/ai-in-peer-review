- Paper investigates why large-batch (LB) training generalizes worse than small-batch (SB), attributing it to LB converging to sharper minima while SB finds flatter minima.  
- Authors provide numerical evidence across architectures/datasets, 1-D and curvilinear plots, and propose a practical local “sharpness” metric.  
- Warm-start experiments show early noisy (SB) training helps escape sharp basins, after which LB can refine near flat minima.  
- Empirical scope is broad (AlexNet-/VGG-like CNNs, FC nets, multiple datasets) with consistent LB→sharper minima and worse generalization despite similar training loss.  
- Parametric loss/accuracy plots and the sharpness metric visually/quantitatively highlight differences in landscape geometry.  
- Warm-start evidence supports stochastic noise aiding exploration and improved generalization when switching to LB later.  
- Mitigations tested (data augmentation, conservative/adversarial training) reduce but do not eliminate the sharpness/generalization gap.  
- Decision: Weak accept — finds a clear, empirically grounded explanation and offers practically useful directions.  
- Suggest explicitly testing rescaled Gaussian noise injected into LB gradients to emulate SB noise and study required noise variance vs. batch size.  
- Recommend clarifying link between sharpness metric and Hessian spectrum, e.g., report approximate top eigenvalues via stochastic Lanczos/power iterations.  
- Ask for sensitivity analyses on learning-rate schedules/scaling rules, optimizer dependence, and initialization effects to isolate batch-size vs hyperparameter effects.  
- Request metric robustness checks: variance over random subspaces A and sweeps over ε.  
- Highlight the observed batch-size threshold behavior and suggest relating thresholds to dataset size, model capacity, and gradient noise (e.g., normalize batch size by dataset size or gradient variance).  
- Encourage tighter discussion of theoretical connections (MDL/Bayesian) to contextualize empirical findings.  
- Questions for authors: have you tried matching SB gradient noise by adding Gaussian noise to LB, and how does it affect sharpness/test accuracy?  
- Questions: can you provide approximate top Hessian/Gauss–Newton eigenvalues at SB vs LB solutions?  
- Questions: how stable are batch-size thresholds across seeds, LR schedules, datasets, and do they correlate with gradient variance or warm-start epochs?  
- Questions: in warm-start experiments, after how many SB epochs does LB consistently find flat minima, and does this align with sharpness drops or training-phase changes?  
- Questions: among mitigations tested, which narrows the gap most per unit computation, and can combining them with dynamic/gradual batch-size increases close the gap?  
- Overall: paper is interesting and useful; adding targeted Gaussian-noise-in-LB experiments would strengthen practical recommendations.
