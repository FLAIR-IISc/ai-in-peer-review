Summary:
This paper presents an empirical study supporting two claims: (1) SGD with smaller mini-batch sizes tends to converge to flatter minima, and (2) flatter minima exhibit better generalization.

Pros and Cons:
While the paper offers limited novelty, it makes a valuable contribution by clarifying important aspects of generalization in deep networks. The results help illuminate why batch size can influence both optimization dynamics and generalization behavior.

Significance:
The findings could impact both theory and practice. Theoretically, they suggest which assumptions are more realistic for modeling learning dynamics and generalization in deep networks. Practically, they motivate heuristics and algorithmic strategies that improve generalization through judicious control of mini-batch size.

Comments:
I previously questioned the correctness of a specific claim: the authors had asserted that their proposed sharpness criterion is scale-invariant. This issue has been addressed in the revised version, where the authors removed that claim.
