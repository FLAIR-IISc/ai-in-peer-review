- Paper presents an empirical study supporting two claims about SGD and generalization in deep networks.
- Claim 1: SGD with smaller batch sizes tends to converge to flatter minima.
- Claim 2: Flatter minima exhibit better generalization.
- Novelty is limited, but the work provides valuable insights into generalization behavior in deep networks.
- Significance: may guide theory by suggesting realistic assumptions and inspire practical heuristics that improve generalization via mini-batch size choices.
- Reviewer raised a concern that the sharpness criterion was claimed scale-invariant; the authors removed that claim in the revised version.
