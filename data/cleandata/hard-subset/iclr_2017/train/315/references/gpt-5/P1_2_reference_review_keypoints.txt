- Paper studies why large-batch (LB) training often generalizes worse than small-batch (SB) training in deep nets.
- Central empirical claim: LB converges to sharper minima while SB finds flatter minima that generalize better.
- Contributions: documents generalization gap across architectures/datasets.
- Contributions: visualizes loss landscapes along linear and curvilinear paths between SB and LB solutions.
- Contributions: proposes a computable “sharpness” proxy using worst-case local loss increase in a small box (optionally in a random low-dimensional subspace).
- Contributions: identifies a batch-size threshold where test accuracy drops and a warm-start effect where SB pretraining enables LB to reach flatter minima.
- Reports preliminary remedies (data augmentation, proximal/conservative, adversarial/robust training) that partially help but don’t eliminate sharpness.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: addresses a timely, practical question and provides consistent multi-faceted empirical evidence linking batch size, sharpness, and generalization.
- Key reason: offers a simple, computationally feasible sharpness diagnostic usable by practitioners.
- Supporting: paper is clearly written, well-motivated, and visualizations make the core claim intuitive.
- Supporting: experiments span multiple architectures/datasets and seeds, showing similar training loss but worse test accuracy for LB.
- Supporting: 1D and curved path plots show LB solutions lie in much sharper basins.
- Supporting: sharpness metric differs by 1–2 orders of magnitude between SB and LB; batch-size threshold and warm-start effects are observed.
- Motivation: connects to MDL/Bayesian intuitions, nonconvex loss theory, and role of gradient noise in SGD exploration.
- Practical implication: warm-start/dynamic batching is a valuable and actionable suggestion.
- Limitation: evidence is correlational; causality (gradient noise causing escape from sharp basins) is not directly proven or measured.
- Limitation: sharpness metric depends on design choices (ε, subspace dim p, box definition, LBFGS-B steps) and may be sensitive to parameterization/scaling (BN/ReLU).
- Limitation: hyperparameter fairness concerns—details on LR schedules, weight decay, momentum, and LR scaling with batch size are missing; ADAM use for CNNs is nonstandard.
- Limitation: optimization budget differences (training until loss stops) may allocate unequal compute to SB vs LB; matched-budget comparisons are needed.
- Limitation: link between sharpness metric and Hessian spectrum is indirect; top-eigenvalue estimates would strengthen the curvature claim.
- Suggestions: provide full training details and ablate their effects on generalization and sharpness.
- Suggestions: report results with momentum SGD and include quantitative comparisons to other optimizers.
- Suggestions: analyze sensitivity of the sharpness metric (vary ε, p), test invariance under reparameterizations, and correlate with Hessian eigenvalues on small models.
- Suggestions: quantify gradient noise vs batch size and relate it to threshold and warm-start phenomena.
- Suggestions: control optimization budget (matched updates/epochs/wall-clock) when comparing SB and LB.
- Suggestions: explore dynamic batch-size schedules systematically as a practical remedy and release code for reproducibility.
- Questions: clarify Equation (4), justify box dependence on A+ x, and report sensitivity to ε, p, and LBFGS-B iterations.
- Questions: how does the sharpness metric behave under layer rescalings/BN and was any normalization attempted for parameterization invariance?
- Questions: what LR and decay schedules were used for SB vs LB, and was linear LR scaling or warmup applied for larger batches?
- Questions: can authors provide quantitative test-accuracy/sharpness results for optimizers other than ADAM?
- Questions: were SB and LB compared under matched parameter-update/epoch budgets, and how sensitive are conclusions to matching?
- Questions: were gradient noise magnitudes measured as a function of batch size and related to the threshold?
- Questions: have top Hessian eigenvalues/vectors been estimated (power/Lanczos) at SB vs LB solutions on smaller networks?
- Questions: can the authors provide a simple dynamic batch-size schedule that reliably reproduces warm-start generalization across architectures?
