- Problem: Large-batch (LB) training often yields worse test accuracy than small-batch (SB) training; paper asks why and how to make LB viable.  
- Main claim: LB converges to sharper minima (higher curvature) while SB finds flatter minima, and sharpness correlates with worse generalization.  
- Evidence — parametric plots: 1D linear and curvilinear loss interpolations show markedly sharper basins at LB solutions across architectures/datasets.  
- Evidence — sharpness metric: A practical local-maximization sharpness proxy (full space or random low-dim subspace) reports 10–100× higher sharpness for LB solutions.  
- Evidence — thresholds and warm-starts: Batch-size thresholds where generalization drops; LB initialized from late SB iterates generalizes well, suggesting SB explores flatter regions.  
- Mitigations tested: Data augmentation, conservative/proximal updates, and adversarial/stability training reduce but do not eliminate sharpness or the generalization gap.  
- Takeaway: SB noise helps avoid sharp basins; dynamic sampling (growing batch size) appears promising per warm-start results.  
- Decision: Weak accept — important question with compelling empirical evidence and actionable insights.  
- Strength: Clear hypothesis (flat vs sharp minima) with consistent patterns across models and multiple visualization/quantitative angles.  
- Strength: Warm-start experiments strongly support a mechanistic story that early SB noise guides to flatter regions.  
- Strength: Authors explore several mitigation strategies and connect to relevant theory (loss landscape, MDL/Bayesian).  
- Concern: Causation vs correlation — strong correlation shown but causality between sharpness and generalization is not established.  
- Concern: Sharpness metric is heuristic, computationally inexact (10 L-BFGS-B steps), hyperparameter-dependent (epsilon, p, A), and sensitivity analyses are limited.  
- Concern: Possible typo/ambiguity in Eq. (4) normalization ("+ f(x) × 100"); needs clarification whether normalization is by (1+f(x)).  
- Concern: Reparameterization invariances (e.g., ReLU/BatchNorm scaling) may change curvature without changing function; robustness of metric to this is unclear.  
- Concern: Experimental controls lacking — unclear if LR schedules, weight decay, and optimizer settings were tuned per batch size; LR scaling/warmup may mitigate LB issues.  
- Concern: Training budget comparisons may be biased — need matched updates/epochs/wall-clock across SB and LB with error bars.  
- Concern: Optimizer coverage limited — most results use ADAM; findings under SGD with momentum and tuned schedules are missing.  
- Concern: Missing direct curvature diagnostics — top Hessian eigenvalues (power/Lanczos) for smaller models would validate the proxy.  
- Suggested improvements: Clarify Eq. (4), run sensitivity ablations over epsilon, p, A, inner steps, and show confidence intervals across seeds.  
- Suggested improvements: Tune LR schedules (linear scaling, warmup), weight decay, label smoothing for LB; match training budgets and report error bars.  
- Suggested improvements: Add direct curvature diagnostics (top eigenvalues/spectral tails) and analyze sharpness concentration across parameter groups/subspaces.  
- Suggested improvements: Include a concrete dynamic batch-size schedule experiment inspired by warm-starts and report outcomes.  
- Presentation fixes: Correct typos, unify notation, and provide per-dataset/architecture batch-threshold details with multiple seeds and plots.  
- Questions for authors: Clarify Eq. (4) normalization and sensitivity to epsilon, p, A, and inner maximization steps.  
- Questions for authors: How are hyperparameters (LR, schedules, weight decay) tuned per batch size; were LR scaling and warmup tried?  
- Questions for authors: Were SB and LB trained under matched budgets (updates/epochs/wall-clock)? Do results hold under SGD with momentum?  
- Questions for authors: Can you report top Hessian eigenvalues for smaller networks to corroborate the sharpness proxy?  
- Questions for authors: Can you include a dynamic batch-size schedule experiment and report results with confidence intervals across architectures?
