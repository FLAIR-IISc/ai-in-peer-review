- Paper studies why large-batch (LB) training generalizes worse than small-batch (SB), attributing it to LB converging to sharper minima via empirical studies.

- Contributions: 1D/curvilinear interpolations, a heuristic “sharpness” metric, batch-size threshold experiments, warm-start studies, and partial mitigation via augmentation/proximal steps.

- Decision: Reject (weak).

- Main reason 1: Empirical setup fails to control known confounders (optimizer, LR scaling, update budget, regularization), so causal claim LB → sharp minima → poor generalization is weak.

- Main reason 2: The sharpness metric is not invariant to reparameterizations and is a coarse proxy for curvature, undermining robustness of conclusions.

- Baseline fairness concern: LB experiments use ADAM and large batches (up to 10% of data) without matching canonical LB recipes (SGD+momentum, LR scaling, warmup, tuned weight decay).

- Update-count confounder: LB has far fewer parameter updates per epoch and the paper does not equalize updates or training effort across batch sizes.

- Optimizer choice issue: ADAM’s implicit regularization differs from SGD+momentum and authors lack systematic tuned LB baselines with SGD.

- Sharpness metric sensitivity: φx,f depends on parameter scaling and can be misleading under reparameterizations like layer rescaling or BN.

- Sharpness approximation issues: Maximization uses few L-BFGS-B iterations over a box and may not reflect true curvature in high dimensions.

- Limited probe coverage: Using p=100 random subspaces in models with millions of parameters samples a tiny slice and lacks verification against Hessian spectrum.

- 1D/curvilinear interpolation limits: Rising loss near LB along specific paths doesn't prove LB minima are globally sharper in high-dimensional space.

- Alternative explanations not excluded: differences could arise from update counts, mismatched effective weight decay, or BN scaling symmetries rather than batch size per se.

- Overstated claims: Paper extrapolates correlations to causation without adequately addressing confounders and metric limitations.

- Suggested experiment fixes: Provide LB baselines with SGD+momentum, proper LR scaling/warmup, calibrated weight decay, and equalized optimizer updates.

- Suggested sharpness analyses: Report top-k Hessian eigenvalues (power/Lanczos), test invariance to reparameterization, and compare to other flatness/PAC-Bayes metrics.

- Additional ablations: Vary p, ε, solver iterations; control data augmentation; test sharpness-aware optimizers like SAM/ASAM for LB.

- Clarify budgets: Report results for fixed epochs and fixed-update budgets, full hyperparameter settings, and equal tuning effort for SB/LB.

- Key questions for authors: effects of SGD+momentum with LR scaling/warmup; equalized updates impact; stability of φx,f under reparameterizations; Hessian eigenvalue/spectrum comparisons; and whether warm-start effects are due to more updates or entering flatter basins.
