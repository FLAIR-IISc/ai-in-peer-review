- Problem: Explores why very large mini-batches often generalize worse than small-batch training despite similar training loss.
- Main claim: Large-batch (LB) training converges to sharper minima with high positive curvature, which correlates with poorer test performance compared to small-batch (SB) training.
- Evidence — interpolations: 1D and curvilinear interpolations show LB solutions lie in consistently sharper basins than SB solutions.
- Evidence — sharpness metric: A practical sharpness proxy (worst-case loss increase in a small box, full-space and random subspaces) is 1–2 orders of magnitude higher for LB across datasets and architectures.
- Evidence — batch-size threshold: Batch-size threshold experiments reveal a regime transition where generalization degrades and sharpness increases as batch size grows.
- Evidence — warm-starting: Warm-starting LB from SB iterates shows LB can recover good generalization only after SB has moved into flatter basins, supporting noise-driven exploration by SB.
- Evidence — attempted remedies: Data augmentation, conservative/proximal training, and adversarial training reduce but do not eliminate the sharpness/generalization gap.
- Impact: Provides a plausible mechanism and actionable insights (e.g., dynamic batching) for large-scale training with broad empirical support.
- Decision: Reviewer recommends accept (weak accept) based on importance, empirical breadth, and consistent evidence for the sharp-minima hypothesis.
- Motivation & placement: Well-motivated by scalability limits of SB SGD and connected to MDL/Bayesian intuitions and recent loss-surface studies.
- Methodology strengths: Uses multiple architectures/domains, repeated runs, complementary diagnostics (paths and sharpness proxy), and a revealing warm-start study.
- Scientific rigor: Sharpness metric is heuristic and maximization approximate, but qualitative consistency and falsification attempts strengthen the findings.
- Fair comparisons/tuning: Clarify hyperparameter selection per batch size, report tuning effort, and include SGD+momentum comparisons in the main text.
- Compute matching: Match both number of epochs and number of parameter updates in ablations to control for compute and gradient-noise exposure.
- Learning-rate exploration: Explore linear/scaled learning rates and warmup for LB and report whether conclusions persist.
- Sharpness metric robustness: Discuss parameterization/scale dependence (BN, ReLU homogeneity), consider layer-wise normalization, function-space measures, and sensitivity to ε and subspace dimension.
- Hessian validation: Complement proxy with top Hessian eigenvalues (power/Lanczos) on some models to validate correlation.
- Additional controls: Test label smoothing, mixup/cutmix, spectral-norm or weight-decay scaling with batch size, and injecting calibrated gradient noise (e.g., SGHMC) into LB.
- Batch-size sweeps: Extend experiments beyond a single large-batch choice to show full batch-size vs. sharpness/generalization curves on more models.
- Presentation and reproducibility: Provide full code, seeds, and exact configs; clarify stopping criteria and effects of early stopping.
- Hyperparameter question: How were learning rates, schedules, weight decay, and Adam betas/eps chosen per batch size, and was linear scaling/warmup used?
- Optimizer question: Do conclusions hold with SGD+momentum (carefully tuned) and can key SGD results appear in the main text?
- Compute-matching question: When matching parameter updates or total epochs, do sharpness and generalization trends persist?
- Hessian question: Can you report top 5–10 Hessian eigenvalues (power/Lanczos) at SB and LB solutions to validate the sharpness proxy?
- Parameterization question: How does the sharpness metric behave under re-scaling in BN networks, and have function-space measures been tried?
- Noise mechanism question: Does adding explicit gradient noise to LB (or using SGHMC) recover flat minima and close the gap without SB warm-starting?
- Regularization question: How does tuning weight decay proportional to batch size, label smoothing, or mixup affect results?
- Generality question: Do batch-size thresholds and warm-start patterns hold for modern residual architectures and larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet)?
