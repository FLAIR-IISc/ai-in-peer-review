- Claim: Large-batch training converges to sharp minimizers (many large positive Hessian eigenvalues) while small-batch training converges to flat minimizers (many small eigenvalues).
- Method: Introduces a computationally feasible sharpness metric (Eq.4) and evaluates it across six network architectures and multiple datasets.
- Contribution: Provides an empirical explanation for the large-batch vs small-batch generalization gap.
- Contribution: Proposes a new metric to measure minimizer sharpness.
- Contribution: Shows warm-starting large-batch training from small-batch iterates yields good generalization.
- Contribution: Systematically evaluates remedies (data augmentation, conservative/robust training) and finds them only partially effective.
- Relevance: Well-motivated problem with thorough literature placement and scalability discussion.
- Decision: Reviewer recommends weak acceptance.
- Strength: Extensive experiments (parametric plots and sharpness metrics) show 1–2 orders-of-magnitude sharpness differences between regimes.
- Evidence: Authors rule out overfitting and use warm-start experiments to support the sharpness hypothesis.
- Limitation: The work is purely empirical and lacks formal theoretical justification.
- Improvement: Sharpen the formal connection between the sharpness metric and Hessian eigenvalues/Ritz values.
- Improvement: Quantify the claimed “small-dimensional subspace” (e.g., the 5% figure) across networks.
- Improvement: Clarify the warm-start threshold (how many small-batch epochs are needed).
- Improvement: Add computational cost/timing measurements for computing the sharpness metric on large networks.
- Questions: How many small-batch epochs are required for warm-starting to achieve good generalization and is there a clear threshold?
- Questions: How was the 5% subspace percentage computed and does it vary by architecture/dataset?
- Questions: How do the authors definitively distinguish the sharpness hypothesis from non-standard forms of overfitting?
- Questions: Can the authors formally justify Eq.4 as a proxy for Hessian eigenvalues in non-convex settings?
