- Paper studies why large-batch (LB) training generalizes worse than small-batch (SB) training, attributing the gap to LB converging to sharper minima.
- Authors propose a computationally feasible sharpness metric (Eq. 4) and show LB finds solutions with much larger Hessian eigenvalues than SB.
- Strong, consistent empirical evidence across multiple architectures (AlexNet, VGG variants) and trials supports the central claim.
- Parametric 1-D plots (Figure 3) and Tables 3–4 quantitatively illustrate orders-of-magnitude sharpness differences between SB and LB solutions.
- The authors rule out simple overfitting as the cause of the generalization gap (Figure 2).
- The paper tests mitigation strategies (data augmentation, conservative training, robust training) but finds they do not fully eliminate LB sharpness or the generalization gap.
- Warm-starting LB with SB iterations helps LB find flatter minima, but the mechanism is not fully explained.
- Paper situates work well in related literature (optimization, generalization, flat vs. sharp minima) and includes a practical performance model (Appendix C) showing potential LB benefits if the gap were resolved.
- Recommendation: accept, due to practical significance and robust empirical support.
- Suggested improvement: provide stronger theoretical justification for why gradient noise leads SB to flatter minima and LB to sharper ones.
- Suggested improvement: evaluate optimizer effects, especially using SGD for LB since ADAM is used for both in the paper.
- Suggested improvement: clarify the sharpness metric and provide more analysis on directional sensitivity (show that minima are not cone-like).
- Suggested improvement: better explain why warm-starting with SB helps LB converge to flatter minima.
- Questions for authors include requests for convergence-path analysis, reasons for sharpness consistency across architectures, effect of using SGD for LB, implications for dynamic sampling strategies, and why data augmentation/conservative training do not fully resolve sharpness.
