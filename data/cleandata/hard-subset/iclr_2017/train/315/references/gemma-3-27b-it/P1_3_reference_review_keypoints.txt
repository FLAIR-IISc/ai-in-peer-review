- Paper studies the generalization gap with large-batch training and hypothesizes they converge to sharper minima, providing empirical evidence across architectures and datasets but finding limited success with remedies.
- Decision: Reject — evidence is preliminary and insufficient to convincingly establish the claimed relationship.
- Limited novelty: large-batch worse generalization is well-known; linking to sharpness is not entirely new.
- Heuristic sharpness measure: proposed metric (Eq. 4) relies on a random matrix A and few L-BFGS iterations, raising validity concerns.
- Sharpness metric lacks rigorous validation or strong correlation checks with true Hessian properties.
- Insufficient exploration of mitigation strategies: parameter settings and deeper analysis of why they fail are missing.
- Lack of theoretical grounding: claim that large-batch methods tend toward sharp minima remains a conjecture with only empirical support.
- Presentation clarity issues: repetitive writing and excessive Appendix references disrupt readability.
- Suggestion: perform full Hessian analysis or better approximation on small networks to validate the metric.
- Suggestion: conduct comprehensive parameter sensitivity studies for mitigation strategies (augmentation, regularization, robust optimization).
- Suggestion: develop theoretical arguments connecting large-batch training to bias toward sharp minima, possibly via SGD dynamics.
- Suggestion: improve visualizations, e.g., 2D/3D loss landscapes, beyond 1D parametric plots.
- Suggestion: define and use “sharpness” and “flatness” consistently and clarify terminology.
- Suggestion: discuss limitations of the proposed metric and compare with alternative landscape metrics.
- Question: how sensitive are results to the choice of random matrix A and the neighborhood definition for sharpness?
- Question: what specific tests rule out overfitting as the cause of the generalization gap, and were other causes explored?
- Question: how might dynamic batch size methods leverage strengths of small- and large-batch training?
- Question: why should the proposed metric correlate with true sharpness given limited L-BFGS iterations, and how does this approximation affect conclusions?
- Question: have optimizer modifications (e.g., sharpness-aware methods) or architecture changes been considered to address the problem?
