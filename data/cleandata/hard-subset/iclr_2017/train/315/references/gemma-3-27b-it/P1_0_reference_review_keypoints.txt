- Paper investigates why large-batch training generalizes worse, linking it to convergence to sharp minima with higher Hessian curvature versus flat minima for small batches.  
- Decision: Reject — interesting empirics but insufficient novelty, rigor, and causal evidence to merit acceptance.  
- Lack of novelty: Confirms known large-batch/generalization effects and prior flatness–generalization connections without offering a clear new theoretical or experimental advance.  
- Empirical focus and limited rigor: Analysis depends on a heuristic sharpness metric and visualizations without full justification of parameter choices or sensitivity analysis.  
- Sharpness metric concerns: Choice of parameters (e.g., p=100, λ) and neighborhood/rand matrix A are not fully justified or tested for robustness.  
- Insufficient remediation exploration: Data augmentation, conservative training, and adversarial training are tried but evaluated cursorily and without deep analysis of why they fail.  
- Weak connection to theory: Related theoretical work is mentioned but not meaningfully integrated or used to explain empirical findings or suggest formal avenues.  
- Recommendation to sharpen hypothesis: Clarify what is novel—method, quantification, or particularly compelling experiments.  
- Request robustness analysis: Perform sensitivity tests over sharpness metric parameters and demonstrate trend stability.  
- Need for theoretical grounding: Link observations to frameworks like PAC-Bayes or Rademacher complexity to provide formal justification.  
- Deeper remediation investigation urged: Explore combinations, hyperparameter sweeps, and varied architectures to find effective fixes.  
- Statistical rigor: Add statistical tests, error bars, and significance analysis to support claimed differences.  
- Computational cost transparency: Discuss the expense of computing the sharpness metric and scalability to larger models.  
- Question on overfitting: Provide evidence that the generalization gap isn’t resolved by early stopping or traditional overfitting explanations.  
- Optimizer dependence question: Have experiments been run with optimizers other than ADAM to test generality of results?  
- Sensitivity question: How do neighborhood size and choice of random matrix A affect the sharpness metric outcomes?  
- Computational limitations question: What prevented a more exhaustive evaluation of remediation techniques?  
- Density of minima question: How do the authors plan to investigate the suggested link between sharpness and minima density?
