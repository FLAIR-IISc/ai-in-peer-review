- Paper studies the large-batch generalization gap and links it to convergence to sharper minima via visualizations and a proposed sharpness metric.  
- Decision: Reject — evidence is largely observational and not rigorous enough for ICLR.  
- The generalization-gap observation is not novel; the sharpness link is the main contribution but is insufficiently supported.  
- The proposed sharpness metric is heuristic, sensitive (matrix A, λ), uses small neighborhoods and L-BFGS, and lacks theoretical grounding.  
- Remedial attempts (data augmentation, conservative training, robust optimization) are limited and not shown convincingly effective.  
- The paper lacks a formal theoretical connection to MDL, Bayesian learning, or existing work on sharpness and batch-size effects.  
- Writing is repetitive and could be more concise despite logical organization.  
- Suggestion: adopt more theoretically motivated sharpness measures and perform sensitivity analysis on metric choices.  
- Suggestion: run more rigorous remedial experiments with hyperparameter tuning, ablations, and statistical significance.  
- Suggestion: develop formal theoretical justification for the link between batch size and minimizer sharpness.  
- Suggestion: broaden experiments across architectures and datasets to test consistency of effects.  
- Suggestion: improve visualizations and consider showing the Hessian spectrum.  
- Suggestion: explore adaptive or gradually increasing batch-size schedules experimentally.  
- Question: justify the specific form of the sharpness metric and what alternatives were considered.  
- Question: provide stronger evidence that the generalization gap is not due to overfitting, e.g., feature analyses.  
- Question: have you approximated the Hessian eigenvalue spectrum, and does it corroborate the sharpness findings?  
- Question: what is the computational cost and scalability of computing the sharpness metric on large networks?  
- Question: any preliminary results on dynamic batch-size adjustment and its effect on sharpness/generalization?
