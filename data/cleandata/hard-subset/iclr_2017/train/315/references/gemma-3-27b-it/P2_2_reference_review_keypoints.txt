- Paper studies the generalization gap in large-batch training, attributing it to sharper minima and supporting this with parametric plots and a sharpness metric across architectures and datasets.  
- Decision: Reject — important topic but presentation lacks rigor and experiments feel exploratory rather than conclusive.  
- Core observation (flat minima -> better generalization) is not novel; authors must clarify what new insight they bring specifically for large-batch training.  
- Proposed sharpness metric seems ad hoc, only loosely tied to Hessian/eigenvalues, and sensitive to parameter choices (e.g., epsilon, random matrix A).  
- Remedies tested (data augmentation, conservative training) showed limited effectiveness and were not analyzed deeply enough to explain failure modes.  
- Presentation and rigor issues: stylistic problems, disjoint organization, and many claims lack proper statistical support or precise quantification.  
- Missing important baselines and comparisons to large-batch techniques like LARS and other optimizer strategies.  
- Recommendation: develop stronger theoretical grounding linking batch size, loss curvature, and generalization bounds.  
- Recommendation: better justify and compare the sharpness metric to Hessian-based and other curvature measures, and clarify its limitations.  
- Recommendation: more thorough exploration of mitigation methods (different optimizers, LARS, etc.) and analysis of why they succeed or fail.  
- Recommendation: add statistical analyses (p-values, confidence intervals, consistent standard deviations) to support experimental claims.  
- Recommendation: improve clarity, organization, and precision of writing to present a coherent narrative.  
- Question: What are the limitations of the proposed sharpness metric and how can it be improved?  
- Question: Were there consistent patterns in which network architectures or datasets exhibited the largest generalization gap?  
- Question: Why did data augmentation and conservative training fail to materially close the gap for large-batch methods?  
- Question: Have you evaluated other optimization algorithms (e.g., LARS) with large batches, and what were the outcomes?  
- Question: What is the computational cost and scalability of computing the sharpness metric for the networks tested and for larger models?
