

This paper makes a compelling contribution to understanding the generalization gap in large-batch deep learning training. The authors clearly articulate the problem: despite achieving similar training accuracy, large-batch methods consistently yield poorer generalization performance compared to small-batch methods. Their main claim is that this gap stems from large-batch methods converging to sharp minimizers (characterized by large positive eigenvalues in the Hessian), while small-batch methods converge to flat minimizers (with numerous small eigenvalues), and this difference in minimizer quality directly impacts generalization. The paper provides strong empirical evidence across six different network architectures using both parametric plots and a well-defined sharpness metric (Equation 4), consistently showing a 1-2 order-of-magnitude difference in sharpness between small-batch and large-batch solutions.

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The paper's main contribution is well-supported by rigorous numerical experiments that systematically investigate the relationship between batch size, minimizer sharpness, and generalization performance. The evidence is compelling, with consistent results across multiple network configurations and careful experimental design (including multiple random seeds). The paper also thoughtfully addresses limitations and attempts to remedy the problem, making a significant contribution to the field.

To further strengthen the paper, I recommend: (1) Adding a brief theoretical discussion on why the sharpness metric (Equation 4) correlates with the Hessian eigenvalues, as this connection is crucial to the paper's core argument; (2) Including convergence speed comparisons between large-batch and small-batch methods to address potential trade-offs in training time; (3) Clarifying the exact relationship between the sharpness metric and the Hessian spectrum in the main text to help readers better understand the theoretical underpinnings.

I have two specific questions for the authors: (1) Can you provide more details on how the sharpness metric (Equation 4) correlates with the actual Hessian eigenvalues in your experiments, particularly for the largest networks? (2) How do your findings relate to recent theoretical work on the landscape of deep learning loss functions (e.g., Choromanska et al., 2015; Soudry & Carmon, 2016)?
