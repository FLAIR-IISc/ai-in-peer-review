- Paper studies the large-batch generalization gap and hypothesizes LB methods converge to sharp minima while SB methods (via noisy gradients) find flat minima that generalize better.  
- Experiments span six architectures and multiple datasets to support the central hypothesis.  
- 1D visualizations along the linear path between SB and LB solutions consistently show LB solutions in much sharper basins.  
- The authors propose a novel, computationally feasible sharpness metric showing LB solutions are orders of magnitude more sensitive to perturbations than SB solutions.  
- SB training appears to perform an initial “exploration” that helps it escape sharp regions and locate flat basins.  
- Simple remedies (e.g., data augmentation, conservative training) yield partial improvement but do not fully resolve the LB problem.  
- Decision: Accept — work addresses an important, timely problem in scaling stochastic optimization for deep learning.  
- Decision: Accept — paper provides strong, compelling empirical evidence supporting its central claim.  
- Strength: Clear, focused hypothesis with experiments dedicated to isolating the phenomenon.  
- Strength: Convincing multi-faceted evidence (visualization + quantitative metric) consistent across architectures/datasets.  
- Strength: Warm-starting experiments give dynamic insight that SB’s early exploration locates flat basins usable by LB.  
- Strength: Thoroughness in investigating attempted fixes strengthens the claim that the issue is fundamental to optimization behavior.  
- Suggestion: Clarify and justify the form of the constraint set C in the sharpness metric (use of |(A+x)i|).  
- Suggestion: Report sensitivity of the sharpness maximization to the number of L-BFGS-B steps (only 10 were used).  
- Suggestion: Provide more details/evidence for the claim that sharpness rises only along a small-dimensional subspace (how observed/measured).  
- Question: Have the authors tried batch-size scheduling (start small to explore, then increase) as a practical mitigation, and with what results?  
- Question: How might adaptive optimizers like ADAM interact with batch size versus SGD with momentum — do they exacerbate or mitigate the sharpness/generalization issue?
