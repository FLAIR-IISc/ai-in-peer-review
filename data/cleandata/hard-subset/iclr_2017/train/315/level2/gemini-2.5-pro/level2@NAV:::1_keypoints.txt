- Central claim: large-batch training converges to sharp minima causing the generalization gap, while small-batch SGD noise guides optimization to flatter, better-generalizing minima.
- Strong empirical support across multiple network architectures and datasets.
- 1D parametric plots effectively visualize the loss-landscape differences between small-batch (SB) and large-batch (LB) solutions.
- Introduces a novel, computationally efficient quantitative sharpness metric moving beyond qualitative visualization.
- Metric shows LB solutions are 1–2 orders of magnitude sharper than SB solutions.
- Warm-starting experiment: LB can find good solutions if initialized in a flat basin discovered by an initial SB phase, implying SB noise enables exploration.
- Recommendation: strongly accept — clear hypothesis, timely problem, practical implications, and well-designed experiments.
- Improvement: better motivate the construction of constraint set C in Eq.(3) and the specific form (|(A+x)i| + 1) to strengthen metric credibility.
- Improvement: elevate discussion of the observation that LB sharpness concentrates in a low-dimensional subspace and speculate on causes.
- Question: does the batch-size threshold for generalization degradation correlate with the signal-to-noise ratio of stochastic gradients?
- Question: can the transition from “exploration” to “convergence” be characterized (e.g., via the sharpness metric or training loss trajectory) during initial SB training?
