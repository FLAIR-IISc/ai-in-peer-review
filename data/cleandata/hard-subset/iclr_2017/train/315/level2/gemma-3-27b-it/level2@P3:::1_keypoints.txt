- Central claim: Large-batch training converges to sharper minima that generalize worse, while small batches find flatter minima.  
- Evidence: Numerical experiments across architectures/datasets with visualizations and a proposed sharpness metric support this correlation.  
- Verdict: Reject — findings align with known phenomena and lack sufficient novelty or theoretical contribution.  
- Strength: Clear empirical presentation and comparisons across network configurations strengthen the observed correlation.  
- Weakness: Paper relies on observation/correlation without explaining why large batches favor sharp minima.  
- Weakness: Limited exploration of optimizers (primarily Adam) and modern regularization techniques reduces generality.  
- Weakness: Remedial strategies (data augmentation, conservative training, robust optimization) are reported unsuccessful but lack detailed hyperparameter/implementation info.  
- Weakness: Performance model in Appendix C is overly simplistic and offers little new insight.  
- Suggested improvement: Broaden literature review, including connections to Marchenko-Pastur and Hessian eigenvalue work.  
- Suggested improvement: Add theoretical analysis (even limited) on how batch size influences expected loss curvature.  
- Suggested improvement: Systematically study hyperparameter sensitivity (learning rate, momentum, weight decay) across batch regimes.  
- Suggested improvement: Evaluate alternative optimizers (SGD with momentum, L-BFGS, etc.) for small and large batches.  
- Suggested improvement: Justify and validate the sharpness metric against other flatness measures and noise robustness.  
- Suggested improvement: Provide detailed implementation and tuning of attempted remedial strategies and explain failures.  
- Suggested improvement: Expand Appendix C to explain assumptions and limitations of the performance model.  
- Open questions: Sensitivity to initialization — are effects stronger for certain initial conditions?  
- Open questions: How does minimizer sharpness correlate with training time to reach a given accuracy?  
- Open questions: Could differences in effective learning rate between batch regimes explain sharpness differences?  
- Open questions: Which dataset properties affect the observed effects, and do results generalize to more challenging datasets?
