- Hypothesis: Large-batch (LB) training converges to sharper minima while small-batch (SB) training finds flatter minima due to gradient noise, explaining LB’s worse generalization.
- Provides 1D and curvilinear parametric plots showing LB solutions lie in sharper loss landscapes than SB solutions.
- Introduces a computational “sharpness” proxy (Eq. 4) based on local sensitivity, evaluated in full space and random subspaces.
- Shows batch-size threshold experiments that reveal a phase transition in generalization and sharpness as batch size grows.
- Warm-start experiments: initializing LB from SB trajectories recovers good generalization once SB reaches flatter regions.
- Tests mitigation strategies (data augmentation, conservative/adversarial training) that help but do not eliminate LB sharp minima.
- Relates findings to MDL/Bayesian intuitions about flatness and recent theory on multiple minima with similar training loss.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Important, timely problem with thorough and compelling empirical evidence across models, optimizers, and seeds.
- Key reason 2: Insightful link between batch-induced gradient noise, basin escape, and flatness with actionable implications (e.g., dynamic sampling/warm-start).
- Supporting: Relevance and originality — warm-starts, batch-size thresholds, and sharpness correlations add novel perspective beyond anecdotes.
- Supporting: Empirical support — consistent results across six networks, multiple optimizers, five seeds, and both full/random-subspace sharpness measures.
- Supporting: Methodological clarity — sharpness proxy motivated as a scalable alternative to Hessian eigenanalysis and validated with complementary tools.
- Supporting: Practical implications — dynamic sampling/warm-start offers a concrete hybrid recipe for scalable training.
- Supporting: Literature connection — situates results within MDL/Bayesian views and acknowledges related landscape analyses.
- Weakness: Sharpness metric depends on parameterization and ε; normalization may not fully address scale invariances (ReLU, BN).
- Weakness: Hyperparameter confounders for LB not fully ruled out; no systematic LR/momentum sweep per batch size and ADAM used by default.
- Weakness: The maximization in Eq. (4) is approximate (10 L-BFGS-B steps) and may be sensitive to ε and subspace dimension p.
- Weakness: Training comparisons matched “until loss ceases to improve” rather than fixed compute/epochs, possibly confounding dynamics.
- Suggestion: Make sharpness more invariant using layer-wise normalization, spectral/path-norm, Fisher-based measures, or PAC-Bayes local entropy.
- Suggestion: Provide batch-size-aware hyperparameter tuning (LR scaling, warmup, momentum, weight decay) and report grid/Bayesian search results per batch size.
- Suggestion: Use Lanczos/Power iteration to estimate top Hessian eigenvalues for spectral validation on at least some models.
- Suggestion: Report sensitivity analyses for ε, p, and ascent iterations in Eq. (4) and include confidence intervals for sharpness and accuracy.
- Suggestion: Provide results under matched compute budgets (same epochs, gradient evaluations, and wall-clock) to rule out duration confounds.
- Suggestion: Evaluate gradient-noise injection or temperature-adjusted updates, and additional regularizers (label smoothing, mixup) as stronger baselines.
- Suggestion: Release code, full hyperparameters, and more seeds to facilitate replication.
- Question 1: How sensitive are sharpness conclusions to ε, p, and number of ascent iterations; can you show sharpness vs ε curves?
- Question 2: How do you address scale invariances from ReLU and BN; do layer-wise or path-norm-normalized measures preserve conclusions?
- Question 3: Did you systematically tune LR (including linear scaling/warmup), momentum, and weight decay per batch size; how much does careful tuning reduce the gap?
- Question 4: Do conclusions hold under fixed compute budgets (same updates or wall-clock)?
- Question 5: Can you report top Hessian eigenvalue estimates (via Lanczos) at SB and LB minima for at least one model?
- Question 6: In warm-starts, if SB is continued alongside LB from the same checkpoint with matched hyperparameters, do trajectories diverge predictably in sharpness?
- Question 7: Do batch-size thresholds scale with dataset size, model capacity, or optimizer choice; is there an empirical scaling law?
- Overall: Well-executed empirical study linking gradient noise, basin escape, and flat minima with actionable observations; stronger invariance controls and hyperparameter tuning would further strengthen the claims.
