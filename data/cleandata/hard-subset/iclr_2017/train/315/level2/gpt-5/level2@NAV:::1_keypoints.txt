- Central claim: Large-batch (LB) training converges to sharper minima with worse generalization, while small-batch (SB) noise guides to flatter minima that generalize better.  
- Empirical contributions: parametric loss plots across six architectures/datasets and a computationally feasible sharpness metric showing consistent LB sharpness and worse test performance.  
- Warm-start experiments: SB “exploration” helps LB reach flatter basins, linking gradient noise to flatness.  
- Mitigation attempts: data augmentation, conservative proximal steps, and adversarial training gave negative/partial results for closing the LB generalization gap.  
- Performance model: simple model motivating when LB can be valuable if the generalization gap is addressed.  
- Decision: Accept — paper is clear, timely, and provides useful empirical characterization and diagnostics.  
- Practical impact: offers diagnostic tools and insights likely to influence practice and follow-on research.  
- Experimental rigor: uses standard architectures/losses, multiple runs, and optimizer variants to build convergent evidence.  
- Sharpness metric caveat: metric is heuristic and maximization is inexact, but authors relate it to Hessian spectra and use full-space and random-subspace variants.  
- Limitations: lack of theory, potential sensitivity of sharpness to scaling/parameterization, and incomplete control of LB hyperparameters (learning-rate scaling/warmup, weight decay).  
- Suggested control: equalize total number of optimizer updates and report schedules, warmup, momentum, and weight decay for SB/LB.  
- Suggested analyses: sensitivity studies for sharpness metric hyperparameters (epsilon, p, L-BFGS-B steps, number of random subspaces).  
- Hessian triangulation: estimate top Hessian eigenvalues (power/Lanczos) to corroborate sharpness findings.  
- Noise baselines: compare to calibrated noise-injection in LB that matches SB gradient noise.  
- Dynamic batching: test simple dynamic batch-size schedules (motivated by warm-start findings) and quantify test sharpness.  
- Parameterization concerns: discuss invariance and batch-norm effects on sharpness measurements.  
- Question — training parity: were experiments equalized by number of updates vs epochs, and how does that affect the gap?  
- Question — hyperparam details: what LR scaling/warmup and weight decay were used for LB, and how sensitive are results to them?  
- Question — sharpness stability: how many random subspaces A were averaged, and how stable is φx,f across subspaces and epsilon?  
- Question — Hessian reporting: can you report top-k Hessian eigenvalues at SB/LB solutions?  
- Question — test-loss sharpness: does the sharpness–generalization link hold when measuring sharpness on the test loss?
