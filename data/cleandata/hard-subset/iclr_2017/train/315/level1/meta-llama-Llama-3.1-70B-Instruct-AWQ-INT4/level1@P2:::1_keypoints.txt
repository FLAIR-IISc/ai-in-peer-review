- Paper shows large-batch training converges to sharp minima causing poor generalization, while small-batch training finds flat minima that generalize better; evaluates remedies like data augmentation, conservative training, and robust optimization.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Clear problem statement: the generalization gap for large-batch methods is well-articulated and motivated.  
- Thorough analysis: provides detailed numerical experiments and some theoretical insights, and evaluates proposed remediation strategies.  
- Significant contribution: offers important insights into large-batch behavior with implications for training design.  
- Clarity and organization: writing is clear and paper is well-organized, though some sections need improved clarity/readability.  
- Theoretical foundations: theoretical analysis is limited and should better connect sharpness of minima to generalization performance.  
- Broader implications: the paper should discuss wider impacts on optimization and regularization in machine learning.  
- Question: please provide more theoretical details on the relationship between minimizer sharpness and generalization.  
- Question: how do these findings relate to other ML areas such as optimization and regularization techniques?  
- Question: provide details on computational resources required for the numerical experiments, especially for large-batch methods.
