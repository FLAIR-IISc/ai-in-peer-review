**Summary of the Paper's Claims**

The authors investigate the degradation in generalization performance of deep learning models when using large-batch methods for training. They provide numerical evidence that large-batch methods tend to converge to sharp minimizers of the training function, which are characterized by a significant number of large positive eigenvalues in the Hessian matrix. In contrast, small-batch methods converge to flat minimizers, which are more robust and generalize better. The authors also explore several strategies to improve the generalization performance of large-batch methods, including data augmentation, conservative training, and robust optimization.

**Decision**

Reject

**Reasons for Rejection**

1. **Lack of Theoretical Rigor**: While the authors provide numerical evidence for their claims, they lack a rigorous theoretical framework to support their findings. The paper relies heavily on empirical results, which may not be generalizable to other deep learning architectures or tasks.
2. **Insufficient Exploration of Alternative Explanations**: The authors attribute the degradation in generalization performance to the convergence of large-batch methods to sharp minimizers. However, they do not thoroughly explore alternative explanations, such as the effect of batch size on the noise in the gradient estimates or the impact of regularization techniques on the generalization performance.

**Supporting Arguments**

1. **Methodology**: The authors use a variety of deep learning architectures and tasks to demonstrate their findings. However, their experiments are limited to a specific set of hyperparameters, and it is unclear whether their results would generalize to other settings.
2. **Related Work**: The authors do not thoroughly discuss the relationship between their work and other research on the topic. For example, they mention the work of Hochreiter and Schmidhuber (1997) on flat and sharp minimizers but do not provide a detailed comparison of their findings.
3. **Sharpness Metric**: The authors introduce a new metric for measuring the sharpness of minimizers, but they do not provide a thorough analysis of its properties or limitations.
