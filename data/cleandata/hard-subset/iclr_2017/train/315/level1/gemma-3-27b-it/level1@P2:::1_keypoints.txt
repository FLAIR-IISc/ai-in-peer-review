- Paper claim: Large-batch training converges to sharper minima than small-batch training, explaining the generalization gap; supported by experiments and a computational “sharpness” metric, with limited remedy attempts.

- Recommendation: Reject — interesting observation but insufficient rigor and novelty for acceptance.

- Correlation vs causality: Empirical results show correlation between batch size and sharpness but do not prove sharpness causes the generalization gap; stronger controlled experiments are needed.

- Limited novelty: The large-batch → sharp-minima → poorer generalization idea is incremental and overlaps prior work; the sharpness metric feels ad-hoc rather than a major contribution.

- Weak remedies: Experiments on data augmentation, conservative training, and robust training are brief, under-detailed, and show limited/no significant improvement.

- Architecture justification: The choice of networks (AlexNet, VGG, etc.) is only justified as “popular”; a clearer rationale for selections is needed.

- Sharpness parameter sensitivity: Only two parameter values were used; a sensitivity analysis is required to assess robustness of the metric.

- Theoretical connection: The paper should better relate empirical findings to existing theoretical results and discuss whether theory predicts the observed relationship.

- Detailed remedy results: Provide more complete experimental details, tables/figures, and quantitative measures for each attempted remedy.

- Computational cost: Discuss the scalability and computational expense of computing the sharpness metric for large networks.

- Question — causality: Can you provide more rigorous evidence that sharp minima cause the large-batch generalization gap rather than merely correlate with it?

- Question — metric justification: What theoretical justification links your sharpness metric to Hessian spectrum or other curvature measures?

- Question — warm-starting LB from SB: Can you more thoroughly explore warm-starting large-batch runs from small-batch solutions and clarify limitations?

- Question — remedy hyperparameters: What hyperparameter tuning was performed for the remedies, and how sensitive are results to those choices?

- Question — metric cost: What is the computational cost of computing your sharpness metric for models with millions of parameters?
