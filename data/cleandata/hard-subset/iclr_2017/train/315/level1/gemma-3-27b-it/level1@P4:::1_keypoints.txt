- Main claim: Large-batch training converges to sharper minima that generalize worse than the flatter minima found by small-batch methods.
- Empirical evidence: Numerical experiments across networks and datasets show a correlation between batch size, sharpness of minima, and generalization performance.
- Mitigations tested: Data augmentation and conservative training were explored but yielded only limited improvements.
- Recommendation: Reject — evidence is interesting but not sufficiently convincing or generalizable in current form.
- Optimizer scope concern: All experiments use ADAM, so results may not generalize to SGD variants (e.g., SGD with momentum) or different learning-rate schedules.
- Sharpness metric issue: The proposed metric is a heuristic approximation (Eq. 4) and needs stronger justification or links to formal generalization measures.
- Theoretical gap: Paper lacks theoretical grounding connecting sharpness to generalization (e.g., PAC-Bayes, Hessian spectral analysis).
- Mitigation analysis weak: Authors do not deeply analyze why augmentation or conservative training fail to close the gap.
- Hyperparameter/ablation need: Systematic ablation on learning rate, regularization strength, and other hyperparameters is missing.
- Loss-landscape visualization: More comprehensive visualizations (e.g., PCA/t-SNE of landscape) are recommended to illustrate differences between minima.
- Matrix A choice unclear: Rationale for using a random matrix A in the sharpness metric and the effect of its dimension p are not explained.
- Convergence reporting missing: Convergence plots (loss vs. iterations) for small- vs large-batch runs are not provided.
- Statistical rigor: No statistical significance tests are reported for differences in sharpness or generalization.
- Question — optimizer generality: Do results hold with optimizers other than ADAM (e.g., SGD with momentum)?
- Question — metric sensitivity: How sensitive are results to the sharpness parameter λ in Eq. 4?
- Question — computational cost: What is the cost of computing the full Hessian versus the proposed sharpness metric?
- Question — mitigation failure explanation: Can authors explain in detail why augmentation and conservative training do not fully mitigate the gap?
- Question — theoretical connection: Can authors better connect observed sharpness to existing theoretical generalization frameworks?
