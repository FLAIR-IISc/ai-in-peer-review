- Paper studies how large-batch training converges to sharper minima and yields worse generalization, supported by experiments, visualizations, and a sharpness metric.
- Recommendation: Reject.
- Strength: Experiments are solid and provide additional empirical characterization across architectures and datasets.
- Main criticism: Lacks novelty — the central claim is already known and the paper mostly re-validates it.
- Analysis is descriptive and fails to deeply explain why large batches lead to sharper minima or fully connect sharpness to generalization.
- The chosen sharpness metric appears ad-hoc and is not rigorously justified or analyzed for limitations and biases.
- Attempts to mitigate the issue (data augmentation, conservative training, robust optimization) are cursory and do not yield insightful or meaningful improvements.
- The paper lacks a substantive theoretical foundation; related theory is cited but no new analysis is offered.
- Appendix C’s “idealized performance model” feels disconnected and adds little to the core argument.
- Suggestion: Provide rigorous justification and limitations for the sharpness metric and explore efficient proxies.
- Suggestion: Incorporate (even preliminary) theoretical analysis linking batch size, optimization dynamics, and loss landscape geometry.
- Suggestion: Perform deeper investigation into remedies, including broader hyperparameter searches or combined techniques, and analyze failure modes.
- Suggestion: More thoroughly compare to prior work on loss surfaces and generalization bounds to clarify novelty and contribution.
- Suggestion: Study how different network architectures influence the sharpness of minima obtained with varying batch sizes.
- Question: Provide stronger evidence that the generalization gap is not due to overfitting (e.g., feature analysis or sensitivity to input perturbations).
- Question: What is the computational cost and scaling of the sharpness metric, and are there cheaper proxies?
- Question: Why did the proposed remedies fail—what aspects of large-batch optimization hinder their effectiveness?
- Question: What are the theoretical implications of the findings — do they suggest modifications to generalization bounds or optimization algorithms?
- Question: Clarify the connection between the sharpness metric and Hessian eigenvalues and consider tools (e.g., random matrix theory) to analyze Hessian properties.
