- Paper studies why large-batch (LB) training often generalizes worse than small-batch (SB), hypothesizing LB finds sharper minima while SB’s gradient noise finds flatter minima.  
- Contributions: practical sharpness metric, 1D/curvilinear loss visualizations, multi-architecture/dataset experiments, warm-start studies, and evaluation of remedies (data augmentation, proximal/adversarial training).  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Reason: Clear, testable hypothesis with consistent, multi-faceted empirical evidence likely to influence research and practice.  
- Problem clarity: The LB-versus-SB generalization question is crisply posed and impactful for scalable training.  
- Motivation/placement: Well motivated via MDL/Bayesian arguments and connected to loss landscape and SGD-noise literature.  
- Evidence: Coherent results across architectures/tasks showing large differences in sharpness metric, batch-size thresholds, and distance-to-initialization effects.  
- Warm-starts: Initializing LB from SB iterates compellingly supports the noise-enables-escape-to-flat-basins mechanism.  
- Remedies: Multiple attempted fixes partially mitigate the gap, underscoring the problem’s nontriviality.  
- Rigor: Sharpness metric is heuristic and maximization inexact, but authors use full-space and random-subspace variants with consistent settings to triangulate effects.  
- Feedback — confounder control: Report learning-rate schedules, momentum/weight decay, and ensure matched training budgets (updates/epochs); analyze optimizer sensitivity (e.g., SGD+momentum vs Adam).  
- Feedback — metric robustness: Discuss scale/reparameterization invariance (especially with batch norm), sensitivity to epsilon and subspace dimension p, and include ablations or Lanczos-based eigenvalue estimates.  
- Feedback — quantify correlations: Provide correlations between sharpness and test accuracy across seeds and distributions of distance-from-initialization.  
- Feedback — noise characterization: Measure gradient noise scale versus batch size to directly link noise magnitude to basin transitions.  
- Feedback — remedies experiment: Try dynamic batch-size schedules motivated by warm-start findings as a concrete additional experiment.  
- Questions — LR tuning: How do results change under learning-rate scaling/warmup tuned for LB, and are conclusions robust under SGD+momentum?  
- Questions — invariance: Is the sharpness measure invariant under weight rescaling/BN reparameterizations, and can it be normalized to reduce such effects?  
- Questions — correlation strength: Do you observe a strong monotonic correlation between the sharpness metric and test error across runs?  
- Questions — Hessian validation: Can you report approximate top Hessian eigenvalues to validate the sharpness metric?  
- Questions — training-budget control: How were training budgets matched across SB/LB (updates, epochs, wall-clock), and does the gap persist under strict controls?  
- Questions — noise threshold: What is the gradient noise scale across batch sizes, and where is the observed “threshold” relative to that scale?
