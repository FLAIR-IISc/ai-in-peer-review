- Proposes a novel multi-view Bayesian non-parametric algorithm for multi-sense word embeddings that leverages multilingual distributional information.
- Leverages multilingual signals to improve sense embeddings beyond bilingual approaches.
- Demonstrates competitive performance with state-of-the-art monolingual models trained on much larger corpora.
- Decision: Accept.
- Reason 1: Tackles an important problem (multi-sense embeddings) with a well-motivated, novel approach.
- Reason 2: Provides strong empirical evidence via word sense induction and contextual word similarity tasks.
- Paper is well-written, with thorough related work and detailed algorithm description (including Dirichlet process prior and multilingual incorporation).
- Experimental results show high-quality embeddings using substantially less data and fewer parameters than prior SOTA.
- Suggestion: Analyze the effect of different language families and the impact of window size on crosslingual context.
- Suggestion: Explore application to other languages (e.g., Chinese) and the possibility of unsupervised multilingual WordNet-like resource generation.
- Suggestion: Consider adding rescaled Gaussian noise to gradients to exploit stochastic batching benefits.
- Question: Provide more implementation details on the Dirichlet process prior and its use for sense assignments.
- Question: How will the approach be extended to model polysemy in foreign languages, and what challenges/benefits are expected?
- Question: Provide analysis on hyperparameters (concentration parameter α and maximum senses T) and their impact on performance.
