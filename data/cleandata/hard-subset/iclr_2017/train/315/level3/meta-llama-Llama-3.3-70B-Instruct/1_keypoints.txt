The paper proposes a novel multi-view Bayesian non-parametric method for learning multi-sense word embeddings using multilingual distributional information.

The approach combines crosslingual training and Bayesian non-parametrics to produce high-quality embeddings with substantially fewer data and parameters than prior methods.

Reviewer recommends Accept, citing the paper’s importance and strong technical contribution to multi-sense embedding learning.

The work is well-motivated and positioned within related literature, building on crosslingual and Bayesian non-parametric research.

Experiments show significant improvements on word sense induction and comparable performance to state-of-the-art using much less data.

The paper includes a clear model description, learning procedure, and experimental setup.

The authors analyze effects of language family distance and context window size on model performance.

Reviewer suggests deeper analysis of learned sense vectors and their relationships to words and contexts.

Reviewer encourages exploring applications to other languages and tasks (e.g., NER, machine translation).

Recommendation to use more advanced evaluation metrics such as semantic similarity measures.

Reviewer requests clarification on parameter tuning strategies, especially hyperparameters and window size selection.

Reviewer asks for more details on computational resources and scalability to larger datasets and more complex tasks.
