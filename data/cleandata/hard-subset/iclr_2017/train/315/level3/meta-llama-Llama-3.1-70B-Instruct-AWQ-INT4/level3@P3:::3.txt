**Summary of the Paper**

This paper explores the drawbacks of large-batch methods in deep learning, specifically the degradation in generalization performance compared to small-batch methods. The authors investigate the cause of this phenomenon and present numerical evidence that large-batch methods tend to converge to sharp minimizers of the training and testing functions, which leads to poor generalization. In contrast, small-batch methods consistently converge to flat minimizers, which are characterized by better generalization properties.

Based on the content and quality of the paper, I decide to **Accept** it.
