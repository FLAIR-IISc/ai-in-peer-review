- Empirical study investigates how batch size affects deep neural network generalization.
- Main claim: large-batch methods tend to converge to sharp minimizers, causing poorer generalization.
- Approach is well-motivated and grounded in a thorough literature review.
- Paper is well-placed in the literature and makes a significant contribution to understanding the generalization gap.
- Extensive numerical experiments conducted across various architectures support the claims.
- Use of parametric plots and sharpness measures to characterize minimizers for large- vs small-batch methods.
- Results show large-batch → sharp minima, small-batch → flat minima, with flat minima generalizing better.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on the thorough empirical study and novel insights into the generalization gap.
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise presentation and well-designed experiments.
- Suggested improvement: provide more discussion of theoretical implications and relation to loss-surface theory.
- Suggested improvement: include more detail on the computational cost of the experiments and comparisons to other methods.
- Clarification requested: definition of the sharpness metric and its relation to the Hessian spectrum.
- Clarification requested: comparison of findings with theoretical models like Choromanska et al. (2015) and Soudry & Carmon (2016).
- Clarification requested: how the computational cost scales with batch size.
