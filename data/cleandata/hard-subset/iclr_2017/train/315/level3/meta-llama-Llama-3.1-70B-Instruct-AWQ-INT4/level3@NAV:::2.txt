**Paper Review**

**Summary**

This paper investigates the generalization gap in deep learning models trained with large-batch methods. The authors empirically study the convergence of stochastic gradient descent (SGD) with smaller batch sizes and find that it converges to flatter minima, which yield better generalization. They also provide numerical evidence that large-batch methods tend to converge to sharp minimizers, which are characterized by a significant number of large positive eigenvalues in the Hessian matrix of the training function.
