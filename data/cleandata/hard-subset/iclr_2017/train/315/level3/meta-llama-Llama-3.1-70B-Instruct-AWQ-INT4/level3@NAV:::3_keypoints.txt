- Paper studies why large-batch training generalizes poorly, showing large-batch converges to sharp minima while small-batch finds flat minima that generalize better.
- Explores remedies for large-batch generalization: data augmentation, conservative training, and robust optimization.
- Recommendation: accept — addresses an important problem with a well-motivated approach and thorough literature grounding.
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and easy to follow.
- Numerical experiments are convincing; parametric plots and sharpness metrics effectively illustrate batch-size effects.
- Suggestion: test adding rescaled Gaussian noise to gradients in large-batch regimes to emulate small-batch noise benefits.
