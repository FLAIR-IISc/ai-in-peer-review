The paper "On Large-Batch Training for Deep Learning: Generalization Gap and Sharp Minima" by Nitish Shirish Keskar, Dheevatsa Mudigere, Jorge Nocedal, Mikhail Smelyanskiy, and Ping Tak Peter Tang presents an empirical study on the effect of batch size on the generalization performance of deep neural networks. The authors investigate the cause of the degradation in generalization performance when using large batch sizes and present numerical evidence that supports the view that large-batch methods tend to converge to sharp minimizers of the training and testing functions, which lead to poorer generalization.

The authors provide parametric plots and sharpness measures to characterize the minimizers obtained by large-batch and small-batch methods. The results show that large-batch methods tend to converge to sharp minimizers, while small-batch methods converge to flat minimizers, which generalize better.
