The authors' main observation is that large-batch methods tend to converge to sharp minimizers of the training function, which are characterized by a significant number of large positive eigenvalues in the Hessian matrix. In contrast, small-batch methods consistently converge to flat minimizers, which are more robust and generalize better. The authors provide a clear explanation of the concept of sharp and flat minimizers and their implications for generalization.
