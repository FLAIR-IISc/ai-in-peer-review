This paper explores the cause of degradation in model quality when using larger batch sizes in stochastic gradient descent (SGD) methods for deep learning tasks. The authors investigate the convergence of large-batch methods to sharp minimizers of the training and testing functions, which are known to lead to poorer generalization. In contrast, small-batch methods consistently converge to flat minimizers, and the authors provide numerical evidence supporting the view that this is due to the inherent noise in the gradient estimation.

I decide to accept this paper with the following key reasons:

1. The paper tackles a specific and important problem in deep learning, namely the degradation in model quality when using larger batch sizes in SGD methods. The authors provide a clear and well-motivated approach to investigating this problem.
2. The paper supports its claims through a combination of numerical experiments and theoretical analysis. The authors provide a range of evidence, including parametric plots, sharpness metrics, and testing accuracy results, to demonstrate the convergence of large-batch methods to sharp minimizers.

The authors' approach is well-placed in the literature, drawing on existing work on SGD methods, deep learning, and optimization. The paper is clearly written and well-organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and results.

One potential limitation of the paper is that it focuses primarily on empirical results, without providing a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the convergence of large-batch methods to sharp minimizers. However, the authors do provide some theoretical insights and references to existing work, which helps to situate their results in the broader context of optimization and deep learning.

To further improve the paper, I suggest that the authors consider investigating whether adding rescaled Gaussian noise to gradients during the large-batch regime can yield benefits of the small-batch regime. This could provide additional insights into the role of noise in gradient estimation and its impact on the convergence of large-batch methods.

Some questions I would like the authors to answer to clarify my understanding of the paper and provide additional evidence to support their claims include:

* Can the authors provide more details on the specific architectures and datasets used in their experiments?
* How do the authors' results generalize to other optimization methods, such as Adam or RMSProp?
* Can the authors provide more theoretical insights into the convergence of large-batch methods to sharp minimizers, and how this relates to the existing literature on optimization and deep learning?

Overall, this paper provides a valuable contribution to the understanding of SGD methods and deep learning, and I believe it would be a valuable addition to the conference program.
