**Summary of Contributions**

This paper tackles the specific problem of understanding why large-batch gradient descent methods tend to perform poorly compared to small-batch methods in deep learning tasks. The authors provide a clear and well-motivated analysis of the issue, supported by numerical evidence and experiments on various deep learning architectures. They identify the convergence to sharp minimizers of the training function as the primary cause of the generalization gap in large-batch methods. The paper also explores several strategies to remedy this problem, including data augmentation, conservative training, and robust optimization.
