- LB training tends to converge to sharp minimizers with worse generalization, while SB training finds flatter minimizers that generalize better.
- Authors introduce a computationally feasible sharpness metric and validate it with extensive experiments across multiple architectures.
- Paper investigates strategies to mitigate the LB generalization gap (data augmentation, conservative training, robust optimization) but finds limited success.
- Work is well-motivated by scalability concerns and the importance of resolving LB limitations.
- Decision: Accept.
- Novel and relevant problem addressing timely issues in deep learning optimization.
- Rigorous empirical evidence: comprehensive experiments, multiple trials, parametric plots, and sensitivity analysis support claims.
- Paper is well-positioned in literature, building on sharp vs. flat minima and MDL theory.
- Sharpness metric aligns with theoretical concepts and shows robust empirical behavior.
- Practical implications: resolving the gap could enable more efficient large-scale training; explored remedies provide a foundation for future work.
- Suggest investigating adding rescaled Gaussian noise to gradients in LB regimes to mimic SB benefits.
- Request clearer analysis of why remedies (data augmentation, adversarial training) fail and conditions where they might help.
- Recommend deeper theoretical study of the density/distribution of sharp vs. flat minima in loss landscapes.
- Encourage more exploration of dynamic sampling/warm-start strategies, including theoretical analysis or additional experiments.
- Questions for authors: sharpness metric computational overhead and scalability; results of combining remedies (e.g., dynamic sampling + augmentation); sensitivity to optimizer choice (SGD vs. ADAM) and generality to other algorithms.
- Conclusion: valuable contribution and strong foundation that would benefit from further investigations and refinements.
