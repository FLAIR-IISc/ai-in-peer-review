- Decision: Reject.

- Summary: Paper studies how batch size affects minima sharpness and generalization, arguing large batches lead to sharper minima and worse generalization due to reduced gradient noise.

- Strength: Well-motivated, practically relevant investigation with extensive experiments across networks and datasets.

- Strength: Proposes a computationally feasible sharpness metric (Eq.4) and shows correlation between batch size and sharpness.

- Strength: Attempts remediation strategies beyond diagnosis, though not fully successful.

- Main concern: Empirical evidence is descriptive and not sufficiently robust to support strong causal claims.

- Metric concern: Proposed sharpness measure appears ad-hoc, its interpretation and sensitivity to hyperparameters are unclear.

- Causality concern: Connection between sharpness and generalization is correlational; necessity and sufficiency are not established.

- Confounding factors: Effects of architecture, regularization, and data augmentation are not systematically explored as interacting variables.

- Optimizer limitation: Experiments mostly use Adam, limiting generalizability to other optimizers (e.g., SGD with momentum, RMSprop).

- Overfitting claim: Assertion that the generalization gap is not due to overfitting is insufficiently supported and possibly overstated.

- Robustness request: Analyze limitations and hyperparameter sensitivity of the sharpness metric and compare with other flatness measures (e.g., Hessian eigenvalues).

- Mechanistic insight request: Investigate why large batches yield sharper minima—noise reduction vs. other high-dimensional optimization dynamics.

- Systematic exploration request: Jointly vary learning rate, regularization strength, data augmentation, and batch size to disentangle effects.

- Optimizer dependence request: Repeat experiments with multiple optimizers to assess whether findings are Adam-specific.

- Theoretical grounding request: Strengthen links to existing theoretical work on generalization in deep learning.

- Remediation request: Explore additional mitigation strategies (e.g., layer-wise adaptive rate scaling, advanced regularization).

- Question 1: How sensitive are conclusions to the regularization parameter in the sharpness metric, and were alternatives tested?

- Question 2: Were there cases where large-batch methods matched or outperformed small-batch generalization, and under what conditions?

- Question 3: Why was Adam chosen as the primary optimizer, and were other optimizers evaluated?

- Question 4: Provide more analysis of warm-starting large-batch training from small-batch solutions and optimal warm-start duration.

- Question 5: How was the claim that the gap is not due to overfitting assessed across architectures and datasets?
