- Claim: Paper attributes the generalization gap at large batch sizes to a shift from flat to sharp minimizers.
- Evidence: Provides numerical evidence that large-batch (LB) methods converge to sharper minima and that sharper minima correlate with worse generalization.
- Motivation: Well-motivated practical problem aligned with recent theoretical work on loss landscapes.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends accept with minor revisions.
- Empirical support: Parametric plots (Figs. 3 & 7) and sharpness metrics (Tables 3 & 4) convincingly support the core argument.
- Robustness: Observed consistent difference in sharpness between small-batch (SB) and LB across various network configurations.
- Ablations: Appendix E reports attempted remedies (data augmentation, conservative and robust training) but they did not fully resolve the gap.
- Analysis: Section 3 plausibly links SB success to gradient noise, connecting to SGD understanding.
- Performance model: Appendix C presents an idealized performance model that contextualizes potential benefits of closing the gap.
- Missing mechanism: Paper lacks a deeper explanation of why LB methods converge to sharper minima beyond attributing it to reduced noise.
- Metric limitations: The sharpness metric is an approximation and its limitations and validity need further discussion.
- Remedy analysis weak: Appendix E remedies are somewhat cursory; more detailed failure-mode analysis and parameter sensitivity are needed.
- Related work: Discussion of related work should be expanded to include more recent advances in loss landscape topology.
- Q1 (authors): Please elaborate mechanisms driving LB methods toward sharp minima beyond lack of noise.
- Q2 (authors): How sensitive are results to sharpness metric parameters (epsilon, manifold dimensionality p); was sensitivity analysis done?
- Q3 (authors): For Appendix E methods, why did they fail to close the gap; were hyperparameter sweeps performed and are there promising regimes?
- Q4 (authors): Any observed systematic effects of different initialization schemes on final sharpness?
- Q5 (authors): Were alternative sharpness measures (e.g., Hessian spectral analysis) considered, and if infeasible, why?
