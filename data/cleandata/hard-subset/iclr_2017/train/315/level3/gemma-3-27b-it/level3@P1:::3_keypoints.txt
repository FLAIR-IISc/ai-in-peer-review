- Paper links large-batch generalization degradation to convergence to sharper minima.  
- Numerical evidence shows large batches yield sharper minimizers while small batches find flatter minima.  
- Uses parametric loss plots and a proposed sharpness metric to illustrate differences.  
- Tested remedies (data augmentation, conservative training, robust optimization) with limited success.  
- Decision: Reject — analysis is descriptive and lacks deep theory or practical solutions.  
- Strength: Clearly articulates sharp minima as a plausible cause and shows consistent empirical correlation across setups.  
- Weakness: Does not investigate why large-batch methods gravitate toward sharp minima or connect to existing theory.  
- Weakness: Remedies are briefly presented and their failures are not deeply analyzed.  
- Weakness: Sharpness metric appears heuristic and needs stronger justification or links to established curvature measures.  
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on empirical observations without rigorous mathematical analysis.  
- Suggestion: Strengthen theoretical grounding by connecting observations to existing loss landscape/generalization theory.  
- Suggestion: Perform a deeper analysis of why proposed remedies fail and explore alternative strategies.  
- Suggestion: Provide rigorous justification for the sharpness metric and relate it to Hessian eigenvalues.  
- Suggestion: Evaluate alternative optimizers that encourage escaping sharp minima.  
- Suggestion: Investigate dynamic batch-size schedules that start small and increase during training.  
- Suggestion: Systematically study the interplay between batch size and learning rate.  
- Suggestion: Experiment with adding rescaled Gaussian noise to gradients in large-batch regimes.  
- Question: Why was ADAM chosen as the primary optimizer and were other optimizers tested?  
- Question: Elaborate how the sharpness metric approximates the Hessian Ritz value and the impact of matrix A.  
- Question: Quantify how much the remedies improve generalization and analyze their limitations.  
- Question: Do different activation functions affect the sharpness of found minima?  
- Question: What is the computational cost of computing the sharpness metric for very large networks/datasets?
