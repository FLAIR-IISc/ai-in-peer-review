- Investigates why large-batch (LB) SGD degrades generalization, showing LB tends to converge to sharp minima while small-batch (SB) finds flat minima.  
- Well-motivated work addressing a practical scaling challenge in deep learning.  
- Strong empirical evidence linking batch size, minimizer sharpness, and generalization performance.  
- Clear and compelling narrative connecting sharp vs. flat minima to generalization.  
- Properly situated within existing theoretical and empirical literature.  
- Experiments are well-designed across multiple architectures and datasets; parametric plots and proposed sharpness metric are informative.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations and discuss future research directions.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Suggest further investigation into mitigating the generalization gap (e.g., dynamic batch-size schedules and adding rescaled Gaussian noise during LB training as a regularizer).  
- Recommend quantifying how different network architectures affect susceptibility to sharp minima with large batches.  
- Recommend a theoretical analysis relating batch size, loss curvature, and gradient-descent dynamics to strengthen the contribution.
