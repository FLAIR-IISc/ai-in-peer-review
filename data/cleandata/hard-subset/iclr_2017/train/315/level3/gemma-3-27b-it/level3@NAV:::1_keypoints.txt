- Paper studies why increasing batch size degrades generalization, arguing large-batch methods converge to sharper minima that generalize poorly.  
- Well-written, engaging, and a valuable contribution to understanding a practical training challenge.  
- Reviewer recommends accepting the paper.  
- Analysis is well-motivated and builds on literature linking flatness of minima to generalization.  
- Empirical results show correlation between batch size, sharpness of minima, and generalization gap across architectures and datasets.  
- Parametric plots and the proposed sharpness metric provide convincing evidence, despite acknowledged limitations.  
- Strength: thorough experimentation (varying network configs and optimizers) and a clear sharpness metric.  
- Authors appropriately acknowledge limitations such as computational cost of sharpness calculation and inexact maximization.  
- Discussion effectively situates the work within recent theoretical advances on loss surfaces.  
- Request: perform sensitivity analysis of the sharpness metric to the choice of parameter λ.  
- Request: consider or discuss alternative, potentially more robust, measures of minimizer sharpness.  
- Request: examine how initialization influences large-batch convergence to sharp minima.  
- Request: provide more specific details on data augmentation used for each dataset and architecture.  
- Request: clarify choice of ADAM as primary optimizer and comment on whether results differ for other optimizers (e.g., SGD with momentum).
