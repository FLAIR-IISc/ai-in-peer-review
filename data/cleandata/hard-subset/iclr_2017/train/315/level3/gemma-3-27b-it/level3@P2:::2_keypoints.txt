- Study shows large-batch training empirically converges to sharper minima, which correlate with worse generalization than small-batch training.  
- Uses parametric loss-landscape plots and a proposed heuristic sharpness metric to support claims.  
- Proposes mitigation strategies (data augmentation, conservative training, robust optimization) but finds they only partially reduce the gap.  
- Decision: Reject — contribution is mainly empirical without strong theoretical grounding or novel algorithms.  
- Positive: Clear writing, consistent notation, insightful visualizations, and experiments across multiple architectures/datasets.  
- Positive: Sharpness metric is reasonable and used consistently, and experiments are relatively thorough.  
- Main limitation: Paper demonstrates correlation rather than explaining underlying mechanisms linking batch size, sharpness, and generalization.  
- Main limitation: The central observation (sharp minima generalize poorly) is known; showing large-batch leads to such minima is interesting but not a major breakthrough.  
- Limitation: Proposed remedies are not novel and do not fully close the generalization gap; analysis remains descriptive.  
- Improvement: Expand theoretical discussion and relate findings to frameworks like PAC-Bayes or MDL and suggest theoretical avenues.  
- Improvement: Provide rigorous justification of the sharpness metric, study sensitivity to hyperparameters (epsilon), and relate it to the Hessian spectrum.  
- Improvement: Conduct more systematic robustness experiments for conservative training and robust optimization across parameter space.  
- Improvement: Report concrete computational costs for calculating the sharpness metric and details to aid reproducibility.  
- Improvement: Provide more details on hyperparameter tuning, especially learning-rate choices for different batch sizes.  
- Questions: How does initialization affect sharpness and can different initializations mitigate the gap?  
- Questions: Were other strategies (optimizers, regularizers) tried but not reported, and why?  
- Questions: Is the threshold batch size for performance degradation dependent on architecture/dataset/optimizer, and can it be predicted a priori?  
- Questions: Provide detailed computational cost and approximations used for the sharpness metric relative to network and batch size.
