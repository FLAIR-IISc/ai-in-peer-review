- Paper studies why large-batch training degrades generalization, showing large batches converge to sharp minima while small batches find flatter minima.
- Decision: Accept — addresses an important practical problem with strong, convincing empirical evidence.
- Demonstrates correlation between batch size and minimizer sharpness; parametric plots (Fig.3, App D) show large-batch minima are sharper.
- Proposes a heuristic sharpness metric (Eq.4) that consistently shows significant sharpness differences (Tables 3–4).
- Experimental results across multiple network architectures and datasets (Table 1) support generality of findings.
- Connects results to prior literature on sharp/flat minima and MDL theory (Hochreiter & Schmidhuber).
- Warm-starting experiments (Fig.5) suggest small-batch exploration can improve subsequent large-batch performance as a mitigation.
- Situates findings within broader theoretical context and cites related work (Choromanska et al., Soudry & Carmon, Lee et al.), noting open questions.
- Major suggestion: a theoretical explanation for why large-batch methods gravitate to sharp minima (e.g., proving claim in Sec.4) would strengthen the paper.
- Feedback: the analysis of attempted remedies (Appendix E) should more deeply explain why approaches (like adversarial training) didn’t fully resolve the issue.
- Concern: sharpness metric relies on an approximate maximization using limited L-BFGS-B iterations; the impact of this approximation is not fully explored.
- Suggestion: include a sensitivity analysis on the number of L-BFGS-B iterations to test robustness of the sharpness metric.
- Question: clarify relation between choice of matrix A and accuracy of Ritz-value approximation; justify p=100 and show effect of varying p.
- Question: discuss dynamic batch-size strategies (e.g., start small and increase) to combine benefits of small- and large-batch training.
- Question: provide a more detailed analysis of why data augmentation and robust training failed to fully remedy large-batch generalization, including promising hyperparameter settings.
- Question: evaluate whether the sharp-minima phenomenon is specific to Adam or also occurs with other optimizers (e.g., SGD with momentum).
