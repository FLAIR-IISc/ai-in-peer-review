- Paper documents a consistent generalization gap as batch size increases.
- Links the generalization gap to convergence to sharper minima via visualizations and a computable sharpness proxy.
- Shows SB gradient noise helps escape basins leading to sharp minima; LB lacks such exploration.
- Evaluates remedies (data augmentation, conservative/adversarial training) and finds partial but insufficient fixes.
- Provides parametric and curvilinear loss-landscape plots contrasting LB and SB solutions.
- Warm-start experiments show LB can succeed after an SB exploration phase.
- Identifies batch-size thresholds where generalization degrades and sharpness rises.
- Well situated in prior literature (visualizations, MDL/Bayesian views, optimization theory) and practically relevant for scalable/distributed training.
- Decision: Accept.
- Main reason: clear, well-motivated empirical investigation isolating attraction to sharp minima as a plausible mechanism for LB generalization gap.
- Results are consistent across architectures/datasets and yield actionable guidance (e.g., dynamic sampling, warm starts).
- Parametric/curvilinear plots show steeper curvature around LB solutions on train and test loss.
- Sharpness metric shows 1–2 orders of magnitude higher sensitivity at LB minima across setups.
- Warm-start and batch-size sweep experiments support the exploration-by-noise hypothesis.
- Mitigation attempts help but do not eliminate sharpness or fully close the gap.
- Empirical rigor: multiple diagnostics, seeds, architectures; sharpness proxy justified though Hessian not computed exactly.
- Limitation: sharpness proxy sensitive to epsilon, subspace dimension p, and reparameterizations; scale invariance and batch-norm not fully addressed.
- Limitation: most experiments use ADAM; broader optimizer coverage and batch-size-aware LR schedules (scaling/warmup) are lacking.
- Limitation: exact Hessian/top eigenvalues not reported to directly corroborate curvature claims.
- Limitation: some controls (epochs vs updates, fixed regularization, BN treatment) could be better standardized.
- Suggestion: report sensitivity analyses for sharpness (vary epsilon, p, L-BFGS-B steps) and discuss reparameterization effects.
- Suggestion: complement proxy with top Hessian eigenvalue estimates (stochastic Lanczos) and per-layer curvature.
- Suggestion: include controlled comparisons with SGD+momentum using batch-size-aware LR schedules and warmup, and match parameter-update budgets.
- Suggestion: explore dynamic batch-size schedules (small-to-large) beyond warm-start experiments.
- Suggestion: examine how BN running statistics are handled across batch sizes and report reproducibility details and code.
- Suggestion: investigate layer-wise concentration of sharp directions and whether regularizers reduce sharpness.
- Question: how sensitive are sharpness measurements to epsilon, p, and inner optimization steps; what is variance across settings?
- Question: were top Hessian eigenvalues (or trace) evaluated at SB vs LB solutions?
- Question: how were LRs/schedules chosen for LB vs SB; were batch-size-aware scaling or warmup tried and are results robust?
- Question: how are BN statistics computed during training/eval for different batch sizes; could BN mismatches contribute to the gap?
- Question: are conclusions consistent when matching number of parameter updates rather than epochs, and when standardizing weight decay/regularizers?
- Question: do sharp directions align with specific layers or parameter groups; can per-layer adaptive regularization help?
- Question: in warm-starts, can the SB-to-convergence transition be quantified (e.g., gradient-noise scale) and predict LB success?
- Question: was sharpness measured on test loss around minima and does it track train-sharpness consistently?
