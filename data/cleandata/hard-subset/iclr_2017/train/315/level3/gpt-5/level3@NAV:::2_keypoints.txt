- Study of generalization gap: small-batch SGD yields flatter minima and better generalization than large-batch methods, supported by loss slices, a sharpness proxy, warm-starts, and batch-size thresholds.
- Decision: Accept — provides compelling, reproducible empirical evidence linking batch size, flatness, and generalization; removed an unsupported scale-invariance claim.
- Importance: Addresses an important question for scalability and theory and situates work among multiple hypotheses (overfitting, saddle attraction, lack of exploration).
- Experimental scope: Experiments span multiple architectures, datasets, and optimizers; classical overfitting is ruled out via training/testing curves.
- Mechanism evidence: Warm-start and threshold experiments suggest gradient noise enables exploration and escape from sharp basins.
- Metric caution: Sharpness metric is heuristic; authors avoid overclaiming and triangulate with complementary visualizations.
- Robustness: Consistency across settings strengthens credibility and suggests assumptions for future theory.
- Recommendation: Control and report training budgets and hyperparameters (iterations/epochs/updates, learning-rate schedules, weight decay, normalization) to avoid confounds.
- Recommendation: Calibrate sharpness with scale-aware or parameterization-invariant measures, and use Hessian spectral approximations and gradient-noise-scale estimates.
- Recommendation: Systematically quantify the dimensionality of the “sharp subspace.”
- Recommendation: Evaluate dynamic batch scheduling and explicitly compare to flatness-seeking methods (e.g., Entropy-SGD, SAM).
- Recommendation: Improve statistical reporting — variability across runs, significance tests, and optimizer ablations.
- Question: How were compute budgets matched across small and large batches (updates vs epochs vs wall-clock)?
- Question: Was learning-rate scaling plus warmup tried for large batches, and how did it affect sharpness/generalization?
- Question: Can you report Hessian top eigenvalues (e.g., power/Lanczos) to validate the sharpness proxy?
