- Main claim: Large-batch (LB) training converges to sharper minima and worse test generalization than small-batch (SB) training.  
- Evidence: 1-D/curvilinear plots, a local worst-case “sharpness” metric, batch-size sweeps, and warm-start experiments across models/datasets.  
- Warm-start result: LB can succeed when initialized near flat regions found by SB, implicating gradient noise in exploration.  
- Remedies tested: Data augmentation, conservative/adversarial training narrow but do not eliminate the sharpness/generalization gap.  
- Overall assessment: Well-executed, engaging study that meaningfully diagnoses a practical obstacle to scaling deep learning — recommendation: Accept.  
- Key reason for acceptance: Strong, consistent empirical support across architectures/datasets showing LB→sharp minima and worse generalization.  
- Scope/placement: Well-motivated by parallelism needs; properly situated among SGD/landscape/flat-vs-sharp literature.  
- Sharpness metric: Practical and shows large SB/LB differences but is heuristic and depends on parameterization and neighborhood choice.  
- Hyperparameter fairness: Unclear whether LB used batch-specific tuning (LR scaling, warm-up) or matched update counts, which could confound results.  
- Optimizer dependence: ADAM-focused analysis with limited main-text results for other optimizers; more optimizer-specific data would strengthen claims.  
- Causality: Link between sharpness and generalization is correlative/MDL-Bayesian intuition rather than formal causal proof.  
- Suggestion — hyperparams: Report detailed tuning per batch size (LR, schedules, momentum/Adam betas, weight decay, clipping) and ablations.  
- Suggestion — update parity: Report number of parameter updates/compute and include matched-updates experiments to disentangle step-count effects.  
- Suggestion — Hessian checks: Complement metric with spectral estimates (top Hessian eigenvalues via power/Lanczos) to corroborate sharpness.  
- Suggestion — parameterization: Discuss effects of BN/ReLU rescaling and whether sharpness is measured in parameter vs. function space.  
- Suggestion — remedies: Give exact adversarial/stability settings and try dynamic sampling (increasing batch size) per warm-start motivation.  
- Suggestion — optimizers: Include broader comparisons (SGD with momentum, RMSProp, Adagrad) with tabulated sharpness/accuracy in main text.  
- Key questions for authors: How were LRs/schedules chosen per batch size and were linear-scaling/warm-up tried?  
- Key questions for authors: What happens under matched parameter-update budgets (fix updates vs. epochs)?  
- Key questions for authors: Can you provide fuller SGD-with-momentum/Adagrad results and tuning details?  
- Key questions for authors: How sensitive is sharpness to ε, random subspace dimension p, inner-max iterations, and randomness in A?  
- Key questions for authors: Do simple reparameterizations (BN/ReLU scaling) change sharpness while preserving function values?  
- Key questions for authors: Have you tried dynamic sampling (gradually increasing batch size) and how does sharpness/generalization evolve?
