- Paper finds large-batch (LB) training tends to converge to sharper minima than small-batch (SB) training, explaining worse generalization.  
- Introduces a practical sharpness metric based on local perturbation maximization (and a random-subspace variant) showing 1–2 orders of magnitude sharper LB solutions at similar training loss.  
- Uses linear/curvilinear path visualizations and warm-start experiments to argue SB gradient noise enables exploration and escape from sharp basins that LB lacks.  
- Tests remedies (data augmentation, conservative updates, adversarial/robust training) that yield partial test-accuracy gains but do not eliminate sharpness.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Reason: Addresses a timely, practical question with clear empirical methodology linking batch size, sharpness, and generalization.  
- Reason: Results are actionable (e.g., warm-starting, dynamic batching) and suggest research on noise shaping for LB training.  
- Strong problem framing and motivation connecting parallelism pressures to observed generalization gaps.  
- Broad and consistent empirical evidence across architectures (FCNs, AlexNet/VGG-style) and datasets with repeated trials.  
- Methodological triangulation: path visualizations, full-space and subspace sharpness metrics, and warm-starts form a coherent narrative.  
- Practical insight: dynamic/warm-start strategies allow LB to succeed when initialized near SB-discovered flat regions.  
- Caveat: Sharpness metric depends on local box size and is only an approximation of spectral curvature; maximization is inexact.  
- Caveat: Hyperparameter fairness across SB/LB (LR scaling, momentum, update vs epoch counts, Adam specifics) needs more systematic control.  
- Caveat: Flatness/sharpness is not invariant to reparameterization; scale-invariant measures would strengthen conclusions.  
- Suggestion: Validate sharpness with spectral estimates—report top-k Hessian eigenvalues (power iteration/Lanczos).  
- Suggestion: Use scale-invariant/normalized flatness metrics (per-filter normalization, PAC-Bayes-inspired measures).  
- Suggestion: Rigorously control and report hyperparameters—use batch-size–aware LR schedules, momentum tuning, and clarify Adam settings and tuning per batch size.  
- Suggestion: Inject rescaled Gaussian noise into LB gradients (isotropic, layer-wise, or preconditioned) to emulate SB gradient covariance and test if it recovers flatness and accuracy.  
- Suggestion: Anneal injected noise with learning-rate decay (Langevin-style temperature) and evaluate LB+noise tradeoffs for parallel efficiency.  
- Suggestion: Explore dynamic batch sizing—start small and increase batch size guided by sharpness or gradient-noise scale.  
- Suggestion: Correlate sharpness with generalization gap across many seeds at equal training loss.  
- Suggestion: Quantify fraction/dimensionality of high-curvature directions to substantiate “small-dimensional steep subspace.”  
- Suggestion: Examine effects of BatchNorm statistics and regularization (weight decay, label smoothing) under LB vs SB.  
- Question: Were LR, momentum/Adam β’s, weight decay, and LR schedules tuned separately per batch size, and was linear LR scaling with warmup tested for LB?  
- Question: Are results consistent under SGD with momentum (vs Adam), and under iso-update or iso-compute regimes?  
- Question: How sensitive are conclusions to ε in the box, number of L-BFGS-B steps, and random subspace dimension p?  
- Question: Have you compared the sharpness metric to direct top Hessian eigenvalue estimates?  
- Question: Can you quantify the dimensionality (rank-k) of the steep subspace at LB minima and relate it to generalization?  
- Question: Have you tried adding rescaled Gaussian noise or SGLD-like updates to LB gradients, and does that reduce sharpness and close the generalization gap without hurting convergence speed?  
- Question: In warm-starts, how far (parameter distance and loss) are SB iterates from final SB minima when LB begins to succeed, and can LB move from sharp to flat basins without SB warm-start if explicit noise is added?
