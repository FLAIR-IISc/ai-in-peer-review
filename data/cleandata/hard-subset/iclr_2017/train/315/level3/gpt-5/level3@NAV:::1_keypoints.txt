- Main claim: Large batches tend to converge to sharp minima and generalize worse because small-batch gradient noise facilitates exploration to flatter minima.  
- Evidence: 1D and curvilinear loss-surface visualizations comparing SB and LB solutions.  
- Sharpness proxy: Computationally feasible local maximization in full space and random subspaces shows 1–2 orders of magnitude sharpness differences.  
- Batch-size threshold: Empirical evidence of a threshold where generalization drops and sharpness increases.  
- Warm-starts: LB training succeeds when initialized from SB-found flat basins, supporting a causal role for exploration.  
- Mitigations evaluated: Data augmentation, conservative/adversarial training tested; dynamic-batch growth suggested.  
- Positioning: Well situated relative to SGD stability, flat vs. sharp minima, and loss-surface literature.  
- Decision: Accept — clear, practically important question with strong empirical support.  
- Reproducibility/robustness: Results consistent across six architectures, multiple optimizers, and repeated trials; warm-start and threshold experiments add mechanistic insight.  
- Sharpness metric defended: Approximate but justified by ambient and random-subspace computations that align with Hessian-spectral intuition.  
- Negative-results value: Showing which remedies fail is informative and supports the proposed dynamic-batch remedy.  
- Request to clarify confounders: Match number of updates or epochs across SB/LB and report LR schedules, weight decay, momentum/Adam tuning fairness.  
- Metric sensitivity: Analyze dependence on ε and p in the sharpness metric and add scale-invariant flatness proxies (e.g., Hessian spectral norm, PAC-Bayes).  
- Batch-size reporting: Report absolute batch sizes (percentage-based can be misleading) and relate thresholds to gradient-noise scale.  
- Additional experiments suggested: Test more modern architectures/datasets and run gradual batch-size schedule experiments.  
- Question on training protocol: Were update steps equalized and were LR schedules tuned per batch size (linear scaling, warmup)? Sensitivity?  
- Question on metric robustness: How robust are sharpness gaps to ε, p, and inner-maximizer optimizer choices?  
- Request for Hessian corroboration: Provide Hessian spectral norm or top-eigenvalue estimates at SB vs. LB solutions.  
- Reparameterization issue: How do batch normalization and weight rescaling affect sharpness conclusions; are results invariant under common reparameterizations?  
- Quantification of threshold: Can the batch-size “threshold” be quantified via a noise-scale metric and is dynamic batch-size growth effective end-to-end?
