- Paper studies why large-batch (LB) training generalizes worse than small-batch (SB), attributing it to LB converging to sharper minima while SB gradient noise biases toward flatter minima.
- Introduces a practical sharpness metric, parametric (linear and curvilinear) loss-surface visualizations, and warm-start experiments; proposes dynamic sampling as a remedy.
- Preliminary mitigations (data augmentation, conservative training, adversarial training) reduce but do not eliminate the LB–SB generalization gap.
- Paper is timely, clearly written, and valuable to practitioners and researchers scaling training.
- Decision: Accept.
- Reason for accept: Tackles an important phenomenon with a well-motivated hypothesis and broad, coherent empirical evidence likely to influence practice and research.
- Problem framing: Generalization gap is clearly articulated and situated in SGD noise, MDL, and Bayesian perspectives with appropriate citations.
- Empirical evidence: Consistent findings across six networks and multiple optimizers; sharpness metric shows 1–2 orders-of-magnitude differences; parametric plots are compelling; warm-starts probe causality and suggest dynamic batch schedules.
- Rigor and transparency: Experimental setups described, multiple trials run, code promised; sharpness metric is heuristic but related to Hessian eigenvalues and consistent with theory.
- Suggestion — LB+noise: Add rescaled Gaussian noise to LB gradients (matched to SB gradient covariance/trace) and compare to SB, Entropy-SGD, and prior noise-injection methods.
- Suggestion — Controls: Match optimization budget fairly (updates vs epochs vs examples) and report LR/momentum/weight-decay tuning for SB vs LB, including linear warmup and LR scaling.
- Suggestion — Geometry: Complement sharpness metric with top-Hessian eigenvalues via power iteration or Lanczos to validate curvature differences.
- Suggestion — Invariance: Use flatness measures that are more reparameterization-invariant (e.g., account for filter rescaling/BatchNorm) to avoid confounds.
- Suggestion — Reproducibility: Release full training scripts and hyperparameter sweeps.
- Question: When SB and LB use matched total updates and carefully tuned LR schedules, how does the generalization gap change?
- Question: Does LB with rescaled Gaussian gradient noise (matched to SB covariance) recover SB-like generalization and flatness?
- Question: How sensitive are results to weight decay, momentum, and BatchNorm statistics (train/eval) across batch sizes?
- Question: Can you report top-1 and top-2 Hessian eigenvalues at SB vs LB solutions to corroborate the sharpness metric?
- Question: How does the “batch-size threshold” scale with model size and dataset size, and does dynamic sampling reliably cross that threshold?
