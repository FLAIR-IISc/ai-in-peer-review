- Empirical study linking SGD batch size to generalization in deep learning.  
- Small batch sizes converge to flatter minima (many small Hessian eigenvalues) and generalize better.  
- Large batch sizes converge to sharper minima and exhibit poorer generalization.  
- Strong numerical evidence across multiple architectures and datasets.  
- Resolves a previous scale-invariance concern by removing that claim, improving rigor.  
- Recommends acceptance; paper makes a significant contribution to understanding the large-batch generalization gap.  
- Practical implication: tuning mini-batch size can be a simple heuristic to improve generalization.  
- Robust visualization techniques and sharpness metrics support the conclusions.  
- Paper situates well within flat vs. sharp minima literature and connects to related work.  
- Methodological novelty is limited; primary value is thorough empirical exploration.
