**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper introduces a novel framework for automatically adapting and choosing the size of a neural network during a single training run, called Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNNs). The authors claim that NNNs can achieve a global training error minimum at some finite dimensionality when using a fan-in or fan-out regularizer with a specific penalty term. They also propose a new training algorithm, Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad), which is designed to work with NNNs. The authors evaluate their framework using three standard benchmark datasets and demonstrate that NNNs can achieve competitive results with those of an exhaustive random search.

**Decision: Accept**

**Key Reasons for Acceptance**

1. The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to automatically adapting and choosing the size of a neural network during a single training run.
2. The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of their framework, including a proof of the soundness of the nonparametric neural network formulation.

**Supporting Arguments**

* The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the proposed framework and its components.
* The authors provide a comprehensive review of related work and clearly position their contribution within the existing literature.
* The experimental evaluation is thorough and well-designed, with a clear presentation of results and a discussion of the implications of the findings.

**Additional Feedback**

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential drawbacks of the proposed framework.
* The authors may want to consider exploring the application of their framework to other domains or tasks, such as natural language processing or computer vision.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the AdaRad algorithm and its relationship to other optimization methods.
