- Presents a novel framework for automatically adapting and choosing neural network size during a single training run.
- Introduces a nonparametric neural network: a non-probabilistic optimization over all network sizes with a soundness proof using an `p penalty.
- Claims the framework can outperform parametric networks of the same size and compete with exhaustive random search.
- Approach is well-motivated with a thorough related-work survey.
- Provides a detailed theoretical proof of the framework’s soundness.
- Experimental evaluation on three standard benchmarks shows convergence to good sizes and higher accuracy than parametric nets.
- Limitation: evaluated only on a specific type of network—applicability to other architectures is unclear.
- Limitation: lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Reasons for acceptance: novelty, rigorous analysis/proof, and empirical support on multiple datasets.
- Suggested improvement: provide more analysis of framework limitations and potential applications to other network types.
- Suggested improvement: include a detailed computational-complexity analysis.
- Suggested improvement: add experiments on larger datasets to demonstrate scalability.
- Question for authors: how can the framework be applied to other types of networks (e.g., CNNs)?
- Question for authors: how does computational complexity scale as network size increases?
- Question for authors: can you provide more experimental results on larger datasets to assess scalability?
