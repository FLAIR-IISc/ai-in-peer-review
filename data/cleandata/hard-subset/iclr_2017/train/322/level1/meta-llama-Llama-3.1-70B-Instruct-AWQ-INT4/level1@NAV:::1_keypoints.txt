- Introduces Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNNs) that adapt and choose network size during a single training run.  
- Claims NNNs can reach a global training error minimum at finite dimensionality using fan-in/fan-out regularizers with a specific penalty.  
- Proposes Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad) as a new training algorithm for NNNs.  
- Evaluated on three standard benchmarks and demonstrates competitive performance versus exhaustive random search.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novel and well-motivated approach for automatic network size adaptation in one training run.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis, including a proof supporting the nonparametric formulation.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and situates contributions within related work.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, with clear results presentation and discussion of implications.  
- Could benefit from a more detailed discussion of limitations and potential drawbacks.  
- Suggest exploring applications to other domains (e.g., NLP, computer vision).  
- Needs a more detailed explanation of AdaRad and its relationship to other optimization methods.  
- Question: Rationale for the specific penalty term used in the fan-in and fan-out regularizers?  
- Question: How to extend the framework to convolutional layers and automatically choose network depth?  
- Question: What is the computational cost of AdaRad and how does it compare to other optimizers?
