The paper "Nonparametric Neural Networks" proposes a novel framework for automatically determining the optimal size of a neural network for a given task without prior information. The authors introduce nonparametric neural networks, a non-probabilistic framework for conducting optimization over all possible network sizes. They prove the soundness of this framework when network growth is limited via an `p penalty. The authors also propose a novel optimization algorithm, Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad), which is used to train nonparametric networks.

The paper claims to contribute to the field of neural networks by providing a framework for automatically adapting and choosing the size of a neural network during a single training run. The authors also claim that their framework can improve the performance of trained nets beyond what is achieved by regular parametric networks of the same size.

The approach is well-motivated, and the authors provide a thorough discussion of the limitations of existing methods for model selection. The use of nonparametric neural networks and the AdaRad algorithm is a novel contribution to the field.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their framework and algorithm. The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the results are promising.
