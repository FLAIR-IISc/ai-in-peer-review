- Introduces "nonparametric neural networks" that dynamically add/remove units during a single training run to adapt network size.
- Provides theoretical analysis proving the objective attains a global minimum at some finite dimensionality with fan-in/fan-out regularizers.
- Proposes a new optimization algorithm, Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad), tailored to the nonparametric framework.
- Experimental results show nonparametric networks outperform parametric networks of the same size on two of three datasets.
- Demonstrates scalability by applying the framework to a large dataset.
- Overall assessment: compelling approach with strong theoretical and experimental support — recommendation: Accept.
- Strength: Clearly addresses automatic determination of network size and is well-motivated within pruning/regularization literature.
- Strength: Thorough theoretical backing and well-written exposition.
- Limitation: Requires careful tuning of hyperparameters (regularization λ and angular step size α); guidance is lacking.
- Suggestion: More discussion needed on how the framework compares to other pruning/regularization approaches, including pros/cons.
- Suggestion: Provide more detailed per-dataset analysis to explain when and why nonparametric networks succeed.
- Question for authors: Provide practical guidance for choosing hyperparameters λ and α.
- Question for authors: Clarify comparisons and trade-offs versus other pruning/regularization methods.
- Question for authors: Offer deeper analysis of dataset-specific performance factors.
