- Proposes a novel nonparametric neural network framework to automatically determine optimal network size during a single training run.  
- Introduces a non-probabilistic optimization over all network sizes and proves soundness when growth is limited via an p penalty.  
- Presents a new optimization algorithm, Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad), to train nonparametric networks.  
- Claims the framework can adapt network size automatically and potentially outperform parametric nets of the same size.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and provides a thorough discussion of existing model-selection limitations.  
- Experimental evaluation is promising but conducted on only a few datasets.  
- Lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability for large-scale networks.  
- Does not provide sufficient details on hyperparameter tuning for AdaRad.  
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions.  
- Requested revisions: add computational-complexity analysis, evaluate on more diverse datasets, and detail AdaRad hyperparameter tuning.  
- Questions for authors: provide hyperparameter-tuning details for AdaRad; describe plans to extend to convolutional layers and automatic depth selection; clarify computational complexity and large-scale implications.
