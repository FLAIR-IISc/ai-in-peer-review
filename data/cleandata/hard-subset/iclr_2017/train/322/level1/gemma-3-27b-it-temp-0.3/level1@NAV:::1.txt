## Review of "NONPARAMETRIC NEURAL NETWORKS"

This paper introduces a novel framework, Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNNs), for automatically determining the optimal size of a neural network during a single training cycle. The core idea is to dynamically grow and shrink the network, guided by an L2 penalty on weights, and employs a new optimization algorithm called Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad). The authors provide theoretical justification for their approach and demonstrate promising results on several datasets.

**Decision: Reject**

While the paper presents an interesting idea and a technically detailed approach, the experimental results are not compelling enough to warrant acceptance at this time. Specifically, the performance gains over well-tuned parametric networks are inconsistent across datasets, and the results on the more complex poker dataset, while promising, lack sufficient detail to fully assess the method’s scalability and practical benefits.

The paper is well-motivated, situating itself within the broader context of neural architecture search and network pruning. The theoretical foundation, establishing conditions for achieving a global minimum, is a significant contribution. The AdaRad algorithm is also a thoughtful attempt to address the challenges of training networks with dynamically changing size. However, the experimental evaluation feels somewhat limited. The comparison to parametric networks is not always fair (e.g., the grid search parameters are not fully detailed), and the ablation studies are lacking. The claim of achieving higher accuracy than possible with black-box methods is not convincingly demonstrated. The analysis of the training process is insightful, but doesn't fully explain *why* NNNs sometimes outperform or underperform parametric networks.

**Additional Feedback:**

*   **Experimental Rigor:** Provide a more thorough comparison to state-of-the-art neural architecture search (NAS) methods, even if they require more computational resources. A more detailed description of the hyperparameter search space for the parametric networks is needed.
*   **Scalability:** The poker dataset experiment is a good start, but more extensive evaluation on larger and more complex datasets is crucial. Report training times for both NNNs and parametric networks to provide a clearer picture of the computational cost.
*   **Ablation Studies:** Investigate the impact of different components of the AdaRad algorithm (radial vs. angular updates, momentum) and the choice of regularization parameter.
*   **Theoretical Implications:** Explore the practical implications of the theoretical results. What specific network architectures are favored by the L2 penalty?
*   **Clarification:** The interplay between the L2 penalty and the CapNorm layer is not entirely clear. How do these two mechanisms interact to prevent overfitting and promote generalization?

**Questions for the Authors:**

*   Could you provide a more detailed breakdown of the computational cost (training time, memory usage) for both NNNs and parametric networks on the poker dataset?
*   What specific strategies were used to tune the hyperparameters for the parametric networks? Were any automated hyperparameter optimization techniques employed?
*   How sensitive are the results to the choice of the initial angular step size (αφ)?
*   Could you elaborate on the limitations of the current framework and potential avenues for future research?
