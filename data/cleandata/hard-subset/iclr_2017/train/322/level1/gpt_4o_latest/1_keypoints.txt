- Paper proposes nonparametric neural networks that grow/prune units during a single training run using fan-in/fan-out regularizers, introduces AdaRad optimizer and CapNorm, and proves existence of a global minimum under conditions.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason — Novelty/significance: provides a practical alternative to costly hyperparameter search by automating network size.  
- Key reason — Theoretical rigor: presents proofs showing convergence/global minimum under the proposed regularization.  
- Key reason — Empirical validation: experiments show competitive or superior accuracy and compact architectures across benchmarks, with scalability evidence.  
- Supporting: Well-motivated positioning relative to prior work (e.g., pruning, Bayesian nets, Wei et al.).  
- Supporting: Design choices (self-similar nonlinearities, CapNorm) are justified theoretically.  
- Supporting: Training-dynamics analysis offers insights; nonparametric nets can beat same-size parametric nets while being computationally efficient.  
- Suggestion: Improve clarity—simplify notation and make AdaRad derivation and appendix proofs more accessible.  
- Suggestion: Add comparisons to other adaptive architecture methods (e.g., RL-based NAS like Zoph & Le).  
- Suggestion: Provide more scalability experiments on larger/complex datasets (e.g., ImageNet).  
- Suggestion: Analyze hyperparameter sensitivity (λ, αr, αφ) and give tuning guidance.  
- Question: How does the method handle highly imbalanced datasets or noisy labels—does the regularizer adapt well?  
- Question: Can the framework be extended to convolutional or recurrent architectures, and what challenges would arise?  
- Question: What is AdaRad’s practical computational cost compared to Adam/RMSprop for very large networks?  
- Conclusion: Strong theoretical and practical contribution with minor clarity and experimental extensions needed to broaden impact.
