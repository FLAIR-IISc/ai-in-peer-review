- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that automatically determine hidden-layer widths within a single training run.  
- Proves the joint optimization over weights and layer sizes attains a finite global minimum when using a fan-in or fan-out p-norm regularizer.  
- Proposes a grow-and-prune training algorithm that adds units periodically and prunes redundant ones via an l2 fan-in regularizer to induce group sparsity.  
- Introduces Capped Batch Normalization (CapNorm) to make normalization compatible with sparsity-inducing regularization.  
- Introduces AdaRad optimizer that decomposes gradients into radial and angular components and normalizes only the angular part to protect new units.  
- Empirical results show the method finds compact, well-performing architectures competitive with exhaustive random search on MNIST and rectangles-images.  
- Provides detailed analyses visualizing layer-size evolution and neuron lifespans to illustrate training dynamics.  
- Transparently reports weaker performance on the 'convex' dataset and includes a scalability experiment on the large 'poker' dataset.  
- Recommendation: Accept — novel framework with strong theoretical justification and a technically sound practical method.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-structured, and motivates the approach by contrasting it with black-box architecture search.  
- Theorem 1 is a significant theoretical contribution that adds rigor to architecture search literature.  
- AdaRad is a creative, well-justified solution to training newly added units without premature regularizer-driven elimination.  
- CapNorm shows careful consideration of interactions between normalization and sparsity regularization.  
- Suggest performing a sensitivity analysis of the radial step size αr relative to regularization λ (αr = 150λ heuristic).  
- Recommend exploring adaptive unit-addition schedules (e.g., only when learning stagnates or targeting high-gradient layers).  
- Suggest experiments/discussion showing how different λ values affect the size–performance trade-off, especially for the 'convex' task.  
- Recommend an ablation comparing AdaRad to Adam/RMSprop on fixed architectures to evaluate its general usefulness.  
- Question: How will l2 fan-in pruning interact with other regularizers like dropout — complementary or interfering?  
- Question: Does the dynamic add/remove process act as implicit regularization explaining better validation but higher training loss?  
- Question: Can you provide empirical wall-clock comparisons of AdaRad vs optimizers like RMSprop on modern GPUs?
