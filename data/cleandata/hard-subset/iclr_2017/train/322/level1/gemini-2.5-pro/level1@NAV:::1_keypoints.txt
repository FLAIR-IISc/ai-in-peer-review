- Introduces "nonparametric neural networks" that dynamically grow and shrink network width during a single training run to automate model selection.  
- Formulates a theoretically-grounded optimization over architectures with a global minimum at finite network size under p fan-in/fan-out regularization (Theorem 1).  
- Proposes a practical training strategy that continuously adds new units and prunes redundant ones.  
- Introduces Capped Batch Normalization (CapNorm), a batch-norm variant compatible with the regularizer.  
- Proposes Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad), an optimizer that helps new units train without being prematurely eliminated.  
- Empirical results show compact, high-performing networks competitive with or superior to fixed-size baselines and random architecture search on several benchmarks.  
- Strong experimental analysis, including visualizations of neuron lifespans and weight dynamics (Section 4.2).  
- Method underperformed on the convex dataset; authors acknowledge and discuss this limitation.  
- Decision: Accept — well-motivated, theoretically sound, strong technical contributions, and thorough empirical evaluation.  
- Suggestion: Clarify the term "nonparametric neural networks" to avoid confusion with Bayesian nonparametric methods.  
- Suggestion: Provide a deeper discussion and hypotheses on the convex dataset failure and characteristics causing suboptimal convergence.  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the unit-addition schedule and have adaptive schedules been tried (e.g., add units when learning plateaus)?  
- Question: New-unit initialization is random — have more informed initialization schemes (e.g., correcting largest current errors) been considered?
