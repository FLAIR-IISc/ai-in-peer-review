- Introduces "nonparametric neural networks" that grow and prune units during a single training run to optimize layer widths and weights jointly.  
- Proves Theorem 1: under lp fan-in/fan-out regularization, a global minimum exists at a finite network size, preventing unbounded growth.  
- Implements dynamic growth/pruning with periodic unit addition and l2 fan-in (group-lasso-like) regularization to remove redundant units.  
- Proposes CapNorm, a capped batch normalization variant that preserves the regularizer's effect on weight magnitudes.  
- Proposes AdaRad, an optimizer that decouples angular (direction) and radial (magnitude) updates so new units can learn before being regularized away.  
- Experiments show competitive, sometimes superior, compact architectures on benchmarks and include a scalability test on the poker dataset.  
- Decision: Recommend accept, citing novelty and theoretical grounding.  
- Strength: Principled, elegant reframing of architecture search as continuous optimization over weights and structure.  
- Strength: New technical contributions (AdaRad and CapNorm) effectively address training dynamics of growing networks.  
- Strength: Insightful analysis and visualizations of growth/shrink dynamics and unit usefulness.  
- Strength: Honest empirical reporting including a failure case on the convex dataset and comparisons to random search.  
- Weakness: Mixed empirical performance; failure on the convex dataset suggests dataset-dependent effectiveness.  
- Weakness: Hard to disentangle contributions of dynamic architecture, AdaRad, and CapNorm because multiple novelties are introduced simultaneously.  
- Weakness: Introduces new hyperparameters (radial/angular steps, unit addition rate), so method is not fully automatic and requires tuning.  
- Feedback: The term "nonparametric" may be misleading; consider clarifying terminology relative to statistics usage.  
- Feedback: Consider adaptive unit-addition schedules (e.g., add units when validation error plateaus) instead of fixed schedules.  
- Feedback: Analysis of fan-in/fan-out norms shows interesting self-organization, especially with self-similar activations.  
- Question: Why did the method perform poorly on the convex dataset—does it need much larger networks or is training unstable for that problem?  
- Question: How critical is the radial-angular decomposition in AdaRad; can standard optimizers like Adam work with this framework?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to coupling αr = 150λ; would decoupling radial step size and regularization strength improve flexibility or performance?
