- Proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that grow by adding zero-initialized units and prune via L2 fan-in/fan-out regularization to optimize width in a single training run.  
- Theorem 1 shows the training objective attains a global minimum at a finite network size under the proposed regularizer.  
- Introduces CapNorm, a modified batch normalization that prevents the regularizer from being nullified.  
- Introduces AdaRad optimizer, decomposing updates into radial and angular parts and normalizing angular updates so new units can learn without being prematurely pruned.  
- Empirical results show the method finds compact, high-performing architectures and outperforms fixed-size networks and random search on MNIST and rectangles-images.  
- Demonstrates some scalability on the larger poker dataset.  
- Acknowledges failure on the convex dataset, openly reporting mixed empirical results.  
- Recommendation to accept based on novelty, theoretical grounding, and principled algorithmic contributions.  
- Suggests deeper analysis/hypotheses for the convex dataset failure (loss landscape, required features, or optimizer interaction).  
- Requests sensitivity analysis of new hyperparameters (λ, αr, αφ, unit-addition schedule ν) and the impact of the αr = 150λ relationship.  
- Recommends a direct empirical comparison with pruning methods (train final architecture from scratch and prune) as a benchmark.  
- Asks for a clearer, high-level intuitive explanation of why AdaRad normalizes angular updates using a running average of squared norms.  
- Question: Do the authors have hypotheses about why the method fails on the convex dataset (e.g., local minima or dataset-method mismatch)?  
- Question: What is the practical runtime overhead of AdaRad compared to standard optimizers like Adam (actual epoch time)?  
- Question: Were experiments run with standard Batch Normalization to empirically confirm the necessity of CapNorm?  
- Question: Have adaptive unit-addition schedules (e.g., add units when validation loss plateaus) been tried instead of the fixed one-per-layer-per-epoch rule?
