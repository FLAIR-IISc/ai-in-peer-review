- Introduces "nonparametric neural networks": joint optimization over weights and network width using group-sparsity regularizer that guarantees a finite global minimum.
- Proposes a dynamic training procedure that periodically adds functionally-neutral units while an l2 fan-in regularizer prunes redundant neurons.
- Develops CapNorm (batch-norm modification) and AdaRad (optimizer separating radial and angular updates) to enable new units to learn before being pruned.
- Proves Theorem 1 guaranteeing the objective does not incentivize infinite network growth; proof covers general nonlinearities and losses.
- Shows strong empirical results: finds compact, high-performing architectures, outperforming fixed-size nets and extensive random search on several benchmarks.
- Provides detailed analysis of training dynamics and a scalability experiment on a larger dataset; honestly reports a failure case on the 'convex' dataset.
