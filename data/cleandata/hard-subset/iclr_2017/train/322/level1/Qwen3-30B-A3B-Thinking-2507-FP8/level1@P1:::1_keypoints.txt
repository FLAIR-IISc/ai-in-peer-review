- Paper proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that automatically determine network size during a single training cycle using AdaRad and CapNorm.  
- Authors prove (Theorem 1) that with an l2 fan-in regularizer the optimization has a global minimum at finite dimensionality.  
- Claim: networks can dynamically grow/shrink and outperform parametric networks of the same size and match exhaustive search.  
- Empirical results are mixed: promising on MNIST and rectangles images but poor on the convex dataset.  
- Recommendation: reject.  
- Main rejection reasons: inconsistent empirical performance across datasets and incremental theoretical novelty relative to pruning/growth literature.  
- On convex data, nonparametric networks underperform comparable parametric networks and retraining does not recover performance.  
- Theorem 1 is technically sound but offers limited novelty beyond existing sparse-regularization and pruning work.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to three datasets and lacks analysis of why the method fails on convex.  
- The claim of "globally best performance" on rectangles images is misleading (based on median error, not absolute best).  
- Paper insufficiently distinguishes itself from prior work (e.g., Alvarez & Salzmann 2016; Molchanov et al. 2017; Guo et al. 2016).  
- Needs expanded comparisons to state-of-the-art network size selection and growth methods (e.g., Chen et al. 2016, Guo et al. 2016).  
- Should include computational-efficiency comparisons (not just memory footprint).  
- More analysis required on dataset properties that cause performance differences, especially why convex fails.  
- Provide details and justification for the unit addition schedule and its effect on convergence.  
- Questions for authors: compare nonparametric vs parametric at equal sizes; explain convex dataset failure causes; clarify poker test-set size and SOTA comparison; compare to Net2Net and Dynamic Network Surgery in performance and efficiency; propose fixes to prevent premature convergence to small sizes.
