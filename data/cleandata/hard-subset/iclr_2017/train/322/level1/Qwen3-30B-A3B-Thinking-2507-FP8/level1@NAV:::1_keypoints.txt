- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that automatically determine optimal network size during a single training run by dynamically adding/removing units.  
- Maintains network functionality via f-equivalence when changing architecture.  
- Proves Theorem 1 showing the nonparametric framework attains a global minimum under specific conditions.  
- Proposes AdaRad, a novel radial-angular gradient update training algorithm (and AdaRad-M variant with momentum).  
- Experimental results show nonparametric networks often match or outperform parametric networks of similar size while using fewer parameters.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, with comparisons to relevant baselines and exhaustive search methods across multiple datasets.  
- MNIST and rectangles experiments are particularly convincing in favor of the approach.  
- Recommends acceptance, citing significant contribution to model selection in deep learning and strong theoretical/empirical support.  
- Suggests adding a detailed discussion of computational efficiency and the trade-off between training time and parameter count.  
- Requests more insight into why the convex dataset was challenging and which dataset characteristics affect method effectiveness.  
- Asks for clarification on scenarios where AdaRad-M (momentum) might be beneficial, given similar performance to AdaRad.
