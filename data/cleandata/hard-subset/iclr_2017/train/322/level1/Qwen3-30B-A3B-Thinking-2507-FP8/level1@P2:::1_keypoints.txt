- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that dynamically adjust network size during training to select architecture automatically.
- Proves Theorem 1 showing nonparametric networks with a 2 fan-in regularizer reach a finite-dimensional global minimum; proof is rigorous.
- Proposes AdaRad, a novel training algorithm separating radial and angular updates, and CapNorm, a normalization adapted to the fan-in regularizer.
- Claims the method can find good network sizes in a single run, outperform parametric networks of the same size, and rival exhaustive search.
- Empirical results are promising on MNIST and rectangles images but perform poorly on the convex dataset.
- Recommendation: accept with reservations — strong theoretical contribution but mixed empirical evidence.
- Key strength: theoretical soundness — the paper thoroughly addresses differentiability and degeneration concerns.
- Key strength: algorithmic novelty — AdaRad and CapNorm are well-motivated to enable adding/removing units safely.
- Major weakness: failure on the convex dataset is unexplained and needs deeper analysis and comparison to parametric baselines.
- Major weakness: scalability and computational cost of AdaRad are not quantified; training-time comparisons are missing.
- Major weakness: unclear how unit addition rate affects convergence and no principled method for choosing the rate is provided.
- Major weakness: regularization parameter λ is used dataset-wise without systematic analysis of its effect on size and performance.
- Question: Why do nonparametric networks fail on the convex dataset — dataset properties or a fundamental limitation?
- Question: How does the small size found by nonparametric training specifically hurt performance on the convex dataset — general or dataset-specific?
- Question: Provide quantitative comparison (test errors) between best nonparametric and best parametric models on the convex dataset.
- Question: Provide detailed comparisons between AdaRad and AdaRad-M (e.g., test error differences and convergence speed).
