- Introduces "nonparametric neural networks" that grow and prune units during a single training run to automatically determine network size.
- Proves (Theorem 1) that under fan-in/fan-out regularization (λ>0, 1≤p<∞) the method attains a global training error minimum at finite dimensionality.
- Proposes AdaRad (Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent) and CapNorm (Capped Batch Normalization) to enable continuous unit addition/pruning.
- Uses L2 penalty to eliminate redundant units while dynamically adding new ones, avoiding multiple full architecture searches.
- Decision: recommendation to accept based on theoretical and empirical contributions.
- Theoretical soundness: proof in Appendix 7.1 is comprehensive and addresses degenerate infinite-network concerns with realistic nonlinearity/error assumptions.
- Empirical validation: outperforms comparable-size parametric nets on MNIST, achieves best results on rectangles with fewer parameters, and scales to large datasets (poker, 1.025M examples).
- Training dynamics analysis (Figure 3) provides insight into how units are added and removed over training.
- Literature positioning: clearly differentiates from black-box model selection, pruning-from-large, prior growth methods, and Bayesian approaches.
- Suggests adding more discussion of limitations, especially why performance is poor on the convex dataset and how to address it.
- Recommends concrete timing/computational-cost comparisons between AdaRad and standard optimizers to strengthen practical relevance.
- Suggests discussing extensions to other architectures (e.g., convolutional networks) beyond the tested feed-forward nets.
- Questions for authors: explain failure on convex dataset and possible fixes; how performance/theory changes if starting with 1 or 0 units; characterize dataset/task types that most benefit from the approach.
