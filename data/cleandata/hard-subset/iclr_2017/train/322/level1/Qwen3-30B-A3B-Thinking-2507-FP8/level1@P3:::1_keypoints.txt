1. Proposes nonparametric neural networks that auto-determine network size during a single training run using L2 fan-in regularization, CapNorm, and AdaRad.  
2. Theorem 1 claims nonparametric networks achieve a global training error minimum at finite dimensionality under specific regularizer conditions.  
3. Empirically, nonparametric networks match or exceed parametric networks on 2 of 3 benchmarks while using fewer parameters.  
4. Recommendation: reject due to inconsistent empirical performance (notably on the convex dataset) and weak theoretical generality.  
5. Theorem 1 is an interesting theoretical contribution to capacity control.  
6. Major empirical weakness: poor performance on the convex dataset undermines the claim of a general solution for network size selection.  
7. On the convex dataset, nonparametric networks performed worse and showed higher variability than parametric counterparts.  
8. The theoretical proof requires restrictive conditions (e.g., σ(0)=0) that may not hold for many practical activations.  
9. Authors note these limitations in the appendix but do not adequately discuss how common the assumptions are in practice.  
10. Key design choices lack justification, e.g., the heuristic αr = 150λ relationship is unexplained.  
11. Requested follow-up: analyze why nonparametric networks fail on the convex dataset (dataset property vs fundamental limitation).  
12. Requested follow-up: more clearly state the scope and practical applicability of the theoretical results.  
13. Requested experiments: detailed ablation studies on regularization strength, unit addition schedules, and CapNorm vs batch normalization.  
14. Requested analysis: address scalability and computational cost relative to standard training methods.  
15. Questions for authors: explain convex failure mode; handle activations with σ(0) ≠ 0; justify αr=150λ; disentangle benefits of smaller size vs selection mechanism; confirm statistical significance and comparatives for the perfect poker test result.
