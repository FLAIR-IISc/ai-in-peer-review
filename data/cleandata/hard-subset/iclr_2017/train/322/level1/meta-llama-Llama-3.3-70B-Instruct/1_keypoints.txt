- Proposes nonparametric neural networks and a non-probabilistic framework to optimize over all network sizes automatically.
- Introduces Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad) as a novel optimization algorithm for the framework.
- Proves theoretical soundness of the approach when network growth is limited by an ℓp penalty.
- Tackles automatic network size selection within a single training run, avoiding expensive global searches.
- Well-motivated contribution addressing manual tuning and costly grid searches in neural network design.
- Empirical results show competitive performance with parametric networks while using fewer parameters and less training time.
- Limitation: may perform poorly on datasets with many classes or complex input–output relationships.
- Limitation: performance is sensitive to hyperparameters, notably the regularization parameter λ.
- Reviewer requests clarity on how λ is chosen and the effect of varying λ on performance.
- Reviewer asks for insight into why the method struggles on high-class-count or complex datasets.
- Reviewer requests empirical comparison of AdaRad to optimizers like Adam or RMSprop regarding convergence and stability.
- Decision: Accept — reasons: significant, novel problem tackled; solid theoretical analysis; competitive empirical results.
- Suggested improvements: more detailed limitation analysis, exploration of future research directions, and more comparisons with state-of-the-art training and hyperparameter-tuning methods.
