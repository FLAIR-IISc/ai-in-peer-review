- Proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that add zero units and remove units via group-sparsity; proves existence of a finite-dimensional global minimizer under ℓp fan-in/out regularization.  
- Introduces CapNorm (a batch-norm variant) and AdaRad/AdaRad-M optimizer that separate radial/ angular gradient components with separate step sizes.  
- Empirical results on MNIST, rectangles, convex, and OpenML poker show mixed outcomes: sometimes fewer parameters and comparable/better accuracy, but failure on convex; includes growth/shrinkage dynamics analysis.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Key reason 1: Empirical validation is limited and confounded — gains may come from AdaRad/CapNorm rather than the nonparametric framework; ablations and stronger baselines are missing.  
- Key reason 2: Theoretical contribution (existence theorem) has limited practical impact — it guarantees a finite minimizer for the penalized objective but not model-selection or generalization; experiments are narrow.  
- No systematic ablations isolating (i) growth/removal with standard optimizers, (ii) AdaRad on parametric nets, and (iii) CapNorm’s standalone effect.  
- Missing comparisons to related pruning/growth and structured-sparsity methods (e.g., Alvarez & Salzmann, Dynamic Network Surgery, Net2Net, group-lasso approaches).  
- Selection protocol may bias results: choosing a "typical representative" from 10 runs and tuning parametric models differently risks unfair comparisons.  
- Unit addition schedule and hyperparameters (one per epoch, αr = 150/λ, etc.) are hand-tuned with no sensitivity analysis.  
- Experimental scope limited to small fully-connected nets; no convolutional architectures, depth selection, or modern vision/NLP benchmarks to demonstrate broader impact.  
- Theory lacks connection to validation/model selection, characterization of preferred width, or convergence guarantees for the training procedure.  
- Practicality concerns: online unit add/remove complicates implementation; no empirical wall-clock or memory overhead metrics; unit addition may slow early learning.  
- Suggestion: Run fixed-width MLP ablations with AdaRad vs standard optimizers to quantify AdaRad’s contribution.  
- Suggestion: Run nonparametric growth/removal with Adam/RMSProp (and compatible normalization) to isolate framework effects.  
- Suggestion: Compare against structured-sparsity and dynamic growth/pruning baselines with matched training budgets.  
- Suggestion: Report medians and variability across all seeds for each selected size and equalize hyperparameter tuning budgets.  
- Suggestion: Study sensitivity to unit addition rate, ν/νfreq, αr/αφ, and λ; show width evolution and robustness.  
- Suggestion: Include computational metrics (training time, memory, overhead of width changes) and analyze AdaRad scaling.  
- Suggestion: Extend experiments to convolutional layers or explicitly limit claims to MLPs if broader generalization isn’t supported.  
- Suggestion: Clarify CapNorm’s interaction with loss gradients and provide intuition/analysis for its impact on learning new units without negating regularization.  
- Q1: How much performance gain comes from AdaRad versus nonparametric growth — can you show fixed-width AdaRad and nonparametric Adam/RMSProp ablations?  
- Q2: How sensitive are results to λ, αr, αφ, and the unit addition schedule — do different schedules converge to similar widths/accuracies?  
- Q3: Can CapNorm be replaced by BN without affine parameters and still preserve the regularizer’s effect — what is the empirical gap?  
- Q4: How does the method perform with convolutional layers and on datasets where architecture selection matters (e.g., CIFAR-10/100)?  
- Q5: Do different random seeds converge to similar widths and is width correlated with validation error across runs?  
- Q6: Provide wall-clock and memory comparisons of AdaRad vs RMSProp/Adam, including cost of adding/removing units.  
- Q7: Beyond existence of a finite minimizer, can you characterize properties or bounds of the selected width (in terms of λ, data, margin) or provide generalization guarantees?
