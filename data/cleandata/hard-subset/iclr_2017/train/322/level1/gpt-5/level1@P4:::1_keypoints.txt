- Problem: automating network size selection within a single training run instead of expensive black-box model selection.  
- Proposal: "Nonparametric neural networks" that grow units during training and remove redundant ones with a group regularizer; Theorem 1 shows a finite-width global minimum exists for fan-in/fan-out regularization (λ>0, 1≤p<∞).  
- Training machinery: CapNorm (capped batch norm), AdaRad (per-unit radial/angular gradient decomposition with separate step sizes and momentum variant), and reliance on self-similar activations to reduce λ tuning.  
- Empirical claim: nonparametric fully-connected models often match or beat same-size parametric models and sometimes rival black-box baselines, but fail on the convex dataset.  

- Decision: Reject.  
- Main reasons for rejection: empirical evidence is insufficient/uneven and the novel training components (AdaRad, CapNorm) are not ablated or compared to strong alternatives.  

- Motivation/positioning: theorem addresses degeneracy concern and is a strength, but extensive prior work on growing/pruning/group sparsity is not empirically compared.  
- Theoretical soundness: Theorem 1 and Proposition 1 are well-proven under reasonable assumptions; AdaRad is well-motivated and computationally plausible.  
- CapNorm critique: reasonable rationale but ad-hoc and lacks theoretical analysis or comparison to alternatives (WeightNorm/LayerNorm).  

- Empirical validation weaknesses: datasets are small and fully-connected only; no convolutional experiments despite broader claims.  
- Poor performance on convex dataset: method tends to converge to overly small networks without mitigation or analysis.  
- Unfair/insufficient baselines: no comparisons to group-lasso + prune, Dynamic Network Surgery, Net2Net, or properly configured parametric baselines with decoupled weight decay/BN handling.  
- No ablation studies: lacks studies isolating unit addition/removal, AdaRad vs other optimizers, CapNorm vs alternatives, and hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Poker result concerns: very low error lacks comparison to modern baselines and needs leakage/reproducibility checks.  

- Clarity: paper is generally clear with detailed algorithms and appendix, though proofs are dense.  

- Suggested stronger baselines: parametric group-lasso + pruning, dynamic pruning/growth methods, Net2Net, and alternatives to CapNorm/AdaRad.  
- Suggested ablations: unit addition rate and schedule, αr vs αφ, CapNorm on/off, AdaRad vs SGD/RMSprop/Adam with fan-in normalization, p=1 vs p=2.  
- Suggested broader experiments: convolutional benchmarks (CIFAR), NLP tasks, reporting accuracy/params/training time and model-selection cost; analyze convex failure modes and λ annealing.  
- Fairness note: revisit claim that L2 is incompatible with BN; use decoupled weight decay or exclude BN params from L2 for fair comparisons.  
- Theory-claims alignment: clarify that Theorem 1 guarantees existence of finite global minima but does not prove the training procedure finds them; consider convergence/stationarity results for AdaRad.  

- Question 1: Include comparisons to group-lasso parametric pruning, Dynamic Network Surgery, Wei et al., and Net2Net under the same compute budget?  
- Question 2: Why not compare CapNorm to LayerNorm or WeightNorm; is CapNorm applied to all layers and are there stability issues?  
- Question 3: Can RMSprop/Adam be adapted to per-fan-in normalization with proximal shrinkage, and how does that compare to AdaRad?  
- Question 4: How sensitive are results to ν, νfreq, αr/αφ, and λ; do you anneal λ and does that help the convex dataset?  
- Question 5: Can you provide parametric baselines with standard weight decay configurations (exclude BN params or use decoupled weight decay) to ensure fair comparisons?  
- Question 6: Provide details for the poker dataset splits, preprocessing, leakage checks, reproducibility across seeds, and class-imbalance handling.  
- Question 7: What is wall-clock time of nonparametric runs versus random/bayesian search baselines for similar accuracy, and how does AdaRad’s overhead scale with model size?  
- Question 8: How would the method extend to convolutional layers — how to define group regularization and angular/radial updates for kernels?
