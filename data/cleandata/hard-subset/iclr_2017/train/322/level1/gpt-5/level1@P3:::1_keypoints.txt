- Proposal: nonparametric neural networks that grow/prune units in one run using zero-fan-out additions and group-regularization on fan-ins/outs; Theorem 1: empirical objective has a finite-width global minimizer under ℓp penalty.

- Training components: growth/prune schedule, CapNorm (capped batch norm), and AdaRad optimizer that decomposes radial/angular gradients and normalizes only angular component.

- Empirical claims: MLPs grow from tiny initial widths to data-driven sizes, often outperform parametric nets on MNIST/rectangles and scale to a large “poker” dataset; includes analysis of growth/shrink dynamics and unit lifetimes.

- Decision: Reject.

- Main weakness 1: Empirical evaluation is limited and not rigorously controlled—different normalization/optimizers and mid-training objective changes confound comparisons.

- Main weakness 2: Theoretical result guarantees existence of a finite-width minimizer for the regularized training objective but does not show the algorithm finds it or that it improves generalization.

- Fairness concern: Nonparametric runs use CapNorm + ℓ2 + AdaRad with post-hoc λ=0 fine-tuning, while baselines use BN + RMSprop/Adam without comparable fine-tuning—confounds source of gains.

- Selection bias: Choosing a “typical representative” nonparametric run using test error and median size then training parametric nets to match couples search to the method and leaks test information.

- Missing ablations: No systematic tests of CapNorm vs BN, p=2 vs p=1, fan-in vs fan-out, AdaRad vs standard optimizers, unit add/remove cadence; AdaRad-M introduced but not evaluated.

- Limited scope: Experiments restricted to fully-connected MLPs on small datasets (MNIST, rectangles, convex) plus one tabular set; no convolutional results despite broader claims.

- Failure mode: On the convex dataset the method underperforms and favors too-small widths, suggesting growth/prune heuristics or regularization can select underpowered models.

- Theoretical limitations: Theorem 1 is a technically careful existence proof under assumptions but does not address algorithmic convergence, generalization bounds, or why CapNorm is principled.

- Baseline recommendation: Run nonparametric with RMSprop/Adam and parametric with CapNorm; apply identical fine-tuning schedules and match compute budgets for apples-to-apples comparison.

- Ablation recommendations: Systematically ablate CapNorm, AdaRad, p=1 vs p=2, fan-in vs fan-out, unit addition rates, initialization scales, and the λ→0 switch.

- Stability reporting: Provide variance across seeds and sensitivity to αr, αφ, and λ; give automatic stopping criteria rather than manual annealing/rewinds.

- Experiment strengthening: Add CIFAR-10/100 MLPs and ideally convolutional extensions; compare to pruning-from-scratch and structured sparsity baselines; report wall-clock and memory overheads versus hyperparameter search.

- CapNorm analysis: Clarify gradients through CapNorm, its interaction with group shrinkage, and whether alternatives (e.g., frozen BN scales or decoupled weight decay) suffice.

- Reproducibility: Release code.

- Question (fairness): Can you rerun comparisons with identical optimizers, normalization, annealing/rewind schedules, and matched training budgets? How do conclusions change?

- Question (ablation): What happens if AdaRad is replaced by RMSprop/Adam within the nonparametric framework, and can AdaRad improve parametric nets?

- Question (CapNorm): Why cap at σ>1? Did you test BN with frozen γ/β or stop-gradient on BN scales as controls?

- Question (regularizer): How do results differ for p=1 (group lasso) and for fan-out penalties in terms of selected widths and generalization?

- Question (growth/pruning): How sensitive are results to unit-add frequency, new-unit initialization scale, and the αr=150/λ heuristic; could adaptive addition criteria improve stability?

- Question (convex dataset): Why does the method pick too-small models—due to CapNorm, λ schedule, or AdaRad angular normalization?

- Question (poker dataset): Can you rule out data leakage or duplicates and provide preprocessing, split methodology, and feature encoding details?

- Question (theory vs practice): Do you have convergence guarantees for the add/prune + AdaRad procedure or bounds connecting selected finite width to generalization?
