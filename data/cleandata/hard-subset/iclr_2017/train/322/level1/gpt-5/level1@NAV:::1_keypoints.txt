- Proposal: "Nonparametric neural networks" that adapt layer widths during a single run by adding zero-units and pruning whole units with an l2 fan-in/out group regularizer.
- Theoretical claim: Theorem 1 shows an lp (1 ≤ p < ∞) fan-in/out penalty admits a global minimum at finite dimensionality, preventing unbounded width growth.
- Practical components: CapNorm (batch-norm variant to avoid negating the regularizer) and AdaRad (optimizer decomposing gradients into angular and radial parts to preserve l2 shrinkage).
- Empirical claim: Method can discover compact architectures and sometimes match or exceed parametric baselines on MNIST, rectangles, convex, and a large Poker dataset.
- Decision: Reject.
- Primary reasons for rejection: mixed empirical support and insufficient ablations to isolate which components drive gains; limited evaluation scope (mostly MLPs, few datasets) leaves robustness/generalizability uncertain.
- Theory assessment: Theorem 1 and Proposition 1 are solidly presented, but theory only guarantees existence of finite-width minima—not convergence, generalization benefits, nor analysis of AdaRad.
- Empirics criticism: Inconsistent improvements (notably worse on convex); Poker result promising but lacks runtime/compute comparisons; baselines use uncapped BN with λ=0, disadvantaging weight decay and omitting modern decoupled weight decay (e.g., AdamW).
- Ablation gap: No experiments isolating (a) width adaptation with standard optimizers, (b) CapNorm alone, (c) AdaRad alone, or (d) unit-addition schedules.
- Methodology concerns: Use of early stopping/rewinding and aggressive hyperparameter heuristics (e.g., αr=150/λ) raises sensitivity and reproducibility issues.
- Suggested improvements: add the missing ablations, include modern baselines (AdamW/SGDW), try l0/l1 group penalties and other dynamic-width/pruning methods, extend to convolutional settings, and report wall-clock/FLOPs/memory and sensitivity analyses.
- Open questions for authors: sensitivity of final widths/accuracy to αr/αφ/ν/νfreq and the 50-epoch grace period; can gains be reproduced with standard optimizers + CapNorm or AdamW; why the convex dataset fails and whether pruning from a larger net helps; and the practical computational overhead of AdaRad and resizing relative to RMSprop/Adam.
