- Problem & goal: Automatically select network width during a single training run by adding/removing units to avoid expensive outer-loop searches.
- Proposal: "Nonparametric neural networks" that grow by adding zero-units and prune units whose fan-in/out shrink to zero under an lp (mainly l2 group) regularizer.
- Theory: Theorem 1 shows the empirical loss plus fan-in/out lp penalty attains a global minimum at a finite network size; Proposition 1 shows self-similar nonlinearities simplify per-layer regularization tuning.
- Optimizer & normalization: Introduces AdaRad (and AdaRad-M) that decomposes fan-in gradients into radial and angular parts, and CapNorm (capped batch normalization) to preserve regularizer effects.
- Training recipe: Start with tiny nets, incrementally add units, prune via l2 fan-in shrinkage, and use a staged λ schedule during training.
- Empirics summary: MLPs on MNIST, rectangles, convex dataset, and a large OpenML poker task; nonparametric nets outperform same-size parametric nets on some tasks and match/exceed baselines on poker.
- Decision: Reject.
- Main rejection reasons: Limited and mixed empirical validation (small MLPs, failure on convex dataset) and missing critical ablations/baselines to isolate sources of gains.
- Literature placement: Method connects to group-lasso pruning and grow-and-prune work; theoretical finite-width guarantee is a notable addition but empirical novelty may be mainly optimizer/normalization choices.
- Theoretical soundness: Existence theorem and proof strategy are technically sound and address infinite-width degeneracy; Proposition 1 is insightful for λ tuning.
- Experimental positives: Detailed pipeline, useful analyses (size trajectories, fan-in/out norms), and a large-scale poker experiment.
- Experimental concerns: Only fully-connected MLPs tested, convex dataset underperformance suggests brittleness, and poker 0% error result is suspicious and needs checking.
- Baseline fairness: Parametric baselines did not receive comparable tricks (CapNorm, AdaRad, group-l2, staged λ), making it unclear whether gains come from nonparametric formulation or engineering choices.
- Missing ablations: Need comparisons isolating AdaRad vs. standard optimizers, CapNorm vs. BN, effect of turning off λ late, and sensitivity to ν, νfreq, αr, αφ.
- Suggested baselines: Compare to structured sparsity/pruning-during-training and grow-and-prune methods from prior work.
- Broaden evaluation: Test on convolutional/sequence architectures (e.g., CIFAR), or diverse real-world tabular datasets, and show behavior for channel selection in conv layers.
- Poker dataset checks: Verify train/test splits for duplicates, report seed variance, and compare to strong tree/boosting baselines.
- Hyperparameter fairness & reproducibility: Nonparametric method uses many crucial hyperparameters and multi-phase training; report means/SDs across seeds and more quantitative summaries.
- Practicality: Report wall-clock, FLOPs, and memory overheads versus strong baselines and time-to-best-validation, since incremental growth may delay reaching useful capacity.
- Presentation & clarity: Provide clearer intuition for radial vs. angular updates, concrete pseudocode for shrinkage, and handle numerical edge cases.
- Questions for authors: How much gain remains if parametric nets use CapNorm and AdaRad? Ablate AdaRad by using RMSProp/Adam? Why does method underfit convex data? Poker seed/duplication checks? Any conv-layer results? What is AdaRad’s per-step overhead?
