- Proposes Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNN) that dynamically grow/shrink during a single training cycle using an L2 penalty; introduces AdaRad optimizer.  
- Reviewer recommends rejecting the paper in its current form due to weak experiments and unclear presentation.  
- Strong theoretical grounding: the framework’s soundness is proven.  
- Experimental results are only compelling on 2/3 datasets; performance is poor on the convex dataset where parametric networks outperform NNNs.  
- AdaRad’s description is complex and hard to follow, hindering reproducibility and evaluation.  
- Ablation studies are lacking; unclear how much improvement comes from nonparametric design vs AdaRad or CapNorm.  
- Comparison is limited (exhaustive random search only); lacks comparisons to established AutoML methods (e.g., NAS, Hyperopt).  
- Hyperparameter choices for comparisons are not clearly justified.  
- Suggestion: expand experimental evaluation to more diverse datasets and architectures.  
- Suggestion: clarify AdaRad with intuitive explanations, pseudocode, and visual/illustrative examples of radial-angular descent.  
- Suggestion: perform ablation studies to isolate contributions of nonparametric aspect, AdaRad, and CapNorm.  
- Suggestion: compare against established AutoML techniques for a fairer benchmark.  
- Suggestion: investigate and explain the poor convex dataset performance and discuss limitations.  
- Suggestion: provide detailed justification and protocol for hyperparameter selection.  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to λ and are there guidelines for choosing it?  
- Question: what is AdaRad’s computational overhead vs Adam/SGD and how does it scale with network size?  
- Question: what is the intuition behind separating angular and radial components and why is that beneficial?  
- Question: how does the choice of self-similar nonlinearity affect performance, and does the framework work with other nonlinearities?
