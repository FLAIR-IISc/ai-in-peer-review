- Proposal: nonparametric neural networks (NNPs) that dynamically adjust network size during training using a p-penalty, and a new optimizer AdaRad.  
- Decision: Reject — idea is interesting but empirical evidence is weak, theory feels disconnected from practice, and presentation is unclear.  
- Limited empirical validation: experiments (MNIST, rectangles, convex) show inconsistent gains; convex dataset performance is worse than parametric baselines.  
- Poker results promising but under-explored and not sufficiently detailed.  
- Insufficient comparison to modern NAS methods (RL-based or differentiable); grid search baseline is inadequate.  
- Clarity issues: many concepts (CapNorm, AdaRad, f-equivalence) are under-explained and motivation for design choices is unclear.  
- Choice of p=2 is justified mainly by hardware considerations, lacking deeper theoretical or empirical rationale.  
- Theorem guaranteeing finite dimensionality is not clearly connected to practical effects of the p-penalty on performance.  
- AdaRad description is dense; claimed benefits over RMSprop/Adam are not convincingly supported by experiments.  
- Lack of ablation studies to isolate the contributions of CapNorm, AdaRad components (e.g., angular update), and the p-penalty.  
- Suggested experiment improvements: broaden comparisons to state-of-the-art NAS and expand poker dataset analysis.  
- Suggested clarity improvements: provide intuitive motivation for p choice, link theory to practice, and add diagrams/illustrations.  
- Suggested ablations: quantify impact of CapNorm vs batchnorm, AdaRad’s angular term, and the p-penalty.  
- Investigate and explain poor convex dataset results to determine framework limitation vs experimental setup issue.  
- Clarify relationship to existing dynamic network approaches (Wei et al., 2016; Chen et al., 2016) and specify novel problems addressed.  
- Questions for authors: justify p=2 beyond hardware — any theoretical preference?  
- Questions: explain how AdaRad avoids normalization issues with fan-in/out regularizers in detail.  
- Questions: what is AdaRad’s computational overhead vs SGD/Adam and how does it scale with network size?  
- Questions: why does NNP perform poorly on the convex dataset — dataset characteristics or method flaw?  
- Questions: how does NNP compare to modern NAS in computational cost, performance, and implementation ease?
