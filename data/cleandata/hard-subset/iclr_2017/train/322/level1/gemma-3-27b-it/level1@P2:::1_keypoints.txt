- Contribution: Introduces nonparametric neural networks that grow/shrink during training, with a soundness proof and a new optimizer (AdaRad).  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main concern: Experimental results are limited and inconsistent across datasets.  
- Poor robustness: Significantly worse performance on the convex dataset undermines generalizability.  
- Marginal gains: Improvements on MNIST and rectangles are small and may not justify method complexity.  
- Missing baselines: No comparison to contemporary Neural Architecture Search (NAS) methods, leaving relative value unclear.  
- Practicality issues: AdaRad introduces multiple hyperparameters (αr, αφ, βarith, βquad) and extra tuning burden.  
- Unproven scaling claim: Assertion that AdaRad scales better than RMSprop lacks runtime or empirical evidence.  
- Ablation studies needed: Must evaluate contributions of CapNorm, AdaRad, and the regularization scheme.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Required analysis of robustness to αr, αφ, λ, etc.  
- Scalability analysis: Need detailed runtime and training-cost comparisons versus parametric networks and NAS.  
- Theory: Expand theoretical justification and clarify limitations and conditions for the soundness proof.  
- Visualization: Provide training-time visualizations showing how networks grow and shrink.  
- Question: Why does the method fail on the convex dataset and how can it be fixed?  
- Question: How does training cost compare to parametric networks of similar performance and to NAS?
