- Paper introduces Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNPs) that add/remove units during training using an l2 penalty and a new optimizer AdaRad; claims promising results.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Empirical results lack convincing advantage over well-tuned parametric networks; performance is mixed across datasets.  
- When compared to hyperparameter-searched parametric networks, NNPs only match or slightly outperform (only on rectangles); they perform significantly worse on the convex dataset.  
- Poker dataset results look promising but are under-discussed and hard to interpret from the brief presentation.  
- AdaRad is complex with many hyperparameters (αr, αφ, β); the separation of radial and angular updates is poorly justified and explanation feels hand-wavy.  
- Explanation of how AdaRad prevents collapse to zero while allowing growth is unclear; parameter tuning and sensitivity details are missing.  
- Theoretical foundation (Theorem 1) requires strong conditions (restriction on p and nonlinearity requirements); practical relevance of these restrictions is not explored.  
- Proof is relegated to the appendix, making verification and accessibility difficult.  
- Paper lacks thorough comparison to existing dynamic architecture methods (e.g., progressive nets, NAS) and clear positioning of NNP’s advantages.  
- Missing ablation studies to evaluate the importance of AdaRad components (radial vs. angular updates, running averages).  
- No sensitivity analysis of key hyperparameters (λ, αr, αφ, β) to assess robustness.  
- Computational cost analysis is insufficient; needs quantitative comparison to standard network training.  
- Would benefit from visualization of network growth during training (e.g., units per layer over time).  
- Question: Provide scenarios and quantitative evidence where NNPs outperform advanced black-box methods (Bayesian optimization, RL).  
- Question: Detail how AdaRad hyperparameters were tuned and present sensitivity analyses.  
- Question: How sensitive are results to violations of Theorem 1’s nonlinearity conditions; have other activations been tested?  
- Question: Quantify the claim that NNPs are most beneficial with no prior task information and show how performance changes with increasing prior knowledge.  
- Question: Provide computational resources and training time for NNP vs. similarly performing parametric network on the poker dataset.
