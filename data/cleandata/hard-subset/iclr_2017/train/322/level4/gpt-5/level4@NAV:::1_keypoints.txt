- Proposes a nonparametric neural architecture that grows by randomly inserting zero-initialized units and uses a sparsity-inducing regularizer to prune irrelevant weights.  
- Method resembles random exploration over a discrete architecture space with sparse regularization removing superfluous units.  
- Paper tackles a significant problem and presents promising empirical results.  
- Major concern: quantify incremental computational complexity—decomposing fan-in into parallel/orthogonal components and converting to radial–angular coordinates may add substantial overhead; report extra operations vs. fixed-size networks and include wall-clock runtimes.  
- Secondary concern: on the convex dataset the nonparametric method yields smaller networks and underperforms the parametric baseline; request an explanation for this behavior.
