- Proposes nonparametric neural networks that grow/shrink hidden units during a single training run via zero-unit addition and group-norm removal.
- Theoretical result: with a fan-in or fan-out p-norm (1 ≤ p < ∞, λ > 0) the training objective attains its minimum at a finite network size.
- Practical methods: introduces AdaRad (separates radial/angular fan-in gradients) and CapNorm (modified normalization to preserve regularizer effects).
- Empirical claim: can match or improve accuracy with fewer units than fixed-size baselines on some tasks, implying potential memory/compute benefits.

- Decision: Reject.
- Primary reasons: insufficient empirical evidence vs strong pruning/compression and unclear scalability/hyperparameter burden.

- Hyperparameter burden remains high (λ, unit addition rate, radial/angular step sizes) with limited evidence these are easier to tune than fixed architectures.
- Missing comparisons to modern pruning/compression/distillation pipelines, making competitiveness unclear.
- Training efficiency and scalability unclear: no wall-clock/compute comparisons or large-scale experiments (e.g., CIFAR/ImageNet).
- Mixed empirical outcomes: wins on some datasets but worse on others, and dependence on bespoke optimizer and normalization leaves component contributions unclear.
- Experimental rigor concerns: baselines may not be robustly tuned and ablations (CapNorm/AdaRad) are limited.

- Suggest adding comparisons to structured pruning, sparsity regularization, and distillation.
- Recommend reporting end-to-end training cost: wall-clock time, updates, compute/energy, and test-time latency/memory.
- Ask for sensitivity analyses and ablations over λ, addition rate, αr/αφ, AdaRad/CapNorm, and p=1 vs p=2.
- Request evaluation on larger, realistic settings (convolutional nets on CIFAR/ImageNet, deeper networks).
- Clarify selection protocol: final size choice, whether retraining discovered architectures helps, and interaction with early stopping.
- Suggest releasing code and reproducible scripts.
- Provide guidance on hyperparameter defaults and robustness.

- How were λ, addition rate/frequency, and αr/αφ chosen across datasets and how sensitive are results?
- What are wall-clock times and total optimization steps vs best-tuned parametric baselines; how does AdaRad’s overhead scale?
- How does the method compare to recent pruning/distillation starting from tuned parametric networks in size-accuracy trade-offs?
- Why does performance degrade on the convex dataset; is there bias toward underfitting and would retraining the found architecture help?
- How much improvement is due to AdaRad and CapNorm versus the growth/shrinkage mechanism; do standard optimizers with the regularizer perform similarly?
- How crucial are zero fan-out/random fan-in initialization and the growth policy; have alternative or data-driven addition schedules been tried?
- How can the method be extended to convolutional channels/filters and does the finite-minimum theorem extend to convnets?
- What practical stopping criteria for growth do the authors recommend beyond validation plateaus?

- Overall: conceptually appealing with solid theory, but needs stronger empirical validation, clearer practical benefits, and comparisons to competitive compression/pruning baselines.
