This paper introduces a nonparametric neural architecture that adapts its size during training. The central mechanism is to randomly insert zero-initialized units and employ a sparsity-inducing regularizer to drive irrelevant weights to zero. The approach resembles a random exploration over a discrete architecture space, with sparse regularization pruning superfluous units. The problem is significant, and the results are promising. My main comments are as follows:
1) What is the incremental computational complexity of the method? Decomposing each unit’s fan-in weights into parallel and orthogonal components, and converting to radial–angular coordinates, could introduce substantial overhead. Please quantify the extra operations relative to a fixed-size parametric network. It would also be helpful to include wall-clock runtime experiments.
2) It is reported that on the convex dataset the nonparametric method yields smaller networks and consequently underperforms the parametric baseline. Do you have an explanation for this behavior?
