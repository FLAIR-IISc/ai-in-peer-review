- Paper tackles an interesting direction: networks that add/remove units so capacity adapts to data rather than a user’s fixed choice.  
- Theorem is appealing: with fan-in or fan-out regularization, the error optimum uses a finite number of parameters (prevents unbounded growth), reminiscent of Lasso/Elastic Net.  
- Authors should provide more plain-English intuition and devote more space to explaining and illustrating the theorem.  
- Recommend reallocating space from prior work to deeper analysis and experiments supporting the central theoretical result.  
- Experiments need a direct comparison to fixed-size networks that match the adaptive model’s final number of units to evaluate efficiency and memory/performance tradeoffs.  
- Figure 2 is unclear; results are mixed (adaptive sometimes outperforms, sometimes underperforms), though method is useful for structure discovery.  
- Need explanation why the nonparametric procedure sometimes outperforms a parametric model trained from scratch even when the final architecture is known.  
- Question whether the regularization parameter λ can be predicted or guided by data—λ likely controls final structure and needs heuristics or discussion.  
- Ask about sensitivity to initialization: do different random seeds yield substantially different architectures, sizes, or performances?  
- Ask what happens when combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers—does the finite-parameter guarantee still hold?  
- Clarify the claim that adding zero units changes the regularizer; for example, L2 norm would be unchanged if added parameters are exactly zero.  
- Clarify definition of “zero units”: both fan-in and fan-out should be zero to allow safe removal without changing the function.  
- Reviewer updated rating to 7 and expects authors to address these comments.
