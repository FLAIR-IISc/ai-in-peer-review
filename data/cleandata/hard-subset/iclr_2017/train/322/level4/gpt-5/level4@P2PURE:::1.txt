This paper introduces a nonparametric neural network that learns its own size during training. The core mechanism is to stochastically add zero-initialized units while using a sparsity-inducing regularizer to drive irrelevant weights to zero. Conceptually, the approach appears to be a random search over a discrete model space, with sparsity serving to prune superfluous units. The problem is important, and the empirical results are interesting. My main comments are:

- Computational complexity: What is the additional computational cost of the proposed algorithm? Decomposing each unit’s fan-in weights into parallel and orthogonal components and converting them to radial–angular coordinates may introduce significant overhead. Please quantify the extra operations relative to a standard parametric neural network, and include running-time experiments.

- Behavior on the convex dataset: It is observed that the nonparametric networks produce small architectures on the convex dataset, leading to worse performance than parametric networks. Do the authors have any insight into why this occurs?
