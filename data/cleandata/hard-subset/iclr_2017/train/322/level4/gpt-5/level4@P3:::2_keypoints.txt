- Proposes nonparametric neural networks that adapt width during a single training run via unit growth and group-regularized pruning.  
- Theorem 1: with fan-in or fan-out group Lp penalty (1 ≤ p < ∞), the training objective attains a global minimum at finite network size, making width optimization well-posed.  
- Introduces CapNorm, a batch-normalization variant that caps variance normalization to preserve weight-shrinkage effects.  
- Introduces AdaRad, an optimizer separating radial and angular gradient components to normalize direction only, helping new units learn fairly while keeping regularization effect.  
- Empirical results: nonparametric networks match or exceed parametric baselines on MNIST, rectangles, and a large OpenML poker dataset, and discover compact architectures; mixed performance on the convex dataset.  
- Decision: Accept — addresses an important problem with principled objective and theoretical guarantee of finite optimum.  
- Training machinery (CapNorm, AdaRad) is technically interesting, practically motivated, and empirically competitive with standard baselines of same final size.  
- Frames width selection as part of a single objective, avoiding expensive black-box hyperparameter searches.  
- Positions work relative to pruning, hyperparameter optimization, and prior growth methods, and explains why standard BN and adaptive optimizers are ill-suited with group shrinkage.  
- Theorem 1’s conditions are reasonable (activations with σ(0)=0) and proof shows objective is coercive in width, analogous to group Lasso.  
- Empirical evidence supports core claims: convergence to finite sizes, potential to outperform same-size parametric nets, and scalability to large datasets.  
- Suggestion: add a plain-English proof sketch for Theorem 1 and intuition connecting group penalty to finite nonzero units.  
- Suggestion: investigate why nonparametric models sometimes outperform parametric ones (curriculum effect, trajectory-dependent regularization, AdaRad benefits) and include retrain-from-scratch ablations.  
- Suggestion: clarify Figure 2 (axes, units per epoch, error bars, distinction between retrain vs best-search parametric baselines).  
- Suggestion: show sensitivity of final width and accuracy to λ, provide heuristics or annealing schedules, and emphasize Proposition 1 to reduce tuning.  
- Suggestion: report variability of discovered architectures across seeds (histograms of widths/accuracy) to assess initialization dependence.  
- Suggestion: comment on existence/coercivity when combining fan-in and fan-out penalties.  
- Suggestion: include CapNorm ablations showing standard BN negates regularizer and CapNorm restores effect while preserving stability.  
- Suggestion: compare AdaRad to Adam/RMSprop with decoupled weight decay and per-group normalization baselines, and report wall-clock/iteration overhead.  
- Questions for authors: plain-English takeaway of Theorem 1 and how nonzero-unit bound scales with λ, p, and data properties.  
- Questions for authors: guidance on λ selection or schedules that control final width and accuracy.  
- Questions for authors: results of retraining discovered architecture from scratch with same optimizer/CapNorm — how close is performance?  
- Questions for authors: how much do final architectures vary across seeds for fixed λ, and does variability correlate with test performance?  
- Questions for authors: diagnose underperformance on the convex dataset (growth policy, λ regime, optimizer interaction).  
- Questions for authors: empirical differences between using fan-in vs fan-out penalties in learned structures and stability.  
- Clarification request: confirm rationale about zero fan-in or fan-out making a unit effectively removable (σ(0)=0) and intended design regarding units with zero fan-out/in.
