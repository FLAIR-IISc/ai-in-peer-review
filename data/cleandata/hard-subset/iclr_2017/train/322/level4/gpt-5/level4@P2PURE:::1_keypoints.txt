- Introduces a nonparametric neural network that learns its own size by stochastically adding zero-initialized units and using a sparsity-inducing regularizer to prune irrelevant weights.

- Conceptually the method resembles a random search over discrete model space with sparsity used to remove superfluous units.

- The problem is important and the empirical results presented are interesting.

- Computational complexity concern: decomposition of fan-in into parallel/orthogonal components and radial–angular conversion may add significant overhead — quantify extra operations vs a standard network and provide running-time experiments.

- Empirical behavior concern: on the convex dataset the method yields small architectures with worse performance than parametric networks — authors should explain why this happens.
