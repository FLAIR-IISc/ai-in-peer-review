- Introduces nonparametric neural nets that adapt layer widths during a single training run by adding zero units and removing redundant units via an ℓ2 fan-in/fan-out group penalty.
- Provides a theoretical result (Theorem 1) showing the training objective with ℓp fan-in/out regularization attains a global minimum at finite dimensionality.
- Proposes CapNorm, a BatchNorm variant that caps variance scaling at 1 and removes affine parameters to avoid canceling weight-penalty effects.
- Introduces AdaRad, an optimizer decomposing fan-in gradients into radial and angular components, normalizing only the angular part to help new units learn while preserving ℓ2 shrinkage.
- Empirical results: finds compact architectures and matches/outperforms same-size parametric baselines on MNIST, rectangles-images, and a large poker dataset, but underperforms on the convex dataset.
- Decision: Weak Accept.
- Reason: Tackles important problem of single-run width selection with a principled combination of theory, optimization, and normalization.
- Reason: Empirical results are promising on multiple datasets, indicating practical value despite gaps in runtime analysis and understanding of failure modes.
- Targets model selection over network width without expensive retraining/search, aligning with resource-efficient architecture discovery.
- Well-motivated relative to pruning and hyperparameter search; grows networks from scratch and connects to prior dynamic growth ideas with stronger theory.
- Theorem 1 and the proposition on self-similar nonlinearities are technically sound under stated assumptions.
- AdaRad’s radial/angular decomposition is plausible for stabilizing new-unit learning, though empirical wall-clock comparisons versus RMSprop/Adam/SGD are missing.
- Empirical methodology for selecting representative parametric baselines is carefully described and appreciated.
- Request to quantify AdaRad’s computational overhead with wall-clock, FLOPs, or throughput comparisons against standard optimizers.
- Suggests ablations isolating AdaRad vs. standard optimizers and CapNorm vs. BatchNorm, ideally on the same final architectures.
- Recommends retraining discovered final architectures as fixed parametric nets to test whether benefits come from architecture or training dynamics.
- Asks for sensitivity analysis to unit addition rate/frequency (ν, νfreq) and learning rates αr/αφ, which may affect results and the convex failure case.
- Requests analysis of the convex dataset failure mode (e.g., interactions between CapNorm and ℓ2 shrinkage, weak gradients, feature scaling) and experiments varying λ, αr, schedules, and fan-in vs fan-out.
- Advises clarifying how random unit sampling differs from random search over widths, emphasizing gradient guidance and structured shrinkage.
- Questions: asymptotic/practical overhead of AdaRad vs SGD/RMSprop/Adam and memory usage.
- Questions: implementation details and numerical stability of per-fan-in orthogonal/parallel decomposition on GPU/CPU.
- Questions: how much CapNorm alone contributes versus standard BatchNorm and any cases where capping harms optimization.
- Questions: whether tuning αr, ν/νfreq, using fan-out regularization, or different p/initial scales alleviates undersizing on convex.
- Questions: effects of replacing ReLU with non-self-similar nonlinearities (tanh/ELU/LeakyReLU) on theory and practice.
- Questions: stability of discovered sizes across seeds and whether retraining fixed discovered architectures recovers similar accuracy.
- Questions: comparison in final accuracy and total training time to a strong train-then-prune pipeline.
