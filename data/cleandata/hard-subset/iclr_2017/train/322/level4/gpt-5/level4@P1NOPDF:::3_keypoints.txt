- Proposes a simple, elegant mechanism to add zero-weight units and remove units via group-sparsity, with a theoretical result that the objective is minimized by a network with finitely many units; initial experiments suggest over/under-fitting is not a major issue.
- Does not truly reduce hyperparameter tuning: several hyperparameters (e.g., unit-add frequency, shrink strength) still implicitly determine final size and may be as hard or harder to tune.
- Claims comparable performance with fewer units, which could lower latency/power/memory, but lacks comparisons to pruning/compression baselines to show competitiveness.
- Single hyperparameters govern both model size and training dynamics, potentially causing significantly slower training and no analysis of scaling to large architectures raises practicality concerns.
- Experiments are encouraging but not comprehensive; larger-scale results are needed to demonstrate practicality and broad usefulness.
- Overall positive assessment: interesting, clearly written, and potentially valuable with a compelling lifelong-learning vision, but empirical support remains speculative.
