- Summary: Proposes a nonparametric neural network that stochastically adds zero-initialized candidate units and prunes with a sparsity regularizer to grow/shrink capacity during training; empirical results are promising.

- Strength: Tackles automatic network-size selection, reducing manual tuning.

- Strength: Uses a simple random-addition plus sparsity mechanism implementable within standard training loops.

- Strength: Empirically can discover compact, parameter-efficient architectures.

- Strength: Radial–angular decomposition provides a structured way to regularize fan-in weights.

- Weakness 1 (compute): Need quantification of per-update cost, memory footprint, scaling with width/depth and candidate units, backprop overhead, and wall-clock time-to-target vs. fixed and well-tuned baselines.

- Weakness 2 (convex/underfitting): Method collapses to very small models on convex datasets; analyze whether regularizer dominates, unit-addition is too conservative, sensitivity to sparsity strength/schedule, addition frequency, and remedies (minimum width, warm-up); show width-vs-time diagnostics.

- Weakness 3 (stability): Report variance across seeds for final size, accuracy, and training time to assess robustness of discovered architectures.

- Weakness 4 (comparisons/ablations): Compare to pruning, dynamic growth/prune, and architecture search; ablate regularizer choice, coordinate parameterization, and addition strategy; compare to strong prune-from-overparameterized baseline at equal compute.

- Weakness 5 (theory/practicals): Formalize the objective under coordinate transform, state when weights go to zero, discuss convergence/oscillation guarantees, and provide full implementation details (addition frequency, counts, init, pruning thresholds, BN interaction).

- Clarity/presentation: Clarify parallel/orthogonal decomposition and radial–angular transform with pseudocode, cost annotations, a worked example, and plots/tables of layer-width evolution.

- Overall: Interesting and timely contribution with encouraging results, but needs thorough computational analysis, convex-data mitigation, stability/ablation studies, and clearer theoretical/implementation details for practical adoption.
