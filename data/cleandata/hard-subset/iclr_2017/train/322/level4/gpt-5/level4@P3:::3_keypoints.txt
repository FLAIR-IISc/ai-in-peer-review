- Paper proposes nonparametric neural networks that add/remove hidden units during a single training run using zero-unit insertion and group-sparsity-based removal; Theorem 1 shows a finite-size global minimum with lp fan-in/out regularization.
- Introduces AdaRad optimizer that separates radial and angular gradient components to protect newly added units, and CapNorm, a batch-norm variant capping rescaling at std=1 to preserve regularization.
- Experiments on MNIST, rectangles-images, convex, and an OpenML poker dataset show networks can grow to data-driven sizes and sometimes match or exceed parametric networks of similar size.
- Recommendation: Reject.
- Main reasons for rejection: empirical evaluation not convincing for broad applicability and method introduces many hyperparameters whose tuning burden and scalability are not well assessed.
- Positive: well-motivated problem, sound theoretical result, and clean use of f-equivalence via zero-unit insertion connecting to growing-network methods.
- Novelty: l2 group-sparsity on fan-in/out and AdaRad’s radial/angular decomposition are interesting and tailored to the nonparametric setting.
- Empirical shortcomings: mixed results (underperformance on convex), lack of comparisons to modern pruning/structured-sparsity baselines, insufficient large-scale benchmarks and variability analysis.
- Practicality concerns: multiple interacting hyperparameters (λ, αr, αφ, unit addition rate/frequency) may be as costly to tune as outer-loop width search; growth-from-scratch can slow reaching good accuracy and wall-clock comparisons are missing.
- Scalability concerns: experiments limited to fully connected nets and modest datasets (except poker); no demonstration on convolutional architectures or tasks where inference cost matters.
- Suggested baselines/ablations: compare to state-of-the-art pruning/structured sparsity; ablate AdaRad vs RMSprop/Adam, CapNorm vs BN/no-norm, and l1 vs l2 group penalties; report FLOPs, memory, and wall-clock times.
- Hyperparameter guidance requested: systematic sensitivity studies for λ, αr, αφ, ν, νfreq and recommended defaults/tuning heuristics; quantify ReLU vs tanh/ELU under CapNorm.
- Efficiency requests: report end-to-end wall-clock to fixed validation targets, profile AdaRad compute/memory on CPU/GPU, and show fraction of time in growth phase.
- Theory clarifications: state precise assumptions on σ for Theorem 1, discuss CapNorm’s interaction with the regularizer, and whether results extend to learned depth or convolutions.
- Scope expansion: validate on at least one convolutional benchmark (e.g., CIFAR-10/100) and explore alternative unit-addition schedules or importance-based insertion.
- Questions for authors: heuristics for αr/αφ, sensitivity to ν/νfreq, CapNorm’s optimization/representational tradeoffs, wall-clock comparisons for AdaRad vs RMSprop/Adam, reasons for convex dataset failure, comparisons to pruning in accuracy/training time, stabilization time for widths on large datasets, and code release plans.
