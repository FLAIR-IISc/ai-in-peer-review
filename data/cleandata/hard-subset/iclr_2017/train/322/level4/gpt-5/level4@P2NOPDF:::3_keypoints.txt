- Proposes a simple, well-motivated method: occasionally add zero-weight units, prune units whose weights shrink to zero, and use group-sparsity regularization; theory shows loss is minimized by a network with finitely many units and experiments suggest over/underfitting is not a major issue.

- Method does not eliminate hyperparameter tuning: choices like unit-add frequency and weight-decay speed implicitly control model size and may be as hard or harder to tune than conventional setups.

- Claims competitive performance with fewer units, which could reduce latency, power, and memory, but lacks comparison to established post-training compression/pruning techniques.

- Same hyperparameters affect both model size and training time, so training may be substantially slower than fixed-size networks; the method’s intrinsic slowness and scaling behavior are not addressed.

- Experiments are informative and encouraging but limited; results on much larger tasks are needed to demonstrate practicality and broad utility.

- Overall, an interesting, clearly written contribution with a compelling lifelong-learning vision, but empirical evidence is preliminary and somewhat speculative.
