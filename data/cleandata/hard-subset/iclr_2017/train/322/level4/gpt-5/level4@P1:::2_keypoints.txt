- Proposal: Introduces “nonparametric neural networks” that adapt network width during a single training run by adding and pruning units.
- Theory: Theorem 1 shows under fan-in or fan-out group-ℓp regularization (λ>0, 1≤p<∞) the training objective attains a global minimum at a finite network size.
- Practical methods: Introduces CapNorm (capped batch normalization) compatible with the regularizer.
- Practical methods: Introduces AdaRad, an optimizer separating radial and angular fan-in updates so new units can learn despite shrinkage.
- Experiments: Evaluated on MNIST, rectangles, convex, and a large OpenML poker dataset.
- Empirical findings: Method can discover network sizes on the fly and sometimes matches or outperforms parametric baselines with fewer parameters.
- Decision: Accept (rating 7).
- Reason for accept 1: Addresses automated model size selection with a clear formulation, sound theoretical guarantee, and concrete algorithmic innovations.
- Reason for accept 2: Promising empirical results that demonstrate the approach’s viability and potential to stimulate further work.
- Supporting argument: Theorem 1 formalizes why group-ℓp fan-in/fan-out regularization prevents unbounded network growth, akin to group sparsity.
- Supporting argument: AdaRad and CapNorm thoughtfully address the tension between weak initial gradients for new units and strong regularization.
- Supporting argument: Empirical evaluation includes multiple datasets and a large-scale case; results are transparent about strengths and weaknesses.
- Literature positioning: Paper situates itself relative to pruning/growth, black-box search, and Bayesian approaches.
