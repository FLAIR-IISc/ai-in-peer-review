- Proposes nonparametric neural networks that grow and shrink during a single training run using group penalties to add/remove whole units.  
- Uses fan-in or fan-out p-norm penalties (λ>0, 1≤p<∞) and proves (Theorem 1) the training objective attains a global minimum at a finite network size.  
- Introduces CapNorm (capped batch normalization) to be compatible with group penalties.  
- Introduces AdaRad, an optimizer that separates radial (length) and angular (direction) updates so new units can learn before being shrunk.  
- Uses self-similar activations (e.g., ReLU) to avoid layer-wise regularization tuning.  
- Experiments on MNIST, rectangles-images, convex, and OpenML poker show the method can find compact models and often match or outperform fixed-size baselines.  
- Recommendation: Accept — novel, well-motivated framework with a clear theoretical guarantee and practical algorithmic contributions.  
- Theorem 1 addresses a core concern about unbounded growth and conceptually anchors the approach.  
- Training scheme (self-similarity, CapNorm, AdaRad) is well-designed to prevent penalty neutralization and allow new-unit learning.  
- Empirical results demonstrate discovery of useful sizes and occasional superiority to same-size parametric networks, with scalable performance on poker.  
- Caveats: mixed empirical results (notably on convex) and some presentation/figure clarity issues.  
- Suggestion: Clarify and motivate Theorem 1 with intuition and a short sketch in the main text.  
- Suggestion: Add apples-to-apples comparisons by retraining discovered architectures from scratch with matched budgets and optimizers.  
- Suggestion: Analyze sensitivity to λ and initialization; report distributions of final sizes and errors across seeds and consider adaptive λ schemes.  
- Suggestion: Explore combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers and whether Theorem 1 extends to their sum.  
- Suggestion: Clarify the definition and effect of “zero units” on the regularizer and when Ω changes upon adding/removing zero units.  
- Suggestion: Improve Figure 2 readability with separate panels, legends, consistent coding, and error bars.  
- Suggestion: Provide ablations comparing AdaRad vs RMSprop/Adam, CapNorm vs BN/no normalization, and different unit-addition schedules/initializations.  
- Suggestion: Report resource trade-offs (parameter counts, FLOPs, memory) and wall-clock comparisons to fixed training.  
- Question: Why can the nonparametric training path outperform training-from-scratch on the final discovered architecture?  
- Question: Restate Theorem 1 in plain English and state whether it extends to combined fan-in + fan-out penalties and any activation/data assumptions.  
- Question: What predictable relationships exist between λ and final widths; any heuristics or adaptive tuning for λ?  
- Question: How sensitive are final architectures and errors across seeds; are learned features functionally similar across runs?  
- Question: Provide CapNorm ablations vs BN/no normalization and sensitivity to the σ≥1 cap and removal of affine re-scaling.  
- Question: Precisely define when adding a zero fan-in/fan-out unit changes the regularizer Ω.  
- Question: Clarify/prove that removing a unit with zero fan-in or zero fan-out preserves the network function when corresponding rows/columns are removed.  
- Question: What is the compute/memory overhead and wall-clock cost of AdaRad and dynamic resizing compared to standard fixed-architecture training?
