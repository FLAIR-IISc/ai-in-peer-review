- Proposes a simple, well-motivated method to add/remove units during training using zeroed input/output weights and a group-sparsity regularizer; theory shows a finite-unit minimizer exists and initial experiments show manageable over-/under-fitting.  
- Claim of reduced hyperparameter tuning is overstated: other hyperparameters (unit-add frequency, weight decay rate) implicitly control unit count and may be as hard to tune.  
- Authors do not compare against post-training model-compression methods (e.g., pruning), so competitiveness for producing smaller models is unclear.  
- Hyperparameters that control unit count also affect training time, potentially making training slower than fixed-architecture approaches and increasing practical cost.  
- Paper lacks discussion and evidence on scaling to much larger networks, raising concerns about practicality for large-scale or time-constrained settings.  
- Experiments are encouraging but limited; evaluations on substantially larger problems are needed to establish broader utility.  
- Overall: clearly written and interesting with a compelling lifelong-learning vision, but current results are promising yet somewhat speculative.
