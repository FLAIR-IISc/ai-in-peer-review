- Positive assessment: agrees with Reviewer 2 that automatic addition/removal of units is an interesting direction allowing model size to adapt to data/task.  
- Theoretical claim: under fan-in or fan-out regularization, the error attains its optimum with a finite number of parameters, preventing unbounded growth.  
- Connection to prior work: result is reminiscent of sparsity-inducing methods like Lasso/Elastic Net; more intuition is needed.  
- Request to reallocate space: shorten prior-work discussion to expand intuition and analysis of the main theorem.  
- Experiments: ask for a direct comparison to a network with the same final number of neurons but fixed from the start to test efficiency.  
- Experimental clarity: Figure 2 is unclear and results are mixed; need explanation why nonparametric sometimes outperforms the fixed architecture.  
- Practical questions about the method: does it truly eliminate unneeded units, replace them usefully, and what is the performance vs. memory tradeoff?  
- Theorem 1 needs clearer plain-English insight and possibly supporting experiments to demonstrate implications.  
- Ask whether regularization parameter λ can be predicted from data, and whether λ mainly determines final network structure.  
- Sensitivity question: how dependent is the final architecture on random initialization and how variable are resulting architectures?  
- Combined regularizers: what happens if fan-in and fan-out regularizers are used together; does the theoretical guarantee still hold?  
- Clarification 1: why do adding zero-valued units change the regularizer (e.g., L2 norm) — request justification.  
- Clarification 2: definition of zero units should require both fan-in and fan-out to be zero; otherwise removing the unit changes the function.
