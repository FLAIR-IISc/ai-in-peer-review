- Proposes nonparametric neural networks that grow and prune units during a single training run using group regularization on fan-in/fan-out.
- Proves that under an ℓp (1 ≤ p < ∞) fan-in or fan-out penalty with λ > 0, the training objective attains a global minimum at a finite network size.
- Introduces CapNorm (capped batch normalization) to be compatible with the regularizer.
- Introduces AdaRad, an optimizer that decomposes gradients into radial and angular components to balance shrinkage and learning for new units.
- Empirical results across datasets and a large OpenML task show the method can find compact models and sometimes match or outperform fixed-size networks.
- Decision: recommend accept — idea is compelling and practically relevant, supported by theory and concrete algorithmic recipe.
- Addresses the high cost of black-box architecture search and uses f-equivalence and self-similar nonlinearities to make growth/pruning coherent.
- Well positioned relative to pruning, hyperparameter optimization, and network growing literature; emphasis on single-run dynamic adaptation is the key differentiator.
- Theorem is valuable though intuitive given sparsity-inducing regularization; empirics are mixed but encouraging and dataset-dependent.
- Request to expand plain-language intuition for Theorem 1 and why ℓp fan-in/fan-out curbs unbounded growth.
- Suggest improving Figure 2 clarity, legends, and explicit mapping between nonparametric final sizes and parametric baselines.
- Recommend an ablation that retrains the discovered final architecture from scratch to isolate benefits of dynamic training vs architecture selection.
- Ask for practical hyperparameter guidance for λ and AdaRad step sizes (rules-of-thumb, sensitivity plots, diagnostics).
- Request robustness analyses across random seeds for final sizes, performance, and architecture similarity.
- Suggest discussing/experimenting with using both fan-in and fan-out penalties together and whether theorem extends.
- Clarify the statement that adding/removing zero units changes the regularizer (implementation subtleties, normalization, counting conventions).
- Clarify definition of “zero unit” (zero fan-in or zero fan-out suffices for f-equivalence) to avoid confusion.
- Questions for authors: why nonparametric training can outperform retraining, core intuition of Theorem 1, role of λ in controlling width, sensitivity to initialization, combined-penalty effects, and confirmation that zero fan-in or fan-out units are removable without changing f.
