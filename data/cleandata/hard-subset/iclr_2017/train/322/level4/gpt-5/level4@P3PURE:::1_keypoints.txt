- Proposes a nonparametric neural network that grows by adding zero-valued units and uses a sparsity regularizer to prune irrelevant units, akin to random search over architectures guided by sparsity.  
- Problem is important and the paper reports interesting results.  
- Major concern: computational complexity—decomposing fan-in weights and converting to radial–angular coordinates may add nontrivial overhead; authors should quantify extra operations relative to standard networks.  
- Practical evaluation requested: report wall-clock time per epoch and total training time to demonstrate real-world overhead.  
- Empirical issue: on the convex dataset the method returns very small networks and underperforms parametric models; authors should provide insight into why this occurs.  
- Overall: clarifying computational overhead and explaining the convex-dataset behavior would significantly strengthen the submission.
