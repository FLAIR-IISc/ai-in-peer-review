- Proposes a framework that grows and prunes units during a single training run using ℓ2 fan-in/fan-out group sparsity, with a theorem showing a finite-size minimizer.  
- Empirical claim: nonparametric-trained networks can match or improve parametric models while often being smaller.  
- Decision: Reject. Primary reasons are insufficient empirical evaluation and added hyperparameter/training-time trade-offs.  
- Experiments are limited to small benchmarks and lack comparisons with strong pruning/compression baselines.  
- No direct comparisons reported for accuracy, parameter count, FLOPs, or inference latency against established compression methods.  
- The method introduces extra hyperparameters (unit addition rate, regularization strength, schedules) whose tunability and practical ease are not demonstrated.  
- Growth/shrinkage hyperparameters also affect training time; wall-clock and data-efficiency gains are not shown and scalability to large models is unclear.  
- Theoretical guarantee of a finite-size minimizer is valuable but doesn’t address generalization or potential under/over-fitting from dynamic addition/removal.  
- Requested: include pruning/compression baselines, larger-scale and convolutional evaluations, and wall-clock training time comparisons.  
- Requested: sensitivity analyses and ablations for regularization strength, addition frequency, and schedule transferability across datasets.  
- Requested: clarify dependence of minimizing size on λ and p, interaction with depth, and practical recommendations for setting λ or annealing strategies.  
- Requested: examine effect of activation/normalization choices, report runtime/memory overheads, and discuss extensions to convolutional/recurrent architectures.  
- Overall: clearly written and theoretically interesting, but empirical robustness, scalability, and hyperparameter evidence are insufficient for acceptance.
