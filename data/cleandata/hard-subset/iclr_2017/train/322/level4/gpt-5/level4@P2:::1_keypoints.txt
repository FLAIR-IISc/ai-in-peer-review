- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that dynamically add and prune units during a single training run using a group L2 (fan-in/fan-out) penalty.  
- Proves that with this regularization a global minimum exists at finite width, preventing unbounded growth.  
- Proposes CapNorm, a capped variant of BatchNorm designed to be compatible with shrinkage-based regularization.  
- Proposes AdaRad, an optimizer decomposing per-unit gradients into radial and angular components and normalizing only the angular part so new units can learn before shrinkage removes them.  
- Experiments on MNIST, rectangles-images, convex, and a large OpenML “poker” dataset show compact, competitive architectures and occasional outperformance of tuned parametric baselines.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main reason: tackles automated width selection with a principled, unified approach combining theory and algorithm rather than an outer-loop search.  
- Main reason: CapNorm and AdaRad are novel, well-motivated, and effective across multiple datasets including a large-scale case.  
- Theoretical contribution: finiteness guarantee for global minimizers under fan-in/fan-out group penalties is valuable.  
- Training pipeline coherence: self-similar activations, CapNorm, and AdaRad together mitigate scale and shrinkage/normalization tensions.  
- Empirical finding: nonparametric networks match or outperform same-size parametric baselines and scale beyond toy settings.  
- Literature placement: clearly distinguishes from post-hoc pruning, black-box hyperparameter search, and relates to pruning/growth and Bayesian selection.  
- Requested: quantify computational overhead (operation counts per forward/backward, memory footprint, wall-clock time per epoch and to target validation error).  
- Requested ablations: AdaRad vs SGD/RMSprop/Adam (holding other components fixed).  
- Requested ablations: CapNorm vs BatchNorm vs no normalization (with/without regularization).  
- Requested ablations: fan-in vs fan-out regularization and p=1 vs p=2 effects.  
- Requested sensitivity analyses: regularization strength λ, unit addition rate/schedule (ν, νfreq), step sizes (αr, αφ), and new-unit initialization scale.  
- Requested comparisons to other growth/pruning baselines (e.g., dynamic network surgery, structured sparsity methods) beyond fixed-width baselines.  
- Requested reproducibility details and suggested code release due to interacting components and schedules.  
- Question: precise per-iteration cost of AdaRad vs SGD/RMSprop/Adam (big-O, FLOPs/epoch) and how per-unit decomposition scales with width/depth.  
- Question: wall-clock comparisons to reach fixed validation error on MNIST and rectangles-images.  
- Question: diagnostics for convex dataset failure—whether caused by λ, addition schedule, CapNorm behavior, or AdaRad radial step size, and whether annealing λ or delaying shrinkage helps.  
- Question: role of CapNorm vs BatchNorm when correcting for regularizer interaction (e.g., freezing BN scales); does capping at 1 limit representation or slow learning?  
- Question: role of AdaRad—how accuracy/stability change if AdaRad is replaced by Adam/RMSprop while keeping add/prune and shrinkage-handling modifications.  
- Question: theory assumptions—how ReLU non-differentiability is handled by directional derivatives and the linearized-network argument.  
- Question: whether a single λ sufficed across layers in experiments or layer-wise tuning improved results.  
- Overall judgment: compelling, principled single-run width learning approach; addressing computational overhead and convex failure mode would strengthen the paper.
