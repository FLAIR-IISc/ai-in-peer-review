- Paper proposes a nonparametric NN that grows/shrinks by randomly inserting zero-valued units and using a sparsity-inducing regularizer to prune irrelevant weights, akin to randomized architecture search guided by sparsity.

- Major concern: quantify computational overhead — decomposing fan-in, radial–angular reparameterization, and unit insertion may be costly; report extra ops per update/epoch, scaling with depth/width and insertion frequency, and provide wall‑clock/runtime comparisons (e.g., time to target accuracy).

- Major concern: on the convex dataset the method shrinks to very small architectures and underperforms fixed baselines — need explanation whether this is due to over-regularization, parameterization, or insertion/pruning dynamics.

- Requested analyses to clarify the above: vary sparsity strength, insertion rate, and minimum layer width; report learning curves, final selected sizes, and ablations to distinguish inherent limitations from tuning issues.
