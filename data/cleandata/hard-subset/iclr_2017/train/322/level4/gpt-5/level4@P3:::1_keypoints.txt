- Contribution: Introduces nonparametric neural networks that learn layer widths in a single training run by adding f-equivalent zero units and removing redundancies via ℓ2 fan-in/fan-out group sparsity.
- Theory: Theorem 1 guarantees a finite global minimizer under fan-in/out regularization (λ>0, 1≤p<∞).
- Hyperparameter reduction: Proposition 1 shows per-layer regularization strengths can be consolidated into one λ for self-similar nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU) via rescaling.
- Practical innovations: Proposes CapNorm (capped batch-normalization) compatible with ℓ2 group penalties and AdaRad, an optimizer that decomposes gradients into radial and angular components and normalizes only angular updates.
- Empirics summary: On MNIST, rectangles-images, convex, and a large OpenML “poker” dataset, the method finds compact architectures that often match or beat same-size parametric baselines and sometimes approach best black-box search results; convex dataset shows over-shrinking.
- Decision: Reviewer recommends Weak Accept.
- Main reasons for acceptance: Clean theoretical result ensuring finite size, a practical training recipe (AdaRad+CapNorm), and empirical evidence for discovering compact competitive models without outer-loop search.
- Problem significance: Integrates network size selection into training, reducing need for expensive black-box hyperparameter searches.
- Theory strength: Theorem 1 is nontrivial and alleviates concerns about unbounded width being preferred; Proposition 1 reduces hyperparam complexity.
- Algorithmic strength: AdaRad preserves intended group shrinkage while stabilizing new units; CapNorm prevents batch-norm from neutralizing regularization.
- Empirical strength: Nonparametric models outperform parametric baselines of same size and scale to large datasets; honest reporting of failure case on convex dataset.
- Request: Report wall-clock per-epoch time, memory footprint, and total time-to-target for parametric+RMSprop/Adam, parametric+SGD/momentum, and nonparametric+AdaRad.
- Stronger baselines requested: Compare to fixed over-parameterized networks with the same ℓ2 fan-in penalty and to representative pruning/growing methods that don’t require strong seeds.
- Ablations requested: Quantify contributions of CapNorm and AdaRad independently (e.g., BN+AdaRad, CapNorm+Adam, AdaRad without CapNorm).
- Sensitivity analyses: Provide sensitivity and schedule studies for λ, ν (unit addition rate), νfreq, αr, and αφ and visualize growth/shrinkage vs final accuracy/size.
- Convex dataset follow-up: Diagnose underperformance (premature elimination?) and test remedies (increase αr, decrease λ, λ-annealing, AdaRad-M) with supporting experiments.
- Implementation clarity: Simplify AdaRad pseudocode, add a high-level schematic, and provide notes on numerical stability, parallelization, and efficient vectorized angular normalization.
- Generalization analysis: Report calibration or margin metrics to support the claim that nonparametric models regularize well despite not driving training cross-entropy to near zero.
- Question on computational cost: Quantify per-iteration and total training time overhead of AdaRad vs SGD/RMSprop/Adam, plus FLOPs estimates and memory for caches; how does overhead scale with units vs weights?
- Question on addition schedules: How sensitive is performance to ν and νfreq, and was adaptive unit addition based on validation or learning progress explored?
- Question on initialization: Were alternatives to random fan-in + zero fan-out tested (e.g., small nonzero fan-out, data-dependent init, copying existing units) and how do they affect unit survival?
- Question on CapNorm design: Why cap std at 1; were other or learnable caps tried; how does CapNorm compare to BN without learned scale/shift with careful λ tuning?
- Question on AdaRad momentum: Why did AdaRad-M not improve results; when does momentum in the angular component help or destabilize, especially with frequent unit churn?
- Theoretical extension question: Can Theorem 1 be extended toward validation risk or generalization bounds when architecture is learned during training?
- Extensions question: How to adapt the framework to convolutional layers (filter-wise group penalties) and depth selection, and does AdaRad’s radial/angular decomposition transfer to convolutional filters?
