This paper proposes a nonparametric neural network model that automatically learns model size during training. The key idea is to randomly add zero-valued units and use a sparse regularizer to drive irrelevant weights to zero. Conceptually, this resembles a random search over a discrete architecture space, guided by sparsity to eliminate useless units. The problem is important, and the paper reports interesting results.

Main comments:

- Computational complexity. What is the additional computational cost of the proposed algorithm? The decomposition of each unit’s fan-in weights into a parallel component and an orthogonal component, along with the transformation into radial–angular coordinates, may introduce nontrivial overhead. Please quantify the extra operations relative to a standard parametric neural network and discuss their practical impact. It would also be helpful to include running-time experiments (e.g., wall-clock time per epoch and total training time) to demonstrate the overhead in practice.

- Performance on the convex dataset. It is observed that the nonparametric networks return small networks on the convex dataset and are therefore inferior to parametric networks. Do you have insight into why this occurs?

Overall, the paper addresses an important problem and presents an interesting approach. Clarifying the computational overhead and providing insight into the behavior on the convex dataset would strengthen the submission.
