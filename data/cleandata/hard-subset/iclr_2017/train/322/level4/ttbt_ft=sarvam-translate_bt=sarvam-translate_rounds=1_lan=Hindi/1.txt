This research paper proposes a non-parametric neural network model that automatically learns the model's size during the training process. The main idea is to randomly add zero units and use a sparse regularizer to automatically remove irrelevant weights. This idea seems like a random search approach in discrete space with the help of sparse regularization to remove redundant units. This is a significant problem, and the research paper provides interesting results. My main comments are listed below:

What is the algorithm's additional computational complexity? The decomposition of each fan-in weight into a parallel component and an orthogonal component, and the conversion into radial-angular coordinates, may require a lot of additional computational time. The authors may need to discuss the amount of additional operation relative to a parametric neural network. Furthermore, it would be useful to show some running time experiments.

It has been observed that non-parametric networks return smaller networks on convex datasets so that it is less than a parametric network. Any information on this?
