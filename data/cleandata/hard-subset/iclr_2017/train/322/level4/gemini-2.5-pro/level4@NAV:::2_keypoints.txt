- Core idea is compelling: dynamic addition/removal of units lets architectures adapt to data rather than user priors and is a promising direction.
- Significant theoretical result: fan-in/fan-out regularization guarantees an optimum with a finite parameter set, preventing unbounded growth and overfitting.
- The theorem feels intuitive; the paper needs more in-depth discussion and intuition rather than extensive background review.
- Suggestion to reallocate space from prior-work review to expand and contextualize the core theoretical claims.
- Key experiment missing: direct comparison to a static network with the same final architecture to validate efficiency and pruning behavior; Figure 2 is mixed and hard to interpret.
- Need explanation for why the dynamic training trajectory can sometimes outperform training an equivalent static network from scratch.
- Theorem 1 requires deeper practical discussion and targeted experiments to substantiate its implications.
- Open theoretical questions: relation between data properties and optimal lambda; sensitivity of final architecture to lambda; dependence on weight initialization; effect of combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers.
- Clarify the claim that adding zero units changes the regularizer value, since many regularizers (e.g., L2) are unaffected by zero parameters.
- Clarify the definition of a “zero unit”: removing a unit without changing f seems to require both fan-in and fan-out to be zero, not just one.
- Reviewer raised score to 7 and expects authors to address these points in revision.
