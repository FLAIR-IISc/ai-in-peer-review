- Proposes a simple, elegant method to dynamically add/remove neural units during training using group sparsity to zero-out entire units.
- Main theoretical result: with appropriate regularization, the loss is minimized by a network with a finite number of hidden units.
- Theory does not guarantee avoidance of overfitting or underfitting; experiments suggest this is not a practical issue but not conclusive.
- Method shifts rather than eliminates hyperparameter tuning by introducing new hyperparameters (unit-addition rate, sparsity strength) that implicitly control final network size.
- No experimental comparison to established compression techniques (e.g., post-hoc pruning), leaving competitiveness unclear.
- Hyperparameters appear to jointly control final size and total training time, implying potentially slower training and scalability concerns for large networks.
- Experiments are encouraging but limited; larger-scale and more complex evaluations are needed to demonstrate practical utility.
- Overall, an interesting, well-written step toward adaptive/lifelong networks, but practical benefits remain speculative and require further validation.
