- Proposes dynamic addition/removal of units so model capacity adapts to data rather than fixed a priori.  
- Theorem 1: fan-in/fan-out regularization guarantees an optimum with a finite number of parameters, preventing unbounded growth/overfitting.  
- Theoretical result likened to Lasso/Elastic Net inducing sparsity.  
- Paper lacks intuition and deeper discussion of Theorem 1; more exposition and reallocation of space from related work suggested.  
- Key experimental need: show dynamic models outperform fixed architectures with the same final unit count to validate the method.  
- Current experiments (Figure 2) are mixed and unclear; sometimes non-parametric nets do better, sometimes worse—needs clearer presentation and analysis.  
- Unclear why dynamically trained models can outperform the same final architecture trained from scratch; authors should explain this phenomenon.  
- Request more experiments/analysis on: predictability of lambda from data, sensitivity of final architecture to random initialization, and combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers.  
- Clarification requested: why does adding a "zero unit" change the regularizer value (counterexample: L2 norm would not)?  
- Clarification requested on "zero unit" definition: removing a unit without changing f seems to require both fan-in and fan-out to be zero—please clarify.  
- Reviewer raised score to 7 and expects these issues to be addressed to strengthen the paper.
