- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that grow zero-initialized units and prune them with a group‑sparse (l2 fan‑in) regularizer to discover network size in one training run.  
- Provides theoretical justification (existence of a global minimum at finite network size) and proposes a new optimizer, Adaptive Radial‑Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad).  
- Empirical results claim the method can find compact, well‑performing architectures on several datasets.  
- Reviewer’s decision is borderline and contingent on authors’ responses to key concerns.  
- Major concern 1: potential computational overhead of AdaRad—per‑unit gradient decomposition and radial/angular updates may be much more expensive than standard optimizers.  
- Reviewer asks for theoretical and empirical (wall‑clock) complexity comparisons to optimizers like RMSprop/Adam to assess practical utility.  
- Major concern 2: method underperforms on the convex dataset, converging to smaller, less accurate models than parametric baselines.  
- Reviewer requests explanation for this failure case and whether it reveals a fundamental limitation of the growth/prune dynamics, fan‑in regularizer, or AdaRad’s exploration.  
- Questions to authors: provide computational complexity analysis and wall‑clock training time comparisons for experiments.  
- Questions to authors: explain characteristics of the convex dataset causing poor performance and whether this limits the method’s general applicability.
