- Proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that automatically determine hidden layer sizes during a single training run.
- Theoretical contribution: shows under l_p fan-in or fan-out regularization the network has a finite optimal size (Theorem 1).
- Practical contribution: training scheme that dynamically adds units and prunes redundant ones using an l2 group sparsity penalty.
- Algorithmic contribution: introduces AdaRad (Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent) tailored to dynamic growth/shrinkage.
- Empirical claim: finds compact, high-performing networks competitive with or better than extensive random search on several datasets.
- Results: strong on MNIST and rectangles images, underperforms on the convex dataset — shows promise but not universally superior.
- Recommendation: Accept — novel, significant, and coherent alternative to costly architecture search.
- Strength: theoretical grounding (Theorem 1) gives crucial soundness that network won’t grow without bound.
- Main weakness: insufficient intuitive explanation of Theorem 1 in the main text reduces its impact.
- Suggestion: add a plain-English explanation of why fan-in/fan-out penalties lead to a finite optimal size.
- Suggestion: analyze why gradual growth can outperform training the same final-size network from scratch (possible curriculum/local-minima effects).
- Suggestion: study sensitivity of results to the regularization parameter lambda and report its influence on final architecture.
- Suggestion: improve Figure 2 clarity and legends to make plots easier to interpret.
- Question: how sensitive is final architecture to lambda and random initialization across runs?
- Question: have you considered combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers (e.g., Elastic Net) and its theoretical/practical effects?
- Question: clarify how adding a "zero unit" changes the fan-in/fan-out regularizer value when its l2 norm is zero.
- Question: clarify the definition and pruning mechanism for a "zero unit" and how removing it preserves the network function f(x).
