- Proposes dynamically adding/removing hidden units when input/output weights go to zero, encouraged by group sparsity regularization — straightforward and well-motivated.  
- Theoretical result: appropriate regularization yields a loss-minimizing network of finite size, though this doesn't guarantee absence of over- or under-fitting in practice.  
- Preliminary experiments indicate over-/under-fitting is not a major issue, but results are limited.  
- Method introduces new hyperparameters (e.g., addition frequency, weight decay) that indirectly determine final size, so it may not simplify hyperparameter tuning.  
- Claims smaller networks can match performance of larger ones, which is important for inference speed, power, and memory on mobile devices.  
- Lacks comparisons to established compression methods like post-training pruning, making competitiveness unclear.  
- Training time and final network size are coupled via the same hyperparameters, potentially slowing training versus fixed architectures; no analysis of scaling to large models.  
- Experiments are promising but not comprehensive; evaluations on much larger and more complex problems are needed.  
- Overall: interesting and well-written with potential for lifelong-adaptive networks, but current results are preliminary and speculative.
