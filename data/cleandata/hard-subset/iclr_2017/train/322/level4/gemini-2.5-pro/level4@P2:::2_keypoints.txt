- Introduces "nonparametric neural networks" that dynamically grow and prune units during a single training run.  
- Proposes a novel optimizer, Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad), for training dynamic networks.  
- Theorem 1: fan-in or fan-out group-norm regularization guarantees a global minimum at a finite network size, preventing unbounded growth.  
- Empirical results show the method can find compact, competitive models without costly architecture hyperparameter search.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key strength: novel and significant approach to automatic architecture selection within one training process.  
- Key strength: strong theoretical grounding (formal proof that complexity is bounded).  
- Method uses group-sparsity regularizer to govern adding/removing units; sometimes outperforms same-size parametric networks and finds smaller models than random search.  
- Critique: Theorem 1 is under-discussed in the main text and needs more intuitive explanation and broader implications.  
- Request: Add plain-English intuition (e.g., analogy to L1/Lasso) and highlight the most surprising/important insights from the theorem.  
- Question: Why do nonparametric networks sometimes outperform fixed-size networks—better optimization path or more robust local minima?  
- Question: How critical is the regularization parameter λ to final structure, and does it relate predictably to dataset properties?  
- Question: How sensitive is final architecture to random initialization and different seeds?  
- Question: If fan-in and fan-out regularizers are combined, do the theoretical guarantees still hold?  
- Technical clarifications requested about how adding/removing units affects regularizer value and the definition/implications of "zero units" when σ(0)=0.  
- Presentation suggestion: Improve clarity of Figure 2 (e.g., separate plots or better labeling) to aid interpretation.
