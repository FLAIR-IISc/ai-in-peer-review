- Proposes a nonparametric neural network that stochastically adds zero‑initialized units and uses sparse regularization to prune irrelevant ones, learning network size during training.  
- Frames the approach as a random search over discrete architectures guided by sparsity, and reports promising results on an important problem (automatic model selection).  
- Major concern about computational overhead from decomposing fan‑in weights into parallel/orthogonal components and converting to radial‑angular coordinates, which may substantially increase training time.  
- Requests theoretical analysis quantifying additional computational complexity (e.g., extra FLOPs per update) relative to baseline feedforward networks.  
- Requests empirical wall‑clock training time comparisons between the proposed method and parametric baselines to clarify practical trade-offs.  
- Notes poorer performance on the simpler/convex dataset where the method converged to smaller models; asks whether sparse regularization is excessively aggressive or if the method has limitations on simple data requiring denser representations.  
- Overall positive: well‑motivated and interesting approach, but addressing computational overhead and behavior on simple datasets would strengthen the contribution.
