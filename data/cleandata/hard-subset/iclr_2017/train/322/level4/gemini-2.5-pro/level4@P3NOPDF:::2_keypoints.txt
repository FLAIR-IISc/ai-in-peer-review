- Proposes a non-parametric method that dynamically adds/removes neural units during training.
- Introduces a novel fan-in/fan-out regularization scheme to enable dynamic architecture changes.
- Theorem 1 proves the regularizer yields an optimal solution with a finite number of units, preventing unbounded growth.
- Lacks a plain-English explanation and intuition for why the regularizer forces units to zero.
- Needs deeper discussion of implications, particularly how the regularization parameter λ shapes the final architecture.
- Should analyze whether the theoretical result holds when fan-in and fan-out regularizers are combined.
- Recommends condensing prior work to make room for expanded theoretical discussion.
- Missing a crucial baseline: compare the final “grown” network to the same final architecture trained from scratch.
- Figure 2 is hard to interpret and shows mixed results that require clearer presentation and analysis.
- Needs investigation into why dynamically discovered architectures sometimes outperform identical architectures trained from scratch.
- Should run sensitivity analyses to assess how random initialization affects the final architecture and robustness.
- Requires clarification of the claim that adding “zero units” changes the regularizer—explain the specific regularizer property causing this.
- Clarify the definition of a “zero unit”: whether both fan-in and fan-out must be zero to remove a unit without changing the network function.
- Reviewer raised score due to the work’s promise and urges addressing these points to improve clarity and impact.
