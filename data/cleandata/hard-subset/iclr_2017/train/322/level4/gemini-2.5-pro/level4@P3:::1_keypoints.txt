- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that dynamically grow (add zero units) and prune with an l2 fan-in/out group sparsity regularizer to find layer widths in one training run.
- Theorem 1 shows the regularized optimization has a global minimum at a finite network size, providing theoretical justification.
- Proposes AdaRad optimizer that separates angular (direction) and radial (magnitude) updates so new units can learn before being pruned.
- Introduces CapNorm, a modified batch-normalization layer compatible with the group regularizer.
- Empirical results show the method can find compact, high-performing architectures on MNIST and rectangles-images, sometimes outperforming fixed-size models and random search while using fewer parameters.
- Provides visualization of neuron lifespans and layer-size evolution to analyze training dynamics.
- Shows scalability evidence on a larger poker dataset, suggesting practical applicability.
- Decision: Accept.
- Limitation: Mixed results overall and notable underperformance on the convex dataset, converging to suboptimally small networks.
- Suggestion: Report empirical wall-clock training time and compare a single nonparametric run to the total time of the random search baseline.
- Suggestion: Consider more adaptive unit-addition schedules (e.g., based on validation plateau) instead of adding one unit per layer per epoch.
- Suggestion: Clarify practical implementation and efficiency of the AdaRad rotation step (e.g., Rodrigues' formula or quaternions) for reproducibility.
- Question: Why does the method underperform on the convex dataset — issues with loss landscape, initialization, or interaction with AdaRad?
- Question: How sensitive are results to the radial step-size coupling (α_r = 150λ) and does the constant factor require tuning that undermines automated model selection?
