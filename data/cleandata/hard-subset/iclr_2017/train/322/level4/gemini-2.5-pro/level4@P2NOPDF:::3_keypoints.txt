- Proposes dynamically adding/removing hidden units during training based on zeroed input/output weights, using a group sparsity regularizer.  
- Provides a theoretical proof that with appropriate regularization the loss is minimized by a network with a finite number of units.  
- Theoretical guarantee does not ensure good generalization; experiments only show reasonable training performance.  
- Motivated by reducing hyperparameter tuning for network size, but method introduces new hyperparameters (e.g., addition frequency, regularization strength).  
- Unclear whether tuning the new hyperparameters is easier than selecting a fixed architecture; authors do not claim reduced training complexity.  
- Claims comparable performance to fixed architectures with fewer units, which would benefit deployment on resource-constrained devices.  
- Experiments omit comparisons to established compression methods (e.g., pruning), so competitiveness is uncertain.  
- Growth-related hyperparameters directly affect training time, potentially making training substantially slower; scalability is not addressed.  
- Empirical evaluation is preliminary and not comprehensive; larger-scale experiments are needed to demonstrate practicality.  
- Overall: interesting and well-written idea aligned with adaptive/lifelong learning, but practicality and scalability remain unproven.
