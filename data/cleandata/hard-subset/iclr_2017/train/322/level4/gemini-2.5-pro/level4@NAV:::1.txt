This work introduces a nonparametric neural network model capable of automatically learning its architectural size throughout the training phase. The central technique involves stochastically adding zero-valued units and employing a sparse regularizer to eliminate irrelevant weights. This approach can be conceptualized as a random search over a discrete architectural space, which is guided by sparse regularization to prune superfluous units. The paper addresses an important problem and delivers interesting findings. My main points of feedback are outlined below:

*   Could the authors elaborate on the computational overhead of the proposed algorithm? The decomposition of fan-in weights into parallel and orthogonal components and the subsequent transformation into radial-angular coordinates seem computationally intensive. It would be valuable for the authors to discuss the additional operational cost compared to a conventional parametric neural network. Furthermore, providing empirical results on runtime would significantly strengthen the paper.

*   The observation that the nonparametric model produces smaller, and consequently inferior, networks on the convex dataset is noteworthy. Do the authors have any insight into the reason for this behavior?
