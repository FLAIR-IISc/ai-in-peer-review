- Proposes "nonparametric neural networks" to automatically determine network size during a single training run.  
- Dynamically adds new units and uses an l2 group-sparsity regularizer (fan-in or fan-out) to prune redundant units.  
- Theorem 1 proves the process converges to a network of finite size.  
- Introduces a specialized optimizer (AdaRad) and a modified normalization layer (CapNorm) to enable dynamic training.  
- Recommends accepting the paper for novelty, practical relevance, and solid theoretical foundation.  
- Main strength: principled dynamic architecture search with a significant formal guarantee analogous to sparsity results like Lasso.  
- Experiments are mixed but show competitiveness or superiority on 2 of 3 datasets and practical advantage of avoiding external architecture search.  
- Noted phenomenon: dynamically grown networks can outperform same-size parametric networks trained from scratch, suggesting growth/pruning may regularize or guide optimization.  
- Suggestion: add clearer, high-level intuition for Theorem 1 in the main text.  
- Suggestion: perform sensitivity analysis to assess stability of final architectures across random initializations.  
- Question: Why does nonparametric training sometimes yield higher accuracy than training the same final architecture from scratch?  
- Question: Does Theorem 1 still hold when combining both fan-in and fan-out regularizers?  
- Question: How crucial is the regularization hyperparameter lambda for final network size, and could lambda be adapted or annealed during training?  
- Question: Clarify whether adding a "zero unit" changes the regularizer because of non-zero initialization of its fan-in or fan-out.  
- Question: Clarify removal condition—if a unit's fan-in becomes zero (making its activation zero), can it be removed even if its fan-out weights are non-zero?
