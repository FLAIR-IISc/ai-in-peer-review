- Proposes a simple method to dynamically add/remove units during training using zero-valued input/output weights and group sparsity regularization.  
- Provides a theoretical proof that with appropriate regularization the loss is minimized by a network with a finite number of units.  
- The theory does not guarantee avoidance of over- or under-fitting, though initial experiments suggest this is not problematic.  
- Learning architecture could reduce hyperparameter tuning burden, but the method introduces new hyperparameters that implicitly control final size.  
- It is unclear whether tuning these new hyperparameters is easier than selecting network size in a fixed-architecture approach.  
- Claims the method produces smaller networks with comparable performance, which would reduce inference time, power, and memory usage.  
- Paper lacks experimental comparisons to existing compression/pruning techniques, so competitiveness is uncertain.  
- The same hyperparameters affect both final size and training duration, potentially making training slower than fixed architectures.  
- Authors do not discuss how training time scales to much larger networks, raising concerns about practicality for large-scale problems.  
- Experiments are encouraging but insufficiently comprehensive; evaluation on substantially larger problems is needed.  
- Overall, an interesting, well-written contribution toward adaptive/lifelong networks, but results remain speculative at this stage.
