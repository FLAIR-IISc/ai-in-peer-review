- Core idea is compelling: dynamically adding/removing units to let model size adapt to data instead of user priors.  
- Theorem 1: under fan-in or fan-out regularization, the error optimum uses a finite number of parameters, preventing unbounded growth/overfitting.  
- The theoretical result is valuable but its intuition and implications are underdeveloped in the main text.  
- Recommend condensing some prior-work discussion to make room for deeper analysis of Theorem 1.  
- Important experiment missing: compare method to a fixed-architecture network with the same final number of units trained from scratch.  
- Figure 2 results are mixed and unclear; surprising cases where dynamic nets outperform fixed ones need explanation (e.g., avoiding poor local minima).  
- Theorem 1 should be accompanied by plain-English interpretation and targeted experiments to validate implications.  
- Specific empirical questions to study: sensitivity to regularization parameter lambda; sensitivity to initialization; behavior when combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers.  
- Clarify claim that adding zero units changes the regularizer’s value (counterintuitive for norms like L2).  
- Clarify definition of “zero unit”: does removal without changing f require both fan-in and fan-out weights to be zero?  
- Reviewer raised score to 7 and urges addressing these points to strengthen the paper.
