- Proposes a simple method to dynamically add/remove hidden units based on whether their input/output weights are zero, using group sparsity regularization.  
- Provides a theoretical proof that the loss is minimized by a network with a finite number of units under this regularization.  
- Theoretical result does not guarantee generalization, though preliminary experiments show no severe over- or under-fitting.  
- Produces compact networks with performance comparable to larger static models, benefiting inference time, power, and memory.  
- Introduces new hyperparameters (e.g., unit addition frequency, weight decay) so it may not reduce the overall hyperparameter tuning burden.  
- Lacks comparison to established network compression techniques (e.g., post-training pruning), making competitiveness unclear.  
- Concerns about coupling between hyperparameters, final network size, and potentially increased training time; scalability to large models is unaddressed.  
- Experiments are encouraging as a proof-of-concept but insufficiently comprehensive; larger and more complex benchmarks are needed.  
- Overall: interesting and well-written with promising direction toward adaptive/life-long learning, but results are preliminary and speculative due to experimental and scalability gaps.
