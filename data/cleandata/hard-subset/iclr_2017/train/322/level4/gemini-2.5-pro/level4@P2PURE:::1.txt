This paper proposes a nonparametric neural network model that automatically determines its size during the training process. The core idea is to randomly add inactive units and use a sparse regularizer to automatically prune those that are irrelevant. This approach can be interpreted as a random search over the discrete space of network architectures, where sparse regularization is used to eliminate superfluous units. The paper addresses an important problem and presents interesting results. My main comments are as follows:

The paper should include a discussion of the algorithm's computational complexity. The decomposition of each set of fan-in weights into parallel and orthogonal components, along with the transformation into radial-angular coordinates, likely introduces significant computational overhead. The authors should analyze the additional operations required relative to a standard parametric neural network. Furthermore, it would be useful to provide empirical results comparing running times.

The paper observes that on the convex dataset, the nonparametric method produces smaller networks, resulting in inferior performance compared to parametric networks. It would strengthen the paper to offer some insight into this phenomenon. Why does the model select a smaller, and apparently suboptimal, architecture in this case?
