- Introduces "nonparametric neural networks" that dynamically grow and prune hidden units during a single training run using group sparsity regularization.
- Proves theoretically (Theorem 1) that the training objective has a global minimum at a finite network size under the proposed regularizer.
- Proposes AdaRad optimizer and CapNorm to separately manage unit direction learning and magnitude shrinkage.
- Empirical results show the method can find compact, high-performing networks competitive with parametric baselines and random search.
- Decision: Weak Accept.
- Strength: conceptually clear, simple, and theoretically grounded approach to automatic architecture selection.
- Concern: practical benefits over existing methods are not conclusively established.
- Hyperparameter trade-off: replaces layer-width tuning with several sensitive hyperparameters (λ, αr, αφ, ν, νfreq) with unclear robustness.
- Lack of comparison to post-hoc pruning: no strong "train-then-prune" baseline to assess performance-vs-size trade-offs.
- Training efficiency issue: nonparametric networks reach low error more slowly due to gradual capacity growth, potentially problematic for large-scale problems.
- Suggestion: consider smarter unit initialization (e.g., Net2Net-style) to integrate new units and speed convergence.
- Suggestion: explore adaptive or more aggressive schedules for adding units instead of fixed one-per-layer-per-epoch.
- Positive note: analysis of training dynamics, fan-in/fan-out behavior, and the role of CapNorm is insightful.
- Questions for authors: clarify hyperparameter trade-offs and robustness; compare to strong pruning pipelines; discuss scalability and strategies (e.g., adding units in larger batches) to mitigate slower initial convergence.
