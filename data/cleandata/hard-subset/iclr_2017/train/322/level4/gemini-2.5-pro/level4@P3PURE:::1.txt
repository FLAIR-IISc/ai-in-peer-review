This paper proposes a nonparametric neural network model that automatically learns its own size during the training process. The core idea is to randomly add new units and employ a sparse regularizer to automatically prune weights associated with irrelevant units. This approach can be viewed as a random search over the discrete space of network architectures, guided by sparse regularization to eliminate ineffective components. The paper addresses the important problem of automated model selection and presents interesting results.

My main comments are as follows:

1.  The paper would benefit from a discussion of the algorithm's computational complexity. The decomposition of each fan-in's weights into parallel and orthogonal components, along with the transformation into radial-angular coordinates, appears computationally intensive. The authors should quantify this overhead by analyzing the additional operations relative to a standard parametric neural network. Furthermore, it would be useful to support this analysis with empirical experiments on running time.

2.  It is observed that the nonparametric model returns small networks on the convex dataset, resulting in performance that is inferior to that of parametric networks. Could the authors provide any insight into this behavior?
