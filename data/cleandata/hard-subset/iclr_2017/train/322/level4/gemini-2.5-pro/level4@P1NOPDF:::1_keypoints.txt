- Proposes a nonparametric neural network that grows by randomly adding zero-initialized units and prunes them via a sparse regularizer to learn network size automatically.  
- Frames the method as random search over discrete architectures guided by sparse regularization.  
- Tackles an important problem and presents interesting empirical results.  
- Requests a detailed analysis of computational complexity, since decomposing fan-in vectors and converting to radial-angular coordinates may add significant overhead.  
- Asks authors to quantify the number of extra operations compared to standard parametric networks.  
- Requests empirical training-runtime results to assess practical trade-offs.  
- Notes that on the convex dataset the nonparametric model yields a smaller but worse-performing network and asks whether this is due to overly strong regularization or a limitation of the architectural search.
