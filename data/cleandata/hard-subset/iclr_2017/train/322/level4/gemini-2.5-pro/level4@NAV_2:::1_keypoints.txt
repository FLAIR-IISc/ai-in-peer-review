- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that automatically determine hidden layer sizes within a single training run.  
- Core method dynamically grows networks by adding units and shrinks them via an L2 fan-in regularizer to remove redundant units.  
- Proposes a specialized optimizer, AdaRad (Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent), and a compatible normalization scheme, CapNorm.  
- Provides a theoretical guarantee (Theorem 1) that the optimization objective attains a global minimum at a finite network size.  
- Empirical results on MNIST and rectangles datasets show the method finds compact, high-performing models competitive with or superior to exhaustive random search.  
- Recommendation: accept, due to novelty in integrating architecture selection into training and promising empirical/theoretical support.  
- Main strength: principled, elegant formulation that avoids expensive outer-loop hyperparameter searches by embedding size selection in optimization.  
- Concern: method underperforms on a convex dataset, converging to smaller, less accurate models, indicating possible failure modes.  
- Concern: practical computational overhead of AdaRad (per-iteration radial/angular decomposition) is unclear and may offset gains from single-run selection.  
- Suggested improvement: include empirical wall-clock training-time comparisons versus standard parametric networks with Adam/RMSprop.  
- Suggested improvement: investigate and explain the convex-dataset failure mode (possible causes: L2 regularization interaction, unit addition schedule, or AdaRad dynamics).
