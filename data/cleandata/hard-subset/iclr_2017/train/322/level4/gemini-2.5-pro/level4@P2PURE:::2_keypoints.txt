- Paper proposes dynamically adding/removing units so architectures adapt to problem/data complexity rather than fixed user choice.
- Theorem 1: with fan-in or fan-out regularization, the error optimum uses a finite number of parameters, preventing unbounded growth/memorization.
- Theorem 1 resembles Lasso/Elastic Net-style regularization-induced sparsity.
- Theorem 1 needs a deeper, intuitive discussion and exploration of its implications; shorten literature review to expand on this.
- Experiments should directly compare the final learned model to a fixed network of the same final size to test benefits of dynamic adaptation.
- Figure 2’s results are mixed and hard to interpret; need clearer presentation and analysis.
- Question why a dynamic training process would outperform training a fixed architecture of the same final size from scratch.
- Request expanded plain-language explanation of Theorem 1 and supporting experiments to illustrate its implications.
- Ask whether the regularizer hyperparameter λ can be predicted from data and whether λ primarily determines final network structure.
- Ask about sensitivity of final architecture to weight initialization and random starts.
- Ask whether Theorem 1 still holds when combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers.
- Clarify why adding zero units is claimed to change the regularizer’s value (zero parameters normally don’t affect L2).
- Clarify definition of zero units: removing a unit without changing function f seems to require both zero fan-in and zero fan-out.
