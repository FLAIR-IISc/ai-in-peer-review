- Proposes a nonparametric neural network that auto-determines size by randomly adding inactive units and using a sparse regularizer to prune irrelevant ones.  
- Frames the method as a random search over discrete architectures where sparse regularization eliminates superfluous units.  
- Addresses an important problem and presents interesting empirical results.  
- Reviewer asks for a detailed analysis of computational complexity; the parallel/orthogonal decomposition and radial–angular transformation likely introduce significant overhead.  
- Authors should quantify additional operations versus standard parametric networks and provide empirical run-time comparisons.  
- On the convex dataset the method yields smaller networks with worse performance; reviewer requests insight into why the model selects a smaller, apparently suboptimal architecture.
