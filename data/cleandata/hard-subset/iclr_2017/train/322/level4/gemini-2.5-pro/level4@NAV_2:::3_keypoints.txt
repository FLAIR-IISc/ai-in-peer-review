- Proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that grow and shrink hidden layers during a single training run using an l2 group-sparsity penalty on unit fan-in.
- Proves that under this regularization the training objective is minimized by a network of finite size.
- Introduces AdaRad, a novel optimizer designed for dynamic network growth/shrinkage.
- Empirical results on several datasets show the approach is viable and promising.
- Recommendation: Weak Accept.
- Main concern: replaces fixed-width hyperparameter with new, potentially sensitive hyperparameters (ν, ν_freq, αr) without analysis of tuning complexity or sensitivity.
- Main concern: insufficient comparison to pruning/compression baselines and unclear advantage over training oversized networks and pruning post-hoc.
- Main concern: potential scalability and computational overhead from continuously adding/removing units not analyzed; unclear practicality for large-scale models.
- Suggestion: include experimental comparisons to established pruning/compression methods (e.g., train-large then prune with l1/l2).
- Suggestion: provide ablation/sensitivity studies on ν, ν_freq, and αr to assess robustness and tuning difficulty.
- Suggestion: present a scalability analysis quantifying training overhead from transient units versus training a final fixed-size network.
- Question for authors: why should dynamic growth from a small network be superior to starting oversized and pruning?
- Question for authors: quantify the computational cost of the long linear growth phase with many transient units compared to a standard training run of the converged network.
