This paper proposes a nonparametric neural network model that automatically learns its own size during the training process. The key idea is to randomly add units with zero-initialized weights and use a sparse regularizer to automatically prune irrelevant units. This approach can be viewed as a form of random search over the discrete space of network architectures, aided by sparse regularization to eliminate unnecessary components. The paper addresses an important problem and presents interesting results. My main comments are listed below:

The paper would benefit from a more detailed analysis of the algorithm's computational complexity. The decomposition of each fan-in weight vector into parallel and orthogonal components, along with the transformation into radial-angular coordinates, seems likely to introduce significant computational overhead. The authors should discuss the number of additional operations this requires relative to a standard parametric neural network. Furthermore, including empirical results on training runtimes would be valuable for assessing the practical trade-offs of the proposed method.

It is observed that on the convex dataset, the nonparametric method produces a smaller network that performs worse than its parametric counterpart. Could the authors provide more insight into this behavior? For instance, is it possible that the regularization is too strong in this setting, or does this result highlight a limitation of the model's architectural search process?
