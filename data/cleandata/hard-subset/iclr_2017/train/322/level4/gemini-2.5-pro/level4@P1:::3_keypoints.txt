1. Proposes nonparametric neural networks that grow units and use fan-in L2 group sparsity to remove redundancies, with a proof of a global minimum at finite size, plus AdaRad optimizer and CapNorm; experiments are promising.

2. Decision: Weak Accept — novel and well-motivated but practical utility remains speculative.

3. Hyperparameter burden: method removes fixed layer widths but introduces hard-to-tune hyperparameters (λ, ν, αr) with no sensitivity analysis.

4. Missing comparison to pruning: lacks experiments against standard post-training pruning/train-large-then-prune baselines to judge competitiveness.

5. Training time and scalability concerns: dynamic grow-and-prune slows convergence (Fig. 3A) and scalability to large modern networks is unclear.

6. Encouraging for lifelong/adaptive learning, but claims of practicality need experiments on larger datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10/100).

7. AdaRad and CapNorm are interesting contributions that require ablation studies to isolate their benefits.

8. Suggestion: Evaluate how a standard optimizer (e.g., Adam) performs with the group sparsity and grow-and-prune scheme.

9. Question: Need intuition or heuristics for setting ν, αr, and λ — are they task-specific or easily generalizable?

10. Question: How does final accuracy and model size compare directly to state-of-the-art pruning methods?

11. Question: Is slower convergence inherent to the dynamic approach, or can the training and convergence be accelerated for large-scale use?
