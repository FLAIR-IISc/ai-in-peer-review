This paper proposes a nonparametric neural network model that automatically learns the model size during the training process. The core idea is to randomly add zero units and use a sparse regularizer to automatically discard irrelevant weights. The concept seems to be a random search approach on a discrete space with the help of sparse regularization, which eliminates unnecessary units. This is an important problem, and this paper has yielded interesting results. My main comments are listed below:

What is the algorithm's additional computation complexity? Converting each fan-in weight into a parallel component and an orthogonal component and converting it to radial-angular coordinates may require a lot of additional computation time. The authors may need to discuss the amount of additional operations compared to parametric neural networks. Furthermore, some running time tests need to be shown.

It has been observed that nonparametric networks provide smaller networks on convex datasets, so it is inferior to parametric networks. Is there any idea about this?
