- Proposes a nonparametric neural network that dynamically grows/shrinks during training by randomly adding zero units and using a sparse regularizer to prune irrelevant weights, effectively performing a regularizer-guided random search over discrete architectures.
- The problem is significant and the empirical results are intriguing.
- Major concern about additional computational complexity: the fan-in decomposition (parallel/orthogonal) and radial–angular transformation may add substantial overhead; authors should quantify relative operation increase versus parametric networks and report running-time experiments.
- Noted empirical behavior: nonparametric networks often yield smaller models on convex datasets and sometimes underperform parametric networks — authors should provide insights or explanations for this observation.
