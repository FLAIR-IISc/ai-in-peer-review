

This paper introduces a nonparametric neural network model that dynamically learns its size during training. The core approach involves randomly introducing zero-valued units and employing a sparse regularizer to automatically prune irrelevant weights. While the technique resembles a continuous relaxation of discrete model selection via sparse regularization, the problem addressed is significant and the results are promising. Key concerns are as follows:  

1. **Computational Complexity**: The algorithm requires decomposing each fan-in weight into parallel and orthogonal components, followed by transformation into radial-angular coordinates. This process likely incurs substantial additional computational overhead compared to standard parametric networks. The authors should quantify the extra operations relative to baseline models and include empirical running time comparisons.  

2. **Performance on Convex Datasets**: The nonparametric model yields smaller architectures on convex datasets but achieves inferior performance relative to parametric networks. The authors should provide insight into why this discrepancy occurs—whether it stems from the model's structural limitations, the regularization strategy, or dataset-specific properties.
