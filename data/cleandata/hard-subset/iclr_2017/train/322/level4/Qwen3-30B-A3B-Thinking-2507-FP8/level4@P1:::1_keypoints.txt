- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that dynamically grow/shrink during training using sparse regularization and AdaRad optimization.  
- Key idea: continuously add units and remove redundant ones via L2 regularization so dimensionality is optimized during training.  
- Provides a theoretical soundness proof (Theorem 1) showing convergence to a global minimum under appropriate regularization.  
- Empirically shows advantages over parametric networks on two of three benchmark datasets.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Strength: addresses costly model selection by avoiding black-box hyperparameter search and pruning.  
- Concern: paper lacks detailed computational complexity analysis of AdaRad and the radial-angular decomposition.  
- Suggestion: include training-time comparisons between nonparametric and parametric approaches.  
- Concern: inconsistent/poor performance on the convex dataset; nonparametric networks converge to smaller sizes that may harm performance.  
- Request: investigate why the convex dataset behaves differently and whether dataset or architectural factors explain it.  
- Feedback 1: expand discussion on why L2 regularization is preferred over L1 for this framework.  
- Feedback 2: make the connection between nonlinearity self-similarity and layer-wise regularization tuning more explicit, ideally with a concrete example.  
- Feedback 3: add analysis of convergence behavior and how network size evolves during training.  
- Question 1: provide a detailed computational complexity/time comparison of AdaRad vs SGD/Adam and scaling with network size.  
- Question 2: clarify causes of poor convex-dataset performance and possible remedies.  
- Question 3: explain omission of AdaRad-M results in the main paper and when the momentum variant might be beneficial.
