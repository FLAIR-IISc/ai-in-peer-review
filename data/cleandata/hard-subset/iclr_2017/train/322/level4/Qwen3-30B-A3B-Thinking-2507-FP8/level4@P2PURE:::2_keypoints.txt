- Agree the main contribution is dynamic addition/removal of units so models adapt structure to data rather than relying on user-specified architectures.  
- Theorem: with fan-in or fan-out regularization, the error optimum uses a finite number of parameters, preventing unbounded growth and overfitting.  
- The result is analogous to sparsity methods (Lasso/Elastic Net); the paper needs more intuitive explanation of the theorem rather than excessive prior-work discussion.  
- Add experiments comparing the proposed dynamic method to a fixed-size network of identical final capacity to validate efficiency and memory/performance tradeoffs.  
- Figure 2 is unclear and shows mixed results; the paper must explain why non-parametric networks sometimes outperform fixed-size networks even when final size is known.  
- Theorem 1 needs a plain-language summary and more space; propose additional experiments to probe its implications.  
- Ask whether the regularization parameter λ can be predicted from data, how sensitive final structure is to λ, whether initialization/random seeds change architectures, and whether combining fan-in and fan-out preserves the finite-parameter guarantee.  
- Question the claim that adding zero units changes the regularizer value; provide justification (e.g., does L2 change when adding zero-weight units?).  
- Clarify definition of “zero units”: removal preserving the output function requires both fan-in and fan-out weights to be zero, not just one; definition is inconsistent.  
- Reviewer revised rating to 7 and expects the authors to address these points.
