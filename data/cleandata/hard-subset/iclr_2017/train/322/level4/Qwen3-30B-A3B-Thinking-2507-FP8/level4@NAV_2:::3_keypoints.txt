- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that dynamically add units during training and remove redundant ones via an l2 penalty.  
- Proves (Theorem 1) that with proper regularization the loss is minimized by a finite-dimensional network.  
- Proposes a novel training algorithm, AdaRad, to implement the nonparametric framework.  
- Theoretical contribution is rigorous and well-motivated.  
- Empirical results show nonparametric networks can match or exceed parametric networks while using fewer parameters on benchmarks.  
- Experiments are limited to three small datasets (MNIST, rectangles images, convex), restricting empirical claims.  
- Insufficient comparison to established network pruning and compression methods (e.g., Molchanov et al. 2017; Guo et al. 2016).  
- Claims of outperforming same-size scratch-trained networks and black-box methods lack meaningful comparison to existing compression techniques.  
- Scalability test on the poker dataset is promising but insufficient to alleviate concerns about training time.  
- Authors acknowledge training may be significantly slower than parametric approaches.  
- Recommendation: include thorough comparisons to existing pruning techniques.  
- Recommendation: provide detailed analysis of computational cost and training time, especially on larger datasets.  
- Recommendation: explore how hyperparameters (λ, αr, αφ) affect resulting network size for practical tuning.  
- Reviewer questions: how does final model size and accuracy compare to recent pruning methods operating on fully trained networks?  
- Reviewer questions: is the training time penalty acceptable for practical applications at larger scales and do benefits generalize to more challenging vision tasks?
