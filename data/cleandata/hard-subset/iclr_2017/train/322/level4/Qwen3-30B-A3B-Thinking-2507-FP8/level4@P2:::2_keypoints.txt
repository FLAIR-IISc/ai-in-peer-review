- Proposes a framework to automatically determine neural network size during training via continuous unit addition/removal with fan-in/fan-out regularization.  
- Proves (Theorem 1) that training error achieves a global minimum at a finite network size under fan-in or fan-out regularization.  
- Introduces AdaRad optimizer and CapNorm normalization layer to implement the nonparametric framework.  
- Empirical results show nonparametric nets outperform parametric nets of equivalent size on MNIST and rectangles, match exhaustive search, and scale to the poker dataset.  
- Analysis of training dynamics offers insight into how units are added, trained, and pruned.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions due to strong theory and promising experiments.  
- Request to provide more intuitive explanation of Theorem 1 and expand the connection to sparse regularization (Lasso/Elastic Net).  
- Ask for clarification/analysis on why nonparametric networks sometimes outperform parametric networks of the same final size (role of gradual growth).  
- Suggest expanding discussion of the regularization parameter λ, its effect on final structure, relation to dataset properties, and how to choose/estimate it.  
- Clarifying question: how does the regularizer value change when adding zero units (does L2 change when weights are zero)?  
- Clarifying question: precise definition of a "zero unit"—must both fan-in and fan-out be zero to keep f(x) unchanged?  
- Clarifying question: how sensitive is the final network structure to random initialization and different seeds?  
- Clarifying question: what happens if both fan-in and fan-out regularizers are used—does the theoretical guarantee still hold?  
- Clarifying question: why does nonparametric training yield higher accuracy than training a same-size network from scratch, given the final size is known?
