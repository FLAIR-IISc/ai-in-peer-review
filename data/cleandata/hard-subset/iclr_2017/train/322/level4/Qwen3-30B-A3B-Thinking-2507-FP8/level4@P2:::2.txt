

**Review for "Nonparametric Neural Networks"**

**Summary of Contributions**  
This paper introduces a novel framework for automatically determining optimal neural network size during training through continuous unit addition and removal. The authors prove that with fan-in or fan-out regularization, the training error achieves a global minimum at finite network size (Theorem 1), addressing a key limitation of traditional black-box model selection approaches. They develop a novel training algorithm (AdaRad) and normalization layer (CapNorm) to implement this framework, demonstrating promising results on benchmark datasets where their approach outperforms parametric networks of equivalent size and matches exhaustive search results.

**Decision**  
I recommend *acceptance* with minor revisions. The theoretical contribution is significant and well-supported, and the experimental results demonstrate the framework's potential to simplify neural network architecture selection.

**Supporting Arguments**  
The paper makes two substantial contributions: (1) a theoretically sound framework for nonparametric neural networks with a rigorous proof of finite-size convergence, and (2) a practical implementation through AdaRad and CapNorm that achieves competitive performance. The theoretical result (Theorem 1) is particularly valuable as it resolves a fundamental concern about unbounded network growth during optimization. The proof is thorough and well-structured, and the authors provide appropriate experimental validation across multiple datasets.

The experimental results are compelling for two of three datasets (MNIST and rectangles images), where nonparametric networks achieve better accuracy than parametric networks of equivalent size while using fewer parameters. The analysis of the training process (Section 4.2) provides valuable insights into how units are added, learned, and pruned during training. The scalability demonstration on the poker dataset further supports the practical applicability of the approach.

**Additional Feedback for Improvement**  
To strengthen the paper, I recommend the following revisions:

1. **Enhance intuition for Theorem 1**: While the proof is rigorous, the paper would benefit from more intuitive explanation of why finite-size convergence occurs. The connection to sparse regularization (Lasso/Elastic Net) is noted but could be expanded to help readers understand the key insight. A brief discussion of why this result is non-obvious (e.g., contrast with unregularized networks that might grow indefinitely) would be valuable.

2. **Clarify the relationship between final network size and performance**: The paper demonstrates that nonparametric networks sometimes outperform parametric networks of the same final size (e.g., MNIST results in Figure 2). However, it's unclear why this occurs. Does the gradual growth process help the network learn better representations? I recommend adding a brief analysis of this phenomenon.

3. **Expand discussion of lambda's role**: The regularization parameter λ appears to be critical for determining final network structure. The paper could benefit from exploring how λ relates to dataset properties (e.g., complexity, noise level) and whether it can be estimated from data. This would strengthen the practical utility of the framework.

**Clarifying Questions for Authors**  
To help me assess the paper more confidently, I'd appreciate answers to these questions:

1. When adding zero units (which have either fan-in or fan-out as zero vectors), how does the regularizer value change? For example, does L2 regularization change when adding units with zero weights?

2. The definition of zero units requires clarification: "a zero unit is any unit for which either the fan-in or fan-out or both are the zero vector." However, to maintain the same output f(x), both fan-in and fan-out should be zero (otherwise, removing the unit would change the network's behavior). Please clarify this point.

3. How much does the final network structure depend on initialization? Do different random seeds yield substantially different architectures?

4. What happens when combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers? Does the theoretical guarantee still hold?

5. The paper mentions that the nonparametric network "achieves a higher accuracy than networks of the same size that are trained from scratch." Could you elaborate on why this occurs, given that the final network size is known in advance during training?
