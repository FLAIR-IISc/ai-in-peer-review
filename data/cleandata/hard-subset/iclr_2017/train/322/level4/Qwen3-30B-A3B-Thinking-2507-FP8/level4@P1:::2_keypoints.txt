- Paper proposes nonparametric neural networks that auto-determine size in one training run using fan-in/fan-out regularization and a new optimizer (AdaRad), showing promising benchmark results.  
- Recommendation: reject due to mixed empirical results and insufficient theoretical discussion.  
- Theorem 1: proves a finite-dimensional global minimum under fan-in/fan-out regularization; technically sound but lacks intuitive explanation and discussion of novelty.  
- The paper fails to explain how this result is non-trivial or distinct from standard sparse regularization (e.g., Lasso).  
- Empirical results are inconsistent: modest gains on MNIST but substantial underperformance on the convex dataset, with no explanation for the divergence.  
- The paper does not convincingly show that nonparametric networks outperform parametric networks of the same final size; the crucial direct comparison is missing.  
- Figure 2 and related experiments are problematic and need deeper analysis.  
- Recommend expanding the theoretical discussion to clarify significance and novelty of Theorem 1.  
- Recommend improved experiments: direct comparisons and ablation studies between parametric and nonparametric networks of identical final size.  
- Recommend investigating and explaining dataset-specific behavior to establish applicability and limitations.  
- Clarify the definition of "zero units": current definition is ambiguous—both fan-in and fan-out must be zero to avoid changing outputs.  
- Question: Why does the nonparametric approach sometimes find better structures than parametric training from scratch?  
- Question: How should the regularization parameter λ be chosen relative to dataset characteristics, and can it be predicted without extensive search?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to initialization and random seeds, and how does variability affect final architectures and performance?  
- Question: What are the theoretical and empirical effects of using both fan-in and fan-out regularizers together?  
- Question: Why does adding zero units change the regularizer value (e.g., L2 norm), and how does that affect optimization?  
- Overall: interesting and theoretically grounded idea, but empirical evidence and theoretical exposition need substantial strengthening to support practical advantages.
