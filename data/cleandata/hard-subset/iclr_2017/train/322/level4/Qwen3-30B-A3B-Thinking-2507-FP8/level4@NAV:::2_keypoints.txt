- Agreement that the paper’s main contribution—dynamically adding/removing units to adapt model size—is compelling and promising.  
- Key theoretical result: with fan-in or fan-out regularization, the error optimum has a finite parameter count, preventing unbounded growth and overfitting (analogous to Lasso/Elastic Net).  
- Theorem lacks intuitive exposition and is underemphasized; suggest reallocating space from prior-work discussion to deepen theoretical intuition.  
- Missing critical experiment: compare the adaptive model to a fixed-size network with the same final node count to evaluate performance vs. memory and clarify Figure 2.  
- Need explanation for why nonparametric/adaptive nets sometimes outperform fixed architectures trained from scratch.  
- Theorem 1 needs further analysis in plain terms and investigation of dependence on the regularizer λ, initialization variability, and combined fan-in/fan-out regularization.  
- Query why adding zero-weight units changes the regularizer value (e.g., effect on L2 norm).  
- Clarify the definition of “zero units”: reviewer notes that setting only fan-in or fan-out to zero may not preserve the output function, so the definition needs precision.
