- Proposes a novel nonparametric neural architecture that dynamically adapts model size during training.  
- Uses zero-valued units plus sparse regularization to automatically prune irrelevant connections.  
- Demonstrates promising empirical results, especially on non-convex tasks where fixed-size networks struggle.  
- Requires explicit quantification of computational overhead from fan-in decomposition and radial-angular transforms (report FLOPs and runtime).  
- Needs runtime benchmarks comparing training and inference times to standard parametric baselines to assess practicality.  
- Shows smaller networks and degraded performance on convex datasets (e.g., MNIST); this counterintuitive result needs investigation (capacity vs. disrupted inductive biases).  
- Request for analysis/theory/experiments explaining differing behavior on convex vs. non-convex tasks.  
- Clarify the "zero units" mechanism: are units pre-allocated and pruned or dynamically added during training (a schematic would help).  
- Soften the claim that model size is “automatically learned”—size depends on sparsity thresholds rather than being purely learned.  
- Overall: compelling and useful direction with solid non-convex results, but unresolved computational costs and convex-dataset issues limit current practical endorsement.
