

I concur with Reviewer 2 on the paper's intriguing contribution: the dynamic adjustment of network size via unit addition/removal represents a compelling direction. This approach enables models to adapt their architecture to problem-specific data demands rather than relying on user-specified structures.  

The authors present a theoretically significant result demonstrating that fan-in or fan-out regularization ensures the error function's optimum is achieved with a finite number of parameters, preventing indefinite growth and overfitting. While this result is intuitive (reminiscent of sparsity-inducing regularizers like Lasso or Elastic Net), the paper lacks sufficient discussion to elucidate the core insight. The current emphasis on prior work detracts from deeper exploration of this key theorem. To strengthen the contribution, the authors should allocate more space to explaining the theorem's implications—particularly in plain English—and provide additional experimental validation. For instance, could the regularization parameter λ be predicted from data properties? Does λ critically determine the final network structure?  

My specific concerns are:  

1. **Empirical Comparison**: A critical experiment would compare the adaptive method against a fixed-size network *with the same final number of units* (trained from scratch). This would directly test whether the method optimizes performance vs. model complexity (e.g., by avoiding unnecessary units). While Figure 2 suggests mixed results, its clarity is insufficient to assess trends. Crucially, the paper does not explain *why* nonparametric networks sometimes outperform fixed-size counterparts when the latter's structure is known in advance.  

2. **Theorem 1 Interpretation**: The theorem is presented without adequate discussion. Beyond the appendix proofs, the paper fails to articulate:  
   - The key insight in plain English (e.g., "regularization enforces structural sparsity by penalizing redundant connections").  
   - Whether the conclusion is genuinely novel or merely an expected consequence of regularization.  
   - How the final network structure depends on initialization (e.g., do different random seeds yield significantly different architectures?).  
   - Whether combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers preserves the theoretical guarantee.  

**Additional Clarifications Needed**:  
- **Zero Units**: The claim that adding zero units alters the regularizer value (e.g., L2 norm) requires justification. If zero-valued weights are added, the L2 norm should remain unchanged. The definition of "zero units" (requiring *both* fan-in *and* fan-out weights to be zero to preserve output function *f*) must be explicitly clarified.  
- **λ Dependence**: The paper should address whether λ can be data-driven (e.g., via properties of the training set) rather than arbitrarily chosen.  

I have revised my rating to 7, pending the authors' responses to these points. Addressing the theoretical discussion, experimental validation, and definitional ambiguities would substantially strengthen the paper.
