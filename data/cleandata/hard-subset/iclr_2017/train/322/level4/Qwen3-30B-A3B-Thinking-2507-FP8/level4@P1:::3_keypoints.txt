- Introduces a nonparametric framework that grows and prunes units during a single training cycle using L2 penalties and a new optimizer (AdaRad).  
- Theoretical result: with fan-in/out Lp regularization (1 ≤ p < ∞) the network converges to a finite size minimizing training error.  
- Empirical evaluation on MNIST, rectangles, and convex datasets shows comparable or better accuracy than same-size parametric networks while using fewer parameters.  
- Recommendation: reject.  
- Major limitation: lacks comprehensive comparisons to established pruning methods and does not demonstrate superiority in final model size or accuracy over state-of-the-art pruning.  
- Claim that the method avoids needing a high-performing starting model is not empirically supported.  
- Experiments only compare to fixed-size parametric networks and random search, not to pruning-focused baselines.  
- Computational cost concerns: authors acknowledge increased training time but fail to provide sufficient evidence that overhead is justified; training-time comparisons are insufficient, especially on larger problems.  
- Theorem 1 is sound but limited: finite-size global minimum does not guarantee good generalization, and empirical underperformance on the convex dataset raises reliability concerns.  
- Claim of "automatically finding a good network size" is misleading because multiple hyperparameters (αr, αφ, λ, ν, νfreq) still require tuning comparable to standard model selection.  
- Suggested improvements: add comparisons to pruning methods (e.g., Molchanov et al. 2017, Figurnov et al. 2016).  
- Suggested improvements: provide detailed training-time comparisons versus parametric training, particularly on larger datasets.  
- Suggested improvements: include hyperparameter sensitivity analysis to assess robustness and ease of tuning.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify how hyperparameters (especially αr and λ) control final network size.  
- Questions for authors: why choose p=2 over p=1 given potential sparsity/hardware benefits of p=1?  
- Questions for authors: how does training time scale with dataset size compared to standard parametric training?  
- Questions for authors: why does the method perform poorly on the convex dataset and can the framework be modified to address this?  
- Questions for authors: how would this framework integrate into practical ML pipelines and what concrete advantages does it offer over existing size-selection approaches?
