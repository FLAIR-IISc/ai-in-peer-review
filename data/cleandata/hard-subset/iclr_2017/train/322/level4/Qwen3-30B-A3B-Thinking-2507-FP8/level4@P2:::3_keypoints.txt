- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that dynamically add/remove units during training via a regularization scheme.
- Proves that with fan-in or fan-out regularizer (p=2) the optimization attains a global minimum at finite network size.
- Proposes AdaRad, a new training algorithm handling dynamic unit addition/removal while preserving stability.
- Empirical results on MNIST and rectangles show comparable performance with fewer parameters than parametric networks.
- Reviewer recommends rejection.
- Major weakness: lacks meaningful comparison to existing network-size reduction methods (e.g., pruning).
- Major weakness: training time and scalability concerns are not adequately addressed and may make the method impractical for large networks.
- Claim of reduced hyperparameter tuning is misleading—method introduces new hyperparameters (unit addition rate, decay rate, angular step) that require tuning.
- No evidence that the new hyperparameters are easier to tune; experiments required extensive tuning.
- Missing comparisons to pruning work (e.g., Molchanov et al. 2017, Guo et al. 2016); compact networks might be achieved more efficiently via pruning.
- Insufficient training-time analysis: no concrete comparisons to standard parametric training on same datasets/hardware.
- Poker-dataset experiments are promising but suggest substantially slower training due to incremental unit addition, raising scalability concerns.
- Theoretical results are interesting but not well connected to practical benefits; experiments are limited to small datasets.
- Suggested improvements: compare to pruning methods, provide detailed training-time comparisons, relate fan-in p=2 regularizer to existing sparsity methods, and clarify scenarios where this approach is preferable.
- Clarifying questions requested: (1) difficulty of tuning new hyperparameters vs. standard architecture search, (2) comparisons to pruning techniques, (3) plans to mitigate training-time overhead for large-scale applications.
