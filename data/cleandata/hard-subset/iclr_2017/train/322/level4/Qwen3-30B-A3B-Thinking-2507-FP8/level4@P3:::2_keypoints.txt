- Introduces "nonparametric neural networks" that dynamically add/remove units during a single training cycle using L2 fan-in/fan-out regularization.  
- Theorem 1: proves network size converges to a finite optimum under the proposed regularization.  
- Proposes AdaRad, a novel training algorithm to implement the dynamic unit adjustment.  
- Empirical results: competitive on MNIST and rectangles, but underperform on the convex dataset.  
- Decision: Reject — interesting theory but empirical evaluation and theoretical development are insufficient.  
- Theorem's proof seems sound but lacks practical intuition and discussion of significance.  
- Experiments are mixed and do not explain when or why the method succeeds or fails.  
- No direct comparison to parametric networks trained from scratch with the same final size.  
- Paper does not convincingly show consistent advantage over standard network-size selection practices.  
- Recommendation: expand theoretical discussion to clarify practical implications of Theorem 1.  
- Recommendation: include direct comparisons to parametric networks of the same final size and analyze dataset-specific behavior.  
- Recommendation: clarify the unit removal mechanism and how adding zero units affects the regularizer.  
- Recommendation: run more datasets and sensitivity analyses for hyperparameters (e.g., λ).  
- Question: clarify why adding zero weights changes the L2 regularizer if zero weights have zero norm.  
- Question: define "zero units" precisely—must both fan-in and fan-out be zero to preserve the same function?  
- Question: provide comparisons to parametric networks trained from scratch with the same final size.  
- Question: explain what properties of the convex dataset cause failure and how to address them.  
- Question: analyze sensitivity of final network structure to weight initialization and the choice of λ.
