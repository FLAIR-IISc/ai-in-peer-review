- Proposes a novel single-training-cycle framework (nonparametric networks) to auto-determine network size, with CapNorm and AdaRad, claiming competitive benchmark performance.
- Reviewer recommends rejection due to weak theoretical support and major experimental limitations.
- Theorem 1 proof contains critical gaps and restrictive assumptions (requires differentiable nonlinearities, excluding ReLU used in experiments) and hinges on strong error-function conditions.
- Experimental results show substantial underperformance of nonparametric networks versus parametric ones on the convex dataset, weakening the paper’s core claim.
- Improve theory by addressing gaps in Theorem 1 and clarifying practical applicability of assumptions.
- Analyze and explain why the framework fails on the convex dataset, exploring architectural differences between successes and failures.
- Provide a comprehensive comparison of computational complexity and the extra operations required by AdaRad versus standard optimizers.
- Discuss how initialization strategies for newly added units affect convergence and performance.
- Clarify the relationship to existing pruning methods, including whether a pre-trained model is needed as a starting point.
- Question: reconcile the theoretical requirement for differentiable nonlinearities with the reported use of ReLU in experiments.
- Question: what specific properties of the convex dataset cause the nonparametric approach to fail and how can the framework be modified to fix this?
- Question: provide empirical evidence of AdaRad’s computational overhead compared to optimizers like Adam or RMSprop.
