- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that automatically determine optimal network size during a single training process.
- Main contribution: theoretical framework proving existence of a global minimum for network size under specific regularization, plus a practical algorithm (AdaRad) that dynamically adds/removes units.
- Uses a fan-in regularizer with p=2 to induce sparsity, enabling elimination of redundant units while allowing additions.
- Empirical results show competitive or superior performance versus parametric networks with far fewer parameters, especially on MNIST and rectangles; scales to larger datasets (poker).
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; core contribution is theoretically sound and addresses an important problem.
- Strong supporting evidence: rigorous proof of Theorem 1 and well-designed experiments including exhaustive random search baselines.
- Primary concern 1: need detailed analysis of AdaRad’s computational complexity and concrete training-time comparisons to SGD/Adam.
- Primary concern 2: poor and variable performance on the convex dataset needs investigation to determine dataset-specific vs. fundamental limitations.
- Suggestion: provide an intuitive explanation and a simple example showing why the fan-in p=2 regularizer yields the desired behavior.
- Suggestion: more explicitly connect the theoretical framework to AdaRad by mapping algorithmic steps to theoretical requirements.
- Suggestion: discuss hyperparameter sensitivity (especially λ and unit addition rate) and clarify how hyperparameters were chosen.
- Question: provide asymptotic per-iteration complexity of AdaRad vs standard optimizers and empirical speedup/slowdown factors on benchmarks.
- Question: explain why the convex dataset is challenging and whether framework modifications could improve performance there.
- Question: clarify applicability to other nonlinearities (Leaky ReLU, Swish) and implications for the theoretical guarantees.
