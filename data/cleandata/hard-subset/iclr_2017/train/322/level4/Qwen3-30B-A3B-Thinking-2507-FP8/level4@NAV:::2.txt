

I concur with Reviewer 2 regarding the paper's compelling contribution. The concept of dynamically adapting model size by adding or removing units represents a promising direction, enabling architectures to scale precisely according to problem and data requirements rather than user-specified constraints.  

The authors present a notable theoretical finding: under fan-out or fan-in regularization, the error function's optimum is attained with a finite parameter count, thereby preventing unbounded growth and avoiding perfect overfitting. This parallels traditional regularization approaches like Lasso or Elastic Net, which induce weight sparsity. I would have appreciated deeper intuition surrounding this theorem, as its significance seems underemphasized relative to the paper's core contribution. Reducing emphasis on prior work (which is well-considered but secondary) could better accommodate expanded discussion of the theoretical results.  

Additional comments:  
1. A critical experiment would compare your adaptive model against a fixed-size network with the *same final node count* (after learning), demonstrating whether automatic unit adjustment outperforms static architectures in efficiency (e.g., performance vs. memory). While Figure 2 suggests mixed results, the figure lacks clarity. Notably, non-parametric nets occasionally surpass parametric counterparts—why does this adaptive process yield better outcomes than training a fixed-size network from scratch when the final architecture is known? Clarifying this would strengthen the argument.  

2. Theorem 1 warrants deeper exploration beyond its proof. What is the core insight in plain terms? While the conclusion appears intuitive (finite growth under regularization), the paper does not articulate its theoretical significance. How does the final architecture depend on:  
   - The regularizer parameter λ (e.g., can λ be inferred from data properties?)?  
   - Initialization (do different random weights yield substantially different architectures)?  
   - Combined fan-in/fan-out regularization (does the finite-parameter guarantee persist)?  

Additional queries:  
- Why does adding zero-weight units alter the regularizer value? (e.g., does L2 norm change upon adding zero values?)  
- Zero units are defined as having *either* fan-in or fan-out weights zero, but both must be zero to preserve output function *f*; this definition requires clarification.  

I have adjusted my rating to 7, pending the authors' response to these points.
