- Core idea: dynamic growth/shrink of network units based on data is interesting and potentially impactful.  
- Theorem 1: novel theoretical result showing fan-in/fan-out regularization leads to a finite-parameter optimum, preventing unbounded growth.  
- Need clearer exposition of theorem implications and intuition—why the result is non-obvious and how it differs from standard L2/Lasso.  
- Move a plain-English summary of the theorem’s insight into the main text rather than burying it in the appendix.  
- Experimental claims are ambiguous: Figure 2 lacks error bars, metrics, and statistical significance.  
- Unclear why dynamic networks sometimes outperform fixed-size models even when final size is known; this undermines efficiency claims.  
- Recommend clearer visualizations, error bars, ablations showing final size vs fixed baselines, and discussion of failure cases.  
- Definition issue: “zero units” is ambiguous—removal should require both fan-in and fan-out to be zero to preserve network output.  
- Regularizer behavior unclear: must justify how adding zero units changes the fan-in/fan-out regularizer and how this differs from L2.  
- Lambda selection: is λ predictable from data properties or must it be tuned empirically?  
- Initialization sensitivity: how much do random seeds affect the final network structure?  
- Combined regularizers: what happens if both fan-in and fan-out regularizers are applied—does the finite-parameter guarantee still hold?
