- Introduces a nonparametric neural network that grows by randomly adding zero-valued units and uses a sparse regularizer to prune irrelevant weights.  
- Method effectively performs a random search over discrete model configurations, guided by sparsity to eliminate unnecessary units.  
- Addresses an important problem and presents interesting empirical results.  
- Major concern: additional computational complexity from decomposing fan-in weights into parallel/orthogonal components and converting to radial-angular coordinates—needs quantified extra operations versus standard networks.  
- Request for runtime experiments to substantiate computational overhead.  
- Empirical concern: on convex datasets the method yields smaller architectures that underperform compared to parametric networks—requires explanation for this performance gap.
