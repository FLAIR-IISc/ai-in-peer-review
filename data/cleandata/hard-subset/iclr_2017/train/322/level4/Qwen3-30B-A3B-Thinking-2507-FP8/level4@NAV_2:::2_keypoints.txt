- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that grow and prune units during a single training cycle using L2 penalties, proves global training-error minimum at finite dimensionality under fan-in/fan-out regularization, proposes AdaRad optimizer, and shows promising benchmark results.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; theoretical contribution is significant and experiments are well-conducted.  
- Addresses a key limitation of black-box architecture search; Theorem 1 rigorously prevents unbounded growth; strong empirical gains on MNIST and rectangles, weaker on convex dataset but not fatal.  
- Suggests adding practical discussion of Theorem 1’s intuition, how regularization parameter λ relates to dataset characteristics, and why nonparametric training can outperform parametric equivalents.  
- Question 1: Explain the mechanism by which nonparametric training sometimes outperforms parametric networks of the same final size.  
- Question 2: How sensitive is the final network structure to λ, and can λ be predicted from dataset characteristics or must it be tuned?  
- Question 3: How dependent is the final architecture on initialization and random seed variability?  
- Question 4: What happens if fan-in and fan-out regularizers are combined—does Theorem 1’s guarantee still hold?  
- Question 5: Clarify the definition and removal of “zero units”: must both fan-in and fan-out be zero to leave the output unchanged?
