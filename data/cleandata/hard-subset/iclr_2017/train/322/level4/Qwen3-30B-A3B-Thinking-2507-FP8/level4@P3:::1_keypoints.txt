- Proposes a framework for automatically determining neural network size during a single training run by adding/removing units (nonparametric neural networks).
- Introduces the AdaRad optimization algorithm and a specific regularization strategy to enable unit growth/pruning.
- Strong theoretical contribution: Theorem 1 shows the framework can reach a global training-error minimum under certain regularization conditions.
- Empirical results are promising on MNIST and rectangles images, achieving competitive accuracy with substantially fewer parameters.
- Poor and unexplained performance on the convex dataset, raising concerns about generalizability.
- Major omission: no quantitative discussion of computational complexity or timing for AdaRad—potentially substantial overhead from radial-angular decomposition and coordinate transforms.
- Recommendation: weak accept—valued theoretical results but empirical evaluation and complexity analysis are insufficient.
- Requested improvements: provide detailed AdaRad computational-cost analysis and timing comparisons to SGD/Adam.
- Requested improvements: analyze why the method fails on the convex dataset (final structures, error distributions, comparisons to pruning methods).
- Requested improvements: broaden empirical evaluation to more challenging datasets and architectures.
- Requested improvements: clarify relationship to prior pruning/growth work and the “f-equivalence” concept.
- Questions to authors: quantify AdaRad’s per-iteration cost and scaling; explain convex dataset discrepancy and possible fixes; why RMSprop and Adam showed no significant performance difference.
