- Agrees with Reviewer 2: dynamic addition/removal of units is a promising approach for adapting network size to data rather than user-specified architectures.  
- Key theoretical claim: fan-in or fan-out regularization ensures a finite-parameter optimum, preventing unbounded growth and overfitting.  
- The theorem is intuitive (similar to Lasso/Elastic Net) but the paper lacks sufficient plain-English explanation and exploration of the core insight.  
- Recommend reallocating space from related work to a deeper discussion of Theorem 1 and its implications.  
- Request additional experiments to validate theory, e.g., whether the regularization parameter λ can be predicted from data and how λ affects final architecture.  
- Empirical comparison needed: compare adaptive method to a fixed-size network trained from scratch with the same final number of units.  
- Figure 2 is unclear and insufficient to assess trends; paper does not explain why nonparametric models sometimes outperform known-structure fixed networks.  
- Theorem 1 needs clearer interpretation: state the key insight in plain English and clarify whether the result is novel or expected.  
- Investigate sensitivity to initialization: do different random seeds produce significantly different final architectures?  
- Clarify whether combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers preserves the theoretical guarantee.  
- Define "zero units" precisely and justify the claim that adding zero units changes the regularizer value (e.g., reconcile with L2 norm behaviour).  
- Address whether λ can be chosen/data-driven (based on training-set properties) rather than arbitrarily selected.  
- Revised reviewer score: 7, contingent on addressing theoretical discussion, experimental validation, and definitional ambiguities.
