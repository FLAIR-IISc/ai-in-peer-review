- Proposes a method to dynamically add/remove neural units during training by zeroing input/output weights and using a group sparsity norm to encourage weight decay.
- Theoretical result: with appropriate regularization, the loss is minimized by a network with a finite number of units.
- Theory does not guarantee against over- or under-fitting, though initial experiments suggest these issues may be avoided.
- Potential benefit: reduces explicit hyperparameter tuning for network size.
- Limitation: still requires implicit hyperparameters (e.g., unit addition frequency, weight decay rate) and unclear whether these are easier to tune than standard hyperparameters.
- Shows comparable performance to parametric networks using fewer units, which could reduce runtime, power, and memory.
- Missing baseline comparisons to established model-compression techniques (e.g., pruning), so competitiveness is unclear.
- Same hyperparameters control final network size and training time, potentially causing slower training and unclear scalability to large networks.
- Experiments are encouraging but not comprehensive; larger-scale testing is needed.
- Well-written and inspiring vision for adaptive networks, but current results are still speculative.
