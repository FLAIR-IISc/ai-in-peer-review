- Proposes a nonparametric neural network that dynamically learns its optimal size during training.  
- Core innovation: randomly introduce zero-valued units plus sparse regularization to automatically prune irrelevant connections.  
- Explores discrete model-size configurations via random initialization combined with sparsity constraints.  
- Empirical results show strong performance on non-convex datasets, promising adaptive model scaling.  
- Technical contribution is well-motivated and experimental validation is generally sound.  
- Major concern: computational overhead from decomposing fan-in vectors and radial-angular transforms needs quantification.  
- Authors should report additional FLOPs per layer and runtime benchmarks (training time/epoch and total wall-clock) vs baselines on standard datasets.  
- Without computational analysis, unclear whether model-size adaptation justifies increased cost for resource-constrained use.  
- Second concern: method yields smaller architectures and underperforms on convex datasets, requiring deeper investigation.  
- Need to determine whether sparsity favors minimal architectures due to task simplicity or biases from random initialization/regularization.  
- Clarify theoretical boundaries: whether automatic pruning can fail when convex tasks require specific model capacity and if this is algorithmic or constraint-driven.  
- Addressing computational and task-specific limitations is essential to strengthen practical impact despite the method’s novelty and reduced need for manual depth tuning.
