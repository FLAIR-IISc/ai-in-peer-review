

This paper introduces a nonparametric neural network model that automatically learns its size during training. The core approach involves randomly adding zero-valued units and employing a sparse regularizer to automatically eliminate irrelevant weights. The method resembles a random search over discrete model configurations, facilitated by sparse regularization to prune unnecessary units. This addresses an important problem, and the results presented are interesting. My main concerns are as follows:  
What is the additional computational complexity of the algorithm? The decomposition of each fan-in weight into a parallel and orthogonal component, along with the transformation into radial-angular coordinates, likely incurs significant computational overhead. The authors should quantify the extra operations relative to standard parametric networks and include runtime experiments to substantiate this.  
The results indicate that nonparametric networks produce smaller architectures on convex datasets, where they underperform compared to parametric networks. What explains this performance gap?
