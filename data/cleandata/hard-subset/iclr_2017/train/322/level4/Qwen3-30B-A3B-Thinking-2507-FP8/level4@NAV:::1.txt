

This paper introduces a nonparametric neural network architecture that autonomously determines model size during training. The core approach involves randomly introducing zero units and employing a sparse regularizer to automatically prune irrelevant weights. The method appears to function as a discrete-space random search, leveraging sparse regularization to eliminate redundant units. This addresses an important problem, and the results presented are compelling. My primary concerns are as follows:  
What is the additional computational complexity of the proposed algorithm? The decomposition of each fan-in weight into parallel and orthogonal components, alongside the transformation into radial-angular coordinates, may impose substantial computational overhead. The authors should quantify the incremental computational cost relative to standard parametric networks and include empirical runtime comparisons.  
Additionally, the nonparametric networks yield significantly smaller architectures on convex datasets, resulting in inferior performance compared to parametric counterparts. Could the authors provide insight into this observed behavior?
