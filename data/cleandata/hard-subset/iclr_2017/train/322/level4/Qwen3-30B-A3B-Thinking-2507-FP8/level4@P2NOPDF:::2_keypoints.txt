- Proposes dynamic neural architectures that add/remove units during training to match problem complexity.  
- Theorem 1: fan-in/fan-out regularization guarantees convergence to an optimum with a finite number of parameters, preventing unbounded growth (analogy to Lasso).  
- Prior-work discussion should be condensed to make room for a clearer, intuitive exposition of the theorem.  
- Paper should explicitly state the practical insight: regularization controls network size, making architecture selection endogenous to learning.  
- Experiments (Figure 2) are ambiguous and need clearer presentation (error bars, statistical significance).  
- Results are inconsistent vs fixed-size networks; paper does not explain why dynamic models sometimes outperform same-final-size fixed models.  
- Must include a controlled comparison: dynamically grown network vs fixed-size network of identical final capacity trained from scratch.  
- Expand Theorem 1 discussion with plain-English interpretation and emphasize that λ directly controls final architecture size.  
- Explore practical implications: sensitivity of final architecture to λ and whether λ can be predicted from data properties (noise, feature correlation).  
- Clarify "zero unit" definition—whether removal requires both fan-in and fan-out to be zero to avoid changing the output.  
- Report initialization dependence: how variable are final architectures across random restarts and implications for reproducibility.  
- Explain behavior with combined fan-in and fan-out regularizers and whether Theorem 1 still applies or needs modification.  
- Address whether adding zero units changes the regularizer value and whether units are explicitly zeroed or merely pruned.  
- Overall: promising idea but requires expanded theory discussion and stronger experiments; reviewer keeps rating 7 (Accept) pending satisfactory revisions.
