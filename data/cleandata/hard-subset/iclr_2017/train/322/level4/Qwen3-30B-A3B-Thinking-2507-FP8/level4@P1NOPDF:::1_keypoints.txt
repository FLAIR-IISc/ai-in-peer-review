- Paper proposes a nonparametric neural architecture that dynamically grows/shrinks by stochastically adding zero-weight units and using sparsity to prune connections.  
- Results are promising but the method may effectively be a random search over discrete architectures guided by sparsity.  
- Major concern: unclear computational overhead from decomposing fan-in weights and converting to radial–angular coordinates, which likely adds substantial cost.  
- Authors should quantify extra operations per layer/iteration and provide empirical runtime comparisons versus standard parametric networks.  
- Observed issue: the nonparametric model produces much smaller architectures on convex datasets and underperforms compared to parametric models.  
- Request deeper analysis of why the method yields overly compact structures—e.g., whether sparsity mistakes signal for noise or random pruning conflicts with the data’s geometric properties—and investigation of applicability limits.
