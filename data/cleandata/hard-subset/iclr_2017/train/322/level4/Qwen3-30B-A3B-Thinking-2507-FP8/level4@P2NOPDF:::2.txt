

The paper presents an intriguing approach to dynamic neural network architecture, where units are added or removed during training to adapt to problem complexity. I concur with Reviewer 2 that this direction is compelling, as it enables models to self-structure based on data requirements rather than fixed user-defined constraints. The theoretical contribution (Theorem 1) is particularly noteworthy: it rigorously demonstrates that fan-in or fan-out regularization ensures the error function converges to an optimum with a finite number of parameters, preventing unbounded growth and overfitting. This parallels sparse regularization techniques like Lasso but merits deeper exploration in the main text. The current discussion of prior work could be condensed to allocate space for a clearer, more intuitive explanation of the theorem’s implications—e.g., why finite convergence is non-trivial despite seeming intuitive. The paper should explicitly state the theorem’s practical insight: *regularization actively controls network size by penalizing redundant connections, making architecture selection endogenous to the learning process.*

Regarding experiments, Figure 2’s results are ambiguous and require clearer presentation (e.g., error bars, statistical significance). While the figure suggests inconsistent performance gains over fixed-size networks (sometimes better, sometimes worse), the core value of the method lies in its ability to *discover* structure. However, the paper does not sufficiently address *why* the dynamic approach occasionally outperforms a fixed-size network trained from scratch *with the same final size*. This is critical for establishing the method’s efficiency—does it achieve superior performance with fewer parameters, or merely adapt to the optimal structure? A direct comparison between a dynamically grown network and a fixed-size network of identical *final* capacity (trained from scratch) would strengthen this claim. Without such an experiment, the advantage remains unproven.

Theorem 1’s discussion requires significant expansion. Beyond the proof (in the appendix), the main text should articulate:  
- **Plain-English interpretation**: *“The regularization term effectively shrinks the network by pruning unnecessary connections, ensuring the model stops growing once it captures all relevant data patterns.”*  
- **Key insight**: The theorem shows architecture size is not a hyperparameter but a *learned outcome* of the regularization, with λ directly controlling final structure.  
- **Practical implications**: How sensitive is the architecture to λ? Can λ be predicted from data properties (e.g., noise level, feature correlation)? The paper should explore this, as λ appears pivotal to the method’s success.  

Additional clarifications are needed:  
1. **Zero units**: The definition states a unit is “zero” if *either* fan-in or fan-out weights are zero. However, this would alter the network output unless *both* are zero. The paper must clarify whether both weights must vanish for the unit to be removable without affecting the output function.  
2. **Initialization dependence**: How much does the final architecture vary with different random initializations? If results are initialization-sensitive, this impacts reproducibility.  
3. **Combined regularizers**: What happens when fan-in and fan-out regularizers are used together? Does Theorem 1 still hold, or does it require modification?  

The question of whether adding zero units changes the regularizer value (e.g., L2 norm) also needs addressing. If zero weights are added, the L2 norm should remain unchanged *only if the weights are truly zero*. The paper should specify whether zero units are explicitly zeroed or merely pruned (which would not alter the norm).  

In summary, the paper’s core idea is promising, but its theoretical contribution and experimental validation require substantial refinement. The authors should:  
- Expand Theorem 1’s discussion with plain-English insights and practical implications.  
- Conduct a controlled experiment comparing dynamic networks to fixed-size networks of *identical final capacity* (trained from scratch).  
- Clarify the definition and impact of “zero units.”  
- Address λ predictability, initialization sensitivity, and combined regularizers.  

These revisions would transform a technically interesting but underdeveloped contribution into a compelling advance. I maintain my rating of 7 (Accept) pending satisfactory responses to these points.
