This paper introduces a novel nonparametric neural network model capable of adaptively determining its own size during training. The core concept involves the stochastic addition of zero-valued units coupled with sparse regularization to effectively prune irrelevant weights. While conceptually resembling a random search within a discrete space, guided by sparse regularization for unit elimination, this work addresses a significant problem and presents compelling results. 

However, the computational overhead of the proposed algorithm warrants further investigation. Specifically, the decomposition of fan-in weights into parallel and orthogonal components, followed by transformation to radial-angular coordinates, potentially introduces substantial computational cost. A discussion of the algorithm’s operational complexity relative to standard parametric neural networks is needed, ideally supported by running time experiments. 

Additionally, the observation that the nonparametric network yields smaller, and thus less performant, networks on convex datasets raises a question regarding the model’s behavior in such scenarios. Providing insight into this phenomenon would strengthen the paper.
