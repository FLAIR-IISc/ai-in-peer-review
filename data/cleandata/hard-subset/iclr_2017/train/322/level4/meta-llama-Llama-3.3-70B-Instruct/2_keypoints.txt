- Main theoretical result: with fan-in or fan-out regularization, the optimal error is achieved with a finite number of parameters, preventing unbounded growth and overfitting.  
- Theorem resembles Lasso/Elastic Net sparsity effects, but needs more intuitive explanation and deeper analysis.  
- Request to reallocate space toward dissecting theoretical results (Theorem 1) rather than prior work.  
- Suggestion for a compelling experiment: show a dynamic (non-parametric) model outperforming a fixed-size network to demonstrate efficiency in performance and memory.  
- Current experiments (Figure 2) are mixed and unclear; need clearer interpretation of when non-parametric helps or hurts.  
- Question why non-parametric sometimes outperforms parametric even when the final network size is known in advance — needs further insight.  
- Ask for a plain-English distillation of Theorem 1’s core insight and discussion of any deeper implications beyond intuition and proofs.
