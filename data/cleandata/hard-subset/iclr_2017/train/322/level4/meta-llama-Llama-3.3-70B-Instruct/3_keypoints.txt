- Proposes a novel, simple method to dynamically add/remove units during training using zero-input/output weights and group sparsity regularization.
- Main theoretical result: appropriate regularization yields a loss-minimizing network with a finite number of units.
- The theoretical guarantee does not ensure optimal training performance; experiments suggest over-/under-fitting may not occur but are preliminary.
- Promises reduced need for manual unit-count tuning as networks can adaptively determine size.
- Does not fully eliminate hyperparameter tuning—implicit hyperparameters (unit-addition frequency, weight decay rate) still control unit emergence.
- Unclear whether tuning these implicit hyperparameters is easier or harder than conventional tuning; authors do not claim simplification, which is disappointing.
- Demonstrates training smaller networks with comparable performance, benefiting runtime, power, and memory—important for mobile deployment.
- Lacks experimental comparison to established model compression methods (e.g., pruning), so competitiveness is uncertain.
- Same hyperparameters affect both model size and training time, potentially causing slower training than fixed-parametric approaches.
- Authors do not address scalability to larger networks; slow training may limit practicality for large-scale problems.
- Experiments are encouraging but limited; more comprehensive, larger-scale evaluations are needed to demonstrate practicality.
- Paper is well-written and interesting with an inspiring lifelong-learning vision, but current results are speculative and require further investigation.
