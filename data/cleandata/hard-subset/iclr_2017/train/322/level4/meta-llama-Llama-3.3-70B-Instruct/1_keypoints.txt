- Proposes a novel nonparametric neural network that dynamically determines model size via random zero units and sparse regularization to remove irrelevant weights.  
- Method can be interpreted as a random search over a discrete architecture space guided by sparsity.  
- Problem is significant and the paper reports intriguing empirical results.  
- Computational complexity is unclear: decomposing fan-in into parallel/orthogonal components and converting to radial-angular coordinates may add substantial overhead and needs analysis and comparison to parametric networks, plus runtime measurements.  
- Nonparametric models tend to produce smaller networks on convex datasets, sometimes performing worse than parametric networks, and the causes of this behavior require deeper investigation.
