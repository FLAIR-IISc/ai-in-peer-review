- Agrees with reviewer 2: core idea of dynamically adjusting network size during training is interesting and avoids user-defined priors.  
- Theoretical result: with fan-in or fan-out regularization, the optimal error uses a finite number of parameters, preventing unbounded growth and overfitting.  
- The theorem is reminiscent of sparsity methods (Lasso/Elastic Net) but needs more intuitive explanation and discussion of implications.  
- Recommend reducing prior-work discussion to allow deeper exploration and intuition for the theorem.  
- Request experiment comparing dynamic network to a conventionally trained fixed-size network matched to the dynamic network’s final size to demonstrate efficiency gains.  
- Figure 2 is hard to interpret; results are mixed and need clarification on when/why nonparametric networks outperform or underperform matched parametric networks.  
- Ask for clarification of why nonparametric approach sometimes surpasses a parametrically-trained network of the same final size.  
- Theorem discussion feels incomplete: ask for an accessible statement of the central insight and whether there are subtle implications worth highlighting.  
- Suggest experiments to validate the theorem: can optimal regularization parameter (lambda) be predicted from data, and does any data property guide lambda selection?  
- Ask about sensitivity: how dependent is final network structure on random weight initialization, and what if fan-in and fan-out regularizers are combined—does the theorem still apply?  
- Specific clarification requested: explain how adding zero units changes the regularizer value (e.g., does L2 change when adding zero-valued weights?).  
- Clarify definition of a “zero unit”: should require both fan-in and fan-out to be zero to permit removal without changing network output.
