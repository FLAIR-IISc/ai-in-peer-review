This paper introduces a nonparametric neural network model capable of automatically determining its size during training. The core concept involves the random addition of zero-valued units coupled with sparse regularization to effectively prune irrelevant weights. While conceptually resembling a random search within a discrete space, guided by sparse regularization for unit elimination, the work addresses a significant problem and presents compelling results. My primary concerns are outlined below:

What is the computational overhead introduced by this algorithm? The decomposition of fan-in weights into parallel and orthogonal components, followed by the transformation to radial-angular coordinates, potentially adds substantial computational cost. The authors should quantify this additional complexity in terms of operations relative to standard parametric neural networks and ideally include running time experiments to demonstrate practical performance.

The results indicate a tendency for the nonparametric network to converge to smaller architectures on convex datasets, leading to performance inferior to parametric counterparts. Can the authors provide any explanation or insight into this observation?
