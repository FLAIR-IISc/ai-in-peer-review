- Decision: Reject, with strong encouragement to revise and resubmit.
- Contribution: Proposes dynamically growing/shrinking nonparametric neural networks with a theoretical guarantee of a finite optimal size under certain regularization.
- Main criticism: Paper does not clearly demonstrate advantages over training comparable parametric networks or standard hyperparameter search.
- Empirical results: Mixed — nonparametric networks sometimes outperform and sometimes underperform parametric baselines.
- Theorem 1: Important but insufficiently explained; practical implications for training and hyperparameter choice are unclear.
- Need for examples: Paper should include illustrative examples showing practical consequences of Theorem 1.
- Regularization discussion: Lacks analysis on how λ affects final network size and whether optimal λ is data-dependent or estimable.
- Experimental comparisons: Requires direct comparison to parametric networks trained from scratch with the same final node count.
- Dataset scope: Positive poker results are promising but more analysis and larger-scale experiments are needed.
- Motivation clarity: Must better articulate scenarios where the method is most beneficial (e.g., limited prior on network size).
- Initialization sensitivity: Should analyze effect of different random initializations on final architecture and performance variance.
- Combined regularizers: Recommend exploring fan-in and fan-out regularizers jointly and reporting their interactions.
- Definition clarity: Define "zero unit" explicitly (both fan-in and fan-out weights zero) and implications for removability.
- Regularizer behavior: Explain why adding zero units changes the regularizer and clarify L2 norm behavior with added zeros.
- AdaRad-M: Include comparative performance analysis between AdaRad and AdaRad-M to justify the momentum variant.
- Theoretical connections: Discuss links to Lasso/Elastic Net and highlight unique theoretical contributions.
- Computational cost: Provide a detailed analysis of AdaRad’s computational cost versus other optimization methods.
- Questions for authors: Request an intuitive explanation of Theorem 1, clear statement of advantages over parametric approaches, evidence of correlation between data properties and optimal λ, and sensitivity statistics across initializations.
