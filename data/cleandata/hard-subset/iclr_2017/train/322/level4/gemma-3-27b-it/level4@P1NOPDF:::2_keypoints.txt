- Paper is interesting: dynamic addition/removal of units lets model grow/shrink per data needs rather than fixed user-specified architecture.
- Theoretical result: under fan-in or fan-out regularization, the error optimum is achievable with a finite number of parameters, preventing unbounded network growth and overfitting.
- Approach is reminiscent of Lasso/Elastic Net where regularization induces sparsity.
- Request more intuition and discussion of the theorem—its implications feel obvious but deserve deeper exposition and space in the paper.
- Suggest reducing prior-work discussion to allow more thorough examination of the core theoretical result.
- Experiment suggestion: compare the adaptive model to a fixed-architecture network with the same final number of nodes to show efficiency gains.
- Figure 2 results are mixed and unclear; need clearer presentation and interpretation.
- Ask why the non-parametric model sometimes outperforms a network trained from scratch with a known architecture—request insight into this phenomenon.
- Ask for plain-language explanation of Theorem 1 and whether there are deeper, non-obvious implications beyond the proof.
- Suggest experiments/analysis on whether lambda (regularization strength) can be predicted from data and whether it determines the final network structure.
- Ask about sensitivity to random initialization: do different initial weights lead to significantly different final networks?
- Question whether the theoretical guarantee still holds when combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers.
- Clarify why adding zero units is said to change the regularizer value—point out that adding zero-valued weights does not change L2 norm.
- Clarify definition of "zero units": both fan-in and fan-out weights must be zero to remove the unit without changing the network output.
- Reviewer adjusted rating to 7 pending authors’ responses to these points.
