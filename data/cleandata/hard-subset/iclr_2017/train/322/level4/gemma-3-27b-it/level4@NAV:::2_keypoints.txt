- Suggests the paper needs a more detailed, intuitive explanation of the theorem’s core insight, even if it reduces some prior-work discussion.  
- Recommends an experiment comparing dynamic (non-parametric) models to fixed architectures of the same final size to directly measure efficacy, pruning behavior, and efficiency.  
- Notes Figure 2 is unclear and that the mixed results (non-parametric sometimes better) require explanation for why structure discovery yields occasional performance gains.  
- Requests expanded discussion of Theorem 1’s central takeaway in accessible terms and whether there are deeper implications beyond the formal proof.

- Asks about sensitivity of final network structure to weight initialization and whether different initializations produce significantly different architectures.

- Raises a technical question: does adding zero-valued units alter the regularizer value (noting L2 would not change)?  
- Seeks clarification of the “zero unit” definition, pointing out that removing a unit without affecting function f requires both fan-in and fan-out weights to be zero.
