- Decision: Reject — interesting idea and theory but practical benefits and scalability are unconvincing, with increased training time.

- Core idea: Dynamically adjust network size during training by adding/removing units, controlled by an L2 penalty.

- Theoretical contribution: Proof that with this regularization a finite-sized network attains a global minimum of training error.

- Algorithm: AdaRad proposed to handle dynamic size and stabilize training.

- Experimental claim: Initial results are promising and show competitive performance with potentially smaller networks.

- Hyperparameter issue: Framework still requires tuning (addition rate, shrinkage) and authors do not show this is easier than tuning parametric size.

- Missing comparisons: No evaluation against state-of-the-art network pruning techniques that aim to reduce size post-training.

- Scalability concern: Increased training time for dynamic sizing not adequately analyzed for larger datasets/architectures; RMSprop comparison insufficient.

- Negative result: Nonparametric network performs significantly worse on the convex dataset without sufficient analysis.

- Ablation finding: AdaRad vs AdaRad-M show negligible differences despite added algorithmic complexity.

- Suggested experiment: Quantify hyperparameter tuning effort (number of trials, time) for nonparametric vs parametric approaches.

- Suggested experiment: Direct comparison to pruning methods to assess size reduction and performance trade-offs.

- Suggested analysis: Provide detailed computational cost/scalability analysis and methods to mitigate training time increases.

- Suggested investigation: Diagnose the poor convex-dataset performance — framework limitation vs sensitivity to initialization/hyperparams.

- Suggested ablations: More thoroughly test AdaRad components (e.g., radial vs angular shifts).

- Suggested improvement: Explore adaptive learning-rate annealing strategies that incorporate dynamic network-size information.

- Question: How does training computational cost scale with dataset size and desired network complexity?

- Question: Can authors provide quantitative comparison of hyperparameter tuning effort for nonparametric vs parametric networks?

- Question: Has the framework been tested on more complex architectures (e.g., CNNs), and what were the outcomes?

- Question: Why does the method fail on the convex dataset and what modifications could address it?

- Question: What specific criteria govern adding/removing units, and are there safeguards to prevent oscillation in network size?
