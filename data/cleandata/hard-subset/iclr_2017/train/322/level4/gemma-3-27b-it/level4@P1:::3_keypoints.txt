- Paper proposes automatically determining network size during training by adding/removing units with zero-valued input/output weights and using a group sparsity norm as regularizer.  
- Main theoretical result: with appropriate regularization, the loss is minimized by a network with a finite number of units (no guarantee on generalization).  
- Initial experiments suggest no obvious overfitting or underfitting, but results are preliminary.  
- Recommendation: reject due to lack of compelling evidence that the method outperforms existing approaches and concerns about scalability.  
- The approach does not eliminate hyperparameter tuning—parameters controlling unit addition frequency and weight decay still govern final size.  
- Unclear whether tuning these new hyperparameters is easier than tuning standard parametric model hyperparameters.  
- No comparison to state-of-the-art network compression/pruning methods, so competitiveness is uncertain.  
- Hyperparameters that control network size are coupled to training time, potentially causing significantly slower training than fixed-size parametric approaches.  
- Authors do not adequately address how the method scales to larger networks or real-world large-scale problems.  
- Experiments are helpful but not comprehensive; need results on significantly larger and more complex datasets to demonstrate practicality.  
- Paper is interesting and well-written with promising long-term vision (networks that grow/adapt for lifelong learning), but current results are speculative.  
- Question: provide detailed computational cost analysis comparing training time to standard parametric networks of comparable performance.  
- Question: compare method performance to state-of-the-art pruning techniques (magnitude pruning and advanced methods).  
- Question: elaborate on strategies to automatically tune angular and radial step sizes or provide guidance for selecting them across datasets.  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to initial network size and are there guidelines for choosing a starting point?  
- Question: have other regularization schemes been explored beyond the p-norm, and how do they affect network size and performance?
