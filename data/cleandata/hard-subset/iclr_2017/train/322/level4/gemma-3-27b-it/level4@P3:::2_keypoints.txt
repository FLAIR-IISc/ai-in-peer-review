- Proposes automatically adapting neural network size during training by dynamically adding/removing units via regularization; proves a finite optimum under fan-in or fan-out regularization.
- Decision: Reject — interesting ideas and solid theory but empirical results are mixed and the paper feels incomplete.
- Main weakness: Experimental validation is unconvincing — Figure 2 is hard to interpret and results are inconsistent across datasets.
- Unclear why non-parametric networks sometimes outperform parametric ones; the claim that structure is “discovered” needs stronger support.
- Theorem 1 is stated but underexplained; needs more intuitive discussion of significance, limitations, and practical implications (e.g., relation of λ to data).
- Paper spends disproportionate space on related work versus deeper treatment of key theoretical results.
- Suggestion: Re-present experimental plots (separate per dataset), highlight differences clearly, and add statistical significance testing.
- Suggestion: Expand and clarify the discussion of Theorem 1, including practical guidance and exploration of λ estimation from data.
- Suggestion: Test sensitivity to weight initialization to see if final structures vary significantly across runs.
- Suggestion: Study combined fan-in and fan-out regularization to assess performance, stability, and whether theoretical guarantees hold.
- Clarify definition of a “zero unit” — removal requires both fan-in and fan-out to be zero to avoid changing network output.
- Explain why adding zero units affects the regularizer value, especially under L2 penalties.
- Suggestion: Run an ablation comparing non-parametric networks to parametric networks of the same final size trained from scratch to isolate benefits of dynamic training.
- Questions for authors: Why do non-parametric networks sometimes outperform parametric ones (dynamic adaptation, exploration, or other)?
- Questions for authors: Can you provide an intuitive explanation and key insight of Theorem 1 beyond existence of a finite optimum?
- Questions for authors: How much does final network structure depend on random initialization; have you quantified variability across initializations?
- Questions for authors: Why does adding zero units change the regularizer value in the L2 context — please clarify?
- Questions for authors: What challenges and computational optimizations are expected when scaling this approach to large datasets and complex architectures?
