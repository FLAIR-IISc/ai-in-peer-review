- Introduces a nonparametric neural network that auto-determines size by randomly adding zero-valued units and using a sparse regularizer to prune irrelevant weights.  
- Method resembles a random search over a discrete model space guided by sparse regularization.  
- Tackles the important problem of model size selection and reports promising results.

- Requests quantification of increased computational operations versus a parametric network and inclusion of running-time experiments.  
- Observes that on convex datasets the method collapses to a smaller network and performs worse than parametric networks; asks for an explanation.
