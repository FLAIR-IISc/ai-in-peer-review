- Proposes a simple, elegant method to dynamically add/remove network units during training using group sparsity to encourage zeroed units.
- Theoretical contribution: shows that with appropriate regularization the loss is minimized by a network with a finite number of units.
- Theory does not guarantee optimal generalization—networks may still over- or under-fit, though initial experiments suggest this is not severe.
- Claims reduced hyperparameter tuning are not fully realized: method still depends on hyperparameters (unit addition frequency, weight decay) that influence final size.
- Authors appropriately avoid overclaiming, but the lack of demonstrated simplification in tuning is disappointing.
- Advantage: can train smaller networks with comparable performance, benefiting inference time, power, and memory (important for mobile).
- Missing direct experimental comparison to standard compression/pruning methods, so competitiveness is unclear.
- Potential drawback: hyperparameters that control size are coupled to training duration, possibly causing substantially slower training and unclear scalability to larger networks.
- Experiments are encouraging but limited; evidence on significantly larger problems is needed to establish practicality.
- Overall assessment: interesting, clearly written, potentially valuable and inspiring vision, but current results remain largely speculative.
