This paper presents an interesting approach to neural network design by allowing for dynamic growth and shrinkage of the network based on data requirements, rather than relying on user-defined priors. I concur with Reviewer 2 regarding the novelty of this direction. The authors provide a theoretical result demonstrating that, under fan-in or fan-out regularization, the optimal solution is achievable with a finite number of parameters, preventing indefinite network growth and potential overfitting. This result is reminiscent of sparsity-inducing regularization techniques like Lasso and Elastic Net, and offers a compelling justification for the proposed method.

However, while the theorem is intriguing, its presentation lacks sufficient intuition. The paper would significantly benefit from a more accessible discussion of the key insights behind Theorem 1. The current presentation feels somewhat detached, with the bulk of the explanation residing in the Appendix. Expanding on the ‘plain English’ interpretation of the theorem—what it fundamentally *says* about the learning process—would greatly enhance its impact. The authors could achieve this by reallocating space from the extensive, though well-executed, related work section. 

I have several specific comments and suggestions:

1. **Comparative Performance:** A compelling experiment would directly compare the performance of the proposed dynamic network architecture with a conventionally trained network of the *same final size*. This would isolate the benefit of adaptive structure and demonstrate whether the method truly optimizes for efficiency – saving unnecessary nodes and allocating resources to more impactful ones. Does this approach improve performance versus memory usage? While Figure 2 attempts to address this, its presentation is unclear and requires careful inspection to interpret. The mixed results observed are interesting; even when non-parametric nets perform worse, the ability to *discover* structure remains valuable. However, a deeper investigation is needed to explain why the non-parametric approach sometimes outperforms a network trained from scratch with a known, fixed architecture. What underlying mechanism allows for this improvement?

2. **Theorem 1 Discussion:** The current discussion of Theorem 1 feels incomplete. Beyond the mathematical proof, what is the core takeaway? The conclusion appears almost intuitive; is there a more profound insight that the authors can articulate? Furthermore, exploring the relationship between the regularization parameter (λ) and the data itself would be beneficial. Can λ be predicted based on data properties, effectively automating the structure determination process? My intuition suggests λ is a crucial determinant of the final network structure, and this hypothesis warrants further investigation. Similarly, it would be valuable to understand the impact of initialization on the resulting network architecture. Do different random initializations lead to significantly different final networks? If so, how substantial are these differences? Finally, what happens when fan-in and fan-out regularization are combined? Does the theoretical guarantee still hold?

I also have a few clarifying questions:

1. The authors state that adding zero units changes the regularizer value. This claim requires clarification. For example, adding zero values does not alter the L2 norm. Could you please elaborate on this point?

2. The definition of a zero unit—having either fan-in or fan-out weights equal to zero—is potentially misleading. To truly remove a unit without affecting the function *f*, both fan-in and fan-out weights should be zero. This point needs clarification.

Overall, this paper presents a promising direction for neural network design. The theoretical result is a valuable contribution, and the idea of dynamically adapting network structure based on data is inherently appealing. However, the paper would be significantly strengthened by a more intuitive explanation of the theorem, a more rigorous comparison to fixed-size networks, and a deeper exploration of the factors influencing network structure. 

I have adjusted my rating to a 7, with the expectation that the authors will address these comments. I believe that addressing these points will substantially improve the clarity, impact, and overall quality of the paper.
