This paper introduces a nonparametric neural network model capable of automatically determining its size during training. The core concept involves randomly adding zero-valued units and employing a sparse regularizer to effectively prune irrelevant weights. This approach resembles a random search over a discrete model space, guided by sparse regularization to identify and eliminate redundant units. Addressing the problem of model size selection is important, and the paper presents promising results. My primary concerns are outlined below:

What is the computational overhead associated with this algorithm? The decomposition of each fan-in weight into parallel and orthogonal components, coupled with the transformation to radial-angular coordinates, may introduce significant computational cost. The authors should discuss the increase in computational operations compared to a parametric neural network, and it would be beneficial to include running time experiments to quantify this overhead.

The results indicate that the nonparametric network collapses to a smaller network size on convex datasets, leading to performance inferior to that of parametric networks. Is there any explanation for this behavior?
