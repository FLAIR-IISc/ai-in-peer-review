- Paper proposes a nonparametric neural network that dynamically adds units and uses sparsity regularization to prune connections, learning model size during training.
- Core idea: decompose fan-in weights into parallel and orthogonal components and use radial-angular coordinates to permit unit growth/shrinkage in one training run.
- Addresses the important problem of automatically determining network size without prior information and presents promising empirical and theoretical results.
- Main concern: potential computational overhead from the weight decomposition and radial-angular transformations; authors should quantify runtime and compare to parametric networks.
- Results are mixed: strong performance on MNIST and rectangles but notably worse performance on the convex dataset, with no clear explanation.
- Decision: Reject.
- Primary reason for rejection: insufficient evidence of consistent improvement over well-tuned parametric networks and lack of analysis on computational cost.
- Supporting positive: convincing critique of black-box hyperparameter search and a valuable theoretical contribution (Theorem 1).
- Supporting negative: empirical evaluation does not consistently show an advantage; claim of outperforming black-box methods is unsubstantiated given mixed results.
- AdaRad’s added complexity and memory overhead (e.g., maintaining running averages φ̃l) are noted but not analyzed in detail.
- Authors should provide runtime comparisons and memory-usage analysis versus standard optimizers (Adam, RMSprop).
- Unexplained performance drop on the convex dataset needs investigation—could be dataset-specific or an algorithmic limitation.
- Suggested ablation study to measure contributions of AdaRad components (radial/angular updates, CapNorm).
- Suggested hyperparameter sensitivity analysis for λ, αφ, β, and ν.
- Recommend scaling experiments on larger datasets beyond the poker dataset to strengthen claims.
- Request deeper theoretical analysis of convergence properties.
- Ask for explicit connections to recent neural architecture search (NAS) literature.
- Clarify vague terminology such as “f-equivalence” with a precise definition.
- Specific questions for authors: provide computational complexity comparison to Adam/RMSprop, explain poor convex performance, report sensitivity to angular step size (αφ), and detail how unit addition rate (ν) affects convergence and final performance.
