- Proposes dynamic network growth/shrinkage so model size adapts to data complexity; reviewer concurs with novelty.  
- Theoretical result: with fan-in or fan-out regularization, the error optimum is achievable with a finite number of parameters, preventing unbounded growth/overfitting.  
- Result is reminiscent of Lasso/Elastic Net; paper needs a more intuitive explanation of the theorem’s underpinnings.  
- Paper spends too much space on related work; recommend reallocating space to deeper discussion of Theorem 1 and its implications.  
- Key missing experiment: compare against a statically sized network with the same final number of nodes to validate dynamic allocation benefits.  
- Figure 2 is hard to interpret and shows mixed results; authors should explain why nonparametric learning sometimes outperforms or underperforms static networks.  
- Theorem 1 is underexplored conceptually—paper should articulate central takeaways and practical consequences (e.g., predicting optimal lambda, sensitivity to initialization, combining fan-in/fan-out).  
- Ask whether optimal regularization parameter lambda can be predicted from data and how sensitive final architecture is to initialization/random seeds.  
- Clarify claim that adding zero-valued units changes the regularizer value (counterintuitive for norms like L2).  
- Clarify definition of a “zero unit”: it should require both fan-in and fan-out weights to be zero to allow removal without changing function.  
- Reviewer adjusted score to 7 and expects authors to address theoretical and experimental concerns.
