This paper proposes a nonparametric neural network model that automatically learns its size during training. The core idea is to randomly introduce zero units and employ a sparse regularizer to effectively prune irrelevant weights. This approach resembles a random search over a discrete model space, guided by sparse regularization to eliminate unnecessary units. Addressing the problem of dynamic model sizing is important, and the paper presents interesting results. My main comments are detailed below:

What is the additional computational complexity of the proposed algorithm? The decomposition of each fan-in weight into parallel and orthogonal components, coupled with the transformation into radial-angular coordinates, may introduce significant overhead. The authors should discuss the increase in computational cost relative to parametric neural networks, and it would be beneficial to include running time experiments to illustrate this. 

The results indicate that nonparametric networks tend to converge to smaller architectures on convex datasets, leading to performance inferior to parametric networks. Is there any insight into the reason for this behavior?
