- Contribution: Proposes a nonparametric neural network framework that dynamically adds zero-valued units and uses L2 sparsity to prune, aiming to auto-select network size during training.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Major concern — computational complexity: AdaRad’s radial/orthogonal decomposition likely incurs significant overhead that the paper neither analyzes nor measures empirically.  
- Major concern — inconsistent performance: Strong results on MNIST/rectangles but degraded performance on the convex dataset with no clear explanation.  
- Requested: Provide empirical runtime comparisons and a formal complexity analysis versus standard parametric training and optimizers (e.g., Adam).  
- Requested: Deeper analysis of the convex dataset to explain why the method regresses there (e.g., sensitivity to dynamic sizing or regularization).  
- Suggested clarification: More concrete justification of why separating radial and angular updates accelerates learning compared to standard/adaptive optimizers.  
- Suggested experiments: Systematic hyperparameter tuning for nonparametric hyperparameters (λ, αr, αφ), ideally with automated search like Bayesian optimization.  
- Suggested experiments: Ablation studies to isolate contributions of components (CapNorm, L2 regularization, AdaRad design choices).  
- Suggested theory: Strengthen theoretical analysis in Section 7.1 and better characterize/relax conditions for achieving a global minimum.  
- Suggested design: Justify or explore alternatives to the unit initialization strategy (random fan-in, zero fan-out).  
- Suggested visualization: Include training-time visualizations of network growth and pruning to illuminate behavior across datasets.  
- Questions for authors: Provide detailed AdaRad complexity and runtime comparisons, explain convex dataset failure modes, report sensitivity of performance to αr/αφ and hyperparameter search results, and justify/compare unit initialization strategies.
