## Review of NONPARAMETRIC NEURAL NETWORKS

This paper tackles the important problem of automatically determining the optimal size of a neural network during training, avoiding the computationally expensive global search required by traditional methods. The authors propose a nonparametric neural network framework that dynamically expands and shrinks the network, guided by a novel optimization algorithm called Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad) and regularization penalties. The core idea of continuously adjusting network size during a single training cycle is compelling and the initial results are promising.

**Decision:** Reject. While the paper presents an interesting approach and demonstrates potential benefits on some datasets, the lack of clear justification for the computational overhead and the performance degradation on the convex dataset raise significant concerns. Further, the empirical evaluation, while extensive, doesn't definitively establish a consistent advantage over well-tuned parametric networks.

**Supporting Arguments:**

The main drawback of this work is the lack of a thorough analysis of the computational cost associated with AdaRad. The proposed decomposition of weight vectors into radial and angular components, along with the coordinate transformations, likely introduces significant overhead. The paper acknowledges this, but only provides a qualitative comparison to other optimization algorithms. A quantitative analysis, including running time experiments comparing AdaRad to standard methods (e.g., Adam, RMSprop) on various network sizes and datasets, is crucial to assess the practical viability of this approach. Without this, the potential benefits are overshadowed by uncertainty regarding scalability.

The significantly poorer performance on the convex dataset is also a concern. The authors acknowledge this, but offer no detailed explanation for *why* the nonparametric approach fails in this case. A deeper investigation into the characteristics of this dataset that hinder the method’s effectiveness is needed. This negative result casts doubt on the generalizability of the proposed framework. The authors claim the convex dataset benefits from larger networks, but do not address why the nonparametric approach fails to find them.

Finally, while the theoretical foundation is presented, the proof of Theorem 1 relies on several conditions that might be restrictive in practice. A more detailed discussion of the limitations of these conditions and their potential impact on the applicability of the framework would strengthen the theoretical contribution.

**Additional Feedback (for improvement):**

*   **Computational Complexity:** Provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of AdaRad compared to standard optimization algorithms. Include running time experiments with varying network sizes and datasets.
*   **Convex Dataset Analysis:** Investigate the reasons for the poor performance on the convex dataset. What properties of this dataset make it unsuitable for the proposed method? Could modifications to the approach improve performance on this type of data?
*   **Regularization Parameter Tuning:** Explore more systematic methods for tuning the regularization parameter (λ). The current grid search approach, while reasonable, might not be optimal. Consider adaptive methods for adjusting λ during training.
*   **Ablation Study:** Conduct an ablation study to evaluate the contribution of different components of the AdaRad algorithm (e.g., radial vs. angular updates, the specific normalization scheme).
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Analyze the sensitivity of the results to the choice of other hyperparameters, such as the learning rate and the mixing rates (βarith, βquad).
*   **Clarify the Role of `p`:** The paper focuses primarily on p=2. Expand the discussion of other p-values and their impact on network sparsity and performance.
* **Detailed Experimental Setup**: Provide more detailed experimental settings, including the specific hardware used, the batch size, and the number of training epochs.

**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  Could you provide a quantitative assessment of the computational overhead introduced by AdaRad compared to standard optimization algorithms (e.g., Adam, RMSprop)? Specifically, what is the increase in training time per epoch?
2.  What are the key characteristics of the convex dataset that explain the observed performance degradation with the nonparametric approach?
3.  How sensitive are the results to the choice of the regularization parameter (λ)? Have you explored any adaptive methods for adjusting λ during training?
4.  What is the rationale behind using a fan-in regularizer specifically, and have you explored the performance of a fan-out regularizer or a combination of both?
5. Could you elaborate on the impact of different values of 'p' on the network's sparsity and generalization performance, considering computational costs?
