- Addresses automatic adaptation of network size during training via dynamic unit addition/removal and group sparsity regularization.  
- Theoretical claim: appropriate regularization yields training-error-minimizing networks with a finite number of units.  
- Initial experiments indicate the framework is viable and competitive, showing comparable performance with fewer units.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form due to unconvincing practical benefits and limited experimental evaluation.  
- Lacks comprehensive comparison to established network pruning and compression methods (accuracy, training/inference time, resource use).  
- Method introduces new hyperparameters (addition rate, shrinkage rate) that shift rather than eliminate tuning; sensitivity not explored.  
- Unclear whether proposed hyperparameters are easier or harder to tune than traditional ones.  
- Concern about potential training-time overhead from dynamic unit changes and continuous optimization; scalability not addressed.  
- Experiments are preliminary, limited in scope, and do not demonstrate generalizability to larger/diverse datasets or architectures.  
- Suggested improvement: thorough comparison to state-of-the-art pruning/compression techniques across accuracy, time, and size metrics.  
- Suggested improvement: empirical scalability analysis showing training-time scaling with network size and dataset complexity.  
- Suggested improvement: detailed hyperparameter sensitivity analysis and methods to automate tuning.  
- Suggested improvement: ablation studies to isolate contributions of regularization, optimizer (AdaRad), and other components.  
- Suggested improvement: extend theoretical analysis to convergence and generalization guarantees.  
- Clarifying questions: effects of radial/angular step sizes on final size/performance, AdaRad computational/memory cost vs SGD/Adam, sensitivity to initial size d0, impact of unit addition rate ν, and limitations/future directions.
