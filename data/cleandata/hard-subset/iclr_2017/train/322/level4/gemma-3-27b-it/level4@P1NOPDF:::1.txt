This paper proposes a nonparametric neural network model that automatically learns its size during training. The core idea involves randomly adding zero units and employing a sparse regularizer to effectively eliminate irrelevant weights. This approach resembles a random search over a discrete space, guided by sparse regularization to prune unnecessary units. This is an important problem, and the paper presents interesting results. My main comments are detailed below:

What is the additional computational complexity of the proposed algorithm? The decomposition of each fan-in weight into parallel and orthogonal components, followed by the transformation into radial-angular coordinates, may introduce significant overhead. The authors should discuss the extra computational cost relative to a parametric neural network with a comparable number of parameters. Furthermore, including some running time experiments would be highly beneficial. 

It is observed that the nonparametric networks tend to converge to smaller networks when applied to convex datasets, resulting in performance inferior to that of parametric networks. Is there any insight into why this occurs?
