- Proposes a simple, well-justified dynamic architecture that adds/removes units based on zero-valued input/output weights with group-sparsity regularization.  
- Theoretical result: with proper regularization the loss is minimized by a network with a finite number of units; initial experiments show over/underfitting not immediately problematic.  
- Claim of reduced hyperparameter tuning is overstated: several hyperparameters (e.g., addition frequency, weight decay) still control network growth and are not easier to optimize than fixed-size networks.  
- Shows promise for yielding smaller networks (lower inference time, power, memory), but no comparison to pruning or other compression methods, so competitiveness is unclear.  
- Potential drawback: coupling of unit count and training time via shared hyperparameters may cause substantially longer training and raises scalability concerns not addressed by the authors.  
- Experiments are helpful but limited in scope; broader evaluation on larger, more complex problems is needed to establish practical viability.  
- Overall: well-written and interesting with potential for adaptable lifelong-learning networks, but results are preliminary and largely theoretical.
