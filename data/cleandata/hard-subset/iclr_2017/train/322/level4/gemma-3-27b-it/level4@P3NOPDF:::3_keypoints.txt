- Proposes a simple, elegant method to dynamically add/remove neural units during training using group sparsity regularization.
- Provides a theoretical result that appropriate regularization yields a loss minimizer with a finite number of units.
- Theoretical guarantee does not prevent overfitting/underfitting in practice; initial experiments suggest these issues are uncommon.
- Learning network size could reduce architecture hyperparameter tuning but introduces new hyperparameters (growth, weight decay) whose tuning difficulty is unclear.
- Authors appropriately avoid claiming simplified training; lack of clear improvement in hyperparameter ease is disappointing.
- Potential to produce smaller networks with comparable performance, reducing inference time, power, and memory for resource-constrained deployment.
- Experimental evaluation omits comparisons to network compression/pruning methods, leaving competitiveness uncertain.
- Coupling between network-size hyperparameters and training time may result in substantially longer training and scalability concerns for larger networks.
- Experiments are encouraging but limited; broader, more complex evaluations are needed to demonstrate practical viability.
- Overall: well-written and promising toward adaptive/lifelong learning, but results are preliminary and speculative, requiring further research.
