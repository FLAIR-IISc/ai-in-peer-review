- Proposes dynamically adding/removing units during training using group sparsity; proves regularization yields a loss-minimizing network with a finite number of units.  
- The finite-unit theoretical result doesn’t guarantee optimal generalization; the learned network could still over- or under-fit, though preliminary experiments are reassuring.  
- Method may reduce hyperparameter tuning burden in principle, but several hyperparameters (unit-add frequency, weight decay, etc.) still indirectly control final size, so tuning may not be easier.  
- Authors do not claim simplified training, and it’s disappointing that hyperparameter complexity remains.  
- Shows networks with fewer units can match performance of parametric baselines, which could reduce inference runtime, power, and memory (useful for mobile).  
- No experimental comparison to post-training size reduction methods (e.g., pruning), leaving competitiveness unclear.  
- Same hyperparameters affect both network size and training time, potentially causing substantially slower training; scalability to large networks is not discussed and may be problematic.  
- Experiments are encouraging but not comprehensive; results on much larger problems are needed to assess practicality and broad applicability.  
- Overall: interesting, well-written, and potentially important for lifelong/adaptive learning, but current results are preliminary.
