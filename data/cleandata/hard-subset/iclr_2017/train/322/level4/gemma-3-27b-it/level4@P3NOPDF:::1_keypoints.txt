- Introduces a nonparametric neural network that adds “zero units” and uses sparse regularization to automatically learn network size — promising idea.  
- Conceptually resembles a guided random search over discrete network sizes via sparse pruning.  
- The fan-in weight decomposition and radial-angular transformation may introduce significant computational overhead.  
- Authors should provide quantitative complexity/runtime comparisons to standard parametric networks to assess practical scalability.  
- Empirical finding: method converges to smaller networks on convex datasets and performs worse than comparable parametric networks.  
- Need analysis/ablation studies to determine if this behavior is due to sparse regularization, initialization, or an inherent method limitation.  
- Zero-unit initialization strategy requires discussion of its impact on optimization, especially early training dynamics.  
- Regularization strength (lambda) is critical; justify chosen value and include sensitivity analysis showing effects on final size and performance.  
- Compare more thoroughly to existing dynamic-sizing methods (pruning, growing neural gas, RL-based NAS) and outline relative advantages/disadvantages.  
- Clarify notation and present a concise summary of the weight decomposition equations and their interpretations.  
- Overall: interesting contribution but must address computational complexity concerns and explain the convex-dataset behavior to strengthen the paper.
