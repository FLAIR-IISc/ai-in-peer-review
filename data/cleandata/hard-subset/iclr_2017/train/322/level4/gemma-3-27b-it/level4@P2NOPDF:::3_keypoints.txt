- Proposes dynamically adding/removing network units during training using zero-valued input/output weights and group sparsity regularization.  
- Theoretical result: with appropriate regularization the loss can be minimized by a network with a finite number of units (no guarantee on generalization).  
- Initial experiments indicate the method does not inherently lead to over- or under-fitting.  
- Claimed benefit of reducing hyperparameter tuning is undermined because the method introduces new hyperparameters (unit addition frequency, weight decay) that control effective size.  
- No clear improvement in hyperparameter simplicity or tuning effort compared to fixed-size networks.  
- Potential for achieving comparable performance with smaller networks, reducing computation, power, and memory for deployment.  
- Major omission: no comparison to established network compression techniques (e.g., pruning), making competitiveness unclear.  
- Concern that hyperparameters controlling size are coupled to training time, potentially causing substantially longer and inherently slow training.  
- Paper does not address scalability to larger networks or complex real-world problems; experiments are limited and only proof-of-concept.  
- Overall: well-written and promising for lifelong/adaptive networks, but results are preliminary and further work is needed to prove practical scalability and advantages.
