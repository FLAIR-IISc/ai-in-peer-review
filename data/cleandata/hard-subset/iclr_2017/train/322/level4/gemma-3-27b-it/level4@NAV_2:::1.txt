This paper proposes a nonparametric neural network model that automatically learns network size during training. The core idea involves dynamically adding units and employing sparsity-inducing regularization to prune irrelevant connections. This addresses an important problem, and the paper presents promising results.

**Decision:** Reject. While the concept is interesting and the experimental results on some datasets are encouraging, the performance on the convex dataset is concerning, and the computational cost of the proposed method is not sufficiently addressed. Further, the theoretical justification, while present, feels somewhat disconnected from the practical implementation and the observed results.

**Supporting Arguments:**

1. **Performance on Convex Dataset:** The substantial underperformance of nonparametric networks on the convex dataset raises significant doubts about the generalizability of the approach. The authors acknowledge this but do not provide sufficient insight into *why* the method fails in this specific scenario. This suggests potential limitations that need further investigation.
2. **Computational Cost:** The proposed “Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent” (AdaRad) algorithm introduces additional computational complexity through the decomposition of weight vectors and the transformation to radial-angular coordinates. The paper mentions this but lacks a detailed analysis of the computational overhead compared to standard training algorithms. Practical running time experiments are needed to assess the feasibility of this approach, especially for large-scale datasets.

**Additional Feedback:**

*   **Ablation Studies:** It would be valuable to conduct ablation studies to evaluate the individual contributions of the different components of the framework (e.g., the `l2` regularization, the AdaRad optimizer, the CapNorm normalization). This could help pinpoint the key factors driving the observed performance.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper provides limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity. How robust are the results to variations in the regularization parameter (λ), the angular and radial step sizes (αφ and αr), and the unit addition rate (ν)?
*   **Theoretical Connection:** The theoretical guarantees provided in Section 7.1 are interesting, but their practical implications for the observed performance are not fully clear. A more detailed discussion of how the theoretical results relate to the empirical findings would be beneficial.
*   **Initialization:** The effect of different unit initialization schemes (e.g., random initialization vs. more sophisticated methods) on the performance of the nonparametric network deserves investigation.
*   **Comparison with Existing Methods:** A more thorough comparison with existing dynamic network expansion techniques (e.g., progressive neural networks) would help to better position the contributions of this work.

**Questions for the Authors:**

*   Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of AdaRad compared to standard optimization algorithms like SGD or Adam? Specifically, how does the cost scale with network size and dataset size?
*   What insights can you offer regarding the poor performance of nonparametric networks on the convex dataset? Are there specific characteristics of this dataset that make it particularly challenging for this approach?
*   How sensitive are the results to the choice of hyperparameters (λ, αφ, αr, ν)? Can you provide some guidance on how to tune these parameters effectively?
*   Could you elaborate on the connection between the theoretical results (Theorem 1) and the observed behavior of the nonparametric networks during training? How does the theorem explain the network's tendency to converge to a specific size?
*   How does the choice of nonlinearity (specifically the self-similarity property) impact the performance of the nonparametric network? Would the results be significantly different with a different nonlinearity?
