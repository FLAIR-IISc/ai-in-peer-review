- Novel approach: proposes dynamic network growth/shrinkage based on data rather than user-defined priors; novelty endorsed.  
- Theoretical result: under fan-in or fan-out regularization, optimal solution uses a finite number of parameters, preventing indefinite growth/overfitting.  
- Relation to prior work: result is reminiscent of sparsity-inducing methods (Lasso/Elastic Net), providing theoretical justification.  
- Presentation weakness: Theorem 1 lacks accessible intuition; most explanation is buried in the Appendix.  
- Suggestion: Expand plain-English interpretation of Theorem 1, possibly by shortening the extensive related-work section.  
- Comparative experiment requested: directly compare proposed dynamic architecture to a conventionally trained network of the same final size to isolate benefits.  
- Figure 2 unclear: current experimental presentation is hard to interpret and mixed results need deeper analysis.  
- Mechanism question: investigate why non-parametric nets sometimes outperform fixed architectures — what allows this improvement?  
- Regularization parameter λ: explore relationship between λ and data, whether λ can be predicted/automated, and how λ determines final structure.  
- Initialization effects: examine whether different random initializations produce substantially different final networks and quantify those differences.  
- Combined regularization: ask whether theoretical guarantees hold when fan-in and fan-out regularization are applied together.  
- Clarification needed: statement that adding zero units changes the regularizer value is unclear (e.g., adding zeros doesn't change L2 norm); request elaboration.  
- Definition of “zero unit”: current definition (either fan-in or fan-out zero) is misleading; true removal without changing f requires both fan-in and fan-out be zero—clarify.  
- Overall assessment: promising direction with valuable theory, but needs clearer theorem intuition, stronger fixed-size comparisons, and deeper analysis of factors shaping learned structure; rating adjusted to 7.
