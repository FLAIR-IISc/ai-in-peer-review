- Proposes a nonparametric neural network that learns its size by randomly introducing zero units and using a sparse regularizer to prune irrelevant weights.  
- Method resembles a random search over discrete model architectures guided by sparse regularization.  
- Addresses the important problem of dynamic model sizing and shows interesting empirical results.  
- Major concern: possible extra computational complexity from decomposing fan-in weights and using radial–angular coordinates—needs analysis.  
- Reviewer requests running-time experiments and a comparison of computational cost against parametric networks.  
- Empirical issue: nonparametric models converge to smaller architectures on convex datasets and perform worse than parametric networks—needs explanation or insight.
