This paper addresses the problem of automatically determining the optimal size of a neural network during training, avoiding the expensive hyperparameter search typically required. The authors propose Nonparametric Neural Networks, a framework where network size is dynamically adjusted by adding and removing units, guided by an L2 regularization penalty to control growth and redundancy. A key theoretical contribution is demonstrating that, under these conditions, a finite-sized network will achieve a global minimum of the training error. The paper presents promising initial experimental results demonstrating competitive performance with potentially smaller network sizes.

**Reject.** While the paper presents an interesting approach and theoretical grounding, the practical benefits and scalability remain unconvincing given the current experimental evaluation. The potential advantages regarding hyperparameter tuning are not fully realized, and the increased training time compared to standard parametric approaches raises concerns about practical applicability, especially for large-scale networks.

The core idea of dynamically adjusting network size is well-motivated. The problem of selecting an appropriate network architecture is a significant challenge in deep learning, and exploring methods to automate this process is valuable. The theoretical result guaranteeing a finite-sized solution under the given regularization is a solid contribution, providing a foundation for the proposed framework. The proposed AdaRad algorithm, designed to handle the dynamic network size, appears thoughtfully constructed and addresses potential training instabilities. The experimental setup, including comparisons to fixed-size parametric networks, is reasonable. The paper is clearly written and logically organized.

However, several factors lead to my rejection decision. First, while the authors acknowledge the need to tune hyperparameters within their framework (addition rate, shrinkage parameters), they don’t convincingly demonstrate that this is *easier* than tuning the size of a parametric network. The paper doesn’t provide a comparative analysis of the tuning effort required for both approaches. Second, the experiments are limited in scope. The comparison to existing network pruning techniques, which directly address the goal of reducing network size post-training, is missing. Demonstrating superiority over these established methods is crucial to establish the value of this approach. Third, the experiments, while encouraging, do not convincingly address the scalability concerns. The increased training time associated with dynamically growing/shrinking the network is not adequately discussed, especially concerning its impact on larger datasets and more complex architectures. The observation that training time scales more gracefully than RMSprop is a positive, but insufficient to allay concerns about overall training cost. The result on the convex dataset, where the nonparametric network significantly underperforms, is also concerning and deserves further investigation. Finally, the ablation study comparing AdaRad and AdaRad-M showed negligible difference, yet a substantial amount of algorithmic complexity was added for little apparent gain.

**Additional Feedback (for potential improvement):**

*   **Hyperparameter Tuning Comparison:** Conduct a more thorough comparison of the effort required to tune hyperparameters in both the nonparametric and parametric settings. Quantify the tuning process (e.g., number of trials, time spent).
*   **Comparison to Pruning Methods:** Include a comparison to state-of-the-art network pruning techniques (e.g., those cited in the related work) to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in terms of network size reduction and performance.
*   **Scalability Analysis:** Provide a more detailed analysis of the computational cost of the proposed approach, including training time as a function of dataset size and network complexity. Investigate methods to mitigate the increased training time.
*   **Convex Dataset Investigation:** Analyze the reason for the poor performance on the convex dataset. Is it a limitation of the framework itself, or a sensitivity to initialization or hyperparameters?
*   **Ablation Studies:** More thoroughly explore the impact of different components of AdaRad (e.g., radial vs. angular shifts) through ablation studies.
*   **Learning Rate Annealing:** Explore the effects of adaptive learning rate annealing strategies, possibly incorporating the dynamic network size information.

**Questions for the Authors:**

*   How does the computational cost of training a nonparametric network scale with the size of the dataset and the desired network complexity?
*   Can you provide a more quantitative comparison of the hyperparameter tuning effort required for nonparametric versus parametric networks?
*   Have you explored the application of this framework to more complex architectures (e.g., convolutional neural networks)? If so, what were the results?
*   Why does the nonparametric network perform so poorly on the convex dataset, and what modifications could be made to address this issue?
*   What are the specific criteria used to determine when to add or remove units? Are there any safeguards to prevent the network from oscillating between different sizes?
