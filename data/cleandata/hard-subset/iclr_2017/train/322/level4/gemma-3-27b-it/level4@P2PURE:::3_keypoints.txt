- Proposes dynamically adding/removing neural units during training by targeting units with zero input/output weights.
- Method is simple and well-motivated, using a group sparsity norm to encourage weight decay.
- Theoretical guarantee: with appropriate regularization, the loss is minimized by a network with a finite number of units.
- Theory does not eliminate the possibility of overfitting or underfitting; experiments suggest these are not prominent.
- Could reduce hyperparameter tuning burden, but does not remove it due to new hyperparameters for unit addition and weight decay.
- Unclear whether the new hyperparameters are easier to optimize than those in standard parametric approaches.
- Demonstrates maintaining comparable performance with fewer units, useful for lowering computation, power, and memory needs.
- No comparison to pruning or other compression techniques, so competitiveness with state-of-the-art is unknown.
- Hyperparameters that control network size are coupled with training time, which may substantially slow training.
- Scalability concerns: authors do not address practicality for larger networks or large-scale applications.
- Experiments are encouraging but limited; further evaluation on larger, more complex problems is needed.
- Overall: interesting, clearly presented contribution toward adaptive lifelong networks, but current results are preliminary and speculative.
