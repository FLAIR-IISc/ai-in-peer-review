- Introduces a novel nonparametric neural network that learns its size during training.  
- Core method: randomly add zero-valued units and use sparse regularization to prune irrelevant connections.  
- Related to randomized search over discrete model space, but sparse regularization gives a principled elimination mechanism.  
- Tackles the important problem of determining optimal network capacity and shows encouraging results.  
- Computational complexity needs clearer analysis: decomposing fan-in into parallel/orthogonal components and radial-angular transforms may add significant overhead.  
- Authors should quantify extra computational cost versus training a fixed parametric network and provide running-time experiments.  
- Empirical observation: the method converges to smaller architectures on convex datasets and underperforms parametric networks in that case.  
- Need explanation for this behavior (e.g., role of dataset convexity, regularization strength, method limitations) and exploration of domain implications.
