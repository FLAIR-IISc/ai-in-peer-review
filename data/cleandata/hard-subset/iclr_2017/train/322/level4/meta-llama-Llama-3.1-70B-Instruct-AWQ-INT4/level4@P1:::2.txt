The paper proposes a novel framework for automatically adapting and choosing the size of a neural network during a single training run. The authors introduce nonparametric neural networks, a non-probabilistic framework for conducting optimization over all possible network sizes. They prove the soundness of this framework when network growth is limited via a p penalty. The authors also develop a novel optimization algorithm, Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad), and demonstrate promising results on several benchmark datasets.

The paper addresses the problem of automatically finding a good network size during a single training cycle, which is a challenging problem in deep learning. The authors' approach is to use a nonparametric framework that can adapt to the optimal network size during training. They also provide a theoretical justification for their approach by proving that the nonparametric neural network achieves a global training error minimum at some finite dimensionality.

The experimental results show that the nonparametric neural network can outperform parametric networks of the same size on some datasets, and achieve competitive results with exhaustive random search on others. The authors also demonstrate the scalability of their approach by applying it to a large dataset.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach and its theoretical justification. The experimental results are also well-presented and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
