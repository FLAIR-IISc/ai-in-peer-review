This paper introduces a nonparametric neural network model that dynamically adjusts its size during training by incorporating zero units and leveraging sparse regularization to eliminate irrelevant weights. At its core, this approach appears to be a random search method over a discrete space, facilitated by sparse regularization to remove unnecessary units. The problem addressed is significant, and the paper presents intriguing results. Key concerns and suggestions are outlined below:

1. What are the additional computational costs associated with the proposed algorithm? The process of decomposing fan-in weights into parallel and orthogonal components, followed by the transformation into radial-angular coordinates, may incur substantial extra computation time. The authors should discuss the relative increase in operations compared to parametric neural networks and consider providing experimental results on running times.

2. It is noted that nonparametric networks tend to yield smaller networks on convex datasets, which can lead to inferior performance compared to parametric networks. The authors may wish to offer insight into this observation to further contextualize their approach.
