- Proposes a nonparametric neural network that dynamically adjusts size by adding zero units and using sparse regularization to remove irrelevant weights.  
- Core mechanism effectively performs a random search over discrete network architectures, with sparse regularization selecting useful units.  
- The problem is important and the empirical results are intriguing.  
- Major concern: potential extra computational cost from decomposing fan-in weights and converting to radial-angular coordinates—need discussion and runtime comparisons to parametric networks.  
- Major concern: on convex datasets the method produces smaller networks that can underperform parametric models—authors should analyze and explain this behavior.
