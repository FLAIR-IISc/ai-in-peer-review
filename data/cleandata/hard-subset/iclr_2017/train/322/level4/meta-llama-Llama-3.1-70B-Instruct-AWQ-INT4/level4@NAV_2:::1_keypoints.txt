- Proposal: Introduces a nonparametric neural network that learns model size during training by randomly adding zero units and using sparse regularization to remove irrelevant units.  
- Core idea assessment: Essentially a random-search over discrete architectures aided by sparse regularization to prune useless units.  
- Importance: Tackles the significant problem of automatic model size selection in deep learning with promising empirical results.  
- Main critique — complexity: Requests analysis of additional computational cost from decomposing fan-in weights and transforming to radial-angular coordinates.  
- Empirical complexity: Suggests reporting running-time experiments to quantify overhead vs. parametric networks.  
- Performance concern: Notes nonparametric method returns small networks on the convex dataset and underperforms parametric networks there.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Acceptance reasons: Novel approach, interesting use of sparse regularization, and promising experimental performance across datasets.  
- Supporting points: Paper gives clear explanation, theoretical justification, good experimental comparisons, and acknowledges limitations.  
- Suggestions for improvement: Provide detailed complexity analysis, test on more/different datasets, explore alternative or combined regularizers, and explain convex-dataset failure modes.  
- Questions for authors: Clarify algorithmic complexity vs. parametric nets, extension plans to CNNs and other complex models, consideration of other regularizers (dropout/weight decay), and deeper insight into poor convex-dataset performance.
