- Proposes dynamically adding/removing units during training by inserting units with zero input/output weights and using group sparsity to push unit weights to zero.  
- Main theoretical result: with proper regularization, the loss is minimized by a network with a finite number of units.  
- The theoretical guarantee does not ensure the resulting network avoids overfitting or underfitting.  
- Preliminary experiments are encouraging but do not conclusively show lack of over/underfitting.  
- Method could reduce runtime, power, and memory at test time by producing smaller networks.  
- No experimental comparison to existing post-training compression/pruning methods, so competitiveness vs. best size-reduction methods is unclear.  
- Approach does not eliminate hyperparameter tuning: frequency of additions and decay rates still control final network size.  
- Same hyperparameters that control unit count also affect training time, potentially making training much slower than fixed-architecture approaches.  
- Unclear how training time and scalability behave for much larger networks; practicality for large-scale training is questioned.  
- Experiments are helpful but limited; larger-scale and more comprehensive evaluations are needed to demonstrate practicality.  
- Paper is clearly written, interesting, and inspiring for lifelong/adaptive networks, but current results remain somewhat speculative.
