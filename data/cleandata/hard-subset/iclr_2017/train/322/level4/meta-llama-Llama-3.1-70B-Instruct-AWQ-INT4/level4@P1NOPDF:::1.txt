This paper proposes a nonparametric neural network model that automatically learns the size of the model during training. The key idea is to randomly add zero units and utilize a sparse regularizer to automatically nullify the weights that are irrelevant. At its core, this approach appears to be a form of random search over a discrete space, leveraging sparse regularization to eliminate useless units. This is an important problem, and the paper presents interesting results. My main comments are listed below:

What is the additional computational complexity of the algorithm? The decomposition of each fan-in weight into a parallel component and an orthogonal component, and the subsequent transformation into radial-angular coordinates, may incur significant extra computation time. The authors should discuss the additional number of operations required relative to a parametric neural network. Furthermore, it would be useful to include running time experiments to provide empirical evidence of the algorithm's efficiency.

It is observed that nonparametric networks yield small networks on convex datasets, resulting in inferior performance compared to parametric networks. Do the authors have any insight into this phenomenon?
