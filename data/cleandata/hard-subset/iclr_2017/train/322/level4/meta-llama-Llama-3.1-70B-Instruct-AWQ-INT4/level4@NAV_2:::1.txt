This paper proposes a nonparametric neural network model, which automatically learns the size of the model during the training process. The key idea is to randomly add zero units and use sparse regularizer to automatically null out the weights that are irrelevant. The idea sounds to be a random search approach over discrete space with the help of sparse regularization to eliminate useless units. This is an important problem and the paper gives interesting results. My main comments are listed below:
What is the additional computation complexity of the algorithm? The decomposition of each fan-in weights into a parallel component and an orthogonal component and the transformation into radial-angular coordinates may require a lot of extra computation time. The authors may need to discuss the extra amount of operations relative to the parametric neural network. Furthermore, it would be useful to show some running time experiments.
It is observed that nonparametric networks return small networks on the convex dataset so that it is inferior to parametric networks. Any insight on this?

Decision: Accept

Reasons:
The paper proposes a novel approach to automatically learning the size of a neural network model during training, which is an important problem in deep learning. The idea of using sparse regularization to eliminate useless units is interesting, and the experimental results show promising performance on several datasets. While there are some potential issues with computation complexity and the performance on the convex dataset, the paper provides a good starting point for further research in this area.

Supporting arguments:
The paper provides a clear explanation of the proposed approach and its theoretical justification. The experimental results are well-presented and provide a good comparison with parametric neural networks. The authors also discuss the potential limitations of their approach and provide some insights into why it may not perform well on certain datasets.

Additional feedback:
To further improve the paper, the authors may want to consider the following suggestions:
- Provide a more detailed analysis of the computation complexity of the algorithm and its impact on training time.
- Investigate the performance of the nonparametric neural network on a wider range of datasets to better understand its strengths and weaknesses.
- Consider alternative approaches to sparse regularization, such as using different types of regularizers or combining multiple regularizers.
- Provide more insights into why the nonparametric neural network performs poorly on the convex dataset and how this can be improved.

Questions for the authors:
- Can you provide more details on the computation complexity of the algorithm and how it compares to parametric neural networks?
- How do you plan to extend the approach to more complex models, such as convolutional neural networks?
- Have you considered using other types of regularizers, such as dropout or weight decay, in combination with sparse regularization?
- Can you provide more insights into why the nonparametric neural network performs poorly on the convex dataset and how this can be improved?
