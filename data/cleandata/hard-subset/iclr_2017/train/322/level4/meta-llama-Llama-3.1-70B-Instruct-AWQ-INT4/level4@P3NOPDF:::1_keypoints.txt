- Proposal: Introduces a nonparametric neural network that learns model size during training by randomly adding zero units and using a sparse regularizer to nullify irrelevant weights.

- Core idea: Effectively performs a random search over a discrete space, leveraging sparse regularization to eliminate useless units.

- Significance: Tackles an important problem and presents interesting empirical results.

- Computational complexity concern: Need a detailed analysis of extra computation from decomposing fan-in weights and converting to radial-angular coordinates, with an operation-count comparison to standard parametric networks.

- Runtime evaluation request: Include running-time experiments to quantify the proposed method’s computational efficiency.

- Convex-dataset performance issue: Nonparametric networks tend to be smaller and perform worse on convex datasets; authors should explain causes and suggest improvements.

- Overall: Addressing the above concerns would strengthen the contribution.
