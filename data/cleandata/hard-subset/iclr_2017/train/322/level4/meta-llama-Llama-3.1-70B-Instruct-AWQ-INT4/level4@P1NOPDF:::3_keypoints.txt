- Proposes dynamically adding/removing units during training using units with zero input/output weights and group-sparsity regularization.  
- Theoretical result: with proper regularization, loss is minimized by a network with a finite number of units.  
- Theoretical guarantee does not ensure absence of over- or under-fitting; experiments suggest this is rarely problematic.  
- Potential benefit: reduces need to pick network size hyperparameter, potentially easing model selection.  
- New hyperparameters (unit-addition frequency, weight-decay rate) still control emergent size, so hyperparameter tuning is not eliminated.  
- Unclear whether the new hyperparameters are easier or harder to tune than standard ones.  
- Claims of producing smaller networks matter for runtime, power, and memory (mobile use), but no comparison to pruning or other compression methods is provided.  
- Same hyperparameters affect both model size and training time, likely making training slower than fixed-size parametric approaches.  
- Scalability concerns: authors do not address how training time and practicality scale to much larger networks.  
- Experiments are encouraging but limited; larger-scale and more comprehensive evaluations are needed to demonstrate broad applicability.  
- Overall judgment: interesting, clearly written, and inspiring work, but current results remain somewhat speculative.
