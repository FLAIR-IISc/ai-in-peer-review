- Proposes a nonparametric neural network that can add/remove units during training using zero input/output weights and group sparsity regularization.  
- Targets automatic determination of network size without prior information.  
- Theoretical result: with proper regularization, the loss is minimized by a network with a finite number of units.  
- Proof provided via lemmas bounding weights and gradients; proof is tedious but instructive and highlights the role of regularization.  
- Introduces AdaRad (Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent) combining radial and angular updates for training nonparametric networks.  
- Experiments show nonparametric networks can achieve competitive performance with parametric networks on benchmarks.  
- Demonstrates applicability to large-scale data (example: OpenML poker dataset).  
- Potential disadvantage: same hyperparameters control both final unit count and training time, possibly slowing training compared to fixed-size parametric approaches.  
- Lacks experimental comparison to existing size-reduction methods like pruning of trained networks.  
- Overall assessment: interesting, clearly written, and inspiring for lifelong/adaptive learning, but results are still somewhat speculative.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Reasons for decision: novel and well-motivated approach, significant theoretical contribution, and promising experimental results.  
- Suggestion: compare empirically to pruning and other model-compression methods.  
- Suggestion: investigate scalability to larger networks and more complex tasks.  
- Suggestion: provide a detailed computational complexity analysis versus parametric approaches.  
- Suggestion: include visualizations and analysis of training dynamics, network structure, and weight evolution.  
- Questions for authors: clarify hyperparameter choices, especially the regularization parameter and unit addition rate.  
- Questions for authors: plans for extending the approach to more complex tasks and larger networks.  
- Questions for authors: provide visualizations or insights into network behavior during training.  
- Questions for authors: how will you address the coupling between hyperparameters that control unit count and training time?
