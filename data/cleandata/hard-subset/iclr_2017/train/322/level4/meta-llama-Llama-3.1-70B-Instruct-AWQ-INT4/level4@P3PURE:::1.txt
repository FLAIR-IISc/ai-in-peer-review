This paper proposes a nonparametric neural network model that automatically learns the size of the model during the training process. The key idea is to randomly add zero units and use a sparse regularizer to automatically null out the weights that are irrelevant. The idea appears to be a random search approach over a discrete space, leveraging sparse regularization to eliminate useless units. This is an important problem, and the paper presents interesting results. My main comments are listed below:

What is the additional computational complexity of the algorithm? The decomposition of each fan-in weight into a parallel component and an orthogonal component, and the transformation into radial-angular coordinates, may require a substantial amount of extra computation time. The authors should discuss the extra amount of operations relative to parametric neural networks. Furthermore, it would be useful to include running time experiments to provide a clearer understanding of the computational overhead.

It is observed that nonparametric networks return small networks on the convex dataset, making them inferior to parametric networks. Can the authors provide any insight into this phenomenon?
