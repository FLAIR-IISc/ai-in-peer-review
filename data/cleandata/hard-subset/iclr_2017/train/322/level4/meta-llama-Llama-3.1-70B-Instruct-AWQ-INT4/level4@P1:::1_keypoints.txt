- Proposes a nonparametric neural network that learns model size by randomly adding zero units and using a sparse regularizer to null irrelevant weights.  
- Reviewer interprets the method as a random search over discrete architectures aided by sparse regularization.  
- Problem is important and the paper presents interesting results.  
- Major concern: potential extra computational cost from decomposing fan-in weights into parallel/orthogonal components and using radial-angular coordinates; authors should quantify additional operations and provide running-time experiments.  
- Empirical issue: on the convex dataset the method yields very small networks and performs worse than parametric networks — reviewer asks for insight/explanation.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
