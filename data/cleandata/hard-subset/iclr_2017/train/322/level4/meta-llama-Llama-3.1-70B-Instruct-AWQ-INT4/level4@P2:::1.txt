This paper proposes a nonparametric neural network model, which automatically learns the size of the model during the training process. The key idea is to randomly add zero units and use sparse regularizer to automatically null out the weights that are irrelevant. The idea sounds to be a random search approach over discrete space with the help of sparse regularization to eliminate useless units. This is an important problem and the paper gives interesting results. My main comments are listed below:

1. What is the additional computation complexity of the algorithm? The decomposition of each fan-in weights into a parallel component and an orthogonal component and the transformation into radial-angular coordinates may require a lot of extra computation time. The authors may need to discuss the extra amount of operations relative to the parametric neural network. Furthermore, it would be useful to show some running time experiments.

2. It is observed that nonparametric networks return small networks on the convex dataset so that it is inferior to parametric networks. Any insight on this?

Decision: Accept

The paper proposes an interesting nonparametric neural network model that automatically learns the size of the model during the training process. The idea of using sparse regularizer to eliminate useless units is novel and the results on the mnist and rectangles images datasets are promising. However, the paper needs to address the additional computation complexity of the algorithm and provide more insights on the convex dataset results.

Supporting arguments:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to automatically learn the size of the neural network during the training process.
* The idea of using sparse regularizer to eliminate useless units is interesting and the results on the mnist and rectangles images datasets are promising.
* The paper provides a detailed analysis of the nonparametric training process and the behavior of the units during training.

Additional feedback:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the convex dataset results. Why do nonparametric networks return small networks on this dataset?
* The authors may need to discuss the extra amount of operations relative to the parametric neural network and provide some running time experiments to support their claims.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods for automatically learning the size of the neural network.

Questions to the authors:

* Can you provide more insights on the convex dataset results? Why do nonparametric networks return small networks on this dataset?
* Can you discuss the extra amount of operations relative to the parametric neural network and provide some running time experiments to support your claims?
* Can you provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods for automatically learning the size of the neural network?
