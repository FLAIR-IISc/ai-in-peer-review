- Introduces nonparametric neural networks to adapt and choose network size automatically during a single training run.  
- Proposes a non-probabilistic optimization framework over all network sizes and proves its soundness under an Lp penalty (Theorem 1).  
- Introduces a novel optimizer, Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad).  
- Empirical results show competitive or superior performance to parametric networks on several benchmarks.  
- Well-motivated problem with a clear discussion of limitations of existing methods.  
- Supports claims with both theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation.  
- Theorem 1 is presented without sufficient discussion of its broader implications or intuition.  
- Theorem 1’s proof needs more intuitive explanation, as it is central to the framework.  
- Experimental results are mixed in places and lack analysis explaining when nonparametric nets succeed or fail.  
- Need to explore how the regularizer parameter (lambda) influences final network structure.  
- Impact of initialization on the resulting network is not sufficiently investigated.  
- Additional experiments and analysis are recommended to better demonstrate the framework’s effectiveness.  
- Rating: 7; Decision: Accept.
