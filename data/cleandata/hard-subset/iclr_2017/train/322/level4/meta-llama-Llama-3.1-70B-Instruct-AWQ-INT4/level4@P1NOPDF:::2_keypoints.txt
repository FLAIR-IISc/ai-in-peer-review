- Agrees with Reviewer 2 that dynamically adding/removing units is an interesting direction allowing models to adapt size to data rather than relying on prior architecture choices.
- Paper presents a theoretical result: the error optimum can be achieved with a finite number of parameters under fan-in or fan-out regularization, preventing unbounded growth and overfitting.
- Theorem resembles Lasso/Elastic Net sparsity effects; reviewer requests more intuition and explanation for why the result is significant.
- Suggests reallocating space from prior work to provide more insight and intuition behind the main theoretical result.
- Requests a compelling experiment comparing dynamic (non-parametric) networks to fixed-size networks chosen after training to validate efficiency in performance vs. memory.
- Notes Figure 2 experiments show mixed results that are unclear and need careful interpretation; non-parametric sometimes outperforms and sometimes underperforms parametric networks.
- Asks why non-parametric learning can sometimes outperform parametric learning even when the final network size is known in advance and requests insight into this phenomenon.
- Requests deeper discussion of Theorem 1’s implications in simple terms, plus supporting experiments exploring its practical impact.
- Asks if the regularizer parameter lambda can be predicted from data or if data properties inform optimal lambda, and whether lambda critically determines final structure.
- Asks how final network structure depends on initialization and whether different random initializations produce substantially different networks.
- Inquires what happens when combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers and whether the theoretical result still holds.
- Requests clarification on why adding zero units changes the regularizer value (e.g., does L2 norm change when zeros are added).
- Points out ambiguity in definition of zero units: likely both fan-in and fan-out must be zero to be removable without changing output, and asks authors to clarify.
- Revised overall rating to 7, expecting the authors to address these comments.
