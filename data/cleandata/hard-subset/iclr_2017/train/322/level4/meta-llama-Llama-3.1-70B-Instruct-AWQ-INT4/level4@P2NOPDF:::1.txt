This paper proposes a nonparametric neural network model that automatically learns its size during the training process. The core idea is to randomly add zero units and utilize a sparse regularizer to eliminate the weights of irrelevant units. At its essence, this approach can be viewed as a random search over a discrete space, leveraging sparse regularization to remove unnecessary units. This is an important problem, and the paper presents interesting results. My main comments are listed below:

The computational complexity of the algorithm is not explicitly stated, and I would like to see a discussion on the additional computational overhead. Specifically, the decomposition of each fan-in weight into a parallel component and an orthogonal component, followed by the transformation into radial-angular coordinates, may incur significant extra computation time. A comparison of the number of operations required by the proposed algorithm relative to a parametric neural network would be helpful. Additionally, running time experiments would provide valuable insights into the practical efficiency of the method.

It is noteworthy that the nonparametric networks tend to produce smaller networks on convex datasets, which can lead to inferior performance compared to parametric networks. I would appreciate any insight the authors can offer on this observation, as it may have implications for the applicability of the proposed approach to certain types of problems.
