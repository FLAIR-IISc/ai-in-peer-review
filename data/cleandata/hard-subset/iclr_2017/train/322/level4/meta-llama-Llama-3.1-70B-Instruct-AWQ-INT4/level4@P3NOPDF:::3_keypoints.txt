- Addresses dynamic adjustment of network units during training by adding/removing units with zero input/output weights and using group sparsity regularization.  
- Theoretical result: with appropriate regularization, the loss is minimized by a network with a finite number of units.  
- Practical guarantee does not ensure avoidance of overfitting or underfitting; initial experiments are encouraging but not definitive.  
- Proposed method could reduce hyperparameter burden by learning unit count, but does not eliminate hyperparameter tuning altogether.  
- Key hyperparameters (unit addition frequency, weight decay rate) implicitly control unit count and may be hard to tune.  
- Unclear whether these new hyperparameters are easier or harder to tune than standard ones; authors acknowledge this limitation.  
- Claims of comparable performance with smaller networks are promising for runtime, power, and memory savings on devices.  
- No experimental comparison to pruning or other size-reduction methods, so competitiveness is unknown.  
- Same hyperparameters affect both model size and training time, potentially making training slower than fixed-parametric approaches.  
- Scalability concerns: unclear how training time and practicality will behave on large-scale networks.  
- Experiments are helpful but limited; more extensive tests on larger problems are needed to validate practicality.  
- Paper is well-written and inspiring for lifelong/adaptive networks, but current results remain speculative and require further work.
