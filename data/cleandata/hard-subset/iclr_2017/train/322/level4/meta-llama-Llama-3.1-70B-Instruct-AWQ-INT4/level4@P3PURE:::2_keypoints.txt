- Agreement with Reviewer 2: the idea of dynamically adding/removing units to let the model adapt to data rather than user priors is interesting.  
- Main theoretical result: under fan-out or fan-in regularization the error optimum uses a finite number of parameters, preventing unbounded growth and overfitting.  
- The theorem resembles sparsity results (Lasso/Elastic Net) and needs more intuitive explanation and space in the paper.  
- Request more discussion and analysis of Theorem 1’s key insight and broader implications beyond the appendix proof.  
- Experimental suggestion: compare dynamic (non-parametric) networks to fixed-size networks chosen after training to show efficiency and memory-performance tradeoffs.  
- Current experiments (Figure 2) are unclear/mixed; sometimes non-parametric outperforms parametric and sometimes not — this deserves deeper interpretation.  
- Ask why non-parametric learning can sometimes beat parametric learning even when the final network size is known in advance.  
- Suggest experiments/analysis on how regularizer strength (λ) relates to data and whether λ can be predicted or guides final network structure.  
- Question sensitivity to initialization: do different random seeds yield different final network structures, and how different are they?  
- Ask what happens when combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers — does the finite-parameter result still hold?  
- Clarify why adding zero units changes the regularizer value (e.g., does L2 change when zero values are appended?).  
- Clarify definition of “zero units”: whether both fan-in and fan-out must be zero to remove a unit without affecting output.  
- Reviewer updated rating to 7 and requests authors address these points.
