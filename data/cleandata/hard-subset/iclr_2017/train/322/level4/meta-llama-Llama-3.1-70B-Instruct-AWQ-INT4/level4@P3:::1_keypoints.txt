- Proposes a nonparametric neural network that automatically learns model size by adding zero units and using a sparse regularizer to prune irrelevant weights.  
- Tackles an important problem (automatic neural network size selection) with a novel approach.  
- Provides theoretical justification: proves a global training error minimum at finite dimensionality with fan-in/fan-out regularizer (λ > 0, 1 ≤ p < ∞).  
- Shows promising empirical results, outperforming parametric networks on several benchmarks (e.g., MNIST, rectangles).  
- Recommends acceptance based on novelty, theoretical support, and empirical performance.  
- Notes inferior performance on the convex dataset and requests explanation and improvement strategies.  
- Raises concern about additional computational complexity and asks for discussion of extra operations versus parametric networks.  
- Requests running-time experiments to demonstrate the method’s efficiency.  
- Suggests the paper needs a more detailed related-work discussion and clearer differentiation from existing automatic model-selection methods.  
- Asks specific questions for the authors: why poor convex-dataset performance, how to improve it, provide running-time results, and clarify differences from prior methods.
