- Agrees with Reviewer 2: dynamically adding/removing units is a promising direction for models to adapt to data without relying on user priors.
- Main theoretical result: under fan-in or fan-out regularization, the error optimum can be achieved with a finite number of parameters, preventing unbounded growth and overfitting.
- The theorem resembles Lasso/Elastic Net sparsity; the reviewer wants more intuition and expanded discussion of this result.
- Recommend reallocating space from prior work discussion to a deeper analysis and explanation of Theorem 1.
- Suggests stronger experiments: show a dynamic (non-parametric) model outperforming a fixed-size network to demonstrate efficiency in performance vs. memory.
- Notes Figure 2’s experiments are mixed and unclear; calls for improved clarity and interpretation of results.
- Requests explanation for why non-parametric learning can sometimes outperform parametric models even when the final network size is known.
- Asks for extensions/clarifications about Theorem 1: predictability of regularizer lambda, lambda’s role in final structure, sensitivity to initialization, and effects of combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers.
- Questions why adding zero units changes the regularizer value (e.g., does L2 norm change when adding zeros?).
- Points out ambiguity in the definition of “zero units” and urges clarification that both fan-in and fan-out should be zero to remove a unit without changing the output.
- Revised overall rating to 7, contingent on authors addressing these comments and strengthening the paper.
