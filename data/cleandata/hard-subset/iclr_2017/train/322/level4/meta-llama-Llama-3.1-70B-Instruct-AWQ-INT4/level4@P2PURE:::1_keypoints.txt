- Proposes a nonparametric neural network that grows by randomly adding zero units and uses a sparse regularizer to null out irrelevant weights, automatically learning model size.
- Framed as a form of random search over a discrete space leveraging sparsity; addresses an important problem and shows interesting results.
- Major concern about added computational complexity from decomposing fan-in weights into parallel and orthogonal components and transforming to radial–angular coordinates.
- Authors should quantify the extra operations relative to parametric networks and discuss the computational overhead.
- Recommend including running-time experiments to empirically measure the computational cost.
- Empirical observation: nonparametric networks tend to return very small models on convex datasets and can be inferior to parametric networks; authors should analyze and explain this behavior.
