- Proposes nonparametric neural networks to automatically adapt and choose network size during a single training run.  
- Introduces a non-probabilistic framework for optimization over all possible network sizes and proves soundness when growth is limited by a p penalty.  
- Develops a novel optimization algorithm, Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad).  
- Demonstrates promising experimental results: can outperform parametric nets of same size and be competitive with exhaustive random search.  
- Shows scalability by applying the approach to a large dataset.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation and theoretical justification.  
- Suggestion: expand discussion of the implications and significance of Theorem 1.  
- Suggestion: provide more insight into why nonparametric learning outperforms parametric learning when the final network is known in advance.  
- Suggestion: add more experiments to validate theory, e.g., study how regularizer parameter λ affects final network structure and predictability from data.  
- Suggestion: investigate dependence of final network structure on initialization and variability across initializations.  
- Suggestion: clarify how fan-in and fan-out regularizers are combined and whether theoretical results still hold.  
- Rating: 8 and Decision: Accept.  
- Reasons for acceptance: novel effective approach, clear theoretical justification, and supportive experimental results.  
- Questions for authors: why nonparametric better than parametric when final net is known, how λ affects final structure and predictability, how initialization influences final nets, and details on combining fan-in/fan-out regularizers.
