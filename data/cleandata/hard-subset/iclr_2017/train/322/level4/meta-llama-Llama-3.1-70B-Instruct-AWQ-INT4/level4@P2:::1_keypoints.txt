- Proposes a nonparametric neural network that learns model size during training by randomly adding zero units and using a sparse regularizer to remove irrelevant weights.  
- Characterized as a random search over discrete model sizes aided by sparse regularization.  
- Important problem with interesting and promising results on MNIST and rectangles images datasets.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Major concern: Potential additional computational complexity from decomposing fan-in weights and converting to radial-angular coordinates; authors should quantify overhead.  
- Recommend providing running-time experiments to compare computational cost with parametric networks.  
- Observation: On the convex dataset the nonparametric method returns very small networks and performs worse than parametric networks; authors should explain why.  
- Suggest more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art automatic model-sizing methods.  
- Specific questions for authors: explain convex dataset behavior, detail extra operations and provide runtime results, and provide a more thorough comparison to related methods.
