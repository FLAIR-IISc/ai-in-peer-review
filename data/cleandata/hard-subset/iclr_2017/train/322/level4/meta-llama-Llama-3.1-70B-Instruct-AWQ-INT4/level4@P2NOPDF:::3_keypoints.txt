- Proposes a method to dynamically add/remove neural units during training by zeroing input/output weights and using a group sparsity regularizer.  
- Theoretical result: with suitable regularization, the loss is minimized by a network with a finite number of units.  
- Theoretical guarantee does not ensure avoidance of overfitting or underfitting, though initial experiments are promising.  
- Potential benefit: may reduce the need to choose the number of units manually as the network adapts its size.  
- Method does not eliminate hyperparameter tuning—parameters like unit-addition frequency and weight decay still must be set.  
- Unclear whether these new hyperparameters are easier or harder to tune than those in standard approaches.  
- Claims the approach can produce smaller networks with comparable performance, aiding runtime, power, and memory efficiency.  
- Lacks experimental comparison to established size-reduction methods (e.g., pruning), so competitiveness is unclear.  
- Same hyperparameters influence both model size and training time, potentially making training much slower than fixed-architecture methods.  
- Scalability to large networks is not discussed, raising practical concerns.  
- Experiments are encouraging but limited; larger-scale and more comprehensive evaluations are needed.  
- Overall assessment: interesting, well-written, and inspiring for lifelong/adaptive learning, but results remain speculative and require further work.
