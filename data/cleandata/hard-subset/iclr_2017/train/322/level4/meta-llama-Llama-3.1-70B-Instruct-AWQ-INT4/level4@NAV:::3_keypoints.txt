- Proposes dynamically adding/removing units during training using negligible input/output weights and group-sparsity regularization.
- Theoretical result: with appropriate regularization, the loss can be minimized by a network with a finite number of units, though this doesn’t guarantee practical generalization.
- Preliminary experiments indicate over- or under-fitting may not be a major issue, but the empirical evidence is limited.
- Adaptive unit count could reduce hyperparameter tuning for network size, but the method introduces new hyperparameters controlling growth and weight decay.
- It’s unclear whether the new hyperparameters are easier to tune than traditional ones; the authors do not claim simplified training.
- No experimental comparison to pruning or other model-compression methods, so competitiveness with state-of-the-art size-reduction techniques is unknown.
- A single set of hyperparameters controls both unit growth and training time, potentially slowing training and raising scalability concerns for large networks.
- Experiments are encouraging but not exhaustive; larger-scale validation is needed to establish practicality and usefulness.
- Overall the idea is interesting, well-written, and potentially valuable for lifelong/adaptive networks, but current results remain speculative and require further validation.
