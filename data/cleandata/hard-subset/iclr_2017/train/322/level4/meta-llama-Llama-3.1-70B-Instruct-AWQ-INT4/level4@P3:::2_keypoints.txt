- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that adapt and choose network size during a single training run.  
- Proposes a non-probabilistic optimization over all network sizes with a soundness proof when growth is limited by a p penalty.  
- Introduces a new optimizer, Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad).  
- Shows promising experimental results on several benchmark datasets.  
- Decision: accept, primarily due to novelty and the theoretical soundness result.  
- Paper is well-motivated with a clear problem statement and a thorough proof in the appendix.  
- Reviewer requests more experiments comparing nonparametric nets to parametric nets of the same final size.  
- Asks for more intuition and discussion about Theorem 1 and its significance for neural networks.  
- Requests experiments on how final network structure depends on initialization and different random seeds.  
- Wants analysis of how different resulting networks are when started from different initial weights.  
- Asks what happens when fan-in and fan-out regularizers are combined and whether the theoretical result still holds.  
- Requests more discussion on selecting the regularizer parameter lambda and its impact on final network structure.  
- Poses explicit questions: why nonparametric can outperform parametric when final net is known, and repeats concerns about initialization and regularizer choices.  
- Reviewer raised rating to 7, expecting authors to address the above comments.
