- Proposes a nonparametric neural network that dynamically learns model size by randomly adding zero units and using a sparse regularizer to null out irrelevant weights.  
- Characterizes the approach as a random search over a discrete space that leverages sparse regularization to eliminate useless units.  
- Problem is important and the paper presents interesting empirical results.  
- Major concern about additional computational complexity: decomposition into parallel/orthogonal components and radial-angular coordinate transforms may add substantial overhead; authors should quantify extra operations versus parametric networks and provide running-time experiments.  
- Empirical issue: nonparametric networks produce very small models on the convex dataset and perform worse than parametric networks; authors should provide insight/explanation for this phenomenon.
