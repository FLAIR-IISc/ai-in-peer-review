- Dynamic addition/removal of units is an interesting direction that lets models adapt to data without user priors.  
- The paper proves the error optimum can be achieved with a finite number of parameters under fan-in or fan-out regularization, preventing unbounded growth and overfitting.  
- Theorem resembles Lasso/Elastic Net sparsity; reviewer wanted more intuition because the result seems somewhat expected.  
- Recommend allocating more space to discuss and study the main theoretical result rather than prior work.  
- Request a clear experimental comparison between dynamic (non-parametric) and fixed-size networks to demonstrate efficiency; Figure 2 is mixed and unclear.  
- Need explanation for why non-parametric learning sometimes outperforms parametric even when the final network is known.  
- Theorem 1 needs a plain-English explanation of its key insight and more discussion of implications, plus supporting experiments.  
- Ask whether the regularizer parameter lambda can be predicted from data and how strongly lambda determines final network structure.  
- Ask how sensitive the final network structure is to initialization and whether different random seeds yield distinct networks.  
- Question whether combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers preserves the theoretical result.  
- Request clarification on why adding zero units changes the regularizer (e.g., effect on L2 norm) and a clearer definition of zero units (both fan-in and fan-out should be zero to remove a unit).  
- Reviewer raised rating to 7 and expects authors to address these points.
