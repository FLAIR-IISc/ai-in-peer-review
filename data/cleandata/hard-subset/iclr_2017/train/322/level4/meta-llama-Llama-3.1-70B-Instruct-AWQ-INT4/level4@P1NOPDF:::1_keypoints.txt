- Proposes a nonparametric neural network that learns model size during training by randomly adding zero units and using a sparse regularizer to nullify irrelevant weights.
- Core approach resembles a random search over a discrete space combined with sparse regularization to eliminate useless units.
- The problem is important and the paper presents interesting empirical results.
- Reviewer requests analysis of additional computational complexity from decomposing fan-in weights (parallel/orthogonal) and transforming to radial-angular coordinates, and asks for a discussion of extra operations versus parametric networks.
- Reviewer asks for running-time experiments to empirically demonstrate the algorithm’s efficiency.
- Notes that nonparametric networks produce very small models on convex datasets and sometimes underperform parametric networks, requesting insight into this phenomenon.
