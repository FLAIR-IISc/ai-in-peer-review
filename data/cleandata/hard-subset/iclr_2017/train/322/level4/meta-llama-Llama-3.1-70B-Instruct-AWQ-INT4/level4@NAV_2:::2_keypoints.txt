- Proposes a non-parametric neural network that automatically adapts its size during training by adding or removing units.  
- Core idea: dynamic growth/shrinkage of the model to find an appropriate network size without prior knowledge.  
- Provides Theorem 1 proving existence of a global minimum for the error under certain conditions, with a detailed proof in the appendix.  
- Experiments on benchmark datasets (MNIST, rectangles images, convex) demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Well-motivated approach building on regularization and pruning literature, with clear positioning and citations.  
- Paper includes a clear algorithmic description and thorough mathematical exposition.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance (Decision: Accept, Rating: 8).  
- Suggests clearer, higher-level intuition for Theorem 1 to aid non-mathematical readers.  
- Requests more discussion of implications of Theorem 1, especially the effect of regularization parameter λ on final network structure and performance.  
- Calls for additional experimental results on more datasets and more detailed performance analysis across tasks.  
- Asks authors for guidance on choosing λ in practice and comparisons on larger datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, ImageNet).
