- Paper proposes dynamically adding/removing units during training using zeroed input/output weights and group-sparsity regularization.
- Main theoretical result: with proper regularization, the loss is minimized by a network with a finite number of units.
- Practical experiments show the method can produce smaller networks with comparable performance, but results are limited and not fully convincing.
- Method does not guarantee avoidance of over- or under-fitting in practice.
- Approach does not eliminate hyperparameter tuning—several hyperparameters still implicitly control emergent unit count.
- Hyperparameters also control how often units are added and weight decay speed, making tuning potentially as hard as standard methods.
- Same hyperparameters affect training time, so learning the architecture may substantially slow training compared to fixed-size parametric approaches.
- Authors did not compare to existing post-hoc size-reduction methods (e.g., pruning), so competitiveness is unclear.
- Concerns about scalability: no discussion or evidence on how training time and practicality scale to much larger networks.
- Recommendation: run experiments on much larger problems to demonstrate practicality and usefulness.
- Suggestions: explore faster optimization, parallelization, and alternative regularizers (dropout, weight decay) to improve training time and performance.
- Decision: Reject. Questions requested: details on hyperparameter selection, plans to address slow training, and consideration of other regularization techniques.
