This paper proposes a nonparametric neural network model that automatically learns the size of the model during the training process. The key idea is to randomly add zero units and use a sparse regularizer to automatically null out the weights that are irrelevant. While this approach can be viewed as a form of random search over a discrete space, leveraging sparse regularization to eliminate useless units, it is an important problem and the paper presents interesting results. My main comments are listed below:

A critical aspect to consider is the additional computational complexity of the algorithm. Specifically, the decomposition of each fan-in weight into a parallel component and an orthogonal component, followed by the transformation into radial-angular coordinates, may incur substantial extra computation time. The authors should discuss the extra amount of operations required relative to a parametric neural network and provide some insight into the computational overhead. Furthermore, it would be useful to include running time experiments to quantify the computational cost.

It is also observed that nonparametric networks tend to return small networks on convex datasets, which can make them inferior to parametric networks in such cases. The authors may want to provide some insight into this phenomenon, as it could be an important consideration when deciding whether to use this approach.
