This paper proposes a nonparametric neural network model that automatically learns the size of the model during the training process. The key idea is to randomly add zero units and utilize a sparse regularizer to nullify the weights of irrelevant units. At its core, this approach resembles a random search over a discrete space, leveraging sparse regularization to eliminate useless units. The problem addressed is significant, and the paper presents interesting results.

My main comments and suggestions for improvement are listed below:

1. Computational Complexity: The authors should provide a detailed analysis of the additional computational complexity of the proposed algorithm. Specifically, the decomposition of each fan-in weight into a parallel component and an orthogonal component, followed by the transformation into radial-angular coordinates, may incur substantial extra computation time. A comparison of the number of operations required by the proposed nonparametric neural network relative to a standard parametric neural network would be beneficial. Furthermore, including running time experiments would help to quantify the computational efficiency of the proposed approach.

2. Performance on Convex Datasets: It is observed that nonparametric networks tend to return smaller networks when trained on convex datasets, resulting in inferior performance compared to parametric networks. The authors should provide insight into this phenomenon, discussing possible reasons behind the reduced performance and potential avenues for improvement. This would enhance the understanding of the proposed method's strengths and limitations.

Overall, the paper addresses an important problem and presents interesting results. Addressing the above concerns would further strengthen the contribution of the proposed nonparametric neural network model.
