- Central idea of dynamically adding/removing units is intriguing and enables models to adapt to data rather than relying on user priors.
- Theorem: under fan-in or fan-out regularization, the error optimum uses a finite number of parameters, preventing unbounded growth and overfitting.
- The result is reminiscent of Lasso/Elastic Net sparsity; more intuitive explanation and emphasis on this theorem are needed.
- Recommend reallocating space from prior work to deeper discussion and study of the main theoretical result.
- Key experiment needed: show dynamic networks can outperform fixed-size networks after full training to demonstrate efficiency.
- Figure 2 results are mixed and unclear; sometimes nonparametric nets outperform, sometimes underperform—this needs clarification.
- Request explanation for why nonparametric learning can outperform parametric even when final network size is known.
- Theorem 1 requires more plain-English insight and expanded experimental validation.
- Ask whether the regularizer parameter lambda can be predicted from data and how crucial lambda is to final structure.
- Question how sensitive final network structure is to weight initialization and how different random inits affect resulting networks.
- Ask what happens when combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers and whether the theoretical guarantees still hold.
- Clarify why adding zero units would change the regularizer value (e.g., does L2 norm change when adding zeros?).
- Clarify definition of “zero units”: both fan-in and fan-out should be zero to remove a unit without changing the network output.
- Reviewer revised rating to 7 pending authors’ responses and improvements addressing these points.
