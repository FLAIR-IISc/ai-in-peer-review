- Paper proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that adjust network size during training, with theoretical guarantees using fan-in/fan-out p=2 regularizers and new methods CapNorm and AdaRad.  
- Claims: higher accuracy than same-size parametric nets, outperforms exhaustive search on 2/3 datasets, avoids pre-trained pruning, and scales efficiently.  
- Decision: Reject — strong theory but empirical evaluation undermines main claims.  
- Major reason 1: Method fails on the standard convex dataset, underperforming parametric networks without adequate explanation.  
- Major reason 2: "Dynamic" resizing is misleading — units are added slowly (one/epoch) and removal occurs after training, not truly dynamic during training.  
- Major reason 3: No comparison to modern neural architecture search (NAS) methods, weakening competitiveness claims.  
- Major reason 4: Experiments only cover fully connected networks with two hidden layers, leaving scalability to convnets and complex architectures untested.  
- Major reason 5: Computational cost of AdaRad is higher than SGD but not quantified or justified by real-world gains.  
- Theoretical contribution (Theorem 1) is significant and rigorously proven; CapNorm and AdaRad are well-motivated.  
- Empirical critique: reported success on MNIST and poker is promising but inconsistent across benchmarks and lacks thorough analysis.  
- Feedback 1: Explicitly clarify limitations of the "dynamic" resizing claim to avoid misleading readers.  
- Feedback 2: Expand empirical comparisons to include state-of-the-art NAS methods and convolutional architectures.  
- Feedback 3: Investigate and explain the convex dataset failure and potential remedies.  
- Feedback 4: Provide concrete measurements of AdaRad's computational overhead versus standard optimizers.  
- Feedback 5: Consider renaming "nonparametric" to a less misleading term since parameters remain but dimensionality is variable.  
- Question 1: Why does the method fail on the convex dataset and can this be addressed?  
- Question 2: Why add one unit per epoch — is this strategy optimal and how would it scale for efficient training?  
- Question 3: What was the exact poker test error and how does it compare to the best published OpenML results?  
- Question 4: How does selecting λ via grid search compare to needing a good starting point in pruning approaches?  
- Question 5: How would p=1 regularizers behave and does the theoretical guarantee extend beyond p=2?
