- Introduces "nonparametric neural networks" that adapt network size during a single training run.  
- Uses a fan-in regularizer with p=2 to dynamically add/remove units and prove existence of a global minimum at finite dimensionality (Theorem 1).  
- Proposes CapNorm (modified batch normalization) and AdaRad (adaptive optimizer that normalizes only orthogonal gradient components) to handle nonparametric training.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: rigorous proof (Theorem 1) with thorough appendix.  
- Methodological innovation: AdaRad and CapNorm specifically address training new units without premature shrinkage.  
- Empirical results: better accuracy with fewer parameters on MNIST and rectangles images; competitive with exhaustive search.  
- Ablation study demonstrates training dynamics of unit addition and removal toward an optimal size.  
- Notable limitation: poor performance on the convex dataset, likely due to convergence to smaller networks.  
- Comparison to related growing-network methods is insufficient (e.g., Wei et al. 2016, Chen et al. 2016, DSD).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions based on strong theory and empirical validation.  
- Suggestion: analyze convex-dataset failure in more depth (role of problem structure, dataset size, noise).  
- Suggestion: include direct comparisons to other growth-based methods and to training from scratch.  
- Suggestion: provide concrete timing/computational-cost comparisons between AdaRad, RMSprop, and standard SGD.  
- Suggestion: study sensitivity to unit-addition schedule (e.g., more units early vs. one per epoch).  
- Questions for authors: explain convex failure; compare to other growing-network approaches; contextualize poker dataset perfect test result against best published results.
