- Proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that adapt network size during training, eliminating expensive model selection.
- Treats network dimensionality as an optimizable parameter with fan-in/fan-out regularizers to ensure finite size.
- Introduces two practical components: CapNorm (modified batch norm) and AdaRad (adaptive optimizer for dynamic networks).
- Provides a theoretical proof (Theorem 1) showing convergence to a finite global minimum under appropriate conditions.
- Demonstrates empirically that nonparametric networks can outperform parametric networks of the same size on MNIST and rectangles images.
- Shows competitive performance with exhaustive search methods and scalability to larger datasets (poker).
- Reviewer decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Main reasons for acceptance: important problem, strong theoretical foundation, and promising experimental results.
- Thorough experimental design including comparisons to parametric baselines, exhaustive search, and ablation studies on network growth dynamics.
- Concern: poor performance on the convex dataset where nonparametric nets underperform same-size parametric nets.
- Suggestion: provide more analysis explaining why the convex dataset is problematic and whether the small converged size is the cause.
- Suggestion: clarify and quantify computational cost and wall-clock time overhead of AdaRad versus standard optimizers.
- Suggestion: discuss practical considerations for large-scale applications (unit addition rate, memory requirements, heuristics).
- Suggestion: provide a more intuitive explanation of Theorem 1 in the main text for non-expert readers.
- Questions for authors: why does the convex dataset cause poor performance—data property, architecture, or other?
- Questions for authors: how was the unit-addition rate chosen for poker, and how sensitive is performance to this rate or can it be automated?
- Questions for authors: quantify how quickly networks reach stable size (epochs to stabilization) for MNIST versus poker.
- Questions for authors: clarify accuracy comparisons—are improvements for same-size networks or due to achieving good accuracy with fewer parameters?
