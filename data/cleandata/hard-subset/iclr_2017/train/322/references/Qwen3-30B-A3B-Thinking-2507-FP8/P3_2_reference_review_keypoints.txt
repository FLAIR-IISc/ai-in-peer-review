- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that dynamically adjust network size during training without prior task knowledge.  
- Claims: automatically selects optimal network size in one run; higher accuracy than same-size parametric nets; outperforms random search on 2/3 benchmarks; theoretical convergence guarantees; introduces CapNorm and AdaRad.  
- Theorem 1: strong theoretical proof that nonparametric networks reach a global training minimum at finite dimensionality with fan-in/fan-out regularizers.  
- Novel methods: CapNorm (modified batch norm) and AdaRad (adaptive radial-angular optimizer); p=2 justified mainly by hardware considerations.  
- Empirical: promising results on MNIST and rectangles datasets, achieving equal/better accuracy with fewer parameters.  
- Main limitation: poor performance on the convex dataset, attributed to convergence to small network sizes and needing deeper analysis.  
- Missing comparison to neural architecture search (NAS), weakening positioning against direct competitors.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions based on strong theory and promising practical benefits.  
- Suggested experiments: extend evaluation to harder datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, ImageNet) for broader applicability.  
- Analyze convex dataset failure: compare final sizes, explain why networks shrink, and whether this is inherent or tunable.  
- Add explicit comparisons and contrasts to NAS methods (e.g., Zoph & Le 2017).  
- Provide computational cost analysis comparing total training time to black-box optimization/NAS methods.  
- Clarify theoretical assumptions about nonlinearities and discuss extension to non-differentiable activations like ReLU.  
- Question: Is convex dataset underperformance a fundamental limitation or tunable?  
- Question: Is p=2 chosen only for hardware reasons, or could p=1 sometimes be better?  
- Question: How will the framework scale to very deep architectures (e.g., ResNets) and automatic depth selection?  
- Question: How to adapt the proof/framework to handle ReLU’s nondifferentiability at 0?  
- Question: How do theoretical training-error guarantees relate to practical test-error minimization?
