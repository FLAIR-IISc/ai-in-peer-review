- Proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that automatically add/remove units during training using fan-in/fan-out regularizers.  
- Theorem 1: with λ>0 and 1≤p<∞, regularization guarantees convergence to a finite optimal network size (proof given).  
- Introduces AdaRad optimizer and CapNorm normalization layer to enable dynamic architecture changes during training.  
- Claims to avoid training multiple full networks and allow improving performance by adjusting size during training.  
- Empirical results: outperform parametric networks of same size on MNIST and rectangles; competitive with exhaustive random search; scaled to the poker dataset.  
- Recommendation: accept — paper makes significant theoretical and practical contributions.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical foundation (detailed appendix, sequence of lemmas, Proposition 1 for layer-wise regularization).  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments comparing to parametric baselines and exhaustive random search, with training dynamics analysis.  
- Weakness: poor performance on the convex dataset — requires deeper analysis of causes.  
- Suggestion: compare and relate to recent neural architecture search (NAS) methods to better situate the work.  
- Suggestion: provide a more detailed computational cost analysis (including wall-clock time) vs. standard black-box model selection.  
- Suggestion: clarify relation to sparse-network work and implications of choosing p=2 for hardware efficiency.  
- Limitation: current focus on fully-connected networks; extension to convolutional networks should be discussed.  
- Questions for authors: explain convex dataset failure, quantify time savings vs. random/Bayesian search, compare to sparse-weight methods, describe adaptation to conv nets, and how this fits with NAS frameworks.
