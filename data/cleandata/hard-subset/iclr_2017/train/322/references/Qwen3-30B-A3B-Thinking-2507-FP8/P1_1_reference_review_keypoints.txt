- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that automatically select network size during a single training run.  
- Dynamically adds and removes units using fan-in/fan-out regularizers to induce zero units for elimination.  
- Proposes Capped Batch Normalization (CapNorm) to work with the sparsity-inducing regularizer.  
- Introduces Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad) to handle training-specific challenges of nonparametric networks.  
- Theorem 1 proves nonparametric networks achieve a global minimum at finite dimensionality with the proposed regularizers.  
- Empirical results show nonparametric networks can outperform parametric networks of the same size on several benchmarks.  
- Nonparametric approach achieved higher accuracy than exhaustive random search on two of three datasets.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Strength: Addresses automated architecture selection with a novel, theoretically grounded solution.  
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical foundation and well-motivated, effective empirical evaluation.  
- Strength: Framework is simple, general, and applicable to standard feed-forward networks without special hardware.  
- Strength: AdaRad is well-motivated and normalizing only the orthogonal gradient component is an elegant solution.  
- Paper is well-written with rigorous proofs in the appendix.  
- Suggestion: Clarify limitations, especially poor performance on the convex dataset and conditions for failure.  
- Suggestion: Explicitly compare to recent adaptive-size methods (e.g., Dynamic Network Surgery, Net2Net).  
- Suggestion: Provide detailed computational complexity (time and memory) analysis versus standard methods.  
- Suggestion: Analyze how unit addition rate (e.g., every tenth epoch vs. every epoch) affects performance.  
- Question: Why did nonparametric networks perform worse on the convex dataset—dataset characteristics vs. method effectiveness?  
- Question: Have you explored p=1 vs p=2 regularizers and hybrid schemes for sparse vs dense hardware efficiency trade-offs?  
- Question: Why does validation error keep improving after training error plateaus—is this due to dynamic size adjustment?  
- Question: How do computational requirements scale to much larger datasets, and what modifications are needed for efficiency?  
- Question: Theorem 1 assumes σ(0)=0; how must the approach change for nonlinearities like sigmoid that do not satisfy this?
