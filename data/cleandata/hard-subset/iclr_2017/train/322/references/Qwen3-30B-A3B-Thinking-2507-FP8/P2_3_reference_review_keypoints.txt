- Introduces "nonparametric neural networks" that adapt network size during a single training run using a fan-in p=2 regularizer, CapNorm, and AdaRad, with theoretical guarantees (Theorem 1).  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — contribution addresses automatic model selection and has strong theoretical and empirical support.  
- Strength: Clearly motivates the problem of expensive black-box model selection and proposes a single-run adaptive solution.  
- Strength: Provides a rigorous proof (Theorem 1) showing a global training error minimum at finite dimensionality with fan-in/fan-out regularizers.  
- Strength (empirical, with reservation): Shows nonparametric nets outperform same-size parametric nets on MNIST and rectangles and are competitive with exhaustive search, but fail on the convex dataset.  
- Strength: Introduces methodological innovations (CapNorm and AdaRad) that effectively handle training challenges of nonparametric nets.  
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison and positioning relative to modern NAS methods (e.g., DARTS, ENAS, Zoph et al.).  
- Revise: Expand related-work discussion to explicitly contrast with contemporary NAS approaches.  
- Revise: Analyze and explain the poor performance on the convex dataset and discuss limitations or potential adaptations.  
- Revise: Provide explicit computational efficiency comparisons (total training time) versus black-box model selection.  
- Revise: Clarify practical guidance on setting the regularization parameter λ and its relationship to final network size.  
- Question: How will the approach scale to more complex architectures (e.g., convolutional nets) and what challenges are expected?  
- Question: Why did the convex dataset perform poorly, and what modifications might improve results on such datasets?  
- Question: Is the claim that nonparametric nets outperform same-size scratch-trained nets general or specific to tested datasets/architectures?  
- Question: How should the claim that nonparametric nets can beat black-box methods be reconciled with the convex-dataset failure — is the claim conditional rather than universal?
