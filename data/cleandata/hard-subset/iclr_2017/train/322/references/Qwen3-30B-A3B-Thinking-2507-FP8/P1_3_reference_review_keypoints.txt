- Introduces "nonparametric neural networks" that adaptively select network size during a single training run without prior task info.  
- Dynamically grows/shrinks networks by adding/removing zero units using fan-in/fan-out regularizers, AdaRad optimizer, and CapNorm layer.  
- Theorem 1 proves a global training error minimum at finite dimensionality under the specified regularizers.  

- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Key reasons: addresses a significant model-selection problem, provides rigorous theoretical foundations, and shows compelling empirical advantages.  

- Strong theoretical contribution with a thorough proof and useful Proposition 1 about layer-wise regularization equivalence.  
- Empirical evaluation shows nonparametric networks outperform same-size parametric networks on MNIST and rectangles, and are competitive with exhaustive random search.  
- Scalability validated on a large poker dataset (1.025M examples).  
- Well-positioned in related work and clearly differentiated from pruning and growth methods that require a good starting network.  

- Clarify the f-equivalence concept with an intuitive example to aid reader understanding.  
- Expand analysis of limitations, especially why the method underperforms on the convex dataset.  
- Provide computational cost comparisons (e.g., wall-clock per epoch) between AdaRad and optimizers like RMSprop.  
- Discuss connections and distinctions to recent neural architecture search (NAS) methods.  

- Questions: What dataset properties make the convex dataset challenging and how might the method be adapted?  
- Questions: Would nonlinearities like tanh (σ(0)=0) behave like ReLU in practice and preserve theoretical guarantees?  
- Questions: How does total training time of nonparametric networks compare to parametric ones given network growth during training?  
- Questions: How sensitive are results to the fixed unit addition rate, and is there a principled way to schedule unit additions?  
- Questions: How does the approach scale to large-scale tasks (e.g., ImageNet) and what additional considerations are needed?
