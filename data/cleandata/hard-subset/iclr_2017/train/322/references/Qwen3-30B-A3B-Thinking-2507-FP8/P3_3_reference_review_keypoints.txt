- Introduces "nonparametric neural networks" that dynamically add/remove units during training, with CapNorm and AdaRad innovations and a formalization where weight dimensionality is learned.  
- Proves Theorem 1 showing fan-in/fan-out (p=2) regularization yields a global minimum at finite dimensionality.  
- Experiments show nonparametric nets outperform same-size parametric nets on MNIST and achieve near-perfect results on the poker dataset.  
- Recommendation: weak accept, citing solid theory and practical relevance (avoids expensive repeated training for model selection).  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical foundation (Theorem 1) addressing network size selection.  
- Strength: tackles a meaningful practical problem of automating model size selection during training.  
- Concern: unexplained failure on the convex dataset contradicts the claim that nonparametric nets outperform same-size parametric nets.  
- Concern: missing comparison to standard model selection baselines (e.g., Bayesian optimization), so practical advantage is unclear.  
- Concern: theoretical assumptions (σ(0)=0, self-similarity) are restrictive and applicability to other activations (e.g., Leaky ReLU, Swish) is not discussed.  
- Concern: paper claims AdaRad is cheaper than RMSprop but provides no concrete scaling or timing numbers.  
- Suggestion: provide detailed analysis of why the method fails on the convex dataset to clarify limitations.  
- Suggestion: add comparisons to Bayesian optimization and other model selection techniques to assess accuracy and compute trade-offs.  
- Suggestion: expand computational cost analysis with concrete scaling and timing measurements.  
- Suggestion: clarify distinctions from other growing-network methods (e.g., Wei et al., 2016) and from neural architecture search (e.g., Zoph & Le).  
- Suggestion: discuss and justify practical initialization strategies and robustness when growing nets from scratch.  
- Question: why does the method perform poorly on the convex dataset but well on MNIST and poker—what dataset characteristics cause failure?  
- Question: how does computational cost scale with dataset size and network complexity compared to Bayesian optimization—provide numbers.  
- Question: how to modify the framework for activations that do not satisfy σ(0)=0 (e.g., sigmoid, tanh)?  
- Question: how does this approach relate to and differ from neural architecture search methods like Zoph & Le (2017)?
