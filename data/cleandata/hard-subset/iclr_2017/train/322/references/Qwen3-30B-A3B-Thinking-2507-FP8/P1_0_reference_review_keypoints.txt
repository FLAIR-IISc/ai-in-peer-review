- Introduces "nonparametric neural networks" that automatically add/remove neurons during a single training run to select architecture.  
- Uses fan-in/fan-out regularizers (p=2) and proves (Theorem 1) convergence to a finite-size network minimizing training error.  
- Proposes CapNorm (a normalization layer) and AdaRad (a new optimizer) to enable dynamic resizing during training.  
- Empirical results show nonparametric networks outperform parametric networks of the same size on MNIST and rectangles images.  
- Shows competitive or better performance than exhaustive random search on some datasets and scales to the large poker dataset.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key acceptance reason: addresses a practical problem with a novel, theoretically sound approach.  
- Key acceptance reason: rigorous theoretical contribution (Theorem 1) providing solid foundation.  
- Key acceptance reason: compelling empirical results on two of three benchmarks.  
- Strength: well-motivated problem statement and clear positioning in related work (pruning, growth, Bayesian methods).  
- Strength: detailed analysis of training dynamics and unit addition/removal behavior (Figure 3).  
- Weakness: terminology—calling variable-size networks "nonparametric" conflicts with statistical meaning and needs clarification.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison to recent Neural Architecture Search (NAS) methods.  
- Weakness: missing concrete computational cost numbers for AdaRad versus standard optimizers like Adam/RMSprop.  
- Weakness: poor performance on the convex dataset needs deeper analysis and explanation.  
- Weakness: unit addition schedule (one unit per epoch) is not justified; an ablation on addition rates is suggested.  
- Question: justification or alternative terminology for calling the method "nonparametric"?  
- Question: explanation for failure on the convex dataset and dataset characteristics causing it.  
- Question: concrete runtime/overhead numbers for AdaRad relative to Adam/RMSprop on standard datasets.  
- Question: how would Theorem 1 and the approach adapt to activations with σ(0) ≠ 0 (e.g., sigmoid, tanh)?  
- Question: how would the method leverage prior information about likely network sizes for similar tasks?
