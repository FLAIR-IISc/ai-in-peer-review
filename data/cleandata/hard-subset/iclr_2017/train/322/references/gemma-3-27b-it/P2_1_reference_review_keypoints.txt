- Proposes Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNNs) that dynamically grow/shrink units during training using fan-in regularization and f-equivalence.  
- Introduces CapNorm (a capped normalization layer) and AdaRad (a tailored optimizer) to support dynamic network size.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Empirical results are inconsistent: strong on MNIST, worse on convex dataset, mixed on poker, raising generalization concerns.  
- Added algorithmic and theoretical complexity (AdaRad, CapNorm, proofs) that often yields marginal gains over well-tuned parametric networks.  
- Paper presentation is dense, verbose, and difficult to follow; motivations for some design choices are unclear.  
- Lacks sufficient ablation studies to isolate the contributions of CapNorm and AdaRad.  
- Recommends simplifying presentation and focusing on the core dynamic sizing contribution.  
- Recommends broader, more robust empirical evaluation and stronger baseline hyperparameter tuning.  
- Requests thorough ablations to quantify individual effects of CapNorm and AdaRad.  
- Suggests scalability analysis on larger datasets beyond poker to assess practicality.  
- Asks for clearer connection to pruning techniques and whether NNNs can be combined with or viewed as dynamic pruning.  
- Requests justification for hyperparameter heuristics (e.g., αr = 150λ and AdaRad-M mixing rates).  
- Questions why NNNs perform poorly on the convex dataset and what dataset traits cause this.  
- Asks for an intuitive explanation of CapNorm’s role and why capping batch-norm scaling is necessary.  
- Requests computational overhead comparison of AdaRad vs Adam/RMSprop and quantified efficiency gains from fan-in–level normalization.  
- Asks whether fan-out regularization was explored and, if so, what the results were.
