- Introduces Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNNs) that dynamically grow/shrink networks using a fan-in/fan-out regularizer, CapNorm, and AdaRad.
- Decision: Reject — conceptually interesting with solid theory, but experiments are mixed and presentation/reproducibility are lacking.
- Mixed empirical results: strong on MNIST/rectangles, poor on convex dataset, and OpenML results promising but insufficiently compared to established methods.
- Missing implementation details and no code release, making reproducibility and assessment of practicality difficult.
- Hyperparameter choices (e.g., AdaRad rates) are not well justified and appear arbitrary.
- Added complexity from CapNorm and AdaRad isn’t clearly justified; notation and presentation reduce readability.
- Paper is theory-heavy but does not clearly establish practical advantages or conditions favoring NNNs over BO/NAS.
- Recommendation to release code to increase impact and credibility.
- Request ablation studies to quantify contributions of CapNorm, AdaRad, and the fan-in/fan-out regularizer.
- Ask for thorough hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.
- Request more comprehensive baseline comparisons (e.g., Bayesian optimization, NAS) across datasets.
- Need a detailed scalability analysis: training time and memory scaling with network and dataset size.
- Authors should clearly state assumptions used in the theoretical analysis.
- Need deeper analysis of why NNNs fail on the convex dataset and whether it’s dataset-specific or methodological.
- Questions for authors: provide AdaRad/CapNorm implementation details (running averages), rationale and tuning of hyperparameters, convex dataset experiment variants, memory footprint comparison, and practical trade-offs versus informed methods like Bayesian optimization.
