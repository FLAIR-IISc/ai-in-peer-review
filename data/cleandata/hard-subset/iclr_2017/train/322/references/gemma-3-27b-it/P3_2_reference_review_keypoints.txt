- Paper proposes Nonparametric Neural Networks (NPNs) that dynamically grow/shrink units via a fan-in regularizer and introduces AdaRad and CapNorm for training.  
- Decision: Reject — interesting idea and solid theory but experimental evidence and robustness are insufficient.  
- NPNs outperform on MNIST and rectangles but underperform significantly on the convex dataset, raising generalizability concerns.  
- Scalability claims are weakly supported; poker dataset results are sparse and lack quantitative training-time comparisons.  
- Method appears highly sensitive to hyperparameters (step sizes αr, αφ and regularization λ), with tuning described as manual and potentially expert-dependent.  
- Paper lacks a thorough ablation study to isolate contributions of NPN framework, AdaRad, and CapNorm.  
- Theoretical results (Theorem 1) are strong but practical benefits (speed, final error) are not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Authors note parametric nets can reach higher accuracy faster, and NPNs often require longer training and settle at slightly higher error.  
- Recommendation to evaluate on more diverse datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, ImageNet) to demonstrate generality.  
- Suggest a rigorous ablation comparing AdaRad/CapNorm to standard optimizers and batch normalization.  
- Propose an automated hyperparameter optimization strategy (e.g., Bayesian optimization) to reduce manual tuning.  
- Request more detailed convergence analysis: plot network size over time and relate size to validation error.  
- Ask for a thorough computational cost analysis comparing training and inference time against parametric networks.  
- Clarify definitions and effects of unit addition hyperparameters ν and νfreq and provide precise timing for additions.  
- Question why NPNs fail on the convex dataset and whether dataset properties cause instability.  
- Ask for total training time comparisons on larger datasets (e.g., poker) versus well-tuned parametric networks and scaling behavior.  
- Request guidance or principled methods for selecting/adapting the regularization parameter λ during training.  
- Inquire about applying the NPN framework to other architectures such as CNNs.  
- Request release of code and implementation details to facilitate reproduction.  
- Overall: novel and promising idea but needs stronger, more systematic empirical validation and clearer exposition to be convincing.
