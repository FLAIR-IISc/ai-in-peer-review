- Proposes Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNNs) that dynamically adjust network size during training to reduce manual architecture tuning.  
- Core mechanics: fan-in regularization for shrink/expand, CapNorm normalization layer, and AdaRad optimizer for stable training.  
- Theoretically proves a global training error minimum exists at finite dimensionality under certain assumptions.  
- Experiments on MNIST, a rectangles dataset, and the poker dataset show NNNs can match or outperform hand-tuned networks and scale to larger data.  
- Decision: Accept (Weakly) — novel and promising but not conclusive.  
- Novelty: Dynamic in-training architecture adjustment is a distinct and efficient alternative to training multiple models.  
- Theoretical grounding is a significant contribution despite somewhat restrictive assumptions.  
- CapNorm and AdaRad are well-motivated and appear crucial for stable nonparametric training.  
- Complexity: The framework combines multiple components, making it complex to understand and implement.  
- Limited baselines: Lacks comparison to strong NAS methods (e.g., DARTS, ENAS), weakening empirical claims.  
- Failure case: Poor performance on the convex dataset is unexplained and concerning.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Results depend on αr and αφ, requiring more systematic analysis.  
- Suggestion: Provide a clearer, more intuitive explanation of AdaRad (e.g., why separating radial and angular updates helps).  
- Suggestion: Perform ablation studies to quantify contributions of CapNorm and AdaRad.  
- Suggestion: Compare thoroughly against state-of-the-art NAS methods to better position NNNs.  
- Suggestion: Investigate the convex dataset failure to identify whether regularization, dynamic sizing, or other factors cause it.  
- Suggestion: Include a computational cost analysis versus parametric training and NAS algorithms.  
- Question: Could exploiting sparsity in weights or hardware/software changes improve NNN performance?  
- Question: What strategies were tried for hyperparameter sensitivity, and would automated tuning (e.g., Bayesian optimization) help?  
- Question: Can the framework be extended to dynamically adjust network depth, and what challenges would that introduce?  
- Question: Are there theoretical insights explaining poor NNN performance on datasets like the convex dataset?
