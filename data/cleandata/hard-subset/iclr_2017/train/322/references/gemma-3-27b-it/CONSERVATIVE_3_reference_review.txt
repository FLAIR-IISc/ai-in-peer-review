This paper introduces a framework called Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNs) aiming to automatically select network size during training, dynamically shrinking and expanding the network to achieve better accuracy than fixed-size networks. The authors also present theoretical justification for their approach and novel training components, CapNorm and AdaRad.

Reject. While the idea of dynamically adjusting network size is intriguing, the presentation lacks sufficient rigor and the empirical validation is not convincing, particularly given the novelty of the approach. The paper introduces several new components (CapNorm, AdaRad) without demonstrating a clear advantage over established techniques. The performance gains on the presented datasets are not substantial enough to justify the complexity of the proposed method.

The primary concern lies with the limited and somewhat selective experimental evaluation. The performance gains on MNIST and the rectangles dataset are modest and heavily reliant on the regularization parameter λ. The failure on the convex dataset is particularly concerning, as it suggests the approach doesn’t generalize well to all problem types. The claim that the method outperforms exhaustive random search is not thoroughly supported; the random search configurations and search spaces are not detailed enough to assess the fairness of the comparison. Furthermore, the experimental setup for the larger poker dataset, while interesting, lacks a comprehensive comparison to established methods on that specific dataset. The authors acknowledge that training takes longer, but don't provide a thorough cost-benefit analysis. The theoretical justification, while presented, feels somewhat disconnected from the practical implementation and doesn’t fully address potential issues like instability during dynamic size changes. The introduction of CapNorm feels ad-hoc, motivated by preventing the regularizer from being circumvented by batch normalization, rather than a principled approach to regularization in non-parametric settings. The AdaRad algorithm introduces significant complexity, and the justification for its specific design choices (radial vs. angular gradients) isn't fully convincing.

Additional feedback:

*   **Clarify the advantages of Nonparametric NNs**: The paper would benefit from a more focused discussion of the specific scenarios where this approach is most likely to be beneficial. Why not simply use a well-tuned Bayesian optimization or Neural Architecture Search (NAS) approach?
*   **More detailed experimental setup**: Provide complete details of the hyperparameter search spaces, training procedures, and hardware used for all experiments. A proper ablation study would be highly valuable—how much of the performance gain comes from AdaRad, CapNorm, and the dynamic size adjustment?
*   **Comparison to strong baselines**: Compare against state-of-the-art NAS methods and well-tuned fixed-size networks (trained with techniques like batch normalization and robust optimizers) on all datasets.
*   **Robustness analysis**: Investigate the sensitivity of the method to different initializations, learning rates, and regularization parameters.
*   **Theoretical analysis**: Expand on the theoretical guarantees. Are there conditions under which convergence is guaranteed? What are the limitations of the theoretical results?
*   **Scalability**: Further investigation of scalability is crucial. The results on the poker dataset are a good start, but a more systematic evaluation on a range of larger datasets would be needed.
*   **Explain the choice of ReLU**: Explain further why only self-similar nonlinearities can be used. What happens if a non-self-similar nonlinearity is used?

Questions for the authors:

*   What is the computational overhead of the dynamic size adjustment compared to training a fixed-size network?
*   How does the performance of Nonparametric NNs change as the number of layers increases?
*   What strategies are used to prevent instability during the dynamic size adjustment process?
*   Can you provide a more detailed justification for the choice of AdaRad’s radial and angular gradient updates?
*   Why does the method fail on the convex dataset, and what modifications could address this issue?
