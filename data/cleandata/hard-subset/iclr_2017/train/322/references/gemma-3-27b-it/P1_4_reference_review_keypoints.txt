- Introduces Nonparametric Neural Networks (NPNs) that dynamically grow and shrink during training to auto-select network architecture.
- Theorem: a global training-error minimum exists at finite dimensionality under fan-in/fan-out regularization.
- Proposes CapNorm (normalization) and AdaRad (optimizer) specifically designed for variable-dimension networks.
- Empirical: competitive or superior results on MNIST, rectangles dataset, and an OpenML dataset; rectangles results notably outperform parametric and exhaustive searches.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — promising, theoretically sound approach.
- Novelty/significance: moves beyond train-and-evaluate hyperparameter search by enabling single-run architecture adaptation.
- Theoretical foundation: addresses uncontrolled growth via regularization and provides a well-motivated existence proof.
- Technical contributions: CapNorm and AdaRad are necessary and convincingly designed for NPN challenges.
- Empirical scaling: demonstration on a larger dataset is encouraging but not extensive.
- Writing and presentation: generally clear, with detailed and well-structured algorithm descriptions.
- Needs more analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity for αr, αφ, λ, β, ν to assess robustness.
- Requires detailed computational-cost comparison and profiling of AdaRad vs standard SGD, including scaling with network and dataset size.
- Ablation studies needed to isolate the individual contributions of CapNorm and AdaRad.
- Deeper analysis required for the convex-dataset failure case to determine causes and possible remedies.
- Explore generalization of NPNs to other architectures (e.g., convolutional networks).
- Provide a clearer comparison and discussion of similarities/differences with pruning methods, potentially with empirical comparison.
- Question: How were initial values for αr and αφ chosen, and are they dataset-dependent?
- Question: How did NPN training time and memory on OpenML compare to similarly performing parametric networks?
- Question: How was the optimal λ determined per dataset, and did optimal λ vary significantly across datasets?
- Question: Were there cases where AdaRad-M had advantages or disadvantages relative to AdaRad?
- Question: Does the theoretical global-minimum guarantee translate to practical convergence, or can training still get trapped in local optima?
