## ICLR 2024 Review: Nonparametric Neural Networks

**1. Summary:**

This paper introduces a novel framework called "Nonparametric Neural Networks" (NPNs) that dynamically adjusts network size *during* training, rather than performing a costly hyperparameter search *before* training.  The core idea is to allow the network to grow and shrink by adding/removing units with zero fan-in/fan-out, guided by a regularization term that prevents uncontrolled growth.  The authors provide a theoretical justification for this approach, proposing a new training algorithm (AdaRad) and a normalization layer (CapNorm) tailored for NPNs.  Experimental results on MNIST, a rectangles dataset, and a convex dataset demonstrate that NPNs can achieve competitive or superior accuracy compared to traditionally sized, pre-defined networks, and even outperform results from exhaustive searches. A final experiment on a large dataset (poker) shows promising scalability.

**2. Decision:**

**Reject.** While the ideas presented are interesting and potentially valuable, the paper lacks sufficient clarity and rigor in its presentation, experimental validation, and comparison to existing methods. Specifically, the experimental section, while showing promising results, is not convincingly presented and lacks a thorough comparison with relevant baselines.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

*   **Clarity and Presentation:** The paper suffers from a dense writing style and lacks clear explanations of key concepts. For example, the rationale behind choosing a specific step size in AdaRad or the precise benefit of CapNorm over standard batch normalization is not fully elucidated. The numerous abbreviations and notation, while typical for the field, make the paper challenging to follow.
*   **Experimental Rigor & Baselines:** The experimental setup lacks sufficient detail. The exhaustive random search baseline in section 4.1 feels inadequate. A more comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art AutoML or neural architecture search (NAS) methods (e.g., using libraries like AutoKeras or NASLib) is crucial to demonstrate the true benefit of NPNs. The results on the convex dataset are particularly concerning, showing poorer performance than parametric networks, and the paper offers limited explanation for this. The poker dataset experiment, while demonstrating scalability, is presented with insufficient detail about the hyperparameter search process used for the parametric networks.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** While Theorem 1 is presented, its practical implications are not sufficiently discussed.  The paper states the theorem guarantees a global minimum *under certain conditions*, but doesn't fully explore the sensitivity of performance to deviations from these conditions. Is the performance strongly dependent on the choice of `p` in the regularizer? How robust is the method to noise in the data?
*   **Ablation Studies:** There's a lack of ablation studies to understand the contribution of each component (CapNorm, AdaRad, dynamic resizing) to the overall performance.  For example, what happens if AdaRad is used with standard batch normalization? Or if the network size is fixed and only AdaRad/CapNorm are used?
* **Unclear Motivation for Design Choices:** Why is the initial network size chosen as ten units per layer? What is the reasoning behind adding one unit per epoch? These choices seem arbitrary and their impact on performance isn't investigated.

**4. Additional Feedback (for potential improvement):**

*   **Expand Related Work:** The related work section feels somewhat superficial. A more in-depth discussion of existing dynamic network architectures, pruning methods, and AutoML techniques would provide valuable context.
*   **Provide Pseudo-code:** Including more detailed pseudo-code for AdaRad, especially the rotation step and the unit addition/removal process, would greatly improve clarity.
*   **Visualize Network Dynamics:** Visualizing how the network size evolves during training (e.g., plotting the number of active units over time) would provide valuable insights into the learning process.
*   **Sensitivity Analysis:** Perform a sensitivity analysis of hyperparameters (λ, αφ, β, ν) to understand their impact on performance.
*   **Error Analysis:** Investigate *why* NPNs perform poorly on the convex dataset.  Is there something inherent in the dataset that prevents them from learning effectively?

**5. Questions for the Authors:**

*   Could you elaborate on the choice of initial network size (10 units per layer) and the unit addition rate (1 unit per epoch)? What are the implications of changing these parameters?
*   How does the performance of NPNs compare to more established AutoML methods (e.g., AutoKeras, NASLib) on the datasets used in your experiments?
*   Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the benefits of CapNorm compared to standard batch normalization in the context of NPNs?
*   What is the computational cost overhead of using AdaRad compared to standard SGD?
*   In the poker dataset experiment, what range of hyperparameters was searched for the parametric networks, and how many random configurations were evaluated?
*   Why do the NPNs perform poorly on the convex dataset? Is this due to the nature of the dataset or a limitation of the approach?
