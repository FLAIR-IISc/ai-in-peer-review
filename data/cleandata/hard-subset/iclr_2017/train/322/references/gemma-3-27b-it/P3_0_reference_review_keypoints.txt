- Summary: Proposes Nonparametric Neural Networks (NPNs) that grow/shrink during training using a fan-in regularizer, CapNorm, and AdaRad, with a theoretical existence result and experiments on MNIST, synthetic rectangles, and a convex dataset.

- Decision: Reject — interesting ideas but insufficient empirical validation, unclear presentation, and problematic convex dataset results.

- Limited baselines & modern comparisons: Lacks strong comparisons to NAS and modern optimizers (e.g., AdamW) and only limited hyperparameter searches for baselines.

- Unconvincing CapNorm justification: Motivation for capping standard deviation is hand-wavy and lacks empirical proof that CapNorm is essential or optimal.

- Problematic convex dataset results: NPNs perform substantially worse on a simple convex dataset, raising concerns about generalizability and lacking a convincing explanation.

- Lack of ablation studies: No systematic removal/replacement of components (CapNorm, AdaRad, regularization) to measure individual contributions.

- Complex algorithm presentation: AdaRad-M is introduced late with limited clarity, and algorithmic descriptions are hard to follow.

- Request stronger empirical validation: Recommend comparisons to modern NAS methods and evaluation across more architectures and datasets.

- Clarify and ablate CapNorm: Provide deeper theoretical/empirical justification and experiments showing its impact.

- Analyze convex dataset failure: Investigate whether the problem stems from the regularizer, dynamic adaptation, or other factors, and propose fixes.

- Perform systematic ablations: Test contributions of CapNorm, AdaRad, regularization strength, and other design choices.

- Simplify presentation: Streamline algorithm descriptions and use visuals to illustrate growth/shrink dynamics.

- Detail hyperparameter tuning: Give specifics on tuning for both NPNs and baselines to improve reproducibility.

- Discuss computational cost: Provide a detailed analysis of training time and overhead from dynamic adaptation.

- Question — convex dataset: What specific aspects cause NPN failure and what modifications could address it?

- Question — sensitivity to seeds: How sensitive are results to initialization and are outcomes consistent across runs?

- Question — computational cost comparison: Provide detailed training-time and overhead comparisons versus static networks.

- Question — angular step size (αφ): What is the impact on final architecture/performance and how to choose it principledly?

- Question — stability: How does the framework prevent oscillations or divergence during dynamic growth/shrinkage?
