- Introduces Nonparametric Neural Networks (NPNs) that dynamically grow/shrink during training using unit addition/removal, regularization, AdaRad optimizer, and CapNorm.  
- Decision: Reject due to insufficient clarity, rigor, and comparative evaluation.  
- Writing is dense and key methods (e.g., AdaRad step size, CapNorm benefits) are not clearly explained.  
- Experimental setup lacks detail and uses inadequate baselines; needs comparisons with AutoML/NAS methods.  
- Convex dataset results are worse than parametric networks with little explanation offered.  
- Poker dataset scalability claim is under-documented—hyperparameter search for parametric baselines is unclear.  
- Theorem 1 is presented but its practical implications and sensitivity to assumptions (e.g., choice of p, noise robustness) are not explored.  
- No ablation studies to isolate the contributions of CapNorm, AdaRad, and dynamic resizing.  
- Key design choices (initial size = 10 units/layer, add 1 unit/epoch) appear arbitrary and are not justified or tested.  
- Related work section is shallow; needs deeper coverage of dynamic nets, pruning, and AutoML literature.  
- Paper should include detailed pseudocode for AdaRad, the rotation step, and the unit add/remove process.  
- Recommend visualizing network size evolution (active units over time) to illustrate dynamics.  
- Recommend sensitivity analysis of hyperparameters (λ, αφ, β, ν) to assess robustness.  
- Recommend error analysis to diagnose poor performance on the convex dataset.  
- Question: justify initial network size and unit addition rate and describe effects of varying them.  
- Question: how do NPNs compare empirically to AutoKeras, NASLib, or other AutoML/NAS tools?  
- Question: clarify the specific benefits of CapNorm over standard batch normalization in NPNs.  
- Question: what is the computational overhead of AdaRad compared to standard SGD?  
- Question: what hyperparameter ranges and number of random configurations were used for parametric networks in the poker experiment?  
- Question: why do NPNs perform poorly on the convex dataset—dataset property or method limitation?
