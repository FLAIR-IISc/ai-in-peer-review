## ICLR 2024 Review: Nonparametric Neural Networks

**1. Summary**

This paper introduces a novel framework called Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNNs) for automatically selecting network size during training. The core idea is to dynamically shrink and expand the network, guided by a regularizer, eliminating the need for costly hyperparameter search. The authors propose a novel regularization technique based on fan-in/fan-out penalties, a new normalization layer (CapNorm), and a training algorithm (AdaRad) to facilitate this dynamic adjustment. They theoretically justify their approach and demonstrate its effectiveness on several datasets, including MNIST, rectangles, and a large-scale dataset from OpenML, achieving competitive or superior performance compared to conventionally-trained networks.

**2. Decision & Key Reasons**

**Reject.** While the idea is conceptually interesting and the theoretical grounding is a strong point, the experimental results are mixed, and the presentation lacks sufficient clarity regarding implementation details and reproducibility. The jump in complexity from well-established datasets to a large OpenML dataset without a more thorough analysis of the challenges and benefits feels somewhat disconnected.

**3. Supporting Arguments**

* **Mixed Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates improvement on MNIST and rectangles images, but performs poorly on the convex dataset. The explanation for this discrepancy feels insufficient. The success on the larger OpenML dataset is encouraging, but lacks the detailed comparison with a wider range of established methods, particularly for large datasets.
* **Lack of Implementation Details:** Crucial implementation details are missing. For example, the code is not provided, making it hard to assess the practicality and reproducibility of the results. The choice of specific hyperparameters (e.g., learning rates, mixing rates in AdaRad) are not fully justified and appear somewhat arbitrarily chosen.  The paper states "growing nets from scratch is a good test case," but doesn’t adequately address the inherent difficulties of this setup.
* **Complexity & Readability:** The paper is densely written and requires significant effort to follow. The introduction of CapNorm and AdaRad adds layers of complexity that aren’t fully justified by a clear demonstration of their necessity. The notation is sometimes cumbersome.
* **Theoretical Focus vs. Practical Impact:** The paper dedicates a significant portion to theoretical justifications. While valuable, the practical advantages and limitations of NNNs aren't thoroughly explored. It remains unclear under what conditions NNNs would be the preferred choice over existing techniques like Bayesian Optimization or Neural Architecture Search.

**4. Additional Feedback (For Improvement)**

* **Code Release:**  Releasing the code would significantly increase the impact and credibility of the work.
* **Ablation Studies:** Conduct ablation studies to assess the contribution of each component (CapNorm, AdaRad, fan-in/fan-out regularizer) to the overall performance.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:**  Provide a more thorough analysis of the sensitivity of the results to the choice of hyperparameters.
* **Comparison to Baselines:** A more comprehensive comparison to relevant baselines (e.g., Bayesian optimization, NAS) on all datasets, including larger datasets, is needed.
* **Scalability Analysis:**  Provide a more detailed scalability analysis. How does the training time scale with network size and dataset size?  How does it compare to training traditional networks?
* **Clarify Assumptions:** Clearly state the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis.  
* **Expand Convex Dataset Analysis:** Provide a more in-depth analysis of why NNNs perform poorly on the convex dataset. Is it due to the dataset's properties or limitations of the approach?

**5. Questions for the Authors**

* **Reproducibility:** Given the lack of code, can you provide more details regarding the specific implementation of AdaRad and CapNorm? Specifically, how are the running averages maintained and updated?
* **Hyperparameter Selection:** What is the rationale behind the chosen values for the hyperparameters (αr, αφ, β, ν, νfreq)? Were any hyperparameter optimization techniques used?
* **Convex Dataset:** What specific characteristics of the convex dataset hinder the performance of NNNs? Have you experimented with different regularization strategies or network architectures on this dataset?
* **Scalability:** How does the memory footprint of NNNs compare to that of traditional networks with similar performance?
* **Practical Considerations:**  In a real-world scenario where prior information about the task is available, would you still recommend using NNNs over more informed approaches like Bayesian optimization? What are the trade-offs?
