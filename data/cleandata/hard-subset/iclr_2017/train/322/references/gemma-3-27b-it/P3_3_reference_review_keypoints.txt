- Paper proposes Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNNs) that grow/shrink during training with CapNorm and AdaRad, claiming theoretical guarantees and competitive performance.
- Decision: Reject — idea is interesting but experimental evidence doesn’t convincingly justify added complexity.
- Inconsistent results: poor performance on the convex dataset undermines broader claims; authors don’t explain why.
- Dataset-dependent gains: improvements on MNIST/rectangles are modest and not uniformly present across tasks.
- Baseline comparison weak: only grid/random search used; modern HPO methods (Bayesian optimization, PBT) are not compared.
- Computational cost unclear: AdaRad’s expense is acknowledged but not compared to cost of advanced HPO methods.
- Initialization concerns: random initialization of newly added units is not detailed; its effect on learning/performance is unclear.
- Limited scalability demonstration: poker experiment is brief and lacks comparisons to state-of-the-art methods for that dataset.
- Missing ablations: need studies isolating contributions of CapNorm and AdaRad, and comparisons to batch norm / standard optimizers.
- Theoretical limitations: theorem requires specific conditions (nonlinearities, regularization) that restrict applicability and need clearer discussion.
- Convex dataset failure needs investigation: potential link to dataset linearity or other characteristics should be analyzed.
- Runtime analysis lacking: provide detailed training time and compute comparisons versus parametric networks with HPO.
- Non-stationarity concerns: changing architecture during training could cause instability and extra tuning; practical guidance is absent.
- Question on automatic size control: how does algorithm prevent unbounded growth or collapse to trivial size in extreme cases?
- Sensitivity questions: clarify choice and sensitivity of initial angular/radial step sizes (αr, αφ) and regularization strength (λ).
- Poker experiment resources: report GPUs, training time, and comparison to a similarly performing parametric network.
- Unit addition stability: explain handling of noisy gradients/instability when newly added units initially don’t contribute.
