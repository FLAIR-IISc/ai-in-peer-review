- Introduces Nonparametric Neural Networks (NPNs) that dynamically grow/shrink network size during training using a fan-in regularizer, with new methods CapNorm and AdaRad and some theoretical guarantees.  
- Decision: Reject — interesting idea but experimental results are not convincing enough for acceptance.  
- Inconsistent performance: NPNs outperform on MNIST and Rectangles but underperform on the Convex dataset, raising generalization concerns.  
- Marginal gains: Improvements over well-tuned parametric networks are often small, questioning the added complexity.  
- Lack of robustness in comparisons: Parametric networks’ tuning details (search space/methods) are insufficient, making fairness of comparisons unclear.  
- Theoretical limitations: Guarantees require restrictive conditions (fan-in/fan-out regularizers, specific nonlinearities) whose practical impact is underexplored.  
- Experimental procedure concerns: Adding one unit per epoch may be slow and sensitive to the unit-addition hyperparameter, with only superficial discussion of these sensitivities.  
- Need deeper analysis of Convex failure: Authors should investigate optimization landscape, regularizer effects, or task mismatch to explain poor Convex results.  
- Require more comprehensive tuning of baselines: Provide full search space and tuning methods for parametric networks to ensure fair comparison.  
- Ablation studies needed: Isolate contributions of CapNorm and AdaRad to quantify their individual effects.  
- Scalability analysis missing: More thorough evaluation of training time and memory scaling with dataset/network size beyond the poker dataset.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Analyze sensitivity to αr, αφ, λ and provide practical selection guidelines.  
- Question on computational overhead: Quantify NPN training cost vs standard nets and how it scales with data/model complexity.  
- Question on hyperparameter sensitivity: How dependent are results on αr and αφ choices?  
- Question on prior knowledge: Does NPN performance degrade when some prior estimate of network size is available?
