## ICLR 2024 Review: Nonparametric Neural Networks

**1. Summary:**

This paper introduces a novel framework called “Nonparametric Neural Networks” (NPNs) aimed at dynamically selecting network size during training, rather than relying on pre-defined architectures or black-box hyperparameter optimization. The core idea is to allow the network to grow and shrink by adding or removing units based on a fan-in regularizer, effectively searching for an optimal size *during* training. The authors provide theoretical justification for this approach (guaranteeing a global minimum under certain conditions), introduce new training mechanisms (CapNorm and AdaRad), and demonstrate competitive results on several datasets, including a large-scale dataset from OpenML.

**2. Decision:**

**Reject.** While the paper presents an interesting and theoretically grounded idea, the experimental results are not convincing enough to warrant acceptance. Specifically, the performance gains are inconsistent across datasets, and the benefits over well-tuned parametric networks are often marginal. The complexity introduced by the framework doesn’t consistently translate to a clear advantage, and the results on the convex dataset are particularly concerning.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

*   **Inconsistent Performance:** The results in Figure 2 show that NPNs outperform parametric networks on MNIST and the Rectangles dataset, but *underperform* on the Convex dataset. This inconsistency raises questions about the generalizability of the approach. The authors acknowledge this but don't provide a satisfying explanation. Why does the method fail on a relatively simple dataset like Convex?
*   **Marginal Gains:** Even on datasets where NPNs perform better, the improvement over well-tuned parametric networks is often small. The authors acknowledge this, stating that the best NPN is "close in performance" to the best parametric network on MNIST. This suggests that the added complexity of NPNs may not be justified by the achieved gains.
*   **Lack of Robustness:** The authors perform a grid search and random search for parametric networks but don’t provide comprehensive details of the parameter space searched. It's difficult to assess whether the parametric networks were truly optimized or if the comparison is unfair.
*   **Theoretical Justification Limitations:** While the theoretical guarantee is valuable, the conditions required (fan-in/fan-out regularizers, specific nonlinearities) are restrictive. The practical impact of these restrictions on performance is not fully explored.
* **Experimental Setup:** The approach of adding one unit per epoch/fraction thereof appears potentially slow and sensitive to hyperparameter tuning of the unit addition rate. The discussion of these sensitivities feels superficial.

**4. Additional Feedback (for potential improvement):**

*   **Further Investigate Convex Dataset Failure:** A deeper analysis of why NPNs fail on the Convex dataset is crucial. This could involve examining the optimization landscape, the role of the fan-in regularizer, or the suitability of the proposed framework for this specific type of problem.
*   **More Comprehensive Parametric Network Tuning:** Provide more details about the search space and methods used to tune the parametric networks. A more rigorous comparison is needed to demonstrate the true benefits of NPNs.
*   **Ablation Studies:** Conduct ablation studies to assess the individual contributions of CapNorm and AdaRad. How much of the performance improvement (when observed) can be attributed to each component?
*   **Scalability Analysis:** While the poker dataset experiment is a good start, a more thorough analysis of the scalability of the NPN framework is needed. How does the training time and memory usage scale with the size of the dataset and the complexity of the network?
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Analyze the sensitivity of NPNs to hyperparameters such as αr, αφ, and the regularization parameter λ. Provide guidelines for selecting appropriate values for these parameters.

**5. Questions for the Authors:**

*   Why do NPNs underperform on the Convex dataset, and what modifications could be made to address this issue?
*   Can you provide more details about the search space and methods used to tune the parametric networks?
*   How sensitive are the results to the choice of hyperparameters, particularly αr and αφ?
*   What is the computational overhead of NPNs compared to training standard parametric networks? How does this overhead scale with the size of the dataset and the complexity of the network?
*   The claim is that NPNs are advantageous when *no prior information* about the dataset is available. Does the performance degrade when some prior knowledge *is* available (e.g., a rough estimate of the optimal network size)?
