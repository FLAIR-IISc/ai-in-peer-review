- Introduces Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNNs) that grow/shrink units during training, with CapNorm and AdaRad, and a theorem guaranteeing a finite-dimensional global minimum under regularization.
- Decision: Reject — interesting idea but experiments unconvincing and theoretical impact limited.
- Limited empirical advantage: inconsistent improvements across datasets (stronger on rectangles, worse on convex, marginal gains on MNIST).
- Theoretical contribution lacks depth: Theorem 1 shows existence of a minimum but gives little guidance on how to find it or implications of regularizer choice.
- Complexity and scalability concerns: AdaRad’s computational cost and overall overhead unclear, and no detailed comparison to NAS or other search methods.
- Insufficient ablation studies: paper does not isolate the contributions of CapNorm and AdaRad (e.g., AdaRad without CapNorm).
- Limited baseline comparison: comparison to random search is weak and not budget-matched.
- Expand empirical evaluation to larger, more complex datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to demonstrate scalability and generalizability.
- Strengthen theoretical analysis with intuitive explanations of Theorem 1 and exploration of regularization strength effects.
- Conduct comprehensive ablation studies to assess individual and combined contributions of CapNorm and AdaRad.
- Provide a thorough comparison with NAS methods, discussing computational cost, search space, and performance trade-offs.
- Elaborate on hyperparameter sensitivity (learning rate, radial/angular step sizes, beta values) and practical recommendations.
- Explore alternative regularizers beyond fan-in/fan-out and analyze their effects.
- Release code to improve reproducibility and impact.
- Why did NNN fail on the convex dataset—what dataset properties caused the failure?
- How should αr be set in practice, and how does it affect training dynamics and final performance?
- What is the computational cost of AdaRad compared to Adam/RMSprop, especially as network size grows?
- Why were self-similar nonlinearities chosen and are they critical beyond regularization reasons?
- How does the framework cope when the optimal network size is very large—does starting small create a bottleneck?
- How could NNN be adapted for lifelong learning to grow with new data while avoiding catastrophic forgetting?
