- Introduces Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNNs) that dynamically grow/shrink units using a fan-in regularizer, AdaRad optimizer, and CapNorm, with theoretical justification and mixed empirical results.
- Decision: Reject — interesting ideas and solid theory but experimental evidence insufficient for acceptance.
- Experiments show inconsistent performance across datasets (good on MNIST/rectangles, poor on convex), raising robustness concerns.
- Practical benefits are limited: method still requires substantial hyperparameter tuning and gains don’t clearly justify added complexity.
- Methodological complexity: dynamic resizing, CapNorm, and AdaRad increase complexity compared to standard training.
- Missing ablation studies to quantify contributions of CapNorm, AdaRad, and resizing rules.
- Scalability discussion is weak and the single scalability experiment feels tacked on.
- Recommend focusing the paper on one key benefit (e.g., model selection with limited prior knowledge) to strengthen narrative.
- Request more thorough analysis of training dynamics: network size evolution, size–performance relationship, and patterns in added/removed units.
- Suggest comparative evaluation against modern AutoML/differentiable architecture search methods to show clear advantages.
- Advise simplifying presentation of CapNorm and AdaRad with intuitive explanations and visual aids.
- Ask authors to clarify motivations for design choices (regularization, step sizes, add/remove frequency).
- Urge investigation into why NNNs fail on the convex dataset (dataset simplicity, regularization, or other factors).
- Question sensitivity of the regularization parameter lambda and prospects for automatic tuning within NNNs.
- Ask for tuning strategy and sensitivity analysis for AdaRad hyperparameters (alpha_r, alpha_f, beta).
- Request detailed analysis of computational cost versus standard parametric training plus black-box optimization, including resizing overhead vs iteration savings.
- Ask what happens with non-self-similar nonlinearities (e.g., tanh) and whether performance degrades.
- Request clarification on choice and role of the poker dataset and how it challenges NNNs.
