- Summary: Proposes Nonparametric Neural Networks (NPNs) that grow/shrink during training using a fan-in regularizer, with CapNorm and AdaRad, claiming comparable accuracy with fewer parameters.  
- Decision: Reject — intriguing theory but empirical variability, complexity, and limited scalability undermine acceptance.

Supporting arguments:
- Inconsistent performance across datasets; strong on MNIST but worse on convex dataset and mixed elsewhere.  
- Limited scalability analysis; training-time and resource overheads not quantitatively compared to fixed networks.  
- High methodological complexity and many hyperparameters (CapNorm, AdaRad, αr, αφ, β, ν) lacking sensitivity analysis or practical tuning guidance.  
- Theoretical guarantee (finite dimensionality) does not ensure practical efficiency or optimal architectures in practice.  
- Missing comparisons to modern NAS methods to contextualize benefits over recent automated architecture search.

Additional feedback / required experiments:
- Provide comprehensive hyperparameter sensitivity and robustness analysis (e.g., box plots across settings).  
- Run detailed scalability experiments on larger, more complex datasets with training-time and resource comparisons.  
- Include ablation studies isolating contributions of CapNorm, AdaRad, and dynamic resizing.  
- Compare against state-of-the-art NAS algorithms on at least one dataset.  
- Explore and report effects of alternative regularizers beyond fan-in.  
- Discuss practical implementation/deployment challenges (hardware/software needs).  
- Improve clarity of algorithmic and theoretical sections with simpler explanations and intuition.

Questions for the authors:
- How sensitive are results to random seed and are experiments reproducible across seeds?  
- What is the computational overhead of dynamic resizing versus fixed-size training?  
- Why do NPNs perform poorly on the convex dataset; what dataset properties cause failure?  
- Have other regularization techniques been tried and with what outcomes?  
- Are learned NPN architectures qualitatively different from architectures found by other methods?  
- Are there simpler alternatives to AdaRad that achieve similar performance?

Conclusion: The idea is novel and theoretically grounded but requires stronger, more consistent empirical evidence, scalability analysis, and clarity to be convincing.
