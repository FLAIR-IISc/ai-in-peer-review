- Introduces Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNNs) that dynamically adjust network size during training using fan-in regularization, CapNorm, and AdaRad.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions based on novelty, theory, and promising empirical results.  
- Novelty: Dynamically changing architecture during training is a fresh alternative to costly black-box architecture search.  
- Theoretical justification: Proven existence of a global minimum for NNNs with fan-in/fan-out regularization.  
- Empirical validation: Competitive or superior performance on several datasets, notably strong results on a large poker dataset.  
- Limited gains: Improvements on small datasets (MNIST, rectangles) are modest.  
- Technical contributions: CapNorm and AdaRad tailored to dynamic networks are well-motivated and useful.  
- Writing: Paper is generally clear and well-explained.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Needs more systematic analysis of λ, αr, αφ, β beyond grid search.  
- Computational cost: Request for detailed wall-clock training time comparisons (e.g., vs. Adam) and runtime vs. performance plots.  
- Limitations: The poor performance on the convex dataset should be analyzed and discussed more thoroughly.  
- Visualization: Recommend plots showing how network size (units per layer) evolves during training.  
- Questions for authors: justify self-similar nonlinearity choice and its drawbacks; explain AdaRad fan-in normalization efficiency; report final architectures and patterns on poker dataset; identify λ ranges and causes for convex underperformance; quantify cost of adding/removing units.  
- Overall: Strong paper with solid theory and scalable empirical promise; addressing suggested analyses would strengthen impact.
