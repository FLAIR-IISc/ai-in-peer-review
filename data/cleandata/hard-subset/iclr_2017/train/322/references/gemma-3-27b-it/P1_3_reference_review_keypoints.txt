- Proposes Nonparametric Neural Networks (NPNs) that grow/shrink during training using fan-in regularization, CapNorm normalization, and AdaRad optimizer, with a supporting theorem and experiments.

- Decision: Reject; encouraged revision and resubmission due to weak empirical rigor.

- Inconsistent empirical performance: strong on MNIST/rectangles but notably worse on the convex dataset, raising generalizability concerns.

- Insufficient baseline comparison: parametric grid search vs a subjectively chosen “typical” NPN; random search details underreported.

- Missing ablation studies: unclear individual contributions of CapNorm and AdaRad versus standard alternatives.

- High complexity and many hyperparameters (αr, αφ, β, ν, νfreq) may negate the benefit of automating architecture selection.

- Scalability claims are mainly theoretical and lack empirical validation on large diverse datasets beyond the poker set.

- Theorem on global minima is interesting but its practical relevance is unclear due to restrictive conditions on nonlinearities.

- Need clearer statement of when NPNs are expected to outperform parametric networks (task/dataset regimes).

- Recommended expanded experiments: more datasets (e.g., image/NLP), deeper hyperparameter searches for both methods, and reporting of standard deviations.

- Recommend ablation studies to quantify the impact of CapNorm and AdaRad and comparisons to batch norm/other optimizers.

- Suggest deeper training-dynamics analysis with visualizations of weight distributions, activations, and growth trajectories.

- Request explicit discussion of limitations and failure modes across datasets and architectures.

- Urge public code release to enable reproducibility.

- Questions for authors: explain why NPNs fail on the convex dataset and what dataset characteristics cause this.

- Questions: justify the subjective selection of a “typical” NPN and propose systematic alternatives (e.g., averaging across runs).

- Questions: provide detailed computational cost comparisons as networks grow relative to parametric training.

- Questions: clarify practical implications of the theorem and identify common architectures that satisfy its conditions.

- Questions: report sensitivity to angular/radial step sizes (αr, αφ) and recommend tuning strategies.
