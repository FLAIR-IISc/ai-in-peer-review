- Proposal: Introduces Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNNs) that grow/shrink during training using a fan-in regularizer, AdaRad optimizer, and CapNorm layer.  
- Decision: Recommend rejection due to inconsistent experimental results and unclear practical advantages.  
- Inconsistent performance: NNNs outperform on MNIST and rectangles but underperform on the convex dataset, raising robustness concerns.  
- Limited experimental scope: Evaluations are narrow (poker, MNIST, rectangles) and exhaustive comparisons are only on some datasets.  
- Complexity and clarity: Method involves many novel components (AdaRad, CapNorm) and dense descriptions; some design choices (e.g., unit initialization) seem arbitrary.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Performance appears sensitive to λ and αφ, implying substantial tuning despite claims of automated selection.  
- Theoretical result limited: The global-minimum theorem is appreciated but its practical implications and assumptions are not well explained.  
- Ablation needed: Must isolate contributions of AdaRad vs. CapNorm to show which components drive gains.  
- Robustness analysis requested: Investigate sensitivity to initializations, learning rates, and dataset properties, and explain poor convex performance.  
- Computational cost: Provide detailed timing and resource comparisons to standard training.  
- Improve clarity: Simplify descriptions, use intuitive notation, and add diagrams/illustrative examples for AdaRad and CapNorm.  
- Broader evaluation: Test on a wider range of real-world, larger-scale datasets (e.g., ImageNet).  
- Compare to AutoML: More comprehensive comparisons to AutoML/architecture search methods are needed.  
- Question — initialization: Why initialize new units with random fan-in and zero fan-out; were alternatives tried?  
- Question — convex dataset: What explains NNNs’ poor performance on the convex dataset—regularizer or data characteristics?  
- Question — computational cost comparison: How does NNN training time compare to training a similarly performing parametric network with Adam?  
- Question — theorem implications: Clarify practical guarantees provided by the theoretical result.  
- Question — stability/reproducibility: What measures ensure stability and reproducibility given hyperparameter sensitivity?  
- Conclusion: Interesting and theoretically grounded idea but currently hampered by inconsistent results, complexity, and limited evaluation, so not ready for acceptance.
