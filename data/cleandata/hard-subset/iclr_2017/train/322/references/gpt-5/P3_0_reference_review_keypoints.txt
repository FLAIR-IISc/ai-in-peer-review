- Proposes nonparametric neural networks that adapt hidden-layer widths during training by adding zero-output units and pruning units via structured Lp penalties.
- Theorem: with fan-in or fan-out group penalties (λ>0, 1≤p<∞), the training objective attains a global minimum at finite dimensionality.
- Shows scaling-invariance for self-similar nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU), allowing consolidation of layer-wise regularization strengths.
- Introduces CapNorm, a capped batch-normalization variant designed to prevent trivial rescaling that would negate the regularizer.
- Introduces AdaRad optimizer that decomposes gradients into radial and angular components, normalizing only the angular part to protect new units from premature pruning; gives a momentum variant AdaRad-M.
- Experiments on MNIST, rectangles, convex, and poker show networks can grow/prune to data-driven widths and sometimes beat parametric baselines, but performance is mixed.
- Decision: Reject.
- Primary rejection reason 1: empirical evidence is limited to small MLPs on simple datasets; no evaluation on modern architectures or challenging benchmarks.
- Primary rejection reason 2: key ablations and comparisons are missing, making it unclear which components drive gains and raising fairness concerns in baselines and tuning.
- Novelty/theory: existence result and scaling proposition are sound and useful, but do not guarantee training dynamics will reach those optima; proofs are heavy.
- Methodology: adding zero-output units and group-l2 fan-in pruning are sensible; AdaRad’s radial/angular idea addresses new-unit learning vs shrinkage tension.
- CapNorm trade-off: removing learnable BN affine parameters may reduce representational capacity, but this is unexplored empirically.
- Empirical limitations: no convnets/ResNets/CIFAR/ImageNet or sequence models; poker results lack comparisons to strong tabular baselines like GBDTs.
- Missing baselines: no proximal group-l2 baseline, inadequate comparisons to RMSprop/Adam with group normalization or BN variants, and unclear HPO/exhaustive search fairness.
- Ablations missing: AdaRad vs standard optimizers, CapNorm vs BN/WeightNorm/LayerNorm variants, p=1 vs p=2, fan-in vs fan-out penalties, addition schedule effects, and activation function dependence.
- Reporting/statistics concerns: uses “typical representatives” and ranges instead of mean/median with CIs and standardized training budgets; runtime/compute overhead not reported.
- Writing/presentation: generally clear but long and dense proofs, typos, reference issues, and pseudocode readability could be improved.
- Constructive suggestions: add convnet experiments, thorough ablations, proximal-group-l2 baselines, runtime/compute reporting, standardized statistics across seeds, and clearer claims about HPO comparisons.
- Theory/practice bridge: analyze convergence conditions, sensitivity to λ/αr/αφ, and provide heuristics or adaptive schemes for λ tuning.
- CapNorm analysis: empirically validate that CapNorm doesn’t unduly limit learning and consider alternatives that allow affine params without neutralizing regularization.
- Reproducibility: release code and exact hyperparameters.
- Key questions for authors: sensitivity and adaptivity of λ/αr/αφ; comparisons to proximal group-l2; CapNorm vs BN variants; performance on convnets and engineering costs for channel add/remove; effect of non-self-similar activations; numerical thresholding for zero-unit removal; reasons for small-width preference on the convex dataset.
