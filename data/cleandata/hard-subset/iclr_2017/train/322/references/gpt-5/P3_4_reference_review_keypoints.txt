- Paper proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that adapt network width during one training run by adding/removing units.  
- Key ingredients: group-structured ℓ2 regularizer on fan-ins/outs to zero-out units for removal.  
- Theorem 1: existence of a global minimizer at finite width under the proposed regularizer.  
- AdaRad optimizer: decomposes per-unit gradients into radial and angular components and normalizes only the angular part; AdaRad-M (momentum) in appendix.  
- CapNorm: modified batch normalization that subtracts mean, caps std-rescaling at ≥1, and removes γ/β to avoid negating the regularizer.  
- Experiments on MNIST, rectangles, convex, and a large "poker" dataset claim sometimes better accuracy per parameter and occasional absolute gains vs random search in a single run.  
- Decision: Reject — idea interesting but experiments are mixed, novelty limited relative to prior structured sparsity/dynamic growth work, and several choices (CapNorm, AdaRad, protocol) lack validation.  
- Positioning/novelty concern: group-lasso/unit pruning and dynamic growth have substantial prior work and the paper does not clearly delineate new contributions versus existing methods.  
- AdaRad resemblance: angular/radial update idea echoes weight normalization, path-SGD, Riemannian approaches, and per-group normalization like RMSprop/Adam; missing ablations against these.  
- Theory limitation: Theorem proves existence but has limited practical import for navigating nonconvex landscapes or predicting selected size; bounds’ dependence on hyperparameters is unclear.  
- Proposition 1 mainly formalizes known rescaling invariances for ReLU networks.  
- Methodology limitation: experiments are limited to small fully-connected benchmarks, no convolutional experiments where structured sparsity is most relevant.  
- Mixed empirical results: wins on some tasks (MNIST, rectangles) but underperformance on others (convex), often selecting models that are too small.  
- Fairness concerns: baselines use BN with λ=0 vs NP method using group-ℓ2 and CapNorm; regularization/normalization interplay may bias comparisons.  
- Protocol concerns: complex NP schedule (rewinding, annealing) not matched for baselines; no wall-clock or memory comparisons to substantiate efficiency claims.  
- Poker dataset suspicious: reported 0% test error on 100k samples is unusual, dataset/task definition and leakage/preprocessing are unclear.  
- CapNorm not properly ablated against BN variants (no γ/β), WeightNorm, Layer/GroupNorm, or frozen BN statistics.  
- AdaRad not ablated vs Adam/RMSprop with per-fan-in normalization or weight normalization; AdaRad-M not evaluated systematically.  
- Reproducibility issues: manual choices (representative run selection, addition rates, rewinds), no code release, long pseudocode/proofs and formatting errors.  
- Suggested ablations: CapNorm vs BN variants and WeightNorm; AdaRad vs Adam/RMSprop with per-fan-in normalization; sensitivity to unit schedule, αr/αφ, and λ.  
- Suggested stronger baselines: compare to modern structured sparsity/pruning and dynamic methods (Network Slimming, Dynamic Network Surgery, ℓ0/variational channel pruning).  
- Suggested experiments: include convolutional architectures on CIFAR/ImageNet-scale subsets to demonstrate practicality.  
- Reporting suggestions: clarify poker task and leakage checks, report compute/memory/wall-clock overheads, and provide standard statistical reporting over seeds.  
- Theoretical/positioning suggestions: discuss connections to path-norm/scale-invariance and optimization on product-of-spheres; position AdaRad relative to these theories.  
- Reproducibility request: release code and configs, remove manual representative-run selection.  
- Questions for authors: clarify poker dataset preprocessing and leakage checks.  
- Questions for authors: how does CapNorm compare to BN without γ/β or WeightNorm and is it essential?  
- Questions for authors: can Adam/RMSprop with per-fan-in normalization reproduce AdaRad’s benefits; provide ablations.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity of selected width to λ and unit addition/removal schedule and possible online adaptation of λ.  
- Questions for authors: do conclusions hold for convolutional networks with channel/group regularizers and any preliminary CIFAR results?
