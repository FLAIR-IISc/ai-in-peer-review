- Proposal: Nonparametric neural networks that dynamically add zero-units and prune units via fan-in/fan-out group-norm regularization; Theorem 1 shows a finite-width global minimizer exists for λ>0 and 1≤p<∞.  
- Practical mechanisms: CapNorm (batch-norm variant that caps scaling and removes affine params) and AdaRad (radial–angular optimizer separating radial shrinkage and angular updates) to enable growth/prune training.  
- Empirical claims: On MNIST, rectangles, convex (two-layer) and a poker tabular dataset, NP training often matches/exceeds parametric baselines and is competitive on poker.  
- Decision: Reject (borderline) — main concerns are limited empirical rigor/ablations and only incremental methodological novelty.  
- Theory critique: Theorem 1 is novel and nontrivial but only an existence result; it does not analyze training dynamics, AdaRad, CapNorm, or generalization.  
- Proposition critique: Proposition 1 on scale equivalence is interesting, but CapNorm breaks scale-invariance for σ<1 and the practical impact is unexplored.  
- Methodology context: Builds on known structured-sparsity pruning and dynamic growth; novelty lies in nonparametric framing plus AdaRad and CapNorm, but connections to prior work and conceptual links (e.g., weight norm/path-SGD) need clearer discussion.  
- AdaRad concerns: Interesting idea but lacks comparisons to RMSprop/Adam (including variants that protect shrinkage), and no wall-clock or memory tradeoff measurements.  
- CapNorm concerns: Ad hoc design (cap at 1, removal of affine params) without theoretical justification; necessary ablations against BN variants or weight normalization are missing.  
- Experimental scope: Limited to small fully-connected settings and tabular data; no convolutional/modern vision or NLP experiments to show broader applicability.  
- Empirical limitations: Performance is inconsistent (fails on convex), sensitive to λ and schedules, and tends toward under-parameterization in some cases.  
- Fairness/statistics issues: Selecting a “typical representative” run per λ from 10 seeds introduces bias; need full distributions, matched-seed comparisons, medians/spreads.  
- Missing ablations: Critical comparisons absent—AdaRad vs standard optimizers, CapNorm vs BN variants, p=1 vs p=2, fan-in vs fan-out, and sensitivity to ν, νfreq, αr, λ.  
- Compute/time metrics: No training-time or time-to-target comparisons; growth delays and overheads are discussed but unquantified.  
- Recommended experiments: Add convolutional/channel-wise experiments (e.g., CIFAR), comprehensive ablations, sensitivity analyses, and wall-clock/training-time reporting.  
- Clarify prior work: More explicit comparisons to channel pruning, network slimming, progressive/growth methods, and structured-sparsity literature; clearly state what is new.  
- Presentation fixes: Clean typos, improve pseudocode/readability, fix references, and provide clearer intuition for dynamics of newly added units and hyperparameter choices.  
- Specific author questions: Why cap at 1 and remove affine BN params? Can AdaRad be matched by modified RMSprop/Adam and what are wall-clock costs? How robust are results to ν/α/λ schedules and can convex failures be mitigated? p=1 vs p=2 trade-offs? Do NP-found networks generalize better than same-size parametric nets after retraining? How to extend to conv/channel-wise penalties?
