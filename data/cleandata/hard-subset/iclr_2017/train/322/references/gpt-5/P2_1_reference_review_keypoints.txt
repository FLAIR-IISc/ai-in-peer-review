- Proposal: Introduces "nonparametric neural networks" that adapt fully-connected layer widths during a single training run by adding zero-output units and removing units driven to zero by a group lp regularizer; theorem guarantees a finite-width global minimum.  
- Practical methods: Adds CapNorm (capped batch normalization) to avoid normalization-regularizer cancellations and AdaRad optimizer that separates radial and angular gradient components for distinct updates.  
- Empirical claims: Shows dynamic width selection can match or beat size-matched MLPs on MNIST/Rectangles and yields a compact, low-error model on Poker, but underperforms on Convex and has mixed results versus black-box search.  
- Decision: Reviewer recommends rejection.  
- Main rejection reasons: Empirical evaluation is limited and lacks strong baselines/ablations; multiple new components are combined without isolating which are necessary or responsible for gains.  
- Motivation/novelty: Framing via f-equivalence and unit-level group regularization is simple and compelling, addressing an underexplored alternative to black-box hyperparameter search.  
- Theoretical contribution: Theorem that fan-in/fan-out group lp regularization yields a finite-dimensional global minimum is plausible and well-proved, though it does not address optimization convergence or generalization.  
- Proposition insight: Self-similar nonlinearities imply layer-wise λ equivalence, reducing hyperparameter burden.  
- Empirical positives: Demonstrates dynamic width adaptation and training dynamics; matches or outperforms parametric baselines on some datasets and shows promising scalability on Poker.  
- Empirical limitations: Experiments limited to MLPs and toy/tabular/image-like datasets; no convolutional or modern vision benchmarks.  
- Missing baselines: Lacks head-to-head comparisons with modern pruning/grow methods, structured sparsity training, and NAS/growth approaches.  
- Convex failure: Method underperforms with high variance on Convex; paper does not analyze failure modes or remedies.  
- CapNorm concerns: Deviates from standard BN practice; necessity and benefit not shown via ablation; fairness requires testing parametric baselines with CapNorm.  
- AdaRad concerns: Novel but its advantage over RMSprop/Adam is not isolated; momentum variant shows similar results, suggesting optimizer choice may be secondary.  
- Growth policy concerns: Unit addition is simplistic (fixed-rate) and sensitivity analyses (addition rate, αr/αφ, λ) are incomplete; rewinding/manual annealing complicate reproducibility.  
- Positioning/language: Paper cites relevant work but omits experimental comparisons to structured sparsity/grow-prune methods; "nonparametric" terminology should be clarified as adaptive dimensionality.  
- Requested ablations: Compare CapNorm vs BN, AdaRad vs RMSprop/Adam with consistent shrinkage handling, p=1 vs p=2 and fan-in vs fan-out, and pruning-only vs growth+pruning.  
- Requested baselines: Include structured sparsity methods, dynamic network surgery, iterative magnitude pruning with regrowth, and simple NAS width baselines.  
- Analyze Convex failures: Test if issues stem from conservative growth (λ), optimizer dynamics, or CapNorm/activation interactions and try adaptive addition rates or different nonlinearities.  
- Extension request: Demonstrate applicability to convolutional layers and at least one modern vision benchmark to show broader relevance.  
- Computational reporting: Provide wall-clock training time and overhead comparisons versus parametric baselines, not just asymptotic arguments.  
- Writing/structure advice: Tighten narrative around core contribution, move secondary components to appendix, and emphasize which innovations are necessary.  
- Questions for authors: Sensitivity to addition rate and αr/αφ; ablation proving CapNorm necessity; AdaRad vs RMSprop/Adam with adjusted shrinkage; p=1/fan-out variants performance; comparisons to pruning/growth baselines; evidence of improved generalization (calibration/robustness); and how to extend to CNNs/attention plus pitfalls.
