- Proposed "nonparametric neural networks" that dynamically grow and shrink fully-connected layer widths during a single training run.
- Core components: fan-in/out group regularizer for unit-level sparsity, occasional addition of zero-output units, CapNorm normalization, and AdaRad optimizer that separates radial/angular gradient components.
- Theorem 1: fan-in/out group regularization (λ>0, 1≤p<∞) yields a finite-width global minimizer of the penalized empirical objective (no infinite-width degeneration).
- Proposition 1: for self-similar activations (e.g., ReLU), per-layer regularization strengths collapse to a single λ up to rescaling.
- Empirical claims: evaluated on MNIST, rectangles-images, convex, and Poker datasets; nonparametric nets sometimes outperform same-size parametric nets and can match random search with fewer parameters.
- Decision: Reject — empirical evaluation too limited and novelty beyond existing grow/prune/group-lasso ideas not convincingly shown.
- Novelty criticism: high-level idea aligns with long-standing grow/prune and group-lasso work; CapNorm and AdaRad are incremental relative to prior decoupling/normalization methods.
- Theory limitation: existence result is nice but does not address learnability, generalization, or guarantees for the discrete add/remove training dynamics.
- Empirical scope concern: experiments restricted to small fully-connected benchmarks and one tabular dataset; missing CNN and sequence tasks (e.g., CIFAR, transformers/RNNs) where capacity selection matters.
- Stability issue: negative/unstable result on the convex dataset indicates fragility of the approach.
- Baselines/fairness issue: comparisons conflate effects of CapNorm and regularization; manual selection of a “representative” run introduces selection bias; missing direct comparisons to group-lasso pruning, dynamic expansion methods, and distillation.
- Missing ablations: no isolation of CapNorm vs BN/LayerNorm, AdaRad vs Adam/RMSprop with decoupled decay, unit addition rate/schedule, fan-in vs fan-out, or p=1 vs p=2.
- Efficiency concern: method may need many epochs because capacity is added slowly; no wall-clock or FLOPs comparisons to random/Bayesian width search and no empirical runtime vs accuracy curves.
- Clarity and correctness: paper is well written and theorem appears sound, but the theorem guarantees a finite penalized minimizer, not that the proposed dynamics find it or generalize better.
- CapNorm tradeoff: capping std and removing scale/shift may prevent cancellation with regularization but could reduce representational flexibility; no empirical evidence provided.
- Suggested empirical improvements: add CNN/transformer benchmarks, comprehensive ablations, comparisons to strong pruning/growing baselines, and wall-clock/compute measurements.
- Suggested theory/practice links: provide convergence/stability evidence for add/remove dynamics, sensitivity analyses for λ/αr/αφ and unit addition schedule, and discussion of failure cases.
- Reproducibility request: release code, remove manual “representative” selection, and fully specify automatic hyperparameter selection protocols.
- Direct questions for authors: sensitivity of performance/width to λ/αr/addition schedule; whether AdaRad's benefits can be matched by standard optimizers with decoupled decay; CapNorm vs BN/LayerNorm comparisons; demonstration on convolutional architectures; wall-clock cost vs basic random width search.
