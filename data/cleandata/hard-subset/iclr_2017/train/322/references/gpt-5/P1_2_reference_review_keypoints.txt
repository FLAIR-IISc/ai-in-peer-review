- Proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that dynamically add/remove hidden units during a single training run to optimize layer widths.  
- Uses group-regularization on fan-ins/outs with an ℓp norm (focus p=2) to induce whole-unit sparsity and prove existence of a finite optimal width (Theorem 1).  
- Maintains f-equivalence by adding zero units and deletes units whose fan-in/out shrinks to zero.  
- Introduces CapNorm, a batch-norm variant that only standardizes when batch std > 1 and removes affine params to avoid cancelling the shrinkage penalty.  
- Proposes AdaRad optimizer that decomposes fan-in gradients into radial and angular components, normalizing only the angular part; AdaRad-M (momentum) in appendix.  
- Experiments on MNIST, Rectangles-Images, Convex, and a large OpenML Poker dataset with analyses of width dynamics and unit lifetimes.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main empirical critique: validation is insufficient and comparisons appear unfair due to mixing CapNorm, different regularization, and different optimizers/step-sizes without ablations.  
- Main methodological critique: novelty is incremental relative to existing structured sparsity, grow-and-prune, and dynamic architecture methods; Theorem 1 unsurprising given group sparsity literature.  
- Positioning: well-motivated goal to reduce architecture search; f-equivalence notion is natural; literature review covers related areas but misses comparisons to competitive dynamic-width baselines.  
- Theorem 1 is technically careful (handles non-smooth nonlinearities) but largely formalizes expected group-sparsity behavior.  
- Proposition 1 on self-similar nonlinearities is practical and simplifies per-layer regularization choices.  
- CapNorm is intuitively reasonable but lacks ablations to quantify necessity versus alternatives (fixed gamma/beta, weight standardization).  
- AdaRad is an interesting tailored optimizer but lacks wall-clock cost comparisons and rigorous gains versus strong baselines (RMSprop/Adam with scaling).  
- Empirical weaknesses: datasets are small and FC-only; method underperforms on Convex and Poker comparisons rely on atypical baseline summaries rather than strong modern baselines.  
- Fairness concerns: NP models use CapNorm and structured shrinkage while parametric baselines use BN and different optimizers/schedules, confounding which factors drive results.  
- Missing ablations isolating contributions of unit addition/removal, CapNorm, AdaRad, and standard optimizers; no comparison to structured-sparsity or grow/prune baselines.  
- Scalability: Poker shows feasibility but no wall-clock/compute accounting or budget-matched baseline comparisons.  
- Constructive feedback: add controlled ablations (fixed-width vs dynamic, CapNorm vs BN, AdaRad vs Adam/RMSprop), ensure fair normalization/regularization baselines, and compare to modern structured-pruning/grow methods including CNN benchmarks like CIFAR.  
- Constructive feedback: report wall-clock time, memory, optimizer steps to target error, clarify addition schedule and hyperparameter sensitivity, provide stability across seeds and variance of final widths.  
- Constructive feedback: justify CapNorm vs alternatives experimentally, streamline theory appendix, fix typos, and release code for reproducibility.  
- Key author questions: sensitivity to addition rate/schedule and αr/αφ; presence of mode collapse or runaway growth?  
- Key author questions: ablations isolating CapNorm effect and whether BN with fixed gamma/beta or weight standardization would suffice?  
- Key author questions: AdaRad vs RMSprop/Adam when shrinkage is scaled, and wall-clock efficiency results?  
- Key author questions: are comparisons matched for compute (epochs, minibatches, flops) and how do NP vs P compare under fixed budget?  
- Key author questions: does the method extend to convolutional layers and modern vision tasks; any preliminaries?  
- Key author questions: stability of final selected width across seeds—please report distributions?  
- Key author questions: will you release code and scripts to reproduce training procedures, rewinds, and hyperparameter searches?
