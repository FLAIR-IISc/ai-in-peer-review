- Proposal: Introduces “nonparametric neural networks” that grow by adding f‑equivalent zero units and shrink via group-l2 fan-in/fan-out regularization to remove units during one training run.
- Theory: Proves (Theorem 1) that with lp (1≤p<∞) fan-in/-out regularization the training objective has a global minimum at finite dimensionality.
- Proposition: Shows for self‑similar activations (e.g., ReLU) layer-wise regularization strengths are equivalent up to rescaling, simplifying hyperparameter tuning.
- Algorithms: Proposes CapNorm (batch‑norm variant that caps variance normalization and removes affine params) to avoid undermining group regularizer.
- Algorithms: Proposes AdaRad, an optimizer that decomposes gradients into radial and angular components and normalizes only the angular part so new units can learn without negating shrinkage.
- Empirics: Experiments on MNIST, rectangles, convex, and Poker datasets show automatic size selection and mixed performance — strong on MNIST/rectangles, weaker on convex, solid on Poker.
- Decision: Reviewer recommends rejection.
- Key reason 1: Empirical support is limited and mixed — small fully connected benchmarks, one negative result, and insufficient ablations to disentangle components.
- Key reason 2: Comparisons and baselines are insufficient to show gains are driven by the nonparametric mechanism rather than optimizer/normalization choices and schedules.
- Novelty/motivation: Idea is interesting and practically motivated but insufficiently positioned against prior structured sparsity, pruning/growing, and Net2Net literature.
- Theory limitation: Theorem ensures existence of finite-width minimizers but does not address generalization or properties of SGD local minima.
- Algorithms critique: Paper bundles three contributions (nonparametric width, CapNorm, AdaRad) without clear attribution of necessary/sufficient parts.
- Missing ablations: No fixed-width training with AdaRad (+/− CapNorm), no fixed-width with group-l2 and standard optimizers, no nonparametric training with standard optimizers/normalization, and no fan-in vs fan-out or p=1 vs p=2 comparisons.
- Benchmark critique: Datasets are small/limited (MNIST, rectangles, idiosyncratic convex); only Poker scales — no convnets or modern vision/NLP tasks where dynamic width matters most.
- Mixed outcomes: On convex the method underperforms parametric nets and the paper doesn’t test alternative growth schedules or adaptive λ to fix under-selection.
- Comparison fairness: Baselines use BN with λ=0 while method uses CapNorm and group-l2; impacts of rewinding, step-size schedules, and growth horizons aren’t controlled.
- Efficiency claim lacking: AdaRad’s computational and memory benefits are unsubstantiated — no wall‑clock or overhead measurements for growth/shrink and CapNorm vs BN.
- Reproducibility concerns: Many moving parts (rewinding, annealing, add/remove frequency) and no provided code or detailed hyperparameter ranges/seeds.
- Recommended ablations: Separate effects of nonparametric width, CapNorm, AdaRad; test fan-in vs fan-out; p=1 vs p=2; and sensitivity to λ, αr, αφ, ν, νfreq; include fixed-width baselines with AdaRad/CapNorm.
- Recommended comparisons: Add structured sparsity baselines (group lasso), pruning-after-training, grow/prune schedules, and knowledge transfer (Net2Net).
- Recommended reporting: Provide training time, epochs to convergence, number of add/remove events, size stabilization curves, and wall‑clock comparisons of AdaRad vs RMSprop/Adam.
- Recommended extensions: Apply to convolutional layers and at least one modern vision/NLP benchmark.
- Fairness clarifications: Re-run parametric baselines with CapNorm, add static group-l2 baselines, and test training the final selected architecture from scratch with the same optimizer/normalization.
- Reproducibility fixes: Release code, hyperparameter ranges, and seeds.
- Question 1: How much of the gain vs same-size parametric nets is due to AdaRad or CapNorm vs nonparametric growth — can fixed-width + AdaRad (+/− CapNorm) match the method?
- Question 2: Why not compare to fixed-width group-l2 (p=2) baselines with standard optimizers — does dynamic add/remove help over static group-l2?
- Question 3: On convex, would increasing addition rate or annealing λ avoid premature under-selection — any adaptive-λ results?
- Question 4: How sensitive are results to ν, νfreq, αr, and αφ, and what practitioner guidelines exist?
- Question 5: Provide wall‑clock cost and memory overhead comparisons of AdaRad vs RMSprop/Adam across datasets.
- Question 6: How does CapNorm compare to BN-without-affine and BN-with-affine when combined with group-l2 (e.g., excluding normalized layers from regularization)?
- Question 7: Any preliminary results for convolutional layers and channel/filter fan-in/out regularization?
- Question 8: Is there a simple practical criterion to choose fan-in vs fan-out penalties?
- Question 9: Will the authors provide code and training scripts to reproduce growth/shrink dynamics and reported curves?
