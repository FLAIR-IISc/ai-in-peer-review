- Proposes nonparametric neural networks that adapt layer widths during one training run using group-norm regularization, dynamic unit addition/removal, CapNorm, and a new optimizer AdaRad.
- Theoretical result (Theorem 1): with fan-in/-out regularization (1 ≤ p < ∞) the regularized training objective has a finite-size global minimizer.
- Shows that for self-similar nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU) layer-wise regularization can be reduced to a single scalar λ via rescaling.
- Empirically, NP networks find reasonable widths on MNIST, rectangles, convex, and OpenML poker; competitive on some tasks but underperforms on convex.
- Scales to large tabular data (>1M examples) on poker with strong accuracy.
- Decision: Reject, primarily due to limited empirical evaluation, theoretical limits, and insufficient comparisons of engineering components.
- Major concern: empirical evaluation has fairness/confounding issues that hinder assessment of practical benefits.
- CapNorm is proposed to prevent BN from negating regularization, but is not compared to alternatives (WeightNorm, LayerNorm, decoupled weight decay).
- AdaRad’s radial/angular decomposition is intuitive but lacks apples-to-apples comparisons with normalized-gradient or group-normalized optimizers.
- Parametric baselines were disadvantaged by using BN with λ=0 while NP used CapNorm, creating an unfair comparison.
- Experimental protocol uses manual “typical representatives” and mismatched early stopping/rewinding between NP and P, introducing subjectivity.
- Datasets are small-scale and lack convolutional benchmarks, limiting claims of broad applicability; convex task shows clear failure mode (too-small widths).
- Claims of outperforming black-box methods are not fully substantiated given limited NAS/random-search baselines.
- Paper does not show compute/training-time benefits versus hyperparameter search; runtime/memory trade-offs not reported.
- Theorem 1 only guarantees existence of a finite minimizer for the regularized loss, not that the proposed training dynamics or AdaRad will find it or generalize better.
- Suggested improvements: stronger baselines (CapNorm for parametric, decoupled WD, LayerNorm/WeightNorm), ablations of CapNorm/AdaRad, and p=1 vs p=2, fan-in vs fan-out studies.
- Suggested broader evaluation: add at least one CNN benchmark (e.g., CIFAR), harmonize early stopping and report medians over seeds, and report wall-clock/compute metrics.
- Practical suggestions: explore adaptive unit-addition schedules, provide guidance or automated tuning for αr, αφ, λ, and discuss stability for non-self-similar activations (e.g., GELU).
- Theoretical clarifications requested: explicitly state limits of Theorem 1 regarding dynamics and generalization and discuss extending results to training dynamics.
- Key questions for authors: effect of parametric baselines using decoupled WD/CapNorm and medians over seeds; ablations isolating dynamic width/CapNorm/AdaRad; wall-clock vs random-search trade-offs; sensitivity to unit addition rate and hyperparameter heuristics; applicability to convolutional architectures and related obstacles.
