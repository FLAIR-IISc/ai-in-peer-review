- Proposal: Nonparametric neural networks that grow by adding zero (f-equivalent) units and prune units with zero fan-in/out via group regularization, jointly optimizing weights and layer widths in one run.
- Theory: Theorem 1 proves a finite-dimensional global minimizer exists with fan-in/out group regularization (λ>0, 1≤p<∞), preventing infinite-width optima; a proposition shows self-similar activations collapse per-layer λ to a single effective λ.
- Algorithms: Introduces CapNorm (batch-norm variant that preserves scale penalties) and AdaRad (optimizer splitting gradients into radial/angular components, normalizing only angular updates), plus an AdaRad-M momentum variant.
- Empirics: On MNIST and Rectangles-Images NP nets beat same-sized parametric nets and match broader random search; underperform on Convex; scale to a large Poker dataset with strong results (one run reported 0% test error); includes training dynamics and layer-size analyses.
- Decision: Reject (borderline)—mainly due to limited/uneven empirical validation and insufficient comparisons to strong related baselines.
- Main criticism: Improvements are modest and dataset coverage narrow, with a notable failure on Convex and no comparisons to modern dynamic/pruning/structured-sparsity methods.
- Methodology concern: Multiple interacting components (CapNorm, AdaRad, grow/prune schedules, rewinding) lack ablations and fair, apple-to-apple baselines to identify which parts drive gains.
- Theory appraisal: Theorem 1 is interesting, technically careful, and supports the modeling choice, though it addresses penalized empirical risk rather than generalization.
- Algorithm appraisal: CapNorm plausibly fixes BN vs regularizer interaction; AdaRad’s radial/angular idea is intuitive but novelty is incremental and missing comparisons to simpler alternatives like Adam/RMSprop, weight normalization, or group norm.
- Experimental protocol strengths/weaknesses: Shows effort (multiple seeds, size-matched comparisons), but selecting a single “typical” NP model biases comparisons, Convex underperformance and the Poker 0% result need sanity checks, no CNN/vision experiments, and crucial ablations are missing.
- Suggested improvements: Add controlled baselines (dynamic surgery, group-lasso under standard optimizers, Net2Net), perform ablations (CapNorm, AdaRad, p=1 vs p=2, fan-in vs fan-out), report medians/confidence intervals, extend to CNNs/vision tasks, justify rewinding, report wall-clock costs, and analyze hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Reproducibility/clarity asks: Release code and scripts, and provide practical default recommendations for λ, αr, αφ, ν and addition frequency.
- Key questions for authors: Why use a single NP representative for size-matched comparisons rather than medians; ablation results for CapNorm vs BN and AdaRad vs Adam/RMSprop; experiments with p=1 or fan-out penalties; sensitivity to hyperparameters and adaptive schedules; details/robustness of the Poker 0% split; diagnosis/mitigation for Convex failure; wall-clock overhead vs standard training plus random search.
