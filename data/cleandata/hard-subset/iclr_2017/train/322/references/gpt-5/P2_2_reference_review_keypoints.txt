- Proposes nonparametric neural networks that add zero-units and remove units via a fan-in/fan-out Lp group-lasso-like regularizer; Theorem 1 claims a finite global minimizer exists under σ(0)=0.  
- Introduces CapNorm (capped batch normalization) to preserve regularizer effect and AdaRad optimizer that decomposes gradients into radial and angular components per fan-in.  
- Empirical results: growth from small seeds and stable sizes on MNIST, rectangles, convex, and poker; competitive on MNIST/rectangles, underperforms on convex, very low error on poker.  
- Decision: Reject (borderline).  
- Main empirical criticism: evaluation limited to fully-connected MLPs and toy/legacy datasets, lacking convnets/CIFAR and broader modern benchmarks.  
- Notable failure case (convex) is acknowledged but unexplained.  
- Key design choices (CapNorm, αr relative to λ, unit-add schedule) are ad hoc and lack rigorous ablation or robustness analysis.  
- Fairness concerns: comparisons to parametric baselines are questionable due to differing regularization/normalization regimes.  
- Positioning/motivation: problem (automated width selection in one run) is relevant and the f-equivalence via zero-units is a useful device.  
- Theory: Theorem 1 and proof approach are meaningful and technically plausible but concern training error only, not generalization.  
- Proposition 1 (self-similarity) is a helpful scaling observation but depends on ReLU-like activations.  
- CapNorm tackles BN’s scale-invariance problem for regularization but is ad hoc and not compared to alternatives (frozen BN affine, weight norm, layer norm).  
- AdaRad’s angular/radial decomposition is an interesting idea to help new units learn while preserving shrinkage, but lacks wall-clock/throughput measurements.  
- Empirics positives: shows size trajectories and cases where nonparametric nets beat parametric baselines of same size; poker dataset suggests scalability.  
- Empirics negatives: limited datasets, missing convnets, lack of comparison to structured pruning/growth baselines (group-lasso pruning, Net2Net, dynamic surgery, L0/ARD).  
- Reporting issues: selecting a single “typical representative” from seeds risks bias; should report medians and variances across seeds.  
- Missing ablations: effect of removing CapNorm; replacing AdaRad with Adam/RMSprop+group shrinkage; p=1 vs p=2; sensitivity to ν, αr, αφ, λ; unit addition schedules.  
- Suggested experiments: add convolutional benchmarks (e.g., CIFAR), compare to structured pruning/growth, match normalization/regularization in baselines, report FLOPs and wall-clock time, measure AdaRad overhead.  
- Methodology clarifications requested: justify αr=150/λ and ~50 epoch choice, provide guidelines for ν/νfreq/αφ, detail CapNorm inference behavior, and release code.  
- Analysis requests: investigate convex failure and whether annealing λ or delaying shrinkage helps; study whether growth/shrinkage implicitly affects generalization.  
- Specific questions for authors: provide convnet results, robustness plots for hyperparameters, baselines using CapNorm+group-l2, AdaRad runtime vs Adam/RMSprop, CapNorm ablation, causes of convex failure, and results of retraining discovered architectures from scratch.  
- Overall verdict: interesting theoretical and algorithmic contributions but empirical validation and baseline fairness are insufficient; recommend broader experiments, systematic ablations, and clearer methodology.
