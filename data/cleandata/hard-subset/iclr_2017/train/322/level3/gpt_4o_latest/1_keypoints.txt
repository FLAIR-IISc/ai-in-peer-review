- Novel contribution: proposes a nonparametric NN framework that dynamically adjusts size via sparse regularization, introduces AdaRad (and CapNorm), and provides theoretical guarantees plus benchmark demonstrations.  
- Decision: Reject — primary concerns are insufficient practical/empirical analysis and poor performance on convex datasets.  
- Computational complexity concern: method adds weight decomposition, coordinate transforms, and unit addition/removal without run-time analysis or comparisons to standard approaches.  
- Empirical limitation on convex data: nonparametric networks are smaller and less effective than parametric ones; no explanation or mitigation is provided.  
- Scalability feedback: experiments (e.g., poker) are limited; need broader evaluation on high-dimensional and imbalanced datasets.  
- Ablation studies missing: contributions of CapNorm, AdaRad, and other components are not isolated to show their individual effects.  
- Clarity issue: paper is dense and hard to follow; experimental section should be more concise.  
- Question — runtime: provide running-time comparisons versus grid search or random search.  
- Question — convex underperformance: explain why nonparametric networks underperform on convex datasets and what dataset characteristics cause this.  
- Question — optimizer comparison: compare AdaRad to Adam/RMSprop in convergence speed and computational cost.
