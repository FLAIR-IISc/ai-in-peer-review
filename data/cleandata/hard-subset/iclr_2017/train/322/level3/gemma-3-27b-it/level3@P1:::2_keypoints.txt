- Introduces “nonparametric neural networks” that dynamically learn both weights and network structure during training.  
- Theoretical claim: with appropriate regularization, an optimal finite network size exists, preventing unbounded growth.  
- Recommendation: Reject (experimentation and clarification insufficient for acceptance).  
- Experimental results are mixed and not consistently superior to well-chosen fixed-size architectures.  
- The claim that the method outperforms black-box architecture search is not convincingly supported.  
- Theorem 1’s practical implications and intuition (why regularization prevents runaway growth) are inadequately explained.  
- Need an experiment comparing nonparametric nets to fixed-size nets trained to the same final size to assess efficiency/memory benefits.  
- Figure 2 is confusing due to overlapping ranges and lacks clear error bars or separated method plots.  
- Require deeper investigation of why nonparametric nets sometimes outperform parametric nets even when final size is known.  
- Clarify the role of the regularization parameter λ: can it be predicted from data and how does it influence final size vs. structure?  
- Evaluate sensitivity to random initialization and whether different seeds yield consistently different final architectures/performance.  
- Explore combined fan-in and fan-out regularization and whether the theoretical result still holds.  
- Clarify the effect of adding “zero units” on the regularizer and explicitly define a removable zero unit (both fan-in and fan-out zero).  
- Provide clearer justification for AdaRad-M’s momentum application differences between weights and the orthogonal gradient component.
