## Review of "Nonparametric Neural Networks"

This paper tackles the important problem of automatically determining neural network size during training, avoiding the expensive process of global search. The core idea of randomly adding zero units and applying a sparse regularizer is clever and resembles a form of random search guided by regularization to prune irrelevant connections. The empirical results presented are promising, demonstrating competitive performance on benchmark datasets.

I recommend **rejecting** the paper in its current form. While the concept is interesting and the results encouraging, the significant additional computational complexity introduced by the “Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent” (AdaRad) algorithm is a major concern. The paper lacks a rigorous analysis of this complexity, particularly in comparison to standard parametric networks.

The authors claim AdaRad scales better than RMSprop, but this claim requires substantial justification. A detailed quantification of the extra operations involved (e.g., decomposing fan-in weights, radial-angular conversions) is crucial. Providing running-time experiments on different dataset sizes and network architectures would directly address this concern and demonstrate the practical feasibility of their approach. Furthermore, the underperformance on the convex dataset is perplexing. The authors attribute this to the method yielding very small networks, but a more thorough explanation of *why* smaller networks are detrimental in this specific case is needed. Is it a limitation of the regularization, the optimization process, or the dataset itself? A comparison of the learned architectures with those found through other methods on this dataset would be insightful.

**Additional Feedback (for improvement, not part of the decision):**

*   **Clarity of AdaRad:** The description of AdaRad is dense and difficult to follow. A more intuitive explanation of the radial and angular shifts, and their interplay with the regularization, would be beneficial. Consider adding a diagram illustrating the process.
*   **Comparison to Existing Pruning Techniques:** A more detailed comparison to existing pruning techniques (e.g., those mentioned in the introduction) is warranted. How does this approach differ in terms of computational cost, performance, and applicability?
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper lacks analysis on the sensitivity of the method to the choice of hyperparameters (αr, αφ, λ). A sensitivity study would strengthen the robustness of the claims.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** While the theorem establishing a global minimum is valuable, exploring the conditions under which this minimum is *easily* found (i.e., through efficient optimization) would be useful.

**Questions for the authors:**

*   What is the asymptotic computational complexity of AdaRad compared to standard SGD/Adam with backpropagation?
*   Can you provide empirical evidence (running times) demonstrating the scalability of AdaRad to larger datasets and networks?
*   Why does the nonparametric approach underperform on the convex dataset, and what modifications could be made to address this issue?
*   What are the limitations of this approach in terms of network depth and complexity? Are there scenarios where it is unlikely to be effective?
