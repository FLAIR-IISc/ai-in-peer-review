1. Proposes nonparametric neural networks that dynamically adjust size by adding zero units and pruning with a sparse regularizer to automate model selection.  
2. Method intuitively resembles random search aided by sparse regularization.  
3. Empirical results are promising on MNIST and rectangles but not consistently superior.  
4. Recommendation: Reject due to high computational complexity of AdaRad and unexplained poor performance on the convex dataset.  
5. Major concern: fan-in decomposition and radial-angular coordinate transforms likely add substantial overhead; paper lacks quantitative runtime/operation analysis.  
6. Claim of graceful scaling is unsupported by empirical timing or complexity comparisons for larger datasets.  
7. Puzzling underperformance on the convex dataset; authors provide no explanation—raises robustness concerns.  
8. Potential causes for convex failure: regularization strength, learning rate, or inherent method limitation—needs investigation.  
9. Clarify how this approach differs from existing pruning techniques; current discussion is insufficient.  
10. Systematically explore regularization parameter λ to show its effect on network size and performance.  
11. Investigate alternative unit-addition schedules or criteria beyond one unit per layer per epoch.  
12. Provide a detailed ablation study to isolate effects of zero-unit addition, sparse regularization, and AdaRad.  
13. Expand experimental evaluation to more datasets of varying complexity to strengthen conclusions.  
14. Question: Provide detailed computational cost breakdown of AdaRad vs SGD/Adam, including decomposition and coordinate-transform impacts.  
15. Question: Theoretical justification for adding zero units specifically vs adding units with small random weights.  
16. Question: Why do nonparametric networks perform poorly on the convex dataset and what fixes could help?  
17. Question: How sensitive is performance to the angular step size (αφ) and can it be adaptively tuned?  
18. Question: Implications and challenges of using nonlinearities that do not satisfy σ(0)=0.  
19. Question: Why re-orthogonalize the orthogonal component after unit deletion in AdaRad-M, and what if this step is omitted?  
20. Question: Rationale for scaling regularization parameters using the geometric mean in Proposition 1 and whether other means would suffice?
