- Proposes Nonparametric Neural Networks that dynamically add/remove units using input/output weight magnitudes and group-sparsity regularization, trained with a new AdaRad optimizer.  
- Theoretical result: under certain conditions a finite-unit network can minimize loss.  
- Recommendation: Reject, with encouragement to revise and resubmit.  
- Primary weakness: very limited empirical validation across few datasets.  
- No comparison to established compression/pruning/distillation or lottery ticket methods, so practical benefit is unclear.  
- Hyperparameter dependence: add-frequency and decay rates effectively control final size, shifting rather than solving model selection.  
- Scalability concern: tuning those hyperparameters for larger networks is worrisome.  
- Training-time trade-off: controlling model size also affects training time, a trade-off not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Theory does not guarantee avoidance of overfitting or underfitting; experiments don’t demonstrate robustness in this regard.  
- Positive: clear presentation and motivation, relevant to lifelong learning and dynamic adaptation.  
- AdaRad shows promising initial empirical results but needs stronger validation.  
- Requested comparative analysis against state-of-the-art pruning, quantization, and distillation methods.  
- Requested hyperparameter-sensitivity studies and development of principled parameter-setting strategies.  
- Requested scalability experiments on larger, more complex datasets (e.g., ImageNet).  
- Requested ablation studies to isolate contributions of AdaRad, regularization, and dynamic addition/removal.  
- Suggested theoretical extension to provide guarantees regarding overfitting/underfitting.  
- Questions for authors: how does AdaRad’s computational cost scale vs. Adam/RMSprop?  
- Questions for authors: how to automate selection of add-frequency and decay rates to reduce tuning?  
- Questions for authors: what specific constraints make this approach necessary over simpler pruning/compression techniques?  
- Questions for authors: what is the memory-footprint impact of dynamically adding/removing units for very large models?
