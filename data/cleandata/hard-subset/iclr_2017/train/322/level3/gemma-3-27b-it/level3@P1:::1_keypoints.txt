- Proposes nonparametric neural networks that add/remove units during a single training run using L2 penalty and a new optimizer AdaRad.  
- Recommendation: Reject due to insufficient justification of AdaRad's overhead and poor performance on one dataset.  
- Major concern: AdaRad’s radial-angular decomposition likely introduces significant computational overhead not quantitatively analyzed.  
- Paper lacks runtime comparisons of AdaRad against standard optimizers (SGD/Adam/RMSprop) for equivalent performance.  
- Empirical issue: on the convex dataset, nonparametric networks underperform same-size parametric networks and tend to converge to overly small models.  
- Authors provide only limited discussion of the convex-dataset failure; deeper analysis of causes (e.g., L2 regularization vs dataset traits) is needed.  
- Theoretical support (Theorem 1) is abstract, with the proof in the appendix and lacking an intuitive explanation in the main text.  
- Suggestion: include a detailed computational complexity analysis and running-time experiments across datasets and network sizes.  
- Suggestion: investigate the convex dataset failure with alternative regularization schemes and architectures.  
- Suggestion: present an intuitive, accessible explanation of Theorem 1 and its practical implications.  
- Suggestion: study sensitivity to hyperparameters, especially radial and angular step sizes (αr and αφ).  
- Suggestion: describe and experiment with different initialization strategies for newly added units.  
- Suggestion: perform an ablation study isolating CapNorm, radial-angular descent, and the unit add/remove mechanism.  
- Question: provide quantitative training-time comparison of AdaRad nonparametric vs Adam-trained parametric networks for MNIST and Rectangles.  
- Question: what dataset properties or method aspects explain the convex-dataset degradation; were alternative regularizers/architectures tried?  
- Question: request an intuitive explanation of why the p-norm restriction (1 ≤ p < ∞) is necessary for Theorem 1.  
- Question: how sensitive are results to αr and αφ; were hyperparameters optimized and what values were best?  
- Question: was training attempted without unit removal, and if so how did performance compare—what specific benefit does removal provide?
