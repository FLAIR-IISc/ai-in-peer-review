- Proposal: nonparametric neural networks that grow and shrink during a single training cycle by adding zero-valued units and using L2 regularization to prune, plus a new optimizer AdaRad.  
- Decision: Reject — interesting idea but clarity, justification, and empirical validation are insufficient.  
- Clarity concern: motivation for AdaRad’s radial-angular gradient decomposition is not convincing and explanations are hand-wavy.  
- Complexity concern: computational cost and complexity of AdaRad versus optimizers like Adam/RMSprop are not analyzed.  
- Empirical results: promising on MNIST and rectangles but significantly worse on the convex dataset without adequate explanation.  
- Unclear cause: paper does not determine whether convex-dataset failure is due to data properties or method limitations.  
- Hyperparameter tuning: sensitivity to angular step size (αφ) and unit addition rate (ν) is not systematically explored.  
- Run selection: selection of a “typical representative” from 10 runs is undefined and lacks statistical reporting (mean, std).  
- Theoretical assumptions: results depend on σ(0)=0 and differentiability conditions; practical implications of these constraints are not discussed.  
- Request: provide detailed theoretical and empirical analysis of AdaRad’s computational cost (e.g., runtime per epoch).  
- Request: investigate and address the convex dataset failure, possibly via alternative regularization or architectures.  
- Request: conduct systematic hyperparameter sensitivity analyses for αφ, ν, λ.  
- Request: clarify selection criteria for representative runs and report aggregate statistics across runs.  
- Request: perform ablation studies to quantify contributions of AdaRad, L2 regularization, and unit add/remove mechanisms.  
- Explanation need: provide a clearer, more intuitive justification of the radial/angular decomposition and alternatives considered.  
- Presentation issues: improve readability and legend of Figure 2.  
- Presentation issues: number missing equations for clarity.  
- Question: provide empirical running-time comparisons of AdaRad versus Adam/RMSprop.  
- Question: what dataset characteristics might explain the convex dataset failure and were alternative regularizers/architectures tried?  
- Question: what exact criteria were used to pick the “typical representative” and what are performance mean/std across runs?  
- Question: rationale for choosing a fan-in regularizer over a fan-out regularizer and any comparative experiments.  
- Question: how does initial network size affect final architecture and performance?
