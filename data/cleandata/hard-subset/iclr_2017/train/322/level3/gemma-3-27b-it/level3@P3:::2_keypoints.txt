- Decision: Accept with major revisions — strong core idea and theory but needs clarification and more experiments.
- Contribution: Introduces nonparametric neural nets that grow/shrink during training and proves finite parameter usage under fan-in/fan-out regularization.
- Algorithm: Proposes AdaRad (and AdaRad-M) to optimize networks with dynamically varying units.
- Strength: Theorem 1 is a significant result addressing unbounded growth of dynamic architectures.
- Strength: Dynamic unit addition/removal offers a promising alternative to black-box model selection and manual sizing.
- Weakness: Theorem 1 discussion is insufficient — the paper needs a high-level intuitive explanation of why finiteness holds.
- Weakness: Comparison to Lasso/Elastic Net is noted but underdeveloped; a deeper analogy would clarify regularization effects.
- Experimental gap: Missing direct comparison to fixed-size nets of the same final size to assess efficiency (performance vs. memory).
- Figure 2 issue: Presentation is unclear and results are mixed, including cases where nonparametric nets outperform same-size parametric nets.
- Conceptual ambiguity: The definition of a “zero unit” is imprecise — removable units must have zero fan-in AND zero fan-out to preserve function.
- Regularizer claim: The claim that adding zero units changes regularizer value (e.g., L2) is subtle and requires justification/nuance.
- Hyperparameter question: It’s unclear whether λ can be predicted from data or whether λ primarily determines final network structure.
- Sensitivity: Need experiments on sensitivity to initialization and variability of final structures/performance across random starts.
- Combined regularizers: Unclear theoretical/empirical effects of combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers — does Theorem 1 still hold?
- AdaRad-M: Momentum inclusion seems promising but requires justification in the context of fan-in regularization and explanation of performance differences.
- Requested experiment: Implement and report comparisons between adaptive nonparametric models and fixed-size nets matched to final size.
- Requested clarification: Provide an intuitive explanation of Theorem 1 in the main text (beyond the appendix proof).
- Requested clarification: Redesign Figure 2 for clarity and to address why nonparametric nets sometimes beat well-sized fixed nets.
- Requested clarification: Precisely define “zero unit” and analyze how adding zero units affects different regularizers.
- Requested investigation: Study whether λ can be inferred from data and how it influences final architecture.
- Requested investigation: Run multiple random initializations to quantify sensitivity and structural variability.
- Requested investigation: Analyze combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers and report theoretical implications.
- Requested analysis: Report computational/memory cost scaling of AdaRad versus Adam/RMSprop as networks grow.
- Specific question 1: Provide more intuition why Theorem 1 guarantees a finite optimal number of parameters under the regularizer.
- Specific question 2: Explain why nonparametric networks sometimes outperform fixed-size networks with the same parameter count.
- Specific question 3: How sensitive are final network structure and performance to random initialization?
- Specific question 4: Is there a reason to prefer fan-in vs. fan-out regularization, and what if both are combined?
- Specific question 5: Can the optimal λ be predicted from data, and if so, how?
- Specific question 6: How does AdaRad’s computational and memory cost scale compared to Adam/RMSprop?
- Specific question 7: Clarify practical implications of regularization preventing indefinite growth and its impact on scaling to very large datasets.
