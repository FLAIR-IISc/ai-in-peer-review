- Introduces Nonparametric Neural Networks (NPNs) that dynamically add/remove units during training using L2 fan-in regularization.  
- Proposes AdaRad, a new optimizer tailored to the dynamic network structure.  
- Provides a theoretical result showing a finite network size can achieve a global training error minimum under certain conditions.  
- Decision: Reject — empirical gains are interesting but not strong enough for acceptance.  
- Main empirical weakness: limited experimental scope and lack of comparison to pruning/compression baselines.  
- Comparisons are mainly to statically sized networks and different optimizers, not to established pruning/compression methods.  
- Scalability and computational cost concerns: training NPNs can be expensive and hyperparameters control a trade-off between size and time.  
- Experiments (e.g., poker dataset) show some scalability but do not address large-scale computational burden.  
- Theoretical guarantees are limited: existence result does not provide practical guidance for finding the optimal size.  
- Practical method relies heavily on hyperparameter tuning without a systematic method for setting them.  
- Poor performance on the convex dataset suggests limited generalization to some problem domains.  
- Requested analyses: clarify per-iteration computational cost of AdaRad vs standard optimizers.  
- Requested studies: hyperparameter sensitivity analysis and comparisons to state-of-the-art pruning/compression techniques.  
- Suggested extensions: explore alternative regularizers, address practical implications of theory, and evaluate NPNs on other architectures (e.g., CNNs).  
- Questions for authors: expected training time increase vs fixed networks, scaling behavior with dataset size, rationale for L2 fan-in choice, sensitivity to AdaRad step sizes, and reasons for poor convex-dataset performance.
