- Tackles automatic determination of neural network size during training using random zero-unit addition plus sparse regularization; empirical results are promising.

- Core idea is clever (random addition + regularizer acts like guided random search) and shows competitive performance on benchmarks.

- Recommendation: reject in current form due to major concerns about added computational complexity from AdaRad.

- Paper lacks rigorous analysis and quantification of AdaRad’s extra computational cost versus standard parametric methods.

- Authors’ claim that AdaRad scales better than RMSprop is unsubstantiated and needs substantial justification.

- Need running-time experiments across dataset sizes and architectures to demonstrate practical scalability and overhead.

- Underperformance on the convex dataset is unexplained; the claim that very small networks cause it needs deeper analysis (regularization, optimization, or dataset issues).

- Suggest comparing learned architectures on the convex dataset with those found by other methods to diagnose failure modes.

- Clarity of AdaRad: description is dense; add intuitive explanations and diagrams for radial/ angular shifts and their interaction with regularization.

- Compare more thoroughly to existing pruning techniques in terms of computational cost, performance, and applicability.

- Provide a hyperparameter sensitivity study for αr, αφ, and λ to prove robustness.

- Theoretical work is helpful, but authors should analyze conditions under which the global minimum is practically and efficiently reachable.

- Questions for authors to address: asymptotic complexity of AdaRad vs SGD/Adam, empirical running times for larger datasets/networks, reasons and fixes for convex-dataset underperformance, and limitations regarding network depth/complexity.
