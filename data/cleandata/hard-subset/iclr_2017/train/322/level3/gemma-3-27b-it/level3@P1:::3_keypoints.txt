- Paper proposes nonparametric neural networks that dynamically add/remove units using L2-driven weight decay and a novel optimizer (AdaRad).  
- Authors provide a theoretical result that a finite network size can minimize loss under certain conditions.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main criticism: experimental validation is limited in scope and not compelling for acceptance.  
- Lack of direct comparisons to established model selection (e.g., Bayesian optimization) and network compression/pruning baselines.  
- Results on rectangles and MNIST are promising but sensitive to regularization and hyperparameter tuning.  
- Method underperforms comparable parametric networks when regularization is large, indicating hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Clear failure on the convex dataset with no convincing explanation, raising concerns about robustness.  
- Scalability concerns: iterative unit addition and hyperparameter tuning may increase training time for large datasets/networks; Poker dataset details are insufficient.  
- Method introduces several new hyperparameters (addition frequency, decay rates, radial/angular step sizes) with unclear sensitivity and tuning guidance.  
- Suggested improvements: rigorous baseline comparisons, detailed scalability/timing analysis, systematic hyperparameter sensitivity studies.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify theoretical conditions and their practical implications, investigate over/underfitting risks.  
- Suggested improvements: explain convex dataset failure and perform ablation studies on components (L2 penalty, AdaRad, CapNorm).  
- Suggested improvements: expand experiments to more datasets and architectures.  
- Questions for authors: how does training cost scale versus static networks?  
- Question: how sensitive are results to unit addition frequency and decay hyperparameters, and how should they be tuned?  
- Question: what explains the convex dataset failure and dataset characteristics that cause it?  
- Question: how does AdaRad compare to Adam/RMSprop in convergence and generalization for nonparametric networks?  
- Question: were alternative regularizers (L1, group sparsity) explored and with what outcomes?
