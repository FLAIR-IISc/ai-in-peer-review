- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that automatically grow and prune during training — a compelling, novel direction.  
- Theoretical contribution: proof that under certain regularization conditions a finite network size optimizes the objective — significant result.  
- Decision/rating: Accept with minor revisions (7/10).  
- Introduces AdaRad, a training algorithm tailored to dynamic network structure — positive contribution.  
- Experiments (multiple datasets, ablations, comparisons) are promising but need clearer presentation.  
- Theorem 1 is overly formal and lacks plain-English intuition; authors should explain why the regularization guarantees a finite solution.  
- Related work section is lengthy/tangential and should be streamlined to focus on core theoretical insights.  
- Figure 2 is hard to interpret due to mixed markers/colors; visualization clarity must be improved.  
- Need a direct experiment: compare nonparametric vs fixed-size parametric network with the same final size on performance, training time, and memory.  
- Provide a detailed explanation for why dynamic addition/removal can outperform training a fixed-size network (role of training dynamics).  
- Regularizer parameter (λ): clarify whether it can be inferred from data or requires hyperparameter search and how it determines final structure.  
- Initialization sensitivity: report variance in final network size and performance across multiple random seeds.  
- Clarify whether Theorem 1 extends to using both fan-in and fan-out regularizers simultaneously.  
- Correct/clarify claim about zero units affecting regularizer — adding a zero weight vector does not change L2 norm.  
- Define removable "zero unit" precisely: both its fan-in and fan-out must be zero to preserve network output.  
- Redesign Figure 2 (e.g., separate plots per dataset, label points with hyperparameter settings) for better readability.  
- Expand Theorem 1 discussion to emphasize intuition and why the regularizer prevents unbounded growth.
