- Paper proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that dynamically add/remove units during a single training run using an ℓ2/group-sparsity style penalty, CapNorm, and AdaRad optimizer.  
- Theoretical result (Theorem 1) proves a global training error minimum is achieved at finite network dimensionality.  
- Experiments on MNIST and rectangles show smaller networks with competitive or better performance; rectangles results even beat exhaustive search.  
- Decision: Reject — strong theory but insufficient empirical validation.  
- Experiments are limited to three small datasets (MNIST, rectangles, convex) and lack large-scale or challenging benchmarks.  
- The method is not compared against state-of-the-art pruning or compression techniques.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., add-frequency, decay rates) is underexplored and may strongly affect final unit count.  
- Claims about reduced runtime, power, and memory are not supported by concrete measurements.  
- Recommend extending experiments to larger datasets (e.g., CIFAR/ImageNet) and benchmark against pruning/distillation/weight-sharing methods.  
- Recommend providing thorough hyperparameter sensitivity analysis and practical tuning guidance.  
- Recommend reporting concrete inference/runtime/memory/energy measurements for the smaller networks.  
- Recommend investigating and addressing the poor performance on the convex dataset to clarify method limits.  
- Recommend better situating the method relative to existing network-size reduction approaches.  
- Clarifying Q1: Was the comparison fair given parametric baselines used batch normalization which is stated to be incompatible with the nonparametric method?  
- Clarifying Q2: Why does the nonparametric approach perform worse on the convex dataset — dataset characteristics or method limitation?  
- Clarifying Q3: How does training time scale with network size; provide quantitative training-time comparisons to standard parametric training.
