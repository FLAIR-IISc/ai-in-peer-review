- Paper proposes nonparametric neural networks that grow and shrink during training to find network size automatically, removing expensive hyperparameter search.  
- Core theoretical contribution (Theorem 1): L2 fan-in/fan-out regularization ensures optimization attains a global minimum at finite network size.  
- Introduces AdaRad, a training algorithm that normalizes gradients in radial-angular coordinates to handle dynamic structure.  
- Introduces CapNorm, a batch-normalization variant compatible with the proposed regularizer.  
- Empirical results show nonparametric networks outperform parametric baselines on MNIST and rectangles while using far fewer parameters.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions due to strong theory and promising experiments.  
- Main clarity concern: Theorem 1 needs a more intuitive, plain-English explanation of why L2 fan-in/fan-out prevents unbounded growth yet allows adaptation.  
- Explain why nonparametric networks sometimes beat parametric networks of the same final size (Figure 2)—this finding needs deeper discussion.  
- Implementation clarification 1: justify the claim that adding zero units changes the regularizer value (a zero vector should not change L2 norm).  
- Implementation clarification 2: clarify that a “zero unit” must have both fan-in and fan-out zero to preserve network output, not just one.  
- Implementation clarification 3: expand discussion on the relationship between lambda and final network structure—can lambda be predicted from data or does it effectively set architecture?  
- Request sensitivity analysis: do different random initializations yield different final network structures and how large are those differences?  
- Suggest a controlled experiment comparing nonparametric networks to parametric networks of the exact same final size to isolate the benefit of dynamic growth.  
- Specific questions for authors: (1) How to choose lambda without hyperparameter search? (2) Why does fan-in regularization empirically outperform fan-out here? (3) Would combining fan-in and fan-out preserve Theorem 1’s guarantee? (4) How sensitive are results to choice of self-similar nonlinearity (e.g., ReLU vs. others)?  
- Overall: significant contribution with strong theoretical grounding and practical implications; address requested clarifications and experiments to strengthen the paper.
