

# Review of "Nonparametric Neural Networks" (ICLR 2017)

## 1. Summary of Claims and Contribution

This paper introduces a novel framework for automatically determining optimal neural network size during training through a nonparametric approach. The authors propose a method that continuously adds new units while eliminating redundant ones via a fan-in or fan-out `2 regularization. They prove theoretically that this approach achieves a global minimum at finite network size (Theorem 1) and introduce a new optimization algorithm, AdaRad, with a novel normalization layer (CapNorm). The experimental results demonstrate that nonparametric networks can outperform fixed-size parametric networks of comparable size, particularly on MNIST and rectangles datasets, while using fewer parameters. The paper makes a compelling contribution by addressing the problem of network size selection in a single training cycle rather than through expensive black-box search.

## 2. Decision

Accept (7/10)

## 3. Supporting Arguments

The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution with Theorem 1, which proves that nonparametric networks with appropriate regularization achieve a global minimum at finite network size. This is a critical theoretical foundation that addresses a fundamental concern about the potential for infinite growth. The experimental results are promising, particularly on MNIST and rectangles datasets where nonparametric networks outperform parametric networks of the same size while using substantially fewer parameters. The authors demonstrate a clear understanding of the problem space and present a well-motivated approach that avoids the need for expensive black-box search. The theoretical soundness of the approach is well-supported, and the experimental methodology is rigorous.

## 4. Additional Feedback to Improve the Paper

The paper makes a strong contribution, but the following points would substantially improve clarity, theoretical understanding, and experimental validation:

- **Theorem 1 Explanation**: The paper would benefit from a more intuitive, plain-English explanation of Theorem 1's key insight beyond the formal proof. The connection to Lasso/Elastic Net is mentioned but not developed. Why does the fan-in/fan-out regularization lead to finite network size when standard L2 regularization does not? A clearer explanation would help readers understand the theoretical novelty.

- **Experimental Comparison**: The authors should include an experiment comparing the adaptive nonparametric model to fixed-size parametric networks with the *same final size* (not just the same starting size). This would directly demonstrate whether the nonparametric approach achieves better performance with the same number of parameters, which is the core claim of the paper.

- **Figure 2 Analysis**: Figure 2 is confusing as it mixes results from different datasets with different scales. The paper claims nonparametric networks sometimes outperform parametric networks even when the final network size is known. The authors should clarify why this happens, particularly for the rectangles dataset where nonparametric networks outperform parametric ones with the same size when λ is small.

- **Lambda Parameter**: The paper should discuss whether λ can be predicted from data (e.g., via cross-validation) and whether λ mainly determines the final network structure. The current presentation treats λ as a hyperparameter to be tuned, but doesn't explain how it relates to the network's final architecture.

- **Initialization Sensitivity**: The paper should investigate whether different random initializations lead to different final network structures. If so, how different are these structures? If not, what makes the approach robust to initialization?

- **Zero Units Clarification**: The paper claims that "adding zero units changes the regularizer value," but this needs clarification. A removable unit must have both fan-in and fan-out weights zero to preserve network output. The authors should explicitly define what constitutes a "zero unit" to avoid confusion.

- **Regularizer Combination**: The paper focuses on either fan-in or fan-out regularization, but does the theoretical result hold when both are combined? This would be valuable to know for practical implementation.

- **Unit Addition/Removal Mechanism**: The paper should provide more intuition for why the nonparametric approach outperforms parametric networks even when the final size is known (as seen in Figure 2). Why would a network that grows and shrinks during training perform better than one trained from scratch at the same size?

The paper is well-structured and makes a valuable contribution to the field of neural network architecture search. With the above clarifications and experiments, the paper would be significantly strengthened and more accessible to the broader community. The theoretical foundation is solid, and the practical results are promising, making this a strong candidate for acceptance.
