- Paper proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that add/remove units during a single training run using group-sparsity weight zeroing, introduces AdaRad, and reports promising small-dataset results.

- Overall recommendation: reject.

- Theoretical critique: Theorem 1 shows existence of a finite-unit minimizer but does not guarantee the algorithm avoids overfitting or underfitting or reliably finds the optimal size.

- Experimental critique: Evaluation is limited to small datasets and lacks comparisons to established pruning/compression baselines (e.g., Molchanov et al. 2017; Wen et al. 2016).

- Scalability concern: Paper does not analyze how unit addition/removal affects training time or scalability for large networks.

- Hyperparameter/sensitivity gap: No detailed analysis of hyperparameter effects, particularly the trade-off between model size and training time.

- Related-work gap: Paper insufficiently distinguishes itself from or compares to existing dynamic-size methods (e.g., Wei et al. 2016; Chen et al. 2016).

- Suggested fixes: Add comprehensive comparisons to pruning/compression methods, larger-scale experiments, hyperparameter sensitivity studies, and clearer discussion of differences from prior dynamic approaches.
