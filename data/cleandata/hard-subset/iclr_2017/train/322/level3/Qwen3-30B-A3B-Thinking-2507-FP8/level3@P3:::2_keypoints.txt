- Introduces a nonparametric framework that automatically adjusts network size during training by adding units and removing redundant ones using fan-in or fan-out L2 regularization.  
- Proves (Theorem 1) that with appropriate regularization the network attains a global minimum at a finite size, avoiding unbounded growth.  
- Proposes a new optimization algorithm (AdaRad) and a normalization layer (CapNorm).  
- Empirical results show nonparametric networks can outperform fixed-size parametric networks on MNIST and rectangles while using fewer parameters.  
- Claims the approach avoids expensive black-box architecture search by determining size in a single training run.  
- Decision recommendation: Accept (7/10).  
- Supporting argument: Theorem 1 is a significant theoretical contribution addressing the potential for infinite growth.  
- Supporting argument: Experimental methodology is rigorous and results on select datasets are promising.  
- Suggestion: Provide an intuitive, plain-English explanation of Theorem 1 and clarify its connection to Lasso/Elastic Net.  
- Suggestion: Add experiments comparing nonparametric models to parametric models of the same final size to validate claims about parameter efficiency.  
- Suggestion: Clarify Figure 2 (mixed datasets/scales) and explain why nonparametric nets outperform parametric ones on rectangles for small λ.  
- Suggestion: Discuss whether λ can be predicted (e.g., via cross-validation) and how λ determines final network structure.  
- Suggestion: Investigate sensitivity to random initialization and report variability in final architectures.  
- Suggestion: Define "zero unit" precisely (both fan-in and fan-out zero) and clarify how adding/removing units affects the regularizer; examine combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers and whether the theory still holds.  
- Suggestion: Provide intuition for why networks that grow and shrink during training can outperform identically sized networks trained from scratch.
