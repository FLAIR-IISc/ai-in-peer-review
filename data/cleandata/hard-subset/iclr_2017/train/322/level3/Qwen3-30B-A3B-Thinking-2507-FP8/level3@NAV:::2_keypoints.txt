- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that dynamically add/remove units during training via fan-in/fan-out regularization to avoid expensive architecture search.
- Presents Theorem 1 proving that with appropriate regularization the optimization attains a global minimum at finite network size, preventing unbounded growth.
- Recommendation: accept (7/10) — addresses a meaningful problem with clear practical motivation.
- Theoretical result is significant and well-supported by proofs but needs a more intuitive explanation.
- Experiments show promising performance on MNIST and rectangles, but poor results on the convex dataset require further investigation.
- Claims nonparametric networks can match exhaustive architecture search while training in a single pass.
- AdaRad optimization algorithm and CapNorm layer are practical contributions that address training challenges.
- Request 1: Provide a clearer plain-English explanation of Theorem 1 and its key insight beyond the formal proof.
- Request 2: Clarify why nonparametric networks sometimes outperform parametric nets of the same final size (Figure 2 confusion).
- Request 3: Discuss whether the regularization parameter λ can be predicted from data and whether it primarily determines final network structure.
- Request 4: Address sensitivity to initialization — do different random starts produce different final structures?
- Request 5: Clarify the “zero units” claim — removable units must have both fan-in and fan-out weights zero to preserve outputs.
- Request 6: Explain whether combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers would preserve the theoretical guarantee.
- Request 7: Add an experiment comparing adaptive models to fixed-size nets of the same final size to evaluate performance vs. memory/efficiency.
