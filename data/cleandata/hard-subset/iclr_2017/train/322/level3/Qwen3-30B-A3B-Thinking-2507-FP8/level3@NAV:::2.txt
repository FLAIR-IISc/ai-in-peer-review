

# Review of "Nonparametric Neural Networks"

This paper introduces a novel framework for automatically determining neural network architecture during training. The authors propose "nonparametric neural networks" that dynamically add and remove units during training using fan-in/fan-out regularization, avoiding the need for expensive global search over network architectures. The key contribution is a theoretical guarantee (Theorem 1) showing that with appropriate regularization, the optimization problem achieves a global minimum at finite network size, preventing unbounded growth.

I recommend acceptance (7/10). The paper addresses a meaningful problem in deep learning with clear practical motivation. The theoretical result is significant and well-supported by the proof in the appendix, though it could benefit from more intuitive explanation. The experimental results demonstrate promising performance, particularly on MNIST and the rectangles dataset, though the poor performance on the convex dataset requires further investigation.

The paper makes a strong contribution by showing that nonparametric networks can achieve performance competitive with exhaustive search while being trained in a single pass. The AdaRad optimization algorithm and CapNorm layer are well-motivated and address important practical challenges in training these models.

I recommend the authors address the following points to strengthen the paper:
1. Provide clearer, plain-English explanation of Theorem 1 and its key insight beyond the proof.
2. Clarify why nonparametric networks sometimes outperform parametric networks of the same final size (Figure 2 is confusing on this point).
3. Discuss whether the regularization parameter λ can be predicted from data and if it primarily determines the final network structure.
4. Address sensitivity to initialization: do different random starts yield different final network structures?
5. Clarify the claim about "zero units" - a removable unit must have both fan-in and fan-out weights zero to preserve output.
6. Explain whether combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers would maintain the theoretical result.
7. Add an experiment comparing adaptive models to fixed-size nets with the same final size to demonstrate efficiency (performance vs. memory).

These clarifications would significantly strengthen the paper's contribution and make the theoretical and practical implications more accessible to readers.
