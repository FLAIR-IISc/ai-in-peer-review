- Proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that grow/shrink units during a single training run using group-sparsity (L2 on fan-in/fan-out).
- Introduces AdaRad, a novel training algorithm, and proves Theorem 1 guaranteeing existence of a finite-unit minimizer under certain conditions.
- Empirical results on MNIST and rectangles show smaller networks with comparable or better performance than some parametric baselines.
- Decision: Reject due to insufficient empirical validation for practical utility and competitiveness.
- Major critique: No comparison to state-of-the-art pruning/compression methods (e.g., Han et al., Molchanov et al.).
- Major critique: Experiments limited to small datasets and shallow networks, lacking evidence of real-world scalability.
- Major critique: Hyperparameters (addition frequency, decay rates) implicitly control final size and lack guidance for tuning.
- Major critique: Theoretical results do not guarantee avoidance of over- or under-fitting, and experiments do not adequately address this.
- Major critique: Potential scalability and training-time concerns since model size and training speed are coupled via single hyperparameters.
- Suggestion: Add comprehensive comparisons to pruning/compression baselines to establish competitiveness.
- Suggestion: Run experiments on larger datasets and deeper architectures to evaluate scalability.
- Suggestion: Provide hyperparameter sensitivity analysis and practical tuning guidelines.
- Suggestion: Clarify theoretical limitations regarding over/under-fitting and back theoretical claims with more experiments.
- Suggestion: Discuss practical trade-offs between training time and final network size in detail.
- Questions for authors: Compare final size and accuracy to pruning/compression methods on standard benchmarks.
- Questions for authors: Provide detailed analysis of how unit addition rate and regularization strength interact.
- Questions for authors: Explain why the method underperformed on the convex dataset and how to fix it.
- Questions for authors: Report computational overhead of AdaRad versus standard optimizers like Adam on large networks.
- Questions for authors: Describe how the approach would scale to very deep networks (100+ layers) and large-scale vision tasks.
