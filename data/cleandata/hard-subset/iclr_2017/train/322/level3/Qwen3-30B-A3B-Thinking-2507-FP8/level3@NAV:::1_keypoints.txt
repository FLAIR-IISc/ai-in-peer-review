- Proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that automatically determine optimal network size during training by dynamically adding/removing units.
- Uses random initialization of zero units combined with sparse regularization (L2 fan-in) to grow and prune the network.
- Provides a theoretical foundation (Theorem 1) proving a global minimum exists for training error at finite dimensionality.
- Introduces AdaRad, an optimization algorithm that separates radial and angular components of weight updates.
- Demonstrates empirical results on MNIST and rectangles datasets achieving competitive performance with smaller networks.
- Decision: Accept with major revisions.
- Key reason for acceptance: tackles automatic network size selection with a theoretically sound and conceptually elegant approach.
- Supporting argument: method can find good network sizes in a single training run without expensive global search.
- Major revision 1: quantify additional computational complexity of the radial-angular decomposition and include running-time comparisons to standard parametric networks.
- Major revision 2: explain why the method underperforms on the convex dataset, specifically why it yields very small networks that underperform parametric counterparts.
- Question 1 for authors: provide detailed computational complexity analysis of AdaRad vs SGD/Adam.
- Question 2 for authors: clarify whether convex-dataset failure is due to problem structure or limitations of the approach and suggest possible adaptations.
