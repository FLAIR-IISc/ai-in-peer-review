- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that grow/shrink during a single training run using zero-initialized units and an L2 fan-in regularizer.  
- Proposes AdaRad, a novel optimizer separating radial and angular weight updates.  
- Proves (Theorem 1) that the approach attains a global minimum at finite dimensionality.  
- Evaluated on MNIST, rectangles images, and convex datasets with promising results on two but poor performance on the convex dataset.  
- Recommendation: reject due to practical concerns despite solid theory and motivation.  
- Major concern 1: AdaRad’s computational complexity and overhead from decomposing weights and radial-angular updates are neither quantified nor compared to Adam/RMSprop.  
- Major concern 2: Inconsistent empirical results—method fails on the convex dataset with networks becoming too small and underperforming, and no explanation is offered.  
- Paper lacks runtime comparisons or complexity analysis to justify practical viability.  
- Authors should analyze and report how regularization λ affects network size and performance, especially on the convex dataset.  
- Suggest investigating alternative unit addition/removal strategies to address undersized networks on convex.  
- Request 1: Quantify AdaRad’s computational overhead vs. standard optimizers and scaling to larger datasets.  
- Request 2: Explain why the method performs poorly on the convex dataset—dataset properties or approach limitations?  
- Request 3: Provide exact performance metrics (e.g., test errors) comparing best nonparametric and best parametric networks found.
