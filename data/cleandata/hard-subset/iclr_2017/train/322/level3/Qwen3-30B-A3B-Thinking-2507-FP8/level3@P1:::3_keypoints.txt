- Introduces "nonparametric neural networks" that dynamically add/remove units via group-sparsity on fan-in/fan-out and a novel AdaRad optimizer; proves a finite network size minimizes training error.

- Recommendation: Reject.

- Major theoretical limitation: Theorem 1 guarantees only training-error minimization, not generalization performance.

- Empirical validation is insufficient and limited to small datasets (MNIST, rectangles, convex), so scalability is unclear.

- No comparisons to relevant pruning/compression baselines (e.g., Molchanov et al. 2017, Guo et al. 2016), leaving competitiveness unknown.

- Key claimed advantage (better accuracy than parametric networks of same size) is only partially supported by MNIST and contradicted by the convex dataset.

- Method adds significant hyperparameter complexity (unit addition rate, decay rates) that affects both model size and training time, undermining the automation claim.

- Paper provides no practical guidance on setting hyperparameters, making the method hard to apply.

- Experiments lack analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity, training-time overhead, and how these trade off with final model size and performance.

- Convex dataset results show nonparametric networks can underperform parametric ones; poker experiment lacks baseline comparisons.

- Theoretical contribution is elegant but practical significance is undermined by weak empirical support and unresolved tuning challenges.

- Suggested improvements: compare directly to pruning/compression baselines, analyze model-size vs training-time trade-offs, detail hyperparameter sensitivity, and evaluate on larger-scale problems.

- Questions for authors requested: How does your method compare to pruning approaches in test accuracy for equal parameter counts? What is the training-time overhead and its scaling with dataset size? How should users set unit-addition frequency and decay rates for optimal performance?
