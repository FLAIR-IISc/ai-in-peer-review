- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that grow/shrink during a single training run using random zero units and a sparse L2 fan-in regularizer.
- Proves the method attains a global minimum at a finite network size and proposes a novel optimizer (AdaRad).
- Empirical results: strong performance on MNIST and rectangles images, outperforming parametric nets while using fewer parameters.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Strength: Tackles expensive model selection and provides a sound theoretical foundation (Theorem 1).
- Strength: Builds on pruning/growth literature and makes a clear theoretical contribution.
- Strength: AdaRad is a well-designed extension of standard optimization for nonparametric training.
- Concern: Computational overhead of AdaRad is not quantified relative to standard optimizers.
- Concern: Method performs poorly on the convex dataset and lacks analysis explaining this failure.
- Request: Quantify AdaRad’s additional operations/time per epoch compared to SGD/Adam/RMSprop.
- Request: Provide runtime comparisons between nonparametric and parametric networks that achieve similar performance.
- Request: Include a detailed analysis of why the convex dataset yields very small, underperforming networks.
- Request: Clarify precisely how the regularization parameter λ influences network size and how to tune it.
- Question: How many additional operations per epoch does AdaRad require and how does this scale with network size?
- Question: Why did nonparametric training converge to too-small networks on the convex dataset and how can this be addressed?
- Question: How do small nonparametric networks compare to parametric networks trained for the same number of epochs (not just the same size)?
