- Introduces "nonparametric neural networks" that dynamically grow and shrink network size during a single training run to auto-determine architecture.  
- Theorem: a fan-in or fan-out group l_p penalty (p >= 1) guarantees a global minimum at finite network size, preventing uncontrolled growth.  
- Presents CapNorm, a normalization layer compatible with group sparsity, and AdaRad, an optimizer that decouples unit direction and magnitude.  
- Empirical results show the approach can find compact, high-performing networks and sometimes outperform fixed-size networks and random search.  
- Decision: Accept (rating 7).  
- Novelty and practicality: addresses costly model selection by adapting complexity to data instead of heavy architecture search.  
- Theoretical soundness: Theorem 1 provides a crucial guarantee distinguishing this from ad-hoc grow/prune methods.  
- Principled framework: can grow from a small start rather than requiring an oversized seed network for pruning.  
- AdaRad motivation: separating radial and angular updates helps new units learn before the regularizer prunes them.  
- Experiments: promising results on MNIST and a large poker dataset, but mixed performance on one dataset.  
- Suggest adding plain-English intuition in the main text linking Theorem 1 to Group Lasso and why the l_p penalty removes useless units.  
- Ask whether Theorem 1 still holds when combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers (e.g., λ_in·Ω_in + λ_out·Ω_out).  
- Request explanation for why nonparametric networks sometimes outperform parametric ones of the same final size (e.g., curriculum learning or better local minima).  
- Ask about sensitivity to random initialization and request reporting variance in final architectures across runs.  
- Note that λ controls final size—ask whether methods exist to set or adapt λ automatically.  
- Clarify ambiguity about adding/removing units: adding a unit with zero fan-in does not change the fan-in regularizer, whereas the paper adds units with random fan-in and zero fan-out, which does change the regularizer.  
- Confirm removal criterion: algorithm removes units when the fan-in becomes zero—ask authors to verify this is the sole removal condition.
