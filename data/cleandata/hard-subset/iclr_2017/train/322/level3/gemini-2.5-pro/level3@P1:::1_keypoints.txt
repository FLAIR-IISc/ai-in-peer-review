- Proposes nonparametric neural networks that grow and shrink during a single training run using randomly added "zero units" and an l2 fan-in group-sparse regularizer; Theorem 1 shows a finite global-minimum network size exists.  
- Introduces Capped Batch Normalization (CapNorm) to avoid batch-norm counteracting the fan-in shrinkage.  
- Proposes AdaRad optimizer that separates radial (magnitude) and angular (direction) updates, normalizing only the angular gradient so new units have time to learn before pruning.  
- Empirical results on MNIST and rectangles-images are promising, often finding compact networks competitive with extensive random search.  
- Decision: Reject, primarily due to unquantified computational overhead and inconsistent empirical performance.  
- Main concern: AdaRad is substantially more complex than Adam/RMSprop, yet the paper lacks empirical wall-clock/time-to-convergence comparisons to quantify its practical cost.  
- Main concern: The method fails on the convex dataset (converging to small, poorly performing networks), raising robustness and generality concerns.  
- Supporting critique: Asymptotic per-unit normalization claims for AdaRad may be misleading without concrete timing comparisons on fixed-size networks.  
- Supporting critique: Collapse on convex suggests delicate, dataset-dependent interactions between unit addition, regularization (λ), and radial learning rate (α_r), implying hidden hyperparameter tuning.  
- Suggested improvement: Replace the fixed add-one-per-layer-per-epoch rule with adaptive growth schedules or batch additions (e.g., trigger on validation plateau).  
- Suggested improvement: Consider smarter initialization for new units (e.g., cloning/perturbing existing units like Net2Net) instead of random zeros.  
- Suggested alternative: Test applying group-sparse regularization after batch-norm as a simpler alternative to CapNorm and compare experimentally.  
- Suggested analysis: Provide sensitivity experiments for α_r (α_r = 150λ is currently used) to show whether the 150 factor is universal or dataset-specific.  
- Question for authors: Provide empirical wall-clock training-time comparisons of AdaRad vs. standard optimizers on specified hardware.  
- Question for authors: Explain hypotheses for failure on the convex dataset and report experiments varying λ or unit-addition rate ν to avoid collapse.  
- Question for authors: How was α_r = 150λ chosen, and how sensitive are results to changing that constant?
