**Summary:** This paper introduces "Nonparametric Neural Networks," a compelling framework for automatically determining a suitable network architecture during a single training run. The core idea is to dynamically add new units while simultaneously pruning redundant ones using a fan-in or fan-out group sparsity regularizer. The authors make a significant theoretical contribution by proving that, under this regularization, the training error optimum is achieved at a finite network size, preventing uncontrolled growth. The proposed training algorithm, AdaRad, is designed to accommodate this dynamic process.

**Decision and Rationale:** I recommend **Accept**. The paper tackles the critical and expensive problem of model selection with a novel, principled approach. The main strengths are (1) the elegant method of growing and shrinking the network based on the data, rather than relying on fixed user priors, and (2) the sound theoretical guarantee (Theorem 1) that provides a solid foundation for this dynamic process.

**Supporting Arguments:** The proposed method is well-motivated and presents a practical alternative to exhaustive black-box hyperparameter searches. The connection drawn to group sparsity methods like the Group Lasso is insightful, and its application to dynamically structuring a neural network is innovative. The theoretical result is the paper's highlight, assuring that model complexity is inherently controlled. While empirical results are somewhat mixed, they are promising, demonstrating on some datasets that the nonparametric approach can find compact, high-performing models that are competitive with, or even superior to, those found via random search.

**Suggestions for Improvement:** To further strengthen the paper, I suggest the following:
*   **Clarify Theorem 1:** Please provide a more intuitive, plain-English discussion of the theorem's key insight. A brief comparison to how Lasso induces sparsity would be more valuable than some of the broader prior work discussion.
*   **Strengthen Experiments:** A crucial baseline is missing. Please compare the final nonparametric model against a standard parametric network of the *same final size* trained from scratch. This would directly show whether the dynamic training process finds a better solution. Figure 2 is also difficult to parse; please clarify why the nonparametric model would outperform a parametric one when the optimal final size is known.
*   **Technical Precision:** The definition of a "zero unit" is ambiguous. A unit is only functionally removable if both its fan-in and fan-out weights are zero. Please clarify this and the related claim that adding a "zero unit" increases the regularizer's value, which seems dependent on the initialization strategy.

**Questions for the Authors:**
1.  How sensitive is the final network structure to different random initializations?
2.  Could the regularization parameter λ, which appears to govern the final network size, be learned or adapted from the data?
3.  Does the theoretical guarantee of Theorem 1 still hold if fan-in and fan-out regularizers are combined?
