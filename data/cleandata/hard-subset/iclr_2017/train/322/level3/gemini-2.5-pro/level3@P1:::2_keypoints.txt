- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that grow and shrink within a single training run to auto-select network size.
- Proposes a non-probabilistic framework for optimizing architectures of varying sizes.
- Theorem 1: fan-in/fan-out regularizer ensures a global minimum at a finite network size, preventing uncontrolled growth.
- Implements periodic unit addition plus an L2 group-sparsity penalty to remove redundant units.
- Introduces a new optimizer (AdaRad) and a modified normalization scheme (CapNorm) for dynamic training.
- Recommendation: Accept — approach is novel and well-motivated as an alternative to expensive hyperparameter search.
- Recommendation: Accept — theoretical guarantee (Theorem 1) provides important soundness for the method.
- Strength: Casts model selection as optimization over an infinite-dimensional space made tractable by regularization.
- Strength: Elegant proof that group regularization yields a finite optimal number of parameters.
- Empirical: On MNIST and rectangles-images the method finds competitive or superior models, often with fewer parameters than exhaustive search.
- Empirical: Section 4.2 provides useful analysis of unit addition, learning, and pruning dynamics.
- Weakness: Underperforms on the convex dataset, indicating sensitivity of dynamics/initialization/regularization to problem type.
- Notable finding: Nonparametric training can sometimes outperform parametric nets of the same final size, suggesting the dynamic process may act as a regularizer or optimizer.
- Suggestion: Add an intuitive plain-English explanation of why Theorem 1 holds and relate it to Group LASSO.
- Suggestion: Analyze or hypothesize why nonparametric nets sometimes outperform parametric counterparts (e.g., curriculum effects or optimizer-regularizer interplay).
- Suggestion: Improve clarity of Figure 2 (explain black/red/blue plots and key takeaways).
- Question: How sensitive is final architecture to the regularization hyperparameter λ and can λ be set adaptively?
- Question: How robust are final architectures to random initialization and seed-to-seed variance?
- Question: Does Theorem 1 still hold if fan-in and fan-out regularizers are combined (additively)?
- Question: Clarify the claim that adding/removing zero units changes the fan-in/fan-out regularizers—adding a zero fan-in seems not to change the regularizer.
- Question: Clarify precise conditions for unit removal (when zero fan-in vs. zero fan-out make a unit removable).
