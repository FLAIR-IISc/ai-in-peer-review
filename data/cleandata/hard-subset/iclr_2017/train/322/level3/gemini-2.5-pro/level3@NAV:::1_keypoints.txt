- Proposes nonparametric neural networks that grow/shrink during a single training run by adding zero units and using a group-sparse fan-in regularizer.
- Provides a theoretical proof of soundness and reports promising results finding compact, high-performing models on benchmarks.
- Reviewer recommends rejection in current form but views the core idea as valuable and encourages resubmission.
- Main concern 1: AdaRad optimizer introduces significant and unquantified computational complexity, undermining the efficiency motivation.
- Main concern 2: Empirical performance is inconsistent, with a notable failure on the convex dataset that yields very small, underperforming networks.
- AdaRad complexity specifics: per-fan-in gradient decomposition and radial-angular updates; no wall-clock time comparisons to standard optimizers provided.
- Lack of analysis of the convex-dataset failure leaves unclear whether the problem is the regularizer, the optimizer, or something else.
- Recommendation: quantify AdaRad’s computational overhead with direct runtime comparisons against parametric training using Adam/RMSprop on identical hardware.
- Recommendation: investigate the convex dataset failure (regularization vs optimizer) and consider alternative unit initialization or adaptive add-unit schedules.
- Question for authors: provide wall-clock training time comparisons between nonparametric AdaRad and parametric Adam for networks of the same final size on identical hardware.
- Question for authors: hypothesize why the method converges to small, poorly performing networks on the convex dataset and whether this indicates a fundamental weakness.
- Question for authors: justify the necessity of radial-angular decomposition in AdaRad versus using a standard adaptive optimizer (e.g., Adam) with scaled regularization for new units.
