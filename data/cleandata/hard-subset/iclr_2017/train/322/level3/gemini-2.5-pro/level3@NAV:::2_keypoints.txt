- Introduces Nonparametric Neural Networks that grow and prune units during one training run using fan-in/fan-out group-sparsity regularization and the AdaRad optimizer.
- Theoretical contribution (Theorem 1): regularization guarantees a finite optimal network size, preventing uncontrolled growth.
- Decision: Reviewer recommends Accept, citing the novel, principled approach to model selection.
- Strength: Elegant data-driven mechanism to grow/shrink networks instead of relying on fixed priors.
- Strength: Sound theoretical guarantee provides a solid foundation for the dynamic process.
- Method is a practical alternative to exhaustive hyperparameter searches.
- Insightful connection to group-sparsity methods (e.g., Group Lasso) applied to dynamic network structure.
- Empirical results are mixed but promising: can find compact, high-performing models competitive with or superior to random search on some datasets.
- Suggestion: Clarify Theorem 1 with an intuitive, plain-English explanation and compare its sparsity effect to Lasso.
- Suggestion: Add a crucial baseline — train a standard parametric network of the same final size from scratch to compare against the nonparametric final model.
- Suggestion: Make Figure 2 clearer and explain why nonparametric training would outperform a parametric model when optimal final size is known.
- Suggestion/Technical: Define "zero unit" precisely (both fan-in and fan-out must be zero) and clarify claims about the regularizer’s change when adding a zero unit, considering initialization.
- Question: How sensitive is the final network structure to different random initializations?
- Question: Can the regularization parameter λ, which influences final size, be learned or adapted from data?
- Question: Does Theorem 1’s guarantee still hold when combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers?
