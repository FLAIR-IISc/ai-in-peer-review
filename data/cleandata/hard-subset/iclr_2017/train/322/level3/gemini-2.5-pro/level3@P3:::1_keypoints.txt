- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that grow and prune units within a single training run to find optimal network size.
- Periodically adds randomly initialized zero fan-out units and uses a group-sparse (L2 on fan-in) regularizer to prune ineffective units.
- Theorem 1: the p-norm fan-in/fan-out regularizer guarantees a global minimum at a finite network size.
- Proposes AdaRad optimizer that decomposes gradients into radial and angular components and normalizes only the angular part.
- Introduces supporting components: self-similar activation requirement and the CapNorm layer.
- Empirical results on MNIST and rectangles-images show compact, competitive, and sometimes superior architectures vs exhaustive random search.
