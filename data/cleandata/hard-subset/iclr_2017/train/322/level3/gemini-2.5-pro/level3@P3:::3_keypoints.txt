- Proposes nonparametric neural networks that dynamically add/remove units using group-sparsity (L2 on fan-in/fan-out) and a new AdaRad optimizer; proves a finite global minimum exists for the regularized objective.

- Decision: Reject.

- Central critique: New sensitive hyperparameters (λ, ν, νfreq, αr) effectively replace discrete architecture search and control final network size, making tuning equally difficult.

- Experimental shortcoming: No comparisons to established pruning/compression baselines, so practical benefit over simpler methods is unclear.

- Supporting: Final architecture and performance vary greatly with λ; tying αr to λ (αr = 150λ) couples model size control with training dynamics and can slow training.

- Supporting: Many prior methods use group-sparsity on fixed large networks; without such baselines the dynamic method’s advantage is unproven.

- Supporting: Experiments use small or nonstandard datasets (MNIST, poker); scalability to competitive benchmarks (e.g., CIFAR-10/100) is not demonstrated.

- Supporting: Poor performance on the "convex" dataset suggests a failure mode where the method converges to an overly small, suboptimal network.

- Recommendation: Add direct comparisons to pruning methods (e.g., train large net + group-lasso pruning) to evaluate accuracy-size trade-offs.

- Recommendation: Provide ablation/sensitivity studies for λ, ν, αr and decouple αr from λ to show how each hyperparameter affects outcomes.

- Recommendation: Benchmark on broader, standard datasets (CIFAR-10/100) to demonstrate practicality and scalability.

- Recommendation: Explicitly discuss theoretical limitations, clarifying the gap between minimizing regularized training loss and achieving good generalization.

- Question: Why were comparisons to network pruning techniques omitted, and how do you expect your method to perform versus pruning an oversized network with group-sparsity?

- Question: How difficult is tuning the new hyperparameters (λ, ν, αr) compared to a standard search over layer widths?

- Question: Did you experiment with decoupling αr from λ, and does that allow better control over unit survival and training dynamics?

- Question: Could the observed better generalization be due mainly to strong group-L2 regularization, and would a parametric network with similar regularization behave similarly?
