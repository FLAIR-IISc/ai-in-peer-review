- Paper proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that grow and shrink units during a single training run using group-sparsity on fan-in weights.  
- Authors prove that with this regularizer a global training-loss minimum can be achieved with a finite number of units.  
- Introduces AdaRad, a custom optimizer designed for dynamic architecture changes.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Primary rejection reason 1: Empirical evaluation lacks comparisons to established pruning and compression baselines.  
- Primary rejection reason 2: Method replaces one hyperparameter (network size) with several sensitive hyperparameters that appear hard to tune.  
- Supporting critique: Without pruning baselines, it’s unclear if the dynamic approach outperforms standard prune-then-finetune pipelines.  
- Supporting critique: Hyperparameters (addition frequency, λ, αr) interact to implicitly control final size and training time, risking a harder tuning problem.  
- Concern: Scalability and training efficiency are unclear due to coupling of model size and training time via hyperparameters.  
- Suggested improvement: Add thorough comparisons to established pruning methods (e.g., iterative magnitude and structured pruning).  
- Suggested improvement: Provide ablation/sensitivity analysis for key hyperparameters (λ, αr, addition frequency).  
- Suggested improvement: Evaluate on larger/standard benchmarks (e.g., CIFAR-10/100) and compare wall-clock time or FLOPs versus fixed architectures and pruning.  
- Questions for authors: Why omit pruning baselines and how do they expect final accuracy/model size to compare?  
- Questions for authors: How sensitive is the final architecture to random seed and unit-addition schedule?  
- Questions for authors: Is AdaRad essential, or does the method work with standard optimizers like Adam (possibly with modifications)?
