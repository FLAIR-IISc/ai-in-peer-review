- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that dynamically grow and prune units during a single training run using group-sparsity regularization.
- Key contributions: p fan-in/fan-out regularization scheme, Theorem 1 proving a finite global training-error minimum, and the AdaRad optimizer for dynamic architectures.
- Decision: Accept — paper is strong, novel, and tackles architecture selection practically.
- Reason 1: Novelty and practicality — allows capacity to adapt during one training pass, avoiding costly hyperparameter search.
- Reason 2: Sound theory — Theorem 1 guarantees the network won't grow without bound under the proposed regularizer.
- Strength: Principled optimization over both weights and network size, going beyond pruning and expensive architecture search.
- Strength: Theorem 1 ties the method to group-sparsity ideas (e.g., Group Lasso) and provides rigorous justification.
- Experiments: Mixed results on the convex dataset but promising results on MNIST and rectangles; sometimes outperforms fixed-size and random-search models.
- Insight: Growing-from-small training may act as curriculum learning or regularization, helping find better optima.
- Request: Add an intuitive, plain-English explanation of Theorem 1 and a brief comparison to Lasso/Group Lasso mechanisms.
- Request: Provide deeper analysis of why nonparametric models sometimes beat fixed-size counterparts (e.g., escaping local minima or implicit regularization).
- Request: Clarify the definition of "zero units," explain removability semantics (need both fan-in and fan-out zero to be functionally removable), and correct claims about effects on regularizer value.
- Request: Discuss sensitivity to λ (its effect on final architecture), possibilities for annealing/adapting λ, and stability across random initializations.
- Request: Theoretical/experimental follow-ups — whether Theorem 1 holds for combined fan-in/out regularizers, and compare final nonparametric models against a sweep of optimally trained fixed-size networks on a performance-vs-parameter plot.
