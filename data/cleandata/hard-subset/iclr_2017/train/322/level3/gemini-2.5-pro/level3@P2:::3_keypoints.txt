- Core idea: nonparametric neural networks dynamically add random units and remove redundant ones via group-sparsity (l2) regularization within a single training run.  
- Contribution 1: Theorem 1 proves a global minimum exists at a finite network size under the framework.  
- Contribution 2: AdaRad optimizer: separates angular (direction) and radial (magnitude) updates to handle growing/shrinking networks.  
- Contribution 3: Empirical results show compact networks can be found competitive with architectures from extensive random search.  
- Decision: Weak Accept.  
- Positive reason: Conceptually novel, well-motivated framework with solid theoretical grounding for automated architecture adaptation.  
- Negative reason: Claim of “automatic” sizing is weakened by sensitive new hyperparameters (λ, unit addition rate ν, radial step αr) that require tuning.  
- Practical concern: The final network size is highly dependent on λ, implying users may still need a search akin to architecture tuning.  
- Optimizer strength: AdaRad thoughtfully decouples feature direction learning from magnitude shrinkage, helping new units become useful.  
- Experimental gap: No comparisons to modern pruning/compression baselines (including pruning during training), which are direct competitors.  
- Scalability concern: Adding units every epoch may be a bottleneck on large datasets/models and could increase total training time.  
- Limited evaluation: Only one larger-scale experiment (poker) — more evidence on complex, large-scale problems is needed.  
- Suggested experiment: Ablation/sensitivity study showing how λ, ν, αr affect final size, performance, and training time.  
- Suggested metric: Report wall-clock training time of the nonparametric method vs total time for random-search baselines.  
- Suggested baseline: Compare against strong pruning methods (e.g., gradual magnitude pruning starting from oversized networks).  
- Question 1: Request intuition/practical guidance for tuning λ, ν, αr, αφ — principled defaults or still extensive trial-and-error?  
- Question 2: Any theoretical/empirical insights on generalization of final networks beyond the explicit l2 penalty?  
- Question 3: Is the observed better generalization with higher training error a consistent property or an artifact of hyperparameter settings?
