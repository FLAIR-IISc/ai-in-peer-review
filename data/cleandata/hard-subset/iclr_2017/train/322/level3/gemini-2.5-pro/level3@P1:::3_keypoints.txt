- Proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that grow by adding units and shrink via an l2 fan-in/out regularizer to find network size in one training run.
- Proves (Theorem 1) that with an l_p penalty (p < ∞) the regularized training objective has a global minimum at a finite network size.
- Introduces Capped Batch Normalization (CapNorm) to avoid interfering with the sparsifying regularizer.
- Introduces AdaRad, an optimizer tailored for units with dynamically changing weight norms.
- Experiments on three benchmarks show the method can produce compact networks competitive with fixed-size architectures found by random search.
- Recommendation: Reject.
- Main reason 1: Method swaps architecture search for a new, complex hyperparameter search (λ, ν, ν_freq, α_r) that appears equally or more difficult to tune.
- Main reason 2: Experimental evaluation omits crucial baselines—particularly pruning/compression methods—and thus fails to demonstrate practical advantages.
- Supporting: Final network size strongly depends on regularization λ, making λ a proxy for capacity that still requires tuning.
- Supporting: Other hyperparameters (unit addition rate ν, addition frequency, optimizer radial step α_r) and the chosen coupling α_r = 150λ introduce additional sensitive "magic" constants.
- Supporting: No evidence that searching the new hyperparameter space is easier than standard architecture search.
- Supporting: The paper dismisses pruning oversimply; many pruning methods operate during training and should be compared (including training time and compute).
- Supporting: Experiments are limited to small/low-complexity datasets; scalability to modern deep architectures (e.g., deeper convnets on CIFAR-10/100) is untested.
- Clarification: Theorem 1 guarantees a finite-size minimizer for the training objective but makes no claim about generalization performance.
- Suggested ablation: Isolate AdaRad's contribution by comparing it to Adam/RMSprop on fixed-size networks.
- Suggested improvement: Use an adaptive unit-addition schedule (e.g., add units when loss plateaus) rather than a fixed one-per-epoch.
- Suggested efficiency metrics: Report FLOPs and actual inference time in addition to parameter counts.
- Question: How sensitive are final architecture and performance to ν and its frequency?
- Question: Why were pruning methods not included as baselines, and how does the method compare in performance, size, and total training cost?
- Question: Was applying weight decay to batch-norm's gamma considered as an alternative to developing CapNorm?
