- Paper proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that grow/shrink width during a single training run via zero-output additions and group-regularizer-driven removals.
- Theoretical result: fan-in/fan-out ℓp (1≤p<∞) penalties guarantee the global training objective has a finite-dimensional minimizer.
- Introduces CapNorm (batch-norm variant that preserves regularizer effect) and AdaRad (optimizer splitting gradients into radial/angular components) for this setting.
- Empirical evaluation on MNIST, rectangles-images, convex, and Poker shows mixed results: sometimes smaller/better models, but underperforms on convex.
- Decision: Reject.
- Primary reasons for rejection: empirical validation is unconvincing (missing strong baselines, mixed results, unclear scalability/wall-clock efficiency).
- Secondary reason: many new hyperparameters (λ, αr, αφ, addition rate/frequency) implicitly control final width, shifting rather than eliminating tuning burden.
- Positioning is reasonable and the finite-minimum theorem addresses a key concern, but experimental baselines omit important structured-sparsity/pruning/regrowth and compression methods.
- Claims oversell "single-run architecture selection" because hyperparameter sensitivity and tuning burden are insufficiently analyzed.
- Efficiency claims lack wall-clock time-to-target, FLOPs, memory, and hardware-efficiency comparisons vs. parametric and pruning baselines.
- CapNorm and AdaRad are thoughtful, and the unit lifecycle analysis is insightful, but no ablations isolate their benefits from the nonparametric framework.
- Experiments should include neuron-level group-lasso baselines, modern pruning with regrowth, Net2Net-style growth, dynamic network surgery, and post-training compression/distillation.
- Report runtime/compute metrics: time-to-target-accuracy, total FLOPs, memory during training and inference, and dense vs. sparse efficiency.
- Study hyperparameter sensitivity systematically and, if possible, propose online controllers or validation-driven adaptation for λ and growth/shrink schedules.
- Broaden benchmarks to convolutional/deeper nets (CIFAR, ImageNet subsets) and report stability across random seeds (variance of final widths).
- Ablations needed: AdaRad vs. Adam/RMSProp, CapNorm vs. BN (with/without affine), p=1 vs. p=2 penalties, fan-in vs. fan-out.
- Clarify theory–practice link: provide partial guarantees or diagnostics about growth/shrink dynamics under stochastic training to avoid under/over-shrinking.
- Reviewer questions for authors include sensitivity of widths/accuracy to hyperparameters, data- or gradient-driven addition policies, comparisons to fixed-width group-lasso and pruning-with-regrowth, wall-clock/runtime comparisons vs. baselines, CapNorm ablations, experiments on conv/deeper nets, and variability of final unit counts across seeds.
