- Proposal: Introduces nonparametric neural networks that learn layer widths in a single run via group-sparsity regularization, dynamic unit add/remove, CapNorm, and AdaRad.  
- Theorem: Proves (Theorem 1) the global training objective attains its minimum at a finite network size for fan-in or fan-out regularizers (1 ≤ p < ∞, λ > 0).  
- CapNorm: Proposes a batch-norm modification compatible with group shrinkage to avoid premature removal of new units.  
- AdaRad: Introduces an optimizer that decouples angular and radial updates so new units can learn before being shrunk.  
- Empirical: Competitive or superior to strong parametric baselines on MNIST, rectangles, and 1M-example Poker; performs worse on a convex dataset.  
- Decision: Accept — work is novel, ambitious, and timely.  
- Main accept reasons: finite-minimizer theorem grounds the approach vs heuristics; the training scheme is practical and scales to large data.  
- Problem significance: Reduces expensive architecture size searches by learning width during training and may find better optimization paths.  
- Literature placement: Distinct from pruning-from-large, black-box search, and scalar sparsity; grows from scratch with unit-wise group shrinkage.  
- Self-similar nonlinearities: Proposition shows for activations like ReLU, layer-wise λ’s can be consolidated, easing tuning.  
- Empirical nuance: Nonparametric nets sometimes outperform parametric models of the same final size, implying path-dependent or curriculum effects.  
- Convex failure: Performance gap on convex dataset indicates limitations and helpful boundary conditions.  
- Clarify theorem intuition: Reviewer asks for a short, high-level plain-English explanation of the proof and main steps.  
- Clarify zero-unit/regularizer interactions: Need explicit statement of when adding zero units changes the regularizer for Ωin vs Ωout.  
- Ablation request (dynamic vs final retrain): Retrain discovered final architecture from scratch (with same normalization/optimizer) to isolate benefits of dynamic growth.  
- Figure 2: Figure is busy—improve legends, colors/markers, and captions to summarize takeaways per dataset.  
- Efficiency metrics: Provide wall-clock, FLOPs, peak memory, and bookkeeping overhead comparisons vs parametric runs.  
- CapNorm ablation: Compare CapNorm to BN (with/without affine), LayerNorm, and GroupNorm within the framework to quantify necessity.  
- AdaRad ablation: Show training with RMSprop/Adam plus scaled shrinkage to justify AdaRad’s radial/angular split and report hyperparameter sensitivity (αr, αφ, β).  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (λ): Empirically report how strongly λ determines final width and performance and whether λ can be predicted or adaptively tuned.  
- Initialization sensitivity: Report dispersion (mean ± std) of final widths and performance across random seeds to assess robustness.  
- Combined regularizers: Ask whether Theorem 1 extends to Ω = Ωin + Ωout (or max), or what modifications are needed.  
- Zero-unit mechanism symmetry: Ask whether adding zero fan-in vs zero fan-out behaves symmetrically when using the opposite regularizer and whether authors tested both.  
- Other activations: Ask whether leaky/parametric ReLU or ELU preserve the λ-equivalence proposition in practice and whether they improve stability/accuracy.  
- Diagnose convex gap: Request analysis of failure mode on convex dataset and whether less aggressive λ, different addition schedule, or delayed shrinkage helps.  
- Fairness of baselines: Suggest including parametric baselines trained with CapNorm or matched regularization to ensure fair comparisons.
