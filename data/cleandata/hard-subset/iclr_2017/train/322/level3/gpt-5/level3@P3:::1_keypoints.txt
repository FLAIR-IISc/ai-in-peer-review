- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that learn layer widths during a single training run by adding zero-initialized units and removing units via a sparse group regularizer; theorem proves a finite-dimensional global optimum exists.

- Proposes CapNorm (batch-norm variant to avoid scale compensation) and AdaRad (optimizer that splits fan-in gradients into radial and angular components, applying shrinkage to radial updates and normalized angular updates).

- Empirically often converges to compact architectures and can outperform same-size parametric nets on MNIST and rectangles, but underperforms on the convex dataset; a large-scale poker experiment shows feasibility.

- Recommendation: Reject (borderline) mainly because AdaRad’s computational and wall-clock overheads are not quantified and empirical support is mixed due to failure on the convex dataset.

- Strength: tackles an important practical problem (online capacity learning) with a simple f-equivalence growth trick and a useful theoretical guarantee under group regularization.

- Weakness: growth-by-random-addition plus regularizer can resemble random architecture search; lacks direct comparisons to structured-sparsity and grow/prune baselines that don’t require AdaRad/CapNorm.

- Attribution unclear: paper does not separate gains from dynamic architecture, AdaRad, and CapNorm—ablation studies are missing.

- Efficiency concerns: AdaRad requires per-fan-in inner products, norms, rotations, and caches which may be costly and GPU-unfriendly, but no FLOP/memory or wall-clock measurements are provided.

- Experimental protocol issues: reliance on “representative” runs and manual annealing/rewinding risks bias; medians, variances, and time-to-accuracy should be reported.

- Requested computational analyses: quantify per-iteration FLOPs and memory vs SGD/Adam/RMSprop, provide GPU/CPU runtimes, throughput vs batch size, and time-to-target-accuracy plots.

- Recommended ablations: fixed-width networks trained with AdaRad vs Adam/RMSprop; nonparametric training with standard optimizers and group-lasso; CapNorm vs BN/weight-normalization alternatives.

- Suggested growth/regularization studies: vary unit addition rate, initialization scale, λ schedules, and test λ annealing or warm-start without regularization.

- Diagnose convex dataset failure: test hypotheses about over-shrinkage, CapNorm caps, or AdaRad’s radial shrinkage; report learned widths over time, gradient norms, and retrain final widths with parametric baselines.

- Statistical/reporting improvements: report medians and interquartile ranges across runs, match compute budgets and early-stopping between methods, and normalize accuracy by model size and time.

- Relate to prior work: compare to structured group-lasso baselines, prune-after-train methods, Net2Net, and modern NAS/grow-prune strategies.

- Implementation practicality: discuss efficient GPU implementation of per-fan-in rotations, numerical stability when fan-in norms are near zero, and applicability beyond ReLU activations.

- Key questions for authors: exact per-iteration cost of AdaRad (dot products/norms/rotations), GPU wall-clock impact, timing curves to fixed validation errors, sensitivity to αr/αφ/λ/addition rate, reasons for convex failure, ablation results isolating each component’s effect, BN compatibility strategies, and scalability to conv/deeper nets.
