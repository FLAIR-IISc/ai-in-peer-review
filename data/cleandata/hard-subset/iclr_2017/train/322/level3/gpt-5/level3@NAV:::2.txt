Summary and contributions
The paper proposes nonparametric neural networks that grow and shrink during a single training run via automatic addition/removal of units, trained with a novel optimizer (AdaRad) and a capped batch-normalization variant (CapNorm). I concur with Reviewer 2 that the automatic control of width is a timely and compelling direction that reduces reliance on user priors. The key theoretical result (Theorem 1) shows that with fan-in or fan-out regularization (ℓp, 1 ≤ p < ∞), the global training error attains its minimum at a finite number of parameters, preventing unbounded growth. Empirical results on MNIST, rectangles, convex, and a large poker dataset demonstrate that the approach can find compact, competitive models.

Decision: Accept (rating 7)
Primary reasons: (1) Strong, well-scoped theoretical guarantee establishing finite optimal size under clear conditions; (2) A practically viable training recipe with promising evidence that adaptive width can match or exceed fixed-size baselines.

Supporting arguments
- The theoretical guarantee is novel and valuable for this line of work; it resembles group-sparsity arguments (Lasso/Elastic Net) and positions the paper well in the literature.
- The empirical evaluation indicates the method can be competitive and sometimes superior in accuracy-parameter trade-offs, with a large-scale demonstration on poker.

Additional feedback and questions (to improve the paper; not all are decision-critical)
- Please provide a plain-English intuition for Theorem 1. Relate it explicitly to group Lasso/Elastic Net: what structural property ensures a finite optimum, and how does p influence which structures are pruned?
- Figure 2 is hard to parse and mixed: clarify axes, depict dispersion across seeds, and explain why nonparametric nets sometimes outperform parametric ones even when the final architecture is known (hypothesis: better curriculum/warm-start and effective regularization?).
- Add an experiment: compare the adaptive model to fixed-size nets with the same final size (train-from-scratch vs grow-then-prune), reporting accuracy, memory, and compute to substantiate efficiency claims.
- Zero units and regularizer: with a fan-in penalty, adding a unit with zero fan-in should not change Ω, whereas your practice adds zero fan-out and random fan-in, which does. Please clarify this subtlety and reconcile the statement that “adding zero units changes the regularizer.”
- Define precisely when a unit is removable without changing f: is “either fan-in or fan-out zero” sufficient, or must both be zero for safe removal?
- Hyperparameters: Can λ be predicted from data (e.g., via cross-validation, SURE, or a proxy) and does λ primarily determine final structure/size?
- Initialization sensitivity: how variable are final structures across seeds, and how does this affect test error?
- Combining fan-in and fan-out penalties: does Theorem 1 extend to their combination (sum or mixed-norm)? If not, what fails?
- Please allocate space from related work to deepen the theorem’s intuition and practical guidance.

I updated my rating to 7, contingent on addressing these clarifications.
