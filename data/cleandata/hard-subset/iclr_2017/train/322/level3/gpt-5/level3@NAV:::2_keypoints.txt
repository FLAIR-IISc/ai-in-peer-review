- Paper proposes nonparametric neural networks that grow and shrink in a single training run via automatic unit addition/removal, trained with AdaRad and CapNorm.  
- Theorem 1: with fan-in or fan-out ℓp regularization (1 ≤ p < ∞) the global training error attains its minimum at a finite number of parameters, preventing unbounded growth.  
- Empirical results on MNIST, rectangles, convex, and a large poker dataset show the method can find compact, competitive models.  
- Decision: Accept (rating 7).  
- Primary reasons for accept: (1) a strong, well-scoped theoretical guarantee of finite optimal size; (2) a practical training recipe with evidence adaptive width matches or exceeds fixed-size baselines.  
- Theoretical guarantee is novel, resembles group-sparsity (Lasso/Elastic Net), and situates the work well in the literature.  
- Empirical evaluation is promising and includes a large-scale demonstration on poker.  
- Ask for a plain-English intuition for Theorem 1 and an explicit relation to group Lasso/Elastic Net, including how p influences pruning structure.  
- Figure 2 is hard to parse: clarify axes, show dispersion across seeds, and explain why nonparametric nets sometimes outperform parametric ones even when final architecture is known.  
- Request an experiment comparing adaptive models to fixed-size nets of the same final size (train-from-scratch vs grow-then-prune) reporting accuracy, memory, and compute.  
- Clarify the “zero units and regularizer” subtlety: with a fan-in penalty, adding a unit with zero fan-in should not change Ω, but practice adds zero fan-out and random fan-in—reconcile this with the paper’s statement.  
- Precisely define when a unit is removable without changing f: is “either fan-in or fan-out zero” sufficient, or must both be zero?  
- Hyperparameters: can λ be predicted from data (e.g., cross-validation, SURE, proxy) and does λ primarily determine final structure/size?  
- Initialization sensitivity: report variability of final structures across seeds and the effect on test error.  
- Combining fan-in and fan-out penalties: does Theorem 1 extend to their combination (sum or mixed-norm)? If not, explain what fails.  
- Suggest reallocating space from related work to deepen theorem intuition and provide practical guidance.  
- Reviewer updated rating to 7, contingent on addressing these clarifications.
