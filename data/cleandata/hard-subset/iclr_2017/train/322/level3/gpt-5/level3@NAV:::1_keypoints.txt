- Problem & contribution: Proposes nonparametric neural networks that grow by adding zero units and shrink via sparse fan-in (group-ℓ2), with a theorem guaranteeing a finite optimal size.
- Optimizer & norm: Introduces AdaRad (decomposes fan-in gradients into parallel/orthogonal components for radial–angular updates) and CapNorm (modified normalization to preserve the regularizer).
- Empirical results: Often finds compact models competitive with fixed-size baselines on MNIST/rectangles and scales to poker, but shows inconsistent support overall.
- Decision: Reject (borderline) primarily because runtime/memory overheads of AdaRad are not quantified and results underperform on a convex dataset with overly small networks.
- Novelty & theory: Clear, well-motivated formulation connecting growth/pruning to sparse regularization and a valuable finite-optimum theoretical result.
- Positioning to literature: Should more directly compare to simpler baselines (standard optimizers + group-ℓ2 + occasional unit injection) and situate between pruning/group-lasso and NAS.
- Runtime/overhead concern: AdaRad’s per-fan-in orthogonal projections/rotations may add significant cost, but no FLOP or wall-clock comparisons are provided.
- Convex-dataset weakness: Method converges to very small widths on the convex dataset without sufficient analysis of why or how to mitigate it.
- Requested complexity reporting: Provide precise per-iteration computational and memory complexity and measured training time to reach target validation error versus SGD/RMSprop/Adam (with/without group-ℓ2).
- Ablation studies requested: (a) AdaRad vs RMSprop/Adam with group-ℓ2 and same add/prune scheme; (b) CapNorm vs BatchNorm-without-learnable-params; (c) unit addition rate/schedule; (d) p=1 vs p=2 penalties.
- Convex dataset analysis requested: Show learning curves of width, validation error vs width, and test early stopping or relaxed λ/αr to see if shrinkage is avoidable.
- Search-nature clarification: Clarify whether the method is akin to random search over discrete architectures or a guided local search and quantify how often added units survive and contribute.
- Question on cost: What are the exact extra FLOPs and memory per layer/unit for AdaRad vs RMSprop/Adam and wall-clock time to reach the same validation error?
- Question on AdaRad alone: Does AdaRad without growth outperform standard optimizers when using group-ℓ2?
- Question on convex failure: Is the very small network outcome on the convex dataset due to CapNorm, λ choice, αr/αφ balance, or data geometry?
- Question on initialization sensitivity: How sensitive are results to initializing new units with zero fan-out versus small nonzero fan-out?
- Question on unit survival: Report the survival rate and usefulness of added units over time and the effects of alternative addition schedules.
