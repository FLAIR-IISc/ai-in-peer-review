- Proposal: Introduces "nonparametric neural networks" that learn layer widths in a single training run by adding zero units and removing units via group sparsity (fan-in/fan-out ℓp penalties, focus p=2).
- Theoretical result: Theorem 1 shows the objective with fan-in/fan-out ℓp regularization attains a global minimum at finite dimensionality, preventing unbounded growth.
- Training components: Proposes CapNorm (batch-norm variant compatible with weight shrinkage) and AdaRad (radial–angular optimizer that normalizes only angular updates); AdaRad-M (momentum) also discussed.
- Empirical findings: On MNIST and Rectangles-Images, nonparametric networks match or exceed parametric baselines and approach best random-search results using fewer parameters.
- Scalability: Demonstrates competitiveness on a large Poker dataset, including a run with zero test errors, and analyzes growth–prune dynamics in detail.
- Decision: Reviewer recommends Weak Accept.
- Reason for acceptance 1: Tackles important automatic width-selection problem with a principled framework and nontrivial theoretical guarantee; CapNorm + AdaRad addresses a real optimization tension.
- Reason for acceptance 2: Experiments show compact, accurate models and feasible scaling beyond toy datasets.
- Motivation/support: Well-positioned relative to pruning, hyperparameter search, and growth literature; f-equivalence via zero units cleanly enables topology changes without changing function.
- Theory support: Theorem 1 and supporting lemmas appear technically sound; Proposition 1 clarifies benefits for self-similar nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU).
- Method support: CapNorm preserves regularizer effect; AdaRad’s radial–angular decomposition elegantly targets incompatibility between gradient normalization and group shrinkage.
- Empirics support: Careful protocol (validation-driven hyperparameter selection, matched baselines, random-search comparisons) and dynamics analysis increase confidence in the method.
- Main concern — computational cost: AdaRad’s per-unit decompositions raise questions about wall-clock runtime and memory overhead; paper lacks practical runtime/memory comparisons to standard optimizers and batchnorm.
- Main concern — robustness: Underperformance on Convex dataset suggests sensitivity to growth/shrinkage hyperparameters (λ, αr, αφ, addition rate) and potential bias toward overly compact models.
- Suggested remedy: Include wall-clock/time/memory measurements and cost breakdown for AdaRad vs. SGD/RMSprop/Adam across datasets.
- Suggested remedy: Provide consolidated ablations (CapNorm vs batchnorm, AdaRad vs RMSprop/Adam, p=1 vs p=2, fan-in vs fan-out, addition frequency/scale).
- Baseline fairness: Ensure parametric random-search baselines use similar compute budgets or report performance as a function of compute.
- Hyperparameter guidance: Clarify sensitivity and guidance for αr, αφ, λ, and addition rate; formalize or empirically justify the αr ≈ 150/λ heuristic.
- Generalization: Discuss applicability beyond MLPs (convolutions, depth selection) or provide preliminary conv experiments to broaden impact.
- Terminology: Note that calling the method “nonparametric” may confuse readers; add a short clarification relating to classical nonparametrics.
- Theory scope: Give concrete examples of admissible nonlinearities and losses, and note any corner cases where CapNorm might affect theoretical assumptions.
- Visualization: Add plots of unit birth/death rates and distributions of fan-in norms across runs to summarize growth–prune dynamics.
- Question — runtime: What are wall-clock overheads, GPU utilization, and peak memory for AdaRad+CapNorm vs RMSprop/Adam+batchnorm on MNIST, Rectangles, Convex, Poker?
- Question — sensitivity: How sensitive are final size and accuracy to λ, αr, αφ, addition rate, and initial widths; provide sweeps or recommendations?
- Question — Convex failure: Why does the method shrink too aggressively on Convex; do adaptive λ schedules, different addition rates, or fan-out regularization help?
- Question — CapNorm effect: Empirically, does CapNorm preserve the group penalty effect (compare fan-in norm distributions vs batchnorm); are there cases with σ < 1 that weaken radial/angular separation?
- Question — retrain parametric: If you select size with nonparametric training then retrain that parametric network with best optimizer+batchnorm, how close is performance?
- Question — extensions: Any preliminary results or comments on convolutional layers or adaptive depth, and does radial–angular decomposition generalize to conv kernels (fan-in per output channel)?
