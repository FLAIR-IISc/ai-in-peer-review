- Proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that dynamically add/remove units during a single training run using lp group penalties on fan-in/fan-out.  
- Proves that with λ>0 and 1≤p<∞, the training objective attains a global minimum at finite width.  
- Introduces CapNorm (batch-norm variant that preserves regularizer shrinkage) and AdaRad (optimizer decomposing radial/angular gradients per unit).  
- Shows self-similar nonlinearities let per-layer regularization strengths collapse to one effective λ up to rescaling.  
- Experiments show compact final architectures and competitive performance on MNIST, rectangles-images, and a large poker dataset, but weak results on the convex task.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Primary empirical concern: missing key comparisons to strong pruning/compression/growing baselines.  
- Primary practicality concern: many new hyperparameters control model size and dynamics; scalability and training-time overhead not convincingly addressed.  
- Motivation and mechanism are timely and coherent; theorem is a meaningful theoretical contribution for the training objective.  
- The existence theorem concerns the training objective only and does not guarantee generalization; method can under-size and hurt accuracy.  
- Novelty of AdaRad and CapNorm is interesting, but limited ablations leave unclear how much they contribute versus the nonparametric mechanism or tuned standard optimizers.  
- Empirical results are mixed: encouraging on some datasets, poor on convex, and lacking comparisons to established structured-sparsity/pruning and growth methods.  
- Missing baselines include group-lasso/structured sparsity training, magnitude pruning, Dynamic Network Surgery, Net2Net growth, and modern compression pipelines.  
- Hyperparameter burden: radial/angular step sizes, add-rate/frequency, moving-average rates, and thresholds many of which implicitly determine final size and training time; sensitivity analyses are limited.  
- Growth strategy can delay convergence; wall-clock and GPU-hour comparisons at matched accuracy are missing.  
- Scope limitation: experiments restricted to fully connected ReLU nets; extensions to convnets and deeper architectures not demonstrated.  
- Suggested experiments: add strong pruning/growth baselines, end-to-end compute/time and final model size comparisons.  
- Suggested ablations: AdaRad vs RMSprop/Adam, CapNorm vs BatchNorm/no-norm, fan-in vs fan-out penalties, p=1 vs p=2, and effects of unit-addition schedule and step-sizes.  
- Suggested reporting: training time/memory overhead of add/remove and AdaRad, convergence speed vs fixed larger nets, and sensitivity plots for key hyperparameters.  
- Suggested robustness work: provide tuning guidance or adaptive controllers for ν, νfreq, αr, αφ, λ and explore λ annealing or per-layer λ.  
- Investigate generalization failure modes (e.g., convex task), and whether relaxing regularization late recovers accuracy.  
- Extend evaluation to convolutional layers via per-channel group penalties and efficient fan-in/out decomposition.  
- Questions for authors include sensitivity of final size/accuracy to αr/αφ/ν/νfreq; wall-clock vs parametric baselines at matched accuracy; empirical comparison to structured sparsity/pruning; zero-unit thresholding strategy in finite precision; independent effects of AdaRad and CapNorm; reasons for convex underperformance and remedies; concrete modifications for convnets/deeper nets; and empirical p=1 vs p=2 tradeoffs.
