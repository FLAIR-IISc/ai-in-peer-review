- Proposes nonparametric neural nets that grow/shrink layer width during one training run via zero-units and group-sparsity, with a theorem guaranteeing a finite-width global minimum under fan-in/out regularization.  
- Introduces CapNorm (batch-norm variant preserving shrinkage) and AdaRad (optimizer splitting radial/angular gradient components) to let new units learn while maintaining group shrinkage.  
- Initial experiments indicate the method can produce smaller networks with similar accuracy and potential runtime/power/memory benefits; paper is clearly written and conceptually elegant.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Primary reason 1: Empirical evaluation is limited and lacks comparisons to strong pruning/compression and NAS baselines, so practical competitiveness is unclear.  
- Primary reason 2: Key hyperparameters (add frequency, λ, radial/angular steps) implicitly control both final model size and training dynamics, risking training-time blowups and difficult tuning.  
- Tackled problem is important: learning network size during training instead of expensive outer-loop search.  
- Placement is partly strong (connects group sparsity and scale-aware training) but underdeveloped relative to modern structured pruning, network slimming, and resource-aware compression.  
- Theory shows existence of finite minimizer but does not address generalization or over/under-fitting.  
- Experiments are small-scale, omit hardware/resource metrics, and do not compare against strong baselines (e.g., network slimming, group-lasso, MorphNet/NetAdapt/AMC, pruning-with-retraining, lightweight NAS).  
- Hyperparameters jointly govern size and cost, raising scalability concerns for large models.  
- Suggested improvements: add head-to-head comparisons with strong baselines and report accuracy, params, FLOPs, latency, energy, and memory on real hardware.  
- Suggested improvements: decouple capacity-controlling hyperparameters from optimization ones (e.g., adaptive λ scheduling or bilevel tuning).  
- Suggested improvements: provide ablations on add/remove frequency, unit initialization, and p=1 vs p=2 group penalties.  
- Suggested improvements: demonstrate on convolutional networks and a large-scale task; quantify AdaRad training-time/memory overhead vs Adam/RMSprop.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss generalization (Rademacher/flatness proxies) and conditions when capacity adaptation helps or hurts.  
- Questions: how to set add-frequency, λ, and αr/αφ to avoid collapse or uncontrolled growth—any adaptive controller?  
- Questions: what are wall-clock and memory overheads of AdaRad vs Adam/RMSprop at scale?  
- Questions: why were structured pruning/compression and resource-aware methods not compared, and do resulting networks remain competitive in latency/energy?  
- Questions: can hyperparameters controlling size be decoupled from optimization to improve scalability?  
- Questions: how sensitive are results to unit initialization and to choosing fan-in vs fan-out penalties?  
- Questions: can the method handle convolutional layers and learn depth (layer-level add/remove)?
