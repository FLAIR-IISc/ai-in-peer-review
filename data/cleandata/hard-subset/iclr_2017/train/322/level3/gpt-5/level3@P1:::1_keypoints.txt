- Tackles selecting network size during training by growing and pruning units in a single run using a nonprobabilistic nonparametric neural network framework.
- Theorem 1: with fan-in or fan-out group-regularization (Lp, 1 ≤ p < ∞) the training objective has a global minimum at a finite network size.
- Introduces CapNorm, a capped batch-normalization variant compatible with norm-based shrinkage.
- Introduces AdaRad, which decomposes gradients into radial and angular components and normalizes only the angular component to protect new units.
- Empirically converges to reasonable sizes, sometimes outperforms comparable parametric networks, competes with stronger black-box searches on one benchmark, and scales to a large poker dataset.
- Decision: Weak accept.
- Key positive reason: novel, well-motivated framework with a nontrivial theoretical guarantee and practical algorithmic tools (CapNorm, AdaRad).
- Key caveat: promising but mixed empirical results and missing wall-clock/runtime evaluations.
- Problem significance: well-positioned as a principled alternative to expensive architecture search, connecting discrete unit add/remove to continuous optimization.
- Literature/motivation: appropriate shift to L2 group penalties for hardware considerations and valid emphasis on self-similar nonlinearities (ReLU).
- Technical validity: Theorem 1 and proof strategy appear sound; CapNorm and AdaRad designs align with group-shrinkage geometry.
- Empirical support: strong MNIST/rectangles results, detailed training-dynamics analysis, and scalable poker experiment.
- Limitation: fails or underperforms on a convex dataset without sufficient explanation or remedies.
- Limitation: no measured wall-clock/GPU runtime, memory overhead, or quantified cost of per-fan-in projection and radial–angular updates.
- Limitation: ablations are limited—need isolation of CapNorm, AdaRad, p=1 vs p=2, fan-in vs fan-out effects, and λ sensitivity.
- Requested: wall-clock/throughput comparisons (images/sec), memory usage, and scaling across SGD, RMSprop/Adam, and AdaRad on CPU/GPU for representative widths.
- Requested ablations: CapNorm vs BatchNorm (with/without affine), AdaRad vs adapted Adam/RMSprop, p=1 vs p=2 and fan-in vs fan-out, unit addition rate/frequency and initialization scale.
- Requested analysis of convex failure: diagnostics such as layerwise activations, fraction of eliminated units, gradient norms, and λ/ν schedules and suggested fixes.
- Requested reporting of early-phase performance overhead (extra epochs to match fixed-width baselines) and whether annealing or pausing pruning helps.
- Requested clarification/comparison to pruning-from-scratch and experiments starting from moderately large networks to separate growth benefits from pruning.
- Requested discussion/experiment on applicability to CNNs and residual architectures and whether CapNorm/AdaRad/group penalties transfer.
- Questions for authors: measured wall-clock overhead of AdaRad vs SGD/RMSprop/Adam (per-iteration time, memory, total time-to-target) for typical hidden sizes on GPU.
- Question about gradient decomposition stability for very small fan-in norms: clipping, ε choices, and sensitivity.
- Question about CapNorm necessity/sufficiency to avoid canceling the regularizer and effect of affine BN parameters.
- Question about hyperparameter robustness (αφ, αr, λ, ν) and whether simple default rules exist.
- Question about concrete diagnostics and remedies for the convex failure case (e.g., relaxing shrinkage or scheduling ν).
- Question about generality: fan-out penalties, deeper MLPs, convolutional layers, and any obstacles applying AdaRad to convolutional kernels.
- Requested baseline: include a “prune-from-large” baseline using the L2 group penalty (no growth) to isolate benefits of dynamic growth.
- Reproducibility request: release code and scripts to reproduce unit add/remove schedules, CapNorm, AdaRad, and analysis plots.
