- Problem: automatic selection of network width in a single training run to avoid expensive multi-run architecture search.
- Method: dynamic nonparametric framework that adds zero-units and removes units via group-sparsity on fan-ins/outs, deleting units when entire fan is zero.
- Theory: proof that ℓp fan-in/out regularization (λ>0, 1≤p<∞) yields a global minimum at finite network size, preventing unbounded width.
- Optimization/normalization: introduces CapNorm (batch-norm variant compatible with shrinkage) and AdaRad, which normalizes angular gradient components so new units can learn before shrinkage.
- Empirics: encouraging results on MNIST/rectangles and a large poker dataset (compact, strong models), but underperforms on a convex dataset and experiments are limited to MLPs.
- Broader vision: elegant approach aligned with lifelong learning and continual capacity allocation.
- Decision: Reject.
- Primary reasons for rejection: limited empirical validation (no direct comparisons to state-of-the-art pruning/compression) and unclear practicality due to many sensitive hyperparameters and potential training slowdown/scalability issues.
- Motivation/literature placement: conceptually clean with links to group sparsity and prior pruning/growth work, but competitiveness is uncertain without modern compression baselines.
- Theory limitations: existence theorem is useful but does not address generalization, convergence of dynamics, avoidance of bad minima, or width oscillations in practice.
- Empirics limitations: only MLPs shown, no convnets or large-scale vision tasks, and wall-clock/compute/memory costs are not systematically reported.
- Missing baselines: no direct comparisons to structured pruning, dynamic network surgery, channel pruning, distillation-based compression, or grow-then-prune pipelines.
- Hyperparameter/practicality concerns: many sensitive knobs (λ, αr, αφ, addition rate/frequency, mixing/rewind schedules) influence both final size and training dynamics, complicating tuning and risking slow training.
- Ablation requests: isolate CapNorm vs BN variants, AdaRad vs Adam/RMSprop (including AdaRad-M), and report per-step cost/memory and wall-clock time-to-target.
- Sensitivity/stability requests: show how final width and accuracy vary with λ, αr, αφ, addition schedule, and random seeds.
- Scaling requests: demonstrate channel-level grouping for conv layers (CIFAR/ImageNet) or provide preliminary results/roadmap for convs and more tasks.
- Generalization analysis: provide train/test curves and investigate whether group regularization encourages or impedes overfitting.
- Algorithmic refinements suggested: adaptive/addition policies, Net2Net-like initializations for new units, and decoupling hyperparameters for training speed vs final size.
- Clarity and reproducibility: succinctly summarize growth/deletion schedule, give practical defaults, and include a resource table with compute, peak memory, and test-time metrics.
- Key questions for authors: sensitivity of final size to hyperparameters and guidance for defaults.
- Key questions for authors: wall-clock training overhead, compute (GPU-hours), peak memory, and test-time latency/energy compared to baselines.
- Key questions for authors: explanation for underperformance on the convex dataset (premature shrinkage, insufficient additions, optimizer/normalization issues).
- Key questions for authors: can AdaRad benefits be replicated with Adam/RMSprop by normalizing only angular components, and what breaks if attempted?
- Key questions for authors: empirical comparison of CapNorm vs BN-without-learnable-params under group-lasso.
- Key questions for authors: plans and preliminary results for channel-level penalties and unit addition in conv layers (CIFAR/ImageNet).
- Key questions for authors: methods to separate hyperparameters controlling training dynamics from those controlling final size (e.g., two-stage training or λ annealing).
- Key questions for authors: have smarter initializations for new units (Net2Net/copying) been tried to shorten trial periods?
- Key questions for authors: does the theorem extend to residual connections, mixed nonlinearities, or p=∞, and can dynamic depth selection be handled?
- Key questions for authors: do parameter count reductions translate to consistent test-time FLOPs/latency reductions on hardware compared to pruning/compression baselines?
