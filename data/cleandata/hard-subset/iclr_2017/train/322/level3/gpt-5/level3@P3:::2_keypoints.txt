- Introduces "nonparametric neural networks" that adapt hidden-layer widths during a single training run by adding/removing units.  
- Theoretical result (Theorem 1): with fan-in or fan-out group-norm regularization (λ>0, 1≤p<∞) the objective attains a global minimum at finite dimensionality (prevents unbounded growth).  
- Practical recipe: CapNorm normalization + AdaRad/AdaRad-M optimizer to enable growth/shrinkage, with empirical studies on MNIST, rectangles, convex, and a large OpenML dataset.  
- Decision: Recommend accept (score 7).  
- Reason for accept 1: Novel, solid formulation with a meaningful theoretical guarantee addressing unbounded growth.  
- Reason for accept 2: Practical algorithm with promising results and potential compute/memory savings over black-box model selection.  
- Theory supporting point: Fan-in/fan-out penalties encourage group sparsity (like group lasso) so entire units can be pruned; the finite-minimizer theorem is central and nontrivial.  
- Method supporting point: AdaRad’s radial vs angular update decomposition thoughtfully handles normalization vs regularization tension; CapNorm avoids defeating the regularizer.  
- Empirics supporting point: Mixed results but some cases where nonparametric nets match or beat parametric baselines at smaller sizes, showing promise.  
- Request 1 (Theorem 1): Provide plain-English intuition, focus Lemmas 2–4 explanation, and discuss whether combining fan-in and fan-out penalties preserves the finite-minimizer guarantee.  
- Request 2 (zero units): Clarify definitions: whether zero fan-in or zero fan-out is a functionally removable unit and correct claims about how adding/removing "zero units" changes the regularizer.  
- Request 3 (why nonparametric can outperform parametric): Analyze hypotheses (optimization path, implicit regularization, CapNorm vs BN, step sizes, conditioning) and run controlled experiments training final architectures from scratch under the same settings.  
- Request 4 (Figure 2 clarity): Improve figure (standardize axes, add error bars/IQR, explicit comparisons) and add a table with median ± MAD over seeds.  
- Request 5 (hyperparameter λ): Quantify how λ controls final width (plots with variance bands), discuss estimating/adapting λ from data, and whether finite-minimizer holds under time-varying λ.  
- Request 6 (sensitivity and variability): Report seed-wise distributions of final widths, test error, structure variability, and overlap/correlation of early-added vs surviving units.  
- Request 7 (combined/alternative penalties): Explore Elastic Net–style mixtures across groups and whether finite-minimizer extends to mixed penalties; compare p=1 vs p=2 effects beyond sparsity/density.  
- Request 8 (experimental controls): Align normalizers across baselines, report peak vs final parameter counts, and wall-clock/epoch costs for AdaRad vs RMSprop.  
- Request 9 (minor clarifications): Elevate the self-similar nonlinearity/ReLU observation to main text, clarify notation, and give geometric intuition for AdaRad’s angular step.  
- Overall verdict: Timely, well-motivated paper with valuable theoretical guarantee and promising practical recipe; accept conditional on clarifying zero-unit/penalty issues, better intuition for Theorem 1, and stronger fixed-size comparison experiments.
