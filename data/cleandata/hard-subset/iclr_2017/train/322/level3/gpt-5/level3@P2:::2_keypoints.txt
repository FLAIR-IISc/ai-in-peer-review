- Tackles selecting neural network width within a single training run to avoid costly architecture search.  
- Proposes “nonparametric neural networks” that add units during training and prune with group Lp (fan-in/out) penalties.  
- Theorem 1: with fan-in or fan-out regularization (λ>0, 1≤p<∞) the training objective attains a global minimum at a finite network size.  
- Introduces CapNorm (capped batch norm) to preserve weight-shrinkage effects.  
- Introduces AdaRad optimizer that separates radial (length) and angular (direction) gradient components, normalizing only angular updates.  
- Empirically matches or outperforms parametric baselines on MNIST and rectangles-images, underperforms on convex, and scales to a large OpenML poker dataset.  
- Decision: Accept (rating 7).  
- Key reasons for accept: novel framework with clean theoretical guarantee and a practical training recipe that shows promising multi-dataset results.  
- Problem significance: single-run architecture selection addresses a central, costly challenge in model design.  
- Positioning: bridges pruning/group-sparsity, black-box model selection, and constructive growth methods.  
- Theory: Theorem 1 is strong and assumptions are reasonable; proposition on self-similar nonlinearities gives practical guidance.  
- Empirics: can outperform fixed-width training and sometimes uses fewer parameters; training dynamics analysis (unit survival) is informative.  
- Honest reporting: mixed results (e.g., convex dataset) are acknowledged and discussed.  
- Request: add plain-English intuition and a short schematic proof sketch for Theorem 1 to improve accessibility.  
- Request: clarify behavior of zero units vs regularizer (Ω) and provide a small table of fan-in vs fan-out cases to remove ambiguity.  
- Request: compare directly to fixed-size networks of the same final architecture including wall-clock, updates to reach validation error, and peak parameter count during training.  
- Request: improve Figure 2 labels, mark baselines/recipes, show median/variance across seeds, and add a table of final sizes, test-error medians, IQRs.  
- Request: explain why nonparametric can beat parametric at same final size and include an ablation that retrains discovered architectures from scratch.  
- Request: report sensitivity/robustness (dispersion in final widths, subspace overlap between runs, sensitivity to ν, αr/αφ, and λ).  
- Request: provide hyperparameter guidance or heuristics for λ (or an adaptive/annealing baseline) since λ governs final width.  
- Request: discuss fan-in vs fan-out vs combined penalties empirically and theoretically (does finiteness theorem extend to combined penalty?).  
- Request: ablation comparing CapNorm to BatchNorm variants (e.g., frozen γ/β or partial normalization).  
- Request: AdaRad ablations and comparisons to RMSprop/Adam with appropriate handling of regularizer; include AdaRad-M quantitative results.  
- Questions for authors: concise intuition for Theorem 1, clarification on zero-unit effects on Ω, predictiveness of λ for width, sensitivity to initialization, whether theorem holds for sum of fan-in and fan-out penalties, CapNorm unit-scaling statistics, end-to-end compute/memory comparisons, and extendability to conv layers and nonparametric depth.
