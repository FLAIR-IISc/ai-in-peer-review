- Proposes Normalized LSTM that preserves means and variances of hidden states and memory cells across time.  
- Method inspired by Normalization Propagation (Norm Prop) to avoid extra computation of BatchNorm and LayerNorm.  
- Authors derive gradients for Normalized LSTM and analyze its impact on gradient flow.  
- Introduces a weight-matrix initialization scheme for the proposed parametrization.  
- Empirical validation on character-level language modeling and image generative modeling tasks.  
- Decision: Accept — approach is well-motivated and yields significant computation-time improvements over existing normalization techniques.  
- Paper quality: well-structured, clear explanation of method and advantages.  
- Supporting evidence: experiments show effectiveness in both computation time and model performance.  
- Requested improvement: more discussion on implications for gradient flow and training dynamics.  
- Requested improvement: more experiments on sensitivity to hyperparameters (weight initialization, rescaling parameters).  
- Requested clarification: relationship between Normalized LSTM and other normalization methods (Weight Normalization, Layer Normalization).  
- Questions for authors: provide intuition for why normalization is preserved and its link to gradient-theoretic results.  
- Questions for authors: expected performance and limitations on harder tasks like speech recognition or machine translation.  
- Questions for authors: provide implementation details, specific hyperparameters, and computational resources used.
