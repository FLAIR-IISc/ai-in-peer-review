- Proposal: Introduces Normalized LSTM, a reparameterization that preserves hidden-state normalization through time, inspired by Normalization Propagation to reduce Batch Normalization cost.
- Theory: Derives gradients for Normalized LSTM and analyzes its effect on gradient propagation, plus proposes a weight-matrix initialization scheme.
- Empirical: Evaluated on character-level language modeling and image generative modeling, achieving comparable performance to SOTA with lower computational cost.
- Decision: Reviewer recommends acceptance—paper is well-motivated, clearly written, addresses vanishing/exploding gradients, and situates well in related work.
- Strengths: Clear method exposition, thorough gradient analysis, and demonstration of computational efficiency.
- Weaknesses / Requests: Needs more extensive experiments on larger tasks (e.g., machine translation, QA) and comparisons to Weight Normalization and Recurrent Batch Normalization for cost/performance.
- Additional investigation: Suggests studying the effect of not fixing variance estimates of cell/hidden states during training.
- Questions for authors: Clarify the weight initialization details and impact, strategies to avoid bad rescaling-parameter initialization, and why Normalized LSTM pairs well with recurrent dropout.
