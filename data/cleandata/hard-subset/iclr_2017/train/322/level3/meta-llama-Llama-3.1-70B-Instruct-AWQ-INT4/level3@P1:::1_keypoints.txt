- Proposes a nonparametric neural network that learns model size during training by randomly adding zero units and using sparse regularization to null out irrelevant weights.
- Approach resembles random search over discrete architecture space aided by sparse regularization to eliminate useless units.
- Well-motivated, clear approach tackling the important problem of automatic model size selection.
- Empirical results are competitive with parametric networks on several benchmarks (notably MNIST and rectangles-images).
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Concern about additional computational complexity introduced by the method; authors should quantify extra operations.
- Request for running-time experiments to demonstrate feasibility.
- Noted poor performance on the convex dataset—method yields very small networks; needs explanation.
- Suggest more experiments to demonstrate robustness to hyperparameter settings and initialization schemes.
- Question: Can authors explain the poor convex dataset performance and the emergence of very small networks?
- Question: How do authors plan to address the additional computational complexity?
- Question: Have authors considered applying the method to other architectures (CNNs, RNNs)?
