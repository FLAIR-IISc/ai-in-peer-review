- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that adaptively choose network size during a single training run.  
- Provides theoretical justification for the framework (Theorem 1) with proof in the appendix.  
- Proposes a novel optimizer, Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad), tailored for nonparametric networks.  
- Thorough literature review on model selection and neural network pruning.  
- Experimental results show nonparametric nets can achieve higher accuracy than parametric nets of the same size.  
- Results are especially promising on MNIST and rectangles images datasets.  
- Need more intuition and discussion about Theorem 1 and its implications beyond the appendix proof.  
- Request a direct comparison to fixed-size nets with the same final size to demonstrate performance vs. memory efficiency.  
- Figure 2 is unclear; authors should better explain results and why nonparametric nets sometimes outperform known-final-size parametric nets.  
- Provide more details on regularizer parameter lambda and whether it primarily determines the final network structure.  
- Report sensitivity to initialization: how different random starts affect final network structures.  
- Discuss combining fan-in and fan-out regularizers and whether the theoretical results still hold in that case.  
- Clarify the claim that adding zero units changes the regularizer value and provide evidence/explanation.  
- Define "zero units" clearly and specify what constitutes a removable unit in terms of fan-in and fan-out weights.  
- Describe the unit addition schedule and its effect on performance of nonparametric networks.  
- Overall assessment: well-written, clear approach, and a good fit for the conference.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (rating 7).
