- Proposes a novel framework ("nonparametric neural networks") that dynamically shrinks and expands a network during a single training run to select size automatically.
- Claims a simple, general method that can outperform parametric nets of the same size and match exhaustive random search results.
- Reviewer accepts the paper based on its novelty and potential impact.
- Provides theoretical proofs and experimental benchmarks supporting the framework’s soundness and performance.
- Requests more intuition and discussion around the theoretical result (Theorem 1) rather than just the formal proof.
- Suggests relating the theoretical result to other regularization methods (e.g., Lasso, Elastic Net).
- Asks for an experiment comparing the adaptive model to fixed-size nets with the same final size to demonstrate efficiency (performance vs memory).
- Notes Figure 2 is unclear/mixed and should be revised for clarity.
- Requests more explanation for why nonparametric nets sometimes outperform parametric ones even when final net size is known.
- Requests a plain-English summary of Theorem 1 and its key insight for broader accessibility.
- Raises specific questions: can lambda be predicted from data, does lambda primarily determine final structure, how sensitive is method to initialization and random starts?
- Asks whether fan-in and fan-out regularizers can be combined and whether theoretical results still hold, and how adding "zero units" affects the regularizer and how "zero units" are defined.
