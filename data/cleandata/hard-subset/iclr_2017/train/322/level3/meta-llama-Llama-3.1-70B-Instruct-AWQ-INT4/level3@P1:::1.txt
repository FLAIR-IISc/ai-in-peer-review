**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a nonparametric neural network framework that automatically learns the model size during training. The key idea is to randomly add zero units and apply a sparse regularizer to null out irrelevant weights. This approach resembles random search over discrete architecture space aided by sparse regularization to eliminate useless units. The authors tackle an important problem and present interesting empirical results.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

Based on the key points, I decide to **accept** the paper with the following key reasons:

1. The paper proposes a novel and interesting approach to automatically learning model size during training, which is an important problem in deep learning.
2. The empirical results show that the proposed method can achieve competitive performance with parametric networks on several benchmark datasets.

**Supporting Arguments for the Reasons**

The paper provides a clear and well-motivated approach to tackling the problem of model size selection. The use of sparse regularization to eliminate useless units is a clever idea that allows the network to adapt its size during training. The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, especially on the mnist and rectangles-images datasets.

However, I do have some concerns about the additional computational complexity introduced by the proposed method. The authors should quantify the extra operations required by their approach and provide running-time experiments to demonstrate its feasibility.

**Additional Feedback**

To further improve the paper, the authors could:

1. Provide more explanations for the poor performance on the convex dataset. Is there a specific reason why the method yields very small networks on this dataset?
2. Quantify the extra operations required by the proposed method and provide running-time experiments to demonstrate its feasibility.
