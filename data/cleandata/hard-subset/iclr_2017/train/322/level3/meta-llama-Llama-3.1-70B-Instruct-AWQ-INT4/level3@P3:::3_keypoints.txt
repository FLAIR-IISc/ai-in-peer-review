- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that adapt and choose network size during a single training run.
- Proposes dynamic addition/removal of units using group-sparsity regularization to control growth.
- Presents AdaRad, a new optimization algorithm designed for the nonparametric framework.
- Claims to address model selection without exhaustively training many networks or needing prior problem knowledge.
- Key contributions: the nonparametric framework, AdaRad, a theoretical finite-unit loss-minimization result, and experimental results.
- Writing is clear and experiments are analyzed thoroughly.
- Theoretical result does not guarantee avoidance of overfitting or underfitting.
- Initial experiments are limited and not comprehensive.
- Framework still requires hyperparameter tuning (e.g., add-frequency and decay rates) that influence final unit count.
- No comparisons to pruning or compression baselines, leaving competitiveness unclear.
- Scalability concerns: single hyperparameters control both model size and training time, potentially slowing large-network training.
- Overall decision: Reject.
- Primary reasons for rejection: lack of comprehensive comparisons to state-of-the-art size-reduction methods and remaining need for hyperparameter tuning.
- Supporting positives: clear problem framing and a novel AdaRad algorithm; experimental results are encouraging but insufficient.
- Recommended improvements: compare to pruning/compression baselines, reduce hyperparameter tuning (e.g., Bayesian optimization), and run larger-scale/diverse experiments.
- Questions for authors: how will hyperparameter tuning be addressed; what is AdaRad’s computational cost vs other optimizers; how to extend the framework to architectures like CNNs?
