- Proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that dynamically add/remove units during a single training run.  
- Uses a group-sparsity regularizer to control network growth.  
- Provides a theoretical result showing a finite-unit network can minimize the loss.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on several benchmark datasets.  
- Paper is clearly written, conceptually elegant, and well-motivated for lifelong learning.  
- Results are limited and not comprehensive; larger-scale tests are needed for practicality and scalability.  
- No comparisons to pruning or compression baselines, so competitiveness versus existing methods is unclear.  
- Method does not eliminate hyperparameter tuning; add-frequency and decay rates still control final unit count and are hard to tune.  
- Single hyperparameter controls both model size and training time, potentially causing slow training and scalability problems.  
- Potential to yield smaller networks that reduce test-time runtime, power, and memory.  
- Recommendation to accept but require more comprehensive experiments and exploration of multiple hyperparameters.  
- Suggests providing computational cost comparisons, testing alternative regularizers (e.g., l1 or l2,1), and analyzing add-frequency/decay effects.  
- Asks authors to address scalability for large networks, detail hyperparameter tuning procedures, and extend to architectures like CNNs.
