- Introduces a nonparametric neural network framework that adapts and chooses network size during a single training run.  
- Novel approach dynamically adds and removes units using a sparse regularizer to eliminate irrelevant weights.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees proving the soundness of the framework.  
- Presents promising empirical results on several benchmark datasets demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Decision: accept, motivated by novelty, potential impact, and theoretical soundness.  
- Concern: additional computational complexity introduced by the method needs detailed analysis and comparison to parametric networks.  
- Suggestion: include running-time experiments to assess practicality.  
- Issue: method underperforms on a convex dataset by producing very small networks; needs clearer explanation.  
- Questions for authors: provide detailed computational complexity analysis and comparison, plans to address running-time complexity, and a detailed explanation of the convex-dataset results.
