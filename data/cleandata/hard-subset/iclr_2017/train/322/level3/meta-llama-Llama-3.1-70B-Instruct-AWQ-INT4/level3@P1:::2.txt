**Summary of the paper's contributions**

This paper introduces a novel framework called nonparametric neural networks (NNNs) that allows for the automatic adaptation and selection of the size of a neural network during a single training run. The authors prove the theoretical soundness of the framework, which guarantees that the error optimum uses a finite number of parameters, preventing the network from growing indefinitely. They also present experimental results showing that NNNs can achieve competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods while requiring fewer parameters.
