This paper proposes a nonparametric neural network that automatically learns its model size during training. The key idea is to randomly add zero units and apply a sparse regularizer to null out irrelevant weights, which resembles random search over discrete architecture space aided by sparse regularization to eliminate useless units. The approach tackles an important problem and presents interesting empirical results.

However, I have some concerns regarding the additional computational complexity introduced by decomposing fan-in weights into parallel/orthogonal components and converting to radial-angular coordinates. The authors should quantify the extra operations versus parametric networks and provide running-time experiments to demonstrate the efficiency of their approach.

Additionally, I found it surprising that on the convex dataset, the method yields very small networks and underperforms parametric networks. The authors should provide an explanation for this phenomenon and discuss potential limitations of their approach.

Overall, I think the paper has potential, but it requires further clarification and improvement to address the concerns mentioned above.

**Decision:** Reject with resubmission

**Reasons:**

1. Additional computational complexity: The authors should provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their approach and compare it to parametric networks.
2. Underperformance on convex dataset: The authors should provide an explanation for the underperformance of their method on the convex dataset and discuss potential limitations of their approach.

**Supporting arguments:**

* The paper proposes a novel approach to automatically learning model size during training, which is an important problem in deep learning.
* The empirical results presented in the paper are interesting and demonstrate the potential of the approach.
* However, the additional computational complexity introduced by the approach is a concern, and the authors should provide a detailed analysis to alleviate this concern.
* The underperformance on the convex dataset is surprising, and the authors should provide an explanation for this phenomenon to improve the paper.

**Questions for the authors:**

* Can you provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of your approach and compare it to parametric networks?
* Can you explain the underperformance of your method on the convex dataset and discuss potential limitations of your approach?
* How do you plan to address the additional computational complexity introduced by your approach in future work?
