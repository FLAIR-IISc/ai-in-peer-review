**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

The paper introduces a novel framework for automatically adapting and choosing the size of a neural network during a single training run, called nonparametric neural networks. The authors prove the theoretical soundness of this framework and demonstrate its effectiveness in various experiments. The key contributions of the paper are:

1. A nonparametric neural network framework that allows the network to grow or shrink dynamically during training.
2. A novel optimization algorithm, called Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad), that is specifically designed for training nonparametric neural networks.
3. Experimental results showing that nonparametric neural networks can achieve competitive performance with traditional parametric networks, while requiring fewer parameters.
