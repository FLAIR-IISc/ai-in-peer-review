- Method: dynamically add/remove units (by zeroing input/output weights) with group-sparsity regularization; theoretical result shows a finite-unit network can minimize the loss.  
- Theory does not guarantee avoidance of over- or under-fitting; initial experiments suggest it may be okay but are limited.  
- Potential advantage: can produce smaller networks that reduce test-time runtime, power, and memory.  
- Does not remove hyperparameter tuning: hyperparameters (e.g., add-frequency, decay rates) still implicitly control final unit count and may be hard to tune.  
- Authors do not compare against pruning/compression baselines, so competitiveness with best size-reduction methods is unclear.  
- Single hyperparameters control both model size and training time, risking much slower training and scalability problems for large networks.  
- Experiments are helpful and encouraging but not comprehensive; larger-scale tests are needed to show practicality.  
- Paper is clearly written and conceptually elegant, fitting lifelong-learning goals, but results remain speculative.
