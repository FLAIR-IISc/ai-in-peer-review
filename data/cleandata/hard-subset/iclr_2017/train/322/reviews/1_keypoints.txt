- Proposes a nonparametric neural network that automatically learns model size during training.  
- Key idea: randomly add zero units and apply a sparse regularizer to null out irrelevant weights.  
- Approach resembles random search over discrete architecture space aided by sparse regularization to eliminate useless units.  
- Tackles an important problem and presents interesting empirical results.  
- Major concern: additional computational complexity from decomposing fan-in weights into parallel/orthogonal components and converting to radial–angular coordinates.  
- Authors should quantify extra operations versus parametric networks and provide running-time experiments.  
- Empirical note: on the convex dataset the method yields very small networks and underperforms parametric networks—needs explanation.
