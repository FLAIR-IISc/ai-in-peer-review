- Proposes nonparametric neural networks that grow/prune during a single training cycle with a theoretical guarantee (Theorem 1) and introduces AdaRad optimizer and CapNorm layer.  
- Decision: Reject — solid theory but insufficient empirical evidence and unclear generality.  
- Theoretical contribution is strong; proof of Theorem 1 is well-structured and establishes soundness under regularization.  
- AdaRad and CapNorm are novel and well-motivated for enabling continuous network growth/pruning.  
- Empirical evaluation is weak: lacks statistical significance tests and shows inconsistent performance across datasets and seeds.  
- Major unexplained failure on the convex dataset undermines the claim of broad applicability.  
- Comparisons are limited to parametric networks of the same size, not to recent automatic sizing or neural architecture search (NAS) methods.  
- No computational efficiency metrics (training time, memory, resource usage) are provided despite claims about cost scaling.  
- Paper should clarify differences from prior pruning work (e.g., Alvarez & Salzmann 2016; Molchanov et al. 2017) and why a high-performing starting point isn't needed.  
- Recommended additional experiments: analyze/justify convex dataset failure, compare against NAS and modern auto-sizing methods, provide quantitative accuracy comparisons to scratch-trained networks, and report computational costs.  
- Questions for authors: why the convex dataset fails, how the method compares to NAS in performance and efficiency, quantitative evidence for higher accuracy vs same-size scratch networks, and feasibility/challenges of extending to automatically determine depth.
