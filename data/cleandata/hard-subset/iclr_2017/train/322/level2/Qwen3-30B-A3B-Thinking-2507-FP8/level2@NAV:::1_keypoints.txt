- Proposes nonparametric neural networks that dynamically grow and shrink units during training to determine optimal size automatically.  
- Proves the framework achieves a global minimum under L2 regularization (Theorem 1).  
- Introduces AdaRad, a novel optimization algorithm for training nonparametric networks.  
- Shows on MNIST and rectangles that nonparametric networks can match or exceed parametric networks while using far fewer parameters.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with a comprehensive proof in Appendix 7.1 addressing degeneration concerns.  
- MNIST experiments report comparable accuracy with approximately 70% fewer parameters.  
- Scalability experiments on the poker dataset demonstrate applicability to larger problems.  
- Focuses on L2 (p=2) regularization, justified for compatibility with modern hardware.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reasons: addresses a meaningful architecture-selection problem with theoretical grounding and practical experimental validation.  
- Contribution: finds competitive network sizes without exhaustive search, representing a significant practical advance.  
- Requested clarification: explain poor performance on the convex dataset and whether it stems from architecture or data characteristics.  
- Requested additional analysis: provide detailed computational cost comparisons with standard approaches to quantify efficiency gains.  
- Requested discussion: more explicit trade-offs between network size, training time, and final performance.  
- Questions for authors: how does the framework perform on more complex architectures (e.g., CNNs) or harder data distributions?  
- Questions for authors: can theoretical guarantees be extended to regularizers beyond L2?
