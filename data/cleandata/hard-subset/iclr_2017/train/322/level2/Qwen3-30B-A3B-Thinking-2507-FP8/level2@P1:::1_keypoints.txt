- Introduces "nonparametric neural networks" that grow network size automatically during a single training run.  
- Proves (Theorem 1) that with a p=2 fan-in/fan-out regularizer the optimization attains a global minimum at finite network size.  
- Proposes a new training algorithm (AdaRad) and a normalization layer (CapNorm) to implement the framework.  
- Empirical results show promising performance across datasets, notably MNIST and rectangles images.  
- Decision: Accept — significant, theoretically sound contribution addressing practical network-size selection.  
- Effectively addresses costly hyperparameter search by finding network size without multiple training runs.  
- Experimental comparisons indicate nonparametric networks can match or outperform parametric nets and exhaustive search in some cases.  
- Provides detailed analysis of training dynamics (Section 4.2) that offers insight into how networks grow and converge.  
- Requests more practical guidance on choosing initial network size, unit addition rate, and regularization parameter.  
- Notes underperformance on the convex dataset and asks for deeper analysis and potential remedies.  
- Observes AdaRad-M (momentum variant) offers little benefit and requests a more detailed comparison/explanation.  
- Asks for clarification on convergence time trade-offs (unit addition cost vs final size/accuracy) and on training vs validation/test error implications (possible overfitting).
