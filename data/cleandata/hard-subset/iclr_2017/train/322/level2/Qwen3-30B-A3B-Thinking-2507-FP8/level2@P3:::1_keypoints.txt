- Introduces nonparametric neural networks that automatically determine optimal network size during a single training cycle by dynamically adding/removing units via L2 regularization.  
- Proposes AdaRad, a novel training algorithm that normalizes gradients at the fan-in level to address nonparametric training challenges.  
- Provides a theoretical soundness result (Theorem 1) under specific conditions, giving a formal foundation for the approach.  
- Demonstrates promising empirical results on MNIST and rectangles datasets, showing better accuracy than parametric networks of the same size.  
- Performs poorly on the convex dataset, significantly underperforming parametric networks of the same size—this is a major limitation.  
- Recommends acceptance, arguing the problem is important and the paper makes a meaningful contribution despite limitations.  
- The theoretical proof is thorough but complex; the required conditions (e.g., self-similar nonlinearities, regularization settings) should be more clearly stated in the main text.  
- Requests clearer analysis and explanation of the convex dataset failure mode and whether the framework can be adapted to handle such cases.  
- Computational cost analysis vs RMSprop is helpful, but a more comprehensive comparison with recent adaptive optimizers is needed.  
- Asks for clearer comparison to recent neural architecture search methods (e.g., Zoph & Le 2017) to position the work relative to popular automatic design approaches.  
- Suggests more comprehensive comparison to dynamic pruning and size-adjustment methods (e.g., Wei et al. 2016, Chen et al. 2016) and post-training pruning (Molchanov et al. 2017).  
- Poses key questions: explain convex dataset failure and possible fixes; expected scalability to deeper networks and larger datasets; and comparison to post-training pruning in efficiency and final model size.
