- Introduces Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNNs) that dynamically adapt network size during a single training cycle.  
- Proposes fan-in/fan-out regularization with theoretical proof of convergence to finite dimensionality.  
- Presents Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad) optimizer tailored for nonparametric networks.  
- Experiments show NNNs often outperform parametric networks of similar size on benchmarks.  
- Demonstrates scalability by applying the framework to a large dataset with competitive results.  
- Eliminates costly hyperparameter searches over network size, increasing practical utility.  
- Dynamic unit addition/removal often yields smaller, high-performing networks that adapt to task complexity.  
- Analysis includes network growth and unit survival, providing insights into training behavior.  
- Proofs in the appendix are dense and need clearer explanations or visual aids for accessibility.  
- Evaluation is limited to relatively simple datasets; testing on more complex tasks (e.g., ImageNet, large-scale NLP) is needed.  
- Lacks direct empirical comparisons to recent neural architecture search and pruning methods.  
- Unit addition schedule is fixed and likely suboptimal; adaptive or learned schedules should be explored.  
- Question: How does AdaRad compare to Adam/RMSprop on parametric networks and can it generalize?  
- Question: Why does the framework struggle on the convex dataset and how can it be improved?  
- Question: Can the framework be extended to convolutional or transformer architectures, and what challenges are expected?
