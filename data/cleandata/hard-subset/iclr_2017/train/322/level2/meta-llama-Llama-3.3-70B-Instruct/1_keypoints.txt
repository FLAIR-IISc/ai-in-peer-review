- Proposes a framework for automatically determining optimal neural network size without prior information via nonparametric neural networks and an ℓp penalty.  
- Introduces a non-probabilistic optimization over all network sizes and proves soundness when growth is limited by an ℓp penalty.  
- Proposes a novel optimizer, Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad).  
- Claims nonparametric networks can adapt network size during a single training run.  
- Claims the framework and AdaRad can outperform parametric networks of the same size.  
- Claims the approach is scalable to large datasets.  
- Supports claims with theoretical analysis plus experiments on MNIST, rectangles images, convex, and a large poker dataset.  
- Demonstrates usefulness by addressing the need for prior network-size selection in deep learning.  
- Shows strong field knowledge and clear explanation of related work, optimization, and regularization.  
- Presents significant novelty in both the nonparametric framework and the AdaRad algorithm.  
- Paper is largely complete but lacks full implementation details for AdaRad, hindering reproducibility.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations: dataset dependence and sensitivity to hyperparameters (e.g., regularization λ).  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Reasons for accept: novel framework/optimizer, empirical effectiveness, and strong domain expertise.  
- Additional feedback: provide AdaRad implementation details, evaluate more datasets/tasks, and analyze hyperparameter effects.  
- Questions for authors: provide AdaRad implementation details, explain hyperparameter selection and effects, and report results on other datasets/tasks.
