This paper introduces nonparametric neural networks, a framework for automatically adapting and choosing the size of a neural network during a single training run. The authors present a novel optimization algorithm, Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad), and demonstrate its effectiveness in achieving promising results.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the nonparametric neural network framework and its theoretical soundness. The introduction of AdaRad is well-motivated, and the algorithm is thoroughly explained. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering three standard benchmark datasets and comparing the performance of nonparametric networks with parametric networks.

The authors provide a detailed analysis of the nonparametric training process, which helps to understand the behavior of the algorithm. The scalability of nonparametric networks is also demonstrated by applying them to a large dataset.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough discussion of related work. The references are comprehensive, covering various aspects of neural networks, optimization algorithms, and related techniques.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a simple and general framework for automatically adapting and choosing the size of a neural network. The authors demonstrate that nonparametric networks can achieve competitive results with parametric networks, even when the size of the network is not known in advance.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. The authors rely heavily on the AdaRad algorithm, which may not be the optimal choice for all problems. The experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive, covering a wider range of datasets and tasks. Additionally, the authors could provide more insights into the behavior of nonparametric networks and how they compare to parametric networks in different scenarios.

In terms of the three key questions, the paper provides a clear answer to the first question: the specific question/problem tackled by the paper is the automatic adaptation and choice of neural network size during a single training run. The approach is well-motivated, and the authors provide a thorough explanation of the nonparametric neural network framework and its theoretical soundness. The paper supports the claims made by the authors, and the results are scientifically rigorous.

Overall, I would recommend accepting this paper. The authors provide a novel and well-motivated framework for automatically adapting and choosing the size of a neural network, and the experimental evaluation is comprehensive and well-executed.
