**Summary of the Paper's Claims**

The paper introduces a new framework called Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNNs) that can automatically adapt and choose the size of a neural network during a single training run. The authors claim that NNNs can achieve a global training error minimum at some finite dimensionality when using a fan-in or fan-out regularizer with a certain condition. They also propose a novel optimization algorithm called Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad) that can efficiently train NNNs. The authors evaluate their framework using three standard benchmark datasets and show that NNNs can outperform parametric networks of the same size and achieve results competitive with those of an exhaustive random search.

Based on the evaluation of the paper's claims and the provided results, I decide to **Accept** the paper.
