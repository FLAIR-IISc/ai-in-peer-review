- Introduces nonparametric neural networks for automatically adapting network size during a single training run.
- Proposes a novel optimizer, Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad), and explains it thoroughly.
- Paper is well-organized with clear explanation and theoretical justification of the framework.
- Experimental evaluation covers three standard benchmarks and compares nonparametric to parametric networks.
- Provides detailed analysis of the nonparametric training process to elucidate algorithm behavior.
- Demonstrates scalability by applying nonparametric networks to a large dataset.
- Well-written manuscript with comprehensive related work and references.
- Strength: offers a simple, general framework that achieves competitive results without prior size knowledge.
- Limitation: heavy reliance on AdaRad, which may not be optimal for all problems.
- Limitation: experimental scope could be broader across more datasets and tasks.
- Limitation: lacks deeper insights into when nonparametric networks outperform or differ from parametric ones.
- Three key questions addressed: problem statement (automatic size adaptation) is clear, approach is well-motivated, and claims are supported.
