- Introduces Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNNs) that automatically adapt and choose network size during a single training run.
- Claims NNNs achieve a global training error minimum at some finite dimensionality with a fan-in or fan-out regularizer under a certain condition.
- Proposes a novel optimization algorithm, Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad), to efficiently train NNNs.
- Evaluates on three standard benchmarks, reporting NNNs outperform same-size parametric networks and rival exhaustive random search.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Reason: Novel and well-motivated framework for automatic network sizing.
- Reason: Provides rigorous theoretical analysis, including a proof of finite-dimensional global training error minimum.
- Reason: AdaRad is described as efficient and effective for training NNNs.
- Reason: Experimental results are comprehensive and demonstrate the framework’s effectiveness.
- Supporting: Framework addresses the practical need to adapt network size within a single run.
- Supporting: Theoretical results give a solid foundation for the approach.
- Supporting: Experiments and implementation details of AdaRad are thorough.
- Feedback: Paper should discuss limitations and potential future work in more detail.
- Feedback: Need clearer insights into hyperparameter choices and their performance impact.
- Feedback: Should include more comprehensive comparisons to state-of-the-art pruning and model-selection methods.
- Question: What is the computational complexity of AdaRad and how does it scale to larger networks?
- Question: How will the framework be extended to convolutional layers and to automatically choose network depth?
- Question: Can the authors provide more insight into hyperparameter selection and its effect on NNN performance?
