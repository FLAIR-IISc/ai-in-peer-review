- Summary: Proposes nonparametric neural networks that adapt network size during a single training run.
- Framework: Non-probabilistic optimization over all network sizes with soundness proof when growth is limited by an ℓ_p penalty.
- Algorithm: Introduces Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad).
- Empirical: Shows promising results on several benchmark datasets.
- Decision: Reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Originality: Novel framework and significant contribution to deep learning.
- Theoretical soundness: Thorough analysis with formal proof under ℓ_p penalty constraint.
- Empirical results: Effective compared to traditional parametric networks.
- Motivation: Clear rationale addressing limits of black-box model selection.
- Technical contributions: AdaRad plus extensive theoretical development.
- Experimental evaluation: Comprehensive experiments and analysis of the nonparametric training process.
- Clarity: Paper needs clearer presentation in parts of the theoretical analysis.
- Comparison gap: Lacks comparison to other nonparametric methods (e.g., Bayesian neural networks).
- Future work: Suggests applying framework to CNNs and developing more efficient optimizers.
- Question: How will overfitting be addressed when the learned network becomes large?
- Question: More insight requested on the choice and impact of the ℓ_p penalty.
- Question: How to extend the framework to more complex architectures like convolutional networks?
