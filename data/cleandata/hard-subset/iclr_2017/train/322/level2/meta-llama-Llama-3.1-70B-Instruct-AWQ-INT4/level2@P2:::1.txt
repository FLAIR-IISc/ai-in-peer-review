**Summary**

This paper introduces nonparametric neural networks, a framework for automatically adapting and choosing the size of a neural network during a single training run. The authors propose a non-probabilistic framework for conducting optimization over all possible network sizes and prove its soundness when network growth is limited via an `p penalty. They also develop a novel optimization algorithm, Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad), and demonstrate promising results on several benchmark datasets.

**Decision**

I recommend accepting this paper.

**Reasons for the decision**

1. **Originality**: The paper introduces a novel framework for nonparametric neural networks, which is a significant contribution to the field of deep learning.
2. **Theoretical soundness**: The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of their framework, including a proof of its soundness when network growth is limited via an `p penalty.
3. **Empirical results**: The paper presents promising empirical results on several benchmark datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed framework.

**Supporting arguments**

1. **Motivation**: The authors provide a clear motivation for their work, highlighting the limitations of traditional black-box model selection methods and the need for a more efficient and effective approach.
2. **Technical contributions**: The paper makes significant technical contributions, including the development of a novel optimization algorithm (AdaRad) and a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed framework.
3. **Experimental evaluation**: The authors conduct a thorough experimental evaluation of their framework, including a comparison with traditional parametric neural networks and an analysis of the nonparametric training process.

**Additional feedback**

1. **Clarity**: The paper could benefit from improved clarity in some sections, particularly in the presentation of the theoretical analysis.
2. **Comparison to other methods**: While the authors provide a comparison with traditional parametric neural networks, it would be beneficial to include a comparison with other nonparametric neural network methods, such as Bayesian neural networks.
3. **Future work**: The authors mention several potential avenues for future work, including the application of their framework to convolutional neural networks and the development of more efficient optimization algorithms.

**Questions for the authors**

1. How do the authors plan to address the issue of overfitting in their framework, particularly in cases where the network size is large?
