**Summary of the Paper**

The paper introduces a novel framework called Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNNs) that can automatically determine the optimal size of a neural network for a given task without prior information. The framework is based on a non-probabilistic approach that optimizes over all possible network sizes during a single training cycle. The authors propose a new optimization algorithm called Adaptive Radial-Angular Gradient Descent (AdaRad) and a novel regularization technique that penalizes the incoming weights of each unit. The experimental results show that NNNs can achieve competitive performance with traditional parametric neural networks, while requiring significantly fewer parameters.
