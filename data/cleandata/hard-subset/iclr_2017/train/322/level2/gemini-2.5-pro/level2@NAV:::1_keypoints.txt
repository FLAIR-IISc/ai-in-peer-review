- Introduces "nonparametric neural networks" to automatically determine network size in a single training run.
- Core contributions: (1) formal framework for optimizing over varying network sizes.
- Core contributions: (2) Theorem 1 proving a global minimum exists at finite network size with fan-in/fan-out regularization.
- Core contributions: (3) Practical method combining unit addition and l2-based pruning, plus AdaRad optimizer and CapNorm batch normalization.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Strength: Principled, well-motivated approach grounded in optimization and rigorous theory rather than heuristics.
- Strength: AdaRad addresses optimization of newly added units; experiments show compact, competitive networks on 2/3 benchmarks and scalability to Poker dataset.
- Strength: Inclusion of a negative result on the 'convex' dataset increases credibility.
- Suggested improvements: Analyze sensitivity to the unit addition schedule and include an ablation study on this hyperparameter.
- Suggested improvements: Briefly discuss how an l1 penalty might change resulting architectures and investigate why the method underperformed on the 'convex' dataset.
- Questions for authors: How does a parametrically trained network (initialized from scratch with the final architecture) compare to the nonparametric model?
- Questions for authors: Have you tried decoupling the radial step size αr from the regularization λ, or is their coupling essential?
