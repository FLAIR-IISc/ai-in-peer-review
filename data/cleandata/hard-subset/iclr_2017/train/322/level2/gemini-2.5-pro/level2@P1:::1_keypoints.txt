- Proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that adapt hidden-layer widths during a single training run to avoid costly architecture searches.  
- Theorem 1: proves a global minimum at a finite network size using fan-in/fan-out p-norm regularizers.  
- Training scheme combines periodic addition of random units with an L2 fan-in regularizer that induces group sparsity to prune redundant units.  
- Introduces AdaRad optimizer that splits gradients into radial (magnitude) and angular (direction) components, normalizing only the angular part.  
- Introduces CapNorm (capped batch normalization) to prevent batch norm from negating the fan-in regularizer.  
- Empirical evaluation on multiple datasets shows the method can find well-performing networks and sometimes outperform parametric baselines and random search.  
- Decision: Accept — considered a strong, novel, and well-motivated contribution.  
- Novelty/significance: frames architecture search as continuous optimization during training, potentially saving compute over black-box searches.  
- Technical soundness: solid theoretical guarantee and well-justified algorithmic components (AdaRad, CapNorm).  
- Strength: principled group-sparsity approach with coherent algorithmic design and clear narrative from formulation to implementation.  
- Strength: strong experimental protocol, including fair parametric baselines and comparisons to random architecture search.  
- Strength: insightful analysis of training dynamics (layer-size evolution, unit lifespans, fan-in/out interplay).  
- Strength: demonstrated practicality/scalability (large 'poker' dataset) and computational efficiency of AdaRad.  
- Weakness: poor performance on the 'convex' dataset with no convincing explanation, raising concerns about generality.  
- Weakness: introduces new hyperparameters (radial/angular step sizes, unit addition rate) without sensitivity analysis; coupling of αr to λ is particularly concerning.  
- Additional feedback: clarify the nonstandard use of "nonparametric" or relate it to standard definition.  
- Additional feedback: provide a high-level proof sketch of Theorem 1 in the main paper for accessibility.  
- Additional feedback: analyze or at least speculate on reasons for failure on 'convex' (e.g., required capacity or optimization issues).  
- Additional feedback: consider exploring more adaptive unit-addition schedules rather than the heuristic one-unit-per-layer-per-epoch.  
- Questions for authors: explain the poor 'convex' performance and dataset characteristics that caused it.  
- Questions for authors: how sensitive are performance and final size to the αr = 150λ choice; is this a hard meta-hyperparameter?  
- Questions for authors: compare total wall-clock time of one nonparametric run vs. total time of the random-search protocol to reach comparable accuracy.  
- Questions for authors: why was the radial component left unnormalized in AdaRad — empirical choice or theoretical reasoning; were alternatives tested?
