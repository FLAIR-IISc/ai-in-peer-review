- Proposes "nonparametric neural networks" that dynamically adjust network width during a single training run to avoid expensive architecture search.  
- Frames model selection as joint optimization over weights and hidden-unit counts, with Theorem 1 proving a finite global minimum under l_p fan-in/fan-out regularization.  
- Training method: periodically add units and use group l2 fan-in penalty to encourage sparsity and remove redundant units.  
- Introduces Capped Batch Normalization (CapNorm) to make BN compatible with shrinkage-inducing regularization.  
- Introduces AdaRad optimizer that decomposes gradients into radial and angular parts, normalizing only the angular component to let new units learn before being shrunk.  
- Empirical results: discovers well-performing networks, often outperforming fixed-size models and competitive with random search while using fewer parameters.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key strength 1: Principled, novel formulation with strong theoretical justification and well-motivated technical components (AdaRad, CapNorm).  
- Key strength 2: Strong empirical support across multiple datasets, detailed training-dynamics analysis, scalability test, and honest reporting of a failure case.  
- Supporting argument: Clear motivation to replace inefficient black-box hyperparameter search with a continuous growth+pruning mechanism.  
- Supporting argument: Theorem 1 gives confidence the objective won’t push network size to infinity; proof appears rigorous.  
- Supporting argument: AdaRad is a non-trivial, problem-motivated optimizer design addressing the tension between learning and regularization.  
- Supporting argument: CapNorm is a simple but crucial adaptation enabling the regularizer to work with batch normalization.  
- Experimental critique: Thorough comparisons to fixed-size and random-search baselines, with insightful analyses like unit lifecycles and network evolution.  
- Suggestion: Analyze the 'convex' failure case to hypothesize why the method underperformed and clarify limitations.  
- Suggestion: Clarify AdaRad’s practical implementation cost and observed wall-clock overhead versus standard optimizers like Adam.  
- Suggestion: Discuss alternative or adaptive unit-addition strategies (e.g., increase addition when learning stagnates).  
- Suggestion: Report sensitivity of radial step size to regularization strength and robustness to the αr = 150λ coupling.  
- Question: Would re-training the final discovered architecture from scratch as a parametric network improve performance on the 'convex' dataset?  
- Question: Were alternative initialization strategies for new units (e.g., gradient-informed placements) explored beyond random fan-in and zero fan-out?
