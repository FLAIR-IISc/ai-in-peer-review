- Proposes nonparametric neural networks that dynamically add zero units and prune via group L2, with a theorem that ℓp fan regularization attains a global minimum at finite dimensionality.  
- Introduces CapNorm (capped batch-normalization compatible with shrinkage) and AdaRad (optimizer decomposing gradients into radial and angular parts to protect new units).  
- Experiments on MNIST, rectangles-images, convex, and Poker show the method can find compact models and sometimes match/exceed parametric baselines and random search at lower parameter counts.  
- Decision: Reject due to insufficient empirical support and analysis.  
- Core critique: Empirical evaluation and baselines do not convincingly isolate or support the central claims.  
- Core critique: Limited ablations and potential fairness issues prevent attributing gains to nonparametric growth, CapNorm, or AdaRad.  
- Missing comparisons to strong modern HPO methods (e.g., early-stopping BO, Hyperband/ASHA) and structured sparsity approaches.  
- Results are inconsistent across datasets (notably poor performance on the convex task), weakening generality claims.  
- Experimental protocol differences (CapNorm vs BN; λ ≠ 0 vs λ = 0) and manual selection of “representative” runs introduce bias.  
- No wall-clock or compute-cost comparisons given despite claims of reduced search cost.  
- Important ablations are missing: NP with standard optimizer; AdaRad on parametric nets; CapNorm vs BN/LayerNorm/GroupNorm; growth vs pruning vs combined; p=1 vs p=2; fan-in vs fan-out.  
- Reproducibility hampered by heuristic schedules and manual choices.  
- Recommendation: Provide rigorous apples-to-apples baselines with identical normalization, regularization, early-stopping, and compute budgets, plus time-to-accuracy and FLOPs.  
- Recommendation: Add the listed ablations to isolate contributions of NP, CapNorm, and AdaRad.  
- Recommendation: Evaluate on modern architectures (e.g., CNNs), include depth selection, and compare to Hyperband/ASHA/BO.  
- Theory feedback: Clarify the scope and assumptions, list which activations/losses satisfy them, and discuss practical behavior for p=1.  
- Ask authors to report sensitivity sweeps for λ, unit-addition rate, and αr/αφ to assess robustness.  
- Ask for wall-clock/compute overhead measurements of AdaRad and dynamic sizing versus parametric baselines with similar accuracy.  
- Ask whether gains persist under identical normalization/regularization across NP and parametric models.  
- Ask for ablations isolating CapNorm and AdaRad contributions separately.  
- Ask for practical/adaptive guidelines for λ and addition/pruning schedules and for empirical results on convolutional layers or depth selection to support generality.
