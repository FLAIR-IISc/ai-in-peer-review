## Review of "NONPARAMETRIC NEURAL NETWORKS"

This paper introduces a novel framework, Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNNs), for automatically determining network size during a single training cycle. The core idea – dynamically growing and shrinking networks guided by an L2 penalty – is intriguing and addresses a significant challenge in deep learning: efficient hyperparameter optimization, specifically network architecture search. The authors provide theoretical justification for their approach, demonstrating a global minimum training error under certain conditions, and propose a new optimization algorithm, AdaRad, to facilitate this dynamic training process.

I recommend **rejecting** this paper in its current form, despite the promising concept. While the authors demonstrate theoretical soundness and introduce a new algorithm, the experimental results are not compelling enough to warrant acceptance. The performance gains on MNIST and rectangles images are nuanced and, critically, the approach *underperforms* significantly on the convex dataset. This suggests a lack of robustness or a sensitivity to dataset characteristics that isn’t adequately explained. 

The paper suffers from a few key weaknesses. Firstly, the experimental section feels somewhat limited. The hyperparameter search, while described, lacks detail regarding the specific search spaces and justifications for choices. The comparison to existing methods, while present, isn't exhaustive enough to definitively claim superiority. More rigorous benchmarking against state-of-the-art neural architecture search (NAS) methods is needed. Secondly, the ablation study is minimal. It's unclear what contribution AdaRad makes relative to simply applying a standard optimizer to a network undergoing dynamic size changes. Finally, the presentation could be improved. The paper is dense and lacks intuitive explanations for some of the more complex concepts, hindering comprehension.

However, the idea behind NNNs is genuinely innovative, and the theoretical framework is a strong foundation. I encourage the authors to significantly expand the experimental evaluation – including more datasets, comparison with stronger baselines, and a detailed ablation study – before resubmission. Investigating the sensitivity of the approach to different datasets and exploring methods to address the poor performance on the convex dataset are crucial. Clarifying the benefits of AdaRad and providing more intuitive explanations of the core concepts would also significantly strengthen the paper. It’s a solid starting point with the potential for valuable contributions to the field, but requires substantial further development.



**Specific questions for the authors:**

*   Could you provide more details regarding the hyperparameter search space used for both the NNN and parametric network experiments?
*   Why does the NNN approach perform so poorly on the convex dataset? What characteristics of this dataset might explain this behavior?
*   What is the computational overhead of AdaRad compared to standard optimization algorithms (e.g., Adam, SGD)?
*   Have you considered exploring alternative regularization techniques beyond the L2 penalty? Could other penalties influence the stability and performance of the network growth process?
*   How sensitive is the performance to the choice of initial network size and the addition rate (ν)?
