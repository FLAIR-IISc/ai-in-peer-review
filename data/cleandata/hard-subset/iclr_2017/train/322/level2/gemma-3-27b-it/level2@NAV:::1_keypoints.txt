- Introduces Nonparametric Neural Networks (NNNs) to automatically grow/shrink network size during a single training cycle using an L2 penalty.  
- Provides theoretical justification showing a global minimum training error under certain conditions.  
- Proposes a new optimizer, AdaRad, to enable dynamic network training.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection in current form despite a promising concept.  
- Experimental results are unconvincing: marginal gains on MNIST/rectangles and significant underperformance on the convex dataset.  
- Experiments lack detail: hyperparameter search spaces and justifications are insufficiently described.  
- Comparisons to existing methods are inadequate; needs stronger benchmarking against state-of-the-art NAS.  
- Ablation study is minimal; unclear how much AdaRad contributes versus standard optimizers.  
- Presentation is dense and lacks intuitive explanations for complex concepts.  
- Reviewer urges expanded experiments: more datasets, stronger baselines, detailed ablations, and analysis of dataset sensitivity.  
- Suggest investigating and addressing the poor performance on the convex dataset.  
- Request clarification on AdaRad’s computational overhead compared to Adam/SGD.  
- Recommend exploring alternative regularization beyond L2 to improve stability and performance.  
- Ask for sensitivity analysis on initial network size and the addition rate (ν).
