This paper introduces nonparametric neural networks (NNs), a framework for dynamically adjusting network size during training, aiming to avoid the expensive hyperparameter search typically required for optimal network architecture. The core idea is to optimize over all possible network sizes within a single training cycle, guided by an L2 penalty to eliminate redundant units and a novel optimization algorithm, AdaRad. The paper also introduces CapNorm, a modified batch normalization technique suitable for this setting.

**Reject.** While the ideas presented are interesting and the problem addressed is relevant, the paper lacks sufficient empirical evidence to support its claims of superiority over standard approaches, and the experimental setup and analysis feel incomplete. The results are mixed and, crucially, the performance on the convex dataset is significantly worse than parametric networks, raising concerns about the generalizability of the approach.

The primary weakness lies in the limited and somewhat unclear demonstration of the benefits of nonparametric NNs. While improvements are shown on MNIST and rectangles, the experimental setup doesn’t convincingly demonstrate that the gains couldn't be achieved through a well-tuned parametric network search. The comparison to random search, although present, isn't robust enough to claim superiority – a more thorough hyperparameter optimization of the parametric networks is needed. The poker dataset experiment is promising, but lacks sufficient detail regarding the hyperparameter tuning for the parametric networks. Simply stating “RMSprop” and stating it was optimized isn’t enough; details on the search space and process are critical. 

Furthermore, the theoretical justification, while present, feels somewhat disconnected from the practical implementation. The theorem establishing a global minimum under certain conditions is valuable, but its practical implications are not thoroughly explored in the context of the experimental results. The explanation of *why* nonparametric NNs sometimes outperform parametric ones remains weak. Is it truly an inherent advantage of the dynamic sizing, or simply a result of the AdaRad optimizer, or specific regularization choices?

Finally, the paper is dense and could benefit from more clarity. The notation is heavy, and the explanations of some concepts (like the radial-angular gradient descent) are difficult to follow without carefully studying the algorithm. The presentation of the results could be improved; more detailed tables showing the best hyperparameters found for both nonparametric and parametric networks would be highly beneficial.

**Additional Feedback (to improve the paper):**

*   **More Rigorous Comparison:** A more thorough comparison with parametric networks is crucial. Implement a robust hyperparameter search strategy (e.g., Bayesian optimization) for the parametric networks, ensuring a fair comparison with the nonparametric approach. Include a wider range of datasets with varying complexities.
*   **Ablation Studies:** Conduct ablation studies to isolate the contributions of different components of the framework. For example, how much does AdaRad contribute compared to a standard optimizer with dynamic sizing? How does CapNorm impact performance?
*   **Theoretical Clarification:** Expand on the practical implications of the theoretical results. Specifically, discuss how the L2 penalty influences the network’s ability to generalize and avoid overfitting.
*   **Analysis of Convex Dataset Failure:** Investigate *why* the nonparametric network performs poorly on the convex dataset. This is a critical failure case that needs to be understood. Is it related to the dataset’s specific properties, or a limitation of the framework?
*   **Improve Clarity and Presentation:** Simplify the notation and provide more intuitive explanations of the core concepts. Use visualizations to illustrate the dynamic sizing process and the effects of the regularization.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Discuss the sensitivity of the approach to the choice of hyperparameters (e.g., alpha\_r, alpha\_f, lambda).
* **Provide code:** Release the code associated with the paper to allow for reproducibility and further investigation.

**Questions for the authors:**

*   What specific strategies were used to optimize the hyperparameters for the parametric networks in the comparison? Can you provide more details about the search space and optimization algorithm?
*   What is the computational overhead of AdaRad compared to standard optimizers like Adam or RMSprop?
*   Why does the nonparametric network perform so poorly on the convex dataset?
*   How sensitive is the performance of the nonparametric network to the choice of the regularization parameter λ?
*   Can you provide some intuition as to why dynamic network sizing helps achieve better results than static networks of similar size? Is it related to avoiding local optima, or improving generalization?
