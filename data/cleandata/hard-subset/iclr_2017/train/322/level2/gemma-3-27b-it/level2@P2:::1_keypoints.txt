- Paper proposes nonparametric NNs that adjust network size during training using an L2 penalty, AdaRad optimizer, and CapNorm.
- Decision: Reject due to insufficient empirical evidence and incomplete experimental analysis.
- Results are mixed; notably worse performance on the convex dataset undermines generalizability.
- Limited demonstration that nonparametric gains aren’t achievable by a well-tuned parametric network search.
- Comparison to random search is not robust; parametric networks need more thorough hyperparameter optimization.
- Poker dataset results look promising but lack details on parametric hyperparameter tuning and search space.
- Theoretical results (global minimum theorem) feel disconnected from practical implementation and experiments.
- Unclear whether improvements stem from dynamic sizing, AdaRad optimizer, or specific regularization choices.
- Paper is dense with heavy notation; explanations (e.g., radial-angular gradient descent) are hard to follow.
- Presentation of results is weak; needs detailed tables of best hyperparameters for both approaches.
- Recommend a more rigorous comparison using robust hyperparameter search methods (e.g., Bayesian optimization) and more datasets.
- Suggest ablation studies to isolate contributions of AdaRad, CapNorm, and dynamic sizing.
- Ask for clearer practical implications of the L2 penalty on generalization and overfitting.
- Investigate and explain the convex dataset failure to determine framework limitations.
- Improve clarity with simplified notation, intuitive explanations, and visualizations of dynamic sizing and regularization effects.
- Discuss sensitivity to hyperparameters (alpha_r, alpha_f, lambda) and provide guidance on their selection.
- Request code release for reproducibility and further evaluation.
- Questions for authors: detailed hyperparameter optimization strategy for parametric baselines and search space specifics.
- Questions for authors: computational overhead of AdaRad compared to Adam/RMSprop.
- Questions for authors: reasons for poor performance on the convex dataset and sensitivity to λ.
- Questions for authors: provide intuition why dynamic sizing outperforms static networks — avoidance of local optima or improved generalization?
