- Proposes Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller trained with REINFORCE using validation accuracy as reward to generate network architectures.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results: CIFAR-10 convnet (3.65% test error) and Penn Treebank recurrent cell (perplexity 62.4), with transfer to other tasks.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- High novelty: reframes architecture design as a learned generator via reinforcement learning, opening a new research direction beyond hyperparameter tuning and neuro-evolution.  
- Strong empirical validation: extensive experiments on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank showing competitive or superior performance to human-designed models.  
- Technical soundness: RNN controller and REINFORCE are appropriate; addresses gradient variance (moving-average baseline) and scalability (large-scale parallel/asynchronous training); adds mechanisms for skip connections.  
- Experimental rigor: thorough baselines, a control comparison to random search, and ablation analyses (e.g., skip connections) that support claims.  
- Clarity and generality: well-written with helpful diagrams; method applies to both convolutional and recurrent architectures.  
- Main limitation: very high computational cost (e.g., 800 GPUs for CIFAR-10 search) needing discussion of efficiency improvements.  
- Suggestion: deeper analysis of discovered architectures to identify recurring motifs or design principles.  
- Suggestion: discuss sensitivity of results to search-space design (choice of operations, filter sizes, activations).  
- Suggestion: justify the non-standard reward transformation (cubing validation accuracy) used for CIFAR-10.  
- Questions for authors: motivation and sensitivity for cubing validation accuracy as reward.  
- Questions for authors: whether a learned value-function baseline was tried and its effect on sample efficiency/stability.  
- Questions for authors: frequency and handling of compilation failures with skip connections and potential bias from heuristics.  
- Questions for authors: number of architectures sampled by random search in Figure 6 and whether it’s directly comparable to the controller’s evaluations.
