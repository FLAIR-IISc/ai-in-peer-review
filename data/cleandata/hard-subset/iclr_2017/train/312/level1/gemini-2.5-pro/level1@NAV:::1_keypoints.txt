- Introduces Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate variable-length neural network descriptions.
- Uses validation accuracy of the generated child network as the reward signal to guide architecture search.
- Primary contribution: a method that can discover state-of-the-art architectures from scratch on multiple benchmarks.
- Demonstrates a novel convolutional network for CIFAR-10 that surpasses human-designed models.
- Demonstrates a novel recurrent cell for Penn Treebank that achieves new state-of-the-art language modeling performance.
- Strong recommendation to accept based on significance of results and novelty of framing architecture design as an RL problem.
- Compelling empirical results: 3.65% test error on CIFAR-10 and 62.4 perplexity on Penn Treebank.
- Thorough experimentation includes a key comparison to random search, supporting the effectiveness of policy gradients.
- Transfer learning experiments show the discovered cell improves character-level language modeling and machine translation, indicating generalizability.
- Large-scale experimental setup adds credibility to the findings.
