- Introduces Neural Architecture Search: an RNN "controller" trained with REINFORCE to generate variable-length network architectures using validation accuracy as reward.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results: a CNN outperforming human designs on CIFAR-10 (3.65% error) and a novel RNN cell beating LSTM on PTB (perplexity 62.4).  
- Decision: Accept — considered a landmark paper likely to change how architectures are designed.  
- Novelty/significance: Combines RNN policy and RL to search flexible, variable-length architecture spaces beyond hyperparameter optimization and neuroevolution.  
- Empirical strength: Strong, diverse benchmarks and results convincingly support the method’s effectiveness.  
- Methodology: Elegantly uses an auto-regressive RNN controller and REINFORCE with a moving-average baseline to optimize non-differentiable rewards.  
- Skip connections: Introduces an attention-like mechanism to add skip connections and modern architectural motifs to the search space.  
- RNN cell search: Frames recurrent cell discovery as constructing a computation tree, an innovative generalization.  
- Evaluation rigor: Includes important controls (random search comparison), ablations, and analyses showing the controller truly learns.  
- Transferability: The discovered RNN cell generalizes to character-level LM and improves GNMT, showing broad applicability.  
- Compute cost: Experiments are extremely expensive (∼800 GPUs), acknowledged as a limitation but justified by scale.  
- Feedback — compute: Authors should quantify total GPU-hours/P100-days and discuss efficiency improvements (weight sharing, surrogates).  
- Feedback — analysis: Deeper analysis of recurring motifs and functional comparison of the discovered RNN cell to LSTM/GRU is needed.  
- Feedback — search space sensitivity: Discuss how results depend on human choices in the search space (e.g., filter sizes, tree base).  
- Question 1: Please provide total compute estimates (e.g., P100-days) for CIFAR-10 and PTB searches.  
- Question 2: How sensitive is REINFORCE to the specific reward forms used, and were simpler rewards tried?  
- Question 3: Why different controller training setups (m=8 for CIFAR-10 vs m=1 for PTB) and differing update accumulation strategies?  
- Question 4: Why choose a "base 8" tree for the RNN cell search space, and were smaller bases (e.g., 2 or 4) evaluated?
