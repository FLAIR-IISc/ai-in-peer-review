- Proposes NAS: a controller RNN generates child-network architectures and is trained with REINFORCE using child validation performance as reward.  
- CIFAR-10 result: discovered ConvNet achieves 3.65% test error, outperforming DenseNet and slightly faster.  
- Penn Treebank result: discovered RNN cell attains test perplexity 62.4, beating LSTM and generalizing to char-level LM and GNMT.  
- Major contribution: reframes architecture design as a learnable RL problem, enabling generation of complex, variable-length architectures with skip connections.  
- Decision: Accept — method is novel and impactful for automating architecture engineering (meta-learning/AutoML).  
- Strong empirical validation across diverse, competitive benchmarks supports the claims.  
- Method advantage over prior work: supports variable-length/complex connectivity vs. fixed-dimensional hyperparameter searches.  
- Well-situated in literature, connecting to neuro-evolution, program synthesis, and RL for non-differentiable objectives.  
- Transferability: discovered architectures/cells generalize across tasks, adding evidence of practical utility.  
- Control experiment: policy-gradient RL outperforms random search, showing the controller learns effective exploration.  
- Main drawback: extremely high computational cost (e.g., 800 networks on 800 GPUs); paper should report total GPU-days and discuss efficiency.  
- Request for deeper analysis of discovered architectures (recurring motifs, precise differences from LSTM) to yield design insights.  
- Concern about sensitivity to the manually designed search space (primitives like filter sizes and activations); needs discussion.  
- Reward shaping choices (accuracy^3, c/(perplexity)^2) require justification.  
- Questions for authors: quantify total compute (GPU-days); importance of curriculum/incremental depth strategy; whether random search baseline had equal evaluations and identical search space; and whether layer/block structures were fixed or part of the search.
