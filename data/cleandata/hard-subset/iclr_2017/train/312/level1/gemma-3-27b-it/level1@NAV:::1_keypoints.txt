- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RNN controller uses reinforcement learning to generate neural network architectures.  
- Shows strong empirical results on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, achieving or exceeding state-of-the-art (e.g., outperforming LSTM).  
- Presents a novel, impactful approach to automating architecture design, reducing expert labor.  
- Well-motivated and situated within related work (hyperparameter optimization, neuro-evolution, program synthesis).  
- Method description is clear, including use of skip connections and distributed training for implementation.  
- Experimental setup is rigorous with detailed reporting, ablation studies, and control experiments.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Weakness: Computational cost unclear — paper should report approximate total GPU/compute hours.  
- Weakness: Search space specifics and constraints need clarification and analysis of their impact on results.  
- Weakness: Lacks sensitivity analysis of controller RNN hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate, hidden size).  
- Weakness/Question: Insufficient insight into why NAS-discovered architectures perform well and whether common structural patterns emerge.
