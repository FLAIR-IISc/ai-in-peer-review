- Proposes an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate neural architectures.  
- Frames architecture design as a sequential prediction problem optimized via policy gradients (REINFORCE).  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main reasons for acceptance: addresses an important automation problem and delivers strong empirical results.  
- RNN controller choice is logical for variable-length architecture specifications.  
- Use of REINFORCE is standard and justified for non-differentiable rewards.  
- CIFAR-10 result: competitive test error (3.65%) with a reported speedup over prior SOTA.  
- Penn Treebank result: discovered recurrent cell surpasses LSTM, achieving new SOTA perplexity.  
- Transfer learning to character language modeling validates generalizability of the learned cell.  
- Control experiments (expanded search space, random search comparison) and ablations strengthen robustness of claims.  
- Implementation details are thorough; authors plan to release code and architectures to aid reproducibility.  
- Uses distributed training and asynchronous updates to scale the method.  
- Inclusion of skip connections and variable layer types makes the search space more flexible.  
- Concern: reward shaping (maximum validation accuracy cubed) appears arbitrary and needs justification or comparison to alternatives.  
- Concern: search space limitations (e.g., fixed filter sizes) may exclude useful architectural motifs.  
- Concern: very high computational cost (800 GPUs concurrent); needs a clearer GPU-hour breakdown to assess practicality.  
- Suggestion: visualize controller policy evolution (action probability distributions) to illuminate the search process.  
- Suggestion: evaluate robustness to controller initialization and report variance across random seeds.  
- Questions for authors: rationale and impact of the cubic reward exponent; primary constraints limiting search space and how to expand it; precise GPU-hours for CIFAR-10 and PTB runs; observed patterns or surprising architectural choices discovered; comparison to other NAS methods under equivalent resources.
