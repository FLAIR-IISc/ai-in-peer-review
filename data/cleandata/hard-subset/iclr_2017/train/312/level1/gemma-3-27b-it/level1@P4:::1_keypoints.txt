- Decision: Reject.
- Proposal: Introduces NAS — an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate neural architectures.
- Method: Controller samples architectures used to build and train child networks; child validation accuracy is the reward to update the controller.
- Empirical scope: Applied to CIFAR-10 (image) and Penn Treebank (language), achieving competitive results.
- Strength: RNN parameterization and RL/policy-gradient connection are reasonable and clearly explained.
- Strength: Small accuracy improvement on CIFAR-10 with speed advantages over prior SOTA.
- Strength: Surpasses LSTM on Penn Treebank — noteworthy result.
- Main concern: Experimental setup lacks sufficient detail for reproducibility (hardware, distributed config, hyperparameters).
- Main concern: Computational cost of NAS (training many child networks) is not adequately quantified or discussed.
- Main concern: Novelty feels incremental relative to existing NAS, neuroevolution, and Bayesian optimization literature.
- Main concern: Appendix referenced is missing, preventing full assessment of architectural details.
- Main concern: Claim of SOTA on character language modeling needs clearer comparison to recent work.
- Requested analyses: Ablation studies to isolate contributions (e.g., skip connections, parameter server).
- Clarity: Reward function (accuracy cubed) rationale is abrupt and needs explanation.
- Generalization: Recommend demonstrating method on a third dataset to assess dataset-specific effects.
- Reproducibility request: Provide exact hardware, software versions, training hyperparameters, and ideally code.
- Computational question: Provide total GPU-hours and scaling behavior of NAS with search-space complexity.
- Related-work request: Stronger comparison to neuroevolution (e.g., NEAT) and Bayesian optimization on scalability, sample efficiency, expressiveness.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Provide details on child-network tuning, controller baseline sensitivity, and controller learning-rate schedule effects.
- Transferability: Report how discovered architectures generalize across datasets/tasks and any observed transfer properties.
