- Summary: NAS uses an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate neural architectures, achieving state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank and discovering a recurrent cell that outperforms LSTMs.  
- Decision: Accept — significant contribution with compelling empirical results.  
- Strong empirical results: Achieves competitive/SOTA performance on CIFAR-10 and PTB (notably a 3.6 perplexity improvement on PTB).  
- Novelty of approach: Using an RNN controller + RL to generate variable-length architectures is a novel advance beyond standard hyperparameter optimization.  
- Clear presentation: Paper is well-written with helpful diagram references for understanding the method.  
- Practical implementation & release: Code and the discovered RNN cell are released (TensorFlow), aiding reproducibility.  
- Ablation studies & controls: Includes control experiments (expanded search space, random search comparisons) that strengthen the claims.  
- Computational cost: Method is computationally expensive; the paper should quantify required resources (e.g., GPU hours).  
- Search space exploration: Limited justification of search space choices and potential biases; clarify any prior architectural insights used.  
- Reward shaping: Use of cubed validation-accuracy reward seems arbitrary; justify and analyze sensitivity to reward choice.  
- Generalization: More experiments needed to assess transferability of learned architectures beyond PTB.  
- Missing figures: Referenced Figures 3, 4, 5, 7, and 8 are absent but essential for full understanding.  
- Q1 (resources): Please provide precise estimates of total computational resources (GPU hours, etc.) used.  
- Q2 (search space): What considerations and prior insights informed the convolutional and recurrent search-space design?  
- Q3 (reward sensitivity): How sensitive are results to the reward function; were alternatives (e.g., linear rewards) tested?  
- Q4 (controller sensitivity): How does performance vary with different controller RNN architectures (layers, hidden units)?  
- Q5 (figures): Please provide the missing referenced figures (3, 4, 5, 7, 8).
