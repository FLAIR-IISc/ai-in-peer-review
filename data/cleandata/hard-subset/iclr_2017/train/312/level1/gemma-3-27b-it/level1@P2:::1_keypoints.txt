- Decision: Accept — novel approach with strong empirical results warrants publication.
- Core idea: Represent architectures as sequences and train an RNN controller with policy gradients to automate NAS.
- Main merit: Automates architecture design, addressing a significant challenge in deep learning.
- Empirical strength: State-of-the-art or competitive results on CIFAR-10 (3.65% error) and Penn Treebank (62.4 perplexity), with good transfer learning.
- Novelty: RNN controller + reinforcement learning enables search over variable-length architecture spaces, distinct from hyperparameter optimization and neuro-evolution.
- Motivation and framing: Clearly defines architecture search as a sequential decision problem suitable for RL.
- Experiments: Thorough experimental validation including control experiments, comparisons to random search, and ablation studies.
- Reproducibility: Authors plan to release code and models to facilitate replication.
- Feedback — search space: Provide more detailed visualization and explanation of architecture possibilities and skip-connection mechanism.
- Feedback — computational cost: Report detailed resource requirements (e.g., number of GPUs, training time) and compare NAS cost to manual design.
- Feedback — controller choice: Justify the two-layer LSTM controller and explore alternative controller architectures.
- Feedback — baselines: Include more comprehensive direct comparisons to existing NAS methods on the same datasets.
- Feedback — sensitivity: Include sensitivity analysis of RL hyperparameters (learning rate, baseline, etc.).
- Question: Provide a detailed breakdown of computational cost for controller training and candidate evaluation.
- Question: How sensitive are results to the choice of baseline function in REINFORCE and were alternatives tested?
- Question: Clarify the convergence criteria for child networks and its effect on the reward signal.
- Question: Compare the discovered Penn Treebank cell to LSTMs of similar size when trained without the paper's regularization to isolate architectural benefits.
- Question: Describe observed limitations or failure cases, and types of architectures the controller struggles to discover.
