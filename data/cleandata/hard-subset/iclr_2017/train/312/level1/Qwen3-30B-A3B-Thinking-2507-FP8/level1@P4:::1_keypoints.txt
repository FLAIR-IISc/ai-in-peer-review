- Introduces NAS: a controller RNN trained with reinforcement learning to generate architectures that maximize validation accuracy, applied to CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.  
- Claims state-of-the-art results: CIFAR-10 test error 3.65% and Penn Treebank perplexity 62.4, and successful transfer to other tasks.  
- Decision: Accept — novel, well-motivated approach with strong, rigorously supported empirical results.  
- Tackles the important problem of automating architecture design, addressing limitations of existing hyperparameter-optimization and neuro-evolution methods.  
- Technical approach is sound: controller RNN as sequence generator and policy gradient training are appropriate and well-justified.  
- Paper provides sufficient detail about controller architecture, search space, and training procedure for reproducibility.  
- Demonstrates discovered architectures that outperform human-designed models on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.  
- Experimental design is rigorous, including control comparisons (random search, expanded search space) and transfer-learning experiments.  
- Results include quantitative metrics and qualitative visualizations, with limitations (search space constraints) acknowledged.  
- Suggestion: report computational resources required (total GPU hours/days) to evaluate practical feasibility.  
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis of discovered architectures (parameter counts and computational complexity vs prior SOTA).  
- Suggestion: give more context on search space constraints (number of architectures evaluated vs total search space).  
- Suggestion: clarify relationship between controller validation set and final test set to rule out data leakage.  
- Question: provide a fairer speed comparison with DenseNet (e.g., include 1x1 convolutions) or justify current comparison.  
- Question: clarify what “more than two times faster” measures for Penn Treebank (inference vs training) and report hardware used.  
- Question: confirm whether the held-out validation set was completely independent and whether the same validation set was reused across controller iterations.
