- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): a controller RNN trained with reinforcement learning to automatically generate neural network architectures.  
- Controller optimizes for validation accuracy to discover novel architectures without human intervention.  
- CIFAR-10 result: discovered architecture achieves 3.65% test error, 0.09% better than prior state-of-the-art and 1.05x faster than best human-designed model.  
- Penn Treebank result: discovered recurrent cell achieves 62.4 perplexity, 3.6 better than prior state-of-the-art and transfers to character language modeling.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: novel, well-motivated approach addressing an important challenge in deep learning.  
- Empirical results are impressive, thorough, and supported across two major benchmarks.  
- Method is a natural extension of reinforcement learning to architecture search with a clear, well-justified approach.  
- Paper includes important control experiments (e.g., comparison with random search) and demonstrates practical utility via transfer learning.  
- Well-situated in literature, distinguishing from fixed-space hyperparameter optimization and slower neuro-evolution methods.  
- Additional feedback: report computational resources required (e.g., total GPU hours), compare more comprehensively with recent NAS methods, and provide deeper analysis of why discovered architectures perform well.  
- Questions for authors: how much computational time was required; how does this compare to Zoph and Le (2017); and what specific architectural features drive the success of the discovered models?
