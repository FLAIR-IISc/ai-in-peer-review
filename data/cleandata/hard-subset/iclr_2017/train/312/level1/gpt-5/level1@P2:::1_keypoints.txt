- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): a recurrent controller trained with policy gradients to generate variable-length neural architectures.  
- Child models sampled from the controller are trained; their validation accuracy/perplexity is used as reward to update the controller.  
- Search spaces: CNNs with skip connections for CIFAR-10 and tree-structured recurrent cells for PTB.  
- CIFAR-10: discovered architecture achieves 3.65% test error after post-hoc filter scaling and is 1.05× faster than a comparable DenseNet.  
- PTB: discovered recurrent cell achieves 62.4 test perplexity, outperforming prior SOTA and transferring to character PTB and GNMT with modest gains.  
- Presents distributed asynchronous training for the controller and an attention-based mechanism to predict skip connections.  
- Compares against random search and shows policy-gradient controller outperforms random baselines in best-found and top-k averages.  
- Paper well-motivated, situates NAS relative to Bayesian optimization and neuro-evolution, and frames controller as an autoregressive sequence model.  
- Experimental methodology is generally strong, with ablations showing skip-connections and discovered cell designs matter.  
- Engineering contribution: large-scale asynchronous setup enabling practical NAS at scale.  
- Major caveat: extremely large computational budget (e.g., hundreds of GPUs/CPUs), raising reproducibility and accessibility concerns.  
- Validation protocol concern: small 5k CIFAR validation split risks controller overfitting; rotating/larger splits would be safer.  
- Fairness concerns: top CIFAR result uses post-hoc filter scaling and baseline architectures differ in constraints; clearer parameter/FLOP comparisons needed.  
- Reward shaping and early stopping biases: cubing accuracy and short child training could skew search; sensitivity analysis needed.  
- Random search baseline needs clearer matching of compute and variance reporting across seeds.  
- Suggested reporting: exact compute budgets (GPU/CPU days), wall-clock, energy and time-to-best-architecture curves.  
- Suggested robustness checks: multiple controller seeds to quantify variance and convergence stability.  
- Validation suggestions: use larger/rotating validation splits or cross-validation and report test performance across top candidates.  
- Fair comparison suggestions: report parameter counts, FLOPs and clarify which improvements are within search space vs. post-hoc tweaks.  
- Recommended ablations: effect of reward shaping, baseline choice, entropy regularization, curriculum on depth, enabling strides/pooling, and RNN operator/connection choices.  
- Failure analysis request: quantify invalid graph rates and how padding/auto-connections affect sample efficiency and quality.  
- Generalization suggestion: run preliminary ImageNet or proxy experiments to support broader claims.  
- Key questions for authors: exact GPU/CPU days and time-to-first-best for CIFAR/PTB; stability across controller seeds; evidence of controller overfitting to 5k validation.  
- Additional questions: rationale and sensitivity for cubing accuracy; whether random search had matched child-evaluation budget; whether adding 40 filters was inside original search space and final model parameter/FLOP comparisons.  
- RNN-specific questions: how connections to c_{t-1} and c_t are constrained, and whether parameter counts are exactly matched to LSTM baselines.
