- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RNN controller that generates network architectures and is trained with REINFORCE using child-model validation accuracy/perplexity as reward.  
- Key contributions: flexible variable-length search space supporting skip connections/branching.  
- Key contributions: RL training with variance-reduction baseline and large-scale asynchronous parallelization.  
- Key contributions: extension to recurrent cell search using a tree-structured parametrization.  
- Key contributions: strong empirical results (3.65% CIFAR-10, 62.4 perplexity PTB word LM) and transfer to other tasks.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Primary reason: Novel, general, well-motivated approach with strong cross-domain empirical performance and transfer.  
- Primary reason: Careful experimental protocol, ablations, and engineering enabling large-scale search.  
- Tackles automated architecture design beyond fixed-length hyperparameter tuning and situates well in related literature.  
- REINFORCE derivation and moving-average baseline are methodologically sound and variance-reducing.  
- Representing skip connections via content-based sigmoids fits naturally in the policy-gradient framework.  
- Recurrent cell search space is well-defined with explicit handling of cell states.  
- Distributed asynchronous training scheme is sensible for the workload.  
- CIFAR-10: used growing depth schedule, evaluated 12.8k architectures for 50 epochs; top model reaches 3.65% and is slightly faster than a DenseNet baseline.  
- CIFAR-10: ablations on skips show sensitivity, indicating meaningful topology search; test set used only once.  
- PTB word LM: searched only the cell, then modest post-hoc hyperparameter tuning; best cell improves ~3.6 perplexity over strong baselines and is cheaper than RHN.  
- Transfer: discovered cell improves PTB char LM and yields a small BLEU gain in GNMT without tuning.  
- Controls: includes random search baseline; policy gradient outperforms best-of-N and top-k averages and is robust to expanded op sets.  
- Significance: Demonstrates end-to-end architecture discovery can rival/exceed human design and is likely influential.  
- Reservation: Extremely high compute requirements limit reproducibility and accessibility despite code release and fixed child budgets.  
- Reservation: Search-space engineering choices may bias outcomes (padding, fallbacks, depth schedules, op sets).  
- Reservation: Some baseline comparisons (e.g., DenseNet) are not perfectly apples-to-apples due to manual capacity adjustments.  
- Suggested improvements: report GPU/CPU-days, wall-clock, learning curves vs samples, and sensitivity to number of architectures evaluated.  
- Suggested improvements: report variance across controller runs and child seeds with confidence intervals for final metrics.  
- Suggested improvements: justify reward shaping (cube of max validation) and ablate alternative reward definitions.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss asynchrony/staleness effects and mitigation, and quantify search-space ablations and fallback frequencies.  
- Suggested improvements: ensure random search baseline has equal budget and consider a strong SMBO baseline.  
- Suggested improvements: provide throughput, parameter/FLOP counts, and separate “as-searched” vs “capacity-scaled” CIFAR results.  
- Suggested improvements: report bpc for character PTB, and provide matched LSTM baselines with identical training recipes.  
- Questions for authors: how does performance scale with number of evaluated architectures and smallest viable budget?  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to controller architecture/optimizer and use of other variance-reduction methods?  
- Questions for authors: why cube max validation over last 5 epochs and does that bias selection?  
- Questions for authors: how often do skip-connection fallbacks trigger and do they correlate with top models?  
- Questions for authors: how much final gain comes from architecture vs post-hoc training hyperparameter tuning?  
- Questions for authors: can you provide apples-to-apples comparisons with DenseNet and matched LSTM training?  
- Questions for authors: how well do discovered architectures generalize to CIFAR-100, SVHN, or WikiText-2 without re-search?  
- Questions for authors: will you release controller and search code and provide guidelines for modest-compute scaling?
