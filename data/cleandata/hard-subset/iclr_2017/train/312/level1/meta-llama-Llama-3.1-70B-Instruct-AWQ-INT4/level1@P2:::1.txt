This paper proposes Neural Architecture Search, a method for automatically designing neural network architectures using a recurrent neural network as a controller. The controller is trained using reinforcement learning to maximize the expected accuracy of the generated architectures on a validation set. The approach is well-motivated, building on the success of deep neural networks in various applications and addressing the challenge of designing architectures, which currently requires significant expert knowledge and time.

I decide to **accept** this paper, with two key reasons for this choice:

1. The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to neural architecture search, which has the potential to significantly impact the field of deep learning.
2. The authors provide strong empirical evidence to support their claims, demonstrating the effectiveness of their method on two challenging benchmarks, CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.

The paper provides a clear and concise description of the proposed method, including the use of a recurrent neural network as a controller, the training process using reinforcement learning, and the techniques used to improve the efficiency of the search process. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the results, including comparisons to state-of-the-art models and ablation studies to understand the contributions of different components of their method.

The experiments are well-designed, and the results are impressive, demonstrating the ability of the proposed method to design architectures that rival or even surpass the performance of state-of-the-art models designed by humans. The authors also provide additional results on transfer learning and robustness, which further demonstrate the effectiveness of their method.

To further improve the paper, I suggest the following:

* Provide more details on the implementation of the controller, including the architecture and hyperparameters used.
* Discuss the potential limitations of the proposed method, such as the requirement for a large amount of computational resources and the potential for overfitting.
* Provide more analysis on the learned architectures, such as visualizations or discussions on the types of architectures that are preferred by the controller.
* Consider providing more comparisons to other neural architecture search methods, such as those based on evolutionary algorithms or Bayesian optimization.

Questions for the authors:

* Can you provide more details on the training process, including the number of iterations and the learning rate schedule used?
* How do you handle the case where the controller generates an invalid architecture, such as one with inconsistent layer sizes or connections?
* Have you considered using other types of controllers, such as feedforward neural networks or decision trees?
* Can you provide more analysis on the robustness of the learned architectures to changes in the input data or hyperparameters?
