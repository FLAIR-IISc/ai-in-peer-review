**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

This paper introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS), a method that leverages a recurrent neural network (RNN) to generate and optimize neural network architectures. The RNN, referred to as the controller, is trained using reinforcement learning to maximize the expected accuracy of the generated architectures on a validation set. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of NAS on two challenging tasks: image classification on CIFAR-10 and language modeling on Penn Treebank. The method is shown to discover novel architectures that rival or outperform state-of-the-art models, including those designed by humans.

**Decision: Accept**

I recommend accepting this paper due to its significant contributions to the field of neural network architecture design and optimization. The authors present a well-motivated and innovative approach that addresses the limitations of existing methods. The empirical results are impressive, demonstrating the potential of NAS to discover high-performing architectures.

**Supporting Arguments**

1. **Well-motivated approach**: The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the limitations of existing methods, including hyperparameter optimization and neuro-evolution algorithms. They demonstrate how NAS addresses these limitations by using a RNN to generate and optimize architectures.
2. **Strong empirical results**: The authors present extensive experimental results on two challenging tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of NAS in discovering novel architectures that rival or outperform state-of-the-art models.
3. **Significant contributions**: NAS has the potential to revolutionize the field of neural network architecture design and optimization. The authors' contributions are significant, and their work is likely to inspire further research in this area.

**Additional Feedback**

1. **Clarify the search space**: While the authors provide a detailed explanation of the search space, it would be helpful to include a visual representation of the search space to facilitate understanding.
2. **Provide more details on the controller RNN**: The authors could provide more details on the architecture and training of the controller RNN, including the choice of hyperparameters and the optimization algorithm used.
3. **Discuss the computational cost**: The authors mention that NAS can be computationally expensive, but they do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost. It would be helpful to include a discussion on the computational cost and potential ways to reduce it.
4. **Explore the transferability of the discovered architectures**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of NAS on two tasks, but it would be interesting to explore the transferability of the discovered architectures to other tasks and domains.

**Questions for the Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the choice of hyperparameters for the controller RNN, including the number of layers, hidden units, and optimization algorithm?
2. How do you handle the exploration-exploitation trade-off in the reinforcement learning algorithm used to train the controller RNN?
3. Can you provide more details on the computational cost of NAS, including the number of GPU hours required to train the controller RNN and evaluate the generated architectures?
4. Have you explored the use of NAS for other tasks and domains, and if so, what were the results?
