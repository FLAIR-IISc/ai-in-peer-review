**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach to neural architecture search (NAS) using a recurrent neural network (RNN) as a controller to generate model descriptions of neural networks. The controller is trained with reinforcement learning to maximize the expected accuracy of the generated architectures on a validation set. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on two challenging benchmarks: CIFAR-10 for image classification and Penn Treebank for language modeling. The results show that the proposed method can design novel architectures that rival or even surpass the best human-invented architectures in terms of test set accuracy.

**Decision**

Based on the review, I decide to **accept** the paper. The two key reasons for this choice are:

1. The paper proposes a novel and well-motivated approach to NAS, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method on two challenging benchmarks, which supports the claims made in the paper.

**Supporting Arguments**

The experimental results are thorough and well-presented, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the results.
