**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to neural architecture search, which uses a recurrent neural network (RNN) as a controller to generate neural network architectures. The controller is trained using reinforcement learning to maximize the expected accuracy of the generated architectures on a validation set. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on two challenging tasks: image classification on CIFAR-10 and language modeling on Penn Treebank. Their method achieves state-of-the-art results on both tasks, outperforming human-designed architectures.
