**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS), a method for automatically designing neural network architectures using a recurrent neural network (RNN) as the controller. The controller generates architectural hyperparameters of neural networks, and is trained with reinforcement learning to maximize the expected accuracy of the generated architectures on a validation set. The authors apply NAS to two challenging tasks: image classification on CIFAR-10 and language modeling on Penn Treebank. They demonstrate that NAS can design novel architectures that rival or outperform the best human-invented architectures in terms of test set accuracy.
