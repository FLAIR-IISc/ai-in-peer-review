- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RNN trained with reinforcement learning to automatically design neural network architectures.
- Claims NAS generates architectures that rival or outperform human-designed models for image classification and language modeling.
- On CIFAR-10, NAS achieves 3.65% test error and is 1.05x faster than the best human-designed model.
- On Penn Treebank, the NAS-discovered recurrent cell gets 62.4 test perplexity, outperforming LSTM and baselines.
- Demonstrates transferability of discovered architectures to character language modeling and machine translation.
- Decision: Accept.
- Motivation: Addresses the time-consuming, expert-driven problem of neural architecture design using reinforcement learning.
- Empirical rigor: Strong experimental validation across benchmarks with competitive accuracy and demonstrated transfer learning.
- Scientific contribution: Presents a flexible, scalable architecture-search framework handling variable-length architectures, skip connections, and distributed training.
- Suggestion: Provide deeper analysis of search space sensitivity (hyperparameter ranges, added functions) and search dynamics.
- Suggestion: Discuss computational cost and feasibility for researchers with limited resources (thousands of child networks, e.g., 800 GPUs).
- Suggestion: Improve reproducibility by including more implementation details (hyperparameters, training schedules) and releasing code.
- Question: How does NAS perform with constrained computational resources, and can the search be made more efficient?
- Question: How generalizable is NAS to other domains (e.g., reinforcement learning, time-series) and what are the limitations?
- Question: Can discovered architectures be improved further by manual fine-tuning, or are they near-optimal as found?
