- Proposes NAS: a controller RNN trained with policy gradients to generate variable-length neural architectures evaluated on validation accuracy.  
- Achieves SOTA on CIFAR-10: 3.65% test error (0.09% improvement) and 1.05x faster than previous best.  
- Achieves SOTA on Penn Treebank: 62.4 test perplexity (3.6 improvement) with architectures that transfer to character LM and machine translation.  
- Recommendation: accept, because it automates architecture design and shows strong empirical gains.  
- Strong motivation and literature positioning, clearly contrasts with hyperparameter optimization, neuro-evolution, and program synthesis.  
- Insightful connection to sequence-to-sequence learning and BLEU optimization in MT.  
- Methodology is sound, with careful practical implementation (parallelism, asynchronous updates, skip connection handling).  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive and robust across vision and NLP, with statistically significant improvements.  
- Transfer and control experiments (expanded search space, random search comparison) indicate systematic discovery rather than luck.  
- Reproducibility concern: request exact CIFAR-10 child network hyperparameters beyond citing Huang et al. (2016a).  
- Reproducibility concern: request explicit details of embedding/recurrent/variational dropout used in the Penn Treebank models.  
- Practicality concern: request clearer computational cost reporting (e.g., GPU-hours) beyond "800 GPUs."  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and makes a significant, high-impact contribution despite current high compute requirements.
