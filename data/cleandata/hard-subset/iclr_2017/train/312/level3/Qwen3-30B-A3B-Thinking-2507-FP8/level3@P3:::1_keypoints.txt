- Introduces Neural Architecture Search: a controller RNN trained with reinforcement learning to automatically generate variable-length neural architectures.  
- Shows strong empirical results: a CIFAR-10 conv model with 3.65% test error and a Penn Treebank recurrent cell with 62.4 perplexity, both improving prior SOTA.  
- Important contribution to automating neural network design, reducing required expert time and effort.  
- Decision: Accept — paper makes a strong contribution with compelling empirical evidence.  
- Effectively addresses the high cost of architecture design and situates itself well in related work (hyperparameter optimization, neuro-evolution, program synthesis).  
- The ability to search variable-length architectures is a meaningful advancement over previous fixed-length hyperparameter methods.  
- Empirical evaluation is rigorous and demonstrates versatility across vision and language tasks.  
- Provides interpretable insights into discovered architectures (e.g., rectangular filters, skip connections, LSTM-like recurrent cells).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and authors commit to releasing code for replication.  
- Reproducibility concern: paper lacks sufficient hyperparameter details; recommend releasing a base hyperparameter set (per Zaremba et al., 2014).  
- Request exact hyperparameters for all experiments, including specific dropout types and configurations used in language modeling.  
- Request detailed hyperparameters for the character language modeling experiment.  
- Questions for authors: provide exact language-modeling hyperparameters (including dropout types), confirm release of a base hyperparameter set, supply char LM hyperparameters, and clarify whether CIFAR-10 filter sizes were limited to [1,3,5,7].
