- Introduces Neural Architecture Search: an RNN "controller" trained with reinforcement learning to generate variable-length neural architectures.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, discovering architectures that rival or outperform human-designed baselines.  
- Key insight: architectures can be represented as variable-length strings, enabling sequence generation with RNNs.  
- Method achieves state-of-the-art results (3.65% CIFAR-10 error, 62.4 perplexity on PTB) and shows improved computational efficiency.  
- Well-motivated and novel approach addressing automatic architecture design, avoiding fixed-length search limitations.  
- Experiments are thorough with ablation studies, control experiments, transfer evaluations, and careful hyperparameter reporting.  
- Recommendation: accept, based on significance, novelty, and strong empirical results.  
- Request for more details on computational resources: total training time and hardware specs for the controller.  
- Clarification needed on the policy-gradient baseline: how it was chosen and its impact on training.  
- Suggest deeper analysis of how the number of sampled architectures affects final architecture quality.  
- Questions for authors: provide GPU-hours for best CIFAR-10 model, explain baseline selection (learned vs hand-crafted), and clarify the role of initial architecture/bootstrap and random-search comparators.
