- Introduces Neural Architecture Search: an RL approach treating DNNs as variable-length sequences, using auto-regressive two-layer LSTM as controller and critic and architecture evaluation error as reward.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, yielding architectures that rival or surpass human-designed models and offering computational advantages.  
- Recommendation: accept — paper judged excellent and a significant contribution to NAS.  
- Strength: well-motivated approach addressing hand-design challenges in deep learning.  
- Strength: clear technical exposition, comprehensive experiments, and compelling results outperforming human designs.  
- Strength: detailed method description and careful related-work discussion.  
- Improvement request: provide training-time and computational-efficiency data for controller and child networks and correlation between compute/time and model quality.  
- Improvement request: clarify the role and impact of human-bootstrapped models in the search process and what happens if they are not used.
