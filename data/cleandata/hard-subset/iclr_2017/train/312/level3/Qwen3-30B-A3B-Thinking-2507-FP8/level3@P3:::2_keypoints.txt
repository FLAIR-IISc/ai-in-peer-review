- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RL approach where an RNN controller generates architectures as variable-length sequences and is trained with policy gradients.
- Frames NAS as sequence generation and proposes a two-layer LSTM controller that serves as both actor and critic.
- Reports state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 (3.65% test error) and Penn Treebank (62.4 perplexity).
- Provides detailed analysis showing discovered architectures differ from human designs (e.g., larger top-layer filters, extensive skip connections).
- Decision: Accept — novel, well-motivated approach with strong empirical evidence and significant contribution.
- Positions the work clearly in literature, addressing limitations of fixed-length hyperparameter search and slow neuro-evolution methods.
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive: two domains, ablation studies, expanded search-space controls, and comparisons to random search.
- Method description is detailed and reproducible, covering controller architecture, reward function, training procedure, and search space.
- Incorporates thoughtful improvements like skip connections and a broader search space including diverse layer types.
- Requests more detail on total compute/training time and how compute correlates with model quality versus human-designed models.
- Asks for results on human-bootstrapped models and whether NAS can discover strong architectures without human initialization.
- Requests clarification on the critic implementation in the actor-critic framework, transfer-learning implementation for character modeling, and explanations for why discovered architectures differ from human designs.
