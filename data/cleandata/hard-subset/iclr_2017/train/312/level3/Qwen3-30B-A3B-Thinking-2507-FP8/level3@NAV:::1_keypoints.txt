- Proposes Neural Architecture Search using a controller RNN to generate architectures and policy gradients to update it.
- Evaluated on vision (CIFAR-10) and language modeling (Penn Treebank) benchmarks with strong results.
- Discovers novel, dataset-specific architectures that outperform human-designed models.
- Recommends acceptance; contribution is well-motivated and addresses an important problem.
- Scientifically rigorous with thorough experiments and clear improvements over state-of-the-art.
- Strength: able to explore beyond standard RNN/CNN search spaces without manual engineering.
- Significant reproducibility concern: missing exact hyperparameters (notably dropout types and settings).
- Requests inclusion of base hyperparameters (as in Zaremba et al. 2014) and full specs for all experiments, including character LM.
- Authors’ note on expected decrease in computational cost is appropriate.
- Suggested improvements: provide complete hyperparameter details, release a base configuration file, and detail dropout implementation (types, rates, application points).
- Overall assessment: well-written paper with strong results; minor additions would substantially improve reproducibility and impact.
