- Introduces Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate architectures that maximize validation accuracy.
- Applied to CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, achieving state-of-the-art results (3.65% test error on CIFAR-10; 62.4 perplexity on PTB).
- Decision: Accept — significant contribution to automated machine learning with strong empirical evidence.
- Tackles the important problem of automating neural architecture design and situates work well in related literature.
- Novel and well-executed RNN+RL approach for architecture generation with a clear training procedure.
- Discovered architectures outperform many human-designed models.
- Method is flexible enough to generate complex structures (e.g., skip connections).
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, including ablation studies and control experiments.
- Major concern: very high computational cost (12,800 architectures, ~800 GPUs), limiting practical applicability.
- Recommend more comparisons with recent NAS methods (e.g., DARTS, ENAS) for better context.
- Recommend deeper analysis of why discovered architectures work, including visualizations and structural comparisons.
- Recommend explicitly discussing computational resource requirements and potential efficiency improvements.
- Recommend additional experiments on other datasets/tasks to demonstrate generalizability.
- Question: How would the approach scale to larger datasets/tasks (ImageNet, object detection) and would patterns generalize?
- Question: Please provide more detailed comparisons between discovered architectures and human-designed ones (ResNet, LSTM) in structure, complexity, and performance.
- Question: How does computational cost compare to other NAS methods and what are the main bottlenecks to address?
- Question: Provide details on transferring the discovered recurrent cell to character language modeling—adaptation steps and observed performance gains.
