- Proposes Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate variable-length network architectures, using validation accuracy as reward.  
- Demonstrates strong results: CIFAR-10 test error 3.65% (0.09% better than prior SOTA) and Penn Treebank perplexity 62.4 (3.6 better than prior SOTA).  
- Generated architectures resemble human-designed networks, suggesting compatibility with human expertise.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revision.  
- Major limitation: lacks evaluation on additional datasets to demonstrate generality; at least one more dataset requested.  
- Practical limitation: long training time not adequately addressed; needs thorough discussion.  
- Request comparison of computational costs versus human-designed architectures.  
- Ask for more detailed comparisons with human-designed models to show advantages of automatic designs.  
- Request ablation studies to quantify the impact of different method components.  
- Questions: How would the method perform on other tasks (e.g., object detection or segmentation)? What are the exact computational resources required compared to human-designed architectures?
