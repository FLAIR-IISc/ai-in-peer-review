- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS) using an RNN controller and reinforcement learning to automate architecture design for CNNs and RNN cells.  
- Applied to CIFAR-10 and PTB, achieving state-of-the-art results (3.65% error on CIFAR-10; 62.4 perplexity on PTB).  
- Demonstrates transferability of the discovered recurrent cell to other tasks like character modeling and machine translation.  
- Decision: Accept — novelty and potential outweigh limitations.  
- Innovation: Novel combination of RNN-based generator and RL, addressing fixed-length search spaces and reliance on human expertise.  
- Empirical rigor: Detailed methodology (policy gradients, distributed training, variance reduction) and robust experimental setup with baselines.  
- Results are compelling and often superior to human-designed architectures; transferability strengthens claims.  
- Major weakness: Very high computational cost (e.g., 800 GPUs for CIFAR-10, 400 CPUs for PTB).  
- Suggestion: Provide strategies to reduce computation (search-space pruning, transfer learning, etc.).  
- Limited generality: Experiments confined to CIFAR-10 and PTB, needing broader dataset evaluation.  
- Suggestion: Test on larger/diverse datasets (e.g., ImageNet, large-scale language modeling) to validate scalability and generality.  
- Lack of in-depth comparison with human-designed architectures in terms of design principles, efficiency, and interpretability.  
- Questions for authors: scalability limits to larger datasets, methods to reduce computational cost (e.g., transfer learning), and interpretability/patterns in discovered architectures.  
- Additional feedback: Expand exploration of real-world application performance and more detailed discussion of limitations and future directions.  
- Conclusion: Significant contribution to automated neural architecture design; novelty, rigor, and results justify acceptance despite scalability and interpretability concerns.
