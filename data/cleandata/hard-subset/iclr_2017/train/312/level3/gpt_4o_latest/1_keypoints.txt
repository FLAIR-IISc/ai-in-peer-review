- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to automate neural network architecture design for CNNs and RNN cells.  
- Demonstrates strong results: 3.65% test error on CIFAR-10 and 62.4 test perplexity on Penn Treebank.  
- Shows transfer/generalization: discovered architectures also perform well on character-level modeling and machine translation.  
- Decision: Paper recommended for acceptance.  
- Significant contribution: Addresses automation of architecture design, reducing expert time and achieving state-of-the-art performance.  
- Scientific rigor: Methodology is well-detailed and experiments compare against strong baselines.  
- Well-motivated approach: Novel application of reinforcement learning to iteratively improve an RNN controller for architecture search.  
- Empirical strength: NAS outperforms human-designed architectures and shows robust transfer learning.  
- Broader impact: Potential to democratize neural network design by lowering reliance on expert knowledge.  
- Suggestion — hyperparameters: Paper lacks full hyperparameter and dropout details; include them to aid reproducibility.  
- Suggestion — computational cost: Need clearer accounting of resources (e.g., GPU hours) and strategies to reduce cost.  
- Suggestion — ablation studies: Additional ablations needed to isolate effects of components like skip connections and RL.  
- Question — reproducibility: Request a detailed list of hyperparameters and dropout techniques used.  
- Question — efficiency: Ask how NAS compares in computational cost to other search methods and plans for efficiency improvements.  
- Question — limitations: Inquire about observed failure cases or limits when applied beyond the presented benchmarks.  
- Conclusion: Strong, impactful work that would benefit from improved reproducibility and discussion of computational efficiency.
