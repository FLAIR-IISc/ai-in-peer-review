- Introduces an actor-critic reinforcement learning method to automate neural architecture design by treating architectures as variable-length sequences.  
- Uses a two-layer auto-regressive LSTM as controller and critic, with node selections as actions and evaluation error as reward.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 (3.65% test error) and Penn Treebank (62.4 perplexity), outperforming several human-designed models.  
- Employs improvements like skip connections and distributed asynchronous training and compares comprehensively to related work.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides detailed methodology, training procedures, and control experiments (e.g., random search comparison).  
- Decision: Accept — strong contributions, novelty, rigorous evaluation, and empirical support justify acceptance.  
- Novelty/impact: RL-based generation of variable-length architectures is a significant advancement over existing hyperparameter optimization methods.  
- Empirical rigor: Thorough experiments across diverse tasks show generalizability and competitive or superior performance.  
- Clarity/completeness: Well-organized presentation with comprehensive comparisons and experimental details.  
- Suggest quantifying computational cost and training time relative to manual designs and other automated methods.  
- Suggest exploring initialization of search with human-designed architectures to speed or improve search.  
- Recommend testing on larger datasets and different modalities (e.g., speech or video) to strengthen generalizability claims.  
- Propose ablation studies to isolate the impact of components like skip connections and distributed training.  
- Questions: How does the method scale to larger/complex tasks and resource constraints?  
- Questions: Can the approach be extended to multi-objective optimization (accuracy vs. size/speed)?  
- Questions: How sensitive are results to controller RNN hyperparameters (learning rate, layers)?  
- Questions: Have the authors examined interpretability or recurring patterns in generated architectures to inform manual design?
