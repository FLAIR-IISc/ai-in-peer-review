- Presents Neural Architecture Search (NAS): a controller RNN trained with reinforcement learning to generate neural network architectures, evaluated on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.  
- Decision: Accept — important problem, novel and compelling approach with strong multi-benchmark results despite high computational cost.  
- Addresses bottleneck of manual architecture design by offering a data-driven automated alternative that finds architectures competitive with or superior to human designs.  
- Demonstrates generality: effective on both vision (CIFAR-10) and language (Penn Treebank), including a discovered recurrent cell that outperforms LSTM and transfers to character-level modeling.  
- Experiments are well-designed, with baseline comparisons, ablation studies, and a practical parallelization strategy to mitigate computational expense.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the methodology.  
- Request more complete hyperparameter details for reproducibility (controller and child networks, learning rates, optimizers, batch sizes, weight decay, dropout types, embedding dims, etc.).  
- Request same hyperparameter transparency for the character-level language modeling experiments.  
- Ask for a more detailed discussion of how the search space was chosen and what guided selection of layer types, filter sizes, and hyperparameters.  
- Recommend a detailed breakdown of computational cost (number of GPUs, training time) to assess practical feasibility.  
- Request additional details on the controller RNN architecture (layers, hidden size, activations).  
- Question: Provide a complete table of all CIFAR-10 hyperparameters, specifying dropout types.  
- Question: What criteria or heuristics defined the search space for each task?  
- Question: Please elaborate the schedule used to increase child network layers during controller training.  
- Question: Clarify the baseline used in REINFORCE — how is the exponential moving average computed and what decay rate is used?  
- Question: For GNMT transfer, what specific modifications beyond replacing the LSTM were made, and were hyperparameters retuned after replacement?
