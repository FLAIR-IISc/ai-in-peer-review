- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate neural network architectures.  
- Demonstrates strong results on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, achieving competitive/state-of-the-art performance.  
- Decision: Accept — compelling, well-executed approach to automating architecture design.  
- Addresses an important problem by automating expert-driven, time-consuming architecture design.  
- NAS supports variable-length architecture descriptions, overcoming fixed-length search limitations.  
- Method is well-motivated, building on reinforcement learning and sequence generation advances.  
- CIFAR-10 result: 3.65% test error, slight improvement and 1.05x speedup over prior state-of-the-art.  
- Penn Treebank result: new state-of-the-art perplexity of 62.4 for language modeling.  
- Transfer learning to character-level language modeling shows robustness and generality.  
- Paper is well-written with clear methodology and detailed experimental setup.  
- Includes control experiments (random search) that strengthen the empirical claims.  
- Authors acknowledge high computational cost of NAS and note it as future work.  
- Request for more hyperparameter details (training schedules, regularization, dropout) to aid reproducibility.  
- Request for detailed resource reporting (GPU count, GPU-hours) to clarify scalability and feasibility.  
- Ask for clearer explanation/examples of how skip connections and padding are handled to avoid compilation failures.  
- Questions for authors: breakdown of computational resources; impact of different baseline functions in RL; hyperparameter tuning protocol for character-level task; full set of layer types the controller can generate.
