- Decision: Accept — significant contribution to automated machine learning and NAS.  
- Main idea: Frame NAS as an RL problem with a recurrent controller generating architectures rewarded by validation accuracy.  
- Execution: The approach is well-executed and well-motivated, using variable-length sequence representation for architectures.  
- Results: Strong empirical performance on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, outperforming some human-designed architectures.  
- Transferability: Learned recurrent cell transfers well to character language modeling, showing generalizability.  
- Baselines and controls: Comparison to random search and inclusion of control experiments strengthen the findings.  
- Method details: Paper provides clear, detailed methodology including skip connections and parallel training improvements.  
- Reproducibility: Code release and integration of NASCell into TensorFlow are valuable for the community.  
- Requested clarification: Provide detailed computational cost (training time) per architecture and correlate search compute with final performance.  
- Human priors: Clarify whether any human-designed architectures were used to seed or guide the search and provide comparisons with/without seeding.  
- Architecture analysis: More in-depth/visual comparison of generated vs. human-designed architectures and recurring design patterns is suggested.  
- Hyperparameters: Provide details on the post-controller grid search (ranges explored) and its impact on reported performance.
