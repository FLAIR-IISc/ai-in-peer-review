- Decision: Accept — novel RL-based neural architecture search (NAS) with strong empirical support.  
- Approach: Frame architecture design as a sequential decision process using an RNN controller optimized with policy gradients (REINFORCE).  
- Representation: Encode neural architectures as variable-length sequences generated by the controller.  
- Training details: Includes parallelization scheme, incorporation of skip connections, and a clear training procedure.  
- Empirical results: Strong performance on CIFAR-10 (3.65% test error) and Penn Treebank, outperforming human-designed baselines.  
- Generalization: Transfer learning experiments on character language modeling further support the method’s generality.  
- Ablation/control studies: Ablations (adding functions to search space) and random-search comparisons provide robustness insights.  
- Positioning: Well situated relative to hyperparameter optimization, neuro-evolution, and program synthesis literature.  
- Limitation — compute: Training is computationally demanding; more details on GPU count and wall-clock time are needed.  
- Suggestion — human-bootstrapping: Investigate initializing the controller from existing architectures to accelerate/improve search.  
- Suggestion — visualization: Provide visualizations of search-space exploration over time to analyze convergence and local optima.  
- Suggestion — architectural analysis: Expand on observed differences between generated and human-designed architectures for deeper insight.  
- Question: What were the total training time and number of GPUs used?  
- Question: Have you tried initializing the controller with knowledge from existing architectures, and what were the results?  
- Question: What strategies are used to prevent the controller from getting stuck in local optima?
