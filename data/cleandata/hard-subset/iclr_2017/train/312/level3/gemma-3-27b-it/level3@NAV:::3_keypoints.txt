- Proposes using an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate neural architectures as a sequential decision process.  
- Novel formulation departs from manual architecture engineering and is a promising automated design approach.  
- Experimental results on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank are strong, showing competitive or superior performance to human-designed models.  
- Generated architectures share characteristics with human designs, indicating captured intuitive design principles.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main reason for acceptance: novelty and potential impact of the RNN+RL framework.  
- Empirical evidence: substantial performance gains (e.g., 3.65% error on CIFAR-10, 3.6 perplexity improvement on Penn Treebank).  
- Transfer learning experiments on character language modeling further support validity.  
- Limitation: reported training time is significant and may hinder practicality.  
- Suggestion: evaluate generality across more datasets and architectures (e.g., beyond recurrent cells to Transformers).  
- Suggestion: explore techniques to accelerate training, such as efficient exploration or transfer from prior searches.  
- Questions for authors: scalability with search-space complexity and computational resource limits.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to RL hyperparameters (robustness).  
- Questions for authors: interpretability insights and recurring architectural patterns from generated models.  
- Questions for authors: direct comparison to evolutionary NAS methods and advantages over evolutionary strategies.
