- Presents a novel formulation of neural architecture search as a sequential decision process with an RNN controller generating architectures and learning via policy gradients (REINFORCE).  
- Implements parallel training with a parameter server and uses validation performance of child networks as the reward signal.  
- Achieves strong empirical results, outperforming or matching state-of-the-art on CIFAR-10 (3.65% error) and Penn Treebank, and finds RNN cells that beat LSTM.  
- Demonstrates good transfer learning results to character language modeling, showing method generality.  
- Positions the method relative to hyperparameter optimization, neuroevolution, and program synthesis, and discusses differences between machine-generated and human-designed architectures (e.g., rectangular filters).  
- Decision: Accept with enthusiasm; paper is a significant contribution with sound approach and compelling results.  
- Notes computational expense of training child networks; requests more detailed reporting of total training time and number of architectures evaluated.  
- Suggests analyzing the relationship between computational resources (e.g., GPU hours) and quality of discovered architectures.  
- Recommends exploring incorporation of human-designed architectures as pre-training/initialization for the controller to potentially accelerate learning.  
- Calls for ablation studies to quantify the contribution of individual components (e.g., skip connections, controller RNN choice).  
- Suggests expanding visualizations to show architectures generated during training, not just the final best ones.  
- Asks authors for a detailed breakdown of total training time (GPU hours) for controller training and architecture evaluation on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.  
- Asks whether there is a clear correlation between compute spent during search and resulting architecture performance.  
- Asks if experiments were done incorporating human-designed architectures as starting points and what effects were observed.  
- Asks about the main challenges when scaling this approach to larger datasets and more complex search spaces.
