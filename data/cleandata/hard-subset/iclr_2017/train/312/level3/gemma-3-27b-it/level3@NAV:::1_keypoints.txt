- Proposes a novel RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to automatically generate neural architectures.  
- Core idea is compelling and demonstrably effective.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art or competitive performance on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.  
- Method can discover architectures beyond those humans typically explore and tailor them to specific datasets.  
- Represents a significant contribution to automated architecture design.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Well-motivated framing of architecture design as a search problem amenable to reinforcement learning.  
- Benefits include automated discovery of superior architectures and reduced reliance on expert knowledge.  
- Experimental results are robust, with clear gains and successful transfer to character language modeling.  
- Analysis yields interesting insights into discovered architectures (e.g., preference for rectangular filters).  
- Reproducibility concerns: missing exact hyperparameter values and types of dropout used.  
- Suggest releasing a base set of hyperparameters with the code (following Zaremba et al.).  
- Requests detailed hyperparameters for all experiments (dropout rates, optimizer settings, data augmentation).  
- Requests quantification of computational cost (e.g., total GPU hours).  
- Asks for clarification on the impact of controller RNN depth and whether different controller architectures were tested.
