- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (RNN controller + reinforcement learning) to automate neural network design, achieving strong results on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.  
- Decision: Accept — approach is innovative, results compelling, and impact is significant despite high computation.  
- Tackles a critical problem by replacing manual architecture design with an automated search capable of variable-length architectures.  
- Method is well-motivated and connected to hyperparameter optimization, neuro-evolution, and program synthesis literature.  
- Employs a recurrent controller with RL to iteratively learn to generate high-performing architectures.  
- Strong empirical evaluation: CIFAR-10 test error 3.65% and PTB perplexity 62.4, outperforming some human-designed models.  
- Transfer learning experiments show the discovered recurrent cell generalizes beyond the original task.  
- Main limitation: computational expense, acknowledged by authors and expected to lessen as resources grow.  
- Reproducibility concerns: request for detailed dropout configuration, complete Penn Treebank hyperparameters, and baseline hyperparameters.  
- Practicality concern: ask for precise estimate of computational resources required (e.g., total GPU hours).  
- Questions for authors: rationale and impact of the reward function (cubing accuracy).  
- Questions for authors: details on how skip connections were implemented and controlled in the search space.  
- Questions for authors: strategies used to prevent the controller from getting stuck in local optima and encourage exploration.  
- Questions for authors: parameter server scaling bottlenecks and how they were addressed.  
- Questions for authors: whether further experiments with different layer types or learning rate prediction have been explored and their outcomes.
