- Paper proposes Neural Architecture Search (controller RNN trained with REINFORCE) to auto-generate variable-length network architectures and RNN cells.  
- Key technical contributions: autoregressive controller including set-selection attention for skips, scalable asynchronous distributed training, and tree-based generation for recurrent cells.  
- Empirical highlights: 3.65% CIFAR-10 error, 62.4 perplexity on PTB word-level, good transfer to PTB-char (1.214) and modest BLEU gain in GNMT.  
- Control experiments show robustness to enlarged search spaces and outperform random search over time.  
- Discovered architectures recover human-like motifs (skip connections, repeated affine transforms).  
- Decision: Accept (weak) — novel, general, and impactful with strong results across vision and language.  
- Motivation: Addresses laborious manual architecture engineering and limitations of fixed-length HPO and heuristic neuro-evolution.  
- Relation to literature: Well-situated relative to HPO, neuroevolution, meta-learning, and RL; sensible extensions linking to modern design patterns.  
- Correctness/rigor: Standard, sound learning setup (REINFORCE with baseline, reward shaping, async updates) with valuable control experiments.  
- External validity: Notable transfer demonstrations (PTB-char, GNMT) strengthen claims beyond proxy tasks.  
- Limitation: Extremely compute-intensive (hundreds of GPUs/CPUs, thousands of models), raising accessibility and reproducibility concerns.  
- Limitation: Narrow empirical scope (CIFAR-10 only for vision, PTB for language) with no CIFAR-100/ImageNet or larger corpora.  
- Limitation: Some manual tuning (e.g., adding 40 filters) blurs the line between automated and manual design.  
- Limitation: Baseline fairness and speed comparisons need clearer normalization and accounting (e.g., DenseNet 1x1 compressions).  
- Limitation: Impact of proxy training length and reward shaping (cubed accuracy) on sample efficiency and stability is not fully ablated.  
- Suggested eval: Test discovered CNNs on CIFAR-100/SVHN and train at least one on ImageNet (even short-proxy) to assess scalability.  
- Suggested eval: Evaluate RNN cells on additional corpora (WikiText-2/103) or domain-shifted PTB subsets for robustness.  
- Suggested reporting: Provide total compute (GPU/CPU hours), energy/cost estimates, and anytime performance curves (best/median vs. architectures evaluated).  
- Suggested fairness: Normalize speed and parameter counts across baselines and clarify measurement details (wall-clock, FLOPs, batch size, hardware).  
- Suggested ablations: Study reward transformation, baseline choice, controller size, depth schedule, and correlation between proxy and final performance.  
- Suggested isolation: Separate contributions of regularization/shared embeddings versus discovered cell in PTB with controlled comparisons.  
- Reproducibility requests: Release seeds, variance across controller runs, performance distributions for top-k architectures, search code, and a small-budget recipe.  
- Questions for authors: Total compute used and scaling with 10x less compute; number of architectures trained to completion and variance across runs.  
- Questions for authors: Correlation between early-stopping validation reward and final test accuracy; sensitivity to reward shaping and moving-average baseline.  
- Questions for authors: Effect of adding more layer types or restricting skip connections; impact of RNN base size (4/6/8) on search efficiency and quality.  
- Questions for authors: Fair comparison to DenseNet with matched 1x1 conv usage and parameter counts; additional transfers to other datasets or language pairs.  
- Practical guidance requested: Recommendations for practitioners with limited compute (1–8 GPUs) and reduced-cost settings that still beat human baselines.
