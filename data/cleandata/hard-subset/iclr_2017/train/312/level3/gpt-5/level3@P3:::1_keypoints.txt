- Paper proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS): a controller RNN that generates variable-length network architectures, trained with REINFORCE using validation accuracy as reward.  
- NAS supports rich search spaces (skip connections, multiple CNN layer types, tree-structured RNN cells), uses large-scale parallel asynchronous child model evaluation, and progressively increases depth.  
- Empirical results: CIFAR-10 CNN with 3.65% test error (competitive, 1.05x faster than comparable DenseNet); novel RNN cell beating LSTM on PTB (62.4 ppl), state-of-the-art PTB char (1.214 ppl), and +0.5 BLEU in GNMT without retuning.  
- Decision: Accept — strong cross-modal empirical gains and high potential impact despite high compute.  
- Positioning: Well-situated relative to hyperparameter optimization, neuro-evolution, seq2seq, and meta-learning; motivates variable-length architecture search beyond fixed hyperparameter search.  
- Methodology: Controller design, reward shaping, content-based skip selection, RNN-cell search with ct/ht integration, and distributed asynchronous training are sound and well-described.  
- Evidence and rigor: Multiple evaluations (CIFAR-10, PTB, ablations, random-search control, transfer to char LM and GNMT) support the claim that NAS finds non-trivial high-performing architectures.  
- Generality: Demonstrated improvements in both CNN and RNN domains; discovered RNN cell shows compelling cross-task transfer.  
- Reproducibility: Request full disclosure of exact hyperparameters and regularization for all experiments (detailed dropout types/rates, recurrent dropout style, gradient clipping, weight decay/init, optimizer hyperparams, LR schedules, batch sizes, BPTT lengths, early stopping).  
- Reproducibility (cont.): Specifically ask for matching granularity of hyperparameter detail for PTB char and GNMT as provided for PTB word-level.  
- Release configs: Recommend publishing base hyperparameter templates (small/medium/large) for PTB word-level, PTB char-level, CIFAR-10 child CNN training, and GNMT substitution.  
- Release artifacts: Request full machine-readable specifications of best discovered architectures (controller outputs/graphs) and code to instantiate them.  
- Reward shaping: Ask justification for accuracy^3 and perplexity-based c(·)^2 rewards, plus sensitivity analysis to exponents, c, advantage normalization, and entropy regularization.  
- Search-space constraints: Question fixed pooling indices in the 39-layer CNN and sensitivity to those choices, and whether allowing controller-chosen pooling yields comparable results.  
- Compute budget and fairness: Request per-model training time and total compute per setting, and clarification of the “1.05x faster” DenseNet comparison (hardware, +40 filters inclusion).  
- Stability and variance: Request reporting variability across controller seeds and retrainings of discovered architectures.  
- Controller training details: Ask for baseline EMA decay rate, gradient accumulation specifics, temperature/softmax scaling, entropy regularization, and other variance-reduction methods.  
- Scope and future work: Suggest reporting performance vs. compute trade-offs (best-of-N curves), preliminary results on larger datasets (ImageNet), and partial-epoch/early-stopping strategies.  
- Implementation robustness: Recommend discussing compilation-failure mitigation and its effect on search efficiency.  
- Specific questions for authors: exact dropout types/rates per task and whether masks are time-/layer-locked; embedding dropout usage beyond PTB word-level.  
- Specific questions (cont.): gradient clipping thresholds and weight initialization schemes per task; any layer norm or stabilization techniques used.  
- Specific questions (cont.): final CIFAR child LR schedule, BN epsilon/momentum, and augmentation parameters for reported best models.  
- Specific questions (cont.): whether entropy regularization, temperature annealing, advantage normalization were used for controller training and exact EMA decay.  
- Specific questions (cont.): sensitivity of results to fixed pooling positions and depth schedule; can controller select pooling locations with similar performance.  
- Specific questions (cont.): how “1.05x faster” was measured (identical hardware/augmentation and inclusion of +40 filters); consistency of discovered architectures across controller initializations and motif convergence.
