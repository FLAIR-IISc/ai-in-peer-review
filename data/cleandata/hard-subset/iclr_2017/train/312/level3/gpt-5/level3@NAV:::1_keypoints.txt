- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (controller RNN trained with policy gradients) to generate variable-length architectures with skip connections and multiple layer types, extended to recurrent cell discovery.
- Strong cross-domain empirical results: CIFAR-10 3.65% error, PTB 62.4 ppl, character PTB 1.214 ppl, and gains in GNMT.
- Paper is clearly written, well positioned in literature, and includes informative ablations and a random-search comparison.
- Decision: Accept.
- Primary reason 1: Flexible, well-motivated approach that expands beyond standard NAS search spaces and yields strong empirical gains.
- Primary reason 2: Demonstrates NAS can produce dataset-specific architectures without manual engineering, supported by convincing ablations and baselines.
- Problem significance: Automating architecture design is timely and impactful; computational cost is high now but likely to decrease.
- Motivation/related work: Properly positioned relative to Bayesian optimization, neuroevolution, and meta-learning; variable-length search and skip-connection mechanism are convincing advances.
- Evidence and rigor: Results are competitive/state-of-the-art with credible transfer experiments; random-search control and proper test-set protocol are good practices.
- Reproducibility request: Provide exact hyperparameters and dropout variants/rates (embedding, recurrent/variational, input/output/hidden masks, embedding tying) and a “base set” of hyperparameters for small/medium/large configs.
- Experimental completeness: Extend hyperparameter disclosure to character PTB and GNMT (optimizer settings, LR schedules, clipping, batch size/BPTT, initialization, weight decay, augmentation, reward shaping, baseline details).
- Resource reporting: Report full compute budgets (total GPU/CPU hours, number of architectures evaluated) and sensitivity to reward shaping (e.g., accuracy^3, perplexity scaling).
- Release artifacts: Share controller/search-space definitions and config files to facilitate replication and future work.
- Specific questions for authors: exact dropout variants/rates per task; gradient clipping, initialization, and optimizer hyperparameters (betas, eps); sensitivity to reward transformation and baseline EMA timescale; and canonical base hyperparameter sets and seeds for standardized comparisons.
