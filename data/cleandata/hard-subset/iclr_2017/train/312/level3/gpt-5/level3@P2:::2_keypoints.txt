- Paper frames neural architecture search as a sequential decision process with a controller RNN that emits variable-length architecture descriptions trained as “child” models using policy-gradient RL.
- Controller is an auto-regressive two-layer LSTM trained with policy gradient and a variance-reducing baseline; search space includes layer hyperparameters, skip connections, and recurrent-cell structure.
- Method scales via large-scale parallel and asynchronous training to handle the computational cost of evaluating many child models.
- Empirical validation on CIFAR-10 (conv architectures) and Penn Treebank (RNN cell discovery) achieves state-of-the-art or near-SOTA results.
- Discovered CIFAR-10 model attains 3.65% error, competitive with human-engineered architectures and favorable in speed.
- Discovered recurrent cell outperforms LSTM baselines on PTB (e.g., 62.4 perplexity), transfers to character-level modeling (1.214 bpc), and improves GNMT BLEU slightly without tuning.
- Paper is clearly written, well-motivated, and includes thoughtful analyses of structural patterns in discovered models (e.g., rectangular filters, one-step skips, LSTM-like RNN motifs).
- Decision: Accept.
- Primary reason 1: Novel and impactful formulation of architecture search as RL over variable-length action sequences with strong empirical results across diverse domains.
- Primary reason 2: Clear methodology and thorough experimental validation, including comparisons to strong human baselines and ablations.
- Supporting: Architecture design is a high-impact problem and automating it is of strong community interest.
- Supporting: Method is well-positioned relative to hyperparameter optimization, neuroevolution, program induction, and meta-learning; controller RNN + RL is an appropriate choice.
- Supporting: Technical choices (policy gradient with baseline, reward shaping, depth curriculum, skip-recurrent extensions) are reasonable and well-specified.
- Supporting: Empirical strength includes SOTA/competitive results, transfer experiments, and evidence that the controller learns a nontrivial policy versus random search.
- Supporting: Paper is readable with comprehensive method details, ablations, visualizations, and related-work coverage.
- Feedback: Report compute budget details—wall-clock time, number of child models, GPU/CPU hours, and async speedups; include compute vs accuracy plots.
- Feedback: Clarify whether a learned critic (actor-critic) was tried versus REINFORCE with moving-average baseline and discuss trade-offs and effects.
- Feedback: Ensure fairness of comparisons by matching parameter counts and training budgets with human baselines and isolating contributions of auxiliary components.
- Feedback: Report stability and reproducibility—variance across controller seeds, distribution of top-k models, and search-space priors; release code/configs if possible.
- Feedback: Analyze reward shaping and early-stopping effects (e.g., cubing accuracy, fixed-epoch training) and sensitivity to reward scalings.
- Feedback: Describe safeguards against overfitting to the validation set and any observed signs of overfitting during search.
- Questions: How many child models were trained end-to-end, total wall-clock and GPU/CPU hours, and how does performance scale with reduced budgets (10/25/50%)?
- Questions: Do early-epoch validation accuracies predict final performance reliably, and can proxy training accelerate search without degrading final quality?
- Questions: Is the baseline an exponential moving average or a learned critic, and how did any critic affect variance/sample efficiency?
- Questions: How sensitive are results to depth-growth schedule, allowed operations, and skip parametrization—what elements were most critical?
- Questions: Was the controller seeded with human templates or warm starts, and how would such priors affect convergence?
- Questions: How was random search matched for budget and sampling distribution; were evolutionary strategies or Bayesian optimization tried?
- Questions: Have searches been attempted on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) or other modalities, and how well do CIFAR-derived architectures transfer?
- Questions: For GNMT integration, what were the compute-matching details, effects of further tuning, and consistency of the discovered cell’s benefits across layers/tasks?
