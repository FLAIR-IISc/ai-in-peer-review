Proposes NAS: a controller RNN trained with REINFORCE to generate architectures that maximize expected validation performance.

Flexible search spaces: supports variable-length architectures, skip connections, pooling, and compositional trees for RNN cells.

CIFAR-10 result: discovered CNNs achieve 3.65% test error and are reported faster than a comparable DenseNet.

PTB word-level result: discovered recurrent cell achieves 62.4 perplexity, outperforming LSTM and prior cells.

Transfer results: discovered cell improves PTB character-level perplexity (1.214) and yields +0.5 BLEU in GNMT under matched compute.

Practical techniques: introduces skip-connection prediction via set-selection attention, distributed asynchronous child training, and reward shaping.

Control experiments: evaluates larger search spaces and compares to random search, showing the controller learns a meaningful distribution.

Decision: Accept — justified by strong empirical gains across vision and NLP and a practical, well-motivated approach.

Problem significance: automating architecture design is impactful and can reduce reliance on expert intuition despite current compute intensity.

Positioning and motivation: clearly situated relative to Bayesian optimization, random search, and neuroevolution; framed naturally as policy optimization.

Method correctness and clarity: REINFORCE, variance-reduction baseline, and distributed training are appropriate; search spaces and skip-connection mechanisms are explicit.

Empirical rigor: results are competitive or SOTA, transfer experiments strengthen claims, and ablations/random-search comparisons add credibility.

Caveats: reproducibility relies on many training details; clearer hyperparameter disclosure and compute accounting are needed.

Reproducibility request: provide exact hyperparameters for PTB (word/char), CIFAR-10, and GNMT (dropouts, optimizers, LR schedules, clipping, initialization, batch/BPTT sizes).

Compute and fairness request: report search cost in GPU/CPU hours, number of child models trained, parameter counts, FLOPs, and measured throughput to justify speed claims.

Sensitivity and variance request: provide variance across multiple NAS runs and sensitivity analyses for reward shaping, baseline choice, and depth schedules.

Search-space/prior request: discuss impact of hand-designed elements (base size/depth, op sets) and include ablations.

Validation-usage concern: address selection bias from optimizing validation directly (use held-out test or discuss overfitting mitigation).

Questions for authors: request many specific details (exact hyperparameters for PTB/CIFAR/char/GNMT, controller hyperparams and baseline decay, search cost/convergence curves, and skip-connection compilation-failure rates).

Overall assessment: well-written, impactful paper that would benefit from fuller hyperparameter disclosure and clearer compute accounting to improve reproducibility.
