- Proposes NAS: an RNN controller trained with REINFORCE to generate variable-length neural architectures for convnets and RNN cells.
- Key techniques: autoregressive controller, EMA baseline, distributed asynchronous child-model evaluation, set-attention for skip connections, tree-structured RNN cell search.
- Empirical wins: CIFAR-10 convnets (3.65% error) competitive with top human designs and reported slightly faster than a DenseNet variant.
- Empirical wins: discovered RNN cell outperforms LSTM on PTB (test PPL 62.4), transfers to PTB-char and yields modest BLEU gain in GNMT.
- Includes analyses: skip-connection ablations, random-search control showing learning improves over naive search, and robustness tests with expanded operation set.
- Decision: weak accept based on novelty, strong empirical results across vision and language, and high potential impact.
- Main contribution: automating architecture design via a learned policy to reduce human effort and find non-obvious designs.
- Positioning: well-motivated and connected to relevant literature (hyperopt, neuroevolution, seq2seq, RL); RNN controller and attention for skips are sensible design choices.
- Results generally support claims with standard training setups and controls, isolating controller effects during search.
- Major caveat: extremely high compute cost (hundreds of replicas, thousands of architectures, up to 800 GPUs), raising accessibility and sample-efficiency concerns.
- Generality claim limited by narrow dataset breadth (CIFAR-10, PTB, PTB-char, one GNMT config); more datasets would strengthen claims.
- Some baseline comparisons (e.g., DenseNet) may be affected by differing parameterizations; speed comparisons need standardized FLOPs and hardware details.
- Request to report compute details: total GPU/CPU hours, number of architectures trained, wall-clock times to assess feasibility.
- Suggest exploring sample-efficiency improvements: early stopping, low-fidelity proxies, parameter sharing, smarter reward normalization/entropy regularization.
- Ask for standardized metrics (FLOPs, params, wall-clock) and expanded image/language benchmarks for fairer comparisons.
- Recommend clarifying search-space inductive biases, operation constraints, and ablations on reward shaping and controller hyperparameters.
- Reproducibility: provide hyperparameter ranges, data augmentation details, random seed variance, and a minimal low-compute controller+search prototype.
- Questions for authors: exact compute budget and number of architectures to reach reported results, and whether low-fidelity proxies or early stopping were used.
- Questions on reward shaping: rationale for cubing accuracy/squaring perplexity, sensitivity to baseline/entropy choices, and whether mode collapse occurred.
- Questions on transfer/generalization: how CIFAR-10 architectures transfer to CIFAR-100/SVHN and whether the PTB cell generalizes to other language corpora.
- Questions on search-space influence: whether skip-connection compatibility rules bias discovered patterns and whether alternative rules were tested.
- Questions on baselines: whether comparisons were made to Bayesian optimization or evolutionary methods under equivalent budgets, and expected relative performance.
