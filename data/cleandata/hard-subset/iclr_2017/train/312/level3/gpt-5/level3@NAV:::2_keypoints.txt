- Casts neural architecture search as a sequential decision process using actor-critic RL where node/connection choices are actions and validation error is the reward.  
- Uses a two-layer auto-regressive LSTM as both controller (actor) and baseline estimator (critic) to search variable-length architectures.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10 (CNNs) and Penn Treebank (RNN cells), finding architectures that match or beat strong human-designed baselines.  
- Decision: Accept — novel, well-motivated formulation and strong empirical support across two distinct tasks.  
- Main motivation: efficiently explore a vast, variable-length architecture space to reduce manual engineering.  
- Positioned clearly against Bayesian optimization, neuro-evolution, and meta-learning, leveraging sequence modeling and RL for variable-length and skip-connection structures.  
- Results largely support claims: top-tier CIFAR-10 performance, novel RNN cell improves PTB perplexity and transfers to other tasks; includes ablations and qualitative analyses.  
- Request to report end-to-end search cost: wall-clock time, total compute (GPU/CPU-days), controller sample efficiency, and a compute vs quality Pareto scatter.  
- Clarify whether the critic is a learned actor-critic or an EMA baseline and ablate variance-reduction choices and their effects.  
- Clarify human-bootstrapping: whether the controller was warm-started with human designs and provide results with/without bootstrapping.  
- Ensure fair comparisons: match training budgets and parameter counts, include parameter/FLOP-normalized comparisons and training-time benchmarks.  
- Provide deeper analysis of discovered topologies (filter shapes, skip density) and their correlation with performance.  
- Improve reproducibility by releasing exact search space specs, controller hyperparameters, and evaluation scripts.
