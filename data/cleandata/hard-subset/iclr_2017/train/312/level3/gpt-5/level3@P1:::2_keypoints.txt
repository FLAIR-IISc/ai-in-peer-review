- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an auto-regressive controller RNN emits variable-length “programs” specifying network hyperparameters and connectivity, trained with REINFORCE using validation performance as reward and an EMA baseline.
- Scales via large-scale parallel asynchronous child training on a parameter-server architecture; search space covers convolutional models (pooling/striding) and recurrent cells as compositional trees.
- Strong empirical results: CIFAR-10 39-layer model achieves 3.65% error and is reported 1.05× faster than a DenseNet variant; PTB RNN cell attains 62.4 perplexity and transfers to char-PTB (1.214) and improves GNMT BLEU by 0.5.
- Includes control experiments (random search vs. policy gradient, expanded search space), qualitative motif analysis, and ablations (e.g., impact of skip connections).
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason for acceptance: novel, well-motivated formulation of architecture design as learnable sequence generation with strong cross-modality empirical improvements.
- Problem significance: automates time-consuming human architecture design by enabling exploration of a vast combinatorial search space via variable-length string representation.
- Methodological soundness: REINFORCE with EMA baseline and tailored reward shaping; skip-connection prediction via set-selection attention; search spaces and memory connections for RNNs are clearly specified.
- Noted limitation: method is not actor-critic (no learned value network), but remains principled and internally consistent.
- Empirical rigor: uses standard preprocessing/regularization and strong baselines, demonstrates transferability, and shows policy gradient outperforms random search on best and top-k metrics.
- Sensitivity analysis: reports effects of architectural perturbations and skip connections to validate search findings.
- Clarity and reproducibility: search spaces, training schedules, distributed setup, and evaluation protocols are well-documented; authors commit to code release and provide the discovered RNN cell in TensorFlow.
- Requested improvement: increase compute-budget transparency (wall-clock, total GPU/CPU hours, per-child time, compute vs. quality plots).
- Requested improvement: provide controller learning curves and correlations between child training time/size and validation accuracy to assess sample efficiency and learned preferences.
- Requested improvement: clarify role of human priors in defining the search space and experiments seeding the controller with human designs vs. from-scratch runs.
- Requested improvement: clarify fairness of speed/comparison claims (measurement protocol for 1.05× DenseNet speedup and impact of the “+40 filters” post-search tweak).
- Requested improvement: report robustness and variance across multiple controller seeds and re-trainings of discovered architectures (error bars, top-k dispersion).
- Requested improvement: discuss potential meta-overfitting from directly optimizing validation metrics and safeguards used (meta-validation, early stopping, controller regularization).
- Specific questions for authors include: total GPU/CPU hours and time to reach near-best models; correlation of validation accuracy with child training time/size; whether learned baselines/actor-critic were tried; sensitivity to reward shaping; impact of search-space constraints and seeding; exact test error without +40 filters and throughput measurement details; variance of PTB cell across runs; and transfer results to CIFAR-100/SVHN/WikiText/Billion Word.
- Overall judgment: excellent, impactful paper that advances automated architecture design and merits acceptance.
