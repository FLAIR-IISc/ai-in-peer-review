- Trains a controller RNN with RL to generate architectures for CNNs and RNN cells, using skip-connection-aware search spaces and distributed asynchronous training; resulting architectures resemble human designs and rival/surpass baselines.  
- Decision: Weak Accept — novel, well-motivated RNN+RL approach with strong vision and language results despite high compute cost.  
- Problem/motivation: Clear—architecture design is labor-intensive and prior methods struggle with variable-length structures and scalability.  
- Claims/evidence: CIFAR-10 3.65% (with speed gains), PTB 62.4 perplexity, character PTB SOTA, modest GNMT improvements; includes random-search baselines and ablations.  
- Limitations: Very large computational footprint and only partial demonstration of generality (limited datasets and sequence-task diversity).  
- Fairness/sensitivity concerns: Some comparisons could be unfair and results may depend on reward shaping (e.g., cubed accuracy).  
- Eval feedback: Broaden benchmarks to CIFAR-100, ImageNet (proxy), larger language datasets (WikiText-103), and more sequential tasks.  
- Efficiency reporting: Provide total FLOPs/GPU-days, controller reward learning curves, variance across runs, and sample efficiency vs. random search.  
- Robustness analyses: Study sensitivity to reward shaping, controller size, baseline choice, search-space definitions, and different compute budgets.  
- Reproducibility: Release search spaces, controller hyperparameters, and search code (not just final architectures).  
- Question: What is the total compute budget (GPU/CPU days) per dataset and how does performance scale with budget?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to the reward transformation (cubing accuracy) and the baseline estimator?  
- Question: What is the variance across multiple NAS runs with different random seeds?  
- Question: How do results change under tighter resource constraints (e.g., 10–50× fewer child models)?  
- Question: Can the discovered PTB cell transfer to other sequence tasks (e.g., WikiText, speech) without retuning, and how does it perform under matched training budgets?
