- Problem: Automate neural architecture design by representing architectures as variable-length sequences and learning a policy to generate them.
- Method: Auto-regressive two-layer LSTM controller samples tokens; child validation performance is reward; trained with REINFORCE + EMA baseline, asynchronous updates, and reward shaping.
- Search spaces: Controller predicts CNNs (filters, widths, depths, strides, skip connections via set-selection attention, pooling) and RNN cell trees with explicit ct/ct−1 injection.
- CIFAR-10 result: Learned CNN achieves 3.65% test error and is reported as ~1.05x faster than a strong human-designed baseline.
- PTB result: Learned recurrent cell outperforms LSTM and other SOTA cells (test perplexity 62.4), transfers to char modeling and yields gains in GNMT.
- Analysis: Discovered novel motifs (rectangular filters, larger top-layer filters, many short skip connections); related work and experimental setup are well covered.
- Decision: Accept — novel and impactful formulation with strong empirical gains on diverse tasks.
- Core reason: Variable-length sequence formulation + RL enables complex wiring (including skips) and yields compelling architectures.
- Motivation and placement: Well-motivated versus fixed-length search and neuroevolution; connects to program synthesis and RL for task-level metrics.
- Empirical support: Controller explored ~12.8K architectures; shows competitive CIFAR-10 models and convincing PTB improvements with robustness checks.
- Scientific rigor: Controller/child training details, skip-connection safeguards, and asynchronous setup are explicitly described.
- Novelty: Significant step beyond fixed-template tuning, producing qualitatively different and insightful architectural motifs.
- Compute request: Report wall-clock time, total GPU/CPU hours, and number of child models needed to reach reported results.
- Sample-efficiency request: Provide correlation plots of compute (samples/time) vs final model quality and learning curves (best/median reward vs time).
- Clarify speedup claim: Specify whether “1.05x faster” is training or inference, hardware, batch sizes, and model sizes compared.
- Actor-critic question: Did you compare REINFORCE+EMA to a learned critic (actor-critic) for variance reduction and sample efficiency?
- Human-bootstrapping question: Did you try seeding the controller with human architectures or warm-starting around them, and what were the effects?
- Proxy fidelity request: Provide scatter plots and correlation coefficients between proxy (short-training) rewards and full-training final accuracy/perplexity.
- Reproducibility request: Report parameter counts, FLOPs, throughput, training recipes for baselines, and variance across controller seeds (best/median/top-k).
- Search-space sensitivity: Describe search-space priors in more detail and provide aggregate stats (histograms) of chosen filter shapes, skip lengths, and widths for top architectures.
- Computational-reduction suggestions: Explore learned critics, performance predictors, partial weight sharing, early stopping, or supernet-style sharing to reduce compute.
- Stability analysis: Analyze asynchronous update stability and staleness effects and whether synchronous alternatives change outcomes.
- Overall assessment: An excellent, influential paper demonstrating RL-driven NAS with clear exposition and strong results; acceptance recommended pending additional reporting.
