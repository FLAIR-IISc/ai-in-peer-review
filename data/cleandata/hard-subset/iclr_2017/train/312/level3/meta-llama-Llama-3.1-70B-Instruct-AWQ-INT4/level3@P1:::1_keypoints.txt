- Presents Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate neural architectures, evaluated on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank with state-of-the-art results and demonstrated transferability.
- Recommendation: Accept — justified by strong empirical performance and a novel, impactful approach to automating architecture design.
- Strong empirical evidence: SOTA on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, showing effectiveness across vision and text tasks.
- Method is versatile: explores beyond standard RNN/CNN search spaces and transfers to other tasks like character language modeling.
- Paper is well-written and includes thorough analysis, ablation studies, and comparisons to prior methods.
- Reproducibility concern: paper should provide more details on hyperparameters and types of dropout used.
- Suggestion to release a base set of hyperparameters (e.g., as in Zaremba et al. 2014) to aid replication.
- Request hyperparameter details specifically for the character language model experiments.
- Questions for authors: clarify hyperparameter/dropout specifics, plans to reduce the high computational cost, and insights on how learned architectures differ from manual designs.
