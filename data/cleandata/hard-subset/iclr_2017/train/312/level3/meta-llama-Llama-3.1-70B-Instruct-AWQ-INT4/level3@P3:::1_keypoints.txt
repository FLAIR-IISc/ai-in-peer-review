- Proposes Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate neural network architectures.  
- Evaluated on vision and text benchmarks, enabling dataset-specific architectures without manual engineering.  
- Demonstrates novel convolutional architectures that rival top human-designed CIFAR-10 models.  
- Demonstrates novel recurrent cells that outperform LSTM on the Penn Treebank dataset.  
- Method explores beyond standard RNN/CNN search spaces and shows strong empirical gains.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly placed in related work, and thoroughly evaluated.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on strong results and importance of the problem.  
- Major reproducibility concern: missing exact hyperparameter details (including types of dropout and char LM settings).  
- Suggestion to release a base set of hyperparameters (akin to Zaremba et al. 2014) to aid future work.  
- Concern about the high computational cost of the proposed method and requests plans to reduce it.  
- Requests more details on transfer learning experiments: specific tasks/datasets and comparative performance.
