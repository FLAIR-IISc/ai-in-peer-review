**Summary of the Paper's Contributions**

This paper presents Neural Architecture Search (NAS), a novel approach to automating the design of neural network architectures. The authors propose using a recurrent neural network as a controller to generate model descriptions of neural networks, which are then trained using reinforcement learning to maximize the expected accuracy of the generated architectures on a validation set. The method is evaluated on two challenging benchmarks, CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, and achieves state-of-the-art results on both tasks. The authors also demonstrate the transferability of the learned architectures to other tasks, such as character language modeling.
