**Summary of the Paper**

The paper presents a novel approach to automate the design of neural network architectures, called Neural Architecture Search (NAS). The authors propose using a recurrent neural network (RNN) as a controller to generate model descriptions of neural networks, which are then trained using reinforcement learning to maximize the expected accuracy of the generated architectures. The method is evaluated on two challenging benchmarks: CIFAR-10 for image classification and Penn Treebank for language modeling. The results show that NAS can design novel architectures that rival the best human-invented architectures, achieving state-of-the-art performance on both tasks.
