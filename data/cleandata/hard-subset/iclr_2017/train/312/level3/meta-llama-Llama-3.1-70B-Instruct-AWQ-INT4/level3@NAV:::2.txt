**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

This paper proposes a novel approach to neural architecture search using actor-critic reinforcement learning (RL). The authors treat deep neural networks (DNNs) as variable-length sequences and employ an auto-regressive two-layer LSTM as both the controller and critic. The controller generates architectural hyperparameters, and the critic evaluates the generated architectures using validation accuracy as the reward signal. The approach is evaluated on two tasks: CIFAR-10 image classification and Penn Treebank language modeling. The results show that the proposed method can design novel architectures that rival or outperform human-designed architectures.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

Based on the evaluation, I decide to **accept** this paper. The two key reasons for this decision are:

1. **Novel and exciting approach**: The paper proposes a new and innovative approach to neural architecture search, which is well-motivated and well-placed in the literature. The use of actor-critic RL to search for variable-length architectures is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Strong empirical performance**: The paper demonstrates strong empirical performance on two challenging benchmarks, CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank. The results show that the proposed method can design novel architectures that rival or outperform human-designed architectures.
