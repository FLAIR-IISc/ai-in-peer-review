This paper proposes a novel approach to neural architecture design by leveraging a recurrent neural network (RNN) to generate network architectures and training the RNN with reinforcement learning for task-specific design. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach, dubbed Neural Architecture Search, on two challenging benchmarks: CIFAR-10 for image classification and Penn Treebank for language modeling.

I decide to accept this paper, with two key reasons for this choice. Firstly, the proposed RNN+RL approach to architecture generation is interesting and promising, showcasing a new direction for automatically designing neural network architectures. Secondly, the experiments demonstrate solid results, with the generated architectures rivaling human-designed ones in terms of performance.

The paper's approach is well-motivated, building upon the observation that the structure and connectivity of a neural network can be specified by a variable-length string, making it amenable to generation by an RNN. The use of reinforcement learning to train the RNN is also well-justified, as it allows the network to learn to improve its search over time. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their method, making it easy to follow and understand.

The experiments are thorough and well-executed, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach on two challenging benchmarks. The results show that the generated architectures can achieve state-of-the-art performance, often rivaling or surpassing human-designed models. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the generated architectures, highlighting their similarities and differences with human-designed ones.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. One major concern is the long training time required for the RNN, even with substantial computing resources. This may limit the practicality of the approach for large-scale problems. Additionally, the experiments do not demonstrate the generality of the approach, and it is unclear how well the method would perform on other similar or different datasets, especially for sequential models.

To improve the paper, I would suggest the following:

* Provide more analysis on the computational cost of the approach and potential ways to reduce the training time.
* Conduct more experiments to demonstrate the generality of the approach, including evaluations on other datasets and sequential models.
* Consider providing more details on the hyperparameter tuning process for the RNN and the reinforcement learning algorithm.
* Discuss potential applications of the approach beyond the two benchmarks presented in the paper.

Some questions I would like the authors to answer:

* How do the authors plan to address the issue of long training times for the RNN?
* Can the authors provide more insights into the design decisions behind the RNN architecture and the reinforcement learning algorithm?
* How do the authors think the approach could be extended to other types of neural networks, such as recurrent neural networks or transformers?
* Are there any plans to release the code for the proposed approach, and if so, what is the timeline for release?
