- Proposes a novel neural architecture search method using an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate architectures.  
- Controller is optimized to maximize expected validation accuracy of generated networks.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, achieving state-of-the-art results.  
- Recommends accepting the paper based on strong empirical performance and well-motivated approach.  
- Method shows flexibility across vision and text benchmarks and enables dataset-specific architectures without manual engineering.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, detailed explanation of the approach.  
- Provides thorough analysis including ablation studies and comparisons to other methods.  
- Major reproducibility concern: insufficient details on hyperparameters and types of dropout used in experiments.  
- Suggests releasing a base set of hyperparameters (e.g., like Zaremba et al. 2014) to aid replication.  
- Requests hyperparameter details for other experiments such as the character language model.  
- Recommends providing more details on the computational cost, which is currently quite high.  
- Notes the parameter server approach to speed up training is interesting and requests more implementation details.  
- Suggests adding more comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods beyond random search.  
- Specific questions for authors: details on hyperparameters/dropout, plans to address computational cost, details on parameter server implementation, and potential practical applications.
