- Proposal: RNN generates neural network architectures and is trained with reinforcement learning (Neural Architecture Search) evaluated on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.  
- Recommendation: Accept—approach is interesting and promising, opening a new direction for automatic architecture design.  
- Empirical reason for acceptance: Generated architectures achieve solid, often state-of-the-art, results rivaling human-designed models.  
- Motivation: Well-motivated by representing network structure as variable-length strings suitable for RNN generation.  
- Justification: Use of reinforcement learning is appropriate to improve the architecture search over time.  
- Clarity: Method is clearly and concisely explained and easy to follow.  
- Experimental quality: Experiments are thorough, well-executed, and include detailed analysis of generated architectures.  
- Limitation: Training the RNN requires long runtimes and substantial compute, limiting practicality.  
- Limitation: Generality is not demonstrated—unclear performance on other datasets or on sequential models.  
- Improvement suggested: Provide analysis of computational cost and strategies to reduce training time.  
- Improvement suggested: Conduct additional experiments on more datasets and sequential models to show generality.  
- Improvement suggested: Give more details on hyperparameter tuning for the RNN and RL algorithm.  
- Improvement suggested: Discuss broader applications beyond the two presented benchmarks.  
- Question: How will the authors address the long RNN training times?  
- Question: Can authors justify design decisions for the RNN architecture and RL algorithm?  
- Question: How could the approach be extended to other network types (e.g., RNNs, transformers)?  
- Question: Are there plans to release the code, and what is the timeline?
