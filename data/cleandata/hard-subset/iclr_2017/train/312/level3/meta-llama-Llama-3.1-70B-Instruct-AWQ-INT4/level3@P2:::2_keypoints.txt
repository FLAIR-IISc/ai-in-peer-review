- Proposes a novel RL-based neural architecture search treating DNNs as variable-length sequences.
- Uses an auto-regressive two-layer LSTM as both controller and critic, with node selection as the action and architecture evaluation error as the reward.
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank and compared against multiple human-designed architectures.
- Reviewer recommendation: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Well-motivated approach addressing challenges of hand-designing architectures.
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical performance on the two benchmarks, outperforming or comparing favorably to human designs.
- Paper provides a clear, detailed description and thorough analysis, noting differences between generated and human architectures.
- Related work coverage and empirical comparisons are handled comprehensively.
- Request for additional information on training time and the compute/time vs. model quality correlation.
- Suggestion to evaluate using human-bootstrapped models as initialization for the method.
- Request for ablation studies to quantify each component’s contribution.
- Question about hyperparameter tuning details.
- Question about extending the approach to more complex tasks and larger datasets.
- Question whether other RL algorithms (e.g., Q-learning, DDPG) were explored.
