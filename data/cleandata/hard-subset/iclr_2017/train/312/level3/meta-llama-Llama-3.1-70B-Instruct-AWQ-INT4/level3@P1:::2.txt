The authors of this paper propose a novel approach to neural architecture search using actor-critic reinforcement learning. By treating deep neural networks (DNNs) as variable-length sequences, they frame node selection as a reinforcement learning action and use the architecture evaluation error as a reward signal. The method employs an auto-regressive two-layer LSTM as both the controller and critic. The authors evaluate their approach on two tasks, CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, and compare it against multiple human-designed architectures.

I decide to accept this paper with the following two key reasons for this choice:

1. The proposed approach is novel and exciting, addressing the challenges of hand-designing architectures in deep learning. The use of reinforcement learning to search for optimal architectures is a significant contribution to the field.
2. The authors provide a detailed and clear description of their method, including necessary improvements and evaluations on challenging benchmarks. The paper is well-written, and the experiments are thorough and convincing.

Supporting arguments for the reasons for the decision:

* The paper presents a significant contribution to the field of deep learning by introducing a new approach to neural architecture search. The use of reinforcement learning to search for optimal architectures is a novel idea that has the potential to improve the performance of deep neural networks.
* The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach on two challenging benchmarks, CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of their method in finding novel architectures that outperform human-designed architectures.
* The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear description of their method, including the necessary improvements and evaluations. The related work coverage and comparisons are handled well, and the authors provide a detailed discussion of the results and their implications.

Additional feedback with the aim to improve the paper:
