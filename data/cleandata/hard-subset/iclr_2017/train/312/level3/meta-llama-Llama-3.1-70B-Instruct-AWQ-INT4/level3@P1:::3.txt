**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach to neural network architecture design using a recurrent neural network (RNN) as a controller to generate architectures. The controller is trained using reinforcement learning to maximize the expected accuracy of the generated architectures on a validation set. The approach is evaluated on two datasets, CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, and achieves promising results, rivaling state-of-the-art human-designed architectures.

**Decision**

I recommend accepting this paper, with the following key reasons:

1. The proposed approach is novel and interesting, and the experiments demonstrate its potential in generating competitive architectures.
2. The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the approach on two datasets, showing its ability to generalize across different tasks.

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper's main contribution is the use of an RNN as a controller to generate neural network architectures, which is a promising approach to automating architecture design. The experiments on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach, with the generated architectures achieving competitive results with state-of-the-art human-designed architectures.

The paper also provides a thorough evaluation of the approach, including an analysis of the generated architectures and their similarities with human-designed ones. This analysis suggests that the generated architectures are compatible with human expertise and can be used as a starting point for further optimization.

However, the paper also has some limitations, which are discussed below.

**Additional Feedback**

To further improve the paper, I would like to see the following:

1. More experiments on other datasets, especially for sequential models, to demonstrate the generality of the approach.
2. A more detailed analysis of the generated architectures and their properties, such as their depth, width, and connectivity.
3. A comparison with other automated architecture design methods, such as Bayesian optimization and evolutionary algorithms.
4. An evaluation of the computational cost of the approach, including the training time and the number of parameters required.

**Questions for the Authors**

To clarify some points in the paper, I would like to ask the authors the following questions:

1. How do you plan to extend the approach to more complex tasks, such as image segmentation and object detection?
2. Can you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process for the controller RNN and the child networks?
3. How do you handle the case where the generated architecture is not compatible with the task requirements, such as the input/output dimensions?
4. Can you provide more insights into the properties of the generated architectures, such as their robustness to adversarial attacks and their interpretability?
