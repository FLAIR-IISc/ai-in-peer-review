- Proposes neural architecture search using reinforcement learning with an RNN controller that generates variable-length architectures evaluated on a validation set.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness on CIFAR-10 (3.65% test error) and Penn Treebank (62.4 perplexity).  
- Decision: accept.  
- Tackles a well-defined problem in neural architecture design.  
- Approach is well-motivated and grounded in existing RL and NAS literature.  
- Strong empirical evidence supporting the method’s effectiveness.  
- Clear description of the method (RNN controller + RL) and thorough analysis.  
- Comparisons show generated architectures rival top human-designed and state-of-the-art models.  
- Large-scale experimental setup with distributed training and asynchronous updates (800 networks on 800 GPUs).  
- Strength: ability to generate novel, competitive architectures automatically.  
- Suggestion: provide more details on training time and computational resources required.  
- Suggestion: analyze the role of human-bootstrapped models in the search process.  
- Suggestion: give more details on hyperparameter tuning procedures.  
- Questions for authors: specifics on training time/resources, potential extensions to other tasks/domains, and deeper analysis of human-bootstrapped models’ role.  
- Overall: excellent, significant contribution with strong potential to automate architecture design.
