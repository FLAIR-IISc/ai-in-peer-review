This paper proposes a novel approach to neural architecture search by utilizing a recurrent neural network (RNN) to generate network architectures and training the RNN with reinforcement learning to maximize the expected accuracy of the generated architectures on a validation set. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on two benchmark datasets, CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, achieving state-of-the-art results in both cases.

I decide to accept this paper with two key reasons for this choice: (1) the proposed approach is well-motivated and has strong empirical performance on challenging benchmarks, and (2) the paper presents a new research direction for automatically finding good neural network architectures.

The authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of their approach, including the use of a recurrent neural network as the controller, the training process with reinforcement learning, and the search space for both convolutional and recurrent architectures. The experimental results are impressive, with the proposed approach achieving state-of-the-art results on both CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the generated architectures, including the use of skip connections and the composition of recurrent cells.

However, I have some concerns regarding the training time of the proposed approach, which is reported to be long even with substantial computing resources. Additionally, the experiments do not demonstrate the generality of the approach, and more evaluation on other datasets, especially for sequential models, is needed.

To improve the paper, I suggest the authors provide more analysis on the training time and potential ways to reduce it. Additionally, more experiments on other datasets and sequential models would strengthen the paper and provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach.

Some questions I would like the authors to answer to clarify my understanding of the paper and provide additional evidence are:

1. How does the proposed approach compare to other neural architecture search methods, such as Bayesian optimization and evolutionary algorithms?
2. Can the authors provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process for the controller RNN and the child networks?
3. How does the proposed approach handle overfitting, especially in the case of recurrent architectures?
4. Can the authors provide more analysis on the transferability of the learned architectures to other tasks and datasets?

Overall, this is a well-written and well-motivated paper that presents a new and promising approach to neural architecture search. With some additional analysis and experiments, it has the potential to make a significant impact in the field of deep learning.

Rating: 8/10

Confidence: 8/10

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
