- Proposes a novel neural architecture search (NAS) method using an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to maximize expected validation accuracy.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, achieving state-of-the-art results.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance because the approach is well-motivated and shows strong empirical performance.
- Reviewer also values the paper for introducing a new research direction in automatic architecture discovery.
- Paper provides clear explanations of the controller RNN, RL training procedure, and search spaces for convolutional and recurrent architectures.
- Experimental results are impressive and include detailed analysis of generated architectures (e.g., skip connections, recurrent cell composition).
- Major concern: reported training time is very long even with substantial computing resources.
- Major concern: experiments do not establish generality; more datasets (especially for sequential models) are needed.
- Suggestion: include more analysis on training time and potential ways to reduce it.
- Suggestion: run additional experiments on other datasets and sequential tasks to strengthen evaluation.
- Questions for authors: compare to other NAS methods (Bayesian optimization, evolutionary algorithms).
- Questions for authors: provide details on hyperparameter tuning for the controller RNN and child networks.
- Questions for authors: explain how the approach handles overfitting, particularly for recurrent architectures.
- Questions for authors: analyze transferability of learned architectures to other tasks and datasets.
- Overall assessment: well-written, promising paper with minor revisions requested; Rating 8/10, Confidence 8/10, Recommend accept with minor revisions.
