**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to neural architecture search (NAS) using reinforcement learning (RL) and a recurrent neural network (RNN) as the controller. The controller generates neural network architectures as variable-length sequences, and the architectures are evaluated using a validation set. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on two tasks: image classification on CIFAR-10 and language modeling on Penn Treebank. The proposed method is able to design novel architectures that rival the best human-invented architectures in terms of test set accuracy.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to generate novel architectures that are competitive with the best human-invented architectures.
