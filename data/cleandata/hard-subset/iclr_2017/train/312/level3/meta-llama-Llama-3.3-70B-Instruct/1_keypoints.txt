- Proposes Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate neural network architectures.
- Applied to CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, producing architectures that rival or surpass human-designed state-of-the-art.
- Recommendation to accept: paper is well-written, tackles an important problem, and reports strong results.
- Methodology is clearly described and experiments are thorough, including ablation studies and comparisons to prior methods.
- Requests more details on controller hyperparameters (learning rate, weight initialization, dropout) to aid replication.
- Suggests adding more visualizations of generated architectures and deeper analysis of the search space and optimization process.
- Asks for clarification on computational resources required to train the controller and child networks.
- Asks how the system handles infeasible/generated architectures that cause compilation or implementation errors.
- Requests more insight into the optimization dynamics: convergence rate and the effect of the reward signal on controller performance.
- Asks whether the approach has been or could be applied to other tasks beyond image classification and language modeling.
