- Frames Neural Architecture Search (NAS) as sequential decision-making using an RNN controller trained with REINFORCE to generate child network specifications.  
- Extends the method to produce complex architectures including skip connections, mirroring modern human-designed networks.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on CIFAR-10 (convolutional architecture) and Penn Treebank (recurrent cell) tasks.  
- Discovered CIFAR-10 architecture achieves 3.65% error and is faster than a comparable DenseNet.  
- Discovered recurrent cell attains PTB perplexity 62.4, outperforming LSTM and transferring to other tasks.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear methodology, experimental setup, and results.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong endorsement by the reviewer.  
- Primary reasons: high novelty/significance (automating architecture design via RL) and strong, convincing empirical validation.  
- Strength: method handles non-differentiable architecture selection and moves beyond fixed-length hyperparameter searches.  
- Strength: uses baseline/variance reduction and a distributed training scheme to make large-scale search feasible.  
- Positioning: thorough related-work discussion and effective comparison to random search, showing the controller truly learns.  
- Concern: very high computational cost (800 GPUs); requests total GPU-hours, wall-clock time, and compute vs. performance analysis.  
- Concern: sensitivity to the human-designed search space—ask for analysis of how choices of operations/sizes affect results.  
- Concern: would like analysis of how the controller’s policy evolves during training (e.g., action probability visualizations).
