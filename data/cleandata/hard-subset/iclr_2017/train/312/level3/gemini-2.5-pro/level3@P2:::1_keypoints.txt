- Introduces "Neural Architecture Search": an RNN controller trained with policy-gradient to generate child network architectures, rewarded by child validation performance.  
- Demonstrates strong results: discovered convnet on CIFAR-10 and a novel recurrent cell on Penn Treebank that outperform many human-designed architectures.  
- Discovered recurrent cell generalizes: improves character-level language modeling and boosts a neural machine translation system.  
- Decision: Accept — method is novel, effective, and addresses an important problem in automated architecture design.  
- Importance/novelty: frames architecture design as a learnable controller problem, moving beyond fixed hyperparameter search and many neuro-evolution approaches.  
- Strong empirical validation across diverse tasks shows the method finds genuinely useful architectural motifs rather than overfitting one task.  
- Thorough evaluation includes control experiments (notably random search) showing the policy-gradient controller learns better architectures.  
- Computational cost is very high (hundreds of GPUs) but viewed as an engineering barrier likely to decrease over time.  
- Additional feedback: paper is well-written but lacks sufficient detail on child network training hyperparameters, especially for Penn Treebank.  
- Recommendation: authors should publish full hyperparameters (dropout types/rates, initialization, optimizer, learning-rate schedules) for best models and character-level experiments to aid reproducibility.  
- Questions for authors: please provide full hyperparameters for the final child models on Penn Treebank and for the character-language-model experiments (including dropout details and training schedules).
