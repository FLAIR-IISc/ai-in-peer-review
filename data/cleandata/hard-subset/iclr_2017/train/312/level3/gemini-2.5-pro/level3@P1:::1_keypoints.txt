- Introduces Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller generates network architectures, trained with REINFORCE using child validation performance as reward.  
- Controller outputs architectures as strings; child networks are trained and evaluated to provide reward signals.  
- Demonstrates method on CIFAR-10, producing a CNN with state-of-the-art 3.65% test error.  
- Demonstrates method on Penn Treebank, discovering a novel recurrent cell with test perplexity 62.4 that outperforms LSTM.  
- Learned recurrent cell transfers well to character-level modeling and improves GNMT translation performance.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: High significance and novelty—automates architecture design and enables exploration of variable-length, compositional search spaces beyond standard hyperparameter tuning.  
- Key reason 2: Excellent empirical results across two distinct, competitive benchmarks showing effectiveness and versatility.  
- Method addresses limitations of prior hyperparameter optimization and neuro-evolution approaches by using policy gradients for systematic search.  
- Empirical validation is thorough: discovers architectures that surpass hand-designed models (e.g., DenseNet) and analyzes discovered design motifs (rectangular filters, skip connections).  
- Comparison to random search on PTB demonstrates the controller is learning a meaningful search strategy, not just lucky sampling.  
- Main limitation: extremely high computational cost (e.g., training ~800 networks concurrently), acknowledged as a current constraint but justified as a proof-of-concept.  
- Primary reproducibility concern: lack of specific child-network hyperparameters (dropout rates, initialization, learning-rate schedules) for reported best models.  
- Requests for additional clarity: provide exact hyperparameters and grid-search ranges for CIFAR-10, word- and character-level PTB experiments.  
- Question 1 for authors: Please provide exact hyperparameter configurations used to train the best child models (learning rates, dropout types/rates, initializations, decay schedules, etc.).  
- Question 2 for authors: Why was the reward defined as the cube of the max validation accuracy over the last 5 epochs; were other transformations tried and how sensitive is performance to reward shaping?
