Introduces Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller trained with REINFORCE to generate architectures, framing design as a learning problem.  
Finds competitive CNN on CIFAR-10 and a recurrent cell that outperforms LSTM on Penn Treebank.  
Decision: Weak Accept — novel and promising but claims of generality are not fully supported and method is computationally costly.  
Strength: Policy-gradient controller is a principled alternative to random/grid search for architecture design.  
Strength: Discovered architectures exhibit modern patterns (e.g., skip connections), suggesting the controller learns meaningful structural priors.  
Weakness: Very high computational cost (hundreds of GPUs for days) limits reproducibility and practical adoption.  
Weakness: Experimental scope is limited (only CIFAR-10 and PTB), insufficient to claim general-purpose applicability.  
Unaddressed: No evaluation on larger-scale tasks like ImageNet or other sequence tasks (e.g., machine translation).  
Question: How sensitive are results to the initial search space (e.g., excluding batch norm or different activations)?  
Request: Provide detailed description of the random search baseline distribution to ensure a fair comparison.
