- Proposes Neural Architecture Search: framing automated network design as a reinforcement learning problem (novel approach).  
- Uses an auto-regressive RNN controller to generate architectures and trains it with actor-critic policy gradients using child validation accuracy as reward.  
- Controller learns to improve search over time, discovering architectures that rival or surpass state-of-the-art on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.  
- Strong Accept recommendation based on novelty and strong empirical results; opens a promising new research direction.  
- Paper is well-written, includes important design choices (e.g., skip connections), and clearly situates itself relative to hyperparameter optimization and neuro-evolution.  
- Experimental validation is thorough and rigorous; discovered architectures show meaningful differences from human designs and support the claims.  
- Reviewer asks for discussion of computational cost and analysis of compute budget vs. final model quality.  
- Reviewer requests clarification on the role and impact of any human-bootstrapped models in the search.
