- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): framing architecture generation as a reinforcement learning problem.  
- Uses a controller RNN (two-layer LSTM) to generate variable-length architecture descriptions, trained with REINFORCE using child network validation performance as reward.  
- Implements practical improvements: parallelized asynchronous training to handle high compute cost.  
- Introduces an attention-like mechanism to generate complex topologies including skip connections.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on CIFAR-10 (convnet) and Penn Treebank (RNN cell), with discovered architectures competitive with or surpassing human designs.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novelty and significance: presents a new, influential approach to automating architecture design and connects to meta-learning.  
- Strong empirical validation: rigorous experiments on two benchmarks showing state-of-the-art results and competitive inference speed.  
- CIFAR-10 result: discovered architecture achieves 3.65% test error for comparable models.  
- Penn Treebank result: discovered recurrent cell outperforms LSTM and transfers well to character modeling and machine translation.  
- Evidence controller learns meaningful search strategy: comparison to random search shows it’s not just brute-force.  
- Additional feedback: clarify rationale and impact of search space constraints (e.g., predicting blocks of layers, fixed bases).  
- Additional feedback: provide deeper analysis of discovered architectures and motifs, and compare novel RNN cell components to LSTM gates.  
- Questions for authors: (1) provide total training time and GPU-hours for controller convergence (wall-clock and aggregate).  
- Questions for authors: (2) show correlation between number of architectures sampled (compute budget) and final model quality (learning curve).  
- Questions for authors: (3) did you try bootstrapping the controller with priors favoring known good patterns (e.g., ResNet-like skips) and does that accelerate search?
