- Decision: Reject — interesting idea and promising results but insufficient methodological detail and justification to assess novelty or reproducibility.  
- Insufficient search-space detail: unclear precise constraints, how filters/strides/numbers combine, and rules for generating architecture strings.  
- Reward function concerns: cubing validation accuracy is unjustified and may cause instability or reward hacking; no ablation on reward designs or baseline impact.  
- Distributed training under-specified: missing total GPU count, communication overhead, and safeguards against stale gradients affecting convergence.  
- Reproducibility issues: code promise aside, many hyperparameters (controller and child networks) and training details are missing or inconsistent.  
- Limited/unequal comparisons: baselines (e.g., DenseNet) differ in parameterization; comparisons don’t control for parameters or compute.  
- Figure 1 clarity: crucial diagram lacks labeling and does not clearly show the RNN controller–architecture string relationship.  
- Skip-connection mechanism unclear: attention-based skip description needs clearer, possibly visual, explanation.  
- Cell-generation complexity: Section 3.4’s tree-structured recurrent cell generation is confusing and needs systematic clarification beyond the single example.  
- Missing ablation studies: no evaluation of contributions from skip connections, pooling, activations, or other NAS components.  
- No error analysis: lacks inspection of failure modes or common patterns in generated architectures.  
- Key questions for authors: provide detailed search-space configurations; justify and test reward choices; detail distributed setup and anti-staleness measures; enumerate reproducibility steps; report sensitivity to hyperparameters.
