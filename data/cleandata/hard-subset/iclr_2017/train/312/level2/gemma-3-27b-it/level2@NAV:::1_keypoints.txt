- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate network architectures.  
- Core idea of treating architecture design as a sequential prediction problem is compelling and well implemented.  
- Achieves strong empirical results on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, with PTB results notably outperforming LSTM cells.  
- Recommendation: accept, based on significant novelty and strong empirical validation.  
- Demonstrates ability to automate neural architecture engineering, combining RNNs and RL as a key contribution.  
- Provides thorough experimental details, clear search-space and training descriptions, and ablation studies (e.g., skip connections).  
- Acknowledges high computational cost, important for transparency.  
- Includes discovered cell in TensorFlow, aiding reproducibility and accessibility.  
- Needs more analysis of why the generated architectures perform well and what characteristics drive success.  
- Should discuss scalability to larger datasets and more complex tasks given high compute requirements.  
- Request to explore sensitivity to different reward functions and controller hyperparameters.  
- Asks for clarification on choice and impact of the “base number” of 8 for recurrent cell generation.  
- Wants evidence of robustness of transfer learning results on character language modeling across initializations and runs.
