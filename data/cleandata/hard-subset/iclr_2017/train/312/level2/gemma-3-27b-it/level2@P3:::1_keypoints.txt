- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to design architectures.
- Core idea: formulate architecture design as a sequential decision process and optimize the controller via policy gradients.
- Demonstrates strong results on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, achieving competitive or state-of-the-art performance.
- Release of the discovered cell in TensorFlow is a notable practical contribution.
- Decision: accept with minor revisions.
- Primary strength: strong empirical performance across image classification and language modeling tasks.
- Discovered recurrent cell outperforms established LSTM architectures.
- Ablation studies and control experiments provide supporting evidence but are brief.
- Includes parallel training details, indicating attention to scalability.
- Search space definition is opaque; needs more rationale and exploration (including analysis of poor-performing architectures).
- Baseline comparisons should be expanded to include more recent or specialized models, especially on Penn Treebank.
- Practical limitations under-addressed: computational cost of training the controller and evaluating many child networks needs quantification.
- Parallelization claims should be quantified (speedup and required infrastructure).
- Connection between the controller’s learned policy and human-designed architectures is unexplored; analyze learned representations.
- Suggested improvements: expand search-space discussion, strengthen baselines, provide detailed computational-cost analysis, investigate controller policy, and clarify scaling to larger datasets like ImageNet.
