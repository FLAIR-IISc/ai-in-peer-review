- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning) to automatically design neural network architectures.
- Core idea (RNN-generated network descriptions optimized via REINFORCE) is compelling and well-motivated given manual design complexity.
- Strong empirical results: state-of-the-art or competitive on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank; discovered recurrent cell outperforms LSTMs.
- Decision: Accept — significant contribution showing a novel and effective approach to automated architecture design.
- Novelty: Using an RNN controller + RL departs substantially from grid search and Bayesian optimization.
- Empirical significance: Improvements over human-designed architectures are statistically meaningful across diverse datasets.
- Motivation and exposition: Problem setup and relation to related work (hyperparameter optimization, neuro-evolution, program synthesis) are clear.
- Practical concerns addressed: acceleration via parallelism, handling skip connections, and release of code/NAS cell in TensorFlow.
- Improvement needed: More detailed discussion of search space choices (filter sizes, counts, combination methods) and their impact.
- Improvement needed: Compare NAS to other NAS or automated architecture methods as baselines.
- Improvement needed: Provide more controller architecture details (hidden state size, embedding dimensions) to assess exploration capacity.
- Improvement needed: Justify or explore alternatives to the reward shaping choice (cubing validation accuracy).
- Improvement needed: Further evaluation of generalization of discovered architectures to other datasets.
- Question: Clarify the specific “compilation failures” from skip connections and how solutions address them.
- Question: How sensitive are results to the choice of baseline in REINFORCE; were alternatives tested?
- Question: Provide an intuitive explanation of how the controller navigates the complex recurrent-cell search space and favored structures.
- Question: Report computational costs and scalability of NAS versus manual architecture design.
- Question: Any insights or principles behind the architectures NAS discovers and why they perform well?
