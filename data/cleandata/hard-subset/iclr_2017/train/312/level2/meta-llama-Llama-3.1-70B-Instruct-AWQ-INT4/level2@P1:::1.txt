**Summary of the paper's claims and contributions**

This paper introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS), a method for automatically designing neural network architectures using a recurrent neural network (RNN) as the controller. The controller generates model descriptions, which are then trained using reinforcement learning to maximize the expected accuracy on a validation set. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of NAS on two challenging tasks: image classification on CIFAR-10 and language modeling on Penn Treebank. The results show that NAS can design novel architectures that rival the best human-invented architectures, achieving state-of-the-art performance on both tasks.

**Decision and key reasons**

I decide to **accept** this paper. The key reasons for this choice are:

1. **Novel and impactful contribution**: The paper introduces a new and innovative approach to neural architecture design, which has the potential to significantly impact the field of deep learning.
2. **Strong empirical results**: The authors provide extensive experimental results on two challenging tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of NAS in designing high-performance architectures.

**Supporting arguments for the decision**

The paper presents a well-motivated and well-placed approach in the literature, building upon existing work in hyperparameter optimization, neuro-evolution, and reinforcement learning. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the NAS method, including the use of a controller RNN to generate model descriptions and the application of reinforcement learning to optimize the controller.

The experimental results are thorough and convincing, demonstrating the ability of NAS to design novel architectures that achieve state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the designed architectures, highlighting their key features and strengths.

**Additional feedback for improvement**

While the paper is well-written and easy to follow, there are a few areas where the authors could provide additional details or clarification:

1. **Controller RNN architecture**: The authors could provide more details on the architecture of the controller RNN, including the number of layers, hidden units, and activation functions used.
2. **Reward function**: The authors could provide more insight into the design of the reward function, including the choice of the validation accuracy as the reward signal and the use of an exponential moving average as the baseline function.
3. **Hyperparameter tuning**: The authors could provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process, including the choice of hyperparameters and the methods used to tune them.
4. **Comparison to other methods**: The authors could provide a more detailed comparison of NAS to other methods for neural architecture design, including Bayesian optimization and neuro-evolution.
