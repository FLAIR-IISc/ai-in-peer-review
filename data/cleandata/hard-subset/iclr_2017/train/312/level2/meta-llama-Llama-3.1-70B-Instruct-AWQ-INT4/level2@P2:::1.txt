This paper presents a novel approach to neural architecture search (NAS) by utilizing a recurrent neural network (RNN) as a controller to generate neural network architectures. The authors propose a gradient-based method to find good architectures by maximizing the expected accuracy of the generated architectures on a validation set. The approach is well-motivated, and the authors provide a thorough discussion of related work, highlighting the limitations of existing methods and the advantages of their approach.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their method, including the use of a policy gradient method to update the controller. The experiments are well-designed, and the authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of their approach on two challenging benchmarks: CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank. The results are impressive, with the proposed method outperforming state-of-the-art models on both datasets.
