**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS), a method for automatically designing neural network architectures using a recurrent neural network (RNN) as a controller. The controller generates architectural hyperparameters, which are then used to train a child network. The reward signal for the controller is the accuracy of the child network on a validation set. The authors claim that NAS can design novel architectures that outperform state-of-the-art models on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank datasets. They also demonstrate that the method can be used to generate recurrent cell architectures that achieve state-of-the-art results on the Penn Treebank dataset.
