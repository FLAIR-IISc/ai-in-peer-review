- Proposes a novel NAS approach using an RNN controller that generates architectures and a gradient-based method to maximize expected validation accuracy.
- Uses a policy gradient method to update the controller.
- Well-motivated with a thorough related-work discussion highlighting limitations of prior NAS methods.
- Paper is clear, readable, and well-organized with detailed method explanation.
- Experiments are comprehensive and well-designed on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.
- Results outperform state-of-the-art models on both benchmarks.
- Decision: accept, primarily due to novelty and strong empirical evaluation.
- Supporting strengths: clear writing, solid motivation, and thorough experiments.
- Suggestion: report computational resources required for experiments.
- Suggestion: discuss potential applications beyond the two evaluated benchmarks.
- Suggestion: provide more insights into the generated architectures and comparisons with human designs.
- Questions for authors: details on hyperparameter tuning of the controller RNN.
- Questions for authors: how infeasible/generated architectures are handled (e.g., resource constraints).
- Questions for authors: further insights/comparisons of generated vs. human-designed architectures.
