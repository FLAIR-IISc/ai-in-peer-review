- Paper proposes using an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate neural network architectures.  
- Controller generates variable-length architecture descriptions, enabling flexible architecture search.  
- Approach is well-motivated and grounded in the existing literature.  
- Detailed method description including training procedure and reinforcement learning use.  
- Empirical results strong on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, producing architectures rivaling top human-designed nets.  
- Ablation studies and control experiments support the effectiveness of the method.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on sound motivation and strong empirical performance.  
- Suggestion: provide more details on computational resources required to train controller and child networks.  
- Suggestion: include more visualizations of generated architectures to clarify the search process.  
- Questions requested: how hyperparameters for controller and child networks were chosen.  
- Questions requested: more details on RL implementation and reward function.  
- Questions requested: plans for extending the approach to more complex tasks and larger datasets.
