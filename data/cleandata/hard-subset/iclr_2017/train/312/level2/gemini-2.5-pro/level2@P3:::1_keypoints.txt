- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RNN "controller" trained with REINFORCE to generate variable-length architecture descriptions.
- Demonstrates SOTA on CIFAR-10 (3.65% test error) with a novel convolutional architecture discovered from scratch.
- Discovers a novel recurrent cell that outperforms LSTM on Penn Treebank (test perplexity 62.4) and generalizes to other tasks.
- Decision: Accept.
- High novelty and impact: framing architecture design as an RL problem is a significant conceptual advance enabling flexible, compositional search.
- Strong empirical support: comprehensive results on competitive benchmarks validate the method’s effectiveness.
- Methodologically distinct from hyperparameter optimization and neuro-evolution; RNN controller + policy gradients enable variable-length architecture search.
- Ablation studies (e.g., removing skip connections) validate the utility of discovered architectural motifs.
- Comparison to random search shows the controller learns an effective search strategy rather than exploiting search-space bias.
- Presentation is clear with helpful figures, and the released NASCell in TensorFlow benefits the community.
- Major limitation: immense computational cost (e.g., training hundreds of networks on hundreds of GPUs).
- Suggest adding discussion of sample-efficiency improvements (weight sharing, surrogate models, early stopping).
- Paper offers limited analysis of why the discovered architectures work; deeper investigation of recurring motifs/principles is recommended.
- Recommend discussing sensitivity to search-space design choices (filter ranges, activation sets, RNN base numbers).
- Clarify the reward formulation for Penn Treebank (motivation for squaring validation perplexity).
