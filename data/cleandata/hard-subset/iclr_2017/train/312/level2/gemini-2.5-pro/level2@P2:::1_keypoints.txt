- Introduces Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller generates architecture descriptions and is trained with REINFORCE using child network validation performance as reward.  
- Demonstrated on CIFAR-10 and PTB, discovering a CNN and an RNN cell that achieve state-of-the-art results and outperform human-designed models.  
- Decision: Accept — regarded as a landmark paper introducing a powerful, general paradigm for architecture design.  
- Novelty: Frames architecture design as a learning problem solved with RL; the RNN controller handles variable-length search spaces and complex connectivity.  
- Empirical strength: CIFAR-10 model achieves 3.65% test error and the PTB cell achieves 62.4 perplexity, both improvements over prior SOTA.  
- Transferability: The discovered RNN cell transfers effectively to character-level modeling and machine translation without task-specific tuning.  
- Key technique: Incorporating skip connections via an attention-like mechanism enables discovery of complex, ResNet/DenseNet-like architectures.  
- Validation: Control experiments show policy gradient learning substantially outperforms random search, supporting the core method.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written with clear method description and thorough experimental setup.  
- Limitation: Extremely high computational cost (e.g., training hundreds of models on many GPUs) makes the method inaccessible to most researchers.  
- Suggestion: Provide deeper analysis of discovered architectures to identify recurring motifs and explain why the new RNN cell outperforms LSTM.  
- Suggestion: Include ablation studies on the components of the discovered cell to provide mechanistic insight.  
- Concern: Search space design is human-driven and may strongly constrain results; discuss sensitivity to choice of operations and activations.  
- Question: Please report total computational cost (e.g., P100-GPU-days) for CIFAR-10 and PTB searches.  
- Question: How sensitive are results to the fixed short training schedule for child models—does it bias toward quickly learning architectures?  
- Question: What motivated the reward shaping (accuracy^3, c/perplexity^2) and how does it affect controller training stability and speed versus raw metrics?
