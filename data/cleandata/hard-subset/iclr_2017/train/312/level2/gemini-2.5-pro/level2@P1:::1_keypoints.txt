- Paper introduces Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller trained with REINFORCE to generate architectures for child networks.  
- Demonstrated on CIFAR-10 (CNN) and Penn Treebank (RNN cell), achieving state-of-the-art or competitive results.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novelty/significance: reframes architecture design as a meta-learning problem using an RNN controller and policy gradients.  
- Strong empirical results: automatically discovered architectures rival or surpass human-designed models on key benchmarks.  
- Method is well-motivated and technically sound, with sensible variance-reduction and distributed training choices.  
- Framework is flexible, extended to generate skip connections (attention-like) and tree-based recurrent cells.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and compares against state-of-the-art models and random search baseline.  
- Transfer learning: discovered NASCell generalizes to character-level modeling and GNMT, indicating non-overfitting.  
- Authors are transparent about substantial computational expense, acknowledged as a drawback.  
- Suggestion: include more discussion on search-space design choices and encoded human priors.  
- Suggestion: quantify computational cost (e.g., GPU-days) and show learning curves to assess search efficiency and diminishing returns.  
- Suggestion: justify the reward transformation (validation accuracy cubed) and explain its effect on learning.  
- Suggestion: provide deeper qualitative analysis of recurring motifs in discovered architectures to inform manual design.  
- Question: was controller training stable given REINFORCE variance, and were actor-critic or other policy-gradient methods tried?  
- Question: why constrain CIFAR-10 search to predict blocks of 3 layers instead of individual layers—complexity management or other reason?  
- Question: clarify random search implementation—was it uniform sampling from the same search space/vocabulary?  
- Question: could hyperparameter tuning of NASCell within GNMT yield further translation performance gains?
