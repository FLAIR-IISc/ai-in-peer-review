- Proposes Neural Architecture Search: an RNN "controller" generates variable-length strings that specify child network architectures.  
- Controller trained with policy-gradient REINFORCE; reward is child network validation performance.  
- Claims method can design convolutional and recurrent architectures from scratch that achieve state-of-the-art results.  
- Demonstrates a convolutional architecture that surpasses human-designed models on CIFAR-10 (3.65% error).  
- Demonstrates a novel recurrent cell that outperforms LSTM on Penn Treebank (62.4 perplexity) and shows cross-task generalization.  
- Strong empirical validation, including direct comparison to a random-search baseline showing the controller learns to improve architectures.  
- Reviewer strongly recommends Accept, citing innovation in reframing architecture design as a learning problem and exceptionally strong results.  
- Core contribution is significant: provides a powerful alternative to manual, expert-driven architecture engineering.  
- Suggested improvement: explicitly acknowledge and discuss the method’s immense computational cost and potential efficiency improvements.  
- Suggested improvement: analyze sensitivity of search to hyperparameter choices (e.g., discrete filter-size set) within the search space.  
- Question: Why cube the validation accuracy for the reward signal; how sensitive are training stability and final performance to this reward shaping?  
- Question: How critical is the curriculum of gradually increasing child-network depth versus searching directly over deeper networks?
