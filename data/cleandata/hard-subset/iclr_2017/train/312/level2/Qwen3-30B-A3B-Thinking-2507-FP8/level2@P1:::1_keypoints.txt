- Introduces Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller using RL to generate architectures, achieving SOTA on CIFAR-10 and PTB without human-designed priors.  
- Decision: Accept (with minor revisions).  
- Novel, well-motivated contribution addressing automatic architecture design; situated well in related work.  
- Strong empirical results: CIFAR-10 3.65% test error and PTB 62.4 perplexity, improving prior SOTA.  
- Methodology and experiments are rigorous, with ablation studies, a random-search control, transfer experiments, and distributed training details for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Claim of “start from scratch” is unclear because the search space is constrained by specific filter sizes, filter counts, and layer types.  
- Weakness: Extremely high computational cost (800 GPUs concurrently); practicality for researchers without such resources is not addressed.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of why the discovered architectures perform well—no clear identification of contributing architectural features.  
- Weakness: Unclear whether the random search baseline was run over the identical search space as the controller.  
- Weakness: Lack of detail on the controller’s baseline (exponential moving average) initialization and its effect on convergence.  
- Clarification requested: How were the search-space constraints (filter sizes, numbers, types) chosen—based on prior knowledge or to make search tractable?  
- Clarification requested: What does “1.05x faster” refer to (inference speed, training speed, or FLOPs)?  
- Clarification requested: What learning rate was used for the initial PTB language-modeling experiments (inconsistent reporting with char LM ADAM lr)?  
- Overall: Strong, influential paper with minor clarifications needed before final acceptance.
