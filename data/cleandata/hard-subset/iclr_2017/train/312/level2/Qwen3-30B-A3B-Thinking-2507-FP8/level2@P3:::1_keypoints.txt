- Introduces NAS: an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to automatically generate variable-length neural network architectures.  
- Demonstrated on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank benchmarks.  
- CIFAR-10: discovered convolutional architecture with 3.65% test error (0.09% improvement) and 1.05x faster.  
- Penn Treebank: discovered recurrent cell with 62.4 perplexity (3.6 improvement) and strong transfer results to character LM and NMT.  
- Paper is well-positioned, noting limits of fixed-length hyperparameter search and slow neuro-evolution methods.  
- Reinforcement learning approach is well-motivated and effectively addresses architecture design.  
- Empirical results are rigorous, including thorough ablation studies and controls against random search and expanded search spaces.  
- Recommendation: should be accepted for novelty, motivation, significance, and strong empirical evidence.  
- Suggestion: report explicit computational cost metrics (e.g., GPU-hours) for the search process.  
- Suggestion: clarify what “two times faster” refers to in the Penn Treebank results (cell computation vs full-model inference).  
- Suggestion: include more detailed structural comparison between the discovered cell and existing cells beyond Figure 8.  
- Question: How many GPU-hours did the CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank searches require?  
- Question: Does the reported speed improvement refer to the cell itself or the full model?  
- Question: What specific architectural features of the discovered cell explain its gains over LSTMs beyond Figure 8?
