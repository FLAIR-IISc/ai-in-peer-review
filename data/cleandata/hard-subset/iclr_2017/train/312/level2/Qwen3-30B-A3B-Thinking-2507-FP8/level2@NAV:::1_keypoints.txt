- Paper proposes Neural Architecture Search using an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate architectures.  
- Authors claim state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 (3.65% test error) and Penn Treebank (62.4 perplexity) and transfer learning capability.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection.  
- Major flaw: fails to cite Zoph and Le (2017), a nearly identical seminal NAS work, undermining novelty and related work.  
- Contains factual errors in CIFAR-10 comparison: misstates DenseNet error (reports 3.74% vs actual 3.46%).  
- Performance/speedup claims (1.05x) lack context regarding parameter counts and computational complexity.  
- Empirical results are strong and method well-motivated, but omission of prior work weakens contribution.  
- Experimental design is sound, but writing needs greater precision in comparative statements.  
- Requested improvements: add proper citations (Zoph & Le 2017 and related), correct CIFAR-10 facts, and clarify computational complexity comparisons.  
- Additional request: provide statistical significance tests (error bars/p-values) instead of single best-run results.  
- Reviewer asks for clarification why Zoph and Le (2017) was omitted from the related work.
