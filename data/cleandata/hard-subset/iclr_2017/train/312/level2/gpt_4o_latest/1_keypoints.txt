- Introduces Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate neural network architectures, claiming SOTA on CIFAR-10 (3.65% error) and Penn Treebank (62.4 perplexity).
- Decision: Accept.
- Novelty/Impact: Automates a traditionally manual process by using RL to design architectures, a significant and underexplored idea.
- Strong empirical results: Competitive or superior performance on multiple benchmarks demonstrating practical utility.
- Experiments: Extensive evaluations and detailed comparisons show improved accuracy and some computational efficiency.
- Literature grounding: Well-situated within hyperparameter optimization, neuro-evolution, meta-learning, and AutoML research.
- Technical contributions: Mitigates policy-gradient variance using baselines and distributed training, enhancing robustness and scalability.
- Reproducibility concern: Needs more explicit hyperparameter settings and training schedules; code release is promised and helpful.
- Limitations/generalization: High computational cost (e.g., training ~800 networks in parallel) and limited evidence across diverse tasks.
- Search-space complexity: Extremely large search space (≈6×10^16 recurrent cells); needs quantification of exploration efficiency versus alternatives.
- Question — reward sensitivity: How sensitive are results to the choice of reward metric (e.g., F1 vs accuracy)?
- Question — multi-objective extension: Can the method optimize multiple objectives (accuracy vs model size/inference speed)?
- Question — computational cost comparison: How does NAS’s compute cost compare to other AutoML methods for researchers with limited hardware?
- Conclusion: Significant advancement with strong empirical support; acceptance justified despite reproducibility and computational-cost concerns.
