- Proposes NAS: an RNN controller trained with REINFORCE to generate variable-length neural architectures and maximize validation performance.  
- Supports complex topologies via learned skip connections, multiple layer types, and flexible recurrent cell computation trees.  
- Uses a large-scale asynchronous parameter-server setup to train hundreds of child models in parallel.  
- Finds a competitive CIFAR-10 convolutional architecture (3.65% test error after widening) comparable to strong human designs.  
- Discovers a novel recurrent cell achieving 62.4 perplexity on PTB, transferring well to character PTB and improving GNMT BLEU.  
- Provides ablations (e.g., skip connections) and control comparisons showing gains over random search.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: Clear, well-motivated extension of architecture search to variable-length, structured designs using RL.  
- Key reason: Strong multi-domain empirical results supporting claims and demonstrating transferability.  
- Method novelty: Controller-as-program-generator optimized with policy gradients expands beyond fixed-length hyperparameter search.  
- Technical soundness: Standard REINFORCE with moving-average baseline, set-selection sigmoids for skips, and pragmatic heuristics for graph validity.  
- Empirical rigor: Competitive benchmark results, noted post-hoc widening, and control experiments with random search and larger search spaces.  
- Clarity: Search spaces, training regimes, and reward shaping are described clearly with helpful figures.  
- Area for improvement: Quantify total compute (child models, epochs, GPU-hours/wall-clock) and provide guidance for scaling down.  
- Area for improvement: Separate raw discovered architecture performance from post-hoc widening and report Pareto curves (accuracy vs latency/params).  
- Area for improvement: Justify reward transformations (cubing/squaring) and include ablations on their effect on variance/convergence.  
- Area for improvement: Provide learning curves and sample-efficiency comparisons vs random search and variance across controller seeds.  
- Area for improvement: Expand search-space ablations to assess sensitivity (strides, pooling, skip mechanisms, base sizes).  
- Area for improvement: Improve reproducibility by releasing code, discovered architectures, controller checkpoints, and low-compute recipes.  
- Area for improvement: Consider multi-objective rewards (accuracy minus FLOPs/latency penalty) and report preliminary results or discussion.  
- Area for improvement: Report if entropy regularization or actor-critic variants were tried to reduce variance and improve stability.  
- Question: How many child models and what aggregate GPU/CPU time/wall-clock were used for CIFAR-10 and PTB?  
- Question: How sensitive is controller training to the cubic/squared reward transforms and were alternatives tried?  
- Question: For the 3.65% CIFAR result, how much gain came from widening vs the discovered topology, and could controller optimize filter counts directly?  
- Question: How were random-search baselines constrained to ensure valid comparisons, and were Bayesian or other baselines considered?  
- Question: Has the RNN cell been evaluated on larger/other sequence tasks (e.g., WikiText-103) to further validate transfer?  
- Question: Any evidence of controller overfitting to a fixed validation set and mitigation strategies used?  
- Question: Practical guidance for limited resources (e.g., 8–16 GPUs): recommended settings for epochs, LR, and search-space pruning?
