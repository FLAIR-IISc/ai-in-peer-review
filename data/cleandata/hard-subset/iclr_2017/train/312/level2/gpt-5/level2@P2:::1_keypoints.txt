- Proposal: Neural Architecture Search (controller RNN trained with REINFORCE) that generates variable-length architectures with skip connections via set-selection; demonstrated on CIFAR-10 and PTB with engineering for large-scale parallel search and control vs random search.  
- Decision: Accept — method is significant, original, and empirically competitive across vision and language tasks.  
- Empirical strength: Found convnet (3.65% CIFAR-10) and novel RNN cell (62.4 perplexity PTB) that transfer to other tasks (char PTB, GNMT).  
- Methodological soundness: Policy-gradient formulation, skip-connection mechanism, and RNN cell search space are sensible and well-specified.  
- Results credibility: Training pipelines and baselines use standard setups; reported gains appear consistent with SOTA at the time.  
- Comparative positioning: Contrasts with Bayesian optimization and neuroevolution; includes direct random-search comparison showing advantage for NAS.  
- Major concern — compute budget & reproducibility: Search uses extreme resources; authors should quantify total GPU/CPU days, wall-clock time, energy, scaling with budget, and provide guidance for smaller-scale reproduction.  
- Major concern — CIFAR-10 comparison fairness: Post-search tuning/widening (+40 filters) needs clarification vs baselines; report “as-found” vs “after-tuned/scaled”, parameter counts, FLOPs, and standardized speed measurements.  
- Major concern — validation overfitting: Selecting one best architecture after 12,800 evaluations risks overfitting; report validation/test gaps across top-k, sensitivity to validation split, and variance across controller re-runs.  
- Major concern — search-space design bias: Macro-constraints (fixed pooling, 3-layer blocks) and compilation fixes may bias outcomes; provide ablations without constraints and detail their impact on architectures and parameter growth.  
- Major concern — stronger baselines: Add budget-matched comparisons to black-box optimizers (e.g., SMAC/TPE or BO extensions) that can handle variable-length structures.  
- Minor — reward shaping: Explain cubing validation accuracy and provide ablations (raw accuracy, temperatures); report controller entropy or entropy regularization if used.  
- Minor — variance reduction: Discuss/consider actor-critic, other control variates, and Gumbel-Softmax/Concrete relaxations to reduce gradient variance for discrete choices.  
- Minor — RNN cell stability: Report training stability metrics and frequency/pruning of specific operations (e.g., elementwise mult with activations).  
- Minor — reporting & reproducibility: Move final discovered architectures, exact hyperparameters, per-layer shapes, and parameter counts into main text or detailed appendix; clean up references.  
- Questions for authors: total compute cost and performance at 10/25/50% budget; number of independent controller runs and variance across runs/top-k; rationale for fixed pooling/grouping in 39-layer setting and effect of lifting them; contribution of widening vs topology to final gains; robustness to validation split and use of cross-validation; effects of zero-padding concatenation on fan-in/parameter explosion; PTB ablations (base number, added ops); commitment to release controller code, search spaces, and logs.
