- Proposes Neural Architecture Search: an RNN “controller” trained with REINFORCE to generate variable-length neural architectures with skip connections and multiple layer types.  
- Contribution: a flexible, variable-length architecture search framework supporting skip connections and diverse layer types.  
- Contribution: large-scale asynchronous distributed training to evaluate thousands of candidate architectures.  
- Contribution: state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 (3.65% error) and PTB (62.4 perplexity) and transfer of discovered RNN cell to character PTB and GNMT with gains.  
- Contribution: control experiments comparing NAS to random search and expanded search spaces.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: clear, significant novelty in framing architecture generation as an RNN-driven, RL-optimized search over variable-length designs.  
- Key reason: strong, carefully benchmarked empirical evidence with new SOTA results and demonstrated transferability.  
- Problem/motivation: automating architecture design is timely, positioned well relative to Bayesian optimization and neuroevolution; auto-regressive controller and skip-connection selection are well motivated.  
- Evidence: competitive/superior results on CIFAR-10 and PTB, use of held-out validation rewards, post-selection grid searches, control experiments, and reproducible training details with plan to release code.  
- Usefulness: framework is broadly applicable and transfers to other tasks with minimal retuning, indicating practical value.  
- Suggested improvement: report full compute budgets (GPU/CPU-days), wall-clock search time, and energy cost; normalize comparisons by search budget.  
- Suggested improvement: provide ablations for reward shaping (accuracy^3), baseline choice, layer-depth schedule, skip-connection attention, and asynchronous scheme.  
- Suggested improvement: clarify fairness of CIFAR comparisons (dense vs added filters, 1x1 bottlenecks), and include parameter/FLOP counts and training-time parity for baselines.  
- Suggested improvement: analyze robustness—variance across seeds, stability of discovered architectures, sensitivity to validation split and risk of controller overfitting.  
- Suggested improvement: extend transfer analysis (e.g., ImageNet) or provide partial results to support scalability.  
- Questions: exact search costs (GPU/CPU-days) for CIFAR/PTB, per-child training time, and sample-efficiency vs random/Bayesian under equal budgets.  
- Questions: sensitivity to reward shaping (accuracy^3) and the moving-average baseline.  
- Questions: frequency of “compilation failures” and how padding/concatenation fixes affect accuracy.  
- Questions: variance across independent NAS runs in best-found accuracy/perplexity.  
- Questions: on CIFAR-10, were speedups measured at equal accuracy and matched FLOPs/params, and can calibrated inference/training throughput be reported?  
- Questions: evidence of controller overfitting to the validation set and mitigation strategies.
