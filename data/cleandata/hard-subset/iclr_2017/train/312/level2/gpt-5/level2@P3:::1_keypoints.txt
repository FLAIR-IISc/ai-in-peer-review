- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RNN controller generates variable-length architecture descriptions, trained with REINFORCE and large-scale distributed evaluation.  
- Architecture space includes skip connections via set-selection attention, branching, layer types, and a tree-based parameterization for RNN cells (memory injection/read locations).  
- CIFAR-10 results: discovers convolutional architectures competitive with human designs, reaching 3.65% test error with favorable speed vs DenseNet baseline.  
- PTB results: discovers recurrent cell achieving new SOTA perplexity (62.4), transfers to character-level modeling (1.214 bpc) and yields modest GNMT improvement without retuning.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reasons for acceptance: novel formulation of architecture search as sequence generation with policy gradients over variable-length spaces.  
- Key reasons for acceptance: strong empirical results, including SOTA on PTB and competitive CIFAR-10, supported by controls and transfer experiments.  
- Positions method relative to prior work (Bayesian optimization, neuro-evolution, seq2seq and sequence-level RL) appropriately.  
- Methodologically sound: REINFORCE with moving-average baseline, clear skip-connection prediction, invalid graph handling, and distributed asynchronous training described.  
- Empirical analyses include effects of skip connections, stride prediction, pooling inclusion, width scaling, and random-search control showing NAS advantage.  
- Generality: same controller framework adapted to both convolutional and recurrent search spaces.  
- Essential improvement requested: report computational budget (GPU/CPU days), fraction of invalid/failed architectures, and sample efficiency metrics.  
- Essential improvement requested: analyze potential validation-overfitting (multiple splits or stability across runs).  
- Essential improvement requested: add stronger baselines (e.g., Bayesian optimization/SMAC/Hyperband) on the same search space.  
- Nice-to-have: sensitivity analysis for reward shaping and baseline choice, controller architecture ablations, and fairness/clarity on post-search hyperparameter tuning and throughput claims.  
- Nice-to-have: provide search-space diagnostics (operation distributions, skip densities, RNN operation frequencies).  
- Clarification questions: how many invalid child models and how were their rewards handled; variance/consistency of best architectures across seeds; disentangle PTB gains from cell design vs hyperparameter tuning.  
- Clarification questions: for CIFAR pooling setting, how does scaling width during search affect results and what are parameter/FLOP comparisons with DenseNet; for GNMT, provide layer sizes, parameter counts, and FLOPs.  
- Overall assessment: compelling, influential approach with solid grounding and validation; heavy compute and some missing efficiency/variance details but merits acceptance.
