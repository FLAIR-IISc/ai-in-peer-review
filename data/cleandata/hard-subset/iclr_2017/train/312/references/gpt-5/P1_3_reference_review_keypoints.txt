- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS): a controller RNN that samples variable-length architectures trained via policy gradient (REINFORCE) with validation accuracy reward.  
- Extends search space with skip/branch prediction via set-selection attention, additional layer types/hyperparameters, and tree-based RNN cell generation producing novel cells.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, achieving near-SOTA/SOTA results and showing transferability to char-PTB and GNMT.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main reason: Novel, well-motivated method that convincingly discovers high-performing architectures in vision and language with cross-task transfer.  
- Main reason: Clear, general framework with concrete technical contributions (skip-connection selection, tree-based RNN cell search) and strong empirical evidence beyond toy problems.  
- Motivation: Tackles costly manual architecture design and handles variable-length discrete choices and complex connectivity better than BO/evolution approaches.  
- Methodology: Controller emits architectural tokens; training uses REINFORCE with a moving-average baseline and large-scale asynchronous distributed training; reward shaping (cubing) disclosed.  
- Skip-connection mechanism: Set-selection attention parameterizes arbitrary DAG connectivity with practical compilation fixes.  
- Recurrent cell search: Tree-structured binary compositions with activation choices and memory injection—expressive yet structured search space.  
- Robustness: Random search baseline and enlarged search-space experiments indicate policy gradient’s advantage.  
- CIFAR-10 results: Discovered ConvNets achieve competitive error rates (e.g., 5.50%, 6.01%, 4.47%, 3.65% after widening) and show qualitative differences (rectangular filters, shallow depth, skip usage).  
- PTB results: NAS cell yields strong perplexities, improves prior SOTA by ~3.6 PPL with fewer inner iterations; transfers well to char-PTB and GNMT (+0.5 BLEU reported).  
- Experimental protocol: Uses held-out validation for reward/model selection and reports test once for best-val model; small post-selection grid search disclosed.  
- Scope/impact: General, modular, and influential work that demonstrates automated search can match/exceed human designs; code and NASCell release aid reproducibility.  
- Limitation: Extremely high computational cost (e.g., hundreds of GPUs/CPUs, thousands of models) limits practicality and reproducibility for typical groups.  
- Limitation: Comparisons lack strong BO/SMBO/evolutionary baselines at matched budgets; some CIFAR-10 search-space constraints and post-hoc widening complicate fairness.  
- Limitation: Statistical rigor issues—mostly single-run best-model reports, no variance estimates across controller runs or selected architectures.  
- Limitation: Search-space bias from manual constraints (depth schedule, pooling indices, channel sets) may steer the controller toward certain families.  
- Suggestion: Report total compute (GPU/CPU days, energy) and strategies to reduce cost (early stopping, weight sharing, low-fidelity proxies).  
- Suggestion: Include stronger baseline comparisons under matched budgets (SMAC/BOHB/TPE, evolutionary algorithms).  
- Suggestion: Provide variance estimates across multiple NAS runs and sensitivity analyses for reward shaping and baseline choices.  
- Suggestion: Clarify speed metrics (training vs inference, hardware) and include FLOPs/params for discovered models; compare under matched FLOPs.  
- Suggestion: Expand ablations on depth schedule, skip-connection mechanism, and search-space constraints.  
- Suggestion: Rephrase “gradient-based method” to emphasize policy gradient for non-differentiable rewards.  
- Questions: Total GPU/CPU days for CIFAR-10 and PTB searches; effects of early stopping or low-fidelity proxies on final rankings?  
- Questions: Sensitivity to cubing validation accuracy and the moving-average baseline; tried advantage normalization or entropy regularization?  
- Questions: How critical are fixed pooling layers and 3-layer blocks for the 39-layer CIFAR model; would similar structures emerge without these constraints?  
- Questions: How was “1.05× faster” measured; provide FLOPs and wall-clock inference comparisons to DenseNet on the same hardware?  
- Questions: Consistency across controller seeds—variance in discovered architectures and validation/test metrics?  
- Questions: Did you run SMBO/TPE or evolutionary strategies in the same search space and budget; how did they compare?  
- Questions: For GNMT, is the +0.5 BLEU consistent across runs, and which NAS cell aspects benefit NMT versus LSTM?
