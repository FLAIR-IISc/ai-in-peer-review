- Proposes NAS: a controller RNN that emits variable-length architecture descriptions and is trained with REINFORCE using child validation accuracy as reward.
- Search space includes convolutional networks (with skip connections and varied layer types) and tree-parameterized recurrent cells with optional cell-state injection.
- Implements a large-scale asynchronous distributed training system to evaluate many child models in parallel.
- Empirically discovers competitive ConvNets on CIFAR-10 and a recurrent cell achieving SOTA perplexity on PTB, with transfer to char-level PTB and small GNMT gains.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reasons for acceptance: novel, general formulation of architecture search as sequence prediction with policy gradients and a practical distributed training recipe.
- Key reasons for acceptance: strong empirical results across vision and language, with transfer experiments and control comparisons.
- Problem tackled: automating neural architecture design over variable-length, structured spaces rather than fixed-length hyperparameters.
- Positioning: well-motivated relative to Bayesian optimization, neuroevolution, sequence modeling, REINFORCE, and meta-learning.
- CIFAR-10 results: finds high-quality architectures (best model 3.65% test error after modest augmentation) though some comparisons differ in training recipe/parameterization.
- PTB results: discovered RNN cell improves perplexity over strong baselines with comparable budgets; efficient versus RHN and transferable to other tasks.
- Controls: random search baseline shows consistent advantage for policy gradient; expanding search space degrades but remains competitive.
- Strength: conceptual novelty—controller RNN over variable-length architectural programs optimized via REINFORCE.
- Strength: flexible search space including learned skip-connection selection and tree-based recurrent cell parameterization.
- Strength: scalable distributed implementation that makes large-scale search practical in wall-clock time.
- Strength: empirical breadth with cross-domain results, transfer, and some ablations/controls.
- Weakness: very high computational cost (hundreds of GPUs/CPUs, thousands of trained candidates), limiting reproducibility and adoption.
- Weakness: risk of overfitting to the validation split when selecting best architectures from tens of thousands on small validation sets.
- Weakness: fairness concerns—post-hoc capacity changes and differing training recipes complicate head-to-head comparisons with baselines.
- Weakness: limited quantitative analysis of what the controller learns (operation distributions, skip patterns, entropy over time).
- Weakness: REINFORCE details underreported—sensitivity to reward shaping, entropy regularization, variance, and baseline choices not fully explored.
- Weakness: heuristic "compilation failure" fixes may bias search and lack ablation.
- Suggestions: report averages over multiple validation splits and search seeds or use a separate selection set to assess overfitting.
- Suggestions: provide capacity-matched comparisons and separate “as found” versus post-hoc tuned numbers with identical training recipes for baselines.
- Suggestions: add ablations on reward shaping and controller regularization, plus controller learning curves and reward variance.
- Suggestions: analyze learned distributions over architectural choices and skip connectivity to explain controller behavior.
- Suggestions: discuss and evaluate compute-efficient variants (early stopping, lower-fidelity evaluations, weight inheritance) to improve accessibility.
- Suggestions: clarify recurrent cell search-space indexing/constraints and quantify effects of automatic fixes for invalid graphs.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to reward shaping, baseline choice, and entropy regularization; any learned critics tried?
- Questions for authors: how is overfitting to validation mitigated; results under re-splitting or separate selection sets?
- Questions for authors: variance across independent NAS runs in best-found performance and search efficiency?
- Questions for authors: provide capacity-matched CIFAR-10 comparisons without post-hoc filter additions and with identical training hyperparameters?
- Questions for authors: frequency and impact of compilation-failure fallbacks on final performance?
- Questions for authors: how does controller performance scale with number of parallel workers and epochs per child; are there diminishing returns?
- Questions for authors: will you release controller code and exact search spaces to aid reproducibility?
