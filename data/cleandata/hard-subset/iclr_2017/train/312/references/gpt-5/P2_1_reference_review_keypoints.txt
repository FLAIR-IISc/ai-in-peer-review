- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (controller RNN with REINFORCE) to generate variable-length neural network architectures trained by using child model validation performance as reward.
- Supports rich search spaces including CNNs with skip connections and flexible RNN cells specified as computation trees with choices over functions and state access.
- Uses large-scale asynchronous distributed training with tens to hundreds of replicas to make the search practical.
- Demonstrates competitive-to-SOTA CNNs on CIFAR-10 and a novel RNN cell improving PTB perplexity, with transfer to char-level PTB and GNMT.

- Decision: Accept.

- Key reason: A flexible, general, and effective method for learning architectures, including variable-length and connectivity patterns.
- Key reason: Strong empirical evidence across vision and language, credible comparison to random search, and demonstrated transferability.

- Tackles automating neural architecture design beyond fixed-length hyperparameters, a timely and important problem.
- Method well positioned relative to BO/TPE, neuroevolution, and meta-learning; addresses fixed-length and scalability limitations.
- Attention-like set selection for skip connections is a neat design enabling complex topologies.

- CIFAR-10: Controller finds a 39-layer model achieving 4.47% test error, improved to 3.65% by scaling filters; learned topology matters per ablations.
- PTB: Learned RNN cell outperforms strong LSTM baselines by ~3.6 perplexity; policy gradient outperforms random search in a huge search space (~6e16).
- Transfer: Learned cell generalizes to character PTB and GNMT, suggesting discovery of reusable primitives.
- Methodological correctness: Uses standard REINFORCE with moving-average baseline; distributed async training and heuristics for invalid graphs are sensible and documented.

- Caveat: Approach requires massive compute (up to ~800 GPUs, 400 CPUs, 12.8k–15k model evaluations); needs reporting of GPU/CPU-days and sample efficiency.
- Caveat: Comparisons (e.g., DenseNet) are not fully apples-to-apples; some final model improvements involve post-search tuning and scaling.
- Caveat: Search space contains hand-crafted choices (pooling positions, activation sets, base numbers) that may bias results; ablations on search space design are needed.
- Caveat: High variance/stability concerns; no multiple independent runs or error bars reported.

- Suggest reporting full compute budgets, wall-clock time, and cost-performance trade-offs.
- Suggest including controller learning curves (reward vs. steps) and analysis of sample efficiency.
- Suggest detailing variance-reduction techniques (entropy reg, temperature, gradient clipping, baseline smoothing) and ablations on reward shaping.
- Suggest sensitivity analyses for child training epochs, depth schedules, and partial vs. full convergence effects on rankings.
- Suggest clarifying fairness by listing exact training/augmentation protocols and any extra tuning for baselines vs. NAS models.
- Suggest characterizing discovered architectures (skip connection distributions, receptive fields, in/out-degree, gate-like behaviors).
- Suggest releasing search code/checkpoints or a smaller-scale reproducible recipe.

- Q1: What are total compute budgets (GPU/CPU-days) for CIFAR-10 and PTB searches, and how does performance scale with evaluated architectures?
- Q2: How sensitive are final selections to training children for only 50 (CIFAR) or 35 (PTB) epochs—do rankings change when training to full convergence?
- Q3: Were entropy regularization, temperature, or other variance-reduction techniques used, and how critical is reward shaping (e.g., cubing/max-of-last-5)?
- Q4: How robust are results to different random seeds—please provide variance across independent controller runs?
- Q5: How much do fixed choices (pooling positions, base number for RNN cells) influence outcomes—any ablations with alternatives?
- Q6: Do compilation-failure heuristics bias the controller toward patterns, and what failure modes were observed?
- Q7: Beyond random search, was there comparison to a strong Bayesian optimization baseline over a comparable discrete space?
