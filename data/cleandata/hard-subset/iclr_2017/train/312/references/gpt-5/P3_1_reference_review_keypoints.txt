- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RNN controller that autoregressively generates variable-length neural architectures, trained with REINFORCE using child validation performance as reward.  
- Supports complex topologies via a set-selection attention mechanism for skip/branch connections and predicts layer types and hyperparameters.  
- Uses asynchronous distributed training to scale, evaluating hundreds of child models in parallel.  
- Two instantiations: convolutional architectures for CIFAR-10 and novel recurrent-cell search for Penn Treebank (PTB).  
- Empirical results: competitive/state-of-the-art — CIFAR-10 down to 3.65% after post-hoc widening; PTB word-level perplexity 62.4 and positive transfer to char-PTB and GNMT.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Primary reason 1: Novel, general, and flexible formulation for searching variable-length architectures with strong empirical results across domains.  
- Primary reason 2: Clear methodological innovations (attention-based skip selection, cell search space, large-scale asynchronous REINFORCE).  
- Primary reason 3: Compelling evidence that the framework discovers high-performing, nontrivial designs.  
- Motivation: Well-motivated given limitations of manual design, BO, and neuro-evolution; controller policy over sequences is expressive for complex graph decisions.  
- Methodological soundness: Autoregressive policy + REINFORCE is appropriate; moving-average baseline and batching reduce variance; skip-attention is elegant; recurrent-cell space is well-designed.  
- Scaling strategy: Practical use of parameter servers and many replicas makes RL-driven NAS viable at scale.  
- Empirical strength: Finds architectures with recognizable motifs, rediscovers LSTM-like structures, and shows transferability; outperforms random search under the given budget.  
- Clarity: Controller, reward shaping, skip mechanism, and cell tree are clearly described with training details and hyperparameters.  
- Concern — computational cost: Extremely resource-heavy (up to 800 GPUs for CIFAR, 400 CPUs for PTB), limiting reproducibility and fairness of comparisons.  
- Concern — fairness of comparisons/post-hoc tweaks: Best CIFAR result uses post-hoc widening (40 filters); unclear whether baselines received comparable tuning; runtime comparisons need standardization.  
- Concern — search space and reward design: Controller benefits from curated search spaces and heuristic reward shaping (cubed accuracy, perplexity^2); limited sensitivity analyses.  
- Concern — statistical rigor and robustness: Results are largely single-run best-of-many; variance across seeds, proxy vs full-training correlation, and potential meta-overfitting are underreported; asynchronous stale updates and lack of entropy/KL control noted.  
- Suggestions — report compute: Provide end-to-end GPU/CPU-day budgets for search and final training and normalize comparisons by budget.  
- Suggestions — runtime/FLOPs: Report runtime/throughput and FLOPs/parameter counts on identical hardware and clarify test-time augmentation.  
- Suggestions — ablations: Study entropy regularization, reward scaling, controller capacity, depth schedule, and skip-selection mechanism; test broader op sets.  
- Suggestions — quantify robustness: Report variance over controller seeds and correlation between proxy rewards and final performance.  
- Suggestions — justify post-hoc widening: Provide performance of unmodified discovered models under matched capacity and clarify tuning applied to baselines.  
- Suggestions — analyze repairs: Quantify architectures requiring padding/input fallbacks and their impact on performance/compute fairness.  
- Suggestions — democratization: Discuss ethical/practical implications and ways to reduce cost (proxy tasks, weight sharing, early stopping).  
- Questions — compute/fairness: What are total compute budgets for CPFAR/PTB search and final training, and how are runtime comparisons normalized?  
- Questions — reward/exploration: How sensitive are results to cubed-accuracy/perplexity^2 rewards; was entropy regularization used; any nonstationarity issues?  
- Questions — robustness: How many independent controller runs, variance of best-found performance, and proxy vs full-training correlation?  
- Questions — validation/meta-overfitting: Any monitoring for controller overfitting to the validation split (hold-out/cross-validation)?  
- Questions — search-space dependence: How do results change when broadening op sets or disallowing skips; are there failure modes?  
- Questions — architecture repairs: What fraction of sampled architectures required repairs and do repairs bias search?  
- Questions — post-hoc tuning fairness: Was CIFAR widening applied to baselines; what is accuracy of unmodified discovered model under matched budgets?  
- Questions — PTB specifics: Are embedding tying and dropout settings identical to baselines; performance under stronger baselines with matched setups?
