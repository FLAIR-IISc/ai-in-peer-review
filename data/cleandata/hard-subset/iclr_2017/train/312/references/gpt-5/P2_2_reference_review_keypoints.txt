- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RNN controller trained with REINFORCE to generate variable-length neural architectures.  
- Uses large-scale distributed asynchronous policy gradient to evaluate child models.  
- Supports flexible architecture components: convolutions (skip, pooling, BN) and tree-structured RNN cells with memory routing.  
- Achieves strong empirical results: competitive CNNs on CIFAR-10 and improved RNN cells on PTB, with transfer to char-level PTB and GNMT.  
- Decision: Accept — judged substantive, timely, and impactful.  
- Main contribution: general, flexible search over variable-length architectures beyond fixed-dimensional HPO.  
- Technical novelties: auto-regressive controller, set-selection attention for skips, tree RNN parameterization, distributed training.  
- Empirical evidence spans modalities and includes transfer experiments and ablations.  
- Compared against strong baselines (ResNet/DenseNet variants) and includes a random-search control.  
- Rigor: validation held-out for reward; test reported only for best model; parameter budgets matched on PTB.  
- Caveat — massive compute: extremely high GPU/CPU usage limits reproducibility and fair comparison.  
- Caveat — post-hoc tuning (grid search, channel scaling) blurs distinction between architecture search and training/hyperparameter tuning.  
- Caveat — limited statistical reporting: mainly single runs, no variance across independent NAS runs or controller seeds.  
- Caveat — search-space heuristics (shape fixes, default inputs) may bias results; invalid-sample rates not reported.  
- Suggested improvement: provide full compute accounting (sampled architectures, training time, FLOPs, wall-clock).  
- Suggested improvement: report distributions/percentiles, top-k averages, and variance over multiple seeds.  
- Suggested improvement: separate “as-found” performance from post-hoc tuned performance; apply matched tuning to baselines.  
- Suggested improvement: run sensitivity/ablation studies on reward shaping, baseline/EMA, depth schedule, and skip-attention.  
- Suggested improvement: fully specify search tokens/options, constraints, failure-handling rules, and fraction of invalid samples.  
- Suggested improvement: release search code or a small-scale reproducible reference setup; consider early-stopping/HB to reduce cost.  
- Questions: how many architectures were fully trained and total compute (GPU/CPU hours); sensitivity to 10x fewer evaluations?  
- Questions: why cube CIFAR validation accuracy, and were other variance-reduction techniques tried?  
- Questions: what fraction of CNNs required default/zero-padding fixes, how often do RNN trees degenerate, and how are such cases handled?  
- Questions: did random-search baselines receive the same post-hoc tuning/channel scaling; can you provide budget-matched comparisons to advanced HPO methods?  
- Questions: what is variance across independent NAS runs for CIFAR-10 and PTB, and do discovered CNNs transfer to datasets like CIFAR-100 or SVHN without further search?
