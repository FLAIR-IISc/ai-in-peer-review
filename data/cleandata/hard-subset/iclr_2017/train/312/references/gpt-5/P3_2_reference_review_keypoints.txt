- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RNN controller trained with policy gradients to generate variable-length neural architectures.  
- Controller samples architectures, child networks are trained, and validation performance is used as the REINFORCE reward with a moving-average baseline.  
- Applies NAS to CNN search on CIFAR-10 with set-selection attention for skip connections and optional pooling/stride choices.  
- Scales search with parameter-server architecture and hundreds of replicas to evaluate thousands of architectures in parallel.  
- Applies NAS to RNN cell search on PTB, producing a novel NASCell via a compositional computation tree and learned cell-state injection.  
- Empirically finds CNNs competitive with or better than human-designed models on CIFAR-10 and an RNN cell that improves PTB perplexity and transfers to other tasks.  
- Demonstrates flexibility: variable-length architectures, complex connectivity, and support for skip connections and recurrent cells.  
- Describes engineering choices to stabilize training and avoid invalid graphs.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: novel, influential formulation—autoregressive controller trained by RL for variable-length architecture generation across vision and sequence tasks.  
- Strong empirical results with wins over strong hand-designed baselines and evidence of transferability.  
- Methodological novelty: RNN controller token-by-token expands search space beyond fixed-length hyperparameter tuning.  
- Set-selection attention provides a principled way to propose sparse, content-informed skip connections.  
- RNN search design (base-8 tree, learned cell-state injection) subsumes standard RNN motifs and enables novel compositions.  
- Optimization: addresses REINFORCE variance via moving-average baseline, reward shaping, depth-growth schedule, and heavy distributed parallelism.  
- Random search baseline shows the controller learns better-than-random policies over time (improves best and top-k averages).  
- CIFAR-10 results: 5.50% (no pooling), 4.47% (with pooling), improved to 3.65% after post-hoc widening—competitive with DenseNet variants.  
- PTB result: discovered cell achieves 62.4 test perplexity, surpassing prior SOTA and running faster than RHN.  
- Transfer: NASCell yields SOTA in a larger char-level LM setting and +0.5 BLEU in GNMT with matched compute.  
- Positioning: situates work relative to Bayesian optimization, random search, neuro-evolution, meta-learning, and RL for non-differentiable objectives.  
- Main concern: extreme compute requirements (e.g., ~12.8k CIFAR architectures, up to 800 GPUs) raise reproducibility and practicality issues.  
- Fairness concern: child models trained with fixed hyperparameters during search and post-hoc tuning for final models may bias comparisons to baselines.  
- Search-space design is handcrafted (fixed pooling layers, RNN tree template) and heuristics for invalid graphs could bias outcomes.  
- Optimization/stability omissions: no ablation of other stabilizers (entropy regularization, advantage normalization), depth-growth schedule, or asynchronous update effects.  
- Statistical rigor lacking: primarily best-of-run reporting, missing variance, confidence intervals, and risk of overfitting to the validation split.  
- Suggestion: report compute budgets (GPU/CPU days), number of architectures evaluated, and sample-efficiency metrics; include cost-aware comparisons.  
- Suggestion: provide ablations on reward shaping, baseline type, entropy regularization, depth-growth schedule, and controller capacity.  
- Suggestion: strengthen fairness by training baselines with identical pipelines and reporting standardized FLOPs/latency metrics.  
- Suggestion: clarify heuristics for invalid graphs, their frequency, and biasing effects.  
- Suggestion: release code or detailed pseudocode/configurations and report results across multiple search seeds for reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: evaluate NASCell on additional datasets/tasks (e.g., WikiText-2) and analyze learned motifs relative to known gating patterns.  
- Questions for authors: clarify PTB reward transformation (c·(validation perplexity)^2—negated/inverted?), sensitivity to c.  
- Questions: report GPU/CPU days for CIFAR-10 and PTB searches and sample-efficiency curves (top-1 vs. architectures evaluated).  
- Questions: how sensitive are results to child training budget (50/35 epochs) and did you try multi-fidelity/early-stopping to reduce cost?  
- Questions: what fraction of sampled architectures triggered invalid-graph heuristics and do those heuristics bias connectivity patterns?  
- Questions: were random-search baselines drawn from the exact same search space with identical handling and budgets, and can you report mean/variance?  
- Questions: did you vary the CIFAR-10 validation split to assess selection bias from the 5k validation set?  
- Questions: how well does NASCell generalize to other corpora (WikiText-2, Enwik8) with matched regularization?  
- Questions: did you try entropy regularization or advantage normalization and what were their effects on exploration/convergence?
