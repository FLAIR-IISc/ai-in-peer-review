- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS): a controller RNN trained with policy gradients to generate architectures, train child models, and use validation accuracy as reward.
- Supports rich, variable-length search spaces including convolutional architectures with skip connections and recurrent cell computation trees with learnable memory connections.
- Describes a large-scale asynchronous distributed training setup to evaluate thousands of candidate architectures in parallel.

- Empirical finding: discovers a competitive CIFAR-10 ConvNet (best test error 3.65% after width scaling).
- Empirical finding: discovers a novel RNN cell achieving state-of-the-art PTB perplexity and transfers well to char-LM and GNMT.
- Empirical finding: policy-gradient-based search outperforms random search and is robust to expanded search spaces.

- Decision: Accept.

- Key reason: Clear, impactful, flexible end-to-end method that automatically discovers competitive architectures across modalities.
- Key reason: Strong empirical results with careful baselines and control experiments demonstrating performance and generality.
- Key reason: Method is general and positions well relative to hyperparameter optimization, neuroevolution, and meta-learning.

- Supporting argument: Novel formulation treating architecture design as an autoregressive RL problem with set-selection attention for skip connections.
- Supporting argument: Empirical strength shown on CIFAR-10 and PTB with meaningful transfer without bespoke tuning.
- Supporting argument: Methodological rigor via variance-reduction baseline, asynchronous training, and control experiments.
- Supporting argument: Practicality and scalability demonstrated with a distributed parameter-server/replica setup and sensible layer-depth schedule.
- Supporting argument: Clear framing and motivation for RL on non-differentiable objectives.

- Caveat: Method is extremely compute-intensive (hundreds of GPUs/CPUs; ~12.8k–15k architectures), limiting accessibility and reproducibility.
- Caveat: Risk of selection bias/overfitting from selecting best model after thousands of trials on a small validation split.
- Caveat: Final CIFAR-10 result uses post-search grid tuning and width scaling, complicating fair comparisons.
- Caveat: Fixed short training for child models may imperfectly rank architectures; multi-fidelity strategies could help.

- Feedback: Report full compute budget (wall-clock time, GPU/CPU hours, cost per architecture) and performance-vs-compute curves.
- Feedback: Justify reward shaping (cubing accuracy, squaring perplexity) and provide sensitivity analyses and baseline smoothing details.
- Feedback: Ensure fair CIFAR-10 comparisons by reporting params, FLOPs, and single-GPU throughput versus DenseNet/WRN under identical settings.
- Feedback: Quantify validation overfitting risk (e.g., retrain top N on fresh validation or cross-validate) given many trials on a small split.
- Feedback: Provide search-space ablations isolating skip connections, layer-type diversity, stride prediction, and padding heuristics.
- Feedback: Show correlation between early-stop proxy performance and fully trained performance; consider Hyperband-style multi-fidelity.
- Feedback: Release controller code, search-space specs, seeds, and child training scripts to improve reproducibility.
- Feedback: Analyze discovered motifs quantitatively/qualitatively to reveal what NAS learns (filters, skips, recurrent structures).

- Question 1: Why cube validation accuracy (and square scaled perplexity), and how sensitive are results to that and baseline hyperparameters?
- Question 2: Did you use entropy regularization or other exploration bonuses to prevent premature controller collapse; with what coefficients/schedules?
- Question 3: What is the Spearman correlation between early validation performance (50 epochs CIFAR-10; 35 epochs PTB) and fully trained performance?
- Question 4: With 12.8k–15k trials on a 5k validation split, did top architectures degrade on an unseen validation set; any estimate of selection bias?
- Question 5: What are the total GPU/CPU hours per experiment (including controller training and post-search tuning), and how does performance scale with fewer replicas/trials?
- Question 6: For the CIFAR-10 best model, report parameter counts, FLOPs, and standardized inference speed vs DenseNet/WRN on identical hardware.
- Question 7: How often do “compilation failures” occur, and how much do padding/concatenation heuristics bias search outcomes toward certain architectures?
- Question 8: For PTB, are shared embeddings, dropout, and decay schedules matched exactly to prior baselines—provide a table aligning settings to each baseline.
- Question 9: Did you try alternative RL algorithms (e.g., A2C) or other variance-reduction techniques beyond EMA baseline; any benefits observed?
- Question 10: Beyond char-LM and GNMT, have you tested transfer of the CIFAR-10 cell/architecture to CIFAR-100, SVHN, or small ImageNet without re-searching?
