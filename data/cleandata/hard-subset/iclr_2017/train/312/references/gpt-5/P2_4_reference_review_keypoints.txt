- Proposal: Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS), a controller RNN trained with REINFORCE to generate variable-length neural architectures across rich search spaces.  
- Training: Uses large-scale asynchronous distributed training of many child models in parallel to make the loop practical.  
- ConvNet results: Discovers CIFAR-10 convnets competitive with SOTA (best 3.65% after post-hoc widening) with novel design motifs (rectangular filters, selective skips).  
- RNN results: Discovers RNN cells that outperform strong LSTM baselines on PTB and transfer to char-level PTB and GNMT with BLEU improvements.  
- Decision: Reviewer recommends accept based on conceptual novelty, generality, and strong cross-modal empirical evidence.  
- Motivation/literature: Well-motivated shift from hyperparameter tuning to architecture engineering; positioned fairly against BO, neuroevolution, meta-learning, and RL.  
- Method soundness: REINFORCE with moving-average baseline is appropriate; set-selection attention and tree-based RNN parametrization are reasonable and well-specified.  
- Reward shaping: Uses pragmatic reward shaping (accuracy^3, scaled perplexity) for variance reduction but could use more justification.  
- Empirical rigor — CIFAR: Explores a large space and finds high-performing designs, though final result uses post-hoc widening complicating direct comparisons.  
- Empirical rigor — PTB: NAS outperforms baselines including random search under matched budgets; PG clearly learns a nontrivial policy.  
- Breadth: Experiments span CV and NLP and include transfer experiments, supporting claimed generality.  
- Main limitation: Extremely high computational cost (up to hundreds of GPUs/CPUs), raising accessibility and reproducibility concerns.  
- Comparison gaps: Lacks random-search/BO baselines on CIFAR under equal compute and unclear parity in hyperparameter tuning for baselines.  
- Validation and overfitting concerns: Reward shaping and using a validation split from training could bias search; more overfitting safeguards needed.  
- Search-space dependency: Outcomes likely influenced by search-space engineering (e.g., tree base, op set); more ablations requested.  
- Requested improvements: Report precise compute budgets, child training stats, controller sample efficiency, failure rates, and variance across controller runs.  
- Additional baselines: Provide fair random/evolutionary/BO baselines on CIFAR under matched compute and show performance vs budget.  
- Robustness checks: Report sensitivity to controller hyperparameters, reward exponent, and seeds; re-rank top-K on fresh validation to check overfitting.  
- Cost-aware suggestions: Consider including latency/param/energy penalties in reward and report parameter/compute counts consistently.  
- Analysis asks: Provide deeper analysis of discovered structures and common motifs across top architectures.  
- Questions for authors: Request exact GPU/CPU-days and wall-clock time, sensitivity to reward shaping and variance-reduction methods, controller architecture sensitivity, overfitting mitigation, fairness of baselines, search-space effects, use of efficiency proxies (weight sharing/early stopping), and broader transfer evaluations.
