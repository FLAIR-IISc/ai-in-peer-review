- Introduces Neural Architecture Search: controller RNN generates architectures trained with policy gradients; claims SOTA on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank and provides code/TensorFlow cell.

- Main contributions: architecture-as-strings representation, policy-gradient controller training, skip/branch and tree-structured RNN cell extensions, and reported results (3.65% CIFAR-10, 62.4 PPL PTB).

- Decision: Reject.

- Primary rejection reasons: comparisons to recent state-of-the-art are insufficient and the reported computational cost makes the approach impractical.

- Comparison critique: CIFAR-10 SOTA comparison is outdated (compares to DenseNet) and ignores stronger recent work like NASNet and DARTS, making claimed improvements unconvincing.

- Parameter-efficiency issue: reported gains may be due to using more parameters (no 1x1 convs) rather than better architecture, and this is not isolated or controlled for.

- Computational cost opacity: mentions 800 GPUs but omits total GPU-hours, cost per architecture, and comparisons to other NAS methods, hiding practical feasibility.

- Validation set handling unclear: paper doesn’t explain how the validation set was used during search, risking overfitting to validation and invalidating reported selection procedure.

- Overstated novelty: claim of designing models "from scratch" is exaggerated given prior work (evolutionary algorithms, Bayesian optimization) that also designs architectures.

- Lack of transferability analysis: limited discussion/experiments on how discovered architectures generalize to other tasks/datasets beyond a single transfer experiment.

- Recommendation: update comparisons to include NASNet, DARTS, ENAS and other recent methods to situate contributions properly.

- Recommendation: provide full computational-cost breakdown (total GPU-hours, cost per architecture, comparisons) to assess practicality.

- Recommendation: clearly describe validation-set usage and safeguards against overfitting during search.

- Recommendation: include visualizations and deeper analysis of discovered architectures and the principles behind their success.

- Recommendation: address parameter-efficiency differences (e.g., 1x1 convs) and provide controlled comparisons with matched parameter counts.

- Recommendation: discuss limitations and scalability (e.g., how the method would scale to ImageNet) and practical challenges.

- Questions for authors: compare to NASNet and DARTS in performance and cost with detailed numbers.

- Questions for authors: quantify how much CIFAR-10 differences are due to parameter-reduction techniques versus architecture itself; compare DenseNet with matched parameter count.

- Questions for authors: explain how the validation set was protected from overfitting during the search and controller training.

- Questions for authors: provide a detailed computational-cost breakdown (total GPU-hours, cost per architecture) and comparisons to other methods.

- Questions for authors: provide evidence that this method meaningfully outperforms or advances beyond evolutionary and Bayesian optimization approaches for architecture design.
