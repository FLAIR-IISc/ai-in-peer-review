- Paper proposes Neural Architecture Search: a gradient-based controller RNN that generates architectures and is trained with policy gradients using child network validation accuracy as reward.  
- Method handles variable-length architecture spaces and can search both convolutional and recurrent architectures.  
- Empirical results: CIFAR-10 3.65% test error (39-layer model, 1.05x faster than prior best) and Penn Treebank 62.4 perplexity (3.6 better than prior SOTA).  
- Thorough experimental validation including ablation studies, control experiments, and transfer learning to character LM and MT.  
- Decision: reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Key reasons for acceptance: addresses an important problem, is well-motivated and novel, and is well-situated relative to hyperparameter optimization, neuro-evolution, and program synthesis.  
- Supporting arguments: conceptually simple yet powerful, automates architecture design without human expertise, and shows robust empirical performance.  
- Controller-based search outperforms random search, with top-model averages significantly better (Figure 6).  
- Suggested improvement: quantify total computational cost (GPU-hours) and compare to training costs of human-designed architectures.  
- Suggested improvement: provide deeper architectural analysis explaining what makes discovered architectures novel.  
- Suggested improvement: discuss sample efficiency—how 12,800 sampled architectures relate to the full search space.  
- Suggested improvement: more direct comparison to other AutoML/NAS methods (e.g., DARTS, ENAS) to clarify novelty and advantages.  
- Questions for authors: percent of architectures failing to compile and impact on controller performance.  
- Questions for authors: provide controller learning curves showing how sampled architectures' validation accuracy evolves during training.  
- Questions for authors: quantify what makes the CIFAR-10 model "inexpensive" (parameters, FLOPs) relative to other top models.  
- Questions for authors: justify the depth-increase schedule (e.g., +2 every 1,600 samples) and effect of alternative schedules.  
- Questions for authors: compare parameters and computational cost of the Penn Treebank/character LM model vs the LSTM baseline.
