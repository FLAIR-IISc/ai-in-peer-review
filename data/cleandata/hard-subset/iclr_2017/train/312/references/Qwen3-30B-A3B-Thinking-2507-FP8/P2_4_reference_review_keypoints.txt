- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): controller RNN trained with REINFORCE to generate variable-length architecture strings, discovering architectures that outperform human designs and transfer across tasks.  
- Decision: Accept — significant, novel contribution with strong empirical support.  
- Novelty/significance: Automates architecture design using variable-length representation and RNN controller, addressing limitations of fixed-length search.  
- Literature context: Clearly distinguishes from hyperparameter optimization, neuro-evolution, and program synthesis; links to seq2seq and RL for non-differentiable objectives.  
- Empirical results: CIFAR-10 3.65% error (1.05x faster than prior best) and Penn Treebank 62.4 perplexity (improves SOTA by 3.6), with transfer to character LM and MT.  
- Empirical rigor: Includes ablation studies, control experiments, and comparisons to random search, demonstrating robustness.  
- Technical soundness: Well-explained controller RNN, REINFORCE training, skip-connection handling, and scalable distributed training.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong candidate for acceptance due to novelty, motivation, and empirical evidence.  
- Feedback — code availability: Provide a clear link to the NAS/TensorFlow implementation for reproducibility.

- Feedback — search space size: Explain how the ~6 × 10^16 architectures for the recurrent cell were calculated.  
- Feedback — computational efficiency: Specify whether the "1.05x as fast" refers to inference, training, or both, and under what hardware conditions.
