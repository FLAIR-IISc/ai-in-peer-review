- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RL controller generates architectures, child networks are trained, validation accuracy used as reward; achieves SOTA on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank; code and NASCell released.

- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.

- Clear problem statement: automates neural architecture design and addresses limitations of prior hyperparameter/search methods.

- Strong literature placement: situates work within hyperparameter optimization, neuro-evolution, program synthesis, and meta-learning.

- Scientific rigor: convincing empirical results, thorough ablations, control experiments (including random search), transfer learning, and proper validation/test protocol.

- Main contribution: a scalable, flexible NAS method that discovers architectures outperforming human-designed ones.

- Request more justification and a summarizing table for the search space design and chosen hyperparameter values.

- Request a detailed computational cost analysis (e.g., total GPU/CPU hours) and discussion of practical limitations due to high compute requirements.

- Ask for clarification on the random search comparison: number of random samples evaluated and NAS performance after the same number of evaluations (e.g., 12,800).

- Request detailed computational complexity comparisons (FLOPs, parameter counts, inference time) with other SOTA models, including DenseNet.

- Question: how were the task-specific search spaces (CIFAR-10, Penn Treebank) determined and ensured to be sufficiently expressive?

- Question: provide the detailed complexity breakdown behind the claim that NAS is "1.05x as fast as DenseNet."

- Question: how many random samples underlie Figure 6, and how does NAS compare when matched for evaluation budget?

- Question: provide more details on performance impact when adding functions like max and sin, and whether NAS is robust to such search-space changes.

- Overall verdict: strong, well-motivated paper with impressive results; minor revisions addressing the above points would strengthen the submission.
