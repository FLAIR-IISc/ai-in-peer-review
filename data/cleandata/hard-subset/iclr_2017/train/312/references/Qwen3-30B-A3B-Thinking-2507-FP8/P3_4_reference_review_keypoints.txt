- Introduces Neural Architecture Search: controller RNN generates variable-length architecture strings and is trained with policy gradients to maximize child network validation accuracy.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results: 3.65% CIFAR-10 test error and 62.4 perplexity on Penn Treebank.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated method addressing variable-length architecture search and positioned well in related work.  
- Strength: strong empirical results and thorough implementation details.  
- Strength: ablation studies (e.g., skip connections) provide useful insights.  
- Concern: insufficient distinction from concurrent Zoph & Le (2016); needs explicit comparison and clarification of methodological differences.  
- Concern: computational cost not fully reported; total GPU-hours/GPU-days to discover architectures is missing.  
- Concern: baseline comparison is weak—random search is insufficient; should compare to Bayesian optimization or Hyperband.  
- Concern: lacks ablation quantifying contributions of specific components (e.g., skip connections, controller architecture).  
- Feedback: explicitly clarify relationship and differences with Zoph & Le (2016).  
- Feedback: report total computational resources (e.g., number of architectures × GPU-days) used in search.  
- Feedback: replace or supplement random search with stronger baselines like Bayesian optimization/Hyperband.  
- Feedback: add a table ablation showing performance impact when removing key components.  
- Feedback: clarify ambiguous speed claim “1.05x as fast” (faster or slower) and provide FLOPs/inference-time comparisons.  
- Feedback: clarify DenseNet comparison regarding use of 1×1 convolutions and which DenseNet variant is being compared.  
- Question: How exactly does this approach differ from Zoph & Le (2016) and why does it achieve better results?  
- Question: What is the actual speedup in FLOPs or inference time versus prior models/LSTM cells?  
- Question: Is “1.05x faster” a 5% speedup or slowdown—please clarify.  
- Question: How much larger is the search space when predicting strides and how does that affect search efficiency (architectures evaluated)?
