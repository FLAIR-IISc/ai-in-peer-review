- Introduces NAS: an RNN controller generates architectures, trains child networks, and updates via policy gradients (REINFORCE) to search variable-length architectures, validated on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.  
- Decision: Accept — significant contribution to automated ML and neural architecture search.  
- Novelty & Significance: Novel gradient-based RL approach that handles variable-length architecture spaces, distinct from hyperparameter optimization and neuroevolution.  
- Strong Empirical Results: Achieves competitive/SOTA results (CIFAR-10 3.65% error, PTB 62.4 perplexity).  
- Technical Soundness: Methodology is well-described; use of REINFORCE with baseline, parallelism, and async updates is appropriate; skip connections and complex recurrent cells enlarge search capabilities.  
- Clear Presentation: Paper is well-written with helpful figures illustrating the method.  
- Search Space Details (improvement): More detailed breakdown and visualization of architectural choices and constraints needed.  
- Computational Cost (improvement): High compute requirement (training thousands of child networks) requires deeper discussion and mitigation strategies.  
- Ablation Studies (improvement): Need targeted ablations to isolate effects of components (baseline, controller RNN architecture, skip connections).  
- Generalizability (improvement): Test discovered architectures on additional datasets to demonstrate transferability.  
- Hyperparameter Sensitivity (improvement): Analyze sensitivity to controller hyperparameters (LSTM size, learning rate, etc.).  
- Question — Distributed Training: What practical challenges and solutions arose when scaling to hundreds of GPUs?  
- Question — Layer Schedule: How was the schedule for increasing child network layers chosen and tuned?  
- Question — Baseline Selection: Why was the exponential moving average baseline chosen and were alternatives explored?  
- Question — Controller Memory: What is the controller network’s memory footprint and how does it scale with search complexity?  
- Question — Computational Comparison: Provide detailed FLOPs and inference-time comparison of NAS-discovered vs. best human-designed architectures (clarify the reported 1.05x speedup).
