- Proposal: Use an RNN controller trained with policy gradients to generate neural network architectures for NAS, evaluated on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Novelty & Impact: Introduces a fundamentally new NAS approach—RNN controller + reinforcement learning—to automate architecture design.  
- Empirical Results: Achieves competitive or state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, including a 3.6 perplexity improvement.  
- Efficiency: Demonstrates improved training efficiency via distributed training and finds architectures with desirable properties (e.g., speed).  
- Problem Framing: Clearly articulates limitations of manual architecture design and situates work among hyperparameter optimization and neuroevolution.  
- Approach Motivation: Framing architecture design as sequence generation is well-motivated; policy gradients are appropriate for optimization.  
- Experimental Rigor: Experiments are thorough with detailed setup, baselines, and ablation studies (e.g., skip connections, stride prediction).  
- Reproducibility: Authors commit to releasing code and discovered architectures.  
- Feedback — Search Space: Needs deeper analysis/visualization of search space exploration and diversity of discovered architectures.  
- Feedback — Computational Cost: Should provide more detail on compute requirements and strategies to reduce child-network evaluation cost.  
- Feedback — Generalization: Should discuss challenges/opportunities for applying the method to other datasets and tasks.  
- Feedback — Hyperparameter Sensitivity: Include analysis of sensitivity to controller hyperparameters (e.g., LSTM size, learning rate).  
- Q — Scalability: How to scale to larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) and optimizations beyond distributed training?  
- Q — Search Space Definition: How sensitive is performance to initial search space choices and any guidelines for defining it?  
- Q — Controller Architecture: Any experiments with alternative controllers (e.g., Transformers) and their impact on performance/stability?  
- Q — Reward Shaping: Were other reward shaping strategies tried beyond cubing validation accuracy to improve learning/stability?
