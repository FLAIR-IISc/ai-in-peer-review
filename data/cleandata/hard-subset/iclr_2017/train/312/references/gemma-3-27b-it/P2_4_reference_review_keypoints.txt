- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS) using an RNN controller and reinforcement learning to generate and evaluate architectures.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Significant AutoML contribution: RL-based exploration of architecture space is innovative and effective.  
- Empirical results: competitive/state-of-the-art on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, surpassing prior SOTA on PTB.  
- Paper is well-written and explains the methodology clearly.  
- Novelty: genuinely novel use of RL for NAS compared to prior constrained search methods.  
- Empirical validation is strong, with benchmark results supporting effectiveness.  
- Technical soundness: clear description of controller, policy gradients, acceleration techniques, and random-search baseline.  
- Impact: automating architecture design could democratize deep learning for non-experts.  
- Ablation studies are limited; need analysis of contributions from components like skip connections and controller design.  
- Computational cost discussion is insufficient; needs resource breakdown and comparison to human design costs.  
- Generalization: should evaluate on more datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to demonstrate broader applicability.  
- Visualization: provide more detailed visualizations and pattern analysis of discovered architectures.  
- Question — Scalability: what are practical limits of the search space and how to scale NAS to larger problems like ImageNet?  
- Question — Controller sensitivity: how sensitive are results to controller RNN choices (layers, hidden size), and were alternatives tested?  
- Question — Reward shaping: were other reward schemes tested besides validation accuracy^3, and what were their effects?  
- Question — Transferability: can architectures discovered on one task transfer effectively to other related tasks beyond GNMT?
