- Introduces NAS: RNN controller generates architectures, trains child networks, and uses RL to update the controller; shows competitive CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank results.  
- Decision: Reject — idea is influential but the paper lacks depth, rigorous evaluation, and feels like a proof-of-concept by today’s standards.  
- Experimental descriptions are high-level with limited rationale for hyperparameter choices and missing comprehensive ablation studies.  
- Fails to directly compare against other NAS methods (e.g., neuroevolution, Bayesian optimization, DARTS, ENAS); comparisons are mainly to hand-designed architectures.  
- Scaling and computational cost are unclear; distributed training is mentioned but GPU-hours and resource requirements are not reported.  
- Reproducibility concerns due to insufficient experimental detail and the complexity of the distributed setup despite promised code release.  
- Presentation of results lacks statistical rigor (no error bars or significance testing).  
- Related work should be expanded to discuss subsequent NAS approaches and better position the contribution within the NAS landscape.  
- Recommend ablation studies to isolate the impact of individual NAS components (e.g., skip connections, controller architecture).  
- Recommend hyperparameter sensitivity analysis for controller learning rate, reward function, and search space choices.  
- Request a detailed computational cost analysis (GPU hours, wall-clock time, memory usage).  
- The reward function (validation accuracy cubed) needs justification and comparison to alternatives.  
- Discuss strategies used to mitigate NAS training instabilities and provide robustness analyses.  
- Question: How do results and costs compare to more recent and efficient NAS algorithms like DARTS or ENAS?  
- Question: What were the primary bottlenecks in distributed training and how were they addressed?  
- Question: Provide precise details of the CIFAR-10 search space and hyperparameter ranges.  
- Question: Why use an EMA baseline for REINFORCE and how does it compare to other baseline choices?  
- Question: What steps and resources are necessary to reproduce the experiments?  
- Question: Has NAS been tested on other domains, and what challenges were encountered?
