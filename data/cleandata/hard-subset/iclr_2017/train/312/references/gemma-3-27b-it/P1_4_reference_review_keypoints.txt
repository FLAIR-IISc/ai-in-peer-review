- Summary: Proposes an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate neural architectures and evaluate them by training child networks.  
- Decision: Accept — compelling, technically sound work with strong results.  
- Novelty & Significance: Automates architecture design using a sequential RNN controller, enabling flexible search beyond fixed-length spaces.  
- Strong Empirical Results: Discovered architectures outperform human-designed models on CIFAR-10 and achieve state-of-the-art perplexity on Penn Treebank.  
- Technical Soundness: Uses policy gradients (REINFORCE) with variance reduction (baseline), distributed training, and skip connections appropriately.  
- Clear Presentation: Well-written, logically organized, and experimentally detailed.  
- Search Space Exploration: Needs more detail and visualization of the defined search space and its limitations.  
- Computational Cost: Lacks thorough analysis of GPU hours and overall resource/cost requirements.  
- Generalization: Should test transferability of found architectures on more datasets beyond Penn Treebank character modeling.  
- Ablation Studies: More ablations needed to isolate contributions of components (e.g., skip connections, distributed training).  
- Hyperparameter Sensitivity: Missing analysis of controller RNN hyperparameter sensitivity (hidden size, learning rate, etc.).  
- Controller Choice: Clarify why a two-layer LSTM was chosen and whether alternatives (e.g., Transformers) were considered.  
- Reward Function: Justify using cubed validation accuracy and report whether other reward shaping strategies were tried.  
- Search Space Ranges: Explain how hyperparameter ranges (filter sizes, filter counts) were determined—prior knowledge or systematic search?  
- Baseline Function: Detail how EMA smoothing factor was chosen and whether different baselines affected results.  
- Scalability: Discuss limitations and potential strategies for scaling the method to larger datasets and architectures (e.g., ImageNet).  
- Conclusion: Important contribution that would be strengthened by addressing the above feedback and questions.
