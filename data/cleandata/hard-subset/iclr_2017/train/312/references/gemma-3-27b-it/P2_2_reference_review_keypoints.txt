- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): controller RNN generates architectures, child networks trained, validation accuracy used as RL reward.  
- Demonstrates NAS effectiveness on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, achieving competitive or state-of-the-art results.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; novel, compelling approach with strong empirical evidence.  
- Novel framing: treats architecture search as reinforcement learning using a recurrent controller, enabling variable-length architecture exploration.  
- Strong empirical results: NAS discovers architectures that often outperform human-designed models on tested benchmarks.  
- Well-motivated: clearly argues need for automated architecture design and relates to prior work (hyperparameter optimization, neuro-evolution, program synthesis).  
- Technical soundness: uses REINFORCE with a baseline and parameter server; introduces skip connections and extensions to recurrent architectures.  
- Reproducibility: authors plan to release code, which is important for validation and future work.  
- Request for deeper search-space analysis: clarify what architectures were favored and visualize search evolution.  
- Concern about computational cost: quantify GPU hours and discuss strategies to reduce resource requirements.  
- Need for more ablation studies to isolate impacts of components like skip connections.  
- Generalizability question: evaluate/adapt NAS for more datasets and tasks beyond CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.  
- Questions on controller initialization and exploration: how was initial search seeded and how to avoid local optima?  
- Ask about limitations, future research directions, and whether NAS is intended for one-time search or continuous adaptation, plus detailed resource breakdown.
