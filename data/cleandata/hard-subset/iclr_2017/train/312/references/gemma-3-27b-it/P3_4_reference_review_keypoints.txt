- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RNN “controller” generates architectures, trained with policy gradients using child-network validation accuracy as reward, evaluated on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — significant contribution with thorough empirical validation.  
- Novelty: Using an RNN controller to generate architectures and learning via REINFORCE is an innovative departure from prior NAS approaches.  
- Generality: Method applies to both CNNs and RNNs, demonstrating transferability across tasks.  
- Strong empirical results: Achieves state-of-the-art or competitive performance on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, with reported speed improvements on CIFAR-10.  
- Clarity and presentation: Paper is well-written, methodology and experiments are clearly explained, with useful related-work discussion and helpful diagrams.  
- Technical soundness: Appropriate use of policy gradients with baseline, and a well-explained parameter-server/asynchronous training setup; extensions (e.g., skip connections) are considered.  
- Search-space details lacking: Needs a clearer characterization of the total search space size and constraints used to make the search tractable.  
- Controller design underexplored: Controller architecture (two-layer LSTM, 35 units) seems arbitrary; an ablation on controller size/architecture is recommended.  
- Visualization could improve: Suggest more intuitive, graph-based visualizations of discovered architectures beyond appendix diagrams.  
- Reproducibility and robustness: Require sensitivity analysis to controller hyperparameters (learning rate, baseline, seeds) and clearer reproduction instructions despite code release.  
- Ablation studies needed: Formal ablation on skip connections and other architectural features (e.g., pooling) to quantify their impact.  
- Computational cost discussion insufficient: Paper should detail computational resources used and strategies to reduce expensive search costs.  
- Questions for authors: Request precise search-space size, controller priors, controller hyperparameter sensitivity, alternative scaling techniques and bottlenecks, generalization to other domains, and future directions (e.g., human constraints).  
- Overall conclusion: Strong, impactful AutoML contribution; addressing the suggested analyses and clarifications will further strengthen the paper.
