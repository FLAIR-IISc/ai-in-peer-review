- Proposes actor-critic RL for neural architecture search by treating DNNs as variable-length sequences.  
- Node selection framed as RL action; architecture evaluation error used as reward.  
- Employs an auto-regressive two-layer LSTM as both controller and critic.  
- Evaluated on two tasks and compared against multiple human-designed architectures.  
- Novel and exciting approach addressing challenges of hand-designing architectures.  
- Authors provide a detailed, clear description including necessary improvements.  
- Datasets and experiments appropriately demonstrate the method’s capability.  
- Interesting observed differences between generated and human-designed architectures.  
- Related work coverage and comparisons are handled well.  
- Reviewer requests data on training time and correlation between compute/time and model quality.  
- Reviewer requests results and role of human-bootstrapped models.  
- Overall assessment: excellent and interesting paper.
