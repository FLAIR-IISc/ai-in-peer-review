- Proposes using an RNN to generate network architectures and training the RNN with reinforcement learning for task-specific design.
- The idea is promising and experiments on two datasets show solid results.
- Pro: The RNN+RL approach to architecture generation is interesting and promising.
- Pro: Generated architectures resemble human-designed ones, suggesting compatibility with human expertise.
- Con: Training time is long even with substantial computing resources.
- Con: Experiments don’t demonstrate generality; need evaluation on other similar/different datasets, especially for sequential models.
- Overall: Nice paper but needs more experiments to prove advantages over human-designed models.
