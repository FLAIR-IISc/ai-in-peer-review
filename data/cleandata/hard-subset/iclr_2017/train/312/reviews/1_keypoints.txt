- Reviewer asks the authors to provide the exact hyperparameters and types of dropout (recurrent, embedding, etc.) used, since replication otherwise requires excessive trial-and-error.  
- Recommends releasing a base set of hyperparameters (as in Zaremba et al., 2014) to aid future work and reproducibility.  
- Requests that hyperparameter details be provided for other experiments as well (e.g., the character language model).  
- Notes the paper addresses an important problem—automating architecture search—and acknowledges the current high computational cost will likely decrease over time.  
- Praises the paper for evaluating on both vision and text benchmarks, demonstrating gains by exploring beyond standard RNN/CNN search spaces and enabling dataset-specific architectures without manual engineering.  
- Overall assessment: well written, strong results, and recommended for acceptance.
