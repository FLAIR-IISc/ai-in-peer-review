This paper introduces a novel approach to neural network architecture search, framed within an actor-critic reinforcement learning (RL) paradigm. The method represents deep neural networks (DNNs) as variable-length sequences and employs RL to discover optimal architectures, where the DNN itself functions as the actor. Node selection is defined as the action within the RL framework, and the resulting architecture’s evaluation error serves as the reward signal. An auto-regressive, two-layer LSTM network is utilized as both the controller and critic. The proposed method is evaluated on two distinct problems, with performance compared against a range of manually designed architectures.

This is a highly promising contribution! Manually designing network architectures is a challenging process, and it’s often difficult to assess how close hand-crafted networks are to optimal solutions. The presented method is genuinely novel, and the authors provide a thorough and detailed description, including a clear articulation of necessary improvements. The experimental results effectively demonstrate the method’s capabilities, and the comparison between learned and human-designed architectures is particularly insightful. The paper is exceptionally well-written and accessible, and the related work is comprehensively covered and effectively contextualized. 

Adding information regarding the training time required for the RL process, and exploring the relationship between training time/resource consumption and model quality, would further strengthen the work. It would also be valuable to investigate the performance of human-designed models initialized with pre-trained weights (“bootstrapped” models) and to explore potential human-RL collaborative approaches.

Overall, this is an excellent and compelling paper.
