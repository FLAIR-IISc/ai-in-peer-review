This paper introduces a novel approach leveraging Reinforcement Learning (RL) and Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) to automate network architecture design for task-specific applications. The core concept is compelling, and the presented experimental results across two datasets demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.

The strengths of this work include:
1. The combination of RNNs for network description generation and RL for RNN training represents a creative and potentially impactful direction.
2. The similarity between the generated architectures and those designed by human experts suggests alignment with established architectural principles and validates the approach.

However, the paper also has limitations:
1. The training process requires substantial computational resources and exhibits a lengthy training duration.
2. The evaluation lacks a comprehensive assessment of the generated architectures’ generalizability. Expanding the experimental evaluation to include additional, related datasets – particularly for the generated sequential models – would strengthen the conclusions.

In conclusion, this is a valuable contribution. Further experimentation is needed to fully establish the advantages of this automated approach compared to manually designed architectures.
