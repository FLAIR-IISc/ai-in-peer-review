

This paper proposes a reinforcement learning (RL) approach to neural architecture search, framing the problem as an actor-critic framework. The method treats deep neural networks (DNNs) as variable-length sequences, where the architecture search itself serves as the actor. Node selection constitutes an action within this RL context, and the evaluation error of the resulting architecture defines the reward signal. An autoregressive two-layer LSTM functions as both the controller and critic. The approach is evaluated on two benchmark tasks, with results compared against multiple human-designed architectures.

This work presents a highly promising contribution to automated neural architecture design. Manual architecture selection is notoriously challenging, and it remains unclear how close human-designed networks are to the optimal solution. The proposed method is novel and thoroughly described, with all critical implementation details and improvements clearly articulated. The experimental results adequately demonstrate the method’s capabilities, and the generated architectures reveal intriguing differences from human-designed counterparts. The paper is well-structured, accessible, and provides a comprehensive comparison with related work.

A minor consideration would be to include training time and resource requirements alongside model performance, as this would clarify the practical trade-offs of the approach. Additionally, comparing against human-designed baselines (e.g., architectures used to bootstrap the search) would further strengthen the evaluation. 

Overall, this is an excellent paper that advances the field with a clear, well-executed methodology and insightful results.
