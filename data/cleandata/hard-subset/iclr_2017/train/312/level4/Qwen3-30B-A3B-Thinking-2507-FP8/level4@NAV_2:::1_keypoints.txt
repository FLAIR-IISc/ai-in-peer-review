- Proposes Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to automatically generate neural network architectures.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, achieving state-of-the-art results that surpass human-designed architectures.  
- Claims improved computational efficiency compared to prior human-invented architectures.  
- Recommends acceptance, citing the method's novelty and strong motivation to automate architecture design.  
- Empirical results are rigorous and thorough, with experiments across multiple benchmarks showing superiority over existing approaches.  
- Situates work well in literature, building on reinforcement learning, network design, and hyperparameter optimization while distinguishing from prior methods.  
- Experiments include multiple control studies and validation across datasets, showing the method discovers novel, better-performing architectures.  
- Requests that authors provide exact hyperparameter settings for embedding and recurrent dropout (and for character language modeling) to aid reproducibility.
