

The paper states that after training the controller RNN, "the best RNN cell is selected based on validation perplexity, followed by a grid search over learning rate, weight initialization, dropout rates, and decay epoch. The optimal cell was then evaluated across three configurations and sizes to enhance capacity." To ensure reproducibility, please provide the specific hyperparameters (including learning rates, weight initialization schemes, and dropout types—e.g., recurrent, embedding, or feature dropout—as well as dropout rates) used in these experiments. Without this detail, replication would require extensive trial-and-error, which contradicts best practices established in the field (e.g., Zaremba et al., 2014). This transparency is critical for future work, especially for the character language modeling experiments and other benchmarks.

This work addresses a timely and significant challenge: automating neural architecture search. While the current computational cost is high, this barrier is expected to diminish as hardware and optimization techniques advance. The paper demonstrates strong results across diverse tasks, including vision (e.g., CIFAR-10, ImageNet) and text (e.g., Penn Treebank, PTB), showing that exploring beyond conventional RNN and CNN architectures yields meaningful gains. The approach not only matches but often exceeds human-designed architectures, suggesting a viable path to dataset-specific architecture optimization without manual engineering at each stage. The evaluation on multiple benchmarks sufficiently validates the method’s competitiveness and potential to surpass human intuition in architectural design.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with compelling empirical results that advance the state of automated architecture search. The methodology is sound, and the findings contribute meaningfully to the field. Given the importance of the topic, the robust experimental validation, and the potential for future impact, I recommend acceptance. Providing the requested hyperparameters would further strengthen the paper’s contribution and reproducibility.
