- Proposes using an RNN to generate network architectures and training it with reinforcement learning — a promising core idea.  
- Experimental results on two datasets show robust performance.  
- Strength: The RNN+RL approach to architecture search is novel and well-motivated.  
- Strength: Generated architectures display structural similarities to human-designed networks, indicating compatibility with human expertise.  
- Limitation: The RL training process is computationally intensive, requiring substantial time and resources.  
- Limitation: Experiments lack evidence of generalizability; performance on other similar or sequential datasets is untested.  
- Overall: A promising contribution that requires additional experiments to demonstrate advantages over manually designed architectures.
