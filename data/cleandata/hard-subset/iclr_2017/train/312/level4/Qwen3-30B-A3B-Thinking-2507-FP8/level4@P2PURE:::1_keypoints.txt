- Authors must provide the specific hyperparameters used (including dropout types like recurrent/embedding dropout); current omission hampers reproducibility and forces trial-and-error.  
- Releasing a baseline set of hyperparameters (as in Zaremba et al., 2014) is necessary, including for character language modeling experiments.  
- The paper tackles automated architecture search, an important problem, acknowledging high computational cost that is likely to decrease with future tech.  
- Experiments span vision and text benchmarks and explore beyond conventional RNN/CNN search spaces, demonstrating performance gains.  
- While more datasets would be beneficial, the current scope convincingly shows the method can match or exceed human-designed architectures and tailor models to datasets.  
- Well-written paper with robust results; recommendation for acceptance.
