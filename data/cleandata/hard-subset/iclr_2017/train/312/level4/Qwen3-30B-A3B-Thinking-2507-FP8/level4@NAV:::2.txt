

This paper introduces a reinforcement learning approach for discovering optimal neural network architectures using an actor-critic framework. The method models deep neural networks as variable-length sequences, with the architecture search process framed as a reinforcement learning problem where the target architecture serves as the actor. Node selection constitutes the action space, and the evaluation error of the resulting architecture defines the reward signal. A two-layer autoregressive LSTM functions as both the controller and critic. The approach is validated across two distinct problem domains and compared against multiple human-designed architectures.

This represents a highly compelling contribution. Hand-selecting architectures is notoriously challenging, and it remains unclear how close hand-designed networks approach optimality. The proposed method is genuinely novel. The authors provide a thorough and well-structured description of the technique, including all necessary refinements. The experimental evaluation effectively demonstrates the method's capabilities. The contrasting architectures generated by the method versus human designs offer particularly insightful observations. The paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and accessible, with comprehensive coverage and critical comparison against relevant literature.

It would be valuable to include data on training time requirements and the relationship between computational demands and model performance. Additionally, an analysis of human-bootstrapped model performance would be insightful. Overall, this is an excellent and highly interesting paper.
