- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to automatically design neural network architectures.  
- Shows NAS can discover architectures that rival or surpass human-designed networks on image classification and language modeling.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art empirical results: 3.65% test error on CIFAR-10 (0.09% improvement) and 62.4 test perplexity on Penn Treebank (3.6 improvement).  
- Controller RNN generates architectural descriptions as token sequences and is trained via policy gradient to maximize validation accuracy.  
- CIFAR-10 results demonstrate discovered architectures with efficient structures and skip connections.  
- Penn Treebank experiments produce novel recurrent cells that outperform LSTMs.  
- Transfer learning results to character language modeling and machine translation support the generality of discovered architectures.
