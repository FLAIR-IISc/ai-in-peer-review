- Proposes an actor-critic RL approach for neural architecture search, modeling architectures as variable-length sequences with discrete actions.
- Uses a two-layer LSTM as the actor and a separate critic network to estimate action-value functions.
- Reward signal is derived from the architecture’s evaluation error.
- Evaluated on two benchmark tasks and shows competitive performance versus multiple human-designed architectures.
- Addresses the challenge of labor-intensive, suboptimal manual architecture design with a principled, scalable RL framework.
- Paper presents a clear, technically sound methodology with detailed controller architecture, reward formulation, and search procedure.
- Includes key improvements over prior work and insightful structural comparisons between machine-generated and human-designed networks.
- Experiments and chosen datasets/metrics provide robust evidence of the method’s efficacy.
- Writing is exceptionally clear and accessible while maintaining technical rigor.
- Main strength: methodological novelty and practical applicability of bridging RL with architecture search.
- Suggestion 1: Report training time and computational resource requirements, and discuss correlation between search time, resources, and model quality.
- Suggestion 2: Investigate human-initiated search (starting from human-designed baselines) to assess effects on convergence, performance, and search complexity.
- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed contribution relevant to ICLR that merits publication.
