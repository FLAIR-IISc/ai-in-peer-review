- Presents an automated RNN architecture search that achieves competitive or superior performance on vision and language benchmarks.
- Results are compelling and the problem is significant, demonstrating the promise of automating architecture design.
- Major reproducibility concern: critical hyperparameter details are missing (ranges, initialization schemes, and dropout types).
- Paper omits the exact type of dropout used (recurrent, embedding, or both), which is essential for replication.
- The lack of base hyperparameter release contradicts standard practice (e.g., Zaremba et al. 2014) and forces impractical trial-and-error.
- The same transparency issue affects the character-level language modeling experiments.
- Authors should provide full hyperparameter and dropout specifications in the appendix/supplementary material.
- Computational cost is high but acceptable given likely future efficiency gains and hardware advances.
- The method convincingly outperforms human-designed architectures across diverse tasks (CIFAR-10, IMDB, Penn Treebank, WikiText-2).
- Breadth of benchmarks and ablation studies support the claim of generality and the search approach’s validity.
- Writing and technical execution are clear and sound; acceptance is recommended pending the minor reproducibility revision.
