- Proposes an actor-critic reinforcement learning approach for neural architecture search, modeling DNNs as variable-length sequences.  
- Architecture search agent acts as the actor (node selection as actions) and uses evaluation error as the reward signal.  
- Controller is an auto-regressive two-layer LSTM; critic provides value estimation.  
- Evaluated on two benchmark problems and compared against several human-designed architectures.  
- Addresses the challenge of manual architecture design and uncertainty about suboptimal hand-crafted networks.  
- Method is genuinely novel, thoroughly described, and includes necessary technical improvements.  
- Experimental setup effectively demonstrates capabilities and provides valuable comparisons to human-designed architectures.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, accessible, well-written, and situates work against related literature.  
- Suggested future work: analyze training time and compute vs. model-quality trade-offs.  
- Suggested future work: evaluate architectures initialized with human knowledge (human-initiated architectures).  
- Overall assessment: excellent and highly interesting contribution to the field.
