

The paper states that "[a]fter the controller RNN completes training, we select the best RNN cell based on the lowest validation perplexity and then perform a grid search over learning rate, weight initialization, dropout rates, and decay epochs. The optimal cell is subsequently evaluated across three distinct configurations and model sizes to enhance its capacity." Could the authors provide the specific hyperparameters used, including the dropout type (e.g., recurrent dropout, embedding dropout)? Without these details, replication would necessitate extensive trial-and-error. As demonstrated in "Recurrent Neural Network Regularization" (Zaremba et al., 2014), releasing a baseline set of hyperparameters significantly aids future research in the field. This would similarly be necessary for other experiments, such as character language modeling.  

The paper addresses a critical area in our field: automating architecture search. While the technique currently demands substantial computational resources, this trade-off is expected to improve with advancing technology. The work examines both standard vision and text tasks across multiple benchmark datasets, demonstrating performance gains from exploring beyond conventional RNN and CNN search spaces. Although expanding to additional datasets would be valuable, the current scope sufficiently establishes that the method not only competes with human architectural intuition but may surpass it. This also suggests a pathway to tailor architectures to specific datasets without requiring manual engineering at each stage.  

This is a well-written paper on a compelling topic with robust results. I recommend acceptance.
