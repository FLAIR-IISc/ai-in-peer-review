- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (controller RNN + policy gradients) to automatically discover architectures, achieving SOTA on CIFAR-10 and PTB and a novel recurrent cell beating LSTM.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Strong empirical evidence: outperforms prior SOTA on CIFAR-10 and PTB with thorough ablations and comparisons to random search.  
- Method is well-motivated and flexible, applicable to both convolutional and recurrent architectures.  
- Sound technical implementation: uses skip connections, parallel training, and a baseline to reduce policy gradient variance.  
- Transfer claim: discovered recurrent cell generalizes to character language modeling but lacks reported hyperparameters.  
- Reproducibility concern: PTB grid-search hyperparameters (learning rates, dropout, weight init, decay epochs) are not specified.  
- Metric ambiguity: unclear what "1.05x faster" relative to DenseNet measures (inference vs. training vs. wall-clock).  
- Request for clarification on how skip connections are encoded by the controller and details of the attention mechanism versus residual network skips.
