

The paper reports that "[a]fter the controller RNN is done training, we take the best RNN cell according to the lowest validation perplexity and then perform a grid search over learning rate, weight initialization, dropout rates, and decay epoch. The best cell found was then evaluated across three different configurations and sizes to increase capacity." To facilitate replication, could the authors please provide the specific hyperparameters and dropout types used (e.g., recurrent dropout, embedding dropout)? Without these details, replication would require extensive trial-and-error. As demonstrated in "Recurrent Neural Network Regularization" (Zaremba et al., 2014), releasing a baseline hyperparameter set significantly aids the broader community’s future work. This would also be valuable for other experiments, such as the character-level language modeling results.

The paper addresses a critical direction in our field: automating neural architecture search. While the current approach is computationally intensive, this trade-off is likely to improve substantially as hardware and optimization techniques advance. The work evaluates on both standard computer vision and text tasks, covering multiple benchmark datasets and demonstrating tangible gains from exploring beyond conventional RNN and CNN search spaces. Though expanding to additional datasets would be welcome, the current scope is sufficiently comprehensive to establish that automated search not only matches but may exceed human-designed architectures. This suggests a promising path toward dataset-specific architecture tailoring without manual engineering at each stage.

The paper is well-written, tackles an important problem, and presents compelling results. I recommend acceptance.
