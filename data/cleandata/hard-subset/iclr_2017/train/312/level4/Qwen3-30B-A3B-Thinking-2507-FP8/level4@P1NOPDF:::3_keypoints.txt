- Method: uses an RNN to generate network architecture descriptions and reinforcement learning to optimize the RNN’s outputs for task-specific architecture design.  
- Result: experiments on two benchmark datasets demonstrate the method’s technical soundness.  
- Strength: novel RNN+RL combination presents a promising direction for automated architecture search.  
- Strength: generated architectures show structural similarities to human-designed networks, suggesting effective alignment with expert designs.  
- Limitation: training is computationally intensive and time-consuming, even with substantial resources, which may hinder practical adoption.  
- Limitation: experiments do not sufficiently demonstrate generalizability; additional evaluations on more datasets, particularly sequential tasks, are needed.  
- Recommendation: perform more experiments across diverse datasets and tasks to establish advantages over human-designed models.
