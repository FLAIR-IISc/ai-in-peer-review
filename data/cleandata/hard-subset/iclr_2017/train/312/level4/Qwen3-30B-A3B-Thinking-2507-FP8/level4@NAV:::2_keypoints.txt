- Proposes an RL-based neural architecture search using an actor-critic framework: networks as variable-length sequences, node selection as actions, evaluation error as reward, with a two-layer autoregressive LSTM as controller and critic.
- Validated on two distinct problem domains and compared against several human-designed architectures.
- Method is novel and tackles the important problem that hand-designed architectures may be far from optimal.
- Paper provides a thorough, well-structured, and clear description including necessary refinements and comparisons to related work.
- Experimental evaluation convincingly demonstrates the approach; contrasts between discovered and human designs are particularly insightful.
- Reviewer requests reporting of training time and the compute vs. performance trade-off.
- Reviewer suggests analysis of human-bootstrapped model performance.
- Overall verdict: an excellent, highly interesting contribution.
