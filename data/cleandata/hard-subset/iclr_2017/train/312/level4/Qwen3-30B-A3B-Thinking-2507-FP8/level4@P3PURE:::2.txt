

This paper proposes a novel approach for discovering optimal neural network architectures using an actor-critic reinforcement learning framework. The method models the DNN as a variable-length sequence, where the architecture search process is framed as a reinforcement learning task. Specifically, the architecture search agent acts as the actor, with node selection constituting the action, and the evaluation error of the resulting architecture serving as the reward signal. The controller employs an auto-regressive two-layer LSTM, while the critic component provides value estimation. The approach is evaluated on two distinct benchmark problems, with performance compared against several human-designed architectures.  

This work addresses a significant challenge in deep learning: the difficulty of manually designing near-optimal network structures and the uncertainty regarding how suboptimal hand-crafted architectures may be. The proposed method is genuinely novel, and the authors provide a thorough, well-structured description of the methodology, including all necessary technical improvements. The experimental setup effectively demonstrates the method's capabilities, and the comparison between generated and human-designed architectures yields valuable insights. The paper is exceptionally clear, accessible, and well-written, with comprehensive coverage and a critical comparison against related literature.  

For future work, it would be valuable to include analysis of the training time requirements and the trade-off between computational resources and model quality. Additionally, investigating the performance of architectures initialized with human knowledge (human-initiated architectures) could further contextualize the method's effectiveness. Overall, this is an excellent and highly interesting contribution to the field.
