- Paper proposes Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller trained with RL to generate neural network architectures, evaluated on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection despite promising technical idea.  
- Main critique: no statistical significance tests for reported performance gains (CIFAR-10 and PTB).  
- CIFAR-10 improvement (3.65% vs 3.74%) and speed claim (1.05x) lack hypothesis testing or confidence intervals.  
- PTB improvement (62.4 vs 66.0 perplexity) is reported without statistical validation.  
- Insufficient ablation studies and limited comparison to human-designed baselines.  
- Baseline selection criticized (compares to DenseNet but omits more recent SOTA like ResNet/EfficientNet).  
- Language-model baselines are incomplete (compares to LSTM but not GRU or Transformer-based models).  
- Lack of evidence for generalizability: only CIFAR-10, PTB, and a single transfer to PTB character modeling.  
- Computational efficiency concerns: method used 800 GPUs and 12,800 child network evaluations, which is prohibitively expensive for most researchers.  
- Paper lacks analysis of cost-benefit tradeoff between compute expense and marginal performance gains.  
- Requested author actions: provide statistical validation, broaden baselines, demonstrate generalizability across more datasets, and analyze computational cost vs performance.  
- Specific reviewer questions: supply statistical tests; justify DenseNet baseline vs newer models like EfficientNet; clarify novelty vs residual architectures; estimate training time on standard (1–2) GPUs and compare compute to human-designed architectures.  
- Conclusion: without addressing these issues, the claims of superiority for automatically designed architectures remain unverified.
