

This paper introduces a novel approach leveraging reinforcement learning (RL) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs) to automate network architecture design for targeted tasks. The core concept demonstrates significant promise, with robust experimental validation on two benchmark datasets.  

Key strengths include:  
1. The innovative integration of RNNs to generate network descriptions and RL to optimize the RNN training process, offering a compelling methodological contribution.  
2. The generated architectures exhibit structural alignment with human-crafted designs, indicating effective compatibility with established domain expertise.  

Notable limitations are:  
1. The substantial training duration remains a concern, persisting even when utilizing extensive computational resources.  
2. The experimental scope lacks demonstration of architectural generalizability; extending performance evaluation to additional, yet distinct, datasets—particularly sequential model benchmarks—would strengthen the claims.  

Overall, this represents a strong contribution. However, additional experiments are required to conclusively establish its advantages over manually designed architectures.
