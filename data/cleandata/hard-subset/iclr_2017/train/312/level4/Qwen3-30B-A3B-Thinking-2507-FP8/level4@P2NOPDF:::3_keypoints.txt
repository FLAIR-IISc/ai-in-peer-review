- Proposes a novel framework using an RNN to generate network architectures and RL to optimize the generator.  
- Conceptually novel integration that bridges algorithmic design and task-specific learning.  
- Generated architectures resemble human-designed models, indicating capture of meaningful design principles.  
- Strong experimental performance on two benchmark datasets, showing promise for automated architecture search.  
- Training is computationally intensive; authors should report exact hardware and per-iteration/training durations.  
- Need discussion of potential optimizations to reduce computational overhead for broader practicality.  
- Generalizability is untested beyond the two benchmarks; additional datasets with different modalities/distributions are required.  
- Recommend testing on sequential/time-series tasks to demonstrate adaptability to other model classes.  
- Missing explicit comparisons against state-of-the-art human-designed baselines in terms of accuracy and parameter efficiency.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on adding training cost analysis, expanding experiments to at least one additional dataset, and comparing to human-designed baselines.
