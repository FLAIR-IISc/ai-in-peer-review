- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RL-based controller RNN that generates variable-length network architectures and is trained to maximize expected accuracy.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art CIFAR-10 performance: 3.65% test error (0.09% improvement) and 1.05x faster than previous best.  
- Discovers a novel recurrent cell on Penn Treebank achieving 62.4 perplexity (3.6 improvement) with strong transferability to character language modeling.  
- Shows controller can learn skip connections and variable layer structures, which materially improve performance over architectures lacking these features.  
- Ablation studies and comparisons (including random search) provide strong evidence of robustness and effectiveness.  
- Advances the field by enabling variable-length architecture search and clearly distinguishing from related hyperparameter and neuro-evolution work.  
- Technical descriptions of the controller, policy-gradient training, and extensions for skip connections/recurrent architectures are clear and coherent.  
- Recommendation: accept — addresses an important automation challenge in deep learning and shows strong empirical results across benchmarks.  
- Reproducibility concern: paper needs detailed hyperparameter specifications (dropout types/rates, weight initialization, learning-rate schedules, weight decay) used for final results.  
- Computational-cost clarification needed: quantify total GPU-hours and discuss potential ways to reduce the method’s high computational expense.  
- Request for deeper analysis: provide qualitative/quantitative insight into which architectural features (beyond skip connections) drive performance gains.  
- Suggest adding a brief comparison to contemporaneous work by Zoph and Le (2017) to better situate contributions.  
- Specific author questions: clarify exact dropout variants and rates used for Penn Treebank; list grid-search values tested for CIFAR-10 final model; explain how the controller’s schedule for increasing child-network layers was chosen or optimized.
