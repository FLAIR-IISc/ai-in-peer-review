

This paper proposes using reinforcement learning (RL) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs) to automatically design network architectures for specific tasks. The core idea is promising, and the method demonstrates strong empirical performance on two datasets.  

The paper's strengths include:  
1. The novel approach of employing an RNN to generate network architecture descriptions and using RL to train the RNN is both interesting and promising.  
2. The generated architectures exhibit structural similarities to human-designed counterparts, indicating compatibility between human expertise and the synthesized network structures.  

Key limitations are:  
1. The training process is computationally intensive and requires significant time, even with substantial computational resources.  
2. The experiments lack sufficient validation of the generated architectures' generalizability. It would be valuable to evaluate their performance on other similar but distinct datasets, particularly for sequential modeling tasks.  

Overall, this is a well-motivated paper with promising results. However, additional experiments are needed to conclusively demonstrate its advantages over human-designed models.
