

This paper proposes a novel approach using reinforcement learning (RL) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs) to automatically design network architectures for specific tasks. The core idea—employing an RNN to generate architectural descriptions and RL to optimize this process—is innovative and well-motivated. Experimental results on two benchmark datasets demonstrate the method’s robust performance, suggesting strong potential for automated architecture design.  

**Strengths:**  
1. **Conceptual Novelty:** The integration of RNNs for architectural description and RL for optimization presents a compelling framework. This approach effectively bridges algorithmic design with task-specific learning, offering a promising direction for automated machine learning.  
2. **Human-Like Architectures:** The generated network structures exhibit clear similarities to manually designed architectures, indicating compatibility with human expertise. This alignment suggests the method captures meaningful design principles, potentially reducing reliance on extensive domain knowledge.  

**Areas for Improvement:**  
1. **Computational Efficiency:** The training process is notably time-intensive, even with substantial computational resources. The authors should clarify the exact hardware setup (e.g., GPU count, training duration per iteration) and discuss potential optimizations to reduce overhead. Without addressing this, the method’s practicality for broader adoption remains questionable.  
2. **Generalizability Assessment:** The experiments lack validation of the generated architectures’ generalizability. It is critical to evaluate performance on *additional* datasets—particularly those with distinct characteristics from the two used (e.g., different domains or data distributions). Testing on sequential models (e.g., time-series tasks) would further strengthen the claim that the approach is adaptable beyond the specific benchmarks presented.  

**Overall Assessment:**  
This is a well-executed and promising study that advances automated architecture search. The results are convincing within the scope of the experiments, and the methodology aligns with current trends in neural architecture search. However, to fully establish its value, the authors must address the computational cost and demonstrate generalizability across diverse tasks. Including comparisons against state-of-the-art *human-designed* models on the same datasets would significantly bolster the argument for the method’s superiority.  

**Recommendation:**  
**Accept (with minor revisions)**. The paper makes a meaningful contribution to automated ML, but the reviewers recommend:  
- Adding a detailed analysis of training time/cost (e.g., wall-clock time, FLOPs).  
- Expanding experiments to at least one additional dataset (preferably with different data modalities or task structures).  
- Explicitly comparing generated architectures against human-designed baselines (e.g., accuracy, parameter efficiency).  

These additions would solidify the paper’s claims and enhance its impact for the ICLR community.
