

This paper introduces a novel reinforcement learning approach for automatically discovering optimal neural network architectures, framed within an actor-critic paradigm. The method models architecture search as a sequential decision process, treating the neural network structure as a variable-length sequence. Here, the actor (a two-layer autoregressive LSTM controller) proposes architectural components (e.g., layer types and connections), while the critic evaluates the resulting architecture’s validation error as the reward signal. The framework is evaluated on two benchmark tasks and compared against multiple human-designed architectures.  

The work addresses a critical challenge in deep learning: the difficulty of manually designing high-performing networks and the uncertainty around how suboptimal human-curated architectures may be. The proposed method is highly innovative, offering a systematic RL-based alternative to ad hoc design. The authors provide exceptionally clear and thorough technical exposition, meticulously detailing all necessary methodological enhancements. Experimental results effectively demonstrate the approach’s capabilities, with generated architectures revealing meaningful distinctions from human-designed counterparts—providing valuable insights into architectural design principles. The literature review is comprehensive, thoughtfully contrasting this work with prior advances in neural architecture search.  

The paper’s clarity and accessibility are outstanding, making complex ideas readily understandable for a broad audience. The writing is precise, well-structured, and free of unnecessary jargon, significantly enhancing its impact.  

To further strengthen the contribution, the authors could include a detailed analysis of training time and resource requirements, alongside their correlation with model quality. This would offer practical insights into the method’s efficiency. Additionally, exploring the performance of human-initialized architectures (e.g., starting from human-designed baselines) would deepen the evaluation of the approach’s scalability and robustness.  

Overall, this is a compelling and well-executed paper that makes a significant advance in automated neural architecture design. The methodology is sound, the experiments are rigorous, and the presentation is exemplary. It represents a strong candidate for acceptance at ICLR 2017.
