- Proposes using an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to automatically design network architectures for specific tasks.  
- Core idea is promising and shows strong empirical performance on two datasets.  
- Strength: Novel approach of an RNN generating architecture descriptions trained via RL.  
- Strength: Generated architectures share structural similarities with human-designed networks, suggesting compatibility with expert designs.  
- Limitation: Training is computationally intensive and time-consuming, even with substantial resources.  
- Limitation: Experiments do not sufficiently validate generalizability; need evaluation on other similar but distinct datasets, especially for sequential tasks.  
- Overall: Well-motivated and promising, but additional experiments are required to conclusively demonstrate advantages over human-designed models.
