Proposes an actor-critic reinforcement learning method for neural architecture search (NAS).

Models DNNs as variable-length sequences and treats the architecture search process as the actor.

Frames node selection as actions and uses the resulting architecture's evaluation error as the reward.

Implements a two-layer auto-regressive LSTM that serves as both controller (actor) and critic.

Evaluated on two tasks and compared against multiple human-designed architectures.

Method is novel, well-motivated, and includes necessary methodological improvements.

Experimental setup adequately demonstrates capabilities and highlights structural differences from human designs.

Paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and situates itself effectively against related work.

Recommendation to report training time and analyze correlation between compute/resources and model quality.

Suggestion to investigate performance of human-bootstrapped models within the proposed framework.

Overall assessment: excellent and highly interesting paper.
