- Proposes an RL-based neural architecture search framed as an actor-critic problem, treating DNNs as variable-length sequences with node selection as actions and evaluation error as reward.  
- Uses an autoregressive two-layer LSTM as both the controller and the critic.  
- Evaluated on two benchmark tasks and compared against multiple human-designed architectures.  
- Novel and promising contribution to automated architecture design.  
- Methodology is thoroughly described with critical implementation details and improvements clearly articulated.  
- Experimental results demonstrate the method’s capabilities and show generated architectures that differ intriguingly from human designs.  
- Paper is well-structured, accessible, and includes comprehensive related-work comparisons.  
- Minor suggestion: report training time and computational resource requirements to clarify practical trade-offs.  
- Minor suggestion: include comparisons against human-designed baselines used to bootstrap the search.  
- Overall verdict: excellent paper that advances the field with a clear, well-executed methodology and insightful results.
