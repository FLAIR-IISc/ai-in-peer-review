- Paper omits specific hyperparameters used in the grid search (learning rates, weight initialization schemes, dropout rates, decay epochs).  
- Authors should clarify the dropout type(s) used (recurrent dropout, embedding dropout, or both).  
- Missing hyperparameter details impede reproducibility and force extensive trial-and-error.  
- Recommend releasing a base hyperparameter configuration (as in Zaremba et al.) to aid reproducibility, including for character-level experiments.  
- Paper tackles the important problem of automating neural architecture search.  
- Computational cost is currently high but expected to decrease with advancing hardware.  
- Strong empirical results across vision and text benchmarks, showing gains from exploring beyond conventional RNN/CNN search spaces.  
- Technique is competitive with or may surpass human architectural intuition, enabling dataset-specific architectures without manual tuning.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, presents compelling evidence, and is recommended for acceptance.
