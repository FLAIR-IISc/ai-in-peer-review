- Introduces Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate neural network architectures.  
- Demonstrates strong results: CIFAR-10 3.65% test error (0.09% better than prior SOTA) and Penn Treebank 62.4 perplexity (3.6 better).  
- Paper is well-situated in hyperparameter optimization and NAS literature and gives thorough methodological details (controller, training, search space).  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Treats architectures as variable-length sequences generated by the controller RNN, using validation accuracy as the RL reward.  
- Technical contributions include a controller that generates skip connections and branched layers and adaptation to design recurrent cells.  
- Empirical advantages: CIFAR-10 model is 1.05x faster than prior SOTA and Penn Treebank model outperforms established LSTM baselines.  
- Evaluation is rigorous, including ablation studies and transfer learning experiments.  
- Suggestion: report computational resources required (total wall-clock time and GPU hours) to train the controller.  
- Suggestion: compare against human-designed architectures that have undergone extensive manual tuning to better assess human effort vs. compute tradeoffs.  
- Suggestion: include a brief discussion of limitations in the search space design and directions to address them.
