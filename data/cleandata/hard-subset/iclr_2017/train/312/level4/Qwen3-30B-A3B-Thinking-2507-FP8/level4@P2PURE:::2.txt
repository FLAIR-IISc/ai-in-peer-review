

This paper proposes a reinforcement learning approach for discovering optimal neural network architectures, framed within an actor-critic methodology. The method models deep neural networks (DNNs) as variable-length sequences, where the architecture search process is treated as an actor. Specifically, node selection constitutes an action within the reinforcement learning (RL) framework, and the evaluation error of the resulting architecture serves as the reward signal. A two-layer auto-regressive LSTM functions as both the controller (actor) and critic. The approach is evaluated on two distinct tasks and compared against multiple human-designed architectures.

This is an exciting contribution. Hand-selecting architectures is challenging, and it remains difficult to assess how close hand-designed networks are to optimality. The proposed method is novel, and the authors provide a thorough, well-motivated description of the approach, including all necessary improvements. The experimental setup adequately demonstrates the method's capabilities. It is particularly insightful to observe the structural differences between the generated architectures and those designed by humans. The paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and accessible, with comprehensive coverage and effective contrast against relevant literature.

It would be valuable to include training time data and to analyze the correlation between training time/resources and model quality. Additionally, it would be interesting to examine the performance of human-bootstrapped models within the framework. Overall, this is an excellent and highly interesting paper.
