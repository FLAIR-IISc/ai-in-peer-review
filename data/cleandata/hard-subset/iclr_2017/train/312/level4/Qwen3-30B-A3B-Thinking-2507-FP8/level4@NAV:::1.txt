

The paper states that "[a]fter the controller RNN completes training, the optimal RNN cell is selected based on minimal validation perplexity, followed by a grid search across learning rate, weight initialization, dropout rates, and decay epoch. The selected cell is then evaluated across three distinct configurations and model sizes to enhance capacity."  
To facilitate replication, could the authors provide the specific hyperparameters and dropout types employed (e.g., recurrent dropout, embedding dropout)? Omitting these details would necessitate extensive trial-and-error replication, contrary to best practices exemplified in "Recurrent Neural Network Regularization" (Zaremba et al., 2014), where sharing baseline hyperparameters significantly aids reproducibility and future research. This transparency is equally essential for other experiments, including character language modeling.  

The paper addresses a critical area in our field: automating neural architecture search. While the current computational demands are substantial, this cost is expected to diminish with advancing technology. The work demonstrates compelling results across standard vision and text tasks, leveraging diverse benchmark datasets to establish gains beyond conventional RNN and CNN search spaces. Although additional datasets would further strengthen the case, the current scope sufficiently demonstrates that automated search not only matches but potentially surpasses human-designed architectures, enabling dataset-specific customization without manual engineering at each stage.  

This well-structured paper presents an engaging topic with robust empirical validation. I recommend acceptance.
