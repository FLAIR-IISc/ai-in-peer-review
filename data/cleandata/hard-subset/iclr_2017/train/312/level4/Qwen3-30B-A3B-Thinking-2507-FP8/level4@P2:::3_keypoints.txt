- Introduces Neural Architecture Search: a controller RNN that generates network architectures via reinforcement learning.  
- Controller is trained to maximize child-network validation accuracy and supports variable-length architectures, skip connections, and novel recurrent cells.  
- Demonstrated competitive or superior performance to human-designed models on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.  
- CIFAR-10: discovered architecture achieves 3.65% test error (0.09% better) and is 1.05x faster; PTB: discovered recurrent cell yields 62.4 perplexity (3.6 better) and transfers to char LM and MT.  
- Strong empirical validation with ablation studies and comparisons to random search.  
- Thorough implementation details and distributed training/hyperparameter documentation improve reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: accept — novel, well-motivated approach that bridges human expertise and automated design.  
- Concern: high computational cost; requests detailed analysis of GPU-hours and scalability improvements.  
- Concern: limited generalization evidence beyond CIFAR-10 and PTB; suggests experiments on ImageNet and additional benchmarks.  
- Concern: limited interpretability of discovered architectures; requests visualizations and qualitative comparisons.  
- Question: how does the method's total computational cost compare to time spent by human experts iterating designs?  
- Question: does the recurrent cell's generalization extend beyond PTB to other domains/datasets?  
- Question: beyond depth, which architectural features most contributed to CIFAR-10 performance and how can they inform human design?  
- Overall: significant AutoML contribution with impressive results; addressing computational cost, generalization, and interpretability would strengthen the paper.
