- Introduces Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller trained with REINFORCE that generates child network architectures evaluated by validation performance.
- Applied to CIFAR-10 (CNN design) and Penn Treebank (RNN cell design), discovering architectures from scratch.
- Decision: Accept.
- Novelty/significance: a novel, general framework automating architecture engineering by framing architecture generation as a sequential decision problem.
- Strong empirical validation: finds state-of-the-art or highly competitive architectures, including an RNN cell that outperforms LSTM and transfers to other tasks.
- Controller-based search supports variable-length architectures and explores a vast search space beyond fixed-length hyperparameter methods.
- Comparison to random search shows the policy gradient controller systematically finds better architectures over time.
- Major weakness: very high computational cost (thousands of child models; e.g., 12,800 CIFAR-10 models using 800 GPUs), limiting accessibility.
- Suggestion: evaluate generality of the discovered CIFAR-10 CNN on other image datasets (e.g., CIFAR-100, SVHN).
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis of the search process (evolution of hyperparameter distributions and controller output probabilities).
- Suggestion: discuss sensitivity to search-space design and include ablations on choices like filter sizes and layer types.
- Question: can the approach be made more efficient (weight-sharing, inheritance, surrogate performance predictors)?
- Question: rationale and sensitivity for reward shaping (cubing validation accuracy in CIFAR-10 reward).
- Question: frequency of compilation failures and whether the controller learned to avoid invalid configurations.
