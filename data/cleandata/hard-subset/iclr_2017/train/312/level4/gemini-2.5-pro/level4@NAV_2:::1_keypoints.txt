- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RL-based method where an RNN controller generates network architectures and is rewarded by child network validation accuracy.  
- Demonstrates NAS can design both convolutional networks and recurrent cells from scratch.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art or highly competitive results on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank benchmarks.  
- Strong recommendation to accept based on elegant, principled method and extensive empirical results.  
- Method is flexible: RNN controller generates variable-length hyperparameter strings, enabling exploration of large search spaces including skip connections.  
- Moves beyond traditional hyperparameter optimization techniques by searching modern architectural features.  
- Empirical validation is thorough and convincing across vision (CIFAR-10) and language (Penn Treebank) tasks.  
- Discovery of a novel recurrent cell for Penn Treebank highlights NAS’s ability to find effective, novel architectural motifs.  
- Comparison against random search shows the policy-gradient controller effectively learns and outperforms a strong baseline.  
- Primary weakness: insufficient detail to fully reproduce final state-of-the-art models.  
- Authors performed a secondary grid search over hyperparameters (learning rate, weight decay, dropout) but did not report the final configurations.  
- Request that authors provide specific training hyperparameters (final learning rate, weight initialization, dropout types/rates) for CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank (word- and character-level) to enable replication.
