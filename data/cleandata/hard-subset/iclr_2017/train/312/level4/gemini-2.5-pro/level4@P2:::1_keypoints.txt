- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RNN controller trained with policy gradients to generate neural network architectures.  
- Shows strong CIFAR-10 results: discovered CNN achieves 3.65% test error and is ~1.05x faster than DenseNet.  
- Shows strong PTB results: discovered recurrent cell achieves perplexity 62.4, outperforming LSTM and other baselines.  
- Demonstrates transferability: PTB-discovered cell generalizes to character-level modeling and improves machine translation.  
- Recommendation: Accept — approach is novel, significant, and could reshape architecture design.  
- Empirical validation is comprehensive and convincing across multiple challenging benchmarks.  
- Method is clearly distinguished from standard hyperparameter search and neuro-evolution via variable-length generation and scalability.  
- Control experiment confirms the controller outperforms random search, indicating it learns to navigate the search space.  
- Reproducibility concern: final best-model hyperparameters (learning rate, weight decay, dropout types/rates, decay schedule, etc.) are not fully reported.  
- Practicality concern: computational cost is substantial (e.g., ~800 GPUs); authors should discuss routes to reduce cost (e.g., weight sharing, performance prediction).
