- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): a controller RNN generates architectures and is trained with REINFORCE using child-network validation performance as reward.
- Demonstrates NAS can discover architectures from scratch for both CNNs and RNN cells.
- Decision: Accept — recommended due to novelty and strong empirical results.
- Novelty/significance: Frames architecture design as an RL-based sequential decision process, a paradigm shift from manual design.
- Exceptional empirical results: achieves state-of-the-art on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, rivaling or surpassing human-designed models.
- Methodological soundness: clear formulation, use of attention-like mechanisms for skip connections, and asynchronous parallel training to scale search.
- CIFAR-10 result: discovered CNN with 3.65% test error, outperforming comparable DenseNet baselines.
- Penn Treebank result: discovered recurrent cell with test perplexity 62.4, a substantial improvement over prior LSTM-based state-of-the-art.
- Rigorous evaluation: includes comparison to random search showing the controller learns a better search strategy; discovered cells transfer to char-level LM and GNMT.
- Clarity and presentation: paper is well-written with clear figures and detailed experimental setup.
- Additional feedback — computational cost: search is very expensive (e.g., 800 GPUs for CIFAR-10); paper should report total GPU-days/models trained for context.
- Additional feedback — search-space sensitivity: discuss how performance depends on the choice of primitives and what happens with suboptimal operation sets.
- Additional feedback — fine-tuning vs. from-scratch: consider initializing or biasing the controller with known architectures to find efficient, incremental improvements.
- Question for authors: quantify total computational cost for searches on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank (e.g., GPU-hours, number of models trained).
- Question for authors: report any stability issues with REINFORCE and whether additional techniques (entropy regularization, variance reduction) were used.
- Question for authors: analyze diversity of high-performing architectures — does the controller converge to one family or find multiple distinct solutions?
