This paper presents a method for searching for optimal neural network architectures using an actor-critic reinforcement learning framework. The approach treats a deep neural network as a variable-length sequence. A controller, implemented as an auto-regressive two-layer LSTM, serves as the actor, generating network architectures. Within this RL context, actions correspond to the selection of network components (e.g., filter sizes, strides), and the reward is derived from the validation accuracy of the resulting trained architecture. This controller is trained with policy gradients, incorporating a critic to reduce variance and stabilize training. The method is evaluated on two different datasets, where the discovered architectures are compared against several strong, human-designed baselines.

This is a very exciting and well-executed paper. The manual design of neural network architectures is a significant challenge, and it is often difficult to gauge how far these hand-designed networks are from an optimal configuration. The presented method is novel and well-motivated. The authors do an excellent job of describing their approach in detail, including the various technical improvements required to make it effective. The experiments on two distinct datasets effectively demonstrate the method's capabilities, and it is particularly insightful to compare the automatically generated architectures with their human-designed counterparts. The paper is exceptionally clear, well-written, and accessible. The discussion and comparison with related literature are also thorough.

To further strengthen the paper, it would be interesting to see data regarding the computational cost of the search process. An analysis of the relationship between the time/resources invested and the quality of the discovered model would be a valuable addition. It would also be interesting to see how the method performs when bootstrapped with a human-designed architecture and how such an architecture evolves during the search.

Overall, this is an excellent and interesting paper that makes a strong contribution to the field of automated machine learning.
