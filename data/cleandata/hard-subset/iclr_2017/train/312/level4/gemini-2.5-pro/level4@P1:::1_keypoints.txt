- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RNN controller generates architectures, trained with reinforcement learning using child validation accuracy as reward.  
- Demonstrates NAS on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, finding novel architectures that achieve state-of-the-art or near-state-of-the-art performance.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Method is novel, general, flexible, and effective for automated architecture search.  
- Key reason 2: Strong empirical results that convincingly validate the approach on competitive benchmarks.  
- Technical strength: Using an RNN controller with policy gradients is creative and applicable across domains (convnets and RNNs).  
- Practical considerations: Addresses implementation challenges (skip connections, parallel training); computationally intensive but justified by results.  
- Empirical impact: Discovered CIFAR-10 model rivals best architectures and is faster; discovered PTB recurrent cell outperforms optimized LSTM.  
- Suggested improvement: Improve reproducibility by publishing final hyperparameters used for best CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank models.  
- Requested clarifications: Provide exact hyperparameters and regularization details (e.g., recurrent/embedding dropout) for the final Penn Treebank and character language models and the three configurations/sizes used.
