The authors report their hyperparameter tuning procedure as follows: "[a]fter the controller RNN is done training, we take the best RNN cell according to the lowest validation perplexity and then run a grid search over learning rate, weight initialization, dropout rates and decay epoch. The best cell found was then run with three different configurations and sizes to increase its capacity."

For the sake of reproducibility, it would be highly beneficial for the authors to include the specific hyperparameters used in the final grid search. Details such as the learning rate, weight initialization scheme, and the specific types and rates of dropout (e.g., recurrent dropout, embedding dropout) are crucial. Without this information, replicating the results would require significant trial and error. As demonstrated by Zaremba et al. (2014) in "Recurrent Neural Network Regularization," providing a baseline set of hyperparameters is invaluable for facilitating future work in the field. This information would also be valuable for the other experiments, such as the character-level language modeling task.

This paper addresses the important and challenging problem of automated architecture search. While the proposed technique is currently computationally intensive, this trade-off is likely to become more favorable in the near future with continued improvements in computational hardware.

A key strength of the work is its comprehensive evaluation across standard vision and text tasks. By tackling numerous benchmark datasets, the authors demonstrate that their approach can discover novel architectures that achieve gains over the standard search space for RNNs and CNNs. While one could always wish for evaluation on more datasets, the experiments presented are more than sufficient to show that the technique is not only competitive with human-designed architectures but may even surpass them. This work also suggests a promising direction for tailoring architectures to specific datasets without requiring extensive, dataset-specific manual engineering.

In summary, this is a well-written paper on a compelling topic, supported by strong empirical results. I recommend acceptance.
