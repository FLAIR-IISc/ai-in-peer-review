This paper proposes a method to automatically design neural network architectures using a recurrent neural network (RNN) trained with reinforcement learning (RL). The core idea is promising, and the experimental results on two datasets demonstrate the method's effectiveness.

**Strengths:**
1.  The central idea of using an RNN to generate a description of a network architecture, and then using reinforcement learning to train the RNN controller, is both interesting and novel.
2.  The architectures generated by this method bear a resemblance to those designed by human experts. This suggests that the approach can independently discover meaningful architectural motifs and that its findings are consistent with established design principles.

**Weaknesses:**
1.  The search process for finding an optimal architecture is computationally expensive, requiring significant training time even with substantial computing resources.
2.  The experiments do not evaluate the generalizability of the discovered architectures. It would strengthen the paper to show how an architecture discovered on one dataset performs on other, similar datasets. This is particularly relevant for the generated sequential models, to see if the learned architectural patterns are transferable.

**Overall Assessment:**
Overall, this is a strong paper with a promising direction. However, its claims would be significantly bolstered by additional experiments that demonstrate the potential advantages of the generated architectures over human-designed models, particularly in terms of generalizability.
