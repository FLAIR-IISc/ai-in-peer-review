- Summary: Proposes an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate neural architectures, validated on Penn Treebank and CIFAR-10 with competitive or superior results.

- Significance/Novelty: Tackles architecture engineering with a novel, promising automated controller RNN approach, an important direction despite high compute needs.

- Strong empirical results: Discovered NASCell achieves new state-of-the-art perplexity on PTB; discovered convolutional models are competitive on CIFAR-10.

- Surpasses human designs: Method finds architectures that outperform hand-tuned models, showing value beyond human intuition.

- Main weakness: Paper lacks detailed hyperparameter settings for training the final discovered architectures, impeding reproducibility.

- Requested details (grid search): Provide ranges and final values for learning rate, weight initialization, and decay epoch used in grid search.

- Requested details (dropout): Specify dropout rates and exact dropout types used (recurrent, embedding, output) for language modeling and other tasks.

- Reproducibility importance: Releasing these hyperparameters is critical to enable replication and accelerate follow-up work.

- Recommendation: Overall strong and impactful — recommend acceptance.
