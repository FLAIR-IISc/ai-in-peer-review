- Proposes an actor-critic RL framework for automatic neural architecture search where an LSTM controller sequentially generates variable-length architecture descriptions and is trained with policy gradients using validation accuracy as reward, with a critic to stabilize training.
- Evaluated on two challenging image-classification benchmarks and compared against multiple human-designed networks.
- Addresses the important problem of reducing reliance on time-consuming manual neural network design.
- Novelty and impact: applying actor-critic RL to automate architecture design is a powerful, promising idea with potential to accelerate progress.
- Clarity and detail: methodology and implementation refinements are described very clearly, making the complex approach accessible.
- Strong empirical results: discovered architectures are competitive with or superior to state-of-the-art and exhibit novel, non-intuitive motifs.
- Thoroughness: related work is discussed comprehensively and situates the contribution well.
- Weakness — computational cost: lacks analysis of total training time or GPU-hours to contextualize the search cost versus model quality.
- Weakness — initialization strategy: does not examine how different controller initializations (e.g., seeding with strong human-designed architectures) affect convergence and results.
- Overall conclusion: excellent, novel, well-executed paper that compellingly demonstrates the viability of RL-based architecture search.
