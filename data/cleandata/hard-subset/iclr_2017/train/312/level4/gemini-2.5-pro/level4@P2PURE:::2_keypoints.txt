- Proposes actor-critic RL for neural architecture search, treating networks as variable-length sequences.
- Uses an auto-regressive two-layer LSTM as the controller (actor) that generates architectures.
- The LSTM also serves as a critic to improve the search process.
- Actions correspond to selecting each network component; reward is architecture performance.
- Evaluated on two tasks and compared against multiple human-designed architectures.
- Demonstrates effective search performance and interesting structural differences from human designs.
- Paper is novel, well-written, detailed, and situates work within related literature.
- Lacks reported computational cost (time/resources) and correlation between cost and model quality.
- Suggests experimenting with bootstrapping the search from a human-designed architecture.
- Overall assessment: excellent, significant contribution.
