- Decision: Strongly recommend accepting the paper.
- Core idea: Frame neural architecture design as a sequence-generation problem solved by an RNN controller trained with REINFORCE.
- Reward signal: Child-model validation performance (e.g., accuracy) is used as the reinforcement signal to train the controller.
- Empirical validation: Automatic architectures are competitive with or superior to state-of-the-art on CIFAR-10 (CNN) and Penn Treebank (RNN).
- Novelty/significance: Introduces a new, promising direction that reduces reliance on manual architecture engineering.
- Search mechanics: Uses skip connections and a parallelized, asynchronous training scheme to make the expensive search feasible.
- Discovered motifs: Generated architectures contain novel motifs distinct from human designs, suggesting exploration of overlooked design regions.
- Writing and positioning: Paper is clear, well-written, and situates contributions effectively within related work.
- Concern: Search is extremely computationally expensive (mentions using ~800 GPUs); more detail on total GPU-hours and cost-quality trade-offs is needed.
- Suggested experiment: Evaluate bootstrapping the controller from strong human-designed architectures (e.g., ResNet/LSTM) to test refinement vs. from-scratch discovery.
