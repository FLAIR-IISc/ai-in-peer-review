- Introduces an actor-critic RL method to discover optimal neural architectures by treating design as a variable-length action sequence.  
- A controller (auto-regressive two-layer LSTM) generates architecture specifications; each component selection is an action.  
- Validation accuracy of sampled architectures is used as the reward signal to train the controller.  
- A critic is trained alongside the controller to improve training stability.  
- Evaluated on two distinct problems with comparisons to strong human-designed baselines.  
- Results show the method can find non-intuitive yet powerful architectures, demonstrating its potential.  
- Paper is novel, well-executed, clearly written, and thoroughly situated in related work.  
- Suggests adding analysis of computational/search costs and correlation between resources invested and final model quality.  
- Recommends experiments initializing search from strong human-designed architectures to test refinement ability.  
- Overall assessment: excellent, impactful work that opens a promising research direction.
