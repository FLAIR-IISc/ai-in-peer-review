- Proposes actor-critic RL method to discover optimal neural architectures by modeling a network as a variable-length sequence with node selection as actions and rewards from evaluation error.  
- Uses an auto-regressive two-layer LSTM as both controller and critic.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on two distinct problems and benchmarks against several manually designed architectures.  
- Novel, principled solution to the laborious problem of manual architecture design.  
- Provides a thorough, detailed description including required implementation improvements.  
- Evaluation datasets are appropriate and comparisons to human-designed architectures yield interesting insights.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written, clear, and well-situated within existing literature.  
- Suggests adding computational cost analysis (training duration and resource vs. final model quality).  
- Recommends investigating initializing the search from a human-designed architecture and tracking performance/evolution.  
- Overall: a high-quality, thought-provoking paper that makes a significant contribution.
