- Proposes neural architecture search using an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning, using child-network validation accuracy as the reward.  
- Applies the method to generate convolutional architectures for CIFAR-10 and recurrent cell architectures for Penn Treebank.  
- Discovered architectures achieve performance competitive with or surpassing state-of-the-art human-designed models.  
- Strength: Novel framing of architecture search as sequence generation optimized with policy gradients.  
- Strength: Learned architectures display human-like motifs (e.g., skip connections), indicating the method captures meaningful design principles.  
- Weakness: Extremely high computational cost (hundreds of GPUs for days), limiting wider adoption; needs discussion of efficiency improvements (e.g., weight sharing, performance prediction).  
- Weakness: Lacks experiments on generalization or transferability to other datasets/tasks (e.g., CIFAR-100, SVHN, other sequential tasks), so transferability is unclear.  
- Conclusion: Well-written and impactful; recommend acceptance but urge addressing computational limitations and demonstrating architecture transferability.
