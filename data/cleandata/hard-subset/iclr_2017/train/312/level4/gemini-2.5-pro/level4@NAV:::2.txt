This work proposes a method for discovering optimal neural network architectures using an actor-critic reinforcement learning framework. The approach models a deep neural network as a variable-length sequence, where an RL agent generates the target architecture (the actor). Within this context, the selection of a network node constitutes an action, and the reward signal is derived from the evaluation error of the resulting architecture. An auto-regressive, two-layer LSTM is utilized to serve as the controller and critic. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated on two distinct problems, with performance benchmarked against several manually designed architectures.

This is a compelling and well-executed paper. It addresses the significant challenge of manual architecture design, a process that is often laborious and provides little insight into the optimality of the resulting network. The proposed methodology is novel and offers a principled approach to architecture search. The authors provide a thorough and detailed description of their method, including the specific improvements required for its successful implementation. The chosen evaluation datasets are appropriate and effectively showcase the method's capabilities. The comparison between the automatically generated architectures and their human-designed counterparts yields particularly interesting insights. The manuscript is exceptionally well-written, clear, and accessible, and the authors effectively situate their work within the existing literature.

The paper could be strengthened by including an analysis of the computational cost. Specifically, data on training duration and the correlation between required computational resources and final model quality would be a valuable addition. It would also be interesting to see an investigation into the performance and evolution of models that are initialized with a human-designed architecture before applying the search algorithm.

Overall, this is a high-quality and thought-provoking paper that makes a significant contribution.
