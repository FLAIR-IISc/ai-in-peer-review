- Novel method: an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to automatically generate neural network architectures is a novel and compelling idea.  
- Systematic framework: provides a learnable, systematic way to explore the large space of network designs.  
- Human-like motifs: discovered architectures contain motifs and structures resembling human-designed models, suggesting learned sensible design principles.  
- High computational cost: architecture search requires extensive training and substantial compute, limiting practical applicability.  
- Limited generalization evaluation: experiments do not test how well discovered architectures transfer to other datasets or tasks.  
- Need for transfer experiments: evaluating architectures discovered on one dataset (e.g., CIFAR-10) on related datasets would clarify whether designs are overfit or transferable.  
- Overall: strong and promising work, but strengthened by addressing computational cost and demonstrating generalizability.
