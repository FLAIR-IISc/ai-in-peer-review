The paper reports that for the best RNN cell, a grid search was performed over "learning rate, weight initialization, dropout rates and decay epoch." Could the authors please include the specific hyperparameters found through this search, as well as the type of dropout used (e.g., recurrent, embedding)? Without these details, reproducing the results would require considerable trial and error. As exemplified by previous work ("Recurrent Neural Network Regularization", Zaremba et al., 2014), releasing a base set of hyperparameters greatly assists future research in the field. This information would also be valuable for the other experiments, such as the character-level language modeling task.

This paper explores the important field of automated architecture search. While the proposed technique is currently computationally intensive, this is a reasonable trade-off that will likely become more favorable as technology improves.

The authors validate their approach across standard vision and text tasks on numerous benchmark datasets, showing that there are gains to be made by exploring beyond the standard RNN and CNN search spaces. While one could always ask for the technique to be applied to more datasets, the experiments are more than sufficient to show that the method is not only competitive with human-designed architectures but may even surpass them. This also suggests a promising approach for tailoring architectures to specific datasets without resorting to manual hand-engineering for each task.

This is a well-written paper on an interesting topic with strong results. I recommend it be accepted.
