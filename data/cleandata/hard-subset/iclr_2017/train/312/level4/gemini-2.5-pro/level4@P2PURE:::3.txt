This paper proposes a method for automatically designing neural network architectures using a recurrent neural network (RNN) controller trained with reinforcement learning (RL). The central idea is novel and promising, and the empirical results on two benchmark datasets demonstrate the viability of the approach.

**Strengths:**
1. The approach of using an RNN to generate a description of a network architecture, and then using RL to optimize the RNN controller, is a novel and powerful paradigm for architecture search.
2. It is compelling that the discovered architectures independently converge on design principles and structural motifs that are similar to those in state-of-the-art, human-designed networks.

**Weaknesses:**
1. The primary weakness is the significant computational expense. The architecture search process is extremely time-consuming and requires substantial hardware resources.
2. The generalizability of the discovered architectures is not explored. It would strengthen the paper to show how an architecture discovered on one dataset performs when transferred to a similar but distinct dataset, particularly for the generated sequential models.

**Overall Assessment:**
In summary, this is a strong paper introducing a promising research direction. However, more extensive experiments are needed to fully demonstrate the potential advantages of this method over manually designed architectures.
