1. Proposes an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to automatically design neural network architectures; experiments on two datasets show effectiveness.

2. Central idea is novel and interesting: the RNN generates architecture descriptions while RL trains the controller.

3. Generated architectures resemble human-designed ones, suggesting the method discovers meaningful architectural motifs.

4. The search process is computationally expensive, requiring substantial training time and resources.

5. Experiments do not evaluate generalizability or transferability of discovered architectures across datasets, especially for sequential models.

6. Overall a strong, promising paper, but needs additional experiments to demonstrate advantages over human-designed models, particularly in generalizability.
