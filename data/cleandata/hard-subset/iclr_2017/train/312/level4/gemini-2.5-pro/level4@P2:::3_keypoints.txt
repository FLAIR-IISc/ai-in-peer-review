- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RNN controller trained with REINFORCE to generate and optimize child network architectures by validation accuracy.  
- Demonstrates strong results on CIFAR-10 (discovered CNN with 3.65% test error, faster than comparable human models).  
- Shows strong results on Penn Treebank (discovered recurrent cell outperforming LSTM with perplexity 62.4) and successful transfer to character modeling and GNMT (≈0.5 BLEU gain).  
- Decision: Accept — paper judged original, high-impact, and empirically strong.  
- Novelty: Frames architecture design as a reinforcement learning problem for a generative controller, opening meta-learning/AutoML directions.  
- Empirical validation: State-of-the-art results on two diverse tasks and clear advantage over random search.  
- Strength: Elegant formulation allowing exploration of large, variable-length, compositional search spaces beyond traditional hyperparameter methods.  
- Strength: Directly optimizes final performance metric (validation accuracy) via policy gradients.  
- Weakness: Extremely high computational cost (≈12,800 child models, 800 GPUs for CIFAR-10), limiting accessibility.  
- Suggestion: Improve sample efficiency—consider weight sharing across child models or surrogate performance predictors.  
- Suggestion: Evaluate transferability of the CIFAR-10 architecture to related datasets (e.g., CIFAR-100, SVHN) to test generality.  
- Suggestion: Provide deeper analysis/intuition for discovered structures (e.g., rectangular filters, one-step skips) to reveal learned design principles.  
- Suggestion: Discuss search-space design choices and sensitivity (filter ranges, operation sets) to help reproducibility and future work.
