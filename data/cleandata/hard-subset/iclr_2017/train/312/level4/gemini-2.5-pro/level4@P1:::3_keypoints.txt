- Proposes Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller generates architecture descriptions and is trained with reinforcement learning using child network validation accuracy as the reward.  
- Applies the method to design a CNN for CIFAR-10 and a recurrent cell for Penn Treebank, finding novel architectures that achieve state-of-the-art or highly competitive results.  
- Recommendation: accept, primarily because the RL formulation for an RNN controller is a novel and compelling approach to automating architecture design.  
- Empirical strength: the method discovers architectures from scratch that rival or surpass top human-designed models on challenging benchmarks.  
- Technical novelty: using a variable-length RNN-generated description and policy gradients enables exploration of a large search space including modern elements like skip connections.  
- Interpretability: discovered architectures show sensible, human-like design patterns (e.g., rectangular filters, skip connections, LSTM-like motifs), indicating the method learns meaningful architectural principles.  
- Main weakness: extremely high computational cost (800 GPUs), which limits accessibility and reproducibility; authors should discuss efficiency improvements (e.g., weight sharing, performance prediction).  
- Generality concerns: recommend additional evaluations of transferability (e.g., test CIFAR-10 architecture on CIFAR-100/SVHN without re-search and broader sequence-task tests for the recurrent cell).
