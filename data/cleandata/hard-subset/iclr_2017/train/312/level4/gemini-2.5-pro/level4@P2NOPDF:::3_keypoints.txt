- Novel method: an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning generates strings describing network architectures, offering a principled, scalable way to search architectures and directly optimize performance.
- Empirical finding: discovered architectures resemble successful human designs (e.g., include skip connections), suggesting the search captures meaningful architectural principles.
- Major weakness: very high computational cost—requires training thousands of networks over many days, limiting practicality and accessibility.
- Missing efficiency discussion: the paper should discuss potential avenues to reduce search cost.
- Limited evaluation of generalizability: experiments do not show whether architectures transfer to related datasets (e.g., CIFAR-100, SVHN) or other sequence tasks.
- Overall: innovative and well-executed with strong results, but weakened by high computational expense and insufficient transfer evaluations; additional transfer experiments would strengthen the claims.
