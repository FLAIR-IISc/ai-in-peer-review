This paper presents a method for searching for optimal neural network architectures using an actor-critic reinforcement learning framework. The approach treats a deep neural network as a variable-length sequence and uses RL to discover a target architecture. In this formulation, a controller, implemented as an auto-regressive two-layer LSTM, serves as the actor that generates the architecture. The selection of each network component is an action, and the performance of the resulting architecture provides the reward signal. The LSTM also functions as a critic to aid the search process. The method is evaluated on two distinct problems, where its performance is compared against a number of human-designed architectures.

This is an exciting and novel paper. The manual design of network architectures is a difficult task, and it is often hard to know how far from optimal these hand-designed networks are. The authors do an excellent job of describing their method in detail, including the various improvements required to make it successful. The experimental evaluation on the chosen datasets effectively demonstrates the method's capabilities. The differences observed between the machine-generated and human-generated architectures are particularly interesting. The paper is very clearly written, accessible, and provides good coverage of the related literature.

The paper could be strengthened by including data on the computational cost, such as the time and resources required for the search process, and the correlation between these costs and the quality of the resulting model. It would also be interesting to see how the method performs when it is bootstrapped with a human-designed architecture and allowed to evolve from that starting point.

Overall, this is an excellent and interesting paper that makes a significant contribution.
