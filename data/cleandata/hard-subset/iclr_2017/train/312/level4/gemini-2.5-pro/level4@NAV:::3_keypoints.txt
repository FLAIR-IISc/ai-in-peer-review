- Proposes an RNN controller that generates variable-length network descriptions and uses reinforcement learning to optimize the controller.  
- Validated with solid experimental results on two datasets.  
- Innovative and promising core idea of combining an RNN controller with RL for architecture search.  
- Discovered architectures resemble human-designed models, indicating meaningful convergence.  
- High computational cost: requires extensive training and heavy hardware resources.  
- Limited evaluation of generalizability; lacks tests of architectures across different but related datasets, especially for sequential models.  
- Overall strong contribution but needs more extensive experiments to demonstrate advantages over state-of-the-art human-designed models.
