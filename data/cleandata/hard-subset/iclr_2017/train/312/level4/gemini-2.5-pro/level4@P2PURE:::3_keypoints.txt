- Novel method: uses an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to automatically design neural network architectures — a novel and promising idea.  
- Empirical viability: demonstrated on two benchmark datasets, showing the approach can produce competitive architectures.  
- Strength: the RNN-generation + RL optimization paradigm is a powerful, new approach to architecture search.  
- Strength: discovered architectures independently converge to design principles and motifs similar to state-of-the-art human-designed networks.  
- Weakness: very high computational cost — the search is extremely time-consuming and needs substantial hardware resources.  
- Weakness: limited evaluation of generalizability — transfer performance to other datasets (especially for sequential models) is not explored.  
- Overall: a strong, promising paper that requires more extensive experiments to fully establish advantages over manually designed architectures.
