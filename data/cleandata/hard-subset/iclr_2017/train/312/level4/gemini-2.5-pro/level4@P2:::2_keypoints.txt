- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RNN controller generates architectures and is trained with REINFORCE using child model validation performance as reward.  
- Demonstrates NAS on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, yielding state-of-the-art results.  
- CIFAR-10: discovered CNN achieves 3.65% error and is faster than prior leading models.  
- Penn Treebank: discovered recurrent cell attains perplexity 62.4, outperforming LSTM and other baselines.  
- Discovered RNN cell transfers successfully to character-level language modeling and machine translation.  
- Method is a novel synthesis of sequence modeling and reinforcement learning with practical techniques (skip connections, parallel training).  
- Comparison to random search confirms the policy-gradient controller effectively learns to navigate the search space.  
- Paper is well-written, thorough, and properly situated relative to hyperparameter optimization, neuro-evolution, and program synthesis.  
- Decision: Accept — merits include novelty, high impact, and exceptional empirical validation.  
- Concern: Very high computational cost (e.g., training 800 networks on 800 GPUs); recommend explicit discussion and analysis of cost vs. performance.  
- Suggestion: Consider bootstrapping the controller with known architectures (e.g., ResNet/LSTM) to potentially reduce search cost and improve results.  
- Suggestion: Move a condensed visualization of a discovered architecture (e.g., the RNN cell) into the main paper for clearer presentation.
