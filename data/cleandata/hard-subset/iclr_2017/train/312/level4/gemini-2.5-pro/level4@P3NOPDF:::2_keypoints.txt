- Introduces an actor-critic RL method for Neural Architecture Search that represents architectures as variable-length sequences generated by an auto-regressive two-layer LSTM controller, with child network performance as reward and a critic to stabilize training.  
- Evaluated on two challenging tasks, producing architectures competitive with or superior to strong human-designed baselines.  
- Addresses an important, impactful problem: automating neural architecture design to reduce manual effort and uncover novel designs.  
- Novel and elegant framing of architecture generation as sequential decision-making by a controller agent.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear exposition and important engineering details (actor-critic updates, auto-regressive controller).  
- Strong empirical validation demonstrating the method’s capabilities on chosen tasks.  
- Provides insightful analysis comparing machine-discovered architectures to human-designed ones.  
- Thorough and comprehensive related work situating the contribution within existing literature.  
- Missing reporting of computational cost (e.g., GPU-days) and lack of discussion on search budget vs. model quality.  
- Suggests studying warm-starting the search from known human architectures to possibly speed convergence or improve final results.  
- Overall recommendation: excellent paper, significant contribution, strongly recommended for acceptance.
