- Proposes an actor-critic reinforcement learning method to search for optimal neural network architectures.  
- Represents deep networks as variable-length sequences generated by a controller.  
- Controller implemented as an auto-regressive two-layer LSTM acting as the actor.  
- Actions correspond to selecting network components (filter sizes, strides, etc.).  
- Reward is validation accuracy of the trained architecture.  
- Trains the controller with policy gradients and uses a critic to reduce variance and stabilize training.  
- Evaluated on two datasets and compared against strong human-designed baselines.  
- Paper is novel, well-motivated, and very well-executed.  
- Descriptions are clear and include necessary technical improvements for effectiveness.  
- Experiments effectively demonstrate capabilities and provide insightful comparisons to human designs.  
- Suggested additions: report computational cost/resource vs. model quality and experiments bootstrapping from a human-designed architecture to track evolution.
