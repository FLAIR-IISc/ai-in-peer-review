- Method: train controller RNN, pick best RNN cell by lowest validation perplexity, then perform grid search over hyperparameters (learning rate, weight init, dropout rates, decay epoch).
- After selection, evaluate the optimal cell across multiple configurations and sizes to scale capacity.
- Paper should include specific hyperparameter details (e.g., types of dropout like recurrent or embedding dropout) to enable precise replication.
- Providing a baseline set of hyperparameters (similar to Zaremba et al. 2014) would reduce trial-and-error for future work.
- The same level of hyperparameter detail is needed for other experiments, such as the character language model.
- Work addresses automating architecture search, noting current computational intensity but expected efficiency gains over time.
- Experiments span vision and text tasks across many benchmarks, showing benefits of searching beyond standard RNN/CNN spaces.
- While more datasets would be desirable, the current scope sufficiently demonstrates competitiveness and potential to surpass human-designed architectures.
- Overall assessment: well-crafted paper with robust results; strong recommendation for acceptance.
