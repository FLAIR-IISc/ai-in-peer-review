- Proposes an actor-critic framework to discover optimal neural network architectures.  
- Represents DNNs as variable-length sequences and uses RL to identify architectures.  
- Frames node selection as actions and evaluation error as the reward signal.  
- Uses a two-layer auto-regressive LSTM as both controller and critic.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on two distinct problems with comparisons to human-designed architectures.  
- Addresses a significant challenge: difficulty and suboptimality of manual architecture selection.  
- Methodology is innovative and comprehensively explained with necessary improvements included.  
- Datasets used effectively showcase the method’s capabilities.  
- Comparisons between generated and human-designed architectures provide intriguing insights.  
- Manuscript is clear, accessible, and contains a thorough related-work review.  
- Suggests adding training-time data and analysis of resource vs. model-quality trade-offs.  
- Recommends exploring human-bootstrapped models and their potential integration.  
- Overall assessment: excellent, compelling contribution and highly interesting read.
