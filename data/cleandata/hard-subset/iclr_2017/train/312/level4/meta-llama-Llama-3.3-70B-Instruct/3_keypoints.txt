- Proposes a novel RNN-based generator trained with reinforcement learning to design task-specific network architectures; shows empirical success on two datasets.
- Strength: Innovative use of RNN to generate network descriptions and RL to train the generator.
- Strength: Generated architectures show similarities to human-designed models, indicating compatibility with expert intuition.
- Limitation: Training is computationally expensive and time-consuming even with substantial resources.
- Limitation: Experiments lack demonstration of generalizability; needs evaluation on additional related datasets, especially for sequential models.
- Conclusion: Well-written paper with a solid foundation but requires additional experiments to fully validate advantages over human-designed architectures.
