This paper presents a search for the best neural-network architecture based on the actor-critic framework. This method considers the DNN as a variable-length sequence and uses RL to find the target architecture, which acts as an actor. The node selection is an action in the RL context, and the evaluation of the resulting architecture is consistent with a reward. An auto-regressive two-layer LSTML is used as a controller and critic. The method has been evaluated on two different problems, and each problem has been compared to the number of other human-made architectures.

This is a very exciting paper! It is difficult to manually select the architecture, and it is difficult to know how far away the manually designed networks are from the best results. The presented method is innovative. The authors have done a great job of describing it in detail, mentioning all the necessary improvements. The tested data well represents the capabilities of the method. It is very interesting to see the difference between the produced architecture and the human-made architecture. The paper is very clearly written and very easy to understand. Its comparison and coverage with related literature is well done.

It would be interesting to see the data related to the time required for training and the time/resources required for training, and the quality of the model. It would be interesting to see how the human-bootstrapped models perform and engage.

Overall, this is an excellent and interesting paper.
