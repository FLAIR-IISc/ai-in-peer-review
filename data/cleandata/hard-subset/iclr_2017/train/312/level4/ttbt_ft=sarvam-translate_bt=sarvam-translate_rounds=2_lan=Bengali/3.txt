This paper proposes using RL and RNN to design network architectures for specific tasks. The concept of this paper is quite promising, and experimental results on two datasets show that the method is reliable.

The advantages of this paper are:

1. The idea of using RL to train RNNs and RNNs to create network descriptions is interesting and promising.
2. The architecture created looks like a human-made architecture, which shows that human skills and the created network architecture are compatible.

The disadvantages of this paper are:

1. The network training time is long, despite the availability of abundant computing resources.
2. The tests do not provide a generalization of the created architecture. It would be good to see the performance of the created architecture on other similar but different datasets, especially on created sequential models.

Overall, I think this is a good paper. However, more testing is needed to show its potential advantages over human-made models.
