This research paper presents a search for the optimal neural network architecture based on an actor-critic framework. This method considers the DNA as a variable-length sequence and uses RL to find the target architecture, which acts as an actor. Node selection is an action in the RL context, and the result is equivalent to an evaluation error reward for the architecture. An auto-regressive two-layer LSTM is used as the controller and critic. The method is evaluated on two different problems, and each is compared to the number of other human-made architectures.

This is a very exciting research paper! It is difficult to manually select the architecture, and it is difficult to know how far a manually designed network is from optimal results. The presented method is innovative. The authors have done an excellent job of describing it in detail, including all the necessary improvements. Well-tested data represents the ability of the method. It is very interesting to see the difference between the generated architecture and the human-made architecture. This research paper is very clearly written and very accessible. The coverage and contrast with related literature are well done.

It will be interesting to see the data on the time required for training, and it will also be interesting to see the correlation between the time/resources required for training and the quality of the model. It will also be interesting to see how the human-bootstrapped models perform and what is included.

Overall, an excellent and interesting research paper.
