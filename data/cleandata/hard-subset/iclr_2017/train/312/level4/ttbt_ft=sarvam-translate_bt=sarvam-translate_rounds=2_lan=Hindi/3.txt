This research paper proposes using RL and RNN to design network structures for specific tasks. The idea behind this paper is quite promising, and experimental results on two datasets demonstrate that the method is robust.

The advantages of this paper are as follows:

1. The idea of using RNN to prepare network descriptions and RL to train RNN is interesting and promising.
2. The generated structure resembles a human-made structure, indicating that human expertise and generated network structures are consistent.

The disadvantages of this paper are as follows:

1. Network training time is lengthy, even with a large number of computing resources.
2. The experiments did not provide generalization of the generated structures. It would be good to see the performance of the generated structure on other similar but different datasets, especially on generated sequential models.

Overall, I think this is a good research paper. However, it needs more experiments to demonstrate its potential on human-made models.
