Here's a paper on how, after training a controller RNN, we take the best RNN cells according to the lowest validation perplexity, and then run grid searches on learning rate, weight initialization, dropout rate, and decay epoch. The best cells are found and then run with three different configurations and sizes to increase their capacity.

Without them, replication would require a lot of trial and error. Releasing a baseline set of hyperparameters, such as in "Recurrent Neural Network Regularization" (Zaremba et al., 2014), is very helpful in future work.

This would also be desirable for other experiments, such as character LMs.

This paper explores automating architecture search, which is an important area in our field. While this technique is currently computationally intensive, this trade-off is likely to improve in the near future as technology continues to improve.

This paper covers both standard vision and text tasks and deals with several benchmark datasets, demonstrating that benefits can be achieved by going beyond standard RNN and CNN search spaces. While one would always like to see the technique applied to more datasets, it is already enough to show that the technique is not only competitive with human architectural intuition but can even surpass it. It also suggests an approach for preparing architectures for specific datasets without hand-engineering at each step.

This is a well-written paper on an interesting topic with strong results. I recommend accepting it.
