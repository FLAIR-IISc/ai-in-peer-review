A research paper here reveals that "[after the training of the controller RNA is completed, we select the best RNA cell according to the lowest validation perplexity, and then run a grid search on learning rate, weight initialization, dropout rate, and decay epoch. Subsequently, the best cell is run in three different configurations and sizes to increase its capacity."

Is it possible (or should it be provided here) to include hyperparameters and the type of dropout used (such as recurrent dropout, embedding dropout, etc.)? Without these, replication may require much more effort and error. Publishing a base set of hyperparameters, like "Recurrent Neural Network Regulation" (Zaremba et al., 2014), would greatly help the field in the future.

This will likely be necessary for other experiments as well, such as character LMs.

This research paper discusses an important aspect of our field: automating architecture search. Although this technique is currently computationally very complex, these difficulties will likely improve in the future as technology advances.

This paper covers both standard vision and text tasks and works with many benchmark datasets, demonstrating that searching outside the standard RNA and CNN search spaces can be very beneficial. While one would always want this technique to be applied to more datasets, it is already sufficient that this technique is not only competitive with human architectural concepts but can even surpass them. This indicates a method for creating architecture for specific datasets without manual work at each stage.

This is a beautiful research paper written on an interesting topic, with strong results. I recommend accepting it.
