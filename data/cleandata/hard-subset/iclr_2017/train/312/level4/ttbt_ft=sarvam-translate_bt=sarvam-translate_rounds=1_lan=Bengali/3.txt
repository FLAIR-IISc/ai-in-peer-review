This paper proposes using RL and RNN to design network architectures for specific tasks. The concept of this paper is quite promising, and the experimental results on two datasets show that the method is reliable.

The advantages of this paper are:
1. The idea of using RNN to create network descriptions and RL to train RNN is interesting and promising.
2. The architecture created looks similar to human-made architectures, which shows that human skills and created network architectures are compatible.

The disadvantages of this paper are:
1. The network training time is long despite having plenty of computing resources.
2. The experiments do not provide generality of the created architecture. It would be good to see the performance of the created architecture on other similar but different datasets, especially created sequential models.

Overall, I think this is a good paper. However, more testing is needed to show its potential advantages over human-made models.
