- Paper studies search for optimal neural network architectures using an actor-critic reinforcement learning framework.
- Models a DNN as a variable-length sequence and uses RL to generate architectures; node selection is the action and evaluation error is the reward.
- Employs an auto-regressive two-layer LSTM as both the controller (actor) and the critic.
- Evaluated on two distinct problems and benchmarked against several human-designed architectures.
- Method is innovative and addresses the challenge of manual architecture design.
- Authors provide a thorough, detailed explanation and document enhancements to the approach.
- Datasets chosen effectively showcase the method’s capabilities.
- Generated architectures show interesting differences compared to human-designed networks.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, accessible, and includes a comprehensive related-work discussion.
- Reviewer recommends reporting training time and analyzing correlation between training resources/time and model quality.
- Reviewer suggests investigating performance and evolution of human-initialized models as future work.
- Overall assessment: excellent and thought-provoking paper.
