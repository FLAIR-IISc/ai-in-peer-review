- Proposes an RL + RNN method to automatically design task-specific network architectures.  
- Experiments on two datasets indicate the approach is promising and produces robust results.  
- Strength: Using an RNN to generate network descriptions and training it with RL is innovative and compelling.  
- Strength: Automatically generated architectures resemble human-designed models, showing alignment with expert designs.  
- Weakness: Training the generated networks is very time-consuming even with large compute resources.  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation lacks evidence of generalizability; needs testing on additional, related datasets, especially for sequential models.  
- Overall: Well-executed paper with significant potential but requires more experiments to clearly outperform manual designs.
