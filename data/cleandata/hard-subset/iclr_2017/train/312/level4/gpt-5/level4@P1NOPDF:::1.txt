The paper states: “After the controller RNN is done training, we take the best RNN cell according to the lowest validation perplexity and then run a grid search over learning rate, weight initialization, dropout rates and decay epoch. The best cell found was then run with three different configurations and sizes to increase its capacity.”

Could you include (or provide here) the exact hyperparameters and the types of dropout used (e.g., recurrent dropout, embedding dropout)? Without these details, replication would likely require substantial trial and error. As in Recurrent Neural Network Regularization (Zaremba et al., 2014), releasing a base set of hyperparameters greatly facilitates future work. The same applies to the other experiments, such as the character-level language model.

This paper addresses an important problem in our field: automating architecture search. Although the approach is currently computationally intensive, this trade-off will likely improve as hardware and infrastructure continue to advance.

The paper covers both standard vision and text tasks and tackles many benchmark datasets, demonstrating gains from exploring beyond standard RNN and CNN search spaces. While it would be ideal to see the technique applied to additional datasets, the current set is already more than sufficient to show that the method is not only competitive with human architectural intuition but may even surpass it. This also suggests a practical way to tailor architectures to specific datasets without extensive hand-engineering at each stage.

Overall, this is a well-written paper on an important topic with strong results. I recommend acceptance.
