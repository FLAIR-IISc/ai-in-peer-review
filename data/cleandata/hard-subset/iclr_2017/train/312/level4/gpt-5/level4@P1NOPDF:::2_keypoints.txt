- Proposes an actor-critic RL method for neural architecture search, modeling DNNs as variable-length sequences with node selection as actions and architecture error as reward.  
- Uses an autoregressive two-layer LSTM as both the controller and the critic.  
- Evaluated on two distinct tasks and compared against several human-designed architectures.  
- Method is novel and addresses the difficulty of hand-designing architectures.  
- Paper provides detailed descriptions and practical implementation improvements.  
- Experiments convincingly demonstrate the approach and reveal interesting differences from human-designed networks.  
- Clear, accessible writing with strong coverage and comparison to related work.  
- Suggests reporting training time and computational resources and analyzing their correlation with model quality.  
- Recommends evaluating human-bootstrapped models (starting from human designs) to track evolution and performance.  
- Overall assessment: excellent and compelling paper.
