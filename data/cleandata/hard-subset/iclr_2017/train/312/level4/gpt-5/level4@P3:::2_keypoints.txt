- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS): a recurrent controller trained with REINFORCE to generate variable-length neural architectures maximizing validation accuracy.  
- Flexible search: handles convolutional architectures with skip connections, pooling, batch norm, rectangular filters, and tree-structured recurrent-cell search generalizing LSTM.  
- Large-scale asynchronous distributed training evaluates thousands of candidate architectures in parallel.  
- CIFAR-10 result: discovered conv architecture with 3.65% test error, competitive with state of the art and reported 1.05x faster than a comparable DenseNet.  
- PTB result: discovered recurrent cell surpasses LSTM baselines (62.4 perplexity), transfers to char-level PTB (1.214 bpc) and yields a small BLEU gain in GNMT without extra tuning.  
- Paper includes practical design details (reward shaping, skip-connection parameterization, handling invalid graphs) and control experiments versus random search.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: Novel, well-motivated automation of architecture design with strong empirical results across domains.  
- Methodologically sound: appropriate use of REINFORCE with EMA baseline, auto-regressive controller, and elegant skip-connection selection.  
- Search space: expressive recurrent-cell space that balances structure and flexibility.  
- Empirical support: competitive/superior results on CIFAR-10 and PTB, transfer experiments, and careful implementation/comparison details.  
- Suggest reporting compute budget: total wall-clock time, GPU/CPU hours, and average time per architecture for CIFAR-10 and PTB.  
- Suggest plotting controller learning curves and comparing to random search on CIFAR-10 as done for PTB.  
- Clarify fairness: explain how “1.05x faster” was measured (training vs inference vs FLOPs) and hardware used.  
- Clarify post-search widening: discuss the effect of adding 40 filters on CIFAR-10, whether baselines were allowed similar widening, and report performance without widening.  
- Ensure PTB comparisons match parameter counts/compute and report exact parameter counts for top models.  
- Ablations requested: effect of skip-connection mechanism, pooling, stride prediction, reward shaping, and expanded search-space robustness.  
- Reproducibility concerns: detail validation/test partitioning to avoid controller overfitting, report variance across independent controller runs, and provide seeds.  
- Release suggestions: share search spaces, sampled-architecture logs, rewards, and sufficient configs to reproduce searches at scale.  
- Method refinements: consider entropy regularization for exploration and multi-fidelity/early-stopping strategies to improve sample efficiency.  
- Question: confirm use of REINFORCE with EMA baseline only and whether learned critics or other variance-reduction methods were tried.  
- Question: how sensitive are results to reward shaping (accuracy^3, c·perplexity^2) and child-network depth schedules?  
- Question: what fraction of sampled architectures caused compilation/training failures and how these affect learning?  
- Question: provide total GPU/CPU hours, wall-clock times, and average evaluation time per architecture.  
- Question: show controller performance over time vs random search on CIFAR-10.  
- Question: detail how 1.05x speedup was measured and whether parameter/FLOP parity was ensured.  
- Question: report performance of the unmodified discovered CIFAR-10 architecture and effects of other post-search modifications (narrowing, etc.).  
- Question: provide variability (mean/std) across multiple retrains of the PTB discovered cell.  
- Question: in GNMT, give exact BLEU scores (tokenization/case details), confidence intervals, and confirm compute parity with LSTM.  
- Question: how does changing recurrent-cell base size (e.g., 4/8/12) affect search tractability and final performance?
