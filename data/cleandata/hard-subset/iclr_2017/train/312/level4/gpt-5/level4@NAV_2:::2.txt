This paper introduces a reinforcement-learning-based approach to searching for high-performing neural network architectures within an actor–critic setup. It treats a DNN architecture as a variable-length sequence, where architectural decisions are actions, and the validation performance of the resulting model provides the reward. An autoregressive two-layer LSTM functions as both controller and critic. The method is evaluated on two distinct tasks and compared against several human-designed architectures.

This is a very exciting contribution. Manually crafting architectures is challenging, and it is difficult to gauge how far hand-designed models are from optimal. The proposed approach is novel, and the authors explain it thoroughly, including the necessary refinements. The chosen benchmarks clearly showcase the method’s capabilities. It is also compelling to observe how the generated designs differ from human-devised ones. The writing is clear and accessible, and the related work is covered and contrasted effectively.

It would be useful to see detailed measurements of training time and a study of how compute/resources correlate with final model quality. It would also be interesting to examine how human-bootstrapped starting points behave and evolve under this framework.

Overall, an excellent and engaging paper.
