- Proposes an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to automatically generate task-specific neural architectures.  
- Experimental results on two benchmarks indicate the method is effective.  
- Novel idea: using an RNN to emit architecture specifications and training it with RL is interesting and promising.  
- Discovered architectures resemble human-designed models, showing compatibility with established design heuristics.  
- Major drawback: the approach is computationally expensive, requiring substantial time and compute.  
- Experiments do not convincingly demonstrate generality; lacking transfer evaluations to related but distinct datasets (especially for sequence models).  
- Overall: solid paper that would benefit from additional experiments to more clearly establish advantages over human-designed architectures.
