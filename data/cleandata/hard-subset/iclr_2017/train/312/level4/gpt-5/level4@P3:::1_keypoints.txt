- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS): a controller RNN trained with policy gradient to generate variable-length network architectures, rewarded by child validation performance.  
- Supports skip connections and branching via an attention-based set-selection mechanism and scales with distributed asynchronous training.  
- CIFAR-10 result: model achieves 3.65% test error, competitive or better than human-designed architectures and ~1.05x faster than a DenseNet baseline.  
- PTB result: discovers a recurrent cell with test perplexity 62.4 (~3.6 improvement), which transfers to character PTB (1.214 bpc) and GNMT (+0.5 BLEU) without bespoke tuning.  
- Paper is well written, well situated in related work, and demonstrates generality across vision and language tasks.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Primary reason 1: Strong, state-of-the-art empirical results across vision and language, including transfer experiments.  
- Primary reason 2: Clear, well-motivated approach that expands architecture search to rich, variable-length spaces with a principled RL and distributed systems design.  
- Problem significance: Addresses automated design of architectures and searching variable-length structures (skip connections, cell structures) rather than shallow hyperparameter tuning.  
- Motivation and placement: Effectively contrasts with Bayesian optimization and neuroevolution, arguing why an autoregressive controller + REINFORCE is more flexible/scalable.  
- Technical soundness: Policy gradient setup, variance reduction (EMA baseline), reward shaping, skip-connection extension, and RNN cell formalization are sound and well described.  
- Evidence for claims: Strong empirical results, transfer experiments, controls (random search vs policy gradient), and sensitivity discussions support the claims.  
- Practical relevance: Computationally intensive but transparent distributed approach and planned code release increase impact and accessibility over time.  
- Reproducibility request: Provide full hyperparameter settings for top PTB/char PTB and best CIFAR models (optimizers, LR schedules, momentum/β, weight decay, initialization, dropout types/rates, batch sizes, BPTT, epochs, early stopping, shared-embedding details, reward-shaping constants).  
- Compute/fairness request: Report total search cost (GPU/CPU-days), average/median child training time, number of architectures evaluated, and hardware-normalized comparisons (params, FLOPs, wall-clock speed, data augmentation parity).  
- Search-space/constraints query: Quantify impact of fixed pooling-position constraint on CIFAR-10 search space and performance; clarify frequency and bias from compilation fixes for skip-connection shape mismatches.  
- Ablations/sensitivity requests: Analyze sensitivity to reward shaping (accuracy^3), EMA timescale, controller capacity and RNN types, depth-schedule necessity, and include a Bayesian optimization baseline if feasible.  
- Methodological clarifications: Specify RNN cell “base 8” tree topology, parameter sharing across nodes, whether internal-node ops have learnable params, gradient clipping, layer norm, and GNMT complexity matching.  
- Data/evaluation protocol: Confirm test sets were evaluated only once (no test feedback to controller) and detail CIFAR-10 preprocessing/augmentations used.  
- Overall judgment: Excellent, timely contribution with practical weaknesses (compute cost, missing training details) that are addressable by releasing full configs and code.
