- Reviewer requests the exact hyperparameters and explicit types of dropout (e.g., recurrent, embedding) used after controller RNN training, noting lack of these details hampers reproducibility and recommending releasing a base set of hyperparameters (as in Zaremba et al., 2014); same info needed for the character-level language model.

- Notes the paper tackles an important problem—automating architecture search—and acknowledges the method is currently computationally intensive but likely to improve with hardware/software advances.

- Praises breadth: experiments span vision and text across many benchmarks, showing that exploring beyond conventional RNN/CNN search spaces yields performance gains and that the method can match or exceed human-designed architectures.

- Suggests more datasets would always be desirable but finds the current experimental scope sufficient to demonstrate practical value for dataset-specific architecture tailoring without hand-engineering.

- Overall evaluation: well-written, interesting topic, strong results, and recommended for acceptance.
