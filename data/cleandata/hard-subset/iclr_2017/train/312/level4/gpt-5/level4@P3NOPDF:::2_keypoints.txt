- Summary: Proposes an actor–critic NAS that models networks as variable-length sequences using an auto-regressive LSTM controller/critic, trains candidate models and uses their performance as reward; evaluated on two tasks against human designs.

- Strength: Addresses an important, timely problem by automating architecture design.

- Strength: Novel and well-motivated RL formulation for variable-length architecture choices.

- Strength: Clear, accessible presentation with careful engineering discussion.

- Strength: Compelling empirical results showing machine-discovered architectures differ qualitatively from human designs.

- Strength: Strong coverage and positioning relative to related work.

- Request: Report computational cost—wall-clock time, hardware, number of candidates, GPU-days, and include a budget–performance curve.

- Request: Clarify reward definition (accuracy, negative error, etc.) and analyze how candidate training variance and budget affect reward reliability and final quality.

- Request: Ablation to quantify the critic’s benefit versus a policy-gradient baseline or simpler baseline.

- Request: Report variance across independent controller runs and provide confidence intervals for results.

- Request: Explore human-bootstrapped searches—seeding or fine-tuning from human architectures and compare outcomes.

- Request: Detail search space (operations, depth/width ranges, constraints) and analyze sensitivity to these priors and controller hyperparameters.

- Request: Discuss generalization/transferability of the learned controller across datasets/tasks.

- Request: Improve reproducibility by providing full training details (preprocessing, optimization schedules, hyperparameters) and releasing code or logs if possible.

- Minor: Fix grammar (“A auto-regressive” → “An auto-regressive”).

- Minor: Explain variable-length termination (e.g., end-of-sequence token or max length).

- Minor: Add a short qualitative comparison of discovered motifs vs common human-designed patterns.

- Overall: Excellent, compelling paper; recommend acceptance pending additional reporting, ablations, sensitivity analyses, and variance measurements.
