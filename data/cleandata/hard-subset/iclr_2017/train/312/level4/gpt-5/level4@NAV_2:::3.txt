This paper presents an approach that employs a recurrent controller trained with reinforcement learning to automatically generate task-specific neural network architectures. The concept is compelling, and the experimental results on two benchmarks suggest the method is effective.

Pros:
1. Using an RNN to emit architecture specifications and training it with RL is an interesting and promising idea.
2. The discovered architectures resemble human-designed models, indicating compatibility with established design heuristics.

Cons:
1. The approach is computationally expensive; training requires substantial time even with significant compute resources.
2. The experiments do not convincingly demonstrate the generality of the discovered architectures. It would be helpful to evaluate transfer to related but distinct datasets, especially for the proposed sequence models.

Overall, this is a solid paper, but it would benefit from additional experiments to more clearly establish its advantages over human-designed architectures.
