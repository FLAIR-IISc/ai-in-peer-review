This paper proposes using a recurrent neural network (RNN), trained with reinforcement learning (RL), to automatically design task-specific neural network architectures. The idea is promising, and results on two datasets indicate the method is sound.

Pros:
- Employing an RNN to generate architectural descriptions and optimizing it with RL is an interesting and promising approach.
- The discovered architectures resemble human-designed networks, suggesting compatibility between human expertise and the automatically generated designs.

Cons:
- Training is computationally intensive and time-consuming, even with substantial resources.
- The experiments provide limited evidence of generality. It would be valuable to evaluate the generated architectures on related but distinct datasets, especially for the sequential models.

Overall, this is a strong and interesting paper. However, it requires additional experiments to more clearly demonstrate its advantages over human-designed models.
