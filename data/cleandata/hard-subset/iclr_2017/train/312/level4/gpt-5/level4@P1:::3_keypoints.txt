- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS): an RNN controller trained with REINFORCE to generate and optimize neural network architectures by sampling, training child models, and updating on validation accuracy.  
- Scales to variable-length architectures and distributed asynchronous training; reports competitive CIFAR-10 CNNs and an RNN cell that outperforms LSTM on PTB.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Primary reason 1: Limited evidence that discovered architectures/cells generalize or transfer beyond the specific datasets used.  
- Primary reason 2: Very high computational cost and long training time, raising practicality and reproducibility concerns.  
- Supporting: Paper lacks transfer experiments to show learned motifs are broadly useful, especially for the RNN cell.  
- Supporting: Search requires training thousands of child models on large distributed infrastructure (hundreds of GPUs/CPUs), making reproduction impractical for most.  
- Concern: Simpler baselines (e.g., random search) might match performance with a similar or smaller budget, not evaluated.  
- Suggestion: For CNNs, test CIFAR-10 architectures on CIFAR-100 and SVHN (zero-shot and fine-tuned) to assess transfer.  
- Suggestion: For RNNs, evaluate the discovered cell on other language benchmarks (e.g., WikiText-2) and other sequence tasks.  
- Suggestion: Report wall-clock time, total compute (GPU/CPU hours), and performance vs. number of architectures to show cost-benefit and diminishing returns.  
- Suggestion: Discuss or investigate sample-efficiency methods (weight sharing, early stopping, learning-curve extrapolation, low-fidelity proxies).  
- Suggestion: Provide deeper analysis/interpretation of learned architecture motifs (skip connections, filter shapes, RNN cell patterns) and comparison to human designs.  
- Reproducibility: Ensure code release includes controller, search spaces, and scripts to reproduce main experiments and smaller-scale defaults.  
- Questions: How do CIFAR-10 architectures perform on CIFAR-100/SVHN without re-search and what fine-tuning is needed?  
- Questions: How does the discovered RNN cell perform on other language datasets or larger corpora when swapped in?  
- Questions: What are exact wall-clock times and total compute for CIFAR-10 and PTB, and how does validation performance scale with sampled architectures?  
- Questions: How sensitive are results to reward shaping (e.g., cubing accuracy) and the moving-average baseline; were alternative variance-reduction methods tried?  
- Questions: How dependent are results on the predefined search space; do smaller search spaces yield similar results with fewer samples?  
- Questions: For the RNN cell, was stability across multiple training runs of the same architecture evaluated to assess variance?
