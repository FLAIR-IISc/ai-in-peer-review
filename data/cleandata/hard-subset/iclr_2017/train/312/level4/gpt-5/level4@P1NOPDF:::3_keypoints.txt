- Proposes using an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to design task-specific network architectures.  
- Idea is promising and experiments on two datasets show the approach is sound.  
- Framing architecture search as an RNN generator trained via RL is interesting and novel.  
- Generated architectures resemble human-designed networks, indicating compatibility with expert designs.  
- Training is very time-consuming, even with substantial computational resources.  
- Experiments do not demonstrate generality or transferability of generated architectures to related datasets.  
- More experiments are needed to substantiate advantages over human-designed models and assess generality.
