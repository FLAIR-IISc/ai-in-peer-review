- Paper studies neural architecture search using a controller RNN, showing strong results on both vision and language tasks despite high compute cost.
- Demonstrates that searching beyond standard CNN/RNN spaces can yield architectures competitive with or superior to human designs.
- Addresses an important, high-impact problem in automating architecture design.
- Method is evaluated across multiple text and vision benchmarks, indicating generality rather than dataset-specific tuning.
- Learned architectures show empirical gains over common human-designed baselines.
- Paper is well written and clearly presented.
- Approach enables adapting architectures to specific datasets without extensive manual engineering.
- Main concern: insufficient hyperparameter and training transparency after architecture search, harming reproducibility.
- Request to specify optimizer and settings, learning rates and decay schedule (including decay epoch), batch size, gradient clipping, weight initialization, and other regularization details.
- Request to clearly describe dropout types and where they are applied (recurrent/variational, embedding, input/output, residual/skip).
- Ask for precise hyperparameter configurations for the three scaled configurations/sizes, including how width/depth were changed.
- Request listing of random seeds or seed selection procedure.
- Request same detailed reporting for other experiments (character-level LM and vision): final architecture specs, full training hyperparameters, task-specific regularization/preprocessing, and, if possible, released cells/blocks and training scripts.
- Suggest reporting the search budget in standardized terms (architectures sampled, epochs per candidate, compute used) and clarifying any manual post-search tuning.
- Overall: strong paper with convincing multi-domain results; main shortcoming is lack of reproducibility details.
- Recommendation: Accept.
