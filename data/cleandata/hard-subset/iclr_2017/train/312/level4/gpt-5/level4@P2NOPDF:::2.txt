This paper proposes an actor–critic approach to neural architecture search. It models a DNN as a variable-length sequence and uses reinforcement learning to select architectural components, where each node selection is treated as an action and the validation performance of the resulting architecture provides the reward signal. The controller (actor) and critic are implemented as autoregressive two-layer LSTMs. The method is evaluated on two tasks and compared against several human-designed architectures.

This is an exciting paper. Manually designing architectures is challenging, and it is unclear how close hand-crafted designs are to optimal. The proposed method is novel and well motivated. The authors present the approach in detail, including practical improvements needed for stable and efficient training. The experiments are thoughtfully executed and demonstrate the method’s capabilities. The analysis comparing automatically discovered architectures with human-designed ones is particularly interesting and informative. The paper is clearly written and accessible, with a solid discussion of related work and how this contribution differs.

I would welcome more information on training cost: time to convergence, resource usage, and how these correlate with final model quality. It would also be valuable to see results on human-initialized or human-bootstrapped models to understand whether combining human priors with the controller accelerates search or improves outcomes over time.

Overall, this is an excellent and compelling paper.
