- Proposes an actor–critic RL method for neural architecture search, modeling DNNs as variable-length sequences with node selections as actions and validation performance as reward.  
- Controller (actor) and critic are implemented as autoregressive two-layer LSTMs.  
- Evaluated on two tasks and compared against several human-designed architectures.  
- Novel and well-motivated approach with detailed methodological presentation.  
- Includes practical improvements for stable and efficient training.  
- Experiments are thoughtfully executed and demonstrate the method’s capabilities.  
- Analysis comparing automatically discovered architectures to human designs is informative.  
- Paper is clearly written, accessible, and situates the contribution in related work.  
- Requests more information on training cost (time to convergence, resources, correlation with model quality).  
- Suggests experiments with human-initialized or human-bootstrapped models to test combining human priors with the controller.  
- Overall assessment: excellent and compelling paper.
