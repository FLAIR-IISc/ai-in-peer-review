The paper states: “[a]fter the controller RNN is done training, we take the best RNN cell according to the lowest validation perplexity and then run a grid search over learning rate, weight initialization, dropout rates and decay epoch. The best cell found was then run with three different configurations and sizes to increase its capacity.” Could you include (or provide here) the specific hyperparameters and the types of dropout used (e.g., recurrent dropout, embedding dropout, etc.)? Without these details, reproducing the results would require substantial trial and error. As in “Recurrent Neural Network Regularization” (Zaremba et al., 2014), releasing a base set of hyperparameters would greatly aid future work. The same information would also be valuable for the other experiments, such as the character-level language model.

This paper addresses an important area—automating architecture search. Although the method is currently computationally intensive, this trade-off will likely improve as hardware and software continue to advance.

The work spans both vision and text tasks and tackles many benchmark datasets, demonstrating that exploring beyond conventional RNN and CNN search spaces can yield gains. While it would always be desirable to test the approach on additional datasets, the current scope is already ample to show that the technique is not only competitive with human architectural intuition but may even surpass it. It also points to a practical way to tailor architectures to specific datasets without requiring hand-engineering at each stage.

This is a well-written paper on an interesting topic with strong results. I recommend it for acceptance.
