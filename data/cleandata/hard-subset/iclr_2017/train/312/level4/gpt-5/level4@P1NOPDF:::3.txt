This paper proposes using an RNN trained with reinforcement learning (RL) to design network architectures for specific tasks. The idea is promising, and experiments on two datasets suggest the approach is sound.

Pros:
- Framing architecture design as an RNN that generates network descriptions, and training this controller via RL, is interesting and promising.
- The generated architectures resemble human-designed ones, indicating that the learned designs are compatible with established human expertise.

Cons:
- Training is very time-consuming, even with substantial computational resources.
- The experiments do not demonstrate the generality of the generated architectures. It would be helpful to evaluate performance on related but distinct datasets to assess transferability, especially for the generated sequential models.

Overall, this is a strong paper with a compelling idea and encouraging results. However, it needs more experiments to substantiate its potential advantages over human-designed models and to better assess generality.
