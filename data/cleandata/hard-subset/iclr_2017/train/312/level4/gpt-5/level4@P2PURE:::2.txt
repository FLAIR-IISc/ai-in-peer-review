This paper proposes a method for searching optimal neural network architectures using an actor-critic reinforcement learning framework. It represents a DNN as a variable-length sequence and uses RL to discover target architectures, where node selection constitutes the actions and the evaluation error of the resulting architecture provides the reward signal. An autoregressive two-layer LSTM is used as both the controller (actor) and the critic. The method is evaluated on two distinct problems and, for each, is compared against several human-designed architectures.

This is a very exciting paper. Manually selecting architectures is difficult, and it is hard to know how close hand-designed networks are to optimal. The approach is novel, and the authors present it in detail, including the necessary practical improvements. The experiments effectively showcase the method’s capabilities. The differences between the generated architectures and those designed by humans are particularly interesting. The paper is clearly written and accessible, with a thorough and well-positioned discussion of related work.

It would be valuable to report the training time and to analyze the correlation between time/resources required and the resulting model quality. It would also be interesting to see how human-bootstrapped models perform and evolve.

Overall, an excellent and compelling paper.
