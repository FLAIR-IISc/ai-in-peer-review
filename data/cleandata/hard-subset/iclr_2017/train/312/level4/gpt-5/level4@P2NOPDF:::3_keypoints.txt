- Proposes using an RNN trained with reinforcement learning to automatically design task-specific neural network architectures.
- Method is promising and shows sound results on two datasets.
- Employing an RNN to generate architectural descriptions optimized via RL is an interesting, novel approach.
- Discovered architectures resemble human-designed networks, suggesting compatibility with human expertise.
- Training is computationally intensive and time-consuming, even with substantial resources.
- Experimental evidence of generality is limited; evaluations on related but distinct datasets (especially for sequential models) are needed.
- Overall: strong and interesting paper, but needs additional experiments to clearly demonstrate advantages over human-designed models.
