This paper presents a method for searching optimal neural network architectures using an actor–critic reinforcement learning framework. The approach treats a DNN as a variable-length sequence and uses RL to find target architectures via an actor; node selection is the action in the RL setting, and the evaluation error of the resulting architecture serves as the reward. An autoregressive two-layer LSTM is used as both controller and critic. The method is evaluated on two different problems, each compared against a number of human-created architectures.

This is a very exciting paper. Hand-selecting architectures is difficult, and it is hard to know how far from optimal the hand-designed networks are. The presented method is novel. The authors do an excellent job of describing it in detail, including the necessary improvements. The experiments effectively represent the capabilities of the method. It is very interesting to see the differences between the generated architectures and human-generated ones. The paper is written clearly and is accessible. The coverage of related literature and the contrasts drawn are well done.

It would be interesting to see data on the time needed for training, and an analysis of the correlation between the time/resources required and the resulting model quality. It would also be interesting to see how human-bootstrapped models perform and evolve.

Overall, an excellent and interesting paper.
