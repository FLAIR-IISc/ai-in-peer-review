This paper proposes using a recurrent neural network (RNN) trained via reinforcement learning (RL) to design network architectures for specific tasks. The idea is promising, and the experimental results on two datasets suggest the method is sound.

Pros:
1. Employing an RNN to generate architecture descriptions and using RL to train the RNN is an interesting and promising approach.
2. The generated architectures resemble human-designed ones, indicating that human expertise and the learned architectures are compatible.

Cons:
1. Training is very time-consuming, even with substantial computational resources.
2. The experiments do not demonstrate the generalization of the generated architectures. It would be valuable to evaluate performance on additional, related but distinct datasets, especially for the generated sequential models.

Overall, this is a strong paper, but it needs more experiments to convincingly show its potential advantages over human-designed models.
