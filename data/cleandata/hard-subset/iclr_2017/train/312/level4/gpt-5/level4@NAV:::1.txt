The best cell found was then run with three different configurations and sizes to increase its capacity."

Without these details, reproduction would likely require extensive trial and error. As in "Recurrent Neural Network Regularization" (Zaremba et al., 2014), releasing a baseline set of hyperparameters substantially aids future research.

This work tackles a crucial area of our field: automating architecture search. While the approach is currently computationally demanding, this trade-off will likely improve as technology advances.
The paper addresses both standard vision and text tasks across many benchmark datasets, demonstrating that gains are achievable by moving beyond conventional RNN and CNN search spaces. Although one could always wish for evaluation on additional datasets, the current coverage is already ample to show the method is not only competitive with human architectural intuition but may even exceed it. This further suggests a path to tailoring architectures to specific datasets without hand-engineering at each stage.
This is a well-written paper on an important topic with strong results. I recommend acceptance.
