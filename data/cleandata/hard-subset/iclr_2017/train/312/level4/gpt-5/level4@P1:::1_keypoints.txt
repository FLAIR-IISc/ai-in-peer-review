- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS): a controller RNN trained with REINFORCE to sample variable-length neural architectures whose child networks are trained and evaluated to provide rewards.  
- Supports flexible search space including skip connections and multiple layer types, and scales via distributed asynchronous training.  
- Discovers novel CNNs on CIFAR-10 and an RNN cell on PTB that rival or surpass strong human-designed baselines and transfer to other tasks (char PTB, GNMT).  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: strong empirical results across vision and language showing practical value and near-/state-of-the-art performance.  
- Key reason: clear, well-motivated method that meaningfully expands architecture search beyond standard hyperparameter optimization.  
- Problem significance: automating architecture design reduces reliance on expert intuition and enables task-specific models.  
- Method placement: positioned relative to Bayesian hyperparameter optimization and neuroevolution, emphasizing flexibility for variable-length spaces and scalability.  
- Empirical support: competitive/efficient CIFAR-10 models; PTB cell improves perplexity and generalizes; control experiments (larger search space, random search comparisons) validate the approach.  
- Limitation: method is computationally intensive, but deemed a reasonable trade-off given compute trends and results.  
- Reproducibility concern: paper should provide consolidated hyperparameters and training settings (final grid-search details, seeds, infrastructure) to enable replication.  
- Requested detailed info: dropout types/rates, optimization schedule and clipping, initialization schemes, model capacities, training regimen, reward shaping constants, data preprocessing/augmentation, search-space enumeration and constraints, final CIFAR-10 architectures and measurement of speedup.
