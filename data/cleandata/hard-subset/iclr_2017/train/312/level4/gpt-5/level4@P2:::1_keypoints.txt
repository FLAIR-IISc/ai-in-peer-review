- Proposes Neural Architecture Search (NAS): a controller RNN trained with REINFORCE to generate variable-length neural architectures.  
- Scales via distributed asynchronous training and includes modern search-space features (skip connections, multiple layer types).  
- Demonstrates strong results: CIFAR-10 CNN (3.65% error, 1.05x faster vs comparable DenseNet), PTB recurrent cell (62.4 perplexity) with transfer improvements (char PTB, GNMT +0.5 BLEU).  
- Paper is well written, well positioned relative to hyperparameter optimization, neuroevolution, and meta-learning.  

Decision: Accept.  
- Key reasons: strong cross-domain empirical evidence for automated architecture search.  
- Key reasons: method is clear, broadens practical search space (variable length, skip connections), and outperforms a strong random-search baseline.  

Supporting arguments:  
- Problem significance: tackles automatic architecture design, addressing a core ML bottleneck beyond fixed-length hyperparameter tuning.  
- Methodological soundness: controller RNN + policy gradient appropriate; includes reward shaping, moving-average baseline for variance reduction, and scalable distributed setup.  
- Positioning: contrasts to Bayesian optimization, neuroevolution, program induction, and meta-learning are accurate and fair.  
- Empirical rigor: evaluations span vision and language, include transfer tasks, and compare to strong baselines including random search.  
- Practical relevance: computationally intensive but potentially amortizable; reported speed-accuracy tradeoffs are promising.  

Requested clarifications / reproducibility questions:  
- Hyperparameters for final training: provide full PTB settings (learning rates, inits, decay schedule, gradient clipping, optimizer, exact dropout configs) and CIFAR-10 final training schedule (LR, decays, weight decay, batchnorm params, momentum/Nesterov, augmentations).  
- Dropout/regularization: specify which dropout variants and exact rates per PTB configuration, and for char PTB and GNMT transfers (embedding dropout, variational dropout, weight decay).  
- Search & compute budget: report total sampled/evaluated architectures per task, avg training time per child, total GPU/CPU hours, and when the best model was found.  
- Child training schedule: provide schedule for increasing child-network depth, increment frequency, and any early-stopping criteria.  
- Reward shaping & baseline: justify cubing accuracy for CIFAR-10 and squaring inverse perplexity for PTB; report sensitivity/ablation to reward scaling and details of moving-average baseline (decay factor, normalization).  
- Search-space specification: enumerate exact CNN options (filter sizes, counts, strides, layer types, pooling, constraints) and RNN cell choices (combinators, activations, tree indexing, ct and ct−1 injections).  
- Skip-connection mechanics: clarify padding/concatenation rules for mismatched shapes and whether projection layers were allowed.  
- Fairness of comparisons: clarify parameter-count differences and architectural choices (bottlenecks/transitions) in CIFAR-10 vs DenseNet to contextualize speedup.  
- Variance reporting: provide variance across multiple runs for both controller training and final child models (different seeds).  
- Reproducibility aids: include configuration files or scripts to reproduce best PTB and CIFAR-10 models (similar to prior released baselines).
