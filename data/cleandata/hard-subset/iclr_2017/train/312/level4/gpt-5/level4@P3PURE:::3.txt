This paper proposes using reinforcement learning (RL) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs) to design architectures for specific tasks. The idea is quite promising, and the experimental results on two datasets show that the method is solid.

Pros:
1. Using an RNN to produce a description of the network and using RL to train the RNN is interesting and promising.
2. The generated architectures look similar to those designed by humans, suggesting that human expertise and the generated architectures are compatible.

Cons:
1. The training time is long, even with substantial computing resources.
2. The experiments do not demonstrate the generality of the generated architectures. It would be valuable to see the performance of the generated architectures on other similar but different datasets, especially for the generated sequential models.

Overall, I believe this is a nice paper, but it needs more experiments to demonstrate its potential advantage over human-designed models.
