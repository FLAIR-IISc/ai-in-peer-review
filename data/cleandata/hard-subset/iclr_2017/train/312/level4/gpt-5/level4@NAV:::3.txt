This paper proposes using reinforcement learning and a recurrent neural network to design network architectures tailored to specific tasks. The idea is promising, and results on two datasets indicate the method is sound.

The pros of the paper are:
1. Employing an RNN to emit network descriptions and training it via RL is interesting and promising.
2. The resulting architectures resemble human-designed ones, suggesting alignment between human expertise and the automatically generated designs.

The cons of the paper are:
1. Training is time-consuming, even with substantial computational resources.
2. The experiments do not establish the generality of the generated architectures. It would be valuable to evaluate their performance on related but distinct datasets, especially for the sequential models.

Overall, I find this to be a solid paper, but it requires additional experiments to substantiate its potential advantages over human-designed models.
