- Introduces Neural Architecture Search (NAS): a controller RNN trained with REINFORCE to sequentially generate variable-length neural architectures evaluated as “child” networks.
- Search space supports modern elements (skip connections, multiple layer types) via set-selection attention and extends to recurrent cell design by labeling computation tree nodes.
- Uses large-scale asynchronous distributed training to evaluate many candidate architectures in parallel.
- CIFAR-10 result: a 39-layer CNN achieves 3.65% test error, outperforming a comparable DenseNet and running ~1.05x faster.
- Simpler CIFAR-10 settings still yield competitive error (5.50%) with shallower architectures.
- PTB result: a learned RNN cell attains 62.4 perplexity, ~3.6 better than prior SOTA, transfers to char-level PTB (1.214) and improves GNMT by +0.5 BLEU without tuning.
- NAS outperforms random search baselines and remains robust when expanding the search space.
- Decision: Accept.
- Reason 1: Novel, general, and well-motivated approach enabling variable-length, richly structured architecture search beyond fixed-length hyperparameter tuning.
- Reason 2: Strong empirical results across vision and language with appropriate controls and clear methodological details.
- Problem/motivation: Tackles automatic architecture design, addressing limitations of Bayesian optimization and neuro-evolution approaches.
- Methodological soundness: Controller, reward design, policy-gradient training, skip-connection/recurrent extensions, and handling of invalid graphs are well described; distributed implementation is appropriate.
- Empirical support: Competitive or SOTA results on CIFAR-10 and PTB, sensible ablations/controls, and credible transfer of learned RNN cell; visualizations aid interpretability.
- Rigor: Comparisons control for training protocols, use held-out validation for rewards, and report unseen test performance; key settings and schedules disclosed.
- Limitation 1: Extremely high computational cost (e.g., 800 GPUs for CIFAR-10, 400 CPUs for PTB), hindering adoption and reproducibility.
- Limitation 2: Transfer demonstrations stronger for RNN cell than CNNs; additional cross-dataset validation would strengthen generality claims.
- Suggestion: Report compute metrics (wall-clock, GPU/CPU-hours, energy) and number of architectures evaluated; plot best/mean reward vs compute.
- Suggestion: Provide results across multiple controller seeds and confidence intervals to quantify variance and robustness.
- Suggestion: Discuss safeguards against overfitting the controller to the validation set and show correlation between validation reward and final test performance.
- Suggestion: Clarify fairness of CIFAR-10 comparisons (parameter counts, FLOPs, training/augmentation) and, if possible, include matched-parameter/FLOP comparisons.
- Suggestion: Analyze sensitivity to search-space choices (filter sizes, strides, pooling, normalization).
- Suggestion: Justify reward shaping (cubing accuracy), baseline choice, and include sensitivity studies on reward normalization and advantage estimation.
- Suggestion: Handle invalid architectures more robustly (penalize/exclude degenerate cases rather than default fixes).
- Suggestion: Test top CIFAR-10 architectures on CIFAR-100 or SVHN to validate CNN transferability.
- Suggestion: Release trained controller checkpoints, top-k architectures, and training scripts to improve reproducibility.
- Questions: Provide total GPU/CPU-hours, number of architectures trained, and fraction invalid/pruned for CIFAR-10 and PTB searches.
- Questions: Report consistency across independent controller runs and variance in best-found performance and top-k distributions.
- Questions: Describe effects of reward normalization and child-depth schedules on stability and performance; were rewards normalized across depths?
- Questions: Did you compare to Bayesian/SMBO methods that handle variable-length structures (e.g., TPE with a grammar)?
- Questions: Provide matched parameter/FLOP comparisons and clarify training differences vs DenseNet; confirm whether 3.65% uses ensembling.
- Questions: Detail regularization/hyperparameter settings for the learned PTB cell versus baselines and whether baselines were equally tuned.
- Questions: For GNMT transfer, were hyperparameters retuned and how sensitive is the +0.5 BLEU gain to such choices?
