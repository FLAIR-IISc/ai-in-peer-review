- Proposal: Use an RNN trained with reinforcement learning to generate network architectures tailored to specific tasks.
- Evidence: Results on two datasets show the method is promising and produces sound architectures.
- Pro 1: Emitting network descriptions with an RNN and training via RL is an interesting and promising approach.
- Pro 2: Automatically generated architectures resemble human-designed ones, suggesting alignment with expert designs.
- Con 1: Training is very time-consuming, even with substantial computational resources.
- Con 2: Experiments do not establish generality; need evaluations on related but distinct datasets (especially for sequential models).
- Overall: Solid paper that requires additional experiments to demonstrate advantages over human-designed models.
