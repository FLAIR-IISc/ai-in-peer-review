This paper presents a method for searching optimal neural network architectures using an actor-critic framework. The method treats a DNN as a variable-length sequence and uses reinforcement learning to find target architectures via an actor. Node selection is framed as an action in the RL setting, and the evaluation error of the resulting architecture serves as the reward. An autoregressive two-layer LSTM is used as both the controller and the critic. The approach is evaluated on two distinct problems and compared against several human-designed architectures.

This is a very exciting paper. Hand-designing architectures is difficult, and it is hard to know how far hand-crafted networks are from optimal. The proposed method is novel, and the authors do an excellent job of describing it in detail, including the practical improvements required. The experiments effectively demonstrate the method’s capabilities. It is particularly interesting to see the differences between the generated architectures and human-designed ones. The paper is clearly written and accessible, and the coverage of and comparison to related work are strong.

It would be useful to report the time and computational resources required for training, and to analyze how these correlate with the final model quality. It would also be interesting to evaluate human-bootstrapped models—starting from human designs—and track how they perform and evolve.

Overall, an excellent and compelling paper.
