This work explores discovering optimal neural network architectures via an actor-critic framework. The approach treats a DNN as a variable-length sequence and employs reinforcement learning to identify the target architecture, which serves as the actor. Node selection constitutes the actions in the RL setup, and the evaluation error of the resulting architecture provides the reward. An autoregressive two-layer LSTM is used as both the controller and the critic. The method is evaluated on two separate problems and, in each case, compared against several human-designed architectures.

This is a very exciting paper. Manually selecting architectures is challenging, and it is difficult to know how far hand-designed networks are from optimal. The proposed method is novel. The authors present it in detail, including all necessary improvements. The test data appropriately represent the method’s capabilities. The differences between the generated architectures and those designed by humans are particularly interesting. The writing is very clear and accessible. The coverage and comparison with related literature are well handled.

It would be useful to include data on training time and the correlation between time/resources required and model quality. It would also be interesting to see how human-bootstrapped models perform and evolve.

Overall, an excellent and engaging paper.
