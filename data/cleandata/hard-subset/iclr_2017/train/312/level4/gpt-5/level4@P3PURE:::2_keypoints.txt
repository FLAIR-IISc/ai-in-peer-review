- Introduces an actor–critic RL method to search for optimal neural network architectures.  
- Treats a DNN as a variable-length sequence where node selection is the RL action.  
- Uses the evaluation error of the sampled architecture as the reward signal.  
- Employs an autoregressive two-layer LSTM as both the controller (actor) and the critic.  
- Evaluated on two tasks and compared against multiple human-designed architectures.  
- Method is novel, well-motivated, and the paper gives detailed descriptions and necessary improvements.  
- Experiments effectively demonstrate the method’s capabilities and highlight differences from human-generated architectures.  
- Writing is clear, accessible, and the related work coverage and contrasts are strong.  
- Suggests adding training-time/resource data and analysis of resource–quality correlation.  
- Suggests evaluating how human-bootstrapped models perform and evolve.  
- Overall assessment: excellent, interesting, and highly promising work.
