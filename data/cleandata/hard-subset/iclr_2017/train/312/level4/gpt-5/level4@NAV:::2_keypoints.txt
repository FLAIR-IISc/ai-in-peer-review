- Proposes an actor-critic framework to discover optimal neural architectures by treating a DNN as a variable-length sequence; node selection are actions and evaluation error is the reward, with an autoregressive two-layer LSTM as controller and critic.  
- Evaluated on two separate problems and compared against several human-designed architectures.  
- Novel method addressing the difficulty of manual architecture selection and the unknown gap to optimality.  
- Detailed presentation including necessary improvements; writing is clear and accessible.  
- Test datasets appropriately reflect the method’s capabilities.  
- Differences between generated and human-designed architectures are particularly interesting.  
- Coverage of and comparison with related literature are well handled.  
- Recommendation to include data on training time and correlation between resources used and model quality.  
- Suggestion to examine performance and evolution of human-bootstrapped models.  
- Overall assessment: excellent and engaging paper.
