- Proposes an actor-critic RL method for neural architecture search, representing DNNs as variable-length sequences with node-selection actions and evaluation error as reward.
- Uses an autoregressive two-layer LSTM as both the controller (actor) and the critic.
- Evaluated on two distinct tasks and compared against several human-designed architectures.
- Novel and exciting approach addressing the difficulty of manual architecture selection.
- Method is presented in detail, including practical improvements.
- Experiments effectively demonstrate the method’s capabilities.
- Notable differences between generated architectures and human-designed ones.
- Paper is clearly written, accessible, and contains a thorough related-work discussion.
- Recommends reporting training time and analyzing resource–quality correlation.
- Suggests investigating how human-bootstrapped models perform and evolve.
- Overall assessment: excellent and compelling paper.
