- Proposes neural architecture search using an RNN controller and reinforcement learning to generate variable-length DNN architectures.  
- Frames node selection as RL actions and uses evaluation error of architectures as the reward signal.  
- Employs an auto-regressive two-layer LSTM as both the controller and critic.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10 (image classification) and Penn Treebank (language modeling).  
- Compared against several human-designed architectures and found competitive results.  
- Clear, accessible writing with detailed method description and well-covered related work.  
- Demonstrates interesting differences between discovered architectures and human designs.  
- Suggests adding analysis of training time and the relationship between compute resources and model quality.  
- Recommends exploring human-bootstrapped models and incorporating them into the framework.  
- Overall assessment: an excellent, promising approach to neural architecture search.
