- Proposes a novel method using RNNs to generate network descriptions and reinforcement learning to train the RNN for task-specific architecture design.
- Underlying idea is promising and experiments on two datasets show the method can be effective.
- Generated architectures exhibit similarities to human-designed networks, suggesting compatibility with human expertise.
- Paper is well-written and built on a solid foundation.
- Training times are lengthy even with substantial computational resources.
- Experimental evidence is insufficient to demonstrate the generality of the generated architectures.
- Needs evaluation of generated architectures on additional, similar but distinct datasets—particularly sequential models.
- Additional experiments are required to demonstrate clear advantages over human-designed models and to fully explore the method’s capabilities.
