This paper proposes a novel approach to designing network architectures for specific tasks by leveraging reinforcement learning (RL) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs). The underlying idea is quite promising, and the experimental results on two datasets demonstrate the solidity of the method.

The paper has several strengths:

1. The concept of utilizing RNNs to produce network descriptions and RL to train the RNNs is interesting and shows promise.
2. The generated architectures exhibit similarities to those designed by humans, indicating that human expertise and generated network architectures are compatible.

However, there are also some weaknesses:

1. The training time for the network is lengthy, even with substantial computing resources.
2. The experiments do not provide sufficient evidence of the generality of the generated architectures. It would be beneficial to see the performance of the generated architectures on other similar yet distinct datasets, particularly for the generated sequential models.

Overall, I believe this is a nice paper, but it requires additional experiments to demonstrate its potential advantages over human-designed models.
