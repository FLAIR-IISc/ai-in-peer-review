This paper proposes a novel approach to designing network architectures for specific tasks by leveraging reinforcement learning (RL) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs). The core idea of the paper is promising, and the experimental results on two datasets demonstrate the solidity of the method.

The paper has several strengths. Firstly, the idea of utilizing RNNs to generate network descriptions and RL to train the RNNs is intriguing and shows promise. Secondly, the generated architectures exhibit similarities to those designed by humans, indicating that human expertise and generated network architectures are compatible.

However, there are also some limitations to the paper. One major concern is the long training time required for the network, even with substantial computational resources. Furthermore, the experiments do not provide sufficient evidence of the generality of the generated architectures. It would be beneficial to see the performance of the generated architectures on other similar but distinct datasets, particularly for the sequential models.

Overall, I believe this is a well-written paper with a compelling idea. Nevertheless, additional experiments are necessary to fully demonstrate its potential advantages over human-designed models. The authors may consider exploring ways to improve the efficiency of the training process and conducting more comprehensive evaluations to establish the generalizability of their approach. With further development and testing, this method could potentially offer a significant contribution to the field.
