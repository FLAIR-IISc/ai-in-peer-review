The paper proposes a novel approach to neural architecture search, utilizing a recurrent neural network as a controller to generate model descriptions of neural networks. The controller is trained with reinforcement learning to maximize the expected accuracy of the generated architectures on a validation set. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank datasets, achieving state-of-the-art results on both tasks.

I recommend accepting this paper. The main reasons for my decision are the paper's strong empirical performance on challenging benchmarks and its potential to contribute to the development of a new research direction in automatic neural architecture search.

The paper provides a thorough explanation of the proposed method, including the architecture of the controller and the training process. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the results, including comparisons with state-of-the-art models and ablation studies. The writing is clear and concise, making the paper easy to follow.

One potential area for improvement is the provision of additional details on the hyperparameters and experimental settings used in the experiments. For example, the authors mention that they use a grid search over learning rate, weight initialization, dropout rates, and decay epoch to find the best RNN cell, but they do not provide the specific values used in this search. Providing these details would facilitate replication and further research in this area.

Additionally, it would be interesting to see the application of this method to more datasets and tasks, as well as comparisons with other automatic architecture search methods. However, the authors have already demonstrated the effectiveness of their approach on two challenging tasks, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.

To further improve the paper, the authors may consider providing more details on the following:

* Hyperparameters and experimental settings used in the experiments
* Comparison with other automatic architecture search methods
* Application of the method to more datasets and tasks
* Analysis of the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method

Overall, I believe that this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of neural architecture search and has the potential to inspire further research in this area.
