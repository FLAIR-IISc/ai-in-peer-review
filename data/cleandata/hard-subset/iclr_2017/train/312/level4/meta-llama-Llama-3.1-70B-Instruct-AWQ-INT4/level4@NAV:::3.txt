This paper presents a novel approach to designing network architectures for specific tasks by leveraging reinforcement learning (RL) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs). The concept is intriguing, and the experimental results on two datasets demonstrate the method's viability.

The paper's strengths include:

1. The innovative use of RNNs to generate network descriptions and RL to train the RNNs is a compelling and promising idea.
2. The generated architectures exhibit similarities to human-designed networks, indicating a compatibility between human expertise and the produced network architectures.

However, the paper also has some limitations:

1. The training process is computationally intensive, resulting in prolonged training times even with substantial computational resources.
2. The experiments lack generality, as they do not demonstrate the performance of the generated architectures on other similar yet distinct datasets, particularly for the generated sequential models.

Overall, this is a well-written paper with potential. Nevertheless, further experiments are necessary to fully realize its advantages over human-designed models and to showcase its broader applicability.
