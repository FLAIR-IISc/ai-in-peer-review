This paper presents a novel approach to searching for optimal neural network architectures using a recurrent neural network (RNN) as a controller, trained with reinforcement learning to maximize the expected accuracy of the generated architectures on a validation set. The method is evaluated on two challenging benchmarks, CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, and demonstrates strong empirical performance, rivaling and even surpassing human-invented architectures.

I decide to accept this paper, with two key reasons for this choice. Firstly, the paper presents a well-motivated and novel approach to neural architecture search, which is a significant problem in the field of deep learning. The use of an RNN as a controller and reinforcement learning to optimize the architecture search process is innovative and shows promise. Secondly, the paper provides comprehensive experiments and results on two challenging benchmarks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

The paper is well-written, and the authors do an excellent job of describing the method in detail, including the improvements that were needed to be done. The coverage and contrast with related literature are also well-done. The tested data represents well the capability of the method, and it is interesting to see the differences between the generated architectures and human-generated ones.

To further improve the paper, I would like to see some additional information about the time and resources needed to train the models, as well as the correlation between these factors and the quality of the models. It would also be interesting to see how human-bootstrapped models perform and how they can be involved in the architecture search process.

Some questions I would like the authors to answer to clarify my understanding of the paper include:

* Can the authors provide more details about the computational resources and time needed to train the models, and how these factors affect the quality of the generated architectures?
* How do the authors plan to extend the method to more complex tasks, such as image segmentation or object detection?
* Can the authors provide more insights into the differences between the generated architectures and human-generated ones, and what do these differences tell us about the strengths and limitations of the proposed method?

Additional feedback to help improve the paper includes:

* Consider adding more visualizations of the generated architectures, such as diagrams or plots, to help readers better understand the structure and complexity of the models.
* Provide more details about the hyperparameter tuning process, including the ranges of values searched and the methods used to select the optimal hyperparameters.
* Consider adding more discussion about the potential applications and implications of the proposed method, such as its potential to accelerate the development of AI systems or improve their performance in real-world tasks.
