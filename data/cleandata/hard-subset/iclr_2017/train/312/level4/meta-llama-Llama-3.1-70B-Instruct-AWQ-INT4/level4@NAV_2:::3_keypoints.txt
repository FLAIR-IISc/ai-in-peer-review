- Proposes Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller trained with RL to generate neural network architectures.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10 (image classification) and Penn Treebank (language modeling).  
- Generated architectures resemble human-designed models, indicating capture of expert patterns.  
- Experimental results show generated models rival state-of-the-art performance.  
- Strength: innovative combination of RL and RNNs to automate architecture design.  
- Major limitation: substantial training time and heavy computational resource requirements for the controller.  
- Limitation: experiments do not fully assess generalizability to other datasets, especially for sequential models.  
- Overall assessment: well-written, thought-provoking, and promising approach.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Reasons for decision: novelty of method and strong empirical results on two benchmarks.  
- Recommendation: explore techniques to reduce controller training time (more efficient RL, transfer learning).  
- Recommendation: provide additional experiments to demonstrate generalizability across similar but distinct datasets.  
- Recommendation: compare against other automated design methods (Bayesian optimization, evolutionary algorithms).  
- Question for authors: provide details on computational resources and convergence time for training the controller.  
- Question for authors: how will you address training time and scalability for larger problems, and will you apply NAS to other domains (NLP, RL)?
