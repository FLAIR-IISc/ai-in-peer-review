- Proposes an actor-critic reinforcement learning approach for neural architecture search (NAS).  
- Represents DNNs as variable-length sequences; node selection is an action and evaluation error is the reward.  
- Uses a two-layer auto-regressive LSTM as both the controller (actor) and the critic.  
- Evaluated on two distinct problems with comparisons to multiple human-designed architectures.  
- Demonstrates that the method effectively discovers architectures and yields intriguing differences from human designs.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides thorough methodological description and refinements.  
- Comprehensive coverage of related work and thoughtful comparisons to existing approaches.  
- Reviewer views the contribution as significant and timely given the difficulty of manual architecture selection.  
- Suggests reporting training time and analyzing correlation between compute/resources and model quality.  
- Recommends exploring performance and role of human-bootstrapped models in the design process.
