This paper presents a novel approach to automating the design of neural network architectures, a problem that has become increasingly important as the field of deep learning continues to grow. The authors propose using a recurrent neural network (RNN) as a controller to generate model descriptions of neural networks, which are then trained using reinforcement learning to maximize the expected accuracy of the generated architectures on a validation set.

The paper claims to make several contributions, including the development of a flexible and efficient method for searching variable-length architecture spaces, and the demonstration of strong empirical performance on several challenging benchmarks, including CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of their approach, including the use of a policy gradient method to update the controller, and the incorporation of skip connections and other layer types to increase the complexity of the generated architectures.

I recommend accepting this paper, as it presents a well-written and well-motivated approach to an important problem in the field of deep learning. The authors provide strong empirical results, and their approach has the potential to make a significant impact on the field.

One minor suggestion I have is to include more details about the hyperparameters and type of dropout used in the experiments, particularly in the section on Penn Treebank. This would make it easier for others to replicate the results and build upon the work presented in the paper.

Additionally, I would like to ask the authors to clarify a few points:

* Can the authors provide more details about the computational resources required to run the experiments, particularly in terms of the number of GPUs and the amount of time required to train the controller?
* How do the authors plan to address the issue of overfitting in the generated architectures, particularly in cases where the validation set is small?
* Can the authors provide more details about the types of architectures that are generated by the controller, and how they compare to human-designed architectures?

Overall, I think this is a strong paper that presents a novel and effective approach to automating the design of neural network architectures. With a few minor revisions, I believe it has the potential to make a significant impact on the field.
