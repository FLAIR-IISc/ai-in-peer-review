This paper proposes a novel approach to designing network architectures for specific tasks by leveraging reinforcement learning (RL) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs). The core idea is promising, and the experimental results on two datasets demonstrate the method's efficacy.

The paper has several strengths:

1. The concept of utilizing RNNs to generate network descriptions and training them with RL is intriguing and shows promise.
2. The generated architectures exhibit similarities to those designed by humans, indicating that human expertise and automatically generated network architectures can be compatible.

However, the paper also has some weaknesses:

1. The training time for the network is substantial, even with significant computational resources.
2. The experiments do not adequately demonstrate the generality of the generated architectures. It would be beneficial to see the performance of the generated architectures on other similar yet distinct datasets, particularly for the sequential models.

Overall, I believe this is a well-written paper, but it requires additional experiments to fully demonstrate its potential advantages over human-designed models.
