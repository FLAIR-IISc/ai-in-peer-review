This paper proposes a novel approach to designing neural network architectures using reinforcement learning (RL) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs). The core idea is to use an RNN as a controller to generate the description of a neural network, which is then trained using RL to maximize its performance on a specific task. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach on two challenging benchmarks: CIFAR-10 for image classification and Penn Treebank for language modeling.

The paper has several strengths. Firstly, the idea of using an RNN to generate neural network architectures is innovative and has the potential to automate the design process. Secondly, the experimental results show that the generated architectures can achieve state-of-the-art performance on both benchmarks. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the generated architectures, which shows that they share similarities with human-designed architectures.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. One major concern is the computational cost of training the controller RNN, which requires a large amount of computing resources and time. The authors report that training the controller takes several days, even with a large cluster of GPUs. This raises concerns about the scalability and practicality of the approach.

Another limitation is the lack of generality of the generated architectures. The authors only evaluate the performance of the generated architectures on the specific benchmarks used for training, but do not provide results on other similar but different datasets. This makes it difficult to assess the robustness and generalizability of the approach.

To strengthen the paper, I would suggest the following:

* Provide more details on the computational cost and scalability of the approach, including the number of GPUs used and the training time.
* Evaluate the performance of the generated architectures on other similar but different datasets to demonstrate their generality.
* Compare the performance of the generated architectures with human-designed architectures on the same benchmarks.
* Discuss the potential applications and limitations of the approach, including its potential to automate the design process and its limitations in terms of computational cost and scalability.

Overall, I believe that this is a promising paper that has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of neural network design. However, it requires more experiments and analysis to demonstrate its effectiveness and generality.
