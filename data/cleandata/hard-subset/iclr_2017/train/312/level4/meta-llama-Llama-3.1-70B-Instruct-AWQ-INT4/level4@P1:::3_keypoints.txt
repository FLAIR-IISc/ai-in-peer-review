- Proposes Neural Architecture Search: a controller RNN trained with reinforcement learning to generate neural network architectures.  
- Applied method to CIFAR-10 (image classification) and Penn Treebank (language modeling).  
- Generated architectures are competitive with state-of-the-art human-designed models.  
- Decision: accept.  
- Key reasons: novel RL+RNN approach and strong experimental results on two challenging tasks.  
- Method is well-motivated, clearly explained, and experiments are thorough with detailed analysis.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations, notably long training times and large compute requirements.  
- Suggestion: add experiments to show generality on other similar but different datasets.  
- Suggestion: discuss potential applications beyond the two presented tasks.  
- Suggestion: provide more details on hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity.  
- Question: how will the authors address long training times and heavy computing needs?  
- Question: can authors provide deeper insights comparing generated architectures to human-designed models?  
- Question: are there plans to apply the method to other tasks or domains, and what challenges/opportunities exist?
