- Proposes a novel actor-critic reinforcement learning approach for neural architecture search.  
- Represents deep neural networks as variable-length sequences; node selection is treated as an action.  
- Uses evaluation error of the sampled architecture as the reward signal.  
- Implements a two-layer auto-regressive LSTM as both the controller (actor) and critic.  
- Evaluated on two distinct problems with comparisons to multiple human-designed architectures.  
- Experimental results show the method’s capability and highlight differences between generated and human architectures.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides thorough methodological details and improvements.  
- Good coverage of related work and effective contrast with existing methods.  
- Reviewer recommends reporting training time and analyzing correlation between compute/resources and model quality.  
- Reviewer suggests exploring human-bootstrapped models and their role in the search process.  
- Overall assessment: an excellent, engaging paper that makes a significant contribution.
