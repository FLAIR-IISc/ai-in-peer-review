- Addresses the important problem of automating neural architecture search, though currently computationally intensive.
- Proposes Neural Architecture Search: an RNN that generates network descriptions and is trained with reinforcement learning to maximize validation accuracy.
- Applies the method to image classification (CIFAR-10) and language modeling (Penn Treebank).
- Demonstrates gains by searching beyond standard RNN/CNN design spaces.
- Achieves strong results on CIFAR-10: test error 3.65%, 0.09% better and 1.05x faster than previous state-of-the-art.
- Designs a novel recurrent cell for Penn Treebank that outperforms LSTM, achieving perplexity 62.4.
- Provides thorough analysis of search space, training details, skip connections, and varied layer types to increase architecture complexity.
- Shows transfer learning results indicating the discovered architectures can generalize to other tasks.
- Minor suggestion: include more details on hyperparameters and dropout (especially for Penn Treebank) to aid replication.
- Recommends acceptance based on novelty, motivation, and strong empirical gains across benchmarks.
- Additional feedback: test on more datasets/tasks to demonstrate generalizability further.
- Additional feedback: include a more detailed discussion of computational resources and potential strategies to reduce computational cost.
- Questions for authors: provide hyperparameter/dropout details for Penn Treebank.
