- Proposes a novel actor-critic RL framework for neural architecture search by treating DNNs as variable-length sequences.  
- Models node selection as the action and architecture evaluation error as the reward.  
- Uses a two-layer auto-regressive LSTM as both the controller and the critic.  
- Evaluated on two problems and compared against multiple human-designed architectures.  
- Method is innovative and addresses the difficulty of manual architecture selection.  
- Paper provides a detailed, clear description and necessary methodological improvements.  
- Experimental results effectively demonstrate the method and reveal interesting differences from human designs.  
- Well-written with thorough literature coverage and clear contrasts to existing work.  
- Reviewer asks for additional data on training time/resource vs. model quality and suggests testing human-bootstrapped models.  
- Overall judgment: excellent paper making a significant contribution to neural architecture search.
