- Proposes an RNN controller that uses reinforcement learning to generate neural network architectures automatically.
- Claims a flexible, efficient method for searching variable-length architecture spaces.
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on benchmarks including CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.
- Provides detailed analysis of the method, using policy gradients and incorporating skip connections and diverse layer types.
- Recommends acceptance, citing strong motivation, writing, and potential impact.
- Suggests including more hyperparameter and dropout details (especially for Penn Treebank) to aid reproducibility.
- Requests clarification on computational resources required (number of GPUs and training time).
- Asks how the authors will address overfitting when validation sets are small.
- Requests more information on the types of architectures generated and comparisons to human-designed architectures.
