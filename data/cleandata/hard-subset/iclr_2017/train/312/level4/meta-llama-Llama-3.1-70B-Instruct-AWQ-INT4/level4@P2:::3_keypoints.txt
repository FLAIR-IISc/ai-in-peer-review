- Novel approach: uses an RNN controller with reinforcement learning to generate neural network architectures.  
- Main contribution: RNN controller + RL enables exploration of a large, variable-length architecture space.  
- Experimental results: strong performance on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, competitive with or better than state-of-the-art.  
- Generated architectures resemble human designs, indicating the method captures domain knowledge.  
- Limitation: very high training time and large computational resource requirements.  
- Limitation: experiments do not fully evaluate generality across other datasets or tasks, especially sequential models.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Reason for acceptance: novel contribution to neural architecture search and strong experimental validation on two datasets.
