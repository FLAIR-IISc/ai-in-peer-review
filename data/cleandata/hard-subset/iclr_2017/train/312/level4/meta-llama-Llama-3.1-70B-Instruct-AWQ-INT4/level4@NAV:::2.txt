This paper proposes a novel approach to searching for optimal neural network architectures using an actor-critic framework. By treating deep neural networks (DNNs) as variable-length sequences and leveraging reinforcement learning (RL), the method identifies target architectures, with node selection serving as actions in the RL context and evaluation errors corresponding to rewards. A two-layer, auto-regressive LSTM functions as both controller and critic. The approach is evaluated on two distinct problems and compared to multiple human-designed architectures.

This paper is particularly noteworthy, as manually selecting architectures can be challenging and it is difficult to determine how far human-designed networks are from achieving optimal results. The authors have made significant contributions by presenting a novel method and providing a detailed description of its implementation, including necessary improvements. The test data effectively showcases the method's capabilities, highlighting intriguing differences between generated and human-designed architectures. The paper is well-written, clear, and accessible, with a thorough coverage of related literature and effective contrast with existing work.

To further enhance the paper, it would be valuable to include data on training time and explore the correlation between training time/resources and model quality. Additionally, investigating the performance of human-bootstrapped models and their potential involvement would be a worthwhile pursuit.

Overall, this is an excellent and engaging paper that makes a significant contribution to the field.
