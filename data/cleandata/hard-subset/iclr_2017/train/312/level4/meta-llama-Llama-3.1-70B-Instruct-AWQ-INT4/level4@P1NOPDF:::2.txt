This paper presents a search for optimal neural network architectures based on the actor-critic framework. The method treats deep neural networks (DNNs) as variable-length sequences and utilizes reinforcement learning (RL) to find the target architecture, which acts as an actor. In this context, node selection is considered an action, and the evaluation error of the resulting architecture corresponds to the reward. An auto-regressive two-layer LSTM is employed as both the controller and critic. The method is evaluated on two distinct problems, with each compared to multiple human-created architectures.

This is a very exciting paper, as hand-selecting architectures can be a challenging and time-consuming task, and it is often difficult to determine how far the hand-designed networks are from optimal results. The presented method is novel and innovative. The authors do an excellent job of describing it in detail, including all the necessary improvements. The tested data effectively demonstrates the capability of the method, and it is intriguing to see the differences between the generated architectures and those created by humans. The paper is written clearly and is very accessible to readers. The coverage and contrast with related literature are also well done.

It would be interesting to see data on the time required for training, as well as the correlation between the time and resources needed to train and the quality of the model. Additionally, it would be beneficial to see how human bootstrapped models perform and evolve over time. 

Overall, this is an excellent and interesting paper.
