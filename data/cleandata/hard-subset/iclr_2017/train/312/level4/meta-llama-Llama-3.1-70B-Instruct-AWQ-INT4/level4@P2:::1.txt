This paper proposes a novel approach to automating neural architecture search using a recurrent neural network as the controller. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on two challenging benchmarks: CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.

The paper clearly states the specific question/problem tackled, which is to design a method for automatically searching for good neural network architectures. The approach is well-motivated, building upon existing work in hyperparameter optimization and neural architecture design. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the strengths and limitations of their method, including its ability to search variable-length architecture spaces and its computational intensity.

The paper supports its claims through extensive experiments on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, demonstrating that the proposed method can design architectures that rival or surpass the best human-invented architectures. The results are scientifically rigorous, with careful consideration of hyperparameter tuning and evaluation metrics.

I recommend accepting this paper, as it presents a significant contribution to the field of neural architecture search. The technique has the potential to automate the design of neural networks, reducing the need for manual tuning and expertise.

To further improve the paper, I suggest the authors provide more details on the hyperparameters and type of dropout used in their experiments, particularly in the Penn Treebank results. This would facilitate replication and future work in the field.

Additionally, I would like to ask the authors to clarify the following points:

1. Can you provide more details on the computational resources required to run the experiments, including the number of GPUs and the training time?
2. How do you plan to address the computational intensity of the method, and what potential solutions do you propose to make it more efficient?
3. Have you considered applying the method to other benchmark datasets or tasks, and if so, what were the results?

Overall, this is a well-written paper on an interesting topic with strong results, and I believe it has the potential to make a significant impact in the field.
