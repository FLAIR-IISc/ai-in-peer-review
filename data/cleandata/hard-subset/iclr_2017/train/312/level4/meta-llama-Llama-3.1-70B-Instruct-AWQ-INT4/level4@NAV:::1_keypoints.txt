- Post-training: authors select the best RNN cell by lowest validation perplexity, then grid-search hyperparameters (learning rate, weight init, dropout rates, decay epoch) and evaluate the top cell in three configurations/sizes.  
- Reviewer requests precise hyperparameter and dropout-type (e.g., recurrent, embedding) details to enable replication.  
- Suggestion to release a baseline hyperparameter set following Zaremba et al. (2014) to aid future work.  
- Such detailed hyperparameter reporting would also benefit related experiments like the character language model.  
- Paper addresses automating architecture search, acknowledging high computational cost that is likely to decrease with technology.  
- Method is validated across standard vision and text tasks and multiple benchmarks, showing gains beyond traditional RNN/CNN search spaces.  
- More datasets would be desirable, but current results already demonstrate competitiveness and potential superiority to human-designed architectures.  
- Approach is promising for dataset-specific architecture tailoring without manual engineering.  
- Paper is well-written with strong results; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
