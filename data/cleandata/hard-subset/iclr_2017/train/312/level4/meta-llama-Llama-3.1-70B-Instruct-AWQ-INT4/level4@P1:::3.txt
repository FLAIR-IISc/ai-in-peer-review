**Summary of the paper's contributions:**
The authors introduce a method called Neural Architecture Search, which uses a controller RNN to generate neural network architectures. The controller is trained using RL to maximize the expected accuracy of the generated architectures on a validation set. The method is applied to two challenging tasks: image classification on CIFAR-10 and language modeling on Penn Treebank. The results show that the generated architectures are competitive with state-of-the-art models designed by humans.
