This paper presents a search for optimal neural network architectures using an actor-critic framework. The method treats deep neural networks (DNNs) as variable-length sequences and leverages reinforcement learning (RL) to find the target architecture, which acts as an actor. Node selection is framed as an action in the RL context, with the evaluation error of the resulting architecture corresponding to the reward. An auto-regressive two-layer LSTM serves as both the controller and critic. The method is evaluated on two distinct problems and compared to multiple human-created architectures.

This is a very exciting paper, as hand-selecting architectures can be challenging, and it is difficult to determine how far hand-designed networks are from achieving optimal results. The presented method is novel, and the authors do an excellent job of describing it in detail, including all necessary improvements. The tested data effectively demonstrates the capability of the method, and it is intriguing to observe the differences between generated architectures and those created by humans. The paper is written clearly and is highly accessible. The authors provide a thorough coverage of related literature and effectively contrast their approach with existing methods.

One potential area for improvement would be to include data on the time required for training, as well as an analysis of the correlation between the time and resources needed to train a model and its resulting quality. Additionally, it would be interesting to see how human bootstrapped models perform and how they can be incorporated into the proposed framework.

Overall, this is an excellent and interesting paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field.
