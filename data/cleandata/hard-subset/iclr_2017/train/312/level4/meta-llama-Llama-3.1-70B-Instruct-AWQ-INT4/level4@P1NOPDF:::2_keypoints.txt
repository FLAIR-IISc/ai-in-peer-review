- Proposes actor-critic RL for neural architecture search, treating DNNs as variable-length sequences with node selection as actions and evaluation error as reward.
- Implements an auto-regressive two-layer LSTM as both the controller and the critic.
- Evaluates method on two distinct problems, comparing results to multiple human-designed architectures.
- Method is novel and innovative, addressing the difficulty of hand-selecting architectures.
- Paper is clearly written and highly accessible.
- Experiments effectively demonstrate the method’s capability and reveal intriguing differences from human-designed architectures.
- Coverage and comparison with related literature are thorough and well done.
- Suggests including data on training time and correlation between compute/resources and model quality.
- Suggests reporting how human-bootstrapped models perform and evolve over time.
- Overall assessment: an excellent and interesting paper.
