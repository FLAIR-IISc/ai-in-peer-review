- Proposes using RNNs to generate network architectures trained with reinforcement learning — a promising, novel approach.  
- Experimental results on two datasets show the method is effective.  
- Generated architectures resemble human-designed networks, suggesting compatibility with expert designs.  
- Training requires substantial time and significant computational resources.  
- Experiments do not sufficiently demonstrate generality; need tests on additional, similar but distinct datasets, especially for sequential models.  
- Well-written overall but requires more experiments to prove advantages over human-designed models.
