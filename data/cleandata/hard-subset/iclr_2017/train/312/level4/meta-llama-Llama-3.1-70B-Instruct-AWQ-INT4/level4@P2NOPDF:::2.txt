This paper proposes a novel approach to searching for optimal neural network architectures using an actor-critic framework. The method represents deep neural networks (DNNs) as variable-length sequences and employs reinforcement learning (RL) to discover the target architecture, which serves as the actor. In this context, node selection is treated as an action, and the evaluation error of the resulting architecture corresponds to the reward signal. A two-layer, auto-regressive LSTM is utilized as both the controller and critic. The approach is evaluated on two distinct problems, with comparisons drawn to multiple human-designed architectures.

This paper is highly exciting, as manually selecting optimal architectures is a challenging task, and it is difficult to determine how far the results of hand-designed networks are from optimal. The proposed method is innovative, and the authors provide a thorough description of it, including the necessary improvements. The experimental data effectively demonstrates the capabilities of the method, and it is intriguing to observe the differences between the generated architectures and those designed by humans. The paper is well-written, clear, and accessible. The authors also provide a good coverage of related literature and effectively contrast their approach with existing methods.

To further strengthen the paper, it would be interesting to see data on the training time required for the proposed method and an analysis of the correlation between training time/resources and model quality. Additionally, exploring the performance of human-bootstrapped models and their involvement in the process could provide valuable insights. Overall, this is an excellent and engaging paper that makes a significant contribution to the field.
