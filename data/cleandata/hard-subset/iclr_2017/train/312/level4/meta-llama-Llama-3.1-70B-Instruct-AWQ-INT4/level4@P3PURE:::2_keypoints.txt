- Proposes an actor-critic RL framework to search for optimal neural network architectures.
- Treats DNNs as variable-length sequences and frames node selection as RL actions.
- Uses evaluation error of an architecture as the reward signal.
- Employs an auto-regressive two-layer LSTM as both controller (actor) and critic.
- Evaluated on two distinct problems and compared against multiple human-designed architectures.
- Demonstrates novel method with effective search behavior and intriguing differences from human architectures.
- Authors provide detailed method description and necessary implementation improvements.
- Experiments effectively showcase the capability of the approach.
- Paper is clearly written, highly accessible, and well-organized.
- Thorough coverage of related work with clear contrasts to existing methods.
- Suggested improvement: include training time data and analyze correlation between training resources/time and model quality.
- Suggested improvement: investigate incorporating human-bootstrapped models into the framework.
