This paper proposes a novel approach to designing neural network architectures using reinforcement learning (RL) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs). The core idea is to use an RNN as a controller to generate the model descriptions of neural networks and train this RNN with RL to maximize the expected accuracy of the generated architectures on a validation set. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach, dubbed Neural Architecture Search, on two challenging benchmarks: CIFAR-10 for image classification and Penn Treebank for language modeling.

The paper's strengths lie in its innovative use of RL and RNNs to automate the design of neural network architectures. The generated architectures exhibit similarities to human-designed models, indicating that the approach can capture expert knowledge. The experimental results show promising performance on both benchmarks, with the generated models rivaling state-of-the-art architectures.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. Firstly, the training time for the controller RNN is substantial, even with significant computational resources. This may limit the practicality of the approach for larger-scale problems. Secondly, the experiments do not fully explore the generalizability of the generated architectures. It would be beneficial to see the performance of the generated architectures on other similar but distinct datasets, particularly for the sequential models.

Overall, this is a well-written and thought-provoking paper that showcases the potential of using RL and RNNs for neural architecture design. While there are some limitations, the approach is promising, and further experiments could help solidify its advantages over human-designed models.

Decision: Accept

Reasons for decision:

1. The paper proposes a novel and promising approach to automating neural network architecture design using RL and RNNs.
2. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach on two challenging benchmarks.

Supporting arguments for the reasons:

1. The use of RL and RNNs to design neural network architectures is a fresh and innovative idea that has the potential to significantly impact the field.
2. The experimental results show that the generated architectures can rival state-of-the-art models, indicating that the approach can capture expert knowledge and generate effective models.

Additional feedback to improve the paper:

1. Consider exploring techniques to reduce the training time of the controller RNN, such as using more efficient RL algorithms or leveraging transfer learning.
2. Provide additional experiments to demonstrate the generalizability of the generated architectures, including their performance on other similar but distinct datasets.
3. Consider comparing the performance of the generated architectures with other automated architecture design methods, such as Bayesian optimization or evolutionary algorithms.

Questions for the authors:

1. Can you provide more details on the computational resources required to train the controller RNN and the time it takes to converge?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of training time and scalability for larger-scale problems?
3. Have you considered exploring other applications of the Neural Architecture Search approach, such as natural language processing or reinforcement learning?
