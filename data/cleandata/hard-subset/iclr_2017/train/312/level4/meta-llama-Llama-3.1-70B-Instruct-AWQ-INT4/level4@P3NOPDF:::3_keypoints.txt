- Proposes a novel method using RNNs to generate network architectures and reinforcement learning to train the RNNs.  
- Core idea is promising and intriguing.  
- Experiments on two datasets show the method is solid.  
- Generated architectures resemble human-designed models, indicating compatibility with human expertise.  
- Major limitation: very long training time even with substantial computational resources.  
- Insufficient evidence of generality; experiments don’t show performance on other similar but distinct datasets.  
- Need additional experiments, especially for sequential models, to demonstrate wider applicability.  
- Suggest exploring ways to improve training efficiency.  
- Overall: well-written and compelling, but requires more comprehensive evaluation to prove advantages over human-designed models.
