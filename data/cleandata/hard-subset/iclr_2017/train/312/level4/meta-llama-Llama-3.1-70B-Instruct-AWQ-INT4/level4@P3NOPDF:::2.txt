This paper proposes a novel approach to searching for optimal neural network architectures using an actor-critic framework. By treating deep neural networks (DNNs) as variable-length sequences, the method leverages reinforcement learning (RL) to discover the target architecture, which serves as the actor. In this context, node selection is considered an action, and the evaluation error of the resulting architecture corresponds to the reward. A two-layer, auto-regressive Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) network is employed as both the controller and critic.

The method is evaluated on two distinct problems and compared to multiple human-designed architectures. This is an exciting paper, as manually selecting architectures can be challenging, and it is often difficult to determine how far from optimal the hand-designed networks are. The presented method is innovative, and the authors provide a detailed description of the approach, including the necessary improvements. The tested data effectively demonstrates the capabilities of the method, and it is intriguing to observe the differences between the generated architectures and those designed by humans.

The paper is well-written, clear, and accessible. The authors have done an excellent job of covering related literature and highlighting the contrasts with existing work. However, it would be interesting to see additional data on the time required for training and the correlation between the time/resources needed to train and the quality of the model. Furthermore, exploring how human-bootstrapped models perform and incorporating them into the analysis could provide valuable insights.

Overall, this is an excellent and interesting paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of neural network architecture search.
