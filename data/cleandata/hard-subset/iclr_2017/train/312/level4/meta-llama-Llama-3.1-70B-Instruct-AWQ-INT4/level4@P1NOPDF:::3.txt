This paper proposes a novel approach to designing network architectures for specific tasks using reinforcement learning (RL) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs). The underlying idea is quite promising, and the experimental results on two datasets demonstrate the method's efficacy.

The paper has several strengths:
1. The concept of leveraging RNNs to generate network descriptions and RL to train the RNN is interesting and shows promise.
2. The generated architectures exhibit similarities to those designed by humans, indicating that human expertise and generated network architectures are compatible.

However, the paper also has some limitations:
1. The training time for the network is lengthy, even with substantial computing resources.
2. The experiments do not provide sufficient evidence of the generality of the generated architectures. It would be beneficial to see the performance of the generated architectures on other similar yet distinct datasets, particularly the generated sequential models.

Overall, I believe this is a well-written paper with a solid foundation. Nevertheless, additional experiments are necessary to demonstrate its potential advantages over human-designed models and fully explore its capabilities.
