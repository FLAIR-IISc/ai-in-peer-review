This paper proposes a novel approach to searching for optimal neural network architectures using an actor-critic framework. The method represents deep neural networks (DNNs) as variable-length sequences and leverages reinforcement learning (RL) to discover the target architecture, which serves as the actor. In this context, node selection is treated as an action, and the evaluation error of the resulting architecture corresponds to the reward. A two-layer, auto-regressive LSTM is employed as both the controller and critic. The proposed method is evaluated on two distinct problems, with comparisons drawn to multiple human-designed architectures.

This paper is particularly exciting, as manually selecting architectures can be challenging, and it is often difficult to determine how far human-designed networks are from achieving optimal results. The authors have made a significant contribution by presenting a novel method that addresses this issue. They provide a thorough description of the approach, including the necessary improvements and refinements. The experimental results effectively demonstrate the capabilities of the proposed method, and the differences between generated architectures and those designed by humans are intriguing. The paper is well-written, clear, and accessible, with a comprehensive coverage of related literature and a thoughtful comparison to existing work.

To further strengthen the paper, it would be interesting to include data on the training time required for the proposed method, as well as an analysis of the correlation between training time/resources and model quality. Additionally, exploring the performance of human-bootstrapped models and their involvement in the design process could provide valuable insights. Overall, this is an excellent and engaging paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field.
