- Proposes a novel actor-critic RL approach for searching optimal neural architectures by treating DNNs as variable-length sequences.  
- Frames node selection as RL actions and uses evaluation error as the reward signal.  
- Uses a two-layer auto-regressive LSTM as both the controller and the critic.  
- Evaluates the method on two distinct problems and compares results to multiple human-designed architectures.  
- Highlights the importance of automated architecture search given the difficulty of manual selection and unknown distance to optimality.  
- Makes significant contributions through a novel method and detailed implementation with necessary improvements.  
- Test data effectively demonstrates the method’s capabilities and reveals differences between generated and human-designed architectures.  
- The paper is well-written, clear, accessible, and contains comprehensive related-work coverage and contrasts with existing methods.  
- Suggestions for improvement: report training time and analyze correlation between training resources and model quality, and investigate human-bootstrapped models.
