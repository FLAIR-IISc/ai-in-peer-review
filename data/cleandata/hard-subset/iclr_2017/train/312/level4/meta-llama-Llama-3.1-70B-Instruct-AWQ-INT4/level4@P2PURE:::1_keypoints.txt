- After controller RNN training, the best RNN cell is chosen by lowest validation perplexity, followed by a grid search over learning rate, weight initialization, dropout rates, and decay epoch, and evaluated in three capacity-increased configurations.  
- For reproducibility, the paper should report exact hyperparameter values and types of dropout used (e.g., recurrent dropout, embedding dropout); omission forces trial-and-error replication.  
- The same detailed hyperparameter/dropout information is needed for other experiments such as the character language model.  
- The paper investigates automated architecture search; although currently computationally intensive, this trade-off is likely to improve with future hardware/software advances.  
- Experiments span standard vision and text benchmarks, show benefits from searching beyond standard RNN/CNN spaces, and suggest the method can match or outperform human-designed architectures and tailor models to specific datasets.  
- Paper is well-written with strong results and the reviewer recommends acceptance.
