- Proposes using an RNN controller trained with RL to generate neural network architectures.  
- Demonstrates method on CIFAR-10 (image classification) and Penn Treebank (language modeling).  
- Innovative idea that could automate neural network design.  
- Generated architectures achieve state-of-the-art performance on the two benchmarks.  
- Provides detailed analysis showing generated architectures resemble human-designed ones.  
- Major limitation: very high computational cost—training the controller requires many GPUs and several days.  
- Concern about scalability and practicality due to the large resource requirements.  
- Limited evaluation: architectures only tested on the training benchmarks, so generality is unclear.  
- Lacks experiments on other similar datasets to assess robustness and transferability.  
- Suggested improvements: report GPU count and exact training time to clarify computational cost.  
- Suggested improvements: evaluate on additional datasets and compare directly with human-designed architectures.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions, contingent on more experiments/analysis addressing cost, scalability, and generality.
