This paper proposes a novel approach to searching for optimal neural network architectures using a recurrent neural network as a controller. The method treats deep neural networks (DNNs) as variable-length sequences and utilizes reinforcement learning (RL) to discover the target architecture. In the RL context, node selection is considered an action, and the evaluation error of the resulting architecture corresponds to the reward. A two-layer LSTM is employed as both the controller and critic in an auto-regressive manner.

The authors evaluate their method on two distinct problems and compare the results with several human-created architectures. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a detailed description of their approach, including the necessary improvements. The tested data effectively demonstrates the capabilities of the method, and it is intriguing to observe the differences between the generated architectures and those designed by humans. The paper also provides a clear and comprehensive coverage of the related literature.

One potential area for improvement is the inclusion of data regarding the time required for training and the correlation between the time/resources needed to train and the quality of the model. Additionally, it would be interesting to explore how human-bootstrapped models perform and involve in the proposed framework.

Overall, the paper presents an exciting and novel approach to neural architecture search, and the authors do an excellent job of describing their method and its applications.

Decision: Accept
