- Authors select the best RNN cell by lowest validation perplexity after controller RNN training and then perform a grid search over learning rate, weight init, dropout rates, and decay epoch.  
- They run the best-found cell with three different configurations and sizes to increase capacity.  
- Paper should include full hyperparameters and specify dropout types (e.g., recurrent, embedding) to facilitate replication.  
- Without those details, reproducing results requires significant trial and error; releasing a base hyperparameter set (as in Zaremba et al. 2014) would help.  
- Hyperparameter details would also aid replication of other experiments, such as the character language model.  
- The work addresses an important problem: automating architecture search.  
- The technique is currently computationally intensive, but this trade-off is likely to improve over time.  
- The paper evaluates both standard vision and text tasks across many benchmark datasets.  
- Exploring beyond standard RNN/CNN search spaces yields performance gains.  
- More dataset evaluations would be desirable, but current results sufficiently show competitiveness with or superiority to human-designed architectures.  
- The approach enables tailoring architectures to specific datasets without manual engineering.  
- The paper is well-written, presents strong results, and the reviewer recommends acceptance.
