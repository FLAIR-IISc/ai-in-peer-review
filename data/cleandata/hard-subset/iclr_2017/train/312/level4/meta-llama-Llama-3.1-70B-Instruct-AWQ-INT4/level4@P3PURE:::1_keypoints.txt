- Authors select the best RNN cell by validation perplexity, then run a grid search over learning rate, weight initialization, dropout rates, and decay epoch, and scale the best cell in three configurations/sizes.

- Reviewer requests full hyperparameter listings and explicit dropout types (e.g., recurrent, embedding) to enable replication; lack of these details forces trial-and-error—cites Zaremba et al. (2014) as precedent.

- The technique automates architecture search, an important direction, though currently computationally intensive (expected to improve with time).

- Evaluation spans standard vision and text benchmarks, shows gains beyond standard RNN/CNN search spaces, and appears competitive with or superior to human-designed architectures; more datasets would be desirable but current evaluation is sufficient.

- Paper is well written with strong results; reviewer recommends acceptance.
