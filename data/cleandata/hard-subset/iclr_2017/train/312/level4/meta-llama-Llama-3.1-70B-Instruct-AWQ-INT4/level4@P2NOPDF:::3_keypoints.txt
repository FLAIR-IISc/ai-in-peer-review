- Proposes a novel method using RNNs and reinforcement learning to design task-specific network architectures.  
- Core idea is promising and shows efficacy on two datasets.  
- Innovative use of RNNs to generate network descriptions trained via RL.  
- Generated architectures resemble human-designed models, suggesting compatibility with human expertise.  
- Major drawback: very long training time and high computational cost.  
- Experiments do not sufficiently demonstrate the generality of the generated architectures.  
- Recommendation: evaluate generated architectures on additional, related datasets (especially sequential tasks).  
- Overall: well-written and compelling idea but requires more experiments to prove advantages over human-designed models.
