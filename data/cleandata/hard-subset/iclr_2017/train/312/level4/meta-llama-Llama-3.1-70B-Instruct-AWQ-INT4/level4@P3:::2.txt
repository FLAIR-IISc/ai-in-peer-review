This paper presents a novel approach to searching for optimal neural network architectures using a recurrent neural network and reinforcement learning. The method treats deep neural networks (DNNs) as variable-length sequences and uses reinforcement learning (RL) to find the target architecture, which acts as an actor. The node selection is an action in the RL context, and the evaluation error of the outcome architecture corresponds to the reward. An auto-regressive two-layer LSTM is used as a controller and critic.

The paper is well-written, and the authors do an excellent job of describing the method in detail, including the improvements that needed to be done. The method is evaluated on two different problems, CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, and compared with several human-created architectures. The results are impressive, with the method designing architectures that rival or even surpass the performance of human-invented architectures.

One of the strengths of the paper is its clarity and accessibility. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the method, including the architecture search space, the controller and critic, and the training procedure. The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise summary of the results.

The comparison with related literature is also well-done. The authors provide a thorough review of existing methods for neural architecture search, including hyperparameter optimization and neuro-evolution algorithms. They also discuss the limitations of these methods and how their approach addresses these limitations.

Overall, this is an excellent and interesting paper that presents a novel approach to searching for optimal neural network architectures. The method has the potential to significantly improve the performance of DNNs, and the results presented in the paper are impressive.
