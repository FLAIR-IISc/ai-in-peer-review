This paper proposes using reinforcement learning (RL) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs) to design neural network architectures for specific tasks. The core idea is quite promising, and the experimental results on two datasets demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.

The strengths of the paper are:

1. The approach of using an RNN to generate network descriptions and then training this RNN with RL is both interesting and promising.
2. The generated architectures bear a resemblance to those designed by humans, suggesting compatibility between human expertise and the architectures produced by the proposed method.

The weaknesses of the paper are:

1. Training the network is computationally expensive and time-consuming, even with significant computing resources.
2. The experiments do not fully establish the generalizability of the generated architectures. It would be valuable to see the performance of these architectures on other datasets that are similar but distinct, particularly for the sequential models they generate. 

Overall, I believe this is a solid paper. However, further experimentation is needed to convincingly demonstrate its potential advantages over manually designed models.
