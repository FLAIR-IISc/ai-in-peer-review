- Proposal: Use an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate neural network architectures.
- Decision: Accept — significant advance in automating architecture design with competitive results.
- Key novelty: RNN controller provides a flexible way to explore a large architecture search space; policy gradients used to optimize it.
- Strong empirical results: CIFAR-10 test error 3.65% (outperforming human designs) and state-of-the-art on Penn Treebank.
- Transferability: Discovered recurrent cell transfers successfully to character-level language modeling, indicating generalizability.
- Implementation detail: Distributed training with asynchronous updates used to mitigate long training times.
- Limitation: Substantial computational cost and training time even with distributed resources.
- Recommendation: Investigate methods to accelerate search (more efficient exploration, transfer learning).
- Recommendation: Evaluate generalizability on more and diverse datasets/tasks beyond language modeling variants.
- Recommendation: Extend the search space to include transformers and other modern architectures to broaden impact.
- Recommendation: Provide a detailed analysis of generated architectures to extract common patterns and design principles.
- Q1 for authors: Provide detailed computational resource breakdown (e.g., total GPU hours) to reproduce results.
- Q2 for authors: How sensitive are results to the reward function (e.g., different powers of validation accuracy)?
- Q3 for authors: Clarify frequency of “compilation failures” with skip connections and effectiveness of solutions.
- Q4 for authors: What steps ensured RL training stability and reproducibility; are results consistent across runs?
- Q5 for authors: How does the choice of base number (e.g., 8) affect the search space and final performance?
