- Proposes an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate neural architectures.
- Method is well-executed and conceptually promising.
- Strong empirical results on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank, achieving competitive or state-of-the-art performance.
- Decision: Accept — significant contribution to automated machine learning.
- Clear motivation and solid positioning within hyperparameter optimization, neuro-evolution, and program synthesis literature.
- Empirical validation strengthened by ablation studies, control experiments, and transfer learning results.
- Major limitation: high computational cost and long training time.
- Suggestion: include discussion on scalability and strategies to reduce training time.
- Limited investigation of generalization across datasets; transferability needs more evaluation.
- Suggestion: test transfer to other image classification and language modeling tasks.
- Search space choices may strongly affect outcomes; require deeper analysis of search space impact.
- Suggestion: explore alternative ranges of filters and activation choices to see if results improve.
- Visualization of discovered architectures is limited; more visual detail, especially for CIFAR-10 models, would help.
- Question: sensitivity of performance to the baseline smoothing factor in REINFORCE.
- Question: can the method effectively search for other architecture families (e.g., transformers)?
- Question: practical challenges and solutions when scaling the distributed training setup.
- Question: have warm-starting or pre-trained-controller strategies been tried to accelerate the search?
