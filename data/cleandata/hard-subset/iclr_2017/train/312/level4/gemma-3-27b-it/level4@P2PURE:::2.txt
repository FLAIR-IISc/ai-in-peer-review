This paper introduces a method for neural network architecture search using an actor-critic reinforcement learning framework. The approach represents a deep neural network as a variable-length sequence and employs reinforcement learning to discover optimal architectures, where the network architecture itself acts as the ‘actor’. Node selection is defined as the action within the RL context, and the evaluation error of the resulting architecture serves as the reward signal. A two-layer LSTM, implemented in an auto-regressive manner, functions as both the controller and critic. The method is evaluated on two distinct problems, with performance compared to that of several manually designed architectures.

This is a very promising paper! Hand-designing neural network architectures is a challenging and often suboptimal process, and it is difficult to assess how close hand-designed networks are to optimal solutions. The proposed method is novel, and the authors provide a detailed and well-explained description of the technique, including a clear account of necessary improvements. The experimental results effectively demonstrate the capability of the method. The observed differences between the learned architectures and those designed by humans are particularly interesting. The paper is exceptionally well-written and accessible, and it provides a comprehensive and well-contextualized review of related literature.

It would be beneficial to include data on the training time required for the architecture search process, and to investigate the correlation between training time/computational resources and the quality of the discovered models. Furthermore, exploring the performance of human-designed networks initialized with architectures found by the method (bootstrapping) would be a valuable addition. 

Overall, this is an excellent and compelling paper.
