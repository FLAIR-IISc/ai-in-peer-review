- Proposes a novel actor-critic RL framework for neural architecture search, framing DNN design as a variable-length sequential decision process.
- RL agent (actor) iteratively selects nodes to build the network, using validation error of the resulting architecture as the reward.
- Uses a two-layer LSTM as both controller (actor) and critic, providing an autoregressive approach to architecture generation and evaluation.
- Evaluated on two distinct tasks with comparisons to various human-designed networks.
- Experimental results demonstrate the method’s capability and reveal architectures that differ meaningfully from human designs.
- Shows promise for automating architecture design and potentially discovering architectures beyond conventional human intuition.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and situates the contribution effectively within related work.
- Lacks reported data on computational cost/time required to train the RL agent, which is important for practical assessment.
- Recommends investigating architectures initialized from human-designed ‘bootstrap’ models to incorporate human intuition.
- Suggests analyzing the impact of different initialization strategies to compare automated and human-guided design strengths.
- Overall assessment: significant, well-presented contribution worthy of acceptance.
