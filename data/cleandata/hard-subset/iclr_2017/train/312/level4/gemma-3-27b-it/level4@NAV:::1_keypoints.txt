- Presents an automated architecture search methodology that refines a controller RNN after training.
- Selects the best-performing RNN cell by lowest validation perplexity, then runs hyperparameter grid search (learning rate, weight init, dropout rates, decay epoch).
- Evaluates the optimal cell across multiple configurations and sizes to study capacity scaling.
- Reproducibility concern: hyperparameter search details are insufficiently reported.
- Critical missing detail: exact values tested and the specific type(s) of dropout (recurrent, embedding, variational, etc.).
- Recommends providing a baseline hyperparameter set (as in Zaremba et al. 2014) to aid replication and interpretation of other experiments (e.g., character LM).
- Tackles an important problem (automating neural architecture search); current computational cost is high but acceptable given potential future improvements.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on standard vision and text tasks, outperforming many manually designed architectures.
- Broader dataset evaluation would strengthen claims, but current experiments sufficiently show competitiveness and promise.
- Well-written, compelling paper with strong results; recommends acceptance.
