- Introduces Neural Architecture Search: an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to automatically design neural network architectures.
- Frames architecture search as a sequential decision process where the RNN outputs a variable-length string describing a network and validation performance is the reward.
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on CIFAR-10 (image classification) and Penn Treebank (language modeling), achieving competitive or state-of-the-art performance.
- Recommends accepting the paper due to its innovation and practical success in automating architecture design.
- Core contribution: effectively formulates architecture design as an RL problem and implements it with an RNN controller.
- Addresses key challenges such as handling variable-length architectures and reducing reward variance with thoughtful techniques.
- Shows transfer learning results on character-level language modeling, suggesting some generalizability of the discovered architectures.
- Provides detailed experimental setup and clear presentation, aiding reproducibility and further research.
- Suggests future work: give a detailed analysis of computational resources, training time, and hardware requirements for the search.
- Suggests exploring different controller architectures (e.g., transformers) and alternative reward shaping strategies for potential improvement.
- Proposes investigating incorporation of human knowledge or architectural priors to accelerate convergence and improve performance.
- Recommends discussing limitations like risk of local optima and high computational cost when searching large architecture spaces.
