- Proposes a novel RL + RNN method to automatically generate task-specific neural architectures; results on two datasets are promising.
- Strength: Using an RNN to encode architectures and training it with RL is an interesting, high-potential idea.
- Strength: Generated architectures show similarities to human-designed networks, suggesting learned designs are sensible.
- Weakness: Training the method is computationally expensive and time-consuming even with substantial compute.
- Weakness: Experiments do not convincingly establish generalizability; more evaluations on additional, similar-but-distinct datasets (especially for sequential models) are needed.
- Overall: Well-executed work, but further experimentation is required to prove clear advantages over manually designed networks.
