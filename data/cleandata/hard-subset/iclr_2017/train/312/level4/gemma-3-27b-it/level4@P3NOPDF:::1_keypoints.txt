- Addresses an important problem: automated neural architecture search for ML.
- Proposes a novel controller-RNN-based NAS method that generates and evaluates architectures.
- Achieves competitive or superior results to hand-designed models on vision and NLP benchmarks.
- Demonstrates results on CIFAR-10, ImageNet, and several text benchmarks.
- Broad applicability beyond standard RNN/CNN search spaces, tailoring architectures to datasets.
- Method is currently computationally intensive, but likely to benefit from future algorithmic/hardware improvements.
- Paper is clearly written with a logical argument; experimental setup is thorough and results convincing.
- Requests clarification for reproducibility: provide detailed hyperparameter ranges used in grid search.
- Specifically request ranges/types for learning rates, weight initializations (e.g., Xavier/He), dropout rates and dropout types (recurrent, embedding, variational).
- Suggests releasing a base set of hyperparameters (following examples like Zaremba et al. 2014).
- Clarification request applies to other experiments (e.g., character language model).
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance to ICLR 2017.
