- Introduces a neural architecture search method using an actor-critic reinforcement learning framework.  
- Represents deep networks as variable-length sequences and treats architecture construction as the actor’s actions.  
- Defines node selection as the RL action and uses evaluation error of the resulting architecture as the reward signal.  
- Implements a two-layer LSTM, operating auto-regressively, as both the controller and the critic.  
- Evaluates the method on two tasks and compares performance against several manually designed architectures.  
- Novel and well-explained approach with clear description of necessary improvements.  
- Experimental results effectively demonstrate the method’s capability and reveal interesting differences from human-designed architectures.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, accessible, and situates itself well in related literature.  
- Suggests reporting training time and studying correlation between compute resources and model quality.  
- Recommends exploring bootstrapping human-designed networks with architectures found by the method.
