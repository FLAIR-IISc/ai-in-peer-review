- Introduces a novel actor-critic reinforcement learning approach for neural architecture search.
- Formulates deep networks as variable-length sequences, with node selection as actions and evaluation error as the reward.
- Employs an auto-regressive two-layer LSTM that functions as both the controller (actor) and the critic.
- Evaluated on two distinct problems and compared against a range of manually designed architectures.
- Experimental results effectively demonstrate the method’s capabilities and reveal insightful differences from human-designed architectures.
- Methodology is genuinely novel, thoroughly described, and includes necessary refinements.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, accessible, and provides a comprehensive, well-contextualized related-work review.
- Suggests adding training-time and computational-resource vs. performance analysis to strengthen the work.
- Recommends investigating bootstrapped models initialized with human-designed components and exploring human-in-the-loop search.
- Overall assessment: an excellent, compelling, and highly promising contribution.
