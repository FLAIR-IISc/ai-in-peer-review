- Introduces a novel method: use an RNN to generate network architectures and train it with RL.  
- Experiments on two datasets demonstrate the approach is viable with encouraging initial results.  
- RNN-based parameterization of the search space is innovative and offers flexible exploration of architectures.  
- Generated architectures show similarities to human-designed networks, suggesting learned design principles.  
- Major limitation: substantial computational cost and long RL training times hinder practicality.  
- Paper lacks concrete mitigation strategies for computational cost (e.g., transfer learning, efficient RL, constraints).  
- Limited assessment of generality: results on two datasets do not prove transferability to unseen but related tasks.  
- Would benefit from evaluations on additional, similar-but-different datasets to test adaptability.  
- Comparative evaluation should include strong, specifically tuned hand-crafted baselines and clearer baseline optimization details.  
- Promising work overall but impact is currently limited until computational and generalization concerns are addressed.
