- Introduces a novel approach using an RNN to generate neural network architectures trained via Reinforcement Learning.  
- Core concept is compelling and experiments on two datasets show effectiveness.  
- Strength: RNN-based generator with RL training is an innovative and potentially impactful direction.  
- Strength: Generated architectures resemble human-designed ones, indicating alignment with established principles.  
- Limitation: Training the method demands substantial computational resources and time.  
- Limitation: Experiments lack thorough assessment of generalizability; more datasets (especially for sequential models) are needed.  
- Conclusion: Valuable contribution that requires further experiments—particularly on generalizability and comparisons to human-designed models—to fully validate its potential.
