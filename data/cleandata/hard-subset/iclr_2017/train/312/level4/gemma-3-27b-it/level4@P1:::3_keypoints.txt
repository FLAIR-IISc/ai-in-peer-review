- Proposes using an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to automatically design neural network architectures for tasks.
- Experimental results on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank show the method is effective.
- Strength: The RNN-as-generator plus RL controller is an innovative approach with high potential.
- Strength: Generated architectures match or sometimes outperform human-designed networks, implying learned capture of expert design patterns.
- Weakness: Training is computationally expensive and time-consuming even with substantial resources.
- Weakness: Experiments do not sufficiently assess generalization; need evaluation on related but distinct datasets, especially for recurrent models.
- Recommendation: Accept — novelty and strong benchmark results outweigh computational cost concerns.
- Contribution: Automates a laborious, ad-hoc process of architecture design, potentially democratizing model creation.
- Method: RNN controller supports variable-length architecture descriptions; RL offers a principled optimization framework.
- Empirical: Achieves competitive or state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank and reports speedup on CIFAR-10.
- Transfer: Learned recurrent cell successfully transfers to character-level language modeling tasks.
- Suggestion: Discuss strategies to reduce computational cost (e.g., transfer learning, pre-training the controller, efficient exploration).
- Suggestion: Expand experimental evaluation to more datasets and test PTB-derived cells on other language tasks.
- Suggestion: Add detailed ablation studies to quantify contributions of elements like skip connections and the REINFORCE baseline.
- Suggestion: Clarify the design choices, limitations, and assumptions of the search space.
- Question: Why is the validation accuracy cubed for the reward signal, has this been tuned, and how much does it affect performance?
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the number of controller replicas (K) and child replicas (m); are there diminishing returns or stability issues?
- Question: How frequent are "compilation failures" with skip connections, and how effective are the implemented workarounds?
- Question: Were any modifications made to the generated recurrent cell before applying it to the character-level language modeling task?
