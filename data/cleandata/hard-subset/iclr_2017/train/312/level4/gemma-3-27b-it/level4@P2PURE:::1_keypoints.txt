- Describes refining the controller RNN post-training: pick best RNN cell by lowest validation perplexity, run grid search over learning rate, weight init, dropout rates, decay epoch, then scale best cell with three configurations/sizes.  
- Requests inclusion of the exact hyperparameters used in the grid search and the specific dropout types (e.g., recurrent, embedding) to enable reproducibility.  
- Notes that providing a baseline hyperparameter set (as in Zaremba et al. 2014) would benefit future work and reduce required experimentation.  
- Requests that the detailed hyperparameter disclosure extend to other experiments, such as the character language model.  
- Acknowledges the importance of automated neural architecture search and accepts the current high computational cost as reasonable given expected hardware/algorithm advances.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness across vision and text benchmarks, showing gains by exploring architectures beyond conventional RNNs/CNNs and suggesting potential to outperform human designs.  
- Suggests broader dataset evaluation would be beneficial, though current results already provide strong evidence of the approach’s promise.  
- Overall assessment: well-written paper with robust results and a recommendation for acceptance.
