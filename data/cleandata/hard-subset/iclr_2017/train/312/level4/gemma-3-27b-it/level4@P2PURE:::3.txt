This paper proposes a novel approach to neural network architecture design using reinforcement learning (RL) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs). The core idea is promising, and the experimental results on two datasets demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.

The strengths of the paper are:
1. The combination of RNNs to generate network descriptions and RL to train the RNN controller is both interesting and shows considerable potential.
2. The generated architectures bear a resemblance to those designed by human experts, suggesting compatibility between learned and human-engineered network structures.

The weaknesses of the paper are:
1. Training the proposed network requires significant computational resources and a substantial amount of time, even with access to substantial computing power.
2. The experiments do not fully establish the generalizability of the generated architectures. It would be beneficial to evaluate their performance on additional datasets that are similar but distinct, particularly for the sequential models produced. 

Overall, this is a valuable contribution. However, further experimentation is needed to definitively demonstrate its advantages over manually designed models.
