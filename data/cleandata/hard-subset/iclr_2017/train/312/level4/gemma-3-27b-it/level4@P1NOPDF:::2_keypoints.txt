- Method: uses an actor-critic RL framework to search neural network architectures by treating a DNN as a variable-length sequence with the architecture as the actor.  
- Actions and rewards: node selection is treated as the RL action and evaluation error of the resulting architecture is used as the reward.  
- Architecture of controller/critic: employs an autoregressive two-layer LSTM for both controller and critic.  
- Evaluation: tested on two problems and compared to several human-designed architectures.  
- Novelty and impact: approach is novel and addresses the challenge of hand-selecting architectures.  
- Clarity and detail: method is described in excellent detail and is highly accessible.  
- Empirical results: datasets effectively demonstrate the method; differences between generated and human architectures are interesting.  
- Related work: coverage and contrast with prior literature are thorough and well-executed.  
- Requested additional data: report training time and analysis of correlation between compute/resource expenditure and resulting model quality.  
- Suggested extension: investigate performance when models bootstrapped by human expertise are integrated into this framework.  
- Overall verdict: excellent and insightful paper.
