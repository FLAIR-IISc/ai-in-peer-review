- Investigates automated neural architecture search and demonstrates competitive or superior architectures on standard vision and text benchmarks.
- Method trains a controller RNN and selects the best-performing cell by validation perplexity.
- After selection, a grid search is performed over learning rate, weight initialization, dropout rates, and decay epoch to optimize the cell.
- Best cell is further refined by exploring different configurations and sizes to adjust model capacity.
- Computational cost of the search is substantial, though expected to improve with future hardware and optimization advances.
- Reviewer strongly urges full reproducibility: provide detailed hyperparameters used in the optimization process.
- Specifically request ranges explored in the grid search and explicit specification of dropout types (recurrent, embedding, variational, etc.).
- This level of hyperparameter documentation should extend to all experiments, including character language modeling.
- Strength: broad applicability across RNN and CNN domains with empirical gains on diverse datasets.
- Evidence suggests the method can rival or surpass human-designed architectures and enable dataset-specific tailoring without heavy manual feature engineering.
- Paper is well-written with strong empirical results; reviewer recommends acceptance.
