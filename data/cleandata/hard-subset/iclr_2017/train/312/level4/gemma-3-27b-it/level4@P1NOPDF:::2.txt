This paper presents a search for optimal neural network architectures using an actor-critic framework. The method treats a deep neural network (DNN) as a variable-length sequence and employs reinforcement learning (RL) to discover the target architecture, with the architecture itself acting as the actor. Node selection is defined as an action within the RL context, and the evaluation error of the resulting architecture serves as the reward. An autoregressive, two-layer LSTM is used as both the controller and the critic. The method is evaluated on two different problems, with performance compared to that of several human-designed architectures.

This is a very exciting paper! Hand-selecting architectures is a challenging task, and it’s difficult to assess how far from optimal hand-designed networks might be. The presented method is novel, and the authors do an excellent job of describing it in detail, including all necessary improvements. The datasets used effectively demonstrate the capabilities of the method, and it’s particularly interesting to observe the differences between the generated and human-designed architectures. The paper is written very clearly and is highly accessible. The coverage of, and contrast with, related literature is thorough and well-executed. 

It would be valuable to see data regarding the training time required for the method, as well as the correlation between training time/resource expenditure and the quality of the resulting model. Furthermore, it would be interesting to investigate how models initially bootstrapped by human expertise perform when integrated into this framework.

Overall, this is an excellent and insightful paper.
