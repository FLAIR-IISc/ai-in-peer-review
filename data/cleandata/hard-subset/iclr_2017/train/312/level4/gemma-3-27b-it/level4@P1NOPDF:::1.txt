The paper reports that “after the controller RNN is done training, we take the best RNN cell according to the lowest validation perplexity and then run a grid search over learning rate, weight initialization, dropout rates, and decay epoch. The best cell found was then run with three different configurations and sizes to increase its capacity.” 

Could you please include (or provide) the specific hyperparameters and dropout type (e.g., recurrent dropout, embedding dropout) used in this process? Without this information, replication would require substantial trial and error. As demonstrated by work like “Recurrent Neural Network Regularization” (Zaremba et al., 2014), releasing a baseline set of hyperparameters significantly aids future research in the field. 

This level of detail would also be beneficial for the other experiments, such as the character language model.

This paper explores an important area of research: automating architecture search. While the technique is currently computationally expensive, this trade-off is likely to improve as technology advances. 

The paper covers both standard vision and text tasks, and tackles numerous benchmark datasets, demonstrating that gains can be achieved by exploring architectures beyond the standard RNN and CNN search spaces. While applying the technique to a wider range of datasets would always be valuable, the current results are sufficient to demonstrate that it is not only competitive with human architectural intuition, but may even surpass it. This also suggests a promising approach for tailoring architectures to specific datasets without requiring extensive manual engineering.

Overall, this is a well-written paper on a compelling topic with strong results. I recommend acceptance.
