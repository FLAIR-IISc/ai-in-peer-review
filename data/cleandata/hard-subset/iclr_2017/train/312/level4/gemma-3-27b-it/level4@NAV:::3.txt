This paper introduces a novel approach to neural network architecture design, leveraging Reinforcement Learning (RL) and Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs). The core concept is compelling, and the presented experimental results on two datasets demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.

The strengths of this work include:
1. The proposed use of an RNN to generate network descriptions, coupled with RL-based training of that RNN, represents an innovative and potentially impactful direction.
2. The similarity between the generated architectures and those designed by human experts suggests alignment between learned and established architectural principles.

However, the paper also has some limitations:
1. Training the proposed network requires substantial computational resources and a considerable amount of time.
2. The experiments currently lack a thorough assessment of the generated architectures’ generalizability. Evaluating performance on additional, related datasets – particularly for the resulting sequential models – would significantly strengthen the findings.

In conclusion, this is a valuable contribution. Further experimentation, specifically addressing generalizability and comparison to human-designed models, is needed to fully realize and demonstrate the method's potential.
