This paper introduces a novel approach to neural network architecture search using an actor-critic reinforcement learning framework. The method formulates deep neural networks as variable-length sequences and employs RL to discover optimal architectures, where the network itself acts as the actor. Node selection is defined as the action within the RL environment, and the resulting architecture’s evaluation error serves as the reward signal. An auto-regressive, two-layer LSTM functions as both the controller and critic. The proposed method is evaluated on two distinct problems, with performance compared against a range of manually designed architectures.

This is a highly promising contribution! Manual architecture design is a challenging process, and quantifying the suboptimality of hand-crafted networks is difficult. The presented methodology is genuinely novel, and the authors provide a thorough and detailed description, including a clear account of necessary refinements. The experimental results effectively demonstrate the method’s capabilities. The observed differences between the learned architectures and those designed by humans are particularly insightful. The paper is exceptionally well-written and accessible, with a comprehensive and well-contextualized review of related work. 

Adding data regarding the training time required for the architecture search process, and an analysis of the relationship between training time/computational resources and model performance, would further strengthen the work. It would also be valuable to investigate the performance of architectures initialized with human-designed components (“bootstrapped” models) and explore methods for human involvement in the search process.

Overall, this is an excellent and compelling paper.
