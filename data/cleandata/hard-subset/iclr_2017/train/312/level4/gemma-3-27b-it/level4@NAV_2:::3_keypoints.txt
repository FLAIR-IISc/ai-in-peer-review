- Proposes a novel neural architecture search method using an RNN controller trained with reinforcement learning to generate architectures.
- Core idea is promising and experimentally effective on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.
- Reviewer recommends accepting the paper.
- Achieves strong, state-of-the-art results and sometimes surpasses human-designed networks.
- Demonstrates the method can discover architectures that align with established network design principles.
- Empirical validation on both image classification and language modeling shows method versatility.
- Reports specific results: 3.65% test error on CIFAR-10 and 62.4 perplexity on Penn Treebank.
- Transfer learning to character language modeling indicates generalization of the learned recurrent cell.
- Suggests providing a detailed analysis of the substantial computational cost and strategies to reduce training time.
- Recommends evaluating discovered architectures on additional datasets to assess robustness and transferability.
- Calls for ablation studies to isolate contributions of components (e.g., skip connections, activation functions).
- Asks for details on the schedule for increasing the number of layers during training and the rationale.
- Asks how sensitive the method is to the choice of reward function and whether alternatives were tested.
- Asks how the smoothing factor for the exponential moving average baseline was determined.
- Asks what modifications, if any, were made when applying the learned cell to the character language modeling task.
