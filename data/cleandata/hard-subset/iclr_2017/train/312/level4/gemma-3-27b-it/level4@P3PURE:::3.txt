This paper proposes a novel approach to neural network architecture design, leveraging reinforcement learning (RL) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs) to automatically generate network structures tailored for specific tasks. The core idea is promising, and the experimental results on two datasets demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.

The strengths of this paper are:
1. The concept of utilizing an RNN to encode network architectures and training this RNN with RL is both interesting and holds significant potential.
2. The generated architectures exhibit similarities to those designed by human experts, suggesting compatibility between learned and human-engineered network structures.

The weaknesses of this paper are:
1. The training process requires substantial computational resources and a considerable amount of time, even with access to significant computing power.
2. The experiments do not fully establish the generalizability of the generated architectures. It would be beneficial to evaluate the performance of these architectures on additional datasets that are similar but distinct, particularly for the sequential models generated by the proposed approach. 

Overall, this is a well-executed paper. However, further experimentation is needed to conclusively demonstrate the advantages of the proposed method over manually designed networks.
