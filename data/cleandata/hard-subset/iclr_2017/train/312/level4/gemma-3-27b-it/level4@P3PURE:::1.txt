The paper reports that "[a]fter the controller RNN is done training, we take the best RNN cell according to the lowest validation perplexity and then run a grid search over learning rate, weight initialization, dropout rates and decay epoch. The best cell found was then run with three different configurations and sizes to increase its capacity." 
To facilitate replication, could the authors please provide the specific hyperparameters used during this grid search, as well as the type of dropout employed (e.g., recurrent dropout, embedding dropout)? As demonstrated by the value of releasing a base set of hyperparameters in work such as "Recurrent Neural Network Regularization" (Zaremba et al., 2014), doing so would greatly assist future research in this area.
This request extends to the other experiments presented, such as the character language model.
This paper explores an important and growing area of research: automated architecture search. While the technique is currently computationally intensive, this trade-off is likely to improve with advancements in hardware and algorithmic efficiency.
The paper demonstrates the method’s effectiveness on both standard vision and text tasks, achieving gains across multiple benchmark datasets by exploring beyond conventional RNN and CNN architectures. While application to a wider range of datasets would be beneficial, the presented results are already sufficient to demonstrate that the technique is not only competitive with human architectural intuition but has the potential to surpass it. This approach also suggests a path towards tailoring architectures to specific datasets, reducing the need for manual, hand-engineered designs.
Overall, this is a well-written paper on an interesting topic that presents strong results. I recommend acceptance.
