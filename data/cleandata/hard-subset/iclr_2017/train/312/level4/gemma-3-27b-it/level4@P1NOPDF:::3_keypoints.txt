- Using an RNN to generate network descriptions and training it with reinforcement learning is an interesting and promising approach.
- The architectures produced resemble human-designed networks, suggesting compatibility with human expertise.
- Training the proposed method is computationally expensive and time-consuming, even with substantial compute resources.
- Experiments do not convincingly demonstrate generalizability; performance on other similar-but-distinct datasets (especially for sequential models) is needed.
- Overall: solid paper, but requires further experimentation to clearly show advantages over manually designed models.
