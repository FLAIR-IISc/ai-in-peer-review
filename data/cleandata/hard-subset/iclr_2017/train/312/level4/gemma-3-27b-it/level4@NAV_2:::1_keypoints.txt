- Paper selects best RNN cell by validation perplexity, then grid-searches learning rate, weight initialization, dropout rates, and decay epoch, and evaluates the best cell in three configurations/sizes.
- Reviewer requests publication of specific hyperparameters and dropout types (e.g., recurrent, embedding) used in the grid search and final training to aid reproducibility.
- Same request for detailed hyperparameters applies to other experimental setups such as the character language modeling task.
- Providing these details aligns with best practices and prior work (e.g., Zaremba et al., 2014) that release baseline hyperparameters.
- Research addresses the important problem of automating neural architecture search.
- Method is currently computationally demanding, but this trade-off is expected to improve with technological advancements.
- Experiments show promising results across standard vision and text tasks and multiple benchmark datasets.
- Approach reveals potential gains beyond conventional RNN/CNN design spaces and can outperform human-designed architectures, suggesting a path for dataset-specific architecture tailoring.
- Paper is well written, presents strong empirical results, and the reviewer recommends acceptance.
