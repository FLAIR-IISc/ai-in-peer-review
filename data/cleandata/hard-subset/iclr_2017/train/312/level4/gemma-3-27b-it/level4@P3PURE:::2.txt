This paper presents a search for optimal neural network architectures based on an actor-critic reinforcement learning framework. The method treats a deep neural network (DNN) as a variable-length sequence and utilizes reinforcement learning to discover the target architecture, with the architecture acting as the ‘actor’. Node selection is defined as an action within the RL context, and the evaluation error of the resulting architecture corresponds to the reward. An auto-regressive, two-layer LSTM is employed as both the controller and critic. The method is evaluated on two different problems, with comparisons made to a number of human-designed architectures.

This is a very exciting paper! Hand-selecting architectures is a challenging task, and it is difficult to assess how far from optimal hand-designed networks may be. The presented method is novel, and the authors do an excellent job of describing it in detail, including a clear explanation of the necessary improvements. The datasets used effectively demonstrate the capability of the method. The observed differences between the generated architectures and those designed by humans are particularly interesting. The paper is written very clearly and is highly accessible, with comprehensive coverage and contrast to related literature. 

It would be beneficial to see data regarding the training time required for the architecture search process, as well as a correlation between training time/resource consumption and the quality of the resulting model. It would also be interesting to explore the performance of human-bootstrapped models and the potential for incorporating human expertise into the search process.

Overall, this is an excellent and highly interesting paper.
