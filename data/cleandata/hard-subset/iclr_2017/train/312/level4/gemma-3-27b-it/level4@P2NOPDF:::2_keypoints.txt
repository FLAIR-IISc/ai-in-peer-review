- Introduces a novel neural architecture search (NAS) method using an actor-critic reinforcement learning framework.  
- Frames deep neural networks as variable-length sequences and uses RL to discover architectures.  
- Treats the architecture as the “actor,” with node selection as actions and evaluation error as the reward.  
- Uses an autoregressive, two-layer LSTM as both controller and critic.  
- Evaluates the method on two tasks and compares performance to various manually designed architectures.  
- Addresses the important challenge of automating and improving over hand-designed architectures.  
- Method is genuinely novel, clearly presented, and development improvements are well-documented.  
- Experimental results effectively demonstrate the approach’s capabilities.  
- Differences between discovered and human-designed architectures provide insightful findings.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, accessible, and provides a thorough related-work review.  
- Suggests adding data on computational training time for reproducibility and context.  
- Recommends investigating the relationship between compute/time and model quality.  
- Proposes exploring initialization from human-designed architectures and incorporating human feedback as future work.  
- Overall assessment: an excellent and insightful contribution to the field.
