- Proposes a novel approach: use an RNN to generate neural network descriptions and train the RNN with reinforcement learning.
- Core idea is interesting and promising for automating architecture design.
- Experimental results on two datasets show the method is viable with robust performance.
- Framework combines RNN-based architectural description and RL optimization, offering flexibility versus manual design or constrained search.
- Generated architectures resemble human-designed networks, suggesting interpretability and adherence to established design principles.
- Major practical limitation: substantial training time and high computational cost.
- Suggests need for methods to accelerate training (e.g., more efficient RL algorithms or architectural constraints).
- Key scientific limitation: limited evaluation—only two datasets—so generalizability is unclear.
- Recommends broader testing on varied datasets and task variations (e.g., different resolutions or sequence lengths) to assess transferability.
- Overall: worthwhile and innovative contribution but requires more experiments and cost/generalizability improvements to demonstrate clear advantages over manual designs.
