Decision: Accept.

Novel approach: Frames neural architecture design as an RL problem using an RNN controller to generate architectures.

Strong empirical results: Automatically discovered architectures achieve competitive or state-of-the-art performance on CIFAR-10 and Penn Treebank.

Practical benefit: Reported speedup on CIFAR-10 and successful transfer learning of found architectures.

Clear presentation: Methodology, training procedure, and experimental setup are well-explained and reproducible.

Comprehensive exploration: Experiments include skip connections, recurrent cell design, comparisons to baselines, and transfer experiments.

Significance: Automating architecture design that rivals or surpasses human-designed models is a substantial contribution.

Computational cost concern: Need more detailed analysis of total training time and GPU hours for controller and child networks.

Search space sensitivity: Results may depend on the defined search space; discussion and ablation on search space choices are needed.

Controller architecture sensitivity: Explore and justify the choice of a two-layer LSTM controller and test alternative controller configurations.

Hyperparameter tuning details: Provide more information on the grid search ranges and tuning process after architectures are found.

Question — resources: Provide a detailed breakdown of GPU hours and computational resources used for experiments.

Question — search space: Report experiments or analysis on sensitivity to different search space configurations.

Question — controller complexity: Investigate the impact of using more complex controllers (deeper LSTM, transformer) on NAS performance.

Question — exploration strategy: Clarify the RL exploration techniques used to traverse the large architecture space and encourage diversity.
