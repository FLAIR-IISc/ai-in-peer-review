- Proposes actor-critic reinforcement learning for neural architecture search, treating a DNN as a variable-length sequence, with node selection as actions and evaluation error as reward.
- Employs an auto-regressive, two-layer LSTM as both controller and critic.
- Evaluated on two tasks with comparisons to several human-designed architectures.
- Method is novel and well-motivated.
- Paper provides a clear, detailed description and necessary improvements.
- Datasets effectively demonstrate the method; generated architectures differ interestingly from human designs.
- Manuscript is clearly written, accessible, and situates work comprehensively in related literature.
- Suggest reporting training time/resource consumption for the search and correlating it with model quality.
- Suggest exploring human-bootstrapped models and ways to incorporate human expertise into the search.
- Overall assessment: excellent and highly interesting paper.
