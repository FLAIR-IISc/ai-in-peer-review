- Proposes a novel method where RNNs generate network architectures and an RL controller trains the RNN — a promising idea.  
- Experimental results on two datasets demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Strength: Combining RNN-based generation with RL training is interesting and shows considerable potential.  
- Strength: Generated architectures resemble human-designed networks, indicating compatibility with human-engineered structures.  
- Weakness: Training the method requires substantial computational resources and long runtimes.  
- Weakness: Experiments insufficiently demonstrate generalizability; need evaluation on additional, similar-but-distinct datasets, especially for sequential models.  
- Overall: Valuable contribution but requires further experimentation to conclusively show advantages over manually designed models.
