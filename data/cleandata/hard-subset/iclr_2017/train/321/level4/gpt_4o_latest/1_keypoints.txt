- Strong contribution: intriguing hierarchical control similar to Heess et al., with robust experimental results achieving benchmarks prior work could not, but experimental analysis is insufficient.
- Terminology concern: calling the pre-training "intrinsic" motivation is misleading—pre-training incentives are task-specific and resemble Heess et al.’s tasks.
- Mutual Information regularizer: sophisticated and usually effective, but provides no improvement on complex mazes 1–3; need deeper interpretation of this failure.
- Sagent / Srest factorization: unclear—Srest is not explicitly defined, hurting clarity and replicability.
- Policy analysis lacking: authors should examine switching behavior, failure modes, and how switching times affect performance.
