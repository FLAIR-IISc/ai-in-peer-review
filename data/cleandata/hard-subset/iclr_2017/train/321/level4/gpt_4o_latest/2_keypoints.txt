- Introduces hierarchical RL method using stochastic neural networks and an information-theoretic option-identifiability auxiliary reward to encourage diverse sub-policies.
- Strong empirical result: a baseline that directly combines intrinsic and sparse rewards fails, while the proposed hierarchical approach succeeds.
- Authors attribute baseline failure to long-term credit assignment and benefits of hierarchical abstraction.
- Alternative plausible explanation: the baseline lacks the diversity constraint applied during sub-policy training, which may explain its failure.
- Raises whether the diversity constraint insight could improve the baseline or enable new end-to-end hierarchical learning (e.g., hierarchical REPS, option-critic).
- Visualizations in the paper are informative and well done.
- Limitation: concatenation and bilinear integration primarily differentiate sub-policies via first hidden-layer weights, restricting diversification.
- Concern this limited diversification may be fine for locomotion tasks but fail on tasks needing highly diverse sub-policies (e.g., manipulation); recommends testing non-locomotion domains.
- Constraint: manager is trained with fixed, pre-trained sub-policies and predetermined sub-policy time steps, which may be restrictive.
- This setup places heavy reliance on effective intrinsic rewards and pre-training; more discussion or experiments on end-to-end hierarchical learning are needed.
- Intrinsic/unsupervised rewards appear domain-specific or supervised-like under the current framework.
- Clarification needed on why the strong baseline in Section 7.3 fails and whether the "CoM maze reward" baseline accounts for extra experience from pre-trained policies.
- Clarify terminology: what is meant by "multi-policy" versus stochastic neural networks (SNN)?
- Verify Figure 1 interpretations: (1) concatenation means categorical value sets a different first-hidden-layer bias; (2) bilinear integration means categorical value selects a different observation-to-first-hidden-layer weight matrix.
- Overall: promising contribution to hierarchical RL; addressing the above points would strengthen impact.
