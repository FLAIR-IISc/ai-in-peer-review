- Paper proposes a two-stage hierarchical RL: pre-train diverse skills via a Stochastic Neural Network (latent-conditioned low-level policy) with bilinear latent integration and a mutual-information diversity regularizer, then fine-tune a high-level policy to sequence skills.  
- Experiments show interpretable multi-modal skills and faster downstream learning on continuous control benchmarks.  
- Reviewer leans to reject but is open to changing decision after rebuttal.  
- Main concern: claims of generality and scalability are unsupported—experiments use a simple 2-link swimmer and may not extend to complex morphologies or richer behavior spaces.  
- Main concern: claim of "very minimal domain knowledge" is overstated because the proxy reward (speed) is a hand-crafted, task-specific prior.  
- Risk: framework’s success depends on pre-trained skill diversity; if a critical downstream skill isn’t discovered, the method may fail and failure modes aren’t analyzed.  
- Question: why discretize (x,y) space for the mutual information bonus, and what are the trade-offs versus non-discretized approaches (density estimators or learned posteriors p(z|c))?  
- Request: run experiments on more complex agents (e.g., 5-link snake, ant, quadruped) to evaluate scalability and changing skill quality/diversity.  
- Request: provide guidance on designing proxy rewards for non-locomotion tasks (manipulation, static postures) to justify claims of general applicability.
