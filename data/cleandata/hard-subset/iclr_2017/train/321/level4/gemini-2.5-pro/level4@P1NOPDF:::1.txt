This paper presents an interesting approach to hierarchical control, building on related work such as that of Heess et al. A key contribution is the strong experimental results, where the proposed method successfully solves several challenging benchmark tasks that were out of reach for previous methods. However, the analysis accompanying these impressive results could be strengthened.

1.  I share the concern of other reviewers regarding the use of the term 'intrinsic motivation'. Given its established meaning in the reinforcement learning literature, this term may be misleading here. The pre-training phase, which rewards skills like locomotion speed for a robot or grasping for a manipulator arm, seems heavily tailored to the downstream tasks. This approach is conceptually similar to the pre-training tasks used by Heess et al., which serve a similar preparatory function rather than representing a truly task-agnostic, intrinsic drive.

2.  The mutual information regularization is an elegant component of the model and appears to be effective in most cases. However, it notably does not seem to help in the more complex mazes (1, 2, and 3). The authors acknowledge this finding, but the paper would benefit from a more detailed interpretation or analysis of why this regularization fails to provide a benefit in these specific, more challenging scenarios.

3.  For clarity and reproducibility, the factorization of the state space into S_agent and S_rest should be detailed more explicitly. While the paper specifies the components of S_agent, a clear definition of S_rest appears to be missing. Providing this detail is crucial for others to be able to replicate the work.

4.  The paper would be more impactful with a deeper analysis of the learned policies. For instance, an analysis of the high-level controller's switching behavior between sub-policies would be insightful. More generally, a discussion of common failure modes or an ablation study on the effects of the sub-policy switching time on overall performance would provide valuable insights into the model's behavior and limitations.
