- Proposes a two-stage hierarchical RL framework: pre-train diverse low-level skills, then train a high-level manager to compose them for downstream tasks.  
- Uses a single Stochastic Neural Network (SNN) to represent multiple skills, improving sample efficiency versus separate policies.  
- Introduces a mutual information (MI) regularizer to encourage diverse, interpretable skills and prevent mode collapse.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on sparse-reward, long-horizon continuous-control benchmarks (e.g., mazes) where prior methods failed.  
- Freezing pre-trained skills provides an effective exploration strategy that shrinks the effective time horizon and aids credit assignment.  
- Visitation plots (Fig. 4) and bilinear integration show the method yields distinct behaviors (forward, backward, turning) from a generic proxy reward.  
- MI bonus improves pre-training diversity but does not consistently boost downstream maze performance; further analysis/explanation requested.  
- Requests explicit specification of state-space factorization (S_agent vs. S_rest) for reproducibility.  
- Asks for deeper analysis of the manager policy (common skill sequences, learned strategies) and clearer summary of failure modes and the switching-time T in the main text.  
- Suggests clarifying the “minimal domain knowledge” claim: a forward-speed proxy reward encodes a locomotion prior distinct from task-agnostic intrinsic rewards.  
- Decision: Accept — novel skill-discovery method combined with strong empirical validation.
