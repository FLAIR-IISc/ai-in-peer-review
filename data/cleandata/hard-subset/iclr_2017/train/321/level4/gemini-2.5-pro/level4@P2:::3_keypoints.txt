- Proposes a hierarchical RL framework: pre-train diverse low-level skills with a proxy reward, then train a high-level manager to sequence skills for sparse-reward tasks.  
- Uses a single Stochastic Neural Network (SNN) conditioned on a latent variable for sample-efficient multi-skill learning via weight sharing.  
- Introduces a mutual information (MI) regularizer to encourage distinct, diverse, and interpretable skills.  
- Employs bilinear integration to condition the policy on the latent code, shown crucial for learning distinct skills.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains in exploration and sample efficiency on challenging locomotion/navigation benchmarks (Swimmer, Snake mazes).  
- Decision: Accept — framework is well-motivated, technically sound, and experimentally effective.  
- Novelty: Clever synthesis of SNNs, intrinsic motivation, and information-theoretic regularization to generate a useful behavior basis without pre-specifying goals.  
- Main strength: Intuitive two-stage approach ("learn to move, then learn where to go") with convincing visitation and ablation evidence.  
- Main weakness: Claims of broad generality and “minimal domain knowledge” for the proxy reward are overstated.  
- Proxy reward concern: Rewarding CoM speed is a strong, task-specific prior that may not generalize to domains like manipulation.  
- Limited evaluation: Experiments focus on planar locomotion; method can fail on more complex/unstable agents (e.g., Ant) due to abrupt skill switching.  
- MI estimation concern: Discretizing (x,y) for MI raises sensitivity and scaling issues for high-dimensional state spaces.  
- Suggestion on robustness: Need discussion/solutions for skill-switching (e.g., transition policies, pre-training for broader initiation states).  
- Fixed skill horizon: Committing to a fixed T may limit reactivity; consider learned termination conditions/options for flexibility.
