- Two-stage hierarchical RL: pre-train diverse low-level skills with a proxy reward using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) + MI regularizer, then freeze skills and train a high-level manager on downstream tasks.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Strength: strong empirical results on challenging continuous control benchmarks with sparse rewards.  
- Strength: SNNs enable learning many skills within a single network, improving sample efficiency vs training separate policies.  
- Strength: MI regularizer and bilinear integration are shown (via ablations) to produce a rich, diverse, and interpretable skill repertoire; visual visitation plots support this.  
- Main critique: analysis is insufficient in places and contributions could be framed more precisely relative to prior work.  
- Terminology concern: calling the proxy reward "intrinsic motivation" is misleading—it's task-guided (e.g., forward speed) and closer to curriculum/auxiliary-task learning.  
- MI regularizer limitation: MI helps generally but does not always improve performance on complex mazes; a deeper analysis of why is missing.  
- Emergent policy analysis: more detailed examination of hierarchical behavior (e.g., switching dynamics, failure modes) should be moved from appendix to main text.  
- Reproducibility request: explicitly specify state factorization into S_agent and S_rest for each downstream environment (e.g., LIDAR, object positions).  
- Request for insight: explain cases where MI breadth may trade off with task-specific utility and why MI sometimes fails to help.  
- Suggestion: include qualitative visualizations of the manager’s skill-selection sequence during successful trajectories to illustrate high-level strategy.
