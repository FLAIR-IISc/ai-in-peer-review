- Strong empirical contribution: method solves challenging benchmarks previous methods could not, but accompanying analysis is insufficient.  
- The term “intrinsic motivation” is misleading — pre-training is task-tailored and conceptually similar to Heess et al., not truly task-agnostic.  
- Mutual information regularization is effective in most cases but fails to help in complex mazes (1–3); the paper lacks analysis explaining this failure.  
- State-space factorization is under-specified: S_rest is not clearly defined, harming reproducibility.  
- Deeper analysis of learned policies is needed (high-level controller switching behavior, common failure modes, and an ablation on sub-policy switching time).
