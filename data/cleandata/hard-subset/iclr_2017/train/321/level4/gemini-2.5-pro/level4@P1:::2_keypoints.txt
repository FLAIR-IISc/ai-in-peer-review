- Proposal: Hierarchical RL that pre-trains diverse skills in a single SNN with a mutual-information regularizer, then trains a high-level manager on fixed skills — strong gains on sparse-reward continuous control.

- Decision: Accept.

- Novelty: Combining SNNs with an information-theoretic bonus for skill discovery is novel and well-motivated.

- Empirical results: Compelling quantitative improvements and strong baseline comparisons; excellent visualizations supporting claims.

- Mutual-information bonus: Prevents mode collapse and yields diverse, interpretable skills (supported by visitation plots).

- Empirical analysis: Thorough; the failure of the dense-proxy baseline indicates benefits come from hierarchical abstraction and improved exploration, not just reward shaping.

- Limitation 1 — Limited sub-policy diversification: Modulating only the first hidden layer may be too restrictive for tasks requiring fundamentally different skill structures (e.g., complex manipulation); needs testing beyond locomotion.

- Limitation 2 — Fixed hierarchy: Freezing low-level skills and fixing skill execution time places heavy burden on pre-training; advocate end-to-end fine-tuning or learnable termination.

- Limitation 3 — Proxy reward specificity: The "unsupervised" proxy reward may implicitly encode task-specific biases, trading generality for downstream usefulness.

- Clarifying Q1: Could the poor performance of the CoM-proxy baseline be due not only to long-term credit assignment but also to lack of explicit diversity pressure?

- Clarifying Q2: Does the CoM-reward baseline receive comparable experience to the pre-trained policy to ensure fair sample-complexity comparison?

- Clarifying Q3: Request confirmation that concatenation alters the first-layer bias per latent code, while bilinear integration alters the first-layer weight matrix per latent code.
