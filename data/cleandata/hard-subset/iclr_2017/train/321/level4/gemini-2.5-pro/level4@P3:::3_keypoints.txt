- Proposes a two-stage hierarchical RL: pre-train diverse low-level skills with an SNN and proxy reward + MI regularizer, then freeze skills as macro-actions for a high-level manager.
- Decision: weak accept.
- Positive: SNN + mutual information bonus is an elegant, sample-efficient way to discover diverse, interpretable skills.
- Positive: Bilinear integration of state and latent code is a crucial architectural choice for learning separable skills.
- Positive: Empirical results show improved exploration and significantly faster learning on sparse-reward continuous control tasks (e.g., Swimmer maze and gather).
- Main concern: Claim that the proxy reward requires “very minimal domain knowledge” is overstated—velocity-based proxy is a strong, hand-engineered prior.
- Generalization concern: The chosen proxy (max forward speed) biases toward locomotion and may not generalize to domains like manipulation without significant engineering.
- Supporting evidence: Visitation plots and Figures 4–6 demonstrate that the architecture plus MI bonus yields richer skill sets and better downstream task performance.
- Included experiments: Appendix experiments on more complex agents (Snake, Ant) and discussion of Ant failure modes strengthen the paper.
- Suggested improvement: Briefly discuss failure modes (e.g., instability when switching skills) and potential fixes (transition policy or skill-switching during pre-training) in main text.
- Question/clarification: MI bonus computed via 2D grid—need sensitivity analysis to grid resolution and viable alternatives (MLP regressor, k-NN density estimation) for higher-dimensional states.
