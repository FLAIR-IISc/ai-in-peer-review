- Presents a two-stage hierarchical RL: pre-train diverse low-level skills with an SNN and MI bonus, then freeze skills and train a high-level manager on downstream tasks.  
- Empirically strong: method solves challenging continuous-control benchmarks (Locomotion + Maze, Gather) previously unsolved.  
- Recommendation: Accept — method is elegant, well-motivated, and empirically effective.  
- Main novelty: use of SNNs with an information-theoretic bonus for unsupervised skill discovery and bilinear integration.  
- Ablations show SNN + bilinear integration + MI bonus produces diverse, interpretable locomotion skills.  
- Visitation plots show hierarchical approach improves exploration coverage versus unstructured exploration.  
- Weakness: analysis of results is limited, reducing insight into why the method works in different settings.  
- MI bonus inconsistency: MI helps generally but not on complex mazes (Mazes 1–3) without explanation or hypothesis.  
- Lack of analysis of the manager policy: unclear when skills are switched, which skills are used where, and common failure modes.  
- Sensitivity concerns: manager switching time T only partially addressed; more discussion needed on this hyperparameter.  
- Framing issue: calling the proxy pre-training reward “intrinsic motivation” may be misleading — it’s more morphology-specific than general-purpose curiosity.  
- Reproducibility/requested details: clarify the exact composition of task-specific state S_rest for each downstream environment.  
- Robustness concern: maze performance depends heavily on the specific pre-trained SNN (high variance); discuss whether pre-training is robust or only some runs yield useful skill sets.
