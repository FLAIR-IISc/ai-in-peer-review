While I find the problem setting presented in this paper compelling, I have several major concerns and questions:

(1) What are the limitations or failure modes of this work? As the complexity of potential behaviors increases, I am concerned that the proposed approach may struggle to discover a sufficiently diverse set of skills.

(2) In Section 5.3, the authors state, "let X be a random variable denoting the grid in which the agent is currently situated." This suggests that the state space is discretized. Could the authors clarify if this is the case, and if so, explain the reasoning for this design choice? Furthermore, how would the approach be affected if a continuous state space were used instead?

(3) Expanding on the first point regarding scalability, how does the approach perform with more complex agent morphologies, for instance, a 5-link swimmer instead of the 2-link version tested? Such an evaluation seems crucial for assessing the generality of the proposed method.

(4) The authors claim that their method improves upon prior work by using an intrinsic reward that "only requires very minimal domain knowledge," in contrast to Heess et al. (2016) which "requires a set of goals to be specified." I do not find this characterization entirely accurate. The intrinsic rewards proposed in this paper also appear to be quite hand-crafted and tailored to a seemingly limited set of control tasks, which challenges the assertion that they require only minimal domain knowledge.
