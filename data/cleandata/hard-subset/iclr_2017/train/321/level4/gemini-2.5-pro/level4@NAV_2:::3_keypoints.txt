- Proposes a two-phase hierarchical RL framework: pre-train diverse skills via an SNN with a proxy reward and info-theoretic regularizer, then train a high-level policy on frozen skills.  
- Core contribution: SNN with bilinear latent integration + mutual information bonus yields diverse, interpretable skills that improve exploration and speed up learning.  
- Recommendation: Reject.  
- Major concern 1: Claim of “very minimal domain knowledge” is unsubstantiated—proxy reward (e.g., speed magnitude) is task-specific engineering that limits generality.  
- Major concern 2: Experimental evaluation is insufficient and incomplete; important results and failure analyses for complex agents are relegated to the appendix.  
- Supporting detail: The chosen proxy reward suits locomotion tasks but is not universally applicable; suggested grasping reward is also hand-engineered.  
- Supporting detail: Main experiments focus on the simple Swimmer; Snake and Ant results should be in the main paper to support generality claims.  
- Critical limitation: Appendix shows Ant instability from hard switching between frozen skills, a significant practical failure mode that impacts applicability.  
- Positive note: The SNN + MI approach is novel and compelling, and visualizations convincingly demonstrate component effectiveness.  
- Question/suggestion: Sensitivity of MI bonus to discretization granularity and comparison to alternatives (e.g., MLP regressors) needs exploration.  
- Question/suggestion: Consider simple mitigations for switching instability (policy blending, short-duration smoothing, partial state resets) in addition to future work on transition policies.  
- Question/suggestion: Using CoM for MI is another form of domain knowledge; including orientation or other state features may change the usefulness of learned skills.
