I liked the framework presented in this paper, but I have some criticisms/questions:

(1) What is the model for the failure of this work? With the increasing complexity of behavior, I expected that this method would have difficulty discovering different types of skills.

(2) Look at paragraph 5.3 - "Suppose X is a random variable representing the grid where the agent is currently located" - is the space discrete? And if so, why, and if not, what happens?

(3) To elaborate on the first point, does this method work with more complex entities? Suppose there were 5-link swimmers instead of 2? I think it's important to evaluate the generality of this method.

(4) The authors claim that "recently, Heess et al. (2016) independently proposed learning different skills in a pre-training environment that would be useful for downstream tasks, which is similar to our framework. However, their pre-training setup requires specific goals. In contrast, we only use internal rewards to signal the agent at the pre-training stage, which requires very little domain knowledge to construct."

I don't completely agree with this. The rewards proposed in this paper are also quite manual and apparently limited to a small number of control tasks.
