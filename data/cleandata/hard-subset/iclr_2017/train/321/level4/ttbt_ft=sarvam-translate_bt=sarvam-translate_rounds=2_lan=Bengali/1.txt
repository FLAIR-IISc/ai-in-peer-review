Interesting work on hierarchical control, similar to the work of Hayes and others.

The experiments are robust and able to complete benchmarks that previous work could not. The analysis of the experiments is somewhat weak.

(1) Like other reviewers, I have found some inconsistencies in the use of 'internal' motivation (mostly due to its current meaning in RL). Pre-training robots with dynamics (or rewarding grasping for a manipulating arm) is very suitable for their subsequent tasks. Although the pre-training tasks of Hayes and others are not identical, they are similar.

(2) Mutual information regularization is refined and generally works well, but does not seem to help in complex mazes numbered 1, 2, and 3. The authors have mentioned this – is there any explanation or analysis for this result?

(3) The paper should clearly describe the product analysis between Sजेंट and Srest. Duan et al. specified Sजेंट, but for the manuscript, Srest should also be clearly mentioned – did I miss this?

(4) It would be interesting to provide some analysis of the agent's switching behavior. More generally, some more analysis of the policies (failure mode, the effect of switching time on performance) would be welcome.
