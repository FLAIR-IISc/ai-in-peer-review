Interesting work on hierarchical control, similar to the work of Hayes and others.

The experiments are robust and able to complete the benchmarks that previous works could not. The analysis of the experiments is somewhat weak.

(1) Like other reviewers, I find the use of the term 'internal' motivation somewhat inappropriate (mostly due to its current meaning in RL). Pre-training robots with dynamics (or rewarding grasping for a manipulating arm) is very suitable for their subsequent tasks. Although the pre-training works of Hayes and others are not identical, they are similar.

(2) Mutual information regularization is refined and generally works well, but does not seem to help in complex mazes numbered 1, 2, and 3. The authors mention this – is there any explanation or analysis for this result?

(3) The paper should clearly detail the product analysis between Sagent and Srest. Duan et al. specified Sagent, but for the manuscript, Srest should also be clearly mentioned – did I miss this?

(4) It would be interesting to provide some analysis of the agent's switching behavior. More generally, some more analysis of the policies (failure modes, the effect of switching time on performance) would be welcome.
