- Introduces hierarchical RL using stochastic neural networks and an info-theoretic option-identifiability reward to encourage diverse sub-policies.  
- Strong empirical comparison against a strong intrinsic+sparse-reward baseline, which fails to solve tasks despite smoother rewards.  
- Baseline failure may stem from lacking an explicit diversity constraint during sub-policy training; adding such constraints could improve baselines and enable end-to-end hierarchical methods.  
- Visualizations of learned behaviors are well done.  
- Limited sub-policy diversification: concatenation and bilinear integration only alter first-layer weights slightly, which may suffice for locomotion but fail for tasks needing highly diverse sub-policies (e.g., manipulation).  
- Recommend demonstrating efficacy on non-locomotion tasks to validate broader applicability.  
- Rigidity in hierarchy: manager is trained with fixed sub-policies and preset durations, making downstream success heavily dependent on intrinsic rewards and pretraining quality.  
- Advise discussing or evaluating scenarios with suboptimal intrinsic rewards/pre-trained sub-policies and connections to end-to-end hierarchical learning frameworks.  
- Intrinsic rewards appear domain-specific due to fixed sub-policy assumption; clarify this limitation.  
- Clarify why the CoM-maze baseline fails (use of extra pre-trained-policy experience? how “multi-policy” differs from SNN).  
- Clarify architecture details: does concatenation use distinct bias vectors per option, and does bilinear integration use distinct observation-to-first-layer weight matrices?  
- Core technical contribution is well-motivated and rigorously evaluated; addressing above points would strengthen the paper’s impact.
