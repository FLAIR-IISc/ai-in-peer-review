- Insufficient discussion of failure modes: authors must explain where and why the method may fail as behavioral complexity and nuance increase, especially for discovering diverse skills.
- Clarify state representation in Section 5.3: is X discretized, why, and how would the method scale/perform in continuous state spaces?
- Test generality on more complex morphologies: e.g., evaluate on a 5-link swimmer to show the approach is not tied to a 2-link embodiment.
- Overstated claim about intrinsic rewards being non–hand-crafted: current intrinsic rewards are task-specific (locomotion-focused); authors should clarify how their reward design truly reduces domain knowledge versus Heess et al.
- Overall: promising idea but current gaps weaken confidence in robustness and broad applicability; addressing them would strengthen the paper.
