- Proposes hierarchical RL with stochastic neural networks and an info-theoretic regularizer to learn diverse pre-trained skills that improve exploration and sparse-reward downstream tasks.
- Shows a strong baseline that directly uses the same proxy reward in downstream tasks fails, attributed to lack of skill diversity learned without the diversity regularizer.
- Limited diversification: concatenation/bilinear integration mainly alters first-layer weights/bias and may be insufficient for tasks needing substantially different sub-policies; test on non-locomotion domains recommended.
- Hierarchical policy limitation: manager is trained with fixed sub-policies and fixed skill durations, making performance dependent on pre-trained skill quality; end-to-end training alternatives should be discussed or evaluated.
- Intrinsic/unsupervised rewards concern: pre-training reward appears domain-specific, which may contradict claims of minimal domain knowledge—clarification needed.
- Question: Why does the CoM-proxy strong baseline perform poorly—could long horizons and credit assignment explain the failure?
- Question: Does the baseline account for extra experience from pre-trained policies, or was the proxy reward simply applied without pre-training?
- Question: What are the implementation and performance differences between the Multi-policy architecture and the SNN approach?
