

I appreciate the conceptual setting presented in this paper but have several critical concerns:  
(1) What is the failure model for this approach? As behavioral complexity increases, I expect scalability issues with discovering diverse skills.  
(2) Section 5.3 states, "let X be a random variable denoting the grid in which the agent is currently situated." Is the state space discretized? If so, what is the rationale, and how does the method behave without discretization?  
(3) Regarding scalability, does the approach generalize to more complex embodiments, such as a 5-link swimmer instead of the 2-link model? This assessment is critical for establishing the framework's broader applicability.  
(4) The authors claim that "recently, Heess et al. (2016) independently proposed learning skills in a pre-training environment, but their setup requires specified goals, whereas we use intrinsic rewards as the sole pre-training signal requiring minimal domain knowledge." I disagree: the proposed rewards are similarly hand-crafted and appear restricted to a narrow set of control tasks.
