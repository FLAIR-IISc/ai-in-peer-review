

This work presents an interesting contribution to hierarchical control, conceptually similar to Heess et al. The experimental results are strong and successfully complete benchmarks previously unattainable by prior methods. However, the experimental analysis is somewhat lacking.  
(1) Like other reviewers, I find the use of "intrinsic" motivation inappropriate (given its established meaning in RL). Pre-training robots via locomotion tasks with speed-based rewards (or grasping rewards for manipulation arms) is inherently task-specific, aligning closely with subsequent task objectives—similar to the pre-training approaches in Heess et al.  
(2) The Mutual Information regularization is elegant and effective in simpler settings, yet fails to improve performance in the more complex mazes (1, 2, and 3). The authors note this limitation—does the paper provide any interpretation or analysis for this result?  
(3) The factorization between Sagent and Srest requires clearer specification in the paper. While Duan et al. detail Sagent, Srest must be explicitly defined for replicability—was this detail omitted?  
(4) Analysis of the agent's switching behavior would be valuable. More broadly, additional investigation into the policies (e.g., failure modes, temporal dynamics of switching) would strengthen the work.
