

Interesting work on hierarchical control, building upon the approach of Heess et al. The experimental results are strong, successfully completing benchmarks previously unattainable by prior methods. However, the experimental analysis is somewhat limited in depth.

(1) As noted by other reviewers, the term 'intrinsic' motivation is misapplied here (given its established meaning in RL). Pre-training robots for locomotion via speed rewards (or grasping rewards for manipulators) is inherently task-specific, aligning closely with the pre-training objectives in Heess et al., despite minor differences.

(2) The Mutual Information regularization is elegant and generally effective, yet appears ineffective for the more complex mazes (1, 2, and 3). The authors acknowledge this; could they provide interpretation or analysis of this outcome?

(3) The factorization between Sagent and Srest requires explicit clarification in the paper. While Duan et al. specify Sagent, Srest must be clearly defined for replicability—this detail appears omitted.

(4) Analysis of the agent's switching behavior would be valuable. More broadly, additional policy analysis (e.g., failure modes, sensitivity to switching timing) would strengthen the contribution.
