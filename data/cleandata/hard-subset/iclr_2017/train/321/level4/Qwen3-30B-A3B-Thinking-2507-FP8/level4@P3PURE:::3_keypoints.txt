- Unclear failure model and limits: the paper does not explain how the approach scales or fails as behavioral complexity increases and whether it can discover sufficiently diverse skills.
- State discretization ambiguity: Section 5.3 implies a discretized state (grid) but the paper does not state whether the state space is discretized, why, or what happens if discretization is removed.
- Questionable generalizability: it is unclear whether the method scales to more complex embodiments (e.g., a 5-link swimmer) beyond the 2-link swimmer used in experiments.
- Mischaracterization of novelty/minimal domain knowledge: the claim of using only intrinsic rewards with minimal domain knowledge is misleading because the intrinsic rewards appear highly hand-crafted and task-specific, contradicting the comparison to Heess et al. (2016).
