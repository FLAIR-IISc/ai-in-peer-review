- Summary: Proposes hierarchical RL that pre-trains diverse skills with SNNs + mutual information regularizer and reuses them to solve sparse-reward downstream tasks.  
- Decision: Reject — core claims not sufficiently supported and comparisons are misleading.  
- Claim 1: Authors claim SNN+MI consistently learns diverse skills without domain-specific reward engineering.  
- Claim 2: Authors claim learned skills significantly outperform prior approaches on sparse-reward tasks.  
- Limited evidence: Skill diversity is demonstrated mainly on the Swimmer robot and a narrow set of envs, so generalization is unproven.  
- Seed sensitivity: Appendix C (Snake) shows high variability across random seeds, questioning reliability on complex embodiments.  
- Misleading baseline: Downstream comparisons use a baseline augmented with the same proxy reward rather than standard sparse-reward baselines.  
- Missing comparisons: Paper fails to compare against established sparse-reward methods (e.g., VIME, HiREPS).  
- Inconsistent gains: Performance improvements are not consistent across different maze configurations.  
- Domain-knowledge contradiction: Proxy rewards (e.g., speed, grasp success) are task-specific, contradicting the “minimal domain knowledge” claim.  
- Problematic comparison to Heess et al. (2016): Authors understate the domain specificity of their proxy rewards relative to claimed minimal prior knowledge.  
- MI regularizer unclear: Section 7.3 shows MI bonus does not consistently help, yet it is presented as a key contribution without rigorous analysis.  
- Stability issues: Appendix D reports instability with Ant robot, a limitation the paper downplays.  
- Requested experiments: Evaluate on more complex embodiments (e.g., 5-link Snake, Ant) to test generality.  
- Baseline improvements: Compare to established sparse-reward baselines (VIME, HiREPS) rather than modified proxy-reward baselines.  
- Analysis & clarifications: Clarify failure modes, stability limitations, when MI regularization helps, and sensitivity to state discretization for MI estimation.  
- Questions for authors: How to design proxy rewards for very different tasks (e.g., 3D navigation) and what domain knowledge is required?  
- Questions for authors: How sensitive is MI estimation to state-space discretization and grid size?  
- Questions for authors: How does pre-training scale to more challenging tasks (cluttered navigation, complex manipulation)?  
- Questions for authors: What evidence supports transferability of pre-trained skills beyond the demonstrated Maze and Gather tasks?
