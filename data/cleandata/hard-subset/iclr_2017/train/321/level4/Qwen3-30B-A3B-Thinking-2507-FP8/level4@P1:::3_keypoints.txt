- Contribution: Proposes Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with an information-theoretic regularizer to pre-train diverse skills from a single proxy reward and reuse them for sparse-reward downstream tasks, improving exploration and sample efficiency.

- Decision: Reject.

- Main critique: Claim of requiring “very minimal domain knowledge” is overstated because proxy rewards are hand-crafted and task-specific.

- Comparison concern: Heess et al. (2016) uses a more general reward structure that may require less domain-specific design than the paper’s proxy rewards.

- Generalization gap: Experiments focus on simple agents (e.g., 2-link swimmer) with insufficient evidence for scaling to more complex embodiments like 5-link swimmer or Ant.

- Failure modes: Appendix D documents instability and failures on complex robots (Ant) that the main paper does not address or analyze.

- Feedback — claim qualification: Authors should acknowledge that proxy rewards require task understanding and more precisely compare required domain knowledge to prior work.

- Feedback — more experiments: Provide results on more complex robots (5-link swimmer, Ant) to demonstrate generalization.

- Feedback — failure analysis: Move and expand analysis of failure modes with unstable robots from Appendix D into the main paper and discuss potential remedies or limitations.

- Question 1: Clarify exactly what domain knowledge is needed to design proxy rewards and how this compares to Heess et al. (2016).

- Question 2: Explain the effects of discretizing state space for mutual information computation and implications for scalability to high-dimensional state spaces.

- Question 3: Provide empirical results for the 5-link swimmer and Ant to assess generalization claims.

- Question 4: Explain how the method ensures consistent, diverse skill learning across different random seeds and the reliability implications for real-world use.
