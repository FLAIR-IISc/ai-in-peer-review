- Presents a hierarchical RL framework that targets sparse rewards and long horizons.  
- Proposes pre-training a diverse skill span with a single proxy reward requiring minimal domain knowledge, then reusing skills for downstream tasks.  
- Integrates intrinsic motivation with hierarchical RL using Stochastic Neural Networks and an information-theoretic regularizer to learn interpretable, diverse skills.  
- Experimental results show significant improvements in exploration and learning across several challenging environments.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection.  
- Novelty claim relative to Heess et al. (2016) is not sufficiently justified.  
- Claim of “very minimal domain knowledge” is undermined because proxy rewards (e.g., speed, grasp success) are domain-specific and hand-crafted.  
- Experiments are limited to simple embodiments (Swimmer, Ant), so generality to more complex embodiments is unverified.  
- Lack of evaluation on more complex bodies (e.g., 5-link swimmer) weakens the paper’s applicability claims.  
- Authors should give a more nuanced comparison with Heess et al. and clearly state the specific benefits of their information-theoretic regularizer.  
- Authors should extend experiments to more complex robotic embodiments to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Reviewer requests examples of proxy rewards that truly require minimal domain knowledge and clarification on how proxy rewards would generalize across diverse task domains.
