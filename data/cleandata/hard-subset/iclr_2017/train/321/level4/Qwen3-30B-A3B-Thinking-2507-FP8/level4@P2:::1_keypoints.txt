- Proposes a hierarchical RL framework that pretrains diverse skills with a simple proxy reward, then uses them to solve sparse-reward downstream tasks.  
- Combines Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with mutual information (MI) regularization to learn a wide, interpretable skill set efficiently.  
- Demonstrates substantially improved exploration and success on challenging benchmarks (Maze 0–3, Gather) compared to prior methods.  
- Decision: Accept — valuable contribution with strong empirical validation.  
- Practicality: Pretraining requires only simple proxy rewards (e.g., speed), avoiding complex reward engineering.  
- Efficiency: SNN+MI learns diverse skills without training multiple policies, reducing sample complexity and maintaining interpretability.  
- Empirical rigor: Comprehensive experiments show clear gains in sample efficiency and performance across multiple tasks.  
- Literature placement: Builds appropriately on hierarchical RL and intrinsic motivation while contributing a distinct SNN-based approach.  
- Clarify terminology: The paper should more precisely distinguish its proxy pretraining reward from standard intrinsic motivation.  
- MI analysis: MI regularization inconsistently helps (beneficial in Maze 0 but not Mazes 1–3); deeper analysis of when MI aids skill learning is needed.  
- State factorization: Explicitly define Sagent/Srest for each task (how Srest was specified) to ensure replicability.  
- Switching behavior: Provide more analysis of switching time effects and failure modes, especially for unstable agents like Ant.  
- Variability in pretrained SNNs: Performance varies across pretrained SNNs; discuss ways to reduce variability and select high-performing pretraining.  
- Questions for authors: Explain the MI discrepancy across mazes, specify Srest definitions, suggest mitigations for Ant instability during switching, and propose methods to reduce pretraining variability.
