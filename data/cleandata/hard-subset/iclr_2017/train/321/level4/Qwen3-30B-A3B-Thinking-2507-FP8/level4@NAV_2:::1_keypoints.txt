- Proposes a hierarchical RL framework that pretrains diverse skills with Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) and an information-theoretic regularizer, then transfers them to sparse-reward downstream tasks.
- Combines intrinsic motivation and hierarchical methods, requiring minimal domain knowledge for the pre-training proxy reward.
- Demonstrates efficient learning of interpretable skills and significant performance gains across challenging sparse-reward benchmarks.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
- Strength: well-motivated skill learning using SNNs with mutual information regularization.
- Strength: strong empirical results showing improved exploration and learning versus baselines.
- Strength: clear evidence that pre-training can be done with minimal domain knowledge.
- Primary weakness: limited analysis of when and why the mutual information (MI) regularizer fails to help in complex mazes (1, 2, and 3).
- Primary weakness: insufficient formalization of the state-space factorization between Sagent and Srest, hurting replicability and applicability.
- Request for clarification 1: Is the lack of MI benefit in complex mazes due to maze structure or a fundamental limitation of the MI approach?
- Request for clarification 2: Need detailed analysis of skill-switching behavior and how switching time T affects performance and optimal T values across tasks.
