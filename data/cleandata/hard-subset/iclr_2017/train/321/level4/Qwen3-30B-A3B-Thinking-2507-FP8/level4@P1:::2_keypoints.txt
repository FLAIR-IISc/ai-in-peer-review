- Proposes a two-stage hierarchical RL framework: pre-train diverse skills with a single proxy reward, then use a high-level policy to solve downstream tasks.  
- Core innovation: use Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with a mutual-information regularizer to learn a broad, interpretable skill set efficiently.  
- Shows significant improvements in exploration and learning across maze navigation and object-gathering tasks while maintaining sample efficiency.  
- Decision: Accept — strong contribution addressing sparse rewards and long horizons with a principled SNN+MI approach.  
- Identifies gap: existing HRL needs domain-specific engineering and intrinsic motivation methods lack transferability; the paper bridges this via minimal-supervision pretraining.  
- Empirical validation is thorough: outperforms strong baselines and ablations demonstrate the importance of bilinear integration and MI regularization for diversity.  
- Technical foundation is sound: SNNs for multimodal policies plus MI regularizer are theoretically grounded and implementation (TRPO, latent integration) is reproducible.  
- Recommend expanding task scope discussion to non-locomotion domains (e.g., manipulation) to assess generalization.  
- Suggest exploring end-to-end training of low- and high-level policies (e.g., straight-through estimators) instead of freezing low-level policies.  
- Request deeper analysis of skill transfer variance: why some pre-trained SNNs fail (e.g., missing critical skills like backward motion).  
- Question: Is the poor performance of the CoM speed baseline primarily due to lack of skill diversity, and could adding a diversity component improve it?  
- Question: How does SNN sample efficiency scale with the number of skills K — does the efficiency advantage persist beyond K=6?  
- Question: Clarify bilinear vs. concatenation integration—does bilinear truly change all first-layer weights vs. concatenation changing only bias, and how does that affect skill expressiveness?
