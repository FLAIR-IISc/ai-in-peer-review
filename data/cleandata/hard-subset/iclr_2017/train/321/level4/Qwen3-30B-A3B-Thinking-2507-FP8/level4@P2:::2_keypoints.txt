Introduces a two-phase hierarchical RL method: pre-train diverse skills with SNNs and an MI regularizer using a single proxy reward, then use a high-level policy to solve downstream tasks, improving exploration and sample efficiency.

Decision: Accept (7/10).

Strong theoretical motivation: MI-based regularizer yields diverse, reusable skills from a single proxy reward without domain-specific shaping.

Rigorous empirical validation: Comprehensive experiments and ablations on Maze and Gather tasks show clear outperforming of baselines and better exploration/learning speed.

Novelty and impact: Meaningful advance in hierarchical RL that requires minimal domain knowledge and is potentially generalizable to robotic scenarios.

Additional feedback — non-locomotion tasks: Evaluate on at least one manipulation or non-locomotion domain to demonstrate broader applicability of learned skills.

Additional feedback — end-to-end training: Provide discussion or brief analysis of potential benefits from end-to-end fine-tuning (e.g., straight-through estimators) beyond frozen sub-policies.

Additional feedback — clarify baseline comparison: Explain whether the CoM/intrinsic-reward baseline had the same pre-training experience to justify the performance gap.

Q1: Did the CoM baseline receive the same pre-training experience or was it trained from scratch in downstream tasks?

Q2: Clarify "multi-policy" baseline: is it six separate policies for each skill and does it use the same amount of pre-training data as SNNs?

Q3: Confirm interpretation that concatenation modifies bias while bilinear integration modifies first-layer weights, and explain why bilinear leads to better skill diversity.

Q4: Explain why SNNs with MI regularizer help the Gather task but not consistently all mazes — is this due to maze structure or the nature of required skills?
