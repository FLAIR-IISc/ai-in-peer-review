- No analysis of failure modes or empirical evidence that the method scales to richer behavioral complexity; claimed robustness is unverified.  
- The use of a discretized “grid” (Section 5.3) is unjustified—unclear if it’s an environmental property or implementation choice, and the impact on continuous state spaces is not addressed.  
- Evaluation is limited to a 2-link swimmer; generality to more complex morphologies (e.g., 5-link swimmer, quadruped) is untested and an ablation on complex agents is needed.  
- Comparison with Heess et al. (2016) is misleading: the intrinsic rewards used are task-specific and hand-engineered (e.g., tuned coefficients for the ant), so claims of minimal domain knowledge are overstated.  
- Overall: promising idea but lacks depth on scalability, implementation assumptions, and true generality of reward design; these gaps must be addressed to strengthen the contribution.
