- Presents hierarchical control with MI regularization to encourage disentangled state representations; achieves strong empirical results on hard benchmarks.  
- Experimental results are compelling and reach state-of-the-art on complex navigation and manipulation tasks.  
- Major terminology issue: using "intrinsic motivation" to mean task-specific pre-training is misleading and conflicts with established RL usage.  
- MI regularization performs well in simple settings but degrades in complex mazes (1–3), and the paper lacks analysis of why.  
- Missing investigation into possible causes for MI failure: representational capacity limits, disentanglement overwhelmed by environment complexity, or suboptimal MI hyperparameters.  
- Replicability concern: S_rest is not clearly defined or constructed from raw observations, hindering reproducibility and possibly hiding domain-specific assumptions.  
- Lack of analysis of hierarchical controller behavior, specifically switching behavior (frequency, conditions, relation to task difficulty).  
- No study of policy robustness or common failure modes (e.g., specific maze layouts or object placements that break the policy).  
- Missing sensitivity analysis of switching timing/hyperparameter and its effect on performance.  
- Recommended fixes: replace misleading terminology, analyze MI failures in mazes 1–3, explicitly define S_rest, and add switching and failure-mode analyses to strengthen the contribution.
