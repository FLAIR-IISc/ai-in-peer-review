- Novelty: Introduces hierarchical RL with stochastic neural networks and an identifiability-based intrinsic reward to encourage diverse sub-policies.  
- Empirical strength: Method outperforms a strong baseline that combines intrinsic and sparse rewards.  
- Key insight: Baseline likely fails because it lacks an explicit diversity requirement during sub-policy training, not just because of credit assignment issues.  
- Visualizations: Effective visualizations clearly illustrate policy diversity and task progress.  
- Limitation — diversity mechanism: Concatenation/bilinear integration that tweaks first-layer weights/biases may not scale to domains requiring fundamentally different sub-policies (e.g., manipulation).  
- Limitation — training rigidity: Manager is trained with fixed, pre-trained sub-policies and predetermined durations, creating reliance on near-optimal pre-training.  
- Suggested fix: Recommend end-to-end joint training or adaptive sub-policy durations and an ablation comparing fixed vs. adaptive lengths.  
- Limitation — intrinsic rewards domain-specificity: Intrinsic rewards may be tailored to tested locomotion domains and might not generalize without more universal diversity objectives.  
- Clarification requested: Explain why the "CoM maze reward" baseline fails—does it use pre-trained sub-policies or is failure due to reward design?  
- Clarification requested: Define "multi-policy" baseline and how it differs from the SNN single-network approach with diverse sub-policies.  
- Clarification requested: Clarify Figure 1’s "concatenation" vs "bilinear integration" in terms of bias vs weight-matrix modifications for reproducibility.  
- Overall assessment: Contribution is novel, well-motivated, and empirically robust; addressing the above points would broaden impact.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept (7).
