I like the concept presented in this paper, but I have some criticisms/questions:

(1) What is the failure model for this work? As the richness of behaviors becomes more complex, I expected that this approach would have problems with the diversity of skills that can be explored.
(2) Given Section 5.3 – "Suppose X is a random variable representing the grid in which the agent is currently located" – is the location discrete? And if so, why, and if not, what happens?
(3) Expanding on the first point, does this approach work with a more complex avatar? Suppose 5-link swimmers instead of 2? I think it's important to assess the generality of this approach.
(4) The authors claim that "Recently, Heiss et al. (2016) independently proposed learning a series of skills in a pre-training environment that would be useful for downstream tasks, which is similar to our framework. However, their pre-training setup requires specifying a set of goals. Comparatively, we use intrinsic rewards as the only signal to the agent during the pre-training phase, which requires only very little domain knowledge to construct."

I don't completely agree with this. The rewards proposed in this paper are also quite handcrafted and specific to a seemingly limited set of control tasks.
