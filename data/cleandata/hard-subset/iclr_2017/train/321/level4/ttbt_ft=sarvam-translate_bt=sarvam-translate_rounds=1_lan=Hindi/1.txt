Interesting work on hierarchical control, similar to the work of Heis et al.

The experiments are robust and manage to meet the benchmarks that previous work could not. The analysis of the experiments is a bit weak on the positive side.

(1) Like other reviewers, I find the use of the term 'internal' motivation to be somewhat inappropriate (mostly due to its current meaning in RL). Pre-training the robot with locomotion by rewarding motion (or rewarding grasping for a manipulative hand) is very well-suited for tasks that it will later complete. Pre-training tasks from Heis et al., although not identical, are similar.

(2) Mutual information regularization is elegant and generally works well, but does not help in the more complex mazes 1, 2, and 3. The authors note this – is there any explanation or analysis for this result?

(3) The factorization between Sagent and Srest should be clearly elaborated in the paper. Duan et al. specify Sagent, but for replication, Srest should also be clearly specified – did I miss it?

(4) It would be interesting to provide some analysis of the agent's switching behavior. More generally, some more analysis of the policies (failure modes, the effect of switching time on performance) would be welcome.
