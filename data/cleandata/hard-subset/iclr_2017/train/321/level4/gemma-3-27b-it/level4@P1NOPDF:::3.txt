I appreciate the setting presented in this paper, but I have several criticisms and questions:

(1) What are the failure modes of this work? As the richness of behaviors increases, I anticipate this approach will struggle with the diversity of skills that can be discovered.

(2) Regarding Section 5.3 – “let X be a random variable denoting the grid in which the agent is currently situated” – is the state space discretized? If so, why is this done, and what would happen if it weren't?

(3) Expanding on the first point, does the approach scale to more complex embodiments? For example, would it work with a 5-link swimmer instead of a 2-link one? Assessing this is important for understanding the generality of the approach.

(4) The authors claim that “Recently, Heess et al. (2016) have independently proposed to learn a range of skills in a pre-training environment that will be useful for downstream tasks, which is similar to our framework. However, their pre-training setup requires a set of goals to be specified. In comparison, we use intrinsic rewards as the only signal to the agent during the pre-training phase, the construction of which only requires very minimal domain knowledge.” I don’t fully agree with this assessment. The rewards proposed in this paper are also quite hand-crafted and appear specific to a limited set of control tasks.
