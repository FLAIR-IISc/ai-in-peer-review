This work presents a compelling approach to hierarchical control, building on related efforts such as those of Heess et al. The experimental results are robust and demonstrate performance exceeding prior methods on challenging benchmarks. However, the analysis of these results could be strengthened.

(1) I concur with other reviewers regarding the use of “intrinsic” motivation. Given the established meaning of this term within reinforcement learning, describing pre-training via task-relevant rewards (speed for locomotion, grasping for manipulation) as intrinsically motivated feels inaccurate. The pre-training tasks, while distinct, share similarities with those employed by Heess et al.

(2) The application of Mutual Information regularization is a conceptually sound and generally effective technique. However, its impact appears limited in the more complex maze environments (1, 2, and 3). The authors acknowledge this observation; further investigation and discussion of the potential reasons behind this result would be valuable.

(3) The factorization of the agent into Sagent and Srest components requires clearer articulation. While Sagent is well-defined based on Duan et al.’s work, a complete description of Srest is essential for reproducibility. I may have overlooked its specification, but a more explicit presentation would be beneficial. 

(4) Providing analysis of the agent’s policy switching behavior would enhance the paper. Broader investigation into policy characteristics – including failure modes and the influence of switching timing on performance – would be a worthwhile addition.
