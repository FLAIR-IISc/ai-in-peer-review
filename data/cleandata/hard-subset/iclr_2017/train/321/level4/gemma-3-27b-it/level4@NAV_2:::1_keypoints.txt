- Proposes a hierarchical RL framework that uses Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) for skill learning plus proxy-reward guided pre-training.  
- Leverages SNNs to learn diverse skills for faster adaptation on downstream sparse-reward tasks; well-motivated addressing sparse rewards and long horizons.  
- Novel, technically sound approach combining SNNs with an information-theoretic regularizer for effective skill discovery.  
- Strong experimental results showing improvements over prior methods, including on tasks previously considered intractable.  
- Rigorous experimental setup and clear articulation of problem and relation to hierarchical RL and intrinsic motivation literature.  
- Ablation studies demonstrate the impact of bilinear integration and the MI bonus, providing insight into component effectiveness.  
- Recommendation: accept — approach is novel, results convincing, and methodology solid.  
- Suggestion: provide a more nuanced discussion of the proxy reward’s role and how it encodes prior knowledge versus being purely intrinsic.  
- Concern: MI bonus does not consistently improve performance in mazes 1–3; this discrepancy needs further investigation and explanation.  
- Request: clarify state-space factorization by giving a precise definition of S_rest (in addition to S_agent) for each task to improve reproducibility.  
- Request: deeper analysis of policy switching behavior, including failure modes and how varying the switching time T affects performance.  
- Specific author questions: (1) what characteristics make the proxy reward suitable versus a task-agnostic intrinsic reward, (2) why MI bonus underperforms in certain mazes and whether alternative regularizers were tried, (3) precise S_rest definitions for each experiment, (4) observed failure modes with policy switching and effect of varying T.
