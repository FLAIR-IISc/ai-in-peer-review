1) Strong, compelling hierarchical control approach with robust experimental gains over prior methods, but the analysis is insufficient.  
2) Calling the pre-training “intrinsic” motivation is misleading — pre-training uses task-relevant rewards and resembles prior work (e.g., Heess et al.).  
3) Mutual information regularization is sensible but shows limited benefit in complex mazes; authors should investigate and discuss why.  
4) The agent factorization into Sagent and Srest is under-specified — Srest needs a clear, explicit definition for reproducibility.  
5) The paper lacks analysis of policy switching behavior and failure modes; studying switching timing and its effect on performance would improve the work.
