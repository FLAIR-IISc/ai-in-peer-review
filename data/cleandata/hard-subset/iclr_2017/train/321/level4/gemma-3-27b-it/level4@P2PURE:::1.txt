This work presents an interesting approach to hierarchical control, building on ideas similar to those of Heess et al. The experimental results are strong, demonstrating successful completion of benchmarks that have proven challenging for prior methods. However, the analysis of these experiments could be more thorough.

(1) I share the concerns of other reviewers regarding the use of the term ‘intrinsic’ motivation. Given its established meaning within the reinforcement learning community, describing pre-training via rewards for speed (locomotion) or grasping (manipulation) as ‘intrinsic’ seems misaligned, as these rewards are clearly directed towards the eventual target tasks. The pre-training tasks employed here, while not identical, bear similarities to those used by Heess et al.

(2) The Mutual Information regularization is an elegant and generally effective technique, but it does not appear to improve performance in the more complex maze environments (1, 2, and 3). The authors acknowledge this observation; further interpretation or analysis of this result would be valuable.

(3) The factorization between the Sagent and Srest networks requires more detailed explanation. While the paper specifies Sagent, complete replicability necessitates a clear description of Srest as well – perhaps this information was inadvertently omitted?

(4) Providing some analysis of the agent’s switching behavior would be insightful. More broadly, further investigation into the learned policies—including their failure modes and the impact of switching time on overall performance—would have strengthened the paper.
