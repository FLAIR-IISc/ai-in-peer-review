Interesting work on hierarchical control, closely related to the work of Heess et al. 
The experiments are strong and successfully complete benchmarks that previous work could not. However, the analysis of the experimental results feels somewhat limited.

(1) Like other reviewers, I question the use of the term ‘intrinsic’ motivation, primarily given its established meaning within the reinforcement learning literature. Pre-training robots with locomotion skills by rewarding speed (or grasping for a manipulation task) feels highly directed towards the tasks they will subsequently perform. The pre-training tasks used by Heess et al., while not identical, share a similar characteristic. 

(2) The Mutual Information regularization is an elegant approach and generally performs well, but doesn’t appear to offer improvements in the more complex mazes (1, 2, and 3). The authors acknowledge this – is there any interpretation or further analysis of this result?

(3) The factorization between the Sagent and Srest networks should be detailed more clearly in the paper. Duan et al. specify Sagent, but for the sake of replicability, Srest should also be clearly defined – did I miss its specification in the text?

(4) It would be insightful to provide some analysis of the agent’s switching behavior. More broadly, additional analysis of the learned policies—including failure modes and the impact of switching time on performance—would have been valuable.
