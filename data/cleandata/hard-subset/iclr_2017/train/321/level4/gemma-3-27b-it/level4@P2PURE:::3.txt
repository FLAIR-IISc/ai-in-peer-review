I find the presented setting compelling, but I have several criticisms and questions:
(1) What are the limitations of this work? As the complexity and richness of behaviors increase, I anticipate challenges regarding the diversity of skills the approach can discover.
(2) Regarding Section 5.3 – “let X be a random variable denoting the grid in which the agent is currently situated” – is the state space discretized? If so, what is the rationale for this discretization, and what are the implications if it were not employed?
(3) Expanding on the first point, how does the approach scale to more complex embodiments? For example, would it perform effectively with a 5-link swimmer rather than the 2-link example used in the paper? Assessing this is crucial for understanding the generality of the method.
(4) The authors state that “Recently, Heess et al. (2016) have independently proposed to learn a range of skills in a pre-training environment that will be useful for the downstream tasks, which is similar to our framework. However, their pre-training setup requires a set of goals to be specified. In comparison, we use intrinsic rewards as the only signal to the agent during the pre-training phase, the construction of which only requires very minimal domain knowledge.” I disagree with this characterization. The intrinsic rewards proposed in this paper are also quite hand-crafted and appear specific to a limited set of control tasks.
