- Decision: Accept (with minor revisions).

- Contribution: Proposes a hierarchical RL framework using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) and an information-theoretic regularizer to learn diverse, reusable skills in pre-training.

- Motivation: Addresses exploration in sparse-reward environments by combining intrinsic motivation and hierarchical learning with minimal domain knowledge.

- Empirical result: Shows significant performance improvements over strong baselines on challenging tasks.

- Experimental quality: Comprehensive evaluation with established benchmarks, insightful visualizations, and thorough ablation studies (including MI bonus and SNN integration methods).

- Baseline comparison: Strong comparison against a CoM speed intrinsic-reward baseline, highlighting benefits of hierarchical abstraction and skill diversification.

- Reproducibility: Code availability is a notable positive for reproducibility and future work.

- Limitation—sub-policy diversity: Current integration methods (concatenation, bilinear pooling) mostly affect the first layer and may limit diversity needed for more complex domains (e.g., manipulation).

- Limitation—hierarchical flexibility: High-level policy is trained with fixed, pre-trained sub-policies and fixed skill durations; end-to-end training or dynamic skill selection/time horizons could improve adaptability.

- Limitation—proxy reward specificity: Effectiveness of the proxy reward depends on its suitability for the pre-training environment; guidance on adapting these rewards is needed.

- Question 1: Why does the CoM speed baseline perform poorly in Section 7.3, and does that baseline use a hierarchical structure or a single policy?

- Question 2: Clarify the distinction between "Multi-policy" (separate independent policies) and the SNN approach (a unified network representing multiple policies).

- Question 3: Confirm whether bilinear integration is indeed more expressive than concatenation by modeling interactions between observations and latent variables beyond bias/weight shifts.
