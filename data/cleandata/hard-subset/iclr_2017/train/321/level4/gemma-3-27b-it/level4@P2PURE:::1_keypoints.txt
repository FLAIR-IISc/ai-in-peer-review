- Strong, interesting hierarchical-control approach building on Heess et al.; experiments succeed on challenging benchmarks but experimental analysis is insufficient.

- Misuse of “intrinsic motivation”: pre-training rewards for locomotion/grasping are task-directed and not aligned with the established RL meaning, resembling Heess et al.’s tasks.

- Mutual information regularization is elegant but fails to improve performance in complex maze environments (1–3); this result needs further interpretation.

- The Sagent–Srest factorization is under-explained; details of Srest appear missing and are required for full replicability.

- Lack of analysis of switching behavior and learned policies; investigation into failure modes and the impact of switching time on performance is needed.
