- Introduces hierarchical RL with stochastic neural networks (SNNs) and an information-theoretic option-identifiability reward to encourage diverse sub-policies.
- Empirical results show intrinsic identifiability rewards outperform smoothing sparse rewards; simple reward shaping is insufficient for tested tasks.
- Visualizations and presentation are clear and informative.
- Novel application of information theory to promote option diversity.
- Strong empirical validation demonstrating limitations of simpler reward-shaping techniques.
- Concatenation and bilinear integration for combining sub-policies may only alter first-layer weights, potentially limiting sub-policy diversification.
- Concern about scalability to non-locomotion tasks (e.g., manipulation) where optimal sub-policies differ greatly; recommends testing on more diverse domains.
- Manager is trained with fixed sub-policies and fixed time horizons, which pressures intrinsic rewards and assumes high-quality pre-trained sub-policies.
- Suggest exploring end-to-end hierarchical learning and connections to hierarchical REPS or option-critic to jointly optimize manager and sub-policies.
- Reliance on intrinsic rewards may be domain-specific and resemble supervised learning; this limitation should be acknowledged.
- Question why the CoM maze reward baseline performs poorly and whether it accounts for extra experience from pre-trained policies.
- Request clarification on distinction between “multi-policy” baseline and the proposed SNN approach.
- Clarify Figure 1: whether concatenation modifies the first-layer bias per sub-policy and bilinear integration yields different first-layer weight matrices per sub-policy.
