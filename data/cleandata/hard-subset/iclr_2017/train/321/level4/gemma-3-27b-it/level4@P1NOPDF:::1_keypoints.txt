1) Strong experimental results that complete benchmarks previous work could not, but the analysis of those results is limited.

2) The term “intrinsic motivation” is misused—pre-training with task-directed rewards (e.g., speed, grasping) conflicts with standard RL meaning.

3) Mutual Information regularization is elegant and effective generally, but shows no improvements on the more complex mazes (1–3); more interpretation/analysis is needed.

4) The factorization between S_agent and S_rest is underspecified for replicability—S_rest should be clearly defined as Duan et al. specify S_agent.

5) The paper lacks analysis of switching behavior and broader policy analysis (failure modes, effect of switching time on performance).
