- Positive: The paper presents an interesting framework for unsupervised skill discovery.

- Failure modes: Concern that as behavior complexity increases, the method may struggle to discover a truly diverse and useful set of skills; how does it perform in more demanding environments?

- Discretization question: Section 5.3 treats X as a grid location—clarify whether the state space is discretized, justify that choice, and discuss performance without discretization.

- Impact of discretization: Need analysis of how discretizing the state affects the learned skills and overall performance.

- Scalability/generalization: Question whether the approach scales to more complex embodiments (e.g., a 5-link swimmer) and real-world robotic systems beyond the 2-link example.

- Domain-knowledge claim disputed: The claim that intrinsic rewards require “very minimal domain knowledge” is overstated—rewards appear task-specific and hand-crafted.

- Request for comparison/discussion: Ask for a more nuanced discussion of reward-design choices and a comparison of the domain knowledge required by this method versus goal-conditioned approaches (e.g., Heess et al.).
