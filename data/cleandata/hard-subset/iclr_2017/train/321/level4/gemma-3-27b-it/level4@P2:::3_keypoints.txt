- Proposes a hierarchical RL framework using stochastic neural networks (SNNs) and an information-theoretic regularizer to pretrain diverse skills and accelerate sparse-reward downstream tasks.
- Recommendation: Reject despite promising idea and encouraging results.
- Strength: Well-motivated use of SNNs for skill discovery and reasonable combination with a mutual information regularizer to encourage diversity.
- Strength: Experiments on locomotion and maze tasks show effective skill learning and improved performance; paper is well-written and organized.
- Major concern: Paper lacks thorough analysis of failure modes and robustness, especially instability with complex robots (e.g., Ant).
- Major concern: The discretization of state space for the MI calculation is insufficiently justified; unclear impact of grid resolution and scalability to high-dimensional states.
- Major concern: Claim of “minimal domain knowledge” is overstated because proxy reward choices (e.g., speed magnitude) embed significant domain knowledge.
- Major concern: Experimental evaluation is limited; more diverse embodiments and tasks (beyond Snake in the appendix) are needed to strengthen claims.
- Suggestion: Conduct ablation studies to quantify contributions of SNNs, MI regularizer, and hierarchical structure.
- Suggestion: Analyze hyperparameter sensitivity, notably the MI coefficient (αH) and number of latent variables (K).
- Suggestion: Provide more visualizations/animations of learned skills and the SNN latent space.
- Suggestion: Compare to more recent and advanced skill-discovery baselines beyond VIME and Heess et al. (2016).
- Suggestion: Discuss limitations like computational cost of training SNNs and propose mitigation strategies.
- Question: Provide detailed analysis of failure modes and mitigation strategies for instability with complex robots.
- Question: Justify the rationale for discretizing state space, report grid-resolution effects, and explore alternatives without discretization.
- Question: Clarify whether proxy rewards are domain-agnostic or require prior knowledge, and report impact of different proxy rewards.
- Question: How does SNN training cost scale with state/action dimensionality and what scalability techniques exist?
- Question: What experiments link properties of pre-trained SNNs to downstream learning curves and which pre-training traits predict better transfer?
