- Novel hierarchical RL framework that disentangles agent-centric and environment-centric dynamics with mutual information regularization; strong experimental navigation results but analysis needs strengthening.

- Terminology concern: calling pre-training “intrinsic motivation” is misleading since objectives (maximizing speed, grasp success) are task-relevant/extrinsic and similar to prior work.

- Mutual information regularization is elegant but inconsistent—provides no benefit in complex mazes (1–3); paper needs deeper analysis of why (e.g., state complexity, trajectory density, representation capacity).

- S_agent (agent-controllable subspace) is well-defined, but S_rest (environment-determined dynamics) is under-specified and requires a precise description for reproducibility.

- Missing analysis of hierarchical decision-making: frequency and triggers for switching between high- and low-level policies, impact of switch timing on performance, and common failure modes.

- Overall assessment: strong, well-written paper addressing long-horizon scaling; addressing the terminology, MI analysis, S_rest specification, and switching behavior analysis would improve clarity, impact, and reproducibility.
