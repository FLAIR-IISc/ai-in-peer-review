- Proposes hierarchical RL using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) plus mutual information (MI) regularizer to learn diverse skills.  
- Experiments show strong performance on challenging benchmarks, outperforming prior methods.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Framework combines intrinsic-motivation-style pretraining with hierarchical RL to learn reusable skills for downstream tasks.  
- SNNs provide efficient multi-modal policy representation and enable weight sharing across skills.  
- MI regularization is used to encourage skill diversity.  
- Empirical results are compelling but experimental analysis could be strengthened.  
- “Intrinsic motivation” label may be misleading; better framed as pre-training for task-relevant behaviors.  
- MI regularization did not consistently improve performance in mazes 1–3 and requires further investigation.  
- Clarify the factorization between S_agent and S_rest by precisely defining S_rest for each environment to improve reproducibility.  
- Request more analysis of policy behavior: skill switching frequency, failure modes of switching, and impact of switching time T.  
- Suggest analyzing and visualizing learned skills to show what behaviors correspond to each latent code.  
- Questions for authors: why MI fails in some mazes; exact contents of S_rest per environment; observed patterns/failures in skill switching; examples/visualizations of latent-code behaviors.
