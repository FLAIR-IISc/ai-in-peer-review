- Presents a strong hierarchical RL framework using Stochastic Neural Networks and an information-theoretic regularizer to learn reusable skills.
- Core idea: pre-train diverse skills with a minimal proxy reward, then reuse them to accelerate learning on downstream tasks with sparse rewards and long horizons.
- Combination of SNNs and mutual information regularization is novel and effective for encouraging skill diversity.
- Experimental results show improved performance over existing methods across multiple tasks.
- Paper is well-structured, technically sound, and accompanied by code and videos that aid reproducibility.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
- Clarify potential failure modes, especially for more complex embodiments (e.g., Ant) and when learned skills are insufficient or switching causes instability.
- Explain and justify the discretization of state space for mutual information calculation, including its necessity and impact on performance.
- Discuss alternative MI estimation approaches that avoid discretization.
- Revisit the claim that the proxy reward requires minimal domain knowledge; acknowledge the trade-offs and design sensitivity (e.g., swimmer speed reward).
- Investigate sensitivity to the fixed switch time T and consider learned or adaptive switching policies as potentially superior.
- Specific questions to address: strategies to mitigate instability when switching skills in complex robots.
- Specific questions to address: how discretization resolution in Section 5.3 affects MI regularization and whether different resolutions were tested.
- Specific questions to address: deeper analysis of proxy reward design choices and their effects on the learned skill set.
- Specific questions to address: how performance varies with different T values and whether there is a systematic way to choose or learn an optimal T.
