- Paper proposes pretraining diverse skills with Stochastic Neural Networks + information-theoretic regularizer and reusing them hierarchically for sparse-reward tasks; well-motivated.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form due to insufficient experimental validation and overstated novelty/generalizability.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited (mainly Mazes and Gather with Swimmer) and does not convincingly show robustness or scalability.  
- Performance variance across different pre-trained SNNs is high and not explained or mitigated.  
- Snake robot experiments are a useful step but broader evaluation across robots/environments is needed.  
- Claim of “minimal domain knowledge” for the proxy reward is overstated; choices like speed/grasping reflect design decisions.  
- Comparison to Heess et al. (2016) downplays similar design considerations in proxy rewards.  
- Paper lacks a systematic analysis of failure modes; Ant instability is mentioned but not investigated in depth.  
- Clarify discretization for mutual information: why it’s needed, impact of grid resolution, and whether alternatives were considered.  
- Expand analysis of switch time T with more results and consider adaptive switching strategies.  
- Provide stronger justification for hyperparameter choices rather than only listing them.  
- Explore techniques to reduce variance across pre-trained SNNs or provide guidance for selecting good SNNs.  
- Add a dedicated limitations section to acknowledge current weaknesses.  
- Questions for authors: what SNN architecture/training modifications would address Ant instability?  
- Questions: how sensitive is performance to the proxy reward and would a more diverse set of proxy rewards yield broader skills?  
- Questions: what is the computational cost of training SNNs versus training multiple independent policies?  
- Questions: have non-parametric methods (e.g., KDE) been considered for MI estimation instead of grid-based discretization?
