- Decision: Reject — interesting idea but experimental results and claims are not convincing enough for acceptance.  
- Contribution: Proposes pre-training diverse skills with Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) and an information-theoretic regularizer to speed sparse-reward RL.  
- Strength: Well-motivated framing and placement within hierarchical RL and intrinsic motivation literature.  
- Main experimental concern: Improvements over simple intrinsic baselines are often marginal, especially in Maze tasks.  
- High variance: Performance varies significantly across different pre-trained SNNs, indicating instability and lack of robustness.  
- Task-specific gains: Larger gains on the Gather task are shown but may not be consistently reproducible due to variability.  
- Lack of analysis: Paper lacks detailed investigation into why the method succeeds or fails across scenarios.  
- Instability on complex agents: Ant robot experiments show significant instability and failure modes not fully explained.  
- Questionable “minimal domain knowledge” claim: Choosing an appropriate proxy reward (e.g., speed) still requires domain insight and may not generalize to other tasks.  
- Clarify discretization: Need explanation of why state-space discretization is used for MI, and how grid resolution affects performance.  
- Robustness/generalization needed: Test sensitivity to initialization, hyperparameters, more tasks, and different robot morphologies.  
- Baselines: Compare to more recent and sophisticated intrinsic motivation methods beyond VIME.  
- Ablation: Run ablation studies to assess sensitivity to the choice of proxy reward.  
- Visualizations: Provide videos or dynamic visualizations of learned skills, not just visitation plots.  
- Key author questions: How to mitigate Ant instability (safety layers, control changes)? How sensitive is MI to grid resolution? How adapt to non-locomotion tasks? What causes variance across pre-trained SNNs and how to reduce it?
