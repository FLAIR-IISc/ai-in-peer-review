Interesting work on hierarchical control, reminiscent of the approach by Heess et al. The experiments are strong and demonstrate successful completion of benchmarks that previous work struggled with. However, the analysis of the experimental results feels somewhat limited.

(1) As noted by other reviewers, the use of the term ‘intrinsic’ motivation feels inappropriate, particularly given its established meaning within the reinforcement learning literature. Pre-training robots with locomotion skills by rewarding speed (or grasping for a manipulation task) is demonstrably geared towards the downstream tasks the agents are ultimately intended to perform. The pre-training tasks employed here, while not identical, share similarities with those used by Heess et al.

(2) The Mutual Information regularization is an elegant technique and generally performs well, but appears to offer limited benefit in the more complex maze environments 1, 2, and 3. The authors acknowledge this; however, further interpretation or analysis of this result would be valuable.

(3) The factorization between the S<sub>agent</sub> and S<sub>rest</sub> networks should be clearly detailed in the paper. While the paper specifies S<sub>agent</sub>, ensuring replicability requires a clear specification of S<sub>rest</sub> as well – did I miss this information?

(4) It would be insightful to provide some analysis of the agent’s switching behavior. More generally, further analysis of the learned policies – including failure modes and the impact of switching time on overall performance – would have strengthened the paper.
