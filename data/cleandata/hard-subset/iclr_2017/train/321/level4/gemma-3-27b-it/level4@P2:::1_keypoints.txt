- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — novel, effective approach addressing sample efficiency in sparse-reward RL with strong experimental validation.
- Core contribution: Combines Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with a mutual-information regularizer to pre-train reusable skills and learn a hierarchical policy.
- Strength: Demonstrates compelling performance on locomotion, maze, and gathering benchmarks, outperforming prior methods; code released for reproducibility.
- Weakness — analysis: Experimental analysis is insufficiently deep, especially interpreting why MI regularization offers limited benefit in complex mazes.
- Weakness — mechanism: Needs clearer explanation of how SNNs learn diverse skills beyond the MI regularizer’s contribution.
- Weakness — sensitivity/failure modes: Lacks discussion of potential failure modes and sensitivity to the switching time parameter T.
- Terminology: “Intrinsic motivation” is misleading; recommend framing the proxy reward as pre-training to learn useful primitives.
- MI regularization: Authors should elaborate why MI does not consistently improve performance in complex mazes and consider alternative regularizers.
- State-space factorization: Require precise definitions of Sagent and Srest (task-specific state components) for each maze and gather environment for replicability.
- Skill switching analysis: Request visualizations/statistics of switching behavior (frequency, consistency across tasks) and analysis of how switching impacts performance.
- Ablations: Recommend formal ablation studies to isolate the effects of the MI bonus and the bilinear integration design.
- Questions for authors: Explain why MI is less effective in complex mazes; specify Srest per task; provide switching behavior visualizations/statistics; report any alternative regularizers tested; describe primary failure modes and mitigation steps.
