1. Term “intrinsic” motivation is misused — pre-training rewards are task-aligned and similar to prior work (Heess et al.), not true intrinsic motivation.  
2. Mutual Information regularization is elegant and helpful overall but shows limited benefit in complex maze environments (1–3) and needs deeper interpretation.  
3. The paper lacks a clear specification of the S_rest network and the exact factorization between S_agent and S_rest, which is needed for replicability.  
4. The authors should analyze switching behavior and learned policies more thoroughly, including failure modes and how switching time affects performance.
