- Paper proposes a hierarchical RL framework using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) and mutual information (MI) regularization to learn reusable skills for sparse, long-horizon tasks.
- Decision: Accept — significant contribution with novel, effective approach and strong empirical results.
- Core idea: Combining SNNs with MI maximization is well-motivated and innovative for skill discovery.
- SNNs naturally represent multi-modal policies, enabling diverse skill learning.
- MI regularizer encourages exploration and prevents the SNN from collapsing to a single mode.
- Experimental results are strong on challenging benchmarks and outperform prior methods.
- The approach effectively addresses sparse reward environments.
- Code availability is a strength for reproducibility.
- Framing pre-training reward as “intrinsic motivation” is misleading; it resembles targeted proxy pre-training and should be rephrased.
- MI regularization shows inconsistent benefits in complex mazes (1–3); the paper lacks analysis or hypotheses explaining this.
- The framework assumes state factorization into S_agent and S_rest; S_rest needs precise, environment-specific definitions for clarity and replicability.
- The paper should expand analysis of learned policies: switching behavior frequency, causes, effects, and the impact of switching time T (including potential dynamic adaptation).
- Recommend an ablation study to quantify the contributions of SNN, MI regularization, and hierarchical structure.
- Questions for authors: design principles for the proxy pre-training reward; reasons MI helps less in complex mazes; concrete definitions of S_rest per environment; observations on undesirable behaviors from frequent skill switching and effects of switching time T.
