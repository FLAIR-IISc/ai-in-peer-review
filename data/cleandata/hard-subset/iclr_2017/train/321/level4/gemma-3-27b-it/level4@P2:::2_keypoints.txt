- Introduces a hierarchical RL framework using stochastic neural networks (SNNs) and a mutual information regularizer to learn diverse skills for exploration in sparse-reward tasks.
- Core idea: pre-train diverse skills and compose them with a high-level policy, addressing a major challenge in RL.
- Key contribution: use of mutual information to encourage skill diversity, yielding interpretable skills and improved downstream performance.
- Visualizations effectively illustrate and help interpret learned skills.
- Decision: Accept (with minor revisions).
- Main reason for acceptance: novel and effective combination of SNNs with an information-theoretic regularizer that improves hierarchical RL and sparse-reward performance.
- Comparison to a strong baseline shows benefits of hierarchical abstraction over directly combining intrinsic and sparse rewards.
- Novelty: SNNs + mutual information moves beyond hand-engineered hierarchies and reward-only skill discovery.
- Effectiveness: experiments show significant gains on sparse, long-horizon tasks; ablations indicate importance of both SNN architecture and regularizer.
- Interpretability: latent-code-linked visualizations provide often-missing interpretability in deep RL.
- Sound methodology: rigorous experimental setup, relevant baselines, and clear result reporting.
- Limitation: concatenation and bilinear integration may limit sub-policy differentiation in more complex domains (e.g., manipulation).
- Limitation: fixing sub-policies and skill time steps reduces hierarchical flexibility; end-to-end joint training could be beneficial.
- Limitation: reliance on proxy rewards during pre-training introduces domain specificity; learning more general reward functions could help.
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis of why the “CoM maze reward” baseline performs poorly and whether it used pre-trained experience.
- Suggestion: briefly define “Multi-policy” and clarify how it differs from the SNN approach.
- Question: confirm whether bilinear integration effectively produces a latent-dependent weight matrix from observations to the first hidden layer.
- Question: clarify if the CoM maze baseline incorporated pre-training or was trained independently and elaborate on reasons for its poor performance.
- Question: discuss how the method could be extended to dynamically adapt the skill set during learning instead of relying on a fixed pre-trained set.
