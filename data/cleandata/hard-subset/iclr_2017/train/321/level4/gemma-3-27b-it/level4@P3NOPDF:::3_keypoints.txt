- Presents a compelling unsupervised skill-discovery approach using intrinsic motivation with promising experimental results.  
- Main concern: scalability and robustness—how does diversity maintenance fail as behavior complexity, action space size, and behavior richness increase?  
- Request for explicit discussion of expected failure modes and performance degradation with larger or higher-dimensional action/behavior spaces.  
- Section 5.3 implies state-space discretization (“let X be the grid”); authors should explicitly state and justify any discretization or clarify if the space is continuous.  
- Discuss implications of discrete vs. continuous state representations and how the method would adapt to continuous state spaces.  
- Generality unclear: experiments limited to simple environments (2-link swimmer, gridworlds); recommend testing on higher-dimensional agents (e.g., 5-link swimmer) to probe scalability and generalization.  
- Challenge the claim that the intrinsic rewards are “minimal”: novelty detection and reward design require tuning and embed assumptions—provide a more nuanced comparison to goal-specification methods and examples of transferability.  
- Overall contribution is valuable and methodology sound; addressing scalability, state-representation clarity, broader experiments, and reward-design assumptions would strengthen the paper.
