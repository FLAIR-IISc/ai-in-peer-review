This paper presents an interesting setting for investigation. However, I have several criticisms and questions regarding the approach:

(1) It would be helpful to understand the limitations of this work. As the complexity and behavioral richness increase, I anticipate challenges in discovering a diverse skill set. What failure modes have been observed or are anticipated?

(2) In Section 5.3, the definition of X as a random variable representing the agent’s grid location raises a question about the environment’s representation. Is the space discretized? If so, what is the rationale for this discretization, and how would the approach perform in a continuous space?

(3) Considering the generality of the method, I am curious about its scalability to more complex morphologies. Would the approach be effective with a more complicated embodiment, such as a 5-link swimmer, compared to the 2-link example used? Assessing performance with increased embodiment complexity is crucial.

(4) The authors contrast their work with Heess et al. (2016), highlighting the use of intrinsic rewards versus specified goals. While I acknowledge the difference, I question the assertion that the proposed rewards are minimal in terms of domain knowledge. The rewards appear to be carefully designed and specific to a relatively narrow range of control tasks, suggesting a significant degree of hand-crafting.
