This paper presents interesting work on hierarchical control, building upon related research such as that of Heess et al. The experimental results are strong, demonstrating successful completion of benchmarks that have proven challenging for prior approaches. However, the analysis accompanying these experiments could be strengthened.

(1) I concur with other reviewers regarding the use of the term ‘intrinsic’ motivation. Given current usage within the reinforcement learning field, it may be a misnomer. The pre-training regime – rewarding speed for locomotion or grasping for manipulation – appears strongly directed towards the eventual downstream tasks, echoing the approach taken by Heess et al., though not identically. 

(2) The Mutual Information regularization is a conceptually sound and generally effective technique. However, it does not appear to provide benefits in the more complex maze environments (1, 2, and 3). The authors acknowledge this observation; further investigation or discussion regarding the potential reasons for this outcome would be valuable.

(3) The paper would benefit from a more detailed specification of the Srest network, in addition to the description of Sagent provided, following the example set by Duan et al. Ensuring clarity on this point is crucial for replicability – I may have overlooked this detail, but a more explicit description would be beneficial.

(4) Providing analysis of the agent’s switching behavior would be insightful. More broadly, further analysis of the learned policies – including an examination of failure modes and the impact of switching timing on performance – would enhance the paper’s contribution.
