I appreciate the setting presented in this paper, but I have several critiques/questions:
(1) What are the failure modes of this work? As behavioral complexity increases, I would expect the approach to encounter difficulties with the diversity of skills that can be discovered.
(2) In Sec. 5.3 — “let X be a random variable denoting the grid in which the agent is currently situated” — is the space discretized? If so, why, and what happens if it is not?
(3) Following up on the first point, does the method extend to more complex embodiments? For example, would it work with a 5-link swimmer instead of a 2-link one? This seems important for assessing the generality of the approach.
(4) The authors claim that Heess et al. (2016) learn a range of skills in a pre-training environment with specified goals, whereas here the agent uses only intrinsic rewards during pre-training, requiring minimal domain knowledge. I’m not fully convinced: the reward designs in this paper also appear quite hand-crafted and targeted to a relatively narrow set of control tasks.
