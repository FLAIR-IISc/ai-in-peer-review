Review- Engaging work on hierarchical control, closely related to Heess et al. The experiments are strong and solve benchmarks that prior work could not, though the analysis of these experiments is comparatively weaker.

(1) As other reviewers noted, the use of the term “intrinsic” motivation seems inappropriate given its established meaning in RL. Pretraining robots for locomotion by rewarding speed (or for manipulation by rewarding grasping) is tightly aligned with the downstream tasks. The pretraining tasks in Heess et al., while not identical, are similar in spirit.

(2) The Mutual Information regularization is elegant and generally effective, but it does not appear to help in the more complex mazes 1, 2, and 3. The authors note this—can you provide an interpretation or further analysis of this outcome?

(3) The factorization between Sagent and Srest should be explained clearly. Duan et al. specify Sagent, but for reproducibility Srest should also be explicitly defined—did I miss this?

(4) An analysis of the agent’s switching behavior would be insightful. More broadly, additional policy analysis (failure cases, and the impact of switching time on performance) would have been valuable.
