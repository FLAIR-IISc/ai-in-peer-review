- Proposes hierarchical RL: pretrain low-level SNN skills with an MI regularizer, then train a high-level manager that selects skills over fixed temporal windows to solve sparse-reward tasks.
- Uses categorical latents fed via bilinear integration into the policy to induce multi-modality in behaviors.
- Adds an MI bonus between the latent code and a subset of state (e.g., CoM) to promote diverse, behaviorally distinct skills.
- Trains both pretraining and manager with TRPO, treating the latent as part of the observation.
- Provides ablations (concatenation vs bilinear, with/without MI), qualitative visitation plots, and downstream results on sparse mazes and a gathering task.
- Evaluates on more complex agents (Snake), analyzes sensitivity to switch time T, and reports failure modes on unstable agents (Ant).
- Decision: Weak accept.
- Strength: Simple, well-motivated framework that empirically improves exploration and sample efficiency in sparse, long-horizon control tasks.
- Strength: SNN+MI yields interpretable, reusable skills with solid qualitative and quantitative evidence.
- Strength: Clear writing, useful ablations/analysis, and provided code/videos increase reproducibility and impact.
- Evidence: Bilinear integration and MI substantially increase skill diversity and translate to better downstream learning.
- Caveat: MI estimator relies on discretization; downstream tasks are still relatively constrained and robustness needs more quantitative analysis.
- Suggestion: Quantify Ant instability under skill switching and evaluate mitigations (blending, learned transitions, latent continuity).
- Suggestion: Report sensitivity to number of skills K and MI coefficient αH with curves for diversity and downstream returns.
- Suggestion: Address variability across SNN pretraining seeds and consider manager augmentations (recurrence, hindsight relabeling) to reduce dependence on specific skills.
- MI detail: Justify discretizing CoM for p(Z|C), test sensitivity to grid resolution, and compare to learned density or classifier-based MI estimators.
- MI applicability: Discuss how to choose the state subset for MI (beyond CoM) in other domains like manipulation.
- Generality: Provide more quantitative comparisons between embodiments (Swimmer vs Snake) and report manager downstream performance for Ant with failure analysis.
- Generality: Test alternative proxy rewards and non-locomotion tasks to substantiate claims of minimal domain knowledge and reusability.
- Prior-work relation: Temper claims of “minimal domain knowledge” relative to Heess et al. and compare MI-based diversity to alternatives (empowerment, DIAYN).
- Hierarchy design: Discuss fixed commitment time T vs learned termination, and explore continuous latents or manager entropy/temperature control.
- Reporting: Include mean±std over seeds, compact diversity metrics (coverage, pairwise divergence), and clarify pretraining compute/sample costs vs downstream savings.
- Key questions for authors: dominant failure modes and mitigations; sensitivity to MI discretization vs continuous estimators; scaling to complex embodiments with quantitative results; guidance on proxy reward design; how to choose K; whether end-to-end fine-tuning of low-level skills with the manager was evaluated.
