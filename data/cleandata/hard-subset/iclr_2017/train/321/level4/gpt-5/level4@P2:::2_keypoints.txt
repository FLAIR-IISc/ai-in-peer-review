- Summary: Proposes hierarchical RL with pre-trained diverse skills (SNN conditioned on discrete latent + MI bonus) and a manager that composes skills every T steps for sparse-reward tasks.
- Empirical claim: Learns interpretable, temporally-extended locomotion skills and substantially speeds learning in maze/gather tasks, outperforming strong baselines.
- Visualization/analysis: Provides informative visualizations and ablations (e.g., bilinear vs. concatenation, MI weight) with fair comparisons.
- Decision: Accept.

Key reasons for acceptance:
- Novelty: Combines SNN conditioning with an information-theoretic objective to induce diverse, identifiable skills for robust exploration.
- Empirical strength: Strong results on sparse-reward tasks with careful ablations showing when MI and bilinear integration matter.

Supporting arguments:
- Problem formulation: Compelling pre-training of reusable skills with minimal domain knowledge and hierarchical composition for downstream tasks.
- Methodological grounding: Choices well-justified and connected to prior work (SNNs, InfoGAN-like MI objective, options/hierarchical control).
- Empirical cohesion: Visitation plots, long rollouts, and learning curves consistently support improved coverage and learning speed.
- Clarity and reproducibility: Paper is clear, includes code/videos, and candidly analyzes failure modes (e.g., Ant).

Feedback to strengthen the paper:
- Skill diversity breadth: Current conditioning (first-layer modulation) may limit diversity for non-locomotion tasks; consider richer conditioning (FiLM, hypernets) or harder domains.
- Hierarchical training assumptions: Manager trained with frozen skills and fixed T; discuss/end-to-end finetuning, learned terminations, or manager-conditioned low-level adaptation.
- Proxy reward generality: Provide sensitivity analyses for proxy choice, MI weight αH, CoM grid discretization, and number of skills K.
- Data-budget accounting: Clarify pre-training vs downstream sample counts and show total environment steps to make transfer benefit transparent.

Questions for authors:
- Baseline failure: Why does “CoM + task reward” baseline perform poorly—lack of enforced behavioral diversity or other causes; any evidence adding diversity helps?
- Baseline data usage: Do baselines get same pre-training experience or are they trained from scratch; report total environment steps per method.
- Multi-policy baseline: Precisely define the multi-policy baseline and confirm matching capacities/training budgets with SNN.
- Integration mechanisms: Confirm concatenation selects different biases per latent and bilinear modulates the first-layer weight matrix.
- MI estimation sensitivity: How sensitive is performance to CoM grid resolution; have you tried learning p̂(Z|c) with an MLP for higher-dim c?
- Hyperparameter sweeps: How do results vary with K, switch period T, and αH; do skills collapse without MI and can bilinear alone prevent collapse?
- Generalization: Anticipated transfer to manipulation or partially observable navigation tasks; preliminary results or obstacles (state subset choice, proxy reward design)?
- End-to-end training: Did you try joint training with straight-through/Gumbel-Softmax estimators and how did stability/performance compare to frozen-skill training?
