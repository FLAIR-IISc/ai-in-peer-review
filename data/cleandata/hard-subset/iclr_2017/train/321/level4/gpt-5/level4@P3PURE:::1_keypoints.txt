1. The term “intrinsic” motivation is misused—pre-training rewards (e.g., speed or grasping) are task-tailored and similar to Heess et al., not true intrinsic motivation.  
2. Mutual Information regularization is elegant and often helps, but it fails to improve performance on complex mazes 1–3; the authors should analyze why.  
3. The factorization between S_agent and S_rest must be clearly specified (Duan et al. define S_agent); S_rest needs explicit definition for replicability or a pointer to its section.  
4. Add analysis of the agent’s switching behavior and learned policies, including failure modes and the effect of switching time on performance.
