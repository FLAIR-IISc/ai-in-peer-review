I appreciate the setting presented in this paper, but I have several questions and concerns:

1) Failure modes. What are the failure modes of this approach? As the richness of behaviors increases, I would expect this method to struggle with the diversity of skills that can be discovered.

2) Discretization in Sec. 5.3. In Section 5.3 you write, “let X be a random variable denoting the grid in which the agent is currently situated.” Is the space discretized? If so, why is discretization necessary, and what happens if the space is not discretized?

3) Generality to more complex embodiments. Extending point (1), does the approach work with more complicated embodiments—for example, a 5-link swimmer instead of a 2-link swimmer? This seems important for assessing the generality of the approach.

4) Comparison to Heess et al. (2016). You claim: “Recently, Heess et al. (2016) have independently proposed to learn a range of skills in a pre-training environment that will be useful for the downstream tasks, which is similar to our framework. However, their pre-training setup requires a set of goals to be specified. In comparison, we use intrinsic rewards as the only signal to the agent during the pre-training phase, the construction of which only requires very minimal domain knowledge.” I do not fully agree. The rewards proposed here also appear hand-crafted and specific to a relatively limited set of control tasks.
