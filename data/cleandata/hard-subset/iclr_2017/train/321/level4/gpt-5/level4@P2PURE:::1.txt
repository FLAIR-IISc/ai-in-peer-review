This is an interesting paper on hierarchical control, with clear parallels to the work of Heess et al. The experimental results are strong and solve benchmarks that prior methods could not, though the accompanying analysis is comparatively weaker.

1) As other reviewers have noted, the term “intrinsic motivation” seems misapplied given its established meaning in RL. Pretraining locomotion by rewarding speed—or a manipulator by rewarding grasping—is closely aligned with the downstream tasks the agents will face. The pretraining tasks in Heess et al., while not identical, are similar in spirit.

2) The Mutual Information regularization is elegant and generally effective, but it does not appear to help in the more complex mazes 1, 2, and 3. The authors acknowledge this; can they offer an interpretation or deeper analysis of why it fails there?

3) The factorization between S_agent and S_rest should be described clearly. Duan et al. specify S_agent, but for replicability, S_rest should also be precisely defined—did I overlook this?

4) It would be valuable to analyze the agent’s switching behavior. More broadly, additional policy analysis—e.g., failure modes and the effect of switching time on performance—would strengthen the paper.
