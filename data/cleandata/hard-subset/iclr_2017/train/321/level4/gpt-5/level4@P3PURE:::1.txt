This is an interesting paper on hierarchical control, with clear connections to Heess et al. The experimental results are strong, successfully completing benchmarks that prior work could not. However, the analysis of the experiments is comparatively weaker.

(1) As other reviewers have noted, the use of the term “intrinsic” motivation feels inappropriate given its established meaning in RL. Pre-training robots for locomotion by rewarding speed (or pre-training a manipulator by rewarding grasping) is highly tailored to the downstream tasks. The pre-training tasks in Heess et al., while not identical, are similar in this respect.

(2) The Mutual Information regularization is elegant and generally effective, but it does not appear to help in the more complex mazes 1, 2, and 3. The authors acknowledge this; can they provide an interpretation or analysis of why MI regularization fails to help in these settings?

(3) The factorization between Sagent and Srest should be clearly detailed in the paper. Duan et al. specify Sagent; for replicability, Srest should be clearly specified as well. If this is already included, please point to the relevant section—otherwise, it should be added.

(4) It would be valuable to include analysis of the agent’s switching behavior. More generally, additional analysis of the learned policies—such as failure modes and the effect of the switching time on performance—would strengthen the paper.
