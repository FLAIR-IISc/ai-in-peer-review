- Paper proposes pre‑training diverse, reusable skills via a discrete latent SNN with an MI bonus on a small subset of state features, then trains a frozen low‑level skill bank with a high‑level Manager to solve sparse, long‑horizon tasks.

- Skills use bilinear integration of observation and latent code for greater expressivity; Manager switches skills every T steps.

- Evaluated on challenging sparse tasks (Locomotion+Maze, Gather) across multiple robots, showing substantial exploration and sample‑efficiency gains over strong baselines.

- Decision: Accept.

- Key reason 1: Strong empirical evidence that SNN skill pre‑training plus a Manager markedly improves exploration and performance on hard sparse‑reward benchmarks.

- Key reason 2: Method is well‑motivated, practical, and integrates hierarchical RL and intrinsic motivation ideas with clear ablations (bilinear integration, MI bonus).

- Problem significance: Addresses core RL challenge of sparse rewards and long horizons by enabling exploration and credit assignment without per‑task shaping.

- Method motivation: SNNs provide multi‑modal, shared skill representations; MI promotes identifiability; bilinear integration improves state–latent interaction.

- Empirical rigor: Multiple environments, ablations, visitation analyses, cross‑agent tests, transparency about failure modes (Ant), and code provided.

- Area to strengthen: Clarify use of the term “intrinsic motivation” since the proxy reward is aligned with downstream capabilities and differs from novelty/uncertainty bonuses.

- Area to strengthen: Provide analysis on when MI regularizer helps vs when benefits are mixed across mazes to guide tuning.

- Area to strengthen: Explicitly list Srest and the chosen subset C per environment and detail C’s discretization for reproducibility.

- Area to strengthen: More compact analysis of switching behavior (dwell times, failure modes, sensitivity to T) and consider learned termination as a baseline.

- Additional feedback: Quantify skill diversity with metrics (pairwise divergence, coverage, MI achieved) beyond visitation plots.

- Additional feedback: Summarize sensitivity to K (number of skills), MI grid resolution, and choice of C; provide consistent defaults.

- Additional feedback: Position baselines more clearly (option‑critic, goal‑conditioned pre‑training) to clarify novelty and trade‑offs.

- Additional feedback: Report computational cost/training time and memory overhead of SNN vs multi‑policy baselines, emphasizing sample efficiency benefits.

- Questions for authors: Sensitivity of results to MI posterior grid resolution and whether learned density models were tried.

- Questions for authors: How was subset C chosen; would including velocity/orientation improve identifiability?

- Questions for authors: Sensitivity to K; do unused/redundant skills harm Manager learning and was pruning/merging attempted?

- Questions for authors: How was T selected and how robust are results to T; could learned termination improve stability (e.g., Ant)?

- Questions for authors: What observations does the Manager receive, how is overfitting to SNN idiosyncrasies prevented, and were recurrent Managers tried?

- Questions for authors: Pros/cons vs goal‑conditioned pre‑training regarding instrumentation, diversity, and transfer?

- Questions for authors: Were end‑to‑end fine‑tuning or straight‑through estimators attempted, and what were the results?
