This is an interesting paper on hierarchical control, closely related to Heess et al. The experiments are strong and solve benchmarks that prior work could not, but the analysis of these results is comparatively weak.

(1) As other reviewers noted, the use of the term “intrinsic motivation” seems inappropriate given its established meaning in RL. Pretraining robots for locomotion by rewarding speed (or pretraining a manipulator by rewarding grasping) is highly task-directed toward the downstream objectives. The pretraining tasks in Heess et al., while not identical, are broadly similar. Please either justify the terminology more clearly or consider alternative phrasing.

(2) The mutual-information (MI) regularization is elegant and generally effective, but it does not appear to help in the more complex Mazes 1–3. The paper acknowledges this; can the authors provide an interpretation or analysis of why MI fails to confer benefits in these settings?

(3) The factorization between S_agent and S_rest should be clearly specified. Duan et al. describe S_agent; for replicability, S_rest should also be detailed. If this is already included, I may have missed it—please point to the exact section or add a concise description.

(4) It would be valuable to include analysis of the agent’s switching behavior. More broadly, additional policy analysis—e.g., common failure modes and how the switching period or timing affects performance—would strengthen the paper.
