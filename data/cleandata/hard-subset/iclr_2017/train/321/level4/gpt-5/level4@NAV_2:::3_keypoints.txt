- Paper proposes a two-phase hierarchical RL framework: pretrain diverse skills with an SNN using a single proxy reward and MI regularization, then freeze skills and train a high-level Manager to solve sparse, long-horizon tasks.  
- Skills are encouraged to be diverse and interpretable via MI between a latent code and a subset of state plus architectural choices like bilinear integration.  
- Empirically, the method learns a broad set of visually interpretable locomotion skills and yields faster learning and stronger performance on sparse downstream tasks versus strong baselines.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason: The approach is well-motivated, coherently combines intrinsic motivation and hierarchical control, and includes concrete algorithmic choices.  
- Key reason: Empirical results show reliable skill acquisition in pretraining and improved exploration and downstream learning when using the hierarchical skill set.  
- Problem/goal strength: Targets exploration in sparse-reward, long-horizon tasks by pretraining reusable skills to reduce downstream sample complexity with minimal domain knowledge.  
- Methodological soundness: Using discrete latent SNNs with MI bonus and treating z as part of the observation is a sensible, TRPO-compatible way to capture multimodality and encourage skill identifiability.  
- Design choice validation: Bilinear integration of latent and observations is a principled and empirically validated improvement over concatenation for skill diversity.  
- Empirical support: Visitation analyses and downstream maze/gather tasks show distinct, interpretable skills and improved coverage/performance compared to naive exploration or proxy-reward baselines.  
- Feedback — failure modes: Need more systematic characterization of mode collapse or behavior entanglement as K, morphology complexity, or horizon increase, and which components prevent collapse.  
- Feedback — MI estimation: Clarify rationale and sensitivity of discretizing state for MI (grid resolution, state subset) and include ablations against learned posterior/density estimators.  
- Feedback — embodiment complexity: Show main-text results on higher-DOF embodiments and discuss whether diversity/stability scale with action/state dimensionality and hyperparameter changes.  
- Feedback — positioning vs goal-conditioned pretraining: Clarify scope where proxy rewards are truly minimal versus task-specific, and discuss sensitivity to proxy misspecification.  
- Questions — failure modes: What failures occur as K or embodiment complexity grow, how often, and which mitigations (αH, bilinear, priors) work best?  
- Questions — MI discretization: Is discretization required or preferred for MI; how robust is performance to grid choices, and how do learned estimators compare in cost/stability?  
- Questions — embodiment generality: Does the method preserve diversity and downstream gains on more complex embodiments (e.g., 5-link swimmer) and require different hyperparameters/architecture?  
- Questions — proxy hand-crafting: How much task-specific hand-engineering do proxies require across domains, and how does the method handle domains where a single proxy is hard to design?
