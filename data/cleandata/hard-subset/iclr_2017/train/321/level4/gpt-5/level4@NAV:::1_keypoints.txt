1) Misuse of “intrinsic motivation”: pretraining rewards (e.g., speed, grasping) are tightly aligned with downstream tasks and similar in spirit to Heess et al., so the term is inappropriate.  
2) Mutual Information regularization: elegant and often effective, but it fails to help on complex mazes 1–3 — the reviewer asks for interpretation or further analysis.  
3) State factorization unclear: the division between S_agent and S_rest must be explained and S_rest explicitly defined for reproducibility.  
4) Policy analysis lacking: need examination of switching behavior, failure cases, and how switching timing affects performance.
