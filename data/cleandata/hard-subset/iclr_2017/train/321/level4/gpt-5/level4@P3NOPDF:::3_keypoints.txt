- Clear, timely motivation: unsupervised skill acquisition to accelerate downstream learning with a simple, interpretable intrinsic objective and promising qualitative/transfer results.
- Major concern — failure modes and scalability: need diagnostics on mode collapse, sensitivity to skill count/regularization/pre-training budget, and whether behaviors become trivial or repetitive.
- Quantitative diversity measures requested: report state-occupancy entropy, coverage, pairwise distances/KL between visitation distributions rather than only qualitative snapshots.
- Scalability question: discuss how diversity scales with environment complexity and horizon length and what prevents redundancy or premature convergence.
- Clarification needed on discretization (Sec. 5.3): is the state space gridded? justify resolution choice, sensitivity, and whether discretization is training-only or evaluation-only.
- Continuous-state alternative: explain how the intrinsic signal would be computed without grids (e.g., learned density/classifier), and the computational/stability trade-offs.
- Generality across embodiments: demonstrate or analyze scaling to higher-DoF agents (e.g., more articulated swimmers/ant-like agents) including effects on training stability, sample complexity, and sparsity of intrinsic reward.
- Positioning vs prior work: temper the “minimal domain knowledge” claim, enumerate hand-crafted design choices, compare to other intrinsic-motivation baselines (prediction-error, count-based, empowerment), and provide ablations.
- Methodological clarity: precisely define random variables, distributions, objectives, and how any discrete codes or grids are estimated online and used in policy updates.
- Skill parameterization and compositionality: specify skill encoding, temporal extent/termination, selection during transfer, composability for long-horizon tasks, and credit-assignment during fine-tuning.
- Evaluation protocol improvements: report zero-shot performance, fine-tuning speed, multiple seeds, and confidence intervals.
- Scaling and compute transparency: provide training curves, wall-clock compute, and sample budgets for pre-training and how these scale with complexity and skill count.
- Sensitivity analyses requested: vary number of skills, pre-training duration, and grid resolution; include at least one environment where discretization is impractical and show adaptation.
- Minor clarifications: fix ambiguous notation in Sec. 5.3, ensure consistent figure color maps/scale bars, and include hyperparameters/implementation details for reproducibility.
- Overall: promising direction with appealing qualitative results, but claims of generality/minimal engineering require clearer discretization account, more complex-embodiment evaluations, quantitative diversity/robustness analyses, and stronger comparisons to related intrinsic-motivation methods.
