- Proposes an HRL framework: pretrain diverse skills with SNNs + MI bonus, then train a high-level Manager to select skills for sparse-reward downstream tasks.  
- Key idea: learn multi-modal, interpretable sub-policies with a single proxy reward and encourage diversity via an information-theoretic regularizer.  
- Implementation: uses TRPO, bilinear integration of latent codes and observations to induce multi-modality.  
- Empirical results: strong performance on long-horizon sparse tasks (maze navigation, gather) with clear visualizations and comparisons.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Primary reason 1: Well-motivated combination of intrinsic motivation and hierarchy; MI bonus yields identifiable, interpretable skills.  
- Primary reason 2: Empirical evaluation shows substantially improved exploration and sample efficiency, outperforming credible baselines.  
- Problem/approach fit: Temporally extended skills reduce effective horizon and improve exploration; proxy reward during pretraining is minimal and avoids task-specific shaping.  
- Technical soundness: SNNs with MI regularizer (measured on relevant state subset like CoM) and bilinear integration are principled and effective.  
- Empirical rigor: Multiple tasks, ablations, visitation plots, learning curves, and a strong intrinsic-reward baseline comparison that fails on long horizons.  
- Limitation 1: Latent primarily modulates first hidden layer—may limit diversification for non-locomotion domains; suggest testing harder domains or richer conditioning architectures.  
- Limitation 2: Manager trained with frozen sub-policies and fixed skill duration T; success depends on proxy reward and skill coverage—end-to-end/adaptive termination would strengthen work.  
- Limitation 3: Proxy/intrinsic rewards can be domain-specific; need discussion/guidance on constructing generic proxy rewards.  
- Questions for authors: why CoM maze reward baseline fails beyond credit assignment; clarify Multi-policy vs SNN differences; confirm concatenation vs bilinear effects in Fig.1; generalization of MI-on-CoM to non-positional factors; and whether joint training (e.g., Gumbel-Softmax) was tried and how it compared.
