- Proposed method: pretrain temporally-extended skills in a proxy environment using Stochastic Neural Networks (categorical latent z) and train a high-level Manager to select skills at fixed intervals for downstream tasks.
- Diversity/interpretability mechanism: add a mutual information (MI) regularizer between latent code and a subset of state (e.g., CoM), estimated via discretization and counts.
- Training detail: train skills with TRPO by treating the latent code as part of the observation.
- Architecture insight: bilinear integration plus MI bonus yields a richer span of skills than independent unimodal policies at similar sample cost.
- Empirical claims: hierarchical policies built on these skills solve sparse long-horizon tasks (mazes, gather) more reliably and efficiently than strong baselines.
- Positioning: well placed within hierarchical RL and intrinsic motivation literature; combines known components in a practical, novel way.
- Evidence: thorough qualitative analyses (visitation plots, ablations) and quantitative gains with open-sourced code support the method’s effectiveness.
- Limitation noted: instability when switching skills on fragile embodiments (e.g., Ant) and sensitivity to switch interval T.
- Limitation noted: potential mode collapse or overlapping skills when MI regularizer is weak.
- Suggestion: foreground and consolidate failure modes and a diagnostic checklist (indicators of collapse, recovery strategies) in the main text.
- MI estimation concern: clarify rationale for discretizing CoM, grid resolution choice, sensitivity, and alternatives (learned classifier or continuous estimator).
- Suggestion: report robustness to the choice of conditioning state c (CoM vs. other subsets).
- Scalability concern: discuss generality to higher-DoF or unstable agents (e.g., 5-link swimmer/snake, Ant) and consider promoting such results to main paper.
- Claim nuance: temper “minimal domain knowledge” claim since proxy rewards are hand-designed (e.g., speed) and may be locomotion-biased.
- Manager design: analyze sensitivity to fixed interval T, consider learned termination policies or recurrent Managers for better switching.
- Quantify diversity: supplement visitation plots with metrics (state visitation entropy, MI estimates, pairwise behavior distances).
- Hyperparameter K: discuss selection of K, behavior when K exceeds useful skills, and evidence of unused/duplicate codes or skill collapse.
- Question (failure modes): how do α (MI weight), K, and T interact as skills scale, and what failure modes emerge (overlap, unstable switching, Manager instability)?
- Question (MI/discretization): why prefer discretization for p(z|c) vs. learned classifier/continuous methods, and sensitivity to grid resolution?
- Question (scalability): does method work out-of-the-box for higher-DoF or unstable agents, and can you quantify switching-induced failures and mitigations?
- Question (proxy reward): how dependent is performance on hand-crafted proxy rewards and how to design them for non-locomotion domains?
- Question (choice of K): how is K chosen in practice, are latent codes unused or redundant, and can you merge/prune skills?
- Question (end-to-end fine-tuning): have you tried joint fine-tuning of low-level skills and Manager (e.g., Gumbel-Softmax) and its effects on performance vs. stability?
- Question (transfer/overfitting): do skills overfit to pretraining layout, how robust is transfer across environment variations, and would domain randomization help?
