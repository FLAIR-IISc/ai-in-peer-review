I find the proposed setting appealing, but I have several concerns and questions:
(1) What are the failure modes of this approach? As the richness of behaviors increases, I would expect the method to struggle with the diversity of skills that can be discovered.
(2) In Sec. 5.3, you write: “let X be a random variable denoting the grid in which the agent is currently situated.” Does this imply the space is discretized? If so, why is discretization necessary, and what happens if it is not discretized?
(3) Following up on point (1), does the approach extend to more complex embodiments—for example, a 5-link swimmer instead of a 2-link? This seems important for assessing the generality of the method.
(4) The paper states: “Recently, Heess et al. (2016) have independently proposed to learn a range of skills in a pre-training environment that will be useful for the downstream tasks, which is similar to our framework. However, their pre-training setup requires a set of goals to be specified. In comparison, we use intrinsic rewards as the only signal to the agent during the pre-training phase, the construction of which only requires very minimal domain knowledge.” I am not entirely convinced by this distinction. The intrinsic rewards proposed here also appear hand-crafted and tailored to a relatively limited set of control tasks.
