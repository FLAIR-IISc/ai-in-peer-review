I appreciate the problem setting in this paper, but I have several concerns and questions:

1) What are the failure modes of this approach? As the richness of possible behaviors increases, I would expect the method to struggle with the diversity of skills that can be discovered.

2) In Section 5.3—“let X be a random variable denoting the grid in which the agent is currently situated”—is the state space discretized? If so, why was this design choice made, and what happens if the space is not discretized?

3) Related to point (1), does the method scale to more complex embodiments? For example, how would it handle a 5-link swimmer instead of a 2-link one? This seems important for assessing the generality of the approach.

4) The paper contrasts its framework with Heess et al. (2016), asserting that their pre-training requires specified goals, whereas this work uses only intrinsic rewards with minimal domain knowledge. I’m not entirely convinced: the intrinsic rewards proposed here also seem hand-crafted and tailored to a relatively limited class of control tasks.

Clarifications and empirical evidence addressing these points would significantly strengthen the paper.
