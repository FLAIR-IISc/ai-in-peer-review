- Proposes a novel hierarchical RL framework using stochastic neural networks (SNNs).
- Introduces an information-theoretic option-identifiability reward to encourage diverse sub-policies.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on challenging tasks (maze navigation, object gathering).
- Shows performance superior to a strong baseline that combines intrinsic and sparse rewards.
- Recommends acceptance with a score of 7.
- Provides a thorough empirical evaluation across multiple tasks.
- Includes clear visualizations that illustrate results well.
- Strength: enables learning a wide range of reusable skills for downstream tasks.
- Strength: empirically outperforms a strong intrinsic+sparse-reward baseline.
- Limitation: may not produce sufficiently diverse sub-policies for harder or non-locomotion domains.
- Limitation: Manager is trained with fixed, pre-trained sub-policies and fixed sub-policy durations—lacks end-to-end hierarchical learning.
- Limitation: intrinsic/unsupervised rewards may be domain-specific and not broadly generalizable.
- Question: Why does the strong baseline in Section 7.3 perform very poorly and fail to solve the task?
- Question: Does the baseline account for the extra experience obtained by pre-trained policies?
- Question: What is meant by “multi-policy” as compared to SNN?
- Question: In Fig.1, does concatenation mean categorical bias to the first hidden layer and bilinear integration mean different observation-to-hidden weight matrices?
