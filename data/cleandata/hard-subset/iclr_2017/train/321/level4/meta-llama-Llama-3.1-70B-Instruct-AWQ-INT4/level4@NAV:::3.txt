I appreciate the framework presented in this paper, but I have several concerns and questions that need to be addressed:

(1) The authors should clarify the failure model of their approach. Given the complexity of behaviors, I anticipate potential issues with the diversity of skills that can be discovered, and I would like to see a discussion on how these challenges are mitigated.

(2) In Section 5.3, the definition of the random variable X denoting the agent's current grid position raises questions about the discretization of the space. Specifically, is the space discretized, and if so, what is the rationale behind this choice? Furthermore, how does the approach behave if the space is not discretized?

(3) Building on my first point, it is essential to evaluate the generality of this approach by applying it to more complex embodiments, such as a 5-link swimmer instead of a 2-link one. This would provide valuable insights into the method's robustness and versatility.

(4) The authors compare their framework to the work of Heess et al. (2016), suggesting that their approach requires minimal domain knowledge due to the use of intrinsic rewards. However, I disagree with this assessment, as the rewards proposed in this paper also appear to be hand-crafted and specific to a limited set of control tasks, which may not be significantly different from the goal-based setup in Heess et al.'s work.
