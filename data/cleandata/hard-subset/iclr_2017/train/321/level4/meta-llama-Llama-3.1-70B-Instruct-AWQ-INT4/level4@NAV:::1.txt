This study presents a compelling exploration of hierarchical control, bearing similarities to the work of Heess et al. The experimental results are robust and successfully complete benchmarks that previous studies were unable to achieve. However, the analysis of these experiments could be improved.

Several points warrant further consideration:

(1) The term 'intrinsic' motivation may be misapplied, given its current connotations in reinforcement learning (RL). Pre-training robots with locomotion tasks that reward speed, or grasping tasks for a manipulating arm, appears to be closely tied to the tasks they will later perform. This approach is reminiscent of Heess et al.'s pre-training tasks, albeit with some differences.

(2) The Mutual Information regularization is an elegant solution that generally yields positive results, but its effectiveness in more complex mazes (1, 2, and 3) is limited. The authors acknowledge this finding; a deeper interpretation or analysis of this result would be beneficial.

(3) To ensure replicability, it is essential to provide a clear explanation of the factorization between Sagent and Srest in the paper. While Duan et al. specify Sagent, Srest should also be explicitly defined to facilitate reproduction of the results.

(4) A more in-depth analysis of the agent's switching behavior would be a valuable addition to the study. Furthermore, exploring the policies in greater detail, including failure modes and the impact of switching time on performance, would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the results.
