This paper presents an interesting work on hierarchical control, similar to the work of Heess et al. The experiments are strong and successfully complete benchmarks that previous work could not. However, the analysis of the experiments is somewhat lacking.

I share the same concern as other reviewers regarding the use of the term 'intrinsic' motivation, as it seems somewhat inappropriate given its current meaning in reinforcement learning (RL). The pre-training of robots with locomotion by rewarding speed (or rewarding grasping for a manipulating arm) appears to be highly task-specific, rather than an intrinsic motivation. This approach is similar to the pre-training tasks employed by Heess et al., albeit not identical.

The Mutual Information regularization is an elegant technique that generally works well, but its effectiveness seems to diminish in the more complex mazes 1, 2, and 3. The authors acknowledge this limitation, but a more in-depth interpretation or analysis of this result would be beneficial.

For the sake of replicability, it would be helpful to clearly detail the factorization between Sagent and Srest in the paper. While Duan et al. specify Sagent, Srest should also be clearly specified to ensure that the method can be accurately reproduced. I may have overlooked this detail, but it does not appear to be explicitly stated in the paper.

Finally, it would be interesting to provide a more thorough analysis of the switching behavior of the agent, as well as a more general analysis of the policies. Specifically, examining failure modes and the effects of switching time on performance would have added depth to the paper and provided valuable insights into the proposed hierarchical control method.
