- Interesting hierarchical control work related to Heess et al.; experiments strong but analysis weaker.  
- Proposes learning a skill set in pre-training and using it for sparse-reward downstream tasks.  
- Uses Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) to represent multi-modal policies.  
- Introduces an information-theoretic regularizer to encourage behavioral diversity.  
- Experiments show learning diverse skills and strong performance on challenging benchmarks.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Novel, effective hierarchical RL approach that learns a wide range of skills.  
- Key reason 2: Strong experiments that solve benchmarks previous work could not.  
- Supporting: Well-motivated placement in literature; SNNs and MI regularizer are notable innovations.  
- Critique: Terming pre-training as "intrinsic" motivation is misleading; better to call it locomotion/manipulation pre-training.  
- Critique: Mutual Information regularization helps in some cases but fails in others; needs deeper analysis.  
- Critique: Factorization between Sagent and Srest is not clearly specified—clarity needed for replicability.  
- Suggestion: Provide more analysis of switching behavior, failure modes, and the effect of switching time on performance.  
- Questions for authors: Explain why MI regularization is sometimes ineffective; clarify Sagent/Srest factorization and Srest specification; analyze switching behavior and failure cases.
