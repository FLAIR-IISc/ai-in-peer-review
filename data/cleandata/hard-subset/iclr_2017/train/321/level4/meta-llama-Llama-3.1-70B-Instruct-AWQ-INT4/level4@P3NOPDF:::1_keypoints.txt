- Presents an interesting hierarchical control approach related to Heess et al., with strong experiments that solve benchmarks previous work could not.
- Experimental analysis is insufficient and could be improved.
- The term "intrinsic motivation" is likely misapplied—pretraining rewards (speed, grasping) are heavily task-specific and resemble Heess et al.'s pretraining.
- Mutual Information regularization is elegant and generally effective but shows no benefit in the more complex mazes (1–3); this result needs deeper interpretation.
- For replicability, the paper must clearly specify the factorization between Sagent and Srest (Srest is not clearly defined).
- Additional analysis of agent switching behavior, failure modes, and the impact of switching time on performance is needed.
