This paper presents an interesting approach to hierarchical control, drawing similarities with the work of Heess et al. The experiments conducted are strong and successfully complete benchmarks that previous work could not. However, the analysis of these experiments could be improved.

One concern I share with other reviewers is the use of the term 'intrinsic' motivation, which seems somewhat misapplied given its current meaning in reinforcement learning. The pre-training of robots with locomotion tasks by rewarding speed, or with manipulation tasks by rewarding grasping, appears to be heavily geared towards the tasks they will later accomplish. This approach bears resemblance to the pre-training tasks employed by Heess et al., albeit with some differences.

The Mutual Information regularization proposed in the paper is elegant and generally effective. However, it does not seem to provide benefits in the more complex mazes 1, 2, and 3. The authors acknowledge this limitation, but a more in-depth interpretation or analysis of this result would be beneficial.

For the sake of replicability, it would be helpful if the paper clearly detailed the factorization between Sagent and Srest. While Duan et al. specify Sagent, a clear specification of Srest is also necessary. Upon reviewing the paper, I could not find this information - it is possible that I may have missed it.

Further analysis of the agent's switching behavior and policies would be a valuable addition to the paper. Specifically, examining failure modes and the effects of switching time on performance would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed approach.
