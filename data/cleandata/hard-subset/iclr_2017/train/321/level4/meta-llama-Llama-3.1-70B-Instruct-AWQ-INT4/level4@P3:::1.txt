**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper proposes a general framework for hierarchical reinforcement learning (HRL) that tackles tasks with sparse rewards and long horizons. The framework first learns useful skills in a pre-training environment using a proxy reward signal, which requires minimal domain knowledge about the downstream tasks. Then, a high-level policy is trained on top of these skills, providing a significant improvement in exploration and allowing the agent to tackle sparse rewards in the downstream tasks. The authors use Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) combined with an information-theoretic regularizer to efficiently pre-train a large span of skills.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

Based on the paper's contributions and the experiments presented, I decide to **Accept** this paper. The key reasons for this choice are:

1. The paper proposes a novel framework for HRL that addresses the challenges of sparse rewards and long horizons.
2. The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed framework in solving challenging tasks that previous work could not.

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper's contributions are well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem they aim to solve. The use of SNNs and the information-theoretic regularizer is a novel approach to learning a diverse set of skills in the pre-training environment. The experiments are strong and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed framework in solving challenging tasks.
