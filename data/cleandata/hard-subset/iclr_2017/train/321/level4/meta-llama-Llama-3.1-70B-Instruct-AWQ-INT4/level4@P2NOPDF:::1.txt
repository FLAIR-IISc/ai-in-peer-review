This paper presents an interesting work on hierarchical control, similar to the work of Heess et al. The experiments are strong and successfully complete benchmarks that previous work could not. However, the analysis of the experiments could be improved.

One concern is the use of the term 'intrinsic' motivation, which may be somewhat misleading given its current meaning in reinforcement learning. The pre-training of robots with locomotion by rewarding speed (or rewarding grasping for a manipulating arm) is highly task-specific, and similar pre-training tasks have been used in previous work, such as Heess et al. This raises questions about the novelty of the approach.

The Mutual Information regularization is an elegant technique that generally works well, but its effectiveness appears to be limited in the more complex mazes 1, 2, and 3. The authors acknowledge this limitation, but a more in-depth interpretation or analysis of this result would be beneficial.

To ensure replicability, the factorization between Sagent and Srest should be clearly detailed in the paper. While Duan et al specify Sagent, a clear specification of Srest is also necessary. A review of the paper did not reveal a clear description of Srest, and it is unclear if this was overlooked or not included.

Finally, an analysis of the switching behavior of the agent would be a valuable addition to the paper. More generally, further analysis of the policies, including failure modes and the effects of switching time on performance, would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the approach and its limitations.
