- Proposes a hierarchical RL framework: pre-train skills with proxy rewards using SNNs + information-theoretic regularizer, then train a high-level policy to handle sparse, long-horizon tasks.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Key reason 1: Novel HRL framework addressing sparse rewards and long horizons.  
- Key reason 2: Experiments demonstrate effectiveness on challenging tasks that prior work could not solve.  
- Supporting: Well-motivated approach; SNNs with information-theoretic regularizer is a novel way to learn diverse skills; experiments are strong.  
- Improvement: Provide more analysis of the agent’s switching behavior and how switching time affects performance.  
- Improvement: Provide more details on the factorization between Sagent and Srest for reproducibility.  
- Question 1: Why did Mutual Information regularization not help in the more complex mazes 1–3?  
- Question 2: How is the Sagent/Srest factorization specified—what exactly is Srest?  
- Question 3: Please provide more analysis of switching behavior and the effects of switching time on performance.
