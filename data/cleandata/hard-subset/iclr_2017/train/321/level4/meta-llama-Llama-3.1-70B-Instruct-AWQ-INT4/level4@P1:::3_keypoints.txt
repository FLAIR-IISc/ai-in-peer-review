- Proposes a hierarchical RL framework using stochastic neural networks to learn diverse, reusable skills for sparse-reward tasks.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on challenging sparse-reward tasks with empirical improvements over a baseline.  
- Uses stochastic neural networks as policies to represent complex action distributions and learn multiple skills concurrently.  
- Introduces an information-theoretic regularizer to encourage diversity of behaviors.  
- Strength: well-motivated placement in literature addressing skill learning under sparse rewards.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation showing learning of a wide range of skills and improved downstream task performance.  
- Concern: pre-training rewards may be hand-crafted and specific to certain control tasks.  
- Recommendation: evaluate on more diverse tasks to better assess generality.  
- Recommendation: include a detailed analysis of failure modes as behavioral richness increases.  
- Clarify whether and why the state space is discretized in Section 5.3, and what happens if it isn’t.  
- Claim of greater generality than Heess et al. (2016) needs more thorough empirical and conceptual comparison.  
- Suggest expanding discussion of potential real-world applications, benefits, and limitations.  
- Question: provide more details on observed failure modes and plans to address them.  
- Question: clarify discretization in Section 5.3 and its implications.  
- Question: how will the approach scale to more complex embodiments (e.g., a 5-link swimmer)?  
- Question: provide details on construction of pre-training rewards and how they avoid being overly hand-crafted.
