- Proposes learning diverse skills with Stochastic Neural Networks and using them in a hierarchical controller; experiments complete benchmarks previous work could not.  
- Strong experimental results demonstrating capability on challenging sparse-reward tasks.  
- Term "intrinsic motivation" is misleading because pre-training rewards (e.g., speed, grasping) are task-biased rather than truly intrinsic.  
- Mutual Information regularization is elegant but does not help in complex mazes 1–3; needs deeper interpretation/analysis.  
- The factorization between S_agent and S_rest is not clearly specified (S_rest missing), harming replicability.  
- Paper lacks analysis of switching behavior and policy dynamics (failure modes, effect of switching time on performance).  
- Overall interesting and promising work that would be stronger with additional analyses and clarifications.
