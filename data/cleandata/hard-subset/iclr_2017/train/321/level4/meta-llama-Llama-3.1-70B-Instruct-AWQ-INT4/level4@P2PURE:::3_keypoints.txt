- Unclear failure modes and limitations: the paper lacks analysis of how the method scales or fails as behavior complexity increases, and may struggle to discover diverse skills in complex settings.

- Ambiguity in Section 5.3: it's not specified whether the state space is discretized (definition of X as a grid), the rationale for discretization, or how the method performs with a continuous space.

- Scalability/generality to complex embodiments: no experiments or theory showing the approach works on more complex agents (e.g., 5-link vs 2-link swimmer).

- Questionable claim of minimal domain knowledge: the intrinsic rewards appear hand-crafted and task-specific, undermining the asserted generality compared to prior work (Heess et al. 2016).
