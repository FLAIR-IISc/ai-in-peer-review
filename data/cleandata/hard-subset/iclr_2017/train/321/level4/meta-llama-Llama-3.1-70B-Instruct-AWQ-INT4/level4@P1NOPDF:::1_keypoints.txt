- Proposes an interesting hierarchical control approach similar to Heess et al.; experiments are strong and solve benchmarks previous work could not, but experimental analysis is insufficient.  
- The term "intrinsic motivation" is likely misused: pretraining rewards (speed, grasping) are heavily task-tailored and resemble prior supervised pretraining.  
- Mutual Information regularization is an elegant contribution and helps overall, but provides no clear benefit on the more complex mazes (1–3); this null result needs deeper interpretation.  
- Reproducibility concern: the factorization between S_agent and S_rest is not clearly specified (S_rest appears missing or underspecified).  
- Request analysis of the agent’s switching behavior (when/how it switches between controllers) and its effect on task performance.  
- Ask for broader policy analysis including failure modes and the impact of switching time on overall performance.
