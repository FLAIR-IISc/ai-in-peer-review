This work presents an interesting approach to hierarchical control, bearing similarities to the research by Heess et al. The experiments are robust and successfully tackle benchmarks that previous studies could not accomplish. However, the analysis of these experiments could be more comprehensive.

(1) I share the concern with other reviewers regarding the use of the term 'intrinsic' motivation, as it may be misleading given its current connotation in reinforcement learning (RL). The pre-training of robots with locomotion tasks by rewarding speed (or grasping for a manipulating arm) appears to be highly task-specific, which is also observed in the pre-training tasks employed by Heess et al.

(2) The Mutual Information regularization is an elegant approach that generally yields positive results, but its effectiveness seems to be limited in more complex mazes (1, 2, and 3). The authors acknowledge this limitation; it would be beneficial to provide further interpretation or analysis of this outcome.

(3) To ensure replicability, it is essential to clearly detail the factorization between Sagent and Srest in the paper. Although Duan et al. specify Sagent, a clear specification of Srest is also necessary. It is possible that this information is present in the paper, but it was not immediately apparent.

(4) A more in-depth analysis of the agent's switching behavior would be a valuable addition to the paper. Furthermore, a more comprehensive examination of the policies, including failure modes and the impact of switching time on performance, would have enhanced the overall quality of the work.
