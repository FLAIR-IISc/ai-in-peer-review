- Proposes SNN-based framework to learn diverse skills in pre-training and use them hierarchically to improve exploration in downstream sparse-reward tasks.  
- Well-motivated combination of intrinsic motivation and hierarchical methods with a clear distinction from prior work (e.g., Heess et al. 2016).  
- Empirical results on mazes and gathering tasks show faster learning and better performance than a baseline using Center-of-Mass speed intrinsic reward.  
- Reviewer requests more analysis of failure modes and limitations as behavioral richness increases and diversity discovery becomes harder.  
- Seeks clarification on the discretization in Section 5.3: why cells are necessary and what happens without discretization.  
- Requests additional experiments on more complex embodiments (e.g., 5-link swimmer) to assess generality.  
- Questions the authors’ claim of greater generality than Heess et al. (2016), arguing the intrinsic rewards used are hand-crafted and task-specific.  
- Accepts the paper conditionally, asking authors to address the above criticisms and provide more analysis and experiments.
