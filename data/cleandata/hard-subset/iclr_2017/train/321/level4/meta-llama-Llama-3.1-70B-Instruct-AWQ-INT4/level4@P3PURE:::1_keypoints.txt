- Paper presents interesting hierarchical control with strong experiments that solve benchmarks previous work could not, but experimental analysis is lacking.
- Term "intrinsic motivation" is misused: pre-training by rewarding speed/grasp is task-specific and not true intrinsic motivation, similar to Heess et al.
- Mutual information regularization is elegant and generally effective but its benefit diminishes in the more complex mazes (1–3) and needs deeper interpretation.
- Replicability requires a clear statement of the factorization between S_agent and S_rest; S_rest is not explicitly specified.
- More thorough analysis of agent switching behavior and policies is needed, including failure modes and how switching time affects performance.
