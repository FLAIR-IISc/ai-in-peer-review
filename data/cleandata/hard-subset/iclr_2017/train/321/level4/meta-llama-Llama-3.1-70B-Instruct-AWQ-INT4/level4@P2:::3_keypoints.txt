- Positive: Proposes a novel SNN-based framework for learning diverse skills with minimal supervision and a hierarchical architecture; paper is well-structured and analyzed.

- Major concern: No analysis of failure modes or limitations as behavior richness increases; unclear how or when the method fails.

- Diversity issue: Approach may struggle to discover diverse skills as complexity grows; reviewer expects problems with richer behavior spaces.

- Discretization ambiguity: Section 5.3 discretizes the space into cells, but the necessity, procedure, alternatives (no discretization), and impact on performance are not explained.

- Scalability worry: Unclear if method generalizes to more complex embodiments (e.g., 5-link swimmer vs. 2-link); lacks experiments showing scalability.

- Reward design critique: Claims of minimal domain knowledge are contested—intrinsic rewards appear hand-crafted and task-specific, raising questions about generality.

- Repro/benchmarking gap: Paper needs more comparisons with state-of-the-art methods to demonstrate effectiveness.

- Decision: Reject.

- Reasons for rejection: missing failure-mode analysis; limited demonstrated scalability; hand-crafted rewards that may not generalize.

- Requested additions: Provide failure-mode analysis, experiments on more complex embodiments, discussion/validation of reward generality, and more SOTA comparisons.

- Specific questions for authors: Explain limitations/failure cases; justify and analyze the discretization in Section 5.3; show experiments for more complicated embodiments; describe plans to generalize the reward design.
