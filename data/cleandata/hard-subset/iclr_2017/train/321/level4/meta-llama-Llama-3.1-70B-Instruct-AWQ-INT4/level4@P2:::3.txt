I like the setting presented in this paper but I have several criticisms and questions.

The authors propose a novel framework for learning a diverse set of skills using Stochastic Neural Networks trained with minimum supervision, and then utilizing these skills in a hierarchical architecture to solve challenging tasks with sparse rewards. The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of their approach.

However, I have several concerns and questions about the proposed method.

Firstly, what are the failure modes of this work? As the richness of behaviors gets complex, I expected this approach to have issues with the diversity of skills that could be discovered. The authors should provide more insights into the limitations of their approach and how it can fail.

Secondly, in Section 5.3, the authors mention that they discretize the space by partitioning it into cells. I would like to know more about this discretization process. Why is it necessary, and what happens if the space is not discretized? How does this affect the performance of the algorithm?

Thirdly, expanding on the first point, does the approach work with more complicated embodiments? For example, a 5-link swimmer instead of a 2-link swimmer? I think this is important to assess the generality of this approach. The authors should provide more experiments or analysis to demonstrate the scalability of their method.

Lastly, the authors claim that their approach is different from Heess et al. (2016) because they use intrinsic rewards as the only signal to the agent during the pre-training phase, which requires minimal domain knowledge. However, I don't entirely agree with this. The rewards proposed in this paper are also quite hand-crafted and specific to a seemingly limited set of control tasks. The authors should provide more discussion on the generality of their reward design and how it can be applied to other tasks.

Overall, I think the paper has some interesting ideas, but it needs more thorough analysis and experimentation to demonstrate its effectiveness and generality.

Decision: Reject

Reasons for rejection:

* Lack of analysis on the failure modes of the proposed method
* Limited scalability of the approach to more complicated embodiments
* Hand-crafted reward design that may not be generalizable to other tasks

Additional feedback:

* The authors should provide more insights into the limitations of their approach and how it can fail.
* The authors should provide more experiments or analysis to demonstrate the scalability of their method to more complicated embodiments.
* The authors should provide more discussion on the generality of their reward design and how it can be applied to other tasks.
* The authors should consider providing more comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods to demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.
