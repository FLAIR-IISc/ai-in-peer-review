- Proposes a framework that pre-trains useful skills via a single proxy reward and then leverages them for faster learning on sparse-reward downstream tasks.  
- Combines strengths of intrinsic motivation and hierarchical methods using Stochastic Neural Networks and information-theoretic regularizers.  
- Reviewer recommends accept with reservations: tackles an important RL problem and is well-motivated and novel.  
- Concern about failure modes: as behavior complexity increases, skill diversity may be limited; requests analysis of limitations.  
- Unclear why the space is discretized in Section 5.3 and what happens without discretization; authors should clarify.  
- Generality not demonstrated: claims about handling more complex embodiments (e.g., 5-link swimmer) are not empirically shown.  
- Criticism that intrinsic rewards are still hand-crafted and task-specific; requester asks for clearer distinction from Heess et al. (2016) and other methods.  
- Suggested improvements: analyze failure modes, explain discretization, test on more complex robots/environments, and discuss reward design and relation to prior work.
