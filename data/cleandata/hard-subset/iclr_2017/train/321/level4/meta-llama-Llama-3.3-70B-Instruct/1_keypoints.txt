1) Terminology: Calling the pre-training "intrinsic" is misleading—pre-training rewards (locomotion, grasping) appear task-specific and resemble Heess et al.'s pre-training.  
2) Mutual information regularization: Generally helpful but offers limited gains in complex mazes (1–3); requires deeper interpretation.  
3) Model factorization: Explicit specification of Sagent and, crucially, Srest is missing and needed for clarity and reproducibility.  
4) Behavioral analysis: More detailed examination of switching behavior, policy failure modes, and how switching timing affects performance is needed.
