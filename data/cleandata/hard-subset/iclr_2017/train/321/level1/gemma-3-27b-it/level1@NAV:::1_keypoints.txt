- Proposes an HRL framework using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) to pre-train skills and a high-level policy for downstream sparse-reward tasks.  
- Core idea combines intrinsic motivation and hierarchical methods via SNNs and an information-theoretic regularizer to encourage diverse skills.  
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly situated within HRL and intrinsic motivation literature.  
- Recommendation: accept — approach is novel and shows clear empirical benefits in sparse-reward environments.  
- Experiments show significant improvements on locomotion+maze and food-collection tasks compared to baselines using intrinsic rewards.  
- Ablation studies demonstrate the impact of bilinear integration and the mutual information (MI) bonus during SNN pre-training.  
- Inclusion of code and videos enhances reproducibility and understanding.  
- Clarification requested: provide more intuitive explanation for why bilinear integration outperforms concatenation.  
- Clarification requested: sensitivity of performance to the MI bonus coefficient (αH) needs more systematic analysis.  
- Generalizability concern: results on more complex/unstable robots (e.g., Ant) are lacking and showed instability.  
- Questions for authors: intuition behind bilinear integration, how to tune αH, how to stabilize learning for Ant, and computational overhead of SNNs versus standard networks.
