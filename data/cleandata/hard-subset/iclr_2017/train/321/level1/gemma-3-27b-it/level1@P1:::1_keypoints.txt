- Proposes a hierarchical RL framework that pre-trains diverse skills using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with an information-theoretic regularizer, then composes those skills to speed learning on sparse-reward downstream tasks.  
- Tackles sparse-reward, long-horizon RL by automatically discovering and reusing skills to improve sample efficiency without heavy domain knowledge.  
- Approach is well motivated and well-placed in the literature, clearly contrasting intrinsic motivation and traditional hierarchical RL and justifying the use of SNNs and an MI regularizer.  
- Empirical results on locomotion + maze/gather tasks are promising and show improvements over baselines, with useful ablation studies (bilinear integration and MI bonus).  
- Evaluation lacks statistical rigor: needs explicit statistical significance tests (e.g., t-tests) for performance comparisons.  
- High variance across runs depending on the pre-trained SNNs; more analysis is needed to explain why some SNNs transfer better than others.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity, particularly to the MI bonus coefficient (alpha), is underexplored and requires a more systematic study.  
- Baselines could be broader—benchmarking against a wider range of recent sparse-reward RL methods would strengthen validation.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions, contingent on more rigorous evaluation and clarifications.  
- Notation could be more consistent (e.g., ambiguous use of 'z' for latent variable vs. distribution).  
- Recommend additional visualizations of learned skills (trajectories/action distributions) beyond visitation plots to aid understanding.  
- Discuss computational cost of training SNNs versus standard networks, as current work omits this comparison.  
- Explore scalability to more complex, high-dimensional environments to validate broader applicability.  
- Questions for authors: provide statistical significance tests for reported comparisons.  
- Questions for authors: identify characteristics of pre-trained SNNs that correlate with better downstream performance (e.g., skill diversity, latent activations).  
- Questions for authors: report sensitivity of results to the MI bonus coefficient (alpha) with performance-vs-alpha plots.  
- Questions for authors: suggest methods to reduce variance across different pre-trained SNNs for more consistent performance.  
- Questions for authors: detail how SNN training computational cost scales with state/action dimensionality compared to standard policy networks.
