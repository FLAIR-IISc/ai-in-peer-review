- Tackles sparse-reward RL for multi-task single-agent settings with focus on sample efficiency.  
- Proposes hierarchical framework: pre-train reusable skills with Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) + mutual information (MI) regularizer, then use a high-level policy for downstream tasks.  
- Recommendation: accept — approach is well-motivated and empirically promising.  
- Key novelty: combination of SNNs and MI regularization to learn diverse, reusable skills.  
- Strength: SNNs represent multimodal policies effectively; MI prevents collapse and encourages diversity.  
- Strength: pre-training/fine-tuning framework improves exploration and sample efficiency on downstream tasks.  
- Empirical results: significant performance gains over baselines; ablations (Fig.7) show importance of both SNN and MI.  
- Visualizations (visitation plots) effectively illustrate learned skill behaviors.  
- Weakness: limited explanation/intuition or theory for why bilinear integration outperforms concatenation.  
- Weakness: comparisons to other skill-discovery methods (e.g., imitation learning / inverse RL approaches) are limited.  
- Weakness: failure case on Ant robot indicates instability when switching skills; more analysis or mitigation needed.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter sensitivity not fully explored—MI bonus coefficient (alpha) requires sensitivity analysis.  
- Question: clarify why bilinear integration improves diversity—representational or optimization reasons?  
- Question: why not compare to recent imitation/inverse-RL based skill discovery methods; what differentiates this approach?  
- Question: can skill-switching instability be mitigated (e.g., smoothing transitions, recovery skills)?  
- Question: provide sensitivity analysis for MI bonus coefficient (alpha) and its effect on diversity and downstream performance.  
- Question: report computational overhead of SNNs vs standard networks and scalability with number of latent skills.  
- Question: discuss generalization to high-dimensional inputs (e.g., images) and any necessary modifications.  
- Question: could pre-trained skills be further leveraged via reward shaping in downstream tasks to amplify benefits?
