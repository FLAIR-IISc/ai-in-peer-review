- Decision: Accept — novel, well-motivated HRL approach with strong empirical benefit.

- Problem & motivation: Addresses sparse rewards and long horizons by combining intrinsic motivation for skill discovery with hierarchy to reduce exploration complexity.

- Approach: Proposes SNN-based skill learning with an information-theoretic (MI) regularizer and a two-level hierarchical policy; justification and relation to prior work are clear.

- Architecture rationale: SNNs chosen for multi-modal policies and weight sharing; MI regularizer promotes diverse skills.

- Empirical results: Substantial improvements over baselines across multiple benchmarks and morphologies, with reproducible code and videos.

- Ablations & analysis: Ablation studies convincingly show benefits of MI bonus and SNN design; visitation plots illustrate skill variety.

- Reproducibility caveat: A more exhaustive hyperparameter sensitivity analysis and a table of hyperparameters would strengthen claims.

- MI computation concern: State-space discretization for MI calculation appears ad-hoc; sensitivity to grid size should be discussed.

- SNN architecture ablation: Recommend systematic comparison of other integration methods beyond bilinear integration.

- Switching policy limitation: Fixed skill switch time (T) may be limiting; learning when to switch could help in complex environments.

- Skill quantification: Need more formal metrics for skill diversity (e.g., distances between learned policies) beyond qualitative visitation plots.

- Ant robot instability: Appendix notes instability with Ant; a brief main-text example of failure modes would improve the narrative.

- Questions for authors — MI discretization: Justification for chosen discretization level and its effect on performance?

- Questions for authors — end-to-end training: What are the main challenges and potential solutions for end-to-end high-/low-level policy training?

- Questions for authors — Gather task: Why does the MI bonus fail to consistently improve performance on Gather; task-specific reasons?

- Questions for authors — computational cost: How does training time scale with number of skills compared to training separate policies?
