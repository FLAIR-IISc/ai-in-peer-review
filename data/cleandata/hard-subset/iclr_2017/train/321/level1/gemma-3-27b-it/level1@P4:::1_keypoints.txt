- Proposal: hierarchical RL combining Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with an information-theoretic (MI) regularizer for pre-training reusable skills and a high-level policy for sparse-reward tasks.
- Recommendation: Reject.
- Primary reason: lack of strong empirical evidence—improvements are modest and sensitive to random seeds.
- Primary reason: insufficient clarity and detail in the experimental setup, hindering reproducibility and interpretation.
- Novelty: combination of SNNs, MI regularization, and hierarchical RL is novel but novelty alone is insufficient for acceptance.
- Technical soundness: conceptually reasonable (SNNs for multi-modal policies and MI for diversity), but some architectural choices lack justification.
- Key architectural concern: bilinear integration choice is not well motivated or explained.
- Empirical support: results on locomotion+maze and Gather tasks are unconvincing; gains are small and variance across runs is high.
- Empirical concern: performance depends heavily on pre-trained SNNs, which show high variability across seeds.
- MI regularizer concern: the MI bonus has inconsistent impact and may require careful tuning.
- Clarity: paper generally readable but mathematical formulations and experimental details need clearer, more concise exposition.
- Reproducibility: code provided but critical hyperparameters, random seeds, and experimental specifics are missing.
- Suggested ablations: isolate effects of SNNs, MI regularization, and bilinear integration (e.g., with/without MI, alternative integrations).
- Suggested hyperparameter analysis: systematically evaluate sensitivity to MI coefficient, learning rate, and architecture.
- Suggested statistics: report statistical significance tests to confirm that improvements aren’t due to chance.
- Suggested experimental detail: provide full hyperparameters, seeds, and exact training procedures for reproducibility.
- Suggested error analysis: analyze failure cases to identify limitations and improvement areas.
- Suggested visualizations: show learned skills and how latent variables map to behaviors to build intuition.
- Suggested justification: explain advantages of bilinear integration versus simpler methods.
- Question: how were pre-trained SNNs selected for downstream tasks and how is variability across SNNs addressed?
- Question: under what conditions does the MI bonus consistently improve performance and what are its drawbacks?
- Question: why choose bilinear integration over other methods and what are its concrete benefits?
- Question: what is the computational cost of this approach compared to other hierarchical RL methods?
- Question: how would this approach scale to more complex/realistic scenarios and what modifications are needed?
