- Proposes a two-stage HRL framework: unsupervised SNN skill pre-training with a proxy reward and MI regularizer, then a high-level policy that selects pre-trained skills for downstream tasks.
- Uses SNNs to learn multi-modal policies and MI bonus to encourage diverse skills.
- Shows significant improvements in exploration and sample efficiency on maze navigation and object collection tasks.
- Decision: Accept.
- Novelty/Impact: Integration of SNNs with MI regularization in a hierarchical setup is an innovative approach to sparse reward problems.
- Empirical Rigor: Thorough experiments demonstrate consistent outperformance of baselines across multiple environments.
- Problem Relevance: Well-motivated solution addressing sparse rewards and long horizons with minimal domain knowledge required.
- Technical Soundness: SNNs and the MI bonus are well-justified; the hierarchical architecture effectively leverages learned skills.
- Experimental Validation: Comprehensive pre-training and downstream experiments plus ablation studies isolate contributions (e.g., MI bonus, bilinear integration).
- Suggestion: Explore adaptive skill switching or end-to-end training to handle unstable agents like the Ant.
- Suggestion: Provide a more detailed hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (MI bonus, switch time) for reproducibility.
- Suggestion: Add direct comparisons to state-of-the-art HRL methods (e.g., Option-Critic) on performance and sample efficiency.
- Suggestion: Test scalability to more complex, dynamic, or multi-agent real-world tasks.
- Question: How sensitive is the framework to the choice of proxy reward during pre-training?
- Question: Have you considered recurrent architectures for the high-level policy to better utilize temporal information?
- Question: Can the framework be extended to handle dynamic environments that require adaptive skill switching?
