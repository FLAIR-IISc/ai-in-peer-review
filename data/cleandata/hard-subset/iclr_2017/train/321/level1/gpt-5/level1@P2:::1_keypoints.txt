- Contribution: Pretrain diverse reusable low-level skills with a proxy reward, then train a high-level Manager to select skills for sparse-reward, long-horizon RL tasks.  
- Modeling: Low-level policy is an SNN with discrete latent z and bilinear observation–z integration to enable multimodal, interpretable behaviors.  
- Diversity regularizer: Encourage skill diversity via an MI-based bonus between z and a chosen subset of state variables (e.g., CoM), implemented with discretized conditional-entropy counts.  
- High-level control and training: Manager commits to skills for T steps; TRPO used throughout; method is simple and code/videos are released.  
- Empirical claims: SNN+MI yields a wide span of interpretable skills, greatly expands exploration when used hierarchically, and outperforms strong baselines on sparse downstream tasks.  
- Ablations: Bilinear integration and (often) the MI term are important; ablations and variance across pretrains are reported.  
- Decision: Accept — clear problem, novel and pragmatic combination (SNN + MI + hierarchy), careful experiments, and released code.  
- Problem significance: Tackles exploration bottleneck in sparse, long-horizon continuous control by amortizing skill learning across tasks.  
- Positioning: Well-motivated combination of hierarchical RL and intrinsic motivation; SNN avoids per-skill training cost; related work is adequately discussed.  
- Experimental rigor: Multiple seeds, sensible baselines, qualitative and quantitative evidence; no theoretical sample-complexity analysis but transparent empirical methodology.  
- Sample-efficiency reporting: Recommend reporting total environment interactions including pretraining and comparisons to learning from scratch to show amortization.  
- Sensitivity analyses needed: Investigate effects of number of skills K, MI coefficient αH, and discretization granularity on diversity and performance.  
- State subset choice: Current MI uses handpicked CoM; discuss generalization to other domains and possibility of learning the subset C.  
- Manager details: Clarify Manager inputs, commitment time T, sensitivity to T, and consider adaptive/learned termination mechanisms.  
- Baselines/comparisons: Add or explain comparisons with option-critic/HiREPS and ensure intrinsic-motivation baselines have matched budgets.  
- MI estimation alternatives: Consider reporting other MI estimators (InfoNCE, NMI) and discuss estimation bias, stability, and training overhead.  
- Robustness to unstable robots: Ant shows instability; suggest safety/transition policies or skill-switching strategies and report failure/success rates.  
- Outstanding empirical questions: How many downstream tasks are needed to amortize pretraining cost; how does varying K affect unused skills or mode collapse?  
- Learned representation for MI: Can MI be computed on learned features (end-to-end) rather than handpicked coordinates and still yield interpretable skills?  
- Conditioning variants: How sensitive are results to bilinear integration versus other multiplicative forms (FiLM, low-rank bilinear)?  
- Fine-tuning frozen skills: If low-level skills are frozen, do tasks needing novel skills suffer, and how effective is joint fine-tuning with the Manager?  
- Coverage during pretraining: For mazes, consider adding a diversity/coverage metric to ensure canonical directions (e.g., backward gait) and reduce seed variance.
