- Proposes a two-phase HRL framework: pretrain diverse low-level skills with a proxy reward, then train a high-level Manager to sequence discrete skills every T steps.
- Uses Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with a discrete latent z to induce multimodal skills and share weights across skills.
- Introduces bilinear integration of z with observations to increase coupling between latent code and state.
- Encourages diversity via an MI bonus I(Z; C) computed on a selected state subset (e.g., CoM) using discretized visitation counts.
- Freezes the pretrained SNN and trains a categorical Manager (TRPO) to select skills temporally for downstream sparse-reward tasks.
- Empirically shows bilinear SNN + MI yields interpretable, distinct locomotion skills.
- Hierarchical control over skills markedly improves exploration in sparse, long-horizon tasks.
- Achieves strong performance on Swimmer/Snake Maze and Gather tasks compared to proxy-reward baselines and multi-policy pretraining.
- Ablations validate bilinear > concatenation, effectiveness of MI coefficient, impact of switching period T, and comparisons to multi-policy baselines.
- Decision: weak accept.
- Key reason 1: The SNN + MI combination is simple, well-motivated, and empirically effective for skill discovery.
- Key reason 2: The hierarchical setup demonstrably improves exploration and downstream learning with informative ablations.
- Targets a central RL issue—exploration in sparse-reward, long-horizon problems—and reduces domain knowledge vs goal-conditioned pretraining.
- Positioned between HRL, intrinsic motivation, and multimodal policy modeling; distinct from Option-Critic and goal-set pretraining approaches.
- Treating z as part of observation keeps TRPO tractable; MI approximated via on-policy discretized CoM counts is simple and stable.
- Sampling z per episode promotes temporal coherence of skills; Manager committing for T steps is justified for credit assignment.
- Ablations convincingly show improved diversity, exploration coverage, and downstream learning; transfer to Snake and sensitivity analyses add credibility.
- Limitation: evaluations confined to MuJoCo locomotion-like tasks, limiting generality.
- Limitation: MI bonus depends on privileged/handpicked state subsets (e.g., CoM xy) and discretization choices.
- Limitation: Missing or partial baselines (e.g., Option-Critic, macro-action methods); sample-accounting across pretrain+downstream could be clearer.
- Limitation: Freezing low-level skills and fixed T reduces adaptivity; end-to-end or learned termination left as future work.
- Additional suggestion: Include comparisons to Option-Critic, end-to-end macro-actions, and K separate policies with diversity regularizers.
- Additional suggestion: Report total sample budgets (pretrain + downstream) and amortized gains across multiple downstream tasks.
- Additional suggestion: Analyze sensitivity to grid resolution, MI weight α_H, and choice of C; consider learned density or discriminator-based MI estimators.
- Additional suggestion: Test robustness to randomized orientations/start positions and transfer to different downstream tasks (e.g., manipulation).
- Additional suggestion: Explore learned termination, recurrent Managers, and end-to-end fine-tuning to improve adaptivity and stability (notably for Ant).
- Additional suggestion: Quantify achieved MI and inter-skill diversity metrics (occupancy overlap, KLs); study effect of K on diversity and stability.
- Questions: How sensitive are results to choice of C and discretization; any experiments using a classifier-based p(z|c) or I(Z;C) curves?
- Questions: What is exact pretraining cost in env steps versus training a unimodal policy; how does cost amortize across tasks?
- Questions: How were K and α_H selected; what happens when K exceeds the natural number of skills?
- Questions: Do learned skills encode world-frame or body-frame directions under randomized initial headings?
- Questions: Were learned termination/variable T or recurrent Managers tried; signs of over-commitment or oscillatory switching?
- Questions: For manipulation or vision-only settings, which state subsets would be targeted for MI and have experiments been tried?
- Questions: For Ant, did skill-specific warm starts, gradual mixing, or joint fine-tuning reduce instability at skill switches?
