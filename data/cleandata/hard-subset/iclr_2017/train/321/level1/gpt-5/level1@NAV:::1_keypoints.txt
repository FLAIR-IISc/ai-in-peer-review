- Core idea: pretrain diverse reusable skills with Stochastic Neural Networks (categorical latent fixed per rollout) plus a mutual-information (MI) bonus on a low-dim state subset, then compose frozen skills via a high-level manager for downstream sparse, long-horizon RL tasks.
- Architectural trick: bilinear integration of latent code and observation yields better mode separation than simple concatenation.
- Skill selection: skills are frozen after pretraining and chosen by a manager policy at fixed period T.
- Empirical gains: learned skills are interpretable, substantially improve exploration, and boost performance on sparse maze and Gather tasks with Swimmer and Snake.
- Ablations: analyses of latent integration, MI strength, and switch time T are informative and support design choices.
- Reproducibility: code and videos are provided.
- Decision: Accept.
- Main reasons for accept: simple, general, well-motivated framework unifying intrinsic motivation and hierarchy with a novel SNN+MI skill learning mechanism.
- Main reasons for accept (empirical): strong empirical evidence that skills enhance exploration and downstream performance, with clear ablations.
- Significance: addresses a high-impact problem where prior HRL/intrinsic methods require task-specific goals or hand-designed options; single proxy reward + MI is minimally domain-dependent.
- Positioning: paper situates itself well relative to options, option-critic, intrinsic motivation, and InfoGAN; MI on a task-agnostic state subset is a neat practical adaptation.
- Empirical details: visitation plots show distinct modes; manager-driven exploration covers space far better than Gaussian noise; downstream curves beat strong baselines including intrinsic CoM reward.
- Robustness/scalability: method is robust to switch-time T for static tasks and scales to Snake; limitations like Ant instability and seed variance are acknowledged.
- Requested stronger baselines: compare to option-critic, hierarchical goal-conditioned policies, FeUdal/HIRO-style planners, and VIME with identical horizons.
- Requested measurements: report pretraining vs downstream sample complexity (wall-clock/interaction counts) and quantify transfer efficiency across tasks.
- Sensitivity analyses requested: vary K (num skills), MI weight αH, and discretization for p(Z|c); consider continuous density models for p(Z|C).
- Extensions suggested: learn termination/joint fine-tuning of skills (e.g., straight-through estimators) to potentially mitigate Ant failures.
- Ablation on proxy reward: evaluate different proxy rewards and fully unsupervised skill discovery (no speed bonus).
- MI target richness and inputs: explore richer state subsets (e.g., orientation) and results with visual inputs.
- Clarification requested: whether the manager uses a subset (Sagent) vs full state and how that affects transferability.
