- Paper goal: address sparse-reward, long-horizon RL by pre-training diverse reusable skills in a proxy environment, then learning a high-level manager to select skills.
- Representation: uses stochastic neural networks (SNNs) with discrete latent codes and bilinear integration of observation and latent to represent multimodal, weight-shared skill policies.
- Diversity objective: encourages skill diversity via an MI bonus between latent codes and a chosen subset of state variables, estimated on-policy by discretized posterior counts.
- Empirical claim: SNN pre-training yields interpretable, temporally coherent skills that substantially improve exploration and downstream learning on sparse locomotion (mazes, gather) tasks; code/videos released.
- Ablations/reporting: provides ablations on integration method and MI strength, reports results across multiple seeds and agents (Swimmer, Snake), and discusses Ant failure modes.
- Decision: Accept — framework is well-motivated and simple, with a novel SNN+MI approach and strong empirical support.
- Strength: bridges hierarchical RL and intrinsic motivation via task-agnostic proxy rewards to reduce downstream sample complexity.
- Clarity: method is conceptually clean—pre-train latent-conditioned SNN with MI, freeze skills, train manager; contrasts appropriately with goal-conditioned pre-training.
- Technical justification: treating latent as input avoids TRPO mixture issues; bilinear integration increases expressivity; sampling one latent per rollout yields temporal coherence; MI estimation via discretization is simple and effective.
- Skill diversity evidence: visitation plots show distinct locomotion modes; MI and bilinear integration reduce mode overlap and increase coverage.
- Exploration evidence: manager rollouts with skills cover much larger areas than Gaussian noise or collections of independently trained policies.
- Downstream performance evidence: hierarchical policies over SNN skills outperform strong baselines (including proxy-reward augmentation) and VIME on benchmark sparse tasks.
- Ablation findings: bilinear integration outperforms concatenation; MI regularizer especially helps behaviors needing turning/sideways motion.
- Limitations: fixed skill-switch period T, frozen skills, high variance across pre-training seeds on some tasks, and instability on Ant when switching skills.
- Suggestion — quantify diversity: report explicit diversity metrics (achieved MI, pairwise trajectory divergence, coverage area) and correlate with downstream results.
- Suggestion — sample accounting: provide end-to-end sample counts (pre-training + downstream) and compare to from-scratch and multi-policy baselines.
- Suggestion — sensitivity analyses: expand ablations on number of skills K and MI coefficient; discuss K selection and redundancy/collapse when K is large.
- Suggestion — MI estimation: consider variational lower bound with learned discriminator/posterior for higher-dimensional C and discuss robustness to choice of state subset.
- Suggestion — stronger baselines: compare to option-critic, feudal architectures, or goal-conditioned policies (e.g., with hindsight/UVFA) where feasible.
- Suggestion — transition robustness: for unstable agents (Ant), explore skill blending/smoothing, warm-up phases, transition policies, or learned termination to reduce falls.
- Suggestion — manager improvements: investigate recurrent managers and adaptive termination to reduce variance and improve responsiveness.
- Suggestion — generality: include a small manipulation pre-training example with a different proxy reward and C to demonstrate domain-general claims.
- Question — sensitivity to C: how sensitive is performance to choice of state subset C; guidance for domains without a natural CoM and experiments with full-state MI or learned posterior?
- Question — choosing K: how is K selected, what happens when K is overestimated (unused skills, manager exploration dilution)?
- Question — sample/time costs: report total sample counts and wall-clock time relative to training K separate policies and from-scratch baselines.
- Question — manager regularization: did you try entropy regularization or usage priors to avoid premature collapse to a few skills?
- Question — switching period T: how does performance change with much shorter switching periods T on Swimmer (you provided Snake analysis; Swimmer results would help)?
- Question — Ant stability fixes: did you try interpolation between latents at switch time or conditioning skills on previous skill to improve stability?
- Question — unimodality and alternatives: are per-z policies unimodal in practice, and how would a mixture-of-experts with learned gating trained end-to-end compare?
- Question — fine-tuning skills: could skills be fine-tuned with a small KL constraint during downstream learning to maintain diversity and reduce variance across seeds?
