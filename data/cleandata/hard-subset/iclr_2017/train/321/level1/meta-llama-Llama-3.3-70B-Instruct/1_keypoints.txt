- Proposes a framework that pre-trains diverse skills in a proxy environment and uses them to speed learning on downstream sparse-reward/long-horizon tasks.
- Skills are learned with Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) and an information-theoretic regularizer to encourage behavioral diversity.
- Pre-trained skills are composed by a high-level policy for downstream tasks, improving exploration and learning speed.
- Demonstrated effectiveness on maze navigation and object-gathering tasks, outperforming existing methods.
- Novelty lies in combining SNNs with an information-theoretic regularizer for diverse skill discovery.
- Decision: Accept — tackles a significant RL problem and builds on hierarchical RL and intrinsic motivation.
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly presented, and includes ablation studies and comparisons to baselines.
- Experimental analysis is thorough, showing empirical benefits of the approach.
- Suggestion: Provide more details on hyperparameter tuning for the SNN and regularizer.
- Suggestion: Evaluate on additional tasks with more complex dynamics or higher-dimensional state/action spaces.
- Suggestion: Analyze failure modes (e.g., unstable robots like Ant) and propose mitigations.
- Question: Provide details on the design of the proxy reward signal and tailoring to downstream tasks.
- Question: How is the number of skills chosen in pre-training, and what is its impact on downstream performance?
- Question: Discuss extensions to more complex settings, such as multi-goal tasks or multi-agent cooperation.
