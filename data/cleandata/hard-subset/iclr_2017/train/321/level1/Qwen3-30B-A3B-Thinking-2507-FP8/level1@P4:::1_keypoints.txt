- Proposes a two-phase hierarchical RL framework: pretrain diverse skills with a simple proxy reward, then leverage them for faster downstream learning.  
- Introduces Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with bilinear integration and a Mutual Information (MI) regularizer to encourage skill diversity.  
- Demonstrates empirically on Swimmer, Snake, and Ant tasks that the method learns a wider span of interpretable skills.  
- Shows improved exploration and faster learning on downstream sparse-reward tasks compared to baselines.  
- Decision: Accept — paper makes a compelling contribution with rigorous empirical evaluation.  
- Addresses the important problem of sparse rewards and long horizons, positioning between hand-engineered hierarchies and intrinsic motivation methods.  
- Thorough literature review and clear comparison to relevant prior work in hierarchical RL and skill learning.  
- Empirical rigor: multiple environments and tasks, 10 random seeds per curve, and detailed ablation studies.  
- Ablations show bilinear integration and tuning the MI bonus (αH) provide consistent improvements over alternatives.  
- Results include visitation plots and comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., VIME, independently trained skills).  
- Recommend more analysis of computational cost and how the MI posterior estimation scales with number of skills K and high-dimensional states.  
- Note that pretraining with the MI bonus does not always help; paper should clarify when the bonus is most beneficial.  
- Suggest providing more theoretical grounding for the MI bonus’s link to skill diversity.  
- Question: How can the framework handle unstable agents (Ant) and costly skill switching more robustly?  
- Question: How to adapt the fixed switch time T when optimal switch times vary across downstream tasks, and what are the learning implications?
