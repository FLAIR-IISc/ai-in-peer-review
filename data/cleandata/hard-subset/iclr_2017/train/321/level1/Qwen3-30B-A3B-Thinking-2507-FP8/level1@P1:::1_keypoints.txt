- Introduces a hierarchical RL framework that pre-trains diverse skills with a single proxy reward then uses them for downstream tasks.  
- Key technical contributions: SNNs for multi-modal policies, mutual-information regularizer for skill diversity, and a hierarchical policy architecture.  
- Demonstrates interpretable skills (e.g., directional locomotion) and improved exploration/learning in sparse reward tasks.  
- Empirical results show superior performance over baselines across multiple benchmarks and robot morphologies.  
- Paper is well-motivated and positioned between hierarchical RL and intrinsic motivation approaches.  
- Good experimental design with multiple environments (mazes, gather) and robots (Swimmer, Snake, Ant) supporting generality.  
- MI regularizer improves skill diversity, but theoretical justification for it is limited.  
- Decision: Accept — significant, sample-efficient, transferable skill learning with minimal supervision.  
- Comparison to related work (e.g., Heess et al. 2016) is clear and relevant.  
- Suggest clarifying theoretical basis for the MI regularization and its relation to exploration efficiency.  
- Recommend adding statistical significance (error bars, confidence intervals, p-values) to reported results.  
- Request sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters, especially the MI coefficient αH.  
- Ask for expanded discussion of limitations, notably failures with unstable robots like Ant.  
- Ask for clearer evidence or direct comparison to justify the "sample-efficient" claim versus training separate policies.  
- Question: Clarify actual sample complexity when learning K skills — does it scale with K or match single-policy training?  
- Question: Explain when the MI bonus helps or hurts — which task characteristics influence its effectiveness?  
- Question: Discuss sensitivity to fixed switch time T in the hierarchical architecture and how to choose T for new tasks.  
- Question: Provide a detailed comparison of SNNs’ representational capacity versus other multi-modal policy methods.  
- Question: Propose modifications to handle unstable robots (Ant), and discuss their computational cost and trade-offs.
