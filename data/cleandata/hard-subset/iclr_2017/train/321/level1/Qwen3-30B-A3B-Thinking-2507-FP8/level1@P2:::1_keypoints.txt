- Proposes a hierarchical RL framework that pre-trains diverse, interpretable skills using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with an information-theoretic regularizer to address sparse rewards and long horizons.  
- Pre-training requires only a simple proxy reward (minimal domain knowledge) and learned skills are reused to improve downstream exploration and learning.  
- Empirically demonstrates wide-ranging, interpretable skills and strong performance gains on Maze navigation and Gather tasks, outperforming baselines.  
- Uses mutual information regularizer to encourage diverse skill learning and employs bilinear vs. concatenation ablation, MI bonus studies, visitation visualizations, and multiple tasks for rigorous evaluation.  
- Acknowledges limitations (e.g., instability with the Ant robot) and outlines thoughtful future work.  
- Decision: accept with minor revisions.  
- Strength: well-motivated problem framing that bridges hierarchical RL and intrinsic motivation, reducing required domain-specific skill design.  
- Strength: novel and justified use of SNNs with MI regularization to ensure skill diversity.  
- Strength: strong empirical rigor with ablations, visualizations, and cross-task evaluations showing reduced sample complexity.  
- Suggestion: include a formal theoretical justification for why the MI regularizer yields diverse skills.  
- Suggestion: clarify what "minimal domain knowledge" concretely means across task types (navigation vs. manipulation).  
- Suggestion: analyze potential benefits and challenges of end-to-end joint optimization of pre-training and downstream training.  
- Suggestion: explicitly state whether and how the skill switch time T should be tuned per task.  
- Question: give concrete examples of the proxy reward/domain knowledge required across domains and compare to prior approaches.  
- Question: explain why the MI bonus improves pre-training diversity but not always downstream performance, and what factors influence this.  
- Question: elaborate on failure modes with the Ant robot and what modifications would be needed to handle unstable agents.
