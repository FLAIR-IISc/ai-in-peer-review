- Proposes pre-training diverse skills with a simple proxy reward, then using them for downstream hierarchical RL tasks.
- Core technical innovation: Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with a mutual-information (MI) regularizer to learn diverse, interpretable skills.
- Shows improved exploration and better performance on sparse-reward Maze and Gather tasks versus baselines.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
- Strength: well-motivated framing that bridges hierarchical RL and intrinsic motivation approaches.
- Strength: sound methodology with analysis of architectural choices (concatenation vs. bilinear integration).
- Strength: MI regularizer effectively encourages skill diversity; empirical ablations demonstrate design impacts.
- Empirical evaluation: thorough ablation studies and strong comparisons, including against using proxy reward directly in downstream tasks.
- Related work: well-positioned and a strong comparison to Heess et al. (2016), emphasizing single proxy reward benefit.
- Revision request: strengthen theoretical justification linking MI maximization to skill diversity and downstream gains.
- Revision request: expand evaluation beyond two environments to support claims of broad improvement.
- Revision request: discuss limitations more thoroughly, especially instability observed with the Ant robot and possible fixes.
- Revision request: clarify computational cost and scalability of the MI regularizer beyond the 2D CoM grid.
- Question: how would MI computation scale to higher-dimensional relevant state spaces (e.g., 3D navigation)?
- Question: provide analysis on when the MI regularizer helps versus when it does not and the determining factors.
- Question: clarify sample-efficiency comparison with the Multi-policy baseline that trains multiple separate policies.
- Question: given unsupervised pre-training, how can one ensure learned skills will be useful for specific downstream tasks?
