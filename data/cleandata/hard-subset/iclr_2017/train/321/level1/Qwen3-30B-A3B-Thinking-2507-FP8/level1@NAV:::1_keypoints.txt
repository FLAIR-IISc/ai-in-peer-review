- Proposes a novel hierarchical RL framework that tackles sparse rewards and long horizons by pre-learning skills with SNNs and an information-theoretic regularizer.  
- Learns a diverse, interpretable span of skills in a pre-training environment using a single proxy reward, enabling transfer to downstream tasks without task-specific reward engineering.  
- Key contribution: general, low domain-knowledge approach for skill acquisition that transfers across multiple tasks.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Clearly identifies the gap between hierarchical methods (need domain knowledge) and intrinsic motivation approaches (poor transferability) and offers a well-motivated solution.  
- Empirical results are strong and scientifically rigorous, outperforming baselines on Swimmer, Snake, and Ant in sparse-reward mazes and object-gathering tasks.  
- Ablation studies show bilinear integration and the mutual information (MI) regularizer are important to performance.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations, e.g., instability with the Ant robot.  
- Strengths: sample efficiency (concurrent skill learning via SNNs), interpretability (latent codes map to distinct behaviors), and minimal-supervision pre-training suited for real-world use.  
- Suggested improvement: clarify relationship between their MI regularizer and prior work (e.g., InfoGAN) in related work.  
- Suggested improvement: provide more detail on how the proxy reward is designed for different domains in the main text rather than only the appendix.  
- Questions for authors: How to adapt the framework to continuous task distributions where optimal skills vary smoothly?  
- Questions for authors: What theoretical guarantees exist regarding the diversity of skills learned using the MI regularizer?
