- Proposes a two-stage HRL framework: task-agnostic pre-training of low-level skills followed by a downstream high-level policy that composes these skills.
- Uses a single Stochastic Neural Network (SNN) conditioned on a discrete latent z to learn multiple skills with weight sharing and improved sample efficiency.
- Introduces an information-theoretic regularizer (mutual information bonus / penalty on H(Z|C)) to encourage distinct, state-predictable skills.
- Implements the MI objective by estimating p(z|c) from batch statistics and conditioning policies on z as part of the observation for straightforward TRPO usage.
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on continuous control benchmarks (maze locomotion and gathering), showing interpretable skills and better exploration.
- Shows faster learning on sparse-reward tasks and superior sample efficiency compared to non-hierarchical and multi-policy baselines.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason 1: Novel and effective skill discovery via SNNs plus mutual-information-based diversity objective.
- Key reason 2: Compelling, rigorously designed experiments with qualitative visualizations, quantitative gains, and multiple seeds.
- Technical support: bilinear latent integration, InfoGAN-inspired MI objective, and simple practical posterior estimation are well-justified.
- Experimental evidence: figures show that bilinear+MI yields diverse skills, improved exploration coverage, and better downstream learning; ablations isolate contributions.
- Additional feedback: current MI estimator uses a discretized 2D grid and may not scale to higher-dimensional C; suggest discussing MLP posterior alternatives and their challenges.
- Additional feedback: sensitivity to fixed switch time T needs analysis; learning termination is future work and T can affect performance.
- Additional feedback: expand discussion on how to design general proxy rewards beyond locomotion (e.g., manipulation) and desired proxy properties.
- Additional feedback: acknowledge failure modes (e.g., Ant instability when switching dynamic skills) and discuss safe transitions between skills.
- Question: Have you tried including other state variables (orientation, velocity) in C and how would that change learned skills?
- Question: Does the manager observe the previous skill z_{t-1} when selecting the next skill, and would that aid planning?
- Question: How does this MI-based bonus conceptually differ from VIME; are they complementary and could VIME-style bonuses serve as proxy rewards in pre-training?
