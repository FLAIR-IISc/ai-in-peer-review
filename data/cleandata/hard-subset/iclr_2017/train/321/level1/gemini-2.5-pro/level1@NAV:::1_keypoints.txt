- Proposes a two-stage hierarchical RL framework for sparse-reward, long-horizon tasks: pre-train low-level skills, then train a high-level policy over them.  
- Pre-training uses a single, generic proxy reward requiring minimal domain knowledge to learn diverse skills.  
- Uses a Stochastic Neural Network (SNN) policy augmented with a novel mutual-information regularizer to encourage behavioral diversity.  
- Low-level skills are frozen and a high-level manager is trained on top to simplify exploration and speed downstream learning.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main strengths: novel, well-motivated unsupervised skill discovery method with strong empirical support.  
- Key technical merit: elegant combination of SNNs and information-theoretic bonus yields sample-efficient, interpretable, multi-modal skills without hand-crafted rewards.  
- Empirical evidence: visitation plots show diverse skills; benchmarks (maze, collection) show large gains over baselines.  
- Evaluation robustness: tested across multiple environments, seeds, and robot morphologies.  
- Limitation: fixed skill execution time T may be restrictive; consider discussing learned termination (options-like) in main text.  
- Limitation: unstable failure modes on the "Ant" robot should be briefly mentioned in the main discussion.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity of learned skills to proxy reward design and what makes a “good” proxy reward.  
- Questions for authors: effect of state-space discretization/grid resolution for the MI bonus on skill diversity.  
- Questions for authors: any experiments fine-tuning the low-level SNN jointly with the high-level manager versus keeping skills frozen?
