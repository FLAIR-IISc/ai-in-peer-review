- Proposes a framework that pre-trains a span of skills in a proxy environment using minimal domain knowledge and sparse rewards.  
- Learns skills via Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with an information-theoretic regularizer.  
- Uses pre-trained skills as building blocks and trains a high-level policy to solve downstream sparse-reward tasks.  
- Claims sample-efficient learning of a wide span of interpretable skills and consistent downstream performance gains.  
- Decision: accept with minor revisions.  
- Reason 1: Novel, well-motivated hierarchical approach combining intrinsic motivation and hierarchical RL.  
- Reason 2: Strong empirical evidence showing learned skill diversity and improved downstream learning.  
- Framework motivated by challenges of sparse rewards and long horizons.  
- Paper gives clear explanations of pre-training, SNN architecture, and high-level policy.  
- Empirical evaluation includes ablations and baseline comparisons that convincingly support claims.  
- Request for more implementation details on the SNN architecture and information-theoretic regularizer.  
- Suggestion for more theoretical analysis (e.g., convergence properties) and hyperparameter justification (number of skills, switch time T).  
- Questions about extending to more complex environments, handling unstable-agent skill switching, and computational resource requirements.
