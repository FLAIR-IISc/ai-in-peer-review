**Summary of the paper's contributions**

This paper proposes a general framework for hierarchical reinforcement learning that tackles the challenges of tasks with sparse rewards or long horizons. The framework first learns a set of useful skills in a pre-training environment using a proxy reward signal that requires minimal domain knowledge. These skills are then used as building blocks for solving downstream tasks with sparse rewards. The authors use Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with an information-theoretic regularizer to efficiently pre-train a large span of skills. The experiments demonstrate that this combination is effective in learning a wide range of interpretable skills and can significantly boost the learning performance uniformly across a wide range of downstream tasks.

**Decision: Accept**

I recommend accepting this paper because it addresses a significant challenge in reinforcement learning, namely, tasks with sparse rewards or long horizons. The proposed framework is well-motivated, and the use of SNNs with an information-theoretic regularizer is a novel and effective approach to learning a diverse set of skills. The experiments are thorough, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed framework.
