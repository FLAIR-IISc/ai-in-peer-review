**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

This paper proposes a general framework for hierarchical reinforcement learning (HRL) that learns useful skills in a pre-training environment and leverages these skills for faster learning in downstream tasks. The authors introduce a stochastic neural network (SNN) architecture with an information-theoretic regularizer to learn a diverse set of skills. The pre-trained skills are then used as basic building blocks for solving tasks with sparse rewards. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on several challenging tasks, including mazes and gathering tasks.
