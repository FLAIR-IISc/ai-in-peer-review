- Proposed framework: hierarchical RL that pre-trains skills with Stochastic Neural Networks and an information-theoretic regularizer to address sparse rewards and long horizons.  
- Main contributions: a general method to learn a diverse span of skills from a single proxy reward (minimal domain knowledge) and a hierarchical architecture that speeds downstream learning.  
- Method detail: SNNs with bilinear integration plus mutual information regularization produce diverse, interpretable skills and improve exploration.  
- Empirical results: consistent improvements on multiple maze and gather tasks versus baselines, demonstrating efficacy with minimal domain knowledge.  
- Ablation studies: analyses of integration methods and the MI bonus provide useful design insights.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; solid, well-motivated contribution.  
- Reproducibility concern: MI bonus implementation needs more detail, particularly how state-space discretization affects outcomes.  
- Unexplained behavior: unclear why the MI bonus does not consistently improve performance across all tasks (Section 7.3 requires clearer explanation).  
- Limitation: approach has acknowledged failures on unstable robots (e.g., Ant); paper should more concretely discuss failure cases and practical limits.  
- Suggested addition: explicitly compare computational efficiency versus training multiple separate policies (beyond sample complexity discussion).  
- Suggested addition: more detailed analysis of trade-offs between concatenation and bilinear integration to guide method selection.  
- Open question: how sensitive is performance to the choice of proxy reward in pre-training, and what precisely counts as “minimal domain knowledge” for different tasks?
