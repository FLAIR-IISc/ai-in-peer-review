- Proposes a novel hierarchical RL framework with a two-phase approach: pre-train diverse skills using SNNs with an information-theoretic regularizer, then leverage those skills for downstream tasks.  
- Pre-training uses a single proxy reward that requires minimal domain knowledge, enabling broad applicability.  
- SNN architecture with mutual information regularization produces diverse, interpretable skills.  
- Demonstrates significant improvements over baselines across challenging tasks (mazes, object gathering).  
- Approach effectively combines intrinsic motivation and hierarchical RL, reducing sample complexity in sparse reward environments.  
- Ablation studies (concatenation vs. bilinear integration, with/without MI) show design choices impact skill diversity and performance.  
- Experiments are comprehensive, include multiple random seeds, and analyze variance across pre-trained SNNs.  
- Clear evidence that pre-trained skills transfer to downstream tasks and speed up learning relative to baselines.  
- Emphasizes interpretability where latent codes map to distinct, meaningful skills—an advantage over prior HRL methods.  
- Recommendation: paper should be accepted; the approach is well-motivated and experimentally supported.  
- Suggests stronger theoretical analysis of the mutual information regularizer to justify its effectiveness.  
- Recommends more systematic exploration of how different proxy rewards and pre-training environments affect skill diversity and transfer.  
- Calls for clearer analysis of the relationship between switch time T and task complexity, beyond the limited Appendix C.3 treatment.  
- Advises deeper investigation of failure modes with unstable agents (e.g., Ant), including why skill switching is challenging and potential mitigations.
