- Decision: Reject.

- Main reason: Fails to adequately substantiate central claims of generality and efficiency; experimental validation is not rigorous enough to support claimed improvements.

- Proxy reward critique: The paper's “minimal domain knowledge” claim is unsubstantiated — the proxy rewards used (e.g., swimmer speed) are domain-specific and not shown to be truly domain-agnostic.

- Baselines/comparisons: Lacks comparisons to approaches using less domain-specific or more intuitive proxy rewards, so claims about minimal domain knowledge are unsupported.

- Statistical rigor: Results omit statistical significance testing and do not address high variance across seeds (notably in Appendix C.4).

- Variance analysis: The paper does not explain why some pre-trained SNNs succeed while others fail, leaving uncertainty about consistency of improvements.

- Sample-efficiency claim: The comparison of sample efficiency is incomplete — the paper compares a single SNN to multiple independent policies per-skill without assessing total sample complexity fairly.

- Limitations handling: Important limitations (e.g., failures on unstable agents like Ant) are relegated to the appendix and not integrated into the main discussion, masking critical weaknesses.

- Suggested fixes — clarity: Define “minimal domain knowledge” concretely and provide truly domain-agnostic proxy reward examples across tasks.

- Suggested fixes — statistics: Address high variance, explain failure modes of pre-trained SNNs, and include statistical significance tests across multiple seeds.

- Suggested fixes — sample complexity: Provide a rigorous total-sample-complexity comparison between SNNs and multi-policy baselines.

- Suggested fixes — limitations & ablations: Move key limitations into the main paper and add ablation studies to isolate contributions of SNN architecture, bilinear integration, and MI regularization.

- Questions for authors: Provide a concrete proxy reward example requiring no domain knowledge and explain cross-domain applicability.

- Questions for authors: How does the method scale when proxy rewards are unclear or multiple proxy rewards are needed?

- Questions for authors: Given high variance, how should one select a pre-trained SNN for downstream tasks and what performance variability should be expected?

- Questions for authors: Why does MI regularization sometimes fail to improve performance, and under what conditions is it effective?
