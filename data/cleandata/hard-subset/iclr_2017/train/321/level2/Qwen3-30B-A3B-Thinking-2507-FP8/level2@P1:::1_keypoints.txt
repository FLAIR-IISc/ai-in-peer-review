- Proposes SNNs with an information-theoretic regularizer to pretrain diverse, interpretable skills for faster learning on sparse-reward downstream tasks.
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
- Strong empirical results showing consistent improvements across multiple challenging environments.
- Good ablation studies that clarify the impact of different architectural choices.
- Clear and well-motivated problem statement addressing sparse rewards in RL.
- Novel combination of stochastic neural networks and a mutual-information bonus to encourage skill diversity.
- Suggests providing stronger theoretical justification for the mutual information bonus beyond empirical evidence.
- Suggests expanding discussion of limitations, especially stability issues with more complex robots noted in Appendix D.
- Requests clearer positioning relative to prior skill-discovery methods (e.g., HiREPS, Option-critic) concerning cross-task skill reuse.
- Notes some figures (especially visitation plots in Figure 4) need clearer labeling to better illustrate learned skill diversity.
- Asks for concrete examples showing how skills differ with vs. without the MI bonus (e.g., new advancing/turning skills).
- Asks why the MI bonus sometimes fails to boost performance in certain mazes and whether its utility can be predicted.
