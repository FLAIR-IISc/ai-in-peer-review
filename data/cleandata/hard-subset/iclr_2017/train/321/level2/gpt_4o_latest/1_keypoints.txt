- Decision: Accept.

- Novel two-stage framework: pre-train diverse skills with Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) + mutual information regularizer, then use them in a hierarchical policy for downstream tasks.

- Combines intrinsic motivation and hierarchical RL to enable efficient exploration and skill reuse.

- Uses a single proxy reward for skill pre-training with minimal domain knowledge, which is innovative.

- SNNs with bilinear integration and MI regularization produce diverse, interpretable, multi-modal skills.

- Hierarchical policy structure reduces sample complexity by reusing pre-trained skills.

- Empirical results show improved sample efficiency and task performance in sparse-reward environments (mazes, object gathering), outperforming baselines.

- Experiments are thorough across multiple tasks and robot morphologies and include ablation studies validating MI bonus and bilinear integration.

- Suggested clarity improvement: simplify Section 7.1 and visitation plot descriptions to focus on key insights.

- Limitation: fixed sub-policies and switching times—issues for unstable agents (e.g., Ant) that need elaboration or solutions.

- Recommendation to explore end-to-end training or adaptive switching policies to address instability and switching limitations.

- Generalization concern: applicability to dynamic or real-world tasks and rapid skill switching / non-stationary environments remains unclear.

- Question: How sensitive is performance to the choice of proxy reward during pre-training?

- Question: Have recurrent architectures for the Manager Network been considered to enable memory-based skill selection?

- Question: Would incorporating a recovery mechanism during pre-training improve robustness for unstable agents like Ant?
