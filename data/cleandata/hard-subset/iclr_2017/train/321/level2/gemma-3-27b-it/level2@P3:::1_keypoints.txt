- Proposes an HRL framework using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) plus an information-theoretic regularizer to learn diverse skills for transfer to sparse-reward tasks.  
- Pre-training diverse skills with minimal domain knowledge and reusing them speeds up learning in downstream sparse-reward tasks.  
- Experimental results show significant performance improvements over existing methods, especially in sparse reward settings.  
- Decision: accept with minor revisions.  
- Strength: well-motivated, technically sound approach addressing sparse rewards and long horizons.  
- Strength: combination of SNNs and mutual information regularizer yields interpretable, reusable skills.  
- Writing and presentation are generally clear.  
- Clarify why bilinear integration in the SNN outperforms simple concatenation (e.g., expressiveness, gradient flow).  
- Provide more sensitivity analysis and guidance for tuning the MI regularization coefficient (αH) across environments.  
- Explain how the discrete action space from SNN latent variables is handled within the TRPO training framework.  
- Expand ablation studies to compare: SNN without MI regularization and standard HRL without SNNs to isolate component contributions.  
- Provide deeper exploration of failure modes (e.g., Ant robot) and potential remedies like recovery mechanisms.  
- Specific reviewer questions: computational cost of bilinear vs concatenation and scalability; strategies for automatic tuning of αH; implementation details of discrete latents in TRPO; limitations for tasks needing precise timing or rapid responses.
