- Proposes an HRL framework that pre-trains diverse skills with Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) and a mutual information (MI) regularizer, then uses a high-level policy to solve sparse-reward tasks.  
- Recommendation: accept — the approach is novel, well-motivated, and addresses exploration in sparse-reward RL.  
- Empirical results show clear improvement over baselines, especially on complex locomotion and maze tasks.  
- Use of SNNs + MI yields richer, more interpretable skills than traditional methods.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and includes thorough experiments and ablation studies.  
- Extensive supplementary material provides helpful experimental details and additional results.  
- Missing discussion of computational cost and scalability of SNNs to higher-dimensional state/action spaces.  
- Ant experiments are presented as a failure case but lack detailed analysis of why the method struggles with unstable robots.  
- More investigation needed into the effect of switch time T on high- and low-level policy interaction.  
- Unclear how sensitive performance is to the choice of proxy reward during pre-training; a poorly chosen proxy could negate benefits.
