- Decision: Reject — interesting idea with promising initial results but insufficient experimental rigor and contribution strength for acceptance.

- Limited baselines and comparisons — weak benchmarking, insufficient or buried comparisons to intrinsic motivation methods (e.g., VIME) and simpler HRL/skill-discovery baselines.

- Comparison justification lacking — paper argues some baselines are hard to apply but does not thoroughly explain or justify excluding alternatives.

- Experimental setup & reproducibility concerns — hyperparameter choices not justified and claims of cross-task hyperparameter reuse are questionable.

- Sensitivity to pre-trained SNNs and random seeds — large performance variability noted without explanation or mitigation strategy.

- Insufficient analysis of MI regularization strength (αH) — impact of this key component not thoroughly analyzed.

- Lack of ablation studies — unclear which components (SNNs, bilinear integration, MI regularizer, hierarchy) drive performance.

- No direct comparison of bilinear integration vs simpler alternatives — unclear benefit over concatenation or other fusion methods.

- Unclear motivation for SNNs — rationale for preferring SNNs over alternatives (mixture-of-Gaussians, VAEs) is not well articulated.

- Proxy reward role unclear — “minimal domain knowledge” claim needs clarification and explicit assumptions about proxy reward design.

- MI regularization needs deeper explanation and visualization — theoretical underpinning and visual demos of learned skills/action distributions are missing.

- Instability on Ant is a significant limitation — discussion of causes and potential solutions (e.g., transition policy) should be expanded.

- Switching time T underexplored — analysis of T's influence and adaptive selection strategies are needed.

- Visualization and presentation quality poor — figures are hard to interpret and clearer visuals of skills, MI effects, and baselines are required.

- Requests for authors: provide stronger baseline comparisons, list key hyperparameters and sensitivity analyses, report seed variability and mitigation, quantify SNN computational cost, and justify bilinear integration and SNN choice vs alternatives.
