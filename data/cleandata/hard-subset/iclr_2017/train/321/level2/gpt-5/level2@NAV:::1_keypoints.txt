- Proposes an HRL framework that pretrains a single stochastic neural network (SNN) with categorical latent codes, bilinear observation–latent integration, and an MI regularizer using a proxy reward.  
- Uses a downstream “Manager” that selects frozen skills with a fixed commitment horizon to improve exploration and sample efficiency on sparse, long-horizon tasks.  
- Claims learning K distinct skills at roughly the cost of training one policy via weight sharing.  
- Claims MI-regularized SNNs reliably produce diverse, directionally distinct locomotion skills.  
- Claims the learned skills uniformly accelerate learning on several maze and gather tasks; code and implementation details are provided.  
- Decision: Accept (weak accept).  
- Key reason: Clear empirical gains on challenging sparse-reward benchmarks with careful ablations (concatenation vs bilinear, MI strength) and multiple seeds.  
- Key reason: Presents a simple, general, and reproducible method that coherently combines unsupervised skill discovery and HRL.  
- Positions the work well relative to HiREPS, Option-Critic, and goal-conditioned pretraining; MI bonus over a meaningful state subset (CoM) is sensible and easy to implement.  
- Provides evidence via visitation plots showing skill diversity, enhanced random exploration under the Manager, and improved learning curves across tasks; analyzes sensitivity to switch time T and acknowledges limitations on unstable agents (Ant).  
- Limitation: The “K skills at the cost of one” claim lacks direct wall-clock/sample accounting to substantiate it.  
- Limitation: Comparisons to stronger HRL baselines (e.g., option learning, feudal methods) are limited.  
- Feedback: Quantify sample efficiency by reporting total environment steps and wall-clock training time versus single-policy and multipolicy baselines.  
- Feedback: Provide direct comparisons to Option-Critic, FeUdal, and PPO-based options on the same tasks.  
- Feedback: Analyze sensitivity to K, αH, grid resolution for MI, and the choice of state subset C; offer guidance for proxy reward design.  
- Feedback: Consider adaptive commitment/termination and joint fine-tuning to mitigate switching failures on unstable agents.  
- Feedback: Discuss scalability to high-dimensional observations (images) and alternative MI estimators.  
- Question 1: How does performance scale with K (under- or over-specification) and αH?  
- Question 2: Can you report exact sample/time savings versus training K separate policies?  
- Question 3: How sensitive are results to the CoM grid resolution and to the choice of C beyond CoM?  
- Question 4: How does the method compare to Option-Critic or goal-conditioned policies on the same sparse tasks?  
- Question 5: What failure rates arise from switching-induced distribution shift, and can adaptive termination reduce this?
