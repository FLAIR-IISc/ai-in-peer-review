- Proposes a two-stage framework: pretrain diverse temporally-extended skills with a proxy reward, then train a high-level manager to sequence skills for sparse, long-horizon tasks.  
- Uses a Stochastic Neural Network (SNN) with discrete latent codes and an MI bonus on a low-dimensional state subset to encourage latent–state dependence and skill diversity.  
- Introduces bilinear latent–observation integration to improve skill separability compared to concatenation.  
- Freezes the low-level skill policy and trains a manager that selects skills every T steps to reduce exploration burden and commit to meaningful primitives.  
- Empirically learns interpretable multi-directional locomotion skills and achieves far broader exploration than Gaussian action noise.  
- Hierarchical policies with pretrained skills improve sample efficiency and success on sparse maze and gather tasks versus strong baselines.  
- Ablations indicate benefits from SNN multimodality, bilinear integration, and MI regularization.  
- Clear motivation: addresses exploration weaknesses in deep RL by decoupling skill acquisition from task learning using minimal supervision.  
- Avoids goal instrumentation and leverages a simple, scalable MI objective inspired by InfoGAN; weight sharing via SNN reduces pretraining cost versus K separate policies.  
- Methodologically clear: discrete latents, bilinear modulation, discretized posterior MI bonus, and TRPO with latent as part of observation are well-motivated.  
- Skill diversity shown by visitation plots with distinct behaviors per latent code; bilinear + MI yields broader, less overlapping skills.  
- Random manager switching among skills explores orders of magnitude more space and covers more directions than multi-policy baselines.  
- Hierarchical approach outperforms proxy-reward-in-task and intrinsic motivation (VIME) baselines; MI especially helpful in longer-horizon tasks.  
- Reports sensitivity analyses (seed variability, switch time T) and transparently notes limitations with unstable agents (Ant).  
- Claims practical impact: simple, general, code available, and advances decoupling skill acquisition from task learning.  
- Essential suggestions: include stronger hierarchical baselines (e.g., option-critic), clarify differences to Heess et al. (2016), and compare empirically where possible.  
- Essential suggestions: report pretraining and downstream sample costs separately and amortized per downstream task to substantiate efficiency claims.  
- Essential suggestions: provide detailed MI estimator analysis (αH sensitivity, grid resolution), alternative estimators for high-dimensional states, and quantitative diversity metrics.  
- Essential suggestions: make Sagent/Srest factorization assumptions explicit and state manager observation privileges.  
- Nice-to-have: ablate number of skills K and latent structure alternatives (continuous latents/clustering).  
- Nice-to-have: test transfer to tasks with less-aligned proxy rewards, pixel observations, and other modalities.  
- Nice-to-have: address Ant instability via termination/transition models or pretraining with switching.  
- Nice-to-have: analyze manager learning to separate effects of temporal abstraction versus skill diversity.  
- Clarification requested: robustness to mis-specified proxy rewards and guidelines for choosing proxies across task families.  
- Clarification requested: rationale for CoM subset selection for MI, effects of different subsets or learned embeddings, and potential trade-offs (e.g., direction vs. gait/stability).  
- Clarification requested: impact of freezing low-level policies versus end-to-end fine-tuning on sample efficiency and stability.  
- Clarification requested: whether pretraining diversity metrics predict downstream variance to avoid cherry-picking; possibility of selection/aggregation strategies.  
- Clarification requested: effect of adaptive termination policies on performance and stability in dynamic environments.  
- Overall: well-motivated, practical contribution with convincing empirical benefits and thoughtful analysis, likely to be influential.
