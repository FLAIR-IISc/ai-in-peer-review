- Proposes hierarchical RL framework that pretrains skills with a proxy reward, then leverages them for faster downstream learning.
- Uses Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) to represent multi-modal policies and an information-theoretic regularizer to encourage diversity.
- Experiments show learning a wide range of skills and uniformly improved performance on downstream tasks.
- Decision: Accept.
- Novel combination of intrinsic motivation and hierarchical methods is a significant contribution.
- Thorough evaluation across multiple tasks and comparisons to state-of-the-art.
- Clear and well-motivated presentation of the framework, SNNs, and the regularizer.
- Empirical results demonstrate effectiveness in skill acquisition and downstream improvement.
- Provides analysis of results, including limitations and future directions.
- Suggest more discussion of relation to other hierarchical RL methods.
- Recommend more insight into hyperparameter choices and sensitivity.
- Ask for analysis of computational complexity and scalability to more complex tasks.
- Question: provide details on the choice of proxy reward signal and its impact on skill learning.
- Question: how to extend the framework to larger, more complex state and action spaces?
- Question: clarify how SNNs and the information-theoretic regularizer contribute to learning diverse behaviors.
