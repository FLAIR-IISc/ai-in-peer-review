**Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions**

The paper proposes a framework for hierarchical reinforcement learning (HRL) that combines the strengths of intrinsic motivation and hierarchical methods. The approach learns useful skills in a pre-training environment using a proxy reward signal that requires minimal domain knowledge about the downstream tasks. A high-level policy is then trained on top of these skills to tackle sparse rewards in the downstream tasks. The authors use Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) combined with an information-theoretic regularizer to efficiently pre-train a large span of skills. The experiments demonstrate that this combination is effective in learning a wide span of interpretable skills in a sample-efficient way and can significantly boost the learning performance uniformly across a wide range of downstream tasks.

**Decision and Key Reasons**

I recommend accepting this paper. The key reasons for this decision are:

1. The paper presents a novel framework for HRL that addresses the challenges of tasks with sparse rewards and long horizons.
2. The approach is well-motivated and grounded in the literature on intrinsic motivation and hierarchical methods.
3. The use of SNNs and information-theoretic regularization is a key innovation that enables the efficient pre-training of a large span of skills.

**Supporting Arguments**

The paper provides a clear and concise introduction to the problem of HRL in tasks with sparse rewards and long horizons. The authors effectively motivate the need for a new approach and provide a comprehensive review of the relevant literature. The proposed framework is well-explained, and the use of SNNs and information-theoretic regularization is a significant contribution to the field.

The experiments are thorough and well-designed, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach in learning a wide span of interpretable skills and boosting the learning performance in downstream tasks. The results are impressive, especially in the maze and gather tasks, where the proposed approach outperforms the baseline methods.

**Additional Feedback and Suggestions**

While the paper is well-written and the approach is well-motivated, there are a few areas where the authors could improve:

1. Provide more details on the implementation of the SNNs and the information-theoretic regularization. This would help readers to better understand the technical aspects of the approach.
2. Consider providing more analysis on the robustness of the approach to different hyperparameters and initialization conditions. This would help to increase confidence in the results and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the approach.
3. In the experiments, the authors could provide more details on the performance of the approach in different tasks and environments. This would help to demonstrate the generality of the approach and its potential applications in various domains.

**Questions for the Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the choice of the proxy reward signal and its impact on the learning of skills?
