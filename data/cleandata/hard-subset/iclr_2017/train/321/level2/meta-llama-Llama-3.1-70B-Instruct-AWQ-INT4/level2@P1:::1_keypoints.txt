- Proposes an HRL framework using stochastic neural networks (SNNs) to learn diverse skills in pre-training and reuse them for downstream sparse-reward tasks.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Tackles the challenging problem of sparse-reward RL with a well-motivated, novel solution.  
- Uses SNNs to learn a diverse skill set during pre-training.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on maze navigation and object gathering.  
- Outperforms state-of-the-art intrinsic motivation methods in experiments.  
- Clear, well-written paper with thorough literature review and well-explained framework.  
- Experimental results are thorough and well-presented.  
- Potential limitation: requires large amounts of data to train SNNs in pre-training.  
- Suggestion: explore transfer learning or meta-learning to reduce data requirements.  
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis of skill interpretability and how learned skills are used.  
- Questions: How to extend to multi-agent or dynamic tasks?  
- Questions: Provide more details on SNN architecture and training procedure.  
- Questions: How to address the large-data requirement for SNN pre-training?
