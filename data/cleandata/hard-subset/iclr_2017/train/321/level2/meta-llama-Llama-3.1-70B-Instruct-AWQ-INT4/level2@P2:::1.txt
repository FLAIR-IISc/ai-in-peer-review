This paper proposes a framework for hierarchical reinforcement learning (HRL) that first learns useful skills in a pre-training environment and then leverages the acquired skills for learning faster in downstream tasks. The approach brings together the strengths of intrinsic motivation and hierarchical methods, using a single proxy reward to guide the learning of useful skills. The authors use Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) combined with an information-theoretic regularizer to efficiently pre-train a large span of skills. The experiments show that this combination is effective in learning a wide span of interpretable skills in a sample-efficient way, and can significantly boost the learning performance uniformly across a wide range of downstream tasks.

Decision: Accept

The paper provides a clear and well-motivated approach to addressing the challenges of tasks with sparse rewards or long horizons in deep reinforcement learning. The use of SNNs and information-theoretic regularizers is a novel and effective way to learn a diverse set of skills that can be reused across tasks. The experiments are thorough and demonstrate the benefits of the proposed approach.

Supporting arguments for the decision:

* The paper provides a clear and well-motivated approach to addressing the challenges of tasks with sparse rewards or long horizons in deep reinforcement learning.
* The use of SNNs and information-theoretic regularizers is a novel and effective way to learn a diverse set of skills that can be reused across tasks.
* The experiments are thorough and demonstrate the benefits of the proposed approach.
* The paper provides a good comparison with existing methods and shows that the proposed approach outperforms them in several tasks.

Additional feedback to improve the paper:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed approach and how it compares to existing methods.
* The authors could provide more insights into how the choice of the proxy reward and the information-theoretic regularizer affects the learning of skills.
* The paper could benefit from more visualizations of the learned skills and how they are used in downstream tasks.
* The authors could provide more details on how the proposed approach can be applied to more complex tasks and environments.
