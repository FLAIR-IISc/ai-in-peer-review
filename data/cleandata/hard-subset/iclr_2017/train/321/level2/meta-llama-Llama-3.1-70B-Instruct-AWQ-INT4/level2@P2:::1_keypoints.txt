- Proposes an HRL framework that pre-trains skills in a proxy environment and reuses them to speed learning on downstream tasks.
- Combines intrinsic motivation and hierarchical methods using a single proxy reward to guide skill discovery.
- Uses Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with an information-theoretic regularizer to efficiently learn a diverse span of skills.
- Demonstrates sample-efficient learning of wide, interpretable skill sets in experiments.
- Shows uniform performance improvements across a range of downstream tasks and outperforms several existing methods.
- Decision: Accept.
- Well-motivated approach addressing sparse rewards and long-horizon RL challenges.
- Novel and effective use of SNNs plus information-theoretic regularization for reusable skill learning.
- Thorough experiments and good comparisons to prior methods.
- Suggest providing a detailed computational complexity analysis versus baselines.
- Recommend more insight into how proxy reward choice and the regularizer influence skill learning.
- Request more visualizations of learned skills and their usage in downstream tasks.
- Ask for details on applying the approach to more complex tasks and environments.
- Questions: how proxy reward and regularizer choices affect skills, how multi-goal/multi-objective tasks are handled, applicability to complex environments, and comparison to option-critic and other HRL methods.
