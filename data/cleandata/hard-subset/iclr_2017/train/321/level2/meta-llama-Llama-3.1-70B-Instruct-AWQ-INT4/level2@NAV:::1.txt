**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a framework for hierarchical reinforcement learning that combines the strengths of intrinsic motivation and hierarchical methods. The framework first learns a set of useful skills in a pre-training environment using a proxy reward signal, and then leverages these skills to learn faster in downstream tasks. The authors use Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) to represent multi-modal policies and introduce an information-theoretic regularizer to encourage diversity of behaviors. The experiments show that the proposed framework can learn a wide range of skills and significantly boost the learning performance uniformly across a wide range of downstream tasks.

**Decision: Accept**

**Reasons for Acceptance**

1. The paper proposes a novel framework that combines the strengths of intrinsic motivation and hierarchical methods, which is a significant contribution to the field of reinforcement learning.
2. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their framework, including experiments on various tasks and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.

**Supporting Arguments**

1. The paper provides a clear and well-motivated explanation of the proposed framework, including the use of SNNs and the information-theoretic regularizer.
2. The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed framework in learning a wide range of skills and improving the learning performance in downstream tasks.
3. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the results, including discussions of the limitations and potential future directions.

**Additional Feedback**

1. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the relationship between the proposed framework and other hierarchical reinforcement learning methods.
2. The authors could provide more insights into the choice of hyperparameters and the sensitivity of the results to these choices.
3. The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed framework and its scalability to more complex tasks.

**Questions for the Authors**
