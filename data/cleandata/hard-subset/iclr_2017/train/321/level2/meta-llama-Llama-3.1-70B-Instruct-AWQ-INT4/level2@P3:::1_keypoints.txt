- Proposes an HRL framework combining intrinsic motivation and hierarchical methods to handle sparse rewards and long horizons.  
- Learns useful skills in a pre-training environment via a proxy reward with minimal domain knowledge, then trains a high-level policy on those skills for downstream tasks.  
- Employs Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with an information-theoretic regularizer to pre-train a large, diverse set of skills.  
- Experiments show sample-efficient learning of wide, interpretable skill sets and uniformly improved downstream performance, notably in maze and gather tasks.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Key reason: novel framework addressing sparse-reward, long-horizon challenges.  
- Key reason: approach is well-motivated and grounded in intrinsic motivation and hierarchical RL literature.  
- Key reason: SNNs + information-theoretic regularization is an important innovation enabling efficient skill pre-training.
