- Addresses RL with sparse rewards/long horizons via a two-stage hierarchical framework: pre-train skills with a generic proxy reward, then train a high-level manager using frozen skills.  
- Uses a single Stochastic Neural Network (SNN) with a discrete latent to represent all skills, enabling efficient weight sharing.  
- Introduces an information-theoretic (mutual information) reward bonus to encourage distinct, interpretable skills.  
- Strong empirical validation on continuous control tasks showing improved exploration and faster learning versus strong baselines.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main reason 1: Novel, practical HRL framework that decouples unsupervised skill acquisition from task-specific control, combining intrinsic motivation and temporal abstraction.  
- Main reason 2: Solid technical contribution—SNN + MI objective is novel and effective, supported by convincing experiments and visualizations.  
- Ablation studies demonstrate bilinear integration and the MI bonus are crucial for learning diverse skills.  
- Strength: Removes need for hand-engineered skills/sub-goals by relying on a simple, generic proxy reward, increasing applicability.  
- Strength: SNN is more sample-efficient than training separate policies; MI bonus prevents mode collapse and yields behaviorally distinct skills.  
- Methodology is sound: results averaged over multiple runs with variance reported.  
- Concern: Scalability of MI estimation—current count-based discretization on 2D states may not scale to higher-dimensional or less obvious state components.  
- Concern: Sensitivity to choice of state subset C for the MI regularizer; guidance for selecting C in other tasks is needed.  
- Suggestion: Provide a more detailed comparison with concurrent goal-conditioned approaches (e.g., Heess et al.) and discuss trade-offs.  
- Question: How sensitive are learned skills to the granularity of the discretized posterior p(Z|c), and have neural-network-based posterior estimators been tried?  
- Question: Given variability in Maze performance depending on pre-trained skills, how can the method encourage a more “complete” set of skills (e.g., adapting proxy rewards or the regularizer)?
