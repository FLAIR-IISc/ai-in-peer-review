- Addresses RL with sparse rewards/long horizons via a two-stage hierarchical framework: pretrain diverse skills with a proxy reward, then train a high-level manager using those skills.
- Uses a single Stochastic Neural Network (SNN) to represent the full low-level skill repertoire, improving sample efficiency through weight sharing.
- Introduces an information-theoretic mutual information (MI) reward bonus to encourage distinct, predictable skills.
- Shows bilinear integration of latent skill and state outperforms simple concatenation for richer skill learning.
- Evaluated on continuous-control locomotion and navigation benchmarks; learns interpretable skills, substantially improves exploration, and solves sparse-reward tasks where baselines fail.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: Novel, well-motivated unsupervised skill discovery combining SNNs and an MI objective for sample-efficient, diverse skill learning with minimal supervision.
- Key reason: Thorough empirical evaluation with ablations (bilinear, MI), qualitative visualizations, and strong quantitative results supporting claims.
- Pre-training with a single proxy reward effectively decouples skill discovery from downstream tasks, making skills broadly applicable.
- The SNN + MI approach is well-grounded (InfoGAN-inspired) and shown to be crucial by ablation studies.
- Random exploration results show dramatic state-space coverage gains that enable solving sparse-reward tasks; experiments run across multiple seeds to avoid cherry-picking.
- Suggestion: Discuss sensitivity of MI bonus estimation to state discretization (e.g., mesh density) and its effect on performance.
- Suggestion: Mention limitations of fixed skill duration T in main text and consider learning termination conditions as an extension.
- Suggestion: Note stability issues on complex agents (e.g., Ant) where skill switching can cause falls.
- Clarifying Q: Does the proxy reward need implicit tailoring to produce downstream-useful skills, or will a general proxy (like CoM speed) plus MI reliably yield useful skills?
- Clarifying Q: Was per-batch re-estimation of the posterior for the MI bonus unstable in practice, and were smoothing strategies (e.g., replay buffer) considered?
