- Proposes a two-stage HRL framework: pre-train diverse reusable skills, then train a high-level policy to compose them for downstream tasks.
- Skill discovery uses a Stochastic Neural Network (SNN) to learn multiple behaviors simultaneously.
- Introduces an information-theoretic (mutual information) regularizer to encourage diversity and interpretability of skills.
- Reviewer recommends Accept, citing strong motivation and convincing empirical support.
- Key strength: elegant unsupervised skill discovery combining SNNs and an MI bonus, which is novel and effective.
- Qualitative results (visitation plots) show the method, especially with bilinear integration + MI bonus, learns richer and more distinct skills than baselines.
- Quantitatively outperforms non-hierarchical methods on sparse-reward benchmarks (Mazes and Gather), improving exploration and learning speed.
- Suggestion: main text should explicitly mention limitations such as instability when switching skills for the Ant robot (currently only in appendix).
- Suggestion: discuss challenges and potential solutions for computing the MI bonus via discretizing low-dimensional state subsets and scaling to higher-dimensional/less-obvious state representations.
- Question: How sensitive are learned skills to the chosen state features (they used 2D center-of-mass), and what heuristics should guide feature selection in new environments?
- Question: Could fine-tuning low-level skills during downstream training reduce variance, and what practical challenges prevented end-to-end training (left as future work)?
