- Proposes a two-phase HRL framework: pre-train diverse skills with a proxy reward, then reuse them for sparse-reward downstream tasks.  
- Uses Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with a mutual-information (MI) regularizer to encourage multi-modal, diverse skills.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — significant, well-motivated, and empirically validated contribution.  
- Addresses a clear problem (sparse rewards, long horizons) and positions well against hand-engineered hierarchies and intrinsic-reward methods.  
- Empirical validation is strong: comprehensive ablations, visitation plots for interpretability, comparisons to relevant baselines, and multiple seeds for robustness.  
- Shows consistent performance gains on Maze and Gather tasks, especially with bilinear integration and the MI bonus.  
- Suggest clarifying theoretical basis for why the MI regularizer prevents mode collapse and yields diverse skills.  
- Recommend expanding discussion of limitations (e.g., unstable robots like Ant) and when the approach may fail or need mitigation.  
- Ask for clearer treatment of sample complexity claims beyond empirical evidence.  
- Suggest adding quantitative diversity metrics (entropy, behavioral distance) to complement visitation plots.  
- Request clearer explanation of differences and relation to Option-Critic regarding skill transferability.  
- Question: Explain how weight-sharing in a single SNN is implemented and what concrete benefits it provides beyond sample efficiency.  
- Question: Why does pretraining with the MI bonus sometimes not improve Maze task performance despite increased pre-training diversity?  
- Question: How does the fixed switch time T affect applicability to tasks needing dynamic skill switching?  
- Question: What are the theoretical or empirical limits on how many downstream tasks a single pre-trained skill set can effectively solve?
