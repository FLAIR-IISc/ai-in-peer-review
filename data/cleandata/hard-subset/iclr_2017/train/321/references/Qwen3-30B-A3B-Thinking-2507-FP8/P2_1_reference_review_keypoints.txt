- Two-phase HRL framework: pre-train diverse skills in a minimal environment with a proxy reward using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) and an MI regularizer, then reuse skills to solve sparse-reward downstream tasks.

- Main contributions: (1) SNNs for multi-modal, weight-shared policies; (2) mutual-information regularizer to encourage skill diversity; (3) empirical gains in exploration and sample efficiency on Maze and Gather benchmarks.

- Recommendation: Accept.

- Motivation: Addresses sparse-reward RL by learning transferable skills without hand-engineered hierarchies or task-specific intrinsic rewards.

- Architectural justification: Bilinear integration and SNNs are appropriate choices; ablation studies show both bilinear integration and MI bonus are necessary for diversity.

- Empirical rigor: Thorough experiments across multiple mazes and Gather task, outperforming standard RL and hierarchical baselines, consistent across random seeds.

- Visualization & analysis: Visitation plots and hierarchical exploration figures provide clear evidence of diverse skills and improved exploration.

- Limitations noted: Failure modes with unstable robots (Ant) and sensitivity to manager switch time T.

- Improvement suggestion 1: Clarify and compare in more detail to Heess et al. (2016), emphasizing differences in pre-training goals and applicability.

- Improvement suggestion 2: Provide concrete quantification of transfer/sample-efficiency gains versus training from scratch.

- Improvement suggestion 3: Expand analysis of the MI bonus, showing how different α_H values affect learned skills, especially for complex robots like Ant.

- Question 1: Why is MI between latent code and CoM coordinates the right measure of skill diversity; any theoretical justification or alternatives considered?

- Question 2: How generalizable are the chosen proxy rewards (e.g., grasping) to downstream tasks that lack those affordances?

- Question 3: Have you tried alternatives to handle instability during skill switching (e.g., termination policy at the Manager level)?
