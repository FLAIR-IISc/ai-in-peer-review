- Proposes a hierarchical RL framework: pre-train a single Stochastic Neural Network (SNN) to learn diverse skills from a minimal proxy reward, then use a high-level policy to select skills for downstream tasks.

- Key innovations: use of SNNs with bilinear integration for multi-modal policies and an information-theoretic (Mutual Information) regularizer to encourage skill diversity.

- Demonstrates significant improvements on sparse-reward benchmarks, enabling more efficient exploration and faster downstream learning.

- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.

- Well-motivated problem and comprehensive literature review situating the work among hierarchical RL, intrinsic motivation, and skill learning.

- Core contribution is theoretically sound: weight-sharing SNNs are more sample-efficient than training separate policies, and MI regularization mitigates mode collapse.

- Ablation studies convincingly show bilinear integration plus MI regularization yields the most diverse and interpretable skills.

- Experiments are rigorous, compare to strong baselines (including VIME), present visitation analyses, and show gains on Maze and Gather tasks.

- Scalability analysis included (works on Snake) and limitations noted for unstable agents (Ant).

- Request to clarify computational efficiency: quantify trade-offs between sample complexity reductions and computational cost.

- Request to address randomness in pre-training: discuss sensitivity to random seeds and propose mitigation strategies (e.g., ensembles).

- Request to expand analysis of the MI regularizer: when it helps, when it doesn’t, and why.

- Suggest stronger comparison with Heess et al. (2016), especially regarding differing pre-training requirements (single proxy reward vs. specified goals).

- Clarifying question: How sensitive is performance to the exact form of the proxy reward and could alternate proxies work?

- Clarifying question: If a pre-trained SNN lacks a needed skill, what strategies (without full retraining) can the high-level policy or system employ?

- Clarifying question: How to choose the MI weight αH in practice and are there theoretical bounds or guidelines?

- Conclusion: Strong, valuable contribution to hierarchical RL with minor addressable weaknesses; suitable and interesting for the ICLR community.
