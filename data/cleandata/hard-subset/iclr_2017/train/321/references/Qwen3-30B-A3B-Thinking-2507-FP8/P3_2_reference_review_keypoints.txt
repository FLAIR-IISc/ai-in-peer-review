- Proposes a hierarchical RL framework that learns diverse skills in a single pre-training phase using only a proxy reward.
- Uses Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) to represent multi-modal, weight-shared policies that learn multiple skills simultaneously.
- Introduces a Mutual Information (MI)-based regularizer to encourage behavioral diversity in the SNN policy.
- Builds a hierarchical architecture that composes learned skills to solve downstream sparse-reward tasks.
- Demonstrates significant empirical improvements over baselines on Maze and Gather tasks, including a strong proxy-reward baseline.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly motivated, and makes a practical, generalizable contribution to hierarchical RL.
- Decision: accept with minor revisions.
- Acceptance reasons: novel and well-motivated approach addressing sparse rewards, strong empirical results, and practical applicability to robotics.
- Strength: practical framework needing minimal domain knowledge and avoiding multiple separate policies for skill learning.
- Strength: thorough experiments, strong baselines, ablation studies showing MI and bilinear integration effects, and skill visitation analysis.
- Good literature placement and clear articulation of how it addresses limitations of prior work.
- Suggestion: provide a stronger theoretical justification for why maximizing I(Z;C) improves skill diversity.
- Suggestion: include scalability analysis regarding number of skills (K) and more complex robots/tasks.
- Suggestion: expand comparisons to recent HRL methods (e.g., Option-Critic and other modern skill-learning approaches).
- Suggestion: provide a more detailed failure analysis (e.g., Ant robot failures) to clarify limitations.
- Suggestion: quantify computational cost and sample-efficiency trade-offs of training SNNs versus separate policies.
- Question: why is a uniform latent distribution chosen and is there theoretical justification for its optimality?
- Question: under what conditions (task types or morphologies) does the MI regularizer most improve performance?
- Question: any preliminary results on end-to-end training with straight-through estimators?
- Question: provide concrete examples of downstream tasks where extra skills (turning/sideways) would be especially beneficial.
