- Proposes a hierarchical RL framework that pre-trains a diverse span of reusable skills with a minimal proxy reward to tackle sparse-reward tasks.  
- Uses Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) to represent multi-modal policies with weight sharing for efficient skill representation.  
- Introduces an information-theoretic Mutual Information (MI) regularizer to encourage skill diversity and prevent collapse.  
- Demonstrates improved exploration and sample efficiency in sparse-reward environments across multiple robotic tasks (Swimmer, Snake, Ant).  
- Provides thorough empirical validation with strong ablation studies, multiple environments, and reproducibility resources (code/videos).  
- Architectural choices (bilinear integration over concatenation) and use of TRPO are well-justified and clearly described.  
- Related work coverage is comprehensive and positions the approach relative to hierarchical RL, skill discovery, and intrinsic motivation methods.  
- Main weakness: lacks theoretical analysis of convergence and how the MI regularizer affects learning dynamics.  
- Recommendation: clarify what constitutes the “minimal domain knowledge” for designing the proxy reward with concrete examples.  
- Recommendation: add a brief theoretical discussion on how the MI bonus influences training and prevents mode collapse.  
- Recommendation: discuss scalability and generalization to more complex, real-world tasks (e.g., diverse interactions, multi-agent).  
- Recommendation: more explicitly distinguish this method from other skill-discovery approaches (e.g., variational/information-theoretic methods).  
- Question: how to handle domains where a proxy reward is hard to define (e.g., speed not meaningful)?  
- Question: provide concrete examples of minimal domain knowledge for proxy rewards beyond the presented robotic domains.  
- Question: explain why the MI bonus helps some task structures (e.g., Gather) more than others (inconsistent Maze results).
