- Problem & approach: Tackles sparse-reward RL by pretraining diverse skills with Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) and an MI regularizer, then using these skills hierarchically for downstream tasks.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Core contribution: Principled method for learning transferable skills with minimal supervision that improves exploration and sample efficiency on sparse-reward tasks.  
- Model innovation: Uses SNNs to represent multi-modal policies that learn multiple skills simultaneously.  
- Diversity mechanism: Introduces an information-theoretic (mutual information) regularizer to encourage diverse, interpretable skills.  
- Empirical validation: Strong experiments on Swimmer, Snake, Ant and Maze/Gather tasks showing superior performance to baselines.  
- Ablations & visualizations: Bilinear integration vs concatenation and MI bonus ablations clearly show component impacts; visitation plots convincingly demonstrate skill diversity.  
- Limitations discussed: Notes instability with Ant robot and use of fixed skill switch time T; acknowledges areas for future work.  
- Key strength: Minimal domain knowledge required for pre-training (simple proxy reward) while enabling large downstream gains.  
- Clarify relation to prior work: Need clearer comparison to Option-Critic and how skill reusability differs.  
- Quantify gains: Request to report exact sample-efficiency improvements (e.g., X× fewer samples).  
- Computational cost: Ask for wall-clock time and memory comparisons between SNN and multi-policy alternatives.  
- Generalization concern: Recommend discussing potential overfitting and whether learned skills transfer to unseen environments.  
- Theory question: Ask for a precise theoretical justification for the claim that training K skills is effectively K times better in sample complexity.  
- Exploration question: Request explanation for why SNNs show wider coverage than multi-policy despite fewer pre-training samples.  
- MI bonus question: Ask when the MI bonus helps most and what factors determine its effectiveness.  
- Ant failure-mode question: Ask for specific modifications to address instability during skill switching on Ant and the likely impact on pre-training sample efficiency.
