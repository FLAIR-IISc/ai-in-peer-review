- Proposal: use Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) plus a Mutual Information (MI) regularizer to pre-train diverse skills with a minimal proxy reward, then reuse them for hierarchical RL to solve sparse-reward tasks.

- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.

- Motivation & positioning: clearly identifies gap between hierarchy methods (require domain knowledge) and intrinsic rewards (poor transfer), and situates work relative to Heess et al. (2016) and HiREPS.

- Methodology: SNNs with bilinear latent integration and an MI regularizer are well-justified; design choices are explained and validated via ablations.

- Empirical validation: thorough experiments on Maze 0-3 and Gather tasks with Swimmer, Snake, Ant; comparisons to multi-policy and proxy-reward baselines, visitation plots, and learning curves provided.

- Claims supported: demonstrates SNN+MI learns a wider span of skills, improves exploration, and enables solving sparse-reward tasks that baseline methods cannot.

- Sample complexity caveat: the claim that training K skills is "K times better" is misleading — SNN learns K skills with similar sample complexity as a single policy and needs clarification.

- Proxy reward guidance: paper asserts proxy rewards need minimal domain knowledge but lacks practical guidance on designing them for other domains.

- MI bonus analysis: MI helps in some tasks (Gather) but not others (Mazes); deeper analysis is needed to explain when and why it helps.

- Skill diversity examples: presented skills are mostly simple translations (forward/back/side); more varied, complex skill examples (turning, trajectories) would strengthen claims.

- Question — MI state choice: why compute MI using Center-of-Mass coordinates instead of richer state information (e.g., joint angles), and how would that affect learned skills?

- Question — skill switching instability: in Ant experiments, skill switching causes instability; have authors considered a learned transition policy or integrating switching into pre-training, and its sample complexity implications?

- Question — proxy reward design across domains: request concrete examples of proxy reward design for very different domains (e.g., robotic manipulation).

- Question — MI in Mazes: what specific Maze characteristics (geometry, reward structure, required skills) determine whether MI is beneficial?

- Question — efficiency baseline: how do SNN-learned skills compare to skills trained as separate policies using K× samples — need this comparison to validate claimed efficiency gains.
