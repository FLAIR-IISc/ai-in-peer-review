- Proposes a two-phase hierarchical RL framework: pretrain diverse skills with SNNs and a proxy reward, then reuse skills for sparse-reward downstream tasks using an MI regularizer.

- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.

- Addresses gap: learns transferable, diverse skills without domain-specific skill design or task-specific rewards.

- Method: bilinear integration of latents with observations and an MI-based regularizer improve multi-modal skill learning; ablations support these choices.

- Empirics: strong, comprehensive results on Maze, Gather, Snake, and Ant showing improved exploration, sample efficiency, and interpretable skills; visitation plots effectively visualize diversity.

- Presentation/reproducibility: well-written, technically appropriate, and code is available.

- Suggestion: analyze high variance across random seeds and provide metrics or selection methods for pretrained SNNs (e.g., entropy of skill distribution).

- Suggestion: add brief theoretical grounding explaining why the MI regularizer encourages diversity.

- Suggestion: compare with more recent hierarchical RL methods beyond 2016 (e.g., PRL, SAVI).

- Suggestion: clarify MI bonus implementation and when/why it fails to boost performance.

- Question: how to address Ant skill-switching instability (e.g., modify switching mechanism or add transition policies)?

- Question: under what conditions does the MI bonus improve downstream performance?

- Question: provide quantitative measures of the “richer” skill span (e.g., entropy, state coverage).

- Question: give a detailed analysis of actual sample-efficiency gains versus training separate policies.

- Question: include Maze task results with longer time horizons to test if hierarchy benefits scale as in Gather.
