- Proposal: Introduces a hierarchical RL framework using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with a Mutual Information (MI) regularizer to learn diverse skills in pre-training and transfer them to sparse-reward tasks.
- Key contributions: (1) simultaneous multi-skill learning via SNNs with bilinear latent integration, (2) MI-based skill diversity, (3) hierarchical use of pre-trained skills for downstream tasks.
- Claim on domain knowledge: Pre-training requires only a simple proxy reward (e.g., speed) claimed to be “minimal” domain knowledge.
- Empirical results: Shows strong improvements in exploration and sample efficiency on Maze and Gather tasks with Swimmer, Snake, and Ant robots.
- Baselines and comparisons: Evaluations include fair baselines and a CoM proxy reward baseline, demonstrating advantages of the method.
- Interpretability: Learned skills are interpretable and analysis links specific skills to maze configurations.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — novel idea, well-executed, strong empirical validation.
- Strength: Clear problem formulation addressing limits of hand-engineered hierarchies and intrinsic reward methods.
- Strength: Methodologically sound—bilinear integration justified and MI regularizer effectively adapted from InfoGAN principles; ablations validate components.
- Weakness / clarification needed: Precisely define “minimal domain knowledge” and justify proxy reward choices (e.g., speed for Swimmer).
- Weakness / theoretical: Provide brief theoretical justification for why maximizing MI between latent codes and state coordinates encourages skill diversity.
- Weakness / scalability: Discuss scalability when increasing the number of skills K and whether MI regularization remains effective for larger K.
- Weakness / comparisons: Include comparisons to modern sample-efficient off-policy algorithms (e.g., SAC, TD3) rather than only TRPO.
- Question: How would the proxy reward translate to a completely unknown robot—what exact domain knowledge is required?
- Question: Why choose CoM coordinates for the MI term rather than other state subsets, and how to generalize when no clear subset exists?
- Question: Can the authors provide a metric linking skill diversity to downstream learning speed?
- Question: Ant instability when switching skills—summarize proposed fixes and their limitations (technical report solutions not included).
