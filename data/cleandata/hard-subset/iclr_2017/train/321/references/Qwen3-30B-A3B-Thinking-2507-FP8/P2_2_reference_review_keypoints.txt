- Proposal: Introduces a hierarchical RL framework that pretrains diverse skills with Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) and a Mutual Information (MI) regularizer, then uses those skills for downstream sparse-reward tasks.  
- Key innovation: Use of SNNs to represent multi-modal policies with an MI-based regularizer to encourage interpretable, diverse skills.  
- Empirical claim: Method improves exploration and outperforms baselines on sparse-reward tasks (mazes, food collection) with clear skill behaviors.  
- Evaluation strength: Thorough experiments with visitation plots, exploration comparisons, learning curves, ablations, multiple seeds, and available code/videos.  
- Decision: Recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Improvement 1: Clarify what “minimal domain knowledge” the proxy reward requires and whether it can be automated or generalized.  
- Improvement 2: Provide deeper theoretical justification for why the MI regularizer encourages skill diversity and any formal guarantees.  
- Improvement 3: Analyze when and why the MI regularizer fails to boost performance (inconsistent effects across environments).  
- Improvement 4: Expand discussion of limitations (e.g., unstable robots like Ant) and propose concrete mitigation strategies.  
- Question 1: Can the proxy reward be determined automatically without human intervention?  
- Question 2: Why does the MI bonus sometimes fail (e.g., Maze 1) and how to select αH automatically across tasks?  
- Question 3: How does the approach scale to more complex robots with higher-dimensional actions and dynamics?  
- Question 4: What causes skill-switching instability in Ant experiments and what architectural/training fixes are recommended?
