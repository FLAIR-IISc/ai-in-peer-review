- Tackles sparse-reward, long-horizon RL by pretraining reusable low-level skills in a proxy environment and training a high-level manager to switch among them.  
- Uses Stochastic Neural Networks with a categorical latent z to represent multiple skills within one shared-weight policy.  
- Introduces a bilinear integration of z with observations to increase expressivity over simple concatenation.  
- Adds an information-theoretic regularizer maximizing I(Z; C) using count-based estimates p(z|c) over chosen state features (e.g., CoM) to encourage diverse, interpretable skills.  
- Employs a hierarchical policy where a manager selects a frozen skill every T steps (no end-to-end fine-tuning).  
- Empirically produces broad, interpretable skill repertoires and substantially improves exploration and downstream performance on sparse-reward locomotion benchmarks.  
- Outperforms strong intrinsic-motivation baselines (e.g., CoM speed reward, VIME) under the benchmark setting.  
- Provides ablations (concatenation vs. bilinear; MI coefficient), sensitivity to switch period T, variance across pretraining seeds, and failure mode analysis (Ant).  
- Code and videos are made available.  
- Decision: weak accept — approach is simple, well-motivated, and empirically convincing in the target domain.  
- Problem significance: addresses an important challenge—efficient exploration and skill reuse across related tasks.  
- Positioning: method aligns with prior HRL and skill-discovery work and removes need for goal labels via a minimal proxy reward.  
- Evidence for diversity: visitation plots show distinct locomotion skills with SNN + bilinear + MI outperforming alternatives.  
- Evidence for exploration: long random-manager trajectories show much broader coverage than Gaussian noise and multi-policy baselines.  
- Downstream gains: hierarchical policies on SNN skills outperform baselines across multiple mazes and Gather, with robust evaluation (10 runs per curve).  
- Ablations: analyzes integration method, MI strength, switch time T, and pretraining variance; Ant instability is acknowledged.  
- Limitation — generality: proxy reward and MI target are hand-designed for locomotion; no non-locomotion domains demonstrated.  
- Limitation — comparisons: lacks direct comparisons to HRL skill-learning baselines (e.g., Option-Critic) in the same environments.  
- Limitation — frozen skills and fixed T: this simplification may limit final performance versus learned terminations or fine-tuning.  
- Limitation — MI estimator: coarse grid-count estimator and task-specific choice of C and αH; no quantitative MI or robustness analysis shown.  
- Limitation — Ant: switching-induced instability in harder environments exposes method weaknesses.  
- Recommendation — report sample complexity: quantify pretraining and total environment interactions versus baselines (including multi-policy).  
- Recommendation — add HRL baselines: include end-to-end option-learning comparisons (e.g., Option-Critic) for context.  
- Recommendation — improve MI estimation: consider learned discriminator p̂(z|c), report MI bounds, and analyze sensitivity to C and αH.  
- Recommendation — analyze skill count K: provide sensitivity analysis and qualitative effects of too few/too many skills.  
- Recommendation — manager design: explore adaptive termination, recurrent managers, and whether smaller T helps dynamic tasks.  
- Recommendation — broaden domains: evaluate at least one non-locomotion task to demonstrate generality.  
- Recommendation — Ant mitigations: try safety filters, smoother transitions, or overlapping skill visitation to reduce instability.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity and principled selection of K and αH?  
- Questions for authors: total samples (pretrain + downstream) vs. training from scratch or multi-policy baseline normalized by data?  
- Questions for authors: how critical is choice of C; tried higher-dimensional C or learned p̂(z|c)?  
- Questions for authors: does MI encourage spatial partitioning rather than distinct actuation patterns; evidence of gait diversity at same heading?  
- Questions for authors: results with end-to-end fine-tuning or learned termination on any task?  
- Questions for authors: computational overhead of bilinear vs concatenation and scalability to high-dimensional observations?  
- Questions for authors: for Ant, did you try regularizing skill overlap (e.g., KL constraints) to stabilize switching?
