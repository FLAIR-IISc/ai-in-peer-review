- Tackles sparse-reward continuous-control by pretraining a reusable discrete-latent SNN low-level policy and training a high-level Manager to select skills for downstream tasks.
- Uses a mutual information (MI) bonus between latent code and a subset of state with a discretized visitation-count posterior estimator to encourage diverse, interpretable skills.
- Shows bilinear integration of latent and observation (vs concatenation) is important to elicit distinct skills.
- Manager selects a latent every fixed T steps while skills are frozen, reducing effective horizon and improving exploration.
- Uses TRPO for training and demonstrates improved exploration and performance on hard sparse tasks (mazes, gather) for Swimmer and Snake; analyzes Ant failure modes.
- Paper is well written, provides code/videos, and includes ablations on key design choices (concat vs bilinear, MI strength).
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reasons for acceptance: simple, well-motivated method combining multimodal policies and MI regularization; convincing empirical evidence of improved exploration and task solving with careful ablations.
- Problem and motivation: addresses sparse rewards and long horizons where action noise fails, and amortized pretraining across tasks is broadly sensible.
- SNNs offer principled multimodality and parameter sharing, plausibly yielding ~K-fold sample savings vs K independent policies.
- Novelty/placement: integrates known ideas (SNNs, MI bonuses) into unsupervised skill discovery for continuous control with a useful bilinear integration insight.
- Skill diversity evidence: visitation plots show bilinear+MI produce distinct locomotion skills (forward/backward/sideways/turning) with single SNN.
- Exploration evidence: random-manager rollouts show SNN hierarchies cover much larger state space than Gaussian noise and multi-policy baselines.
- Downstream performance: hierarchical policies with SNN skills learn faster and to higher returns than strong baselines (including VIME) across several tasks.
- Scalability: Snake results show scalability; Ant analysis candidly reports instability when switching skills.
- Rigor/transparency: reports variance across seeds, discusses pretrain failures, and includes hyperparameters and implementation details in appendix.
- Limitations: evaluation limited to MuJoCo locomotion and simple downstream tasks; MI estimator uses discretization heuristic.
- Limitations: lacks direct comparisons to contemporary HRL baselines (e.g., option-critic) and uses fixed switch time with frozen low-level skills.
- Claim caveat: sample-efficiency claims are empirical without theoretical grounding.
- Suggested baseline improvements: compare to option-critic and independently trained multi-policy hierarchies with matched pretraining budgets.
- Suggested metrics: report quantitative diversity/separability metrics (e.g., MI values, pairwise divergence) beyond plots.
- Suggested sensitivity analyses: study sensitivity to K, MI coefficient αH, MI-grid resolution, and switch time T; provide practitioner guidance.
- Suggested sample accounting: present amortized sample-efficiency analysis (pretrain cost vs per-task gains) and scaling with number of downstream tasks.
- Suggested robustness experiments: explore manager fine-tuning of low-level skills, learned termination policies, and recurrent managers for stability (e.g., Ant).
- Suggested generality: discuss applicability beyond locomotion and to high-dimensional observations (images) and alternative MI state subsets.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to MI grid/αH and heuristics for choosing αH/K; whether MI estimator biases learning in non-uniform visitation regimes.
- Questions for authors: whether learned termination policies or recurrent managers were tried and their effects on stability, especially for Ant.
- Questions for authors: effects of joint fine-tuning of low-level skills with manager (e.g., straight-through estimators) on failures/asymptotic performance.
- Questions for authors: amortization threshold—after how many downstream tasks does pretraining become net sample-efficient vs training from scratch?
- Questions for authors: robustness of skills to changes in orientation/morphology or mild dynamics shifts.
- Questions for authors: ability to include or justify lack of comparisons to option-critic/HiREPS on these tasks and report MI or other quantitative diversity metrics correlated with downstream performance.
