- Problem: Learn reusable, diverse low-level skills for multiple sparse-reward, long-horizon tasks without task-specific shaping.  
- Approach: Pretrain a stochastic neural network (SNN) conditioned on categorical latent z with an MI bonus on CoM position, bilinear latent integration, then freeze SNN and train a high-level Manager that selects skills every T steps with TRPO.  
- Claim: SNN + MI yields multiple interpretable, temporally consistent skills.  
- Claim: Hierarchical selection of these skills improves exploration and sample-efficiency on sparse tasks.  
- Claim: Method achieves strong performance on challenging locomotion mazes and a gather task.  
- Claim: Pretraining is sample efficient and requires minimal domain knowledge (generic proxy reward).  
- Evidence: Ablations on integration and MI strength, skill visitation plots, exploration coverage under random managers, learning curves, T analysis, Snake scaling, Ant failure discussion, code/hyperparams provided.  
- Decision: Weak accept.  
- Key reason: Simple, well-motivated, practically effective framework that improves diversity and hierarchical exploration, with careful empirical study and code release.  
- Significance/originality: Novel/pragmatic combination of SNN with MI regularization and bilinear integration to induce interpretable locomotion skills.  
- Motivation/related work: Well situated relative to options/HRL and intrinsic motivation; contrasts with goal-set/demonstration methods.  
- Empirical: Bilinear+MI produces distinct locomotion modes; concat performs worse in ablations.  
- Empirical: Random-manager exploration covers far more area than Gaussian noise or multi-policy baselines.  
- Empirical: Hierarchical policies outperform baselines on multiple mazes/gather tasks across seeds; robust to T choices and scales to Snake.  
- Rigor/clarity: Algorithm, TRPO adaptation, MI estimation, training protocol, hyperparams, and failure modes are clearly described.  
- Limitation: Scope/generalization primarily demonstrated on locomotion with simple agents; Ant shows instability at skill switches.  
- Limitation: Diversity metrics largely qualitative (visitation plots); lacks explicit quantitative MI or dissimilarity measures.  
- Limitation: Missing empirical comparisons to other HRL/skill-discovery methods (e.g., option-critic, HiREPS).  
- Limitation: Limited sensitivity analysis for K, MI coefficient, MI grid resolution, and bilinear embedding size.  
- Limitation: Freezing skills and fixed switch time T may cap performance; no end-to-end training explored.  
- Suggested improvement: Add quantitative diversity measures (MI estimates, pairwise action disagreement, state coverage).  
- Suggested improvement: Report sensitivity studies for K, αH, grid density, and bilinear size with curves/metrics.  
- Suggested improvement: Strengthen baselines including option-critic, monolithic policies with parameter noise, and independent skills with MI.  
- Suggested improvement: Clarify sample efficiency with wall-clock time and env steps vs training K independent policies and manager cost.  
- Suggested improvement: Try stability strategies for unstable agents (skill warm-start blending, action smoothing, skill-specific stabilizers).  
- Suggested improvement: Demonstrate generality beyond locomotion (e.g., manipulators or non-locomotion proxies).  
- Questions for authors: Sensitivity of MI estimation to CoM grid and αH, and whether a parametric p(z|c) was tried.  
- Questions for authors: Does batch-computed MI bonus introduce nonstationarity for TRPO, and any variance reduction used?  
- Questions for authors: How was K=6 chosen and what happens when K exceeds distinguishable skills?  
- Questions for authors: How does switch time T interact with different dynamics and does learning termination help?  
- Questions for authors: What manager inputs are required—full Srest vs Sagent effects?  
- Questions for authors: Are skills robust to initial orientation or small dynamics shifts (mass/friction)?  
- Questions for authors: For Ant instability, are particular skills brittle at switch and would blending reduce falls?  
- Questions for authors: Computational cost overhead of bilinear vs concat and SNN vs multiple independent policies?
