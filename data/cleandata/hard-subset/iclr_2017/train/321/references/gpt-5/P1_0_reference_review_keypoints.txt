- Proposes pretraining a multi-modal low-level policy (SNN with categorical latent z) to learn reusable skills, then training a high-level manager to compose them for sparse-reward RL.
- Uses a categorical latent sampled per rollout to induce temporally coherent, distinct skills within a single policy.
- Encourages skill diversity with an information-theoretic regularizer: per-step bonus proportional to mutual information between z and a state subset (e.g., CoM), estimated via discretized visitation counts.
- Introduces bilinear integration between z and observations to increase expressivity and skill separability, outperforming simple concatenation.
- Freezes the pretrained SNN and trains a high-level TRPO manager that selects a skill every T steps (temporal commitment).
- Empirically shows interpretable, diverse locomotion skills, greatly improved exploration coverage, and success on sparse-reward mazes and gather tasks where standard methods fail.
- Ablations demonstrate that both bilinear integration and the MI bonus materially improve performance and skill diversity.
- Method scales to a higher-dimensional Snake robot; documents failure modes and instability for Ant.
- Decision: recommend accept due to a simple, well-motivated, general approach that improves exploration and sample efficiency.
- Strong empirical validation with multiple ablations (SNN vs multi-policy, concatenation vs bilinear, with/without MI) and transparent reporting of variance across seeds.
- Motivation: addresses sparse-reward, long-horizon exploration via skill abstraction rather than reward shaping, with pragmatic intrinsic proxy reward for pretraining.
- Methodological soundness: sampling z per rollout and treating z as observation avoids mixture complications with TRPO.
- MI regularizer justification: maximizing I(Z;C) via minimizing H(Z|C) encourages distinguishable skills tied to CoM position; discretized posterior is simple and cheap.
- Bilinear integration is a meaningful architectural choice with intuitive justification and empirical benefits.
- Pretraining evidence: visitation plots show distinct skills; SNN achieves K× data efficiency versus training K independent policies.
- Exploration evidence: one-million-step trajectories under a random manager show markedly improved coverage and interpretable skill switches.
- Downstream tasks: hierarchical agents with SNNs solve mazes and gather tasks that standard RL fails; performance improves with bilinear integration and MI in many cases.
- Scaling evidence: positive results on Snake and honest reporting of Ant instability.
- Suggestion: quantify skill diversity with empirical MI, state coverage area, classifier accuracy, or action-distribution diversity.
- Suggestion: include sensitivity analyses for K, MI coefficient αH, MI grid resolution, and switch time T (surface key findings from appendix).
- Suggestion: report sample complexity (env steps to success thresholds, AUC) and compare to baselines.
- Suggestion: add baselines like option-critic or HiREPS or explain why they’re impractical here.
- Suggestion: discuss pretraining-task generality and limits of the “shared agent-space” assumption; consider transfer to different task classes.
- Suggestion: clarify manager inputs, observation subsets, training budgets, reward normalization, advantage estimation, and entropy regularization for reproducibility.
- Suggestion: specify bilinear integration implementation (outer product shape), discuss bias of discretized MI, and consider learning p(z|c) with a classifier.
- Suggestion: investigate smoothing/interpolating skill transitions or transition policies to improve robustness for unstable agents like Ant.
- Questions: sensitivity to K and whether redundant/unused skills emerge.
- Questions: how the subset C for MI was chosen and effects of other choices (heading, velocity).
- Questions: request quantitative MI or decoding accuracy (predict z from c) to back visitation plots.
- Questions: exact bilinear integration dimensionalities and whether alternatives (multiplicative gating, FiLM) were tried.
- Questions: consistency of SNN pretraining across seeds for larger robots and impact on downstream success variability.
- Questions: report gather-task success under fixed and longer horizons to isolate temporal abstraction effects.
- Questions: whether limited fine-tuning of low-level skills with the manager was attempted to mitigate mismatch.
- Questions: for Ant, whether regularizing skills via off-manifold robustness (domain randomization, adversarial perturbations) was explored to reduce falls at switches.
