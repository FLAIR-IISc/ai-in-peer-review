- Pretrain reusable low-level skills with a minimal proxy reward and no task-specific supervision.  
- Use a stochastic neural network (SNN) with discrete latent z and bilinear integration to induce multimodal conditional policies.  
- Encourage diverse, interpretable skills via a mutual information (MI) bonus I(Z; C) computed with count-based p(z|c) on CoM state.  
- Freeze the SNN and train a high-level “Manager” that selects skills every T steps for downstream sparse-reward tasks.  
- Empirically shows diverse skills, dramatically improved exploration, and better learning curves on locomotion sparse tasks (Swimmer, Snake, Maze, Gather).  
- Ablations indicate bilinear integration and the MI regularizer both materially contribute to performance.  
- Decision: Weak accept; framework is simple, well-motivated, and practically useful.  
- Paper is well situated relative to HRL, skill discovery, and intrinsic motivation literature; it synthesizes existing ideas effectively for skill discovery.  
- SNN design choices (discrete z fixed per rollout, bilinear modulation, TRPO training treating z as observation) are sensible.  
- MI targeted at CoM is pragmatic, computationally light, and disentangles directional/locomotion modes.  
- Empirical study includes ablations, exploration visualizations, multiple seeds, and provided code/videos.  
- Evaluation scope is limited to locomotion with proprioception/LIDAR-like inputs; generality to other domains is untested.  
- Missing stronger baselines: Option-Critic, end-to-end mixture-of-experts/gated policies, and a conditional policy without MI.  
- MI design is narrow (CoM-only, coarse counts) and may bias toward directional skills, limiting discovery of abstract behaviors.  
- Claims about K-fold sample-efficiency from training K skills in one SNN are not quantified versus training K separate policies.  
- Fixed skill commitment time T and freezing low-level skills limit adaptability; switching instability shown on Ant.  
- Statistical robustness is limited: variances are high in some tasks and success can depend on pretrain seed.  
- Suggest adding stronger baselines including Gumbel-Softmax end-to-end training for comparison.  
- Suggest analyzing scaling with K (coverage, pairwise distances, downstream performance, training stability).  
- Suggest deeper MI analysis: sensitivity to αH and grid resolution and comparing learned density estimators versus counts.  
- Recommend quantitative diversity metrics beyond visitation plots (mutual information estimates, occupancy overlap, dispersion).  
- Suggest exploring partial finetuning of low-level skills or learned termination to reduce switching failures.  
- Ask for reported computational cost and environment-step budgets for fair comparisons.  
- Key questions: sensitivity to αH and CoM discretization; scaling behavior with K; head-to-head sample costs versus K separate policies.  
- Additional questions: effects of alternative conditioning (FiLM/hypernetworks), learned p̂(Z|C) vs counts, other proxy rewards, quantification of turning skills in Gather, and switch-smoothing for Ant.
