- Proposes a two-stage hierarchical RL framework: pre-train latent-conditioned SNN skills with a proxy reward and MI on CoM, then freeze skills and train a high-level manager to select skills every T steps.  
- Uses categorical latents and bilinear state-latent integration to induce multimodal, parameter-shared skills and avoid mode collapse.  
- Adds a mutual information bonus between latent z and a subset of state (robot CoM) estimated by discretization to encourage interpretable diversity.  
- Empirically learns distinct, interpretable locomotion primitives and improves state-space coverage versus Gaussian exploration.  
- Significantly outperforms strong baselines on sparse-reward tasks (multiple mazes, Gather) and scales to Snake; Ant is a noted failure mode.  
- Ablations show bilinear integration and MI improve skill diversity and orientation-dependent tasks.  
- SNNs match or exceed multiple independently trained policies at reduced sample cost (K skills for the price of one).  
- Hierarchical use of skills (even with a random manager) greatly increases exploration coverage in long rollouts.  
- Results averaged over multiple pre-trained SNNs and seeds, with variance reported; code and hyperparameters are released.  
- Decision: Accept — method is practical, conceptually clean, simple to implement, and well-validated.  
- Motivation: Addresses sparse-reward exploration weaknesses with low assumptions and clear contrast to goal-conditioned pre-training.  
- Methodological clarity: Straightforward architecture/training, treating z as observation avoids marginalization issues in TRPO.  
- Reproducibility: Hyperparameters provided and code released.  
- Limitation: Baseline comparisons lack some hierarchical/skill-discovery methods (e.g., HiREPS, option-critic) and mixture-of-experts gating.  
- Limitation: Quantitative diversity metrics (empirical MI, coverage/entropy, pairwise divergence) are missing beyond visitation plots.  
- Limitation: Sensitivity to K, αH, discretization resolution, and subset c is not fully analyzed.  
- Limitation: Sample complexity accounting (pre-train + downstream vs baselines) needs clearer reporting.  
- Limitation: Proxy reward and MI choice are locomotion-specific; generality to non-locomotion domains is unclear.  
- Limitation: Fixed switch time T and frozen skills limit adaptability; learned termination or fine-tuning not explored.  
- Key questions: sensitivity to K/αH/discretization and empirical MI as function of αH.  
- Key questions: effects of richer/learned c (e.g., heading or discriminative network) instead of CoM discretization.  
- Key questions: total environment interaction budget and wall-clock comparison to baselines.  
- Key questions: can manager compose skills or use continuous latents for interpolation; recovery when needed behavior is absent.  
- Key questions: robustness to mismatch between pre-train and downstream dynamics/sensors and smoothing skill switches for Ant.  
- Suggestions: add quantitative diversity metrics, sensitivity analysis (K, αH, T), and total sample counts; include another hierarchical baseline.  
- Suggestions: clarify bilinear implementation and manager I/O, provide guidance for choosing c in non-locomotion domains, and test learned termination or light fine-tuning.
