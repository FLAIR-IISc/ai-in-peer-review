- Pretrain a stochastic neural network (SNN) with a discrete latent z sampled per rollout so each z defines a temporally-extended skill.
- Encourage diverse, interpretable skills via an MI bonus between z and a low-dimensional state subset c (e.g., CoM), implemented as α log p̂(z|c) using discretized visitation counts.
- Use bilinear integration of z with observations to induce distinct modes, shown to outperform simple concatenation.
- Downstream: freeze the SNN and train a high-level manager (option-like) to choose z every T steps via TRPO.
- Empirical results on MuJoCo (Swimmer, Snake) show skills are distinct/ interpretable and broaden exploration.
- Hierarchical policy with SNN skills learns faster and solves sparse-reward tasks that naïve exploration cannot.
- SNN-based multi-modal skills are more sample-efficient and cover more behaviors than training multiple separate uni-modal policies.
- MI regularization improves skill diversity and downstream performance, especially on the Gather task.
- Code, hyperparameters, videos, and limitations (e.g., Ant instability) are provided for reproducibility.
- Decision: Accept — method is simple, novel in combination, well-motivated, and reproducible with strong empirical support.
- Strength: Unifies intrinsic-motivation skill discovery with a practical, reusable categorical skill representation and a simple hierarchical controller.
- Methodological soundness: treating z as part of observation enables TRPO use; fixing z per rollout yields temporal coherence; MI proxy via discretized counts is low-variance and easy to implement.
- Empirical support includes visitation plots, broad coverage under random high-level selection, downstream improvements across mazes/Gather, Snake scaling, and analysis of switch time T.
- Limitation: MI defined over CoM (x,y) biases toward locomotion and may not generalize to non-locomotion domains like manipulation.
- Limitation: Missing head-to-head comparisons with contemporary HRL skill discovery baselines (e.g., option-critic).
- Limitation: Diversity mainly shown visually; lack of quantitative diversity metrics and sensitivity analyses (binning resolution, α, K, integration type).
- Limitation: Fixed low-level skills and fixed option length T may underperform in dynamic settings; end-to-end fine-tuning and learned termination left for future work.
- Suggestions: Add quantitative diversity measures, ablations on MI coefficient/grid resolution/K/integration, and comparisons to at least one HRL baseline.
- Suggestions: Report sensitivity and guidance for choosing T and α, summarize variance across seeds with confidence intervals and success counts.
- Suggestions: Discuss choosing c beyond 2D CoM and consider a learned discriminator p̂(z|c) to scale to other domains.
- Suggestions: Explore partial fine-tuning of low-level skills or learned termination to improve robustness (e.g., for Ant).
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to CoM discretization and α; ablations and practical guidelines?
- Questions for authors: how results vary with number of skills K and whether redundant modes emerge?
- Questions for authors: can you report quantitative diversity metrics and correlate with downstream performance?
- Questions for authors: how does the method compare to option-critic or other HRL baselines on same tasks (sample complexity/success rate)?
- Questions for authors: what c to choose for non-CoM domains (manipulation), and have you tested alternatives?
- Questions for authors: does partial end-to-end fine-tuning help on unstable agents or reduce variance?
- Questions for authors: what is the exact training-budget equivalence across SNN vs multi-policy baselines (env steps and wall-clock), quantifying the K× sample-efficiency claim?
