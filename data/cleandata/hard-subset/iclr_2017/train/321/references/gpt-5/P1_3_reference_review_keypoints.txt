- Proposes pretraining a single SNN with a categorical latent code to learn reusable skills, then training a high-level manager to select skills for downstream tasks.  
- Introduces an MI-inspired bonus that maximizes predictability of the latent code from a subset of state (e.g., CoM) to encourage diverse and interpretable skills.  
- Uses bilinear integration of latent code with observations for more expressive skill conditioning than simple concatenation.  
- Pretraining relies on a simple proxy reward (e.g., speed magnitude) and skills are frozen when training the manager.  
- Demonstrates improved exploration and sample efficiency on sparse-reward downstream tasks (Swimmer/Snake Maze and Gather) compared to naive and intrinsic-motivation baselines.  
- Ablations show the importance of latent integration and the MI coefficient; analyzes the effect of the switching horizon T.  
- Visitation plots and analyses show diverse locomotion primitives and that hierarchical rollouts cover much larger areas than Gaussian action noise.  
- SNN-based hierarchy generally outperforms a multi-policy hierarchy while using fewer pretraining samples.  
- Results include multiple seeds, variance reporting, and honest discussion of failure modes (e.g., Ant instability).  
- Decision: Accept — simple, novel combination, well-motivated, empirically supported, and code is provided.  
- Suggestion: Broaden evaluation beyond locomotion (e.g., manipulation) to support broader transfer claims.  
- Suggestion: Include stronger hierarchical baselines (e.g., option-critic) or explain why they’re inapplicable to these sparse-reward settings.  
- Suggestion: Equalize or normalize pretraining sample budgets when comparing to multi-policy baselines to avoid confounds.  
- Suggestion: Report quantitative diversity metrics (MI estimate, state coverage, behavior distances) alongside visitation plots.  
- Suggestion: Report sensitivity analyses for αH, grid resolution for p̂(Z|C), number of skills K, and commit horizon T in the main text.  
- Suggestion: Compare bilinear integration to deeper/wider concatenation networks to isolate whether gains are due to capacity or inductive bias.  
- Method detail request: Clarify smoothing of p̂(Z|C), grid resolution choice, and robustness to zero counts.  
- Method suggestion: Consider replacing discretized posterior with a learned classifier/density model or variational MI lower bound.  
- Suggestion: Explore learned termination policies or variable T and small experiments with end-to-end fine-tuning of low-level skills.  
- Discussion request: Address how to choose the subset C when CoM is not meaningful and potential failure cases when C is misspecified.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to C, grid resolution/smoothing, quantitative diversity vs downstream performance, equalized pretraining comparisons, sensitivity to K, impact of learned termination/variable T, bilinear vs larger concatenation, and transfer beyond locomotion.
