- Proposes pretraining reusable temporally-extended skills and a high-level manager that selects skills for sparse-reward downstream RL.
- Uses stochastic neural networks (SNNs) with a discrete latent z and bilinear integration between observation and z to represent multimodal skill-conditioned policies.
- Encourages skill diversity and interpretability via a mutual information (MI) regularizer between z and a subset of states (e.g., robot CoM), implemented as a per-step bonus using discretized p̂(z|c) counts.
- Freezes the low-level skill policy and trains a manager to commit to a skill every T steps, improving exploration and reducing effective horizon.
- Empirically shows SNN + bilinear + MI yields diverse, interpretable locomotion behaviors and dramatically larger exploration coverage.
- Demonstrates improved performance on sparse downstream tasks (mazes, gather) versus baselines, with ablations on latent integration and MI strength; code and videos provided.
- Decision: Accept.
- Key reason: Simple, well-motivated framework that learns diverse skills with minimal supervision and yields clear empirical gains on challenging sparse-reward tasks.
- Strong ablations and qualitative/quantitative evidence that SNN multimodality, bilinear integration, and MI regularization materially improve skill diversity and exploration.
- Problem relevance: Skill/options learning for exploration and transfer is central to RL and the unsupervised pretraining + hierarchical reuse framing is appropriate for ICLR.
- Novelty/positioning: Compelling integration of latent-conditioned SNNs and MI maximization on a semantically chosen state subset; pragmatic and easy-to-implement MI framing.
- Technical soundness: Treating z as part of the observation for TRPO is sensible; discretized MI bonus is a low-variance, aligned surrogate; hierarchical manager is standard and well-defined.
- Empirical evaluation: Multiple lines of evidence (visitation plots, coverage, learning curves, ablations, transfer to Snake); multiple seeds and variance analysis; candid discussion of Ant limitations.
- Clarity & reproducibility: Clear descriptions, pseudocode, hyperparameters, and released code aid reproducibility.
- Limitation: Domains are mostly locomotion-centric with CoM MI target, limiting claims about generality to manipulation or visual tasks.
- Limitation: MI estimator relies on discretization and tuning of α; sensitivity not fully quantified and high-dimensional C is problematic.
- Limitation: Manager uses fixed commit time T with no learned termination; skills are frozen and end-to-end fine-tuning is not explored.
- Limitation: Comparisons to contemporaneous hierarchical baselines (e.g., option-critic) are limited; pretraining variance across seeds can be non-trivial.
- Feedback: Report quantitative diversity metrics (e.g., MI achieved, coverage entropy, trajectory diversity) alongside visitation plots.
- Feedback: Expand sensitivity analyses for K (number of skills), MI α, discretization granularity, and bilinear dimensionality; report trade-offs.
- Feedback: Broaden comparisons to hierarchical baselines and curiosity/count-based methods with matched budgets.
- Feedback: Discuss generalization beyond locomotion and how to choose or learn MI target C (e.g., unsupervised embeddings).
- Feedback: Report sample complexity (env steps) and wall-clock comparisons for SNN training vs training multiple independent policies.
- Feedback: Consider and evaluate learned terminations or variable commitment times; provide bilinear integration implementation details for reproduction.
- Questions: How sensitive is performance to K, α, and cell grid resolution, and are there failure modes like redundancy or fragmentation?
- Questions: Can you provide quantitative diversity metrics and correlate them with downstream performance?
- Questions: How does the method handle high-dimensional C where discretization is impractical; any experiments with learned density models or regressors?
- Questions: How robust is transfer when pretraining dynamics differ moderately from downstream tasks?
- Questions: Did light end-to-end fine-tuning of the low-level SNN help reduce variance or aid unstable robots?
- Questions: Can you compare to option-critic or other hierarchical methods on a shared benchmark?
- Questions: For Ant, did longer commitment times, cautious switching, or learned switching policies stabilize downstream performance?
