- Proposes a two-phase hierarchical RL: pretrain a stochastic neural network (SNN) with a discrete latent code to learn diverse skills, then freeze skills and train a high-level Manager to select skills every T steps for downstream sparse-reward tasks.
- SNN uses bilinear integration of state and latent code to increase expressiveness and induce multimodal action distributions corresponding to distinct skills.
- Skill diversity is encouraged via a mutual-information (MI) regularizer approximated by a discretized posterior p̂(z|C) over a subset of state (e.g., CoM), encouraging low conditional entropy of Z given C.
- Treats latent z as part of the observation and trains with TRPO, sampling z per episode to yield temporally coherent skills.
- Experiments on MuJoCo locomotion (Swimmer, Snake; Ant as failure case) show SNN+MI produces interpretable, diverse skills (forward/backward/turning).
- Hierarchical composition with frozen skills substantially improves exploration coverage versus Gaussian action noise.
- Downstream learning curves show improved performance over non-hierarchical baselines and a proxy-reward baseline; ablations validate bilinear integration and the MI regularizer.
- Ant is reported as a failure mode due to instability on skill switches, highlighting limits on unstable agents.
- Decision: Weak Accept.
- Key reason 1: Simple, well-motivated, practically effective way to learn reusable temporally-extended primitives without task rewards; novel combo of SNN + MI + hierarchy.
- Key reason 2: Empirical results and informative ablations convincingly support core claims, with qualitative analysis of exploration and skill interpretability.
- Motivation/positioning: Targets exploration in long-horizon sparse-reward settings and connects appropriately to options, intrinsic motivation, and prior concurrent work.
- Methodology: Categorical latent and bilinear integration are well-reasoned; treating z as observation avoids TRPO marginalization issues.
- MI regularizer: Intuitive and computationally cheap via binned CoM posterior, effective in practice despite being heuristic.
- Manager design: Fixed-duration skill commitment (T steps) is standard and reduces effective horizon to boost exploration.
- Empirical support: Visitation plots and one-million-step random-manager trajectories demonstrate dramatically wider coverage than Gaussian noise.
- Empirical support: Reports multiple pretrain seeds (no cherry-picking), honest variance analysis, and some scaling evidence from Snake experiments.
- Limitation: Baselines and fairness—multi-policy baseline uses 6× more pretraining samples; missing comparisons to learned-option frameworks (option-critic, HiREPS) weaken claims.
- Limitation: Environment scope—mostly planar locomotion with privileged state and proxy reward (speed magnitude); MI bonus tailored to CoM limits generality (manipulation, pixels).
- Limitation: MI estimation via discretized posterior is ad hoc and potentially brittle; a learned classifier or variational lower bound would be more principled and scalable.
- Limitation: Transfer/sample-efficiency claims not fully substantiated—no total pretrain+downstream sample-cost plots or comparisons to K independent policies under equal budgets; downstream variance tied to pretrain seed.
- Limitation: Fixed commitment T and frozen low-levels can be limiting, especially for unstable agents and dynamic tasks.
- Suggested improvements: Add stronger baselines (option-critic, HiREPS-like, learned macros) under comparable budgets and report total sample complexity (pretrain+downstream).
- Suggested improvements: Replace count-based MI estimator with a learned discriminator qφ(z|c) and optimize a variational MI bound; quantify MI and diversity metrics.
- Suggested improvements: Broaden evaluation to non-planar C (manipulation, contacts), pixel observations with encoder, analyze sensitivity to K and αH, randomize initial orientations, and include significance tests.
- Suggested improvements: Add hierarchical enhancements—learned termination/variable T, limited end-to-end fine-tuning, and switching-aware pretraining to mitigate Ant instability.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity of results to choice of C and inclusion of orientation/velocities; effect of randomized initial heading.
- Questions for authors: how were K and αH chosen; evidence of diminishing returns or mode collapse for larger K?
- Questions for authors: report total sample budgets (pretrain+downstream) and learning curves vs environment steps for sample-efficiency claims.
- Questions for authors: performance with image observations—do you learn an encoder first and then apply bilinear integration with z?
- Questions for authors: include comparisons to option-critic or other hierarchical baselines under matched budgets?
- Questions for authors: did you try a learned q(z|s) classifier for MI estimation and did it improve stability vs grid-based estimator?
- Questions for authors: for Ant, did you try overlap between skills, behavior-cloning warm-starts, or switching-aware pretraining to reduce falls?
