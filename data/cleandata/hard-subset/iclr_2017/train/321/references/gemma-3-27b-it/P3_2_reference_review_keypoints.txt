- Paper proposes pre-training Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) to learn diverse skills with a proxy reward, then using those skills hierarchically to solve sparse-reward RL tasks.  
- Uses mutual-information (MI) regularization to encourage diverse, multi-modal skill discovery in SNNs.  
- Experiments on locomotion and maze tasks show the approach outperforms existing methods, especially in sparse-reward settings, with skill visualizations provided.  
- Code is released, aiding reproducibility.  
- Decision: accept with minor revisions — promising, well-motivated work but needs clarifications and stronger analysis.  
- Strength: addresses an important sparse-reward RL problem with a logical hierarchical approach that learns general skills before task-specific training.  
- Strength: novel combination of SNNs and MI regularization applied to skill learning and exploration in RL.  
- Strength: empirical results and qualitative visitation plots convincingly demonstrate improved performance.  
- Weakness: insufficient justification for why the chosen MI regularizer specifically produces useful skill diversity beyond encouraging exploration.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough ablation studies quantifying contributions of SNNs, MI regularizer, and hierarchical structure, and comparison to standard neural networks.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter sensitivity (especially MI weight alpha_H) is not explored, limiting understanding of robustness.  
- Weakness: evaluation tasks are relatively simple; scalability/generalization to higher-dimensional tasks (e.g., Ant) needs more detail and analysis.  
- Feedback: clarify and visually explain the bilinear integration mechanism for accessibility.  
- Feedback: provide detailed discussion of failure cases and instability (notably with Ant) and potential remedies.  
- Feedback: expand related work to include recent advances in skill discovery and hierarchical RL (e.g., option discovery with transformers).  
- Feedback: discuss limitations and adaptations for the key assumption of a factorable state space (S_agent vs S_rest).  
- Questions: why does maximizing MI between latent and robot CoM yield diverse/useful skills — any theoretical grounding or deeper intuition?  
- Questions: provide ablation results comparing SNNs to standard neural networks to quantify SNN benefits.  
- Questions: summarize Ant robot instability issues and potential fixes in the paper (beyond the technical report).  
- Questions: how does SNN training cost scale with state/action dimensionality and what strategies mitigate computational expense?
