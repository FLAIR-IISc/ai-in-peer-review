This paper proposes a hierarchical reinforcement learning (HRL) framework leveraging Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) to learn a diverse set of skills in a pre-training phase, subsequently used to accelerate learning in downstream tasks with sparse rewards. The core idea is to use SNNs to represent multi-modal policies, capturing a range of skills, and further encourage skill diversity through a mutual information (MI) regularizer. The learned skills are then utilized by a high-level policy for task-specific control.

**Reject.** While the motivation for addressing exploration in sparse reward environments and utilizing HRL is sound, the paper presents a complex approach with limited convincing evidence of its superiority over established methods. The experimental validation lacks sufficient rigor and comparison to strong baselines, and the benefits of SNNs and the MI regularizer are not demonstrably established.

The primary concern lies with the limited novelty and justification for the proposed method. HRL is a well-studied area, and the use of intrinsic rewards or skill discovery is not new. The authors position their work as combining these ideas, but the incremental contribution feels limited given the architectural complexity. Furthermore, the reliance on SNNs, a relatively niche area of neural networks, without a clear advantage over more standard approaches (e.g., variational autoencoders for latent skill spaces) is questionable. The authors claim that the bilinear integration is crucial for skill diversity, but the justification for this specific architectural choice is weak. The use of TRPO, a well-established algorithm, doesn't inherently validate the novelty of the surrounding framework.

The experimental results are presented with a lack of sufficient comparative analysis. The comparison to a baseline using a proxy reward is a positive step, but doesn’t provide strong evidence as this baseline isn’t a state-of-the-art intrinsic motivation approach. The performance gains observed on the maze and gather tasks are not substantial enough to justify the added complexity. Critically, the authors acknowledge the significant variability in performance based on the random seed used for pretraining, which casts doubt on the reliability of the results. The provided ablation studies (e.g., varying the switch time T) are insufficient to demonstrate the individual contribution of different components. The discussion around the instability issues encountered with the Ant robot further highlights the practical limitations of the proposed method. The claim of outperforming VIME (Houthooft et al., 2016) is made in the Gather task description, but not clearly substantiated with direct comparison figures.

**Additional Feedback (for potential improvement):**

*   **Stronger Baselines:** The paper would significantly benefit from a more comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art intrinsic motivation methods (e.g., VIME, RND) and HRL algorithms (e.g., Option-Critic).
*   **Ablation Studies:** Conduct thorough ablation studies to isolate the impact of each component of the framework – SNNs, bilinear integration, MI regularizer. Quantify the improvement gained by each addition.
*   **Statistical Significance:** Provide statistical significance tests (e.g., t-tests) for the performance comparisons to demonstrate that the observed improvements are not due to chance.
*   **Explain SNN Choice:** Provide a more compelling justification for choosing SNNs over alternative approaches for representing multi-modal policies. Why is the bilinear integration superior to simpler integration methods?
*   **Address Seed Sensitivity:** Investigate the reasons for the significant seed sensitivity and propose strategies to mitigate this issue.
*   **Clarify Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Discuss the sensitivity of the approach to key hyperparameters (e.g., the MI coefficient αH).
*   **Expand on Failure Cases:** Provide a more detailed analysis of the failure cases encountered with the Ant robot and potential solutions.

**Questions for the Authors:**

*   What is the computational overhead of using SNNs compared to standard neural networks in this framework?
*   Why was TRPO chosen as the optimization algorithm, and have other algorithms been explored?
*   Can you provide a more detailed explanation of why the bilinear integration is crucial for learning diverse skills?
*   What specific measures were taken to ensure the fairness of the comparison with the proxy reward baseline (e.g., equal hyperparameter tuning effort)?
*   What strategies could be employed to reduce the seed sensitivity observed in the experiments?
