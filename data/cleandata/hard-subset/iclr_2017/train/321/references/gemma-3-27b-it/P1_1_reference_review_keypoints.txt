- Paper proposes pre-training Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) to learn diverse skills with a proxy reward, then using those skills hierarchically to solve sparse-reward tasks, with MI regularization to encourage diversity.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Limited empirical advantage: reported improvements are marginal or comparable to baselines and not consistently convincing.  
- Lack of ablation studies: components like bilinear integration and MI bonus are not isolated to show individual contributions.  
- Sensitivity to hyperparameters and random seeds: performance varies greatly with pre-training quality, raising robustness concerns.  
- Insufficient experimental detail: neural network and TRPO configuration details are sparse, hindering reproducibility despite provided code.  
- Limited novelty: combination of SNNs, MI regularization, and hierarchical RL lacks clearly articulated unique contributions beyond prior work.  
- Clarify MI bonus role: theoretical justification is good but practical impact on learned skills is unclear and needs more analysis/visualization.  
- Explore alternative skill representations: compare SNNs to other methods (e.g., VAEs) for learning diverse skills.  
- Scalability concerns: unclear how approach scales to higher-dimensional or more complex environments.  
- Need more thorough baseline comparisons: compare against state-of-the-art intrinsic motivation and hierarchical RL methods on identical tasks/hyperparameters.  
- Address Ant robot instability: provide details on instability issues and potential fixes for Ant experiments.  
- Question: explain why bilinear integration is crucial and its advantages over simpler integration methods.  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to the MI coefficient (αH) and is there a principled tuning method?  
- Question: provide visualizations (e.g., t-SNE/PCA) of learned skill distributions to demonstrate diversity.  
- Question: why fix the switch time T, and how would adaptive T affect performance?  
- Question: clarify the impact of different random seeds on pretraining and strategies to improve skill consistency.  
- Question: provide link or details for the Ant robot technical report mentioned.
