- Summary: Proposes an HRL framework using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with mutual information (MI) regularization to pre-train diverse skills that accelerate downstream sparse-reward, long-horizon tasks.

- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — well-motivated, thorough experiments, clear benefit from SNNs and MI regularization.

- Motivation: Addresses critical sparse-reward, long-horizon RL problems by learning reusable skills to reduce exploration burden.

- Novelty: Uses SNNs for multi-modal policy representation and MI regularization to systematically encourage skill diversity.

- Empirical validation: Experiments on locomotion+maze and food-collection tasks show improvements over baselines; ablations and visitation plots support claims.

- Technical soundness: Methodology, architecture, MI regularizer, optimization, and implementation details are clearly described, aiding reproducibility.

- Clarify bilinear integration: Need a more intuitive explanation of why bilinear integration outperforms concatenation for skill diversity.

- Discuss failure cases: Expand analysis of Ant robot instability, underlying causes, and more potential mitigation strategies.

- MI coefficient ablation: Recommend a systematic study of the MI coefficient (αH) across tasks to balance diversity and task performance.

- Computational cost: Add discussion comparing training cost of SNNs versus traditional policy networks.

- Q on bilinear integration: Request intuitive or theoretical justification for bilinear integration improving skill diversity.

- Q on Ant robot: Ask whether Ant-specific environment or action-space characteristics caused instability and if additional stabilization techniques were tried.

- Q on scaling: Ask about anticipated challenges and necessary modifications for scaling to higher-dimensional, more complex tasks.

- Q on reproducibility: Ask for any special considerations or dependencies needed to reproduce results despite public code.
