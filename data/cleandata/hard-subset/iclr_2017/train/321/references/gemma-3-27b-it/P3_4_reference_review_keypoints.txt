- Tackles exploration in sparse-reward, long-horizon RL by pre-training diverse skills via Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with a mutual information (MI) regularizer, then training high-level policies on those skills.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Strong motivation: clearly addresses sparse rewards and long horizons, positioning well against hand-engineered hierarchies and intrinsic-reward methods.
- Novelty: combining SNNs with an MI regularizer for skill discovery is new and helps prevent mode collapse.
- Empirical validation: outperforms baselines on locomotion+maze and gathering tasks; ablations and visitation visualizations strengthen claims; Snake robot results show generalization.
- Presentation and reproducibility: well-written, method clearly explained, experimental details sufficient, and code released.
- Request more discussion of computational cost and how SNN complexity affects training time.
- Ask for analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity, especially the MI regularization strength (alpha).
- Want deeper theoretical justification for using MI, and links to frameworks like the information bottleneck.
- Suggest addressing Ant robot instability, e.g., via robustness-focused pre-training or recovery mechanisms in high-level policies.
- Question scalability to higher-dimensional state/action spaces and whether architecture changes are needed.
- Ask for guidelines on designing proxy rewards and the effects of poorly chosen proxies.
- Question robustness of pre-trained skills to distributional shifts and whether curriculum learning for pre-training would help.
- Ask whether other information-theoretic regularizers (e.g., total correlation) were tried and why MI was selected.
- Overall: strong, novel contribution with compelling empirical support; addressing the suggested feedback would further strengthen the paper.
