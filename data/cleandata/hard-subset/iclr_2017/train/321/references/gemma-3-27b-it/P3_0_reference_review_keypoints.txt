- Proposes HRL using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) to pre-train diverse low-level skills with a mutual information (MI) regularizer, then reuse them for downstream tasks.
- Decision: Reject — experimental validation is insufficient to support strong claims.
- Limited baselines: compares mainly to a simple intrinsic reward baseline, missing comparisons to Option-Critic, HIREPS, and other contemporary skill-discovery/exploration methods.
- High variance & sensitivity: large performance variance across SNN initializations with no clear explanation, raising robustness and reproducibility concerns.
- Insufficient ablation studies: needs analysis of bilinear integration choices, alternative regularizers, and per-component contributions.
- Lack of theoretical justification: paper is primarily empirical and lacks formal analysis for why SNNs or the MI regularizer are effective.
- Overstated claims: assertions of “clearly interpretable” and broadly transferable skills are subjective and may rely on chosen visualizations.
- Proxy reward concern: learned skills might be optimized for pre-training proxy reward and not truly transferable to downstream tasks.
- Recommendation: expand baselines to include recent and competitive HRL/exploration methods.
- Recommendation: investigate and reduce variance across initializations, e.g., averaging SNNs or stabilizing pre-training.
- Recommendation: run more comprehensive ablation studies to quantify contributions of architecture, MI, and integration methods.
- Recommendation: provide (even preliminary) theoretical analysis to strengthen claims.
- Recommendation: rigorously assess interpretability — verify skills are distinct and useful rather than minor variations.
- Recommendation: perform robustness analysis under noise and environmental perturbations.
- Questions for authors: what SNN characteristics drive gains vs deterministic nets; why initialization variance occurs and how to mitigate it; SNN training computational cost; sensitivity of MI hyperparameter αH; detailed action-level analysis of learned skills; downsides and mitigations for using a proxy reward.
