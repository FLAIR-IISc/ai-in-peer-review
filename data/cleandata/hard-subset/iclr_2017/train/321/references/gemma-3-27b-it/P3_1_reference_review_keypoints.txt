- Paper proposes pre-training Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) to learn diverse skills with a proxy reward, then training high-level policies on those skills for sparse-reward downstream tasks.
- Introduces a Mutual Information (MI) regularizer to encourage skill diversity during pre-training.
- Demonstrates significant performance improvements over baselines, especially in sparse reward locomotion and manipulation tasks.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Novelty: Using SNNs for multi-modal skill learning and weight sharing is a novel approach to exploration in HRL.
- Motivation: Pre-training skills in a proxy environment addresses limitations of hand-engineered hierarchies and task-specific priors.
- Technical soundness: Methodology, MI regularizer, bilinear integration, and TRPO optimization are logically consistent and well-justified.
- Empirical validation: Comprehensive experiments, comparisons to intrinsic motivation baselines, and ablation studies support the method’s effectiveness and generalizability.
- Transferability: Skills learned transfer well across multiple maze variations, strengthening generalization claims.
- Presentation: Paper is well-written, with informative figures/tables and provided code/videos aiding reproducibility.
- Limitation: Need a more thorough discussion of drawbacks, including computational cost and sensitivity to hyperparameters.
- Analysis gap: Quantitative measures of skill diversity (beyond visitation plots) are needed.
- Ablation gap: More detailed study varying MI coefficient (alpha) to show its impact on performance and diversity.
- Scalability concern: Training instability observed with complex robots (Ant) requires deeper analysis and mitigation strategies.
- Runtime question: Provide runtime and computational resource details for pre-training and fine-tuning phases.
- Question — bilinear integration: Why chosen over other integration methods (e.g., concatenation) and were alternatives tested?
- Question — MI coefficient: Is there a principled way to choose alpha or is it tuned empirically?
- Question — Ant robot: What specific architecture, reward shaping, or exploration changes would stabilize training for Ant?
- Question — switch time T: How does T influence downstream performance and does optimal T vary across tasks?
- Question — computational cost: What are approximate training times for pre-training and fine-tuning on the reported tasks?
