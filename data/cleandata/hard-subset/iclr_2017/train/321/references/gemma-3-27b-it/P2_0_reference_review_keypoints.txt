- Summary: Proposes using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with a mutual information (MI) regularizer to pre-train diverse skills for hierarchical RL, improving exploration and sample efficiency on sparse-reward tasks.  
- Decision: Recommend accept with minor revisions due to novelty, empirical gains, and solid presentation.  
- Clear problem & motivation: Well-motivated focus on exploration limits in sparse rewards and rationale for pre-training skills before task-specific learning.  
- Novel approach: Combining SNNs and an MI regularizer for skill diversity is original and theoretically justified for multimodal policies.  
- Strong empirical validation: Demonstrates improved performance and useful ablations on locomotion+maze/gather tasks and generalization to a Snake robot.  
- Well-written and structured: Paper is clear, organized, and provides sufficient methodological detail for reproduction.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Need more analysis on sensitivity and rationale for chosen hyperparameters, especially MI weight (alpha).  
- Computational cost: Missing discussion/analysis of training overhead for SNNs versus standard networks or separate skill policies.  
- Quantitative skill diversity: Recommend adding quantitative metrics (e.g., average pairwise distance or KL) beyond visitation plots.  
- Random seeds clarity: Results should explicitly state if they are averages over seeds and include standard deviations.  
- Failure case analysis: Expand on Ant robot skill-switching failures, underlying causes, and potential remedies.  
- Q1 (Hyperparameter tuning): Ask how hyperparameters, particularly alpha, were tuned and whether high alpha caused diminishing returns or instability.  
- Q2 (Skill transferability): Ask sensitivity of pre-trained skills to differences between pre-training and downstream environments.  
- Q3 (Computational cost): Request rough training time comparisons between SNN-based approach and training separate skill policies.  
- Q4 (Skill evaluation metrics): Ask whether other quantitative diversity metrics were tried and their outcomes.  
- Q5 (Ant robot fixes): Ask which modifications (transition policies, robust optimizers, switching mechanisms) the authors consider promising to address Ant instability.  
- Overall: Strong contribution to hierarchical RL; addressing the above suggestions would further strengthen impact.
