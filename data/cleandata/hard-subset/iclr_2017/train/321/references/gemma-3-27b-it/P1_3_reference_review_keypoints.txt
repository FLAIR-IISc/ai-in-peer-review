- Summary: Proposes a hierarchical RL framework using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) to pre-train diverse skills with an information-theoretic regularizer and use them to speed up downstream sparse-reward tasks.

- Decision: Reject (encouraged to revise and resubmit) — interesting idea but experimental validation and presentation are insufficient.

- Limited baselines: Compares mainly to weak baselines and claims outperforming VIME without providing quantitative in-paper comparisons to strong HRL methods.

- Presentation clarity: Paper is densely written, with inconsistent notation and unclear explanations (especially for the MI regularizer and bilinear integration).

- Result variability: Reported high variability (Appendix C.4) undermines claims of consistently learning diverse skills and lacks analysis of causes or remedies.

- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Sensitivity to hyperparameters (e.g., MI bonus weight, network architecture) is not systematically explored.

- Need for stronger baselines: Must implement/compare to state-of-the-art HRL algorithms and document any adaptations.

- Improve clarity: Rewrite for clarity, define notation consistently, and provide intuitive explanations for complex components.

- Investigate variability sources: Analyze whether issues stem from random seeds, hyperparameters, or other factors and propose mitigation strategies.

- Ablation studies: Provide systematic ablations to quantify contributions of SNN architecture, MI bonus, and bilinear integration.

- Better visualizations: Add latent-space visualizations (e.g., t-SNE) and more informative plots beyond visitation maps.

- Code and reproducibility: Ensure well-documented, reproducible code and clear experiment instructions.

- Discuss limitations: More thoroughly discuss applicability limits, environment/robot constraints, and failure modes.

- Question — Baseline comparison: Provide quantitative comparisons to VIME and at least one strong HRL baseline within the same framework.

- Question — Variability analysis: Identify specific causes of result variability and steps to mitigate them.

- Question — MI bonus sensitivity: Report sensitivity/grid-search results for the MI bonus weight (αH).

- Question — Latent-space visualization: Provide visualizations linking latent codes to learned skills.

- Question — Scaling to complex robots: Explain what modifications are needed to handle instability (e.g., with the Ant robot).

- Question — Computational cost: Report training compute/time for the SNN versus deterministic policies.
