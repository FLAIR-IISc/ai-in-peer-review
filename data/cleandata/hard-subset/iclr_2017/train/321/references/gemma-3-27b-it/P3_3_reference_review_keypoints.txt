- Tackles sparse-reward, long-horizon RL via hierarchical framework using SNNs to learn diverse low-level skills in pre-training and a high-level policy for downstream tasks.
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.
- Strong motivation: clearly identifies limitations of naive exploration and situates work within HRL and intrinsic motivation.
- Novelty/technical soundness: introduces SNNs + mutual information (MI) regularizer for skill diversity and uses bilinear integration for latent-action conditioning.
- Empirical validation: outperforms baselines on locomotion+maze and gather tasks, includes ablations on MI and SNN architecture, and demonstrates results on a complex Snake robot.
- Clear presentation: well-written with informative figures and tables.
- Suggest deeper analysis of MI regularizer impact, e.g., visualize state distributions per latent code.
- Request explicit discussion of computational cost and training overhead of SNNs versus standard policies.
- Ask for hyperparameter sensitivity study, especially for the MI coefficient (αH).
- Clarify how the proxy reward during pre-training shapes the learned skills and which proxy characteristics matter.
- Address Ant robot instability: discuss potential fixes like adaptive switching times or more robust SNN architectures.
- Question sensitivity of MI estimation to state-space discretization and whether alternative MI estimators were tried.
- Ask why bilinear integration was chosen over concatenation or other integration methods and whether comparisons were made.
- Suggest exploring manager fine-tuning across downstream tasks to further improve sample efficiency.
- Question scalability to higher-dimensional state/action spaces and limitations of the SNN-based approach.
- Ask if incorporating domain knowledge or shaping rewards in pre-training could mitigate Ant instability despite minimal-domain-knowledge goals.
