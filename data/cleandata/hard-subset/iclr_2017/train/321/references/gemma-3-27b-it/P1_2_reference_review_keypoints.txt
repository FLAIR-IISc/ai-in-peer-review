- Proposes using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with a mutual information (MI) regularizer for pre-training diverse skills in hierarchical RL to improve exploration in sparse-reward, long-horizon tasks.  
- Decision: Reject (encouraged to revise and resubmit) due to concerns about clarity, rigor, and reproducibility.  
- Motivation unclear: the paper does not convincingly explain why SNNs (beyond multi-modality) are particularly suited for hierarchical skill learning or how it distinctively builds on prior work.  
- Empirical evaluation lacks important baselines and analyses—no comparisons to stronger HRL methods (e.g., Option-Critic, HIRO) or recent exploration techniques.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity is under-analyzed; the impact of MI weight (αH), architecture, and TRPO settings is inconsistently reported.  
- Skill diversity claims are not fully supported—plots show skill overlap, especially without the MI bonus.  
- Results show high variance across SNN initializations, indicating instability in the method.  
- Reproducibility concerns: many hyperparameters, reliance on TRPO, and insufficient experimental protocol details hinder replication despite code release.  
- Scope is overly broad, sacrificing depth in theoretical justification, architectural choices, and experimental rigor.  
- Strengthen motivation by linking theoretical properties of SNNs and MI regularization directly to exploration and hierarchical learning benefits.  
- Include stronger comparative baselines (e.g., Option-Critic, HIRO) and more recent HRL/exploration methods.  
- Perform thorough hyperparameter sensitivity studies (αH, network design, TRPO) and analyze sources of variance across runs.  
- Provide detailed experimental setup: hyperparameter tuning procedures, training time, compute resources, and implementation specifics.  
- Clarify technical components, especially MI computation and bilinear integration in the SNN architecture.  
- Streamline the paper to focus on the most compelling contributions and address other reviewers’ comments in revision.  
- Questions for authors: specifics of Ant robot instability and fixes; strategies for automatically tuning αH; effects of alternative intrinsic rewards; computational scaling of bilinear integration to high-dimensional inputs; and quantitative sample-efficiency comparisons with different budgets.
