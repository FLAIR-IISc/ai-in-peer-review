## Review of "Stochastic Neural Networks for Hierarchical Reinforcement Learning"

**1. Summary:**

This paper tackles the challenge of sparse reward reinforcement learning, particularly in environments requiring long horizons. The authors propose a hierarchical reinforcement learning (HRL) framework that leverages Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) to learn a diverse set of low-level skills during a pre-training phase. These skills are then used as building blocks for a higher-level policy trained on downstream tasks with sparse rewards. The key innovation lies in using SNNs to represent multi-modal policies, enabling simultaneous learning of various skills, combined with a mutual information (MI) regularizer to encourage diversity.  The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on benchmark locomotion + maze and gather tasks, showing improved sample efficiency and performance compared to existing methods.

**2. Decision:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. The paper presents a compelling approach to HRL that effectively combines the benefits of SNNs and intrinsic motivation. The results are promising, demonstrating significant improvements in sparse reward environments. The use of MI regularization to encourage skill diversity is particularly noteworthy.

**3. Supporting Arguments:**

* **Clear Problem Definition and Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the problem of sparse rewards and long horizons in RL and provides a strong motivation for hierarchical approaches. The limitations of existing exploration strategies and HRL methods (reliance on domain knowledge, lack of transferability) are well-explained.
* **Novelty and Technical Soundness:** The use of SNNs for skill learning is a novel contribution. The authors correctly identify the benefit of SNNs in representing multi-modal policies and explicitly address the need to encourage diversity through the MI regularizer. The bilinear integration method seems justified and reasonable.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results on the locomotion + maze and gather tasks are convincing. The authors demonstrate that their framework outperforms existing baselines (including VIME).  The ablation studies (showing the impact of bilinear integration and MI bonus) are helpful in understanding the contribution of each component.  The comparison against other methods, while not exhaustive, is sufficient to establish the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
* **Code Availability:** Providing code is crucial for reproducibility and further research, and the authors have done so.

**4. Additional Feedback (for improvement):**

* **Clarify MI Implementation:** The explanation of the MI calculation could be more detailed. Specifically, how the state space is discretized and the potential impact of the bin size on the results should be discussed.
* **Robustness Analysis:** While the experiments show promising results, a more thorough robustness analysis would be valuable. Investigating the sensitivity of the approach to different hyperparameters (e.g., the MI weight) and the impact of the pre-training environment on downstream task performance would strengthen the paper.
* **Ablation Study – End-to-End Training:** The authors mention end-to-end training as a potential future direction. A brief ablation study exploring the benefits of joint optimization of the low and high-level policies would be a valuable addition.  Even preliminary results could demonstrate the potential of this approach.
* **Theoretical Justification:** While the empirical results are strong, some theoretical justification for why SNNs are particularly well-suited for this task would be beneficial. Are there any theoretical guarantees regarding the diversity of learned skills?
* **Discussion of Failure Cases:** The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of failure cases.  For example, why does the method perform differently on different maze configurations? The mention of instability with the Ant robot and the link to the technical report is good, but a brief summary within the paper would be helpful.



**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  The MI regularization seems sensitive to the discretization of the state space. How was the bin size chosen, and did you perform a sensitivity analysis to assess its impact on the learned skills?
2.  You mention that the bilinear integration improves skill diversity.  Could you elaborate on *why* this integration is more effective than simple concatenation? Is there an intuitive explanation or theoretical basis for this?
3.  In the ablation studies, you show the impact of the MI bonus. Did you explore the effect of different weight values for the MI bonus, and if so, what did you observe?
4.  The performance on the Maze tasks seems to vary depending on the specific maze configuration. Could you explain this observation? Are some maze layouts more conducive to the learned skills than others?
5.  You briefly mention instability issues with the Ant robot. Could you provide a more concrete example of the types of failures that occur and how you attempted to address them (even if unsuccessfully)?



Overall, this is a well-written and well-motivated paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of hierarchical reinforcement learning. The use of SNNs and MI regularization is a clever and effective approach, and the empirical results are promising. Addressing the feedback and answering the questions above will further strengthen the paper and increase its impact.
