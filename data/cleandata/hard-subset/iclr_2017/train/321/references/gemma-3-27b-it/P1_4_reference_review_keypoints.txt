- Summary: Proposes HRL with Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) pre-trained with proxy rewards to learn diverse skills, using MI regularization and a high-level policy for sparse downstream tasks.  
- Decision: Reject (encouraged to revise and resubmit) due to insufficient experimental rigor and presentation issues.  
- Insufficient baselines & comparison: Lacks direct comparison to strong, recent HRL and pre-training-based skill discovery methods (e.g., Heess et al., 2016).  
- Experimental rigor & statistical significance: Results vary substantially across seeds (notably on Ant); paper lacks error bars and statistical tests to support claims.  
- Lack of ablation studies: No thorough ablation to quantify contributions of SNNs, bilinear integration, and MI regularizer or sensitivity to MI coefficient.  
- Overly complex presentation: Dense, hard-to-follow exposition with heavy notation; needs clearer, more gradual explanations.  
- Clarify the novelty: Authors should specify what is new—combination of SNNs, MI regularization, pre-training setup, or overall framework.  
- Expand experimentation: Include more challenging downstream tasks, additional HRL baselines, ablations, varying latent count (K), and alternative pre-training environments.  
- Improve presentation: Simplify notation, use descriptive variable names, clearer explanations, diagrams, and remove redundancy.  
- Address Ant stability: Provide detailed analysis of failure modes for Ant and explore remedies (e.g., damping, online skill adaptation).  
- Question — baseline selection: Explain rationale for chosen baselines and omission of specific relevant HRL methods.  
- Question — MI regularization sensitivity: Report sensitivity to αH and whether grid search was performed to tune it.  
- Question — SNN architecture choices: State whether other SNN variants were tried and their outcomes.  
- Question — Ant instability details: Describe steps taken to diagnose/mitigate Ant instability and likely causes.  
- Question — statistical significance: Provide statistical analyses (error bars, p-values) to substantiate performance claims.
