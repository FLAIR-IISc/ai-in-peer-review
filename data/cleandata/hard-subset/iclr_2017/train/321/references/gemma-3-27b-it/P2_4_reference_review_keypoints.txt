- Summary: Tackles sparse-reward, long-horizon RL by pretraining diverse low-level skills with Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) and an MI regularizer, then training a high-level policy on downstream tasks; shows improved sample efficiency on locomotion+maze and gather tasks.

- Decision: Accept with minor revisions — promising approach and results; MI regularization is a notable contribution.

- Clear problem definition and motivation: Well-articulated limitations of existing exploration and HRL methods.

- Novelty and technical soundness: SNNs for multi-modal skill learning is novel; MI regularizer and bilinear integration are reasonable and justified.

- Empirical validation: Outperforms baselines (including VIME), with useful ablations showing contributions of bilinear integration and MI bonus; code is available.

- Clarify MI implementation: Explanation lacking detail on state-space discretization and bin-size choice/impact.

- Robustness analysis needed: Sensitivity to hyperparameters (e.g., MI weight) and dependence on pre-training environment should be investigated.

- Ablation on end-to-end training: Recommend exploring joint optimization of low- and high-level policies, even with preliminary results.

- Theoretical justification: Provide theory or guarantees explaining why SNNs yield diverse skills.

- Discussion of failure cases: More detail needed on per-maze performance differences and instability issues (e.g., Ant robot), with summaries in the paper.

- Q1: How was MI discretization/bin size chosen and how sensitive are results to it?

- Q2: Why does bilinear integration improve skill diversity versus simple concatenation — any intuition or theory?

- Q3: What effects were observed when varying the MI bonus weight in ablations?

- Q4: Why does performance vary across different maze configurations; are some layouts more conducive to learned skills?

- Q5: What specific failures occurred with the Ant robot and what mitigation attempts were made?

- Overall: Well-written, valuable contribution to HRL; addressing the above feedback will further strengthen the paper.
