- Paper proposes pretraining Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with a mutual information (MI) regularizer to learn diverse skills, then uses those skills in hierarchical RL for sparse-reward, long-horizon tasks.  
- Decision: Accept (Weak).  
- Novelty & Motivation: Combining SNNs with an MI regularizer for skill discovery is novel and well-motivated.  
- Technical Soundness: Use of SNNs for multi-modal policies and MI regularization is theoretically sound and consistent.  
- Empirical Results: Shows improvements and convincing skill visualizations; gains over strong baselines are not always large.  
- Strength — Clear problem definition: The sparse-reward HRL challenge is well articulated.  
- Strength — Well-defined framework: Integrates SNNs, MI regularization, and hierarchical policy learning effectively.  
- Strength — Reproducibility: Code availability is a significant plus.  
- Statistical Significance: Lacks robust statistics—needs means, standard deviations, and significance tests presented in the main text.  
- Baseline Comparisons: Comparisons are incomplete; more and clearer justification for chosen baselines and SOTA comparisons required.  
- Hyperparameter Sensitivity: Missing analysis of sensitivity to MI bonus coefficient (αH) and switch time (T).  
- Ablation Studies: Need ablations to isolate the contributions of the MI regularizer and the SNN vs. standard networks.  
- Clarity of Presentation: Paper is dense; needs simpler language, clearer figures, and more intuitive explanations (especially bilinear integration).  
- Failure Cases: Briefly notes Ant robot limitations—needs deeper analysis of failure modes and potential fixes.  
- Impact of Pretraining: Should analyze pre-trained skill quality and how skill diversity correlates with downstream performance.  
- Question — Statistical table: Provide a table with p-values/statistical significance vs. baselines.  
- Question — MI coefficient: How sensitive is performance to αH and was a hyperparameter sweep performed?  
- Question — Computational cost: What is the training cost of the SNN vs. a standard neural network of similar size?  
- Question — Switch time T: Provide experiments for different T values and justify the chosen T in main experiments.  
- Question — Bilinear integration: Give a clearer, intuitive explanation of why bilinear integration helps skill diversity.
