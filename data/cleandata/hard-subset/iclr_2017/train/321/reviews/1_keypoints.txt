- Interesting hierarchical-control contribution (similar to Heess et al.); experiments are strong and solve benchmarks previous work could not, but the experimental analysis is weak.

- The term "intrinsic motivation" is misleading here — pre-training with speed/grasp rewards is task-aligned and resembles prior pre-training (Heess et al.), not true intrinsic motivation.

- Mutual information regularization is elegant and usually helpful, but it does not improve performance on the more complex mazes (1–3); the paper notes this but lacks interpretation or analysis.

- The factorization between S_agent and S_rest must be clearly specified for reproducibility — Duan et al. define S_agent, but S_rest is not clearly detailed.

- The paper needs more analysis of switching behavior and policies (failure modes, and how switching timing affects performance).
