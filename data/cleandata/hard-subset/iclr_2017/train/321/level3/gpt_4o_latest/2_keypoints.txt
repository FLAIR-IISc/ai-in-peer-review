- Novel HRL framework using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) and an information-theoretic (mutual information) regularizer to learn diverse sub-policies during pre-training.  
- Learned skills are reused in downstream sparse-reward tasks via a hierarchical policy, improving exploration and performance.  
- Bilinear integration and MI bonuses are highlighted as critical components for achieving skill diversity.  
- Empirical results show improved sample efficiency and success on challenging tasks (mazes, food collection) compared to strong baselines.  
- Code and videos provided, and experiments are reproducible.  
- Decision: Accept — paper is novel, scientifically rigorous, and empirically validated.  
- Strength: Tackles relevant problem of exploration in sparse-reward, long-horizon RL with innovative use of SNNs and MI.  
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments across multiple tasks convincingly demonstrate superiority over baselines.  
- Strength: Methodology is well-grounded with clear explanations of SNNs, bilinear integration, and MI regularization.  
- Limitation: Applicability to non-locomotion tasks that require more diverse sub-policies is unclear.  
- Limitation: Fixed sub-policies and fixed switching intervals may limit adaptability in dynamic environments.  
- Limitation: Reliance on domain-specific proxy intrinsic rewards during pre-training raises generalizability concerns.  
- Question: Why do smooth intrinsic rewards fail compared to the proposed method, and were baseline design choices a factor?  
- Question: How sensitive is downstream performance to the quality of pre-trained policies and random-seed variability?  
- Question: Can the authors clarify concatenation vs. bilinear integration in Figure 1 and how critical integration choice is for skill diversity?  
- Question: How do multi-policy approaches compare to SNNs in performance and sample efficiency, and when is each preferable?
