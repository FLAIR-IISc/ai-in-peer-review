- Proposed hierarchical RL: SNN skill pre-training with an MI regularizer plus a high-level policy for sparse-reward downstream tasks, evaluated on mazes and object collection.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main weaknesses: poor clarity, insufficient analysis, and limited reproducibility.  
- Weak analysis: paper lacks explanation for failures in complex mazes and the MI regularizer’s interaction with SNNs.  
- Terminology issue: calling the pre-training "intrinsic motivation" is misleading—it's task-specific proxy rewards, not true intrinsic motivation.  
- Reproducibility gap: factorization between S_agent and S_rest is under-specified; S_rest is vague.  
- Suggest deeper analysis of switching behavior: study failure modes and the impact of switching time T on performance.  
- Investigate MI regularizer failure: assess whether discretization or other factors limit its effectiveness in complex environments.  
- Broader evaluation needed: test on more tasks and robot types to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Consider end-to-end or adaptive switching: fixed sub-policies and switching times limit flexibility and performance.  
- Questions for authors: how does MI discretization choice affect performance; provide details on S_rest factorization and its generalization; have alternative architectures or regularizers been considered to improve SNN skill diversity?
