- Proposes a novel hierarchical RL framework combining intrinsic motivation and hierarchical methods for pre-training skills and reusing them in downstream tasks.
- Uses Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with an information-theoretic regularizer to pre-train a diverse, interpretable span of skills.
- Claims skills are learnable with minimal supervision and enable strong performance on long-horizon, sparse-reward tasks when used hierarchically.
- Strength: novel combination of intrinsic-motivation-style pre-training with hierarchical architectures.
- Strength: strong empirical results demonstrating effectiveness on challenging sparse-reward tasks.
- Strength: elegant and effective use of SNNs plus mutual-information regularization for diverse skill pre-training.
- Weakness: labeling the pre-training as “intrinsic motivation” is misleading because speed/grasp rewards are task-aligned and resemble prior pre-training.
- Weakness: experimental analysis is weak—insufficient interpretation of results and of the role of mutual-information regularization.
- Weakness: unclear definition and factorization of S_agent vs. S_rest, harming reproducibility.
- Weakness: lacks analysis of switching behavior and policies, including failure modes and the impact of switching timing.
- Decision: Reject.
- Reasons for rejection: insufficient clarity and depth in result analysis (mutual information and switching), misleading use of “intrinsic motivation,” and missing specification of S_agent/S_rest factorization.
- Additional feedback: clarify interpretation of mutual-information regularizer and switching behavior, explicitly define S_agent and S_rest factorization, analyze failure modes and switching timing effects, and better situate contributions within hierarchical RL.
