- Summary: Proposes hierarchical RL using stochastic neural networks to pretrain diverse skills and apply them to sparse-reward tasks (locomotion, mazes, object gathering).  
- Decision: Reject, but paper has high potential for resubmission after addressing concerns.  
- Main rejection reasons: unclear state-space discretization, questionable generality to complex morphologies, and hand-designed proxy rewards.  
- Discretization concern: Paper implies grid-based discretization but doesn’t explain whether the state is discretized, why, or how continuous states are handled.  
- Generality concern: Experiments use simple morphologies (Swimmer, Snake); unclear if method scales to more complex agents (e.g., 5-link swimmer, Ant).  
- Reward design concern: Proxy reward appears hand-crafted, contradicting claims of minimal domain knowledge; need explanation of design process and transferability.  
- Failure modes: Paper lacks detailed analysis of failure cases, especially instability or tasks requiring precise navigation.  
- Visualizations: More visualizations of learned skills and their deployment in downstream tasks are needed.  
- Baseline comparisons: Should include more detailed comparison to other hierarchical RL approaches (e.g., Option-Critic).  
- Compute details: Missing information on computational resources required to train SNNs and the Manager Network.  
- Question — proxy reward: Please explain the proxy reward design process and its generalizability to other tasks.  
- Question — discretization: How is discretization handled, and what are the implications for continuous state spaces?  
- Question — generality: Please provide evidence or experiments showing scalability to more complex morphologies.  
- Question — instability: How does the method handle robot instability, falls, or tasks needing precise control?
