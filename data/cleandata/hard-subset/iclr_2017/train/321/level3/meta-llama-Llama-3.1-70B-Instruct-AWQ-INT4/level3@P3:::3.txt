**Paper Review: Stochastic Neural Networks for Hierarchical Reinforcement Learning**

**Summary:**
This paper proposes a framework for hierarchical reinforcement learning using stochastic neural networks (SNNs) to learn a diverse set of skills in a pre-training environment. The learned skills are then utilized in a hierarchical architecture to solve challenging tasks with sparse rewards. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach in various tasks, including locomotion, maze navigation, and object gathering.

**Decision:**
I decide to reject this paper, but with high potential for resubmission after addressing the concerns raised below. The main reasons for rejection are the lack of clarity on the discretization of the state space, concerns about the generality of the approach to more complex morphologies, and the hand-design of rewards.

**Supporting Arguments:**

1. **Discretization:** The paper implies a grid-based discretization of the state space in Section 5.3, but it is unclear whether the state space is indeed discretized, why this is necessary, and what happens if the state space is continuous. The authors should provide a clear explanation of their discretization approach and its implications.
2. **Generality:** The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the approach on relatively simple morphologies, such as the Swimmer and Snake robots. However, it is unclear whether the approach scales to more complex morphologies, such as the 5-link swimmer or the Ant robot. The authors should provide more evidence of the generality of their approach.
3. **Hand-design of rewards:** The authors claim that their approach requires minimal domain knowledge, but the design of the proxy reward appears to be hand-crafted and tailored to specific control tasks. The authors should provide more insight into the design process of the proxy reward and its generalizability to other tasks.

**Additional Feedback:**

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the failure modes of the approach, particularly in cases where the robot is unstable or the task requires precise navigation.
* The authors should consider providing more visualizations of the learned skills and their application in the downstream tasks.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other hierarchical reinforcement learning approaches, such as the Option-Critic architecture.
* The authors should consider providing more details on the computational resources required to train the SNNs and the Manager Network.

**Questions for the Authors:**

* Can you provide more insight into the design process of the proxy reward and its generalizability to other tasks?
* How do you plan to address the issue of discretization in the state space, and what are the implications of this approach?
* Can you provide more evidence of the generality of your approach to more complex morphologies?
* How do you plan to address the issue of instability in the robot, particularly in cases where the robot may fall over or require precise navigation?
