- Paper proposes a hierarchical RL framework that pre-trains skills via a single proxy reward and uses a high-level policy over those skills to improve exploration on sparse-reward downstream tasks.  
- Approach is well-motivated for long-horizon and sparse-reward problems and combines intrinsic motivation and hierarchical methods.  
- Related work coverage is clear and comprehensive, highlighting limitations of existing methods and benefits of the proposed approach.  
- Experiments (mazes, gathering) show the framework learns diverse skills and uniformly boosts downstream learning performance.  
- Detailed analysis of design choices (latent variables, mutual information bonus) is provided and affects skill quality.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on originality, significance, clarity, and strong experimental results.  
- Request for more analysis of switching behavior and policies, including failure modes and the impact of switching timing on performance.  
- Need clear specification of factorization between S_agent and S_rest for reproducibility across tasks.  
- Concern that calling the pre-training “intrinsic motivation” is misleading since speed/grasp rewards are task-aligned and resemble prior pre-training.  
- Mutual information regularization did not improve performance on complex mazes; reviewer asks for deeper interpretation and analysis of this result.  
- Suggest additional experiments to demonstrate robustness of the framework across different environments and tasks.  
- Questions for authors: provide details on how S_agent / S_rest factorization is specified for each task.  
- Questions for authors: explain plans to address switching behavior and policies in more complex tasks/environments.  
- Questions for authors: provide more interpretation of mutual information regularization and its impact.  
- Questions for authors: discuss how the framework would scale to more complex tasks and anticipated challenges.  
- Minor feedback: fix typos, improve some figure/table captions, and better connect some references to the proposed framework.
