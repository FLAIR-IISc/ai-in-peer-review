- Proposes a hierarchical RL framework using stochastic neural networks (SNNs) and an information-theoretic regularizer to learn diverse skills.  
- Demonstrates these skills enable solving challenging tasks with sparse rewards.  
- Main contribution: a general framework combining unsupervised skill learning with a hierarchical architecture.  
- Reviewer recommends accept, citing strong empirical results on sparse-reward tasks.  
- Thorough evaluation including comparisons to state-of-the-art, showing significant performance improvements.  
- Strength: effective integration of hierarchical RL, SNNs, and information-theoretic regularization with clear motivation and design choices.  
- Paper is well-written with a comprehensive related-work discussion and acknowledgement of limitations.  
- Empirical analysis is detailed, evaluating component effects (e.g., regularizer, integration method).  
- Limitation: reliance on a fixed sub-policy setup may limit sub-policy diversity.  
- Limitation: manager trained with fixed sub-policies and time scales may force dependence on strong intrinsic rewards and prelearned policies.  
- Requested clarifications: explain poor performance of the strong baseline in Sec 7.3 and whether it used extra experience; define “multi-policy” vs “snn”; confirm Fig.1’s concatenation vs bilinear integration effects on first-layer bias/input weights.
