- Decision: Accept.

- Novel approach: Proposes a hierarchical RL framework using stochastic neural networks and an information-theoretic regularizer to learn diverse skills.

- Practical utility: Skills learned in pre-training are reused to solve sparse-reward downstream tasks.

- Empirical evidence: Strong experiments showing improved performance on maze navigation and object gathering versus baselines.

- Clarity: Paper is well-written and explains the architecture and regularizer clearly.

- Related work: Thorough analysis highlighting limitations of prior methods and the paper's contributions.

- Experimental quality: Results demonstrate the ability to learn diverse skills and tackle challenging tasks.

- Benefits claimed: Improved exploration and reduced sample complexity from the approach.

- Minor issues: Some formatting and grammar errors need fixing.

- Implementation details lacking: More specifics on network architectures and hyperparameters are needed.

- Limitations missing: Paper should analyze potential failure modes and areas for improvement.

- Multi-policy clarification: More context required on how the mentioned multi-policy approach differs from the proposed method.

- Extensibility question: Authors should explain plans to scale the approach to higher-dimensional, more complex domains.

- Questions for authors (implementation): Provide architectures and hyperparameters for the stochastic network and regularizer.

- Questions for authors (limitations & future work): How will you address limitations and extend to complex tasks and domains?
