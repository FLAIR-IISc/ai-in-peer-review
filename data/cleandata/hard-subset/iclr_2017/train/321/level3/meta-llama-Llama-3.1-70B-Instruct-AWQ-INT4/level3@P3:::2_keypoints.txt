- Proposes an HRL framework using stochastic neural networks (SNNs) with an information-theoretic regularizer to learn diverse skills in pre-training and apply them to sparse-reward tasks.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on robotic tasks (locomotion, maze navigation, object manipulation).  
- Decision: accept (score 7).  
- Strong empirical findings: comprehensive experiments show the framework outperforms state-of-the-art intrinsic motivation methods and improves exploration and learning.  
- Well-motivated approach: combines intrinsic motivation and hierarchical methods; SNNs and the information-theoretic regularizer are well-justified.  
- SNNs enable flexible weight-sharing and learning of multi-modal policies.  
- The information-theoretic regularizer encourages skill diversity and helps prevent mode collapse.  
- Hierarchical structure enables skill reuse across tasks and reduces sample complexity.  
- Limitation: requires careful tuning of hyperparameters, especially the mutual information bonus.  
- Limitation: risk of mode collapse in the SNN when the number of skills is large.  
- Limitation: limited ability to handle very long-horizon tasks or highly complex dynamics.  
- Requested improvement: analyze the impact of switch time T on performance, particularly for long-horizon/complex tasks.  
- Requested improvement: provide more detailed comparisons with other HRL methods and alternative network/regularization choices.  
- Requested improvement: discuss potential applications beyond robotics (e.g., game playing, recommendation systems).  
- Questions for authors: provide implementation details of the information-theoretic regularizer, mutual information bonus choice, and state discretization.  
- Questions for authors: how to handle very large numbers of skills and mitigate SNN mode collapse?  
- Questions for authors: provide analysis of robustness to different types of environmental noise or perturbations.
