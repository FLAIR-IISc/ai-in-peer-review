- Proposes hierarchical RL: pre-train diverse skills with Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) using a proxy reward, then train a high-level policy to compose skills for sparse-reward tasks.  
- Shows effectiveness on mazes and gathering tasks, reportedly outperforming state-of-the-art intrinsic motivation methods.  
- Decision: accept with minor revisions.  
- Strength: well-motivated approach addressing skill diversity for sparse rewards with clear explanation and strong experiments.  
- Weakness: labeling the proxy reward as “intrinsic motivation” is misleading because it is not truly intrinsic.  
- Weakness: unclear interpretation of results in complex mazes where mutual information regularization does not improve performance.  
- Weakness: insufficient details on factorization between S_agent and S_rest, harming reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: provide detailed description of the S_agent / S_rest factorization and how it is used during pre-training.  
- Suggestion: clarify and justify the relationship between the method and intrinsic motivation.  
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis of switching behavior and policies, including failure modes and timing effects.  
- Suggestion: report full experimental setup details — hyperparameters and computational resources.  
- Question: how will the approach be extended to more complex tasks and environments?
