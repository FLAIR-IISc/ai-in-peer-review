- Proposed framework: pre-train diverse skills with a proxy reward, then learn downstream tasks via a high-level policy using SNNs and an information-theoretic regularizer.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main concern 1: Method may fail as behavior richness increases; scalability to complex tasks not demonstrated.  
- Main concern 2: Unclear discretization of state/space (grid implied); unclear handling of continuous spaces and generality.  
- Supporting: Proxy reward appears hand-crafted and task-specific, contradicting the claim of minimal domain knowledge.  
- Supporting: Experiments are limited to simple tasks and do not show performance on more challenging environments.  
- Supporting: No clear justification why SNNs and the information-theoretic regularizer are necessary or sufficient for diverse skill learning.  
- Suggestion: Provide more empirical evidence of scalability to complex tasks.  
- Suggestion: Clarify whether and how the state/space is discretized and why that choice is necessary.  
- Suggestion: Give detailed design and motivation for the proxy reward and argue it is not hand-crafted.  
- Suggestion: Evaluate the method on more complex, varied tasks.  
- Questions for authors: provide scalability evidence, explain discretization, detail proxy reward design, and describe how failure modes will be addressed as behavior richness increases.
