- Proposes a hierarchical RL framework that pre-trains skills in a proxy environment and reuses them for faster downstream learning.  
- Combines intrinsic motivation and hierarchical methods, using a single proxy reward to guide skill learning.  
- Uses Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with an information-theoretic regularizer to pre-train a diverse set of skills.  
- Main contribution claimed: combination of SNNs and information-theoretic regularization to learn diverse behaviors.  
- Decision: reviewer recommends rejection due to concerns about generality and potential failure modes.  
- Concern: approach may fail as behavior richness increases, limiting the diversity of discovered skills.  
- Concern: claim of “minimal domain knowledge” is disputed because intrinsic rewards appear hand-crafted and task-specific.  
- Experimental results (maze navigation, object gathering, 5-link snake) are promising but insufficient to demonstrate generality or scalability to complex morphologies.  
- Unclear whether state/space in Section 5.3 is discretized (implied grid); need clarification on why and how this affects continuous environments.  
- Paper is well written and clear, but lacks thorough analysis of limitations and failure modes.  
- Request for additional analysis on the impact of the switch time T on performance.  
- Suggested improvements: analyze failure modes as behavior richness grows; clarify discretization and continuous-setting implications; run more diverse experiments; detail reward hand-design.  
- Questions for authors: clarify discretization in Sec 5.3 and implications for continuous spaces; how to address failure modes as behavior richness increases; provide more diverse experiments to test scalability; respond to criticism that intrinsic rewards are hand-crafted.
