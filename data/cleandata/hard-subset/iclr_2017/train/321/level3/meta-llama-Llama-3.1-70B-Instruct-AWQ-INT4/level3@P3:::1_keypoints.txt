- Proposal: HRL framework that pre-trains diverse skills via SNNs with a mutual-information regularizer using a proxy reward, then composes those skills for sparse-reward downstream tasks.  
- Decision: Recommend rejection, mainly due to weak experimental analysis and misleading use of "intrinsic motivation."  
- Weak experiments: Insufficient analysis of switching behavior, failure modes, switching timing effects, and unclear interpretation of the mutual-information regularizer.  
- Misleading terminology: The proxy pre-training reward is task-aligned, not a true intrinsic motivation signal as traditionally defined.  
- Clarify factorization: The assumed factorization between S_agent and S_rest is underspecified and its impact on results is unclear.  
- More switching analysis: Need deeper evaluation of how switching timing affects performance and where it fails or succeeds.  
- Alternative architectures: Explore other Manager network designs (e.g., recurrent networks) beyond the simple feed-forward used.  
- Regularizer details: Provide implementation details and empirical effects of the information-theoretic (mutual information) regularizer.  
- Q1: How exactly is the S_agent vs S_rest factorization defined and how does it influence outcomes?  
- Q2: How will switching be managed for unstable agents (e.g., Ant) to avoid destabilization?  
- Q3: Can you provide detailed analysis of switching timing effects, including failure modes and success cases?  
- Q4: How will the method be scaled or extended to more complex tasks and environments?
