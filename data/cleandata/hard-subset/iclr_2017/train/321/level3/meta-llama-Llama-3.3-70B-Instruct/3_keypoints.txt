- Proposes ENet, a novel deep neural network architecture for real-time semantic segmentation on low-power mobile devices.
- ENet achieves state-of-the-art results on Cityscapes, CamVid, and SUN RGB-D while using significantly fewer FLOPs and parameters.
- Reviewer recommends Accept, citing impressive performance gains and mobile deployment potential.
- Primary concerns about some design choices and the evaluation methodology.
- Paper is well-motivated and includes a thorough analysis of design choices and experimental results.
- Evaluation across multiple benchmarks provides a comprehensive assessment of ENet’s performance.
- Requests clearer discussion of ENet’s failure model: behavior on complex scenes or poorly represented objects.
- Asks whether the input/representation space is discretized, and what implications discretization has on performance.
- Questions the generality of the approach and whether ENet applies to more complex embodiments (e.g., a 5-link swimmer).
- Challenges the claim that intrinsic rewards require minimal domain knowledge, arguing the proposed rewards appear hand-crafted and task-specific.
- Requests from authors: details on failure modes, effects of discretization, experiments on more complex embodiments, and justification/design of the rewards.
