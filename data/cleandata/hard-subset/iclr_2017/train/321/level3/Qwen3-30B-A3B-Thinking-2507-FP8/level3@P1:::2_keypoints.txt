- Paper proposes a hierarchical RL framework that pre-trains diverse skills with a proxy reward and uses them via a high-level Manager for sparse downstream tasks.  
- Architecture uses Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) for low-level skills and a Manager to select among them.  
- Key innovation: an information-theoretic regularizer (mutual information bonus) to encourage skill diversity.  
- Empirical claim: approach significantly improves exploration and learning on sparse-reward tasks (mazes, object gathering).  
- Method outperforms standard RL and intrinsic-reward baselines across multiple tasks.  
- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Primary reasons for acceptance: meaningful contribution to sparse-reward RL, well-motivated framework, theoretical soundness, and strong empirical validation.  
- Strength: addresses need for domain-agnostic skill learning without hand-designed rewards, avoiding domain-specific skill specification.  
- Ablations (concatenation vs bilinear, MI bonus) provide useful design insights.  
- Important empirical finding: directly combining intrinsic and sparse rewards fails, highlighting value of hierarchical abstraction and enforced skill diversity.  
- Visualizations (visitation plots) effectively demonstrate interpretability and diversity of learned skills.  
- Concern 1: skill diversity appears limited (especially with concatenation/bilinear) and experiments are confined to locomotion/simple mazes; need more analysis or experiments in complex manipulation tasks.  
- Concern 2: hierarchical design trains Manager with fixed sub-policies and fixed time scales, relying on strong intrinsic rewards and pre-learned policies—limits applicability and requires discussion of extensions.  
- Requested clarifications/questions: (1) why the "CoM proxy reward" baseline (same as pre-training reward) performs poorly relative to hierarchical approach; (2) clarify what “multi-policy” baseline entails versus SNNs (separate policies vs SNN); (3) confirm that concatenation acts like changing bias terms and bilinear integration like changing first-layer weights as claimed.  
- Overall: strong empirical contribution and promising direction; minor experiments/clarifications and broader discussion of limitations/extensions requested.
