- Proposal: uses Stochastic Neural Networks with an information-theoretic regularizer to pre-train diverse skills from a single proxy reward for faster downstream learning.  
- Recommendation: reject.  
- Main concern: limited generality—method not shown to scale beyond the 2-link swimmer; 5-link results in Appendix C are not conclusive.  
- Claim of “minimal domain knowledge” is undermined by hand-designed intrinsic rewards and discretization choices.  
- MI bonus depends on discretizing the state space (grid), implying significant domain knowledge and tuning.  
- Experimental validation limited to simple tasks and a single robot morphology; failure modes with richer behaviors are not explored.  
- Unclear whether the discretization approach works in continuous state spaces or scales to complex environments.  
- Requested improvement 1: demonstrate the method on more complex morphologies beyond the 2-link swimmer.  
- Requested improvement 2: provide a thorough analysis of the discretization approach and its implications for continuous state spaces.  
- Requested improvement 3: clarify the actual domain knowledge required for designing the proxy reward.  
- Question 1: How would the method handle continuous state spaces without discretization, and what theoretical guarantees exist?  
- Question 2: How is the MI bonus justified as “minimal domain knowledge” given the need to tune discretization parameters (e.g., grid size)?  
- Question 3: Can you provide evidence the method scales to a 5-link swimmer with much higher skill diversity requirements?
