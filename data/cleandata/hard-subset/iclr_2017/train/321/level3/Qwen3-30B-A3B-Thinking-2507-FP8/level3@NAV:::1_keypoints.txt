- Proposed method: hierarchical RL that pre-trains diverse skills with Stochastic Neural Networks and mutual-information regularization, then uses them for sparse-reward downstream tasks; strong empirical results on mazes and object-gathering.

- Decision: Reject.

- Major rejection reason 1: Misuses the term "intrinsic motivation" — pre-training uses task-aligned proxy rewards (e.g., speed/grasp) similar to Heess et al. 2016, not true intrinsic motivation.

- Major rejection reason 2: Experimental analysis is weak, especially around mutual-information regularization which reportedly does not improve performance on mazes 1–3 with no interpretation provided.

- Supporting concern: Mischaracterizing the relationship to prior hierarchical RL work undermines the paper’s positioning and claimed contribution.

- Critical omission: No explanation for why MI regularization fails on more complex mazes despite being presented as a key component.

- Reproducibility issue: The factorization between S_agent and S_rest is not fully specified (S_rest undefined), hindering reproducibility.

- Missing analysis: Lack of investigation into switching behavior between skills, including failure modes and the effect of switching timing on performance.

- Terminology suggestion: Reconsider using "intrinsic motivation" and more accurately describe the method’s relation to prior work.

- Question 1 for authors: Why does mutual-information regularization not improve performance on mazes 1–3, and what does this imply for future work on skill diversity?

- Question 2 for authors: How would you formally define S_rest to ensure reproducibility of your factorization approach?
