- Proposes hierarchical RL framework that pre-trains diverse skills with Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) and an information-theoretic regularizer, then leverages them for downstream sparse-reward tasks.
- Uses a single proxy reward for pre-training to minimize domain knowledge and obtain transferable, multi-modal policies.
- Demonstrates learning of interpretable skills and significant improvement on long-horizon, sparse-reward tasks.
- Recommendation: accept.
- Main reasons for acceptance: strong empirical gains on sparse-reward tasks and a well-motivated framework combining intrinsic motivation with hierarchical learning.
- Experiments are thorough, showing SNNs with MI regularizer learn a wider, interpretable span of skills while remaining sample-efficient.
- Method outperforms strong baselines, including adding the same proxy reward to downstream tasks, on maze navigation and food collection benchmarks.
- Positioning in related work is appropriate, highlighting limitations of hand-engineered hierarchies and non-transferable intrinsic rewards.
- SNNs are a natural fit for multi-modal policy representation; the MI regularizer encourages skill diversity.
- Suggest quantifying skill diversity (e.g., mutual information metrics) beyond visual visitation plots.
- Ask to clarify the distinction and relationship between "multi-policy" and "SNN" approaches.
- Recommend a deeper discussion of limitations, especially fixed time scales for skill execution and implications for complex tasks.
- Question: why does the CoM speed proxy-reward baseline perform poorly on maze tasks, and were sample counts matched to hierarchical methods?
- Question: confirm the architectural interpretation that bilinear integration changes first-layer weights while concatenation changes biases.
- Question: provide insight into when the MI bonus helps and what task characteristics make it effective.
