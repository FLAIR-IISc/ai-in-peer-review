- Proposes SNN-based pretraining to learn diverse, reusable skills for hierarchical RL and uses them for sparse-reward downstream tasks.
- Experiments (maze navigation, food collection) show improved exploration and performance over naive and intrinsic-reward baselines.
- Decision: Reject due to methodological limitations and misleading claims despite solid experimental results.
- Claim that proxy rewards require “very minimal domain knowledge” is misleading because examples (speed, grasping) are task-specific.
- Discretization in Sec. 5.3 for mutual information is problematic, lacks justification, and conflicts with continuous-control claims.
- Authors should clarify why discretization is needed, analyze grid-size effects, and explain how the method would work without discretization.
- Authors should re-evaluate or better justify the “minimal domain knowledge” claim or show proxy rewards that generalize across domains.
- Address scalability by testing more complex morphologies (e.g., 5-link Snake) and discussing limitations for complex systems.
- Question: Why is state-space discretization necessary, how would the method handle continuous spaces, and what theoretical guarantees exist?
- Question: Provide examples of proxy rewards that work across domains or explain how to design them for novel tasks.
- Question: What causes skill-switching failures for unstable agents (Ant), what modifications are needed, and how would those affect pre-training sample complexity?
