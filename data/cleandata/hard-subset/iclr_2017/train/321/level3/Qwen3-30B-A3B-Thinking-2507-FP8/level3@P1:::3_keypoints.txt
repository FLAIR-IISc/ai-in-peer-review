- Proposes hierarchical RL framework that pre-trains diverse skills with SNNs and an information-theoretic regularizer, then reuses skills for sparse-reward downstream tasks.
- Claims improved exploration and learning efficiency across tasks with minimal domain knowledge for pre-training rewards.
- Shows empirical success on maze navigation and object gathering, and skill reuse across downstream tasks.
- Recommendation: reject due to insufficient support for core claims.
- Major issue: no theoretical justification for discretizing continuous state space (Section 5.3), undermining generality.
- Proxy rewards (e.g., speed, grasp success) appear hand-crafted for specific tasks, contradicting the “minimal domain knowledge” claim.
- Experimental scope is narrow, primarily a 2-link swimmer, limiting evidence of scalability to complex morphologies.
- Appendix C’s Snake results are mentioned but not meaningfully compared; Appendix D shows failure modes with the Ant robot causing instability and rollout termination.
- Discretization into grid cells for mutual information is unjustified and unclear how it generalizes to higher-dimensional or visual state spaces.
- The paper lacks a clear path to address instability issues when switching skills on more complex robots.
- Contribution limited by narrow scope and lack of theoretical grounding for key methodological choices.
- Suggested fix 1: remove or qualify the “minimal domain knowledge” claim and specify required proxy reward knowledge per task.
- Suggested fix 2: provide theoretical justification for discretization or propose an alternative that handles continuous state spaces.
- Suggested fix 3: expand evaluation to more complex morphologies beyond the 2-link swimmer.
- Suggested fix 4: address instability/failure modes (e.g., Ant) and propose modifications to handle such cases.
- Clarification requested: how discretization scales or is justified for continuous/high-dimensional/visual state spaces?
- Clarification requested: what exact domain knowledge is required to design proxy rewards for new tasks, and how this compares to explicit reward approaches?
- Clarification requested: how does the method scale to more complex morphologies like the 5-link Snake, and what changes are needed to maintain performance?
