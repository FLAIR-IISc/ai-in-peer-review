- Proposes a hierarchical RL framework that pre-trains diverse skills using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with an information-theoretic regularizer and leverages them for sparse-reward downstream tasks.  
- Shows improved exploration and interpretable skills across tested tasks, claiming minimal domain knowledge required for the proxy reward.  
- Decision: Reject — key claims insufficiently supported due to scalability concerns and hand-crafted proxy rewards.  
- Criticism: The proxy reward examples (speed, grasp success) appear carefully designed to match downstream tasks, contradicting the “minimal domain knowledge” claim.  
- Experiments are limited to simple morphologies (2-link, 3-link); scalability to more complex robots (5-link, Ant) is not demonstrated.  
- Failure modes (Section D.2) show instability during skill switching on complex agents, raising generality concerns.  
- Section 5.3 discretization for the MI bonus is unclear; paper does not clarify necessity or behavior in continuous state spaces.  
- Major gap: how the method scales to high-dimensional continuous state spaces where discretization is intractable.  
- Suggestion: Re-evaluate and better justify the “minimal domain knowledge” claim with broader analyses and examples.  
- Suggestion: Expand experiments to more complex morphologies (5-link, Ant) and address instability/failure modes.  
- Suggestion: Clarify or remove discretization; provide continuous-space alternatives or experiments showing applicability.  
- Suggestion: Test whether the MI bonus is necessary for diversity or if other latent-variable mechanisms can replace it.  
- Question: Provide a concrete example of designing the proxy reward for a completely different task/morphology to justify minimal domain knowledge.  
- Question: How does the discretized MI computation scale to continuous, high-dimensional state spaces; is there a continuous alternative?  
- Question: How would the framework be modified to handle unstable agents (e.g., Ant) and would those modifications require extra domain knowledge?  
- Question: How would the approach scale to more complex, longer-horizon navigation tasks requiring substantially greater skill diversity?
