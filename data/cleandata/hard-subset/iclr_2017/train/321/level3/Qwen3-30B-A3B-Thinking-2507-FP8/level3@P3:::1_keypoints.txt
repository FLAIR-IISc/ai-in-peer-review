- Proposes hierarchical RL framework: pre-train diverse skills with a task-aligned proxy reward, then use them in a high-level policy for sparse-reward downstream tasks.  
- Uses Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with a mutual information (MI) regularizer to learn multiple skills within a single network.  
- Shows strong empirical results on challenging benchmarks (mazes, Gather), improving sample efficiency where naive exploration failed.  
- Claims minimal domain knowledge required for proxy reward specification.  
- Decision: Weak Accept — valuable contribution but needs significant revisions for clarity, analysis, and reproducibility.  
- Strength: Clear hierarchical framework that empirically solves benchmarks previously intractable with naive exploration.  
- Strength: SNN + MI effectively learns diverse skills, supported by visitation plots and comparisons to prior work (Heess et al.).  
- Weakness: Labeling the proxy reward as “intrinsic motivation” is misleading; it is task-aligned and similar to prior pre-training work.  
- Weakness: Experimental analysis is weak — lacks interpretation for why MI fails on Mazes 1–3.  
- Weakness: Missing analysis of switching behavior, failure modes (notably Ant instability), and how switching timing affects performance.  
- Weakness: State factorization between S_agent and S_rest is not clearly specified, harming reproducibility.  
- Feedback: Remove or reframe “intrinsic motivation” and call the pre-training signal a “task-aligned proxy reward.”  
- Feedback: Add analyses explaining MI’s variable effect across mazes and relate skill diversity to maze complexity.  
- Feedback: Provide detailed analysis of switching behavior, failure modes (Ant), and potential mitigations.  
- Feedback: Explicitly specify S_rest for each task and explain how factorization was chosen.  
- Feedback: Include ablation studies on MI regularization strength and its impact on skill diversity and downstream performance.  
- Clarification needed: What exactly constitutes “minimal domain knowledge” given proxy rewards like locomotion speed?  
- Clarification needed: Why does MI help Maze 0 but not Mazes 1–3, and how can the method adapt to more complex mazes?  
- Clarification needed: How was the S_agent / S_rest factorization determined (e.g., which Gather state components belong to S_rest)?  
- Clarification needed: How were pretrained SNNs selected for final experiments given high variance, and is that selection practical without prior knowledge?
