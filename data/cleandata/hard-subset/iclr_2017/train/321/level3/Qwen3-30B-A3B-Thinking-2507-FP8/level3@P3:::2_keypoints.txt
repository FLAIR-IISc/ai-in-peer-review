- Presents SNN-based hierarchical RL that learns diverse skills via unsupervised pre-training with a simple proxy reward and an information-theoretic regularizer.  
- Learned skills are leveraged by a high-level manager to solve downstream sparse-reward tasks, improving exploration and long-horizon learning.  
- Strong empirical results and visualizations demonstrate distinct locomotion patterns and exploration benefits from the learned skills.  
- Key insight: directly combining intrinsic and sparse rewards fails, motivating hierarchical abstraction.  
- SNN approach consistently outperforms baselines across multiple environments; mutual information (MI) bonus further helps in some cases.  
- The paper addresses the critical problem of sparse rewards and shows pre-trained diverse skills improve learning efficiency.  
- Limitation: current integration methods (concatenation, bilinear) allow only limited sub-policy diversification, which may fail on manipulation tasks.  
- Limitation: fixed sub-policies and fixed time scales restrict hierarchy flexibility and increase reliance on good intrinsic rewards and pretraining.  
- Claim that intrinsic/unsupervised rewards appear domain-specific is interesting but under-explained and needs elaboration.  
- Request clarification on why the strong baseline (CoM + sparse reward) performs poorly and whether it accounts for extra pretraining experience.  
- Request clarification on the distinction between "multi-policy" and "SNN" (whether multi-policy means multiple independent policies and how implementations/results differ).  
- Request confirmation of architectural interpretation for Fig.1: concatenation biases first hidden layer vs. bilinear integration altering input weight matrix.
