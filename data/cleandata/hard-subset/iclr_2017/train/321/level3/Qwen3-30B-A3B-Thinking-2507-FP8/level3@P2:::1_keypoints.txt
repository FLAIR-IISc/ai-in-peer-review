- Contribution: Proposes pre-training diverse skills with Stochastic Neural Networks + MI regularizer and using them for faster learning on sparse-reward downstream tasks, showing gains on locomotion+maze and food-collection benchmarks.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Weakness 1: Misuses the term "intrinsic motivation"—pre-training uses task-aligned proxy rewards (speed/grasp) rather than task-agnostic intrinsic signals, conflating prior work.  
- Weakness 2: Fails to analyze why MI regularization does not help on more complex mazes (1–3) despite aiding simpler cases.  
- Fix 1: Clarify terminology—label pre-training as task-aligned proxy rewards and distinguish from true intrinsic motivation literature.  
- Fix 2: Expand analysis of MI regularization to determine whether skill diversity was already sufficient or maze reward structure negates MI benefits.  
- Fix 3: Explicitly define state-space factorization (S_agent vs S_rest) for all experiments to ensure reproducibility.  
- Fix 4: Provide detailed analysis of switching behavior, including failure modes (e.g., Ant) and how switching time T impacts performance.  
- Question 1: How is the speed/grasp proxy reward meaningfully different from directly rewarding desired downstream behaviors, given the "minimal domain knowledge" claim?  
- Question 2: Why does the MI bonus increase skill diversity yet not improve performance on mazes 1–3—any interaction with maze reward structure?  
- Question 3: Precisely how was the state decomposed into S_agent and S_rest for the maze environments?  
- Question 4: What modifications to the SNN or switching mechanism could mitigate failures with unstable robots like Ant?
