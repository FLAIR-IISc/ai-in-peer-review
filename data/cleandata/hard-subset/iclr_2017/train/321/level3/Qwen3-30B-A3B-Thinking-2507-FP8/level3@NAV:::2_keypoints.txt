- Proposes a hierarchical RL framework using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with an information-theoretic regularizer to learn diverse skills during pre-training.
- Key contribution: mutual-information (MI)-based reward that encourages option identifiability, enabling interpretable skills without domain-specific supervision.
- Empirical result: hierarchical approach significantly improves exploration and learning efficiency across maze navigation and object gathering tasks.
- Baseline finding: directly combining intrinsic and sparse rewards (strong baseline) fails to solve sparse-reward tasks.
- Recommendation: Accept (score 7).
- Positioning: Well-motivated and properly situated within hierarchical RL and intrinsic motivation literature.
- Experimental rigor: Strong, scientifically rigorous results that consistently outperform baselines; visualizations effectively demonstrate learned skills and hierarchy.
- Strength: Can learn diverse skills without requiring task-specific rewards during pre-training.
- Limitation: Concatenation and bilinear integration allow only minimal sub-policy differences, which may be insufficient for complex manipulation tasks.
- Limitation: Fixed sub-policies and time scales reduce flexibility; intrinsic rewards may appear domain-specific due to this fixed setup.
- Clarification requested: Why does the strong baseline in Section 7.3 perform poorly, and does the comparison account for extra experience from pre-trained policies?
- Clarification requested: What is meant by "multi-policy" versus "SNN"?
- Clarification requested: Confirm that concatenation corresponds to categorical biases to the first hidden layer while bilinear integration yields different input weight matrices.
- Suggestion: Strengthen the paper with a more detailed discussion of limitations and possible remedies in future work.
