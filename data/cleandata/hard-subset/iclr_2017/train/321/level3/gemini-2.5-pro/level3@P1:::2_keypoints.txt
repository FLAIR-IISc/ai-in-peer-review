- Paper proposes a two-stage hierarchical RL framework: pre-train diverse low-level skills with a proxy reward, then train a high-level manager to compose them for downstream sparse-reward tasks.  
- Uses a single Stochastic Neural Network (SNN) conditioned on a latent variable to efficiently learn multiple skills.  
- Introduces a novel mutual-information-based regularizer to encourage distinct, diverse skills.  
- Experiments on continuous-control locomotion tasks show interpretable skills and strong gains on sparse-reward maze and gathering tasks.  
- Decision: Accept (score 7) — method is novel, effective, and well-executed.  
- Strong empirical evidence that hierarchical temporal abstraction, not just reward shaping, enables solving long-horizon sparse-reward tasks.  
- The SNN+MI combination is sample-efficient and produces richer behaviors than independent-policy baselines or simpler SNNs.  
- Ablations show both bilinear integration and the MI bonus are important for skill diversity.  
- Visitation plots and qualitative results convincingly illustrate the learned skill span.  
- Limitation: conditioning via concatenation/bilinear may lack expressiveness for qualitatively different skill families (e.g., complex manipulation).  
- Limitation: frozen pre-trained sub-policies and fixed skill duration T make the approach sensitive to the proxy reward and limit hierarchical flexibility.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss sensitivity to proxy reward, explore end-to-end fine-tuning or learned termination policies.  
- Question 1: Clarify why the dense "CoM maze reward" baseline performs poorly and whether it was trained from scratch with comparable environment interactions.  
- Question 2: Confirm that "Multi-policy" denotes K independently trained policies used as a baseline manager input.  
- Question 3: Confirm interpretation that concatenation with one-hot z equals per-skill bias vectors, while bilinear integration yields per-skill input-to-hidden weight matrices.
