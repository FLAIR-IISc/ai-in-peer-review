- Proposed method: two-stage hierarchical RL—pre-train a diverse skill set with a single Stochastic Neural Network (latent categorical variable) using a proxy reward and mutual-information regularizer, then freeze skills and train a high-level manager on downstream tasks.  
- Decision: Accept — novel combination of stochastic latent models and information-theoretic regularization with strong empirical results.  
- Strength: Pre-training skills yields a powerful exploration strategy that improves sample efficiency and solves long-horizon sparse-reward tasks.  
- Strength: Using one SNN for multiple skills is efficient (weight sharing) and more general than training separate policies.  
- Weakness — framing: Calling the proxy reward “intrinsic motivation” is misleading; it’s task-aligned pre-training or curriculum, not task-agnostic intrinsic objectives.  
- Weakness — MI regularizer: Impact under-analyzed; MI bonus gives no benefit (and may harm) performance on complex mazes without explanation.  
- Weakness — hierarchical analysis: Paper lacks insight into high-level behavior (skill switching patterns, failure modes) and underplays importance of switching interval T.  
- Reproducibility concern: Need precise specification of S_agent vs S_rest state factorization for each environment.  
- Pre-training variance: Downstream performance varies significantly with random seed in SNN pre-training, threatening method reliability and needing mitigation.  
- Failure modes: Skill switching can destabilize agents (e.g., Ant falls); safe transition mechanisms and these analyses should be highlighted in the main paper.  
- Questions for authors: (1) Specify S_rest components per task; (2) explain why MI fails on Mazes 1–3 (e.g., encourages complex/unstable gaits vs simple motions); (3) advise practitioners on handling pre-training variance and model selection without downstream task access.
