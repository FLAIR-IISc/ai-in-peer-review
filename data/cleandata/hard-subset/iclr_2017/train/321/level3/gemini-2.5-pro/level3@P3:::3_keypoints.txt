- Paper proposes a two-stage hierarchical RL framework: pre-train diverse low-level skills with a Stochastic Neural Network (SNN) conditioned on a discrete latent, then train a high-level manager to select skills for downstream sparse-reward tasks.  
- Key contribution: single-network multi-modal skill representation via SNN improves sample efficiency.  
- Key contribution: mutual information-based regularizer to encourage distinct, diverse skills.  
- Key contribution: demonstrates improved exploration and success on sparse-reward continuous control tasks (e.g., maze navigation) compared to standard RL.  
- Decision: Reject.  
- Main reason 1: Claims of generality and minimal domain knowledge are unsupported; intrinsic rewards appear hand-crafted for locomotion.  
- Main reason 2: Methodological details (e.g., reliance on 2D grid for MI) raise concerns about scalability and robustness.  
- Concern: Proxy rewards (e.g., speed for locomotion, “any successful grasp” for manipulators) require significant domain knowledge and may critically determine pre-training success.  
- Concern: Experiments limited to simple planar agents (Swimmer, Snake); failure on Ant in appendix suggests instability and limited applicability to complex morphologies.  
- Concern: MI bonus computed via (x,y) grid discretization does not scale to high-dimensional state spaces; MLP alternative is mentioned but neither detailed nor evaluated.  
- Concern: Fixed number of skills K may not scale to environments requiring richer or continuous behavior spectra; sensitivity to K is unexplored.  
- Suggestion: Move Ant failure discussion into main paper to honestly present limitations.  
- Suggestion: Soften claims of “minimal domain knowledge” and provide principles for designing proxy rewards.  
- Suggestion: Add experiments beyond locomotion (e.g., manipulation) to support generality claims.  
- Suggestion: Provide implementation details and experiments for the MI regularizer alternative (MLP posterior) and an ablation on grid resolution.  
- Questions for authors: How does grid discretization scale to high-dimensional states and does the MLP posterior work in practice?  
- Question: Does Ant instability indicate a fundamental limitation of freezing low-level skills during downstream training and how would this scale to complex morphologies or non-locomotion tasks?  
- Question: Provide a concrete example of a “minimal domain knowledge” proxy reward for a non-locomotion task (e.g., block stacking).  
- Question: How does skill quality/diversity change as K increases, and can the method discover sufficiently rich skill sets for highly capable agents?
