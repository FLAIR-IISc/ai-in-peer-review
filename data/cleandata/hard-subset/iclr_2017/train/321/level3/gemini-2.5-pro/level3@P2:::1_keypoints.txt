- Paper proposes a two-stage hierarchical RL: pre-train diverse low-level skills, then train a high-level manager to compose them for downstream sparse-reward tasks.
- Skill acquisition uses a single Stochastic Neural Network (SNN) with latent codes mapping to skills, enabling weight sharing and ~K-fold sample-efficiency.
- Introduces a mutual information (MI) regularizer between latent code and state to encourage diverse, distinct skills.
- Pre-training is guided by a simple, generic "proxy reward" (e.g., forward speed), requiring minimal domain knowledge.
- Strong empirical results: solves challenging continuous-control benchmarks (mazes, Gather) where prior naive-exploration methods fail.
- Decision: Accept — framework is effective and empirically compelling on sparse-reward tasks.
- Novelty and efficiency: SNN + MI regularizer is a novel, sample-efficient method for unsupervised/minimally supervised skill discovery.
- Improved exploration demonstrated qualitatively (visitation plots) and quantitatively, outperforming a baseline that adds the proxy reward to downstream tasks.
- MI bonus shows clear benefit on Gather but gives inconsistent or no improvement on Mazes 1–3, with limited analysis of why.
- Framing critique: calling the proxy-guided pre-training "intrinsic motivation" is misleading because the proxy rewards align closely with downstream capabilities.
- Under-analysis of hierarchical components: skill-switching mechanism and switch time T are insufficiently studied, with important failure modes (e.g., Ant) buried in the appendix.
- Reproducibility concern: exact composition of S_rest vs S_agent (e.g., LIDAR, object positions) should be explicitly listed for each environment.
- Ablation request: separate analysis of bilinear integration versus concatenation and isolation of MI bonus effects would clarify component contributions.
- Specific questions for authors: explain MI bonus task-dependent effects, clarify differences from Heess et al. (2016) and "intrinsic motivation" framing, elaborate on switch-time insensitivity, and list S_agent/S_rest components for Maze and Gather.
