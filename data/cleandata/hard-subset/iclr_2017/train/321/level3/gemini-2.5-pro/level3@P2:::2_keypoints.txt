1. Proposes a two-stage HRL framework: pre-train diverse low-level skills with a proxy reward, then train a high-level manager over frozen skills for downstream tasks.  
2. Uses a single Stochastic Neural Network (SNN) with a latent categorical variable to condition multiple skills, enabling weight sharing and sample efficiency.  
3. Introduces an information-theoretic mutual information regularizer to encourage distinct, diverse skills.  
4. Manager selects skills for fixed time periods, providing temporal abstraction that improves exploration in sparse, long-horizon tasks.  
5. Strong empirical results on continuous-control benchmarks show interpretable skills and success where standard RL fails.  
6. Decision: Accept — framework is well-motivated, elegant, and practically addresses sparse-reward exploration.  
7. Empirical evidence convincingly shows hierarchical abstraction, not just the proxy reward, yields the main benefits.  
8. SNN is a sample-efficient alternative to training separate policies and blends HRL, intrinsic motivation, and info-theoretic objectives.  
9. Ablations show bilinear integration of latent and observation outperforms simple concatenation for producing diverse skills.  
10. Visualizations (e.g., visitation plots) effectively demonstrate diverse, interpretable locomotion gaits.  
11. Experiments show significantly improved exploration and success on maze/gathering tasks; proxy-reward-as-intrinsic baseline fails.  
12. Authors transparently report limitations, including failure modes on unstable agents (e.g., Ant).  
13. Suggestion: Skill-generation mechanism may be too restrictive for tasks needing fundamentally different skills (e.g., manipulation); discuss alternative architectures.  
14. Suggestion: Freezing low-level skills and using fixed-duration T is rigid—allowing end-to-end fine-tuning or learned termination would increase flexibility.  
15. Suggestion: The claim that proxy rewards require minimal domain knowledge is optimistic; discuss challenges in designing proxy rewards for other domains.  
16. Clarifying Q1: Clarify how the mixed-reward baseline (proxy + sparse) was trained—scratch or with pre-training?  
17. Clarifying Q2: Confirm whether the "Multi-policy" baseline trains K separate policies for pre-training and uses them as skills for the manager.  
18. Clarifying Q3: Confirm SNN details—does concatenation select different biases for the first layer and bilinear select different input-to-hidden weight matrices?
