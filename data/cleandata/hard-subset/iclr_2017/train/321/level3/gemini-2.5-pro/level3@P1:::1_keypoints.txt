- Paper proposes a two-stage hierarchical RL: pre-train diverse low-level skills via a Stochastic Neural Network (SNN) with a mutual information (MI) regularizer, then freeze skills and train a high-level manager on downstream sparse-reward tasks.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main empirical claim: the hierarchical approach significantly improves exploration and solves tasks that standard RL cannot on challenging locomotion/navigation benchmarks.  
- Technical novelty: using SNNs to represent multi-modal skills and an MI bonus to encourage diverse, interpretable behaviors within one policy.  
- Strong qualitative evidence: visitation and exploration plots show the SNN (with bilinear integration + MI) learns a rich span of skills and superior state-space coverage.  
- MI regularizer is not consistently beneficial: it helps Maze 0 and Gather but not Mazes 1–3, with no hypothesis or analysis provided for this discrepancy.  
- Framing critique: calling the proxy reward “intrinsic motivation” is misleading; it is better described as curriculum learning or task-family-specific pre-training (e.g., similar to Heess et al. 2016).  
- Limited analysis of the high-level manager: paper focuses on pre-training but offers little on the skill-sequence behavior of the manager policy.  
- Sensitivity to fixed switch time T is underexplored for main experiments (only brief Snake analysis in appendix); Swimmer sensitivity should be analyzed.  
- Reproducibility concern: state factorization into S_agent and S_rest needs explicit definitions (e.g., exact LIDAR-like sensor readings) for downstream tasks.  
- Failure modes: Ant robot shows instability when switching skills; this limitation should be discussed in the main text.  
- Questions for authors requested: (1) hypothesis for why MI helps some mazes but not others, (2) exact composition of S_rest for maze/gather tasks, (3) whether Swimmer experiments show robustness to switch time T similar to Snake.
