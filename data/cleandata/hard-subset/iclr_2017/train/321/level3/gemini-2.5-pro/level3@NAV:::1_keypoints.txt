- Problem & approach: Proposes a hierarchical two-stage RL framework—pre-train diverse low-level skills with an SNN using a proxy reward, then train a high-level manager on frozen skills to solve sparse-reward, long-horizon tasks.
- Core contribution: Learning a diverse skill set via a shared-architecture SNN regularized by a mutual information (MI) bonus, enabling sample-efficient downstream learning.
- Empirical results: Strong performance on challenging continuous-control benchmarks (e.g., complex mazes) where prior methods struggled.
- Recommendation: Accept—performance gains on established benchmarks justify acceptance.
- Strength: The SNN shared-architecture pretraining is elegant and more sample-efficient than training multiple independent policies.
- Weakness: Experimental analysis is underdeveloped and does not match the strength of the empirical results.
- Terminology concern: The term “intrinsic motivation” is used loosely—proxy rewards (e.g., speed) are well-aligned with downstream tasks and resemble task-aligned pretraining rather than task-agnostic intrinsic motivation.
- MI regularizer issue: The mutual information bonus diversifies skills but does not improve performance on several maze tasks, and the paper lacks analysis explaining this gap.
- Limited hierarchy analysis: Little investigation into the manager’s switching strategy, failure modes, or how timing of skill switches affects performance.
- Reproducibility request: Explicitly define the factorization of state space into S_agent and S_rest for each domain.
- Qualitative analysis request: Visualize trajectories and correlate them with manager skill selections, especially in failure cases, to illuminate system dynamics.
- Question for authors: Why does MI-driven skill diversity fail to translate to better maze performance—does it produce skills too specialized for sequencing?
- Question for authors: What is the manager’s learned switching behavior—are specific skills used in specific state-space regions (e.g., a “turning” skill near corners)?
