- Proposes a hierarchical RL framework: pre-train diverse skills with a proxy reward, then freeze skills and train a manager to compose them for downstream tasks.
- Uses Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with bilinear latent-observation integration to learn multiple skills in one policy, reducing sample complexity.
- Introduces an MI-based regularizer between latent skill and agent coordinates to encourage distinct, diverse skills.
- Demonstrates interpretable locomotion skills and faster downstream learning on navigation and collection tasks compared to baselines.
- Decision: Reject.
- Primary concern 1: The claim of “minimal domain knowledge” is overstated because the proxy reward (e.g., speed) is hand-designed and tailored to locomotion.
- Supporting critique: Success depends on proxy reward having useful optima; no guidance on designing proxy rewards for other domains like manipulation.
- Primary concern 2: Scalability and robustness issues due to MI estimator relying on discretizing (x,y) into a grid, which won’t scale to higher-dimensional state spaces.
- Supporting critique: The paper mentions an MLP alternative but does not implement or evaluate it, leaving feasibility in complex settings untested.
- Critical failure mode: Method fails on the Ant robot because switching frozen skills leads to unfamiliar states and instability, questioning generality of freeze-and-switch.
- Suggested reframing: Present the approach as using task-agnostic but domain-specific proxy rewards (e.g., general locomotion) rather than requiring minimal knowledge.
- Suggested experiments: Evaluate a scalable MI estimator (neural posterior) and investigate remedies for instability such as fine-tuning, transition policies, or skill-compatibility measures.
- Key questions for authors: details of grid discretization and scaling to high-dimensional state features; clarification on proxy reward requirements for manipulators; whether freezing skills is fundamentally flawed for dynamically complex agents and effects of joint training/fine-tuning.
