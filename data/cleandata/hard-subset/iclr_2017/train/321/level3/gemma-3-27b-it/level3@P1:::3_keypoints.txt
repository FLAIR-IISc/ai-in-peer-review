- Paper proposes using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with a hand-designed proxy reward and mutual information regularization to pre-train skills for hierarchical RL, showing improvements on locomotion and maze tasks.
- Decision: Reject.
- Main critique: Claim of “minimal domain knowledge” is misleading because the proxy reward is hand-crafted (e.g., rewarding speed) and tailored to locomotion tasks.
- Generalization concern: Unclear whether proxy rewards and the approach will transfer to other robots/tasks (e.g., grasping) without task-specific redesign.
- Instability and variability: Downstream performance varies across pre-trained SNNs and shows notable failure modes (especially with the Ant robot), indicating sensitivity to initialization/training.
- Skill-switching issues: Skill transitions can destabilize complex agents, and current fixes (end-to-end training) are left as future work.
- MI computation is unclear: The paper lacks detail on whether and how the state space is discretized for mutual information, and how this affects theory and results.
- Ablation needed: The contribution of SNNs, MI regularization, and the proxy reward should be separated via thorough ablation studies.
- Scalability concern: More experiments are needed to show robustness to diverse morphologies (e.g., humanoids) and whether significant retuning is required.
- Reward shaping discussion: The paper should acknowledge the proxy reward as reward shaping, discuss limitations, and offer design guidance.
- Switching policy suggestion: More elaboration on learning a transition/switching policy and its pros/cons is warranted.
- Theoretical rigor: A stronger theoretical analysis of mutual information maximization and its effect on skill diversity would strengthen the work.
- Question for authors: Provide principles for designing proxy rewards across platforms to avoid task-specific solutions.
- Question for authors: Describe methods to mitigate variability across pre-training runs and ensure consistent downstream performance.
- Question for authors: Clarify the MI discretization process, grid resolution effects, or how MI is computed in continuous spaces.
- Question for authors: Compare performance and trade-offs against hierarchical RL methods that use hand-designed skill hierarchies.
- Question for authors: Discuss limitations for very high-dimensional continuous state spaces where discretization is impractical.
