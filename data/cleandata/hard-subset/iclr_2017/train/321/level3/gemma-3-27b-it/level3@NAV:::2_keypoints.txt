- Proposes hierarchical RL using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with an information-theoretic reward to learn diverse, reusable skills via pre-training.
- Decision: Accept.
- Core idea is compelling for tackling sparse rewards and long-horizon tasks by pre-training skills with minimal domain knowledge.
- Empirical results show hierarchical abstraction and enforced diversity outperform directly combining intrinsic and sparse rewards.
- Visualizations of learned skills are particularly insightful.
- SNNs plus the information-theoretic regularizer learn a diverse skill set in pre-training that improves downstream sparse-reward performance.
- Ablation studies convincingly demonstrate the importance of bilinear integration and the mutual information (MI) bonus.
- Comparative results against simpler baselines and other intrinsic motivation methods provide good contextual validation.
- Limited diversification: concatenation and bilinear integration may be insufficient for tasks needing more diverse sub-policies.
- Hierarchy limitation: fixed sub-policy timescales and reliance on predefined intrinsic rewards restrict broader applicability.
- Request to clarify whether the strong baseline in Sec 7.3 accounts for extra experience from pre-training (e.g., relation to "CoM maze reward").
- Request to clarify what "multi-policy" means versus the SNN approach (independent policies vs architectural differences).
- Request confirmation of Fig.1 interpretation: concatenation biases the first hidden layer; bilinear integration modulates the input weight matrix.
