- Decision: Reject.

- Contribution: Proposes HRL framework using Stochastic Neural Networks for skill discovery and a high-level policy, demonstrated on locomotion and maze tasks.

- Primary concern: Scalability and generality unclear—experiments focus on a 2-link swimmer (and limited 5-link Snake), so applicability to higher-DOF or different morphologies is uncertain.

- Domain-knowledge claim disputed: The proxy reward (e.g., speed) constitutes domain knowledge and biases skill discovery, so the “minimal domain knowledge” claim is misleading.

- Methodological clarity: Unclear whether the state space is discretized or the grid is used only for MI estimation; impact of discretization resolution is not discussed.

- Failure modes: Paper briefly notes instability on agents like Ant but lacks a thorough discussion of robustness, transfer between tasks, and failure modes.

- Ablation studies needed: Missing analyses isolating SNNs’ contribution versus separate policies and the specific impact of the mutual information (MI) bonus.

- Hyperparameter sensitivity: No thorough sensitivity analysis for key hyperparameters (latent K, MI coeff αH, switch time T).

- Computational cost: Lacks discussion of training/deployment computational overhead relative to other HRL or skill discovery methods.

- Question 1: Need more details on Snake experiments and whether downstream performance matches swimmer gains.

- Question 2: Clarify whether continuous states are discretized pre-SNN or only for MI estimation, justification for resolution, and sensitivity to it.

- Question 3: Request characterization of required domain knowledge for designing proxy rewards and scenarios where a truly domain-agnostic approach is feasible.

- Question 4: Ask what changes (e.g., manager-level recurrence) would address Ant instability and other failure modes.
