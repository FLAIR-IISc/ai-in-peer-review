- Decision: Reject despite interesting contribution and strong empirical results due to significant analysis shortcomings.
- Main critique: Experimental analysis is weak and does not explain why the method succeeds or fails on different tasks.
- Mutual information (MI) regularization sometimes degrades performance on complex mazes, but the paper offers no investigation or explanation.
- Need to examine whether MI causes overly specialized skills, hyperparameter sensitivity, or other failure modes.
- Impact of different pre-trained SNNs on downstream performance is not analyzed despite apparent sensitivity.
- Framing pre-training reward as “intrinsic motivation” is misleading; the pre-training uses task-aligned shaped rewards and should be reframed.
- State-space factorization is unclear: how S_rest is identified and separated from S_agent is not specified, harming reproducibility.
- Suggest analyzing high-level policy switching behavior: switch frequency, timing, and failure cases.
- Suggest deeper policy analysis: interpret and visualize behaviors corresponding to each latent code in the SNN.
- Recommend a thorough ablation study to isolate contributions of SNNs and MI regularization.
- Recommend robustness checks across initial conditions and hyperparameter settings.
- Questions for authors: clarify S_rest identification, explain why MI sometimes degrades performance, provide skill usage frequency distributions, and explore alternative diversity methods.
