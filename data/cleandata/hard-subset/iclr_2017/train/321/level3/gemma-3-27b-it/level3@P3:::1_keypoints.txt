- Paper proposes an HRL framework using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with mutual information (MI) regularization to pre-train skills via a proxy reward and use a high-level policy, showing improved performance on locomotion and maze tasks.
- Decision: Reject.
- Claim of “intrinsic motivation” is misleading because pre-training uses task-aligned proxy rewards (speed/grasp), not genuine curiosity-driven exploration.
- Experimental analysis is shallow: improvements are shown but not explained; contributions of bilinear integration are asserted without rigorous comparison to alternatives.
- MI regularization does not improve performance on complex mazes (1–3) and the paper fails to investigate why (e.g., state complexity or MI estimation issues).
- Reproducibility concerns: the factorization into S_agent and vaguely defined S_rest (task-specific attributes) is underspecified, hindering replication.
- Switching behavior between skills is under-analyzed: impact of fixed switching time T, failure modes from switching frequency, and high-level policy orchestration are not examined.
- Suggested improvements: refine terminology (avoid “intrinsic motivation”), analyze MI regularization failures, clarify S_rest definitions per task, study switching time and adaptive/learned termination, perform ablation studies, broaden baseline comparisons, and fully document/executable code release.
- Questions for authors: how is S_rest defined per task and S_agent kept consistent; why MI fails on complex mazes and alternative diversity methods; sensitivity to switching time T and adaptive strategies; what skill usage/exploration patterns emerge; how sensitive results are to SNN initialization/local optima.
