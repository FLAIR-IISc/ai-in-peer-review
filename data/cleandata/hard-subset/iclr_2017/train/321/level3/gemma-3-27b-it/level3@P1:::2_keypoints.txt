- Proposes pre-training diverse skills with Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) using a proxy reward and an MI regularizer, then training a high-level policy on those skills.  
- Claims the method learns interpretable skills and substantially improves performance on sparse-reward, long-horizon tasks.  
- Decision: Reject — experimental evidence and methodological clarity are insufficient to justify strong claims.  
- Positive: Clear motivation for HRL, reasonable use of MI to encourage skill diversity, and informative visualizations.  
- Main critique: Results don’t conclusively show advantage over simpler baselines; contributions of each component aren’t isolated.  
- Concern: Reliance on a carefully tuned proxy reward questions the claim of minimal domain knowledge and generalizability.  
- Question whether the proxy reward used in downstream baselines equals the pretraining (CoM maze) reward — needs clarification.  
- Lacks comparisons to established intrinsic motivation baselines (e.g., Duan et al., 2016).  
- The “multi-policy” baseline is undefined and performs comparably, raising doubt about the necessity of SNN complexity.  
- Experimental presentation makes it hard to attribute gains to SNN architecture vs. hierarchical abstraction vs. MI regularizer.  
- Important missing ablations: SNN architecture, MI regularizer, and hierarchical structure contributions.  
- Insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, notably for the MI bonus alphaH.  
- Limitation: Fixed sub-policy timescales and inability to adapt skills during downstream learning reduce robustness and recovery ability.  
- Stability issue with the Ant robot needs addressing — consider adaptive skill switching or online skill refinement.  
- Recommendation: Address these clarifications, add ablations and sensitivity analyses, evaluate on more complex/adaptive tasks, then resubmit.
