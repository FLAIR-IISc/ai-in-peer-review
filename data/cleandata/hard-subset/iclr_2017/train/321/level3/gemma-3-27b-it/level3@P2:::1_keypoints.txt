- Decision: Accept with minor revisions.

- Tackles sparse-reward RL with a well-motivated hybrid of hierarchical RL and intrinsic motivation.

- Novelty: uses stochastic neural networks (SNNs) plus a mutual information (MI) regularizer to learn diverse skills in one network.

- Empirical results: shows strong performance and outperforms prior methods on several challenging tasks; code is available for reproducibility.

- Presentation needs stronger analysis, especially about why the method works and when it fails.

- Clarify that the pre-training reward (speed, grasping) is task-aligned shaping rather than pure intrinsic motivation.

- Investigate MI regularizer more deeply: inconsistent gains across mazes need explanation (hyperparams vs. environment structure).

- Define the state-space factorization S_agent and S_rest clearly with concrete examples for reproducibility.

- Analyze skill-switching behavior: switching frequency, triggers, failure cases, and how switch time T affects performance.

- Summarize failure modes (e.g., Ant robot instability) in the main paper rather than only in the appendix.

- Question: how sensitive are results to the MI coefficient αH and was a systematic hyperparameter search performed?

- Question: provide specific definitions of S_rest for each environment (locomotion+maze, locomotion+gather).

- Question: elaborate on Ant robot challenges, instability during skill switching, and mitigation strategies considered.

- Question: have you attempted end-to-end training (e.g., straight-through estimators) and what challenges/results were observed?

- Question: provide visitation plots for the Snake robot demonstrating the effect of the MI bonus and bilinear integration.
