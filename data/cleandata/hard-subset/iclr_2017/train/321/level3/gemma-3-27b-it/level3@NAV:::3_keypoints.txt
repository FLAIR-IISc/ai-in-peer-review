- Paper proposes hierarchical RL using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) for skill discovery with an information-theoretic regularizer to encourage diversity.
- Idea of pre-training diverse skills to speed downstream learning in sparse-reward tasks is well-motivated and appealing.
- Use of SNNs to represent multi-modal policies is novel and the design rationale is clearly explained.
- Recommendation: reject in current form due to insufficient evidence of generality and robustness.
- Main concern: experiments focus on simple morphologies (mostly 2‑link swimmer), so scalability to complex bodies (e.g., manipulators) is unproven.
- Question whether learned skill diversity transfers to larger action/state spaces and more complex morphologies.
- Claim of “minimal domain knowledge” is overstated because the proxy reward is a hand-designed intrinsic reward tailored to locomotion.
- Need clarification on whether mutual information is computed via state-space discretization, why discretization is used, and how its granularity affects performance or continuous-state alternatives.
- Request more detailed ablation studies to isolate effects of SNNs, the MI regularizer, and the hierarchical architecture.
- Ask for stronger quantitative comparisons to other HRL and intrinsic motivation methods beyond VIME.
- Recommend adding discussion of failure modes, stability issues, and limitations for transfer to harder tasks.
- Clarify notation and procedure for the latent variable z: when and how it is sampled during pre-training vs downstream execution.
