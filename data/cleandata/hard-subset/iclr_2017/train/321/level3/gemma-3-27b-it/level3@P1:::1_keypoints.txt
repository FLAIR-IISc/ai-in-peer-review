- Proposal: Hierarchical RL using Stochastic Neural Networks to pre-train diverse skills with a proxy reward, then learn a high-level policy on top.  
- Decision: Accept.  
- Main strength: Combining SNNs with a mutual information regularizer yields diverse, useful skills that improve downstream performance.  
- Experimental support: Outperforms prior methods on Locomotion+Maze and Locomotion+Food; ablations show contributions of bilinear integration and MI regularizer.  
- Reproducibility: Thorough experiments across mazes and Snake robot; code, videos, and hyperparameters provided.  
- Terminology issue: Calling the proxy reward “intrinsic motivation” is misleading—it's task-aligned (speed/grasping) rather than curiosity-driven; relation to Heess et al. (2016) needs clearer contrast.  
- State definition gap: The factorization into S_agent and S_rest is under-specified; S_rest needs clearer definition for reproducibility.  
- MI regularizer unclear impact: Mutual information bonus does not consistently improve performance and needs deeper analysis (e.g., regularization strength, task dependence).  
- Skill switching concerns: Limited analysis of switching behavior—frequency, timing T, and effects on unstable robots require investigation and visualization.  
- Failure analysis missing: More detailed failure-mode analysis (particularly for the Ant) is needed to understand why switching leads to failures.  
- Q1: Why choose speed/grasping as proxy rewards and how do these choices shape the learned skills?  
- Q2: Please specify which features comprise S_rest for the benchmark tasks.  
- Q3: Why might the MI regularizer not consistently help; were different strengths or alternatives tried?  
- Q4: How does switch time T affect performance and can T be adapted automatically?  
- Q5: Can you provide qualitative visualizations of skill switching (state trajectories with switch times)?
