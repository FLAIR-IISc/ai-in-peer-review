- Paper proposes using Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with an information-theoretic regularizer to learn diverse skills for hierarchical RL and accelerate sparse-reward tasks.  
- Decision: Accept (Weak) — promising approach but needs more investigation and clarification.  
- Strength: Novel combination of intrinsic motivation and hierarchical RL to learn transferable skills with minimal domain supervision.  
- Strength: SNNs effectively represent multi-modal policies and the regularizer encourages skill diversity.  
- Strength: Empirical results show hierarchical skill use outperforms simply adding intrinsic task-level rewards.  
- Strength: Visualizations (e.g., visitation plots) clearly illustrate learned skills.  
- Weakness: Current SNN architectures (concatenation, bilinear) offer limited diversity, constraining performance on complex tasks.  
- Weakness: Hierarchical design is restrictive — fixed sub-policies and fixed skill execution horizon place heavy reliance on pre-training quality and chosen time horizon.  
- Weakness: Strong dependence on a well-tuned proxy reward for pretraining raises concerns about domain specificity.  
- Requested clarification: Explain why the baseline in Sec. 7.3 fails and whether it uses pre-training experience; clarify the term “CoM maze reward.”  
- Requested clarification: Define “Multi-policy” — is it separate independent policies or another SNN configuration?  
- Requested clarification: Confirm Fig.1 interpretation — does concatenation change first-layer bias while bilinear integration changes weights?  
- Suggested experiments: Provide ablation studies isolating contributions of concatenation vs bilinear integration and the information-theoretic regularizer.  
- Suggested expansion: Broaden discussion of limitations and outline future directions (e.g., learning switch times, adaptive skill selection).
