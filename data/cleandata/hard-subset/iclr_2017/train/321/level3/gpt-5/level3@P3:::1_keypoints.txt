- Proposes hierarchical RL: pre-train low-level multi-modal SNN skills with a proxy reward + MI regularizer, then train a high-level manager to switch skills for sparse-reward tasks.
- Technical choices: categorical latents, bilinear latent–state integration, and a discretized CoM-based MI bonus.
- Empirical claim: solves challenging sparse-reward locomotion mazes and a gather task, producing interpretable, reusable skills.
- Decision: Weak Accept.
- Main positive: interesting, practical hierarchical control with simple, effective SNN+MI design and strong empirical results on some benchmarks.
- Main negative: experimental analysis is shallow in critical areas, limiting clarity and reproducibility.
- Problem clarity: addresses long-horizon sparse-reward tasks by amortizing exploration via reusable skills—relevant and important.
- Relation to prior work: aligns with HRL and Heess et al. (2016); MI and bilinear choices fit InfoGAN-style and multi-modal policy literature.
- Terminology concern: calling the proxy reward “intrinsic motivation” is misleading; it’s task-aligned proxy reward pre-training.
- Empirical nuance: MI improves diversity and performance in Gather but not on harder mazes (1–3); this negative result lacks interpretation.
- Reproducibility gap: Sagent vs Srest factorization is underspecified (exact sensors, indices, encodings per task not listed).
- Switching behavior under-analyzed: interaction of commitment horizon T, skill set, manager policy, and failure modes (e.g., Ant instability) not probed quantitatively.
- MI estimator questions: discretization choices (grid size), bias/variance, and sensitivity to αH and state subset C not studied.
- Ablation gaps: need quantitative ablations on K, bilinear vs concatenation, and fair sample-budget comparisons versus multi-policy baselines.
- Diversity claims rely on visitation plots; quantitative metrics (coverage, pairwise divergence, option durations, MI on held-out rollouts) are missing.
- Suggested terminology fix: reframe “intrinsic motivation” as “proxy reward” or “self-supervised skill pre-training.”
- Requested MI analyses: report sensitivity to grid resolution/αH/C choice and measure actual MI or conditional entropy reductions.
- Reproducibility requests: precisely list Sagent and Srest per environment, K, bilinear layer details, and manager observation inputs.
- Switching analysis requests: report dwell time distributions, switching frequency, transition matrices, T ablations, and quantify switching-induced failures.
- Fairness/ablations: normalize pre-training budgets across baselines and include concatenation vs bilinear and manager architecture ablations (FF vs recurrent).
- Baselines: include or better discuss Option-Critic and Heess-style goal-conditioned pre-training differences/trade-offs.
- Questions for authors: specify exact Sagent/Srest components per environment and whether the manager sees both fully or uses masking.
- Questions for authors: report sensitivity of results to CoM grid resolution, αH, alternative C choices, and estimated MI over training.
- Questions for authors: provide quantitative performance vs K and bilinear vs concatenation beyond visitation plots.
- Questions for authors: show impact of T on manager learning/final performance and detail switching-related failure modes.
- Questions for authors: compare SNN vs multi-policy when given equal total pre-training sample budgets.
- Questions for authors: report Ant transition smoothing experiments, fall-rate metrics pre/post-switch, and any stabilization techniques.
- Questions for authors: did recurrent managers help mitigate long-T needs or improve performance on harder mazes?
