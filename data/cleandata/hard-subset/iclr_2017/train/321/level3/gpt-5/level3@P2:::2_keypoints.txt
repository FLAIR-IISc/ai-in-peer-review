- Problem & approach: Pretrain diverse temporally-extended skills as modes of a single SNN conditioned on categorical z, then learn a manager to sequence skills for sparse, long-horizon RL.
- Diversity objective: Encourage skill diversity via an MI regularizer between z and a subset of state (e.g., CoM position).
- Architecture: Bilinear integration of observation and z yields interpretable multimodal low-level policies; manager commits to a chosen z for T steps.
- Empirical results: Substantial improvement in exploration and downstream learning on maze and gather tasks versus standard policies and a CoM-proxy baseline; ablations support design choices.
- Reproducibility: Code and videos provided; multi-seed reporting and acknowledgement of variance included.
- Overall assessment: Simple, well-motivated combination of intrinsic motivation and hierarchy with clear visualizations and solid empirical evidence.
- Decision: Accept (score 7).
- Key reasons for accept: Novel specific combination (SNN skills + MI identifiability + hierarchical manager) and convincing empirical gains on hard sparse-reward tasks.
- Problem fit and positioning: Addresses sparse long-horizon control, clearly positioned relative to HRL, intrinsic motivation, prior skill discovery, and goal-conditioned pretraining.
- Evidence quality: Visitation plots, random-manager rollouts, learning curves, and ablations largely support claims; limitations are honestly discussed.
- Main caveats: Focus on locomotion domains, unclear fairness of the CoM-proxy downstream baseline w.r.t. pretraining budget, heuristic MI estimation, and lack of comparisons to strong HRL baselines like option-critic.
- Baselines & fairness suggestions: Equalize/report sample budgets including pretraining, clarify CoM baseline experience, and consider comparisons to option-critic/HiREPS and goal-conditioned pretraining.
- Diversity quantification suggestions: Provide quantitative identifiability/diversity metrics (classification accuracy, MI estimate, coverage entropy), sensitivity to α_H and MI grid resolution, and guidance on choosing K.
- Architecture & training suggestions: Clarify concatenation vs bilinear implementation and parameterization, report sensitivity to commitment horizon T and manager architecture, and report environment-steps and wall-clock for pretraining vs downstream.
- Generality & scope suggestions: Discuss proxy reward domain-specificity, test on manipulation/navigation tasks, and explore continuous latents or end-to-end fine-tuning.
- Robustness suggestions: For unstable agents (Ant), evaluate transition smoothing, warm-up when switching skills, or learned inter-skill transitions.
- Questions for authors — baseline/sample budgeting: Does the CoM-proxy baseline receive pretraining-equivalent experience, and are total env-steps matched across methods?
- Questions for authors — multi-policy vs SNN: Precisely define “multi-policy” (ensemble of K independent policies) versus a single SNN conditioned on z and confirm manager/training parity.
- Questions for authors — concatenation vs bilinear: Confirm whether concatenation conditions only first-layer bias and bilinear conditions the full first-layer weight matrix, and give implementation details.
- Questions for authors — MI estimator: What grid resolution is used for p̂(z|c), and how sensitive are results to this and to α_H (any mode collapse or fragmentation)?
- Questions for authors — diversity metrics: Can you report numeric MI/identifiability accuracy and visitation coverage for with/without MI and for concatenation vs bilinear?
- Questions for authors — manager time scale: How sensitive are results to T, and is there benefit from a learned termination policy?
- Questions for authors — transfer & reuse: Are pretrained skills reused unchanged across downstream tasks, and can one SNN generalize across different environment layouts without re-pretraining?
- Questions for authors — Ant instability: Have you tried transition smoothing or handover horizons to reduce falls at skill switches, and can you report fall rates per switch?
