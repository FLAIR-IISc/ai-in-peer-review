- Proposes a two-phase hierarchical RL framework: pretrain diverse low-level skills using an SNN with discrete latent and MI regularizer, then train a high-level Manager to select frozen skills at timescale T.  
- Pretraining uses a simple proxy reward (e.g., CoM speed) and an information-theoretic regularizer maximizing mutual information between latent and task-relevant state subset, with a discretized estimator.  
- Key technical choices: bilinear integration of latent and observations (better than concatenation) and TRPO training treating latent as part of the observation.  
- Experiments (Swimmer, Snake, Gather) show interpretable multi-directional locomotion skills from pretraining.  
- Hierarchical control substantially increases state-space coverage and exploration compared to standard methods.  
- Downstream learning succeeds on sparse-reward, long-horizon tasks where standard exploration fails; code and videos provided.  
- Decision: Accept — strong empirical evidence that SNN+MI pretraining with a simple Manager solves challenging sparse-reward benchmarks.  
- Method is presented as clean, general, and practical, advancing hierarchical control and sample-efficient exploration.  
- Tackles an important problem: reusable skills for families of sparse-reward, long-horizon tasks to reduce planning depth.  
- Motivation grounded in prior work (InfoGAN, Heess et al.); use of SNN + MI is elegant for inducing distinct skills.  
- Caveat: labeling the approach “intrinsically motivated” is misleading—proxy rewards are task-aligned shaping signals, not classical curiosity bonuses.  
- Ablations show bilinear integration > concatenation and MI improves skill separation and exploration metrics.  
- Qualitative evidence (visitation plots, videos) supports distinct skills and improved coverage under hierarchical control.  
- Learning curves show consistent gains and outperform strong baselines including shaped CoM reward and prior intrinsic-motivation methods.  
- Limitation: MI regularizer does not improve performance on more complex mazes (1–3) and lacks detailed diagnosis.  
- Sensitivity analyses (K, T, α_H, discretization grid) and switching behavior/failure modes are only partially addressed; more robustness reporting needed.  
- Reproducibility concern: unclear definition/factorization of S_agent vs. S_rest and exact observation interface for the Manager.  
- Recommend reframe terminology to “proxy-reward skill pretraining” rather than “intrinsic motivation.”  
- Request analyses: when MI helps downstream performance, skill-usage statistics, representative trajectories, and hypotheses for failures in mazes 1–3.  
- Ask for sensitivity sweeps (K, T, α_H, MI grid resolution) and report means ± CI across seeds for pretrain diversity and downstream returns.  
- Clarify MI estimator details (e.g., Laplace smoothing for p̂(Z|c), handling rarely visited cells, batch nonstationarity) and consider learned density models.  
- Suggest experiments/analysis on switching: quantify instabilities, dwell times, T tradeoffs, and try learned or termination-based switching as ablations.  
- Recommend stronger baseline comparisons (e.g., Heess et al. goal-conditioned pretraining, pretraining with multiple goal directions vs. MI).  
- Ask for additional diversity metrics: MI estimates, inter-skill KLs over action distributions, and quantitative spatial coverage metrics.  
- Note instability on Ant when switching; recommend summarizing mitigation attempts (warm-starts, interpolation) and clarifying generality of the limitation.  
- Questions for authors: provide exact S_agent/S_rest factorization table and Manager observation interface per environment.  
- Questions: sensitivity of performance and diversity to K, T, α_H, and grid resolution; any evidence of over-diversification harming controllability.  
- Questions: details on MI estimator smoothing, handling rare cells, and alternatives tried (learned densities).  
- Questions: does MI reduce locomotion efficiency during pretraining—report proxy reward vs. MI across α_H.  
- Questions: why MI fails to help mazes 1–3; show skill-selection histograms and usage along trajectories.  
- Questions: include per-pretrain-seed results in main paper to show robustness and variance across seeds.  
- Questions: did you try brief fine-tuning of low-level SNNs during downstream training and does it improve failures or Ant stability?  
- Questions: how general is the approach to tasks with strong S_rest–S_agent interaction (e.g., contact-rich manipulation), what proxy rewards and MI definitions would apply?
