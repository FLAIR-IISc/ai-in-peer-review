- Contribution: Pretrain diverse low-level skills in a proxy environment and train a high-level manager to sequence them for sparse-reward, long-horizon RL.
- Method: Model low-level policies as Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with a categorical latent to induce multimodal, shared-parameter skills.
- MI regularizer: Use mutual information between latent and a small state subset (e.g., CoM) to encourage distinct, interpretable skills.
- Hierarchy: Manager commits to a chosen skill for T steps to improve exploration and credit assignment.
- Empirics: Skills widen exploration and enable learning on locomotion+maze and gather benchmarks where standard exploration fails.
- Ablation: Bilinear integration outperforms concatenation for integrating latent into policy.
- Practicality: Framework is simple, sample-efficient (K skills for cost of one policy), and comes with code and engineering details.
- Decision: Weak accept.
- Main reasons for accept: Simple, effective hierarchical pretrain-and-reuse with SNNs; MI consistently improves skill diversity and interpretability and often downstream performance.
- Problem statement: Tackles efficient exploration and reuse of agent-centric, task-agnostic low-level skills for sparse-reward tasks.
- Positioning: Well placed in HRL/skill discovery literature, related to options, HiREPS, and Heess et al.; MI echoes InfoGAN-style disentanglement.
- Distinctions: Uses a single proxy reward, SNN-based multimodality with weight sharing, and a simple manager over frozen skills.
- Results support claims: SNN+MI yields diverse locomotion behaviors; random manager over skills covers larger areas than Gaussian/action-noise baselines.
- Downstream gains: Hierarchy outperforms strong baselines on mazes and gather, especially at longer horizons.
- Shortcomings in experiments: Analysis is thinner than results warrant; MI sometimes fails to boost performance on harder mazes and seed variance can be high.
- Misleading framing: Calling the proxy reward “intrinsic motivation” is misleading—it's a designer-specified extrinsic proxy aligned to pretraining.
- Request to clarify Sagent/Srest: Reproducibility requires explicit listing of observation components assigned to low-level and manager inputs.
- MI vs downstream: Ask for analysis on why MI helps diversity but not performance in some mazes, with hypotheses about wall-aided reorientation, overspecialization, and T interactions.
- Switching analysis: Request systematic studies of commitment horizon T, learned termination vs fixed T, and metrics on instability/failure after switches (e.g., Ant fall rate).
- Skill diversity metrics: Recommend quantitative diversity measures (coverage area, trajectory divergence, z-classifier confusion) and reported MI estimates.
- Ablations/baselines requested: SNN without bilinear, end-to-end fine-tuning vs frozen skills, varying K, comparisons to option-critic and goal-conditioned pretraining.
- MI implementation details: Report sensitivity to αH and grid density, use of learned posterior vs counting, and generalization beyond CoM targets.
- Variance reporting: Provide success rates across seeds and per-pretrained-SNN diagnostics predicting downstream success.
- Key author questions: Precisely define Sagent and Srest for all tasks and sensor channels.
- Key author questions: Sensitivity of results to αH, grid resolution, state-subset choice, and use of learned posteriors.
- Key author questions: How was T chosen for Swimmer and performance vs T?
- Key author questions: Provide manager statistics (skill selection distribution, dwell times, entropy) and correlate with success.
- Key author questions: Did end-to-end fine-tuning of low-level skills change results?
- Key author questions: For Ant, quantify failure probability after switches and explore mitigations (transition policies, smoother blending).
- Key author questions: How does performance scale with number of skills K and is there diminishing returns or redundancy?
- Key author questions: Can you directly compare to Heess et al. (2016)-style goal-conditioned pretraining under matched budgets to isolate MI benefits?
