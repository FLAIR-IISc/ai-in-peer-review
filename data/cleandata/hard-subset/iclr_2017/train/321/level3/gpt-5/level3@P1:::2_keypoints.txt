- Tackles long-horizon, sparse-reward RL by pretraining reusable skills and training a high-level manager to select them in downstream tasks.
- Key techniques: Stochastic Neural Networks (SNNs) with categorical latents for multimodal low-level policies.
- Key techniques: Information-theoretic regularizer maximizing mutual information between latent code and a subset of state (e.g., CoM) to encourage diverse, identifiable skills.
- Key techniques: Hierarchical controller that freezes pretrained skills and selects a skill every T steps via a manager policy.
- Empirical claim: Learns interpretable, diverse locomotion skills that substantially improve exploration and outperform strong baselines on Swimmer mazes and Gather.
- Empirical claim: Ablations show bilinear latent integration and MI regularization produce more clearly differentiated skills.
- Reproducibility: Visualizations are compelling and reproducible details are provided.
- Decision: Accept (7/10) — important challenge, simple well-motivated combination, and clear empirical gains.
- Reason for acceptance: Framework is general, requires minimal domain knowledge for pretrain rewards, and yields interpretable, reusable skills.
- Motivation/literature: Well-placed between hierarchical RL and intrinsic motivation, inspired by InfoGAN, and compared to Option-Critic/HiREPS and goal-based pretraining.
- Results validity: Visitation plots and multiple seeds support claims; authors acknowledge MI does not uniformly help all tasks.
- Limitation noted: Ant instability, fixed T, and frozen subpolicies are candidly discussed.
- Feedback — diversity/generalization: Current CoM proxy is locomotion-specific; suggest richer proxies or discussion for manipulation and how to choose c.
- Feedback — fixed subpolicies/timescale: Freezing skills and fixed T may constrain performance; suggest ablation with end-to-end fine-tuning or learned termination.
- Feedback — skill usefulness: Recommend reporting correlation between I(Z;C) and downstream performance and option-usage histograms.
- Feedback — MI estimation: Discuss sensitivity to discretization and alternative estimators (e.g., parametric p(z|c) classifier).
- Feedback — fairness/sample accounting: Clarify whether total env steps (pretrain + downstream) are matched across baselines and pretraining exposure.
- Feedback — clarity: Fix minor typos/formatting and add a schematic comparing “Multi-policy” vs “SNN” hierarchies.
- Question — baseline fairness: Does the “CoM in-task” baseline get matched total interaction and does it combine sparse extrinsic and CoM proxy rewards?
- Question — Multi-policy vs SNN: Confirm definitions and whether pretraining budgets were matched or SNN amortizes K skills for one-policy cost.
- Question — Fig.1 interpretation: Confirm concatenation acts as category-specific bias while bilinear yields category-specific input weight matrices.
- Question — sensitivity: Report sensitivity to K, MI weight α_H, and discretization density; does larger K yield diminishing returns or unused modes?
- Question — skill usage: Do downstream managers use most skills or only a subset; provide option-usage and switching frequency plots.
- Question — unstable robots: Any preliminary fixes for Ant instability (latent switching during pretrain, stabilizing priors/KL, or warm-start rollouts)?
