- Proposes a two-stage HRL framework: pretrain diverse skills with an SNN conditioned on discrete latent codes, then freeze skills and learn a high-level manager for sparse long-horizon tasks.  
- Key technical contributions: SNNs for multi-modal actions, bilinear integration of latent and observations, MI bonus on selected state components, and hierarchical composition trained with TRPO.  
- Empirical claims: visibly diverse locomotion skills and significant improvement on sparse-reward mazes and gather tasks, with ablations supporting bilinear integration and MI.  
- Decision: Weak accept — practical, well-motivated HRL with interpretable skills and open-sourced code, but some claims overreach given current evidence.  
- Positive placement in literature: Bridges intrinsic motivation and HRL, relates sensibly to options, InfoGAN-style MI, and pretraining-for-transfer work.  
- Bilinear integration is well argued and empirically beneficial compared to concatenation.  
- Concern: Failure modes increase with behavior/morphology complexity—MI on a small state subset may be insufficient as K and embodiment grow.  
- Concern: Switching/fixed commitment can destabilize complex agents (Ant), causing terminations and manager exploitation of few robust skills.  
- Concern: Discretization for MI estimation (grid size, bounds) is a nontrivial design choice that may bias results and limit scalability; continuous estimators are mentioned but not evaluated.  
- Concern: Generality is mixed—Snake results are encouraging but Ant exposes fragility; scaling to larger state/action spaces is not convincingly shown.  
- Concern: Claim of “minimal domain knowledge” is overstated—proxy reward (speed) and MI state choice (CoM x,y) are hand-designed and locomotion-specific.  
- Concern: Fixed switching time T and frozen skills may be suboptimal; limited sensitivity analyses and no learned termination are provided.  
- Request: Clarify what is discretized for MI, report sensitivity to grid resolution/bounds, and how grid choice affects diversity/downstream performance.  
- Request: Evaluate continuous MI estimators (e.g., classifier-based bounds) and compare to grid-based approach as K grows.  
- Request: Describe handling of coverage bias for low-count cells and any smoothing/prior used.  
- Request: Analyze scaling with K (saturation/collapse), provide quantitative diversity metrics (e.g., inter-skill distances or coverage), and report skill-usage distributions.  
- Request: Provide more evidence on embodiment generality (main-text Snake vs Swimmer figures, mitigation strategies for Ant instability, training time scaling with state/action dims).  
- Request: Soften claims about minimal domain knowledge, discuss selecting proxy/MI signals for other domains, explore learned subset c or less task-specific proxies (e.g., coverage/novelty).  
- Request: Expand sensitivity analysis for switch time T, test learned termination, consider manager shaping (skill-usage entropy), compare to hierarchical baselines with learned terminations, and report per-seed/statistical significance.
