- Problem & approach: Pretrain reusable skills in a proxy environment using SNNs with discrete latents, an MI diversity bonus, and a high-level Manager that sequences frozen skills for downstream sparse-reward tasks.
- Empirical claim: Single pretraining run yields diverse, interpretable skills and improves exploration and sample efficiency on mazes and a gather task.
- Architectures & analysis: Uses bilinear latent-observation integration, provides ablations, code, videos, and analyses including failure modes.
- Decision summary: Weak Accept — timely, well-motivated combination of intrinsic motivation and HRL that shows empirical gains.
- Main strength: Novel, practical fusion of SNNs + MI bonus + hierarchical reuse that demonstrably helps sparse, long-horizon RL.
- Main limitation: Proxy reward and MI discretization reduce generality; authors acknowledge and analyze these limits.
- Related work placement: Well-situated among HRL, intrinsic motivation, and skill-discovery lines, with clear differences highlighted.
- Evidence: Visitation plots and ablations support skill diversity; hierarchy improves transfer vs strong baselines; some generality beyond Swimmer shown.
- Failure cases: Instability for Ant under skill switching; sensitivity to MI coefficient and variability across pretrained SNNs.
- Method detail: Treating latent z as observation simplifies TRPO; Manager’s fixed commitment horizon T is analyzed; end-to-end fine-tuning left for future work.
- Feedback — proxy design: “Minimal domain knowledge” claim should be tempered since proxy reward and MI target require hand-design of informative state coordinates.
- Feedback — diversity risks: As modes grow, skill collapse/entanglement risk exists; recommend diagnostics (entropy, trajectory divergence) and anti-redundancy mechanisms.
- Feedback — MI estimation: Grid-based posterior may not scale; suggest learned p(z|ϕ(c)) classifiers and sensitivity analyses to grid resolution and batch size.
- Feedback — baselines: Add Option-Critic and goal-conditioned policy baselines or qualitative comparisons to strengthen positioning.
- Feedback — unstable morphologies: Ant failures suggest pretraining with mid-episode switches, per-skill entry policies, termination policies, or transition controllers.
- Feedback — skill economy: Discuss choosing K, pruning or merging redundant skills, and avoiding negative transfer or Manager overhead.
- Question — MI choices: How sensitive are results to choice of c (CoM vs pose), learned classifiers vs grids, grid resolution, and αH?
- Question — proxy rewards: What proxy rewards apply beyond locomotion (manipulation/contact-rich), and guidance for “minimal domain knowledge”?
- Question — scalability/embodiment: Any pretraining-with-switch experiments, end-to-end (Gumbel-Softmax) attempts, or expectations for more complex robots?
- Question — choosing K: How to select K, observed diminishing returns or collapse, and metrics/procedures for post-hoc merging/pruning?
- Question — sample accounting: Provide total sample counts for pretraining + downstream vs training from scratch (with/without intrinsic motivation).
- Question — Manager horizon: Robustness to commitment horizon T and any preliminary results for learned termination heads?
- Question — negative transfer: Do some pretrained skill sets hinder specific mazes, and can the Manager naturally ignore unhelpful skills or is pruning required?
- Overall takeaway: A compelling, practical HRL recipe with clear sparse-reward benefits that would be strengthened by clarifying hand-design assumptions, MI estimation, and scalability to complex embodiments.
