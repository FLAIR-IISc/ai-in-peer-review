- Tackles long-horizon, sparse-reward RL by pretraining diverse temporally-extended skills and composing them via a high-level manager.
- Key technical ideas: SNNs for multi-modal low-level policies; MI-based option-identifiability reward on a state subset (e.g., CoM) to encourage diversity; hierarchical controller that selects frozen skills at fixed intervals.
- Well positioned in HRL and intrinsic motivation literature, with clear visualizations, open-sourced code, and ablation studies (concatenation vs bilinear; MI bonus).
- Decision: Accept (score 7) — sound, well-motivated approach with strong empirical evidence that hierarchical abstraction and enforced diversity beat directly mixing intrinsic and sparse rewards.
- Problem addressed: Effective exploration under sparse rewards and transfer of exploration structure across related tasks; formulation and shared-agent-space assumption are reasonable.
- Motivation and placement: Synthesizes SNN-based multi-modality with an MI regularizer, bridging intrinsic motivation and HRL; related work coverage is adequate.
- Empirical support: Directly combining intrinsic (CoM) and sparse rewards performs poorly; bilinear integration + MI yields more distinct skills and better exploration; hierarchical training improves sample efficiency across tasks.
- Ask to clarify the “strong baseline” in Sec. 7.3: is it exactly the CoM maze reward, why it underperforms, and whether total environment interactions are matched.
- Ask to confirm terminology: “multi-policy” = K independent Gaussian policies vs “snn” = single SNN with K latent codes.
- Ask to confirm Fig.1 interpretation: concatenation acts as a bias selector for the first hidden layer; bilinear integration selects a different input weight matrix.
- Limitations: Skill expressivity is modest—concatenation/bilinear may suffice for locomotion but may not diversify manipulation; fixed skills and commitment time make pretraining task-shaped and reliant on domain-specific MI (CoM).
- Requests for additional experiments/details: report how many distinct skills the manager uses, option-switch sensitivity to T, and control experiments equalizing total samples between multi-policy and snn.
- Positive note: Visualizations are compelling and the direction is promising.
